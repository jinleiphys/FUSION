# Stability of Covariant Relativistic Quantum Theory

> In this paper we study the relativistic quantum mechanical interpretation of the solution of the inhomogeneous Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter equation. Our goal is to determine conditions on the input to the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter equation so the solution can be used to construct a model Hilbert space and a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group. We prove three theorems that relate the stability of this construction to properties of the kernel and driving term of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The most interesting result is that the positivity of the Hilbert space norm in the non-interacting theory is not stable with respect to Euclidean covariant perturbations defined by Bethe-Salpeter kernels. The long-term goal of this work is to understand which model Euclidean Green functions preserve the underlying relativistic quantum theory of the original field theory. Understanding the constraints imposed on the Green functions by the existence of an underlying relativistic quantum theory is an important consideration for formulating field-theory motivated relativistic quantum models.

## Key claim

The paper proves that when the inhomogeneous Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved using a free-field two-point Schwinger function as the driving term, the positivity of the resulting Hilbert space norm (reflection positivity) is unstable with respect to arbitrarily small Euclidean-covariant Bethe-Salpeter kernels, contrary to the expectation that small perturbations would preserve the quantum mechanical interpretation.

## Method

The authors analyze the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter equation for the four-point Schwinger function, where the driving term is the free-field two-point Schwinger function and the kernel is a Euclidean-covariant model kernel. They derive necessary conditions for reflection positivity on subspaces of test functions with ordered positive Euclidean times, and test whether the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation satisfies these conditions. Three theorems are proved: the first two establish the instability of reflection positivity for arbitrarily small kernels when the driving term is the free two-point function; the third theorem shows that this instability proof breaks down if the free two-point function is replaced by a two-point function with a Kallen-Lehmann weight having a continuous component in its mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The instability result holds for "arbitrarily small Euclidean-covariant kernels" when the driving term is the free-field two-point Schwinger function; no specific numerical bound on the kernel size is given in the text.
* The paper proves three theorems relating stability to properties of the kernel and driving term; the most interesting result is that "the positivity of the Hilbert space norm in the non-interacting theory is not stable with respect to Euclidean covariant perturbations defined by Bethe-Salpeter kernels."
* The third theorem shows the instability proof breaks down when the two-point function has a Kallen-Lehmann weight with "a continuous component to its mass spectrum"; no specific mass values or spectral densities are provided.

## Context

This work extends the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction program (Osterwalder and Schrader, 1973) by examining whether model Euclidean Green functions constructed via the Bethe-Salpeter equation can define a relativistic quantum theory. The paper directly addresses the common approximation in applications (e.g., lattice QCD as discussed in Montvay, perturbative methods, and Schwinger-Dyson equations as in Roberts) where the free two-point function replaces the exact one. The results show that this approximation is unstable under small Euclidean-covariant perturbations, motivating the need for more realistic two-point functions with continuous mass spectra. The long-term goal is to understand which model Euclidean Green functions preserve the underlying relativistic quantum theory, enabling the formulation of field-theory motivated relativistic quantum models.
