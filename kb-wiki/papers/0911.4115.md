# Surface roughness effect on ultracold neutron interaction with a wall and implications for computer simulations

> We review the diffuse scattering and the loss coefficient in ultracold neutron reflection from slightly rough surfaces, report a surprising reduction in loss coefficient due to roughness, and discuss the possibility of transition from quantum treatment to ray optics. The results are used in a computer simulation of neutron storage in a recent neutron lifetime experiment that re-ported a large discrepancy of neutron lifetime with the current particle data value. Our partial re-analysis suggests the possibility of systematic effects that were not included in this publication.

# Surface roughness effect on ultracold neutron interaction with a wall and implications for computer simulations

## Key claim

Surface roughness can reduce the loss coefficient for ultracold neutrons reflecting from a wall, contrary to a general statement in Ignatovich (prior work), with the reduction occurring for small mean-square slope (α² << 1) and near-normal incidence; this roughness correction depends only on mean-square slope and is independent of the specific roughness model used, but the effect reverses for grazing incidence where loss is enhanced.

## Method

The authors extended first-order perturbation theory for diffuse scattering of ultracold neutrons from slightly rough surfaces to second order, using a distorted Green's function method to compute the interference terms between the specular and second-order perturbed wave functions. They derived the loss coefficient for a rough wall by combining the specular reflectivity, first-order interference, second-order interference, and first-order diffuse scattering intensity, then evaluated the resulting double integrals analytically for small mean-square slope using expansions in terms of the roughness spectrum's moments (mean-square height, mean-square slope, and mean-square curvature). Numerical results were obtained for both a Gaussian correlation model and a K₀-model (representing liquid-like surfaces), and the analytical approximations were compared to exact numerical integrations.

## Key numbers

- Mean-square slope α² = 2b²/w² for the Gaussian model, where b is root-mean-square roughness and w is lateral correlation length
- For the numerical examples: k_c0 = 0.0802 nm⁻¹ (critical wave number for LTF oil in glass state at ~110 K), α = 10⁻³, w = 2 μm, giving b = 1.4 nm
- Total diffusely scattered intensity for η=0: p_D0(θ_i) ≈ 4k²b²cos²θ_i (first term of asymptotic expansion, valid for α << 1 and kw >> 1)
- Relative roughness correction to loss coefficient: Δμ(θ_i)/μ₀(θ_i) = -α²/[2(1-ζ²c_i²)] to leading order, where ζ = k/k_c0 ≤ 1 and c_i = cosθ_i
- Isotropic-flux average of diffuse fraction: ⟨p_D0⟩ = 2k²b²
- Isotropic-flux average of roughness correction: ⟨Δμ⟩/⟨μ₀⟩ ≈ k_c0²b²[1+3(ζk_c0w/2)²]⁻³/⁴(1-ζ⁴/2)
- For ζ << 1: ⟨μ₀⟩ = 4ηζ/3; for ζ = 1: ⟨μ₀⟩ = πη

## Context

This work extends the second-order perturbation analysis of Ignatovich (prior work) by providing the first numerical evaluation of the roughness effect on the loss coefficient, which had been derived analytically but never numerically evaluated or applied. The results contradict a general statement in Ignatovich (prior work) that roughness always increases loss, while confirming a decrease predicted for a specific model in a separate prior work. The findings are directly applied to a re-analysis of the neutron lifetime experiment of Serebrov et al. (2005), which reported a value six standard deviations from the world average; the authors suggest that systematic effects from surface roughness, particularly for very low energy neutrons "hopping" near their maximum jump height, may not have been adequately included in that experiment's Monte Carlo simulations. The work also provides practical approximations (Eqs. 28 and 29) that can be used for analytical averaging over UCN spectra under gravity in storage experiments.
