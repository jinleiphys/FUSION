# Additive empirical parametrization and microscopic study of the deuteron breakup

> Comparative assessment of the total breakup proton-emission cross sections measured for 56 MeV deuteron interaction with target nuclei from $^{12}$C to $^{209}$Bi, with an empirical parametrization and recently calculated microscopic neutron-removal cross sections has been done at the same time with similar data measured at 15, 25.5, 70, and 80 MeV. Comparable mass dependances of the elastic-breakup (EB) cross sections provided by the empirical parametrization and the microscopic results have been also found at the deuteron energy of 56 MeV, while the assessment of absolute-values variance up to a factor of two has been not possible due to the lack of EB measurements at energies higher than 25.5 MeV. While the similarities represent an additional validation of the microscopic calculations, the cross-section difference should be considered within the objectives of further measurements.

# Additive empirical parametrization and microscopic study of the deuteron breakup

## Key claim
The empirical parametrization and microscopic CDCC-based calculations show comparable mass dependences for elastic-breakup cross sections at 56 MeV, but a variance of absolute values up to a factor of two could not be assessed due to the lack of elastic-breakup measurements at energies above 25.5 MeV.

## Method
The authors compared total breakup proton-emission cross sections measured by Matsuoka et al. (1980) for 56 MeV deuterons on targets from 12C to 209Bi with two approaches: an empirical parametrization of total breakup and elastic-breakup fractions (f_BU and f_EB) as functions of target Z, A, and deuteron energy E, and microscopic neutron-removal cross sections calculated using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method within the eikonal reaction theory. The empirical parametrization was normalized at energies above the maximum of the elastic-breakup fraction and constrained for targets with A > 200 to keep the total breakup fraction below 0.9.

## Key numbers
- Total breakup proton-emission cross sections were measured for 56 MeV deuterons on targets from 12C to 209Bi by Matsuoka et al. (1980), and compared with similar data at 15, 25.5, 70, and 80 MeV.
- The empirical parametrization for the total breakup nucleon-emission fraction is f_BU^{n/p} = 0.087 - 0.0066 Z + 0.00163 Z A^{1/3} + 0.0017 A^{1/3} E - 0.000002 Z E^2, valid for targets from 27Al to 232Th and energies up to 80 MeV.
- The elastic-breakup fraction parametrization is f_EB = 0.031 - 0.0028 Z + 0.00051 Z A^{1/3} + 0.0005 A^{1/3} E - 0.000001 Z E^2, valid for energies up to 30 MeV.
- For targets with A > 200, the total breakup fraction was constrained not to exceed 0.9 of the total-reaction cross section.
- Corrections for (d,p) stripping-reaction contribution to the neutron-removal cross sections were less than 1% at 56 MeV.
- The elastic-breakup cross sections from the empirical parametrization and microscopic results at 56 MeV differ by up to a factor of two.

## Context
This work extends the empirical parametrization developed by the same authors (Avrigeanu and Avrigeanu, referenced as FED) for deuteron breakup systematics, and compares it with microscopic CDCC calculations by Neoh et al. (referenced as neoh) for neutron-removal cross sections at 56 MeV/nucleon. The study is motivated by the need for improved nuclear data for fusion and accelerator applications (ITER, IFMIF, SPIRAL2-NFS) and medical deuteron therapy. The results validate the microscopic calculations for elastic-breakup mass dependence but highlight the need for new elastic-breakup measurements above 25.5 MeV to resolve absolute cross-section discrepancies, and for additional total breakup data for heavy targets (A > 200) to confirm the imposed constraint.
