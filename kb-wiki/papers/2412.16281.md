# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: $η$ and $η'$ poles

> The pseudoscalar-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering are determined by the respective transition form factors (TFFs) into two virtual photons. These TFFs constitute complicated functions of the photon virtualities that, in turn, can be reconstructed in a dispersive approach from their discontinuities. In this work, we present such an analysis for the $η^{(\prime)}$ TFFs, implementing a number of constraints from both experiment and theory: normalizations from the $η^{(\prime)}\toγγ$ decay widths, unitarity constraints from the $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^+π^-γ$ spectra, chiral symmetry for the $η^{(\prime)}\to 2(π^+π^-)$ amplitudes, vector-meson couplings, singly-virtual data from $e^+e^-\to e^+e^-η^{(\prime)}$, and the asymptotic behavior predicted by the light-cone expansion. In particular, we account for the leading left-hand-cut singularity by including effects from the $a_2$ resonance, necessitating the solution of an inhomogeneous Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem via a carefully chosen path deformation. The resulting TFFs allow us to evaluate the $η^{(\prime)}$-pole contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ^{η\text{-pole}}=14.7(9)\times 10^{-11}$ and $a_μ^{η'\text{-pole}}=13.5(7)\times 10^{-11}$, completing a dedicated program for the lowest-lying pseudoscalar intermediate states in a dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering, $a_μ^{\text{PS-poles}}=91.2^{+2.9}_{-2.4}\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The authors present a dispersive reconstruction of the η and η' transition form factors (TFFs) that accounts for the leading left-hand-cut singularity from the a₂(1320) resonance, requiring the solution of an inhomogeneous Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem via a carefully chosen path deformation, and they report the resulting η- and η'-pole contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with uncertainties that are competitive with or smaller than previous estimates.

## Method

The η and η' TFFs are decomposed into isovector, isoscalar, effective-pole, and asymptotic contributions. The isovector part is reconstructed dispersively from the η' → 2(π⁺π⁻) decay amplitude, using unitarity constraints from η' → π⁺π⁻γ spectra and chiral symmetry, with the a₂ resonance treated as a left-hand cut via an inhomogeneous Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem. The integral in the Omnès solution is evaluated by deforming the integration contour into the complex plane to avoid branch cuts from the Källén function and the logarithm in the a₂ amplitude. Isoscalar contributions and effective poles are included to match the experimental normalizations and singly-virtual data from e⁺e⁻ → e⁺e⁻η⁽'⁾, and the asymptotic behavior from the light-cone expansion is imposed at high virtualities.

## Key numbers

- η-pole contribution to a_μ: a_μ^{η-pole} = 14.7(9) × 10⁻¹¹
- η'-pole contribution to a_μ: a_μ^{η'-pole} = 13.5(7) × 10⁻¹¹
- Total pseudoscalar-pole contribution (π⁰, η, η') from the dispersive program: a_μ^{PS-poles} = 91.2^{+2.9}_{-2.4} × 10⁻¹¹
- Experimental TFF normalization for η: F^{exp}_{ηγγ} = 0.2736(48) GeV⁻¹, derived from e⁺e⁻ → e⁺e⁻η
- Experimental TFF normalization for η': F^{exp}_{η'γγ} = 0.3437(55) GeV⁻¹, from the global η' fit of the Review of Particle Physics

## Context

This work completes a dedicated dispersive program for the lowest-lying pseudoscalar intermediate states in hadronic light-by-light scattering, extending the earlier dispersive analysis for the π⁰ TFF by Hoferichter et al. (2018) and building on previous studies of the η⁽'⁾ TFFs by Stollenwerk et al. (2011), Hanhart et al. (2013), Kubis et al. (2015), and Holz et al. (2015, 2022). The results improve upon the white-paper consensus a_μ^{HLbL} = 92(19) × 10⁻¹¹ from Aoyama et al. (2020) and enable a more precise dispersive evaluation of the full hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, which is needed to match the experimental precision from the Fermilab experiment.
