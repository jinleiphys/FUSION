# Conserved charge fluctuations are not conserved during the hadronic phase

> We study the correlation between the distributions of the net-charge, net-kaon, net-baryon and net-proton number at hadronization and after the final hadronic decoupling by simulating ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions with the hybrid version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model. We find that due to the hadronic rescattering these distributions are not strongly correlated. The calculated change of the correlation, during the hadronic expansion stage, does not support the recent paradigm, namely that the measured final moments of the experimentally observed distributions do give directly the values of those distributions at earlier times, when the system had been closer to the QCD crossover.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hadronic rescattering after hadronization strongly decorrelates the distributions of net-charge, net-kaon, net-baryon, and net-proton numbers between the hadronization hypersurface and the final observed state, contradicting the assumption that measured final cumulants directly reflect values near the QCD crossover.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid version of the UrQMD model, which couples a 3+1 dimensional ideal fluid dynamical evolution for the hot and dense phase with the UrQMD hadronic transport cascade for the non-equilibrium initial and final stages. Particles were produced from the fluid via the Cooper-Frye prescription on an iso-energy density hypersurface of approximately 350 MeV/fm³, with global conservation of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge enforced during sampling. A time-dependent correlation function r_IF(t) was defined to quantify the correlation between the net charge in a given rapidity window at time t and the final net charge in the same acceptance after all interactions cease.

## Key numbers

- At the particle production time (approximately 10 fm/c), the correlation r_IF(t) is approximately 0.5 for most net-charges in central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, for the rapidity interval |y| < 0.5.
- The Gaussian width of the change in net-proton number during the full hadronic rescattering phase is σ = 6.5 at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV, compared to the final net-proton distribution width of σ_final = 7.5.
- The Gaussian width of the change in net-proton number is approximately constant across beam energies: 6.5 at 7.7 GeV, 6.3 at 11 GeV, and 6.9 at 200 GeV.
- The correlation between the Cooper-Frye hypersurface and the final state shows only a very weak beam energy dependence down to √s_NN = 7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Shuryak and Stephanov (2000), who showed via the Fokker-Planck equation that the hadronic phase changes long-range charge correlations, and by Kitazawa and Asakawa (2012) and Jeon (2000), who found that hadronic rescattering may significantly impact observed cumulants. The results contradict the paradigm advanced by Karsch (2010) and others that measured final moments directly give values at earlier times near the QCD crossover. The paper enables a more cautious interpretation of experimental cumulant measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, suggesting that without accounting for hadronic rescattering effects, comparisons to lattice QCD or hadron resonance gas model calculations may produce misleading conclusions about the QCD phase structure.
