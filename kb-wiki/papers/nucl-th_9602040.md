# Evaporation of light particles from a hot, deformed and rotating nucleus

> The dependence of the transmission coefficient on the deformation, the collective rotation and excitation energy of the compound nucleus emitting light particles is introduced in the framework of Weißkopf's evaporation theory. The competition between fission and particle evaporation is treated by a~Langevin equation for the fission variable coupled to the emission process. Detailed calculations are presented on the decay of different Gd and Yb isotopes at an excitation energy of about 250~MeV. These calculations demonstrate the importance of the effects of nuclear deformation and of the initial spin distribution on the evaporation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the transmission coefficient for light particle evaporation from a hot compound nucleus depends significantly on nuclear deformation and, to a lesser extent, on collective rotation, contrary to the standard Weisskopf evaporation theory which only includes deformation through level densities. The authors show that these effects are important for describing the decay of Gd and Yb isotopes at excitation energies around 250 MeV, but the calculations are limited to symmetric fission and neglect photon emission.

## Method

The authors couple a Langevin equation for the fission coordinate to a statistical evaporation model based on Weisskopf theory, using a single collective coordinate q = ρ_cm/R_0 for symmetric fission. The transmission coefficients for neutron, proton, and alpha emission are averaged over emission directions and the nuclear surface, then incorporated into the Langevin dynamics through a Monte Carlo algorithm that decides at each time step whether a particle is emitted and of which type. The potential V(q) is calculated as the difference between Helmholtz free energies of deformed and spherical nuclei, while friction coefficients come from the wall and window friction model, with the diffusion coefficient related to friction through the Einstein relation D(q) = γ(q) T.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies studied: 150 MeV to 300 MeV for various Gd and Yb isotopes
- Initial angular momentum values: L = 0 ħ to 60 ħ, with detailed calculations at L = 40 ħ and L = 45 ħ
- For 160Yb at 293 MeV excitation energy and L = 45 ħ, the transient time for fission current buildup is approximately 8 × 10⁻²¹ seconds
- Alpha particle emission rate grows by 10%–20% as rotational angular momentum increases from 0 ħ to 60 ħ
- Emission of protons and alpha particles ceases after about 3 × 10⁻²¹ seconds for 160Yb at 293 MeV and L = 45 ħ, while neutron emission continues
- Calculations use approximately 10⁶ trajectories for spectral distributions

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach of Str91 (1991) and the Langevin-based fission descriptions of Frobrich, Abe, and Carjan (Fro92, Abe90) by incorporating deformation-dependent transmission coefficients into the evaporation width, following earlier work by Dietrich (Die95). The paper builds on Kramers' original transport equation for fission (Kra40) and the recognition by Grange and Weidenmuller (Gra79) of non-statistical aspects in fission dynamics. The results enable more realistic comparisons with experimental evaporation and fission data, particularly for nuclei with large ground-state deformations like Gd and Yb isotopes, and point toward future inclusion of asymmetric fission and gamma emission.
