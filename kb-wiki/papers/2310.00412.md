# Determination of the moments of the proton charge density: is there a proton radius puzzle?

> The charge radius of the proton can be determined using two different kinds of experiments: the spectroscopy technique, measuring the hyperfine structure of hydrogen atoms, and the scattering technique, deducing the radius from elastic lepton scattering off a proton target. These two methods lead to quite different results, a discrepancy known as the "proton radius puzzle ". To shed light on this problem, we have proposed a novel method for the determination of spatial moments from densities expressed in the momentum space. This method provides a direct access not only to the second order moment, directly related to the proton radius, but to all moments of any real order larger than -3. The method is applied to the global analysis of proton electric form factor experimental data from Rosenbluth separation and low-$Q^2$ experiments, paying specific attention to the evaluation of the systematic errors. Within this analysis, the integer order moments of the proton charge density are evaluated, the moment of second order leading to a new determination of the proton charge radius.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel "integral method" for determining spatial moments of the proton charge density directly from momentum-space form factor data, which overcomes the limitation of the standard derivative method that can only access even-order moments. The method yields a proton charge radius of 0.8261 ± 0.0012 (stat.) ± 0.0076 (syst.) fm, which is in fair agreement with the muonic hydrogen spectroscopy value, suggesting the "proton radius puzzle" may be resolvable through improved scattering data analysis.

## Method

The authors developed an integral method that evaluates moments of the charge density ⟨r^λ⟩ for any real order λ > -3 by regularizing the Fourier transform of the form factor G_E(k^2) using an exponential convergence factor e^{-εr} and taking the weak limit as ε → 0⁺. They applied this method to a global analysis of 21 experimental data sets of the proton electric form factor G_E(k^2) from Rosenbluth separation and low-Q² experiments, covering k² values up to 226 fm⁻² (8.8 GeV²), using a simultaneous fit with a polynomial ratio parameterization (Kelly model) multiplied by a normalization factor per data set.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: R_p = 0.8261 ± 0.0012 (stat.) ± 0.0076 (syst.) fm, determined from the second moment ⟨r²⟩
- Reduced chi-squared of the global fit: χ²_r = 2.96
- Integration cut-off used: Q² = 52 fm⁻², which only impacts negative order moments
- Second moment (truncated): ⟨r²⟩_Q = 0.6824 fm² (identical to the asymptotic value and to the derivative method result)
- First moment (truncated): ⟨r¹⟩_Q = 0.7186 fm (asymptotic value: 0.7158 fm)
- Negative second moment (truncated): ⟨r⁻²⟩_Q = 6.5826 fm⁻² (asymptotic value: 8.9093 fm⁻²)
- The method provides meaningful moment determination up to λ = 5; for λ ≥ 6 statistical errors become too large (e.g., ⟨r⁶⟩ = -3.6823 ± 2.0835 stat. ± 9.5186 syst. fm⁶)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formal development of the integral method (Hoballah 2020) and applies it to resolve the "proton radius puzzle" originating from the discrepancy between electron scattering measurements (Bernauer 2010, 0.879(8) fm) and muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, 0.84184(67) fm). The method addresses criticisms of the scattering technique raised by Lee (2015) and Sick (2017) regarding the limited accuracy of zero-momentum extrapolation. The result (0.8261 fm) is in fair agreement with the muonic hydrogen value and the recent PRad result (Xiong 2019, 0.831(14) fm), and the approach enables future applications to magnetic charge distributions and relativistic nucleon structure studies.
