# The broad away side of azimuthal correlations: 3 vs 2 final state particles in high energy nuclear collisions

> In high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC there are important aspects of the medium induced dynamics, that are still not well understood. In particular, there is a broadening and even a double hump structure of the away-side peak appearing in azimuthal correlation studies in Au+Au collisions which is absent in p+p collisions at the same energies. These features are already present but suppressed in p+p collisions: 2 to 3 parton processes produce such structures but are suppressed with respect to 2 to 2 processes. We argue that in A+A collisions the different geometry for the trajectories of 3 as opposed to 2 particles in the final state, together with the medium induced energy loss effects on the different cross sections, create a scenario that enhances processes with 3 particles in the final state, which gives on average this double hump structure.

## Key claim

The double-hump structure observed in the away-side of azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by the enhancement of 2→3 parton processes relative to 2→2 processes, caused by medium-induced energy loss and the different path-length geometry for three versus two final-state particles, without invoking collective phenomena such as Mach cones or triangular flow.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross sections for 2→2 and 2→3 parton processes in both p+p and Au+Au collisions at √S = 200 GeV, using CTEQ6 parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions. For Au+Au collisions, they replaced the vacuum fragmentation functions with modified fragmentation functions from Zhang et al. that incorporate medium-induced radiative energy loss, parameterized by the average number of scatterings ⟨L/λ⟩ and the average energy loss ΔE. They also computed the distribution of path lengths for away-side particles in a nuclear overlap area with a non-uniform density of scattering centers.

## Key numbers

- At √S = 200 GeV, the ratio of the 2→3 to 2→2 cross section ratio in Au+Au divided by the same ratio in p+p is approximately 2.26, indicating a relative enhancement of 2→3 processes in heavy-ion collisions.
- The calculation considered hadrons carrying 10 GeV/c at midrapidity (y_i = 0) as a representative kinematic point.
- For 2→2 processes, the away-side peak appears at Δφ = π; for 2→3 processes, the peaks appear at Δφ = 2π/3 and 4π/3.
- The minimum path length for 2→3 processes (L_min^{2→3}) is smaller than the path length for 2→2 processes (L^{2→2}), and the maximum path length for 2→3 processes (L_max^{2→3}) is larger than L^{2→2}.

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle of the broad away-side and double-hump structure in azimuthal correlations observed by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC, which was absent in p+p collisions. It contrasts with models invoking Mach cones or Cerenkov radiation from medium response (proposed by various authors) and triangular flow from initial geometry fluctuations (triangularity). The authors argue that their mechanism, based on perturbative QCD with energy loss, can be tested by three-particle correlation measurements, which they state are needed to distinguish their scenario from collective-flow-based explanations.
