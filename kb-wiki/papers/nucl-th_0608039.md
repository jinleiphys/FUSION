# Meson spectroscopy and properties using Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We study pseudoscalar and scalar mesons using a practical and symmetry preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. We investigate and compare properties of ground and radially excited meson states. In addition to exact results for radial meson excitations we also present results for meson masses and decay constants from the chiral limit up to the charm-quark mass, e. g., the mass of the chi_{c0} (2P) meson.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, which successfully describes meson ground states, also yields predictions for radially excited mesons; however, the masses and properties of radial excitations depend on the model parameter ω (the range of the infrared interaction), unlike ground-state properties which remain constant when ωD is fixed.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark pairs using the rainbow-ladder truncation, with the dressed-quark propagator determined from the QCD gap equation. The effective running coupling used an Ansatz with perturbative QCD ultraviolet behavior and an infrared enhancement parameterized by a range ω and strength D, with parameters fixed to reproduce the pion mass, decay constant, and chiral condensate. Results were computed for pseudoscalar and scalar mesons from the chiral limit up to the charm-quark mass.

## Key numbers

- For the K(1460) radial excitation, the ratio M_Kex/M_piex was calculated as 1.167; using the experimental M_piex = 1.3 ± 0.1 GeV yields M_Kex = 1.52 ± 0.12 GeV.
- The ratio f_Kex/f_piex for leptonic decay constants of these excited states was found to be approximately 10.
- The ratio of calculated masses for the χ_c0(2P) to the η_c(2S) is 1.066; using the experimental η_c(2S) mass of 3.64 GeV predicts the χ_c0(2P) mass to be 3.88 GeV.
- For u/d quarks (current mass 0.0037 GeV at ζ=19 GeV): f_gr = 0.092 GeV, -f_ex = 0.0015 GeV, ratio -f_ex/f_gr = 0.016.
- For s quarks (current mass 0.0835 GeV at ζ=19 GeV): f_gr = 0.13 GeV, -f_ex = 0.023 GeV, ratio -f_ex/f_gr = 0.18.
- For c quarks (current mass 0.905 GeV at ζ=19 GeV): f_gr = 0.23 GeV, -f_ex = 0.19 GeV, ratio -f_ex/f_gr = 0.83.

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of pseudoscalar meson radial excitations by Krassnigg (2003) and Holl (2004) to include scalar mesons and quark masses up to the charm quark. It builds on the successful rainbow-ladder truncation used extensively by Maris (1997, 1999) for meson ground states. The results enable predictions for unobserved states such as the χ_c0(2P) and provide a method to probe the long-range part of the strong interaction through the parameter dependence of radial excitation properties, with further work planned on vector meson radial excitations.
