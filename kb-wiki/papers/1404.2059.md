# Oblate deformation of light neutron-rich even-even nuclei

> Light neutron-rich even-even nuclei, of which the ground state is oblately deformed, are looked for, examining the Nilsson diagram based on realistic Woods-Saxon potentials. One-particle energies of the Nilsson diagram are calculated by solving the coupled differential equations obtained from the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space with the proper asymptotic behavior for $r \rightarrow \infty$ for both one-particle bound and resonant levels. The eigenphase formalism is used in the calculation of one-particle resonant energies. Large energy gaps on the oblate side of the Nilsson diagrams are found to be related to the magic numbers for the oblate deformation of the harmonic-oscillator potential where the frequency ratios ($ω_{\perp} : ω_{z}$) are simple rational numbers. In contrast, for the prolate deformation the magic numbers obtained from simple rational ratios of ($ω_{\perp} : ω_{z}$) of the harmonic-oscillator potential are hardly related to the particle numbers, at which large energy gaps appear in the Nilsson diagrams based on realistic Woods-Saxon potentials. The argument for an oblate shape of $^{42}_{14}$Si$_{28}$ is given. Among light nuclei the nucleus $^{20}_{6}$C$_{14}$ is found to be a good candidate for having the oblate ground state. In the region of the mass number $A \approx 70$ the oblate ground state may be found in the nuclei around $^{76}_{28}$Ni$_{48}$ in addition to $^{64}_{28}$Ni$_{36}$.

# Oblate deformation of light neutron-rich even-even nuclei

## Key claim
Large energy gaps on the oblate side of Nilsson diagrams based on realistic Woods-Saxon potentials can be reliably related to the magic numbers from the deformed harmonic-oscillator potential with simple rational frequency ratios, while on the prolate side such correspondence is absent; this allows prediction of candidate oblate ground-state nuclei, but the actual ground-state shape depends on both proton and neutron numbers and the shell-structure change in very neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method
One-particle energies for bound and resonant levels were calculated by solving coupled differential equations from the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space with proper asymptotic behavior for r → ∞, using Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters from Bohr and Mottelson (1969). The eigenphase formalism was used to determine one-particle resonant energies, defined as energies where an eigenphase increases through π/2. Nilsson diagrams were constructed for neutrons in the deformation region |β| < 0.6, including resonant levels for nuclei close to the neutron drip line.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴²Si, the energy distance ε(2p₃/₂) − ε(1f₇/₂) is only 1.65 MeV, much smaller than the N=28 energy gap known in the j-j coupling shell-model
- The observed ratio E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.93(5) for ⁴²Si from Steppenbeck and Takeuchi (2013)
- For ²⁰C, measured E(2₁⁺) ≈ 1.6 MeV and B(E2;2₁⁺ → 0⁺) ≈ 7.5 e²fm⁴ from Moschner and Petri (2008, 2011)
- Large energy gaps on the oblate side appear at N=14, 18, 28 for ⁴²Si at β ≈ −0.4; at N=6, 8, 14 for ²⁰C; and at N=36 and 48 in the A ≈ 70 region
- The Ωπ = 1/2⁺ level connected to 2s₁/₂ in ²⁰C cannot survive as a resonant state for β < −0.215

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies of shell-structure change in neutron-rich nuclei (Hamamoto 2007, 2012) and addresses the longstanding puzzle of prolate dominance in deformed nuclei discussed by Hamamoto and Mottelson (2009). The analysis uses the deformed harmonic-oscillator magic numbers from Bohr and Mottelson (1975) as a reference. The paper provides a simple, intuitive explanation for the oblate shape of ⁴²Si, complementing shell-model calculations by Furutani and Neyens (2009) and Yoneda and Utsuno (2012) that used harmonic-oscillator wave-functions without the shell-structure change from weakly-bound neutrons. The results enable targeted experimental searches for oblate ground states in ²⁰C, ⁴²Si, ⁶⁴Ni, and ⁷⁶Ni.
