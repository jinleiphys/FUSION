# Isoscaling behavior in $^{48,40}$Ca+$^{9}$Be collisions at intermediate energy investigated by HIPSE model

> The fragment production cross sections for 140 MeV/nucleon $^{48,40}$Ca$+^{9}$Be reactions have been calculated by the Heavy Ion Phase Space Exploration (HIPSE) model. Isoscaling behavior is observed. The isoscaling parameters $α$ and $β$ for both heavy and light fragments from HIPSE model calculations are in good agreement with the experiment data. Studies show that the potential parameters in the HIPSE model have very small effect on the isoscaling parameters. The effect of the excitation energy and evaporation on the isoscaling behavior has also been discussed.

## Key claim

The Heavy Ion Phase Space Exploration (HIPSE) model reproduces the isoscaling parameters α and β for both heavy and light fragments from 140 MeV/nucleon ⁴⁸,⁴⁰Ca+⁹Be reactions in good agreement with experimental data, but the sequential decay effect on isoscaling parameters in HIPSE (using the SIMON code) shows an opposite trend compared to other dynamical and statistical models, increasing the parameter values rather than reducing them.

## Method

The HIPSE model was used to simulate 140 MeV/nucleon ⁴⁸,⁴⁰Ca+⁹Be reactions, employing three adjustable energy-dependent parameters (αa=0.55, xtr=0.09, xcoll=0.18). Fragment production cross sections were calculated for both hot fragments (before evaporation) and cold fragments (after evaporation using the SIMON event generator), and yield ratios R21(N,Z)=Y2(N,Z)/Y1(N,Z) were extracted for isotopes (Z=5-19) and isotones (N=6-19) to obtain isoscaling parameters α and β by exponential fitting.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 140 MeV/nucleon for both ⁴⁸Ca+⁹Be and ⁴⁰Ca+⁹Be reactions
- HIPSE model parameters used: αa=0.55, xtr=0.09, xcoll=0.18 at 140 MeV/nucleon
- Isoscaling parameters extracted for isotopes with Z=5-19 and isotones with N=6-19
- Symmetry energy coefficient Csym extracted from α is around 20 MeV and decreases with increasing excitation energy E*/A
- Excitation energy range studied: E*/A=2.0-4.5 MeV
- Inverse level density parameter a=10 used for temperature calculation via Fermi-gas relationship E*/A=(1/a)T²

## Context

This work extends previous isoscaling studies by Tsang et al. (various years) and comparisons with the statistical abrasion (STA) model by Fang et al. (various years), which predicted the correct decreasing trend of isoscaling parameters with decreasing Z or N but gave values much smaller than experimental data for light fragments. The HIPSE model results are compared with other theoretical frameworks including the antisymmetrical molecular dynamics (AMD) model by Ono (various years), the statistical multifragmentation (SMM) model by Botvina (various years), and the expanding emitting source (EES) model by Tsang et al. (various years). The study enables extraction of the symmetry energy coefficient from isoscaling parameters and highlights the need for a detailed comparison between the SIMON and GEMINI evaporation codes to understand the sequential decay effect on isoscaling in the HIPSE model.
