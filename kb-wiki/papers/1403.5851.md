# Dihadron correlations in d+Au collisions from STAR

> Dihadron correlations are reported for peripheral and central d+Au collisions at 200 GeV from STAR. The ZYAM background subtracted correlation yields are larger in central than peripheral collisions. The difference is mainly caused by centrality biases to jet-like correlations. Fourier coefficients of the raw dihadron correlations are also reported. It is found that the first harmonic coefficient is approximately inversely proportional to event multiplicity, whereas the second harmonic coefficient is approximately independent of event multiplicity.

## Key claim

The observed differences in dihadron correlated yields between central and peripheral d+Au collisions at 200 GeV are mainly caused by centrality biases to jet-like correlations, rather than by new physics such as hydrodynamic flow or Color Glass Condensate effects.

## Method

Dihadron correlations were measured using TPC tracks within |η|<1 and FTPC tracks within 3.8<|η|<2.8, with trigger and associated particles both in the range 1<pT<3 GeV/c. The ZYAM (Zero-Yield-At-Minimum) method was used to subtract the combinatorial background from the correlation functions, and Fourier decomposition was applied to the raw dihadron correlation functions without background subtraction. Centrality was defined using three different measures: TPC multiplicity at mid-rapidity, FTPC-Au multiplicity in the Au-beam direction, and ZDC-Au neutral energy.

## Key numbers

- Trigger and associated particle pT ranges: both 1 < pT < 3 GeV/c
- Associated particle tracking efficiencies: 85% for TPC tracks, 70% for FTPC tracks, each with ±5% relative systematic uncertainty
- Gaussian+constant fits to near-side correlations indicate a 20% difference in Gaussian area between central and peripheral collisions
- The first harmonic Fourier coefficient (V1) is approximately inversely proportional to event multiplicity (dN/dη)
- The second harmonic Fourier coefficient (V2) is approximately independent of event multiplicity and is finite at forward/backward rapidity of |Δη|≈3
- The third harmonic Fourier coefficient (V3) is consistent with zero

## Context

This work extends prior STAR studies of dihadron correlations in d+Au collisions at RHIC (STAR 2009, Horner, Nattrass) and responds to recent observations of ridge correlations in p+Pb collisions at the LHC (CMS 2010, ALICE 2012, ATLAS 2013) and in d+Au collisions by PHENIX (PHENIX 2013). The large acceptance of STAR allows detailed investigation of centrality biases that were estimated to be 10-20% of the observed ridge yield in ALICE p+Pb measurements. The next step is to quantify the centrality bias contributions to isolate possible additional physics mechanisms beyond jet phenomenology.
