# Bose-Einstein Correlations from Opaque Sources

> Bose-Einstein correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions are very different for opaque sources than fortransparent ones. The Bose-Einstein radius parameters measured in two-particle correlation functions depend sensitively on the mean free path of the particles. In particular we find that the outward radius for an opaque source is smaller than the sidewards radius for sufficiently short duration of emission. A long duration of emission can compensate the opacity reduction of the longitudinal radius parameter and explain the experimental measurements of very similar side- and outward radius parameters.

# Bose-Einstein Correlations from Opaque Sources

## Key claim

For an opaque source with a short duration of emission, the outward HBT radius parameter is smaller than the sideward radius parameter, contrary to the standard transparent-source expectation, but a long duration of emission can compensate this opacity reduction and produce the experimentally observed similarity of sideward and outward radii.

## Method

The authors model the source as either a sphere or a longitudinally expanding cylinder with an emission layer of thickness equal to the particle mean free path λₘₚ, using a Glauber-type surface-emission probability. They compute the HBT radius parameters Rₛ, Rₒ, Rₗ from the second-order expansion of the correlation function, then average over a time-dependent mean free path that increases linearly with proper time up to freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- For a spherical opaque source (λₘₚ ≪ R) with short duration of emission: Rₒ² = (1/18)R² + (29/36)λₘₚ² + βₒ²σ(t); Rₛ² = (1/4)R² − (1/8)λₘₚ²; thus Rₒ² = (2/9)Rₛ² in the strict surface limit.
- For a cylindrical opaque source (λₘₚ ≪ R) with short duration of emission: Rₒ² = (2/3 − (π/4)²)R² + (7/6 − π²/32)λₘₚ² + βₒ²σ(τ); Rₛ² = (1/3)R² − (1/6)λₘₚ²; giving Rₒ ≈ 0.22R = 0.39Rₛ.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 A·GeV (NA44 data): Rₛ ≈ Rₒ ≈ 4.5–5.0 fm, Rₗ ≈ 5–6 fm; average transverse momentum p⊥ ≈ 165 MeV so βₒ ≈ 0.76; temperature T ≈ 120 MeV; freeze-out time τf ≈ 11 fm/c; initial transverse source size R ≈ 9 fm.
- The opacity reduction ΔR² is estimated as ≈ 16 fm² for λₘₚ(τf) = 1.0 R and ≈ 9 fm² for λₘₚ(τf) = 2.0 R.
- The duration-of-emission contribution βₒ²σ(τ) = βₒ²τf²/18 ≈ 4 fm² for constant emission per surface element.

## Context

This work extends the standard HBT formalism of Heinz (1994) and Csörgő (1995) for transparent sources by introducing opacity via a finite mean free path, motivated by hydrodynamical freeze-out calculations and cascade codes such as RQMD and QGSM. It contradicts the transparent-source relation Rₒ² = Rₛ² + βₒ²δτ², which would require a very short emission duration to explain the similar Rₒ and Rₛ measured by NA44, NA49, and WA80. The results enable separation of opacity effects from duration-of-emission and transverse-flow contributions in future p⊥- and rapidity-dependent HBT analyses.
