# Radiative decays of dynamically generated charmed baryons

> In this work we study the radiative decay of dynamically generated $J^P=\oh^-$ charm baryons into the ground state $J^P=\oh^+$ baryons. Since different theoretical interpretations of these baryonic resonances, and in particular of the $Λ_c(2595)$, give different predictions, a precise experimental measurement of these decays would be an important step for understanding their nature.

# Radiative decays of dynamically generated charmed baryons

## Key claim
The radiative decay widths of dynamically generated (meson-baryon molecular) charmed baryons differ dramatically from predictions of quark-model approaches, by up to two orders of magnitude in some channels; a precise experimental measurement of these decays would discriminate between the molecular and conventional three-quark interpretations of states such as the Λc(2595).

## Method
The authors solve a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a vector-meson exchange driving force to generate J^P = 1/2^- charmed baryon resonances as poles in the scattering T-matrix. They then evaluate the one-photon radiative decay of these resonances to ground-state J^P = 1/2^+ baryons using a gauge-invariant scheme where the photon couples to the constituent mesons and baryons in the loop diagrams, with couplings determined from the residues at the pole positions and from SU(4) flavor symmetry.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel dynamical generation approach previously applied to charmed baryons by Tolos (2004), Lutz (2003), Hofmann (2005, 2006), Mizutani (2006), Garcia-Recio (2008), and Jimenez-Tejero (2009) to the calculation of radiative decays. It contrasts with quark-model calculations by Ivanov (1999), light-cone QCD sum-rules by Zhu (2000), and the bound D*N picture by Chow (1995), all of which treat the excited baryons as orbital excitations of three-quark systems rather than as meson-baryon molecules. The large predicted widths for Λc(2595) → Λc γ and Ξc(2790) → Ξc γ make these decays experimentally accessible and could resolve the nature of these resonances.
