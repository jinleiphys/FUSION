# No Pain, No Gain: Hard Probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma Coming of Age

> This lecture presents a concise review of the current status of hard QCD and electromagnetic processes as probes of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Hard probes of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) have not yet fulfilled their promise of providing precise quantitative tools, because in the case of jets and quarkonia the investigation is mainly theory limited, and for photons and di-leptons better data are needed.

## Method

This lecture reviews the status of hard QCD and electromagnetic probes of the QGP, including high-\(p_T\) partons and jets, heavy quarks and open flavor hadrons, heavy quarkonia (\(J/\psi\), \(\Upsilon\)), and electroweak probes (lepton pairs, photons, \(Z\) and \(W^\pm\)). The methodology involves formulating production in \(A+A\) collisions as hard QCD processes with factorizable final-state interactions, expressing those interactions in terms of medium properties such as transport coefficients \(\hat{q}\) and \(\hat{e}\), and identifying observables sensitive to the structure of the medium (e.g., weakly vs. strongly coupled QGP). The review compares experimental data from RHIC and LHC with theoretical calculations, including viscous hydrodynamics, perturbative QCD energy loss schemes, and lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(1 \leq 4\pi\eta/s \leq 2.5\) from precision analysis of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC beam energy (Song 2010).
- Onset of the truly "hard" part of the hadron \(p_T\)-spectrum located at \(p_T \geq 6\) GeV/c, from baryon-to-meson ratios compared with hydrodynamic and recombination model calculations.
- Direct photons unsuppressed in central Au+Au collisions up to \(p_T \approx 10\) GeV/c (PHENIX) and in central Pb+Pb collisions up to \(p_T = 60\) GeV/c (CMS).
- For a 20 GeV/c parton at RHIC: \(\hat{q}L \approx 4.5\ \text{GeV}^2 \gg E/L = 1.5\ \text{GeV}^2\); for a 200 GeV/c parton at LHC: \(\hat{q}L \approx 9\ \text{GeV}^2 < E/L = 13\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- \(\Upsilon\)(1s) state melts at \(T \approx 270\) MeV, when still bound by approximately 50 MeV (Laine 2011).
- Initial temperature of QGP from thermal photon data: \(T_{\text{in}} \geq 2T_c \approx 300\) MeV, with early thermalization \(\tau_{\text{th}} \leq 1\) fm/c.

## Context

This review extends the program outlined by Lourenco and Satz (2005) at the first Hard Probes conference, which called for developing hard probes into precise tools for studying deconfined matter. It builds on the established description of bulk QGP evolution via second-order viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, with the equation of state now known from lattice QCD with sufficient accuracy. The paper identifies open questions for future work, including the need for quantitative pQCD theory of jet quenching, a better understanding of the \(Q\bar{Q}\) potential for quarkonium suppression, and high-statistics measurements of quarkonium suppression in \(p(d)+A\) collisions to isolate hot medium effects from initial-state effects.
