# Screening of Nucleon Electric Dipole Moments in Nuclei

> A partial screening of nucleon electric dipole moments (EDMs) in nuclear systems, which is related to the Schiff mechanism known for neutral atomic systems, is discussed. It is shown that the direct contribution from the neutron EDM to the deuteron EDM is partially screened by about 1% in a zero-range approximation calculation.

## Key claim
The direct contribution of the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) to the deuteron EDM is partially screened by about 1% in a zero-range approximation calculation, demonstrating that a Schiff-type screening mechanism exists in charged nuclear systems with strong interactions, not only in neutral atomic systems.

## Method
The authors derived the second-order energy shift for the deuteron in an external electric field using perturbation theory, separating the Hamiltonian into center-of-mass and relative coordinates. They calculated the screening effect analytically using a zero-range approximation for the deuteron wave function, then estimated uncertainties by including effective range corrections and by modeling the deuteron with a square well potential for various radii.

## Key numbers
- The second-order energy shift from screening of the neutron EDM in the deuteron is approximately 0.013 times the direct term (d_n · E) in the zero-range approximation.
- Including effective range effects (rho_t ≈ 1.76 fm) increases the screening to approximately 0.021 times the direct term.
- For the square well model with r_0 = 2.0 fm, the screening increases to 0.018 times the direct term.
- The deuteron binding energy used is E_B = 2.23 MeV, corresponding to a binding momentum kappa = sqrt(m_N * E_B).
- The screening scales as A^(2/3) for heavier nuclei based on a toy model of the giant dipole resonance.

## Context
This work extends the Schiff theorem (Schiff, 1963) from neutral atomic systems to charged nuclear systems, showing that partial screening of nucleon EDMs occurs due to interactions with electric fields from other charged particles in the nucleus. The screening effect had not been considered in prior calculations of light-nuclei EDMs by Liu (2004), de Vries (2011), Song (2012), Bsaisou (2012-2014), and Yamanaka (2015). The results are consistent with a power-counting estimate from the one-photon-exchange TV potential derived by Maekawa (2011). This work enables more precise calculations of nuclear EDMs for planned storage-ring measurements, particularly for the deuteron (Khriplovich, 1998; Farley, 2003; Semertzidis, 2009; Lehrach, 2012).
