# Limiting fragmentation in hadron-hadron collisions at high energies

> Limiting fragmentation in proton-proton, deuteron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed in the framework of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation in high energy QCD. Good agreement with experimental data is obtained for a wide range of energies. Further detailed tests of limiting fragmentation at RHIC and the LHC will provide insight into the evolution equations for high energy QCD.

## Key claim

Limiting fragmentation in hadron-hadron collisions emerges naturally from the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation when the target reaches the "black disk" limit, but the agreement with experimental data depends on using artificially low fixed coupling values rather than running coupling, which gives too fast an energy dependence.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive gluon production using k_perp-factorization, convolving unintegrated gluon distributions from the projectile and target that were evolved via the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation. For x values below 0.01, the BK equation was solved numerically in both fixed and running coupling cases; for x above 0.01, the distributions were extrapolated using a phenomenological form with parameters beta=4 and lambda_0 varied between 0 and 0.15. The initial conditions for BK evolution were taken from the McLerran-Venugopalan model with a saturation scale Q_s^A(x_0) near 2 GeV^2 for gold nuclei, and parton-hadron duality was assumed to compare gluon distributions to charged hadron data.

## Key numbers

- Beam rapidity defined as Y_beam = ln(sqrt(s)/m_N) with m_N the nucleon mass
- RHIC data used at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV for Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions
- The saturation scale exponent in fixed coupling case is c approximately 4.84, giving Q_s^2 proportional to exp(c * alpha_bar_s * Y)
- For typical alpha_s values of 0.2-0.3, the saturation scale exponent lambda_s is at least a factor of three higher than fits to HERA and RHIC data suggest
- The parameter beta=4 was fixed by QCD counting rules, with results insensitive to variation in the range 4-5
- The parameter lambda_0 was varied between 0 and 0.15 in fits to data
- For gold nucleus, (Q_s^A(x_0))^2 approximately 2 GeV^2 from HERA extrapolations and RHIC fits
- Proton saturation scale taken as Q_s^2(x_0) = (Q_s^A(x_0))^2 * (197/A)^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach to limiting fragmentation previously studied by Jalilian-Marian (2004) and by Kharzeev and Levin (2001) and later Kharzeev, Levin and Nardi (2001), but uses the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation rather than saturation ansatze. The authors note that Bialas and Jezabek (2004) explained qualitative features of limiting fragmentation using a two-step model involving multiple gluon exchange and subsequent radiation of hadronic clusters. The results enable further detailed tests at RHIC and the LHC to provide insight into the evolution equations for high energy QCD, particularly regarding deviations from limiting fragmentation that may probe both large-x and small-x dynamics in hadronic wavefunctions.
