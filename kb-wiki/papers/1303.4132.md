# Hadronic Parity Violation

> The history and phenomenology of hadronic parity nonconservation (PNC) is reviewed. We discuss the current status of the experimental tests and theory. We describe a re-analysis of the asymmetry for polarized proton-proton scattering that, when combined with other experimental constraints and with a recent lattice QCD calculation of the weak pion-nucleon coupling, reveals a much more consistent pattern of PNC couplings. In particular, isoscalar coupling strengths are similar to but somewhat larger than the "best value" estimate of Donoghue, Desplanques, and Holstein, while both lattice QCD and experiment indicate a suppressed parity-nonconserving pion-nucleon coupling. We discuss the relationship between meson-exchange models of hadronic PNC and formulations based on effective theory, stressing their general compatibility as well as the challenge presented to theory by experiment, as several of the most precise measurements involve significant momentum scales. Future directions are proposed.

# Hadronic Parity Violation

## Key claim
The re-analysis of the polarized proton-proton scattering asymmetry, when combined with other experimental constraints and a recent lattice QCD calculation of the weak pion-nucleon coupling, reveals a much more consistent pattern of parity-nonconserving couplings than previously recognized, though the isoscalar coupling strengths are similar to but somewhat larger than the "best value" estimate of Donoghue, Desplanques, and Holstein.

## Method
The authors review the history and phenomenology of hadronic parity nonconservation (PNC) using the traditional meson-exchange picture with seven phenomenological weak NN-meson couplings (h^1_pi, h_rho^0, h_rho^1, h_rho^2, h_omega^0, h_omega^1, and h_rho^1') based on the DDH potential from Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980). They then describe a re-analysis of the pp asymmetry that incorporates the TRIUMF 221 MeV measurement and a recent lattice QCD calculation of h^1_pi, comparing these results with constraints from other experiments including p-alpha scattering, 18F, and 19F.

## Context
This review extends the DDH meson-exchange framework from Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and the 2001 analysis by Haxton and Wieman, incorporating the TRIUMF 221 MeV pp measurement and the lattice QCD calculation of h^1_pi by Wasem (2012). The results show that the isovector pion coupling h^1_pi is substantially suppressed compared to the DDH best value, consistent with both the 18F experiments and the lattice calculation, while isoscalar couplings are somewhat larger than DDH best values. The work enables future comparisons between experiment and lattice QCD calculations, and motivates further few-body experiments with quasi-exact methods such as Faddeev-Yakubovsky and hyperspherical harmonic approaches being developed by Viviani and others.
