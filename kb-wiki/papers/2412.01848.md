# Ladder equation for the three-particle vertex and its approximate solution

> We generalize the three two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equations to ten three-particle ladders. These equations are exact and yield the exact three-particle vertex, if we knew the three-particle vertex irreducible in one of the ten channels. However, as we do not have this three-particle irreducible vertex at hand, we approximate this building block for the ladder by the sum of two-particle irreducible vertices each connecting two fermionic lines. The comparison to the exact solution shows that this approximation is only good for rather weak interactions and even than only qualitatively - at least for the non-linear response function analyzed.

# Ladder equation for the three-particle vertex and its approximate solution

## Key claim

The authors generalize the three two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equations to ten three-particle ladder equations, which are exact if the three-particle vertex irreducible in one of the ten channels is known. However, since this irreducible vertex is not available, they approximate it by the sum of two-particle irreducible vertices connecting two fermionic lines each, and this approximation is only qualitatively good for rather weak interactions, at least for the non-linear response function analyzed.

## Method

The authors derive ten three-particle ladder equations by generalizing the two-particle Bethe-Salpeter formalism, accounting for the increased complexity of reducibility on the three-particle level (one-particle, two-particle, and three-particle reducible diagrams). They approximate the fully three-particle irreducible vertex by summing the three irreducible two-particle vertices that each connect two of the particle/hole lines, then solve this approximate ladder for an Anderson impurity model (AIM) at DMFT self-consistency, comparing the results to exact solutions obtained from exact diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- The inverse temperature used for the AIM calculations is $\beta = 1/T = 10$ (Boltzmann constant set to 1)
- The hopping parameter is set to $t \equiv 1$
- The approximation yields good results only for "very small values of the local Coulomb interaction $U$" (no specific numerical value given for the interaction strength)
- The bath hybridization is taken as real and $k$-independent: $\hybridization_k \equiv \hybridization \in \realNumbers$

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism from two-particle to three-particle vertices, addressing a largely unexplored level of Feynman diagrams for strongly correlated electrons. The three-particle ladder equations bear similarities to the Faddeev equations (Faddeev, 1961) used in nuclear physics and QCD for the three-body problem, but are more involved because particle/hole number is not fixed. The paper is motivated by the need for systematic three-particle vertex calculations in diagrammatic extensions of DMFT (Toschi, 2007; Rubtsov, 2008) and for computing non-linear response functions such as Raman and Hall responses (Kubo, 1957; Rostami, 2017, 2021). The results show that the simple two-particle-vertex-based approximation is insufficient except for very weak interactions, highlighting the need for better approximations or numerical methods for the local three-particle irreducible vertex.
