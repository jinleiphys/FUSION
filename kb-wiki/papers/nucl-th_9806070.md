# Status of Effective Field Theory of NN Scattering

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) can provide a systematic power counting scheme for nucleon-nucleon scattering, but the original Weinberg power counting proposal may be inconsistent when pions are treated nonperturbatively; a new scheme proposed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW) that treats pions perturbatively appears to be consistent with renormalization, though it sacrifices Weinberg's explanation of the hierarchy of n-body forces.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of EFT to NN scattering by comparing two approaches: the Weinberg program, which applies chiral perturbation theory power counting to an n-nucleon effective potential and then iterates it in the Schrödinger equation, and the KSW approach, which uses dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS) to make the power counting manifest. The pionless EFT is analyzed analytically for the ¹S₀ (np) channel, and the role of pions is examined in the ³S₁–³D₁ channel, including calculations of phase shifts and the deuteron electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ¹S₀ (np) scattering length: a = –23.714 ± 0.013 fm
- Experimental ¹S₀ (np) effective range: r_e = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm
- Theoretical prediction for the quartet N-deuteron scattering length: ⁴a = 6.33 fm, compared to experimental value ⁴a = 6.35 ± 0.02 fm

## Context

This talk reviews progress in developing systematic EFT power counting for nuclear scattering, extending the work of Weinberg (1990, 1991) who first proposed applying chiral perturbation theory to nuclear potentials. It contrasts the Weinberg program with the later approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996, 1997, 1998), who identified inconsistencies in Weinberg's power counting when using dimensional regularization and proposed a perturbative-pion scheme. The work enables systematic connections between NN scattering and few-nucleon processes, including N-deuteron scattering as studied by Bedaque, van Kolck, and Hammer (1997), and predictions for electromagnetic probes of deuteron structure.
