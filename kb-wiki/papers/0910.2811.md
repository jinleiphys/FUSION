# All coordinates transformations that separate the center of mass kinetic energy, their group structure and geometry

> The most general coordinates transformations that allow for the exact separation of the kinetic energy operator of a quantum many-body system into total center of mass kinetic energy and internal kinetic energy are found and discussed. We find i) that the suitable transformations, depending on the number of particles, have a certain number of free parameters and this allows for the generalization of the Jacobi coordinates to a much larger class of coordinates with the same properties and ii) that there is a new, uncommon, additive group structure hidden in the transformation matrices that is connected to certain geometric properties of the set of coordinates.

# All coordinates transformations that separate the center of mass kinetic energy, their group structure and geometry

## Key claim
The paper derives the most general linear coordinate transformations that allow exact separation of the total center-of-mass kinetic energy from the internal kinetic energy in a quantum many-body system, showing that such transformations form a much larger class than the familiar Jacobi coordinates. The transformations contain free parameters whose number depends on the particle count, and the transformation matrices exhibit an additive group structure under a specific composition law, not the usual matrix multiplication.

## Method
The author imposes conditions on the transformation matrix A such that the sum of pure second derivatives (Laplacians) in the original coordinates transforms into a sum of pure second derivatives in the new coordinates without mixed derivative terms, while forcing the last row of the matrix to map to the center-of-mass coordinate. For n particles, this yields n(n−1)/2 orthogonality conditions among rows and n−1 conditions that each internal-coordinate row sums to zero. The resulting system has n(n−1)/2 free parameters, and explicit matrices are constructed for n=2, n=3, and generalized to n particles.

## Key numbers
- For n=2 particles: the general transformation matrix has 1 free parameter a, giving A₂(a) = [[a, −a], [1/2, 1/2]].
- For n=3 particles: the system has 3 free parameters (a, b, c) in the general matrix A₃(a,b,c), with the last row fixed to [1/3, 1/3, 1/3].
- For n=3 particles: the additive group structure under the operation of matrix addition followed by division by two exists only under two restricted parameter conditions: either b = k·a or a = k′·c − 2b, where k and k′ are constants.
- For n particles: the system has n(n−1)/2 free parameters in the upper triangle of the (n−1)×(n−1) submatrix, with the last row fixed to all elements equal to 1/n.

## Context
This work generalizes the well-known Jacobi coordinates, which are standard in atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics for separating center-of-mass motion (Fano, Greiner). The author explicitly shows that certain coordinate systems, such as the "necklace" or sequential coordinates, do not satisfy the derived conditions and therefore cannot separate the center-of-mass kinetic energy. The results enable the construction of alternative coordinate systems for quantum many-body models, such as describing cluster states in light nuclei or scattering of composite particles like two deuterons, where internal wavefunctions of subsystems can be factorized from relative motion wavefunctions.
