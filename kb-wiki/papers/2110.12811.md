# Impacts of (inverse) magnetic catalysis on screening masses of neutral pions and sigma mesons in hot and magnetized quark matter

> We investigate the screening masses of neutral pions and sigma mesons in hot and magnetized quark matter in the framework of a two-flavor lattice-improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a magnetic field dependent coupling constant, which is determined by utilizing the results from lattice QCD simulations. Since such model can well reproduce inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC), by comparing with the standard NJL model, we systemically analyze the impacts of IMC on the temperature and magnetic field dependences of the longitudinal and transverse screening masses of the chiral partners, i.e. π^0 and σ mesons, as well as the screening mass differences between them. Particularly, it is found that the eB dependences of two alternative (pseudo)critical temperatures for the chiral transition defined by σ-π^0 meson screening mass differences are consistent with that defined by the quark condensate.

## Key claim
The paper shows that incorporating inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) into a lattice-improved NJL model, via a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant, qualitatively changes the temperature and magnetic field dependence of neutral pion and sigma meson screening masses compared to the standard NJL model, and that the (pseudo)critical temperatures defined by the screening mass differences between these chiral partners are consistent with those defined by the quark condensate.

## Method
The authors use a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a magnetic-field-dependent four-fermion coupling constant \(G(eB)\), determined by fitting to lattice QCD results for baryon masses in a magnetic field. They compute the screening masses of neutral pions and sigma mesons in the random phase approximation (RPA) using the proper-time regularization scheme with both ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs, and compare results with the standard NJL model (constant \(G\)) to isolate the effects of IMC.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass at zero field: \(m^2 = 0.097\ \text{GeV}^2\) (corresponding to \(m \approx 311\ \text{MeV}\)).
- Coupling constant at zero field: \(G(T=B=0)\Lambda_{\text{UV}}^2 = 5.5\), with \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} = 708\ \text{MeV}\) and current quark mass \(m_0 = 11.8\ \text{MeV}\).
- Infrared cutoff: \(\Lambda_{\text{IR}} = 240\ \text{MeV}\).
- At \(eB = 0.7\ \text{GeV}^2\), the coupling constant drops to \(G = 5.69\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (from \(10.97\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) at \(eB=0\)).
- The pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{pc}\) decreases with increasing magnetic field in the lattice-improved model, consistent with lattice QCD results of Bali (2011), in contrast to the standard NJL model where \(T_{pc}\) increases.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of neutral pion screening masses under magnetic fields (Sheng, 2020) to include sigma mesons and their mass difference as an alternative order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration. It contrasts with the standard NJL model (which exhibits only magnetic catalysis) and uses the magnetic-field-dependent coupling approach of Endrodi (2019) to reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis seen in lattice QCD by Bali (2011, 2012). The results enable future studies of chiral phase transition using screening masses, which avoid the discontinuity issues of pole masses at the Mott transition.
