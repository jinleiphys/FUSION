# Lorentz and CPT violation in the Neutrino Sector

## Key claim
Lorentz and CPT violation in the neutrino sector can be tested through neutrino oscillations, time-of-flight measurements, and beta-decay experiments, but a set of "oscillation-free" operators in the Standard-Model Extension (SME) remain unobservable in oscillations and require alternative probes such as beta decay to be accessed.

## Method
The paper reviews the SME framework for Lorentz violation in neutrinos, which generalizes the Standard Model to include all possible Lorentz-breaking terms in the action. It examines three experimental approaches: neutrino oscillation experiments that constrain flavor-mixing SME coefficients via sidereal variations; neutrino time-of-flight measurements that probe operators affecting group velocity; and beta-decay experiments (neutron, tritium, double beta decay) that access "countershaded" operators invisible to both oscillations and time-of-flight.

## Key numbers
- Limits on 66 different SME coefficients producing sidereal variations of the event rate were presented using public MINOS data.
- 15 coefficients for Lorentz violation produce time-independent neutrino-antineutrino oscillations, with preliminary results from Double Chooz.
- Published data from Mainz and Troitsk tritium experiments imply a tenfold improvement in the limit on the isotropic coefficient previously constrained using IceCube meson thresholds.
- First limits on anisotropic effects and on an effective-dimension-two coefficient that mimics the neutrino mass-squared parameter near the endpoint energy were obtained from Mainz and Troitsk data.

## Context
This work extends the SME framework developed by Kostelecky and collaborators (2004, 2012) and applies it to neutrino physics, building on prior experimental searches by Double Chooz, IceCube, LSND, MiniBooNE, MINOS, and Super-Kamiokande. It enables future tests using beam experiments measuring neutrino speed and beta-decay experiments studying neutron, tritium, and double beta decay, particularly for operators that remain unexplored by oscillation experiments. The paper also introduces the first exploration of neutrino-antineutrino oscillations as a new probe of Lorentz violation.
