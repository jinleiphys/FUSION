# Coherent photoproduction of $ψ$ and $Υ$ mesons in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at the CERN LHC

> Exclusive photoproduction of vector mesons in the perturbative two-gluon exchange formalism depends significantly on nucleon and nuclear gluon distributions. In the present study we calculate total cross sections and rapidity distributions of $J/ψ(1s)$, $ψ(2s)$, $Υ(1s)$, $Υ(2s)$, and $Υ(3s)$ in ultraperipheral proton-lead (pPb) and lead-lead (PbPb) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=5$ TeV and $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV respectively. Effects of gluon shadowing are investigated and potentials for constraining nuclear gluon modifications are discussed.

# Coherent photoproduction of ψ and Υ mesons in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at the CERN LHC

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of ψ and Υ mesons in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at LHC energies shows significant sensitivity to gluon shadowing, with the potential to constrain nuclear gluon modifications, but the constraining power depends on the collision system and meson species, with PbPb collisions offering stronger discrimination than pPb collisions, and Υ mesons in pPb collisions offering better sensitivity than ψ mesons in the same system.

## Method

The authors calculate total cross sections and rapidity distributions for J/ψ(1s), ψ(2s), Υ(1s), Υ(2s), and Υ(3s) in the perturbative two-gluon exchange formalism, using gluon distributions from MSTW08 (no shadowing), HKN07 (weak shadowing), EPS09 (moderate shadowing), and EPS08 (strong shadowing). The calculations are performed for ultraperipheral pPb collisions at √s_NN = 5 TeV and PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, with the additional assumption that the multiplicative correction factor ζ_V previously determined for J/ψ(1s) is applicable to ψ(2s).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for J/ψ(1s) in pPb collisions: 86.5 μb (MSTW08), 69.1 μb (EPS08), 74.2 μb (EPS09), 79.9 μb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for J/ψ(1s) in PbPb collisions: 34.4 mb (MSTW08), 6.6 mb (EPS08), 14.6 mb (EPS09), 23.3 mb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for ψ(2s) in pPb collisions: 18.6 μb (MSTW08), 14.6 μb (EPS08), 15.8 μb (EPS09), 17.0 μb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for ψ(2s) in PbPb collisions: 7.1 mb (MSTW08), 1.9 mb (EPS08), 3.5 mb (EPS09), 5.0 mb (HKN07)
- Shadowing factor S_F for J/ψ(1s) in PbPb collisions: approximately 32% reduction (HKN07) to approximately 80% reduction (EPS08) relative to MSTW08
- Shadowing factor S_F for ψ(2s) in PbPb collisions: approximately 30% reduction (HKN07) to approximately 72% reduction (EPS08) relative to MSTW08
- Ratio σ(ψ(2s))/σ(J/ψ(1s)) in pPb collisions: approximately 0.21 for all gluon distributions
- Total cross section for Υ(1s) in pPb collisions: 291.0 nb (MSTW08), 207.5 nb (EPS08), 236.1 nb (EPS09), 254.9 nb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for Υ(1s) in PbPb collisions: 52.1 μb (MSTW08), 32.7 μb (EPS08), 39.5 μb (EPS09), 41.9 μb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for Υ(2s) in pPb collisions: 94.2 nb (MSTW08), 66.8 nb (EPS08), 76.2 nb (EPS09), 82.3 nb (HKN07)
- Total cross section for Υ(3s) in pPb collisions: 56.1 nb (MSTW08), 39.7 nb (EPS08), 45.4 nb (EPS09), 49.0 nb (HKN07)
- Shadowing factor S_F for Υ mesons in pPb collisions: approximately 29% reduction (EPS08), 19% reduction (EPS09), 12% reduction (HKN07) relative to MSTW08
- Shadowing factor S_F for Υ mesons in PbPb collisions: approximately 19% reduction (HKN07), 22-24% reduction (EPS09), 34-37% reduction (EPS08) relative to MSTW08

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Adeluyi and Nguyen, 2012) that considered only J/ψ(1s) and Υ(1s) photoproduction, now including ψ(2s), Υ(2s), and Υ(3s) states. The calculations use the same perturbative two-gluon exchange formalism as Ryskin (1993) and Ryskin (1995), and employ nuclear parton distributions from MSTW08 (Martin, 2009), EPS08 (Eskola, 2008), EPS09 (Eskola, 2009), and HKN07 (Hirai, 2007). The results enable experimental comparisons with ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb measurements, particularly the ALICE Collaboration (2012) results on J/ψ(1s) in PbPb collisions, and provide specific rapidity windows (e.g., -2 < y < 2 for PbPb ψ production, -1 < y < 1 for PbPb Υ production, -4 < y < -1 for pPb Υ production) where gluon shadowing can be most effectively constrained.
