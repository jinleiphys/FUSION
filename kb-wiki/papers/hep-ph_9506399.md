# Reinterpretation of Thermal Dilepton Emission Rate by Spectral Functions

> We reinterpret the dilepton emission rate from a hadronic gas expected to be produced in heavy ion collisions in terms of the spectral functions available from the $e^+e^-$ annihilation and the $τ$ lepton decays experiments. We take into account all possible hadronic state especially the multi-pion contributions to the dilepton emission and the parity mixing phenomenon due to the soft final-state corrections. A new compilation of the experimental data for the spectral functions is presented.

# Reinterpretation of Thermal Dilepton Emission Rate by Spectral Functions

## Key claim

The dilepton emission rate from a hadronic gas can be expressed non-perturbatively in terms of experimentally measured spectral functions from e+e- annihilation and tau lepton decays, avoiding the need for detailed reaction-by-reaction counting in kinetic theory. This reinterpretation is valid under the condition that T^2 is much smaller than M^2 (the dilepton invariant mass squared), T^2 is much smaller than 6F_pi^2, and T is below the critical temperature T_c.

## Method

The author derives a low-temperature expansion of the thermal structure function W_munu(q) for a hadronic gas, expressing the dilepton emission rate in terms of three spectral functions: rho^em(s) from the total hadronic cross section of e+e- annihilation, rho^V(s) from e+e- annihilation into even numbers of pions (isovector channel), and rho^A(s) from tau lepton decays into odd numbers of pions (axial vector channel). The soft final-state pion corrections, computed using PCAC and current algebra, introduce a vector-axial vector mixing term proportional to epsilon = T^2/(6F_pi^2). A new compilation of experimental data for these spectral functions is presented in the energy range from 360 MeV to 2150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant F_pi = 93 MeV, used in the parameter epsilon = T^2/(6F_pi^2) for the soft pion correction
- The spectral functions are compiled in the energy range from 360 MeV to 2150 MeV
- The axial spectral function rho^A is extracted from tau decays up to sqrt(s) < m_tau ~ 1.7 GeV
- The dilepton emission rate is plotted for a typical temperature T = 160 MeV
- The a_1 resonance enhancement appears at m_{a1} ~ 1230 MeV in the axial channel contribution

## Context

This work extends the low-temperature expansion approach of McLerran and Toimela (1985) and the soft-pion theorem calculations of Dey, Eletsky, and Ioffe (1990) by providing a complete data-driven parameterization of the dilepton rate. It addresses the double-counting problems and theoretical uncertainties in previous kinetic theory calculations by Gale and Lichard (1994) and others. The formalism enables direct comparison with experimental data from CERN SPS experiments and future RHIC and LHC measurements, though the author notes that integrating the rate over the space-time history of the thermal source requires additional assumptions to be reported elsewhere.
