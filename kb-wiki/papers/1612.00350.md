# $ND$ and $NB$ systems in quark delocalization color screening model

> The $ND$ and $NB$ systems with $I=0$ and $1$, $J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{\pm}$, $\frac{3}{2}^{\pm}$, and $\frac{5}{2}^{\pm}$ are investigated within the framework of quark delocalization color screening model. The results show that all the positive parity states are unbound. By coupling to the $ND^{*}$ channel, the state $ND$ with $I=0,~J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ can form a bound state, which can be invoked to explain the observed $Σ(2800)$ state. The mass of the $ND^{*}$ with $I=0,~J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ is close to that of the reported $Λ_{c}(2940)^{+}$, which indicates that $Λ_{c}(2940)^{+}$ can be explained as a $ND^{*}$ molecular state in QDCSM. Besides, the $ΔD^{*}$ with $I=1,~J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ is also a possible resonance state. The results of the bottom case of $NB$ system are similar to those of the $ND$ system. Searching for these states will be a challenging subject of experiments.

# ND and NB systems in quark delocalization color screening model

## Key claim

The quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) predicts that the observed Σ(2800) and Λc(2940)+ can be explained as molecular states composed of a nucleon and a D or D* meson, but only for negative parity states and only when channel coupling is included for the Σ(2800) candidate; all positive parity states are unbound in this model.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical calculations using the resonating group method (RGM) within the QDCSM framework, solving the generalized eigen-equation for the ND and NB systems with isospin I=0 and 1 and spin-parities J^P = 1/2^±, 3/2^±, and 5/2^±. They computed effective potentials between colorless clusters via adiabatic approximation and included channel-coupling effects between ND, ND*, ΔD, and ΔD* channels for the charm case, and the corresponding bottom counterparts for the NB system.

## Key numbers

- The ND bound state with I=0, J^P=1/2^- has a mass of 2801.0 MeV (binding energy -2.0 MeV), close to the observed Σ(2800)
- The ND* bound state with I=0, J^P=3/2^- has a mass of 2940.3 MeV (binding energy -5.7 MeV), close to the reported Λc(2940)+ mass of 2939.8 ± 1.3(stat) ± 1.0(syst) MeV/c^2
- The ΔD* state with I=1, J^P=5/2^- has a mass of 3210.1 MeV (binding energy -28.9 MeV), identified as a possible resonance state
- For the NB system, the NB bound state with I=0, J^P=1/2^- has a mass of 6214.8 MeV (binding energy -3.2 MeV)
- The NB* state with I=0, J^P=3/2^- has a mass of 6260.6 MeV (binding energy -3.4 MeV)
- The ΔB* state with I=1, J^P=5/2^- has a mass of 6530.9 MeV (binding energy -26.1 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the QDCSM approach, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions, dibaryon candidates, and the Pc(4380) pentaquark (Huang et al., recent), to the ND and NB molecular systems. It addresses the ongoing debate about whether Σ(2800) and Λc(2940)+ are molecular states or conventional charmed baryons, with prior molecular interpretations from Lutz and Kolomeitsev (chiral molecule), Jimenez-Tejero et al. (dynamically generated resonance), He and Liu (one-boson-exchange model), and Ortega et al. (constituent quark model), as well as conventional baryon interpretations from Capstick (relativized potential model) and Ebert et al. (orbital excitations). The results enable experimental searches for predicted bottom-sector analogs and the ΔD* resonance, and support the channel-coupling mechanism previously used to explain the X(3872) state (Swanson, Fernandez-Carames et al.) and the H-dibaryon.
