# Pion-nucleon P_33 and P_11 scatterings in the Lippmann-Schwinger approach

> We study the pion-nucleon P_33 and P_11 scattering, where the Delta and the Roper resonances are seen, respectively. We use the Lippmann-Schwinger equation extended to couple to a one-body state, and investigate nature of these resonances by taking and omitting a one-baryon state into account. We see validity and puzzle of the standard quark model interpretation, for the Delta and the Roper resonances, respectively.

## Key claim

The Delta resonance is well described by a three-constituent-quark (3Q) ground state, supporting the standard quark model interpretation; however, the Roper resonance cannot be explained by a 3Q state because coupling to the pion-nucleon scattering state shifts the bare baryon energy upward, whereas the quark model would require a large downward shift to match the observed mass.

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for pion-nucleon P33 and P11 partial waves, extending the equation to couple a one-baryon (3Q) state (Q-space) to meson-baryon scattering states (P-space). The T-matrix is decomposed into a P-space-only term and a term representing the effect of the baryon state, which includes a self-energy from coupling to pion-nucleon states. Elementary processes are parametrized with Gaussian form factors and a semi-relativistic prescription, and the calculation is performed both with and without the baryon pole term.

## Key numbers

- For P33 scattering with a baryon pole, the bare baryon state is placed around 1235 MeV to reproduce data.
- For P33 scattering without a baryon pole, a resonance at the correct energy requires a potential with range a = 0.3 fm and strength V_PP = -308 MeV.
- For P11 scattering with a baryon pole, two baryons are placed at 939 MeV and 1410 MeV; the Roper resonance appears slightly above the bare energy of 1410 MeV.
- For P11 scattering without a baryon pole, a resonance at the correct energy requires a potential with range a = 0.15 fm and strength V_PP = -1180 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the Delta and Roper resonances by comparing the standard quark model interpretation with a hadron-dynamical picture using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The Delta result supports the quark model scenario, while the Roper result contradicts it, echoing the level-reverse puzzle noted by the Graz group (Glozman, 1995) and aligning with the Juelich group's argument (Krehl, 1999) that the Roper may be a temporal bound state of nucleon and correlated two pions. The findings enable further investigation into the role of explicit pi-pi-N channels and the origin of the deep effective potential needed to reproduce the Roper resonance without a 3Q state.
