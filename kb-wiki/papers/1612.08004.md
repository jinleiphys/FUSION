# Effective forces between quantum bound states

> Recent ab initio lattice studies have found that the interactions between alpha particles (4He nuclei) are sensitive to seemingly minor details of the nucleon-nucleon force such as interaction locality. In order to uncover the essential physics of this puzzling phenomenon without unnecessary complications, we study a simple model involving two-component fermions in one spatial dimension. We probe the interaction between two bound dimers for several different particle-particle interactions and measure an effective potential between the dimers using external point potentials which act as numerical tweezers. We find that the strength and range of the local part of the particle-particle interactions play a dominant role in shaping the interactions between the dimers and can even determine the overall sign of the effective potential.

## Key claim
The strength and range of the local part of the particle-particle interactions play a dominant role in shaping the effective forces between quantum bound states, and can even determine the overall sign of the effective potential, but this conclusion is drawn from a simple one-dimensional model of two-component fermions and may be a universal feature.

## Method
The authors studied a one-dimensional lattice model of two-component fermions (up and down spins) with a Hamiltonian that includes a kinetic term and five different particle-particle interactions, each tuned to produce a bound dimer with the same dimensionless binding energy of 1/40. They probed the effective interaction between two bound dimers by applying an external point potential (numerical tweezers) that holds down-spin particles at a fixed separation distance, and computed the ground state energy of the full Hamiltonian as a function of separation distance to extract the tweezer effective potential.

## Key numbers
- Dimer binding energy for all five interactions: 1/40 in dimensionless lattice units (for the main results) and 1/100 for a second set of calculations.
- Kinetic parameter t = 1/20 in dimensionless lattice units, corresponding to a dimensionless mass parameter m = 10.
- Interaction coupling constants: c^(1) = -0.103078 (point-like, range 0), c^(2) = -0.049851 (local, range 1), c^(3) = -0.038321 (local, range 2), c^(4) = -0.038321 (nonlocal, range 2), c^(5) = -0.052504 (nonlocal, range 2).
- For the weaker binding energy of 1/100: c_1 = -0.064031, c_2 = -0.027454, c_3 = -0.019684, c_4 = -0.019684, c_5 = -0.031440.
- Tweezer couplings u = 0.100, 0.125, 0.150 were used; for u = 0.100 the tweezer binding energy is about half the dimer binding energy, while for u = 0.150 it is about the same.

## Context
This work extends the surprising finding from Elhatisari (2016) that the local or nearly local part of nuclear interactions is important in determining the strength of the alpha-alpha interaction, which was observed in ab initio lattice studies of 4He nuclei. The authors use a simplified one-dimensional model to uncover the essential physics, building on earlier work by Combescot and collaborators on interactions of composite bosons such as excitons. The results suggest that local dominance in effective forces between quantum bound states is a universal feature, and the findings are being used to develop new lattice chiral interactions with improved order-by-order convergence in ab initio nuclear structure calculations of medium mass nuclei.
