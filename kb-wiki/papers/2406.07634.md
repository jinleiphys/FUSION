# Lighting up the Photon Wigner Distribution via Dilepton Productions

> We present a systematic investigation of lepton pair production through photon-photon fusion processes in heavy-ion collisions. It is demonstrated that the dilepton production at a given impact parameter ($b_\perp$) with a fixed transverse momentum imbalance ($q_\perp$) can be factorized into a unified formula in terms of the Wigner photon distribution of heavy nuclei. We show that this framework provides a comprehensive description of all the relevant data from RHIC to the LHC, with a strong evidence that the quasi-real photon can be radiated not only from the nucleus as a whole, standing for the coherent contribution, but also from the sub-structures inside the nucleus, representing the incoherent contribution. Further predictions are made for the anisotropies in the correlations between $q_\perp$, $b_\perp$, and the dilepton transverse momentum ($P_\perp$). This will help us to constrain the photon Wigner distribution which plays a crucial role to study the gluonic matter of nucleus at small-$x$ through the diffractive photoproduction processes in heavy ion collision.

# Lighting up the Photon Wigner Distribution via Dilepton Productions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that dilepton production via photon-photon fusion in heavy-ion collisions can be factorized into a unified formula in terms of the photon Wigner distribution, but only in the back-to-back correlation limit where the relative transverse momentum of the lepton pair is much larger than the total transverse momentum imbalance. The framework provides evidence that quasi-real photons can be radiated both coherently from the nucleus as a whole and incoherently from sub-structures inside the nucleus, with the incoherent contribution being one of the mechanisms responsible for transverse momentum broadening in events with neutron emissions.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized factorization formula that separates the dilepton production cross-section into a hard scattering part (depending on the lepton pair relative transverse momentum) and soft photon Wigner distributions (containing five-dimensional information of longitudinal momentum fraction, transverse momentum, and impact parameter). They parametrize the generalized transverse momentum dependent (GTMD) photon distributions into unpolarized and linearly-polarized components, and compute the cross-section by contracting these distributions with the hard factor containing both isotropic and anisotropic terms. The framework is applied to describe experimental data from RHIC and the LHC, including both coherent contributions from the whole nucleus and incoherent contributions from protons and quarks inside the nucleus, with Sudakov resummation included for final-state soft multi-photon radiation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius set to R_A = 6.6 fm for lead nucleus, with form factor parameter a = 0.71 fm
- Proton dipole form factor uses scale 0.71 GeV^2
- Maximum transverse momentum imbalance in experimental measurements is approximately 1.2 GeV
- Lepton pair mass range M = 8-45 GeV and transverse momentum P_perp = 4-20 GeV used for anisotropy calculations at LHC kinematics
- Impact parameter fixed at b_perp = 10 fm for anisotropy calculations
- Rapidity integrated over region [-1,1] for LHC anisotropy calculations
- In the small-alpha region, transverse momentum imbalance q_perp is less than 30 MeV; in the large-alpha region, q_perp can rise up to 1-3 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies on dilepton production in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) at RHIC and the LHC, building upon the GTMD framework developed by Klein (2020), Li (2019), and Xiao (2020). The authors address tensions between theoretical results and experimental data at large acoplanarity and high transverse momentum imbalance in near-miss events. The formalism provides a foundation for studying gluon tomography in nuclei at small-x through diffractive photoproduction processes, as explored by Mantysaari (2020, 2023) and others. The framework enables future experimental studies at RHIC and the LHC to probe the intrinsic properties of linearly polarized photons in heavy-ion collisions through multi-dimensional angular correlations.
