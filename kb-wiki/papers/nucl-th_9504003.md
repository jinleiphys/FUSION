# Strangeness Enhancement in $p+A$ and $S+A$ Interactions at SPS Energies

## Key claim

The large strangeness enhancement observed in $S+Au$ collisions, previously interpreted as possible evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation, originates instead from non-equilibrium dynamics of few nucleon systems, as demonstrated by a factor of two enhancement already in minimum bias $pS$ collisions where one projectile nucleon interacts with only two target nucleons on average.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING Monte Carlo model to perform a linear extrapolation of strangeness production from $pp$ to $AA$ collisions, incorporating nuclear geometry and kinematical constraints but no final state cascading or color rope formation. They used the VENUS model (version 4.13) to estimate effects of final state cascading and non-conventional production mechanisms, specifically "double strings" that form when one projectile nucleon interacts with two or more target nucleons, with hyperon production enhanced by a factor of two relative to single string rates. Both models were compared to experimental data on $pp$, $pS$, $pAg$, $pAu$, $SS$, $SAg$, and $SAu$ collisions at 200 AGeV from the NA35 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- A factor of two enhancement of $\Lambda^{0}$ at mid-rapidity is indicated by recent $pS$ data, where on average one projectile nucleon interacts with only two target nucleons
- Another factor of two enhancement appears in the light ion reaction $SS$ relative to $pS$, where on average only two projectile nucleons interact with two target ones
- HIJING overpredicts $\bar{\Lambda}$ multiplicity in $pp$ and $pA$ interactions by a factor of 2-3 compared to data
- VENUS overpredicts the $pp \rightarrow \Lambda^0$ rapidity density at mid-rapidity by 50-100%
- The ratio $E_{S} = (<\Lambda> + 4<K_{s}^{0}>)/(3<\pi^{-}>)$ shows much less discrepancy between HIJING and data than individual distributions
- Calculations were performed at $\sqrt{s} \simeq 20A$ GeV for SPS energies (200 AGeV) and for $Pb+Pb$ at 170 AGeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of strangeness production at CERN/SPS energies using the HIJING and VENUS models, comparing with data from Alber et al. (1995) from the NA35 collaboration. It contradicts the interpretation of strangeness enhancement as evidence for equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formation, as advocated in reviews by Heinz (1993) and Rafelski (1982). The results support non-equilibrium mechanisms such as color rope formation, similar to those incorporated in the RQMD model by Sorge et al. (1993) and the double string phenomenology in VENUS by Werner (1993). The paper notes that earlier NA5 data and preliminary NA36 data on heavier targets show substantially less enhancement than the NA35 data on $p+S$, indicating a discrepancy that must be resolved before strangeness enhancement can be used as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in future $Au+Au$ and $Pb+Pb$ experiments.
