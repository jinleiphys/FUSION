# Accurate determination of $^{6,7}$Li nuclear magnetic moments

> We report an accurate determination of the nuclear magnetic dipole moments of $^{6,7}$Li from the measured ratio of the nuclear and the electron $g$-factors in atomic Li. The obtained results significantly improve upon the literature values and stress the importance of reliable theoretical calculations of the nuclear shielding corrections.

# Accurate determination of \(^{6,7}\)Li nuclear magnetic moments

## Key claim

The nuclear magnetic dipole moments of \(^{6,7}\)Li are determined from measured ratios of nuclear and electron \(g\)-factors in atomic Li, with the accuracy limited by the estimated 30% uncertainty in the relativistic shielding correction \(\alpha^4 \sigma^{(4)}\), which the authors approximate from hydrogenic formulas rather than calculate exactly.

## Method

The authors extract nuclear magnetic moments from the measured ratio of nuclear to electron \(g\)-factors in atomic Li (Beckmann 1974) by combining it with calculated electron \(g\)-factors (Yan 2001, 2002; Shabaev 2017) and a theoretical nuclear shielding constant \(\sigma\). The shielding constant is computed as a double expansion in powers of the fine-structure constant \(\alpha\) and the electron-to-nucleus mass ratio, with the leading nonrelativistic term taken from Yan (1995), the relativistic correction estimated from hydrogenic formulas with a 30% uncertainty, and the finite nuclear-mass correction approximated by a simplified formula.

## Key numbers

- Shielding constant \(\sigma = 101.62(4) \times 10^{-6}\) for both \(^{6}\)Li and \(^{7}\)Li
- Leading-order shielding contribution \(\alpha^2 \sigma^{(2)} = 101.499 \times 10^{-6}\) (infinite nuclear mass limit)
- Relativistic shielding correction \(\alpha^4 \sigma^{(4)} = 0.138(41) \times 10^{-6}\) for Li
- Finite nuclear-mass correction \(\alpha^2 (m_e/m_N) \sigma^{(2,1)} \approx -0.012 \times 10^{-6}\) for \(^{6}\)Li and \(-0.013 \times 10^{-6}\) for \(^{7}\)Li
- Nuclear magnetic moment of \(^{6}\)Li: \(\mu/\mu_N = 0.822\,044\,63(37)\)
- Nuclear magnetic moment of \(^{7}\)Li: \(\mu/\mu_N = 3.256\,416\,19(57)\)
- Electron \(g\)-factor ratio \((g_J/g_e) - 1 = -9.124\,99(17) \times 10^{-6}\) for \(^{6}\)Li and \(-9.125\,19(15) \times 10^{-6}\) for \(^{7}\)Li
- Measured \(g'_I/g_J\) ratio: \(-2.235\,697\,8(10) \times 10^{-4}\) for \(^{6}\)Li and \(-5.904\,271\,9(10) \times 10^{-4}\) for \(^{7}\)Li (Beckmann 1974)

## Context

This work extends the approach used for deuterium, tritium (Puchalski 2022), \(^{3}\)He (Wehrli 2021), and \(^{9}\)Be (Pachucki 2010) to the lithium isotopes, providing improved nuclear magnetic moments that supersede earlier tabulations (Stone 2005, Stone 2019). The new values are particularly important because the \(^{7}\)Li moment serves as a reference for determining magnetic moments of unstable \(^{8,9,11}\)Li isotopes (Winnacker 1978, Borremans 2005, Neugart 2008), and the shift in moments changes the effective Zemach radii \(\tilde r_Z\) by 0.02 fm for \(^{6}\)Li and 0.03 fm for \(^{7}\)Li, comparable to the claimed uncertainties in prior determinations (Puchalski 2013, Sun 2023, Pachucki 2023).
