# Variational Approach to Real-Time Evolution of Yang-Mills Gauge Fields on a Lattice

> Applying a variational method to a Gaussian wave ansatz, we have derived a set of semi-classical evolution equations for SU(2) lattice gauge fields, which take the classical form in the limit of a vanishing width of the Gaussian wave packet. These equations are used to study the quantum effects on the classical evolutions of the lattice gauge fields.

## Key claim

Applying a variational method to a Gaussian wave ansatz yields semi-classical evolution equations for SU(2) lattice gauge fields that reduce to the classical form only in the limit of vanishing width of the Gaussian wave packet; the quantum corrections are found to enhance, rather than suppress, the chaotic behavior of the gauge field.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent variational principle with a link-separable Gaussian trial wavefunction on each link of a three-dimensional lattice, parametrized by classical link variables \(U_{l0}\), electric fields \(E_{l0}\), and a complex width parameter \(b = v + iw\). They derived semi-classical equations of motion for these parameters from the expectation value of the Kogut-Susskind lattice Hamiltonian for pure SU(2) gauge theory, and solved them numerically to follow the real-time evolution of gauge field configurations. The maximal Lyapunov exponent was extracted from the exponential divergence of initially nearby trajectories in phase space.

## Key numbers

- The scaled Planck constant is \(\hbar = 4\pi\alpha_s\), where \(\alpha_s\) is the dimensionless coupling constant.
- The normalization constant for the wavefunction on each link is \(N(v) = 2\pi^2 I_1(2v)/v\), where \(I_1\) is a modified Bessel function.
- The function relating classical parameters to expectation values is \(f(v) = I_2(2v)/I_1(2v)\), which approaches unity as \(v \rightarrow \infty\) and behaves as \(v/2\) for \(v \ll 1\).
- The ensemble-averaged width parameter satisfies \(\langle\langle v \rangle\rangle \approx 7 / \hbar\) from numerical fits.
- For \(\hbar = 0.1\), the maximal Lyapunov exponent deviates very little from the classical result \(\lambda_c = T_e/3\).
- The critical \(\hbar\) for a first-order phase transition in the free energy is \(\hbar \approx 1.73\) at \(T=0\) and \(\hbar \approx 3\) at \(T=0.5\).
- The zero-point energy in the strong coupling limit is \(4\) (in scaled units, or \(4/g^2 a\) in original variables).

## Context

This work extends the classical real-time evolution method for non-abelian gauge fields on a lattice introduced by Muller (1992), which demonstrated chaotic behavior via a positive maximal Lyapunov exponent. The variational Gaussian approach was previously applied to scattering problems by Saraceno (1983) and to fermion many-body systems by Feldmeier (1990) and Coriano (1992). The results enable the study of how quantum corrections affect thermalization rates in pure gauge systems, suggesting that quantum effects accelerate the approach to equilibrium compared to purely classical evolution.
