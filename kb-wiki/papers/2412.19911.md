# Gauge covariance of the gap equation: from the rainbow truncation to gauge symmetry constraints

> The gauge covariance of the quark gap equation is compared for three quark-gluon vertices: the bare vertex, a Ball-Chiu like vertex constrained by the corresponding Slavnov-Taylor identity, and a full vertex including the transverse components derived from transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities. The covariance properties are verified with the chiral quark condensate and the pion decay constant in the chiral limit.

Here is the wiki page body based on the provided paper.

## Key claim
The gauge covariance of the quark gap equation is tested for three different quark-gluon vertex Ansatze (bare, Ball-Chiu, and a full vertex including transverse components), and the study finds that only the full vertex, constrained by both longitudinal and transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities, preserves gauge covariance of the chiral quark condensate and the pion decay constant in the chiral limit.

## Method
The author solves the quark gap equation in the rainbow truncation and then with a Ball-Chiu vertex that satisfies the longitudinal Slavnov-Taylor identity. Finally, a full vertex is constructed that also incorporates transverse components derived from transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities. The gauge covariance of the resulting solutions is verified by computing the chiral quark condensate and the pion decay constant in the chiral limit under different gauge choices.

## Key numbers
*   Chiral quark condensate: computed in the chiral limit to verify gauge covariance.
*   Pion decay constant: computed in the chiral limit to verify gauge covariance.

## Context
This work extends studies of the quark gap equation beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation by enforcing gauge symmetry constraints from Slavnov-Taylor identities. It builds on the Ball-Chiu vertex construction (Ball and Chiu, 1980) and more recent work on transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities (e.g., by Qin, Chang, and Roberts). The results enable more reliable, gauge-consistent calculations of hadron properties within the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework.
