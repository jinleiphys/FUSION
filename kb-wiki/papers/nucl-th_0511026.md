# Coulomb deexcitation of pionic hydrogen within close-coupling method

> The Coulomb deexcitation of $(πp)$ atom in collisions with the hydrogen atom has been studied in the fully quantum mechanical close-coupling approach for the first time. The total Coulomb deexcitation cross-sections of $nl\to n'l'$ transitions and $l$-averaged cross-sections are calculated for $n=3 - 8$ and at the relative energies $E=0.01 - 100$ eV. The strong interaction and vacuum polarization shifts of $ns$-states are taken into account. It is shown that the $Δn > 1$ transitions are very important and make up a substantial fraction of the Coulomb deexcitation cross-section (up to $\sim 47%$).

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully quantum mechanical close-coupling calculation of Coulomb deexcitation for pionic hydrogen colliding with atomic hydrogen, showing that transitions with principal quantum number change greater than one (Δn > 1) contribute substantially (up to ~47%) to the total cross section, contrary to earlier semiclassical and adiabatic models that underestimated these contributions.

## Method

The close-coupling method was applied to the scattering system (π⁻p)ₙₗ + H₁ₛ, solving coupled second-order differential equations for the radial wavefunctions of relative motion. The interaction potential included all four Coulomb pair interactions between the exotic atom and the hydrogen target, with multipole expansion of the coupling matrix elements. The calculations included all exotic atom states from n=1 up to the initial n₀, and accounted for strong interaction shifts (Δε_str₁ₛ = -7.11 eV) and vacuum polarization shifts of ns-states.

## Key numbers

- Strong interaction shift for 1s state: Δε_str₁ₛ = -7.11 eV, scaled as Δε_str_ns = Δε_str₁ₛ/n³
- Vacuum polarization shifts: -3.24 eV (1s), -0.37 eV (2s), -0.11 eV (3s)
- Energy range studied: E_cm = 0.01 to 100 eV
- Principal quantum numbers studied: n = 3 to 8
- Δn > 1 transitions contribute 20-47% of total CD cross section for n ≥ 4 at all energies considered
- For 3l → 2l' transitions at energies near 0.1 eV, suppression of cross sections is about one order of magnitude when ns-state energy shifts are included
- Transitions 3s → 2s, 2p are enhanced at threshold by a factor of about 3-4 when energy shifts are included
- For n=4, the relative contribution of Δn=2 transitions (4 → 2) to total CD transitions 4 → n' ≤ 3 varies from 20% to 39%
- At E_cm < 1 eV and n > 4, CD cross sections show approximately 1/√E behavior rather than 1/E

## Context

This work extends the close-coupling method previously applied to muonic hydrogen (Pomerantsev and Popov, 2005) to the pionic hydrogen case, and contradicts earlier semiclassical (Bracci and Fiorentini, 1978) and adiabatic (Korenman, 1996) calculations that gave CD cross sections about one order of magnitude smaller for n=3-5 transitions. The results enable more reliable analysis of K X-ray yields and neutron time-of-flight spectra from pionic atom experiments, and the authors note that their calculations explain the high-energy component around 105 eV due to the 5 → 3 transition and lead to good agreement with the experimental weight of the 3 → 2 component at 209 eV.
