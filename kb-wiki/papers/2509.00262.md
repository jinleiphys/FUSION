# Theory overview: electroweak emission from heavy-ion collisions

> An important class of observables in the heavy-ion collision programme concerns probes which are not sensitive to the prevailing strong interactions of QCD. The emission of photons, weak gauge bosons, and leptons fall into this category. Here I will describe the current status of such investigations, focusing on the emerging theoretical picture and its uncertainties.

## Key claim

Electroweak probes (photons, dileptons, weak bosons) are valuable for diagnosing the quark-gluon plasma because they escape the medium unmodified, but the theoretical picture remains uncertain due to the challenge of disentangling multiple emission sources (prompt, pre-equilibrium, thermal, hadronic) and the difficulty of treating the earliest, highly anisotropic stage of the collision.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework for electroweak emission in heavy-ion collisions, which proceeds in two steps: (i) microscopic calculation of production rates using field theory (perturbative QCD, effective theories, or lattice QCD) and (ii) macroscopic embedding of those rates into hydrodynamic simulations (e.g. MUSIC, Trajectum) that integrate over the spacetime history of the collision. The rates are expressed in terms of the vector spectral function, which can be evaluated either by weak-coupling expansion in alpha_s or by non-perturbative lattice QCD with spectral reconstruction.

## Key numbers

- Thermal equilibration time tau_eq ~ 1 fm/c for the longitudinal momentum distributions to approach local thermal equilibrium
- Lattice QCD simulations at T = 220 MeV with lattice spacing a = 0.028 fm and unphysical pion mass m_pi = 320 MeV, measuring correlators at momenta k = T x 2pi n/3 for n = 1,2,3
- Fixed coupling alpha_s ~ 0.3 (g ~ 2) commonly used in perturbative calculations, though not always stated
- Drell-Yan cross section at LO involves quark charge fractions Q_i = {2/3, -1/3} and factorization/renormalization scales mu_R, mu_F = M varied by a factor of 2 to estimate uncertainty
- Thermal rates scale as T^4, with effective temperature T_eff extracted from exponential fits to pT spectra (photons) or M spectra (dileptons)
- The angular coefficient lambda_theta receives large NLO correction for M <~ 2 GeV in the QGP phase
- For Drell-Yan at LO with large longitudinal quark momenta: lambda_theta^CS = 1; for purely transverse initial quark momenta: lambda_theta^CS = -1/3

## Context

This review extends the theoretical discussions in Du (2025), Vujanovic (2024), Paquet (2023), and Tripolt (2020), and complements the experimental overview by Sebastian (2025). The work synthesizes recent progress in perturbative QCD calculations (Jackson 2019), lattice QCD studies of the vector spectral function (Ali 2024, Ce 2020, Ce 2022), pre-equilibrium kinetic theory (Garcia-Montero 2024, Coquet 2021), and dilepton polarization (Wu 2024, Coquet 2023). Open questions include the tension between direct photon v_2 measurements (underestimated by theory) and lattice indications that perturbation theory overestimates thermal photons, as well as the scaling of direct photons with charged particle multiplicity.
