# Three-flavor neutrino oscillations using the Phase Space Approach

> The Phase-Space Approximation (PSA) approach, originally applied in [Phys. Rev. D 106, 123006 (2022)] to describe neutrino oscillations from a stellar object in the two-flavor limit, is extended here to describe the more realistic case where neutrinos can oscillate between three different flavors. The approach is successfully validated against the exact solutions up to eight neutrinos. In all cases where the exact solution is feasible, the PSA provides excellent reproduction of the neutrino oscillation dynamics. By replacing the full problem with a set of simple mean-field equations, the PSA offers a versatile, predictive, and easily parallelizable approach for tackling three-flavor problems. This enables the simulation of large-scale neutrino oscillations, as illustrated here with simulations involving up to 300 neutrinos. Additionally, the method provides insight into the system's equilibration properties.

## Key claim

The Phase-Space Approximation (PSA) method, previously validated for two-flavor neutrino oscillations, is extended to the three-flavor case and shown to reproduce exact quantum dynamics for systems up to eight neutrinos, with the caveat that some deviations between PSA and exact results appear at very long times (beyond approximately 100 inverse coupling constant units), though the method still closely tracks the exact dynamics and correctly captures asymptotic flavor equilibration.

## Method

The authors map the quantum many-body problem of interacting three-flavor neutrinos onto a statistical ensemble of independent mean-field trajectories, each initialized with stochastically sampled initial conditions that reproduce the quantum fluctuations of the initial Slater determinant state. Each trajectory evolves according to the mean-field equations of motion for the eight-component polarization vectors in SU(3) space, derived from the Ehrenfest theorem with the factorization approximation for two-body expectation values. The statistical ensemble of trajectories is propagated using an adaptive fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration method with 500 time steps, and observables are obtained as statistical averages over the ensemble with error bars computed using Jackknife binning.

## Key numbers

* For 6 neutrinos initialized to the state |ν_e ν_μ ν_τ ν_τ ν_μ ν_e⟩, PSA with 10^4 Metropolis iterations shows excellent agreement with exact solutions, correctly reproducing the damping of flavor oscillations induced by neutrino-neutrino interactions.
* For 8 neutrinos initialized to |ν_e ν_μ ν_e ν_τ ν_τ ν_e ν_μ ν_e⟩, PSA results are hardly distinguishable from exact solutions at short times, with some deviations appearing for times beyond approximately 100 inverse coupling constant units (t > 100 μ^{-1}).
* At very long times, the system exhibits flavor equilibration reaching an equipartition where each flavor has a probability of 1/3, which is properly captured by the PSA method.
* The method enables simulations involving up to 300 neutrinos, demonstrating scalability far beyond the exact solution limit of approximately 10 neutrinos for the three-flavor case.

## Context

This work extends the Phase-Space Approximation previously applied to two-flavor neutrino oscillations by Lacroix (2022) and Lacroix (2024) to the more realistic three-flavor case, building on the Hamiltonian formulation used in quantum computing studies by Turro (2024), Chen (2025), and Spagnolo (2025). The approach provides a classical computational method that can simulate systems with hundreds of neutrinos, enabling exploration of the transition from microscopic to macroscopic regimes and serving as a potential classical benchmark for future quantum computer simulations of neutrino oscillations.
