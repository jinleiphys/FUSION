# Heavy Diquark Symmetry Constraints for Strong Decays

> The Heavy Diquark Symmetry (HDS) of Doubly Heavy Baryons (DHBs) provides new insights into the spectroscopy of these hadrons. We derive the consequences of this symmetry for the mass spectra and the decay widths of DHBs. We compare these symmetry constraints to results from a nonrelativistic quark model for the mass spectra and results from the $^3P_0$ model for strong decays. The quark model we implement was not constructed with these symmetries and contains interactions which explicitly break HDS. Nevertheless, these symmetries emerge. We argue that the $^3P_0$ model and any other model for strong transitions which employs a spectator assumption explicitly respects HDS. We also explore the possibility of treating the strange quark as a heavy quark and apply these ideas to $Ξ$, $Ξ_c$, and $Ξ_b$ baryons.

## Key claim

Heavy Diquark Symmetry (HDS) provides constraints on the mass spectra and strong decay widths of Doubly Heavy Baryons (DHBs), and these symmetry constraints emerge even in a nonrelativistic quark model that was not constructed with these symmetries and contains interactions that explicitly break HDS.

## Method

The authors derive the consequences of Heavy Diquark Symmetry for DHB mass spectra and strong decay widths, then compare these symmetry constraints to results from a nonrelativistic quark model (the Roberts-Pervin or RP model) for mass spectra and results from the $^3P_0$ model for strong decays. They use the $^3P_0$ model to calculate pion-emission decay amplitudes and widths for DHBs, applying spin-counting relations derived from the decoupling of the heavy diquark's total angular momentum from the light degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The energy of an orbital excitation of a heavy diquark is about 230 MeV and nearly independent of flavor for doubly heavy hadrons containing $c$ and/or $b$ quarks
- The energy required to orbitally excite the light degrees of freedom from a $J_l^{\pi_l}=1/2^+$ state to a $J_l^{\pi_l}=1/2^-$ state is about 350 MeV in the RP model, and is the same for a $J_d^{\pi_d}=1^+$ diquark as for a $J_d^{\pi_d}=1^-$ diquark
- The phenomenological constant $g$ in the $^3P_0$ model is chosen to have the value 1.4 to reproduce the decay width of the $\Xi(1530)$ (9.1 $\pm$ 0.5 MeV)
- The size parameter $\beta$ for the daughter pion ground state harmonic oscillator wave function is 0.623 GeV

## Context

This work extends the superflavor symmetry first proposed by Savage and Wise (1990), which relates heavy mesons to DHBs containing a heavy diquark in the ground state, to include excited heavy diquarks. The authors compare their symmetry constraints to results from the Roberts-Pervin quark model (2008) and the $^3P_0$ model of Le Yaouanc et al. (1973, 1974). The only prior treatment of DHB strong decays in the literature is by Hu and Mehen (2006), who used Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory incorporating the superflavor symmetry to calculate strong decay widths of the lowest lying $\Xi_{cc}$ and $\Xi_{bb}$ excited states. This work enables future experimental identification of DHB resonances by showing that pion-emission decays provide a reliable way to determine if a resonance contains an excited heavy diquark.
