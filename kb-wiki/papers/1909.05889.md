# Towards the Hadron-Quark Continuity Via a Topology Change in Compact Stars

> We construct a generalized EFT approach to dense compact-star matter that exploits the CCP for hadron-quark continuity at high density, hidden topology and hidden symmetries of QCD. No Landau-Ginzburg-Wilsonian-type phase transition is involved. The microscopic DoF of QCD possibly intervening at high baryonic density are traded in for fractionalized topological objects. Essential in the description are symmetries invisible in QCD in the matter-free vacuum: Scale symmetry, flavor local symmetry and parity-doubling. The partial emergence of scale symmetry is signaled by a dilatonic scalar in a "pseudo-conformal" structure. Flavor gauge symmetry manifests with the $ρ$ meson mass going toward a Wilsonian RGFP identified with the VMFP at which the gauge boson mass goes to zero. Parity doubling is to take place as the quasi-nucleon mass converges to the chiral invariant $m_0$. The theory accounts satisfactorily for all known properties of normal nuclear matter and makes certain predictions that are drastically different from what's available in the literature. In particular, it provides a topological mechanism, argued to be robust, for the cross-over from soft-to-hard EoS that predicts the star properties in overall agreement with the presently available data, including the maximum star mass $M_{max}\sim 2.3 M_\odot$ and the recent LIGO/Virgo GW data. What is most glaringly different from all other approaches known, however, is the prediction for the rapid convergence to a sound velocity of star $v_s^2\approx 1/3$ at a density $n\gsim 3 n_0$, far from the asymptotic density $\gsim 50n_0$ expected in pQCD. We interpret this to signal the onset of albeit approximate conformal symmetry in dense compact-star matter. The model developed here could bring out a new paradigm in nuclear/hadron physics, exploiting ideas in condensed matter physics, nuclear and particle physics and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a generalized effective field theory (GnEFT) for dense compact-star matter that avoids any Landau-Ginzburg-Wilsonian-type phase transition, instead using a topology change in hadronic variables to encode hadron-quark continuity. The key hedge is that the theory predicts a rapid convergence of the sound velocity to the conformal value \(v_s^2\approx 1/3\) at densities \(n\gsim 3 n_0\), far below the asymptotic density \(\gsim 50 n_0\) expected from perturbative QCD, which the authors interpret as the onset of approximate conformal symmetry in dense matter.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian called \(bs\)HLS (baryon, scalar dilaton, hidden local symmetry) that incorporates three symmetries invisible in QCD in vacuum: scale symmetry (with a dilatonic scalar), flavor local symmetry (with vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\)), and parity doubling. They simulate dense matter using a skyrmion crystal approach on a lattice, where a topology change (a cusp in the equation of state) arises from the interplay of the dilaton and vector mesons, leading to fractionalized skyrmions and the emergence of parity doubling at high density. The theory is then applied to compute the equation of state and compact-star properties, with parameters constrained by normal nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum star mass predicted: \(M_{max}\sim 2.3 M_\odot\)
- Sound velocity converges to \(v_s^2\approx 1/3\) (in units \(c=1\)) at density \(n\gsim 3 n_0\), where \(n_0\approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density
- The asymptotic density for perturbative QCD is \(\gsim 50 n_0\), far above the density where the conformal sound speed appears
- The vector manifestation fixed point (VMFP) where the \(\rho\) meson mass goes to zero occurs at a critical density \(n_{\rm vm}\gsim 25 n_0\)
- Observed maximum masses: PSR J1614-2230 at \(1.908\pm 0.016 M_\odot\), PSR J0348+0432 at \(2.01\pm 0.04 M_\odot\), PSR J0740+6620 at \(2.17^{+0.11}_{-0.10} M_\odot\)
- Tidal deformability bound from GW170817: \(400 < \tilde{\Lambda} < 800\) for the mass-weighted tidal deformability
- Pressure bounds from GW170817: \(P(2n_0)=3.5^{+2.7}_{-1.7}\times 10^{34}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) and \(P(6n_0)=9.0^{+7.9}_{-2.6}\times 10^{34}\) dyn/cm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat Principle approach to dense matter, building on hidden local symmetry (HLS) ideas from Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and scale-chiral symmetry from Crewther and Tunstall (2015). It contrasts sharply with standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT), which includes only pions and nucleons and is limited to densities near \(n_0\), and with relativistic mean field models that do not incorporate the topology change. The predictions enable tests via gravitational wave observations (LIGO/Virgo), rare-ion-beam experiments (such as HIAF in China and RAON in Korea) that could probe the cusp in nuclear symmetry energy, and future measurements of neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities.
