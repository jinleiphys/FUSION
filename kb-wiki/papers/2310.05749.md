# A new approach to the 3-momentum regularization of the in-medium one and two fermion line integrals with applications to cross sections in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We propose the 3-momentum sphere intersection regularization applied to the one and two fermion line integrals at finite temperature and chemical potential. The quark-antiquark polarization function in this new regularization approach is equivalent to the usual 3-momentum regularization, when the absolute value of the external 3-momentum of the polarization is zero. Additionally, it respects the particle-antiparticle symmetry of meson states in the Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for all values of temperature and chemical potential. Without this symmetry, in-medium cross sections calculated in the 3-momentum regularized NJL model are not consistent. In order to demonstrate the difference between the usual 3-momentum regularization with the one proposed in this work, we study the quark-quark and quark-antiquark cross sections in both regularization schemes. To this end we use the standard $SU(3)$ NJL model, with four and six quark interactions. We observe major quantitative and qualitative differences when comparing quark-quark cross sections in both schemes. The quark-antiquark cross sections, on the other hand, are very similar in both regularizations, owning to the equivalence between the regularizations when the absolute value of the external 3-momentum is zero.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "3-momentum sphere intersection regularization" for one and two fermion line integrals in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that preserves particle-antiparticle symmetry of meson states for all values of temperature and chemical potential, unlike the usual 3-momentum regularization which breaks this symmetry and leads to inconsistent in-medium cross sections.

## Method

The authors derive the one fermion line integral (A) and two fermion line integral (B0) from linear and quadratic expansions of the SU(3) NJL Lagrangian with four and six quark interactions, using the mean field approximation and Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations. They implement the new regularization by requiring that each quark propagator's individual 3-momentum be smaller than the cutoff Λ, which corresponds to integrating over the intersection of N spheres for N fermion line integrals, rather than the usual approach of shifting integration variables that breaks symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The 3-momentum cutoff Λ is the maximum value of the absolute value of the internal 3-momentum of quarks, |p| < Λ, used in both regularization schemes
- If the external 3-momentum of a meson is larger than 2Λ, the spheres corresponding to each quark propagator in the quark-antiquark polarization function no longer intersect, and the polarization function is automatically zero
- The quark-antiquark polarization function in the new regularization is equivalent to the usual 3-momentum regularization when the absolute value of the external 3-momentum of the polarization is zero

## Context

This work extends the NJL model regularization framework of Rehberg (1995) and Rehberg (1996), who identified that the usual 3-momentum regularization breaks particle-antiparticle symmetry in the quark-antiquark polarization loop mixing light and strange quarks. The new approach solves the problem that physical scattering processes which ought to be identical (such as us → us and su → su) give different results in the standard regularization, and enables consistent calculation of transport coefficients like shear and bulk viscosities and electrical and thermal conductivities of strongly interacting matter.
