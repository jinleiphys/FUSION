# The QCD Equation of State to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^4)$ from Lattice QCD

> We present first results from a first-principles calculation of the QCD equation of state to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^4)$, where $μ_B$ is the baryon chemical potential. We find that second-order corrections are sufficient for a large part of the freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential range achieved by the RHIC beam energy scan. Nevertheless, higher-order corrections are necessary to extend the validity of the equation of state down to beam energies $s^{1/2}_{NN}\sim 20$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents first-principles lattice QCD results for the equation of state expanded to fourth order in baryon chemical potential, finding that second-order corrections are sufficient for most of the RHIC beam energy scan range, but higher-order corrections become necessary for beam energies below about 20 GeV per nucleon pair.

## Method

The authors used the HISQ action for staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extents Nt=6 and Nt=8, computing all quark number susceptibilities up to sixth order in a temperature range from 140 MeV to 330 MeV. They applied Taylor expansion of the pressure in baryon chemical potential, using constraints of zero net strangeness and fixed electric charge-to-baryon ratio appropriate for Pb-Pb collisions to reduce the expansion to a single chemical potential variable.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range covered: 140 MeV to 330 MeV
- Chiral crossover temperature: Tc = 154(9) MeV
- Baryon chemical potential values at freeze-out expected to reach 400-450 MeV at lowest RHIC energies
- Baryon number density leading-order description good down to sqrt(s_NN) ~ 30 GeV, with next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order results starting to differ below sqrt(s_NN) ~ 15 GeV
- Energy density on freeze-out curve roughly constant at approximately 0.3 GeV/fm^3 down to sqrt(s_NN) ~ 30 GeV
- Fourth-order corrections to energy density produce a 10-15% increase over second-order values in the temperature range 140-160 MeV

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD equation of state calculations previously available only at zero baryon chemical potential (Bazavov 2014, Borsanyi 2013) to finite baryon density using Taylor expansion methods (Gavai 2001, Allton 2002). The results are directly applicable to modeling the RHIC beam energy scan and will enable hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at lower beam energies, with the goal of producing an equation of state valid down to sqrt(s_NN) ~ 20 GeV. The freeze-out curve parametrization follows Cleymans (1999) and Karsch (2010).
