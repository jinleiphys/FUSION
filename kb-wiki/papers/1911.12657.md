# Two photon transition form factors of neutral pseudoscalar mesons

> Starting from the axial vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identity, and the necessity of preserving this identity when attempting to calculate the properties describing pseudoscalar mesons, we describe a Bethe-Salpeter kernel which takes into account the non-Abelian anomaly and therefore is suitable to describe the flavor mixing states $η,η^\prime$. We then report results for the two photon transition form factors of $η,η^\prime$, and compare them with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Bethe-Salpeter kernel combining a standard Rainbow-Ladder truncation with a nonperturbative Ansatz for the anomalous kernel, derived from the axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identity, can successfully describe the two-photon transition form factors of the flavor-mixing η and η′ mesons, with the caveat that the anomalous kernel's form is not derived from first principles but rather constructed via an educated Ansatz.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations for the η and η′ systems, using a kernel that is the sum of a Rainbow-Ladder term (standard for light quarks) and an anomalous term that accounts for the non-Abelian axial anomaly via purely gluonic configurations. The parameters in the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations are fixed by requiring that the solutions reproduce the empirical η and η′ masses and the four phenomenologically-determined values of the light- and strange-quark η, η′ decay constants. The resulting bound-state wave functions are then used to compute the γ*γ → η, η′ transition form factors.

## Key numbers

- mη = 0.56 GeV (computed) compared to experimental value 0.55 GeV
- mη′ = 0.96 GeV (computed) compared to experimental value 0.96 GeV
- Asymptotic limit of the π0 form factor: 2 fπ = 0.186 GeV, used as a reference curve in both η and η′ form factor plots

## Context

This work extends the Rainbow-Ladder truncation approach, which is known to be accurate for ground-state light-quark vector- and isospin-nonzero-pseudoscalar mesons (Horn 2016, Eichmann 2016, Fischer 2018, Burkert 2019), to the flavor-mixing η and η′ systems where the non-Abelian anomaly is essential. The anomalous kernel Ansatz was previously put forward in Ding (2018). The results enable future extension to doubly off-shell transition form factors, for which first data on γ*(k1) γ*(k2) → η′ now exist from BaBar (2018).
