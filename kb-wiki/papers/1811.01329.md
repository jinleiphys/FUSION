# First calculation of $\hat{q}$ on a quenched SU(3) plasma

> The jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ is the leading transport coefficient that controls the modification of hard jets produced in heavy-ion collisions. This coefficient is inherently non-perturbative, and hence, is challenging to compute from first principles. In this report, we present a perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) and lattice gauge theory based formulation to study $\hat{q}$. We formulate $\hat{q}$ within a 4-dimensional (4D) quenched SU(3) lattice. We consider a leading order diagram for a hard parton passing through the quark-gluon plasma. The non-perturbative part is expressed in terms of a non-local (two-point) Field-Strength-Field-Strength (FF) operator product which can be Taylor expanded after analytic continuation to the Euclidean region. Such an expansion allows us to write $\hat{q}$ in terms of the expectation of local operators. Finally, we present our results for $\hat{q}$ in a pure gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) for a quenched SU(3) plasma, but the result is obtained by truncating the operator product expansion to the first two terms and relies on a dispersion relation that connects the Euclidean computed quantity to the physical Minkowski-space \(\hat{q}\) via an estimate of the thermal discontinuity width from hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations.

## Method
The authors formulate \(\hat{q}\) as a series of local operators in Minkowski space by analytically continuing a generalized coefficient \(\hat{Q}(q^{+})\) to the Euclidean region and performing a Taylor expansion of the non-local field-strength-field-strength (FF) operator product. They then rotate the operator products to Euclidean space and compute the expectation values of the first two local operators on a 4D quenched SU(3) lattice using the Wilson gauge action with a heat-bath algorithm. The lattice spacing is set using the two-loop perturbative renormalization group equation with non-perturbative correction determined by matching the Polyakov loop to the known critical temperature \(T_c \sim 265\) MeV for pure SU(3) gauge theory.

## Key numbers
- \(\hat{q}/T^3 \sim 0.5 - 1\) at high temperature for a pure gluon plasma, extracted from the real part of the transport coefficient.
- The critical temperature for pure SU(3) gauge theory is taken as \(T_c \sim 265\) MeV.
- The hard quark momentum \(q^{-}\) is set to 20 GeV for the operator evaluation.
- The width of the thermal discontinuity in the \(q^{+}\) complex plane is estimated from HTL as \(T_1 + T_2 \sim 2T - 4T\).
- Lattice sizes used: \(n_{\tau} = 4, 6, 8\) with \(n_s = 4 n_{\tau}\) (i.e., \(n_s = 16, 24, 32\) respectively).
- Statistical average over 5000 gauge configurations per parameter set.

## Context
This work extends a previous lattice formulation for quenched SU(2) plasma by one of the authors (Majumder, Ref. QuenchedSU2Calculation) to the physically relevant SU(3) case. It provides a first-principles alternative to phenomenological extractions such as those from the JET collaboration (Burke et al.), which parameterize \(\hat{q}\) as a constant times \(T^3\) and fit to experimental \(R_{AA}\) data at RHIC and LHC. The result enables future unquenched calculations including dynamical quarks and improved actions, and opens the possibility of computing \(\hat{q}\) for a full quark-gluon plasma from lattice QCD.
