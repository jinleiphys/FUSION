# Phase diagram of two-color quark matter at nonzero baryon and isospin density

> We investigate the properties of cold dense quark matter composed of two colors and two flavors of light quarks. In particular, we perform the first model calculation of the full phase diagram at nonzero baryon and isospin density, thus matching the model-independent predictions of chiral perturbation theory at low density to the conjectured phase structure at high density. We confirm the presence of the Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase in the phase diagram and study its dependence on the tunable parameter in the Lagrangian that simulates the effects of the quantum axial anomaly. As a byproduct, we clarify the calculation of the thermodynamic potential in the presence of the FF pairing, which was previously based on an ad hoc subtraction of an unphysical cutoff artifact. Furthermore, we argue that close to the diquark (or pion) Bose-Einstein condensation transition, the system behaves as a dilute Bose gas so that our simple fermionic model in the mean-field approximation is not quantitatively adequate. We suggest that including thermal fluctuations of the order parameter for Bose-Einstein condensation is crucial for understanding available lattice data.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first model calculation of the full phase diagram of two-color quark matter at nonzero baryon and isospin density, confirming the presence of a Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase at high chemical potential. However, the authors note that close to the diquark (or pion) Bose-Einstein condensation transition, the system behaves as a dilute Bose gas so that their simple fermionic model in the mean-field approximation is not quantitatively adequate, and they suggest that including thermal fluctuations of the order parameter is crucial for understanding available lattice data.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two colors and two flavors of light quarks, employing a generalized interaction Lagrangian that includes a parameter ζ to simulate the effects of the quantum axial anomaly. They perform a mean-field approximation that includes chiral, diquark, and pion condensates with the possibility of spatial (FF) modulation of the latter two, and evaluate the thermodynamic potential numerically without further approximation, using a proper regularization scheme to avoid unphysical cutoff artifacts.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 75.4 MeV
- One-flavor chiral condensate: ⟨ψ̄_u ψ_u⟩ = (-218 MeV)³
- NJL parameters: G = 7.23 GeV⁻², m_0 = 5.4 MeV, Λ = 657 MeV
- BCS-BEC crossover occurs at μ_{B,I} ≈ 229 MeV, determined by the expression (2M_0 m_π²)^{1/3} where M_0 is the constituent quark mass in vacuum
- For ζ=1, the critical value of μ_d at which a strong first-order chiral symmetry breaking/restoration phase transition occurs is μ_d ≈ 330 MeV

## Context

This work extends the model-independent predictions of chiral perturbation theory at low density (Splittorff 2000) to the conjectured phase structure at high density, and confirms the presence of the FF phase previously investigated by Fukushima (2007). The results enable comparison with lattice simulations of two-color QCD (Hands 2005, 2006, 2010) and provide a framework for understanding the BCS-BEC crossover studied by Sun (2007), while also clarifying the calculation of the thermodynamic potential in the presence of FF pairing that was previously based on an ad hoc subtraction of an unphysical cutoff artifact.
