# Charged and Neutral Current Pion Production in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> In this article, we present the charged and neutral current coherent pion production in the neutrino-nucleus interaction in the resonance region using the formalism based on the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) theorem which relates the neutrino-nucleus cross section to the pion-nucleus elastic cross section. The pion nucleus elastic cross section is calculated using the Glauber model approach. We calculate the integrated cross sections for neutrino-carbon, neutrino-iron and neutrino-oxygen scattering. The results of integrated cross-section calculations are compared with the measured data

## Key claim
The paper claims that the PCAC-based model, which relates neutrino-nucleus cross sections to pion-nucleus elastic cross sections calculated via the Glauber model, gives a good description of MINERvA data for neutrino-carbon scattering and of T2K data for neutrino-oxygen scattering, but only at low neutrino energy for the latter.

## Method
The authors used the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) theorem to relate the differential cross section for coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering to the pion-nucleus elastic cross section. The pion-nucleus elastic cross section was calculated using the Glauber model approach. Integrated cross sections were computed for neutrino-carbon, neutrino-iron, and neutrino-oxygen scattering as functions of neutrino energy, with a parameter xi controlling the pion-dominance picture.

## Key numbers
- For neutrino-carbon scattering, the total cross section as a function of neutrino energy was calculated for xi = 1 and 2, and compared with MINERvA data; the calculations are compatible with the data.
- For neutrino-oxygen scattering, the total cross section as a function of neutrino energy was calculated for xi = 1 and 2, and compared with T2K data; the calculations are compatible with the data at lower neutrino energy.
- The cross section is reduced with increasing the value of xi from 1 to 2 for all three targets (carbon, iron, oxygen) in both charged current and neutral current channels.

## Context
This work extends the PCAC-based formalism previously applied by Kopeliovich (1992) and Saraswat (2016) to coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering. The calculations are compared with experimental data from the MINERvA collaboration (Higuera, 2014) for carbon targets and the T2K collaboration (Abe, 2016) for oxygen targets. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino interactions in the resonance region, which is important for neutrino oscillation experiments using carbon, oxygen, and iron detectors.
