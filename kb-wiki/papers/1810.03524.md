# QCD critical end point from a realistic PNJL model

> With parameters fixed by critical temperature and equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential, a realistic Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (rPNJL) model predicts a critical end point of chiral phase transition at $(μ_B^E= 720 {\rm MeV}, T^E=93 {\rm MeV})$. The extracted freeze-out line from heavy ion collisions is close to the chiral phase transition boundary in the rPNJL model, and the kurtosis $κσ^2$ of baryon number fluctuations from the rPNJL model along the experimental freeze-out line agrees well with the BES-I measurement. Our analysis shows that the dip structure of measured $κσ^2$ is determined by the relationship between the freeze-out line and chiral phase transition line at low baryon density region, and the peak structure can be regarded as a clean signature for the existence of CEP.

## Key claim

The realistic Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (rPNJL) model predicts a critical end point (CEP) of the chiral phase transition at \((\mu_B^E = 720\ \mathrm{MeV}, T^E = 93\ \mathrm{MeV})\), but the authors note that the location is model-dependent and that the peak structure of the kurtosis \(\kappa\sigma^2\) along the freeze-out line, rather than the exact CEP coordinates, is the clean signature for the existence of the CEP.

## Method

The authors constructed a 3-flavor rPNJL model that includes 8-quark interactions and a Polyakov-loop potential with a logarithmic term. Parameters for the NJL part were fixed by vacuum properties, and parameters for the Polyakov-loop part were fixed by global fitting of the pressure density at zero chemical potential, so that the critical temperature and equation of state at \(\mu_B = 0\) agree with lattice data. The kurtosis \(\kappa\sigma^2 = \chi_4^B / \chi_2^B\) of baryon number fluctuations was then computed along two fitted experimental freeze-out lines and compared with BES-I measurements.

## Key numbers

* CEP location: \((\mu_B^E = 720\ \mathrm{MeV}, T^E = 93\ \mathrm{MeV})\).
* Chiral phase transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential: \(T_c = 161\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Kurtosis \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of baryon number fluctuations in the hadron resonance gas (HRG) limit: 1; in the ideal free quark gas (FQG) limit at very high temperature: \(\kappa\sigma^2 \simeq 0.068\).
* The dip structure of \(\kappa\sigma^2\) along freeze-out line \(f1\) occurs around \(\mu_B = 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(\sqrt{s} = 20\ \mathrm{GeV}\)), and the peak structure occurs around \(\mu_B = 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(\sqrt{s} = 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
* The measured \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of net proton number distributions from BES-I starts from about 1 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\ \mathrm{GeV}\), decreases to around 0.1 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 20\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and rises quickly up to 3.5 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD CEP using effective chiral models such as the NJL model, PNJL model, quark-meson (QM) models, Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE), and 5-dimensional holographic QCD models (Critelli 2017, Li 2017). It specifically improves upon the regular NJL model by including gluodynamics via the Polyakov loop, which brings the kurtosis of baryon number fluctuations into agreement with lattice data at zero chemical potential and with BES-I measurements along the freeze-out line. The analysis enables the interpretation that the dip structure of \(\kappa\sigma^2\) reflects the relative position of the freeze-out line to the chiral phase boundary at low baryon density, while the peak structure is a clean signature for the existence of the CEP.
