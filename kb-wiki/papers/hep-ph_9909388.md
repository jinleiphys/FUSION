# The Boltzmann equation for gluons at early times after a heavy ion collision

> A Boltzmann equation is given for the early stages of evolution of the gluon system produced in a head-on collision. The collision term is taken from gluon- gluon approximation. < p_\perp > and < p_z^2 > are evaluated as a function of time using initial conditions taken from the McLerran-Venugopalan model.

## Key claim

The Boltzmann equation for gluons at early times after a heavy ion collision reduces to a Fokker-Planck equation in the logarithmic approximation for small-angle scattering, but the analytic solution only holds while typical longitudinal momenta remain much smaller than typical transverse momenta, breaking down at a time when the two become comparable.

## Method

The author writes a Boltzmann equation for the gluon distribution function f(p⊥, pz, t) with the collision term taken from elastic gluon-gluon scattering in the one-gluon exchange approximation. In the logarithmic approximation for integrating over the scattering angle, with an infrared cutoff θm³ = t0/t, the Boltzmann equation reduces to a Fokker-Planck equation. Initial conditions are taken from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, with the gluon distribution immediately after the collision given by f_s(t, p) = δ(pz) Θ(Qs² - p⊥²) c (Nc²-1)/(4π³ α Nc).

## Key numbers

- The gluon number density N(t) decreases like 1/t, as shown by ∂η/∂ξ = 0 where η = tN and ξ = (1/3) ln(t/t0)
- For the McLerran-Venugopalan model, η = c (Nc²-1)/(4π² α Nc) Qs² and η_{-1} = c (Nc²-1)/(2π² α Nc) Qs
- The typical transverse momentum evolves as ⟨p⊥/Q̄⟩ = 1 - c (α Nc)/(4π) ξ², where Q̄ = (2/3) Qs
- The typical longitudinal momentum squared evolves as ⟨(pz/Q̄)²⟩ = c (3α Nc)/(4π) ξ
- The system reaches ⟨pz²⟩ ≈ ⟨p⊥⟩² when ξ₁ = √(2π/(c α Nc)) + O(α⁰)

## Context

This paper extends the author's earlier work (Mueller 1999) which studied the early stages of the gluon system using a simpler equation for the rate of change of typical transverse momentum. The earlier approach broke down when typical transverse and longitudinal momenta became equal, and this Boltzmann equation reformulation provides a more general framework. The work uses initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model and enables future numerical solutions of the Fokker-Planck equation to follow the gluon system beyond the point where analytic estimates fail, toward thermal equilibrium.
