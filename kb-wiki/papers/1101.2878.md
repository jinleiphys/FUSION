# Fluctuations and asymmetric jet events in PbPb collisions at the LHC

> Recent LHC results concerning full jet-quenching in PbPb collisions have been presented in terms of a jet asymmetry parameter, measuring the imbalance between the transverse momenta of leading and subleading jets. We examine the potential sensitivity of this distribution to fluctuations from the heavy-ion background. Our results suggest that a detailed estimate of the true fluctuations would be of benefit in extracting quantitative information about jet quenching. We also find that the apparent impact of fluctuations on the jet asymmetry distribution can depend significantly on the choice of low-pt threshold used for the simulation of the hard pp events.

## Key claim

The paper argues that fluctuations from the heavy-ion background can significantly distort the measured dijet asymmetry distribution in PbPb collisions, and that a precise estimate of these fluctuations is necessary to extract quantitative information about jet quenching. The authors caution that the apparent impact of fluctuations depends critically on the low-\(p_t\) threshold used in the simulation of the hard \(pp\) events, a detail the abstract does not emphasize.

## Method

The authors performed a toy Monte Carlo study using Pythia 6.423 (DW tune) for \(pp\) events at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\ \text{TeV}\), adding Gaussian smearing with standard deviation \(\sigma_{\text{jet}}\) to each jet’s transverse momentum to mimic residual background fluctuations after subtraction. They also embedded Pythia events into a simulated PbPb background generated with HYDJET 1.6 (with quenching switched off), processed through a simplified calorimeter simulation, and applied the area/median background subtraction method using FastJet. Jet clustering used the anti-\(k_t\) algorithm with \(R=0.4\).

## Key numbers

- For central PbPb collisions (0–10% centrality) in HYDJET, the measured standard deviation of jet \(p_t\) fluctuations was \(\sigma_{\text{jet}} \simeq 17\ \text{GeV}\) for full jets (anti-\(k_t\), \(R=0.4\)).
- For charged-track jets in the same HYDJET simulation, \(\sigma_{\text{jet(chg)}} \simeq 11.4\ \text{GeV}\), in agreement with ALICE preliminary data which gave \(\sigma_{\text{jet(chg)}} \simeq 11.5\ \text{GeV}\).
- The average background level in HYDJET for 0–10% centrality was \(210\ \text{GeV}\) per unit area for \(|\eta|<2.8\), compatible with ATLAS measurements.
- The average charged-particle multiplicity for 0–10% centrality in HYDJET was 1400 for \(|\eta|<0.5\), consistent with ALICE data (Aamodt 2010).
- In the Gaussian smearing toy model, a value of \(\sigma_{\text{jet}} = 20\ \text{GeV}\) produced a broadening of the low \(\Delta\phi\) tail comparable to ATLAS results for 0–10% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of jet quenching measurements from ATLAS (2010) and CMS (2011) by highlighting the role of background fluctuations, which had not been quantitatively emphasized in those experimental papers. The authors compare their HYDJET simulations to ALICE preliminary data on charged-track jet resolution (ALICE 2011) and find good agreement. The paper enables future analyses to account for fluctuation biases when interpreting the jet asymmetry parameter \(A_J\), and suggests that unfolding or raising jet \(p_t\) thresholds may be necessary to isolate genuine quenching effects.
