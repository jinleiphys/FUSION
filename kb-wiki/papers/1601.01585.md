# Probing the Small-$x$ Gluon Tomography in Correlated Hard Diffractive Dijet Production in DIS

> We investigate the close connection between the quantum phase space Wigner distribution of small-$x$ gluons and the color dipole scattering amplitude, and propose to study it experimentally in the hard diffractive dijet production at the planned electron-ion collider. The angular correlation between the nucleon recoiled momentum and the dijet transverse momentum will probe the nontrivial correlation in the phase space Wigner distribution. This experimental study will not only provide us with three-dimensional tomographic pictures of gluons inside high energy proton, but also give a unique and interesting signal for the small-$x$ dynamics with QCD evolution effects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the angular correlation between the dijet transverse momentum and the nucleon recoil momentum in hard diffractive dijet production at a future electron-ion collider can directly probe the gluon Wigner distribution at small \(x\), but the observable asymmetry is predicted to be only a few percent in typical EIC kinematics, requiring careful measurement.

## Method

The authors derive the connection between the gluon Wigner distribution at small \(x\) and the impact-parameter-dependent dipole scattering amplitude, using the BFKL equation to compute the dipole T-matrix in impact parameter space. They then compute the cross section for diffractive dijet production in deep inelastic scattering from a transversely polarized virtual photon, showing that in the back-to-back kinematic region (\(|P_\perp| \gg |\Delta_\perp|\)) the cross section is proportional to the square of the dipole gluon generalized transverse momentum distribution (GTMD). The elliptic angular correlation \(\langle \cos 2(\phi_{P_\perp} - \phi_{\Delta_\perp}) \rangle\) is identified as the key observable.

## Key numbers

- The BFKL characteristic function is \(\chi(n,\nu) \equiv \frac{2\alpha_s N_c}{\pi} \left[\psi(1) - \text{Re}\psi\left(\frac{|n|+1}{2} + i\nu\right)\right]\).
- The saturation momentum from the BFKL solution gives \(Q_s^2 \sim \frac{x_\perp^2}{b_\perp^4} e^{\frac{\chi(\gamma_s)}{\gamma_s}Y} + \frac{\cos 2\phi_{br}}{2b_\perp^2}\) with \(\gamma_s = 0.628\).
- The elliptic angular asymmetry \(\langle \cos 2(\phi_{P_\perp} - \phi_{\Delta_\perp}) \rangle\) is predicted to be a few percent in typical EIC kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of parton Wigner distributions (Ji 2003, Belitsky 2003) to the small-\(x\) regime by connecting them to the dipole scattering amplitudes used in small-\(x\) factorization (Dominguez 2011). It builds on prior studies of diffractive dijet production (Altinoluk 2015) and impact-parameter-dependent Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution (Golec-Biernat 2003, Berger 2011). The proposed measurement at the future electron-ion collider would provide the first experimental access to the gluon Wigner distribution and test nontrivial angular correlations predicted by small-\(x\) QCD evolution.
