# Thermalization in small system of hadron gas and high-multiplicity pp events

> We study the system-size dependence of Knudsen number, a measure of degree of thermalization, for hadron resonance gas that follows the Lattice-QCD equation of state at zero chemical potential. A comparison between Knudsen numbers for the AuAu collisions at RHIC and the hadron gas of size similar to the size of high-multiplicity pp events at LHC, reassures the applicability of hydrodynamics in interpreting the features of particle production in high-multiplicity pp events.

# Thermalization in small system of hadron gas and high-multiplicity pp events

## Key claim
The degree of thermalization (quantified by the Knudsen number) of a hadron gas with system size comparable to high-multiplicity pp events at LHC is consistent with that of AuAu collisions at RHIC, provided the hadron gas follows the Lattice-QCD equation of state and includes Hagedorn states with excluded volume effects. The claim is conditional on using the most conservative cross-section approximation (pion gas cross-sections) and a temperature of 160 MeV.

## Method
The authors model a hadron resonance gas (HRG) in the grand canonical ensemble at zero chemical potential, incorporating the Hagedorn mass spectrum and excluded volume effects with a hard-core radius. Finite system-size effects are introduced by imposing a low-momentum cutoff p_cutoff = 197 pi / R (MeV), where R is the characteristic system size. The temperature-dependent number density and pion mean free path are computed for system sizes R = 2.5, 3, and 5 fm, using temperature-dependent pion-pion cross-sections from the literature. The Knudsen number Kn = lambda / R is then calculated as a function of system size at T = 160 MeV and compared with estimates from hydrodynamic models for AuAu collisions.

## Key numbers
- For a hadron gas of size R = 2.5 fm (corresponding to p_cutoff ≈ 250 MeV), the system can be described by the Lattice-QCD equation of state at zero chemical potential.
- The mean free path of pions asymptotically approaches approximately 1 fm at T ≈ 160 MeV for all considered system sizes.
- For system size R ≈ 4 fm, Kn ≈ 0.28; for R ≈ 2.5 fm, Kn ≈ 0.48.
- For AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, hydrodynamic estimates give Kn ≈ 0.3 for central events and Kn ≈ 0.5 for semi-central events.
- High-multiplicity pp events at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV with average charged multiplicity ⟨N_ch⟩ ≈ 136 have an effective emission radius R' = 3.1 fm (from Bose-Einstein correlation parameterization R'(⟨N_ch⟩) = a * ⟨N_ch⟩^(1/3) with a = 0.612 ± 0.007(stat.) ± 0.068(syst.) fm), corresponding to Kn ≈ 0.36.

## Context
This work extends studies of thermalization in small systems, motivated by the observation of long-range two-particle angular correlations and elliptic flow in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC (CMS and ATLAS collaborations, 2010-2013). It builds on the Hadron Resonance Gas model with Hagedorn mass spectrum and excluded volume effects, as developed by earlier authors (Hagedorn, 1965; Rischke et al., 1991), and uses finite-size corrections from momentum cutoff methods (Gorenstein and Yang, 1995). The comparison with AuAu collisions at RHIC uses hydrodynamic estimates of Knudsen number from elliptic flow analyses (Heinz and Snellings, 2013; Niemi et al., 2016). The results enable the interpretation of collective phenomena in small pp systems within the framework of relativistic fluid dynamics, provided the Knudsen number remains below 0.5, the threshold for applicability of dissipative fluid dynamics established by comparisons with the Boltzmann equation (Denicol et al., 2012; El et al., 2010).
