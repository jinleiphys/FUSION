# Simultaneous study of scattering and fusion hindrance near Coulomb barrier in $F+Pb$ systems

> A phenomenological optical potential is used to study the elastic angular distributions for the system $^{19}F+^{208}Pb$ close to the Coulomb barrier. This potential is constructed by taking into account the flexible potential developed by Ginocchio. The fluctuations in the real and imaginary parts of the optical model potential follow the trends of the threshold anomaly. The set of optical potential parameters needed to analyze the fusion cross sections of the same system are obtained through analysis of the scattering cross sections. Theoretical fusion cross-sections and results from four different experimental groups well agree for a range of energies. Several Fluorine (F) isotopes are used as projectiles in this study of fusion cross-sections by slightly altering the radial parameter. It was found that the fusion process occurs unfettered in the $^{19}F +^{208}Pb$ system below the Coulomb barrier but is seriously hindered in the case of its isotopic projectiles.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a phenomenological optical potential based on the Ginocchio potential, with a very small imaginary-to-real ratio (0.0127), can simultaneously describe elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion cross sections for the 19F+208Pb system. The fusion process occurs without hindrance below the Coulomb barrier for 19F+208Pb, but is seriously hindered for isotopic projectiles 21F and 23F, which the authors attribute to the decreasing two-neutron separation energy and binding energy per nucleon as one moves toward neutron drip-line nuclei.

## Method
The authors constructed a complex optical potential using the analytically solvable Ginocchio potential with lambda=1, featuring a neck-like structure at the junction between volume and surface regions at r=R0. They solved the Schrodinger equation with this potential to obtain scattering amplitudes and cross sections, fitting six energy-independent parameters (R0=12.5 fm, R0W=12.8 fm, B2=0.6, B0=118 MeV, W0=1.5 MeV, W1=1.4) and four energy-dependent parameters (B1, VB, W2, VBW) to experimental elastic scattering data from Lin et al. at six energies from 80.6 to 94.0 MeV in the center-of-mass frame. For fusion cross-section analysis, they altered R0W from 12.8 fm to 8.2 fm and W2 from 3.0 to 0.8, then compared theoretical fusion cross sections with experimental data from four independent groups (Back et al., Hinde et al., Rehm et al., and Zhang Huanqiao et al.) over energy ranges from approximately 76 to 174 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb barrier for 19F+208Pb is at approximately Ec.m. = 84 MeV
- Elastic scattering data were analyzed at six center-of-mass energies: 80.6, 83.4, 85.2, 87.9, 89.8, and 94.0 MeV
- The ratio of imaginary to real parts of the optical potential is 0.0127 (1.5 MeV / 118.0 MeV) for all colliding energies
- The radial parameter R0 = 12.5 fm and R0W = 12.8 fm for scattering; R0W was changed to 8.2 fm for fusion analysis
- The two-neutron separation energies (S2n) for 19F, 21F, and 23F are 19.582 MeV, 14.742 MeV, and 12.81 MeV respectively
- The binding energies per nucleon (B.E./A) for 19F, 21F, and 23F are 7.779 MeV, 7.738 MeV, and 7.622 MeV respectively
- The 19F nucleus has static deformations beta2 = 0.44 and beta4 = 0.14

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological optical potential approach previously applied by Mallick et al. to 16O+28Si and 12C+24Mg systems, now applied to the deformed projectile 19F on the doubly magic target 208Pb. The study builds on experimental measurements by Lin et al. for elastic scattering and by Back et al. (1985), Zhang Huanqiao et al. (1990), Rehm et al. (1998), and Hinde et al. (1999) for fusion cross sections. The finding of fusion hindrance for 21F and 23F isotopes parallels observations by Oganessian et al. (1987, 2001) and Satou et al. (2002) regarding the role of nuclear shell structure and valence nucleons in fusion probability near the Coulomb barrier. The work enables future studies of how projectile deformation and neutron separation energy affect fusion dynamics, particularly for systems with charge products ZPZT greater than 1800 where fusion hindrance is typically observed.
