# Astrophysical Reaction Rates as a Challenge for Nuclear Reaction Theory

> The relevant energy ranges for stellar nuclear reactions are introduced. Low-energy compound and direct reactions are discussed. Stellar modifications of the cross sections are presented. Implications for experiments are outlined.

## Key claim

The standard approximation formulas for the Gamow window (the energy range dominating stellar reaction rates) are not always valid because they assume the projectile width is much smaller than the exit-channel width; when this assumption fails, the relevant energy window can shift by several MeV, requiring newly derived ranges for experimental planning.

## Method

The paper analyzes the Hauser-Feshbach model (HFM) and Breit-Wigner formula (BWF) for compound reactions, focusing on how the relative sizes of partial widths (entrance, exit, and total) determine the energy dependence of cross sections and the location of the astrophysically relevant energy window. It introduces sensitivity plots to visualize which width dominates the cross section at different energies, and discusses stellar enhancement factors (SEF) that account for thermal population of excited target states in stellar plasmas. For direct reactions, the paper describes an averaged direct capture (DC) model using energy-dependent spectroscopic factors based on BCS and Lipkin-Nogami occupation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{96}$Ru(p,$\gamma$)$^{97}$Rh at the p-process temperature T=2.5 GK, the Gamow window is 1.63 ≤ E ≤ 3.42 MeV (center-of-mass energy)
- Knowledge of cross sections across the full relevant energy window determines the reaction rate integral to 10%
- Stellar enhancement factors (SEF) for selected ($\gamma$,n) reactions from NON-SMOKER calculations: $^{186}$W: 400, $^{185}$Re: 1300, $^{187}$Re: 1200, $^{190}$Pt: 5500, $^{192}$Pt: 3300, $^{198}$Pt: 310, $^{197}$Au: 1100, $^{196}$Hg: 1700, $^{198}$Hg: 750, $^{204}$Hg: 43, $^{204}$Pb: 160
- For Sn isotopes (except N=92), the sum of modified HFM and DC cross sections approximates the unmodified HFM result within a factor of 10

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of astrophysical reaction rates by Rauscher and collaborators (2000s) that previously derived simple Gamow window approximations, correcting cases where those approximations fail for intermediate and heavy target nuclei. The paper introduces the SMARAGD code as an improved successor to NON-SMOKER$^\mathrm{WEB}$ (version 4.0w), incorporating J-pi dependent weighting to handle low nuclear level densities at compound formation energy. The findings enable more reliable experimental planning by identifying which energy ranges must be measured and which reaction directions (positive Q-value) minimize stellar enhancement factors, particularly for photodisintegration reactions where SEF values can exceed 5000.
