# Combining the in-medium similarity renormalization group with the density matrix renormalization group: Shell structure and information entropy

> We propose a novel many-body framework combining the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) with the valence-space (VS) formulation of the in-medium similarity renormalization group. This hybrid scheme admits for favorable computational scaling in large-space calculations compared to direct diagonalization. The capacity of the VS-DMRG approach is highlighted in ab initio calculations of neutron-rich nickel isotopes based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, and allows us to perform converged ab initio computations of ground and excited state energies. We also study orbital entanglement in the VS-DMRG, and investigate nuclear correlation effects in oxygen, neon, and magnesium isotopes. The explored entanglement measures reveal nuclear shell closures as well as pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid many-body framework combining the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) with the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) that achieves favorable computational scaling compared to direct diagonalization, but the method's accuracy depends on the bond dimension truncation and the VS-IMSRG truncation of three-body operators at the normal-ordered two-body level.

## Method

The authors first use the VS-IMSRG to decouple a valence-space Hamiltonian from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (the 1.8/2.0 NN+3N Hamiltonian) in a single-particle space of 15 major harmonic-oscillator shells with E3max = 16. This valence-space Hamiltonian is then used as input for large-scale DMRG calculations employing a matrix product state (MPS) parametrization, where nuclear orbitals are mapped onto a one-dimensional chain using two-orbital mutual information to minimize long-range correlations. The DMRG algorithm iteratively optimizes the MPS matrices through forward and backward sweeps, keeping only matrix components corresponding to the highest Schmidt weights up to a maximum bond dimension M.

## Key numbers

- For Ni-78, the full configuration interaction (FCI) dimension is 2.3 x 10^11, while the largest CI calculations involved 1.9 x 10^9 configurations with a truncation at Tmax = 7 particle-hole excitations
- The DMRG space dimension for Ni-78 at bond dimension M = 10240 is approximately 10^7, two orders of magnitude below the largest accessible CI dimension
- VS-DMRG extrapolated 2+ excitation energy for Ni-78: 3.007 +/- 0.017 MeV, compared to CI extrapolation of 3.141 +/- 0.205 MeV
- Experimental 2+ excitation energy for Ni-78 is 2.6 MeV
- For O-24, the ground state is dominated by the reference state (approximately 92%) with admixtures from 2-particle-2-hole excitations (approximately 7%)
- For Ne-26 and Mg-28, the ground states show enhanced mixing from 3-particle-3-hole excitations (10% and 17% respectively) and 4-particle-4-hole excitations (12% and 15% respectively)

## Context

This work extends the application of DMRG, well-established in condensed matter physics and quantum chemistry (White 1992, Schollwoeck 2011), to ab initio nuclear structure calculations of medium-mass nuclei for the first time. Previous nuclear DMRG studies focused on phenomenological shell-model applications (Papenbrock 2005, Pittel 2006, Legeza 2015) and open quantum systems using a Gamow basis (Papadimitriou 2013), while the VS-IMSRG approach for decoupling valence-space Hamiltonians was developed by Stroberg et al. (2017, 2019). The VS-DMRG enables converged calculations of neutron-rich nickel isotopes and provides entanglement-based measures that reveal nuclear shell closures and pairing correlations, with future applications possible for deformed nuclei requiring multi-shell decoupling (Miyagi 2020) and for other observables such as radii or electroweak transitions.
