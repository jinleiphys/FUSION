# Peripheral transverse densities of the baryon octet from chiral effective field theory and dispersion analysis

> The baryon electromagnetic form factors are expressed in terms of two-dimensional densities describing the distribution of charge and magnetization in transverse space at fixed light-front time. We calculate the transverse densities of the spin-1/2 flavor-octet baryons at peripheral distances b = O(M_π^{-1}) using methods of relativistic chiral effective field theory (χEFT) and dispersion analysis. The densities are represented as dispersive integrals over the imaginary parts of the form factors in the timelike region (spectral functions). The isovector spectral functions on the two-pion cut t > 4 M_π^2 are calculated using relativistic χEFT including octet and decuplet baryons. The χEFT calculations are extended into the ρmeson mass region using an N/D method that incorporates the pion electromagnetic form factor data. The isoscalar spectral functions are modeled by vector meson poles. We compute the peripheral charge and magnetization densities in the octet baryon states, estimate the uncertainties, and determine the quark flavor decomposition. The approach can be extended to baryon form factors of other operators and the moments of generalized parton distributions.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities of all spin-1/2 flavor-octet baryons (nucleon, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) at distances b ~ 1 fm and larger, using chiral effective field theory combined with dispersion analysis, with the hedge that the isovector spectral functions are computed up to t ~ 0.3 GeV^2 with an estimated uncertainty less than 30%, and up to t ~ 1 GeV^2 with somewhat larger uncertainty.

## Method
The authors compute the isovector spectral functions on the two-pion cut (t > 4 M_pi^2) using relativistic SU(3) chiral effective field theory at O(epsilon^3) in the small-scale expansion, including octet and decuplet baryons. These calculations are extended into the rho meson mass region using an N/D method that incorporates the empirical pion electromagnetic form factor data from e+e- annihilation. The isoscalar spectral functions are modeled by vector meson poles (omega, phi) using SU(3) symmetry. The transverse densities are then obtained by dispersive integrals of the spectral functions with Bessel function weights.

## Key numbers
- The two-pion threshold is at t = 4 M_pi^2, with M_pi the pion mass
- The rho meson resonance appears at t ~ 30 M_pi^2 = 0.6 GeV^2
- The isovector spectral functions are computed up to t ~ 0.3 GeV^2 with estimated uncertainty < 30%, and up to t ~ 1 GeV^2 with larger uncertainty
- At b = 3 fm, the dominant contribution to the dispersive integrals comes from the near-threshold region t < 0.3 GeV^2
- At b = 1 fm, the vector meson mass region gives the main contribution to the integrals
- The method allows computation of isovector transverse densities at distances b >= 1 fm with controlled accuracy
- The densities decay exponentially at large b with a range of order 1/(2 M_pi)

## Context
This work extends the previous study of nucleon peripheral transverse densities by Granados et al. (2013) from the nucleon to the full baryon octet, using SU(3) chiral effective field theory with consistent treatment of decuplet baryons via the extended-on-mass-shell scheme. The approach goes beyond earlier calculations by combining chiral EFT with a dispersive technique based on elastic unitarity, enabling controlled calculations down to b ~ 1 fm. The results enable future studies of baryon form factors for other operators and moments of generalized parton distributions, and can be extended to spectral functions of other form factors with two-pion cuts.
