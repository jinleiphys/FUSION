# Particle Interferometry in a Moat Regime

> Dense strongly interacting matter can exhibit regimes with spatial modulations, akin to crystalline phases. In this case particles can have a moat spectrum with minimal energy at nonzero momentum. We show that particle interferometry is a sensitive probe of such a regime in heavy-ion collisions. To this end, we develop a field-theoretical formalism that relates particle spectra to in-medium real-time correlation functions of quantum fields on curved hypersurfaces of spacetime. This is then applied to the study of Bose-Einstein correlations in a moat regime in heavy-ion collisions. The resulting two-particle spectra exhibit peaks at nonzero average pair momentum, in contrast to the two-particle spectra in a normal phase, which peak at zero momentum. These peaks lead to non-trivial structures in the ratio of two-particle correlation functions, which should be experimentally measurable if the resolution in the direction of average pair momentum is sufficiently large. We propose these structures in the correlation-function ratios as clear signature of a moat regime and spatially modulated phases in quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

## Key claim

Particle interferometry (Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations) can detect a moat regime in heavy-ion collisions through peaks in ratios of two-particle correlation functions at nonzero average pair momentum, provided the experimental resolution in the direction of average pair momentum is sufficiently large; these peaks reflect the wave number of the underlying spatial modulation and serve as a clear signature of spatially modulated phases in QCD.

## Method

The authors develop a field-theoretical formalism that relates particle spectra to in-medium real-time correlation functions of quantum fields on curved hypersurfaces of spacetime, using quantization of quasiparticles in curved spacetime and a Gaussian approximation. They apply this formalism to compute Bose-Einstein correlations (HBT interferometry) for pions in a moat regime, modeling the energy spectrum with a low-momentum expansion E_p = sqrt(z p^2 + w p^4 + m^2) where z < 1 and w > 0, and numerically analyze the resulting single- and two-particle spectra and correlation ratios.

## Key numbers

* The moat regime is expected at intermediate baryon chemical potential with mu_B/T greater than or approximately 4, based on exploratory QCD results from Fu (2019).
* Additional peaks in correlation-function ratios appear around average pair momentum P approximately 100 MeV, reflecting the wave number of the underlying spatial modulation.
* The normal phase uses z = 1 and w = 0 in the energy dispersion, while the moat regime uses z < 1 and w > 0 with minimal energy at nonzero momentum p_min not equal to 0.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Pisarski (2020, 2021) that a moat regime could be detected in heavy-ion collisions, and provides the first microscopic derivation of particle spectra on curved hypersurfaces incorporating in-medium modifications. Prior theoretical evidence for a moat regime in QCD was found by Fu (2019) using functional continuum methods. The formalism enables systematic study of particle correlations in the intermediate density region accessible to experiments such as STAR at RHIC, HADES at GSI, CBM at FAIR, and NA61/SHINE at CERN, and could be applied to detect spatially modulated phases including crystalline phases, liquid-crystal like phases, or a quantum pion liquid.
