# Investigations of decuplet baryons from meson-baryon interactions in the HAL QCD method

> We study decuplet baryons from meson-baryon interactions, in particular, $Δ$ and $Ω$ baryons from P-wave $I=3/2$ $Nπ$ and $I=0$ $Ξ\bar{K}$ interactions, respectively. The interaction potentials are calculated in the HAL QCD method using 3-quark-type source operators at $m_π \approx 410~\textrm{MeV}$. We use the conventional stochastic estimation of all-to-all propagators combined with the all-mode averaging to reduce statistical fluctuations. We have found that two potentials have quite similar behaviors, suggesting that a mass difference between $Δ$ and $Ω$ comes mainly from a difference of kinematical structure between $Nπ$ and $Ξ\bar K$, rather than their interactions. The scattering phase shifts calculated from the potentials indicate that $Δ$ and $Ω$ baryons exist as bound states in this lattice setup, whose binding energies are consistent with those obtained from 2-point functions.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that the mass difference between the Δ and Ω baryons arises primarily from the different kinematical structure of their respective meson-baryon systems (Nπ vs. ΞK̅), rather than from differences in their strong interactions, based on the observation that the two interaction potentials are quite similar. This conclusion is hedged by the lattice setup at mπ ≈ 410 MeV, where the Δ baryon exists as a bound state rather than a resonance.

## Method
The authors employed the time-dependent HAL QCD method to extract leading-order (LO) meson-baryon interaction potentials from NBS wave functions, using 3-quark-type source operators for Δ and Ω baryons on (2+1)-flavor gauge configurations. They computed 4-point correlation functions for P-wave I=3/2 Nπ and I=0 ΞK̅ systems, using stochastic estimation of all-to-all propagators with dilution and all-mode averaging to reduce statistical fluctuations. The Schrödinger equation was solved with fitted potentials (three-Gaussian forms) to obtain scattering phase shifts and binding energies via the Gaussian Expansion Method.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 410 MeV (specifically 411.2(1.7) MeV from 2-point function fit)
- Δ baryon mass: 1522.9(7.8) MeV; Ω baryon mass: 1847.0(6.5) MeV
- Nπ binding energy from potential: 82.6(8.2)(+90.1/-38.4) MeV
- ΞK̅ binding energy from potential: 193.9(3.2)(+163.8/-11.4) MeV
- Nπ binding energy from 2-point function: mN + mπ - mΔ = 105.5(5.2) MeV
- ΞK̅ binding energy from 2-point function: mΞ + mK̅ - mΩ = 293.5(2.8) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.09 fm on a 32³ × 64 lattice

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009, Ishii 2012) to meson-baryon systems with decuplet baryons, specifically the Δ and Ω, using 3-quark-type source operators. It builds on prior lattice QCD studies of hadron interactions by the HAL QCD Collaboration and uses gauge configurations from the PACS-CS Collaboration (2008). The results enable future studies of the Δ baryon as a resonance at lighter quark masses, where the Nπ system would become a scattering state, and provide a foundation for investigating exotic hadrons such as pentaquarks.
