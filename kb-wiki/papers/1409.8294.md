# Heavy Dark Matter Annihilation from Effective Field Theory

> We formulate an effective field theory description for SU(2)$_L$ triplet fermionic dark matter by combining nonrelativistic dark matter with gauge bosons in the soft-collinear effective theory. For a given dark matter mass, the annihilation cross section to line photons is obtained with 5% precision by simultaneously including Sommerfeld enhancement and the resummation of electroweak Sudakov logarithms at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic order. Using these results, we present more accurate and precise predictions for the gamma-ray line signal from annihilation, updating both existing constraints and the reach of future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for SU(2)_L triplet fermionic dark matter (wino-like), the annihilation cross section to line photons can be computed with 5% precision by simultaneously including Sommerfeld enhancement and resummation of electroweak Sudakov logarithms at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order, but this precision claim applies only for a given dark matter mass and the theoretical uncertainty bands are derived from scale variation.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective field theory (NRDM-SCET) combining nonrelativistic dark matter with soft-collinear effective theory for gauge bosons, matching at a high scale mu ~ 2 m_chi onto two spin-singlet S-wave operators. They compute the anomalous dimension matrix for these operators, run the Wilson coefficients from the high scale down to mu ~ m_Z using renormalization group evolution at NLL order, and separately solve the Schroedinger equation numerically to obtain Sommerfeld enhancement factors s_00 and s_0±. The final annihilation cross section for chi0 chi0 -> gamma gamma, gamma Z is obtained by combining the resummed Sudakov form factors Sigma_1 and Sigma_2 with the Sommerfeld coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The annihilation cross section for a given dark matter mass is obtained with 5% precision (stated in abstract and text as "~5% theoretical uncertainty" for the NLL+SE result at high masses)
- The mass splitting between chi0 and chi± is taken as delta = 0.17 GeV for the Sommerfeld calculation
- H.E.S.S already constrains wino DM for masses below ~4 TeV (for an NFW profile with local DM density 0.4 GeV/cm^3)
- Five hours of observation with CTA could extend this bound to ~10 TeV
- The cusp anomalous dimensions used are Gamma_0^g = 8 and Gamma_1^g = 8(70/9 - 2 pi^2/3) for SU(2) with C_A = 2
- The one-loop beta-function coefficient is b_0 = 19/6 and two-loop is b_1 = -35/6

## Context

This work extends the study of electroweak corrections to heavy dark matter annihilation beyond the one-loop fixed-order calculation of Hryczuk (2011), which found O(1) corrections changing the cross section by a factor of a few, and beyond the tree-level-plus-Sommerfeld-enhancement approximation used in earlier phenomenological studies such as Cohen (2013). The method enables systematically improvable effective field theory predictions for dark matter annihilation, allowing precision constraints to be placed on wino DM models. Two complementary papers appeared concurrently: Baumgart (2014) computing semi-inclusive cross-sections at LL, and Bauer, Cohen, Hill, Solon investigating exclusive line annihilation for scalar DM up to NLL.
