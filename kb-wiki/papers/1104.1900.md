# The critical indices of the Quark-Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model with tricritical endpoint

> The critical indices α', β, γ' and δof the Quark Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model with the tricritical endpoint are calculated as functions of the usual parameters of this model and two newly introduces parameters (indices). They are compared with the critical exponents of other models. It is shown that for the newly introduced indices χ= 0 and ξ^T < 1 there is a branch of solutions for which the critical exponents of the present model and the statistical multifragmentation model coincide, otherwise these models belong to different universality classes. It is shown that for realistic values of the parameter \varkappa the critical exponents α', β, γ' and δof simple liquids and 3-dimensional Ising model can be only described by the branch of solutions in which all indices except for α' correspond to their values within the statistical multifragmentation model. The scaling relations for the found critical exponents are verified and it is demonstrated that for the standard definition of the index α' the Fisher and Griffiths scaling inequalities are not fulfilled for some values of the model parameters, whereas the Liberman scaling inequality is always obeyed. Although it is shown that the specially defined index α'_s recovers the scaling relations, another possibility, an existence of the non-Fisher universality classes, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The critical indices of the Quark-Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model (QGBSTM) with a tricritical endpoint are calculated as functions of the model's usual parameters and two newly introduced indices, χ and ξ^T; the paper shows that for χ = 0 and ξ^T ≤ 1 the critical exponents coincide with those of the statistical multifragmentation model, but otherwise the models belong to different universality classes, and that for realistic values of the parameter ϰ the exponents of simple liquids and the 3-dimensional Ising model can only be described by the branch where all indices except α' match the statistical multifragmentation model.

## Method

The author analytically calculates the critical exponents α', β, γ', and δ of the QGBSTM near its tricritical endpoint by analyzing the behavior of the system's pressure, baryonic density, entropy density, and isothermal compressibility along the phase coexistence curve and critical isotherm. The calculations use a parameterization of the surface tension coefficient Σ(T,μ) and the phase coexistence curves T_c(μ) and T_Σ(μ) in terms of the new indices ξ^T and χ, and evaluate the singular behavior of integrals I_τ(0,Σ) and I_τ(Δs,Σ) in the limits Σ→+0 and Δs→+0. The results are compared with the critical exponents of the Fisher droplet model, the statistical multifragmentation model, the 2-dimensional and 3-dimensional Ising models, and simple liquids.

## Key numbers

- For the 2-dimensional Ising model, the QGBSTM reproduces α' = 0, β = 1/8, γ' = 7/4, and δ = 15 with parameters χ = 0, ξ^T between 8/15 and 1, τ = 31/16, and ϰ = 1/2 when ζ = 1.
- For simple liquids, the critical exponents are α' = 0.09–0.11, β = 0.32–0.35, γ' = 1.2–1.3, and δ = 4.2–4.8; the QGBSTM reproduces these with χ = 0, τ ≈ 1.826 ± 0.02, and ϰ > 0.49.
- For the 3-dimensional Ising model, the critical exponents are α' = 0.1096 ± 0.0005, β = 0.3265 ± 0.0001, γ' = 1.2373 ± 0.0002, and δ = 4.7893 ± 0.0008; the QGBSTM reproduces these with χ = 0 and τ ≈ 1.826 ± 0.02.
- The critical exponent α' is given by α' = 2 − 2 min(1, 1/ξ^T), so α' > 0 only for ξ^T > 1.
- The index β is given by β = (ζ/ϰ)(2 − τ) plus a term depending on χ and ξ^T.
- The index γ' is given by γ' = ζ/ϰ − 2β.
- The index δ for χ = 0 is δ = (τ − 1)/(2 − τ) when ξ^T ζ / max(τ − 1, ϰ) ≥ 1/(τ − 1).

## Context

This work extends the Quark-Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model (QGBSTM) developed by Bugaev (2007) and Bugaev (2007) by calculating its critical exponents at the tricritical endpoint, introducing two new indices (χ and ξ^T) that were not considered in the Fisher droplet model (Fisher, 1967) or the statistical multifragmentation model (Bondorf; Bugaev, 2000). The results enable a comparison with the universality class of the 3-dimensional Ising model, which is expected to match the QCD tricritical endpoint, and suggest that the volume distribution of large QGP bags at the tricritical endpoint follows a power law v^(−τ) with τ ≈ 1.826 ± 0.02, as also discussed in String (2011). The paper also verifies scaling relations and finds that the Fisher and Griffiths scaling inequalities are not fulfilled for some parameter values, while the Liberman scaling inequality is always obeyed, raising the possibility of non-Fisher universality classes.
