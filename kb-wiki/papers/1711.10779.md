# Overlap between Lattice QCD and HRG with in-medium effects and parity doubling

> We investigate the fluctuations and correlations involving baryon number in hot hadronic matter with modified masses of negative-parity baryons, in the context of the hadron resonance gas. Temperature-dependent masses are adopted from the recent lattice QCD results and from a chiral effective model which implements the parity doubling structure with respect to the chiral symmetry. Confronting the baryon number susceptibility, baryon-charge correlation, and baryon-strangeness correlation and their ratios with the lattice QCD data, we show that the strong downward mass shift in hyperons can accidentally reproduce some correlation ratios, however it also tends to overshoot the individual fluctuations and correlations. This indicates, that in order to correctly account for the influence of the chiral symmetry restoration on the fluctuation observables, a consistent framework of in-medium effects beyond hadron mass shifts is required.

# Overlap between Lattice QCD and HRG with in-medium effects and parity doubling

## Key claim

The strong downward mass shift in hyperons, as observed in lattice QCD, can accidentally reproduce some correlation ratios (such as χ₁₁^{BQ}/χ₂^{B} and -χ₁₁^{BQ}/χ₁₁^{BS}) when inserted into the hadron resonance gas model, but it simultaneously overshoots the individual fluctuations and correlations (χ₂^{B}, χ₁₁^{BQ}, χ₁₁^{BS}) compared to lattice QCD data, indicating that a consistent framework of in-medium effects beyond hadron mass shifts is required to account for chiral symmetry restoration effects on fluctuation observables.

## Method

The authors employed the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with zero-width approximation, incorporating temperature-dependent masses for negative-parity octet and decuplet baryons from two sources: a lattice-motivated parameterization using data from the FASTSUM collaboration (with a tanh function fit to masses at T/Tc = 0.24, 0.76, 0.84, and 0.95) and a chiral effective model with parity doubling structure. They computed the baryon number susceptibility χ₂^{B}, baryon-charge correlation χ₁₁^{BQ}, and baryon-strangeness correlation χ₁₁^{BS} at vanishing chemical potentials, comparing results from three different assignments (A, B, C) of negative-parity states to PDG particles against lattice QCD data from the HotQCD and Budapest-Wuppertal collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature from lattice QCD is T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV (physical) and T_c = 185 MeV (for the FASTSUM lattice with m_π = 384 MeV)
- Negative-parity nucleon mass at T = 0.24 T_c: 1779 MeV (lattice), dropping to 1254 MeV at T_c (parameterized)
- Negative-parity Λ mass at T = 0.24 T_c: 1899 MeV (lattice), dropping to 1172 MeV at T_c
- Negative-parity Ω mass at T = 0.24 T_c: 2193 MeV (lattice), dropping to 1383 MeV at T_c
- The parameter b_i controlling the crossover width ranges from 0.213 (Ω) to 0.369 (Λ) across the negative-parity states
- Positive-parity nucleon mass on the lattice: m_{N+} = 1158 MeV (compared to 939 MeV in PDG)
- Positive-parity Ω mass on the lattice: m_{Ω+} = 1661 MeV (compared to 1672 MeV in PDG)

## Context

This work extends the conventional HRG model, which successfully describes the equation of state and fluctuations below the chiral crossover temperature (Karsch 2011, Bazavov 2012), by incorporating in-medium mass modifications of parity-doubled baryons motivated by recent lattice QCD results from the FASTSUM collaboration (Aarts 2017). The study contradicts the suggestion that parity doubling alone can explain the missing contributions in baryon-charge and baryon-strangeness correlations (Aarts SQM2017). The results indicate that proper inclusion of resonance widths, as previously examined for K-π (Friman 2015) and π-N-Δ (Lo 2017) systems using S-matrix formalism, may be necessary alongside mass shifts to correctly describe fluctuation observables in the presence of chiral symmetry restoration.
