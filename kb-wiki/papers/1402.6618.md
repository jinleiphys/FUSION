# Constraining neutron star matter with QCD

> In recent years, there have been several successful attempts to constrain the equation of state of neutron star matter using input from low-energy nuclear physics and observational data. We demonstrate that significant further restrictions can be placed by additionally requiring the pressure to approach that of deconfined quark matter at high densities. Remarkably, the new constraints turn out to be highly insensitive to the amount --- or even presence --- of quark matter inside the stars.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that requiring the neutron star equation of state (EoS) to approach the perturbative QCD prediction for deconfined quark matter at high densities significantly narrows the allowed EoS band, even at low densities and for stars containing no quark matter. The constraints are highly insensitive to the amount or presence of quark matter inside the stars.

## Method
The authors interpolate between a low-density chiral effective field theory (EFT) EoS from Tews (2012) for densities below 1.1 times nuclear saturation density and a high-density perturbative QCD (pQCD) EoS from Fraga (2013) for baryon chemical potentials above 2.6 GeV. The interpolation uses two smoothly matched polytropes of the form P(n) = κ n^γ, with polytropic indices γ1 and γ2 varied over ranges limited only by causality and the requirement of matching to both the low- and high-density limits. The resulting EoS band is further constrained by requiring that the EoS support a two solar mass neutron star.

## Key numbers
- The low-density chiral EFT EoS has an uncertainty of ±24% at n = 1.1 n0, with pressure values of 3.542 MeV/fm³ (soft) and 2.163 MeV/fm³ (stiff) at that density.
- The high-density pQCD EoS is matched at μB = 2.6 GeV, with the renormalization scale parameter X = 3Λ̄/μB varied from 1 to 4.
- The polytropic indices for the two monotropes vary over γ1 ∈ [2.23, 9.2] and γ2 ∈ [1.0, 1.5], with the matching chemical potential μc between 1.08 and 2.05 GeV.
- With the two solar mass constraint, the relative uncertainty in the EoS is reduced to less than ±30% at all densities.
- Maximal star masses fall in the interval Mmax ∈ [1.4, 2.5] M⊙ for bitropic interpolation, with no configurations above 2.5 M⊙.
- For 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars, radii range between 11 and 14.5 km; for 2 M⊙ pulsars, radii range between 10 and 15 km.
- Reaching a two solar mass star requires γ1 > 2.86; reaching 2.4 M⊙ requires γ1 > 3.5.
- The maximal speed of sound squared must satisfy cs² > 0.55 to fulfill the 2 M⊙ constraint.
- Maximal central densities for stars satisfying the mass constraint are n < 8.0 n0, with maximal chemical potentials μcenter < 1.77 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Hebeler (2013), who combined low-energy chiral EFT with the two solar mass constraint but applied no microphysical constraints beyond nuclear saturation density. The key advance is the addition of the high-density pQCD constraint, which automatically restricts the polytropic parameters without the ad hoc constraints needed in Hebeler (2013). The results enable tighter predictions for neutron star mass-radius relations and provide an a posteriori justification for many choices made in earlier work. The ease of matching nuclear and quark matter EoSs contrasts with earlier attempts by Fraga (2001) and Alford (2004).
