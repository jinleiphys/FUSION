# Positivity Constraints for off-Forward Parton Distributions

> Off-Forward Parton Distributions (OFPD's) are new hadronic objects which may be measured in various exclusive reactions. We derive non-trivial positivity constraints for them that should allow to get extra restrictions for model inputs.

## Key claim

The paper derives non-trivial positivity constraints for Off-Forward Parton Distributions (OFPDs) based on the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality applied to the density matrix, providing rigorous bounds that should help restrict model inputs for these distributions, which lack a direct probabilistic interpretation.

## Method

The authors express forward and non-forward parton distributions as sums over intermediate states using light-cone decompositions of quark and gluon fields, then apply the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality to the matrix elements. By introducing a positive parameter and minimizing the right-hand side, they obtain optimized inequalities for both spinor and scalar quarks, as well as for gluons, and extend the method to include spin degrees of freedom and combined non-diagonality in helicity and momentum.

## Key numbers

- For spinor quarks: $|q_\zeta (x)| \le \sqrt{ q (x_1) q (x_2) / (1-\zeta) }$, where $x_1 = x$ and $x_2 = x'/(1-x+x')$
- For Ji's off-forward quark distribution: $|H_q(x, \xi)| \le \sqrt{ q (x_1) q (x_2) / (1-\xi^2) }$, with $x_{1,2}=(x \pm \xi)/(1 + \xi)$ and $\xi=\zeta/(2-\zeta)$
- For gluons: $|x^{'} g(x_1,x_2)| \le \sqrt {x_1 x_2 g(x_1) g(x_2)}$
- For the ratio $R = x'g(x,x')/xg(x)$: $R \le \sqrt{ x'/(1-x+x') g(x'/(1-x+x')) / [x g(x)] } \approx \sqrt{ x^{'} g(x^{'}) / [x g(x)] }$
- Using parametrization $g(x)= Nx^{-\alpha} (1-x)^\beta$, the growth of $R$ for small $x,x^{'}$ is bounded as $R \le (x / x^{'})^{(\alpha-1)/2}$

## Context

This work extends the Soffer bound (Soffer, 1995) on the chiral-odd quark distribution $h_1^q(x)$ to the non-forward case, and improves upon an earlier inequality for gluon OFPDs derived by Martin and Ryskin (1998). The constraints are relevant for model building and rate estimates for proposed electroproduction experiments at CEBAF, CERN, and DESY, particularly for deeply virtual Compton scattering and diffractive electroproduction of mesons.
