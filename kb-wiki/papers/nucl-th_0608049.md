# Alpha decay rate enhancement in metals: An unlikely scenario

> It has been recently suggested that one might drastically shorten the alpha lifetime of nuclear waste products, if these are embedded in metals at low temperatures. Using quantum mechanical tunneling arguments, we show that such an effect is likely to be very small, if present at all.

# Alpha decay rate enhancement in metals: An unlikely scenario

## Key claim
Using standard quantum mechanical tunneling arguments and screening models (Thomas-Fermi and Debye), the paper shows that any enhancement of alpha decay rates for nuclei embedded in metals at low temperatures is likely to be very small, if present at all. The claimed drastic shortening of alpha lifetimes for nuclear waste products is not supported when the in-medium modification of the decay energy is properly accounted for.

## Method
The author calculates the ratio of screened to unscreened alpha-decay half-lives by numerically evaluating WKB action integrals for the barrier penetration probability, using a Yukawa-type effective potential to model in-medium screening. The screening potential is treated as constant over nuclear dimensions, with the screening energy shift ΔU set equal to the screening potential at zero radius. Calculations are performed for the Thomas-Fermi model (appropriate for degenerate electrons in metals) and the Debye-Hückel model, for the specific nuclei ²¹⁰Po and ²²⁶Ra.

## Key numbers
- For ²¹⁰Po (alpha-decay energy 5.30 MeV) in a metal: half-life ratio ℛ = 1.009 (Thomas-Fermi model with screening radius R_TF = 3.7×10³ fm) and ℛ = 1.000 (Debye model at 4 K with R_D = 4×10⁵ fm)
- For ²²⁶Ra (alpha-decay energy 4.78 MeV) in a metal: ℛ = 1.013 (Thomas-Fermi) and ℛ = 1.000 (Debye)
- Thomas-Fermi screening radius for typical metals: R_TF = 3.7×10³ fm (using ρ ~ 10×10²² cm⁻³ and E_F ~ 10 eV)
- Debye screening radius at 4 K: R_D = 2.18×10⁻⁸ √(T[K]) cm, yielding approximately 4×10⁵ fm
- For the screening radius R = 562 fm (corresponding to ΔU = 420 keV estimated by Kettner et al.), the ²¹⁰Po lifetime is enhanced by 45% (ℛ = 1.45)
- Table of half-life ratios for Z=90, A=232 shows ℛ ranges from 1.000 (for R = 1.0×10⁶ fm, E = 5.0 MeV) to 10.95 (for R = 5.0×10² fm, E = 3.0 MeV)

## Context
This work directly contradicts the claims of Kettner et al. (2006) and Liolios (2006) that alpha-decay lifetimes can be significantly reduced by embedding nuclei in metals at low temperatures, and instead agrees with the earlier findings of Rubinson (1972) and Emery (1972) that changes due to electronic screening are negligibly small. The paper clarifies that the in-medium screening effect for alpha decay is fundamentally different from the well-known screening enhancement of low-energy fusion reactions studied by Salpeter (1954), Assenbaum (1987), and Raiola et al. (2002-2005), because the decay energy shift cancels the barrier reduction in first order. The results imply that the proposed application for nuclear waste disposal via metal embedding is not viable based on standard screening models.
