# Momentum Dependence of Single Particle Potential in Dirac Brueckner Approach

> The momentum dependence of the empirical scalar and vector potentials needed for describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon is compared with that derived from self-consistent Dirac-Brueckner calculations using the Bonn-A potential. Our calculated scalar and vector potentials exhibit a weak momentum dependence and their magnitudes decrease with nucleon momentum, in close similarity with the momentum dependence required by the empirical potentials. The effects of explicit momentum dependence on the properties of equilibrium nuclear matter are found to be small.

# Momentum Dependence of Single Particle Potential in Dirac Brueckner Approach

## Key claim
The scalar and vector potentials derived from self-consistent Dirac-Brueckner calculations using the Bonn-A potential exhibit a weak momentum dependence that decreases in magnitude with nucleon momentum, closely matching the momentum dependence required by empirical potentials for describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon. However, the explicit momentum dependence has only a small effect on the properties of equilibrium nuclear matter, with the binding energy decreasing by about 0.2 MeV when it is included.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Dirac-Brueckner (DBHF) calculations using the Bonn-A potential, solving the in-medium Thompson equation for the $\tilde G$ matrix and computing the single-particle potential $\Sigma_{DB}(k)$. The scalar and vector potentials $U_S(k)$ and $U_V(k)$ were parameterized as functions of momentum using the form $U_{S,V}(k) = (U_{S,V})_0 / (1 + \alpha_{S,V} (k/k_F)^{\beta_{S,V}})$ with $\beta_{S,V} \approx 0.5$, and the four remaining parameters were determined by minimizing $\chi^2$ against the DBHF single-particle potential iteratively until self-consistency was achieved.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density in both momentum-dependent and momentum-independent calculations is approximately $0.18 \;{\rm fm}^{-3}$.
- The binding energy decreases by about 0.2 MeV when explicit momentum dependence is included in the scalar and vector potentials.
- The self-consistent DBHF results for the momentum dependence of scalar and vector potentials at normal nuclear matter density are very close to those of ter Haar and Malfliet (1987), while the results of Sehn et al. (1997) show a much stronger momentum dependence due to lack of self-consistency.

## Context
This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner approach of Machleidt (1989) by incorporating explicit momentum dependence in the scalar and vector potentials, which had previously been assumed momentum independent in most DBHF calculations. The results are compared with the Dirac phenomenology analysis of Cooper et al. (1993) and with earlier momentum-dependence studies by ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) and Sehn et al. (1997). The momentum-dependent potentials are intended for use in relativistic transport models such as RBUU and RQMD for heavy-ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon, building on the phenomenological momentum-dependent parameterization introduced by Li et al. (1997) for nucleon flow calculations.
