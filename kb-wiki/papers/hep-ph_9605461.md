# When Is A Semiclassical Approximation Self-consistent?

> A general condition for the self-consistency of a semiclassical approximation to a given system is suggested. It is based on the eigenvalue distribution of the relevant Hessian evaluated at the streamline configurations (configurations that almost satisfy the classical equations of motion). The semiclassical approximation is consistent when there exists a gap that separates small and large eigenvalues and the spreading among the small eigenvalues is much smaller than the gap. The idea is illustrated in the case of the double-well potential problem in quantum mechanics. The feasibility of the present idea to test instanton models of QCD vacuum is also briefly discussed.

# When Is A Semiclassical Approximation Self-consistent?

## Key claim
The self-consistency of a semiclassical approximation (saddle-point expansion around instantons) can be diagnosed by examining the eigenvalue distribution of the Hessian evaluated at streamline configurations; the approximation is consistent only when there exists a gap separating small from large eigenvalues **and** the spreading among the small eigenvalues is much smaller than that gap, and this information must be correlated with the behavior of the physical observable under cooling.

## Method
The author generates thermalized configurations for the one-dimensional double-well potential using the Metropolis algorithm with embedded cluster updating, then obtains streamline configurations by locally minimizing the action (cooling). The eigenvalue spectrum of the Hessian (second derivative of the action) is computed at these streamline configurations. The energy splitting between ground and first excited state is extracted from the exponential decay of the two-point correlation function at large separation, and this observable is monitored as a function of cooling time alongside the Hessian eigenvalue distribution.

## Key numbers
- For the weak coupling case (g=0.20, a=0.4, L=200): uncooled energy splitting ΔE⁽⁰⁾=0.060(2); after 50 cooling sweeps ΔE⁽⁵⁰⁾=0.057(2); exact value ΔE⁽ᵉˣᵃᶜᵗ⁾=0.0609. The Hessian shows a gap between eigenvalues near zero and those near λ=0.35, with small-eigenvalue spreading less than 0.1.
- For the strong coupling case (g=0.50, a=0.2, L=40): uncooled ΔE⁽⁰⁾=0.643(10); after 50 cooling sweeps ΔE⁽⁵⁰⁾=0.43(2); exact value ΔE⁽ᵉˣᵃᶜᵗ⁾=0.6374. The Hessian shows no gap and large spreading of the would-be zero modes.
- For the intermediate coupling case (g=0.35, a=0.2, L=40): uncooled ΔE⁽⁰⁾=0.399(7); after 50 cooling sweeps ΔE⁽⁵⁰⁾=0.33(2); exact value ΔE⁽ᵉˣᵃᶜᵗ⁾=0.3870.
- For an idealized instanton-anti-instanton pair with separation ~6: the two lowest eigenvalues are λ⁺=-0.025041 and λ⁻=0.004947; higher eigenvalues start at 0.308339; total action 1.9683 S₀ (where S₀=1/(6g²)).
- For a closely overlapping pair (separation comparable to instanton radius): lowest four eigenvalues are -0.137194, 0.116332, 0.379692, 0.501244; total action 1.4628 S₀.
- The perturbative result for ΔE(g) up to three loops is ΔE(g) = (2/√(πg²)) exp(-1/(6g²)) {1 - (71/12)g² - (315/8)g⁴ + O(g⁶)}; this deteriorates at g>0.25.

## Context
This work extends the streamline concept introduced by the author (1994) and the instanton-anti-instanton pair analysis of the flucton (1994). It provides a practical numerical criterion to test whether the semiclassical instanton picture is self-consistent, directly addressing the scale-invariance problem in theories like the 2D O(3) non-linear sigma-model (Jevicki, 1977) and 4D SU(N) Yang-Mills theory ('t Hooft, 1976). The method is intended to test instanton models of the QCD vacuum, where phenomenological estimates give average instanton size ~0.3 fm and inter-instanton distance ~1.0 fm, and to determine whether the semiclassical expansion around these configurations is reliable.
