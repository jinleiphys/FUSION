# Lambda(1405) as a multiquark state

> In the QCD sum rule approach we predict the $Λ$ (1405) mass by choosing the $π^0Σ^0$ multiquark interpolating field. It is found that the mass is about 1.419 GeV from $Π_1 (q^2)$ sum rule which is more reliable than $Π_q (q^2)$ sum rule, where $Π_q (q^2)$ and $Π_1 (q^2)$ are two invariant functions of the correlator $Π(q^2)$. We also present the sum rules for the $K^+ p$ and the $π^+Σ^+$ multiquark states, and compare to those for the $π^0Σ^0$ multiquark state. The mass of the $Λ$ (1600) can be also reproduced in our approach.

## Key claim

Using a QCD sum rule approach with a $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ multiquark interpolating field, the mass of the $\Lambda$(1405) is predicted to be about 1.419 GeV from the $\Pi_1 (q^2)$ sum rule, which the author argues is more reliable than the $\Pi_q (q^2)$ sum rule; however, the author notes that at the present stage it is difficult to determine whether there is a binding effect in the $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ multiquark state, as the $\Pi_q$ sum rule shows a binding effect of about 32 MeV while the $\Pi_1$ sum rule shows a slight repulsive effect of about 3 MeV above the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold.

## Method

The author constructs a QCD sum rule using a five-quark interpolating field corresponding to a $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ multiquark state, with the correlator decomposed into two invariant functions $\Pi_q (q^2)$ and $\Pi_1 (q^2)$. The operator product expansion (OPE) is computed including quark condensates up to dimension 6, neglecting gluon condensates and assuming vacuum saturation, and the phenomenological side is parameterized with a single pole plus continuum. The mass is extracted from the Borel-transformed sum rules, with the continuum threshold $s_0$ determined by first fitting to the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold mass of 1.328 GeV using only two-loop-type diagrams, then including all diagrams to check for binding effects.

## Key numbers

* Predicted mass of the $\Lambda$(1405) from the $\Pi_1$ sum rule: 1.419 GeV, using quark condensate $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -(0.230\ \mathrm{GeV})^3$, strange quark condensate $\langle\bar{s}s\rangle = 0.8\ \langle\bar{q}q\rangle$, and strange quark mass $m_s = 0.150\ \mathrm{GeV}$, with continuum threshold $s_0 = 3.012\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* Binding effect in the $\Pi_q$ sum rule for the $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ multiquark state: average mass $m^\prime = 1.296\ \mathrm{GeV}$, which is about 32 MeV below the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold of 1.328 GeV, using the same condensate values and $s_0 = 3.122\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ over the Borel interval $M^2 = 0.98 - 1.80\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* Repulsive effect in the $\Pi_1$ sum rule for the $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ multiquark state: average mass $m^\prime = 1.331\ \mathrm{GeV}$, about 3 MeV above the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold, using $s_0 = 3.012\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ over the Borel interval $M^2 = 1.18 - 1.90\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* For the $K^+ p$ multiquark state (I=1), the $\Pi_1$ sum rule gives an average mass $m^\prime = 1.440\ \mathrm{GeV}$ (repulsive) compared to the $K^+ p$ threshold of 1.435 GeV, using $s_0 = 3.852\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ over $M^2 = 0.94 - 2.22\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* For the $\pi^+\Sigma^+$ multiquark state (I=2), the $\Pi_1$ sum rule gives an average mass $m^\prime = 1.330\ \mathrm{GeV}$ (repulsive) compared to the $\pi^+\Sigma^+$ threshold of 1.329 GeV, using $s_0 = 3.112\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ over $M^2 = 1.20 - 1.88\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* The mass of the $\Lambda$(1600) can be reproduced: using the $K^+ p$ threshold $s_0 = 3.852\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, the $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ sum rule gives 1.625 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of the $\Lambda$(1405) by Liu (1984), who showed that five-quark operators have large contributions, and by Leinweber (1990), who used a three-quark interpolating field. It also builds on the approach of Kim and Lee (1996) who proposed a three-quark field with a covariant derivative. The author notes that the $\Pi_1$ (chiral-odd) sum rule is more reliable than the $\Pi_q$ (chiral-even) sum rule, consistent with the work of Jin and Tang (1997). The results enable further investigation of whether the $\Lambda$(1405) is a multiquark state, a $\bar{K}N$ bound state, or a mixed configuration, and suggest that sum rules for the full $\pi\Sigma$ basis ($\pi^+\Sigma^- + \pi^0\Sigma^0 + \pi^-\Sigma^+$) would be needed for a complete quantitative analysis.
