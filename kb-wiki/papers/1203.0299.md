# Criticality and Continuity of Explosive Site Percolation in Random Networks

> This Letter studies the critical point as well as the discontinuity of a class of explosive site percolation in Erdös and Rényi (ER) random network. The class of the percolation is implemented by introducing a best-of-m rule. Two major results are found: i). For any specific $m$, the critical percolation point scales with the average degree of the network while its exponent associated with $m$ is bounded by -1 and $\sim-0.5$. ii). Discontinuous percolation could occur on sparse networks if and only if $m$ approaches infinite. These results not only generalize some conclusions of ordinary percolation but also provide new insights to the network robustness.

## Key claim

For the best-of-m explosive site percolation rule on Erdős–Rényi random networks, the percolation transition is continuous for any finite m but becomes discontinuous only when m approaches infinity, and only on sparse networks.

## Method

The authors implement explosive site percolation on Erdős–Rényi random networks using a best-of-m rule: at each time step, m empty nodes are randomly selected as candidates, and the one that minimizes the sum of the sizes of the clusters it connects is occupied. The critical point in the thermodynamic limit t_c(m,k) is determined via finite-size scaling of the susceptibility maximum for network sizes up to 10^5 nodes, averaged over 50 realizations. The discontinuity is assessed by measuring the scaling of the largest jump in the order parameter and by extracting the critical exponent β from finite-size scaling collapses.

## Key numbers

- For ordinary percolation (m=1), the critical point is t_c(k)=1/k.
- For the limiting case m=N→∞, the critical point scales as T_c(k) ∼ k^{−τ} with τ = 0.49 ± 0.02.
- The critical exponent β decays as β(m) ∼ m^{−1.1}, with measured values β ≈ 0.41, 0.26, 0.15, 0.074, 0.031 for m = 2, 3, 5, 10, 20 respectively (k=6).
- For m=2, the largest jump of the order parameter scales with network size as ΔS_max ∼ N^{−0.15}; for m=5, ΔS_max ∼ N^{−0.088}; for m=20, ΔS_max ∼ N^{−0.05}.
- The sum (β+γ)/ν equals 1 with good approximation for all m studied (e.g., β/ν ≈ 0.221, γ/ν ≈ 0.77 for m=2).

## Context

This work extends the debate on explosive percolation initiated by Achlioptas, D'Souza, and Spencer (2009), who first proposed that bond percolation with competitive rules could be discontinuous. Subsequent studies by da Costa et al. (2010) and others showed such transitions are actually continuous with unusually small critical exponents. The present paper systematically studies the critical point location, which prior work had not addressed, and provides a general scaling relation t_c(m,k) ∼ k^{−λ(m)} with λ ranging from 1 to 0.5. The results enable a framework for network robustness where the parameter m quantifies the attacker's information about the network structure.
