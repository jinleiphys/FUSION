# Transverse momentum imbalance of back-to-back particle production in p+A and e+A collisions

> We study the nuclear enhancement of the transverse momentum imbalance for back-to-back particle production in both p+A and e+A collisions. Specifically, we present results for photon+jet and photon+hadron production in p+A collisions, di-jet and di-hadron production in e+A collisions, and heavy-quark and heavy-meson pair production in both p+A and e+A collisions. We evaluate the effect of both initial-state and final-state multiple scattering, which determine the strength of the nuclear-induced transverse momentum imbalance in these processes. We give theoretical predictions for the experimentally relevant kinematic regions in d+Au collisions at RHIC, p+Pb collisions at LHC and e+A collisions at the future EIC and LHeC.

## Key claim

The nuclear enhancement of the transverse momentum imbalance for back-to-back particle production in p+A and e+A collisions can be calculated from initial-state and final-state multiple parton scattering using a higher-twist QCD factorization formalism, with the strength of the effect depending on the color representation of the scattered parton system and on a characteristic scale parameter ξ² extracted from deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The authors compute the average transverse momentum imbalance ⟨q⊥²⟩ for back-to-back particle pairs and define the nuclear broadening Δ⟨q⊥²⟩ as the difference between the imbalance in h+A collisions and that in h+p collisions. They evaluate both initial-state and final-state double scattering contributions using twist-4 quark-gluon and gluon-gluon correlation functions within a well-established QCD factorization formalism, with the hard part functions calculated exactly for each partonic channel. The twist-4 correlation functions are parametrized as ξ² (A¹/³ − 1) times the standard leading-twist parton distribution functions, where ξ² = 0.12 GeV² is the characteristic scale of parton multiple scattering.

## Key numbers

- ξ² = 0.12 GeV²: the characteristic scale of parton multiple scattering, extracted from deep inelastic scattering data
- For LHC p+Pb collisions at √s = 5 TeV, the jet transverse momentum is integrated over 30 GeV < p⊥ < 40 GeV; for RHIC d+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the jet transverse momentum is integrated over 15 GeV < p⊥ < 25 GeV
- For LHC kinematics, the yellow band in predictions represents a variation of the ξ² parameter from 0.12 GeV² to 0.20 GeV², with the upper limit guided by the growth of the inelastic scattering cross section from σ_in^RHIC = 42 mb at √s = 200 GeV to σ_in^LHC = 70 mb at √s = 5 TeV
- For photon+hadron production at LHC kinematics, the integration ranges are 10 < p_γ⊥ < 20 GeV and 5 < p_h⊥ < 10 GeV; for RHIC kinematics, 5 < p_γ⊥ < 15 GeV and 5 < p_h⊥ < 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of di-jet and di-hadron transverse momentum imbalance (Kang, 2011) to new channels including photon+jet, photon+hadron, heavy-quark pair, and heavy-meson pair production in both p+A and e+A collisions. The formalism follows the higher-twist approach developed by Luo, Qiu, and collaborators for multiple scattering in nuclear matter, and has been previously used to describe cold nuclear matter effects such as energy loss, dynamical shadowing, and broadening effects. The predictions are relevant for d+Au collisions at RHIC, p+Pb collisions at LHC, and future measurements at the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) and Large Hadron Electron Collider (LHeC).
