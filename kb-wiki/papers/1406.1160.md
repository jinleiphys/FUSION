# Ab Initio study of neutron drops with chiral Hamiltonians

> We report ab initio calculations for neutron drops in a 10 MeV external harmonic-oscillator trap using chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions. We present total binding energies, internal energies, radii and odd-even energy differences for neutron numbers N = 2 - 18 using the no-core shell model with and without importance truncation. Furthermore, we present total binding energies for N = 8, 16, 20, 28, 40, 50 obtained in a coupled-cluster approach. Comparisons with Green's Function Monte Carlo results, where available, using Argonne v8' with three-nucleon interactions reveal important dependences on the chosen Hamiltonian.

# Ab Initio study of neutron drops with chiral Hamiltonians

## Key claim
Chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions produce total energies for neutron drops in a 10 MeV harmonic-oscillator trap that are surprisingly insensitive to the inclusion of the chiral three-nucleon interaction, with the ratio of results with and without the initial three-nucleon interaction fluctuating at less than 1% over the range N=2-18. This contrasts sharply with the 2-4% deviations seen for phenomenological Argonne v8' plus three-nucleon interactions over the same range.

## Method
The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) and importance-truncated NCSM (IT-NCSM) calculations for neutron drops with N=2-18, and coupled-cluster calculations at the Lambda-CCSD(T) level for closed subshells at N=8, 16, 20, 28, 40, 50. All calculations used a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO with 500 MeV/c cutoff combined with a chiral three-nucleon interaction at N2LO, evolved via the similarity renormalization group to flow parameters alpha = 0.04 and 0.08 fm^4, with the three-nucleon interaction treated in the normal-ordered two-body approximation for the coupled-cluster calculations.

## Key numbers
- Total ground-state energy for N=8 neutrons with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha=0.08 fm^4: 135.34(7) MeV (NCSM), 135.32(2) MeV (IT-NCSM), 135.3(1) MeV (Lambda-CCSD(T))
- Total ground-state energy for N=16 neutrons with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha=0.08 fm^4: 333.1(8) MeV (NCSM), 332.8(3) MeV (IT-NCSM), 332.4(5) MeV (Lambda-CCSD(T))
- Total ground-state energy for N=50 neutrons with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha=0.08 fm^4: 1448(2) MeV (Lambda-CCSD(T))
- Ratio of total energies with initial three-nucleon interaction to those without fluctuates at less than 1% over N=2-18 for chiral interactions
- For AV8'+IL7, the ratio deviates by up to 4% below the NN-only result; for AV8'+UIX, it deviates by up to 2% above
- Correlation energy for N=50 with NN+3N-full at alpha=0.08 fm^4: -23.3 MeV (Lambda-CCSD(T))

## Context
This work extends the neutron-drop studies of Bogner (2011), Gandolfi (2010), and Maris (2013) by providing the first ab initio results using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians rather than phenomenological interactions like Argonne v8' with Urbana IX or Illinois-7 three-nucleon forces. The results enable systematic comparisons between chiral and phenomenological interactions in pure neutron systems, which can inform the development of energy-density functionals for neutron-rich matter and neutron star physics. The weak sensitivity to the chiral three-nucleon interaction in the T=3/2 channel raises open questions about whether this will persist when chiral three-nucleon interactions complete through N3LO become available.
