# A new boson expansion theory utilizing a norm operator

> We propose a new boson expansion method using a norm operator. The small parameter expansion, in which the boson approximation becomes the zeroth-order approximation, requires the double commutation relations between phonon operators that are not closed between the phonon excitation modes adopted as boson excitations. This results in an infinite expansion regardless of whether the type of the boson expansion is Hermitian or non-Hermitian. The small parameter expansion does not hold when the commutation relations are closed. The norm operator is expressed as a function of the number operator in the physical subspace, which enables us to obtain substantially a finite boson expansion regardless of the Hermitian or non-Hermitian type. We also point out the problems of the conventional boson expansion methods. The normal-ordered linked-cluster expansion theory has failed to refute Marshalek's claim that KT-1 and KT-2 are of chimerical boson expansion. The Dyson boson expansion theory does not have exceptional superiority over other types. Previous studies using the boson expansion methods should be re-examined.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new boson expansion method (the norm operator method, NOM) that can handle both Hermitian and non-Hermitian types of boson expansions, with or without limiting the phonon excitation modes and the number of excitations, and claims that the conventional boson expansion methods (including the normal-ordered linked-cluster expansion theory and the Dyson boson expansion theory) contain unresolved problems that require re-examination of previous studies.

## Method

The author introduces a norm operator defined as a function of the number operator in the physical subspace, constructed from the multi-phonon norm matrices, and uses it to build a mapping operator U_ξ = Z^(ξ-1/2) Ũ that maps fermion operators onto boson operators. The method is applied to three cases: mapping all phonon excitation modes, mapping with a maximum phonon excitation number of 1 (which reproduces the boson approximation), and the general restricted case where only selected phonon modes are used as boson excitations.

## Key numbers

- The norm matrix eigenvalues for the case of all phonon excitation modes are either (2N-1)!! or 0, where (2N-1)!! = (2N)!/(2^N N!), and zero eigenvalues appear even at N=2.
- For the case where the maximum phonon excitation number is 1, the mapping operator becomes U_ξ = |0)(0| + Σ_μ |μ)(μ|, independent of ξ, and the boson expansions become (X_μ)_B = b_μ, (X_μ^†)_B = b_μ^†, and (B_q)_B = Σ_{μμ'} Γ^{μ'μ}_q b_μ^† b_{μ'}.
- The relation Z^(A)(N)^2 = (2N-1)!! Z^(A)(N) holds for the norm operator when all phonon modes are adopted.

## Context

This work extends the boson expansion theory lineage from the original formulations by Beliaev and Zelevinsky (1962) and the MYT mapping by Marumori, Yamamura, and Tokunaga (1964), and directly addresses the problems raised by Marshalek (1980a, 1980b) regarding the KT-1 and KT-2 methods. The paper criticizes the normal-ordered linked-cluster expansion theory (KT-3) for failing to refute Marshalek's claims and argues that the Dyson boson expansion theory has no exceptional superiority over other types. The new NOM method enables comparison with the boson-fermion expansion theory (BFEXP) proposed by Taniguchi and Matsuyanagi (1990, 1991) and provides a framework to handle the contributions of phonon excitation modes that were neglected in conventional methods.
