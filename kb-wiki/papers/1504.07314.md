# Hadronic Matter in the Robertson-Walker Metric and the Early Universe

> In this work, the Friedman equations for hadronic matter in the Robertson-Walker metric in the early Universe are obtained. We consider the hadronic phase, formed after the hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma, that means times from 10^{-6}s to 1s. The set of equations is derived and the behavior of the system is studied considering one approximate analytical solution.

## Key claim

The authors derive the Friedmann equations for the hadronic phase of the early Universe (times from 10⁻⁶ s to 1 s) using a relativistic mean-field model with σ, ω, and ρ meson exchange, but they only present an approximate analytical solution under simplifying assumptions (radiation-dominated regime and non-relativistic baryon approximation) while stating that the full numerical solution will be published later.

## Method

The authors start from the Robertson-Walker metric and a σ-ω-ρ relativistic mean-field Lagrangian density adapted to curved spacetime, then apply the mean-field approximation to obtain equations of motion for the meson fields. They derive the energy density and pressure for the hadronic matter, including baryons, electrons, neutrinos, photons, and meson fields, and combine these with the Friedmann equation and baryon number conservation to obtain a set of coupled differential equations for the scale factor, scalar field, temperature, and baryon chemical potential. An approximate analytical solution is obtained by considering only the T⁴ radiation term in the energy density and pressure, and by making a non-relativistic approximation for the baryons with negligible meson field contributions.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic phase is considered for times from 10⁻⁶ s to 1 s after the Big Bang
- The approximate analytical solution for the scale factor in the radiation-dominated regime gives ℛ = √(12.3 × 10³ t + 1), where t is in seconds
- The temperature scales as T = T₀/ℛ in the radiation-dominated approximation
- The baryon density evolves as n_b = n_b0 (R₀/R)³, where n_b0 is the initial baryon density at the start of the hadronic epoch
- The radiation contribution to energy density and pressure is proportional to T⁴ with coefficient 43/120 π² for energy density and 43/360 π² for pressure

## Context

This work extends the application of relativistic mean-field models (Walecka model) from heavy-ion collision studies (Deb et al., Barros Jr. et al.) and neutron star structure calculations (Rufini et al.) to the early Universe's hadronic phase. The authors note that their results for baryon and anti-baryon densities as functions of temperature are consistent with those obtained by Rafelski using a different model. The formalism enables future refinements including spin connections, neutrino modeling, and full numerical solutions of the coupled equations.
