# Collision-Induced Decay of Metastable Baby Skyrmions

> Many extensions of the standard model predict heavy metastable particles which may be modeled as solitons (skyrmions of the Higgs field), relating their particle number to a winding number. Previous work has shown that the electroweak interactions admit processes in which these solitons decay, violating standard model baryon number. We motivate the hypothesis that baryon-number-violating decay is a generic outcome of collisions between these heavy particles. We do so by exploring a 2+1 dimensional theory which also possesses metastable skyrmions. We use relaxation techniques to determine the size, shape and energy of static solitons in their ground state. These solitons could decay by quantum mechanical tunneling. Classically, they are metastable: only a finite excitation energy is required to induce their decay. We attempt to induce soliton decay in a classical simulation by colliding pairs of solitons. We analyze the collision of solitons with varying inherent stabilities and varying incident velocities and orientations. Our results suggest that winding-number violating decay is a generic outcome of collisions. All that is required is sufficient (not necessarily very large) incident velocity; no fine-tuning of initial conditions is required.

## Key claim
Collisions between metastable baby skyrmions in a 2+1 dimensional toy model generically induce winding-number-violating decay, provided the incident velocity exceeds a threshold that is less than or of order half the speed of light; no fine-tuning of initial conditions (impact parameter or relative orientation) is required.

## Method
The authors studied a 2+1 dimensional O(3) sigma model with a Skyrme term and a symmetry-breaking potential, using a linear sigma model (finite λ) to render solitons metastable. They found static soliton configurations via conjugate-gradient relaxation on a 125×125 lattice, then simulated classical head-on and off-center collisions of pairs of these solitons using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta-Fehlberg algorithm with adaptive time stepping, varying λ (which controls the barrier height), incident velocity, impact parameter, and relative orientation.

## Key numbers
- The critical coupling for soliton existence is λ_c between 7.6 and 7.7; no classically stable soliton exists for λ = 7.6.
- Static soliton energy at λ = 15 is 19.1792 (in units with F=κ=1); at λ = 7.7 it is 18.7131.
- Soliton radius (containing 90% of energy) is 3.31 for λ = 15 and 2.83 for λ = 7.7.
- The critical incident velocity v_c for inducing decay is less than or of order half the speed of light for λ ≲ 2λ_c and impact parameter b less than or of order the soliton size.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of baby skyrmion collisions in the λ→∞ (absolutely stable) limit by Piette et al. (1995), where solitons never decay. By introducing finite λ, the authors create metastable solitons analogous to electroweak solitons studied by D'Hoker and Farhi (1984), Ambjorn and Rubakov (1985), and others, which can decay via baryon-number-violating processes. The results motivate future work on baryon-number-violating scattering of TeV-scale particles modeled as electroweak solitons in 3+1 dimensions, though the authors caution that their toy model lacks gauge fields and the explicit symmetry-breaking term has no electroweak analogue.
