# Algebraic-eikonal approach to medium energy proton scattering from odd-mass nuclei

> We extend the algebraic-eikonal approach to medium energy proton scattering from odd-mass nuclei by combining the eikonal approximation for the scattering with a description of odd-mass nuclei in terms of the interacting boson-fermion model. We derive closed expressions for the transition matrix elements for one of the dynamical symmetries and discuss the interplay between collective and single-particle degrees of freedom in an application to elastic and inelastic proton scattering from $^{195}$Pt.

## Key claim

The algebraic-eikonal approach for medium energy proton scattering is extended to odd-mass nuclei by combining the eikonal approximation with the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM), enabling closed-form expressions for transition matrix elements in the \(SO(6) \otimes SU(2)\) dynamical symmetry limit, but the results depend on the peripheral approximation and the assumption of a pseudo-spin coupling scheme for the single-particle orbits.

## Method

The authors combine the eikonal approximation for the projectile scattering with the IBFM description of odd-mass target nuclei, deriving the eikonal phase in terms of bosonic (collective) and fermionic (single-particle) quadrupole operators. They then specialize to the \(SO(6) \otimes SU(2)\) dynamical symmetry limit of the IBFM, obtaining closed analytic expressions for the eikonal transition matrix elements for elastic and inelastic scattering, and apply the formalism to 800 MeV proton scattering from \(^{195}\)Pt using Woods-Saxon nuclear densities and Tassie-form transition densities normalized to known \(B(E2)\) values.

## Key numbers

* Elastic and inelastic differential cross sections are calculated for 800 MeV protons on \(^{195}\)Pt, using a nuclear radius of \(1.2 A^{1/3} = 6.96\) fm and a diffusivity of 0.75 fm, normalized to total nucleon number \(A=195\).
* The isospin-averaged proton-nucleon cross section is taken as \(\sigma = 46(1+0.38i)\) mb for the forward scattering amplitude \(f = ik\sigma/4\pi\).
* For the excitation of the \(L=2\) doublet with quantum numbers \([6,1],(6,1,0)\), the full calculation including single-particle coupling differs from the collective-only calculation by almost a factor of two (logarithmic scale).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic-eikonal approach previously applied to even-even nuclei (Amos, Morrison, and Smith; Ginocchio, Oset, and Strottman; Ginocchio and Wenes) to odd-mass nuclei, using the IBFM framework developed by Iachello and collaborators. The specific application to \(^{195}\)Pt builds on the identification of this nucleus as a paradigm of \(SO(6) \otimes SU(2)\) dynamical boson-fermion symmetry by Bijker and Iachello. The results enable future experimental tests of the pseudo-spin symmetry assumption in proton scattering and can be extended to other IBFM dynamical symmetries and to odd-odd nuclei.
