# Inversion doublets of 3N+N cluster structure in excited states of $^4$He

> Excited states of $^4$He are studied in four-body calculations with explicitly correlated Gaussian bases. All the levels below $E_{\rm x}$=26 MeV are reproduced reasonably well using realistic potentials. An analysis is made to show how the $0^+_2$ state becomes a resonance but those having almost the same structure as this state in different spin-isospin channels are not observed as resonances. The role of tensor force is stressed with a particular attention to the level spacing between the two $0^-$ states. The calculation of spectroscopic amplitudes, nucleon decay widths, and spin-dipole transition strengths demonstrates that the $0^+_2$ state and the three lowest-lying negative-parity states with $0^-$ and $2^-$ have $^3$H+$p$ and $^3$He+$n$ cluster configurations, leading to the interpretation that these negative-parity states are the inversion doublet partners of the $0^+_2$ state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the three lowest-lying negative-parity states of \(^4\)He (\(0^-0\), \(2^-0\), and \(2^-1\)) are the inversion-doublet partners of the first excited \(0^+_2\) state, based on their shared \(^3\)H+\(p\) and \(^3\)He+\(n\) cluster structure. The claim is hedged by noting that the negative-parity states show a somewhat reduced degree of clustering compared to the \(0^+_2\) state, and that the calculation uses a bound-state approximation which gives only qualitative predictions for broad levels.

## Method

The authors performed four-body variational calculations for \(^4\)He using explicitly correlated Gaussian basis functions, with the stochastic variational method to optimize parameters. They employed two realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (G3RS and AV8\(^\prime\)), including central, tensor, and spin-orbit terms, and solved the Schrödinger equation without any cluster ansatz or shell-model restriction. Spectroscopic amplitudes, nucleon decay widths (via an \(R\)-matrix formula), and spin-dipole transition strengths were computed to characterize the cluster structure of the excited states.

## Key numbers

- The \(0^+_2\) state is calculated at \(E_{\rm x}=20.21\) MeV, the \(0^-0\) state at 21.01 MeV, and the \(2^-0\) state at 21.84 MeV, all reproduced very well by the calculation.
- The proton decay width of the \(0^+_2\) state is 0.69, 0.74, and 0.67 MeV at channel radii \(r=4, 5, 6\) fm, respectively, in good agreement with the empirical value of 0.50 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for the \(0^+_2\) state in the \(^3\)He+\(n\) channel is 1.03; for the \(0^-0\), \(2^-0\), and \(2^-1\) states, the spectroscopic factors are 0.58, 0.52, and 0.53, respectively.
- The tensor force contributes about 9 MeV more attraction to the \(0^-0\) state than to the \(0^-1\) state (G3RS potential), and about 12 MeV more with the AV8\(^\prime\) potential.
- The isoscalar spin-dipole transition strength \(|\langle 0^+_20 ||{\cal O}_0||0^-0\rangle |^2\) is 11.9 fm\(^2\), which is 6.9 times larger than that to the ground state and occupies 58% of the sum rule.
- The isoscalar spin-dipole strength \(|\langle 0^+_20 ||{\cal O}_2||2^-0\rangle |^2/5\) is 21.7 fm\(^2\), about 24 times larger than the ground-state transition, occupying 78% of the sum rule.
- The isovector spin-dipole strength \(|\langle 0^+_20 |||{\cal O}_{2,1}|||2^-1\rangle |^2/15\) is 17.4 fm\(^2\), 16 times larger than the ground-state transition, occupying 87% of the sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the cluster interpretation of the \(0^+_2\) state in \(^4\)He, previously established by Hiyama (2000), to the negative-parity states, which were traditionally considered \(s^3p\) shell-model states. The calculation contradicts the shell-model picture by showing that the negative-parity states are dominated by \(^3\)N+\(N\) cluster configurations, similar to the \(0^+_2\) state. The results enable a unified description of the \(^4\)He spectrum in terms of inversion doublets, analogous to molecular spectroscopy and to the \(\alpha\)-core molecular structure proposed for \(^{16}\)O and \(^{20}\)Ne by the molecular-structure community. The paper also notes that the RGM phase-shift analysis of Hofmann (1970) supports the \(P\)-wave resonance interpretation for these negative-parity states.
