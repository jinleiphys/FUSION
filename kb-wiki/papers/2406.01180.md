# Parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions of spin-1 hadrons

> Structure functions of the spin-1 deuteron will be investigated experimentally from the late 2020's at various facilities such as Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility, Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, nuclotron-based ion collider facility, and electron-ion colliders. We expect that a new high-energy spin-physics field could be created by these projects. In this paper, the current theoretical status is explained for the structure functions of spin-1 hadrons, especially on parton distribution functions, transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions, and fragmentation functions. Related multiparton distribution functions are also shown.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical status of spin-1 hadron structure functions, arguing that future experimental measurements of the tensor-polarized structure function \(b_1\) and the gluon transversity \(\Delta_T g\) in the deuteron could reveal new hadronic mechanisms beyond the simple bound-state picture of a proton and neutron, though the existing HERMES data have large uncertainties and the standard deuteron model calculations differ significantly from those measurements.

## Method

The paper presents the theoretical framework for spin-1 hadron structure functions, including the hadron tensor decomposition into eight structure functions (\(F_{1,2}\), \(g_{1,2}\), and \(b_{1-4}\)) and the parton-model relation between \(b_1\) and tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) \(\delta_T q(x)\). It derives a sum rule for the first moment of \(b_1\) using the optical theorem and elastic form factors, provides a parametrization of tensor-polarized PDFs fitted to HERMES data, and calculates \(b_1\) using two convolution-model approaches (scaling-limit relations and virtual nucleon approximation) with the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function and MSTW-2008 LO PDFs.

## Key numbers

- HERMES measurement: \(\int_{0.02}^{0.85} dx \, b_1(x) = [0.35 \pm 0.10 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.18 \text{ (sys)}] \times 10^{-2}\) at \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\)
- The parametrized \(b_1\) gives \(\int dx \, b_1(x) = 0.0058 \pm 0.0047\) from the fit to HERMES data
- The theoretical calculations use \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) as the average scale of the HERMES experiment
- The gluon transversity cross section formula involves the color factor \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\) with \(N_c = 3\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin-1 hadron structure functions, which has been at a premature stage since the HERMES \(b_1\) experiment in 2005 (HERMES Collaboration, 2005). The paper reviews the sum rule approach analogous to the Gottfried sum rule (Kumano, 1998; Garvey and Peng, 2001; Peng, 2014) and provides parametrized tensor-polarized PDFs for planning future experiments at JLab (JLab \(b_1\) project), Fermilab (SpinQuest project, Keller, 2022), NICA (NICA Collaboration, 2021), and the electron-ion colliders (EIC, 2022; EicC, 2021). The convolution-model calculations (Kumano, 2017) serve as predictions for the upcoming JLab experiment, and the gluon transversity formalism enables searches for exotic hadron physics through Drell-Yan processes (Kumano, 2020).
