# Comment on Constraints on the Low-Energy E1 Cross Section of 12C(alpha,gamma)16O from the Beta-Delayed Alpha Spectrum of 16N

> We dispute the alteration by Azuma et al. of the energy calibration of the Mainz('71) data on the beta-delayed alpha-particle emission of $^{16}N$ as well as the very justification of the recalibration. We use the unaltered data to observe a pronounced disagreement between the TRIUMF('94) and Mainz('71) data sets on both the high and low energy sides of the primary peak (at 2.36 MeV) of the $^{16}N$ alpha-spectrum. We cannot support the dismissal of the Mainz('71) spectrum by the TRIUMF collaboration and emphasize the need to include it in R-matrix fits. We discuss the need for new improved data to resolve this disagreement and its implication for the extracted p-wave astrophysical S-factor of the $^{12}C(α,γ)^{16}O$ reaction.

# Comment on Constraints on the Low-Energy E1 Cross Section of 12C(alpha,gamma)16O from the Beta-Delayed Alpha Spectrum of 16N

## Key claim

The authors dispute the recalibration of the Mainz(1971) 16N beta-delayed alpha spectrum performed by Azuma et al. (1994, 1997), arguing that the justification for the recalibration is illogical and that the unaltered Mainz data show a pronounced disagreement with the TRIUMF(1994) data, contrary to claims that the Mainz spectrum is difficult to fit and can be dismissed.

## Method

The authors compare the original unaltered Mainz(1971) data (32 million counts) with the TRIUMF(1994) data (1 million counts) by computing the ratio of the two data sets normalized at the highest yield point near E_cm = 2.36 MeV, using linear interpolations and including uncertainties from both data sets. They also re-examine the Mainz spectrum in the region of the 2+ state at E_alpha = 2.0115 MeV, fitting a fourth-order polynomial background and a Gaussian with fixed centroid to assess the claimed self-calibration procedure of Azuma et al.

## Key numbers

- The Mainz(1971) data set contains 32 million counts, compared to 1 million counts for the TRIUMF(1994) data.
- The ratio of the unaltered Mainz data to the TRIUMF data deviates from unity by as much as 30-40%, with a chi-square per data point of 123.
- The primary peak of the 16N alpha spectrum is at 2.36 MeV (center-of-mass energy).
- The 2+ state has an expected alpha energy of E_alpha = 2.0115 MeV, corresponding to channel 105.9 in the Mainz spectrum.
- The Mainz group's own calibration using the 10B(n,alpha)7Li procedure claimed an uncertainty of ±10 keV.
- Azuma et al. reported that their recalibration produces energy shifts ranging from 6.5 keV at the low end to 18 keV at higher energies.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the analysis of Azuma et al. (1994, 1997) regarding the 16N beta-delayed alpha spectrum and its use in R-matrix fits to extract the p-wave astrophysical S-factor of the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction. The authors argue that the Mainz(1971) data, which had been largely ignored due to claims of a broader line-shape, should be included in future R-matrix fits. They reference their own preliminary report (France 1996) showing new data that disagree with the TRIUMF data but agree with the unaltered Mainz singles data, and also mention unpublished data from the Seattle group (Zhang 1995). The resolution of this disagreement is critical for determining whether the small S-factor solution (S_E1 < 20 keV-b) is ruled out, as the line-shape differences at the interference minimum near 1.4 MeV could significantly alter the extracted p-wave astrophysical S-factor.
