# Neutron skins and neutron stars

> Background: The neutron skin of a heavy nucleus as well as many neutron-star properties are highly sensitive to the poorly constrained density dependence of the symmetry energy. Purpose: To provide for the first time meaningful theoretical errors and to assess the degree of correlation between the neutron-skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb and several neutron-star properties. Methods: A proper covariance analysis based on the predictions of an accurately-calibrated relativistic functional {\sl "FSUGold"} is used to quantify theoretical errors and correlation coefficients. Results: We find correlation coefficients of nearly one (or minus one) between the neutron-skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb and a host of observables of relevance to the structure, dynamics, and composition of neutron stars. Conclusions: We suggest that a follow-up PREX measurement, ideally with a 0.5% accuracy, could significantly constrain the equation of state of neutron-star matter.

## Key claim

The paper uses a covariance analysis based on the FSUGold relativistic mean-field model to quantify, for the first time with meaningful theoretical errors, the degree of correlation between the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and a host of neutron-star observables; the authors find correlation coefficients of nearly one (or minus one) but note that these correlations are derived from a single calibrated model and depend on the assumed 20% uncertainty in the slope of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed a covariance analysis using the FSUGold relativistic mean-field model, starting from a chi-squared minimization procedure with eight nuclear and astrophysical observables as input (saturation density, binding energy at saturation and at twice saturation density, compression modulus, effective Dirac mass, symmetry energy at sub-saturation density, slope of the symmetry energy, and maximum neutron-star mass). They computed the matrix of second derivatives of the chi-squared function around the minimum, diagonalized it to identify stiff and soft parameter directions, and then calculated the covariance and Pearson correlation coefficients between the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and various neutron-star properties using the inverse of the curvature matrix.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb predicted by FSUGold: R_skin = (0.2069 ± 0.0366) fm [17.698%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the slope of the symmetry energy L: ρ = 0.9952, with L = (60.5152 ± 12.1011) MeV [19.997%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the pressure of pure neutron matter at saturation density: ρ = 0.9882, with P(ρ0) = (3.1842 ± 0.6349) MeV fm^-3 [19.940%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the pressure of pure neutron matter at half saturation density: ρ = 0.9861, with P(ρ0/2) = (0.4874 ± 0.1721) MeV fm^-3 [35.304%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the pressure of pure neutron matter at twice saturation density: ρ = 0.8016, with P(2ρ0) = (21.8569 ± 1.2735) MeV fm^-3 [5.827%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: ρ = 0.9486, with R_1.4 = (12.6568 ± 0.4603) km [3.637%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the radius of a 0.6 solar mass neutron star: ρ = 0.9953, with R_0.6 = (13.9785 ± 1.5183) km [10.862%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the threshold density for the direct Urca process: ρ = -0.9928, with ρ_Urca = (0.4668 ± 0.1324) fm^-3 [28.359%]
- Correlation coefficient between R_skin and the threshold mass for the direct Urca process: ρ = -0.9927, with M_Urca = (1.3012 ± 0.2658) solar masses [20.427%]

## Context

This work extends the covariance analysis framework previously developed by Fattoyev and Piekarewicz (2011) to systematically quantify correlations between the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and neutron-star properties, building on earlier qualitative correlations established by Horowitz and collaborators (2000, 2001, 2002) and by Steiner and collaborators (2004). The results demonstrate that a follow-up PREX measurement with 0.5% accuracy could significantly constrain the equation of state of neutron-star matter, and the quantified correlation coefficients provide a rigorous statistical basis for using laboratory measurements of neutron skins to predict neutron-star radii, the onset of direct Urca cooling, and the pressure of pure neutron matter.
