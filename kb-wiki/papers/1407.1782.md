# Hydrodynamics and Jets in Dialogue

> Energy and momentum loss of jets in heavy ion collisions can affect the fluid dynamic evolution of the medium. We determine realistic event-by-event averages and correlation functions of the local energy-momentum transfer from hard particles to the soft sector using the jet-quenching Monte-Carlo code JEWEL combined with a hydrodynamic model for the background. The expectation values for source terms due to jets in a typical (minimum bias) event affect the fluid dynamic evolution mainly by their momentum transfer. This leads to a small increase in flow. The presence of hard jets in the event constitutes only a minor correction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy and momentum transfer from hard jets to the soft medium in heavy ion collisions, as modeled by the JEWEL Monte Carlo code combined with a hydrodynamic background, produces only a minor correction to the fluid dynamic evolution; the effect is mainly through momentum transfer leading to a small increase in radial flow, while the presence of hard jets constitutes only a minor correction overall.

## Method

The authors used the jet-quenching Monte Carlo code JEWEL to simulate jets propagating through a hydrodynamic background medium described by a (2+1)-dimensional viscous fluid dynamic model with Bjorken boost invariance and azimuthal symmetry. They extracted event-by-event averages and correlation functions of the local energy-momentum transfer from hard particles to the soft sector by tracking the explicit energy and momentum exchange in individual scattering processes between jet partons and thermal scattering centers. The source terms were then inserted into the fluid dynamic evolution equations to quantify the back-reaction of jets on the bulk medium.

## Key numbers

- The initial condition for the hydrodynamic evolution used a maximal temperature of T = 485 MeV at proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm for central collisions.
- The radial component of the fluid velocity u^r increased by up to about 10% when source terms from jets were included, compared to the case without source terms.
- The temperature at large radii increased by a few percent due to the larger radial flow induced by jet momentum transfer.
- The jet nuclear modification factor R_AA and di-jet asymmetry A_J = (p⊥,1 - p⊥,2)/(p⊥,1 + p⊥,2) were compared to ALICE and CMS data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, with the sub-leading jet required to have p⊥,2 > 30 GeV and Δφ > 2π/3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Mach cone formation and jet-induced flow by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2005), Chaudhuri and Heinz (2006), and Neufeld et al. (2008) by using a more realistic, microscopically motivated model for the energy-momentum deposition from jets. It contrasts with approaches that assume all radiated energy is dissipated locally, such as those by Bass et al. (2009) and Chen et al. (2011), by explicitly tracking the energy and momentum exchange in individual scattering events. The results enable future quantitative studies of how jet-induced fluctuations affect harmonic flow coefficients v_n, which the authors state they plan to investigate in a separate publication.
