# Polarization Phenomena in Small-Angle Photoproduction of e+e- Pairs and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule

> Photoproduction of $e^+e^-$ pairs at small angles is investigated as a tool to determine the functions $f_1$ and $f_2$ entering the real-photon forward Compton scattering amplitude. The method is based on an interference of the Bethe-Heitler and the virtual Compton scattering mechanisms, generating an azimuthal asymmetry in the $e^+$ versus $e^-$ yield. The general case of a circularly polarized beam and a longitudinally polarized target allows one to determine both the real and imaginary parts of $f_1$ as well as $f_2$. The imaginary part of $f_2$ requires target polarization only. We calculate cross sections and asymmetries of the reaction $p(γ,e^+e^-)p$, estimate corrections and backgrounds, and propose suitable kinematical regions to perform the experiment. Our investigation shows that photoproduction of $e^+e^-$-pairs off the proton and light nuclei may serve as a rather sensitive test of the validity of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule.

# Polarization Phenomena in Small-Angle Photoproduction of e+e- Pairs and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule

## Key claim
The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the isovector (I=1) component of the nucleon appears to be violated by existing data, with the integral up to 1 GeV giving +46.8 microbarns versus the predicted -14.2 microbarns; the authors propose that measuring the forward Compton amplitude f2 via azimuthal asymmetries in e+e- pair photoproduction can test whether this discrepancy arises from inaccurate spin-dependent photoabsorption data or from a genuine failure of the sum rule.

## Method
The authors calculate the Bethe-Heitler and virtual Compton scattering amplitudes for the reaction p(gamma, e+e-)p in the small-angle, high-energy regime, deriving the azimuthal asymmetry between positron and electron yields that arises from their C-parity-odd interference. They evaluate the asymmetry for circularly polarized photons and longitudinally polarized targets, expressing it in terms of the real and imaginary parts of the forward Compton amplitudes f1 and f2, and estimate backgrounds from pi0 decay and corrections from finite photon mass, non-forward scattering, and longitudinal virtual photon contributions.

## Key numbers
- GDH integral for I=1 component up to 1 GeV: 38.8 (1-pion) + 8.0 (2-pion) = 46.8 microbarns, compared to the sum rule prediction of -14.2 microbarns
- GDH integral for I=0 component up to 1 GeV: 164.6 (1-pion) + 52.9 (2-pion) = 217.5 microbarns, matching the predicted 218.9 microbarns
- For a target asymmetry measurement at omega ~ 1 GeV, W ~ 50 MeV, Q ~ 100 MeV: a value Delta_sigma = +/- 29 microbarns yields asymmetry A_N = +/- 1%
- For the real part of f2 at the same kinematics: R = Re f2 / f2(0) = +/- 5% yields asymmetry A_gammaN = -/+ 0.07%
- The Bethe-Heitler cross section in the proposed kinematics is a few tens of nanobarns; with photon intensity 10^9 s^-1 and target density 5 x 10^23 cm^-2, the count rate reaches a few events per second
- Background from pi0 decay at omega' = 600 MeV, W = 50 MeV, Q = 100 MeV: 0.20 nb
- Corrections to the forward Compton approximation at Q = 100 MeV and omega = 1 GeV: f1 decreases by 1.4% (real) and 3.9% (imaginary); f2 decreases by 0.6% (real) and 5.2% (imaginary); helicity-flip amplitudes are 0.3% and 0.15% of f1 and f2 respectively

## Context
This work extends the method of Alvager et al. (1973), who used unpolarized beam and target to determine Re f1 at 2.2 GeV, to the polarized case that can access both f1 and f2. The GDH sum rule violation in the isovector channel was previously noted by Sandorfi et al. (1995), and the present calculations use the VPI multipole solution SP97K from Arndt et al. (1996) for single-pion photoproduction and the model of L'vov (1997) for double-pion photoproduction. The proposed measurement would provide an independent determination of Im f2 (proportional to the GDH cross section difference) and Re f2 (which constrains the high-energy behavior via dispersion relations), complementing direct measurements of sigma_1/2 and sigma_3/2 with 4pi hadron detectors.
