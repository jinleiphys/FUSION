# Hyperon I: Study of the $Λ(1405)$

> Low-energy data on the three charge states in $γp \to K^+(Σπ)$ from CLAS at JLab, on $K^-p\to π^0π^0Λ$ and $π^0π^0Σ$ from the Crystal Ball at BNL, bubble chamber data on $K^-p\toπ^-π^+π^{\pm}Σ^{\mp}$, low-energy total cross sections on $K^-$ induced reactions, and data on the $K^-p$ atom are fitted with the BnGa partial-wave-analysis program. We find that the data can be fitted well with just one isoscalar spin-1/2 negative-parity pole, the $Λ(1405)$, and background contributions.

## Key claim
The data can be fitted well with just one isoscalar spin-1/2 negative-parity pole, the Λ(1405), and background contributions, contradicting many chiral unitary approaches that find two poles in this region. When a second pole was admitted in the fit, it moved into the non-physical region below the πΣ threshold or to the K⁻p threshold with an anomalously small hadronic width, which the authors do not consider physically meaningful.

## Method
The BnGa partial-wave-analysis program was used to fit low-energy data on the three charge states in γp → K⁺(Σπ) from CLAS at JLab, K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Λ and π⁰π⁰Σ from the Crystal Ball at BNL, bubble chamber data on K⁻p → π⁻π⁺π^±Σ^∓, low-energy total cross sections on K⁻-induced reactions, and data on the K⁻p atom. The Λ(1/2⁻) amplitude was described by a five-channel D-matrix amplitude with decays to π⁻Σ⁺, π⁰Σ⁰, π⁺Σ⁻, K⁻p and K⁰n, using one-pole and two-pole parametrizations.

## Key numbers
- Pole position of Λ(1405): M_pole = (1422 ± 3, −i(21 ± 3)) MeV
- Residue for K⁻p → Λ(1405) → K̅N: magnitude 63 ± 4 MeV, phase (155 ± 15)°
- Residue for K⁻p → Λ(1405) → πΣ: magnitude 42 ± 3 MeV, phase (0 or 180 ± 15)°
- Kaonic hydrogen 1S level shift and width: ΔE − iΓ/2 = (283 ± 42) − i(271 ± 55) eV
- Ratio γ = Γ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻) / Γ(K⁻p → π⁻Σ⁺) = 2.38 ± 0.04
- Ratio R_n = Γ(K⁻p → π⁰Λ) / Γ(K⁻p → neutral) = 0.189 ± 0.015
- Ratio R_c = Γ(K⁻p → π^±Σ^∓) / Γ(K⁻p → inelastic) = 0.664 ± 0.011
- χ² of fit to CLAS data: 41320 for 16076 cells
- χ²/(N_data − N_param) for bubble chamber data: 3.3/(12 − 7)

## Context
This work extends the BnGa partial-wave-analysis program to the Λ(1405) region and directly contradicts the two-pole structure found in chiral unitary approaches by Oller and Meissner (2000), Jido et al. (2003), and many subsequent studies such as Cieply et al. (2009), Ikeda et al. (2012), and Mai et al. (2012). The result supports earlier single-pole interpretations from HADES data (Agakishiev et al., 2012) and the CLAS collaboration (Moriya et al., 2013), and is consistent with quark model expectations where Λ(1405) is the SU(3) singlet state. The analysis enables a forthcoming companion study (Hyperon II and III) of the full hyperon resonance spectrum.
