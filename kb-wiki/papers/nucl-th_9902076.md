# Resonances, Gamow Vectors and Time Asymmetric Quantum Theory

> Decaying states can be represented by Gamow vectors with an exponential, asymmetric time evolution. This asymmetric evolution is a manifestation of irreversibility on the microphysical level. The Rigged Hilbert Space provides a mathematical theory.

# Resonances, Gamow Vectors and Time Asymmetric Quantum Theory

## Key claim

Decaying states can be represented by Gamow vectors with exponential, asymmetric time evolution, but only if the time evolution is restricted to a semigroup with $t \geq 0$ rather than the full unitary group with $-\infty < t < \infty$; the apparent "exponential catastrophe" of Gamow wave functions arises from incorrectly assuming the probability density is defined for all times beginning at $t \rightarrow -\infty$, whereas the emission process begins at a finite time $t_0 = 0$.

## Method

The authors construct a Rigged Hilbert Space (RHS) formulation $\Phi \subset \mathcal{H} \subset \Phi^{\times}$ in which two distinct RHS's are used: $\Phi_- \subset \mathcal{H} \subset \Phi_-^{\times}$ for prepared states and $\Phi_+ \subset \mathcal{H} \subset \Phi_+^{\times}$ for registered observables, with $\Phi_+$ and $\Phi_-$ chosen as Hardy class spaces of energy wave functions analytic in the upper or lower half of the complex energy plane on the second Riemann sheet of the analytically continued S-matrix. The Hamiltonian $H$ is self-adjoint on the Hilbert space, but its extension $H^{\times}$ to the dual space $\Phi_+^{\times}$ admits generalized eigenvectors with complex eigenvalues, giving Gamow vectors $|E_R - i\Gamma/2\rangle$ that evolve under a semigroup $U_+^{\times}(t) = e_+^{-iH^{\times}t}$ defined only for $0 \leq t < \infty$.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow vector eigenvalue is $z_R = E_R - i\frac{\Gamma}{2}$, where $E_R$ is the resonance energy and $\Gamma$ is the width, with lifetime $\tau = \hbar/\Gamma$.
- The Gamow vector time evolution is $e^{-iE_R t} e^{-\frac{\Gamma}{2}t}$ for $t \geq 0$ only.
- The Breit-Wigner energy distribution of a Gamow vector is $\langle^-E|\psi^G\rangle = i\sqrt{\frac{\Gamma}{2\pi}} \frac{1}{E-(E_R-i\frac{\Gamma}{2})}$ for $-\infty_{II} < E < \infty$ on the second sheet.
- The probability density for a Gamow wave function at distance $r$ is $|\psi^G(\vec{r},t)|^2 \sim e^{-\Gamma(t - mr/|\vec{p}|)}$ for $t \geq mr/|\vec{p}|$, where $|\vec{p}| = \sqrt{2mE_R}$, and zero for $t < mr/|\vec{p}|$.

## Context

This work extends the Rigged Hilbert Space formulation of quantum mechanics, which previously provided mathematical justification for Dirac kets and continuous basis vector expansions (circa 1965), to incorporate time asymmetry and decaying states. The theory provides a rigorous mathematical foundation for phenomenological effective theories that have been enormously successful, including the Weisskopf-Wigner approximation methods for atomic decaying states, the Lee-Oehme-Yang effective two-dimensional theory of the neutral Kaon system, and finite-dimensional models with complex effective Hamiltonian matrices used in nuclear physics. The complex basis vector expansion derived here allows one to mathematically isolate exponentially decaying states, with the Weisskopf-Wigner approximation corresponding to omitting the background integral term.
