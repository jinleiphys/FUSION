# Ground-state nuclear properties of neutron-rich copper isotopes and lepton capture rates in stellar matter

> This study consists of two separate investigations centered on neutron-rich isotopes of copper, utilizing two distinct nuclear models. In the first part, the nuclear ground-state properties of copper isotopes in the mass range 72 <= A <= 82 were analyzed using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. Quadrupole moment-constrained RMF calculations were carried out with DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 density-dependent interactions to compute the ground-state binding energies, charge radii, proton and neutron radii, quadrupole moments, and deformation parameters for the 71-82Cu isotopes. The results show good agreement with the limited experimental data available and previous theoretical predictions. In addition, potential energy curves were evaluated to investigate the ground-state geometrical configurations of these isotopes. The second part of the study is devoted to calculating lepton capture rates under stellar conditions. While earlier works have provided allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and unique first-forbidden (U1F) beta-decay rates for selected neutron-rich Cu isotopes in stellar environments, the corresponding lepton capture rates had not yet been computed. This paper presents, for the first time, those lepton capture rates.

# Ground-state nuclear properties of neutron-rich copper isotopes and lepton capture rates in stellar matter

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of lepton capture rates (electron capture and positron capture) for neutron-rich copper isotopes in stellar conditions, complementing previously published beta-decay rates for the same isotopes; however, the authors note that the comparison of calculated half-lives with measured data could be further improved by integrating rank 0 and 1 operators (non-unique forbidden transitions) which were not included in this work.

## Method

The study uses two distinct nuclear models: the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 density-dependent interactions for ground-state properties, and the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with schematic separable Gamow-Teller forces for lepton capture rates. Quadrupole moment-constrained RMF calculations were performed to generate potential energy curves and determine ground-state binding energies, charge radii, and deformation parameters for copper isotopes in the mass range 71-82. The pn-QRPA model computed allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden transition strengths, with lepton capture rates calculated over a temperature range of (0.01-30)×10⁹ K and stellar density range of (10-10¹¹) g/cm³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Majid et al. (2017) which calculated allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden beta-decay rates for neutron-rich copper isotopes but did not compute the corresponding lepton capture rates. The RMF ground-state calculations are compared with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1997) and experimental data from Audi et al. (2012). The pn-QRPA half-life calculations are compared with measurements by Hosmer et al. (2010) and theoretical results from Borzov (2005) using the DF3+CQRPA model, as well as from Pfeiffer et al. (2002). The results enable improved presupernova evolution simulations of massive stars by providing both electron capture and positron capture rates on neutron-rich copper isotopes, which the authors show dominate over competing beta-decay rates at high stellar temperatures.
