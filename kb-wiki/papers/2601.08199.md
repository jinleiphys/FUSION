# Chiral three-nucleon forces for the new local position-space two-nucleon potential in $\textit{ab initio}$ many-body calculations

> Three-nucleon force (3NF) plays an important role in understanding the structure of finite nuclei and the saturation properties of infinite nuclear matter. The chiral 3NF derived from the chiral effective field theory has been successful in $\textit{ab initio}$ studies of atomic nuclei. However, challenges remain, such as parameterizing low-energy constants and applying regulators. Most of established chiral nuclear forces have a nonlocal form in the momentum space. In this work, we construct local and hybrid local-nonlocal chiral 3NFs for the newly established Idaho local position-space two-nucleon potential, and calculate binding energies and radii of nuclei up to $^{132}$Sn. The two low-energy constants of 3NF are constrained by the ground-state energies of $^3$H and $^{16}$O, as suggested in a recent work. The chiral Hamiltonian obtained with the local-nonlocal regulator can simultaneously reproduce the experimental ground-state energies and charge radii of nuclei over a large range from $^4$He to $^{132}$Sn.

# Chiral three-nucleon forces for the new local position-space two-nucleon potential in *ab initio* many-body calculations

## Key claim

The authors construct chiral three-nucleon forces (3NF) at N²LO that complement the new local position-space two-nucleon potential from the Idaho group, and show that a hybrid local-nonlocal regularization scheme for the 3NF enables simultaneous reproduction of experimental ground-state energies and charge radii for nuclei from ⁴He to ¹³²Sn, whereas a purely local 3NF regulator underestimates charge radii systematically.

## Method

The chiral 3NF at N²LO is constructed with two low-energy constants cD and cE, constrained by the ground-state energies of ³H (calculated via no-core shell model in Jacobi coordinates) and ¹⁶O (calculated via in-medium similarity renormalization group in the Magnus formulation). The 2NF is the local position-space Idaho potential at N³LO with a position-space regulator cutoff Rπ = 1.2 fm, evolved via similarity renormalization group to a low-momentum scale λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹. Two regularization schemes for the 3NF are tested: a purely local regulator with cutoff Λ = 500 MeV, and a hybrid local-nonlocal regulator with Λ = 700 MeV and Λ′ = 500 MeV. Binding energies and charge radii are computed for closed-shell nuclei using single-reference IMSRG and for open-shell isotopes using valence-space IMSRG with ensemble normal ordering.

## Key numbers

- The ³H ground-state energy is calculated with Nmax = 44 harmonic oscillator shells at ℏΩ = 24 MeV; the χEFT truncation uncertainty at N²LO is 446 keV and at N³LO is 149 keV for the ³H ground-state energy.
- Optimal cD and cE values: for the local 3NF regulator, cD = −2.0 and cE = −0.541; for the local-nonlocal (lnl) 3NF regulator, cD = 6.0 and cE = 0.894.
- Calculated ³H ground-state energy: −8.78 MeV (local 3NF) and −8.73 MeV (lnl 3NF), compared to the experimental value of −8.482 MeV.
- Calculated ¹⁶O ground-state energy: −126.01 MeV (local 3NF) and −127.15 MeV (lnl 3NF), compared to the experimental value of −127.619 MeV.
- The change from a local 3NF regulator to a local-nonlocal 3NF regulator brings an approximately 7% increase in the charge radius for medium-mass nuclei.
- Deviations from experimental data for binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei from ⁴He to ¹³²Sn are below 2% when using the 2NF(local)+3NF(lnl) family.

## Context

This work extends the local position-space 2NF developed by the Idaho group (PhysRevC.107.034002) by constructing complementary chiral 3NFs, enabling *ab initio* calculations beyond the light-mass nuclei (A ≲ 16) accessible to quantum Monte Carlo methods. The strategy of using ¹⁶O ground-state energy as the second constraint on cD and cE follows the suggestion of Huther et al. (2021). The hybrid local-nonlocal regularization scheme was previously explored by Soma et al. (2021) and is shown here to be particularly effective for reproducing charge radii. The resulting Hamiltonian enables systematic studies of ground-state energies and charge radii across isotopic chains of oxygen, magnesium, silicon, sulfur, argon, and calcium, and provides charge density distributions for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca in excellent agreement with experimental data.
