# Coupling and higher-order effects in the 12C(d,p)13C and 13C(p,d)12C reactions

> Coupled channels calculations are performed for the 12C(d,p)13C and 13C(p,d)12C reactions between 7 and 60 MeV to study the effect of inelastic couplings in transfer reactions. The effect of treating transfer beyond Born approximation is also addressed. The coupling to the 12C 2+ state is found to change the peak cross-section by up to 15 %. Effects beyond Born approximation lead to a significant renormalization of the cross-sections, between 5 and 10 % for deuteron energies above 10 MeV, and larger than 10 % for lower energies. We also performed calculations including the remnant term in the transfer operator, which has a small impact on the 12C(d,p)13C(g.s.) and 13C(p,d)12C(g.s.) reactions. Above 30 MeV deuteron energy, the effect of the remnant term is larger than 10 % for the 12C(d,p)13C(3.09 MeV) reaction and is found to increase with decreasing neutron separation energy for the 3.09 MeV state of 13C. This is of importance for transfer reactions with weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

Inelastic couplings to the 12C 2+ state change the peak cross-section by up to 15%, and effects beyond Born approximation renormalize cross-sections by 5-10% for deuteron energies above 10 MeV and larger than 10% for lower energies; the remnant term in the transfer operator has a small impact on ground-state transitions but exceeds 10% above 30 MeV for the 12C(d,p)13C(3.09 MeV) reaction, with the effect increasing as neutron separation energy decreases.

## Method

Coupled Channels Born Approximation (CCBA) calculations were performed for 12C(d,p)13C and 13C(p,d)12C reactions between 7 and 60 MeV, using adiabatic deuteron potentials (the Adiabatic Coupled Channels, or ACC, method). The calculations included inelastic couplings to the 4.44 MeV 2+ state of 12C (treated as a rotational collective excitation with deformation length β2R = -1.54 fm) and the 3.09 MeV 1/2+ state of 13C (treated as a single-particle E1 excitation). Spectroscopic amplitudes were obtained from shell-model calculations in the psd model space using the PSDMK interaction. Additional Coupled Reaction Channels (CRC) calculations were performed to treat transfer beyond Born approximation, including the remnant term and non-orthogonality corrections.

## Key numbers

- Coupling to the 12C 2+ state changes the peak cross-section by up to 15% for the reactions studied.
- Effects beyond Born approximation (CRC vs ACC) lead to renormalization of 5-10% for deuteron energies above 10 MeV, and larger than 10% for energies below 10 MeV.
- For the 12C(d,p)13C(g.s.) and 13C(p,d)12C(g.s.) reactions, the remnant term effect is smaller than 5% for energies above 8 MeV.
- For the 12C(d,p)13C(3.09 MeV) reaction above 30 MeV deuteron energy, the remnant term effect is larger than 10%.
- At 30 MeV, the coupling changes the cross-section at the ADW peak angle by -3% for the transition to the 13C ground state and +22% for the transition to the 13C first excited state.
- The neutron separation energy of the 3.09 MeV state in 13C is 1.86 MeV, compared to 4.95 MeV for the ground state.
- For an artificially reduced binding energy of 0.5 MeV for a 1/2+ state, inclusion of the remnant term reduces the ADW peak cross-section by 15%.
- The computed B(E1) for the 13C 3.09 MeV state is 0.28 Weisskopf units, while the experimental value is 0.047 ± 0.010 W.u.
- The deformation length for the 12C 2+ state was β2R = -1.54 fm, derived from B(E2) = 39.7 ± 3.3 e²fm⁴.

## Context

This work extends the DWBA reanalysis of Liu et al. (2004) for the same reactions, which used adiabatic deuteron potentials and found an average spectroscopic factor of 0.61 ± 0.09 for the ground-state transition. The present study quantifies systematic uncertainties from neglecting inelastic couplings and higher-order effects in that analysis. The results are important for transfer reactions with radioactive beams and weakly bound nuclei, particularly for extracting spectroscopic factors from reactions involving loosely bound s1/2 orbitals such as neutron halo states. The authors note that Keeley et al. (2004) performed CDCC calculations for the 12C(d,p)13C reaction at 15 and 30 MeV including the 12C 2+ excitation, achieving similar agreement with data.
