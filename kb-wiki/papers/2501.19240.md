# Hydrodynamic attractor in periodically driven ultracold quantum gases

> Hydrodynamic attractors are a universal phenomenon of strongly interacting systems that describe the hydrodynamic-like evolution far from local equilibrium. In particular, the rapid hydrodynamization of the Quark-Gluon Plasma is behind the remarkable success of hydrodynamic models of high-energy nuclear collisions. So far, hydrodynamic attractors have been explored only in systems undergoing monotonic expansion, such as Bjorken flow. We demonstrate that a system with an oscillating isotropic expansion exhibits a novel cyclic attractor behavior. This phenomenon can be investigated in ultracold quantum gases with externally modulated scattering length, offering a new avenue for experimentally discovering hydrodynamic attractors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a system undergoing periodic expansion and contraction exhibits a novel cyclic hydrodynamic attractor, which does not converge to the Navier-Stokes prediction even at late times, unlike the monotonic expansion attractors studied previously.

## Method

The authors employ Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) hydrodynamics to describe the linear response of bulk pressure in a periodically driven ultracold Fermi gas, and relativistic kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for massive particles to study the nonlinear regime. The periodic drive is realized by modulating the inverse scattering length \(a^{-1}(t)=a^{-1}_0(1+A\sin\omega t)\), which is equivalent to homogeneous isotropic expansion with rate \(\theta = 3a(t)\partial_t a^{-1}(t)\). The bulk pressure \(\Pi(t)\) is computed and plotted against its Navier-Stokes expectation \(\Pi_\mathrm{NS}(t)\) to visualize deviations from Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* For slow drives \(\omega\tau_\zeta \ll 1\), different initial conditions collapse onto a narrow ellipse along the Navier-Stokes limit; the width of the ellipse is \(\Delta\Pi/(3A\omega\zeta) = 2\omega\tau_\zeta/[1+(\omega\tau_\zeta)^2] \leq 1\), with maximum width when \(\omega = \tau_\zeta^{-1}\).
* The relaxation time near a scattering resonance scales as \(\tau_\zeta \simeq 0.7\hbar/k_BT\).
* For massive kinetic theory with \(m/T=10\), \(\omega\tau_R=0.5\), and \(\zeta/(\tau_R P_\text{eq})=0.39\) at \(t=0\), convergence onto the attractor occurs within \(t \simeq 3\tau_R^0\).
* The bulk susceptibility \(\zeta/(P_\text{eq}\tau_R)\) in massive kinetic theory is a monotonically increasing function of \(m/T\) between zero and \(2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors, previously limited to monotonically expanding systems such as Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1982), to periodically driven systems. It builds on the proposal to measure attractor behavior with ultracold atoms (Fujii, 2024) and on microscopic computations of bulk viscosity and relaxation times for strongly interacting Fermi gases (Enss, 2019; Fujii, 2024). The cyclic attractor enables experimental determination of the relaxation time \(\tau_\zeta\) from the phase shift \(\phi\) at long times, and can be tested in existing ultracold atom experiments with periodic modulation of the scattering length via Feshbach resonances (Chin, 2010).
