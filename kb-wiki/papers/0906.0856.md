# Neutrino deuteron reaction in the heating mechanism of core-collapse supernovae

> We examine a potential role of the neutrino deuteron reactions in the mechanism of supernova explosion by evaluating the energy transfer cross section for the neutrino heating. We calculate the energy loss rate due to the neutrino absorptions through the charged-current process as well as the neutrino scattering through the neutral-current process. In so doing, we adopt a detailed evaluation of cross sections for the neutrino deuteron reactions with the phenomenological Lagrangian approach. We find the energy transfer cross section for the deuteron is larger than those for $^{3}$H, $^{3}$He and $^{4}$He for neutrino temperatures (T$_ν$ $\sim 4$ MeV) relevant to supernova core. Because of the low energy threshold for the deuteron breakup, the energy transfer rate rapidly increases from low temperature, T$_ν$ $\sim 1$ MeV. This suggests that the neutrino deuteron reactions may contribute effectively to the heating mechanism during the dissociation of irons into light elements and nucleons in the shocked material of supernova core.

## Key claim

The neutrino-deuteron reactions may contribute effectively to the neutrino heating mechanism in core-collapse supernovae, but only if deuterons appear abundantly in the heating region, as can occur during the dissociation of iron into light elements and nucleons in the shocked material.

## Method

The authors calculated total cross sections and energy transfer cross sections for charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino and antineutrino reactions on deuterons, including breakup and elastic scattering channels. They used the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) with the ANLV18 potential to generate deuteron and two-nucleon scattering wave functions, incorporating one-body impulse terms and two-body meson exchange currents. The energy transfer rates were thermally averaged using a Fermi-Dirac distribution for neutrino energies with zero chemical potential, parameterized by neutrino temperature.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino CC reaction cross section on deuteron is about 1/3 of the neutrino-nucleon CC cross section at E_nu = 10 MeV, and about 1/2 at E_nu = 50 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV, giving a low breakup threshold.
- The average energy transfer cross section for the CC reaction exceeds 10^-40 cm^2 MeV at T_nu >= 4 MeV.
- The heating rate due to nu_d reactions for nu_e and anti-nu_e amounts to 99.1 MeV/sec per nucleon, assuming T_nu_e = T_anti-nu_e = 5 MeV.
- The heating rate due to nu_d reactions for mu and tau type neutrinos is 55.6 MeV/sec per nucleon at T_nu_mu = 10 MeV.
- These values are 25-44% of the heating rate of 223 MeV/sec per nucleon due to neutrino-nucleon reactions at the same neutrino temperature of 5 MeV for nu_e/anti-nu_e.
- The average energy transfer cross section for nu_d CC at T_nu = 4 MeV is 1.71 x 10^-40 MeV cm^2; at T_nu = 5 MeV it is 3.67 x 10^-40 MeV cm^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Haxton (1988) on neutrino heating through neutrino-nucleus interactions for 4He and 56Fe, and by O'Connor et al. (2007) on triton and 3He neutrino reaction rates. It also builds on the finding by Sumiyoshi and Ropke (2008) that deuterons can appear abundantly in supernova environments. The results suggest that deuteron reactions should be included in multi-dimensional supernova simulations, particularly those exploring the standing accretion shock-wave instability (SASI) studied by Ohnishi et al. (2006), where light elements may be present with sufficient advection time for neutrino absorption.
