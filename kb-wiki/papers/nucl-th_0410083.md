# Multi-deformed Configurations and Shape Coexistence for Superheavy Elements

> We use the considered axial deformed relativistic mean field theory to perform systematical calculations for Z=112 and 104 isotopic chains with force parameters NL3, NL-SH and NL-Z2 sets. Three deformed chains (oblate, moderate prolate and super-deformed chain) are found for Z=112 and 104 isotopic chains. It is found that there is a chain of super-deformed nuclei which can increase the stability of superheavy nuclei in the Z=112 isotopic chain. Shape coexistence is found for Z=112, 104 isotopic chain and the position is defined. For moderate prolate deformed chains of Z=112 and 104, there is shell closure at N=184 for moderate prolate deformed chain. For oblate deformed chain of Z=112, the shell closure appears around at N=176. For super-deformed chains of Z=112 and 104, the position of shell closure have strong parameter dependence. There is shell anomalism for oblate or superdeformed nuclei.

# Multi-deformed Configurations and Shape Coexistence for Superheavy Elements

## Key claim

Using axial deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory without constraint calculations, three distinct deformed chains (oblate, moderate prolate, and super-deformed) exist systematically across entire isotopic chains of superheavy elements Z=112 and Z=104, not just in isolated nuclei. The super-deformed chain can become the ground state and increase stability for Z=112 isotopes when the neutron number is sufficiently large, but this stabilization does not occur for Z=104 isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed systematic axial deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for Z=112 (neutron numbers 160 to 200) and Z=104 (neutron numbers 152 to 198) isotopic chains using three force parameter sets: NL3, NL-SH, and NL-Z2. The coupled RMF equations were solved via harmonic basis expansion with N_f=12 and N_b=20 basis states, and pairing was included using the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps Δ_n = Δ_p = 11.2/√A MeV. For each nucleus, the calculation was performed without constraint, allowing the self-consistent iteration to converge to different deformed solutions depending on the initial quadrupole deformation β₀.

## Key numbers

- For Z=112 isotopic chain, three deformed chains are found: oblate (β₂ ≤ -0.3), moderate prolate, and super-deformed (β₂ ≥ 0.4), with the super-deformed chain exceptions of β₂ = 0.34, 0.33, 0.32 for N=196, 198, 200 in NL-SH calculation.
- For Z=104 isotopic chain, super-deformed configurations appear with neutron number N ≥ 168 (for NL3 and NL-Z2) or N ≥ 170 (for NL-SH).
- Binding energy differences between deformed configurations range from -14.5 MeV to 8 MeV across the isotopic chains.
- The gap between neutron and proton rms radii is 0.15 to 0.38 fm for Z=112 and 0.17 to 0.45 fm for Z=104 isotopic chains.
- NL-SH underestimates rms radii by about 0.05 fm and NL-Z2 overestimates rms radii by about 0.1 fm relative to NL3 calculations for the same deformed configuration.
- For Z=112, the super-deformed chain becomes the ground state when N ≥ 188 (NL3), N ≥ 190 (NL-SH), and N ≥ 180 (NL-Z2).
- Shell closure is found at N=184 for moderate prolate deformed chains of both Z=112 and Z=104, and at N=176 for the oblate deformed chain of Z=112.

## Context

This work extends prior constrained RMF calculations by Ren et al. (2002-2003) that predicted shape coexistence and super-deformation in individual superheavy nuclei, by demonstrating that multiple deformed configurations exist systematically across entire isotopic chains without constraint. The results confirm the suggestion by Bohr and Mottelson that deformation can increase stability of heavy nuclei, but show this occurs only in specific regions of the super-deformed chain. The findings enable future studies of shape coexistence regions around magic numbers (particularly N=184 and N=176) and motivate further investigation of shell anomalism in oblate and super-deformed configurations, where shell closure positions show strong parameter dependence.
