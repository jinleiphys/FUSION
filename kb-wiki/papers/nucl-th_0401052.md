# Effects of color superconductivity on the structure and formation of compact stars

> We show that if color superconducting quark matter forms in hybrid or quark stars it is possible to satisfy most of recent observational boundaries on masses and radii of compact stellar objects. An energy of the order of $10^{53}$ erg is released in the conversion from a (metastable) hadronic star into a (stable) hybrid or quark star in presence of a color superconducting phase. If the conversion occurs immediately after the deleptonization of the proto-neutron star, the released energy can help Supernovae to explode. If the conversion is delayed the energy released can power a Gamma Ray Burst. A delay between the Supernova and the subsequent Gamma Ray Burst is possible, in agreement with the delay proposed in recent analysis of astrophysical data.

## Key claim

If color superconducting quark matter forms in hybrid or quark stars, it is possible to satisfy most recent observational boundaries on masses and radii of compact stellar objects, provided the superconducting gap is treated as dependent on the chemical potential rather than constant.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for hadronic matter using relativistic non-linear models (GM1, GM3) and for quark matter using a MIT-bag model extended to include a diquark condensate in the Color-Flavor Locking (CFL) phase, with the gap obtained from solving the gap equation. The two phases are connected via Gibbs equilibrium conditions, and the resulting mass-radius relations are compared with observational constraints from X-ray satellites. For the delayed conversion scenario, quantum tunneling nucleation rates are computed in WKB approximation to determine the critical mass for a given nucleation time.

## Key numbers

- Energy released in conversion from a 1.4 solar mass hadronic star to hybrid or quark star can reach up to 333 foe (1 foe = 10^51 erg) for the GM1 hadronic model with B^(1/4)=160 MeV and the largest gap (Delta_4)
- For the delayed conversion scenario with surface tension sigma=10 MeV/fm^2 and mean lifetime tau=1 year, the critical mass M_cr ranges from 1.12 to 1.65 solar masses depending on model parameters
- Hybrid and quark stars in the proposed scheme have masses in the range 1.2-1.8 solar masses and radii approximately 8.5-10 km
- The energy released in the delayed conversion with CFL phase can be one order of magnitude larger than the corresponding energy with unpaired quark matter (Delta=0)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deconfinement in compact stars by Benvenuto and Pons (various years) and the authors' own previous work (noiapj) by incorporating a chemical-potential dependent superconducting gap from the CFL phase. The results address the apparent contradiction between observations indicating very compact objects (radius less than about 10 km) and those suggesting larger radii (about 12 km or more), as reported by Sanwal et al. (2002), Cottam et al. (2002), and others. The mechanism enables a possible delay between a supernova and a subsequent gamma-ray burst, consistent with analyses by Amati et al. (2000) and Reeves et al. (2002).
