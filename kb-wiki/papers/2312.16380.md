# Analytic continuation of the finite-volume three-particle amplitudes

> One has to study multivariable scattering amplitudes to extract properties of the three-body states from the generalizations of the Lüscher finite-volume formalism. In particular, a three-body amplitude obtained from a Lattice QCD calculation must be analytically continued to unphysical Riemann sheets of the complex energy plane, where resonances of interest appear as poles. In this article, we provide a pedagogical overview of a method for solving and analytically continuing the on-shell integral equations describing a three-body elastic scattering process. We illustrate the procedure by applying it to a relativistic system of three identical bosons characterized by pair-wise interactions. We describe the analytic structure of the reaction amplitude, show how to access its physical and unphysical Riemann sheets, and analyze the behavior of the three-body spectrum under variations of the interaction parameters.

## Key claim

The paper provides a pedagogical overview of a method for solving and analytically continuing the on-shell integral equations describing a three-body elastic scattering process, but the method is illustrated only for a simplified model where short-range three-body couplings (the three-body K matrix) are set to zero, meaning the scattering is generated solely by repeated on-shell particle exchanges.

## Method

The author solves the ladder integral equation for the S-wave projected three-body amplitude of three identical scalar bosons, using a low-energy approximation for the two-body amplitude parameterized by a scattering length a. The analytic continuation to complex energies is achieved by deforming the integration contour in the complex momentum plane to avoid singularities of the integration kernel, with the contour shape defining which Riemann sheet of the amplitude is accessed. The self-consistency criterion requires that the integration path detour the domain of non-analyticity that it itself defines, and different contours (bypassing the two-body pole or branch point from top or bottom) yield the amplitude on different Riemann sheets associated with the bound-state-particle and three-body thresholds.

## Key numbers

* The two-body amplitude uses a scattering length a as the only parameter, with the amplitude given by Mc_2(k') = 16 pi sqrt(sigma_{k'}) / (-1/a - i sqrt(sigma_{k'}/4 - m^2)).
* The three-body threshold occurs at s_{3phi} = (3m)^2, and the bound-state-particle threshold at s_{phi b} = (m + m_b)^2 where m_b^2 = 4(m^2 - 1/a^2).
* The integration upper limit is k_max = k_l, the pseudo-threshold value of the two-body amplitude, given by k_l = lambda^{1/2}(s, m^2, 0) / (2 sqrt(s)).
* The three-body spectrum is plotted using the generalized binding momentum kappa = sign(Re Delta E) sqrt(|m Re Delta E|) versus 1/(ma), for the first three states (trimers), with parameters ma = -6 and (E/m)^2 = 9.01 - 0.01i used in the contour example.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic three-body techniques of Hetherington and Schick (1965), Brayshaw (1968), and Glockle (1978) to the relativistic context, building on the three-body quantization condition formalism of Hansen and collaborators (2014-2015). The method is directly applicable to infinite-volume analyses of lattice QCD three-body spectra, enabling the extraction of resonance poles for states like omega(782), pi_1(1600), or T_cc^+(3872) from finite-volume calculations. The results recover both the known non-relativistic Efimov phenomenon (Efimov, 1970) and finite-volume results (Romero-Lopez, 2019), and the author notes that generalization to coupled-channel problems with non-degenerate particles is underway.
