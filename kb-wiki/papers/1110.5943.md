# Nuclear Physics from Lattice QCD

> I review recent progress in the development of Lattice QCD into a calculational tool for nuclear physics. Lattice QCD is currently the only known way of solving QCD in the low-energy regime, and it promises to provide a solid foundation for the structure and interactions of nuclei directly from QCD.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD is currently the only known way to solve QCD in the low-energy regime, but present-day calculations are performed at unphysical light quark masses yielding pion masses of approximately 200 MeV, requiring further extrapolation to the physical pion mass and not yet including strong isospin breaking or electromagnetism.

## Method

The authors review the lattice QCD methodology where space-time is discretized into a four-dimensional grid and the QCD path integral over quark and gluon fields is evaluated using Monte Carlo methods in Euclidean space-time. Calculations are performed at multiple lattice volumes and lattice spacings, with effective field theory descriptions used to extrapolate to the continuum and infinite-volume limits. The review covers calculations of correlation functions for meson-meson scattering, two-body bound states including the H-dibaryon and deuteron, and nucleon-nucleon interactions, using Luscher's method to extract scattering amplitudes from finite-volume energy eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in present-day dynamical LQCD calculations: \(m_\pi \sim 400\) MeV, with lattice spacings \(b \sim 0.1\) fm and volumes with spatial extent \(L \sim 4\) fm
- LQCD prediction for the \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering length at physical pion mass: \(m_{\pi^+} a_{\pi\pi}^{I=2} = -0.04330 \pm 0.00042\) (mixed-action \(n_f=2+1\) calculation)
- ETM collaboration \(n_f=2\) result for \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering length: \(m_{\pi^+} a_{\pi\pi}^{I=2} = -0.04385 \pm 0.00028 \pm 0.00038\)
- H-dibaryon binding energy at \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV: \(B_H^{(L=\infty)} = 13.2 \pm 1.8 \pm 4.0\) MeV
- \(\Xi^-\Xi^-\) binding energy at \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV: \(B_{\Xi^-\Xi^-}^{(L=\infty)} = 14.0 \pm 1.4 \pm 6.7\) MeV
- Deuteron binding energy at \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV: \(B_d^{(L=\infty)} = 11 \pm 5 \pm 12\) MeV
- Di-neutron binding energy at \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV: \(B_{nn}^{(L=\infty)} = 7.1 \pm 5.2 \pm 7.3\) MeV
- Roy equation determination of s-wave \(\pi\pi\) scattering lengths: \(m_{\pi^+} a_{\pi\pi}^{I=0} = 0.220 \pm 0.005\), \(m_{\pi^+} a_{\pi\pi}^{I=2} = -0.0444 \pm 0.0010\)

## Context

This review extends the program of using lattice QCD to compute nuclear observables directly from the fundamental theory of the strong interaction, building on earlier work by Luscher (1986, 1990) on finite-volume methods for extracting scattering amplitudes and by the NPLQCD and HALQCD collaborations on multi-baryon systems. The results enable future matching to traditional nuclear many-body methods such as the No-Core Shell Model and Green's Function Monte Carlo, allowing predictions for larger nuclear systems with quantifiable uncertainties, and provide constraints on exotic systems like the H-dibaryon and \(\Xi^-\Xi^-\) that are difficult to access experimentally.
