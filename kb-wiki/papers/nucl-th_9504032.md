# NUCLEAR SUPPRESSION OF HADROPRODUCTION

> We demonstrate that the main part of the observed nuclear suppression of dilepton and charmonium production, especially the x-dependence of the suppression, can be explained by the absorption of fast initial partons. We assume the absorption to be x1-dependent and determine the empirical form of this dependence. Several reactions at different energies are described with the same parametrization of the absorption cross section. The factorization theorem constraints and some alternative models are discussed. The obtained description of data allows to make conclusions about the origin of nuclear effects in hadro- and electroproduction. In particular, it is concluded that the parton recombination and the excess pion contribution are unimportant in nuclei and an indication of excess gluons at $x_{2}\sim 0.1$ is observed.

## Key claim

The observed nuclear suppression of dilepton and charmonium production, particularly its dependence on the Feynman variable \(x_F\), can be explained primarily by the absorption of fast initial partons in the nucleus, assuming the absorption cross section depends on the projectile parton momentum fraction \(x_1\). The paper claims that this mechanism, rather than parton recombination or excess pions, is the dominant effect, and that the data provide an indication of excess gluons at \(x_2 \sim 0.1\).

## Method

The author assumes an \(x_1\)-dependent parton-nucleon absorption cross section of the form \(\sigma^{i}_{abs}(x_1) = \sigma_{max}^{i} \sin^6(x_1 \pi/2)\), with \(\sigma_{max}^{g} = 30\) mb for protons and \(\sigma_{max}^{q} = (4/9)\sigma_{max}^{g}\) for quarks, and a reduced value for pions (0.4 times the proton value). This absorption is incorporated into an eikonal factor \(F\) that multiplies the projectile parton distribution in a weak-factorization framework. For charmonium production, a constant final-state interaction cross section \(\sigma_f = 5.8\) mb is also included. The model is applied to \(J/\psi\) production by protons at 200 and 800 GeV and by pions at 200 GeV, as well as Drell-Yan (DY) dilepton production by pions at 140 and 286 GeV and by protons at 800 GeV, using free nucleon structure functions and, for DY, the EMC-effect in target structure functions.

## Key numbers

*   \(\sigma_{max}^{g} = 30\) mb for protons, the maximum value of the gluon-nucleon absorption cross section at \(x_1=1\).
*   \(\sigma_f = 5.8\) mb, the constant cross section used to simulate the \(x\)-independent final-state interaction of produced heavy quarks in charmonium production.
*   For DY production by pions at 140 GeV, the model yields \(\chi^2 = 1.1\) for the \(x_2\)-dependence of the nuclear ratio \(R\) and \(\chi^2 = 0.5\) for the mass dependence, compared to \(\chi^2 = 2.3\) and \(\chi^2 = 2.6\) for the energy-loss model, respectively.
*   For DY production by protons at 800 GeV, the model yields \(\chi^2 = 0.7\) for the \(x_2\)-dependence of \(R\).
*   An excess of gluons in tungsten at \(0.06 < x_2 < 0.15\), carrying about 3% of the total momentum, is inferred to improve the charmonium description, yielding a \(\chi^2\) of about 1 at both 200 and 800 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier model (Akulinichev 1993) which used a constant parton absorption cross section, and contrasts with the energy-loss (EL) model (Eloss) and the parton-recombination model (recomb1, recomb2). It argues that the strong factorization theorem is violated in hadron-nucleus reactions and that weak factorization is more appropriate. The paper enables a self-consistent description of nuclear suppression in charmonium and dilepton production across different energies and projectiles, and suggests that the missing parton momentum in nuclei (the EMC-effect) may be carried by excess gluons rather than excess pions, a point that distinguishes the binding correction model (Akulinichev, Kulagin, Vagradov) from pionic models (Magda).
