# Flavor Asymmetry in Hyperons and Drell-Yan Processes

> SU(3), baryon octets and a meson cloud model are compared for the flavor asymmetry of sea quarks in the $Σ^+$, as an example. Large differences are found, especially between SU(3) and the meson cloud model. We suggest Drell-Yan measurements of $Σ^{+}-p$ and $Σ^{+}- d$ to test the prediction of various models. We use the meson cloud model to predict both valence and sea quark distributions.

## Key claim

The flavor asymmetry of sea quarks in the $\Sigma^+$ baryon differs dramatically between SU(3) symmetry predictions and the meson cloud model, with the ratio $\bar{d}/\bar{u}$ in the $\Sigma^+$ possibly being less than 1 (SU(3)) or greater than 1 (meson cloud model), and Drell-Yan measurements on $\Sigma^+$ beams could distinguish between these competing theoretical frameworks.

## Method

The authors compared three theoretical approaches for predicting sea quark flavor asymmetry in the $\Sigma^+$ baryon: SU(3) symmetry, a baryon octet $\otimes$ meson octet model with SU(3) coupling ratio F/D = 0.6, and a meson cloud model using the Sullivan process with time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame. The meson cloud calculation included pseudoscalar coupling, Gaussian form factors with cutoff $\Lambda = 1.08$ GeV, coupling constants from Dumbrajs, Koch, and Pilkhun, and assumed 20% of meson momenta carried by sea quarks, while neglecting masses above 1700 MeV and $\Delta \bar{K}$ states.

## Key numbers

- At $x \sim 0.2$, the measured proton ratio $\bar{u}/\bar{d} \approx 1/2$ (NA51) or 2/3 (E866)
- At $x \sim 0.2$, the measured proton ratio $\bar{s}/(\bar{u} + \bar{d}) \approx 1/4$
- SU(3) prediction for $\bar{d}/\bar{u}(\Sigma^+) \sim 4/3$ at $x \sim 0.2$
- Baryon octet model prediction for $\bar{d}/\bar{u}(\Sigma^+) = 0.54$ at $x \sim 0.2$
- Meson cloud model prediction for $\bar{d}/\bar{u}(\Sigma^+) \sim 0.3$ at $x \sim 0.2$
- Meson cloud model predicts $\bar{r} \equiv \bar{d}/\bar{u}$ in the $\Sigma^+$ begins at approximately 1 at small $x$ and climbs to 2 at $x \sim 0.35$
- Quark-diquark model predicts $s/u(\Sigma^+)$ is more than three times the SU(3) value at $x \sim 0.7$

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model explanation for the proton's flavor asymmetry, originally proposed by Thomas and confirmed by the NA51 and E866 Drell-Yan experiments, to hyperon systems. The paper contrasts SU(3) symmetry predictions with the baryon octet $\otimes$ meson octet model and the meson cloud model, finding that Drell-Yan measurements using $\Sigma^+$ beams on proton and deuteron targets could resolve which model correctly describes sea quark distributions in hyperons. The calculations use the Sullivan process formalism developed by Speth and collaborators, and the results enable experimental tests at facilities capable of producing hyperon beams.
