# A Lattice Test of 1/N_c Baryon Mass Relations

> 1/N_c baryon mass relations are compared with lattice simulations of baryon masses using different values of the light-quark masses, and hence different values of SU(3) flavor-symmetry breaking. The lattice data clearly display both the 1/N_c and SU(3) flavor-symmetry breaking hierarchies. The validity of 1/N_c baryon mass relations derived without assuming approximate SU(3) flavor-symmetry also can be tested by lattice data at very large values of the strange quark mass. The 1/N_c expansion constrains the form of discretization effects; these are suppressed by powers of 1/N_c by taking suitable combinations of masses. This 1/N_c scaling is explicitly demonstrated in the present work.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that existing lattice QCD simulations of ground-state baryon masses at N_c=3 already exhibit the predicted 1/N_c and SU(3) flavor-symmetry breaking hierarchies, but notes that more accurate simulations are needed to test the most suppressed mass combinations, and that the 1/N_c expansion constrains discretization effects at finite lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors compared twelve 1/N_c baryon mass relations (eight from Jenkins and Manohar (2004) assuming perturbative SU(3) breaking, and four from Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994) assuming only isospin symmetry) against lattice QCD data from the LHP Collaboration. The lattice calculation used a mixed-action approach with domain-wall valence propagators on Asqtad-improved dynamical MILC gauge ensembles at a single lattice spacing a ~ 0.125 fm and fixed spatial volume L ~ 2.5 fm, with six ensembles having pion and kaon masses ranging from {293,586} MeV to {758,758} MeV.

## Key numbers

- R_1 (the average O(N_c) baryon mass) ranges from 192(1) GeV to 242(1) GeV across the six ensembles, consistent with O(1000) MeV expectation
- R_2 (the hyperfine splitting, O(1/N_c)) ranges from -12.2(5) GeV to -8.2(4) GeV, showing clear 1/N_c suppression relative to R_1
- R_3 (O(epsilon)) ranges from -8.07(20) GeV to 0 at the SU(3) symmetric point, with R_3/epsilon giving O(300) MeV magnitude
- R_4 (O(epsilon/N_c)) ranges from -0.214(10) GeV to 0, suppressed relative to R_3 by an additional factor of 1/N_c
- R_A through R_D (all O(1/N_c^2)) are all consistent with zero within errors, with values typically below 30 MeV
- The SU(3) breaking parameter epsilon = (M_K^2 - M_pi^2)/Lambda_chi^2 ranges from 0 to 0.2579 GeV^2 across the ensembles

## Context

This work extends the 1/N_c baryon mass analysis of Jenkins and Manohar (2004) and Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994) from experimental data to lattice QCD simulations, enabling tests of the 1/N_c expansion as a function of quark masses and SU(3) breaking strength. The results confirm the earlier experimental findings of Jenkins and Manohar (2004) and demonstrate that the 1/N_c hierarchy holds even at unphysical quark masses. The paper enables future lattice studies to test the most suppressed mass combinations with improved precision, and to explore the crossover from light-quark to heavy-quark behavior in the m_s to infinity limit.
