# Semiclassical Analysis of the Supershell Effect in Reflection-Asymmetric Superdeformed Oscillator

> An oscillatory pattern in the smoothed quantum spectrum, which is unique for single-particle motions in a reflection-asymmetric superdeformed oscillator potential, is investigated by means of the semiclassical theory of shell structure. Clear correspondence between the oscillating components of the smoothed level density and the classical periodic orbits is found. It is shown that an interference effect between two families of the short periodic orbits, called supershell effect, develops with increasing reflection-asymmetric deformations. Possible origins of this enhancement phenomena as well as quantum signatures of period-multipling bifurcations are discussed in connection with stabilities of the classical periodic orbits.

## Key claim

The supershell effect, an interference pattern between two families of short periodic orbits in the smoothed level density, develops and becomes more significant with increasing reflection-asymmetric (octupole) deformation, contrary to the expectation that shell effects would weaken as degeneracies are reduced.

## Method

The authors model single-particle motion in a reflection-asymmetric superdeformed oscillator potential using a Hamiltonian with an axially-symmetric 2:1 deformed harmonic oscillator plus a doubly-stretched octupole (Y₃₀) deformation term. They apply the Gutzwiller trace formula and the Einstein-Brillouin-Keller (EBK) torus quantization method to compute the smoothed level density, and analyze classical periodic orbits via Poincaré maps and the monodromy method to identify bifurcations and stability changes as functions of the octupole deformation parameter λ₃₀.

## Key numbers

- The frequency ratio for the superdeformed oscillator is ω_⊥/ω_z = 2, with ω_x = ω_y ≡ ω_⊥ = 2ω_z ≡ 2ω_sh.
- The mean level density for the SD oscillator is given by ḡ(E) = E²/(8(ħω_sh)³).
- A period-tripling bifurcation of orbit A occurs at λ₃₀ ≈ 0.36.
- A period-doubling bifurcation of orbit B occurs at λ₃₀ ≈ 0.4.
- The degeneracy of the N-th shell in the SD oscillator is d_N = ([N/2]+1)([N/2]+2)/2 + 3/32, where [*] is the Gauss symbol.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical theory of shell structure originally developed by Balian and Bloch (1972) and the supershell concept observed in metal clusters by Nishioka, Hansen and Mottelson (1990). It builds on the suggestion by Nazarewicz and Dobaczewski (1992) regarding the odd-even effect in the shell quantum number N_sh for octupole instability in superdeformed nuclei, and on earlier work by Arita and Matsuyanagi (1993) and Mizutori and Nakatsukasa (1993). The analysis enables a semiclassical interpretation of the enhancement of supershell structure with octupole deformation, relevant to experimental investigations of reflection-asymmetric superdeformed nuclei.
