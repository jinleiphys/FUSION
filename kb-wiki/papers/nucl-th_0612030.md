# Transport model analysis of the transverse momentum and rapidity dependence of pion interferometry at SPS energies

> Based on the UrQMD transport model, the transverse momentum and the rapidity dependence of the Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii $R_L$, $R_O$, $R_S$ as well as the cross term $R_{OL}$ at SPS energies are investigated and compared with the experimental NA49 and CERES data. The rapidity dependence of the $R_L$, $R_O$, $R_S$ is weak while the $R_{OL}$ is significantly increased at large rapidities and small transverse momenta. The HBT "life-time" issue (the phenomenon that the calculated $\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}$ value is larger than the correspondingly extracted experimental data) is also present at SPS energies.

## Key claim
Using the UrQMD transport model, the authors find that the calculated HBT radii \(R_O\) at large transverse momentum and \(R_S\) at small transverse momentum are somewhat larger than the NA49 and CERES experimental data at SPS energies, and that the "HBT-puzzle" (the calculated \(\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}\) being larger than data) persists at these lower energies, though the overall transverse-momentum and rapidity dependences are in reasonable agreement with data.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v2.2) transport model, which includes hadronic and string degrees of freedom in cascade mode (no potential interactions), to simulate central Pb+Pb and Pb+Au collisions at beam energies from 20 to 160 A GeV. They then analyzed the three-dimensional two-pion correlation functions with the CRAB program (v3.0\(\beta\)), fitting them to a standard Gaussian form in the Bertsch-Pratt ("Out-Side-Long") coordinate system to extract the HBT radii \(R_L\), \(R_O\), \(R_S\), and the cross term \(R_{OL}\). The results were compared with preliminary NA49 and CERES experimental data as functions of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers
- At small \(k_T\) values, the calculated \(R_L\) and \(R_S\) values are seen up to 25% lower than the NA49 data (central Pb+Pb at 20-160 A GeV, \(0<Y_{\pi\pi}<0.5\)).
- The deviation of calculated \(R_O\) from data is strongest in most central collisions, with the calculated \(R_O\) values larger than both NA49 and CERES data, especially at relatively large \(k_T\).
- For the rapidity dependence of \(R_O\) compared with NA49 data, the deviation is up to 20%.
- At beam energy 160 A GeV, adopting a zero formation time for strings (\(\tau_s=0\) fm/\(c\)) makes the \(k_T\) fall-off of the HBT radii steeper and increases \(R_S\) values, bringing \(\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}\) at \(k_T<100\) MeV/\(c\) into agreement with the measured "duration time" data from NA49 and CERES.
- The cross term \(R_{OL}^{2}\) increases significantly at large rapidities and small transverse momenta, approaching zero as \(k_T\) approaches 0 MeV/\(c\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of system size and centrality dependence of HBT radii at RHIC energies (Li et al., 2006) down to the SPS energy regime (20-160 A GeV). It directly compares with the rapidity-dependent HBT measurements from the NA49 collaboration (Kniege, 2004, 2006) and the CERES collaboration (Adamova, 2002). The finding that the "HBT-puzzle" (large \(R_O/R_S\) ratio) persists at SPS energies, as previously reported at RHIC by most models (e.g., Soff, 2001; Molnar, 2002; Hirano, 2002), suggests that the omission of string-string interactions and potential interactions in transport models may be responsible, and that a more careful treatment of resonance production and lifetime is needed to consistently explain both single-particle flows and two-particle correlations.
