# Contributions of vector meson photoproduction to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule

> An improved version of a recently developed quark model approach to vector meson photoproduction is applied to the investigation of contributions of vector meson photoproduction to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule. We find that the sum rule converges at a few GeV's. Contributions to the proton channel are found to be small while to the neutron are relatively large.

# Contributions of vector meson photoproduction to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule

## Key claim
The vector meson photoproduction contributions to the GDH sum rule converge at a few GeV, with the cross section difference becoming negligible by approximately 6 GeV. The total contribution to the proton sum rule is small (+0.26 μb) while the contribution to the neutron sum rule is relatively large (-2.05 μb), though the abstract does not specify that these values represent sums over all vector meson channels and that the convergence relies on spin-dependent terms being suppressed by the quark model form factors and Pomeron exchange being spin-independent at high energies.

## Method
The authors applied a quark model approach with an effective Lagrangian to vector meson (ω, ρ, and φ) photoproduction, incorporating s- and u-channel resonance excitations, t-channel light meson exchange (pion exchange for ω, σ exchange for ρ), and t-channel Pomeron exchange for neutral vector meson production. The 12 independent helicity amplitudes were computed for each reaction channel, and the cross section difference Δσ = σ₁/₂ − σ₃/₂ was integrated from threshold to infinite energy, with a practical energy cut at 6 GeV where the spin-dependent terms become negligible.

## Key numbers
- Proton vector meson sum: I^v_GDH = +0.26 μb (sum over ωp, ρ⁰p, and ρ⁺n channels)
- Neutron vector meson sum: I^v_GDH = −2.05 μb (sum over ωn, ρ⁰n, and ρ⁻p channels)
- ω meson threshold energy: ν_ω = 1.108 GeV (lab system)
- ρ meson threshold energy: ν_ρ = 1.086 GeV (lab system)
- Energy cut for convergence: ν ≈ 6 GeV, above which spin-dependent terms change the integral only at the fourth decimal place
- γp → ωp contribution: −2.01 μb
- γp → ρ⁰p contribution: +0.05 μb
- γp → ρ⁺n contribution: +2.22 μb
- γn → ωn contribution: +0.93 μb
- γn → ρ⁰n contribution: −0.05 μb
- γn → ρ⁻p contribution: −2.93 μb
- GDH sum rule prediction for proton: −205 μb
- GDH sum rule prediction for neutron: −233 μb
- Experimental result for proton (200–800 MeV, extrapolated): −210 μb

## Context
This work extends previous exclusive channel studies of the GDH sum rule by the Mainz group (Tiator, 2000), which included πN, ηN, ππ Born terms, and ππD₁₃(1520) channels and found those accounted for 97% of the proton sum rule but left large discrepancies for the neutron. It also follows the kaon photoproduction study by Sumowidagdo and Mart (1999) and the Regge parametrization estimate by Bianchi and Thomas (1999) for contributions above the resonance region. The results show that vector meson contributions add to the neutron sum rule in the correct direction to help resolve the neutron discrepancy, while providing small corrections to the proton. This enables future experimental tests at facilities such as JLab, MAMI, GRAAL, ELSA, and SPring8 to constrain the model with more accurate data.
