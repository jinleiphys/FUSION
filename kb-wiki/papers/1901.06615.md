# Fate of spin polarization in a relativistic fluid: An entropy-current analysis

> We derive relativistic hydrodynamic equations with a dynamical spin degree of freedom on the basis of an entropy-current analysis. The first and second laws of local thermodynamics constrain possible structures of the constitutive relations including a spin current and the antisymmetric part of the (canonical) energy-momentum tensor. Solving the obtained hydrodynamic equations within the linear-mode analysis, we find spin-diffusion modes, indicating that spin density is damped out after a characteristic time scale controlled by transport coefficients introduced in the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor in the entropy-current analysis. This is a consequence of mutual convertibility between spin and orbital angular momentum.

## Key claim

Spin density in a relativistic fluid is inherently dissipative and not a conserved quantity, meaning there is no rigorous "ideal spin hydrodynamics" even at leading order in the gradient expansion; the spin density decays after a characteristic time scale controlled by new transport coefficients introduced in the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors performed a phenomenological entropy-current analysis, extending the standard Landau-Lifshitz approach to relativistic hydrodynamics by including a dynamical spin degree of freedom. They derived constitutive relations for the spin current and the antisymmetric part of the canonical energy-momentum tensor, constrained by the first and second laws of local thermodynamics. The resulting hydrodynamic equations were then solved within a linear-mode analysis around a global equilibrium state with vanishing background spin density and fluid velocity.

## Key numbers

- Spin-diffusion mode gap: \(\omega = -2i D_s\), where \(D_s \equiv 4\gamma / \chi_s\) and \(\gamma\) is the rotational viscosity
- Boost-diffusion mode gap: \(\omega = -2i D_b\), where \(D_b \equiv 4\lambda / \chi_b\) and \(\lambda\) is the boost heat conductivity
- Shear mode dispersion: \(\omega = -i \gamma_\perp k_z^2 + O(k_z^4)\), with \(\gamma_\perp \equiv \eta/(e_0+p_0)\)
- Sound mode dispersion: \(\omega = \pm c_s k_z - i (\gamma_\parallel/2) k_z^2 + O(k_z^3)\), with \(\gamma_\parallel \equiv (\zeta + 4\eta/3)/(e_0+p_0)\)
- Six non-hydrodynamic modes (gapped) and four massless hydrodynamic modes (two longitudinal propagating, two transverse diffusive) were identified

## Context

This work resolves a disharmony between nonrelativistic spin hydrodynamics formulations (Matsuo 2013, Kobayashi 2017, Takahashi 2016) and the earlier "ideal" relativistic spin hydrodynamics proposed by Florkowski (2017) for the quark-gluon plasma. The authors show that spin is not conserved even at leading order, contrary to the ideal formulation, due to mutual convertibility between spin and orbital angular momentum. This framework enables quantitative description of spin dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and spintronics applications, and provides a basis for deriving Kubo formulas and extending to second-order causal formulations.
