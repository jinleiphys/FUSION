# Radiative parton processes in perturbative QCD - an improved version of the Gunion and Bertsch cross section from comparisons to the exact result

> In this work we compare the Gunion-Bertsch approximation of the leading order perturbative QCD radiation matrix element to the exact result. To this end, we revisit the derivation of the Gunion-Bertsch approximation as well as perform extensive numerical comparisons of the Gunion-Bertsch and the exact result. We find that when employing the matrix elements to obtain rates or cross sections from phase space integration, the amplitude by Gunion and Bertsch deviates from the correct result in characteristic regions of the phase space. We propose an improved version of the Gunion-Bertsch matrix element which agrees very well with the exact result in all phase space regions.

## Key claim

The Gunion-Bertsch (GB) approximation to the leading-order pQCD radiation matrix element, when used to compute cross sections or rates via phase-space integration, deviates from the exact result in characteristic phase-space regions; the authors propose an improved version that includes a kinematic factor \((1-x)^2\) and respects the symmetry of the process by combining results from \(A^+=0\) and \(A^-=0\) gauges, yielding agreement with the exact result at the level of a few percent over the entire rapidity range.

## Method

The authors revisited the derivation of the Gunion-Bertsch approximation for \(qq' \to qq'g\) in light-cone gauge, identifying that the original derivation omitted the \((1-x)^2\) factor (valid only at mid-rapidity where \(x \ll 1\)) and that the \(A^+=0\) gauge result is only valid for forward-emitted gluons. They combined the \(A^+=0\) result (valid for forward rapidity) with the analogous \(A^-=0\) result (valid for backward rapidity) to produce an improved matrix element with a factor \((1-\bar{x})^2\), where \(\bar{x} = (k_\perp/\sqrt{s}) e^{|y|}\). They performed extensive numerical comparisons of differential cross sections (e.g., \(d\sigma/dy\), \(d\sigma/dk_\perp^2\), \(d\sigma/dq_\perp^2\)) and total cross sections between the exact leading-order matrix elements from Berends (1981) and Ellis (1985) and the original GB, GB with \((1-x)^2\), and improved GB approximations, using a cut-off prescription \(\Lambda^2 = \epsilon m_D^2\) to regulate divergences.

## Key numbers

- The improved GB matrix element agrees with the exact result to within "a few percent" over the entire rapidity range for \(qq' \to qq'g\) (Figure 1).
- At small \(k_T^2\) and \(q_T^2\), where the cross-section contribution is largest, the deviation between the improved GB and exact result is less than 5% (Figure 2).
- Numerical comparisons were performed at a temperature of \(T = 400\) MeV, with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(s = 18 T^2 \simeq 2.88\) GeV\(^2\), and Debye mass \(m_D^2 \simeq 0.73\) GeV\(^2\) (for \(n_f=3\)).
- The cut-off parameter was set to \(\epsilon = 0.001\) for most comparisons, i.e., \(\Lambda^2 = 0.001 \, m_D^2\).

## Context

This work extends the original Gunion-Bertsch approximation (Gunion and Bertsch, 1981) by correcting its behavior in forward and backward rapidity regions, which had been previously used in transport models such as BAMPS (Xu, 2004) and in rate-equation approaches (Xiong, 1992; Biro, 1993; Srivastava, 1996; Wong, 1996; Mustafa, 1997; Chen, 2009; Chen, 2010; Gossiaux, 2010). It addresses discrepancies raised by Chen (2011) regarding symmetry-factor counting and by Zhang (2012) regarding numerical differences between GB and full leading-order results. The improved GB matrix element enables more accurate incorporation of inelastic \(2 \leftrightarrow 3\) processes in microscopic transport simulations and rate equations for heavy-ion collisions, without requiring the full complexity of exact matrix elements.
