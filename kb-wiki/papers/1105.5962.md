# On the study of momentum correlations in fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions

> The role of momentum correlations in fragmentation is studied within the framework of quantum molecular dynamics model. Our study is carried out by imposing momentum cut in the clusterization algorithm. The study reveals a strong effect of momentum cut in the fragmentation pattern. A comparison with experimental data is also presented. Our theoretical results are in agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that imposing a momentum cut (relative momentum ≤ 150 MeV/c) in the clusterization algorithm (MSTP method) yields a significant difference in the fragmentation pattern compared to the standard spatial-only MST method, and that the MSTP method matches experimental charge distributions quantitatively, whereas the MST method overpredicts the data.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions, then applied two clusterization algorithms to the phase-space output: the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method, which uses a spatial cut of 4 fm, and the Minimum Spanning Tree with momentum cut (MSTP) method, which adds a relative momentum cut of 150 MeV/c. They simulated reactions of four symmetric systems (12C+12C, 40Ca+40Ca, 96Zr+96Zr, and 197Au+197Au) at incident energies of 100 and 400 MeV/nucleon across impact parameters from 0.0 to 0.8, using a soft equation of state with an energy-dependent Cugon cross section.

## Key numbers

- Momentum cut value used in MSTP method: p_min = 150 MeV/c.
- Spatial cut value used in both MST and MSTP methods: r_min = 4 fm.
- Systems simulated: 12C+12C, 40Ca+40Ca, 96Zr+96Zr, and 197Au+197Au at 100 and 400 MeV/nucleon.
- Impact parameters studied: b̂ = 0.0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8.
- For 197Au+197Au, experimental comparison was made at 150 and 250 MeV/nucleon for central (b = 0-3.5 fm) and semi-central (b = 0-8 fm) geometries.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Puri et al. (Kumar, 2001) which applied the MSTP method only to heavier systems like 93Nb+93Nb and 197Au+197Au; the present paper addresses the role of momentum cut for lighter colliding systems and examines the dependence on colliding geometry. The results enable a more realistic description of multifragmentation in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly for lighter systems where spectator matter is small and fragments originate from the participant region.
