# Stochastic quantization at finite chemical potential

> A nonperturbative lattice study of QCD at finite chemical potential is complicated due to the complex fermion determinant and the sign problem. Here we apply the method of stochastic quantization and complex Langevin dynamics to this problem. We present results for U(1) and SU(3) one link models and QCD at finite chemical potential using the hopping expansion. The phase of the determinant is studied in detail. Even in the region where the sign problem is severe, we find excellent agreement between the Langevin results and exact expressions, if available. We give a partial understanding of this in terms of classical flow diagrams and eigenvalues of the Fokker-Planck equation.

## Key claim

Stochastic quantization via complex Langevin dynamics can yield numerically accurate results for QCD-like models at finite chemical potential even when the sign problem is severe, and the method's range of applicability is not restricted to small chemical potential. The authors provide a partial understanding of this success through classical flow diagrams and eigenvalues of the Fokker-Planck equation, but note that a systematic analysis for full lattice QCD has not yet been performed.

## Method

The authors apply complex Langevin dynamics to U(1) and SU(3) one-link models, as well as to QCD with the fermion determinant treated in the hopping expansion. For the one-link models, the complex Langevin equations are discretized in fictitious Langevin time, with the drift terms derived from the complex effective action including both the bosonic part and the fermion determinant. The phase of the determinant is studied in detail, and classical flow diagrams in the complexified variable plane are analyzed alongside eigenvalues of the Fokker-Planck equation to understand convergence properties.

## Key numbers

* Polyakov loop and conjugate Polyakov loop for the U(1) model at fixed κ=1/2 show excellent agreement between Langevin results and analytical expressions across a range of β values (0.5, 1.0, 2.0) and μ values from 0 to approximately 5.
* Density ⟨n⟩ for the U(1) model at κ=1/2 and β=1.0 matches the analytical expression ⟨n⟩ = (1/Z) κ I₁(β) sinh μ, with the numerical result increasing linearly at small μ and approaching 1 exponentially fast at large μ.
* The average phase factor ⟨e^{2iφ}⟩ for the U(1) model at β=1, κ=1/2 becomes very small at large μ (e.g., approximately 0.1 at μ≈3), yet the Langevin results remain in excellent agreement with semi-analytical integration.
* Langevin simulations used a stepsize ε=5×10⁻⁵ and 5×10⁷ time steps, with errors estimated via jackknife procedure.
* The four smallest nonzero eigenvalues of the complex Fokker-Planck equation for the hopping-expanded U(1) model at β=1, κ=1/2 are all strictly positive and increase with μ.

## Context

This work extends the application of complex Langevin dynamics, previously applied to effective three-dimensional spin models related to lattice QCD at strong coupling (Karsch 1985, Ilgenfritz 1986, Bilic 1987), to one-link models and QCD in the hopping expansion. The approach is manifestly independent of other methods developed since 2000 for the sign problem, including reweighting (Fodor 2001-2004), Taylor series expansion in μ/T (Allton 2002-2005, Gavai 2003), imaginary chemical potential with analytic continuation (de Forcrand 2002-2006, D'Elia 2002), and the canonical ensemble (Kratochvila 2005, Ejiri 2008). The results enable further application of complex Langevin dynamics to full lattice QCD at nonzero density, though the authors note that a systematic analysis for the full theory has not yet been performed.
