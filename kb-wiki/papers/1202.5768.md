# How stable are transport model results to changes of resonance parameters? A UrQMD model study

> The Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics [UrQMD] model is widely used to simulate heavy ion collisions in broad energy ranges. It consists of various components to implement the different physical processes underlying the transport approach. A major building block are the shared tables of constants, implementing the baryon masses and widths. Unfortunately, many of these input parameters are not well known experimentally. In view of the upcoming physics program at FAIR, it is therefore of fundamental interest to explore the stability of the model results when these parameters are varied. We perform a systematic variation of particle masses and widths within the limits proposed by the particle data group (or up to 10%). We find that the model results do only weakly depend on the variation of these input parameters. Thus, we conclude that the present implementation is stable with respect to the modification of not yet well specified particle parameters.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model's results depend only weakly on variations of hadron masses and widths within PDG limits (or up to 10%), but the paper qualifies this by noting that discontinuities can occur (e.g., a step in pion yield at ~1.8% upward shift of Delta masses) and that systematic errors can reach ±20% in low-energy collisions dominated by resonance dynamics.

## Method

The authors used Python modules to read PDG data and automatically rewrite the Fortran source code of UrQMD with systematically varied baryon masses and widths. They performed separate scans for variations of mass and width of each baryon family (nucleon resonances and Delta resonances) within ±10% of PDG averages, simulating up to 10,000 events per parameter set for Pb+Pb collisions at 2 AGeV and 30 AGeV beam energies. They analyzed the total pion-nucleon cross section, total pion yields, and transverse momentum spectra of pions.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon cross section varies strongly when nucleon resonance masses are changed by ±10%, but is unaltered when resonance widths are changed by ±10%.
- At 2 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions, variation of nucleon resonance masses and widths by ±10% produces at worst a linear variation in relative pion yield.
- At 30 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions, variation of Delta masses by ~+1.8% produces a step-like discontinuity in pion yield, traced to the Δ(1920) and Δ(1950) resonances exchanging roles.
- Variation of Delta masses by ±5% results in modifications of the pion yield by ±20% in given pT regions at low energy.
- At 2 AGeV, a ±5% variation of nucleon resonance masses shifts high-pT pion yields by up to ±20%.
- The branching ratios Δ(1920) → πΔ(1232) and Δ(1950) → πΔ(1232) differ by a factor of 2 (40% vs. 20%).

## Context

This work extends the systematic error quantification for transport models used in heavy-ion physics, specifically for the upcoming FAIR program. It builds on the UrQMD model (Bleicher 1999, Bass 1998, Petersen 2008) and is qualitatively transferable to other transport approaches like PHSD and AMPT. The study enables better estimates of systematic uncertainties in simulations of nuclear matter at high baryon densities, though the authors note that the simultaneous variation of all parameters in one direction represents a worst-case scenario, and uncorrelated errors would induce smaller systematic bias.
