# Polaronic neutron in dilute alpha matter: A $p$-wave Bose polaron

> We theoretically investigate quasiparticle properties of a neutron immersed in an alpha condensate, which is one of the possible states of dilute symmetric nuclear matter. The resonant $p$-wave neutron-alpha scattering, which plays a crucial role in forming halo nuclei, is considered. This system is similar to a Bose polaron near the $p$-wave Feshbach resonance that can be realized in cold-atomic experiments. Calculating the self-energy within the field-theoretical approach, we give an analytical formula for the effective mass of a polaronic neutron as a function of alpha condensation density. Moreover, two adjacent neutrons in a medium, each of which behaves like a stable polaron having an enhanced effective mass, can form a bound dineutron, with the help of $^1S_0$ neutron-neutron attraction. This is in contrast to the case of the vacuum, where a dineutron is known to be unbound. Our result would be useful for understanding many-body physics in astrophysical environments as well as the formation of multi-nucleon clusters in neutron-halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a neutron immersed in a dilute alpha condensate forms a stable Bose polaron with an enhanced effective mass due to resonant p-wave neutron-alpha scattering, and that two such polaronic neutrons can form a bound dineutron in the medium even though a dineutron is unbound in vacuum. The key condition is that this binding occurs only with the help of the residual \(^1S_0\) neutron-neutron attraction acting on the polaronic neutrons, and the effect depends on the alpha condensate density remaining below a critical value.

## Method

The authors employ a two-channel Hamiltonian that includes the resonant \(J^\pi = 3/2^-\) p-wave neutron-alpha scattering channel, modeled via a Yamaguchi-type form factor with parameters fitted to the empirical scattering volume \(a_p = -67.1\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and effective range \(r_p = -0.87\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) of the \(^5\)He resonance. They calculate the neutron self-energy in the alpha condensate using a Beliaev-type diagram within a field-theoretical approach at zero temperature, treating the alpha condensate within mean-field theory and neglecting the alpha-alpha interaction in the dilute regime. For the dineutron binding, they solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two neutrons in the \(^1S_0\) channel using a separable interaction and the approximated polaronic neutron propagator with an enhanced effective mass at low momenta.

## Key numbers

- The p-wave scattering volume for the \(J^\pi = 3/2^-\) neutron-alpha resonance is \(a_p = -67.1\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and the effective range is \(r_p = -0.87\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), with the \(^5\)He resonance energy \(E_{\rm res} \sim 0.93\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The critical alpha condensate density for divergence of the polaronic neutron effective mass is \(\rho_{\rm c} = 7.41 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- At low densities (e.g., \(\rho_\alpha = 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)), the quasiparticle peak lies close to the bare neutron dispersion; at \(\rho_\alpha = 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the effective mass becomes larger than the bare mass; at \(\rho_\alpha = 3 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), spectral broadening is prominent; and at \(\rho_\alpha = 7 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the spectral weight flattens in the low-momentum limit.
- The quasiparticle residue \(Z = 1\) and the polaron energy \(E_{\rm P} = 0\) at zero momentum.
- The effective mass enhancement is given by \(M_\nu / M_{\rm eff} = 1 + 8\pi M_\alpha a_p \rho_\alpha / (M_\nu + M_\alpha) < 1\) (since \(a_p < 0\)).
- The high-frequency tail of the spectral weight at low momenta scales as \(\omega^{-9/2}\) and is proportional to \(\rho_\alpha\) and \(p^2\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of Bose polarons, previously studied in cold atomic gases (e.g., PhysRevLett.117.055301, PhysRevA.88.053632), to nuclear systems by considering a neutron as an impurity in an alpha condensate, which is a possible state of dilute symmetric nuclear matter relevant to supernova explosions and heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with earlier studies of Fermi polarons in neutron matter (PhysRevC.102.055802, PhysRevC.104.065801) and with p-wave Fermi polarons (PhysRevLett.109.075302), as the bosonic medium and p-wave resonance lead to an isotropic self-energy and a critical density for effective mass divergence rather than a transition to a Feshbach molecular state. The predicted dineutron binding due to the polaron effect may help understand multi-nucleon cluster formation in neutron-halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He (Hammer 2017) and the crossover from dineutron BEC to BCS neutron superfluid (PhysRevC.73.044309).
