# Temperature Dependence of Hadron Masses from the Zimanyi-Moszkowski Model

> An extended version of the Zimanyi-Moskowski (ZM) model has been used to calculate the temperature dependence of the hadronic masses. The calculation of meson masses in the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) gives an increase in the $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ effective masses with temperature.

## Key claim

The effective masses of the sigma, omega, and pion mesons increase with temperature in the Zimanyi-Moskowski (ZM) model, but the pion mass in the ZM models changes much less than in the Walecka model; however, the authors note that neither model incorporates chiral symmetry, so the argument that the pion mass should be protected as a Goldstone boson is somewhat extraneous.

## Method

The authors extended the Zimanyi-Moskowski model to include pions via pseudovector coupling and calculated the nucleon effective mass at finite temperature in the Mean Field Approximation (MFA) by solving a self-consistent equation. They then used the temperature-dependent nucleon propagator to compute the polarization functions and obtained the meson effective masses from the poles of the full propagator in the Random Phase Approximation (RPA).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility from the ZM model: K = 224.49 MeV, compared to the experimental value of K = 210 +/- 30 MeV and the Walecka model value of K = 524 MeV.
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation density (rho = 0.17 fm^-3) and zero temperature: M_N* = 797.64 MeV.
- The calculation is considered valid for temperatures T less than or equal to 200 MeV, above which deconfinement to QCD matter is expected.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier zero-temperature finite-density calculation of hadron masses in the ZM model (Bhattacharyya and Raha, 1995) to finite temperature and zero baryon density, a scenario relevant for the central rapidity region of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The ZM model improves upon the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) by using derivative coupling between the scalar meson and nucleon, yielding a more realistic nuclear incompressibility. The results enable further studies including rho and a1 mesons and meson-meson couplings, which the authors state are in progress.
