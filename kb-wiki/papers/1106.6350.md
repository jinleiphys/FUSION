# The QGP shear viscosity -- elusive goal or just around the corner?

> With the new viscous hydrodynamic + hadron cascade hybrid code VISHNU, a rather precise (O(25%)) extraction of the QGP shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP from heavy-ion elliptic flow data is possible if the initial eccentricity of the collision fireball is known with <5% accuracy. At this point, eccentricities from initial state models differ by up to 20%, leading to an O(100%) uncertainty for (eta/s)_QGP. It is shown that a simultaneous comparison of elliptic and triangular flow, v_2 and v_3, puts strong constraints on initial state models and can largely eliminate the present uncertainty in (eta/s)_QGP. The variation of the differential elliptic flow v_2(p_T) for identified hadrons between RHIC and LHC energies provides additional tests of the evolution model.

## Key claim

A precise (O(25%)) extraction of the QGP shear viscosity (η/s)QGP from heavy-ion elliptic flow data is possible only if the initial eccentricity of the collision fireball is known with less than 5% accuracy; currently, eccentricities from initial state models differ by up to 20%, leading to an O(100%) uncertainty for (η/s)QGP.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid code VISHNU, which couples viscous hydrodynamics of the QGP to the hadron cascade model UrQMD for the late hadronic stage, to compute elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 for Au+Au collisions at RHIC (200 A GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (2.76 A TeV). They compared the eccentricity-scaled total charged hadron elliptic flow v2ch/εx from smooth averaged initial profiles (single-shot hydrodynamics) and event-by-event hydrodynamics of fluctuating fireballs, using both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial state models, to extract (η/s)QGP and test its sensitivity to initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The extraction of (η/s)QGP from elliptic flow data alone has an O(100%) uncertainty due to initial eccentricity differences of up to 20% between MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models.
- For RHIC 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, the extracted range is 1 < 4π(η/s)QGP < 2.5 for temperatures Tc < T < 2Tc.
- Event-by-event ideal fluid evolution reduces v2/ε by about 5% for pions, with a downward shift for (η/s)QGP of at most 0.02-0.03.
- A straightforward extrapolation from RHIC to LHC with fixed (η/s)QGP overpredicts the LHC v2ch values by 10-15%; increasing (η/s)QGP from 0.16 to 0.20 (for MC-KLN) gives better agreement with ALICE data.
- At fixed pT < 1 GeV, v2(pT) increases from RHIC to LHC for pions but decreases for all heavier hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions by Song and Heinz (2010) and Song et al. (2010) by introducing the hybrid code VISHNU that couples viscous QGP hydrodynamics to the UrQMD hadron cascade, improving the description of the hadronic stage. It shows that simultaneous measurement of elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 can strongly constrain initial state models (MC-Glauber vs. MC-KLN) and largely eliminate the present factor-of-two uncertainty in (η/s)QGP, enabling a more precise extraction of QGP transport properties at both RHIC and LHC energies.
