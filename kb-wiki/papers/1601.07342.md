# Off-equilibrium sphaleron transitions in the Glasma

> We perform first classical-statistical real time lattice simulations of topological transitions in the non-equilibrium Glasma of weakly coupled but highly occupied gauge fields created immediately after the collision of ultra-relativistic nuclei. Simplifying our description by employing SU(2) gauge fields, and neglecting their longitudinal expansion, we find that the rate of topological transitions is initially strongly enhanced relative to the thermal sphaleron transition rate and decays with time during the thermalization process. Qualitative features of the time dependence of this non-equilibrium transition rate can be understood when expressed in terms of the magnetic screening length, which we also extract non-perturbatively. A detailed investigation of auto-correlation functions of the Chern-Simons number ($N_{CS}$) reveals non-Markovian features of the evolution distinct from previous simulations of non-Abelian plasmas in thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of topological transitions in the non-equilibrium Glasma, finding that the sphaleron transition rate is initially strongly enhanced relative to the thermal equilibrium rate and decays with time during thermalization, with the time dependence qualitatively understood when expressed in terms of the magnetic screening length.

## Method

The authors solve classical Yang-Mills equations on a 3D spatial lattice with SU(2) gauge fields in a fixed box, neglecting longitudinal expansion. They extract the Chern-Simons number using two independent methods: a calibrated cooling method and a slave field method, both adapted from previous thermal equilibrium studies. The magnetic screening scale is extracted non-perturbatively from spatial Wilson loop measurements, and the sphaleron transition rate is computed from the long-time behavior of the Chern-Simons number auto-correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The deviation from integer values in the calibration check of the Chern-Simons number measurement is typically less than 2%
- For the thermal equilibrium case, the non-perturbative constant in the parametric estimate Gamma_sph^eq = kappa * alpha_S^5 * T^4 is kappa ~ 132 +/- 4 for N_c=3, N_f=0 at very weak couplings
- The cooling step size used is delta_tau = a^2/8
- Cooling depth tau a^2 >= 1 is sufficient before each level of blocking to keep errors below the 1% level
- For the slave field method on small lattices in the thermal case, accurate tracking is achieved with N_steps=5 and PS_max=1.2

## Context

This work extends previous real-time lattice studies of sphaleron transitions in thermal equilibrium by Ambjorn (1997), Moore (1996, 1997, 1998) to the far-from-equilibrium Glasma created immediately after ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. It contrasts with earlier work by Kharzeev (2001) on Chern-Simons number fluctuations in the boost-invariant Glasma, where topological transitions are not allowed due to the trivial second homotopy group of SU(2) in 2+1 dimensions. The results enable improved theoretical estimates of the Chiral Magnetic Effect signal in heavy-ion collisions by providing the sphaleron transition rate at early times when the system is far off-equilibrium.
