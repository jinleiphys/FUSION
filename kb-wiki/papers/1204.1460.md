# Second-order equation of state with the full Skyrme interaction: toward new effective interactions for beyond mean-field models

> In a quantum Fermi system the energy per particle calculated at the second order beyond the mean-field approximation diverges if a zero-range interaction is employed. We have previously analyzed this problem in symmetric nuclear matter by using a simplified nuclear Skyrme interaction, and proposed a strategy to treat such a divergence. In the present work, we extend the same strategy to the case of the full nuclear Skyrme interaction. Moreover we show that, in spite of the strong divergence ($\sim$ $Λ^5$, where $Λ$ is the momentum cutoff) related to the velocity-dependent terms of the interaction, the adopted cutoff regularization can be always simultaneously performed for both symmetric and nuclear matter with different neutron-to-proton ratio. This paves the way to applications to finite nuclei.

# Second-order equation of state with the full Skyrme interaction: toward new effective interactions for beyond mean-field models

## Key claim
The authors extend their previously proposed cutoff regularization strategy for treating ultraviolet divergences in second-order nuclear matter calculations from a simplified $t_0-t_3$ Skyrme model to the full Skyrme interaction, and demonstrate that despite the much stronger divergence (scaling as $\Lambda^5$ rather than linearly), the regularization can be performed simultaneously for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, including pure neutron matter.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions for the second-order correction to the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter using the full Skyrme interaction (nine free parameters, excluding the spin-orbit term which does not contribute in uniform matter). They then perform a cutoff regularization procedure by adjusting the Skyrme parameters for each chosen momentum cutoff $\Lambda$ to reproduce the SLy5 mean-field EoS as a benchmark, fitting separately for symmetric matter ($\delta=0$), pure neutron matter ($\delta=1$), asymmetric matter ($\delta=0.5$), and finally a global simultaneous fit for all three cases.

## Key numbers
- The second-order correction diverges as $\Lambda^5$ for large momentum cutoff, much stronger than the linear divergence ($\sim \Lambda$) found in the simplified $t_0-t_3$ model.
- For $\Lambda = 2$ fm$^{-1}$ using SLy5 parameters, the second-order correction at saturation density ($\rho = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) is approximately -80 MeV for symmetric matter.
- The second-order incompressibility modulus at saturation density for $\Lambda = 2$ fm$^{-1}$ is approximately 625 MeV using SLy5 parameters.
- After refitting, the saturation density and incompressibility modulus for symmetric matter are 0.16 fm$^{-3}$ and 229.9 MeV, respectively, for all four cutoff values.
- The global fit yields $\chi^2$ values less than 1 for $\Lambda$ up to 1 fm$^{-1}$, and between 1 and 2 for larger cutoff values.
- Maximum deviations from the reference EoS across all asymmetry values are not larger than 0.4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the cutoff regularization approach of Moghrabi et al. (previous work, Ref. [mog]) from a simplified $t_0-t_3$ Skyrme model in symmetric matter to the full Skyrme interaction including velocity-dependent terms and asymmetric matter. The approach addresses a known problem: conventional phenomenological interactions like Skyrme are fitted at the mean-field level, so using them in beyond-mean-field calculations (such as particle-vibration coupling models by Bernard, Colo, and Ring) risks double-counting correlations. The results enable the construction of new effective interactions specifically adjusted for beyond-mean-field applications to finite nuclei, though the authors note that dimensional renormalization (as used in electroweak theory) could provide a unique parameter set rather than cutoff-dependent parametrizations.
