# Impact of unitarization on the $J/ψ$-light meson cross section

> Hidden charm mesons continue playing an essential role as relevant probes to understand the evolution of partonic matter. It is expected that the charmonia that survived the quark-gluon plasma phase suffer collisions with other particles composing the hadronic matter. In this work, we intend to contribute on this subject by presenting an updated study about the interactions of $J/ψ$ with surrounding hadronic medium. The meson-meson interactions are described with a $SU(4)$ effective Lagrangian, and within the framework of unitarized coupled channel amplitudes projected onto s-wave. The symmetry is explicitly broken to $SU(3)$ by suppression of the interactions driven by charmed mesons. We calculate the cross sections for $J/ψ$ scattering by light pseudoscalar mesons ($π, K, η$) and vector mesons ($ρ, K^\ast, ω$), as well as their inverse processes. Keeping the validity of this present approach in the low CM energy range, the most relevant channels are evaluated and a comparison of the findings with existing literature is performed.

## Key claim

The unitarized coupled-channel amplitudes, projected onto s-wave and derived from a lowest-order SU(4) effective Lagrangian with explicit symmetry breaking to SU(3), produce non-vanishing cross sections for J/ψ scattering by light mesons only after the unitarization procedure; at tree level, only reactions with open-charmed final states have non-zero cross sections, and the approach is valid only in the low center-of-mass energy range.

## Method

The authors describe meson-meson interactions using an SU(4) effective Lagrangian based on lowest-order chiral perturbation theory, with the symmetry explicitly broken to SU(3) by suppressing interactions driven by charmed mesons. They project the tree-level amplitudes onto s-wave and then solve a Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain unitarized coupled-channel amplitudes, using dimensional regularization for the two-meson loop integrals with subtraction constants a_H = -1.55 for channels involving at least one heavy meson and a_L = -0.8 for channels involving only light mesons, at a scale μ = 1.5 GeV. Cross sections are computed for J/ψ scattering by π, K, η, ρ, K*, and ω mesons, as well as their inverse processes, considering only s-wave reactions.

## Key numbers

* Cross sections for J/ψπ → ωπ, ρη, (K̅*K + c.c.) are smaller than those for J/ψπ → J/ψπ, ρηc, (D̅D* + c.c.) by about a factor 10^5
* Direct and inverse cross sections for J/ψX scattering are between 0.1 and 1 mbarn in the range 4 GeV < √s < 5 GeV
* For J/ψK* → D̅Ds + c.c., the cross section is suppressed relative to J/ψK* → D̅*Ds* + c.c. by about a factor 10^2
* The pion decay constant used is fπ = 93 MeV; the D meson decay constant is fD = 165 MeV
* The J/ψ mass is taken as mJ/ψ = 3097 MeV; the ηc mass is mηc = 2979 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of J/ψ absorption by light hadrons derived from chiral Lagrangians, such as those by Wong (1999, 2001), Haglin (2000), and Lin and Ko (2000), which relied on form factors with different functional forms and cutoff values that could not be justified a priori. The unitarization approach, previously applied to hadronic resonances and meson-meson scattering by Roca (2005), Gamermann (2007), and Abreu (2011, 2013), provides a controlled high-energy behavior without ad hoc form factors. The results enable improved calculations of J/ψ abundance evolution in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and can be compared with the coupled-channel calculations of Mitra (2016) and Abreu (2017).
