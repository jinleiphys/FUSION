# Constraining The Early-Universe Baryon Density And Expansion Rate

> We explore constraints on extensions to the standard models of cosmology and particle physics which modify the early-Universe expansion rate S = H'/H (parametrized by the effective number of neutrinos N_nu). The constraints on N_nu and the baryon density parameter (eta_B = n_B/n_gamma = 10^(-10)*eta_10) from BBN at 20 minutes are compared with those from the CMB at 400 kyr and LSS at 14 Gyr. BBN provides the strongest constraint on N_nu (1.6 < N_nu < 3.3 at 95% confidence), but a weaker constraint on eta_B. The CMB/LSS best constrain the baryon density (5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4 at 95% confidence), independent of N_nu, but provide a relatively weak N_nu constraint, consistent with N_nu = 3. Using the best fit values and the allowed ranges of the CMB/LSS-derived parameters to calculate the BBN-predicted primordial abundances yields excellent agreement with the observationally inferred abundance of deuterium and good agreement with 4He, confirming the consistency between the BBN and CMB/LSS results. However, the BBN-predicted abundance of 7Li is high, by a factor of 3 or more. We comment on the value of N_nu and a possible anomaly in the matter power spectrum inferred from observations of the Ly-alpha forest. The good agreement between our BBN and CMB/LSS results permit us to constrain any post-BBN entropy production as well as to limit the production of any non-thermalized relativistic particles and, allow us to combine them finding 95% ranges, 1.8 < N_nu < 3.2 and 5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the early-Universe expansion rate (parametrized by the effective number of neutrinos N_nu) and the baryon density parameter eta_B by comparing Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) results at 20 minutes with Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) and Large Scale Structure (LSS) data at later epochs, finding that while BBN provides the strongest constraint on N_nu (1.6 < N_nu < 3.3 at 95% confidence), the CMB/LSS data best constrain the baryon density (5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4 at 95% confidence), and the combined 95% ranges are 1.8 < N_nu < 3.2 and 5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4.

## Method

The authors used analytic fits to BBN predictions from Kneller (2004) updated by Steigman (2007) to compute primordial abundances of deuterium (D) and helium-4 (4He) as functions of eta_10 and N_nu, then compared these to observationally-inferred primordial abundances (D/H = 2.68^{+0.27}_{-0.25} times 10^5, Y_P = 0.240 +/- 0.006). For the CMB/LSS analysis, they used Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods with the COSMOMC code, assuming a flat Lambda-CDM cosmology with seven parameters, incorporating WMAP 5-year data, small-scale CMB data from ACBAR and other experiments, a Gaussian prior on H_0 = 72 +/- 8 km/s/Mpc from the HST Key Project, SNIa data from the Supernova Legacy Survey, and matter power spectrum data from SDSS and 2dFGRS truncated at k <= 0.07 h/Mpc.

## Key numbers

- BBN-only constraint on N_nu: N_nu = 2.4 +/- 0.4 at 68% confidence (1.6 < N_nu < 3.3 at 95% confidence)
- BBN-only constraint on eta_10: eta_10 = 5.7 +/- 0.4 at 68% confidence
- CMB/LSS constraint on eta_10: eta_10 = 6.1^{+0.2}_{-0.1} at 68% confidence (5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4 at 95% confidence)
- Combined 95% confidence ranges: 1.8 < N_nu < 3.2 and 5.9 < eta_10 < 6.4
- Standard BBN (N_nu = 3) gives eta_10 = 6.0 +/- 0.4 from deuterium alone
- Primordial deuterium abundance adopted: y_DP = 2.68^{+0.27}_{-0.25} (times 10^5 D/H)
- Primordial 4He mass fraction adopted: Y_P = 0.240 +/- 0.006
- Robust upper bound on Y_P: Y_P < 0.251 +/- 0.002
- BBN-predicted 7Li abundance is high by a factor of 3 or more compared to observed values uncorrected for depletion

## Context

This work extends earlier constraints on non-standard expansion rates from Barger (2003) and Cyburt (2004) by simultaneously comparing BBN and CMB/LSS constraints on both N_nu and eta_B, finding consistency between the two epochs that permits combining them. The results enable constraints on any post-BBN entropy production and on the production of non-thermalized relativistic particles, and the authors note a possible anomaly in the matter power spectrum inferred from Ly-alpha forest observations that may relate to the value of N_nu. The paper also confirms the well-known lithium problem, where BBN predictions overproduce 7Li by a factor of 3 or more relative to observations.
