# The DIS 1-Jettiness Event Shape at N$^3$LL+${\cal O}(α_s^2)$

> We present results for the $τ_1$ and $τ_{1a}$ 1-Jettiness global event shape distributions, for Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS), at the N$^3$LL + ${\cal O}(α_s^2)$ level of accuracy. These event-shape distributions quantify and characterize the pattern of final state radiation in electron-nucleus collisions. They can be used as a probe of nuclear structure functions, nuclear medium effects in jet production, and for a precision extraction of the QCD strong coupling. The results presented here, along with the corresponding numerical codes, can be used for analyses with HERA data, in EIC simulation studies, and for eventual comparison with real EIC data.

## Key claim
The authors present the first numerical results for the $\tau_1$ and $\tau_{1a}$ 1-Jettiness event shape distributions in Deep Inelastic Scattering at N$^3$LL+${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$ accuracy, but the results are limited to the resummation region where $\tau_1 \ll P_{J_T}$ or $\tau_{1a} \ll 1$, and the non-perturbative soft function is modeled rather than computed from first principles.

## Method
The authors use a factorization theorem derived in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to resum large Sudakov logarithms that arise in the small $\tau_1$ or $\tau_{1a}$ limit. The resummation at N$^3$LL accuracy is combined with fixed-order ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$ contributions implemented numerically using the NLOJET++ program. The hard, jet, beam, and soft functions are evolved via renormalization group equations from their natural scales to a common scale, and the resummed and fixed-order results are matched using the standard subtraction formula $d\sigma = [d\sigma_{\rm resum} - d\sigma^{\rm FO}_{\rm resum}] + d\sigma^{\rm FO}$.

## Key numbers
- The hard scale for the $\tau_1$ observable is set by $\mu_H \sim P_{J_T}$, while for $\tau_{1a}$ it is $\mu_H \sim \sqrt{Q^2}$.
- The renormalization scales for the $\tau_1$ observable scale as $\mu_J \sim \mu_B \sim \sqrt{\tau_1 P_{J_T}}$ and $\mu_S \sim \tau_1$.
- For the $\tau_{1a}$ observable, the scales scale as $\mu_J \sim \mu_B \sim \sqrt{\tau_{1a} Q^2}$ and $\mu_S \sim \tau_{1a}\sqrt{Q^2}$.
- The non-perturbative soft function model uses parameters $a=1.80$, $b=-0.05$, and $\Lambda=0.4$ GeV for the numerical plots.
- Kinematic parameters for the $\tau_1$ plots include $Q_e=90.0$ GeV, $20$ GeV $< P_{JT} < 30$ GeV, and $|y_J| < 2.5$.
- Kinematic parameters for the $\tau_{1a}$ plots include $Q_e=319$ GeV, $60$ GeV$^2 < Q^2 < 30$ GeV$^2$ (note: the upper bound appears to be a typo in the text), and $0.2 < y < 0.6$.

## Context
This work extends previous results on DIS 1-Jettiness event shapes that were presented at NLL (Kang, 2012) and NNLL (Kang, 2013) levels of accuracy, and at NNLL+${\cal O}(\alpha_s)$ (Kang, 2013). It also builds on the $\tau_{1b}$ event shape results at N$^3$LL (Kang, 2015) and recent measurements using HERA data (Hessler, 2021). The results enable precision studies of nuclear structure functions, nuclear medium effects in jet production, and extraction of the QCD strong coupling at the Electron-Ion Collider, and can be used for analyses with HERA data and EIC simulation studies.
