# Examining possible neutron-halo nuclei heaver than $^{37}$Mg

> The even-Z odd-N neutron-halo nuclei, which are the possible lightest neutron-halo nuclei heavier than $^{37}$Mg, are explored by studying the shell-structure unique in weakly-bound neutrons for spherical or deformed shape. It is pointed out that due to the narrowed N=50 spherical energy-gap and a few resulting close-lying neutron one-particle levels, 1g$_{9/2}$, 3s$_{1/2}$, and 2d$_{5/2}$, for spherical shape, nuclei with some weakly-bound neutrons filling in those levels may be deformed and have a good chance to show deformed s-wave halo. Promising candidates are $^{71}_{24}$Cr$_{47}$, $^{73}_{24}$Cr$_{49}$, $^{75}_{24}$Cr$_{51}$ and $^{77}_{26}$Fe$_{51}$ in the case that those nuclei lie inside the neutron drip-line. An interesting possibility of the deformed p-wave or s-wave halo is suggested also for the nucleus $^{53}_{18}$Ar$_{35}$.

# Examining possible neutron-halo nuclei heavier than $^{37}$Mg

## Key claim
The paper identifies candidate even-Z odd-N neutron-halo nuclei heavier than $^{37}$Mg by analyzing shell structure unique to weakly-bound neutrons, but the candidates are conditional on those nuclei lying inside the neutron drip-line and on their neutron separation energies being sufficiently small. The analysis relies on evaluated (extrapolated) S$_n$-values that carry unavoidable ambiguity.

## Method
The author used Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters from Bohr and Mottelson (1969) to calculate both bound and resonant one-particle neutron energies as functions of axially-symmetric quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$. For deformed potentials, bound-state energies were obtained by solving coupled differential equations in coordinate space with asymptotic boundary conditions, while resonant energies were defined by eigenphase increases through $\pi/2$. The probabilities of $\ell=0$ and $\ell=1$ components in the wave function of the last-odd neutron were examined for candidate nuclei, neglecting pair correlation as a first approximation.

## Key numbers
- The known deformed p-wave halo nucleus $^{37}$Mg has neutron separation energy S$_n$ = 0.22$^{+0.12}_{-0.09}$ MeV (from Nakamura et al., 2014).
- For the lowest-lying $\Omega^\pi = 1/2^+$ level from the N$_{ho}=4$ major shell, the probability of $\ell=0$ component is 0.014, 0.035, and 0.041 for $\beta = 0.2$, 0.4, and 0.6, respectively.
- S$_n$-values for Ni isotopes: $^{71}$Ni (4.3 MeV), $^{73}$Ni (4.0 MeV), $^{75}$Ni (3.9$^*$ MeV), $^{77}$Ni (3.2$^*$ MeV).
- S$_n$-values for Ti isotopes: $^{55}$Ti (4.1 MeV), $^{57}$Ti (2.7 MeV), $^{59}$Ti (2.6$^*$ MeV), $^{61}$Ti (2.1$^*$ MeV), $^{63}$Ti (1.3$^*$ MeV).
- S$_n$-values for Cr isotopes: $^{61}$Cr (4.0 MeV), $^{63}$Cr (2.9 MeV), $^{65}$Cr (2.6$^*$ MeV), $^{67}$Cr (2.0$^*$ MeV).
- S$_n$-values for Fe isotopes: $^{65}$Fe (4.3 MeV), $^{67}$Fe (4.0 MeV), $^{69}$Fe (3.3$^*$ MeV), $^{71}$Fe (2.8$^*$ MeV).
- S$_n$-values for Ar isotopes: $^{45}$Ar (5.17 MeV), $^{47}$Ar (3.55 MeV), $^{49}$Ar (3.60 MeV from Zhang et al., 2015), $^{51}$Ar (1.0$^*$ MeV).
- The decoupling parameters for relevant $\Omega^\pi = 1/2^+$ levels are in the range $-1 < a < +4$.

## Context
This work extends the search for neutron-halo nuclei beyond $^{37}$Mg, which was identified as a deformed p-wave halo by Nakamura et al. (2014). The study builds on earlier work by Hamamoto (2012, 2014) on shell-structure in weakly-bound neutrons and on the analysis of the "island of inversion" region near N=20, noting similarities with the N=40-51 region as discussed by Gade and collaborators (2016). The candidates identified ($^{71}$Cr, $^{73}$Cr, $^{75}$Cr, $^{77}$Fe, and $^{53}$Ar) provide targets for future Coulomb break-up experiments to detect halo signatures, and the predicted narrowing of the N=50 spherical energy gap challenges the traditional magic number in neutron-rich systems. The work also notes that proper Hartree-Fock calculations with reliable two-body interactions for these neutron-drip-line nuclei are not yet available, leaving the deformation predictions as qualitative guidance.
