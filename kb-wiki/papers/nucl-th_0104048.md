# Coulomb displacement energies, energy differenced and neutron skins

> A Fock space representation of the monopole part of the Coulomb potential is presented. Quantum effects show through a small orbital term in $l(l+1)$. Once it is averaged out, the classical electrostatic energy emerges as an essentially exact expression, which makes it possible to eliminate the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and to estimate neutron skins and the evolution of radii along yrast states of mirror nuclei. The energy differences of the latter are quantitatively reproduced by the monopole term and a schematic multipole one.

## Key claim

The Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, the systematic underestimation of Coulomb displacement energies by mean-field calculations, can be eliminated if a small neutron skin (parameter ζ ≈ 0.29) is present, contrary to earlier assumptions that rejected such a skin as a solution.

## Method

The authors constructed a Fock-space representation of the monopole part of the Coulomb potential, diagonalizing the interaction matrix over harmonic-oscillator shells to extract a dominant eigenvalue. They fitted a radius formula with three parameters (uniform contraction υ, skin parameter ζ, and shell/deformation corrections) to measured proton radii for 634 nuclei, then used the resulting radii to compute Coulomb monopole energies and compare with 183 experimental Coulomb displacement energies. For Coulomb energy differences in mirror yrast states of pf-shell nuclei (A=47–51), they separated monopole and multipole contributions, using shell-model wavefunctions and an effective Coulomb interaction derived from the ⁴²Ti-⁴²Ca pair.

## Key numbers

- Fits to proton radii for 634 nuclei yielded: r₀=1.226 fm, υ=0.45, ζ=0.29, λ=24.0, μ=24.0, rmsd=0.011 fm (Minute Skin fit)
- The zero-skin fit gave r₀=1.220 fm, υ=0.61, ζ=0.00, rmsd=0.012 fm
- The huge-skin fit gave r₀=1.236 fm, υ=0.00, ζ=0.94, rmsd=0.018 fm
- For the Minute Skin fit: d=0.705 MeV/fm, d_f=0.71 MeV/fm, c=0.9, χ²=1.20 (d_f ≈ d, resolving the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly)
- For the Zero Skin fit: d=0.708 MeV/fm, d_f=0.69 MeV/fm, c=1.3, χ²=1.46 (small underestimate of d)
- For the Huge Skin fit: d=0.699 MeV/fm, d_f=0.77 MeV/fm, c=-0.5, χ²=0.91 (large overestimate of d)
- The dominant eigenvalue from diagonalization over 8 major shells (τ=36 orbits) was E₃₆=0.192, 30 times larger than the next eigenvalue
- The single-particle p₃/₂ state in ⁴¹Sc lies 200 keV below its analogue in ⁴¹Ca
- The L² orbital term contributes approximately 150 keV to this splitting
- The monopole parameter a_m was set to 0.15 MeV for A≈50, reduced from 0.175 MeV by a factor (41/50)^(2/3) ≈ 0.88
- Effective f₇/₂ multipole matrix elements from ⁴²Ti-⁴²Ca: 86.9 keV (J=0), 116.9 keV (J=2), 10.9 keV (J=4), -59.1 keV (J=6)

## Context

This work extends the Fock-space decomposition of the Coulomb potential introduced by Duflo and Zuker (1996) and provides a unified microscopic description of both Coulomb displacement energies (the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, first identified by Nolen and Schiffer in 1969) and Coulomb energy differences in mirror nuclei. The resolution of the anomaly through a small neutron skin (ζ ≈ 0.29) contradicts earlier mean-field calculations that overestimated neutron radii, as illustrated by Bartel and collaborators. The results enable estimation of neutron skins from Coulomb data and open the question of a full treatment of the L² orbital term and non-diagonal monopole contributions for isospin impurities.
