# Azimuthal Anisotropy Scaling Functions for Identified Particle and Anti-Particle Species across Beam Energies: Insights into Baryon Junction Effects

> Azimuthal anisotropy scaling functions are constructed from species-resolved anisotropy measurements in Pb+Pb ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$, $5.02$ TeV) and Au+Au ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$-$200$~GeV) collisions to probe baryon transport and medium response at finite baryon chemical potential ($μ_B$). Within this data-driven framework, meson and baryon anisotropies spanning the collective-flow and quenching regimes collapse onto common scaling curves, enabling quantitative separation of viscous attenuation, radial flow, and hadronic re-scattering. The attenuation scale $k_β$ exhibits a non-monotonic beam-energy dependence, coincident with the low-energy rise of hadronic re-scattering, consistent with a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity featuring a near-minimum near the QCD critical region. A charge-odd baryon-antibaryon separation in the effective radial-flow response is negligible at LHC energies but grows toward lower $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. This species-uniform, baryon-number-scaling separation across $p,Λ,Ξ,Ω,$ and $d$ disfavors a purely hadronic origin and supports junction-driven net-baryon transport at finite $μ_B$, enhancing the experimental visibility of critical dynamics in finite, rapidly evolving systems. Together, these results establish species-resolved scaling functions as a compact and robust tool for constraining baryon stopping, medium opacity, and QGP transport properties.

## Key claim

The paper constructs data-driven azimuthal anisotropy scaling functions (ASF) that collapse meson and baryon $v_2$ measurements onto common curves across beam energies, enabling separation of viscous attenuation, radial flow, and hadronic re-scattering; the key hedge is that the baryon-antibaryon separation attributed to junction-driven net-baryon transport is inferred from scaling patterns rather than directly measured, and the interpretation relies on the assumption that the species-uniform, baryon-number-scaling behavior disfavors purely hadronic origins.

## Method

Species-resolved anisotropy scaling functions were constructed from published $v_2(p_T,\text{cent})$ measurements for identified mesons ($\pi^\pm, K^\pm, K_S^0, \phi$) and baryons ($p, \Lambda^0, \Xi^-, \Omega^-, d$) and their antiparticles in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV (20-30% centrality) and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$-$200$ GeV (10-40% centrality). The scaling framework uses charged kaons in ultra-central Pb+Pb at $5.02$ TeV as a common reference to fix the attenuation baseline $\beta_0$, with species-dependent response coefficients for hadronic re-scattering ($\zeta_{\text{hs}}$) and radial-flow response ($\zeta_{\text{rf}}$) extracted from the data. Initial-state eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$ were evaluated using the Monte Carlo quark-Glauber (MC-qGlauber) model, and the transverse kinetic energy $\text{KE}_T = m_T - m_0$ was used as the scaling variable to suppress trivial kinematic mass effects.

## Key numbers

- Attenuation scale $k_\beta$ ranges from $1.00$ (Pb+Pb $5.02$ TeV, 20-30%) to $0.70$ (Au+Au $11.5$ GeV, 10-40%), with a non-monotonic beam-energy dependence showing a minimum near $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 39$ GeV
- Meson re-scattering parameter $\zeta_{\text{hs}}$ is $0.00$ at LHC energies (Pb+Pb $2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV) and rises to $0.095$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV (Au+Au, 10-40%)
- Baryon radial-flow response $\zeta_{\text{rf}}^{(B)}$ decreases from $0.40$ (Pb+Pb $5.02$ TeV) to $0.11$ (Au+Au $11.5$ GeV), while antibaryon response $\zeta_{\text{rf}}^{(\bar B)}$ decreases from $0.40$ to $0.25$ over the same range
- Charge-odd separation $\frac12\Delta\zeta_{\text{rf}} = \frac12(\zeta_{\text{rf}}^{(\bar B)} - \zeta_{\text{rf}}^{(B)})$ grows from $0.00$ at LHC energies to $0.07$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV (Au+Au, 10-40%)
- Viscous correction parameter $\kappa = 0.17$ (GeV/$c$)$^{-2}$, held fixed above $p_T^{\text{thresh}} \sim 4.5$ GeV/$c$
- Systematic uncertainties on eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$ estimated at 2-3% from model-parameter variations

## Context

This work extends the ASF framework previously developed for charged hadrons (Lacey 2024) and identified particle species (Lacey 2024) to probe baryon transport at finite baryon chemical potential, building on the established understanding that scaling functions encode sensitivity to viscous attenuation ($\eta/s$), partonic energy loss ($\hat q$), radial-flow dynamics, and late-stage hadronic interactions. The baryon-antibaryon separation analysis tests predictions from baryon junction models (Kharzeev 1996, Rossi 1977, Vance 1998, Lewis 2022, Magdy 2024) against alternative hadronic mechanisms such as baryon-antibaryon annihilation (Steinheimer 2012, Sun 2017, Xu 2012, Zhou 2024). The results enable future data-driven constraints on baryon stopping, medium opacity, and QGP transport properties across the beam energy scan, with the correlated evolution of $k_\beta$, $\zeta_{\text{hs}}$, and $\Delta\zeta_{\text{rf}}$ providing linked observables for critical dynamics near the QCD phase transition.
