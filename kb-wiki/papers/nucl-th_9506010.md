# Hadronic cross-section fluctuations and proton coherent diffractive dissociation on helium

> The differential cross-section of inelastic coherent diffractive dissociation off nuclei $p~+~^4$He $\rightarrow X ~+ ~^4$He is expressed in terms of the relative cumulants of the cross-section distribution $P_N(σ)$. The theoretical result for the ratio $r=\Bigg(\frac{dσ_{diff}}{dt}\Bigg)^{pHe}_{t=0} \Bigg/ \Bigg(\frac{dσ_{diff}}{dt}\Bigg)^{pp}_{t=0}=6.8 ÷7.6$ is close to the value $r=7.1 \pm 0.75$ which we extracted from the FNAL data. These are the only $A>2$ data of this kind. The comparison provides the first confirmation of the cross-section fluctuation approach to the description of the absolute value of the inelastic diffraction cross-section off nuclei. It provides also a new constraint on the first 4 cumulants of the cross-section distribution.

## Key claim

The ratio of the differential cross-section for proton coherent diffractive dissociation on helium to that on a proton at t=0, calculated using the cross-section fluctuation approach, yields a theoretical range of r = 6.8–7.6, which is consistent with the value r = 7.1 ± 0.7 extracted from FNAL data, providing the first confirmation of this approach for absolute inelastic diffraction cross-sections off nuclei with A > 2.

## Method

The authors expressed the differential cross-section for inelastic coherent diffractive dissociation of protons on ⁴He at t=0 in terms of the relative cumulants of the cross-section distribution P_N(σ). They used the Glauber method with a parameterized hadron-nucleon amplitude and a simple Gaussian wave function for ⁴He, then averaged over target nucleon configurations to obtain the elastic amplitude for each hadron cross-section eigenstate. The theoretical ratio r was computed for three forms of the distribution function P_N(σ) (with n=2, 6, 10) fitted to κ₂ = 0.25, and compared to experimental data from the FNAL jet target experiment and proton-proton data at average energy 300 GeV, integrated over the diffractive mass range 2.5 (GeV)² < M² < 8 (GeV)².

## Key numbers

- Theoretical ratio r = 6.8–7.6 for the differential cross-section ratio (pHe)/(pp) at t=0, depending on the form of P_N(σ) (n=2 gives r = 6.79 ± 0.13; n=6 gives r = 7.53 ± 0.13; n=10 gives r = 7.54 ± 0.13)
- Experimental ratio extracted from FNAL data: r = 7.1 ± 0.7, integrated over M² from 2.5 (GeV)² to 8 (GeV)² at average energy 300 GeV
- Parameter β (slope of diffractive amplitude) used in the calculation: β = 10.5 ± 0.6 (GeV/c)⁻², derived as the mean of β = 8 ± 1 (GeV/c)⁻² for the diffractive process and β = 13 ± 0.5 (GeV/c)⁻² for elastic pp scattering
- Parameter γ = ⟨σ⟩/[π(α+β)] = 1.01 ± 0.02
- For the n=2 distribution: ⟨σ³⟩/⟨σ⟩³ = 1.82, ⟨σ⁴⟩/⟨σ⟩⁴ = 2.97, ⟨σ⁵⟩/⟨σ⟩⁵ = 5.3; relative cumulants κ₃ = 0.07, κ₄ = 0.19, κ₅ = 0.15

## Context

This work extends the cross-section fluctuation formalism developed by Good and Walker (1960) and the distribution function P(σ) determined for pion and nucleon projectiles by Blaettel et al. (1993) and Blaettel et al. (1993) using data on diffraction off nucleons and deuterons. The analysis uses previously overlooked FNAL data from Bujak et al. (1980) on proton diffractive dissociation off ⁴He, which are the only A > 2 data of this kind. The results provide new constraints on the first four cumulants of the cross-section distribution and suggest that new measurements using ⁴He and ³He with modern jet targets could separate different terms in the differential cross-section expression and improve sensitivity to details of P_N(σ).
