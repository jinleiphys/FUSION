# On the deuteron relativistic charge distributions

> We study the relativistic 2D charge distributions in the case of a spin-$1$ target. These charge distributions are based on a phase-space approach allowing one to study their frame dependence, and hence to relate the familiar rest-frame picture with the light-front picture developed in the last two decades. Like in the spin-$1/2$ case, we show that relativistic kinematical effects associated with spin are responsible for the distortions of the charge distributions seen in a moving frame. Applying our results to the deuteron, we observe a mild frame dependence compared to the nucleon case.

## Key claim

The paper shows that relativistic 2D charge distributions for a spin-1 target, such as the deuteron, exhibit a mild frame dependence compared to the nucleon case, contrary to the significant distortions observed for spin-1/2 targets. The authors qualify that this mild dependence reflects the deuteron being intrinsically less relativistic than a nucleon, with relativistic kinematical effects driven by a single combination of electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors extended a phase-space approach, previously applied to spin-1/2 targets, to define relativistic 2D charge distributions for spin-1 targets as a function of the average momentum P_z. They derived the charge amplitudes in elastic frames using a covariant parametrization of the spin-1 matrix elements in terms of multipole form factors (G_C, G_M, G_Q), and performed 2D Fourier transforms to obtain charge distributions for longitudinally and transversely polarized deuterons. The analysis used the phenomenological deuteron form factor parametrization "fit II" from JLABt20 (2000) to compute distributions for selected values of P_z.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron mass is M = 1.875 GeV (from Tiesinga (2021)).
- The deuteron electric quadrupole moment at Q^2=0 is G_Q(0) = 25.84(3) (from Ericson (1982)).
- The deuteron magnetic dipole moment at Q^2=0 is G_M(0) = 1.71 (from Tiesinga (2021)).
- The induced electric dipole moment for a transversely polarized deuteron vanishes and changes sign at P_z ≈ 11 GeV, based on G_M(0) = 1.71 and G_C(0) = 1.
- The 2D electric quadrupole moment in the Breit frame (P_z = 0) is half as large as the 3D quadrupole moment.

## Context

This work extends the phase-space approach of Lorce (2017, 2018, 2020) from spin-1/2 targets (nucleons) to spin-1 targets (deuterons), building on the light-front formalism developed by Burkardt (2000, 2002) and Carlson (2007, 2009). The results enable a smooth interpolation between the Breit-frame rest-frame picture and the infinite-momentum-frame light-front picture, clarifying the physical interpretation of deuteron electromagnetic form factors. The paper notes that improved measurements of the tensor analyzing power T_{20}(Q^2) from Jefferson Lab experiment C12-15-005 (Long, 2015) and future EIC data (AbdulKhalek, 2021) are needed for more reliable extraction of the individual multipole form factors.
