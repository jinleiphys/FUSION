# On the extraction of electromagnetic properties of the Delta(1232) excitation from pion photoproduction

> Several methods for the treatment of pion photoproduction in the region of the Delta(1232) resonance are discussed, in particular the effective Lagrangian approach and the speed plot analysis are compared to a dynamical treatment. As a main topic, we discuss the extraction of the genuine resonance parts of the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole multipoles of the electromagnetic excitation of the resonance. To this end, we try to relate the various values for the ratio R_{EM} of the E2 to M1 multipole excitation strengths for the Delta(1232) resonance as extracted by the different methods to corresponding ratios of a dynamical model. Moreover, it is confirmed that all methods for extracting resonance properties suffer from an unitary ambiguity which is due to some phenomenological contributions entering the models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the various methods for extracting the E2/M1 ratio \(R_{EM}\) of the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance from pion photoproduction data yield different numerical values because they correspond to different, unitarily equivalent splittings of the total amplitude into background and resonant parts; consequently, a meaningful comparison of the extracted ratios is not possible without specifying the underlying representation.

## Method

The authors compare three approaches for extracting resonance properties from pion photoproduction in the \(\Delta(1232)\) region: a dynamical model (DM) that solves the coupled \(\pi N\)-\(\Delta\)-\(\gamma N\) Lippmann-Schwinger equation, the effective Lagrangian (EL) approach with three unitarization schemes (Olsson, Noelle, K-matrix), and the speed plot (SP) analysis. They derive analytic relations connecting the EL parameters (resonance mass, width, coupling constants) to the DM quantities and compute the various \(R_{EM}\) ratios from the same underlying DM of Tanabe and Ohta (1985) to isolate the representation dependence.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\Delta\) masses extracted from the DM of Tanabe and Ohta (1985) via the three unitarization schemes are: \(M_\Delta \text{(Olsson)} = 1220\ \text{MeV}\), \(M_\Delta \text{(K-matrix)} = 1230\ \text{MeV}\), and \(M_\Delta \text{(Noelle)} = 1243\ \text{MeV}\).
*   The dressed \(R_{EM}\) ratios evaluated at the respective \(M_\Delta\) masses are: \(R^R_{EM}\text{(Olsson)} = -5.7\%\), \(R^R_{EM}\text{(Noelle)} = -0.2\%\), and \(R^R_{EM}\text{(K-matrix)} = -0.7\%\).
*   The \(\pi N\) scattering pole parameters from the speed plot analysis of Hohler (1992) are \(M_R = 1210\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\Gamma_R = 100\ \text{MeV}\), with a background phase shift at resonance of \(\delta_b = -23.5^\circ\).
*   The coupling constant \(g_{\pi N\Delta}(s)\) shows a 15 percent variation between the Olsson and K-matrix methods.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Davidson, Mukhopadhyay, and Wittman (1991) on the dependence of \(R_{EM}\) on the unitarization scheme in the effective Lagrangian approach, and the dynamical model of Tanabe and Ohta (1985). It also critiques the speed plot analysis of Hanstein, Drechsel, and Tiator (1996) for pion photoproduction, proposing a modified ansatz that respects Watson's theorem and treats hadronic and electromagnetic reactions consistently. The paper enables a clearer understanding of why published \(R_{EM}\) values vary between \(+4\%\) and \(-8\%\), and it provides a framework for comparing future extractions by specifying the underlying representation.
