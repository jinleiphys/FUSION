# The Neutron Spin Structure Function from the Deuteron Data in the Resonance Region

> Nuclear effects in the spin-dependent structure function $g_1$ of the deuteron are studied in the kinematics of future experiments at CEBAF, ($ν\leq 3~GeV, ~Q^2 \leq 2~GeV^2$). The magnitude of nuclear effects is found to be significantly larger than the one occurring in deep inelastic scattering ($ν\to \infty, ~Q^2\to \infty$). A possibility to measure the neutron structure functions in the CEBAF experiments with deuterium is analysed. It is found that disregarding or improperly treating nuclear effects in the region of nucleon resonances would lead to the ``extraction" of an unreliable function. A procedure aimed at correctly extracting the neutron structure function from the deuterium data is illustrated and conclusions about the experimental study of the $Q^2$ dependence of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the neutron are drawn.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in the spin-dependent structure function \(g_1\) of the deuteron are significantly larger in the resonance region (up to \(\sim 50\%\) at resonance maxima) than in deep inelastic scattering (\(\sim 7-9\%\)), meaning that the simple approximate formula used to extract the neutron structure function from deuteron data in the deep inelastic regime becomes completely unreliable in the resonance region; however, the integrals of the structure functions (such as for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule) can still be estimated with better than 3% accuracy using the same simple formula.

## Method

The authors computed the deuteron spin-dependent structure function \(g_1^D(x,Q^2)\) using a convolution formula that accounts for Fermi motion and the depolarizing effect of the D-wave, with the nucleon structure function parametrization from Burkert (which includes Breit-Wigner resonance structures) and the Bonn potential deuteron wave function. They compared the exact calculation with the approximate extraction formula \(g_1^D \approx (1-3P_D/2)(g_1^n + g_1^p)/2\) and demonstrated the failure of the latter in the resonance region. They also proposed a rigorous unfolding method that solves the integral equation as a set of linear algebraic equations to extract the neutron structure function exactly.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects in the resonance region are up to \(\sim 50\%\) in the maxima of the resonances, compared to \(\sim 7-9\%\) in the deep inelastic regime (with \(\sim 6-7\%\) from the depolarization factor and \(\sim 1-2\%\) from binding effects and Fermi motion).
- The effective number of nucleons \(N_{eff}(Q^2)\) is \(1.02\) at \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(0.997\) at \(Q^2 = 1.0\) GeV\(^2\), meaning setting \(N_{eff}=1\) leads to errors no larger than \(3\%\) for \(Q^2 = 0.1-2.0\) GeV\(^2\).
- The unfolding method extracts the neutron structure function with errors not larger than \(10^{-4}\), much smaller than expected experimental errors.
- The kinematics considered are \(\nu \leq 3\) GeV and \(Q^2 \leq 2\) GeV\(^2\), with specific calculations shown at \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(1.0\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear effects in deuteron spin structure functions from the deep inelastic regime (where the EMC effect was first observed) into the resonance region, where experiments were planned at CEBAF. It contradicts the assumption that the simple approximate extraction formula valid in deep inelastic scattering (used by groups such as the EMC, SMC, and SLAC collaborations) can be applied in the resonance region. The paper enables the correct extraction of the neutron spin structure function from future CEBAF deuterium data and allows the experimental study of the \(Q^2\) dependence of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the neutron, connecting low-energy theorems (\(Q^2 \to 0\)) with perturbative QCD (\(Q^2 \gg m^2\)).
