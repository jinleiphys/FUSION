# Roper + Pion decays as a test of the radial resonances wavefunction

> We calculate the decays of baryon resonances to the pion + Roper resonance channel in the frame of the flux-tube breaking model. The baryon and meson wavefunctions we use are obtained from a linear confinement semi-relativistic constituent quark model. The results, in good agreement with the existing data, provide a spectrum independant test on the radial form of the main component of the Roper resonance. They shed light on a likely orbital nature for the second delta resonance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the flux-tube breaking model's predictions for decays of baryon resonances to the Roper resonance plus a pion are in good agreement with existing data, but this agreement is conditional on the Roper wavefunction having a specific radial form (k=1) and on the second delta resonance (Delta(1600)) being interpreted as a mainly orbital excitation rather than a radial one.

## Method

The authors calculate partial decay widths for nonstrange baryon resonances decaying to the Roper resonance (N(1440)) plus a pion using the flux-tube breaking model, also known as the 3P0 quark pair creation model. The baryon and meson wavefunctions are obtained from a linear confinement semi-relativistic constituent quark model, with the Roper wavefunction given by phi* = N(1 - alpha(rho^2 + lambda^2)^(k/4)) where k=1,2,4 are tested. The decay amplitudes are integrated over the Roper mass using a relativistic Breit-Wigner distribution to account for the Roper's large width (350 MeV), and the experimental masses of the decaying resonances are used to ensure correct phase space.

## Key numbers

*   For the N(1710)P11 resonance, the square root of the decay width is 9.5 MeV^(1/2) at nominal mass and 12.0 MeV^(1/2) with Breit-Wigner weighting, compared to a PDG upper bound of 12.2 MeV^(1/2).
*   For the Delta(1620)S31 resonance, the square root of the decay width is 8.6 MeV^(1/2) at nominal mass and 8.0 MeV^(1/2) with Breit-Wigner weighting, compared to a PDG upper bound of 8.8 MeV^(1/2).
*   For the Delta(1910)P31 resonance, the square root of the decay width is 17.6 MeV^(1/2) at nominal mass and 14.9 MeV^(1/2) with Breit-Wigner weighting, compared to a PDG upper bound of 15.1 MeV^(1/2).
*   For the Delta(1600) resonance, the square root of the decay width is 2.8 MeV^(1/2) for k=1, 5.9 MeV^(1/2) for k=2, and 9.5 MeV^(1/2) for k=4 at nominal mass; the PDG data gives 8.5^(+4.1)_(-3.5) MeV^(1/2). Only the k=1 case falls within the experimental bounds.
*   The Roper resonance itself has a width of 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model calculations of Capstick and Roberts (no year given) who calculated decays of higher-lying N>2 band resonances to the Roper + pion channel, by also including N=1 and N=2 band resonances. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that one-gluon exchange potentials could not reproduce the ordering of the N(1440) Roper resonance and the N(1520) resonance, a problem noted by Scal (no year given). The results enable a test of the radial wavefunction form independent of the spectrum, and suggest that any hyperfine splitting interaction must place the first radial delta resonance above the first positive parity orbital delta resonances, contrary to the ordering for nucleon resonances.
