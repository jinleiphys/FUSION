# Diffractive photoproduction of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ using holographic QCD: gravitational form factors and GPD of gluons in the proton

> We present a holographic analysis of diffractive photoproducton of charmonium $J/ψ$ and upsilonium $Υ$ on a proton, considered as a bulk Dirac fermion, for all ranges of $\sqrt{s}$, i.e., from near threshold to very high energy. Using the bulk wave functions of the proton and vector mesons, within holographic QCD, and employing Witten diagrams in the bulk, we compute the diffractive photoproduction amplitude of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$. The holographic amplitude shows elements of the strictures of vector meson dominance (VMD). It is dominated by the exchange of a massive graviton or $2^{++}$ glueball resonances near threshold, and its higher spin-j counterparts that reggeize at higher energies. Both the differential and total cross sections are controlled by the gravitational form factor $A(t)$, and compare well to the recent results reported by the GlueX collaboration near threshold and the world data at large $\sqrt{s}$. The holographic gravitational form factors, including the D-term, which is due to the exchange of massive spin-0 glueballs, are in good agreement with lattice simulations. We use it to extract the holographic pressure and shear forces inside the proton. Finally, using a pertinent integral representation of the holographic gravitational form factor $A(t)$ near threshold, and its Pomeron counterpart way above threshold, we extract the generalized parton distribution (GPD) of gluons inside the proton at different resolutions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that diffractive photoproduction of heavy vector mesons (J/ψ and Υ) on a proton can be described within a single holographic QCD framework across all center-of-mass energies, from near threshold to very high energy, with the amplitude dominated by massive graviton (2⁺⁺ glueball) exchange near threshold and higher-spin exchanges that reggeize at higher energies, and that this framework successfully reproduces both GlueX collaboration data near threshold and world data at large √s.

## Method

The authors model the proton as a bulk Dirac fermion and vector mesons using bulk wave functions within a bottom-up holographic QCD approach (both hard-wall and soft-wall models). They compute the diffractive photoproduction amplitude using Witten diagrams in the bulk, where the exchange is mediated by a massive graviton (spin-2 glueball) near threshold and by reggeized higher-spin exchanges at higher energies. The holographic amplitude incorporates vector meson dominance (VMD) and is expressed in terms of the gravitational form factor A(t), from which the gluonic generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the proton is extracted via an integral representation.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass: M_V = 3.097 GeV; Υ mass: M_V = 9.460 GeV
- Proton mass: m_N = 0.94 GeV
- Threshold energy for J/ψ: √s_tr = m_N + M_V = 4.04 GeV
- At threshold for J/ψ: -t_min(s = s_tr) = m_N M_V²/(m_N + M_V) = 1.5² GeV²
- Decay constants: e_V f_V = 270 MeV for J/ψ (e_V = 2/3) and 238 MeV for Υ (e_V = 1/3)
- Soft-wall glueball mass spectrum: m_n² = 8κ̃_N²(n+1) with decay constant g̃_5 f_n = 2κ̃_N
- Soft-wall vector meson mass spectrum: m_n² = 4κ̃_V²(n*+1) with g_5 f_n = √2 κ̃_V (n+1/n*+1)^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends earlier holographic analyses of exclusive heavy meson production at high energy by Djuric and collaborators and by Lee, and complements the hybrid holographic analysis of threshold charmonium data by Hatta and collaborators (2018, 2019) that combined general QCD arguments with lattice results. The paper provides a unified holographic description from threshold to high energy, enabling extraction of the gluonic gravitational form factors, pressure and shear forces inside the proton, and the gluon GPD, with the gravitational form factors showing good agreement with lattice simulations.
