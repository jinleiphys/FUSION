# Constraining the pΛ interaction from a combined analysis of scattering data and correlation functions

> This work provides the first combined analysis of low-energy p$Λ$ scattering, considering both cross section and correlation data. The obtained results establish the most stringent constraints to date on the two-body p$Λ$ interaction, pointing to a weaker attraction than so far accepted. The best set of scattering lengths for the spin singlet and triplet are found to range from $f_0, f_1 = (2.1, 1.56)$ to $(3.34, 1.18)~$fm. With a chiral NY potential fine-tuned to those scattering parameters, the in-medium properties of the $Λ$ are explored and a potential depth of $U_Λ= -36.3\pm 1.3 \mathrm{(stat)}^{+2.5}_{-6.2}\mathrm{(syst)}$ MeV is found at nuclear matter saturation density.

# Constraining the pΛ interaction from a combined analysis of scattering data and correlation functions

## Key claim

The combined analysis of low-energy pΛ scattering and femtoscopic correlation data yields the most stringent constraints to date on the two-body pΛ interaction, indicating an overall weaker attraction than previously accepted from scattering data alone. The chiral NLO19 potential predictions are disfavored by up to 4.8σ, though the exact deviation depends on the cutoff value, with the Λ=600 MeV version showing only 1.1σ deviation from the best combined fit.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of 12 pΛ elastic cross section data points (six from Alexander et al. and six from Sechi-Zorn et al.) and six pΛ correlation functions measured by ALICE in pp collisions at 13 TeV, using an Usmani-type potential integrated into the CATS framework. The source for femtoscopic correlations was modeled using the CECA framework with a pre-fit of pp correlations using the Argonne v18 potential, and the six interaction parameters (repulsive core parameters for spin singlet and triplet states) were fitted alongside three source parameters. Multiple constrained fits were performed scanning the f0-f1 phase space to create exclusion plots, with the total χ² converted to standard deviations accounting for 9 degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Best scattering length range: f0, f1 = (2.1, 1.56) to (3.34, 1.18) fm
- Maximum allowed f0: 3.34 fm; minimum allowed f1: 1.18 fm
- With f0 fixed to 2.1 fm, best f1 = 1.56 ± 0.11 fm
- With f0 fixed to 2.91 fm (NLO19(600) value), best f1 = 1.32 ± 0.08 fm
- N²LO(550) potential disfavored at 4.8σ; NLO19(600) at 1.1σ; NLO13(600) at 4.6σ
- Λ single-particle potential at saturation density: UΛ(ρ0) = -36.3 ± 1.3 (stat) +2.5/-6.2 (syst) MeV
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 = 0.166 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pΛ scattering data from Alexander et al. (1960s) and Sechi-Zorn et al. (1960s) by incorporating high-precision femtoscopic correlation data from ALICE (2022), providing the first combined treatment of both datasets. The results contradict the stronger attraction predicted by chiral potentials such as NLO19 (Haidenbauer, 2019) and N²LO (Haidenbauer, 2023), which were fitted to scattering data alone. The derived UΛ value of approximately -36 MeV is more attractive than the semi-empirical value of -28 to -30 MeV obtained from hypernuclear constraints (Millener, 1988; Gal, 2016), consistent with the need for repulsive three-body forces as explored by Gerstung (2020) and Logoteta (2019) to resolve the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star studies.
