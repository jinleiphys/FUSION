# Dispersive analysis of the $\boldsymbol{ϕ\to γπ^0 π^0}$ process

> We present an analysis of the radiative decay $ϕ\to γπ^0 π^0$ in a dispersive framework, where the two-pion subsystem undergoes strong final-state interactions that cover the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ regions. We employ a coupled-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès framework that allows for a consistent treatment of two scalar resonances and crossed-channel singularities induced by the Born and vector-meson exchanges. We explicitly verify the equivalence between the modified and standard Muskhelishvili-Omnès representations for vector-meson pole contributions when the isoscalar Omnès matrix is chosen asymptotically bounded, and we adopt the standard representation in decay kinematics. This yields, for the first time, a parameter-free dispersive prediction for the kaon Born rescattering, which provides a dominant contribution. To obtain a good fit to the KLOE and SND data, we employ a once-subtracted coupled-channel dispersion relation with heavier left-hand cut contributions and two unknown subtraction constants. The results demonstrate the consistency among the data for $ππ$ scattering, $γγ$ fusion, and $ϕ$ radiative decay, thereby validating the underlying dispersive formalism and the input used for the hadronic Omnès matrix and left-hand cuts.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first parameter-free dispersive prediction for the kaon Born rescattering contribution to $\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \pi^0$, but to achieve a good fit to experimental data from KLOE and SND, a once-subtracted coupled-channel dispersion relation with two unknown subtraction constants is required, meaning the prediction is not fully parameter-free for the complete amplitude.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès (MO) dispersive framework for the $\pi\pi/K\bar{K}$ $S$-wave system, using an asymptotically bounded Omnès matrix constructed from a data-driven $N/D$ analysis of $\pi\pi$ scattering and $\pi\pi\to K\bar{K}$ data. They incorporate left-hand-cut contributions from charged-kaon Born exchange and vector-meson ($\rho$, $K^*$) exchanges, treating the $\rho$ width via a dispersively improved Breit-Wigner parameterization with an energy-dependent width, and verify the equivalence between modified and standard MO representations for vector-meson pole contributions.

## Key numbers

- The $\phi\to K^+K^-$ coupling extracted from the decay width is $|g_{\phi K^+K^-}| = 4.51(2)$.
- The $\rho^0\to\pi^0\gamma$ radiative coupling is $|C_{\rho^0\pi^0\gamma}| = 0.366(31)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$, extracted from a partial width of $69.3(11.8)\ \text{keV}$.
- The $\phi\rho\pi$ coupling is $|C_{\phi\rho\pi}| = 1.18(2)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$, obtained from the $\phi\to 3\pi$ decay in the narrow-width limit.
- The $K^*$ exchange coupling is taken as $|C_{\phi K^{*} K}| \approx 10\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$, estimated from $U(3)$ flavor symmetry and large-$N_c$ arguments.
- An integration cutoff $\sqrt{s_\text{cut}} = 2.0(2)\ \text{GeV}$ is used for the dispersive $\rho$ propagator, with its variation treated as a systematic uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive formalism previously applied to $\gamma^*\gamma^* \to \pi\pi$ for hadronic light-by-light scattering (Colangelo 2017, Danilkin 2021) and to the related decay $\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \eta$ (Moussallam 2021). It provides the first dispersive analysis of $\phi \to \gamma \pi^0 \pi^0$, demonstrating consistency among data for $\pi\pi$ scattering, $\gamma\gamma$ fusion, and $\phi$ radiative decay, thereby validating the hadronic Omnès matrix and left-hand-cut input used in the dispersive framework for future applications to the muon $g-2$ calculation.
