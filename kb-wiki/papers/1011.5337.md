# Traveling Dark Solitons in Superfluid Fermi Gases

> Families of dark solitons exist in superfluid Fermi gases. The energy-velocity dispersion and number of depleted particles completely determines the dynamics of dark solitons on a slowly-varying background density. For the unitary Fermi gas we determine these relations from general scaling arguments and conservation of local particle number. We find solitons to oscillate sinusoidally at the trap frequency reduced by a factor of $1/\sqrt{3}$. Numerical integration of the time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation determines spatial profiles and soliton dispersion relations across the BEC-BCS crossover and proves consistent with the scaling relations at unitarity.

## Key claim

For the unitary Fermi gas, the oscillation frequency of dark solitons in a harmonic trap is reduced by a factor of \(1/\sqrt{3}\) relative to the trap frequency, independent of the many-body parameter \(\beta\) in the equation of state; this result follows from general scaling arguments and conservation of local particle number, without requiring detailed knowledge of the soliton structure.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic energy-velocity dispersion relation for dark solitons in the unitary Fermi gas from three assumptions: adiabatic adjustment of the soliton to a slowly-varying background, vanishing of energy and particle number as the soliton velocity approaches the speed of sound, and a well-defined phase step across the soliton that also vanishes at the sound speed. They then solve the time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently to obtain spatial profiles and dispersion relations across the BEC-BCS crossover, parameterized by the coupling strength \(\eta = 1/(k_F a)\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi wave number and \(a\) the s-wave scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Oscillation frequency ratio \(\omega/\omega_t = 0.480\) for \(\eta = -0.5\) (BCS regime), 0.572 for \(\eta = 0\) (unitary), and 0.687 for \(\eta = 1\) (BEC regime), from BdG numerical calculations.
- Analytic prediction for the unitary case: \(\omega/\omega_t = 1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.577\), in excellent agreement with the numerical value 0.572.
- Speed of sound at unitarity: \(c = \sqrt{(1+\beta)/3}\, v_F\), where \(v_F\) is the Fermi velocity and \(\beta\) is the many-body parameter (taken as \(1+\beta = 0.6082\) from the BdG calculation).
- Phase step across the soliton at unitarity: \(\delta\phi = \pi (1 - v_s/c)\), where \(v_s\) is the soliton velocity.
- Soliton energy at unitarity: \(E_s = \mu^2 B \mathring{e} (\mathring{v}^2 - \tilde{v}^2)^2\), with \(\mathring{v} = \sqrt{(1+\beta)/3}\) and \(\mathring{e} = \mathring{v}^{-3}/(8\pi)\), where \(\tilde{v} = v_s/v_F\) and \(B = 2Am/[(1+\beta)\hbar]^2\) (with \(A\) the transverse area and \(m\) the atomic mass).

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of stationary dark solitons in Fermi gases using BdG mean-field theory (STR07) and provides the first analytic description of traveling (grey) solitons across the BEC-BCS crossover, including the unitary regime. The predicted oscillation frequency \(1/\sqrt{3}\) for the unitary gas differs from the known BEC result of \(1/\sqrt{2}\) (Anglin 1999; Becker 2008; Weller 2008) and agrees with parallel time-dependent simulations (Trento). The results enable experimental tests of soliton dynamics in trapped Fermi gases and motivate beyond-mean-field studies of soliton stability and quantum fluctuations.
