# Anisotropic flow of charged and identified hadrons in the quark-gluon string model for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

> The pseudorapidity behaviour of the azimuthal anisotropy parameters v_1 and v_2 of inclusive charged hadrons and their dependence on the centrality has been studied in Au+Au collisions at full RHIC energy of sqrt(s) = 200 GeV within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The QGSM simulation results for the directed flow v_1 show antiflow alignment within the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 2 in a fair agreement with the experimental v_1(eta) data, but cannot reproduce the further development of the antiflow up to |eta| around 3.5. The eta dependence of the elliptic flow v_2 extracted from the simulations agrees well with the experimental data in the whole pseudorapidity range for different centrality classes. The centrality dependence of the integrated elliptic flow of charged hadrons in the QGSM almost coincides with the PHOBOS experimental distribution. The transverse momentum dependence of the elliptic flow of identified and inclusive charged hadrons is studied also. The model reproduces quantitatively the low p_T part of the distributions rather good, but underestimates the measured elliptic flow for transverse momenta p_T > 1 GeV/c. Qualitatively, however, the model is able to reproduce the saturation of the v_2(p_T) spectra with rising p_T as well as the crossing of the elliptic flow for mesons and baryons.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon string model (QGSM) reproduces the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow \(v_2\) for charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV across the full pseudorapidity range and for different centrality classes, but it underestimates the measured elliptic flow for transverse momenta \(p_T > 1\) GeV/\(c\) by up to about 50%, indicating that new physical effects not included in the model (such as jets) are required.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), which is based on Gribov-Regge theory and incorporates string formation and fragmentation, resonance formation, and hadronic rescattering. They computed the directed flow \(v_1\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) as functions of pseudorapidity \(\eta\), centrality, and transverse momentum \(p_T\) for inclusive charged hadrons and identified hadrons (\(\pi^\pm, K^\pm, p, \bar{p}\)), and compared the results to experimental data from the PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow \(v_1\) of charged hadrons shows antiflow alignment within \(|\eta| \leq 2\) with magnitude less than 1% at \(|\eta| \leq 2\), in fair agreement with PHOBOS data for 6% to 55% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) for inclusive charged hadrons at mid-rapidity \(|\eta| < 1.0\) is almost constant; the mean value of \(v_2^{\rm ch}(|\eta| \le 2.5)\) is practically as large as the PHOBOS mid-rapidity value.
- For identified hadrons, the model reproduces the crossing of elliptic flow for mesons and baryons at \(p_T \approx 1.7\) GeV/\(c\); at \(p_T < 1.4\) GeV/\(c\) kaon \(v_2\) is larger than (anti)proton \(v_2\), while for \(p_T > 1.4\) GeV/\(c\) the opposite holds.
- The model underestimates the experimental elliptic flow of charged particles for \(p_T > 1.0\) GeV/\(c\) by up to about 50%; the QGSM saturates at \(v_2^{\rm ch} \approx 6\%\) while experimental data reach \(v_2^{\rm ch} \approx 14-16\%\) before declining for \(p_T \ge 3.0\) GeV/\(c\).
- The transverse mass spectra of \(\pi^\pm\) and \(K^\pm\) for simulated \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV agree well with STAR data.

## Context

This work extends earlier QGSM studies of elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\) GeV (Burau et al., 2004) to full RHIC energy and more differential observables, and it contrasts with hydrodynamic models (Kolb et al., 2000; Hirano et al., 2001) that predict boost-invariant \(v_2\) and with other microscopic models such as UrQMD (Bleicher and Stoecker, 2002) and AMPT (Chen et al., 2004) which show different directed flow behavior. The results enable future investigations into whether the missing high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow requires partonic energy loss mechanisms (jet quenching) or modifications to hadronic formation times, and they provide a baseline for identifying signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation beyond string-model dynamics.
