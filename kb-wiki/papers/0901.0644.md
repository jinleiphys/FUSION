# Irreducible SU(3) Schwinger Bosons

> We develop simple computational techniques for constructing all possible SU(3) representations in terms of irreducible SU(3) Schwinger bosons. We show that these irreducible Schwinger oscillators make SU(3) representation theory as simple as SU(2). The new Schwinger oscillators satisfy certain Sp(2,R) constraints and solve the multiplicity problem as well. These SU(3) techniques can be generalized to SU(N).

## Key claim

The paper claims that by defining new "irreducible" Schwinger boson operators that satisfy Sp(2,R) constraints, the construction of all SU(3) irreducible representations becomes as simple as the SU(2) case, with the caveat that this simplicity holds only after imposing the tracelessness condition via the Sp(2,R) constraint that the operator \(k_{-}\) annihilates the states.

## Method

The authors start with the standard SU(3) Schwinger boson construction using two independent harmonic oscillator triplets \(a^{\dagger}_\alpha\) (in the fundamental 3 representation) and \(b^{\dagger\alpha}\) (in the anti-fundamental \(3^*\) representation). They then define irreducible Schwinger boson creation operators \(A^{\dagger\alpha} = a^{\dagger\alpha} - \frac{1}{N_a+N_b+1} k_+ b^\alpha\) and \(B^{\dagger}_\alpha = b^{\dagger}_\alpha - \frac{1}{N_a+N_b+1} k_+ a_\alpha\), where \(k_+ = a^{\dagger} \cdot b^{\dagger}\) and \(k_- = a \cdot b\) are SU(3) invariant operators satisfying an Sp(2,R) algebra. The general \((n,m)\) irreducible representation is then constructed as a product of \(n\) copies of \(A^{\dagger}\) and \(m\) copies of \(B^{\dagger}\) acting on the vacuum, which automatically satisfies the tracelessness condition and solves the multiplicity problem.

## Key numbers

- The Sp(2,R) algebra generators are defined as \(k_+ \equiv a^{\dagger} \cdot b^{\dagger}\), \(k_- \equiv a \cdot b\), and \(k_0 \equiv \frac{1}{2}(N_a + N_b + 3)\), with the positive discrete series labeled by \(k = \frac{1}{2}(n+m+3)\) (taking values \(3/2, 2, 5/2, \ldots\)).
- The coefficient in the irreducible Schwinger boson definition is fixed as \(\mathrm{L}(n,m) = -1/(n+m+1)\).
- The projection operator coefficients are \(l_r(n,m) = (-1)^r (n+m+1-r)! / [r! (n+m+1)!]\), with the sum running up to \(q = \min(n,m)\).

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation of SU(2) (Schwinger, 1952) to SU(3) by solving the multiplicity problem that arises for \(N \ge 3\) due to SU(N) invariants. It builds on prior work by Mathur (2005) that used Sp(2,R) labeling to resolve SU(3) multiplicity, and provides a simpler computational technique that makes SU(3) representation theory as accessible as SU(2). The results enable straightforward generalization to SU(N) and have applications in lattice gauge theories, where irreducible SU(3) Schwinger bosons can create electric flux along links in SU(3) lattice gauge theory.
