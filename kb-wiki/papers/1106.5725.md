# Lattice Monte Carlo calculations for unitary fermions in a harmonic trap

> We present a new lattice Monte Carlo approach developed for studying large numbers of strongly interacting nonrelativistic fermions, and apply it to a dilute gas of unitary fermions confined to a harmonic trap. Our lattice action is highly improved, with sources of discretization and finite volume errors systematically removed; we are able to demonstrate the expected volume scaling of energy levels of two and three untrapped fermions, and to reproduce the high precision calculations published previously for the ground state energies for N = 3 unitary fermions in a box (to within our 0.3% uncertainty), and for N = 3, . . ., 6 unitary fermions in a harmonic trap (to within our ~ 1% uncertainty). We use this action to determine the ground state energies of up to 70 unpolarized fermions trapped in a harmonic potential on a lattice as large as 64^3 x 72; our approach avoids the use of importance sampling or calculation of a fermion determinant and employs a novel statistical method for estimating observables, allowing us to generate ensembles as large as 10^8 while requiring only relatively modest computational resources.

## Key claim

The authors present a new lattice Monte Carlo method for unitary fermions that avoids importance sampling and fermion determinant calculations, enabling simulations of up to 70 unpolarized fermions in a harmonic trap. The key hedge is that while the method reproduces benchmark results for few-body systems to within 0.3-1% uncertainty, the approach faces a serious distribution overlap problem that cannot be overcome simply by increasing statistics, requiring a novel statistical estimation method.

## Method

The theory is defined on a four-dimensional Euclidean lattice with open temporal boundary conditions, using an auxiliary scalar field to induce fermion-fermion interactions. The lattice action is highly improved with a Galilean-invariant interaction and a perfect single-particle dispersion relation up to a momentum cutoff, and the fermion determinant is independent of the auxiliary field making the quenched approximation exact. Multi-fermion correlators are computed by averaging over an ensemble of random Z2-valued auxiliary fields, with ground state energies extracted using a cumulant expansion technique to address the distribution overlap problem.

## Key numbers

- For N=3 unitary fermions in a box, the method reproduces previous high precision calculations to within 0.3% uncertainty
- For N=3,...,6 unitary fermions in a harmonic trap, the method reproduces precision calculations to within approximately 1% uncertainty
- Ground state energies determined for up to 70 unpolarized fermions in a harmonic trap on lattices as large as 64^3 x 72
- Ensembles as large as 10^8 generated using relatively modest computational resources
- Two-particle energy eigenvalues tuned using the first 27 roots eta_k* of the three-dimensional zeta-function S(eta), with the first root at eta_1* = -0.0959007

## Context

This work extends the lattice construction of Chen (2003) and preliminary findings from the Lattice 2010 conference proceedings, building on extensive prior numerical work on unitary fermions by groups including Lee (2005), Bulgac (2005), and others. The method enables studies of larger numbers of fermions than previous variational calculations (such as those by Chang (2004, 2007) and others), and finds evidence for persistent deviations from the thermodynamic limit for the range of N considered, in contrast to variational results. The approach opens the possibility for future applications of the lattice construction to other strongly interacting fermion systems.
