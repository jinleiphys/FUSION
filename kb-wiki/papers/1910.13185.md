# QCD phase diagram in chiral imbalance with self-consistent mean field approximation

> We employ a new self-consistent mean field approximation of NJL model, which introduces a free parameter $α$ ($α$ reflects the weight of different interaction channels), to study the effects of the chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ on QCD phase structure, especially the location of the QCD critical end point (CEP). We find that, at a high temperature, the critical temperature of QCD phase transition smoothly increases with $μ_5$ at the beginning, and then decreases rapidly. At low temperature and high baryon density region, the increase of the chiral chemical potential will reduce the critical chemical potential of phase transition. The temperature of the CEP shows a non-monotonic dependence on the chiral chemical potential with a long plateau around the maximum. At $μ_5=0$, we found that the CEP will disappear when the $α$ value is larger than 0.71 and will reappear when the $μ_5$ increases. This study is important for exploring the QCD phase structure and the location of CEP in chiral imbalanced systems.

## Key claim

Using a self-consistent mean field approximation of the NJL model with a free parameter α (which weights different interaction channels), the authors find that the chiral chemical potential μ₅ can cause the QCD critical end point (CEP) to reappear at finite μ₅ even when α > 0.71, a regime where the CEP is absent at μ₅ = 0.

## Method

The authors employ a new self-consistent mean field approximation of the two-flavor NJL model, constructing a Lagrangian as a linear combination (1−α)ℒ_NJL + αℒ_F of the original NJL Lagrangian and its Fierz-transformed form. They introduce a chiral chemical potential μ₅ to study chiral imbalance, derive self-consistent gap equations for the quark condensate σ, quark number density n, and chiral number density n₅, and solve these numerically to map the QCD phase diagram on the μ–T plane for various values of α and μ₅.

## Key numbers

- At α = 0.5 and μ₅ = 0, the CEP is located at (μ, T)^CEP = (340.1, 44) MeV.
- At α = 0.5, the maximum temperature of the CEP is T^CEP_max = 96 MeV.
- At α = 0.8 and μ₅ = 0, there is no CEP; the CEP reappears at μ₅ = 54 MeV with (μ, T)^CEP ≈ (359.2, 0) MeV.
- At α = 0.9, the CEP appears at a critical μ₅ = 90 MeV with μ = 351.3 MeV at T = 0; the maximum CEP temperature is T^CEP_max ≈ 49 MeV at μ₅ ≈ 270 MeV; the CEP vanishes at μ₅ ≈ 396 MeV.
- At α = 0.9, the minimum chemical potential of the CEP is μ^CEP_min = 252.1 MeV.
- The critical value of α for disappearance of the CEP at μ₅ = 0 is α = 0.71.
- The model parameters are: cutoff Λ = 631 MeV, current quark mass m₀ = 5.5 MeV, and coupling constant g = 5.074 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral imbalance effects on the QCD phase diagram using the PNJL model (Ruggieri 2011), Dyson-Schwinger equation (Cui 2016), and quark-meson model (Ruggieri 2011). It also builds on the new self-consistent mean field approximation introduced by Wang (2019) and PhysRevD.100.043018, which showed that for α > 0.71 the CEP disappears at μ₅ = 0. The key new result is that introducing a chiral chemical potential can restore the CEP even for α > 0.71, which the authors note has not been reported before. This suggests that adjusting centrality in heavy-ion collisions to vary μ₅ (estimated at 10–100 MeV) could help in experimental searches for the CEP.
