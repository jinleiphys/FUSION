# Effect of Shear viscosity on the nucleation of antikaon condensed matter in neutron stars

> We investigate a first-order phase transition from hadronic matter to antikaon condensed matter during the cooling stage of protoneutron stars. The phase transition proceeds through the thermal nucleation of antikaon condensed matter. In this connection we study the effect of shear viscosity on the thermal nucleation rate of droplets of antikaon condensed matter. Here we adopt the same equation of state for the calculation of shear viscosity and thermal nucleation time. We compute the shear viscosity of neutron star matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons and muons using the relativistic mean field model. The prefactor in the nucleation rate which includes the shear viscosity, is enhanced by several orders of magnitude compared with the $T^4$ approximation of earlier calculations. Consequently the thermal nucleation time in the $T^4$ approximation overestimates our result. Further the thermal nucleation of an antikaon droplet might be possible in our case for surface tension smaller than 20 MeV fm$^{-2}$.

## Key claim

The inclusion of shear viscosity in the dynamical prefactor of the thermal nucleation rate enhances the prefactor by several orders of magnitude compared with the earlier \(T^4\) approximation, and thermal nucleation of an antikaon condensed droplet might be possible during protoneutron star cooling only for surface tension smaller than 20 MeV fm\(^{-2}\).

## Method

The authors adopt the homogeneous nucleation formalism of Langer to compute the thermal nucleation rate of antikaon condensed droplets in metastable nuclear matter. They calculate the shear viscosity of neutron star matter (composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) using coupled Boltzmann transport equations and a relativistic mean field model (GM1 parameter set), and use the same equation of state for both the shear viscosity and the nucleation rate calculation. The prefactor in the nucleation rate includes the shear viscosity term while neglecting thermal conductivity and bulk viscosity, and the critical droplet radius is obtained from the balance of surface tension and pressure difference between the two phases.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity is calculated for temperatures \(T = 1\), 10, and 30 MeV as a function of baryon density; the prefactor including shear viscosity is several orders of magnitude higher than the prefactor including only thermal conductivity for both correlation lengths \(\xi = 2\) and 5 fm.
- At baryon density \(n_b = 2.3 n_0\) and surface tension \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\), the critical droplet radius is 6.32 fm; for \(\sigma = 15\) and 20 MeV fm\(^{-2}\), the critical radii are 9.48 and 12.6 fm respectively.
- For \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and \(\xi = 5\) fm, the thermal nucleation time is 1 s at a temperature \(T \sim 10\) MeV; the \(T^4\) approximation gives a nucleation time of \(\sim 10^3\) s at the same temperature.
- Thermal nucleation of an antikaon droplet is possible for surface tension \(\sigma < 20\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) because the nucleation time is less than the protoneutron star cooling time of a few \(10^2\) s.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, specifically the thermal nucleation of antikaon condensed matter (Noronha and Sanches 2007; Sanches and Noronha 2008) and quark matter (Bombaci and Datta 2000; Lasota and Rajagopal 2001), by incorporating a realistic calculation of shear viscosity rather than a parametrized form. The authors previously computed shear viscosity in antikaon condensed matter (Banik and Bandyopadhyay 2010) but did not include nucleation; here they use the same equation of state for both. The results enable more accurate predictions of the onset of antikaon condensation during protoneutron star cooling and highlight the need to include bulk viscosity in future studies of rotating neutron stars.
