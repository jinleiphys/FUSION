# Pion Superfluidity beyond Mean Field Approximation In Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> We investigate pion superfluidity in the frame of two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model beyond mean field approximation. We calculate the thermodynamics to the next to leading order in an expansion in the inverse number of colors, including both quark and meson contributions at finite temperature and baryon and isospin density. Due to the meson fluctuations, the Sarma phase which exists at mean field level is washed away, and the Bose-Einstein condensation region at low isospin density is highly suppressed.

# Pion Superfluidity beyond Mean Field Approximation In Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

## Key claim

Meson fluctuations, when included beyond mean field approximation in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, wash away the Sarma phase that exists at mean field level and highly suppress the Bose-Einstein condensation region at low isospin density; these conclusions are drawn from a next-to-leading order calculation in an expansion in the inverse number of colors, using a first-order derivative expansion around the mean field minimum, which is valid primarily in the BEC region but may require higher-order terms in the BCS region.

## Method

The authors calculate the thermodynamic potential to order 1/N_c in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, including both the mean field quark contribution and the meson fluctuation contribution from sigma and charged pion modes via the random phase approximation. The meson contribution is evaluated in pole approximation (neglecting scattering phase shifts), and the total thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain modified gap equations for the chiral condensate (or effective quark mass) and the pion condensate, with the meson fluctuations encoded as medium-dependent effective coupling constants G_sigma and G_pi. Numerical solutions are obtained using a hard three-momentum cutoff regularization with parameters m_0 = 5 MeV, G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}, and Lambda = 653 MeV, which reproduce the vacuum pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV and decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of pion superfluidity is suppressed from about 150 MeV in mean field treatment to about 80 MeV when meson fluctuations are included (at fixed baryon density n_B/n_0 = 0.5, where n_0 = 0.17/fm^3 is normal nuclear density).
- The BEC-BCS crossover point, defined by mu_I/2 - m = 0, shifts from n_I/n_0 = 1.68 in mean field to n_I/n_0 = 1.29 when meson fluctuations are included (at T = 50 MeV and n_B/n_0 = 0.5).
- The meson mass M_{pi+} and quark mass m cross at approximately n_I/n_0 = 1.4 at the critical temperature (n_B/n_0 = 0.5), consistent with the BEC-BCS crossover determined by the effective chemical potential.
- The model parameters are: current quark mass m_0 = 5 MeV, coupling constant G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}, cutoff Lambda = 653 MeV, yielding vacuum pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV and pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends mean field studies of pion superfluidity in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite baryon and isospin density, such as those by He et al. (2005) and Ebert et al. (2005), which identified the Sarma phase and BEC-BCS crossover at mean field level. The inclusion of meson fluctuations follows the 1/N_c expansion approach of Zhuang et al. (1994) for the quark-meson plasma. The results show that the Sarma phase, which appeared as intermediate-temperature superfluidity in mean field calculations by He et al. (2005), is eliminated by meson fluctuations, and the BEC region is significantly reduced. This enables more realistic predictions for the QCD phase diagram at finite isospin density relevant to compact stars and heavy ion collisions, and suggests that higher-order derivative terms in the fluctuation expansion may be needed for accurate treatment of the BCS region.
