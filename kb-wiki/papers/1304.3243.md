# Relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics with memory functions and colored noises

> Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics including hydrodynamic fluctuations is formulated by putting an emphasis on non-linearity and causality. As a consequence of causality, dissipative currents become dynamical variables and noises appeared in an integral form of constitutive equations should be colored ones from fluctuation-dissipation relations. Nevertheless noises turn out to be white ones in its differential form when noises are assumed to be Gaussian. The obtained ifferential equations are very useful in numerical implementation of relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, to maintain causality in relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics, dissipative currents must be treated as dynamical variables and the noise terms in the integral form of the constitutive equations must be colored noises; however, if the noises are assumed to be Gaussian, the differential form of the constitutive equations contains white noises, making numerical implementation practical.

## Method

The authors formulate relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics by writing constitutive equations for dissipative currents (bulk pressure, shear stress tensor, and diffusion) as convolutions of thermodynamic forces with retarded memory functions (Green's functions). They then apply the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to relate the power spectrum of the hydrodynamic fluctuations to these memory functions. For the specific case of second-order dissipative hydrodynamics with relaxation times, they derive the memory functions and show that the corresponding fluctuations are colored; they then demonstrate that, under a Gaussian noise assumption, the differential form of the constitutive equations yields white noise correlations.

## Key numbers

* The memory function for bulk pressure in the second-order case is given by \(G_{\Pi}(x-x') = \zeta \frac{1}{\tau_\Pi} \exp\left(-\frac{\tau-\tau'}{\tau_\Pi}\right) \theta^{(4)}(x-x')\), where \(\zeta\) is the bulk viscosity and \(\tau_\Pi\) is the relaxation time for bulk pressure.
* The memory function for shear stress is \(G_{\pi}(x-x')^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} = 2\eta \frac{1}{\tau_\pi} \exp\left(-\frac{\tau-\tau'}{\tau_\pi}\right) \Delta(\tau;\tau')^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} \theta^{(4)}(x-x')\), where \(\eta\) is the shear viscosity and \(\tau_\pi\) is the relaxation time for shear stress.
* For the first-order dissipative hydrodynamics, the memory functions are local: \(G^*_{\Pi}(x) = 2\zeta \delta^{(4)}(x)\), \(G^*_{\pi}(x)^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} = 4\eta \delta^{(4)}(x) \Delta^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}\), and \(G^*_{ij}(x)^{\mu\alpha} = -2\kappa_{ij} \delta^{(4)}(x) \Delta^{\mu \alpha}\).
* In the differential form of the second-order constitutive equations, the noise correlations are white: \(\langle\xi_{\Pi}(x) \xi_{\Pi}(x')\rangle = 2 T \zeta \delta^{(4)}(x-x')\), \(\langle\xi_\pi^{\mu\nu}(x) \xi_\pi^{\alpha\beta}(x')\rangle = 4 T \eta \delta^{(4)}(x-x')\Delta^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}\), and \(\langle\xi_i^\mu(x) \xi_j^\alpha(x')\rangle = 2 T \kappa_{ij}\delta^{(4)}(x-x')\Delta^{\mu\alpha}\).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations initiated by Landau and Lifshitz (Landau and Lifshitz, 1959; Lifshitz and Pitaevskii, 1980) to the relativistic regime while respecting causality, which is violated by the first-order relativistic Navier-Stokes theory (Eckart, 1940; Hiscock and Lindblom, 1985). It builds on the second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics of Israel and Stewart (Israel, 1976; Israel and Stewart, 1979) and on earlier relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics studies (Calzetta and Hu, 1997; Kapusta et al., 2011; Kumar et al., 2013). The formulation enables event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions, allowing for the extraction of transport coefficients such as shear and bulk viscosities.
