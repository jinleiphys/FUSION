# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov study of quantum shell effects on the path to fission in $^{180}$Hg, $^{236}$U and $^{256}$Fm

> Quantum shell effects stabilising fission fragments with various shapes have been invoked as a factor determining the distribution of nucleons between the fragments at scission. Shell effects also induce asymmetric shapes in the nucleus on its way to fission well before the final fragments are (pre)formed. These shell effects are studied in fission of $^{180}$Hg, $^{236}$U and $^{256}$Fm with constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the D1S parametrisation of the Gogny interaction. Strutinsky shell energy correction and single-particle energy level density near the Fermi surface are computed. Several neutron and proton shell effects are identified as drivers towards asymmetric fission. Shell effects are also used to identify the preformation of the fragments in the later stage of fission.

## Key claim

Quantum shell effects drive asymmetric fission well before the final fragments are preformed, with multiple neutron and proton shells acting sequentially along the fission path rather than a single shell effect determining the final asymmetry. The authors show that both the Strutinsky shell energy correction and a newly introduced single-particle level density near the Fermi surface can identify these shell effects, though the two methods do not always agree on which shells are dominant.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the D1S parametrisation of the Gogny interaction for three fissioning nuclei: 180Hg, 236U, and 256Fm. Potential energy surfaces were generated by constraining the quadrupole moment Q20 and octupole moment Q30, with higher multipole moments left free. The Strutinsky shell energy correction and a newly defined single-particle energy level density near the Fermi surface (ηn,p) were computed from the HFB single-particle spectra to identify shell effects along the minimum-energy fission path up to scission, defined as when the neck density falls below 0.08 fm−3.

## Key numbers

- The fission path of 180Hg shows a discontinuity at Q20 ≈ 100 b, corresponding to a jump in octupole moment Q30.
- 256Fm exhibits a first barrier of about 10.5 MeV, followed by a shallow second minimum at Q20 ≈ 52 b and a shallow third minimum at Q20 ≈ 72 b, separated by a ∼1 MeV barrier.
- The level density window ΔE was set to 3 MeV for 180Hg and 2.5 MeV for 236U and 256Fm.
- The Strutinsky smoothing width was chosen as γ = 41 A−1/3, where A is the number of neutrons or protons.
- Convergence tests for 256Fm show ground-state energies of −1891.998 MeV (N=14), −1892.412 MeV (N=15), and −1892.710 MeV (N=16), with barrier heights of 11.220 MeV, 11.248 MeV, and 11.265 MeV respectively; at N=20 the barrier is 11.236 MeV and at N=24 it is 11.175 MeV.
- The pre-fragments at scission are identified as: 180Hg → 82Kr + 98Ru, 236U → 96Sr + 140Xe, and 256Fm → 112Ru + 144Ba.
- Deformed shell gaps identified in the pre-fragments include N=46, Z=38,44 with elongated shapes, and Z=52,56, N=84,88 with compact octupole shapes.

## Context

This work extends studies of shell effects in fission that began with Mayer (1948) and Meitner (1950) on spherical magic nuclei, and later work by Wilkins et al. (1976) on deformed shell effects in fragments and Scamps et al. (2018) on pear-shaped fragments. The authors build on the Strutinsky method (Strutinsky 1967, 1968) and earlier suggestions by Gustafsson (1971) that neutron orbital energy decreases induce asymmetric shapes near the second saddle point of actinides, and by Ichikawa (2019) for the mercury region. The results contradict an earlier interpretation by Moller (2009) for 232Th that a single shell effect from a partially formed spherical Sn-like fragment is responsible for lowering the level density from the asymmetric saddle to scission. The work enables future studies combining shell effect identification with time-dependent mean-field approaches to investigate the interplay between energy dissipation and shell effects in fission dynamics.
