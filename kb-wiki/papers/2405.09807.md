# Three-body forces and Efimov physics in nuclei and atoms

> This review article presents historical developments and recent advances in our understanding on the three-body forces and Efimov physics, from an interdisciplinary viewpoint encompassing nuclear physics and cold atoms. Theoretical attempts to elucidate the three-body force with the chiral effective field theory are explained, followed by an overview of experiments aimed at observing signatures of the nuclear three-body force. Some recent experimental and theoretical works in the field of cold atoms devoted to measuring and engineering three-body forces among atoms are also presented. As a phenomenon arising from the three-body effect, Efimov physics in both cold atoms and nuclear systems is reviewed.

## Key claim

This review argues that three-body forces and Efimov physics provide a unifying interdisciplinary framework connecting nuclear physics and cold-atom systems, but the authors emphasize that the three-body force in nuclei is still not fully constrained from first principles and requires input from few-nucleon data to fix low-energy constants such as \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) that cannot be determined from two-nucleon scattering alone.

## Method

The authors review the chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) derivation of three-nucleon forces (3NFs) up to N\(^4\)LO, including tree-level and one-loop diagrams classified by six topologies (two-pion exchange, two-pion-one-pion exchange, ring, one-pion-contact, two-pion-contact, and purely contact). They also survey experimental signatures of nuclear three-body forces in few-nucleon scattering and binding energies, and present cold-atom experiments that engineer three-body forces via exchange of virtual excitations to quantum-simulate the Fujita-Miyazawa mechanism. The review covers Efimov physics as a universal three-body phenomenon in both cold atoms and nuclear systems.

## Key numbers

* The leading 3NF contributions arise at N\(^2\)LO (order \(Q^3\)) and depend on the \(\pi N\) LECs \(c_1\), \(c_3\), \(c_4\) and the short-range LECs \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) that must be fitted to three-nucleon data.
* The N\(^3\)LO (order \(Q^4\)) two-pion exchange 3NF is parameter-free and involves the loop function \(A(q_2) = \frac{1}{2 q_2} \arctan \frac{q_2}{2 M_\pi}\).
* The N\(^4\)LO (order \(Q^5\)) two-pion exchange 3NF introduces two additional LECs \(\bar e_{14}\) and \(\bar e_{17}\) from \(\mathcal{L}_{\pi N}^{(4)}\).
* The state-of-the-art SMS \(NN\) potential at N\(^4\)LO\(^+\) uses cutoff values \(\Lambda = 400, 450, 500, 550\) MeV and achieves a statistically perfect description of the Granada-2013 database of neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below pion production threshold.
* The Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon force model was proposed in 1957 and is driven by virtual pion-nucleon scattering with intermediate excitation of the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance.

## Context

This review extends the long line of work on three-nucleon forces that began with Fujita and Miyazawa (1957) and continued with phenomenological models such as Tucson-Melbourne 99 and Urbana IX, as well as the chiral EFT framework pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991). It connects to cold-atom experiments that realize the Fujita-Miyazawa mechanism via engineered three-body forces and to universal Efimov physics observed in both cold atoms and nuclear systems. The review enables future work on constraining short-range 3NF LECs from few-nucleon data, extending three-body force studies to strange baryonic systems such as \(NN\Lambda\) interactions for neutron star properties, and further developing quantum simulations of nuclear forces with ultracold atoms.
