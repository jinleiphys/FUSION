# Vector Meson Propagator and Baryon Current Conservation

> If baryons couple only with $ω$-mesons, one found the baryon spectral function may be negative. We show this unacceptable result is caused by the $k_μk_ν$-terms in the $ω$-meson propagator. Their contribution may not vanish in approximate calculations which violate the baryon current conserves. A rule is suggested, by which the calculated baryon spectral function is well behaved.

# Vector Meson Propagator and Baryon Current Conservation

## Key claim
The negative baryon spectral function found by Krein, Nielsen, Puff, and Wilets (KNPW) in self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations with only ω-meson coupling is caused by neglecting the k_μk_ν terms in the ω-meson propagator; if an adequate part of these terms is included, the spectral function becomes non-negative and well behaved, provided the contribution of a spurious zero-mass pole is removed.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (SCHF) calculations of the renormalized baryon propagator for zero-density nuclear matter with baryons coupled only to ω-mesons, using the Dyson-Schwinger equations. They included the k_μk_ν terms from the ω-meson propagator (Eq. 12) in the self-energy calculations, then removed the contribution from the spurious zero-mass pole by discarding the Z_c(0) term from the y-integration in Eqs. (21a,b). The coupled equations were solved iteratively for schemes BP (baryon self-consistent) and FSC (fully self-consistent), using the spectral representation method of Redmond to obtain ghost-free results.

- Ghost pole positions found in the uncorrected calculation: -100.4468 ± i55.2553 fm⁻²
- Threshold for continuum spectrum: m₁ = M_t + m_v
- Threshold for ω-meson continuum: th = (2M_t)²

## Context
This work extends the findings of Krein, Nielsen, Puff, and Wilets (KNPW), who first reported the negative baryon spectral function problem in SCHF calculations with only ω-meson coupling. The authors show that the problem arises from neglecting k_μk_ν terms in the propagator, contrary to the standard approximation based on baryon current conservation. The results confirm earlier work by the same group (Wu et al., 2000) that there is no need to include the meson propagator in the self-consistency requirement, as schemes BP and FSC give nearly identical results. The method enables correct calculation of baryon spectral functions in models where baryon current conservation is violated by the approximation scheme, and the authors note that studies of more complicated cases including π, σ, and chiral π-σ mesons are ongoing.
