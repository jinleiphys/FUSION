# The X17 boson and the $^3$H$(p,e^+ e^-)^4$He and $^3$He$(n,e^+ e^-)^4$He processes: a theoretical analysis

> The present work deals with $e^+$-$e^-$ pair production in the four-nucleon system. We first analyze the process as a purely electromagnetic one in the context of a state-of-the-art approach to nuclear strong-interaction dynamics and nuclear electromagnetic currents, derived from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). Next, we examine how the exchange of a hypothetical low-mass boson would impact the cross section for such a process. We consider several possibilities, that this boson is either a scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial particle. The ab initio calculations use exact hyperspherical-harmonics methods to describe the bound state and low-energy spectrum of the $A\,$=$\,4$ continuum, and fully account for initial state interaction effects in the $3+1$ clusters. While electromagnetic interactions are treated to high orders in the chiral expansion, the interactions of the hypothetical boson with nucleons are modeled in leading-order $χ$EFT (albeit, in some instances, selected subleading contributions are also accounted for). We also provide an overview of possible future experiments probing pair production in the $A\,$=$\,4$ system at a number of candidate facilities.facilities.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of electron-positron pair production in the four-nucleon system, finding that while the angular correlation between leptons is sensitive to the low-energy structure of the helium-4 continuum, the calculations show no resonance-like structure that could mimic the X17 signal observed in the ATOMKI experiments, contrary to a conjecture by Kalman (2020).

## Method

The authors performed ab initio calculations using hyperspherical-harmonics methods to describe the bound state and low-energy continuum of the A=4 system, with nuclear interactions and electromagnetic currents derived from two versions of chiral effective field theory (one with only pions and nucleons, and one also including Delta-isobars). They computed the five-fold differential cross sections for the 3H(p,e+e-)4He and 3He(n,e+e-)4He processes first as purely electromagnetic, then by including the exchange of a hypothetical X17 boson with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial couplings modeled at leading order in chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- The X17 boson mass is taken as M_X = 17 MeV, consistent with the ATOMKI anomaly claims
- For the vector coupling case, the most stringent lower bound on the electron coupling is |epsilon_e| > 2 x 10^-4 from the SLAC E141 experiment, while the upper bound |epsilon_e| < 2 x 10^-3 comes from the KLOE-2 experiment
- The critical opening angle for the lepton pair in the ATOMKI setup is theta*_ee approximately 112 degrees, above which two electron energies satisfy the condition Q^2 = M_X^2
- The five-fold differential cross section at angles theta_ee of 115 degrees and 155 degrees is of the order of picobarns, making accurate measurement experimentally challenging
- The effective width used for illustration in the calculations is Gamma^eff_X = 0.4 MeV, while the natural width from decay into e+e- pairs is of order eV or less

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical analyses of the X17 anomaly by Feng (2016, 2020) and Kozaczuk (2016), which used resonance-saturation approaches for the 7Li(p,e+e-)8Be and 3H(p,e+e-)4He reactions, by instead performing fully microscopic ab initio calculations that account for direct non-resonant capture contributions. The calculations enable future experimental searches at facilities such as MEGII, DarkLight, SHiP, BelleII, and NA64 to be interpreted with reduced nuclear-structure uncertainties, and provide a framework for determining the nature (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial) of the hypothetical X17 boson from the energy and angular dependence of the cross section.
