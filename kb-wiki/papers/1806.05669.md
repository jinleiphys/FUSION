# Probing the in-Medium QCD Force by Open Heavy-Flavor Observables

> The determination of the color force in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is a key objective in the investigation of strong-interaction matter. Open and hidden heavy-flavor observables in heavy-ion collisions (HICs) are believed to provide insights into this problem by comparing calculations of heavy-quark (HQ) and quarkonium transport with pertinent experimental data. In this work, we utilize the $T$-matrix formalism to compute charm-quark transport coefficients for various input potentials previously extracted from simultaneous fits to lattice-QCD data for HQ free energies, quarkonium correlators and the QGP equation of state. We investigate the impact of off-shell effects (spectral functions) in the QGP medium on the HQ transport, and compare to earlier results using the free or internal HQ energies as potential proxies. We then employ the transport coefficients in relativistic Langevin simulations for HICs to test the sensitivity of heavy-flavor observables to the HQ interactions in the QGP. We find that a strongly-coupled $T$-matrix solution generates a HQ elliptic flow comparable to the results from the internal energy at low momentum, driven by a long-range remnant of the confining force, while falling off stronger with increasing 3-momentum. The weakly coupled $T$-matrix solution, whose underlying potential is close to the free energy, leads to an elliptic flow well below the experimentally observed range.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a strongly-coupled T-matrix solution generates a charm-quark elliptic flow comparable to results from the internal energy at low momentum, driven by a long-range remnant of the confining force, while falling off stronger with increasing 3-momentum; the weakly-coupled T-matrix solution, whose underlying potential is close to the free energy, leads to an elliptic flow well below the experimentally observed range.

## Method

The authors utilized a thermodynamic T-matrix formalism to compute charm-quark transport coefficients for various input potentials previously extracted from simultaneous fits to lattice-QCD data for heavy-quark free energies, quarkonium correlators, and the QGP equation of state. They incorporated off-shell effects (spectral functions) in the QGP medium and employed the resulting transport coefficients in relativistic Langevin simulations for heavy-ion collisions to test the sensitivity of heavy-flavor observables to the heavy-quark interactions in the QGP.

## Key numbers

- The force from the internal energy U at r approximately 0.5 fm and T = 0.194 GeV amounts to about 2.5 GeV/fm, exceeding the vacuum force by about a factor of 2.
- The force from the SCS potential V_s at r approximately 0.5 fm and T = 0.194 GeV is larger than in vacuum by about 20 percent.
- The maximum coupling peak for V_s is located at r_max = 0.85 fm at T = 0.194 GeV down to r_max = 0.5 fm at T = 0.400 GeV.
- The strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.27 for all four potentials at short distance.
- For low-momentum charm quarks at low temperatures, most momentum transfers occur in a 0.5 GeV window around k = 0.4 GeV.
- The zero-momentum relaxation rate for the SCS is about four times larger than for the WCS at small momenta and small temperatures.
- Off-shell effects almost double the transport coefficients in the small-momentum and low-temperature region for the SCS.
- Switching back to a delta-function for the outgoing charm quark reduces the low-momentum low-temperature relaxation rate by almost 20 percent.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic T-matrix approach developed by the authors in Liu (2017) and earlier work by Mannarelli (2005) and Riek (2010), which assessed consequences of the in-medium potential for quarkonia, individual heavy quarks, and the surrounding medium. The paper builds on lattice-QCD computations of the free energy of a heavy quark-antiquark pair by Petreczky (2004) and Kaczmarek (2005), and potential model studies by Wong (2004), Cabrera (2006), Alberico (2006), Mocsy (2007), and Riek (2010). The results enable discrimination between different potential strengths via experimental observables such as the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v_2) of charm quarks and D mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
