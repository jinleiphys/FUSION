# Occurrence conditions for two-dimensional Borromean systems

> We search for Borromean three-body systems of identical bosons in two dimensional geometry, i.e. we search for bound three-boson system without bound two-body subsystems. Unlike three spatial dimensions, in two-dimensional geometry the two- and three-body thresholds often coincide ruling out Borromean systems. We show that Borromean states can only appear for potentials with substantial attractive and repulsive parts. Borromean states are most easily found when a barrier is present outside an attractive pocket. Extensive numerical search did not reveal Borromean states for potentials without an outside barrier. We outline possible experimental setups to observe Borromean systems in two spatial dimensions.

## Key claim

Borromean three-body states of identical bosons in two-dimensional geometry can only occur for two-body potentials that have both substantial attractive and repulsive parts, and are most easily found when a repulsive barrier is present outside an attractive pocket. The abstract's claim that Borromean states "can only appear for potentials with substantial attractive and repulsive parts" is hedged by the authors' extensive numerical search, which did not reveal Borromean states for potentials without an outside barrier, though they cannot rigorously exclude this possibility.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body radial Schrödinger equation in two dimensions for identical bosons, deriving threshold conditions for binding using the Jost function formalism and zero-energy wave functions. They then analyze three-body binding using the hyperspherical expansion method with the lowest hyperradial potential, and perform numerical searches using the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions for potentials composed of linear combinations of Gaussians. The study focuses on square well potentials with a barrier or core, and a Gaussian-based potential with zero net volume, to identify conditions for Borromean binding.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of Borromean systems in three dimensions (Richard 1994, Richard 2000) to two-dimensional geometry, where the problem is fundamentally different because two-body binding is easier to achieve in 2D. Previous numerical studies in momentum space (Tjon 1980, Frederico 1988) and coordinate space (Cabral 1979, Lim 1980, Nielsen 1999, Hammer 2004, Blume 2005, Hammer 2011) found no Borromean states for many potential classes in 2D, with only one example of an appropriate two-body potential in the literature (Nielsen 1999). The results put limitations on zero-range formalisms used to describe few-body dynamics in 2D (Kartavtsev 2006, Bellotti 2011, 2012, 2013), which cannot describe Borromean states where finite-range techniques must be used. The work enables experimental searches for Borromean systems in 2D using cold ionized atoms, polar molecules in external fields, or dipolar systems in multilayer geometries.
