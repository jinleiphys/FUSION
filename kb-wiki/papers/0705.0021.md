# Quark Coalescence based on a Transport Equation

> We employ the Boltzmann equation for describing hadron production from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We propose resonance formation in quark-antiquark scattering as the dominant meson-production channel, which, in particular, ensures that energy is conserved in the recombination process. This, in turn, facilitates a more controlled extension of hadronization to low transverse momenta ($p_T$), and to address the experimentally observed transition from a hydrodynamic regime to constituent quark-number scaling (CQNS). Based on input distributions for strange and charm quarks with azimuthal asymmetries, $v_2(p_T)$, characteristic for RHIC energies, we recover CQNS at sufficiently high $p_T$, while at low $p_T$ a scaling with transverse kinetic energy is found, reminiscent to experiment. The dependence of the transition regime on microscopic QGP properties, i.e. resonance widths and $Q$-values in the $q+\bar q \to M$ process, is elucidated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Boltzmann transport equation with resonant quark-antiquark scattering as the dominant meson-production channel, which explicitly conserves 4-momentum and recovers a well-defined thermal equilibrium limit, thereby enabling a more controlled extension of hadronization to low transverse momenta than conventional coalescence models.

## Method

The authors formulate hadron production via a Boltzmann equation for meson phase-space distributions, where mesons are formed through resonant $q+\bar q \to M$ processes described by a relativistic Breit-Wigner cross section. They compute $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ for $\phi$, $D$, and $J/\psi$ mesons in the quasi-equilibrium limit, using input quark distributions with azimuthal asymmetries characteristic of RHIC energies and a homogeneous cylindrical fireball with collective transverse expansion.

## Key numbers

- $\phi$ meson: mass $m_\phi \simeq 1020$ MeV, default width $\Gamma_\phi = 50$ MeV, strange quark mass $m_s = 400$ MeV
- $D$ meson: mass $m_D = 1.9$ GeV, width $\Gamma_D = 100$ MeV, charm quark mass $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, light quark mass $m_u = 350$ MeV
- $J/\psi$: mass $m_{J/\psi} = 3.1$ GeV, width $\Gamma_{J/\psi} = 100$ MeV
- Hadronization temperature $T_c \simeq 170$ MeV
- Fireball eigenvolume $V^* \simeq 1500$ fm$^3$ for 0-10% central Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Surface flow velocity $\beta_0 = 0.55$ adjusted to reproduce $\phi$ $p_T$ spectra
- Quark $v_2$ saturation value $v_2^{\rm sat} = 7\%$ for light quarks ($u$, $d$, $s$) at $p_T^{\rm sat} = 1.1$ GeV; for charm quarks $p_T^{\rm sat} \simeq 2$ GeV
- Charm-quark fugacity $\gamma_c(T=170\ \text{MeV}) = 7.8$ from Ref. Grandchamp (2003)

## Context

This work extends conventional quark coalescence models (QCMs) such as those by Greco (2003), Fries (2003), and Molnar (2003) by replacing the collinear, instantaneous approximation with a transport equation that conserves energy and has a proper equilibrium limit. It addresses the transition from the hydrodynamic regime at low $p_T$ (described by models of Teaney (2001), Hirano (2002), Kolb (2003)) to constituent quark-number scaling at intermediate $p_T$, and recovers the universal kinetic-energy scaling of $v_2$ recently observed by PHENIX (Adare, 2006). The approach enables future studies of time evolution, baryon formation via quark-diquark pictures as in Miao (2007), and hadron channels with negative $Q$ values such as pions and kaons.
