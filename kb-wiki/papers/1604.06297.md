# Octupole correlations in low-lying states of 150Nd and 150Sm and their impact on neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We present a generator-coordinate calculation, based on a relativistic energy-density functional, of the low-lying spectra in the isotopes $^{150}$Nd and $^{150}$Sm and of the nuclear matrix element that governs the neutrinoless double-beta decay of the first isotope to the second. We carefully examine the impact of octupole correlations on both nuclear structure and the double-beta decay matrix element. Octupole correlations turn out to reduce quadrupole collectivity in both nuclei. Shape fluctuations, however, dilute the effects of octupole deformation on the double-beta decay matrix element, so that the overall octupole-induced quenching is only about 7\%.

## Key claim

Octupole correlations reduce quadrupole collectivity in both \(^{150}\)Nd and \(^{150}\)Sm, but shape fluctuations dilute the effect of octupole deformation on the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element, so that the overall octupole-induced quenching is only about 7%.

## Method

The authors performed a generator-coordinate-method (GCM) calculation based on a relativistic point-coupling energy-density functional (PC-PK1), mixing symmetry-projected mean-field reference states with constraints on both quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and octupole (\(\beta_3\)) mass moments. They constructed physical states for \(^{150}\)Nd and \(^{150}\)Sm by superposing projected reference states with restored angular momentum, parity, and particle number, then solved the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to obtain spectra and nuclear matrix elements for \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay in the closure approximation.

## Key numbers

* The ground-state to ground-state \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay matrix element \(M^{0\nu}(0^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\) for \(^{150}\)Nd is 5.2, which is 7% smaller than the result of 5.6 obtained without octupole deformation.
* The matrix element for decay to the excited \(0^+\) state in \(^{150}\)Sm is \(M^{0\nu}(0^+_1 \to 0^+_2) = 0.72\).
* At the configurations that minimize the projected energies (\(\beta_2 \approx 0.2\), \(\beta_3 \approx 0.1\) for both nuclei), the normalized matrix element \(\tilde{M}^{0\nu}\) is 4.76.
* At the configuration best fitting experimental \(B(E2)\) and \(B(E3)\) values (\(\beta_2^I = 0.285\), \(\beta_3^I = 0.113\), \(\beta_2^F = 0.193\), \(\beta_3^F = 0.145\)), \(\tilde{M}^{0\nu}\) is 1.38.
* The diagonal contribution \(\tilde{M}^{0\nu}\) drops from 6.4 to 2.2 as \(\beta_3\) increases to 0.3 with \(\beta_2\) fixed at 0.2.

## Context

This work extends previous GCM studies of \(^{150}\)Nd and \(^{150}\)Sm that neglected octupole degrees of freedom (Song 2014) by including octupole correlations as a generator coordinate. It also builds on prior studies of octupole correlations in \(N \approx 90\) nuclei using the \(sdf\) interacting boson model (Nomura 2014, Nomura 2015) and various mean-field approaches (Nazarewicz 1992, Robledo 2011). The results enable comparison with other theoretical approaches to the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay matrix element, including QRPA (Fang 2015, Mustonen 2013, Terasaki 2014), the interacting boson model (Barea 2009), and non-relativistic GCM with the Gogny D1S interaction (Rodriguez 2010, Vaquero 2013), showing that the relativistic GCM result remains about twice those of QRPA and IBM and about three times that of the Gogny-based GCM.
