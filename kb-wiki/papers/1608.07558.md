# Gradient expansion for anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We compute the gradient expansion for anisotropic hydrodynamics. The results are compared with the corresponding expansion of the underlying kinetic-theory model with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation. We find that a recent formulation of anisotropic hydrodynamics based on an anisotropic matching principle yields the first three terms of the gradient expansion in agreement with those obtained for the kinetic theory. This gives further support for this particular hydrodynamic model as a good approximation of the kinetic-theory approach. We further find that the gradient expansion of anisotropic hydrodynamics is an asymptotic series, and the singularities of the analytic continuation of its Borel transform indicate the presence of non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Key claim

The gradient expansion of anisotropic hydrodynamics based on the anisotropic matching principle yields the first three terms in exact agreement with the corresponding gradient expansion of kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, providing support for this hydrodynamic model as a good approximation of the kinetic-theory approach; the gradient expansion of anisotropic hydrodynamics is an asymptotic series whose Borel transform singularities indicate the presence of non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Method

The authors computed the gradient expansion of the energy-momentum tensor for two formulations of anisotropic hydrodynamics (moment-based and anisotropic matching principle) in the special case of boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous systems, using the Bjorken flow. They compared these expansions term-by-term with the gradient expansion obtained directly from the Boltzmann kinetic equation with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation (RTA), and also compared with the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) and BRSSS formulations. The large-order behavior of the gradient series was analyzed using Borel transform techniques.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time is given by tau_eq = c/T, where c is a numerical constant; for the RTA kinetic theory, tau_eq = 5*eta_bar/T, where eta_bar = eta/s is the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density.
- The Bjorken perfect-fluid solution gives energy density scaling as epsilon = epsilon_0 * (tau_0/tau)^(4/3) and temperature scaling as T = T_0 * (tau_0/tau)^(1/3).
- The first three terms of the gradient expansion from anisotropic hydrodynamics (anisotropic matching principle) agree exactly with the corresponding terms from kinetic theory in RTA.

## Context

This work extends the program of comparing hydrodynamic theories to underlying microscopic models via gradient expansions, following the approach of Heller et al. (2015) who studied the MIS theory. The authors compare two formulations of anisotropic hydrodynamics: the moment-based approach of Tinti (2013) and the anisotropic matching principle of Tinti (2015). The results show that anisotropic hydrodynamics based on the matching principle provides a better approximation to kinetic theory than the general MIS/BRSSS framework, which only guarantees matching up to second order. This work enables further development of anisotropic hydrodynamics as a tool for describing the early stages of quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions, where large momentum-space anisotropies are present.
