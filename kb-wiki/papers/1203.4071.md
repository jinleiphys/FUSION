# Examination of scaling of Hanbury-Brown--Twiss radii with charged particle multiplicity

> In the light of the recent LHC data on proton-proton and lead-lead collisions we examine the question of the multiplicity scaling of HBT radii in relativistic nuclei and particle interactions. Within the UrQMD transport approach we study a large variety of system sizes at different beam energies and extract the HBT radii. In the calculation, we find a good scaling of the radii as a function of charged particle multiplicity, if the change in the multiplicity is caused by a change of centrality at the same energy. However, the scaling is only approximate when the energy, $\sqrt{s}$, is changed and breaks down when comparing pp to AA reactions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that HBT radii scale well with charged particle multiplicity when the multiplicity change is due to varying centrality at a fixed beam energy, but this scaling is only approximate when the center-of-mass energy is changed and breaks down completely when comparing proton-proton to nucleus-nucleus reactions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 3.3) to simulate collisions of various systems (proton-proton, carbon-carbon, copper-copper, lead-lead) at different beam energies and centralities. They extracted the pion freeze-out distribution from the model and calculated three-dimensional HBT correlation functions, fitting them to obtain the radii \(R_{out}\), \(R_{side}\), and \(R_{long}\) in the pair longitudinal comoving system (LCMS). The fit ranges were adjusted for system size: \(|q_i| < 800\) MeV/c for proton-proton, \(|q_i| < 300\) MeV/c for carbon-carbon, and \(|q_i| < 150\) MeV/c for all other collisions.

## Key numbers

- For nucleus-nucleus reactions, a linear scaling of HBT radii with \((dN_{ch}/d\eta)^{1/3}\) is observed, with an offset of 2-3 fm when extrapolated to \(N_{ch} \rightarrow 0\), attributed to the finite size of the nuclei.
- For lead-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2760\) GeV, 20-50% centrality, the average charged particle multiplicity is \(\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle \approx 670\); for lead-lead at \(\sqrt{s}=200\), 130, 62.4 GeV at 0-5% centrality, values are 665, 595, and 509 respectively.
- For carbon-carbon at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, 0-5% centrality, \(\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle \approx 23\); for lead-lead at \(\sqrt{s}=200\), 130, 62.4 GeV at 50-80% centrality, values are 32, 28, and 23 respectively.
- The freeze-out volume shows an offset for AA reactions of about 25 fm\(^3\) for \(R_{side}^2 R_{long}\) and 50 fm\(^3\) for \(R_{out} R_{side} R_{long}\).
- The minimal decoupling time \(\tau_f^{min}\) is estimated to be 4-8 fm/c in AA reactions and less than 2 fm/c in proton-proton collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier HBT studies from the SPS and RHIC experiments (Adams 2004, Lisa 2000, Alt 2007, Afanasiev 2002, Adamova 2002, Abelev 2009, Back 2004) and addresses the surprising scaling violation observed when comparing LHC proton-proton data to nucleus-nucleus data at similar charged particle densities. The results confirm the expectation from Sinyukov (2011) that the interferometry volume depends not only on multiplicity but also on the initial system size. The findings enable further investigation into how different particle production mechanisms (bulk emission in AA versus string/jet dominated emission in pp) affect femtoscopic observables, and suggest that the UrQMD model reproduces the \(k_\perp\) dependence of HBT radii but with a weaker flow dependence than seen in data.
