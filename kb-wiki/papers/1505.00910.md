# Semi-phenomenological description of the chiral bands in $^{188,190}Os$

> A set of interacting particles are coupled to a phenomenological core described using the generalized coherent state model. Among the particle-core states a finite set which have the property that the angular momenta carried by the proton and neutron quadrupole bosons and the particles, separately, are mutually orthogonal are identified. The magnetic properties of such states are studied. All terms of the model Hamiltonian exhibit chiral symmetry except the spin-spin interaction. There are four bands of the type with two-quasiparticle-core dipole states, exhibiting properties which are specific for magnetic twin bands. An application is presented, for the isotopes $^{188, 190}$Os.

# Semi-phenomenological description of the chiral bands in $^{188,190}Os$

## Key claim

The authors claim that four distinct chiral bands (rather than the usual two) exist in the even-even isotopes $^{188,190}$Os, arising from a particle-core model where the spin-spin interaction breaks the chiral symmetry of the otherwise symmetric Hamiltonian; however, two of these bands are degenerate in the restricted model space and only become distinct when the particle-core space is enlarged to include mixed-parity states.

## Method

The authors couple two quasiparticles (protons in the $h_{11/2}$ orbital) to a phenomenological core described by the Generalized Coherent State Model (GCSM), which uses separate proton and neutron quadrupole bosons. The particle-core Hamiltonian includes a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and a spin-spin interaction, with the core described by a six-band collective Hamiltonian fitted to experimental ground, beta, and gamma band energies via a least-square procedure. The gyromagnetic factors for protons and neutrons are calculated self-consistently from the core Hamiltonian parameters rather than taken as free parameters. The chiral bands are generated by applying chiral transformations (changing the sign of one of the three angular momenta: ${\bf J}_p$, ${\bf J}_n$, or ${\bf J}_F$) to the spin-spin interaction term.

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to $^{192}$Pt by Raduta and Raduta (2014) to the triaxial isotopes $^{188,190}$Os, with the improvement that gyromagnetic factors are now calculated self-consistently rather than using the approximation $g_n = g_p/5$. The approach differs from most chiral band descriptions in the literature, which focus on odd-odd nuclei (with some work on odd-even nuclei by Mukhopadhyay and even-even isotopes by Luo), by treating even-even systems and predicting four chiral bands instead of the usual two. The model generalizes both the two-rotor model of Lo Iudice and Palumbo and the two-drop model of Greiner, and the results suggest that the mechanism for chiral symmetry breaking favoring large transverse magnetic dipole moments is not unique. The predicted magnetic bands await experimental confirmation, as no data on magnetic states in these isotopes are currently available beyond tentative assignments of the 1304.82 keV state in $^{188}$Os and 1115.5 keV state in $^{190}$Os as possible dipole band heads.
