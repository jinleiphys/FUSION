# Emergence of soft quark excitations by the coupling with a soft mode of the QCD critical point

> We study the quark spectrum at nonzero temperature and density near the critical point (CP) of the chiral phase transition incorporating effects of the scalar- and pseudoscalar-density fluctuations in a chiral effective model with a nonzero current quark mass. It is known that the soft mode associated with the second-order transition at the CP lies in the spacelike region of the scalar-density fluctuation. We find that the soft mode influences the quark spectrum significantly near the CP, resulting in the shift of the quasiquark peak. Effects of the composite stable pions on the quark spectrum near the CP are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that the soft mode associated with the QCD critical point, a scalar-density fluctuation in the spacelike region, significantly modifies the quark spectrum near the CP, causing a shift of the quasiquark peak to an energy substantially lower than the constituent quark mass, even though the chiral symmetry is explicitly broken by a nonzero current quark mass.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model without vector coupling, with parameters fitted to vacuum pion properties, and compute the quark spectral function at zero momentum in the random-phase approximation. The retarded quark self energy is obtained from the coupling of quarks to scalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar (pion) fluctuations, with the soft mode identified as a sharp peak in the scalar spectral function in the spacelike region near the CP. The quark propagator is then decomposed into quark and antiquark components, and the spectral functions are evaluated numerically for various temperatures and chemical potentials along the ridge of the chiral susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- The critical point (CP) is located at temperature \(T_c \simeq 47\) MeV and chemical potential \(\mu_c \simeq 329\) MeV.
- At the CP, the constituent quark mass in the mean-field approximation is \(m \simeq 184\) MeV.
- Near the CP (\(T = 48\) MeV, \(\mu \simeq 328\) MeV), the quasiquark peak in \(\rho_+(p_0)\) appears at \(p_0 + \mu \simeq 105\) MeV, shifted downward from the mean-field value of \(m = 184\) MeV.
- The peak in Im\(\Sigma^+(p_0)\) responsible for this shift occurs at \(p_0 + \mu \simeq 190\) MeV.
- The pionic modes remain stable at the CP, with the pion zero-binding temperature \(T_{\rm ZB}\) lying above the CP.
- The dispersion relation \(\omega_\pi(p)\) exceeds the threshold \(E_{\rm thr}(p) = \sqrt{p^2 + 4m^2}\) at \(p \simeq 575\) MeV near the CP.
- The spectral weight of the sharp van Hove singularity peaks is \(Z \lesssim 10^{-2}\).
- Away from the CP, the soft-mode effect is already insignificant at \(T \simeq 1.05 T_c\) for fixed \(\mu = \mu_c\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kitazawa, Kunihiro, Nemoto, 2014) of quark spectral modifications near the pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential, where a three-peak structure and van Hove singularities from pionic modes were found. Here, the focus shifts to the nonzero chemical potential region near the CP, where the soft mode, identified by Fujii (2003) and Son (2004) as a superposition of number-density fluctuation and sigma mode belonging to the "model H" universality class, dominates the quark self energy. The results show that the soft mode produces a significant quasiquark peak shift near the CP, while the van Hove singularities from pionic modes become weak and likely negligible compared to neglected higher-order effects. This work enables future studies incorporating hydrodynamic modes (entropy and number-density fluctuations) and their coupling to quarks for a more complete description near the CP.
