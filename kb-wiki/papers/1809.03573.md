# $J/ψ$ polarization in the CGC+NRQCD approach

> We compute the $J/ψ$ polarization observables $λ_θ$, $λ_ϕ$, $λ_{θϕ}$ in a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) + nonrelativistic QCQ (NRQCD) formalism that includes contributions from both color singlet and color octet intermediate states. Our results are compared to low $p_T$ data on $J/ψ$ polarization from the LHCb and ALICE experiments on proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and 8 TeV. Our CGC+NRQCD computation provides a better description of data for $p_T \leq 15$ GeV relative to extant next-to-leading (NLO) calculations within the collinear factorization framework. These results suggest that higher order computations in the CGC+NRQCD framework have the potential to greatly improve the accuracy of extracted values of the NRQCD universal long distance matrix elements.

## Key claim

The CGC+NRQCD computation provides a better description of low-\(p_T\) \(J/\psi\) polarization data from LHCb and ALICE than extant NLO collinear factorization calculations, but only for \(p_T \leq 15\) GeV; the agreement is achieved through a cancellation between the polarizations of the \(^3S_1^{[8]}\) and \(^3P_J^{[8]}\) channels already at leading order in \(\alpha_s\), and the results suggest that higher-order computations in this framework could improve the accuracy of extracted NRQCD long-distance matrix elements.

## Method

The authors computed the spin density matrix elements for \(J/\psi\) production in proton-proton collisions using the CGC effective field theory combined with NRQCD factorization, including both color-singlet (\(^3S_1^{[1]}\)) and color-octet (\(^1S_0^{[8]}\), \(^3S_1^{[8]}\), \(^3P_J^{[8]}\)) intermediate states. The short-distance coefficients were evaluated in the dilute-dense approximation of the CGC, using dipole forward scattering amplitudes obtained from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with McLerran-Venugopalan initial conditions, and the long-distance matrix elements were taken from a previous NLO collinear-factorization fit to Tevatron data. The angular distribution coefficients \(\lambda_\theta\), \(\lambda_\phi\), and \(\lambda_{\theta\phi}\) were then extracted from the helicity-dependent cross-sections in both the Collins-Soper and recoil frames.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV, approximately half the \(J/\psi\) mass.
* Color-singlet LDME: \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[1]}) \rangle = 1.16/(2N_c)\) GeV\(^3\), estimated from a potential model.
* Color-octet LDMEs from Chao et al. (2012): \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]}) \rangle = 0.089 \pm 0.0098\) GeV\(^3\), \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]}) \rangle = 0.0030 \pm 0.0012\) GeV\(^3\), and \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3P_J^{[8]}) \rangle / m_c^2 = 0.0056 \pm 0.0021\) GeV\(^3\).
* Initial saturation scale for the proton: \(Q_{s0,\text{proton}}^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\), with initial \(x_0 = 0.01\).
* The \(^1S_0^{[8]}\) state dominates the unpolarized cross-section but gives vanishing polarization coefficients because the produced quark-antiquark pair has no spin or orbital momentum.
* For \(\lambda_\theta\) at \(p_T \lesssim 6\) GeV, the CGC+NRQCD results prefer a small transverse polarization (\(\lambda_\theta > 0\)), while data show weak longitudinal polarization (\(\lambda_\theta < 0\)) with considerable variation between experiments; at \(p_T \gtrsim 6\) GeV, theory agrees with data within two standard deviations.
* For \(\lambda_\phi\) and \(\lambda_{\theta\phi}\), the CGC+NRQCD results are consistent with zero and agree well with data, with the data lying within the theory band for both frames.

## Context

This work extends the CGC+NRQCD framework previously applied to \(J/\psi\) yields by Kang et al. (2013) and Ma et al. (2014) to address the long-standing "\(J/\psi\) polarization puzzle" at low transverse momentum. It contrasts with NLO collinear-factorization pQCD+NRQCD calculations by Butenschoen and Kniehl (2012), Gong et al. (2012), and Chao et al. (2012), which either predict polarization coefficients with absolute values significantly larger than measured or provide results only for limited kinematic regions. The CGC+NRQCD approach naturally incorporates small-\(x\) resummation and higher-twist contributions relevant at low \(p_T\) and forward rapidities (down to \(x \sim 10^{-4}\)), enabling a unified description of both yields and polarization; future work should include higher-order \(\alpha_s\) corrections and feeddown from higher charmonium states to improve the accuracy of extracted LDMEs.
