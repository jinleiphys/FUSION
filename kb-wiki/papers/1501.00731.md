# Running condensate in moving superfluid

> A possibility of the condensation of excitations with non-zero momentum in moving superfluid media is considered in terms of the Ginzburg-Landau model. The results might be applicable to the superfluid $^4$He, ultracold atomic Bose gases, various superconducting and neutral systems with pairing, like ultracold atomic Fermi gases and the neutron component in compact stars. The order parameters, the energy gain, and critical velocities are found.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when a superfluid flows faster than the Landau critical velocity, excitations with non-zero momentum can condense into a new condensate described by the same macroscopic wavefunction as the mother condensate, with the transition occurring as second-order at the Landau critical velocity and first-order at a higher second critical velocity where superfluidity is destroyed.

## Method

The authors use a Ginzburg-Landau free-energy functional to describe the superfluid subsystem, adding a plane-wave condensate of excitations with momentum k₀ to the mother condensate order parameter. They minimize the free-energy density with respect to both condensate amplitudes independently, imposing momentum conservation and particle number conservation, for both zero-temperature (fully superfluid) and finite-temperature (superfluid-normal composite) cases. The analysis is applied to neutral bosonic superfluids (⁴He, cold Bose gases) and fermionic systems with Cooper pairing (neutron star matter, cold Fermi gases, metallic superconductors).

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He at saturated vapor pressure: roton energy Δᵣ = 8.71 K at T = 0.1 K and Δᵣ = 7.63 K at T = 2.10 K, with kᵣ ≈ 1.9 × 10⁸ ħ/cm
- For ⁴He: T_c = 2.17 K, a₀/T_c^(4/3) = 1.11 × 10⁻¹⁶ erg/K^(4/3), b₀/T_c^(2/3) = 3.54 × 10⁻³⁹ erg·cm³/K^(2/3)
- For ⁴He: Landau critical velocity v_c^L(T→0) ≈ 60 m/s; at t = 0.5 (T ≈ 1.09 K) v_c^L = 59 m/s, at t = 0.1 (T ≈ 1.95 K) v_c^L = 55 m/s
- For ⁴He: k₀²/(b₀ρ) ≈ 47, a₀/k₀ ≈ 16 m/s
- For ⁴He: second critical velocity v_c₂ ≈ v_c^L(t) + (363 t^(2/3) - 23.5 t^(4/3)) m/s
- For ⁴He: energy release at v = v_c₂ is δF(v_c₂) ≈ 5.9 t^(4/3) × (T_c ΔC_p) where ΔC_p = 0.76 × 10⁷ erg/(cm³ K)
- For BCS fermionic superfluids: renormalized critical temperature T̃_c/T_c ≈ 1 - (v - v_c^L)/v_F
- For BCS fermionic superfluids: maximal second critical velocity v_c₂^max ≈ v_c^L + v_F
- For neutron stars: critical angular velocity for condensate formation Ω_c ∼ Δ/(p_F R) ∼ 10² Hz for Δ ∼ MeV, p_F ∼ 300 MeV/c at nuclear density
- For neutron stars: Ω_c₂ ≲ 10⁴ Hz; most rapidly rotating pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad has angular velocity 4500 Hz

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Pitaevskii (1984) of roton condensation in moving He-II and the general treatment of excitation condensation in moving media by Voskresensky (1993), but crucially includes the nonlinear interaction between the mother condensate and the condensate of excitations that those works neglected. It also extends the analysis to finite temperatures where a normal subsystem is present, which had not been studied previously. The results enable predictions for the coexistence of superfluidity with a condensate of excitations in rapidly rotating pulsars, and provide testable predictions for superfluid ⁴He experiments and ultracold atomic gas systems.
