# Minimal Entanglement and Emergent Symmetries in Low-energy QCD

> We study low-energy scattering of spin-1/2 baryons from the perspective of quantum information science, focusing on the correlation between entanglement minimization and the appearance of accidental symmetries. The baryon transforms as an octet under the SU(3) flavor symmetry and its interactions below the pion threshold are described by contact operators in an effective field theory (EFT) of QCD. Despite there being 64 channels in the 2-to-2 scattering, only six independent operators in the EFT are predicted by SU(3). We show that successive entanglement minimization in SU(3)-symmetric channels are correlated with increasingly large emergent symmetries in the EFT. In particular, we identify scattering channels whose entanglement suppression are indicative of emergent SU(6), SO(8), SU(8), and SU(16) symmetries. We also observe the appearance of non-relativistic conformal invariance in channels with unnaturally large scattering lengths. Improved precision from lattice simulations could help determine the degree of entanglement suppression, and consequently the amount of accidental symmetry, in low-energy QCD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that minimizing spin entanglement in low-energy scattering of SU(3) octet baryons correlates with the emergence of increasingly large accidental symmetries (SU(6), SO(8), SU(8), SU(16)) in the effective field theory, but this correlation is conditional on which specific scattering channels are chosen for entanglement suppression and whether the scattering length is natural or unnatural.

## Method

The authors analyze 2-to-2 scattering of spin-1/2 baryons transforming as an octet under SU(3) flavor symmetry, using contact operators in a pionless effective field theory valid below the pion production threshold. They compute the S-matrix in the s-wave for all 64 scattering channels classified by total electric charge and strangeness, then determine the entanglement power of the S-matrix as a quantum gate acting on spin states. By imposing conditions for minimal entanglement (S-matrix proportional to Identity or SWAP gates), they derive constraints on the six independent SU(3)-invariant Wilson coefficients and identify which emergent symmetry groups appear at each entanglement minimum.

## Key numbers

- There are 64 channels in 2-to-2 scattering of octet baryons, but only 6 independent operators in the SU(3)-invariant EFT Lagrangian
- The product of two SU(3) octets decomposes into 6 irreducible representations: 27, 10, 10̅, 8_S, 8_A, and 1
- For nucleon-nucleon scattering, the spin-triplet scattering length is a_1 ≈ 5.4 fm and the spin-singlet scattering length is a_0 ≈ -23.7 fm, both much larger than the pion Compton wavelength of 1/m_π ≈ 1.4 fm
- The maximal entanglement for a two-qubit system is 1/2, achieved by Bell states such as (|↑↑⟩ ± |↓↓⟩)/√2 and (|↑↓⟩ ± |↓↑⟩)/√2

## Context

This work extends the earlier analyses of Beane (2018) and Low (2021) on neutron-proton scattering, which first observed the correlation between entanglement suppression and emergent SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry and Schrödinger symmetry. The paper generalizes these findings to the full SU(3) octet of baryons, identifying new emergent symmetries (SU(6), SO(8), SU(8), SU(16)) that could be tested by improved lattice QCD simulations of baryon-baryon interactions, such as those by Wagman (2017) which suggested possible SU(16) symmetry.
