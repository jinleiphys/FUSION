# Investigation of N-N* Electromagnetic Form Factors within a Front-Form CQM

> The helicity amplitudes for the transitions $N-S_{11}$ and $N-S_{31}$ are presented. The amplitudes have been obtained within our front-form CQM model, based on hadron eigenstates of a relativistic mass operator and CQ current with Dirac and Pauli form factors.

## Key claim

The paper presents parameter-free predictions for the helicity amplitudes of the transitions from the nucleon to the negative-parity resonances S11(1535), S11(1650), and S31(1620) within a front-form constituent quark model, with the caveat that the overall agreement with data is only encouraging and a more accurate data set is needed to reliably discriminate between models.

## Method

The authors used a front-form constituent quark model with hadron eigenfunctions of a relativistic mass operator that includes an effective q-q interaction and reproduces hadron spectra for a large set of quantum numbers. The one-body electromagnetic current operator contains phenomenological Dirac and Pauli form factors for constituent quarks, previously determined by fitting experimental data for pion and nucleon elastic form factors. The helicity amplitudes A1/2 and S1/2 for the negative-parity transitions were computed from the invariant form factors F1 and F2, which were obtained by approximating the plus component of the transition current as a sum of one-body constituent quark currents.

## Key numbers

- For the transition p → S11(1535), the transverse helicity A1/2 and longitudinal helicity S1/2 are shown as functions of Q², compared against PDG '96 data and data analysis from Volker
- For the transition p → S11(1650), the transverse helicity A1/2 and longitudinal helicity S1/2 are shown as functions of Q², compared against PDG '96 data and data analysis from Volker
- For the transition p → S31(1620), the transverse helicity A1/2 and longitudinal helicity S1/2 are shown as functions of Q²
- For S31(1620), the results for proton and neutron coincide, since only the isovector part of the constituent quark current is effective given the isospin of the resonance

## Context

This work extends the front-form constituent quark model previously applied to transitions to N*(1440) and Δ(1232) by Pace, Salmè, and Simula (1995), now applied to negative-parity resonances S11(1535), S11(1650), and S31(1620). The results are compared with non-relativistic constituent quark model calculations by Giannini and with experimental data from the PDG '96 compilation and data analysis by Volker. The paper enables future discrimination between relativistic and non-relativistic models of nucleon structure, though the authors note that more accurate experimental data are needed for reliable model discrimination.
