# Precision determination of pion-nucleon coupling constants using effective field theory

> The pion-nucleon coupling constants determine the strength of the long-range nuclear forces and play a fundamental part in our understanding of nuclear physics. While the charged- and neutral-pion couplings to protons and neutrons are expected to be very similar, owing to the approximate isospin symmetry of the strong interaction, the different masses of the up- and down-quarks and electromagnetic effects may result in their slightly different values. Despite previous attempts to extract these coupling constants from different systems, our knowledge of their values is still deficient. In this Letter we present a precision determination of these fundamental observables with fully controlled uncertainties from neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data using chiral effective field theory. To achieve this goal, we use a novel methodology based on the Bayesian approach and perform, for the first time, a full-fledged partial-wave analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering up to the pion production threshold in the framework of chiral effective field theory, including a complete treatment of isospin-breaking effects and our own determination of mutually consistent data. The resulting values of the pion-nucleon coupling constants are accurate at the percent level and show no significant charge dependence. These results mark an important step towards developing a precision theory of nuclear forces and structure.

## Key claim

The authors determine the three pion-nucleon coupling constants from neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data using chiral effective field theory, finding values accurate at the percent level and showing no significant charge dependence, contrary to some previous analyses that had claimed evidence for charge-symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors performed a full partial-wave analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering data up to the pion production threshold (laboratory energies up to 300 MeV) within the chiral effective field theory framework, including all charge-independence-breaking and charge-symmetry-breaking isospin-violating interactions up through fifth order (N⁴LO). They employed a Bayesian approach to determine the joint conditional probability density of the coupling constants given the data, integrating over 31 nuisance parameters (short-range low-energy constants and the cutoff parameter) using the Laplace approximation. To account for systematic uncertainties, they performed separate analyses for five different maximum laboratory energies (220, 240, 260, 280, and 300 MeV) and three different models of isospin-breaking interactions, then averaged the results.

## Key numbers

- Final values for the pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling constants: fp² = 0.0770(5)ᵃ(0.8)ᵇ, f₀² = 0.0779(9)ᵃ(1.3)ᵇ, fc² = 0.0769(5)ᵃ(0.9)ᵇ, where the first error (a) includes statistical and systematic uncertainties from truncation and energy range choice, and the second error (b) reflects uncertainty in higher-order pion-nucleon low-energy constants
- For the central model with energy range 0-280 MeV and cutoff Λ = 463.5 MeV, the fit yielded χ² = 4950.72 for N_dat = 4926 data points, giving χ²/N_dat = 1.005
- The charge-dependence difference f₀² − fp² = 0.0010(10)ᵃ(2)ᵇ, consistent with no charge dependence, compared to the Granada group's result of 0.0029(10)
- The analysis used 33 adjustable parameters (3 pion-nucleon coupling constants plus 25 isospin-invariant and 5 isospin-breaking short-range contact terms), versus 55 parameters in the Granada partial wave analysis

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering previously developed by the same group (Reinert, Krebs, Epelbaum 2017) by including a complete treatment of isospin-breaking effects up through N⁴LO. It contradicts the claim by the Granada group (Navarro Pérez et al. 2016) of significant charge dependence in the pion-nucleon coupling constants, suggesting that the Granada analysis may have had unquantified systematic uncertainties from its phenomenological modeling. The results provide reference values for future lattice QCD and QED calculations (Borsanyi et al. 2014) and enable precision studies of nuclear structure, as demonstrated by the calculation of the deuteron quadrupole moment (Filin et al. 2020) where these isospin-breaking corrections were found to be important.
