# Non-equilibrium effects on stability of hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions

> The stability of hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions as determined by calculating fundamental radial oscillation modes is known to differ from the predictions of the widely-used Bardeen--Thorne--Meltzer criterion. We consider the effects of out-of-chemical-equilibrium physics on the radial modes and hence stability of these objects. For a barotropic equation of state, this is done by allowing the adiabatic sound speed to differ from the equilibrium sound speed. We show that doing so extends the stable branches of stellar models, allowing stars with rapid phase transitions to support stable higher-order stellar multiplets similarly to stars with multiple slow phase transitions. We also derive a new junction condition to impose on the oscillation modes at the phase transition. Termed the reactive condition, it is physically motivated, consistent with the generalized junction conditions between two phases, and has the common rapid and slow conditions as limiting cases. Unlike the two common cases, it can only be applied to nonbarotropic stars. We apply this junction condition to hybrid stellar models generated using a two-phase equation of state consisting of nuclear matter with unpaired quark matter at high densities joined by a first-order phase transition and show that like in the slow limiting case, stars that are classically unstable are stabilized by a finite chemical reaction speed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including out-of-chemical-equilibrium physics in hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions extends the stable branches of stellar models, allowing stars with rapid phase transitions to support stable higher-order stellar multiplets similarly to stars with multiple slow phase transitions, provided the adiabatic sound speed is allowed to differ from the equilibrium sound speed or a new "reactive" junction condition is applied.

## Method

The authors computed fundamental radial oscillation modes of hybrid stars in general relativity by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the coupled differential equations for the Lagrangian displacement and pressure perturbation. For barotropic equations of state, they modeled non-equilibrium effects by allowing the adiabatic sound speed to differ from the equilibrium sound speed, constrained by causality and the Ledoux criterion. For nonbarotropic stars, they derived a new "reactive" junction condition from the generalized junction conditions of Karlovini (2004) by introducing a parameter Z characterizing the rate of restoration of chemical equilibrium at the phase transition, which interpolates between the rapid and slow limiting cases.

## Key numbers

- The three-phase barotropic equation of state used has phase transition energy densities of 420.7 MeV/fm³ (nuclear-2SC) and 774.1 MeV/fm³ (2SC-CFL), with density discontinuities of 263.6 MeV/fm³ and 168.3 MeV/fm³ respectively
- The two-phase nonbarotropic equation of state has a phase transition pressure of 167.6 MeV/fm³, with energy density at the low-density end of 627.2 MeV/fm³ and a density discontinuity of 480.2 MeV/fm³
- The strange quark mass is set to 100 MeV, the bag constant to (190 MeV)⁴, and the vector meson coupling constant a_V to 1.541 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻²
- The three-phase EoS supports stellar masses up to 1.88 solar masses, consistent with neutron star maximum mass constraints within 3-sigma confidence intervals
- The physically meaningful range of the equilibration parameter Z is from 0 (slow limit) to -(dY/d ln n_b)_+ (rapid limit)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid star stability by Pereira (2018), Curin (2021), and Lugones (2023) that demonstrated the existence of "slow stable" stars, and by Rau and Salaben (2023, RS23) who studied intermediate-speed phase transitions. The paper generalizes out-of-equilibrium studies of white dwarfs by Chanmugam (1977) and neutron stars by Gourgoulhon (1995) to systems with strong first-order phase transitions. The reactive junction condition is consistent with the generalized junction conditions introduced by Karlovini (2004) and provides a physically motivated interpolation between the rapid and slow limiting cases, unlike the ad-hoc interpolation parameter used in RS23. This work enables more realistic stability calculations for hybrid stars that could host twin and higher-order stellar multiplets, which are relevant for interpreting neutron star observations from gravitational wave and electromagnetic astronomy.
