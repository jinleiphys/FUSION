# Probing the boundary of phase transition of nuclear matter using proton flows in heavy-ion collisions at 2-8 GeV/nucleon

> Based on the relativistic transport model ART with the hadronic equation of state extended to have a phase transition via the use of the MIT bag model, properties of phase transition of dense nuclear matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are investigated. Proton sideward and directed flows are calculated with different equation of states in Au + Au collisions at beam energies of 2, 4, 6 and 8 GeV/nucleon. Compared with AGS experimental data in existence, the boundary of first-order phase transition is roughly confined, i.e., in the range of 2.5-4 times saturation density with temperature about 64-94 MeV. Such constraints are useful for ongoing RHIC Beam Energy Scan-II program to study the QCD matter phase diagram.

## Key claim
The boundary of the first-order phase transition of dense nuclear matter is roughly confined to a density range of 2.5-4 times saturation density with a temperature of about 64-94 MeV, based on comparing proton flow calculations from the ART transport model with existing AGS experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport model ART, which includes baryons (N, Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Λ, Σ) and mesons (π, ρ, ω, η, K), and extended the hadronic equation of state to include a phase transition via the MIT bag model with bag constants B = 150 MeV/fm³ (first-order) and B = 90 MeV/fm³ (crossover). They calculated proton sideward flow (average transverse momentum ⟨p_x⟩) and directed flow (v₁) in semi-central Au + Au collisions at beam energies of 2, 4, 6, and 8 GeV/nucleon, comparing results from hadronic, first-order, and crossover equations of state against E895 experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The first-order phase transition boundary is confined to densities of 2.5-4 times saturation density (ρ₀).
- The temperature range of the formed compression matter is about 64-94 MeV.
- Maximum baryon densities reached in the collisions are approximately 3.5-6 times saturation density.
- The bag constant B = 150 MeV/fm³ is used for the first-order phase transition, and B = 90 MeV/fm³ for the crossover transition.
- The hadronic equation of state uses parameters α = -232 MeV, β = 179 MeV, and γ = 1.3, giving binding energy E₀ = -16 MeV, pressure P₀ = 0 MeV/fm³, and incompressibility K₀ = 230 MeV at saturation density.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of phase transitions in dense nuclear matter formed in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, where densities beyond 3ρ₀ and temperatures above 50 MeV are reached (Li 2001, Li 2002). The results are compared with experimental data from the E895 collaboration (Li 2004) and are relevant for the ongoing RHIC Beam Energy Scan-II program (BES-II). The study contradicts the possibility of a crossover transition at these energies and high baryochemical potential, and it builds on earlier work using the ART model and MIT bag model (Li 2002, Bu 2001, Ni 2001).
