# Signals of strong parity violation in deep inelastic scattering

> We include strong parity-violating contributions to inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of longitudinally polarized leptons off an unpolarized target. At variance with standard results, we obtain nonvanishing parity-violating structure functions in the case of pure photon exchange. The addition of these strong parity-violating contributions improves the description of existing experimental data on DIS parity-violating asymmetries. We find the size of these contributions small but exhibiting a deviation from zero of about 1.5 $σ$. The associated $p$-value is 0.063, indicating that the probability of making an error by rejecting the hypothesis of no parity-violating contributions is 6.3\%, which is small but not negligible. Further improvement on the limit of the strong parity-violation can be expected from the future SoLID program at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The authors find that including strong parity-violating contributions to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) improves the description of existing experimental data on parity-violating asymmetries, but the deviation from zero is only about 1.5 sigma with a p-value of 0.063, indicating the result is not statistically significant and the hypothesis of no parity violation cannot be firmly rejected.

## Method

The authors introduced a new parity-violating parton distribution function (PDF) \(g_{1}^{\rm PV}\) into the quark correlator for unpolarized nucleons, which generates non-zero contributions to the \(F_3\) structure function in pure photon exchange. They assumed this new PDF is proportional to the standard helicity distribution \(g_1\) via a small parameter \(a\), and performed a fit at NLO accuracy to 287 experimental data points from HERA, SLAC E122, and Jefferson Lab PVDIS experiments using the bootstrap method with 100 Monte Carlo replicas.

## Key numbers

- The fitted parameter \(a = (-1.01 \pm 0.66) \times 10^{-4}\), representing the ratio of the strong parity-violating PDF to the standard helicity distribution
- The p-value for rejecting the null hypothesis of no parity-violating contributions is 0.063 (6.3%)
- The total dataset comprises 287 experimental points: 136 for positron asymmetry and 151 for electron asymmetry from HERA at \(\sqrt{s}=319\) GeV, 2 points from JLab PVDIS at \(\sqrt{s}=4.77\) GeV, and 11 points from SLAC E122 at \(\sqrt{s}=5.5-6.5\) GeV
- The global \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dat}\) improved from 1.042 in the Standard Model to 1.037 in the analysis including strong parity violation
- Including SoLID pseudodata on deuteron target reduces the uncertainty on \(a\) to \(\pm 0.21 \times 10^{-4}\), and on proton target to \(\pm 0.15 \times 10^{-4}\), representing at least a factor 3 reduction

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of parity violation in DIS, where pure photon exchange gives no contribution to the \(F_3\) structure function, by introducing strong parity-violating PDFs that are P-odd and CP-even. The results are connected to the anapole moment of the proton and nuclei studied in elastic scattering experiments such as SAMPLE (2000) and Qweak (2018). The analysis enables future searches with the SoLID program at Jefferson Lab, which could potentially achieve a 5-sigma deviation from the Standard Model if the central value of the parameter is confirmed, while the Electron Ion Collider is found to have limited sensitivity due to large projected uncertainties at higher energies.
