# Neutron star structure and nuclear matter properties from a general Walecka-type model with Bayesian analysis

> We establish a Bayesian analysis framework with a general Walecka-type relativistic mean-field model to study dense nuclear matter under constraints from nuclear matter properties and neutron star observations. With experimental and observational data well described, we find that pure hadronic descriptions can generate a peak structure in sound velocity by $ω$, $ρ$, $σ$, and $a_0$ meson mixing, which is crucial for describing both medium and massive neutron stars. As the peak structure is frequently interpreted as a signature of phase transitions, our findings provide a new perspective on the microscopic origin of the sound velocity peak just with pure hadronic matter.

# Neutron star structure and nuclear matter properties from a general Walecka-type model with Bayesian analysis

## Key claim
Pure hadronic matter described by a general Walecka-type relativistic mean-field model can generate a peak structure in the sound velocity through mixing of σ, ω, ρ, and a₀ mesons, without requiring a phase transition. This peak structure is crucial for simultaneously describing both medium-mass (around 1.4 M⊙) and massive (around 2 M⊙) neutron stars.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of a 21-dimensional parameter space for a general Walecka-type relativistic mean-field model that includes σ, ω, ρ, and a₀ mesons as degrees of freedom below 1 GeV. They incorporated constraints from nuclear matter properties around saturation density (binding energy, pressure, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and slope of symmetry energy) and from neutron star mass-radius relations, using uniform prior distributions for all parameters within physically reasonable ranges. The equation of state was obtained from the RMF Hamiltonian and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, with the neutron star crust interpolated to a BPS equation of state below 0.5 n₀.

## Key numbers
- Meson masses fixed from experiments: m_ω = 782 MeV, m_ρ = 763 MeV, m_a₀ = 980 MeV, m_N = 939 MeV; m_σ varied from 400 to 800 MeV
- One-boson-exchange couplings g_σNN, g_ωNN, g_ρNN, g_a₀NN limited within -20 to 20
- Three-meson coupling magnitudes taken less than 10000 MeV; four-meson coupling magnitudes less than 1000
- The mixing term b₈ σ ω ρ a₀ is identified as crucial for producing the sound velocity peak and reducing neutron star radii around 1.4 M⊙ while preserving the 2 M⊙ constraint
- At high densities, v_s² approaches the conformal limit of 1/3

## Context
This work extends previous Walecka-type models such as those by Sugahara (1993), Reinhard (1986), Lalazissis (1996), Li (2022), and Todd-Rutel (2005) by incorporating a broader set of mesonic degrees of freedom and performing a systematic Bayesian analysis. The finding that pure hadronic matter can produce a sound velocity peak challenges the common interpretation that such peaks signal phase transitions to quark matter. The framework enables future connections to chiral effective field theories as developed by Ma (2025) and Zhang (2024) to explore the microscopic origin of the sound speed peak and its implications for neutron star structures.
