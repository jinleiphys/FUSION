# Chiral Two-Body Currents and Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay in the QRPA

> We test the effects of an approximate treatment of two-body contributions to the axial-vector current on the QRPA matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in a range of isotopes. The form and strength of the two-body terms come from chiral effective-field theory. The two-body currents typically reduce the matrix elements by about 20%, not as much as in shell-model calculations. One reason for the difference is that standard practice in the QRPA is to adjust the strength of the isoscalar pairing interaction to reproduce two-neutrino double-beta decay lifetimes. Another may be the larger QRPA single-particle space. Whatever the reasons, the effects on neutrinoless decay are significantly less than those on two-neutrino decay, both in the shell model and the QRPA.

## Key claim

The two-body axial-vector currents from chiral effective-field theory reduce QRPA matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay by about 20%, which is less than the reduction seen in shell-model calculations; the smaller effect in QRPA arises partly because the isoscalar pairing interaction strength is adjusted to reproduce two-neutrino double-beta decay lifetimes, and partly because the QRPA uses a larger single-particle space.

## Method

The authors implemented approximate effective one-body current operators derived from chiral effective-field theory two-body currents, using two separate approximation schemes from Menendez et al. (2011) and Klos et al. (2013). They computed neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements within the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) for ten isotopes, using both the Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn interactions as starting points, and tested four combinations of chiral EFT parameters and nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- Two-body currents reduce the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element by 11% for Ca-48 and 22% for Ge-76 (Argonne V18 interaction, averaged over parameter choices)
- The averaged matrix element for Xe-136 is 2.02(0.18) with two-body currents, compared to 2.570 with one-body currents only (Argonne V18)
- For Se-82, the averaged matrix element with two-body currents is 4.11(0.39), representing 23% suppression from the one-body value of 5.313 (Argonne V18)
- The effective coupling ratio g_A^eff(0)/g_A ranges from 0.66 to 0.85 across the parameter choices
- The two-neutrino double-beta decay quenching from the same currents is closer to 40-50%, significantly larger than the approximately 20% quenching for neutrinoless decay

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective-field theory two-body currents from shell-model calculations by Menendez et al. (2011) to the QRPA framework, and builds on the more complete current formulation by Klos et al. (2013) for WIMP-nucleus scattering. The results contradict older meson-exchange models by Towner (1987) that suggested small effects from many-body currents. The finding that QRPA quenching is smaller than shell-model quenching enables more reliable estimates of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, though the paper notes that if two-body currents are not the dominant source of quenching, experimental sensitivity to Majorana neutrino mass could be worse than currently believed.
