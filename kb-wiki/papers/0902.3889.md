# Structure of many-body continuum states in the proximity of exceptional points

> We demonstrate existence of exceptional points in many-body scattering continuum of atomic nucleus and discuss their salient effects on the example of one-nucleon spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim
Exceptional points (EPs) exist in the many-body scattering continuum of atomic nuclei, and their influence on one-nucleon spectroscopic factors is observable not only at the exact singularity but also relatively far from it in parameter space, making the phenomenon robust and potentially accessible to experiment.

## Method
The authors used the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), a real-energy continuum shell model, to study the one-proton continuum of \(^{16}\)Ne. They solved the eigenvalue problem for the energy-dependent, non-hermitian effective Hamiltonian \(H_{QQ}^{\text{eff}}(E)\) in the space of discrete shell-model states, coupling these states to three lowest one-proton decay channels via a residual delta interaction \(V_{12}=V_0\delta(r_1-r_2)\). The strength parameter \(V_0\) and total energy \(E\) were varied to search for a true degeneracy (EP) of two \(0^+\) resonances.

## Key numbers
- The exceptional point for the \(0_1^+\) and \(0_2^+\) eigenstates in \(^{16}\)Ne occurs at energy \(E = 26.523\) MeV, with continuum-coupling strength \(V_0 = -1182.32\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\).
- The elastic channel threshold is at \(E = 0\) MeV; the second channel threshold is at \(E = 0.671\) MeV.
- For a fixed-point solution, if the ground state energy is set to \(E_{0_1^+} = -0.5\) MeV, the \(0_2^+\) state energy becomes \(E_{0_2^+} = 4.918\) MeV; if the ground state is at \(+0.05\) MeV, the \(0_2^+\) state energy becomes 6.251 MeV.
- Spectroscopic factors are shown for \(V_0 = -1100\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), chosen to reproduce the experimental width of the \(5/2_1^+\) state in \(^{15}\)F.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of exceptional points in schematic many-body models such as the Lipkin model (Heiss 1991), the interacting boson model (Cejnar 2007), and the 3-level pairing model (Dukelsky 2008) to a realistic nuclear many-body system using the SMEC framework (Okołowicz 2003). It demonstrates that the entanglement of continuum states near an EP produces compensating variations in spectroscopic factors, a signature that could be searched for in nuclear reaction experiments. The results also connect to earlier theoretical work on degeneracies in open quantum systems (Kylin 1998, Rotter 2002, Znojil 2006) and to the topological phase effects predicted for adiabatic transport around EPs (Berry 1984).
