# The Symmetry Energy: Current Status of Ab Initio Predictions vs. Empirical Constraints

> Infinite nuclear matter is a suitable laboratory to learn about nuclear forces in many-body systems. Modern theoretical predictions of neutron-rich matter are particularly timely in view of recent and planned measurements of observables which are sensitive to the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric matter. For these reasons, over the past several years we have taken a broad look at the equation of state of neutron-rich matter and the closely related symmetry energy, which is the focal point of this article. Its density dependence is of paramount importance for a number of nuclear and astrophysical systems, ranging from neutron skins to the structure of neutron stars. We review and discuss ab initio predictions in relation to recent empirical constraints. We emphasize and demonstrate that free-space NN data pose stringent constraints on the density dependence of the neutron matter equation of state, which essentially determines the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation.

## Key claim
The paper argues that free-space nucleon-nucleon (NN) data impose stringent constraints on the density dependence of the neutron matter equation of state, which essentially determines the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation; however, the author notes that ab initio predictions generally agree on a relatively soft symmetry energy, in contrast to recent constraints extracted from electroweak scattering experiments such as PREX-II.

## Method
The author performs nonperturbative particle-particle ladder (Brueckner) calculations of infinite nuclear matter using chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions from leading order (LO) to fifth order (N⁴LO), with a regulator cutoff of Λ = 450 MeV. Three-nucleon forces (3NFs) are incorporated as density-dependent effective NN interactions up to N³LO, and uncertainty quantification is performed using order-by-order truncation error estimates following the prescription of Epelbaum et al. (2015).

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter at N³LO: ρ₀ = (0.161 ± 0.015) fm⁻³
- Saturation energy at N³LO: e(ρ₀) = (-14.98 ± 0.85) MeV
- Incompressibility at N³LO: K₀ = (216 ± 33) MeV
- The radius of a 1.0 M⊙ neutron star correlates with the density slope of the symmetry energy at saturation with correlation coefficient 0.87, and for 1.4 M⊙ with coefficient 0.75 (from reference LR1)
- The NN potential at fifth order reproduces NN data below pion production threshold with χ²/datum = 1.15

## Context
This review extends the author's previous work on ab initio predictions of neutron-rich matter (Sammarruca, 2021) and compares them against empirical constraints from phenomenological models such as Skyrme forces and relativistic mean-field models. The paper discusses how chiral EFT predictions for a soft symmetry energy contrast with recent constraints from the PREX-II electroweak scattering experiment, and notes that the radius of light to medium neutron stars provides a suitable observable to constrain microscopic theories of the equation of state at densities where they are reliable.
