# Multifragmentation and the symmetry term of the nuclear equation of state

> We investigate the possibility to extract the symmetry energy from multifragmentation data. The applicability of the grandcanonical formula earlier proposed by Ono {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 68}, 051601(R)] in the case of finite excited nuclei is tested within a microcanonical framework. Relatively good results are obtained except for large residual nuclei, especially when large sources are highly excited. Effects of secondary particle emission and the extent in which relevant information may be inferred from experimental observables are finally discussed.

## Key claim

The grandcanonical formula of Ono et al. (2003) for extracting the symmetry energy from multifragmentation data is reliable for large, highly excited sources, but overestimates the true symmetry energy by up to 4 MeV for the largest fragments; low experimental values of the symmetry coefficient (around 15 MeV) may be compatible with standard ground-state values when surface contributions and the use of the simplified formula neglecting isospin fractionation are accounted for.

## Method

The authors tested the grandcanonical formula of Ono et al. (2003) and a simplified version from Tsang et al. (2001) within a microcanonical multifragmentation model (MMM) that treats break-up fragments as normal-density objects with a liquid-drop binding energy. They simulated fragmenting sources with masses from A=100 to A=230, excitation energies from 2 to 10 MeV/nucleon, and freeze-out volumes from 4V₀ to 8V₀, then compared the symmetry energy extracted from isoscaling of the simulated isotopic yields to the input symmetry energy of the model. The effect of secondary particle evaporation on the extracted symmetry energy was also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- For sources (210,82) and (190,82) at V=4V₀ and 6 MeV/nucleon excitation, the additional surface, Coulomb, and isospin corrections to the symmetry energy are of order unity (MeV), making them negligible even for light fragments.
- For the largest fragments from Z=82 sources at 10 MeV/nucleon excitation, Eq. (1) (Ono formula) overestimates the input symmetry energy by less than 1 MeV.
- For Z=50 sources at 2 and 4 MeV/nucleon excitation, the Ono formula shows bending due to microcanonical conservation, with overestimation up to 4 MeV for the largest fragments.
- The simplified formula neglecting fractionation (Eq. 2) systematically underestimates the symmetry energy in the fragmentation regime, producing values around 15 MeV that may be compatible with standard ground-state values.
- After secondary decay, using asymptotic α values and break-up Z/⟨A⟩ values, the simplified formula yields extracted C_sym values ranging from 20 to 25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ono et al. (2003), who proposed the grandcanonical formula for extracting the symmetry energy, and Tsang et al. (2001), who introduced the simplified version neglecting fractionation. It addresses concerns raised by experimental results from the FRS collaboration (obtaining C_sym=14 MeV) and the INDRA-ALADIN collaboration (finding values decreasing from 25 to 15 MeV), showing that low extracted values may reflect surface effects or the neglect of fractionation rather than dilute freeze-out matter. The results enable future work to use isoscaling to probe fractionation in spinodal decomposition versus equilibrium phase separation, provided the effect of sequential decay on the isoscaling parameter α is experimentally controlled.
