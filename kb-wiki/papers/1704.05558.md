# Dynamical vs. equilibrium properties of the QCD phase transition: a holographic perspective

> We employ an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model, which is known to be in good agreement with lattice results for the QCD equation of state with $(2+1)$ flavors and physical quark masses, to investigate the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of the susceptibilities, conductivities, and diffusion coefficients associated with baryon, electric, and strangeness conserved charges. We also determine how the bulk and shear viscosities of the plasma vary in the plane of temperature and baryon chemical potential. The diffusion of conserved charges and the hydrodynamic viscosities in a baryon rich quark-gluon plasma are found to be suppressed with respect to the zero net baryon case. The transition temperatures associated with equilibrium and non-equilibrium quantities are determined as a function of the baryon chemical potential for the first time. Because of the crossover nature of the QCD phase transition even at moderately large values of the chemical potential, we find that the transition temperatures associated with different quantities are spread in the interval between $130-200$ MeV and they all decrease with increasing baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper uses a holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) model to compute transport coefficients and equilibrium properties of the quark-gluon plasma across the QCD phase transition, finding that the transition temperatures associated with different quantities are spread between 130 and 200 MeV and all decrease with increasing baryon chemical potential, but the authors note that the model's agreement with lattice QCD results at finite baryon chemical potential is only "reasonable" and could be improved by refining the holographic parameters.

## Method

The authors employ a bottom-up holographic EMD model in 5-dimensional anti-de Sitter spacetime, where the dilaton potential and Maxwell-dilaton coupling are fixed by matching the holographic equation of state and baryon susceptibility at zero baryon chemical potential to lattice QCD results for (2+1)-flavor QCD with physical quark masses. Transport coefficients are computed by solving linearized perturbation equations around the black hole backgrounds, using holographic Kubo formulas for conductivities and the membrane paradigm for electric charge transport, with the baryon, electric charge, and strangeness sectors treated separately.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperatures for different quantities are spread in the interval between 130 and 200 MeV, all decreasing with increasing baryon chemical potential
- The holographic model uses a scaling factor Lambda = 831 MeV to convert gravity-side observables to field theory units
- The 5-dimensional gravitational constant is fixed as kappa_5^2 = 12.5
- The baryon diffusion is found to be suppressed by the presence of a nontrivial baryon chemical potential, consistent with the expected behavior near a critical end point
- The electric charge conductivity at zero baryon chemical potential is compared to lattice QCD results from Aarts (2014), showing better agreement than many other models

## Context

This work extends previous holographic EMD studies by Rougemont (2015), Finazzo (2015), and Critelli (2016) that matched lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential, now computing transport coefficients across the (T, mu_B) plane for the first time. The results provide the first calculations of strangeness transport at finite baryon density, which is relevant for interpreting the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA, and enable hydrodynamic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma with temperature- and density-dependent transport coefficients.
