# Fate of Heavy Quark Bound States inside Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Transport equations have been applied successfully to describe the quarkonium evolution inside the quark-gluon plasma, which include both plasma screening effects and recombination. We demonstrate how the quarkonium transport equation is derived from QCD by using the open quantum system framework and effective field theory. Weak coupling and Markovian approximations used in the derivation are justified from a separation of scales. By solving the equations numerically, we study the Upsilon production in heavy ion collisions.

# Fate of Heavy Quark Bound States inside Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonium in-medium dynamics can be derived from QCD using the open quantum system framework and effective field theory, but only under the specific hierarchy of scales \(M \gg Mv \gg Mv^2 \gtrsim T \gtrsim m_D\) that justifies both the weak coupling and Markovian approximations.

## Method
The authors used the open quantum system framework, treating bound and unbound heavy quark-antiquark pairs as the subsystem and the quark-gluon plasma as the environment. They expanded the evolution equation to second order in perturbation theory to obtain the Lindblad equation, then applied a Wigner transform under the Markovian approximation to derive the Boltzmann transport equation. The coupled transport equations for quarkonium and open heavy quarks were solved numerically via Monte Carlo simulations for bottomonium in lead-lead collisions.

## Key numbers
- The hierarchy of scales requires \(Mv^2 \sim 500\) MeV for both charmonium and bottomonium, with plasma temperature \(T \lesssim 500\) MeV in current heavy ion experiments.
- Results were calculated for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions and compared with CMS measurements of \(R_{AA}\) and ALICE measurements of \(v_2\).
- The interaction vertex between color singlet and octet states scales as \(r T \sim T/(Mv) \sim v\), where \(v\) is assumed small.

## Context
This work extends prior phenomenological applications of transport equations to quarkonium evolution (Grandchamp 2003, Yan 2006, Krouppa 2015, Du 2017, Zhao 2017, Yao 2018) by providing a first-principles derivation from QCD. It connects to the effective field theory potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) developed by Brambilla (1999) and Fleming (2005). The results enable controlled theoretical studies of Upsilon suppression in heavy ion collisions and can be extended to doubly heavy baryon systems. Alternative approaches for a different hierarchy of scales (\(T \gg Mv^2\)) have been discussed by Brambilla (2017) and Miura (2019).
