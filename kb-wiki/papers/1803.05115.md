# Continuity of vortices from the hadronic to the color-flavor locked phase in dense matter

> We study how vortices in dense superfluid hadronic matter can connect to vortices in superfluid quark matter, as in rotating neutron stars, focusing on the extent to which quark-hadron continuity can be maintained. As we show, a singly quantized vortex in three-flavor symmetric hadronic matter can connect smoothly to a singly quantized non-Abelian vortex in three-flavor symmetric quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, without the necessity for boojums appearing at the transition.

## Key claim

A singly quantized vortex in three-flavor symmetric hadronic matter can connect smoothly to a singly quantized non-Abelian vortex in three-flavor symmetric quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, without the necessity for boojums appearing at the transition, provided the vortices carry the same baryon circulation.

## Method

The authors analyzed the circulation and angular momentum per baryon of vortices in both the hadronic phase (where baryon pairs form the condensate) and the CFL quark phase (where diquark condensates form). They compared the winding numbers and superfluid velocities of hadronic vortices, Abelian CFL vortices, and non-Abelian CFL vortices, using both gauge-fixed and gauge-invariant descriptions of the order parameters. The gauge-invariant six-quark order parameter \(\Upsilon^{ijk}\) was used to demonstrate continuity between the phases by projecting onto flavor-singlet and flavor-octet channels.

## Key numbers

- A singly quantized hadronic vortex (\(\nu_B = 1\)) has circulation \(C_B = 2\pi/(2\mu_B)\), where \(\mu_B\) is the baryon chemical potential.
- A singly quantized Abelian CFL vortex (\(\nu_A = 1\)) has circulation \(C_A = 3\pi/\mu_B\), three times that of a hadronic vortex.
- A singly quantized non-Abelian CFL vortex (\(\nu_1 = 1\)) has circulation \(C_{(1)} = \pi/\mu_B\), matching the hadronic vortex circulation.
- The angular momentum per baryon for a singly quantized hadronic vortex is \(L_{Bz}/N_B = 1/2\); for a singly quantized non-Abelian CFL vortex it is also \(L_{(1)z}/N_B = 1/2\).
- For an Abelian CFL vortex, the angular momentum per baryon is \(L_{Az}/N_B = 3/2\) for \(\nu_A = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of quark-hadron continuity (Schafer 1998, Alford 1999) from ground-state properties to angular-momentum-carrying states in rotating neutron stars. It contradicts the earlier suggestion by Cipriani (2012) and Eto (2013) that three non-Abelian CFL vortices must merge into three hadronic vortices via a boojum; instead, the authors show a single non-Abelian CFL vortex can connect directly to a single hadronic vortex. The results enable future studies of vortex arrays in rotating CFL matter and their implications for neutron star phenomenology.
