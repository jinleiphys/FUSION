# Evolution of nonlocal observables in an expanding boost-invariant plasma

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we compute analytically the late-time behavior of two-point functions, Wilson loops and entanglement entropy in a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills plasma undergoing a boost-invariant expansion. We take into account the effects of first order dissipative hydrodynamics and investigate the effects of the (time-dependent) shear viscosity on the various observables. The two-point functions decay exponentially at late times and are unaffected by the viscosity if the points are separated along the transverse directions. For longitudinal separation we find a much richer structure. In this case the exponential is modulated by a nonmonotonic function of the rapidities and a dimensionless combination of the shear viscosity and proper time. We show that this peculiar behavior constrains the regime of validity of the hydrodynamical expansion. In addition, similar results are found for certain Wilson loops and entanglement entropies.

## Key claim
Using AdS/CFT, the paper computes the late-time behavior of nonlocal observables in a strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma undergoing boost-invariant expansion, including first-order dissipative hydrodynamics effects. The key hedge is that the results are valid only in the regime where the dimensionless parameter chi = L * tau^{-1/3} * epsilon^{1/4} is much less than 1 (late times) and where the hydrodynamic parameter xi = tau^{-2/3} * eta_0 is small enough for the hydrodynamic description to apply.

## Method
The authors use the fluid/gravity duality to construct the bulk metric in Fefferman-Graham coordinates, assuming boost invariance along the collision axis and translational/rotational symmetry in the transverse plane. They expand the metric coefficients as series in tau^{-2/3} around the scaling variable v = z * tau^{-1/3} * epsilon^{1/4}, keeping terms up to first order in dissipative hydrodynamics. They then compute the renormalized lengths/areas of extremal surfaces (geodesics for two-point functions, Nambu-Goto surfaces for Wilson loops, and minimal surfaces for entanglement entropy) in the small-chi regime by expanding around the pure AdS solutions.

## Key numbers
- The two-point function for transverse separation decays as exp(-epsilon * Delta * |x1 - x1'|^4 / (90 * tau^{4/3})), independent of viscosity.
- For longitudinal separation, the two-point function exponent is modulated by f(Delta y, xi), which at small Delta y gives f = (1 - 6*xi)/90 + O((Delta y)^2), where xi = tau_0^{-2/3} * eta_0.
- The hydrodynamic description breaks down when xi > 1/6, as the function f(Delta y, xi) flips sign.
- For the Wilson loop with longitudinal side, the small Delta y behavior gives g(Delta y, xi) = Gamma(1/4)^4 * (4 - 9*xi) / (240 * pi^4) + O((Delta y)^2), imposing the constraint xi < 4/9.
- For entanglement entropy of a longitudinal strip, the small Delta y behavior gives h(Delta y, xi) = Gamma(1/6)^9 * (1 - xi) / (1280 * pi^{13/2}) + O((Delta y)^2), with the weaker constraint xi < 1.

## Context
This work extends the holographic thermalization program (which studies early-time equilibration via gravitational collapse) to the late-time hydrodynamic expansion regime, building on the boost-invariant plasma metric of BIPlasma (2010) and the fluid/gravity duality of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008). The results enable analytic understanding of how nonlocal observables approach equilibrium in an expanding, cooling plasma, and provide constraints on the validity of first-order hydrodynamics. The approach parallels the low-temperature expansion for static plasmas by Fischler et al. (2012), but now for a time-dependent background.
