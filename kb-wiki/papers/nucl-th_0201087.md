# Nuclear magnetic dipole properties and the triaxial deformation

> Nuclear magnetic dipole properties of ground bands and gamma-vibrational bands are studied for the first time using the triaxial projected shell model approach. The study is carried out for the Dy and Er isotopic chains, ranging from transitional to well-deformed region. It is found that the g-factor ratio of the 2^+ state in ground bands to that of gamma-bands, r=g(2^+, gamma-vib)/g(2^+, ground), varies along an isotopic chain. With the gamma-deformations, which best reproduce the energy levels for both bands, we obtain a qualitative agreement with the experimental data. This result thus suggests that study of the ratio may provide an important information on the triaxial deformation of a nuclear system. The angular-momentum dependence of the ground band g-factor on the triaxial deformation is also investigated.

## Key claim

The ratio of the g-factor of the 2⁺ state in the γ-vibrational band to that of the 2⁺ state in the ground band, r = g(2⁺, γ-vib)/g(2⁺, ground), varies along an isotopic chain and may serve as a fingerprint of nuclear triaxiality, but the agreement with experiment is only qualitative and depends on using the γ-deformations that best reproduce the energy levels.

## Method

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) was applied to even-even Dy and Er isotopes, using a triaxially deformed Nilsson basis with three-dimensional angular momentum projection. The Hamiltonian included pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole and quadrupole-pairing terms, and was diagonalized in a basis consisting of the triaxial quasiparticle vacuum with all allowed K-components. The resulting wave functions were used to compute g-factors for ground bands and γ-vibrational bands for spins 0 to 10.

## Key numbers

- For Dy isotopes, the deformation parameters used were ε = 0.21 to 0.29 and ε′ = 0.120 to 0.140, corresponding to γ values of 25° to 30° (Table 1).
- The calculated g-factor ratio r for Dy and Er isotopes starts around 0.8 at neutron number N=88, crosses r=1, reaches a value close to 1.1, then turns back as neutron number increases to N=100.
- For ¹⁵⁴Dy, the g-factors of the 2⁺ ground-band state and the 2⁺ γ-band state cross at a triaxiality around ε′ = 0.16 (Fig. 3).
- The monopole pairing strength used was G_M = [21.24 ∓ 13.86(N−Z)/A]/A, with the quadrupole pairing strength G_Q = 0.16 × G_M.
- The spin g-factors were quenched by a factor of 0.75: g_s^π = 5.586 × 0.75, g_s^ν = −3.826 × 0.75.

## Context

This work extends the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, previously developed by Sun and Sheikh (1999), to magnetic dipole properties for the first time. It addresses the lack of a microscopic systematic study treating ground bands and γ-bands simultaneously, which earlier cranking models (e.g., Bengtsson and Åberg, 1986; Törmänen and Tjøm, 1988; Cederwall et al., 1991; Satula and Szymanski, 1993), angular momentum projection methods (Andersson et al., 1990; Sun et al., 1994; Velázquez et al., 1999), and the interacting boson model (Kota and Sahu, 1995) had not provided. The results suggest that accurate measurement of the g-factor ratio r could help determine triaxial deformation, and they call for new experiments to resolve controversies such as the conflicting data for ¹⁶⁰Dy.
