# Production Uncertainties of p-Nuclei in the $γ$-Process in Massive Stars Using a Monte Carlo Approach

> Proton-rich nuclei, the so-called p-nuclei, are made in photodisintegration processes in outer shells of massive stars in the course of the final supernova explosion. Nuclear uncertainties in the production of these nuclei have been quantified in a Monte Carlo procedure. Bespoke temperature-dependent uncertainties were assigned to different types of reactions involving nuclei from Fe to Bi and all rates were varied randomly within the uncertainties. The resulting total production uncertainties of the p-nuclei are below a factor of two, with few exceptions. Key reactions dominating the final uncertainties have been identified in an automated procedure using correlations between rate and abundance uncertainties. Our results are compared to those of a previous study manually varying reaction rates.

# Production Uncertainties of p-Nuclei in the γ-Process in Massive Stars Using a Monte Carlo Approach

## Key claim
The total production uncertainties of p-nuclei from the γ-process in massive stars are below a factor of two for most nuclei, with few exceptions, when using a Monte Carlo procedure with bespoke temperature-dependent uncertainties assigned to different reaction types. This result is conditional on the specific stellar models (15 and 25 solar mass stars at solar metallicity) and the assumption that theoretical uncertainties for excited-state contributions dominate at typical γ-process temperatures.

## Method
The authors performed 10,000 Monte Carlo variations of reaction rates across approximately 100 trajectories in a network containing 3,800 nuclides, for several stellar models. Temperature-dependent uncertainty factors were assigned to different reaction types using Equation (1), combining experimental ground-state uncertainties with theoretical uncertainties for excited-state contributions, with asymmetric uncertainties for proton-induced (factor 2.0 up, 0.33 down) and alpha-induced (factor 2.0 up, 0.1 down) reactions and symmetric factor 2 uncertainties for neutron-induced reactions. Key reactions dominating final production uncertainties were identified through an automated correlation procedure between rate variations and abundance uncertainties, distinguishing multiple levels of importance.

## Key numbers
- Total production uncertainties for most p-nuclei are below a factor of two, with few exceptions, for both 15 solar mass and 25 solar mass solar metallicity stars.
- 10,000 rate variations were performed in the Monte Carlo procedure.
- The reaction network contained 3,800 nuclides.
- Theoretical uncertainty limits for neutron-induced reactions: factor of 2 (0.5) symmetric.
- Theoretical uncertainty limits for proton-induced reactions: factor of 2.0 (0.33) asymmetric.
- Theoretical uncertainty limits for alpha-induced reactions: factor of 2.0 (0.1) asymmetric.
- Key reactions confirmed from prior work include ⁹¹Nb + p ↔ γ + ⁹²Mo and ⁷⁷Br + p ↔ γ + ⁷⁸Kr as level 1 key rates, and ⁹⁵Tc + p ↔ γ + ⁹⁶Ru as a level 2 rate.
- Level 1 key rates include ¹⁶⁰Er + α ↔ γ + ¹⁶⁴Yb and ¹⁷⁶W + α ↔ γ + ¹⁸⁰Os.

## Context
This work extends prior γ-process studies by replacing manual variation of reaction rates, as done by Rapp (2006), with a Monte Carlo approach that simultaneously varies individual rates with temperature-dependent uncertainties. The automated correlation procedure identifies key reactions more reliably than visual inspection of flows or manual variation of limited rate sets, revealing that previous work omitted certain inner zones and used arbitrarily chosen variation factors without temperature dependence. The resulting lists of key reactions (mostly (n,γ)↔(γ,n) types, plus some (p,γ)↔(γ,p) and (α,γ)↔(γ,α)) provide targets for experimentalists and theorists to reduce production uncertainties, though many involve unstable target nuclei or strong excited-state contributions limiting experimental feasibility.
