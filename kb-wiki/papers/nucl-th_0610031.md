# $J/ψ$ production in Au+Au/Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=200 GeV and the threshold model

> Using the QGP motivated threshold model, where all the $J/ψ$'s are suppressed above a threshold density, we have analyzed the preliminary PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of nuclear modification factor for $J/ψ$'s in Cu+Cu and in Au+Au collisions, at RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=200 GeV. Centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions are well explained in the model for threshold densities in ranges of 3.6-3.7 $fm^{-2}$. $J/ψ$ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions on the other hand are not explained in the model.

## Key claim

The threshold model, which assumes complete suppression of all J/ψ mesons above a critical local transverse density, can explain the centrality dependence of J/ψ suppression in Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV for threshold densities in the range 3.6-3.7 fm⁻², but the same model fails to describe the suppression pattern observed in Cu+Cu collisions at the same energy.

## Method

The author applied the Blaizot threshold model, where J/ψ survival depends on local transverse density n(b,s) computed from the Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile. For each impact parameter b and transverse position s, if the local density exceeds a threshold density n_c, the anomalous suppression factor S_anom becomes zero (complete destruction); otherwise only conventional nuclear absorption with cross-section σ_abs applies. The model predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_AA as a function of participant number N_part were compared to preliminary PHENIX data for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at √sNN=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum transverse density in central Cu+Cu collisions: ~2.63 fm⁻²
- Maximum transverse density in central Au+Au collisions: ~4.32 fm⁻²
- Threshold density range that fits Au+Au data: 3.6-3.7 fm⁻²
- J/ψ suppression factor in most central Au+Au collisions: factor of ~3
- Sudden change of slope in R_AA observed around N_part ~150 in Au+Au collisions
- J/ψ-nucleon absorption cross-section from d+Au collisions: σ_abs ≈ 1-3 mb
- For Cu+Cu collisions, Glauber model with σ_abs=1-3 mb explains only very peripheral collisions (N_part=10-20)
- Mean square transverse momentum in p+p collisions: ⟨p²_T⟩_{NN} = 4.2 ± 0.7 GeV²
- Best fit for gluon-nucleon broadening parameter: σ_{gN}δ₀ = 0.31 ± 0.48 GeV² (ill-determined due to poor data quality)

## Context

This work extends the threshold model previously applied by Blaizot et al. (2000, 1996) to NA50 data on J/ψ suppression in 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, where a threshold density of 3.7 fm⁻² was obtained with σ_abs=6.4 mb. The analysis contradicts models predicting J/ψ enhancement at RHIC due to c-cbar recombination (Thews 2000, Braun-Munzinger 2000), and shows that the comover model (Capella 2000), statistical coalescence model (Kostyuk 2003), and kinetic model (Gorenstein 2000) all fail to explain the PHENIX preliminary data. The author's prior QCD-based nuclear absorption model (Chaudhuri 2003) explained Cu+Cu data but failed for Au+Au, leading to the present threshold model analysis. The results enable future work incorporating feed-down from ψ' and χ states and transverse expansion effects, which would lower the estimated threshold density from its present upper limit of 3.6-3.7 fm⁻².
