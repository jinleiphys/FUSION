# The Crystallography of Three-Flavor Quark Matter

> We analyze and compare candidate crystal structures for the crystalline color superconducting phase that may arise in cold, dense but not asymptotically dense, three-flavor quark matter. We determine the gap parameter Delta and free energy Omega(Delta) for many possible crystal structures within a Ginzburg-Landau approximation, evaluating Omega(Delta) to order Delta^6. In contrast to the two-flavor case, we find a positive Delta^6 term and hence an Omega(Delta) that is bounded from below for all the structures that we analyze. This means that we are able to evaluate Delta and Omega as a function of the splitting between Fermi surfaces for all the structures we consider. We find two structures with particularly robust values of Delta and the condensation energy, within a factor of two of those for the CFL phase which is known to characterize QCD at asymptotically large densities. The robustness of these phases results in their being favored over wide ranges of density. However, it also implies that the Ginzburg-Landau approximation is not quantitatively reliable. We develop qualitative insights into what makes a crystal structure favorable, and use these to winnow the possibilities. The two structures that we find to be most favorable are both built from condensates with face-centered cubic symmetry: in one case, the <ud> and <us> condensates are separately face centered cubic; in the other case <ud> and <us> combined make up a face centered cube.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in three-flavor crystalline color superconducting quark matter, the Ginzburg-Landau free energy expansion to order Δ⁶ yields a positive sextic term for all analyzed crystal structures, making the free energy bounded from below and allowing quantitative evaluation of the gap parameter Δ and condensation energy, but the authors caution that the Ginzburg-Landau approximation is not quantitatively reliable because the most favorable structures have Δ/Δ₀ as large as ~1/2.

## Method

The authors analyze candidate crystal structures for crystalline color superconductivity in three-flavor quark matter within a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a point-like four-quark interaction, evaluated in mean field theory. They compute the free energy Ω(Δ₂,Δ₃) to sextic order in a Ginzburg-Landau expansion for many possible sets of wave vectors q₃^a and q₂^a describing ⟨ud⟩ and ⟨us⟩ condensates, with up to eight plane waves per condensate. They assume Δ₂ = Δ₃ in magnitude and work in the weak-coupling limit Δ₀ ≪ μ, treating the strange quark mass Mₛ as a parameter and enforcing electric and color neutrality with chemical potentials μₑ = Mₛ²/(4μ) and μ₃ = μ₈ = 0 as in unpaired quark matter.

## Key numbers

- The favored crystal structures have gap parameters Δ as large as Δ₀/3, with the CubeX structure reaching Δ/Δ₀ = 1/2.
- The condensation energies of the most favorable structures are 1/3 to 1/2 of that in the CFL phase with Mₛ = 0, which is 3Δ₀²μ²/π².
- The crystalline phase has lower free energy than both gCFL and unpaired quark matter within the window 2.9 Δ₀ < Mₛ²/μ < 10.4 Δ₀.
- In the Ginzburg-Landau approximation, there remains a regime 2 Δ₀ < Mₛ²/μ < 2.9 Δ₀ where no crystalline phase has lower free energy than the gCFL phase.
- The energetically favored value of |q| for a single plane wave in the two-flavor case is q = η δμ with η = 1.1997, giving pairing rings with opening angle ψ₀ = 67.1°.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor crystalline color superconductivity analysis of Bowers (2002) to three flavors, where the additional ⟨us⟩ condensate and the presence of three Fermi surfaces with splittings δμ₂ = δμ₃ = Mₛ²/(8μ) change the Ginzburg-Landau structure qualitatively, in contrast to the two-flavor case, the sextic term is positive. The paper builds on the three-flavor crystalline analysis initiated by Casalbuoni (2005) and the simpler one-parameter family studied by Mannarelli (2006), and it provides candidate ground states that may resolve the magnetic instability of the gapless CFL phase found by Alford (2004) and Casalbuoni (2004). The results enable future work comparing these crystalline phases to the meson supercurrent phase proposed by Kryjevski (2005) and Gerhold (2006), and they motivate analysis beyond the Ginzburg-Landau approximation.
