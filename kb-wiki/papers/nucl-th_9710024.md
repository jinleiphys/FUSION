# Relative Properties of Smooth Terminating Bands

> The relative properties of smooth terminating bands observed in the A~110 mass region are studied within the effective alignment approach. Theoretical values of $i_{eff}$ are calculated using the configuration-dependent shell-correction model with the cranked Nilsson potential. Reasonable agreement with experiment shows that previous interpretations of these bands are consistent with the present study. Contrary to the case of superdeformed bands, the effective alignments of these bands deviate significantly from the pure single-particle alignments < j_x > of the corresponding orbitals. This indicates that in the case of smooth terminating bands, the effects associated with changes in equilibrium deformations contribute significantly to the effective alignment.

# Relative Properties of Smooth Terminating Bands

## Key claim

The effective alignment approach, previously successful for superdeformed bands, can be applied to smooth terminating bands in the A~110 mass region, but unlike the superdeformed case the effective alignment in these bands is significantly influenced by changes in equilibrium deformation rather than being dominated purely by single-particle alignments. The reasonable agreement between calculated and experimental effective alignments confirms that previous configuration assignments for these bands are consistent.

## Method

The authors used the configuration-dependent shell-correction model with a cranked Nilsson potential, diagonalized in a rotating harmonic oscillator basis with small couplings between different N-shells neglected to obtain smooth diabatic orbitals. Calculations were performed on a mesh in deformation space (ε₂, ε₄, γ) with total energy minimized separately for each fixed configuration and spin, neglecting pairing correlations (considered realistic above I~20ħ in this mass region). Effective alignment i_eff was defined as the difference in spin between two bands at constant γ-transition energy, and theoretical values were compared with experimental data for bands in ¹⁰⁸,¹⁰⁹Sn and ¹⁰⁹,¹¹⁰Sb.

## Key numbers

- The effective alignment i_eff for the ¹⁰⁹Sn(2)/¹¹⁰Sb(1) pair is 3.8ħ; changing the energy of the transition depopulating the terminating state in ¹⁰⁹Sn(2) by ±100 keV (≈4% of the transition energy) changes i_eff by ±0.74ħ (≈20% of the original value)
- For the ¹⁰⁹Sn(2)/¹¹⁰Sb(1) pair, increasing calculated γ-transition energies between the 39.5⁻ and 37.5⁻ states by 166 keV and between the 37.5⁻ and 35.5⁻ states by 33 keV brings theory into excellent agreement with experiment near termination
- The difference in γ-deformation between neighboring states near termination is generally 20-30° at ε₂ values of 0.10-0.15
- The average gain in i_eff at highest rotational frequencies for pairs involving ¹⁰⁹Sn(4,5) or ¹¹⁰Sb(2) bands is approximately 0.5ħ
- The fourth (g₇/₂d₅/₂)(α=-1/2) orbital contributes -1/2ħ at termination, while the lowest (d₃/₂s₁/₂)(α=-1/₂) orbital contributes +3/2ħ

## Context

This work extends the effective alignment approach previously applied to superdeformed bands in the A~140-150 mass region (Ragnarsson 1991, 1993) to smooth terminating bands in the A~110 region, where bands in ¹⁰⁸,¹⁰⁹Sn and ¹⁰⁹,¹¹⁰Sb had been identified as terminating bands by earlier experimental studies (Sn108, Sn109, Sb109new, Sb110). The study enables systematic testing of configuration assignments for unlinked bands (where spins and parities are not firmly established experimentally) and provides a sensitive probe of how well the cranked Nilsson model describes shape changes near termination, particularly the prolate-oblate energy difference and polarization effects induced by active particles. The results suggest that the energy gap between the fourth (g₇/₂d₅/₂)(α=-1/2) orbital and the lowest (d₃/₂s₁/₂)(α=-1/2) orbital in the current Nilsson parametrization may need adjustment by approximately 0.5-1.0 MeV to reproduce experimental observations.
