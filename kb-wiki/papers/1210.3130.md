# Deformation and $Î±$ clustering in excited states of $^{42}$Ca

> The coexistence of various low-lying deformed states in $^{42}$Ca and $Î±$--$^{38}$Ar correlations in those deformed states have been investigated using deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Wave functions of the low-lying states are obtained via parity and angular momentum projections and the generator coordinate method (GCM). Basis wave functions of the GCM calculation are obtained via energy variations with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameter $Î˛$ and the distance between $Î±$ and $^{38}$Ar clusters. The rotational band built on the $J^Ď€= 0_2^+$ (1.84 MeV) state as well as the $J^Ď€= 0_3^+$ (3.30 MeV) state are both reproduced. The coexistence of two additional $K^Ď€= 0^+$ rotational bands is predicted; one band is shown to be built on the $J^Ď€= 0_3^+$ state. ## Key claim

The paper claims that three low-lying rotational $K^\pi = 0^+$ bands with $6p4h$, $4p2h$, and $8p6h$ configurations coexist in $^{42}$Ca, but the ND2 and SD bands are theoretical predictions that have not yet been observed experimentally; the ND1 band is identified with the experimental $K^\pi = 0_2^+$ band, while the ND2 band head corresponds to the $J^\pi = 0_3^+$ state.

## Method

The author used deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective interaction, performing parity and angular momentum projections and the generator coordinate method (GCM). Basis wave functions were obtained via energy variations with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ and on the distance $d$ between $\alpha$ and $^{38}$Ar clusters, sampling configurations with $2p$, $4p2h$, $6p4h$, and $8p6h$ particle-hole configurations as well as $\alpha$-$^{38}$Ar cluster structures up to 9.0 fm intercluster distance.

## Key numbers

- The $J^\pi = 0_2^+$ state is at 1.84 MeV and the $J^\pi = 0_3^+$ state is at 3.30 MeV in $^{42}$Ca.
- The dominant quadrupole deformation parameters for the ND1, ND2, and SD bands are $\beta = 0.43$, 0.28, and 0.53, respectively.
- Theoretical in-band $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the ND1 band: $2^+ \to 0^+$ = 28.55 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, $4^+ \to 2^+$ = 33.12 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, $6^+ \to 4^+$ = 38.45 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (in Weisskopf units with $B_\mathrm{W.u.} = 8.67~e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$).
- Theoretical in-band $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the SD band: $2^+ \to 0^+$ = 82.12 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, $4^+ \to 2^+$ = 107.99 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, $6^+ \to 4^+$ = 130.18 $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$.
- Experimental $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the $K^\pi = 0_2^+$ band: $4_2^+ \to 2_2^+$ = $57 \pm 42$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, $6_2^+ \to 4_2^+$ = $50^{+35}_{-16}$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$.
- The GCM calculation converged such that energies change by less than 0.3 MeV when the number of basis wave functions is halved.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deformed states and cluster structures in the $A \sim 40$ region, where normal-deformed and superdeformed bands have been confirmed experimentally in $^{36,38,40}$Ar, $^{40}$Ca, $^{42}$Ca, and $^{44}$Ti. The $\alpha + ^{38}$Ar orthogonality condition model (OCM) by PhysRevC.51.586 described $4p2h$ states with $\alpha$-$^{38}$Ar cluster structures but did not produce a rotational band with a $6p4h$ configuration in low-lying states. The present full-microscopic calculation predicts two additional $K^\pi = 0^+$ rotational bands beyond the one observed experimentally, and shows that the members of the ND2 band could potentially be observed by combining $\alpha$-transfer reactions with $\gamma$-spectroscopy experiments.
