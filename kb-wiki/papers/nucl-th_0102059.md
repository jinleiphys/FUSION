# Correlations in Many-Body Systems with Two-time Green's Functions

> The Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations are solved numerically for infinite nuclear matter. In particular we calculate correlation energies and correlation times. Approximating the Green's functions in the KB collision kernel by the free Green's functions the Levinson equation is obtained. This approximation is valid for weak interactions and/or low densities. It relates to the extended quasi-classical approximation for the spectral function. Comparing the Levinson, Born and KB calculations allows for an estimate of higher order spectral corrections to the correlations. A decrease in binding energy is reported due to spectral correlations and off-shell parts in the reduced density matrix.

## Key claim

The Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations are solved numerically for infinite nuclear matter, and a decrease in binding energy is reported due to spectral correlations and off-shell parts in the reduced density matrix; the Levinson equation, obtained by approximating Green's functions in the KB collision kernel by free Green's functions, is valid only for weak interactions and/or low densities.

## Method

The KB equations for two-time Green's functions were time-evolved numerically for infinite nuclear matter starting from an initially uncorrelated Fermi gas at specified density and temperature, using a local time-independent effective interaction with a Gaussian form factor. The Levinson equation was obtained from the KB equations by replacing the self-consistently calculated Green's functions in the collision kernel with free (quasi-particle) Green's functions, and the second-order Born approximation was calculated using constant time-independent distribution functions. The momentum mesh used 57 points along each Cartesian axis with Δp = 0.2 fm⁻¹, and typically 80 time steps with Δt = 0.25 fm/c were employed.

## Key numbers

- Correlation times t_c from KB calculations scale roughly as t_c = 1/(2E_F), where E_F is the Fermi energy, giving times half as large as the previous analytic Levinson result of t_c = 1/E_F.
- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.18 fm⁻³), after 80 time steps the total energy decreased by 3.8% in the Levinson case and increased by 1.3% in the KB case.
- The effective interaction used had parameters η = 0.57 fm and V₀ = -453 MeV, with the form V(p) = π^(3/2) η³ V₀ exp(-¼ η² p²).
- Correlation times were found to be roughly independent of interaction strength for times smaller than 2 fm/c, though an overshoot in correlation energy appears as strength decreases.

## Context

This work extends previous numerical solutions of the KB equations by Danielewicz (1984) and earlier work by Kohler (1995, 1996) for nuclear matter, and extends comparisons with the Levinson equation from Morawetz (1999). The results enable estimates of higher-order spectral corrections to correlations in nuclear matter and are relevant for understanding correlation times in heavy-ion collisions, with implications for interferometry measurements as discussed in Morawetz (2000).
