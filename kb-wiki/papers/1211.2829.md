# A combined study of the pion's static properties and form factors

> We study consistently the pion's static observables and the elastic and γ*γ-> π^0 transition form factors within a light-front model. Consistency requires that all calculations are performed within a given model with the same and single adjusted length or mass-scale parameter of the associated pion bound-state wave function. Our results agree well with all extent data including recent Belle data on the γ*γ-> π^0 form factor at large q^2, yet the BaBar data on this transition form factor resists a sensible comparison. We relax the initial constraint on the bound-state wave function and show the BaBar data can partially be accommodated. This, however, comes at the cost of a hard elastic form factor not in agreement with experiment. Moreover, the pion charge radius is about 40% smaller than its experimentally determined value. It is argued that a decreasing charge radius produces an ever harder form factor with a bound-state amplitude difficultly reconcilable with soft QCD. We also discuss why vector dominance type models for the photon-quark vertex, based on analyticity and crossing symmetry, are unlikely to reproduce the litigious transition form factor data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that within a consistent light-front model using a single adjusted length-scale parameter, the Belle data on the γ*γ → π⁰ transition form factor at large q² can be reproduced, but the BaBar data on the same transition form factor cannot be accommodated without simultaneously producing a hard elastic form factor that disagrees with experiment and a pion charge radius about 40% smaller than the experimentally determined value.

## Method

The authors employed a light-front quark model with two parametrizations of the pion bound-state wave function (Gaussian and hydrogen-type), each containing a single length-scale parameter r_nr adjusted to reproduce the pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV. They calculated the pion's static properties (decay constant and charge radius), the elastic electromagnetic form factor F_π(Q²), and the γ*γ → π⁰ transition form factor F_γπ(Q²) within the impulse approximation on the light cone, using constituent quark masses of 220 MeV and 330 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used to fix the wave function scale parameter: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- For the Gaussian model with m_u,d = 220 MeV and f_π = 92.4 MeV: r_nr = 0.345 fm, charged pion rms radius ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.637 fm, neutral pion rms radius ⟨r_π⁰²⟩^{1/2} = 0.683 fm
- For the hydrogen model with m_u,d = 220 MeV and f_π = 92.4 MeV: r_nr = 0.593 fm, charged pion rms radius ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.795 fm, neutral pion rms radius ⟨r_π⁰²⟩^{1/2} = 0.782 fm
- Experimental charged pion rms radius: ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm (Beringer 2012)
- The asymptotic pQCD limit for the transition form factor: lim_{Q²→∞} Q² F_γπ⁰(Q²) = 2f_π = 0.185 GeV
- When the Gaussian model with f_π = 110 MeV was used to partially accommodate BaBar data, the charged pion charge radius became ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.406 fm, which is about 40% smaller than the experimental value

## Context

This work extends the light-front model developed by Frederico (1992, 1994) and refined in subsequent studies by de Melo (1997, 1999, 2003, 2005), El-Bennich (2008), and da Silva (2012). It differs from numerous recent attempts by Radyushkin (2009), Dorokhov (2010), Kroll (2010), and others to reproduce the large-Q² BaBar data by insisting on a consistent simultaneous treatment of all extant data rather than fitting only the γ*γ → π⁰ transition form factor. The results enable a critical assessment of whether vector dominance type models for the photon-quark vertex are likely to reproduce the disputed transition form factor data.
