# Causal hydrodynamics of gauge theory plasmas from AdS/CFT duality

> We study causal hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart theory) of gauge theory plasmas from the AdS/CFT duality. Causal hydrodynamics requires new transport coefficients (relaxation times) and we compute them for a number of supersymmetric gauge theories including the N=4 SYM. However, the relaxation times obtained from the "shear mode" do not agree with the ones from the "sound mode," which implies that the Israel-Stewart theory is not a sufficient framework to describe the gauge theory plasmas.

## Key claim

The Israel-Stewart theory of causal hydrodynamics is not a sufficient framework to describe gauge theory plasmas, because the relaxation time for shear viscous stress obtained from the "shear mode" does not agree with the one obtained from the "sound mode."

## Method

The authors solve perturbation equations in Schwarzschild-AdS black hole (SAdS) backgrounds in various dimensions, corresponding to different supersymmetric gauge theories including N=4 SYM. They compute the dispersion relations for the diffusive mode, shear mode, and sound mode to subleading order in frequency and momentum, extracting the relaxation times by matching to the Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic dispersion relations. The bulk gauge field perturbations give the diffusive mode, while metric perturbations give the shear and sound modes.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 SYM (SAdS5), the relaxation time from the shear mode is tau_pi = (1 - ln 2)/(2 pi T), which is approximately 0.049 fm for T^{-1} = 1 fm.
- For N=4 SYM (SAdS5), the relaxation time from the sound mode is tau_pi = (2 - ln 2)/(2 pi T), which is approximately 0.21 fm for T^{-1} = 1 fm.
- For SAdS4 (M2-brane), the relaxation time from the sound mode is tau_pi = (18 - (9 ln 3 - sqrt(3) pi))/(24 pi T), approximately 0.18 fm for T^{-1} = 1 fm.
- For SAdS7 (M5-brane), the relaxation time from the sound mode is tau_pi = (36 - (9 ln 3 + sqrt(3) pi))/(24 pi T), approximately 0.27 fm for T^{-1} = 1 fm.
- For N=4 SYM, the relaxation time for charge diffusion from the diffusive mode is tau_R = (ln 2)/(2 pi T), approximately 0.11 fm for T^{-1} = 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT computation of first-order transport coefficients (such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 1/(4 pi), previously found by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2002) and Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2003)) to second-order coefficients in the Israel-Stewart formalism. The results show that the Israel-Stewart theory is insufficient for gauge theory plasmas, motivating the need for more general causal hydrodynamic frameworks such as divergence-type theories. The computed relaxation times are relevant for numerical simulations of causal hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, as performed by groups such as Romatschke (2007), Chaudhuri (2007), Song and Heinz (2007), and Dusling and Teaney (2007).
