# GSI Oscillations as Interference of Neutrino Flavour Mass--Eigenstates and Measuring Process

> This paper is addressed to the proof of the important role of measuring apparatus, i.e. the measuring process, in the formation of necessary and sufficient conditions for the explanation of a time modulation of K-shell electron capture (EC) decay rates of hydrogen-like (H-like) heavy ions (or the GSI oscillations) as the interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates of the electron neutrino constituents. For our analysis we use a toy-model, which has been recently proposed by Peshkin arXiv: 1403.4292 [nucl-th] for a verification of the mechanism of the GSI oscillations as the interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates by Ivanov and Kienle Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502 (2009).

## Key claim

The GSI oscillations (time modulation of K-shell electron capture decay rates of hydrogen-like heavy ions) can be explained as interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates, but only when the measuring apparatus (resonant and capacitive pickups in the experimental storage ring) is included in the analysis; the authors argue that Peshkin's (2014) toy-model fails to produce the interference because it omits the non-unitary evolution caused by interactions of ions with the measuring apparatus.

## Method

The authors analyze Peshkin's (2014) toy-model for the GSI oscillations, which uses a translational-invariant Hamiltonian to describe the decay of parent ions into daughter ions and neutrino mass-eigenstates. They then extend this model by adding a time-dependent, translational non-invariant potential U(t) representing the interactions of ions with the resonant pickup (245 MHz resonator) and capacitive pickup (Schottky noise detector) in the ESR. Using Heisenberg equations of motion, they show that this potential causes non-conservation of energy and momentum, leading to non-vanishing off-diagonal matrix elements in the survival probability that enable interference between decay channels.

## Key numbers

* The resonant pickup causes an energy loss of order Δℰ ∼ 15 μeV per interaction with the fundamental mode, corresponding to an observation time Δt ∼ 0.28 ns during which ions evolve non-unitarily.
* Ions in the ESR move with velocity v = 0.71 and complete a total revolution over the circumference Π = 108.36 m in T_rev = 510 ns.
* The optimized measuring period is 54 s, with sampling times δt = 64 ms for the resonant pickup and δt = 32 ms for the capacitive pickup.
* Electron cooling reduces the relative transverse velocity spread to Δv/v ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁷.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Ivanov and Kienle, 2009) that GSI oscillations arise from interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates, and directly addresses a critique by Peshkin (2014) who argued that translational invariance of the Hamiltonian prevents such interference. The paper argues that the measuring process, as described by Wigner (1963) and von Neumann (1932), must be included to break translational invariance and enable the interference; this enables a consistent explanation of why the time modulation appears in electron capture decays but not in β⁺ decays of the same ions, as observed at GSI.
