# Neutrino oscillations with a polarized laser beam: an analogical demonstration experiment

> The underlying physics of neutrino oscillation in vacuum can be demonstrated by an optical analogical experiment. Two different neutrino flavors are represented by two polarization states of a laser beam, whereas the different phase propagation in vacuum is mimicked by the propagation difference of an ordinary and an extraordinary beam in a birefringent crystal. This allows us to demonstrate neutrino oscillation by optical methods in a fully microscopic way at the particle level. The description of both realizations of oscillation is also mathematically identical. In our demonstration experiment we can vary the oscillation parameters such as propagation length L and mixing angle Theta.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the underlying physics of two-flavor neutrino oscillation in vacuum can be demonstrated by an optical analogical experiment using a polarized laser beam and a birefringent crystal, with the mathematical description being fully identical; however, the demonstration is strictly an analogy for vacuum oscillation only and does not address three-flavor mixing or matter effects.

## Method

The experiment uses a red laser pointer (wavelength 633 nm) whose beam is linearly polarized to define a "flavor" state, then passed through a doublet of lithium tetra borate (Li2B4O7) birefringent crystals with ordinary refractive index n_ord = 1.552 and extraordinary refractive index n_extra = 1.609. The crystals have a trapezoidal shape with a 0.2 degree wedge angle, allowing variation of the propagation length L through the birefringent material by moving the crystals transversely relative to each other. After the crystals, the beam is split and sent through two orthogonal analyzers to photodiodes, which record the intensity in both "flavor" states simultaneously as a function of L and mixing angle theta.

## Key numbers

- Laser wavelength: lambda = 633 nm
- Ordinary refractive index of LTB crystal: n_ord = 1.552
- Extraordinary refractive index of LTB crystal: n_extra = 1.609
- Oscillation length in the optical analog: lambda_osc = lambda / Delta n = 11 micrometers (where Delta n = n_extra - n_ord)
- Crystal dimensions: 15 x 10 x 10 mm^3 in the [100], [010], and [001] directions
- Wedge angle of crystal surfaces: 0.2 degrees
- Transversal movement of crystals: 5.5 mm each, corresponding to a change in geometrical path Delta L = 38 micrometers (about 3.5 oscillation lengths)

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical approach of demonstrating neutrino oscillation through analogical experiments, contrasting with the purely mathematical teaching that relies on published data from experiments such as Super Kamiokande (1998). The author notes that pendulum demonstration experiments with coupled pendula (Kobel) exist but do not represent microscopic quantum states, whereas this optical analog operates at the particle level and can be understood by first-year or second-year students in an optics class. The experiment enables direct visualization of oscillation parameters (propagation length L and mixing angle theta) in a portable setup suitable for outreach and high school physics education.
