# Boson-Faddeev in the Unitary Limit and Efimov States

> A numerical study of the Faddeev equation for bosons is made with two-body interactions at or close to the Unitary limit. Separable interactions are obtained from phase-shifts defined by scattering length and effective range. In EFT-language this would correspond to NLO. Both ground and Efimov state energies are calculated. For effective ranges $r_0 > 0$ and rank-1 potentials the total energy $E_T$ is found to converge with momentum cut-off $Λ$ for $Λ> \sim 10/r_0$ . In the Unitary limit ($1/a=r_0= 0$) the energy does however diverge. It is shown (analytically) that in this case $E_T=E_uΛ^2$. Calculations give $E_u=-0.108$ for the ground state and $E_u=-1.\times10^{-4}$ for the single Efimov state found. The cut-off divergence is remedied by modifying the off-shell t-matrix by replacing the rank-1 by a rank-2 phase-shift equivalent potential. This is somewhat similar to the counterterm method suggested by Bedaque et al. This investigation is exploratory and does not refer to any specific physical system.

## Key claim

For bosons in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length and zero effective range) with a rank-1 separable potential, the three-body total energy diverges as \(E_T = E_u \Lambda^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is the momentum cut-off; this divergence can be remedied by replacing the rank-1 potential with a rank-2 phase-shift equivalent potential, similar to the counterterm method of Bedaque et al.

## Method

The author solved the Faddeev equation for three bosons numerically, using separable two-body interactions obtained via inverse scattering from phase-shifts defined by a scattering length \(a\) and effective range \(r_0\). For the unitary limit, a rank-1 potential with a momentum cut-off \(\Lambda\) was used, and the resulting ultraviolet divergence was addressed by constructing a rank-2 phase-shift equivalent potential with an additional repulsive component parameterized by \(r_c\). Energies of both the ground state and Efimov states were computed as functions of \(\lambda\) (interaction strength), \(1/a\), \(r_0\), and \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy in the unitary limit: \(E_u = -0.108\) (dimensionless, with \(E_T = E_u \Lambda^2\)).
- Single Efimov state energy in the unitary limit: \(E_u = -1.0 \times 10^{-4}\) (dimensionless, with \(E_T = E_u \Lambda^2\)).
- For effective ranges \(r_0 > 0\) with rank-1 potentials, the total energy \(E_T\) converges for momentum cut-off \(\Lambda > \sim 10/r_0\).
- The ground state energy \(E_u(\lambda)\) becomes zero at \(\lambda \sim 0.77\).
- For the rank-2 potential, convergence for large \(\Lambda\) was achieved with \(r_c = 0.12\) fm.
- With \(r_0 = 0.1\) and \(\Lambda = 75\), only one Efimov state was found; from the formula \(N = (1/\pi)\ln(|a|/r_0)\), two states would be expected only for \(1/a < 0.025\).

## Context

This work extends numerical studies of the three-boson system at and near the unitary limit, following the theoretical prediction of Efimov states by Efimov (1970) and the renormalization approach using a three-body counterterm by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). The author shows that the ultraviolet divergence in the unitary limit can be controlled by modifying the off-shell t-matrix via a rank-2 potential, which provides an alternative to the counterterm method. The results are exploratory and not tied to any specific physical system, but they enable further investigation of three-body forces and off-shell effects in few-body systems with large scattering lengths.
