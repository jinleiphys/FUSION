# Correlation between the Shape Coexistence and Stability in Mo and Ru isotopes

> In a rapidly changing shape phase region, the presence of shape coexistence and its possible impact on the decay modes and half$-$lives, has been explored in astrophysically interesting Mo and Ru isotopes, in an extensive study within the microscopic theoretical framework using Nilsson Strutinsky Method and Relativistic Mean Field Model. The isotopic chains of Mo and Ru exhibit rapid shape phase transitions, triaxial $γ$ softness, shape instability along with many coexisting states mostly with oblate and triaxial shapes. Proton and neutron separation energies have been calculated and compared with the available data. Results obtained from both the formalisms are in good agreement with each other as well as the available experimental data. Our computed $β-$decay half$-$lives and separation energy for nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence were examined for the decay mode from second minima state of the parent nuclei to the ground or excited state of the daughter nuclei. The second minima state of the coexisting shapes in Mo and Ru isotopes, were seen to impact the structural properties, $β-$decay half$-$lives, separation energy and hence the stability of the nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape coexistence (the presence of second minima states with different deformations lying close to the ground state) in Mo and Ru isotopes can significantly impact beta-decay half-lives, separation energies, and thus nuclear stability, but this effect is demonstrated only through theoretical calculations using the Nilsson Strutinsky Method and Relativistic Mean Field Model, with the caveat that the results are model-dependent and require experimental verification.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary theoretical frameworks: the Nilsson Strutinsky Method (NSM) with triaxially deformed Nilsson model and Strutinsky's shell correction prescription, and the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model using NL3* and DD-PC1 parameter sets. They calculated potential energy surfaces as functions of deformation parameters (beta_2 and gamma) for the entire isotopic chains of Mo (A=78-144) and Ru (A=84-152) to locate ground state and second minima, then computed beta-decay half-lives and separation energies for nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence, considering decays from second minima states to ground or excited states of daughter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Shell correction to energy reaches minima of about -5 MeV at N=82 and -2 MeV at N=50 for both Mo and Ru isotopes
- For 92Mo, the second minima at prolate shape (beta_2=0.03, gamma=-120 degrees) lies only 222 keV above the oblate ground state minima (beta_2=0.11, gamma=-180 degrees)
- For 90Mo, the energy difference between triaxial minima (beta_2=0.15, gamma=-140 degrees) and prolate minima (beta_2=0.14) is Delta E=0.016 MeV, while oblate minima lies at Delta E=0.614 MeV
- For 80Mo, the triaxial and oblate minima have an energy difference of around 1 MeV
- For 104Ru, oblate and triaxial minima have very close energies, with a prolate minima at Delta E=0.732 MeV
- The pairing strength used in RMF calculations is V_0=350 MeV fm^3 for the delta-force pairing interaction
- The DD-PC1 separable pairing force parameters are G=728 MeV fm^3 and alpha=0.644 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in the A=80-120 mass region, particularly building on the authors' earlier work on 2p-radioactivity (Aggarwal, 2023) and prior investigations of Zr, Mo, and Ru isotopes by Nabi (2019), Abusara (2017), and Hukkanen (2021). The study also references the Weizsacker-Skyrme (WS4) model and KTUY model for comparison of deformation predictions. The results enable future investigations of how shape coexistence affects astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis paths and provide theoretical predictions for experimental probes of shape isomers in neutron-rich nuclei.
