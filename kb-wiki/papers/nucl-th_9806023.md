# Charmonium Production in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> This is a review of theoretical attempts to describe production of heavy quark bound states in nucleus-nucleus collisions, in particular, the relative suppression of $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ production observed in these reactions. The review begins with a survey of experimental data for proton-induced reactions and their theoretical interpretation. The evidence for additional suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions is discussed and various theoretical models of charmonium absorption by comoving matter are presented and analyzed. The review concludes with suggestions for future research that would help clarify the implications of $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb + Pb collisions observed by the NA50 experiment.

## Key claim

This review argues that the "anomalous" suppression of $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ production observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS may indicate quark-gluon plasma formation, but only after all other conceivable mechanisms, particularly absorption by comoving hadrons, are ruled out through quantitative analysis of the data.

## Method

The author surveys experimental data from proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions (NA38 and NA50 at CERN) and analyzes them within the Glauber model framework, using a nuclear absorption cross section parameter $\sigma_{c\bar cN}^{\rm (abs)}$. The review then examines three categories of theoretical models for charmonium absorption by comoving matter: deconfinement in a quark-gluon plasma, thermal dissociation in a hadronic gas, and pre-thermal dissociation mechanisms (pre-equilibrium parton plasma or color flux tubes). Specific calculations are compared, including the Bhanot-Peskin perturbative approach and the constituent quark model of Martins, Blaschke, and Quack.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross section from Glauber analysis of $p+A$ data: $\sigma_{c\bar cN}^{\rm (abs)} = (7.3 \pm 0.6)$ mb
- Critical dissociation temperature for $J/\psi$: $T_{\rm d}^{(J/\psi)} \approx 1.2\; T_{\rm c}$
- Critical dissociation temperature for $\Upsilon$: $T_{\rm d}^{(\upsilon)} \approx 2T_{\rm c}$
- Peak cross section for $J/\psi$ dissociation by a gluon in the dipole approximation: about 3 mb at a gluon momentum around 1 GeV/$c$
- Pion momentum must reach 5 GeV/$c$ before attaining an absorption cross section in excess of 1 mb for $J/\psi$ dissociation
- Thermally averaged cross section for $J/\psi$ absorption on a pion gas remains less than 0.1 mb within any realistic temperature range
- Cross section for $\pi + J/\psi \to D^* + \bar D$ calculated in the constituent quark model peaks around 1 GeV kinetic energy at a value of 15 mb
- Neutralization time for color octet to singlet transition: $\tau_{8\to 1} \approx 0.3 n \; {\rm fm}/c$ (where $n$ is the principal quantum number)
- Nuclear $A$-dependence exponent for $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ in $p+A$: $\alpha \approx 0.92$

## Context

This review extends the work of Matsui and Satz (1985), who first proposed charmonium suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature, and the systematic framework of Karsch and Satz (1991) for heavy quark bound states in superdense matter. It addresses the puzzle that all data up to 1994 (from O+U and S+U collisions) could be explained by nuclear final state interactions alone, as argued by Capella et al. (1988) and Gerschel and Hufner (1992), while the 1996 NA50 Pb+Pb data show additional suppression. The review enables future theoretical work to sharpen charmonium spectroscopy as a tool for exploring superdense hadronic matter, particularly by resolving the orders-of-magnitude discrepancy between the Bhanot-Peskin perturbative approach and the constituent quark model results of Martins, Blaschke, and Quack.
