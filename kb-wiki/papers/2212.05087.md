# Weak decay of halo nuclei

> We investigate the weak decay of one-neutron halo nuclei into the proton-core continuum, i.e., beta-delayed proton emission from the halo nucleus using a cluster effective field theory for halo nuclei. On the one hand, we calculate the direct decay into the continuum. On the other hand, we consider the case of resonant final state interactions between the proton and the core. We present our formalism and discuss the application to the decay of $^{11}$Be in detail. Moreover, we compare to recent experimental results for the branching ratio and resonance parameters. As another example, we consider the case of $^{19}$C and predict the branching ratio for beta-delayed proton emission.

## Key claim

The authors use a cluster effective field theory (Halo EFT) to calculate beta-delayed proton emission from one-neutron halo nuclei, finding that the branching ratio for \(^{11}\)Be is consistent with recent experimental results only when a resonance with definite isospin \(T=1/2\) is assumed in the final state, and that the branching ratio for \(^{19}\)C is predicted to be almost six orders of magnitude smaller than for \(^{11}\)Be without resonant final state interactions.

## Method

The authors construct a Halo EFT Lagrangian for a one-neutron halo nucleus and a low-lying resonance in the core-proton system, including strong interactions up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the expansion in \(R_{\text{core}}/R_{\text{halo}}\). They include the weak interaction current for neutron-to-proton decay, incorporating both one-body and two-body currents, and account for Coulomb interactions via a Sommerfeld factor. The decay rate is computed by integrating the squared matrix element over phase space, and the branching ratio is obtained by dividing by the total decay width from the known half-life.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{11}\)Be with a resonance at \(E_R = (171 \pm 20)\) keV (Ayyad 2022), the branching ratio is \(b_p = (5.7_{-2.9}^{+5.0}\text{(exp.)}_{-1.1}^{+4.1}\text{(theo.)}) \times 10^{-6}\), the resonance width is \(\Gamma_R = (6.2^{+3.8}_{-2.6}\text{(exp.)}^{+3.9}_{-1.4}\text{(theo.)})\) keV, and the Gamow-Teller strength is \(B_{\text{GT}} = 1.63\).
* For \(^{11}\)Be with a resonance at \(E_R = (196 \pm 20)\) keV (Ayyad 2019), the branching ratio is \(b_p = (2.3_{-1.3}^{+2.5}\text{(exp.)}_{-0.4}^{+1.8}\text{(theo.)}) \times 10^{-6}\), the resonance width is \(\Gamma_R = (11.3^{+6.9}_{-4.2}\text{(exp.)}^{+7.0}_{-2.7}\text{(theo.)})\) keV, and the Gamow-Teller strength is \(B_{\text{GT}} = 1.59\).
* For \(^{19}\)C without resonant final state interactions, the predicted branching ratio is \(b_p = (4.1 \pm 2.5) \times 10^{-14}\).
* The one-neutron separation energy of \(^{19}\)C is \(S_n = 0.577\) MeV.
* The maximum one-neutron separation energy for beta-delayed proton emission is \(S_n^{\text{max}} = m_n - m_p - m_e \approx 782\) keV.

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT framework developed by Hammer (2011) for halo nuclei to include weak decays into the proton-core continuum, building on the authors' previous analysis (Elkamhawy 2019) of the impact of a resonance on the branching ratio. The results for \(^{11}\)Be are compared to experimental measurements by Ayyad (2019, 2022) and the upper limit from Riisager (2020), and to theoretical calculations using the shell model embedded in the continuum (Okolowicz 2019) and the no-core shell model in the continuum (Atkinson 2022). The prediction for \(^{19}\)C provides a testable target for future experiments, as no resonances are currently known in its decay window.
