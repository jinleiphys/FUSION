# Quantum effects with an X-ray free electron laser

> A quantum kinetic equation coupled with Maxwell's equation is used to estimate the laser power required at an XFEL facility to expose intrinsically quantum effects in the process of QED vacuum decay via spontaneous pair production. A 9 TW-peak XFEL laser with photon energy 8.3 keV could be sufficient to initiate particle accumulation and the consequent formation of a plasma of spontaneously produced pairs. The evolution of the particle number in the plasma will exhibit non-Markovian aspects of the strong-field pair production process and the plasma's internal currents will generate an electric field whose interference with that of the laser leads to plasma oscillations.

## Key claim
A 9 TW-peak XFEL laser with photon energy 8.3 keV could be sufficient to initiate accumulation of spontaneously produced electron-positron pairs and the formation of a plasma, but this requires a peak power roughly nine times higher than that planned at the DESY and SLAC XFEL facilities, and the authors note their estimates are lower bounds because they assume an idealized standing-wave geometry that is impossible to achieve in practice.

## Method
The authors coupled a quantum Vlasov equation (kinetic equation) with Maxwell's equation to model electron-positron pair production in a spatially homogeneous, time-dependent electric field representing an idealized XFEL standing wave. They solved the coupled system numerically for a range of laser field strengths at a fixed wavelength of 0.15 nm, focusing on the first 100 laser periods (approximately 0.1% of the pulse duration) and neglecting collision terms to isolate quantum statistical and non-Markovian effects.

## Key numbers
- A laser with peak power P = 0.2 TW at wavelength λ = 0.4 nm produces a peak electric field E_a ≈ 1.2 × 10^16 V/m; boosting P to 1 TW and reducing λ to 0.1 nm yields E_g = 1.1 × 10^17 V/m.
- The critical Schwinger field is E_cr = m_e^2/e = 1.3 × 10^18 V/m; proposed XFEL facilities could generate E ≈ 0.1 E_cr.
- For E_0 = 0.5 E_cr at λ = 0.15 nm, the particle number density fit parameters are: a_0 = 1.2 × 10^(-11) fm^(-3), ρ = 5.4 × 10^(-12) fm^(-3) per laser period, ρ'/ρ = 0.0033 per period.
- Peak particle number N_σ at k = 50 laser periods: for E_0/E_cr = 0.1, N = 1.3 × 10^3; for 0.2, N = 5.4 × 10^3; for 0.3, N = 17.9 × 10^3; for 0.4, N = 148 × 10^3; for 0.5, N = 1110 × 10^3.
- The onset of significant particle accumulation occurs at E_0 ≈ 0.25 E_cr.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of QED vacuum decay via pair production in strong fields, notably the Schwinger formula (Schwinger, 1951) and time-dependent treatments by Brezin and Popov (1970) and by the authors' own prior work (Bastiprl, 2001; Kme, 2001; Sms, 2001). The paper shows that at field strengths achievable with a 9 TW-peak XFEL, the non-Markovian nature of pair production and the onset of plasma oscillations become observable, effects absent from the constant-field Schwinger formula. This enables experimental tests of non-equilibrium quantum field theory and motivates future inclusion of collision terms for quantitative predictions of plasma behavior.
