# On unifying the description of meson and baryon properties

> A Poincare' covariant Faddeev equation is presented, which enables the simultaneous prediction of meson and baryon observables using the leading-order in a truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations that can systematically be improved. The solution describes a nucleon's dressed-quark core. The evolution of the nucleon mass with current-quark mass is discussed. A nucleon-photon current, which can produce nucleon form factors with realistic Q^2-evolution, is described. Axial-vector diquark correlations lead to a neutron Dirac form factor that is negative, with r_1^{nu}>r_1^{nd}. The proton electric-magnetic form factor ratio falls with increasing Q^2.

## Key claim

A Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation built from a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder interaction can simultaneously predict meson and baryon observables, but the truncation omits meson cloud contributions and diquark confinement effects, so the calculated nucleon mass at the physical pion mass is 1.26(2) GeV, approximately 34% above the experimental value, with the expectation that systematic corrections will shift results into agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon, where the kernel is constructed from dressed-quark propagators obtained via the rainbow gap equation and diquark correlation amplitudes from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using only scalar (0+) and axial-vector (1+) diquark channels in the colour-antitriplet representation. The interaction is a one-parameter renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder model with current-quark-mass dependence, and the nucleon-photon current is constructed with a Ball-Chiu vertex augmented by a resonant rho-meson contribution and modified seagull terms to suppress diquark substructure at high momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass at physical pion mass (hat m = 6.1 MeV): M_N = 1.26(2) GeV, with corrections to rainbow-ladder truncation expected to reduce the mass by 320 MeV
- Ratio of calculated to experimental nucleon mass at physical pion mass: M_N / M_N^expt = 1.34; corresponding ratio for rho meson: m_rho / m_rho^expt = 1.28
- Scalar-axial-vector diquark mass difference at physical pion mass: m_av - m_sc = 0.27(3) GeV, compared to experimental Delta-N mass splitting M_Delta - M_N = 0.29 GeV
- Calculated neutron magnetic moment: mu_n = -1.58(3) nuclear magnetons; proton magnetic moment: mu_p = 2.56(5) nuclear magnetons
- Calculated neutron Dirac radii: r_1^nu = 0.85(2) fm, r_1^nd = 0.76(1) fm
- Quark-core contributions to proton magnetic moment: mu_p^u = 2.36(5), mu_p^d = 0.20(1)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to baryons by Eichmann et al. (2007) by introducing an improved current-quark-mass-dependent interaction kernel and a refined nucleon-photon current that suppresses diquark substructure at high Q^2. It enables the simultaneous calculation of meson and baryon properties within a systematically improvable truncation scheme, complementing phenomenological studies such as Hecht et al. (2002) and Cloet et al. (2008), and providing continuum predictions that can be compared with lattice-QCD results from Ali Khan et al. (2003) and Frigori et al. (2007).
