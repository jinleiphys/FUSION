# Quasi-particle random phase approximation with quasi-particle-vibration coupling: application to the Gamow-Teller response of the superfluid nucleus $^{120}$Sn

> We propose a self-consistent quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) plus quasi-particle-vibration coupling (QPVC) model with Skyrme interactions to describe the width and the line shape of giant resonances in open-shell nuclei, in which the effect of superfluidity should be taken into account in both the ground state and the excited states. We apply the new model to the Gamow-Teller resonance in the superfluid nucleus $^{120}$Sn, including both the isoscalar spin-triplet and the isovector spin-singlet pairing interactions. The strength distribution in $^{120}$Sn is well reproduced and the underlying microscopic mechanisms, related to QPVC and also to isoscalar pairing, are analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a self-consistent quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) plus quasi-particle-vibration coupling (QPVC) model that extends previous RPA+PVC approaches to superfluid nuclei, and demonstrates that the Gamow-Teller strength distribution in the benchmark nucleus \(^{120}\)Sn can be well reproduced only when both the QPVC mechanism and isoscalar spin-triplet pairing are included, with the subtraction method needed to remove double counting of static correlations.

## Method

The authors first perform a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation of the ground state, then solve the QRPA equations for the Gamow-Teller response using Skyrme interactions (SAMi, SGII, SkM*). The QRPA+QPVC model couples the QRPA two-quasi-particle configurations to doorway states consisting of a two-quasi-particle excitation combined with a collective phonon (natural parity \(L^\pi = 0^+, 1^-, 2^+, 3^-, 4^+, 5^-, 6^+\) with energy below 20 MeV and carrying more than 5% of the non-energy weighted sum rule). The spreading matrix elements are computed in the BCS quasi-particle basis, and the subtraction method is applied at \(E=0\) to remove static correlations.

## Key numbers

- The neutron pairing gap in \(^{120}\)Sn is taken as \(\Delta_n = 1.34\) MeV, used to determine the pairing strength.
- A configuration space cutoff of \(E_{cut} = 100\) MeV and a product \(|uv|\) threshold of \(10^{-3}\) are adopted for convergence of the QRPA and QRPA+QPVC calculations.
- With \(b_{cut} = 10^{-3}\) (strength cutoff for QRPA basis states), the QRPA+QPVC calculation reproduces 97% of the Ikeda sum rule when integrating strength up to 80 MeV excitation energy.
- For the SkM* interaction, the QRPA centroid energy \(m_1/m_0\) integrated up to 25 MeV is 13.9 MeV; QRPA+QPVC without subtraction gives 12.6 MeV; QRPA+QPVC with subtraction gives 13.8 MeV.
- The lowest \(2^+\) phonon energy in \(^{120}\)Sn is calculated as 1.420 MeV (SkM*), 1.941 MeV (SGII), and 2.708 MeV (SAMi), compared to the experimental value of 1.171 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA+PVC approach previously developed for magic nuclei (Niu 2012, Niu 2014) to superfluid open-shell nuclei by incorporating the HFB ground state and QRPA excitations. It builds on earlier studies of the Gamow-Teller response in \(^{120}\)Sn that demonstrated the importance of isoscalar pairing (Fujita 2014, Bai 2014, Sagawa 2016). The model enables future studies of exotic nuclei far from stability where pairing correlations and continuum effects become crucial, and provides a framework for improving predictions of \(\beta\)-decay half-lives and neutrino-nucleus reaction rates relevant to nuclear astrophysics.
