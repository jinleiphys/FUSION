# Conditional variational autoencoder inference of neutron star equation of state from astrophysical observations

> We present a new inference framework for neutron star astrophysics based on conditional variational autoencoders. Once trained, the generator block of the model reconstructs the neutron star equation of state from a given set of mass-radius observations. While the pressure of dense matter is the focus of the present study, the proposed model is flexible enough to accommodate the reconstructing of any other quantity related to dense matter equation of state. Our results show robust reconstructing performance of the model, allowing to make instantaneous inference from any given observation set.

## Key claim

The conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE) framework can reconstruct the neutron star equation of state (pressure versus baryonic density) from a single set of five mock mass-radius observations, but the reconstruction quality depends on whether the observation set includes massive neutron stars that constrain the high-density behavior; the model shows unbiased median reconstructions but only 74% of test-set equations of state are fully enclosed within the 95% credible interval across all density points.

## Method

The authors generated 39573 equations of state using five-segment piecewise polytropes with random transition densities and adiabatic indices, solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, and created mock observations by sampling five masses uniformly between 1.0 and the maximum mass, then perturbing both mass and radius with Gaussian noise of 0.1 solar masses and 0.1 km standard deviation. They trained a conditional variational autoencoder with a 6-dimensional latent space, a three-layer encoder (30, 15, 6 neurons) and a three-layer decoder (16, 18, 20 neurons) using the beta-VAE objective with beta=7, then used the trained decoder as a generative model by Monte Carlo sampling 2000 latent vectors to produce a mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distribution for the pressure at 20 fixed baryonic densities.

## Key numbers

- Training dataset: 39573 valid equations of state, split 90%/10% for training/validation
- Test set: 4398 equations of state, each with a single mock observation set (n_s=1)
- Mock observations: 5 mass-radius pairs per equation of state, with Gaussian uncertainties sigma_R = 0.1 km and sigma_M = 0.1 solar masses
- Latent space dimension: D = 6
- Beta parameter: beta = 7
- Monte Carlo samples for inference: L = 2000
- Residuals median across test set: approximately zero for all baryon densities
- Fraction of test-set equations of state fully enclosed within 95% credible interval: 74%
- Fraction with at most one density point outside the 95% credible interval: 92%

## Context

This work extends prior machine-learning approaches to neutron star equation of state inference, including Gaussian process representations (Essick 2019, Landry 2020), neural network inference (Ferreira 2019, Fujimoto 2017, Morawski 2020), and Bayesian neural networks (Carvalho 2023, Carvalho 2024). It differs from earlier variational autoencoder applications to neutron star equations of state (2023 ApJ 950 77H) by conditioning the reconstruction on a specific set of mass-radius observations, enabling instantaneous inference without Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling. The framework enables future inclusion of additional observables such as tidal deformability from gravitational wave detections and can be extended to reconstruct other thermodynamic quantities like sound velocity.
