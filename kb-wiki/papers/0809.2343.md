# Isospin dependence of reactions $^{48}$Ca+$^{243-251}$Bk

> The fusion process of $^{48}$Ca induced reactions is studied with the two-step model. In this model, the fusion process is devided into two stages: first, the sticking stage where projectile and target come to the touching point over the Coulomb barrier from infinite distance, and second, the formation stage where the di-nucleus formed with projectile and target evolve to form the spherical compound nucleus from the touching point. By the use of the statistical evaporation model, the residue cross sections for different neutron evaporation channels are analyzed. From the results, optimum reactions are given to synthesize $Z$ = 117 element with $^{48}$Ca induced reactions.

## Key claim

The two-step model, calibrated by fitting capture cross sections for neighboring systems and using a shell-correction reduction factor of 0.45, predicts that the optimum reaction to synthesize element Z=117 is \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{249}\)Bk, with a 3n evaporation residue cross section of 1.04 pb at a center-of-mass energy of 203.3 MeV; however, the absolute cross sections depend on the uncertain shell correction energy and its reduction factor.

## Method

The fusion process was divided into two stages: the approaching phase, where the projectile and target come to the touching point over the Coulomb barrier, and the formation phase, where the di-nucleus evolves to a spherical compound nucleus. The sticking probability in the approaching phase was calculated using an empirical formula from Swiatecki et al. (2005), with parameters re-fitted to capture cross sections of \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U, \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{244}\)Pu, and \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{248}\)Cm. The formation probability was obtained by solving two-dimensional Langevin equations for the evolution of the amalgamated system in the coordinates of relative distance and mass asymmetry. Residue cross sections were then computed using the HIVAP statistical evaporation code, with a shell correction reduction factor \(f = 0.45\) calibrated against measured 3n and 4n channels for \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{248}\)Cm.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{249}\)Bk, the predicted 3n evaporation residue cross section is 1.04 pb at \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 203.3\) MeV.
- For \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{247}\)Bk, the predicted 3n evaporation residue cross section is 0.75 pb at \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 204.4\) MeV.
- For \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{251}\)Bk, the maximum 3n residue cross section is 2.77 pb at \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 200.9\) MeV, but the target lifetime (0.9 h) is too short for practical experiments.
- The ratio of fusion cross sections between \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{243}\)Bk and \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{251}\)Bk at \(E^{*} = 20\) MeV is 5.85.
- The barrier shift \(\Delta B\) for \({}^{48}\)Ca + Bk was extrapolated to be 4.5 MeV.
- The shell correction reduction factor \(f\) was set to 0.45 to reproduce data for \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{248}\)Cm.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously developed by the authors (Shen et al., Abe et al., 2000, 2002) and applies it to predict optimum reactions for synthesizing element Z=117, which had not yet been observed at the time of publication. The model builds on the fusion hindrance mechanism proposed by Swiatecki (1981, 2005) and uses the HIVAP code of Reisdorf for statistical decay. The predictions enable experimental searches for Z=117, specifically recommending \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{249}\)Bk as the most practical target-projectile combination given the trade-off between cross section and target lifetime.
