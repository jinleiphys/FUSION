# Simple Scaling Laws for Energy Correlators in Nuclear Matter

> Collider experiments involving nuclei provide a direct means of studying exotic states of nuclear matter. Recent measurements of energy correlators in both proton-nucleus (p-A) and nucleus-nucleus (A-A) collisions reveal sizable modifications, attributable to nuclear effects, compared to proton-proton (p-p) collisions. Energy correlators, and their associated light-ray operator product expansion (OPE), allow scaling behaviors of the measured spectrum to be directly mapped to properties of the underlying quantum field theory. Here, we demonstrate for the first time how this mapping occurs in nuclear collisions, and highlight how the light-ray OPE characterizes leading nuclear effects. We show that the leading modification to the energy correlator distribution is characterized by an enhancement of the expectation value of twist-4 light-ray operators, resulting in a scaling for the ratio of the two-point correlator in nuclear matter to that in vacuum of $\sim 1+aθ^2$ up to quantum corrections. We verify that this leading twist-4 correction accurately describes recent A-A and p-A data, and is thus sufficient to capture the scaling behavior within the angular range measured for jet radii used in nuclear experiments. Our light-ray OPE based approach lays the groundwork for a rigorous characterization of nuclear modification to energy correlator observables.

## Key claim

The leading nuclear modification of the two-point energy correlator (EEC) in the perturbative small-angle regime is characterized by an enhancement of twist-4 light-ray operator expectation values, producing a scaling of the nuclear-to-vacuum ratio of the form ~1 + aθ², where a encodes medium properties; this leading twist-4 correction is sufficient to describe data within the angular range measured for jet radii used in nuclear experiments, but the authors note that the twist-4 operators are treated as classical (omitting their presently unknown anomalous dimensions) and that the analysis assumes the light-ray OPE converges for angles small enough that medium scales do not dominate the EEC entirely.

## Method

The authors apply the light-ray operator product expansion (OPE) to the two-point energy correlator in nuclear collisions, expanding the observable in the angular separation θ between detectors. They treat the leading vacuum scaling as governed by twist-2 light-ray operators (~1/θ²) and identify the leading nuclear modification as arising from enhanced twist-4 light-ray operators, which contribute a constant term in the ratio. For A-A collisions, they fit the twist-4 matrix element to the lowest pT bin of CMS Pb-Pb data (120-140 GeV, 0-10% centrality) and scale it to higher pT bins using classical dimensional analysis (pT⁻⁴); for p-A collisions, they include both the twist-4 term and logarithmic modifications from changes in quark/gluon fractions, fitting to ALICE p-Pb data.

- CMS Pb-Pb data at √sNN = 5.02 TeV for inclusive jets with reconstructed pT in bins 120-140 GeV, 140-160 GeV, and 160-180 GeV within 0-10% centrality are compared to the OPE fit (Figure 1).

- For p-A collisions, assuming an even quark/gluon fraction in p-p, the extracted approximate p-A quark/gluon fraction is 30/70, consistent with expectations from nuclear PDFs.

## Context

This work extends the light-ray OPE framework, previously successful in proton-proton collisions for precision calculations and the most precise extraction of αs from jet substructure (CMS 2024), to nuclear collisions for the first time. It builds on higher-twist factorization theorems for inclusive hadron production in nuclear matter (Luo 1993, Kastella 1989, Qiu 1991) and on recent energy correlator measurements in Pb-Pb (CMS 2024) and p-Pb (ALICE 2024) collisions. The approach enables systematic calculation of energy correlator modifications in nuclear matter by relating them to well-understood higher-twist matrix elements, and motivates future measurements of higher-point correlators and studies of twist-4 light-ray operator anomalous dimensions.
