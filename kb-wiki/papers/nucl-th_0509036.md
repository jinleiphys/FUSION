# The Semiclassical Coulomb Interaction

> The semiclassical Coulomb excitation interaction is at times expressed in the Lorentz gauge in terms of the electromagnetic fields and a contribution from the scalar electric potential. We point out that the potential term can make spurious contributions to excitation cross sections, especially when the the decay of excited states is taken into account. We show that, through an appropriate gauge transformation, the excitation interaction can be expressed in terms of the electromagnetic fields alone.

## Key claim

The semiclassical Coulomb excitation interaction expressed in the Lorentz gauge in terms of both electromagnetic fields and the scalar electric potential can produce spurious, unphysically large cross sections, especially when the decay of excited states is taken into account; the authors show that through an appropriate gauge transformation the interaction can be expressed in terms of the electromagnetic fields alone, yielding physically reasonable results.

## Method

The authors analyze the semiclassical Coulomb excitation interaction Hamiltonian, focusing on the longitudinal dipole excitation term that contains a long-range potential-like component. They derive the time evolution equations for ground-state and dipole-mode amplitudes including a finite width for the dipole resonance, then estimate the asymptotic occupation probability of the residual decay state using first-order perturbation theory. They perform coupled-channels calculations for the system 208Pb + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon and 10 GeV/nucleon, comparing results from the mixed (Lorentz gauge) representation and the pure field representation. Finally, they demonstrate a gauge transformation to the multipolar (Poincaré) gauge that eliminates the spurious potential term.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon with zero width: field representation gives 4.26 b, mixed representation gives 4.46 b
- For 208Pb + 208Pb at 10 GeV/nucleon with zero width: field representation gives 13.98 b, mixed representation gives 98.84 b
- For 208Pb + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon with Γ = 4 MeV: field representation gives 4.53 b, mixed representation gives 15.09 b
- For 208Pb + 208Pb at 10 GeV/nucleon with Γ = 4 MeV: field representation gives 13.73 b, mixed representation gives 334.06 b
- The production cross section of decay products from a dipole transition in the mixed representation grows as γ² (square of the Lorentz factor)
- The perturbative estimate of the decay product cross section is σ_dec ≈ 2πβγ (Γ V₀²/(ħc)³) (b_max − b_min)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the semiclassical Coulomb excitation interaction presented in Ber96 and refined in Esb02, which expressed the interaction in the Lorentz gauge in terms of fields and the scalar potential. The authors show that this mixed representation yields unphysical results at high energies, consistent with findings by Bayman and Zardi (Bay05) comparing Lorentz and Coulomb gauges, and by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown and Weneser (Bal91, Bal93, Bal93b) in studies of e⁺e⁻ production. The pure field representation (multipolar or Poincaré gauge) advocated here enables physically reasonable coupled-channels calculations incorporating finite resonance widths, which is essential for treatments of multiple Coulomb excitation with fluctuation contributions such as those of the Brink-Axel type (Car99a, Car99b, Car99c, Hus04).
