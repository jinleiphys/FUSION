# Infrared and Ultraviolet QCD dynamics with quark mass for J=0,1 mesons

> By using a previously developed phenomenological kernel for the study of the light quark QCD sector and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking effects we will examine the relative infrared and ultraviolet QCD dynamics for J=0,1 meson properties. For the same reasons we extend and explore a quark mass depended generalization of the kernel in the heavy quark region and we also compare with the original model. The relation between the dynamics of the quark propagator and the effective kernel with the J=0,1 QQ and qQ mesons and quarks Compton size is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that as the current quark mass increases, the infrared (IR) term of the Maris-Tandy (MT) model becomes progressively marginalized in determining meson observables, while the ultraviolet (UV) perturbative tail term becomes dominant; this transition is a consequence of the heavy quark mass suppressing the propagator amplitudes in the IR region, not a change in the effective kernel itself.

## Method

The author solves the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the quark propagator in the rainbow truncation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the ladder truncation, using the Maris-Tandy phenomenological kernel for the effective running coupling. The gap equation is solved in the complex plane for c and b quarks by changing the integration variable from quark internal momentum to gluon momentum, enabling access to the parabolic region required for BSE integration. Meson masses and decay constants for pseudoscalar and vector quarkonia (s\bar{s}, c\bar{c}, b\bar{b}) are computed using the full MT model, then compared with calculations retaining only the IR term or only the UV tail term of the kernel.

## Key numbers

- MT model parameters: ω = 0.4 GeV, D = 0.93 GeV², with current quark masses at renormalization scale μ = 19 GeV: m_u/d = 0.00374 GeV, m_s = 0.083 GeV, m_c = 0.88 GeV, m_b = 3.8 GeV
- Full MT model pseudoscalar meson masses: s\bar{s} = 0.696 GeV, c\bar{c} = 3.035 GeV, b\bar{b} = 9.585 GeV
- Full MT model pseudoscalar decay constants: s\bar{s} = 0.182 GeV, c\bar{c} = 0.387 GeV, b\bar{b} = 0.692 GeV
- Full MT model vector meson masses: s\bar{s} = 1.072 GeV, c\bar{c} = 3.235 GeV, b\bar{b} = 9.658 GeV
- Full MT model vector decay constants: s\bar{s} = 0.259 GeV, c\bar{c} = 0.415 GeV, b\bar{b} = 0.682 GeV
- For b\bar{b} pseudoscalar, using only the UV tail term gives mass 9.530 GeV (0.6% decrease from full) and decay constant 0.606 GeV (12.4% decrease); using only the IR term gives mass 8.106 GeV (15.4% decrease) and decay constant 0.172 GeV (75.1% decrease)
- For c\bar{c} pseudoscalar, using only the IR term gives mass 2.41 GeV (20.6% decrease) and decay constant 0.276 GeV (28.7% decrease); no bound state could be reached with the UV tail term only
- Approximate singularity locations for c quark propagator: full MT at (-2.7, ±3.5) GeV², IR term only at (-1.7, ±2.0) GeV², UV term only at (-1.9, 0.0) GeV²
- Approximate singularity locations for b quark propagator: full MT at (-24.0, ±12.0) GeV², IR term only at (-17.2, ±7.5) GeV², UV term only at (-23.0, 0.0) GeV²

## Context

This work extends the Maris-Tandy model (Maris 1999), previously applied successfully to light quark mesons, into the heavy quark region for c and b quarks, building on efforts by Maris (2006) and Krassnigg (2009). The paper systematically separates the IR and UV contributions of the MT kernel to quantify how heavy quark mass shifts the dominant dynamics from the IR-enhanced term to the perturbative UV tail, which enables a qualitative understanding of when constituent-like approximations for heavy quark propagators may be justified. The analysis of propagator singularity locations and the product of the kernel with propagator amplitudes provides a framework for interpreting the relative importance of IR versus UV QCD dynamics as a function of quark mass.
