# Partial conservation of the axial current and axial exchange currents in the nucleon

> We discuss the axial form factors of the nucleon within the context of the nonrelativistic chiral quark model. Partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) imposed at the quark operator level enforces an axial coupling for the constituent quarks which is smaller than unity. This leads to an axial coupling constant of the nucleon $g_A$ in good agreement with experiment. PCAC also requires the inclusion of axial exchange currents. Their effects on the axial form factors are analyzed. We find only small exchange current contributions to $g_A$, which is dominated by the one-body axial current. On the other hand, axial exchange currents give sizeable contributions to the axial radius of the nucleon $r_A^2$, and to the non-pole part of the induced pseudoscalar form factor $g_P$. For the latter, the confinement exchange current is the dominant term.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic chiral quark model can reproduce the experimental nucleon axial coupling constant $g_A$ when partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) is imposed at the quark operator level, which forces the constituent quark axial coupling $g_{Aq}$ to be approximately 0.774 rather than unity; axial exchange currents contribute only small corrections to $g_A$ but give sizeable contributions to the axial radius $r_A^2$ and the non-pole part of the induced pseudoscalar form factor $g_P$.

## Method

The authors constructed one-body and two-body axial current operators from Feynman diagrams within the nonrelativistic chiral quark model, including gluon, pion, sigma, and confinement exchange currents, and verified that these operators satisfy the PCAC condition to leading order when the quark-level Goldberger-Treiman relation $g_{Aq}=f_\pi g_{\pi q}/m_q$ holds. They then evaluated the axial form factors $g_A(q^2)$, $g_P^{non-pole}(q^2)$, and $g_T(q^2)$ using both unmixed harmonic oscillator wave functions and configuration-mixed wave functions (up to $N=2$ excitation quanta) for the three-quark system.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark axial coupling constant obtained from PCAC is $g_{Aq} = 0.774$, using $g_{\pi q}=2.62$, $f_\pi=92.4$ MeV, and $m_q=313$ MeV.
- The nucleon axial coupling constant from the one-body current with unmixed wave functions is $g_A(0) = 1.29$, compared to the experimental value $g_A(0)/g_V(0) = 1.2670(35)$.
- Exchange current contributions to $g_A(0)$ are small, giving a $3-7\%$ increase over the one-body result.
- The tensor form factor $g_T(q^2)$ is exactly zero for all $q^2$ in both impulse approximation and with exchange currents.
- The non-pole part of the induced pseudoscalar form factor at $q^2=0$ is related to the axial radius by $g_P^{non-pole}(0) \simeq -\frac{1}{6} g_A m_\pi^2 r_A^2$ (Adler-Dothan-Wolfenstein relation).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model approach of Buchmann et al. (1997) by imposing the PCAC constraint on axial operators, which had not been checked in that earlier study. It addresses the longstanding discrepancy between the NRQM prediction $g_A(0) = 5/3$ (assuming $g_{Aq}=1$) and experiment, previously approached via D-wave admixtures (Glashow, 1979), relativistic bag models (Tegen et al., 1983), and relativistic corrections (Danko, 1996). The result that $g_{Aq} \approx 3/4$ is consistent with independent determinations from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Vogl, 1991) and from pion-loop renormalization (Peris, 1994). The findings enable future studies of three-body axial currents and their effects on nucleon properties.
