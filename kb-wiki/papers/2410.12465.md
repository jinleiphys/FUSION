# Revisiting the proton-antiproton scattering using a constituent-quark-model based coupled-channels calculation

> Motivated by the last experimental and theoretical advances in the analysis of possible baryonium resonances, the $X(1835)$ and their partners, we perform a constituent-quark-model based coupled-channels calculation of the proton-antiproton scattering in order to analyze the possible existence of bound states or near-threshold structures. The used $N\overline N$ potential is derived from a $G$-parity transformation of the quark-model-based $NN$ interaction which has described well deuteron properties, $NN$ phase shifts, and even hadron-hadron phenomenology. The additional $N\overline N$ annihilation is taken into account by a complex phenomenological potential whose real part is generated by one-pion and one-gluon exchange annihilation potentials and its imaginary part is an energy-independent potential of Gaussian form. Then, all the parameters of the interaction are constrained by the $NN$ sector except those determining the imaginary part of the annihilation potential. Our study concludes that the nucleon-antinucleon dynamics is complex and rich in scattering singularities near the proton-antiproton and neutron-antineutron thresholds.

## Key claim

The authors perform a non-perturbative coupled-channels calculation of proton-antiproton scattering using a constituent-quark-model-based potential, concluding that the nucleon-antinucleon dynamics is complex and rich in scattering singularities near the proton-antiproton and neutron-antineutron thresholds, but caution that it is still premature to make assignments between their theoretical states and the experimentally observed signals such as the X(1835) and its partners.

## Method

The N̅N potential is derived from a G-parity transformation of a constituent-quark-model-based NN interaction that has successfully described deuteron properties and NN phase shifts. The N̅N annihilation is included via a complex phenomenological optical potential whose real part comes from one-pion and one-gluon exchange annihilation potentials and whose imaginary part is an energy-independent Gaussian potential; all parameters except those of the imaginary potential are constrained by the NN sector. The Resonating Group Method is used to extract the nucleon-level interaction from the underlying quark dynamics, and poles of the T-matrix are found by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation via matrix inversion.

## Key numbers

* Quark mass: m_q = 313 MeV (model parameter)
* Nucleon size parameter: b = 0.518 fm
* Mass difference between n̅n and p̅p thresholds: m_{n̅n} - m_{p̅p} = 2.59 MeV
* Without optical potential: bound state in J^{PC}=0^{++} (³P_0) with binding energy E_B = -0.1 MeV relative to p̅p threshold; bound state in J^{PC}=1^{--} (³S_1-³D_1) with E_B = -0.4 MeV
* Without optical potential: resonance in J^{PC}=2^{++} (³P_2-³F_2) with mass 1886.3 MeV and width 58.8 MeV
* With optical potential: J^{PC}=0^{++} pole at mass 1896.2 MeV and width 33.9 MeV in (F,F) Riemann sheet
* With optical potential: J^{PC}=1^{--} pole at mass 1896.2 MeV and width 8.1 MeV in (F,F) Riemann sheet
* Optical potential parameters: κ = -0.74 GeV^{-2}, ς = 0.49 fm

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of Entem (2006) by performing a non-perturbative coupled-channels analysis of proton-antiproton scattering, motivated by recent BESIII observations of structures near the p̅p threshold including X(1835), X(1840), X(1870), and X(1880). The quark-model-based approach differs from conventional one-boson-exchange N̅N potentials because the short-range repulsion in the NN sector comes from quark antisymmetry rather than ω-exchange, leading to different spin-orbit and tensor force behavior. The results enable future comparisons with experimental line shapes from BESIII decays such as J/ψ → γπ⁺π⁻η′ and J/ψ → γ3(π⁺π⁻), potentially explaining threshold enhancements through poles in non-physical Riemann sheets.
