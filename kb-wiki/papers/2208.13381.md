# QED theory of the nuclear recoil with finite size

> We investigate the modification of the transverse electromagnetic interaction between two point-like particles when one particle acquires a finite size. It is shown that the correct treatment of such interaction cannot be accomplished within the Breit approximation but should be addressed within the QED. The complete QED formula is derived for the finite-size nuclear recoil, exact in the coupling strength parameter $Z\,α$. Numerical calculations are carried out for a wide range of $Z$ and verified against the $(Z\,α)^5$ contribution. The comparison with the $Z\,α$ expansion identifies the contribution of order $(Z\,α)^6$, which is linear in the nuclear radius and numerically dominates over the lower-order $(Z\,α)^5$ term.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete QED formula for the finite-size nuclear recoil correction that is exact in the coupling strength parameter \(Z\alpha\), showing that previous treatments within the Breit approximation were incomplete and that the correct handling requires full QED.

## Method

The authors construct the photon propagator in the modified Coulomb gauge with a finite-size vertex, using the exponential model for the nuclear charge distribution with \(\lambda = 2\sqrt{3}/r_C\). They then evaluate the finite-size nuclear recoil correction for the \(1s\) state of hydrogen-like ions by taking the difference between the extended and point nuclear models in the all-order formula originally derived for point nuclei, performing numerical calculations after Wick rotation to imaginary energies.

## Key numbers

- For the \(1S\)-\(2S\) transition frequency in hydrogen, the finite-size nuclear recoil correction is \(-1.62\) kHz, compared to experimental uncertainty of \(0.01\) kHz in hydrogen and \(5.4\) kHz in antihydrogen.
- The higher-order \((\Za)^{5+}\) contribution for the \(1S\)-\(2S\) transition in hydrogen is \(-0.04\) kHz.
- The coefficient \(a^{(6)}\) for the \((\Za)^6\) contribution \(E^{(6)}_{\rm recfns} = -(m^3/M)\, a^{(6)} \,(\Za)^6\,r_C\) is approximately \(1.0\).
- For \(Z=1\) with \(r_C = 0.8409\) fm, the all-order result (multiplied by prefactor \((m^2/M)(\Za)^5/\pi\)) is \(-0.00419\), while previous incomplete treatment gave \(-0.00425\) (1.5% difference).
- For \(Z=92\) with \(r_C = 5.8571\) fm, the all-order result is \(-0.819\), while previous treatment gave \(-0.757\) (9% difference).
- The effective proton radius \(R_{pF} = 1.947\,(75)\) fm and neutron radius \(R_{nF} = 1.43\,(16)\) fm are extracted from Tomalak (2019).

## Context

This work extends the QED theory of nuclear recoil from point-like nuclei (Salpeter 1952; Shabaev 1985, 1988; Pachucki 1995; Yelkhovsky 1996) to finite-size nuclei, correcting incomplete Breit-approximation treatments by Borie and Rinker (1982) and Aleksandrov (2015). The results enable more accurate theoretical predictions for the bound-electron \(g\)-factor, improved spectra of muonic atoms, and non-perturbative treatment of vacuum-polarization combined with nuclear recoil in muonic atoms, as well as exact formulas for recoil effects on hyperfine splitting.
