# Radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter in dilute and dense media

> We extend the calculation of radiative corrections to momentum broadening in a QCD medium to include processes where single scattering dominates over multiple soft scatterings. Our analysis focusses on processes that are enhanced by a potentially large double logarithmic contribution. We demonstrate that such double logarithmic contributions emerge in calculations of momentum broadening in dilute systems where only single scattering occurs. We show how the different regimes, multiple versus single scattering, constrain different parts of the phase space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that double logarithmic radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter, previously found only in multiple scattering frameworks, also emerge in dilute systems where single scattering dominates, provided the proper phase space variables are identified and the formation time of the radiated gluon remains shorter than the medium length.

## Method

The authors calculate the radiative correction to transverse momentum broadening from an unmeasured soft gluon emission, using a mixed representation where propagators depend on light-cone time and energy. They evaluate the three-point correlator of in-medium propagators in the harmonic approximation, then extend the calculation to the single scattering regime by treating the measured transverse momentum of the hard particle as an independent variable rather than equating it to \(\hat q L\). The phase space boundaries are analyzed in terms of formation time \(\tau = \omega/k_\perp^2\) and transverse momentum \(k_\perp^2\).

## Key numbers

- The leading-order jet quenching parameter is given by \(\hat q(\p^2) = \frac{g^4 N_c n}{4\pi} \ln \frac{\p^2}{m_D^2}\), where \(m_D\) is the Debye screening mass.
- The radiative correction to the average transverse momentum squared is \(\langle \Delta p_\perp^2 \rangle \simeq \bar\alpha L \int \frac{d\omega}{\omega} \int \frac{d\tau}{\tau} \hat q\), with \(\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha N_c/\pi\).
- The double logarithmic phase space is bounded by \(\tau_{\text{min}} < \tau < \p^2/\hat q\), where \(\tau = \omega/k_\perp^2\) is the formation time and \(\p^2\) is the measured transverse momentum of the hard particle.
- The multiple scattering condition corresponds to \(\tau \lesssim \sqrt{\omega/\hat q}\) or equivalently \(\hat q \tau \lesssim k_\perp^2\).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of radiative corrections to momentum broadening by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013) and Blaizot, Dominguez, Iancu, and Mehtar-Tani (2013, 2014), which were set in a multiple scattering framework where the medium length is much larger than the mean free path. The paper clarifies that the double logarithmic enhancement is a universal feature of the interaction between probe and medium, and it addresses the observation by Iancu and Mueller (2014) and Mueller and Wu (2016) that the collinear divergence cutoff can be larger than \(\hat q L\). The results enable a consistent treatment of radiative corrections to \(\hat q\) across both dilute and dense regimes, supporting the interpretation of these corrections as a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter to leading logarithmic accuracy.
