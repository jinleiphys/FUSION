# The formation region of emitted $α$ and heavier particles inside radioactive nuclei

> We investigate the formation distance ($R_0$) from the center of the radioactive parent nucleus at which the emitted cluster is most probably formed. The calculations are microscopically performed starting from solving the time-independent Schrödinger wave equation for the $α$-core system, using nuclear potential based on the Skyrme-SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interaction and folding Coulomb potential, to determine the incident and transmitted wave functions of the system. Our results advocate that the emitted cluster is mostly formed in the pre-surface region of the nucleus, under the effect of Pauli blocking from the saturated core density. The deeper $α$-formation distance inside the nucleus gives rise to less preformation probability, and indicates more stable nucleus of longer half-life. Also, the $α$-particle tends to be formed at a bit deeper region inside the nuclei having larger isospin asymmetry and in the closed shell nuclei. Regarding the emitted nuclei heavier than $α$-particle, we find that the formation distance of the emitted clusters heavier than $α$-particle increases with increasing the isospin asymmetry of the formed cluster, rather than with increasing its mass number. The partial half-life of a certain cluster-decay mode increases upon increasing either the mass number or the isospin asymmetry of the emitted cluster.

## Key claim

The emitted cluster is most probably formed in the pre-surface region of the parent nucleus, at a distance from the center that is smaller than the proton and neutron half-density radii, under the effect of Pauli blocking from the saturated core density; the exact formation distance depends on the isospin asymmetry of the parent and the emitted cluster, not simply on the cluster mass number.

## Method

The authors solved the time-independent Schrödinger wave equation for the cluster-core dinuclear system using a nuclear potential derived from the Skyrme-SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interaction within the Hamiltonian energy-density formalism, combined with a folding Coulomb potential. The incident and transmitted radial wave functions were obtained numerically, and the formation distance \(R_0\) was iteratively determined from Eq. (11) by equating the integrated squared incident wave function from the origin to \(R_0\) with the preformation probability extracted from the experimental half-life, calculated penetration probability, and assault frequency.

## Key numbers

- Average \(\alpha\)-formation distance inside \(^{105-109}\)Te isotopes: about 4.87 fm, which is 0.47 fm smaller than the average proton half-density radius (5.34 fm) and 0.3 fm smaller than the average neutron half-density radius (5.18 fm).
- Average \(\alpha\)-formation distance inside \(^{186,190,191,194-202,204-208,210,212-216,218,219}\)Po isotopes: about 5.22 fm, which is 1.43 fm smaller than the average proton half-density radius (6.65 fm) and 1.51 fm smaller than the average neutron half-density radius (6.73 fm).
- Average \(\alpha\)-formation distance inside \(^{204,207,209,211,213-219}\)At isotopes: about 5.31 fm, which is 1.42 fm smaller than the average proton half-density radius (6.73 fm) and 1.54 fm smaller than the average neutron half-density radius (6.85 fm).
- For \(^{238}\)Pu decays: the \(\alpha\) cluster (isospin asymmetry \(I=0\)) forms at 6.12 fm; \(^{28}\)Si (\(I=0.067\)) at 6.15 fm; \(^{28}\)Mg (\(I=0.143\)) at 6.49 fm; \(^{30}\)Mg (\(I=0.2\)) at 6.53 fm.
- Branching ratios for \(^{238}\)Pu heavy-cluster decays: \(^{28,30}\)Mg at about \(6 \times 10^{-15}\%\) and \(^{32}\)Si at about \(1.4 \times 10^{-14}\%\).

## Context

This work extends microscopic cluster-decay studies that previously focused on preformation probabilities and half-lives (e.g., R-matrix calculations for \(^{212}\)Po by Ref. [ref_A10], multistep shell model calculations by Ref. [ref_A6,ref_A7]) by directly locating the formation region. The results confirm the qualitative picture from earlier work (Refs. [ref10,ref12,ref123,ch1_ref165]) that Pauli blocking pushes cluster formation to the pre-surface region. The finding that formation distance depends on isospin asymmetry rather than mass number enables more refined predictions of cluster preformation probabilities and half-lives for exotic decays, and the microscopically obtained preformation probabilities are shown to follow the phenomenological Blendowske-Walliser relation \(S_C = (S_{\alpha})^{(A_C-1)/3}\).
