# Centrality criteria of inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions

> A process of inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions with the impact parameter less than a radius of the nucleon quark core is considered. The criteria for selection of such collisions from a full array of the nucleon-nucleon interactions are suggested.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions with impact parameter less than the nucleon quark core radius (≈0.4 fm) can be identified experimentally by selecting events where both final-state nucleons form a joined pair (deuteron or S-wave diproton) emitted near 90° in the center-of-mass frame, and that this selection is valid only for collision energies in the range 2.9 GeV ≲ √s ≲ 7.5 GeV where a six-quark chiral constituent state can form.

## Method

The authors derive kinematic criteria for central NN collisions based on the formation of an intermediate six-quark bag (6q)* in the s-channel. They calculate the interaction region size r_int from the four-momentum transfer Q between initial nucleons and the final joined pair, requiring r_int < r_core ≈ 0.4 fm. The criteria are applied to reactions pp → {pp}_S + 𝔐 and pn → d + 𝔐, where 𝔐 is a meson system, with the joined pair emitted at 90° in the CMS.

## Key numbers

- Quark core radius: r_core ≈ 0.4 fm
- Geometrical probability of central collisions: k = 0.50 fm² / 40 mb = 0.125
- Threshold energy for nucleon core overlap: √s_min ≈ 2.4–2.9 GeV (from repulsive potential U_rep(0) ≈ 0.5–1 GeV)
- Maximum energy for chiral constituent quark regime: √s_max ≈ 7.5 GeV (from Λ_χSB = 1.2 GeV/c and m_q = 0.34 GeV)
- Quark momentum at threshold: p_q = 0.34 GeV/c (for √s = 2.9 GeV, m_q = 0.34 GeV)
- Ratio of elastic 90° cross section to central cross section: 0.2 at √s = 2.9 GeV; 5×10⁻⁷ at √s = 6 GeV
- Estimated differential cross section for deuteron emission at 90°: dσ_centr(90°)/dΩ_d ≈ 15 μb/sr
- Estimated event rate: ≈ 30 s⁻¹ (for Ω = 2 sr, L = 10³⁰ cm⁻²s⁻¹)

## Context

This work extends the Anderson-Collins estimation (1970s) that central pp collisions make up (34 ± 13)% of all inelastic collisions, but which did not establish a relation between centrality and impact parameter. The authors build on the constituent counting rule of Matveev (1973) and Brodsky (1973) for elastic scattering, and on theoretical evaluations of the repulsive NN potential by Maltman (1990s), Stancu (1990s), Bartz (2000s), and Ishii (2000s). The proposed criteria enable experimental searches for dibaryon resonances in the (6q)* system, as recently proposed by Komarov (2018), and could provide a new test-bench for non-perturbative QCD structure of nucleons.
