# Abnormal solutions of Bethe--Salpeter equation with massless and massive exchanges

> We summarize the main properties of the so called ''abnormal solutions'' of the Wick--Cutkosky model, i.e. two massive scalar particles interacting via massless scalar exchange ("photons"), within the Bethe--Salpeter equation. These solutions do not exist in the non-relativistic limit, in spite of having very small binding energies. They present a genuine many-body character dominated by photons, with a norm of the valence constituent wave function (two-body norm) that vanishes in the limit of zero binding energy. We present new results concerning the massive-exchange case, in particular determine under which conditions is it possible to obtain such peculiar solutions without spoiling the model by tachyonic states ($M^2<0$).

## Key claim

The paper argues that "abnormal solutions" of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, bound states with no non-relativistic counterpart, persist when the exchanged particle has a nonzero mass, provided the coupling constant is large enough, but only if the model is restricted to avoid tachyonic states (total mass squared \(M^2<0\)). The authors hedge that the existence of these states in the full quantum field theory remains an open question, as Wick himself doubted whether the ladder approximation can be trusted for such large couplings.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean space for two equal-mass scalar particles interacting via scalar exchange, using both the Wick-Cutkosky integral representation (for the massless case) and the Nakanishi two-dimensional integral representation (for the massive case). They compute the two-body norm \(N_2\) by projecting the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude onto the Light-Front plane, and they determine the coupling constant \(\lambda\) as a function of binding energy \(B\) for both normal and abnormal states, scanning for the onset of tachyonic solutions.

## Key numbers

* For the massless-exchange case, the abnormal states exist only for coupling constant \(\lambda \ge 1/4\) (i.e., \(\alpha \ge \pi/4\)), with the asymptotic value \(\lambda_{\text{min}} = 1/4\) reached very slowly as binding energy \(B \to 0\); for \(\kappa=2\) and \(B = 10^{-6} m\), \(\lambda\) is still a factor two larger than \(1/4\).
* The normal ground state (\(n=1\), \(\kappa=0\)) becomes tachyonic (\(M^2<0\)) for \(\lambda > 2\), so the coupling must be limited to \(\lambda \le \lambda_{\text{max}} = 2\) to avoid unphysical states.
* The first abnormal state (\(\kappa=2\)) crosses the line \(\lambda = \lambda_{\text{max}}\) at binding energy \(B \approx 0.00903 m\).
* For the massive-exchange case, a sample solution is shown for exchange mass \(\mu = 0.15 m\), binding energy \(B = 0.2 m\), and coupling constant \(\alpha = 2.10\).
* The two-body norm \(N_2\) of abnormal states tends to 0 in the limit \(B \to 0\), while for normal states it tends to 1.

## Context

This work extends the classic Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick, 1954; Cutkosky, 1954) for massless scalar exchange to the massive-exchange case, building on the authors' previous characterization of abnormal states (Carbonell et al., 2021). It addresses Wick's own skepticism about whether these states survive beyond the ladder approximation by showing they have a genuine many-body character (vanishing two-body norm), which the authors argue makes them plausible even with more complete kernels. The results enable future studies of whether abnormal states exist in more realistic theories, such as QED or Yukawa models, and provide a benchmark for testing non-ladder contributions.
