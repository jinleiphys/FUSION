# On the sigma sigma term

> We give some estimates for the light-quark mass dependence of the pole position of the sigma ($f_{0}(500)$) resonance in the complex energy plane, with the help of a chiral Lagrangian for the resonance field and some input from hadronic models constrained by Chiral Perturbation Theory and elastic unitarity. We also speculate on the fate of the sigma resonance when the quark masses become unphysically large.

## Key claim

The paper provides estimates for the light-quark mass dependence of the sigma (f0(500)) resonance pole position using a chiral Lagrangian for the resonance field, but the author emphasizes that the study has "exploratory character" because the large width of the sigma means the perturbative loop expansion may not converge, and the resonance Lagrangian is treated as a convenient parameterization rather than a fundamental description.

## Method

The author constructs a one-loop approximation to the sigma self-energy from a resonance chiral Lagrangian, similar to previous work on vector mesons (Bruns 2013). The free parameters in the self-energy expression are fixed by requiring consistency with a unitarized chiral perturbation theory model for pi-pi scattering in the chiral limit, which is constrained by two-loop ChPT results and elastic unitarity. The pole position of the sigma is then extracted by solving the equation (D_sigma)^{-1}(s_sigma) = 0 for the complex pole s_sigma, using random parameter sets to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Sigma pole mass in the chiral limit: m_sigma = (395 +/- 17) MeV
- Sigma pole width in the chiral limit: Gamma_sigma = (630 +/- 135) MeV
- Physical sigma pole mass (from Caprini 2005): m_sigma^phys = 441 (+16, -8) MeV
- Physical sigma pole width (from Caprini 2005): Gamma_sigma^phys = 544 (+18, -25) MeV
- Coupling in the chiral limit: (3 g_sigma^2)/(32 pi^2) = (0.886 +/- 0.170) GeV^{-2}, corresponding to |c_d^sigma| approximately 36 MeV
- Leading quark-mass coefficient for the mass: b_m = (1.95 +/- 1.65) GeV^{-1}, giving a "sigma sigma term" estimate of (38 +/- 32) MeV
- Leading quark-mass coefficient for the width: b_Gamma = (-6.70 +/- 6.95) GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends unitarized ChPT studies of the sigma resonance quark mass dependence (Hanhart 2008, Nebreda 2010, Pelaez 2010, Nebreda 2011) by adding a complementary resonance chiral Lagrangian viewpoint, aiming to reduce model dependence. The formalism is designed to be applied to precise lattice QCD data as a next step, and the author notes that the sigma may become a bound state near M_pi approximately 300 MeV, consistent with a recent lattice study (Briceno 2016).
