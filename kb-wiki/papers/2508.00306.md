# Nonlocal {\it in-medium} effective interaction for nucleon scattering off isospin-asymmetric targets

> We have investigated the role of isospin asymmetry of the $NN$ effective interaction in the context of $NA$ elastic scattering. To this purpose we represent the {\it in-medium} $g$ matrix as an admixture of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter solutions of Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations for infinite nuclear matter, denoted as $g[ρ,β]$. We use the Argonne $v_{18}$ bare potential to represent the $NN$ interaction in free space, due to its ability to describe the $NN$ scattering amplitudes up to 350 MeV. The density-dependent isospin-asymmetric $g$ matrices are then used to calculate optical model potentials for elastic nucleon scattering off closed-shell nuclei. For this aim, we make use of the Arellano-Bauge $δg$-folding approach suited for an explicit treatment of nonlocal density matrices. This approach allows to account for the local isospin-asymmetry and density dependence of the {\it in-medium} $NN$ interaction. The resulting optical potentials are nonlocal since the entire nonlocal structure of the $g$ matrix is retained. We observe that including the isospin asymmetry in the $g$ matrix allows for a reasonable description of differential cross-sections at nucleon beam energies between 40 and 200 MeV. In the case of proton scattering at energies below $\sim$65 MeV, at momentum transfers $q$ below $\sim$1 fm$^{-1}$, the inclusion of neutronic-matter $g$ matrices yields better agreement with the data as compared to the case when symmetric nuclear matter $g$ matrices are used. These results provide evidence that the isospin-asymmetry in the $NN$ effective interaction yield non-negligible effects in nucleon scattering off isospin-asymmetric targets at beam energies below 65 MeV.

## Key claim

The isospin asymmetry of the in-medium NN effective interaction produces non-negligible effects in nucleon scattering off isospin-asymmetric targets, but only at beam energies below approximately 65 MeV and at momentum transfers below approximately 1 fm^{-1}; at higher energies the effects are less significant.

## Method

The authors constructed an isospin-asymmetric in-medium g matrix by linearly interpolating between Brueckner-Hartree-Fock solutions for symmetric nuclear matter (beta=0) and pure neutron matter (beta=1), using the Argonne v18 bare NN potential. These density- and asymmetry-dependent g matrices were then used within the Arellano-Bauge delta-g folding approach to calculate nonlocal optical model potentials for elastic nucleon scattering off closed-shell nuclei (48Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb), retaining the full nonlocal structure of the g matrix. The resulting potentials were applied to compute differential cross sections for proton and neutron scattering at beam energies between 40 and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 40 to 200 MeV for nucleon scattering off closed-shell targets
- Energy threshold for isospin asymmetry effects: below approximately 65 MeV for proton scattering
- Momentum transfer threshold: below approximately 1 fm^{-1} for observable isospin asymmetry effects
- Argonne v18 potential describes NN scattering amplitudes up to 350 MeV
- Saturation Fermi momentum of symmetric nuclear matter: k_F = 1.55 fm^{-1}
- Slope parameter L = 56.67 MeV for the symmetry energy
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Root-mean-squared radii for 48Ca: 3.46 fm (protons) and 3.65 fm (neutrons)
- g matrices calculated at twenty values of k_F from 0 to 1.6 fm^{-1} for SNM and 0 to 1.9 fm^{-1} for PNM

## Context

This work extends the parameter-free optical model introduced by Arellano and Bauge (2007), which previously used only isospin-symmetric nuclear matter g matrices. It builds on earlier investigations of density and asymmetry dependence of g matrices within BHF approximation by Bombaci and Lombardo (1991), who found that single-particle potentials vary nearly linearly with isospin asymmetry beta. The approach enables microscopic optical potential calculations for exotic, neutron-rich nuclei relevant to radioactive ion beam facilities such as FRIB, GSI, and RIKEN, going beyond the isospin-symmetric approximations used in prior work.
