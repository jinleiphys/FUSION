# e^+e^- Pair Production from $γ$ A Reactions

> We present a calculation of e^+e^- production in $γA$ reactions at MAMI and TJNAF energies within a semi-classical BUU transport model. Dilepton invariant mass spectra for $γ$C, $γ$Ca and $γ$Pb are calculated at 0.8, 1.5 and 2.2 GeV. We focus on observable effects of medium modifications of the $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. The in-medium widths of these mesons are taken into account in a dynamical, consistent way. We discuss the transport theoretical treatment of broad resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that observable medium modifications of the ρ and ω mesons in photonuclear reactions can be seen in dilepton invariant mass spectra, but the actual hedge is that the collisional broadening treatment produces an unphysical divergence at the two-pion mass threshold due to the semi-classical approximation, and that the strong broadening of the ω peak is not realistic because most ω mesons contributing to the dilepton spectrum decay in vacuum.

## Method

The authors used a semi-classical BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model extended to include dilepton production, with baryonic resonances from the Manley and Saleski analysis and mesonic resonances treated analogously. The model incorporates in-medium widths of ρ and ω mesons in a dynamically consistent way through the collision term, using spectral functions for broad resonances rather than propagating them at their pole masses. Elementary γN cross sections were parameterized from experimental data for energies below 2.1 GeV and using the Lund string fragmentation model FRITIOF for higher energies.

## Key numbers

- Dilepton invariant mass spectra calculated for γC, γCa, and γPb at photon energies of 0.8, 1.5, and 2.2 GeV
- Collisional width of the ρ meson at nuclear matter density ρ₀ reaches up to about 600 MeV, corresponding to complete melting of the ρ meson
- Collisional width of the ω meson at nuclear matter density ρ₀ and momentum p=1 GeV is about 80 MeV, about a factor of 10 larger than the vacuum decay width
- Inclusion of collisional broadening leads to depletion of the ρ meson peak by about 30% with a shift of strength to lower dilepton masses
- Bethe-Heitler contribution is about a factor of 4 smaller than hadronic contributions in the ρ and ω meson region, and can be suppressed by a factor of 10 with cuts (k·p), (k·p₊) > 0.01 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' BUU transport model previously applied to heavy ion collisions at SIS energies and photoproduction of pions and etas (Effenberger et al., 1997; Teis et al., 1997) to dilepton production in photonuclear reactions. It provides predictions for experiments at MAMI and TJNAF (CEBAF) energies, complementing earlier work on dilepton production in pion nucleus reactions (Effenberger et al., 1998) for the HADES collaboration at GSI. The paper enables comparison of in-medium vector meson modifications under "cleaner" conditions than heavy ion collisions, where the nuclear medium is close to equilibrium at normal nuclear density and temperature zero, in contrast to the far-from-equilibrium conditions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion reactions studied by CERES and HELIOS collaborations.
