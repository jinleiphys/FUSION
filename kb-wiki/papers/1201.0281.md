# Examining a reduced jet-medium coupling in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Recent data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of jet fragments in 2.76 ATeV Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) indicate that the jet-medium coupling in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is reduced at LHC energies and not compatible with the coupling deduced from data at the Relativistic Hadron Collider (RHIC). We estimate the reduction factor from a combined fit to the available data on $R_{AA}(\sqrt{s},p_T,b)$ and the elliptic flow $v_2(\sqrt{s},p_T,b)$ at $\sqrt{s}=0.2,2.76$ ATeV over a transverse momentum range $p_T$ 10-100 GeV and a broad impact parameter, b, range. We use a simple analytic "polytrope" model ($dE/dx=- κE^{a} x^z T^{c}$) to investigate the dynamical jet-energy loss model dependence. Varying a=0-1 interpolates between weakly-coupled and strongly-coupled models of jet-energy dependence while z=0-2 covers a wide range of possible jet-path dependencies from elastic and radiative to holographic string mechanisms. Our fit to LHC data indicates an approximate 40% reduction of the coupling $κ$ from RHIC to LHC and excludes energy-loss models characterized by a jet-energy exponent with a>1/3. In particular, the rapid rise of $R_{AA}$ with $p_T$>10 GeV combined with the slow variation of the asymptotic $v_2(p_T)$ at the LHC rules out popular exponential geometric optics models (a=1). The LHC data are compatible with $0\leq a\leq 1/3$ pQCD-like energy-loss models where the jet-medium coupling is reduced by approximately 10% between RHIC and LHC.

# Examining a reduced jet-medium coupling in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

The jet-medium coupling in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is reduced at LHC energies compared to RHIC energies, but the reduction factor depends strongly on the assumed energy-loss model: pQCD-like models with jet-energy exponent a ≤ 1/3 require only an approximately 10% reduction in coupling between RHIC and LHC, while the data rule out models with a > 1/3, including the popular exponential geometric optics models (a=1) that had described RHIC data well.

## Method

The authors used a simple analytic "polytrope" model of jet energy loss, dE/dx = -κ E^a x^z T^c, where exponents a (jet-energy dependence), z (path-length dependence), and c (temperature dependence) are varied to interpolate between weakly-coupled pQCD and strongly-coupled AdS/CFT holographic models. They performed combined fits to the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 at both RHIC (√s = 0.2 ATeV) and LHC (√s = 2.76 ATeV) over a transverse momentum range p_T = 10-100 GeV and a broad impact parameter range, using both Glauber and deformed CGC-like (dcgc1.2) initial geometries, with full pQCD jet-production spectra and KKP pion fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of effective coupling κ at LHC to that at RHIC for the (a=1/3, z=1, c=8/3) polytrope with Glauber initial conditions and τ_0 = 1.0 fm/c is 0.71 (κ_LHC/κ_RHIC = 0.66/0.93)
- For the same polytrope with dcgc1.2 initial conditions, the LHC/RHIC coupling ratio is 0.61 (κ_LHC/κ_RHIC = 0.66/1.09)
- For the (a=0, z=1, c=3) polytrope with Glauber initial conditions, the LHC/RHIC coupling ratio is 0.82 (κ_LHC/κ_RHIC = 2.72/3.30)
- For the "Jia" optical limit (a=1, z=2, c=3) with dcgc1.2 initial conditions, the LHC/RHIC coupling ratio is 0.33 (κ_LHC/κ_RHIC = 0.017/0.057)
- The pQCD coupling at LHC is estimated as α_LHC ∼ 0.24-0.27 for the a=1/3 scenario, compared to α_RHIC ∼ 0.3
- For the a=0 scenario, α_LHC ∼ 0.28
- For the "Jia" optical limit, α_LHC ∼ 0.21
- The QGP density increases by a factor of approximately 2.2 from RHIC to LHC, with the temperature field scaled by a factor (2.2)^(1/3)
- The initial QGP-formation time assumed is τ_0 = 1 fm/c, with a freeze-out isotherm temperature T_f = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the finding of Horowitz (2011), Zakharov (2011), and Buzzatti (2011) that the jet-medium coupling at LHC is weaker than expected from fixed-coupling extrapolations from RHIC, demonstrating this result is robust across a much broader class of energy-loss models parameterized by the polytrope exponents (a,z,c). The paper rules out the "survival probability" geometric optics models (a=1) used by Jia (2010) and Adare (2010) that had successfully described RHIC data, and shows that LHC data favor pQCD-like models with 0 ≤ a ≤ 1/3. The results enable quantitative extraction of the running of the strong coupling over the temperature range from RHIC to LHC, and challenge non-conformal holographic models to explain the factor of 2-4 reduction in the t'Hooft coupling required by the data.
