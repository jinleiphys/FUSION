# Neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of ${}^{82}$Se in the shell model: beyond closure approximation

> We recently proposed a new method to calculate the standard nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay ($0νββ$) of ${}^{48}$Ca going beyond the closure approximation. Here we extend this analysis to the important case of ${}^{82}$Se, which was chosen as the base isotope for the upcoming SuperNEMO experiment. We demonstrate that using a mixed method that considers information from closure and non-closure approaches, one can get excellent convergence properties for the nuclear matrix elements, which allows one to avoid unmanageable computational costs. We show that in contrast with the closure approximation the mixed approach has a very weak dependence on the average closure energy. The matrix elements for the heavy neutrino-exchange mechanism that could contribute to the $0νββ$ decay of ${}^{82}$Se are also presented.

# Neutrinoless double-β decay of ⁸²Se in the shell model: beyond closure approximation

## Key claim

The mixed-method approach, which combines nonclosure calculations for low-lying intermediate states with closure approximation for higher states, yields nuclear matrix elements for the 0νββ decay of ⁸²Se that converge rapidly (within 30–50 states per spin-parity) and exhibit a very weak dependence on the average closure energy (variation of only 0.6% over 1–14 MeV), in contrast to the 15% variation seen in pure closure calculations.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the jj44 model space (⁵⁶Ni core with f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂, and g₉/₂ valence orbitals) using the JUN45 effective interaction fine-tuned for the ⁸²Se region. They computed one-body transition densities for the first 250 intermediate states of ⁸²Br for each J^π using the NUSHELLX code, then constructed running nonclosure, running closure, and mixed-method nuclear matrix elements for both light and heavy neutrino-exchange mechanisms, including short-range correlations from CD-Bonn and AV18 potentials.

## Key numbers

- Mixed-method total NME for light neutrino exchange with CD-Bonn SRC: M^{0ν}_{tot} = 3.377 (with GT = 2.983, F = -0.632, T = -0.011)
- Mixed-method total NME for light neutrino exchange with AV18 SRC: M^{0ν}_{tot} = 3.195
- Pure closure total NME with CD-Bonn SRC at ⟨E⟩ = 10.08 MeV: M^{0ν}_{tot} = 3.127, which is about 8% smaller than the mixed-method result
- Mixed NME varies by only 0.6% when average closure energy ⟨E⟩ is varied from 1 to 14 MeV, while pure closure NME varies by 15%
- Heavy neutrino-exchange pure closure NME with CD-Bonn SRC: M^{0ν}_N = 187 (GT = 150, F = -58.1, T = -0.55)
- Heavy neutrino-exchange pure closure NME with AV18 SRC: M^{0ν}_N = 129
- Number of intermediate states of ⁸²Br considered: 1.0 × 10⁷ including all spins and parities
- Ground-state energy difference parameter: (E_{g.s.}(⁸²Br) - E_{g.s.}(⁸²Se) + Q_{ββ}/2) = 1.595 MeV
- Oscillator length: b_{osc} = 2.143 fm; nuclear radius: R₀ = 5.213 fm
- Predicted light neutrino-exchange NME range: M^{0ν} = 3.3 ± 0.1
- Predicted heavy neutrino-exchange NME range: M^{0ν}_N = 158 ± 29

## Context

This work extends the beyond-closure method previously developed by Senkov and Horoi (2013) for ⁴⁸Ca to the case of ⁸²Se, which was chosen as the base isotope for the upcoming SuperNEMO experiment. The results are compared with five other approaches: interacting shell model (ISM) calculations by Menendez et al., QRPA calculations from the Tubingen-Bratislava-Caltech group and the Jyvaskyla group, interacting boson model (IBM-2) calculations, and generator coordinate method (EDF) calculations. The mixed-method approach enables accurate shell-model NME calculations for nuclei where full nonclosure summation is computationally prohibitive, and the authors suggest it can be successfully applied to ⁷⁶Ge, which requires about 1.5 × 10⁸ intermediate states in ⁷⁶As.
