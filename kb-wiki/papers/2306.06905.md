# Correlation of density fluctuation in a magnetized QCD matter near the critical end point

> The dynamical correlation of density fluctuation in quark gluon plasma with a critical end point has been investigated within the scope of the Müller-Israel-Stewart theory in the presence of static ultra-high external magnetic field. The dynamic structure factor of the density fluctuation exhibits three Lorentzian peaks in absence of external magnetic field- a central Rayleigh peak and two Brillouin peaks situated symmetrically on the opposite sides of the Rayleigh peak. The spectral structure displays five peaks in presence of the magnetic field due to the coupling of the magnetic field with the hydrodynamic fields in second-order hydrodynamics. The emergence of the extra peaks is due to the asymmetry in the pressure gradient caused by the external magnetic field in the system. Interestingly, it is observed that near the critical end point, all the Brillouin peaks disappear irrespective of the presence or absence of the external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The dynamic structure factor of density fluctuations in a magnetized QCD matter near the critical end point (CEP) exhibits five Lorentzian peaks in the presence of a static ultra-high external magnetic field, rather than the three peaks seen without a field; however, near the CEP all Brillouin peaks disappear regardless of whether the magnetic field is present.

## Method

The authors used linearized Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) second-order relativistic magnetohydrodynamics to derive the density-density correlation function (dynamic structure factor) for a non-expanding, baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma in a static external magnetic field. They solved the linearized hydrodynamic equations in Fourier-Laplace space, assuming no correlations between density fluctuations and other fluctuating hydrodynamic variables, and extracted the spectral structure of the density correlator.

## Key numbers

- The transient magnetic field in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can be as high as ~10¹⁷–10¹⁸ Gauss.
- The dynamic structure factor exhibits three Lorentzian peaks (one central Rayleigh peak and two symmetric Brillouin peaks) in the absence of an external magnetic field.
- The spectral structure displays five peaks in the presence of the magnetic field, with the extra peaks arising from asymmetry in the pressure gradient caused by the magnetic field.
- Near the critical end point, all Brillouin peaks disappear irrespective of the presence or absence of the external magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of density fluctuation correlations in QCD matter using relativistic hydrodynamics without magnetic fields (Minami 2009; Hasanujjaman 2020) by incorporating a static ultra-high magnetic field within the MIS framework. It also builds on relativistic magnetohydrodynamics formulations (Huang 2009; Huang 2015) and second-order hydrodynamics (Israel 1976; Israel 1979). The results enable future investigations of the speed of sound and transport coefficients in magnetized QCD matter, and may inform experimental searches for the QCD critical end point in heavy-ion collisions.
