# Phenomenological aspects of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon

> Using an isobar model which can reproduce the existing experimental data of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon we investigate some related phenomenological aspects, i.e. the hadronic form factors, contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon, and missing nucleon resonances. By reggeizing the appropriate propagators we extend the model to the higher photon energy regime.

## Key claim

Using an isobar model that can reproduce existing experimental data, the authors investigate hadronic form factors, contributions of kaon-hyperon final states to the nucleon's anomalous magnetic moment, and missing nucleon resonances; the model is extended to higher photon energies by reggeizing the appropriate propagators, but the reggeization study is presented as only a qualitative investigation of effects, with a full proper treatment still underway.

## Method

The authors constructed an isobar model including the S11(1650), P11(1710), and P13(1720) resonances, which have significant decay widths into KΛ and KΣ channels, and fitted free parameters (coupling constants) to elementary kaon photoproduction data. They implemented gauge-invariant hadronic form factors using Haberzettl's prescription, which was found superior to Ohta's method for accommodating coupling constants near SU(3) predictions. For the high-energy extension, they multiplied the Feynman propagators of the K*(892) and K1(1270) resonances with a Regge propagator factor that reduces to the Feynman propagator in the limit t → m_K*^2.

## Key numbers

- Contribution of kaon-hyperon final states to the squared anomalous magnetic moment of the proton: κ_p²(K) = -0.063 (using Eq. 2) and 0.105 (using Eq. 3); for the neutron: κ_n²(K) = 0.031 (Eq. 2) and 0.191 (Eq. 3)
- Ratio of kaon-hyperon contribution to total anomalous magnetic moment: |κ_p(K)|/κ_p ≤ 0.14 and κ_n(K)/κ_n ≤ 0.094
- Experimental values for comparison: κ_p² = 3.214 and κ_n² = 3.660
- The GDH integral was evaluated up to ν_max = 2.2 GeV
- The new SAPHIR data for p(γ, K⁺)Λ show a structure around W = 1900 MeV, which the model reproduces by including a D13(1960) resonance

## Context

This work extends previous isobar model analyses of kaon photoproduction by addressing the long-standing problem of hadronic form factors and gauge invariance, building on the prescriptions of Ohta (1989) and Haberzettl (1997). The investigation of missing nucleon resonances follows quark model predictions by Capstick and Roberts (1998), who suggested that many unobserved resonances may couple strongly to KΛ and KΣ channels. The reggeization approach, following Guidal et al. (1997), enables extension of the model to higher photon energies where standard isobar models diverge, which is necessary for exploring higher-lying nucleon resonances and accounting for higher-energy contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule integral.
