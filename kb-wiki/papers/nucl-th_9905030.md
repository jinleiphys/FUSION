# Quaiselastic scattering from relativistic bound nucleons: Transverse-Longitudinal response

> Predictions for electron induced proton knockout from the $p_{1/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ shells in $^{16}$O are presented using various approximations for the relativistic nucleonic current. Results for the differential cross section, transverse-longitudinal response ($R_{TL}$) and left-right asymmetry $A_{TL}$ are compared at $|Q^2|=0.8$ (GeV/c)$^2$ corresponding to TJNAF experiment 89-003. We show that there are important dynamical and kinematical relativistic effects which can be tested by experiment.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the transverse-longitudinal response \(R_{TL}\) and left-right asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) in quasielastic \((e,e'p)\) knockout from \(^{16}\)O exhibit important relativistic effects of both kinematical and dynamical origin, with the dynamical enhancement of the lower (negative-energy) components of the relativistic nucleon wave functions being particularly visible for the \(p_{1/2}\) shell and for missing momenta above 300 MeV/c, and with preliminary data from TJNAF experiment 89-003 appearing to favor the fully relativistic calculations over approximations that neglect these components.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for proton knockout from the \(p_{1/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) shells of \(^{16}\)O, using Dirac equation solutions for bound nucleons (from the NLSH relativistic mean-field parameter set) and for the outgoing proton (from energy-dependent global optical potentials). They computed the differential cross section, the transverse-longitudinal response \(R_{TL}\), and the left-right asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) using the \(cc1\) and \(cc2\) forms of the relativistic nucleon current operator, and compared these to results obtained by projecting out the negative-energy components of the wave functions, by using asymptotic projection, and by various nonrelativistic approximations including a "relativized" current with and without a spin-orbit correction to the charge density.

## Key numbers

* The calculations are performed at \(|Q^2| = 0.8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), with beam energy 2445 MeV, \(|\vec{q}| \simeq 1\) GeV/c, \(\omega \simeq 439\) MeV, and outgoing proton kinetic energy about 427 MeV, corresponding to quasielastic kinematics and the conditions of TJNAF experiment 89-003.
* A spectroscopic factor \(S_\alpha = 0.7\) is used, as previously obtained for \(^{208}\)Pb in RDWIA analyses.
* The effect of omitting the Darwin term in nonrelativistic calculations reduces the spectroscopic factor by 15–20%.
* The differential cross sections for \(|p_m| < 300\) MeV/c are similar across approximations, but show substantial dependence on negative-energy components for \(|p_m| > 300\) MeV/c for both \(p_{1/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) shells.
* The asymptotic projection method gives results for \(A_{TL}\) that deviate from the fully relativistic calculation beyond \(p_m \simeq 200\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) studies (Caballero et al., 1998) by including full distorted waves for the outgoing proton, and addresses a controversy in which standard nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations (Boffi et al., 1993) showed large deviations from measured \(R_{TL}\) data in some cases (Spaltro et al., 1993) but not others (Chinitz et al., 1991). The results enable more stringent tests of relativistic dynamics using high-precision data from TJNAF and other facilities, particularly in the missing momentum range \(200 \le p_m \le 400\) MeV/c, and provide a benchmark for "relativized" nonrelativistic approaches such as those of Amaro et al. (1996) and Jessup et al. (1998).
