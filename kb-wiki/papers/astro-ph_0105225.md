# Neutrino Pair Emission from Cooper Pair Breaking and Recombination in Superfluid Quark Matter

> For the low energy Standard Model neutrino-matter interactions, we calculate neutrino pair ($ν\barν$) emissivites in superfluid quark matter. Just below the critical temperature, Cooper pairs continuously break and recombine, resulting in the emission of $ν\bar ν$ pairs with a rate that greatly exceeds the standard quark modified Urca and bremsstrahlung rates. At the same baryon density in baryonic and quark matter, the ratio of baryon to quark $ν\barν$ emissivites lies in the range 2-5 for the densities of interest in the long-term cooling of solar mass compact stars. We also find that in matter containing hyperons, $ν\barν$ emission can occur with hyperons of all species.

# Neutrino Pair Emission from Cooper Pair Breaking and Recombination in Superfluid Quark Matter

## Key claim

Just below the critical temperature, Cooper pair breaking and recombination in superfluid quark matter produces neutrino-antineutrino pairs at a rate that greatly exceeds the standard quark modified Urca and bremsstrahlung rates, but only under the condition that the temperature is sufficiently close to the critical temperature such that the superfluid gap is small compared to the chemical potential (Δ ≪ μ). The ratio of baryon to quark neutrino-antineutrino emissivities lies in the range 2-5 for densities relevant to long-term cooling of solar mass compact stars.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutrino pair emissivity from Cooper pair breaking and recombination in superfluid quark matter using the Standard Model neutral-current weak interaction Hamiltonian with vector and axial-vector couplings. They perform a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to describe quasiparticle excitations in isotropic ¹S₀ pairing, compute the squared matrix elements for vector, axial, and mixed channels, and integrate over phase space assuming Δ ≪ μ. The calculation is performed for massless quarks and then extended to massive quarks in limiting cases of m/p_F ≪ 1 and p_F/m ≪ 1.

## Key numbers

- Emissivity for massless quarks: ε_q^{νν̄} ≅ 1.4 × 10²⁰ N_ν T₉⁷ F a_q (n_B/n₀)^{2/3} erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹, where T₉ is temperature in 10⁹ K, n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear matter equilibrium density, and a_q = c_V²[1 + (1/7)(c_A/c_V)²]

- Quark bremsstrahlung emissivity: ε_q^{brems} ≃ 3 × 10¹⁹ N_ν T₉⁸ (n_B/n₀)^{1/3} erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹

- Baryon PBF emissivity: ε_B^{νν̄} ≅ 1.65 × 10²¹ N_ν T₉⁷ (n_B/n₀)^{1/3} (Σ_i F_i a_i Y_i^{1/3} m_i*/m_i) erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹

- Ratio ε_B^{νν̄}/ε_q^{νν̄} lies in the range 2-5 for densities of interest in solar mass compact stars

- Superfluid gap near T_c: Δ(T) = 3.06 k_B T_c (1 - T/T_c)^{1/2} for T_c - T ≪ T_c, with Δ(0) = 1.76 k_B T_c

- For ³P₂ pairing in neutrons: Δ(T) = 1.98 k_B T_c (1 - T/T_c)^{1/2} for T_c - T ≪ T_c

## Context

This work extends the pair breaking and formation (PBF) process, originally calculated for neutron superfluidity by Flowers, Ruderman, and Sutherland (1976), to superfluid quark matter likely present in neutron star interiors. The authors compare their results to the quark modified Urca and bremsstrahlung processes calculated by Iwamoto (1980) and to baryonic PBF results from Yakovlev and collaborators. The calculation enables inclusion of quark matter PBF processes in long-term cooling simulations of neutron stars, which previously only considered baryon superfluidity. The authors note that strong and electromagnetic correlations, as recently studied by Leinson for proton superfluidity, may substantially enhance the emissivity in quark matter as well.
