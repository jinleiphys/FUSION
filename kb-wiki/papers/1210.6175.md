# Mass dependence of short-range correlations in nuclei and the EMC effect

> An approximate method to quantify the mass dependence of the number of two-nucleon (2N) short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei is suggested. The proposed method relies on the concept of the "local nuclear character" of the SRC. We quantify the SRC and its mass dependence by computing the number of independent-particle model (IPM) nucleon pairs in a zero relative orbital momentum state. We find that the relative probability per nucleon for 2N SRC follows a power law as a function of the mass number $A$. The predictions are connected to measurements which provide access to the mass dependence of SRC. First, the ratio of the inclusive inelastic electron scattering cross sections of nuclei to $^{2}$H at large values of the Bjorken variable. Second, the EMC effect, for which we find a linear relationship between its magnitude and the predicted number of SRC-prone pairs.

## Key claim

The authors propose an approximate, model-independent method to quantify the mass dependence of two-nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei, finding that the relative probability per nucleon for 2N SRC follows a power law as a function of mass number A, and that the magnitude of the EMC effect has a linear relationship with the predicted number of SRC-prone pairs. The hedge is that the method relies on the concept of "local nuclear character" of SRC and uses the number of independent-particle model (IPM) nucleon pairs in a zero relative orbital momentum state as a proxy for SRC-prone pairs.

## Method

The authors compute the number of two-nucleon pairs in a zero relative orbital momentum state (n12=0, l12=0) using harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions with Moshinsky brackets for coordinate transformation. They count pp, nn, and pn pairs (with spin projection for pn pairs to discriminate triplet and singlet states) for 11 nuclei from 4He to 197Au, then relate these counts to experimental observables: the a2(A/D) scaling factor from inclusive electron scattering at 1.5 ≤ xB ≤ 2, and the slope of the EMC effect -dR/dxB in the region 0.3 ≤ xB ≤ 0.7.

## Key numbers

- The pn (n12=0, l12=0, S=1) SRC-prone pairs scale as A^(1.35 ± 0.03)
- The fitted linear relation between EMC effect magnitude and per nucleon SRC probability: -dR/dxB = (0.11 ± 0.03) + (0.036 ± 0.005) · (2/A)(Npn+Npp+Nnn)
- The fitted linear relation between effective mass ratio η=m/m* and per nucleon SRC probability: η = (1.0012 ± 0.0006) + (0.0033 ± 0.0001) · (2/A)(Npn+Npp+Nnn)
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used: ħω (MeV) = 45 A^(-1/3) - 25 A^(-2/3)
- The c.m. correction factor for a2 predictions: 1.7 ± 0.3, with little mass dependence

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Frankfurt (1988), Ciofi degli Atti (1995), and Janssen (2000) on SRC in nuclei, and connects to experimental measurements of a2(A/D) scaling factors from PhysRevLett.96.082501 and PhysRevLett.108.092502. The linear relationship found between SRC-prone pairs and the EMC effect builds on the empirical correlation reported by Weinstein (2010) between the EMC slope and a2(A/D). The results enable a systematic, parameter-free method to predict SRC probabilities across the nuclear chart and suggest that the EMC effect may be driven by local nuclear dynamics related to SRC.
