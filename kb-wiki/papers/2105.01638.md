# The impact of nuclear deformation on relativistic heavy-ion collisions: assessing consistency in nuclear physics across energy scales

> In the hydrodynamic framework of heavy-ion collisions, elliptic flow, $v_2$, is sensitive to the quadrupole deformation, $β$, of the colliding ions. This enables one to test whether the established knowledge on the low-energy structure of nuclei is consistent with collider data from high-energy experiments. We derive a formula based on generic scaling laws of hydrodynamics to relate the difference in $v_2$ measured between collision systems that are close in size to the value of $β$ of the respective species. We validate our formula in simulations of 238U+238U and 197Au+197Au collisions at top Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy, and subsequently apply it to experimental data. Using the deformation of 238U from low-energy experiments, we find that RHIC $v_2$ data implies $0.16 \lesssim |β| \lesssim 0.20$ for 197Au nuclei, i.e., significantly more deformed than reported in the literature, posing an interesting puzzle in nuclear phenomenology.

# The impact of nuclear deformation on relativistic heavy-ion collisions: assessing consistency in nuclear physics across energy scales

## Key claim

The authors derive a formula relating elliptic flow differences between collision systems close in size to the quadrupole deformation parameter β, and find that RHIC v₂ data implies |β| ≈ 0.16–0.20 for ¹⁹⁷Au nuclei, which is significantly more deformed than the 0.10–0.14 range reported in low-energy nuclear structure literature, indicating a potential inconsistency between nuclear physics across energy scales.

## Method

The authors derive a linear relation between the mean-squared elliptic flow v₂{2}² and β² based on generic scaling laws of hydrodynamics, then validate it using AMPT simulations of ²³⁸U+²³⁸U and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at top RHIC energy (√sNN = 193 GeV and 200 GeV respectively). They apply this relation to experimental v₂ data from the STAR Collaboration in the 0–1% most central collisions, using hydrodynamic expectations for the coefficients r_b, r_a, and r_U to extract β for ¹⁹⁷Au given the known β for ²³⁸U from low-energy measurements.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸U, low-energy experimental determinations give β = 0.286 (Raman 2001), while theoretical calculations give β = 0.29 (Delaroche 2009) and β = 0.292 (Bender 2005); the authors adopt 0.28 < β_U < 0.29
- For ¹⁹⁷Au, low-energy calculations (5DCH) give 0.10 < |β_Au| < 0.14
- The ratio of mean-squared v₂ between 0–1% central ²³⁸U+²³⁸U and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions from STAR data is r_{v₂²} = 1.49 ± 0.05
- The ratio r_U = a_U/b_U is 25.6 in the 0–1% centrality bin from AMPT simulations
- Using β_U ≈ 0.29, the v₂ data implies 0.16 ≲ |β_Au| ≲ 0.20 for ¹⁹⁷Au nuclei
- The ratio of mean-squared v₂ between 0–1% ¹²⁹Xe+¹²⁹Xe and ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions from ALICE is r_{v₂²} ≈ 2.56, leading to β_Xe ≈ 0.24 via the same method

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework for heavy-ion collisions (Bernhard 2019, Gardim 2019, Nijs 2020, Everett 2020) to test consistency between low-energy nuclear structure measurements and high-energy collider data. It builds on previous studies showing that elliptic flow v₂ is sensitive to nuclear quadrupole deformation (Heinz 2004, Filip 2007, Giacalone 2018) and on STAR measurements of v₂ in ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions (Adamczyk 2015). The results pose an interesting puzzle for nuclear phenomenology and suggest that systematic comparisons across more collision systems, such as ¹⁴⁴Sm, ¹⁴⁸Sm, and ¹⁵⁴Sm isotopes, could further test whether the discrepancy between low-energy and high-energy data is systematic.
