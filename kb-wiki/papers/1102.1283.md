# Core-crust transition in neutron stars: predictivity of density developments

> The possibility to draw links between the isospin properties of nuclei and the structure of compact stars is a stimulating perspective. In order to pursue this objective on a sound basis, the correlations from which such links can be deduced have to be carefully checked against model dependence. Using a variety of nuclear effective models and a microscopic approach, we study the relation between the predictions of a given model and those of a Taylor density development of the corresponding equation of state: this establishes to what extent a limited set of phenomenological constraints can determine the core-crust transition properties. From a correlation analysis we show that a) the transition density $ρ_t$ is mainly correlated with the symmetry energy slope $L$, b) the proton fraction $Y_{p,t}$ with the symmetry energy and symmetry energy slope $(J,L)$ defined at saturation density, or, even better, with the same quantities defined at $ρ=0.1$ fm$^{-3}$, and c) the transition pressure $P_t$ with the symmetry energy slope and curvature $(J,K_{\rm sym})$ defined at $ρ=0.1$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the core-crust transition properties in neutron stars can be reliably predicted from a limited set of symmetry-energy coefficients defined at subsaturation density (0.1 fm⁻³), rather than at saturation density, but this predictability depends on the specific transition property and the choice of coefficients, with model dispersion remaining significant for some quantities.

## Method

The authors used 35 nuclear effective models (Skyrme and relativistic) plus a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach to calculate the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter. They constructed a generalized liquid-drop model (GLDM) as a Taylor series expansion of the EOS around a reference density (either saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ or ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³) and compared the core-crust transition properties (density ρ_t, proton fraction Y_{p,t}, pressure P_t) predicted by the GLDM with those from the complete functionals. The transition was defined as the crossing between β-equilibrium and the thermodynamic spinodal border, with additional analysis of the dynamic spinodal border.

## Key numbers

- Transition density ρ_t ranges from 0.061 to 0.097 fm⁻³ across all models (thermodynamic transition)
- Transition proton fraction Y_{p,t} ranges from 0.009 to 0.042 across all models
- Transition pressure P_t ranges from 0.144 to 0.689 MeV fm⁻³ across all models
- Symmetry energy at saturation J = S(ρ₀) = 33 ± 3 MeV (quoted from literature as well-known value)
- Symmetry energy at ρ = 0.11 fm⁻³ converges to S(0.11 fm⁻³) = 24 ± 4 MeV across models
- Symmetry energy slope L at saturation ranges from 32.39 to 129.34 MeV across the models in Table I
- Symmetry energy slope L at ρ = 0.06 fm⁻³ shows a sharp minimum in variance across models
- Second-order GLDM around ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³ (D₂(0.1)) gives nearly as good predictions as the infinite-order development D∞

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ducoin et al., letter-corecrust) which found that ρ_t and Y_{p,t} correlate unambiguously with the symmetry energy slope L, while the link between L and P_t is very model-dependent. The paper addresses this limitation by investigating correlations with additional symmetry-energy coefficients (J, K_sym, Q_sym) and by testing whether a Taylor expansion around subsaturation density (0.1 fm⁻³) can improve predictions. The results enable future work to connect experimental constraints on symmetry energy from nuclear observables (neutron-skin thickness, isovector resonances, isospin diffusion) directly to neutron star crust properties, particularly the crust moment of inertia relevant for pulsar glitches (Link 1999, Lattimer 2000).
