# Impact of the Delta (1232) resonance on neutral pion photoproduction in chiral perturbation theory

> We present an ongoing project to assess the importance of D-waves and the $Δ(1232)$ resonance for descriptions of neutral pion photoproduction in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. This research has been motivated by data published by the A2 and CB-TAPS collaborations at MAMI [1]. This data has reached unprecedented levels of accuracy from threshold through to the $Δ$ resonance. Accompanying the experimental work, there has also been a series of publications studying the theory that show that, to go beyond an energy of $E_γ=170$ MeV, it is necessary to include other aspects, in particular the $Δ(1232)$ as a degree of freedom [2] and possibly higher partial waves [3].

## Key claim

The inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in an O(p⁴) Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory framework, using the "little δ" counting scheme, improves the description of neutral pion photoproduction data from threshold up to Eγ ≈ 260 MeV, with vertex corrections to the Δ propagator further improving the fit beyond Eγ ≈ 290 MeV, though the theory still overestimates data above Eγ ≈ 260 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the O(p⁴) HBχPT calculation of Bernard et al. (1996, 2001) by including the Δ(1232) as a dynamical degree of freedom using the "little δ" power counting scheme of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003), which treats the Δ propagator scaling differently near threshold (p⁻¹/²) and near resonance (p⁻³). They fitted six photoproduction low-energy constants (LECs) plus the magnetic γNΔ coupling gM to differential cross section and photon asymmetry data from the A2 and CB-TAPS collaborations at MAMI, covering Eγ = 154–350 MeV, while fixing the electric coupling gE = −1.0 and extracting c4 = 1.18 GeV⁻¹ from πN scattering.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between Δ(1232) and nucleon is ΔM = mΔ − mN ≈ 290 MeV.
- The threshold photon energy for neutral pion photoproduction is Eγ ≈ 145 MeV.
- Previous O(p⁴) HBχPT and relativistic EOMS calculations fail beyond Eγ ≈ 170 MeV.
- The fitted magnetic γNΔ coupling is gM = 2.66, reduced from the original extraction of 2.9 by Pascalutsa et al. (2006).
- The LEC c4 was extracted as c4 = 1.18 GeV⁻¹ from a second-order HBχPT fit to SAID WI08 data.
- The fit covers Eγ = 154–350 MeV, with systematic errors of 4% for dσ/dΩ and 5% for Σ.
- The reduced χ² shows the calculation is accurate up to Eγ ≈ 260 MeV, with vertex corrections improving the fit beyond Eγ ≈ 290 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the O(p⁴) HBχPT calculations of Bernard et al. (1996, 2001), which were limited to about 20 MeV above threshold (Eγ ≈ 165 MeV), and addresses the failure of both the HBχPT approach of Fernández-Ramírez et al. (2013) and the relativistic EOMS calculation of Hilt et al. (2013) beyond Eγ ≈ 170 MeV. It builds on the O(p³) EOMS calculation including the Δ by Blin et al. (2015) and uses the "little δ" scheme of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003). The results enable improved chiral EFT descriptions of neutral pion photoproduction from threshold through the Δ resonance region, with the next step being to test whether the energy dependence of the LECs can be contained within a χ²red.+1 error band.
