# Hadron-Resonance Gas at Freeze-out: Reminder on Importance of Repulsive Interactions

> An influence of the repulsive interactions on matter properties is considered within the excluded volume van der Waals hadron-resonance gas model. Quantitative results are presented for matter at the chemical freeze-out in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energies. In particular, it is shown that repulsive interactions connected to non-zero size of created particles lead to a significant decrease of collision energy at which the net-baryon density has a maximum. A position of the transition point from baryon to meson dominated matter depends on the difference between baryon and meson hard-core radiuses.

## Key claim

Repulsive interactions, modeled via excluded volume van der Waals corrections with hadron hard-core radii, significantly shift the collision energy at which the net-baryon density at chemical freeze-out reaches its maximum, and the position of the baryon-to-meson transition point depends on the difference between baryon and meson hard-core radii; these effects are not captured by the ideal hadron-resonance gas model.

## Method

The authors use the excluded volume van der Waals hadron-resonance gas (EV–HRG) model, where the system volume is replaced by an available volume that subtracts the excluded volumes of all hadrons, leading to a transcendental equation for the pressure. They compute net-baryon density, entropy density, and the baryon/meson entropy density ratio along the chemical freeze-out line parameterized from fits to central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collision data, using the THERMUS package with all mesons up to K*_4(2045) and baryons up to Ω⁻, including resonance widths and quantum statistics. They compare results for equal hard-core radii (r = 0.5 fm and 1 fm) and for different baryon and meson radii (r_B = 0.5 fm, r_M = 0).

## Key numbers

- For the ideal hadron-resonance gas (I–HRG), the net-baryon density maximum occurs at E_lab ≅ 34A GeV.
- For the EV–HRG with equal hard-core radius r = 0.5 fm, the net-baryon density maximum shifts to E_lab ≅ 17A GeV.
- For the EV–HRG with equal hard-core radius r = 1 fm, the net-baryon density maximum shifts to E_lab ≅ 7A GeV.
- The suppression factor R for r = 0.5 fm is about 0.9 at low E_lab and about 0.4 at high E_lab.
- The baryon-to-meson transition point (where s_B = s_M) for equal radii is at E_lab ≅ 46A GeV in both I–HRG and EV–HRG.
- For unequal radii (r_B = 0.5 fm, r_M = 0), the baryon-to-meson transition point shifts to E_lab ≅ 23A GeV.
- The dense packing limit for hard spheres is 0.26; the available volume fraction κ remains above this limit for all considered radii.

## Context

This work extends the ideal hadron-resonance gas model (I–HRG) used in statistical fits to hadron multiplicities (e.g., Becattini, Cleymans 2005) by incorporating repulsive interactions via the van der Waals excluded volume procedure (vdw, vdwa). It directly addresses the collision energy dependence of net-baryon density (previously studied by RC2006) and the baryon/meson entropy density ratio (s-bar, Cleymans 2005, Cleymans 2011), which have been conjectured to relate to the onset of deconfinement observed in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS (Horn). The results show that repulsive interactions must be accounted for before drawing conclusions about the energy of the net-baryon density maximum or the baryon-meson transition, and they motivate renewed experimental and theoretical efforts to determine hadron hard-core radii, e.g., via particle number fluctuations as in JFu (2012).
