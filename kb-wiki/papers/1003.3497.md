# Cold Nuclear Matter Effects on J/psi and Upsilon Production at the LHC

> The charmonium yields are expected to be considerably suppressed if a deconfined medium is formed in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. In addition, the bottomonium states, with the possible exception of the Upsilon(1S) state, are also expected to be suppressed in heavy-ion collisions. However, in proton-nucleus collisions the quarkonium production cross sections, even those of the Upsilon(1S), are also suppressed. These "cold nuclear matter" effects need to be accounted for before signals of the high density QCD medium can be identified in the measurements made in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We identify two cold nuclear matter effects important for midrapidity quarkonium production: "nuclear absorption", typically characterized as a final-state effect on the produced quarkonium state and shadowing, the modification of the parton densities in nuclei relative to the nucleon, an initial-state effect. We characterize these effects and study the energy, rapidity, and impact-parameter dependence of initial-state shadowing in this paper.

## Key claim

Cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing and nuclear absorption) must be quantified and subtracted from heavy-ion collision data before any signal of deconfinement (quark-gluon plasma formation) can be identified in J/psi and Upsilon production at the LHC; the paper assumes that nuclear absorption is negligible at LHC energies so that the (p+A)/(p+p) ratios depend only on shadowing and are equivalent for all charmonium and bottomonium states respectively.

## Method

The author uses the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) at next-to-leading order to calculate quarkonium production cross sections in p+p, p+A, d+A, and A+A collisions, implementing nuclear parton distributions (nPDFs) with spatial dependence via a Woods-Saxon nucleon density profile. Five shadowing parameterizations (EKS98, nDSg, HKN, EPS08, EPS09) are employed to model the modification of gluon densities in nuclei, and the impact-parameter dependence of shadowing is studied by allowing the shadowing ratio to vary with the path length of the parton through the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For J/psi production in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, the effective A-dependence exponent alpha is approximately 0.76 and 0.52 respectively.
- For Upsilon production in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, alpha is approximately 0.88 and 0.76 respectively.
- The direct J/psi cross section per nucleon pair in p+p at sqrt(s_NN) = 14 TeV is 32.9 microbarns; in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV it is 11.7 microbarns.
- The direct Upsilon(1S) cross section per nucleon pair in p+p at sqrt(s_NN) = 14 TeV is 0.43 microbarns; in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV it is 0.15 microbarns.
- The NA50 collaboration measured the difference between psi' and J/psi absorption cross sections as Delta sigma = 4.2 +/- 1 mb at 400 GeV and 2.8 +/- 0.5 mb at 450 GeV.
- At x_F near 0, the E866 results imply Delta alpha = alpha_J/psi - alpha_psi' < 0.2, corresponding to Delta sigma < 1.6 mb.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions (Vogt, various years) by providing systematic predictions for LHC energies across multiple collision systems (p+A, d+A, A+A) using the CEM framework. It builds on fixed-target measurements from NA50 (CERN SPS) and E866 (Fermilab) that established the energy dependence of nuclear absorption, and on RHIC data from PHENIX and STAR that constrain the nuclear gluon density. The results enable experimental collaborations at the LHC (ALICE, CMS, ATLAS) to disentangle cold nuclear matter effects from hot medium effects in quarkonium suppression measurements, and provide a baseline for extracting the scale evolution of nuclear gluon distributions by comparing J/psi and Upsilon production ratios.
