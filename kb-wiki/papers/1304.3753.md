# Second order viscous corrections to the harmonic spectrum in heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the second order viscous correction to the kinetic distribution, $δf_{(2)}$, and use this result in a hydrodynamic simulation of heavy ion collisions to determine the complete second order correction to the harmonic spectrum, $v_n$. At leading order in a conformal fluid, the first viscous correction is determined by one scalar function, $χ_{0p}$. One moment of this scalar function is constrained by the shear viscosity. At second order in a conformal fluid, we find that $δf(\p)$ can be characterized by two scalar functions of momentum, $χ_{1p}$ and $χ_{2p}$. The momentum dependence of these functions is largely determined by the kinematics of the streaming operator. Again, one moment of these functions is constrained by the parameters of second order hydrodynamics, $τ_π$ and $λ_1$. The effect of $δf_{(2)}$ on the integrated flow is small (up to $v_4$), but is quite important for the higher harmonics at modestly-large $p_T$. Generally, $δf_{(2)}$ increases the value of $v_n$ at a given $p_T$, and is most important in small systems.

## Key claim
The second-order viscous correction to the distribution function, δf_(2), has a modest effect on integrated flow harmonics up to v_4, but becomes quite important for higher harmonics at modestly-large p_T, and is most important in small systems.

## Method
The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in a relaxation time approximation for a conformal gas, expanding the distribution function to second order in gradients. They decomposed δf_(2) into irreducible tensors characterized by two scalar functions of momentum, χ_{1p} and χ_{2p}, whose moments are constrained by the second-order transport coefficients τ_π and λ_1. The resulting functional form was implemented in a 2+1 boost-invariant hydrodynamic simulation to compute the linear response of harmonic flow v_n to initial deformations.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity η is determined by the moment: η = [2/((d-1)(d+1) T^3)] ∫_p (p^4/E_p) χ_{0p}
- The second-order transport coefficients satisfy: λ_1 + ητ_π = [8/((d-1)(d+1)(d+3) T^6)] ∫_p χ_{1p} (p^6/E_p)
- The second-order transport coefficient ητ_π satisfies: ητ_π = [2/((d-1)(d+1) T^5)] ∫_p χ_{2p} p^4
- For a classical gas with the quadratic ansatz (α=0), the scalar functions simplify to: χ_{0p} = (η/(2s)) n_p, χ_{1p} = (1/4)(η/s)^2 n_p, χ_{2p} = (1/2)(η/s)^2 n_p

## Context
This work extends the second-order hydrodynamic framework of Baier, Romatschke, Son, Starinets, and Stephanov (BRSSS) (2008) by computing the corresponding second-order viscous corrections to the distribution function at freezeout, which were previously missing from simulations. Prior simulations used second-order equations of motion but only first-order corrections to the distribution function, as in the work of Dusling (2009). This enables more consistent freezeout treatment in viscous hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, particularly for higher harmonic flow at moderate transverse momentum.
