# Pressure Anisotropy in Heavy Ion Collisions from Color Glass Condensate

> We generalize calculations of the energy-momentum tensor for classical gluon fields in the McLerran-Venugopalan model using the small-$τ$ power series expansion method. Results to all orders for the energy density and pressures are given in the leading $Q^2$ approximation and with the inclusion of running coupling effects. The energy density and transverse pressure decrease monotonically with time while the longitudinal pressure starts from a negative value and increases towards zero.

## Key claim

The authors analytically compute the energy-momentum tensor of classical gluon fields in the Color Glass Condensate framework to all orders in proper time using a small-τ power series expansion, finding that the classical gluon field description cannot realize pressure isotropization because the longitudinal pressure remains negative and only tends toward zero, never becoming positive.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the McLerran-Venugopalan model using a power series expansion in proper time τ, assuming transverse homogeneity and isotropy. They apply the leading Q² approximation, which decouples and linearizes the recursion relations, and include running coupling effects by replacing α_s with α_s(M²) at the relevant energy scales. The series for the energy density and pressures are resummed into expressions involving generalized hypergeometric functions and infinite power series, which are then evaluated numerically up to order (Qτ)^100.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at τ=0: ε₀ = 2πα_s³(C_A/d_A)μ₁μ₂ ln(Q₁²/m₁²) ln(Q₂²/m₂²)
- Initial pressures: P_T = ε₀ and P_L = -ε₀ (highly anisotropic)
- At Qτ = 10.0 (τ ≈ 0.5 fm/c): P_T/ε ≈ 0.5 and P_L/ε ≈ 0
- Numerical calculations use a = ln(Q²/m²) = 6, giving Q = 4.0 GeV when m = Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV, and Q_s ≈ 1.2 GeV
- The ratio Q²/Q_s² ≈ 12, consistent with the assumption Q² ≫ Q_s²

## Context

This work extends the small-τ power series expansion method previously applied by Fries et al. (2006) and Chen et al. (2015) to all orders in τ, generalizing calculations of the energy-momentum tensor. The results are essentially identical to those obtained in classical SU(2) lattice simulations by Epelbaum and Gelis (2013), who found P_T/ε ≈ 0.5 and P_L/ε ≈ 0 when Q_sτ > 1. The paper contrasts with strongly coupled plasma studies using AdS/CFT by Chesler and Yaffe and by Heller, Janik and Witaszczyk, which found sizable anisotropy when viscous hydrodynamics becomes applicable. The results provide initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations and inform whether isotropic or anisotropic initial conditions are needed for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution.
