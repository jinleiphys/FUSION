# Universality of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations: the factorization property of the nuclear wave function, the relative and center-of-mass momentum distributions, and the nuclear contacts

> The two-nucleon momentum distributions have been calculated for nuclei up to A=40 and various values of the relative and center-of-mass momenta and angle between them. For complex nuclei a parameter-free linked-cluster expansion, based upon a realistic local two-nucleon interaction of the Argonne family and variational wave function featuring central, tensor, spin and iso-spin correlations, has been used. The obtained results show that: 1) independently of the mass number A, at values of the relative momentum k_rel> 2 fm^{-1} the proton-neutron momentum distributions for back-to-back (BB) nucleons (K_cm=0) exhibit the factorization property n_A^{pn}(k_rel,K_cm=0)=C_A^{pn} n_D(k_rel) n_{cm}^{pn}(K_cm=0), where n_D is the deuteron momentum distribution, n_{cm}^{pn}(K_{cm}=0) the momentum distribution of the c.m. motion of the pair and C_A^{pn} the nuclear contact measuring the number of BB pn pairs with deuteron-like momenta; 2) the values of the proton-neutron nuclear contact C_A^{pn} are obtained in a model-independent way from the ratio n_A^{pn}(k_rel,K_cm=0)/n_D(k_rel) n_{cm}^{pn}(K_cm=0); 3) also the K_cm-integrated pn momentum distributions divided by the deuteron momentum distribution exhibits a constant behavior equal to C_A^{pn}, but only at very high values of k_{rel}> 3.5fm^{-1}, where the relative momentum distribution is entirely governed by BB short-range correlated nucleons; 4) the absolute value of the number of pn and pp short-range correlated pairs is calculated, illustrating that the high values (K_cm>1 fm^{-1}) of the pair c.m. momentum appreciably reduce the dominance of the pn over pp pairs produced by the tensor force when K_cm=0; 5) calculations are in good agreement with the VMC calculations for light nuclei and with available experimental on the processes A(e,e'pn)X and A(e,e'pp)X.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for nuclei from A=3 to A=40, the two-nucleon momentum distributions of proton-neutron pairs exhibit a universal factorization property at relative momenta above approximately 2 fm^{-1}, where the distribution can be expressed as a product of the deuteron momentum distribution, the center-of-mass momentum distribution of the pair, and a nucleus-dependent constant called the nuclear contact. This factorization holds specifically for back-to-back configurations (center-of-mass momentum equal to zero) and requires that the relative momentum be sufficiently large to suppress mean-field contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated two-nucleon momentum distributions for ^3He, ^4He, ^12C, ^16O, and ^40Ca using a linked-cluster expansion approach with realistic local two-nucleon interactions from the Argonne family (AV18 for ^3He and AV8' for heavier nuclei) and variational wave functions incorporating central, tensor, spin, and isospin correlations. For A=3 and A=4, exact ab initio wave functions were used, while for A>4 the linked-cluster expansion included up to four-body cluster contributions. The momentum distributions were computed as functions of relative momentum k_rel, center-of-mass momentum K_cm, and the angle between them, and the nuclear contact C_A^{pn} was extracted from the ratio of the back-to-back momentum distribution to the deuteron momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The factorization property holds for relative momenta k_rel > 1.5-2 fm^{-1} for back-to-back configurations (K_cm=0)
- For K_cm-integrated distributions, the constant behavior equal to C_A^{pn} appears only at k_rel > 3.5-4 fm^{-1}
- Nuclear contact values: C_3^{pn} = 2.0 ± 0.1 (^3He), C_4^{pn} = 4.0 ± 0.1 (^4He), C_12^{pn} = 20 ± 1.6 (^12C), C_16^{pn} = 24 ± 1.8 (^16O), C_40^{pn} = 60 ± 4.0 (^40Ca)
- The threshold for angle-independence and factorization is given by k_rel^- = 1.5 fm^{-1} + K_cm, with K_cm in fm^{-1}
- The center-of-mass momentum distribution is well described by a Gaussian with parameter alpha_A up to K_cm approximately 1 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the factorization concept previously demonstrated for few-nucleon systems by Schiavilla (2006) and the phenomenological assumption of Ciofi degli Atti (1995) to complex nuclei up to A=40, confirming the universal, A-independent nature of short-range nucleon-nucleon dynamics. The results agree with variational Monte Carlo calculations by Wiringa (2013) for light nuclei and with experimental data from A(e,e'pn)X and A(e,e'pp)X reactions. The nuclear contact formalism provides a model-independent way to quantify the number of deuteron-like proton-neutron pairs in nuclei, enabling systematic studies of short-range correlations across the periodic table and providing input for interpreting electron-scattering experiments.
