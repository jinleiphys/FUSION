# Dibaryon Condensate in Nuclear Matter and Neutron Stars: Exact Analysis in One-Dimensional Models

> We investigate dense nuclear matter with a dibaryon Bose-Einstein condensate as a possible intermediate state before the quark-gluon phase transition. An exact analysis of this state of matter is presented in a one-dimensional model. The analysis is based on a reduction of the quantization rules for the N-body problem to N coupled algebraic transcendental equations. We observe that when the Fermi momentum approaches the resonance momentum, the one-particle distribution function increases near the Fermi surface. When the Fermi momentum is increased beyond the resonance momentum, the equation of state becomes softer. The observed behavior can be interpreted in terms of formation of a Bose-Einstein condensate of two-fermion resonances (dibaryons). In cold nuclear matter, it should occur if 2(m_N + epsilon_F) is greater or equal to m_D, where m_N and m_D are respectively the nucleon and dibaryon masses and epsilon_F is the nucleon Fermi energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in one-dimensional models of dense nuclear matter, a Bose-Einstein condensate of two-fermion resonances (dibaryons) can form as an intermediate state before the quark-gluon phase transition, but this occurs only under the condition that \(2(m_N + \varepsilon_F) \geq m_D\), where \(m_N\) and \(m_D\) are the nucleon and dibaryon masses and \(\varepsilon_F\) is the nucleon Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors reduce the quantization rules for the N-body Schrodinger equation in one dimension to N coupled algebraic transcendental equations, using periodic boundary conditions and matching conditions for the wave function across regions of integrability. They classify eigenfunctions for both bosonic and fermionic systems with zero-range singular potentials, deriving generalized Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rules that depend only on the two-body scattering phase shifts. In the thermodynamic limit, they obtain a linear integral equation for the distribution function of particle quasi-momenta, which is completely determined by the scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The condition for dibaryon condensate formation in cold nuclear matter is \(2(m_N + \varepsilon_F) \geq m_D\), where \(m_N\) is the nucleon mass, \(m_D\) is the dibaryon mass, and \(\varepsilon_F\) is the nucleon Fermi energy.
- For a delta-function potential \(V(x) = \alpha \delta(x)\), the S-matrix for the symmetric case takes the form \(S_{+}(k) = (k - ik_0)/(k + ik_0)\) with \(k_0 = m\alpha > 0\), giving \(\delta_{+}(0) = \pi\) and \(S_{+}(0) = -1\).
- In the limit \(a \rightarrow 0\) with finite \(\kappa_{+}\) and \(\kappa_{-}\), the scattering phase shifts become \(\delta_{+}(k) \rightarrow \pi \theta(k)\) and \(\delta_{-}(k) \rightarrow 0\).
- The resonance S-matrix in the Breit-Wigner form is parametrized as \(S_{-}(k) = \frac{(k + k_0)(k - k_0^{*})}{(k + k_0^{*})(k - k_0)}\), where \(k_0 = k_1 - ik_2\) with real positive \(k_1\) and \(k_2\).

## Context

This work extends the exact solution methods for one-dimensional many-body systems developed by Girardeau (1960), Lieb and Liniger (1963), and McGuire (1964) for delta-function interacting bosons, generalizing them to arbitrary zero-range singular potentials and to fermionic systems with resonances. The paper builds on earlier work by Krivoruchenko (1995) on dibaryon condensation softening the equation of state for neutron stars, and by Faessler et al. on heterogeneous nucleon-dibaryon matter in the Walecka model. The results enable exact calculation of dispersion laws for elementary excitations, ground state energy, and equation of state in one-dimensional models, which can guide approximation methods for three-dimensional nuclear matter and neutron star stability analysis.
