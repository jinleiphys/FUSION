# The reactor antineutrino spectrum calculation

> New fissile isotopes antineutrino spectra ($^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{239}$Pu and $^{241}$Pu) calculation is presented. On base of summation method the toy model was developed. It was shown that total antineutrino number is conserved in framework of given database on individual fragments yields. The analysis of antineutrino spectrum shape says that any presented antineutrino spectrum should satisfy to the total antineutrino number conservation.

## Key claim

The authors present a new calculation of reactor antineutrino spectra for the four main fissile isotopes using the summation method with an updated nuclear database, and they argue that any valid antineutrino spectrum must conserve the total antineutrino number per fission, which is a physical constant for each isotope. The key hedge is that this conservation holds only within the framework of a given database on individual fragment yields, and the authors use a "toy model" to demonstrate the principle rather than a full reactor simulation.

## Method

The authors used the summation method, summing individual antineutrino spectra from each beta transition of fission fragments weighted by their yields per fission and branching ratios. They developed a computer code that computes antineutrino spectra at any moment of the reactor fuel cycle based on a database of 1039 nuclei (588 known beta-decayers, 318 unknown, and 133 stable isotopes). They tested the sensitivity of the total antineutrino number to individual fragment spectrum shapes by using toy shapes (rectangles, triangles, sine functions) and compared their results with spectra from Vogel (2007), MEPHI, and Huber (2011) after recalculating all spectra to a common exposure time of 2 years.

## Key numbers

- Total antineutrino number per fission for 235U at infinity exposure time: this work 6.318, Vogel 6.14, MEPHI 6.08.
- Total antineutrino number per fission for 235U at 2 years exposure time: this work 5.687, Vogel 5.527, MEPHI 5.473.
- Total antineutrino number per fission for 239Pu at 2 years exposure time: this work 5.153, Vogel 5.007, MEPHI 4.980.
- Total antineutrino number per fission for 238U at 2 years exposure time: this work 6.838, Vogel 6.528, MEPHI 6.537.
- Total antineutrino number per fission for 241Pu at 2 years exposure time: this work 5.967, Vogel 5.856, MEPHI 5.783.
- Inverse beta decay cross section for 235U: this work 6.28 x 10^-43 cm^2/fission, compared to a mean value from reactor experiments of 6.33 +/- 0.08 x 10^-43 cm^2/fission (Giunti, 2017).
- Antineutrino number for 235U in the 2-9.5 MeV region at 2 years: this work 1.5747, Vogel 1.5722, MEPHI 1.5901, Huber 1.7077 (8.15% higher than the mean of the others).
- Total antineutrino number for 235U at 2 years after extrapolating Huber spectrum: this work 5.6875, Vogel 5.527, MEPHI 5.473, Huber 6.0174.

## Context

This work extends the summation method approach to reactor antineutrino spectra, updating earlier calculations from the 1970s and 1980s that used limited fission fragment databases. The authors compare their results with the converted spectra of Huber (2011), which were derived from the electron measurements of Schreckenbach (1985) and Schreckenbach (1988), and with the summation-method spectra of Vogel (2007) and MEPHI. The paper introduces the total antineutrino number per fission as a conservation criterion for validating spectra, and it enables future "on-line" calculations of reactor antineutrino spectra during the reactor operating cycle.
