# Exclusive processes with a leading neutron in $ep$ collisions

> In this paper we extend the color dipole formalism to the study of exclusive processes associated with a leading neutron in $ep$ collisions at high energies. The exclusive $ρ$, $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ production, as well as the Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering, are analysed assuming a diffractive interaction between the color dipole and the pion emitted by the incident proton. We compare our predictions with the HERA data on $ρ$ production and estimate the magnitude of the absorption corrections. We show that the color dipole formalism is able to describe the current data. Finally, we present our estimate for the exclusive cross sections which can be studied at HERA and in future electron-proton colliders.

## Key claim

The color dipole formalism, previously applied to semi-inclusive leading neutron production, can be extended to exclusive processes (vector meson and DVCS) associated with a leading neutron in ep collisions, provided that absorption corrections are included via a multiplicative factor K whose value depends on the choice of the ratio Rq between the dipole-pion and dipole-proton scattering amplitudes.

## Method

The authors factorize the cross section into a pion flux fπ/p(xL,t) describing the virtual pion emitted by the proton and a γ*π → Eπ cross section computed in the color dipole formalism. The dipole-pion scattering amplitude is related to the dipole-proton amplitude by a constant factor Rq (taken between 1/3 and 2/3), and the bCGC model is used for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude. The overlap between photon and vector meson wave functions is computed using both Boosted Gaussian and Gauss-LC models, and the absorption corrections are parameterized by a factor K that multiplies the cross section.

## Key numbers

* For exclusive ρ photoproduction at HERA (W = 60 GeV, Q² = 0.04 GeV², pT < 0.2 GeV), the absorption factor K ranges from 0.134 to 0.210 depending on the pion flux model, assuming Rq = 2/3.
* For exclusive φ production at HERA kinematics (W = 60 GeV, Q² = 0.04 GeV², pT < 0.2 GeV), the predicted total cross section is σ(γp → φπn) = 25.47 ± 3.70 nb.
* For exclusive J/Ψ production at HERA kinematics (W = 60 GeV, Q² = 0.04 GeV², pT < 0.2 GeV), the predicted total cross section is σ(γp → J/Ψπn) = 0.22 ± 0.03 nb.
* For DVCS at HERA kinematics (W = 60 GeV, Q² = 10 GeV², pT < 0.2 GeV), the predicted total cross section is σ(γ*p → γπn) = 0.008 ± 0.001 nb.
* For future ep collider energies (W = 1 TeV, Q² = 5 GeV², pT < 0.2 GeV), the predicted cross sections are: σ(γ*p → ρπn) = 6.55 ± 0.95 nb, σ(γ*p → φπn) = 1.71 ± 0.25 nb, σ(γ*p → J/Ψπn) = 1.20 ± 0.17 nb, and σ(γ*p → γπn) = 0.16 ± 0.02 nb.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole formalism previously applied to semi-inclusive leading neutron production by the same authors (Goncalves, Navarra, Spiering, 2015) to exclusive final states (ρ, φ, J/Ψ mesons and DVCS). It builds on the HERA measurements of exclusive ρ photoproduction with leading neutrons reported by the H1 Collaboration (2015) and on the bCGC model for dipole-proton scattering developed by Kowalski, Motyka, and Watt (2006) and updated by Amir and collaborators. The approach enables predictions for future electron-proton colliders and suggests that exclusive leading neutron processes can be studied in ultraperipheral hadronic collisions at the LHC.
