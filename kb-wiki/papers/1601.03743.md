# Transport coefficients of heavy baryons

> We compute the transport coefficients (drag and momentum diffusion) of the low-lying heavy baryons $Λ_c$ and $Λ_b$ in a medium of light mesons formed at the later stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We employ the Fokker-Planck approach to obtain the transport coefficients from unitarized baryon-meson interactions based on effective field theories that respect chiral and heavy-quark symmetries. We provide the transport coefficients as a function of temperature and heavy-baryon momentum, and analyze the applicability of certain nonrelativistic estimates. Moreover we compare our outcome for the spatial diffusion coefficient to the one coming from the solution of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport equation and we find a very good agreement between both calculations. The transport coefficients for $Λ_c$ and $Λ_b$ in a thermal bath will be used in a subsequent publication as input in a Langevin evolution code for the generation and propagation of heavy particles in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper computes the drag and momentum diffusion coefficients for the low-lying heavy baryons Λ_c and Λ_b in a thermal bath of light mesons (π, K, K̅, η) using unitarized effective field theories, but the results are specifically applicable only to the hadronic phase formed at the later stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, where the baryochemical potential is set to zero.

## Method

The authors employed the Fokker-Planck approach to obtain transport coefficients from unitarized baryon-meson interactions based on effective field theories that respect chiral and heavy-quark symmetries. The scattering amplitudes were computed by solving the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation using a kernel derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction extended with SU(6)×HQSS symmetry, and the momentum integrals for the transport coefficients were evaluated using Monte Carlo techniques with constant cross sections at high energies to remove the UV-momentum cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of transport coefficients for heavy mesons (D and B̅ mesons) in a hadronic medium (Tolos 2013, Torres-Rincon 2014) to the heavy baryon sector (Λ_c and Λ_b). The results are compared with an earlier calculation by Ghosh (2014) that used scattering lengths and Born terms without unitarization, finding that unitarization enhances the transport coefficients due to resonant states. The transport coefficients will be used as input in a Langevin evolution code for heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies to predict observables such as R_AA, v₂, and baryon-to-meson ratios.
