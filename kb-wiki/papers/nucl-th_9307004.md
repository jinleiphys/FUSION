# Effects of the final-state interaction in ($γ$,pn) and ($γ$,pp) processes

> A model is presented to describe electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission processes in a shell-model picture. Distortions in the outgoing nucleon waves are accounted for by performing a partial-wave expansion in a real mean-field potential. The antisymmetry condition for the A-body wavefunctions is shown to be naturally preserved. The model is used to calculate ($γ$,pn) and ($γ$,pp) cross sections off the target nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{12}$C for photon energies ranging from 50 MeV up to the $\bigtriangleup$(1232) isobar threshold. Effects due to the pionic currents and intermediate $\bigtriangleup$ creation are implemented. The impact of the distortions due to the interaction of the outgoing nucleon waves with the (A-2) core is examined. Hadronic form factors are introduced to regularize the $π$NN vertices and the sensitivity of the cross section to the pion cut-off mass is examined. The relative contribution of the ($γ$,pp) and the ($γ$,pn) channel to the total photoabsorption strength is discussed. Further, the photon energy dependence of the ($γ$,pp)/($γ$,pn) ratio is investigated.

# Effects of the final-state interaction in (γ,pn) and (γ,pp) processes

## Key claim

The paper presents an unfactorized shell-model approach to electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission that preserves antisymmetry of the A-body wavefunctions, but the calculations are restricted to direct two-nucleon knockout following photoabsorption on two-body currents (pionic and intermediate Δ-isobar), explicitly neglecting short-range correlations, mutual interactions between the two escaping nucleons, and multi-step rescattering processes of the RPA or coupled-channel type.

## Method

The authors perform a partial-wave expansion of the outgoing nucleon waves in a real mean-field potential (Skyrme-type SkE2 Hartree-Fock) to account for distortions from the residual (A-2) core, constructing final states as linear combinations of 2h2p Slater determinants. The Feynman amplitude is evaluated via multipole decomposition of the electromagnetic transition operators (electric and magnetic, up to J=5 at 200 MeV), using currents from seagull and pion-in-flight diagrams (πsea, πfli) and intermediate Δ-isobar creation with a static current, regularized by monopole hadronic form factors at the πNN and πNΔ vertices. Cross sections are calculated for ¹⁶O and ¹²C targets at photon energies from 50 MeV to the Δ(1232) threshold (≈300 MeV), comparing plane-wave and distorted-wave results.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 50 MeV up to the Δ(1232) isobar threshold (≈300 MeV)
- At Eγ=200 MeV, the multipole expansion for electric and magnetic transition operators converges at J=5
- Partial waves for escaping nucleons up to i13/2 produce no visible change in angular cross sections
- For ¹⁶O(γ,pn) at Eγ=200 MeV with Tp=48 MeV, distortions reduce the pionic (seagull + pion-in-flight) angular cross section strength by about 50%
- A pion cutoff mass Λπ of 1200 MeV is used as default, with reference to alternative values of 810 MeV from Wakamatsu and Matsumoto (1983) and from triton binding energy studies

## Context

This work extends earlier (γ,pn) calculations by Weise et al. (1970) and the factorized approaches of Gari and Hebach (1981), Wakamatsu and Matsumoto (1983), and Boato and Gianninni (1989), which neglected or approximated final-state interactions. It directly addresses the need for an unfactorized treatment of distortions identified in the authors' prior work (Ryckebusch et al., 1992), and contrasts with the optical-potential factorized approach of Boffi and Giannini (1991). The model enables systematic investigation of the relative contribution of (γ,pp) versus (γ,pn) channels to total photoabsorption, which is relevant for disentangling short-range correlation effects (expected to appear in the weaker pp channel) from the dominant meson-exchange contributions to the pn channel.
