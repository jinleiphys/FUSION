# Coupled-channel $Λ_{c}K^{+}-pD_{s}$ Interaction in Flavor $ \textrm{SU}\left(3\right) $ Limit of Lattice QCD

> We study $S$-wave interactions in the $I\left(J^{p}\right)=1/2\left(1/2^{-}\right)$ $Λ_{c}K^{+}-pD_{s}$ system on the basis of the coupled-channel HAL QCD method. The potentials which are faithful to QCD S-matrix below the $ pD^{*} $ threshold are extracted from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions on the lattice in Flavor $ \textrm{SU}\left(3\right) $ Limit. For the simulation, we employ $ 3 $-flavor full QCD gauge configurations on a $\left(1.93 \:\textrm{fm} \right)^{3}$ volume at $m_π\simeq 872$ MeV. %\textcolor{red}{For the charm quark, the relativistic heavy quark action is employed to treat its dynamics on the lattice}. We present our results of the S-wave coupled-channel potentials for the $Λ_{c}K^{+}-pD_{s}$ system in the $1/2\left(1/2^{-}\right)$ state as well as scattering observables obtained from the extracted potential matrix. We observe that the coupling between $Λ_{c}K^{+}$ and $pD_{s}$ channels is weak. The phase shifts and scattering length obtained from the extracted potential matrix show that the $Λ_{c}K^{+}$ interaction is attractive at low energy and stronger than the $pD_{s}$ interaction though no bound state at $m_π\geq872$ MeV.

## Key claim

The S-wave coupled-channel $\Lambda_{c}K^{+}-pD_{s}$ interaction in the $I(J^{p})=1/2(1/2^{-})$ state is attractive at low energy, with the $\Lambda_{c}K^{+}$ attraction stronger than the $pD_{s}$ attraction, but no bound state exists at the simulated pion mass of $m_{\pi}\simeq 872$ MeV and above.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled-channel HAL QCD method to extract energy-independent non-local potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions computed on a $16^{3}\times 32$ lattice with volume $(1.93\ \text{fm})^{3}$ and lattice spacing $a=0.1209$ fm. They used 3-flavor full QCD gauge configurations at the flavor SU(3) symmetric point with $m_{\pi}\simeq 872$ MeV, employing the non-perturbatively $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson quark action for light and charm quarks. The leading-order potential matrix was obtained from the time-dependent HAL QCD method at time separation $t-t_{0}=8$, and scattering observables (phase shifts and scattering length) were computed by solving the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in infinite volume.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel HAL QCD method, previously applied to hyperon-baryon systems (Sasaki, 2015; Sasaki, 2020) and charmed tetraquark states (Ikeda, 2014; Ikeda, 2016; Yan, 2023), to the $\Lambda_{c}K^{+}-pD_{s}$ system relevant for understanding charmed hadron interactions in nuclear matter. The results provide input for phenomenological studies of the Kondo effect induced by $D_{s}$ mesons in nuclear matter (Yasui, 2017) and for future experimental investigations at facilities such as GSI-FAIR and J-PARC. The weak coupling between channels and the absence of a bound state at this heavy pion mass motivate future calculations at lighter pion masses closer to the physical point.
