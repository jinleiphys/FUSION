# Nucleon decay in the deuteron

> We calculate the lifetime of the deuteron from dimension-six quark operators that violate baryon number by one unit. We construct an effective field theory for $|ΔB|=1$ interactions that give rise to nucleon and $ΔB=1$ deuteron decay in a systematic expansion. We show that up to and including next-to-leading order the deuteron decay rate is given by the sum of the decay rates of the free proton and neutron. The first nuclear correction is expected to contribute at the few-percent level and comes with an undetermined low-energy constant. We discuss its relation to earlier potential-model calculations.

## Key claim

The deuteron decay rate is equal to the sum of the free proton and neutron decay rates up to and including next-to-leading order in the effective field theory expansion; the first nuclear correction is expected at the few-percent level but depends on an undetermined low-energy constant.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for baryon-number-violating interactions (|ΔB|=1) at the dimension-six quark level, treating nucleons and pions as the relevant low-energy degrees of freedom. They introduce imaginary parts into the low-energy constants of the ΔB=0 nuclear interactions to generate inclusive decay widths, then calculate the deuteron decay rate from the imaginary part of the deuteron propagator using a systematic expansion in Q/Λχ and Q/ΛNN, where Q ~ mπ ~ κ (the deuteron binding momentum).

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy: Bd ≈ 2.225 MeV, giving binding momentum κ = √(mN Bd) ≈ 45 MeV
* Pion mass: mπ ≈ 140 MeV; pion decay constant: Fπ ≈ 185 MeV; chiral-symmetry-breaking scale: Λχ ~ 2πFπ
* Axial-vector coupling constant: gA ≈ 1.27
* NDA estimate for free nucleon decay rate: Γp,n = O(Λχ⁵ C²/(4π)⁴) ~ 10⁻⁴ C² GeV⁵, consistent with lattice QCD result of ≈ 3·10⁻⁴ C² GeV⁵ for the largest contribution
* The first nuclear correction to the deuteron decay rate is expected at relative O(κ ΛNN/Λχ²) ~ 10⁻² (few percent level)
* Lower bounds on proton lifetime: p→π⁰+e⁺ at 1.6·10³⁴ y and p→π⁺+ν̄ at 3.9·10³² y (90% confidence level)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to ΔB=2 processes (neutron-antineutron oscillations) by Oosterhof et al. (2019) and others to the ΔB=1 case. The result that free-nucleon decay dominates the deuteron decay rate agrees with earlier potential-model calculations by Dover (1981) and Alvarez-Estrada (1982), but those works found larger nuclear corrections; the authors attribute the discrepancy to the use of pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling in those models, which violates chiral symmetry unless accompanied by a seagull vertex. The framework enables systematic calculation of ΔB=1 decays in heavier nuclei, where corrections from short-range interactions and pion exchange are both expected at the few-percent level.
