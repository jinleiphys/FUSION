# Elliptic flow from quark coalescence: mass ordering or quark number scaling?

> We show that either mass ordering or quark number scaling of anisotropic flow can result from quark coalescence, depending on the nature of phase space correlations at hadronization. Quark number scaling signals nonequilibrium dynamics because it can only appear when hydrodynamic correlations break down. However, the scaling does not hold for all nonthermal distributions, and is compatible with covariant transport theory only if remarkable cancellations occur at RHIC.

## Key claim

Either mass ordering or quark number scaling of elliptic flow can result from quark coalescence, depending on the type of phase space correlations present at hadronization; quark number scaling signals nonequilibrium dynamics because it can only appear when hydrodynamic correlations break down, but the scaling does not hold for all nonthermal distributions and is compatible with covariant transport theory only if remarkable cancellations occur at RHIC.

## Method

The author analytically derives the relationship between constituent quark anisotropies and hadron anisotropies from quark coalescence on a 3D spacetime hypersurface, using flow addition formulas for mesons (two constituents) and baryons (three constituents). The analysis compares the mass-ordering pattern from thermal (Boltzmann) constituent distributions with the quark-number-scaling pattern from distributions where local anisotropies dominate over nonlinear correction terms, and illustrates the latter with results from the covariant parton transport model MPC for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with b=8 fm.

## Key numbers

- For thermal constituent distributions at low pT, local anisotropy |v_n| ≈ (sh y_T * p_T / 2T)^n / n! ∝ p_T^n
- At freezeout at RHIC, T ~ 100-130 MeV, while chemical potential μ increases from ~80 MeV for pions to ~350 MeV for heavy particles
- In the MPC transport calculation for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with b=8 fm, the denominator in the exact meson flow addition formula exceeds 2 above constituent p_T,i >~ 1 GeV
- In the dynamical coalescence approach, the end result is only a modest ~20% and ~30% reduction of pion and proton elliptic flow at RHIC relative to quark number scaling

## Context

This work addresses the controversy between the mass-ordering pattern predicted by ideal hydrodynamics (Huovinen 2001, Kolb 2000, Teaney) and the quark-number-scaling pattern predicted by quark coalescence (Voloshin, the "coal" approach, charm coal). The paper shows there is no contradiction: thermal constituent distributions produce mass ordering from coalescence, while quark number scaling requires departure from hydrodynamic behavior. The growing experimental evidence for quark number scaling in the intermediate pT ~ 2-5 GeV region at RHIC signals the breakdown of hydrodynamics above pT > 2 GeV, complementing other indications such as the saturation of elliptic flow. The results underscore the importance of local momentum anisotropies and odd-order flow coefficients that have been ignored in analytic studies (the "coal" approach, Kolb's coalescence work) but are naturally incorporated in dynamical approaches.
