# $S$ factor of $^{13}$C($α$,$n$)$^{16}$O at low energies in cluster effective field theory

> The $^{13}$C($α$,$n$)$^{16}$O reaction at low energies is studied by constructing an effective field theory. We choose a separation scale at $E=1$~MeV, where $E$ is the initial $α$-$^{13}$C energy in center-of-mass frame, just below the sharp resonant $5/2^+$ state of $^{17}$O, and include two open channels, $α$-$^{13}$C and $n$-$^{16}$O, and resonant $1/2^+$, $5/2^-$, $3/2^+$ states of $^{17}$O in the study. Parameters of the theory are fitted to experimental data, $S$ factor of $^{13}$C($α$,$n$)$^{16}$O at the energies below $E=1$~MeV, including the data sets recently reported by the LUNA and JUNA collaborations, and the $S$ factor of $^{13}$C($α$,$n$)$^{16}$O is extrapolated to the Gamow peak energy $E_G= 0.19$~MeV in the low mass AGB stars. We discuss an uncertainty in the estimate of the $S$ factor and confirm that the main part of the uncertainty emerges from the parameter fit of the near-breakup threshold $1/2^+$ state of $^{17}$O.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S factor of the 13C(α,n)16O reaction at the Gamow peak energy EG = 0.19 MeV in low-mass AGB stars is extrapolated using cluster effective field theory, with the dominant uncertainty arising from the parameter fit of the near-breakup threshold 1/2+ state of 17O rather than from the truncation of the perturbative expansion.

## Method

An effective field theory was constructed with a separation scale at E = 1 MeV (initial α-13C center-of-mass energy), including two open channels (α-13C and n-16O) and three resonant states of 17O (1/2+, 5/2-, 3/2+). Parameters were fitted via MCMC analysis to experimental S factor data below E = 1 MeV, including recent LUNA and JUNA collaboration data sets, using the emcee package.

## Key numbers

* S factor at EG = 0.19 MeV from the seven-parameter fit: S = (1.12 ± 0.08) × 10^6 MeV b
* S factor at EG = 0.19 MeV from the ten-parameter fit: S = (1.09 ± 0.11) × 10^6 MeV b
* Gamow peak energy: EG = 190 ± 40 keV for the α-13C system at approximately 90 × 10^6 K
* Q value of the reaction: Q = 2.2156 MeV
* Resonant energy of the 5/2- state (seven-parameter fit fixed value): ER(5m) = 0.806612(17) MeV
* Resonant energy of the 3/2+ state (seven-parameter fit): ER(3p) = 0.8416(15) MeV
* Neutron width of the 1/2+ state (seven-parameter fit): ΓnR(1p) = 136+192-100 keV
* χ2/N = 0.67 for the seven-parameter fit with N = 56 data points

## Context

This work extends the cluster effective field theory methodology previously applied by Ando and collaborators to α-12C elastic scattering, β-delayed α emission from 16N, and the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction. The study addresses the uncertainty in extrapolating the 13C(α,n)16O S factor to stellar energies, which was highlighted by the recent precise measurements from the LUNA collaboration (2021) and JUNA collaboration (2022). The results enable improved predictions for the neutron source in the s-process in low-mass AGB stars with masses 1.5 ≤ M/M⊙ ≤ 3.
