# Theory of Jet Quenching in Ultra-Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We present a short overview of recent progress in the theory of jet quenching in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, including phenomenological studies of jet quenching at RHIC and the LHC, development in NLO perburbative QCD calculation of jet broadening and energy loss, full jet evolution and modification, medium response to jet transport, and lattice QCD and AdS/CFT studies of jet quenching.

## Key claim

This review argues that while perturbative QCD-based jet energy loss models have been successful in describing jet quenching at RHIC and the LHC, significant model discrepancies remain due to specific approximations (eikonal, soft, collinear) and no formal proof of factorization exists for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, so quantitative extraction of transport coefficients like \(\hat{q}\) requires collaborative efforts combining multiple models with realistic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The paper surveys theoretical approaches to jet quenching including radiative energy loss formalisms (BDMPS-Z, ASW, AMY, GLV, HT) and collisional energy loss, then describes phenomenological extraction of the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}\) by the JET Collaboration using five different energy loss models (McGill-AMY, Martini-AMY, HT-M, HT-BW, DGLV-CUJET) combined with viscous hydrodynamics simulations. It also reviews next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations of transverse momentum broadening, full jet evolution via Boltzmann transport equations, medium response modeled through hydrodynamic equations with source terms, and non-perturbative results from lattice QCD and AdS/CFT.

## Key numbers

- Scaled jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}/(C_s T^3) = 3.5 \pm 0.9\) at \(T \approx 370\) MeV (RHIC) and \(2.8 \pm 1.1\) at \(T \approx 470\) MeV (LHC), from JET Collaboration fits to single hadron \(R_{AA}\)
- This translates to \(\hat{q} \approx 1.2\)–\(1.9\) GeV\(^2\)/fm for a quark jet at the highest temperatures in central Au+Au (RHIC) and Pb+Pb (LHC) collisions
- For bottom quarks, collisional energy loss dominates below about 15 GeV, while radiative energy loss dominates at higher energies (Cao 2013)
- Lattice QCD in quenched SU(2) extrapolated to SU(3) with 2 quark flavors yields \(\hat{q} = 1.3\)–\(3.3\) GeV\(^2\)/fm for a gluon jet at \(T = 400\) MeV (Majumder 2012)
- Electrostatic QCD calculation gives \(\hat{q} = 6\) GeV\(^2\)/fm for RHIC energies (Panero 2013)
- NLO BDMPS calculation finds double logarithmic contribution \(\ln^2(L/l_0)\) to transverse momentum broadening, leading to modified energy loss \(\Delta E \propto \hat{q}_0 L^{2+\gamma}\) with \(\gamma = 2\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/\pi}\) (Iancu 2014, Blaizot 2014)

## Context

This review extends the line of work comparing jet energy loss formalisms in a "brick" of QGP (Armesto 2011) and the JET Collaboration extraction of \(\hat{q}\) (Burke 2013). It enables future systematic mapping of the temperature dependence of jet transport coefficients, development of a full NLO framework for jet energy loss, and combined simulations of jet transport with medium response using realistic hydrodynamics. Open questions include the thermalization mechanisms for deposited energy, the role of color decoherence in full jet evolution, and the precise determination of \(\hat{q}/T^3\) across collision energies.
