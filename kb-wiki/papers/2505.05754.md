# Synthesis of the superheavy elements beyond Og: extrapolating from ${}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ to ${}^{54}\mathrm{Cr}$

> Theoretical predictions on the optimal reaction energies are essential for producing superheavy elements (SHEs) beyond Og. Due to the limitation of the targets, synthesizing elements 119 and 120 will require beams of ${}^{50}\mathrm{Ti}$ and/or ${}^{54}\mathrm{Cr}$ ions. However, is it reliable to theoretically extrapolate from the well-investigated ${}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ induced reactions to those with heavier projectiles? In this work, we apply the Fusion-by-Diffusion (FBD) concept to answer this question from two perspectives: radial and mass asymmetry degrees of freedom. The FBD concept is employed in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom for the first time, in which by fitting the calculations to experimental evaporation residue cross sections (ERCS) for the reactions of ${}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ as projectiles with the actinide targets, a strong linear correlation between the contact distance ($D_\mathrm{cont}$) and center-of-mass energy excess above the Coulomb barrier ($E_\mathrm{c.m.}-B_0$) is found and a parametrization of the $D_\mathrm{cont}$ is introduced. Using the result of parametrization, the calculations satisfactorily reproduce the shapes of all hot fusion excitation functions and values of the ERCS. Furthermore, thanks to the recent experimental data, we extrapolate the calculation in the reactions \({}^{50}\mathrm{Ti}+{}^{242}\mathrm{Pu}\), \({}^{50}\mathrm{Ti}+{}^{244}\mathrm{Pu}\), and \({}^{54}\mathrm{Cr}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}\). The calculations reproduce the experimental data rather well within the experimental errors in both perspectives. Our results demonstrate that there is no non-negligible systematic deviation in extrapolating the projectiles from $^{48}\text{Ca}$ to $^{50}\text{Ti}$ and $^{54}\text{Cr}$ for synthesizing SHEs beyond Og.

# Synthesis of the superheavy elements beyond Og: extrapolating from 48Ca to 54Cr

## Key claim

The Fusion-by-Diffusion (FBD) concept, applied for the first time to the mass asymmetry degree of freedom, demonstrates that there is no non-negligible systematic deviation when extrapolating theoretical predictions from 48Ca-induced reactions to those using 50Ti and 54Cr projectiles for synthesizing superheavy elements beyond Oganesson, provided the calculations are calibrated against experimental evaporation residue cross sections for 48Ca reactions with actinide targets.

## Method

The authors applied the Fusion-by-Diffusion (FBD) concept from two perspectives: the radial degree of freedom and the mass asymmetry degree of freedom. For the radial degree of freedom, they calibrated the injection point distance (S_inj) by fitting calculations to experimental evaporation residue cross sections (ERCS) for 48Ca-induced reactions with actinide targets, finding a strong linear correlation between S_inj and the center-of-mass energy excess above the Coulomb barrier (E_c.m. - B_0). For the mass asymmetry degree of freedom, they introduced a similar parametrization of the contact distance (D_cont) as a function of E_c.m. - B_0. They then extrapolated these parametrizations to calculate ERCS for reactions with 50Ti and 54Cr projectiles and compared with recent experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The linear parametrization for the injection point distance in the radial degree of freedom is S_inj = 2.253 - 0.0165 x (E_c.m. - B_0) fm/MeV, with an estimated error margin of +/- 0.1 fm.
* The linear parametrization for the contact distance in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom is D_cont = 2.435 fm - 0.0618 x (E_c.m. - B_0) fm/MeV.
* For synthesizing element Z=119, the optimal incident energies (OIEs) predicted in the radial degree of freedom are 224.5 MeV for 50Ti+249Bk, 232.3 MeV for 51V+248Cm, and 242.3 MeV for 54Cr+243Am.
* For the same reactions in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom, the predicted OIEs are 224.5 MeV, 232.3 MeV, and 240.3 MeV, respectively.
* The ERCS for heavier projectiles 50Ti and 54Cr in synthesizing SHEs with Z=116 are nearly one order of magnitude lower than for 48Ca-induced reactions.

## Context

This work extends the Fusion-by-Diffusion concept previously applied to cold and hot fusion reactions (Siwek-Wilczynska 2012, Hagino 2018, Cap 2022) by employing it in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom for the first time. It addresses the reliability of extrapolating from well-studied 48Ca-induced reactions to heavier projectiles needed for elements 119 and 120, a concern raised by recent experimental efforts at Dubna (Oganessian 2024) and LBNL (Gates 2024). The results support the OIE law proposed by Zhu (2023) and enable more confident predictions for synthesizing superheavy elements beyond Oganesson, while also motivating future work using microscopic theories such as density functional theories (Bender 1998, Sekizawa 2019, Sun 2022) for multidimensional potential energy surfaces within the FBD framework.
