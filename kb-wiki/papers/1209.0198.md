# Heavy flavor suppression in a dynamical QCD medium with finite magnetic mass

> Reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions require accurate computation of radiative energy loss. While all available energy loss calculations assume zero magnetic mass, in accordance with the one-loop perturbative calculations, different non-perturbative approaches report a non-zero magnetic mass at RHIC and LHC. We generalized the dynamical energy loss formalism, to consistently include a possibility for existence of non-zero magnetic screening. We show that this generalization indicates a fundamental constraint on electric to magnetic mass ratio, which appears to be supported by lattice QCD simulations. Jet suppression patterns, obtained from this newly developed generalization, show reasonable agreement with the available RHIC and LHC measurements.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the dynamical energy loss formalism to consistently include a non-zero magnetic mass, showing that this generalization imposes a fundamental constraint that magnetic mass cannot exceed electric mass (otherwise a quark jet would gain energy, violating the second law of thermodynamics). The resulting suppression patterns show "reasonable agreement" with RHIC and LHC data, but the agreement is robust across the allowed range of magnetic-to-electric mass ratios rather than being uniquely determined.

## Method

The author extends the previously developed dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) by modifying the effective cross-section in the radiative energy loss expression to include both electric screening mass μ_E and magnetic screening mass μ_M. The formalism incorporates finite magnetic mass into the gluon propagator, separating contributions from longitudinally (electric) and transversely (magnetic) polarized gluon exchanges. Numerical suppression predictions are then computed including multigluon fluctuations and path length fluctuations, and compared to experimental data from PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic-to-electric mass ratio from non-perturbative approaches at RHIC and LHC is constrained to 0.4 < μ_M/μ_E < 0.6
- Introducing finite magnetic mass reduces the radiative energy loss by 25% to 50% compared to the zero magnetic mass case
- For RHIC conditions, the calculation assumes temperature T = 225 MeV and constant coupling α_S = 0.3
- For LHC conditions, the calculation assumes temperature T = 290 MeV and constant coupling α_S = 0.25
- The path length assumed for energy loss calculations is L = 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) which abolished the static approximation used in earlier models such as GLV (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev), Gyulassy and Wang (1994), and Wiedemann (2000). The paper addresses the discrepancy between one-loop perturbative QCD calculations (which assume zero magnetic mass) and non-perturbative approaches such as Maezawa (2011) that report non-zero magnetic mass at RHIC and LHC. The constraint μ_M < μ_E derived here is supported by lattice QCD simulations. This work enables more reliable jet suppression predictions for heavy flavor (charm and bottom quarks) at both RHIC and LHC energies, with the dynamical formalism showing significantly improved agreement with experimental data compared to static medium approximations.
