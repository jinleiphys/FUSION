# Gravitational form factor of the kaon in holographic QCD

> The gravitational form factor (GFF) of the kaon is investigated in a bottom-up holographic QCD model, in which the strange quark mass breaks the SU(3) flavor symmetry. The probe energy ($Q^2$) dependence of the kaon GFF is explicitly shown and compared to that of the pion. It is presented that our result shows the $1/Q^2$ behavior in the high energy region, which is consistent with the perturbative QCD prediction. The gravitational radius of the kaon is also calculated, and it is found that the result is quite close to that of the pion but slightly smaller.

# Gravitational form factor of the kaon in holographic QCD

## Key claim
The kaon gravitational form factor (GFF) calculated in a bottom-up holographic QCD model shows a $1/Q^2$ behavior at high energy consistent with perturbative QCD, and the resulting gravitational radius of the kaon is slightly smaller than that of the pion, indicating a quite small SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effect despite the mass differences between up/down and strange quarks.

## Method
The authors employ a hard-wall bottom-up holographic QCD model with SU(3)L × SU(3)R flavor symmetry broken by the strange quark mass. They compute the three-point correlation function involving two axial-vector currents and the energy-momentum tensor, from which the kaon GFF A_K(Q^2) is extracted using the bulk-to-boundary propagator of the graviton in the transverse-traceless gauge. The model parameters (m_q = 8.31 MeV, m_s = 188.5 MeV, σ = (213.7 MeV)^3, z_0 = (322.5 MeV)^{-1}) are taken from a prior fit to hadron masses and decay constants by Abidin (2009).

## Key numbers
- Kaon mass M_K = 495.7 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 104 MeV, used to determine the strange quark mass m_s = 188.5 MeV
- Pion mass M_π = 139.6 MeV and pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, used to determine m_q = 8.31 MeV and σ_q = (213.7 MeV)^3
- In the high energy limit, Q^2 A_K(Q^2) = 16π^2 f_K^2 = 1.71 GeV^2
- Gravitational radius of the kaon: ⟨r_K^2⟩_grav = 0.12 (fm)^2 = (0.35 fm)^2
- For comparison, the charge radius of the kaon is ⟨r_K^2⟩_E = (0.56 ± 0.031 fm)^2 (Particle Data Group 2022)
- For comparison, the preceding study result for the pion gravitational radius is ⟨r_π^2⟩_grav = (0.36 fm)^2 (Abidin 2008)

## Context
This work extends the bottom-up holographic QCD analysis of gravitational form factors from the pion (Abidin 2008) and proton (Abidin 2009) to the kaon, following the SU(3) flavor-broken extension of the model by Abidin (2009). The results are consistent with lattice QCD calculations for the pion and proton GFFs (Hackett 2023). The study enables future comparisons with experimental data from the Electron-Ion Colliders (EICs), which will probe kaon structure and test the small SU(3) breaking effect predicted here.
