# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> The main progress in the field of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, which we have seen in recent years, is the construction of some very quantitative (high-quality/high-precision) NN potentials. These potentials will serve as excellent input for microscopic nuclear structure calculations and will allow for a systematic investigation of off-shell effects. After this enormous quantitative work, it is now time to re-think the NN problem in fundamental terms. We need a derivation of the nuclear force which observes Lorentz invariance and the symmetries of QCD.

# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

## Key claim

The paper argues that while recent high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials fit scattering data with near-perfect accuracy, it is now necessary to derive the nuclear force from first principles that respect Lorentz invariance and the symmetries of QCD, particularly chiral symmetry. The author claims that a relativistic chiral Lagrangian including vector bosons is required to achieve a truly fundamental NN interaction, but notes that this program remains largely incomplete and faces significant challenges.

## Method

The paper reviews the current status of high-precision NN potentials (Nijmegen, Argonne, and CD-Bonn) and compares their predictions for deuteron properties and triton binding energy using charge-dependent Faddeev calculations. It then discusses the theoretical framework for constructing a chiral Lagrangian that includes pions, nucleons, rho and omega mesons, following the work of Weinberg (1968) and Callan, Coleman, Wess, and Zumino (1969), and proposes an expansion in powers of fields and their derivatives as a systematic approach to derive a relativistic NN model consistent with chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The Nijmegen phase-shift analysis of all pp and np data below 350 MeV lab energy achieved a chi-squared per datum of 0.99 for 4301 data points (Stoks 1993).
- The CD-Bonn, Nijmegen-II, and Reid'93 potentials each have a chi-squared per datum of 1.03; the Argonne V18 potential has 1.09.
- The deuteron quadrupole moment predicted by all high-precision potentials is 0.270 fm^2 (CD-Bonn, Nijm-II, Reid'93, V18), compared to the experimental value of 0.276(3) fm^2.
- The deuteron D-state probability is 4.83% for the non-local CD-Bonn potential versus approximately 5.7% for local potentials (Nijm-II: 5.64%, Reid'93: 5.70%, V18: 5.76%).
- The triton binding energy from non-relativistic Faddeev calculations is 8.00 MeV for CD-Bonn, compared to 7.62-7.63 MeV for local potentials; the experimental value is 8.48 MeV.
- A relativistic Faddeev calculation with CD-Bonn yields 8.19 MeV triton binding.
- The non-locality in CD-Bonn explains 44% of the 0.86 MeV gap between local potential predictions (7.62 MeV) and experiment (8.48 MeV).
- The pion decay constant F_pi is approximately 93 MeV; the axial coupling constant g_A is approximately 1.26.
- The Skyrme model pi-NN form factor is compared to monopole form factors with cutoff masses Lambda = 0.8 GeV and 1.7 GeV.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on high-precision NN potentials developed by the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1994), the Argonne group (Wiringa, Smith, and Stoks 1995), and the Bonn group (CD-Bonn, Machleidt, Sammarruca, and Song 1996). It contrasts these phenomenological potentials with the chiral perturbation theory approach initiated by Weinberg (1990) and implemented by the Texas group (Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck 1994), which used 26 parameters to achieve only qualitative fits. The paper calls for a relativistic chiral Lagrangian approach that includes vector bosons, building on the work of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996), and identifies the need to systematically identify higher-order chiral terms and test them against NN scattering data.
