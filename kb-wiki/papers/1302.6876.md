# Systematics of Evaluated Half-Lives of Double-Beta Decay

> A new evaluation of 2beta-decay half lives and their systematics is presented. These data extend the previous evaluation and include the analysis of all recent measurements. The nuclear matrix elements for 2beta-decay transitions in 12 nuclei have been extracted. The recommended values are compared with the large-scale shell-model, QRPA calculations, and experimental data. A T2nu 1/2 ~ 1/E8 systematic trend has been observed for recommended 128,130Te values. This trend indicates similarities for nuclear matrix elements in Te nuclei and was predicted for 2beta(2nu)-decay mode. The complete list of results is available online at http://www.nndc.bnl.gov/bbdecay/.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated evaluation of double-beta decay half-lives and nuclear matrix elements for 12 nuclei, claiming a systematic trend of \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu} \sim 1/E^8\) for the recommended \(^{128,130}\)Te values. However, this trend is derived from a ratio of central values and is described as an approximation (the exponent is 7.9, rounded to 8), and the author notes that the tellurium half-lives are based on geochemical measurements that combine both two-neutrino and neutrinoless modes, so the systematic trend is inferred rather than directly measured.

## Method

The author compiled experimental double-beta decay half-lives from the literature, including recent measurements, and produced recommended values following US Nuclear Data Program guidelines. Nuclear matrix elements for two-neutrino double-beta decay were extracted from the evaluated half-lives using phase-space factors from the Yale group (Kotila and Iachello, 2012). The recommended values were compared with large-scale shell-model calculations (Caurier et al., 2012) and quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations (Radenkovic et al., 2012), and the half-life systematics of \(^{128,130}\)Te were analyzed to identify an energy-dependent trend.

## Key numbers

- Recommended half-life for \(^{100}\)Mo \(2\beta^{-}\) (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \((6.98 \pm 0.44) \times 10^{18}\) y.
- Recommended half-life for \(^{130}\)Te \(2\beta^{-}\) (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \((7.14 \pm 1.04) \times 10^{20}\) y.
- Recommended half-life for \(^{136}\)Xe \(2\beta^{-}\) (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \((2.34 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{21}\) y.
- Recommended half-life for \(^{76}\)Ge \(2\beta^{-}\) (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \((1.43 \pm 0.53) \times 10^{21}\) y.
- Effective nuclear matrix element for \(^{100}\)Mo (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \(0.208 \pm 0.007\) (dimensionless).
- Effective nuclear matrix element for \(^{136}\)Xe (0\(^+\) → 0\(^+\)): \(0.0173 \pm 0.0005\) (dimensionless).
- Ratio of \(^{128}\)Te to \(^{130}\)Te half-lives: approximately \(4.9 \times 10^3\), corresponding to a \(\sim 1/E^{7.9}\) trend.

## Context

This work extends the previous evaluation by Tretyak and Zdesenko (2002) and the ongoing NNDC compilation (Pritychenko, 2006), incorporating recent measurements such as those for \(^{136}\)Xe from 2011. The recommended values and extracted nuclear matrix elements provide a benchmark for theoretical models, including large-scale shell-model calculations (Caurier et al., 2012) and QRPA calculations (Radenkovic et al., 2012). The observed \(1/E^8\) trend for tellurium isotopes supports the theoretical prediction by Primakoff and Rosen (1969) that the phase space for two-neutrino double-beta decay is roughly proportional to the 8th through 11th power of the energy release, and it enables improved understanding of the interplay between phase factors and nuclear matrix elements for future theoretical and experimental work.
