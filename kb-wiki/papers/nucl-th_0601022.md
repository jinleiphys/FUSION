# Properties of a protoneutron star in the Effective Field Theory

> The complete form of the equation of state of strangeness rich proto-neutron and neutron star matter has been obtained. The currently obtained lower value of the Lambda Lambda potential at the level of 5 MeV permits the existence of additional parameter set which reproduces the weaker Lambda Lambda interaction. The effects of the strength of hyperon-hyperon interactions on the equations of state constructed for the chosen parameter set have been analyzed. It has been shown that replacing the strong Y-Y interaction model by the weak one introduces large differences in the composition of a proto-neutron star matter both in the strange and non-strange sectors. Also concentrations of neutrinos have been significantly altered in proto-neutron star interiors. The performed calculations have indicated that the change of the hyperon-hyperon coupling constants affects the value of the proto-neutron star maximum mass.

# Properties of a protoneutron star in the Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that replacing the strong hyperon-hyperon (Y-Y) interaction model (with a ΛΛ potential of approximately 20 MeV) by a weak Y-Y interaction model (with a ΛΛ potential at the level of 5 MeV) introduces large differences in the composition of protoneutron star matter and significantly alters neutrino concentrations, while also affecting the maximum mass of the protoneutron star. The claim is conditional on the choice of the G2 parameter set from the Furnstahl-Serot-Tang effective field theory Lagrangian.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for strangeness-rich protoneutron star matter using a relativistic mean field approach based on the chiral effective Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (FST) with the G2 parameter set. The model includes the full octet of baryons interacting through σ, ω, ρ mesons plus two hidden-strangeness mesons (σ* and φ) to reproduce hyperon-hyperon interactions. The equations of state were solved in the mean field approximation under conditions of charge neutrality and β-equilibrium, with trapped neutrinos (Y_L = 0.4) and specified entropy values, for four evolutionary stages from the post-bounce phase to the cold deleptonized object. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation was then solved to obtain mass-radius relations for both strong and weak Y-Y interaction parameter sets.

## Key numbers
- The Λ hyperon potential depth in saturated nuclear matter is constrained to U^(N)_Λ(ρ₀) ≈ 27-28 MeV
- The Ξ hyperon potential depth in saturated nuclear matter is constrained to U^(N)_Ξ(ρ₀) ≈ 18-20 MeV
- The weak ΛΛ interaction corresponds to U^(Λ)_Λ ≈ 5 MeV (from Takahashi et al.), while the strong ΛΛ interaction corresponds to U^(Λ)_Λ ≈ 20 MeV (from Schaffner et al.)
- For the strong Y-Y interaction model at T=0: maximum mass configuration A has ρ/ρ₀ = 5.00, radius R = 12.73 km, gravitational mass M = 1.298 M⊙, baryonic mass M_B = 1.407 M⊙; configuration B has ρ/ρ₀ = 21.76, R = 8.22 km, M = 1.320 M⊙, M_B = 1.634 M⊙
- For the strong Y-Y interaction model at s=2: ρ/ρ₀ = 8.76, R = 13.528 km, M = 1.670 M⊙, M_B = 1.859 M⊙
- For the weak Y-Y interaction model at T=0: ρ/ρ₀ = 8.00, R = 11.63 km, M = 1.527 M⊙, M_B = 1.749 M⊙
- For the weak Y-Y interaction model at s=2: ρ/ρ₀ = 9.12, R = 13.14 km, M = 1.754 M⊙, M_B = 1.916 M⊙
- The saturation density ρ₀ is approximately 2.5 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean field approach of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) to protoneutron star matter including hyperons, building on the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interaction studies by Schaffner et al. and Gal et al. The paper uses the experimentally determined weaker ΛΛ interaction from Takahashi et al. (2001) to construct an alternative parameter set, contrasting with the stronger interaction previously assumed. The results enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star evolution and cooling, particularly regarding the role of strangeness in determining maximum masses and the possibility of black hole formation during deleptonization. The authors note that the existence and stability of an additional branch in the mass-radius relation for the strong Y-Y interaction model requires further investigation.
