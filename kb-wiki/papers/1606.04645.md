# Nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan process

> We study the nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan process using quark parton distribution functions calculated in a microscopic nuclear model which takes into account the effects of Fermi motion, nuclear binding and nucleon correlations through a relativistic nucleon spectral function. The contributions of $π$ and $ρ$ mesons as well as shadowing effects are also included. The beam energy loss is calculated using a phenomenological approach. The present theoretical results are compared with the experimental results of E772 and E886 experiments. These results are applicable to the forthcoming experimental analysis of E906 Sea Quest experiment at Fermi Lab.

## Key claim
The paper claims that nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan processes can be described using a microscopic nuclear model based on a relativistic nucleon spectral function, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the phenomenological treatment of beam energy loss, where the parameter alpha in the linear energy loss model is set to 1, though other values (0.7 to 1.27) have been found in the literature when shadowing is included.

## Method
The authors calculate Drell-Yan cross sections for proton-nucleus scattering using a relativistic nucleon spectral function that incorporates Fermi motion, nuclear binding, and nucleon correlations via the Lehmann representation of the Dirac propagator in nuclear matter, with local density approximation applied for finite nuclei. They add contributions from pion and rho meson clouds using the imaginary parts of the meson propagators, include shadowing effects following the model of Kulagin and Petti, and incorporate beam energy loss using a phenomenological linear model where the fractional parton energy shift is Delta x_b = alpha * <L>_A / E_p with alpha = 1. The results are compared with data from the E772 and E866 experiments for targets including carbon, calcium, iron, and tungsten at beam energies of 800 GeV and 120 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy of 800 GeV corresponds to center-of-mass energy per nucleon sqrt(s_N) = 38.8 GeV
- Beam energy of 120 GeV corresponds to sqrt(s_N) = 15 GeV
- For the linear energy loss model, the parameter alpha is set to 1, with literature values ranging from 1.27 to 1.99 GeV/fm without shadowing and 0.7 to 1.27 with shadowing
- For the quadratic energy loss model, the parameter gamma ranges from 0.2 to 0.3 GeV/fm^2
- For the Gavin-Milana model, beta = 0.0004
- Results are presented for nuclear targets: Be-9, C-12, Ca-40, Fe-56, and W-184
- The kinematic region covers x_t > 0.1, extending up to x_t = 0.45
- For the E866 comparison, the kinematic cuts are 0.01 < x_t < 0.12, 0.21 < x_b < 0.95, and 0.13 < x_F < 0.93
- The meson-nucleon form factor cutoff parameters Lambda_pi and Lambda_rho are both set to 1 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic nuclear model, which was successfully used to describe deep inelastic scattering of charged leptons and neutrinos from nuclei (Haider 2015, Haider 2016), to the Drell-Yan process. The model addresses the long-standing difficulty noted by some authors (Scheinbein 2008) in providing a consistent description of both DIS and Drell-Yan data using the same set of nuclear parton distribution functions, while others (Eskola 2009) have succeeded. The results are applicable to the forthcoming E906 SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab and enable comparisons between different phenomenological models of beam energy loss, such as those by Duan (2005), Johnson (2002), Garvey and Peng (2003), and Gavin and Milana.
