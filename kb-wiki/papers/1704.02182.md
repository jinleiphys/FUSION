# Effects of pairing correlations on the neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy

> We investigated effects of pairing correlations on the neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy of finite nuclei. In this calculation we used Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with Skyrme forces and effective pairing interactions. The results have been compared with available experimental data, Hartree-Fock (HF) results as well as the predictions by droplet model (DM). Finally, our discussion was extended to study of the pairing interaction in nuclear matter. Roles of isospin T = 0 pairing in the nuclear matter were also discussed.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations have a noticeable effect on the neutron skin thickness (NST) and the symmetry energy coefficient in open-shell nuclei, but the effect is almost negligible in doubly magic nuclei; the mean-field models that include full shell and pairing effects systematically give larger NST but smaller symmetry energy and coefficient compared to the droplet model.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with three Skyrme functionals (SkI3, SLy4, SVI) and a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) for the like-pairing field, using a box-discretized method with a box size of 20 fm and mesh size of 0.1 fm. They computed the neutron skin thickness as the difference between neutron and proton root-mean-square radii for isotopic chains of Pb and Sn, and extracted the symmetry energy and symmetry energy coefficient from the Skyrme functional. The results were compared with Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations without pairing and with predictions from the droplet model (DM), and the analysis was extended to infinite nuclear matter using the BCS approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the DDDI pairing parameters: \(v_0 = -458\ \mathrm{MeV/fm^3}\), \(\eta = 0.71\), \(\alpha = 0.59\), \(\rho_0 = 0.08\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\) (determined for Sn isotopes)
- Quasiparticle energy cut-off: \(E_{cut} = 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- For SLy5 functional with mixed-type pairing (\(\eta = 0.5\)): \(v_0 = -325\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm^3}\); for volume-type (\(\eta = 0\)): \(v_0 = -218\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm^3}\)
- The slope parameter \(L\) for SLy4 interaction is fixed at \(L = 100.53\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- For the Sn isotope \(A = 120\), the pairing gap used to calibrate the SLy5 pairing strength is \(\Delta \simeq 1.321\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- The pairing effects in infinite matter change the energy per nucleon by less than a few electron-volt (eV) for the standard strength \(v_0\)

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of the neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy, such as those by Centelles (2008) and Warda (2009), by including pairing correlations self-consistently via the HFB method. The results show that the droplet model, which lacks shell and pairing effects, systematically overestimates the symmetry energy and underestimates the NST compared to the full mean-field calculations. The paper enables more refined constraints on the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy by demonstrating that pairing correlations, particularly in open-shell nuclei, must be accounted for when relating NST measurements to nuclear matter properties.
