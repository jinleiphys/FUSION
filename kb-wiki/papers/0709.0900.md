# Isospin-dependent properties of asymmetric nuclear matter in relativistic mean-field models

> Using various relativistic mean-field models, including the nonlinear ones with meson field self-interactions, those with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, and the point-coupling models without meson fields, we have studied the isospin-dependent bulk and single-particle properties of asymmetric nuclear matter. In particular, we have determined the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy from these different relativistic mean-field models and compare the results with the constraints recently extracted from analyses of experimental data on isospin diffusion and isotopic scaling in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions as well as from measured isotopic dependence of the giant monopole resonances in even-A Sn isotopes. Among the 23 parameter sets in the relativistic mean-filed model that are commonly used for nuclear structure studies, only a few are found to give symmetry energies that are consistent with the empirical constraints. We have also studied the nuclear symmetry potential and the isospin-splitting of the nucleon effective mass in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. We find that both the momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential at fixed baryon density and the isospin-splitting of the nucleon effective mass in neutron-rich nuclear matter depend not only on the nuclear interactions but also on the definition of the nucleon optical potential.

## Key claim

Among the 23 commonly used relativistic mean-field parameter sets, only a few produce symmetry energies consistent with empirical constraints from isospin diffusion data, isoscaling analyses, and giant monopole resonance measurements; the momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential and the isospin-splitting of the nucleon effective mass depend not only on the nuclear interactions but also on the definition of the nucleon optical potential.

## Method

The authors systematically calculated the isospin-dependent bulk and single-particle properties of asymmetric nuclear matter using 23 parameter sets across three versions of the relativistic mean-field model: nonlinear models with meson field self-interactions, models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, and point-coupling models without meson fields. They determined the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy from each model and compared it with empirical constraints extracted from isospin diffusion data (using the IBUU04 transport model), isoscaling analyses of isotope ratios, and isotopic dependence of giant monopole resonances in even-A Sn isotopes. They also computed the nuclear symmetry potential using three different definitions of the non-relativistic optical potential (Schrödinger-equivalent potential, optical potential from total energy difference, and second-order Dirac potential) and evaluated the isospin-splitting of the Dirac mass, Landau mass, and Lorentz mass.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³) is known to be around 30 MeV from the empirical liquid-drop mass formula.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density has been determined to be 231 ± 5 MeV from nuclear giant monopole resonance measurements.
- The isospin diffusion data are consistent with a density-dependent symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) ≈ 31.6(ρ/ρ₀)^γ with γ = 0.69–1.05 at subnormal density, leading to a slope parameter L = 88 ± 25 MeV and an isospin-dependent incompressibility K_asy = –500 ± 50 MeV.
- The isoscaling analyses give E_sym(ρ) = 31.6(ρ/ρ₀)^0.69, corresponding to L ≈ 65 MeV and K_asy ≈ –453 MeV.
- The giant monopole resonance measurements in even-A Sn isotopes give K_asy = –550 ± 100 MeV.
- The empirical Lane potential from nucleon-nucleus scattering and (p,n) charge-exchange reactions at beam energies up to about 100 MeV is parametrized as U_sym = a – b E_kin with a ≈ 22–34 MeV and b ≈ 0.1–0.2.
- The Dirac mass in finite nuclei is constrained to 0.55 M ≤ M*_Dirac ≤ 0.6 M from spin-orbit partner state energy spacings.
- The nucleon Landau mass from non-relativistic effective interactions is M*_Landau/M = 0.8 ± 0.1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin-dependent nuclear matter properties using relativistic mean-field models (Walecka 1974; Serot and Walecka 1986; Reinhard 1989; Ring 1996) and compares with constraints from heavy-ion collision experiments (Tsang et al. 2004; Chen et al. 2005; Li et al. 2005) and giant monopole resonance measurements (Garg et al. 2007). It also relates to earlier Skyrme interaction studies (Chen et al. 2005; Steiner et al. 2005) that found only 4 of 21 Skyrme parameter sets consistent with the same empirical constraints. The results enable further refinement of nuclear effective interactions and provide benchmarks for future radioactive beam experiments at facilities such as FAIR/GSI, SPIRAL2/GANIL, and FRIB.
