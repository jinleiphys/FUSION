# Effects of $Z_b$ states and bottom meson loops on $Υ(4S) \to Υ(1S,2S) π^+π^-$ transitions

> We study the dipion transitions $Υ(4S) \rightarrow Υ(nS) π^+π^-$ $(n=1,2)$. In particular, we consider the effects of the two intermediate bottomoniumlike exotic states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ as well as bottom meson loops. The strong pion-pion final-state interactions, especially including channel coupling to $K\bar{K}$ in the $S$-wave, are taken into account model-independently by using dispersion theory. Based on a nonrelativistic effective field theory we find that the contribution from the bottom meson loops is comparable to those from the chiral contact terms and the $Z_b$-exchange terms. For the $Υ(4S) \rightarrow Υ(2S) π^+π^-$ decay, the result shows that including the effects of the $Z_b$-exchange and the bottom meson loops can naturally reproduce the two-hump behavior of the $ππ$ mass spectra. Future angular distribution data are decisive for the identification of different production mechanisms. For the $Υ(4S) \rightarrow Υ(1S) π^+π^-$ decay, we show that there is a narrow dip around 1 GeV in the $ππ$ invariant mass distribution, caused by the final-state interactions. The distribution is clearly different from that in similar transitions from lower $Υ$ states, and needs to be verified by future data with high statistics. Also we predict the decay width and the dikaon mass distribution of the $Υ(4S) \rightarrow Υ(1S) K^+ K^-$ process.

## Key claim

The two-hump structure in the $\pi\pi$ mass spectrum of $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$ can be reproduced only when both $Z_b$-exchange and bottom meson loop contributions are included alongside the chiral contact terms; for $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(1S) \pi^+\pi^-$, a narrow dip near 1 GeV in the $\pi\pi$ invariant mass distribution is predicted, caused by final-state interactions, but this requires future high-statistics data for verification.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level amplitudes from chiral contact terms and $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ exchange, plus box diagrams with intermediate bottom mesons ($B$, $B^*$, $B_s$, $B_s^*$) using a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) with power counting in the heavy-meson velocity. Strong pion-pion final-state interactions in the $S$-wave, including coupled-channel effects from $K\bar{K}$, were treated model-independently via dispersion theory with modified Omnès solutions; $D$-wave FSI was treated in a single-channel elastic approximation. The subtraction functions in the dispersive integrals were fixed by matching to the chiral contact terms at low energies.

## Key numbers

*   $F_\pi = 92.2\ \text{MeV}$ and $F_K = 113.0\ \text{MeV}$ used as the pseudo-Goldstone boson decay constants.
*   $g_\pi = 0.5$ (from lattice QCD, precise value $0.492 \pm 0.029$) used for the axial coupling of Goldstone bosons to bottom mesons.
*   $g_{JHH(4S)} = 1.43\ \text{GeV}^{-3/2}$ extracted from the measured open-bottom decay widths of $\Upsilon(4S)$.
*   The $Z_b$ widths (order $10\ \text{MeV}$) are neglected as much smaller than the gap between $Z_b$ masses and $\Upsilon(lS)\pi$ thresholds.
*   The dispersive integrals for both $S$-wave and $D$-wave amplitudes require three subtractions to converge, based on the high-energy behavior of the Omnès functions and the inhomogeneities.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $\Upsilon(3S) \to \Upsilon(1S) \pi\pi$ (Chen et al., 2016), where $Z_b$ exchange and $\pi\pi$ FSI explained the two-hump anomaly that the QCD multipole expansion method (Voloshin, 1980; Novikov, 1981; Kuang, 1981) could not reproduce. It also builds on earlier proposals of an isovector $b\bar b q\bar q$ state (Voloshin, 1982; Anisovich, 1995). The inclusion of bottom meson loops is novel for $\Upsilon(4S)$ decays, as the $\Upsilon(4S)$ lies above $B\bar{B}$ threshold. The results enable future Belle-II data to discriminate between production mechanisms via angular distributions, and predict the $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(1S) K^+ K^-$ decay width and dikaon mass distribution.
