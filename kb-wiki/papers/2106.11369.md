# The strong CP problem solved by itself due to long-distance vacuum effects

> The vacuum of quantum chromodynamics has an incredibly rich structure at the nonperturbative level, which is intimately connected with the topology of gauge fields, and put to a test by the strong CP problem. We investigate the long-distance properties of the theory in the presence of the topological $θ$ term. This is done on the lattice, using the gradient flow to isolate the long-distance modes in the functional integral measure and tracing it over successive length scales. The key point is that the vacuum splits into disconnected topological sectors with markedly different physical characteristics, which gives rise to a nontrivial behavior depending on $θ$. We find that the color fields produced by quarks and gluons are screened, and confinement is lost, for bare vacuum angles $|θ| > 0$, thus providing a natural solution of the strong CP problem. The renormalized vacuum angle $θ$ is found to flow to zero in the infrared limit, leading to a self-consistent solution within QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strong CP problem is solved naturally within QCD because the renormalized vacuum angle \(\theta\) flows to zero in the infrared limit, and that for any bare \(|\theta| > 0\) the color fields are screened and confinement is lost, so that only \(\theta = 0\) is physically allowed in the confining phase.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory using the plaquette action at \(\beta = 6.0\) on \(16^4\), \(24^4\), and \(32^4\) lattices, with lattice spacing \(a = 0.082(2)\,\mathrm{fm}\). They applied the gradient flow to isolate long-distance modes and traced the functional integral measure over successive length scales, measuring the energy density, the running coupling, the topological susceptibility, and the Polyakov loop susceptibility as functions of flow time and topological charge \(Q\). The \(\theta\)-dependence was obtained by discrete Fourier transform of the charge-dependent observables weighted by the topological charge distribution \(P(Q)\).

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.082(2)\,\mathrm{fm}\), set using \(\sqrt{t_0} = 0.147(4)\,\mathrm{fm}\) with \(t_0\) defined by \(t_0^2 \langle E(t_0)\rangle = 0.3\).
* Topological susceptibility: \(\sqrt{t_0}\,\chi_t^{1/4} = 0.162(3)\), agreeing with the Witten-Veneziano relation and with a prior result \(\sqrt{t_0}\,\chi_t^{1/4}=0.161(4)\) from Ce et al. (2015).
* Polyakov loop susceptibility at \(\theta=0\): \(\chi_P = 0.289(7)\), consistent with a 2D Gaussian distribution value of \(4/\pi - 1 = 0.273\).
* Gradient flow coupling in the infrared: \(\alpha_{GF}(\mu) = \Lambda_{GF}^2 / \mu^2\) with \(\sqrt{t_0}\,\Lambda_{GF} = 0.475(16)\).
* String tension: \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 445(19)\,\mathrm{MeV}\), derived from \(\sigma = \frac{2}{3}\Lambda_V^2\) with \(\sqrt{t_0}\,\Lambda_V = 0.406(14)\).
* Screening radius: \(\lambda_S \approx 0.31/|\theta|\,\mathrm{[fm]}\) from the running coupling analysis.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing idea from Callan, Dashen, and Gross (1979) and 't Hooft (1981) that a \(\theta\) term may change the phase of QCD by screening color fields, and it provides the first direct lattice derivation of the phase structure. It also connects to the dual superconductor model of confinement, where monopoles acquire fractional electric charge proportional to \(\theta\) as noted by Witten (1979). The result that \(\theta\) flows to zero in the infrared parallels the behavior of the Hall conductivity in the quantum Hall effect, as described by the \(CP^{N-1}\) model at large \(N\) (Pruisken, 2000), and matches analytical RG studies by Reuter (1996). The work enables future studies of the \(\theta\)-dependence of hadron observables and confirms that the gradient flow is a powerful tool for multi-scale problems in nonperturbative QCD.
