# Neutrino versus antineutrino cross sections and CP violation

> We discuss the nuclear interactions of neutrinos versus those of antineutrinos, a relevant comparison for CP violation experiments in the neutrino sector. We consider the MiniBooNE quasielastic-like double differential neutrinos and antineutrinos cross sections which are flux dependent and hence specific to the MiniBooNE set-up. We combine them introducing their sum and their difference. We show that the last combination can bring a general information, which can be exploited in other experiments, on the nuclear matrix elements of the axial vector interference term. Our theoretical model reproduces well the two cross sections combinations. This confirms the need for a sizeable multinucleon component in particular in the interference term.

## Key claim

The difference between neutrino and antineutrino double differential cross sections on carbon, as measured by MiniBooNE, is dominated by the axial vector interference term even when the normalized neutrino and antineutrino flux profiles are not identical; this allows experimental access to the nuclear matrix elements of that term, which is essential for mastering the nuclear background to CP violation measurements.

## Method

The authors combine the MiniBooNE flux-folded double differential quasielastic-like cross sections for muon neutrinos and antineutrinos on 12C into a sum (S) and a difference (D). They evaluate these combinations using their theoretical model, which incorporates the multinucleon emission component (np-nh) and treats nuclear responses in the random phase approximation (RPA). They compare calculations using the real MiniBooNE fluxes against calculations using only the mean flux to isolate the role of the axial vector interference term, and then compare the full model predictions to the MiniBooNE experimental data points.

## Key numbers

* The MiniBooNE flux difference Φ− changes sign from negative at low neutrino energies to positive above approximately 0.7 GeV.
* For the difference D, the mean-flux approximation and the real-flux calculation are quite close up to a muon kinetic energy of about 0.6 to 0.7 GeV; beyond this energy they progressively depart.
* The complete RPA evaluation including the multinucleon emission channel reproduces the MiniBooNE data for both the sum S and the difference D across the full range of muon kinetic energies and emission angles; the quasielastic-only calculation (without multinucleon component) fails to account for the data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the multinucleon component in neutrino (Martini 2011) and antineutrino (Martini 2013) cross sections separately, now directly comparing the two to address the needs of CP violation experiments. It confirms the necessity of a sizeable multinucleon component in the axial vector interference term, as originally argued in Martini (2010). The analysis enables future investigations of the Q2 distribution of the axial vector interference term in other experiments such as MINERvA (Fiorentini 2013, Fields 2013) and T2K (Abe 2012), where the normalized neutrino and antineutrino flux difference is even smaller.
