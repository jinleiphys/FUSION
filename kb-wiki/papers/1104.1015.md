# Measured g factors and the tidal-wave description of transitional nuclei near A = 100

> The transient-field technique has been used in both conventional kinematics and inverse kinematics to measure the g factors of the 2+ states in the stable even isotopes of Ru, Pd and Cd. The statistical precision of the g(2+) values has been significantly improved, allowing a critical comparison with the tidal-wave version of the cranking model recently proposed for transitional nuclei in this region.

## Key claim

The paper presents significantly improved statistical precision for the g factors of the 2+ states in stable even isotopes of Ru, Pd, and Cd, enabling a critical comparison with the tidal-wave version of the cranking model for transitional nuclei near A = 100, though the absolute g-factor scale depends on calibration to previous measurements on 106Pd.

## Method

The transient-field technique was employed in both conventional kinematics (using 95 MeV 32S beams to Coulomb excite targets) and inverse kinematics (using approximately 2.3 MeV/A beams of the isotopes themselves on carbon or magnesium targets). The precession of gamma-ray angular correlations was measured in coincidence with backscattered or forward-scattered particles using HPGe and NaI detectors, with the ferromagnetic layer (iron for conventional, gadolinium for inverse kinematics) magnetized by an external 0.09 T field. The Rutgers parametrization of the transient-field strength was used to calibrate absolute g factors, with the velocity dependence verified for Pd ions in gadolinium.

## Key numbers

- g(2+1; 110Cd) = +0.404(29), measured relative to 116Cd with improved precision
- g(2+1; 112Cd) = +0.356(24), measured in conventional kinematics
- g(2+1; 114Cd) = +0.321(21), measured in conventional kinematics
- g(2+1; 116Cd) = +0.292(24), used as reference for relative measurements
- g(2+1; 100Ru) = +0.434(23), measured in conventional kinematics with Rutgers parametrization
- g(2+1; 102Ru) = +0.457(12), measured in conventional kinematics
- g(2+1; 104Ru) = +0.401(12), measured in conventional kinematics
- g(2+1; 112Cd) = +0.365(30) and g(2+1; 114Cd) = +0.313(25) from inverse kinematics measurements, used to set absolute scale
- Transient-field strength for Pd ions in gadolinium required scaling the Rutgers parametrization parameter aTF by a factor of about 1.4 for Target I

## Context

This work extends previous g-factor measurements on Mo isotopes by Man (2001) and on neutron-rich fission fragments by Smith (2004, 2005), where reduced g-factor magnitudes were attributed to h11/2 neutron contributions. The improved precision allows critical comparison with the tidal-wave cranking model proposed by Frauendorf, Sun, and Gu for transitional nuclei with 44 ≤ Z ≤ 48 and 54 ≤ N ≤ 68, which had previously been shown to describe yrast energies and B(E2) values well. The results enable examination of how angular momentum is shared between protons and neutrons in these vibrational-to-rotational transitional nuclei, and confirm the Rutgers parametrization of transient-field strength for ions with Z ~ 46-48 traversing iron foils at velocities between 2v0 and 4v0.
