# High-spin intruder states in the fp shell nuclei and isoscalar proton-neutron correlations

> We perform a systematic shell-model and mean-field study of fully-aligned, high-spin f_{7/2}^{n} seniority isomers and d_{3/2}^{-1} f_{7/2}^{n+1} intruder states in the A~44 nuclei from the lower-fp shell. The shell-model calculations are performed in the full sdfp configuration space allowing 1p-1h cross-shell excitations. The self-consistent mean-field calculations are based on the Hartree-Fock approach with the Skyrme energy density functional that reproduces empirical Landau parameters. While there is a nice agreement between experimental and theoretical relative energies of fully-aligned states in N>Z nuclei, this is no longer the case for the N=Z systems. The remaining deviation from the data is attributed to the isoscalar proton-neutron correlations. It is also demonstrated that the Coulomb corrections at high spins noticeably depend on the choice of the energy density functional.

## Key claim

The paper finds that shell-model and mean-field calculations agree well with experimental data for fully-aligned high-spin states in N>Z nuclei, but systematically deviate for N=Z systems; the remaining deviation is attributed to isoscalar proton-neutron correlations that are missing in both approaches due to truncation to 1p-1h cross-shell excitations.

## Method

The authors performed systematic shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code in the full sdfp configuration space limited to 1p-1h cross-shell excitations from the sd shell to the fp shell, using the FPD6 interaction for the fp-shell space. They also carried out self-consistent mean-field calculations based on the Hartree-Fock approach with modified Skyrme energy density functionals (SkO and SLy4 parameterizations) that reproduce empirical Landau parameters, with the spin-orbit strength reduced by 5% compared to the original SkO value.

## Key numbers

- In N>Z nuclei, the shell model systematically overestimates experimental data for the energy difference ΔE by approximately 280 keV
- In N=Z nuclei, the shell model systematically underestimates experimental data for ΔE by approximately 410 keV
- The self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations underestimate experimental ΔE values in N>Z nuclei by approximately 200 keV, giving an average offset of approximately 480 keV between the two models
- The Coulomb correction δE_C calculated in the SkO model is approximately 130 keV, while values for SLy4 are very small
- The isospin correlation energy δE_T(A) shows a strong decrease with increasing mass number A

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field analyses of maximum-spin states in the A~44 mass region by Zduńczuk (2005) and Satuła (2005), which demonstrated that constraining the Skyrme energy density functional to empirical spin-isospin Landau parameters could yield good agreement with data. The study enables further refinement of the isovector Landau parameter g'_0 and highlights the need for extended configuration spaces involving 2p-2h and higher cross-shell transitions, as well as a proper treatment of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei.
