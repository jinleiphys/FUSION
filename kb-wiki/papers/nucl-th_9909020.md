# Superdeformations in Relativistic and Non-Relativistic Mean Field Theories

> The applications of the extensions of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory to the rotating frame, such as cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory and cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, for the description of superdeformed bands in the $A\sim 60$, 140-150 and 190 mass regions are overviewed and compared briefly with the results obtained in non-relativistic mean field theories.

# Superdeformations in Relativistic and Non-Relativistic Mean Field Theories

## Key claim

Cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) and cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory reproduce the properties of superdeformed bands across the A~60, A~140-150, and A~190 mass regions at a level of agreement comparable to, and in many cases better than, non-relativistic mean field approaches, despite the fact that the relativistic Lagrangian parameters were fitted only to spherical nuclei and contain no information on single-particle properties at high spin.

## Method

The authors apply cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory, which solves the Dirac equation in the Hartree approximation with a Coriolis term, and cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, which adds pairing correlations via a finite-range Gogny force in the particle-particle channel together with approximate particle number projection by the Lipkin-Nogami method. The calculations use the NL1 parametrization of the relativistic Lagrangian and, for CRHB, the D1S set of Gogny force parameters; the spatial components of the vector meson fields (nuclear magnetism) are included self-consistently. Results are compared with non-relativistic approaches: cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS), cranked Hartree-Fock (CHF) with Skyrme forces, and cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) with Gogny or Skyrme forces.

## Key numbers

- In the A~140-150 region, CRMF calculations reproduce experimental effective alignments i_eff better on average than the CNS approach; the discrepancy at rotational frequencies Omega_x <= 0.5 MeV in the 149Gd(1) band is attributed to residual pairing correlations.
- In the A~60-70 region, the dynamic moment of inertia J^(2) of the SD band in 60Zn is only approximately 60% of the kinematic moment J^(1) at the highest rotational frequencies; the "maximum" spin of the [22,22]+ configuration is I=36+.
- In the A~190 region, CRHB calculations with no adjustable parameters reproduce the increase of J^(2) with rotational frequency; a small peak in J^(2) appears at Omega_x ~ 0.45 MeV in 192Pb and a plateau at Omega_x ~ 0.4 MeV in 196Pb.
- In the A~140-150 region, Skyrme-force CHF calculations overestimate the magnitude of J^(2) by typically 10%, while CRMF calculations give J^(2) values close to experiment.
- The effective mass in both relativistic and non-relativistic microscopic mean field approaches is typically in the range 0.6-0.7.

## Context

This review extends the systematic application of CRMF and CRHB theory to superdeformed bands across three mass regions, following earlier work by Koepf and Ring (1989, 1990) who first developed the cranked relativistic framework. The results are compared with non-relativistic calculations using the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky method of Bengtsson and Ragnarsson (1985) and the Woods-Saxon based approach of Nazarewicz et al. (1989), as well as with Skyrme-force CHF calculations by Dobaczewski and Dudek (1995) and Gogny-force CHFB calculations by Girod et al. (1994). The work enables systematic configuration assignments for superdeformed bands and provides a foundation for future studies of pairing correlations in the regime of weak pairing, where the Lipkin-Nogami method remains an open theoretical problem.
