# Radial flow has little effect on clusterization at intermediate energies in the framework of the Lattice Gas Model

> The Lattice Gas Model was extended to incorporate the effect of radial flow. Contrary to popular belief, radial flow has little effect on the clusterization process in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions except adding an ordered motion to the particles in the fragmentation source. We compared the results from the lattice gas model with and without radial flow to experimental data. We found that charge yields from central collisions are not significantly affected by inclusion of any reasonable radial flow.

## Key claim

Contrary to the suggestion of Kunde et al. (1995) that radial flow might explain the different charge yields observed in central versus peripheral collisions, the authors find that radial flow has little effect on clusterization in the lattice gas model; thermal motion is roughly 50 to 70 times more efficient at breaking bonds between neighboring nucleons than radial flow of the same energy.

## Method

The microcanonical lattice gas model was extended by adding a radial flow momentum to each nucleon after configuration sampling, with the flow momentum proportional to the nucleon's distance from the center of mass. Clusters were identified using the standard Coniglio-Klein prescription, where two neighboring nucleons belong to the same cluster if their pair energy (relative kinetic plus bond energy) is negative. The authors simulated two systems on cubic lattices: A=150, Z=60 on an 8^3 lattice (peripheral Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon) and A=320, Z=130 on a 10^3 lattice (central Au+Au at 100 MeV/nucleon), and compared charge yields with and without radial flow.

## Key numbers

- For the A=320 system, thermal energy of 13 AMeV produces an exponential charge yield; to match this yield by adding radial flow to a system with only 12 AMeV thermal energy requires about 72 AMeV of radial flow energy.
- The estimated ratio of bond-breaking efficiency (thermal motion vs. radial flow) from a continuous calculation is a factor of 45 for a 320-nucleon system; a more elaborate lattice estimate gives a factor of 50, while the simulation yields a factor of 72.
- The lattice spacing is (6.25)^(1/3) fm, so the maximum momentum difference from radial flow between nearest neighbors is c * l where |l| = (6.25)^(1/3) fm, independent of system size.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice gas model with radial flow (Das 2001) from a small A=84 system to much larger systems where experimental data from Kunde et al. (1995) exist. It directly challenges the interpretation of Kunde et al. (1995) that radial flow causes the transition from power-law to exponential charge yields. The result is consistent with the independent observation by Gulminelli and Chomaz (2004) in a canonical lattice gas model. The authors argue that their conclusion should hold for any thermal model that assumes sudden dissociation, and they suggest that future dynamical simulations (e.g., QMD with ECRA/SACA algorithms or BUU with stochastic Langevin methods) should confirm the inefficiency of radial flow in breaking clusters.
