# Test of Bowler-Sinyukov Treatment of Coulomb Interaction

> 6 pages, 2 figures, presented by R. Maj at the Fourth Workshop on Particle Correlations and Femtoscopy (WPCF2008), Cracow, Poland, September 11-14, 2008

# Test of Bowler-Sinyukov Treatment of Coulomb Interaction

## Key claim

The Bowler-Sinyukov procedure for removing Coulomb effects from two-particle correlation functions works very well for pion-pion correlations even with strongly anisotropic sources, but when a halo from long-lived resonances is included, the extracted correlation functions are distorted at small relative momenta, although the source parameters are still reproduced accurately. For kaon-kaon correlations, the procedure works significantly worse than for pion-pion correlations.

## Method

The authors computed Coulomb correlation functions for identical pions using the Koonin formula with a Lorentz-covariant Gaussian source function that allows anisotropic source sizes and finite lifetime. They then applied the Bowler-Sinyukov correction procedure (both the dilution method and the proper Bowler-Sinyukov method) to extract the free correlation function, and compared the extracted parameters to the known input source parameters. The study included sources with halo contributions from long-lived resonances, using the Bertsch-Pratt coordinate system (out, side, long).

## Key numbers

- The Bohr radius for the pion-pion system is 388 fm (eta^{-1} = 388 fm for pi pi)
- The free correlation function of two identical noninteracting bosons equals 2 at vanishing momentum (C_free(q=0) = 2)
- When halo is present, the measured correlation function at q=0 is 1 + lambda, where lambda = f^2 and f is the fraction of particles from the fireball (0 <= f <= 1)
- The effective radius used in the Bowler-Sinyukov correction factor is R = sqrt((R_o^2 + R_s^2 + R_l^2)/3)

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopy methodology used in heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, where the "HBT Puzzle" (the unexpectedly short emission time of about 1 fm/c) motivated a re-examination of the reliability of the femtoscopy method. The study is similar to that of Kisiel (2006) and tests the Bowler-Sinyukov procedure originally proposed by Bowler (1991) and Sinyukov (1998). The results enable continued use of the Bowler-Sinyukov correction for pion-pion correlations with confidence, while cautioning that the procedure is less reliable for kaon-kaon correlations.
