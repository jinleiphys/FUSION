# Soft Physics, Centrality and Multiplicity at RHIC

> The inclusive spectra so far accumulated at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at energies of $\sqrt{s} = 56, 130$ and 200 GeV are examined within the hadronic simulation LUCIFER. What emerges at this juncture is a comprehensive and clear picture of soft physics which apparently dominates the intermediate and later stages of the ion collisions. The focus is on energy and centrality dependence of the mid-rapidity charged spectra, using an analysis based for the most part on production and rescattering of intermediate generic resonances. The bosonic Rho and K(*)-like resonances, produced in initial nucleon-nucleon interactions and materialising only after some delay time, behave like an incompressible fluid with saturated number and energy density.

## Key claim

The observed charged particle spectra at RHIC can be described by a purely hadronic simulation (LUCIFER) without requiring a quark-gluon plasma, provided that the density of generic bosonic resonances (rho-like and K*-like) in the overlap region of the colliding nuclei is constrained to remain nearly constant, behaving like an incompressible fluid with saturated number and energy density.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic cascade simulation LUCIFER, which operates in two sequential phases: Phase I models initial nucleon-nucleon interactions on the light cone using inputs from NN data (total cross-sections, branching ratios, and multiplicity distributions obeying KNO scaling), while Phase II is a soft hadronic cascade where generic rho-like and K*-like resonances, produced in Phase I, rescatter and decay after a formation time tau_f = 1.2-1.6 fm/c. The simulation imposes a saturation constraint that prevents spatial overlap of these resonances, effectively limiting their areal density in the transverse overlap region (the "fire cylinder") of the colliding gold nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The predicted rise in central charged particle pseudo-rapidity density (dN/deta)^ch at eta=0 from sqrt(s)=130 GeV to 200 GeV is 12-14%, consistent with the experimentally observed slight increase of 10-14%.
- The three-dimensional number density of resonances at the start of Phase II for central collisions is approximately 0.70-1.10 particles per fm^3, depending on choices of nuclear radius (6.8-7.2 fm) and formation time.
- The average resonance size extracted from the saturated density is approximately 0.70 fm.
- The total transverse energy density at the start of Phase II, including both bosonic and baryonic contributions, is approximately 1.7 GeV/fm^3; combining contributions from both phases yields approximately 2.8 GeV/fm^3.
- The fraction of final mesons produced via collisions in Phase II is approximately 40%, varying by only a few percent from sqrt(s)=56 to 200 GeV.
- Strange meson production at mid-rapidity contributes approximately 10% of the particle number there.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier LUCIFER simulations (Kahana and Kahana, 1998-2001) that successfully described the earliest PHOBOS and STAR measurements at RHIC, including the prediction of the slight rise in multiplicity from 130 to 200 GeV. It contrasts with partonic models (such as those by Wang and Gyulassy, or Geiger and Mueller) and hybrid partonic-hadronic models by demonstrating that purely hadronic degrees of freedom can reproduce the global inclusive features of RHIC data. The results suggest that non-standard medium effects (such as quark-gluon plasma signatures) must be sought in more exclusive, rare events rather than in the inclusive spectra.
