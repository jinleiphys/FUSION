# Polarization contributions to the spin-dependence of the effective interaction in neutron matter

> We calculate the modification of the effective interaction of particles on the Fermi surface due to polarization contributions, with particular attention to spin-dependent forces. In addition to the standard spin-spin, tensor and spin-orbit forces, spin non-conserving effective interactions are induced by screening in the particle-hole channels. Furthermore, a novel long-wavelength tensor force is generated. We compute the polarization contributions to second order in the low-momentum interaction V_{low k} and find that the medium-induced spin-orbit interaction leads to a reduction of the 3P2 pairing gap for neutrons in the interior of neutron stars.

# Polarization contributions to the spin-dependence of the effective interaction in neutron matter

## Key claim
The medium-induced spin-orbit interaction from particle-hole screening reduces the ³P₂ pairing gap for neutrons in neutron star interiors, contrary to the increase obtained when only central-force polarization effects are included. The authors emphasize that their results are qualitative; a quantitative calculation would require the full polarization contributions for non-central interactions and solution of the full coupled-channel BCS gap equation.

## Method
The authors compute the modification of the effective interaction of particles on the Fermi surface due to polarization contributions to second order in the low-momentum interaction V_low_k, using Landau-Fermi liquid theory. They evaluate contributions from the exchange particle-hole (zero sound) channel and the BCS (particle-particle) channel, as well as boost corrections from relativity, for pure neutron matter at a Fermi momentum of 1.7 fm⁻¹ with effective mass m*/m = 0.83.

## Key numbers
- Fermi liquid parameters for neutron matter at k_F = 1.7 fm⁻¹ with m*/m = 0.83: total exchange tensor H_0 = 0.077, H_1 = 0.140, H_2 = -0.266; center-of-mass tensor K_0 = -0.258, K_1 = 0.147, K_2 = 0.124; cross vector L_0 = -0.061, L_1 = -0.089, L_2 = -0.035
- The ratio of the induced pairing matrix element to the free-space V_low_k contribution ranges from 0.15 to 0.5 for the densities shown in the pairing gap figure
- The ³P₂ pairing gap is computed using weak coupling BCS theory with the formula Δ = (k_F²/m) exp(π / [2 k_F m V_low_k;³P₂(k_F,k_F)])
- Results are presented only for k_F ≲ 2 fm⁻¹, where the NN interaction is strongly constrained by data

## Context
This work extends the program of determining effective nuclear interactions using renormalization group methods (Schwenk and Friman, 2003) and builds on prior studies of tensor interactions in nuclear matter (Haensel, 1975; Haensel and Dabrowski, 1975; Dickhoff et al., 1981). The novel non-central Fermi-liquid interactions K (center-of-mass tensor) and L (cross vector) have not been considered in previous work. The results enable improved calculations of neutron star properties including superfluid gaps, cooling by neutrino emission, spin-isospin response, and neutrino transport in stellar collapse. The polarization force of Kuo and Brown (1966) implicitly includes these effects in microscopic shell model calculations.
