# Benchmarking $^{136}$Xe Neutrinoless $ββ$ Decay Matrix Element Calculations with the $^{138}{\rm Ba}(p,t)$ Reaction

> We used a high-resolution magnetic spectrograph to study neutron pair-correlated $0^+$ states in $^{136}$Ba, produced via the $^{138}{\rm Ba}(p,t)$ reaction. In conjunction with state-of-the-art shell model calculations, these data benchmark part of the dominant Gamow-Teller component of the nuclear matrix element (NME) for $^{136}$Xe neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay. We demonstrate for the first time an evaluation of part of a $0νββ$ decay NME by use of an experimental observable, presenting a new avenue of approach for more accurate calculations of $0νββ$ decay matrix elements.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time an evaluation of part of a neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) using an experimental observable, specifically the $^{138}{\rm Ba}(p,t)$ reaction cross sections. However, this benchmarking only addresses the dominant Gamow-Teller component of the NME for $^{136}$Xe decay, and the agreement between theory and experiment is limited to approximately 14-22% depending on the Hamiltonian used, with core-polarization corrections required to achieve this level of agreement.

## Method

The authors used a high-resolution Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium in Garching to measure triton angular distributions from the $^{138}{\rm Ba}(p,t)$ reaction at 23 MeV proton beam energy, identifying twelve $0^+$ states in $^{136}$Ba up to approximately 4.6 MeV excitation energy. They performed distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) and coupled-reaction channels calculations using the DWUCK4 and Fresco codes, combined with configuration interaction shell model calculations using NuShellX with three different Hamiltonians (sn100pn, sn100t, and GCN50:82), to compute two-neutron transfer amplitudes and compare with experimental cross sections. Core-polarization corrections to the two-nucleon transfer amplitudes were calculated by considering ladder-diagram corrections involving twenty three orbitals beyond the valence space up to $i_{11/2}$.

## Key numbers

- Measured ground state cross section at $\theta_{\rm lab} = 5^\circ$: $2.17(12)$ mb/sr
- Integrated $L = 0$ strength to excited $0^+$ states relative to ground state: $\sum\epsilon_i = 53(3)\%$
- Approximately 30% of ground state strength concentrated at 2.3 and 2.8 MeV excitation energy
- Energy resolution of triton peaks: $\lesssim$ 10 keV
- Core-polarization corrections enhanced predicted cross sections by a factor of approximately 1.5
- After core-polarization corrections, calculations agree with experiment to approximately 22% for GCN50:82 Hamiltonian and approximately 14% for the other Hamiltonians
- Five-orbital valence space ISM calculation of the $J^\pi = 0^+$ Gamow-Teller component of the NME (for light neutrino exchange, CD-Bonn SRC, including HOC): $M_{GT}^{0\nu} (J^\pi = 0^+) = 5.67$

## Context

This work extends the approach of using two-nucleon transfer reactions to benchmark double beta decay matrix elements, following earlier work by Freeman (2012) on $^{76}$Ge and related studies. The results indicate a breakdown of the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) pairing approximation for neutrons in $^{136}$Ba, which was not observed in the $^{128-134}$Ba isotopes studied by Pascu (2010, 2009) and Suliman (2008). The method enables future benchmarking of NME calculations for $^{136}$Xe neutrinoless double beta decay by providing experimental constraints on the two-neutron transfer amplitudes that contribute to the dominant Gamow-Teller component of the NME, and the authors recommend similar analyses for other double beta decay candidates.
