# Model studies of V0 production ratios in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{\mathrm{s}}$ = 0.2, 0.9, and 7 TeV

> A comparative study of $V^0$ ratios has been performed between HIJING, Sibyll and QGSJET model-based event generators in this paper. The ratios under study are {\alam}/{\lam}, {\alam}/{\ks} and {\xim}/{\lam} as a function of rapidity $y$, rapidity loss ($Δy$) and {\ppt} from $pp$ collisions at \sqrts~= 0.2, 0.9, and 7 TeV and these simulations are then compared with the STAR and LHCb fiducial phase spaces in different {\ppt} regions. Although the models could produce some of the ratios in a limited {\ppt} and/or $y$ region, none of them completely predicts the experimental results. The QGSJET has good predictions with the data in most of the cases but since the model does not include the $Ξ$ particle definition, therefore it does not give any predictions for $Ξ$/{\lam} ratios. The extrapolation to the highest possible energies can be studied by re-tune some of the basic parameters based on current and previous measurements. These kinds of systematic comparison studies are also useful to apply certain constraints on the pQCD and non-pQCD-based hadronic event generators to significantly improve the predictions of Standard Model physics at the RHIC and LHC experimental data for the understanding of underlying physics mechanisms in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

None of the three event generators (HIJING, Sibyll, QGSJET) completely predicts the experimental V0 production ratios across all kinematic regions, although QGSJET shows good agreement with data in most cases except that it cannot predict Xi/Lambda ratios because it lacks the Xi particle definition.

## Method

The authors generated events for strange particles (K0S, Lambda, anti-Lambda, Xi-, anti-Xi+) using HIJING, Sibyll2.3d, and QGSJETII-04 event generators for pp collisions at center-of-mass energies of 0.2, 0.9, and 7 TeV. They computed the ratios anti-Lambda/Lambda, anti-Lambda/K0S, and Xi-/Lambda as functions of transverse momentum (pT), rapidity (y), and rapidity loss (Delta y), then compared these simulations with experimental data from the STAR experiment at 200 GeV and the LHCb experiment at 0.9 and 7 TeV within their respective fiducial phase spaces.

## Key numbers

* At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the anti-Lambda/Lambda ratio as a function of pT: all models describe experimental data with reasonable agreement; Sibyll gives a better description than the other models for both anti-Lambda/Lambda and anti-Xi+/Xi- ratios.
* At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, for the anti-Lambda/Lambda ratio as a function of rapidity in the pT region 0.65 < pT < 1.0 GeV/c, QGSJET slightly overpredicts experimental results below y < 3 but agrees at high y.
* At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, for the anti-Lambda/K0S ratio as a function of rapidity in the pT region 0.25 < pT < 0.65 GeV/c, Sibyll completely underpredicts the experimental results as well as HIJING and QGSJET results.
* At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, for the anti-Lambda/Lambda ratio as a function of pT in the rapidity range 2.0 < y < 4.0, only QGSJET agrees with data at pT > 0.8 GeV/c, while HIJING and Sibyll significantly overshoot the data.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of hadronic event generators with experimental data from STAR (Abelev et al., 2007) and LHCb (Aaij et al., 2011) by systematically testing three models (HIJING, Sibyll, QGSJET) across multiple energies and kinematic variables. The study enables future re-tuning of model parameters based on current and previous measurements, and helps apply constraints on both pQCD-based and non-pQCD-based hadronic event generators to improve predictions for Standard Model physics at RHIC and LHC energies.
