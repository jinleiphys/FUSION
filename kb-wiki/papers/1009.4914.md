# Chiral transition and deconfinement transition in QCD with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action

> We report preliminary results on the chiral and deconfinement aspects of the QCD transition at finite temperature using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on lattices with temporal extent of N_τ=6 and 8. The chiral aspects of the transition are studied in terms of quark condensates and the disconnected chiral susceptibility. We study the deconfinement transition in terms of the strange quark number susceptibility and the renormalized Polyakov loop. We made continuum estimates for some quantities and find reasonably good agreement between our results and the recent continuum extrapolated results obtained with the stout staggered quark action.

# Chiral transition and deconfinement transition in QCD with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action

## Key claim

The HISQ action yields a chiral transition temperature that is significantly lower than previous estimates from asqtad and p4 actions on lattices with temporal extent N_tau <= 8, and shows reasonable agreement with continuum-extrapolated stout action results for the renormalized Polyakov loop and strange quark number susceptibility, though direct comparison of chiral quantities is complicated by slightly different light quark masses used in the two calculations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the HISQ action in the fermion sector with tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action on 24^3 x 6 and 32^3 x 8 lattices for light quark mass m_l = 0.05 m_s (pion mass ~160 MeV) and on 16^3 x 6 lattices for m_l = 0.20 m_s (pion mass ~320 MeV). The strange quark mass was set to its physical value using the condition sqrt(2m_K^2 - m_pi^2) = 686.57 MeV. The chiral transition was studied via the subtracted chiral condensate, the renormalized chiral condensate Delta_l^R, and the disconnected chiral susceptibility; the deconfinement transition was studied via the renormalized Polyakov loop and the strange quark number susceptibility. Continuum estimates were obtained by combined polynomial fits of N_tau = 6 and 8 results allowing for lattice spacing dependent coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For m_l = 0.05 m_s on N_tau = 6 lattices: T_c = 168(4)(5) MeV from the disconnected chiral susceptibility peak
- For m_l = 0.20 m_s on N_tau = 6 lattices: T_c = 185(4)(5) MeV from the disconnected chiral susceptibility peak
- For m_l = 0.05 m_s on N_tau = 8 lattices: T_c = 165(4)(5) MeV from the disconnected chiral susceptibility peak
- The lightest pion mass used was about 160 MeV (for m_l = 0.05 m_s) and about 320 MeV (for m_l = 0.20 m_s)
- The Sommer scale r_0 = 0.469 fm was used to convert lattice units to physical units
- The strange quark mass was set using the physical value m_eta_{s\bar s} = 686.57 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of studying finite-temperature QCD transitions with improved staggered fermion actions, following earlier studies using p4 and asqtad actions that showed large taste symmetry violations. The HISQ action, developed by the HPQCD/UKQCD collaboration (Follana et al., 2007), reduces taste symmetry breaking by a factor of about three compared to asqtad. The results are compared to continuum-extrapolated stout action results (Fodor et al., 2010), showing reasonable agreement for deconfinement observables but discrepancies in chiral quantities attributed to different quark masses. The lower chiral transition temperatures found here (165-168 MeV for m_l = 0.05 m_s) are consistent with recent asqtad results on N_tau = 12 lattices, and the authors note that future calculations on N_tau = 12 lattices with smaller quark masses will be needed to clarify quantitative issues.
