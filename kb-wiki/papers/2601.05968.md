# Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models on a 3D mesh: V. The N2LO extension of the Skyrme EDF

> We present BSkG5, the latest entry in the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid (BSkG) series and the first large-scale nuclear structure model based on next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) Skyrme energy density functional (EDF). By extending the traditional Skyrme EDF ansatz with central terms containing up to four gradients, we are able to combine an excellent global description of nuclear ground state properties with a stiff equation of state for pure neutron matter that is consistent with all astronomical observations of neutron stars. More precisely, the new model matches the accuracy of earlier BSkG models but with two parameters less: we achieve root-mean-square deviations of 0.649 MeV for 2457 atomic masses, 0.0267 fm for 810 charge radii, and 0.43 MeV for 45 primary fission barriers of actinide nuclei. We demonstrate that the complexities of N2LO EDFs are not insurmountable, even for demanding many-body calculations.

## Key claim
The BSkG5 model demonstrates that an N2LO Skyrme energy density functional can simultaneously achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for nuclear ground-state properties and a stiff neutron-matter equation of state consistent with massive neutron stars, matching the performance of earlier NLO models but with two fewer parameters and without the formal issues associated with extended density dependence.

## Method
The authors constructed BSkG5 by extending the traditional Skyrme EDF ansatz to include central N2LO terms containing up to four gradients, parameterized by four additional coupling constants. They adjusted the model to 2457 atomic masses, 810 charge radii, and fission properties of 45 actinide nuclei using a three-dimensional coordinate-space representation that allows triaxial and octupole deformation, while imposing realistic infinite nuclear matter properties and a maximum neutron star mass above 2 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square deviation of 0.649 MeV for 2457 atomic masses (AME2020, nuclei with Z, N ≥ 8)
- Root-mean-square deviation of 0.0267 fm for 810 charge radii (Angeli13 data, excluding systematics)
- Root-mean-square deviation of 0.43 MeV for 45 primary fission barriers of actinide nuclei (RIPL-3 data)
- Maximum neutron star mass of 2.2 solar masses from Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations
- N2LO contribution to binding energy of 208Pb is about 109 MeV (LO: -6818 MeV, NLO: 355 MeV)
- Centroid energy of isoscalar giant monopole resonance in 208Pb: 14.4 MeV (experimental: 13.7 ± 0.2 MeV)
- Direct Urca process onset at baryon density 0.456 fm^-3, corresponding to neutron star mass of about 1.44 solar masses

## Context
This work extends the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid (BSkG) series, following BSkG3 (Grams 2023) and BSkG4 (Grams 2025), and is the first large-scale nuclear structure model based on an N2LO Skyrme EDF. It improves upon the only previous N2LO parameterization SN2LO1 (Becker 2017), which was limited to a proof-of-concept and did not systematically improve predictive power. The model enables future applications of N2LO EDFs to advanced many-body calculations, including finite-amplitude method RPA and cranked HFB for superdeformed bands, while avoiding the finite-size instabilities that plagued earlier attempts.
