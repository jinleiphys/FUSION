# Separable Representation of Energy-Dependent Optical Potentials

> Background. One important ingredient for many applications of nuclear physics to astrophysics, nuclear energy, and stockpile stewardship are cross sections for reactions of neutrons with rare isotopes. Since direct measurements are often not feasible, indirect methods, e.g. (d,p) reactions, should be used. Those (d,p) reactions may be viewed as three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. Purpose. Faddeev equations in momentum space have a long tradition of utilizing separable interactions in order to arrive at sets of coupled integral equations in one variable. Optical potentials representing the effective interactions in the neutron (proton) nucleus subsystem are usually non-Hermitian as well as energy-dependent. Potential matrix elements as well as transition matrix elements calculated with them must fulfill the reciprocity theorem. The purpose of this paper is to introduce a separable, energy-dependent representation of complex, energy-dependent optical potentials that fulfill reciprocity exactly. Results. Starting from a separable, energy-independent representation of global optical potentials based on a generalization of the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) scheme, a further generalization is needed to take into account the energy dependence. Applications to n$+^{48}$Ca, n$+^{208}$Pb, and p$+^{208}$Pb are investigated for energies from 0 to 50~MeV with special emphasis on fulfilling reciprocity. Conclusions. We find that the energy-dependent separable representation of complex, energy-dependent phenomenological optical potentials fulfills reciprocity exactly. In addition, taking into account the explicit energy dependence slightly improves the description of the $S$ matrix elements.

# Separable Representation of Energy-Dependent Optical Potentials

## Key claim

The authors introduce an explicitly energy-dependent separable representation (eEST) of complex, energy-dependent phenomenological optical potentials that fulfills the reciprocity theorem exactly, whereas the previously developed energy-independent separable representation only approximately satisfies reciprocity when the underlying potential is energy-dependent. The improvement from including explicit energy dependence is slight for on-shell S-matrix elements but essential for exact off-shell symmetry.

## Method

The authors generalize the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separable expansion scheme, previously extended to non-Hermitian potentials, by allowing the coupling matrix elements lambda(E) to be energy-dependent. They construct separable representations of the Chapel Hill (CH89) global optical potential for neutron scattering off Ca-48 and Pb-208, and for proton scattering off Pb-208, evaluating the required matrix elements in momentum space. For the proton case, the separable representation is constructed in the basis of Coulomb scattering states using the Coulomb-distorted Green's function.

## Key numbers

- Energy range investigated: 0 to 50 MeV for n+Ca-48, n+Pb-208, and p+Pb-208 systems
- For p+Pb-208 s-wave (l=0, j=1/2), a rank-5 representation keeps relative error below 2% in the 0 to 50 MeV range
- Using cubic Hermite spline interpolation on the energy variable with 4 interpolation points and a 100-point energy grid reduces computational effort by a factor of 23
- With 5 EST support points (interpolation points), computation time is decreased by a factor of 18

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous energy-independent EST separable representation for complex optical potentials (Hlophe 2013) and its Coulomb-distorted version for proton-nucleus scattering (Hlophe 2014). It incorporates suggestions by Pearce (1987) for accommodating energy dependence in Hermitian potentials, which were previously applied to pion-nucleon interactions (Pearce 1989, Saito 2000). The eEST representation is designed for use in momentum-space Faddeev-AGS three-body calculations of (d,p) reactions, where separable interactions reduce coupled integral equations to one variable, and where exact fulfillment of reciprocity is required for the off-shell transition matrix elements.
