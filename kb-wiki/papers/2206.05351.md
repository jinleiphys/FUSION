# Nuclear Josephson-like $γ$-emission

> Josephson-like junctions, transiently established in heavy ion collisions between superfluid nuclei, few MeV below the Coulomb barrier, allow for the back and forth transfer of a nuclear Cooper pair of effectively charged nucleons and thus the emission of $γ$-rays. The second order DWBA $T$-matrix formulation of single Cooper pair alternating current is shown to contain the gauge phases and gauge rotational frequencies as required by the Josephson (ac) effect, in keeping with the derivation of the transfer (tunneling) Hamiltonian in a gauge invariant representation. We describe the emergence of two strongly convergent parameters (conserved quantities) within the time the abnormal densities of the two superfluid nuclei overlap: a) the correlation length (dc); b) the number of emitted $γ$-rays per cycle (ac), and thus the dipole moment of the successively transferred nucleons. Result which leads to a nuclear parallel with the direct current (dc) and alternating current (ac) Josephson effects, and which testifies to the validity of BCS theory of superconductivity down to few Cooper pair condensates, and single Cooper pair alternating currents. The physics at the basis of a quantitative description of Cooper pair tunneling between weakly coupled superconductors or superfluid nuclei at energies below the Coulomb barrier, is that the process is dominated by the successive transfer of the two partner fermions entangled over distances of the order of the coherence length, $\approx10^4$Å${}$ in the case of lead, and 13.5 fm in the case of the reaction $^{116}\text{Sn}+^{60}\text{Ni}\to^{114}\text{Sn(gs)}+^{62}\text{Ni(gs)}$ at few MeV below the Coulomb barrier.

# Nuclear Josephson-like γ-emission

## Key claim

The paper argues that a transient Josephson-like junction can be established between two superfluid nuclei in a heavy-ion collision a few MeV below the Coulomb barrier, leading to back-and-forth transfer of a single nuclear Cooper pair and consequent γ-ray emission. The claim is hedged by the condition that this process is only observable when the distance of closest approach is at or below a critical value (D₀)c ≈ 13.5 fm, corresponding to the nuclear coherence length, and that the effect relies on the successive transfer of the two entangled fermion partners over distances of order the coherence length.

## Method

The authors formulate the two-nucleon transfer process using a second-order Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) T-matrix approach, showing its equivalence to the Cohen-Falicov-Phillips tunneling Hamiltonian used in condensed-matter Josephson junctions. They analyze experimental data from the reaction ¹¹⁶Sn + ⁶⁰Ni → ¹¹⁴Sn(gs) + ⁶²Ni(gs) at E_cm = 154.26 MeV and θ_cm = 140°, extracting the ratio of two-nucleon to one-nucleon transfer cross sections (σ₂ₙ/σ₁ₙ) as a function of the distance of closest approach D₀. The critical distance (D₀)c is defined empirically as the point where (4/π)² × (σ₂ₙ/σ₁ₙ) ≳ 0.5, and this is compared with the nuclear coherence length ξ derived from the BCS relation ξ ≈ ħv_F/(πΔ).

## Key numbers

- Reaction Q-value: Q₂ₙ = 1.307 MeV for ¹¹⁶Sn + ⁶⁰Ni → ¹¹⁴Sn + ⁶²Ni
- Center-of-mass energy: E_cm = 154.26 MeV at θ_cm = 140°
- Critical distance of closest approach: (D₀)c = 13.49 fm
- Nuclear coherence length: ξ ≈ 13.5 fm, derived from v_F/c ≈ 0.27 and Δ ≈ 1.27 MeV
- Collision time: τ_coll ≈ 0.51 × 10⁻²¹ s, from v_cm ≈ 26.49 × 10²¹ fm/s
- Critical depairing velocity: v_c ≈ 4.44 × 10²¹ fm/s
- Junction width at critical distance: w ≈ 2.26 fm, consistent with (D₀)c − (R(⁶⁰Ni) + R(¹¹⁶Sn) + a) = 13.49 fm − 11.25 fm = 2.24 fm
- Differential cross section for two-nucleon transfer: dσ₂ₙ/dΩ|θ_cm=140° = 2.58 mb/sr
- Coherence length in lead (for comparison): ξ ≈ 10⁴ Å
- Predicted nuclear Josephson angular frequency: ω_J^N = Q₂ₙ/ħ

## Context

This work extends the analogy between nuclear pairing and condensed-matter superconductivity first explored in the context of two-particle transfer reactions by Dietrich (1970), Hara (1971), and von Oertzen (2001), and more recently by Potel (2021). It builds on experimental two-nucleon transfer data from Montanari (2014, 2016) for the ¹¹⁶Sn + ⁶⁰Ni system. The paper provides a quantitative framework connecting the BCS coherence length, the critical distance of closest approach, and the predicted γ-ray emission from single Cooper pair alternating currents, enabling experimental searches for nuclear Josephson radiation in heavy-ion collisions below the Coulomb barrier.
