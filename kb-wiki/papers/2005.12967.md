# $J/ψ$ meson production in association with an open charm hadron at the LHC: A reappraisal

> We critically (re)examine the associated production process of a $J/ψ$ meson plus an open charm hadron at the LHC in the proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-lead ($p{\rm Pb}$) collisions. Such a process is very intriguing in the sense of tailoring to explore the double parton structure of nucleons and to determine the geometry of partons in nuclei. In order to interpret the existing $pp$ data with the LHCb detector at the center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, we introduce two overlooked mechanisms for the double parton scattering (DPS) and single parton scattering (SPS) processes. Besides the conventional DPS mode, where the two mesons are produced almost independently in the two separate scattering subprocesses, we propose a novel DPS mechanism that the two constituent (heavy) quarks stemming from two hard scatterings can form into a composite particle, like the $J/ψ$ meson, during the hadronization phase. However, it turns out the corresponding contribution is small in $J/ψ+c\bar{c}$ hadroproduction. On the contrary, we point out that the resummation of the initial state logarithms due to gluon splitting into a charm quark pair is crucial to understand the LHCb measurement, which was overlooked in the literature. We perform a proper matching between the perturbative calculations in different initial-quark flavor number schemes, generically referring to the variable flavor number scheme. The new variable flavor number scheme calculation for the process strongly enhance the SPS cross sections, almost closing the discrepancies between theory and experiment. Finally, we present our predictions for the forthcoming LHCb measurement in $p{\rm Pb}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.16$ TeV. Some interesting observables are exploited to set up the control regions of the DPS signal and to probe the impact-parameter dependent parton densities in lead.

## Key claim

The existing LHCb data on \(J/\psi\) + open charm hadron production at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV, which had been interpreted as dominated by double parton scattering (DPS), can be reconciled with single parton scattering (SPS) once the resummation of initial-state logarithms from gluon splitting into a charm quark pair is included via a variable flavor number scheme (VFNS); a newly proposed DPS mechanism where the \(J/\psi\) is formed from charm quarks originating from two different hard scatterings (DPS\(_2\)) is found to give only a small contribution.

## Method

The author performed a data-driven extraction of effective matrix elements for single-inclusive prompt \(J/\psi\) and open charm hadron production from LHCb differential cross sections, using a four-parameter empirical formula with CT10NLO parton distribution functions. For the SPS channel, the author implemented a variable flavor number scheme (VFNS) matching that combines the gluon-fusion process \(gg\to J/\psi+c\bar{c}\) with the charm-initiated process \(cg\to J/\psi+c\) and its counterterm, using a momentum-reshuffling procedure to maintain on-shell conditions for massive charm quarks. The DPS\(_2\) mechanism was computed at leading order in the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework with the color-singlet projector, assuming zero initial-state correlation and using the same effective cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},pp}\) as for conventional DPS.

## Key numbers

* The measured cross section of \(J/\psi+D^0\) (plus charge conjugate) in the LHCb acceptance at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV is \(161.0\pm 3.7\pm 12.2\) nb, while the gluon-fusion SPS cross section alone ranges from 3.7 to 16 nb.
* The conventional DPS (DPS\(_1\)) normalization uses \(\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},pp}=15\) mb for the sum of DPS and SPS contributions.
* The LHCb measurement was based on an integrated luminosity of 355 pb\(^{-1}\) of Run-I \(pp\) data at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV.
* The LHCb acceptance for the measurement is \(2.0<y_{J/\psi},y_C<4.0\), \(P_{T,J/\psi}<12\) GeV, and \(3<p_{T,C}/\mathrm{GeV}<12\).
* Predictions are presented for \(p\mathrm{Pb}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.16\) TeV.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy between the LHCb measurement (Aaij 2012) and earlier theoretical calculations that found the gluon-fusion SPS process \(gg\to J/\psi+c\bar{c}\) to be negligible compared to the observed rate, leading to a claimed DPS dominance that could not explain the shape of differential distributions. The author extends prior work on heavy-quark-initiated processes (Maltoni 2012) and the ACOT variable flavor number scheme (Aivazis 1993) to the case of quarkonium production, and introduces a new DPS mechanism (DPS\(_2\)) that is specific to same-flavor heavy-quark pair production. The results enable future LHCb measurements in \(p\mathrm{Pb}\) collisions to probe impact-parameter-dependent nuclear parton distribution functions.
