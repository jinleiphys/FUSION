# Efficient Monte Carlo Event Generation for Neutrino-Nucleus Exclusive Cross Sections

> Modern neutrino-nucleus cross section predictions need to incorporate sophisticated nuclear models to achieve greater predictive precision. However, the computational complexity of these advanced models often limits their practicality for experimental analyses. To address this challenge, we introduce a new Monte Carlo method utilizing Normalizing Flows to generate surrogate cross sections that closely approximate those of the original model while significantly reducing computational overhead. As a case study, we built a Monte Carlo event generator for the neutrino-nucleus cross section model developed by the Ghent group. This model employs a Hartree-Fock procedure to establish a quantum mechanical framework in which both the bound and scattering nucleon states are solutions to the mean-field nuclear potential. The surrogate cross sections generated by our method demonstrate excellent accuracy with a relative effective sample size of more than $98.4 \%$, providing a computationally efficient alternative to traditional Monte Carlo sampling methods for differential cross sections.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that normalizing flows can generate surrogate neutrino-nucleus cross sections that closely approximate the original Hartree-Fock mean-field model while significantly reducing computational overhead, but only for the specific case of 1-particle-1-hole (1p1h) interactions on carbon-12, with the method validated for neutrino energies between 180 MeV and 1050 MeV.

## Method
The authors trained masked autoregressive rational-quadratic neural spline flows (RQ-NSF) on pre-computed differential cross sections from the Ghent group's Hartree-Fock mean-field model for charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) neutrino-carbon scattering. They modeled each nuclear shell separately (1s1/2 and 1p3/2) across four overlapping neutrino energy ranges, using importance sampling with a uniform proposal distribution to compute the symmetric Kullback-Leibler divergence during training. The flows were conditioned on neutrino energy to produce conditional probability distributions over the four kinematic variables (theta_mu, omega, theta_N, phi_N) for fixed incoming neutrino energy.

## Key numbers
- Relative effective sample size of more than 98.4% for the surrogate cross sections
- Separation energies of 36.20 MeV for the 1s1/2 shell and 18.72 MeV for the 1p3/2 shell in carbon-12
- Neutrino energy coverage from 180 MeV to 1050 MeV, divided into four overlapping ranges: [180, 350], [300, 500], [450, 750], and [700, 1050] MeV
- Training used 10 million pre-generated samples per model, with batches of 2^14 samples per epoch over 100,000 epochs
- Learning rate of 0.0001 with cosine annealing to a minimum of 5 x 10^-7

## Context
This work extends the application of normalizing flows from high-energy collider physics (as demonstrated by Gao et al. (2020) for Drell-Yan processes and Bothmann et al. (2020) for top-quark production) to neutrino-nucleus interactions. It provides a computationally efficient alternative to traditional accept-reject Monte Carlo sampling and to pre-computed hadron tensor tables (Dolan et al., 2020; Dolan, 2021) which lack flexibility and scale poorly with dimensionality. The method enables faster event generation for the Ghent group's mean-field model, which goes beyond the plane-wave impulse approximation used in models like the Relativistic Fermi Gas (PhysRevC.72.025501) and Spectral Function (Benhar et al., 1994), and is complementary to the recent implementation of a relativistic DWIA-based CCQE model in NEUT (McKean et al., 2025).
