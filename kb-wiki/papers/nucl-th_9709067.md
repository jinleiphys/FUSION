Coupled integral equations for the S and P wave multipoles were derived from the dispersion relations and solved by the method of Omnes and Muskhelishvili. The free parameters were determined by a fit to the most recent data for π^{+} and π^{0} production on the proton as well as π^{-} production on the neutron, in the energy range 160 MeV \leq E_γ \leq 420 MeV. The lack of high precision data on the neutron and of polarization observables leads to some limitations of our results. Especially the multipole M_{1-} connected with the Roper resonance P_{11}(1440) cannot be determined to the required precision. Our predictions for the threshold amplitudes are in good agreement with both the data and chiral perturbation theory. In the region of the Δ(1232) we have determined the ratio of electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole excitation. The position of the resonance pole is obtained in excellent agreement with pion-nucleon scattering, and the complex residues of the multipoles are determined with the speed-plot technique.

## Key claim

The authors present a multipole analysis of pion photoproduction that imposes constraints from fixed t dispersion relations and unitarity, but they acknowledge that the lack of high precision data on the neutron and of polarization observables leads to limitations, particularly that the multipole M1- connected with the Roper resonance P11(1440) cannot be determined to the required precision.

## Method

Coupled integral equations for the S and P wave multipoles were derived from fixed t dispersion relations and solved using the method of Omnes and Muskhelishvili. The free parameters were determined by a fit to data for pi+ and pi0 production on the proton as well as pi- production on the neutron in the energy range 160 MeV to 420 MeV. The complex phases of the multipoles were taken from pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts below two-pion threshold, with a cut-off at 2 GeV and high energy tails represented by t-channel exchange of rho and omega mesons.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of the analysis: 160 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 420 MeV
- Cut-off for the dispersion integrals: Λ = 2 GeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant used: g²/4π = 14.28
- Total data points included in the fit: 1751
- Overall χ² per data point: 2.8
- Number of free parameters in the fit: 10

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Schwela and Weizel (1969) to analyze new high precision data from MAMI and ELSA, particularly for pi0 production near threshold and beam asymmetries. The method provides a natural parametrization that fulfills unitarity constraints, and the results enable improved determination of threshold amplitudes and the electromagnetic excitation of the Delta(1232) resonance, with predictions for the ratio of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole excitation. The analysis is restricted to energies up to about 500 MeV due to convergence issues with the partial wave expansion and the need to know complex phases of partial waves, which are most reliable below two-pion threshold.
