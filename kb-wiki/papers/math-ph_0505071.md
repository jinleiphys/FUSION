# Solutions of the Gaudin Equation and Gaudin Algebras

> Three well-known solutions of the Gaudin equation are obtained under a set of standard assumptions. By relaxing one of these assumptions we introduce a class of mutually commuting Hamiltonians based on a different solution of the Gaudin equation. Application of the algebraic Bethe ansatz technique to diagonalize these Hamiltonians reveals a new infinite dimensional complex Lie algebra.

## Key claim

By relaxing the antisymmetry condition on the coefficients \(w_{ij}^\alpha\) to \(w_{ij}^\alpha + w_{ji}^\alpha = -2q\) (with \(q\) a real parameter), the authors obtain a new solution of the Gaudin equation that is distinct from the three standard rational, trigonometric, and hyperbolic solutions, and which cannot be unitarily transformed into any of them for \(q \neq 0\).

## Method

The authors start from the Gaudin equation for the coefficients \(w_{ij}^\alpha\) that ensure mutual commutation of the operators \(h_i\). They keep the assumptions that each coefficient depends only on the difference of two real parameters \(u_i, u_j\) and that each \(h_i\) commutes with the total \(z\)-component of \(SU(2)\), but they replace the antisymmetry condition \(w_{ij}^\alpha + w_{ji}^\alpha = 0\) with \(w_{ij}^\alpha + w_{ji}^\alpha = -2q\). Substituting the resulting solution \(w_{ij}^\alpha = q\coth[q(u_i-u_j)] - q\) into the definition of \(h_i\) yields a new set of mutually commuting Hamiltonians \(h_i^{(q)}\). The algebraic Bethe ansatz is then applied to diagonalize these Hamiltonians, leading to a new infinite-dimensional complex Lie algebra whose commutation relations and \(r\)-matrix structure are derived.

## Key numbers

* The new solution for the coefficients is \(w_{ij}^\alpha = q\coth[q(u_i-u_j)] - q\) for \(\alpha = 0,1,2\), where \(q\) is a real parameter.
* In the limit \(q \to 0\), the new solution approaches the rational solution \(w_{ij}^\alpha = 1/(u_i-u_j)\).
* The eigenvalue of \(h_i^{(q)}\) on the lowest weight state is \(E^{(q)}_{0,i} = \sum_{j \neq i} s_i s_j \, q(\coth[q(u_i-u_j)] - 1)\), where \(s_j(s_j+1)\) is the Casimir eigenvalue of the \(j\)-th \(SU(2)\).
* The eigenvalue of \(h_i^{(q)}\) on a Bethe state \(|\xi_1,\dots,\xi_n\rangle\) is \(E^{(q)}_{n,i} = E^{(q)}_{0,i} - s_i \sum_{\alpha=1}^n q(\coth[q(u_i-\xi_\alpha)] - 1)\).
* The Bethe ansatz equations for the parameters \(\xi_\alpha\) are \(W_q(\xi_\alpha) = \sum_{\beta \neq \alpha} q(\coth[q(\xi_\alpha - \xi_\beta)] - 1)\), where \(W_q(\lambda)\) is the eigenvalue of \(J_q^0(\lambda)\) on the lowest weight state.

## Context

This work extends the known set of Gaudin integrable models (rational, trigonometric, hyperbolic) by providing a new solution of the Gaudin equation under a relaxed symmetry condition. It generalizes the approach of Gaudin (1970s) and Richardson (1963) for pairing in many-body systems, and contrasts with a different generalization by Ortiz et al. (2004) that was based on the three standard solutions. The new algebra and its associated \(r\)-matrix structure, which satisfies a modified version of the classical Yang-Baxter equation, open the possibility of constructing new integrable models in quantum many-body physics, particularly for applications of the Richardson-Gaudin method.
