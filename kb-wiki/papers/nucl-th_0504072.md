# Di-Electron Bremsstrahlung in Intermediate-Energy pn and Dp Collisions

> Invariant mass spectra of di-electrons stemming from bremsstrahlung processes are calculated in a covariant diagrammatical approach for the exclusive reaction D p \to p_{\rm sp} n p e^+ e^- with detection of a forward spectator proton, p_{sp}. We employ an effective nucleon-meson theory for parameterizing the sub-reaction n p \to n p e^+ e^- and, within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, derive a factorization of the cross section in the form {dσ_{D p \to p_{\rm sp} n p e^+ e^-}}/{dM}= {dσ_{n p \to n p e^+ e^-}}/{dM} \times kinematical factor related solely to the deuteron (M is the e^+ e^- invariant mass). The effective nucleon-meson interactions, including the exchange mesons π, σ, ωand ρas well as excitation and radiative decay of Δ(1232), have been adjusted to the process pp \to pp e^+ e^- at energies below the vector meson production threshold. At higher energies, contributions from ωand ρmeson excitations are analyzed in both, NN and Dp collisions. A relation to two-step models is discussed. Subthreshold di-electron production in Dp collisions at low spectator momenta is investigated as well. Calculations have been performed for kinematical conditions envisaged for forthcoming experiments at HADES.

## Key claim

The paper calculates di-electron invariant mass spectra from bremsstrahlung in deuteron-proton collisions, deriving a factorization that relates the Dp cross section to the np sub-reaction cross section multiplied by a kinematical factor from the deuteron, but this factorization relies on the spectator mechanism and the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a realistic deuteron wave function, and the effective meson-nucleon parameters are adjusted to pp data below the vector meson production threshold.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant diagrammatical approach within an effective meson-nucleon theory, using one-boson-exchange amplitudes with π, σ, ω, and ρ mesons plus Δ(1232) isobar excitation and radiative decay, to compute the hadronic current for di-electron production. For the deuteron-proton reaction Dp → p_sp n p e⁺e⁻, they apply the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a realistic deuteron wave function obtained from the same effective theory, deriving a factorization formula that separates the np sub-reaction cross section from a deuteron-related kinematical factor. Gauge invariance is maintained through inclusion of contact terms and appropriate form factor prescriptions for charged meson exchange and off-shell nucleon vertices.

## Key numbers

- Calculations are performed at kinetic energies of 1.04 GeV and 2.09 GeV for pp and pn collisions, corresponding to energies considered in prior work (Mosel et al.)
- The ΔNπ coupling constant is f_{ΔNπ} = 2.13 GeV, and the ΔNρ coupling constant is f_{ΔNρ} = 7.14 GeV
- Cut-off parameters for Δ form factors: Λ_{NΔπ} = 1.421 GeV and Λ_{NΔρ} = 2.273 GeV
- The ΔNγ coupling constants are g₁ = 5.416, g₂ = 6.612, and g₃ = 7.0
- Nucleon anomalous magnetic moments: κ = 1.793 for protons and κ = -1.913 for neutrons

## Context

This work extends the covariant model of Mosel et al. (2000) and Kaptari and Kämpfer (2004) for di-electron production in NN collisions to the exclusive deuteron-proton reaction Dp → p_sp n p e⁺e⁻, which is planned for measurement by the HADES collaboration at SIS/GSI Darmstadt. The approach enables separation of the bremsstrahlung component for the tagged np sub-reaction, which together with the directly measurable pp → pp e⁺e⁻ reaction provides a necessary prerequisite for understanding di-electron emission in heavy-ion collisions and extracting in-medium vector meson modifications. The calculations also relate to two-step models and investigate subthreshold di-electron production in Dp collisions at low spectator momenta.
