# Three particle states and t-exchanges

> The multiple particle final states in modern data from $4 π$ detector experiments offer a wealth of information about higher hadronic resonances and hadron interactions. However, it requires careful analysis to extract model independent results from those data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three-particle final states and \(t\)-exchange processes in hadronic scattering must be treated in full, rather than reduced to effective two-particle states, in order to extract model-independent results from modern \(4\pi\) detector data; however, the practical challenge lies in handling the singularities of three-particle states going on-shell, which requires careful regulation and integration across three distinct scales.

## Method

The author develops an approach based on expanding amplitudes and relevant functions in an orthogonal basis with known dispersion integrals, thereby avoiding singular integration entirely. The three-particle state is implicitly defined through the imaginary part of the two-particle-to-two-particle transition amplitude, and the two-particle amplitude is determined by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with elementary transition amplitudes like \(T^{(0)}\) in the kernel. For the real part of the amplitude, an expansion in Legendre polynomials of the variable \(\xi = (\omega^2-(m_a+m_b)^2)/\omega^2\) is used, while the imaginary part requires a separate treatment due to its restricted but infinite domain in energy space.

## Key numbers

- The second resonance region is defined as energies of \(1.5\) GeV and above.
- The threshold for a three-particle state originating from a two-particle state at zero momentum (\(E=m_a+m_b\)) lies at \(E=m_a+m_b+\mu\), where \(\mu\) is the mass of the exchange particle.
- The maximum and minimum energies for a three-particle state produced in a \(t\)-exchange are given by \(E_{\rm max/min} = \sqrt{(k_i \pm k_f)^2 + \mu^2} + \sqrt{k_i^2 + m_b^2} + \sqrt{k_f^2 + m_a^2}\), where \(k\) is the momentum associated with the two-particle energy \(\omega\).
- The domain of the imaginary part of \(T^{(0)}\) is illustrated for the case \(m_a=m_b=10\mu\) at increasing scattering energies.

## Context

This work extends prior approaches that model three-particle states as effective two-particle states (e.g., rho-nucleon or sigma-nucleon states) by advocating for a full treatment of three-particle states and \(t\)-exchange processes. It builds on methods such as the Lorentz transform, which moves calculations away from the real axis and singularities, but notes those methods are rather complicated. The proposed orthogonal-basis expansion with known dispersion integrals aims to enable straightforward calculation of analytic and unitary transition amplitudes that incorporate resonant and \(t\)-exchange contributions, with the work under investigation for future application to systems such as \(\pi\pi N\) and \(\pi\rho N\).
