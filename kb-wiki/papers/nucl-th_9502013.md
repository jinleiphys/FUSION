# Up-Down Quark Mass Difference Effect in Nuclear Many-Body Systems

> A charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-nucleon force due to the up-down quark mass difference is evaluated in the quark cluster model. It is applied to the shell-model calculation for the isovector mass shifts of isospin multiplets and the isospin-mixing matrix elements in 1s0d-shell nuclei. We find that the contribution of the quark mass difference effect is large and agrees with experiment. This contribution may explain the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, alternatively to the meson-mixing contribution, which is recently predicted to be reduced by the large off-shell correction.

# Up-Down Quark Mass Difference Effect in Nuclear Many-Body Systems

## Key claim
The up-down quark mass difference, evaluated via a quark cluster model, produces a charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-nucleon force whose contribution to isovector mass shifts and isospin-mixing matrix elements in 1s0d-shell nuclei is large and agrees with experiment. This effect may explain the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly as an alternative to the meson-mixing contribution, which recent work suggests may be reduced by large off-shell corrections.

## Method
The authors constructed a charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-nucleon potential from the up-down quark mass difference using a nonrelativistic quark cluster model with one-gluon exchange, retaining only the local term of the hyperfine contact interaction. This potential was then applied in a shell-model calculation for 1s0d-shell nuclei using Wildenthal's effective hamiltonian, with short-range correlations included via a correlation function. The resulting isovector mass shifts and isospin-mixing matrix elements were computed in first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The up-down quark mass difference was taken as δm = 6 MeV, with average quark mass m̂ = 330 MeV, strong coupling constant αs = 1.624, and nucleon size parameter β⁻¹ = 0.616 fm.
- The quark effect contributions to isovector mass shifts are around 100 keV, with an average ratio of quark effect to Coulomb contributions of 5.3% for 143 multiplets.
- The calculated isospin-mixing matrix elements agree with most experimental values extracted from isospin-forbidden beta decays, with the quark contribution comparable to the electromagnetic contributions.

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological analyses by three of the present authors (Nakamura, Muto, Oka) which showed that experimental isovector mass shifts and isospin-mixing matrix elements in 1s0d-shell nuclei require a short-range charge-symmetry-breaking force, not a long-range one. The paper provides a microscopic origin for that force, contrasting with the meson-mixing (ρ-ω mixing) mechanism which Goldman et al. (1992) argued is reduced by large off-shell corrections, a finding supported by Piekarewicz (1993), Krein et al. (1993), and Hatsuda et al. (1994). The results enable further tests of whether the quark mass difference or meson mixing is the dominant source of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and motivate searches for short-range charge-symmetry-breaking effects in other nuclear systems.
