# Chiral dynamics: Quo vadis?

> I review the status of chiral dynamics. Topics include pion-pion scattering, dynamically generated states in the hadron spectrum and the emergence of two-pole structures, chiral symmetry in nuclear physics and chiral dynamics in the Big Bang.

# Chiral dynamics: Quo vadis?

## Key claim

Chiral dynamics remains a vital framework for low-energy QCD, but the field faces unresolved tensions between lattice QCD determinations and dispersive analyses, particularly for the pion-nucleon sigma-term and the precise properties of the lowest resonances. The author argues that lattice QCD errors are often underestimated, generating unnecessary tensions, and that chiral dynamics will not become obsolete even if lattice QCD reaches full physical-point control.

## Method

The paper is a review talk covering four areas: (1) pion-pion scattering, where chiral perturbation theory predictions are compared with Roy equation analyses and lattice QCD; (2) dynamically generated resonances via coupled-channel unitarization of chiral potentials, using the on-shell approximation T = V/[1+GV] to resum meson-baryon scattering; (3) chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces, including the N3LO chiral Hamiltonian and the wavefunction matching method that transforms high-fidelity interactions into easily computable forms; (4) Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints on fundamental constant variations, using the pion mass dependence of nuclear reactions mapped from light quark mass variations via the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.

## Key numbers

- Pion-pion S-wave scattering length a0: CHPT predictions range from 0.16 (LO) to 0.217 ± 0.009 (NNLO); Roy equation analysis gives a0 = 0.220 ± 0.005 (Colangelo 2000)
- Sigma meson (f0(500)) mass and half-width from dispersive analysis: Mσ = 441^(+16)_(-8) MeV, Γσ/2 = 272^(+9)_(-13) MeV (Caprini 2005)
- Lattice QCD determination of a0 = 0.2132^(+0.008)_(-0.009) and f0(500) mass Mσ = (443(3) - i 221(6)) MeV (Mai 2019)
- Pion-nucleon sigma-term from Roy-Steiner equations: σπN = 59.0(3.5) MeV (Hoferichter 2023)
- Dimension-two πN LECs from Roy-Steiner analysis: c1 = 1.10(3), c2 = 3.57(4), c3 = -5.54(6), c4 = 4.17(4), all in GeV^(-1)
- Delta resonance mass from Roy-Steiner analysis: MΔ = (1209.5(1.1) + i 98.5(1.2)) MeV
- Roper resonance mass: MR = (1374(3)(4) + i 215(18)(8)) MeV
- Higgs VEV variation bounds from BBN: δv/v ∈ [-0.0069, 0.0039] from ^4He abundance; δv/v ∈ [-0.0007, -0.0002] from ^2H abundance (Meyer 2024)
- Strong neutron-proton mass splitting: (mn - mp)_QCD = (1.87 ± 0.16) MeV (Gasser 2020)

## Context

This review extends the author's previous talks at CD2012 and CD2018, updating the status of chiral dynamics across multiple subfields. The Roy equation program for ππ scattering (Colangelo 2000) and πN scattering (Hoferichter 2015) provides high-precision benchmarks that lattice QCD is now approaching. The two-pole structure of the Λ(1405), first observed in coupled-channel chiral dynamics (Oller 2000) and explained from SU(3) symmetry breaking (Jido 2003), has been verified by many groups and now appears in the PDG tables as two separate resonances. The wavefunction matching method (Elhatisari 2022) enables NLEFT calculations beyond N2LO, demonstrating that pions are indispensable for precision nuclear structure despite the success of pionless approaches based on Wigner's SU(4) symmetry (Shen 2022). The BBN constraints on quark mass variations improve upon earlier work (Burns 2024) by including updated strong neutron-proton mass splitting and lattice QCD input for NN contact terms.
