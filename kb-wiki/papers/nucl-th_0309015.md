# Nuclear profile dependence of elliptic flow from a parton cascade

> The transverse profile dependence of elliptic flow is studied in a parton cascade model. We compare results from the binary scaling profile to results from the wounded nucleon scaling profile. The impact parameter dependence of elliptic flow is shown to depend sensitively on the transverse profile of initial particles, however, if elliptic flow is plotted as a function of the relative multiplicity, the nuclear profile dependence disappears. The insensitivity was found previously in a hydrodynamical calculation. Our calculations indicate that the insensitivity is also valid with additional viscous corrections. In addition, the minimum bias differential elliptic flow is demonstrated to be insensitive to the nuclear profile of the system.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow as a function of impact parameter depends sensitively on the initial transverse particle distribution (binary collision scaling versus wounded nucleon scaling), but when plotted against relative central rapidity density or as minimum bias differential elliptic flow, the nuclear profile dependence disappears even when viscous corrections are included.

## Method

The study uses Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) model with isotropic differential cross sections and thermal initial momentum distributions at 700 MeV temperature. Two initial transverse spatial distributions are compared: one proportional to the number of binary collisions and one proportional to the number of wounded nucleons, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile with a nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section of 40 mb. The total parton-parton elastic cross section is varied among 10, 20, and 40 mb to study the system's response, and the Boltzmann limit is approached by rescaling particle number and cross section simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: 700 MeV in the local rest frame
- Particle formation proper time: 0.1 fm/c
- Total of 2100 gluons per central event
- Parton-parton elastic cross sections varied: 10 mb, 20 mb, 40 mb
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section: 40 mb
- At impact parameter b = 10 fm, the wounded nucleon scaling has about twice as many particles as the binary collision scaling case
- Rescaling factors for binary collision scaling at b = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14 fm: lambda = 100, 100, 100, 220, 450, 1100, 5000, 50000
- Rescaling factors for wounded nucleon scaling at same impact parameters: lambda = 100, 100, 100, 200, 300, 600, 2000, 11000
- Minimum bias results are about 10% higher than the impact parameter averages

## Context

This work extends a previous hydrodynamical calculation by Kolb (2001) that found elliptic flow as a function of relative multiplicity is insensitive to nuclear profile, demonstrating that the insensitivity also holds when viscous corrections are included via a parton cascade. The study uses the same initial condition setup as Molnar's Parton Cascade (MPC) study from 2002, and the results for the sigma_gg = 40 mb binary collision scaling case are consistent with set D of that work. The findings suggest that elliptic flow as a function of relative rapidity density and minimum bias differential elliptic flow are promising observables for extracting information about final state interactions, independent of the initial nuclear profile.
