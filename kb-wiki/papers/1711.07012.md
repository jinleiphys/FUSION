# New quasibound states of the compound nucleus in $α$-particle capture by the nucleus

> We generalize the theory of nuclear decay and capture of Gamow that is based on tunneling through the barrier and internal oscillations inside the nucleus. In our formalism an additional factor is obtained, which describes distribution of the wave function of the $α$ particle inside the nuclear region. We discover new most stable states (called quasibound states) of the compound nucleus (CN) formed during the capture of $α$ particle by the nucleus. With a simple example, we explain why these states cannot appear in traditional calculations of the $α$ capture cross sections based on monotonic penetrabilities of a barrier, but they appear in a complete description of the evolution of the CN. Our result is obtained by a complete description of the CN evolution, which has the advantages of (1) a clear picture of the formation of the CN and its disintegration, (2) a detailed quantum description of the CN, (3) tests of the calculated amplitudes based on quantum mechanics (not realized in other approaches), and (4) high accuracy of calculations (not achieved in other approaches). These peculiarities are shown with the capture reaction of $α+ ^{44}{\rm Ca}$. We predict quasibound energy levels and determine fusion probabilities for this reaction. The difference between our approach and theory of quasistationary states with complex energies applied for the $α$ capture is also discussed. We show (1) that theory does not provide calculations for the cross section of $α$ capture (according to modern models of the $α$ capture), in contrast with our formalism, and (2) these two approaches describe different states of the $α$ capture (for the same $α$-nucleus potential).

## Key claim

The authors claim that new quasibound states of the compound nucleus (maximally stable states at specific incident alpha-particle energies) appear in a complete quantum description of the compound nucleus evolution, but these states cannot appear in traditional calculations of alpha capture cross sections that are based on monotonic barrier penetrabilities.

## Method

The authors generalize Gamow's theory of nuclear decay and capture by using the Multiple Internal Reflection (MIR) method, which approximates the potential barrier by rectangular steps with exact analytical solutions for wave functions at any energy. They calculate the probability of existence of the compound nucleus as the integral of the squared modulus of the wave function over the internal nuclear region, which factorizes into the product of the barrier penetrability, an oscillation amplitude factor, and a new localization factor describing the spatial distribution of the alpha particle inside the nucleus. The method is applied to the capture reaction of alpha particles by Ca-44.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous high-precision MIR method (Maydanyuk 2015) for determining fusion in alpha capture by including a new formalism for the evolution of the compound system after tunneling, with possible fusion. The approach contrasts with traditional methods based on the WKB approximation or the Hill-Wheeler approach (Hill and Wheeler 1953) and Wong's formula (Wong 1973), which the authors argue ignore the shape of the internal nuclear region and the external tail of the potential. The discovery of quasibound states enables future experimental tests of predicted cross sections for alpha capture by Ca-46, which is of interest due to possible new neutron magic numbers at N=16 and N=26.
