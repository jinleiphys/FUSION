# The virtual-state character of the {9}^Be 1/2^+ state in the {9}^Be(γ,n){8}^Be reaction

> We study the character of the first excited 1/2^{+} state of {9}^Be, which is observed as a low-lying sharp peak in the cross section of 9^Be(γ,n)2α just above the {8}^Be+n threshold. Using the α+α+n three-body model, we describe the ground and excited unbound states of {9}^Be above the α+α+n threshold. Applying the complex scaling method to the three-body model, we find no 1/2^{+} resonant solutions, while the low-lying peak in the photodisintegration cross section is reproduced in the present calculation. It is found that the low-lying peak is dominantly explained by the 8^Be+n component. Furthermore, using the analytical continuation of the coupling constant of the three-body interaction for the α+α+n system, we discuss the virtual-state character of the 1/2^{+} state.

## Key claim

The first excited 1/2+ state of 9Be, observed as a sharp peak just above the 8Be+n threshold in the 9Be(γ,n)2α cross section, is not a three-body resonance but is consistent with a virtual state of the 8Be+n system. This conclusion is drawn from the absence of any 1/2+ resonant solutions in the complex scaling method with a scaling angle of θ ≤ 15 degrees, combined with the reproduction of the experimental peak using an attractive three-body potential.

## Method

The authors employed an α+α+n three-body model for 9Be, solving the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method. They applied the complex scaling method (CSM) to treat unbound states and calculated the photodisintegration cross section via the E1 transition from the ground 3/2- state to 1/2+ states using the complex-scaled Green's function. The pole trajectory of the 1/2+ state was investigated by varying the strength of the three-body potential V3 through analytical continuation of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 9Be: 1.57 MeV (with three-body potential), compared to experimental value 1.5736 MeV
- Ground state charge radius: 2.53 fm (with three-body potential), compared to experimental value 2.519 ± 0.012 fm
- Ground state matter radius: 2.42 fm (with three-body potential), compared to experimental value 2.38 ± 0.01 fm
- Three-body potential parameters for ground state: v3 = 1.10 MeV, μ = 0.02 fm−2
- Three-body potential strength for reproducing the experimental peak: v3 = −1.02 MeV
- For v3 = −1.3 MeV, a 1/2+ resonance appears with energy 0.091 MeV and decay width 0.002 MeV, just below the 8Be+n threshold at 0.0918 MeV (both measured from the α+α+n threshold)

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing contradiction in the literature regarding the nature of the 9Be 1/2+ state. Efros et al. (1999) and Arai et al. (2003) previously suggested a virtual-state character based on scattering length and R-matrix analysis, while Garrido et al. (2010) and Rodriguez et al. (2010) argued for a three-body resonance with strong 5He+α configuration mixing. The present results support the virtual-state interpretation and show that the 8Be+n component dominates the photodisintegration cross section. This enables a more reliable determination of the 8Be(n,γ)9Be reaction rate for nuclear astrophysics applications, particularly for understanding heavy element production in supernova explosions.
