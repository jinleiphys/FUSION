# Ground state properties and bubble structure of superheavy nuclei

> We calculate the ground state properties of recently synthesized superheavy nuclei starting from $Z$=105-120. The nonrelativistic and relativistic mean field formalisms is used to evaluate the binding energy, charge radius, quadrupole deformation parameter and the density distribution of nucleons. We analyzed the stability of the nuclei based on the binding energy and neutron to proton ratio. We also studied the bubble structure of the nucleus which reveals about the special features of the superheavy nucleus.

## Key claim

The ground state properties of superheavy nuclei with Z=105-118 and the predicted proton magic Z=120 are calculated using both nonrelativistic (Skyrme Hartree-Fock with SkI4 parameters) and relativistic (RMF with NL3* parameters) mean field formalisms, with the caveat that experimental binding energies for these nuclei are not known, so the finite range droplet model (FRDM) is used as a reference for comparison.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SkI4 parameter set with BCS pairing and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* parameter set, solving the coupled equations in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with N_F = N_B = 20. For each nucleus, calculations were initiated with spherical, prolate, and oblate quadrupole deformation parameters to identify the ground state as the configuration with maximum binding energy. The binding energy, charge radius, quadrupole deformation parameter, and nucleon density distributions were computed, and alpha decay Q-values and half-lives were estimated using the Viola-Seaborg formula.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies from SHF(SkI4) and RMF(NL3*) deviate from FRDM predictions by a maximum of about 0.5%
- The neutron to proton ratio N:Z for the studied nuclei ranges from a minimum of 1.433 to a maximum of 1.614
- For $^{270}$Db, the experimental Q_alpha is 8.80 +/- 0.1 MeV, compared to SHF prediction of 8.38 MeV, RMF prediction of 8.19 MeV, and FRDM prediction of 7.84 MeV
- For $^{274}$Mt, the experimental alpha decay half-life is 472.6 ms, compared to SHF prediction of 8.48 ms and RMF prediction of 249.99 ms
- The charge radius r_ch for $^{304}$120 is 6.59 fm (SHF) and 6.678 fm (RMF)
- The quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 for $^{304}$120 is -0.008 (spherical SHF) and 0.000 (spherical RMF)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of superheavy nuclei stability and structure, including the macro-microscopic FRDM calculations of Moller et al. (1995, 1997) and earlier RMF and SHF calculations by Patra et al. (1997, 1999), Rutz et al. (1997), and Meng et al. (2007). The paper addresses the ongoing debate about the location of the next doubly magic nucleus beyond $^{208}$Pb, with some calculations (Ren et al., 2001, 2002) predicting deformed ground states while others (Muntian et al., 2004) find spherical shapes. The results enable comparison of two self-consistent mean field approaches for predicting properties of recently synthesized superheavy elements and candidate doubly magic nuclei such as $^{292,304}$120.
