# Peripheral heavy ion collisions as a probe of the nuclear gluon distribution

> At high energies a quark-gluon plasma is expected to be formed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The theoretical description of these processes is directly associated to a complete knowledge of the details of medium effects in the nuclear gluon distribution. In this paper we analyze the possibility to constraint the behavior of this distribution considering peripheral heavy ion collisions. We reanalyze the photoproduction of heavy quarks for the deduction of the in-medium gluon distribution using three current parameterizations for this parton distribution. Moreover, we show that the elastic photoproduction of vector mesons is a potential process to probe the nuclear gluon distribution.

## Key claim
Peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can constrain the nuclear gluon distribution, but the photoproduction of heavy quarks has only a linear dependence on the gluon distribution, making discrimination between models difficult; the elastic photoproduction of vector mesons, with its quadratic dependence on the gluon distribution, is a more sensitive probe of medium effects including high-density saturation, antishadowing, and the EMC effect.

## Method
The authors calculate cross sections for charm photoproduction and elastic J/psi photoproduction in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions using the equivalent photon (Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams) approximation with nuclear absorption. They compare predictions from three nuclear gluon distribution parameterizations: the unmodified GRV nucleon gluon distribution scaled by atomic number A, the EKS parameterization (DGLAP-based with medium effects), and the AG parameterization (which includes high-density saturation effects). For vector meson production, they use the leading-order pQCD color dipole formalism with two-gluon exchange, where the forward differential cross section is proportional to the square of the nuclear gluon distribution.

## Key numbers
- For charm photoproduction mass distribution in Pb+Pb at LHC, the EKS prediction is a factor 1.25 larger than the AG prediction at small invariant mass, while the GRV prediction is a factor 2.4 larger.
- The charm quark mass is taken as m_c = 1.45 GeV.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC, the Lorentz factor is gamma = 3000, and the nucleon momentum is p = 3000 GeV, giving x = (M/6000 GeV) e^{-y}.
- The maximum photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy W_gamma N reaches 950 GeV for LHC heavy-ion mode.
- The nuclear density uses a Woods-Saxon distribution with parameters c = 6.63 fm, a = 0.549 fm, and rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section used in the absorption factor is sigma_NN = 40 mb.

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals for using peripheral heavy ion collisions as photonuclear colliders (Hofmann et al., 1991; Baron and Baur, 1993; Greiner et al., 1995) by comparing three specific nuclear gluon distribution parameterizations: the unmodified GRV nucleon distribution (Gluck, Reya, Vogt, 1995), the EKS parameterization with DGLAP-based medium effects (Eskola, Kolhinen, Salgado, 1999), and the AG parameterization including high-density saturation (Ayala Filho and Goncalves, 2001). The authors propose for the first time that elastic J/psi photoproduction in peripheral heavy ion collisions, previously studied in ep collisions at HERA, can probe the nuclear gluon distribution across the full kinematic range including the small-x saturation regime, the antishadowing region, and the EMC effect region. This enables constraints on the initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC before future electron-ion colliders become operational.
