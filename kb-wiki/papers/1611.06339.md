# Inverse Magnetic Catalysis from improved Holographic QCD in the Veneziano limit

> We study the dependence of the chiral condensate on external magnetic field in the context of holographic QCD at large number of flavors. We consider a holographic QCD model where the flavor degrees of freedom fully backreact on the color dynamics. Perturbative QCD calculations have shown that $B$ acts constructively on the chiral condensate, a phenomenon called "magnetic catalysis". In contrast, recent lattice calculations show that, depending on the number of flavors and temperature, the magnetic field may also act destructively, which is called "inverse magnetic catalysis". Here we show that the holographic theory is capable of both behaviors depending on the choice of parameters. For reasonable choice of the potentials entering the model we find qualitative agreement with the lattice expectations. Our results provide insight for the physical reasons behind the inverse magnetic catalysis. In particular, we argue that the backreaction of the flavors to the background geometry decatalyzes the condensate.

## Key claim

The holographic V-QCD model in the Veneziano limit can exhibit both magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis depending on the choice of the coupling function \(w(\lambda)\), specifically the parameter \(c\) that controls its magnitude; inverse magnetic catalysis occurs for sufficiently small values of \(c\) (i.e., \(c < 0.4\)), where the coupling of the magnetic field to the glue dynamics becomes stronger.

## Method

The authors construct a fully backreacted holographic QCD model (V-QCD) in the Veneziano limit (\(N_f, N_c \to \infty\) with fixed ratio \(x = N_f/N_c\)), incorporating flavor degrees of freedom through a Sen-like action with a tachyon field dual to the chiral condensate. They introduce a background magnetic field via the vector combination of the left and right gauge fields, breaking rotational symmetry, and solve the coupled Einstein, dilaton, and tachyon equations numerically by shooting from the horizon to the boundary. The chiral condensate is extracted from the normalizable mode of the tachyon field at the boundary, with the non-normalizable mode set to zero (massless quarks).

## Key numbers

- For \(x=1\) and \(c=0.4\), the chiral transition temperature \(T_\chi\) decreases with increasing magnetic field, signaling inverse magnetic catalysis; the condensate vanishes at \(B/\Lambda^2 = 0.116\) for \(T/\Lambda = 0.143\).
- The normalized condensate at zero temperature behaves as \(\Delta \Sigma(0,B) = D_{\bar qq}(c) B^2 + \cdots\) with \(D_{\bar qq}(0.25) \simeq 124\), \(D_{\bar qq}(0.4) \simeq 58\), \(D_{\bar qq}(1) \simeq 5.3\), and \(D_{\bar qq}(3) \simeq 1.8\) (units of \(\Lambda^{-4}\)).
- For \(x=1\) and \(c=0.4\), the deconfinement temperature \(T_d\) decreases with \(B\) for small \(c\) values; for \(c \gtrsim 1\) the dip is suppressed and \(T_d\) grows with \(B\).
- The magnetic susceptibility \(\chi_B\) at \(B=0\) is larger for smaller \(c\) and for larger \(x\); kinks appear at the chiral transition \(T = T_\chi\) for \(x=1\).
- For \(x=0.1\) and \(c=0.4\), an additional deconfined chirally symmetric phase appears, separated by a first-order transition, with a jump in susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of magnetic catalysis (e.g., Gusynin (1994), Zayakin (2008), Filev (2011)) to the Veneziano limit where flavor backreaction is fully included, enabling the first holographic demonstration of inverse magnetic catalysis. It qualitatively reproduces lattice results from Bali (2011, 2012) and D'Elia (2012), which showed that the magnetic field suppresses the chiral condensate at temperatures near the deconfinement crossover. The model enables future phenomenological matching to QCD observables such as the electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma, and provides a framework to disentangle valence and sea quark contributions to the condensate in the strong-coupling regime.
