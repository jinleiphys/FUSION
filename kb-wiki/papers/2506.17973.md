# Investigation of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting via heavy ion collisions: Constraints and Implications

> The neutron-proton effective mass splitting ($Δm^*_{np}$) is investigated through analyses of heavy-ion collisions using the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with both standard and extended Skyrme interactions. By uncovering the strong correlation between the slope of the neutron-to-proton yield ratio with respect to the kinetic energy (i.e., $S_{n/p} $) and $Δm^*_{np}$, we reveal that the constraints of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting via heavy ion collisions depend on the kinetic energy region of the emitted nucleons. At low kinetic energies, the data favor $m_n^*>m_p^*$ which is consistent with the nucleon-nucleus scattering analysis, while at high kinetic energies, they favor $m_n^*<m_p^*$. Our findings partly resolve the longstanding discrepancy in the constraints of neutron-proton effective mass splitting with heavy ion collisions and nucleon-nucleus scattering, and significantly advance the understanding of nucleon effective mass splitting through heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The constraints on the neutron-proton effective mass splitting from heavy-ion collisions depend on the kinetic energy region of the emitted nucleons: at low kinetic energies the data favor \(m_n^* > m_p^*\) (consistent with nucleon-nucleus scattering), while at high kinetic energies they favor \(m_n^* < m_p^*\), thereby partly resolving the longstanding discrepancy between constraints from heavy-ion collisions and nucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Method

The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with both standard and extended Skyrme interactions to simulate \(^{112,124}\text{Sn}+^{112,124}\text{Sn}\) collisions. They calculated the slope of the neutron-to-proton yield ratio with respect to kinetic energy (\(S_{n/p}\)) and the double ratio slope (\(S_{DR}\)) across a multi-parameter space including the symmetry energy coefficient \(S_0\), its slope \(L\), the isoscalar effective mass \(m_s^*/m\), and the effective mass splitting parameter \(f_I = (m/m_n^* - m/m_p^*)/(2\delta)\). By comparing the calculated slopes to experimental data, they extracted the sign of the effective mass splitting favored in different kinetic energy regions.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^{124}\text{Sn}+^{124}\text{Sn}\) system, the correlation coefficient between \(S_{n/p}\) and \(\Delta m_{np}^*\) reaches 0.928.
* The nucleon-nucleus scattering analysis constrains the effective mass splitting at normal density as \(\Delta m_{np}^* = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\delta\) (where \(\delta\) is the isospin asymmetry), for nucleon kinetic energies below 100 MeV.
* Bayesian analysis of giant resonances in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) yields \(\Delta m_{np}^* = (0.084_{-0.123}^{+0.143})\delta\).
* Bayesian analysis of neutron and proton single and double ratios from heavy-ion collisions gives \(\Delta m_{np}^* = (-0.05 \pm 0.09)\delta\).
* Combined analysis of S\(\pi\)RIT flow and stopping data constrains \(\Delta m_{np}^* = (-0.07_{-0.06}^{+0.07})\delta\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Yang et al., 2024) which first noted that emitted nucleons at different kinetic energies favor different signs of effective mass splitting, but only varied the slope of symmetry energy \(L\) and \(\Delta m_{np}^*\) while fixing \(S_0 = 32\) MeV and \(m_s^*/m = 0.77\). The present study expands the analysis to a multi-parameter space including \(S_0\), \(L\), \(m_s^*/m\), and \(\Delta m_{np}^*\). The findings reconcile the discrepancy between constraints from nucleon-nucleus scattering (Li et al.) which favor \(m_n^* > m_p^*\), and previous heavy-ion collision analyses (Morfouace et al., 2019; Sang et al., 2024) which favor \(m_n^* \approx m_p^*\) or \(m_n^* < m_p^*\). This work enables future studies of the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and its implications for neutron star properties.
