# Ground and excited states Gamow-Teller strength distributions of iron isotopes and associated capture rates for core-collapse simulations

> This paper reports on the microscopic calculation of ground and excited states Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions, both in the electron capture and electron decay direction, for $^{54,55,56}$Fe. The associated electron and positron capture rates for these isotopes of iron are also calculated in stellar matter. These calculations were recently introduced and this paper is a follow-up which discusses in detail the GT strength distributions and stellar capture rates of key iron isotopes. The calculations are performed within the framework of the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory. The pn-QRPA theory allows a microscopic \textit{state-by-state} calculation of GT strength functions and stellar capture rates which greatly increases the reliability of the results. For the first time experimental deformation of nuclei are taken into account. In the core of massive stars isotopes of iron, $^{54,55,56}$Fe, are considered to be key players in decreasing the electron-to-baryon ratio ($Y_{e}$) mainly via electron capture on these nuclide. The structure of the presupernova star is altered both by the changes in $Y_{e}$ and the entropy of the core material. Results are encouraging and are compared against measurements (where possible) and other calculations. The calculated electron capture rates are in overall good agreement with the shell model results. During the presupernova evolution of massive stars, from oxygen shell burning stages till around end of convective core silicon burning, the calculated electron capture rates on $^{54}$Fe are around three times bigger than the corresponding shell model rates. The calculated positron capture rates, however, are suppressed by two to five orders of magnitude.

# Ground and excited states Gamow-Teller strength distributions of iron isotopes and associated capture rates for core-collapse simulations

## Key claim

The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) calculation, incorporating for the first time experimentally measured nuclear deformations, produces Gamow-Teller strength distributions and stellar electron/positron capture rates for 54,55,56Fe that are in overall good agreement with shell model results, but with the important qualification that during presupernova evolution from oxygen shell burning through convective core silicon burning, the calculated electron capture rates on 54Fe are around three times larger than the corresponding shell model rates, while positron capture rates are suppressed by two to five orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic state-by-state calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions in both the electron capture (GT+) and electron decay (GT-) directions for 54,55,56Fe using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with a Nilsson model single-particle basis, BCS pairing, and separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions characterized by strength parameters chi = 0.15 MeV and kappa = 0.07 MeV. Experimentally adopted deformation parameters from Raman et al. (1987) were used for 54,56Fe, while the deformation of 55Fe was calculated from its electric quadrupole moment; an effective axial-vector coupling quenching factor of (gA/gV)^2_eff = 0.60 times the bare value was applied. Stellar electron and positron capture rates were then computed by summing over 246 parent excited states for 54Fe, 297 states for 55Fe, and 266 states for 56Fe, each with transitions to 150 daughter excited states, incorporating experimental excitation energies and log ft values where available.

## Key numbers

- Total GT- strength for 54Fe: pn-QRPA = 7.56, experiment = 7.5 ± 0.7, large-scale shell model (LSSM) = 7.11
- Total GT+ strength for 54Fe: pn-QRPA = 4.26, experiment = 3.5 ± 0.7, LSSM = 3.56
- Total GT- strength for 56Fe: pn-QRPA = 10.74, experiment = 9.9 ± 2.4, LSSM = 9.80
- Total GT+ strength for 56Fe: pn-QRPA = 3.71, experiment = 2.9 ± 0.3, LSSM = 2.70
- GT+ centroid for 54Fe: pn-QRPA = 4.06 MeV, LSSM = 3.78 MeV, experiment = 3.7 ± 0.2 MeV (from Ronnqvist et al. 1993)
- GT+ centroid for 56Fe: pn-QRPA = 3.13 MeV, LSSM = 2.60 MeV, experiment = 2.9 ± 0.2 MeV (from El-Kateb et al. 1994)
- GT- centroid for 54Fe from discrete peaks: experiment = 7.63 MeV, pn-QRPA = 5.08 MeV
- GT- centroid for 56Fe from discrete peaks: experiment = 8.27 MeV, pn-QRPA = 5.61 MeV
- Pairing gaps used: Delta_p = Delta_n = 12/sqrt(A) MeV
- Nilsson oscillator constant: hbar-omega = 41 A^(-1/3) MeV
- D value for capture rate formula: 6295 s
- Effective coupling ratio: (gA/gV)^2_eff = 0.60 × (gA/gV)^2_bare, with (gA/gV)_bare = -1.254

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of stellar weak interaction rates previously performed by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999, 2004) for sd-shell and fp/fpg-shell nuclei, and is a follow-up to a recent introduction (Nabi 2009) that reported improvements from incorporating measured deformation values. The calculations address the need for reliable microscopic weak interaction rates identified by Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1982) in their pioneering FFN compilation, and respond to the finding by Aufderheide et al. (1994) that 54,55,56Fe rank among the most influential nuclei for electron capture during early presupernova collapse, as well as the ranking by Heger et al. (2001) of these isotopes as top-three nuclei for decreasing the electron-to-baryon ratio in 25 solar mass stars. The results provide an alternative microscopic input for core-collapse supernova simulators, potentially improving the determination of presupernova core structure, entropy, and lepton fraction compared to earlier parameterized approaches.
