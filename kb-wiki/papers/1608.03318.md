# Can Ab Initio Theory Explain the Phenomenon of Parity Inversion in ${}^{11}$Be?

> The weakly bound exotic ${}^{11}$Be nucleus, famous for its ground-state parity inversion and distinct n+ ${}^{10}$Be halo structure, is investigated from first principles using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. An explicit treatment of continuum effects is found to be indispensable. We study the sensitivity of the ${}^{11}$Be spectrum to the details of the three-nucleon force and demonstrate that only certain chiral interactions are capable of reproducing the parity inversion. With such interactions, the extremely large E1 transition between the bound states is reproduced. We compare our photodisintegration calculations to conflicting experimental data and predict a distinct dip around the $3/2^-_1$ resonance energy. Finally, we predict low-lying $3/2^+$ and $9/2^+$ resonances that are not or not sufficiently measured in experiments.

## Key claim

Only the simultaneously fitted chiral NN+3N interaction (N2LO_SAT) can reproduce the parity inversion between the 1/2+ and 1/2- bound states in 11Be, and then only marginally; traditionally fitted three-nucleon forces with varying cutoffs fail to achieve this inversion.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations for 11Be, combining a no-core shell model (NCSM) expansion for short-range correlations with an explicit treatment of n+10Be cluster states for continuum effects. They used chiral two- and three-nucleon forces softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG), and solved the coupled-channel equations using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, extracting resonance energies and widths from complex S-matrix poles.

## Key numbers

- The 1/2+ ground state of 11Be is bound by 502 keV with respect to the n+10Be threshold, separated by only 320 keV from its parity-inverted 1/2- partner
- For the N2LO_SAT interaction at Nmax=9, the 1/2+ state energy is -0.40 MeV and the 1/2- state energy is -0.35 MeV relative to the n+10Be threshold
- The B(E1: 1/2- to 1/2+) transition strength is 0.127 e2fm2 for N2LO_SAT NCSMC and 0.117 e2fm2 for the phenomenologically adjusted NCSMC-pheno, compared to the experimental value of 0.102(2) e2fm2 from KwWu14
- The 3/2-1 resonance energy is 2.14 MeV with width 0.21 MeV for N2LO_SAT at Nmax=9, matching the experimental 2.15 MeV and 0.21 MeV
- The 5/2+ resonance energy is 1.47 MeV with width 0.12 MeV for N2LO_SAT, compared to experimental 1.28 MeV and 0.1 MeV
- The 9/2+ resonance is predicted at 5.42 MeV with width 0.80 MeV for N2LO_SAT
- The S-wave asymptotic normalization coefficient for the 11Be ground state is 0.786 fm-1/2
- The n+10Be threshold energy at Nmax=9 is -58.4 MeV, with an extrapolated value of -60.9(10) MeV, compared to the experimental -64.976 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier ab initio investigations of 11Be by FoNa05 (which did not include three-nucleon forces) and QuNa08, QuNa09 (which were incomplete in treatment of short-range correlations). The NCSMC framework combines the capabilities of those prior approaches. The study demonstrates that 11Be provides a stringent benchmark for chiral nuclear forces, and the N2LO_SAT interaction (EkJa15, HaEk16, MiBa16) succeeds where traditionally fitted three-nucleon forces fail. The results enable future investigation of the mirror system p+10C and provide predictions for unmeasured 3/2+ and 9/2+ resonances that can guide experiments.
