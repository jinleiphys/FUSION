# Hot and dense quark-gluon plasma thermodynamics from holographic black holes

> We present new results on the equation of state and transition line of hot and dense strongly interacting QCD matter, obtained from a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model. We considerably expand the previous coverage in baryon densities in this model by implementing new numerical methods to map the holographic black hole solutions onto the QCD phase diagram. We are also able to obtain, for the first time, the first-order phase transition line in a wide region of the phase diagram. Comparisons with the most recent lattice results for the QCD thermodynamics are also presented.

## Key claim

The authors present a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model that, for the first time, maps the first-order phase transition line beyond the critical point in a wide region of the QCD phase diagram, though the model's predictions are contingent on the specific choice of free parameters fixed by lattice QCD inputs at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors solve the classical equations of motion of a five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton gravity action in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter spacetime, using a new numerical procedure with a variable-step Adams-Bashforth-Moulton solver (ode113 in MATLAB) to integrate the bulk field equations from the black hole horizon to the boundary. The free parameters of the model (the dilaton potential V(phi), the Maxwell coupling f(phi), the five-dimensional Newton constant, and the energy scale Lambda) are fixed by matching the holographic equation of state and second-order baryon susceptibility at zero chemical potential to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2013) and Bellwied (2015). Thermodynamic observables at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential are then extracted from the holographic black hole solutions using standard gauge/gravity duality formulas.

## Key numbers

- The energy scale Lambda is fixed to 1058.83 MeV.
- The model predicts a critical point at T approximately 89 MeV and mu_B approximately 724 MeV.
- The equation of state is computed over a temperature range from 2 MeV to 550 MeV and a baryon chemical potential range from 0 to 1100 MeV.
- The five-dimensional Newton constant is fixed as kappa_5^2 = 8 pi G_5 = 8 pi (0.46).
- The dilaton potential is given by V(phi) = -12 cosh(0.63 phi) + 0.65 phi^2 - 0.05 phi^4 + 0.003 phi^6.
- The Maxwell coupling function parameters are c1 = -0.27, c2 = 0.4, c3 = 1.7, and c4 = 100.

## Context

This work extends the earlier holographic EMD model of Critelli (2017) by overcoming numerical difficulties that previously prevented locating the first-order phase transition line beyond the critical point and by covering a much larger region of the (T, mu_B) phase diagram. The model is built on the general gauge/gravity duality framework developed by Gubser (2008), DeWolfe (2010), and DeWolfe (2011), but differs by using state-of-the-art lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential to fix the free parameters rather than older lattice data. The results enable comparisons with the latest lattice QCD thermodynamics from Borsanyi (2021) and provide an equation of state needed for simulations of heavy-ion collisions at low beam energies, particularly for experiments at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.
