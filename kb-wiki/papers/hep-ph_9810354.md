# Reason for the large scale differences in the results for the neutron-antineutron oscillation problem

> It is shown that there is a double counting in the standard model of $n\bar {n}$ mixing in the medium, resulting in full cancellation of leading terms. The direct calculation of $n\bar{n}$ transition followed by annihilation is performed. # Reason for the large scale differences in the results for the neutron-antineutron oscillation problem

## Key claim

The standard potential model of neutron-antineutron mixing in a nucleus contains a double counting that leads to full cancellation of the leading terms, producing a result that is incorrect by 16 orders of magnitude. When the direct calculation of the two-step process (neutron-to-antineutron conversion followed by annihilation) is performed without this double counting, the free-space oscillation time limit is only about τ_nn̄ > 10^16 years, not the much longer limit previously claimed.

## Method

The author performs a direct calculation of the process (nucleus) → (antineutron-nucleus) → (annihilation products) using an approach with a finite time interval to avoid infrared singularities that arise in the standard S-matrix formulation. The interaction Hamiltonian is taken in general form, making the basic part of the calculation model-independent. The amplitude for the whole process is expressed as an integral over the exact antineutron-nucleus decay amplitude, and the probability is evaluated in the lowest order in the small mixing parameter ε = 1/τ_nn̄.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit for free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time: τ_nn̄ > 10^16 years
- This limit exceeds the previous one by 16 orders of magnitude
- Annihilation width of antineutron-nucleus state: Γ ∼ 100 MeV
- Characteristic annihilation time of antineutron: Δ = 1/Γ ∼ 10^-23 seconds
- Experimental parameters used: observation time T0 = 1.3 years, total number of neutrons Nn = 2.4 × 10^32, overall detection efficiency ε1 = 0.33, average number of observable events θ = 2.3
- Previous limit from free neutrons: τ_nn̄ > 10^7 seconds
- Previous limit from nuclear experiments (potential model): τ_nn̄ > 10^8 seconds ∼ 1 year

## Context

This work contradicts the standard potential model of neutron-antineutron mixing in nuclei developed by Dover et al. (1980s) and the nonrelativistic diagram technique of other authors, which predicted drastic suppression of the transition in nuclei. The paper extends the earlier finite-time-interval approach of Nazaruk (1998) and argues that the potential model is inapplicable because it includes redundant final states (antineutron-nucleus) that are forbidden by unitarity when only annihilation products are detected. The result enables a much more stringent experimental test of grand unified theories, since the free-space oscillation time limit from nuclear stability experiments becomes comparable to the limit from free neutron experiments rather than being many orders of magnitude weaker.
