# Chiral asymmetry and axial anomaly in magnetized relativistic matter

> The induced axial current and the chiral anomaly are studied in the normal phase of magnetized relativistic matter. A special attention is paid to the role of the chiral shift parameter Delta, leading to a relative shift of the longitudinal momenta in the dispersion relations of opposite chirality fermions. In the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, it is shown directly from the form of the gap equation that Delta necessarily exists in the normal phase in a magnetic field. By making use of the gauge invariant point-splitting regularization, we then show that the presence of Delta essentially modifies the form of the axial current, but does not affect the conventional axial anomaly relation. By recalculating the axial current with the proper-time regularization, we conclude that the result is robust with respect to a specific regularization scheme used.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the normal phase of magnetized relativistic matter, the chiral shift parameter \(\Delta\) necessarily exists due to interactions, and while it substantially modifies the form of the induced axial current, it does not alter the conventional axial anomaly relation.

## Method

The authors analyze the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with one fermion flavor in a constant external magnetic field, using the mean field approximation. They derive gap equations for the effective chemical potential, chiral shift parameter, and fermion mass, then compute the induced axial current and its divergence using gauge invariant point-splitting regularization and proper-time regularization.

## Key numbers

- The induced axial current density for free fermions in a magnetic field is \(\langle j^3_5\rangle_0 = \frac{eB}{2\pi^2}\mu_0\) (Eq. MZ).
- The chiral shift parameter in the NJL model scales as \(\Delta \simeq -g \mu\frac{eB}{\Lambda^2\left(1+2 a g\right)}\), where \(g\) is the dimensionless coupling and \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet cutoff.
- The total axial current density including both topological and dynamical contributions is \(\langle j^{3}_5 \rangle \simeq \frac{eB}{2\pi^{2}}\frac{\mu}{\left(1+ 2 a g\right)}\).
- In the proper-time regularization, the chiral shift parameter is \(\Delta \simeq -g \mu \frac{eB}{\Lambda^2 (1+g/2)}\) and the axial current density is \(\langle j_{3}^5 \rangle \simeq \frac{eB}{2\pi^{2}}\frac{\mu}{\left(1+g/2\right)}\), with the constant \(a = 1/4\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Metlitski (2005) of a topological contribution to the axial current at the lowest Landau level, and the discovery by Gorbar (2009) of the chiral shift parameter \(\Delta\) in interacting magnetized matter. The paper clarifies that the dynamical contribution from \(\Delta\) is generated by all Landau levels (mostly in the ultraviolet), while the topological part is confined to the lowest Landau level and connected to the axial anomaly. The results enable further studies of chiral asymmetry in compact stars and heavy ion collisions, and suggest that in renormalizable theories like QED and QCD, the cutoff \(\Lambda\) would be replaced by a physical scale such as \(\sqrt{|eB|}\).
