# Proton structure corrections to hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen

> We present the derivation of the formulas for the proton structure-dependent terms in the hyperfine splitting of muonic hydrogen. We use compatible conventions throughout the calculations to derive a consistent set of formulas that reconcile differences between our results and some specific terms in earlier work. Convention conversion corrections are explicitly presented, which reduce the calculated hyperfine splitting by about 46 ppm. We also note that using only modern fits to the proton elastic form factors gives a smaller than historical spread of Zemach radii and leads to a reduced uncertainty in the hyperfine splitting. Additionally, hyperfine splittings have an impact on the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift/proton radius measurement, however the correction we advocate has a small effect there.

## Key claim

The paper derives a consistent set of formulas for proton structure-dependent corrections to the hyperfine splitting (HFS) in muonic hydrogen, and identifies that combining elastic and polarizability terms from incompatible sources introduces a missing correction term of about 46 ppm, which reduces the calculated HFS.

## Method

The authors performed a dispersive calculation of the two-photon exchange diagram for muonic hydrogen, keeping the lepton mass general throughout. They derived analytic expressions for the Zemach, recoil, and polarizability contributions using dispersion relations for the spin-dependent Compton amplitude, with the imaginary parts given by the measured spin structure functions \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) for inelastic scattering and by elastic form factors for the elastic contribution. They then compared their results with prior calculations to identify where convention differences arise, particularly in the treatment of the \(F_2^2\) term in the polarizability correction.

## Key numbers

- The convention conversion correction reduces the calculated hyperfine splitting by about 46 ppm, or about 1.0 \(\mu\)eV.
- For the 2S state of muonic hydrogen, the HFS values using different form factor fits range from 22.8105 meV (Arrington and Sick fit) to 22.8187 meV (Mainz 2010 fit).
- The Zemach radii from modern form factor fits range from 1.045 fm (Mainz 2010) to 1.091 fm (Arrington and Sick).
- The uncertainty in the polarizability contribution is \(\pm 114\) ppm or \(\pm 2.6\ \mu\)eV.
- The uncertainty in the elastic contributions from the spread of form factor parameterizations is \(\pm 4.1\ \mu\)eV (\(\pm 180\) ppm).
- The combined uncertainty gives a recommended value \(E_{HFS}^{2S} = 22.8146 (49)\) meV, compared with 22.8148 (78) meV from Martynenko (2004).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier calculations (Nazaryan 2005, Carlson 2008) and reconciles differences with the polarizability calculation of Faustov (2001) and the elastic calculations of Bodwin (1987) and Pachucki (1996). The correction has a small effect on the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement of the proton charge radius (Pohl 2010), translating to less than 1% of the discrepancy between that value and the CODATA value. The paper enables more accurate future HFS measurements by clarifying which combinations of elastic and polarizability terms are consistent, and by noting that modern form factor fits reduce the spread of Zemach radii and thus the uncertainty in the HFS.
