# Properties of Mesons in a Strong Magnetic Field

> By extending the $Φ$-derivable approach in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to finite magnetic field we calculate the properties of pion, $σ$ and $ρ$ mesons in a magnetic field at finite temperature in not only the quark-antiquark bound state scheme but also the pion-pion scattering resonant state scenario. Our calculation results manifest that the masses of $π^{0}$ and $σ$ meson can be nearly degenerate at the pseudo-critical temperature which increases with increasing the magnetic field strength, and the $π^{\pm}$ mass ascends suddenly at almost the same critical temperature. While the $ρ$ mesons' masses decrease with the temperature but increase with the magnetic field strength. We also check the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and find that the relation can be violated obviously with increasing the temperature, and the effect of the magnetic field becomes pronounced around the critical temperature. With different criteria, we analyze the effect of the magnetic field on the chiral phase transition and find that the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral phase cross, $T_{c}^χ$, is always enhanced by the magnetic field. Moreover our calculations indicate that the $ρ$ mesons will get melted as the chiral symmetry has not yet been restored, but the $σ$ meson does not disassociate even at very high temperature. Particularly, it is the first to show that there does not exist vector meson condensate in the QCD vacuum in the pion-pion scattering scheme.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is always enhanced by the magnetic field (magnetic catalysis), but this result is obtained within the specific framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model extended with a Phi-derivable approach and a smooth cutoff, and the paper acknowledges that lattice QCD calculations have shown an opposite behavior (inverse magnetic catalysis).

## Method

The authors extended the Phi-derivable approach in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to finite magnetic field and finite temperature, calculating meson properties both in the quark-antiquark bound state scheme and in the pion-pion scattering resonant state scenario. They used the Matsubara formalism for finite temperature, introduced a smooth cutoff to handle the discretization of transverse momentum into Landau levels, and solved the gap equation for the constituent quark mass along with the pole equations for meson propagators to obtain meson masses.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field, the pseudo-critical temperature from the chiral susceptibility peak is T_c = 191 MeV.
- The zero-temperature vacuum masses used for parameter fixing: f_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_pi = 135.0 MeV, m_rho = 770.0 MeV, and quark condensate <u-bar u>^{1/3} = -240.8 MeV.
- The calculated masses of pi^0 and pi^pm at very weak magnetic field and zero temperature are 135.1 MeV and 142.4 MeV respectively (experimental m_pi+ = 139.6 MeV, error about 2%).
- The phase boundary in the T-eB plane is parameterized as T_c = 191 + 1.827(eB) - 0.109(eB)^2 + 0.00264(eB)^3, with eB in units of m_pi^2.
- At eB = 10.0 m_pi^2, the pseudo-critical temperature from chiral susceptibility is T_c^chi = 201 MeV; at eB = 20.0, T_c^chi = 205 MeV; at eB = 30.0, T_c^chi = 222 MeV; at eB = 40.0, T_c^chi = 258 MeV.
- The rho^0 meson melting temperature (where its mass degenerates with 2M) is T_m^{rho0} = 155 MeV at eB = 0, and increases to 194 MeV at eB = 10.0, 215 MeV at eB = 20.0, and 245 MeV at eB = 30.0 m_pi^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, particularly addressing the debate between magnetic catalysis (where the critical temperature increases with magnetic field, as found by Miransky (2002) and others) and inverse magnetic catalysis (where the critical temperature decreases, as found in lattice QCD calculations by Bali (2012) and Ilgenfritz (2013)). The paper also addresses the open question of whether a rho meson condensate exists in strong magnetic fields, which had been debated in works by Andreichikov (2013), Hidaka (2013), Taya (2015), and Huang (2015). The results enable further investigation of the chiral phase transition and meson melting phenomena in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
