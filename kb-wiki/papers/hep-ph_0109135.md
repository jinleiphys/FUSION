# Crystalline Color Superconductivity

> We give an introduction crystalline color superconductivity, arguing that it is likely to occur wherever quark matter in which color-flavor locking does not occur is found. We survey the properties of this form of quark matter, and argue that its presence in a compact star may result in pulsar glitches, and thus in observable consequences. However, elucidation of this proposal requires an understanding of the crystal structure, which is not yet in hand.

## Key claim

Crystalline color superconductivity is likely to occur generically wherever quark matter that is not in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase exists, but the paper acknowledges that elucidation of this proposal requires an understanding of the crystal structure, which is not yet in hand.

## Method

The author analyzes crystalline color superconductivity using a simplified two-flavor model in which up and down quarks have a chemical potential difference introduced by hand, rather than considering CFL pairing in the presence of quark mass differences. The gap equation is derived variationally and diagrammatically, first assuming a point-like four-fermion interaction, then extended to single-gluon exchange with a medium-modified gluon propagator in the hard dense loop approximation. The free energy of the LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) state with nonzero momentum Cooper pairs is compared to that of the BCS state and the unpaired state to determine the window of chemical potential differences where crystalline pairing is favored.

## Key numbers

- The BCS state persists for chemical potential differences $\delta\mu < \delta\mu_1 = \Delta_0/\sqrt{2}$, where $\Delta_0$ is the BCS pairing gap at $\delta\mu=0$
- The LOFF gap parameter decreases from $0.23 \Delta_0$ at $\delta\mu=\delta\mu_1$ to zero at $\delta\mu=\delta\mu_2$
- For a point-like interaction in the weak-coupling limit, $\delta\mu_2 = 0.754\Delta_0$
- For single-gluon exchange at coupling $g=3.43$ (corresponding to $\mu=400$ MeV), $\delta\mu_2/\Delta_0 \approx 1.2$, giving $(\delta\mu_2-\delta\mu_1) \approx (1.2-1/\sqrt{2})\Delta_0$
- For single-gluon exchange at weak coupling $g=0.79$, $\delta\mu_2/\Delta_0 > 1000$
- The critical temperature above which the LOFF state melts is $T_c = \Delta\sqrt{3/2\pi^2}$

## Context

This work extends the LOFF mechanism originally discovered by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) in electron superconductors to the context of QCD, building on earlier studies of color superconductivity such as the 2SC phase and the CFL phase. The paper argues that because gluon exchange is forward-scattering dominated (unlike point-like interactions), the crystalline color superconductivity window is much wider than previously thought, making this phase generic wherever quark matter not in the CFL phase occurs. The results enable future work on three-flavor analyses, determination of the favored crystal structure, and quantitative predictions for pulsar glitch phenomena if a shell of crystalline color superconducting quark matter exists in compact stars.
