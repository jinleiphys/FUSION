# Basic features of the pion valence-quark distribution function

> The impulse-approximation expression used hitherto to define the pion's valence-quark distribution function is flawed because it omits contributions from the gluons which bind quarks into the pion. A corrected leading-order expression produces the model-independent result that quarks dressed via the rainbow-ladder truncation, or any practical analogue, carry all the pion's light-front momentum at a characteristic hadronic scale. Corrections to the leading contribution may be divided into two classes, responsible for shifting dressed-quark momentum into glue and sea-quarks. Working with available empirical information, we use an algebraic model to express the principal impact of both classes of corrections. This enables a realistic comparison with experiment that allows us to highlight the basic features of the pion's measurable valence-quark distribution, $q^π(x)$; namely, at a characteristic hadronic scale, $q^π(x) \sim (1-x)^2$ for $x\gtrsim 0.85$; and the valence-quarks carry approximately two-thirds of the pion's light-front momentum.

## Key claim

The impulse-approximation expression used previously to define the pion's valence-quark distribution function is flawed because it omits contributions from gluons that bind quarks into the pion; a corrected leading-order expression produces the model-independent result that quarks dressed via the rainbow-ladder truncation carry all the pion's light-front momentum at a characteristic hadronic scale, but corrections that shift momentum into glue and sea-quarks are necessary for realistic comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors corrected the standard handbag diagram for virtual Compton scattering by including two additional diagrams (B and C) that had been overlooked, which together ensure preservation of Ward-Green-Takahashi identities in the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. They derived an amended expression for the quark distribution function that includes a derivative of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, then used an algebraic model with a Nakanishi-type representation for the dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude to compute the distribution analytically. Corrections to the rainbow-ladder truncation were separated into two classes: those redistributing baryon-number into the dressed-quark sea (class C1) and those shifting momentum into the dressed-gluon distribution (class C2), with empirical Drell-Yan data used to constrain the sea-quark profile.

## Key numbers

- At a characteristic hadronic scale, the pion's valence-quark distribution behaves as \(q^\pi(x) \sim (1-x)^2\) for \(x\gtrsim 0.85\).
- Valence-quarks carry approximately two-thirds of the pion's light-front momentum.
- In rainbow-ladder truncation, the dressed-quark and -antiquark each carry exactly half the pion's momentum: \(\langle x \rangle_q^\pi = 1/2\).
- The meson-loop correction to the pion electromagnetic form factor at \(Q^2=0\) is an O(5%) effect, leading to the choice \(Z=0.05\) for the total flux associated with resonant fluctuations.
- The characteristic hadronic scale is taken as \(\zeta_{\rm H} \geq 2\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 0.5\) GeV.
- The algebraic model with \(\nu=1\) yields the asymptotic pion parton distribution amplitude \(\varphi_\pi(x) = 6x(1-x)\).
- The empirical sea-quark distribution at \(\zeta_{\rm H}=0.51\) GeV is parameterized with \(\bar\alpha=0.16\), \(\bar\beta=5.20\), \(\bar\gamma=3.243\), \(\bar\delta=5.206\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the pion's parton distribution function beyond the standard handbag diagram used in earlier studies such as Shigetani (1993), Davidson (1994), and Nguyen (2011), correcting a flaw in the impulse approximation that omitted gluon binding contributions. The corrected expression produces a symmetric dressed-quark distribution in rainbow-ladder truncation, resolving an apparent disagreement between leading-order Drell-Yan analyses by Conway (1989) that suggested \(q^\pi(x) \sim (1-x)\) and the QCD parton model prediction of \((1-x)^2\) from Ezawa (1974) and Farrar (1975). The framework enables systematic inclusion of sea-quark and gluon corrections via DGLAP evolution from a hadronic scale, providing a path to realistic comparison with experimental Drell-Yan data.
