# Symmetry energy effects on location of the inner edge of neutron star crusts

> The symmetry energy effects on the location of the inner edge of neutron star crusts are studied. Three phenomenological models are employed in order to check the accuracy of the well known parabolic approximation of the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter in the determination of the transition density $n_t$ and transition pressure $P_t$. The results corroborate the statement that the error due to the assumption that a priori the equation of state is parabolic may introduce a large error in the determination of related properties of a neutron star as the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia and the critical frequency of rotating neutron stars.

## Key claim
The widely used parabolic approximation (PA) for the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter can introduce large errors in the determination of the transition density \(n_t\) and transition pressure \(P_t\) at the inner edge of neutron star crusts, and consequently in derived properties such as the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia and the critical frequency of rotating neutron stars. The magnitude of the error is model-dependent and is especially severe for the transition pressure, which can increase by a factor of two or three when using the PA instead of the full equation of state (FEOS).

## Method
The author employed three phenomenological nuclear models, a momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) model with three parametrizations (MDI-1, MDI-2, MDI-3), a Skyrme model with three parametrizations (Sly4, SKI4, SKa), and a Thomas-Fermi (TF) model, to compute the core-crust transition density \(n_t\) and pressure \(P_t\) in neutron star matter. The thermodynamical method was used to find the density at which uniform \(\beta\)-stable matter becomes unstable, and results from the full equation of state (FEOS) were compared with those from the parabolic approximation, which assumes the energy per baryon can be written as \(E(n,I) \approx E(n,0) + E_{sym}(n) I^2\).

## Key numbers
- Transition densities \(n_t\) (FEOS) for the models: MDI-1 = 0.0771 fm\(^{-3}\), MDI-2 = 0.0706 fm\(^{-3}\), MDI-3 = 0.0508 fm\(^{-3}\), TF = 0.1368 fm\(^{-3}\), Sly4 = 0.0979 fm\(^{-3}\), SKI4 = 0.0806 fm\(^{-3}\), SKa = 0.0788 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Transition densities \(n_t\) (PA) for the models: MDI-1 = 0.0866 fm\(^{-3}\), MDI-2 = 0.0934 fm\(^{-3}\), MDI-3 = 0.1027 fm\(^{-3}\), TF = 0.1419 fm\(^{-3}\), Sly4 = 0.0942 fm\(^{-3}\), SKI4 = 0.0909 fm\(^{-3}\), SKa = 0.0933 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Transition pressures \(P_t\) (FEOS) in MeV: MDI-1 = 0.3456, MDI-2 = 0.2338, MDI-3 = 0.3133, TF = 2.9020, Sly4 = 0.5781, SKI4 = 0.3365, SKa = 0.5295.
- Transition pressures \(P_t\) (PA) in MeV: MDI-1 = 0.2975, MDI-2 = 0.7055, MDI-3 = 1.2482, TF = 3.7300, Sly4 = 0.5459, SKI4 = 0.4965, SKa = 0.8651.
- A linear correlation was found between the derivative of the symmetry energy at \(n = 3n_0/2\) and the transition density (FEOS): \(E'_{sym,2}(3n_0/2) = 400.18 - 3483.3 n_t\) (MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)).
- The contribution of the fourth-order symmetry energy term \(E_{sym,4}(u)\) relative to the second-order term \(E_{sym,2}(u)\) is less than 4% for most models at low densities (\(u \leq 2\)), but becomes comparable in the TF model even at low densities.

## Context
This work extends studies of the core-crust transition in neutron stars by Lattimer (2007), Kubis (2007), and Xu (2009), who showed the sensitivity of the transition density to the symmetry energy. It directly tests the accuracy of the parabolic approximation, which is commonly used in neutron star research when only the energy of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are known (e.g., from microscopic calculations or relativistic mean field theories). The findings enable more reliable predictions of the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia, relevant for interpreting pulsar glitches as in Link (1999), and of the r-mode instability critical frequency for rotating neutron stars, as studied by Lindblom (2000) and Andersson (1998).
