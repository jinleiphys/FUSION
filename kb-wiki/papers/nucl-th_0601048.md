# Gluonic Structures of Tetraquarks

> We formulate quantitative flux-tube overlap functions to account for the nonperturbative gluonic behaviors. In order to deduce systematic functional form, we introduce connection amplitude which can be defined between two boundary points occupied by quark or antiquark. With the deduced flux-tube overlap function, we can figure out long range gluonic structures of various quark combinations. In this paper, we report our calculated results for tetraquarks, $q^2 \bar{q}^2$, by considering two different configurations of boundary points for possible explanations of X(3872).

## Key claim

The paper formulates a systematic, quantitative flux-tube overlap function for tetraquarks by introducing a "connection amplitude" defined between boundary points occupied by quarks or antiquarks, but the actual gluonic structures depend on the unknown relative positions of the four quarks, so the authors consider two specific configurations (rectangular and tetrahedron) as illustrative cases rather than definitive predictions.

## Method

The authors classify flux-tubes by the number of quark and antiquark boundary points (e.g., \(F_{2,\bar{2}}\) for tetraquarks) and construct topological spaces from these sets. They define a connection amplitude \(A\) between two quarks, derive its measure as \(M(A) = -k \ln(A/A_0)\), and use this to build a flux-tube overlap function \(\gamma = A_i A_f\) (product of initial and final connection amplitudes). For tetraquarks, they fix the four quark positions in either a rectangular planar shape or a tetrahedron shape, set the parameter \(k=1\), and compute \(\gamma\) for three values of the parameter \(a\) (0.5, 1.0, and 2.0) to produce equi-\(\gamma\) curves and three-dimensional gluonic structures.

## Key numbers

- The flux-tube formalism is stated to be useful for ranges between 0.1 fm and 1 fm, with ordinary hadrons estimated to have extensions of 1 fm.
- The parameter \(\nu\) describing the distance function \(|\vec{x}-\vec{y}|^\nu\) is restricted to the range \(1 \leq \nu \leq 2\) for smooth flux-tube shapes.
- For the rectangular configuration, quark positions are set at (0,1,0), (0,-1,0), (2,-1,0), and (2,1,0) (units not specified but presumably in fm or arbitrary length units).
- For the tetrahedron configuration, quark positions are set at (0,1,0), (0,-1,0), (\(\sqrt{2}\),0,1), and (\(\sqrt{2}\),0,-1).
- The overlap function \(\gamma\) is calculated for parameter values \(a = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0\) with \(k=1\) fixed.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model previously applied to mesons and baryons (e.g., by Isgur and Paton for baryon spectra, and by Kokoski and Isgur for strong decays of mesons) to tetraquark systems, motivated by the discovery of X(3872). The authors classify flux-tubes into topological spaces following earlier work on topological structures (referenced as R5 and R6). The paper enables future quantitative comparisons of different tetraquark configurations (diquark-antidiquark vs. \(D^{*0}\bar{D}^0\) molecule) and immediate extension to pentaquarks and hexaquarks, though the authors note the critical unsolved problem of fixing the positions of boundary quarks.
