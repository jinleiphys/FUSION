# Isospin-projected nuclear level densities by the shell model Monte Carlo method

> We have developed an efficient isospin projection method in the shell model Monte Carlo approach for isospin-conserving Hamiltonians. For isoscalar observables this projection method has the advantage of being exact sample by sample. The isospin projection method allows us to take into account the proper isospin dependence of the nuclear interaction, thus avoiding a sign problem that such an interaction introduces in unprojected calculations. We apply our method in the calculation of the isospin dependence of level densities in the complete $pf+g_{9/2}$ shell. We find that isospin-dependent corrections to the total level density are particularly important for $N \sim Z$ nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors develop an exact isospin projection method for the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach that works sample by sample for isoscalar observables, enabling the inclusion of isospin-dependent interactions that would otherwise cause a sign problem in unprojected calculations; they find that isospin-dependent corrections to the total level density are particularly important for \(N \sim Z\) nuclei.

## Method

The method uses the Hubbard-Stratonovich representation of the imaginary-time propagator for an isospin-conserving Hamiltonian, then exploits the fact that for isoscalar observables the trace at fixed isospin \(T\) can be obtained from the difference of traces at fixed isospin component \(T_z\) (i.e., fixed neutron and proton numbers) sample by sample. The Monte Carlo sampling is performed for a reference nucleus \((N,Z)\), and thermal observables for a given isospin \(T\) are evaluated using particle-number reprojection onto the nucleus \((N+1, Z-1)\) and higher \(T_z\) values. The lowest energy for each isospin \(T\) is then shifted to match experimental excitation energies, correcting the isospin dependence of the effective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{58}\)Cu, the experimental excitation energy of the lowest \(T=1\) state is \(E_x(T=1)=0.203\) MeV.
* For \(^{58}\)Cu after projection and \(\delta E(T)\) correction, the BBF fit yields \(a=5.550\pm 0.021\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Delta=-0.658\pm 0.076\,\mathrm{MeV}\); for the unprojected density, \(a=5.827\pm 0.022\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Delta=1.069\pm 0.078\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(^{60}\)Zn after correction, the BBF fit gives \(a=5.947\pm 0.020\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Delta=2.102\pm 0.053\,\mathrm{MeV}\); for the unprojected density, \(a=6.185\pm 0.009\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Delta=1.679\pm 0.059\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(^{60}\)Zn, the energy shift for the \(T=1\) component is \(\delta E(T=1)\approx 1.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC level density method of Nakada and Alhassid (1997) and Alhassid, Liu, and Nakada (1999) by adding exact isospin projection, complementing earlier parity projection (Nakada and Alhassid, 1997; Alhassid et al., 2007) and spin projection (Alhassid, Liu, and Nakada, 2007) methods. It overcomes the sign problem that arises when including an isospin-dependent interaction term (e.g., a \(\alpha\mathbf{T}^2\) term as in the modified surface delta interaction of Brussaard and Glaudemans, 1977), which was previously treated only perturbatively by Ormand (1997) and Langanke (1998). The method enables microscopic calculation of isospin-dependent level densities for \(N\sim Z\) nuclei, which are important for astrophysical reaction rates in the \(rp\) process.
