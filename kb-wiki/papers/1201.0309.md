# X(3872) as a D-D* molecule bound by quark exchange forces

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-Matrix of D-D* scattering is solved with a meson-meson potential that results from 2nd order Born approximation of quark exchange processes. This potential turns out to be complex and energy dependent due to the pole contribution from the coupling to the intermediate J/psi-rho meson pair propagator. As a consequence, a bound state with a mass close to 3.872 GeV occurs in the J/psi-rho continuum. This result suggests that quark exchange forces may provide the solution to the puzzling question for the origin of the interaction which leads to a binding of D and D* mesons in the X(3872) state.

## Key claim
Quark exchange forces, extended to second-order Born approximation, can produce a complex, energy-dependent meson-meson potential that generates a bound state in the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ system with a mass near 3.872 GeV, suggesting a mechanism for the X(3872) resonance that is "rather independent of the detailed dynamics of the model."

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix of $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering in the ladder approximation, using a separable interaction kernel derived from second-order Born approximation of quark exchange processes. This kernel couples the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ channel to the $J/\psi-\rho$ channel via an intermediate propagator, producing a complex, energy-dependent potential whose imaginary part arises from the pole contribution of the $J/\psi-\rho$ two-particle propagator. The scattering phase shift is then computed to identify bound-state formation in the energy region between the $J/\psi+\rho$ and $D^0+\bar{D}^{*0}$ thresholds.

## Key numbers
- Bound state mass of 3.872 GeV, occurring at the $D^0+\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold (3.872 GeV).
- Coupling strength $\lambda = 20.3~\text{GeV}^{-2}$ and form factor width $\gamma = 0.8$ GeV used in the numerical example producing the bound state.
- Binding energy below 1 MeV, as cited from Brambilla (2010) for the X(3872) as a $D\bar{D}^{*}$ bound state.

## Context
This work extends the separable quark exchange interaction model of Martins (1994) to second-order Born approximation, following the approach of Pena (2011). It provides a specific binding mechanism for the X(3872) resonance, which was first observed by Belle (2003) and confirmed by BaBar (2008), and which has been previously studied in T-matrix approaches by Gamermann (2009) and others. The result enables future coupled-channel calculations including charged $D$ meson states and the $J/\psi-\omega$ channel, and may inform models of heavy-quarkonium modification in heavy-ion collisions, such as the generalized Matsui model applied to CERN-NA60 data.
