# On Mass and Matter

> The visible Universe is largely characterised by a single mass-scale; namely, the proton mass, $m_p$. Contemporary theory suggests that $m_p$ emerges as a consequence of gluon self-interactions, which are a defining characteristic of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the theory of strong interactions in the Standard Model. However, the proton is not elementary. Its mass appears as a corollary of other, more basic emergent phenomena latent in the QCD Lagrangian, e.g. generation of nuclear-size gluon and quark mass-scales, and a unique effective charge that may describe QCD interactions at all accessible momentum scales. These remarks are explained herein; and focusing on the distribution amplitudes and functions of $π$ and $K$ mesons, promising paths for their empirical verification are elucidated. Connected therewith, in anticipation that production of $J/ψ$-mesons using $π$ and $K$ beams can provide access to the gluon distributions in these pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone modes, predictions for all $π$ and $K$ distribution functions are provided at the scale $ζ=m_{J/ψ}$.

## Key claim

The proton mass (≈0.939 GeV) is not generated by the Higgs mechanism but emerges from gluon self-interactions in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), specifically through the dynamical generation of a gluon mass-scale and a process-independent running coupling; the paper argues that this emergent hadronic mass (EHM) can be tested empirically by measuring the distribution amplitudes and functions of π and K mesons, with predictions provided at the scale ζ = m_{J/ψ}.

## Method

The paper synthesizes continuum (Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations) and lattice-QCD results to compute the renormalization-group-invariant gluon mass function and the process-independent QCD running coupling. Using modern symmetry-preserving kernels for the quark gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equations, the authors compute the leading-twist distribution amplitudes (DAs) of π and K mesons, then derive all parton distribution functions (PDFs) for valence quarks, sea quarks, and glue in these mesons via a factorized light-front wave-function approach, evolving them from the hadronic scale to ζ = m_{J/ψ}.

## Key numbers

- Gluon mass scale (RGI): m₀ = 0.43(1) GeV, extracted from lattice-QCD configurations with three domain-wall fermions at physical pion mass.
- Process-independent running coupling at zero momentum: α̂(0)/π = 0.97(4).
- Proton mass: mₚ = 0.939 GeV; more than 98% of this mass is not from Higgs-generated current-quark masses.
- In the chiral limit, 94% of the proton mass is due to EHM (blue domain in mass budget).
- For the π meson, EHM+Higgs-boson interference is responsible for 95% of the physical π mass; for the K meson, the sum of valence-quark current masses amounts to 20% of its measured mass, with EHM+Higgs interference producing 80%.
- At ζ₂ = 2 GeV, valence quarks carry a fraction 0.47(3) of the π-meson’s light-front momentum and 0.49(3) in the kaon.
- Low-order Mellin moments at ζ₂: ⟨x⟩_π^u = 0.24(2), ⟨x⟩_K^u = 0.23(2), ⟨x⟩_K^{s̄} = 0.27(2); ⟨x²⟩_π^u = 0.094(13), ⟨x²⟩_K^u = 0.086(12), ⟨x²⟩_K^{s̄} = 0.11(2).
- At ζ₃ = m_{J/ψ} = 3.1 GeV: ⟨x⟩_π^u = 0.22(2), ⟨x⟩_K^u = 0.21(2), ⟨x⟩_K^{s̄} = 0.25(2); ⟨x²⟩_π^u = 0.085(11), ⟨x²⟩_K^u = 0.078(10), ⟨x²⟩_K^{s̄} = 0.097(12).

## Context

This work extends the unification of bottom-up (matter-sector) and top-down (gauge-sector) approaches to QCD achieved by Binosi et al. (2014) and the subsequent determination of the process-independent running coupling by Binosi et al. (2016) and Cui et al. (2019). It provides predictions for π and K distribution functions at ζ = m_{J/ψ} to enable empirical tests of EHM via J/ψ-meson production using π and K beams, a process expected to proceed through gluon-gluon fusion. The results enable future analyses of existing data from experiments such as those by McEwen et al. (1982) and Badier et al. (1983), and connect the emergent mass phenomenon in pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone modes to high-energy parton distribution measurements.
