# Weakly bound states with spin-isospin symmetry

> We discuss weakly bound states of a few-fermion system having spin-isospin symmetry. This corresponds to the nuclear physics case in which the singlet, $a_0$, and triplet, $a_1$, $n-p$ scattering lengths are large with respect to the range of the nuclear interaction. The ratio of the two is about $a_0/a_1\approx-4.31$. This value defines a plane in which $a_0$ and $a_1$ can be varied up to the unitary limit, $1/a_0=0$ and $1/a_1=0$, maintaining its ratio fixed. Using a spin dependant potential model we estimate the three-nucleon binding energy along that plane. This analysis can be considered an extension of the Efimov plot for three bosons to the case of three $1/2$-spin-isospin fermions.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Efimov plot for three identical bosons to the case of three 1/2-spin-isospin fermions (nucleons) by fixing the ratio of singlet to triplet scattering lengths at its experimental value of \(a_0/a_1 \approx -4.31\), and they find that the three-nucleon excited state disappears into the \(n-d\) continuum before reaching the physical nuclear point, unlike the bosonic case.

## Method

The authors use a spin-dependent Gaussian potential with equal range (1.65 fm) and different strengths in the singlet (\(V_0 = -37.90\) MeV) and triplet (\(V_1 = -60.575\) MeV) channels to reproduce the experimental \(n-p\) scattering lengths and deuteron binding energy. They vary the potential strengths to move the scattering lengths toward the unitary limit while keeping the ratio \(a_0/a_1\) fixed at \(-4.31\), and compute the three-nucleon binding energies for the ground and first excited states. The results are compared to the three-boson case using a modified Efimov radial law that includes finite-range corrections via a shift parameter \(\Gamma_n^3\).

## Key numbers

- Ratio of singlet to triplet scattering lengths: \(a_0/a_1 \approx -4.31\), fixed to the experimental value.
- Experimental scattering lengths: \(a_0 = -23.740 \pm 0.020\) fm and \(a_1 = 5.424 \pm 0.003\) fm.
- Gaussian potential range: \(r_0 = r_1 = 1.65\) fm; singlet strength \(V_0 = -37.90\) MeV, triplet strength \(V_1 = -60.575\) MeV.
- Ground state energy at the unitary limit (both scattering lengths infinite): \(E_3^0 \approx 3.6\) MeV.
- Ratio of ground to excited state momentum at the unitary limit: \(K_*/22.9\) (excited state value), compared to the zero-range prediction of \(K_*/22.7\).
- Ratio of the scattering lengths at which the three-boson ground and excited states disappear into the continuum: about 17.6, well below the zero-range prediction of 22.7.
- Shift parameter for the three-fermion ground state: \(\Gamma^0_3 \approx -0.2\), compared to \(\Gamma^0_3 \approx 0.8\) for three bosons.
- Excited state disappears into the \(n-d\) continuum at \(a^1_* \approx 20\) fm, before reaching the physical point.

## Context

This work extends the Efimov effect, originally formulated for three identical bosons by Efimov (1970), to the nuclear physics case of three 1/2-spin-isospin fermions with different singlet and triplet interactions. It builds on the authors' previous modifications to the Efimov radial law incorporating finite-range effects (Gattobigio and Kievsky, 2014; Kievsky and Gattobigio, 2015) and uses a Gaussian potential similar to the LM2M2 interaction of Aziz (1987) and the MT-III interaction of Tjon (1975). The finding that the three-nucleon excited state is unbound at the physical point, unlike the three-boson case, is consistent with prior studies of the \(n-d\) continuum by Fonseca (1989) and Tobias (1995), and the curvature of the \(n-d\) effective range function near threshold by Friar (1993) and Kievsky (1997). This analysis enables future work on the role of three-body forces and predictions for systems with \(A>3\).
