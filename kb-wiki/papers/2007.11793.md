# Temperature evolution of the nuclear shell structure and the dynamical nucleon effective mass

> We study the fermionic Matsubara Green functions in medium-mass nuclei at finite temperature. The single-fermion Dyson equation with the dynamical kernel of the particle-vibration-coupling (PVC) origin is formulated and solved in the basis of Dirac spinors, which minimize the grand canonical potential with the meson-nucleon covariant energy density functional. The PVC correlations beyond mean field are taken into account in the leading approximation for the energy-dependent self-energy, and the full solution of the finite-temperature Dyson equation is obtained for the fermionic propagators. Within this approach, we investigate the fragmentation of the single-particle states and its evolution with temperature for the nuclear systems $^{56,68}$Ni and $^{56}$Fe relevant for the core-collapse supernova. The energy-dependent, or dynamical, nucleon effective mass is extracted from the PVC self-energy at various temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fragmentation of single-particle states due to particle-vibration coupling (PVC) persists with temperature increase up to 4 MeV, but the specific evolution pattern depends strongly on the state's proximity to the Fermi energy and its spin-parity quantum numbers, with some states showing a counterintuitive weakening of fragmentation at moderate temperatures (1-2 MeV) before reinforcement at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors formulated and solved the finite-temperature Dyson equation for fermionic Matsubara Green functions in the basis of Dirac spinors from the relativistic mean field (RMF) with the NL3 parametrization. The dynamical self-energy was computed in the leading particle-vibration coupling approximation using phonon vertices and frequencies from the finite-temperature relativistic random phase approximation (FT-RRPA), with truncation of the phonon space to modes with J^pi = 2+, 3-, 4+, 5-, 6+ below 20 MeV and reduced transition probabilities at least 5% of the maximal value. The Dyson equation was solved in the diagonal approximation for the neutron and proton states in an approximately 20 MeV energy window around the Fermi energies of 56,68Ni and 56Fe.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps used: Delta_p^(n) = 1.6 MeV for neutrons in 68Ni; Delta_p^(n) = 1.8 MeV and Delta_p^(p) = 2.1 MeV for neutrons and protons in 56Fe, with critical temperatures in the 0 to 1.3 MeV interval
- The neutron 1f7/2 state in 68Ni at T=0 shows two major fragments approximately 6 MeV apart with spectroscopic factors of 0.28 and 0.17
- The neutron 3d3/2 state in 68Ni at T=1 MeV shows a competing two-peak structure with spectroscopic factors of 0.21 and 0.39
- The proton 1d3/2 state in 68Ni at T=0 shows a dominant fragment with a second fragment at 0.23/0.45 share of spectroscopic factors
- The phonon space cutoff was set at 20 MeV for the considered nuclei
- The particle-hole basis for FT-RRPA was limited to configurations with energies epsilon_ph <= 100 MeV and antiparticle-hole configurations with epsilon_alpha h >= -1800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature relativistic PVC framework of Litvinova and Ring (2006) and Litvinova (2012) to finite temperature, building on earlier finite-temperature studies of nuclear structure by Donati (1994) and Fantina (2011). The approach enables detailed investigation of single-particle state evolution in nuclei relevant for core-collapse supernova modeling, and complements previous finite-temperature studies of the spin-isospin response by Litvinova, Robin, and Wibowo (2020). The results show that the common assumption of a temperature-independent mean field in the 0-3 MeV range is not fully justified, as mean-field states can shift by 1-2 MeV.
