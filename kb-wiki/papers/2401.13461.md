# Neutron star matter as a dilute solution of protons in neutrons

> Neutron stars contain neutron-rich matter with around 5% protons at nuclear saturation density. In this Letter, we consider equilibrium between bulk phases of matter based on asymmetric nuclear matter calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions rather than, as has been done in the past, by interpolation between the properties of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. Neutron drip (coexistence of nuclear matter with pure neutrons) is well established, but from earlier work it is unclear whether proton drip (equilibrium between two phases, both of which contain protons and neutrons) is possible. We find that proton drip is a robust prediction of any physically reasonable equation of state, but that it occurs over a limited region of densities and proton fractions. An analytical model based on expanding the energy in powers of the proton density, rather than the neutron excess, is able to account for these features of the phase diagram.

## Key claim
Proton drip (coexistence of two bulk nuclear phases both containing protons and neutrons) is a robust prediction of any physically reasonable equation of state, but it occurs only over a limited region of densities and proton fractions, contrary to earlier work that found no evidence for it.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter using chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) with a cutoff of 450 MeV, including two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. They used many-body perturbation theory up to third order with a Gaussian process emulator to evaluate the equation of state and its derivatives, then solved the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium between bulk phases at zero temperature.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Neutron drip region extends up to proton fraction x ≈ 0.37 at N3LO
- Proton drip occurs for neutron chemical potentials μ_n ≳ 14.47 MeV at N3LO
- In the analytical model, proton drip exists for the dimensionless parameter ν in the range 5^(3/4)/3^(5/4) ≈ 0.8469 < ν < 1
- The lowest ν for which pure neutron drip has a solution is ν = 2/3^(3/4) ≈ 0.8774
- In the proton drip phase, the filling factor reaches maximum values around u ≈ 0.4

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of neutron star crust phase diagrams, particularly addressing the disagreement between Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (1971), who found no evidence for proton drip, and Pethick, Ravenhall, and Lorenz (1995) and Douchin and Haensel (2000), who did find it. The authors attribute the earlier negative result to the proton drip region occurring between grid points. The new chiral EFT calculations avoid the simple interpolation between symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter used in prior work. The results enable renewed investigation of whether pasta phases (string-like and plate-like nuclei) are stable in neutron stars, as the larger filling factors in the proton drip phase (up to u ≈ 0.4) make pasta phases more likely.
