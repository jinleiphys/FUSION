# Some examples of uses of Dirac equation and its generalizations in particle physics

> Applications of the Dirac equation with an anomalous magnetic moment are considered for description of characteristics of electrons, muons and quarks. The Dirac equation with four-dimensional scalar and vector potentials is reduced to a form suitable for a numerical integration. When a certain type of the potential is chosen, solutions can approximate quark states inside hadrons. In view of complicated behaviour of quarks in a confinement domain some generalizations are considered such as the Dirac-Gursey-Lee equation, the Dirac equation in a five-dimensional Minkowski space, the Dirac equation in a quantum phase space. Extended symmetries for the Dirac equation and its generalizations are considered, which can be used for investigation of properties of solutions of these equations and subsequent applications in particle physics.

# Some examples of uses of Dirac equation and its generalizations in particle physics

## Key claim
The Dirac equation with an anomalous magnetic moment successfully describes electrons, muons, and quarks within the Standard Model, but in the confinement domain of quantum chromodynamics the standard spacetime symmetry may break down, requiring generalized Dirac-type equations such as the Dirac-Gursey-Lee equation, the Dirac equation in a five-dimensional Minkowski space, and the Dirac equation in a quantum phase space to model quark behavior.

## Method
The author reviews the Dirac equation with a Pauli term for anomalous magnetic moments of leptons and quarks, then reduces the Dirac equation with four-dimensional scalar and vector potentials to a form suitable for numerical integration within the relativistic model for quasi-independent quarks (RMQIQ). Several generalizations are examined: the Dirac-Gursey-Lee equation with a de Sitter space parameter, the Dirac equation in a five-dimensional Minkowski space with an extra momentum dimension, and the Dirac equation in a quantum phase space based on the generalized Snyder-Yang algebra with O(2,6) symmetry, which yields an oscillator confinement potential for color particles.

- Experimental electron g-factor: g/2(exp) = 1.00115965218073 (28), yielding the fine structure constant alpha^{-1} = 137.035 999 084 (51) at 0.37 ppb precision
- Experimental muon anomalous magnetic moment: a_mu(exp) = 11659208.9 (6.3) x 10^{-10} from the E821 experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory
- Standard Model prediction for muon AMM: a_mu(SM) = 11659177.3 (4.8) x 10^{-10}, giving a discrepancy of 31.6 (7.9) x 10^{-10} corresponding to a 4 sigma difference
- String tension parameter from meson spectra: sigma = (0.20 +/- 0.01) GeV^2, with alpha_s running from 0.7 (light mesons) to 0.25 (heavy mesons)
- Constituent quark energies in MeV: E_U = 335 +/- 2, E_D = 339 +/- 2, E_S = 485 +/- 8, E_C = 1610 +/- 15, E_B = 4952 +/- 20
- Estimated imaginary constant from Dirac-Gursey-Lee equation: |mu_s| ~ 0.16 GeV
- Estimated confinement oscillator parameter: sqrt(sigma) ~ 0.3 GeV, corresponding to confinement length lambda_conf ~ 0.6 fm

## Context
This work extends the relativistic model for quasi-independent quarks (RMQIQ) developed by Khruschov, Savrin, and Semenov (2002) and the constituent quark model using the Zeldovich-Sakharov formula (1966, 1980). It addresses the open problem of quark confinement by proposing generalized Dirac-type equations with extended symmetries beyond the Poincare group, specifically the Dirac-Gursey-Lee equation following Dirac (1935) and Gursey and Lee (1963), the five-dimensional Dirac equation of Fushchich and Krivsky (1969), and the quantum phase space approach based on the Snyder-Yang algebra (1947). The work enables future investigation of quark and lepton mass generation mechanisms, particularly in light of the then-undiscovered Higgs boson and the known small neutrino masses, and suggests that the Dirac equation in a quantum phase space with an oscillator potential can serve as a model for color particle confinement.
