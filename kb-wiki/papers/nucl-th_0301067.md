# Exploring isospin, strangeness charm distillation in heavy ion collisions

> The isospin and strangeness dimensions of the Equation of State are explored. RIA and the SIS200 accelerator at GSI will allow to explore these regions in compressed baryonic matter. $^{132}$Sn+$^{132}$Sn and $^{100}$Sn+$^{100}$Sn collisions as well as the excitation functions of $K/π$, $Λ/π$ and the centrality dependence of charmonium suppression from the UrQMD and HSD transport models are presented and compared to data. Unambiguous proof for the creation of a 'novel phase of matter' from strangeness and charm yields is not in sight.

## Key claim

The paper argues that unambiguous proof for the creation of a 'novel phase of matter' (such as a Quark-Gluon Plasma) from strangeness and charm yields is not yet in sight, because conventional hadron transport models (UrQMD and HSD) can reproduce many of the observed excitation functions and charmonium suppression patterns without invoking a phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v1.2/v1.3 and HSD hadron transport models to simulate central collisions of various nuclear systems: $^{132}$Sn+$^{132}$Sn and $^{100}$Sn+$^{100}$Sn at 2 and 11 AGeV for isospin studies, and Au+Au (AGS) or Pb+Pb (SPS) collisions from 1 to 160 AGeV for strangeness and charm studies. They calculated time evolutions of neutron-to-proton and pion ratios, excitation functions of pion, kaon, and hyperon yields, and the centrality dependence of $J/\Psi$ suppression, comparing all results to available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{132}$Sn collisions at 2 AGeV, the (n/p) ratio drops from an initial value of 1.64 to a saturation value of approximately 1.4 after 15 fm/c; at 11 AGeV it saturates earlier (10 fm/c) to a lower final value of approximately 1.2.
- The $(\pi^-/\pi^+)$ ratio for $^{132}$Sn+$^{132}$Sn saturates at approximately 1.7 at 2 AGeV and approximately 1.2 at 11 AGeV, far below the first-chance Delta resonance model prediction of $(N/Z)^2 \approx 2.7$.
- The $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio from UrQMD shows a maximum around 10 AGeV and then saturates to a slightly lower value towards RHIC energies, qualitatively reproducing the behavior of the data; HSD gives a monotonous increase with bombarding energy.
- The new NA50 data on $J/\Psi$ to Drell-Yan production in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV fall very close to the published UrQMD calculations by Spieles et al. (1999).

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of isospin asymmetric matter (Li, Wiringa (1988), Margueron (2001)) and strangeness distillation (Greiner, DiToro (2003)) to a systematic comparison of two major transport codes (UrQMD and HSD) with data from the NA49 and NA50 collaborations. The paper enables future experimental programs at the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) and SIS200 at GSI, and calls for improved treatment of multi-particle interactions and in-medium hadron properties in transport codes to resolve remaining discrepancies with data.
