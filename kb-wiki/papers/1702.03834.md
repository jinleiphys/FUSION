# Large single transverse spin asymmetry for forward neutrons in ultraperipheral polarized proton-nucleus collisions in explanation of measurements at RHIC-PHENIX

> The PHENIX experiment at BNL-RHIC recently reported the single transverse spin asymmetry $A_N$ for forward neutrons measured in polarized proton-nucleus collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\,$GeV; $A_N$ in proton-aluminum and proton-gold collisions are -0.015 and 0.18, respectively, which are clearly different from $A_N=-0.08$ in proton-proton collisions. In this paper, we propose large $A_N$ for forward neutrons in ultraperipheral polarized proton-nucleus collisions as an explanation of the PHENIX measurements. The proposed model is demonstrated using two Monte Carlo simulations organized as follows. In the ultraperipheral collision simulation, we use the STARlight event generator for the simulation of the virtual photon flux and then use the MAID2007 unitary isobar model for the simulation of the neutron production in the interactions of a virtual photon with a polarized proton. In the proton-nucleus hadronic interaction simulation, the differential cross sections of forward neutron production are predicted by a simple one-pion exchange model and the Glauber model. The simulated $A_N$ values accumulating the both contribution of ultraperipheral collisions and hadronic interactions are in good agreement with the PHENIX results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the large positive single transverse spin asymmetry ($A_N$) for forward neutrons in polarized proton-gold collisions measured by PHENIX can be explained by the contribution of ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs), where virtual photons from the nucleus interact with the polarized proton, rather than by hadronic interaction mechanisms alone. The model is demonstrated using Monte Carlo simulations and the agreement with PHENIX data depends on the relative cross sections of UPCs and hadronic interactions in each nuclear target.

## Method

The author developed two separate Monte Carlo simulation frameworks: one for ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) and one for hadronic interactions. For UPCs, the virtual photon flux from the relativistic nucleus was simulated using the STARlight event generator, and the subsequent $\gamma^* p \to \pi^+ n$ interaction was simulated using the MAID2007 unitary isobar model, which includes baryon resonances up to 2 GeV and accounts for target asymmetry $T(\theta_\pi)$. For hadronic interactions, forward neutron production was modeled using a one-pion exchange model for proton-proton collisions, scaled to proton-nucleus collisions using the Gribov-Glauber model with an $A^{0.42}$ factor, and the single spin asymmetry was incorporated by multiplying the unpolarized cross section by $1 + \cos\Phi \, A_N^{\text{HAD}}$, taking $A_N^{\text{HAD}} = -0.05$ from prior work by Kopeliovich et al.

## Key numbers

- $A_N$ for forward neutrons in $p^\uparrow$Al collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV measured by PHENIX: $-0.015 \pm 0.005$
- $A_N$ for forward neutrons in $p^\uparrow$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV measured by PHENIX: $0.18 \pm 0.03$
- $A_N$ for forward neutrons in $p^\uparrow p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV: $-0.08 \pm 0.02$
- Simulated $A_N$ from UPCs alone ($A_N^{\text{UPC}}$) for both $p$Al and $p$Au: $0.36$
- Simulated $A_N$ from hadronic interactions alone ($A_N^{\text{HAD}}$) for both $p$Al and $p$Au: $-0.05$
- Combined simulated $A_N$ ($A_N^{\text{UPC+HAD}}$) for $p$Al: $-0.02$; for $p$Au: $0.16$
- Total UPC cross section for $p$Au $\to nX$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with $y > 6.9$ and $x_F > 0.4$: $19.6$ mb
- Total hadronic cross section for $p$Au $\to nX$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with $y > 6.9$ and $x_F > 0.4$: $19.2$ mb
- Total UPC cross section for $p$Al $\to nX$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with $y > 6.9$ and $x_F > 0.4$: $0.7$ mb
- Total hadronic cross section for $p$Al $\to nX$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with $y > 6.9$ and $x_F > 0.4$: $8.3$ mb
- UPC cross section for $p$Au at $W < 1.1$ GeV is $2.1\%$ of the cross section at $1.1 < W < 2.0$ GeV; at $W > 2.0$ GeV it is $6.6\%$
- Two-pion production cross section in UPCs is $14\%$ of the single-pion production cross section
- Nonzero $Q^2$ effects change the UPC cross section by less than $2\%$ and reduce $A_N^{\text{UPC}}$ by about $10\%$

## Context

This work extends the study of single transverse spin asymmetry for forward neutrons from polarized proton-proton collisions, where the mechanism of pion and $a_1$-Reggeon interference was established by Kopeliovich et al., to proton-nucleus collisions. The author notes that the same interference model with nuclear corrections (Kopeliovich et al.) predicts negative and small $A_N$ for $p$A collisions, which cannot explain the positive $A_N = 0.18$ observed by PHENIX in $p$Au collisions. The paper introduces ultraperipheral collisions as a new mechanism, building on the author's prior work (Mitsuka) showing that UPCs have comparable cross sections to hadronic interactions in the very forward region. The results enable future experimental studies using beam-beam counter vetoes to isolate UPC-enriched event samples and suggest extensions to include two-pion production and Coulomb-nuclear interference.
