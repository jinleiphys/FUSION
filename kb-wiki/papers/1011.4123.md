# How Perfect a Gluon Plasma Can Be in Perturbative QCD?

> The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, η/s, characterizes how perfect a fluid is. We calculate the leading order η/s of a gluon plasma in perturbation using the kinetic theory. The leading order contribution only involves the elastic gg -> gg (22) process and the inelastic gg<->ggg (23) process. The Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) treatment is used for the 22 matrix element, while the exact matrix element in vacuum is supplemented by the gluon Debye mass insertion for the 23 process. Also, the asymptotic mass is used for the external gluons in the kinetic theory. The errors from not implementing HTL and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect in the 23 process, and from the uncalculated higher order corrections, are estimated. Our result for η/s lies between that of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (AMY) and Xu and Greiner (XG). Our result shows that although the finite angle contributions are important at intermediate α_s (α_s \sim 0.01-0.1), the 22 process is still more important than 23 when α_s < 0.1. This is in qualitative agreement with AMY's result. We find no indication that the proposed perfect fluid limit η/s \simeq 1/(4π) can be achieved by perturbative QCD alone.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the leading-order shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of a gluon plasma using perturbative QCD without the soft-gluon-bremsstrahlung or collinear approximations for the inelastic 23 process, finding that the 22 (elastic) process dominates over the 23 (inelastic) process when α_s < 0.1, and that the perfect fluid limit η/s ≈ 1/(4π) cannot be achieved by perturbative QCD alone.

## Method

The shear viscosity is computed via the linearized Boltzmann equation for a gluon plasma, using the Kubo formula and a systematic polynomial expansion method to solve for the deviation function B(p). The elastic gg→gg (22) process uses Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) regulated matrix elements, while the inelastic gg↔ggg (23) process uses the exact vacuum matrix element supplemented by the Debye mass as an infrared regulator for internal gluon propagators, with asymptotic mass m_∞ used for external gluons.

## Key numbers

- η_LL/s ≈ 3.9/(g^4 ln(1/g)) for the leading-log approximation
- At α_s = 0.1, η_{22+23}/s = 2.30 (central value), with lower bound η_-/s = 2.08 and upper bound η_+/s = 2.58
- At α_s = 0.01, η_{22+23}/s = 103 (central value), with lower bound η_-/s = 99.9 and upper bound η_+/s = 106
- At α_s = 0.001, η_{22+23}/s = 7.02×10^3 (central value), with lower bound η_-/s = 6.94×10^3 and upper bound η_+/s = 7.11×10^3
- The ratio η_{22+23}/η_{22} reaches a minimum of approximately 0.6 at α_s ≈ 0.1
- At α_s = 0.1, the HTL correction to the 22 process changes η by about 30% compared to using the Debye mass regulator
- The 23 process contributes about 60% of the 22 collision rate at α_s ≈ 0.1 (from η_{22+23}/η_{22} ≈ 0.6)

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between the perturbative QCD calculations of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (AMY) (2003) and Xu and Greiner (XG) (2007) regarding the relative importance of the inelastic 23 process. AMY found the 23 process gives only about a 10% correction to the 22 process using collinear approximations, while XG found the 23 process is about 7 times more important than 22 using soft-gluon-bremsstrahlung approximations. The authors' result lies between these two calculations, agreeing qualitatively with AMY but not with XG, and shows that perturbative QCD alone cannot reach the conjectured perfect fluid bound η/s = 1/(4π) from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). This enables more reliable comparisons between perturbative QCD predictions and experimental measurements of η/s in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.
