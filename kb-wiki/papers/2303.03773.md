# Search for jet quenching effects on the plain jet mass in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC with a multiphase transport model

> The plain jet mass distributions of reconstructed jets are investigated in p+p and 0-10$\%$ most central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76~{\rm TeV}$ using a dynamical multiphase transport model with a string melting mechanism. It is observed that the mean charged jet mass increases with increasing jet transverse momentum and jet radius in central Pb+Pb collisions. It is demonstrated that the plain jet mass of reconstructed partonic jet is shifted to a higher value after the evolution of partonic stage due to jet quenching in central Pb+Pb collisions. However, the jet mass shift effect is strongly weakened by non-perturbative effects from hadronization and hadron rescatterings. This makes it difficult to observe significant hot medium modification effects on the plain jet mass distribution in the final state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The plain jet mass of reconstructed partonic jets is shifted to a higher value after the partonic evolution stage due to jet quenching in central Pb+Pb collisions, but this shift is strongly weakened by non-perturbative effects from hadronization and hadron rescatterings, making it difficult to observe significant hot medium modification effects on the plain jet mass distribution in the final state.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model with a string melting mechanism to simulate p+p and 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. A triggered high-pT dijet was embedded into the initial condition using the HIJING model, and jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm using the FastJet package with jet radii R = 0.2, 0.3, and 0.4. The evolution stages were separated into initial state, after parton cascade (ZPC model with parton cross sections of 0 mb or 3 mb), after hadronization (quark coalescence), and after hadronic rescatterings (ART model), allowing comparison of jet mass distributions at each stage.

## Key numbers

- Charged jet mass distributions were calculated for three pT intervals: 60-80, 80-100, and 100-120 GeV/c, with jet radius R = 0.4 in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The mean charged jet mass increases with increasing jet transverse momentum and jet radius; the difference between 3 mb and 0 mb is most obvious for the largest jet radius R = 0.4 with the hardest pT interval (100-120 GeV/c).
- For full jets with pT,jet > 100 GeV/c, the ratio of jet mass distributions between 3 mb and 0 mb in central Pb+Pb collisions is consistent with unity after hadronization and hadronic rescatterings, indicating no significant jet quenching effect in the final state.
- The jet mass distribution after parton cascade in central Pb+Pb collisions with 3 mb shows a right shift compared to the initial state, while no such shift is seen with 0 mb.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet quenching effects on jet mass, following the ALICE measurement (ALICE, 2018) which found that plain charged jet mass distributions in central Pb+Pb collisions agreed with p+Pb data within uncertainties, and that quenching models with medium response overestimated the mean jet mass. The authors use the AMPT model to isolate the effects of parton cascade, hadronization, and hadronic rescatterings, showing that non-perturbative effects from hadronization and hadronic rescatterings mask the jet quenching signal. This motivates future studies using groomed jet mass, which has less contamination from non-perturbative effects, as suggested by CMS (2018) results on groomed jet mass.
