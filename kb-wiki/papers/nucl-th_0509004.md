# Use of V_{low-k} in a chiral-perturbation-theory description of the $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction

> Previously, the near-threhold $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction was studied with the use of transition operators derived from chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) and the nuclear wave functions generated by high-precision phenomenological potentials. A conceptual problem in that approach was that the transition amplitude receives contributions from very high momentum components (above the cutoff scale of $χ$PT) in the nuclear wave functions. In the present work, we avoid this problem by replacing the "bare" phenomenological potentials with V_{low-k}, which is an effective potential derived from a bare potential by integrating out momentum components higher than a specified cutoff scale. The use of V_{low-k} is found to give an enhancement of the $pp\to ppπ^0$ cross sections over the values obtained with bare potentials. Although this enhancement brings the calculated cross sections closer to the experimental values, the incident-energy dependence of the cross section is not well reproduced, a problem that seems to indicate the necessity of including higher chiral order terms than considered in the present work.

## Key claim

Using the low-momentum effective potential V_{low-k} (which removes momentum components above a cutoff scale) in a hybrid chiral perturbation theory calculation of the near-threshold pp→ppπ⁰ reaction enhances the cross section compared to calculations using bare phenomenological potentials, bringing it closer to experimental values; however, the incident-energy dependence of the cross section is not well reproduced, indicating the need to include higher chiral order terms beyond those considered in this work.

## Method

The authors performed a hybrid chiral perturbation theory (χPT) calculation of the near-threshold pp→ppπ⁰ reaction, using transition operators derived from χPT at tree level (chiral orders ν=-1 and ν=+1, corresponding to impulse approximation and rescattering diagrams) while generating the initial and final two-nucleon wave functions from the V_{low-k} effective potential (cutoff Λ=2 fm⁻¹) derived from the CD-Bonn potential. The transition amplitude was evaluated in partial-wave decomposition, restricting to the dominant channels (initial ³P₀ state, final ¹S₀ state, s-wave pion), and the total cross section was computed as a function of incident proton energy. For comparison, the authors also performed calculations using the bare Argonne V18, Bonn-B, and CD-Bonn potentials, and examined two prescriptions for the exchanged-pion four-momentum (fixed kinematics approximation and a more general energy-conserving choice).

## Key numbers

- The impulse-approximation cross section σ^Imp calculated with V_{low-k} is significantly smaller (by a factor of 3 or more) than those obtained with bare potentials (Argonne V18, Bonn-B, CD-Bonn), which vary by up to 40% among themselves.
- The rescattering-term cross section σ^Resc shows variations of about 30% among the three bare potentials, while the V_{low-k} result lies within this range.
- The low-energy constants used are: c₁ = -0.87 ± 0.11 GeV⁻¹, c₂ = 3.34 ± 0.27 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -5.25 ± 0.22 GeV⁻¹.
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_π = 93 MeV and the axial coupling as g_A = 1.25.
- The V_{low-k} cutoff scale is Λ = 2 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid χPT calculations of pp→ppπ⁰ by Park et al. (1996) and Sato et al. (1997) that used bare phenomenological potentials (Argonne V18 and Reid soft-core), which suffered from the conceptual problem that the transition amplitude received contributions from momentum components above the chiral cutoff scale (~1 GeV). The use of V_{low-k}, developed by the Stony Brook group (2001, 2003), removes these high-momentum components and provides a more consistent framework. The results enable future work to examine whether including higher chiral order terms or using alternative counting schemes (such as that of Cohen et al., 1996) can improve the energy dependence of the cross section, and highlight the need for formal consistency between the regularization scheme used for the transition operators (dimensional regularization) and that used for the nuclear potential (momentum cutoff).
