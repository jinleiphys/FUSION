# Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model for deformed nuclei

> The Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model for axially deformed nuclei (RHFBz) is introduced. The model is based on an effective Lagrangian with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings in the particle-hole channel, and the pairing part of the Gogny force is used in the pairing channel. The RHFBz quasiparticle equations are solved by expansion in the basis of a deformed harmonic oscillator. Illustrative RHFBz calculations are performed for Carbon, Neon and Magnesium isotopes. The effect of the explicitly including the pion field is investigated for binding energies, deformation parameters, and charge radii.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model for axially deformed nuclei (RHFBz), extending the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework from spherical to deformed systems; the authors note that the explicit inclusion of the pion field has an impact on the nuclei's shape, but the results are presented as illustrative calculations for Carbon, Neon, and Magnesium isotopes rather than as a comprehensive validation.

## Method

The model is based on an effective Lagrangian with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings in the particle-hole channel, and the central part of the Gogny force (D1S parameter set) is used in the pairing channel. The RHFBz quasiparticle equations are solved by expanding the nucleon spinors and meson fields in the basis of a deformed harmonic oscillator with axial symmetry. Calculations are performed both without the pion field (PKO2 parametrization) and with the pion field included (PKO3 parameter set) to investigate its effect on binding energies, deformation parameters, and charge radii.

## Key numbers

- The effective Lagrangian is characterized by 8 free parameters: m_sigma, g_sigma(rho_sat), b_sigma, d_sigma, g_omega(rho_sat), b_omega, a_rho, and a_pi, adjusted to experimental masses of 12 spherical nuclei (O-16, Ca-40, Ca-48, Ni-56, Ni-68, Zr-90, Sn-116, Sn-132, Pb-182, Pb-194, Pb-208, Pb-214) and nuclear matter properties (saturation point, incompressibility modulus K_infty, and symmetry energy at saturation J).
- The pairing interaction uses the Gogny force D1S set with parameters mu_i, W_i, B_i, H_i, and M_i for i=1,2.
- Typical calculations with 6 fermionic oscillator shells (arrays of size 119^4) and 20 bosonic shells converge in 8 to 30 hours on a 2x4 core Intel Xeon L5420 at 2.50 GHz processor.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) framework, previously limited to spherical nuclei (Long et al., 2006; Long et al., 2010; Liang et al., 2008; Liang et al., 2010), to axially deformed systems, building on the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach (Vretenar et al., 2005) and the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model. The explicit treatment of Fock terms, including the pion field and tensor rho-nucleon coupling, enables the study of shell evolution in deformed nuclei and provides a fully self-consistent framework for future extensions to symmetry restoration and collective excitations.
