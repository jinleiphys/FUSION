# Vertex renormalization in weak decays of Cooper pairs and cooling compact stars

> At temperatures below the critical temperature of superfluid phase transition baryonic matter emits neutrinos by breaking and recombination of Cooper pairs formed in the condensate. The strong interactions in the nuclear medium modify the weak interaction vertices and the associated neutrino loss rates. We study these modifications non-perturbatively by summing infinite series of particle-hole loops in the S-wave superfluid neutron matter. The pairing and particle-hole interactions in neutron matter are described in the framework of the BCS and Fermi-liquid theories derived from microscopic interactions. Consistent with the f-sum rule, the leading order contribution to the polarization tensor arises at O(q^2) in the small momentum transfer, q, expansion. The associated neutrino emission rate is parametrically suppressed compared to its one-loop counterpart by a factor of the order of 5\times 10^{-3}, the parameter being the baryon recoil in units of temperature.

## Key claim

The neutrino emission rate from Cooper pair breaking and recombination in S-wave superfluid neutron matter is parametrically suppressed compared to its one-loop counterpart by a factor of order \(5\times 10^{-3}\), with the suppression arising from the leading-order contribution to the polarization tensor at \(O(q^2)\) in the small momentum transfer expansion, consistent with the f-sum rule. This result is at variance with earlier work by LP (2005), which predicted a suppression by many orders of magnitude, because the present study derives vertices at finite temperature consistent with the polarization tensor, preserving unitarity of the S-matrix.

## Method

The authors computed the neutrino emissivity from Cooper pair breaking and recombination in S-wave superfluid neutron matter by summing infinite series of particle-hole loops (RPA resummation) to renormalize the weak interaction vertices non-perturbatively. The pairing interaction was described within BCS theory using the Gogny DS1 force, while the particle-hole interactions were described by Landau Fermi-liquid parameters derived from the CD Bonn potential, truncated at leading order in Legendre expansion. The full polarization tensor was constructed from the renormalized vertices and expanded in small momentum transfer \(q\), with the leading \(O(q^2)\) contribution evaluated analytically.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino emissivity including vertex corrections is suppressed relative to the one-loop result by a factor of approximately \(4\times 10^{-3}\) (parametrically \(T/m^*\)).
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) for S-wave pairing ranges from 0.38 MeV (at \(p_F = 1.6\) fm\(^{-1}\)) to 1.80 MeV (at \(p_F = 1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- The zero-temperature pairing gap \(\Delta(p_F)\) ranges from 0.57 MeV (at \(p_F = 1.6\) fm\(^{-1}\)) to 3.15 MeV (at \(p_F = 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- The process operates in the temperature domain \(T^* \le T \le T_c\), where \(T^* \sim 0.2 T_c\), corresponding to the neutrino cooling era spanning \(10^2 \le t \le 10^5\) years in neutron star cooling.
- The sound velocity in the superfluid at zero temperature and \(v^V = 0\) reduces to \(c^2 = v_F^2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculations of neutrino emission from Cooper pair breaking and recombination originally performed by Flowers, Ruderman, and Sutherland (1978) and Voskresensky and Senatorov (1987) by including non-perturbative vertex renormalization via RPA summation. It contradicts the earlier RPA treatment by LP (2005), which found a much stronger suppression, because the present study uses finite-temperature vertices consistent with the polarization tensor rather than zero-temperature vertices. The results enable more accurate cooling simulations of neutron stars in the temperature range \(10^8\) to \(10^9\) K, where the pair-breaking process is a potentially viable cooling mechanism, and highlight the sensitivity of cooling rates to the poorly known magnitude of pairing gaps.
