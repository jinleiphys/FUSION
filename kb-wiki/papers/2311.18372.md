# General features and contact modeling of $N$-body isolated resonances near threshold

> $N$-body non efimovian bound or quasi-bound states for particles with short range interactions are considered in arbitrary dimensions. The different resonance regimes near the threshold are depicted by using a generalization of the effective range approximation. This two-parameter description can be used in various contexts from ultra-cold to hadronic physics. The universal character of these states makes it possible a formulation in terms of a contact model. The singularity at the contact imposes the introduction of a modified scalar product to solve the normalization catastrophe and to restore the self-adjoint character of the model. An equivalence with the standard scalar product used for realistic finite range models is derived.

## Key claim

The paper argues that isolated $N$-body resonances near threshold for particles with short-range interactions can be universally described by a two-parameter contact model (generalized scattering length and range parameter) for all real positive values of the index $s$, not only for $s<1$ as previously believed, provided a modified scalar product is introduced to resolve the normalization catastrophe when $s\ge 1$.

## Method

The author generalizes the effective range approximation to the hyperradial Schrödinger equation with an inverse-square potential tail, deriving the low-energy expansion of the scattering phase shift in terms of a generalized scattering length $\xi_s$ and a range parameter $\alpha_s$. A contact model is constructed by imposing a log-derivative boundary condition at a separation radius $R_{\rm sep}$, and a modified scalar product is introduced using the wronskian of two reference states to restore self-adjointness and normalize states that are not square-integrable under the standard inner product when $s\ge 1$.

## Key numbers

- The resonance condition is $\upsilon_0 = s$, where $\upsilon_0$ is the zero-energy log-derivative at $R_{\rm sep}$ and $s$ is the hyperradial index; the detuning is $\delta = s - \upsilon_0$.
- At resonance ($|\xi_s| = \infty$), the range parameter is $\alpha_s^{\rm res} = \frac{4^{s-1} R_{\rm sep}^{2-2s} \Gamma(s)^2}{\pi (s-1)} \left(1 + 2(s-1) \upsilon'_0\right)$ for $s>1$.
- The generalized scattering length near resonance is $\xi_s = -\frac{\pi R_{\rm sep}^{2s}}{s 4^s \Gamma(s)^2} \left(\frac{2s-\delta}{\delta}\right)$.
- The probability to find particles in the small hyperradius region at threshold is $\lim_{E\to 0} \mathcal P_{<R_{\rm sep}}(E) = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{2(s-1)\upsilon'_0}}$ for $s>1$, and $0$ otherwise.
- The width-radius inequality for $s>1$ is $\alpha_s^{\rm res} R_{\rm sep}^{2(s-1)} > \frac{\Gamma(s)^2}{4^{(1-s)} (s-1) \pi}$.

## Context

This work extends the contact model approach of Pricoupenko (2023) for the unitary limit to general short-range interactions in arbitrary dimensions, and generalizes earlier single-parameter models by Werner and Castin (2006, 2008) and Nishida and Son (2008) that were limited to $s<1$. The formalism enables universal predictions for $N$-body resonances in ultracold atoms, nuclear halo states (e.g., two-neutron halos studied by Naidon and collaborators, 2023), and hadronic systems, and provides a rigorous foundation for modeling states where the hyperradial potential is repulsive in the separability region.
