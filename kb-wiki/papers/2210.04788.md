# Dilepton Decay of Low-mass $ρ$ Mesons

> The HADES collaboration has extracted dilepton mass spectra for $π^-$ induced reactions on the proton from a comparison of data taken on C and CH$_2$ targets. The spectra were interpreted in terms of different versions of vector meson dominance. Here we present results obtained from the theory and generator GiBUU. We first check the subtraction procedure used and then discuss the obtained mass spectra for the proton target. We point out that any conclusions on the version of VMD requires the knowledge of the $ρ$ spectral function in the interesting mass region.

# Dilepton Decay of Low-mass ρ Mesons

## Key claim

The HADES collaboration’s conclusion that the data favor VMD1 over VMD2 is not definitive, because the observed discrepancy at low dilepton masses can equally be explained, within VMD2, by an unmeasured change in the ρ spectral function below the 2π threshold, or by a form factor at the N*Nρ vertex, without invoking a different version of vector meson dominance.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU quantum-kinetic transport model and event generator to simulate π⁻ induced reactions on proton, carbon, and CH₂ targets at a beam momentum of 0.69 GeV (√s = 1.49 GeV). They first validated the subtraction procedure used by HADES to extract proton-target dilepton spectra from CH₂ and C data, then compared the resulting invariant mass distributions with GiBUU calculations that employ the VMD2 variant for ρ → e⁺e⁻ decay, using resonance parameters from the Manley-Saleski partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: p_π = 0.69 GeV, corresponding to √s = 1.49 GeV
- The πN invariant mass of the experiment is 1.46 GeV
- The 2π threshold for the ρ meson is m = 1.22 GeV
- The dilepton decay width constant C_ρ = 9.078 × 10⁻⁶
- The GiBUU cross section for CH₂ is larger than experimental values by about a factor 3
- The ratio of C to H cross sections from GiBUU is about 1.75, significantly smaller than the factor 2.9 used in the HADES analysis
- For the H target, the GiBUU spectrum is smaller than data by about 30% for masses between 0.3 and 0.5 GeV, and larger by about 100% for masses around 0.2 GeV
- The extracted experimental ρ mass distribution lies about a factor of 1.3 above the GiBUU calculation

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the HADES collaboration (HADES 2022), which measured dilepton spectra in π⁻ + p → e⁺e⁻ + n and interpreted them as evidence favoring VMD1 over VMD2. The authors use the GiBUU model to check the subtraction procedure that isolates the proton contribution from CH₂ and C targets. They point out that the low-mass region where VMD1 and VMD2 differ most lies far from the ρ meson peak, where the Breit-Wigner spectral function is unreliable. The paper argues that distinguishing between VMD versions requires experimental knowledge of the ρ spectral function below the 2π threshold, which is currently unavailable. The work also notes that alternative explanations exist, such as the radial excitation model of Faessler (2000) or the quark-core and pion-cloud model of Ramalho and Pena (2016), which predicted the data before it was measured.
