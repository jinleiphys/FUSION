# Compositeness of Hadrons and Near-Threshold Dynamics

> We present the recent developments in the studies of the structure of hadron resonances, focusing on the compositeness in terms of the hadronic degrees of freedom. We discuss the model dependence of the compositeness, and show that the structure of the near-threshold bound states and resonances is model-independently determined. The applications to various hadrons are summarized.

## Key claim

The compositeness of hadrons, which quantifies the molecular nature of a state in terms of asymptotic hadronic degrees of freedom, is in general a model-dependent quantity; however, in the weak-binding limit where the binding energy is much smaller than the cutoff scale, the compositeness becomes model-independent and can be determined from the scattering length and binding energy.

## Method

The paper formulates compositeness using a nonrelativistic effective field theory (EFT) with a Hamiltonian that couples a bare state \(B_0\) to a two-body scattering continuum \(\psi\phi\) via contact interactions. The compositeness \(X\) and elementariness \(Z\) are defined as the probabilities of finding the continuum and bare components in the bound state wavefunction, and are expressed in terms of the scattering amplitude and its derivative at the bound-state pole. The renormalization-scale dependence of \(X\) and \(Z\) is examined by varying the ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\), and the weak-binding limit is studied by expanding the loop function \(G(E)\) for energies small compared to \(\Lambda^2/(2\mu)\).

## Key numbers

* For a weakly-bound \(s\)-wave state, the scattering length \(a_0\) and the binding energy \(B\) (via the length scale \(R = 1/\sqrt{2\mu B}\)) satisfy \(a_0 = R \left\{ \frac{2X}{1+X} + \mathcal{O}(R_{\mathrm{typ}}/R) \right\}\), where \(R_{\mathrm{typ}} = 1/\Lambda\) is the range of the interaction.
* In the scaling limit \(R_{\mathrm{typ}} \to 0\), the relation reduces to \(a_0 = R\), which implies \(X = 1\).
* For unstable (quasi-bound) states, the compositeness \(X\) is complex and satisfies \(Z+X = 1\) with \(Z, X \in \mathbb{C}\); the prescription defines real probabilities \(\tilde{X} = (1 - |Z| + |X|)/2\) and \(\tilde{Z} = (1 - |X| + |Z|)/2\), with uncertainty \(U = |Z| + |X| - 1\).

## Context

This work extends the original compositeness concept introduced by Weinberg (1965) for stable bound states to hadron resonances, including unstable particles, using modern EFT methods. It builds on prior studies of hadronic molecules and exotic hadrons such as the \(f_0(980)\), \(\Lambda(1405)\), and \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) by Baru (2003), Sekihara (2014), and Guo (2015). The paper enables model-independent extraction of the molecular component of near-threshold resonances from scattering data, and provides a framework for interpreting the structure of newly discovered exotic states like the \(P_c(4450)\) pentaquark.
