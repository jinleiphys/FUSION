# Correlating $B\to K^{(\ast)} ö§\barö§$ and flavor anomalies in SMEFT

> The recent measurement of $\mathcal{B}(B^+\to K^+ö§\barö§)$ by Belle-II reveals a $2.8~ü$ deviation from the Standard Model (SM) prediction. ## Key claim

The Belle II measurement of $\mathcal{B}(B^+\to K^+\nu\bar{\nu})$ at $2.8\sigma$ above the Standard Model prediction, combined with the Belle upper limit on $\mathcal{B}(B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu})$ that makes the ratio of these two branching fractions smaller than the Standard Model expectation, can be simultaneously accommodated within Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) using only four dimension-six operators, provided that a right-handed operator $\mathcal{O}_R^{\nu_\ell}$ is included alongside the left-handed ones.

## Method

The authors describe the $b\to s\nu\bar{\nu}$ transition and related flavor-changing neutral-current and charged-current processes using Low-Energy Effective Field Theory (LEFT) operators below the electroweak scale, then match these onto four dimension-six SMEFT operators at the electroweak scale. They perform a global fit of the Wilson coefficients for these four operators to simultaneously accommodate the Belle II measurement of $\mathcal{B}(B^+\to K^+\nu\bar{\nu})$, the Belle upper limit on $\mathcal{B}(B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu})$, and existing flavor anomalies including $R_{K^{(\ast)}}$ and $R_{D^{(\ast)}}$, while also predicting branching fractions for related decays such as $B^+\to\tau^+\nu_\tau$, $D_s^+\to\tau^+\nu_\tau$, $B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu}$, and $B_s\to\tau^+\tau^-$.

## Key numbers

* $\mathcal{B}(B^+\to K^+\nu\bar{\nu})_\mathrm{exp} = (2.3 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-5}$ from Belle II, a $2.8\sigma$ deviation from the SM prediction of $(4.43 \pm 0.31) \times 10^{-6}$
* $\mathcal{B}(B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu})_\mathrm{exp} < 1.8 \times 10^{-5}$ from Belle, translated to $(0.87 \pm 0.66) \times 10^{-5}$ for analysis, compared to the SM prediction of $(9.47 \pm 1.40) \times 10^{-6}$
* The experimental ratio $R_\mathrm{exp} = 0.38 \pm 0.31$ for $\mathcal{B}(B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu})/\mathcal{B}(B^+\to K^+\nu\bar{\nu})$, compared to the SM prediction $R_\mathrm{SM} = 2.14 \pm 0.35$
* Predicted branching fraction for $B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu}$ from the fit: $(1.42 \pm 0.74) \times 10^{-5}$
* Predicted branching fraction for $B_s\to\tau^+\tau^-$ on the order of $10^{-4}$
* SM Wilson coefficient $C^{\nu_\ell}_{L,\mathrm{SM}} = -6.32(7)$ for the $b\to s\nu\bar{\nu}$ transition

## Context

This work extends the SMEFT approach of Calibbi (2015), which used only two operators ($\mathcal{O}_{\ell q}^{(1)}$ and $\mathcal{O}_{\ell q}^{(3)}$) to reconcile $b\to s\ell\ell$, $B\to K^{(*)}\nu\bar{\nu}$, and $B\to D^{(*)}\tau\nu$, by adding a right-handed operator $\mathcal{O}_R^{\nu_\ell}$ (requiring the additional SMEFT operator $\mathcal{O}_{\ell d}$) to simultaneously accommodate the new Belle II measurement and the Belle upper limit on $B^0\to K^{\ast0}\nu\bar{\nu}$. The study is among the first to systematically connect constraints from $B$ meson decays with those from $D$ meson decays within the same SMEFT framework, predicting that $\mathcal{B}(B^+\to\tau^+\nu_\tau)$ and $\mathcal{B}(D_s^+\to\tau^+\nu_\tau)$ align with Belle and BESIII measurements, and that $B_s\to\tau^+\tau^-$ should be observable in the near future.
