# Search for $α$-cluster states in even-even Cr isotopes

> The $α+ \mathrm{core}$ structure is investigated in even-even Cr isotopes from the viewpoint of the local potential model. The comparison of $Q_α/A$ values for even-even Cr isotopes and even-even $A = 46,54,56,58$ isobars indicates that $^{46}$Cr and $^{54}$Cr are the most favorable even-even Cr isotopes for $α$-clustering. The ground state bands of the two Cr isotopes are calculated through a local $α+ \mathrm{core}$ potential with two variable parameters. The calculated spectra give a very good description of most experimental $^{46}$Cr and $^{54}$Cr levels. The reduced $α$-widths, rms intercluster separations and $B(E2)$ transition rates are determined for the ground state bands. The calculations reproduce the order of magnitude of the available experimental $B(E2)$ values without using effective charges and indicate that the first members of the ground state bands present a stronger $α$-cluster character. The volume integral per nucleon pair and rms radius obtained for the $α+^{50}$Ti potential are consistent with those reported previously in the analysis of $α$ elastic scattering on $^{50}$Ti.

## Key claim

The paper argues that among even-even Cr isotopes, \(^{46}\)Cr and \(^{54}\)Cr are the most favorable for \(\alpha\)-clustering, based on a comparison of \(Q_{\alpha}/A\) values for isotopes and isobars, but notes that \(^{56}\)Cr and \(^{58}\)Cr have \(Q_{\alpha}/A\) values very close to those of the selected nuclei, suggesting the influence of a neutron subshell closure above \(N=28\).

## Method

The authors used the local potential model (LPM) with a phenomenological \(\alpha + \text{core}\) potential of the form \((1 + \text{Gaussian}) \times (\text{W.S.} + \text{W.S.}^3)\), where W.S. is a Woods-Saxon shape. The potential has two variable parameters (\(R\) and \(\sigma\)) fitted to reproduce the experimental \(0^+\) and \(4^+\) members of the ground state bands of \(^{46}\)Cr and \(^{54}\)Cr, while four other parameters (\(V_0\), \(a\), \(b\), \(\lambda\)) were fixed from previous work on nuclei in different mass regions. The Schrödinger radial equation was solved for the reduced mass of the \(\alpha + \text{core}\) system, and the Pauli principle was enforced via the Wildermuth condition with global quantum number \(G_{\text{g.s.}} = 12\).

## Key numbers

- \(Q_{\alpha}/A_T\) for \(^{46}\)Cr: \(\approx -147.7\) keV; for \(^{54}\)Cr: \(\approx -146.8\) keV; for \(^{56}\)Cr: \(\approx -147.1\) keV; for \(^{58}\)Cr: \(\approx -149.4\) keV (from Fig. 1 and text).
- Potential parameters for \(^{46}\)Cr (\(\alpha+^{42}\)Ti): \(R = 4.658\) fm, \(\sigma = 0.248\) fm; for \(^{54}\)Cr (\(\alpha+^{50}\)Ti): \(R = 4.674\) fm, \(\sigma = 0.210\) fm (Table I).
- Volume integral per nucleon pair \(J_R\) for \(^{46}\)Cr: 355.0 MeV fm\(^3\); for \(^{54}\)Cr: 301.1 MeV fm\(^3\) (Table II).
- RMS radius of the potential \(r_{\text{rms},R}\) for \(^{46}\)Cr: 4.314 fm; for \(^{54}\)Cr: 4.323 fm (Table II).
- RMS intercluster separation \(\langle R^2 \rangle^{1/2}\) for the \(0^+\) state of \(^{46}\)Cr: 4.339 fm; for \(^{54}\)Cr: 4.290 fm (Table III).
- Dimensionless reduced \(\alpha\)-width \(\theta_\alpha^2\) for the \(0^+\) state of \(^{46}\)Cr: \(6.916 \times 10^{-3}\); for \(^{54}\)Cr: \(2.184 \times 10^{-3}\) (Table III).
- Calculated \(B(E2; 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+)\) for \(^{46}\)Cr: 9.657 W.u. (experimental: 19(4) W.u.); for \(^{54}\)Cr: 7.456 W.u. (experimental: 14.4(6) W.u.) (Table IV).
- Calculated \(B(E2; 4^+ \rightarrow 2^+)\) for \(^{54}\)Cr: 10.049 W.u. (experimental: 26(9) W.u.) (Table IV).

## Context

This work extends the local potential model (LPM) previously applied by Souza and Miyake (2015) to nuclei around \(^{90}\)Zr, now to the Cr isotopic chain near the well-studied \(\alpha\)-cluster nucleus \(^{44}\)Ti (e.g., work by Michel et al. 1988, and others). The results are consistent with the real part of the optical potential for \(\alpha\) elastic scattering on \(^{50}\)Ti at 140 MeV reported by Khoa et al. (1982). The paper enables future \(\alpha\)-transfer experiments to confirm the cluster structure of higher-spin states in \(^{46}\)Cr and \(^{54}\)Cr, and provides a baseline for comparing \(\alpha\)-clustering in neutron-rich Cr isotopes with shell-model calculations (e.g., Kaneko et al. 2008, Togashi et al. 2015) that require effective charges.
