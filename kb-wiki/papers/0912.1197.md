# Nature of light scalars through photon-photon collisions

> The surprising thing is that arising almost 50 years ago from the linear sigma model (LSM) with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, the light scalar meson problem has become central in the nonperturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) for it has been made clear that LSM could be the low energy realization of QCD. First we review briefly signs of four-quark nature of light scalars. Then we show that the light scalars are produced in the two photon collisions via four-quark transitions in contrast to the classic $P$ wave tensor $q\bar q$ mesons that are produced via two-quark transitions $γγ\to q\bar q$. Thus we get new evidence of the four-quark nature of these states.

## Key claim

The light scalar mesons (sigma, kappa, f0(980), a0(980)) are produced in two-photon collisions via four-quark transitions (rescattering through charged meson loops) rather than direct two-quark transitions, providing new evidence for their four-quark nature; this conclusion depends on the assumption that the SU_L(2) x SU_R(2) linear sigma model is the correct low-energy realization of QCD.

## Method

The authors analyze the reactions gamma gamma -> pi+ pi-, gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0, and gamma gamma -> pi0 eta using a non-perturbative treatment of the linear sigma model that includes chiral shielding (a negative background phase that hides the sigma meson). They compute the amplitudes by combining Born terms with rescattering through charged pion and kaon loops, and compare their results to Belle data on these reactions. The direct coupling of scalar resonances to the gamma gamma system is extracted by fitting the data while conserving unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson pole position: sqrt(s_R) = (518 - i250) MeV (authors' result), compared to (441 +16/-8 - i272 +12.5/-9) MeV from Leutwyler and collaborators using Roy equations
- Sigma meson Breit-Wigner mass M_res = 0.43 GeV, total width Gamma_res(M^2_res) = 0.67 GeV, renormalized width = 0.53 GeV
- Two-photon widths averaged over resonance mass distributions: <Gamma_{sigma -> gamma gamma}>_{pi pi} ≈ 0.45 keV, <Gamma_{f0 -> gamma gamma}>_{pi pi} ≈ 0.19 keV, <Gamma_{a0 -> gamma gamma}>_{pi eta} ≈ 0.34 keV
- Direct coupling of sigma to gamma gamma gives Gamma^(0)_{sigma gamma gamma} ≈ 0.0034 keV, consistent with the authors' 1982 prediction
- Predicted four-quark model widths for a0(980) and f0(980) to gamma gamma: approximately 0.27 keV each (from 1982), consistent with experimental measurements (Crystal Ball: Gamma(a0 -> gamma gamma) = (0.19 ± 0.07 +0.1/-0.07)/B(a0 -> pi eta) keV; JADE: (0.28 ± 0.04 ± 0.1)/B(a0 -> pi eta) keV; Crystal Ball: Gamma(f0 -> gamma gamma) = (0.31 ± 0.14 ± 0.09) keV; MARK II: (0.24 ± 0.06 ± 0.15) keV)
- In contrast, the q\bar q model predicted Gamma(a0 -> gamma gamma) = (1.5 - 5.9) x (1.04 ± 0.09) keV and Gamma(f0 -> gamma gamma) = (1.7 - 5.5) x (2.8 ± 0.4) keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-standing program (Achasov and Shestakov, 1994, 2007) applying the linear sigma model with chiral shielding to light scalar mesons, and directly confronts the four-quark model of Jaffe (1977) from the MIT bag model. The analysis of Belle data on gamma gamma -> pi pi and gamma gamma -> pi0 eta (2007-2009) provides the first high-statistics experimental test of the production mechanism, showing that the direct coupling of light scalars to two photons is negligible and that their two-photon decays proceed through charged meson loops (four-quark transitions), in contrast to the classic P-wave tensor q\bar q mesons (a2(1320), f2(1270), f'2(1525)) which decay via direct two-quark transitions. The results enable future phenomenological treatments of the U_L(3) x U_R(3) linear sigma model incorporating sigma-f0 mixing and Adler self-consistency conditions.
