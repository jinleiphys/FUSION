# The Role of Meson Retardation in the NN Interaction above Pion Threshold

> A model is developed for the hadronic interaction in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold which is based on meson, nucleon and $Δ$ degrees of freedom and which includes full meson retardation in the exchange operators. For technical reasons, the model allows maximal one meson to be present explicitly. Thus the Hilbert space contains besides $NN$ and $NΔ$ also configurations consisting of two nucleons and one meson. For this reason, only two- and three-body unitarity is obeyed, and the model is suited for reactions in the two nucleon sector only, where one pion is produced or absorbed. Starting from a realistic pure nucleonic retarded potential, which had to be renormalized because of the additional $π$ and $Δ$ degrees of freedom, a reasonable fit to experimental $NN$-scattering data could be achieved.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for the hadronic interaction in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold that includes full meson retardation in the exchange operators, but it is limited by the one-meson-approximation (maximally one meson present explicitly) and therefore obeys only two- and three-body unitarity, making it suited only for reactions where one pion is produced or absorbed.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian in a Hilbert space containing three orthogonal sectors: bare nucleons (NN), one nucleon replaced by a Delta isobar (NΔ), and two nucleons plus one meson (π, η, σ, δ, ω, ρ). They use time-ordered perturbation theory with meson-nucleon and πNΔ vertices, incorporating a realistic pure nucleonic retarded potential (the Elster potential) as input, which is renormalized to avoid double counting when the additional π and Δ degrees of freedom are included. The NN scattering T-matrix is derived via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with dressed propagators and renormalization operators, and the deuteron state is obtained from a Schrödinger equation with the mesonic component eliminated.

## Key numbers

- The fit to πN scattering in the P33 channel and the M1+^(3/2) multipole of photopion production yields: (f^0_ΔNπ)^2/(4π) = 0.9452, Λ_ΔNπ = 482.11 MeV, M^0_Δ = 1281.7 MeV, with dipole form factor (n_Δπ = 2)
- For comparison, prior work (WiA93, PoS87) used: (f^0_ΔNπ)^2/(4π) = 1.393, Λ_ΔNπ = 287.9 MeV, M^0_Δ = 1315 MeV, with monopole form factor (n_Δπ = 1)
- The model is applicable for excitation energies up to about 500 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy ε_B appears in the relation M_d = 2 M_N - ε_B

## Context

This work extends the coupled channel approach of Wilhelm et al. (1993, 1995, 1996) and Bulla and Sauer (1992) by including full meson retardation, which had previously been neglected in static approximations. It addresses the failure of static approaches to describe deuteron photodisintegration data in the Δ resonance region, as conjectured by Wilhelm et al. (1993). The model overcomes shortcomings of earlier retarded treatments such as those by Kloet and Silbar (1980) and Tanabe and Ohta (1988), which lacked realistic short-range interactions, by using the Elster potential (1986, 1988) as input. The formalism enables future applications to electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron (photo- and electrodisintegration, pion production) and πd scattering.
