# Selected Configuration Interaction for Resonances

> Electronic resonances are metastable states that can decay by electron loss. They are ubiquitous across various fields of science, such as chemistry, physics, and biology. However, current theoretical and computational models for resonances cannot yet rival the level of accuracy achieved by bound-state methodologies. Here, we generalize selected configuration interaction (SCI) to treat resonances using the complex absorbing potential (CAP) technique. By modifying the selection procedure and the extrapolation protocol of standard SCI, the resulting CAP-SCI method yields resonance positions and widths of full configuration interaction quality. Initial results for the shape resonances of \ce{N2-} and \ce{CO-} reveal the important effect of high-order correlation, which shifts the values obtained with CAP-augmented equation-of-motion coupled-cluster with singles and doubles by more than \SI{0.1}{\eV}. The present CAP-SCI approach represents a cornerstone in the development of highly-accurate methodologies for resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by modifying the selection procedure and extrapolation protocol of standard selected configuration interaction (SCI) to incorporate a complex absorbing potential (CAP), the resulting CAP-SCI method yields resonance positions and widths of full configuration interaction (FCI) quality, with the caveat that the CAP strength parameters were taken from prior CAP-EOM-EA-CCSD calculations rather than optimized within the new method.

## Method

The authors combined the complex absorbing potential (CAP) technique with the CIPSI variant of selected configuration interaction (SCI), modifying the Hamiltonian to be complex-symmetric and adapting the Davidson diagonalization, perturbative selection, and extrapolation procedures for complex-valued energies. They tested four orbital sets (restricted Hartree-Fock and natural orbitals for both neutral and anionic species) and three selection criteria (based on the norm, real part, or imaginary part of the second-order perturbative contribution) to determine the optimal protocol, then applied it to the shape resonances of N2- and CO- using the aug-cc-pVTZ+3s3p3d basis set with CAP parameters from Zuev et al. (2014).

## Context

This work extends the selected configuration interaction methodology, which has been used to systematically approach the FCI limit for bound states (Eriksen 2020, Loos 2020, Damour 2023), to the treatment of electronic resonances via the CAP technique. It addresses the gap between bound-state and resonance methodologies noted by Jagau (2017, 2022), and provides a benchmark for more approximate methods such as the CAP-EOM-EA-CCSD approach of Zuev et al. (2014). The CAP-SCI approach enables future highly-accurate calculations of resonance positions and widths with uncertainties below 1 kcal/mol.
