# Axially Symmetric Quadrupole-Octupole Model incorporating Sextic Potential

> We present an extended application of the analytic quadrupole octupole axially symmetric model, originally employed to study the octupole deformation and vibrations in light actinides using an infinite well potential (IW). In this work, we extend the model's applicability to a broader range of nuclei exhibiting octupole deformation by incorporating a sextic potential instead of the Davidson potential.Similarly to conventional models, such as AQOA-IW (for infinite square potential) and AQOA-D (for the Davidson potential), our proposed model is referred to as AQOA-S. By employing the sextic potential, phenomenologically represented as $v(\tildeβ) = a_1\tilde β^2+a_2\tilde β^4+a_3\tilde β^6$, we can derive analytical expressions for the energy spectra and transition rates (B(E1), B(E2), B(E3)). The energy spectra of the model are essentially governed by two critical parameters: $ϕ_0$, indicating the balance between octupole and quadrupole strain, and $α$, a key factor in adjusting the shape and behavior of the spectra through the sextic potential. In terms of applications, the study encompasses five isotopes, namely $^{222-226}$Ra and $^{224,226}$Th. Significantly, our model demonstrates remarkable agreement with the corresponding experimental data, particularly for the recently determined B(EL) transition rates of $^{224}$Ra, surpassing the performance of the model that employs the Davidson potential. The stability of the octupole deformation in $^{224}$Ra adds particular significance to these findings.

## Key claim

The AQOA-S model, which replaces the Davidson potential with a sextic potential in the analytic quadrupole-octupole axially symmetric framework, achieves remarkable agreement with experimental data for five actinide isotopes, particularly for the recently determined B(EL) transition rates of $^{224}$Ra, surpassing the performance of the model employing the Davidson potential, though the model's applicability is demonstrated only for nuclei at the boundary between octupole deformation regions and octupole vibrations.

## Method

The authors solve the collective Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for axially symmetric quadrupole-octupole deformations by separating variables into a radial coordinate $\tilde\beta$ and an angular coordinate $\phi$, where the radial potential is a sextic oscillator $v(\tilde\beta) = a_1\tilde\beta^2 + a_2\tilde\beta^4 + a_3\tilde\beta^6$ and the angular potential consists of two harmonic oscillators centered at $\pm\phi_0$. The $\tilde\beta$ equation is solved quasi-exactly using polynomial ansatz wavefunctions, while the $\phi$ equation reduces to a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator, yielding analytical expressions for energy spectra and B(E1), B(E2), and B(E3) transition rates. The model is applied to five isotopes ($^{222-226}$Ra and $^{224,226}$Th) by fitting two critical parameters: $\phi_0$ (balance between octupole and quadrupole strain) and $\alpha$ (shape parameter from the sextic potential).

## Context

This work extends the analytic quadrupole-octupole axially symmetric (AQOA) model previously developed by Bonatsos et al. (2015) using an infinite well potential (AQOA-IW) and by Chabab et al. (2016) using a Davidson potential (AQOA-D), by incorporating a sextic oscillator potential that has been successfully applied in collective Bohr-Mottelson models for shape coexistence phenomena. The model enables analytical description of alternating parity bands in actinides at the boundary between octupole deformation and octupole vibration regions, and its success for $^{224}$Ra, where octupole deformation is particularly stable, suggests it can be applied to study similar nuclei where the sextic potential may better represent the nuclear potential energy surface than previous potentials.
