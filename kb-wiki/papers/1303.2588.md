# Improved Search for $\bar ν_μ\rightarrow \bar ν_e$ Oscillations in the MiniBooNE Experiment

> The MiniBooNE experiment at Fermilab reports results from an analysis of $\bar ν_e$ appearance data from $11.27 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target in antineutrino mode, an increase of approximately a factor of two over the previously reported results. An event excess of $78.4 \pm 28.5$ events ($2.8 σ$) is observed in the energy range $200<E_ν^{QE}<1250$ MeV. If interpreted in a two-neutrino oscillation model, $\barν_μ\rightarrow\barν_e$, the best oscillation fit to the excess has a probability of 66% while the background-only fit has a $χ^2$-probability of 0.5% relative to the best fit. The data are consistent with antineutrino oscillations in the $0.01 < Δm^2 < 1.0$ eV$^2$ range and have some overlap with the evidence for antineutrino oscillations from the Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector (LSND). All of the major backgrounds are constrained by in-situ event measurements so non-oscillation explanations would need to invoke new anomalous background processes. The neutrino mode running also shows an excess at low energy of $162.0 \pm 47.8$ events ($3.4 σ$) but the energy distribution of the excess is marginally compatible with a simple two neutrino oscillation formalism. Expanded models with several sterile neutrinos can reduce the incompatibility by allowing for CP violating effects between neutrino and antineutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE experiment observes a 2.8σ excess of antineutrino-mode events consistent with \(\bar{\nu}_\mu \rightarrow \bar{\nu}_e\) oscillations in the 0.01 to 1.0 eV² \(\Delta m^2\) range, but the neutrino-mode excess is only marginally compatible with a simple two-neutrino oscillation formalism, requiring expanded models with several sterile neutrinos to reduce the incompatibility.

## Method

The MiniBooNE Collaboration analyzed \(\bar{\nu}_e\) appearance data from \(11.27 \times 10^{20}\) protons on target in antineutrino mode, using a 541 m baseline and a 806-ton mineral oil Cherenkov detector. Event selection targeted charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) interactions, with backgrounds constrained by in-situ measurements of \(\nu_\mu/\bar{\nu}_\mu\) CCQE events, NC \(\pi^0\) production, and external neutrino interactions. A likelihood-ratio technique with frequentist corrections from fake data studies was used to extract oscillation parameters from a combined fit of muon-like and electron-like event energy distributions.

## Key numbers

- Event excess of \(78.4 \pm 28.5\) events (2.8σ) in antineutrino mode for \(200 < E_\nu^{QE} < 1250\) MeV
- Best oscillation fit has probability 66% while background-only fit has \(\chi^2\)-probability 0.5% relative to best fit
- Best antineutrino oscillation fit at (\(\Delta m^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta\)) = (0.043 eV², 0.88) for \(200 < E_\nu^{QE} < 3000\) MeV
- Neutrino-mode excess of \(162.0 \pm 47.8\) events (3.4σ) for \(200 < E_\nu^{QE} < 1250\) MeV
- Best neutrino oscillation fit at (\(\Delta m^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta\)) = (3.14 eV², 0.002) with \(\chi^2/ndf = 13.2/6.8\) (6.1% probability)
- Total of 478 data events in antineutrino mode vs. background expectation of \(399.6 \pm 20.0\) (stat.) \(\pm 20.3\) (syst.) events
- Total of 952 data events in neutrino mode vs. background expectation of \(790.1 \pm 28.1\) (stat.) \(\pm 38.7\) (syst.) events

## Context

This work extends the MiniBooNE antineutrino oscillation search (previously reported with approximately half the statistics) and provides a direct test of the LSND (Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector) oscillation signal, with some overlap in the allowed parameter region. The results are consistent with KARMEN limits but show tension between neutrino and antineutrino modes in simple two-neutrino models, motivating expanded sterile neutrino frameworks (3+N models) that can accommodate CP-violating effects, as explored by Sorel, Karagiorgi, Giunti, and Kopp. The analysis also incorporates constraints from SciBooNE kaon production measurements and improved treatment of nuclear effects from models by Martini, Mosel, and Nieves.
