# Euclidean Dynamical Symmetry in Nuclear Shape Phase Transitions

> The Euclidean dynamical symmetry hidden in the critical region of nuclear shape phase transitions is revealed by a novel algebraic F(5) description. With a nonlinear projection, it is shown that the dynamics in the critical region of the spherical--axial deformed and the spherical--$γ$ soft shape phase transitions can indeed be manifested by this description, which thus provides a unified symmetry--based interpretation of the critical phenomena in the region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Euclidean dynamical symmetry Eu(5) is the hidden underlying symmetry of the critical region of nuclear shape phase transitions, but this is demonstrated only through an algebraic F(5) scheme that approximates the infinite-well critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) within a finite boson-number subspace of the interacting boson model.

## Method

The authors constructed an algebraic Hamiltonian with Eu(5) dynamical symmetry using d-boson creation and annihilation operators, then diagonalized it within a finite U(6) subspace of the interacting boson model for fixed boson number N. They introduced a nonlinear projection that maps the seniority quantum number τ of the E(5) critical point symmetry onto the angular-momentum-dependent index ν of the X(5) critical point symmetry, parameterized by a continuous variable χ that interpolates between the two limits.

## Key numbers

- Energy ratio E(4_1)/E(2_1) = 2.19 for the F(5) scheme with χ=0 at N=1000, compared to 2.20 for the E(5) model and 2.91 for the X(5) model
- Energy ratio E(6_1)/E(0_2) = 1.19 for χ=0 at N=1000, compared to 1.18 for E(5) and 0.96 for X(5)
- Energy ratio E(0_2)/E(2_1) = 3.02 for χ=0 at N=1000, compared to 3.03 for E(5) and 5.67 for X(5)
- B(E2; 4_1 → 2_1)/B(E2; 2_1 → 0_1) = 1.67 for χ=0 at N=1000, compared to 1.68 for E(5) and 1.58 for X(5)
- B(E2; 0_2 → 2_1)/B(E2; 2_1 → 0_1) = 0.86 for χ=0 at N=1000, compared to 0.86 for E(5) and 0.63 for X(5)
- The F(5) scheme with χ = -1.32 at N=1000 reproduces the X(5) model results: E(4_1)/E(2_1)=2.89, E(6_1)/E(0_2)=0.96, E(0_2)/E(2_1)=5.61
- Ratio E(0_3)/E(0_2) = 2.50 for N=1000 in the F(5) scheme, independent of χ
- Ratio E(14_1)/E(0_ξ) = 3.64 in the F(5) scheme, independent of χ
- Excited level energies scale with N^{-1} and E2 transition rates scale with N^1 in the F(5) scheme

## Context

This work extends the critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello, 2000) and X(5) (Iachello, 2001) from the Bohr-Mottelson geometric model into an algebraic framework based on the interacting boson model (Iachello and Arima, 1987). It provides a unified symmetry-based description of the entire transitional region between the spherical-to-gamma-soft and spherical-to-axially-deformed shape phase transitions, which previously required separate treatments. The F(5) scheme enables future systematic classification of transitional nuclei as candidates for the Eu(5) dynamical symmetry, with experimental data for nuclei such as 102Pd, 128Xe, 146,148Ce, and 150Nd shown to be well reproduced.
