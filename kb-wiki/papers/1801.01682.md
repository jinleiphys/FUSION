# Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in a parallel electromagnetic field

> We explore the features of the $U_A(1)$ and chiral symmetry breaking of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model without the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant term in the presence of a parallel electromagnetic field. We show that the electromagnetic chiral anomaly can induce both finite neutral pion condensate and isospin-singlet pseudo-scalar $η$ condensate and thus modifies the chiral symmetry breaking pattern. In order to characterize the strength of the $U_A(1)$ symmetry breaking, we evaluate the susceptibility associated with the $U_A(1)$ charge. The result shows that the susceptibility contributed from the chiral anomaly is consistent with the behavior of the corresponding $η$ condensate. The spectra of the mesonic excitations are also studied.

## Key claim
The electromagnetic chiral anomaly in a parallel electromagnetic field induces both a finite neutral pion condensate and an isospin-singlet pseudoscalar η condensate, thereby modifying the chiral symmetry breaking pattern; however, the U_A(1) symmetry is not completely restored at any value of the second Lorentz invariant I₂ because the triangle anomaly remains finite.

## Method
The authors adopt a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model without the Kobayashi–Maskawa–'t Hooft determinant term, preserving U_L(2) ⊗ U_R(2) symmetry, and introduce a parallel electromagnetic field via the covariant derivative. They derive and solve four coupled gap equations for the neutral condensates (scalar σ, isospin-breaking a₃, pseudoscalar η, and neutral pion π⁰) using the Schwinger proper-time method, then compute the U_A(1) susceptibility and mesonic excitation spectra in the random phase approximation.

## Key numbers
- The η condensate is given by η = (N_c m*)/(4π² m_π² f_π²) [(q_u √I₂)² + (q_d √I₂)²], where m* is the quark mass in vacuum, m_π the pion mass, f_π the pion decay constant, and I₂ = E·B.
- The π⁰ condensate is given by π⁰ = (N_c m*)/(4π² m_π² f_π²) [(q_u √I₂)² − (q_d √I₂)²].
- Model parameters are fixed as G = 4.93 GeV⁻², Λ = 0.653 GeV, and m₀ = 5 MeV.
- For the case E = B, the critical I₂ at which m_f = 0 differs for u and d quarks; the critical I₂ for the u quark corresponds to the peak of |δm|, while that for the d quark corresponds to the peak of η.
- The total U_A(1) susceptibility χ always decreases with increasing I₂, indicating effective restoration of U_A(1) symmetry.
- In the small I₂ limit, the masses of the lighter modes Π⁰_u and Π⁰_d behave as m_{Π⁰_f} ≈ m_π (1 − (N_c/(48π²))(g²_{πqq}/m*⁴) q_f² I₂).

## Context
This work extends the previous study by Cao (2015) which found π⁰ condensation in a parallel EM field, now including both isospin-singlet η condensation and flavor mass splitting δm. The results are consistent with the Wigner function formalism calculations by Fang (2016) and Guo (2017). The paper enables future extensions to finite temperature and chemical potential, as already begun by Wang (2017), and to three-flavor NJL models with the Kobayashi–Maskawa–'t Hooft determinant to study the interplay between QCD and QED anomalies.
