# Hadron matter in neutron stars in view of gravitational wave observations

> In this review we highlight a few physical properties of neutron stars and their theoretical treatment inasmuch as they can be useful for nuclear and particle physicists concerned with matter at finite density (and newly, temperature). Conversely, we lay out some of the hadron physics necessary to test General Relativity with binary mergers including at least one neutron star, in view of the event GW170817: neutron stars and their mergers reach the highest matter densities known, offering access to the matter side of Einstein's equations. In addition to minimum introductory material for those interested in starting research in the field of neutron stars, we dedicate quite some effort to a discussion of the Equation of State of hadron matter in view of gravitational wave developments; we address phase transitions and how the new data may help; we show why transport is expected to be dominated by turbulence instead of diffusion through most if not all of the star, in view of the transport coefficients that have been calculated from microscopic hadron physics; and we relate many of the interesting physics topics in neutron stars to the radius and tidal deformability.

## Key claim

This review argues that neutron star mergers, particularly GW170817, provide a unique laboratory for simultaneously constraining both the equation of state of hadron matter at high density and modifications to General Relativity, but cautions that current tests of gravity using neutron stars may suffer from circular reasoning because the equations of state used in those tests already assume General Relativity when incorporating astrophysical constraints like the 2 solar mass maximum.

## Method

The authors compile a pedagogical review of neutron star physics organized around the connection between hadron physics and gravitational wave observations. They discuss the anatomy of gravitational wave signals from binary mergers, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static neutron stars, constraints on masses and radii from observations, the equation of state of cold hadron matter including model-independent constraints from QCD, possible phase transitions in dense matter, transport properties dominated by turbulence rather than diffusion, and stellar oscillation modes. They also examine post-Newtonian constraints on modifications to General Relativity from GW170817 and discuss merger rate predictions from binary population synthesis models and short gamma-ray burst associations.

## Key numbers

* The chirp mass of a binary system is given by M = ((m1 m2)^3/(m1+m2))^(1/5), which for GW170817 was extracted from the frequency evolution df/dt of the gravitational wave signal
* The black hole merger GW150914 had a ringdown damping time tau ~ 4 milliseconds, commensurate with a Schwarzschild-radius light-crossing time of about 2.5 ms
* The Gravity Probe B constraint on the R^2 coefficient in f(R) gravity is a < 5 x 10^5 km^2
* Current bounds on the R^2 parameter from neutron star studies give a < 10^3 x (10 km)^2, where 10 km is the characteristic neutron star scale
* The bound on the first post-Newtonian coefficient from GW170817 improved from ~1000 before aLIGO to ~1 after black hole mergers to ~0.1 with the neutron star merger
* The detection rate per unit volume for LIGO is R = 0.18(+0.13/-0.06) x (1/100 Mpc)^3 yr^(-1) and R = 0.09(+0.12/-0.06) x (1/100 Mpc)^3 yr^(-1)
* The aLIGO third run O3 reach is expected to be up to ~170 Mpc, yielding a volume of 0.02 Gpc^3
* The Milky Way should contain O(10^8-10^9) pulsars, yielding a cosmological number density of perhaps 0.055/Mpc^3, of which 2300 are known, 18 are binary systems, and up to a dozen have orbital periods of order hours
* Europium production from r-process in binary mergers yields an estimated detection rate of 2.5-11 yr^(-1)
* The estimated number of events for the third run is 0.11-3.4 for double neutron star mergers and 0.46-3.9 for neutron star-black hole mergers
* From short gamma-ray burst populations, a lower estimate of 1.87 events/year for the aLIGO-VIRGO network has been put forward
* The DLT40 survey had a sample of 2200 luminous galaxies matching the GW170817 trigger, with only 23 in the sky patch marked by gravitational wave detectors

## Context

This review extends the body of work following the first binary neutron star merger event GW170817 (LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2017) and updates earlier reviews such as Vidana (2018). It connects hadron physics predictions for the equation of state to astrophysical constraints from gravitational wave observations, and discusses how post-Newtonian bounds from GW170817 (Abbott, 2018) improve upon earlier bounds from black hole mergers like GW150914 (LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2016). The authors note that their own research program provides hadron physics equations of state using only laboratory and theoretical information, without astrophysical input that assumes General Relativity, hosted at http://teorica.fis.ucm.es/nEoS/. The review enables future work on simultaneously constraining both the equation of state and modifications to General Relativity from neutron star observations, and discusses open questions about merger rates from binary population synthesis models (Chruslinska, 2018) and the association between gravitational wave events and short gamma-ray bursts (Wu, 2019).
