# Understanding close-lying exotic charmonia states within QCD sum rules

> Motivated by the experimental findings of some new exotic states decaying into channels like $J/ψϕ$, we investigate the formation of resonances/bound states in the $D^*_s\bar D^*_s$ system using QCD sum rules. To do this we start with a current of the type vector times vector and use spin projectors to separate the spin 0, 1 and 2 contributions to the correlation function. We find three states with isospin 0, nearly spin degenerate, with a mass around 4.1 GeV. We have also investigated the decay of these states to $J/ψϕ$ and provide the corresponding partial widths. Such information should be useful for experimental studies in future.

## Key claim

The paper claims that QCD sum rules predict three nearly spin-degenerate isoscalar states in the $D^*_s\bar D^*_s$ system with masses around 4.1 GeV, but the authors explicitly note that the mass calculations alone do not provide enough information to associate these states with the experimentally observed $X(4140)$ and $X(4160)$ states, and that the results are compatible within errors with states found in the $\bar D^* D^*$ system studied earlier.

## Method

The authors constructed a vector-times-vector interpolating current for the $D^*_s\bar D^*_s$ system and applied spin projectors to separate the spin 0, 1, and 2 contributions to the two-point correlation function. They performed an operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-eight terms, including perturbative, quark condensate, gluon condensate, and mixed condensate contributions, then used Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to extract masses and couplings. For the decay widths to $J/\psi\phi$, they calculated a three-point correlation function at leading order in $\alpha_s$ with condensates up to dimension five, applying the same spin projection and Borel transformation procedure.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the spin-0 ($0^{++}$) state: $M_0 = (4.114 \pm 0.130)$ GeV, with coupling $\lambda_0 = (2.215 \pm 0.564) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^5$
- Mass of the spin-1 ($1^{+-}$) state: $M_1 = (4.120 \pm 0.127)$ GeV, with coupling $\lambda_1 = (3.852 \pm 0.958) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^5$
- Mass of the spin-2 ($2^{++}$) state: $M_2 = (4.117 \pm 0.123)$ GeV, with coupling $\lambda_2 = (4.963 \pm 1.243) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^5$
- The Borel window for the mass sum rules was found in the range $M_B^2 = [2.15, 2.45]$ GeV$^2$ for $\sqrt{s_0} = 4.3$ GeV
- The continuum threshold was varied from $\sqrt{s_0} = 4.3$ GeV to 4.7 GeV
- Input parameters include $m_s = (0.13 \pm 0.03)$ GeV, $m_c = (1.23 \pm 0.05)$ GeV, $\langle q\bar q\rangle = -(0.23 \pm 0.03)^3$ GeV$^3$, $\langle g^2 G^2\rangle = (0.88 \pm 0.25)$ GeV$^4$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule study of the $\bar D^* D^*$ system (Martinez Torres et al., 2015) to the strange sector, finding that the masses from both systems are compatible within errors, which the authors attribute to approximate heavy quark spin symmetry. The results are compared with coupled-channel effective field theory calculations (Hidalgo-Duque et al., 2012) that treat $D^*\bar D^*$ and $D_s^*\bar D_s^*$ as coupled channels. The paper aims to help clarify whether $X(4140)$ and $X(4160)$ are distinct states or the same state, and whether the $X(4140)$ signal is affected by a nearby cusp effect, as suggested by some studies.
