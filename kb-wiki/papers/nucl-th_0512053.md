# Fragment isospin distributions and the phase diagram of excited nuclear systems

> Fragment average isospin distributions are investigated within a microcanonical multifragmentation model in different regions of the phase diagram. The results indicate that in the liquid phase $<N/Z>$ versus $Z$ is monotonically increasing, in the phase coexistence region it has a rise and fall shape and in the gas phase it is constant. Deviations from this behavior may manifest at low fragment multiplicity as a consequence of mass/charge conservation. Characterization of the "free" and "bound" phases function of fragment charge reconfirms the neutron enrichment of the "free" phase with respect to the "bound" one irrespectively the localization of the multifragmentation event in the phase diagram.

# Fragment isospin distributions and the phase diagram of excited nuclear systems

## Key claim

The shape of the fragment average isospin distribution <N/Z> as a function of fragment charge Z is a signature of the thermodynamic phase of the excited nuclear system, but deviations from the expected shape may occur at low fragment multiplicity due to mass/charge conservation constraints rather than reflecting the true phase state.

## Method

The Microcanonical Multifragmentation Model (MMM) was used to simulate the break-up of excited nuclear systems (200,82) and (50,23) with and without Coulomb interaction, scanning the phase diagram along constant beta-P (inverse temperature times pressure) trajectories. Fragment average isospin distributions were computed as a function of fragment charge for states identified as liquid, phase coexistence, and gas regions based on the previously established phase diagram of the model. The effects of secondary particle evaporation were also evaluated using a standard Weisskopf evaporation scheme.

## Key numbers

- Critical point for (200,82) without Coulomb: T_C = 9.1 MeV, P_C = 1.25 x 10^-1 MeV/fm^3, E_C = 6.75 MeV/nucleon, (V/V0)_C = 1.33
- Critical point for (50,23) with Coulomb: T_C = 4.9 MeV, P_C = 7.8 x 10^-3 MeV/fm^3, E_C = 4.6 MeV/nucleon, (V/V0)_C = 12
- Critical point for (200,82) with Coulomb: T_C = 3.7 MeV, P_C = 1.7 x 10^-3 MeV/fm^3, E_C = 7.75 MeV/nucleon, (V/V0)_C = 130
- For (200,82) without Coulomb along beta-P = 3 x 10^-3 fm^-3: excitation energy from 2 to 12 MeV/nucleon, temperature around 6 MeV, average freeze-out volume from 2V0 to 16V0
- For (50,23) with Coulomb along beta-P = 1 x 10^-3 fm^-3: liquid phase for E <= 3.5 MeV/nucleon, phase coexistence for 3.5 <= E <= 6.3 MeV/nucleon, gas phase for E > 6.3 MeV/nucleon
- Source isospin I_s = 1.44 for (200,82) systems and I_s = 1.174 for (50,23)

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin fractionation in nuclear multifragmentation, previously predicted by mean-field models such as Mueller and Serot (relativistic mean-field model), the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics model, and the Stochastic Mean Field model. The microcanonical approach complements earlier grandcanonical isoscaling analyses applied to experimental data from Sn+Sn reactions at 50 MeV/nucleon (Xu et al.), Sn+Ni collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon (Geraci et al.), and proton-induced multifragmentation (Martin et al.). The results enable experimental identification of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition region through fragment isospin distributions, provided finite-size effects from mass/charge conservation are properly accounted for.
