# Quantum nucleation of up-down quark matter and astrophysical implications

> Quark matter with only $u$ and $d$ quarks ($ud$QM) might be the ground state of baryonic matter at large baryon number $A>A_{\rm min}$. With $A_{\rm min}\gtrsim 300$, this has no direct conflict with the stability of ordinary nuclei. An intriguing test of this scenario is to look for quantum nucleation of $ud$QM inside neutron stars due to their large baryon densities. In this paper, we study the transition rate of cold neutron stars to $ud$ quark stars ($ud$QSs) and the astrophysical implications, considering the relevant theoretical uncertainties and observational constraints. It turns out that a large portion of parameter space predicts an instantaneous transition, and so the observed neutron stars are mostly $ud$QSs. We find this possibility still viable under the recent gravitational wave and pulsar observations, although there are debates on its compatibility with some observations that involve complicated structure of quark matter. The tension could be partially relieved in the two-families scenario, where the high-mass stars ($M\gtrsim2 M_{\odot}$) are all $ud$QSs and the low-mass ones ($M\sim1.4\, M_{\odot}$) are mostly hadronic stars. In this case, the slow transition of the low-mass hadronic stars points to a very specific class of hadronic models with moderately stiff EOSs, and $ud$QM properties are also strongly constrained.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a large portion of the parameter space for stable up-down quark matter predicts an instantaneous transition of cold neutron stars to up-down quark stars, meaning most observed neutron stars would actually be quark stars; however, this conclusion depends on the hadronic matter equation of state being sufficiently soft, and the scenario remains viable under recent gravitational wave and pulsar observations only if certain tensions regarding the complicated structure of quark matter can be partially relieved by a two-families scenario where high-mass stars are quark stars and low-mass stars remain hadronic.

## Method

The authors calculated the quantum nucleation rate of up-down quark matter droplets inside cold neutron stars using a semiclassical WKB approximation for tunneling through a potential barrier, where the potential energy combines a negative volume term favoring quark matter and a positive surface tension term. They then determined the transition time for a hadronic star by evaluating the minimum droplet formation time and multiplying by the number of nucleation sites, scanning over different hadronic matter equations of state and effective bag constant values for the quark matter. The resulting transition times were compared to the age of the universe to determine whether observed stars would have converted, and the predictions for quark star masses and tidal deformabilities were compared to pulsar mass measurements and gravitational wave observations.

## Key numbers

- Minimum baryon number for stable up-down quark matter: \(A_{\rm min}\gtrsim 300\), corresponding to \(\varepsilon_{\rm min}\gtrsim 900\) MeV
- Effective bag constant range for stable up-down quark matter: \(50\;\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}} \lesssim B_{\rm eff}\lesssim 57\;\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}}\)
- Surface tension for quark-vacuum interface: \(\sigma_{s0}\approx 20\;\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-2}}\)
- Maximum rescaled mass for up-down quark stars: \(\bar{M}_{\rm max}\approx 0.052\), corresponding to \(M_{\rm max}\approx 15.2 M_\odot (B_{\rm eff}/\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}})^{-1/2}\)
- Upper bound on effective bag constant from pulsar J0740+6620 (\(M\approx 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\)): \(B_{\rm eff}\lesssim 50.3^{+4.5}_{-4.4}\;\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}}\) at 68% confidence level
- Minimum tidal deformability at maximum mass: \(\Lambda_{\rm min}\approx 23\)
- Central pressure for maximum mass quark stars: \(P_{\rm max}\approx 5.1 B_{\rm eff}\)
- Maximum Fermi momentum in quark stars: \(p^{\rm max}_{\rm F}\approx 590\) MeV for the upper end of the \(B_{\rm eff}\) range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ren and Zhang, 2017) which demonstrated that up-down quark matter can be more stable than strange quark matter in a phenomenological quark-meson model, and it addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where heavy pulsars above \(2 M_\odot\) rule out soft hadronic equations of state with hyperons. The paper builds on the quantum nucleation formalism developed by Iida and Sato (1998) and the Lifshitz-Kagan theory, and it connects to the two-families scenario proposed by Drago and collaborators (2013) where quark stars and hadronic stars coexist. The results enable future work to distinguish between the all-quark-star scenario and the two-families scenario using gravitational wave observations and pulsar timing measurements.
