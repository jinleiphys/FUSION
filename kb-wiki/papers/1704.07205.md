# $K^-$- nuclear states: Binding energies and widths

> $K^-$ optical potentials relevant to calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states were developed within several chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models. The applied models yield quite different $K^-$ binding energies and widths. Then, the $K^-$ multinucleon interactions were incorporated by a phenomenological optical potential fitted recently to kaonic atom data. Though the applied $K^-$ interaction models differ significantly in the $K^-N$ subthreshold region, our self-consistent calculations of kaonic nuclei across the periodic table lead to conclusions valid quite generally. Due to $K^-$ multinucleon absorption in the nuclear medium the calculated widths of $K^-$ nuclear states are sizable, $Γ_{K^-} \geq 90$ MeV, and exceed substantially their binding energies in all considered nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, despite significant differences among chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models in the subthreshold region, self-consistent calculations of kaonic nuclei across the periodic table lead to a general conclusion: when \(K^-\) multinucleon absorption is included, the calculated widths of \(K^-\) nuclear states are sizable (\(\Gamma_{K^-} \geq 90\) MeV) and exceed their binding energies in all considered nuclei. The hedge is that this conclusion holds only when the phenomenological multinucleon potential fitted to kaonic atom data is added to the single-nucleon chiral amplitudes.

## Method

The authors constructed \(K^-\) optical potentials from six chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models (Prague, Kyoto-Munich, Murcia M1 and M2, Bonn B2 and B4) by deriving in-medium \(K^-N\) amplitudes using the multiple scattering WRW approach. They solved the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently for the kaon-nucleus system, incorporating the energy- and density-dependent kaon self-energy operator. Then they added a phenomenological optical potential representing \(K^-\) multinucleon interactions (\(K^-NN \rightarrow YN\)), with parameters fitted to kaonic atom data for each model separately by Friedman and Gal (2016), and applied kinematical suppression factors for the non-pionic conversion channel.

## Key numbers

* The \(K^-\) widths exceed 90 MeV in all considered nuclei when multinucleon absorption is included, and they substantially exceed the corresponding binding energies.
* The real part of the \(K^-\)-nucleus potential in \(^{40}\)Ca ranges from 30 MeV deep (M2 model) to 110 MeV deep (P model) when only single-nucleon interactions are considered.
* The subthreshold energy shift \(\delta \sqrt{s}\) in \(^{16}\)O reaches between approximately \(-40\) MeV (M2 model) and \(-100\) MeV (P model) at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) for single-nucleon potentials.
* In \(^{208}\)Pb with multinucleon interactions included, the downward energy shift ranges from \(-35\) to \(-230\) MeV at nuclear center across all models.
* The phenomenological multinucleon potential parameters for the P1 model are: \(\alpha = 1\), Re \(B = -1.3 \pm 0.2\) fm, Im \(B = 1.5 \pm 0.2\) fm; for KM1: \(\alpha = 1\), Re \(B = -0.9 \pm 0.2\) fm, Im \(B = 1.4 \pm 0.2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent calculations of \(K^-\)-nuclear quasi-bound states previously performed with the Prague model (Hrtankova and Mares, 2015; Gazda and Mares, 2012) by applying all six chiral interaction models considered in the kaonic atom analysis of Friedman and Gal (2016). It contradicts earlier expectations that some chiral models might yield narrow \(K^-\)-nuclear states, showing instead that including multinucleon absorption (as required by kaonic atom data) produces widths that always exceed binding energies. The results enable future experimental searches for kaonic nuclei to focus on states with widths of order 100 MeV, and they identify the Prague and Kyoto-Munich models as the only ones consistent with both kaonic atom data and bubble chamber absorption fractions.
