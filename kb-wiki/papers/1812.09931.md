# Possible bound states with hidden bottom from $\bar{K}^{(*)}B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ systems

> We study the three-body systems of $\bar{K}^{(*)}B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ by solving the Faddeev equations in the fixed-center approximation, where the light particle $\bar{K}^{(*)}$ interacts with the heavy bound states of $B\bar{B}$ ($B^*\bar{B}^*$) forming the clusters. In terms of the very attractive $\bar{K}^*B$ and $\bar{K}^*B^*$ subsystems, which are constrained by the observed $B_{s1}(5830)$ and $B_{s2}^*(5840)$ states in experiment, we find two deep bound states, containing the hidden-bottom components, with masses $11002\pm 63$ MeV and $11078\pm 57$ MeV in the $\bar{K}^*B\bar{B}$ and $\bar{K}^*B^*\bar{B}^*$ systems, respectively. The two corresponding states with higher masses of the above systems are also predicted. In addition, using the constrained two-body amplitudes of $\bar{K}B^{(*)}$ and $\bar{K}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ via the hidden gauge symmetry in the heavy-quark sector, we also find two three-body $\bar{K}B\bar{B}$ and $\bar{K}B^{*}\bar{B}^*$ bound states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that deeply bound three-body states with hidden-bottom components exist in the \(\bar{K}^*B\bar{B}\) and \(\bar{K}^*B^*\bar{B}^*\) systems, with masses \(11002\pm 63\) MeV and \(11078\pm 57\) MeV respectively, but these results depend on the choice of momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_R\) (varied between 415 and 830 MeV) and on the method used to distribute the total three-body energy among the two-body subsystems, leading to variations of about 100 MeV in the predicted masses.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in the fixed-center approximation (FCA), treating the \(B\bar{B}\) or \(B^*\bar{B}^*\) system as a heavy cluster and the \(\bar{K}^{(*)}\) meson as a light particle scattering off it. The two-body scattering amplitudes for the \(\bar{K}^{(*)}B^{(*)}\) and \(\bar{K}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}\) subsystems were computed using a chiral unitary approach with local hidden gauge symmetry in the SU(4) sector, with the \(\bar{K}^*B\) and \(\bar{K}^*B^*\) interactions constrained by the experimentally observed \(B_{s1}(5830)\) and \(B_{s2}^*(5840)\) states. The cluster form factor was regularized with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_R\) taken from prior work on \(B\bar{B}\) bound states, and two different kinematic schemes (method A and method B) were used to relate the three-body total energy to the two-body subsystem energies.

## Key numbers

* Deep bound state in \(\bar{K}^*B\bar{B}\) system: mass \(11002\pm 63\) MeV, binding energy \(360\pm 34\) MeV (isospin \(I=1/2\), \(J^P=1^-\))
* Deep bound state in \(\bar{K}^*B^*\bar{B}^*\) system: mass \(11078\pm 57\) MeV, binding energy \(374\pm 38\) MeV (isospin \(I=1/2\), \(J^P=3^-\))
* Heavier bound state in \(\bar{K}^*B\bar{B}\) system: mass \(11264\pm 65\) MeV, binding energy \(99\pm 28\) MeV
* Heavier bound state in \(\bar{K}^*B^*\bar{B}^*\) system: mass \(11339\pm 60\) MeV, binding energy \(114\pm 31\) MeV
* Bound state in \(\bar{K}B\bar{B}\) system: mass \(10659\pm 69\) MeV, binding energy \(305\pm 32\) MeV
* Bound state in \(\bar{K}B^*\bar{B}^*\) system: mass \(10914\pm 62\) MeV, binding energy \(140\pm 32\) MeV
* The \(\bar{K}^*B\) subsystem binding energies producing \(B_{s1}(5830)\) and \(B_{s2}^*(5840)\) are 344 MeV and 350 MeV respectively (from Sun (2018))

## Context

This work extends the fixed-center approximation approach previously applied to the charm sector in Ren (2018) to the bottom sector, specifically studying \(\bar{K}^{(*)}B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}\) systems. It builds upon the two-body bound state analysis of \(B\bar{B}\) and \(B^*\bar{B}^*\) clusters by Ozpineci (2013) and the \(\bar{K}^*B\) and \(\bar{K}^*B^*\) interactions constrained by the observed \(B_{s1}(5830)\) and \(B_{s2}^*(5840)\) states. The predicted superbound states are similar in character to those found in the \(\rho B^*\bar{B}^*\) system by Bayar (2015), and the results enable future experimental searches for hidden-bottom three-body molecular states in the mass region around 11 GeV.
