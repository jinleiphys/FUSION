# NNLO Corrections to Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering and Perturbative Pions

> The 1S0, 3S1, and 3D1 nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts are calculated at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in an effective field theory. Predictions for the 1P1, 3P0, 3P1, 3P2, 1D2, 3D2, and 3D3 phase shifts at this order are also compared with data. The calculations treat pions perturbatively and include the NNLO contributions from order $Q_r^3$ and $Q_r^4$ radiation pion graphs. In the 3S1, 3D1, 3P0, and 3P2 channels we find large disagreement with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis at NNLO. These spin triplet channels have large corrections from graphs with two potential pion exchange which do not vanish in the chiral limit. We compare our results to calculations within the Weinberg approach, and find that in some spin triplet channels the summation of potential pion diagrams seems to be necessary to reproduce the observed phase shifts. In the spin singlet channels the nonperturbative treatment of potential pions does not afford a significant improvement over the perturbative approach.

## Key claim

The KSW perturbative-pion expansion works well in the spin-singlet channel (where NNLO agrees with data to <1% at p = mπ) but breaks down at NNLO in several spin-triplet channels (³S₁, ³D₁, ³P₀, ³P₂) due to large non-analytic corrections from two-potential-pion exchange graphs that do not vanish in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, order Q in the amplitude) in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory, treating pions perturbatively. They include all order Q³ and Q⁴ radiation pion graphs, potential pion exchange diagrams, and contact interactions, using dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS) and solving renormalization group equations to ensure scale independence. The six independent linear combinations of coupling constants are fixed by imposing "good fit" conditions (cancelling spurious double and triple poles) and weighted least-squares fits to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- In the ¹S₀ channel at NNLO, the error at p = mπ (137 MeV) is less than 1%; the LO error at p = mπ is 48%, and the NLO error is 17%.
- The expansion parameter estimate is mπ/ΛNN ≈ 0.5, where ΛNN = 8πf²/(g_A² M) with g_A = 1.25, f = 131 MeV, M = 939 MeV.
- For the ¹P₁ channel at p = mπ, the NNLO error is 13%; for ¹D₂, the error is 33%.
- For the ³P₁ channel at p = mπ, the NNLO error is 15%; for ³D₂, the error is 8%.
- For the ³P₀ channel at p = mπ, the NNLO error is 170%; for ³P₂, the error is 52%.
- The fitted parameters for the ¹S₀ channel at NNLO (fit range p = 7-200 MeV) are ζ₁ = 0.0777, ζ₂ = 0.0313, ζ₃ = 0.1831, ζ₄ = 0.245; at NLO (fit range p = 7-80 MeV) ζ₁ = 0.216, ζ₂ = 0.0318.
- The pole location in the ¹S₀ channel is γ = -7.88 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the KSW perturbative-pion program (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998a, 1998b) from NLO to NNLO for multiple partial waves, including the full radiation pion contributions missing from earlier NNLO calculations of the ¹S₀ phase shift (Rupak 1999; Mehen and Stewart 1999). The results contradict the expectation that the KSW expansion converges uniformly: in spin-triplet channels the perturbative treatment of pions fails, while in spin-singlet channels it works well. The authors compare with the Weinberg approach (Weinberg 1990; Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck 1994; van Kolck 1994) and find that in some triplet channels (³S₁, ³P₁, ³D₁) summing potential pions nonperturbatively gives significant improvement, while in others (³P₀, ³P₂) the Weinberg LO does no better than the KSW LO. This work enables future studies of whether resummation of potential pion exchange can be incorporated into a consistent power counting for triplet channels.
