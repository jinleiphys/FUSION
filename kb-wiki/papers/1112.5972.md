# Power counting and renormalization group invariance in the subtracted kernel method for the two-nucleon system

> We apply the subtracted kernel method (SKM), a renormalization approach based on recursive multiple subtractions performed in the kernel of the scattering equation, to the chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO). We evaluate the phase-shifts in the 1S0 channel at each order in Weinberg's power counting scheme and in a modified power counting scheme which yields a systematic power-law improvement. We also explicitly demonstrate that the SKM procedure is renormalization group invariant under the change of the subtraction scale through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik flow equation for the evolution of the renormalized NN interactions.

## Key claim

The subtracted kernel method (SKM) provides a renormalization group invariant regularization of chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions, but only when the subtraction scale is kept below approximately 1.1 fm⁻¹; above this scale, the Wigner causality bound prevents fitting the low-energy constants while maintaining a hermitian potential and unitary scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors apply the subtracted kernel method to chiral NN interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) in the ¹S₀ channel, replacing the standard momentum cutoff regularization with recursive multiple subtractions performed in the kernel of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation at a negative energy subtraction scale -μ². They construct n-fold subtracted LS equations for the K-matrix, with n=1 subtraction at LO, n=3 at NLO, and n=4 at NNLO, and fix the renormalized strengths of contact interactions by fitting to Nijmegen-II phase-shifts at very low momenta (k ≤ 0.1 fm⁻¹). They evaluate phase-shifts in both Weinberg's power counting scheme and a modified scheme where the C'₄ p² p'² contact interaction is promoted from N³LO to NNLO.

## Key numbers

- Subtraction scale μ = 1.03 fm⁻¹ used for the main phase-shift calculations at LO, NLO, and NNLO
- At LO, relative errors in phase-shifts scale like O(k²/μ²) at low energies
- At NLO, relative errors scale like O(k⁴/μ⁴) at low energies
- At NNLO in the modified power counting scheme, relative errors scale like O(k⁶/μ⁶) at low energies
- For μ = 1.03 fm⁻¹, SKM results agree with Nijmegen-II phase-shifts up to laboratory energy E_LAB ∼ 60 MeV
- Maximum subtraction scale μ_max ∼ 1.1 fm⁻¹ above which the fitting procedure breaks down due to the Wigner causality bound
- Experimental values for ¹S₀ channel: scattering length a = -23.7 fm, effective range r_e = 2.77 fm
- Minimum range from Wigner bound for ¹S₀ channel with OPE interaction included: R_min = 1.1 fm

## Context

This work extends the subtracted kernel method (previously developed by the authors and collaborators in references cited as npa99, plb00, hep01, plb05, npa07, ijmpe07, prc11, fb19, efb21, aop2010) to chiral NN interactions, addressing the ongoing controversy about non-perturbative renormalization in Weinberg's approach to chiral effective field theory. The modified power counting scheme resembles that of Valderrama (valderrama6, valderrama7, valderrama8), who promoted contact interactions within a perturbative treatment of subleading chiral two-pion-exchange interactions, but here the promotion is implemented within a fully non-perturbative framework. The results support Lepage's prescription that the cutoff should not be taken much larger than the relevant hard scale (the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV), and the demonstration of renormalization group invariance through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik flow equation provides a formal consistency check for the SKM approach that is absent in standard cutoff regularization schemes.
