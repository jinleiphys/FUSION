# Exotic baryons from a heavy meson and a nucleon

> We evaluate a hadronic molecule formed by a heavy meson and a nucleon respecting heavy quark symmetry. The tensor force of pion exchange potential plays a dominate role to produce an strong attraction in this system. Solving coupled channel Schrödinger equations for PN and P* N, we find many bound and resonant states with isospin I=0 while there are few resonances in I=1 state. The rich structures with I=0 indicate that the spectrum of heavy baryons near the threshold is influenced by the contributions from such hadron composite structures.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many bound and resonant heavy baryon states exist as hadronic molecules formed by a heavy meson and a nucleon, but this richness is strongly conditional on isospin: the authors find many such states for isospin \(I=0\) while there are few resonances in the \(I=1\) channel.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Schrodinger equations for the \(PN\) and \(P^\ast N\) systems (where \(P\) is a heavy pseudoscalar meson and \(P^\ast\) is a heavy vector meson), including pion exchange and vector meson (\(\rho,\omega\)) exchange potentials. They employed heavy quark symmetry to construct the interaction Lagrangians and introduced form factors with cutoff parameters determined from the Bonn model and quark-model size ratios. Bound and resonant states were identified for both exotic (\(\bar{D}N\), \(BN\)) and non-exotic (\(DN\), \(\bar{B}N\)) configurations across multiple total angular momenta \(J^P = 1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm, 7/2^\pm\).

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of \(\bar{D}N\) bound state with \((I,J^P)=(0,1/2^-)\): 1.60 MeV (pion-only potential) and 2.13 MeV (pion+rho+omega potential).
* Binding energy of \(BN\) bound state with \((I,J^P)=(0,1/2^-)\): 19.50 MeV (pion-only potential) and 23.04 MeV (pion+rho+omega potential).
* Root-mean-square radius of \(\bar{D}N\) bound state: 3.5 fm (pion-only) and 3.2 fm (pion+rho+omega).
* Root-mean-square radius of \(BN\) bound state: 1.3 fm (pion-only) and 1.2 fm (pion+rho+omega).
* Mass splitting between \(B\) and \(B^\ast\) is about 45 MeV; mass splitting between \(D\) and \(D^\ast\) is about 140 MeV.
* Pion decay constant used: \(f_\pi = 132\) MeV.
* Cutoff parameters: for pion potential, \(\Lambda_N = 830\) MeV, \(\Lambda_D = 1121\) MeV, \(\Lambda_B = 1070\) MeV; for combined pion+rho+omega potential, \(\Lambda_N = 846\) MeV, \(\Lambda_D = 1142\) MeV, \(\Lambda_B = 1091\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular picture previously applied to meson-meson systems such as the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) resonances observed by Belle (2011), which are considered \(B\bar{B}^\ast\) and \(B^\ast\bar{B}^\ast\) molecules. The authors apply similar heavy-quark-symmetry-based pion exchange dynamics to the heavy meson-nucleon system, building on earlier studies by Tornqvist (2004), Cohen (2005), and their own prior work (Yasui 2009, Yamaguchi 2011). The results enable future experimental searches for exotic heavy baryons near threshold and motivate further theoretical work coupling these molecular states to ordinary charmed baryons such as \(\Lambda_c\) and \(\Sigma_c\).
