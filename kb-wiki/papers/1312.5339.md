# Hidden charm molecules in a Finite Volume

> In the present paper we address the interaction of charmed mesons in hidden charm channels in a finite box. We use the interaction from a recent model based on heavy quark spin symmetry that predicts molecules of hidden charm in the infinite volume. The energy levels in the box are generated within this model, and several methods for the analysis of these levels ("inverse problem") are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that analyzing finite-volume energy levels from lattice QCD simulations using a potential parameterization or effective range expansion (fitting both subthreshold and above-threshold levels simultaneously) yields more accurate bound-state masses with smaller uncertainties than the standard Lüscher method applied only to above-threshold levels.

## Method

The authors take a leading-order contact-interaction model for charmed meson-antimeson scattering, derived from heavy-quark spin symmetry and light-flavor SU(3) symmetry, and place it in a finite cubic box of size L with periodic boundary conditions. They compute the discrete energy levels from the poles of the finite-volume T-matrix, generate synthetic data points with errors mimicking actual lattice QCD results, and then apply three inverse-analysis methods: (1) standard Lüscher formula to extract phase shifts from above-threshold levels only, (2) fitting the potential parameters directly to the energy levels, and (3) parameterizing the infinite-volume amplitude with an effective range expansion and fitting both subthreshold and above-threshold levels simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- For the I=0 J^PC=0^{++} D\bar{D} channel, the infinite-volume model predicts a bound state with mass E_B = 3715 MeV.
- Method 1 (Lüscher formula from above-threshold levels) yields E_B = 3721^{+10}_{-25} MeV.
- Method 2 (potential parameterization fit) yields E_B = 3715^{+3}_{-6} MeV.
- Method 3 (effective range expansion fit to both levels) yields E_B = 3716^{+4}_{-5} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1985) and its generalization by Doring et al. (2011) to the hidden-charm molecular states predicted by the heavy-quark spin symmetry model of Hidalgo-Duque et al. (2012) and Nieves et al. (2012). The results provide concrete predictions for energy levels that can be compared with actual lattice QCD simulations such as those of Prelovsek et al. (2013), and the inverse-analysis methods developed here enable more reliable extraction of bound-state properties from future lattice data.
