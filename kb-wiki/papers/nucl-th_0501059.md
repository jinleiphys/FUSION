# Chiral dynamics of hadrons in nuclei

> In this talk I report on selected topics of hadron modification in the nuclear medium using the chiral unitary approach to describe the dynamics of the problems. I shall mention how antikaons, $η$, and $ϕ$ are modified in the medium and will report upon different experiments done or planned to measure the $ϕ$ width in the medium.

# Chiral dynamics of hadrons in nuclei

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach, combined with coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations and self-consistent treatment of in-medium modifications, can describe the modification of antikaons, eta mesons, and phi mesons in nuclear matter; however, the resulting spectral functions and widths depend critically on including many-body decay channels (pion selfenergies, baryon mean-field potentials, and vertex corrections) which produce much broader in-medium widths than earlier simplified treatments.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations with kernels taken from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for meson-baryon scattering, using dimensional regularization or a cutoff (630 MeV) to regulate loop functions. For in-medium calculations, they evaluate the antikaon selfenergy by integrating the self-consistently modified effective T-matrix over the Fermi sea of nucleons, including both s-wave contributions and p-wave contributions from hyperon-nucleon hole (YN^{-1}) excitations (Lambda, Sigma, Sigma*(1385)). The phi meson selfenergy is computed from its decay into K and anti-K, using the updated in-medium kaon selfenergies, and the eta selfenergy is obtained from the same chiral unitary framework incorporating the N*(1535) resonance and its medium modifications.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum of 630 MeV used in all channels for the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the strangeness S = -1 sector
- Eta-nucleus optical potential at threshold: (-54 - i29) MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- Eta binding energies and half-widths for 1s states: C-12 (B = -9.71 MeV, -Gamma/2 = -17.5 MeV); Pb-208 (B = -21.25 MeV, -Gamma/2 = -15.88 MeV)
- Total phi meson width at normal nuclear matter density (rho = rho_0): approximately 22 MeV from this work, about 27 MeV from a related evaluation (Dani, 2004)
- Predicted deeply bound K- states are never bound by more than 50 MeV, with widths of order 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-meson scattering (Oller, 1999) and meson-baryon scattering (Kaiser, 1995; Oset, 2001) to the nuclear medium. It builds on earlier studies of antikaon selfenergies that considered Pauli blocking and resonance shifts (Koch, 1994; Waas, 1997) and introduced self-consistency (Lutz, 1998), but adds new decay channels (pion selfenergy, baryon mean fields) that produce broader spectral functions. The phi width calculation updates earlier work (Klingl, 1997; Norbert, 1999) with improved kaon selfenergies and vertex corrections. The results enable experimental tests via phi photoproduction at Spring8/Osaka (Ishikawa, 2004) and proposed pA reactions at COSY (Magas, 2004), where ratios of cross sections normalized to C-12 could distinguish between different in-medium widths.
