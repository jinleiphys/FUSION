# A perturbative description of the deconfinement transition in Yang-Mills theories

> We investigate the deconfinement transition of static quarks in SU(N) Yang-Mills theories using a perturbative approach based on a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge-fixed action, where the gluon mass term is related to the issue of Gribov ambiguities. A leading-order, one-loop calculation of the effective potential for the Polyakov loop produces a deconfinement transition of second order for the SU(2) theory and of first order for SU(3) with transition temperatures in qualitative agreement with known values. We also report on the results of a two-loop calculation of the critical temperature and of thermodynamical quantities in the SU(2) case.

# A perturbative description of the deconfinement transition in Yang-Mills theories

## Key claim

The deconfinement transition in SU(N) Yang-Mills theories can be described perturbatively using a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge, where the gluon mass term is related to Gribov ambiguities; the one-loop calculation already captures the correct order of the transition (second order for SU(2), first order for SU(3)), but the resulting transition temperatures are only in qualitative agreement with lattice values, and the approach still leaves open conceptual issues regarding unphysical contributions to thermodynamic quantities at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors quantize SU(N) Yang-Mills theory using the background field method with a temporal background field in the Cartan subalgebra, employing the Curci-Ferrari model which augments the Faddeev-Popov action with a bare gluon mass term. They compute the effective potential for the Polyakov loop at one-loop order for SU(2) and SU(3), and at two-loop order for SU(2), evaluating Matsubara sums analytically and performing remaining momentum integrals numerically. The gluon mass parameter is fixed by fitting lattice data for the vacuum gluon propagator in Landau gauge against tree-level expressions.

## Key numbers

- SU(2) one-loop critical temperature: T_c/m ≈ 0.33, giving T_c ≈ 238 MeV when using m = 710 MeV from lattice fits
- SU(2) two-loop critical temperature: T_c ≈ 284 MeV, obtained with m = 680 MeV and g = 7.5 at scale μ = 1 GeV
- SU(3) one-loop critical temperature: T_c/m ≈ 0.36, giving T_c ≈ 185 MeV when using m = 510 MeV from lattice fits
- Lattice reference values: T_c^{latt} = 295 MeV for SU(2) and T_c^{latt} = 270 MeV for SU(3) (Lucini 2012)
- FRG/DSE reference values: T_c^{FRG} = 230 MeV (or 300 MeV with backreaction) for SU(2) and T_c^{FRG} = 275 MeV for SU(3) (Fister 2013)
- Two-loop correction to SU(2) critical temperature: approximately 20% increase from the one-loop value

## Context

This work extends the massive perturbative approach developed by Tissier and Wschebor (2010) for vacuum propagators, which was shown to be quantitatively consistent with lattice data for ghost and gluon propagators, and which Serreau (2012) connected to a novel quantization procedure averaging over Gribov copies. The approach contrasts with nonperturbative continuum methods such as functional renormalization group (FRG) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) used by Fischer (2009) and Fister (2013), and with phenomenological models by Pisarski (2000) and Dumitru (2012). The one-loop results reproduce the correct order of the transition (second order for SU(2), first order for SU(3)) that lattice calculations have established, but the transition temperatures are lower than lattice values. The two-loop calculation for SU(2) resolves unphysical artifacts of the one-loop result, such as a spurious singularity in thermodynamic quantities and negative pressure near the critical temperature. Future work includes extending the two-loop calculation to SU(3), applying the approach at finite chemical potential, and computing finite-temperature ghost and gluon propagators in the nontrivial background field.
