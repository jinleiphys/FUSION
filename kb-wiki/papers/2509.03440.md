# Wigner function of a rigidly rotating and magnetized QED plasma

> We determine the Wigner function of a rigidly rotating quantum electrodynamics (QED) plasma in the presence of a constant magnetic field by utilizing the Riemannian normal coordinate approximation, which has been previously proposed in the literature. In this approach, the angular velocity appears only in a specific phase factor, allowing us to compute the point-split fermion two-point correlation function in flat spacetime. To ensure that the fermion correlation function is gauge invariant, we introduce a background gauge field that is fixed to produce a constant magnetic field. Using this Wigner function, we derive the energy-momentum tensor for this medium, which consists of both diagonal and off-diagonal components. By comparing our results with the energy-momentum tensor of an ideal spinful and vortical magnetized fluid, we establish a connection between these components and thermodynamic quantities, such as energy density and different types of pressure. We demonstrate that rigid rotation leads to pressure anisotropy in plasma. Additionally, we compute the associated vector and axial vector currents for this medium, utilizing the previously presented Wigner function. Our results are consistent with existing literature on the subject.

## Key claim

The Wigner function for a rigidly rotating QED plasma in a constant magnetic field can be computed using the Riemannian normal coordinate approximation, which confines the angular velocity to a phase factor and allows the calculation to proceed in flat spacetime; the resulting energy-momentum tensor shows that rigid rotation induces pressure anisotropy in the direction perpendicular to the rotation axis, beyond the anisotropy already caused by the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors start from the general definition of the Wigner function in curved spacetime and apply the Riemannian normal coordinate approximation of Fonarev (1993) to the metric describing rigid rotation about the z-axis with angular velocity Ω. They introduce a background gauge field in symmetric gauge to produce a constant magnetic field B along the z-direction, then quantize the fermion fields using the Ritus eigenfunction formalism (1972) and compute the point-split two-point correlation function. The resulting Wigner function is used to derive the energy-momentum tensor and the vector and axial vector currents by taking traces and integrating over momentum.

- Magnetic length: ℓ_b = 1/√(eB)
- Fermi-Dirac distribution: N_f(E_n) = (1 + e^(βE_n))^(-1)
- Energy density component: T^(tt) = (eB/π^2) Σ_n α_n ∫_0^∞ dk_z [N_f(E_n)/E_n] (E_n^2 + m^2)
- Transverse pressure components: T^(xx) = T^(yy) = (eB/π^2) Σ_n α_n ∫_0^∞ dk_z [N_f(E_n)/E_n] (m^2 + neB)
- Longitudinal pressure component: T^(zz) = (eB/π^2) Σ_n α_n ∫_0^∞ dk_z [N_f(E_n)/E_n] (k_z^2 + m^2)

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function formalism for magnetized plasmas developed by Tabatabaee et al. (2020) to include rigid rotation, using the curved-spacetime approach of Fonarev (1993) and the Ritus eigenfunction method previously applied by Fayazbakhsh et al. (2011-2014) and Taghinavaz et al. (2012, 2016). The results are compared with the ideal spinful vortical magnetohydrodynamics (SVMHD) formalism recently introduced by Sedighi et al. (2024), showing consistency for an observer in the corotating frame. The derived energy-momentum tensor, with its off-diagonal components proportional to Ω, enables future studies of how rotation modifies the hydrodynamic evolution of magnetized plasmas, particularly the pressure anisotropy that resembles viscous effects studied by Florkowski (2011) and Strickland (2018).
