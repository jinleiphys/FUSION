# Two lectures on color superconductivity

> The first lecture provides an introduction to the physics of color superconductivity in cold dense quark matter. The main color superconducting phases are briefly described and their properties are listed. The second lecture covers recent developments in studies of color superconducting phases in neutral and beta-equilibrated matter. The properties of gapless color superconducting phases are discussed.

## Key claim

Color superconductivity in cold dense quark matter is a theoretically robust phenomenon driven by the Cooper instability from attractive quark-quark interactions, but the quantitative properties of the resulting phases depend sensitively on whether the matter is neutral and beta-equilibrated, and on the density regime (asymptotic versus compact-star densities).

## Method

The first lecture reviews the basic mechanism of color superconductivity via the Cooper instability, deriving the gap equation in both the QCD one-gluon exchange approximation (using the hard dense loop approximation for the gluon propagator) and in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model with a local four-fermion interaction. The second lecture examines color superconducting phases under the constraints of charge neutrality and beta equilibrium, focusing on the properties of gapless color superconducting phases.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass: \(m_{\rm p} \approx 938.3\,\text{MeV}/c^2\); neutron mass: \(m_{\rm n} \approx 939.6\,\text{MeV}/c^2\); electron mass: \(m_{\rm e} \approx 0.511\,\text{MeV}/c^2\)
- Typical nuclear density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.15\,\text{fm}^{-3}\); typical density inside nuclei: \(10^{14}\,\text{g}/\text{cm}^{3}\)
- Color superconducting gap could be as large as \(100\,\text{MeV}\) at baryon densities a few times larger than normal nuclear density
- In the NJL model, the scalar coupling constant \(G_S = 5.01\,\text{GeV}^{-2}\) and the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.65\,\text{GeV}\) are adjusted to give \(F_\pi = 93\,\text{MeV}\) and \(\langle\bar{u}u\rangle = \langle\bar{d}d\rangle = (-250\,\text{MeV})^3\)
- The Debye mass squared in the hard dense loop approximation: \(m_D^2 \equiv 2N_f \alpha_s \mu^2 / \pi\)
- The gap solution at asymptotic densities: \(\Delta(0) \simeq \Lambda \exp\left(-\frac{3\pi^{3/2}}{2^{3/2}\sqrt{\alpha_s}}\right)\) with \(\Lambda = 2 (4\pi)^{3/2} \mu \alpha_s^{-5/2}\)

## Context

This paper provides a pedagogical review of color superconductivity, extending the earlier work of Barrois (1977) and Bailin and Love (1984) who first suggested that dense quark matter should be a color superconductor, and triggered by the observation in Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) that the gap could be as large as 100 MeV. The lectures enable future work on the QCD phase diagram and on phenomenological signatures of color superconducting phases in compact stars, such as modified cooling rates, rotational slowing down, mass-radius relations, and the possible existence of a new family of compact stars.
