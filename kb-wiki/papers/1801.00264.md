# Goldstonic pseudoscalar mesons in Bethe-Salpeter-inspired setting

> For a two-particle bound-state equation closer to its Bethe-Salpeter origins than Salpeter's equation, with effective-interaction kernel deliberately forged such as to ensure, in the limit of zero mass of the bound-state constituents, the vanishing of the arising bound-state mass, we scrutinize the emerging features of the lightest pseudoscalar mesons for their agreement with the behaviour predicted by a generalization of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using an effective interaction kernel derived from Dyson-Schwinger quark propagator solutions, yields pseudoscalar meson properties that satisfy a generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation, but only within the limitations of their instantaneous approximation and with the caveat that the bound-state mass vanishes in the chiral limit by deliberate construction of the interaction kernel.

## Method

The authors employ an instantaneous-limit reduction of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for fermion-antifermion bound states, incorporating momentum-dependent quark propagator functions (mass and wave-function renormalization) from Dyson-Schwinger solutions. They invert the chiral-limit bound-state equation to extract a configuration-space central potential V(r) that ensures Goldstone behavior, then solve the resulting eigenvalue problem for pseudoscalar mesons with finite quark masses corresponding to u/d and s quarks, computing bound-state masses, decay constants, and in-hadron condensates.

## Key numbers

- Bound-state mass for chiral quarks: 6.8 MeV, vanishing in the chiral limit as required for Goldstone bosons
- Bound-state mass for u/d quarks: 148.6 MeV; for s quarks: 620.7 MeV
- Decay constant for chiral quarks: 151 MeV; for u/d quarks: 155 MeV; for s quarks: 211 MeV
- In-hadron condensate for chiral quarks: 0.585 GeV²; for u/d quarks: 0.598 GeV²; for s quarks: 0.799 GeV²
- Quark mass extracted from Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for u/d: 2.85 MeV; for s: 51.0 MeV, compared to PDG averages of (3.5+0.7-0.3) MeV for (m_u+m_d)/2 and (96+8-4) MeV for m_s at renormalization scale 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inversion technique (Lucha and Schoberl, 2013-2016) for extracting effective interquark potentials from Dyson-Schwinger propagator solutions, specifically applying it to test the generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (Maris, Roberts, and Tandy, 1998) in an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter framework. The approach enables future studies of how well three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism can reproduce chiral symmetry breaking patterns, though the authors note their three data points cannot determine the functional form of the mass-squared rise with quark mass.
