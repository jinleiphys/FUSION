# Heavy-quarkonium suppression in p-A collisions from parton energy loss in cold QCD matter

> The effects of parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter on heavy-quarkonium suppression in p-A collisions are studied. It is shown from first principles that at large quarkonium energy E and small production angle in the nucleus rest frame, the medium-induced energy loss scales as E. Using this result, a phenomenological model depending on a single free parameter is able to reproduce J/psi and Upsilon suppression data in a broad xF-range and at various center-of-mass energies. These results strongly support energy loss as the dominant effect in heavy-quarkonium suppression in p-A collisions. Predictions for J/psi and Upsilon suppression in p-Pb collisions at the LHC are made. It is argued that parton energy loss scaling as E should generally apply to hadron production in p-A collisions, such as light hadron or open charm production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter, which scales linearly with quarkonium energy \(E\) at large \(E\) and small production angle in the nucleus rest frame, is the dominant mechanism for heavy-quarkonium suppression in p–A collisions, but this scaling is derived under the specific condition that the heavy-quark pair remains a compact color octet for a time much longer than the perturbative production time.

## Method

The authors derive the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum for a fast color octet charge undergoing small-angle scattering in cold nuclear matter, obtaining a spectrum that depends on the nuclear transverse momentum broadening \(\ell_{\perp A}^2\) and the transverse mass \(M_\perp\) of the \(Q\bar{Q}\) pair. They then construct a phenomenological model with a single free parameter (the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) in cold matter) to compute the nuclear attenuation factor \(R_{\mathrm{pA}}^\psi(x_F, \sqrt{s})\) for J/\(\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) production, using the energy loss probability distribution to relate the induced energy loss to the suppression of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- The medium-induced average energy loss is \(\Delta E \simeq N_c \, \alpha_s \, (\sqrt{\ell_{\perp A}^2} - \Lambda_{\mathrm{p}})/M_\perp \, E\), where \(\ell_{\perp A}^2\) is the nuclear broadening, \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{p}}^2 = \max(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2, \ell_{\perp \mathrm{p}}^2)\), and \(M_\perp = (M^2 + p_\perp^2)^{1/2}\).
- The transport coefficient in cold nuclear matter is taken as \(\hat{q} = 0.08 \, \mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\) (derived from fits to J/\(\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) suppression data).
- For a large nucleus with \(L = 7 \, \mathrm{fm}\), the nuclear broadening is \(\sqrt{\hat{q} L} \simeq 0.7 \, \mathrm{GeV}\), comparable to the non-perturbative scale \(\tau_{\psi}^{-1} \simeq 0.6 \, \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the scaling properties of induced gluon radiation identified in Arleo (2010) to heavy-quarkonium hadroproduction, and contrasts with earlier approaches such as Gavin (1991) which postulated \(\Delta E \propto E\) without parametric justification, and Brodsky and Hoyer (1992) which derived an energy-independent bound on \(\Delta E\) in an abelian model. The results enable predictions for J/\(\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) suppression in p–Pb collisions at the LHC, and the authors argue that the same energy-loss mechanism should apply to light hadron and open charm production in p–A collisions.
