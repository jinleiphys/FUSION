# J/Psi dissociation in parity-odd bubbles

> We calculate the quarkonium dissociation rate in the P and CP-odd domains (bubbles) that were possibly created in heavy-ion collisions. In the presence of the magnetic field produced by the valence quarks of colliding ions, parity-odd domains generate electric field. Quarkonium dissociation is the result of quantum tunneling of quark or antiquark through the potential barrier in this electric field. The strength of the electric field in the quarkonium comoving frame depends on the quarkonium velocity with respect to the background magnetic field. We investigate momentum, electric field strength and azimuthal dependence of the dissociation rate. Azimuthal distribution of quarkonia surviving in the electromagnetic field is strongly anisotropic; the form of anisotropy depends on the relation between the electric and magnetic fields and quarkonium momentum. These features can be used to explore the properties of the electromagnetic field created in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The dissociation rate of J/Psi in parity-odd bubbles is sensitive to the electric field generated by the Chiral Magnetic Effect only when the electric-to-magnetic field ratio ρ₀ = E₀/B₀ is at least 0.1, which corresponds to a Θ-angle about an order of magnitude larger than required for charge separation observations.

## Method

The authors calculate the quarkonium dissociation rate using the WKB approximation for quantum tunneling through the potential barrier in the presence of parallel electric and magnetic fields. The electromagnetic fields in the quarkonium comoving frame are obtained by boosting the laboratory-frame fields, and the dissociation rate is expressed in terms of the dimensionless parameters γ, ε, and ρ. The calculation treats the quarkonium binding potential in the non-relativistic approximation and includes the pre-exponential factor from the imaginary time method of Popov, Karnakov, and Mur (1997, 1998).

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength used in calculations: eB₀ = 15 m_π²
- J/Psi binding energy in vacuum: ε_b^vac = 0.16 GeV
- Dissociation rate w ~ 0.3-0.5 fm⁻¹ corresponds to complete destruction of J/Psi's
- At eB₀ = 15 m_π², all J/Psi's with P_⊥ ≳ 0.5 GeV are destroyed independently of E₀ strength
- For E₀ ≲ 0.1 B₀ (ρ₀ ≲ 0.1), the dissociation probability is exponentially small
- Sphaleron bubble size estimate: R₀ ≃ 0.4 fm; J/Psi size: R_J ≃ 0.1-0.2 fm
- The Θ-parameter required for observable effect: |Θ|/π ≃ 0.1/α

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of Lorentz ionization of quarkonium in pure magnetic fields (Marasinghe and Tuchin, 2011) to include the effect of electric fields generated by parity-odd domains via the Chiral Magnetic Effect proposed by Kharzeev (2007). The paper builds on the theoretical framework for quarkonium dissociation in electromagnetic fields developed by Popov, Karnakov, and Mur (1997, 1998). The results enable experimental investigation of the electromagnetic field properties in heavy-ion collisions through azimuthal anisotropy measurements of J/Psi production, complementing the charge asymmetry fluctuation measurements used to study the Chiral Magnetic Effect.
