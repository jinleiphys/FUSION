# Transverse flow induced by inhomogeneous magnetic fields in the Bjorken expansion

> We investigate the magnetohydrodynamics in the presence of an external magnetic field following the power-law decay in proper time and having spatial inhomogeneity characterized by a Gaussian distribution in one of transverse coordinates under the Bjorken expansion. The leading-order solution is obtained in the weak-field approximation, where both energy density and fluid velocity are modified. It is found that the spatial gradient of the magnetic field results in transverse flow, where the flow direction depends on the decay exponents of the magnetic field. We suggest that such a magnetic-field-induced effect might influence anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The spatial gradient of an inhomogeneous external magnetic field induces transverse flow in a Bjorken-expanding fluid, with the flow direction depending on the decay exponent of the magnetic field: the transverse flow points inward for decay exponents \(n < -1\) and outward for \(n > -1\), while no transverse flow is generated when \(n = -1\). This result is obtained under the weak-field approximation where \(B^2/\epsilon \ll 1\), and the authors suggest such an effect might influence anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic magnetohydrodynamic conservation equations perturbatively for an inviscid, conformal fluid (\(p = \epsilon/3\)) undergoing longitudinal Bjorken expansion, with an external magnetic field pointing along the \(y\) direction that depends on proper time \(\tau\) and one transverse coordinate \(x\). The magnetic field is treated as a small perturbation (\(B^2/\epsilon \ll 1\)), and its spatial dependence is expanded in a Fourier cosine series; the leading-order corrections to the energy density and transverse fluid velocity are then obtained analytically by solving the resulting ordinary differential equations for each Fourier mode.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of magnetic energy density to fluid energy density is \(\sim 0.2\) in a typical Au-Au collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (citing Roy (2015)).
- The initial energy density of the medium is \(\epsilon_0(\tau) = \epsilon_c / \tau^{4/3}\), where \(\tau\) is rescaled by the initial time \(\tau_0\).
- For the Gaussian magnetic field profile \(B_y(\tau,x) = B_c \tau^{n/2} e^{-x^2/2}\), the Fourier coefficients used for the approximation within \(-\pi < x < \pi\) are: \(B_0^2 = 0.28 B_c^2\), \(B_1^2 = 0.44 B_c^2\), \(B_2^2 = 0.21 B_c^2\), \(B_3^2 = 0.06 B_c^2\), \(B_4^2 = 0.01 B_c^2\).
- The perturbative solution is valid for \(1 \leq \tau < \left( \sum_k \epsilon_c B_k^{-2} \right)^{1/(n+4/3)}\) when \(n > -4/3\); for \(n \leq -4/3\) the expansion is legitimate for arbitrary late times \(\tau \geq 1\).
- For the special case \(n = -1\), the transverse velocity vanishes (\(u_x = 0\)) and the energy density correction is \(\epsilon_1(\tau,x) = -3 B_y^2(\tau,x)/2\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic study of magnetohydrodynamics in the Bjorken expansion from the homogeneous magnetic field case considered by Roy (2015) to an inhomogeneous field with spatial dependence in the transverse plane. The authors build on the classic Bjorken (1982) solution and the perturbative approach of Pu (2016) for homogeneous magnetic fields. The results enable future numerical simulations of 3+1 dimensional MHD to incorporate magnetic-field-induced transverse flow effects, and provide analytic benchmarks for understanding how spatial gradients of the magnetic field can generate anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions.
