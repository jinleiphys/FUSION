# Semi-central In-In collisions and Brown-Rho scaling

> In connection with the claim made at the Quark Matter 2005 Conference that the Brown-Rho scaling is ruled out by NA60 data we consider dimuon production from semi-central In+In collisions in a full dynamical model. It is shown that if only a modification of the density-dependent $ρ$-mass is allowed, the maximum of dimuon invariant mass spectra is only slightly below experimental one. The additional inclusion of the temperature-dependent modification factor shifts the spectrum maximum toward lower invariant masses making calculation results incompatible with data. A further study is needed to disentangle the BR dropping $ρ$ mass scaling and strong broadening.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis, which predicts a dropping ρ-meson mass, is not definitively ruled out by NA60 data if only a density-dependent mass modification is used; however, the additional inclusion of a temperature-dependent modification factor shifts the spectrum maximum toward lower invariant masses, making the calculation results incompatible with the data.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid model combining the transport Quark Gluon String Model (QGSM) for the initial interaction stage with relativistic 3D hydrodynamics for the subsequent isoentropic expansion, employing a mixed-phase equation of state. Dimuon production from the ππ → l⁺l⁻ channel is calculated by integrating the emission rate over the full space-time evolution, with the in-medium ρ-meson mass modified according to either a density-dependent scaling or a combined density- and temperature-dependent scaling. The freeze-out is treated locally and continuously, and the contribution from free ρ-meson decay at freeze-out is added separately.

## Key numbers

- Average initial energy density in semi-central In+In collisions at 158A GeV: 3.2 GeV/fm³
- Average initial compression ratio n_B/n_0: 1.7
- Critical temperature at finite baryon chemical potential: about 160 MeV
- End point of temperature evolution (freeze-out): about 135 MeV
- Vacuum ρ-meson mass used: 770 MeV
- Coupling constants in vacuum: g_{ρππ} = 6.05, g = 5.03
- Density-dependent scaling factor: m*_ρ(x) = m_ρ (1 − 0.15 · n_B(x)/n_0)
- Temperature-dependent scaling factor: m*_ρ(x) = m_ρ (1 − 0.15 · n_B(x)/n_0) (1 − [T(x)/T_c]²)^{0.3}
- Impact parameter used: b = 4 fm
- Transition time from kinetics to hydrodynamics: t_kin = 1.3 fm/c

## Context

This work directly addresses the claim made at the Quark Matter 2005 Conference that Brown-Rho scaling is ruled out by NA60 data, following objections raised by Brown and Rho (2005) in two letters. The study extends prior work by Rapp and Wambach (2000) on in-medium ρ-meson broadening and by Hatsuda and Lee on QCD sum-rule estimates of density-dependent masses. The results indicate that further theoretical investigations and comparisons with absolute muon yields are needed to disentangle the Brown-Rho dropping mass scaling from the strong broadening scenario.
