# The nature of $X(3872)$ from high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions

> The structure of exotic resonances that do not trivially fit the usual quark model expectations has been a matter of intense scientific debate during the last two decades. A possible way of estimating the size of these states is to study their behavior when immersed in QCD matter. Recently, LHCb has measured the relative abundance of the exotic $X(3872)$ over the ordinary $ψ(2S)$. We use the comover interaction model to study the yield of a compact $X(3872)$. To confirm the reliability of the model in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions, we describe the suppression of excited over ground $Υ$ states. With this at hand, we show that the size of the compact $X(3872)$ would be slightly larger than that of the $ψ(2S)$. If the $X(3872)$ is instead assumed to be a meson molecule of large size, we argue that its evolution in QCD matter should be described via a coalescence model, as suggested by data on deuteron production. We show that the predictions of this model for the $X(3872)$ are in contrast with data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the LHCb measurement of the decreasing ratio of promptly produced X(3872) to ψ(2S) with increasing event multiplicity in pp collisions is consistent with a compact tetraquark interpretation of X(3872) (diameter ~1.3 fm), while a molecular interpretation (size ~10 fm) is ruled out because the coalescence model that successfully describes deuteron production fails to reproduce the X(3872) data.

## Method

The authors apply the comover interaction model (CIM) to describe the suppression of quarkonium states in high-multiplicity pp collisions, first validating it against CMS data on Υ(3S)/Υ(1S) and Υ(2S)/Υ(1S) ratios at 2.76 TeV. They then compute the breakup cross sections for compact states using a geometrical cross section formula with parameters fitted to pPb data (Teff = 250 ± 50 MeV, n = 1), and apply this to a compact tetraquark X(3872) with radius 0.65 fm. For the molecular hypothesis, they develop a Boltzmann equation approach incorporating both destruction and coalescence of hadron pairs, extracting effective couplings from πN and πD(*) scattering data and using Pythia8 simulations for momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) tetraquark radius: 0.65 fm, giving geometrical cross section 13.3 mb and average dissociation cross section ⟨vσ⟩ = 11.55 ± 1.82 mb
- X(3872) molecule radius: 6.6 fm, giving geometrical cross section 1368 mb and average dissociation cross section ⟨vσ⟩ = 1188 ± 187 mb
- ψ(2S) radius: 0.45 fm, giving geometrical cross section 6.36 mb and average dissociation cross section ⟨vσ⟩ = 4.89 ± 0.76 mb
- X(3872) binding energy relative to D0D̄*0 threshold: 44 ± 116 keV (using 1σ error of 116 keV)
- Effective coupling for πD(*) scattering: g2/(4π) ≃ 5.07, extracted from Lagrangian with y ≃ 0.8 and λ ≃ 25
- Effective coupling for πN scattering: g2/(4π) ≃ 10, matching PDG data in [0,300] MeV kinetic energy range
- The compact tetraquark model predicts approximately 20% decrease in the X(3872)/ψ(2S) ratio from the first to the second multiplicity bin

## Context

This work extends the comover interaction model previously applied to quarkonium suppression in pPb collisions by Ferreiro (2018) to high-multiplicity pp collisions, and contrasts with the molecular interpretation of X(3872) proposed by Guo (2017) and others. The coalescence approach for molecules follows earlier proposals by Esposito (2013) and Guerrieri (2014) for pp collisions, and by Gyulassy (2003) and Cho (2010) for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results enable discrimination between the compact tetraquark and molecular hypotheses for X(3872) using existing LHCb and ALICE data, and suggest that future high-multiplicity measurements can further test the nature of other XYZ states.
