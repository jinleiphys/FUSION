# Indications for freeze-out of charge fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma at the LHC

> The D-measure of net-charge fluctuations quantifies the variance of net charge in strongly interacting matter. It was introduced over 20 years ago as a potential signal of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions, where it is expected to be suppressed due to the fractional electric charges of quarks. Measurements have been performed at RHIC and LHC, but the conclusion has been elusive in the absence of quantitative calculations for both scenarios. We address this issue by employing a recently developed formalism of density correlations and incorporate resonance decays, local charge conservation, and experimental kinematic cuts. We find that the hadron gas scenario is in fair agreement with the ALICE data for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions only when a very short rapidity range of local charge conservation is enforced, while the QGP scenario is in excellent agreement with experimental data and largely insensitive to the range of local charge conservation. A Bayesian analysis of the data utilizing different priors yields moderate evidence for the freeze-out of charge fluctuations in the QGP phase relative to hadron gas. The upcoming high-fidelity measurements from LHC Run 2 will serve as a precision test of the two scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV are in excellent agreement with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) scenario for freeze-out of charge fluctuations, while the hadron gas scenario only agrees if a very short rapidity range of local charge conservation is enforced; a Bayesian analysis yields moderate evidence for the QGP scenario, but the conclusion depends on the prior assumption about the range of local charge conservation.

## Method

The authors employed a recently developed formalism of density correlations in spatial rapidity to calculate the D-measure of net-charge fluctuations, incorporating resonance decays, local charge conservation, collective flow, and experimental kinematic cuts. They contrasted two scenarios: hadron gas (with ω = 1.1) and QGP (with ω = 0.36), using the blast-wave model to evaluate momentum acceptance probabilities for pions, kaons, and protons. A Bayesian analysis of the ALICE data in the (ω, Vc/Vtot) parameter space was performed using a Gaussian likelihood function with two different priors for the local charge conservation volume.

## Key numbers

- The D-measure for a pion gas in the grand-canonical ensemble is Dπ ≈ 4, while a hadron gas yields DHG ≈ 3 and the QGP estimate is DQGP ≈ 1
- The normalized charge variance at hadronization is ωHG ≈ 1.1 for hadron gas at T = 155-160 MeV and ωQGP = 0.36 ± 0.04 for the QGP scenario
- The entropy per charged hadron at LHC energies is S/⟨Nch⟩ = 6.7 ± 0.8
- The ratio of final to primordial charged multiplicities from resonance decays is γQ = ⟨Nch⟩/⟨Nch^prim⟩ ≈ 1.67
- For the QGP scenario, the charge susceptibility is (χ2^Q)QGP/T^3 = 2/3 and entropy density sQGP/T^3 = 19π^2/9
- The Bayesian analysis with uniform prior yields ω^68%_CI = 0.34^{+0.28}_{-0.16} and Bayes factor B_QGP/HG = 9.7 (moderate bordering strong evidence)
- With a local conservation prior, ω^68%_CI = 0.53^{+0.30}_{-0.27} and B_QGP/HG = 4.7 (moderate evidence)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to use the D-measure of net-charge fluctuations, introduced by Asakawa (2000) and Jeon (2000), as a signal of QGP formation in heavy-ion collisions. Previous measurements by STAR and ALICE had been inconclusive due to the lack of quantitative calculations for both hadron gas and QGP scenarios on an equal footing. The formalism enables precision tests with upcoming high-statistics data from LHC Run 2 at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and can be extended to finite baryon densities, RHIC energies, higher-order fluctuations, and balance functions.
