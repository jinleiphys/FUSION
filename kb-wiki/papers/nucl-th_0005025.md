# Description of Multi Quasi Particle Bands by the Tilted Axis Cranking Model

> The selfconsistent cranking approach is extended to the case of rotation about an axis which is tilted with respect to the principal axes of the deformed potential (Tilted Axis Cranking). Expressions for the energies and the intra bands electromagnetic transition probabilities are given. The mean field solutions are interpreted in terms of quantal rotational states. The construction of the quasiparticle configurations and the elimination of spurious states is discussed. The application of the theory to high spin data is demonstrated by analyzing the multi quasiparticle bands in the nuclide-s with $N=102,103$ and $Z=71,72,73$.

## Key claim

The Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) approach extends the selfconsistent cranking model to rotation about an axis that is tilted with respect to the principal axes of the deformed potential, enabling the description of Delta I=1 rotational bands; the paper provides a comprehensive presentation of the theory and calculational methods, using multi quasiparticle bands in nuclides with N=102,103 and Z=71,72,73 as examples, but only semi-quantitative agreement with data is sought, the focus being qualitative structure rather than full parameter optimization.

## Method

The mean field Routhian is constructed using either the pairing plus quadrupole model (PQTAC) or the Strutinsky shell correction method (SCTAC), with the rotational axis direction treated as a variational parameter. The HFB equations are solved self-consistently for the quadrupole deformation, pair field, and tilt angle, with the condition that the angular velocity and angular momentum vectors must be parallel. Quasiparticle configurations are traced diabatically as functions of rotational frequency and tilt angle to follow the same physical configuration across avoided crossings, and the resulting Routhians are converted to energies and electromagnetic transition probabilities using semiclassical expressions.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole-quadrupole coupling constant for the rare earth region is chi = 91 MeV A^{-5/3} r^{-4}_{osc}, scaled from a value of chi = 0.0174 MeV r^{-4}_{osc} found for A=170
- For A=200, the scaled coupling constant is chi = 0.0133 MeV r^{-4}_{osc}, giving a deformation of eps approximately -0.11 in PQTAC (SCTAC gives eps approximately -0.15)
- For A=110 and A=140, scaling from the rare earth value gives chi = 0.036 MeV r^{-4}_{osc} and chi = 0.024 MeV r^{-4}_{osc} respectively
- A new adjustment for 110Cd gave chi = 0.036 MeV r^{-4}_{osc} and for 139Sm gave chi = 0.022 MeV r^{-4}_{osc}
- The step size for diabatic tracing in frequency is Delta omega = 0.05 MeV and in tilt angle is Delta theta = 5 degrees

## Context

This work extends the TAC approach introduced by Frauendorf (2000) and builds on earlier studies of rotation about non-principal axes by Kerman and Onishi (1981), Frisk and Bengtsson (1987), and Goodman (1986), who demonstrated the possibility of uniform tilted rotation but did not provide the physical interpretation of TAC solutions. The paper provides a systematic reference for applying TAC to analyze multi quasiparticle bands in well-deformed nuclei, enabling future extensions to more sophisticated mean field approximations such as Hartree-Fock or Relativistic Mean Field approaches, and complements the Cranked Shell Model approach of Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979) by treating bands with broken signature symmetry.
