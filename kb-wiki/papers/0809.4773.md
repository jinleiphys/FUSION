# Instantons and Spin-Flavor effects in Hadron Physics

> We discuss the role of instantons in the spectroscopy of ordinary and exotic hadrons as well as in high energy reactions. We argue that the instanton induced flavor- and spin-dependent quark-quark and quark-gluon interactions can explain many features of the hadron spectrum. The observed anomalous spin and flavor effects in various reactions with hadrons can also be understood within the instanton model for QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

Instantons, as nonperturbative fluctuations of gluon fields in the QCD vacuum, can explain many features of hadron spectroscopy and spin/flavor effects in high-energy reactions, but the paper argues this through a review of existing models rather than presenting a single new experimental result or calculation.

## Method

The paper reviews the instanton-induced t'Hooft two-body and three-body quark-quark interactions and the nonperturbative quark-gluon chromomagnetic interaction, discussing their application to ordinary hadron spectroscopy, exotic multiquark states (pentaquarks, tetraquarks, H-dibaryon), nucleon spin structure, and high-energy scattering. It compares predictions from instanton-based constituent quark models with those from perturbative one-gluon exchange (OGE) models and with experimental data on sea quark polarization, flavor asymmetry, and single-spin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- The instanton packing fraction in the QCD vacuum is approximately \( f = n_c \pi^2 \rho_c^4 \approx 0.1 \), where \( n_c \) is the instanton density and \( \rho_c \) is the average instanton size.
- The instanton-induced quark-quark scattering cross section exceeds the perturbative one-gluon exchange contribution in the region of transverse momentum \( p_\bot < 3 \) GeV.
- The paper references a calculation showing that the instanton-induced quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment is proportional to the packing fraction \( f \approx 0.1 \).

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based approach to hadron physics developed by Shuryak (1998), Diakonov (2003), and the author's own prior work (Kochelev 1985, Dorokhov and Kochelev 1992), contrasting it with the perturbative one-gluon exchange models of Jaffe (1977) and Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). It enables further study of exotic hadron spectroscopy, the origin of the proton spin crisis, and the mechanism behind large single-spin asymmetries observed by HERMES, STAR, and COMPASS collaborations, while also suggesting that the perturbative QCD regime for quark-quark scattering only sets in above \( p_\bot \approx 3 \) GeV.
