# Cumulants of net-charge distribution from particle-antiparticle sources

> It is shown how high-order cumulants of net-charge distribution in hadronic collisions at LHC energies can be expressed via lower-order terms under the assumption that particle-antiparticle pairs are produced in independent local processes. It is argued and tested with HIJING model that this assumption is typically valid for net-proton fluctuations in the case when no critical behaviour is present in the system. Values estimated in such a way can be considered as baselines for direct measurements of high-order net-charge fluctuations in real data.

# Cumulants of net-charge distribution from particle-antiparticle sources

## Key claim

Under the assumption that particle-antiparticle pairs are produced in independent local processes, high-order cumulants of net-charge distribution can be expressed via lower-order terms; this assumption is typically valid for net-proton fluctuations at LHC energies when no critical behaviour is present, as tested with the HIJING model.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the ratio of the fourth to the second cumulant of net-charge distribution, assuming independent two-particle sources (particle-antiparticle pairs) that are nearly uncorrelated in rapidity. The resulting formula expresses the cumulant ratio in terms of the second cumulant and the robust variance of the number of positive particles, both measurable in experiment. The validity of the assumptions is tested by comparing direct calculations of net-proton cumulant ratios in HIJING Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with the analytic expression, and by examining factorial cumulants.

## Key numbers

- Net-proton cumulant ratio kappa_4/kappa_2 in HIJING Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, with protons and antiprotons in transverse momentum range 0.6–2 GeV/c and rapidity acceptance |y|<2, is shown as a function of rapidity acceptance Y for centrality classes from 50-60% to 90-100% (Figure 1a).
- The analytic expression kappa_4/kappa_2 = 1 + 3 kappa_2(Delta N) R_2(N^+) reproduces direct HIJING calculations across all centrality classes, with the slope reflecting volume fluctuations via the second term.
- Values of kappa_4/kappa_2 decrease when centrality class width narrows from 20% to 5% (Figure 1b), explained by reduction of volume fluctuations.
- Net-proton factorial cumulant f_4 in HIJING is compatible with zero for centrality classes 70-80% and 80-90% (Figure 2), indicating no genuine high-order net-proton correlations in the generator.

## Context

This work extends the decomposition of cumulants for independent sources developed by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2017) and Altsybeev (2020), applying it specifically to particle-antiparticle pair production at LHC energies. The derived baseline expressions provide an alternative to the Poissonian (Skellam) baseline for experimental measurements of high-order net-charge fluctuations, against which signals of critical phenomena near the QCD critical point could be identified. The study also examines factorial cumulants as proposed by Kitazawa and Asakawa (2017) and Ollitrault (2018), noting that while they remove volume fluctuations and self-correlations, they cannot be directly compared with lattice QCD calculations.
