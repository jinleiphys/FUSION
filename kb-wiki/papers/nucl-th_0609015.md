# Two-pion-exchange parity-violating potential and $\vec{n} p \to d γ$

> We calculate the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-next-to-leading order. The one-pion exchange comes in the leading order and the next-to-next-to-leading order consists of two-pion-exchange and the two-nucleon contact terms. In order to investigate the effect of the higher order contributions, we calculate the parity-violating asymmetry in $\vec{n} p \to d γ$ at the threshold. The one-pion dominates the physical observable and the two-pion contribution is about or less than 10% of the one-pion contribution.

## Key claim

The parity-violating two-pion-exchange potential contributes about or less than 10% of the one-pion-exchange contribution to the photon asymmetry in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$, but this estimate carries significant uncertainty because the two-pion-exchange contribution varies in magnitude and sign depending on the cutoff value used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors derived the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order, retaining the one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange terms while neglecting other next-to-next-to-leading order contributions such as two-nucleon contact terms. They then calculated the parity-violating photon asymmetry $A_\gamma$ in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ at threshold using the Argonne v18 potential for the parity-conserving strong interaction and the Siegert theorem for the electromagnetic current operators, with a monopole form factor to regularize the short-range behavior.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion-exchange contribution to the asymmetry coefficient $a_\gamma$ (where $A_\gamma = a_\gamma h^1_\pi$) ranges from $-0.1074$ to $-0.1126$ for cutoff values $\Lambda$ from 500 to 2000 MeV.
- The two-pion-exchange contribution to $a_\gamma$ ranges from $-0.0022$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $0.0133$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV, including a sign change between these cutoff values.
- The two-pion-exchange contribution to the asymmetry is about $-2\%$ to $12\%$ of the one-pion-exchange contribution over the cutoff range 500 to 2000 MeV.
- In the long-range region (beyond about 1 fm), the magnitude of the two-pion-exchange potential is smaller than the one-pion-exchange potential by an order of 10.

## Context

This work extends the DDH one-meson-exchange potential approach (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein, 1980) by providing the first estimation of the intermediate-range two-pion-exchange contribution to the parity-violating asymmetry in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ using effective field theory. The results confirm that the one-pion-exchange dominates the observable, as found in earlier DDH-based calculations (Hyun, 2001; Rocco, 2004), but show that the two-pion-exchange correction is significantly larger than the heavy-meson contributions (less than 1% of one-pion-exchange) and must be accounted for to reliably extract the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant $h^1_\pi$ from future measurements.
