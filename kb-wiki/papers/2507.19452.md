# Modeling direct and pre-equilibrium processes of neutron-induced reactions with noniterative finite amplitude method and distorted-wave Born approximation

> We develop a calculation method for describing the direct and pre-equilibrium processes in neutron-induced reactions based on the framework of noniterative finite amplitude method (FAM) and distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA). The noniterative FAM is used to derive equations of quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) for neutron-induced inelastic scatterings to both the discrete and continuum states in a consistent manner. The Skyrme force is employed as an interaction between the projectile neutron with nucleons inside the target nucleus. We apply this method to the neutron-induced reaction on 208Pb. We demonstrate that the calculated differential inelastic scattering cross sections to low-lying states reproduce available experimental data without any phenomenological parameters that are often introduced in conventional DWBA calculations. The calculated double differential cross section to the continuum state also agrees with the experimental data in the energy region relevant to the direct and pre-equilibrium processes. These results are used to investigate the spin distribution of the populated states in the residual nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors develop a calculation method for direct and one-step pre-equilibrium neutron-induced reactions that combines the noniterative finite amplitude method (FAM) with distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), using the Skyrme force as the projectile-target interaction. The method reproduces experimental differential cross sections for inelastic scattering on 208Pb without introducing phenomenological deformation parameters that are typically required in conventional DWBA calculations.

## Method

The noniterative FAM is used to derive the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) equations for neutron-induced inelastic scattering, replacing the electromagnetic transition operator with the nuclear interaction between the incident neutron and nucleons in the target nucleus. The Skyrme effective interaction is employed as the nuclear force, and the distorted waves for incoming and outgoing neutrons are calculated from the optical model. The method decomposes the inelastic scattering operator into multipole components for natural parity and unnatural parity transitions, then calculates both differential cross sections for discrete final states and double-differential cross sections for continuum states using the QRPA transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The method is applied to neutron-induced reactions on 208Pb at incident neutron energies above 10 MeV, where direct and pre-equilibrium mechanisms dominate
- The calculated differential inelastic scattering cross sections to low-lying states reproduce available experimental data without any phenomenological parameters
- The calculated double differential cross section to the continuum state agrees with experimental data in the energy region relevant to direct and pre-equilibrium processes
- The QRPA equation includes configurations restricted to mu >= nu and alpha >= beta for single-particle states from the Hartree-Fock calculation

## Context

This work extends the noniterative FAM approach previously developed by Sasaki (2022, 2023) for electromagnetic transitions to nuclear reactions, replacing the electromagnetic operator with the nuclear interaction. It provides a unified microscopic framework for treating direct and one-step pre-equilibrium processes consistently, building on earlier quantum mechanical models of multistep direct reactions by Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (1980) and the self-consistent RPA approach with Gogny D1S force by Dupuis (2011). The method enables future calculations of neutron-induced reaction cross sections without phenomenological parameters and allows prediction of the spin distribution of the residual nucleus.
