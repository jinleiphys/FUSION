# Phase diagram of hot magnetized two-flavor color superconducting quark matter

> A two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is introduced at finite temperature T, chemical potential mu and in the presence of a constant magnetic field eB. The effect of (T,mu,eB) on the formation of chiral and color symmetry breaking condensates is studied. The complete phase portrait of the model in T-mu, mu-eB, and T-eB phase spaces for various fixed eB, T, and mu is explored. A threshold magnetic field eB_t~ 0.5 GeV^2 is found above which the dynamics of the system is solely dominated by the lowest Landau level (LLL) and the effects of T and mu are partly compensated by eB.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a threshold magnetic field of approximately 0.5 GeV² exists above which the dynamics of the two-flavor color superconducting quark matter system is solely dominated by the lowest Landau level (LLL), and the effects of temperature and chemical potential are partly compensated by the magnetic field. This claim is qualified by the specific parameter set used (Λ = 0.6533 GeV, G_S = 5.0163 GeV⁻², G_D = 3/4 G_S) and the mean-field approximation at one-loop level.

## Method

The authors introduce a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with meson and diquark condensates at finite temperature T, chemical potential μ, and constant rotated magnetic field eB. They derive the one-loop thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation using Nambu-Gorkov formalism, proper-time integrals, and Poisson resummation. The chiral and diquark gaps are obtained by numerically solving the gap equations derived from minimizing the effective potential, using smooth cutoff functions with parameter Λ = 0.6533 GeV and A = 0.05Λ.

## Key numbers

- Threshold magnetic field: eB_t ≈ 0.5 GeV² (corresponding to B ≈ 8.5 × 10¹⁹ Gauss), above which the system is dominated by the lowest Landau level
- At zero magnetic field and zero temperature: meson mass σ₀ ≈ 323.8 MeV at μ = 250 MeV; diquark mass Δ₀ ≈ 126 MeV at μ = 460 MeV
- Parameter values: Λ = 0.6533 GeV, G_S = 5.0163 GeV⁻², G_D = 3/4 G_S
- First order phase transition between χSB and CSC phases at T = 0 occurs at μ ∼ 350–450 MeV
- Magnetic field range explored: eB ∈ [0, 0.8] GeV²; temperature range: T ∈ [0, 250] MeV; chemical potential range: μ ∈ [0, 800] MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature analysis of Fayazbakhsh and Sadooghi (2010) on a color-neutral magnetized 2SC-NJL model to finite temperature, building on prior studies of magnetic catalysis by Miransky (1995) and the effect of strong magnetic fields on color superconductivity by Manuel (2006). The results are relevant for understanding quark matter in heavy ion collisions, where magnetic fields up to eB ≈ 0.3 GeV² may be created at LHC energies as estimated by Skokov (2009), and for assessing whether color superconducting phases are accessible in experiments such as FAIR-CBM and NICA-MPD as discussed by Blaschke (2010).
