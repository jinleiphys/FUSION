# Unified relativistic description of piNN and gamma-piNN

> We present a unified description of the relativistic piNN and gamma-piNN systems where the strong interactions are described non-perturbatively by four-dimensional integral equations. A feature of our approach is that the photon is coupled in all possible ways to the strong interaction contributions. Thus the hadronic processes NN -> NN, NN -> pid, pid -> pid, etc. and corresponding electromagnetic processes NN -> gamma-NN, gamma-d -> NN, gamma-d -> pid, ed -> ed, ed -> e'pid, etc., are described simultaneously within the one model of strong interactions. Our formulation obeys two and three-body unitarity, and as photons are coupled everywhere in the strong interaction model, gauge invariance is implemented in the way prescribed by quantum field theory. Our formulation is also free from the overcounting and undercounting problems plaguing four-dimensional descriptions of piNN-like systems. The unified description is achieved through the use of the recently introduced gauging of equations method.

## Key claim
The paper presents a unified relativistic description of the πNN and γπNN systems where the photon is coupled in all possible ways to the strong interaction contributions, but this unified description is achieved specifically through the recently introduced gauging of equations method and is limited to strong interaction models that can be expressed as four-dimensional integral equations.

## Method
The authors apply their previously developed "gauging of equations" method to the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter integral equations describing the relativistic πNN system. They first restate the πNN equations in a form convenient for gauging, handling both distinguishable and indistinguishable nucleon cases, then gauge these equations to obtain expressions for all electromagnetic transition currents of the πNN system. The gauging procedure attaches photons to every part of every strong interaction diagram, ensuring gauge invariance as prescribed by quantum field theory.

## Key numbers
- The πNN equations are represented as 4×4 matrices for distinguishable nucleons and 3×3 matrices for indistinguishable nucleons
- The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the πNN system is written as T = V + V G_t T, where T, V, and G_t are matrix quantities
- The physical amplitudes for NN → NN, NN → πd, πd → NN, and πd → πd are given by X_NN = T_NN, X_dN = ψ̄_d T_3N, X_Nd = T_N3 ψ_d, and X_dd = ψ̄_d T_33 ψ_d respectively
- The subtraction term Δ that eliminates overcounting contains contributions from W_ππ, W_πN, W_NN, X, Y, and other terms as defined in equation (Delta)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous gauging of equations method, which was first applied to the relativistic three-nucleon system (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1993-1994) and later to the three-dimensional spectator equation for three nucleons. The πNN system is more complicated than the three-nucleon case because it includes pion absorption, coupling to the NN channel, and an overcounting problem that was only recently solved (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1997; Pena and Adam, 1997). The formulation enables simultaneous description of hadronic processes (NN → NN, NN → πd, πd → πd) and electromagnetic processes (γd → NN, γd → πd, ed → ed, ed → e'πd) within one model of strong interactions, and provides a framework for computing deuteron electromagnetic form factors with all possible meson exchange currents included.
