# Extend Brown-Rho Scaling Law with QCD Sum Rules

> The scalar and vector self-energies obtained through QCD sum rules are introduced in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) equations. The results indicate that the ratios of coupling constants to respective meson mass have a very small dependence on density. Then, an extended Brown-Rho scaling law is conjectured.

# Extended Brown-Rho Scaling Law with QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The ratios of scalar and vector meson-nucleon coupling constants to their respective meson masses (g_s/m_s and g_v/m_v) have only a very small dependence on nuclear density, which allows an extension of the Brown-Rho scaling law to include coupling constants, but this result is strictly valid only at low densities, where the low-density approximation for the in-medium quark condensate holds.

## Method
The author computed the scalar and vector nucleon self-energies using QCD sum rules at finite density, then equated these to the self-energies expressed in the QHD-I (Quantum Hadrodynamics) model in the Relativistic Hartree Approximation. By comparing the two expressions, the ratios g_s^2/m_s^2 and g_v^2/m_v^2 were extracted as functions of density, first in a low-density analytic limit using the linear density approximation for the quark condensate, and then numerically using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the in-medium quark condensate beyond the low-density limit.

## Key numbers
- The QCD sum rule calculation yields g_s^2/m_s^2 = 2.793 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} and g_v^2/m_v^2 = 1.964 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} at saturation density (k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}), compared to the QHD-I fitted values of 3.029 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} and 2.222 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-2}, respectively.
- The saturation condition used is ε/ρ_B - M = 15.75 MeV at normal nuclear matter density with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}.
- The Borel mass parameter is taken as M_B^2 ≈ 1 GeV^2.
- The sigma term is σ_N ≈ 45 ± 10 MeV.
- The NJL model parameters used are Λ_NJL = 900 MeV and G_NJL = 3.54/Λ_NJL^2.
- The interpolating field parameter t = -1.1 was used for the numerical calculations shown in the figure.

## Context
This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling law (Brown and Rho, 1991), which originally proposed that in-medium meson masses and the nucleon effective mass scale similarly, to include the coupling constants g_s and g_v, showing they scale with the same rate as their respective meson masses. The results corroborate the observation by Li, Lee, and Brown (1997) that nucleon flow probing higher density requires g_v/m_v to be independent of density at low densities and decrease slightly at high densities. The approach connects QCD sum rules (Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov, 1979) to QHD (Serot and Walecka, 1986), and uses the NJL model to go beyond the low-density linear approximation for the quark condensate. The work enables future studies of density-dependent coupling constants in finite nuclei, where the author notes the ratios do not appear explicitly and density dependence may be important.
