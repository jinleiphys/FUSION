# Regularization of the Nambu-Jona Lasinio model under a uniform magnetic field and the role of the anomalous magnetic moments

> The vacuum contribution to quark matter under a uniform magnetic field within the SU(3) version of the Nambu and Jona-Lasinio model is studied. The standard regularization procedure is examined and a new prescription is proposed. For this purpose analytic regularization and a subtraction scheme are used to deal with divergencies depending on the magnetic intensity. This scheme is combined with the standard three momentum cutoff recipe, and reduces to it for vanishing magnetic intensity. Furthermore, the effects of a direct coupling between the anomalous magnetic moments of the quarks and the magnetic field is considered. Single particle properties as well as bulk thermodynamical quantities are studied for a configuration of matter found in neutron stars. A wide range of baryonic densities and magnetic intensities are examined at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new analytic regularization scheme for the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under a uniform magnetic field that reduces to the standard three-momentum cutoff at zero magnetic field, and it claims that previous regularization procedures may produce unphysical results such as tachyonic poles and unphysical oscillations in thermodynamical quantities, particularly for magnetic intensities above approximately 5 × 10^18 G.

## Method

The author uses the SU(3) NJL model extended with an anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) term, employing a fermion propagator that includes the full interaction with a uniform magnetic field and the AMM. An analytic regularization based on the Hurwitz zeta function combined with a subtraction scheme is applied to the divergent vacuum contributions, with the regularization point chosen at zero magnetic field and fixed baryonic density. The regularized model is then used to study electrically neutral quark matter in beta equilibrium, including leptons, at zero temperature across a range of baryonic densities and magnetic intensities relevant to neutron star interiors.

## Key numbers

- NJL parameters used: current quark masses M_u0 = M_d0 = 5.5 MeV, M_s0 = 135.7 MeV; cutoff Λ = 631.4 MeV; coupling constants G = 1.835/Λ^2, K = 9.29/Λ^5 (from Kunihiro)
- AMM set AMM1: κ_u = 0.074, κ_d = 0.127, κ_s = 0.053 (in units of nuclear magneton), derived from constituent quark model with M_u = M_d = 363 MeV, M_s = 538 MeV
- AMM set AMM2: κ_u = κ_d = 0.38, κ_s = 0.25 (compatible with Mekhfi)
- Magnetic intensity range studied: 10^15 G ≤ B ≤ 10^19 G
- Baryonic density range studied: up to n = 7 n_0, where n_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 (nuclear saturation density)
- For B < 2 × 10^18 G, qualitative agreement between regularization schemes is found; discrepancies become significant for B > 5 × 10^18 G
- At zero baryonic density, the vacuum energy density variation is only a few MeV in the AMM0 approach, while it exceeds 10 MeV for intensities above 10^19 G in the comparison case C
- The effect of AMM on quark masses is at most 10 MeV for the u flavor and around 0.1 MeV for the s quark at B = 10^19 G
- For B < 5 × 10^18 G, all AMM corrections diminish by about 30%
- Magnetization values: |M/e| < 0.08 fm^-2 in the low-density regime; asymptotic value M/e ≈ -0.14 fm^-2 at high density for B = 10^17 G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the NJL model under magnetic fields, particularly addressing the regularization issues raised by Avancini et al. (various years) regarding the distinction between non-magnetic and magnetic vacuum contributions. The author compares results with the regularization approach of Ebert et al. (various years), finding qualitative agreement only for low magnetic intensities. The inclusion of AMM builds on work by Fayaz et al. (various years) and Chaudhuri et al. (various years), who used different cutoff prescriptions, and the propagator formalism follows Aguirre (various years). The results are relevant for modeling magnetar interiors, where magnetic fields up to 5 × 10^18 G and baryonic densities up to 7 n_0 are expected.
