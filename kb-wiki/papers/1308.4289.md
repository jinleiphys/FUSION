# Effect of the Wood-Saxon nuclear distribution on the chiral magnetic field in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> The formation of the QCD vacuum with nonzero winding number $Q_w$ during relativistic heavy-ion collisions breaks the parity and charge-parity symmetry. A new kind of field configuration can separate charge in the presence of a background magnetic field-the "chiral magnetic effect". The strong magnetic field and the QCD vacuum can both completely be produced in the noncentral nuclear-nuclear collision. Basing on the theory of Kharzeev,Mclerran and Warringa, we use the Wood-Saxon nucleon distribution to replace that of the uniform distribution to improve the magnetic field calculation method of the noncentral collision. The chiral magnetic field distribution at LHC(Large Hadron Collider) energy regions are predicted. We also consider the contributions to the magnetic field of the total charge given by the produced quarks.

## Key claim

Using a Wood-Saxon nucleon distribution instead of the uniform distribution to model the nuclear density in noncentral heavy-ion collisions yields a slightly larger predicted chiral magnetic field than the original Kharzeev-Mclerran-Warringa (KMW) model, particularly at early proper times after the collision.

## Method

The authors modified the KMW magnetic field calculation by replacing the uniform nuclear density with a Wood-Saxon distribution (parameters: n0=0.17 fm^-3, d=0.54 fm, R=1.12A^1/3 fm) for Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions. They computed the magnetic field at the origin (η=0, x⊥=0) from spectator and participant nucleons using Eqs. ## Key numbers

- For Au-Au at √s = 64 GeV, b = 8 fm: the magnetic field using Wood-Saxon distribution is slightly larger than the KMW uniform-distribution result at proper times τ < 1.2 fm, with a cross point at τ ≈ 1.2 fm where the ordering reverses
- For Au-Au at √s = 200 GeV, b = 8 fm: the cross point where magnetic field values for different impact parameters cross occurs at τ ≈ 0.3 fm, much smaller than the τ ≈ 1.2 fm cross point at √s = 64 GeV
- For Pb-Pb at √s = 900 GeV: at τ ≈ 0, the magnetic field magnitudes for b = 4, 8, and 12 fm are nearly the same, but the field for b = 12 fm drops dramatically with time
- For Au-Au at √s = 64 GeV and b = 4 fm: the magnetic field from produced quarks is larger than for b = 8 or 12 fm; at b = 12 fm the produced-quark contribution is approximately zero
- The constituent quark mass used is m = 0.308 GeV, average transverse momentum pT = 0.2 GeV, temperature T = 0.2 GeV; quark chemical potential μq = 0.01 GeV at √s = 200 GeV and μq = 0.02 GeV at √s = 64 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Kharzeev-Mclerran-Warringa (KMW) model (2008) for the chiral magnetic effect in noncentral heavy-ion collisions by replacing the uniform nuclear density with a more realistic Wood-Saxon distribution. The authors also incorporate contributions from produced quarks using a thermal fireball model with anisotropic rapidity distributions. The results enable more accurate predictions of the chiral magnetic field at LHC energies (√s = 900, 2760, and 5500 GeV for Pb-Pb collisions) and show that the magnetic field at LHC energies is not as large as at RHIC energies.
