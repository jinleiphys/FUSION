# Semi-inclusive Deep Inelastic Electron Scattering off the Deuteron and the Neutron to Proton Structure Function Ratio

> The production of slow nucleons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off the deuteron is investigated in the region $x \gsim 0.3$. It is shown that within the spectator mechanism the semi-inclusive cross section exhibits a scaling property even at moderate values of $Q^2$ ($\sim$ few $(GeV/c)^2$) accessible at present facilities, like $CEBAF$. Such a scaling property can be used as a model-independent test of the dominance of the spectator mechanism itself and provides an interesting tool to investigate the neutron structure function. The possibility of extracting model-independent information on the neutron to proton structure function ratio from semi-inclusive experiments is illustrated. The application of the spectator scaling to semi-inclusive processes off complex nuclei is outlined.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive cross section for slow-nucleon production in deuteron electrodisintegration exhibits a "spectator-scaling" property even at moderate Q² (~ few (GeV/c)²) accessible at CEBAF, provided the detected nucleon momentum is low (p₂ ≲ 0.3 GeV/c). This scaling holds only when the spectator mechanism dominates and is violated at higher momenta or by fragmentation processes at low x*.

## Method

The author calculates the semi-inclusive cross section for ²H(e,e'N)X within the plane-wave impulse approximation using a non-relativistic deuteron wave function, relating the deuteron response to the struck nucleon's structure function via the nucleon momentum distribution n^(D)(p₂). The spectator-scaling function F^(sp) is defined as M p₂ n^(D)(p₂) F₂^N(x*,Q²)/x*, where x* is a modified scaling variable that accounts for the struck nucleon's invariant mass. The ratio of semi-inclusive cross sections for proton and neutron detection, R^(s.i.), is shown to directly yield the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio R^(n/p)(x*,Q²) when the spectator mechanism dominates.

## Key numbers

- The spectator-scaling property is tested at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² with incident electron energy E_e = 6 GeV, for detected nucleon momenta p₂ = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5 GeV/c.
- At p₂ ≈ 0.1 GeV/c, spectator scaling is "almost completely fulfilled"; at p₂ = 0.5 GeV/c, violations are "relevant" and prevent observation of nucleon resonance peaks.
- Spectator-scaling violations at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² are ~15% for forward proton emission (θ₂ < 90°) and within ~5% for backward proton emission (θ₂ > 90°).
- The limiting value D^n = 1 (perfect scaling) is reached within ~20% at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)².
- Fragmentation processes produce relevant violations of spectator scaling only at x* ≲ 0.4; backward kinematics are most appropriate for extracting R^(n/p).
- At x ≈ 0.85, the sensitivity of the inclusive ratio R^(n/p) to different deuteron wave functions reaches ~20%.

## Context

This work extends the standard inclusive method of extracting the neutron structure function from deuteron data (SLAC, 1990s), which suffers from model dependence at large x (x ≳ 0.65) due to Fermi motion, off-shell effects, and nuclear binding corrections. The spectator-scaling approach offers a model-independent alternative using semi-inclusive measurements, building on earlier proposals by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981). The paper also generalizes the spectator-scaling concept to semi-inclusive processes off complex nuclei of the type A(e,e'(A-1)_gr)X, where no competing nucleon fragmentation processes exist.
