# Virial expansion for almost diagonal random matrices

> Energy level statistics of Hermitian random matrices $\hat H$ with Gaussian independent random entries $H_{i\geq j}$ is studied for a generic ensemble of almost diagonal random matrices with $ <|H_{ii}|^{2} > \sim 1$ and $<|H_{i\neq j}|^{2} >= b {\cal F}(|i-j|) \ll 1$. We perform a regular expansion of the spectral form-factor $K(τ) = 1 + b K_{1}(τ) + b^{2} K_{2}(τ) + ... $ in powers of $b \ll 1$ with the coefficients $K_{m}(τ)$ that take into account interaction of (m+1) energy levels. To calculate $K_{m}(τ)$, we develop a diagrammatic technique which is based on the Trotter formula and on the combinatorial problem of graph edges coloring with (m+1) colors. Expressions for $K_{1}(τ)$ and $K_{2}(τ)$ in terms of infinite series are found for a generic function ${\cal F}(|i-j|)$ in the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) and in the crossover between them (the almost unitary Gaussian ensemble). The Rosenzweig-Porter and power-law banded matrix ensembles are considered as examples.

## Key claim

The paper develops a regular expansion of the spectral form-factor \(K(\tau)\) in powers of the small parameter \(b\) (controlling off-diagonal matrix elements) for almost diagonal random matrices, where the expansion is valid in the thermodynamic limit \(N \to \infty\) at arbitrary values of the product \(b N \tau\), a regime where naive perturbation theory would fail because the small parameter can be compensated by the large product \(N \tau\).

## Method

The authors separate the Hamiltonian into a diagonal part \(\hat{H}_\varepsilon\) and an off-diagonal part \(\hat{V}\) with small entries \(\sim b\), then use the Trotter formula to represent the time-evolution operator as an infinite product of exponentials of \(\hat{H}_\varepsilon\) and \(\hat{V}\) separately. Expanding each exponential of \(\hat{V}\) only to linear order in \(\hat{V}\) (justified by the Trotter limit), they classify terms by the number of distinct energy levels ("colors") involved, leading to a virial expansion where the coefficient \(K_m(\tau)\) accounts for interaction of \(m+1\) levels. The combinatorial factors arising from distributing off-diagonal matrices across the two traces are solved via graph edge coloring with \(m+1\) colors, and the Trotter sums become integrals over continuous Trotter variables in the \(n \to \infty\) limit.

## Key numbers

- Variance of diagonal entries: \(\langle H_{ii}^2 \rangle = 1/\beta\) with \(\beta=1\) for GOE and \(\beta=2\) for GUE.
- Variance of off-diagonal entries: \(\langle |H_{i \neq j}|^2 \rangle = b^2 \, \mathcal{F}(|i-j|)\) with \(b \ll 1\).
- Mean level spacing: \(\Delta \simeq (1/N) \sqrt{2\pi/\beta}\).
- Level compressibility relation: \(\chi \simeq 1 - (1 - \chi_0)/\Upsilon\) with \(\Upsilon^{-1} \simeq \sqrt{2}\).
- For the two-level (two-color) contribution, the leading correction to the form-factor is \(b \tilde{K}_1 = - b \sqrt{\beta \pi} \, (b \tilde{N} |\tau|) \, \mathcal{R}_N(1)\), where \(\tilde{N} = 1/\Delta\) and \(\mathcal{R}_N(1) = (1/N) \sum_{i>j} \mathcal{F}(|i-j|)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of spectral statistics beyond the Wigner-Dyson ensembles and the supersymmetric sigma-model approach (Efetov, 1983), which requires large bandwidth \(\lambda \gg 1\). It provides a controlled perturbation theory for almost diagonal random matrices, relevant for power-law banded random matrices (PLBRM) at \(\alpha > 1\) (power-law localization) and at \(\alpha = 1\) (critical multifractal eigenstates, studied by Levitov (1990) and Mirlin & Fyodorov (1991)). The formalism enables a rigorous detection of delocalization starting from a diagonal (localized) reference, complementary to conventional field-theoretic methods that start from delocalized diffusive modes.
