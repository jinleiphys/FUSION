# Finite temperature collective modes in a two phase coexistence region of asymmetric nuclear matter

> The relation between collective modes and the phase transition in low density nuclear matter is examined. The dispersion relations for collective modes in a linear approach are evaluated within a Landau-Fermi liquid scheme by assuming coexisting phases in thermodynamical equilibrium. Temperature and isospin composition are taken as relevant parameters. The in-medium nuclear interaction is taken from a recently proposed density functional model. We found significative modifications in the energy spectrum, within certain range of temperatures and isospin asymmetry, due to the separation of matter into independent phases. We conclude that detailed calculations should not neglect this effect.

## Key claim

The paper finds that when a low-density asymmetric nuclear matter system is in a two-phase liquid-gas coexistence region (the binodal), the collective zero-sound modes are supported only by the denser phase, leading to almost constant phase speeds over a wide density range and a stabilization of these modes, in contrast to the unstable modes found when phase separation is neglected.

## Method

The authors use a Kohn-Sham density functional model of the nuclear interaction, fitted to symmetric and pure neutron matter, to construct the equation of state. They determine the binodal coexistence region using the Gibbs conditions for a given temperature and isospin asymmetry. Collective modes are then studied by applying a linearized, collisionless Landau kinetic equation to the stable coexisting phases, solving for the dispersion relation of zero sound modes at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition is found to be \(T_c \simeq 12.8\) MeV.
* For isospin symmetric matter (\(w=0\)), the mixed phase extends up to near the saturation density \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* For symmetric matter, the iso-vector mode has an average damping coefficient \(\delta \approx 0.15\), while the iso-scalar mode has \(\delta \approx 0.09 - 0.19\).
* For asymmetric matter with \(w=0.6\), the neutron-neutron Landau parameter \(F_{2 2}^0\) vanishes around \(n \approx 0.53 n_0\) and remains negative up to \(n \approx 0.81 n_0\), suppressing collective modes in that density range.

## Context

This work extends studies of collective modes and the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, specifically contrasting with prior work by Greco-Colonna and Providencia-Brito, which studied spinodal instability and collective modes in a neutral mixture of nucleons and electrons. By using the coexisting phases (binodal) as the reference state rather than the unstable spinodal region, the authors provide a more realistic description for systems in thermodynamical equilibrium. The results are relevant for understanding neutrino scattering rates in proto-neutron star matter, as the stable collective modes in the denser phase could open a channel for neutrino dispersion, potentially strengthening the effects discussed by Iwamoto (year not given in text).
