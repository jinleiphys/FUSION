# Mott dissociation of pions and kaons in hot, dense quark matter

> We describe the Mott dissociation of pions and kaons within a Beth-Uhlenbeck approach based on the PNJL model, which allows for a unified description of bound, resonant and scattering states. Within this model we evaluate the temperature and chemical potential dependent modification of the phase shifts both in the pseudoscalar and scalar isovector meson channels for $N_f=2+1$ quark flavors. We show that the character change of the pseudoscalar bound states to resonances in the continuum at the Mott transition temperature is signaled by a jump of the phase shift at the threshold from $π$ to zero, in accordance with the Levinson theorem. In particular, we demonstrate the importance of accounting for the scattering continuum states, which ensures that the total phase shift in each of the meson channels vanishes at high energies, thus eliminating mesonic correlations from the thermodynamics at high temperatures. In this way, we prove that the present approach provides a unified description of the transition from a meson gas to a quark-gluon plasma. We discuss the occurrence of an anomalous mode for mesons composed of quarks with unequal masses which is particularly pronounced for $K^+$ and $κ^+$ states at finite densities a a possible mechanism to explain the "horn" effect for the $K^+/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Mott dissociation of pions and kaons in hot, dense quark matter can be described within a Beth-Uhlenbeck approach based on the PNJL model, where the transition from bound states to resonances in the continuum is signaled by a jump of the phase shift at threshold from π to zero in accordance with the Levinson theorem. The authors claim that accounting for scattering continuum states is essential for ensuring that mesonic correlations vanish from thermodynamics at high temperatures, but they note that a quantitative comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics data is premature at this stage due to the lack of hadronic states and missing self-consistency.

## Method

The authors employed a 2+1 flavor PNJL model with a polynomial Polyakov-loop potential, solving the gap equation for dynamical quark masses and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson masses in the pseudoscalar (π, K) and scalar (a₀, κ) isovector channels. They computed the energy-dependent phase shifts for quark-antiquark correlations in each mesonic channel as functions of temperature and chemical potential, and used the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula to evaluate the mesonic pressure contributions along trajectories of constant μ/T = 0, 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 in the phase diagram.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for two-particle correlations from previous two-flavor NJL and PNJL studies (Hufner 1994, Zhuang 1994, Blaschke 2013, Dubinin 2013, Wergieluk 2012, Yamazaki 2012, Yamazaki 2013, Blaschke 2014, Dubinin 2015, Torres-Rincon 2016) to include strangeness degrees of freedom with N_f = 2+1 quark flavors. The approach provides a unified description of the transition from a meson gas to a quark-gluon plasma, and the authors discuss the occurrence of an anomalous mode for mesons composed of quarks with unequal masses (particularly pronounced for K⁺ and κ⁺ states at finite densities) as a possible mechanism to explain the "horn" effect for the K⁺/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions. Future work should include more hadronic states and self-consistency for quantitative comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics.
