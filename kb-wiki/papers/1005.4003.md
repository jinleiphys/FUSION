# Production of exotic atoms at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We study in details the space-time dependence of the production of muonic, pionic, and other exotic atoms by the coherent photon exchange between nuclei at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN. We show that a multipole expansion of the electromagnetic interaction yields an useful insight of the bound-free production mechanism which has not been explored in the literature. Predictions for the spatial, temporal, and angular distribution, as well as the total cross sections, for the production of exotic atoms are also included.

## Key claim

The authors show that a multipole expansion of the electromagnetic interaction provides a useful space-time description of bound-free production of exotic atoms in ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC, and that the electric dipole (E1) multipolarity alone dominates the production cross section due to the extremely large Lorentz factors involved, with the condition that the impact parameter must be larger than the Bohr radius of the exotic atom for the dominant contribution.

## Method

The authors use first-order perturbation theory with the Lienard-Wiechert potential to calculate the amplitude for bound-free pair production, where a negative particle (muon, pion, antiproton) is captured into a K-shell orbit around a proton or lead nucleus while a positive particle is emitted. They separate the electromagnetic interaction into distant collisions (using a multipole expansion of the potential) and close collisions (using a separate potential for impact parameters smaller than the Bohr radius), and compute the production probability by integrating over the free positive particle momentum and summing over spins. The cross section is obtained by integrating the production probability over all impact parameters, using distorted hydrogenic wavefunctions for the captured particle and plane waves with Coulomb corrections for the free particle.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at the LHC, the effective Lorentz factor is Gamma ≈ 10^8; for Pb-Pb collisions, Gamma ≈ 10^7.
- The cross section for muonic atom production in pp collisions is 44.8 × 10^(-4) pb; in Pb-Pb collisions it is 0.16 mb.
- The cross section for pionic atom production in pp collisions is 21.3 × 10^(-4) pb; in Pb-Pb collisions it is 0.09 mb.
- The cross section for kaonic atom production in pp collisions is 1.3 × 10^(-4) pb; in Pb-Pb collisions it is 4.3 μb.
- The cross section for protonium (antiprotonic hydrogen) production in pp collisions is 0.09 × 10^(-4) pb; in Pb-Pb collisions it is 0.3 μb.
- The cross section for hydrogenic (ordinary) atom production in pp collisions is 63.4 pb; in Pb-Pb collisions it is 132 b.
- The production probability at zero impact parameter is 5 orders of magnitude smaller than at b = a_muon = 255 fm (the muonic Bohr radius).
- The maximum impact parameter for muon capture is b_max ≈ 10^8 fm; for antiproton capture it is b_max ≈ 10^7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for bound-free pair production developed by Bertulani and Baur (1988, 1994) and applied to antihydrogen production by Baur et al. (1998), by applying it to the production of muonic, pionic, kaonic, and antiprotonic atoms at LHC energies. The multipole expansion approach provides a clearer space-time picture than previous momentum-space formulations, and the results enable experimental searches for exotic atoms in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, where the predicted cross sections are large enough to produce abundant events for studying their properties and decay widths.
