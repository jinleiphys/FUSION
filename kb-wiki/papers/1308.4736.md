# Comparison of the Color Glass Condensate to multiplicity dependence of the average transverse momentum in p+p, p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> The first moment <p_T> of the charged-particle transverse momentum spectrum and its correlation with the charged-particle multiplicity N_{ch} provide vital information about the underlying particle production mechanism. The ALICE collaboration recently reported that <p_T> versus N_{ch} in Pb+Pb collisions is smaller than in p+p and p+Pb collisions. Other interesting features of data is rather flatness of <p_T> at high N_{ch} in Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions in seemingly striking contrast to the case of p+p collisions. With a detailed calculation, we show all these peculiar features in a wide range of energies and system sizes can be well described by the idea of gluon saturation within the Color Glass Condensate framework using the k_T-factorization. This establishes an important fact that the bulk of the produced particles in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC carries signature of the initial stage of collisions. We also show that the recent scaling property seen by the CMS collaboration between the number of tracks in p+p and p+Pb collisions may provide a strong evidence in favor of geometric-scaling phenomenon and gluon saturation, indicating that the underlying dynamics of high multiplicity events in p+p and p+Pb collisions should be similar.

# Comparison of the Color Glass Condensate to multiplicity dependence of the average transverse momentum in p+p, p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate framework with gluon saturation, implemented via k_T-factorization and the b-CGC dipole saturation model, can describe the multiplicity dependence of the average transverse momentum in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions across a wide range of energies and system sizes, provided that the saturation scale is made multiplicity-dependent via the relation Q²_s,min → (N_ch / ⟨N_ch⟩) Q²_s,min and that the equality ⟨⟨q_T⟩⟩ = ⟨q_T⟩ between multiple-gluon and single-inclusive yields holds deep in the saturation regime.

## Method

The author computes the average transverse momentum of charged particles using k_T-factorization for single-inclusive gluon production, with the unintegrated gluon density obtained from the impact-parameter Color Glass Condensate (b-CGC) dipole saturation model fitted to HERA data. The multiplicity dependence is introduced by scaling the minimum saturation scale linearly with charged-particle multiplicity relative to the minimum-bias average, and hadronization is treated via the Local Parton-Hadron Duality principle with a mini-jet mass parameter and average fragmentation fraction ⟨z⟩ ≈ 0.5–0.6. The three free parameters (overall normalization, mini-jet mass, and intrinsic transverse momentum) are fixed by fitting to minimum-bias p+p data at low energy, making all results at higher energies and for different system sizes parameter-free predictions.

## Key numbers

- ⟨z⟩ ≈ 0.5–0.6 (average fraction of mini-jet energy carried by the hadron)
- ⟨p^intrinsic_T⟩_proton ≈ 0.2–0.45 GeV (intrinsic transverse momentum for proton target)
- ⟨p^intrinsic_T⟩_nucleus ≈ A^{1/6} × ⟨p^intrinsic_T⟩_proton ≈ 0.45 GeV (intrinsic transverse momentum for lead target)
- λ ≈ 0.22 (energy-dependence exponent of the saturation scale, extracted from LHC p+p data)
- K ≈ 1.076 (pre-factor accounting for different center-of-mass energies: (7 TeV / 5.02 TeV)^λ)
- Average minimum-bias charged-particle multiplicities: ⟨N_ch⟩ ≈ 259.9 (Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV), 11.9 (p+Pb at 5.02 TeV), 4.42 (p+p at 7 TeV), for |η| < 0.3 and 0.15 < p_T < 10 GeV
- CMS scaling factor between p+p and p+Pb track multiplicities: approximately 1.8 (i.e., N_tracks^{p+p} ≈ 0.55 × N_tracks^{p+Pb})

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate phenomenology of Rezaeian (2012, 2013) to the multiplicity dependence of average transverse momentum, addressing a challenge where other models fail: final-state color reconnection models (e.g., PYTHIA) describe p+p but not p+Pb or Pb+Pb data, while the EPOS model with collective flow over-predicts Pb+Pb data and gives the opposite trend. The paper also shows that the CMS scaling between p+p and p+Pb track multiplicities, observed for identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons), follows from geometric scaling and the saturation scale relation N_tracks ∝ S_⊥ Q²_s,min, using interaction areas from the IP-Glasma model of Schenke et al. (2012). This enables a unified description of particle production across collision systems and suggests that high-multiplicity events in p+p and p+Pb collisions share similar underlying dynamics driven by gluon saturation.
