# On the mechanism of formation of the Efimov states in the helium ^4He trimer

> A mechanism of disappearance and formation of the Efimov levels of the helium ^4He trimer is studied when the force of the interatomic interaction is changed. It is shown that these levels arise from virtual levels which are in turn formed from (quasi)resonances settled on the real axis. The resonances including virtual levels are calculated by the method based on the solution of the boundary value problem, at complex energies, for the Faddeev differential equations describing the scattering processes (2+1\to 2+1; 1+1+1). All the calculations are performed with the known interatomic Aziz He-He potential HFD-B. A very strong repulsive component of this potential at short distances between helium atoms is approximated by a hard core. A special attention is paid to the substantiation of the method used for computing resonances and to the investigation of its applicability range.

## Key claim

The Efimov excited state of the $^4$He trimer disappears when the interatomic potential is multiplied by an amplification factor $\lambda \approx 1.18$, merging with the dimer threshold $\epsilon_d$ and transforming into a virtual level in the unphysical energy sheet; for $\lambda < 1$, new excited Efimov states arise from virtual levels that themselves form from pairs of conjugate (quasi)resonances fusing on the real axis.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev differential equations in configuration space for the $^4$He$_3$ system with total angular momentum $L=0$, using the Aziz HFD-B He-He potential with its strong short-range repulsion approximated by a hard core. They analytically continued the elastic scattering matrix $S_0(z)$ into the complex energy plane by solving the boundary value problem at complex energies, identifying resonances and virtual levels as roots of $S_0(z)$ in the physical sheet. The method's applicability was restricted to a parabolic domain $\Pi^{(S)}$ in the complex plane defined by the condition $\operatorname{Im}\sqrt{z-\epsilon_d} < \min\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\sqrt{|\epsilon_d|},\,\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\mu\right\}$, where $\mu$ characterizes the exponential decay of the potential.

## Key numbers

- The dimer binding energy $\epsilon_d$ is approximately $-1$ mK.
- The trimer excited level $E_t^{(1)}$ merges with the dimer threshold $\epsilon_d$ at $\lambda \approx 1.18$.
- For $\lambda$ decreasing by no more than 2% below 1, a second excited state $E_t^{(2)}$ appears in the trimer.
- The excited (Efimov) state disappears when the interatomic potential is multiplied by an amplification factor $\lambda$ of order 1.2.
- The domain $\Pi^{(S)}$ for analytic continuation of $S_0(z)$ is bounded by the parabola $\operatorname{Re} z > -\frac{4}{3}|\epsilon_d| + \frac{3}{4|\epsilon_d|}(\operatorname{Im} z)^2$ when $\mu > \frac{2}{3}\sqrt{|\epsilon_d|}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the $^4$He$_3$ system (Kolganova, Motovilov, and collaborators, 1998) using the Boundary-Condition Model with hard-core approximation, and confirms the Efimov nature of the trimer excited state previously reported by Esry, Lin, and Greene (1996), Glöckle (1997), and the authors' own work (1998). The paper provides a detailed mechanism for how Efimov levels appear and disappear as the interatomic coupling strength is varied, showing they arise from virtual levels that themselves originate from (quasi)resonances. This enables future studies of three-body recombination rates and the behavior of helium clusters near the Efimov threshold, and provides a rigorous mathematical framework for computing resonances in three-body systems with hard-core potentials.
