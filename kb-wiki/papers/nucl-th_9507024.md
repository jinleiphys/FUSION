# Realistic Model of the Nucleon Spectral Function in Few- and Many- Nucleon Systems

> By analysing the high momentum features of the nucleon momentum distribution in light and complex nuclei, it is argued that the basic two-nucleon configurations generating the structure of the nucleon Spectral Function at high values of the nucleon momentum and removal energy, can be properly described by a factorised ansatz for the nuclear wave function, which leads to a nucleon Spectral Function in the form of a convolution integral involving the momentum distributions describing the relative and center-of-mass motion of a correlated nucleon-nucleon pair embedded in the medium. The Spectral Functions of $^3He$ and infinite nuclear matter resulting from the convolution formula and from many-body calculations are compared, and a very good agreement in a wide range of values of nucleon momentum and removal energy is found. Applications of the model to the analysis of inclusive and exclusive processes are presented, illustrating those features of the cross section which are sensitive to that part of the Spectral Function which is governed by short-range and tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the high-momentum and high-removal-energy parts of the nucleon Spectral Function in any nucleus can be accurately described by a factorized ansatz where a correlated nucleon-nucleon pair with high relative momentum and low center-of-mass momentum is embedded in a mean field created by the remaining (A-2) nucleons, provided the center-of-mass motion of the pair is treated as a 0s state.

## Method
The authors derive a convolution formula for the nucleon Spectral Function P(k,E) by expressing the nuclear ground-state wave function as an antisymmetrized product of a relative-motion wave function for a correlated NN pair, a 0s center-of-mass wave function for the pair, and a wave function for the (A-2) spectator system. They then compare the Spectral Functions of ³He and infinite nuclear matter obtained from this convolution formula with results from many-body calculations using realistic NN interactions such as the Reid Soft Core potential, the Paris potential, and the Argonne v14 interaction.

## Key numbers
- The minimum removal energy E_min is 2.226 MeV for the deuteron, 5.5 MeV for ³He, and 19.8 MeV for ⁴He.
- The spectroscopic factor S_0 for the ground-to-ground transition is approximately 0.65 for ³He and approximately 0.8 for ⁴He, implying S_1 values of approximately 0.35 and 0.2 respectively.
- For infinite nuclear matter, many-body calculations yield S_0 approximately 0.75, meaning S_1 approximately 0.25.
- The high-momentum tail of n(k) for k > 2 fm⁻¹ is similar for all nuclei and is essentially given by the deuteron momentum distribution times an A-dependent scale factor, with plateaux heights of approximately 2 for the proton in ³He, approximately 4 in ⁴He and ¹⁶O, approximately 4.5 in ⁵⁶Fe, and approximately 5 in nuclear matter.
- The threshold momentum separating low- and high-momentum regimes is approximately k = 1.5 fm⁻¹ for the momentum distribution and approximately k = 2.0 fm⁻¹ for the deuteron-like tail.

## Context
This work extends the convolution model first sketched in Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1991) to any mass number A, providing a general formulation applicable to both few-nucleon systems and complex nuclei. It builds upon prior many-body calculations of the Spectral Function for ³He using Faddeev or variational wave functions, and for infinite nuclear matter using the orthogonal correlated basis approach and perturbation expansions of the one-nucleon propagator. The model enables the analysis of inclusive and exclusive quasi-elastic electron scattering processes, particularly those sensitive to short-range and tensor NN correlations, and provides a practical tool for interpreting experiments such as A(e,e'p)X knockout reactions where multi-nucleon emission from correlations is observed.
