# Consequences of increased hypertriton binding for $s$-shell $Λ$-hypernuclear systems

> Consequences of increasing the binding energy of the hypertriton ground state ${_Λ^3}{\rm H}(J^P={\frac{1}{2}}^+)$ from the emulsion value $B^{\rm EMUL}_Λ({_Λ^3}{\rm H}_{\rm g.s.})$=0.13$\pm$0.05 MeV to the STAR value $B^{\rm STAR}_Λ(^{3}_Λ{\rm H}) = (0.41\pm 0.12 \pm 0.11)$ MeV are studied for $s$-shell hypernuclei within a pionless EFT approach at leading order, constrained by the binding energies of the $0^+$ and $1^+$ ${_Λ^4} {\rm H}$ states. The stochastic variational method is used in bound-state calculations, whereas the inverse analytic continuation in the coupling constant method is used to locate $S$-matrix poles of continuum states. It is found that the $Λnn({\frac{1}{2}}^+)$ resonance becomes broader and less likely to be observed experimentally, whereas the ${_Λ^3}{\rm H}({\frac{3}{2}}^+)$ spin-flip virtual state moves closer to the $Λd$ threshold to become a shallow bound state for specific $ΛN$ interaction strengths. The effect of such a near-threshold ${_Λ^3}{\rm H}({\frac{3}{2}}^+)$ state on femtoscopic studies of $Λ$-deuteron correlations, and its lifetime if bound, are discussed. Increasing $B_Λ({_Λ^3} {\rm H}_{\rm g.s.})$ moderately, up to $\sim$0.5 MeV, hardly affects calculated values of $B_Λ({_Λ^5}{\rm He})$.

## Key claim

Increasing the hypertriton ground state binding energy from the emulsion value of 0.13±0.05 MeV to the STAR value of 0.41±0.12±0.11 MeV, within a pionless EFT approach at leading order constrained by the 0+ and 1+ states of 4ΛH, makes the Λnn(1/2+) resonance broader and less likely to be observed, while the 3ΛH(3/2+) spin-flip virtual state moves closer to the Λd threshold and may become a shallow bound state for specific ΛN interaction strengths.

## Method

The authors used leading-order pionless effective field theory (πEFT) with Gaussian regularization, fitting two-body ΛN low-energy constants to spin-singlet and spin-triplet scattering lengths from various interaction models, and three-body ΛNN constants to the hypertriton binding energy (either emulsion or STAR value) plus the experimental Λ separation energies of 4ΛH(0+) and 4ΛH*(1+). Bound-state calculations employed the stochastic variational method (SVM), while continuum-state S-matrix poles were located using the inverse analytic continuation in the coupling constant (IACCC) method.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton ground state binding energy: emulsion value BEMULΛ(3ΛHg.s.) = 0.13±0.05 MeV; STAR value BSTARΛ(3ΛH) = (0.41±0.12±0.11) MeV
- Experimental Λ separation energy of 5ΛHe: BexpΛ(5ΛHe) = 3.12(2) MeV (Davis 2005)
- Experimental Λ separation energy of 4ΛH(0+): BΛ(4ΛH;0+) = 2.16(8) MeV
- Excitation energy of 4ΛH*(1+): Eexc(4ΛH*;1+) = 1.09(2) MeV
- Relative difference Δ(5ΛHe) between calculations using emulsion versus STAR hypertriton constraints is less than approximately 16% for all considered ΛN models and cutoff values
- Lower bound on Λnn resonance width: Γ(Λnn) > 0.8 MeV, estimated from LO calculations including different BΛ(3ΛH) values, experimental errors, spread of ΛN scattering lengths, and cutoff variation

## Context

This work extends previous LO πEFT calculations of s-shell hypernuclear trios by Schafer et al. (2020, 2021), which predicted Λnn and 3ΛH*(3/2+) to be unbound, and addresses the "overbinding problem" of 5ΛHe first noted by Dalitz and Herndon (1972) and recently resolved within LO πEFT by Contessi et al. (2018). The study is motivated by the STAR collaboration's larger hypertriton binding energy (STAR 2020) and the HypHI collaboration's evidence for a bound Λnn state (HypHI 2013). The results enable future femtoscopic studies of Λ-deuteron correlations and inform proposed experiments at MAMI, JLab, and J-PARC aiming to resolve the hypertriton lifetime puzzle.
