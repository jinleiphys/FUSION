# Annihilation of $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-+ π^0$ and $\bar p+p\to γ+ π^0$ through $ω$-meson intermediate state

> The s-channel annihilation of proton and antiproton into a neutral pion and a real or virtual photon followed by lepton pair emission is studied. Such mechanism is expected to play a role at moderate values of the total energy $\sqrt{s}$, when the pion is emitted around $90^{\circ}$ in the center of mass. A fair comparison with the existing data is obtained taking into account scattering and annihilation channels. The cross section is calculated and numerical results are given in the kinematical range accessible in the PANDA experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim

The s-channel annihilation mechanism through an intermediate ω-meson is expected to dominate the cross section for the reactions p̄ + p → γ + π⁰ and p̄ + p → e⁺ + e⁻ + π⁰ only at moderate total energy √s and when the pion is emitted near 90° in the center-of-mass frame; outside this angular region, t- and u-channel scattering exchanges with Regge suppression must be included to reproduce existing data.

## Method

The authors calculated the matrix element for the s-channel annihilation through an intermediate ω-meson, using a vector current parametrized by a single form factor F₁^ω (neglecting F₂^ω) and an anomalous ωπγ vertex described either by a triangle quark-loop integral or a phenomenological monopole parametrization. For the real-photon reaction, they also computed t- and u-channel nucleon-exchange diagrams with Regge suppression factors and compared the combined model to angular distributions and integrated cross sections from the Fermilab E760 Collaboration. For the virtual-photon reaction leading to e⁺e⁻ pairs, they derived the double differential cross section as a function of pion energy and angle at fixed total energy.

## Key numbers

- The ω-meson mass used is M_ω = 782.65 ± 0.19 MeV, with total width Γ_ω = 8.49 ± 0.08 MeV.
- The ωNN coupling constant is g²_ωNN/(4π) = 20, and the cutoff parameter for the ωNN form factor is Λ_ω = 1.25 GeV.
- The calculated branching ratio for ω → π⁰γ is 6.5%, compared to the experimental value (8.28 ± 0.28)% from the Particle Data Group.
- The E760 data cover the range 2.911 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 3.686 GeV for the reaction p̄ + p → γ + π⁰.
- The Coulomb factor at √s = 2 GeV is C = 1.6, with relative velocity v = 0.34 c.
- The double differential cross section for p̄ + p → γ* + π⁰ is shown at q² = 7 GeV² as a function of pion energy E_π and cos θ_π.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Dubnickova (1995) near threshold and by Adamuscin (2007) that introduced a larger set of diagrams for the same reactions, and it provides a model for the s-channel annihilation mechanism that is complementary to the Generalized Distribution Amplitudes approach of Kroll (2005) and the transition distribution amplitude framework of Pire (2005). The results are directly applicable to the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where antiproton beams with momentum from 1.5 to 15 GeV/c and average luminosity 1.6 × 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹ will be available, enabling future measurements of time-like proton form factors and vector meson properties.
