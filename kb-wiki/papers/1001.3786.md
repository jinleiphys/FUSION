# Comment to "On the field-theoretical approach to the neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei"

> We briefly outline the present state of the $n\bar{n}$ transition problem and analyse the recent manuscript of the Vladimir Kopeliovich (arXiv: 0912.5065).

# Comment to "On the field-theoretical approach to the neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei"

## Key claim

The author argues that the diagram-based field-theoretical model for neutron-antineutron (n-nbar) transitions in nuclei, as presented in Kopeliovich (arXiv:0912.5065) and earlier works, is fundamentally incorrect because it uses a propagator for the neutron line entering the n-nbar transition vertex instead of a wave function, leading to a cardinal error and an infrared divergence that the author claims cannot arise in that model.

## Method

The paper reviews the history of theoretical approaches to n-nbar oscillations in nuclear matter, contrasting the standard potential model (where interactions are described by potentials U_a and U_b, with ImU_b accounting for antineutron absorption) with diagram-based models. The author identifies a critical flaw in the diagram technique: the neutron line entering the n-nbar transition vertex must be a wave function, not a propagator, and using a propagator leads to an infrared divergence when the neutron binding energy goes to zero, as shown in Eqs. (18)-(19) of Nazaruk (2003) and Eqs. (15) and (17) of a subsequent repetition. The author states that the model in Kopeliovich (2009) repeats these earlier erroneous calculations with nothing new except Section 5, which the author claims is completely wrong.

## Key numbers

- The infrared divergence in the diagram-based model occurs when the neutron binding energy goes to zero, as shown in Eqs. (18) and (19) of Nazaruk (2003) and Eqs. (15) and (17) of a later repetition.
- No other numerical results are presented in this comment paper.

## Context

This comment extends a long-standing debate on the correct theoretical treatment of n-nbar oscillations in nuclei, following the standard potential model approach of Dover-Gal (1983) and subsequent works. The author previously proposed a diagram-based model in Nazaruk (2003) but later abandoned it in Nazaruk (2008) after identifying the infrared divergence problem. The comment criticizes Kopeliovich (2009) for repeating these abandoned calculations, and contrasts this with the author's own corrected model in Nazaruk (2008) and Nazaruk (2010), which uses a wave function for the neutron line and inevitably produces an infrared singularity for plane waves or bound-state wave functions.
