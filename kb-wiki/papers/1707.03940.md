# Neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements in large shell-model spaces with the generator-coordinate method

> We use the generator-coordinate method with realistic shell-model interactions to closely approximate full shell-model calculations of the matrix elements for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, and $^{82}$Se. We work in one major shell for the first isotope, in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space for the second and third, and finally in two major shells for all three. Our coordinates include not only the usual axial deformation parameter $β$, but also the triaxiality angle $γ$ and neutron-proton pairing amplitudes. In the smaller model spaces our matrix elements agree well with those of full shell-model diagonalization, suggesting that our Hamiltonian-based GCM captures most of the important valence-space correlations. In two major shells, where exact diagonalization is not currently possible, our matrix elements are only slightly different from those in a single shell.

## Key claim

The generator-coordinate method (GCM) with realistic shell-model interactions, including triaxial deformation and neutron-proton pairing as generator coordinates, can closely approximate full shell-model calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements in spaces where exact diagonalization is possible; in two-major-shell spaces where exact diagonalization is not currently feasible, the resulting matrix elements are only slightly different from those in a single shell, suggesting that enlarging the space further may not dramatically change the matrix element.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using realistic shell-model effective Hamiltonians, with generator coordinates including the axial quadrupole moment Q20, the triaxial quadrupole moment Q22, and either isoscalar or isovector neutron-proton pairing amplitudes. They then projected the resulting HFB vacua onto good angular momentum, neutron number, and proton number, and solved the Hill-Wheeler equations to obtain the GCM ground states. The neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element was computed in the closure approximation between the lowest J=0 states of the initial and final nuclei, using the Argonne correlation function for short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca in the pf-shell with the KB3G interaction, the GCM matrix element including isoscalar pairing agreed closely with exact diagonalization (figure shown, exact value not given in text)
- For 76Ge in one shell with the GCN2850 interaction: axial GCM gave M0nu = 2.71, triaxial GCM gave 2.33, exact diagonalization gave 2.81 (from Menendez 2008)
- For 76Ge in one shell with the JUN45 interaction: axial GCM gave M0nu = 3.42, triaxial GCM gave 2.94, exact diagonalization gave 3.37 (from Senkov 2014)
- For 76Ge in two shells with the pfsdg interaction: axial GCM total M0nu = 3.65, full (triaxial) GCM total M0nu = 2.34
- For 48Ca in two shells with the SDPFMU-DB interaction: GCM result 1.082, compared to 1.073 from Iwata (2016)
- B(E2; 0+ to 2+) values in one shell with GCN2850: 0.172 e2b2 in 76Ge (exact 0.158) and 0.275 e2b2 in 76Se (exact 0.209); without triaxial deformation: 0.154 in 76Ge and 0.268 in 76Se
- B(E2) values in two shells: 0.126 e2b2 in 76Ge and 0.221 e2b2 in 76Se (experimental values 0.274 and 0.432 respectively)
- Minimum deformation parameters for 76Ge with GCN2850: beta2 = 0.23, gamma = 24 degrees; for 76Se: beta2 = 0.28, gamma = 45 degrees

## Context

This work extends the GCM approach of Hinohara (2014) by including triaxial deformation as a generator coordinate and using realistic shell-model interactions rather than simple pairing-plus-quadrupole forces. It provides the first realistic shell-model-like calculation of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements for 76Ge and 82Se in more than one major shell, and the first exploration of triaxiality effects on double-beta decay. The results enable future work using better effective Hamiltonians from normal-ordering with respect to ensemble references (Stroberg 2017) or multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group methods.
