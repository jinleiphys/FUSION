# Wavelet analysis of monopole strength in highly deformed $^{24}$Mg

> Experimental data on $α$-particle inelastic scattering for monopole excitations in $^{24}$Mg in the excitation-energy region $E_{\rm x}$$=$$9$$-$$25$ MeV, obtained at the iThemba Laboratory for Accelerator Based Sciences (iThemba LABS), have been analyzed within a fully self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) framework using two Skyrme parametrizations. A good overall agreement with the experimental data is achieved, particularly with the SkP$^δ$ force, which corresponds to a low nuclear incompressibility of $K_{\infty}$$=$$202$ MeV. Extraction of energy scales, by means of wavelet analysis, characterizing the observed fine structure of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) as well as the low-energy region $10$$-$$18$ MeV of the deformation-induced monopole-quadrupole coupling (MQC) in order to investigate the damping mechanism contributing to their decay widths. Characteristic energy scales are extracted from the fine structure using continuous wavelet transforms. The experimental results are compared to QRPA calculations employing the Skyrme parameterizations SkP$^δ$ and SVbas. A significant, if not decisive, impact of the MQC strength on the wavelet power spectra is observed across the entire excitation-energy range of $10$$-$$24$ MeV. Wavelet features derived from the QRPA and from unperturbed two-quasiparticle (2qp) monopole strengths are compared. The results demonstrate that the residual interaction plays a key role in reproducing realistic wavelet powers and characteristic energy scales. Overall, a continuous range of scales $δE$$=$$200$$-$$1000$ keV is obtained rather than distinct isolated scales. The deformation softness of $^{24}$Mg is found to significantly influence both the monopole strength distribution and the wavelet characteristics.

# Wavelet analysis of monopole strength in highly deformed $^{24}$Mg

## Key claim
The wavelet analysis demonstrates that the deformation-induced monopole-quadrupole coupling (MQC) predominantly determines the fine structure and characteristic energy scales across the entire $10$–$24$ MeV excitation-energy region in $^{24}$Mg, with Landau damping confirmed as the main decay mechanism of the ISGMR; however, remaining discrepancies between experiment and QRPA calculations highlight the possible importance of couplings to more complex configurations beyond the one-phonon approximation.

## Method
Experimental isoscalar monopole strength distributions in $^{24}$Mg were extracted from $196$ MeV $\alpha$-particle inelastic scattering data measured at the iThemba LABS K$600$ magnetic spectrometer using the Difference-of-Spectra technique, achieving approximately $70$ keV energy resolution. Continuous wavelet transforms with a complex-Morlet mother wavelet were applied to both the experimental strength functions and fully self-consistent QRPA calculations using the Skyrme parametrizations SkP$^{\delta}$ and SVbas, with characteristic energy scales extracted from wavelet power spectra summed over selected excitation-energy intervals.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear incompressibility $K_{\infty}=202$ MeV for SkP$^{\delta}$ and $K_{\infty}=234$ MeV for SVbas
- Experimental quadrupole deformation $\beta=0.613$ for $^{24}$Mg; calculated $\beta=0.545$ (SkP$^{\delta}$) and $\beta=0.527$ (SVbas)
- Experimental binding energy per nucleon $BE/A=8.26$ MeV; calculated $7.82$ MeV (SkP$^{\delta}$) and $8.02$ MeV (SVbas)
- Continuous range of characteristic energy scales $\delta E=200$–$1000$ keV obtained from wavelet analysis across $10$–$24$ MeV excitation energy
- Experimental dominant scales in the full $10$–$24$ MeV region: $270$, $360$, $510$, $710$, $1150$, $1600$ keV
- SkP$^{\delta}$ dominant scales in the full $10$–$24$ MeV region: $160$, $300$, $510$, $800$, $1400$ keV
- SVbas dominant scales in the full $10$–$24$ MeV region: $185$, $270$, $450$, $650$, $1300$ keV
- Experimental dominant scales in the ISGMR region $18$–$24$ MeV: $230$, $320$, $530$, $870$, $1300$, $2000$ keV
- Energy resolution of $\Delta E=70$ keV FWHM and energy grid step of $dE=10$ keV used for wavelet analysis
- QRPA calculations exhausted $97$–$99$% of the isoscalar energy-weighted sum rule EWSR(IS$0$)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the ISGMR in $^{24}$Mg by Bahini et al. (2022) and Adsley et al. (2021), which analyzed the monopole strength distributions and MQC effects using the same experimental data and QRPA framework. The wavelet analysis methodology follows the approach developed for giant resonances by Shevchenko et al. (2008, 2009) and applied to the ISGQR in spherical and deformed nuclei, the IVGDR in sd-shell nuclei, and the ISGMR in spherical systems by Bahini et al. (2024). The results confirm the dominant role of Landau damping in deformed nuclei, consistent with findings for the IVGDR by Donaldson et al. (2018) and Kureba et al. (2018), while the remaining discrepancies between experiment and QRPA point toward the need for including couplings to more complex configurations, as demonstrated for spherical nuclei by Colo et al. (2023).
