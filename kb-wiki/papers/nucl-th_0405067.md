# Large transverse momentum suppression of $π^0$'s in Au+Au and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV

> We propose a model of suppression of $π^0$'s based on two different effects: at low $p_T$ we take into account the shadowing corrections, while at large $p_T$ the suppression is produced due to the interaction of the large $p_T$ pion with the dense medium created in the collision. The main features of the data on $AuAu$ and $dAu$ collisions are reproduced both at mid and at forward rapidities.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) \(\pi^0\) yields in Au+Au collisions at RHIC arises from two distinct mechanisms: shadowing corrections at low \(p_T\) and final-state interactions (a \(p_T\) shift due to scattering in the dense medium) at high \(p_T\), with the latter effect accounting for the persistence of suppression at large transverse momenta where shadowing alone would vanish.

## Method
The authors model the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) using a two-component approach. At low \(p_T\), nonlinear shadowing corrections are computed from diffractive cross sections via a triple-Pomeron formalism. At high \(p_T\), final-state interactions are described by gain-and-loss differential equations for the \(\pi^0\) density in a longitudinally expanding medium, assuming a constant interaction cross-section \(\widetilde{\sigma} \sim 1\) mb and a \(p_T\) shift \(\delta p_T\) proportional to \((p_T - \langle p_T \rangle_b)\). The \(\pi^0\) \(p_T\) distribution in pp collisions is parametrized as \((1 + p_T/p_0)^{-n}\) with \(n = 9.99\) and \(p_0 = 1.219\) GeV/c, and the same shape is used for Au+Au and d+Au collisions with centrality-dependent \(p_0\).

## Key numbers
- In central Au+Au collisions (10% most central, \(n_{part} = 350\), \(b = 2\) fm), the average \(p_T\) is \(\langle p_T \rangle_{exp} = 0.453\) GeV/c, giving \(p_0 = 1.583\) GeV/c.
- In pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV, the \(\pi^0\) \(p_T\) distribution is fitted with \(n = 9.99\) and \(p_0 = 1.219\) GeV/c, yielding \(\langle p_T \rangle = 0.349\) GeV/c.
- With a constant \(p_T\) shift \(\delta p_T = 0.5\) or 1.5 GeV/c, the model gives \(R_{AuAu}^{\pi^0}(p_T = 7\) GeV/c) = 0.24 for central collisions, consistent with data.
- With a \(p_T\)-dependent shift \(\delta p_T \propto (p_T - \langle p_T \rangle_b)\), the model yields \(R_{AuAu}^{\pi^0}(p_T = 0.5\) GeV/c) = 0.34–0.38 and \(R_{AuAu}^{\pi^0}(p_T = 10\) GeV/c) = 0.24–0.25 for central collisions.
- 75% of the suppression effect occurs in the partonic phase (from \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm to \(\tau_p = 3.36\) fm), and 25% in the hadronic phase (from \(\tau_p = 3.36\) fm to \(\tau_f = 5-7\) fm).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of jet quenching and shadowing in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, specifically addressing the failure of perturbative QCD (which predicts \(R_{AA} \to 1\) at high \(p_T\)) to explain the persistent suppression observed by PHENIX. The model reproduces both Au+Au and d+Au data at mid and forward rapidities, including the nuclear attenuation and shadowing effects seen by BRAHMS. It enables a quantitative separation of partonic and hadronic contributions to energy loss, and provides a framework for interpreting future measurements of \(\pi^0\) and other hadron suppression at higher \(p_T\) and different collision systems.
