# Phenomenological model of multiphoto-production of charged pion pairs on the proton

> The production of charged pion pairs via multiphoton absorption from an intense X-ray laser wave colliding with an ultrarelativistic proton beam is studied. Our calculations include the contributions from both the electromagnetic and hadronic interactions where the latter are described approximately by a phenomenological Yukawa potential. Order-of-magnitude estimates for $π^+π^-$ production on the proton by two- and three-photon absorption from the high-frequency laser field are obtained and compared with the corresponding rates for $μ^+μ^-$ pair creation.

## Key claim

The paper provides order-of-magnitude estimates for charged pion pair production via two- and three-photon absorption in ultrarelativistic proton-laser collisions, using a simplified phenomenological Yukawa potential to approximate the hadronic interaction, with the caveat that the model is only reliable up to approximately 480 MeV photon energy in the proton rest frame.

## Method

The authors combine laser-dressed QED (using scalar Volkov states for the pions) with an effective Yukawa potential for the hadronic pion-nucleon interaction, treating the coupling constant g as a free parameter. They calculate total production rates and angular distributions for π⁺π⁻ pairs created by multiphoton absorption in the collision of an ultrarelativistic proton beam (γ=7000) with an intense X-ray laser wave, and compare these with corresponding μ⁺μ⁻ pair creation rates.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the effective Yukawa potential was set to g=4.3 to reproduce the measured π⁺π⁻ photoproduction cross section at 450 MeV.
- The threshold for two-photon π⁺π⁻ production lies at an X-ray laser frequency of ω_lab ≈ m_π/2γ ≈ 10 keV in the laboratory frame (with γ=7000).
- At a photon energy of 200 MeV in the proton frame, the π⁺π⁻ production rate by two-photon absorption is R ∼ 10³ s⁻¹.
- For a proton beam of 10¹¹ particles colliding with a 100 fs X-ray pulse, the probability for producing one pion pair is 10⁻³, yielding an average rate of 10 events per second at 10 kHz pulse repetition.
- Two-photon π⁺π⁻ production dominates over direct μ⁺μ⁻ production by three orders of magnitude at 200 MeV photon energy.
- The dominance of π⁺π⁻ over μ⁺μ⁻ production holds in the frequency range approximately 150–210 MeV.
- The angular distribution maximum shifts from θ≈29° for one-photon absorption to θ≈35° for two-photon and θ≈38° for three-photon absorption (total absorbed energy 360 MeV in all cases).

## Context

This work extends the study of multiphoton pair creation from QED processes (e⁺e⁻ and μ⁺μ⁻) into hadronic physics, building on prior theoretical work on laser-induced muon pair creation by Kuchiev and Deneke, and on experimental π⁺π⁻ photoproduction studies at MAMI (Daphne detector), GRAAL, and Jefferson Laboratory. The results suggest that in the 150–210 MeV photon energy range, muons are predominantly produced indirectly via two-photon pion pair creation followed by pion decay, rather than by direct muon pair creation. The model enables future studies of multiphoton pion production as a probe of short-range nuclear structure and polarization observables.
