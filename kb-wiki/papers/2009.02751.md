# Measurements of heavy-flavor jets with ALICE at the LHC

> Heavy quarks created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are mostly produced in hard QCD processes during the early stages of the reaction. They interact with the hot and cold nuclear matter throughout the evolution of the medium via semi-hard and soft processes such as energy loss via gluon radiations and collisions. Nuclear modification of heavy flavors in p-A systems provides insight into cold nuclear matter effects such as (anti)shadowing and $k_{T}$-broadening, and serves as a baseline for A-A studies. In addition to that the fully reconstructed heavy-flavor jets provide additional information on the flavor (or mass) dependence of fragmentation, color charge effects as well as insight into the contribution of late gluon splitting. In this contribution, we present the measurements of $b$-jet production in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV and $c$-jet production in pp, p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions measured by the ALICE experiment at the LHC. The measurements of the nuclear modification factors for $c$-jet in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions are also presented. The experimental measurements are compared with the results from Monte Carlo event generators (PYTHIA 6, PYTHIA 8 and Herwig 7) and the NLO pQCD calculations (POWHEG+PYTHIA6). We find good agreement of the measurements with the results from Monte Carlo event generators and from NLO pQCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents first ALICE measurements of heavy-flavor jet production in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, finding that the nuclear modification factor for heavy-flavor jets in p-Pb collisions is consistent with unity within uncertainties, which implies negligible cold nuclear matter effects, while the strong suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions confirms final-state in-medium energy loss.

## Method

Heavy-flavor jets were reconstructed using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with resolution parameter \(R = 0.4\) from charged tracks in the ALICE detector. \(b\)-jets were identified via reconstruction of displaced secondary vertices using significance of the decay length (\(SL_{xy}\)) and secondary vertex resolution (\(\sigma_{SV}\)) cuts. \(c\)-jets were tagged by reconstructing \(\mathrm{D}^0\) mesons via their hadronic decay channel \(\mathrm{D}^0 \rightarrow K^{-} \pi^{+}\) (and charge conjugate), while heavy-flavor electron (HFe) tagged jets used electron identification via specific energy loss in the TPC combined with TOF and EMCal. The measured cross sections were corrected for tagging efficiency, purity, detector effects via unfolding, and compared with NLO pQCD calculations (POWHEG+PYTHIA6) and Monte Carlo event generators (PYTHIA 6, PYTHIA 8, Herwig 7).

## Key numbers

- \(b\)-jet production cross section measured in p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV for charged jet \(p_T\) range \(10 < p_{T,\mathrm{jet}}^{\mathrm{ch}} < 100\) GeV/\(c\)
- \(\mathrm{D}^0\)-meson tagged jet cross section measured in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\), 7, and 13 TeV for charged jet \(p_T\) range \(5 < p_{T,\mathrm{jet}}^{\mathrm{ch}} < 30\) GeV/\(c\)
- HFe tagged jet cross section measured in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV with HFe momentum range \(4 < p_{T,e} < 18\) GeV/\(c\)
- \(z_{||}^{\mathrm{ch}}\)-differential cross section of \(\mathrm{D}^0\)-meson tagged jets measured for two jet momentum intervals: \(5 < p_{T,\mathrm{jet}}^{\mathrm{ch}} < 15\) GeV/\(c\) and \(15 < p_{T,\mathrm{jet}}^{\mathrm{ch}} < 30\) GeV/\(c\) in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{pPb}\) for HFe tagged jets in p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV consistent with unity over the full measured \(p_T\) range
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for \(\mathrm{D}^0\)-meson tagged jets in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV for centrality 0-20% shows strong suppression below unity

## Context

This work extends ALICE heavy-flavor measurements to fully reconstructed jets, complementing earlier open-charm and open-beauty meson measurements that provided information on jet quenching and collective motion. The \(b\)-jet cross section in p-Pb collisions agrees with NLO pQCD calculations (POWHEG+PYTHIA6), while the \(\mathrm{D}^0\)-meson tagged jet cross sections in pp collisions show good agreement with POWHEG heavy-quark implementation but the POWHEG di-jet implementation systematically overestimates the yield at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV by a constant factor of approximately 1.5. The \(z_{||}^{\mathrm{ch}}\) measurements show softer fragmentation as jet \(p_T\) increases, consistent with inclusive jet measurements by ATLAS (2011). The \(R_{pPb}\) results establish that cold nuclear matter effects are negligible for heavy-flavor jets, enabling the interpretation of the observed \(R_{AA}\) suppression in Pb-Pb collisions as a pure final-state effect from the quark-gluon plasma.
