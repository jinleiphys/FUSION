# Quantum Anomalous Energy Effects on the Nucleon Mass

> Apart from the quark and gluon kinetic and potential energies, the nucleon mass includes a novel energy of pure quantum origin resulting from anomalous breaking of scale symmetry. We demonstrate the effects of this quantum anomalous energy (QAE) in QED, as well as in a toy 1+1 dimensional non-linear sigma model where it contributes non-perturbatively, in a way resembling the Higgs mechanism for the masses of matter particles in electro-weak theory. The QAE contribution to the nucleon mass can be explained using a similar mechanism, in terms of a dynamical response of the gluonic scalar field through Higgs-like couplings between the nucleon and scalar resonances. In addition, the QAE sets the scale for other energies in the nucleon through a relativistic virial theorem, and contributes a negative pressure to confine the colored quarks.

# Quantum Anomalous Energy Effects on the Nucleon Mass

## Key claim

The nucleon mass contains a quantum anomalous energy (QAE) contribution arising from the anomalous breaking of scale symmetry, which sets the scale for other energy components through a relativistic virial theorem and contributes non-perturbatively to the mass through a dynamical Higgs-like mechanism involving the gluonic scalar field's response to the nucleon, with the coupling to scalar resonances proportional to the fermion mass.

## Method

The authors decompose the QCD Hamiltonian into tensor and scalar parts using the symmetric energy-momentum tensor, where the scalar part originates entirely from scale-breaking effects. They demonstrate the QAE concept first in QED perturbation theory, calculating its contribution to the Lamb shift, then in a large-N 1+1 dimensional non-linear sigma model where the anomalous Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of an auxiliary scalar field and its contribution to the pion mass is computed via the zero-momentum propagator of the sigma field. For the nucleon, they relate the QAE contribution to the response of the gluonic scalar field \(\phi(x) = \Phi(x) - \Phi_0\) and express it through a dispersion relation involving scalar resonance masses, decay constants, and nucleon-scalar couplings.

## Key numbers

- In QED, the electron pole mass at one-loop receives a QAE contribution \(\langle e|H_a|e\rangle = \frac{3\alpha m_e}{8\pi}\).
- The QAE contribution to the Lamb shift at order \(\alpha^5\) is \(\langle n,j|H_a|n,j\rangle^{(5)} = -\frac{7m_e\alpha^5}{24\pi n^4}\left(\frac{3}{8}-\frac{1}{2j+1}\right)\).
- In the 1+1 dimensional non-linear sigma model, the QAE contributes exactly \(\langle \pi^i|H_a|\pi^i \rangle = \frac{m}{2}\) to the pion mass, independent of regularization scheme.
- The zero-momentum sigma propagator in the sigma model is \(\langle \sigma(0)\sigma(0) \rangle = 8\pi/(Nm^2)\).
- For the lowest glueball state \(\sigma\), the decay constant satisfies \(f_{\sigma}=m_\sigma\sqrt{|\Phi_0|}\), and the predicted nucleon-scalar coupling in the chiral limit is \(g_{NN\sigma}=\frac{m_N m_\sigma}{4\sqrt{|\Phi_0|}}\).
- For the pion state, the QAE contributes \(\frac{1}{8}\) of the total pion mass.

## Context

This work extends the original mass decomposition of Ji (1994, 1995) by establishing the physical significance of the trace anomaly contribution to the nucleon mass, which had been studied in the context of low-energy theorems by Collins (1976) and Shifman (1978). The paper contrasts its approach with the mass decomposition of Metz (2020), which the authors argue missed the key role of the anomalous term. The QAE contribution is related to the MIT bag model constant of Chodos (1974) and provides a dynamical mechanism for confinement. The results enable experimental determination of the QAE contribution through heavy-quarkonium electroproduction at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider, as proposed by Kharzeev (1998), Hatta (2018), and Meziani (2020).
