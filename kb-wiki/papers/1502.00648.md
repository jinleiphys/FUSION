# Sign Structure of Susceptibilities of Conserved Charges in the (2+1) Polyakov Quark Meson Model

> The sign structure of correlations of conserved charges are investigated in a QCD like model: the (2+1) flavor Polyakov Quark Meson model. We compute all susceptibilities of the conserved charges on the $(μ_{B}-T)$ plane up to fourth order and a few at higher order as well. By varying the mass of the sigma meson, we are able to study and compare scenarios with as well as without a critical point. In the hadron-quark transition regime we identify certain correlations that turn negative unlike expectation from ideal hadron resonance gas calculations. The striking feature being that these remain negative deep into the hadronic side and thus could be measured in experiments. Measurement of such quantities in heavy ion collision experiments can elucidate the location of the QCD transition curve and possibly the critical point.

## Key claim

The authors compute all susceptibilities of conserved charges up to fourth order (and some higher-order ones) in the (2+1) flavor Polyakov Quark Meson model on the (mu_B, T) plane, and find that certain off-diagonal correlations turn negative in the hadron-quark transition regime and remain negative deep into the hadronic side, unlike the expectation from ideal hadron resonance gas calculations. However, they do not find any unique sign structure that could be attributed solely to the presence of a critical point; the negative regions appear whether or not a critical point exists in the model.

## Method

The authors work with the (2+1) flavor Polyakov Quark Meson model at the mean field level, computing generalized susceptibilities (derivatives of pressure with respect to chemical potentials) numerically using the ADOL-C package. They study three model variants: msig400 and msig400-phys (sigma meson mass 400 MeV, with and without physical strangeness and charge neutrality constraints) which possess a critical point, and msig600 (sigma meson mass 600 MeV) which has no critical point. The susceptibilities are mapped on the (mu_B, T) plane, and the sign structures of diagonal and mixed (off-diagonal) susceptibilities up to sixth order are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson masses used: 400 MeV (msig400, msig400-phys) and 600 MeV (msig600)
- mu_S/mu_B ratio saturates to approximately 1/3 in the QGP phase (Stefan-Boltzmann limit), shown for mu_B values of 3, 300, and 450 MeV
- At mu_B/T approximately 1, the model overpredicts the lattice ratio R^Q_12 = chi^Q_1/chi^Q_2 by about 20 percent compared to HotQCD lattice data
- The negative region for chi^B_4 extends from approximately (mu_B/mu_c = 0.3, T/T_c = 1.0) to the critical point, with an extent into the hadronic side characterized by R = 0.98
- For chi^{QS}_{11}, the negative region extends from approximately (mu_B/mu_c = 0.9, T/T_c = 0.6) to the critical point, with R = 0.99
- For chi^{BQS}_{111}, the negative region extends from approximately (mu_B/mu_c = 0.7, T/T_c = 0.7) to the end of the first order line, with R = 0.91
- For chi^{BQS}_{112}, the negative region extends from approximately (mu_B/mu_c = 0.6, T/T_c = 0.8) to the end of the first order line, with R = 0.84

## Context

This work extends previous studies of sign structures of conserved charge susceptibilities in QCD-like models, particularly the Polyakov Quark Meson model. Prior work by Skokov (2011) and Friman (2011) showed that higher-order cumulants of baryon number and electric charge become negative in the transition regime, while Schaefer (2011) studied higher-order generalized susceptibilities for baryon number in the same model. The present work extends these investigations to off-diagonal (mixed) susceptibilities of conserved charges, which had been largely ignored. The authors compare their model results with lattice QCD data from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2012) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2013) at non-zero but small mu_B/T, finding good qualitative agreement. The results suggest that systematic measurement of off-diagonal susceptibilities in heavy ion collision experiments could help locate the QCD transition curve and possibly the critical point, complementing the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC.
