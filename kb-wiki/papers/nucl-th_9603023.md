# Foundations of the Constituent Quark Model

> 'Constituent quarks' means massive quarks, in contrast to the nearly massless u,d,s quarks of the QCD lagrangian. The dynamical or constituent masses appear owing to the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. A realistic mechanism of the chiral symmetry breaking is provided by instantons. Therefore, I review the present status of the QCD instanton vacuum, emphasizing the mechanism of chiral symmetry breaking and of the generation of the constitutent quark mass. I end up with the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio-type effective theory to which QCD is reduced at low momenta.

## Key claim

Constituent quarks with masses of order 350-400 MeV arise from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, and instantons provide the most realistic mechanism for generating both the dynamical quark mass and the chiral condensate, though the derivation relies on a small packing fraction of instantons (about 1/8 of the 4-dimensional volume) which justifies the semiclassical approximation used.

## Method

The paper reviews the QCD instanton vacuum formalism, starting from the one-instanton weight and building an ensemble of interacting instantons and antiinstantons using a Feynman variational principle to compute the vacuum energy. The quark propagator is then calculated in the instanton background by averaging over the ensemble, treating the delocalization of would-be zero fermion modes as analogous to Anderson conductivity in disordered systems, which yields a momentum-dependent dynamical quark mass. Finally, the instanton ensemble is averaged to derive an effective Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type four-fermion interaction with fixed strength and explicit U_A(1) violation.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass at zero momentum: M(0) approximately 350-400 MeV, estimated as one half of the rho meson mass or one third of the nucleon mass
- Chiral condensate: <bar psi psi> approximately -(250 MeV)^3 for u,d,s quarks
- Gluon condensate: <F_{mu nu}^a F_{mu nu}^a>/32 pi^2 approximately (200 MeV)^4
- Average instanton separation: bar R approximately 1 fm (1/200 MeV)
- Ratio of average instanton size to average separation: bar rho / bar R approximately 1/3 (from variational calculation) versus 0.37-0.4 (from lattice cooling measurements)
- Pion decay constant from instanton vacuum: F_pi approximately 100 MeV versus 93 MeV experimental
- Pion charge radius: sqrt(r_pi^2) approximately (340 MeV)^{-1} versus (310 MeV)^{-1} experimental
- Dynamical mass from gap equation with standard parameters: M(0) approximately 350 MeV (or 420 MeV if M^2 is neglected in denominator)
- Quark condensate from instanton calculation: approximately -(255 MeV)^3

## Context

This review extends the instanton-based approach to chiral symmetry breaking developed by Diakonov and Petrov (1984a, 1984b, 1986a) and builds on the foundational instanton work of 't Hooft (1976), Belavin, Polyakov, Schwartz and Tiupkin (1975), and the QCD sum rules of Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979). The instanton vacuum formalism provides a microscopic derivation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (originally proposed by Vaks, Larkin, Nambu and Jona-Lasinio in 1961) and enables calculation of hadronic properties including the pion decay constant and charge radius, with results that agree with lattice measurements by Chu et al. (1994) and phenomenological studies by Shuryak (1993a, 1993b).
