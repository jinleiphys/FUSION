# Nucleon Energy Correlators for the Odderon

> We investigate the T-odd nucleon energy correlator (NEC) at small $x$ and establish its connection with the spin-dependent odderon. Probing the T-odd NEC involves measuring a single transverse spin asymmetry (SSA) for the energy pattern in the target fragmentation region in deep inelastic scattering. We find that while the inclusive energy pattern results in a vanishing SSA, a nonzero SSA emerges when restricting the measurement to charged hadrons, which are directly measured in tracking detectors. To describe this effect, we introduce the track-based NEC and evaluate its small-$x$ limit from fracture functions using the energy sum rule. The resulting SSA exhibits an opposite sign between positively and negatively charged hadrons, providing a clear experimental signature of the odderon. Furthermore, by incorporating charge weighting into the NEC, we construct a C-odd tag in the final state, eliminating potential contamination from C-even gluonic contributions. Our results demonstrate that these track-based energy-pattern observables offer a clean and sensitive probe for unveiling odderon dynamics at the future electron-ion collider.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the single transverse spin asymmetry (SSA) in the track-based nucleon energy correlator (NEC) provides a clean probe of the spin-dependent odderon at small-x, but only when the measurement is restricted to charged hadrons rather than all hadrons; the inclusive energy pattern yields a vanishing SSA due to cancellation between quark and antiquark contributions.

## Method
The authors introduce track-based NECs for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in the target fragmentation region (TFR) and factorize the energy pattern in terms of these NECs at leading power. They evaluate the small-x limit of the track-based NECs using the energy sum rule that connects NECs to fracture functions, and compute the Sivers-type NEC from the spin-dependent odderon within the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism. The SSA is then expressed as the ratio of the Sivers-type to unpolarized track-based NECs, and the charge-weighted energy pattern is constructed to isolate C-odd contributions.

## Key numbers
* The SSA for the track-based energy pattern is given by \(A_{UT}^{\mathbb{S}} = \frac{\sum_{a=q,\bar q} e_a^2 f^{t,a}_{1T,\mathbb{S}}(x_B,\theta)}{\sum_{a=q,\bar q} e_a^2 f^a_{1,\mathbb{S}}(x_B,\theta)}\), where the Sivers-type NEC \(f^{t,q}_{1T}\) is proportional to the spin-dependent odderon \(O_{1T,x_g}^{\perp}(\bs k_{g\perp}^2)\).
* The small-x quark NEC is expressed as \(f^q(x,\theta,\phi,\bs S_\perp) = \frac{N_c}{\theta^2 (2\pi)^4} \int_0^{1-x} d\xi \int d[2]\bs k_{g\perp} \bs k_\perp^2 {\cal H}_q(\xi,\bs k_\perp,\bs k_{g\perp}) {\cal F}_{x_g}(\bs k_{g\perp},\bs S_\perp)\), with the dipole gluon distribution containing the spin-dependent odderon term \(\frac{\epsilon_{\perp}^{i j} \bs S_{\perp i} \bs k_{g\perp j}}{M} O_{1T,x_g}^{\perp}(\bs k_{g\perp}^2)\).
* The flavor-singlet Sivers NEC for inclusive measurement vanishes: \(\sum_{a=q,\bar q} e_a^2 f^{t,a}_{1T}(x,\theta)=0\), due to the sign relation \(f^{t,\bar q}_{1T}(x,\theta) = - f^{t,q}_{1T}(x,\theta)\).

## Context
This work extends the study of the spin-dependent odderon, previously linked to T-odd transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) such as the Sivers function by Zhou (2013) and Dong (2018), into the target fragmentation region using energy correlator observables. It builds on the nucleon energy correlator (NEC) framework introduced by Liu (2022) and Chen (2024), which connects NECs to fracture functions, and applies the charge-weighting technique from Lee (2023) to construct a C-odd tag. The results enable a new experimental probe of odderon dynamics at the future electron-ion collider (EIC), avoiding limitations of traditional hadron-level SSA observables that depend on fragmentation functions and allow C-even contamination.
