# Neutron matter on the lattice with pionless effective field theory

> We study neutron matter by combining pionless effective field theory with non-perturbative lattice methods. The neutron contact interaction is determined by zero temperature scattering data. We simulate neutron matter on the lattice at temperatures 4 and 8 MeV and densities below one-fifth normal nuclear matter density. Our results at different lattice spacings agree with one another and match bubble chain calculations at low densities. The equation of state of pure neutron matter obtained from our simulations agrees quantitatively with variational calculations based on realistic potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining pionless effective field theory with non-perturbative lattice methods can reproduce the equation of state of dilute neutron matter at temperatures 4 and 8 MeV and densities below one-fifth normal nuclear matter density, with results that agree quantitatively with variational calculations based on realistic potentials, but only in the regime where the relevant momenta are smaller than the pion mass and the effective theory is limited to a single four-fermion contact interaction.

## Method

The authors constructed a lattice action for neutron matter using the lowest-order pionless effective field theory containing a single four-fermion contact interaction with no derivatives, tuned to reproduce the neutron-neutron scattering length. They performed hybrid Monte Carlo simulations on a 3+1 dimensional space-time lattice at temperatures of 4 and 8 MeV, using five different lattice spacings ranging from inverse spatial spacings of 50 to 80 MeV and inverse temporal spacings of 24 to 72 MeV. The contact interaction coefficient was determined by matching the bubble chain sum to the scattering length using Luscher's formula for energy levels in a finite periodic box, and the results were compared with bubble chain calculations and free Fermi gas results.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering length used: a_scatt = -24 fm (though using -18 fm changes the operator coefficient by only 1%)
- Neutron mass taken as 939 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Contact potential coefficients C range from -11.6 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2} (for a^{-1}=50 MeV, a_t^{-1}=24 MeV) to -6.96 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2} (for a^{-1}=80 MeV, a_t^{-1}=72 MeV)
- Simulations performed at temperatures 4 and 8 MeV
- Densities studied are below one-fifth normal nuclear matter density (0.034 fm^{-3})
- The dimensionless parameter k_F|a_nn| >> 1 for densities above 10^{-4} rho_N
- Effective range r_nn = 2.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of effective field theory methods, pioneered by Weinberg (1990), to neutron matter using lattice simulations, building on earlier lattice studies by Muller (1999) and Lee (2004). The approach provides an alternative to traditional variational methods and Green function Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson (2003) that rely on realistic two-body potentials fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The results enable future studies of neutron matter at higher densities and temperatures, and can be extended to include higher-order interactions in the effective field theory expansion.
