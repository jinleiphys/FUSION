# Comment on ``Specific Heat and Shape Transitions in Light sd Nuclei''

> This comment re-examines the origin of structure seen in the computed specific heat of finite nuclei. In a recent paper, Civitarese and Schvellinger suggest that such structure is due to model-space truncation in the calculations. We reaffirm our conclusion that the structure is caused by a collective-to-non-collective phase transformation at low temperatures, signaled by a change in the nuclear level density below 10 MeV excitation energy.

# Comment on "Specific Heat and Shape Transitions in Light sd Nuclei"

## Key claim

The authors reaffirm that the peaked structure in the computed specific heat of finite nuclei is caused by a collective-to-non-collective phase transformation at low temperatures, signaled by a change in the nuclear level density below 10 MeV excitation energy, contrary to the suggestion by Civitarese and Schvellinger (1994) that such structure is due to model-space truncation.

## Method

The authors compare finite-temperature Hartree-Fock (FTHF) calculations with canonical calculations using exact eigenstates of the same realistic effective Hamiltonian operator in the sd shell, where exact shell-model calculations are possible. They also perform canonical calculations of the specific heat using experimental nuclear structure information for 20Ne, and examine the behavior of the ensemble average of the quadrupole moment and the many-body level density. The continuum contribution to the specific heat is taken into account in some calculations.

## Key numbers

- The change in nuclear level density that signals the phase transformation occurs below 10 MeV excitation energy.
- In 20Ne, a peaked structure is observed in the specific heat in both canonical calculations using sd shell eigenstates and calculations using experimental nuclear structure information, which agrees with FTHF predictions.
- The ensemble average of the quadrupole moment in FTHF calculations for both 20Ne and 24Mg vanishes, indicating a shape transition, but this behavior is an artifact of the finite-temperature mean-field calculation.

## Context

This comment responds to Civitarese and Schvellinger (1994), who used SU3 model calculations of eleven even-even sd nuclei to argue against phase transitions in light nuclei, and also addresses similar results by Dukelsky et al. (1991). The authors extend their own prior work (Miller et al., 1989; Quick et al., 1991; Rossignoli et al., 1992) on low-temperature collective-to-non-collective phase transitions in nuclei. The work is consistent with a recent semi-empirical determination of nuclear matter properties by Das et al. (1993) that provides evidence for such a low-temperature phase transition, and with realistic finite-temperature BCS calculations by Baldo et al. (1990).
