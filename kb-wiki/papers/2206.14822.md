# The f0(1370) controversy from dispersive meson-meson scattering data analyses

> We establish the existence of the long-debated $f_0(1370)$ resonance in the dispersive analyses of meson-meson scattering data. For this, we present a novel approach using forward dispersion relations, valid for generic inelastic resonances. We find its pole at $(1245\pm 40)- i\,(300^{+30}_{-70})$ MeV in $ππ$ scattering. We also provide the couplings as well as further checks extrapolating partial-wave dispersion relations or with other continuation methods. A pole at $(1380^{+70}_{-60})-i\,(220^{+80}_{-70})$ MeV also appears in the $ππ\to K\bar K$ data analysis with partial-wave dispersion relations. Despite settling its existence, our model-independent dispersive and analytic methods still show a lingering tension between pole parameters from the $ππ$ and $K\bar K$ channels that should be attributed to data.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the existence of the long-debated $f_0(1370)$ resonance using model-independent dispersive and analytic methods applied to meson-meson scattering data, but finds a lingering tension between pole parameters extracted from $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ and $\pi\pi\to K\bar K$ channels that is attributed to inconsistencies in the underlying experimental data.

## Method

The authors apply forward dispersion relations (FDRs) to $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes, using as input the Constrained Fits to Data (CFD) from GarciaMartin et al. (2011) which satisfy three FDRs as well as Roy and GKPY equations. The dispersive output is analytically continued to the complex plane using sequences of continued fractions ($C_N$) of order $N=7$ to $51$ over the energy segment 1.2-1.4 GeV. For $\pi\pi\to K\bar K$, the output of partial-wave Roy-Steiner equations is similarly continued. Additional checks employ Padé approximant sequences and direct analytic continuation of global parameterizations.

## Key numbers

* $f_0(1370)$ pole from $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ FDR method: $(1245\pm 40) - i\,(300^{+30}_{-70})$ MeV, with $\pi\pi$ coupling $|g_{\pi\pi}| = 5.6^{+0.7}_{-1.2}$ GeV
* $f_0(1370)$ pole from $\pi\pi\to K\bar K$ Roy-Steiner method: $(1390^{+40}_{-50}) - i\,(220^{+60}_{-40})$ MeV, with coupling $|g_{\pi\pi}g_{K\bar K}|^{1/2} = 3.5^{+2.7}_{-1.7}$ GeV
* $f_2(1270)$ pole from FDR method: $1267.5 - i\,94$ MeV (with negligible error, fixed by tensor partial-wave phase reaching $\pi/2$ at 1274.5 MeV)
* $f_0(1500)$ pole from FDR method: $(1523^{+16}_{-10}) - i\,(52^{+16}_{-11})$ MeV
* Review of Particle Physics (RPP) range for $f_0(1370)$: $(1200-1500) - i\,(150-250)$ MeV

## Context

This work settles the longstanding controversy over the $f_0(1370)$ resonance, which was absent in classic $\pi\pi$ scattering analyses (Hyams et al., 1973; Grayer et al., 1974; Estabrooks et al., 1978) and whose existence has been disputed in reviews (Klempt, 2007; Ochs, 2013) and recent experiments (COMPASS, 2015). The dispersive approach extends the methodology that previously settled the existence of the $\sigma$ and $\kappa$ resonances (Caprini et al., 2006; GarciaMartin et al., 2011; Moussallam, 2011) to the inelastic regime where the $f_0(1370)$ lies. The method can be applied to other processes to avoid model dependence in hadron spectroscopy and enables the use of total cross-section data without requiring partial-wave analyses.
