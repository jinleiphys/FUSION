# Composite-Field Goldstone States and Higgs Mechanism in Dilute Bose Gases

> We show that a composite-field (diatom) Goldstone state is expected in a dilute Bose gas for temperatures between the Bose gas critical temperature where the atom Bose-Einstein condensate appears and the temperature where superfluidity sets in. The presence of superfluidity is tied to the existence of a U(1) charge-two diatom condensate in the system. By promoting the global U(1) symmetry of the theory to a gauge symmetry, we find that the mass of the gauge particle generated through the Anderson-Higgs mechanism is related to the superfluid density via the Meissner effect and the superfluid density is related to the square of the anomalous density in the Bose system.

# Composite-Field Goldstone States and Higgs Mechanism in Dilute Bose Gases

## Key claim
The authors show that a composite-field (diatom) Goldstone state is expected in a dilute Bose gas for temperatures between the Bose gas critical temperature where the atom Bose-Einstein condensate appears and the temperature where superfluidity sets in, but only within the leading-order auxiliary-field (LOAF) approximation to the dilute Bose gas theory. The presence of superfluidity is tied to the existence of a U(1) charge-two diatom condensate in the system, and the mass of the gauge particle generated through the Anderson-Higgs mechanism is related to the superfluid density via the Meissner effect.

## Method
The authors reformulated the theory of dilute Bose gases in terms of normal and anomalous auxiliary-field densities using the leading-order auxiliary-field loop expansion (LOAF). They performed a path integral over the boson fields exactly and approximated the integral over the auxiliary fields by steepest descent, then derived the effective potential at finite temperature. The Goldstone theorem was applied to the composite auxiliary field A, and the U(1) symmetry was promoted to a gauge symmetry by introducing a gauge field W_mu to study the Anderson-Higgs mechanism.

## Key numbers
- The LOAF phase diagram predicts a temperature range T* - Tc of approximately 20% relative to Tc for a dimensionless parameter value rho^(1/3)a = 1.
- The superfluid density rho_s and the square of the anomalous density A^2 are compared for an interaction strength rho^(1/3)a = 0.4, showing close resemblance in their temperature dependence.
- The London penetration depth for the gauge field is given by lambda_L = sqrt( m c^2 / (4 pi rho_s g^2) ).
- The composite-field Higgs mass is M_H^2 = kappa A^2, where kappa is the four-point field interaction at zero momentum transfer and temperature T.
- The effective mass of the gauge field is M_W^2 = (2 g A)^2, which is identified with rho_s g^2 / (m c^2).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LOAF reformulation of dilute Bose gases (Cooper et al., 2010) which found two critical temperatures in the phase diagram. The paper connects to the Josephson relationship (Josephson, 1966) for superfluidity and parallels the BCS theory treatment of fermionic atom gases where the superfluid density is proportional to the square of the gap field Delta, as shown by Taylor (2008). The results enable experimental tests of the Higgs mechanism in ultracold atomic gases using synthetic magnetic fields, building on work by Spielman (2009, 2011) and Cooper and Hadzibabic (2010).
