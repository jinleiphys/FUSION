# Constraints on the High-Density Nuclear Equation of State from Neutron Star Observables

> Depending on the density reached in the cores of neutron stars, such objects may contain stable phases of novel matter found nowhere else in the Universe. This article gives a brief overview of these phases of matter and discusses astrophysical constraints on the high-density equation of state associated with ultra-dense nuclear matter.

# Constraints on the High-Density Nuclear Equation of State from Neutron Star Observables

## Key claim
The paper argues that current neutron star observations, particularly mass measurements of PSR B1516+02B, radius constraints from RX J1856.5-3754, and kilohertz QPO frequencies, do not yet rule out any of the tested nuclear equations of state, but future detection of a pulsar with mass exceeding 1.8–1.9 solar masses at the 2-sigma or 3-sigma level would severely constrain the stiffness of the nuclear EoS, leaving only the stiffest models (D³C, DD-F4, BBG, and DBHF) viable.

## Method
The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for a broad collection of nuclear equations of state, including relativistic mean-field models (NLρ, NLρδ, D³C, DD-F4, KVR, KVOR), a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) model, and a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) model, each combined at sub-nuclear densities with the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EoS. They then compare the resulting mass-radius relations, gravitational mass–baryon number relations, moments of inertia, rotational frequencies, and cooling curves against observational constraints from pulsar timing, quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in low-mass X-ray binaries, thermal radiation from isolated neutron stars, and surface redshift measurements.

## Key numbers
- PSR B1516+02B mass: 1.96^{+0.09}_{-0.12} M⊙ (68% probability), with 95% probability above 1.68 M⊙
- Average mass of binary radio pulsars: 1.35 ± 0.04 M⊙
- PSR J0751+1807 mass: 2.1 ± 0.2 M⊙
- RX J1856.5-3754 radius: R∞ = 16.8 km (continuous temperature fit), with conservative lower distance 140 pc giving radius 17 km
- Maximum QPO frequency observed: 1330 Hz for 4U 0614+091
- Pulsar B in J0737-3039 gravitational mass: 1.249 ± 0.001 M⊙, with predicted baryon mass range 1.366–1.375 M⊙
- Critical density for ONeMg white dwarf collapse: 4.5 × 10⁹ g/cm³
- Mass-shedding limit formula: νK = 1045 Hz × (M/M⊙)^{1/2} (R/10 km)^{-3/2} (with general relativistic correction)
- XTE J1739-285 rotation period: 0.89 ms (1122 Hz), giving constraint R < 9.52 (M/M⊙)^{1/3} km
- Critical density for deconfinement suggested by mass clustering: about 0.4 fm⁻³ (2.5 n_s)
- Phase transition critical density in cooling calculations: n_c = 2.75 n_0 = 0.44 fm⁻³

## Context
This review extends the line of work connecting neutron star observables to the nuclear equation of state, building on earlier compilations by Glen (1997), Weber (1999), and Lattimer (2007). It specifically tests the collection of EoS models from Klaehn (2006) against recent observational constraints including the high-mass pulsar PSR B1516+02B from Freire (2007), the radius measurement of RX J1856 from Trumper (2003) and Walter (2002), and QPO constraints from Miller (2003) and Barret (2005). The paper enables future work by identifying which EoS models survive current constraints and by demonstrating how future observations, particularly of higher-mass pulsars, higher-frequency QPOs, or more precise moment-of-inertia measurements, could discriminate between competing models for the high-density nuclear EoS.
