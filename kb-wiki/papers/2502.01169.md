# Renormalization-group approach to the Kohn-Luttinger superconductivity: Amplification of the pairing gap from $\ell^4$ to $\ell$

> We revisit the renormalization group (RG) analysis of the Kohn-Luttinger (KL) mechanism for superconductivity. The KL mechanism leads to superconductivity in a system with a repulsive bare interaction. The key ingredient is the screening effect that renders the induced interaction attractive in channels with nonzero angular momentum $\ell \neq 0$, thereby triggering the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) instability. According to the original argument, the resulting gap is exponentially small, with its exponent scaling as $-\ell^4$. However, the KL mechanism was originally formulated within perturbation theory, where the series is known to converge poorly in certain cases -- most notably, for the p-wave paring gap induced by a repulsive s-wave contact interaction. This poor convergence may be attributed to a divergent integrand in a specific class of diagrams containing both the BCS logarithm and the Kohn anomaly, suggesting that one must resum the Kohn anomaly contributions separately from the BCS logarithm. In this work, we incorporate the Kohn anomaly contribution into the beta function of the RG equation governing the BCS instability near the Fermi surface. Our solution shows that the KL gap exponent is then proportional to $-\ell$, indicating a significant enhancement of the KL mechanism beyond the previously known result. To illustrate this, we study the spin-triplet p-wave pairing gap arising from a repulsive s-wave contact interaction and compare our RG-based results with those obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating the zero-sound (ZS) diagram contribution into the renormalization group (RG) beta function for the BCS instability changes the exponent of the Kohn-Luttinger pairing gap from scaling as \(-\ell^4\) to scaling as \(-\ell\), representing a significant enhancement of the KL mechanism beyond the previously known perturbative result.

## Method

The authors revisit the renormalization group analysis of the Kohn-Luttinger mechanism near the Fermi surface, incorporating the zero-sound diagram contribution into the beta function of the RG equation governing the BCS instability. They compute the ZS diagram contribution using a hard momentum cutoff and evaluate the resulting beta function in partial-wave expansion. The RG solution is then compared with results from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in perturbation theory for the specific case of spin-triplet p-wave pairing arising from a repulsive s-wave contact interaction.

## Key numbers

- The conventional KL gap exponent scales as \(-\ell^4\), with \(\ln(\Delta / \mu) \propto -\ell^4\) for large angular momentum \(\ell\).
- The new RG-based result gives a gap exponent proportional to \(-\ell\), indicating a substantial amplification of the KL pairing gap.
- For constant bare interaction \(U\) and \(\ell \geq 2\), the irreducible interaction in the KL mechanism is \(\hat{K}_{\ell} = -\nu_F U^2 / (4 \ell^4) < 0\), where \(\nu_F = m k_F / (2\pi^2)\) is the density of states at the Fermi surface.
- For \(\ell = 1\) with constant \(U\), the exact irreducible interaction is \(K_{\ell=1} = -\nu_F U^2 (2\ln 2 - 1)/5\).
- The ZS beta function in partial-wave expansion is \(\beta_{\mathrm{ZS},\ell} = -\nu_F V^2(\pi) (\ln 2)/(2\ell+1) (\Lambda/k_F)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the cutoff and \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum.

## Context

This work extends the original Kohn-Luttinger mechanism (Kohn and Luttinger, 1965) which predicted a pairing gap with exponent scaling as \(-\ell^4\) from one-loop perturbation theory. The paper addresses the poor convergence of perturbation theory noted by Efremov et al. (2000) for the p-wave pairing gap from a repulsive s-wave contact interaction. The RG approach builds on earlier RG treatments of the Fermi surface by Shankar (1993) and others, and the ZS diagram analysis revisits calculations from Appendix B of Shankar's review. The result enables a more accurate determination of pairing gaps in systems where the KL mechanism operates, including neutron-star matter, unconventional superconductors, and cold atom systems.
