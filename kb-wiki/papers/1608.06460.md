# The $σ$ exchange effect in the Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen by two photons and two pions exchange

> Based on the simple phenomenological $σππ$, $σN N$ and $ππγ$ interactions, we estimate the $σ$ exchange effect in the Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen. We at first calculate the effective couplings of $σμμ$ by two photons and two pions exchange, then calculate the corresponding corrections to the energy shift of the 2S/2P states of muonic hydrogen. We find the correction to the energy shift of 2S state is about -14$μ$eV by the current used parameters, which is about $44\%$ of the usual two photons exchange contribution, and is larger than the current experimental precision, and should be considered in the experimental analysis.

# The σ Exchange Effect in the Lamb Shift of Muonic Hydrogen by Two Photons and Two Pions Exchange

## Key claim
The scalar meson σ exchange between the muon and the proton, mediated by two photons and two pions, contributes a correction of about -14 μeV to the 2S state Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen. This correction is approximately 44% of the usual two-photon exchange contribution and is larger than the current experimental precision, but the author notes that the input parameters (σ mass, couplings g_σNN and g_σππ) carry at least 10% uncertainty each, leading to at least 30% uncertainty in the final result.

## Method
The author constructs phenomenological effective Lagrangians for σNN, σππ, and ππγ interactions, then computes the two-loop diagrams for the effective σμμ coupling via two-photon and two-pion exchange. The effective coupling is expanded at Q²=0 as g_σμμ = g_σππ [c₁ + c₂ Q], and the resulting non-relativistic potential is used in a quasipotential matching to compute the energy shifts of the 2S and 2P states of muonic hydrogen via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The correction to the 2S state energy shift is -14 μeV.
- The correction to the 2P state energy shift is -0.0029 μeV.
- The two-photon exchange correction to the Lamb shift is typically taken as 33 μeV (Carlson 2015); the -14 μeV result is about 44% of that value.
- The current discrepancy in the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift is 0.316 meV (Jentschura 2011); the -14 μeV correction is about 4% of that discrepancy.
- The σ pole mass is taken as m_σ = 0.45 GeV (real part of the PDG range 400-550 MeV).
- The coupling constants used are |g_σππ| = 3.1 GeV (from Γ_σ = 0.5 GeV) and |g_σNN| = 14.
- The coefficient c₂ is -29 × 10⁻⁶ GeV⁻¹; c₁ is determined numerically and is insensitive to the cutoff Λ = 0.77 GeV.

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle, which arose from the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement (Antognini 2013) giving r_E = 0.84087(39) fm and the CODATA2014 value r_E = 0.8751(61) fm from ep scattering and hydrogen data. The calculation extends prior work on one-pion exchange corrections (which were found to be very small) and differs from chiral effective theory approaches (e.g., two-pion exchange calculations) by using a phenomenological σ meson with direct σNN and σππ couplings. The result suggests that σ exchange should be included in future experimental analyses of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, and the method could be extended to estimate similar effects in ordinary hydrogen (though the author notes those would be very small due to the larger Bohr radius).
