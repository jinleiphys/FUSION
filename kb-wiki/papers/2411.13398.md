# Dispersive Determination of Nucleon Gravitational Form Factors

> Being closely connected to the origin of the nucleon mass, the gravitational form factors of the nucleon have attracted significant attention in recent years. We present the first model-independent determinations of the gravitational form factors of the pion and nucleon at the physical pion mass, using a data-driven dispersive approach. The so-called "last global unknown property" of the nucleon, the $D$-term, is determined to be $-3.38^{+0.34}_{-0.35}$. The root mean square radius of the scalar trace density inside the nucleon is determined to be $(0.97 \pm0.03)~\text{fm}$. Notably, this value is larger than the proton charge radius, suggesting a modern structural view of the nucleon where gluons, responsible for most of the nucleon mass, are distributed over a larger spatial region than quarks, which dominate the charge distribution, indicating that the radius of the trace density may be regarded as a confinement radius. We also predict the nucleon angular momentum and mechanical radii, providing further insights into the intricate internal structure of the nucleon.

# Dispersive Determination of Nucleon Gravitational Form Factors

## Key claim
The authors present the first model-independent determination of the nucleon gravitational form factors at the physical pion mass using a data-driven dispersive approach, claiming that the nucleon D-term is determined to be -3.38 with uncertainties, and that the root mean square radius of the scalar trace density (0.97 fm) is larger than the proton charge radius, suggesting gluons are distributed over a larger spatial region than quarks. The hedge is that the results depend on matching to next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory for low-energy constants and on effective poles to satisfy sum rules for high-energy contributions.

## Method
The authors use dispersion relations with elastic unitarity for pion gravitational form factors, employing the Omnès representation for the A-pi form factor and a coupled-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem for the trace form factor incorporating pi-pi and K-Kbar intermediate states. For nucleon gravitational form factors, they use unsubtracted dispersion relations with spectral functions constructed from the meson form factors and pi-pi/K-Kbar to N-Nbar partial wave amplitudes from Roy-Steiner equation analyses, supplemented by effective zero-width poles to satisfy Poincaré symmetry sum rules.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon D-term: -3.38 (+0.34, -0.35)
- Root mean square radius of scalar trace density: (0.97 ± 0.03) fm
- Root mean square mass radius: (0.70 +0.03, -0.04) fm
- Root mean square mechanical radius: (0.72 +0.09, -0.08) fm
- Root mean square angular momentum radius: (0.70 ± 0.02) fm
- Pion decay constant used: F_pi = 92.1 MeV
- f_2(1270) resonance mass: (1275 ± 20) MeV
- Slope of pion trace GFF at t=0: dot_Theta_pi(0) = 0.98(2)
- Slope of kaon trace GFF at t=0: dot_Theta_K(0) = 0.94(14)

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach pioneered by Donoghue (1990) for pion gravitational form factors and applies it to the nucleon for the first time with full error analysis, overcoming limitations of earlier work by Pasquini (2014) that used model-dependent estimates and neglected K-Kbar intermediate states. The results agree with lattice QCD calculations at unphysical pion masses by Hackett (2023) and Wang (2024), and provide a model-independent benchmark at the physical pion mass that can be compared with chiral soliton models, holographic QCD, and bag model predictions. The determination enables future studies of nucleon structure and has potential implications for understanding strongly interacting matter in neutron stars.
