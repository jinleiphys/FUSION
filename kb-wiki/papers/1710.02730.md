# Average CsI neutron density distribution from COHERENT data

> Using the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data of the COHERENT experiment, we determine for the first time the average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$. We obtain the practically model-independent value $ R_{n} = 5.5 {}^{+0.9}_{-1.1} \, \text{fm} $ using the symmetrized Fermi and Helm form factors. We also point out that the COHERENT data show a $2.3σ$ evidence of the nuclear structure suppression of the full coherence.

## Key claim

Using COHERENT's coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data, the authors determine for the first time the average neutron rms radius of Cs-133 and I-127, obtaining a practically model-independent value of 5.5 fm with asymmetric uncertainties, while noting that the data show only a 2.3 sigma evidence of nuclear structure suppression of full coherence rather than a definitive measurement.

## Method

The authors fitted the COHERENT CsI detector data (12 energy bins from 4 to 15) using a least-squares function with nuisance parameters for signal and background systematic uncertainties, assuming the neutron form factors of Cs-133 and I-127 are identical due to similar expected structures and current data limitations. They parameterized the neutron form factor using both a symmetrized Fermi distribution and a Helm form factor, with surface thickness parameters fixed to values determined from proton form factor studies of similar nuclei, and minimized chi-squared to extract the neutron rms radius.

## Key numbers

- Average neutron rms radius for Cs-133 and I-127: R_n = 5.5 (+0.9, -1.1) fm, obtained using both symmetrized Fermi and Helm form factors
- Evidence for nuclear structure suppression of full coherence: 2.3 sigma, with chi-squared improvement of 5.5 over the full coherence hypothesis (p-value of 1.9%)
- Proton rms radii from muonic atom spectroscopy: R_p(Cs) = 4.804 fm, R_p(I) = 4.749 fm
- Neutron skin value: Delta R_np = 0.7 (+0.9, -1.1) fm
- Current sensitivity gives relative uncertainty Delta R_n / R_n = 17%
- With ten times the current protons on target and current systematics, R_n can be determined within about 0.5 fm; with half or quarter systematics, within about 0.4 or 0.3 fm respectively

## Context

This work extends the only previous measurement of nuclear neutron distribution via weak interactions, which was the PREX experiment on Pb-208 (Abrahamyan 2012), and provides the first determination using neutrino-nucleus scattering. The result is compatible with all theoretical model calculations (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field models such as SkM*, SkP, SkI4, Sly4, UNEDF1, NL-SH, NL3, NL-Z2) but tends to favor larger R_n values, while the large uncertainty prevents discrimination between models. Future COHERENT data with increased statistics and reduced systematics could enable meaningful tests of nuclear models and provide input for neutron star equation of state studies, tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave observations (LIGO/Virgo 2017), and neutrino background calculations for dark matter detectors like DARWIN, XENONnT, and LZ.
