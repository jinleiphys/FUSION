# Quasiparticle parameterization of meanfields, Galilei invariance and universal conserving response functions

> The general possible form of meanfield parameterization in a running frame in terms of current, energy and density functionals are examined under the restrictions of Galilean invariance. It is found that only two density-dependent parameters remain which are usually condensed in a position-dependent effective mass and the selfenergy formed by current and mass. The position-dependent mass induces a position-dependent local current which is identified for different nonlinear frames. In a second step the response to an external perturbation and relaxation towards a local equilibrium is investigated. The response function is found to be universal in the sense that the actual parameterization of the local equilibrium does not matter and is eliminated from the theory due to the conservation laws. The explicit form of the response with respect to density, momentum and energy is derived. The compressibility sum rule as well as the sum rule by first and third-order frequency moments are proved analytically to be fulfilled simultaneously. The results are presented for Bose- or Fermi systems in one- two and three dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Galilean-invariant quasiparticle meanfield can be parameterized by only two density-dependent parameters (effective mass and selfenergy), and that the resulting response function to external perturbations is universal in the sense that the specific form of the local equilibrium is eliminated by conservation laws, allowing simultaneous fulfillment of the compressibility sum rule and first- and third-order frequency sum rules.

## Method

The author derives the general form of a quasiparticle energy functional in terms of density, current, and kinetic energy under Galilean invariance constraints, finding that only two density-dependent parameters remain: a position-dependent effective mass and a selfenergy formed by current and mass. A kinetic equation is then linearized around a local equilibrium to derive the response function, with explicit transformation rules between different frames (lab, local, and mixed frames with quadratic dispersion). The response function is expressed in terms of polarization and local field corrections, and the sum rules are verified analytically using commutator relations.

## Key numbers

* The compressibility of the interacting system is given by \( n^2 K = K_0 / [1 - (D/2) (\partial \ln m^* / \partial \ln n) + n^2 (\partial_n \Sigma) K_0 ] \), where \( D = 1, 2, 3 \) is the spatial dimension, \( m^* \) is the effective mass, \( \Sigma \) is the selfenergy, and \( K_0 \) is the noninteracting compressibility.
* The static local field must satisfy \( \lim_{q \to 0} \xi_q(0) = \partial_n \Sigma - (D / 2 n^2 K_0) (\partial \ln m^* / \partial \ln n) \) for the compressibility sum rule to be obeyed.
* The mean local-frame quasiparticle energy is \( \tilde{E}_{\rm qp} = (m_0/m) (m^*/m + 2m/m^* - 2) (E_K - J^2 / 2 n m_0) + n \varepsilon_0 \), which is Galilean invariant.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to construct conserving response functions for density-dependent meanfields, particularly addressing the violation of Galilean invariance in Skyrme-force parameterizations used in nuclear physics (Skyrme 1956, 1959; Engel et al. 1975; Giai et al. 1992) and the problem of simultaneously fulfilling the compressibility and third-order frequency sum rules, which was impossible with a single static local field (Vashishta and Singwi 1973; Ichimaru 1984). The author previously showed that introducing both selfenergy and effective mass as two degrees of freedom allows both sum rules to be satisfied (Morawetz 2001). The present paper generalizes this to include current and energy functionals, providing a universal response function applicable to Bose or Fermi systems in one, two, and three dimensions, and enabling consistent treatment of collective excitations such as giant resonances in nuclei and plasmon modes in solids.
