# Universal geometry of two-neutron halos and Borromean Efimov states close to dissociation

> The geometry of Borromean three-body halos, such as two-neutron halo nuclei or triatomic molecules close to dissociation, is investigated using a three-body model. This model enables to analytically derive the universal geometric properties found recently within an effective-field theory for halos made of a core and two resonantly-interacting particles [Phys. Rev. Lett., 128, 212501 (2022)]. It is shown that these properties not only apply to the ground three-body state, but also to all the excited (Efimov) states where the core-particle interaction is resonant. Furthermore, a universal geometry persists away from the resonant regime between the two particles, for any state close to the three-body threshold. This "halo universality" is different from the Efimov universality which is only approximate for the ground state. It is explained by the separability of the hyper-radius and hyper-angles close to the three-body dissociation threshold.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the universal geometric properties of Borromean three-body halos (such as the ratio of matter radius to core radius) apply not only to the ground state but also to all excited Efimov states, and persist away from the two-particle resonant regime for any state close to the three-body dissociation threshold. This "halo universality" is distinct from Efimov universality, which is only approximate for the ground state.

## Method

The author uses a three-body Faddeev approach with separable interactions to analytically derive universal geometric relations for a core and two identical particles. The system is solved numerically using Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian integral equations with Gaussian form factors, and the results are compared to analytical formulas derived in the hyper-spherical representation. The calculations are performed for various mass ratios and scattering lengths, focusing on the Borromean region where no two-body bound states exist.

## Key numbers

- For the lithium-11 halo nucleus, the model gives a mean-square radius ⟨R²₁₂,₃⟩ = 4.70 fm, compared to the experimental value 5.01 ± 0.32 fm derived from data.
- The two-neutron separation energy of ¹¹Li is |E| = 369.15 ± 0.65 keV (Smith 2008).
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is set to a₁₂ = −18.8 fm, with effective range r₁₂ = 2.83 fm from the AV18 model (Wiringa 1995).
- The matter/core radius ratio ⟨r²ₘ⟩/⟨r²_c⟩ tends to the limit A for β ≫ 1 and to (2/3)A for β ≪ 1, where A is the mass ratio m₃/m₁ and β = √(E₁₂/|E|).
- For ¹¹Li, the ratio ⟨r²ₘ⟩/⟨r²_c⟩ is around 7.0, while the analytical formula gives 6.3 (a 10% error).
- The two-neutron separation energies for ⁶He, ¹⁹B, and ²²C from AME2016 are |E(⁶He)| = 975.45 ± 0.05 keV, |E(¹⁹B)| = 90 ± 560 keV, and |E(²²C)| = 100 ± 640 keV.

## Context

This work extends the universal geometric relations found by Hongo (2022) using effective-field theory for Borromean three-body halos, showing they apply to all Efimov states and not just the ground state. The results are consistent with the earlier hyper-spherical analysis by Fedorov (1994) for the off-resonant limit. The paper enables experimental tests of halo universality in two-neutron halo nuclei such as ¹⁹B and ²²C, as well as in ultracold atomic mixtures of caesium-133 and lithium-7 atoms, where scattering lengths can be controlled via Fano-Feshbach resonances.
