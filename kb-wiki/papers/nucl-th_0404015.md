# Hadronic signals of deconfinement at RHIC

> This article reviews (soft) hadronic signals of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration in hot QCD matter in the light of the results from the first three years of the experimental program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hadronic observables at RHIC provide compelling evidence for the formation of a deconfined quark-gluon plasma, but acknowledges that the evidence remains indirect and relies on the argument that hadrochemical equilibrium cannot be established by interactions among hadrons with confined quarks.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models of quark recombination and fragmentation to describe hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It compares predictions from recombination models, where hadrons form by coalescence of thermal quarks from a deconfined medium, with experimental data on hadron yields, nuclear suppression patterns, baryon-to-meson ratios, and elliptic flow measurements. The analysis uses light-cone and medium-rest-frame formulations of recombination to compute hadron spectra and flow parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QCD vacuum change: T_c ≈ 160 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature deduced from RHIC data: T_ch = 177 MeV
- Quark condensate value: ⟨q̄q⟩ = (235 MeV)^3
- Gluon condensate value: ⟨g²G_μνG^{μν}⟩ = (840 MeV)^4
- Energy threshold for strangeness production in hadronic environment: about 700 MeV (≈ 4T_c)
- Energy threshold for strangeness production in QGP via gg → s̄s: about 300 MeV (less than 2T_c)
- Transverse momentum where fragmentation dominates over recombination: about 4 GeV/c for mesons and 6 GeV/c for baryons
- Suppression factor for power law tail in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC: about a factor 5
- Beam energy for RHIC data shown: √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Constituent quark scaling for elliptic flow: v₂^(M)(P) ≈ 2 v₂(P/2) for mesons, v₂^(B)(P) ≈ 3 v₂(P/3) for baryons
- Onset of nuclear suppression for baryons: between 4 and 6 GeV/c
- Quark momentum at onset of saturation in elliptic flow: p_T ≈ 1 GeV/c

## Context

This review extends the earlier strangeness enhancement observations from the CERN-SPS program (WA97 collaboration, NA57 collaboration) to the higher energies available at RHIC. The paper builds on theoretical work by Rafelski (1982), Koch, Muller, and Rafelski (1986), and Voloshin (2003) on strangeness production and quark recombination, and by Fries, Greco, and others on recombination models. It enables future investigations of two-body correlations among energetic hadrons and the role of gluonic degrees of freedom near the critical temperature.
