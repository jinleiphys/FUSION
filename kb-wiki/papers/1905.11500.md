# Rotational excitations in near neutron-drip line nuclei: the birth and death of particle-bound rotational bands and the extension of nuclear landscape beyond spin zero neutron drip line

> Two new mechanisms active in rotating nuclei located in the vicinity of neutron drip line have been discovered. Strong Coriolis interaction acting on high-$j$ orbitals transforms particle-unbound (resonance) nucleonic configurations into particle-bound ones with increasing angular momentum. The point of the transition manifests the birth of particle-bound rotational bands. Alternative possibility of the transition from particle-bound to resonance rotational band (the death of particle-bound rotational bands) with increasing spin also exists but it is less frequent in the calculations. The birth of particle-bound rotational bands provides a mechanism for the extension of nuclear landscape to neutron numbers which are larger than those of the neutron drip line in non-rotating nuclei.

# Rotational excitations in near neutron-drip line nuclei: the birth and death of particle-bound rotational bands and the extension of nuclear landscape beyond spin zero neutron drip line

## Key claim
Strong Coriolis interaction acting on high-j orbitals can transform particle-unbound (resonance) nucleonic configurations into particle-bound ones with increasing angular momentum, but this mechanism is conditional on the specific single-particle orbital crossing the continuum threshold at a characteristic rotational frequency; the reverse transition (particle-bound to resonance) also occurs but is less frequent in the calculations.

## Method
The authors performed cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) calculations using the NL3* functional, with no pairing correlations, for even-even Mg isotopes (42,44,46,48,50). The calculations employed an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis in Cartesian coordinates with up to N_F=18 fermionic shells and N_B=20 bosonic shells, and nucleonic configurations were specified by the occupation numbers of intruder, hyperintruder, and megaintruder orbitals of given parity and signature.

## Key numbers
- In 46Mg, the [3,0] configuration becomes particle-bound at spin I > 16 hbar (rotational frequency Omega_x >= 1.03 MeV), where the 3/2[431](r=-i) orbital crosses from positive to negative single-particle energy.
- The transition frequency omega_tran for the [3,0] configuration in 46Mg converges to 1.05 MeV at N_F=16 and above, with values of 1.27, 1.17, 1.11, and 1.05 MeV at N_F=10, 12, 14, and 16 respectively.
- In 46Mg, the [5,1] configuration becomes particle-bound at Omega_x ~ 1.6 MeV, and the [411,1] configuration becomes particle-bound at Omega_x ~ 1.8 MeV (I ~ 32.7 hbar).
- The difference in binding energy between N_F=18 and N_F=20 calculations for the [3,0] configuration in 46Mg is approximately 150 keV (0.06% of total binding energy), and the difference in moments of inertia is about 0.6%.
- In 48Mg, the [0,0] ground state band has only two particle-bound states at spins I=4 and 6 hbar; no particle-bound states exist in the [0,0] band of 50Mg.
- The [511,1] configuration in 50Mg, located four neutrons beyond the I=0 two-neutron drip line, becomes particle-bound at very high spin.

## Context
This work extends the study of rotating nuclei near the neutron drip line beyond previous investigations of non-rotating ground states, which defined the I=0 neutron drip line (e.g., Erler et al. 2012, Afanasjev et al. 2013, 2014). The mechanism of Coriolis-induced binding is analogous to the deformation-driven transition from proton-unbound to proton-bound toroidal shapes recently seen in hyperheavy nuclei (Afanasjev et al. 2019). The results enable the possibility of extending the nuclear landscape to neutron numbers beyond the spin-zero drip line through collective rotation, and suggest that particle-bound rotational bands in 48Mg and 50Mg could be observable via gamma-spectroscopy if gamma-decay half-lives are shorter than neutron emission half-lives.
