# Spin-orbit term and spin-fields: extension of Skyrme-force induced local energy density approach

> A systematic study of terminating states in A$\sim$50 mass region using the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model is presented. The objective is to demonstrate that the terminating states, due to their intrinsic simplicity, offer unique and so far unexplored opportunities to study different aspects of the effective NN interaction or nuclear local energy density functional. In particular, we demonstrate that the agreement of the calculations to the data depend on the spin fields and the spin-orbit term which, in turn, allows to constrain the appropriate Landau parameters and the strength of the spin-orbit potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy differences between terminating states in the A~50 mass region depend sensitively on both the spin fields and the spin-orbit term, allowing constraints on the Landau parameters and spin-orbit strength; however, the agreement between theory and experiment requires a unified treatment of spin fields (the L-LEDF approach) and a subsequent tuning of the spin-orbit strength, as the standard Skyrme-force-induced coupling constants systematically underestimate the experimental data by at least 10%.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the code hfodd for terminating states in the f7/2^n and d3/2^{-1}f7/2^{n+1} configurations of nuclei with 20 ≤ Z < N ≤ 24. They compared the calculated excitation energy differences ΔE_th between these two configurations against experimental data ΔE_exp, and systematically varied the spin-field coupling constants (via Landau parameters g0, g1) and the spin-orbit strength W0 to improve agreement.

## Key numbers

- The mean experimental energy difference between terminating states in the d3/2^{-1}f7/2^{n+1} and f7/2^n configurations is 5.489 MeV, with a standard deviation of 0.251 MeV (about 5%).
- For the 2p-2h excitation in 45Sc, the empirical excitation energy is 10.284 MeV, while the sum of the two known 1p-1h excitations gives 10.536 MeV, showing additivity holds within about 2%.
- The preferred isoscalar Landau parameter g0 is found to be in the range 0.4–0.8, with the minimum average deviation from data occurring near g0 ~ 0.4–0.8, while values g0 > 1.2 or negative g0 are ruled out.
- The standard deviation σ_ΔE is minimized for g0 ~ 0.8–1.2, above the preferred value of 0.4 from Gamow-Teller resonance studies.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-orbit splitting beyond the traditional method of comparing single-particle energies of spin-orbit doublets (e.g., Rutten (1998), Lopez (2000)), which suffers from scarce and uncertain data on deep-hole states. The authors instead use high-spin terminating states, which offer cleaner single-particle motion with suppressed correlations, building on the success of Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations reviewed by Afanasjev (1999). The results enable constraints on the time-odd components of the Skyrme energy density functional, complementing the analysis of Gamow-Teller resonances by Bender (2002) and providing a path toward improved effective interactions for N~Z nuclei.
