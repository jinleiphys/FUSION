# The imaginary part of the gap function in color superconductivity

> We clarify general properties of the energy gap regarding its functional dependence on the energy-momentum dictated by the invariance under a space inversion or a time reversal. Then we derive perturbatively the equation of the imaginary part of the gap function for dense QCD in weak coupling and generalize our results from 2SC case to CFL case. We confirm that the imaginary part is down by $g$ relative to the real part in weak coupling. The numerical results show that, up to the leading order, the imaginary part is no larger than one MeV at extremely large densities and can be as large as several MeV the densities are of physical interest.

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the gap function in color superconductivity is suppressed by a factor of the coupling constant \(g\) relative to the real part in weak coupling; the imaginary part is no larger than one MeV at extremely large densities but can reach several MeV at densities of physical interest (chemical potential below 1 GeV).

## Method

The authors derived the equation for the imaginary part of the gap function by analytically continuing the Eliashberg gap equation from Matsubara (Euclidean) energy to real energy, using spectral representations for the gluon propagators and the quark propagator. They then solved the coupled integral equations for the real and imaginary parts of the gap function in the weak-coupling limit, first analytically using the leading-order real gap solution from Schäfer and Wilczek (1999), and then numerically for the 2SC case at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The imaginary part of the gap function is suppressed by a factor of \(g\) relative to the real part in weak coupling.
- At extremely large densities (\(\mu = 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\)), the imaginary part of the gap function is smaller than \(1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At densities of physical interest (\(\mu = 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the imaginary part can be as large as several MeV.
- The leading-order analytic result for the imaginary part for \(\omega > \Delta_0\) is \(\mathrm{Im}\phi(\omega) \simeq \bar{g} \Delta_0 \frac{\pi}{2} \sin[\bar{g} \ln(\omega/\Delta_0)]\), where \(\bar{g} = g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)\) and \(\Delta_0 = 2\omega_c \exp(-3\pi^2/(\sqrt{2}g) - (\pi^2+4)/8)\).
- The cutoff scale \(\omega_c = 256\pi^4 \mu / g^5\) for two quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of color superconductivity in dense QCD, building on the Eliashberg theory framework used for strong-coupling electronic superconductors (Eliashberg, 1960) and on prior calculations of the real gap function by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and Schäfer and Wilczek (1999). The results confirm and generalize the earlier analysis by Rueter (2004) for the imaginary part near the Fermi surface, and enable more accurate calculations of transport and thermodynamic properties of quark matter in compact stars, where the imaginary part may affect damping and relaxation processes.
