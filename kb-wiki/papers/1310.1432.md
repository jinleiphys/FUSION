# ${}_{Î›Î›}^{4}$H in halo effective field theory

> The ${}_{Î›Î›}^{\ \ 4}$H bound state and the $S$-wave hypertriton(${}_Î›^{ \, 3}$H)-$Î›$ scattering in spin singlet and triplet channels below the hypertriton breakup momentum scale are studied in halo/cluster effective field theory at leading order by treating the ${}_{Î›Î›}^{\ \ 4}$H system as a three-cluster ($Î›$-$Î›$-deuteron) system. In the spin singlet channel, the amplitude is insensitive to the cutoff parameter $Î›_c$ introduced in the integral equation, and we find that there is no bound state. In this case, the scattering length of the hypertriton-$Î›$ scattering is found to be $a_0^{} = 16.0\pm 3.0$ fm. In the spin triplet channel, however, the amplitude obtained by the coupled integral equations is sensitive to $Î›_c$, and we introduce the three-body contact interaction $g_1^{} (Î›_c)$. After phenomenologically fixing $g_1^{} (Î›_c)$, we find that the correlation between the two-$Î›$ separation energy $B_{Î›Î›}$ from the $_{Î›Î›}^{\ \ 4}$H bound state and the scattering length $a_{Î›Î›}^{}$ of the $S$-wave $Î›$-$Î›$ scattering is significantly sensitive to the value of $Î›_c$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a bound state of ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H exists using halo effective field theory, but cannot make a robust prediction due to the lack of empirical data to constrain the three-body contact interaction; instead, the authors examine how the correlation between the two-$\Lambda$ separation energy $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ and the $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length $a_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ depends sensitively on the choice of cutoff parameter $\Lambda_c$.

## Method

The ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H system was treated as a three-cluster ($\Lambda$-$\Lambda$-deuteron) system in halo/cluster effective field theory at leading order. Integral equations for $S$-wave hypertriton-$\Lambda$ scattering were constructed for spin singlet and triplet channels below the hypertriton breakup momentum, with a sharp momentum cutoff $\Lambda_c$ introduced for regularization. For the spin triplet channel, a three-body contact interaction $g_1(\Lambda_c)$ was introduced to make results cutoff-independent, and its value was fixed phenomenologically using potential model calculations of $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ from Filikhin and Gal (2002) and Nemura, Akaishi, and Myint (2002).

## Key numbers

* Scattering length of $S$-wave hypertriton-$\Lambda$ scattering in the spin singlet channel: $a_0 = 16.0 \pm 3.0$ fm
* Hypertriton $\Lambda$ separation energy: $B_\Lambda^{\rm expt.} \simeq 0.13 \pm 0.05$ MeV
* Typical momentum scale from $\Lambda$ separation: $\gamma_{\Lambda d} = \sqrt{2\mu_{\Lambda d} B_\Lambda} \simeq 13.5 \pm 2.6$ MeV
* Deuteron binding momentum (high momentum scale): $\gamma = \sqrt{m_N B_2} \simeq 45.7$ MeV, with deuteron binding energy $B_2 \simeq 2.22$ MeV
* Expansion parameter: $Q/\Lambda_H \sim \gamma_{\Lambda d}/\gamma \simeq 1/3$
* For the spin triplet channel without three-body contact interaction, with $\Lambda_c \simeq \Lambda_H \sim 45.7$ MeV, scattering lengths $a_1 \simeq -21.7$, $-22.7$, $-23.8$, $-24.8$ fm for $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.5$, $-1.0$, $-1.5$, $-2.0$ fm respectively
* $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length from literature: $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -1.2 \pm 0.6$ fm (from ${}^{12}$C($K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda X)$ reaction, GHH11)

## Context

This work extends halo/cluster effective field theory methods, previously applied to few-nucleon systems and hypertriton by Hammer (2001), to the double-$\Lambda$ hypernucleus ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H. The study contradicts earlier Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2002) that found no bound state, while being consistent with subsequent potential model studies by Nemura, Akaishi, and Myint (2002) and others that predicted a possible bound state. The results enable future connections between bound state properties and scattering observables for double-$\Lambda$ systems, though experimental data to constrain the three-body contact interaction remain unavailable.
