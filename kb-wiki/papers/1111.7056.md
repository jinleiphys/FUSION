# Hyperfine structure splitting of the bound $S(L = 0)-$states in the symmetric muonic molecular ions

> The hyperfine structure splittings are determined for all five bound $S(L = 0)-$states in the three symmetric muonic molecular ions: $ppμ, ddμ$ and $ttμ$. The expectation values of all interparticle delta-functions used in our calculations have been determined in recent highly accurate computations.

# Hyperfine structure splitting of the bound $S(L = 0)-$states in the symmetric muonic molecular ions

## Key claim

The hyperfine structure splittings for all five bound $S(L = 0)-$states in the symmetric muonic molecular ions $pp\mu$, $dd\mu$, and $tt\mu$ are determined, with the caveat that the nuclear-nuclear spin-spin contribution is negligible for the heavy $dd\mu$ and $tt\mu$ ions (less than 0.0001% of the total), while for $pp\mu$ it is small but non-negligible.

## Method

The hyperfine structure operator is expressed in terms of expectation values of interparticle delta-functions for the nuclear-nuclear and nuclear-muon distances, using the fine structure constant, particle masses, and g-factors for the proton, deuteron, triton, and muon. Highly accurate computations of the delta-function expectation values were performed for each bound $S(L=0)$ state in the three symmetric muonic molecular ions, and the resulting hyperfine energy levels and splittings were tabulated in MHz.

## Key numbers

- For the $tt\mu$ ion ground state, the hyperfine energy of the $J=1/2$ level is approximately 11.0591 MHz; for the first excited state it is approximately 12.4307 MHz.
- For the $dd\mu$ ion, the hyperfine energies of the analogous $J=1/2$ levels are 6.8996 MHz (ground state) and 4.7378 MHz (excited state).
- The expectation value of the nuclear-nuclear delta-function for the $dd\mu$ ground state is $\langle \delta_{dd} \rangle \approx 2.43871205 \times 10^{-6}$ muon atomic units; for the excited state it is $\approx 1.67460229 \times 10^{-6}$ muon atomic units.
- The expectation value of the nuclear-nuclear delta-function for the $tt\mu$ ground state is $\langle \delta_{tt} \rangle \approx 2.15893994 \times 10^{-7}$ muon atomic units; for the excited state it is $\approx 2.42670033 \times 10^{-7}$ muon atomic units.
- The conversion factor used from atomic units to MHz is $6.57968392061 \times 10^9$ MHz/a.u.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis of hyperfine structure splitting in the non-symmetric muonic molecular ions $pd\mu$, $pt\mu$, and $dt\mu$ (Frolov, 2001) to the symmetric cases $pp\mu$, $dd\mu$, and $tt\mu$. The results enable precise predictions for muon-catalyzed fusion studies and tests of quantum electrodynamics in three-body muonic systems, where the hyperfine structure influences reaction rates. The calculations rely on the general formalism for hyperfine splitting in three-body systems (Landau, Lifshitz, and others) and on highly accurate delta-function expectation values from recent computations (Frolov and Ward, 2011).
