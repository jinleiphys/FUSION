# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Approach for Nuclear Matter with Non-Linear Coupling Terms

> We investigate the pairing property of nuclear matter with Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov(RHB) approach. Recently, the RHB approach has been widely applied to nuclear matter and finite nuclei. We have extended the RHB approach to be able to include non-linear coupling terms of mesons. In this paper we apply it to nuclear matter and observe the effect of non-linear terms on pairing gaps.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach to include non-linear self-coupling terms of mesons in the pairing channel, and find that these non-linear terms reduce the pairing gap in nuclear matter by a few MeV, especially around normal nuclear density, an effect neglected in prior RHB calculations.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Lagrangian with non-linear sigma and omega meson self-couplings, separate the meson fields into classical mean values and fluctuations, and integrate out the fluctuation fields under the harmonic approximation (neglecting terms of third order and higher in fluctuations). They then derive a relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation, but drop the Fock term and the Dirac sea, restrict to \(^1S_0\) pairing only, and solve the resulting BCS-type gap equation for nuclear matter using the TM1 and NL1 parameter sets with form factors from the Bonn C potential.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap at Fermi momentum is reduced by "a few MeV" when non-linear terms are included, with the effect largest around \(k_F = 1.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (normal nuclear density).
- Cut-off parameters used in form factors: \(\Lambda_\sigma = 1800\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_\omega = 1500\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_\rho = 1300\ \text{MeV}\) for the main calculation.
- With alternative cut-off values (\(\Lambda_\sigma = 2200\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_\omega = 1900\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_\rho = 1700\ \text{MeV}\)), the pairing gap increases largely but the peak position does not change much.
- For the NL1 parameter set, at higher density the pairing gap becomes "abnormally large" (exact numerical values not given in the truncated text) due to a negative sign in the quadratic self-coupling term causing \(\mu_\sigma\) to decrease with density.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism of Kucharek et al. (1991) by incorporating non-linear meson self-coupling terms, which were known to be essential in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory for reproducing nuclear matter and finite nucleus properties (Boguta and Bodmer, 1977; Sugahara and Toki, 1994). The non-linear terms modify the effective meson masses in the pairing channel propagators, reducing the pairing interaction; this effect was previously neglected in RHB calculations. The authors note that future work should include the Fock term and apply the formalism to asymmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei.
