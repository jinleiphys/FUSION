# Single Spin Asymmetries in Charged Pion Production from Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering on a Transversely Polarized $^3$He Target

> We report the first measurement of target single spin asymmetries in the semi-inclusive $^3{He}(e,e'π^\pm)X$ reaction on a transversely polarized target. The experiment, conducted at Jefferson Lab using a 5.9 GeV electron beam, covers a range of 0.14 $< x <$ 0.34 with 1.3 $<Q^2<$ 2.7 GeV$^2$. The Collins and Sivers moments were extracted from the azimuthal angular dependence of the measured asymmetries. The extracted $π^\pm$ Collins moments for $^3$He are consistent with zero, except for the $π^+$ moment at $x=0.34$, which deviates from zero by 2.3$σ$. While the $π^-$ Sivers moments are consistent with zero, the $π^+$ Sivers moments favor negative values. The neutron results were extracted using the nucleon effective polarization and the measured cross section ratio of proton to $^3$He, and are largely consistent with the predictions of phenomenological fits and quark model calculations.

## Key claim

The first measurement of target single spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on a transversely polarized 3He target finds that the extracted Collins moments for charged pions are mostly consistent with zero, with the exception of the π+ moment at x=0.34 which deviates from zero at the 2.3σ level, while the π+ Sivers moments favor negative values but do not reach a definitive significance.

## Method

The experiment was conducted at Jefferson Lab Hall A using a 5.9 GeV electron beam incident on a transversely polarized 3He target, with scattered electrons detected in the BigBite spectrometer at a central angle of 30 degrees and coincident charged hadrons detected in the High Resolution Spectrometer at a central angle of 16 degrees. The Collins and Sivers moments were extracted from the azimuthal angular dependence of the measured asymmetries using the formula A ≈ A_C sin(φ_h + φ_S) + A_S sin(φ_h − φ_S), where φ_h and φ_S are the azimuthal angles of the hadron momentum and target spin relative to the lepton scattering plane. Neutron results were extracted from the 3He data using the nucleon effective polarization approach and measured cross section ratios of proton to 3He.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic coverage: 0.14 < x < 0.34 with 1.3 < Q^2 < 2.7 GeV^2 (abstract) or 0.16 < x < 0.35 with 1.4 < Q^2 < 2.7 GeV^2 (full text)
- Beam energy: 5.9 GeV with average current of 12 μA
- Average target polarization: 55.4 ± 2.8%
- The π+ Collins moment at x = 0.34 deviates from zero by 2.3σ
- Central kinematics for four x bins: x = 0.156 (Q^2 = 1.38 GeV^2), x = 0.206 (Q^2 = 1.76 GeV^2), x = 0.265 (Q^2 = 2.16 GeV^2), x = 0.349 (Q^2 = 2.68 GeV^2)
- Pair production background fractions range from 1.0 ± 0.5% (π+ at highest x) to 24.0 ± 4.8% (π− at lowest x)
- Proton dilution factors (1 − f_p) range from 0.107 ± 0.026 (π+ at highest x) to 0.348 ± 0.032 (π− at lowest x)

## Context

This work extends the program of transverse spin measurements in SIDIS previously carried out by the HERMES collaboration on transversely polarized proton targets at Q^2 = 1.3–6.2 GeV^2 and by the COMPASS collaboration on deuteron and proton targets at Q^2 = 1.3–20.2 GeV^2. The 3He measurement provides direct access to neutron information, which is more sensitive to the d-quark contribution than combined proton and deuteron data, and the results are largely consistent with phenomenological fits by Anselmino et al. and quark model calculations. These measurements enable improved global extractions of the transversity and Sivers parton distribution functions, particularly for the d-quark, and help establish a consistent picture among proton, deuteron, and neutron data.
