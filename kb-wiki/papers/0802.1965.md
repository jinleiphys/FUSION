# Influence of the hadronic equation of state on the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars. The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory is adopted to describe the hadronic matter phase, while the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used for the quark matter phase. The influence of the hadronic equation of state on the phase transition and neutron star properties are investigated. We find that a neutron star possesses a large population of hyperons, but it is not dense enough to possess a pure quark core. Whether the mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter exist in the core of neutron stars depends on the RMF parameters used.

## Key claim
The paper finds that whether a mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter exists in neutron star cores depends on the specific RMF parameter set (NL3 vs. TM1) and the strength of hyperon-hyperon interactions, but in all cases the central density of the maximum-mass star is insufficient to produce a pure quark core.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with NL3 and TM1 parameter sets to model hadronic matter including the full baryon octet, and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter. They applied the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium to construct a charge-neutral mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute neutron star properties for each combination of RMF parameters and weak or strong hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions.

## Key numbers
- Mixed phase onset density for NL3 with weak YY: n_B^(1) ≈ 0.50 fm^{-3}; with strong YY: n_B^(1) ≈ 0.71 fm^{-3}
- Mixed phase end density for NL3 with weak YY: n_B^(2) ≈ 0.75 fm^{-3}; with strong YY: n_B^(2) ≈ 0.98 fm^{-3}
- Mixed phase onset density for TM1 with weak YY: n_B^(1) ≈ 1.31 fm^{-3}; with strong YY: n_B^(1) ≈ 1.75 fm^{-3}
- Mixed phase end density for TM1 with weak YY: n_B^(2) ≈ 2.03 fm^{-3}; with strong YY: n_B^(2) ≈ 2.49 fm^{-3}
- Maximum neutron star mass range across all RMF parameters: 1.62 to 2.05 solar masses (M_⊙)
- Hyperon potential depths used: U_Λ^(N) = -28 MeV, U_Σ^(N) = +30 MeV, U_Ξ^(N) = -18 MeV
- Weak YY interaction uses U_Λ^(Λ) ≈ -5 MeV; strong YY uses U_Λ^(Λ) ≈ -20 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron stars by Glendenning (1992) and others, using the same NJL model as in a prior study by the authors of Reference [PRC99] but with updated hyperon potentials constrained by the Nagara event observation of the double-Lambda hypernucleus ^6_ΛΛHe. The results are compared with those of Reference [NPA00] (which used an MIT bag model and found mixed phase onset at much lower densities around 2 times normal nuclear density) and with Reference [Menezes] (which found the onset sensitive to hyperon couplings). The paper shows that the deconfinement phase transition is highly sensitive to both the hadronic and quark equations of state, and that the choice of RMF model determines whether a mixed phase core can exist in neutron stars.
