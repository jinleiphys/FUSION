# Probing Nuclear Structure of Heavy Ions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We perform high-statistics simulations to study the impacts of nuclear structure on the ratios of anisotropic flow observables in $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb and $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Even with $40\%$ difference in atomic numbers between $^{208}$Pb and $^{129}$Xe nuclei, the ratios of anisotropic flow in the same centrality class between the two collision systems are strongly affected by the nuclear structure inputs in the initial state. The ratios of $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ in these collisions are sensitive to the nuclear skin thickness of the colliding nuclei, providing indirect constraints on the nuclei's neutron skin. Our model predictions serve as a benchmark to compare with experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The ratios of anisotropic flow observables between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can serve as probes of nuclear structure, with final-state hydrodynamic and hadronic effects canceling to high precision in these ratios, but only for specific centrality ranges and flow harmonics; the sensitivity to sub-nucleonic fluctuations and QGP viscosity varies by observable and centrality.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the IP-Glasma initial-state model coupled to MUSIC relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and the UrQCD hadronic transport model, with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities calibrated to Pb+Pb data at 5.02 TeV. They generated at least 100k minimum-bias events per parameter set, varying the Woods-Saxon parameters for the Xe nucleus (four sets with different surface thickness a_WS and quadrupole deformation beta_2) and for the Pb nucleus (three sets with different neutron skin thicknesses). The ratios of anisotropic flow coefficients and nonlinear response coefficients between Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV and Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV were computed across centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- The mass numbers of Pb and Xe differ by approximately 40%
- Minimum distance between nucleon pairs: d_min = 0.9 fm
- Hydrodynamic starting time: tau_hydro = 0.4 fm/c
- Switching energy density for hadronization: e_sw = 0.18 GeV/fm^3
- In 0-1% centrality, the v2{2} ratio between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb increases by about 20% as beta_2^WS for Xe changes from 0 to 0.207
- The rho_2 (v2-[pT] correlation) ratio between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb varies from 1.5 to 0.25 in central collisions when beta_2^WS for Xe varies from 0 to 0.207
- The ALICE measurement of the rho_422 ratio between the two systems reaches a value above 1.7 for 0-5% central collisions
- Three Pb neutron skin thicknesses studied: Delta_R_np = 0.149 fm, 0.201 fm, and 0.250 fm
- The RMS radius for the proton density of Pb: R_p = 5.435 fm

## Context

This work extends the program of using high-energy heavy-ion collisions to image nuclear structure, following studies of isobar collisions at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2021) and earlier comparisons of Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions (Giacalone, 2017). It provides benchmark predictions for existing ALICE measurements (2024) of flow ratios between these two systems, and identifies the v2{4}/v2{2} ratio as a new observable sensitive to neutron skin thickness, complementing low-energy nuclear experiments. The open-source simulation data enable future Bayesian inference analyses to simultaneously constrain nuclear structure and QGP properties.
