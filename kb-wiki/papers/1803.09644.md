# Effect of thresholds on the width of three-body resonances

> It has been recently reported an intriguing theoretical result of a narrow three-body resonance with a large available phase space. The resonance was reported in the $NΛΛ-ΞNN$ system near the $Ξd$ threshold, having a very small width in spite of the open $NΛΛ$ channel lying around 23 MeV below the $ΞNN$ channel. We use first-order perturbation theory as a plausible argument to explain this behavior. We apply our result to realistic local interactions. Other systems involving several thresholds are likely to follow the same behavior.

# Effect of thresholds on the width of three-body resonances

## Key claim
A narrow three-body resonance with a large available phase space (approximately 23 MeV) can be explained by first-order perturbation theory: when the resonance lies very near the upper channel threshold, the lower channel acts as a perturbation because the unperturbed wave function has a long range in coordinate space while the perturbation is short-range, resulting in a small overlap and thus a small width.

## Method
The authors used first-order perturbation theory, treating the $\Xi NN$ channel as the main interaction and the $N\Lambda\Lambda$ channel as the perturbation, to calculate the complex energy shift $\delta E$ from the overlap of the long-range unperturbed wave function with the short-range perturbation operator. They applied this formalism to both a separable potential model (from Garcilazo et al. 2015) and a local Malfliet-Tjon type potential model (using Nijmegen ESC08 potentials) for the $N\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi NN$ system with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}^+)$.

## Key numbers
- The three-body resonance from the exact calculation lies at $E_0 = 23.408 - i\,0.045$ MeV measured with respect to the $N\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold, which is 0.012 MeV below the $\Xi d$ threshold.
- The $\Xi NN$ and $\Xi d$ thresholds are 25.604 MeV and 23.420 MeV above the $N\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold, respectively.
- The exact complex energy shift from coupling the $N\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\Xi NN$ channels is $\delta E = 0.022 - i\,0.045$ MeV.
- The first-order perturbation theory calculation with the separable model gives $\delta E = 0.014 - i\,0.015$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ two-body subsystem in the $(i,j^p)=(0,0^+)$ channel (the $H$ dibaryon channel) is bound with a binding energy of 6.4 MeV in the constituent quark model.
- The $\Xi NN$ state in the local model lies less than 0.01 MeV above the $\Xi d$ threshold.

## Context
This work extends the findings of Garcilazo et al. (2015), who reported a narrow three-body resonance in the $N\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi NN$ system using separable potentials fitted to the ESC08 Nijmegen potential, which accounts for the NAGARA event (Takahashi et al. 2001) and the KISO event (Nakazawa et al. 2015). The authors provide a theoretical explanation for why such a resonance can have a very small width despite a large available phase space, contradicting naive expectations. The mechanism may also help explain narrow widths in heavy hadron spectra, such as the $XYZ$ states near meson-antimeson thresholds discussed by Chen et al. (2016) and Pilloni et al. (2017), and the LHCb pentaquarks, where similar threshold effects could produce narrow resonances from meson-antimeson molecules.
