# How strange are compact star interiors ?

> We discuss a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quantum field theoretical approach to the quark matter equation of state with color superconductivity and construct hybrid star models on this basis. It has recently been demonstrated that with increasing baryon density, the different quark flavors may occur sequentially, starting with down-quarks only, before the second light quark flavor and at highest densities also the strange quark flavor appears. We find that color superconducting phases are favorable over non-superconducting ones which entails consequences for thermodynamic and transport properties of hybrid star matter. In particular, for NJL-type models no strange quark matter phases can occur in compact star interiors due to mechanical instability against gravitational collapse, unless a sufficiently strong flavor mixing as provided by the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction is present in the model. We discuss observational data on mass-radius relationships of compact stars which can put constraints on the properties of dense matter equation of state.

## Key claim

For NJL-type models, strange quark matter phases cannot occur in compact star interiors due to mechanical instability against gravitational collapse, unless a sufficiently strong flavor mixing interaction (the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction) is present in the model.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star models by combining a hadronic equation of state (the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach) with a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that includes color superconductivity via scalar diquark condensates. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for nonrotating compact star sequences, varying the diquark coupling strength (η_D = G_D/G_S) and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) flavor-mixing coupling to determine which parameter combinations yield stable hybrid star configurations with quark matter cores.

## Key numbers

- For diquark coupling η_D = 1.35, the onset of a 2SC (two-flavor superconducting) quark core occurs at a stellar mass of M = 0.6 M_⊙, followed by a stable branch of hybrid stars with CFL (color-flavor-locked) quark matter cores in the mass range 0.9 < M/M_⊙ < 1.46.
- For η_D = 1.2, no stable configurations with quark matter exist.
- In the parameter range 1.25 < η_D < 1.3, mass twin solutions are possible, where the more compact configuration has a CFL quark matter core.
- The density range for intermediate softening of the EoS due to diquark condensation is approximately 0.4 - 0.8 fm^{-3}.
- The measured mass of binary radiopulsars is M_BRP = 1.35 ± 0.04 M_⊙ (Thorsett 1998).

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies by Ruester (2005) and Blaschke (2005) that differed in whether they included the KMT flavor-mixing interaction, and builds on the finding by Agrawal (2010) that this flavor mixing is crucial for the possible stability of strange quark matter phases in hybrid stars. The results contradict earlier bag model approaches (Baldo 2003) that did not predict the sequential flavor appearance or the mechanical instability without flavor mixing. The work enables observational tests of the "third family" of supercompact stars through future high-precision measurements of compact star masses and radii (Freire 2009, Ozel 2009), which could constrain the dense matter equation of state.
