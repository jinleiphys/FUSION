# Application of realistic effective interactions to the structure of the Zr isotopes

> We calculate the low-lying spectra of the zirconium isotopes Z=40 with neutron numbers from N=52 to N=60 using the 1p1/20g9/2 proton and 2s1d0g7/20h11/2 neutron sub-shells to define the model space. Effective proton-proton, neutron--neutron and proton-neutron interactions have been derived using 88Sr as closed core and employing perturbative many-body techniques. The starting point is the nucleon-nucleon potential derived from modern meson exchange models. The comprehensive shell-model calculation performed in this work provides a qualitative reproduction of essential properties such as the sub-shell closures in 96Zr and 98Zr.

## Key claim

The paper claims a "qualitative reproduction" of essential properties of Zr isotopes, such as sub-shell closures in 96Zr and 98Zr, but the results for 98Zr are described as "disappointing" and only the 2+1 level is "reasonably reproduced."

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for Zr isotopes (Z=40, N=52-60) using a model space of 1p1/2 and 0g9/2 proton orbitals and 2s1d0g7/2 and 0h11/2 neutron orbitals, with 88Sr as the closed core. Effective proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron interactions were derived from the charge-dependent Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using perturbative many-body techniques, including the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix and a Q-box expansion through third order in G. The eigenvalue problem was solved using the Lanczos algorithm in an m-scheme Slater determinant basis.

## Key numbers

- Experimental 0+1 to 2+1 gap in 96Zr: 1.751 MeV; calculated gap: 1.426 MeV
- Experimental 0+1 to 2+1 gap in 98Zr: 1.223 MeV; calculated gap: 1.463 MeV
- Experimental 0+1 to 2+1 gap in 94Zr: 0.919 MeV; calculated gap: 0.520 MeV
- 96Zr ground state 1d5/2 occupation number: 5.66
- 98Zr ground state 1d5/2 occupation number: 5.76; 2s1/2 occupation number: 1.87
- Effective charges used: 1.8e for protons, 1.5e for neutrons
- Oscillator parameter b: 2.25 fm
- Experimental E2 transition rate 0+2 to 2+1 in 92Zr: 14.3(5) W.u.; calculated rate two to three orders of magnitude smaller
- Experimental E2 transition rate 0+2 to 2+1 in 94Zr: 9.3(4) W.u.; calculated rate two to three orders of magnitude smaller
- Experimental E2 transition rate 0+3 to 2+1 in 98Zr: 51(5) W.u.; calculated rate negligible
- Single-proton energy splitting epsilon(1p1/2) - epsilon(0g9/2): 0.9 MeV
- G-matrix and Q-box evaluated at omega = -20 MeV
- 98Zr system: more than 26,000,000 basis states

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of lighter systems (sd- and pf-shell nuclei) and heavier systems with like valence nucleons (Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones) to the Zr isotopes, which contain both valence protons and neutrons. The calculation is the first non-truncated shell-model study of Zr isotopes up to 98Zr using a fully realistic effective interaction derived from modern meson exchange models. The results enable further testing of realistic effective interactions in heavy nuclei with both proton and neutron valence spaces, though the authors note that bulk properties such as binding energies remain problematic, as discussed in prior work by Zuker (1994).
