# Theory of two-pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon

> A theory of two-pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon is derived considering all explicit three-body mechanisms of the interacting $ππN$ system. The full three-body dynamics of the interacting $ππN$ system is accounted for by the Faddeev-type ordering structure of the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations. The formulation is valid for hadronic two-point and three-point functions dressed by arbitrary internal mechanisms provided all associated electromagnetic currents are constructed to satisfy their respective (generalized) Ward-Takahashi identities. It is shown that coupling the photon to the Faddeev structure of the underlying hadronic two-pion production mechanisms results in a natural expansion of the full two-pion photoproduction current $M_{ππ}^μ$ in terms of multiple dressed loops involving two-body subsystem scattering amplitudes of the $ππN$ system that preserves gauge invariance as a matter of course order by order in the number of (dressed) loops. A closed-form expression is presented for the entire gauge-invariant current $M_{ππ}^μ$ with complete three-body dynamics. Individually gauge-invariant truncations of the full dynamics most relevant for practical applications at the no-loop, one-loop, and two-loop levels are discussed in detail. An approximation scheme to the full two-pion amplitude for calculational purposes is also presented. It approximates, systematically, the full amplitude to any desired order of expansion in the underlying hadronic two-body amplitude. Moreover, it allows for the approximate incorporation of all neglected higher-order mechanisms in terms of a phenomenological remainder current. The effect and phenomenological usefulness of this remainder current is assessed in a tree-level calculation of the $γN \to K K Ξ$ reaction.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete, gauge-invariant theoretical framework for two-pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon that accounts for all explicit three-body mechanisms of the interacting ππN system via Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations, but the authors acknowledge that the full amplitude is highly nonlinear and truncations are unavoidable in practical calculations, with gauge invariance maintained at each truncation level.

## Method

The authors apply the gauge-derivative procedure of Haberzettl (1997) to couple a photon to the full N → ππN four-point hadronic vertex, whose microscopic structure is described using Faddeev-type three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations to sum the multiple-scattering series of the interacting ππN system. The resulting two-pion photoproduction current M^μ_ππ is expressed in closed form and decomposed into contributions at no-loop, one-loop, and two-loop levels, each individually gauge invariant. An approximation scheme is presented that expands the full amplitude in powers of the underlying hadronic two-body T-matrix elements, with neglected higher-order mechanisms incorporated via a phenomenological remainder current.

## Key numbers

- The S11(1535), S31(1620), and D13(1520) resonances are found to be relevant to two-pion photoproduction up to center-of-mass energy W = 1.7 GeV, based on the EBAC/ANL-Osaka group's dynamical coupled-channels approach.
- The tree-level calculation of the γN → KKΞ reaction is used to assess the effect and phenomenological usefulness of the remainder current.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach of Haberzettl (1997) for single-pion photoproduction to the two-pion case, subsuming and surpassing all existing approaches based on hadronic degrees of freedom. It provides a more complete theoretical framework than the tree-level effective Lagrangian approaches used by groups such as Gomez and Oset (1994, 1995), Nacher et al. (2000), and others, as well as the isobar-type approximations of the EBAC/ANL-Osaka group (Kamano et al., 2009). The formalism enables systematic inclusion of final-state interactions and sequential decay mechanisms needed to address the "missing resonance" problem in baryon spectroscopy, and can be applied to future analyses of data from MAMI, ELSA, GRAAL, and Jefferson Lab.
