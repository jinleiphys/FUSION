# Tests of Lorentz and CPT symmetry with hadrons and nuclei

> We explore the breaking of Lorentz and CPT invariance in strong interactions at low energy in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. Starting from the set of Lorentz-violating operators of mass-dimension five with quark and gluon fields, we construct the effective chiral Lagrangian with hadronic and electromagnetic interactions induced by these operators. We develop the power-counting scheme and discuss loop diagrams and the one-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential. The effective chiral Lagrangian is the basis for calculations of low-energy observables with hadronic degrees of freedom. As examples, we consider clock-comparison experiments with nuclei and spin-precession experiments with nucleons in storage rings. We derive strict limits on the dimension-five tensors that quantify Lorentz and CPT violation.

## Key claim

Lorentz and CPT violation in the strong interaction sector can be systematically studied using chiral perturbation theory, with the strongest experimental constraints coming from dimension-five operators that generate nucleon two-point functions, while operators that do not produce such tree-level couplings (like those involving the $D^\pm_{\mu\nu\rho}$ tensors) are less constrained and may be probed through loop-induced effects or nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors start from a set of Lorentz-violating operators of mass-dimension five with quark and gluon fields, then construct the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, and photons using chiral perturbation theory with the heavy-baryon approach. They develop the power-counting scheme for these operators, discuss pion-loop diagrams that induce Lorentz-violating contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factor, and derive the one-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential from the Lorentz-violating pion-nucleon vertices.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant $F_\pi \simeq 185$ MeV sets the scale of chiral symmetry breaking $\Lambda_\chi \simeq 2\pi F_\pi \sim 1$ GeV
- The axial-vector coupling $g_A \simeq 1.27$ appears in the loop contributions to the Lorentz-violating form factors
- The Lorentz-violating coupling constants scale as $\Lambda_\chi^2/\Lambda_{\rm LV}$ or $\Lambda_\chi F_\pi/\Lambda_{\rm LV}$, giving a suppression of order $10^{-18}$ to $10^{-19}$ if $\Lambda_{\rm LV}$ is the Planck scale
- The isoscalar form factor at zero momentum transfer contains a chiral logarithm $\ln(m_N^2/m_\pi^2)$ with coefficient $\tilde{D}^{-}_{\nu\rho\sigma} \cdot 8 e g_A / (2\pi F_\pi)^2$

## Context

This work extends the standard-model extension (SME) framework for Lorentz and CPT violation into the nonperturbative regime of quantum chromodynamics, where most precision tests of Lorentz symmetry occur. It builds on the classification of dimension-five operators by the same group (Noordmans et al., 2015) and applies chiral perturbation theory techniques previously used for parity violation (Kaplan and Manohar, 1993) and time-reversal violation (Mereghetti et al., 2010). The results enable systematic calculations of Lorentz-violating effects in clock-comparison experiments with nuclei and spin-precession experiments with nucleons in storage rings, and provide a framework for future lattice QCD calculations of the relevant low-energy constants.
