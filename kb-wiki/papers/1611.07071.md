# Effects of Induced Surface Tension in Nuclear and Hadron Matter

> Short range particle repulsion is rather important property of the hadronic and nuclear matter equations of state. We present a novel equation of state which is based on the virial expansion for the multicomponent mixtures with hard-core repulsion. In addition to the hard-core repulsion taken into account by the proper volumes of particles, this equation of state explicitly contains the surface tension which is induced by another part of the hard-core repulsion between particles. At high densities the induced surface tension vanishes and the excluded volume treatment of hard-core repulsion is switched to its proper volume treatment. Possible applications of this equation of state to a description of hadronic multiplicities measured in A+A collisions, to an investigation of the nuclear matter phase diagram properties and to the neutron star interior modeling are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel equation of state (IST EoS) that, in addition to the usual hard-core repulsion accounted for by particle proper volumes, explicitly includes a surface tension term induced by another part of the hard-core repulsion; at high densities this induced surface tension vanishes, switching the treatment from excluded volume to proper volume, and the model is shown to reproduce the third and fourth virial coefficients of a hard-sphere gas with small deviations.

## Method

The authors derive a thermodynamically consistent equation of state from the virial expansion for multicomponent mixtures with hard-core repulsion, resulting in two coupled nonlinear equations for the pressure \(p\) and the induced surface tension coefficient \(\Sigma\) (Eqs. I and II). A dimensionless parameter \(\alpha\) is introduced to control the switch between excluded-volume and proper-volume regimes; its value is fixed by comparing the model's virial coefficients to those of the Carnahan-Starling (CS) equation of state for a one-component hard-sphere gas. The IST EoS is then applied to fit 111 independent hadronic multiplicity ratios from central A+A collisions at 14 center-of-mass energies (2.7 to 200 GeV) using a hadron resonance gas model, and separately to construct a version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) with compressible nuclear liquid to study the nuclear matter phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(\alpha\) is fixed to 1.25; this yields third and fourth virial coefficients \(B_3(\alpha=1.25) \simeq 11.5\,v^2\) (relative error +15%) and \(B_4(\alpha=1.25) \simeq 17.59\,v^3\) (relative error -4.1%) compared to the exact hard-sphere values \(B_3^{hs}=10\,v^2\) and \(B_4^{hs}=18.36\,v^3\).
- The IST EoS reproduces the CS equation of state up to a baryon packing fraction \(\eta \sim 0.22\), corresponding to about five times normal nuclear density; the excluded-volume model (EVM) is only valid up to \(\eta \simeq 0.11\).
- The best global fit to 111 hadronic multiplicity ratios gives \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 57.099/55 \simeq 1.038\) with hard-core radii: baryons \(R_b = 0.365\) fm, mesons \(R_m = 0.42\) fm, pions \(R_\pi = 0.15\) fm, kaons \(R_K = 0.395\) fm, and \(\Lambda\)-hyperons \(R_\Lambda = 0.085\) fm.
- In the SMM version, the tricritical endpoint is found at temperature \(T = 18\) MeV and baryonic density \(\rho_{cep} = \rho_0 / 3\), where \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear density; the proper surface tension at zero temperature is \(\sigma_0 = 18\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bugaev (2013) by formulating a thermodynamically consistent equation of state that explicitly includes induced surface tension from hard-core repulsion, going beyond the usual Van der Waals excluded-volume model. It improves upon the multicomponent Hadron Resonance Gas Model (MHRGM) of PBM (2006), Horn, SFO, and Veta14 by reducing the number of coupled equations to two regardless of the number of particle species, and it provides a more realistic version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) of Bondorf (1995) with a compressible nuclear liquid that avoids non-monotonic isotherms in the mixed-phase region. The results enable more accurate modeling of hadron multiplicities in heavy-ion collisions, nuclear matter phase diagram properties, and neutron star interiors.
