# A fully relativistic description of Hypernuclear production in proton- and pion-Nucleus Collisions

> Exclusive A(p,K^+){_Λ}B and A(π,K^+){_Λ}B^\prime reactions leading to two body final states, have been investigated in a fully covariant model based on an effective Lagrangian picture. The explicit kaon production vertex is described via creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of N*(1650), N*(1710) and N*(1720) intermediate baryonic resonance states, in the initial collision of the projectile with one of the target nucleons. The bound state wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. The calculated cross sections show strong sensitivity to the final hypernuclear state excited in the reaction. Cross sections of 1 - 2 nb/sr are obtained at peak positions of favored transitions in case of the A(p,K^+){_Λ}B reaction on heavier targets.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully covariant two-nucleon model (TNM) for hypernuclear production via proton- and pion-induced reactions yields cross sections that are strongly sensitive to the final hypernuclear state, with peak values of 1–2 nb/sr for favored transitions on heavier targets, but this sensitivity and the angular distribution shapes depend critically on the inclusion of medium corrections to the pion propagator and on the relativistic treatment of bound-state Dirac spinors.

## Method

The authors model the exclusive \(A(p,K^+){_\Lambda}B\) and \(A(\pi,K^+){_\Lambda}B^\prime\) reactions using a fully covariant effective Lagrangian approach. The kaon production vertex proceeds via creation, propagation, and decay of intermediate baryonic resonances \(N^*(1650)\), \(N^*(1710)\), and \(N^*(1720)\) excited in the initial collision of the projectile with a target nucleon, mediated by \(\pi\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) exchange. Bound-state wave functions for the target nucleons and the final hyperon are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with scalar and vector Woods-Saxon potentials, and the pion self-energy is renormalized with a Landau-Migdal parameter \(g^\prime\) to account for medium effects.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^{40}\text{Ca}(p,K^+){^{41}_\Lambda}\text{Ca}(0d_{3/2})\) reaction at 2.0 GeV incident proton energy, the differential cross section shows a strong dependence on the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g^\prime\), with values of 0.5, 0.6, and 0.7 producing distinct curves (Fig. 4).
* For the \(^{12}\text{C}(p,K^+){^{13}_\Lambda}\text{C}\) reaction at 1.8 GeV, the cross section is maximum for the hypernuclear state with the largest orbital angular momentum, with angular distributions peaking at angles larger than \(0^\circ\) for heavier targets.
* For the \(^{4}\text{He}(p,K^+){^{5}_\Lambda}\text{He}\) reaction at 2.0 GeV, the differential cross section peaks near \(0^\circ\) (Fig. 6).
* The momentum density of the \(\Lambda\) hyperon shell in the momentum region around 0.35 GeV/c is at least 2–3 orders of magnitude larger than that around 1.0 GeV/c, explaining the larger cross sections in the TNM compared to the one-nucleon model (ONM).

## Context

This work extends the covariant two-nucleon model previously developed by Shyam (2004, 2006) for the \((p,K^+)\) reaction, and contrasts with earlier one-nucleon model (ONM) calculations by Krippa (1995) and Cooper (1982), which suffered from very low cross sections due to sensitivity to high-momentum components of bound-state wave functions. The study also builds on experimental hypernuclear spectroscopy using \((K^-,\pi^-)\) reactions (Chrien 1989, Bando 1990) and \((\pi^+,K^+)\) reactions (Hashimoto 2006), and aims to enable a comparison of hypernuclear states excited by different probes to better understand hyperon-nucleon interactions and medium effects on pion propagation in nuclei.
