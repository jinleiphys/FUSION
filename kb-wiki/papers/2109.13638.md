# Uncertainties in the pasta-phase properties of catalysed neutron stars

> The interior of a neutron star is expected to exhibit different states of matter. In particular, complex non-spherical configurations known as `pasta' phases may exist at the highest densities in the inner crust, potentially having an impact on different neutron-star phenomena. We study the properties of the pasta phase and the uncertainties in the pasta observables which are due to our incomplete knowledge of the nuclear energy functional. To this aim, we employed a compressible liquid-drop model approach with surface parameters optimised either on experimental nuclear masses or theoretical calculations. To assess the model uncertainties, we performed a Bayesian analysis by largely varying the model parameters using uniform priors, and generating posterior distributions with filters accounting for both our present low-density nuclear physics knowledge and high-density neutron-star physics constraints. Our results show that the nuclear physics constraints, such as the neutron-matter equation of state at very low density and the experimental mass measurements, are crucial in determining the crustal and pasta observables. Accounting for all constraints, we demonstrate that the presence of pasta phases is robustly predicted in an important fraction of the inner crust. We estimate the relative crustal thickness associated with pasta phases as $R_{\rm pasta}/R_{\rm crust}=0.128\pm 0.047$ and the relative moment of inertia as $I_{\rm pasta}/I_{\rm crust}=0.480\pm 0.137$. Our findings indicate that the surface and curvature parameters are more influential than the bulk parameters for the description of pasta observables. We also show that using a surface tension that is inconsistent with the bulk functional leads to an underestimation of both the average values and the uncertainties in the pasta properties, thus highlighting the importance of a consistent calculation of the nuclear functional.

## Key claim

The presence of pasta phases in the inner crust of catalysed neutron stars is robustly predicted, but the quantitative properties (thickness, moment of inertia) carry substantial uncertainties that depend critically on the nuclear energy functional, with surface and curvature parameters being more influential than bulk parameters.

## Method

The authors employed a compressible liquid-drop model extended to non-spherical pasta geometries (rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) and performed a Bayesian analysis with uniform priors over 14 nuclear model parameters, generating posterior distributions by applying filters from low-density chiral effective field theory calculations, experimental nuclear mass measurements from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016, and high-density neutron-star constraints including stability, causality, and a maximum mass above 1.97 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Relative crustal thickness associated with pasta phases: R_pasta/R_crust = 0.128 ± 0.047
- Relative moment of inertia of the pasta layer: I_pasta/I_crust = 0.480 ± 0.137
- Posterior distribution for symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = (30.8 ± 1.3) MeV
- Posterior distribution for slope of symmetry energy: L_sym = (47.3 ± 9.2) MeV
- Transition density from spheres to cylinders for BSk24: 4.84 × 10^{-2} fm^{-3}
- Crust-core transition density for BSk24: 8.20 × 10^{-2} fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop model of Carreau (2019, 2020) to include non-spherical pasta structures and provides a Bayesian uncertainty quantification that goes beyond earlier point estimates from specific functionals. The results are compared with extended Thomas-Fermi calculations by Pearson (2020) for BSk24 and with calculations by Martin (2015), Douchin (2001), Vinas (2017), and Grill (2012) for other functionals. The posterior distributions are shown to be compatible with constraints from the gravitational-wave event GW170817 (Abbott 2018) and NICER observations (Miller 2019, 2021), while disfavoring the high symmetry energy values reported by Reed (2021) from PREX measurements.
