# The signal of $Z^\pm(4430)$ in nucleon-antinucleon scattering

> We study the production of $Z^\pm(4430)$ at a nucleon-antinucleon scattering experiment. Considering the PANDA experiment to be an ideal platform to explore the production of the charmonium and charmonim-like states, we suggest the forthcoming PANDA experiment to pay attention to the production of $Z^\pm(4430)$.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the PANDA experiment at FAIR could observe the exotic charmonium-like state \(Z^\pm(4430)\) in nucleon-antinucleon scattering, but the predicted event rates depend strongly on the unknown decay width of \(Z^+(4430) \to p\bar n\); if that width is as small as keV, only \(10\) to \(10^2\) events per year would be produced.

## Method

The authors use a hadron-level pole diagram, exchanging a neutron between the proton and antiproton, to compute the cross sections for \(p\bar p \to Z^+(4430)\pi^-\) and the subsequent decay \(Z^+(4430) \to \psi'\pi^+\). They also compute the process \(\bar p n \to Z^-(4430) \to \psi'\pi^-\) including background diagrams. Three possible quantum numbers for \(Z^\pm(4430)\) are considered: \(J^P = 0^-, 1^-, 2^-\). Coupling constants for the \(Z^+ p \bar n\) vertex are estimated by assuming the total width of \(Z^+(4430)\) (44 MeV) is entirely due to the \(p\bar n\) decay mode, while the \(Z\pi\psi'\) couplings are fixed by taking \(\Gamma(Z^+ \to \psi'\pi^+) = 1\) MeV based on a molecular-state calculation by Meng and Chao (2007).

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(Z^\pm(4430)\): \(m_Z = 4433\) MeV, total width \(\Gamma_Z = 44\) MeV (from Belle).
- For \(p\bar p \to Z^+(4430)\pi^-\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 5\) GeV, the maximum annual event rates at PANDA (assuming \(\Gamma_{Z^+ \to p\bar n} = 44\) MeV) are: \(8.9 \times 10^{10}\) events for \(J^P = 0^-\), \(1.4 \times 10^{12}\) events for \(J^P = 1^-\), and \(2.9 \times 10^{12}\) events for \(J^P = 2^-\).
- If \(\Gamma_{Z^+ \to p\bar n}\) is about 1 MeV, the above event numbers are suppressed by a factor \((1/44)^2 \sim 5 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The cross section for \(p\bar p \to Z^+(4430)\pi^- \to \psi'\pi^+\pi^-\) is \(3\) to \(4\) orders of magnitude smaller than that for \(p\bar p \to Z^+(4430)\pi^-\).
- The coupling constants used for \(Z^+ \to \psi'\pi^+\) are: \(g_{Z\pi\psi'}^{(0)} = 0.08\), \(g_{Z\pi\psi'}^{(1)} = 0.05\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(g_{Z\pi\psi'}^{(2)} = 0.33\).
- The maximum coupling constants for \(Z^+ p \bar n\) (assuming \(\Gamma_{Z^+ \to p\bar n} = 44\) MeV) are: \(g_{Zpn}^{(0)} = 0.52\), \(g_{Zpn}^{(1)} = 1.27\), \(g_{Zpn}^{(2)} = 0.36\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the hadron-level pole diagram method previously applied to charmonium production in \(p\bar p\) scattering by Gaillard, Maiani and Petronzio (1987) and later by Barnes and Li (2003) and Lundborg, Barnes and Wiedner (2005). It specifically targets the exotic candidate \(Z^\pm(4430)\), which was observed only by the Belle Collaboration (2007) in \(B \to K\psi'\pi^\pm\) and has been interpreted as a molecular state, tetraquark, or cusp effect. The study enables the PANDA experiment to search for \(Z^\pm(4430)\) in a complementary production channel, which could help establish its existence and clarify its internal structure.
