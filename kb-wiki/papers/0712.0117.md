# Color superconducting quark matter in compact stars

> Recent indications for high neutron star masses (M \sim 2 M_sun) and large radii (R > 12 km) could rule out soft equations of state and have provoked a debate whether the occurence of quark matter in compact stars can be excluded as well. We show that modern quantum field theoretical approaches to quark matter including color superconductivity and a vector meanfield allow a microscopic description of hybrid stars which fulfill the new, strong constraints. For these objects color superconductivity turns out to be an essential ingredient for a successful description of the cooling phenomenology in accordance with recently developed tests. We discuss the energy release in the neutrino untrapping transition as a new aspect of the problem that hybrid stars masquerade themselves as neutron stars. Quark matter searches in future generations of low-temperature/high-density nucleus-nucleus collision experiments such as low-energy RHIC and CBM @ FAIR might face the same problem of an almost crossover behavior of the deconfinement transition. Therefore, diagnostic tools shall be derived from effects of color superconductivity.

# Color superconducting quark matter in compact stars

## Key claim

Modern quantum field theoretical approaches to quark matter that include both color superconductivity and a vector meanfield can produce hybrid star configurations satisfying the new strong observational constraints on high masses (M ~ 2 M_sun) and large radii (R > 12 km), but only if the diquark coupling and vector coupling parameters are tuned within narrow ranges (η_D = 1.02–1.03, η_V = 0.5) to simultaneously satisfy the flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid star equation of state using a two-phase description: the hadronic phase is described by the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, and the quark matter phase by a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with color superconductivity and an isoscalar vector meson mean field. The phase transition between hadronic and quark matter is obtained via a Maxwell construction, and the resulting equation of state is used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for compact star configurations under constraints of charge neutrality and β-equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Observed neutron star masses: M = 1.96 +0.09/-0.12 M_sun for PSR B1516+02B and M = 2.73 ± 0.25 M_sun for PSR J1748-2021B
- Radius constraint: R > 12 km for the isolated neutron star RX J1856.5-3754
- Mass constraint for EXO 0748-676: M ≥ 2.10 ± 0.28 M_sun and R ≥ 13.8 ± 0.18 km
- Diquark coupling parameter range for appropriate deconfinement density: η_D = 1.02 – 1.03
- Vector coupling parameter for flow constraint satisfaction: η_V = 0.5
- Energy release (mass defect) from neutrino untrapping transition: ΔM = 0.04 M_sun, nearly independent of star mass and structure
- Minimal mass for direct Urca cooling in DBHF neutron star matter: M_DU (indicated by a red dot in Fig. 2, no numerical value given in text)
- CFL pairing gap scale: Δ_CFL ∼ 100 MeV
- Small gap required for viable quark matter: Δ_X ∼ 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the debate initiated by Ozel (2006), who argued that new compact star constraints would exclude quark matter in compact star interiors, and the counter-examples provided by Alford et al. (2006) demonstrating that quark matter cannot be excluded. The authors show that hybrid stars with color superconducting quark matter can masquerade as neutron stars by having very similar mechanical properties (mass-radius relations, moments of inertia), and add a new aspect: the neutrino untrapping transition releases the same energy (0.04 M_sun) for both neutron stars and hybrid stars. The results enable future diagnostic tests based on transport properties modified by color superconductivity, particularly for distinguishing quark cores from hadronic ones through cooling behavior and r-mode stability, with implications for upcoming experiments such as CBM at FAIR.
