# Quantized Skyrmions from SU(4) Weight Diagrams

> Starting from solutions of the lightly-bound Skyrme model, we construct many new Skyrmion solutions of the standard Skyrme model with tetrahedral or octahedral symmetry. These solutions are closely related to weight diagrams of the group SU(4), which enables us to systematically derive some geometric and energetic properties of the Skyrmions, up to baryon number 85. We discuss the rigid body quantization of these Skyrmions, and compare the results with properties of a selection of observed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper constructs many new Skyrmion solutions of the standard Skyrme model with tetrahedral or octahedral symmetry by using weight diagrams of SU(4) as starting configurations, but the abstract's claim of systematic derivation up to baryon number 85 is qualified by the fact that some small clusters do not give particularly stable Skyrmions, and the clusters must be numerically relaxed from the lightly-bound model to obtain true solutions.

## Method

The authors start with clusters of B=1 Skyrmions placed at points of an FCC lattice corresponding to distinct weights of SU(4) irreducible representations, with orientations assigned periodically (using quaternions 1,i,j,k) to maximize nearest-neighbor attraction. These initial configurations, which are approximate solutions of the lightly-bound Skyrme model, are then numerically relaxed using gradient flow in the standard Skyrme model with pion mass m=1 to obtain true Skyrmion solutions. The resulting Skyrmions are quantized using rigid body quantization, where the allowed spin, isospin, and parity states are determined by the residual tetrahedral or octahedral symmetry and Finkelstein-Rubinstein sign representations.

## Key numbers

- Baryon numbers of constructed Skyrmions: B = 1, 4, 6, 10, 13, 16, 19, 20, 28, 31, 35, 38, 40, 44, 50, 52, 55, 56, 68, 79, 80, 84, 85
- Binding energy per baryon for B=28 Skyrmion: 0.147 (in Skyrme units)
- Binding energy per baryon for B=38 Skyrmion: 0.146 (in Skyrme units)
- Binding energy per baryon for B=85 Skyrmion: 0.146 (in Skyrme units), noted as particularly low for its size
- Cluster number formula for pure octahedron [0,Q,0]: c(0,Q,0) = (1/3)(Q+1)(2Q^2+4Q+3), giving values 6, 19, 44, 85 for Q=1,2,3,4
- Dimension formula for SU(4) irrep: d(P,Q,R) = (1/12)(P+1)(Q+1)(R+1)(P+Q+2)(Q+R+2)(P+Q+R+3)
- Number of nearest neighbor bonds for [1,1,1] cluster: N_short = 144, N_long = 51, giving E_bond/B = 2.72

## Context

This work extends the approach of Halcrow, Manton, and Rawlinson (2017) and earlier work by the Leeds group (Har, GHS, GHKMS) on the lightly-bound Skyrme model, building on Wigner's insight into SU(4) symmetry in nuclear physics and its development by Cook, Dallacasa, and others who viewed SU(4) weight diagrams as illustrating spatial structure of selected nuclei with baryon numbers 4, 16, 40, 80, 140. The paper enables systematic construction of Skyrmions for many new baryon numbers and their quantization, with particular success matching data for B=31 and B=38 Skyrmions that had not been considered before.
