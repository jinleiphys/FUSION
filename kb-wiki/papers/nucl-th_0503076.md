# The K^- αscattering length and the reaction dd \to αK^+ K^-

> We present predictions for the the K^- αscattering length obtained within the framework of the multiple scattering approach. Evaluating the pole position of the K^- αscattering amplitude within the zero range approximation, we find a loosely bound K^- αstate with a binding energy of E_R = -2 ... -7 MeV and a width Γ_R = 11 ... 18 MeV. We propose to measure the K^- αscattering length through the final state interaction between the αand K^- meson produced in the reaction dd \to αK^+ K^-. It is found that the K^-αinvariant mass distribution from this reaction at energies near the threshold provides a new tool to determine the s-wave K^- αscattering length.

# The K⁻α Scattering Length and the Reaction dd → αK⁺K⁻

## Key claim

The authors predict a loosely bound K⁻α state with binding energy between 2 and 7 MeV and width between 11 and 18 MeV, but note that this result depends strongly on the choice of elementary K⁻N scattering lengths and contradicts the deeply bound state prediction of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2003), which gave a binding energy of 86 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculate the s-wave K⁻α scattering length using the Foldy-Brueckner adiabatic approach within the multiple scattering formalism, averaging over nucleon coordinates in ⁴He using a Gaussian single-nucleon density. They then determine the pole position of the K⁻α scattering amplitude in the zero range approximation to find possible bound states, and compute the final-state interaction enhancement factor for the reaction dd → αK⁺K⁻ to assess whether the K⁻α invariant mass distribution can be used to extract the scattering length experimentally.

## Key numbers

- K⁻α scattering length for Set 1 (Barret, 1998): A = -1.80 + i0.90 fm
- K⁻α scattering length for Set 4 (Conboy, 1987): A = -2.24 + i1.58 fm
- K⁻α scattering length for Set 5 (Oller, 2001): A = -1.98 + i1.08 fm
- Pole position for Set 1: E* = (-6.7 - i18/2) MeV (binding energy 6.7 MeV, width 18 MeV)
- Pole position for Set 4: E* = (-2.0 - i11.3/2) MeV (binding energy 2.0 MeV, width 11.3 MeV)
- Pole position for Set 5: E* = (-4.8 - i14.9/2) MeV (binding energy 4.8 MeV, width 14.9 MeV)
- Ratio of cross sections with and without FSI at the lowest K⁻α invariant masses: R = 1.26-1.34 at excess energy 30 MeV, 1.49-1.56 at 50 MeV, and 1.84-2.18 at 100 MeV
- Total cross section of dd → αK⁺K⁻ estimated at 0.4-1 nb for excess energy 40-50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the multiple scattering formalism previously applied to reactions such as pd → ³Heη (Faldt, 1997) and pp → dK̄⁰K⁺ (Grishina, 2004) to the K⁻α system. The authors note that the K⁻α scattering length has not been measured and no theoretical calculations existed prior to this work. The results contradict the deeply bound K⁻α state predicted by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2003) using a strongly attractive phenomenological K̄N potential, and the authors propose that the reaction dd → αK⁺K⁻ at COSY-Jülich can provide a new experimental tool to determine the s-wave K⁻α scattering length, which cannot be reliably extracted from kaonic helium atom measurements due to complete absorption from p states.
