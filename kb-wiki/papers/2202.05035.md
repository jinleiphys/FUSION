# Isospin symmetry breaking in the charge radius difference of mirror nuclei

> Isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) effects in the charge radius difference $ΔR_{\rm ch}$ of mirror nuclei are studied using the test example of ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{48}$Ni. This choice allows for a transparent study of ISB contributions since paring and deformation effects, commonly required for the study of mirror nuclei, can be neglected in this specific pair. The connection of $ΔR_{\rm ch}$ with the nuclear Equation of State and the effect of ISB on such a relation are discussed according to an Energy Density Functional approach. We find that nuclear ISB effects may shift the estimated value for the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$ by more than 10 MeV while Coulomb corrections can be neglected. ISB effects on the ground-state energy and charge radii in mirror nuclei have been recently predicted by {\it ab initio} calculations to be relatively small, pointing to a negligible effect for the extraction of information on the nuclear EoS. These contrasting results call for a dedicated theoretical effort to solve this overarching problem that impacts not only the neutron skin thickness or the difference in mass and charge radii of mirror nuclei but also other observables such as the Isobaric Analog State energy.

## Key claim

Nuclear isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) effects in the charge radius difference of mirror nuclei, specifically the pair ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ni, may shift the estimated value of the symmetry energy slope parameter L by more than 10 MeV when analyzed using current Energy Density Functionals, even though recent ab initio calculations suggest such effects are small.

## Method

The authors used the SAMi-J family of Skyrme-type Energy Density Functionals (EDFs) to calculate the charge radius difference ΔRch between the mirror nuclei ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ni, a pair chosen because both are predicted to be doubly-magic, avoiding complications from pairing and deformation. They implemented Coulomb corrections at different levels of approximation (LDA, GGA, finite size effects, vacuum polarization) and added nuclear ISB terms in the form of contact charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) and charge-independent-breaking (CIB) interactions, with parameters taken from a semi-phenomenological fit to the Isobaric Analog State in ²⁰⁸Pb (Roca-Maza 2018). The effects of CSB and CIB on ΔRch and on the relation between ΔRch and the L parameter were studied separately and together.

## Key numbers

- The SAMi-ISB EDF predicts ΔRch = 0.33 fm for the full calculation (including Coulomb and nuclear ISB) and 0.28 fm when Coulomb is neglected, for the ⁴⁸Ca-⁴⁸Ni pair.
- Ab initio Coupled Cluster calculations (Novario 2021) predict ΔRch = 0.238 ± 0.038 fm for the full calculation and 0.261 ± 0.035 fm when Coulomb is neglected, showing an opposite trend.
- The binding energy difference ΔB for ⁴⁸Ca-⁴⁸Ni from the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME2020) is 68.67 MeV; SAMi-ISB predicts 68.72 MeV, with an ISB contribution of 7.88 MeV.
- The effective CSB strength s0 inferred from the ab initio ΔB is about -2 MeV fm³, one order of magnitude smaller than the value of -26.3 ± 0.7 MeV fm³ from Roca-Maza (2018).
- The shift in the estimated L parameter due to nuclear ISB, based on the SAMi-J family, is slightly larger than 11 MeV (about 9 MeV from CSB alone), and is rather constant across the range of L values from 30 to 115 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Brown (2017) that connects the charge radius difference of mirror nuclei to the symmetry energy slope parameter L, and it directly addresses the role of ISB effects that were neglected in that original proposal. The results contrast with recent ab initio calculations by Novario (2021) and Variational Monte Carlo calculations by Wiringa (based on AV18), which find much smaller nuclear ISB effects, and with the study by Reinhard (2022) that emphasized pairing and continuum effects as the dominant uncertainty. The paper calls for a dedicated theoretical effort to resolve this discrepancy, as it impacts not only the extraction of L from mirror charge radii but also the neutron skin thickness, Isobaric Analog State energies, and neutron star properties.
