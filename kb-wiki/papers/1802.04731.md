# $η$ and $ω$ mesons as new degrees of freedom in the intranuclear cascade model INCL

> The intranuclear cascade model INCL (Liège Intranuclear Cascade) is now able to simulate spallation reactions induced by projectiles with energies up to roughly 15 GeV. This was made possible thanks to the implementation of multipion emission in the NN, $Δ$N and $π$N interactions. The results obtained with reactions on nuclei induced by nucleons or pions gave confidence in the model. A next step will be the addition of the strange particles, $Λ$, $Σ$ and Kaons, in order to not only refine the high-energy modeling, but also to extend the capabilities of INCL, as studying hypernucleus physics. Between those two versions of the code, the possibility to treat the $η$ and $ω$ mesons in INCL has been performed and this is the topic of this paper. Production yields of these mesons increase with energy and it is interesting to test their roles at higher energies. More specifically, studies of $η$ rare decays benefit from accurate simulations of its production. These are the two reasons for their implementation. Ingredients of the model, like elementary reaction cross sections, are discussed and comparisons with experimental data are carried out to test the reliability of those particle productions.

## Key claim

The paper reports the implementation of $\eta$ and $\omega$ mesons as new degrees of freedom in the INCL intranuclear cascade model, but the authors explicitly note that the parametrizations for several key cross sections (especially for the np channel and for inclusive $\omega$ production) rely on hypotheses, extrapolations, or sparse experimental data, and that the lack of experimental data on $\omega$ production from nuclei prevents any direct test of the model for that meson.

## Method

The authors added $\eta$ and $\omega$ mesons to the INCL code by implementing elementary production, elastic scattering, and absorption cross sections for NN and $\pi$N interactions, using fits to experimental data where available and models or isospin symmetry where data are missing. They also incorporated the mesons' decays (with branching ratios above 1%) and, for the $\omega$, its in-medium decay during the cascade, while the $\eta$ decay is considered only after emission from the nucleus. The multipion emission mechanism already present in INCL was extended to handle inclusive $\eta$ and $\omega$ production, with the associated pions generated via the existing resonance and phase-space treatments.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta$ lifetime is approximately 150000 fm/c, much larger than the intranuclear cascade duration of around 70 fm/c.
- The $\omega$ lifetime is approximately 23 fm/c, requiring its decay to be considered during the cascade.
- For the $\pi^-p \rightarrow \eta n$ reaction, the high-energy parametrization is $\sigma = 1.47 \, P_{\text{Lab}}^{-1.68}$ (with $P_{\text{Lab}}$ in MeV/c and $\sigma$ in mb) for $E_{\text{cm}} \geq 1714$ MeV.
- For the $\pi^-p \rightarrow \omega n$ reaction, the parametrization is $\sigma = 13.76 \, (P_{\text{Lab}}-1.0903) / (P_{\text{Lab}}^{3.33} - 1.07)$ (with $P_{\text{Lab}}$ in GeV/c and $\sigma$ in mb) for $P_{\text{Lab}} \geq 1.0903$ GeV/c.
- For the pp channel, the exclusive $\eta$ production cross section is parametrized as a polynomial in $E_{\text{cm}}$ (in GeV) up to $E_{\text{cm}} = 3.05$ GeV, and above that as $2.5 \times 10^3 \, ((E_{\text{cm}}^2/5.88)-1)^{1.47} (E_{\text{cm}}^2/5.88)^{-1.25}$ (in $\mu$b).
- For the np channel, the exclusive $\eta$ production cross section is set equal to the pp exclusive cross section for $E_{\text{cm}} \geq 3.9$ GeV.
- The $\eta$N elastic scattering cross section parametrization is set to zero for $P_{\text{Lab}} \geq 2025$ MeV/c.
- The $\omega$N elastic scattering cross section is parametrized as $\sigma = 5.4 + 10^{-0.6 P_{\text{Lab}}}$ (with $P_{\text{Lab}}$ in GeV/c and $\sigma$ in mb).
- The $\omega$N inelastic cross section is parametrized as $\sigma^{\text{inelastic}}_{\omega N} = 20. + 4./P_{\text{Lab}}$ (with $P_{\text{Lab}}$ in GeV/c and $\sigma$ in mb).

## Context

This work extends the INCL intranuclear cascade model, which previously implemented multipion emission (Pedoux and Cugnon, 2011) to reach projectile energies up to roughly 15 GeV, by adding $\eta$ and $\omega$ mesons as intermediate steps toward the inclusion of strange particles (hyperons and kaons). The implementation enables more accurate simulations of $\eta$ production for studies of its rare decays (Kupsc, 2011; Gatti, 2016) and provides a framework for future hypernucleus physics. The paper relies on cross-section parametrizations from Sibirtsev (1997) for inclusive $\eta$ production, from Cassing (1996) and Sibirtsev (1997) for $\omega$ production, and on the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels model (Kamano, 2013) for $\eta$N elastic and absorption cross sections.
