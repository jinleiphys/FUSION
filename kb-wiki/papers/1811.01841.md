# Approximate sum rule for the electric dipole moment of light nuclei

> The measurement of the electric dipole moment (EDM) is an excellent test of the standard model of particle physics, and the detection of a finite value is signal of a new source of CP violation beyond it. Among systems for which the EDM can be measured, light nuclei are particularly interesting due to their high sensitivity to new physics. In this proceedings contribution, we examine the sensitivity of the EDM of several light nuclei to the CP-odd one pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction within the cluster model. We suggest an approximate sum rule for the nuclear EDM.

## Key claim

The paper suggests an approximate sum rule for the electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei based on cluster-model calculations, but the rule is conditional on the nuclear structures of opposite parity states not differing significantly; the EDM of 13C is noted as an exception that does not respect the counting rule due to poor overlap between opposite parity states.

## Method

The author models the CP-odd nuclear force via one-pion exchange between nucleons, with coupling constants Gπ(0), Gπ(1), and Gπ(2). The nuclear wavefunctions are constructed using α (4He), triton (3H), and nucleon clusters interacting through phenomenological potentials, with antisymmetrization included via the orthogonality condition model. The CP-odd α-N and α-3H potentials are obtained by folding the CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction with oscillator constants b = 1.358 fm (α-N) and b = 1.482 fm (α-3H). The nuclear EDM is then calculated as the sum of linear coefficients aπ(0), aπ(1), aπ(2) multiplying the respective CP-odd coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- 2H EDM: aπ(1) = 0.0145 e fm (only isovector component contributes)
- 3He EDM: aπ(0) = 0.0059 e fm, aπ(1) = 0.0108 e fm, aπ(2) = 0.0168 e fm
- 3H EDM: aπ(0) = -0.0059 e fm, aπ(1) = 0.0108 e fm, aπ(2) = -0.0170 e fm
- 6Li EDM: aπ(1) = 0.022 e fm (only isovector component contributes)
- 7Li EDM: aπ(0) = -0.006 e fm, aπ(1) = 0.016 e fm, aπ(2) = -0.017 e fm
- 9Be EDM: aπ(1) = 0.014 e fm (only isovector component contributes)
- 11B EDM: aπ(0) = -0.004 e fm, aπ(1) = 0.02 e fm, aπ(2) = -0.01 e fm
- 13C EDM: aπ(1) = -0.0020 e fm (only isovector component contributes)
- 129Xe EDM: aπ(0) = 7 × 10-5 e fm, aπ(1) = 7 × 10-5 e fm, aπ(2) = 4 × 10-4 e fm
- The α-N polarization contribution is approximately (0.005 - 0.007) Gπ(1) e fm
- Predicted 10B EDM: ~ 0.03 Gπ(1) e fm
- Predicted 14N EDM: ~ 0.04 Gπ(1) e fm

## Context

This work extends cluster-model calculations of nuclear EDMs previously performed for 2H, 3He, 3H, 6Li, 7Li, 9Be, 11B, and 13C (Yamanaka, 2018; Bsaisou, 2015; Liu, 2007) to derive a systematic counting rule. The rule enables predictions for unexplored nuclei such as 10B and 14N, which the author suggests may be more sensitive to CP violation than previously studied systems. The work also notes that for heavy nuclei like 129Xe, the EDM is suppressed by destructive interference from configuration mixing (Yoshinaga, 2013), supporting the conclusion that light nuclei are the most promising targets for storage-ring EDM experiments.
