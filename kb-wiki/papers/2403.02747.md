# A numerical algorithm for solving the coupled Schrödinger equations using inverse power method

> The inverse power method is a numerical algorithm to obtain the eigenvectors of a matrix. In this work, we develop an iteration algorithm, based on the inverse power method, to numerically solve the Schrödinger equation that couples an arbitrary number of components. Such an algorithm can also be applied to the multi-body systems. To show the power and accuracy of this method, we also present an example of solving the Dirac equation under the presence of an external scalar potential and a constant magnetic field, with source code publicly available.

## Key claim

The authors generalize Crater's (1994) inverse power method algorithm, previously limited to non-coupled systems or systems with two or four coupling components, to solve Schrödinger equations coupling an arbitrary number of components, with the condition that the algorithm converges to the eigenstate whose eigenvalue is closest to a chosen trial energy λ.

## Method

The algorithm discretizes the radial coordinate into N grid points with spacing h, converting the continuous coupled Schrödinger equation into a matrix eigenvalue problem. For each iteration, instead of explicitly computing the inverse of the large (N×M)-dimensional matrix (Ĥ − λI), the authors solve the linear system (Ĥ − λI)Φ = Ψ by constructing auxiliary M×M matrices D̄[k] and vectors Ψ̄[k] via forward substitution, then obtaining the solution Φ via backward substitution. The wavefunction is renormalized after each iteration, and the process repeats until the wavefunction and energy converge.

## Key numbers

- The example Dirac equation uses a harmonic oscillator potential V = mω²r²/2 with harmonic frequency ω = m/5.
- The radial coordinate is discretized into N = 2500 grid sites with spacing h = 10⁻³/ω.
- Angular momentum states are truncated to keep terms with k < 10 (where k indexes the angular momentum quantum number).
- The energy error after n iterations scales as ΔEℓ ∼ (Eℓ − λ) Σ_{i≠ℓ} (R_i)^{2n+1}, where R_i = |Eℓ − λ|/|Ei − λ|.

## Context

This work extends the inverse power method algorithm originally developed by Crater (1994) for non-coupled and few-component (M=2,4) Schrödinger equations to systems with an arbitrary number of coupled components. The method is applicable to multi-body systems (e.g., few-nucleon bound states, multi-quark bound states) after transformation to hyperspherical coordinates and basis expansion, following approaches used by Zhao (2020, 2023) and others. The algorithm enables efficient direct computation of both ground and excited states without requiring prior knowledge of lower-energy states, and remains valid for complex potentials describing decay effects.
