# Bayesian Analysis of the Neutron Star Equation of State and Model Comparison: Insights from PSR J0437+4715, PSR J0614+3329, and Other Multi-Physics Data

> We perform a comprehensive Bayesian analysis to constrain the neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) using a wide range of terrestrial and astrophysical data. The terrestrial inputs include quantities related to symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and symmetry energy up to two times saturation density ($ρ_0\sim$0.16 fm$^{-3}$), derived from finite nuclei and heavy-ion collisions (HICs). The astrophysical constraints incorporate NS radii and tidal deformabilities from recent NICER observations and GW170817, respectively. We consider five different EoS models: Taylor, $n/3$, Skyrme, RMF, and sound speed(CS), are analyzed by sequentially updating the priors with (i) $χ$EFT based pure neutron matter, (ii) terrestrial, empirical and earlier astrophysical data, (iii) case (ii) including NICER radii of PSR J0437+4715 and J0614+3329, (iv) all data combined, and (v) excluding empirical nuclear inputs. We also perform Bayesian model comparison which favors the Skyrme model under all combined data (scenario (iv)), yielding tight constraints on symmetry energy parameters: $L_0 = 56 \pm 3$~MeV, $K_{\mathrm{sym}0} = -132 \pm 15$~MeV and also on SNM parameters: $K_0 = 265 \pm 12$~MeV, $Q_0 = -366 \pm 43$~MeV. The mass-radius and mass-tidal deformability posterior distributions are also well constrained. The radius and tidal deformability of a $1.4\,M_\odot$ neutron star are found to be $R_{1.4} = 11.85 \pm 0.11$~km and $Λ_{1.4} = 354 \pm 25$, respectively.

## Key claim

The Bayesian model comparison favors the Skyrme model over Taylor, n/3, RMF, and CS models when all data (chiral EFT, terrestrial, empirical, GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0437+4715 and PSR J0614+3329) are combined, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific prior choices and the particular set of constraints included in scenario (iv); the favored model changes when different data subsets are used.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using five different equation of state (EoS) models: Taylor expansion, n/3 expansion, Skyrme, relativistic mean field (RMF), and speed-of-sound (CS). They sequentially updated priors with five data scenarios: (i) chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) pure neutron matter constraints, (ii) terrestrial data from heavy-ion collisions and empirical nuclear inputs plus earlier astrophysical data (GW170817 and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620), (iii) scenario (ii) plus new NICER radii of PSR J0437+4715 and PSR J0614+3329, (iv) all data combined, and (v) scenario (iv) excluding empirical nuclear inputs. Bayesian model comparison using the Bayes factor was performed to identify the most favored EoS model.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy slope parameter: L0 = 56 ± 3 MeV (from the Skyrme model under all combined data, scenario (iv))
* Symmetry energy curvature parameter: K_sym0 = -132 ± 15 MeV (from the Skyrme model under scenario (iv))
* Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility: K0 = 265 ± 12 MeV (from the Skyrme model under scenario (iv))
* Symmetric nuclear matter skewness: Q0 = -366 ± 43 MeV (from the Skyrme model under scenario (iv))
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_1.4 = 11.85 ± 0.11 km (from the Skyrme model under scenario (iv))
* Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: Lambda_1.4 = 354 ± 25 (from the Skyrme model under scenario (iv))

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the neutron star EoS (e.g., Tews 2012, Hebeler 2013, Malik 2018, Huth 2021) by incorporating the new NICER radius measurements of PSR J0437+4715 (Choudhury 2024) and PSR J0614+3329 (Mauviard 2025) alongside chiEFT constraints, heavy-ion collision data, and GW170817 tidal deformability measurements. The systematic model comparison across five different EoS parameterizations enables identification of the Skyrme model as the most favored by current multi-messenger data, and the tight constraints on higher-order nuclear matter parameters (K_sym0, Q0) provide improved input for future studies of neutron star structure and composition at suprasaturation densities.
