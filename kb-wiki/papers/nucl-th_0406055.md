# The N(1710)P11 state is confirmed in the re-analysis of the pi N --> K Lambda production; it is a good candidate for a non-strange pentaquark

> Re-analyzing the old pi N --> K Lambda data, the additional proof is given for the existence of the N(1710) P11 state, critically needed in light of reported observations of exotic Theta(1539) and Xi(1862) pentaquarks. An existing single-resonance model with S11, P11 and P13 Breit-Wiegner resonances in the s-channel has been applied. It has been shown that the standard set of resonant parameters fails to reproduce the shape of the differential cross section. The new resonance parameter determination has been performed keeping in mind the most recent knowledge about nucleon resonances. The extracted set of parameters has confirmed the need for the strong contribution of a N(1710) P11 resonance. The need for any significant contribution of the P13 resonance has been eliminated. To reproduce the total cross section at the same time with the linear dependence of the differential cross sections with the cos(theta) in the energy range 1650 MeV < W < 1800 MeV the P11 resonance can not but be quite narrow.

## Key claim

Re-analyzing old πN → KΛ data with a single-resonance model confirms the existence of the N(1710) P11 resonance, which is a candidate for a non-strange pentaquark; however, this conclusion is conditional on the resonance having a small branching fraction to the KΣ channel, otherwise the observed narrow width could be a cusp effect.

## Method

The authors applied an existing single-resonance model with Breit-Wigner parameterization for S11, P11, and P13 resonances in the s-channel, neglecting t-channel K*(892) contributions. They fitted the model to an amalgamated data set of total and differential cross sections from four experiments (Dyc69, Jon71, Kna75, Bak78), normalizing all data to a common peak of 920 μb at 1694 MeV and shifting the Knasel et al. data by 9 MeV in energy. The fit varied KΛ branching ratios while keeping masses and widths of the N(1650) S11, N(1710) P11, and N(1720) P13 resonances fixed to values from Bat98, then released the width of the P11 resonance to obtain optimal agreement.

## Key numbers

- The amalgamated data show a peak of 920 μb at an energy of 1694 MeV.
- The best-fit solution (Sol 3) gives the N(1710) P11 resonance a mass of 1700 MeV and a width of approximately 68 MeV.
- The PDG standard width for the N(1710) P11 resonance is 90 < Γ < 480 MeV, while pentaquark models predict a width Γ < 40 MeV.
- The fit with two P11 resonances yields one with Γ ≈ 40 MeV and another with Γ ≈ 240 MeV, both degenerate in mass at approximately 1700 MeV.
- The analysis covers the energy range 1650 MeV < W < 1800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the single-channel phase-shift analysis of Baker (1977, 1978) and addresses the discrepancy between coupled-channel Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix methods (Arndt 1985, 1995, 2004), which do not see the N(1710) P11 state, and Carnegie-Mellon Berkeley type models (Cutkosky 1979, Bat98, Vra00), which do. The results support the identification of the N(1710) P11 as the non-strange member of the pentaquark anti-decuplet predicted by chiral soliton models (Diakonov 1997, 2003) and qqqq\bar{q} color-spin models (Jaffe 2003). The paper calls for new measurements of πN → KΛ and πN → KΣ cross sections in the range 1613 MeV < W < 1900 MeV to resolve ambiguities between resonant and cusp interpretations.
