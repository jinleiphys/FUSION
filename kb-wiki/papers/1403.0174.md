# Weak solution of the non-perturbative renormalization group equation to describe the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

> We analyze the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (D$χ$SB) in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by using the non-perturbative renormalization group (NPRG) equation. The equation takes a form of two-dimensional partial differential equation for the multi-fermion effective interactions $V(x,t)$ where $x$ is $\barψψ$ operator and $t$ is the logarithm of the renormalization scale. The D$χ$SB occurs due to the quantum corrections, which means it emerges at some finite $t_{\rm c}$ in the mid of integrating the equation with respect to $t$. At $t_{\rm c}$ some singularities suddenly appear in $V$ which is compulsory in the spontaneous symmetry breakdown. Therefore there is no solution of the equation beyond $t_{\rm c}$. We newly introduce the notion of weak solution to get the global solution including the infrared limit $t\rightarrow \infty$ and investigate its properties. The obtained weak solution is global and unique, and it perfectly describes the physically correct vacuum even in case of the first order phase transition appearing in finite density medium. The key logic of deduction is that the weak solution we defined automatically convexifies the effective potential when treating the singularities.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the notion of a "weak solution" to the non-perturbative renormalization group (NPRG) equation, which allows the construction of a global solution beyond the blowup singularity that inevitably appears at the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DχSB) scale \(t_{\rm c}\); the weak solution automatically convexifies the effective potential and correctly describes the physical vacuum even for first-order phase transitions.

## Method

The authors start from the Wegner-Houghton (WH) equation for the fermion potential \(V(x;t)\) in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under the local potential approximation and large-\(N\) leading approximation, which reduces to a partial differential equation of conservation-law type for the mass function \(M(x;t)=\partial_x V\). They define the weak solution via integration against a smooth test function, which requires no differentiability of \(M\). The weak solution is constructed by the method of characteristics: the characteristics are solved as ordinary differential equations, and where the resulting string of orbits becomes multi-folded (multi-valued), the Rankine-Hugoniot condition (continuity of \(V\)) is used to patch the leaves into a single-valued function with a jump discontinuity at the origin.

## Key numbers

* The critical dimensionless four-fermi coupling constant in the NJL model is \(\tilde G_{\rm c}=4\pi^2\); this is the ultraviolet unstable fixed point of the \(\beta\)-function.
* The blowup time for the four-fermi coupling is \(t_{\rm c}= \frac{1}{2}\log\frac{\tilde G_0}{\tilde G_0-\tilde G_{\rm c}}\), where \(\tilde G_0\) is the initial dimensionless coupling.
* For the gauge theory case (QCD-like), the critical gauge coupling above which the \(\beta\)-function has no zero is \(\alpha_{\rm s}^{\rm c}=\pi/3\) (with \(\xi=0\), \(C_2=1\)).
* The numerical example shown for the NJL model uses a super-critical initial coupling \(G_0=1.005\,G_{\rm c}\) to generate the folded surface and the weak solution.

## Context

This work extends the NPRG analysis of DχSB in the NJL model and QCD, where previous approaches (e.g., Aoki (1998, 1999), Braun (2011)) encountered a blowup singularity in the four-fermi coupling at the symmetry-breaking scale. The weak-solution method avoids the need for bosonization (the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation used by Aoki (1996), Gies (2001)) and provides a global solution down to the infrared limit. It enables direct calculation of infrared quantities such as the chiral condensate and dynamical mass without introducing auxiliary bosonic fields, and it automatically handles first-order phase transitions at finite chemical potential by convexifying the effective potential.
