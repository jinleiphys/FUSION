# Elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies with the resonant $2_2^+$ and $2_3^+$ states of $^{16}$O

> The elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=2$ at low energies is studied in an effective Lagrangian approach. We explicitly include two resonant $2_2^+$ and $2_3^+$ states of $^{16}$O in the scattering amplitudes and construct an $S$ matrix assuming that three amplitudes, the non-resonant part of the amplitude which has the sub-threshold $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O and those of the two resonant states, are represented by the summation of corresponding parts of the phase shift. Then, we fit the parameters in the $S$ matrix to the phase shift data by imposing three conditions at very low energies where the phase shift data are not available. By using the fitted parameters, we calculate the asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) of the $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O and find that the previously reported small and large values of the ANC can be reproduced depending on the imposed conditions, but we obtain large error bars for the large ANC values which are reported from the $α$ transfer reactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both the small (≈2×10⁴ fm⁻¹/²) and large (≈1.1–1.8×10⁵ fm⁻¹/²) asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) of the 2₁⁺ state of ¹⁶O can be reproduced from elastic α-¹²C scattering phase shift data, depending on which of three low-energy conditions is imposed on the non-resonant amplitude, but the large ANC values come with very large error bars that make the two values statistically consistent.

## Method

The author constructs an S-matrix for elastic α-¹²C scattering in the d-wave (l=2) channel using an effective Lagrangian approach, explicitly including the sub-threshold 2₁⁺ state and the resonant 2₂⁺ and 2₃⁺ states of ¹⁶O. The phase shift is decomposed into non-resonant and two resonant parts, each expressed via effective range expansions with parameters fitted to the experimental phase shift data of Tischhauser et al. (2009) over the range E_α = 2.6–6.62 MeV using a Markov chain Monte Carlo method, while three different conditions (I, II, III) are imposed on the inverse propagator D₂(p) at very low energies (0 ≤ E_α ≤ 2.6 MeV) where no data exist.

## Key numbers

* ANC of the 2₁⁺ state of ¹⁶O under condition (I): |C_b| = 2.0(2)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (without 2₄⁺ state) and 3.1(6)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (with 2₄⁺ state)
* ANC under condition (II): |C_b| = 3.2(6)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (without 2₄⁺) and 3.6(9)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (with 2₄⁺)
* ANC under condition (III): |C_b| = 11(26)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (without 2₄⁺) and 13(30)×10⁴ fm⁻¹/² (with 2₄⁺)
* Binding energies of sub-threshold states: E(1₁⁻) = −0.045 MeV and E(2₁⁺) = −0.245 MeV from the α-¹²C breakup threshold
* Resonant energies and widths fitted: E_r(2₂⁺) ≈ 2.68309 MeV, Γ_r(2₂⁺) ≈ 0.74–0.75 keV; E_r(2₃⁺) ≈ 4.3544–4.3549 MeV, Γ_r(2₃⁺) ≈ 74.55–74.66 keV
* χ²/N values for all fits range from 0.66 to 1.13 with N = 354 data points

## Context

This work extends the author's previous effective field theory studies of elastic α-¹²C scattering (Ando 2018, 2016) by explicitly including the resonant 2₂⁺ and 2₃⁺ states of ¹⁶O, enabling use of the full phase shift data set from Tischhauser et al. (2009). The paper addresses a long-standing discrepancy between small ANC values (≈2×10⁴ fm⁻¹/²) obtained from effective range expansion methods and large ANC values (1.14–1.82×10⁵ fm⁻¹/²) from α-transfer reactions (Belhout et al. 2007, Avila et al. 2015) and theoretical calculations by Sparenberg (2004) and Dufour and Descouvemont (2008). The results indicate that additional experimental input beyond elastic scattering phase shifts is needed to determine the ANC of the 2₁⁺ state of ¹⁶O, which is crucial for the E2 transition rate of the astrophysical ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction at the Gamow peak energy of 0.3 MeV.
