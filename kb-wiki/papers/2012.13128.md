# Relativistic Coulomb S-factor of Two Spinor Particles with Arbitrary Masses

> A new resummation of the $S$-factor of a composite system of two relativistic spin-1/2 particles of arbitrary masses interacting via a Coulomb-like chromodynamical potential is presented. The analysis is performed in the framework of a relativistic quasipotential approach based on the Hamiltonian formulation of the covariant quantum field theory in the relativistic configuration representation. The pseudoscalar, vector, and pseudovector systems are considered and the behaviour of the $S$-factor near the threshold and in the relativistic limit is investigated in detail. The spin dependence of the $S$-factors is discussed as well. It is argued that at the threshold the contribution of spins significantly reduces the Sommerfeld effect, while at ultrarelativistic velocities their role diminishes and the $S$-factor becomes basically the same as for the spinless systems. A connection between the new and previously obtained S-factors for spinless particles of arbitrary masses and for relativistic spinor particles of equal masses is established.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new resummation of the Coulomb S-factor for two relativistic spin-1/2 particles of arbitrary masses, showing that at threshold the spin contribution significantly reduces the Sommerfeld effect, while at ultrarelativistic velocities the S-factor becomes essentially the same as for spinless systems. The authors note that the relativistic generalization of the S-factor is not unique, and their result differs from previous formulations by Fadin (1988, 1995), Hoang (1997), and Yoon and Wong (2000) in the relativistic limit.

## Method

The analysis is performed within the relativistic quasipotential (RQP) approach based on the Hamiltonian formulation of covariant quantum field theory in the relativistic configuration representation, using Lobachevsky space. The authors solve the RQP equation for the radial wave function of two spin-1/2 particles interacting via a Coulomb-like chromodynamical potential V(ρ) = -α̃_s/ρ, where α̃_s = 4α_s/3, and obtain an analytical expression for the S-factor by approximating an oscillating exponential factor to unity, which preserves all symmetry characteristics. The pseudoscalar, vector, and pseudovector systems are considered, with the S-factor defined in terms of the wave function evaluated at r = iλ, where λ is the Compton wavelength.

## Key numbers

- For equal masses, the relativistic S-factor for spinless particles is S(χ) = X(χ)/[1 - exp(-X(χ))] with X(χ) = π α̃ / sinh χ, where χ is the rapidity related to total c.m. energy as √s = 2m cosh χ.
- The velocity u for unequal masses is defined as u = √[1 - 4m'²/(s - (m₁ - m₂)²)], where m' = √(m₁m₂) is the effective particle mass.
- The parameters a' and b' for the unequal-mass spinor case take values: for pseudoscalar (Ô = γ₅), a' = g'², b' = 1 - g'²; for vector (Ô = γ_μ), a' = g'²/2, b' = 3/4 - g'²/2; for pseudovector (Ô = γ₅γ_μ), a' = -g'²/2, b' = 1/4 + g'²/2, where g' = (m₁ + m₂)/(2√(m₁m₂)).
- The S-factor for two spinors of arbitrary masses is S_uneq,s(χ') = X_uneq,s(χ')/[1 - exp(-X_uneq,s(χ'))] × exp(-πρ̃')|Γ(2 + iρ̃') ₂F₁(1 + iB', -iρ̃'; 2; 1 - e^{-2χ'})|², with X_uneq,s(χ') = π α̃_s (a' cosh² χ' + b')/(2 sinh χ').
- The relativistic limit (χ' ≫ 1) of the S-factor for spinors of equal masses is S_eq,s(χ)|_{χ≫1} ≈ 2π(B - ρ̃)/[1 - exp(-2π(B - ρ̃))], valid for all spin parameters a and b.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasipotential approach previously applied to spinless particles by Milton and Solovtsov (2001) and to spinor particles of equal masses by Skachkov (1980) and Chernichenko, Kaptari, and Solovtsova (2019, 2020). The results connect to earlier nonrelativistic treatments of the Sommerfeld effect by Sommerfeld (1931), Gamov (1928), and Sakharov (1948), as well as to relativistic generalizations for spinless particles of arbitrary masses by Arbuzov (1994, 2011, 2019). The derived S-factor enables improved calculations of threshold phenomena in QCD processes such as heavy quark-antiquark annihilation, dark matter pair annihilation in the early Universe, and di-lepton production in heavy-ion collisions at NICA and LHC.
