# Calculations of three-nucleon reactions with N3LO chiral forces: achievements and challenges

> We discuss the application of the chiral N3LO forces to three-nucleon reactions and point to the challenges which will have to be addressed. Present approaches to solve three-nucleon Faddeev equations are based on a partial-wave decomposition. A rapid increase of the number of terms contributing to the chiral three-nucleon force when increasing the order of the chiral expansion from N2LO to N3LO forced us to develop a fast and effective method of automatized partial wave decomposition. At low energies of the incoming nucleon below about 20MeV, where only a limited number of partial waves is required, this method allowed us to perform calculations of reactions in the three-nucleon continuum using N3LO two- and three-nucleon forces. It turns out that inclusion of consistent chiral interactions, with relativistic 1/m corrections and short-range 2pi-contact term omitted in the N3LO three-nucleon force, does not explain the long standing low energy Ay-puzzle. We discuss problems arising when chiral forces are applied at higher energies, where large three-nucleon force effects are expected. It seems plausible that at higher energies, due to a rapid increase of a number of partial waves required to reach convergent results, a three-dimensional formulation of the Faddeev equations which avoids partial-wave decomposition is desirable.

## Key claim

Inclusion of consistent chiral N³LO three-nucleon forces (with relativistic 1/m corrections and the short-range 2π-contact term omitted) does not explain the long-standing low-energy Ay-puzzle in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, despite the expectation that consistent chiral interactions at this order would resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon Faddeev equations in a partial-wave decomposition for neutron-deuteron scattering at incoming neutron lab energies below approximately 20 MeV, using N³LO chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. The three-nucleon force at N³LO was implemented via an automatized partial-wave decomposition method, with the low-energy constants c_D and c_E fixed by fitting the triton binding energy and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length. Calculations were performed for five versions of the Bochum N³LO potentials with different cutoff parameters.

## Key numbers

- Incoming neutron lab energy below approximately 20 MeV for low-energy calculations
- Triton binding energy used as one of two observables to fix c_D and c_E parameters
- Neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length used as second observable to fix c_D and c_E
- For convergence at low energies, two-nucleon force components with total two-nucleon angular momenta j ≤ 3 in three-nucleon partial-wave states with total angular momentum J ≤ 25/2 were sufficient
- Three-nucleon force matrix elements with j ≤ 3 and J ≤ 5/2 were sufficient for convergence
- The Ay data are underestimated by approximately 30% in the region of the Ay maximum at center-of-mass angles around 120°
- At 14.1 MeV incoming neutron energy, negligible three-nucleon force effects were found for the elastic scattering cross section
- For quasi-free-scattering configurations, theory underestimates neutron-neutron QFS data by approximately 20%
- At higher energies, calculations were performed at 65 MeV, 135 MeV, and 200 MeV incoming nucleon lab energy
- At 200 MeV, the relative nucleon-deuteron momentum q₀ = 2.07 fm⁻¹
- At 200 MeV, the expansion parameter p/Λ ≈ 0.45
- Cutoff parameters for N³LO potentials ranged from 414 to 700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory forces to three-nucleon reactions, building on the development of N³LO two-nucleon potentials by Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum (2005) and the derivation of consistent N³LO three-nucleon forces by Bernard (2008) and Epelbaum (2008). The results confirm that the low-energy Ay-puzzle, previously documented with standard phenomenological potentials such as AV18, CD Bonn, and Nijmegen forces combined with TM99 or Urbana IX three-nucleon forces, persists even with consistent chiral interactions. The paper identifies the need for a three-dimensional formulation of Faddeev equations that avoids partial-wave decomposition to enable calculations at higher energies where large three-nucleon force effects are expected.
