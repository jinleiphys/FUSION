# A multistage framework for studying the evolution of jets and high-$p_T$ probes in small collision systems

> Understanding the modification of jets and high-$p_T$ probes in small systems requires the integration of soft and hard physics. We present recent developments in extending the JETSCAPE framework to build an event generator, which includes correlations between soft and hard partons, to study jet observables in small systems. The multi-scale physics of the collision is separated into different stages. Hard scatterings are first sampled at binary collision positions provided by the Glauber geometry. They are then propagated backward in space-time following an initial-state shower to obtain the initiating partons' energies and momenta before the collision. These energies and momenta are then subtracted from the incoming colliding nucleons for soft-particle production, modeled by the 3D-Glauber + hydrodynamics + hadronic transport framework. This new hybrid approach (X-SCAPE) includes non-trivial correlations between jet and soft particle productions in small systems. We calibrate this framework with the final state hadrons' $p_T$-spectra from low to high $p_T$ in $p$-$p$, and and then compare with the spectra in $p$-$Pb$ collisions from the LHC. We also present results for additional observables such as the distributions of event activity as a function of the hardest jet $p_T$ in forward and mid-rapidity for both $p$-$p$ and $p$-$Pb$ collisions.

# A multistage framework for studying the evolution of jets and high-$p_T$ probes in small collision systems

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE collaboration's new X-SCAPE framework can produce a consistent description of the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for both hadrons and jets in $p$-$Pb$ collisions from a single simulation, but only after calibrating the minimum hard transverse momentum exchange $\hat{p}_T^0$ to fit minimum bias charged pion spectra in $p$-$p$ collisions, and the results are preliminary rather than fully validated.

## Method

The authors extended the JETSCAPE framework into X-SCAPE, which separates the collision into stages: hard scatterings are sampled at binary collision positions from a 3D MC-Glauber geometry, then propagated backward in space-time using i-MATTER initial-state showers to obtain the initiating partons' energies and momenta before the collision. These energies and momenta are subtracted from the incoming colliding nucleons for soft-particle production, which is modeled by 3D-Glauber plus MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics plus hadronic transport via iSS Cooper-Frye freezeout and Lund hadronization for the hard sector. The framework was calibrated against charged pion $p_T$-spectra in $p$-$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV and then compared to $p$-$Pb$ collisions at the same energy.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion spectra and jet spectra were computed for $p$-$p$ and $p$-$Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, compared to ALICE experiment data.
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for charged hadrons in minimum bias $p$-$Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV was computed and shown as a function of $p_T$ from $0 \lesssim p_T \lesssim 20$ GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor for jets in $p$-$Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV was computed and shown as a function of jet $p_T$.
- Transverse energy $E_T$ production as a function of the leading jet $p_T$ was computed for $p$-$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV in two rapidity ranges: mid-rapidity ($|\eta| < 1$) and forward rapidity ($-4.9 < \eta < -3.2$).
- The same $E_T$ versus leading jet $p_T$ distributions were computed for $p$-$Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV in the same rapidity ranges.
- A residual azimuthal anisotropy at high $p_T$ above 20 GeV is cited as indicative of jet modification, based on prior ATLAS measurements (ATLAS 2014, ATLAS 2019).

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework (JETSCAPE Collaboration 2017, 2021, 2022, 2023) to address small collision systems where exact initial-state energy-momentum conservation between hard and soft sectors becomes important, a problem identified by Kordell (2016). The approach contrasts with standard heavy-ion simulations that treat bulk medium evolution and hard jets independently without balancing their energy-momentum contributions. The results enable future Bayesian calibration of parameters determining hard and soft sector partitioning, and allow studies of nuclear modification of parton distribution functions, parton energy loss within small QGP droplets, and initial-state fluctuations leading to azimuthal anisotropies in both hard and soft sectors.
