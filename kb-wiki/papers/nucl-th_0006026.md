# Electro-weak Reactions for Astrophysics

> The status of "ab initio" microscopic calculations of the 2H(p,gamma)3He and 3He(p,e+nu_e)4He reactions is reviewed. The methods used to generate accurate nuclear ground- and scattering-state wave functions, and to construct realistic electro-weak transition operators are described. The uncertainties in the theoretical predictions, particularly those relevant to the p-3He weak capture, are discussed. For the dp radiative capture, the theoretical results are compared with the TUNL data in the energy range 0--100 keV.

## Key claim

The paper reviews "ab initio" microscopic calculations of the 2H(p,gamma)3He and 3He(p,e+nu_e)4He reactions, finding that while the theoretical predictions for the dp radiative capture agree reasonably well with TUNL data, significant discrepancies remain in the S-factor below 40 keV and in the small-angle behavior of the differential cross section and tensor analyzing power; for the hep weak capture, the predicted S-factor is about 4.5 times larger than the standard solar model value, but this enhancement is still insufficient to explain the observed high-energy distortion in the Super-Kamiokande solar neutrino spectrum.

## Method

The correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) method was used to obtain wave functions for A=3 and A=4 bound and scattering states, with the nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon interaction and the Urbana-IX (UIX) three-nucleon interaction. The nuclear electromagnetic current included one-body terms and many-body terms constructed consistently with the interactions, with model-dependent currents from Delta-isobar excitation treated within the transition-correlation-operator (TCO) scheme; the weak current was constructed from vector and axial-vector parts, with the axial current's Delta-excitation contributions calibrated using tritium beta-decay data.

## Key numbers

- 3He binding energy: 7.74 MeV with AV18/UIX model (experimental: 7.72 MeV)
- 4He binding energy: 27.89 MeV with AV18/UIX model (experimental: 28.3 MeV)
- p-3He singlet S-wave scattering length: 11.5 fm with AV18/UIX (experimental: 10.8 +/- 2.6 fm)
- p-3He triplet S-wave scattering length: 9.13 fm with AV18/UIX (experimental: 8.1 +/- 0.5 fm)
- nd capture cross section at thermal neutron energy: 578 microbarn with AV18/UIX (experimental: 508 +/- 15 microbarn)
- hep S-factor at zero energy: 9.64 x 10^{-20} keV b with full current including all S- and P-wave channels (AV18/UIX)
- hep S-factor at zero energy with one-body current only: 29.0 x 10^{-20} keV b (AV18/UIX)
- hep S-factor at zero energy with AV14/UVIII model: 10.2 x 10^{-20} keV b
- Ratio of predicted hep S-factor to standard solar model value: 4.4 (using 10.1 x 10^{-20} keV b divided by 2.3 x 10^{-20} keV b)

## Context

This work extends the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics calculations of the Pisa group for A=3 (Kievsky, 1993) and A=4 (Viviani, 1995) nuclei, using the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian that reproduces trinucleon and alpha particle binding energies exactly but underbinds A=6-8 systems. The results contradict the earlier suggestion by Bahcall and Krastev (1998) of a larger hep S-factor enhancement, and show that the predicted 4.5-fold increase over the standard solar model value (Bahcall, 1998) cannot explain the Super-Kamiokande high-energy spectrum distortion. The calculations enable future work using variational and Green's function Monte Carlo methods for systems with A >= 6, and provide constraints on models of nuclear currents through experimentally known electromagnetic and weak transitions in the mass range A=4-9.
