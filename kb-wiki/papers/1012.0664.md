# Nonlocal PNJL model beyond mean field and the QCD phase transition

> A nonlocal chiral quark model is consistently extended beyond mean field using a strict 1/Nc expansion scheme. The parameters of the nonlocal model are refitted so that the physical values of the pion mass and the weak pion decay constant are obtained. The size of the 1/Nc correction to the quark condensate is carefully studied and compared with the usual local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. It is found that even the sign of the corrections can be different. This can be attributed to the mesonic cut-off of the local model. The model is also applied to finite temperature. We find that the 1/Nc corrections dominate the melting of the chiral condensate at low temperatures, T<100 MeV, in agreement with chiral perturbation theory. On the other hand, the relative importance of the 1/Nc corrections in the cross-over regime depends on the parameter T0 of the Polyakov loop potential. For T0=270 MeV, corresponding to a fit of lattice data for pure gluodynamics, the correction terms are large and lead to a lowering of the chiral phase transition temperature in comparison with the mean-field result. Near the phase transition the 1/Nc expansion breaks down and a non-perturbative scheme is needed to include mesonic correlations in that regime. Lowering T0 leads to a more rapid cross-over even at the mean-field level and the unstable region for the 1/Nc corrections shrinks. For T0< 220 MeV the temperatures of deconfinement and chiral restoration are practically synchronized.

## Key claim

The paper extends a nonlocal PNJL model beyond mean field using a strict 1/Nc expansion, finding that the sign of the 1/Nc correction to the quark condensate can be opposite to that in the local NJL model, and that the relative importance of these corrections near the chiral crossover depends on the parameter T0 of the Polyakov loop potential.

## Method

The authors start from a nonlocal chiral quark model with Gaussian form factors and bosonize it to introduce scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields. They then perform a systematic 1/Nc expansion beyond the mean-field level, computing next-to-leading-order corrections to the quark self-energy, the quark condensate, the pion propagator, and the weak pion decay constant. The model parameters (Λ, mc, GΛ²) are refitted at T=0 to reproduce the physical pion mass (139.57 MeV) and weak decay constant (92.42 MeV), and the finite-temperature behavior is studied by coupling the quark sector to a Polyakov loop potential with variable parameter T0.

## Key numbers

* For parameter set No. 4 (Λ=670.3 MeV, mc=9.38 MeV, GΛ²=17.64), the mean-field pion mass is 142.2 MeV and the mean-field weak decay constant is 87.6 MeV; the 1/Nc corrections are about -2 MeV for the pion mass and +5 MeV for fπ.
* The quark condensate as a function of the dimensionless coupling GΛ² shows that the full 1/Nc corrected result (black solid line) lies above the mean-field contribution (red dashed line) for all parameter sets, opposite to the sign found in the local NJL model.
* For T0=270 MeV (fit to pure gluodynamics lattice data), the chiral phase transition temperature Tc is 194 MeV (full 1/Nc) versus 207 MeV (mean field), with deconfinement temperature Td=214 MeV.
* For T0=208 MeV, Tc=169 MeV (full 1/Nc) versus 168 MeV (mean field), and Td=169 MeV, showing synchronization of deconfinement and chiral restoration for T0 < 220 MeV.
* The 1/Nc corrections dominate the melting of the chiral condensate at low temperatures T < 100 MeV, in agreement with chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field treatments of nonlocal PNJL models (Blaschke 2007, Contrera 2007, Hell 2009) by including mesonic fluctuations via a strict 1/Nc expansion, which is a natural expansion parameter for gauge theories (t Hooft 1973). It contrasts with the local NJL model where Oertel (1999, 2000) found negative 1/Nc corrections to the quark condensate; the present nonlocal model yields positive corrections, attributed to the absence of a separate mesonic cutoff. The results enable future non-perturbative treatments of mesonic correlations near the phase transition, where the 1/Nc expansion breaks down, and provide a framework for comparing with lattice QCD data on the quark mass function and wave-function renormalization (Kamleh 2007).
