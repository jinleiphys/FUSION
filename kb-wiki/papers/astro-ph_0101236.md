# Determination of Omega_b From Big Bang Nucleosynthesis in the Presence of Regions of Antimatter

> Production of regions of antimatter in the early universe is predicted in many baryogenesis models. Small scale antimatter regions would annihilate during or soon after nucleosynthesis, affecting the abundances of the light elements. In this paper we study how the acceptable range in Omega_b changes in the presence of antimatter regions, as compared to the standard big bang nucleosynthesis. It turns out that it is possible to produce at the same time both a low 4He value (Y_p < 0.240) and a low D/H value (D/H < 4e-5), but overproduction of 7Li is unavoidable at large Omega_b.

## Key claim

The presence of small-scale antimatter regions in the early universe can reconcile a low primordial helium-4 abundance (Y_p < 0.240) with a low deuterium abundance (D/H < 4e-5) at baryon densities that would be inconsistent in standard big bang nucleosynthesis, but overproduction of lithium-7 is unavoidable at large Omega_b, so the upper limit on Omega_b from lithium-7 remains nearly unchanged.

## Method

The author models antimatter regions as spheres of constant antibaryon density surrounded by a spherical matter shell, characterized by the antimatter fraction R, the net baryon density (given as the baryon-to-photon ratio eta), and the physical radius r_A of the antimatter region. A BBN code is used to compute light-element yields including the effects of annihilation, photodisintegration, nonthermal nuclear reactions, and diffusion, with particular attention to lithium-6 and lithium-7 production channels. The allowed range in eta is then mapped by comparing computed abundances to observational constraints on helium-4, deuterium, and lithium-7.

## Key numbers

- The baryon-to-photon ratio eta is related to Omega_b by eta = 274 x 10^{-10} Omega_b h^2.
- For eta = 6 x 10^{-10} (Omega_b h^2 = 0.0219), ABBN finds consistency with D and He-4 constraints for antimatter fraction R > 2 x 10^{-4} (for Y_p < 0.248) or R > 0.005 (for Y_p < 0.240).
- The lower limit on eta from deuterium overproduction is eta > 4.8 x 10^{-10}, corresponding to Omega_b h^2 > 0.0177.
- The upper limit from lithium-7 overproduction (ZLiH < 4 x 10^{-10}) gives eta < 5.9 x 10^{-10} (Omega_b h^2 < 0.0216).
- The SBBN allowed range is 4.8 < eta_10 < 5.8, while ABBN extends this to 4.8 < eta_10 < 5.9.
- For eta = 8 x 10^{-10} (Omega_b h^2 = 0.0292), D and He-4 can be brought into observed ranges, but ZLiH = 7.3 x 10^{-10} exceeds the lithium-7 limit.
- The threshold for lithium-6 production via photodisintegration of He-4 begins when the temperature falls below T = 67 eV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the author and collaborators (Kurki-Suonio and Sihvola, 2000a,b) that fixed the baryon density to eta = 6 x 10^{-10} and derived constraints on the antimatter fraction, by now scanning a range of eta values. It builds on the initial proposal by Rehm and Jedamzik (1998) that antimatter domains could affect BBN abundances. The paper shows that antimatter BBN alone cannot resolve the discrepancy between the high Omega_b suggested by Boomerang and Maxima-1 CMB experiments (Omega_b h^2 ~ 0.030) and BBN, due to lithium-7 overproduction, but it can help reconcile D and He-4 at intermediate densities.
