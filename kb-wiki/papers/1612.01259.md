# Quarkonium wave functions on the light front

> We study heavy quarkonium within the light-front Hamiltonian formalism. Our effective Hamiltonian is based on the holographic QCD confining potential and the one-gluon exchange interaction with a running coupling. The obtained spectra are compared with experimental measurements. We present a set of light-front wave functions, which exhibit rich structure and are consistent with the nonrelativistic picture. Finally, we use the wave functions to compute the charge and mass radii.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating a running strong coupling (based on perturbative QCD evolution) into the light-front Hamiltonian model for heavy quarkonium significantly improves the mass spectra compared to the fixed-coupling version, reducing the r.m.s. deviation from experimental values by about 50% for charmonium and 25% for bottomonium, while also moderating the violation of rotational symmetry.

## Method

The authors solve an effective light-front Hamiltonian for heavy quarkonium that combines a holographic QCD confining potential (the "soft-wall" model) with a one-gluon exchange interaction. The key improvement over prior work is the introduction of a running strong coupling constant α_s(Q^2) as a function of the exchanged gluon's four-momentum squared, with an infrared cutoff to avoid the perturbative IR catastrophe. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis function representation using harmonic oscillator functions in the transverse holographic variable and Jacobi polynomials in the longitudinal momentum fraction x, with basis truncation parameters N_max and L_max.

## Key numbers

- r.m.s. deviation of calculated charmonium masses from PDG values: 31 MeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- r.m.s. deviation of calculated bottomonium masses from PDG values: 38 MeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- Mean mass spreads (indicating rotational symmetry violation) for charmonia below threshold: 17 MeV
- Mean mass spreads for bottomonia below threshold: 8 MeV
- Confining strength κ for charmonium: 0.966 GeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- Confining strength κ for bottomonium: 1.389 GeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- Effective quark mass for charm: 1.603 GeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- Effective quark mass for bottom: 4.902 GeV (at N_max = L_max = 32)
- Charge radius of η_c: 0.165(7) fm
- Charge radius of η_b: 0.109(1) fm
- Mass radius of η_c: 0.150(6) fm
- Mass radius of η_b: 0.107(4) fm
- Charge radius of η_c(2S): 0.3468(8) fm
- Charge radius of η_b(3S): 0.306(2) fm

## Context

This work extends the light-front holographic model for mesons previously proposed by Li (2016) by incorporating running coupling effects based on perturbative QCD, improving upon the fixed-coupling treatment used in that earlier work. The results are competitive with other approaches such as the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter method (Hilger, 2015) and the covariant spectator theory (Leitao, 2017). The presented light-front wave functions, including those for excited states like ψ(2S), ψ(1D), and η_b(3S), provide a bridge between nonrelativistic quark model intuition and relativistic quantum field theory, and can be used to compute hadronic observables and distributions relevant for deep inelastic scattering and exclusive processes.
