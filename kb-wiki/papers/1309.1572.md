# Symmetry energy from the nuclear collective motion: constraints from dipole, quadrupole, monopole and spin-dipole resonances

> The experimental and theoretical studies of Giant Resonances, or more generally of the nuclear collective vibrations, are a well established domain in which sophisticated techniques have been introduced and firm conclusions reached after an effort of several decades. From it, information on the nuclear equation of state can be extracted, albeit not far from usual nuclear densities. In this contribution, which complements other contributions appearing in the current volume, we survey some of the constraints that have been extracted recently concerning the parameters of the nuclear symmetry energy. Isovector modes, in which neutrons and protons are in opposite phase, are a natural source of information and we illustrate the values of symmetry energy around saturation deduced from isovector dipole and isovector quadrupole states. The isotopic dependence of the isoscalar monopole energy has also been suggested to provide a connection to the symmetry energy: relevant theoretical arguments and experimental results are thoroughly discussed. Finally, we consider the case of the charge-exchange spin-dipole excitations in which the sum rule associated with the total strength gives in principle access to the neutron skin and thus, indirectly, to the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper surveys how nuclear collective excitations (giant resonances) can provide constraints on the symmetry energy parameters \(J\), \(L\), and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) near saturation density, but notes that these constraints are limited to densities close to the usual nuclear density and carry uncertainties from both experimental data and theoretical models.

## Method

The authors review correlations between measured properties of isovector giant dipole resonances (IVGDR), pygmy dipole resonances (PDR), isovector giant quadrupole resonances (IVGQR), isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR), and spin-dipole resonances, and the symmetry energy parameters. They use Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF-RPA) calculations with Skyrme interactions and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models to establish linear correlations between resonance energies or sum-rule fractions and symmetry energy quantities, then extract parameter values by comparing with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{208}\)Pb, the symmetry energy at an average density of 0.1 fm\(^{-3}\) is constrained to \(23.3\ \text{MeV} < S(0.1) < 24.9\ \text{MeV}\) from the IVGDR centroid energy.
* From the PDR in \(^{68}\)Ni and \(^{132}\)Sn, the slope parameter is \(L = 64.8 \pm 15.7\ \text{MeV}\), implying \(J = 32.3 \pm 1.3\ \text{MeV}\).
* Neutron skin values extracted from the PDR analysis are \(\Delta R(^{68}\text{Ni}) = 0.200 \pm 0.015\ \text{fm}\), \(\Delta R(^{132}\text{Sn}) = 0.258 \pm 0.024\ \text{fm}\), and \(\Delta R(^{208}\text{Pb}) = 0.194 \pm 0.024\ \text{fm}\).
* From the IVGQR in \(^{208}\)Pb, assuming \(J = 32 \pm 1\ \text{MeV}\), the extracted values are \(L = 37 \pm 18\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\Delta R(^{208}\text{Pb}) = 0.14 \pm 0.03\ \text{fm}\).
* The asymmetry term of the finite nucleus incompressibility is \(K_\tau = (-400 \pm 100)\ \text{MeV}\) for Skyrme interactions and \(K_\tau = (-620 \pm 180)\ \text{MeV}\) for RMF Lagrangians.
* The nuclear matter incompressibility from ISGMR data is \(K_\infty = 240 \pm 20\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on extracting symmetry energy from giant resonances, such as the hydrodynamical model of Lipparini and Stringari (1989) and the Skyrme-HF-RPA studies of Trippa et al. (2008) and Colo et al. (1995). It complements contributions in the same volume by Piekarewicz on dipole polarizability and by others on astrophysical and heavy-ion collision constraints. The results enable tighter constraints on the symmetry energy parameters \(J\) and \(L\) and their correlation with neutron skin thickness, though the nature of the PDR remains debated due to its mixed isoscalar/isovector character.
