# Intrinsic charm contribution to the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux

> In this work we investigate the impact of intrinsic charm on the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux. The color dipole approach to heavy quark production is generalized to include the contribution of processes initiated by charm quarks. The prompt neutrino flux is calculated assuming the presence of intrinsic charm in the wave function of the projetile hadron. The predictions are compared with previous color dipole results which were obtained taking into account only the process initiated by gluons. In addition, we estimate the atmospheric (conventional + prompt) neutrino flux and compare our predictions with the ICECUBE results for the astrophysical neutrino flux. Our results demonstrate that the contribution of the charm quark initiated process is non - negligible and that the prompt neutrino flux can be enhanced by a factor $\approx$ 2 at large neutrino energies if an intrinsic charm component is present in the proton wave function.

## Key claim

The prompt atmospheric neutrino flux can be enhanced by a factor of approximately 2 at large neutrino energies if an intrinsic charm component is present in the proton wave function, but this enhancement depends on including charm-initiated processes in the color dipole formalism, which had not been done in previous studies.

## Method

The authors generalized the color dipole approach to heavy quark production to include processes initiated by charm quarks in addition to the standard gluon-initiated processes. They calculated the charm Feynman x_F distribution using the BUW saturation model for the dipole-nucleon scattering amplitude and the CTEQ 6.5C parton distribution functions with the BHPS intrinsic charm parametrization, then used the semi-analytical Z-moment approach to compute the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux.

## Key numbers

* The prompt neutrino flux can be enhanced by a factor of approximately 2 at large neutrino energies when intrinsic charm is present in the proton wave function.
* When only the gluon channel is considered, the presence of intrinsic charm implies a reduction of the prompt neutrino flux of approximately 10 percent.
* When the charm channel is included with intrinsic charm, the enhancement is approximately 160 percent in the flux compared to the no-intrinsic-charm case.
* When intrinsic charm is absent, the impact of the charm-initiated channel is approximately 10 percent and almost energy independent.
* The BHPS model assumes an average longitudinal momentum fraction carried by charm and anticharm of 2 percent at the initial scale of QCD evolution.
* The charm quark mass was set to 1.5 GeV and the factorization scale to 4 times the charm mass squared.

## Context

This work extends previous color dipole calculations of the prompt neutrino flux by Enberg, Reno, and Stasto (2008) and Enberg, Stasto, and collaborators, which only considered gluon-initiated processes. It also builds on the authors' earlier study of D-meson production at forward rapidities (Giannini, Goncalves, Navarra, 2017). The results enable more accurate predictions for the atmospheric neutrino background in IceCube and future neutrino telescopes, and demonstrate that intrinsic charm effects cannot be neglected in these calculations.
