# Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction calculations which include N=2 mixing

> We carry out a study of the study of the $Q \cdot Q$ interaction in a model space which consists of several nucleons in an open shell and all $2 \hbar ω$ excitations. This interaction is $ -t (X_o/2) Q \cdot Q$, where for t=1 we get the `accepted strength'. In the $0p$ space, the spectrum would scale with $t$. In this space, the $2^+_1$ and $2^+_2$ states of $^{10}$Be are degenerate, as are the [330] and [411] sets of $J=0^+, 1^+$ and $2^+$ triplets. When $2 \hbar ω$ admixtures are included, the degeneracies are removed. For $t \geq 1.8$ we have new ground state and a new $2_1^+$ state. These are states in which two particles are excited from the $0p$ to the $1s-0d$ shell. There is no mixing of these 2p-2h states with the other states. For these 2p-2h states the occupancy for 0s,0p,1s-0d and 1p-of are 4,4,2 and 0 respectively.

# Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction calculations which include N=2 mixing

## Key claim

The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in an extended model space that includes all 2ħω excitations removes accidental degeneracies present in the 0p valence space and, for sufficiently strong coupling (t ≥ 1.8), produces a new ground-state band of 2p-2h character with integer occupancies that does not mix with the normal 0p-0h band. This behaviour goes beyond simple renormalization of the Q·Q strength and depends on the parity selection rule that forbids matrix elements coupling the 2p-2h configuration to other configurations in the 2ħω space.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code for ¹⁰Be in a model space consisting of the (0p)⁶ valence configuration plus all 2ħω excitations. The Hamiltonian included the single-particle harmonic-oscillator terms and a two-body quadrupole-quadrupole interaction parameterized as V = -t (χ₀/2) Q·Q, with χ₀ = 0.36146 MeV fm⁻¹ and t varied from 0 to 2. The authors computed energies, B(E2) and B(M1) transition strengths, static quadrupole moments, and single-particle occupancies as functions of the coupling strength t.

## Key numbers

- For t=1 in the large space, the 2₁⁺ state in ¹⁰Be lies at 2.19 MeV with B(E2)↑ = 63.8 e² fm⁴ (isoscalar) or 4.954 e² fm⁴ (physical with eₚ=1, eₙ=0); the 2₂⁺ state lies at 3.40 MeV with B(E2)↑ = 113.4 e² fm⁴ (isoscalar) or 47.170 e² fm⁴ (physical).
- The critical coupling strength for the ground-state change is t ≈ 1.76; for t ≥ 1.8 the new 2p-2h band becomes the ground-state band.
- At t=1.7, the normal 0⁺ ground state has occupancies 3.84, 5.78, 0.18, 0.20 for 0s, 0p, 1s-0d, 0f-1p respectively; the 2p-2h 0⁺ state at 0.585 MeV has integer occupancies 4, 4, 2, 0.
- At t=1.1, the static quadrupole moments are Q(2₁⁺) = 11.56 e fm² and Q(2₂⁺) = -11.46 e fm²; at t=2.0, Q(2₁⁺) = -10.85 e fm² and Q(2₂⁺) = 12.34 e fm².
- For t ≥ 1.8, the B(E2) from the new ground state to the 2₁⁺ state stabilizes at 139.9 e² fm⁴.

## Context

This work extends the study of schematic Q·Q interactions beyond the single-major-shell Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) by including N=2 mixing, which the Elliott model explicitly avoids through momentum terms. The authors contrast their full diagonalization results with the simpler Bertsch-Kuo-Brown bubble diagram renormalization (Bertsch, 1965; Kuo and Brown, 1966), which would only renormalize the Q·Q strength and not remove degeneracies. The 2p-2h intruder band found here is analogous to the superdeformed 4p-4h state in ¹⁶O studied by Haxton and Johnson (1990) and to the multi-shell calculations of Zheng et al. (1995) for light nuclei. The work enables future studies of how other multipole components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (dipole-dipole, octupole-octupole) or realistic interactions could mix the 2p-2h band with normal states, and motivates extending the model space to 4ħω excitations to study 4p-4h states and the renormalization of the interaction in the intruder band.
