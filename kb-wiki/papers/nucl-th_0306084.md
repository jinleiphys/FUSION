# Antiprotonic Potentials from Global Fits to the PS209 Data

> The experimental results for strong interaction effects in antiprotonic atoms by the PS209 collaboration consist of high quality data for several sequences of isotopes along the periodic table. Global analysis of these data in terms of a $\bar p$-nucleus optical potential achieves good description of the data using a s-wave finite-range \bar p N interaction. Equally good fits are also obtained with a poorly-defined zero-range potential containing a p-wave term.

## Key claim

Global fits to the PS209 antiprotonic-atom data require a finite-range s-wave isoscalar optical potential, but the data do not require an isovector term or a p-wave term, although a p-wave term improves fits when a zero-range form is used.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of a \(\bar p\)-nucleus optical potential to 90 data points from the PS209 collaboration covering isotopes of Ca, Fe, Ni, Zr, Cd, Sn, Te, and Pb, plus earlier data for \(^{16,18}\)O. They tested zero-range and finite-range (Gaussian folding) potentials with various neutron density shapes (skin, halo, average) and values of the neutron-proton rms radius difference \(r_n - r_p\) taken from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations and from the Trzcinska et al. (2001) analysis.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit finite-range parameters (recommended set): Re \(b_0 = 1.3 \pm 0.1\) fm, Im \(b_0 = 1.9 \pm 0.1\) fm, with folding ranges \(\beta_R = \beta_I = 0.85\) fm.
- For zero-range fits, \(\chi^2/N\) values ranged from 2.7 to 7.7 depending on neutron density shape and \(r_n - r_p\) source; the best zero-range fit gave \(\chi^2/N = 2.7\) (RMF-skin and \(\bar p\)-ave. options).
- For finite-range fits, \(\chi^2/N\) values ranged from 2.2 to 2.6; the best was 2.2 (\(\bar p\)-halo option).
- Including an isovector term \(b_1\) in fits to the full 90-point data set produced no reduction in \(\chi^2\); a nonzero \(b_1\) was obtained only when fitting the Sn isotopes alone, yielding \(\chi^2/N = 1.0\) for those isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of Friedman and Gal (1995, 1997) by incorporating the high-quality PS209 data (Trzcinska et al., 2001), which provided isotope sequences across the periodic table. The results contradict the earlier zero-range-only approach by demonstrating that a finite-range interaction is required, and they show that the isovector term and p-wave term are not statistically justified for the full data set. The recommended potential parameters enable improved predictions for antiprotonic atom strong-interaction shifts and widths, and the density-shape analysis informs future studies of neutron distributions in nuclei.
