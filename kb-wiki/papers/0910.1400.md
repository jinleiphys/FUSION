# Energy Dependence of Directed Flow in Au+Au Collisions from a Multi-phase Transport Model

> The directed flow of charged hadron and identified particles has been studied in the framework of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, for $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200, 130, 62.4, 39, 17.2 and 9.2 GeV. The rapidity, centrality and energy dependence of directed flow for charged particles over a wide rapidity range are presented. AMPT model gives the correct $v_1(y)$ slope, as well as its trend as a function of energy, while it underestimates the magnitude. Within the AMPT model, the proton $v_1$ slope is found to change its sign when the energy increases to 130 GeV - a feature that is consistent with ``anti-flow''. Hadronic re-scattering is found having little effect on $v_1$ at top RHIC energies. These studies can help us to understand the collective dynamics at early times in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and they can also be served as references for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key claim

The AMPT model reproduces the correct shape and energy trend of directed flow (v1) as a function of rapidity for Au+Au collisions, but systematically underestimates the magnitude of v1 by approximately 75%, likely due to the absence of mean-field potentials in the hadronic cascade component of the model.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at six beam energies, employing the string melting scenario for energies of 39 GeV and above and the default scenario for 17.2 and 9.2 GeV. Directed flow v1 was calculated as a function of rapidity for charged hadrons and identified particles (protons and pions) across three centrality bins (0-30%, 30-60%, 60-80%), integrating over transverse momentum up to 4.0 GeV/c with a parton cross section of 3 mb.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron v1 slope at midrapidity (|y'| < 0.5) was calculated for six collision energies: 9.2, 17.3, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV, with the slope decreasing as energy increases.
- The AMPT model underestimates the magnitude of v1 by a factor of approximately 0.25 (75% reduction) compared to STAR and PHOBOS data at top RHIC energies.
- Proton v1 slope changes sign when the collision energy increases to 130 GeV, consistent with the "anti-flow" scenario where protons flow in the same direction as pions.
- Hadronic rescattering has little effect on v1 at top RHIC energies (200 GeV) but a significant effect at low energies (9.2 GeV), as shown by comparing AMPT calculations with and without the hadron cascade (ART).

## Context

This work extends prior AMPT model studies of directed flow (AMPTv1old, 2008) to a wider energy range spanning 9.2 to 200 GeV, providing reference calculations for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The results confirm the "anti-flow" scenario for protons proposed by earlier theoretical work (Stocker, 1980s) and show that the string melting scenario is appropriate for high energies while the default scenario works better at 9.2 GeV, consistent with findings by Jinhui Chen and Jiaxu Zuo (2008). The study enables systematic comparisons between model predictions and experimental data from STAR and PHOBOS across multiple beam energies, helping to constrain the equation of state and search for signatures of the QCD phase transition.
