# Linear Response Theory with finite-range interactions

> This review focuses on the calculation of infinite nuclear matter response functions using phenomenological finite-range interactions, equipped or not with tensor terms. These include Gogny and Nakada families, which are commonly used in the literature. Because of the finite-range, the main technical difficulty stems from the exchange terms of the particle-hole interaction. We first present results based on the so-called Landau and Landau-like approximations of the particle-hole interaction. Then, we review two methods which in principle provide numerically exact response functions. The first one is based on a multipolar expansion of both the particle-hole interaction and the particle-hole propagator and the second one consists in a continued fraction expansion of the response function. The numerical precision can be pushed to any degree of accuracy, but it is actually shown that two or three terms suffice to get converged results. Finally, we apply the formalism to the determination of possible finite-size instabilities induced by a finite-range interaction.

## Key claim

This review demonstrates that for finite-range interactions (Gogny and Nakada families), the exchange terms of the particle-hole interaction can be treated exactly using either a multipolar expansion or a continued fraction expansion, and that two or three terms in either expansion suffice to obtain converged response functions, contrary to the common assumption that many terms would be required.

## Method

The authors review the calculation of infinite nuclear matter response functions using phenomenological finite-range interactions with central, density-dependent, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. They first present results based on the Landau and Landau-like approximations of the particle-hole interaction, then describe two numerically exact methods: a multipolar expansion of both the particle-hole interaction and the particle-hole propagator, and a continued fraction expansion of the response function. The formalism is applied to detect finite-size instabilities induced by the finite-range interaction.

## Key numbers

* The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, denoted ρ₀, is used as a reference: instabilities appearing at densities lower than approximately 1.3 ρ₀ may affect convergence of calculations in finite nuclei.
* For the Gogny interaction, the Gaussian range parameters μᵢ are fixed between 0.5 and 0.8 fm for the shorter range and between 1 and 1.2 fm for the longer range.
* The density power γ in the Gogny density-dependent term is usually fixed to the value 1/3.
* For the Skyrme N2LO interaction, evaluating the effective mass at the Fermi momentum leads to a violation of the energy-weighted sum rule of at most 10% for large transferred momentum (approximately 2k_F).

## Context

This review extends the methods previously developed for zero-range Skyrme interactions (reviewed in Report (2013)) to the more complex case of finite-range interactions, specifically the Gogny family (Gogny (1975), Decharge and Gogny (1980), Robledo et al. (2019)) and the Nakada family (Nakada (2003), Nakada (2020)). The work enables the detection of finite-size instabilities in phenomenological interactions before they are used in finite-nucleus calculations, building on earlier studies by Pastore et al. (2012), Hellemans et al. (2013), and Pastore et al. (2015) that linked singularities in infinite matter to instabilities in atomic nuclei.
