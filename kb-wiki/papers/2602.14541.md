# The initial data of effective field theories of relativistic viscous fluids and gravity

> There has been recent progress in developing well-posed theories of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and of gravitational effective field theories. These have in common the feature that they introduce unphysical degrees of freedom. We address the problem of how these should be treated. We propose a ''reduction of order'' approach which is applied not at the level of equations of motion but only to initial data. This specifies uniquely the data for the unphysical modes in terms of the data for the physical modes. We argue that the apparent breaking of Lorentz invariance associated with this approach is not a problem provided one restricts to Lorentz frames for which the assumptions of effective field theory are manifestly valid.

## Key claim
The authors propose that the unphysical degrees of freedom introduced in well-posed formulations of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and gravitational effective field theories should be eliminated not by modifying the equations of motion (which would break Lorentz invariance and often destroy well-posedness), but by applying a reduction-of-order procedure exclusively to the initial data, thereby fixing the unphysical modes uniquely in terms of the physical ones.

## Method
The authors demonstrate their approach on two linear toy models in 1+1 dimensions: a BDNK-type heat conduction equation for a moving rod and a Ruderman-Bludman dispersive sound model. In each case, they use the fact that the effective field theory expansion allows replacing the highest time derivatives in the higher-order equation with spatial derivatives (order reduction), producing a lower-order equation accurate to the same truncation order. This lower-order equation is then used to constrain the initial data for the higher time derivatives, leaving only the physical degrees of freedom as free initial data. The authors provide general theorems for linear theories showing that the extra degrees of freedom are always fast (their frequencies diverge as the UV cutoff goes to zero) and that a suitable order-reduction polynomial always exists.

## Key numbers
- For the heat conduction model, the diffusion coefficient lambda is of the order of the mean free path and serves as the UV cutoff scale; the order-reduced equation is accurate to O(lambda^2).
- For the dispersive sound model, the infrared speed of sound c_s is taken as 1/2 in the numerical example, with lambda k = 0.2.
- The characteristic frequency of the unphysical fast oscillations in the sound model is of order lambda^{-1}, placing them outside the EFT's regime of validity.
- In the sound model, the order-reduced equation contains a term lambda^2 (1 - c_s^2)^2 partial^4_x phi, showing the explicit coefficient of the spatial derivative correction.

## Context
This work extends the recent breakthroughs by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2019-2020) in formulating well-posed relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (BDNK theory) and by Figueras, Held, and Kovacs (2024) in gravitational effective field theories. Both approaches introduce unphysical degrees of freedom that must be handled. The reduction-of-order approach for initial data was suggested by Figueras, Held, and Kovacs (2024) for gravity and used for scalar field EFTs by Figueras and collaborators (2025), but the authors note it has not been discussed or implemented previously for hydrodynamics. The paper argues that the apparent breaking of Lorentz invariance from choosing a time coordinate for the reduction is not problematic if one restricts to Lorentz frames where the EFT assumptions (small gradients compared to the UV scale) are manifestly valid.
