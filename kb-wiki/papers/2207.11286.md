# Novel relaxation time approximation: a consistent calculation of transport coefficients with QCD-inspired relaxation times

> We use a novel formulation of the relaxation time approximation to consistently calculate the bulk and shear viscosity coefficients using QCD-inspired energy-dependent relaxation times and phenomenological thermal masses obtained from fits to lattice QCD thermodynamics. The matching conditions are conveniently chosen to simplify the computations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a novel formulation of the relaxation time approximation (RTA) can consistently compute bulk and shear viscosity coefficients for a quasi-particle model with QCD-inspired energy-dependent relaxation times, but only when using alternative matching conditions that allow nonzero out-of-equilibrium corrections to the energy density.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for quasi-particles with a temperature-dependent mass, using a new RTA collision term that preserves conservation laws even with momentum-dependent relaxation times. They parametrize the relaxation time as \(\tau_R = t_R (E_{\mathbf{p}}/T)^\gamma\) and impose matching conditions where \(\langle 1 \rangle \equiv \langle 1 \rangle_0\), which defines temperature such that the interaction energy depends only on temperature out of equilibrium. Transport coefficients are then extracted via the Chapman-Enskog expansion truncated at first order.

## Key numbers

- At low temperatures where \(M/T \rightarrow \infty\), all three normalized coefficients (bulk viscosity, energy correction, shear viscosity) behave as \((M/T)^{\gamma-1}\)
- For \(\gamma = 1\), the shear viscosity satisfies \(\eta = t_R (\varepsilon_0 + P_0)\) at all temperatures
- In the high-temperature limit \(M/T \rightarrow 0\), \(\zeta = -(1/3)\chi \propto M(T) (d/dT)(M(T)/T)\) and \(\eta \sim \Gamma(\gamma + 5)/120\)
- At high temperatures, the mass ratio saturates at \(M(T)/T \approx 1.1\)

## Context

This work extends the effective kinetic model of Alqahtani (2015) and Rocha (2022) by implementing the novel RTA framework proposed by Rocha (2021), which improves upon the traditional RTA of Anderson (1974) by preserving conservation laws for momentum-dependent relaxation times. The results enable future computation of transport coefficients in other hydrodynamic theories, such as those developed by Denicol (2012) and Bemfica (2017), using the same quasi-particle model with temperature-dependent masses fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics from Borsanyi (2010).
