# Effective chiral theory of kaon-nucleon scattering

> We apply the relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian density to kaon-nucleon scattering imposing constraints from the pion-nucleon s- and p-wave threshold parameters at chiral order $Q^2$. The s and u channel decouplet baryon exchange is included explicitly and is found to play a crucial role in understanding the empirical s- and p-wave nuclear kaon dynamics quantitatively.

## Key claim

The relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, when combined with a Bethe-Salpeter equation and a subtraction scheme that identifies the subtraction point with the hyperon mass, can quantitatively describe both s- and p-wave kaon-nucleon scattering, but only if the explicit decouplet baryon exchange (particularly the Σ*(1385)) is included and the relativistic (rather than heavy-baryon) form of the Lagrangian is used.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian density, truncated at chiral order Q², to kaon-nucleon scattering. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the scattering amplitude using a subtraction scheme (rather than a cutoff scheme) with the subtraction point identified with the hyperon mass to protect s-channel hyperon exchange contributions. The 11 free parameters of the local 4-point interaction terms were adjusted to reproduce elastic and inelastic K⁻p cross section data, angular distributions, the spectral forms of the Λ*(1405) and Σ*(1385) resonances, and pion-nucleon s- and p-wave threshold parameters.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_π ≃ 93 MeV is used as input, determined from the weak decay width of charged pions.
- The parameters F ≃ 0.45 and D ≃ 0.80 are constrained by weak decay widths of baryon octet states, and C ≃ 1.5–1.7 is constrained by hadronic decay widths of baryon decouplet states.
- The empirical estimates for chiral symmetry breaking parameters are b_D ≃ (0.064 ± 0.004) GeV⁻¹, b_F ≃ −(0.209 ± 0.004) GeV⁻¹, and d_D ≃ (0.87 ± 0.04) GeV⁻¹.
- The pion-nucleon sigma term is σ_πN = (45 ± 10) MeV, related to the parameter b₀.
- The Λ*(1405) resonance structure disappears when the kaon mass is reduced to a hypothetical 300 MeV, demonstrating the non-perturbative nature of the strangeness sector.
- The data set for K⁻p cross sections is reproduced including precise data points for laboratory momenta 200 MeV < p_lab < 300 MeV.
- The low density theorem for the kaon self energy in isospin symmetric nuclear matter is given by Π_K(ω, q) = −4π (√s/m_N) [ f^(s-wave)_KN(√s) + (3ω²/(5m_N²) k_F² + q²) f^(p-wave)_KN(√s) ] ρ + ... where k_F is the Fermi momentum.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) approach to kaon-nucleon scattering by including both s- and p-waves, whereas earlier coupled-channel K-matrix approaches (such as those by A.D. Martin) and previous chiral analyses (Kaiser, Ramos) were limited to s-waves or used cutoff schemes with channel-dependent parameters. The authors argue that their subtraction scheme avoids the uncontrolled SU(3) symmetry breaking and strong cutoff sensitivity of prior work. The results enable a more reliable determination of subthreshold K⁻-nucleon scattering amplitudes, which are essential for computing kaon spectral functions in nuclear matter and understanding kaonic atoms and subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that the low density theorem becomes unreliable at densities below 0.1 ρ₀ for energies near the kaon mass due to the Λ*(1405) resonance, motivating self-consistent in-medium calculations.
