# A simple model to investigate jet quenching and correlated errors for centrality-dependent nuclear-modification factors in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We apply Bayesian techniques to compare a simple, empirical model for jet-quenching in heavy-ion collisions to centrality-dependent jet-$R_{AA}$ measured by ATLAS for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$~TeV. We find that the $R_{AA}$ values for central collisions are adequately described with a model for the mean $p_T$-dependent jet energy-loss using only 2-parameters. This model is extended by incorporating 2D initial geometry information from TRENTO and compared to centrality-dependent $R_{AA}$ values. We find that the results are sensitive to value of the jet-quenching formation time, $τ_f$, and that the optimal value of $τ_f$ varies with the assumed path-length dependence of the energy-loss. We construct a covariance error matrix for the data from the $p_T$ dependent contributions to the ATLAS systematic errors and perform Bayesian calibrations for several different assumptions for the systematic error correlations. We show that most-probable functions and $χ^2$ values are sensitive to assumptions made when fitting to correlated errors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a simple two-parameter model for mean jet energy loss can adequately describe centrality-dependent jet nuclear-modification factors measured by ATLAS, but only when systematic error correlations are properly accounted for; the optimal jet-quenching formation time varies with the assumed path-length dependence of energy loss, and the shape of the energy-loss distribution is not constrained by R_AA measurements alone.

## Method

The authors fit a two-parameter power-law-plus-logarithm function for mean transverse momentum loss to ATLAS inclusive jet cross-sections in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, then apply Bayesian calibration using the emcee package with 10,000 samples and 8 walkers to centrality-dependent jet R_AA data from Pb+Pb collisions at the same energy. The model is extended by incorporating two-dimensional initial geometry from the TRENTO model with parameters from a prior Bayesian analysis (JETSCAPE framework), using 10,000 minimum-bias events separated into ATLAS centrality bins with 10 dijet paths per event. Sensitivity studies are performed for linear and quadratic path-length weighting of energy loss at three formation times (0.1, 0.5, 0.9 fm/c), using both semi-diagonal and fully-covariant treatments of systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS jet R_AA data span 158 < p_T < 631 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The pp jet cross-section fit uses p_T range 100-800 GeV with parameters c_4=700 GeV and c_5=150 GeV fixed; chi-squared values are 9.5 (statistical errors only) and 3.4 (combined errors)
- For central (0-10%) collisions, the gamma-distribution fit yields alpha=0.22, beta=0.18, k=7.18 (diagonal errors) and alpha=0.14, beta=0.25, k=8.19 (covariant errors); delta-distribution yields alpha=1.41, beta=0.20 (diagonal) and alpha=1.08, beta=0.26 (covariant)
- For centrality-dependent fits with linear path-weighting and tau_f=0.9 fm/c: semi-diagonal chi-squared=0.74, fully-covariant chi-squared=2.98; with quadratic weighting and tau_f=0.1 fm/c: semi-diagonal chi-squared=0.74, fully-covariant chi-squared=2.16
- TRENTO parameters: p=0.0 (reduced thickness), k=1.0 (fluctuation), w=1.0 fm (nucleon width), d=1.4 fm (minimum distance), sigma=6.4 fm (cross-section); grid size 10x10 fm with 0.2 fm spacing

## Context

This work extends the simple empirical jet-quenching approach of the prior study by the same group (Soltz et al., 2024) that used a similar model for central collisions only, and is related to the Bayesian framework of JETSCAPE (2021) for multi-stage hydrodynamic models. The paper demonstrates that R_AA measurements alone constrain only the mean energy loss, not the shape of the loss distribution, and that proper treatment of correlated systematic errors is essential for reliable parameter extraction. The results enable future studies that combine R_AA with dijet asymmetry measurements to constrain the full energy-loss distribution, and provide a fast modeling platform for sensitivity studies before deploying more computationally expensive frameworks.
