# Debye screening and Meissner effect in a two-flavor color superconductor

> I compute the gluon self-energy in a color superconductor with two flavors of massless quarks, where condensation of Cooper pairs breaks SU(3)_c to SU(2)_c. At zero temperature, there is neither Debye screening nor a Meissner effect for the three gluons of the unbroken SU(2)_c subgroup. The remaining five gluons attain an electric as well as a magnetic mass. For temperatures approaching the critical temperature for the onset of color superconductivity, or for gluon momenta much larger than the color-superconducting gap, the self-energy assumes the form given by the standard hard-dense loop approximation. The gluon self-energy determines the coefficient of the kinetic term in the effective low-energy theory for the condensate fields.

## Key claim

At zero temperature, the three gluons of the unbroken SU(2)_c subgroup in a two-flavor color superconductor exhibit neither Debye screening nor a Meissner effect, while the remaining five gluons acquire both an electric and a magnetic mass; this result is conditional on the specific symmetry-breaking pattern where condensation of Cooper pairs breaks SU(3)_c to SU(2)_c.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop quark contribution to the gluon self-energy in the color-superconducting phase using Nambu–Gor'kov spinors, working in Euclidean space-time with massless quarks and equal chemical potentials for both flavors. The self-energy is evaluated in the zero-energy, zero-momentum limit to extract Debye and Meissner masses, and also in the limit where gluon momentum is much larger than the superconducting gap. The calculation treats the three gluons of the unbroken SU(2)_c subgroup separately from the five broken gluons.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature gluon mass scale is \( m_g^2 = g^2 \frac{N_f}{6\pi^2} \mu^2 \), where \( N_f = 2 \) and \( \mu \) is the quark chemical potential.
- The critical temperature for melting the Cooper pair condensate is \( T_c \simeq 0.57 \, \phi_0 \), where \( \phi_0 \) is the zero-temperature gap.
- In the static limit \( p_0 = 0 \), the normal-phase Debye mass squared is \( \Pi_0^{00}(0) \simeq -3 m_g^2 \).
- The zero-temperature gap scales as \( \phi_0 \sim \mu \exp(-c_{\rm QCD}/g) \) with \( c_{\rm QCD} = 3\pi^2/\sqrt{2} \).

## Context

This work extends the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation, previously applied to the normal phase by LeBellac and others, to the color-superconducting phase where the quark loop must account for the condensate. It corrects the inconsistency in earlier HDL treatments that used normal-phase quark excitations in the loop. The results enable improved gap equation calculations, following initial estimates by E. H. et al. (1999) that found a reduction of the zero-temperature gap when including the Meissner effect.
