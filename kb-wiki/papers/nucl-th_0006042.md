# Charge Symmetry Violation in $pn\to d π^0$ as a Test of Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Chiral effective field theory predicts a specific charge symmetry violating amplitude for pion production. This term is shown to provide the dominant contribution to the forward-backward asymmetry in the angular distribution for the reaction $pn\to dπ^0$, for reasonable values of the mass difference between down and up quarks, $δm_N$. Using a value $δm_N\approx$ 3 MeV leads to a prediction of a 10 standard deviation effect for a TRIUMF experiment.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory predicts that a specific charge-symmetry-violating (CSB) amplitude, arising from the quark mass difference between up and down quarks, provides the dominant contribution to the forward-backward asymmetry in the reaction \(pn\to d\pi^0\) near threshold, provided the quark-mass contribution to the nucleon mass difference \(\delta m_N\) is approximately 3 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the forward-backward asymmetry \(A_{fb}\) for \(pn\to d\pi^0\) at a laboratory energy of 279.5 MeV (pion center-of-mass momentum \(\eta = q_\pi/m_\pi = 0.17\)) by including the leading isospin-violating seagull interactions from chiral effective field theory, proportional to \(\delta m_N\) (quark-mass contribution) and \(\bar\delta m_N\) (electromagnetic contribution). These are added to a previously developed nucleon-delta coupled-channel model that includes \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing and describes both pion production and \(NN\) phase shifts. The asymmetry arises from interference between \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave pion amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The predicted total forward-backward asymmetry at 279.5 MeV is \(A_{fb} = 0.69\%\), using the quark model estimate \(\delta m_N - \bar\delta m_N/2 \simeq 4\) MeV (with \(\delta m_N = 3.1\) MeV and \(\bar\delta m_N = -1.8\) MeV).
- The contribution from the quark-mass and electromagnetic seagull terms alone is \(0.97\%\), which is about \(-3.5\) times the strength of (and opposite in sign to) the sum of contributions from \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing, which gave \(A_{fb} = -0.28\%\).
- The predicted asymmetry corresponds to a 10 standard deviation effect for the TRIUMF experiment of Ref. allena, which aims to measure \(A_{fb}\) with a precision of \(\pm 0.06\%\).
- The ratio of the CSB to isospin-conserving matrix elements is approximately \(0.03\) from a rough estimate using \(\delta m_N - \bar\delta m_N/2 \simeq 4\) MeV.
- The asymmetry is parametrized as \(A_{fb} \simeq -0.28\% \times \left(1 - \frac{0.87}{\text{MeV}} (\delta m_N - \frac{\bar\delta m_N}{2})\right)\) at 279.5 MeV.
- The theoretical uncertainty on the asymmetry is estimated at about 20\%.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn\to d\pi^0\) by Niskanen (1999), which included nucleon-mass splitting effects and \(\pi\)-\(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing but did not include the seagull interactions from chiral effective field theory. The authors show that the chiral-predicted seagull terms dominate over \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing by a factor of about \(m_N/m_\pi\) near threshold, contrary to earlier expectations. The measurement of \(A_{fb}\) at TRIUMF (experiment by Allen et al.) can be combined with the known nucleon mass difference \(m_n - m_p = 1.3\) MeV to extract the separate quark-mass and electromagnetic contributions \(\delta m_N\) and \(\bar\delta m_N\), providing a test of chiral effective field theory and QCD. The authors also suggest that the reaction \(dd\to\alpha\pi^0\), planned at IUCF, could provide complementary information on charge symmetry violation.
