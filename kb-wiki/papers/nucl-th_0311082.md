# Isotopic Yields and Isoscaling in Fission

> A simple model is proposed to examine the isotopic yields of the fragments from binary fission. For a given charge partition the peaks and widths in the isotope distributions are studied both with the liquid-drop model and with shell modifications. The basis for isoscaling is also explored. The symmetry energy plays a dominant role in both the distributions and the isoscaling behavior. A systematic increase in the isoscaling parameter, $α$, with the proton number of the fragment element is predicted in the context of the liquid-drop model. Deviations arising from shell corrections are explored.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy dominates isotopic yields and isoscaling in binary fission, but shell corrections near magic numbers (N=50 and N=82) can shift peak positions by up to 3 mass units, reduce widths by about 20%, and cause strong deviations from the linear isoscaling behavior predicted by the liquid-drop model alone.

## Method

The author assumes that at scission the fissioning system is in thermal equilibrium, so isotopic yields for a given charge partition follow a Boltzmann factor exp((BE₁+BE₂)/T) using the binding energies of the two complementary fragments. The model first uses liquid-drop binding energies with conventional coefficients, then adds shell corrections taken from tabulated mass differences between experimental masses and liquid-drop predictions. Isoscaling ratios R₂₁(n,z) are computed by comparing yields from two fissioning systems of different N/Z (e.g., ²³⁴U and ²³⁹U) at the same temperature.

## Key numbers

- For the fission of ²³⁴U (Z=92), the observed independent yields for complementary fragments with z₁=30 and z₂=62 are consistent with a temperature T=1.7-1.9 MeV, after shifting peak positions by 0.75 mass units for the lighter element and 2.0 mass units for the heavier element (attributed to evaporation).
- The Gaussian width σ of the isotope distribution is given by σ⁻² = 8(C_sym/T)(Z/A)³(Z/(z₁z₂)), where C_sym ≈ 23 MeV.
- Shell corrections shift the peak of the isotope distribution by up to approximately 3 mass units for the pair with charges z=50 and z=42, where the heavier fragment has neutron number 82 (a closed shell).
- The binding energy difference (tabulated minus liquid-drop) at the peak reaches approximately 8 MeV for the same pair (z=50 and z=42).
- Shell corrections reduce the width of the isotope distribution by about 20% for the binary pair with charges 50 and 42.
- For the comparison of ²³⁴U and ²³⁹U fission, the isoscaling parameter α ranges from about 0.40 to 1.0 over accessible z values at temperatures of 1.7-1.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of symmetry energy and isoscaling, previously examined in deep-inelastic collisions, evaporation, and multifragmentation (Tsang et al., 2001; other works cited as ts1, ts2, ts3), to the low-energy, mass-conservation-constrained regime of binary fission. The model is consistent with fission data from the literature (Wahl, 1988; LBL compilation) and predicts a systematic increase of the isoscaling parameter α with fragment proton number, a feature not seen in other reactions because fission covers a wider range of fragment charges. The findings enable future work to use isoscaling in fission as a probe of the symmetry energy, but caution that shell corrections near N=50 and N=82 can obscure the signal; a recent preprint by Veselsky et al. (2003) reports related observations in the fission database, though with a different interpretation.
