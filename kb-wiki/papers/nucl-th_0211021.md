# Quadrupole and octupole softness in the N=Z nucleus 64Ge

> Quadrupole and octupole softness in the even-even N=Z nucleus 64Ge is studied on the spherical shell model basis. We carry out the shell model calculation using the pairing plus quadrupole (QQ) plus octupole (OO) interaction with monopole corrections. It is shown that 64Ge is an unstable nucleus with respect to both the quadrupole and octupole deformations, which is consistent with the previous discussions predicting the gamma softness and octupole instability. It is demonstrated that proton-neutron part QpQn of the QQ interaction is important for the gamma softness or triaxiality.

# Quadrupole and octupole softness in the N=Z nucleus 64Ge

## Key claim

Using a spherical shell model with a pairing-plus-quadrupole-plus-octupole interaction including monopole corrections, the authors show that 64Ge is unstable with respect to both quadrupole and octupole deformations, but the octupole force strength adopted (0.275 MeV) lies very close to a critical point, indicating the nucleus is near octupole instability rather than exhibiting permanent octupole deformation.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in a restricted model space comprising the 2p3/2, 1f5/2, 2p1/2, and 1g9/2 orbitals (pfg-shell) above a closed 56Ni core, using an isospin-invariant Hamiltonian that includes monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ), and isoscalar octupole-octupole (OO) interactions with monopole corrections. The force strengths were adjusted phenomenologically to reproduce low-lying energy levels of 64Ge and neighboring nuclei, and the dependence of level energies and B(E2) values on the quadrupole and octupole force strengths was studied to identify critical points for shape transitions.

## Key numbers

- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 245.3 e²fm⁴, corresponding to quadrupole deformation β ∼ 0.2
- Ratio of excitation energies E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 2.65, indicating γ softness (compared to 3.33 for rigid rotor, 2.0 for pure vibrator, ∼2.4 for γ-soft nucleus)
- Ratio B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 2₁⁺)/B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 0₁⁺) ∼ 27, corresponding to γ ∼ 26° in the Davydov model
- Adopted quadrupole force strength χ₂⁰ = 0.567 (42/A)⁵/³/b², with critical point for quadrupole phase transition at χ₂cr⁰ ∼ 0.45 MeV
- Adopted octupole force strength χ₃⁰ = 0.275 (42/A)²/b⁶, with critical point for octupole phase transition at χ₃cr⁰ ∼ 0.3 MeV
- Critical point for proton-neutron quadrupole force strength at χ₂pncr⁰ ∼ 0.35 MeV
- Single-particle energies relative to 2p3/2: ε(p3/2)=0.0, ε(f5/2)=0.77, ε(p1/2)=1.11, ε(g9/2)=3.70 MeV
- Occupation numbers in ground-state band: p3/2=3.6, f5/2=3.2, p1/2=0.6, g9/2=0.6 (average)

## Context

This work extends the extended P+QQ force model of Hasegawa (2000) by introducing an octupole-octupole interaction to describe negative-parity states, building on earlier mean-field predictions of γ instability in 64Ge by Ennis (1997) and octupole-driving effects near nucleon numbers 34, 56, 88, and 134 as discussed by Butler (1996). The results are compared with full fp-shell calculations using the FPD6 interaction by Honma (2001) and the quantum Monte Carlo diagonalization method, and with projected shell model calculations by Sun (2000). The finding that the proton-neutron QpQn interaction drives γ softness and triaxiality enables future studies of prolate-oblate shape coexistence in the neighboring N=Z nucleus 68Se, recently observed by Skoda (2000) and Fischer (2000).
