# Isospin effects in N~Z nuclei in extended Density Functional Theory

> This paper overviews various phenomena related to the concept of isospin symmetry. The focus is on N~Z nuclei, which are excellent laboratories of isospin physics. The theoretical framework applied is nuclear Density Functional Theory and its isospin- and angular-momentum projected extensions, as well as symmetry-projected multi-reference models. The topics covered include: isospin impurities, superallowed beta decays, beta-transitions in mirror nuclei, isospin breaking hadronic interactions, mirror and triplet binding energy differences, and isoscalar pairing.

## Key claim

This overview argues that self-consistent multi-reference Density Functional Theory with isospin and angular-momentum projection provides a rigorous no-core framework for calculating isospin symmetry breaking effects in N≈Z nuclei, but the lack of a reasonable Hamiltonian-based Skyrme energy density functional with standard saturation properties remains the most critical deficiency of the current approach.

## Method

The authors apply nuclear Density Functional Theory and its extensions, including isospin-projected and double-projected (isospin plus angular-momentum) multi-reference models, to study isospin impurities, superallowed beta decays, mirror beta transitions, and binding energy differences in N≈Z nuclei. Self-consistent mean-field states are decomposed into good-isospin basis via projection after variation, and the total Hamiltonian is rediagonalized in the space of projected states to obtain true isospin-mixed wave functions. For superallowed decays, the formalism uses anti-aligned configurations in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, with dynamical mixing of states projected from multiple reference configurations solved via the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation.

## Key numbers

- Isospin impurities α_C in even-even N=Z nuclei calculated with SLy4 EDF show mean-field (BR) values are quenched by approximately 30% relative to after-rediagonalization (AR) values
- For ⁸⁰Zr, the average theoretical isospin impurity from nine Skyrme EDFs is ᾱ_C^TH ≈ 4.4% ± 0.3%, lying within experimental uncertainty limits
- The SV EDF yields α_C ≈ 2.8% for ⁸⁰Zr, considerably smaller than the mean value of 4.4% ± 0.3%
- The universal constant K/(ħc)⁶ = 8120.2787(11) × 10⁻¹⁰ GeV⁻⁴ s
- The nucleus-independent radiative correction Δ^V_R = 2.361(38)%
- From MR-DFT calculations with SV force: ᾱℱt = 3073.6(12) s giving |V_ud| = 0.97397(27)
- From improved calculations with dynamical mixing: ᾱℱt = 3073.7(11) s and |V_ud| = 0.97396(25)
- CKM unitarity: |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² = 0.99935(67)
- The χ² per degree of freedom for ISB corrections drops from 10.2 to 6.3 when using dynamical mixing of configurations
- The ⁶²Ga→⁶²Zn transition contributes more than 62% to the total χ² budget
- Quenching factor expression for shell-model calculations: q_s = 1 − 0.19(A/16)^0.35 for A ≤ 40
- Quenching from two-body currents estimated as q_s = 0.84–0.92 from Ikeda sum rule study

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent DFT framework developed by Satula et al. (2009, 2010, 2011, 2012) for isospin physics, building on early estimates of isospin impurities from perturbation theory by Sliv (1965) and the hydrodynamical model by Bohr (1967). The calculated ISB corrections for superallowed decays are compared with the standard shell-model results of Hardy and Towner (2008, 2014, 2015) and the RPA results of Liang et al. (2009), showing reasonable agreement. The framework enables more rigorous treatment of isospin symmetry breaking corrections needed for precision tests of the Standard Model, including determination of V_ud from superallowed beta decays and mirror beta transitions, though the lack of a Hamiltonian-based Skyrme EDF with standard effective mass remains a limitation.
