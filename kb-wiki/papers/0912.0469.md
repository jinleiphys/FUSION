# On strange SU(3) partners of Theta+

> We propose a scenario in which Roper octet can mix with a putative antidecuplet of exotic baryons and predict the properties of its strange members. We show that 1795 MeV < M_Sigma_{\bar{10}} <1830 MeV and 1900 MeV < M_Xi_{\bar{10}}<1970 MeV. We also estimate total widths: 10 MeV < Gamma_Σ_{\bar{10}}< 30 MeV} and Gamma_Ξ_{\bar{10}}\sim 10 MeV and branching ratios for different decay modes.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the Roper octet of baryons can mix with the putative antidecuplet of exotic baryons, and they predict the masses and decay properties of the strange members of the antidecuplet (Sigma and Xi states) under the assumption that the Theta+ exists with mass 1540 MeV and width below 1 MeV, and that a new narrow nucleon resonance N(1685) exists with total width below 25 MeV.

## Method

The authors perform a three-state mixing analysis among the ground-state octet, the Roper octet (N(1440) family), and the antidecuplet, using SU(3) flavor symmetry with mixing angles alpha (antidecuplet-ground octet) and phi (antidecuplet-Roper octet). They constrain the mixing angles by requiring that the known properties of Theta+ and the putative N(1685) are reproduced, then use the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formulae and decay width formulas based on the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation to predict masses, total widths, and branching ratios for the Sigma and Xi members of the antidecuplet.

## Key numbers

- Mass range for Sigma_antidecuplet: 1795 MeV < M_Sigma < 1830 MeV
- Mass range for Xi_antidecuplet: 1900 MeV < M_Xi < 1970 MeV
- Total width range for Sigma_antidecuplet: 10 MeV < Gamma_Sigma < 30 MeV
- Total width for Xi_antidecuplet: Gamma_Xi ~ 10 MeV
- Assumed mass of Theta+: M_Theta = 1540 MeV
- Assumed mass of N(1685): 1685 MeV
- Assumed total width of N(1685): Gamma_N(1685) < 25 MeV
- Assumed Theta+ width: Gamma_Theta < 1 MeV
- Input pion-nucleon coupling: g_piNN = 13.21
- Input Roper decay widths from PDG: Gamma_R->Npi = 152.1 MeV, Gamma_R->Deltapi = 58.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM) predictions for the antidecuplet baryons originally proposed by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997), and builds on the experimental evidence for Theta+ from LEPS and DIANA and for a narrow N(1685) resonance from GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, and LNS-Sendai. The paper provides specific mass and width predictions for the strange antidecuplet members that can guide experimental searches, in contrast to earlier work that focused primarily on the nonstrange members.
