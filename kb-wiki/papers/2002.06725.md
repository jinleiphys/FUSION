# Jet quenching parameters in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas in the presence of magnetic fields

> I use the holographic gauge/gravity duality to systematically calculate the jet quenching parameters in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas in the presence of external magnetic fields. The magnetic field breaks down spatial rotation symmetry from $SO(3)$ to $SO(2)$, leading to the presence of multiple anisotropic jet quenching parameters, which are evaluated here in two quite different holographic settings. One of them corresponds to a top-down deformation of the strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ Super Yang-Mills plasma triggered by an external magnetic field, while the other one is a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model of phenomenological relevance for high energy peripheral heavy ion collisions, since it is able to provide a quantitative description of $(2+1)$-flavors lattice QCD thermodynamics with physical quark masses at zero and nonzero magnetic fields. I find for both models an overall enhancement of all the anisotropic jet quenching parameters with increasing magnetic fields. Moreover, I also conclude that for both models transverse momentum broadening is larger in transverse directions than in the direction of the magnetic field. Since these conclusions are shown to hold for two rather different holographic setups at finite temperature and magnetic fields, they are suggested as fairly robust features of strongly coupled anisotropic magnetized plasmas.

# Jet quenching parameters in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas in the presence of magnetic fields

## Key claim

The paper claims that in two different holographic models of strongly coupled plasmas with external magnetic fields, all anisotropic jet quenching parameters are enhanced with increasing magnetic field, and transverse momentum broadening is larger in directions transverse to the magnetic field than along it. The author suggests these conclusions are fairly robust features of strongly coupled anisotropic magnetized plasmas because they hold for both a top-down magnetic brane model and a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model, but the results are model-dependent and the EMD model is phenomenological rather than derived from first principles QCD.

## Method

The author uses the holographic gauge/gravity duality to compute jet quenching parameters via light-like adjoint Wilson loops, following the nonperturbative definition of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006). The calculation is performed for two distinct holographic settings: a top-down magnetic brane model (deformation of N=4 Super Yang-Mills by an external magnetic field) and a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model whose parameters are fixed by matching (2+1)-flavors lattice QCD thermodynamics with physical quark masses at zero and nonzero magnetic fields. For each model, the author numerically solves the background Einstein equations and evaluates three anisotropic jet quenching parameters corresponding to different orientations of the light parton's motion and transverse momentum broadening relative to the magnetic field direction.

## Key numbers

- For the magnetic brane model in the conformal limit (zero magnetic field), the isotropic jet quenching parameter is given by $\hat{q}_{(\text{CFT})}/\sqrt{\lambda_t}T^3 = \pi^{3/2}\Gamma(3/4)/\Gamma(5/4)$, where $\lambda_t$ is the 't Hooft coupling and $T$ is the temperature.
- The magnetic brane model is valid for the dimensionless combination $\mathcal{B}/T^2 \in [0,\infty)$, where $\mathcal{B}$ is the physical boundary magnetic field, with the bulk magnetic field parameter $b$ restricted to $b \in [0,\sqrt{3})$.
- For the magnetic EMD model, the scaling factor $\Lambda = 1058.83$ MeV is used to convert dimensionless observables to physical units, and the dilaton potential parameters include $V(\phi) = -12\cosh(0.63\phi) + 0.65\phi^2 - 0.05\phi^4 + 0.003\phi^6$.
- The scaling dimension of the dual operator in the EMD model is $\Delta \approx 2.73$, obtained from the dilaton potential.
- The magnetic field estimates for peripheral heavy ion collisions range from $eB \sim m_\pi^2 \sim 0.02$ GeV$^2$ at RHIC to $eB \sim 15 m_\pi^2 \sim 0.3$ GeV$^2$ at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of jet quenching parameters in anisotropic holographic plasmas, particularly the analytical approximation of Li et al. (2016) for the magnetic brane model in the strong magnetic field limit ($\mathcal{B} \gg T^2$), by providing full numerical results valid for any value of $\mathcal{B}/T^2$. The paper also applies the phenomenological EMD model originally developed by Finazzo et al. (2016) and Rougemont et al. (2015, 2016) to the calculation of jet quenching parameters, building on that model's successful predictions for lattice QCD thermodynamics at finite temperature and magnetic field. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and could be used as inputs for phenomenological codes describing the spacetime evolution of the quark-gluon plasma.
