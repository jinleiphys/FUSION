# Off-shell Behavior of the $π\!-\!η$ Mixing Amplitude

> We extend a recent calculation of the momentum dependence of the $ρ-ω$ mixing amplitude to the pseudoscalar sector. The $π\!-\!η$ mixing amplitude is calculated in a hadronic model where the mixing is driven by the neutron-proton mass difference. Closed-form analytic expressions are presented in terms of a few nucleon-meson parameters. The observed momentum dependence of the mixing amplitude is strong enough as to question earlier calculations of charge-symmetry-breaking observables based on the on-shell assumption. The momentum dependence of the $π\!-\!η$ amplitude is, however, practically identical to the one recently predicted for $ρ-ω$ mixing. Hence, in this model, the ratio of pseudoscalar to vector mixing amplitudes is, to a good approximation, a constant solely determined from nucleon-meson coupling constants. Furthermore, by selecting these parameters in accordance with charge-symmetry-conserving data and SU(3)-flavor symmetry, we reproduce the momentum dependence of the $π\!-\!η$ mixing amplitude predicted from chiral perturbation theory. Alternatively, one can use chiral-perturbation-theory results to set stringent limits on the value of the $NNη$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous hadronic calculation of the momentum dependence of the rho-omega mixing amplitude to the pseudoscalar sector, finding that the pi-eta mixing amplitude shows a strong momentum dependence that questions earlier charge-symmetry-breaking calculations based on the on-shell assumption, yet the ratio of pseudoscalar to vector mixing amplitudes remains essentially constant.

## Method

The pi-eta mixing amplitude was calculated in a hadronic model where the mixing is generated by nucleon-antinucleon (N anti-N) loops and driven by the neutron-proton mass difference. Closed-form analytic expressions were derived for the mixing amplitude in terms of a few nucleon-meson parameters, using pseudoscalar coupling for both the NNpi and NNeta interactions. The calculation was performed to one-loop order, with divergences removed by counterterm subtractions and the amplitude renormalized to reproduce the experimental on-shell value at the eta-meson point.

## Key numbers

* The on-shell pi-eta mixing amplitude at q^2 = m_eta^2 is set to -4200 MeV^2 as the renormalization condition.
* The slope parameter a_1 from chiral perturbation theory is constrained to the range 280 MeV^2 <= a_1 <= 360 MeV^2.
* Using chiral perturbation theory constraints, the NNeta coupling constant is limited to the range 0.32 <= g_eta^2/4pi <= 0.53.
* The NNeta coupling constant is conventionally assumed to vary in the range g_eta^2/4pi ~ (0.50-1.00) from SU(3)-flavor symmetry.
* The NNpi coupling constant is g_pi^2/4pi = 14.1, with a cutoff Lambda_pi = 1300 MeV.
* The NNeta coupling constant used in the main calculation is g_eta^2/4pi = 0.90, with a cutoff Lambda_eta = 1500 MeV.
* The ratio of pseudoscalar to vector mixing amplitudes shows variations of less than 10% over the entire momentum range shown, even with form factor corrections.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model calculation of rho-omega mixing by Piekarewicz and Williams (1993) to the pseudoscalar sector, and addresses the same questions raised by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1991) about the on-shell assumption for mixing amplitudes. The results are compared to chiral perturbation theory calculations by Maltman (1992), and the agreement gives confidence in extending the model beyond the region of applicability of chiral perturbation theory. The findings suggest that previous calculations of charge-symmetry-breaking observables based on the on-shell assumption for pi-eta mixing should be re-examined, though the off-shell corrections to the NN potential are less dramatic than those observed for rho-omega mixing.
