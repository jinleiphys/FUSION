# Masses of ground and excited-state hadrons

> We present the first Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation of the light hadron spectrum that simultaneously correlates the masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states within a single framework. At the core of our analysis is a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction. In comparison with relevant quantities the root-mean-square-relative-error/degree-of freedom is 13%. Notable amongst our results is agreement between the computed baryon masses and the bare masses employed in modern dynamical coupled-channels models of pion-nucleon reactions. Our analysis provides insight into numerous aspects of baryon structure; e.g., relationships between the nucleon and Delta masses and those of the dressed-quark and diquark correlations they contain.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation that simultaneously correlates meson and baryon ground- and excited-state masses within a single framework, using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction; the authors note that the root-mean-square-relative-error per degree of freedom is 13% when compared with relevant quantities, and that the computed baryon masses agree with bare masses used in modern dynamical coupled-channels models of pion-nucleon reactions.

## Method

The authors employ a vector-vector contact interaction (momentum-independent gluon propagator) regularized with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs to implement confinement and preserve chiral symmetry. Meson and diquark masses are obtained from homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations in rainbow-ladder truncation, with spin-orbit repulsion introduced phenomenologically in scalar and axial-vector channels via a coupling factor \(g_{\rm SO}^2 = 0.240\) to match the experimental \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) mass splitting. Baryon masses are computed from a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation using a static approximation for the exchanged quark, with diquark correlations generated by the same contact interaction.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square-relative-error per degree of freedom: 13% in comparison with relevant quantities.
* Parameters fixed in the chiral limit: \(M^0 = 0.40\) GeV, \(m_\rho^0 = 0.78\) GeV, \(f_\pi^0 = 0.088\) GeV, \(f_\rho^0 = 0.15\) GeV, \(\kappa_\pi^0 = (0.22\ \text{GeV})^3\).
* Dressed-quark mass from current-quark mass \(m = 7\) MeV: \(M = 0.37\) GeV.
* Computed meson masses (RL truncation with spin-orbit correction): \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV, \(m_\rho = 0.93\) GeV, \(m_\sigma = 1.29\) GeV, \(m_{a_1} = 1.38\) GeV; radially excited states: \(m_{\pi^\ast} = 1.38 \pm 0.06\) GeV, \(m_{\rho^\ast} = 1.29 \pm 0.07\) GeV, \(m_{\sigma^\ast} = 1.47 \pm 0.04\) GeV, \(m_{a_1^\ast} = 1.47 \pm 0.03\) GeV.
* Computed diquark masses (RL with spin-orbit correction): scalar \(m_{qq_{0^+}} = 0.78\) GeV, axial-vector \(m_{qq_{1^+}} = 1.06\) GeV, pseudoscalar \(m_{qq_{0^-}} = 1.37\) GeV, vector \(m_{qq_{1^-}} = 1.45\) GeV; radially excited states: \(m_{qq_{0^+}^\ast} = 1.39 \pm 0.06\) GeV, \(m_{qq_{1^+}^\ast} = 1.32 \pm 0.05\) GeV, \(m_{qq_{0^-}^\ast} = 1.50 \pm 0.03\) GeV, \(m_{qq_{1^-}^\ast} = 1.52 \pm 0.02\) GeV.
* Spin-orbit coupling factor: \(g_{\rm SO} = 0.240\).
* Regularization scales: \(\Lambda_{\rm ir} = 0.24\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{\rm uv} = 0.905\) GeV, gluon mass-scale \(m_G = 0.132\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of mesons (Roberts 2000, Maris 2003) to a unified treatment of meson and baryon spectra, including ground and excited states, using a single contact interaction. It addresses the longstanding puzzle of parity-partner mass splittings (e.g., \(\sigma\) and \(a_1\) mesons) by incorporating spin-orbit repulsion, following the reformulation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation by Chang (2009). The computed baryon masses agree with bare masses used in dynamical coupled-channels models of pion-nucleon reactions, and the results enable future DSE studies of baryon structure and excited states with more sophisticated interactions.
