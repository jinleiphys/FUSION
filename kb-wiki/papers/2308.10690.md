# Diffractive Processes at Next-to-Leading Order in the Dipole Picture

> In this thesis, we calculate diffractive processes at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the high-energy limit, with an emphasis on exclusive vector meson production and inclusive diffraction in deep inelastic scattering (DIS). Calculations in the high-energy limit can be done using the dipole picture, the basics of which are briefly reviewed. This includes using the color-glass condensate effective field theory to describe the nonperturbative dipole-target scattering amplitude which appears in practically all calculations in the dipole picture. The universality of the dipole-target scattering amplitude at NLO is shown numerically, in the sense that the same dipole-target scattering amplitude can be used to describe the data in both massless and massive quark production in inclusive DIS, and also in diffractive processes where exclusive vector meson production is considered. The analytical NLO calculations of exclusive vector meson production and inclusive diffraction in DIS are also explained. Exclusive vector meson production is calculated in the nonrelativistic limit for heavy mesons and the limit of large photon virtuality for light mesons. Also, the importance of including relativistic corrections to the heavy vector meson wave function in exclusive vector meson production is considered. For inclusive diffraction in DIS, we focus on the NLO corrections to the final state and show how the divergences cancel.

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates numerically that the same nonperturbative dipole-target scattering amplitude, when evolved to next-to-leading order (NLO), can simultaneously describe data for inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) with both massless and massive quarks, as well as diffractive exclusive vector meson production, but this universality is shown only within the specific approximations used (nonrelativistic limit for heavy mesons and large photon virtuality for light mesons).

## Method

The author performed analytical NLO calculations of exclusive vector meson production and inclusive diffraction in DIS using the dipole picture, which factorizes the process into a photon fluctuating into a quark-antiquark dipole, the dipole's interaction with the target described by the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory, and the formation of the final state. For exclusive vector meson production, the calculations were done in the nonrelativistic limit for heavy mesons and the limit of large photon virtuality for light mesons, including relativistic corrections to the heavy vector meson wave function. For inclusive diffraction in DIS, the author focused on NLO corrections to the final state and demonstrated the cancellation of divergences.

## Key numbers

- The thesis reports that at HERA, about 10% of Deep Inelastic Scattering events exhibit a large rapidity gap, and this ratio remains roughly constant as a function of energy.
- The high-energy condition is expressed as Bjorken x = Q^2 / (W^2 + Q^2), where the high-energy limit W^2 >> Q^2 corresponds to small Bjorken x.
- The lifetime of the virtual photon fluctuation is given by x^+_gamma ~ 1/|q^-| = 2q^+/Q^2, while the interaction time with the target is x^+_interaction ~ 1/P^-_n, with their ratio scaling as Q^2/W^2.

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture calculations from leading order to next-to-leading order for diffractive processes, building on the foundational work of Balitsky (1995) and Kovchegov (1999) on the BK equation, and the JIMWLK evolution equations developed by Jalilian-Marian, Iancu, McLerran, Weigert, Leonidov, and Kovner (1996-2001). The calculations are motivated by the need to find unambiguous signals of gluon saturation, which has been hinted at in data from RHIC (Braidot 2011, PHENIX 2011, STAR 2021) and the LHC (ALICE 2012, CMS 2016, LHCb 2021), and will be further probed at the future Electron-Ion Collider (Abdul Khalek 2021). The universality of the NLO dipole amplitude shown numerically enables consistent phenomenological studies across inclusive and diffractive channels.
