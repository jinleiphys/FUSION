# Three-particle quantization condition in a finite volume: 1. The role of the three-particle force

> Using non-relativistic effective Lagrangians in the particle-dimer picture, we rederive the expression for the energy shift of a loosely bound three-particle bound state of identical bosons in the unitary limit. The effective field theory formalism allows us to investigate the role of the three-particle force, which has not been taken into account in the earlier treatment of the problem. Moreover, we are able to relax the requirement of the unitary limit of infinite scattering length and demonstrate a smooth transition from the weakly bound three-particle state to a two-particle bound state of a particle and a deeply bound dimer.

## Key claim

Using non-relativistic effective Lagrangians in the particle-dimer picture, the authors rederive the leading-order finite-volume energy shift of a shallow three-particle bound state of identical bosons, showing that the three-body force does not affect the analytic form of the leading-order volume dependence but does multiply the overall coefficient by the square of the asymptotic normalization coefficient, which equals one only when derivative three-body forces are absent.

## Method

The authors formulate the three-body problem using a non-relativistic effective field theory with a dummy dimer field, which is mathematically equivalent to the original three-particle Lagrangian. They solve the homogeneous Faddeev equation in infinite volume, obtaining the Minlos-Faddeev wave function in the unitary limit, and then compute the first-order finite-volume energy shift by replacing the continuous momentum integration with a discrete sum and including the finite-volume correction to the dimer propagator via Poisson summation.

## Key numbers

* The numerical constant \(s_0 \simeq 1.00624\) solves the transcendental equation \(s_0 \cosh(\pi s_0/2) = (8/\sqrt{3}) \sinh(\pi s_0/6)\) and characterizes the Minlos-Faddeev wave function.
* The ratio of successive three-body bound-state momenta in the unitary limit is \(\kappa_{n+1}/\kappa_n = \exp(-\pi/s_0) \simeq 1/22.69\).
* The leading finite-volume energy shift scales as \(\Delta E_1 / |E| = c (\kappa L)^{-3/2} \exp(-2\kappa L/\sqrt{3})\) with \(c = -2\pi^{1/2} 3^{5/4} I_0^{-1} \sinh^2(\pi s_0/2)\), where \(I_0 = (1/\sqrt{3})(1 - \pi s_0 / \sinh \pi s_0) + (8\pi/9 \sinh \pi s_0)(\sinh(2\pi s_0/3) - 2 \sinh(\pi s_0/3))\).
* The subleading correction \(\Delta E_2\) behaves as \((\kappa L)^{-5/2} \exp(-2\kappa L/\sqrt{3})\) for large \(L\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier finite-volume treatment of three-particle bound states by Meissner, Rios, and Rusetsky (2014) and by Hansen and Sharpe (2016), explicitly incorporating the three-body force that was omitted in those derivations. The particle-dimer formalism provides a simpler framework than the general three-particle quantization condition developed by Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe (2012–2017), and it enables a smooth transition from the weakly bound three-particle state to a particle-dimer bound state described by the Luscher formula, as well as generalization beyond the unitary limit to finite scattering lengths.
