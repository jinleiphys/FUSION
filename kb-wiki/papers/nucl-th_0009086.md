# Unfactorized versus factorized calculations for ^2H(e,e'p) reactions at GeV energies

> In the literature, one often finds calculations of (e,e'p) reactions at GeV energies using the factorization approach. Factorization implies that the differential cross section can be written as the product of an off-shell electron-proton cross section and a distorted missing momentum distribution. While this factorization appears in the non-relativistic plane wave impulse approximation, it is broken in a more realistic approach. The main source of factorization breaking are final state interactions. In this paper, sources of factorization breaking are identified and their numerical relevance is examined in the reaction ^2H(e,e'p) for various kinematic settings in the GeV regime. The results imply that factorization should not be used for precision calculations, especially as unfactorized calculations are available.

## Key claim

Factorization of the (e,e'p) cross section into a product of an off-shell electron-proton cross section and a distorted missing momentum distribution is broken primarily by final state interactions, and the approximation fails for precision calculations at GeV energies, especially for missing momenta above 300 MeV/c or in non-parallel kinematics.

## Method

The author performed unfactorized and factorized calculations of the ^2H(e,e'p) reaction using the Argonne V18 deuteron wave function, with final state interactions treated via Glauber theory. The relativistic on-shell positive energy (OSPE) current operator was employed, and the ratio of factorized to unfactorized cross sections was computed for various kinematic settings including fixed outgoing proton kinetic energy (1 GeV) and fixed three-momentum transfer (q = 2.0 GeV/c) with different energy transfers. A strictly non-relativistic current operator was also used for comparison to isolate the sources of factorization breaking.

## Key numbers

- For fixed kinetic energy of 1 GeV of the outgoing proton in parallel kinematics with the relativistic current, deviations from factorization are about 5% at higher missing momenta.
- For θ_m = 10° (near-parallel), deviations rise to 6% at missing momenta around 400 MeV/c and to 11% at missing momenta around 600 MeV/c.
- For θ_m = 15°, deviations rise to 8% at missing momenta around 400 MeV/c and to 14% at missing momenta around 600 MeV/c.
- In perpendicular kinematics with the relativistic current, deviations from factorization become large for missing momenta p_m > 300 MeV/c and are non-negligible for p_m from 100 MeV/c to 300 MeV/c.
- For fixed three-momentum transfer q = 2.0 GeV/c and fixed energy transfers ω = 1.11, 1.27, and 1.48 GeV, factorization breaking is present for missing momenta from 200 to 300 MeV/c and very large beyond that.
- In the non-relativistic case with perpendicular kinematics, deviations from 1 range from 5% to 15% for p_m > 300 MeV/c.
- For fixed q = 2.0 GeV/c in the non-relativistic case, deviations range roughly from 6% to 9% for p_m > 400 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of factorization breaking in (e,e'p) reactions, which is well known at lower energies (see e.g. Raskin and Donnelly, Boffi et al., Arenhoevel, and Jan and Lowen), to the GeV regime where many calculations (e.g. by Frankfurt and Strikman, Kolya, Ciofi degli Atti, and the author's own earlier work) still assume factorization. The paper demonstrates that unfactorized calculations are available and necessary for precision work, and that factorization introduces uncertainties comparable to the predicted color transparency effects at Jefferson Lab, which could mislead data interpretation.
