# Constraints on symmetry energy and nucleon effective mass splitting with Heavy Ion Collisions

> A new version of the improved quantum molecular dynamics model has been developed to include standard Skyrme interactions. Four commonly used Skyrme parameter sets, SLy4, SkI2, SkM* and Gs are adopted in the transport model code to calculate the isospin diffusion observables as well as single and double ratios of transverse emitted nucleons. While isospin diffusion observables are sensitive to the symmetry energy term, they are not very sensitive to the nucleon effective mass splitting parameters in the interactions. Our calculations show that the high energy neutrons and protons and their ratios from reactions at different incident energies provide a robust observable to study the momentum dependence of the nucleon effective mass splitting. However the sensitivity of effective mass splitting effect on the n/p yield ratios decreases with increasing beam energy, even though high energy proton and neutron are produced more abundantly at high beam energy. Our calculations show that the optimum incident energy to study nucleon effective masses is between 100-200 MeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

Isospin diffusion observables are sensitive to the symmetry energy term but not very sensitive to the nucleon effective mass splitting parameters, while high energy neutron and proton yield ratios from reactions at different incident energies provide a robust observable to study the momentum dependence of the nucleon effective mass splitting, with the optimum incident energy between 100-200 MeV per nucleon.

## Method

The authors developed a new version of the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD-Sky) that incorporates standard Skyrme interactions without the spin-orbit term. They used four Skyrme parameter sets (SLy4, SkI2, SkM*, and Gs) to simulate collisions of 124Sn and 112Sn isotopes at various beam energies, calculating isospin diffusion transport ratios and single and double ratios of transverse emitted neutrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- The four Skyrme parameter sets have similar incompressibility K0 = 230 ± 20 MeV, symmetry energy coefficient S0 = 32 ± 2 MeV, and isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.7 ± 0.1.
- SLy4 has L = 46 MeV with m_n* < m_p* (m_n*/m = 0.68, m_p*/m = 0.71); SkI2 has L = 104 MeV with m_n* < m_p* (m_n*/m = 0.66, m_p*/m = 0.71).
- SkM* has L = 46 MeV with m_n* > m_p* (m_n*/m = 0.82, m_p*/m = 0.76); Gs has L = 93 MeV with m_n* > m_p* (m_n*/m = 0.81, m_p*/m = 0.76).
- A crossing of Lane potentials for SLy4 (SkI2) and SkM* (Gs) appears at approximately 30 MeV at ρ = 0.5ρ0 and approximately 70 MeV at ρ = ρ0.
- The optimum incident energy to study nucleon effective masses is between 100-200 MeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on constraining the symmetry energy from heavy ion collisions (Tsang 2004, Tsang 2009, Zhang 2012) by incorporating real Skyrme interactions into a transport model. It builds on earlier findings by Rizzo (2005) that neutron/proton yield ratios are sensitive to effective mass splitting, and by Li (2004, 2008) who used optical potential data to constrain the splitting. The new code enables simultaneous study of both symmetry energy and effective mass splitting, and the results suggest that future neutron and proton spectral data with smaller uncertainties at beam energies around 100-200 MeV per nucleon could determine the magnitude and momentum dependence of the nucleon mass splitting.
