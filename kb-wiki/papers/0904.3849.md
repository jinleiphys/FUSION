# The equation of state and composition of hot, dense matter in core-collapse supernovae

> The equation of state and composition of matter are calculated for conditions typical for pre-collapse and early collapse stages in core collapse supernovae. The composition is evaluated under the assumption of nuclear statistical equilibrium, when the matter is considered as an `almost' ideal gas with corrections due to thermal excitations of nuclei, to free nucleon degeneracy, and to Coulomb and surface energy corrections. The account of these corrections allows us to obtain the composition for densities a bit below the nuclear matter density. Through comparisons with the equation of state (EOS) developed by Shen et al. we examine the approximation of one representative nucleus used in most of recent supernova EOS's. We find that widely distributed compositions in the nuclear chart are different, depending on the mass formula, while the thermodynamical quantities are quite close to those in the Shen's EOS.

## Key claim

The authors find that while the thermodynamical quantities (pressure, energy density) from their multi-nuclear-composition Saha approach are quite close to those from the single-nucleus approximation of Shen et al. (1998), the detailed nuclear composition (the distribution of abundances across the nuclear chart) differs significantly and depends on the choice of nuclear mass formula.

## Method

The composition of matter in nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) was calculated using a Saha-like approach that treats the matter as an almost ideal gas, adding corrections for thermal excitations of nuclei, free nucleon degeneracy, Coulomb energy, surface energy, and nuclear bulk interactions. The code uses a large nuclear network covering approximately 20,000 nuclides for the KTUY mass formula (Koura et al. 2005) and approximately 9,000 nuclides for the FRDM mass formula (Moller et al. 1995), and solves the NSE equations for fixed temperature, density, and electron fraction without imposing beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers

* The KTUY mass formula (Koura et al. 2005) has a root-mean-square deviation from experimental masses of 666.7 keV.
* The calculations cover densities from 10^10 g/cm^3 to 10^13 g/cm^3, temperatures down to approximately 2 MeV, and electron fractions down to approximately 0.3.
* The code is applicable up to densities a bit below nuclear matter density, but is limited at low temperature (approximately 2 MeV) and high density (approximately 10^13 g/cm^3) due to nuclear interaction treatment and iteration convergence.

## Context

This work extends the Saha-based NSE approach of Mazurek et al. (1979) by including a more complete treatment of Coulomb and surface energy corrections and by using updated, larger nuclear mass tables (KTUY and FRDM). It enables more accurate predictions of the detailed nuclear composition (including free proton fractions) for use in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for calculating electron capture rates on a wide range of nuclei, which is necessary for determining the electron fraction at neutrino trapping. The results are compared directly with the single-nucleus equation of state of Shen et al. (1998), which is widely used in supernova simulations.
