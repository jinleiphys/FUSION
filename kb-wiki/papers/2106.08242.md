# Imprints of high-momentum nucleons in nuclei on hard photons from heavy-ion collisions around the Fermi Energy

> The short-range correlation (SRC) induced by the tensor force in the isosinglet neutron-proton interaction channel leads to a high-momentum tail (HMT) in the single-nucleon momentum distributions n(k) in nuclei. Owing to the remaining uncertainties about the tensor force, the shape of the nucleon HMT may be significantly different from the dilute interacting Fermi gas model prediction $n(k) \sim1/k^4$ similar to the HMT in cold atoms near the unitary limit. Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model incorporating approximately the nucleon HMT, we investigate hard photon emissions in $^{14}$N+$^{12}$C and $^{48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn reactions at beam energies around the Fermi energy. Imprints of different shapes of the HMT on the energy spectrum, angular distribution and transverse momentum spectrum of hard photons are studied. While the angular distribution does not carry any information about the shape of the nucleon HMT, the energy spectra and especially the mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of hard photons are found to bare strong imprints of the shapes of nucleon HMTs in the two colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The shape of the high-momentum tail (HMT) in single-nucleon momentum distributions may differ significantly from the dilute interacting Fermi gas model prediction of n(k) ~ 1/k^4, and while the angular distribution of hard photons carries no information about the HMT shape, the energy spectra and especially the mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of hard photons show strong imprints of different HMT shapes in the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU04) transport model incorporating approximate nucleon HMTs in the initial momentum distributions of colliding nuclei. Hard photon production was calculated perturbatively by folding the time-dependent phase-space distribution functions of neutrons and protons from the transport model with the elementary cross section for the p+n → p+n+γ process from a neutral scalar σ meson exchange model. The HMT was modeled with three different shapes (n(k) ~ 1/k^4, 1/k^6, and 1/k^9) using a 20% fraction of nucleons in the HMT based on experimental findings, with the n-p dominance model requiring equal numbers of neutrons and protons in the HMT.

## Key numbers

- 20% of total nucleons are distributed in the HMT according to experimental findings from Sube08 and OHen14
- The high-momentum cutoff λ ≈ 2 times the Fermi momentum k_F
- For ¹⁴N+¹²C reactions at beam energies of 20, 30, and 40 MeV/nucleon, calculations without HMT significantly underpredict hard photon yields compared to data from Stevenson86, while calculations with HMT (1/k^4) better describe the data
- In head-on ⁴⁸Ca+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 45 MeV/nucleon, the single differential probability dN/dΩ_γ at θ=90° is larger by a factor of about 1.5 compared to mid-central collisions with impact parameter b=5 fm
- At beam energy 60 MeV/nucleon, the single differential probability increases by a factor of about 1.2 compared to 45 MeV/nucleon, and 1.6 compared to 30 MeV/nucleon at θ=90°
- Hard photon transverse momentum spectra show typical exponential shapes for p_t ≥ 50 MeV/c with all three HMT shapes considered

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Yong (2017, 2018) that incorporated HMT effects on hard photon production but only used the n(k) ~ 1/k^4 shape. It is motivated by ongoing electron/nucleon-nucleus scattering experiments probing short-range correlations (SRC) and the HMT, as reviewed by Hen (2016). The study provides theoretical input for future heavy-ion experiments aiming to probe the shape of the nucleon HMT, which has implications for understanding the tensor force in the isosinglet neutron-proton interaction channel and the nature of strong interactions at short distances. The authors note that earlier analyses of y-scaling and superscaling in inclusive electron-nucleus scatterings by Antonov (2007) found n(k) scaling as 1/k^(4+m) with m ≈ 4-4.5 for momenta up to (1.59-1.97)k_F with k_F=250 MeV/c.
