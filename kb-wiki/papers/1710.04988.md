# Fermi-momentum dependence of relativistic effective mass below saturation from superscaling of quasielastic electron scattering

> The relativistic effective mass $M^*$, and Fermi momentum, $k_F$, are important ingredients in the determination of the nuclear equation of state, but they have rarely been extracted from experimental data below saturation density where translationally invariant nuclear matter becomes unstable against clusterization into the existing atomic nuclei. Using a novel kind of superscaling analysis of the quasielastic cross section electron scattering data involving a suitable selection criterion and $^{12}$C as a reference nucleus, the global scaling properties of the resulting set of data for 21 nuclei ranging from $^2$H to $^{238}$U are then analyzed. We find that a subset of a third of the about $20000$ data approximately scales to an universal superscaling function with a more constrained uncertainty band than just the reference $^{12}$C case and provides $M^*$ as a function of $k_F$.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the relativistic effective mass \(M^*\) and Fermi momentum \(k_F\) from quasielastic electron scattering data for 21 nuclei, finding that a subset of about one third of the roughly 20000 data points approximately scales to a universal superscaling function, but this universality holds only within an uncertainty band and the extraction depends on a multi-step selection procedure that discards kinematics where the quasielastic peak is not clearly visible or where momentum transfer is below 100 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors apply the SuSAM* (Super Scaling Analysis with Relativistic Effective Mass) approach, which factorizes the quasielastic cross section into an elementary electron-nucleon cross section (using nucleons with effective mass \(m_N^*\)) and an effective number of nucleons expressed in terms of a scaling function \(f^*(\psi^*)\). Starting from a scaling band previously fitted for \(^{12}\)C (with \(M^*=0.8\) and \(k_F=225\) MeV/c), they visually tune \(M^*\) and \(k_F\) for each nucleus so that the quasielastic peak maximum occurs at \(\psi^*=0\) and the width matches the \(^{12}\)C band, then perform a \(\chi^2\) fit to the parameterized band (a sum of two Gaussians modified by a Fermi function) to obtain final values with statistical errors.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{12}\)C, the \(\chi^2\) fit yields \(k_F = 217\) MeV/c and \(M^* = 0.80\).
* For \(^{208}\)Pb, the \(\chi^2\) fit yields \(k_F = 233\) MeV/c and \(M^* = 0.56\).
* For \(^{238}\)U, the \(\chi^2\) fit yields \(k_F = 219\) MeV/c and \(M^* = 0.51\).
* For \(^{4}\)He, the \(\chi^2\) fit yields \(k_F = 180\) MeV/c and \(M^* = 0.86\).
* For \(^{2}\)H, the \(\chi^2\) fit yields \(k_F = 115\) MeV/c and \(M^* = 0.99\).
* The scaling function band parameters include central values: \(a_1 = -0.1335\), \(a_2 = 0.4319\), \(a_3 = 1.3885\), \(b_1 = 0.5741\), \(b_2 = 0.6539\), \(b_3 = 0.6083\), \(c_1 = 0.3405\), \(c_2 = 2.2947\).
* The selection criterion kept data points with more than 160 neighbors within a circle of radius \(r=0.1\) in the scaling plot.

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* analysis previously applied only to \(^{12}\)C by Amaro et al. (2015, 2017) to 21 nuclei from \(^2\)H to \(^{238}\)U, providing the first systematic extraction of \(M^*\) as a function of \(k_F\) below saturation density from experimental data. The results are compared to the \(\sigma-\omega\) model of Serot and Walecka (1986) for nuclear matter, showing a similar trend, and enable predictions for neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments.
