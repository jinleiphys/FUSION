# Renormalizing Two-Neutron Halo Nuclei Without Neutron-Core Interaction

> We consider the Effective Field Theory (EFT) proposed by Hongo and Son to describe two-neutron halo nuclei where the neutron-core interaction is subleading. In this EFT, the ratio of the mean-square matter radius and charge radius is universal in so far that it only depends on the two-neutron separation energy of the nucleus and the neutron-neutron scattering length. By investigating the divergence structure of this theory, we find that one further renormalization condition is required to predict both radii separately. Our renormalization scheme uses one of the mean square radii or the scattering amplitude as input. We use Hongo and Son's theory to calculate the matter radii of the two-neutron halo nuclei $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, $^{17}$B, $^{19}$B, and $^{22}$C and compare to the values obtained with standard Halo EFT. In this comparison we use both the physical value of the neutron-core scattering length and rescaled values. We observe good convergence against Hongo and Son's results for the case of a negligible neutron-core interaction. Similar agreement for the radii is also found in the case of the halo nucleus $^6$He, where the $nc$ interaction is in the p-wave. Our renormalization scheme makes the restriction in the ultraviolet cutoff range from the Landau pole explicit. We calculate the position of the Landau pole for various halo nuclei. In all cases the Landau pole restricts the cutoff to rather low values. Finally, we derive an explicit expression for the three-to-three neutron-neutron-core scattering amplitude and discuss its cut structure.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Effective Field Theory (EFT) proposed by Hongo and Son for two-neutron halo nuclei, where the neutron-core interaction is subleading, requires one additional renormalization condition beyond the binding energy to predict charge and matter radii separately, despite the ratio of these radii being universal and independent of that condition.

## Method

The authors analyze the divergence structure of the self-energy integral for the trimer (halo) field in Hongo and Son's EFT, finding two distinct divergences: a quadratic divergence and a logarithmic divergence. They implement a renormalization scheme that uses either the experimental charge radius or matter radius as a second renormalization condition, allowing prediction of the other radius via the universal ratio. The authors then calculate matter radii for several two-neutron halo nuclei and compare with results from standard Halo EFT using Faddeev equations, and also derive an explicit expression for the three-to-three neutron-neutron-core scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of mean-square matter radius to mean-square charge radius is universal and depends only on the two-neutron separation energy \(B\), the neutron-neutron scattering length \(a\), and the core mass number \(A\), as given by \(\langle r_\mathrm{m}^2\rangle / \langle r_\mathrm{c}^2\rangle = K_\mathrm{m}/K_\mathrm{c}\).
- The self-energy integral \(I_\Lambda(E)\) diverges quadratically as \(\sim \Lambda^2\) and its derivative \(I_\Lambda'(B)\) diverges logarithmically as \(\sim \ln(\Lambda)\) in the ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\).
- The product \(Z_h g_0^2\) vanishes as \(\sim 1/\Lambda^2\) in the limit \(\Lambda \to \infty\) when only one renormalization condition (the binding energy) is imposed.
- The Landau pole cutoff \(\Lambda_\mathrm{max}\) is determined by solving \(g_0^{-2}(\Lambda)=0\) from the renormalization condition, and its value depends on the input radius and system parameters.
- Matter radii are calculated for the two-neutron halo nuclei \(^{11}\mathrm{Li}\), \(^{14}\mathrm{Be}\), \(^{17}\mathrm{B}\), \(^{19}\mathrm{B}\), and \(^{22}\mathrm{C}\) and compared with standard Halo EFT results.
- Similar agreement for radii is found for the halo nucleus \(^6\mathrm{He}\), where the neutron-core interaction is in the p-wave.

## Context

This work extends the EFT developed by Hongo and Son (2022) for two-neutron halo nuclei where the neutron-core interaction is subleading, which previously could only predict the ratio of radii but not the individual radii. The authors show that full renormalization requires an additional input (one radius or the scattering amplitude), and they compare their results with standard Halo EFT calculations using Faddeev equations as described by Gobel (2019, 2022). The analysis makes explicit the restriction from the Landau pole on the ultraviolet cutoff, which limits the range of applicability of this EFT to rather low cutoff values. The derived three-body scattering amplitude enables future calculations of scattering observables within this framework.
