# Comments on high-energy total cross sections in QCD

> We discuss how hadronic total cross sections at high energy depend on the details of QCD, namely on the number of colours $N_c$ and the quark masses. We find that while a "Froissart"-type behaviour $σ_{\rm tot}\sim B\log^2s$ is rather general, relying only on the presence of higher-spin stable particles in the spectrum, the value of $B$ depends quite strongly on the quark masses. Moreover, we argue that $B$ is of order ${\cal O}(N_c^0)$ at large $N_c$, and we discuss a bound for $B$ which does not become singular in the $N_f=2$ chiral limit, unlike the Froissart-Łukaszuk-Martin bound.

## Key claim

The authors argue that while a Froissart-type behavior (σ_tot ∼ B log² s) for high-energy hadronic total cross sections is generic and depends only on the existence of higher-spin stable particles, the prefactor B depends strongly on the quark masses and is of order O(N_c⁰) at large N_c, contrary to naive large-N_c counting rules that would suggest O(1/N_c²).

## Method

The authors use a nonperturbative Euclidean-space approach to soft high-energy scattering, which relates scattering amplitudes to correlation functions of Wilson loops. They compute the prefactor B by maximizing the ratio B^(a) = ((j^(a)-1)/M^(a))² over all stable hadronic species a with spin j^(a) > 1. They analyze how the stability of hadrons and thus B changes when varying the number of colors N_c and quark masses, using the large-N_c baryon mass formula of Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994, 1995) fitted to physical octet and decuplet baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- For physical QCD (N_c=3), the dominant particle is the Ω baryon, yielding B_QCD ≈ 0.56 GeV⁻², compared to the experimental value B_exp ≈ 0.69–0.73 GeV⁻².
- The Froissart-Łukaszuk-Martin bound prefactor B_FLM = π/M_π² is about two orders of magnitude larger than 2B_QCD.
- In the quenched approximation, B is 2–3.5 times larger than the unquenched value, giving B_Q ≳ 1.09 GeV⁻².
- The mass parameter δm' = m₁ + 2m₃ – 2M_K evaluated at N_c=3 gives δm' = –67 ± 10_stat ± 12_Nc MeV, supporting Ω stability at large N_c.
- The mass parameters from fitting octet and decuplet baryons: m₀ ≈ 287.7 MeV, m₁ ≈ 429 MeV, m₂ ≈ 102 MeV, m₃ ≈ 198 MeV, m₄ ≈ –110 MeV (all with uncertainties of a few MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous result (Giordano and Meggiolaro, 2014) connecting total cross sections to the hadronic spectrum, and contrasts with the standard Froissart-Łukaszuk-Martin bound (Froissart, 1961; Łukaszuk and Martin, 1967) which becomes singular in the chiral limit. The paper enables a deeper understanding of how QCD parameters control high-energy scattering, and shows that the quenched approximation (pure SU(3) gauge theory) is not equivalent to full QCD for total cross sections even at large N_c, contrary to common expectations.
