# Hamiltonian flow equations for a Dirac particle in large scalar and vector potentials

> An efficient solution of the Dirac Hamiltonian flow equations has been proposed through a novel expandsion with the inverse of the Dirac effective mass. The efficiency and accuracy of this new expansion have been demonstrated by reducing a radial Dirac Hamiltonian with large scalar and vector potentials to two nonrelativistic Hamiltonians corresponding to particles and antiparticles, respectively. By solving the two nonrelativistic Hamiltonians, it is found that the exact solutions of the Dirac equation, for both particles and antiparticles, can be reproduced with a high accuracy up to only a few lowest order terms in the expansion. This could help compare and bridge the relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear energy density functional theories in the future.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new expansion of the Dirac Hamiltonian flow equations using the inverse of the Dirac effective mass (M̃ = M + S) rather than the bare mass M, which achieves much faster convergence for large scalar and vector potentials typical in nuclear covariant density functional theory; the exact solutions of the Dirac equation for both particles and antiparticles can be reproduced with high accuracy using only a few lowest-order terms in this expansion.

## Method

The authors solve the similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow equations for a radial Dirac Hamiltonian with spherical Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials, using a perturbative expansion in 1/M̃ (where M̃ = M + S is the Dirac effective mass) instead of the conventional 1/M expansion. They derive analytic expressions for the even (block-diagonal) operator up to 1/M̃³ order, yielding two nonrelativistic Hamiltonians H^F and H^D for particles and antiparticles respectively, and solve these in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The scalar and vector potentials used are Σ₀ = -66.0 MeV and Δ₀ = 650.0 MeV, with radius r₀ = 7.0 fm and diffuseness a₀ = 0.6 fm, fitted to single-neutron energies of ²⁰⁸Pb
- Nucleon mass M = 939 MeV
- Up to 1/M̃³ order, deviations of single-particle energies from exact solutions are less than 0.1 MeV for well-bound levels and about 0.2 MeV for weakly bound levels
- The M̃ expansion up to 1/M̃³ order reproduces the exact total energy (sum of lowest 126 levels) with a relative deviation of about 0.5%
- Spin-orbit splitting deviations from exact results are smaller than 0.2 MeV up to 1/M̃³ order
- Root mean square radii of the total density for the lowest 126 levels: 5.61 fm (exact), 5.59 fm (M̃ expansion), 5.58 fm (M expansion)
- For antiparticles, deviations are less than 0.1 MeV for deeply bound levels, growing to near 10 MeV for levels approaching -450 MeV
- Pseudospin-orbit splitting deviations are smaller than 0.6 MeV up to pseudo-orbital quantum number l̃ = 5

## Context

This work extends the SRG method for Dirac Hamiltonians originally developed by Bylev et al. (1998) and previously applied to nuclear pseudospin symmetries by Guo et al. (2012) and Li et al. (2013, 2015). The previous 1/M expansion showed insufficient accuracy for self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations, with third-order errors still too large. The new 1/M̃ expansion achieves much faster convergence and enables future implementation in self-consistent CDFT, potentially allowing direct comparison between relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear energy density functionals. A parallel reconstituted SRG method by Guo (2019) using resummation techniques is noted as complementary.
