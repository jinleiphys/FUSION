# Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in a covariant diquark model

> We present a simple covariant constituent diquark-quark model for the nucleon. The nucleon is assumed to be composed of a scalar diquark and a quark which interact via a quark exchange. Starting from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, the instantaneous approximation leads to a diquark-quark Salpeter equation. In the Mandelstam formalism, the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated for momentum transfers up to $q^2 = - 3 \; (\mbox{GeV/c})^2$. A remarkable description of the experimental data is obtained. Especially, the model gives nearly the right values for the proton and (negative) neutron charge radii, and a qualitative description of the magnetic form factors.

# Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in a covariant diquark model

## Key claim

The nucleon can be described as a bound state of a scalar diquark and a constituent quark interacting via quark exchange, and this simple two-body relativistic model reproduces the electric form factors of both proton and neutron up to momentum transfers of -3 (GeV/c)^2, though the magnetic form factors are only qualitatively correct and too small by a factor of about 2. The model achieves this description without a phenomenological confinement potential, using only a short-range quark exchange interaction, but requires the introduction of a diquark form factor with a size parameter to obtain a stable solution.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a bound state of a scalar diquark and a fermion (quark), apply the instantaneous approximation to derive a Salpeter-type equation, and cast it into a Schrödinger-like eigenvalue equation. The interaction kernel is a quark exchange mechanism where the diquark couples pointlike to two quarks via a pseudoscalar coupling, and a Gaussian diquark form factor with parameter lambda is introduced to regularize the interaction. The Salpeter equation is solved numerically by expanding the wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis and applying the Ritz variational principle, with the coupling constant adjusted to reproduce the nucleon mass of 939 MeV/c^2. Electromagnetic form factors are then computed using the Mandelstam formalism, summing separate contributions from the quark current and the pointlike scalar diquark current.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass fixed to 939 MeV/c^2 by adjusting the diquark-quark coupling constant g_dqq = 14.15
- Constituent quark mass: 350 MeV/c^2; diquark mass: 650 MeV/c^2
- Diquark size parameter lambda = 0.18 fm (1.8 fm in the text, likely a unit conversion issue; the text states 0.18 fm in the discussion and 1.8 fm in the table)
- Oscillator parameter alpha = 1.15 fm, obtained from the minimum of the mass curve
- Proton charge radius: sqrt(<r^2>_p) = 0.88 fm (experimental: 0.862 ± 0.012 fm)
- Neutron mean square charge radius: <r^2>_n = -0.28 fm^2 (experimental: -0.117 ± 0.002 fm^2)
- Ratio of magnetic moments: mu_n/mu_p = -0.5 (experimental: -0.685)
- Form factors calculated up to q^2 = -3 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the diquark-quark model approach of Meyer (who solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-diquark bound states without calculating dynamical observables) and complements the light-cone distribution amplitude approach of Kroll et al. (applicable only at higher momentum transfers). The model is related to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio calculations by Ishii, Huang, Reinhardt, and Hanhart, which treat two quarks as diquark states analogous to mesons. The results enable future extension to baryons with strangeness and to electromagnetic processes such as kaon photoproduction, and suggest that including axial-vector diquark components would be necessary to improve the magnetic form factors.
