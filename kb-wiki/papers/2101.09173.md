# Deconfining Phase Boundary of Rapidly Rotating Hot and Dense Matter and Analysis of Moment of Inertia

> We discuss the effect of rapid rotation on the phase diagram of hadronic matter. The energy dispersion relation is shifted by an effective chemical potential induced by rotation. This suggests that rotation should lower the critical temperature of chiral restoration, but it is still controversial how the deconfinement temperature should change as a function of angular velocity. We adopt the hadron resonance gas model as an approach free from fitting parameters. We identify the deconfinement from the thermodynamic behavior and find that rotation decreases the deconfinement temperature. We also discuss the spatial inhomogeneity of the pressure and give a semi-quantitative estimate of the moment of inertia.

## Key claim
The paper claims that increasing angular velocity lowers the deconfinement transition temperature, contrary to some lattice-QCD results that suggest it increases. This conclusion is drawn from a hadron resonance gas model analysis that is free from fitting parameters, but the authors note that the effect of the ultraviolet mass cutoff introduces a possible error of approximately 5 MeV.

## Method
The authors formulate the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in a rotating frame by shifting the energy dispersion relation by the cranking term \(-\mathbf{J}\cdot\boldsymbol{\omega}\). They impose an infrared cutoff on the radial momentum integration to enforce the causality bound \(R\omega \le 1\) and an ultraviolet mass cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) on the hadron spectrum. The deconfinement transition is identified using a thermodynamic criterion \(p/p_{\mathrm{SB}} = \gamma\) with \(\gamma = 0.18\) chosen to reproduce the lattice-QCD critical temperature \(T_c = 154\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at zero chemical potential and zero angular velocity.

## Key numbers
- The angular velocity of matter created in non-central heavy-ion collisions is \(\omega \simeq (9\pm1) \times 10^{21}\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}\), corresponding to \(\omega \sim 6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in natural units.
- The ultraviolet mass cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) shifts the critical temperature by \(3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from \(p/T^4\)), \(5.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from \(\varepsilon/T^4\)), and \(5.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from \(s/T^3\)), with an overall error of approximately \(5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The critical temperature at zero chemical potential and zero angular velocity is set to \(T_c = 154\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in accordance with lattice-QCD results from Bazavov (2014).
- For \(\omega = 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(T = 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the quadratic coefficient of the radial pressure dependence is \(\Delta p / r^2 \simeq 8.19141 \times 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{GeV}^6\), yielding an effective thermal degrees of freedom \(\nu \simeq 7\).

## Context
This work extends the Hagedorn picture of deconfinement (Hagedorn 1965; Cabibbo and Parisi 1975) to rotating systems using the hadron resonance gas model, which has been successful in reproducing particle abundances in heavy-ion collisions (Andronic 2017) and is consistent with lattice-QCD thermodynamics up to \(T_c\) (Andronic 2012; Vovchenko 2014). The result that rotation lowers the deconfinement temperature contradicts the lattice-QCD calculation of Braguta (2020) which found an increasing \(T_c\) with \(\omega\), but agrees with the holographic QCD approach of Chen (2020). The work enables future studies of the spatial distribution of angular momentum and the moment of inertia of hot and dense matter, as well as combined analyses of rotation and magnetic fields for phenomenological applications.
