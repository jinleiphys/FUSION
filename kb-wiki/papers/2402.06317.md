# The $ppp$ correlation function with a screened Coulomb potential

> The correlation function is a useful tool to study the interaction between hadrons. The theoretical description of this observable requires the knowledge of the scattering wave function, whose asymptotic part is distorted when two or more particles are charged. For a system of three (or more) particles, with more than two particles asymptotically free and at least two of them charged, the asymptotic part of the wave function is not known in a closed form. In the present study we introduce a screened Coulomb potential and analyze the impact of the screening radius on the correlation function. As we will show, when a sufficiently large screening radius is used, the correlation function results almost unchanged if compared to the case in which the unscreened Coulomb potential is used. This fact allows the use of free asymptotic matching conditions in the solution of the scattering equation simplifying noticeably the calculation of the correlation function. As an illustration we discuss the $pp$ and $ppp$ correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the $pp$ and $ppp$ correlation functions, a screened Coulomb potential with a sufficiently large screening radius (around 50 fm) produces results almost identical to the unscreened Coulomb case, provided the source function has finite size. This allows the use of free asymptotic matching conditions in the scattering equation, simplifying the calculation of correlation functions for systems where the asymptotic wave function is not known in closed form.

## Method

The authors compute the $pp$ correlation function using the Koonin-Pratt equation with a Gaussian source function of radius $R=1.249$ fm, solving the Schrodinger equation for a screened Coulomb potential $V_{sc}(r)=(e^2/r)\exp[-(r/r_{sc})^4]$ with varying screening radii $r_{sc}=10,20,50,100$ fm. For the $ppp$ case, they first use a hypercentral Coulomb approximation (averaged over hyperangles) and then a full hyperspherical adiabatic expansion limited to the first adiabatic channel, comparing screened and unscreened Coulomb potentials with a Gaussian short-range nuclear interaction.

## Key numbers

- Source radius for $pp$ correlation function: $R=1.249$ fm
- Screening radius $r_{sc} \ge 50$ fm is sufficient for the $pp$ correlation function to agree with the unscreened Coulomb case
- For the $ppp$ hypercentral Coulomb case, screening hyperradius $\rho_{sc}=50$ fm gives complete agreement for the $K=1$ term; $\rho_{sc}=10$ fm suffices for the $K=2$ term
- Source hyperradius for $ppp$: $\rho_0=2$ fm
- Gaussian nuclear potential parameters: $V_0=30.45$ MeV, $r_0=1.815$ fm
- Screening radius $r_{sc}=50$ fm is sufficient for the full $ppp$ correlation function with the first adiabatic channel

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for three-body correlation functions previously developed for the $ppp$ system (Kievsky et al., 2022) and the $pd$ system (Kievsky et al., 2022). It addresses the difficulty that for three or more charged particles asymptotically free, the scattering wave function has no closed-form asymptotic expression. The screening approach enables practical calculations using free asymptotic matching conditions, which is particularly relevant for interpreting ALICE Collaboration measurements of $ppp$ and $pd$ correlation functions. The method opens the possibility of studying correlation functions for systems with more than two charged particles in the asymptotic configuration.
