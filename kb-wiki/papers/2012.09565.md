# Leading-order QED radiative corrections to timelike Compton scattering on the proton

> We evaluate the leading-order QED radiative corrections to the timelike Compton scattering (TCS) process $γp \to l^- l^+ p$. We study these corrections in two energy regimes using different models for the TCS amplitude. In the low-energy regime we calculate the contribution due to the proton and its lowest-energy excitation, the $Δ(1232)$ resonance. In the high-energy near-forward kinematical regime we calculate the TCS amplitude in a handbag approach in terms of Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs). On the level of cross sections we find the QED radiative corrections to be in the $5 -10\%$ range in the low-energy regime and around $20\%$ in the high-energy regime. We show that in both the di-lepton forward-backward asymmetry as well as in the photon beam helicity asymmetry these corrections nearly cancel out, making them gold-plated observables to extract the real and imaginary parts of the TCS amplitude. We demonstrate in particular the sensitivity of these asymmetries on GPD parameterizations for a recent CLAS12@JLab TCS experiment.

## Key claim

The leading-order QED radiative corrections to the timelike Compton scattering (TCS) process γp → l⁻l⁺p are evaluated in two distinct energy regimes using different models for the TCS amplitude; while the corrections to the unpolarized cross section are in the 5–10% range at low energies and around 20% at high energies, the di-lepton forward-backward asymmetry and the photon beam helicity asymmetry are shown to be "gold-plated observables" because the radiative corrections nearly cancel in these asymmetries.

## Method

The authors calculate the tree-level Bethe-Heitler (BH) and TCS amplitudes for γp → l⁻l⁺p, then compute the leading-order QED one-loop corrections including vacuum polarization, vertex corrections to the TCS amplitude, and one-loop corrections to the BH process, using dimensional regularization with on-shell renormalization. Soft-photon emission is included to obtain infrared-finite results. For the low-energy regime, the doubly virtual Compton amplitude is modeled by the proton Born term plus the Δ(1232) resonance s-channel pole; for the high-energy near-forward regime, the amplitude is computed in a handbag approach using Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) with the VGG model parameterization.

## Key numbers

* Low-energy regime: QED radiative corrections to the unpolarized cross section are in the 5–10% range.
* High-energy regime: QED radiative corrections to the unpolarized cross section are around 20%.
* The Δ(1232) resonance mass is M_Δ = 1232 MeV, with a width Γ_Δ(W²) appearing in the s-channel propagator.
* The magnetic dipole transition form factor G_M* is parameterized as 3.00 (1 + 0.01 Q²) e^{-0.23 Q²} G_D(Q²) with G_D(Q²) = 1/(1 + Q²/0.71)² and Q in GeV.
* The electric quadrupole transition form factor G_E* is parameterized as 0.064 (1 − 0.021 Q²) e^{-0.16 Q²} G_D(Q²).
* The Coulomb quadrupole transition form factor G_C* is parameterized as 0.124 × (1 + 0.12 Q²)/(1 + 4.9 Q²/(4M²)) × [4 M_Δ² e^{-0.23 Q²} G_D(Q²)]/(M_Δ² − M²).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of QED radiative corrections for the γp → l⁻l⁺p reaction (Heller 2018, Heller 2019) by including the TCS process and generalizing to two energy regimes. The low-energy model is motivated by applications to muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 1900ns) and the proton charge radius puzzle, where the forward-backward asymmetry in e⁻p → e⁻p e⁺e⁻ can empirically constrain proton structure corrections (Pauk 2020). The high-energy GPD-based calculation is relevant for the CLAS12@JLab TCS experiment (Chatagnon 2020) and sets the stage for future studies of QED radiative corrections to the double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS) process e⁻p → e⁻p l⁻l⁺, which is of interest for the SoLID@JLab project (Accardi 2020).
