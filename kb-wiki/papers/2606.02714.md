# Projected Energy Correlators: Two-Loop Jet Functions and NNLL Resummation

> We present the next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) collinear resummation of projected $N$-point energy correlators (ENCs) up to $N=6$, matched to fixed-order predictions at NLO, in both electron-positron annihilation and Higgs decay to gluons. The key new ingredient is the two-loop jet function for $N=4,5,6$, which we compute semi-analytically using Integration-by-Parts and differential equations. We further include the leading non-perturbative corrections for ENCs, described by two universal soft matrix elements $\overlineΩ_{1q},\overlineΩ_{1g}$ of order $Λ_{\rm QCD}$, whose evolution is governed by anomalous dimensions for $(N-1)$-point correlators. The matched distributions are compared with parton-shower simulations from Pythia8 and Herwig7, and we study the sensitivity of both the absolute spectra and their ratios to the two-point energy correlator under variations of $α_s$ and $\overlineΩ_{1q,1g}$. Our results show that higher-point projected energy correlators are now under quantitative control at NNLL accuracy, opening the door to future $α_s$ extractions with complementary systematics.

## Key claim

The paper achieves next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) collinear resummation for projected N-point energy correlators up to N=6, matched to NLO fixed-order predictions, but this accuracy is restricted to the collinear limit and relies on the leading-power factorization theorem; the two-loop jet functions for N=4,5,6 are computed semi-analytically, and the leading non-perturbative corrections are included via two universal soft matrix elements.

## Method

The authors compute the two-loop jet functions for projected N-point energy correlators with N=4,5,6 using Integration-by-Parts reduction and differential equations, extracting the boundary constants by matching the singular x_L to 0 limit of the fixed-order correlator at O(alpha_s^2) onto the factorization formula. The collinear resummation is performed using the expanded solution to the jet function renormalization group equation, with the hard function evolved via timelike DGLAP evolution, and the resummed cumulant is matched to NLO fixed-order results from Event2 (for e+e- annihilation) and Eerad3 (for H to gg decay). Leading non-perturbative corrections are included through two universal soft matrix elements Omega_bar_1q and Omega_bar_1g of order Lambda_QCD, with evolution governed by anomalous dimensions for (N-1)-point correlators.

## Context

This work extends the NNLL resummation program for projected energy correlators beyond the N=2 case (Dixon 2019) and the N=3 case (Chen 2023), where the two-loop jet function was previously computed. The projected N-point correlator framework was introduced by Chen (2020) and provides a simpler route to higher-order calculations compared to fully differential correlators. The results enable future alpha_s extractions with complementary systematics to traditional event shapes such as thrust, C-parameter, and heavy jet mass, which have shown tension with lattice QCD determinations (Flavour Lattice Averaging Group 2024). The CMS collaboration has already extracted alpha_s from the projected three-point correlator in hadron-collider jets (CMS 2024), demonstrating experimental viability. The non-perturbative corrections governed by Omega_bar_1q and Omega_bar_1g are related across different N by a calculable N-dependent prefactor, making the family of projected correlators a natural setting to extract these parameters and lift their degeneracy with alpha_s.
