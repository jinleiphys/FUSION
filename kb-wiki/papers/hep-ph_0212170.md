# Flavor Symmetry Breaking and Strangeness in the Nucleon

> We suggest that breaking of SU(3) flavor symmetry mainly resides in the baryon wave--functions while the charge operators have no (or only small) explicit symmetry breaking components. We utilize the collective coordinate approach to chiral soliton models to support this picture. In particular we compute the $g_A/g_V$ ratios for hyperon beta--decay and the strangeness contribution to the nucleon axial current matrix elements and analyze their variation with increasing flavor symmetry breaking.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking resides mainly in the baryon wave-functions rather than in the charge operators, and that this picture can reproduce hyperon beta-decay data while predicting a significantly smaller strangeness contribution to the nucleon axial current than the flavor-covariant analysis suggests.

## Method
The authors use the collective coordinate approach to chiral soliton models, quantizing large amplitude fluctuations about the soliton and constructing exact eigenstates in the presence of symmetry breaking. They parameterize the axial charge operator with only flavor-covariant components (three parameters c1, c2, c3) and compute matrix elements using baryon wave-functions that are strongly distorted by flavor symmetry breaking, with the effective strength parameter ω² determined from baryon mass differences. They also present results from a realistic vector meson soliton model containing pseudoscalar and vector meson fields.

## Key numbers
- The effective symmetry breaking strength ω² is required to be in the range 5 ≤ ω² ≤ 8 to obtain reasonable agreement with empirical mass differences for the 1/2⁺ and 3/2⁺ baryons
- The strangeness contribution to the nucleon axial current reduces from ΔS_N ≈ -0.13 in the symmetric treatment to ΔS_N ≈ -0.07 in the realistic case
- In the realistic vector meson model, the strangeness projection ΔS for the nucleon ranges from -0.016 to -0.034 across three parameter sets (b.f., mag. mom., g_A)
- The nucleon axial charge g_A is predicted as 0.88 (b.f. set), 0.98 (mag. mom. set), and reproduced exactly (g_A set)
- The empirical g_A/g_V ratios for hyperon beta-decay are: Λ→p = 0.718±0.015, Σ→n = 0.340±0.017, Ξ→Λ = 0.25±0.05, Ξ→Σ = 1.287±0.158, Σ→Λ = 0.61±0.02

## Context
This work extends the Cabibbo scheme (1963) for hyperon beta-decay and the F&D parameterization, which works unexpectedly well for flavor-changing axial charges, to investigate whether flavor covariance can be carried over to strangeness-conserving axial current matrix elements. The analysis was motivated by the European Muon Collaboration (1988) data suggesting a large negative polarization of strange quarks in the nucleon (ΔS_N ≈ -0.15), and the results contradict the flavor-covariant analysis by predicting a significantly smaller strangeness admixture. The approach enables future studies of baryon axial properties using wave-functions with significant admixture of higher-dimensional SU(3) representations.
