# Shape Coexistence at Zero Spin in 64Ni Driven by the Monopole Tensor Interaction

> The low-spin structure of the semimagic 64Ni nucleus has been considerably expanded: combining four experiments, several 0+ and 2+ excited states were identified below 4.5 MeV, and their properties established. The Monte Carlo shell model accounts for the results and unveils an unexpectedly complex landscape of coexisting shapes: a prolate 0+ excitation is located at a surprisingly high energy (3463 keV), with a collective 2+ state 286 keV above it, the first such observation in Ni isotopes. The evolution in excitation energy of the prolate minimum across the neutron N = 40 subshell gap highlights the impact of the monopole interaction and its variation in strength with N.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shape coexistence in the stable, semi-magic nucleus 64Ni is driven by the monopole tensor interaction, with a prolate 0+ state identified at 3463 keV and a collective 2+ state 286 keV above it, representing the first such observation in Ni isotopes. The abstract's claim of an "unexpectedly complex landscape" is qualified by the condition that this complexity is only reproduced by Monte Carlo shell-model calculations that include the monopole tensor interaction, while mean-field calculations had predicted only a single spherical minimum for 64Ni.

## Method

The low-spin structure of 64Ni was investigated through four complementary experiments: two-neutron transfer reactions (62Ni(18O,16O) at 39 MeV) and one-proton transfer reactions (65Cu(11B,12C) at 26 MeV) at IFIN-HH with the ROSPHERE array; thermal-neutron capture on a 63Ni sample at ILL with the FIPPS array; and multi-step Coulomb excitation at 272 MeV on a 208Pb target at ANL with GRETINA and CHICO2. Lifetimes were measured via the recoil-distance technique, and angular correlations from neutron-capture data provided spin-parity assignments and mixing ratios.

## Key numbers

- Prolate 0+ excitation (0+4) located at 3463.1 keV, with a 2+ state (2+6) at 3749.1 keV, 286 keV above it
- Half-lives: 0+2 state T1/2 = 1.4(6) ps; 0+3 state T1/2 = 3.6(1.2) ps
- B(E2; 0+4 -> 2+1) < 1.3 e2fm4 (< 0.08 W.u.), indicating a prolate-to-spherical shape-changing transition
- B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) = 140(20) e2fm4 (9(1) W.u.)
- B(E2; 0+2 -> 2+1) = 48(3) e2fm4 (3.2(2) W.u.)
- B(E2; 0+3 -> 2+1) = 10(1) e2fm4 (0.65(7) W.u.)
- Mixing ratio for 2+6 -> 2+1 transition: delta = +1.23(10), indicating dominant E2 character
- Lower limit of 0.5 ps for the 2+6 state half-life, giving upper limits of 0.02, 0.4, and 0.02 W.u. for transitions to 0+1, 2+1, and 2+2 respectively
- Five new 0+ states identified at 3463.1, 3856.0, 4268.1, 4703.9, and 5768.6 keV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in Ni isotopes, where 68Ni exhibits oblate and prolate 0+ excitations (Tsunoda 2014, Cridge 2016), 70Ni has a prolate 0+2 at 1567 keV (Prokop 2015), and 66Ni shows spherical, oblate, and prolate 0+ states (Leoni 2017). The results contradict mean-field calculations (Hilaire 2007, Bertsch 1991, Decharge 1980, Moller 2004, Moller 2009) that predicted only a single spherical minimum for 64Ni. The evolution of the prolate minimum energy from 1567 keV in 70Ni to 3463 keV in 64Ni demonstrates Type II shell evolution driven by the monopole tensor interaction, enabling future studies of how this mechanism operates across the nuclear chart.
