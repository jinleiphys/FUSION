# Insights on pion production mechanism and symmetry energy at high density

> The $NΔ\to NN$ cross sections, which take into account the $Δ$-mass dependence of M-matrix and momentum $p_{NΔ}$, are applied on the calculation of pion production within the framework of the UrQMD model. Our study shows that UrQMD calculations with the $Δ$-mass dependent $NΔ\to NN$ cross sections enhance the pion multiplicities and decrease the $π^-/π^+$ ratios. By analyzing the time evolution of the pion production rate and the density in the overlapped region for Au+Au at the beam energy of 0.4A GeV, we find that the pion multiplicity probes the symmetry energy in the region of 1-2 times normal density. The process of pion production in the reaction is tracked including the loops of $NN\leftrightarrow NΔ$ and $Δ\leftrightarrow Nπ$, our calculations show that the sensitivity of $π^-/π^+$ to symmetry energy is weakened after 4-5 N-$Δ$-$π$ loops in the pion production path, while the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in reactions at near threshold energies remains its sensitivity to the symmetry energy. By comparing the calculations to the FOPI data, we obtain a model dependent conclusion on the symmetry energy and the symmetry energy at two times normal density is $S(2ρ_0)$=38-73 MeV within $1σ$ uncertainties. Under the constraints of tidal deformability and maximum mass of neutron star, the symmetry energy at two times normal density is reduced to $48-58$ MeV and slope of symmetry energy $L=54-81$ MeV, and it is consistent with the constraints from ASY-EOS flow data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using a Delta-mass-dependent NDelta to NN cross section in UrQMD calculations enhances pion multiplicities and decreases the pi-/pi+ ratios compared to the standard UrQMD treatment, but the authors note that the resulting constraints on the symmetry energy at twice normal density are model-dependent, with the range S(2rho0)=38-73 MeV within 1sigma uncertainties before applying neutron star constraints.

## Method

The authors implemented Delta-mass-dependent NDelta to NN cross sections, calculated from a one-boson-exchange model, into the UrQMD transport model for simulations of Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.4A to 1.0A GeV. They tracked the time evolution of pion production rates, central density in the overlapping region, and the number of NN to NDelta, NDelta to NN, Npi to Delta, and Delta to Npi processes to analyze the pion production mechanism and the density region probed by pion observables. The calculations used five different density-dependent symmetry energy parametrizations and compared results to FOPI experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at two times normal density from fitting FOPI data alone is S(2rho0)=38-73 MeV within 1sigma uncertainties
- Under constraints from neutron star tidal deformability and maximum mass, the symmetry energy at two times normal density is reduced to S(2rho0)=48-58 MeV
- The slope of symmetry energy under neutron star constraints is L=54-81 MeV
- For Au+Au at 0.4A GeV, approximately 87% of total NN collisions are elastic, with only about 6% being inelastic NN to NDelta collisions
- At 0.4A GeV, 67% of Deltas decay into nucleon and pion, while 33% participate in NDelta to NN collisions
- The pion production rate weighted average density is around 1.6 times normal density across beam energies from 0.4A to 1.0A GeV
- The system at 1A GeV spends about 19 fm/c at densities above normal density and 41 fm/c below normal density; at 0.4A GeV it spends about 26 fm/c above and 34 fm/c below normal density

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD studies (Li et al., 2006) that underestimated pion multiplicities, and addresses the ongoing debate on symmetry energy constraints from pion ratios where different groups (Xiao et al., 2009; Xie et al., 2013; Feng, 2010; Kumar and S., 2015) reached different conclusions. The paper connects to the code comparison project (Akira et al., 2019) and the FOPI experimental data (Reisdorf et al., 2007), and the results are consistent with constraints from ASY-EOS flow data and neutron star observations from GW170817. The analysis enables future studies of subthreshold pion production measurements at MSU and RIKEN for Sn+Sn at 270A MeV.
