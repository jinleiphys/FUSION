# Photon-fusion reactions from the chiral Lagrangian with dynamical light vector mesons

> We study the reactions $╬│╬│\rightarrow ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$, $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$, $K^0\bar{K}^0$, $K^+K^-$, $╬Ę╬Ę$ and $ŽĆ^0╬Ę$ based on a chiral Lagrangian with dynamical light vector mesons as formulated within the hadrogenesis conjecture. At present our chiral Lagrangian contains 5 unknown parameters that are relevant for the photon fusion reactions. They parameterize the strength of interaction terms involving two vector meson fields. These parameters are fitted to photon fusion data $╬│╬│\rightarrow ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$, $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-, ŽĆ^0╬Ę$ and to the decay $╬Ę\rightarrowŽĆ^0╬│╬│$. In order to derive gauge invariant reaction amplitudes in the resonance region constraints from micro-causality and exact coupled-channel unitarity are used. Our results are in good agreement with the existing experimental data from threshold up to about 0.9 GeV for the two-pion final states. The $a_0$ meson in the $ŽĆ^0╬Ę$ channel is dynamically generated and an accurate reproduction of the $╬│╬│\rightarrow ŽĆ^0╬Ę$ data is achieved up to 1.2 GeV. Based on our parameter sets we predict the $╬│╬│\rightarrow $ $K^0\bar{K}^0$, $K^+K^-$, $╬Ę╬Ę$ cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a unified description of six photon-fusion reactions from threshold up to about 0.9ŌĆō1.2 GeV by combining a chiral Lagrangian with explicit light vector mesons with constraints from maximal analyticity and exact coupled-channel unitarity, but the description relies on five unknown coupling constants that are fitted to data and the analysis is limited to energies below the region where tensor resonances such as \(f_2(1270)\) and \(a_2(1320)\) become important.

## Method

The authors construct tree-level amplitudes for \(\gamma\gamma \to PP\) from a chiral Lagrangian containing Goldstone bosons and light vector mesons in the antisymmetric tensor-field representation, with five undetermined parameters (\(g_{1-3}, g_5, h_O\)) that enter interaction terms involving two vector-meson fields. They then apply a dispersive coupled-channel formalism that separates the generalized potential into a part from close-by left-hand cuts (computed from the Lagrangian) and a part from far-distant cuts (expanded in a conformal variable), and solve the dispersion relation (Eq. 4) to obtain unitarized partial-wave amplitudes. The unknown parameters are fitted to experimental data on \(\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0,\ \pi^+\pi^-,\ \pi^0\eta\) and the decay \(\eta\to\pi^0\gamma\gamma\).

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic charge is taken as \(e = 0.303\).
- The vector-meson coupling constant is \(f_V = 0.140 \pm 0.014\ \text{GeV}\).
- The product \(h_P f_V = 0.23\ \text{GeV}\).
- The parameter \(b_D = 0.92\) and \(b_A = 0.27\).
- The axial-vector coupling \(h_A \simeq 2.10\).
- The pion decay constant is \(f \simeq 0.90\ \text{GeV}\).
- Good agreement with two-pion final-state data is achieved from threshold up to about \(0.9\ \text{GeV}\).
- Accurate reproduction of \(\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\eta\) data is achieved up to \(1.2\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel dispersive framework previously applied to Goldstone-boson scattering by Danilkin et al. (2011) and Gasparyan et al. (2010ŌĆō2012) to photon-fusion reactions, incorporating explicit light vector mesons as suggested by the hadrogenesis conjecture of Lutz et al. (2001ŌĆō2008). It goes beyond chiral perturbation theory at two loops (Gasser et al., 2005ŌĆō2006) by including exact coupled-channel unitarity and the dynamical generation of the \(a_0(980)\) resonance. The results enable predictions for \(\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar{K}^0,\ K^+K^-,\ \eta\eta\) cross sections and provide a foundation for future inclusion of vector-meson channels and tensor resonances such as \(f_2(1270)\) and \(a_2(1320)\).
