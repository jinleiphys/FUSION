# N/Z and N/A dependence of balance energy as a probe of symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the N/Z and N/A dependence of balance energy (E$_{bal}$) for isotopic series of Ca having N/Z (N/A) varying from 1.0 to 2.0 (0.5 to 0.67). We show that the N/Z (N/A) dependence of E$_{bal}$ is sensitive to symmetry energy and its density dependence at densities higher than saturation density and is insensitive towards the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon (nn) cross section and Coulomb repulsion. We also study the effect of momentum dependent interactions (MDI) on the N/Z (N/A) dependence of E$_{bal}$. We find that although MDI influences the E$_{bal}$ drastically, the N/Z (N/A) dependence of E$_{bal}$ remains unchanged on inclusion of MDI.

## Key claim

The N/Z (neutron-to-proton ratio) and N/A (neutron-to-mass-number ratio) dependence of balance energy (E$_{bal}$) for isotopic series of Ca+Ca collisions is a sensitive probe of the symmetry energy and its density dependence at densities above saturation density, but only when the Coulomb repulsion is held constant across the isotopic series; the dependence is insensitive to the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section and to momentum-dependent interactions (MDI), even though MDI changes the absolute value of E$_{bal}$ by about 30%.

## Method

The study used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate semicentral (impact parameter 0.2-0.4 of maximum) collisions of symmetric Ca+Ca systems with N/Z varying from 1.0 to 2.0 (N/A from 0.5 to 0.67), corresponding to isotopes from $^{40}$Ca to $^{60}$Ca. The balance energy was extracted from the directed transverse momentum $\langle p_x^{dir}\rangle$ as a function of incident energy, using a soft equation of state with and without momentum-dependent interactions, and with different forms of the symmetry energy potential: F$_1(u) \propto u$, F$_2(u) \propto u^{0.4}$, F$_3(u) \propto u^2$, and F$_4$ (no symmetry potential), where $u = \rho/\rho_0$.

## Key numbers

- E$_{bal}$ follows a linear behavior $\propto m \times \text{N/Z}$; the slope magnitude $|m|$ increases by a factor of 3 when symmetry energy is included (F$_1(u)$) compared to calculations without symmetry energy (F$_4$).
- For E$_{bal}$ as a function of N/A, the linear slope m = -191 for F$_1(u)$ and m = -68 for F$_4$; the percentage difference $\Delta E_{bal}\%$ between F$_1(u)$ and F$_4$ is about 65% for both N/Z and N/A representations.
- For isospin-independent cross section over the full N/Z range 1.0-2.0, the slope m = -40; for the restricted N/Z range 1.2-2.0, m = -34 with isospin-dependent cross section and m = -37 without, showing reduced sensitivity.
- MDI changes the absolute value of E$_{bal}$ by about 30%, but the N/Z (N/A) dependence remains unchanged.
- For asymmetric reactions with fixed $^{40}$Ca target and varying projectile ($^{40}$Ca to $^{60}$Ca), the N/Z (N/A) dependence of E$_{bal}$ is the same as for symmetric reactions, with E$_{bal}$ determined by the N/Z (N/A) of the combined system.

## Context

This work extends the study of Gautam and Sood (2010), who showed that Coulomb repulsion dominates over symmetry energy in isospin effects on collective transverse flow and its disappearance for isobaric systems. The present paper demonstrates that by using an isotopic series (constant Coulomb repulsion), the N/Z and N/A dependence of balance energy isolates the symmetry energy contribution, unlike previous observables such as neutron/proton ratios (Li 1997), neutron-proton differential flow (Li 2000), and $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratios (Li 2002, Gaitanos 2004) where Coulomb force competes strongly. The results enable future experiments with radioactive beams to constrain the density dependence of symmetry energy above saturation density using balance energy measurements, independent of uncertainties in the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross sections and momentum-dependent interactions.
