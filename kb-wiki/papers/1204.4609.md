# J/psi suppression in p-A collisions from parton energy loss in cold QCD matter

> The effects of energy loss in cold nuclear matter on J/psi suppression in p-A collisions are studied. A simple model based on first principles and depending on a single free parameter is able to reproduce J/psi suppression data at large xF and at various center-of-mass energies. These results strongly support energy loss as a dominant effect in quarkonium suppression. They also give some hint on its hadroproduction mechanism suggesting color neutralization to happen on long time-scales. Predictions for J/psi and Upsilon suppression in p-Pb collisions at the LHC are made.

# J/psi suppression in p-A collisions from parton energy loss in cold QCD matter

## Key claim

The authors argue that parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter is the dominant mechanism for J/psi suppression in p-A collisions at large xF, based on a model with a single free parameter (the transport coefficient qhat_0) that reproduces existing data across multiple energies and targets. The model assumes the heavy-quark pair is produced in a compact color octet state and remains color octet for a time much longer than the hard production time, which holds for hadroproduction models where color neutralization is a soft process.

## Method

The J/psi nuclear modification factor RpA(xF) is computed by shifting the xF distribution from pp collisions by an amount corresponding to the energy loss epsilon incurred by the color octet c-cbar pair propagating through the nucleus, using a quenching weight derived from the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum. The transport coefficient qhat(x) is related to the gluon distribution in the target nucleon, with its x-dependence modeled as qhat(x) = qhat_0 * (10^-2 / x)^0.3, and the nuclear broadening Delta q_perp^2 is obtained from the difference between the transport coefficient in the nucleus and in the proton. The single free parameter qhat_0 is fitted to E866 p-W over p-Be data at sqrt(s)=38.7 GeV in the xF range 0.2-0.8, and saturation effects can optionally be included via a parameterization of the saturation scale Q_s^2 = qhat(x) * L.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient from fit to E866 data (energy loss only): qhat_0 = 0.09 GeV^2/fm
- Transport coefficient from fit including saturation effects: qhat_0 = 0.05 GeV^2/fm
- Corresponding saturation scales at x=10^-2: Q_s^2 = 0.08-0.15 GeV^2
- J/psi mass used in calculations: M = 3 GeV; Upsilon mass: M = 9 GeV
- Transverse momentum assumed: pT = 1 GeV
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s = 0.5
- QCD scale: Lambda_QCD = 0.25 GeV
- Nuclear radius parameter: r_0 = 1.12 fm
- Predicted RpPb at LHC (sqrt(s)=5 TeV): RpPb ~ 0.7-0.8 at y=1, RpPb < 0.5 at y > 4
- Predicted Upsilon suppression at LHC: R_Upsilon_pPb ~ 0.8 at y=3
- Exponent for pp differential cross section fit at NA3 energies: n_pp = 4.3; for pi-p: n_pi-p = 1.5

## Context

This work extends the energy loss formalism developed by Arleo (2010) for induced gluon radiation in cold nuclear matter, applying it to quarkonium suppression in p-A and pi-A collisions. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the phenomenological energy loss model of Gavin (1991) and the bound derived by Brodsky (1992), and provides a first-principles calculation with a single parameter. The results enable predictions for the upcoming p-Pb run at the LHC (scheduled for 2012) that can be tested by ALICE and LHCb, and establish a baseline for interpreting J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC as measured by ATLAS (2010), CMS (2012), and ALICE (2012).
