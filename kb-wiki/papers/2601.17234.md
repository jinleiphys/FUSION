# Longitudinal Dynamics of Large and Small Systems from a 3D Bayesian Calibration of RHIC Top-energy Collision Data

> A comprehensive Bayesian analysis of the 3D dynamics of high-energy nuclear collisions is presented. We perform a systematic model-to-data comparison using simulations of large and small collision systems, and a broad range of measurements from the PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS collaborations spanning nearly two decades of RHIC operations. In particular, we perform fully 3D multi-stage simulations including rapidity-dependent energy deposition with global energy conservation using the 3D Glauber model, along with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with MUSIC. We calibrate the model on rapidity- and $p_T$-differential observables and analyze the respective constraints on initial state and transport properties they provide. We emphasize the additional constraints provided by rapidity-dependent measurements, the differences in large and small system calibrations, and the tension exhibited by particular observables. We use our calibrated model to make predictions of observables in p-Au and $^3$He-Au collisions. Furthermore, we facilitate direct comparison of experimental measurements by highlighting the dependence of flow measurements on the rapidity of the regions of interest and reference, as well as the importance of the centrality selection. In particular, we examine the apparent differences between the STAR and PHENIX $v_2$ and $v_3$ measurements in small systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents a comprehensive Bayesian calibration of a fully (3+1)D multi-stage model of nuclear collisions against rapidity- and pT-differential data from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, but the abstract's claim of constraining 3D dynamics is qualified by the fact that the analysis uses a specific parametrization of the initial state (3D Glauber model) and transport coefficients whose temperature dependence is encoded by a fixed functional form, and the equation of state is not varied, representing an unaccounted source of uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed fully (3+1)D multi-stage simulations using the 3D Glauber model for initial-state energy deposition with global energy conservation, followed by relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with MUSIC and hadronic afterburner with UrQMD. They calibrated 20 model parameters (covering initial state, hydrodynamization, shear and bulk viscosity parametrizations, and particlization) via Bayesian inference using Gaussian process emulators as fast surrogates for the full model, comparing to rapidity- and pT-differential observables from PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS collaborations at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The analysis uses 20 model parameters with uniform priors, including initial state parameters such as B_G in range [2, 25] GeV^-2 (sub-nucleonic fluctuation width), sigma_x in [0.1, 0.5] fm (transverse smearing width), and tau_form in [0.2, 1] fm (string formation time)
* The specific shear viscosity is parametrized with a kink temperature T_kink in [0.13, 0.3] GeV and value (eta/s)_kink in [0.01, 0.2], with slopes m_low in [-2, 1] GeV^-1 and m_high in [-1, 2] GeV^-1
* The specific bulk viscosity uses a skewed Cauchy distribution with maximum value (zeta/s)_max in [0.01, 0.2] at temperature T_max in [0.12, 0.3] GeV, width w_zeta in [0.025, 0.15] GeV, and asymmetry lambda_zeta in [-0.8, 0.6]
* The switching energy density for particlization e_switch is varied in [0.1, 0.6] GeV/fm^3
* The rapidity loss parameters y_2, y_4, y_6 are defined at incoming beam rapidities of 2, 4, and 6 respectively, with y_beam = 5.36 for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV collisions

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian calibrations of heavy-ion collisions that used (2+1)D boost-invariant models (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2019, JETSCAPE 2020) by incorporating full (3+1)D dynamics and rapidity-dependent measurements. It builds on prior 3D Bayesian studies (Auvinen 2017, Ke 2016, Soeder 2023, Shen 2023) but uses the 3D Glauber model with global energy conservation, differing from some earlier approaches in the initial-state prescription. The calibrated model enables predictions for p-Au and 3He-Au collisions and allows direct comparison of flow measurements across different rapidity regions, particularly addressing apparent differences between STAR and PHENIX v2 and v3 measurements in small systems.
