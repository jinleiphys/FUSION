# The nucleon-nucleon interaction

> We review the major progress of the past decade concerning our understanding of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The focus is on the low-energy region (below pion production threshold), but a brief outlook towards higher energies is also given. The items discussed include charge-dependence, the precise value of the $πNN$ coupling constant, phase shift analysis and high-precision NN data and potentials. We also address the issue of a proper theory of nuclear forces. Finally, we summarize the essential open questions that future research should be devoted to.

## Key claim

This review asserts that the nucleon-nucleon interaction is the empirically best-known piece of strong interactions, but it also acknowledges that about 25% of the charge-dependence of the singlet scattering length remains unexplained by current meson-exchange models, despite major progress in the 1990s.

## Method

The authors review experimental and theoretical progress on the nucleon-nucleon interaction below pion-production threshold, focusing on charge dependence, the πNN coupling constant, phase shift analysis, and high-precision NN potentials. They analyze charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) and charge-independence breaking (CIB) using the Bonn Full Model for the NN interaction, which includes one-boson-exchange, two-pion-exchange, πρ-exchange, and higher-order diagrams, comparing calculated effective-range parameters and phase shifts to empirical data from scattering experiments and multi-particle reactions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length from D(π⁻,γn)n: a_nn = -18.59 ± 0.40 fm (combined world average from three studies)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length from D(n,nnp) by TUNL group (1999): a_nn = -18.7 ± 0.6 fm
- Neutron-neutron scattering length from D(n,nnp) by Bonn group (2000): a_nn = -16.27 ± 0.4 fm
- Pure nuclear nn scattering length (corrected for magnetic interaction): a_nn^N = -18.9 ± 0.4 fm
- Pure nuclear pp scattering length: a_pp^N = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm
- Charge-symmetry breaking in scattering length: Δa_CSB = a_pp^N - a_nn^N = 1.6 ± 0.6 fm
- np singlet scattering length: a_np = -23.740 ± 0.020 fm
- Charge-independence breaking in scattering length: Δa_CIB = (a_pp^N + a_nn^N)/2 - a_np = 5.64 ± 0.60 fm
- π-η mixing angle from D(π⁺,η)pp and D(π⁻,η)nn comparison: (1.5 ± 0.4) degrees
- Pion masses: m_π⁰ = 134.977 MeV, m_π± = 139.570 MeV

## Context

This topical review summarizes the major progress of the 1990s on the nucleon-nucleon interaction, extending earlier summaries by Machleidt (1989) and Machleidt and Li (1994). It addresses the long-standing problem of charge dependence, building on the meson-exchange framework of Yukawa (1935) and the Bonn Full Model of Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster (1987). The review enables future work on a proper theory of nuclear forces from QCD, while identifying open questions such as the unresolved discrepancy between the TUNL and Bonn groups' a_nn values from neutron-induced deuteron breakup and the missing 25% of charge-independence breaking.
