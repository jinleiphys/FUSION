# Wood-Saxon alpha potential for p-nuclei $^{106}$Cd and $^{113}$In

> Alpha elastic scattering of p-nuclei were studied for calculating optical potentials. Choice of the $α$-optical potentials are important to measure the reaction rates of p-process. $^{106}$Cd$(α,α)^{106}$Cd and $^{113}$In$(α,α)^{113}$In elastic scattering cross-section data were used to determine the potential parameter sets at E$_{lab}$= 16.1-27 MeV for $^{106}$Cd and E$_{lab}$=16.14, 19.4 MeV for $^{113}$In system. A Wood-Saxon potential form factor is used for both real and imaginary part. The potential parameters extracted in the present study exhibit a satisfactory result with respect to existing global potential parameters.

# Wood-Saxon alpha potential for p-nuclei $^{106}$Cd and $^{113}$In

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a simple Wood-Saxon form factor for both the real and imaginary parts of the alpha-nucleus optical potential can satisfactorily describe elastic scattering data for the p-nuclei $^{106}$Cd and $^{113}$In, in contrast to prior work that predominantly used microscopic folding models for the real potential. However, the fit quality worsens significantly at higher energies (22-27 MeV) for $^{106}$Cd, with $\chi^2$/N values rising from near 1 to over 12, indicating the Wood-Saxon parameterization is less reliable at those energies.

## Method
Alpha elastic scattering cross-section data for $^{106}$Cd($\alpha$,$\alpha$)$^{106}$Cd and $^{113}$In($\alpha$,$\alpha$)$^{113}$In were fitted using the optical model code SFRESCO, employing a Wood-Saxon form factor for both the real volume potential and the imaginary potential (including volume and optional surface terms). For $^{113}$In, a spin-orbit term was also included. The extracted potential parameters were compared with existing global potentials from McFadden and Satchler and from Avrigeanu.

## Key numbers
- For $^{106}$Cd, data were analyzed at laboratory energies E$_{lab}$ = 16.1-27 MeV; the best fit ($\chi^2$/N = 0.998) was obtained at E$_{lab}$ = 17.0 MeV (Notre Dame data) with real depth V$_0$ = 85.91 MeV, radius R$_v$ = 1.10 fm, diffusivity a$_v$ = 0.60 fm, volume imaginary depth W$_v$ = 23.87 MeV, R$_w$ = 1.18 fm, a$_w$ = 0.45 fm.
- For $^{106}$Cd at E$_{lab}$ = 27.0 MeV, the fit worsened to $\chi^2$/N = 12.402 with V$_0$ = 68.85 MeV, R$_v$ = 1.07 fm, a$_v$ = 0.62 fm, W$_v$ = 11.95 MeV, R$_w$ = 1.16 fm, a$_w$ = 0.55 fm (volume only); adding a surface imaginary term (W$_s$ = 11.52 MeV) improved $\chi^2$/N to 10.960.
- For $^{113}$In at E$_{lab}$ = 16.14 MeV, the fit gave $\chi^2$/N = 1.163 with V$_0$ = 78.38 MeV, R$_v$ = 1.10 fm, a$_v$ = 0.57 fm, W$_v$ = 11.33 MeV, R$_w$ = 1.11 fm, a$_w$ = 0.39 fm, and spin-orbit depth V$_{so}$ = 5.004 MeV.
- For $^{113}$In at E$_{lab}$ = 19.4 MeV, the fit gave $\chi^2$/N = 1.460 with V$_0$ = 118.59 MeV, R$_v$ = 1.09 fm, a$_v$ = 0.54 fm, W$_v$ = 24.55 MeV, R$_w$ = 1.15 fm, a$_w$ = 0.36 fm, and V$_{so}$ = 5.19 MeV.
- A global energy-dependent parameterization for $^{106}$Cd was derived: V$_0$ $\approx$ 320.88 - 22.58 E$_{cm}$ + 0.4958 E$_{cm}^2$ (MeV) and W$_v$ $\approx$ 111.168 - 8.6602 E$_{cm}$ + 0.18763 E$_{cm}^2$ (MeV), with fixed radii R$_v$ $\approx$ 1.10 fm, a$_v$ $\approx$ 0.60 fm, R$_w$ $\approx$ 1.18 fm, a$_w$ $\approx$ 0.45 fm.

## Context
This work extends prior alpha elastic scattering studies on p-nuclei by Kiss et al. (2010s) for $^{106,110,116}$Cd and $^{113}$In, and by Ornelas et al. and Palumbo et al., all of whom used microscopic folding models (DDM3Y or M3Y) for the real potential with Wood-Saxon imaginary parts. The present study is the first to apply a full Wood-Saxon parameterization for both real and imaginary potentials on these specific p-nuclei, following the approach of Avrigeanu et al. for Sn isotopes. The extracted global parameter set for $^{106}$Cd enables reaction rate calculations for the p-process network, while the authors note that a global potential for $^{113}$In requires additional measurements at higher energies.
