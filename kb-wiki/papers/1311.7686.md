# Two-Baryon Systems with Twisted Boundary Conditions

> We explore the use of twisted boundary conditions in extracting the nucleon mass and the binding energy of two-baryon systems, such as the deuteron, from Lattice QCD calculations. Averaging the results of calculations performed with periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions imposed upon the light-quark fields, or other pair-wise averages, improves the volume dependence of the deuteron binding energy from ~exp(-kappa*L)/L to ~exp(-sqrt(2)kappa*L)/L. However, a twist angle of pi/2 in each of the spatial directions improves the volume dependence from ~exp(-kappa*L)/L to ~exp(-2kappa*L)/L. Twist averaging the binding energy with a random sampling of twist angles improves the volume dependence from ~exp^(-kappa*L)/L to ~exp(-2kappa*L)/L, but with a standard deviation of ~exp(-kappa*L)/L, introducing a signal-to-noise issue in modest lattice volumes. Using the experimentally determined phase shifts and mixing angles, we determine the expected energies of the deuteron states over a range of cubic lattice volumes for a selection of twisted boundary conditions.

## Key claim

Twisted boundary conditions can exponentially improve the volume dependence of the deuteron binding energy extracted from Lattice QCD calculations, but the improvement depends on the specific choice of twist angle or averaging procedure, and twist averaging introduces a signal-to-noise issue with a standard deviation that scales as exp(-kappa L)/L.

## Method

The authors derived the finite-volume quantization condition for two-nucleon systems with twisted boundary conditions, generalizing the Luscher formalism to arbitrary twist angles. They then used experimentally determined nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and mixing angles (including the S-D mixing parameter epsilon_1) as inputs to this quantization condition to predict the deuteron binding energy spectra over a range of cubic lattice volumes for several choices of twist angles: periodic boundary conditions (PBCs), anti-periodic boundary conditions (APBCs), and i-PBCs (twist angle pi/2 in each spatial direction).

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy in nature is B_d^infinity = 2.224644(34) MeV
- For PBCs, percent-level accuracy in the deuteron binding energy requires volumes with L greater than or approximately equal to 17 fm
- For i-PBCs (twist angle pi/2 in each spatial direction), the volume dependence improves from exp(-kappa L)/L to exp(-2 kappa L)/L
- For i-PBCs, calculations in the E irrep of the C3v group provide a few percent-level accurate determination of the deuteron binding energy at L approximately 9 fm
- Averaging PBC and APBC results improves volume dependence from exp(-kappa L)/L to exp(-sqrt(2) kappa L)/L
- Twist averaging with random sampling improves volume dependence from exp(-kappa L)/L to exp(-2 kappa L)/L, but with a standard deviation of exp(-kappa L)/L
- For PBCs at L = 9 fm, the finite-volume deuteron is approximately twice as bound as the infinite-volume deuteron

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism for two-nucleon systems developed by Luscher (1990) and later generalized to boosted systems by Davoudi (2011) and Briceno (2013). The use of twisted boundary conditions in Lattice QCD was introduced by Bedaque (2004), who showed that TBCs are equivalent to having a U(1) background gauge field. The paper demonstrates that specific twist angles (i-PBCs) can achieve volume improvements comparable to full twist averaging but without the associated signal-to-noise problem, and that partial twisting (applying TBCs only to valence quarks) is valid for nucleon-nucleon systems in sufficiently large volumes (L greater than or approximately equal to 9 fm). This enables future Lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron binding energy with percent-level accuracy in significantly smaller volumes than previously required.
