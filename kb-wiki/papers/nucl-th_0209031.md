# Polarization phenomena for heavy meson production in NN-collisions near threshold

> Polarization phenomena are essential for the understanding of elementary processes, and are typically large in the threshold region. The selection rules with respect to P-parity, angular momentum, isotopic spin and the Pauli principle allow to parametrize in a model independent way, the spin structure of the matrix elements for the near-threshold heavy meson production in NN-collisions in terms of a small number of partial amplitudes. Such parametrization is very powerful for the analysis of polarization phenomena and possible $t-$exchange mechanisms.

# Polarization phenomena for heavy meson production in NN-collisions near threshold

## Key claim

The spin structure of matrix elements for near-threshold heavy meson production in NN-collisions can be parametrized in a model-independent way using a small number of partial amplitudes, based only on symmetry properties (P-parity, angular momentum, isotopic spin, and the Pauli principle), and this parametrization yields specific predictions for polarization observables that are largely independent of the detailed dynamics of the particular meson produced.

## Method

The authors apply selection rules from P-invariance, isotopic invariance, and the Pauli principle to derive the allowed partial-wave transitions for vector meson production (V-meson) and strange particle production (N+N to N+Y+K) in the threshold region where all final particles are produced in relative S-states. For V-meson production in pp-collisions, a single partial amplitude (triplet-singlet transition) describes the process, while np-collisions require two amplitudes; for strange particle production, two amplitudes are needed even for pp-collisions due to non-identical final baryons. The formalism is then used to express spin correlation coefficients, polarization transfer coefficients, and cross section ratios in terms of these amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For pp to pp+V^0 at threshold: spin correlation coefficients are A1(pp)=0 and A2(pp)=1, independent of the vector meson type.
- For np to np+V^0 at threshold: A1(np) = -|f01|^2/(|f01|^2+|f10|^2) and A2(np) = |f10|^2/(|f01|^2+|f10|^2), with the relation -A1(np)+A2(np)=1.
- The ratio R of total unpolarized cross sections for np and pp collisions is R = sigma(np to npV^0)/sigma(pp to ppV^0) = 1/2 + (1/2)(|f01|^2/|f10|^2).
- For the reaction p+p to p+p+phi at P_Lab=3.67 GeV (Q=83 MeV), DISTO measured the phi-meson spin density matrix element rho_00 = 0.23 +/- 0.04, from which the predicted spin correlation coefficients are C_kk=0.74, C_nn=C_mm=0.13, and A_y=0.
- For strange particle production at threshold, the unpolarized cross section is (d sigma/d omega)_0 = |f10|^2 + 2|f11|^2, with correlation coefficients satisfying 2C_nn + C_kk = 1.
- For K-exchange only in p+p to p+Lambda+K+, D_nn = -2/3; for pi-exchange only, D_nn = 2/3; for both exchanges, C_nn = (1-2r+r^2)/(3+2r+3r^2) and D_nn = 2(r^2-2)/(3+2r+3r^2), where r = f^(pi)/f^(K).
- DISTO data (D_nn approximately -0.4) suggest two possible values for r: r^- = -0.64 and r^+ = 0.39, leading to predicted C_nn values of C_nn^- = 0.91 or C_nn^+ = 0.09.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Tomasi-Gustafsson and Rekalo (2002) for polarization phenomena in hadronic processes near threshold, and builds on earlier analyses of vector meson production by Rekalo, Arvieux, and Tomasi-Gustafsson (1997) and strange particle production by the same authors (1997). The paper provides model-independent predictions that can be tested at COSY, where complete experiments (measurement of unpolarized cross section, spin correlation coefficients, and polarization transfer coefficients) could determine the energy dependence of partial wave amplitudes, discriminate between K-exchange and pi-exchange mechanisms in strange particle production, and extract information on the Lambda N scattering lengths, complementing microscopic calculations from Julich (Haidenbauer, 2002).
