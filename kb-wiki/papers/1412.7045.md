# Status of the Thermal Model and Chemical Freeze-Out

> A brief review is presented of the status of the thermal model and chemical freeze-out in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Some interewsting aspects at lower energies are emphasized.

# Status of the Thermal Model and Chemical Freeze-Out

## Key claim
The thermal-statistical model successfully describes particle yields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions across a wide range of beam energies, but a gap in data near 11 GeV center-of-mass energy prevents clarifying whether the observed sharp variations in particle ratios (e.g., the "horn" in K+/π+) correspond to a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated hadronic gas or to a more exotic phase such as a quarkyonic phase.

## Method
The author reviews the extraction of chemical freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB from experimental particle yields using the thermal-statistical model, comparing results from multiple parameterizations (e.g., Wheaton, Becattini). The freeze-out curve is mapped onto the energy-density vs. net-baryon-density plane following Randrup (2006), and the entropy density divided by T³ is computed to identify the transition between baryon-dominated and meson-dominated regimes.

## Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at ALICE: 155 ± 2 MeV, slightly lower than expected.
- Transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated hadronic gas occurs at T = 151 MeV, μB = 327 MeV, corresponding to √sNN = 11 GeV.
- The s/T³ ratio is constant over the entire energy range, with the transition visible as a change in the relative contributions of mesons and baryons.

## Context
This review extends the thermal-model analysis of chemical freeze-out, which began with early work by Fermi, Hagedorn, and Koppe, and was applied to heavy-ion collisions by Cleymans and Satz (1993) and Becattini (1996). The paper highlights a gap in data around 11 GeV that will be addressed by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, FAIR, and NICA. The observed sharpness of particle-ratio peaks (e.g., K+/π+ from NA49) exceeds thermal-model predictions, motivating future measurements of strangeness content over full 4π phase space to test for a possible quarkyonic phase as discussed by McLerran (2014).
