# Shear viscosity $η$ to electric conductivity $σ_{el}$ ratio for the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The transport coefficients of strongly interacting matter are currently subject of intense theoretical and phenomenological studies due to their relevance for the characterization of the quark-gluon plasma produced in ultra relativistic heavy-ion collisions (uRHIC). We discuss the connection between the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, and the electric conductivity, $σ_{el}$. Once the relaxation time is tuned to have a minimum value of $η/s=1/4π$ near the critical temperature $T_c$, one simultaneously predicts $σ_{el}/T$ very close to recent lQCD data. More generally, we discuss why the ratio of $(η/s)/(σ_{el}/T)$ supplies a measure of the quark to gluon scattering rates whose knowledge would allow to significantly advance in the understanding of the QGP phase. We also predict that $(η/s)/(σ_{el}/T)$, independently on the running coupling $α_s(T)$, should increase up to about $\sim 20$ for $T \rightarrow T_c$, while it goes down to a nearly flat behavior around $\simeq 4$ for $T \geq 4\, T_c$. Therefore we in general predict a stronger T dependence of $σ_{el}/T$ with respect to $η/s$ that in a quasiparticle approach is constrained by lQCD thermodynamics. A conformal theory, instead, predicts a similar T dependence of $η/s$ and $σ_{el}/T$.

# Shear viscosity $\eta$ to electric conductivity $\sigma_{el}$ ratio for the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The ratio $(\eta/s)/(\sigma_{el}/T)$ provides a measure of the relative quark to gluon scattering rates that is independent of the running coupling $\alpha_s(T)$, and this ratio should increase to about 20 as temperature approaches $T_c$ while flattening to about 4 for $T \geq 4\,T_c$, but only if the quasiparticle approach constrained by lattice QCD thermodynamics is valid.

## Method

The authors employ a quasiparticle (QP) model tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics, with temperature-dependent quark and gluon masses given by $m^2_g = 3/4\,g^2 T^2$ and $m^2_q = 1/3\,g^2 T^2$, where the running coupling $g(T)$ is fitted to the lattice energy density. Transport coefficients are computed using the relaxation time approximation (RTA), with relaxation times determined from transport cross sections regulated by a Debye screening mass $m_D = g(T)T$ and the pQCD-like cross section formula $\sigma_{tr}^{ij}(s) = \beta^{ij}\frac{\pi \alpha^2_s}{m_D^2}\frac{s}{s+m_D^2}h(a)$. The shear viscosity and electric conductivity are then evaluated from Green-Kubo relations expressed in terms of these relaxation times and thermodynamic averages.

## Key numbers

- The minimum value of $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$ (approximately 0.08) near the critical temperature $T_c$ is used to tune the relaxation time.
- The ratio $(\eta/s)/(\sigma_{el}/T)$ increases up to about 20 for $T \rightarrow T_c$, and goes down to a nearly flat behavior around 4 for $T \geq 4\,T_c$.
- The critical temperature is $T_c \simeq 160$ MeV.
- The relaxation times obtained are $\tau_g \simeq \tau_q/2 \sim 0.2$ fm/c.
- The QP model coupling parametrization uses $\lambda = 2.6$ and $T_s/T_c = 0.57$, valid only for $T > 1.1\,T_c$.
- The pQCD Casimir coefficients are $C^{q}|_{pQCD} = 28/9 \simeq 3.1$ and $C^{g}|_{pQCD} = 9/2$.
- The ratio of gluon to quark relaxation times in the pQCD case is $\tau_g/\tau_q \sim 0.3-0.4$.
- In the massless limit, the gluon to quark density ratio is $\rho_g/\rho_q^{tot} \simeq d_g/d_{q+\bar q} = 4/9$.
- The electric conductivity from lattice QCD data is approximately $\sigma_{el} \simeq 10^{-2} T$ for $T < 2\,T_c$.

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients for the quark-gluon plasma produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, connecting the well-studied shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ with the much less studied electric conductivity $\sigma_{el}$. The authors build on prior quasiparticle models (Plumari et al., 2011; Levai et al., 1997; Peshier et al., 2002; Bluhm et al., 2010) and the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM) of Cassing (2009), and compare their results with lattice QCD data from multiple groups (Gupta, Aarts, Amato, Ding, Brandt). The ratio $(\eta/s)/(\sigma_{el}/T)$ is identified as a quantity that can discriminate between conformal theories (such as AdS/CFT from Kovtun et al., 2004, and Huot et al., 2006) which predict similar temperature dependence for both coefficients, and non-conformal QCD where the interaction measure drives a stronger temperature dependence for $\sigma_{el}/T$. Future lattice QCD evaluations of this ratio could provide insight into the relative role of quarks and gluons in the QGP phase.
