# Theta^+ baryon production in KN and NN reactions

> We study $Œò^+(1540)$ productions in kaon-nucleon ($KN$) and nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions by assuming that the $Œò^+$ is an isosinglet with $J^P = \frac12^+$. Possible $t$-channel diagrams with $K^*$ exchange are considered in both reactions as well as $K$ exchange in $NN$ reaction. The cross section for $np \to Œõ^0 Œò^+$, which has not been considered in previous calculations, is found to be about a factor of 5 larger than that for $np \to Œ£^0 Œò^+$ due to the large coupling of $KNŒõ$ interaction. We also find that the isospin relations hold for these reactions.

## Key claim

The cross sections for $\Theta^+(1540)$ production in $KN$ and $NN$ reactions are calculated under the assumption that the $\Theta^+$ is an isosinglet with $J^P = \frac12^+$, with the coupling $g_{KN\Theta}$ set to 1 and the ratio $g_{K^*N\Theta}/g_{KN\Theta}$ treated as a free parameter, so that future experimental data can be used to estimate these couplings.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for $KN \to \pi\Theta^+$ and $NN \to Y\Theta^+$ (with $Y = \Lambda^0$ or $\Sigma$) reactions, including $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange in both reactions and $K$ exchange in the $NN$ reaction. Effective Lagrangians based on SU(3) symmetry for the baryon anti-decuplet are used, with coupling constants for known vertices taken from the literature and form factors applied with cutoff parameters $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV and $1.8$ GeV. The cross sections are presented for various values of the ratio $g_{K^*N\Theta}/g_{KN\Theta}$.

- The cross section for $np \to \Lambda^0 \Theta^+$ is about a factor of 5 larger than that for $np \to \Sigma^0 \Theta^+$.
- Setting $g_{KN\Theta} = 1$ corresponds to a $\Theta^+$ decay width of approximately 1.03 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Lin and Ko (2003) for $\Theta^+$ production in $KN$ and $NN$ reactions by including $K^*$ exchange contributions and by considering the $np \to \Lambda^0 \Theta^+$ channel, which was not studied before. The results enable future experimental data to determine the unknown $K^*N\Theta$ coupling and provide input for estimating $\Theta^+$ yields in heavy-ion collisions, where hadronic final-state effects are important.
