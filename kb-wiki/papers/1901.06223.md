# Nucleon resonance production in the $γp \to pηϕ$ reaction

> In this work, we perform a study of nucleon resonance production in the $γp \to pηϕ$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach. In our model, we consider the excitation of the $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$ and $N^*(1720)$ in the intermediate state and the background term. We find that this reaction is dominated by the excitation of the $N^*(1535)$ in the near threshold region. Especially, we study the possible role of the scalar meson exchange in this reaction. It is found that the $f_0(980)$ exchange may give a significant contribution and the parity asymmetry can be used to identify its role in this reaction.

## Key claim

The reaction $\gamma p \to p\eta\phi$ is dominated by the excitation of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance in the near-threshold region, and the scalar meson $f_0(980)$ exchange may give a significant contribution to this process; the parity asymmetry $P_\sigma$ can be used to identify the role of the $f_0$ exchange, but the magnitude of the $f_0$ contribution depends on the poorly known $N^*(1535)Nf_0$ coupling constant and the cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{f_0}$.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach to calculate the total and differential cross sections for the $\gamma p \to p\eta\phi$ reaction. They consider Feynman diagrams with $\pi$, $\eta$, and $f_0(980)$ meson exchanges for the excitation of the $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$ resonances in the intermediate state, plus a background $t$-channel $\rho$ exchange term. Coupling constants are extracted from experimental decay widths, and form factors with cutoff parameters are introduced to account for the finite size of the hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The $N^*(1535)$ dominates the total cross section near threshold (beam energy $E_\gamma$ just above 2.5 GeV), with a total width of 150 MeV and branching ratios of 0.42 to $N\pi$ and 0.42 to $N\eta$.
- The coupling constant $g_{\pi NN}$ is taken as 13.45; the $\phi f_0\gamma$ coupling squared is $g_{\phi f_0\gamma}^2 = 1.73$ (from $\phi \to f_0\gamma$ branching ratio $3.2 \times 10^{-4}$).
- With $\Lambda_{f_0} = 1.3$ GeV, the $f_0$ exchange contribution is comparable to the $\eta$ exchange contribution; with $\Lambda_{f_0} = 1.8$ GeV, the parity asymmetry $P_\sigma$ reaches about 0.8, while without $f_0$ exchange $P_\sigma$ approaches -1.
- The $N^*(1535)Nf_0$ coupling is estimated using $|g_{N^*(1535)N\sigma}| = 2.55$ as a substitute, since the $N^*(1535)Nf_0$ coupling is unknown.

## Context

This work extends studies of nucleon resonances in multi-meson photoproduction, following the approach used for $\gamma p \to \phi K^+\Lambda$ by Lv et al. (2017) and Liu et al. (2017), where the $N^*(1535)K\Lambda$ coupling was investigated. The authors note that vector meson exchange is forbidden by C-parity conservation, and they focus on scalar $f_0(980)$ exchange, which was usually ignored in previous studies. The use of spin density matrix elements and parity asymmetry to identify scalar exchange was inspired by the work of Schilling (1970) and Tannoudji (1965), and previously applied to $\kappa$ meson exchange by Oh et al. (2006, 2012). The results enable experimental searches for the $N^*(1535)Nf_0$ coupling and tests of the molecular $K\bar K$ interpretation of the $f_0(980)$.
