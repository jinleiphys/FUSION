# Formation time of hadrons in photon induced reactions on nuclei

> We present a way to account for coherence length effects in a semi-classical transport model. This allows us to describe photo- and electroproduction at large nuclei (A>12) and high energies using a realistic coupled channel description of the final state interactions that goes far beyond simple Glauber theory. We show that the purely absorptive treatment of the final state interactions as usually done in simple Glauber theory might lead to wrong estimates of color transparency and formation time effects in particle production. As an example we discuss exclusive rho^0 photoproduction on Pb at a photon energy of 7 GeV as well as K^+ and K^- production on C and Pb in the photon energy range 1-7 GeV.

# Formation time of hadrons in photon induced reactions on nuclei

## Key claim
The purely absorptive treatment of final state interactions in simple Glauber theory can lead to incorrect estimates of color transparency and formation time effects in particle production; a semi-classical transport model with coupled channel final state interactions reveals that apparent signatures of finite formation time can be mimicked by side-feeding processes even with zero formation time, particularly when kinematic cuts are not chosen properly.

## Method
The authors use a semi-classical transport model based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation to describe final state interactions in photon-induced reactions on nuclei. They implement coherence length effects (shadowing) by using vector meson dominance and Glauber theory to calculate how the vector meson components of the photon change due to multiple scattering before the primary reaction, then propagate the final state particles through the nuclear medium using the BUU transport model with a coupled channel description that includes particle creation, absorption, and side-feeding.

## Key numbers
- Exclusive rho^0 photoproduction on Pb at photon energy 7 GeV: the mass differential cross section shows that a calculation with formation time tau_f = 0.8 fm/c gives a result similar to a calculation with tau_f = 0 but including inclusive primary reactions such as gamma N -> rho^0 pi N
- For K^+ and K^- production on C and Pb in the photon energy range 1-7 GeV: at 7 GeV, shadowing reduces the nuclear production cross section to about 75% for Carbon and 65% for Lead
- Formation length estimates: for tau_f = 0.8 fm/c, the formation length is about 30 fm for a 5 GeV pion, about 7 fm for a 5 GeV kaon or a 7 GeV rho meson; nuclear radii are 2.7 fm for C-12 and 7.1 fm for Pb-208
- For exclusive rho^0 production on Pb at E_gamma = 7 GeV, Glauber theory becomes applicable only for |t| > 0.1 GeV^2, while coherent rho^0 photoproduction can be neglected above |t| = 0.05 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends earlier transport model studies of inclusive photoproduction by Effenberger et al. (2000) and Falter et al. (2000, 2001) by introducing a new method to implement shadowing effects in the semi-classical framework. It contrasts with simple Glauber theory approaches (Glauber 1959, Yennie 1971) that treat final state interactions as purely absorptive, showing that such treatments can misidentify formation time effects. The results enable more reliable extraction of formation times from experiments at TJNAF and DESY, particularly for the HERMES experiment (Ackermann 2000) studying exclusive vector meson production at photon energies between 10 and 20 GeV, and suggest future applications to charm production where similar final state interaction effects as seen for K^+ are expected for the anti-D meson.
