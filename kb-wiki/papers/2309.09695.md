# Triangle and box diagrams in coupled-channel systems from the chiral Lagrangian

> We perform an analysis of triangle- and box-loop contributions to the generalized potential in the scattering of Goldstone bosons off the J^P= 0^- and 1^- charmed mesons. Particular emphasis is put on the use of on-shell mass parameters in such contributions in terms of a renormalization scheme that ensures the absence of power-counting violating terms. This is achieved with a systematically extended set of Passarino--Veltman basis functions, that leads to manifest power-counting conserving one-loop expressions and avoids the occurrence of superficial kinematical singularities. Compact expressions to chiral order three and four are presented that are particularly useful in coding such coupled-channel systems. Our formal results are generic and prepare analogous computations for other systems, like meson-baryon scattering from the chiral Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic renormalization scheme for triangle- and box-loop contributions to the generalized potential in Goldstone boson scattering off charmed mesons, ensuring that power-counting violating terms are absent when on-shell hadron masses are used inside loop expressions, which previous approaches (such as the extended-on-mass-shell scheme) could not achieve without using bare masses.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop triangle and box diagrams at chiral orders three and four for the scattering of Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) off charmed mesons with quantum numbers J^P = 0^- and 1^-, using the chiral Lagrangian with explicit symmetry breaking terms. They employ a systematically extended set of Passarino-Veltman basis functions that preserves manifest power-counting and avoids superficial kinematical singularities, while keeping the scalar basis functions unexpanded to maintain proper causal structure. The loop contributions are decomposed into s-channel and u-channel pole terms with on-shell masses and a smooth background term, with the background term receiving the loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant |g_P| = 0.57 ± 0.07 from the charged D* meson decay
- The ratio r = (M̃² - M²)/M² ≈ 0.16, where M and M̃ are the chiral limit masses of the 0^- and 1^- charmed mesons respectively
- The renormalization group running of the low-energy constants L₄ and L₅: μ² d/dμ² L₄ = -1/(256π²), μ² d/dμ² L₅ = -3/(256π²)
- The renormalization group running of the wave-function LEC ζ₀ and ζ₁: μ² d/dμ² ζ₀ = -13g_P²/(384π²f²), μ² d/dμ² ζ₁ = -5g_P²/(128π²f²)

## Context

This work extends the generalized potential approach (GPA) developed by Gasparyan (2010), Danilkin (2010-2011), and Gasparyan (2012) to include triangle and box diagrams that had not been studied with sufficient rigor from the chiral Lagrangian. Previous coupled-channel studies in the open-charm sector by Kolomeitsev (2003), Hofmann (2003), Lutz (2007), and others focused on s-wave interactions only and ignored the quark-mass dependence of charmed meson masses, while works by Yao (2015) and Du (2017) provided first estimates of such effects but only for s-wave scattering. The results enable more quantitative coupled-channel computations for meson-baryon scattering systems, where lattice QCD data is significantly more scarce.
