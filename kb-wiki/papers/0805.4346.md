# On AdS/QCD correspondence and the partonic picture of deep inelastic scattering

> We critically examine the question of scaling of the Deep Inelastic Scattering process in the medium Bjorken x region on a scalar boson in the framework of the AdS/QCD correspondence. To get the right polarization structure of the forward electroproduction amplitude, we show that one needs to add (at least) the scalar to scalar and scalar to vector hadronic amplitudes. This illustrates how the partonic picture may emerge from a simple scenario based on the AdS/QCD correspondence, provided one allows the conformal dimension of the hadronic field to equal 1 and use the concept of "hadron - parton duality" .

## Key claim

The AdS/QCD correspondence can reproduce the partonic picture of deep inelastic scattering (scaling and the Callan-Gross relation) only if one allows the conformal dimension of the hadronic field to equal 1 and sums over both scalar and vector final states, with a fine-tuned superposition of their amplitudes.

## Method

The authors computed the forward Compton scattering amplitude for a virtual photon on a scalar hadron in the hard-wall AdS/QCD model, first with a scalar intermediate state (as in Polchinski and Strassler (2002)) and then with a vector intermediate state. They derived the structure functions F1 and F2 from the hadronic tensor, then imposed the Callan-Gross relation F2 = 2xF1 to determine the conditions under which the AdS/QCD result matches the parton model.

## Key numbers

- The structure function F2 for scalar intermediate states scales as (Λ/Q)^(2Δ0−2), where Δ0 is the conformal dimension of the initial hadron; for Δ0 = 2 (meson), this gives F2 ∝ (Λ/Q)^2, missing the scaling behavior of DIS.
- For Δ0 = 1, the Q^2 dependence becomes F2 ∝ (Λ/Q)^0, i.e., Bjorken scaling (up to logarithmic modifications).
- The density of states in the hard-wall model is ∑_n δ(s−m_n^2) ≈ (2π√s Λ)^(−1), with Λ = 1/z_∞ the infrared cutoff.
- The vector intermediate state structure functions also scale as (Λ/Q)^(2Δ0−2), independent of the vector conformal dimension Δ_V.

## Context

This work critically examines the AdS/QCD approach to DIS initiated by Polchinski and Strassler (2002), who found scaling behavior but an incorrect tensor structure (F1 = 0). The authors extend that analysis by including vector final states, motivated by the concept of quark-hadron duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970; Dokshitzer et al., 1991). They show that with Δ0 = 1 and a tuned superposition of scalar and vector amplitudes, the Callan-Gross relation can be recovered, enabling a partonic interpretation. This contrasts with the small-x regime studied by Ciafaloni and Colferai (2005), where Δ0 = 2 yields geometric scaling.
