# Chemical thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We compute by numerical integration of the Dirac equation the number of quark-antiquark pairs initially produced in the classical color fields of colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei. While the number of qqbar pairs is parametrically suppressed in the coupling constant, we find that in this classical field model their production rate is comparable to the thermal ratio of gluons/pairs = 9 N_f/32. After isotropization one thus would have quark-gluon plasma in chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

While quark-antiquark pair production is parametrically suppressed in the coupling constant, the authors find that in this classical color field model the production rate is comparable to the thermal ratio of gluons to pairs (9 N_f / 32), suggesting that after isotropization one could have a quark-gluon plasma in chemical equilibrium. The hedge is that this result depends on the classical field model and the specific numerical value of the coupling constant (g=2, alpha_s=0.3) used in the computation.

## Method

The authors numerically integrate the Dirac equation for quark spinors in the classical color fields of colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei, which are obtained by solving the Yang-Mills equations on a transverse lattice. They compute the overlap between a negative energy antiquark spinor evolved forward in time from the distant past and a positive energy quark spinor, using the 2-dimensional Coulomb gauge, to obtain the number of quark-antiquark pairs per unit rapidity. The computation is performed on a N_T^2 N_L lattice with N_T=180, N_L=400, using a transverse lattice spacing a=11.8 fm/N_T and proper time step d_tau=0.02a.

## Key numbers

- For g^2 mu = 2 GeV and 3 flavors, about 600 quark-antiquark pairs would be produced per unit rapidity (from Fig. 1, "inftimelim").
- For g^2 mu = 2 GeV, the gluon number grows rapidly to 0.29 pi g^2 mu^2 R_A^2 ~ 1000 by the time 1/(g^2 mu) ~ 0.1 fm/c.
- The saturation scale Q_s is approximately 1 GeV at RHIC energies and approximately 2 GeV at LHC energies.
- The computation uses a transverse lattice with N_T=180, N_L=400, d_z = 0.2a, d_tau = 0.02a, requiring 1.2 GB of memory.
- The number of timesteps to reach tau = 0.25 fm is of order 500.
- For chemical thermal equilibrium, 1000 particles per unit rapidity should consist of 330 gluons and 330 pairs, corresponding to g^2 mu ~ 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the classical color field (color glass condensate) approach to include quark-antiquark pair production, which had previously been applied only to gluon production (Krasnitz and Venugopalan, 2000; Lappi, 2003). It contradicts the conventional assumption that the initial state of a heavy ion collision is dominated by gluons, as assumed in perturbative 2-to-2 parton collision models (Eskola et al., 1999). The result enables further study of chemical equilibration in the early stages of heavy ion collisions and suggests that thermal dilepton radiation in the 2 GeV mass range at LHC energies may be more observable.
