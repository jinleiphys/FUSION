# Instabilities in Nuclei

> The evolution of dynamical perturbations is examined in nuclear multifragmentation in the frame of Vlasov equation. Both plane wave and bubble type of perturbations are investigated in the presence of surface (Yukawa) forces. An energy condition is given for the allowed type of instabilities and the time scale of the exponential growth of the instabilities is calculated. The results are compared to the mechanical spinodal region predictions. PACS: 25.70 Mn

## Key claim

The paper shows that dynamical instabilities in expanding nuclear matter can grow after freeze-out only when the system penetrates into the isothermal spinodal region, and the growth rate depends on the surface diffuseness and bubble radius. The authors hedge that the instability condition derived from the Vlasov equation coincides with the mechanical spinodal only in the limit of infinite droplet radius or when surface terms are neglected.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Vlasov equation for a spherically expanding nuclear system after freeze-out, using a Fermi distribution boosted by a scaling flow. They examine both plane-wave and spherical bubble perturbations, incorporating a Skyrme mean-field potential with a Yukawa surface term. The dispersion relation is derived for each perturbation type, and the energy change due to perturbation formation is evaluated to determine whether instabilities grow or are damped.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density chosen as \(n_{fr} = 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for the calculations.
- Critical bubble radii considered in the physical parameter region: \(R^* \approx 2\)–\(3\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Surface diffuseness parameter range: \(k \approx 4\)–\(6\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- Growth parameter \(\kappa \approx 0.06\) yields a time scale of approximately \(20\ \mathrm{fm}/c\) for the expansion of the perturbation radius.
- SOFT EOS parameters: \(\beta = 1051.76\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\), \(\gamma = 1107.41\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^{3(\sigma+1)}\), \(\sigma = 1/6\), \(V_0 = 83.5\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}\), \(\mu = 2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- HARD EOS parameters: \(\beta = 365.0\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\), \(\gamma = 808.65\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^{3(\sigma+1)}\), \(\sigma = 1\), \(V_0 = 83.5\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}\), \(\mu = 2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the plane-wave instability analysis of Randrup and Møller (1992) and Miskowiec (1993) to spherical bubble perturbations, which are more realistic for nuclear multifragmentation. The results are compared to the mechanical spinodal region predictions for infinite nuclear matter. The paper enables future calculations of break-up times and fragment sizes in expanding nuclear systems, and it provides a framework for distinguishing dynamical post-freeze-out instabilities from slower thermally driven nucleation processes.
