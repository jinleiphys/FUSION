# On the chromoelectric permittivity and Debye screening in hot QCD

> We study the response functions (chromo-electric susceptibilities) for an interacting quark-gluon plasma. The interaction effects have been encoded in the effective fugacities for quasi-partons which are extracted self-consistently from the two equations of state for hot QCD. The first one is the fully perturbative $O(g^5)$ EOS and, the second one which is $O(g^6\ln(1/g))$, incorporates some non-perturbative effects. We find that response function shows large deviations from the ideal behavior. We further determine the temperature dependence of the Debye mass by fixing the effective coupling constant $Q^2$ which appears in the transport equation. We show that our formalism naturally yields the leading order HTL expression for the Debye mass if we employ the ideal EOS. Employing the Debye mass, we estimate the dissociation temperatures for various charmonium and bottomonium bound states. These results are consistent with the current theoretical studies.

## Key claim

The interaction effects in hot QCD, encoded via effective fugacities for quasi-partons extracted from non-ideal equations of state, significantly reduce the chromo-electric susceptibilities and Debye mass relative to the ideal (leading-order HTL) values, with the reduction approaching zero only asymptotically at high temperature.

## Method

The authors map two hot QCD equations of state (EOS1, a fully perturbative O(g⁵) EOS, and EOSδ, an O(g⁶ ln(1/g)) EOS that incorporates non-perturbative effects) into effective fugacities z_g and z_q for quasi-gluons and quasi-quarks. These fugacities are then inserted into the equilibrium distribution functions used in a semi-classical Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation in extended phase space (including SU(3) color degrees of freedom) to compute the Abelian and non-Abelian chromo-electric permittivities. The Debye mass is obtained from the static limit of the permittivity, and dissociation temperatures for quarkonia are estimated using the criterion that a bound state dissociates when its rms radius exceeds the Debye screening length.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical transport approach of Ranjan and Ravishankar (2007) from ideal partons to interacting QGP by incorporating the quasi-particle description of hot QCD equations of state developed by Chandra, Kumar, and Ravishankar (2007). It provides a method to compute Debye screening and quarkonia dissociation temperatures that are consistent with lattice results of Karsch (2002) and Zantow and Kaczmarek (2005), and with theoretical estimates by Satz (2005) and Alberico et al. (2007). The approach enables phenomenological predictions for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by linking the effective coupling in transport equations to lattice and experimental observables.
