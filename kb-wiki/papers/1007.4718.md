# Transverse hydrodynamics and the early-thermalization problem at RHIC

> The problem of early thermalization of matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC is discussed in the framework of a hybrid model that consists of the transverse-hydrodynamics stage followed by the standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics stage and freeze-out. The two hydrodynamic regimes are connected with the help of Landau matching conditions. A satisfactory description of the soft hadronic observables is achieved. This indicates that the early-thermalization problem and also the HBT puzzle may be to large extent circumvented.

## Key claim
A hybrid model combining an initial transverse-hydrodynamics stage (where only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized) with a subsequent perfect-fluid hydrodynamics stage can satisfactorily describe soft hadronic observables at RHIC, indicating that the early-thermalization problem and the HBT puzzle may be "to large extent circumvented" rather than solved, with the caveat that proton elliptic flow is overestimated by about 50%.

## Method
The system evolves from initialization time τᵢ to transition time τₜᵣ under transverse hydrodynamics, then undergoes a step-like transition to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics via Landau matching conditions (energy and momentum conserved, entropy required to increase). After the transition, the system evolves as a perfect fluid until freeze-out on a constant-temperature hypersurface at T₃f = 140 MeV, with observables computed using the THERMINATOR statistical hadronization Monte-Carlo code.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature T₂ᵢ = 530 MeV for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, centrality class 20-30%
- Freeze-out temperature T₃f = 140 MeV
- Fraction of binary collisions in initial source density profile: 0.25
- Density of transverse clusters in rapidity n₀ = 0.43
- Transverse-momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons reproduced at the level of 16% agreement with PHENIX data (Adler 2003)
- Elliptic flow v₂ for pions+kaons reproduced at the level of 21% agreement with PHENIX data (Adler 2003)
- Proton v₂ overestimates PHENIX data by about 50%
- All three pionic HBT radii reproduced with accuracy better than 10% compared to STAR data (Adams 2004)

## Context
This work extends the transverse-hydrodynamics approach of Bialas (1999), Bialas (2007), Chojnacki (2007), Ryblewski (2009), and Florkowski (2009) by adding an intermediate perfect-fluid hydrodynamics stage rather than a sudden isotropization and freeze-out. The model addresses both the early-thermalization problem and the HBT puzzle simultaneously, where previous perfect-fluid models (Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003, Shuryak 2004, Teaney 2001, Hama 2005, Hirano 2007, Nonaka 2006, Bozek 2009) assumed full thermalization from early times. The results enable future work replacing the perfect-fluid stage with viscous hydrodynamics, constructing a continuous transition from transverse to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (as begun by Florkowski 2010), and including hadronic rescattering after freeze-out to correct the proton v₂ overestimate.
