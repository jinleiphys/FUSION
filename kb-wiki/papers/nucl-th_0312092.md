# Case of Almost Redundant Components in 3 alpha Faddeev Equations

> The 3 alpha orthogonality condition model using the Pauli-forbidden bound states of the Buck, Friedlich and Wheatly alpha alpha potential can yield a compact 3 alpha ground state with a large binding energy, in which a small admixture of the redundant components can never be eliminated.

## Key claim

The 3-alpha orthogonality condition model using the Buck-Friedlich-Wheatly potential can yield a compact 3-alpha ground state with a large binding energy, but this solution necessarily contains a small admixture of redundant (Pauli-forbidden) components on the order of 10^-4 that cannot be eliminated without losing the dominant shell-model-like [3](04) component.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the 3-alpha system using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) with the Buck-Friedlich-Wheatly (BFW) alpha-alpha potential, whose Pauli-forbidden bound states are the lowest two S-wave and one D-wave states. They analyze the eigenvalue equation of the rearrangement matrix to identify [21]-symmetric redundant solutions and construct a modified Faddeev equation that incorporates an extra term for the almost-redundant component. The ground-state energy is determined by solving this modified equation and comparing with direct variational calculations using translationally invariant harmonic oscillator basis states.

## Key numbers

- The 3-alpha ground-state energy obtained with the BFW potential is -19.897 MeV (with Coulomb force) and -27.748 MeV (without Coulomb force), in contrast to variational results of -0.26 MeV and -6.003 MeV respectively from Tursunov, Baye, Descouvemont and Daniel (2003)
- The norm of the redundant component in the ground state, N_R = sum_u ⟨f_u|f_u⟩, is (2.6 to 2.7) × 10^-4 for the BFW Pauli-forbidden states, compared to N_R ∼ 10^-12 for harmonic oscillator Pauli-forbidden states
- The eigenvalue τ for the second [21]4(40) dominant solution is -0.999037, and the overlap of the resulting [3]-symmetric state φ^[3]_τ with the shell-model [3]8(04) state has amplitude 0.865401
- The ground-state energy obtained from the modified Faddeev equation is -27.625 MeV, close to the exact value -27.748 MeV
- The amplitude of the φ^[3]_τ component in the total wave function Ψ is -0.8952, and the redundant component admixture ⟨f_u|f_u⟩ is 0.26 × 10^-3

## Context

This work extends the orthogonality condition model (OCM) formalism of Saito (1968) for 3-alpha systems and the authors' previous Faddeev formulation (2003), while contradicting the variational calculations of Tursunov, Baye, Descouvemont and Daniel (2003) who found much smaller binding energies. The paper shows that the BFW potential's bound-state solutions produce an "almost redundant" [21]-symmetric component that generates the physical [3](04) ground state, unlike the harmonic oscillator case where exact elimination is possible. This enables a precise formulation of the 3-alpha OCM equation that retains the compact shell-model-like ground state, and the authors argue that from a microscopic resonating-group method perspective, the small redundant component is physically acceptable.
