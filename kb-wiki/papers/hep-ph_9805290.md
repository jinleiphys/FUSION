# Meson Correlators at Finite Temperature

> We evaluate equal time point to point spatial correlation functions of mesonic currents at finite temperature. For this purpose we consider the QCD vacuum structure in terms of quark antiquark condensates and their fluctuations in terms of an irreducible four point structure of the vacuum. The temperature dependence of quark condensates is modeled using chiral perturbation theory for low temperatures and lattice QCD simulations near the critical temperature.We first consider the propagation of quarks in a condensate medium at finite temperature. We then determine the correlation functions in a hot medium. Parameters such as mass, coupling constant and threshold energy are deduced from the finite temperature correlators. We find that all of them decrease close to the critical temperature.

# Meson Correlators at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The authors evaluate equal-time point-to-point spatial correlation functions of mesonic currents at finite temperature using a nonperturbative QCD vacuum model with quark-antiquark condensates and irreducible four-point fluctuations, finding that the extracted masses, coupling constants, and threshold energies all decrease near the critical temperature of approximately 150 MeV, though the pion mass remains nearly constant up to that temperature.

## Method

The authors model the QCD vacuum using a condensate function with a Gaussian ansatz whose width becomes temperature dependent, determined by fitting the temperature-dependent quark condensate from chiral perturbation theory (low temperatures) and lattice QCD O(4) scaling behavior (near T_c). They compute the equal-time thermal quark propagator using thermofield dynamics, then construct meson correlation functions as the sum of a squared thermal propagator term and a four-point fluctuation term, where the fluctuation strength is scaled with the square of the condensate ratio. The resulting spatial correlation functions are fitted to a phenomenological spectral density parameterization to extract temperature-dependent masses, couplings, and continuum thresholds for the pion and rho meson channels.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature taken as T_c = 150 MeV, with order parameter scaling exponent beta = 0.39 for the O(4) critical behavior near T_c
- At T = 0 MeV, vector channel mass M_rho = 0.780 ± 0.005 GeV, coupling lambda = (0.420 ± 0.041 GeV)^2, threshold sqrt(s_0) = 2.070 ± 0.035 GeV
- At T = 144 MeV, vector channel mass M_rho = 0.679 ± 0.001 GeV, coupling lambda = (0.325 ± 0.064 GeV)^2, threshold sqrt(s_0) = 1.192 ± 0.024 GeV
- At T = 0 MeV, pseudoscalar channel mass M_pi = 0.136 ± 0.00014 GeV, coupling lambda = (0.473 ± 0.021 GeV)^2, threshold sqrt(s_0) = 2.110 ± 0.152 GeV
- At T = 150 MeV, pseudoscalar channel mass M_pi = 0.136 ± 0.00014 GeV (unchanged), coupling lambda = (0.131 ± 0.006 GeV)^2, threshold sqrt(s_0) = 0.777 ± 0.020 GeV
- The chirality flip component of the interacting quark propagator at T = 0 MeV reaches a maximum of about 250 MeV at a distance of about 0.9 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier zero-temperature nonperturbative approach (Sheel, Mishra, and Parikh, 1996) to finite temperature, where the QCD vacuum structure changes and chiral symmetry is restored around 150 MeV as indicated by lattice Monte Carlo simulations (Laermann, 1996). The temperature dependence of extracted parameters is qualitatively similar to that found by Hatsuda, Koike, and Lee (1993) using sum rule methods, and the near-constancy of the pion mass below T_c agrees with results from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1987). The results enable calculation of lepton pair production rates from rho mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions to estimate vector meson mass shifts in medium.
