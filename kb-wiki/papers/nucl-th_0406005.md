# W(5): Wobbling Mode in the Framework of the X(5) Model

> Using in the Bohr Hamiltonian the approximations leading to the Bohr and Mottelson description of wobbling motion in even nuclei, a W(5) model for wobbling bands, coexisting with a X(5) ground state band, is obtained. Separation of variables is achieved by assuming that the relevant potential has a sharp minimum at gamma_0, which is the only parameter entering in the spectra and B(E2) transition rates (up to overall scale factors). B(E2) transition rates exhibit the features expected in the wobbling case, while the spectrum for gamma_0=20 degrees is in good agreement with experimental data for Dy-156.

# W(5): Wobbling Mode in the Framework of the X(5) Model

## Key claim

The W(5) model describes wobbling bands in even nuclei that coexist with an X(5) ground state band, with the wobbling bands becoming yrast above a certain angular momentum that depends (weakly) on the single free parameter γ₀, the triaxiality angle at which the potential has a sharp minimum. The model is parameter-free up to overall scale factors for a given γ₀, but the approximation is valid only for large angular momenta L where the angular momentum is aligned along the axis of largest moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr Hamiltonian and achieve approximate separation of variables by assuming the potential has a sharp minimum at γ = γ₀, freezing γ at this value in the β-equation. The β-part is solved using an infinite well potential, yielding Bessel function solutions with order ν determined by L, n_w (wobbling phonon number), and γ₀. The γ-part is treated as a harmonic oscillator around γ₀. The ground state band is taken from the X(5) model (γ₀ = 0), while wobbling bands with n_w = 0, 1, 2 are obtained from the W(5) solution, with energies normalized to the X(5) ground state and first excited state.

## Key numbers

- For γ₀ = 15°, the n_w = 0 wobbling band crosses the X(5) ground state band above L = 12 and becomes yrast from L = 14 upward.
- For γ₀ = 20°, the n_w = 0 wobbling band crosses the X(5) ground state band above L = 8 and becomes yrast from L = 10 upward.
- The spectrum for γ₀ = 20° is in good agreement with experimental data for ¹⁵⁶Dy, where the ground state band matches X(5) predictions and two additional bands (K and M bands) correspond to the n_w = 0 and n_w = 1 W(5) bands.
- The ratio B(E2)out/B(E2)in for (n_w = 1) → (n_w = 0) transitions is expected to be of order 0.2–0.3, much larger than typical interband transitions.
- The B(E2)out values for (n_w = 1) → (n_w = 0) transitions are expected to scale as 1/L, not as 1/L².

## Context

This work extends the series of critical point symmetry models, E(5) (Iachello 2000), X(5) (Iachello 2001), Y(5) (Iachello 2003), and Z(5) (Bonatsos et al. 2004), to describe wobbling motion in even nuclei, following the Bohr and Mottelson (1975) description of wobbling. The model assumes a relatively rigid triaxial shape as in earlier work by Davydov and collaborators (Davydov and Filippov 1958, Davydov and Rostovsky 1959, Davydov 1961). The results enable comparison with experimental data for nuclei like ¹⁵⁶Dy, where the ground state band is well described by X(5), and suggest that wobbling bands may be identified in even nuclei through characteristic B(E2) patterns, including strong L → L+1 interband transitions and the 1/L scaling of B(E2)out values.
