# Phase transitions and symmetry energy in nuclear pasta

> Cold and isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities is known to form the so-called pasta structures, which, in turn, are known to undergo peculiar phase transitions. Here we investigate if such pastas and their phase changes survive in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, and whether the symmetry energy of such pasta configurations is connected to the isospin content, the morphology of the pasta and to the phase transitions. We find that indeed pastas are formed in isospin asymmetric systems with proton to neutron ratios of x=0.3, 0.4 and 0.5, densities in the range of 0.05 1/fm$^3$<$ρ$< 0.08 1/fm$^3$, and temperatures T<2 MeV. Using tools (such as the caloric curve, Lindemann coefficient, radial distribution function, Kolmogorov statistic, and Euler functional) on the composition of the pasta, determined the existence of homogeneous structures, tunnels, empty regions, cavities and transitions among these regions. The symmetry energy was observed to attain different values in the different phases showing its dependence on the morphology of the nuclear matter structure.

## Key claim

Pasta structures and their solid-liquid phase transitions survive in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, but the symmetry energy depends on the morphology of the pasta and attains different values in different phases, with the caveat that standard indicators (pressure, radial distribution function) may fail to detect pasta formation in asymmetric systems because excess neutrons obscure the signal when mean values are computed.

## Method

Classical Molecular Dynamics (CMD) simulations using the Pandharipande (Medium) pair potentials were performed on systems of 4000 or 6000 nucleons in a cubic cell with periodic boundary conditions, cooled from T = 4 MeV down to 0.2 MeV in steps of ΔT = 0.01 MeV using a Nosé-Hoover thermostat. The pasta structures were characterized using the caloric curve, Lindemann coefficient, radial distribution function, Kolmogorov statistic, and Minkowski functionals (Euler characteristic and integral mean curvature), while the symmetry energy was extracted by fitting the internal energy as a function of density and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Pasta structures form at densities in the range 0.05 fm⁻³ < ρ < 0.08 fm⁻³ and temperatures T < 2 MeV for proton fractions x = 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5.
- The solid-liquid phase transition within the pasta occurs close to T = 0.5 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter at ρ = 0.05 fm⁻³, signaled by a sharp change in the caloric curve and Lindemann coefficient.
- The cold nuclear matter saturation density used to set the Pandharipande potentials is ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with a binding energy E(ρ₀) = -16 MeV/nucleon and compressibility of about 250 MeV.
- The screening length for the Thomas-Fermi Coulomb potential in neutron star matter comparisons is λ = 20 fm, with a cutoff distance of 20 fm.

## Context

This work extends the low-density calculations of the symmetry energy from Lopez (2017) to lower temperatures (below 1 MeV) and connects them to the morphological and thermodynamic properties of pasta phases found by Dorso (2014) for symmetric nuclear matter, now investigating proton fractions from 30% to 50%. The results corroborate the Natowitz conjecture (Natowitz, 2015) that the asymptotic limit of the symmetry energy does not tend to zero at small densities as predicted by mean-field theories, and enable future studies of phase transitions and symmetry energy in asymmetric neutron star crusts, including the role of pasta structures in neutrino emission and neutron star cooling.
