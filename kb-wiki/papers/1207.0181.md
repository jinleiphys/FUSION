# Pion-Exchange and Fermi-Motion Effects on the Proton-Deuteron Drell-Yan Process

> Within a nuclear model that the deuteron has NN and πNN components, we derive convolution formula for investigating the Drell-Yan process in proton-deuteron (pd) reactions. The contribution from the πNN component is expressed in terms of a pion momentum distribution that depends sensitively on the πNN form factor. With a πNN form factor determined by fitting the πN scattering data up to invariant mass W = 1.3 GeV, we find that the pion-exchange and nucleon Fermi-motion effects can change significantly the ratios between the proton-deuteron and proton-proton Drell-Yan cross sections, R_{pd/pp} = σ^{pd}/(2σ^{pp}), in the region where the partons emitted from the target deuteron are in the Bjorken x_2 > 0.4 region. The calculated ratios R_{pd/pp} at 800 GeV agree with the available data. Predictions at 120 GeV for analyzing the forthcoming data from Fermilab are presented.

## Key claim

Within a nuclear model where the deuteron has both NN and πNN components, the pion-exchange and nucleon Fermi-motion effects can significantly change the ratio R_pd/pp = σ^pd/(2σ^pp) in the region where partons from the target deuteron have Bjorken x_2 > 0.4, but only when using a πNN form factor determined by fitting πN scattering data up to invariant mass W = 1.3 GeV.

## Method

The authors derived a convolution formula for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section using an impulse approximation, expressing the cross section in terms of the elementary q-anti-q → μ+μ- cross section and momentum distributions for nucleons and pions in the deuteron. The nucleon momentum distribution was calculated from the NN component of the deuteron wave function using realistic NN potentials, while the pion momentum distribution was computed from the πNN component using a πNN form factor constrained by fitting πN scattering data up to W = 1.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ratios R_pd/pp at 800 GeV agree with available data from Fermilab experiments
- Predictions at 120 GeV are presented for analyzing forthcoming data from Fermilab
- The πNN form factor was determined by fitting πN scattering data up to invariant mass W = 1.3 GeV
- The pion-exchange and Fermi-motion effects become significant in the region where Bjorken x_2 > 0.4

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of Drell-Yan data on deuteron targets (Peng 2001) that commonly neglected nuclear effects, despite such effects being known to be important in deep inelastic scattering analyses. The approach builds on πNN studies (Garcilazo) and uses realistic NN potentials (v14) to generate the NN component of the deuteron wave function. The results enable more precise extraction of the anti-d/anti-u ratio in the proton at higher x > 0.35, which will be needed to distinguish between different models of the proton's sea quark distributions in forthcoming Fermilab experiments (Peng 2011).
