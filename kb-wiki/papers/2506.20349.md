# Resonant Structures in $p{}^7\mathrm{Be}$ Scattering and Their Connection to the Astrophysical $S$-Factor

> In this paper, we employ the Variable Phase Approach (VPA) to obtain the scattering phase shifts \( δ(E, r) \), amplitude function \( A(r) \), and radial wavefunction \( u(r) \) for various channels involved in the astrophysical reaction \( {}^7\mathrm{Be}(p,γ)^8\mathrm{B} \). Using the extracted phase shifts, we compute the total and partial cross sections. It is observed that the peaks in the partial cross section correspond to resonant states in the compound nucleus, which also manifest as enhancements in the astrophysical S-factor. These resonances significantly increase the reaction probability at certain energies, particularly in the low-energy regime relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis. The VPA thus serves as a reliable and efficient method for calculating scattering phase shifts and, in turn, extracting the resonance energies of different partial waves. These resonance energies can provide valuable insight into the energy region where the astrophysical \textit{S}-factor is likely to peak.

## Key claim

The Variable Phase Approach (VPA) can identify the energy region where the astrophysical S-factor is expected to peak by extracting resonance energies from scattering phase shifts, but it cannot provide an exact calculation of the S-factor because it does not provide information about electromagnetic form factors or transition rates needed for the capture cross-section.

## Method

The authors employed the Variable Phase Approach (VPA), solving the first-order nonlinear Riccati-type differential equation for the phase function using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta method with the initial condition delta_ell(0) = 0. They used intercluster potentials of simple Gaussian form with a point-like Coulomb term, taking potential parameters directly from Dubovichenko et al. without optimization. From the extracted phase shifts, they computed the amplitude function A(r), radial wavefunction u(r), and total and partial cross sections for various channels in the p+^7Be system.

## Key numbers

- First resonance energy for the ^3P_1(R1) state: 0.565 MeV in center-of-mass (compared to experimental value 0.632 MeV)
- Second resonance energy for the ^3P_1(R2) state: 3.586 MeV in center-of-mass (compared to 3.16 MeV)
- ^3P_2 bound state excitation energy: 3.376 MeV (compared to 2.41 MeV), with a binding energy of -0.137500 MeV
- ^3D_2 resonance energy: 3.742 MeV in center-of-mass (compared to 3.66 MeV)
- ^3F_3 resonance energy: 2.523 MeV in center-of-mass (compared to 2.18 MeV)
- The most prominent resonance occurs around E_cm approximately 633 keV, corresponding to an excited state of ^8B at approximately 0.77 MeV excitation energy
- Masses used: M_Be-7 = 6534.19 MeV/c^2, M_p = 938.272046 MeV/c^2; hbar = 197.3269718 MeV.fm

## Context

This work extends the application of the Variable Phase Approach, previously applied by the author to systems such as n-n, n-p, n-alpha, n-d, alpha-alpha, and alpha-^3He scattering, to the astrophysically important p+^7Be system. The authors use potential parameters directly from Dubovichenko et al. without optimization, focusing on whether resonance energies alone can extract meaningful information relevant to the S-factor. The work enables identification of energy regions where the S-factor is likely to peak, which is important for extrapolating reaction rates to solar energies where direct measurements are challenging due to the instability of ^8B and ^7Be and limited intensity of radioactive beams.
