# Sensitivity of Au+Au collisions to the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state at 2 -- 5 nuclear saturation densities

> We demonstrate that proton and pion flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions at incident energies ranging from 1 to 20 GeV per nucleon in the fixed target frame can be used for an accurate determination of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state at baryon densities equal 2--4 times nuclear saturation density $n_0$. We simulate Au+Au collisions at these energies using a hadronic transport model with an adjustable vector mean-field potential dependent on baryon density $n_B$. We show that the mean field can be parametrized to reproduce a given density-dependence of the speed of sound at zero temperature $c_s^2(n_B, T = 0)$, which we vary independently in multiple density intervals to probe the differential sensitivity of heavy-ion observables to the equation of state at these specific densities. Recent flow data from the STAR experiment at the center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = \{3.0, 4.5 \}\ $ GeV can be described by our model, and a Bayesian analysis of these data indicates a hard equation of state at $n_B \in (2,3) n_0$ and a possible phase transition at $n_B \in (3,4) n_0$. More data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2-5$ GeV, as well as a more thorough analysis of the model systematic uncertainties will be necessary for a more precise conclusion.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that proton and pion flow measurements in Au+Au collisions at incident energies of 1–20 GeV per nucleon in the fixed target frame can constrain the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state at baryon densities of 2–4 times nuclear saturation density, but they caution that more data at center-of-mass energies of 2–5 GeV and a more thorough analysis of model systematic uncertainties are necessary for a more precise conclusion.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport code SMASH (version 2.1) with an adjustable vector mean-field potential dependent on baryon density to simulate Au+Au collisions. They parametrized the mean field to reproduce a given density dependence of the speed of sound at zero temperature, varying the speed of sound independently in multiple density intervals to probe differential sensitivity of heavy-ion observables to the equation of state at specific densities. A Bayesian analysis was performed comparing simulated flow observables to recent STAR data at center-of-mass energies of 3.0 and 4.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Bayesian analysis of STAR flow data indicates a speed of sound squared of \(c_s^2[n_B \in (2,3)n_0] = 0.47 \pm 0.12\) for baryon densities between 2 and 3 times nuclear saturation density
- Bayesian analysis indicates a speed of sound squared of \(c_s^2[n_B \in (3,4)n_0] = -0.08 \pm 0.14\) for baryon densities between 3 and 4 times nuclear saturation density
- The nuclear matter saturation density is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The default Skyrme potential in SMASH produces a nuclear matter ground state at \(n_0 = 0.166\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy per nucleon \(E_{\text{bin}} = -15.65\) MeV and incompressibility \(K_0 = 236.73\) MeV
- The study covers incident kinetic energies per nucleon \(E_{\text{lab}}\) from 1 to 20 GeV in the fixed target frame, corresponding to center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 6.4\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Danielewicz (2002), who constrained the incompressibility \(K_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter to between 210 and 380 MeV using E895 flow data but could not simultaneously describe directed and elliptic flow measurements. By allowing the stiffness of the equation of state to vary independently in different density intervals, the authors aim to resolve this tension and provide more precise constraints. The study enables future Bayesian analyses incorporating additional observables and data from the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC and the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR.
