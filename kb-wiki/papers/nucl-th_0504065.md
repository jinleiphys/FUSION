# $危^-/危^+$ ratio as a candidate for probing the density dependence of the symmetry potential at high nuclear densities

> Based on the UrQMD model, we have investigated the influence of the symmetry potential on the negatively and positively charged $蟺$ and $危$ hyperon production ratios in heavy ion collisions at the SIS energies. We find that, in addition to $蟺^-/蟺^+$ ratio, the $危^-/危^+$ ratio can be taken as a sensitive probe for investigating the density dependence of the symmetry potential of nuclear matter at high densities (1-4 times of normal baryon density). This sensitivity of the symmetry potential to both the $蟺^-/蟺^+$ and $危^-/危^+$ ratios is found to depend strongly on the incident beam energy. Furthermore, the $危^-/危^+$ ratio is shown to carry the information about the isospin-dependent part of the $危$ hyperon single-particle potential.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the $\Sigma^-/\Sigma^+$ ratio in heavy ion collisions can serve as a sensitive probe of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential at high densities (1-4 times normal baryon density), but this sensitivity depends strongly on the incident beam energy and is only observed for neutron-rich systems, not for nearly isospin-symmetric ones.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 1.3) with a hard Skyrme-type equation of state to simulate collisions of the neutron-rich system $^{132}Sn+^{132}Sn$ and the nearly isospin-symmetric system $^{112}Sn+^{112}Sn$ at beam energies of 1.5A, 2.5A, and 3.5A GeV. Two extreme forms of the density dependence of the symmetry energy were implemented: a stiff form ($F_1^{\gamma=1}$, linear with density) and a soft form ($F_2^{a=3}$, which becomes negative above three times normal density). The symmetry potential was included for nucleons, resonances ($\Delta$(1232) and N$^*$(1440)), and $\Sigma$ hyperons, with the $\Sigma$ hyperon symmetry potential treated as neutron-like for $\Sigma^-$ and proton-like for $\Sigma^+$.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy at normal density was taken as $S_0 = 30$ MeV.
- For the soft symmetry potential, the reduced critical density parameter was $a = 3$, meaning the symmetry potential becomes negative when $u > 3$ (density above three times normal density).
- At $E_b = 1.5A$ GeV for $^{132}Sn+^{132}Sn$, the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio calculated with the soft symmetry potential was higher than with the stiff one, consistent with prior work.
- At $E_b = 3.5A$ GeV, the sensitivity of the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio to the symmetry potential disappeared.
- For $^{132}Sn+^{132}Sn$ at $E_b = 1.5A$ GeV, the $\Sigma^-/\Sigma^+$ ratio with the stiff symmetry potential was higher than with the soft one; at $E_b = 3.5A$ GeV, this ordering reversed, with the soft symmetry potential giving the higher ratio.
- More than 75% of pions and $\Sigma$ hyperons were produced in the density region $u > 1$ (above normal nuclear density) at both 1.5A and 3.5A GeV.
- The yield of $\Sigma$ hyperons was enhanced by about 20% and the yield of $\Lambda$ hyperons suppressed by about 22% when comparing UrQMD version 1.3 to version 1.2.

## Context
This work extends the earlier finding by Li (2002, 2003) that the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio is sensitive to the symmetry potential at high densities, and addresses the concern raised by Gaitanos et al. (2003, 2004) that this sensitivity is largely reduced at beam energies above 2 AGeV. The paper introduces the $\Sigma^-/\Sigma^+$ ratio as a complementary probe that retains sensitivity at higher energies due to the dynamical effect of the $\Sigma$ hyperon's own symmetry potential. The results enable future experimental studies at SIS energies to extract information about both the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities and the isospin-dependent part (Lane potential) of the $\Sigma$ hyperon single-particle potential, though the authors note large quantitative uncertainties remain about the $\Sigma$ hyperon symmetry potential itself.
