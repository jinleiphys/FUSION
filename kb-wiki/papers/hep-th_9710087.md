# Renormalization-group Resummation of Divergent Series of the Perturbative Wave Function of Quantum Anharmonic Oscillator

> The renormalization group method is applied for obtaining the asymptotic form of the wave function of the quantum anharmonic oscillator by resumming the perturbation series. It is shown that the resumed series is the cumulant of the naive perturbation series. Working out up to the sixth order and performing a further resummation proposed by Bender and Bettencourt, we find that the agreement with the WKB result becomes worse in the higher orders than the fourth at which the agreement is the best.

# Renormalization-group Resummation of Divergent Series of the Perturbative Wave Function of Quantum Anharmonic Oscillator

## Key claim

The renormalization group method can resum the divergent perturbation series of the anharmonic oscillator wave function, producing a cumulant series; however, when worked out to sixth order with a further resummation proposed by Bender and Bettencourt, the agreement with the WKB result becomes worse beyond fourth order, where the agreement is best.

## Method

The author applies the renormalization group method as formulated geometrically via the theory of envelopes to the time-independent Schrödinger equation for the quantum anharmonic oscillator with Hamiltonian H = p² + x²/4 + ε x⁴/4. The wave function and energy are expanded in power series of ε using the Bender-Wu method for Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory. The RG method constructs a family of perturbative solutions parametrized by an initial point x₀, then imposes the envelope condition dψ(x; x₀)/dx₀|_{x₀=x}=0 to obtain a global solution, yielding resummed wave functions expressed as exponentials of cumulants f_n(x) of the naive perturbation polynomials P_n(x). The calculation is carried out to sixth order, and the asymptotic behavior of the resummed series is compared with the WKB result.

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian is H = p² + (1/4)x² + (1/4)ε x⁴, with ground state energy E₀ = 1/2 at lowest order.
- The WKB asymptotic form for large x is ψ(x) ~ exp{-√ε |x|³/6}.
- At fourth order, the coefficient of -√ε|x|³ from the resummed highest-power terms is approximately 5.96663, in excellent agreement with the WKB value of 6.
- At fifth order, the coefficient becomes approximately 5.73827, deviating from 6 more than the fourth order result.
- At sixth order, the coefficient becomes approximately 5.26181, showing further degradation of agreement.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group method of Goldenfeld, Martin, Oono, and Liu (1989-1996) and the geometric formulation by Kunihiro (1995-1997) to obtain asymptotic wave functions of the quantum anharmonic oscillator. It directly builds on the multiple-scale perturbation theory (MSPT) treatment by Bender and Bettencourt (1996), who obtained the asymptotic wave function up to third order using a further resummation method also employed by Ginsburg and Montroll (1978). The present work shows that the RG method reproduces the Bender-Bettencourt results mechanically and extends them to sixth order, revealing that the cumulant series is at best asymptotic and that higher orders do not improve agreement with WKB. This enables systematic application of the RG method to other quantum mechanical problems, though the author notes the method is unlikely to work for excited states beyond the first.
