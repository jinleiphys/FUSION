# Full leading-order nuclear polarization in highly charged ions

> The nuclear-polarization corrections to the energy levels of highly charged ions are systematically investigated to leading order in the fine-structure constant. To this end, the notion of effective photon propagators with nuclear-polarization insertions is employed, where the nuclear excitation spectrum is calculated by means of the Hartree-Fock-based random-phase approximation. The effective Skyrme force is used to describe the interaction between nucleons, and the model dependence is analyzed. To leading order, the formalism predicts two contributions given by the effective vacuum-polarization and self-energy diagrams. The existing ambiguity around the vacuum-polarization term is resolved by demonstrating that it is effectively absorbed in the standard finite-nuclear-size correction. The self-energy part is evaluated with the full electromagnetic electron-nucleus interaction taken into account, where the importance of the effects of the nuclear three-currents is emphasized.

## Key claim

The authors systematically show that the leading-order nuclear-polarization correction to energy levels of highly charged ions consists of two contributions (effective self-energy and effective vacuum-polarization), but the vacuum-polarization term is effectively absorbed into the standard finite-nuclear-size correction and therefore does not need to be included separately, provided the nuclear charge radius is determined from muonic spectroscopy.

## Method

The nuclear excitation spectrum was calculated using the Hartree-Fock-based random-phase approximation with effective Skyrme forces for seven nuclear excitation modes (0⁺, 1⁻, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻, and 1⁺). The atomic part solved the stationary Dirac equation numerically using B-spline expansions within the dual-kinetic-balance approach. The effective self-energy diagram was evaluated with full electromagnetic electron-nucleus interaction including nuclear three-currents, while the effective vacuum-polarization diagram was compared to the change in finite-nuclear-size effects induced by introducing the Uehling potential into the nuclear mean field.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb with a bound electron in the 1s₁/₂ state, the VP-NP correction is 21.1 meV (SKX), 23.6 meV (KDE0), and 26.9 meV (SkP), while the corresponding ΔFNS values are 21.6 meV, 24.8 meV, and 27.6 meV, respectively.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with a bound muon in the 1s₁/₂ state, the VP-NP correction is 1110 keV (SKX), 1247 keV (KDE0), and 1407 keV (SkP), while the corresponding ΔFNS values are 1124 keV, 1289 keV, and 1432 keV, respectively.
- The nuclear RMS charge radius change due to the Uehling potential for ²⁰⁸Pb is 0.00112 fm (SKX), 0.00128 fm (KDE0), and 0.00142 fm (SkP).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with a bound electron in the 2s₁/₂ state, the VP-NP correction ranges from 3.65 meV (SKX) to 4.66 meV (SkP), with ΔFNS from 3.75 meV to 4.78 meV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with a bound electron in the 2p₁/₂ state, the VP-NP correction ranges from 0.32 meV (SKX) to 0.40 meV (SkP), with ΔFNS from 0.32 meV to 0.41 meV.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach to nuclear polarization originally developed by Plunien et al. (1989) and the microscopic RPA calculations for hydrogenlike ²⁰⁸Pb⁸¹⁺ by Haga et al. (2002), which used only one set of Migdal-force parameters. The paper resolves the ambiguity raised by Labzowsky et al. (1996) regarding double counting in the effective vacuum-polarization term, and demonstrates that the transverse electron-nucleus interaction (nuclear three-currents) is important for the self-energy contribution. This enables more reliable theoretical predictions for high-precision spectroscopy of highly charged ions, particularly for tests of quantum electrodynamics and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
