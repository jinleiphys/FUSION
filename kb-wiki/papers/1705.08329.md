# Mass differences and neutron pairing in Ca, Sn and Pb isotopes

> Various estimates of the even-odd effect of the mass shell of atomic nuclei are considered. Based on the experimental mass values of the Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes, the dependence of the energy gap on the neutrons number is traced and the relationship of this characteristic to the properties of external neutron subshells is shown. In nuclei with closed proton shells, effects directly related to neutron pairing and effects of nucleon shells are discussed.

# Wiki Page: Mass differences and neutron pairing in Ca, Sn and Pb isotopes

## Key claim
The even-odd staggering (EOS) of nuclear masses in semi-magic Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes arises from a combination of genuine neutron pairing and mean-field effects such as shell and subshell filling, not from pairing alone. The simplest pairing indicators (three-point formulas) are contaminated by single-particle energy jumps at magic numbers, whereas the direct two-neutron pairing energy Δ_nn provides a smoother measure that more closely isolates the pairing contribution, especially in the middle of subshells.

## Method
The authors computed neutron pairing energy indicators from experimental atomic masses (AME16) for Ca (Z=20), Sn (Z=50), and Pb (Z=82) isotopes using three-point, four-point, and five-point finite-difference formulas, as well as the direct two-neutron pairing energy Δ_nn defined as the difference between the two-neutron separation energy from an even-even nucleus and twice the neutron separation energy from the neighboring odd nucleus. They compared these indicators to each other and to the excitation energies of the first 2+ states, and interpreted the results within the seniority model of identical-nucleon pairing in a degenerate j-shell.

## Key numbers
- For Ca isotopes in the f7/2 subshell (N=22, 30, 36), the ratio Δ_nn/(2j+1) ≈ 0.35 MeV, consistent with the pairing strength parameter G_n ≈ 25/A MeV.
- For Sn isotopes, the ratio Δ_nn/(2j+1) ≈ 0.15 MeV when comparing N=76 (subshell group with 16 projections) to N=84 (subshell with 8 projections).
- The three-point indicator Δ_nn^(3) shows a sharp leap at N=20 in Ca, while Δ_nn shows the corresponding change at N=21, demonstrating the one-neutron shift between the two indicators.
- For Ca isotopes 42,44,46Ca, the values of Δ_nn^(3) and Δ_nn are nearly equal, giving a small δe value.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of even-odd mass staggering from earlier studies (Dobaczewski 2001, Bertulani 2003, Qi 2016) by systematically comparing multiple EOS indicators across three isotopic chains with closed proton shells. It builds on the seniority model (Racah 1943, Talmi 1993) and the concept of direct two-neutron pairing energy (Preston). The results enable improved separation of pairing effects from mean-field contributions in mass formulas, and show that the difference δe = Δ_nn^(3) - Δ_nn can serve as a probe of core polarization and three-body interactions (Brown 2013). The paper also notes that for neutron-rich isotopes beyond N=132, experimental errors in separation energies are too large (tens of percent) for reliable conclusions about δe sign changes that might indicate shape transitions.
