# The relativistic entrainment matrix of a superfluid nucleon-hyperon mixture at zero temperature

> We calculate the relativistic entrainment matrix Y_ik at zero temperature for nucleon-hyperon mixture composed of neutrons, protons, Lambda and Sigma^- hyperons, as well as of electrons and muons. This matrix is analogous to the entrainment matrix (also termed mass-density matrix or Andreev-Bashkin matrix) of non-relativistic theory. It is an important ingredient for modelling the pulsations of massive neutron stars with superfluid nucleon-hyperon cores. The calculation is done in the frame of the relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid theory generalized to the case of superfluid mixtures; the matrix Y_ik is expressed through the Landau parameters of nucleon-hyperon matter. The results are illustrated with a particular example of the sigma-omega-rho mean-field model with scalar self-interactions. Using this model we calculate the matrix Y_ik and the Landau parameters. We also analyze stability of the ground state of nucleon-hyperon matter with respect to small perturbations.

## Key claim

The relativistic entrainment matrix \(Y_{ik}\) for a superfluid nucleon-hyperon mixture at zero temperature is calculated using the relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid theory generalized to superfluid mixtures, with the result expressed through the Landau parameters \(f_1^{ik}\); the calculation assumes that all baryon species (neutrons, protons, \(\Lambda\)-hyperons, and \(\Sigma^{-}\)-hyperons) are superfluid, which is treated as a reasonable assumption at zero temperature despite uncertainties about hyperon critical temperatures.

## Method

The authors generalized the relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid theory of Baym and Chin (1976) to mixtures of multiple baryon species, deriving a relation between the effective mass \(m_i^{\ast}\) and the Landau parameters \(f_1^{ik}\) from Lorentz transformation properties of quasiparticle energy and distribution functions. Using the shifted Fermi-sphere distribution function for superfluid quasiparticles at zero temperature (Leggett 1965, 1975), they computed the particle current density \(\mathbf{j}_i\) and identified the entrainment matrix \(Y_{ik}\) as \(Y_{ik} = (n_i / m_i^{\ast}) \delta_{ik} + G_{ik}\), where \(G_{ik}\) is defined in terms of the Landau parameters \(f_1^{ik}\). The results were illustrated using the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) mean-field model with scalar self-interactions (Glendenning 1985), within which the matrix \(Y_{ik}\) and all spin-averaged Landau parameters were calculated, and the stability of the ground state was analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The number density of baryon species \(i\) is given by \(n_i = p_{Fi}^3 / (3 \pi^2)\), where \(p_{Fi}\) is the Fermi momentum.
- The symmetric matrix \(G_{ik}\) is defined as \(G_{ik} = (1 / 9 \pi^4) \, p_{Fi}^2 p_{Fk}^2 \, f_1^{ik}\), where \(f_1^{ik}\) are the spin-averaged Landau parameters.
- The effective mass relation is \(\mu_i / m_i^{\ast} = 1 - \sum_k (\mu_k G_{ik} / n_i)\), where \(\mu_i\) is the relativistic chemical potential.
- The entrainment matrix satisfies the zero-temperature sum rule \(\sum_k \mu_k Y_{ik} = n_i\).
- Stability conditions require \(Y_{ii} \geq 0\) and \(Y_{ii} Y_{kk} - Y_{ik}^2 \geq 0\) for \(i \neq k\).

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic calculations of the entrainment matrix (also called the Andreev-Bashkin matrix) for nucleon matter by Bedaque et al. (1996), Comer and Joynt (2003), and Gusakov and Haensel (2005) to the relativistic regime and to matter containing hyperons (\(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^{-}\)). It also generalizes the relativistic treatment of the entrainment matrix for neutron-proton matter by Comer and Joynt (2003) to a four-component baryon mixture. The results enable more realistic modeling of pulsations of massive neutron stars with superfluid nucleon-hyperon cores, and the formalism can be applied to any number of superfluid baryon species.
