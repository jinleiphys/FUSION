# Axially deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov with separable pairing force

> A separable form of pairing interaction in the $^{1}S_{0}$ channel has been introduced and successfully applied in the description of both static and dynamic properties of superfluid nuclei. By adjusting the parameters to reproduce the pairing properties of the Gogny force in nuclear matter, this separable pairing force is successful in depicting the pairing properties of ground states and vibrational excitations of spherical nuclei on almost the same footing as the original Gogny force. In this article, we extend these investigations for Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in deformed nuclei with axial symmetry (RHBZ) using the same separable pairing interaction. In order to preserve translational invariance we construct one- and two-dimensional Talmi-Moshinsky brackets for the cylindrical harmonic oscillator basis. We show that the matrix elements of this force can then be expanded in a series of separable terms. The convergence of this expansion is investigated for various deformations. We observe a relatively fast convergence. This allows for a considerable reduction in computing time as compared to RHBZ-calculations with the full Gogny force in the pairing channel. As an example we solve the RHBZ equations with this separable pairing force for the ground states of the chain of Sm-isotopes. Good agreement with the experimental data as well as with other theoretical results is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously introduced separable pairing force, which reproduces the pairing properties of the Gogny force in nuclear matter, to axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHBZ) calculations; the authors claim that the matrix elements of this force can be expanded in a series of separable terms with relatively fast convergence, allowing a considerable reduction in computing time compared to RHBZ calculations with the full Gogny force in the pairing channel.

## Method

The authors construct one- and two-dimensional Talmi-Moshinsky brackets for the cylindrical harmonic oscillator basis to preserve translational invariance, then expand the matrix elements of the separable pairing interaction in a series of separable terms. They solve the RHBZ equations with this separable pairing force for the ground states of a chain of Sm isotopes, using the NL3 effective interaction for the particle-hole channel and the separable force adjusted to the Gogny D1S force for the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

* For the separable force adjusted to Gogny D1S: strength parameter G = 728 MeV·fm³ and range parameter a = 0.644 fm.
* For the separable force adjusted to Gogny D1: strength parameter G = 738 MeV·fm³ and range parameter a = 0.636 fm.
* For the nucleus ¹⁶⁴Er, the ground state deformation is prolate with β = 0.325.
* For the nucleus ²⁴⁰Pu, the ground state has a normal prolate deformation at β = 0.278, with additional minima at normal oblate deformation β = -0.277 and super prolate deformation β = 0.865.
* For normal deformed prolate nuclei, the expansion converges with Nz⁰ = 14 and Np⁰ = 5 separable terms.
* For the super deformed minimum in ²⁴⁰Pu, Nz⁰ = 18 is needed for convergence; for the oblate minimum, Np⁰ = 7 is needed.
* The RHBZ calculations with the separable force deviate from experimental binding energies for Sm isotopes by less than 0.2%.

## Context

This work extends the separable pairing force previously introduced for spherical nuclei by Tian and Ma (2006) and Tian, Ma, and Ring (2009) to axially deformed nuclei, building on the RHBZ framework developed by Geng, Ring, and Toki (1990) and others. The results are compared with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny force D1S by Hilaire and Girod (2007) and with RMF + BCS calculations using constant pairing gaps from the prescription of Moller and Nix (1992). The approach enables efficient RHBZ calculations for deformed nuclei far from stability, replacing the numerically more demanding full Gogny force in the pairing channel.
