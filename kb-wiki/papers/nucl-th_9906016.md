# The properties of \bar{K} in the nuclear medium

> The self-energy of the K^- meson in nuclear matter is calculated in a self-consistent microscopic approach, using a \bar{K}N interaction obtained from the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian. The effective \bar{K}N interaction in the medium is derived by solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation including Pauli blocking on the nucleons, mean-field binding potentials for the baryons and the self-energy of the πand \bar{K} mesons. The incorporation of the self-consistent {\bar K} self-energy in the description, in addition to the Pauli blocking effects, yields a weaker attractive in-medium {\bar K}N interaction and a Λ(1405) which dissolves faster with increasing matter density, as a result of the {\bar K} spectral function being spread out over a wide range of energies. These effects are further magnified when the intermediate pions are dressed.

## Key claim

The self-consistent inclusion of the \(\bar{K}\) self-energy in the calculation, in addition to Pauli blocking, yields a weaker attractive in-medium \(\bar{K}N\) interaction and a \(\Lambda(1405)\) which dissolves faster with increasing matter density; these effects are further magnified when the intermediate pions are dressed.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction in nuclear matter, using the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian as the kernel. Medium effects are incorporated by including Pauli blocking on intermediate nucleon states, mean-field binding potentials for baryons (nucleons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\)), and the self-energies of the \(\pi\) and \(\bar{K}\) mesons in a self-consistent iterative scheme. The \(\bar{K}\) self-energy is obtained by summing the in-medium \(\bar{K}N\) \(T\)-matrix over the Fermi sea, and the pion self-energy includes \(1p1h\), \(\Delta h\), and \(2p2h\) excitations with short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The nucleon mean-field potential depth is \(U_0 = -50\) MeV; the \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) potential depths are both \(U_0 = -30\) MeV.
- The loop regularization cut-off used to reproduce \(K^-p\) scattering data is \(q_{\rm max} = 630\) MeV.
- The weak decay constant used is \(f = 1.15 f_\pi\).
- The in-medium pion spectral function peak positions at \(\rho = \rho_0\) are at 158, 179, and 212 MeV for momenta \(q = 100\), 200, and 300 MeV/c, respectively (free values: 172, 244, 331 MeV).
- The \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance lies 27 MeV below the \(K^-p\) threshold in free space.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of \(\bar{K}\) in nuclear matter that included only Pauli blocking (Koch (1994), Waas and Weise (1997)) and self-consistent kaon dressing (Lutz (1998)) by additionally incorporating the dressing of pions in the intermediate \(\pi\Lambda\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) states. The results show that the combined dressing of both kaons and pions softens the in-medium interaction and suppresses the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance more strongly than in previous approaches, which has implications for the interpretation of kaonic atom data and the possibility of kaon condensation in dense matter.
