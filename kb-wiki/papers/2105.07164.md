# Derivation of the Grodzins relation in collective nuclear model

> Basing on the Bohr collective quadrupole Hamiltonian the $A$-dependence of the Grodzins product is derived and the proportionality coefficient for the Grodzins relation is evaluated. The result obtained is in a correspondence with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The Grodzins product \(E(2^+_1) \times B(E2;0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) is derived from the Bohr collective quadrupole Hamiltonian, yielding an \(A\)-dependence of \(Z^2 A^{-2/3}\) and a proportionality coefficient of 2.9, but the authors caution that the calculation is only qualitative and the agreement with the phenomenological coefficient should not be overestimated.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr collective quadrupole Hamiltonian and use the double commutator of the quadrupole operator with the Hamiltonian to derive an energy-weighted sum rule. They then approximate the sum over all \(2^+\) states by keeping only the first \(2^+_1\) state, obtaining an expression for the Grodzins product in terms of the inertia coefficient \(B_2\). To determine the \(A\)-dependence of \(B_2\), they use the cranking model approach with a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field, include pair correlations, and replace the single-particle sums by integrals using average values for the pairing gap \(\Delta = 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV and the Fermi gas level density \(g = 3A/2\epsilon_F\).

## Key numbers

- The derived Grodzins product is \(E(2^+_1) \times B(E2;0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 2.9 \frac{Z^2}{A^{2/3}} \, (e b)^2 \, \text{keV}\) (Equation 19).
- The average diagonal matrix element \(|\langle s| r dV_{WS}/dr Y_{20} |s \rangle|\) is 10.5 for \(^{168}\text{Er}\) and 10.9 for \(^{238}\text{U}\), taken as approximately \(A\)-independent.
- The pairing gap is taken as \(\Delta = 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV, from Bohr and Mottelson (1969).
- The Fermi gas single-particle level density is \(g = 3A/2\epsilon_F\), from Bohr and Mottelson (1969).

## Context

This work provides a theoretical foundation for the empirical Grodzins relation, originally found by Grodzins (1962) and later refined by Raman et al. (1987, 2001) to the form \(E(2^+_1) \times B(E2;0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 2.57(45) Z^2 A^{-2/3}\). The derivation extends earlier collective model treatments by Jolos and Gupta (2015) and addresses the breakdown of the product rule noted by Gupta (2018, 2019) and the need for individual fits highlighted by Pritychenko (2016). The result enables future microscopic calculations of \(B_2\) for superheavy nuclei to predict \(2^+_1\) energies using computed quadrupole deformations.
