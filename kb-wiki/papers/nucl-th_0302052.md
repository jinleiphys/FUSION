# Dissociation cross sections of ground-state and excited charmonia with light mesons in the quark model

> We present numerical results for the dissociation cross sections of ground-state, orbitally- and radially-excited charmonia in collisions with light mesons. Our results are derived using the nonrelativistic quark model, so all parameters are determined by fits to the experimental meson spectrum. Examples of dissociation into both exclusive and inclusive final states are considered. The dissociation cross sections of several C=(+) charmonia may be of considerable importance for the study of heavy ion collisions, since these states are expected to be produced more copiously than the J/psi. The relative importance of the productions of ground-state and orbitally-excited charmed mesons in a pion-charmonium collision is demonstrated through the $\sqrt {s}$-dependent charmonium dissociation cross sections.

# Dissociation cross sections of ground-state and excited charmonia with light mesons in the quark model

## Key claim

The quark interchange model predicts that dissociation cross sections of excited charmonia (particularly the C=(+) states such as χ_cJ and ψ') with pions and rho mesons are significantly larger than those of the J/ψ, but the absolute scale remains uncertain because the dominant scattering mechanism at low energies has not been experimentally established. The authors present these results as one model's predictions among several competing approaches, noting that the actual size of low-energy charmonium dissociation cross sections is "currently very controversial."

## Method

The authors used the nonrelativistic quark model with a Hamiltonian consisting of color Coulomb, linear confinement, and OGE spin-spin hyperfine terms, with all parameters fixed by fits to the experimental meson spectrum. They evaluated Born-order quark interchange scattering amplitudes for meson-meson collisions by computing spatial overlap integrals via adaptive Monte Carlo integration, combining these with spin matrix elements and color factors, and summing over the four quark Born diagrams (two capture and two transfer diagrams). Cross sections were computed using relativistic phase space and physical meson masses, with results averaged between "prior" and "post" formalisms to minimize the post-prior ambiguity.

## Key numbers

- π + J/ψ → D*D̄ + h.c. + D*D̄* cross section: peak of roughly 1 mb near √s ≈ 4.1 GeV (Wong et al. 2000 result, cited as prior work using the same approach)
- π + J/ψ total cross section fit parameter: σ_max = 1.40 mb for the DD* channel, with ε_max = 0.059 GeV
- π + ψ' total cross section fit parameter: σ_max = 13.04 mb for the DD* channel, with ε_max = 0.027 GeV
- π + χ_c0 total cross section fit parameter: σ_max = 2.02 mb for the DD* channel, with ε_max = 0.147 GeV
- π + χ_c1 total cross section fit parameter: σ_max = 3.02 mb for the DD* channel, with ε_max = 0.154 GeV
- π + χ_c2 total cross section fit parameter: σ_max = 3.64 mb for the DD* channel, with ε_max = 0.157 GeV
- Quark model parameters: α_s = 0.6, σ = 0.9 GeV (spin-spin hyperfine regulator), b = 0.16 (string tension), light quark mass = 0.33 GeV, charm quark mass = 1.6 GeV
- The ψ' cross sections are roughly ten times larger than corresponding ψ cross sections

## Context

This work extends the quark interchange model calculations of Wong et al. (2000) for π + J/ψ and ρ + J/ψ dissociation to include orbitally-excited (χ_cJ) and radially-excited (ψ') charmonia, using exact numerical wavefunctions from the full quark model Hamiltonian rather than Gaussian approximations. The results stand in contrast to the much larger cross sections (5-30 mb) predicted by meson exchange models such as those of Haglin (1999), Lin and Ko (2000), and Oh, Song and Lee (2001), and to the extremely small (sub-μb) low-energy cross sections from the Peskin-Bhanot diffractive model (1979). The calculations enable more realistic simulations of heavy flavor production in heavy ion collisions, particularly for C=(+) charmonia which are expected to be produced more copiously than J/ψ via gluon fusion and may feed down to J/ψ through radiative transitions.
