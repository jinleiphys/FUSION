# Elliptic flow of strange and multi-strange hadrons in isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}} = 200\mathrm{~GeV}$ at RHIC

> We report a systematic measurement of elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) of $K_{s}^{0}$, $Λ$, $\overlineΛ$, $ϕ$, $Ξ^{-}$, $\overlineΞ^{+}$, and $Ω^{-}$+$\overlineΩ^{+}$ at mid-rapidity ($|y| < 1.0$) for isobar, $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr, collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. The transverse momentum ($p_\mathrm{T}$) dependence of $v_{2}$ is studied for various centrality classes. The number of constituent quark scaling of (multi-)strange hadrons is found to hold approximately within 20%, suggesting the development of partonic collectivity in isobar collisions similar to that observed in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. The average $v_{2}$ ratio shows $\sim$2% deviation from unity in central and mid-central collisions for strange hadrons, indicating a difference in nuclear structure and deformation between the isobars. The $v_{2}$ in isobaric collisions is compared to Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at similar collision energies. We observe an increase in $v_{2}(p_\mathrm{T})$ with increasing system size. The difference in $v_{2}$ between the isobar and other collision systems increases with $p_\mathrm{T}$. The results are compared with a multi-phase transport model calculations with a deformed density profile to provide further insight into the nuclear structure of these isobars.

## Key claim

The number of constituent quark scaling of (multi-)strange hadrons holds approximately within 20% in isobar collisions, suggesting partonic collectivity similar to Au+Au collisions, but the abstract's claim of "development of partonic collectivity" is hedged by the observation that the scaling is only approximate and the average v2 ratio between the two isobars shows only a ~2% deviation from unity for strange hadrons in central and mid-central collisions.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured elliptic flow (v2) of Ks0, Λ, anti-Λ, φ, Ξ-, anti-Ξ+, and Ω-+anti-Ω+ at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1.0) in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using the η sub-event plane method with a gap of Δη = 0.1 to reduce non-flow effects. Short-lived particles were reconstructed via their hadronic decay channels using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-Of-Flight detectors for particle identification, with combinatorial background handled by rotational background or mixed-event techniques depending on the particle species.

## Key numbers

- Number of constituent quark scaling of (multi-)strange hadrons holds approximately within 20% in minimum bias (0-80%) collisions
- Average v2 ratio between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr shows ~2% deviation from unity in central and mid-central collisions for strange hadrons
- Fit to the v2 ratio in 20-50% centrality range yields 1.026±0.007 for Ks0, 1.028±0.003 for Λ, and 1.020±0.003 for anti-Λ
- The v2 ratio deviations are significant at 3.7σ for Ks0 and 9.3σ (6.7σ) for Λ (anti-Λ)
- Total analyzed events: ~1.77 billion for Ru+Ru and ~1.84 billion for Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Event plane resolution in isobar collisions is lower than in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV due to smaller number of participating nucleons
- Systematic uncertainties range from 0.3% (Ks0, 0-80%) to 19% (Ω-+anti-Ω+, 0-10% Ru) depending on particle species and centrality

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow of strange and multi-strange hadrons from larger systems like Au+Au (STAR, 2005) and U+U (STAR, 2015) to smaller deformed isobar systems, providing a systematic comparison across Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at similar energies. The results enable further constraints on nuclear structure models of Ru and Zr nuclei, particularly the quadrupole deformation parameters (β2 = 0.162 for Ru versus β2 = 0.06 for Zr), and complement the Chiral Magnetic Effect studies from the same isobar data set (STAR, 2021). The comparison with a multi-phase transport model using deformed density profiles provides insight into how nuclear deformation affects collective flow in small collision systems.
