# Spin-Isospin Excitations and Muon Capture by Nuclei

> By analyzing the energy-weighted moments of the strength function calculated in RPA and beyond it is shown that the explanation of the effect of missing strength of Gamow-Teller transitions requires that residual interaction produce high-excited $1^{+}$ particle-hole collective states. The example of this interaction is presented. The manifestations of spin-isospin nuclear response in nuclear muon capture are discussed.

# Spin-Isospin Excitations and Muon Capture by Nuclei

## Key claim

The explanation of the missing Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength requires that the residual nuclear interaction produce high-excited 1+ particle-hole collective states by strongly mixing ΔN = 0 and ΔN ≥ 2 particle-hole configurations; this conclusion follows from the conservation of the zero and first energy-weighted moments of the strength function when going from RPA to SRPA or to the fragmentation problem, which the authors argue contradicts earlier SRPA calculations that claimed to shift strength via 2p-2h admixtures alone.

## Method

The authors analyze the energy-weighted moments S0 and S1 of the Gamow-Teller strength function calculated in the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), the Second Random Phase Approximation (SRPA), and within the fragmentation problem, showing analytically that these moments are conserved across all three approaches. They then demonstrate that a phenomenological separable residual interaction with a surface-peaked radial form factor (derivative of the Saxon-Woods potential) can mix ΔN = 0 and ΔN ≥ 2 particle-hole configurations, producing high-lying collective 1+ states, and apply this interaction to calculate total ordinary muon capture (OMC) rates for spherical nuclei and partial capture rates for A=28 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing problem of missing Gamow-Teller strength by providing a formal argument based on moment conservation that contradicts the interpretation of earlier SRPA calculations by Drozdz et al. (1986) and Drozdz and Oster (1987), who attributed the strength shift to 2p-2h admixtures. The authors propose a specific separable residual interaction with a surface form factor (derivative of the Saxon-Woods potential) that can produce the required mixing, building on the formalism of Vdovin and Soloviev (1983). The analysis of muon capture rates shows that reproducing the missing GT strength via high-excited 1+ states leads to overestimation of total OMC rates in heavy nuclei (140Ce, 208Pb), creating a tension between the two observables. The shell-model calculations for A=28 nuclei using Wildenthal's Hamiltonian (Wildenthal) with the OXBASH code show that theoretical summed strengths exceed experimental values, and the authors propose an orthogonal transformation to redistribute strength while preserving the sum rules.
