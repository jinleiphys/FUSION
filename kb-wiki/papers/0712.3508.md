# Relative motion correction to fission barriers

> We discuss the effect of kinetic energy of the relative motion becoming spurious for separate fragments on the selfconsistent mean-field fission barriers. The treatment of the relative motion in the cluster model is contrasted with the necessity of a simpler and approximate approach in the mean-field theory. A scheme of the energy correction to the Hartree-Fock is proposed. The results obtained with the effective Skyrme interaction SLy6 show that the correction, previously estimated as $\sim$ 8 MeV in $A=70-100$ nuclei, amounts to 4 MeV in the medium heavy nucleus $^{198}$Hg and to null in $^{238}$U. However, the corrected barrier implies a shorter fission half-life of the latter nucleus. The same effect is expected to lower barriers for multipartition (i.e. ternary fission, etc) and make hyperdeformed minima less stable.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a shape-dependent correction to subtract the spurious kinetic energy of fragments' relative motion from Hartree-Fock fission barriers, finding that the correction is significant (3.8 MeV) in medium-heavy nuclei like $^{198}$Hg but vanishes at the second barrier of $^{238}$U because the neck is not yet developed and single-particle orbitals are not well localized.

## Method

The author defines a localization measure based on the dispersion of single-particle wave functions in two spatial volumes corresponding to the nascent fragments, then constructs a correction $E_{c.m.}(rel,shape)$ that subtracts the relative-motion kinetic energy in proportion to the number of localized orbitals. Fission barriers for $^{198}$Hg and $^{238}$U are calculated using the Skyrme interaction SLy6 with BCS pairing, comparing results with and without the correction.

## Key numbers

- In $^{198}$Hg, the uncorrected fission barrier of 27.5 MeV is lowered by the correction to 23.7 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 20.4 MeV (Mor72) and 19.3 MeV from Mos72.
- The correction at the $^{198}$Hg fission barrier is 3.8 MeV, smaller than the previously estimated ~8 MeV for $A=70-100$ nuclei.
- In $^{238}$U, the triaxial first hump is 7.55 MeV at $Q=60$ b and the mass-asymmetric second hump is 8 MeV at $Q=125$ b; the correction vanishes at the second barrier.
- At $Q=200$ b in $^{238}$U, the correction amounts to 1.9 MeV.
- The estimated change in fission half-life for $^{238}$U is $\Delta \log T_{sf}=1.94$, corresponding to about two orders of magnitude, compared to the experimental $\log T_{sf}=23.4$ (BL80).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (JSnew) on $A=70-100$ nuclei to heavier systems, contrasting the mean-field approach with the full resonating group method (RGM) treatment of relative motion used in light nuclei (Lov98, SLYV03). The correction is analogous in effect to a correction introduced on different grounds in macroscopic-microscopic calculations (MSC, fiss, newfis). The results imply that fission barriers for multipartition (ternary fission, etc.) will be further lowered and hyperdeformed minima become less stable, while for superheavy nuclei the correction should still be accounted for in fission dynamics.
