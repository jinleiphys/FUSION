# A random walk with heavy flavours

> We focus on evaluating transport coefficients like drag and diffusion of heavy quarks (HQ) passing through Quark Gluon Plasma using perturbative QCD (pQCD). Experimental observable like nuclear suppression factor (RAA) of HQ is evaluated for both zero and non-zero baryonic chemical potential (μ_B) scenarios using Fokker- Planck equation. Theoretical estimates of RAA are contrasted with experiments.

## Key claim

The authors compute drag and diffusion coefficients for heavy quarks in quark-gluon plasma using perturbative QCD with a Debye-screened gluon propagator (rather than full hard thermal loop resummation), and find that the momentum dependence of these coefficients is non-negligible for the momentum range studied, with the caveat that radiative processes become dominant at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the Fokker-Planck equation for heavy quark momentum distributions, using collisional drag and diffusion coefficients computed from elastic scattering matrix elements (with t-channel divergence shielded by the gluon thermal mass m_D) and radiative drag obtained from the Gyulassy-Wang potential model with the Gunion-Bertsch gluon distribution formula weighted by a dead-cone factor. The nuclear suppression factor R_AA is evaluated for charm and bottom quarks at both zero and non-zero baryonic chemical potential, and the initial momentum distributions are taken from the MNR code.

## Key numbers

* Drag coefficient for charm quarks due to collisional processes at T=300 MeV: 0.036 fm^{-1} at p=5 GeV, reducing to 0.018 fm^{-1} at p=10 GeV
* Critical temperature for quark-hadron transition: T_c ~ 170 MeV (with a note that latest lattice QCD results give T_c ~ 160 MeV)
* Radiative energy loss of a 15 GeV gluon traversing 4 fm of gluonic plasma is compared across three different generalized gluon spectrum formulas (DA, GR, and the authors' approach) as a function of temperature
* At LHC energy (2.76 TeV/A), collisional energy loss from hard collisions (momentum transfer > 2 GeV) is about one-third of the total energy loss

## Context

This work extends prior perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark transport coefficients (Moore and Teaney, 2005; Das et al., 2010; Mustafa, 2005) by including both collisional and radiative processes and evaluating R_AA at non-zero baryon chemical potential. The results are contrasted with experimental data from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) and LHC (ALICE) collaborations. The approach enables systematic comparison of momentum-dependent versus constant drag coefficients and their effects on nuclear suppression, and opens the question of whether full hard thermal loop calculations would significantly modify the results obtained with the simpler Debye-screened propagator.
