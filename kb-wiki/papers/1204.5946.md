# Comment on "First Observation of Ground State Dineutron Decay: 16Be"

> A recent measurement [Spyrou et al., PRL 108, 102501 (2012)] of the in-flight decay of 16Be into 14Be+n+n has been interpreted as the first case of dineutron emission. Here we point out that the inclusion of the n-n interaction neglected in the description of the direct three-body decay can generate strong enhancements at low n-n relative energy and angle, as observed, without any need to invoke dineutron decay.

## Key claim

The interpretation by Spyrou et al. (2012) that the observed enhancements at low n-n relative energy and angle in the decay of 16Be constitute the first case of dineutron emission is premature, because including the n-n final-state interaction in a direct three-body decay model can produce the same enhancements without invoking a dineutron cluster.

## Method

The authors performed three calculations of 14Be+n+n decay using the same decay-energy distribution as observed by Spyrou et al. (2012): (1) pure three-body phase space, (2) three-body phase space modulated by the n-n final-state interaction (FSI) following the formalism of Lednicky and Lyuboshits (1982) as described in Marques et al. (2001), and (3) sequential dineutron decay via 14Be+2n breakup followed by 2n→n+n. For the FSI calculation, an initial n-n average separation of r_rms_nn = 4 fm was assumed.

## Key numbers

- The 16Be ground state lies approximately 1.5 MeV above the 14Be+n+n threshold, as identified by Spyrou et al. (2012).
- The n-n FSI calculation assumed an initial n-n average separation of r_rms_nn = 4 fm.
- The dineutron decay model used a 2n decay energy similar to that of Spyrou et al. (2012) (shown in their Figure 1a).

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the claim of Spyrou et al. (2012) that the decay of 16Be represents the first observation of ground state dineutron decay. The authors note that the low-energy 1S0 n-n interaction is well known to produce characteristic enhancements near zero relative momentum, as exploited in determinations of the n-n scattering length (Goncharova, 2006), and that such enhancements appear in almost any final state where two neutrons are emitted over a short time scale (Ieki et al., 1993; Marques et al., 2000, 2001; Aumann et al., 1999). The work implies that discriminating between direct three-body decay with FSI and true dineutron decay in 16Be would require higher statistics and careful treatment of the experimental response function.
