# Transverse momentum dependent PDFs at N$^3$LO

> We compute the quark and gluon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in perturbative QCD. Our calculation is based on an expansion of the differential Higgs boson and Drell-Yan production cross sections about their collinear limit. This method allows us to employ cutting edge techniques for the computation of cross sections to extract the universal building blocks in question. The corresponding perturbative matching kernels for all channels are expressed in terms of simple harmonic polylogarithms up to weight five. As a byproduct, we confirm a previous computation of the soft function for transverse momentum factorization at N$^3$LO. Our results are the last missing ingredient to extend the $q_T$ subtraction methods to N$^3$LO and to obtain resummed $q_T$ spectra at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy both for gluon as well as for quark initiated processes.

# Transverse momentum dependent PDFs at N$^3$LO

## Key claim

The authors compute the quark and gluon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) at N$^3$LO in perturbative QCD, providing the last missing ingredient to extend $q_T$ subtraction methods to N$^3$LO and to obtain resummed $q_T$ spectra at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy. However, the calculation relies on an expansion of differential cross sections about their collinear limit and is valid only in the perturbative regime where $b_T^{-1} \sim q_T \gg \Lambda_\mathrm{QCD}$.

## Method

The calculation uses the framework of collinear expansion of cross sections presented in Ebert (2020), expanding the diagrams for Drell-Yan and gluon fusion Higgs boson production cross sections at N$^3$LO in the collinear limit. The authors employ reverse unitarity to enforce measurement and on-shellness constraints, integration-by-part identities to reduce the 492 master integrals (172 for RRV contributions, 320 for RRR contributions), and the method of differential equations to obtain the cross sections differential in rapidity and transverse momentum. The bare matching kernels are extracted from the collinear limit of these differential cross sections, then renormalized using the beam function counterterm predicted from renormalization group equations.

## Key numbers

- The N$^3$LO beam function boundary term $\tilde I_{ij}^{(3)}(z)$ is expressed in terms of harmonic polylogarithms up to transcendental weight five.
- The K-factor (ratio of N$^3$LO to NNLO beam function) at canonical scales with $b_T = (10\ \mathrm{GeV})^{-1}$ and $\omega = 100\ \mathrm{GeV}$ shows a correction of approximately 0.2-0.5%.
- The eikonal limit of the beam function boundary term satisfies $\lim_{z\to1} \tilde I_{ij}^{(3)}(z) = \frac{\gamma_2^r}{64} \, \delta_{ij} \, \cL_0(1-z)$, where $\gamma_2^r$ is the three-loop coefficient of the rapidity anomalous dimension.
- The calculation requires 492 master integrals total, with 172 for RRV contributions and 320 for RRR contributions.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of TMDPDF matching kernels from NNLO (known for both quark and gluon beam functions) to N$^3$LO, filling the gap where the gluon beam function was only known at two loops from Catani (2011), Gehrmann (2014), Echevarria (2016), and Luo (2019). The results confirm the N$^3$LO soft function calculation of Li (2016) and disagree with a recent calculation by Luo (2019) in the $d_{abc} d^{abc}$ color structure, which was subsequently corrected after private communication. The N$^3$LO hard function for Drell-Yan and gluon fusion Higgs production was previously computed by Gehrmann (2010). These results enable fully-differential cross sections for color-singlet processes at N$^3$LO and TMD resummation at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy, and also apply to $q_T$-dependent event shapes such as the Transverse Energy-Energy Correlator and azimuthal angle in vector boson plus jet production in the back-to-back limit.
