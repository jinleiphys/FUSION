# Transition from direct to sequential two-proton decay in $s$-$d$ shell nuclei

> Transitions among different mechanisms of two-proton decay are studied in general. The introduced improved direct-decay model generalizes the semi-analytical models used before and provides flawless phenomenological description of three-body correlations in $2p$ decays. This is demonstrated by examples of the low-lying $^{16}$Ne state decays. Different forms of transition dynamic are shown to be highly probable beyond the proton dripline for the $s$-$d$ shell nuclei. It is demonstrated that transition dynamic of $2p$ emitters can provide means for extraction of a width of the ground-state resonance of a core+$p$ subsystem of the core+$2p$ system. Practical applicability of the method is demonstrated by properties of the $^{14}$F ground state derived from the $^{15}\mbox{Ne}\rightarrow ^{\,13\!\!}\mbox{O}+2p$ decay data and of the $^{29}$Cl ground state derived from the $^{30}\mbox{Ar}\rightarrow ^{\,28\!\!}\mbox{S}+2p$ decay data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the transition dynamic between different two-proton decay mechanisms (true, democratic, and sequential) is highly probable for s-d shell nuclei beyond the proton dripline, and this sensitivity can be exploited to extract the width of a ground-state resonance in a core+proton subsystem, provided experimental resolution and statistics are sufficient.

## Method

The authors develop an improved direct decay model (IDDM) based on R-matrix-type analytical approximations for decay amplitudes, which combines the positive features of the earlier "no p-p Coulomb" (NPP) and "effective p-p Coulomb" (EFC) approximations while correctly accounting for angular momentum coupling, proton-proton final-state interactions, and finite core mass. The model is validated by comparing its predictions for the decay of low-lying states in 16Ne against full three-body calculations, and then applied to analyze energy and angular correlations in the two-proton decays of 15Ne and 30Ar.

## Key numbers

*   Width of the 16Ne 0+ ground state: IDDM gives 4.1 keV, compared to 3.1 keV from full three-body calculations and an experimental upper limit of less than 80 keV (Brown 2014).
*   Width of the 16Ne 2+ first excited state: IDDM gives 12 keV, compared to 56 keV from three-body calculations and an experimental value of 175 plus or minus 75 keV (Brown 2015a).
*   Decay energy of the 15Ne ground state: ET = 2.52 MeV (Wamers 2014).
*   Ground-state energy and width of 14F: Er = 1.56(4) MeV, Gamma_r = 0.9(2) MeV (Goldberg 2010).
*   Derived decay energy of the 30Ar ground state: ET = 2.45 (+5, -10) MeV, based on the analysis in this work.
*   Derived width of the 29Cl ground state: Gamma_r = 85(30) keV, based on the analysis in this work.

## Context

This work extends the fragmentary considerations of transitions between true, democratic, and sequential two-proton decay mechanisms found in earlier studies (Grigorenko 2003b, Mukha 2012, Pfutzner 2012). The improved direct decay model (IDDM) generalizes the semi-analytical models of Grigorenko (2007, 2007a) and provides a tool for systematic studies of two-proton emitters. The practical applicability is demonstrated by re-analyzing data from the 15Ne decay (Wamers 2014) and the 30Ar decay (Mukha 2015), showing that the transition dynamic can be used to extract the width of the 14F and 29Cl ground states, which are otherwise difficult to measure directly.
