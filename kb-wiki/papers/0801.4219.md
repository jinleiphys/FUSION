# Statistical Ensembles with Fluctuating Extensive Quantities

> We suggest an extension of the standard concept of statistical ensembles. Namely, we introduce a class of ensembles with extensive quantities fluctuating according to an externally given distribution. As an example the influence of energy fluctuations on multiplicity fluctuations in limited segments of momentum space for a classical ultra-relativistic gas is considered.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a generalized class of statistical ensembles (the "α-ensemble") where extensive quantities like energy fluctuate according to an externally given distribution, rather than being fixed as in the micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) or Boltzmann-weighted as in the grand canonical ensemble (GCE). The key hedge is that this construction only applies when all microstates with the same set of extensive quantities are assumed equiprobable, and the authors demonstrate the effect only for a simple classical ultra-relativistic gas in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors define the α-ensemble by first calculating the micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) multiplicity distribution at fixed extensive quantities, then averaging it over an externally prescribed distribution of those quantities. For a concrete example, they take a Gaussian energy distribution with width controlled by a parameter α, where α=0 reproduces the MCE and α=1 reproduces the GCE energy fluctuations. They then compute the resulting multiplicity distribution and its scaled variance in full momentum space and in limited momentum bins for a classical ultra-relativistic Boltzmann gas of massless particles.

## Key numbers

- For the MCE (α=0) in the thermodynamic limit, the scaled variance of the total multiplicity is ω_mce = 1/4.
- For the GCE (α=1) in the thermodynamic limit, the scaled variance of the total multiplicity is ω_gce = 1.
- The scaled variance of the α-ensemble total multiplicity follows ω_α = ω_mce + α²(ω_gce - ω_mce), giving ω_α = 1/4, 7/16, 1, and 31/16 for α = 0, 0.5, 1, and 1.5 respectively.
- The GCE energy expectation value is E_bar = 3gVT⁴/π², and the GCE scaled variance of energy fluctuations is ω_E = 4T.
- The average multiplicity in the MCE thermodynamic limit is ⟨N⟩ ≈ (x/27)^{1/4} where x = gVE³/π².

## Context

This work extends the standard statistical ensemble framework (MCE, CE, GCE) by introducing a tunable parameter α that interpolates between fixed-energy (MCE) and Boltzmann-weighted energy (GCE) fluctuations. It builds on prior findings by the same group (fluc, mce, res, 2005-2007) that even in the thermodynamic limit, scaled variances of multiplicity distributions differ between ensembles, unlike mean values which become equivalent. The formalism enables future quantitative comparisons with event-by-event multiplicity fluctuation measurements in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, provided it is extended to include non-zero masses, quantum statistics, multiple particle species, and several conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) as outlined in the paper.
