# Neutrino Induced Charged Current 1$π^+$ Production At Intermediate Energies

> The charged current one pion production induced by $ν_μ$ from nucleons and nuclei like $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O nuclei has been studied. The calculations have been done for the incoherent and the coherent processes from nuclear targets assuming the $Δ$ dominance model and take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion of the nucleon and renormalization of $Δ$ properties in a nuclear medium. The effect of final state interactions of pions has been taken into account. The theoretical uncertainty in the total cross sections due to various parameterizations of the weak transition form factors used in literature has been studied. The numerical results for the total cross sections are compared with the recent preliminary results from the MiniBooNE collaboration on $^{12}$C and could be useful in analyzing future data from the K2K collaboration.

# Neutrino Induced Charged Current 1π⁺ Production At Intermediate Energies

## Key claim

The delta dominance model, when including nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, delta self-energy) and final state interactions of pions, can describe the MiniBooNE ratio of charged current 1π⁺ production to quasielastic scattering on ¹²C only below Eν = 1.0 GeV; at higher energies the model underestimates the data, suggesting that higher mass resonance excitations become important.

## Method

The authors calculated charged current single π⁺ production from nucleons and from ¹²C and ¹⁶O nuclei using the delta dominance model, where a Δ resonance is excited by the neutrino and decays to a nucleon and pion. For nuclear targets, they treated incoherent and coherent processes separately in a local density approximation, incorporating Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, renormalization of the Δ mass and width via a self-energy (including two- and three-body absorption channels), and final state interactions of pions using an eikonal approximation with a pion optical potential. They compared results using three different parameterizations of the N-Δ weak transition form factors: those of Schreiner and von Hippel (1973), Paschos et al., and Lalakulich et al.

## Key numbers

- For the incoherent process on ¹²C, nuclear medium effects alone reduce the cross section by 12-15% for neutrino energies Eν = 0.7-2 GeV; including pion absorption gives a total reduction of 30-40%.
- For the coherent process on ¹²C, nuclear medium effects reduce the cross section by 45% at Eν = 0.7 GeV, 35% at Eν = 1 GeV, and 25% at Eν = 2 GeV; including pion absorption gives a suppression of 80% at Eν around 1 GeV and 70% at Eν around 2 GeV.
- The coherent process contributes less than 4-5% of the total charged current 1π⁺ production in the neutrino energy region of 1-2 GeV.
- A 20% change in the axial dipole mass MA (from 1.0 to 1.2 GeV) results in a change of around 20% in the total cross section for 1π⁺ production on ¹²C.
- The theoretical uncertainty in the total cross section due to different N-Δ transition form factor parameterizations is 10-20% in the energy region 0.7-2.0 GeV.
- For the ratio of CC1π⁺ to CCQE on ¹²C, there is an additional uncertainty of 2-3% from different quasielastic form factor parameterizations.
- For the incoherent process differential cross section dσ/dQ² averaged over the MiniBooNE spectrum, nuclear medium effects give a reduction of about 14% in the peak region; including final state interactions gives a total reduction of about 38%.

## Context

This work extends the delta dominance model developed by Oset and collaborators to neutrino-induced pion production on ¹²C and ¹⁶O at intermediate energies relevant for the MiniBooNE and K2K experiments. It contrasts with neutrino event generators like NUANCE and NEUGEN, which the authors note do not include nuclear effects appropriately in their resonance production models and treat pion absorption in an ad hoc way. The results enable improved analysis of MiniBooNE and K2K data by providing a microscopic treatment of nuclear medium effects, though the model's underestimation of data above 1 GeV indicates that higher resonance contributions (beyond the Δ) must be included in future work, as also noted by Cassing et al. in a separate model.
