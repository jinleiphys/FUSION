# Solving the Nose-Hoover thermostat for Nuclear Pasta

> In this work we present a calculation of the hamiltonian variables solving the molecular dynamics equations of motion for a system of nuclear matter relevant to the description of nuclear pasta. The temperature is kept fixed by using the Nosé-Hoover Thermostat and the interaction is modelled via a semiclassical potential depending on both positions and momenta.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that molecular dynamics simulations of nuclear pasta phases can be performed at fixed temperature using the Nosé-Hoover thermostat with a semiclassical potential that depends on both positions and momenta, but the temperature control quality depends on the thermal inertial parameter Q, with lower Q giving better control at the cost of inducing rapid oscillations in the thermostat energy.

## Method

The author solves the extended Hamiltonian equations of motion for a system of A nucleons interacting via a potential that includes a hadronic term (with intermediate-range attraction and short-range repulsion), a screened Coulomb interaction with screening length set to 10 fm, and a Pauli potential that enforces phase-space repulsion for fermions via Kronecker deltas in spin and isospin. The Nosé-Hoover thermostat is implemented with an extended variable s and its conjugate momentum, and the equations are integrated using a Numerov-type algorithm that achieves order 8 or 12 accuracy. Simulations are performed with up to 1,000 particles in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, at fixed baryon density, lepton fraction, and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Thermal inertial parameter Q is set in the range \(10^6\) to \(10^8\) MeV \((fm/c)^2\)
- Simulation at baryon density \(n_b = 0.016\) fm\(^{-3}\) (about one-tenth of normal nuclear density), temperature \(T = 1\) MeV, and electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.2\)
- Maximum relative extended energy error \(\Delta E/E = 10^{-4}\) over time \(\Delta t = 5 \times 10^3\) fm/c using timesteps \(dt = 0.025\) fm/c and \(Q = 10^6\) MeV \((fm/c)^2\)
- Timesteps used in simulations range from \(dt = 0.01\) to \(0.1\) fm/c
- Thermalization times are of order \(10^5\) fm/c
- Model parameters: \(a = 133\) MeV, \(b = -47\) MeV, \(c = 11\) MeV, \(d = 29\) MeV, \(q_0 = 3\) fm, \(p_0 = 120\) MeV/c, \(\Lambda = 1.5\) fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of nuclear pasta phases (pasta1, pasta2, pasta3) by incorporating a momentum-dependent potential that mimics the Pauli principle for fermionic nucleons, following the approach of Dorso (year not given) and Peilert (year not given). The use of the Nosé-Hoover thermostat enables simulations in the NVT ensemble, which is relevant for modeling neutron-rich plasmas in supernova core collapse conditions. The results enable future studies of dynamical responses in low-energy excitation modes of nuclear pasta, though the induced oscillations from the thermostat must be carefully considered.
