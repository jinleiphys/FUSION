# Effects of Symmetry Energy on the Equation of State for Hybrid Neutron Stars

> In this paper, the implications of the symmetry energy on the hadron and quark phase transitions in the compact star, including the properties of the possible configurations of the quark-hadron hybrid stars, are investigated in the frameworks of the energy-density functional (EDF) models and the flavor SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the help of the Schwinger's covariant proper-time regularization (PTR) scheme. In this {theoretical setup}, the equations of states (EoSs) of hadronic matter for various values of symmetry energies obtained from the EDF models are employed to describe the hadronic matter, and the {flavor} SU(2) NJL model with various repulsive-vector interaction strengths are used to describe the quark matter. We then observe the obtained EoS in the mass-radius properties of the hybrid star configurations for various vector interactions and nuclear symmetry energies by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation. We obtain that the critical density at which the phase transition occurs varies over the density (3.6--6.7)$ρ_0$ depending on the symmetry energy and the strength of the vector coupling $G_v$. The maximum mass of the neutron star (NS) is susceptible to $G_v$. When there is no repulsive force, the NS maximum mass is only about $1.5M_\odot$, but it becomes larger than $2.0M_\odot$ when the vector coupling constant is about half of the {attractive} scalar coupling constant. Surprisingly, the presence of the quark matter does not affect the canonical mass of NS ($1.4M_\odot$), so observing the canonical mass of NSs can provide unique constraints to the EoS of hadronic matter at high densities.

## Key claim

The critical density at which hadronic matter transitions to quark matter in hybrid neutron stars varies between 3.6 and 6.7 times nuclear saturation density, depending on both the nuclear symmetry energy in the hadronic phase and the strength of the repulsive vector coupling in the quark phase; the presence of quark matter does not affect the canonical mass neutron star of 1.4 solar masses, so observations of that mass can constrain only the hadronic equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the KIDS energy-density functional models (KIDS0, KIDS-A, KIDS-B, KIDS-C, KIDS-D) and the Skyrme SLy4 model to describe hadronic matter, and the flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Schwinger proper-time regularization to describe quark matter. They determined the critical density for the hadron-to-quark phase transition by finding the density where the pressures of the two phases are equal, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid neutron star configurations with different vector coupling strengths and symmetry energies.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for phase transition ranges from 2.86 to 6.65 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0) across all model combinations; for example, KIDS-C with G_v=0.00 G_s gives rho_c=2.86 rho_0, while KIDS-A with G_v=0.50 G_s gives rho_c=6.65 rho_0
- Maximum neutron star mass is about 1.5 solar masses when the vector coupling G_v=0.00 G_s, but exceeds 2.0 solar masses when G_v=0.50 G_s (half the scalar coupling constant)
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is unaffected by the presence of quark matter, remaining determined solely by the hadronic equation of state
- The NJL model parameters were fitted to pion mass m_pi=0.14 GeV and pion decay constant f_pi=0.093 GeV, giving G_s=3.17 GeV^{-2}, Lambda_UV=1.0789 GeV, constituent quark mass M_0=0.20 GeV, and current quark mass m_q=0.0055 GeV

## Context

This work extends the investigation of symmetry energy effects on hyperon appearance in neutron stars from Hutauruk et al. (2022) to the hadron-quark phase transition, using the KIDS functional framework previously developed by Papakonstantinou et al. (2016). The results differ quantitatively from prior hybrid star calculations using relativistic mean-field models by Malfatti et al. (2020) and chiral effective field theory by Kojo et al. (2022), particularly in the required strength of vector repulsion (G_v=0.50 G_s in this work versus larger values in those studies) and in the predicted radii of massive neutron stars. The finding that canonical mass neutron stars are unaffected by quark matter enables future precise measurements of the 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius to provide clean constraints on the hadronic equation of state at densities up to three times nuclear saturation.
