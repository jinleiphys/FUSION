# Precise determination of the f0(600) and f0(980) pole parameters from a dispersive data analysis

> We use our latest dispersive analysis of pion-pion scattering data and the very recent Kl4 experimental results to obtain the mass, width and couplings of the two lightest scalar-isoscalar resonances. These parameters are defined from their associated poles in the complex plane. The analytic continuation to the complex plane is made in a model independent way by means of once and twice subtracted dispersion relations for the partial waves, without any other theoretical assumption. We find the f0(600) pole at (457^{+14}_{-13})-i(279^{+11}_{-7}) MeV and that of the f0(980) at (996\pm7)-i(25^{+10}_{-6}) MeV, whereas their respective couplings to two pions are 3.59^{+0.11}_{-0.13} GeV and 2.3\pm0.2 GeV.

# Precise determination of the f0(600) and f0(980) pole parameters from a dispersive data analysis

## Key claim

The f0(600) and f0(980) pole parameters are determined from data alone using once- and twice-subtracted dispersion relations (GKPY and Roy equations) without chiral perturbation theory input, yielding results compatible with ChPT-based analyses but with the f0(980) width no longer as narrow as typical unconstrained fits to pion-pion scattering data.

## Method

The authors first performed unconstrained fits (UFD) to pion-pion scattering data and recent NA48/2 K_{l4} decay data for each partial wave up to 1420 MeV, with Regge fits above that energy. They then obtained constrained fits to data (CFD) by varying the UFD parameters to satisfy crossing sum rules, forward dispersion relations, and both Roy and GKPY equations simultaneously while describing the data. The CFD parametrization was used as input for the dispersion relations to provide a model-independent analytic continuation to the complex plane, determining the second Riemann sheet pole positions and residues.

## Key numbers

- f0(600) pole from GKPY equations: (457^{+14}_{-13}) - i(279^{+11}_{-7}) MeV
- f0(980) pole from GKPY equations: (996 ± 7) - i(25^{+10}_{-6}) MeV
- f0(600) coupling to two pions from GKPY: 3.59^{+0.11}_{-0.13} GeV
- f0(980) coupling to two pions from GKPY: 2.3 ± 0.2 GeV
- f0(600) pole from Roy equations: (445 ± 25) - i(278^{+22}_{-18}) MeV
- f0(980) pole from Roy equations: (1003^{+5}_{-27}) - i(21^{+10}_{-8}) MeV
- rho(770) pole from GKPY: (763.7^{+1.7}_{-1.5}) - i(73.2^{+1.0}_{-1.1}) MeV
- f0(980) width from GKPY: 50^{+20}_{-12} MeV (derived from half-width of 25^{+10}_{-6} MeV)
- Systematic uncertainty from kaon mass treatment on f0(980) half-width: ±4.4 MeV for GKPY equations

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis program using Roy equations previously applied to low-energy pion-pion scattering by Ananthanarayan (2000), Colangelo (2001), and Caprini (2005), where the latter found the f0(600) at 441 - i272 MeV and f0(980) at 1001 - i14 MeV using ChPT input. The key advance is the use of GKPY equations developed by the same group (Garcia-Martin, 2011), which have only one subtraction and thus much smaller uncertainties in the resonance region than standard Roy equations, enabling a data-only determination. The results settle the long-standing "dip" versus "no-dip" controversy in the inelasticity above 1 GeV (Au, 1986) and show that the f0(980) width from scattering data alone is compatible with production experiment results, correcting the tendency toward a too-narrow f0(980) from unconstrained fits.
