# In-medium properties of SU(3) baryons

> Changes of baryon properties in nuclear matter are investigated within the framework of an in-medium modified SU(3) Skyrme model. Introducing the medium functionals in the SU(2) sector and considering the alteration of kaon properties in nuclear medium, we are able to examine the medium modification of the nucleon and hyperons. The functionals introduced in the SU(2) sector are related to ordinary nuclear matter properties near the saturation point. The results indicate that the changes of the baryon properties in the strange sector are strongly correlated with the in-medium properties of kaons.

# In-medium properties of SU(3) baryons

## Key claim
The changes of baryon properties in the strange sector are strongly correlated with the in-medium properties of kaons, but the analysis is qualitative because the parameter controlling kaon medium modification (C) is not fixed by data and is instead varied to explore trends.

## Method
The authors extend an in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model to SU(3) by introducing density-dependent functionals that reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties near saturation, and by incorporating kaon medium effects through a linear density-dependent modification of the kaon decay constant and/or mass. The model is quantized via rigid rotation of the static soliton, and baryon masses are computed as functions of nuclear matter density for two values of the kaon medium parameter (C = 0 and C = 0.2).

## Key numbers
- Free-space input parameters: pion decay constant Fπ = 108.783 MeV, Skyrme parameter e = 4.854, pion mass mπ = 134.976 MeV, kaon mass mK = 495 MeV.
- Density-dependent coefficients fixed from nuclear matter: C1 = -0.279, C2 = 0.737, C3 = 1.782.
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ0, the Λ* mass decreases by about 17% according to SU(3) chiral effective field theory (Petschauer 2015), cited for comparison.
- At ρ0, the Ξ* mass changes by about 6% for C = 0 and about 16% for C = 0.2.
- Mass splittings among octet members (except m*Σ - m*Λ) tend to decrease up to (1.5-2)ρ0.

## Context
This work extends the in-medium Skyrme model approach of Yakhshiev (2013) from the non-strangeness SU(2) sector to the full SU(3) sector including hyperons. The results are compared qualitatively with SU(3) chiral effective field theory (Petschauer 2015). The study enables future investigations of hyperon properties at higher densities relevant to neutron star interiors, as discussed by Gal (2016) and Lattimer (2015).
