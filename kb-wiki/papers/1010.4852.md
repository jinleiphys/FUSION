# Effects of periodic potentials on the critical velocity of superfluid Fermi gases in the BCS-BEC crossover

> We study the effects of an external periodic potential on the critical velocity of a superfluid Fermi gas in the crossover between the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase and Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC). We numerically solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations to model a three-dimensional (3D) gas of ultracold atoms in the superfluid phase flowing through a 1D optical lattice. We find that when the recoil energy is comparable to the Fermi energy, the presence of the periodic potential reduces the effect of pair-breaking excitations. This behavior is a consequence of the peculiar band structure of the quasiparticle energy spectrum in the lattice. When the lattice height is much larger than the Fermi energy, the periodic potential makes pairs of atoms to be strongly bound even in the BCS regime and pair-breaking excitations are further suppressed. We have also found that when the recoil energy is comparable to or larger than the Fermi energy, the critical velocity due to long-wavelength phonon excitations shows a non-monotonic behavior along the BCS-BEC crossover.

## Key claim

The periodic potential reduces the effect of pair-breaking excitations when the recoil energy is comparable to the Fermi energy, and further suppresses them when the lattice height is much larger than the Fermi energy; the critical velocity due to long-wavelength phonon excitations shows a non-monotonic behavior along the BCS-BEC crossover when the recoil energy is comparable to or larger than the Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for a three-dimensional superfluid Fermi gas uniform in the transverse directions and subject to a one-dimensional optical lattice along the z-direction. They calculated the energy density as a function of average density and quasimomentum, then determined the critical velocity for energetic instability from two conditions: the hydrodynamic condition for long-wavelength phonon excitations and the condition that quasiparticle energies become negative for fermionic pair-breaking excitations.

## Key numbers

- For $E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=0.1$, $s=5$, and $1/k_{\rm F}a_s=-1$: the energetic instability due to long-wavelength phonons occurs at $P=0.226\hbar q_{\rm B}=0.452P_{\rm edge}$, with critical velocity $v_c=0.0662 v_{\rm F}$
- For $E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=0.1$, $s=5$ at unitarity: $v_c=0.0429 v_{\rm F}$
- For $E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=0.1$, $s=5$ at $1/k_{\rm F}a_s=1$: $v_c=0.0302 v_{\rm F}$
- The sound velocity of a uniform system at unitarity is $c_s^{(0)}/v_{\rm F}=(1+\beta)^{1/2}/\sqrt{3}\simeq 0.443$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BdG study at unitarity (Watanabe et al., 2009) to the full BCS-BEC crossover, and builds on earlier theoretical work on critical velocity in uniform Fermi gases by Andrenacci et al. and Combescot et al. The results show that periodic potentials produce effects unique to Fermi superfluids, not present in single barrier potentials studied by the same group. The findings enable future investigations of roton-like minima in the bosonic dispersion curve using quasiparticle random phase approximation, and suggest connections to nuclear pasta phases in neutron star crusts.
