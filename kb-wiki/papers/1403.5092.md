# Pairing properties of the inner crust of neutron stars at finite temperature

> We investigate the thermal properties of the inner crust of a neutron star using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) formalism at finite temperature. We compare our results with the ones obtained solving the same equations, but within the BCS approximation. We observe that for the outermost regions of the inner crust, the two methods can show important differences, in particular when we use them to calculate the neutron specific heat of the system.

# Pairing properties of the inner crust of neutron stars at finite temperature

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) formalism and the BCS approximation yield significantly different predictions for the neutron specific heat in the outermost (low-density) regions of the inner crust of a neutron star, with the BCS approximation missing a second phase transition that appears only in the full HFB treatment.

## Method

The author solves the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations at finite temperature for ten Wigner-Seitz cells representing different density layers of the inner crust, using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction for the particle-hole channel and a separable form of the Gogny D1 interaction for the particle-particle channel. The same equations are then solved within the BCS approximation for comparison. The cells are characterized by proton number Z, neutron number N, Wigner-Seitz radius R_WS, average density, and background neutron density, following the Negele and Vautherin (1973) prescription.

## Key numbers

- Ten Wigner-Seitz cells are studied, with Z ranging from 40 to 50, N from 140 to 1750, and R_WS from 19.6 fm to 53.6 fm; the average cell density ranges from 2.79 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ to 4.76 × 10⁻² fm⁻³.
- For the low-density cell ²⁰⁰Zr (Z=40, N=160, R_WS=49.2 fm, average density 4.01 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³), the first critical temperature for the disappearance of pairing in the neutron gas is k_B T_c¹ = 0.1 MeV, consistent with the BCS formula k_B T_c = (exp(ζ)/π) Δ(T=0) where ζ ≈ 0.577.
- A second critical temperature k_B T_c² appears only in the HFB calculation for the ²⁰⁰Zr cell, corresponding to the disappearance of pairing inside the nuclear cluster; the BCS approximation predicts only one phase transition.
- The neutron Fermi momentum k_F^n for the cells ranges from 0.13 fm⁻¹ (zone 11, lowest density) to 1.08 fm⁻¹ (zone 2, highest density).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature investigation of Pastore (2013) on pairing properties of Wigner-Seitz cells to include finite temperature effects. It directly compares the full HFB approach with the computationally simpler BCS approximation, motivated by earlier studies of the inner crust structure by Negele and Vautherin (1973) and subsequent work on pairing interactions by Fortin (2010) and Pizzochero (2002). The finding that only HFB captures the two-peak structure of the neutron specific heat in low-density cells, also observed by Fortin (2010) using density-dependent contact pairing interactions, establishes that the BCS approximation is inadequate for thermal calculations in the outermost inner crust. This result is important for modeling the cooling times of isolated neutron stars, as the specific heat directly affects thermal evolution.
