# Heavy Quark Momentum Diffusion Coefficient from Lattice QCD

> The momentum diffusion coefficient for heavy quarks is studied in a deconfined gluon plasma in the static approximation by investigating a correlation function of the color electric field using Monte Carlo techniques. The diffusion coefficient is extracted from the long distance behavior of such a correlator. For temperatures Tc < T <~ 2 Tc, our nonperturbative estimate of the diffusion coefficient is found to be very different from the leading order perturbation theory, and is in the right ballpark to explain the heavy quark flow seen by PHENIX at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors nonperturbatively extract the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from lattice QCD in a pure gluon plasma and find it is nearly an order of magnitude smaller than the leading-order perturbative result, which is the direction needed to explain the large heavy quark elliptic flow observed at RHIC, but the extraction relies on a specific ansatz for the spectral function and the choice of a cutoff parameter Λ.

## Method

The momentum diffusion coefficient κ is obtained from the long-distance behavior of the Euclidean correlator of color electric fields connected by timelike Wilson lines, computed using Monte Carlo simulations with a multilevel algorithm on lattices with Nt = 20–24. The spectral function ρ(ω) is modeled with a two-parameter ansatz: a linear term a ω for the diffusive part (giving κ = limω→0 2T ρ(ω)/ω) plus a leading-order perturbative b ω³ term, with a cutoff Λ separating the two regimes; the correlator is then fitted in the range τa ∈ [Nt/4, Nt/2] using χ² minimization with the full covariance matrix.

## Key numbers

- At T = 1.5 Tc, the leading-order perturbative estimate gives DT ≈ 14, while the lattice result is nearly an order of magnitude smaller.
- The multilevel algorithm is about 300 times more efficient than the standard algorithm for GE(3) on a β = 6.9, Nt = 20, Ns = 36 lattice, and about 2000 times more efficient for GE(10).
- The nonperturbative αs extracted from the b ω³ coefficient is about 0.25 near Tc and decreases to about 0.18 at 2 Tc.
- The diffusive part contributes nearly 20% of the total correlator at τT = 0.5 for the T = 1.5 Tc data set.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Casalderrey-Solana and Teaney (2006) and Caron-Huot et al. (2009) for extracting the heavy quark diffusion coefficient from electric field correlators in the static limit, applying it to pure SU(3) gauge theory on the lattice. It contradicts the leading-order perturbative result of Moore and Teaney (2005), which gives DT values an order of magnitude larger, and addresses the discrepancy between perturbation theory and the heavy quark flow observed by the PHENIX collaboration at RHIC. The results enable more reliable input for Langevin-based models of heavy quark thermalization in the quark-gluon plasma and motivate further lattice studies with dynamical quarks and improved spectral function reconstruction.
