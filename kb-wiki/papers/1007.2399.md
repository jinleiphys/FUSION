# Relativistic U(3) Symmetry and Pseudo-U(3) Symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian

> The Dirac Hamiltonian with relativistic scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials has been solved analytically in two limits. One is the spin limit for which spin is an invariant symmetry of the the Dirac Hamiltonian and the other is the pseudo-spin limit for which pseudo-spin is an invariant symmetry of the the Dirac Hamiltonian. The spin limit occurs when the scalar potential is equal to the vector potential plus a constant, and the pseudospin limit occurs when the scalar potential is equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to the vector potential plus a constant. Like the non-relativistic harmonic oscillator, each of these limits has a higher symmetry. For example, for the spherically symmetric oscillator, these limits have a U(3) and pseudo-U(3) symmetry respectively. We shall discuss the eigenfunctions and eigenvalues of these two limits and derive the relativistic generators for the U(3) and pseudo-U(3) symmetry. We also argue, that, if an anti-nucleon can be bound in a nucleus, the spectrum will have approximate spin and U(3) symmetry.

## Key claim

The Dirac Hamiltonian with relativistic scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials has a higher U(3) symmetry in the spin limit (scalar equals vector plus constant) and a pseudo-U(3) symmetry in the pseudospin limit (scalar equals minus vector plus constant); if an anti-nucleon can be bound in a nucleus, its spectrum will have approximate spin and U(3) symmetry, though the pseudospin limit does not produce bound valence states and thus perturbation theory cannot describe nuclei even though they exhibit approximate pseudospin symmetry.

## Method

The Dirac Hamiltonian with spherical harmonic oscillator potentials was solved analytically in two limits: the spin symmetry limit (scalar potential equals vector potential plus a constant) and the pseudospin symmetry limit (scalar potential equals minus vector potential plus a constant). The relativistic generators for the U(3) and pseudo-U(3) algebras were derived by requiring that the quadrupole and monopole operators commute with the Dirac Hamiltonian and form closed commutation relations. The pseudospin Hamiltonian was obtained from the spin Hamiltonian via a gamma-5 transformation with a sign change of the mass.

## Key numbers

- For the spin symmetry limit, the eigenvalue equation is sqrt(E_N + M)(E_N - M) = sqrt(2 hbar^2 omega^2 M)(N + 3/2), where N = 2n + ell is the total harmonic oscillator quantum number.
- For the spin symmetry limit, the binding energy for large M (non-relativistic limit) is E_N - M approx hbar omega (N + 3/2).
- For the spin symmetry limit, the spectrum for small M (large A_N) goes as E_N approx M (A_N^(2/3) + 1/3 + ...), where A_N = sqrt(2) hbar omega (N + 3/2)/M.
- For the pseudospin symmetry limit, the eigenvalue equation is sqrt(E_tildeN - M)(E_tildeN + M) = sqrt(2 hbar^2 omega^2 M)(tildeN + 3/2), where tildeN = 2 tilde n + tilde ell is the pseudo total harmonic oscillator quantum number.
- For the pseudospin symmetry limit, even for large M the binding energy goes as E_tildeN - M approx M (A_tildeN^2/4 + ...), where A_tildeN = sqrt(2) hbar omega (tildeN + 3/2)/M, showing quadratic dependence on the total pseudo-number of quanta.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic harmonic oscillator solutions of Ginocchio (2004) by explicitly constructing the U(3) and pseudo-U(3) symmetry generators that commute with the Dirac Hamiltonian in the spin and pseudospin limits. Pseudospin symmetry, which explains the approximate degeneracy of pseudospin doublets in nuclei (Ginocchio 2005), occurs when the sum of vector and scalar potentials is constant (Ginocchio 1997); spin symmetry, relevant for hadrons and anti-nucleons (Ginocchio 1999), occurs when their difference is constant (Bell 1975). The derived generators enable future work on axially deformed relativistic harmonic oscillators, which will have U(2)xU(1) and pseudo-U(2)xpseudo-U(1) symmetries, and provide a framework for understanding anti-nucleon spectra if anti-nucleons can be bound in nuclei (Burov 2002).
