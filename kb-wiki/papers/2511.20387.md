# Anisotropic flows in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.4\,\text{GeV}$ with a Skyrme pseudopotential

> Within the framework of the lattice Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, we present a systematic study of proton anisotropic flow observables measured by the HADES collaboration, by utilizing the recently developed density-, momentum- and isospin-dependent N$5$LO Skyrme pseudopotential. In particular, we investigate the impacts of the momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potentials, the stiffness of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) EOS, the high-density behaviors of the symmetry energy and the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections on proton $v_{1}$, $v_{2}$, $v_{3}$, and $v_{4}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.4\,\text{GeV}$. Our results show that the proton anisotropic flows are strongly sensitive to the momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potential as well as the incompressibility coefficient $K_0$ of SNM. In addition, the transverse momentum dependence of the proton $v_2$ exhibits a modest sensitivity to the higher-order skewness coefficient $J_0$ and kurtosis coefficient $I_0$ of SNM as well as the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential, while the transverse momentum dependence of the proton $v_1$ is shown to modestly depend on the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections. Moreover, the high-density symmetry energy seems to have limited effects on the proton anisotropic flows. These findings highlight the necessity of considering the momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potentials including the symmetry potential, the higher-order characteristic parameters of SNM EOS beyond $K_0$, and the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections, in future Bayesian transport model analyses on proton anisotropic flows in heavy-ion collisions at HADES energies, thereby to extract information on nuclear matter EOS as well as the associated underlying nuclear effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that proton anisotropic flows in Au+Au collisions at HADES energies are strongly sensitive to the momentum dependence of the nucleon mean-field potential and the incompressibility coefficient \(K_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter, while the high-density symmetry energy has limited effects on these observables. The authors qualify that these findings establish a foundation for future Bayesian analyses to extract nuclear matter equation of state information, but the results are specific to the lattice BUU transport model with the N5LO Skyrme pseudopotential framework.

## Method

The authors employed the lattice Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (LBUU) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.4\,\text{GeV}\), using the recently developed density-, momentum-, and isospin-dependent N5LO Skyrme pseudopotential with fourth-order density-dependent terms (N5LODD4). They systematically varied the momentum dependence of nucleon mean-field potentials, the stiffness of symmetric nuclear matter equation of state (characterized by \(K_0\), \(J_0\), and \(I_0\)), the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, and the in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections, then calculated the proton directed (\(v_1\)), elliptic (\(v_2\)), triangular (\(v_3\)), and quadrangular (\(v_4\)) flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The beam energy corresponds to \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.4\,\text{GeV}\) (incident beam energy \(E_{\rm beam} = 1.23A\,\text{GeV}\)) for Au+Au collisions.
- The in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section parameterization uses \(\alpha = 1.8\) in \(\sigma_{\mathrm{NN}}^* = \sigma_{\mathrm{NN}}^{\mathrm{free}} \exp\{-\alpha (\rho/\rho_0) / [1 + (T_{\mathrm{c.m.}}/(0.15\,\text{GeV}))^2]\}\).
- The baseline interaction SP10DD4L45D03 uses a slope parameter of the symmetry energy \(L = 45\,\text{MeV}\) and a linear isospin splitting coefficient of the nucleon effective mass at saturation density \(\Delta m_{1}^{\ast}(\rho_0) = 0.3\).
- The lattice spacing was set to \(1\,\text{fm}\) with \(100,000\) test particles, and the evolution was terminated at \(60\,\text{fm}/c\) with a time step of \(0.2\,\text{fm}/c\).
- A constant isotropic elastic scattering cross section of \(\sigma = 40\,\text{mb}\) was adopted for two-body elastic scatterings between baryons other than nucleon-nucleon scatterings.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flows in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the HADES collaboration measurements (HADES 2020, HADES 2022), by using the highly flexible N5LO Skyrme pseudopotential developed by Wang (2024) that can reproduce the empirical nucleon optical potential up to \(2\,\text{GeV}\). The study builds upon the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP 2016, 2017, 2019, 2021, 2022, 2023) efforts to quantify uncertainties in transport models, and enables future Bayesian inference analyses to extract nuclear matter equation of state parameters and nucleon single-particle potential information from heavy-ion collision data at HADES energies.
