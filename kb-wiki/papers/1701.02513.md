# pNRQCD determination of E1 radiative transitions

> This contribution contains the first numerical computation of the complete set of relativistic corrections of relative order $v^{2}$ for electric dipole (E1) transitions in heavy quarkonium; in particular, for the processes $χ_{bJ}(1P) \to Υ(1S) + γ$ with $J=0,\,1,\,2$. We assume that the momentum transfer of the heavy mesons involved in the reactions lies in the weak-coupling regime of the low-energy effective field theory potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and thus a full perturbative calculation can be performed.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first numerical computation of the complete set of relativistic corrections of relative order v² for electric dipole (E1) transitions in heavy quarkonium, specifically for χ_bJ(1P) → Υ(1S) + γ with J=0,1,2, under the assumption that the momentum transfer lies in the strict weak-coupling regime of pNRQCD where non-perturbative effects are suppressed and a full perturbative calculation can be performed.

## Method
The authors compute the decay width for χ_bJ(1P) → Υ(1S) + γ using potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) in the weak-coupling regime, where the static potential and higher-order potentials are treated perturbatively. They include relativistic corrections from higher-order electromagnetic interaction terms in the pNRQCD Lagrangian, corrections to the initial and final wave functions from higher-order potentials (including radiative corrections to the static potential at O(α_s) and O(α_s²), 1/m and 1/m² potentials), and corrections from higher-order Fock states involving singlet-octet transitions. The wave function corrections are computed using quantum mechanical perturbation theory with the Coulomb Green function, assuming hydrogen-like wave functions from the leading-order Schrödinger equation with a Coulomb potential.

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD framework previously applied to M1 transitions by Brambilla et al. (2005) and Pineda (2013) to the more complex case of E1 transitions, for which the formulae were presented by Brambilla et al. (2012) but numerical computations had been hindered by the complexity of the corrections. The results enable model-independent, systematic comparisons with experimental data from B-factories (BaBar, Belle, CLEO), tau-charm facilities (CLEO-c, BESIII), and hadron colliders (CDF, D0, LHCb, ATLAS, CMS), and provide a foundation for future calculations that include non-perturbative effects or extend to the strong-coupling regime of pNRQCD.
