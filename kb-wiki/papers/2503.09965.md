# Effects of symmetry energy on the properties of hadron-quark mixed phase in hybrid stars

> We study how the nuclear symmetry energy slope affects the properties of hadron-quark pasta phases and their sizes in massive hybrid stars ($> 2 M_{\odot}$). We utilize the relativistic mean-field model with a density-dependent isovector coupling constant fitted by varying values of the symmetry energy slope $L$ to describe the hadronic matter, while the quark matter is described by a modified MIT bag model with vector interactions. We construct the hadron-quark phase transition using the energy minimization (EM) method and compare with the Gibbs construction (GC) in the hybrid star. We find that the massive hybrid stars generally tend to have a hadron-quark pasta phase core rather than a pure quark core for most values of $L$, except in some special cases where they have a small pure quark core. Furthermore, we show that the mass and size of the pasta phase core are strongly influenced by the symmetry energy. The radius associated with the onset of pasta phases is also influenced by the symmetry energy. Additionally, we also evaluate the effects of the surface tension $σ$, the bag constant $B$, and the vector interaction $G_{V}$.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy slope $L$ strongly influences the mass and size of the hadron-quark pasta phase core in massive hybrid stars (above 2 $M_{\odot}$), with massive hybrid stars generally having a pasta phase core rather than a pure quark core for most values of $L$, except in some special cases where a small pure quark core appears.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field model with a density-dependent isovector coupling constant (RMFL model with NL3L parametrization) to describe hadronic matter, fitting the symmetry energy slope $L$ by adjusting the parameter $a_\rho$ while keeping saturation properties unchanged. Quark matter was described by a modified MIT bag model with vector interactions (vMIT model). The hadron-quark phase transition was constructed using the energy minimization (EM) method within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, which incorporates finite-size effects including surface and Coulomb contributions, and compared with the Gibbs construction (GC) method.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass ranges from 1.813 $M_{\odot}$ (for $L$ = 118.5 MeV, $G_V$ = 0.1 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 40 MeV fm$^{-2}$) to 2.355 $M_{\odot}$ (for $L$ = 30 MeV, $G_V$ = 0.2 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 60 MeV fm$^{-2}$)
- For $L$ = 30 MeV with $G_V$ = 0.2 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 40 MeV fm$^{-2}$: mixed phase mass fraction is 49.2% of total mass (0.956 $M_{\odot}$ out of 2.263 $M_{\odot}$) and radius fraction is 63.2% (8.288 km out of 13.11 km)
- For $L$ = 118.5 MeV with $G_V$ = 0.2 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 40 MeV fm$^{-2}$: mixed phase mass fraction is 59.4% of total mass (1.294 $M_{\odot}$ out of 2.178 $M_{\odot}$) and radius fraction is 71.1% (9.365 km out of 13.18 km)
- Onset density of pasta phases for $L$ = 30 MeV with $G_V$ = 0.2 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 40 MeV fm$^{-2}$: droplet at 0.400 fm$^{-3}$, rod at 0.476 fm$^{-3}$, slab at 0.526 fm$^{-3}$, tube at 0.619 fm$^{-3}$, bubble at 0.653 fm$^{-3}$, pure quark at 0.689 fm$^{-3}$
- Onset density of pasta phases for $L$ = 118.5 MeV with $G_V$ = 0.2 fm$^2$, $B^{1/4}$ = 180 MeV, $\sigma$ = 40 MeV fm$^{-2}$: droplet at 0.346 fm$^{-3}$, rod at 0.434 fm$^{-3}$, slab at 0.500 fm$^{-3}$, tube at 0.608 fm$^{-3}$, bubble at 0.645 fm$^{-3}$, pure quark at 0.682 fm$^{-3}$
- The symmetry energy slope values studied range from $L$ = 30 MeV to $L$ = 118.5 MeV (the original NL3 model value)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars by Ju et al. (2021) and Wu et al. (2018) that used the energy minimization method for hadron-quark pasta phases, and is motivated by the suggestion from Annala et al. (2019) that quark cores are expected inside massive neutron stars. The study systematically varies the symmetry energy slope $L$ using the NL3L parametrization from Wu et al. (2021), and shows that the symmetry energy slope significantly affects the size and mass of the mixed phase core, with larger $L$ leading to earlier onset of pasta phases and larger mixed phase cores. The results enable future constraints on the symmetry energy slope from observations of hybrid star properties, particularly from NICER measurements of PSR J0740+6620 (mass 2.08 $\pm$ 0.07 $M_{\odot}$, radius 12.2 km $<$ $R$ $<$ 16.3 km).
