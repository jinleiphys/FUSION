# Spin dynamics with realistic hydrodynamic background for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The equations of perfect spin hydrodynamics are solved for the first time using a realistic (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic background, calibrated to reproduce a comprehensive set of hadronic observables, including rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow coefficients for Au+Au collisions at the beam energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV. The spin dynamics is governed by the conservation of the spin tensor, describing spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ particles, with particle mass in the spin tensor treated as an effective parameter. We investigate several scenarios, varying both the effective mass and the initial evolution time for the spin polarization tensor. The model predictions are then compared with experimental measurements of global and longitudinal spin polarization of Lambda hyperons. Our results indicate that a successful description of the data requires a delayed initial evolution time for the perfect spin hydrodynamics of about 4 fm/$c$ (in contrast to the standard initial time of 1 fm/$c$ used for the hydrodynamic background). This delay marks a transition from the phase where spin-orbit interaction is significant to the regime where spin-conserving processes dominate. Our findings suggest that the spin-orbit dissipative interaction plays a significant role only in the very early stages of the system's evolution.

## Method

The authors solve the equations of perfect spin hydrodynamics using a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic background calibrated to reproduce hadronic observables for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The spin dynamics is governed by conservation of the spin tensor for spin-1/2 particles in the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) pseudogauge, with the particle mass in the spin tensor treated as an effective parameter. The background hydrodynamics uses the DNMR framework with a lattice-QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-BQS), and the spin polarization of Lambda hyperons is computed on the switching hypersurface using the GLW polarization formula.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Standard initial time for hydrodynamic background: tau_0 = 1 fm/c
- Delayed initial evolution time for spin hydrodynamics: tau_0^s = 4 fm/c
- Switching energy density threshold: epsilon_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Effective mass values tested: m = m_Lambda (approximately 1116 MeV) and m = 300 MeV
- Shear viscosity coefficient: C_eta = 0.12
- Relaxation times: tau_pi = tau_Pi = 5*C_eta/T

## Context

This work extends the framework of perfect spin hydrodynamics developed by Florkowski et al. (2017-2018) from simple expansion geometries (such as Bjorken flow) to realistic (3+1)-dimensional simulations. It contrasts with approaches based on quantum kinetic theory that determine polarization from gradients of standard hydrodynamic variables at freeze-out, such as those by Becattini et al. (2021) and Liu et al. (2021). The results enable future studies of spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions by establishing that spin-conserving processes dominate the later stages of evolution, while spin-orbit coupling is significant only at very early times.
