# Effects of magnetic field on the evolution of energy density fluctuations

> We study the effects of a static and uniform magnetic field on the evolution of energy density fluctuations present in a medium. By numerically solving the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation within the relaxation time approximation, we explicitly show that magnetic field can affect the characteristics of energy density fluctuations at the timescale the system achieves local thermodynamic equilibrium. A detailed momentum mode analysis of fluctuations reveals that magnetic field increases the damping of mode oscillations, especially for the low momentum modes. This leads to a reduction in the ultraviolet (high momentum) cutoff of fluctuations and also slows down the dissipation of relatively low momentum fluctuation modes. We discuss the phenomenological implications of our study on various sources of fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Effects of magnetic field on the evolution of energy density fluctuations

## Key claim

The authors show that a static and uniform magnetic field, marginally weaker than the thermal energy of the medium (√|qB| < T), can affect the evolution of energy density fluctuations at the timescale the system achieves local thermodynamic equilibrium, specifically by increasing the damping of mode oscillations for low momentum modes and reducing the ultraviolet cutoff of fluctuations.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a system of charged pions (π±) with mass mπ = 140 MeV at global equilibrium temperature T0 = 50 MeV and charge chemical potential μQ = 0-100 MeV. They perform (3+1)-dimensional phase-space simulations on a lattice of dimension 1500×500×500 with spatial and momentum step sizes Δx' = Δk_x' = Δk_z' = 0.01, using the second-order Leapfrog method with periodic boundary conditions. The magnetic field parameter β0 = |qB|τc/m0 is varied between 0 and 0.7, corresponding to the condition β0 < τcT²/m0 for the marginally weak field limit.

## Key numbers

- Global equilibrium temperature T0 = 50 MeV for the pionic medium
- Magnetic field parameter β0 varied in range 0 to 0.7, constrained by √|qB| < T condition giving β0 ≲ 1 for τc ≳ 15 fm
- Charge chemical potential μQ varied between 0-100 MeV, with most results presented at μQ = 100 MeV
- Dimensionless energy density at equilibrium ε̂0 ≈ 2.4 for T0 = 50 MeV and μQ = 100 MeV
- Peak momentum of initial fluctuation spectrum at κpτc = 0.88 for fluctuation parameter r = 2.0
- Momentum range of fluctuations studied: κpτc ∈ [0.64, 2.0] obtained by varying fluctuation parameter r ∈ [0.5, 4.8]
- Wavelength cutoff at zero magnetic field: λc(β0=0) ≈ 2.5τc
- Maximum difference between modes with and without magnetic field occurs at momentum κτc ≃ 1.0
- Magnetic field effects become noticeable at times t/τc ≳ 1

## Context

This work extends kinetic transport studies of fluctuations (Rischke, 2004; Heinz, 2005) to include external magnetic fields, which are relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions where strong magnetic fields are generated in noncentral collisions (Larry, 2010; Tuchin, 2013). The study contrasts with ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamic (RMHD) simulations that assume local thermodynamic equilibrium (Pal, 2018; Tuchin, 2014), by addressing out-of-equilibrium evolution where RMHD is not applicable. The results enable improved model-to-data comparisons for fluctuation-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the Quark-Gluon Plasma formed at moderate collision energies where magnetic fields are stronger and critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point may arise (Stephanov, 2004).
