# Complete Fusion of Weakly Bound Cluster-Type Nuclei at Near Barrier Energies

> We consider the influence of breakup channels on the complete fusion of weakly bound cluster-type systems in terms of dynamic polarization potentials. It is argued that the enhancement of the cross section at sub-barrier energies may be consistent with recent experimental observations that nucleon transfer, often leading to breakup, is dominant compared to direct breakup. The main trends of the experimental complete fusion cross sections are analyzed in the framework of the Dynamic Polarization Potential approach. The qualitative conclusions are supported by CDCC calculations including a sequential breakup channel, the one neutron stripping of $^7$Li followed by the breakup of $^6$Li.

# Complete Fusion of Weakly Bound Cluster-Type Nuclei at Near Barrier Energies

## Key claim
The enhancement of complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies for weakly bound cluster-type projectiles may be consistent with the dominance of nucleon transfer (often leading to breakup) over direct breakup, as supported by Dynamic Polarization Potential (DPP) calculations and CDCC calculations that include a sequential breakup channel (one-neutron stripping of ⁷Li followed by breakup of ⁶Li).

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental complete fusion cross sections for ⁶Li and ⁷Li projectiles on a ²⁰⁹Bi target using the Dynamic Polarization Potential approach, comparing reduced data to the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) derived from a rescaled Wong formula. They performed CDCC calculations that included a sequential breakup channel, specifically, one-neutron stripping of ⁷Li followed by breakup of ⁶Li, to evaluate the real part of the dynamic polarization potential and its effect on the fusion barrier.

## Key numbers
- Complete fusion cross sections for ⁶Li + ²⁰⁹Bi and ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi are suppressed by about 30% at energies above the Coulomb barrier.
- At sub-barrier energies, complete fusion cross sections for ⁶Li + ²⁰⁹Bi are enhanced by nearly one order of magnitude.
- The DPP real part was calculated for ⁷Li + ¹⁴⁴Sm at laboratory energies of 30 MeV (above barrier), 25 MeV (near barrier), and 20 MeV (below barrier) for the sequential breakup channel.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of fusion suppression and enhancement for weakly bound cluster-type nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be) previously studied by Canto (2006), Liang, and Keeley, and builds on the Universal Fusion Function formalism developed by Gomes (2005) and Canto (2009). The paper specifically addresses the experimental data from Dasgupta (2002) for ⁶,⁷Li on ²⁰⁹Bi, and the recent observation by Luong (the Canberra group) that transfer followed by breakup is a significant process. The findings enable improved modeling of complete fusion for weakly bound projectiles by incorporating sequential transfer-breakup channels into coupled-channels calculations.
