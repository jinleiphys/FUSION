# Toward Description of $pp$ and $p{\rm C}$ Interactions at High Energies: Problems of Fritiof-based Models

> How do the models (Fritiof 1.6, Fritiof 7.0, UrQMD 3.3 and Hijing 1.383) describe experimental data of NA61/SHINE and NA49 Collaborations on $pp$ and $p{\rm C}$ interactions at high energies? An answer on this question is given in the paper. It is shown that the UrQMD model does not reproduce the energy dependence of $π^-$ meson production in $pp$-interactions; the Fritiof 1.6 and the Fritiof 7.0 models underestimate the meson production on $\sim$ 10 %; the Hijing model overestimates the data on $\sim$ 10 %. A change of a LUND fragmentation function parameter in the Fritiof models 1.6/7.0 allows to describe the data. A decreasing of probabilities of binary processes in the UrQMD model like $p+p\rightarrow N+N'$ and so on, allows one to describe the data, thus a problem of a correct accounting of the processes arises. An increasing of a probability of the single diffraction dissociation in the Hijing model from 35 % to 50 % allows to describe the data, though a spectrum of masses produced in the diffraction is not satisfactory. A description of the diffraction is a problem in all the models.

## Key claim

The Fritiof-based models (Fritiof 1.6, Fritiof 7.0, UrQMD 3.3, and Hijing 1.383) can be tuned to describe NA61/SHINE and NA49 data on pion production in pp and pC interactions, but all models fail to correctly simulate low-mass diffraction dissociation, and the proton spectra remain problematic.

## Method

The author compared rapidity distributions of pi- mesons from the four models against NA61/SHINE data at beam momenta of 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c, and against NA49 data at 158 GeV/c for pp and pC interactions. Tuning was performed by adjusting the LUND fragmentation function parameter "a" in Fritiof 1.6/7.0, reducing binary reaction probabilities in UrQMD, and increasing the single diffraction dissociation probability in Hijing from 35% to 50%. The models' predictions for diffraction dissociation were further tested against experimental mass distributions of n pi+ and p pi+ pairs and t-dependence of cross sections.

## Key numbers

* Fritiof 1.6 and Fritiof 7.0 underestimate pi- meson production in pp interactions by approximately 10%
* Hijing 1.383 overestimates pi- meson production in pp interactions by approximately 10%
* The single diffraction dissociation probability in Hijing was increased from 35% to 50% to improve data description
* The string mass threshold Md in Fritiof 1.6 and 7.0 is 1.2 GeV for nucleons; in UrQMD 3.3 it is mN + 0.52 GeV; in Hijing 1.383 it is mqq + 1.5 GeV
* The average transverse momentum parameter in Fritiof 1.6 is 0.282 GeV/c; in Fritiof 7.0 it is 0.1 GeV/c; in UrQMD 3.3 it is 1.6 GeV/c
* The LUND fragmentation function parameter "a" was changed from 0.5 to 1.25 in Fritiof 1.6 to improve data description
* The average number of intra-nuclear collisions in pC interactions is approximately 1.4
* The UrQMD model reproduces diffraction dissociation cross sections reasonably at sqrt(s) ~ 15-25 GeV but underestimates data above that range

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to describe NA61/SHINE and NA49 data using FLUKA, VENUS, UrQMD (as cited in NA61pC), and the FTF model of Geant4 (Uzhinsky, 2009; Uzhinsky, 2010). The paper identifies that all Fritiof-based models (which underpin Hijing and UrQMD) share a common deficiency in simulating low-mass diffraction dissociation, particularly the failure to reproduce the kinematic peak at Mx ~ 1400 MeV seen in experimental data. The results enable future improvements to event generators used at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA facilities, and suggest that implementing binary reactions (such as p+p -> N+N*) in all Fritiof-based models and accounting for Gribov inelastic screening in hadron-nucleus interactions may resolve remaining discrepancies.
