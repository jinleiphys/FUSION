# Quantum Group SU_{q}(2) and Pairing in Nuclei

> A scheme for treating the pairing of nucleons in terms of generators of Quantum Group SU_{q}(2) is presented. The possible applications to nucleon pairs in a single orbit, multishell case, pairing vibrations and superconducting nuclei are discussed. The formalism for performing BCS calculations with q-deformed nucleon pairs is constructed and the role played by deformation parameter q analyzed in the context of nucleons in a single orbit and for Sn Isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nucleon pairing in nuclei can be described using the quantum group SU_q(2) rather than the conventional SU(2) quasi-spin algebra, with the deformation parameter q serving as a quantitative measure of correlations that are omitted in standard BCS and RPA treatments; the q-deformed formalism reduces to the usual theories only in the limit q → 1.

## Method

The authors construct q-deformed nucleon pair creation and destruction operators that satisfy SU_q(2) commutation relations, then formulate a q-deformed seniority scheme, random phase approximation (RPA) for pairing vibrations, and a q-deformed BCS theory (qBCS). They apply the single-orbit qBCS to the sdg major shell with Ω = 16 and valence nucleon numbers 4, 10, 14, 20, 24, and 30, and also to the heavy Sn isotopes 114–124Sn using a model space of five single-particle orbits (1d5/2, 0g7/2, 2s1/2, 1d3/2, 0h11/2) with excitation energies 0.0, 0.22, 1.90, 2.20, and 2.80 MeV respectively.

## Key numbers

- For 20 nucleons in two shells, q-deformed boson approximation results for τ = 0.104 and q-deformed quasiboson approximation energies for τ = 0.15 overlap exact shell model results in a wide region away from the phase transition region.
- For the nucleus 208Pb, with τ = 0.405 the experimental excitation energy of the double pairing vibration state and the transfer cross section for two neutron transfer are well reproduced.
- For 120Sn, the critical pairing strength G_c is approximately 0.065 MeV for conventional BCS (q = 1.0), while for q = 1.3 the critical strength drops to G_c approximately 0.04 MeV.
- The model space for Sn isotopes includes single-particle orbits with excitation energies 0.0, 0.22, 1.90, 2.20, and 2.80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-spin SU(2) formalism for nuclear pairing originally developed by Racah and Talmi (1952) and the BCS theory applied to nuclei by Belyaev (1959), by introducing a q-deformed version of the pair algebra. It contrasts with the approach of Bonatsos et al. (1992, 1994, 1999), who treated nucleon pairing in a single j-shell using two Q-oscillators with a different deformation scheme. The formalism enables future applications to other systems where Cooper pairing plays a role, such as metal grains, and opens the possibility of obtaining exact correlation energies by choosing appropriate combinations of G and q values.
