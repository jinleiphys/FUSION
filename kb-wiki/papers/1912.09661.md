# $Σ_cN$ interaction in chiral perturbation theory

> We adopt the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to calculate the $Σ_cN$ interaction to the next-to-leading order. We consider the contact interactions, one-pion-exchange contributions, two-pion-exchange diagrams, and renormalization effects of the vertices, masses and wave functions. With the pion mass dependent expression, we fit the $Σ_cN$ interaction from HAL QCD calculation with $m_π\approx 410$ MeV and $m_π\approx 570$ MeV, and then extrapolate it to the physical pion mass. The $^3S_1(I=1/2)$ $Σ_{c}N$ interaction is weakly attractive but no bound solution is found. We also propose a quark model to estimate the leading order $Σ_cN$ contact interaction with the $NN$ interaction as input. This approach combining the quark model and the chiral effective field theory predicts a very attractive interaction in $^1S_0(I=3/2)$ $Σ_{c}N$ channel and a two-body bound state.

## Key claim

The paper uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to calculate the ΣcN interaction to next-to-leading order, fitting lattice QCD results at unphysical pion masses and extrapolating to the physical pion mass. The key qualifier is that the ³S₁(I=1/2) ΣcN interaction is found to be weakly attractive but with no bound state solution, while a combined quark model and chiral effective field theory approach predicts a bound state in the ¹S₀(I=3/2) channel.

## Method

The authors employed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute the ΣcN effective potential to next-to-leading order, including contact interactions, one-pion-exchange contributions, two-pion-exchange diagrams with Σc and Σc* as intermediate states, and renormalization effects of vertices, masses, and wave functions. They fit the pion-mass-dependent analytical expressions to the HAL QCD lattice results for the ³S₁(I=1/2) channel at mπ ≈ 410 MeV and mπ ≈ 570 MeV, then extrapolated to the physical pion mass. Additionally, they developed a quark model using SU(3) flavor symmetry to relate the leading-order ΣcN contact interaction to the NN interaction, allowing predictions for other channels.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length for ³S₁(I=1/2) ΣcN: a_s = -0.53⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₁₁ fm (scenario I with n=1, Λ=0.8 GeV)
- Scattering length for ³S₁(I=1/2) ΣcN: a_s = -1.83⁺⁰·³²₋₀·₄₂ fm (scenario II with n=2, Λ=0.5 GeV)
- Lattice QCD pion masses used for fitting: mπ ≈ 410 MeV and mπ ≈ 570 MeV
- Physical pion mass: mπ = 139 MeV (used in hadron mass table)
- Mass splitting δ₁ = MΣc* - MΣc at physical pion mass: 64 MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λχ = 4πF(mπ)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework previously applied to NN interactions (Weinberg 1990, 1991; Epelbaum 2008; Machleidt 2011) to the charmed baryon-nucleon system, specifically ΣcN. It builds upon lattice QCD calculations by the HAL QCD collaboration (Miyamoto 2017) and earlier phenomenological approaches including the one-boson-exchange model (Liu 2011), the quark delocalization color screening model (Huang 2013), and the constituent quark model (Garcilazo 2019). The results enable future investigations of charmed hypernuclei and can be extended to study Σc(*)D(*) interactions relevant to the Pc pentaquark states.
