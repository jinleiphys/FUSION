# Double neutron/proton ratio of nucleon emissions in isotopic reaction systems as a robust probe of nuclear symmetry energy

> The double neutron/proton ratio of nucleon emissions taken from two reaction systems using four isotopes of the same element, namely, the neutron/proton ratio in the neutron-rich system over that in the more symmetric system, has the advantage of reducing systematically the influence of the Coulomb force and the normally poor efficiencies of detecting low energy neutrons. The double ratio thus suffers less systematic errors. Within the IBUU04 transport model the double neutron/proton ratio is shown to have about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy as the single neutron/proton ratio in the neutron-rich system involved. The double neutron/proton ratio is therefore more useful for further constraining the symmetry energy of neutron-rich matter.

# Double neutron/proton ratio of nucleon emissions in isotopic reaction systems as a robust probe of nuclear symmetry energy

## Key claim

The double neutron/proton ratio (the n/p ratio in a neutron-rich system divided by that in a more symmetric system) has about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy as the single n/p ratio in the neutron-rich system alone, but with reduced systematic errors from Coulomb forces and low-energy neutron detection inefficiencies. This conclusion is drawn from IBUU04 transport model simulations using two specific parameterizations of the symmetry energy (x=0 and x=-1) that already lie within the range constrained by prior isospin diffusion data.

## Method

The authors used the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model with a single-nucleon potential derived from a modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI), using in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections calculated via an effective-mass scaling approach. They simulated central and mid-central collisions of Sn isotopes: 124Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon, and 132Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon, extracting the double neutron/proton ratio of free nucleons (identified as those with local baryon density below rho0/8) for two values of the parameter x that controls the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy parameterizations used: E_sym(rho) ≈ 31.6 (rho/rho0)^1.1 for x=-1 and E_sym(rho) ≈ 31.6 (rho/rho0)^0.69 for x=0, both with normal density rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and symmetry energy ≈ 30 MeV at rho0.
- At 50 MeV/nucleon, effects of the symmetry energy on the double n/p ratio are about 10% to 15% when changing from x=-1 to x=0 for energetic nucleons, at impact parameters of 2 fm and 6 fm.
- At 400 MeV/nucleon, effects of the symmetry energy on the double n/p ratio are about 5% to 10% when changing from x=0 to x=-1, at impact parameters of 1 fm and 5 fm.
- Maximum density reached is about 1.2 rho0 at 50 MeV/nucleon and about 2 rho0 at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The double n/p ratio at 50 MeV/nucleon is insensitive to symmetry energy for kinetic energies below about 50 MeV, suggesting a detector threshold of 50 MeV is sufficient.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Lynch et al. (2005) to use the double neutron/proton ratio as an observable, providing transport model validation for the idea. It builds on prior studies using the single n/p ratio (Li 1997) and isospin diffusion data from the MSU group (Betty 2004, Chen 2005) that constrained the symmetry energy to between the two forms used here. The double ratio is shown to be more useful than the single ratio for further constraining the symmetry energy because it cancels Coulomb effects and reduces systematic errors from neutron detection; systematic studies over a broad beam energy range could map out the density dependence of the symmetry energy from sub-saturation to supra-saturation densities.
