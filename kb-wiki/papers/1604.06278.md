# Second--order equation of state with the Skyrme interaction. Cutoff and dimensional regularization with the inclusion of rearrangement terms

> We evaluate the second--order (beyond--mean--field) contribution to the equation of state of nuclear matter with the effective Skyrme force and use cutoff and dimensional regularizations to treat the ultraviolet divergence produced by the zero--range character of this interaction. An adjustment of the force parameters is then performed in both cases to remove any double counting generated by the explicit computation of beyond--mean--field corrections with the Skyrme force. In addition, we include at second order the rearrangement terms associated to the density--dependent part of the Skyrme force and discuss their effect. Sets of parameters are proposed to define new effective forces which are specially designed for second--order calculations in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper provides corrected full formulae and results for second-order (beyond-mean-field) contributions to the nuclear matter equation of state using the Skyrme interaction, where previous work by the same group (Moghrabi et al., 2012) contained incorrect formal derivations. The authors show that including rearrangement terms associated with the density-dependent part of the Skyrme force at second order is necessary, and that these terms were neglected in their prior studies.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the second-order contribution to the equation of state for symmetric and pure neutron matter using the Skyrme interaction (omitting spin-orbit and tensor terms), treating the ultraviolet divergence from the zero-range interaction with both cutoff regularization (using a momentum cutoff Lambda on the transferred momentum) and dimensional regularization. The second-order integrals were solved analytically for symmetric and neutron matter, while for asymmetric matter (asymmetry parameter delta=0.5) they were computed numerically using Monte Carlo integration. Parameter adjustments were performed by fitting to the SLy5 mean-field equation of state benchmark, using 9 points up to 0.3 fm^-3, with chi-squared values calculated using 1% of the reference value as the uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The second-order contribution for symmetric matter (l=0) contains terms proportional to lambda^5, lambda^3, and lambda in the asymptotic expansion, with the strongest divergence provided by the t1 term
- For symmetric matter, the l=0 asymptotic term includes a coefficient of -9mk_F^4/(2hbar^2 pi^4) multiplied by terms such as (k_F^4 T_1^2/360)lambda^5
- For neutron matter, the l=0 asymptotic term has a coefficient of -3mk_F_N^4/(hbar^2 pi^4) multiplied by similar lambda^5, lambda^3, and lambda terms
- The l=1 contribution for symmetric matter has asymptotic terms proportional to lambda^3 and lambda, with coefficient -9mk_F^8/(2hbar^2 pi^4) times (1/720)lambda^3 + (1/560)lambda
- The parameter adjustment used 9 points from the SLy5 mean-field equation of state, with 7 points up to 0.16 fm^-3 and 2 points between 0.16 and 0.3 fm^-3

## Context

This work corrects and extends previous studies by Moghrabi et al. (2012) on second-order calculations with the Skyrme interaction, which had incorrect formal derivations and neglected rearrangement terms. The authors compare their results to those of Kaiser (2015), noting that unlike that work they include the density-dependent term (necessary for reproducing saturation density), treat asymmetric matter, and perform cutoff regularization. The paper provides parameter sets designed for second-order calculations that can serve as a starting point for constructing a bridge between infinite matter and finite nuclei, where the authors plan to eventually employ these effective regularized interactions.
