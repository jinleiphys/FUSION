# Chiral Ward identities and pion propagation at finite temperature in the linear sigma model

> Working within the linear sigma model at finite temperature, we construct effective one-loop vertices and propagators satisfying the chiral Ward identities, for small pion momentum and mass compared to the sigma mass. We obtain an expansion for the pion dispersion relation up to second order in the parameter m_pi^2/4pi^2f_pi^2. At leading order, this expansion reproduces the dispersion curve obtained in chiral perturbation theory. We also study pion damping and compute the mean free path for pions traveling in the medium before forming a sigma resonance. These results are also in good agreement with chiral perturbation theory when using a value for the sigma mass of 600 MeV.

# Chiral Ward identities and pion propagation at finite temperature in the linear sigma model

## Key claim

Within the linear sigma model at finite temperature, the leading-order modification to the pion mass in a thermal pion medium reproduces the result from chiral perturbation theory, but the next-to-leading order correction has the same sign as the leading-order shift, contrary to previous chiral perturbation theory calculations at second order which found an opposite sign and three times larger magnitude.

## Method

The authors constructed one-loop effective sigma propagator and one-sigma two-pion and four-pion vertices that satisfy the chiral Ward identities, keeping only zeroth-order terms in an expansion in the parameter m_pi^2/m_sigma^2. They used these effective vertices and propagators to compute the two-loop order correction to the pion self-energy in a pion medium, working in the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory and treating momentum and temperature as small quantities of order m_pi.

## Key numbers

- The effective expansion parameter is xi = m_pi^2 / (4 pi^2 f_pi^2), which is much less than 1.
- The sigma mass used for numerical comparison with chiral perturbation theory is 600 MeV.
- The mean free path for a pion traveling in the medium before forming a sigma resonance reaches a maximum at pion momentum p ~ 0.4 m_pi, approximately independent of temperature.
- The pion elastic scattering rate is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the resonance formation rate, being proportional to xi^2.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model treatment of pions at finite temperature to next-to-leading order, showing agreement with the leading-order chiral perturbation theory result of Gasser (1987) but disagreement with the next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory results of Schenk (1993) and Toublan (1997), which found an opposite sign for the second-order mass shift. The results enable further study of pion propagation in hot hadronic matter relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and support the use of the linear sigma model as more than a toy model given evidence for a sigma meson near 600 MeV from Tornqvist (1996) and Svec (1999).
