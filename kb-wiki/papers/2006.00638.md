# The isoscalar features of Pygmy Dipole Resonance: a subtle game of symmetry energy

> The vibrational structure of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) is investigated within a quantum many-body treatment with extended separable interactions able to encode the dependence of nuclear symmetry energy on density. A new picture of PDR is unveiled in terms of a combined dynamics of the neutron skin and of the core isovector polarization, which determines the isoscalar features of PDR while reproducing the isovector properties of Giant Dipole Resonance. The key role played by the variation with density of the symmetry energy on shaping the low-lying dipole response and its isoscalar-isovector structure is underlined. Our results provide insights for the challenge of clarifying the transition from skin oscillation to a highly bulk collective dynamics.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the isoscalar character of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) arises from the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which causes a partial decoupling of excess neutrons from the core, rather than from a simple oscillation of the neutron skin against an inert core.

## Method
The authors employed a quantum many-body model based on the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) with extended separable interactions that encode the density dependence of the symmetry energy. They generalized the Brown-Bolsterli model by introducing three different coupling constants (λ₁, λ₂, λ₃) for particle-hole interactions at saturation density, intermediate density, and lower density regions, respectively. Analytical solutions were obtained in the degenerate case (εᵢ = ℏω₀ = 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV) and compared with numerical results from Vlasov transport simulations for ¹⁴⁸Sn.

## Key numbers
- The unperturbed single-particle energy used in the degenerate case: ε = ℏω₀ = 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV
- For ¹⁴⁸Sn with Nₑ = 48 excess neutrons, the coupling constants were set to λ₂/λ₁ = 0.6 and λ₃/λ₁ = 0.3, with b_sym^(pot)(ρ₀) = 16 MeV
- The PDR energy centroid from the schematic model: E_c^(PDR) = 7.6 MeV
- The PDR exhausted EWSR fraction: f_PDR = 4%
- The neutron and proton radii used for ¹⁴⁸Sn: R_n = 5.22 fm, R_p = 4.80 fm
- Gaussian folding widths applied to the schematic model strength function: 0.44 MeV for PDR and 1.23 MeV for GDR

## Context
This work extends the Brown-Bolsterli model (Brown, 1959, 1961) by incorporating density-dependent separable interactions to describe the PDR. It contradicts the classical scenario interpreting PDR as a simple oscillation of the neutron skin against an inert core (Lanza, 2015), instead revealing a combined dynamics of neutron skin oscillation and core isovector polarization. The results provide a framework for interpreting experimental data from facilities such as HIγS (Weller, 2009) and ELI (Camera, 2016; Gales, 2018), and can be extended to other collective modes where pygmy-like structures manifest, such as in metallic clusters (Palade, 2016).
