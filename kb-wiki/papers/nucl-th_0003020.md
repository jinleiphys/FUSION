# Color Dynamics On Phase Space

> We describe the properties of quark matter at zero temperature and finite baryon densities within microscopic Vlasov/molecular dynamics approaches. We use an inter-quark Richardson's potential consistent with the indications of Lattice QCD calculations. The color degrees of freedom are explicitly taken into account. We explicitly demonstrate that the Vlasov approach alone is insufficient in the hadronization region. In order to overcome this problem we prepare the initial condition for many events using molecular dynamics with frictional cooling and a Thomas-Fermi approximation to the Fermi motion. These events are averaged and propagated in time using the Vlasov approach. We find some evidence for a second order phase transition from nuclear to quark matter at high baryon densities. An order parameter suitable to describe the phase transition is discussed. At low densities the quark condensate into approximately color white clusters (nucleon).

## Key claim

The paper claims evidence for a second-order phase transition from nuclear to quark matter at high baryon densities, but the evidence is qualified as "some evidence" and the transition is predicted rather than definitively established. The Vlasov approach alone is explicitly shown to be insufficient in the hadronization region, requiring molecular dynamics with frictional cooling and Thomas-Fermi approximation to prepare initial conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov equation for quark matter at zero temperature using an inter-quark Richardson's potential with explicit color degrees of freedom (using the diagonal Gell-Mann matrices λ₃ and λ₈ in the Abelian approximation). Initial conditions are prepared by molecular dynamics with frictional cooling, where the Fermi motion is treated via a Thomas-Fermi approximation using local density and local color number to define the Fermi momentum locally. These events are averaged and then propagated in time using the Vlasov approach, with test particle numbers ranging from 5000 to 50000 for systems of 150-300 quarks.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass mᵢ = 10 MeV for u and d quarks
- Critical density ρ_c = 2.08 fm⁻³ where a minimum in energy per nucleon is found at E_t/A = 2 GeV/A
- Nuclear matter ground state density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ with E_t/A = 0.938 - 0.016 GeV/A
- Richardson potential parameter Λ = 0.25 GeV
- Number of flavors n_f = 2
- Order parameter M_c follows a power law |1 - ρ_B/ρ_c|^β with critical exponent β = 1/3 or 1/2 (mean field value)
- Maximum calculated M_c value is approximately 0.9
- At high densities, M_c saturates at about 0.3-0.4

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Vlasov approach (Bonasera 1999) by explicitly including color degrees of freedom rather than using an effective Debye screening radius. The results are qualitatively compared to Hartree-Fock calculations by Horowitz, Moniz and Negele (1985) and to stochastic ground state calculations by Roepke, Blaschke and Schulz (1986). The approach is intended to enable dynamical simulations of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions and the study of quark-gluon plasma formation after the introduction of a suitable collision term.
