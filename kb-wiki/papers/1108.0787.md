# A study of Lambda hypernuclei within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Model

> We investigate the properties of the single Lambda hypernuclei within a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model. The parameters of the Skyrme type effective lambda-nucleon (Lambda N) interaction are obtained by fitting to the experimental Lambda binding energies of hypernuclei with masses spanning a wide range of the periodic table. Alternative parameter sets are also obtained by omitting nuclei below mass number 16 from the fitting procedure. The SHF calculations are performed for the binding energies of the Lambda single-particle states over a full mass range using the best fit parameter sets obtained in these fitting procedures and the results are compared with the available experimental data. The data show some sensitivity to the parameter sets obtained with or without including the nuclei below mass 16. The radii of the Lambda orbits in the hypernuclear ground states and the Lambda effective mass in nuclear matter show some dependence on different parameter sets. We present results for the total binding energy per baryon of the hypernuclei over a large mass region to elucidate their stability as a function of the baryon number. We have also employed the our best fit Lambda N parameter sets to investigate the role of hyperons in some key properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with a density-dependent Lambda-nucleon interaction, fitted to a broad set of experimental binding energies, can reproduce hypernuclear single-particle spectra across the periodic table, but the resulting parameter sets show sensitivity to whether hypernuclei with mass number below 16 are included in the fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors extended the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model to single-Lambda hypernuclei by adding a Lambda-nucleon energy density functional with a density-dependent term (either with gamma = 1 or gamma = 1/3). They determined the six parameters of the Lambda-nucleon Skyrme interaction by chi-squared minimization using the simulated annealing method, fitting to experimental Lambda binding energies of hypernuclei with baryon numbers from 8 to 208, and also performed alternative fits excluding nuclei below mass 16.

## Key numbers

- The parameter set HPΛ2 (gamma = 1) achieved the lowest chi-squared value of 3.06 among fits including all nuclei, with parameters u0 = -399.946 MeV fm^3, u1 = 83.426 MeV fm^5, u2 = 11.455 MeV fm^5, u3' = 2046.818 MeV fm^(3+3γ), y0 = -0.486, y3 = -0.660
- The alternative fit OΛ1 (gamma = 1, excluding 16N data) gave the lowest overall chi-squared of 1.82, with parameters u0 = -236.5835 MeV fm^3, u1 = 116.8704 MeV fm^5, u2 = -112.8812 MeV fm^5, u3' = 1453.3493 MeV fm^(3+3γ), y0 = 0.1271, y3 = -0.3110
- The depth of the Lambda-nucleon mean field at nuclear matter density (0.16 ± 0.01 fm^-3) was about 28.00 ± 0.58 MeV across parameter sets
- Experimental binding energies used in fitting ranged from 7.16 ± 0.70 MeV for 8ΛHe (1s) to 26.30 ± 0.80 MeV for 208ΛPb (1s)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach to hypernuclei previously developed by Rayet (1976, 1981) and Fernandez (1989), but uses a much larger experimental dataset spanning masses from 8 to 208. The density-dependent Lambda-nucleon interaction follows suggestions by Lanskoy (1997) and Moroz (2005). The results enable more reliable predictions for hypernuclear structure across the periodic table and provide input for neutron star studies, complementing relativistic mean field approaches (Rufa 1990, Glendenning 1993) and in-medium chiral dynamics (Finelli 2009).
