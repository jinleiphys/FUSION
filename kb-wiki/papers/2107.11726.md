# Analytic solutions of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics with Bjorken expansion

> We have studied analytically the longitudinally boost-invariant motion of a relativistic dissipative fluid with spin. We have derived the analytic solutions of spin density and spin chemical potential as a function of proper time $τ$ in the presence of viscous tensor and the second order relaxation time corrections for spin. Interestingly, analogous to the ordinary particle number density and chemical potential, we find that the spin density and spin chemical potential decay as $\simτ^{-1}$ and $\simτ^{-1/3}$, respectively. It implies that the initial spin density may not survive at the freezeout hyper-surface. These solutions can serve both to gain insight on the dynamics of spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and as testbeds for further numerical codes.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic solutions for spin density and spin chemical potential in a longitudinally boost-invariant (Bjorken) relativistic dissipative fluid with spin, finding that these quantities decay as \(\sim\tau^{-1}\) and \(\sim\tau^{-1/3}\) respectively, but only under the assumption that the spin chemical potential is at first order in the gradient expansion (\(\omega^{xy}/T \ll 1\)) and that the system is homogeneous in the transverse plane.

## Method

The authors impose the Bjorken flow velocity profile on the canonical form of relativistic spin hydrodynamics, assuming boost invariance and transverse homogeneity. They solve the coupled conservation equations for energy-momentum and total angular momentum, using a high-temperature equation of state \(e=3p\) and a linear relation \(S^{xy}=a_1 T^2 \omega^{xy}\) between spin density and spin chemical potential. The dissipative terms include shear and bulk viscosities, a first-order spin transport term \(\phi^{\mu\nu}\), and a second-order relaxation time correction for spin, and the solutions are obtained via a power expansion in the small parameter \(\omega^{xy}/T\).

## Key numbers

* Spin density \(S^{xy}(\tau)\) decays as \(\sim\tau^{-1}\) at leading order (Eq. 3.22 in the text).
* Spin chemical potential \(\omega^{xy}(\tau)\) decays as \(\sim\tau^{-1/3}\) at leading order (Eq. 3.19 in the text).
* Temperature decays as \(T(\tau) = T_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{1/3}\) at leading order, with viscous corrections of order \(\frac{1}{2\tau}\left(\frac{2}{3}\frac{\eta_s}{s}+\frac{\zeta}{s}\right)\left[1-(\tau/\tau_0)^{2/3}\right]\).
* Energy density decays as \(e(\tau) = e_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{4/3}\) at leading order.
* In the \(\tau_\phi \rightarrow \infty\) limit (no relaxation), \(\omega^{xy}(\tau) = \omega_0^{xy} (\tau_0/\tau)^{1/3}\) and \(S^{xy}(\tau) = a_1 T_0^2 \omega_0^{xy} (\tau_0/\tau)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic spin hydrodynamics, which has been developed from the entropy principle (Hattori 2019, Fukushima 2020), kinetic approaches (Florkowski 2018, Becattini 2019), and Lagrangian effective theory (Montenegro 2017), by providing the first analytic solutions for dissipative spin dynamics in a Bjorken-expanding background. It contrasts with numerical studies of spin hydrodynamics in Bjorken flow (Florkowski 2019, Singh 2020) and Gubser flow (Singh 2020) by focusing on self-consistent analytic solutions including viscous and relaxation-time corrections. The results enable the use of these solutions as testbeds for future numerical codes for dissipative spin hydrodynamics and suggest that initial spin density may not survive to the freezeout hypersurface, potentially explaining why spin polarization calculations can neglect spin density contributions.
