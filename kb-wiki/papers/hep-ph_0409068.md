# Chiral Meson Physics at Two Loops

> An overview of Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations in the mesonic sector at the two Loop level is given. Discussed in some detail are the partially quenched case relevant for lattice QCD, the general fitting procedures and $ππ$,$πK$ scattering as well as the determination of $V_{us}$ and $K_{\ell3}$ decays.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) at two loops provides a systematic framework for meson physics, but the convergence is sometimes slow and the large number of free parameters at order p^6 (90+4 for three flavors) requires careful fitting procedures and external input from resonance saturation to obtain predictive results.

## Method

The author reviews the status of two-loop ChPT calculations in the mesonic sector, covering the Lagrangians at orders p^2, p^4, and p^6 with their associated low-energy constants. The fitting procedure uses experimental inputs including K_{l4} decay data from the E865 BNL experiment, meson masses, decay constants F_pi and F_K/F_pi, and the quark mass ratio 2m_s/(m_u+m_d), with the p^6 constants C_i^r estimated using single resonance approximation and varied by an overall factor of two.

## Key numbers

- F_pi+ = 92.4 MeV, F_K+/F_pi+ = 1.22 +/- 0.01 (experimental inputs for fits)
- 2 m_s/(m_u+m_d) = 24 or 26 (input quark mass ratio)
- m_u/m_d = 0.45 +/- 0.05 (from fit 10, the main p^6 fit)
- F_0 = 87.7 MeV (from fit 10, the chiral limit decay constant)
- 10^3 L_1^r = 0.43 +/- 0.12, 10^3 L_2^r = 0.73 +/- 0.12, 10^3 L_3^r = -2.53 +/- 0.37, 10^3 L_5^r = 0.97 +/- 0.11, 10^3 L_7^r = -0.31 +/- 0.14, 10^3 L_8^r = 0.60 +/- 0.18 (from fit 10)
- The value of |f_+(0)V_us| changes by 0.9% (CPLEAR data) or 0.6% (PSE246 data) when a linear fit is used compared with a quadratic one where curvature is determined from ChPT

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Weinberg, Gasser, and Leutwyler on Chiral Perturbation Theory, summarizing the progress since the p^4 calculations of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984-1985) and the p^6 classification by Bijnens, Colangelo, and Ecker (1999-2000). The two-loop calculations enable more precise determinations of fundamental parameters like V_us from K_{l3} decays, resolving the apparent unitarity violation in the CKM matrix, and provide the theoretical framework for partially quenched ChPT needed to connect lattice QCD results to continuum physics.
