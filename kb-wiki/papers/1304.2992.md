# Fermionization of two-component few-fermion systems in a one-dimensional harmonic trap

> The nature of strongly interacting Fermi gases and magnetism is one of the most important and studied topics in condensed-matter physics. Still, there are many open questions. A central issue is under what circumstances strong short-range repulsive interactions are enough to drive magnetic correlations. Recent progress in the field of cold atomic gases allows to address this question in very clean systems where both particle numbers, interactions and dimensionality can be tuned. Here we study fermionic few-body systems in a one dimensional harmonic trap using a new rapidly converging effective-interaction technique, plus a novel analytical approach. This allows us to calculate the properties of a single spin-down atom interacting with a number of spin-up particles, a case of much recent experimental interest. Our findings indicate that, in the strongly interacting limit, spin-up and spin-down particles want to separate in the trap, which we interpret as a microscopic precursor of one-dimensional ferromagnetism in imbalanced systems. Our predictions are directly addressable in current experiments on ultracold atomic few-body systems.

## Key claim
In the strongly interacting limit (|g|→∞), spin-up and spin-down particles in a one-dimensional harmonic trap tend to separate spatially, which the authors interpret as a microscopic precursor of one-dimensional ferromagnetism in imbalanced systems, but this behavior is specific to the few-body regime and is not due to energetics but to different correlations in the wave functions.

## Method
The authors solved the few-body Schrödinger equation for harmonically trapped fermions with zero-range interactions between different spin components using a rapidly converging effective-interaction technique that takes the exact two-body Busch solution as input. For the N↑=2, N↓=1 case, they also developed an analytical model using Jacobi coordinates and angular matching conditions that becomes exact at infinite interaction strength. The numerical method uses a harmonic-oscillator basis with a Lawson projection term to remove center-of-mass excitations, and the effective interaction is constructed via a unitary transformation of the exact two-body solutions.

## Key numbers
- For the 2+1 system (N↑=2, N↓=1), the lowest non-interacting totally antisymmetric state lies at E = 4 ℏω (in harmonic-oscillator units where ℏω is the trap energy)
- With the effective interaction at 1/g = 0.01, the ground state energy converges to within 0.01% of the fully converged value already at model space truncation n_tot = 14
- With the bare interaction at 1/g = 1, the relative error in ground state energy is ~1% for n_tot = 64; at 1/g = 0.01, the relative error is still ~4% for the same model space
- The analytical model yields energies E = 2ν + μ + 1 (in ℏω units) for the non-interacting case, where μ and ν are non-negative integers

## Context
This work extends the classic Busch model (Busch 1998) for two fermions in a harmonic trap to the imbalanced few-body case with N↑ > N↓ = 1, and builds on the McGuire solution (McGuire 1965, 1966) for untrapped systems with periodic boundary conditions. The results agree with Gharashi and Blume (2013) and Volosniev et al. (2013) but disagree with symmetry-based predictions from Guan et al. (2009). The findings enable direct experimental tests using current ultracold atom setups (Serwane et al. 2011, Zürn et al. 2012) through tunneling measurements that map out few-body wave function occupancies, and provide a microscopic few-body perspective on the ongoing search for Stoner ferromagnetism in one-dimensional imbalanced Fermi systems.
