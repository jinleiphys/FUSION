# Dynamical symmetries of the Klein-Gordon equation

> The dynamical symmetries of the two-dimensional Klein-Gordon equations with equal scalar and vector potentials (ESVP) are studied. The dynamical symmetries are considered in the plane and the sphere respectively. The generators of the SO(3) group corresponding to the Coulomb potential, and the SU(2) group corresponding to the harmonic oscillator potential are derived. Moreover, the generators in the sphere construct the Higgs algebra. With the help of the Casimir operators, the energy levels of the Klein-Gordon systems are yielded naturally.

## Key claim

The Klein-Gordon equation with equal scalar and vector potentials (ESVP) in two dimensions possesses the same dynamical symmetries as the corresponding non-relativistic Schrödinger equation, specifically SO(3) for the Coulomb potential and SU(2) for the harmonic oscillator potential, but only when the energy condition \(\epsilon \neq -m\) holds and after introducing a "quasi-Hamiltonian" that commutes with the symmetry generators.

## Method

The authors introduce a quasi-Hamiltonian \(\widetilde{H} = p^2/(2\widetilde{m}) + V(r)\) (where \(\widetilde{m} = (\epsilon+m)/2\) and \(E = \epsilon - m\)) that transforms the Klein-Gordon equation with ESVP into a Schrödinger-like form. They then derive the generators of the symmetry groups for the Coulomb and harmonic oscillator potentials in both a flat plane and on a sphere, verifying that these generators commute with \(\widetilde{H}\). The Casimir operators of the resulting SO(3) and SU(2) groups (and the Higgs algebra on the sphere) are used to compute the relativistic energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- For the Coulomb potential in a plane: relativistic energy levels \(\epsilon = \frac{n^2 - k^2}{n^2 + k^2} m\), where \(n = 2j+1 = 1,3,5,\dots\) and \(k\) is the coupling constant.
- For the harmonic oscillator potential in a plane: the relativistic energy \(\epsilon\) is the real root of \((\epsilon - m)^2 (\epsilon + m) = 2m\omega^2 (n+1)^2\), with \(n = 0,1,2,\dots\).
- For the Coulomb potential on a sphere: energy levels satisfy \(\epsilon - m = -\frac{\epsilon + m}{(2j+1)^2} k^2 + \frac{\lambda}{\epsilon + m} j(j+1)\), where \(\lambda\) is the curvature and \(j = 0,1,2,\dots\).
- For the harmonic oscillator on a sphere: energy levels satisfy \(\epsilon - m = \frac{\lambda (n+1)^2}{\epsilon + m} + \sqrt{\frac{2m}{\epsilon + m} \omega^2 + \frac{\lambda^2}{(\epsilon + m)^2}} (n+1)\), with \(n = 0,1,2,\dots\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry analysis previously applied to the Dirac equation with equal scalar and vector potentials by Ginocchio (for U(3) and pseudo-U(3) symmetry) and by the same authors (Zhang and Chen) for the two-dimensional Dirac equation. It also generalizes the Higgs algebra, originally found by Higgs (1979) for non-relativistic motion on a sphere, to the relativistic Klein-Gordon case. The results enable a unified approach to solving the spectra of both the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations with ESVP, suggesting that if the Schrödinger equation with a given potential is integrable, the corresponding relativistic energy can be obtained via a simple substitution rule.
