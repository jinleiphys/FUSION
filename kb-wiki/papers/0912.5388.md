# New approach to NN with perturbative pions

> The current status of effective field theory (EFT) descriptions of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions is briefly reviewed, and a new formulation of EFT which treats pion interactions perturbatively is presented. This approach differs from the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) expansion in that the singular short distance part of the pion tensor interaction is summed to all orders.

## Key claim

Beane proposes a modified effective field theory (EFT) for nucleon-nucleon interactions that treats pions perturbatively, differing from the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) expansion by summing the singular short-distance part of the pion tensor interaction to all orders via a Pauli-Villars-like modification of the pion propagator, with the claim that this reordering yields convergent results in spin-triplet channels where the original KSW expansion fails.

## Method

The author replaces the standard pion propagator with a modified version that includes two fictitious meson propagators: one with the same spin-isospin structure as the pion but opposite sign and mass \(\lambda\), and another with isospin \(I=1\), spin \(J=0\) and mass \(\lambda\). This modification cancels the \(1/r^3\) singularity of the tensor pion interaction at short distances, shifting that physics into contact interactions. The scattering amplitudes for the \({}^{3}S_1\), \({}^{3}D_1\), and \(\epsilon_1\) partial wave channels are then computed to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using dimensional regularization with renormalization scale \(\mu = m_\pi\), treating \(\lambda\) as an \(O(Q)\) scale in the power counting.

## Key numbers

- The scale governing KSW convergence is \(\Lambda_{NN} = 16\pi f_\pi^2/(g_A^2 M) = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The modified propagator uses a heavy mass scale \(\lambda = 750\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the central results shown in Fig. 2.
- The variation of phase shifts is studied over the range \(600\ \mathrm{MeV} \le \lambda \le 1000\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Fig. 3), with results not extremely sensitive at low center-of-mass momentum \(p\).
- The \({}^{1}S_0\) phase shift in the original KSW expansion is shown up to NNLO compared with the Nijmegen PWA93 partial wave analysis (Fig. 1), with momentum \(p\) in the center-of-mass frame.
- The original KSW expansion fails to converge above \(p \sim 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the \({}^{3}S_1\) channel.

## Context

This work extends the KSW expansion of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996, 1998) by addressing its failure in spin-triplet channels, which was identified by Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (1999) as arising from the singular \(-1/r^3\) tensor potential. The approach contrasts with the Weinberg EFT program (Weinberg, 1990, 1991, 1992), which solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly with a truncated potential but suffers from regulator-scale dependence as shown by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005). The new method enables analytic, renormalized calculations of NN scattering to NNLO and can be extended to electromagnetic processes such as form factors, Compton scattering, polarizabilities, and radiative capture, though generalization to higher partial waves remains an open challenge.
