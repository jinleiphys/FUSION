> 4 pages, 4 figures, proceedings of "Diffraction 2016", International Workshop on Diffraction in High-Energy Physics, Acireale (Catania, Sicily), Sept. 2-8, 2016; to be published in the conference proceedings by AIP. arXiv admin note: substantial text overlap with arXiv:1609.05144

# Accessing Generalized Parton Distributions in Exclusive Photoproduction of a γρ Pair with a Large Invariant Mass

## Key claim
The photoproduction of a γρ pair with large invariant mass and small transverse momentum of the final nucleon can access generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at JLab 12-GeV kinematics, with the claim of feasibility depending on the modeling of GPDs via double distribution ansätze using two scenarios ("valence" and "standard") for polarized parton distribution functions.

## Method
The authors compute the scattering amplitude for the process γ + N → γ + ρ⁰ + N' using collinear QCD factorization, where a wide-angle Compton scattering subprocess γ(qq̄) → γρ at large M_{γρ} factorizes from GPDs. The hard amplitude is calculated at leading twist from 20 Feynman diagrams (8 contribute for chiral-odd transversely polarized ρ, all 20 for chiral-even longitudinally polarized ρ), and the differential cross section is obtained by integrating over t and u' after introducing a phenomenological factorized dipole t-dependence. The GPDs are modeled via double distribution ansätze based on unpolarized, polarized, and transversity parton distributions.

## Key numbers
- The squared invariant mass M²_{γρ} of the γρ⁰ system provides the hard scale, approximated as M²_{γρ} ≈ p̅_t²/(αᾱ) in the generalized Bjorken limit.
- The total cms energy squared S_{γN} = (q + p₁)² = (1+ξ)s + M², with values varied across the set 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20 GeV² covering the JLab energy range.
- The differential cross section dσ/dM²_{γρ} for longitudinally polarized ρ (chiral-even) is shown in nb·GeV⁻², with integrated cross sections σ_even on the order of nb.
- The differential cross section for transversely polarized ρ (chiral-odd) is shown in pb·GeV⁻², with integrated cross sections σ_odd on the order of pb.
- The squared transferred momentum on the nucleon side is t = -(1+ξ)/(1-ξ)Δ̅_t² - 4ξ²M²/(1-ξ²).

## Context
This work extends the factorization approach used for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production, as reviewed in the literature, to a 2→3 process with a γρ final state. It builds on the strategy of Ref. IPST-eps for high-energy diffractive photoproduction of two vector mesons and on similar proposals by Beiyad (2010) and Kumano to enlarge the set of processes for extracting chiral-even GPDs. The separation of ρ_L and ρ_T polarizations opens access to chiral-odd GPDs, which are very poorly known compared to chiral-even GPDs, and enables a new test of GPD universality at JLab 12-GeV.
