# Probing short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions with an Electron-Ion Collider

> We derive the cross-section for exclusive vector meson production in high energy deeply inelastic scattering off a deuteron target that disintegrates into a proton and a neutron carrying large relative momentum in the final state. This cross-section can be expressed in terms of a novel gluon Transition Generalized Parton Distribution (T-GPD); the hard scale in the final state makes the T-GPD sensitive to the short distance nucleon-nucleon interaction. We perform a toy model computation of this process in a perturbative framework and discuss the time scales that allow the separation of initial and final state dynamics in the T-GPD. We outline the more general computation based on the factorization suggested by the toy computation: in particular, we discuss the relative role of "point-like" and "geometric" Fock configurations that control the parton dynamics of short range nucleon-nucleon scattering. With the aid of exclusive $J/Ψ$ production data at HERA, as well as elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections, we estimate rates for exclusive deuteron photo-disintegration at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Our results, obtained using conservative estimates of EIC integrated luminosities, suggest that center-of-mass energies $s_{NN}\sim 12$ GeV$^2$ of the neutron-proton subsystem can be accessed. We argue that the high energies of the EIC can address outstanding dynamical questions regarding the short-range quark-gluon structure of nuclear forces by providing clean gluon probes of such "knockout" exclusive reactions in light and heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The cross-section for exclusive vector meson production in high-energy deeply inelastic scattering off a deuteron target that disintegrates into a proton and neutron with large relative momentum can be expressed in terms of a novel gluon Transition Generalized Parton Distribution (T-GPD), and the authors estimate that center-of-mass energies of the neutron-proton subsystem up to \(s_{NN}\sim 12\) GeV\(^2\) can be accessed at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) using conservative luminosity estimates, though the extension to significantly higher \(s_{NN}\) will be challenging if the energy dependence follows that measured in nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors derive the cross-section for exclusive \(J/\Psi\) electroproduction off a deuteron target with coincident detection of a back-to-back proton and neutron, using light-front perturbation theory to separate the time scales of the projectile dipole evolution from the instantaneous gluon exchange with the target. They perform a toy model computation with valence quarks replacing the nucleons to illustrate factorization of the T-GPD into the deuteron wavefunction and a final-state nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude, then generalize this to a realistic ansatz expressing the cross-section as the product of the deuteron wavefunction, the exclusive \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction cross-section off the proton, and the elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section. Rates for the EIC are estimated using exclusive \(J/\Psi\) data from HERA and elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- The photon-deuteron center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}\) is the hardest scale, on the order of \(\sim 100\) GeV per nucleon at the EIC.
- The nucleon recoil transverse momentum \(p_T\) is an intermediate scale of \(\sim 1\) GeV, ensuring \(p_T^2 \gg \Lambda_{QCD}^2\) (where \(\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 200\) MeV).
- The invariant momentum transfer \(|T|\) to the \(J/\Psi\) is a soft scale of a few hundred MeV\(^2\).
- The mass of the \(J/\Psi\) is \(M_J \sim 3\) GeV, providing a hard scale for perturbative QCD.
- The center-of-mass energy squared of the neutron-proton subsystem is \(s_{NN} \sim 4 p_T^2\), with accessible values up to \(s_{NN} \sim 12\) GeV\(^2\).
- The EIC peak luminosity is estimated to be \(\sim 10^{33-34} \, cm^{-2} s^{-1}\), compared to the HERA peak luminosity of \(\sim 10^{31} \, cm^{-2} s^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture of exclusive vector meson production, previously applied to proton targets at HERA (Chekanov 2002), to deuteron targets with large relative momentum between the final-state nucleons, enabling a gluon probe of short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions. It builds on studies of short-range correlations from Jefferson Lab (Piasetzky 2006) and their connection to the EMC effect (Hen 2013, Higinbotham 2013), as well as parton-based models of large-angle nucleon-nucleon scattering such as the "geometric" Landshoff mechanism (Landshoff 1974) and the "point-like" quark counting mechanism (Brodsky 1974, Matveev 1973). The results enable future EIC experiments to distinguish between these parton models of the short-range nuclear force and to study the transition from hadronic to partonic degrees of freedom in nucleon-nucleon interactions.
