# Some Three-body force cancellations in Chiral Lagrangians

> The cancellation between off-shell two body forces and three body forces implies a tremendous simplification in the study of three body resonances in two meson-one baryon systems. While this can be done by means of Faddeev equations we provide an alternative and simpler derivation using just the chiral Lagrangian and the field re-parametrization invariance.

## Key claim

The cancellation between off-shell two-body forces and three-body forces in chiral Lagrangians for two-meson–one-baryon systems can be derived simply from field reparameterization invariance, without invoking Faddeev equations or equations of motion; the cancellation holds off-shell and depends on a specific choice of the field coordinate parameter \(\xi = -1/12\).

## Method

The author expands the chiral Lagrangian for baryons and mesons in powers of the pion field \(\vec{\phi}\), using a generalized polar decomposition of the unitary field \(u = e^{iH}\) with \(H = \vec{\tau}\cdot\vec{\phi} + \xi \vec{\tau}\cdot\vec{\phi}\,(\vec{\phi}\cdot\vec{\phi}) + \mathcal{O}(\phi^5)\). By computing the connection \(\Gamma^\mu\) to order \(\phi^4\) and discarding total derivatives, the coefficient of the four-pion term is shown to be proportional to \((2\xi + 1/6)\). Setting \(\xi = -1/12\) eliminates all three-body \(\pi\pi N\) forces at order \(1/f^4\) in the chiral Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The pion weak decay constant for massless quarks is \(f \sim 88\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The \(SU(3)\) coupling constants from semileptonic hyperon decays are \(\mathcal{F} \sim 0.46\) and \(\mathcal{D} \sim 0.79\), with \(\mathcal{F}+\mathcal{D} = g_A = 1.25\).
- The cancellation of three-body forces occurs at order \(1/f^4\) in the chiral Lagrangian.
- The field parameter choice that cancels the four-pion terms is \(\xi = -1/12\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Martinez Torres and Khemchandani (2007, 2008), who studied three-hadron resonances with baryon number \(B=1\) using Faddeev equations and found a similar cancellation using on-shell conditions with standard polar coordinates (\(\xi=0\)). The present derivation shows the cancellation is a consequence of field reparameterization invariance and holds off-shell, simplifying the three-body problem to a linear algebraic eigenvalue problem. The result also connects to earlier work by Weinberg (1992) on the suppression of three-body forces in chiral EFT for nuclear physics, and to the analysis of pion-deuteron scattering by Beane (2002), where three-body corrections at threshold vanish at order \(1/f^4\).
