# Test the chiral magnetic effect with isobaric collisions

> The quark-gluon matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may contain local domains in which P and CP symmetries are not preserved. When coupled with an external magnetic field, such P- and CP-odd domains will generate electric currents along the magnetic field --- a phenomenon called the chiral magnetic effect (CME). Recently, the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC released data of charge-dependent azimuthal-angle correlators with features consistent with the CME expectation. However, the experimental observable is contaminated with significant background contributions from elliptic-flow-driven effects, which makes the interpretation of the data ambiguous. In this Letter, we show that the collisions of isobaric nuclei, $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr, provide an ideal tool to disentangle the CME signal from the background effects. Our simulation demonstrates that the two collision types at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV have more than $10\%$ difference in the CME signal and less than $2\%$ difference in the elliptic-flow-driven backgrounds for the centrality range of $20-60\%$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that collisions of isobaric nuclei (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at √sNN = 200 GeV can disentangle the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal from elliptic-flow-driven background contributions, because simulations show more than 10% difference in the CME signal but less than 2% difference in the backgrounds for 20-60% centrality. The claim is conditional on the assumed background level of two-thirds and on the specific nuclear deformity parameters used.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations to compute the multiplicity distributions and initial spatial eccentricities for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They used the HIJING model to calculate the initial magnetic field squared with azimuthal fluctuation correction (B_sq) for both systems. Using a two-component perturbative approach, they projected the relative difference in the charge-separation observable S = N_part Δγ between the two collision types, assuming a background level of two-thirds and using STAR measurements of Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions as baseline.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in B_sq between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions approaches 15% (case 1) or 18% (case 2) for peripheral events, and reduces to about 13% (both cases) for central events.
- The relative difference in the initial eccentricity (R_ε2) is highly consistent with 0 for peripheral events in the 20-60% centrality range.
- The relative difference in S (R_S) is about 5% for the centrality range of 20-60% for both parameter sets of Glauber inputs.
- When combining events of 20-60% centralities, R_S is 5σ above R_ε2 for both parameter sets.
- With 400 million events for each collision type, the background level can be determined with an accuracy of about 7%.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, following measurements by the STAR Collaboration (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2013) and ALICE Collaboration (Abelev 2012) that showed charge-dependent correlator features consistent with CME but contaminated by elliptic-flow-driven backgrounds. The approach builds on an earlier proposal by Voloshin (2010) to use isobaric collisions, and contrasts with the uranium-uranium collision approach where isolating tip-tip from body-body collisions proved difficult (Adamczyk 2015). The results enable a definitive test of whether the observed charge separation in heavy-ion collisions originates from the CME or from background effects.
