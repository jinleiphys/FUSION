# Nuclear Forces and Chiral Symmetry

> We review the main achievements of the research programme for the study of nuclear forces in the framework of chiral symmetry and discuss some problems which are still open.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the achievements of the chiral symmetry program for nuclear forces, but notes that the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) derived from heavy baryon and covariant chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) do not coincide, and that the leading-order central isospin-independent component begins at O(q^3) rather than O(q^2), indicating poor convergence for that term.

## Method

The authors review the chiral perturbation theory framework for nuclear forces, focusing on the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) derived from both heavy baryon and covariant ChPT up to O(q^4). They analyze the dynamical content of the TPEP in terms of three families of diagrams: family I (minimal chiral realization with only pion-nucleon couplings g_A and f_pi), family II (pion-pion correlations), and family III (low-energy constants, LECs). They compare chiral TPEP components with partial wave analyses and with results from the Argonne group.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion uses a typical scale q set by either pion four-momenta or nucleon three-momenta, such that q < 1 GeV.
- The leading contribution to the TPEP is O(q^2), with expansions available up to O(q^4).
- The influence of the regularization scheme is restricted to distances smaller than 1 fm.
- The central isospin-independent component V_C^+ begins at O(q^3), not O(q^2) as formally predicted for the leading TPEP terms.

## Context

This work extends the research program initiated by Taketani, Nakamura and Sasaki (1950s) and later systematized by Weinberg (early 1990s) for applying chiral symmetry to nuclear forces. It builds on the heavy baryon ChPT approach (Epelbaum, 2005) and the covariant ChPT approach by the authors' own group (Higa, Robilotta, da Rocha). The paper identifies open problems including the need for a full infrared regularization calculation, the role of explicit delta degrees of freedom, and the compatibility of LECs extracted from NN and pi-N scattering.
