# Anisotropy effects on heavy quark dynamics in Gribov modified gluon plasma

> In the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the momentum distribution of the quark-gluon plasma is anisotropic, leading to instabilities in the system due to chromomagnetic plasma modes. In this work, we consider the anisotropic momentum distribution of the medium constituents to investigate its effects on heavy quark dynamics using the nonperturbative Gribov resummation approach within the framework of the Fokker-Planck equation. Specifically, we study the influence of nonperturbative effects and weak anisotropies on the heavy quark transport coefficients, taking into account the angular dependence between the anisotropy vector and the direction of heavy quark motion. Furthermore, the calculated drag and diffusion coefficients are employed to estimate the energy loss of heavy quarks and the nuclear modification factor, incorporating both elastic collisions and inelastic processes. Our findings indicate that momentum anisotropy, angular dependence, and nonperturbative effects-captured through the scattering amplitudes-play a significant role in determining the transport properties of heavy quarks.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that momentum anisotropy, angular dependence between the anisotropy vector and heavy quark direction, and nonperturbative effects from Gribov resummation all significantly influence heavy quark transport coefficients, energy loss, and the nuclear modification factor, but only for weak anisotropies (ξ ≪ 1) where the anisotropic distribution function can be treated as a small deviation from the isotropic case.

## Method

The authors extended the Gribov-Zwanziger nonperturbative resummation approach from isotropic to anisotropic QCD medium by modifying the isotropic distribution function of medium constituents through a rescaling along a preferred direction n, using the ansatz of Romatschke and Strickland (2003). They computed heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients within the Fokker-Planck equation framework, decomposing the drag into two components and the diffusion tensor into four components due to the presence of the anisotropy vector. Both elastic 2→2 scattering processes and inelastic radiative processes (including gluon emission with LPM suppression and dead-cone effect) were incorporated, with scattering amplitudes calculated using the Gribov propagator that provides a natural infrared cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The Gribov mass parameter γ_G behaves as a magnetic scale in the asymptotically high-temperature limit, with scale g²T
- The strong running coupling is defined as α_s(T) = 6π/[(11N_c - 2N_f) ln(2πT/Λ_MS)] with Λ_MS = 176 MeV for N_f = 3
- The anisotropy parameter ξ ranges from -1 to ∞, with ξ < 0 giving prolate shape and ξ > 0 giving oblate shape; the work considers ξ ≪ 1
- QGP thermalization occurs within less than 1 fm/c according to hydrodynamic models
- The normalization constant for the anisotropic distribution is C_ξ = √(1+ξ)

## Context

This work extends previous isotropic heavy quark transport studies using the Gribov-Zwanziger approach (Sumit et al., 2023) to include momentum anisotropy, building on the anisotropic distribution formalism of Romatschke and Strickland (2003). It also connects to prior work on heavy quark diffusion in the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase (Mrowczynski, 2017; Ruggieri, 2018; Khowal, 2021) and to studies of collective modes in anisotropic QCD matter (Carrington, 2014; Kumar, 2017; Srivastava, 2015). The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark dynamics in the early, anisotropic stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the momentum distribution is not yet in thermal equilibrium.
