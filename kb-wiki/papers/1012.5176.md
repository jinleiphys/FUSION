# On the possibility to measure nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay in charge-exchange reactions

> As shown in Ref.\cite{Rod09}, the Fermi nuclear matrix element $M^{0ν}_F$ of neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay can be reconstructed if one is able to measure the isospin-forbidden single Fermi transition matrix element from the ground state of the final nucleus to the isobaric analog state (IAS) of the initial nucleus, for instance by means of charge-exchange reactions of the $(n,p)$-type. Here, simple estimates for $^{82}$Se are made which show that indeed the tiny cross section $σ_{np}(0_f^+\to IAS)$ is dominated by the admixture of the double IAS in the ground state of the final nucleus provided that the isospin mixing is weak and can be treated perturbatively. A measurement of such a cross section would definitely be a very difficult task, but it can advance a lot our knowledge of the $0νββ$ nuclear matrix element.

## Key claim

The Fermi nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double beta decay can be reconstructed from a measurement of the isospin-forbidden single Fermi transition from the final nucleus ground state to the isobaric analog state of the initial nucleus, provided that the isospin mixing is weak and can be treated perturbatively; the cross section for this transition is dominated by the admixture of the double isobaric analog state in the final nucleus ground state.

## Method

The authors perform simple estimates for the nucleus \(^{82}\)Se using two complementary methods: \(\Delta N = 2\) particle-hole excitations are treated in the independent Bogolyubov quasiparticle picture within a large \(7\hbar\omega\) single-particle space, while \(\Delta N = 0\) particle-hole excitations are described within the continuum-QRPA with zero-range Landau-Migdal forces. They compute the ratios \(r^{\mathrm{(I)}}\) and \(r^{\mathrm{(II)}}\) of the \((n,p)\) reaction cross sections via the two competing \(\Delta T=1\) isospin-mixing channels relative to the \((p,n)\) reference cross sections, and compare these to the cross section from the double isobaric analog state admixture.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-forbidden matrix element \(\langle IAS | \hat T^{+} | 0_f \rangle\) is estimated as \(\sim 0.005\), about a thousand times smaller than the first-leg matrix element \(\langle IAS | \hat T^{-} | 0_i \rangle \approx \sqrt{N-Z}\).
- For \(^{82}\)Se, the contribution of \(\Delta N = 2\) particle-hole excitations gives \(r^{\mathrm{(I)}} = 6.6 \times 10^{-7}\) and \(r^{\mathrm{(II)}} = 1.3 \times 10^{-5}\).
- For \(^{82}\)Se, the contribution of \(\Delta N = 0\) particle-hole excitations gives \(r^{\mathrm{(I)}} = 6.4 \times 10^{-8}\) and \(r^{\mathrm{(II)}} = 2.2 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The ratios of the competing channel cross sections to the DIAS-admixture cross section are both \(\sim 0.1\), confirming dominance of the DIAS channel.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Rodin (2009) that the Fermi matrix element of neutrinoless double beta decay can be reconstructed from charge-exchange reactions, by providing quantitative estimates for a specific nucleus (\(^{82}\)Se). It contrasts with earlier attempts to reconstruct two-neutrino double beta decay amplitudes from charge-exchange reactions (Frekers and collaborators), which relied on single-state dominance and could not determine relative phases. The results enable future experimental searches for the tiny \((n,p)\) cross section on the final nucleus, which would directly constrain the Fermi part of the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) nuclear matrix element.
