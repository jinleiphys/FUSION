# Scaling properties of nuclear parton distributions in short-range-correlation motivated two-component parametrization

> We provide some critical remarks on the recently proposed two-component parametrization of nuclear parton distribution functions, which was motivated by the apparent correlation between the nuclear modifications of structure functions and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation phenomena. This parametrization, we show, is invariant under a rescaling transformation of the involved abundance coefficients, which means that the global normalization of these coefficients cannot be meaningfully determined in a fit, and only their ratios should be studied for finding evidence of short-range-correlation type behavior at parton level. As we show, however, the current constraints for the nuclear-mass dependence of these coefficients allow also for interpretations different from short-range correlations. Nevertheless, this two-component parametrization exhibits a similar scaling relation for DIS structure functions as demonstrated in earlier works, and, as we demonstrate, yields testable predictions for structure-function and hard-process cross-section ratios. We also note on the non-trivial isospin dependence of the short-range-correlation motivated parametrization, which under proton-neutron pair dominance assumption can lead to charge-symmetry-violation resembling terms.

## Key claim

The two-component parametrization of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), motivated by short-range correlations (SRCs), is invariant under a rescaling transformation of the abundance coefficients, meaning that only the ratios of these coefficients (not their absolute normalization) can be meaningfully determined from a fit; furthermore, the current constraints on the nuclear-mass dependence of these coefficients allow for interpretations other than SRCs, such as a simple volume-plus-surface-correction model.

## Method

The author analytically examines the two-component SRC-motivated nPDF parametrization proposed in PhysRevLett.133.152502, where bound-nucleon PDFs are written as a linear combination of free-nucleon PDFs and SRC-nucleon PDFs weighted by abundance coefficients \(C^A_p\) and \(C^A_n\). The paper demonstrates a rescaling invariance of this parametrization, derives isospin relations for the coefficients, and reformulates the parametrization in terms of a reference nucleus to avoid the normalization ambiguity. The author then derives scaling relations for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) structure functions and hadron-nucleus cross sections that follow from the two-component hypothesis, and compares the resulting nuclear-mass dependence with a simple geometric model of a central volume plus surface correction.

## Key numbers

* The rescaling transformation allows arbitrary nonzero scalar factors \(X\) and \(Y\) to rescale \(C^A_p \to X C^A_p\) and \(C^A_n \to Y C^A_n\) while adjusting the SRC PDFs accordingly, leaving all physical observables invariant.
* For isoscalar nuclei, the ratio of structure functions satisfies \(\frac{F^{Z=N}_i(x,Q)}{F^\mathrm{D}_i(x,Q)} = 1 + C^{(Z=N)}_p \left[ \frac{F^{\mathrm{SRC}p}_i(x,Q) + F^{\mathrm{SRC}n}_i(x,Q)}{F^p_i(x,Q) + F^n_i(x,Q)} - 1 \right]\).
* The ratio of structure-function deviations for two isoscalar nuclei is \(\frac{F^{Z_1=N_1}_i(x,Q)/F^\mathrm{D}_i(x,Q) - 1}{F^{Z_2=N_2}_i(x,Q)/F^\mathrm{D}_i(x,Q) - 1} = \frac{C^{(Z_1=N_1)}_p}{C^{(Z_2=N_2)}_p}\).
* For hadron-nucleus cross sections, the analogous scaling for isoscalar nuclei is \(\frac{\sigma_{h+A_1}^{k+X}/\sigma_{h+\mathrm{D}}^{k+X} - 1}{\sigma_{h+A_2}^{k+X}/\sigma_{h+\mathrm{D}}^{k+X} - 1} = \frac{C^{(Z_1=N_1)}_p}{C^{(Z_2=N_2)}_p}\).
* In the geometric model, the fraction of modified protons is approximated as \(C_p^\mathrm{V} + C_p^\mathrm{S} A^{-1/3}\), with the surface term coefficient involving \(3T/(2 \times 1.12\ \mathrm{fm})\) where \(T\) is the surface thickness and the nuclear radius is \(R \approx 1.12\ \mathrm{fm} \times A^{1/3}\).

## Context

This work provides critical commentary on the SRC-motivated nPDF parametrization introduced by PhysRevLett.133.152502, which was the first to apply the two-component formalism to a global fit of nPDFs. The paper extends earlier scaling relations for DIS structure functions found in CLAS (2019), PhysRevLett.124.092002, Xu (2019), PhysRevD.104.033002, PhysRevD.108.053005, and Huang (2025), showing they apply to any structure function or collinearly factorizable hadron-nucleus cross section. The author notes that the scaling property was already demonstrated 40 years ago in PhysRevLett.52.2344 and FRANKFURT (1988) using an "exotic component" interpretation. The reformulation in terms of a reference nucleus (analogous to the EPPS21 parametrization of Eskola (2021)) enables testable predictions for future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and LHC, particularly for dijet production in proton-oxygen and proton-lead collisions.
