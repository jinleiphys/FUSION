# Doubly Charmed Pentaquarks

> The LHCb Collaboration, using its full data set from runs $1$ and $2$, announced in $2019$ a surprising update of the hidden-charm pentaquark states $P_c(4380)^+$ and $P_c(4450)^+$, observed in 2015. A new state, $P_c(4312)^+$, was clearly seen at lower energies; furthermore, the original $P_c(4450)$ resonance was resolved into two individual states, named the $P_c(4440)^+$ and the $P_c(4457)^+$. Motivated by the fact that these new hidden-charm pentaquark states were successfully predicted by our chiral quark model, we extend herein such study to the doubly charmed sector. The analyzed total spin and parity quantum numbers are $J^P=\frac12^-$, $\frac32^-$ and $\frac52^-$, in the $I=\frac12$ and $\frac32$ isospin channels. We find several possible narrow baryon-meson resonances (theoretical masses in parenthesis): $IJ^P = \frac12 \frac12^-$ $Σ_c D(4356)$, $\frac12 \frac32^-$ $Σ^*_c D(4449)$, $\frac32 \frac12^-$ $Σ_c D(4431)$, $\frac32 \frac12^-$ $Σ_c D(4446)$, $\frac32 \frac32^-$ $Σ_c D^*(4514)$ and $\frac32 \frac52^-$ $Ξ^*_{cc} ρ(4461)$ whose widths are $4.8$, $8.0$, $2.6$, $2.2$, $4.0$ and $3.0\,\text{MeV}$, respectively. Moreover, one shallow bound-state is found, too, with quantum numbers $IJ^P = \frac12 \frac32^-$ $Ξ^*_{cc} π(3757)$. These doubly charmed pentaquark states are expected to be identified in future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors extend their chiral quark model, which successfully predicted the hidden-charm pentaquark states later observed by LHCb, to the doubly charmed sector; they find several narrow baryon-meson resonances and one shallow bound state, but these are theoretical predictions that "are expected to be identified in future experiments" rather than claimed observations.

## Method

The authors solve a five-body bound state problem using a nonrelativistic chiral quark model Hamiltonian that includes color-confining, one-gluon-exchange, and Goldstone-boson-exchange interactions, with the complex scaling method (CSM) applied to distinguish bound, resonance, and continuum states. The wave function is expanded using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with infinitesimally-shifted Gaussians, considering four different baryon-meson configurations for the ccqqq system with total spin and parity J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^- and 5/2^- in the I = 1/2 and 3/2 isospin channels.

## Key numbers

- Σ_c D state with IJ^P = 1/2 1/2^- at theoretical mass 4356 MeV and width 4.8 MeV
- Σ*_c D state with IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^- at theoretical mass 4449 MeV and width 8.0 MeV
- Σ_c D state with IJ^P = 3/2 1/2^- at theoretical mass 4431 MeV and width 2.6 MeV
- Σ_c D state with IJ^P = 3/2 1/2^- at theoretical mass 4446 MeV and width 2.2 MeV
- Σ_c D* state with IJ^P = 3/2 3/2^- at theoretical mass 4514 MeV and width 4.0 MeV
- Ξ*_cc ρ state with IJ^P = 3/2 5/2^- at theoretical mass 4461 MeV and width 3.0 MeV
- Ξ*_cc π shallow bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^- at theoretical mass 3757 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model study of hidden-charm pentaquarks (Yang, Ping, Segovia, 2015), which successfully predicted the P_c(4312)^+, P_c(4440)^+ and P_c(4457)^+ states later observed by LHCb in 2019, to the doubly charmed sector. The study uses the same theoretical framework and parameter set as those earlier hidden-charm and hidden-bottom pentaquark calculations, and is the first application of the complex scaling method to pentaquark systems. The predicted doubly charmed pentaquark states provide targets for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb.
