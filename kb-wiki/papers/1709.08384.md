# Quenching the CME via the gravitational anomaly and holography

> In the presence of a gravitational contribution to the chiral anomaly, the chiral magnetic effect induces an energy current proportional to the square of the temperature in equilibrium. In holography the thermal state corresponds to a black hole. We numerically study holographic quenches in which a planar shell of scalar matter falls into a black hole and rises its temperature. During the process the momentum density (energy current) is conserved. The energy current has two components, a non-dissipative one induced by the anomaly and a dissipative flow component. The dissipative component can be measured via the drag it asserts on an additional auxiliary color charge. Our results indicate strong suppression very far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) induced by the gravitational anomaly is strongly suppressed far from equilibrium; the anomalous energy current does not build up during the initial energy injection but only activates as the system approaches thermal equilibrium, contradicting the possibility that it could be governed by the energy density.

## Method

The authors numerically study holographic quantum quenches in a 5D Einstein-Maxwell-scalar theory with a mixed Chern-Simons term that implements the gravitational anomaly. A planar shell of scalar matter falls into a black hole, raising its temperature from T0 to T; the evolution of the momentum density (energy current) is monitored via the drag it exerts on an auxiliary, anomaly-free U(1) charge. The system is solved to first order in the Chern-Simons coupling and magnetic field, with the auxiliary gauge field treated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium energy current from the gravitational anomaly is J_epsilon = 32 pi^2 T^2 lambda B (equation 1).
- The benchmark equilibrium curve for the normalized auxiliary current is j_X = (T^2/T0^2 - 1) / (T^4/T0^4), with maximum value 1/4 at T_m/T0 = sqrt(2).
- For fast quenches (2 tau T < 1), the current overshoots its final equilibrium value by approximately 4% before stabilizing.
- The current equilibrates at t T approximately 1.2, independent of the quench timescale tau, while the energy density equilibrates within t/tau approximately 1.75.
- The transition from fast to intermediate quenches occurs when 2 tau T approximately 0.65 to 1.
- For slow quenches with 2 tau T > 2, a maximum reappears; for processes with tau T0 = 0.175 and very high final temperature, the maximum of j_X slowly decreases with increasing final temperature rather than approaching 1/4.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of anomalous transport in quenches that focused on the pure U(1)^3 anomaly (Lin 2013, Ammon 2016) to the gravitational anomaly contribution. It also builds on hydrodynamic treatments of anomalous transport (Son 2009, Neiman 2010, Stephanov 2015) and drag in anomalous fluids (Rajagopal 2015, Stephanov 2015). The results show that the gravitational anomaly contribution to the CME is strongly suppressed far from equilibrium, implying that applications to heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2015) must account for the short-lived magnetic field decaying before local thermal equilibrium is reached (Skokov 2009). The work enables future studies of how different anomaly contributions behave out of equilibrium and what controls the onset of anomalous transport.
