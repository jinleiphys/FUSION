# QCD evolution of the orbital angular momentum of quarks and gluons: Genuine twist-three part

> We present the numerical solution of the one-loop QCD evolution equation for the genuine twist-three part of the orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions of quarks and gluons inside a longitudinally polarized nucleon. This is based on the observation that the evolution is identical to that of the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman function for transverse single spin asymmetry. Together with the known evolution of the Wandzura-Wilczek part, the one-loop evolution of OAM distributions is now practically under control. We also study, for the first time, the scale dependence of the potential angular momentum defined as the difference between the Ji and Jaffe-Manohar definitions of OAM.

## Key claim

The one-loop QCD evolution of the genuine twist-three part of quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions is identical to that of the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman (ETQS) function for transverse single spin asymmetry, and the authors demonstrate the feasibility of numerically solving this evolution equation using a publicly available C++ code, though the evolution does not close in moment space (different moments mix).

## Method

The authors adapted a C++ code originally developed by Pirnay (2013) for the ETQS function evolution to solve the one-loop evolution equations for the genuine twist-three OAM distributions \(L_{3q}(x)\) and \(L_{3g}(x)\). They converted the operator definitions of the quark-gluon and three-gluon correlators \(\Phi_F, \widetilde{\Phi}_F, M_F, \widetilde{M}_F\) into the \(C\)-even combinations \(S^+\) and \(F^+\) used in the code, implemented three different model initial conditions at \(\mu_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), and evolved to \(\mu^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) using a lattice of 2001 points in \(x\) with spacing \(\Delta x = 0.001\).

## Key numbers

- The potential angular momentum \(L_{\text{pot}}\) (the difference between Ji and Jaffe-Manohar OAM definitions) evolved from \(\mu_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) to \(\mu^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\): for initial condition 1, from 0.234 to 0.162; for initial condition 2, from 0.185 to 0.077; for initial condition 3, from 0.069 to 0.032.
- The sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx L_{3g,F}(x) = 0\) was satisfied to within 0.00007 at \(\mu^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) for initial condition 1, 0.0004 for condition 2, and 0.00016 for condition 3.
- The sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx (L_{3q}(x) + L_{3g,S}(x)) = 0\) was satisfied to within 0.00014 at \(\mu^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) for initial condition 1, -0.0007 for condition 2, and -0.0002 for condition 3.
- The large-\(n\) behavior of the extracted anomalous dimensions showed \(A_n = -\frac{N_c^2-1}{N_c}\ln n + \text{const.}\) and \(D_n = -2N_c\ln n + \text{const.}\), matching the cusp anomalous dimensions of the twist-two splitting functions \(\gamma^{n+1}_{qq}\) and \(\gamma^{n+1}_{gg}\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of OAM distributions by Hatta (2012) and the evolution of the Wandzura-Wilczek part by Hoodbhoy (1998) and Boussarie (2019), providing the missing genuine twist-three evolution. It relies on the equivalence to the ETQS function evolution derived by Braun (2009) and the numerical code of Pirnay (2013). The results enable future global QCD analyses of OAM distributions at next-to-leading order and provide the first scale dependence study of the potential angular momentum, which has been calculated on the lattice by Engelhardt (2017, 2019).
