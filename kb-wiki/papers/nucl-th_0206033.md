# Multipole Amplitudes of Pion Photoproduction on Nucleons up to 2GeV within Dispersion Relations and Unitary Isobar Model

> Two approaches for analysis of pion photo- and electroproduction on nucleons in the resonance energy region are checked at $Q^2=0$ using the results of GWU(VPI) partial-wave analysis of photoproduction data. The approaches are based on dispersion relations and unitary isobar model. Within dispersion relations good description of photoproduction multipoles is obtained up to $W=1.8 GeV$. Within unitary isobar model, modified with increasing energy by incorporation of Regge poles, and with unified Breit-Wigner parametrization of resonance contributions, good description of photoproduction multipoles is obtained up to $W=2 GeV$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dispersion relations (DR) can reliably describe pion photoproduction multipole amplitudes up to a center-of-mass energy of W = 1.8 GeV, while a modified unitary isobar model (UIM) that incorporates Regge poles at higher energies can extend this description up to W = 2 GeV, but only if the background contributions are properly modified to avoid the strong contradictions with experimental data that arise from the unmodified UIM above the first resonance region.

## Method

Fixed-t dispersion relations were applied to invariant amplitudes for pion photoproduction on nucleons at Q² = 0, using the GWU(VPI) partial-wave analysis results as input. For the P33(1232) resonance, the dispersion relations were transformed into singular integral equations and solved analytically with the Watson theorem. The unitary isobar model was modified by constructing a background that transitions from the effective Lagrangian approach at low energies to Regge-pole amplitudes at high energies using a smooth interpolation function, with resonance contributions parametrized in a unified Breit-Wigner form.

## Key numbers

- Dispersion relations provide good description of photoproduction multipoles up to W = 1.8 GeV
- Modified unitary isobar model provides good description up to W = 2 GeV
- The P33(1232) resonance phase δ₁₊³/² at W = 1.45 GeV is 157°
- The transition parameter s₀ in the modified UIM background is 1.2 GeV²
- The πN coupling constant g²/4π = 14.2
- The fine structure constant e²/4π = 1/137
- The mixed pseudovector/pseudoscalar cutoff parameter Λ² = 0.12 GeV²
- The Regge trajectory slope α'π = 0.7 GeV⁻²
- Breit-Wigner parameters for nine resonances are tabulated, including masses (e.g., P33(1232) at 1.23 GeV, P11(1440) at 1.45 GeV, S11(1535) at 1.52 GeV, D13(1520) at 1.51 GeV, S11(1650) at 1.65 GeV, F15(1680) at 1.68 GeV, P13(1720) at 1.8 GeV, S31(1620) at 1.61 GeV, D33(1700) at 1.65 GeV, F37(1950) at 1.92 GeV) and widths (e.g., P11(1440) width 0.3 GeV, S11(1535) width 0.11 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of earlier studies and the unitary isobar model developed in Ref. [1] (Aznauryan, 1999) to higher energies, addressing the known limitations of the UIM background above the first resonance region. The modified UIM incorporates Regge-pole behavior from the analysis of high-energy photoproduction data by Ref. [10] (Guidal, Laget, and Vanderhaeghen, 1997), enabling reliable extraction of γ*N → N* transition form factors from electroproduction data at Jefferson Lab. The approach can be continued to non-zero Q², making it applicable to ongoing experimental programs on high duty-factor electron accelerators.
