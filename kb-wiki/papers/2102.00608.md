# Neutrino-nucleon DIS from Holographic QCD: PDFs of sea and valence quarks, form factors, and structure functions of the proton

> We discuss unpolarized neutrino- and anti-neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering (DIS) using a chiral doublet of baryonic sources with explicit symmetry breaking, in a slice of AdS$_5$ with both a hard and soft wall. We explicitly derive the direct and transition form factors for the vector and axial-vector currents for the holographic dual of a proton and neutron. We use them to derive the s-channel structure functions for neutrino and anti-neutrino scattering on a proton and neutron in bulk. The t-channel contributions stemming from the Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges are also evaluated explicitly. The pertinent even and odd structure functions in the limit of large and small parton momentum fraction $x$ are given. The results allow for the extraction of the nonperterbative parton distribution functions carried by the sea and valence quarks both at large-x and small-x regimes. Our holographic PDF sets compare well with LHAPDF and CTEQ PDF sets in the large-x and small-x regimes in the intermediate range of $Q^2<10~\rm{GeV^2}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering structure functions using a holographic QCD model with a chiral doublet of baryonic sources, and extracts nonperturbative parton distribution functions (PDFs) for sea and valence quarks that compare well with LHAPDF and CTEQ PDF sets, but only in the intermediate range of \(Q^2 < 10~\rm{GeV^2}\) and specifically in the large-x and small-x regimes.

## Method

The authors construct a bottom-up holographic model in a slice of AdS\(_5\) with both hard and soft wall confinement, using a Dirac chiral doublet of bulk fermions coupled to U(2) vector and axial-vector gauge fields with explicit chiral symmetry breaking via a bi-fundamental scalar. They derive the direct and transition form factors for vector and axial-vector currents for the holographic proton and neutron, then compute the s-channel structure functions for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nucleons, and evaluate t-channel contributions from Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges. The even and odd parity structure functions are used to extract valence and sea quark PDFs at both large and small parton momentum fraction \(x\).

## Key numbers

- The hard wall IR scale is fixed at \(z_0 = 3.103 / \rm{GeV}\) by identifying the first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0\) (\(\gamma_{0,1} = 2.40483\)) with the rho meson mass \(m_{1=\rho} = 0.775~\rm{GeV}\).
- For the soft wall model without a tachyon coupling, the nucleon spectrum Reggeizes with \(M_n^2 = 4\tilde\kappa^2 (n + \tau - 1)\), where the ground state proton and neutron (\(n=0\)) are degenerate.
- The axial-to-rho meson mass ratio in the hard wall without bi-fundamentals is \(\tilde m_1 / M_1 = \gamma_{1,1} / \gamma_{0,1} = 1.593\), consistent with the empirical ratio \(m_A / m_\rho = 1.587\).
- The holographic PDF sets are compared with LHAPDF and CTEQ PDF sets in the range \(Q^2 < 10~\rm{GeV^2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD studies of deep inelastic scattering (such as those by Polchinski and collaborators, and by Hatta) to neutrino-nucleon scattering, enabling the separation of sea and valence quark distributions through charged-current interactions. The model builds on the Sakai-Sugimoto construction and the hard/soft wall approaches of Erlich, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2005) and Karch, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2006), and the results allow for comparison with global PDF fits from LHAPDF and CTEQ. The approach enables extraction of nonperturbative PDFs at large and small \(x\) in an intermediate \(Q^2\) regime where perturbative QCD methods are less reliable.
