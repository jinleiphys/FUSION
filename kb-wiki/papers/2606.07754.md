# Short-range correlated pair formation and nuclear shell structure

> Short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs - caused by brief, high-momentum interactions between two nucleons - are dominated by neutron-proton pairs with large relative and smaller center-of-mass momenta. However, the underlying dynamics that determines which nucleons form such pairs remains uncertain. Previous measurements showed that proton pairing probabilities increased strongly with nuclear asymmetry N/Z, but could not rule out an increase with nuclear mass A. We measured high-missing-momentum protons knocked out in electron scattering from selected nuclei with a range of shell configurations, A, and N/Z, including 9Be, 10B, 11B, 12C, 40Ca, 48Ca, 54Fe and 197Au. Unexpectedly, we found that while the pairing probability increased with A, the slope of the increase was much greater from Be to C and from 40Ca to Fe, than from Be to Au. This shows the importance of long-range nuclear shell structure on the probability of short-range nucleon pairing.

## Key claim

The probability of short-range correlated (SRC) pair formation depends more strongly on long-range nuclear shell structure than on nuclear mass A or neutron-to-proton asymmetry N/Z; while the pairing probability increased with A, the slope of the increase was much greater from Be to C and from 40Ca to Fe than from Be to Au, indicating that shell structure, not global nuclear properties, governs which nucleons form SRC pairs.

## Method

The experiment used a 10.5 GeV electron beam at Jefferson Lab Hall C to measure high-missing-momentum protons knocked out in (e,e'p) reactions from 9Be, 10B, 11B, 12C, 40Ca, 48Ca, 54Fe, and 197Au. Protons from SRC pairs were selected by requiring missing momentum between 375 and 700 MeV/c, Q2 ≥ 1.8 (GeV/c)2, xB ≥ 1.2, and θrq < 40°. Per-proton cross-section ratios to carbon were extracted after correcting for target impurities, radiative effects, and nuclear transparency, then integrated over missing momentum to obtain relative SRC-pair probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 9Be: 0.80 ± 0.04
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 10B: 0.92 ± 0.04
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 11B: 1.00 ± 0.04
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 40Ca: 0.93 ± 0.05
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 48Ca: 1.02 ± 0.06
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 54Fe: 1.17 ± 0.06
- Per-proton cross-section ratio to carbon for 197Au: 1.26 ± 0.10
- Adding eight 1f7/2 neutrons to 40Ca (making 48Ca) increased the number of high-initial-momentum protons by only 10 ± 2%
- Adding six 1f7/2 protons to 40Ca (making 54Fe) increased the number of high-initial-momentum protons by 49 ± 3% (ratio 1.49 ± 0.03)
- Central four-momentum transfer Q2 = 1.97 GeV2/c2; beam energy 10.5 GeV; missing momentum range 250–700 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends inclusive A(e,e') measurements by Egiyan (2002, 2006), Fomin (2012), and Schmookler (2019) that showed SRC fractions increase by about 10% from C to Pb, and inclusive 48Ca/40Ca measurements by Nguyen (2020) that found adding 40% more neutrons increased np pairs by only 17%. It also extends exclusive (e,e'p) measurements by Duer (2018) on C, Al, Fe, and Pb that could not separate A from N/Z effects. The results enable testing of theoretical models including AV18 many-body calculations, SRG-evolved momentum distributions by Tropiano (2024), LCA calculations by Ryckebusch (2019), and pair-counting models with relative and total angular momentum constraints (l=0, L=0). None of the available calculations described all the data well, with the most restrictive l=0, L=0 np-pair counting model coming closest except for 9Be.
