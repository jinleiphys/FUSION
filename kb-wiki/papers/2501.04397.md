# Understanding thermalization in a non-Abelian gauge theory in terms of its soft modes

> We measure the maximal Lyapunov exponent $λ_L$ of physical states in a SU(2) gauge theory consisting of soft momentum modes both in and out-of-thermal equilibrium conditions using ab-initio lattice techniques. We have implemented different algorithms to appropriately describe the dynamics of soft-modes for a wide range of temperatures and under non-equilibrium conditions. The non-equilibrium state has been realized starting from an over-occupied initial condition for low momentum soft gluons whereas the thermal state comprises of strongly interacting soft gluons at temperatures where these are well separated from the hard momentum modes. Spectra of positive Lyapunov exponents is observed in both these states, similar to a chaotic dynamical system. From the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate measured in terms of this spectrum, we estimate a typical time-scale of $\sim 0.50(3)$ fm/c to achieve thermalization at $T\sim 600$ MeV starting from the non-thermal state. We also measure, for the first time, the $λ_L$ for long wavelength critical modes of SU(2) using the out-of-time-ordered correlator of a classical $Z_2$ scalar field theory, which shares the same universal behavior with SU(2), near the deconfinement phase transition. The $λ_L$ is observed to maximize at the transition temperature.

# Understanding thermalization in a non-Abelian gauge theory in terms of its soft modes

## Key claim

The thermalization time of soft gluon modes in SU(2) gauge theory starting from a non-thermal classical attractor state is estimated to be approximately 0.50(3) fm/c at T ~ 600 MeV, based on the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate derived from the full spectrum of positive Lyapunov exponents. This estimate is consistent with a recently proposed scale-matching procedure by Pandey (2024) for infrared magnetic modes of QCD.

## Method

The authors measure the maximal Lyapunov exponent λ_L in SU(2) gauge theory using ab-initio lattice techniques in three distinct regimes: (1) a non-equilibrium state initialized with over-occupied soft gluon modes following a phase-space distribution with saturation scale Q_s = 1.5 GeV, evolved via classical Hamiltonian equations of motion; (2) thermal equilibrium states at high temperatures (T >> T_c) where hard, electric, and magnetic scales are well separated, simulated using an effective Langevin dynamics for magnetic modes with perturbative color conductivity; and (3) near the deconfinement phase transition temperature T_c, where the long-wavelength critical modes are modeled via a classical Z_2 scalar field theory that shares the same universality class as SU(2). The Lyapunov exponent is extracted from the exponential growth of a gauge-invariant distance measure between initially close trajectories for the SU(2) cases, and from the out-of-time-ordered correlator (OTOC) for the scalar field theory.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time: ~0.50(3) fm/c at T ~ 600 MeV, estimated from the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate
- Lyapunov exponent in the non-thermal state: λ_L = 0.66 Q_s, with Q_s = 1.5 GeV
- For thermal SU(2) at high temperatures (0.6-3 GeV): λ_L varies linearly with temperature with slope λ_L/T ~ 0.52
- For Z_2 scalar fields near T_c: λ_L varies as T^(6.8(7)) below T_c and as T^(-2.8(3)) in the range (1, 1.05) T_c
- Butterfly velocity at high temperatures: v_B = 0.8c, varying by only ~10% across the temperature range
- Diffusion coefficient for SU(2) at T >> T_c: varies as (T/T_c)^(-0.95(3))
- Diffusion coefficient for Z_2 scalar fields above T_c: varies as (T/T_c)^(-3.7(2))
- Lattice spacing for scalar field calculations: a.T_c = 9.37
- Deconfinement phase transition temperature for SU(2): T_c ~ 300 MeV, from Fingberg (1992)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chaos in non-Abelian gauge theories by Savvidy (1983), Muller (1992), and Gong (1994), but with a focus on phase space mixing and entropy maximization. The non-equilibrium state studied is the classical attractor regime identified by Berges (2013), and the thermalization time estimate is consistent with the scale-matching procedure recently proposed by Pandey (2024) for QCD infrared magnetic modes. The observation that λ_L maximizes at the deconfinement phase transition temperature parallels findings in spin systems by Bilitewski (2018, 2021) and in two-dimensional scalar field theories by Schuckert (2019). The results enable quantitative estimates of local thermal equilibration timescales relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology at temperatures 450-600 MeV, comparable to initial fireball temperatures measured experimentally by the STAR collaboration (2024).
