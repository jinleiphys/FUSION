# Towards solar measurements of nuclear reaction rates

> Nuclear reaction rates are a fundamental yet uncertain ingredient in stellar evolution models. The astrophysical S-factor pertaining to the initial reaction in the proton-proton chain is uncertain at the 1% level, which contributes a systematic but generally unpropagated error of similar order in the theoretical ages of stars. In this work, we study the prospect of improving the measurement of this and other reaction rates in the pp chain and CNO cycle using helioseismology and solar neutrinos. We show that when other aspects of the solar model are improved, then it shall be possible using current solar data to improve the precision of this measurement by nearly an order of magnitude, and hence the corresponding uncertainty on the ages of low-mass stars by a similar amount.

## Key claim

When other aspects of the solar model are improved (specifically the remaining problems with opacities and atmospheric abundances), it shall be possible using current helioseismic and neutrino data to improve the precision of the pp reaction rate measurement by nearly an order of magnitude, and hence the corresponding uncertainty on the ages of low-mass stars by a similar amount.

## Method

The authors constructed standard solar models using the Aarhus Stellar Evolution Code (ASTEC), calibrating the convective mixing length parameter, initial helium abundance, and initial metallicity to match solar radius, luminosity, and surface metallicity at the solar age. They then varied five nuclear reaction rates (pp, 3He-3He, 3He-alpha, 7Be-p, and 14N-p) in isolation by factors of their standard deviations, recalibrated new solar models for each value, and compared the resulting frequency separation ratios from BiSON helioseismic data and neutrino fluxes from the literature to determine achievable constraints.

## Key numbers

* The astrophysical S-factor for the pp reaction is 4.01 x 10^-22 keV b with a current uncertainty of 1.0% (0.04 x 10^-22 keV b)
* Helioseismology alone could reduce the pp S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 30, the 3He-3He S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 7, and the 3He-alpha S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 16
* Neutrino flux measurements could reduce the pp S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 1.3, the 3He-alpha S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 2.3, and the 7Be-p S-factor uncertainty by a factor of 3.6
* A 1-sigma increase to the pp rate causes a 0.5% change to the central solar density
* The 8B neutrino flux shows tension with the standard solar model at 4.1 sigma (measured 5.16 x 10^-4 versus SSM 5.61 x 10^-4 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Adelberger (2011) who provided the current theoretical estimates of solar nuclear reaction rates, and of Antia (2000) who constrained the pp rate to 6% using 6 months of helioseismic data. The authors aim to show that using the full BiSON dataset combined with solar neutrino flux measurements from experiments such as Borexino and Super-Kamiokande, the constraint can be improved to 0.14%. This enables more precise stellar ages for galactic archaeology and potentially drives down uncertainties in electroweak theory parameters from chiral effective field theory as studied by Acharya (2016) and Carlsson (2016).
