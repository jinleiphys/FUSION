# Gluon Entanglement Entropy inside a Nucleon: A Toy Model

> We construct a toy model of a nucleon, in which three static quarks interact via a SU(3) gauge field on a planar honeycomb lattice. The dynamics of the gauge field is described by the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian, truncated to the lowest three SU(3) irreducible representations. We show that the internal structure of the toy nucleon reflects salient features of the physical nucleon state. We then find the entanglement entropy of the gauge field within the nucleon state and compute its time evolution after a quench, in which all three valence quarks are suddenly removed. We show that the entanglement entropy in the final state is dominated by the dynamically generated contribution rather than the initial state entropy.

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy of the gauge field inside the unperturbed toy nucleon accounts for only a small fraction of the final-state entropy after the sudden removal of the three valence quarks; the final entropy is dominated by dynamically generated statistical entropy from the far-out-of-equilibrium evolution, not by the initial-state gluon entanglement entropy.

## Method

The authors construct a toy nucleon with three static SU(3) fundamental color charges at the corners of a planar triangular lattice, using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian truncated to the lowest three irreducible representations (singlet, triplet, and antitriplet). They compute the ground state of the gauge field in the presence of the three static quarks, then perform a quench by suddenly setting all external links to the singlet state (removing the valence quarks) and evolve the resulting state in the vacuum-sector eigenbasis. The entanglement entropy of the gauge field is computed for various subregions of the lattice, with vacuum subtraction, at both the initial time and after time evolution.

## Key numbers

* For the N=4 lattice at g²=0.3, the vacuum-subtracted entanglement entropy ΔS_A(0) for subregions of size N_A=1,3,5,7 is 0.499, 0.535, 0.479, and 0.308 respectively; at t=20 the values rise to 0.861, 2.184, 2.607, and 1.642.
* For the N=4 lattice at g²=0.5, ΔS_A(0) for N_A=1,3,5,7 is 0.558, 0.692, 0.727, and 0.642; at t=20 the values are 1.007, 2.770, 3.732, and 2.554.
* The vacuum entanglement entropy S_A^vac for the N=4 lattice at g²=0.3 ranges from 0.064 (N_A=1) to 0.169 (N_A=7); at g²=0.5 it ranges from 0.026 (N_A=1) to 0.050 (N_A=7).
* For the N=5 lattice at g²=0.5, the vacuum-subtracted energy density per plaquette is 0.714 (body-centered), 0.561 (edge-centered), and 0.690 (off-center); the planar pressure is 0.083, 0.138, and 0.116 respectively.
* For the N=5 lattice at g²=0.3, the vacuum-subtracted energy density per plaquette is 0.425 (body-centered), 0.619 (edge-centered), and 0.493 (off-center); the planar pressure is 0.166, 0.334, and 0.143 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the line of inquiry into the origin of coarse-grained entropy in high-energy reactions, particularly the proposal by Kharzeev (2017) that final-state entropy in deep inelastic scattering is proportional to the gluon distribution function and reflects gluon entanglement entropy inside the proton. The toy model uses a lattice configuration similar to that of Chen (2026) for computing string tension with three static color charges. The results challenge the interpretation of entanglement entropy measurements from inelastic scattering, suggesting that such measurements are contaminated by statistical entropy from the out-of-equilibrium evolution, and raise the open question of whether nondestructive experimental probes can reliably determine ground-state entanglement entropy of partons.
