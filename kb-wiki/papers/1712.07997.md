# D mesons in strongly magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter

> The medium modifications of the open charm mesons ($D$ and $\bar D$) are studied in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic fields, using a chiral effective model. The mass modifications of these mesons in the effective hadronic model, arise due to their interactions with the protons, neutrons and the scalar mesons (non-strange isoscalar $σ$, strange isoscalar, $ζ$ and non-strange isovector, $δ$), in the magnetized nuclear matter. In the presence of magnetic field, for the charged baryon, i.e., the proton, the number density as well as the scalar density have contributions due to the summation over the Landau energy levels. For a given value of the baryon density, $ρ_B$, and isospin asymmetry, the scalar fields are solved self consistently from their coupled equations of motion. The modifications of the masses of the $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons are calculated, from the medium modifications of the scalar fields and the nucleons. The effects of the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleons on the masses of the open charm mesons are also investigated in the present work. The effects of isospin asymmetry as well as of the anomalous magnetic moments are observed to be prominent at high densities for large values of magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The mass modifications of D and anti-D mesons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are studied under strong magnetic fields using a chiral effective model, with the hedge that the effects of isospin asymmetry and anomalous magnetic moments are prominent only at high baryon densities and large magnetic fields, and that mixing between pseudoscalar and vector mesons is not considered as it lies outside the mean-field approximation used.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled equations of motion for the scalar meson fields (sigma, zeta, delta) self-consistently in the mean-field approximation for given baryon density and isospin asymmetry, incorporating Landau level contributions to the proton number density and scalar density due to the external magnetic field. The in-medium masses of D and anti-D mesons are then calculated from their dispersion relations using self-energies derived from interactions with nucleons and scalar mesons, with charged D mesons receiving an additional positive mass shift from Landau quantization. The effects of anomalous magnetic moments of protons and neutrons are included via tensorial electromagnetic interactions and compared to calculations without them.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field values considered: eB = 2 m_pi^2, 4 m_pi^2, 6 m_pi^2, and 8 m_pi^2, where m_pi is the pion mass
- Magnetic fields in heavy ion collisions estimated as eB ~ 2 m_pi^2 (corresponding to ~6 x 10^18 Gauss) at RHIC, BNL, and eB ~ 15 m_pi^2 at LHC, CERN
- Anomalous magnetic moment parameters: kappa_p = 3.5856 for proton, kappa_n = -3.8263 for neutron
- Nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 used as the unit for baryon density rho_B
- Charged D meson effective mass includes Landau level contribution: m^{eff}_{D^pm} = sqrt( (m*_{D^pm})^2 + |eB| )

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D and anti-D mesons in nuclear matter using the chiral SU(4) model (Amarindam PRC, Amarv Dmeson T PRC, Amarvepja) to include strong magnetic fields, building on prior investigations of kaons and antikaons in asymmetric matter (Isoamss, Isoamss1, Isoamss2). It complements QCD sum rule studies of D mesons in magnetic fields (Gubler, Machado 1, Machado 2) and semiclassical approaches, while enabling future work on charmonium decay widths to D anti-D pairs in magnetized matter and applications to neutron star interiors where magnetic fields can reach 10^15-10^16 Gauss.
