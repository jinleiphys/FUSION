# Tagged jets and jet reconstruction as a probe of QGP induced partonic energy loss

> Recent experimental advances at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the large center-of-mass energies available to the heavy-ion program at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) will enable strongly interacting matter at high temperatures and densities, that is, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), to be probed in unprecedented ways. Among these exciting new probes are fully-reconstructed inclusive jets and the away-side hadron showers associated with a weakly or electromagnetically interacting boson, or, tagged jets. Full jet reconstruction provides an experimental window into the mechanisms of quark and gluon dynamics in the QGP which is not accessible via leading particles and leading particle correlations. Theoretical advances in this growing field can help resolve some of the most controversial points in heavy ion physics today. I here discuss the power of jets to reveal the spectrum of induced radiation, thereby shedding light on the applicability of the commonly used energy loss formalisms and present results on the production and subsequent suppression of high energy jets tagged with Z bosons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) parton energy loss approach.

# Tagged jets and jet reconstruction as a probe of QGP induced partonic energy loss

## Key claim
Jet reconstruction and Z-tagged jets can reveal the angular distribution of medium-induced bremsstrahlung and discriminate between partonic energy loss formalisms, but next-to-leading order (NLO) production processes significantly compromise the tagging power of electroweak bosons, making experimental extraction of the initial associated jet energy difficult.

## Method
The author uses the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) partonic energy loss formalism to compute jet suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies and in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. NLO calculations for Z-tagged jet production are performed using the Monte Carlo for FeMtobarn processes (MCFM) code developed by Campbell and Ellis. The jet reconstruction cone radius R and particle tower energy selection are varied to probe the angular distribution of medium-induced bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon with impact parameter b = 3 fm: predictions for jet R_AA as a function of jet reconstruction cone size R are presented using the GLV formalism.
- Z0/gamma* tagging window: 92.5 < p_T < 112.5 GeV for the tagging boson in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 4 TeV.
- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 4 TeV/nucleon pair: NLO p_T differential cross section per nucleon pair for jets tagged with Z/gamma* -> mu+ mu- is shown for two jet cone radii, R = 0.2 and R = 0.8.
- At LO, the jet p_T distribution is entirely inside the tagging window 92.5 - 112.5 GeV; at NLO this correspondence is largely washed away due to the possibility of an extra jet in the final state.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet quenching beyond leading particle suppression measurements at RHIC (Gyulassy 2003, Jacobs 2004, Adler 2006), which alone cannot discriminate between energy loss formalisms (Bass 2008). The author builds on prior work on jet shapes (Vitev 2008, Vitev 2009) and Z-tagged jets (Neufeld 2010, Srivastava 2002) using the GLV approach (Gyulassy 2000). The results enable future experimental measurements at the LHC and RHIC upgrades to quantitatively extract the jet quenching properties of the QGP and probe the medium response to hard partons (Neufeld 2009), provided NLO smearing effects are brought under sufficient theoretical control.
