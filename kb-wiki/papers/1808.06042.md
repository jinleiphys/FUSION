# Quark Cluster Model Equations for $ββ$ Decay

> In a separate paper we have discussed the possibility that six quark clusters can affect the rate of double-beta decay. In this article we develop the formalism needed in the evaluation of the energy of all six-quark cluster configurations, which can arise in a harmonic oscillator basis up to $2 \hbar ω$ excitations. The symmetries that were found useful for this purpose were the combined spin color symmetry $SU_{cs}(6)$, the orbital symmetry $SU_{o}(6)$ and the isospin symmetry $SU_I(2)$.

## Key claim

The paper develops the formal group-theoretic framework needed to evaluate the energy of six-quark cluster configurations in a harmonic oscillator basis up to \(2\hbar\omega\) excitations, as a prerequisite for calculating whether such clusters can affect double-beta decay rates. The authors explicitly note that the six-quark cluster must be a color singlet, but acknowledge this is a restriction because the entire nucleus could be a color singlet even if the six quarks and the remaining \(A-2\) system are individually non-singlet.

## Method

The authors construct six-quark basis states as products of spatial, color, spin, and isospin components, using the combined spin-color symmetry \(SU_{cs}(6)\), orbital symmetry \(SU_o(6)\), and isospin symmetry \(SU_I(2)\). They enumerate all allowed six-quark configurations at \(0\hbar\omega\) (all quarks in \(0s\)), one-particle \(2\hbar\omega\) excitations (\(0s^5\) plus a \(1s0d\) quark), and two-particle \(2\hbar\omega\) excitations (\(0s^4\) plus \(0p^2\)). They then derive the two-body matrix elements for the quark-quark interaction by decoupling the six-quark system into a four-quark and a two-quark subsystem using coefficients of fractional parentage and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the relevant symmetry groups.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier discussion of six-quark clusters in double-beta decay by providing the explicit group-theoretical machinery needed to compute cluster energies. It builds on the quark-cluster approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay originally proposed by Vergados (1985), where the nuclear hard-core suppression of conventional nuclear matrix elements can be circumvented if the interacting quarks reside in the same hadron. The formalism enables future calculations of the probability of finding six-quark clusters in nuclei and their contribution to neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by heavy neutrinos or other exotic particles.
