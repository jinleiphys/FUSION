# Three-body resonant states of $^9_Λ$Be with $α+α+Λ$ cluster model

> We study the structure of $^9_Λ$Be in the framework of three body $α+α+Λ$ cluster model using YNG-NF interaction with the Gaussian expansion method. Employing the complex scaling method, we obtain the energies of bound states as well as energies and decay widths of the resonant states. By analyzing our wave functions of bound states and resonant states, we confirm three analogue states of $^9_Λ$Be pointed out by Band${\rm \bar{o}}$ and Motoba {\it et al.} \cite{motoba1983,motoba1985,bando1983}, $^8$Be analogue states, $^9_Λ$Be genuine states and $^9$Be analogue states. The new states of $^9_Λ$Be are also obtained at a high energy region with broader decay widths.

## Key claim

The authors confirm the three categories of states in \(^9_\Lambda\)Be (\({}^{8}\)Be analogue, genuine hypernuclear, and \({}^{9}\)Be analogue) previously identified by Bandō and Motoba et al. (1983, 1985), but they find that some states previously identified as resonances using bound-state approximation are not stable resonances when treated with the correct boundary condition via complex scaling; new resonant states are also found 10–20 MeV above the \(\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) threshold.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body \(\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method with two sets of Jacobian coordinates, employing a modified YNG-NF \(\Lambda\)N interaction (with adjusted odd-state strength and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 0.963\) fm\(^{-1}\)) and an \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) interaction that reproduces the observed \({}^{8}\)Be ground state and scattering phase shifts. Bound and resonant states are obtained via the complex scaling method, and the wave functions are analyzed by computing overlaps with SU(3) shell-model configurations to classify the states.

## Key numbers

- Ground state \(0_1^+\): \(\Lambda\)-separation energy \(B_\Lambda = 6.74\) MeV, in agreement with observed \(6.71 \pm 0.04\) MeV.
- \(2_1^+\) state: excitation energy \(E_{\text{ex}} = 2.83\) MeV, compared with observed \(3.079 \pm 0.040\) MeV; bound by 6.65 MeV relative to \(\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) threshold.
- \(4_1^+\) resonance: \(E_r = 3.2\) MeV above threshold, width \(\Gamma = 0.78\) MeV.
- \(1_1^-\) resonance (genuine hypernuclear): \(E_r = 0.1\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 2.5\) MeV.
- \(5_1^-\) resonance (genuine hypernuclear): \(E_r = 10.6\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 14.6\) MeV.
- \(3_1^-\) resonance (\({}^{9}\)Be analogue): \(E_r = 8.0\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 6.1\) MeV.
- \(4_1^-\) resonance (\({}^{9}\)Be analogue): \(E_r = 10.0\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 10.4\) MeV.
- Squared overlap of the \(1_I^-\) (artificially bound) state with the SU(3) \((5,0)_1\) configuration: 0.45; with \((3,1)_1\): 0.01.
- Squared overlap of the \(1_{II}^-\) (artificially bound) state with the SU(3) \((3,1)_1\) configuration: 0.39; with \((5,0)_1\): 0.01.
- Squared overlap of the \(0_1^+\), \(2_1^+\), \(4_1^+\) states with harmonic oscillator \((4J,00)_J\) wave functions: 37%, 37%, 39% respectively.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Bandō and Motoba et al. (1983, 1985) on \(^9_\Lambda\)Be within the \(\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) cluster model, which used bound-state approximation and a restricted single Jacobian coordinate channel. By employing the complex scaling method with a full two-channel basis, the authors obtain resonant energies and widths with correct boundary conditions, revealing that some states previously identified as resonances (e.g., a \(3^-\) state in the genuine band, and \(1^-\) and \(2^-\) states in the \({}^{9}\)Be analogue band) dissolve into continuum when the \({}^{8}\)Be+\(\Lambda\) channel is included. The new high-energy states (e.g., \(4_2^+\), \(1_2^-\), \(2_1^-\), \(2_2^+\), \(3_2^-\), \(4_2^-\), \(4_3^+\)) found 10–20 MeV above the three-body threshold have never been reported before and open the possibility for experimental searches in that energy region.
