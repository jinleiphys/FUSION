# Anomalous $η/η^\prime$ Decays: The Triangle and Box Anomalies

> We examine the decay modes $η/\etp\ra π^+ π^- γ$ within the context of the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model. Using numerical information derived in previous fits to $VPγ$ and $Ve^+e^-$ decay modes in isolation and the $ρ$ lineshape determined in a previous fit to the pion form factor, we show that all aspects of these decays can be predicted with fair accuracy. Freeing some parameters does not improve the picture. This is interpreted as a strong evidence in favor of the box anomaly in the $η/\etp$ decays, which occurs at precisely the level expected. We also construct the set of equations defining the amplitudes for $η/\etp\ra π^+ π^- γ$ and $ η/\etp \ra \ggam $ at the chiral limit, as predicted from the anomalous HLS Lagrangian appropriately broken. This provides a set of four equations depending on only one parameter, instead of three for the traditional set. This is also shown to match the (two--angle, two--decay--constant) $η-\etp$ mixing scheme recently proposed and is also fairly well fulfilled by the data. The information returned from fits also matches expectations from previously published fits to the $VPγ$ decay modes in isolation.

## Key claim

The paper claims strong evidence for the box anomaly in \(\eta/\eta^\prime \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma\) decays, but only under the condition that the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model with a specific extended BKY symmetry-breaking scheme is used; the box anomaly occurs "at precisely the level expected" when all parameters are fixed from independent data sets, and freeing parameters does not improve the fit.

## Method

The authors used the anomalous HLS Lagrangian, including the \(VVP\) (vector-vector-pseudoscalar) and \(APPP\) (axial-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar) terms from the Fujiwara et al. (FKTUY) construction, and applied the extended BKY breaking scheme with SU(3) and Nonet symmetry breaking to derive amplitudes for \(\eta/\eta^\prime \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma\) and \(\eta/\eta^\prime \to \gamma\gamma\) at the chiral limit. They then compared predicted partial widths and dipion invariant-mass spectra to published experimental data, using numerical parameters previously fixed from fits to \(VP\gamma\) decays, \(Ve^+e^-\) decays, and the pion form factor, without allowing free parameters in the main prediction.

## Key numbers

- The predicted partial width for \(\eta \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma\) is consistent with the PDG (2002) value, known to about 10% accuracy.
- The predicted partial width for \(\eta^\prime \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma\) is consistent with the PDG (2002) value, known to about 10% accuracy.
- The Nonet symmetry breaking parameter \(x\) was extracted from \(VP\gamma\) data as \(x = 0.90 \pm 0.02\).
- The mixing angle \(\theta_P\) is derived as \(\theta_P = -10.32^\circ \pm 0.20^\circ\) when requiring the constraint \(\tan\theta_P = \sqrt{2} (1-z)/(2+z) \, x\) to hold exactly, with \(z = [f_K/f_\pi]^2\).
- The ratio of VMD to contact term contributions at the chiral limit is predicted as \(-3 : 1\) from the HLS anomalous Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the broken HLS Model, previously applied successfully to \(VP\gamma\) and \(Ve^+e^-\) decays (Benayoun et al., 1999; 2000) and to the pion form factor, into the \(\eta/\eta^\prime \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma\) sector. It contradicts the traditional one-angle mixing scheme of Chanowitz (1981) and Gilman (1987) for the box anomaly amplitudes, and instead supports the two-angle, two-decay-constant mixing scheme from Extended Chiral Perturbation Theory (Kaiser, 1998; Feldmann, 1998). The results enable a consistent description of triangle and box anomalies with only one free parameter, and provide a framework for future high-precision tests of the box anomaly in \(\eta/\eta^\prime\) decays.
