# Enhanced effects of the Lorentz invariance and Einstein equivalence principle violation in 229Th nuclear transition

> The Lorentz invariance and Einstein equivalence principle violating effects in the narrow 7.8 eV transition in 229Th nucleus may be 100 000 times larger than in atoms. This transition may be investigated using high precision laser spectroscopy methods, has a very small width, and suppressed systematic effects. Similar values of the effects are expected in 73 eV 235U nuclear transition which is coming within the reach of the laser spectroscopy. Mossbauer transitions give another possibility.

# Enhanced effects of the Lorentz invariance and Einstein equivalence principle violation in 229Th nuclear transition

## Key claim

The Lorentz invariance and Einstein equivalence principle violating effects in the 7.8 eV nuclear transition of 229Th may be 10^5 times larger than in atomic transitions, but this enhancement depends on the difference in kinetic energy between the excited and ground states being approximately 0.5 MeV, which arises from the specific single-particle structure of the deformed nucleus.

## Method

The author calculates the difference in kinetic energy between the ground state (5/2+[633]) and the 7.8 eV excited state (3/2+[631]) of 229Th using a deformed nuclear shell model with oscillator potential and spin-orbit coupling. The kinetic energy difference is derived from the relation between total energy, kinetic energy, potential energy, and spin-orbit energy, using the known spin-orbit coupling constant C = -0.85 MeV and the many-body correction factor of 1.2 C. The enhancement factor for Lorentz violation is then obtained by comparing this nuclear kinetic energy difference (∼0.5 MeV) to typical atomic kinetic energy differences (∼few eV).

## Key numbers

- The 229Th transition energy is ω = 7.8(5) eV, with an estimated natural width of ∼10^-3 to 10^-4 Hz
- The spin-orbit coupling constant in 229Th is C = -0.85 MeV
- The difference in kinetic energy between the excited and ground states is estimated as 0.5 MeV
- The Lorentz invariance violating effects in 229Th may be 10^5 times larger than in atoms
- A similar kinetic energy difference of 0.1 - 1 MeV is expected in the 73 eV 235U nuclear transition

## Context

This work extends the search for Lorentz invariance violation from atomic spectroscopy, as performed by Pruttivarasin (2014), Hohensee (2013), and Dzuba (2015), to nuclear transitions where the kinetic energy differences are orders of magnitude larger. The 229Th transition has been proposed for nuclear clock applications by Campbell, Rellergert, Peik (2015) and others, and the enhancement of fundamental constant variation in this transition was previously noted by Flambaum and collaborators. The results enable laboratory searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model using laser spectroscopy of nuclear transitions, with 235U (73 eV transition) becoming accessible soon and Mossbauer transitions offering an alternative but less precise approach.
