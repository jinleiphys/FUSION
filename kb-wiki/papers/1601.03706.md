# Sensitivity of the COSY Dibaryon Candidate to np Elastic Scattering Measurements

> The case for a dibaryon resonance, appearing in np scattering, has support from a WASA-at-COSY measurement of the polarization quantity A_y over a center-of-mass energy region suggested by structures seen earlier in two-pion production experiments. Here we compare fits with and without an associated pole in order to clarify the impact of these COSY data. We then consider what further np scattering measurements would most clearly distinguish between the pole and non-pole fit results.

## Key claim

The dibaryon candidate near 2.38 GeV in np scattering is supported by WASA-at-COSY polarization data, but the need for a pole in the coupled \(^3D_3\)-\(^3G_3\) partial waves would gain confidence if lower-energy data errors could be reduced and if additional measurements of specific two-spin observables distinguish between pole and non-pole fits.

## Method

The authors performed partial wave analyses (PWA) of the full np elastic scattering database using the GW SAID framework, generating fits both with and without a pole in the coupled \(^3D_3\)-\(^3G_3\) partial waves. They compared these fits to the COSY \(A_y\) data at neutron kinetic energies from 1.108 to 1.197 GeV (CM energies 2.367 to 2.403 GeV) and then examined predictions for a range of spin-dependent observables to identify which measurements would most clearly discriminate between the pole and non-pole solutions.

## Key numbers

- Pole location in the coupled \(^3D_3\)-\(^3G_3\) partial waves: [(2380 \(\pm\) 10) - \(i(40 \pm 5)\)] MeV, corresponding to a resonance mass near 2.38 GeV with width about 70 MeV.
- COSY experiment measured 7 angular distributions for \(A_y\) in np scattering with neutron kinetic energies from 1.108 to 1.197 GeV, corresponding to CM energies between 2.367 and 2.403 GeV.
- Previous LAMPF measurements achieved statistical uncertainties of order \(\Delta(RP) \sim 0.05\), \(\Delta(A) \sim 0.05\), \(\Delta(DT) \sim 0.03\), and \(\Delta(RPT) \sim 0.04\).
- Improved measurements of \(\Delta\sigma_L\) with uncertainties comparable to LAMPF measurements of order \(\Delta\sigma_L \sim 10\%\) would greatly improve the comparison between pole and non-pole fits.

## Context

This work extends the GW SAID partial wave analysis (SP07) to include the WASA-at-COSY \(A_y\) data (WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, 2014, 2015) and the dibaryon candidate previously seen in two-pion production experiments. The analysis builds on earlier dibaryon searches from the ZGS era (Auer, 1970s) and the difficulties distinguishing true and pseudo-resonances (pseudo-resonance literature). The results enable future experimental programs to test the dibaryon interpretation by measuring two-spin observables (RP, A, DT, RPT) or improved \(\Delta\sigma_L\) measurements at energies near 2.38 GeV, with precision comparable to earlier LAMPF measurements.
