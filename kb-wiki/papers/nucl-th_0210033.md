# Pairing in nuclear systems: from neutron stars to finite nuclei

> We discuss several pairing-related phenomena in nuclear systems, ranging from superfluidity in neutron stars to the gradual breaking of pairs in finite nuclei. We focus on the links between many-body pairing as it evolves from the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction and the eventual experimental and theoretical manifestations of superfluidity in infinite nuclear matter and of pairing in finite nuclei. We analyse the nature of pair correlations in nuclei and their potential impact on nuclear structure experiments. We also describe recent experimental evidence that points to a relation between pairing and phase transitions (or transformations) in finite nuclear systems. Finally, we discuss recent investigations of ground-state properties of random two-body interactions where pairing plays little role although the interactions yield interesting nuclear properties such as 0+ ground states in even-even nuclei.

## Key claim

This review argues that pairing correlations in nuclear systems, from neutron stars to finite nuclei, can be traced back to the attractive components of the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the authors note that the density ranges in which pairing gaps occur remain uncertain and that the possibility for hyperon pairing is an entirely open issue.

## Method

The authors review the connection between the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, expressed in terms of partial waves and phase shifts from scattering experiments, and the resulting pairing gaps in infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars. They then examine spectroscopic evidence for pairing in finite nuclei, including neutron-proton pairing near the N=Z line, pair transfer reactions, and the role of random two-body interactions. Finally, they analyze thermodynamic signatures of pairing from level density measurements in rare-earth nuclei, comparing nuclear pairing phenomena to those in ultrasmall metallic grains.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetrical nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- In the low-density outer part of a neutron star, neutron superfluidity is expected at densities rho ~ rho_0/10 in the ^1S_0 channel
- The ^1S_0 potential is attractive for momenta k <= 1.74 fm^{-1} (or interparticle distances r >= 0.6 fm)
- The critical temperature for pair breaking in finite nuclei is measured to be T_c ~ 0.5 MeV/k_B (Schiller 2001)
- Neutron, proton, and hyperon superfluidity gaps are a few MeV or less
- The repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon interaction has a characteristic radius ~ 0.5-1 fm
- The ^3P_2 phase shift is positive for energies up to 1 GeV and is the most attractive T=1 phase shift at energies above about 160 MeV

## Context

This review extends the long history of pairing studies in nuclear matter that predate the 1967 discovery of pulsars (Hewish 1968, Gold 1968), building on the BCS theory (Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer 1957) and its first nuclear applications (Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines 1958; Belyaev 1959; Migdal 1959). The work connects recent experimental developments from X-ray satellites (Einstein, EXOSAT, ROSAT, ASCA, Chandra) studying neutron star thermal emission with terrestrial radioactive-beam facilities exploring neutron-rich nuclei. It enables further theoretical investigations of neutron star cooling mechanisms, the nature of neutron-proton pairing in N=Z nuclei, and the interpretation of level density measurements that show pair breaking occurs over several MeV of excitation energy rather than at a sharp critical point.
