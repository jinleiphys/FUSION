# Cross Sections of Neutron Reactions $(n,p)$, $(n, α)$, $(n, 2n)$ on Isotopes of Dysprosium, Erbium and Ytterbium at $\sim$ 14 MeV Neutron Energy

> The cross sections of the nuclear reactions induced by neutrons at $E_n$= 14.6 MeV on the isotopes of Dy, Er, Yb with emission of neutrons, proton and alpha-particle are studied by the use of new experimental data and different theoretical approaches. New and improved experimental data are measured by the neutron-activation technique. The experimental and evaluated data from EXFOR, TENDL, ENDF libraries are compared with different systematics and calculations by codes of EMPIRE~3.0 and TALYS~1.2. Contribution of pre-equilibrium decay is discussed. Different systematics for estimations of the cross-sections of considered nuclear reactions are tested.

# Cross Sections of Neutron Reactions (n,p), (n,α), (n,2n) on Isotopes of Dysprosium, Erbium and Ytterbium at ∼ 14 MeV Neutron Energy

## Key claim
The measured cross sections for (n,2n), (n,p), and (n,α) reactions on isotopes of Dy, Er, and Yb at 14.6 ± 0.2 MeV generally agree with existing experimental data, but the EMPIRE 3.0 code with pre-equilibrium processes included agrees better with experiment than TALYS 1.2 with default parameters, and among systematics the expressions from Broeders (2006) and Belgya (2005) are most consistent with measured values.

## Method
Cross sections were measured using the neutron-activation technique with a NG-300 neutron generator producing neutrons at En = 14.6 ± 0.2 MeV at Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv. Theoretical calculations were performed using the EMPIRE 3.0 code (with and without pre-equilibrium processes, using the generalized superfluid model for nuclear level density and the Koning-Delaroche global optical potential) and the TALYS 1.2 code with default parameters. Experimental results were compared with data from the EXFOR, ENDF/B-VII, TENDL-2010, and JENDL-4.0 libraries, as well as with various systematics.

## Key numbers
- Neutron energy: En = 14.6 ± 0.2 MeV
- Measured (n,2n) cross section on 158Dy (N=92) coincides with available experimental data within uncertainties
- Measured (n,2n) cross section on 156Dy (N=90) is lower than previous measurements but coincides with the TENDL-2010 evaluated value
- Inclusion of pre-equilibrium processes in EMPIRE 3.0 increases (n,p) cross sections by approximately a factor of five
- Measured (n,2n) cross section on 176Yb (N=106) is placed higher than data for 168Yb and 170Yb

## Context
This work extends measurements of neutron-induced reaction cross sections on rare-earth isotopes at 14 MeV, addressing disagreements noted between existing experimental data, evaluated data, systematics, and code calculations (Koning 2009). The results enable improved nuclear data for fusion reactor design and nuclear power plant applications (Forrest 2006, Koning 2009). The study tests systematics from earlier works including Lu and Wei (1971), Konobeyev (1999), Broeders (2006), Belgya (2005), Kadenko (2008), and Konobeyev (2009), finding that Broeders (2006) and Belgya (2005) give the best agreement with measured cross sections.
