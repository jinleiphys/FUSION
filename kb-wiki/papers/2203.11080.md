# Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with a fractal inspired coupling

> The Nambu--Jona-Lasino model is modified by the inclusion of a running-coupling that was obtained by a fractal approach to Quantum Chromodynamics. The coupling follows a $q$-exponential function and, in the context of high energy collisions, explains the origin of the Tsallis non-extensive statistics distributions. The parameter $q$ is completely determined in terms of the number of colours and the number of quark flavours. We study several aspects of the extended model and compare our results to the standard NJL model, where a constant coupling is used in combination with a sharp cutoff to regularize the gap equation. We show that the modified coupling regularizes the model in a smooth cutoff fashion and reproduces the pion mass and decay constant, providing an almost identical Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation as in the standard NJL model. In both models the relation is satisfied in similar cutoff scales. An important novelty of this work is the physical explanation, in terms of the fractal QCD vacuum, for a running coupling that renormalizes the quark condensate.

## Key claim

The paper modifies the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by replacing the constant coupling with a running coupling derived from a fractal approach to Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), showing that this running coupling regularizes the model in a smooth cutoff fashion and reproduces the pion mass and decay constant, but only when the entropic index \(q\) is set to the theoretically determined value \(q = 1 + 3/29 \approx 1.103\) (for \(N_c = 3, N_f = 2\)) and the cutoff parameters are adjusted accordingly.

## Method

The authors replaced the constant coupling \(G\) in the standard NJL gap equation with a \(q\)-exponential running coupling \(G_{\rm eff}(p) = G_q \cdot [1 + (q-1)\sqrt{p^2+m^2}/\lambda]^{-1/(q-1)}\), derived from the thermofractal structure of Yang-Mills fields. They solved the resulting gap equation for the dynamical quark mass \(m\) and quark condensate \(\langle \bar\psi \psi \rangle\), and computed the pion mass and decay constant from the pole of the T-matrix and the standard NJL formulas, respectively, using the fractal running coupling in the integrands. The parameters \(\lambda\) and \(G_q\) were tuned so that the critical coupling matched that of the standard NJL model and the physical condensate value was reproduced.

## Key numbers

* The entropic index is \(q = 1 + 3/29 \approx 1.103\) for \(N_c = 3, N_f = 2\), and \(q = 1 + 1/7 \approx 1.143\) for \(N_c = 3, N_f = 6\).
* The physical quark condensate is \(\langle \bar\psi \psi \rangle_{\rm Phys} = -(0.255\ {\rm GeV})^3\), obtained with pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\ {\rm MeV}\), decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\ {\rm MeV}\), and current quark mass \(m_0 = 5\ {\rm MeV}\).
* In the standard NJL model with \(\Lambda = 0.65\ {\rm GeV}\), the physical coupling is \(G_{\rm Phys} = 5.22\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\); in the fractal NJL model with \(q = 1.103\), the parameters that reproduce the same condensate are \(\lambda = 0.429\ {\rm GeV}\), \(G_q = 3.703\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\), and \(\Lambda = 0.719\ {\rm GeV}\).
* The critical coupling for the fractal NJL model is \(G_{q,{\rm crit}} = 3.185\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\), matching the standard NJL critical value.
* The integral in the gap equation converges in the UV only if \(q < 3/2\); the asymptotic condensate for large coupling tends to zero for \(q < 4/3\) and to infinity for \(4/3 < q < 3/2\), with a constant limit at \(q = 4/3\): \(\langle \bar\psi \psi \rangle \to -27 N_c \lambda^3 / (4\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) by incorporating a running coupling motivated by the thermofractal approach to QCD (Deppman, 2016; Deppman, 2018), which explains the origin of Tsallis non-extensive statistics in high-energy collisions. The fractal NJL model provides a physical interpretation for the running coupling that renormalizes the quark condensate, in contrast to previous qNJL models (Rozynek, 2015; Zhao, 2020; Mitra, 2017; Islam, 2023) that introduced non-extensivity via Tsallis entropy or transport approaches. The results enable future studies of the three-flavor NJL model and comparisons with other non-local or proper-time regularization schemes.
