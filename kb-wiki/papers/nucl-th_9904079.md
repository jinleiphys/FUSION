# Meson-loop contributions to the rho-omega mass splitting & rho charge radius

> Contributions of two-pseudoscalar and vector-pseudoscalar meson loops to the rho-omega mass splitting are evaluated in a covariant model based on studies of the Schwinger-Dyson equations of QCD. The role and importance of the different time orderings of the meson loops is analyzed and compared with those obtained within time-ordered perturbation theory. It is shown that each meson loop contributes less than 10% of the bare mass, and decreases as the masses of the intermediate mesons increase beyond approximately m_rho/2. A mass splitting of m_omega - m_rho ~= 25 MeV is obtained from the pi pi, K K(bar), omega pi, rho pi, omega eta, rho eta and K* K channels. The model is then used to determine the effect of the two-pion loop on the rho-meson electromagnetic form factor. It is shown that the inclusion of pion loops increases the rho-meson charge radius by 10%.

## Key claim

The authors show that each two-meson loop contributes less than 10% of the bare vector-meson mass, and that contributions decrease rapidly as the masses of the intermediate mesons increase beyond approximately m_rho/2, which suggests rapid convergence of the sum over intermediate states, in contrast to the slow convergence found in the earlier nonrelativistic calculation of Geiger and Isgur (1997).

## Method

The authors evaluate contributions of two-pseudoscalar and vector-pseudoscalar meson loops to the rho and omega meson self energies using a covariant quantum field theoretic model based on the Schwinger-Dyson equations of QCD. Meson transition form factors are calculated from dressed-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes that describe the quark-antiquark substructure of the mesons, and the self energies are obtained from loop integrals involving these form factors and scalar-boson propagators. The model is fit to properties and electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon, and has been shown to give reasonable results for decays and electromagnetic form factors of light vector mesons in earlier work by Hawes et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

- Each meson loop contributes less than 10% of the bare mass of the rho meson.
- A mass splitting of m_omega - m_rho approximately 25 MeV is obtained from the pi pi, K Kbar, omega pi, rho pi, omega eta, rho eta, and K* K channels.
- The experimentally observed mass splitting is m_omega - m_rho = 12 +/- 1 MeV.
- The inclusion of pion loops increases the rho-meson charge radius by 10% relative to the quark-antiquark-only calculation of Hawes et al. (1998).
- The coupling constant g_{rho pi pi} = 6.03 is extracted from the experimentally observed rho -> pi pi decay width.
- In the earlier work of Geiger and Isgur (1997), the pi pi loop contributed -142 MeV to the rho mass, and the omega pi loop contributed -146 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier covariant calculation of Mitchell and Tandy (1997) for the pi pi intermediate state by adding six additional channels (K Kbar, omega pi, rho pi, omega eta, rho eta, and K* K), and reconciles the small two-pion-loop mass shift found in that work with the much larger shifts found in the nonrelativistic, time-ordered perturbation theory calculation of Geiger and Isgur (1997). The results indicate that the sum over two-meson intermediate states converges rapidly as intermediate meson masses increase, contrary to the slow convergence found by Geiger and Isgur (1997), and suggest that the valence quark-antiquark picture of mesons receives only small corrections from meson-loop effects. The calculation also provides a prediction for the increase in the rho-meson charge radius due to pion loops, which could be probed indirectly through diffractive photoproduction measurements.
