# An aberrant "spin-orbit interaction" persists in the literature since more than thirty years

> An expression for the spin-orbit interaction coupling between different levels, which was shown to be aberrant more than thirty years ago is used in a recent article [K. Amos, L. Canton, G.Piseni, J. P. Svenne and D. van der Knijff, Nucl. Phys. A 728, 65 (2003)]. It leads to expressions quite simpler than they should be. Its behavior is in fact of a character opposite to that of the spin-orbit interaction used in two-body studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a simplified expression for the spin-orbit interaction coupling between different nuclear levels, used in Amos et al. (2003) and persisting for over thirty years, is "aberrant" because its behavior is opposite to that of the spin-orbit interaction derived from two-body studies or from the Dirac equation, despite yielding simpler expressions.

## Method

Raynal compares the incorrect spin-orbit coupling term (Eq. E1), sometimes called the Oak-Ridge term, with the correct "full Thomas term" (Eq. E3) derived from transforming the Dirac equation into a Schrödinger equation, as used by Blair and Sherif. He shows that the correct expression (Eq. E4) contains three distinct form factors, whereas the aberrant expression uses only one. He parametrizes both forms in the ECIS coupled-channels codes (Eq. E5) to demonstrate how setting specific parameters (z1–z6) reproduces either the aberrant or the correct coupling.

## Key numbers

- The correct spin-orbit coupling from Blair and Sherif uses a parameter λ, with λ = 2 giving excellent results for experiments around 20 MeV.
- The first publications on the subject overestimated the spin-orbit interaction by a factor of 4, an error that does not affect the similarity between the two-body and one-body forms.
- The correct expression (Eq. E4) contains three form factors: (1/r)(d/dr)V_λ(r), V_λ(r)/(2r²), and (V_λ(r)/r)(d/dr); the aberrant expression (Eq. E1) contains only one form factor with the combination -1/(2αr) W_ls {[ℓ·s]_c' + [ℓ·s]_c}.

## Context

This comment extends the critique first made over thirty years ago regarding the incorrect spin-orbit coupling term, which was used in Amos et al. (2003) and earlier in works by other authors (Raynal cites RAP2 and RAP3). The correct form, derived from the Dirac equation by Blair and Sherif, has been implemented in the ECIS code series (ECIS68 through ECIS97) and the DWBA code series (DWBA70 through DWBA98). The paper enables future users of coupled-channels codes to recognize and avoid the aberrant term, and highlights that the correct form involves three distinct form factors rather than the single simplified one.
