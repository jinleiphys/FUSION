# An expedition to the islands of stability in the first-order causal hydrodynamics

> The recently proposed connection between the Lorentz invariance of stability and the speed of signal propagation has been tested for a first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic theory. The fact that the stability situation in different reference frames agrees with each other only as long as the signal propagation respects causality, has been explicitly established for the theory, which is microscopically derived from the covariant kinetic equation in general hydrodynamic frames with arbitrary momentum-dependent interactions.

## Key claim

The authors explicitly demonstrate that for the first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic theory derived from kinetic theory (the BMR approach), stability is a Lorentz-invariant property if and only if the signal propagation respects causality; when the asymptotic group velocity exceeds the speed of light, no choice of parameters yields stability across all reference frames.

## Method

The authors linearize the conservation equations of the BMR theory around a hydrostatic equilibrium state with a boosted background fluid (boost velocity **v** along the x-axis) and perform a Fourier analysis to obtain dispersion relations for shear and sound (longitudinal) channels. They extract asymptotic group velocities from the large-wave-number limit and apply the Routh-Hurwitz stability criteria to the polynomial equations governing the non-hydrodynamic modes, analyzing the parameter space spanned by the hydrodynamic frame indices (i,j,k), the momentum-dependent relaxation time parameter Λ, and the boost velocity **v**.

## Key numbers

* The critical value of Λ for reaching asymptotic causality (v_g < 1) is Λ_c = 6.65 for frame (i,j,k) = (4,3,2) and Λ_c = 2.9 for frame (i,j,k) = (6,4,4).
* For small values of Λ (Λ < 1.2 for frame (4,3,2) and Λ < 0.6 for frame (6,4,4)), three real roots of the group velocity squared exist, among which two are always negative and one is always greater than one, excluding any subluminal root.
* The stability condition for the shear channel in a boosted frame is θ > η **v**^2, where θ = -θ_1 and η is shear viscosity, which is automatically satisfied for all 0 < **v** < 1 when the asymptotic causality condition θ > η holds in the local rest frame.
* The coefficient G_3 = γ^3 n_0 (ε_0 + P_0) (1 - **v**^2 c_s^2) is always positive, where c_s is the speed of sound.

## Context

This work extends the general theorems of Gavassino (2021) and Bemfica (2020) by providing an explicit demonstration for a first-order theory derived from first-principle kinetic theory with arbitrary momentum-dependent interactions (the BMR approach of Biswas, Mitra, and Roy, 2022). It confirms that the connection between causality and Lorentz-invariant stability holds beyond the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory studied by Hiscock (1983), Olson (1990), and Pu (2009), and it enables future studies to check stability only in the local rest frame rather than in all boosted frames, significantly simplifying the analysis for causal theories.
