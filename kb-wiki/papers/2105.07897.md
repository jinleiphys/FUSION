# Current-Conserving Relativistic Linear Response for Collisional Plasmas

> We investigate the response of a relativistic plasma to electromagnetic fields in the framework of the Boltzmann equation incorporating a collision term in the relaxation rate approximation selected in a form assuring current conservation. We obtain an explicit solution for the linearized perturbation of the Fermi-Dirac equilibrium distribution in terms of the average relaxation rate $κ$. We study the resulting covariant, gauge invariant, and current conserving form of the polarization tensor in the ultrarelativistic and non-relativistic limits. We evaluate the susceptibility in the ultrarelativistic limit and explore their dependence on $κ$. Finally, we study the dispersion relations for the longitudinal and transverse poles of the propagator. We show that for $κ> 2ω_p$, where $ω_p$ is the plasma frequency, the plasma wave modes are overdamped. In the opposite case, $κ\ll ω_p$, the propagating plasma modes are weakly damped.

## Key claim

The paper derives a covariant, gauge-invariant, and current-conserving polarization tensor for a relativistic plasma by incorporating a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) modification of the Anderson-Witting collision term in the relaxation rate approximation; the key condition is that for an average relaxation rate \(\kappa > 2\omega_p\) (where \(\omega_p\) is the plasma frequency) the plasma wave modes become overdamped, while for \(\kappa \ll \omega_p\) they are weakly damped.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized relativistic Boltzmann equation with a collision term in the relaxation rate approximation, using a BGK-type modification that ensures current conservation. They obtain an explicit solution for the perturbation of the Fermi-Dirac equilibrium distribution in momentum space, from which they compute the induced four-current and the covariant polarization tensor. The integrals are evaluated analytically in the ultrarelativistic (massless) and non-relativistic (low-temperature) limits, and the resulting susceptibilities and dispersion relations are studied as functions of the average relaxation rate \(\kappa\).

## Key numbers

* Plasma frequency in the ultrarelativistic limit: \(\omega_p^2 = m_D^2/3\), where the Debye mass is \(m_D^2 = q^2 T^3/3\).
* Plasma frequency in the non-relativistic limit: \(\omega_p^2 = m_L^2\), with \(m_L^2 \equiv q^2 (2mT/\pi)^{3/2} e^{-m/T}/(2m)\).
* Condition for overdamped plasma modes: \(\kappa > 2\omega_p\); condition for weakly damped propagating modes: \(\kappa \ll \omega_p\).
* In the optical limit (\(k \ll m_D\)), the longitudinal and transverse susceptibilities both expand to leading order as \(\chi_L(\omega,k) = \chi_T(\omega,k) = -\omega_p^2/[\omega(\omega+i\kappa)] + O(k^2)\).
* In the static limit (\(k \gg m_D\)), the longitudinal susceptibility gives \(\chi_L(\omega,k) = m_D^2/k^2 + O(1/k^3)\), describing Debye screening with inverse screening length \(m_D\).

## Context

This work extends the collisionless linear response formalism of Weldon (1982) and the semiclassical transport approach of Blaizot and Iancu (2001) by including collisional effects via a current-conserving BGK modification of the Anderson-Witting collision term. It contrasts with earlier treatments by Carrington et al. (2003) and Schenke et al. (2006), who studied collective modes in massless equilibrium and non-equilibrium plasmas, respectively, but focused on internal plasma dynamics rather than the response to external electromagnetic fields. The results enable the calculation of frequency- and wavelength-dependent electric and magnetic susceptibilities and conductivities for collisional relativistic plasmas, and provide a covariant starting point for generalizations to momentum-dependent relaxation rates or to quark-gluon plasmas.
