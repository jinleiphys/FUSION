# Multiple shape coexistence in the $N=Z$ $^{84}$Mo nucleus

> The structure of the nucleus $^{84}$Mo has been studied using the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) with the Gogny D1S interaction. The calculations incorporate a mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected intrinsic wave functions, defined over triaxial quadrupole degrees of freedom. This approach yields an excellent agreement with the scarce experimental data for this nucleus and several bands based on different shapes are predicted.

# Multiple shape coexistence in the $N=Z$ $^{84}$Mo nucleus

## Key claim
The $^{84}$Mo nucleus exhibits multiple shape coexistence with a triaxially deformed ground state and several low-lying excited $0^{+}$ states, but the excellent agreement with experimental ground-state band energies may be partly incidental due to the interaction being adjusted at the mean-field level and the absence of proton-neutron pairing and time-reversal symmetry breaking terms.

## Method
The projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) was applied with the Gogny D1S interaction, mixing particle-number and angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle vacua defined over triaxial quadrupole degrees of freedom $(\beta_2,\gamma)$. The intrinsic wave functions were obtained by minimizing the particle-number-projected HFB energy with constraints on deformation, and the final nuclear states were determined by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation for each angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- Particle-number-projected energy surface shows minima at: spherical; triaxial $(\beta_2,\gamma)\approx(0.4,20^\circ)$; highly deformed prolate $(0.65,0^\circ)$; and $(0.5,40^\circ)$
- A triaxial minimum at $(\beta_2,\gamma)\approx(0.4,20^\circ)$ is connected to deformations toward the oblate part at $(0.25,30^\circ-60^\circ)$
- Theoretical ground-state band energies lie below experimental values up to angular momentum $J=8$
- Up to five $0^{+}$ states appear in the lower part of the spectrum, each associated with different shapes

## Context
This work extends the PGCM studies of shape coexistence in the $N=Z$ region previously applied to $^{80}$Zr (Rodriguez 2011), where multiple shape coexistence was also predicted. The results for $^{84}$Mo show a structure similar to $^{80}$Zr but with a triaxial rather than prolate ground state. The calculations enable future inclusion of cranking terms and proton-neutron pairing, which are noted as work in progress to resolve the potential overestimation of deformation and the "too good" agreement with experiment.
