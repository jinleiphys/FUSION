# Test of Nuclear Wave Functions for Pseudospin Symmetry

> Using the fact that pseudospin is an approximate symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian with realistic scalar and vector mean fields, we derive the wave functions of the pseudospin partners of eigenstates of a realistic Dirac Hamiltonian and compare these wave functions with the wave functions of the Dirac eigenstates.

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry is an approximate symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian with realistic nuclear mean fields, but because the exact symmetry limit (V_S = -V_V) produces no bound Dirac valence states, the pseudospin partners of eigenstates are not necessarily eigenstates themselves; the paper tests how much the wave functions of these partners deviate from the true Dirac eigenstates.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the pseudospin partner wave functions of Dirac eigenstates by applying the pseudospin generators (which depend on the unit momentum vector) to eigenstates expanded in a spherical basis, then transforming to momentum space and back to coordinate space. They compared the resulting upper and lower radial components of the pseudospin partners with the actual Dirac eigenstates for realistic relativistic mean-field wave functions in ²⁰⁸Pb, using a relativistic point coupling model parameterized in prior work.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the pseudospin partners of the 0d₃/₂ and 1d₃/₂ eigenstates (ℓ̃=1, j=3/2) were compared with the 1s₁/₂ and 2s₁/₂ eigenstates; the lower components agreed very well except for some surface disagreement, while upper components showed shape agreement but magnitude differences.
- For the j=ℓ̃−1/2 case, the pseudospin partners of 1s₁/₂ and 2s₁/₂ (ℓ̃=1, j=1/2) were compared with 0d₃/₂ and 1d₃/₂ eigenstates; the partners did not fall off exponentially but as r⁻³, consistent with the predicted r⁻(ℓ̃+2) behavior.
- For intruder states with ℓ̃=4, j=7/2 (0f₇/₂), the pseudospin partner [P(0f₇/₂)]h₉/₂ had an upper component falling off as r⁻⁶, with no quasi-degenerate h₉/₂ eigenstate to compare to.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic pseudospin symmetry framework introduced by Ginocchio (1997) and Leviatan (1999), which showed that pseudospin arises from an SU(2) symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian when V_S ≈ −V_V. Prior studies by Ginocchio (1999), Ring (1999), and Arima (1999) had compared only the lower components of Dirac wave functions in pseudospin doublets; the present paper provides the first systematic comparison of the upper components, which dominate the Dirac eigenstates. The results enable future tests of whether the observed near-degeneracy of pseudospin doublets in deformed and superdeformed nuclei (Bohr 1982, Dudek 1987, Twin 1986, Stephens 1990) is accompanied by similarly small wave-function deviations.
