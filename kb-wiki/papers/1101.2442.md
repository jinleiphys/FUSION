# Influence of the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma on elliptic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the influence of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio eta/s on the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of hadrons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that the elliptic flow in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC is dominated by the viscosity in the hadronic phase and in the phase transition region, but largely insensitive to the viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). At the highest LHC energy, the elliptic flow becomes sensitive to the QGP viscosity and insensitive to the hadronic viscosity.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC is dominated by the viscosity in the hadronic phase and phase transition region, but largely insensitive to the viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP); at the highest LHC energy, this sensitivity reverses and elliptic flow becomes dominated by QGP viscosity and insensitive to hadronic viscosity.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics simulations using the Israel-Stewart approach for the shear stress tensor, solved numerically with the SHASTA algorithm and centered second-order finite differences. They used a temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s) with a minimum of 0.08 at T=180 MeV, comparing four parametrizations: constant low eta/s in both phases, low eta/s in hadron gas with lattice-QCD-inspired increase in QGP, hadron-gas eta/s with constant low eta/s in QGP, and realistic parametrizations for both phases. Initial conditions were based on the optical Glauber model with longitudinal boost invariance, and hadron spectra were computed using Cooper-Frye freeze-out at T_dec=100 MeV with viscous corrections to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- Minimum eta/s value used: 0.08 at T=180 MeV
- Initialization time tau_0 = 1.0 fm for sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, with maximum energy density epsilon_0 = 24.0 GeV/fm^3 and T_max = 335 MeV
- Initialization time tau_0 = 0.6 fm for sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, with epsilon_0 = 187.0 GeV/fm^3 and T_max = 506 MeV
- Initialization time tau_0 = 0.6 fm for sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, with epsilon_0 = 240.0 GeV/fm^3 and T_max = 594 MeV
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 100 MeV; chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem = 150 MeV
- Relaxation time tau_pi = 5 eta/(epsilon + p) from kinetic theory

## Context

This work extends prior viscous hydrodynamic studies at RHIC (Song 2010, Shen 2010) that assumed constant eta/s, and corroborates findings by Hirano (2005) and Song (2010) on the importance of hadronic viscosity at RHIC energies. The paper demonstrates that extracting the QGP shear viscosity from elliptic flow requires LHC data, as RHIC data alone are insensitive to the high-temperature QGP viscosity. This enables future determination of the temperature dependence of eta/s in the QGP phase using LHC elliptic flow measurements, potentially revealing the transition from strongly coupled plasma near T_c to weakly coupled QGP.
