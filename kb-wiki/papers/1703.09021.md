# Corona effect in AA collisions at LHC

> Following our earlier finding based on RHIC data on the dominant jet production from nucleus corona region, we reconsider this effect in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energy. Our hypothesis was based on experimental data, which raised the idea of a finite formation time for the produced medium. At RHIC energy and in low density corona region this time reaches about 2 fm/c. All observed high p_t particles are produced in the corona region and have a chance to escape during this 2 fm/c. After that, the formed high density matter absorbs all jets. Following this hypothesis, the nuclear modification factor R_{AA} should be independent on particle momentum and be flat versus p_t. At the same time, we describe the finite azimuthal anisotropy of high p_t particles, v_2. A separate prediction held that, at LHC energy, the formation time in the corona region should be two times smaller, about 1 fm/c. New data at LHC show that R_{AA} is not flat and is rising with p_t. We add to our original hypothesis an assumption that a fast parton traversing the produced medium loses the fixed portion of its energy. A shift of about 7 GeV (I would call it as a parton binding energy) from the original power law p^{-6} production cross section in pp explains well all the observed R_{AA} dependencies. The shift of about 7 GeV is also valid at RHIC energy, where the cross section follows a power law with about p^{-8} and this shift explains a very slow rise of R_{AA} seen for neutral pions with p_t above 15 GeV/c. We also show that the observed at LHC dependence of v_2 at high p_t and our previous predictions agree.

# Corona effect in AA collisions at LHC

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor R_AA in PbPb collisions at LHC is not flat with transverse momentum as originally predicted, but rises with p_t; this can be explained by adding a fixed parton energy loss of about 7 GeV to the original corona model with finite formation time, where the formation time in the corona region at LHC energy is about 1 fm/c compared to 2 fm/c at RHIC.

## Method

The author extends a previous corona model (Pantuev, 2007) that assumed a finite formation time for the produced medium, with partons escaping from the low-density corona region before the medium becomes opaque. To account for LHC data showing a rising R_AA with p_t, the model adds the assumption that a fast parton traversing the produced medium loses a fixed portion of its energy, implemented as a constant shift of 7 GeV from the original power-law production cross section in pp collisions. The formation time at LHC is estimated from the rise of the total nucleon-nucleon cross section relative to RHIC, using the relation that formation time is proportional to the inverse square root of the density of hard scatterings.

## Key numbers

- Formation time in the corona region at RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): about 2 fm/c (also given as T_0=2.3 fm/c)
- Formation time in the corona region at LHC energy (around 5 TeV): about 1 fm/c (estimated as 1.2 fm/c, valid within 5% uncertainty over the LHC energy range of 2.7-5 TeV)
- Parton energy loss (binding energy shift): about 7 GeV, applied at both LHC and RHIC energies
- Formation time in the center of the collision zone at LHC: about three times shorter than the corona value (approximately 0.4 fm/c)
- Predicted constant R_AA=0.1 for high p_t particles in most central collisions at LHC from the corona contribution alone
- At RHIC, the production cross section follows a power law p^{-8}; at LHC, it follows p^{-6}
- Rise of NN total cross section: at 200 GeV the cross section rises by 13 mb from its minimum; at 5500 GeV it rises by 49 mb

## Context

This work extends the corona model originally developed for RHIC data (Pantuev, 2007) to LHC energies, where new data from ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS show that R_AA rises with p_t rather than being flat. The model contradicts approaches that assume the core region is completely opaque; instead it introduces a constant 7 GeV energy loss that also explains the slow rise of R_AA for neutral pions above 15 GeV/c at RHIC (PHENIX data). The prediction for v_2 at high p_t from the earlier work (Pantuev, "The last call for prediction") is confirmed by CMS data at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. The work enables a unified description of R_AA and v_2 across RHIC and LHC energies using a single energy-loss parameter, and suggests that the formation time scales with the density of hard scatterings in nucleon-nucleon collisions.
