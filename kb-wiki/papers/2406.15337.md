# Hybrid Star Properties with NJL and MFTQCD Model: A Bayesian Approach

> The composition of the core of neutron stars (NS) is still under debate. One possibility is that because of the high densities reached in their cores, matter could be deconfined into quark matter. We investigate the existence of hybrid stars, using microscopic models to describe different phases of matter. Within the adopted microscopic models we calculate properties of NS and properties of matter. We want to probe the pQCD calculations influence and analyze properties that identify a transition to deconfined matter. Bayesian approach is applied to generate 8 sets of equations of state (EOS). A Maxwell construction is adopted to describe the deconfinement transition. For the hadron phase, we consider a stiff and a soft EOS obtained from the Relativistic Mean Field model with nonlinear meson terms. For the quark phase, we use 2 different models: the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with multiquark interactions and the Mean Field Theory of QCD, a model similar to the vector MIT bag model. Bayesian inference was applied to determine the model parameters that satisfy the X-ray observations from NICER and have phase transition at densities between 0.15 - 0.40 fm$^{-3}$. We also applied restrictions from the pQCD calculations to half of the sets. Hybrid stars are compatible with current observational data. The pQCD restrictions reduce the value of the $M_{max}$. However, even applying this restriction, the models were able to reach values of $2.1 - 2.3 M_\odot$. The conformal limit was still not attained at the center of the most massive stars. The vector interactions are essential to describe hybrid stars with a mass above $2 M_\odot$. The multiquark interactions introduced may affect the limits of some quantities considered as indicators of the presence of a deconfined phase. It is possible to find a set of EOS, that predict that inside NS the renormalized matter trace anomaly is always positive.

## Key claim

Hybrid stars with a deconfined quark core are compatible with current NICER observations, but the perturbative QCD (pQCD) constraint reduces the maximum mass; even with this restriction, the models reach maximum masses of 2.1-2.3 solar masses, though the conformal limit is not attained at the center of the most massive stars.

## Method

A Bayesian approach with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling generated eight sets of equations of state (EOS) using a Maxwell construction for the deconfinement transition. For the hadron phase, a soft (BMPF 220) and a stiff (BMPF 260) EOS from the Relativistic Mean Field model with nonlinear meson terms were used; for the quark phase, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with multiquark interactions and the Mean Field Theory of QCD (MFTQCD) were employed. Bayesian inference determined model parameters satisfying NICER X-ray observations from PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, with phase transition densities between 0.15 and 0.40 fm^-3, and pQCD restrictions were applied to half of the sets.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass range with pQCD restriction: 2.1-2.3 solar masses (M_sun)
- Phase transition density range allowed: 0.15-0.40 fm^-3
- Phase transition likelihood centered at rho_trans = 0.275 fm^-3 with standard deviation sigma = 0.08 fm^-3
- pQCD constraint applied at baryon density rho_B = 7 rho_0 (seven times nuclear saturation density)
- Hadronic EOS BMPF 220 (soft): maximum mass 2.200 M_sun, radius at 1.4 M_sun = 12.94 km, tidal deformability at 1.4 M_sun = 548
- Hadronic EOS BMPF 260 (stiff): maximum mass 2.592 M_sun, radius at 1.4 M_sun = 13.51 km, tidal deformability at 1.4 M_sun = 828
- NJL model cutoff Lambda = 623.58 MeV, up/down quark mass = 5.70 MeV, strange quark mass = 136.60 MeV
- MFTQCD model quark masses: m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV
- MFTQCD parameter B prior range: 50-180 MeV.fm^-3
- NJL parameter B prior range: 0-50 MeV.fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian studies of neutron star EOS using RMF models (constrain_apply, 2022) by incorporating quark matter phases and testing two different quark models. It builds on model-independent analyses of deconfinement indicators such as the speed of sound, polytropic index (Annala, 2019), and renormalized trace anomaly (Fujimoto, 2022), now using physically motivated microscopic models. The results enable future comparisons with Gibbs construction approaches and studies including hyperons, which could soften the hadronic EOS and shift the transition density.
