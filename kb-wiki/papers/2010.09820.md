# Solving relativistic three-body integral equations in the presence of bound states

> We present a systematically improvable method for numerically solving relativistic three-body integral equations for the partial-wave projected amplitudes. The method consists of a discretization procedure in momentum space, which approximates the continuum problem with a matrix equation. It is solved for different matrix sizes, and in the end, an extrapolation is employed to restore the continuum limit. Our technique is tested by solving a three-body problem of scalar particles with an $S$ wave two-body bound state. We discuss two methods of incorporating the pole contribution in the integral equations, both of them leading to agreement with previous results obtained using finite-volume spectra of the same theory. We provide an analytic and numerical estimate of the systematic errors. Although we focus on kinematics below the three-particle threshold, we provide numerical evidence that the methods presented allow for determination of amplitude above this threshold as well.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematically improvable numerical method for solving relativistic three-body integral equations that works even when the two-body subsystem contains a bound state pole, a scenario where perturbative approaches fail; the method is validated by showing agreement with finite-volume results from Romero-Lopez (2019) below the three-particle threshold, and numerical evidence suggests it also works above this threshold.

## Method

The authors discretize the one-dimensional partial-wave projected integral equation for the amputated ladder amplitude \(d_S^{(u,u)}\) on a momentum-space mesh, converting the continuum integral equation into a matrix equation of size \(N\). They handle the two-body bound state pole singularity by introducing a finite \(i\epsilon\) regulator that shifts the pole off the real axis, using two regularization variants: a standard \(+i\epsilon\) prescription and an \(\epsilon\)-regulated delta function method. Solutions are obtained for multiple mesh sizes \(N\) and regulator values \(\epsilon\), then extrapolated to the continuum limit (\(N\to\infty\), \(\epsilon\to 0\)) to restore the physical amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body scattering length \(ma = 2\), the \(\varphi b\) scattering amplitude is computed below the three-particle threshold and compared to finite-volume results; the \(q\cot\delta_{\varphi b}\) values agree with Romero-Lopez (2019) to within sub-percent level deviation from \(S\) matrix unitarity.
- The two-body bound state pole position is given by \(s_b = 4(m^2 - \kappa_{2k}^2)\) with binding momentum \(\kappa_{2k} = 1/a\); the residue at the pole in the leading-order effective range expansion is \(g = 8\sqrt{2\pi\sqrt{s_b}\,\kappa_{2k}}\).
- The bound state–spectator phase space is \(\rho_{\varphi b}(E) = q/(8\pi E)\), where \(q = \frac{1}{2E}\lambda^{1/2}(E^2, s_b, m^2)\) is the on-shell spectator momentum at the bound state pole.
- Three cases of scattering length are studied: \(ma = 2\), \(6\), and \(16\), corresponding to increasingly bound two-body subsystems.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-body integral equation framework of Hansen and Sharpe (2015) and Jackura et al. (2018) to systems with two-body bound states, which was previously excluded by assumption. It directly compares to the finite-volume study of the same toy model by Romero-Lopez (2019), showing agreement below the three-particle threshold. The method enables future extraction of three-body scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD for systems like three-nucleon interactions where the deuteron bound state appears, and provides a pathway to incorporate nonzero three-body \(K\) matrix \(\mathcal{K}_{\text{df},3}\) in subsequent analyses.
