# Low-Energy Quantum Effective Action for Relativistic Superfluids

> We consider relativistic superfluids where the U(1) baryon symmetry is spontaneously broken. Using the formalism of the quantum effective action, we show that the low-energy dynamics of the superfluid Goldstone boson is completely determined by the equation of state. Applying the general formalism to asymptotically high densities, we find the effective action for the superfluid Goldstone field to leading order in alpha_s, and show that terms containing fifth and higher powers of the Goldstone boson appear only at the alpha_s^2 level with known coefficients.

## Key claim

The low-energy quantum effective action for the superfluid Goldstone boson in a relativistic superfluid is completely determined by the equation of state, but only to leading order in derivatives (i.e., keeping only terms with the minimal number of spatial derivatives at each power of the field), which limits its applicability to energies below the gap energy.

## Method

The author starts from the quantum effective action formalism for a theory with a spontaneously broken U(1) baryon symmetry, introduces a background gauge field \(A_\mu\) to enforce gauge invariance, and integrates out the modulus of the order parameter by minimization. By expanding the effective action in powers of the Goldstone field \(\varphi\) and keeping only terms with the smallest number of spatial derivatives, the effective action density is shown to depend only on \(D_\mu\varphi = \partial_\mu\varphi - A_\mu\). The unknown function is then fixed by noting that at constant fields the effective action equals the ground-state energy density, which is minus the thermodynamic pressure \(P(\mu)\), yielding the final result \(\Gamma[\mu,\varphi] = \int d^4x\, P\bigl(\sqrt{(\partial_0\varphi-\mu)^2 - (\partial_i\varphi)^2}\bigr)\).

## Key numbers

- For asymptotically high-density quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, the pressure to leading order in \(\alpha_s\) is \(P(\mu) = \frac{N_c N_f}{12\pi^2} \mu^4\), with \(N_c = N_f = 3\).
- The resulting effective Lagrangian expanded to quadratic order gives a Goldstone boson velocity of \(1/\sqrt{3}\), which equals the hydrodynamic sound speed in an ultrarelativistic gas.
- The first correction to the pressure including \(\alpha_s\) is \(P(\mu) = \frac{N_c N_f}{12\pi^2} \bigl(1 - \frac{3(N_c^2-1)}{N_c} \frac{\alpha_s(\mu)}{\pi}\bigr) \mu^4\).
- Terms with five and higher powers of the Goldstone field appear only at \(\alpha_s^2\) order, with coefficients proportional to the beta function coefficient \(\beta_0\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of quantum effective actions to relativistic superfluids, showing that the low-energy dynamics of the Goldstone boson is fixed by the equation of state alone, without requiring detailed microscopic calculations. It generalizes earlier hydrodynamic approaches by Lebedev and Khalatnikov (1960s) and Carter (1980s) by providing a quantum effective action valid for tree-level calculations of scattering amplitudes, not just classical equations of motion. The result enables computation of Goldstone boson scattering amplitudes, neutrino emission and absorption rates, and thermal conductivity in superfluid neutron star cores, and can be systematically improved by including higher-order corrections to the equation of state from QCD perturbation theory.
