# Kaon photoproduction in a multipole approach

> The recently published experimental data on K+Lambda photoproduction by the SAPHIR, CLAS, and LEPS collaborations are analyzed by means of a multipole approach. For this purpose the background amplitudes are constructed from appropriate Feynman diagrams in a gauge-invariant and crossing-symmetric fashion. The results of our calculation emphasize the lack of mutual consistency between the SAPHIR and CLAS data previously found by several independent research groups, whereas the LEPS data are found to be more consistent with those of CLAS. The use of SAPHIR and CLAS data, individually or simultaneously, leads to quite different resonance parameters which, therefore, could lead to different conclusions on ``missing resonances''. Fitting to the SAPHIR and LEPS data simultaneously indicates that the S_{11}(1650), P_{13}(1720), D_{13}(1700), D_{13}(2080), F_{15}(1680), and F_{15}(2000) resonances are required, while fitting to the combination of CLAS and LEPS data leads alternatively to the P_{13}(1900), D_{13}(2080), D_{15}(1675), F_{15}(1680), and F_{17}(1990) resonances. Although yielding different results in most cases, both SAPHIR and CLAS data indicate that the second peak in the cross sections at W = 1900 MeV originates from the D_{13}(2080) resonance with a mass between 1911 - 1936 MeV. Furthermore, in contrast to the results of currently available models and the Table of Particle Properties, both data sets do not exhibit the need for a P_{11}(1710) resonance. The few data points available for target asymmetry can not be described by the models proposed in the present work.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the SAPHIR and CLAS datasets for K+Lambda photoproduction are mutually inconsistent, leading to different sets of required nucleon resonances depending on which dataset is used, while both datasets agree that the second peak in the cross section near W = 1900 MeV originates from the D13(2080) resonance with a mass between 1911 and 1936 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed background amplitudes from tree-level Feynman diagrams including Born terms and t-channel K*(892) and K1(1270) exchanges, maintaining gauge invariance through hadronic form factors and crossing symmetry via the Davidson-Workman prescription. Resonant electric and magnetic multipoles were parameterized with Breit-Wigner forms for nucleon resonances up to spin 9/2, and the model was fitted to differential cross section, recoil polarization, and photon asymmetry data from SAPHIR, CLAS, and LEPS collaborations using three different data combinations (SAPHIR+LEPS, CLAS+LEPS, and all data combined).

## Key numbers

- The D13(2080) resonance mass extracted from fits lies between 1911 and 1936 MeV across all three fits, with Fit 3 giving 1911 +/- 4 MeV
- The hadronic form factor cut-off Lambda was 0.50 +/- 0.02 GeV for Fit 1 (SAPHIR+LEPS), 1.13 +/- 0.01 GeV for Fit 2 (CLAS+LEPS), and 0.91 +/- 0.02 GeV for Fit 3 (all data)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom was 1.02 for Fit 1 (834 data points), 0.98 for Fit 2 (1694 data points), and 1.31 for Fit 3 (2444 data points)
- The SAPHIR differential cross sections contributed 84% to the chi-squared in Fit 1, while CLAS differential cross sections contributed 74% in Fit 2
- The S11(1650) helicity amplitude A1/2 was extracted as 27 +/- 1 (Fit 1), 3 +/- 1 (Fit 2), and 23 +/- 1 (Fit 3) in units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, compared to the PDG value of 53 +/- 16

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Mart 2004) which used only SAPHIR data with a slightly different method, and applies the multipole formalism developed for pion photoproduction by Hanstein and Tiator (2003). The study confirms the mutual inconsistency between SAPHIR and CLAS data previously reported by Bydzovsky (2006) and Julia-Diaz (2006), and shows that unlike existing models and the Particle Data Group tables, neither dataset requires a P11(1710) resonance. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses that could incorporate constraints from piN, pipiN, and etaN channels to better determine resonance parameters.
