# New parameterization of effective nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrix interaction for scattering at intermediate energies

> The model suggested by Love and Franey for description of the nucleon-nucleon interaction was used as the base. The new fitting of the model parameters was done in the energy range from 100 MeV up to 1100 MeV. It is based on the modern partial-wave-analysis solution for NN-amplitudes. The three observables: differential cross section, vector analyzing power, and spin correlation coefficient -- were obtained at every energy. The results are compared with existing the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper provides a new parameterization of the Love-Franey effective nucleon-nucleon t-matrix interaction, fitted to the modern SP07 partial-wave-analysis solution for NN amplitudes, covering the energy range from 100 MeV up to 1100 MeV. The claim is that this parameterization reproduces differential cross section, vector analyzing power, and spin correlation coefficient for both pp and np systems, but the validation is limited to comparison with the same SAID database used for fitting, not independent experimental data.

## Method

The author adopts the phenomenological model of Love and Franey (1981, 1985), which expresses the antisymmetrized nucleon-nucleon t-matrix in terms of five amplitudes (A', B', C', E', F') parameterized by Yukawa-like form factors with range parameters R_i and strength coefficients V_i. The coefficients R_i and V_i were refitted by minimizing the difference between the model and the SP07 partial-wave-analysis NN amplitudes from the SAID database (http://gwdac.phys.gwu.edu), separately for isospin I=0 and I=1 channels. The fitting was performed at laboratory kinetic energies from 100 MeV to 1100 MeV, and the resulting t-matrix was used to compute differential cross sections, vector analyzing powers, and spin correlation coefficients, which were then compared graphically to the SAID amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* Energy range of the fit: 100 MeV to 1100 MeV in the laboratory frame (T_lab).
* The t-matrix is connected to the NN amplitude M by t_NN = -(4 pi / E*) M, where E* is the center-of-mass energy.
* At T_lab = 100 MeV, the real part of the I=1 amplitude VSE for range R=0.11 fm is 8352.18 (units not specified, but consistent with the Love-Franey convention).
* At T_lab = 100 MeV, the imaginary part of the I=0 amplitude VSO for range R=0.11 fm is -2103.44.
* At T_lab = 1100 MeV, the real part of the I=1 amplitude VSE for range R=0.11 fm is 69410.4.
* At T_lab = 1100 MeV, the imaginary part of the I=0 amplitude VSO for range R=0.11 fm is -218411.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Love-Franey parameterization (Love and Franey, 1981, 1985) by refitting it to the more recent SP07 partial-wave analysis from the SAID database, whereas the original parameterization was based on older phase-shift solutions. The new parameter set enables nucleon-nucleus scattering calculations at intermediate energies (100-1100 MeV) using an updated effective interaction that better reproduces modern NN scattering observables. The paper does not discuss applications to specific nuclear reactions or comparisons with other modern NN interaction models.
