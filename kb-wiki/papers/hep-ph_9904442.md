# Generalized polarizabilities of the pion in chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude for $γ^\ast+π\to γ+π$ in the framework of chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$. We explicitly derive expressions for generalized electromagnetic polarizabilities and discuss alternative definitions of these quantities.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the virtual Compton scattering amplitude for a pion at one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory, but the results are strictly valid only at ${\cal O}(p^4)$ and the generalized polarizabilities derived exhibit a degeneracy ($\alpha_L = \alpha_T = -\beta$) that is expected to be lifted at two-loop order.

## Method

The authors computed the invariant amplitude for $\gamma^\ast+\pi\to\gamma+\pi$ using the chiral Lagrangian at ${\cal O}(p^4)$ in the momentum expansion, separating the amplitude into a gauge-invariant soft-photon piece (involving the pion electromagnetic form factor) and a residual one-particle-irreducible part. They then expanded the residual amplitude in the limit of vanishing final photon energy to extract generalized electric and magnetic polarizabilities, using two alternative definitions: one based on a multipole expansion in the center-of-mass frame (following Guichon, Liu, and Thomas) and another based on a covariant parametrization interpreted in the pion Breit frame.

## Key numbers

* Charged pion real Compton scattering polarizability at ${\cal O}(p^4)$: $\bar{\alpha}_{\pi^\pm} = -\bar{\beta}_{\pi^\pm} = (2.68 \pm 0.42) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
* Neutral pion real Compton scattering polarizability at ${\cal O}(p^4)$: $\bar{\alpha}_{\pi^0} = -\bar{\beta}_{\pi^0} = -0.50 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
* Low-energy constant combination: $2l^r_5 - l^r_6 = (2.85 \pm 0.42) \times 10^{-3}$, determined from $\pi^+ \to e^+\nu_e\gamma$ decay
* Asymptotic limit for charged pion generalized polarizability as $q^2 \to -\infty$: $\alpha^{\pi^\pm}_L(q^2) \to 1.18 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
* Asymptotic limit for neutral pion generalized polarizability as $q^2 \to -\infty$: $\alpha^{\pi^0}_L(q^2) \to -3.0 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
* Pion-decay constant: $F_\pi = 92.4$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of real Compton scattering on the pion (Holstein, 1990; Buergi, 1996) to the virtual photon case, providing the first ${\cal O}(p^4)$ calculation of generalized polarizabilities for $\gamma^\ast+\pi\to\gamma+\pi$. The results enable interpretation of upcoming experiments such as the SELEX E781 experiment (Moinester, 1999) studying $\pi+e^-\to\pi+e^-+\gamma$, and provide predictions that can be compared with future extractions of pion polarizabilities from radiative pion photoproduction and pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung.
