# Evidence for Shape Co-existence at medium spin in 76Rb

> Four previously known rotational bands in 76Rb have been extended to moderate spins using the Gammasphere and Microball gamma ray and charged particle detector arrays and the 40Ca(40Ca,3pn) reaction at a beam energy of 165 MeV. The properties of two of the negative-parity bands can only readily be interpreted in terms of the highly successful Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky model calculations if they have the same configuration in terms of the number of g9/2 particles, but they result from different nuclear shapes (one near-oblate and the other near-prolate). These data appear to constitute a unique example of shape co-existing structures at medium spins.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two negative-parity rotational bands in 76Rb can only be interpreted with Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model calculations if they share the same configuration in terms of the number of g9/2 particles but arise from different nuclear shapes (one near-oblate and one near-prolate). The hedge is that this interpretation is conditional on the CNS model being the correct framework, and the authors state the data "appear to constitute" a unique example of shape co-existing structures at medium spins.

## Method

The experiment used the Gammasphere array (99 Compton-suppressed Ge detectors) and the Microball charged-particle detector array to detect gamma rays and charged particles from the 40Ca(40Ca,3pn) reaction at a beam energy of 165 MeV. Events with no alpha particles and 1 or 2 protons were selected offline to isolate 76Rb, and a gamma-gamma-gamma cube was constructed from 1.5x10^9 high-fold events. The level scheme was extended using the RADWARE graphical analysis package, with spins and parities assigned assuming stretched E2 transitions. Band-mixing calculations were performed with parameterized smooth unperturbed bands interacting with constant matrix elements, and the results were compared to CNS model calculations without pairing.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 165 MeV for the 40Ca beam
- Target thickness: 350 microgram/cm2 40Ca flashed with 150 microgram/cm2 gold on both sides
- Total events recorded: 1.5x10^9 high-fold events
- Over 40 new gamma rays observed
- Bands extended to spins of approximately 30 hbar
- Interaction matrix element of 16 keV for the 3' and 5' bands crossing at I approximately 11
- Interaction matrix element of 44 keV for the 4' and 5' bands crossing at I approximately 15
- Interaction matrix element of 28 keV for the 3' and 4' bands (less well determined)
- For odd-spin sequences, coupling strength of 38 keV at the I approximately 12 crossing
- For odd-spin sequences at I approximately 20, interaction strength of 14 keV (poorly constrained)
- All observed states in the I=6-20 spin range reproduced within plus or minus 5 keV by the three-band-mixing calculation
- Differences between calculated and experimental energies for I greater than or approximately 15 are considerably smaller than the expected accuracy of plus or minus 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the known rotational bands in 76Rb originally reported by Harder et al. (1995) to higher spins, and builds on the extensive study of shape coexistence in the A=70-80 region reviewed by Heyde and Wood (2010). The results are interpreted using the CNS model, which has been successfully applied to neighboring nuclei such as 77Rb (Harder et al., 1996) and other nuclei in the mass region (e.g., references to work by Afanasjev et al., 2005; Valiente-Dobon et al., 2005; Davies et al., 2010). The finding of shape coexistence with the same configuration but different shapes at medium spin is claimed as a first example, and the extracted interaction matrix elements (approximately 50 keV at I approximately 15 for even-spin states) are compared to the much larger interactions found for coexistent shapes at low spin in 72-78Kr (Bouchez et al., 2003), suggesting a decreasing matrix element with increasing spin. The work enables future experiments to extend bands to higher spins to study termination or non-termination as fingerprints of configuration.
