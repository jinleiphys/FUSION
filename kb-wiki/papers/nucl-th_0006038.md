# Ginzburg-Landau equations for superconducting quark matter in neutron stars

> We investigate magnetic properties of color superconducting quark matter within a Ginzburg-Landau approach. The simultaneous coupling of the quark fields to gluonic and electromagnetic gauge fields leads to rotated electromagnetism with a massive (Higgsed) and a massless photon-gluon field. We derive the Ginzburg-Landau equations for superconducting quark matter taking into account the rotated electromagnetism in the general case when the rotation angle is an arbitrary function of the coordinates. We solve these equations for an isolated vortex in superconducting quark matter. We obtain a solution for the magnetic and gluomagnetic fields and expressions for the calculation of the penetration depth and the quantized magnetic flux in quark matter. For this case we have demonstrated that the occurence of electric and color Meissner currents is a consequence of the color superconducting state of quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives Ginzburg-Landau equations for color-superconducting quark matter that incorporate "rotated electromagnetism," where the rotation angle between the photon and gluon fields is treated as an arbitrary function of coordinates. The authors claim that for an isolated vortex, the magnetic flux is quantized in units of six times the ordinary flux quantum and the penetration depth is about 20 times smaller than in hadronic matter, but only for the case where the rotation angle is constant.

## Method

The authors start from a free energy density for two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter with ud diquark pairing and impose the condition that the rotated charge operator \(\hat Q = Q + \eta P_8\) annihilates all Cooper pairs. They introduce rotated gauge fields \(\vec A_x\) and \(\vec A_y\) as linear combinations of the electromagnetic vector potential \(\vec A\) and the gluonic field \(\vec G_8\), then derive coupled Ginzburg-Landau equations by varying the free energy with respect to the order parameter and the gauge fields. They solve these equations for an isolated vortex in homogeneous superconducting quark matter using cylindrical symmetry and modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic flux quantum for a vortex in quark matter is \(\Phi_q^\prime = 6\Phi_0 = 6 \times (2 \times 10^{-7} \text{ G cm}^2)\), i.e., six times the ordinary magnetic flux quantum.
- The rotation angle \(\alpha\) between photon and gluon fields is approximately \(\alpha \approx 1/20\) for the ud diquark condensate, where \(\eta = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
- The charge \(q\) entering the penetration depth is about 20 times larger than \(e/3\), making the penetration depth \(\lambda_q\) about 20 times smaller than that of hadronic matter.
- The gluomagnetic vortex flux exceeds the magnetic vortex flux by a factor \(\cot \alpha\).
- The critical temperature for the 2SC phase is approximately 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Ginzburg-Landau treatment of Blaschke, Sedrakian, and Shahabasyan (1999) by incorporating the rotated electromagnetism formalism introduced by Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (2000). The paper contradicts the earlier conclusion of Bailin and Love (1984) that quark matter would be a type-I superconductor, instead finding type-II behavior with vortex formation. The results enable calculations of magnetic field distributions in neutron star cores composed of color-superconducting quark matter, and provide the basis for comparing the magnetic field evolution in 2SC versus color-flavor locked (CFL) phases.
