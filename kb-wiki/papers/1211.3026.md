# A walk through the world of chiral dynamics

> Hadron-hadron scattering lengths are fine probes of our understanding of nonperturbative QCD. I discuss the status of a variety of scattering processes sensitive to the spontaneous and explicit chiral symmetry breaking of QCD, such as pion-pion, pion-kaon, pion-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon and Goldstone boson scattering off D-mesons. The fruitful interplay of theory, experiment and lattice QCD is emphasized. I point out what has to be done in these fields to gain further insight into these fundamental parameters.

# A walk through the world of chiral dynamics

## Key claim

Hadron-hadron scattering lengths provide a fine probe of nonperturbative QCD, but the precision and consistency of theoretical predictions, experimental data, and lattice QCD results vary dramatically across different scattering processes, with pion-pion scattering being the most advanced and pion-kaon scattering remaining unsatisfactory.

## Method

The paper reviews chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) and its unitarized extensions (UCHPT) applied to five classes of hadron-hadron scattering processes: pion-pion, pion-kaon, pion-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon, and Goldstone boson scattering off D-mesons. For each process, the author compares leading-order (LO), next-to-leading-order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) chiral predictions with experimental data from kaon decays, hadronic atoms, and scattering experiments, as well as with lattice QCD determinations. The analysis of antikaon-nucleon and D-meson scattering employs unitarized CHPT with Bethe-Salpeter or Lippmann-Schwinger equations to handle nonperturbative channel couplings and dynamically generated resonances.

## Key numbers

- Pion-pion S-wave scattering lengths from Roy equation analysis: a0 = 0.220 ± 0.005, a2 = -0.0444 ± 0.0010 (Colangelo 2000)
- Experimental determination from K decays: a0 = 0.2210 ± 0.0047(stat) ± 0.0040(sys), a2 = -0.0429 ± 0.0044(stat) ± 0.0028(sys) (Batley 2010)
- Pionium lifetime measurement: |a0 - a2| = 0.2533 +0.0080/-0.0078(stat) +0.0078/-0.0073(sys) (Adeva 2011)
- Pion-nucleon scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen and deuterium: a+ = (7.6 ± 3.1) × 10^(-3)/Mπ, a- = (86.1 ± 0.9) × 10^(-3)/Mπ (Baru 2011)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant from GMO sum rule: gπN^2/(4π) = 13.69(12)(15) (Baru 2011)
- Antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths from SIDDHARTA and scattering data: a0 = -1.81 +0.30/-0.28 + i 0.92 +0.29/-0.23 fm, a1 = +0.48 +0.12/-0.11 + i 0.87 +0.26/-0.20 fm (Mai 2012)
- Kaonic hydrogen from SIDDHARTA: ΔE1s = -283 ± 36(stat) ± 6(syst) eV, Γ1s = 541 ± 89(stat) ± 22(syst) eV (Bazzi 2011)
- DK(I=0) scattering length from UCHPT fit to lattice data: a = -0.85 +0.07/-0.05 fm (Liu 2012)
- Predicted isospin-violating width of Ds0*(2317): Γ(Ds0*(2317)+ → Ds+ π0) = (89 ± 27) keV (Liu 2012)

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Weinberg (1966) on current algebra predictions for pion scattering lengths and by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) on one-loop CHPT calculations. It covers subsequent developments including two-loop CHPT by Bijnens (1995), Roy equation analyses by Colangelo (2000), and Roy-Steiner equation analyses by Buettiker (2003). The paper emphasizes the fruitful interplay between theory, experiment, and lattice QCD, and identifies open problems: lattice QCD has yet to provide a direct determination of a0 for pion-pion scattering; pion-kaon scattering requires better experimental isospin decomposition and more precise lattice results; and a measurement of kaonic deuterium would further constrain antikaon-nucleon scattering. The D-meson sector demonstrates how unitarized CHPT combined with lattice data can probe the molecular nature of states like the Ds0*(2317).
