# Multi-neutron transfer in $^{8}$He induced reactions near the Coulomb barrier

> The measured inclusive $^6$He and $^4$He production cross sections of G. Marqu{í}nez-Dur{á}n {\em et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 98}, 034615 (2018) are reexamined and the conclusions concerning the relative importance of 1n and 2n transfer to the production of $^6$He arising from the interaction of a 22 MeV $^8$He beam with a $^{208}$Pb target revised. A consideration of the kinematics of the 2n-stripping reaction when compared with the measured $^6$He total energy versus angle spectrum places strict limits on the allowed excitation energy of the $^{210}$Pb residual, so constraining distorted wave Born approximation calculations that the contribution of the 2n stripping process to the inclusive $^6$He production can only be relatively small. It is therefore concluded that the dominant $^6$He production mechanism must be 1n stripping followed by decay of the $^7$He ejectile. Based on this result we present strong arguments in favor of direct, one step four-neutron (4n) stripping as the main mechanism for $^4$He production.

## Key claim

The paper revises the earlier conclusion that two-neutron (2n) stripping dominates $^6$He production in the $^8$He + $^{208}$Pb reaction at 22 MeV, arguing instead that one-neutron (1n) stripping followed by decay of $^7$He is the dominant mechanism, with direct four-neutron (4n) stripping as the main source of $^4$He production. The revision is conditional on kinematic constraints that limit the allowed excitation energy of the $^{210}$Pb residual to $7 \leq E_\mathrm{x} \leq 13$ MeV, which when applied to distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations exclude 2n stripping as the main contributor.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the inclusive $^6$He and $^4$He production data from Marquínez-Durán et al. (2018) for 22 MeV $^8$He incident on a $^{208}$Pb target, using the two-dimensional total energy versus scattering angle spectra to impose kinematic constraints on the excitation energy of the residual nuclei. DWBA calculations were performed with the code FRESCO for 1n, 2n, and 4n stripping reactions, with the entrance channel potential taken from the earlier work and exit channel potentials derived from literature values for $^6$He + $^{208}$Pb (Sanchez-Benitez et al., 2008) and $^4$He + $^{208}$Pb (Hudson et al., 1976). The 1n stripping calculations used a double-folded real potential based on a theoretical $^7$He density from Neff (2004) with an "interior" Woods-Saxon imaginary potential.

## Key numbers

- The measured inclusive $^6$He total cross section is $871 \pm 31$ mb; the best fit assigns approximately 67% (about 583 mb) to 1n stripping, 16% (about 130 mb) to 2n stripping, and 17% to background
- The 2n stripping contribution is constrained to at most about one third of the total $^6$He cross section, with a lower limit of about 12%
- The measured inclusive $^4$He total cross section is $393^{+10}_{-33}$ mb; direct 4n stripping accounts for 73% (355 mb) of this total
- The optimum Q value for 2n stripping is $-0.8$ MeV, leading to preferential population of $^{210}$Pb states near $E_\mathrm{x} = 8$ MeV (close to the two-neutron binding energy of 9.1 MeV)
- The optimum Q value for 4n stripping is $-1.7$ MeV, preferentially populating $^{212}$Pb states around $E_\mathrm{x} = 16.7$ MeV
- The kinematic limits for 2n stripping allow $^{210}$Pb excitation energies in the range $7 \leq E_\mathrm{x} \leq 13$ MeV, with $E_\mathrm{x} \approx 10$ MeV most likely
- The kinematic limits for 4n stripping allow $^{212}$Pb excitation energies in the range $14 \leq E_\mathrm{x} \leq 22$ MeV
- The 2n stripping DWBA calculation for $E_\mathrm{x} = 10$ MeV and $L = 4\hbar$ peaks at $\theta_\mathrm{lab} \approx 84^\circ$, about $10^\circ$ larger than the measured $^6$He angular distribution peak

## Context

This work reexamines the data of Marquínez-Durán et al. (2018) for the $^8$He + $^{208}$Pb system, revising the earlier conclusion that 2n stripping dominated $^6$He production. The analysis extends earlier studies of multi-neutron transfer in $^8$He reactions, including the work of Tribble et al. (1978) on the $^{64}$Ni($^4$He,$^8$He)$^{60}$Ni reaction that suggested strong one-step 4n transfer, and the study by Lemasson et al. on $^8$He + $^{65}$Cu at Coulomb barrier energies. The results are consistent with the five-body cluster orbital shell model approximation (COSMA) calculations of Zhukov et al. (1994) for the $^8$He ground state, which predict configurations resembling a 4n cluster and a pair of 2n clusters. The finding that 2n stripping is relatively unimportant does not contradict predictions of a dineutron condensate component in $^8$He from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Hagino et al., 2008) and the alpha-dineutron condensate method (Kobayashi et al., 2013), as different reactions emphasize different structural aspects.
