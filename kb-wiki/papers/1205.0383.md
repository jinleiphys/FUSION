# Effective shell-model hamiltonians from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials within a perturbative approach

> This paper discusses the derivation of an effective shell-model hamiltonian starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential by way of perturbation theory. More precisely, we present the state of the art of this approach when the starting point is the perturbative expansion of the Q-box vertex function. Questions arising from diagrammatics, intermediate-states and order-by-order convergences, and their dependence on the chosen nucleon-nucleon potential, are discussed in detail, and the results of numerical applications for the p-shell model space starting from chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order potentials are shown. Moreover, an alternative graphical method to derive the effective hamiltonian, based on the Z-box vertex function recently introduced by Suzuki et al., is applied to the case of a non-degenerate (0+2) hbaromega model space. Finally, our shell-model results are compared with the exact ones obtained from no-core shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that realistic shell-model effective Hamiltonians derived from chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials via perturbative Q-box expansion can reproduce no-core shell-model results for p-shell nuclei, but the convergence and accuracy depend critically on the choice of intermediate-state space and the inclusion of folded-diagram contributions up to third order.

## Method

The authors derive an effective shell-model Hamiltonian by expanding the Q-box vertex function in irreducible valence-linked Goldstone diagrams up to third order in the interaction, then solving the decoupling equation using the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK), Lee-Suzuki (LS), and Z-box graphical methods. They apply this to the p-shell model space (0 hbar-omega) and a non-degenerate (0+2) hbar-omega psd model space, starting from chiral N3LO potentials, and compare the results with exact no-core shell-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- The KK and LS iterative methods produce effective Hamiltonian matrix elements that differ by at most a few keV.
- The Z-box method yields matrix elements that differ from the KK/LS results by no more than 80 keV.
- The N3LO potential used has a smooth Gaussian cutoff around 2.5 fm^{-1}.
- The Q-box diagrams include up to 3p-2h excitations for one-valence-nucleon systems and up to 4p-2h excitations for two-valence-nucleon systems.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of deriving realistic shell-model interactions from free nucleon-nucleon potentials using linked-diagram perturbation theory, as developed by Kuo and collaborators (Kuo, 1990) and reviewed by Hjorth-Jensen et al. (1995) and Coraggio et al. (2009). It specifically addresses convergence issues of the Q-box expansion and introduces the Z-box method of Suzuki et al. (2011) as an alternative to the standard KK and LS techniques. The results enable systematic benchmarking of perturbative effective interactions against ab initio no-core shell-model calculations for light nuclei, and provide a foundation for future inclusion of three-nucleon forces in the same framework.
