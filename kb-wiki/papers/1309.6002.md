# Assessing the adequacy of the bare optical potential in near-barrier fusion calculation

> We critically examine the differences among the different bare nuclear interactions used in near-barrier heavy ion fusion analysis and Coupled-Channels calculations, and discuss the possibility of extracting the barrier parameters of the bare potential from above-barrier data. We show that the choice of the bare potential may be critical for the analysis of the fusion cross sections. We show also that the barrier parameters taken from above barrier data may be very wrong.

## Key claim

The choice of bare nuclear potential in near-barrier heavy-ion fusion calculations critically affects the extracted barrier parameters and the conclusions about the importance of channel couplings; barrier parameters extracted from above-barrier fusion data using the classical 1/E linear fit can be very wrong, especially when channel coupling effects are present above the barrier.

## Method

The authors compared three bare potentials (Akyüz-Winther, Proximity, and São Paulo double-folding) by computing their Coulomb barrier parameters (height, radius, curvature) for several systems including ⁶He+²³⁸U, ⁴He+²⁰⁹Bi, and ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm. They performed single-channel and coupled-channels calculations of fusion cross sections using each potential, and compared the results with experimental data. They also tested the classical method of extracting barrier height and radius from the linear fit of fusion cross section versus 1/E at above-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He+²⁰⁹Bi, the barrier heights from the three potentials are: AW = 20.6 MeV, Prox = 19.3 MeV, SP = 21.2 MeV; barrier radii are: AW = 10.9 fm, Prox = 11.6 fm, SP = 10.6 fm
- For ⁶He+²³⁸U, the barrier heights are: AW = 21.6 MeV, Prox = 20.6 MeV, SP = 20.9 MeV; barrier radii are: AW = 11.6 fm, Prox = 12.1 fm, SP = 11.9 fm
- For ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm, the barrier height extracted from experimental data via the 1/E linear fit is 60.8 MeV, compared to the correct value of 61.4 MeV (a difference of 0.6 MeV)
- For ⁶He+²⁰⁹Bi, the barrier radius extracted from above-barrier data is 9.8 fm, which is approximately 2 fm smaller than the radii from all three bare potentials (AW = 11.3 fm, Prox = 11.8 fm, SP = 11.9 fm)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of bare potential choice in heavy-ion fusion, which was previously examined in the context of coupled-channels calculations (Canto et al., 2006). The paper directly compares the Akyüz-Winther, Proximity, and São Paulo potentials, showing that the São Paulo potential, based on realistic experimental densities, gives the best description of fusion data for ⁴He+²⁰⁹Bi. The results contradict the assumption that barrier parameters can be reliably extracted from above-barrier data as attempted by Kolata et al. (1998), and demonstrate that such extraction fails badly for halo nuclei like ⁶He due to breakup coupling effects. This enables future work to focus on using realistic densities and to avoid relying on the 1/E linear fit method for systems with strong channel couplings.
