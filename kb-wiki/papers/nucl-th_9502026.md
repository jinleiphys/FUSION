# Evidence for excess pions in nuclei

> We analize two reactions: the electroproduction at small x and the Drell-Yan production by protons on nuclei. We show that nuclear shadowing in electroproduction is larger if the virtuality of inter- mediate hadrons is properly taken into account. In the Drell-Yan production, initial state interactions of fast partons are included. With this input, the excess pion contribution improves the description of data for both reactions.

## Key claim

Nuclear shadowing in electroproduction is significantly larger than previously believed when the virtuality of intermediate hadrons is properly accounted for, and including the contribution of excess pions improves the description of both electroproduction and Drell-Yan data, contrary to earlier claims that excess pions are absent.

## Method

The author analyzes electroproduction at small $x$ using a generalized vector meson dominance (GVMD) model with Glauber theory, but replaces the total cross section with the inelastic cross section for virtual hadrons because the Fraunhofer diffraction condition fails. For Drell-Yan production by protons, initial state absorptive interactions of fast partons are included via a flux attenuation factor. The excess pion contribution is calculated using a renormalized pion distribution function constrained by the momentum sum rule, with the nucleon distribution determined from independent measurements of the average nucleon separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Average nucleon separation energy $\epsilon \geq 40$ MeV for heavy nuclei; the calculation uses $\epsilon = 35$ MeV.
- Fermi momentum $p_F = 230$ MeV/c.
- Number of excess pions per nucleon obtained: $n_\pi \approx 0.07$.
- For virtual mesons, $\sigma_{inel}^N \approx 5/6 \, \sigma_{tot}^N$.
- Cross section for the meson continuum: $\sigma^{N}_{inel} = 18 \text{ mb} \cdot \text{GeV}^2 / \mu^2$.
- Absorptive cross section for initial state partons: $\sigma_{abs}^q \approx 2$ mb.
- The excess pion contribution changes the structure function ratio $R$ by less than 2% between the two extreme choices of pion energy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the EMC effect by Akulinichev et al. (1991) and addresses the conclusion by several authors (e.g., Bickerstaff et al., 1984) that there are no excess pions in nuclei based on NMC electroproduction data and Drell-Yan data from Alde et al. (1990). The paper shows that when virtual hadron interactions are treated correctly and initial state interactions in Drell-Yan are included (following Akulinichev et al., 1994), the data are consistent with excess pions. This enables a unified description of nuclear structure functions at low $x$, Drell-Yan production, and $K^+$–$A$ scattering.
