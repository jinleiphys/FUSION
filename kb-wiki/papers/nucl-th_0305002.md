# Spin effects and baryon resonance dynamics in phi-meson photoproduction at few GeV

> The diffractive $ϕ$-meson photoproduction amplitude is dominated by the Pomeron exchange process and contains the terms that govern the spin-spin and spin-orbital interactions. We show that these terms are responsible for the spin-flip transitions at forward photoproduction angles and appear in the angular distributions of $ϕ\to K^+K^-$ -decay in reactions with unpolarized and polarized photon beams. At large momentum transfers, the main contribution to the $ϕ$-meson photoproduction is found to be due to the excitation of nucleon resonances. Combined analysis of $ω$ and $ϕ$ photoproduction indicates strong OZI-rule violation in $ϕNN^*$ - couplings. We also show that the spin observables are sensitive to the dynamics of $ϕ$-meson photoproduction at large angles and could help distinguish different theoretical models of nucleon resonances. Predictions for spin effects in $ϕ$-meson photoproduction are presented for future experimental tests.

## Key claim

The paper argues that spin-dependent terms in the Pomeron exchange amplitude, which are negligible at high energies, become important at a few GeV and are responsible for spin-flip transitions at forward photoproduction angles; at large momentum transfers, nucleon resonance excitations dominate and indicate strong OZI-rule violation in φNN* couplings, but the predictions for spin observables are presented as qualitative estimations due to potential double-counting issues in the interference model used.

## Method

The authors model φ-meson photoproduction using a Regge phenomenology approach for the diffractive region (Pomeron exchange plus pseudoscalar π,η exchange and possible f2' and glueball trajectories) combined with an effective Lagrangian approach for baryon and baryon-resonance exchange amplitudes, including 12 known nucleon resonances from the Particle Data Group. The spin-density matrix elements governing φ→K+K− decay angular distributions are calculated for unpolarized, linearly polarized, and circularly polarized photon beams in the Gottfried-Jackson system.

## Key numbers

- The Pomeron trajectory is αP(t) = 1.08 + 0.25t, with strength factor CP = 6g²√(4παem)/γV where 2γω = 17.05 and 2γφ = 13.13
- The Pomeron coupling parameters are βs = 1.61 and βu = 2.05 GeV⁻¹, giving gPqq = 4.1 and gPss = 3.22
- The pseudoscalar coupling constants used: gπNN = 13.26, gηNN = 3.527; gφγπ = -0.141, gφγη = -0.707
- The diffractive region is limited to |t| ≲ 0.5-0.7 GeV² for Eγ ≈ 2-3 GeV (s ≈ 5-7 GeV²)
- The f2' trajectory is αf2' = 0.55 + 0.7t with mass scale sf2' = 1 GeV²; the glueball trajectory has αgl(0) = -0.75 and αgl' = 0.25 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the Donnachie-Landshoff Pomeron exchange model (1984-1992) for vector meson photoproduction by analyzing spin properties at low energies, and builds on the authors' previous effective Lagrangian approach for ω-meson photoproduction (Lee and Titov, 2002) which included all known nucleon resonances. The paper provides predictions for spin observables that could distinguish between different theoretical models of nucleon resonances, such as the approach of Zhao (2001) which gives significantly different predictions for some spin observables, and aims to guide experimental tests at facilities like Jefferson Lab, LEPS at SPring-8, ELSA-SAPHIR at Bonn, and GRAAL at Grenoble.
