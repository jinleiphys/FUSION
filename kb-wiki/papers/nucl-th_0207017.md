# SU(3) versus deformed Hartree-Fock

> Deformation is fundamental to understanding nuclear structure. We compare two ways to efficiently realize deformation for many-fermion wavefunctions, the leading SU(3) irrep and the angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock state. In the absence of single-particle spin-orbit splitting the two are nearly identical. With realistic forces, however, the difference between the two is non-trivial, with the angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock state better approximating an ``exact'' wavefunction calculated in the fully interacting shell model. The difference is driven almost entirely by the single-particle spin-orbit splitting.

# SU(3) versus deformed Hartree-Fock

## Key claim
With realistic nuclear interactions that include single-particle spin-orbit splitting, the angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock state provides a significantly better approximation to the exact shell-model ground state than the leading SU(3) irrep; however, when the single-particle spin-orbit splitting is removed, the two approaches become nearly identical, indicating that spin-orbit splitting is the primary driver of the difference.

## Method
The authors compared two approximate many-fermion wavefunctions, the leading SU(3) irreducible representation and the angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock state, against exact shell-model diagonalization results for even-even nuclei in the sd shell (using the USD interaction) and the lower pf shell (using the KB3 interaction). They computed ground-state energies relative to the exact solution, wavefunction overlaps, and quadrupole deformation parameters (beta and gamma) for each approximation, both with and without single-particle spin-orbit splitting removed from the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- For $^{24}$Mg with USD interaction: projected Hartree-Fock energy difference from exact = 1.75 MeV, leading SU(3) energy difference = 7.97 MeV; with spin-orbit removed, PHF = 1.45 MeV and SU(3) = 2.42 MeV.
- For $^{32}$S with USD interaction: projected Hartree-Fock energy difference = 3.26 MeV, leading SU(3) energy difference = 10.07 MeV; with spin-orbit removed, PHF = 1.50 MeV and SU(3) = 2.36 MeV.
- For $^{44}$Ti with KB3 interaction: projected Hartree-Fock energy difference = 1.18 MeV, leading SU(3) energy difference = 6.46 MeV; with spin-orbit removed, PHF = 1.11 MeV and SU(3) = 2.54 MeV.
- Overlap of exact wavefunction with projected Hartree-Fock for $^{32}$S (USD): 0.847; with leading SU(3): 0.358; with spin-orbit removed, PHF overlap = 0.925 and SU(3) overlap = 0.865.
- Overlap between SU(3) and projected Hartree-Fock for $^{20}$Ne (USD-nls): 0.999; for $^{32}$S (USD): 0.171, increasing to 0.944 when spin-orbit is removed.
- Deformation beta for $^{44}$Ti: KB3 = 0.440, KB3-nls = 0.527, SU(3) = 0.556.

## Context
This work extends comparisons between algebraic SU(3) methods and mean-field approaches for nuclear structure, building on earlier studies by Gueorguiev et al (2000) who found that single-particle spin-orbit splitting causes significant mixing of SU(3) irreps in exact shell-model wavefunctions. The results serve as a caveat for the "mixed-mode" approach proposed by Gueorguiev et al (2002) that combines SU(3) irreps with spherical configurations, showing that projected Hartree-Fock may be a better starting point for realistic interactions. The paper also discusses computational tradeoffs: SU(3) provides analytic insight and is advantageous for multi-shell calculations via the symplectic model, while projected Hartree-Fock requires fewer Slater determinants for axial nuclei but faces difficulties with center-of-mass motion in multi-shell spaces.
