# Low-lying resonances of Be9Lambda : Faddeev calculation with Pade-approximants

> Configuration space Faddeev equations are applied to describe the Be9Lambda low-lying resonances of the ground band in the alpha+alpha+Lambda cluster model. The method of analytical continuation in coupling constant is used.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of a 2⁺₂ resonance state near the ααΛ threshold and 0⁺₂ and 4⁺₁ virtual states in ⁹_ΛBe, but these predictions depend on the choice of αΛ phenomenological potential and the orbital momentum configurations included; the simple ⁸Be(L⁺)+Λ(s-wave) classification for the ground band does not agree with the calculated results.

## Method

Configuration space Faddeev equations for the α+α+Λ cluster model were solved numerically using finite-difference methods and spline approximation. An auxiliary three-body potential with a variable strength parameter was added to convert resonance states into bound states; the energy trajectory as a function of this parameter was analytically continued onto the complex plane using Padé approximants to extract resonance energies and widths. Two phenomenological αΛ potentials (Gibson and Isle) were used, with the αα interaction described by the Ali-Bodmer potential including s, d, and g-wave components.

## Key numbers

- The 2⁺₂ resonance energy (from the ⁵_ΛHe+α threshold) for the Gibson potential is 3.83 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 4.32 MeV (N=4 Padé approximant)
- The 2⁺₂ resonance energy for the Isle potential is 3.09 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 3.26 MeV (N=4 Padé approximant)
- The Isle potential overbinds the ground state by approximately 2 MeV relative to the Gibson potential
- The three-body potential range parameter b = 0.1 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the method of analytical continuation in coupling constant, previously applied by Kukulin and collaborators (Kukulin and Krasnopolsky, 1977) and Filikhin et al. (2005), to the ⁹_ΛBe hypernucleus within the ααΛ cluster model of Hiyama and collaborators (2006). The results contradict the simple ⁸Be(L⁺)+Λ(s-wave) classification scheme proposed by Hiyama (2006), Motoba et al., and Bando, showing that higher partial waves significantly shift energy levels and that the 4⁺₁ state becomes virtual rather than resonant when maximal orbital configurations are included. This enables refined calculations of hypernuclear structure beyond the core-plus-s-wave approximation.
