# Few body impulse and fixed scatterer approximations for high energy scattering

> The elastic scattering differential cross section is calculated for proton scattering from $^6$He at 717 MeV, using single scattering terms of the multiple scattering expansion of the total transition amplitude (MST). We analyse the effects of different scattering frameworks, specifically the Factorized Impulse Approximation (FIA) and the Fixed Scatterer (adiabatic) Approximation (FSA) and the uncertainties associated with the use different structure models.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for proton scattering from \(^6\)He at 717 MeV, the Factorized Impulse Approximation (FIA) and the Fixed Scatterer (adiabatic) Approximation (FSA) produce essentially identical elastic scattering differential cross sections in the high-energy limit, but only when the momentum transfers probed are constrained by the rms radius of the halo cluster.

## Method

The authors calculate elastic scattering differential cross sections for p+\(^6\)He at 717 MeV using the single-scattering term of the multiple scattering expansion of the total transition amplitude (MST). They compare several factorized impulse approximations (Kujawski-Lambert, Rihan, Crespo-Johnson, and projected on-energy shell) against the fixed scatterer (adiabatic) approximation, using different structure models for the \(^6\)He target described as a three-body (\(\alpha\)+n+n) system.

## Key numbers

- Proton scattering from \(^6\)He at 717 MeV incident energy
- The momentum transfers studied "still essentially probe only that part of the few-body dynamics of the halo cluster which is constrained by the rms radius"
- For the 3-body case, the Kujawski-Lambert approximation gives projectile-subsystem relative momentum \(\vec{\cal Q}_{12} = (\mu_{12}/\mu_{1,23})\vec{k}_1\) and energy \(\omega_{12} = (\mu_{12}/\mu_{1,23})E\)
- For the Rihan approximation, the energy parameter includes a factor \(\widehat{\cal C}_{12}\) which for small scattering angles satisfies \(\widehat{\cal C}_{12} \sim 1\)
- The FSA uses the total energy \(E = \hbar^2 k_1^2 / (2\mu_{1,23})\) with reduced mass \(\mu_{1,23}\) rather than the two-body reduced mass

## Context

This work extends the multiple scattering expansion (MST) formalism of Goldberger and Watson (1964) and Watson (1957) to halo nuclei, specifically comparing the factorized impulse approximation (FIA) variants of Kujawski and Lambert (1973), Rihan (1977, 1996), and Crespo and Johnson (1999, 2002) with the fixed scatterer (adiabatic) approximation. The calculations are applied to \(^6\)He at 717 MeV where new data at higher transferred momentum have been obtained. The results enable future application of these approximations to other halo nuclei such as \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{11}\)Li, and clarify which approximations are valid for extracting structural information from elastic scattering at these energies.
