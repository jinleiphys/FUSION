# Regularities with random interactions in energy centroids defined by group symmetries

> Regular structures generated by random interactions in energy centroids defined over irreducible representations (irreps) of some of the group symmetries of the interacting boson models $sd$IBM, $sdg$IBM, $sd$IBM-$T$ and $sd$IBM-$ST$ are studied by deriving trace propagations equations for the centroids. It is found that, with random interactions, the lowest and highest group irreps in general carry most of the probability for the corresponding centroids to be lowest in energy. This generalizes the result known earlier, via numerical diagonalization, for the more complicated fixed spin ($J$) centroids where simple trace propagation is not possible.

## Key claim

The paper shows that with random interactions, the lowest and highest group irreducible representations (irreps) of the subalgebra \(G_2\) in the chain \(G_1 \supset G_2\) carry most of the probability for the corresponding energy centroids to be lowest in energy, generalizing a result previously known only from numerical diagonalization for fixed-spin (\(J\)) centroids where simple trace propagation is not possible.

## Method

The author derives trace propagation equations for energy centroids over irreps of group symmetries of interacting boson models (IBM), specifically for \(SU(3)\) in \(sd\)IBM, fixed-\((m_s,m_d,v_d,m_g,v_g)\) configurations in \(sdg\)IBM, and for \(SU_T(3)\) and \(U_{ST}(6)\) symmetries in \(sd\)IBM-\(T\) and \(sd\)IBM-\(ST\). These equations express the centroid for any boson number \(m\) and irrep labels as linear combinations of centroids for \(m \leq 2\), using Casimir invariants of the relevant groups. The centroids are then evaluated for ensembles of 1000 random Hamiltonians with independent Gaussian two-body matrix elements (zero center, unit variance) to compute probabilities for each irrep's centroid to be lowest in energy.

## Key numbers

* For \(sd\)IBM \(SU(3)\) centroids: the lowest \(SU(3)\) irreps \((00)\), \((20)\), and \((02)\) (for \(m=3k\), \(3k+1\), \(3k+2\) respectively) are lowest in energy with 50% probability, and the highest irrep \((2m,0)\) is lowest with 50% probability.
* For \(sdg\)IBM at \(m=15\): the four configurations \((m_s,m_d=v_d=m-m_s,m_g=v_g=0)\), \((m_s,m_d=m-m_s,v_d=\pi(m_d),m_g=v_g=0)\), \((m_s,m_d=v_d=0,m_g=v_g=m-m_s)\), and \((m_s,m_d=v_d=0,m_g=m-m_s,v_g=\pi(m_g))\) exhaust about 91% of the probability for being lowest in energy.
* For \(sd\)IBM-\(T\) with \(m=3k\): the lowest \(SU_T(3)\) irrep's centroid is lowest with approximately 35% probability, while the highest irrep's centroid is lowest with approximately 60% probability (split into about 30% each for the lowest and highest isospin \(T\)).
* For \(sd\)IBM-\(ST\): the centroid of the highest \(U_{ST}(6)\) irrep is lowest with approximately 56% probability, splitting into about 34% and 22% for the highest and lowest \(O_{ST}(6)\) irreps.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of regularities from random interactions, such as the spin-0 ground state dominance found by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) in the nuclear shell model and the vibrational/rotational structures found by Bijker and Frank (2000) in \(sd\)IBM. It generalizes the numerical results of Zhao et al. (2002, 2004a) for fixed-\(L\) and fixed-\(J\) centroids to group-theoretical symmetries higher than angular momentum, where trace propagation via Casimir invariants is possible. The derived propagation equations enable analytic understanding of centroid regularities without constructing many-body Hamiltonian matrices, and the author suggests they may be useful for quantifying geometric chaos, a concept introduced by Zelevinsky et al. (2000).
