# SIDIS at small $x$ at next-to-leading order: gluon contribution

> We calculate the contribution of gluons to single inclusive hadron production at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small $x$ using the Color Glass Condensate formalism. It is shown that the only divergence present is the standard collinear divergence which is absorbed into evolution of gluon-hadron fragmentation function. Our calculations are performed at finite $N_c$ and we provide general finite $N_c$ expressions for the structure of Wilson lines appearing in inclusive dihadron and single hadron production cross sections. We also comment on how one can obtain rapidity distribution of hadron multiplicities from our results.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the contribution of gluons to single inclusive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, showing that the only divergence present is the standard collinear divergence which can be absorbed into the scale evolution of the gluon-hadron fragmentation function; the calculation is performed at finite \(N_c\) rather than in the large \(N_c\) approximation, and the results are given for longitudinal photon exchange only, with the transverse photon case deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors start from the NLO corrections to dihadron production derived in Bergabo (2022) and "undo" the integration over the final-state gluon phase space, then integrate out the final-state quark and antiquark to leave only the produced gluon. The resulting partonic cross sections are expressed in coordinate space in terms of dipole and quadrupole Wilson line correlators encoding multiple scatterings on the target. The collinear divergence appearing in the terms where the gluon is radiated after the quark scatters (Eqs. (1×1) and (2×2)) is isolated using dimensional regularization and absorbed into the bare gluon-hadron fragmentation function via the quark-gluon splitting function \(P_{gq}(\xi) = C_F [1+(1-\xi)^2]/\xi\), yielding a renormalized fragmentation function \(D_{h/q}(z_h, \mu^2)\) that includes both quark-quark and quark-gluon splitting channels.

## Key numbers

* The quark-gluon splitting function is given as \(P_{gq}(\xi) = C_F \, \frac{1 + (1 - \xi)^2}{\xi}\), where \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\).
* The collinear divergence is regulated in dimensional regularization, producing a pole \(1/\varepsilon\) and a finite term \(-\log(\pi e^{\gamma_E} \mu |\mathbf{x}_{1'1}|)\).
* The LO single inclusive quark production cross section (Eq. (LOdsig-sidis-quark)) contains an overall factor \(e^2 Q^2 N_c/(2\pi)^5\).
* The NLO gluon contribution terms (Eqs. (1×1) through (2×4)) each carry an overall factor \(4 e^2 Q^2 g^2 N_c/(2\pi)^6\).
* The final renormalized cross section for the collinear-divergent part is written with an overall factor \(8 e^2 Q^2 N_c/(2\pi)^5\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of single inclusive hadron production in DIS at small \(x\) from Bergabo and Jalilian-Marian (2022), which considered hadronization of a quark or antiquark, by now including the gluon hadronization channel that first appears at NLO. The results are complementary to the single inclusive jet production calculation in DIS by Caucal, Salazar, and collaborators (2024). The finite \(N_c\) expressions for Wilson line structures provided in the appendix generalize the large \(N_c\) results of Bergabo (2022) and enable future phenomenological studies at the Electron Ion Collider (EIC), where the interplay between saturation effects and Sudakov logarithms (when the hadron transverse momentum is much smaller than the photon virtuality \(Q\)) must be understood.
