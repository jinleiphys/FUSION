# Effective-range function methods for charged particle collisions

> Different versions of the effective-range function method for charged particle collisions are studied and compared. In addition, a novel derivation of the standard effective-range function is presented from the analysis of Coulomb wave functions in the complex plane of the energy. The recently proposed effective-range function denoted as $Δ_\ell$ [Phys. Rev. C 96, 034601 (2017)] and an earlier variant [Hamilton et al., Nucl. Phys. B 60, 443 (1973)] are related to the standard function. The potential interest of $Δ_\ell$ for the study of low-energy cross sections and weakly bound states is discussed in the framework of the proton-proton ${}^1S_0$ collision. The resonant state of the proton-proton collision is successfully computed from the extrapolation of $Δ_\ell$ instead of the standard function. It is shown that interpolating $Δ_\ell$ can lead to useful extrapolation to negative energies, provided scattering data are known below one nuclear Rydberg energy (12.5 keV for the proton-proton system). This property is due to the connection between $Δ_\ell$ and the effective-range function by Hamilton et al. that is discussed in detail. Nevertheless, such extrapolations to negative energies should be used with caution because $Δ_\ell$ is not analytic at zero energy. The expected analytic properties of the main functions are verified in the complex energy plane by graphical color-based representations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the reduced effective-range function Δℓ can be used to extrapolate scattering data to negative energies (e.g., to find bound or resonant states) only when data are known below one nuclear Rydberg energy (12.5 keV for proton-proton), and even then such extrapolations must be used with caution because Δℓ is not analytic at zero energy.

## Method

The authors derive the analytic structure of Coulomb wave functions in the complex energy plane, factorizing the singular energy dependence from regularized functions. They then relate three versions of the effective-range function: the standard Coulomb-modified function, the reduced function Δℓ from Sparenberg (2017), and an earlier variant Δ⁺ℓ from Hamilton et al. (1973). The properties of these functions are verified using graphical color-based complex-plane representations, and the method is applied to the proton-proton ¹S₀ collision to test extrapolation to negative energies.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear Bohr radius for a two-proton system is 57.64 fm.
- One nuclear Rydberg energy equals 12.49 keV for the proton-proton system and 10.72 MeV for the ¹²C+α system.
- The nuclear Bohr radius for a ¹²C+α system is 0.806 fm, nearly one hundred times smaller than for proton-proton.
- The digamma function ψ(z) has a unique zero on the positive real axis at z = 1.4616.

## Context

This work extends the effective-range function method originally developed by Bethe (1949) and Landau (1944) for charged-particle collisions, and builds on the reduced effective-range function Δℓ proposed by Sparenberg (2017). It clarifies the connection between Δℓ and the earlier variant by Hamilton et al. (1973), and provides a novel derivation of the standard effective-range function from complex-plane analysis of Coulomb wave functions. The results enable improved extrapolation of scattering data to negative energies for studying weakly bound states and resonances, particularly in light systems like proton-proton, while warning that the method becomes less reliable for heavier systems where the nuclear Bohr radius is much smaller.
