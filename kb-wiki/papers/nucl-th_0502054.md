# In-matter three-body problem

> We formulate three-dimensional equations for the finite temperature in-matter three-body problem. Our approach takes into account the full infinite series for the effective pair-interaction kernel, so that all possible two-body sub-processes allowed by the underlying Hamiltonian are retained.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact three-dimensional equations for the finite-temperature in-matter three-body problem that retain the full infinite series for the effective pair-interaction kernel, in contrast to prior work which approximated this kernel by terms linear in the physical two-body potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the four-dimensional Green function for three identical particles at finite density and temperature, using the real-time formulation of perturbation theory with doubled degrees of freedom. They implement a three-dimensional reduction by equating times in initial and final states, defining a two-time Green function. To handle the non-invertibility of the equal-time disconnected Green function in the many-body case, they introduce modified Green functions with a fully disconnected operator. They then show that the exact three-body equal-time disconnected t matrix is simply related to the four-dimensional t matrix, bypassing the need to sum the infinite series for the quasi-potential.

## Key numbers

* No explicit numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal theoretical derivation.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature equal-time formulation of Schuck, Villars, and Ring (Schuck et al.) by incorporating the full infinite series for the effective pair-interaction kernel, which the earlier work approximated linearly. The authors note that the linear approximation is unsatisfactory for strong coupling cases such as when two-body bound states are possible. The derived exact relation between the three-dimensional and four-dimensional t matrices enables practical three-dimensional calculations for in-matter three-body problems, including studies of bound-state modification in nuclear matter and color superconductivity in quark matter.
