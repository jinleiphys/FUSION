# Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham Sum Rules for Proton, Neutron and Deuteron

> A description of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rules for proton, neutron and deuteron is suggested. The proton consideration confirms the earlier conjecture, that the structure function $g_T$ features a smooth $Q^2-$dependence, while the structure function $g_2$ is changing rapidly, due to the elastic contribution to the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule. The neutron case features the close connection to the Bjorken sum rule. The deuteron case reveals the crucial role of photodesintegration channel, resulting in the strong nuclear correction at very low $Q^2$. The new approximate sum rules for this channel reflecting the delicate interplay between nucleon and nuclear structure are suggested.

# Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham Sum Rules for Proton, Neutron and Deuteron

## Key claim

The paper argues that the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rules for proton, neutron, and deuteron can be described through the interplay of the Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rule and the small binding energy approximation (SBEA), with the crucial caveat that the smooth interpolation for the neutron case fails because the neutron's anomalous magnetic moment is zero, requiring instead a parameterization of the proton-neutron difference.

## Method

The author decomposes the generalized GDH integral \(I_1(Q^2)\) into contributions from the structure functions \(g_T = g_1 + g_2\) and \(g_2\), using the BC sum rule to relate the elastic contribution of \(g_2\) to nucleon form factors. For the proton, a smooth interpolation for \(I_T(Q^2)\) is constructed between the real-photon limit and the perturbative QCD region. For the neutron, the isovector combination \(I_T^{p-n}\) is parameterized instead. For the deuteron, the small binding energy approximation is applied, separating the photodesintegration channel from the inelastic channel, leading to new finite-energy sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The crossover point for the proton GDH integral occurs at \(Q^2 \sim 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\), below the resonance region.
- The transition scale for the proton is \(Q_0^2 = (16M^2/\mu_A) \Gamma_1^p \sim 1\ \text{GeV}^2\), where \(\Gamma_1^p = \int_0^1 g_1^p(x) dx\) is given by world average proton data.
- The transition scale for the isovector combination is \(Q_1^2 \sim 1.3\ \text{GeV}^2\), of the same order as the proton case.
- The small binding energy parameter for the deuteron is \(\beta = 2M\epsilon / m_\pi^2 \sim 10\%\), where \(\epsilon\) is the deuteron binding energy and \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
- The photodesintegration channel sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(\int_{\nu_{PD}}^{\nu_\pi} \frac{d\nu}{\nu} \Delta\sigma_d(\nu) = -\frac{8\pi^2}{M^2} \mu_A^p \mu_A^n\).
- The neutron magnetic form factor is described by the dipole formula \(G_M(Q^2) = 1/(1+Q^2/0.71)^2\) with \(Q^2\) in \(\text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier predictions by Teryaev and collaborators (1993, 1995) for the proton GDH sum rule to the neutron and deuteron cases. The neutron treatment connects to the Bjorken sum rule, while the deuteron analysis builds on an early estimate by Gerasimov and more recent relativistic calculations by Arenhövel. The paper enables experimental checks of the photodesintegration sum rules at JLAB and TUNL, and suggests that the small binding energy approximation provides a controlled framework for nuclear corrections down to \(Q^2 \sim m_\pi^2\).
