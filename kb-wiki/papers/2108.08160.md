# Bottomonium suppression and flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The strong suppression of bottomonia production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is a smoking gun for the creation of a deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In this proceedings contribution, I review recent work that aims to provide a more comprehensive and systematic understanding of bottomonium dynamics in the QGP through the use of pNRQCD and an open quantum systems approach. This approach allows one to evolve the heavy-quarkonium reduced density matrix, taking into account non-unitary effective Hamiltonian evolution of the wave-function and quantum jumps between different angular momentum and color states. In the case of a strong coupled QGP in which E << T,m_D << 1/a_0, the corresponding evolution equation is Markovian and can therefore be mapped to a Lindblad evolution equation. To solve the resulting Lindblad equation, we make use of a stochastic unraveling called the quantum trajectories algorithm and couple the non-abelian quantum evolution to a realistic 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical background. Using a large number of Monte-Carlo sampled bottomonium trajectories, we make predictions for bottomonium R_AA and elliptic flow as a function of centrality and transverse momentum and compare to data collected by the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations.

# Bottomonium suppression and flow in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Bottomonium suppression in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be described from first principles using potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and an open quantum systems approach, provided the hierarchy of scales satisfies \(E_{\rm bind} \ll T, m_D \ll 1/a_0\) (strongly coupled QGP regime), which makes the evolution Markovian and of Lindblad form. The approach reproduces experimental data on \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow within current theoretical and experimental uncertainties, but the results depend on the transport coefficients \(\kappa\) and \(\gamma\), which are not fully constrained.

## Method
The authors solve the Lindblad equation for the heavy-quarkonium reduced density matrix using a quantum trajectories algorithm (implemented in the open-source code QTraj), which maps the 3D non-abelian evolution to an ensemble of independent 1D Schrodinger equation evolutions subject to stochastic color and angular momentum transitions (quantum jumps). The quantum evolution is coupled to a realistic 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical background provided by the aHydro3p1 code, which uses quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics with a lattice-based equation of state. The transport coefficients \(\kappa\) and \(\gamma\) are constrained by lattice QCD measurements, and the survival probabilities are combined with a feed-down matrix constructed from measured branching ratios and cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The lattice spacing and time steps require \(N \sim 4000\) lattice points and \(l_{\rm max} \sim 5\) for reliable computation; direct Lindblad evolution would scale in memory as \((l_{\rm max}+1)^2 N^2\) and per-step time as \((l_{\rm max}+1)^4 N^4\).
- The transport coefficient \(\kappa\) is varied over three temperature-dependent parameterizations: \(\hat\kappa(T) = \kappa(T)/T^3 \in \{ \hat\kappa_L(T), \hat\kappa_C(T), \hat\kappa_U(T) \}\) using results from Brambilla (2020).
- The transport coefficient \(\gamma\) is varied over three values: \(\hat{\gamma} = \gamma / T^{3} = \{-3.5, -1.75, 0\}\) from lattice extractions by Brambilla (2019), Kim (2018), Aarts (2011), Larsen (2019), and Shi (2021).
- Predictions are made for bottomonium \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) in 5 TeV Pb-Pb collisions and compared to data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations.

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD and open quantum systems framework developed by Brambilla (2016, 2017, 2020, 2021) to make phenomenological predictions for bottomonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions. It applies the quantum trajectories algorithm, originally developed for quantum optics by Dalibard (1992) and Molmer (1993), to the non-abelian QCD problem of heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma. The results enable future extraction of the transport coefficient \(\gamma\) from double ratio measurements of \(\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)\) and \(\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)\), and motivate inclusion of next-to-leading order corrections in \(E/T\) to assess convergence of the high-temperature expansion and incorporate drag/recoil effects.
