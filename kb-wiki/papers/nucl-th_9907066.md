# Reconstruction of the Proton Source in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We describe a direct method to reconstruct the transverse proton source formed in a relativistic heavy ion collision, making use of experimentally measured proton and deuteron spectra and assuming that deuterons are formed via two-nucleon coalescence. We show that an ambiguity with respect to the source temperature still persists and we indicate a possible solution to the problem.

# Reconstruction of the Proton Source in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a direct method to reconstruct the transverse proton source from measured proton and deuteron spectra using two-nucleon coalescence, but shows that an ambiguity in the source temperature persists and cannot be resolved from these spectra alone. The authors indicate that including heavier clusters such as tritons, or proton-proton correlations, may resolve this temperature ambiguity.

## Method
The authors reconstruct the proton phase-space distribution by inverting measured proton and deuteron invariant momentum spectra from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS, using a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion model with a transverse collective velocity profile. They apply the coalescence model to relate deuteron and proton phase-space distributions, then derive a first-order differential equation that yields the transverse velocity profile and local proton density through numerical integration of the extracted auxiliary functions. The procedure is tested on NA44 collaboration data, and the authors extend the analysis to triton production using an improved coalescence model with modified statistical prefactors that account for sequential cluster formation processes.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-to-proton ratio in the source: \( R_{np} = 1.2 \)
- Freeze-out proper time fixed at \( \tau_0 = 10 \) fm/c
- Temperature range explored: \( T_0 = 50 \) MeV to \( T_0 = 150 \) MeV
- Statistical prefactor for deuterons: \( g_d = 3/8 \)
- Statistical prefactor for tritons (improved model): \( g_t = 1/3 \), enhancing yield by a factor of 4 compared to the conventional value of \( 1/12 \)
- Statistical prefactor for \( ^4 \)He (improved model): \( g_{^4\text{He}} = 13/48 \), enhancing yield by a factor of 26 compared to the conventional value of \( 1/96 \)

## Context
This work extends the coalescence-based description of deuteron production from Polleri et al. (1998) by providing a direct inversion method to extract the proton source properties rather than relying on parametric fits. The reconstructed shell-like density structures at high temperature are consistent with analytic solutions of non-relativistic hydrodynamics from Csernai (1998). The paper enables future experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider to constrain source temperatures by combining single-particle and composite spectra with proton-proton correlation measurements, as suggested by similar work from Schlei and Heiselberg (1999) using pion-pion correlations.
