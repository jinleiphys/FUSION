# Sigma Decay at Finite Temperature and Density

> Sigma decay and its relation with chiral phase transition are discussed at finite temperature and density in the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The decay rate for the process sigma -> 2 pions to first order in a 1/N_c expansion is calculated as a function of temperature T and baryon density n_b. In particular, only when the chiral phase transition happens around the tricritical point, the sigma decay results in a non-thermal enhancement of pions in the final state distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Sigma Decay at Finite Temperature and Density

## Key claim
The sigma meson decay into two pions can produce a non-thermal enhancement of pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but only when the chiral phase transition occurs around the tricritical point (or critical end-point) in the temperature-density plane; if the decay happens in low density or low temperature regions far from this point, the sigma decay begins with maximum rate at or near the freeze-out and no significant non-thermal enhancement occurs.

## Method
The authors calculated the sigma-to-two-pion decay rate in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using a 1/N_c expansion to first order, with the Hartree approximation for quarks and the RPA approximation for mesons. The decay rate was evaluated as a function of temperature T and baryon density n_b by computing the sigma-pi-pi coupling strength and the phase-space factor, including Bose-Einstein statistics for the final-state pions, with model parameters fixed by vacuum values of the dynamical quark mass (0.32 GeV), pion decay constant (0.093 GeV), and pion mass (0.134 GeV).

## Key numbers
- Vacuum sigma decay rate: approximately 0.09 GeV
- Maximum decay rate at finite temperature: approximately 0.11 GeV at T ≈ 0.17 GeV (zero baryon density)
- Threshold temperature for sigma decay (where m_sigma = 2 m_pi): T ≈ 0.19 GeV
- Vacuum sigma-pi-pi coupling strength: approximately 2 GeV
- At T = 0, the mixing phase of the first-order chiral transition lies in the density region 0.33 < n_b/n_0 < 1.56, where n_0 is normal nuclear matter density
- At T = 0.02 GeV, the mixing region narrows to 0.59 < n_b/n_0 < 1.44
- The critical end-point E of the first-order transition occurs at T ≈ 0.03 GeV
- Model parameters: coupling constant G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}, momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.653 GeV, current quark mass m_0 = 0.005 GeV

## Context
This work extends the study of sigma decay at finite temperature and density, previously investigated at zero density by Hatsuda and Kunihiro, using the NJL model which is believed to describe chiral properties well. The paper builds on the expectation from Rajagopal that sigma decay after freeze-out could produce a non-thermal pion enhancement, and on earlier work by Rapp and others showing that the chiral phase transition in the chiral limit is second order at high temperature and first order at high density, with a tricritical point in the T-n_b plane. The results enable experimental searches for the critical end-point in relativistic heavy ion collisions by identifying the specific freeze-out conditions under which sigma decay produces a detectable non-thermal pion signal at low transverse momentum.
