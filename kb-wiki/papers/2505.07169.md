# Enhancement of photon emission rate near QCD critical point

> We compute photon emission rate enhancement near the QCD critical point using an effective theory of dynamic critical phenomena and derive a universal photon spectrum. The emission rate scales similarly to conductivity, increasing with the correlation length ($ξ$), diverging at the critical point. The spectrum exhibits $ωdN_γ/d^3k \propto ω^{-1/2}$ in the scaling regime, with the transition occurring at a frequency comparable to shear damping rate $ω\sim γ_η/ξ^2$, reflecting the nonequilibrium properties of the near-critical liquid.

## Key claim

The photon emission rate near the QCD critical point diverges as the correlation length increases, but this divergence is regularized at frequencies below the shear damping rate; the universal scaling function derived applies only for photon frequencies much smaller than the temperature, and the result depends on an unknown overall coefficient that cannot be fixed by universality alone.

## Method

The authors used the effective theory of dynamic critical phenomena known as model H in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification, which describes the coupling between the diffusive entropy mode and the transverse shear mode near the QCD critical point. They computed the photon emission rate at one-loop order in thermal fluctuations by evaluating the transverse electric current correlation function for lightlike momenta, using the Langevin formulation of model H with the appropriate fluctuation-dissipation relations. The sound mode contribution was evaluated separately and shown to be subleading in the scaling regime.

## Key numbers

* The photon spectrum in the scaling regime exhibits $\omega dN_{\gamma}/d^3k \propto \omega^{-1/2}$ for frequencies $\gamma_{\eta}/\xi^2 \ll \omega \ll c_s^2/\gamma_{\eta}$, where $\gamma_{\eta} = \bar\eta/w$ is the shear diffusion coefficient and $\xi$ is the correlation length.
* The transition between the Ohmic regime and the scaling regime occurs at $\omega \sim \gamma_{\eta}/\xi^2$, where the emission rate saturates to a value proportional to $\xi/\gamma_{\eta}$.
* The universal scaling function $\Phi(\nu)$ has asymptotic forms $\Phi(0)=1$ and $\Phi(\nu\gg 1)\simeq 1/\sqrt{2\nu}$, with $\nu = \omega\xi^2/\gamma_{\eta}$.
* The critical enhancement factor relative to normal QGP photon emission is estimated as $\xi T \sim 3$ for $\xi\sim 3$ fm and $T\sim 1$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of critical signatures in heavy-ion collisions beyond fluctuation observables (Stephanov 1998, Stephanov 1999) to electromagnetic probes, which escape the medium without further interaction. It corrects earlier model calculations by Nishimura (2023, 2024) that predicted dilepton divergences using a different critical behavior than model H, which is the correct universal description for the QCD critical point (Son 2004). The result enables a direct theoretical prediction for soft photon spectra that could be compared with experimental data from the RHIC Beam-Energy Scan program and future facilities, though the absolute magnitude remains uncertain due to unknown non-universal coefficients.
