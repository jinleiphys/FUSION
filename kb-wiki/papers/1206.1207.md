# Causality bounds for neutron-proton scattering

> We consider the constraints of causality and unitarity for the low-energy interactions of protons and neutrons. We derive a general theorem that non-vanishing partial-wave mixing cannot be reproduced with zero-range interactions without violating causality or unitarity. We define and calculate interaction length scales which we call the causal range and the Cauchy-Schwarz range for all spin channels up to J = 3. For some channels we find that these length scales are as large as 5 fm. We investigate the origin of these large lengths and discuss their significance for the choice of momentum cutoff scales in effective field theory and universality in many-body Fermi systems.

## Key claim

Non-vanishing partial-wave mixing in neutron-proton scattering cannot be reproduced with zero-range interactions without violating causality or unitarity; this is a general theorem derived from the observation that the causality bound for the lower partial-wave effective range is driven to negative infinity as the interaction range goes to zero when the mixing parameter \(q_0\) is nonzero.

## Method

The authors derived causality bounds for coupled partial-wave channels using Wronskian identities applied to the radial Schrodinger equation for spin-triplet scattering with mixing between orbital angular momentum \(L=J-1\) and \(L=J+1\). They defined the causal range \(R^b\) from the effective range bound and the Cauchy-Schwarz range \(R^{\text{C-S}}\) from an inequality relating the coupled-channel wave functions, then computed these length scales for all neutron-proton spin channels up to \(J=3\) using low-energy threshold parameters from the NijmII and Reid93 potentials.

## Key numbers

- For some channels, the causal range and Cauchy-Schwarz range are as large as 5 fm.
- The \(^{3}S_1\) channel scattering length is 5.418 fm (NijmII) and 5.422 fm (Reid93); the effective range is 1.7531 fm (NijmII) and 1.7554 fm (Reid93).
- The mixing parameter \(q_0\) for \(\varepsilon_1\) is 0.303987 fm\(^2\) (NijmII) and 0.303394 fm\(^2\) (Reid93); \(q_1\) is -2.00228 fm\(^4\) (NijmII) and -1.99129 fm\(^4\) (Reid93).
- For the \(^{3}D_3\)-\(^{3}G_3\) coupled channel, the \(^{3}D_3\) effective range is 288.428 fm\(^{-5}\) (NijmII) and 198.528 fm\(^{-5}\) (Reid93); the \(^{3}G_3\) scattering length is 648.813 fm\(^{7}\) (NijmII) and 534.594 fm\(^{7}\) (Reid93).
- The \(^{1}S_0\) scattering length is -23.727 fm (NijmII) and -23.735 fm (Reid93); the effective range is 2.670 fm (NijmII) and 2.753 fm (Reid93).

## Context

This work extends the causality bounds derived by Wigner (1954) for finite-range interactions and the effective range bounds for arbitrary angular momentum by Hammer and Lee to the case of coupled partial-wave channels. The results show that the large causal ranges (up to 5 fm) in higher partial waves are generated by the one-pion exchange tail, and the authors demonstrate using a potential model that these ranges are significantly reduced when the one-pion exchange tail is chopped off beyond 5 fm. This finding has implications for the choice of momentum cutoff scales in effective field theory and for universality in many-body Fermi systems, particularly for dilute neutron matter relevant to neutron star crusts.
