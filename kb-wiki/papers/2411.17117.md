# Effect of chiral imbalance on the electrical conductivity of hot and dense quark matter using Green-Kubo Method within the 2-flavour gauged NJL model

> The electrical conductivity of hot and dense quark matter is calculated using the 2-flavour gauged Nambu-Jona--Lasinio (NJL) model in the presence of a chiral imbalance quantified in terms of a chiral chemical potential (CCP). To this end, the in-medium spectral function corresponding to the vector current correlator is evaluated employing the real time formulation of finite temperature field theory. Taking the long wavelength limit of the spectral function we extract the electrical conductivity using the Green-Kubo relation. The thermal widths of the quarks/antiquarks that appear in the expression of electrical conductivity are calculated by considering the $2\to2$ scattering in the NJL model. The scattering amplitudes containing the polarization functions of the mesonic modes in the scalar and pseudoscalar channels are also evaluated by considering finite value of CCP. We find that the ratio of electrical conductivity to temperature has significant dependence on CCP especially in the low temperature region.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of hot and dense quark matter shows significant dependence on the chiral chemical potential (CCP), particularly in the low temperature region, when calculated using the one-loop Green-Kubo formalism within the 2-flavour gauged NJL model, with the caveat that the thermal widths of quarks/antiquarks are introduced by hand to avoid pinch singularities and are calculated from 2-to-2 scattering processes.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium spectral function for the vector current correlator using the real time formulation of finite temperature field theory, then extracted the electrical conductivity via the Green-Kubo relation in the long wavelength limit. The thermal widths of quarks and antiquarks, which serve as the dynamical input to avoid pinch singularities in the one-loop formalism, were computed from 2-to-2 scattering amplitudes that incorporate polarization functions of mesonic modes in the scalar and pseudoscalar channels at finite chiral chemical potential. The constituent quark mass was obtained by solving the gap equation in the mean field approximation with a smooth three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electrical conductivity in hot and dense quark matter using NJL-like models (Lang 2013, Lang 2015, Iwasaki 2007, Alberico 2007, Ghosh 2015) by incorporating a chiral chemical potential to account for parity and CP violation from topological gluon configurations. It also builds on earlier investigations of the electromagnetic spectral function (Ghosh 2022), dilepton production rates (Chaudhuri 2022), and mesonic modes (Ghosh 2023) in chirally imbalanced media. The results enable future studies of charge transport in heavy-ion collisions where local chiral imbalance may coexist with strong electromagnetic fields, and may inform phenomenological modeling of the chiral magnetic effect.
