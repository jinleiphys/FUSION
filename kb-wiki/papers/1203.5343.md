# Two-dimensional chiral crystals in the NJL model

> We investigate the phase structure of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at zero temperature, allowing for a two-dimensional spatial dependence of the chiral condensate. Applying the mean-field approximation, we consider various periodic structures with rectangular and hexagonal geometries, and minimize the corresponding free energy. We find that these two-dimensional chiral crystals are favored over homogeneous phases in a certain window in the region where the phase transition would take place when the analysis was restricted to homogeneous condensates. It turns out, however, that in this regime they are disfavored against a phase with a one-dimensional modulation of the chiral condensate. On the other hand, we find that square and hexagonal lattices eventually get favored at higher chemical potentials. Although stretching the limits of the model to some extent, this would support predictions from quarkyonic-matter studies.

## Key claim

Two-dimensional chiral crystals (square and hexagonal lattices) are disfavored against one-dimensional modulations at intermediate chemical potentials near the would-be homogeneous phase transition, but become favored at higher chemical potentials, qualitatively supporting predictions from quarkyonic-matter studies.

## Method

The authors perform a mean-field analysis of the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit at zero temperature, allowing the chiral condensate to vary in two spatial dimensions. They assume specific periodic ansätze for the mass function, an egg-carton shape on a square lattice and a hexagonal modulation, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian numerically in momentum space using Bloch’s theorem, then minimize the thermodynamic potential with respect to amplitude and wave number.

## Key numbers

- Onset of the two-dimensional crystalline phase occurs around μ ≈ 310 MeV for both square and hexagonal shapes.
- The two-dimensional crystals reach the chirally restored phase at μ ≈ 345 MeV via a second-order phase transition.
- At μ = 325 MeV, saddle points in the free-energy landscape for a rectangular lattice are found at (Q_x, Q_y) ≈ (175 MeV, 400 MeV) and (400 MeV, 175 MeV).
- For a parameter set with vacuum constituent quark mass M_vac = 330 MeV, the two-dimensional square lattice becomes favored over one-dimensional modulations above μ ≈ 450 MeV, and the hexagonal shape becomes favored above μ ≈ 900 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies that considered only one-dimensional modulations of the chiral condensate, such as the chiral density wave and solitonic solutions from Nickel (2009). It contradicts the expectation that two-dimensional structures might be favored at intermediate chemical potentials, but supports the sequence of crystalline phases predicted for quarkyonic matter by Kojo et al. (2011). The results enable future studies that include diquark pairing and three-dimensional modulations, and confirm Ginzburg-Landau arguments from Abuki (2011) that one-dimensional modulations are favored near the Lifshitz point.
