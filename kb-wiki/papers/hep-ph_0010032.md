# QCD Potentiology

> We review the connection between QCD and potential models of quarkonia in the framework of effective field theories, with an emphasis on non-perturbative methods such as lattice simulations. The static and heavy quark potentials are introduced and the applicability of the non-relativistic and adiabatic approximations are discussed. The roles of so-called hybrid potentials are explored and we comment on the QCD analogue of the QED Lamb shift.

## Key claim

The paper argues that potential models of quarkonia can be derived from QCD through effective field theory methods, but the static potential alone is insufficient because the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) approximation breaks down when ultra-soft gluons are exchanged, requiring an enlarged basis of states that includes hybrid excitations.

## Method

The author reviews the connection between QCD and potential models by introducing the static potential \(V_0(r)\) computed from Wilson loops on the lattice, then derives a non-relativistic Hamiltonian from NRQCD (Nonrelativistic QCD) by factorizing gluonic effects from quark dynamics. The derivation is extended to include hybrid potentials (gluonic excitations classified by \(D_{\infty h}\) symmetry) and mixing between standard quark model states and hybrid states, with transition matrix elements treated as perturbations of order \(gv\).

## Key numbers

- String tension \(\sigma \approx 425^2\ \text{MeV}^2\) from the funnel parametrization of the static potential
- Funnel parametrization Coulomb coefficient \(e \approx 0.5\) from experimental spin-averaged quarkonia levels; lattice fits give \(e \approx 0.3\) (quenched) and \(e \approx 0.36\) (with two flavours of sea quarks)
- Lightest glueball mass about 1.6 GeV in the quenched setting
- String breaking occurs around \(r \approx 2.3\, r_0\) when sea quarks are included, with \(r_0 \approx 0.5\) fm
- For bottomonium (\(\Upsilon\)): \(m = 4.7\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv = 1.3\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv^2 = 0.4\ \text{GeV}\), \(v = 0.29\)
- For charmonium (\(J/\psi\)): \(m = 1.4\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv = 0.7\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv^2 = 0.4\ \text{GeV}\), \(v = 0.5\)
- For toponium (\(t\bar{t}\)): \(m = 175\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv = 45\ \text{GeV}\), \(mv^2 = 12\ \text{GeV}\), \(v = 0.26\)
- QCD Lamb shift naively enhanced relative to QED by a factor \(\alpha_s v^2_{\Upsilon} / (\alpha_{\text{fs}} v^2_{e^+e^-}) \approx 6 \times 10^4\)
- Uncertainty of 25% on \(\Upsilon\) fine structure splittings from matching constants between QCD and NRQCD; relativistic order \(v^6\) corrections unlikely to change order \(v^4\) predictions by more than 10%

## Context

This review extends the long-standing potential model approach to quarkonia, originally parameterized by the Cornell (funnel) form of Eichten (1975), by connecting it to lattice QCD and effective field theories such as NRQCD (Caswell and Lepage, 1986; Thacker and Lepage, 1991) and pNRQCD (Brambilla et al., 2000). The work enables systematic inclusion of relativistic corrections and hybrid meson mixing, with lattice calculations of transition matrix elements between \(\Sigma_g^+\) and hybrid states currently underway, and highlights the need for improved matching coefficients between QCD and lattice NRQCD for phenomenological predictions.
