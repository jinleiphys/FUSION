# Medium effects on the thermal conductivity of a hot pion gas

> We investigate the effect of the medium on the thermal conductivity of a pion gas out of chemical equilibrium by solving the relativistic transport equation in the Chapman-Enskog and relaxation time approximations. Using an effective model for the pi-pi cross-section involving rho and sigma meson exchange, medium effects are incorporated through thermal one-loop self-energies. The temperature dependence of the thermal conductivity is observed to be significantly affected.

## Key claim

The thermal conductivity of a pion gas is significantly affected when medium-dependent cross-sections (via thermal one-loop self-energies of the exchanged rho and sigma mesons) are used instead of vacuum cross-sections, and this effect is further modified by a temperature-dependent pion chemical potential that accounts for chemical freezeout.

## Method

The relativistic transport equation (Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation) was solved in the first Chapman-Enskog approximation and separately in the relaxation time approximation for a pion gas slightly out of equilibrium. The pi-pi scattering amplitude was constructed from an effective Lagrangian with rho and sigma meson exchange, and medium effects were introduced by replacing the vacuum widths of these mesons with in-medium widths obtained from the imaginary parts of their thermal one-loop self-energies, evaluated using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory. A temperature-dependent pion chemical potential, parametrized from Hirano (2002), was used to enforce fixed pion number after chemical freezeout.

## Key numbers

- The mean free path of pions using the peak value of the in-medium cross-section is approximately 1-2 fm at T = 160 MeV.
- The thermal conductivity lambda at T = 160 MeV lies within approximately 0.4-1.2 in units of fm^{-2} across the various approximations and medium treatments.
- The temperature-dependent pion chemical potential is parametrized as mu_pi(T) = a + bT + cT^2 + dT^3 with a = 0.824, b = 3.04, c = -0.028, d = 6.05 x 10^{-5}, where T and mu_pi are in MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients for a pion gas, where most earlier calculations (e.g., Gavin, Prakash, Davesne) used vacuum cross-sections. It follows the same authors' previous work on shear and bulk viscosities (Mitra and Sarkar, 2012, 2013) where medium-dependent cross-sections were first applied. The results enable inclusion of medium-modified thermal conductivity in fluid-dynamical simulations of the late stages of heavy ion collisions, particularly at FAIR energies or low-energy RHIC runs where baryon chemical potential is significant.
