# EPOS4 Model Predictions for Global Observables in Pb-Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.36 TeV

> The study of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), a deconfined state of nuclear matter, remains a central focus of high-energy heavy-ion collision experiments. The recent operation of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) in Run 3 at the new center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.36$ TeV necessitates theoretical predictions to characterize the energy dependence and bulk properties of the medium. In this study, we present comprehensive EPOS4 model predictions for key global observables in Pb-Pb collisions at $5.36$ TeV. We focus on the centrality dependence of the charged-particle pseudorapidity density ($dN_{ch}/dη$), integrated yields ($dN/dy$), mean transverse momentum ($\langle p_{T}\rangle$) for light-flavor hadrons ($π^{\pm}, K^{\pm}, p(\bar{p})$), and the charged particle nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$). The EPOS4 framework successfully captures the strong mass-dependent rise of $\langle p_{T}\rangle$ with multiplicity, a definitive signature of collective radial flow. Furthermore, the predicted charged hadron $R_{AA}$ demonstrates a clear suppression, consistent with energy loss mechanisms incorporated into the model. By comparing these predictions to existing $5.02$ TeV data, we demonstrate that the EPOS4 model offers a consistent and robust description of heavy-ion dynamics, projecting minimal energy evolution for these bulk and hard-probe observables between the two energies.

# EPOS4 Model Predictions for Global Observables in Pb-Pb Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV

## Key claim

The EPOS4 model predicts only minimal energy evolution for bulk and hard-probe observables between sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and 5.36 TeV, with the model slightly overestimating pion mean transverse momentum and slightly underestimating the nuclear modification factor in the intermediate-pT region, suggesting refinements to freeze-out dynamics or energy-loss parameters may be needed.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS4 event generator, which incorporates hydrodynamic evolution with final-state interactions via UrQMD, to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV. They computed charged-particle pseudorapidity density distributions, transverse momentum spectra, integrated yields, and mean transverse momentum for pions, kaons, and protons/antiprotons at mid-rapidity, as well as the charged hadron nuclear modification factor. Predictions at 5.36 TeV were compared systematically against ALICE and CMS experimental data at 5.02 TeV and against EPOS4 calculations at 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density distributions were calculated for charged particles with pT > 0.05 GeV/c for centrality classes 0-5%, 50-55%, and integrated 0-80% at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons in 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions plateaus around 0.2 at high pT (pT > 6 GeV/c), indicating strong suppression
- Integrated yields of pions, kaons, and protons span almost two orders of magnitude from peripheral to central collisions as a function of charged particle multiplicity density
- The EPOS4 calculation at 5.36 TeV shows a minimum suppression in R_AA around pT approximately 2 GeV/c, influenced by soft flowing particles

## Context

This work extends EPOS4 model predictions to the new LHC Run 3 collision energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV, following prior measurements by ALICE and CMS collaborations at 5.02 TeV (ALICE 2018, CMS 2017). The predictions provide theoretical benchmarks for upcoming experimental analyses at the higher energy, enabling tests of whether the energy dependence of bulk observables and jet quenching remains minimal as the model suggests. The study also identifies specific areas where EPOS4 may require tuning, particularly in the soft particle production sector and intermediate-pT energy-loss modeling.
