# Coherent vector meson production at an electron ion collider

> Exclusive vector meson electroproduction over a broad $Q^2$ range offers a unique opportunity to probe the gluon structure of nuclei to measure nuclear shadowing, and to search for gluon saturation and/or the colored glass condensate at an Electron-Ion Collider. Understanding the kinematic distributions and cross sections for specific processes can impact detector design to maximize their acceptance and strengthen the physics case. We will discuss predictions from a Monte Carlo generator eSTARlight, a tool developed to study production of exclusive vector meson final states. We present final state distributions and production rates for the $ρ$, $ϕ$, J/$ψ$, $ψ'$ and the $Υ$ states in $ep$ and $eA$ collisions at the different energies.

# Coherent vector meson production at an electron ion collider

## Key claim
Exclusive vector meson electroproduction and photoproduction at an Electron-Ion Collider can probe gluon structure and nuclear shadowing, but the quantum Glauber approach used for heavy ion targets neglects more complex nuclear effects such as shadowing, which may result in overestimates for the rates in heavy ions.

## Method
The study uses the Monte Carlo generator eSTARlight, which models photon emission over a wide energy range using the equivalent photon approach, then parameterizes the gamma-nucleon cross-section for vector meson production using fits to existing HERA data. For nuclear targets, a quantum Glauber calculation accounts for multiple interactions of the quark-antiquark dipole with the target, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution. Rejection sampling generates kinematic variables from the modeled distributions, and the vector meson decay angular distributions are described in the helicity system with parametrized Q^2 dependence of the longitudinal-to-transverse cross-section ratio.

## Key numbers
- Photoproduction is defined as Q^2 < 1 GeV^2 and electroproduction as Q^2 > 1 GeV^2
- The photon flux minimum Q^2 is bounded by m_e^2 k^2 / E_e(E_e - k) and maximum by 4 E_e(E_e - k), with the condition k < E_e - 10 m_e to avoid the endpoint region
- Electroproduction rates are roughly 100 times lower than photoproduction rates
- For eRHIC: electron energy 18 GeV, proton energy 275 GeV, heavy ion energy 100 GeV/A (gold)
- For JLEIC: electron energy 10 GeV, proton energy 100 GeV, heavy ion energy 40 GeV/A (lead)
- For LHeC: electron energy 60 GeV, proton energy 7 TeV, heavy ion energy 2.8 TeV/A (lead)
- For HERA: electron energy 27.5 GeV, proton energy 920 GeV
- The scaled cross-section ratio on gold versus iron targets drops for low Q^2 values, demonstrating shadowing effects; for both rho and J/psi, the ratio for Q^2 > 5 GeV^2 is greater than one

## Context
This work extends the STARlight generator framework, originally developed by Nystrand, Seger, Butterworth, and Gorbunov, to electron-ion collisions as eSTARlight. The calculations use parameterizations from HERA data by Adloff (2000) and Aaron (2010), and follow the quantum Glauber approach of Frankfurt (2002). The results enable detector design studies for proposed EIC facilities (eRHIC, JLEIC, LHeC) and can inform the feasibility of measuring nuclear shadowing and gluon saturation. The rapidity distributions show that overlap with data from CEBAF would require instrumentation at large negative rapidities or running at lower center-of-mass energies.
