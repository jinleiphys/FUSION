# Coulomb corrections to two-particle interaction in artificial traps

> In present work, we discuss the effect of Coulomb interaction to the dynamics of two-particle system bound in various traps. The strategy of including Coulomb interaction into the quantization condition of trapped system is discussed in a general and non-perturbative manner. In most cases, Coulomb corrections to quantization condition largely rely on numerical approach or perturbation expansion. Only for some special cases, such as the spherical hard wall trap, a closed-form of quantization condition with all orders of Coulomb corrections can be obtained.

## Key claim

This work extends the standard Luscher and BERW quantization conditions, which connect discrete energy spectra in traps to infinite-volume scattering phase shifts, to include long-range Coulomb interactions between charged particles. The derivation is presented in a general, non-perturbative Lippmann-Schwinger framework, but the authors note that in most trap geometries the Coulomb-corrected Green's function must be solved numerically or via perturbation expansion; only for the spherical hard wall trap can a closed-form quantization condition incorporating all orders of Coulomb corrections be obtained.

## Method

The authors formulate the two-particle Schrodinger equation in a trap with both a short-range separable zero-range potential and the Coulomb potential, then derive the quantization condition by matching the trapped-system dynamical equation to the infinite-volume scattering equation. The Coulomb-modified Green's function in the trap is obtained via the Dyson equation, and the infinite-volume Coulomb Green's function and Coulomb wave functions are expressed analytically using Kummer and Whittaker functions. The generalized zeta function for the trap is constructed by subtracting the ultraviolet-divergent part of the infinite-volume Coulomb Green's function from the trap Coulomb Green's function, yielding a finite result.

## Key numbers

* For the harmonic oscillator trap, the leading-order Coulomb-corrected zeta function is given by: \( \frac{C^2_l (\gamma)}{C^2_l (0)} \mathcal{M}^{(C,0th)}_{l}(\varepsilon) = (-1)^{l+1} \left( \frac{4\mu\omega}{q^2} \right)^{l+\frac{1}{2}} \frac{\Gamma(\frac{l}{2}+\frac{3}{4}-\frac{\varepsilon}{2\omega})}{\Gamma(\frac{1}{4}-\frac{l}{2}-\frac{\varepsilon}{2\omega})} \), where \(\gamma = -Z\mu/q\) and \(\omega\) is the harmonic oscillator frequency.
* For the spherical hard wall trap of radius \(R\), the closed-form quantization condition is: \( \cot \delta^{(S)}_l (q) = \frac{C^2_l (0)}{C^2_l (\gamma)} \frac{n_l^{(C)}(\gamma, qR)}{j_l^{(C)}(\gamma, qR)} \), where \(j_l^{(C)}\) and \(n_l^{(C)}\) are Coulomb-modified regular and irregular functions defined in the text.
* The Coulomb phase shift is defined as \( e^{2i\delta_l^{(C)}} = \Gamma(l+1+i\gamma)/\Gamma(l+1-i\gamma) \), with the Sommerfeld factor \( C_l(\gamma) = 2^l |\Gamma(l+1+i\gamma)| e^{-\pi\gamma/2} / (2l+1)! \).

## Context

This work extends the standard Luscher formula for periodic cubic boxes (Luscher, 1991) and the BERW formula for harmonic oscillator traps (Busch et al., 1998) to include long-range Coulomb interactions, which become dominant at low energies for charged particles. Prior treatments of Coulomb corrections in finite volume by Beane et al. (2014) and Stellin et al. (2020) used effective field theory perturbation theory; the present work provides a non-perturbative Lippmann-Schwinger framework applicable to multiple trap geometries. The results enable extraction of short-range scattering phase shifts from lattice QCD calculations of charged hadrons and from ab initio nuclear structure calculations in harmonic traps, where Coulomb effects must be accounted for non-perturbatively.
