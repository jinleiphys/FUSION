# Selfconsistent Description of a Thermal Piongas

> We examine a hot pion gas by including medium modifications of the two-body scattering amplitude as well as mean-field effects selfconsistently. In contrast to earlier calculations, the in-medium T-matrix is rather close to the free one while the mean-field potential agrees well with lowest-order estimates. We also discuss the validity of the quasiparticle approximation. It is found that it is reliable for temperatures up to $\sim 150$ MeV. Above this temperature off-shell effects in the pion selfenergy become important, especially if the pions are strongly out of chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

The in-medium T-matrix for a hot pion gas, when computed selfconsistently including both medium-modified two-body scattering and mean-field effects, is rather close to the free T-matrix, in contrast to earlier calculations that found a reduction; the quasiparticle approximation is reliable only for temperatures up to approximately 150 MeV, above which off-shell effects in the pion selfenergy become important.

## Method

The authors model the vacuum ππ interaction using meson-exchange potentials (Lohse et al. model) and solve the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix. They then implement a selfconsistent Brueckner scheme: the in-medium two-pion propagator includes thermal Bose factors and a pion selfenergy (mean field) computed from the forward-scattering T-matrix, and the T-matrix is recalculated with this in-medium propagator; the coupled equations are iterated to convergence. Two approaches are compared: the quasiparticle approximation (QPA), which expands the selfenergy around the quasiparticle pole, and a full numerical integration over the off-shell selfenergy.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle approximation is reliable for temperatures up to approximately 150 MeV; above this temperature off-shell effects become important.
- At T = 200 MeV and μπ = 0, the peak value of the ρ-resonance in the p-wave T-matrix increases by approximately 30% relative to the vacuum curve when full off-shell effects are included.
- For a scenario with μπ = 125 MeV at T = 200 MeV, the maximal pion density reaches nπ ∼ 0.7 fm⁻³.
- At T = 200 MeV and around k = 300 MeV/c, the ratio of decay width to quasiparticle energy Γk/ek ∼ 0.6, indicating the quasiparticle lifetime becomes very short.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the thermal pion gas that considered either mean-field effects (Shuryak) or medium modifications of the ππ cross section (Birse, Banerjee, et al.) separately, by providing a unified selfconsistent treatment. The finding that the in-medium T-matrix is close to the free one and that the quasiparticle approximation fails above 150 MeV sets limits on simpler approaches and suggests that full off-shell calculations are needed for temperatures relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results enable future work on thermalization of the pionic fireball and on signals such as dilepton pairs from the ρ-resonance.
