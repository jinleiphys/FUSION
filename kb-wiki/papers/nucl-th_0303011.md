# Hadron formation and attenuation in deep inelastic lepton scattering off nuclei

> We investigate hadron formation in deep inelastic lepton scattering on N, Kr and Xe nuclei in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment. The elementary electron-nucleon interaction is described within the event generator PYTHIA while a full coupled-channel treatment of the final state interactions is included by means of a BUU transport model. We find a good agreement with the measured charged hadron multiplicity ratio $R_M^h$ for N and Kr targets by accounting for the deceleration and absorption of the primarily produced particles as well as for the creation of secondary hadrons in the final state interactions.

# Hadron formation and attenuation in deep inelastic lepton scattering off nuclei

## Key claim

The observed attenuation of charged hadrons in deep inelastic scattering off nuclear targets can be reproduced without modifying the quark fragmentation function in the nuclear medium, provided that the leading prehadrons interact with a reduced cross section (approximately one-third of the full hadronic cross section) during a formation time of at least 0.3 fm/c, and that a full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions is included.

## Method

The authors split the lepton-nucleus interaction into two stages: first, the virtual photon absorption on a bound nucleon is simulated with the PYTHIA v6.2 event generator, which produces hadronic strings that fragment into colorless prehadrons; second, these prehadrons and their subsequent reaction products are propagated through the nucleus using a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that includes coupled-channel final state interactions, allowing for elastic and inelastic scattering, absorption, and secondary particle production. The formation time is taken as a constant in each hadron's rest frame, leading to a laboratory-frame formation time proportional to the hadron's energy via time dilation, and leading prehadrons (those containing constituents from the target nucleon or the hadronic component of the photon) are assigned a reduced effective cross section during this formation period.

## Key numbers

- Formation time extracted: $\tau_f \gtrsim 0.3$ fm/c, with little sensitivity to higher values, compatible with the range 0.4–0.8 fm/c from antiproton attenuation studies in p+A reactions at AGS energies (Cas02).
- Leading prehadron cross section required to match data: $\sigma_\mathrm{lead} = 0.33\,\sigma_h$, where $\sigma_h$ is the full hadronic cross section taken from the Particle Data Group.
- Kinematic cuts applied: Bjorken $x > 0.06$, photon virtuality $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$, energy fraction $y = \nu/E_\mathrm{beam} < 0.85$, and invariant mass $W \geq 4$ GeV (above the experimental constraint $W > 2$ GeV), leading to a minimal photon energy $\nu_\mathrm{min} = 8.6$ GeV (compared to 7 GeV in the HERMES experiment).
- The Lund model production time formula uses string tension $\kappa \approx 1$ GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier purely absorptive treatments of final state interactions in hadron attenuation, such as the phenomenological formation time model of the HERMES collaboration (HERMES1) and the rescaling of fragmentation functions proposed by Acc02, by incorporating a full coupled-channel transport description that allows deceleration and secondary particle production rather than simple absorption. The approach contrasts with pQCD parton models (Wang, Arl03) that attribute attenuation solely to partonic multiple scattering and induced gluon radiation. The extracted formation times are consistent with those from antiproton attenuation in p+A reactions at AGS energies (Cas02), and the results enable future studies of species-dependent formation times for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons, as well as improved modeling of the primary photon-nucleon interaction below the PYTHIA threshold of W = 4 GeV.
