# Chiral Radiation Transport Theory of Neutrinos

> We construct the chiral radiation transport equation for left-handed neutrinos in the context of radiation hydrodynamics for core-collapse supernovae. Based on the chiral kinetic theory incorporating quantum corrections due to the chirality of fermions, we derive a general relativistic form of the chiral transfer equation with collisions. We show that such quantum corrections explicitly break the spherical symmetry and axisymmetry of the system. In the inertial frame, in particular, we find that the so-called side jump leads to quantum corrections in the collisions between neutrinos and matter. We also derive analytic forms of such corrections in the emission and absorption rates for the neutrino absorption process. These corrections result in the generation of kinetic helicity and cross helicity of matter, which should then modify the subsequent evolution of matter. This theoretical framework can be applied to investigate the impacts of the chirality of neutrinos on the evolution of core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The authors construct a chiral radiation transport equation for left-handed neutrinos that incorporates quantum corrections from the chirality of fermions, showing that these corrections explicitly break spherical symmetry and axisymmetry in core-collapse supernova radiation hydrodynamics, though the free-streaming part remains unchanged from the classical Boltzmann equation in the inertial frame.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral kinetic theory (CKT) derived via the Wigner function approach in curved spacetime, including collision terms up to order \(\hbar\). They then specialize to the inertial frame with Minkowski metric, taking the frame vector \(n^\mu = \xi^\mu = (1/c, 0, 0, 0)\), which simplifies the free-streaming part to the classical form while retaining quantum corrections in the collision terms via the side-jump effect. The emission and absorption rates are derived analytically for the neutrino absorption process, incorporating fluid vorticity and magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The quantum corrections in the chiral radiation transport equation appear at order \(\hbar\) (explicitly kept in the text, e.g., Equations (Gamma_decomp)–(Gamma_omega)).
- The side-jump term in the Wigner function is proportional to \(\hbar c S^{\mu\nu}_{(n)} \mathcal{D}_\nu\), where \(S^{\mu\nu}_{(n)} = \epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} q_\alpha n_\beta / (2 q \cdot n)\).
- The emission and absorption rates \(\Gamma^{\lessgtr}_{(\xi)q}\) in the inertial frame are given by \(q^\nu - \hbar c S^{\mu\nu}_{(\xi)q} D^{({\rm i})}\) (Equation (Gamma_inertial)).

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory developed by Hidaka et al. (2016, 2017) and Liu et al. (2018) to include collisions in curved spacetime, specifically for neutrino radiation transport in core-collapse supernovae. It builds on earlier work by Yamamoto (2015) showing that parity violation from neutrino chirality can affect supernova hydrodynamics, and on the chiral magnetohydrodynamics simulations by Masada (2018) that revealed inverse energy cascade dominance. The framework enables future numerical investigations of how neutrino chirality impacts the evolution of core-collapse supernovae, particularly through the generation of kinetic helicity and cross helicity in matter.
