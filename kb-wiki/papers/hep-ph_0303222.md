# What about a beta-beam facility for low energy neutrinos?

> A novel method to produce neutrino beams has recently been proposed : the beta-beams. This method consists in using the beta-decay of boosted radioactive nuclei to obtain an intense, collimated and pure neutrino beam. Here we propose to exploit the beta-beam concept to produce neutrino beams of low energy. We discuss the applications of such a facility as well as its importance for different domains of physics. We focus, in particular, on neutrino-nucleus interaction studies of interest for various open issues in astrophysics, nuclear and particle physics. We suggest possible sites for a low energy beta-beam facility.

## Key claim

The paper proposes extending the beta-beam concept, originally designed for high-energy neutrino beams, to produce intense, collimated, and pure neutrino beams at low energies (a few tens of MeV up to about 100 MeV), arguing that such a facility would have significant impact on neutrino-nucleus interaction studies relevant to open issues in astrophysics, nuclear physics, and particle physics.

## Method

The author analyzes the physics potential of a low-energy beta-beam facility by examining the role of forbidden transitions (spin-dipole and higher) in neutrino-nucleus charged-current reactions, using the general cross-section formula for ν_l + A_Z X_N → l + A_{Z+1} X_{N-1}. The paper evaluates the contribution of different nuclear states to flux-averaged cross sections for nuclei such as carbon-12, iron-56, and lead-208, and discusses how a low-energy beta-beam could provide experimental data to constrain theoretical models of these transitions.

## Key numbers

- Forbidden spin-dipole states (J^π = 0^-, 1^-, 2^-) contribute about 40% of the flux-averaged cross section in carbon-12 and iron-56, and about 68% in lead-208, for low-energy neutrinos such as supernova neutrinos or those from muon decay-at-rest.
- Higher forbidden states contribute about 5% in iron and 25% in lead for low-energy neutrinos, and their contribution increases to about 30% in carbon and 60% in lead in the intermediate energy region (neutrinos from pion decay-in-flight).
- For neutrino energies of 30-50 MeV, the contribution of forbidden states (J^π ≠ 0^+, 1^+) to the ν_e(Pb,Bi)e^- reaction becomes significant.

## Context

This work extends the beta-beam concept proposed by Zucchelli (2002) for high-energy neutrino beams to the low-energy regime (tens of MeV), complementing existing proposals such as the ORLAND project (Oak Ridge Laboratory for Neutrino Detectors). The paper addresses the scarcity of experimental data on neutrino-nucleus interactions, noting that the largest dataset exists for carbon from LSND and KARMEN, with only one measurement each for deuteron and iron, and argues that a low-energy beta-beam facility would enable measurements on deuterium, carbon, oxygen, iron, and lead to improve theoretical predictions for supernova neutrino detection and nucleosynthesis studies.
