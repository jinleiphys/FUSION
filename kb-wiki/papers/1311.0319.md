# On the proton radius problem

> The recent values of the proton charge radius obtained by means of muonic-hydrogen laser spectroscopy are about $4\%$ different from the electron scattering data. It has been suggested that the proton radius is actually measured in different frames and that, starting from a non relativistic quark model calculation, the Lorentz transformation of the form factors accounts properly for the discepancy. We shall show that the relation between the charge radii measured in different frames can be determined on very general grounds by taking into account the Lorentz transformation of the charge density. In this way, the discrepancy between muonic-hydrogen laser spectroscopy and electron scattering data can be removed.

# On the proton radius problem

## Key claim
The discrepancy between the proton charge radius measured via muonic-hydrogen laser spectroscopy (0.84087(39) fm) and electron scattering data (0.8775(51) fm) can be removed by accounting for the Lorentz transformation of the charge density between the Breit frame (used in electron scattering) and the proton rest frame (used in muonic hydrogen), without invoking any specific quark model.

## Method
The authors derive the relation between charge radii measured in different reference frames starting from the general covariant form of the nucleon electromagnetic current in momentum space, expressed in terms of the Lorentz-invariant Dirac and Pauli form factors. They compute the charge density in momentum space explicitly in both the Breit frame and the nucleon rest frame, then extract the root-mean-square charge radius from the small-momentum expansion of each density. The resulting relation between the two radii is obtained on purely relativistic grounds, independent of any particular model of the proton's internal structure.

## Key numbers
- Muonic-hydrogen laser spectroscopy gives a proton charge radius of 0.84087(39) fm (Antognini et al., 2013), which is at 7σ variance with the CODATA 2010 value.
- The CODATA 2010 recommended value for the proton charge radius is 0.8775(51) fm, obtained from hydrogen atom and electron-proton elastic scattering measurements.
- The relation between the Breit-frame radius and the rest-frame radius is <r²>_B = <r²>_RF + 3/(4M²), where M is the proton mass.
- The rest-frame radius is related to the Breit-frame radius by <r²>_RF = <r²>_B - 3/(4M²).

## Context
This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the 4% difference between muonic-hydrogen results (Pohl et al., 2010; Antognini et al., 2013) and the CODATA 2010 average from electron scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy. The authors show that the frame-dependent explanation proposed by Robson (2005), which used a non-relativistic quark model and the Lorentz transformation of form factors, can be derived on general relativistic grounds without model dependence. They contrast their result with alternative relativistic correction schemes from Holzwarth (1996) and from the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (M. De Sanctis et al.), which produce different relations between rest-frame and Breit-frame radii, demonstrating that the earlier quark-model approaches introduced unacceptable model dependence. The work enables a consistent interpretation of proton radius measurements from different experimental techniques by properly accounting for the frame dependence of the charge density.
