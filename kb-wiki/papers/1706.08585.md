# Rapidity distribution of particle multiplicity in DIS at small x

> Analytical study of the rapidity distribution of the final state particles in deep inelastic scattering at small x is presented. We separate and analyse three sources of particle production: fragmentation of the quark-antiquark pair, accompanying coherent soft gluon radiation due to octet color exchange in the t-channel, and fragmentation of gluons that form parton distribution functions. Connection to Catani-Ciafaloni-Fiorani-Marchesini (CCFM) equations and the role of gluon reggezation are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic pQCD predictions for the pseudorapidity distribution of final-state particles in deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, showing that three distinct contributions (quark box fragmentation, coherent soft t-channel gluon radiation, and fragmentation of structural gluons including an anomalous reggeization term) must be summed, with the third contribution being formally of next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic order but enhanced by a factor of ln(1/x).

## Method

The authors separate particle production in the Breit frame into three sources: (1) fragmentation of the struck quark and its partner antiquark (the "quark box"), treated analogously to e+e- annihilation; (2) coherent soft gluon radiation due to octet color exchange in the t-channel, which mimics a gluon jet; and (3) fragmentation of structural gluons that form the parton distribution functions, combined with an "anomalous" contribution arising from gluon reggeization. They derive analytic expressions for each contribution using the collinear approximation and the DGLAP evolution equations, then sum them to obtain the full pseudorapidity distribution dN/dη.

## Key numbers

* The quark box contribution (dn^(1)) yields a total multiplicity n^(1) = 2 N_q(Q), where N_q is the mean parton multiplicity in a quark jet at hardness scale Q, coinciding with the mean multiplicity in e+e- → qqbar at invariant energy s = Q^2.
* The third contribution (structural gluons plus anomalous term) is estimated to be of order n^(3)/N_g = O(α_s ln(1/x)), where N_g is the gluon jet multiplicity, making it formally NNLL but enhanced by the small-x logarithm.
* Numerical calculations use Λ_QCD = 320 MeV (from the "limiting spectrum" MLLA approximation fitted to LEP data) and Λ_MSbar = 230 MeV for PDF evolution, with a collinear cutoff μ^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 (the initial scale of GRV parton distributions).
* The third contribution becomes collinear-sensitive for pseudorapidities η ≥ log(Q/2μ), with an uncertainty of 0.5–1 units in rapidity around that threshold, amounting to several percent and decreasing with increasing Q^2.
* Numerical results are presented for Q = 10, 100, and 1000 GeV at x = 10^{-2} and x = 10^{-4}, and for HERA-typical kinematics Q^2 = 20 GeV^2 at x = 10^{-3} and 10^{-4}.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Gribov, Dokshitzer, and Troian (GDKT, 1988) which derived inclusive energy spectra of final-state particles in small-x DIS, now providing the pseudorapidity (angular) distribution. It connects to the Catani-Ciafaloni-Fiorani-Marchesini (CCFM) scheme for generating DIS final states, and the "anomalous" contribution corresponds to radiation that compensates the non-Sudakov form factor suppression in the CCFM language. The results enable comparison with HERA data from H1 (2017) and can be generalized to double-differential distributions and to pp collisions at the LHC.
