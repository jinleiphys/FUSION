# Sketching the pion's valence-quark generalised parton distribution

> In order to learn effectively from measurements of generalised parton distributions (GPDs), it is desirable to compute them using a framework that can potentially connect empirical information with basic features of the Standard Model. We sketch an approach to such computations, based upon a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations and exemplified via the pion's valence dressed-quark GPD, $H_π^{\rm v}(x,ξ,t)$. Our analysis focuses primarily on $ξ=0$, although we also capitalise on the symmetry-preserving nature of the RL truncation by connecting $H_π^{\rm v}(x,ξ=\pm 1,t)$ with the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude. We explain that the impulse-approximation used hitherto to define the pion's valence dressed-quark GPD is generally invalid owing to omission of contributions from the gluons which bind dressed-quarks into the pion. A simple correction enables us to identify a practicable improvement to the approximation for $H_π^{\rm v}(x,0,t)$, expressed as the Radon transform of a single amplitude. Therewith we obtain results for $H_π^{\rm v}(x,0,t)$ and the associated impact-parameter dependent distribution, $q_π^{\rm v}(x,|\vec{b}_\perp|)$, which provide a qualitatively sound picture of the pion's dressed-quark structure at an hadronic scale. We evolve the distributions to a scale $ζ=2\,$GeV, so as to facilitate comparisons in future with results from experiment or other nonperturbative methods.

## Key claim

The impulse approximation (handbag diagram) used in prior continuum computations of the pion's valence dressed-quark GPD is generally invalid because it omits contributions from gluons that bind dressed-quarks into the pion, and this flaw becomes more severe as the momentum transfer grows, producing unphysical negative values for the GPD.

## Method

The authors employ a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to compute the pion's valence dressed-quark GPD, focusing primarily on the zero-skewness case (ξ=0). They derive an expression for the Mellin moments of the GPD from the triangle diagram, introduce five Feynman parameters and two convolution parameters to compute momentum integrals analytically, and then augment the calculation with a correction term that extends the handbag diagram correction identified for the valence-quark PDF to nonzero momentum transfer. The resulting GPD is expressed as the Radon transform of a single amplitude, with the correlation function between x and t given by a simple algebraic form.

## Key numbers

* The hadronic scale at which the valence-quark GPD is obtained is ζ_H = 0.51 GeV.
* The GPD is evolved to ζ_2 = 2 GeV using leading-order DGLAP equations.
* The best comparison with pion form factor data is obtained with dressed-quark mass M = 0.40 GeV, with a band showing results for M = 0.40 ± 0.05 GeV.
* The global maximum of the impact-parameter-dependent distribution at the hadronic scale is located at (x = 0.76, |b⊥| = 0).
* The mean-square transverse extent of the dressed-quarks within the pion is ⟨|b⊥|²⟩ = (0.52 fm)².
* The pion form factor is fit by Fπ(t = M²z) = (1 + 0.16z)/(1 + 0.44z + 0.060z² + 0.00033z³).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach previously applied to the pion's valence-quark PDF by Chang et al. (2014) and elastic form factor by Chang et al. (2013) to the case of generalised parton distributions. It corrects deficiencies in earlier continuum computations of the pion's GPD by Broniowski et al. (2003, 2007) and others, which delivered impact-parameter distributions that were not positive definite. The results provide a qualitatively sound picture of the pion's dressed-quark structure at an hadronic scale that can be evolved to higher scales for comparison with future experimental results or other nonperturbative methods.
