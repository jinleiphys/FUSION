# Extended relaxation time approximation and relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics

> Development of a new framework for derivation of order-by-order hydrodynamics from Boltzmann equation is necessary as the widely used Anderson-Witting formalism leads to violation of fundamental conservation laws when the relaxation-time depends on particle energy, or in a hydrodynamic frame other than the Landau frame. We generalize an existing framework for consistent derivation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation with a energy-dependent relaxation-time by extending the Anderson-Witting relaxation-time approximation. We argue that the present framework is compatible with conservation laws and derive first-order hydrodynamic equations in landau frame. Further, we show that the transport coefficients, such as shear and bulk viscosity as well as charge and heat diffusion currents, have corrections due to the energy dependence of relaxation time compared to what one obtains from the Anderson-Witting approximation of the collision term. The ratio of these transport coefficients are studied using a parametrized relaxation-time, and several interesting scaling features are reported.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the Anderson-Witting relaxation-time approximation (RTA) to allow for an energy-dependent relaxation time while preserving conservation laws, by defining the equilibrium distribution in a "thermodynamic frame" distinct from the hydrodynamic frame; the resulting first-order transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, charge diffusion) acquire corrections from the energy dependence of the relaxation time that vanish when the relaxation time is energy-independent.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and replace the standard Anderson-Witting collision term with an extended RTA where the distribution function relaxes to a local equilibrium distribution \(f_{\rm eq}^*\) defined in a thermodynamic frame \((u_\mu^*, T^*, \mu^*)\) rather than the hydrodynamic frame \((u_\mu, T, \mu)\). They perform a Chapman-Enskog expansion to first order in gradients, relate the two frames via small corrections \(\delta u^\mu, \delta T, \delta \mu\), and impose Landau frame and matching conditions to fix these corrections. The first-order dissipative quantities (shear stress, bulk pressure, charge diffusion) are then expressed in terms of thermodynamic integrals involving the energy-dependent relaxation time \(\tau_R(x,p)\).

## Key numbers

- For a system of chargeless massless particles, the shear viscosity is \(\eta = \frac{\tau_{\rm eq}\,g\,T^4\,\Gamma(5+\ell)}{15\,\pi^2} \left[\frac{\mathrm{Li}_{4+\ell}(-a)}{-a}\right]\), where \(\ell\) is the exponent in the power-law parametrization \(\tau_R = \tau_{\rm eq}(u\cdot p/T)^\ell\) and \(a=0,-1,1\) for Maxwell-Boltzmann, Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac statistics respectively.
- For chargeless massless particles, the ratio \(\eta/(s\,\tau_{\rm eq} T)\) at \(\ell=0\) equals \(1/5\) independent of statistics.
- For chargeless massive particles, the bulk viscosity \(\zeta/(s\,\tau_{\rm eq} T)\) shows a non-monotonous dependence on \(m/T\) for all three statistics and for \(\ell = 0, 1/2, 1\).
- In the small-\(m/T\) limit for Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics, \(\Gamma_{\rm MB} = \frac{15 (\ell^3+6\ell^2-13\ell+30)}{(\ell+1)(\ell+2)(\ell+3)}\) for positive \(\ell\), yielding \(\Gamma_{\rm MB}=75\) for \(\ell=0\) and \(\Gamma_{\rm MB}=15\) for \(\ell=1\).
- For Fermi-Dirac statistics at \(\ell=0\), \(\Gamma_{\rm FD}=48\); for \(\ell=1\), \(\Gamma_{\rm FD} \approx 12.8\).

## Context

This work extends the Anderson-Witting RTA (Anderson and Witting, 1974) which violates conservation laws when the relaxation time depends on particle energy or when working outside the Landau frame. It builds on a modification proposed by Teaney (2013) and the thermodynamic frame concept of Banerjee (2012), Jensen (2012), and Kovtun (2019). The framework enables consistent derivation of dissipative hydrodynamics with energy-dependent relaxation times, relevant for realistic QCD systems where collision time scales depend on energy (Dusling, 2009; Chakraborty, 2010; Kurkela, 2017). The results allow study of transport coefficient ratios and their scaling with the conformality measure \(1/3-c_s^2\), and can be compared with other recent approaches such as those of Mitra (2020) and Rocha (2021).
