# Parity-violating neutron spin rotation in hydrogen and deuterium

> We calculate the (parity-violating) spin rotation angle of a polarized neutron beam through hydrogen and deuterium targets, using pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. Our result is part of a program to obtain the five leading independent low-energy parameters that characterize hadronic parity-violation from few-body observables in one systematic and consistent framework. The two spin-rotation angles provide independent constraints on these parameters. Using naive dimensional analysis to estimate the typical size of the couplings, we expect the signal for standard target densities to be 10^-7 to 10^-6 rad/m for both hydrogen and deuterium targets. We find no indication that the nd observable is enhanced compared to the np one. All results are properly renormalized. An estimate of the numerical and systematic uncertainties of our calculations indicates excellent convergence. An appendix contains the relevant partial-wave projectors of the three-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The parity-violating neutron spin rotation angles for hydrogen and deuterium targets are calculated using pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, providing two independent constraints on the five leading low-energy parameters of hadronic parity-violation. The authors find no indication that the neutron-deuteron observable is enhanced compared to the neutron-proton one, contrary to some earlier expectations.

## Method

The authors used pionless effective field theory (EFT(π/)) with dibaryon auxiliary fields to compute parity-violating neutron spin rotation from neutron-proton and neutron-deuteron forward scattering. For the two-nucleon system, tree-level and next-to-leading order diagrams were evaluated using the Z-parameterization of dibaryon propagators. For the three-nucleon system, the parity-conserving amplitudes were obtained by solving Faddeev equations in the partially-resummed formalism, and the parity-violating contributions were included via tree-level, one-loop, and two-loop diagrams with the five leading-order parity-violating operators.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-proton spin rotation at LO+NLO: (1/ρ) dφ_PV^(np)/dl = [4.5 ± 0.5] rad MeV^(-1/2) (2g^(³S₁-³P₁) + g^(³S₁-¹P₁)) - [18.5 ± 1.9] rad MeV^(-1/2) (g^(¹S₀-³P₀)_(ΔI=0) - 2 g^(¹S₀-³P₀)_(ΔI=2))
* Neutron-deuteron spin rotation at LO+NLO: (1/ρ) dφ_PV^(nd)/dl = [8.0 ± 0.8] rad MeV^(-1/2) g^(³S₁-¹P₁) + [17.0 ± 1.7] rad MeV^(-1/2) g^(³S₁-³P₁) + [2.3 ± 0.5] rad MeV^(-1/2) (3 g^(¹S₀-³P₀)_(ΔI=0) - 2 g^(¹S₀-³P₀)_(ΔI=1))
* Expected signal magnitude for standard target densities: |dφ_PV/dl| ≈ 10^(-7) to 10^(-6) rad/m for both hydrogen and deuterium targets
* The NLO correction for P-³S₁ waves is 35% and for P-¹S₀ waves is 5%, consistent with the naive power counting estimate

## Context

This work extends the program of applying pionless effective field theory to parity-violating few-nucleon observables, following earlier calculations by Phillips (2008) and Schindler (2009). The results provide a systematic, model-independent framework that contrasts with the phenomenological meson-exchange approach of Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1979) and the hybrid formalism used by Liu (2006) and Schiavilla (2008). The calculations enable experimental planning and interpretation of parity-violating neutron spin rotation measurements, which can determine two independent combinations of the five leading low-energy constants that characterize hadronic parity-violation.
