# Unblocking of stellar electron capture for neutron-rich $N=50$ nuclei at finite temperature

> We have calculated electron capture rates for neutron-rich $N=50$ nuclei ($^{78}$Ni, $^{82}$Ge, $^{86}$Kr, $^{88}$Sr) within the Thermal QRPA approach at temperatures $T=0$, corresponding to capture on the ground-state, and at $T=10$ GK (0.86 MeV), which is a typical temperature at which the $N=50$ nuclei are abundant during a supernova collapse. In agreement with recent experiments, we find no Gamow-Teller (GT$_+$) strength at low excitation energies, $E<7$ MeV, caused by Pauli blocking induced by the $N=50$ shell gap. At the astrophysically relevant temperatures this Pauli blocking of the GT$_+$ strength is overcome by thermal excitations across the $Z=40$ proton and $N=50$ neutron shell gaps, leading to a sizable GT contribution to the electron capture. At the high densities, at which the $N=50$ nuclei are important for stellar electron capture, forbidden transitions contribute noticeably to the capture rate. Our results indicate that the neutron-rich $N=50$ nuclei do not serve as an obstacle of electron capture during the supernova collapse.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, contrary to the assumption that the Pauli-blocked Gamow-Teller (GT\(_+\)) strength in neutron-rich \(N=50\) nuclei makes them an obstacle to electron capture during supernova collapse, thermal excitations at astrophysically relevant temperatures (around \(T=10\) GK) unblock the GT\(_+\) strength and, together with forbidden transitions, produce capture rates much larger than those estimated from ground-state GT\(_+\) distributions alone.

## Method

The authors used the Thermal Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (TQRPA) within the thermofield dynamics formalism to calculate temperature-dependent GT\(_+\) strength functions and electron capture cross sections for the neutron-rich \(N=50\) nuclei \(^{78}\)Ni, \(^{82}\)Ge, \(^{86}\)Kr, and \(^{88}\)Sr. They employed two Skyrme parametrizations (SkM* and SkO') for the mean field and included both allowed (\(0^+\), \(1^+\)) and first-forbidden (\(0^-\), \(1^-\), \(2^-\)) transitions, folding the thermal-averaged cross sections with a Fermi-Dirac electron distribution to obtain stellar capture rates at \(T=0\) and \(T=10\) GK (0.86 MeV).

## Key numbers

*   At \(T=0\), no GT\(_+\) strength is found at low excitation energies (\(E<7\) MeV) for \(^{86}\)Kr and \(^{88}\)Sr, in agreement with experimental measurements by Zamora (2019) and Titus (2019).
*   The ground-state thresholds \(Q\) are: \(Q(^{78}\text{Ni})=20.7\) MeV, \(Q(^{82}\text{Ge})=13.0\) MeV, \(Q(^{86}\text{Kr})=8.1\) MeV, \(Q(^{88}\text{Sr})=5.8\) MeV (from Moller, 2016).
*   At \(T=10\) GK (0.86 MeV), thermally unblocked GT\(_+\) strength appears below the ground-state threshold for all four nuclei, including negative-energy transitions for \(^{86}\)Kr and \(^{88}\)Sr.
*   The strength of the thermally unblocked \(g^p_{9/2} \to g^n_{7/2}\) transition increases by almost three orders of magnitude from \(^{78}\)Ni to \(^{88}\)Sr at \(T=10\) GK.
*   For \(^{86}\)Kr and \(^{88}\)Sr, the electron capture rates at \(T=10\) GK are significantly larger than those derived from the experimental ground-state GT\(_+\) distributions at all densities; at densities above a few \(10^{10}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), forbidden transitions dominate the total rate.

## Context

This work extends the TQRPA studies by Dzhioev (2010, 2019) on neutron-rich nuclei to a chain of \(N=50\) nuclei, including those with measured GT\(_+\) distributions (Zamora, 2019; Titus, 2019). It directly contradicts the argument by Sullivan (2016) and Titus (2018) that the \(N=50\) shell gap severely blocks electron capture, which was based on ground-state GT\(_+\) data and parametrized rates. The results enable improved supernova simulations by showing that these nuclei do not act as an obstacle to electron capture, and they highlight the need to include thermal unblocking and forbidden transitions in stellar capture rate calculations.
