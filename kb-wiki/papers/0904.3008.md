# Leading Chiral Logarithms for Pion Form Factors to Arbitrary Number of Loops

> We develop the method of calculation of the leading chiral (infrared) logarithms to an arbitrary loop order for various form factors of Nambu-Goldstone bosons. The method is illustrated on example of scalar and vector form factors in massless 4D O(N+1)/O(N) sigma-model. The analytical properties of the form factors are derived. The leading chiral (infrared) logarithms are summed up in the large N limit.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to compute leading chiral (infrared) logarithms for pion form factors to arbitrary loop order in the massless 4D O(N+1)/O(N) sigma-model, but the method is explicitly illustrated only for scalar and vector form factors and the results are summed analytically only in the large N limit.

## Method

The method reduces the calculation of leading-logarithm (LL) coefficients for form factors to solving linear recursion relations that involve the LL coefficients of the pion-pion scattering amplitude (which themselves satisfy a nonlinear recursion). The key step is to express the form factor LL coefficients \(f_n^{S,V}\) in terms of the gamma-function coefficients \(\Gamma^{(p,l)}\) (computed from a single one-loop diagram) and the scattering amplitude LL coefficients \(\omega_{nj}\), leading to the recursion \(f_n^{S,V} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{m=0}^{n-1} \sum_{j} \Gamma_{S,V}^{(n-m, j)} f_m^{S,V} \omega_{(n-m-1)j}\). The generating function for the form factor LL coefficients is then shown to satisfy a differential equation whose solution is an exponential of an integral over the generating function of the partial-wave amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For the scalar form factor with \(N=3\): \(f_1^S = 1\), \(f_2^S = 43/36\), \(f_3^S = 143/108\), \(f_4^S = 15283/9720\) (Table 1).
- For the vector form factor with \(N=3\): \(f_1^V = 1/6\), \(f_2^V = 1/72\), \(f_3^V = 91/1296\), \(f_4^V = 3607/155520\) (Table 2).
- In the large N limit, the scalar form factor LL coefficients are \(f_n^S = (N/2)^n (1 + O(1/N))\) for \(n \ge 1\).
- In the large N limit, the vector form factor LL coefficients are \(f_n^V = \frac{1}{(n+1)(n+2)} (N/2)^{n-1} (1 + O(1/N))\) for \(n \ge 1\).
- The 33-loop chiral LL coefficient can be computed in a dozen seconds on a PC using the recursion.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for computing leading chiral logarithms in pion-pion scattering (Kivel, Polyakov, Vladimirov, 2008) to the case of form factors. It reproduces the four-loop scalar form factor results of Bissegger and Fuhrer (2007) and the two-loop vector form factor results of Bijnens, Colangelo, and Talavera (1998), while enabling calculations to arbitrarily high loop order. The method also shows that the LL approximation for form factors satisfies the Omnes solution of dispersion relations, connecting the form factors to the partial-wave phase shifts of pion-pion scattering.
