# The Proton Spin Structure in a Light-Cone Qaurk-Spectator-Diquark Model

> It is shown that the proton spin problem raised by the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule violation does not in conflict with the SU(6) quark model, provided that the relativistic effect from the quark transversal motions, the flavor asymmetry between the $u$ and $d$ valence quarks, and the intrinsic quark-antiquark pairs generated by the non-perturbative meson-baryon fluctuations of the nucleon sea are taken into account. The meson-baryon fluctuations of the nucleon sea provide a consistent picture for connecting the proton spin structure with a number of other anomalies observed in the proton's structure, such as the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea implied by the violation of the Gottfried sum rule and the discrepancy between two different measurements of the strange quark distributions in the nucleon sea.

# The Proton Spin Structure in a Light-Cone Quark-Spectator-Diquark Model

## Key claim
The Ellis-Jaffe sum rule violation (the "proton spin crisis") does not conflict with the SU(6) quark model, provided that three effects are simultaneously taken into account: the relativistic Wigner rotation from quark transverse motions, the flavor asymmetry between u and d valence quarks, and intrinsic quark-antiquark pairs from non-perturbative meson-baryon fluctuations of the nucleon sea. The quantity ΔΣ measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering does not represent the vector sum of quark spins in the proton rest frame.

## Method
The author constructs a light-cone quark-spectator-diquark model where the proton wavefunction contains both scalar and vector diquark Fock states with a mixing angle. Valence quark distributions are derived from the impulse approximation, with the mass difference between scalar (600 MeV) and vector (900 MeV) spectators generating the u/d valence asymmetry. Polarized distributions incorporate a Wigner rotation factor W_D(x,k⊥) that connects the quark spin in the proton rest frame to the helicity measured in the infinite momentum frame. The model is extended with a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation picture for intrinsic sea quark-antiquark pairs.

## Key numbers
- The Wigner rotation factor <M_q> for light flavor quarks is around 0.75, ranging from 0 to 1, and approaches 1 for heavy flavor quarks.
- To reproduce the observed Ellis-Jaffe sums, the model requires <W_S> = 0.56 and <W_V> = 0.92 to produce Γ₁^p = 0.136 and Γ₁^n = -0.03.
- The scalar spectator mass is chosen as m_S = 600 MeV and the vector spectator mass as m_V = 900 MeV, with quark mass m_q = 330 MeV and harmonic oscillator scale β_D = 330 MeV.
- A global fit of polarized deep inelastic scattering data yields Δu = 0.83 ± 0.03, Δd = -0.43 ± 0.03, Δs = -0.10 ± 0.03.
- The standard SU(6) quark model gives constraints |Δu_v| ≤ 4/3 and |Δd_v| ≤ 1/3; the empirical |Δd| > 1/3 implies an additional negative contribution Δd_s from the sea.

## Context
This work extends the light-cone quark-spectator approach of Ma (1991) and Brodsky (1994) on Wigner rotation effects, and the meson-baryon fluctuation model of Brodsky (1996) for intrinsic sea quarks. It addresses the proton spin puzzle triggered by the EMC measurement that violated the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule, and connects it to the Gottfried sum rule violation (implying flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea) and the discrepancy between two measurements of strange quark distributions. The model enables predictions of quark/antiquark asymmetries in momentum and helicity distributions for the nucleon sea, testable in processes such as forward neutron events at HERA and asymmetric hadronization in e⁺e⁻ annihilation.
