# Sensitivity of the fusion cross section to the density dependence of the symmetry energy

> It is the aim of this paper to discuss the impact of nuclear fusion on the EOS. This is a timely subject given the expected availability of increasingly exotic beams at rare isotope facilities\,\cite{balantekin2014}. In practice, we focus on $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca fusion. We employ three different approaches to calculate fusion cross-sections for a set of energy density functionals with systematically varying nuclear matter properties. Fusion calculations are performed using frozen densities, using a dynamic microscopic method based on density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) approach, as well as direct TDHF study of above barrier cross-sections. For these studies, we employ a family of Skyrme parametrizations with systematically varied nuclear matter properties. We find a slight preference for forces which deliver a slope of symmetry energy of $L\approx 50$\,MeV that corresponds to a neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca of $R_\mathrm{skin}\!=\!(0.180\!-\!0.210)$\,fm.

## Key claim
The paper finds a slight preference for Skyrme forces with a symmetry energy slope of L ≈ 50 MeV, corresponding to a neutron-skin thickness in ⁴⁸Ca of 0.180–0.210 fm, but this conclusion is hedged: the preference is described as a trend read off from the results, not a definitive constraint, and the authors note that the folding-potential model systematically underestimates sub-barrier cross sections while DC-TDHF gives much better agreement.

## Method
The authors calculated ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca fusion cross sections using three approaches: (1) a double-folding potential with frozen Hartree-Fock densities and the M3Y effective NN interaction; (2) the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method, which incorporates dynamical polarization and neck formation; and (3) direct TDHF calculations for above-barrier energies. They employed a family of Skyrme energy density functionals (the SV series) with systematically varied nuclear matter properties, incompressibility K, effective mass m*/m, symmetry energy J, and Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule enhancement factor κ_TRK, plus four additional forces (SV-L) with a dedicated variation of the symmetry energy slope L.

## Key numbers
- The base parametrization SV-bas has K = 234 MeV, m*/m = 0.9, J = 30 MeV, and κ_TRK = 0.4.
- The neutron-skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca for the preferred forces is R_skin = (0.180–0.210) fm.
- The folding potential peak varies by up to 0.5 MeV between SV-sym28 (J = 28 MeV) and SV-sym34 (J = 34 MeV); variation with incompressibility K is about 0.25 MeV.
- The experimental neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb from PREX is R_skin²⁰⁸ = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm; PREX-II aims to reduce the error to ±0.06 fm.
- CREX will measure the neutron radius of ⁴⁸Ca with a precision of 0.02 fm at a momentum transfer q ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹.
- The fusion cross section varies over six orders of magnitude in a 10 MeV interval of center-of-mass energy below the barrier.

## Context
This work extends studies of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which have been constrained by giant resonances (Blaizot 1980, Reinhard 1999), electric dipole polarizability (Tamii 2011, Roca-Maza 2013), and neutron-skin measurements such as PREX (Abrahamyan 2012). It connects to the upcoming CREX experiment on ⁴⁸Ca and to ab-initio calculations by Hagen (2016). The paper shows that fusion reactions, particularly sub-barrier fusion, are sensitive to the symmetry energy slope L and can serve as a complementary probe of the EOS, but the approach requires reduced experimental uncertainties in fusion data and further theoretical development coupling microscopic structure to fusion dynamics.
