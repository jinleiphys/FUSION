# Collective flows of pions in Au+Au collisions at energies 1.0 and 1.5 GeV/nucleon

> Based on the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, the pion potentials obtained from the in-medium dispersion relation of the $Δ$-hole model and from the modified phenomenological approach are further introduced. Both the rapidity $y_0$ and transverse-velocity $u_{t0}$ dependence of directed $v_1$ and elliptic $v_2$ flows of $π^{+}$ and $π^{-}$ charged mesons produced from Au+Au collisions at two beam energies of 1.0 GeV/nucleon and 1.5 GeV/nucleon and within a large centrality region of $0<b_0<0.55$ are scanned. Calculations with pion potentials as well as without considering the pion potential are compared to the newly experimental data released by the FOPI collaboration at GSI. It is found that the directed flow is more sensitive to the pion potential than the elliptic one, and the attractive pion potential from the phen.B mode of the phenomenological approach is too strong to describe the flow data and can be safely ruled out. The relatively weak pion potential from the $Δ$-hole model can supply a good description for the FOPI data of both flows as functions of both centrality and rapidity. A two-peak/valley structure occurs in the transverse-velocity dependent directed flow but the elliptic flow drops monotonously with increasing $u_{t0}$. Finally, both $v_1$ and $v_2$ flows with large $u_{t0}$ from semi-central heavy ion collisions can be taken as sensitive probes for the pion potential.

## Key claim

The directed flow of pions is more sensitive to the pion potential than the elliptic flow, and the relatively weak attractive pion potential from the Δ-hole model can describe the FOPI data fairly well, while the stronger attractive potential from the phenomenological phen.B mode is too strong and can be ruled out.

## Method

The authors used the updated ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.0 and 1.5 GeV/nucleon. They introduced three treatments of the pion potential: none, a phenomenological potential (phen.B mode), and a potential derived from the in-medium dispersion relation of the Δ-hole model. They calculated the directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2 of π+ and π- mesons as functions of rapidity, centrality, and transverse velocity, and compared them to FOPI collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 1.0 GeV/nucleon and 1.5 GeV/nucleon for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality range: 0 < b0 < 0.55, divided into four bins: b0 < 0.15, b0 < 0.25, 0.25 < b0 < 0.45, and 0.45 < b0 < 0.55
- Scaled transverse velocity cut: 1.0 < u_t0 < 4.2 for rapidity-dependent flow analysis
- The two-peak/valley structure in directed flow appears at u_t0 approximately 1.5 (corresponding to transverse momentum p_t roughly 0.18 GeV/c)
- The Δ-hole model uses parameters: m_N = 938 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, m_Δ = 1232 MeV, f_Δ = 2, g' = 0.6
- The Skyrme interaction SV-mas08 has incompressibility K_0 = 233 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion potentials in transport models, particularly following the FOPI collaboration measurements (Reisdorf, 2006) of pion flows at GSI. It builds on earlier QMD calculations by Fuchs (1996) and the authors' own prior work (zly) that tested phenomenological potentials. The study enables more reliable extraction of the density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy from π-/π+ yield ratios, which has been debated in the literature between groups using the IBUU model (Xiao, 2009), the LQMD model (Feng, 2009), and other approaches. The authors note that future work should include in-medium modifications of Δ production cross sections and decay widths, as studied in their previous work (Li, 2016; Li, 2017).
