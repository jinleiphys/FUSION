# QCD-inspired determination of NJL model parameters

> The QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density has attracted considerable interest over many decades now, not least because of its relevance for a better understanding of heavy-ion collision experiments. Models provide some insight into the QCD phase structure but usually rely on various parameters. Based on renormalization group arguments, we discuss how the parameters of QCD low-energy models can be determined from the fundamental theory of the strong interaction. We particularly focus on a determination of the temperature dependence of these parameters in this work and comment on the effect of a finite quark chemical potential. We present first results and argue that our findings can be used to improve the predictive power of future model calculations.

# QCD-inspired determination of NJL model parameters

## Key claim

The parameters of low-energy QCD models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can be determined from fundamental QCD using renormalization group arguments, and these parameters acquire a temperature dependence that reduces the chiral critical temperature by approximately 15% compared to conventional NJL model studies with fixed parameters. The authors present this as a first exploratory study and note that the projection of gluon-induced four-quark interactions onto the NJL model is to some extent ambiguous, so they aim to provide qualitative guidance rather than quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors employ the Wetterich renormalization group equation to compute the flow of gluon-induced four-quark interactions in QCD at finite temperature, using a Fierz-complete ansatz for the effective action in Landau gauge with an exponential regulator function. They then project these gluon-induced four-quark couplings onto a two-flavor NJL model at a specific matching scale Lambda_I, defined as the intersection point where the NJL model coupling equals the gluon-induced coupling at zero temperature. The resulting temperature-dependent four-quark coupling is inserted into the NJL gap equation in the mean-field approximation to compute the constituent quark mass as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The projection scale Lambda_I where the NJL model coupling matches the gluon-induced coupling is approximately 0.243 GeV at zero temperature and chemical potential.
- The dimensionless NJL model coupling in the limit Lambda_0 to infinity is approximately 6.58.
- The chiral critical temperature from the conventional NJL model is T_c,NJL approximately 0.103 GeV.
- The chiral critical temperature is reduced by approximately 15% when using the temperature-dependent four-quark coupling in the gluon-induced NJL (GI-NJL) model.
- The strong coupling is fixed at alpha(k=20 GeV) approximately 0.163.
- The fit parameters for the temperature-dependent coupling are lambda_tilde_NJL approximately 1.09 and Lambda_tilde_I approximately 0.405 GeV, with Lambda_QCD approximately 0.371 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to QCD low-energy models previously developed by Gies (2005), Braun (2005, 2006), and others, who used fixed-point analyses of gluon-induced four-quark interactions to estimate the chiral phase transition temperature. The authors note that their projection scale Lambda_I is sensitive to the running of the strong coupling at intermediate momentum scales, and that a smooth transition between QCD and hadronic degrees of freedom would require more elaborate techniques such as dynamical hadronization as used by Mitter (2014) and Braun (2014). The results enable future model calculations to incorporate temperature-dependent parameters derived from fundamental QCD, potentially improving predictive power for studies of the QCD phase diagram.
