# A background-free signal of jet-induced diffusion wake in quark-gluon plasma

> Rapidity asymmetry of jet-hadron correlation has been proposed as a robust signal of dijet-induced diffusion wake in quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We generalize this observable to other jet configurations such as γ/Z0-jets and propose a new method to compute the rapidity asymmetry that is free of background. In this new method, the rapidity of the trigger is fixed or restricted to a symmetrical range while the rapidity of the associated jet is varied. The rapidity asymmetry is defined as the difference between the hadron rapidity distribution in the opposite azimuthal direction of the jet (or the same direction of the trigger) with different associated jet rapidities. Since the background in this hadron rapidity distribution with different associated jet rapidity is identical, it will be completely canceled in the rapidity asymmetry. Such background-free rapidity asymmetry caused by jet-induced diffusion wake is demonstrated in CoLBT-hydro simulations of dijets and γ-jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, which weakens as hadron pT increases, and subtraction of a p+p baseline has a negligible effect.

# A background-free signal of jet-induced diffusion wake in quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The rapidity asymmetry between hadron rapidity distributions in events with different associated jet rapidities provides a signal of the jet-induced diffusion wake that is genuinely free of background, because the QGP background is identical in the two event classes and cancels completely in the difference. This claim is demonstrated in CoLBT-hydro simulations but the authors note that the asymmetry weakens as hadron transverse momentum increases, and that subtraction of a p+p baseline has a negligible effect.

## Method

The authors use the Coupled Linear Boltzmann Transport hydrodynamic (CoLBT-hydro) model to simulate gamma-jet and dijet production in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, with initial conditions from the Trento model and hard processes from PYTHIA8. For gamma-jets, events are divided into two rapidity classes (|eta_jet| > 0.8 and |eta_jet| < 0.8); for dijets, events are divided by whether the two jets are in the same hemisphere (eta_jet1 * eta_jet2 > 0) or opposite hemispheres (eta_jet1 * eta_jet2 < 0). The rapidity asymmetry is defined as the difference between the hadron rapidity distributions in the opposite azimuthal direction of the jet (or same direction as the trigger) for these two event classes, with final jets reconstructed using FASTJET with anti-kT algorithm R=0.4.

## Key numbers

* Gamma-jet events: photon pT > 100 GeV/c, |eta_gamma| < 1.44; jet pT > 50 GeV/c, |eta_jet| < 1.6; jet radius R=0.4
* Dijet events: leading jet pT > 120 GeV/c, subleading jet pT > 90 GeV/c, |eta_jet1,jet2| < 1.6, |Delta_phi_jet1,jet2| > pi/2
* Rapidity asymmetry is observed for soft hadrons with pT_h in [1.0, 2.0] GeV/c, but is "completely negligible" for pT_h in [2.0, 4.0] GeV/c
* The diffusion wake signal in the total hadron rapidity distribution is on the order of "a fraction of a percentage" of the total distribution
* ATLAS measurement reported a diffusion wake signal of approximately 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of rapidity asymmetry in dijet events (Yang 2025) by generalizing the observable to gamma/Z0-jet configurations and introducing a method that avoids the background dependence on jet rapidity. It builds on recent experimental observations of diffusion wake signals by ATLAS (2024) in gamma-jet events and CMS (2024, 2025) in Z0-hadron correlations, which required mixed-event background subtraction. The new observable enables experimental measurements at both RHIC and LHC without background subtraction, addressing the limitation that the CMS mixed-event method produces a negative Z0-hadron correlation even in p+p collisions. The approach can be extended to search for jet-induced Mach-cone structures in heavy-ion collisions.
