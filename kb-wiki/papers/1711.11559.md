# Non-perturbative Approach to Equation of State and Collective Modes of the QGP

> We discuss a non-perturbative $T$-matrix approach to investigate the microscopic structure of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Utilizing an effective Hamiltonian which includes both light- and heavy-parton degrees of freedoms. The basic two-body interaction includes color-Coulomb and confining contributions in all available color channels, and is constrained by lattice-QCD data for the heavy-quark free energy. The in-medium $T$-matrices and parton spectral functions are computed selfconsistently with full account of off-shell properties encoded in large scattering widths. We apply the $T$-matrices to calculate the equation of state (EoS) for the QGP, including a ladder resummation of the Luttinger-Ward functional using a matrix-log technique to account for the dynamical formation of bound states. It turns out that the latter become the dominant degrees of freedom in the EoS at low QGP temperatures indicating a transition from parton to hadron degrees of freedom. The calculated spectral properties of one- and two-body states confirm this picture, where large parton scattering rates dissolve the parton quasiparticle structures while broad resonances start to form as the pseudocritical temperature is approached from above. Further calculations of transport coefficients reveal a small viscosity and heavy-quark diffusion coefficient.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a non-perturbative T-matrix approach, constrained by lattice-QCD data for the heavy-quark free energy and the equation of state, can describe a gradual transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom in the QGP near the pseudocritical temperature, but only in one of two possible solutions (the "strongly-coupled solution"), the alternative "weakly-coupled solution" does not show this transition and produces transport coefficients inconsistent with heavy-ion phenomenology.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic Hamiltonian for light quarks, gluons, charm quarks, and bottom quarks with a two-body interaction kernel including screened color-Coulomb and confining (string) contributions in all available color channels. They solved the in-medium T-matrix equation and parton selfenergies selfconsistently by numerical iteration, using a matrix-log technique to resum the Luttinger-Ward functional for the equation of state. The interaction kernel was constrained by fitting to lattice-QCD data for the static heavy-quark free energy, and the bare light-parton masses were fitted to reproduce the lattice-QCD equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The screening mass for the Coulomb term increases strongly with temperature, roughly in accord with perturbative estimates m_d ~ gT, while the screening of the string term is rather weak.
- At low temperatures near T_c, the low-momentum light-parton spectral functions acquire widths of order 1 GeV, significantly exceeding their masses.
- For the strongly-coupled solution, the dimensionless spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(2πT) and the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density 4πη/s are both within a factor of two of the conjectured quantum lower bounds of one.
- The ratio r ≡ [2πT D_s] / [4πη/s] is around one for low temperatures in the strongly-coupled solution, slowly increasing with temperature but remaining significantly below 5/2 at T = 400 MeV; for the weakly-coupled solution the ratio is close to 5/2 even at low temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous T-matrix calculations that used free and internal energies as potential proxies for heavy-quark diffusion (vanHees 2007, Riek 2010, Prino 2016). It provides a unified treatment of light and heavy partons that can be constrained by lattice-QCD results for the heavy-quark free energy (Bazavov 2014) and the equation of state (Bazavov 2014). The approach enables calculation of spectral functions and transport coefficients for applications to heavy-ion phenomenology, and future applications include calculation of high-p_T parton transport, quark-number susceptibilities for finite chemical potential studies, and explicit inclusion of condensate formation for chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity.
