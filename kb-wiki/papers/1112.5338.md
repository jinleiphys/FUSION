# Testing the continuum discretized coupled channel method for deuteron induced reactions

> The Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method is a well established theory for direct nuclear reactions which includes breakup to all orders. Alternatively, the 3-body problem can be solved exactly within the Faddeev formalism which explicitly includes breakup and transfer channels to all orders. With the aim to understand how CDCC compares with the exact 3-body Faddeev formulation, we study deuteron induced reactions on: i) $^{10}$Be at $E_{\rm d}= 21.4, 40.9 \; {\rm and} \; 71$ MeV; ii) $^{12}$C at $E_{\rm d} = 12 \; {\rm and} \; 56$ MeV; and iii) $^{48}$Ca at $E_{\rm d} = 56$ MeV. We calculate elastic, transfer and breakup cross sections. Overall, the discrepancies found for elastic scattering are small with the exception of very backward angles. For transfer cross sections at low energy $\sim$10 MeV/u, CDCC is in good agreement with the Faddeev-type results and the discrepancy increases with beam energy. On the contrary, breakup observables obtained with CDCC are in good agreement with Faddeev-type results for all but the lower energies considered here.

# Testing the continuum discretized coupled channel method for deuteron induced reactions

## Key claim

The Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method provides a good approximation to the exact three-body Faddeev-AGS solution for deuteron-induced elastic, transfer, and breakup reactions, but its accuracy depends strongly on beam energy and observable: transfer cross sections agree well at low energies (around 10 MeV/u) but discrepancies increase with beam energy, while breakup observables agree well at higher energies but CDCC breaks down at the lowest energies studied.

## Method

The authors compared CDCC calculations with exact Faddeev-AGS (FAGS) solutions for deuteron-induced reactions on 10Be, 12C, and 48Ca at multiple beam energies, using the same three-body Hamiltonian in both formulations. The Hamiltonian included a Gaussian neutron-proton interaction and CH89 global optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus interactions, with spins neglected to reduce computational cost. CDCC calculations discretized the deuteron continuum into bins and solved coupled-channel equations, while FAGS calculations solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas integral equations in momentum space with screening and renormalization for Coulomb.

## Key numbers

- For 10Be(d,p)11Be at Ed = 21.4 MeV, CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections at the peak show a discrepancy of 3 ± 1%
- For 12C(d,p)13C at Ed = 56 MeV, the discrepancy between CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections at the peak is -21 ± 1%
- For 10Be(d,p)11Be at Ed = 71 MeV, the discrepancy between CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections at the peak is -53 ± 47%
- For 12C(d,p)13C at Ed = 12 MeV, the discrepancy between CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections at the peak is 6 ± 3%
- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed = 56 MeV, the discrepancy between CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections at the peak is 39 ± 14%
- CDCC model space included p-n partial waves up to lmax = 4 for 10Be and lmax = 6 for 12C and 48Ca
- Faddeev-AGS calculations used maximal total angular momenta Jmax = 60 and Coulomb screening radius RC = 16 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between CDCC and Faddeev methods by Nunes et al. (2011) that found good agreement for elastic and breakup on 12C and 58Ni but disagreement for 11Be on protons. It also connects to studies by Nguyen et al. (2010) and Nunes et al. (2011) that benchmarked the finite-range adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) against Faddeev-AGS, finding ADWA validity for (d,p) reactions at low energies. The results establish a quantified range of validity for CDCC, showing it is reliable for transfer at low energies (around 10 MeV/u) but requires caution at higher beam energies, while for breakup the opposite energy dependence is observed. This enables future users of CDCC to assess uncertainties when applying the method to experimental data analysis.
