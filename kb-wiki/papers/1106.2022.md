# On a translationally invariant nuclear single particle picture

> If one assumes a translationally invariant motion of the nucleons relative to the c. m. position in single particle mean fields a correlated single particle picture of the nuclear wave function emerges. A single particle product ansatz leads for that Hamiltonian to nonlinear equations for the single particle wave functions. In contrast to a standard not translationally invariant shell model picture those single particle s-, p- etc states are coupled. The strength of the resulting coupling is an open question. The Schroedinger equation for that Hamiltonian can be solved by few- and many -body techniques, which will allow to check the validity or non-validity of a single particle product ansatz. Realistic nuclear wave functions exhibit repulsive 2-body short range correlations. Therefore a translationally invariant single particle picture -- if useful at all -- can only be expected beyond those ranges. Since exact A = 3 and 4 nucleon ground state wave functions and beyond based on modern nuclear forces are available, the translationally invariant shell model picture can be optimized by an adjustment to the exact wave function and its validity or non-validity decided.

## Key claim

If one assumes translationally invariant single-particle motion relative to the center of mass, a correlated single-particle product ansatz for the nuclear wave function leads to nonlinear equations for the single-particle wave functions; however, the strength of the coupling between s-, p-, and other states that arises in this picture (in contrast to the standard non-translationally invariant shell model) remains an open question, and the validity of the product ansatz can only be expected beyond the range of repulsive two-body short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors formulate a translationally invariant shell-model Hamiltonian for A=3 and A=4 nucleons using coordinates u_i = x_i - X (the position relative to the center of mass), which obey the constraint sum_i u_i = 0. They derive the nonlinear Schroedinger equations that result from a product ansatz for the spatial wave function (a symmetric product of s-wave functions R(u_i)) and show that for a harmonic oscillator potential the ansatz is exact. They then rewrite the Hamiltonian in standard Jacobi coordinates and propose solving the resulting Schroedinger equation exactly via Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space, using a Lanczos-type iterative algorithm, to test the quality of the product ansatz against the exact solution. Finally, they outline an optimization procedure to extract the optimal single-particle function R(u) from realistic three- and four-nucleon wave functions by minimizing the squared difference between the exact wave function and the product ansatz, restricted to pair distances above a cutoff r_0.

## Key numbers

- The spin-isospin state chi_3 for the ppn system accounts for about 90% of the norm of realistic 3He wave functions.
- The spin-isospin state chi_4 for the ppnn system accounts for about 90% of the norm of realistic alpha-particle wave functions.
- For the harmonic oscillator potential with four particles, the product ansatz yields the lowest energy E = (9/2) omega.
- For the harmonic oscillator potential with three particles, the product ansatz yields the lowest energy E = 3 omega.
- The two-body correlation function for finding two nucleons at distance r has its maximum around r = 1 fm universally for all light nuclei.
- The cutoff distance r_0 for which the single-particle picture might apply is expected to be somewhat smaller than 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of the nuclear shell model by addressing its violation of translational invariance, which has been approached previously by methods such as the generator coordinate method (Giraud, Hill, Griffin, Wong). The authors propose a translationally invariant formulation using coordinates relative to the center of mass, which introduces coupling between single-particle states that is absent in the standard shell model. The paper enables a quantitative test of whether realistic nuclear wave functions (routinely generated for A=3 and A=4 from modern two- and three-nucleon forces such as AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, and chiral effective field theory forces) can be represented by a correlated single-particle product ansatz at distances beyond the short-range repulsive core, and provides the formal framework (Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations and optimization algorithms) to perform that test.
