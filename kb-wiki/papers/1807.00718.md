# The $Ω(2012)$ as a hadronic molecule

> Recently the Belle collaboration has discovered a narrow $S=-3$ baryon, the $Ω(2012)$. We explore the possibility that the $Ω(2012)$ is a $Ξ(1530)\,\bar K$ molecule, where the binding mechanism is the coupled channel dynamics with the $Ω\,η$ channel. The characteristic signature of a molecular $Ω(2012)$ will be its decay into the three body channel $Ξπ\bar{K}$, for which we expect a partial decay width of $2-3\,{\rm MeV}$. The partial decay width into the $Ξ\bar{K}$ channel should lie in the range of $1-11\,{\rm MeV}$, a figure compatible with experiment and which we have deduced from the assumption that the coupling involved in this decay is of natural size. For comparison purposes the decay of a purely compact $Ω(2012)$ into the $Ξ\bar{K}$ and $Ξπ\bar{K}$ channels is of the same order of magnitude as and one order of magnitude smaller than in the molecular scenario, respectively. This comparison indicates that the current experimental information is insufficient to distinguish between a compact and a molecular $Ω(2012)$ and further experiments will be required to determine its nature. A molecular $Ω(2012)$ will also imply the existence of two- and three-body molecular partners. The two-body partners comprise two $Λ$ hyperons located at $1740$ and $1950\,{\rm MeV}$ respectively, the first of which might correspond to the $Λ(1800)$ while the second to the $Λ(2000)$ or the $Λ(2050)$. The three-body partners include a $Ξ(1530) K\bar{K}$ and a $Ξ(1530) η\bar{K}$ molecule, with masses of $M = 2385-2445\,{\rm MeV}$ and $M = 2434-2503\,{\rm MeV}$ respectively. We might be tempted to identify the first with the $Ξ(2370)$ and the latter with the $Ω(2470)$ listed in the PDG.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $\Omega(2012)$ baryon discovered by Belle could be a $\Xi(1530)\,\bar K$ hadronic molecule bound by coupled-channel dynamics with the $\Omega\,\eta$ channel, but explicitly states that current experimental information is insufficient to distinguish between this molecular hypothesis and a compact three-quark state.

## Method

The author uses a Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) interaction in a coupled-channel formalism for the $\Xi(1530)\bar K$ and $\Omega\eta$ systems, with a Gaussian regulator and a cut-off parameter. The binding is determined by solving a dynamical equation equivalent to the Kadyshevsky equation with non-relativistic baryons and relativistic mesons. Decay widths are computed for the $\Xi\pi\bar K$ channel (from the $\Xi(1530)$ decay) and the $\Xi\bar K$ channel (via a short-range contact operator), with theoretical uncertainties estimated by varying the cut-off in the range $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0\,{\rm GeV}$.

## Key numbers

- The $\Omega(2012)$ mass from Belle: $M = 2012.4 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.6$ and width $\Gamma = 6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} \pm 1.6$ (units MeV)
- Cut-off required to generate a pole at $2012\,{\rm MeV}$: $\Lambda = 721\,{\rm MeV}$ (compared to $571\,{\rm MeV}$ for the $\Lambda(1405)$ and $823\,{\rm MeV}$ for the $D_{s0}(2317)$)
- Predicted partial decay width for $\Omega(2012) \to \Xi \pi \bar{K}$ in the molecular scenario: $2-3\,{\rm MeV}$
- Predicted partial decay width for $\Omega(2012) \to \Xi \bar{K}$ in the molecular scenario: $1-11\,{\rm MeV}$ (for $f = f_\pi$) or $1-5\,{\rm MeV}$ (for $f = f_K \simeq 160\,{\rm MeV}$)
- For a compact $\Omega(2012)$ with $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-$, the $\Xi \bar{K}$ decay width is $5-14\,{\rm MeV}$
- Predicted two-body molecular partners: a $\Xi K$ bound state at $1739-1743\,{\rm MeV}$ (candidate $\Lambda(1800)$) and a $\Xi^* K$ bound state at $1948-1951\,{\rm MeV}$ (candidate $\Lambda(2000)$ or $\Lambda(2050)$)
- Predicted three-body molecular partners: a $\Xi^* K\bar{K}$ state at $2385-2445\,{\rm MeV}$ (candidate $\Xi(2370)$) and a $\Xi^* \bar{K} \eta$-$\Omega \eta \eta$ state at $2434-2503\,{\rm MeV}$ (candidate $\Omega(2470)$)

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of hadrons previously applied to the $\Lambda(1405)$ (as an $N\bar K$ molecule, studied by Jido (2003), Magas (2005), and Hyodo (2007)) and the $D_{s0}(2317)$ (as a $D K$ molecule, studied by Guo (2006, 2009)). The paper directly builds on the suggestion by Polyakov (2018) that the $\Omega(2012)$ could be molecular, and uses the same WT interaction framework previously employed by Sarkar (2004) and Si-Qi (2016) for the $\Xi(1530)\bar K$-$\Omega\eta$ system. The results enable future experimental searches for the $\Omega(2012) \to \Xi \pi \bar{K}$ decay as a discriminant between molecular and compact interpretations, and predict partner states that could be identified with known PDG baryons such as the $\Lambda(1800)$, $\Lambda(2000)$, $\Xi(2370)$, and $\Omega(2470)$.
