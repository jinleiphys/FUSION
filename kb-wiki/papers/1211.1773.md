# Elastic enhancement factor as a quantum chaos probe

> Recent development of the resonance scattering theory with a transient from the regular to chaotic internal dynamics inspires renewed interest to the problem of the elastic enhancement phenomenon. We reexamine the question what the experimentally observed value of the elastic enhancement factor can tell us about the character of dynamics of the intermediate system. Noting first a remarkable connection of this factor with the time delays variance in the case of the standard Gaussian ensembles we then prove the universal nature of such a relation. This reduces our problem to that of calculation of the Dyson's binary form factor in the whole transition region. By the example of systems with no time-reversal symmetry we then demonstrate that the enhancement can serve as a measure of the degree of internal chaos.

# Elastic enhancement factor as a quantum chaos probe

## Key claim

The elastic enhancement factor, which quantifies the excess of elastic over inelastic resonance cross sections, can serve as a measure of the degree of internal chaos in an open quantum system, but only when the openness parameter η is small enough that the dwell time exceeds the Heisenberg time; otherwise the factor reverts to the value typical of regular dynamics regardless of the actual chaoticity.

## Method

The authors derive a general relation between the elastic enhancement factor F and the variance of time delays, valid for arbitrary degree of internal chaoticity, by expressing F in terms of the Dyson binary form factor B₂(s|κ) for a transient ensemble that interpolates between Poissonian (regular, κ=0) and Gaussian (chaotic, κ→∞) level statistics. They then compute the binary form factor analytically for systems with broken time-reversal symmetry (β=2) using the Brezin-Hikami approach, and obtain the enhancement factor F(η|κ) as a function of openness η and chaoticity parameter κ through integral representations and asymptotic expansions.

## Key numbers

- The universal slope of the enhancement factor at zero openness is ∂F(η|κ≠0)/∂η|_(η=0) = −1/2, independent of the chaoticity parameter κ.
- For the fully regular limit (κ=0), the enhancement factor is F(η|0) ≡ 2.
- For the fully chaotic GUE limit (κ→∞), the enhancement factor is F_GUE = 1 + (1−e^(−η))/η, which expands as 2 − η/2 + ... for small η.
- The small-κ expansion of F(η|κ) is given as: F(η|κ) = 2 − κ/η + (6+η)κ²/η³ − (60 + η(20+η))κ³/η⁵ + ...
- The large-κ approximation is: F(η|κ) ≈ 1 + (1−e^(−η))/η + η/(η+κ) − η/(η+κ)².
- The critical openness η_c(κ) where the enhancement factor reaches its minimum F_min is defined by ∂F(η|κ)/∂η|_(η=η_c(κ)) = 0.

## Context

This work extends the random-matrix theory of elastic enhancement, originally developed for fully chaotic systems by Lewenkopf, Pluhar, Verbaarshot, and Fyodorov, to the transition from regular to chaotic internal dynamics. It builds on the numerical discovery by Celardo (2012) that the enhancement factor is sensitive to the interaction strength controlling internal chaos, and provides the analytical foundation for using F as a quantum chaos probe. The results enable experimental determination of the degree of internal chaos in compound nuclei, quantum dots, and microwave cavities by measuring the elastic enhancement factor and openness. The authors note that the corresponding binary form factor for time-reversal-invariant systems (β=1) remains to be derived, with a method suggested by Brezin (2003).
