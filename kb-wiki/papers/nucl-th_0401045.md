# Present Status of the Theory of Fission of hot Nuclei

> Recent progress in the theory of fission of hot nuclei is reported. We discuss in particular the properties of the friction formfactor as function of the deformation (and possibly of the temperature) which are necessary to reproduce data concerning fission of hot nuclei and its accompanying light particle and $γ$-ray emission. Recent theoretical work gives support to a phenomenological friction form factor (proposed some time ago), which is weak for compact shapes and increases on the way to scission.

## Key claim

Recent theoretical work supports a phenomenological friction form factor that is weak for compact nuclear shapes and increases on the way to scission, but the temperature dependence of this friction form factor predicted by microscopic theory still needs to be clarified by using it in Langevin calculations and comparing with experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews multi-dimensional Langevin dynamics calculations coupled to a Monte Carlo treatment of evaporation processes for fission of hot compound nuclei. It compares the chaos-weighted wall formula of Pal and coworkers (1996) and the temperature-dependent friction form factor of Aleshin (2003) against the phenomenological friction form factor of Frobrich and Gontchar (1993), evaluating their ability to reproduce experimental data on fission probabilities, pre-scission neutron multiplicities, and kinetic energy distributions of fission fragments.

## Key numbers

- Wall friction strength must be reduced by a factor K_s = 0.25–0.5 to reproduce experimental data for systems such as 172Y, 205,215Fr, and 224Th in multi-dimensional Langevin calculations
- The strength at scission for the phenomenological friction form factor is beta = 30 × 10^21 sec^-1, comparable to the surface friction model in the exit channel of deep-inelastic collisions
- A fission rate calculated with an influence functional path integral technique gives a 20% enhancement compared to a Kramers rate for fission of 224Th at a temperature of 1.57 MeV
- The temperature-dependent friction form factor of Aleshin is calculated for 208Pb in the temperature range T = 2.1–3.3 MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier review by Frobrich and Gontchar (1998) on fission of hot nuclei and its accompanying processes. The work contradicts the approach of Abe and collaborators (1993, 2003), who apply the wall formula without reducing its strength. The paper enables future systematic multi-dimensional Langevin analyses using temperature- and deformation-dependent form factors from Aleshin (2003) and Ivanyuk et al. (1997), and calls for a unified model that can also handle fast fission components identified in increasing amounts of experimental data.
