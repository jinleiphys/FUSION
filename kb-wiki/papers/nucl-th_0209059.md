# Possible Origin of RHIC R_{out}/R_{sid} HBT Results

> The effects of opacity of the nuclei together with a blackbody type of emission along the system history are considered as a means to explain the ratio $R_{out}/R_{sid}$ observed by STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC. Within our model, no flow is required to explain the data trend of this ratio for large surface emissivities.

## Key claim

The ratio \(R_{out}/R_{sid}\) observed by STAR and PHENIX at RHIC can be explained without requiring transverse flow, provided the source is highly opaque and emits like a blackbody from its surface throughout its evolution; the model succeeds for full emissivity (\(\kappa=1\)) but fails for reduced emissivity (\(\kappa=0.5\)).

## Method

The system is modeled as a longitudinally expanding cylinder following Bjorken hydrodynamics, with a blackbody-type radiation from its external surface and an opacity parameter \(\kappa\) controlling the surface emissivity. The evolution proceeds through a Quark-Gluon Plasma phase, a mixed phase at constant temperature \(T_c = 175\) MeV, and a pion gas phase until freeze-out at \(T_f = 150\) MeV; the two-particle correlation function is computed using the Covariant Current Ensemble formalism. The single-inclusive transverse momentum distribution and the HBT radii ratio \(R_{out}/R_{sid}\) are compared to PHENIX minimum-bias data and to preliminary STAR and PHENIX interferometry data for \(\pi^+\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\pi^-\).

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature \(T_0 \sim 411\) MeV and formation time \(\tau_0 \sim 0.160\) fm/c, derived from an initial entropy matching an average pion multiplicity per unit rapidity of \(\mathcal{N} \sim 1000\) at RHIC.
* For emissivity \(\kappa = 1\): \(\tau_c = 1.54\) fm/c, \(\tau_h = 5.73\) fm/c, \(\tau_f = 6.97\) fm/c; surface emission fraction \(\mathcal{S}/\mathcal{N}_{tot} = 0.844\) and volume emission fraction \(\mathcal{V}/\mathcal{N}_{tot} = 0.156\).
* For emissivity \(\kappa = 0.5\): \(\tau_c = 1.75\) fm/c, \(\tau_h = 8.37\) fm/c, \(\tau_f = 10.5\) fm/c; surface emission fraction \(\mathcal{S}/\mathcal{N}_{tot} = 0.758\) and volume emission fraction \(\mathcal{V}/\mathcal{N}_{tot} = 0.242\).
* The model with \(\kappa = 1\) describes the single-inclusive spectrum well up to \(k_{i_T} \approx 1\) GeV/c and reproduces the \(R_{out}/R_{sid}\) ratio within experimental error bars; the \(\kappa = 0.5\) curve lies above the data limits.

## Context

This work extends the opacity model of Heiselberg and Vischer (CERN energies) and the later treatment by Heinz and Tomasik, applying it to RHIC energies to address the unexpected decrease of \(R_{out}/R_{sid}\) with increasing \(K_T\) that contradicted predictions from hydrodynamic models and microscopic simulations. The results suggest that high surface emissivity (about 80% of particles emitted from the surface) is the key mechanism, a feature not captured by standard Cooper-Frye decoupling in hydrodynamic simulations; the model enables future inclusion of a time-varying transverse radius and transverse flow, as noted in the work of Heinz and Kolb and of Teaney.
