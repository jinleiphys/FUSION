# Cornell potential in generalized Soft Wall holographic model

> We derive and analyze the confinement potential of the Cornell type within the framework of the generalized Soft Wall holographic model that includes a parameter controlling the intercept of the linear Regge spectrum. In the phenomenology of Regge trajectories, this parameter is very important for the quantitative description of experimental data. Our analysis shows that the ''linear plus Coulomb'' confinement potential obtained in the scalar channel is quantitatively consistent with the phenomenology and lattice simulations while the agreement in the vector channel is qualitative only. This result indicates the key role of the vacuum scalar sector in the formation of the confinement potential. As a by-product the overall consistency of our holographic description of confinement potential seems to confirm the glueball nature of the scalar meson $f_0(1500)$.

## Key claim

The generalized Soft Wall holographic model, which includes a parameter controlling the intercept of the linear Regge spectrum, yields a "linear plus Coulomb" confinement potential that is quantitatively consistent with phenomenology and lattice simulations in the scalar channel, but only qualitatively consistent in the vector channel; this result suggests the vacuum scalar sector plays a key role in forming the confinement potential and supports the glueball interpretation of the scalar meson f0(1500).

## Method

The authors generalized the Soft Wall holographic model by introducing a Tricomi hypergeometric function U(b,0,|cz^2|) into the dilaton background, which modifies the AdS metric and produces a linear Regge spectrum with arbitrary intercept b. They derived the heavy-quark potential from the holographic Wilson loop using the Nambu-Goto action in the modified AdS metric, obtaining parametric integral expressions for the distance r and energy E. The large and small distance asymptotics of the potential were then extracted analytically by expanding the integrals in the limits of large and small parameter lambda = cz_0^2.

## Key numbers

* The slope of the linear Regge spectrum was fixed to a = 4c = 1.1 GeV^2, based on the mean slope from the compilation of light meson Regge trajectories.
* For the standard SW model (b=0), the large-distance string tension asymptotics reduce to sigma_infinity(0) = (e/pi)c approximately 0.87c.
* The physical intercept value b = -1/2 leads to the prediction m_rho^2 = 2c = a/2, which correctly reproduces the rho-meson mass.
* The small-distance asymptotics yield the Coulomb coefficient kappa_0 = 1/(Gamma(1+b)^4 * 2*pi*rho^2) and the linear coefficient sigma_0 = (1+2b*psi(b+1)+4b*gamma)/(Gamma(1+b)^4) * c * rho^2, where rho = Gamma(1/4)^2/(2*pi)^(3/2).

## Context

This work extends the original Soft Wall holographic model analysis of the heavy-quark potential by Andreev and Zakharov (2006), which was performed only for the vector case with fixed intercept b=0, to include arbitrary intercept and the scalar channel. The results are consistent with lattice simulations of the Cornell potential reviewed by Bali (2001) and with the light-front holographic QCD approach of Brodsky et al. (2010) and Trawinski et al. (2014). The analysis enables quantitative phenomenological tests of holographic confinement models and provides evidence for the glueball nature of the scalar meson f0(1500).
