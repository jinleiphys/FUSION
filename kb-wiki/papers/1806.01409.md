# Coupling charge-exchange vibrations to nucleons in a relativistic framework: effect on Gamow-Teller transitions and beta-decay half-lives

> The nuclear response theory for isospin-transfer modes in the relativistic particle-vibration coupling framework is extended to include coupling of single nucleons to isospin-flip (charge-exchange) phonons, in addition to the usual neutral vibrations. This new coupling introduces dynamical pion and rho-meson exchange, beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation, up to infinite order. We investigate the impact of this new mechanism on the Gamow-Teller response of a few doubly-magic neutron-rich nuclei, namely $^{48}$Ca, $^{78}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb. It is found that the coupling to isospin-flip vibrations can have a non negligible impact on the strength distribution and quenching of the giant resonance, globally improving the agreement with the experimental data. The corresponding beta-decay half-lives of $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn are also calculated, and found to be decreased by a factor $\sim 2$ by the inclusion of the new phonons.

# Coupling charge-exchange vibrations to nucleons in a relativistic framework: effect on Gamow-Teller transitions and beta-decay half-lives

## Key claim

The coupling of single nucleons to isospin-flip (charge-exchange) phonons, in addition to the usual neutral vibrations, has a non-negligible impact on Gamow-Teller strength distributions and quenching of the giant resonance, globally improving agreement with experimental data; however, the resulting beta-decay half-lives of 78Ni and 132Sn are decreased by a factor of approximately 2 and become slightly underestimated compared to experiment, suggesting that other mechanisms such as meson-exchange currents or ground-state correlations may also be needed.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic particle-vibration coupling (PVC) framework for isospin-transfer modes by including coupling of single nucleons to charge-exchange phonons, which introduces dynamical pion and rho-meson exchange beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation up to infinite order. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the time-blocking approximation (TBA) for the proton-neutron response function with Jπ = 1+, using the NL3 parametrization of the relativistic mean field, and included neutral phonons with Jπ = 2+,3-,4+,5-,6+ and charge-exchange phonons with Jπ = 0± through 7± having excitation energies below 20 MeV. The Gamow-Teller strength distributions were calculated for 48Ca, 78Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb, and beta-decay half-lives were computed for 78Ni and 132Sn using the bare weak axial coupling constant gA ≈ 1.27.

## Key numbers

- In 48Ca, the main Gamow-Teller giant resonance peak around 13 MeV is reduced by approximately 43% when charge-exchange phonons are added to the coupling with neutral phonons.
- In 132Sn, the giant resonance peak value is reduced by approximately 24% when charge-exchange phonons are included.
- The beta-decay half-life of 78Ni decreases from 1.8806 s (pn-RRPA) to 0.0731 s (with neutral phonons) and further to 0.0395 s (with neutral and charge-exchange phonons), compared to the experimental value of 0.110 (+0.210, -0.05) s.
- The beta-decay half-life of 132Sn decreases from 1626.729 s (pn-RRPA) to 36.427 s (with neutral phonons) and further to 16.592 s (with neutral and charge-exchange phonons), compared to the experimental value of 39.7 (+0.8, -0.8) s.
- In 78Ni, the coupling to neutral phonons moves about 10% of the Gamow-Teller strength above 20 MeV (compared to 2% in pn-RRPA), and this grows to 15% when charge-exchange phonons are included.
- The smearing parameter Δ was taken as 200 keV for 48Ca and 1 MeV for 132Sn and 208Pb to reproduce experimental energy resolution.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic particle-vibration coupling framework previously developed by Litvinova and collaborators (Litvinova 2006, 2007, 2008; Robin 2016), which had only included neutral (non isospin-flip) vibrations, to incorporate charge-exchange phonons for the first time in the description of two-body response. The new mechanism addresses the long-standing problem of Gamow-Teller strength quenching observed in charge-exchange experiments, which had previously been attributed to Delta resonance excitation (Kurasawa 2003) or higher-order 2p-2h configurations. The results have direct implications for beta-decay half-lives of r-process waiting point nuclei such as 78Ni and 132Sn, and the formalism represents a conceptual advancement of the isovector sector of Quantum Hadrodynamics.
