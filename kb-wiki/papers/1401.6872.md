# Scaling Properties of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 200$ GeV

> We present the scaling properties of mass number of light (anti)nuclei production in midrapidity Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=200$ GeV based on the PACIAE + DCPC model. It is found that the integrated yield of light (anti)nuclei decreased exponentially with the increase of mass numbers which depends on the centrality, this properties of the system can be described quantitatively by temperature $T$ at hadronic freeze-out, and the model results are consistent with STAR data. Furthermore, we found that the integrated yield of heavier (anti)nuclei per participant nucleon increases from peripheral to central collisions more rapidly than that of $d(\bar{d})$, indicating that the mass scale of light (anti)nuclei production was presented in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The integrated yield of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV decreases exponentially with mass number in a centrality-dependent manner, and this can be described quantitatively by a temperature \(T\) at hadronic freeze-out; the model results are consistent with STAR data, but the claim is conditional on the specific parameters of the PACIAE + DCPC model, including fitted parameters for strange particle production and the chosen mass uncertainty \(\Delta m\) for each nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to generate final-state hadrons from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV, with parameters tuned to reproduce STAR data on \(\Lambda\), \(\overline{\Lambda}\), \(\Xi^-\), and \(\overline{\Xi}^-\) yields. They then applied the Dynamically Constrained Phase-space Coalescence (DCPC) model to these final states, imposing constraints on invariant mass and spatial separation to form light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton. Yields were computed for seven centrality bins (0-5% to 40-60%) and fitted to an exponential function of mass number to extract a freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- Integrated yield \(dN/dy\) of deuterons in 0-5% centrality: \(0.0964\) (for \(d\)) and \(0.0433\) (for \(\overline{d}\)).
- Integrated yield \(dN/dy\) of \(^4\)He in 0-5% centrality: \(5.14 \times 10^{-7}\) (for \(^4\)He) and \(1.70 \times 10^{-7}\) (for \(\overline{^4}\)He).
- Temperature extracted from antinuclei yield fit: \(149 \pm 3\) MeV for 0-5% centrality, \(142 \pm 4\) MeV for MiniBias events, and \(125 \pm 6\) MeV for 40-60% centrality.
- The integrated yield \(dN/dy\) spans almost 5 orders of magnitude across mass numbers \(A = 1\) to \(A = 4\).
- Model parameters: \(K = 3\), parj(1) = 0.12, parj(2) = 0.55, parj(3) = 0.65; mass uncertainty \(\Delta m = 0.00035\) GeV for \(d,\overline{d}\), 0.00055 GeV for \(^3\)He and hypertriton, and 0.0007 GeV for \(^4\)He.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of light nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the mass-number scaling observed by the STAR Collaboration (STAR, 2009; STAR, 2007) and earlier theoretical work using coalescence and statistical models (e.g., Greiner, 1995; Chen, 2008; Ma, 2012). The authors' previous studies using the PACIAE + DCPC model had already predicted yields and centrality dependence for these nuclei (Chen, 2013; Chen, 2014). The new finding of a scaling property where the relative yield invariant \(R_{CYA}(N_{\rm part})\) is independent of the type of constituent hadrons provides a potential new tool for exploring hadronic final-state dynamics and production mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors expect this scaling to be testable in future experiments.
