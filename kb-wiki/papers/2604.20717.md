# Rank-2 Electromagnetic Backgrounds and Angular Momentum Barriers in Gravitomagnetic Spin-Quadrupole Searches

> We present a complete analysis of the angular momentum selection rules and electromagnetic backgrounds that constrain any spectroscopic search for the gravitomagnetic spin-quadrupole coupling in highly charged ions. A sequence of four barriers is identified: (i)~the Wigner-Eckart theorem mandates $j \geq 3/2$ electronic states for sensitivity to the rank-2 gravitomagnetic operator, excluding the deformation-immune $j=1/2$ states; (ii)~the nuclear electric quadrupole hyperfine interaction (HFS-E2) generates an $\sim 18$-orders-of-magnitude electromagnetic background in the required $j=3/2$ channel; (iii)~second-order HFS mixing between fine-structure levels leaves a residual $\sim 10^{-6}$ eV even after centroid extraction; (iv)~tensor nuclear polarizability (TNP), scaling with $B(E2)$ rather than $Q_s$, introduces an independent rank-2 background of $\sim 10^{-12}$ eV. We derive the algebraic conditions under which a multi-isotope, multi-transition Generalized King Plot can separate these backgrounds from the gravitational signal, and show that the minimum experimental topology requires three transitions and $N_{\text{odd}} \geq N_{\text{bkg}} + 1$ odd-spin isotopes with linearly independent nuclear parameters. For the molybdenum chain, this yields a first laboratory-derivable bound $|χ- 1| \lesssim 10^{8} - 10^9$ on the gyrogravitational ratio, limited by current precision on nuclear quadrupole moments and transition rates. We quantify the experimental milestones needed to improve this bound by each order of magnitude, providing a roadmap for future searches.

# Rank-2 Electromagnetic Backgrounds and Angular Momentum Barriers in Gravitomagnetic Spin-Quadrupole Searches

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spectroscopic searches for the gravitomagnetic spin-quadrupole coupling in highly charged ions face a hierarchy of four electromagnetic barriers that collectively produce a structured gap of approximately eight orders of magnitude between achievable sensitivity and the predicted General Relativity signal, with the algebraic separability condition requiring at least three odd-spin isotopes or two rank-2-sensitive transitions to extract the gravitational coupling from the backgrounds.

## Method

The authors perform a complete angular-momentum analysis of the rank-2 gravitomagnetic operator using the Wigner-Eckart theorem, then compute the successive electromagnetic backgrounds: first-order nuclear electric quadrupole hyperfine interaction (HFS-E2), second-order HFS mixing between fine-structure levels, and tensor nuclear polarizability (TNP). They derive the algebraic conditions for a multi-isotope, multi-transition Generalized King Plot to separate these backgrounds from the gravitational signal, and apply the formalism to the molybdenum isotope chain using known nuclear data from Coulomb excitation and spectroscopic compilations.

## Key numbers

- The gravitomagnetic signal in the $2p_{3/2}$ state of hydrogen-like ${}^{95}\text{Mo}^{41+}$ is estimated as $\Delta E_{GM} \sim [10^{-22},\, 10^{-20}]$ eV, with a nominal baseline of $\sim 2\times 10^{-21}$ eV
- The first-order HFS-E2 background is $\sim 10^{-4}$ eV for ${}^{95}\text{Mo}$ ($Q_s = -0.022$ b) and $\sim 10^{-2}$ eV for ${}^{97}\text{Mo}$ ($Q_s = +0.255$ b) in the $2p_{3/2}$ state
- The second-order HFS mixing residual after centroid extraction is $\sim 7 \times 10^{-14}$ eV for ${}^{95}\text{Mo}$, calculated from $(10^{-4})^2/150$ eV with $\Delta E_{\text{FS}}(2p) \approx 150$ eV and 0.1% atomic theory accuracy
- The TNP shift is of order $10^{-11}$--$10^{-12}$ eV, with current $B(E2)$ knowledge at $\sim 10$--$15\%$ precision
- The combined current electromagnetic residual is $\sim 10^{-13}$ eV, yielding a bound $|\chi - 1| \lesssim 10^{8} - 10^{9}$ on the gyrogravitational ratio
- The fine-structure splitting $\Delta E_{\text{FS}}(2p) \approx 150$ eV for Mo$^{41+}$
- The nuclear radius $R_N \approx 5.5$ fm for molybdenum
- The mixing coefficient between $P_{1/2}$ and $P_{3/2}$ states is $\delta_{\text{mix}} \sim 7 \times 10^{-7}$ for ${}^{95}\text{Mo}$
- The Ramsey interrogation time for radioactive ${}^{91}\text{Mo}$ ($t_{1/2} = 15.5$ min) is $T_R^{\text{opt}} \approx 11$ min, giving per-ion statistical sensitivity $\delta\nu \sim 2 \times 10^{-4}$ Hz

## Context

This work extends the Generalized King Plot methodology previously applied to scalar new-physics searches (Berengut 2018, Counts 2020, Hur 2022) to the rank-2 gravitomagnetic operator, revealing that rank-2 operators face a qualitatively harder structural topology than scalar operators because they require $j \geq 3/2$ electronic states. The paper contrasts with macroscopic frame-dragging experiments such as Gravity Probe B (Everitt 2011), which constrain orbital angular momentum coupling at the 0.3% level but do not probe intrinsic spin coupling. The results provide a quantitative roadmap for future experimental searches, identifying specific nuclear data improvements needed from FRIB Coulomb excitation and muonic atom spectroscopy, and metrological advances in quantum logic spectroscopy and X-ray frequency combs.
