# Helicity Evolution at Small $x$: Revised Asymptotic Results at Large $N_c\& N_f$

> We present a numerical solution of the revised version of the small-$x$ helicity evolution equations at large $N_c$ and $N_f$. (Here $N_c$ and $N_f$ are the numbers of quark colors and flavors, respectively.) The evolution equations are double-logarithmic in the Bjorken $x$ variable, resumming powers of $α_s \, \ln^2 (1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant. The large-$N_c \& N_f$ evolution we consider includes contributions of small-$x$ quark emissions and is thus more realistic than the large-$N_c$ one, which only involves gluon emissons. The evolution equations are written for the so-called ``polarized dipole amplitudes", which are related to the helicity distribution functions and the $g_1$ structure function. Unlike the previously reported solution of the earlier version of helicity evolution equations at large $N_c \& N_f$, our solution does not exhibit periodic oscillations in $\ln (1/x)$ for $N_f < 2 N_c$, while only showing occasional sign reversals. For $N_f = 2 N_c$, we report oscillations with $\ln (1/x)$, similar to those found earlier. We determine the intercept of our evolution for $N_f < 2 N_c$ as well as the parameters of the oscillatory behavior for $N_f = 2 N_c$. We compare our results to the existing resummation and finite-order calculations for helicity-dependent quantities in the literature.

## Key claim
The revised large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution equations, which include small-\(x\) quark emissions, produce asymptotic solutions for helicity parton distribution functions that do **not** exhibit periodic oscillations in \(\ln(1/x)\) for \(N_f < 2N_c\), contrary to the earlier version of the equations; oscillations reappear only for the special case \(N_f = 2N_c\).

## Method
The authors numerically solved the revised double-logarithmic small-\(x\) helicity evolution equations (resumming powers of \(\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)\)) in the large-\(N_c \& N_f\) limit, using a discretization of the evolution variables \(\eta = \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)} \ln(zs/\Lambda^2)\) and \(s_{10} = \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)} \ln(1/(x_{10}^2 \Lambda^2))\). The system of six linear integral equations for the polarized dipole amplitudes \(Q\), \(\widetilde{G}\), \(G_2\), \(\overline{\Gamma}\), \(\widetilde{\Gamma}\), and \(\Gamma_2\) was solved recursively, with the initial conditions taken either in the Born-level approximation or in a constant ("all-one") approximation to extract the asymptotic small-\(x\) behavior.

## Key numbers
- The intercept \(\alpha_h\) (the power in \(\Delta\Sigma \sim \Delta G \sim (1/x)^{\alpha_h}\)) is found to be \(\alpha_h \approx 3.66 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)}\) for the large-\(N_c\) limit (from prior work, cited as Cougoulic (2022) and Borden (2023)).
- For the large-\(N_c \& N_f\) case with \(N_f < 2N_c\), the solution shows no periodic oscillations in \(\ln(1/x)\); only occasional sign reversals occur.
- For \(N_f = 2N_c\), oscillations with \(\ln(1/x)\) are reported, similar to those found earlier in Kovchegov (2020).
- The intercepts for different \(N_f/N_c\) ratios are compared to the BER intercepts (Bartels (1996)) and are found to be very close yet slightly different, continuing the trend from the large-\(N_c\) analysis.

## Context
This work revises the earlier numerical solution of the large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution by Kovchegov (2020), which had exhibited oscillatory behavior for all \(N_f < 2N_c\), by implementing the corrections from Cougoulic (2022) (KPS-CTT). The results extend the large-\(N_c\) analysis of Cougoulic (2022) and Borden (2023) to include quark emissions, making the evolution more realistic. The findings enable more accurate predictions for the small-\(x\) asymptotics of the \(g_1\) structure function and helicity parton distribution functions, and they provide a benchmark for comparing with the infrared evolution equation approach of Bartels (1996) and with finite-order perturbative calculations.
