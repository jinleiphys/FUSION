# Bose-Einstein condensation of dilute alpha clusters above four $α$ threshold in $^{16}$O in field theoretical superfluid cluster model

> Observed well-developed $α$ cluster states in $^{16}$O, located above the four $α$ threshold, are investigated from the viewpoint of Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ clusters by using a field-theoretical superfluid cluster model in which the order parameter is defined. The experimental energy levels are reproduced well for the first time by calculation. In particular, the observed 16.7 MeV $0_7^+$ and 18.8 MeV $0_8^+$ states with low-excitation energies from the threshold are found to be understood as a manifestation of the states of the Nambu-Goldstone zero-mode operators, associated with the spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase, which is caused by the Bose-Einstein condensation of the vacuum 15.1 MeV $0^+_6$ state with a dilute well-developed $α$ cluster structure just above the threshold. This gives evidence of the existence of the Bose-Einstein condensate of $α$ clusters in $^{16}$O. It is found that the emergence of the energy level structure with a well-developed $α$ cluster structure above the threshold is robust, almost independently of the condensation rate of $α$ clusters under significant condensation rate. The finding of the mechanism why the level structure that is similar to $^{12}$C emerges above the four $α$ threshold in $^{16}$O reinforces the concept of Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ clusters in addition to $^{12}$C.

## Key claim

The observed 16.7 MeV $0_7^+$ and 18.8 MeV $0_8^+$ states in $^{16}$O, located just above the four-$\alpha$ threshold, are manifestations of Nambu-Goldstone zero-mode states arising from spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase due to Bose-Einstein condensation of the vacuum $0_6^+$ (15.1 MeV) state, but the level structure is robust and almost independent of the condensation rate as long as significant condensation occurs.

## Method

The authors used a field-theoretical superfluid cluster model with an order parameter, solving three coupled equations: the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the order parameter $\xi(r)$, the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for collective oscillations, and a zero-mode equation for the Nambu-Goldstone operators $\hat{Q}$ and $\hat{P}$. The Hamiltonian included a harmonic trapping potential $V_{\text{ex}}(r) = m\Omega^2 r^2/2$ and a residual Ali-Bodmer two-range Gaussian $\alpha$-$\alpha$ interaction, with parameters $\Omega$ and $V_r$ adjusted to reproduce the experimental $0_7^+$ energy and minimize mean square errors for higher-spin states.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum $0_6^+$ state at 15.1 MeV has 61% of its $\alpha$ clusters sitting in the 0s state, used as the condensation rate $N_0 = 0.61 \times 4$.
- The calculated rms radius of the $0_6^+$ (15.1 MeV) state is 5.22 fm, compared to 2.71 fm for the $^{16}$O ground state.
- The first zero-mode state $0_{\nu=1}^+$ is calculated at 1.6 MeV above the vacuum, matching the experimental $0_7^+$ at 16.7 MeV (16.7 - 15.1 = 1.6 MeV).
- The second zero-mode state $0_{\nu=2}^+$ is calculated near the experimental $0_8^+$ at 18.8 MeV.
- The BdG states $2_{n=1}^+$, $4_{n=1}^+$, and $6_{n=1}^+$ agree with experimental centroids; the calculation predicts an $8_{n=1}^+$ state at 23.7 MeV and a $10_{n=1}^+$ state at 26.4 MeV.
- The rotational constant $k$ for the BdG band ($2^+$ to $8^+$) is 93 keV, consistent with experimental values of $95 \pm 20$ keV from Freer (1995) and 86 keV from Ohkubo (2010).
- Calculated $B(\text{E}2: 2_{n=1}^+(\text{BdG}) \rightarrow 0_{\nu=0}^+(\text{ZM}))$ is 369 $e^2\text{fm}^4$ for 61% condensation and 606 $e^2\text{fm}^4$ for 100% condensation.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical superfluid cluster model previously applied to $^{12}$C by Nakamura (2016) and Katsuragi (2018) to the four-$\alpha$ system in $^{16}$O. It confirms the conjecture by Ohkubo (2013) that the $0_7^+$ state is a Nambu-Goldstone state, and provides the first successful reproduction of the observed level structure above the four-$\alpha$ threshold, which had not been achieved by full microscopic four-$\alpha$ cluster model calculations or ab initio methods. The results reinforce the concept of $\alpha$-cluster Bose-Einstein condensation in $^{16}$O, analogous to the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C, and enable future studies of fragmentation of BEC states due to coupling with non-dilute $\alpha$+$^{12}$C configurations.
