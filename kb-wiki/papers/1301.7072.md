# QCD as a topologically ordered system

> We argue that QCD belongs to a topologically ordered phase similar to many well-known condensed matter systems with a gap such as topological insulators or superconductors. Our arguments are based on an analysis of the so-called "deformed QCD" which is a weakly coupled gauge theory, but nevertheless preserves all the crucial elements of strongly interacting QCD, including confinement, nontrivial $θ$ dependence, degeneracy of the topological sectors, etc. Specifically, we construct the so-called topological "BF" action which reproduces the well known infrared features of the theory such as non-dispersive contribution to the topological susceptibility which can not be associated with any propagating degrees of freedom. Furthermore, we interpret the well known resolution of the celebrated $U(1)_A$ problem where the would be $η'$ Goldstone boson generates its mass as a result of mixing of the Goldstone field with a topological auxiliary field characterizing the system. We then identify the non-propagating auxiliary topological field of the BF formulation in deformed QCD with the Veneziano ghost (which plays the crucial role in resolution of the $U(1)_A$ problem). Finally, we elaborate on relation between "string -net" condensation in topologically ordered condensed matter systems and long range coherent configurations, the "skeletons", studied in QCD lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD belongs to a topologically ordered phase similar to condensed matter systems with a gap, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the passage from weakly coupled "deformed QCD" to strongly coupled physical QCD is smooth without any phase transitions, as claimed by Shifman and Yaffe (2008).

## Method

The author analyzes "deformed QCD," a weakly coupled gauge theory on the manifold R^3 x S^1 with a deformation action built from the Polyakov loop that preserves confinement, nontrivial theta dependence, and degeneracy of topological sectors. A topological "BF" action is constructed for this deformed QCD by introducing auxiliary fields that reproduce the infrared features of the theory, including the non-dispersive contact term in topological susceptibility. The non-propagating auxiliary topological field in the BF formulation is identified with the Veneziano ghost that resolves the U(1)_A problem.

## Key numbers

- The topological charge of a fundamental monopole in deformed QCD is Q = ±1/N
- The topological susceptibility in deformed QCD is χ_YM = (ζ/NL) ∫ d^3x [δ(x)], where ζ is the monopole fugacity and L is the compactification length
- The Debye correlation length of the monopole gas is m_σ^2 = Lζ(4π/g)^2
- The η' mass in the model with light quarks is m_η'^2 = ζ/(cN) = χ_YM · (L/c)
- The average number of monopoles in a Debye volume is N = (g/4π)^3 (1/√(L^3ζ)) ≫ 1

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deformed QCD developed by Shifman and Yaffe (2008) and the computation of topological susceptibility by Thomas (2011). It draws an analogy between the infrared behavior of deformed QCD and topologically ordered condensed matter systems such as quantum Hall states (Wen, 1989), superconductors, and topological insulators, which are described by Chern-Simons effective Lagrangians in BF form. The paper interprets lattice QCD results showing long-range coherent structures (Horvath, 2003-2005; Ilgenfritz, 2007-2008; Bruckmann, 2011) as potential evidence for topological order in QCD, and suggests that topological entanglement entropy (Kitaev, 2005; Levin, 2006) could serve as a complementary diagnostic.
