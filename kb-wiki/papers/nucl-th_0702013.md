# Scaling of $v_2$ in heavy ion collisions

> We interpret the scaling of the corrected elliptic flow parameter w.r.t. the corrected multiplicity, observed to hold in heavy ion collisions for a wide variety of energies and system sizes. We use dimensional analysis and power-counting arguments to place constraints on the changes of initial conditions in systems with different center of mass energy $\sqrt{s}$. Specifically, we show that a large class of changes in the (initial) equation of state, mean free path, and longitudinal geometry over the observed $\sqrt{s}$ are likely to spoil the scaling in $v_2$ observed experimentally. We therefore argue that the system produced at most Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies is fundamentally the same as far as the soft and approximately thermalized degrees of freedom are considered. The ``sQGP'' (Strongly interacting Quark-Gluon Plasma) phase, if it is there, is therefore not exclusive to RHIC. We suggest, as a goal for further low-energy heavy ion experiments, to search for a ``transition'' $\sqrt{s}$ where the observed scaling breaks.

## Key claim

The universal scaling of $v_2/\epsilon$ with $\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN}{dy}$ across AGS to RHIC energies implies that microscopic properties (equation of state, mean free path, longitudinal geometry) cannot vary significantly between these systems, meaning the "perfect fluid" is not exclusive to RHIC but is a common characteristic of all experimentally studied heavy ion collisions so far.

## Method

The author uses dimensional analysis and Taylor expansion of elliptic flow and multiplicity around small dimensionless parameters (eccentricity, speed of sound, mean free path divided by system size) to derive constraints on how initial conditions can vary with energy and system size. By comparing the expected scaling from these expansions (using toy models for the equation of state and mean free path) against the experimentally observed universal curve, the author shows that any significant variation in microscopic properties would produce branching structures in the scaling plot that are not observed.

## Key numbers

- The universal scaling holds across systems ranging from AGS ($\sqrt{s} \sim 4.24$ GeV for Au) to RHIC ($\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV for Au), covering a factor of approximately 50 in center-of-mass energy
- The toy model uses a critical temperature $T_c$ for the phase transition, with hadron gas sound speed of 0.1 and QGP sound speed approaching the ideal value
- The mean free path parametrization uses $l_{mfp} = 5$ fm in the hadron gas phase and $l_{mfp} = 0.1$ fm in the QGP phase
- A possible hint of scaling breaking is noted at $\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN}{dy} \sim 6$ fm$^{-2}$, coinciding with the "kink," "horn," and "step" anomalies

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation of universal $v_2$ scaling reported by the PHOBOS and STAR collaborations (2006-2007) and the phenomenological scaling analysis by Lacey and Taranenko (2006). The paper contradicts the narrative that only RHIC achieves a "perfect fluid" sQGP phase, arguing instead that systems from AGS to RHIC are fundamentally similar. The analysis enables future low-energy experiments to search for a transition energy where the scaling breaks, potentially identifying the critical point of the QCD phase diagram. The author notes that the scaling was predicted by Voloshin (1999) for a free-streaming regime, but the observed magnitude of $v_2/\epsilon$ is much larger than that prediction, supporting the hydrodynamic interpretation.
