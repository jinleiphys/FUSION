# Non-Extensive Quantum Statistics with Particle - Hole Symmetry

> Based on Tsallis entropy and the corresponding deformed exponential function, generalized distribution functions for bosons and fermions have been used since a while. However, aiming at a non-extensive quantum statistics further requirements arise from the symmetric handling of particles and holes (excitations above and below the Fermi level). Naive replacements of the exponential function or cut and paste solutions fail to satisfy this symmetry and to be smooth at the Fermi level at the same time. We solve this problem by a general ansatz dividing the deformed exponential to odd and even terms and demonstrate that how earlier suggestions, like the kappa- and q-exponential behave in this respect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that generalized quantum statistical distributions based on Tsallis entropy must satisfy the relation \(e_k(x) \cdot e_k(-x) = 1\) to preserve particle-hole symmetry and smoothness at the Fermi level, a condition that the original Tsallis \(q\)-exponential fails to meet but the Kaniadakis \(\kappa\)-exponential satisfies.

## Method

The authors derive a general ansatz for deformed exponential functions by splitting them into odd and even terms, requiring that the product \(e_k(x) \cdot e_k(-x) = 1\) holds. They show that this leads to a general form \(e_k(x) = (\sqrt{1 + (b k x)^2} + b k x)^{1/k}\), where \(b(x^2)\) is an even function, and demonstrate how earlier suggestions like the \(q\)-exponential and \(\kappa\)-exponential behave under this condition. They also analyze "cut and paste" solutions that use dual deformed exponentials for positive and negative arguments, showing these introduce discontinuities in higher derivatives at the Fermi level.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis \(q\)-exponential is defined as \(e_q(x) = [1 + (1-q)x]^{1/(1-q)}\), with \(q = 1 + k\) and \(q^* = 1 - k\) for the dual form, satisfying \(q + q^* = 2\).
- The Kaniadakis \(\kappa\)-exponential is given by \(e_{\kappa}(x) = (\sqrt{1 + (\kappa x)^2} + \kappa x)^{1/\kappa}\), which satisfies \(e_{\kappa}(x) \cdot e_{\kappa}(-x) = 1\).
- The general ph-symmetric Bose/Fermi distribution is \(n_{B,F}(x) = 1 / [(\sqrt{1 + b^2(k x)^2} + b k x)^{1/k} \mp 1]\), with asymptotic limit \(\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} n_{B,F}(x) = (2 b k x)^{-1/k}\) for positive \(b k\).
- The symmetrized \(q\)-exponential ansatz \(\tilde{e}_q(x) = e_q(x/2) \cdot e_{q^*}(x/2) = \left( \frac{1 + (1-q)x/2}{1 - (1-q)x/2} \right)^{1/(1-q)}\) yields a Bose distribution \(\tilde{n}(x) = [1 - (1-q)x/2]^{1/(1-q)} / \left( [1 + (1-q)x/2]^{1/(1-q)} - [1 - (1-q)x/2]^{1/(1-q)} \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive statistical mechanics framework based on Tsallis entropy (Tsallis, 1988) to quantum statistics, addressing a fundamental flaw in earlier generalized Bose and Fermi distributions proposed by Teweldeberhan (2003) and Silva (2010). The paper resolves the particle-hole symmetry problem by deriving a general ph-symmetrization procedure, showing that the Kaniadakis \(\kappa\)-exponential (Kaniadakis, 2001) naturally satisfies the required KMS relation, while the Tsallis \(q\)-exponential does not. This enables the construction of smooth, symmetric generalized quantum distributions for applications in finite-size systems and high-energy physics, with the "cut and paste" approach of Teweldeberhan (2005) shown to introduce derivative discontinuities at the Fermi level.
