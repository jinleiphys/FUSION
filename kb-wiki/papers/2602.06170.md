# How Neutron Star Observations Point Towards Exotic Matter: Existing Explanations and a Prospective Proposal

> Multi-messenger astronomical observations of neutron stars, together with more precise calculations and constraints coming from dense matter microphysics, are generating tension with regard to equations of state models used to describe neutron star cores. Assuming an abrupt first-order phase transition with a slow conversion speed between phases, we propose different slow stable hybrid star configurations aiming to reconcile all current constraints simultaneously; within this framework, we also introduce a novel non-CSS parametrization to the quark matter equation of state and discuss its strengths and limitations. We analyze our model results in conjunction with a review of other relevant theoretical possibilities existing in the literature. We found that modern neutron star observations seem to favor the existence of some type of exotic matter in the neutron star cores; in particular, our slow stable hybrid star scenario remains a proposal capable of satisfying these constraints. However, due both to the existing skepticism regarding some of the adopted hypotheses in most extreme neutron star measurements and to the precise adjustment needed for the equation-of-state parameters, significant tension and open questions remain.

## Key claim

Modern multi-messenger neutron star observations appear to favor the existence of some type of exotic matter in neutron star cores, but the authors' proposed "slow stable hybrid star" scenario can satisfy current constraints only if the equation-of-state parameters are precisely adjusted and if one accepts certain skeptical assumptions about the most extreme measurements.

## Method

The authors assume an abrupt first-order phase transition with a slow conversion speed between hadronic and quark matter phases, and construct different slow stable hybrid star configurations. They introduce a novel non-CSS (non-Constant Speed of Sound) parametrization for the quark matter equation of state, analyze its strengths and limitations, and compare their model results against a review of other theoretical possibilities in the literature.

## Key numbers

- The paper reviews constraints from multi-messenger observations but provides no specific numerical results from the authors' own model calculations in the available text.
- Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to reconcile neutron star observations with dense matter microphysics, building on prior studies of hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions. The authors review existing explanations alongside their own slow stable hybrid star proposal, noting that significant tension and open questions remain due to skepticism about the most extreme neutron star measurements and the need for precise equation-of-state parameter tuning. The work enables future observational tests that could distinguish between hadronic, hybrid, and exotic matter scenarios in neutron star cores.
