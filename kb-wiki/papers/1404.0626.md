# Next-to-leading order thermal photon production in a weakly-coupled plasma

> We summarize the recent determination to next-to-leading order of the thermal photon rate at weak coupling. We emphasize how it can be expressed in terms of gauge-invariant condensates on the light cone, which are amenable to novel sum rules and Euclidean techniques. For the phenomenologically interesting value of alpha_s=0.3, the NLO correction represents a 20% increase and has a functional form similar to the LO result.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) thermal photon production rate at weak coupling, for the phenomenologically interesting value of \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), represents a 20% increase over the leading-order (LO) result and has a similar functional form, but this increase arises from a cancellation between a much larger positive correction from the collinear sector and a negative one from the semi-collinear and soft regions, which appears largely accidental.

## Method

The calculation evaluates the photon production rate from the backward Wightman correlator of the electromagnetic current at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling \(g\) (relative \(\mathcal{O}(g)\)) for a weakly-coupled, infinitely extended, static, equilibrated quark-gluon plasma. The rate is expressed in terms of gauge-invariant condensates on the light cone, and the NLO corrections are obtained by computing \(\mathcal{O}(g)\) corrections to the asymptotic mass \(m_\infty\) and the scattering kernel \(\mathcal{C}(x_\perp)\) that enter the Schrödinger-like equation resumming multiple soft scatterings (the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect), as well as by evaluating loop corrections and mistreated regions in the soft and semi-collinear sectors using Euclidean techniques and contour deformations based on analyticity.

## Key numbers

* For \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(N_c = N_f = 3\), the NLO correction represents a 20% increase over the LO rate.
* The LO asymptotic mass is \(m_\infty^2 = g^2 C_F T^2 / 4\), where \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\).
* The LO scattering kernel is \(\mathcal{C}(x_\perp) = \frac{g^2 C_F T}{2\pi} \big[ K_0(x_\perp m_D) + \gamma_E + \ln(x_\perp m_D / 2) \big]\), where \(m_D\) is the Debye mass.
* The photon production rate at LO and NLO is parametrized by the function \(C(k/T) \equiv d\Gamma/(d^3k) \big( 4 \alpha_{\rm EM} n_F(k) g^2 T^2 / (3k) \big)^{-1}\), where \(n_F\) is the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order perturbative calculation of thermal photon production by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) to next-to-leading order in the strong coupling, providing the first estimate of the theory uncertainty for this dynamical, time-dependent quantity. The results are enabled by Euclidean techniques and sum rules developed by Caron-Huot (2008) for computing light-cone operators, and the approach serves as a pattern for future calculations of related quantities such as jet energy loss and transport coefficients.
