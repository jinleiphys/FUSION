# Analysis of $Ω(2012)$ as a molecule in the chiral quark model

> Inspired by the updated information on $Ω(2012)$ by the Belle Collaboration, we conduct a study of all possible $S$-wave pentaquark systems with quark contents $sssq\bar{q},q=u,d$ in a chiral quark model with the help of Gaussian expansion method. Channel coupling is also considered. The real-scaling method (stabilization method) is employed to identify and check the bound states and the genuine resonances. In addition, the decay widths of all resonances are given. The results show that $Ω(2012)$ can be interpreted as a $Ξ^*K$ molecular state with quantum number of $IJ^P=0(\frac{3}{2})^-$. Other resonances are obtained: $Ξ^* K^*$ with $IJ^P=0(\frac{1}{2})^-$ and$0(\frac{3}{2})^-$, $Ωπ$ with $IJ^P=1(\frac{3}{2})^-$. These pentaquark states is expected to be further verified in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper interprets the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance as a $\Xi^*K$ molecular state with quantum numbers $IJ^P=0(\frac{3}{2})^-$, based on a chiral quark model calculation that includes channel coupling and the real-scaling method; the claim is conditional on the model's ability to resolve a previous mass inversion problem between the $\Xi^*\bar{K}$ and $\Omega\eta$ channels by using the Gaussian expansion method instead of a single Gaussian approximation.

## Method

The authors studied all possible $S$-wave pentaquark systems with quark content $sssq\bar{q}$ ($q=u,d$) using a constituent chiral quark model that includes color confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone boson exchange potentials. They constructed five-body wave functions using the Gaussian expansion method with geometric progression basis sizes, considered channel coupling between color-singlet and color-octet configurations, and employed the real-scaling (stabilization) method to identify genuine bound states and resonances and to compute decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The measured mass of $\Omega(2012)$ from Belle Collaboration: $2012.4 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.6$ MeV, with width $6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} \pm 1.6$ MeV.
- For the $IJ^P=0(\frac{3}{2})^-$ system, the full channel coupling yields a bound state at 2025 MeV (theoretical threshold 2035 MeV), giving a binding energy of 10 MeV relative to the $\Xi^*\bar{K}$ threshold.
- The corrected energy (using experimental thresholds) for this bound state is 2028 MeV, matching the $\Xi^*\bar{K}$ experimental threshold.
- For the $IJ^P=1(\frac{3}{2})^-$ system, a bound state is found at 1809 MeV (full channel coupling), 2 MeV below the $\Omega\pi$ threshold (experimental threshold 1812 MeV).
- A resonance in the $IJ^P=0(\frac{1}{2})^-$ system is identified at 2426 MeV (main component $\Xi^*\bar{K}^*$).
- A resonance in the $IJ^P=0(\frac{3}{2})^-$ system is identified at 2436 MeV (main component $\Xi^*\bar{K}^*$).
- The binding energy of the $\Xi\bar{K}^*$ channel in the $IJ^P=0(\frac{1}{2})^-$ system is 54 MeV (theoretical), compared to 8 MeV in the prior work by the same group (Hu and Ping, 2021).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study using the chiral quark model and quark delocalization color screening model (Hu and Ping, 2021), which suffered from a mass inversion problem between the $\Xi^*\bar{K}$ and $\Omega\eta$ channels due to the single Gaussian approximation. By adopting the Gaussian expansion method, the present calculation resolves that issue and provides a more objective description of channel-coupling effects. The results support the molecular interpretation of $\Omega(2012)$ as a $\Xi^*K$ state, consistent with the updated Belle Collaboration measurement (2022) that found a three-body to two-body decay branching fraction ratio of $0.97\pm0.24\pm0.07$, and contradict the earlier Belle result (2021) that had disfavored the molecular picture. The predicted additional resonances ($\Xi^*K^*$, $\Omega\pi$) await future experimental verification.
