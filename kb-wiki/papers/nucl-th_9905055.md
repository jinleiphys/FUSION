# Canonical and Microcanonical Distributions for Fermi Systems

> Recursion relations are presented that allow exact calculation of canonical and microcanonical partition functions of degenerate Fermi systems, assuming no explicit two-body interactions. Calculations of the level density, sorted by angular momentum, are presented for Ni-56 are presented. The issue of treating unbound states is also addressed.

## Key claim

Recursion relations enable exact calculation of canonical and microcanonical partition functions for degenerate Fermi systems, but only under the assumption of no explicit two-body (residual) interactions. The method is exact for independent fermions and can treat unbound states through a subtraction procedure, though the correction is only exact in the limit of an infinite confining well radius.

## Method

Recursive algorithms were derived that sum over all possible arrangements of fermions among independent single-particle levels, using diagrammatic representations of permutation cycles. The canonical partition function is built from cycle sums \(C_n = \sum_i e^{-n\beta\epsilon_i}(-1)^{n-1}\), and the recursion \(Z_A(\beta) = \frac{1}{A}\sum_n C_n Z_{A-n}(\beta)\) allows instantaneous calculation given single-particle energies. For microcanonical calculations, energy is discretized into arbitrarily small bins, and the recursion \(N_{A,E,M} = \frac{1}{A}\sum_{n,i}(-1)^{n-1}N_{A-n,E-n\epsilon_i,M-nm_i}\) counts states at fixed energy and angular momentum projection. The method was extended to count multiplets of total angular momentum \(J\) via \(\tilde{N}_J = N_J - N_{J+1}\), and continuum contributions were subtracted by removing contributions from eigenstates of an infinite confining spherical well.

## Key numbers

- Level density for \(^{56}\)Ni was calculated using single-particle levels from Hartree-Fock, with the Fermi energy set at -15.5 MeV for neutrons and -6.0 MeV for protons; energy was discretized in units of 0.25 MeV.
- All levels within 50 MeV of the Fermi surface were included; the lowest 20 single-particle energy levels are tabulated, with proton levels ranging from -32.59 MeV (\(s_{1/2}\)) to 21.42 MeV (\(p_{3/2}\)) and neutron levels from -42.61 MeV (\(s_{1/2}\)) to 10.00 MeV (\(f_{5/2}\)).
- The \(^{56}\)Ni calculations required approximately 5 minutes of CPU time on a low-end workstation, with most time spent on the particle-hole coupling described in Eq. (phole_eq).
- The continuum correction only becomes substantial above excitations of 25 MeV for \(^{56}\)Ni.
- For uniformly spaced non-degenerate levels, the density of states is well matched by the Bethe formula \(\rho(E) \approx \exp(2\pi\sqrt{gE^*/6})/(\sqrt{48}E^*)\), where \(g\) is the single-particle level density and \(E^*\) is excitation energy above ground state.
- The two-point function at fixed excitation energy of 20 \(g^{-1}\) is described by a Fermi function with temperature \(T = \sqrt{6E^*/(\pi^2 g)}\).

## Context

This work extends recursive techniques previously applied to multi-particle symmetrization in heavy-ion collisions for both Bose and Fermi systems (Pratt, 1993; Pratt and Bauer, 1994) and to weakly interacting Bose gases (Mekjian, 1998). The method provides an alternative to Monte Carlo shell-model calculations such as those by Nakada and Alhassid (1997), who considered only the \(fp\) and \(g_{9/2}\) shells, and to studies by Ormand (1997), Langanke (1998), and Dean (1999). The recursive approach enables exact enumeration of states and their angular momentum for arbitrarily large numbers of single-particle levels, which is impractical for combinatorial shell-model codes when many levels are involved. The work also addresses the treatment of unbound continuum states following Shlomo (1997), and suggests that residual two-body interactions could be included perturbatively using the four-point functions derived in the paper.
