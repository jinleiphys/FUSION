# Cluster radioactivity in trans-lead region: A systematic study with modified empirical formulas

> The possibility of cluster emission from trans-lead (86$\leq$Z$\leq$96) region of periodic chart has been explored comprehensively by employing few empirical formulas which are modified by adding angular momentum ($l$) or isospin-dependent ($I=(N-Z)/A$) or both terms for the calculation of cluster decay half-lives. These modified versions of the formulas are found with lesser $χ^2$ per degree of freedom and root mean-square error, in addition to the smaller values of some other statistical parameters, while compared to their corresponding old versions on available 61 experimental data of cluster radioactivity. By applying the modified version of the formula given by Balasubramaniam \textit{et al.} [PRC 70 (2004) 017301], the most accurate formula among these, half-lives of several clusters i.e. isotopes of Be, B, C, N, O, F, Ne, Na, Mg, and Si are predicted systematically for the several isotopes in the trans-lead region. The contest of cluster emission with $α$-decay has been investigated in form of branching ratio which brings several potential cluster emissions into the probable decay modes of these nuclei. The accurate prediction of half-lives of such clusters is expected to be crucial for the future experimental observations where $α$-decay is observed dominantly.

## Key claim

The authors show that adding angular momentum (l) and isospin (I) terms to six existing empirical formulas for cluster decay half-lives significantly improves their accuracy, with the modified BKAG formula (MBKAG) emerging as the most precise; however, the improvement from the isospin term is only effective for the BKAG and RenA formulas, not for the others.

## Method

The authors took six existing empirical formulas (BKAG, RenA, Horoi, NRDX, UDL, UNIV) and modified each by adding an angular momentum term l(l+1) and, where beneficial, an isospin term I(I+1) with I=(N-Z)/A. They fitted these modified formulas to 61 experimental cluster decay data points from trans-lead nuclei (Z=86-96), using spin and parity values from NUBASE2020 to determine the minimum angular momentum l. The best-performing MBKAG formula was then used to predict half-lives for clusters from Be to Si across the trans-lead region, and branching ratios relative to alpha-decay were calculated using the NMHF formula for alpha half-lives.

## Key numbers

* The MBKAG formula achieved the lowest RMSE of 0.63 and chi-squared of 0.44 among all modified formulas, compared to the original BKAG RMSE of 0.98 and chi-squared of 1.01 (fitted on 61 experimental cluster decay data).
* The MBKAG formula gave a standard deviation sigma of 0.64, uncertainty u of 0.08, average deviation factor x-bar of 0.02, and mean deviation delta-bar of 0.51, outperforming the New UDL (sigma=0.81), IUF (sigma=0.84), and ISEF (sigma=0.93) formulas from recent literature.
* For the 61 data points, the original formulas had RMSE values ranging from 0.90 (NRDX) to 1.34 (UDL); after modification, RMSE values ranged from 0.63 (MBKAG) to 0.83 (MHoroi).
* Predicted half-lives for specific decays include: 221Fr emitting 14C with log10 T1/2 = 15.44 sec (experimental 14.52 sec), and 238Pu emitting 32Si with log10 T1/2 = 25.27 sec (experimental 25.30 sec).

## Context

This work extends the approach of including angular momentum and isospin effects, previously shown to be important for alpha-decay half-lives by Saxena et al. and Sharma et al. (2021), to the domain of cluster radioactivity. It builds on recent modifications by Soylu and Qi (2021) for the UDL formula and by Ismail et al. (2022) for the NRDX formula, but applies the modification systematically across six different empirical formulas. The predictions of half-lives and branching ratios for clusters from Be to Si in the trans-lead region (Z=86-96) provide guidance for future experiments where alpha-decay currently dominates, potentially enabling the observation of new cluster decay modes.
