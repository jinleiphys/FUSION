# Pion-Nucleus Scattering at Medium Energies with Densities from Chiral Effective Field Theories

> Recently developed chiral effective field theory models provide excellent descriptions of the bulk characteristics of finite nuclei, but have not been tested with other observables. In this work, densities from both relativistic point-coupling models and mean-field meson models are used in the analysis of meson-nucleus scattering at medium energies. Elastic scattering observables for 790 MeV/$c$ $π^{\pm}$ on $^{208}$Pb are calculated in a relativistic impulse approximation, using the Kemmer-Duffin-Petiau formalism to calculate the $π^{\pm}$ nucleus optical potential.

# Pion-Nucleus Scattering at Medium Energies with Densities from Chiral Effective Field Theories

## Key claim
Densities from chiral effective field theory models, when used as inputs to relativistic impulse approximation calculations of elastic pion-nucleus scattering at 790 MeV/c, give good agreement with experimental data for 208Pb, but the analysis shows sensitivity only to differences in neutron rms radii and does not otherwise constrain the many distinct chiral EFT parameter sets.

## Method
The authors applied the Kemmer-Duffin-Petiau (KDP) formalism to construct a relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) optical potential for pion-nucleus scattering. Scalar and vector optical potentials were obtained by folding invariant pion-nucleon amplitudes (from the summer 1997 SAID amplitudes) with nuclear densities from two types of chiral EFT models: relativistic point-coupling models and mean-field meson models. Elastic scattering differential cross sections for 790 MeV/c π± on 208Pb were then calculated by solving the KDP equation.

## Key numbers
- Beam momentum: 790 MeV/c for π± on 208Pb
- Neutron rms radii differ by 0.18 fm between the two representative models (VA3: 5.770 fm; FZ4: 5.588 fm)
- Proton rms radii differ by only 0.01 fm between the two models (VA3: 5.438 fm; FZ4: 5.428 fm)
- Effective nucleon mass M*/M in the new EFT models reaches as high as 0.74, in contrast to conventional models where it was always close to 0.6

## Context
This work provides the first independent test of densities from chiral effective field theory models (Furnstahl, Serot, Tang 1997; Rusnak, Furnstahl 1997) by using them as inputs to pion-nucleus scattering calculations, extending the KDP-RIA formalism previously applied in Clark et al. (1985) and Kurth et al. (1994). The results validate the impulse approximation at these energies and show that the analysis can distinguish models based on neutron rms radii differences, but does not otherwise constrain the many parameter sets. The authors note that analogous studies of proton-nucleus scattering will be discussed in a forthcoming publication.
