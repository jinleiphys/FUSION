# Initial state anisotropies and their uncertainties in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions from the Monte Carlo Glauber model

> In hydrodynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions, the initial-state spatial anisotropies are translated into momentum anisotropies of the final-state particle distributions. Thus, understanding the origin of the initial-state anisotropies and their uncertainties is important before extracting specific QCD matter properties, such as viscosity, from the experimental data. In this work we review the wounded nucleon approach based on the Monte Carlo Glauber model, charting in particular the uncertainties arising from modeling of the nucleon-nucleon interactions between the colliding nucleon pairs and nucleon-nucleon correlations inside the colliding nuclei. We discuss the differences between the black disk model and a probabilistic profile function approach for the inelastic nucleon-nucleon interactions, and investigate the influence of initial-state correlations using state-of-the-art modeling of these.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies that uncertainties in Monte Carlo Glauber model initial-state anisotropies from both the nucleon-nucleon interaction modeling and the nucleon-nucleon correlations are each at most about 10%, but the effects of central-only correlations are largely canceled when more realistic full state-dependent correlations are used.

## Method

The authors generated Au-Au collision events at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using a Monte Carlo Glauber model, comparing two inelastic nucleon-nucleon interaction models: a black disk (hard-sphere) approximation and a probabilistic profile function approach based on the elastic scattering amplitude. They also compared three types of nucleon configurations inside the nuclei: uncorrelated (random Woods-Saxon sampling), configurations with central correlations only, and configurations with full state-dependent correlations (including spin, isospin, and tensor operators) generated via a Metropolis method. The first three spatial anisotropy harmonics (epsilon_1, epsilon_2, epsilon_3) and their relative fluctuations were computed from the wounded nucleon positions as functions of the number of participants.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the black disk and profile function interaction models causes uncertainties up to 10% in epsilon_1, epsilon_2, and epsilon_3 in peripheral collisions.
- At impact parameters b=10-15 fm, the difference in the number of wounded nucleons and binary collisions between the two interaction models is over 5%.
- For the profile function approach at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the input values used were: B=14 GeV^{-2}, sigma^{tot}_{NN}=52 mb, sigma^{in}_{NN}=42 mb, and sigma^{el}_{NN}=9.9 mb.
- The asymmetries from the profile function approach are slightly (less than or about 10%) smaller than those from the black disk case.
- Central correlations produce a smaller anisotropy than the uncorrelated case, but full correlations bring the results back close to the uncorrelated case.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo Glauber modeling used in event-by-event hydrodynamics (e.g., Holopainen 2010, Schenke 2010) by systematically charting uncertainties from two sources: the nucleon-nucleon interaction model and nucleon-nucleon correlations. It confirms the finding of Broniowski (2010) that central correlations affect eccentricity, but shows as a new result that full state-dependent correlations largely cancel this effect. The study enables more precise extraction of QCD matter properties like shear viscosity from flow measurements by quantifying that these initial-state uncertainties are at most about 10%.
