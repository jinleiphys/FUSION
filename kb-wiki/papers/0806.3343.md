# PACIAE Model Predictions for Pb+Pb Collisions at LHC Compared to the Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> The parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE is used to simulate the 0-6, 15-25, and 35-45 % most central Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV and the 0-10 % most central Pb+Pb collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=5500 GeV. The calculated charged multiplicity and the charged particle transverse momentum distribution, pseudorapidity distribution, and extended longitudinal scaling for Au+Au collisions well agree with the corresponding PHOBOS data. Thus the above observables calculated for Pb+Pb collisions would be a reliable prediction.

## Key claim
The PACIAE model reproduces PHOBOS data for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (19.6–200 GeV) and is therefore used to predict observables for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy (5500 GeV), but the model's agreement with data is notably worse at the lowest energy (19.6 GeV), consistent with PYTHIA being more suitable for higher energies.

## Method
The PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model, based on PYTHIA for hadron-hadron collisions, was used to simulate nucleus-nucleus collisions in four stages: parton initialization, parton evolution via 2→2 LO-pQCD scattering with a color screening mass regulator, hadronization via the Lund string fragmentation model, and hadron rescattering. All model parameters were fixed except the Lund string fragmentation parameter b, which was varied as a function of center-of-mass energy (b = 0.58 at 19.6 GeV, b = 6 at 200 GeV, b = 12 at 5500 GeV) to account for the temperature dependence of the fireball.

## Key numbers
- Charged multiplicity in 0-6% most central Au+Au collisions: PACIAE gives 1533 (experiment: 1689±100) at 19.6 GeV, 2919 (2845±142) at 62.4 GeV, 4140 (4170±210) at 130 GeV, and 5001 (5060±250) at 200 GeV.
- Predicted charged multiplicity in 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at 5500 GeV: 14695.
- Predicted pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for Pb+Pb at 5500 GeV: dN_ch/dη|_(η=0) ≈ 1196 (estimated from |η|<0.12).
- Lund string fragmentation parameter b values: 0.58 at 19.6 GeV, 2 at 62.4 GeV, 4 at 130 GeV, 6 at 200 GeV, and 12 at 5500 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the PACIAE model validation from RHIC energies (where it agrees with PHOBOS data from Back et al. (2003) and Back et al. (2005)) to make predictions for LHC Pb+Pb collisions, complementing earlier thermal model predictions (e.g., Cleymans et al. (2006) and Braun-Munzinger et al. (2004)) and the compilation of sixteen model predictions for LHC compiled by Armesto et al. (2008). The predicted pseudorapidity density of ~1200 falls within the range of those sixteen models. The work enables comparison of transport model predictions with future LHC data, and demonstrates extended longitudinal scaling holds across both RHIC and LHC energies.
