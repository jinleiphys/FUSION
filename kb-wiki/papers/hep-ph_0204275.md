# Anisotropic admixture in color-superconducting quark matter

> The analysis of color-superconducting two-flavor deconfined quark matter at moderate densities is extended to include a particular spin-1 Cooper pairing of those quarks which do not participate in the standard spin-0 diquark condensate. (i) The relativistic spin-1 gap Delta' implies spontaneous breakdown of rotation invariance manifested in the form of the quasi-fermion dispersion law. (ii) The critical temperature of the anisotropic component is approximately given by the relation T_c'~ Delta'(T=0)/3. (iii) For massless fermions the gas of anisotropic Bogolyubov-Valatin quasiquarks becomes effectively gapless and two-dimensional. Consequently, its specific heat depends quadratically on temperature. (iv) All collective Nambu-Goldstone excitations of the anisotropic phase have a linear dispersion law and the whole system remains a superfluid. (v) The system exhibits an electromagnetic Meissner effect.

## Key claim

The paper extends the standard analysis of two-flavor color-superconducting quark matter by including a spin-1 Cooper pair condensate for the otherwise unpaired color-3 quarks, finding that even though the spin-1 gap Δ' is much smaller than the spin-0 gap Δ for instanton-motivated couplings, it can become comparable to Δ if the coupling constant H_t is doubled, and its presence qualitatively changes the low-energy properties of the system.

## Method

The authors employ a mean-field thermodynamic potential Ω(T,μ) derived from an effective four-fermion Lagrangian containing a quark-antiquark term and separate scalar and tensor diquark interaction terms, with coupling constants initially set by the instanton relation G:H_s:H_t = 1:3/4:3/16. They solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate M, the spin-0 gap Δ, and the spin-1 gap Δ' simultaneously at zero and finite temperature, using a sharp 3-momentum cutoff Λ = 600 MeV and bare quark mass m = 5 MeV, and compute the specific heat from the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- For parameter set 1 (instanton couplings) at T=0, Δ' is small compared with Δ across the chemical potential range corresponding to baryon densities of about 4–7 times nuclear matter density.
- For parameter set 2 (H_t doubled) at μ = 450 MeV, Δ'(T=0) = 30.8 MeV, and the critical temperature T_c' ≈ 0.40 Δ'(T=0).
- The critical temperature of the anisotropic component is approximately given by T_c' ≈ Δ'(T=0)/3.
- The minimal gap in the quasiparticle spectrum for color-3 quarks is Δ'_0 = M|Δ'|/√(μ² + |Δ'|²); for parameter set 2 at μ = 450 MeV, Δ'_0 = 0.074 T_c'.
- The specific heat at low temperature (T ≪ T_c') follows c_v ∝ T² for T ≳ Δ'_0, with an exponential suppression at lower temperatures; for M=0 the T² behavior extends to arbitrarily low T.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of two-flavor color superconductivity (Bailin and Love, 1984; Rajagopal and Wilczek, 2000) by addressing the instability of the Fermi sea of color-3 quarks that remain unpaired in the usual spin-0 isospin-singlet condensate. It builds on earlier estimates by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) that the spin-1 gap would be much smaller than the spin-0 gap, and provides a quantitative analysis of its consequences for the specific heat and superfluid properties. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling, since the smallest gap among all quark species governs transport properties such as specific heat, neutrino emissivity, and viscosity, and the anisotropic phase also implies an electromagnetic Meissner effect that could influence neutron star magnetic fields.
