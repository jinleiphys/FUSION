# Occupation numbers in Self Consistent RPA

> A method is proposed which allows to calculate within the SCRPA theory the occupation numbers via the single particle Green function. This scheme complies with the Hugenholtz van Hove theorem. In an application to the Lipkin model it is found that this prescription gives consistently better results than two other commonly used approximations: lowest order boson expansion and the number operator method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to calculate occupation numbers within Self-Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA) via the single-particle Green's function, claiming this prescription gives consistently better results than two other commonly used approximations (lowest order boson expansion and the number operator method) when applied to the Lipkin model, while also complying with the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem.

## Method

The authors derive SCRPA equations from a variational principle for the mean excitation energy, then couple the single-particle Green's function to SCRPA by constructing a mass operator from the SCRPA T-matrix such that the ground-state energy calculated from the single-particle Green's function agrees with that from the two-body Green's function. They apply this formalism to the Lipkin model with N = Omega particles, solving the resulting nonlinear eigenvalue problem iteratively to obtain the SCRPA amplitudes X and Y, the excitation energy E, and the occupation number expectation value ⟨J0⟩.

## Key numbers

* For Omega = 14, the SCRPA correlation energy E_corr as a function of chi = V(Omega-1)/epsilon stays consistently above the exact results, while RPA and RRPA deviate strongly near the mean-field phase transition point chi = 1.
* The SCRPA excitation energy E as a function of chi for Omega = 14 also stays consistently above the exact results, with differences becoming more pronounced for chi greater than approximately 1.
* The ratio r = -sqrt(⟨J0^2⟩)/⟨J0⟩ at chi = 1 (the mean-field transition point) shows significant deviation from unity only for Omega values lower than 4, with the exact results represented by full squares and SCRPA by a full line in Figure 5.
* The Green's function expression for ⟨J0⟩ (Equation H22) reduces to the lowest order boson expansion result ⟨J0⟩ = -Omega/2 + Y^2 in the RPA limit, and to the number operator method result ⟨J0⟩ = -(Omega/2)/(1 + (2/Omega)Y^2) under additional approximations.

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA formalism previously developed by the same authors (Dukelsky et al., 1990, 1996, 1998) and independently by the Cluster Hartree-Fock group (Ropke, 1995), which itself traces back to Hara (1964) and Rowe's equation-of-motion method (1968). The paper addresses the long-standing problem of consistently evaluating single-particle quantities within SCRPA, contrasting the proposed Green's function method with the number operator method advocated by Catara et al. (1994, 1996) and the lowest order boson expansion. The approach enables future applications to more realistic many-body systems where the vacuum condition cannot be solved explicitly, and provides a framework for calculating occupation numbers that satisfies the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem.
