# Ab initio computation of the 17F proton-halo state and resonances in A = 17 nuclei

> We perform coupled-cluster calculations of the energies and lifetimes of single-particle states around the doubly magic nucleus 16O based on chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next- to-next-to-leading order. To incorporate effects from the scattering continuum, we solve the coupled- cluster equations with a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis. Our calculations for the 1/2+ proton-halo state in 17F and the 1/2+ state in 17O agree well with experiment, while the calculated spin-orbit splitting between d5/2 and d3/2 states is too small due to the lack of three-nucleon forces. We find that continuum effects yield a significant amount of additional binding energy for the 1/2+ and 3/2+ states in 17O and 17F.

## Key claim
Coupled-cluster calculations using a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis (Berggren representation) reproduce the 105 keV binding energy of the 1/2+ proton-halo state in 17F and the 1/2+ state in 17O, but the calculated d5/2-d3/2 spin-orbit splitting is too small because three-nucleon forces are omitted.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations with singles and doubles (CCSD) and the ΛCCSD(T) approximation for triples, using the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO. They solved the coupled-cluster equations in a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis (Berggren representation) that includes bound, resonant, and continuum scattering states for the s1/2, d3/2, and d5/2 partial waves, with 30 Gauss-Legendre mesh points along the contour for each partial wave. Single-particle energies and resonance widths for A=17 nuclei were obtained via the particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster method (PA-EOM-CCSD) acting on the mass-shifted 16O ground state.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of 16O: -107.6 MeV (CCSD) and -120.9 MeV (ΛCCSD(T)) at the energy minimum ℏω=34 MeV
- Calculated single-particle energy of the 1/2+ state in 17F: -0.082 MeV (GHF basis); experimental value: -0.105 MeV
- Calculated single-particle energy of the 1/2+ state in 17O: -2.8 MeV (GHF basis); experimental value: -3.272 MeV
- Calculated spin-orbit splitting (d3/2-d5/2) in 17F: 3.7 MeV; experimental value: 5.000 MeV
- Calculated spin-orbit splitting (d3/2-d5/2) in 17O: 4.3 MeV; experimental value: 5.084 MeV
- Calculated 3/2+ resonance energy in 17O: 1.1 MeV with width Γ=0.014 MeV; experimental: 0.942 MeV with Γ=0.096 MeV
- Calculated 3/2+ resonance energy in 17F: 3.9 MeV with width Γ=1.0 MeV; experimental: 4.399 MeV with Γ=1.530 MeV
- Continuum effects yield approximately 1.0 MeV additional binding for the 1/2+ and 3/2+ states compared to oscillator-basis calculations
- Norm of one-particle amplitude for the 1/2+ state in 17F: |r^a r^a| = 0.92

## Context
This work extends coupled-cluster methods with continuum (Berggren) basis, previously applied to helium isotopes by Hagen et al. (2007), to the A=17 mirror nuclei 16O, 17O, and 17F. It provides the first ab-initio description of the 17F proton-halo state and resonances in A=17 nuclei, complementing earlier continuum shell-model studies by Bennaceur et al. (2000) and Quaglioni et al. (2008) for 11Be. The results enable future ab-initio shell-model calculations with a core for sd-shell nuclei as pursued by Lisetskiy et al. (2008), and demonstrate that short-range three-nucleon forces are needed to reproduce the d5/2-d3/2 spin-orbit splitting while the halo state is insensitive to them.
