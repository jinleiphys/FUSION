# New T=1 effective interactions for the f5/2 p3/2 p1/2 g9/2 model space; Implications for valence-mirror symmetry and seniority isomers

> New shell model Hamiltonians are derived for the T=1 part of the residual interaction in the f5/2 p3/2 p1/2 g9/2 model space based on the analysis and fit of the available experimental data for 57Ni-78Ni isotopes and 77Cu-100Sn isotones. The fit procedure, properties of the determined effective interaction as well as new results for valence-mirror symmetry and seniority isomers for nuclei near 78Ni and 100Sn are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives new shell-model Hamiltonians for the T=1 part of the residual interaction in the f5/2 p3/2 p1/2 g9/2 model space, but the fit is limited to the linear combinations of parameters to which the experimental data are sensitive; not all 65 two-body matrix elements are individually determined.

## Method

The authors performed a least-squares fit of single-particle energies and T=1 two-body matrix elements to experimental binding energies and excitation energies for neutron-rich Ni isotopes (A=57-78) and N=50 isotones (77Cu-100Sn). The starting point was a realistic G-matrix interaction based on the Bonn-C NN potential with core-polarization corrections on a 56Ni core, and the Linear Combination Method (LCD) was used to identify the 20 most important linear combinations of parameters that drive the chi-squared convergence. For the neutron fit, 15 binding energies and 91 energy levels were used; for the proton fit, 19 binding energies and 113 energy levels were used.

## Key numbers

- Average deviation between experiment and theory: 241 keV for neutron binding and excitation energies, 124 keV for proton binding and excitation energies.
- The effective g^2_{9/2} J=0 to J=2 two-body matrix element gap is 0.373 MeV for neutrons and 0.727 MeV for protons (differing by approximately 0.3 MeV).
- The [g_{9/2}]^{A-68}_{0+} component in the ground state wave functions of 70Ni, 72Ni, 74Ni, and 76Ni is 44%, 53%, 67%, and 83%, respectively.
- The [g_{9/2}]^{A-90}_{0+} component in the ground state wave functions of 92Mo, 94Ru, 96Pd, and 98Cd is 51%, 60%, 71%, and 84%, respectively.
- Calculated lifetime of the 8+1 state in 70Ni: 326.0 ns (experimental: 335(4) ns); in 72Ni: 6.1 ns (experimental: <26 ns); in 74Ni: 5.1 ns (experimental: <87 ns); in 76Ni: 1.2 μs.
- Calculated B(E2; 8+1 → 6+1) for 70Ni: 12 e^2fm^4; for 72Ni: 1.9 e^2fm^4; for 74Ni: 9.2 e^2fm^4; for 76Ni: 15 e^2fm^4.
- Calculated B(E2; 8+1 → 6+2) for 70Ni: 3.3 e^2fm^4; for 72Ni: 52 e^2fm^4; for 74Ni: 47 e^2fm^4.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of the Ni region by Brown (1995), Grawe (1998, 2002), and Sawicka (2003), and provides a much improved Hamiltonian for Z=28 over those considered in smaller model spaces. For the N=50 isotones, it provides a new Hamiltonian similar to those obtained previously by Ji and Wildenthal (1988) and Sinatkas (1992). The new interactions enable full proton-neutron configuration mixing calculations for the A=56-100 mass region, which can be applied to studies of valence-mirror symmetry, seniority isomers, and weak-interaction and astrophysical phenomena.
