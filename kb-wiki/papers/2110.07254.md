# Neutron transfer in $^{9}$Be + $^{159}$Tb system

> One neutron stripping cross sections ($σ_{-1n}$) are measured in $^{9}$Be+$^{159}$Tb system in the energy range E$_{cm}$/V$_{B}$ $\sim$ 0.79 - 1.24 using offline gamma counting technique. The CRC model calculations including the ground state and the 2$^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be, carried out using the FRESCO code, give a reasonable description of the measured data. In addition, comparisons of reduced 1n-stripping cross sections- $σ_{red}$ with $^{9}$Be for different target nuclei (A $\sim$150-200), and $σ_{red}$ for $^{9}$Be, $^{6}$Li with $^{159}$Tb target are presented. While no strong target dependence is observed with $^{9}$Be projectile, $σ_{red}$($^{9}$Be) is significantly larger than that for $^{6}$Li, which is consistent with the Q-value for transfer reactions and breakup threshold energy of projectiles.

## Key claim

One-neutron stripping cross sections in the \(^{9}\text{Be}+^{159}\text{Tb}\) system are measured over a wide energy range and are well described by coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations that include both the ground state and the \(2^{+}\) resonance state of \(^{8}\text{Be}\); however, the abstract's claim of "reasonable description" glosses over the fact that the best agreement requires including \(^{160}\text{Tb}\) excited states up to 1.198 MeV, while calculations with states only up to 0.572 MeV under-predict the data and those up to 1.397 MeV over-predict it.

## Method

One-neutron stripping cross sections (\(\sigma_{-1n}\)) were measured using the offline gamma counting technique with a \(^{9}\text{Be}\) beam (30–47 MeV) incident on self-supporting \(^{159}\text{Tb}\) targets (1.3–1.7 mg/cm\(^2\)) backed by aluminum catcher foils. The 879 keV gamma ray from \(^{160}\text{Tb}\) (\(I_\gamma = 30\%\)) was used to extract cross sections after decay corrections, with the half-life measured as \(77 \pm 9\) days. CRC calculations were performed using the FRESCO code, including the ground state and \(2^{+}\) resonance state of \(^{8}\text{Be}\) and excited states of \(^{160}\text{Tb}\) up to 1.198 MeV, with spectroscopic factors taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

- \(\sigma_{-1n}\) for \(^{9}\text{Be}+^{159}\text{Tb}\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 27.1\) MeV: \(13 \pm 1\) mb (center-of-mass energy 25.6 MeV)
- \(\sigma_{-1n}\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 37.0\) MeV: \(138 \pm 16\) mb (center-of-mass energy 35.0 MeV)
- \(\sigma_{-1n}\) at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 42.6\) MeV: \(175 \pm 20\) mb (center-of-mass energy 40.3 MeV)
- Measured half-life of 879 keV gamma ray: \(77 \pm 9\) days, consistent with the literature value of 72.3 days
- Ground state Q-value for 1n-stripping in \(^{9}\text{Be}+^{159}\text{Tb}\): 4.71 MeV
- CRC calculations for \(^{6}\text{Li}+^{159}\text{Tb}\) require a scale factor of 0.65 to describe the data from Pradhan et al. (2015)

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of one-neutron stripping with weakly bound projectiles on heavy targets, complementing earlier measurements for \(^{9}\text{Be}\) on targets such as \(^{197}\text{Au}\) (Kaushik et al., 2020) and \(^{186}\text{W}\) (Yadav et al., 2019). The comparison with \(^{6}\text{Li}+^{159}\text{Tb}\) data from Pradhan et al. (2015) shows that reduced cross sections for \(^{9}\text{Be}\) are significantly larger, consistent with its higher Q-value (4.71 MeV vs. 0.71 MeV) and lower breakup threshold. The finding that reduced cross sections for \(^{9}\text{Be}\) are nearly independent of target nucleus (A \(\sim\) 150–200) enables future systematic studies with other weakly bound unstable projectiles.
