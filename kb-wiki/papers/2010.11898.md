# The subtraction contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift: a point for lattice QCD calculations of the polarizability effect

> The proton-polarizability contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift is a major source of theoretical uncertainty in the extraction of the proton charge radius. An empirical evaluation of this effect, based on the proton structure functions, requires a systematically improvable calculation of the "subtraction function", possibly using lattice QCD. We consider a different subtraction point, with the aim of accessing the subtraction function directly in lattice calculations. A useful feature of this subtraction point is that the corresponding contribution of the structure functions to the Lamb shift is suppressed. The whole effect is dominated by the subtraction contribution, calculable on the lattice.

# The subtraction contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift: a point for lattice QCD calculations of the polarizability effect

## Key claim

Choosing the subtraction point at the imaginary photon energy nu = iQ for the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering amplitude makes the inelastic structure-function contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift negligible (about 1.6 micro-eV), so that the entire polarizability effect is dominated by the subtraction function, which can be calculated directly in lattice QCD without requiring a zero-energy extrapolation.

## Method

The authors reformulate the standard dispersion-relation approach for the two-photon exchange correction to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift by shifting the subtraction point from nu = 0 to nu = iQ. They derive new expressions for the inelastic and subtraction contributions at this subtraction point, and evaluate the inelastic contribution numerically using the Bosted-Christy parametrization of proton structure functions (limited to Q^2 between 0 and 8 GeV^2 and invariant mass W between 1.1 and 3.1 GeV). The total polarizability contribution remains unchanged, but the relative size of the inelastic and subtraction pieces is redistributed.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic contribution to the 2S Lamb shift at the standard subtraction point (nu = 0): approximately -12.3 micro-eV, using the Bosted-Christy parametrization
- Inelastic contribution to the 2S Lamb shift at the proposed subtraction point (nu = iQ): approximately 1.6 micro-eV, using the same parametrization
- The full data-driven evaluation in the dispersive approach, including higher Q^2 and larger W, yields Delta E^(inel.)_2S = -12.7(5) micro-eV (Carlson 2011)
- Current experimental precision for the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift is about 2 micro-eV

## Context

This work extends the data-driven dispersive evaluations of the proton-polarizability contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013, Carlson 2011) by addressing the problem that the subtraction function at nu = 0 is not determined experimentally and must be modeled. The approach is inspired by a recent proposal by Gasser, Leutwyler and Rusetsky (2020) to use subtraction at nu = iQ/2 for the Cottingham formula in the proton-neutron mass splitting. The new subtraction point enables lattice QCD calculations (Can 2020, Hannaford-Gunn 2020, Chambers 2017) to access the subtraction function directly by setting the three-momentum of the external photon to zero, avoiding the need for extrapolation to nu = 0, and suppresses the large Delta-resonance cancellation that plagues the standard approach.
