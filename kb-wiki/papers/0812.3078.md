# Semi-Inclusive Distributions in Statistical Models

> The semi-inclusive properties of the system of neutral and charged particles with net charge equal to zero are considered in the grand canonical, canonical and micro-canonical ensembles as well as in micro-canonical ensemble with scaling volume fluctuations. Distributions of neutral particle multiplicity and charged particle momentum are calculated as a function of the number of charged particles. Different statistical ensembles lead to qualitatively different dependencies. They are being compared with the corresponding experimental data on multi-hadron production in $p+p$ interactions at high energies.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive distributions of neutral particle multiplicity and charged particle momentum as functions of fixed charged multiplicity show qualitatively different dependencies across grand canonical, canonical, micro-canonical, and micro-canonical with scaling volume fluctuations ensembles; only the micro-canonical ensemble with scaling volume fluctuations (MCE/sVF) follows the trends observed in experimental data on p+p interactions, while the other ensembles disagree qualitatively.

## Method

The authors consider a system of non-interacting massless Boltzmann particles (neutral, positively charged, and negatively charged) with net charge zero, using degeneracy factors g0=g+=g-=1 and temperature T=160 MeV. They calculate joint multiplicity distributions P(N0,N-) and semi-inclusive mean neutral multiplicities ⟨N0⟩* as functions of fixed N- within the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), canonical ensemble (CE), micro-canonical ensemble (MCE), and MCE with scaling volume fluctuations (MCE/sVF). They also compute semi-inclusive momentum spectra of negatively charged particles at fixed N- and compare the model predictions to experimental data on multi-hadron production in p+p interactions at high energies.

## Key numbers

- Temperature parameter set to T = 160 MeV for all quantitative calculations
- Scaled variance for negatively charged particles in GCE: ω⁻_gce = 1; in CE: ω⁻_ce = 1/2; in MCE: ω⁻_mce = 1/4
- Scaled variance for neutral particles in GCE and CE: ω⁰_gce = ω⁰_ce = 1; in MCE: ω⁰_mce = 3/4
- Correlation coefficient between neutral and negatively charged particles in MCE (Boltzmann): ρ⁰⁻_mce = -1/√3 ≈ -0.577
- For Bose statistics: ω⁺_gce ≈ 1.368, ω⁺_ce ≈ 0.684, ω⁺_mce ≈ 0.407; for Fermi statistics: ω⁺_gce ≈ 0.912, ω⁺_ce ≈ 0.456, ω⁺_mce ≈ 0.218
- MCE/sVF scaled variance: ω⁻_α ≈ (1/k)⟨N⟩ + ω⁻_mce with k = 4, giving ω⁻_α ≈ ⟨N⟩/4 + 1/4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Begun, Gaździcki, Gorenstein, 2008) where the MCE/sVF was introduced to address power-law behavior of transverse momentum spectra and heavy particle multiplicities, problems that standard statistical approaches fail to reproduce including KNO scaling violations noted by Koba, Nielsen, and Olesen (1972). The paper enables systematic comparison of statistical ensembles against semi-inclusive experimental data from p+p collisions, showing that only the MCE/sVF reproduces the observed positive correlation between neutral and charged particle multiplicities, while GCE, CE, and MCE all fail qualitatively.
