# Reconstruction of the optical potential in the inverse quantum scattering. Application to the relativistic inelastic NN scattering

> The numerical algorithm of the inverse quantum scattering is developed. This algorithm is based on the Marchenko theory, and includes three steps. The first one is the algebraic Pade approximation of the unitary S-matrix, what is realized by solving a system of linear equations. Second step is the exact solution of the Marchenko equation. The used approximant reduces it to another system of linear equations. At this step we get the real-valued potential. It is shown numerically that the developed algorithm is able not only to generate the given S-matrix dependence, but converges to the initial potential. At third step we construct the optical complex-valued potential which gives the needed S-matrix. It is shown that the modern phase shift analysis data allow to construct the nucleon-nucleon optical potentials of two kinds. These potentials describe the deuteron properties and the phase shift analysis data up to 3 GeV and they have different behavior at short distances. One is a repulsive core potential and another is a Moscow attractive potential with forbidden states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that modern phase shift analysis data for nucleon-nucleon scattering up to 3 GeV allow the construction of two distinct kinds of local optical potentials (a repulsive core potential and a Moscow attractive potential with forbidden states) that both describe the data within experimental errors, meaning the available scattering data are not discriminative with respect to the short-range behavior of the NN potential.

## Method

The authors develop a numerical algorithm for inverse quantum scattering based on the Marchenko theory. The algorithm first uses an algebraic Pade approximation of the unitary S-matrix, solving a system of linear equations; second, it solves the Marchenko equation exactly, which reduces to another system of linear equations yielding a real-valued potential; third, it constructs a complex-valued optical potential from the real potential using an iterative procedure based on the phase equation, where the imaginary part is parameterized by a factor alpha(q) derived from the inelasticity parameter rho. The method is applied to the coupled \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) and single \(^1S_0\) partial waves of nucleon-nucleon scattering, with relativistic kinematics treated in the point-form dynamics of relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Key numbers

* The phase shift analysis data used as input cover energies up to 1100 MeV for the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) state and up to 3 GeV for the \(^1S_0\) state.
* The constructed potentials describe the deuteron properties and phase shift analysis data up to 3 GeV.
* The \(^1S_0\) phase shift of the Moscow potential begins from \(\pi\); the \(^3S_1\) phase shift of the Moscow potential begins from \(2\pi\).
* The mixing parameter \(\epsilon_1\) of the Moscow potential differs from that of the repulsive core potential by sign.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Geramb (year not given) by using a more general Pade approximant for the S-matrix that allows poles anywhere off the real axis, unlike the earlier form used by Wiesner (year not given) which restricted poles to the imaginary axis. The resulting potentials provide local configuration-space descriptions of NN scattering up to 3 GeV, which the authors argue are needed for exact nuclear calculations; they note that discriminating between the two potential types requires experimental examination of other inelastic processes such as \(pp\rightarrow pp\gamma\) or \(^2H+\gamma\rightarrow n+p\).
