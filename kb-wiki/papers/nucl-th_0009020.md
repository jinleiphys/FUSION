# Classification of the Nuclear Multifragmentation Phase Transition

> Using a recently proposed classification scheme for phase transitions in finite systems [Phys.Rev.Lett.{\bf 84},3511 (2000)] we show that within the statistical standard model of nuclear multifragmentation the predicted phase transition is of first order.

## Key claim

Using a classification scheme based on complex zeros of the canonical partition function, the authors show that within the canonical statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), nuclear multifragmentation is a first-order phase transition, with the qualification that the classification parameters approach first-order behavior only in the limit of infinite nucleon number.

## Method

The authors applied a classification scheme that analyzes the distribution of zeros of the canonical partition function in the complex temperature plane, extracting three parameters: the exponent α describing the density of zeros, the crossing angle γ of the line of zeros with the real axis, and the imaginary part τ₁ of the zero closest to the real axis. They computed the partition function for the canonical statistical multifragmentation model using a recursion relation for the multiplicity distribution, and located the zeros by mapping the absolute value of the variance of the multiplicity in the complex temperature plane for nucleon numbers A=25, 75, 150, and 225.

## Key numbers

- For all nucleon numbers A > 25, the classification parameter α is almost equal to zero, indicating a first-order phase transition.
- The imaginary part τ₁ of the zero closest to the real axis scales as τ₁ ∼ A⁻⁰·⁹⁶, meaning the phase transition approaches a true first-order transition as 1/A.
- The critical temperature T = 1/β₁ reaches about 5 MeV at A ≈ 200, matching the experimentally found value.
- The accessible volume was assumed to be three times larger than the normal nucleus volume V₀, with additional calculations for volumes twice, three, and five times V₀ showing only quantitative changes in critical temperature.
- The surface tension parameters used were W₀ = 16 MeV, σ₀ = 18 MeV, and T_c = 18 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classification scheme for phase transitions in finite systems proposed by Borrmann and Muelken (2000) to the specific case of nuclear multifragmentation. The results agree with recent micro-canonical and grand-canonical calculations by Chomaz (2000) and Bugaev (2000). The finding that multifragmentation is a first-order phase transition is a necessary condition for interpreting it as the onset of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, though the authors note this is not yet sufficient. The work also draws a comparison to Bose-Einstein condensation, noting that despite similar recursion structures, multifragmentation is first-order while Bose-Einstein condensation is second or third order depending on the single particle density of states.
