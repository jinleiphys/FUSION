# Medium-induced gluon branching

> We study the evolution of an energetic jet which radiates gluons while propagating through a dense QCD medium modeled as a random distribution of color sources. Motivated by the heavy ion experimental program at the LHC, we focus on the medium-induced radiation of (relatively) soft gluons, which are abundantly emitted at large angles and thus can transport a small fraction of the jet energy far away from the jet axis. We perform a complete calculation of the medium-induced gluon branching in the regime where the gluons that take part in the branching undergo multiple soft scattering with the medium. We extend the BDMPSZ theory of radiative energy loss by including the transverse momentum dependence in the kernel that describes the branching and by analyzing the correlations between the two offspring gluons. We demonstrate that these gluons lose color coherence with respect to each other over a time scale that is comparable to the duration of the branching. It follows that interference effects between successive emissions are suppressed, a necessary ingredient for a description of multiple emission of soft gluons by a probabilistic, branching process.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that in the regime of multiple soft scattering, the two gluons produced in a medium-induced branching lose color coherence with respect to each other over a time scale comparable to the duration of the branching itself, which suppresses interference effects between successive emissions and enables a probabilistic description of multiple soft gluon emissions.

## Method
The authors perform a complete calculation of medium-induced gluon branching in the regime where gluons undergo multiple soft scattering with the medium, modeled as a random distribution of color sources with Gaussian correlations. They extend the BDMPSZ theory by including transverse momentum dependence in the branching kernel and analyzing correlations between the two offspring gluons, using path integral methods and a 4-point function analysis in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Key numbers
- The branching time scale \(\tau_{\rm br}(\omega) \sim \sqrt{2\omega/\hat q}\), where \(\omega\) is the gluon energy and \(\hat q\) is the jet quenching parameter
- The characteristic transverse momentum acquired during branching: \(k_{\rm br}(\omega) \sim (2\omega\hat q)^{1/4}\)
- The characteristic emission angle: \(\theta_{\rm br} \sim k_{\rm br}/\omega \sim (\hat q/\omega^3)^{1/4}\)
- The critical frequency \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2}\hat q L^2\), where \(L\) is the medium length
- The BDMPSZ energy spectrum: \(\omega \frac{dN}{d\omega} \simeq \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{\pi} \sqrt{\omega_c/\omega}\) for \(\omega \lesssim \omega_c\)
- The energy loss estimate: \(\Delta E \sim \bar\alpha \omega_c \sim \bar\alpha \hat q L^2\), where \(\bar\alpha = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\)
- The soft gluon regime where multiple emissions become important: \(\omega \lesssim \omega_s \equiv \bar\alpha^2 \omega_c\)
- The typical time interval between emissions: \(\tau_{\rm rad}(\omega_0) = (1/\bar\alpha) \sqrt{2\omega_0/\hat q}\)
- For rough LHC parameters: \(\hat q \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(L \sim 6\) fm, giving \(\omega_c \sim 100\) GeV

## Context
This work extends the BDMPSZ theory of radiative energy loss (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1996-1998; Zakharov, 1996-1997) by providing a complete calculation of the medium-induced gluon branching kernel with transverse momentum dependence. It enables the development of a probabilistic branching process description for multiple soft gluon emissions, which is necessary for interpreting LHC heavy ion data on jet shapes and particle correlations. The results support previous phenomenological studies and Monte Carlo codes (Baier, 2000-2001; Jeon, 2003; Armesto, 2009; Schenke, 2009; Zapp, 2011) that assumed factorization of subsequent emissions, and extend earlier work on color antenna interference (Mehtar-Tani, 2010-2012; Casalderrey-Solana, 2011).
