# Time-dependent heavy-quark potential at finite temperature from gauge-gravity duality

> The potential between a heavy quark and an anti-quark inside the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is studied on the basis of the gauge/gravity duality. A real-time complex potential V_{Q\bar{Q}}(t,r) is derived from the Wilson loop with the Euclidean five-dimensional anti-de-Sitter black hole metric. To make the analytic continuation from the imaginary time to the real time, specific variational configurations of the string world sheet in the Euclidean metric are introduced. Rapid approach of V_{Q\bar{Q}}(t,r) to its stationary value is found at the time scale t=1/πT independent of the 't Hooft coupling λ. Also, the imaginary part of V_{Q\bar{Q}}(\infty,r) is found to be significant above the length scale r=1.72/πT independent of λ. Implications of these results to the properties of heavy quarkonia in QGP are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The heavy-quark potential in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) approaches its stationary value within a time scale of \(t \simeq (\pi T)^{-1}\) that is independent of the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\), and the imaginary part of the asymptotic potential becomes significant above a length scale \(r = 1.72 (\pi T)^{-1}\) also independent of \(\lambda\); these results are derived from a variational ansatz for the string world sheet in Euclidean AdS\(_5\) black hole metric, not from an exact solution of the string equations of motion.

## Method

The authors start from a Euclidean rectangular Wilson loop \(W_{\mathrm{E}}(\tau,r)\) and evaluate it using the gauge-gravity duality by extremizing the Nambu-Goto action for a string world sheet in the Euclidean AdS\(_5\) black hole metric, using three variational configurations (box type, wedge type, and antibox type) with a single variational parameter \(a\) (the bottom string coordinate in the fifth dimension). They then perform an analytic continuation \(\tau \rightarrow it\) to obtain the Minkowski Wilson loop \(W_{\mathrm{M}}(t,r)\) and extract the complex time-dependent potential \(V_{Q\bar{Q}}(t,r)\) via the logarithmic derivative \(i\partial_t \ln W_{\mathrm{M}}(t,r)\).

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time scale: \(t_{\mathrm{eq}} \simeq (\pi T)^{-1}\), independent of the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\).
- Threshold distance for the imaginary part of the asymptotic potential: \(r_{\mathrm{th}} = (4/27)^{1/4} (\pi T)^{-1}\).
- Dissociation distance where the imaginary part of the potential starts to dominate over the real part: \(r_{\mathrm{dis}} = 1.72 (\pi T)^{-1}\), independent of \(\lambda\).
- For \(T = 300\) MeV: \(t_{\mathrm{eq}} \simeq 0.22\) fm and \(r_{\mathrm{dis}} \simeq 0.36\) fm.
- Asymptotic potential at \(T=0\) and \(t\rightarrow\infty\): \(V_{Q\bar{Q}}(\infty,r)\big|_{T=0} = -\sqrt{2\lambda}/(2\pi r)\), which is \(30\%\) smaller than the exact value \(4\pi^2/\Gamma(1/4)^4\) from Maldacena (1998).
- Imaginary part at large distance: \(\mathrm{Im}\, V_{Q\bar{Q}}(\infty, r \gg r_{\mathrm{th}}) \rightarrow -\sqrt{2\lambda}(2\pi)^{-1}(\pi T)^2 r\).

## Context

This work extends the gauge-gravity duality approach to heavy-quark potentials beyond the static limit studied by Kovchekov (2008), who used the Minkowski AdS\(_5\) black hole metric and could not address time-dependent phenomena. The results contrast with weak-coupling thermal perturbation theory (Laine 2007), where the equilibration time in weakly coupled QGP is \(t_{\mathrm{eq}} \simeq 10 m_{\mathrm{D}}^{-1} \sim 10(gT)^{-1}\) for similar distances, an order of magnitude longer than the sQGP result. The findings enable phenomenological studies of sequential melting of heavy quarkonia in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that the imaginary part of the potential may be more relevant than color Debye screening for understanding the suppression patterns of \(J/\Psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) states.
