# Pairing and short-range correlations in nuclear systems

> The structure and density dependence of the pairing gap in infinite matter is relevant for astrophysical phenomena and provides a starting point for the discussion of pairing properties in nuclear structure. Short-range correlations can significantly deplete the available single-particle strength around the Fermi surface and thus provide a reduction mechanism of the pairing gap. Here, we study this effect in the singlet and triplet channels of both neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. Our calculations use phase-shift equivalent interactions and chiral two-body and three-body interactions as a starting point. We find an unambiguous reduction of the gap in all channels with very small dependence on the NN force in the singlet neutron matter and the triplet nuclear matter channel. In the latter channel, short range correlations alone provide a 50% reduction of the pairing gap.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) reduce the pairing gap in all channels of neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, with a 50% reduction of the maximum gap in the triplet channel of symmetric nuclear matter; this reduction is largely independent of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) force in the singlet neutron matter and triplet nuclear matter channels, but the triplet neutron matter channel shows strong sensitivity to the underlying NN interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation for infinite nuclear matter using a Gorkov-inspired treatment that incorporates short-range correlations via a "double normal" denominator constructed from zero-temperature extrapolated normal-state spectral functions obtained from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations. They employ phase-shift equivalent interactions (CD-Bonn, Argonne v18) and chiral N3LO two-body forces with N2LO three-body forces, comparing BCS results with results that include single-particle energy shifts, Z-factor renormalization, and full SRC treatment via the fragmented energy denominator.

## Key numbers

- Maximum singlet gap in neutron matter (BCS with NN-only): Δ_max ≈ 2.96–3.23 MeV at k_F ≈ 0.86 fm⁻¹, closing above k_F ≈ 1.52 fm⁻¹
- Maximum singlet gap in neutron matter (BCS with NN+3NF): Δ_max ≈ 2.81–2.96 MeV
- Maximum singlet gap in neutron matter (BCS+ε_k+Z_k): Δ_max ≈ 2.40–2.65 MeV
- Maximum singlet gap in neutron matter (SRC with chiral NN+3NF): Δ_max ≈ 2.15–2.34 MeV at k_F ≈ 0.84 fm⁻¹
- Maximum triplet gap in neutron matter (BCS with NN-only): ranges from below 0.3 MeV (N3LO414, N3LO450) to above 0.5 MeV (CD-Bonn, Av18, N3LO500)
- Maximum triplet gap in neutron matter (BCS with NN+3NF): Δ_max = 0.5–0.6 MeV for N3LO414 and N3LO450, 1.22 MeV for N3LO500
- Maximum triplet gap in neutron matter (SRC with chiral NN+3NF): up to about 0.45 MeV for N3LO450
- Maximum triplet gap in symmetric nuclear matter (BCS with NN-only): Δ_max ≈ 10–13 MeV (Av18 ≈ 13 MeV, CD-Bonn ≈ 12 MeV, chiral ≈ 10–11.5 MeV)
- Maximum triplet gap in symmetric nuclear matter (SRC): Δ_max ≈ 4.7–5.5 MeV, approximately 50% of the BCS maximum value
- Triplet gap closure in symmetric nuclear matter (SRC with CD-Bonn and Av18): around k_F ≈ 1.5–1.7 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous BCS calculations of pairing gaps in infinite nuclear matter (Dean 2003, Baldo 1995, Vonderfecht 1991) by incorporating short-range correlations through the self-consistent Green's function approach developed by Muther (2005) and Ding (2016). The results contradict the naive expectation that BCS predictions for the triplet channel in symmetric nuclear matter are valid, showing instead that SRC reduce the gap by about 50% regardless of the interaction. The findings enable more realistic predictions for neutron star cooling physics (Page 2011, Ho 2015) and provide a benchmark for future fully self-consistent Gorkov-Green's function calculations that do not rely on zero-temperature extrapolation.
