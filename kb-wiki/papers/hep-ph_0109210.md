# Strong nuclear enhancement in intermediate mass Drell-Yan production

> We calculate nuclear effect in Drell-Yan massive lepton-pair production in terms of parton multiple scattering in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We present the nuclear modification to inclusive Drell-Yan cross section $dσ/dQ^2$ in terms of multiparton correlation functions. By extracting the size of the correlation functions from measured Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening in nuclear medium, we determine the nuclear modification at $O(α_s/Q^2)$. We find that the nuclear modification strongly enhances the inclusive Drell-Yan cross section in the intermediate mass region (IMR): $1.5 < Q < 2.5$ GeV. We argue that the Drell-Yan process is responsible for the majority of the observed dimuon enhancement in the IMR in the Pb-Pb collisions at CERN SPS.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that nuclear size-enhanced power corrections (high-twist contributions) to the Drell-Yan cross section, arising from parton multiple scattering in the nuclear medium, produce a strong positive enhancement of the inclusive Drell-Yan continuum in the intermediate mass region (IMR: 1.5 < Q < 2.5 GeV), and argue that this enhancement can explain the majority of the observed dimuon excess in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN SPS, provided the sign of the correction is positive (which they demonstrate it is).

## Method

The authors use the generalized factorization theorem in perturbative QCD to calculate the leading power corrections (at O(alpha_s/Q^2)) to the inclusive Drell-Yan cross section d sigma/dQ^2 in hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. These corrections are expressed in terms of a twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function T_qF(x), which is extracted from measured Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening in nuclear medium using a model T_qF(x) = lambda^2 A^(4/3) phi_q/N(x) with lambda^2 = 0.01 GeV^2. The ratio R_AB(Q) of double scattering to single scattering contributions is then evaluated numerically using leading order CTEQ5L parton distributions.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear modification ratio R_AB(Q) for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(S)=17.8 GeV/nucleon with c.m. rapidity coverage 0<y_cm<1 reaches approximately 50% enhancement at Q=2 GeV.
* The nuclear modification ratio R_AB(Q) for p-W collisions at sqrt(S)=30 GeV/nucleon with c.m. rapidity coverage -0.52<y_cm<0.48 reaches approximately 20% enhancement at Q=2 GeV.
* The extracted parameter lambda^2 = 0.01 GeV^2 from Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening data, with an alternative extraction from 2-jet momentum imbalance in photon-nucleus collisions giving lambda^2 ~ 0.05 GeV^2.
* The ratio R_AB(Q) is estimated to be about 5% at Q=4 GeV for hadron-nucleus collisions, within experimental uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Qiu and Sterman (1991) on generalized factorization and high-twist contributions in hard processes, and builds on the calculation of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening by Guo (1998) which used the same quark-gluon correlation function. The paper addresses the dimuon excess observed by the NA38 and NA50 Collaborations (2000) in heavy nucleus-nucleus collisions between the phi and J/psi masses, which had previously been attributed to open charm enhancement, thermal dimuons, or secondary meson-meson scattering. The results enable a new fit for expected dilepton sources in the IMR that includes power corrections to the Drell-Yan continuum, which is important because the J/psi cross section in Pb-Pb collisions was normalized to the measured Drell-Yan continuum in the same experiment.
