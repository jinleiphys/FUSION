# Medium dependent relativistic NN potential: Application to the fusion dynamics

> In-medium effects are introduced in the microscopic description of the effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction potential entitled DDR3Y in terms of the density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings within the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach. The nuclear densities of the interacting target and projectile nuclei and NN potentials are obtained for non-linear NL3$^*$ and TM1 parameter sets within the relativistic mean-field approach and density-dependent DDME1 and DDME2 parameter sets within the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) formalism. The DDR3Y NN potential and the densities are used to obtain the nuclear potential by adopting the double folding approach. This nuclear potential is further used to probe the fusion dynamics within the $\ell-$summed Wong model for a few {\it even-even} systems leading to the formation of light, heavy and superheavy nuclei. The calculations are also performed for the relativistic R3Y, density-dependent and independent M3Y interaction potentials for the comparison. We observed that the DDR3Y NN potential gives a better overlap with the experimental data as compared to non-relativistic M3Y and DDM3Y NN potentials. From the comparison of R3Y and DDR3Y interactions, it is manifested that the inclusion of in-medium effects in terms of density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings raises the fusion barrier and consequently decreases the fusion and/or capture cross-section. Moreover, the nuclear densities, as well as the relativistic R3Y NN potential obtained for the NL3$^*$ parameter set, are observed to give a comparatively better fit to the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing in-medium effects into the relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential via density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings (creating the DDR3Y potential) yields a better description of fusion cross-sections compared to non-relativistic M3Y and DDM3Y potentials, but the improvement is conditional on the specific parameter set used (NL3* gives the best overall fit among the relativistic sets).

## Method

The authors derived a density-dependent relativistic NN potential (DDR3Y) within the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach using DDME1 and DDME2 parameter sets, where the nucleon-meson coupling constants depend on the projectile and target densities. They obtained nuclear densities for target and projectile nuclei from the RHB approach (DDME1, DDME2) and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach (NL3*, TM1), then folded these densities with the DDR3Y, R3Y, DDM3Y, and M3Y NN potentials using the double-folding model. The resulting nuclear potentials were used to calculate fusion cross-sections for six even-even reaction systems within the l-summed Wong model, and results were compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the 48Ca+208Pb system, the DDR3Y-DDME2 potential gives a barrier height of 173.64 MeV and barrier position of 12.8 fm, compared to 169.81 MeV and 13.2 fm for R3Y-NL3* and 179.28 MeV and 12.3 fm for DDM3Y-NL3*
- The nuclear matter incompressibility values used are: NL3* (K=258.8 MeV), TM1 (K=281 MeV), DDME1 (K=244.5 MeV), DDME2 (K=250.89 MeV), and BDM3Y1 (K=232 MeV)
- The saturation density for both DDME1 and DDME2 parameter sets is 0.152 fm^-3
- For the 16O+40Ca system, the DDR3Y-DDME2 barrier height is 23.41 MeV at 9.2 fm, while the M3Y-NL3* barrier is 24.01 MeV at 8.9 fm
- For the 40Ca+90Zr system, the DDR3Y-DDME2 barrier height is 99.28 MeV at 10.9 fm, compared to 101.77 MeV at 10.5 fm for M3Y-NL3*

## Context

This work extends the relativistic R3Y NN potential approach previously developed by Singh (2012), Sahu (2014), and Lahiri (2016) for cluster radioactivity and fusion studies by Bhuyan (2018, 2020), Rana (2021), and Raj (2022) by introducing explicit density dependence in the meson-nucleon couplings. It parallels the development of density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) potentials by Khoa (1994, 1995) but uses a microscopic RHB framework rather than a phenomenological density weight function. The DDR3Y potential enables more accurate fusion cross-section calculations for reactions forming light, heavy, and superheavy nuclei, and the authors note that the frozen density approximation (FDA) used for DDM3Y is inappropriate for DDR3Y because it yields densities exceeding saturation density at small separations, while the relaxed density approximation (RDA) gives closer agreement to the full DDR3Y treatment.
