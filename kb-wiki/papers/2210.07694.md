# Spheroidal expansion and freeze-out geometry of heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime

> A spheroidal model of the expansion of hadronic matter produced in heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime is proposed. It constitutes an extension of the spherically symmetric Siemens-Rasmussen blast-wave model used in our previous works. The spheroidal form of the expansion, combined with a single-freeze-out scenario, allows for a significantly improved description of both the transverse-mass and the rapidity distributions of the produced particles. With the model parameters determined by the hadronic abundances and spectra, we make further predictions of the pion HBT correlation radii that turn out to be in a qualitative agreement with the measured ones. The overall successful description of the data supports the concept of spheroidal symmetry of the produced hadronic systems in this energy range.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a spheroidal (rather than spherical) expansion symmetry of the hadronic fireball, combined with a single-freeze-out scenario, provides a significantly improved description of both transverse-mass and rapidity distributions of particles produced in heavy-ion collisions at few-GeV energies, though the authors note that the agreement with measured pion HBT correlation radii is only qualitative.

## Method

The authors extend the spherically symmetric Siemens-Rasmussen blast-wave model by introducing two eccentricity parameters: δ for momentum-space deformation and ε for position-space deformation along the beam axis. They use the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo generator to simulate particle production from a spheroidal freeze-out hypersurface with Hubble-like radial flow, fitting the model parameters (Hubble parameter H, momentum-space eccentricity δ, and position-space eccentricity ε) to experimental transverse-mass spectra of protons and pions measured by HADES in Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 2.4 GeV, using two different sets of thermodynamic parameters (Case A and Case B) obtained from fits to particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 49.6 MeV and baryon chemical potential μB = 776 MeV for Case A; T = 70.3 MeV and μB = 876 MeV for Case B
- Fireball radius R = 15.7 fm for Case A; R = 6.06 fm for Case B
- Momentum-space eccentricity δ = 0.2 for Case A; δ = 0.4 for Case B
- Hubble expansion parameter H = 0.01 GeV for Case A; H = 0.0225 GeV for Case B
- Mean relative error Q = 0.238 for Case A; Q = 0.256 for Case B; Q = 0.285 for the spherical model
- χ²/Ndf = 1.13/2 for Case B (Case A has zero degrees of freedom)
- Particle multiplicities used include: free protons 77.6 ± 2.4, π⁺ 9.3 ± 0.6, π⁻ 17.1 ± 1.1, K⁺ 5.98×10⁻² ± 6.79×10⁻³, K⁻ 5.6×10⁻⁴ ± 5.96×10⁻⁵, Λ 8.22×10⁻² with asymmetric uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous spherically symmetric Siemens-Rasmussen blast-wave model (Harabasz 2020), which described transverse-mass spectra to about 20% accuracy but produced systematically narrower rapidity distributions than observed experimentally. The spheroidal generalization addresses this shortcoming and supports the concept of thermal equilibrium in the few-GeV energy regime, where non-equilibrium transport model approaches (Bass 1998, Buss 2011, Petersen 2018) are commonly used. The framework enables further predictions of HBT correlation radii that can discriminate between different thermodynamic parameter sets (Case A vs. Case B from Motornenko 2021) and provides a basis for implementing final-state electromagnetic interactions in future Monte-Carlo simulations.
