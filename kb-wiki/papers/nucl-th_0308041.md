# Warm stellar matter with deconfinement: application to compact stars

> We investigate the properties of mixed stars formed by hadronic and quark matter in $β$-equilibrium described by appropriate equations of state (EOS) in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. We use the non- linear Walecka model for the hadron matter and the MIT Bag and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models for the quark matter. The phase transition to a deconfined quark phase is investigated. In particular, we study the dependence of the onset of a mixed phase and a pure quark phase on the hyperon couplings, quark model and properties of the hadronic model. We calculate the strangeness fraction with baryonic density for the different EOS. With the NJL model the strangeness content in the mixed phase decreases. The calculations were performed for T=0 and for finite temperatures in order to describe neutron and proto-neutron stars. The star properties are discussed. Both the Bag model and the NJL model predict a mixed phase in the interior of the star. Maximum allowed masses for proto-neutron stars are larger for the NJL model ($\sim 1.9$ M$_{\bigodot}$) than for the Bag model ($\sim 1.6$ M$_{\bigodot}$).

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter can exist inside neutron and proto-neutron stars, finding that the existence and onset density of such a mixed phase is highly sensitive to the choice of hyperon coupling constants and the quark model used, with the NJL model requiring a sufficiently strong Lambda-sigma coupling (x_sigma >= 0.65) for a mixed phase to appear.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for beta-equilibrium matter using the nonlinear Walecka model (GL parametrization) for hadronic matter including the full baryonic octet, and both the MIT Bag model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for quark matter. They enforced Gibbs conditions for phase coexistence (equal baryon chemical potentials, temperatures, and pressures) with global charge neutrality, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar mass profiles for temperatures from 0 to 30 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum allowed mass for proto-neutron stars with the NJL model: ~1.9 solar masses
- Maximum allowed mass for proto-neutron stars with the MIT Bag model: ~1.6 solar masses
- NJL model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 631.4 MeV, g_S * Lambda^2 = 1.824, g_D * Lambda^5 = -9.4, m_u = m_d = 5.6 MeV, m_s = 135.6 MeV
- MIT Bag model parameters: Bag = (180 MeV)^4 or Bag = (190 MeV)^4, m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 150.0 MeV
- GL hadronic model saturation density: rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}, binding energy = -16.3 MeV, compression modulus = 300 MeV, effective mass = 0.7 M
- For the NJL model with x_H = sqrt(2/3), the mixed phase onset occurs at ~2 rho_0 at T=0 and at ~5 rho_0 for other coupling choices
- The effective bag pressure in the NJL model shows a plateau around 161-163 MeV between rho = 3 rho_0 and 5 rho_0 at T=0

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid stars by Glendenning (2000) and Prakash et al. (1997) by systematically comparing the MIT Bag model and the NJL model for quark matter, and by including finite temperature effects relevant for proto-neutron stars. The results contradict Schertler et al. (1999) regarding the density range over which a mixed phase exists, while confirming some findings of Splittorff et al. (2000) about the possibility of central densities falling in the quark phase. The study enables future work on the thermal evolution and cooling of hybrid stars, and on the role of strangeness content in determining observable star properties.
