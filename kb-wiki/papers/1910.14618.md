# Blast-wave description of $Υ$ elliptic flow at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> A simultaneous blast-wave fit to particle yields and elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) measured as a function of transverse momentum in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies is presented. A compact formula for the calculation of $v_2(p_T)$ for an elliptic freeze-out surface is used which follows from the Cooper-Frye ansatz without further assumptions. Over the full available $p_T$ range, the $Υ$ elliptic flow data is described by the prediction based on the fit to lighter particles. This prediction shows that, due to the large $Υ$ mass, a sizable elliptic flow is only expected at transverse momenta above 10 GeV/$c$.

# Blast-wave description of $\Upsilon$ elliptic flow at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim
A simultaneous blast-wave fit to lighter hadrons (kaons, protons, $\phi$, $\Omega$) predicts that $\Upsilon$ elliptic flow ($v_2$) should be consistent with zero within the current kinematic reach of ALICE data at LHC energies, with a sizable $v_2$ only expected above 10 GeV/$c$ transverse momentum due to the large $\Upsilon$ mass. The authors state that with current $\Upsilon$ $v_2$ data alone, one cannot rule out a scenario in which $b$ quarks thermalize and $\Upsilon$ mesons follow the mass ordering of $v_2(p_T)$ from collective expansion.

## Method
A blast-wave model with an elliptical freeze-out hyper-surface was used, where the flow velocity vector of a fluid cell points perpendicular to the freeze-out ellipse. A compact formula for $v_2(p_T)$ was derived from the Cooper-Frye ansatz without additional assumptions, and a simultaneous fit was performed to transverse momentum spectra and $v_2(p_T)$ data for kaons, protons, $\phi$, and $\Omega$ mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, with pions excluded to reduce feed-down effects. The fit parameters were then used to predict $v_2(p_T)$ for heavier particles including $D^0$, $J/\psi$, and $\Upsilon$.

## Key numbers
- Fit temperature $T = 128.4 \pm 5.1$ MeV for the standard fit (K, p, $\phi$, $\Omega$ with $\beta^\mathrm{max}=0.68$)
- Radial flow rapidity $\rho_0 = 1.05 \pm 0.01$ for the standard fit
- Elliptic flow parameter $\rho_2 = 0.09 \pm 0.004$ for the standard fit
- Ellipse axis ratio $R_x/R_y = 0.83 \pm 0.004$ for the standard fit
- Fit temperature $T = 107.5 \pm 2.2$ MeV for the ALICE fit range using only $\pi$, K, p spectra
- $\Upsilon$ $v_2$ prediction shows sizable elliptic flow only at transverse momenta above 10 GeV/$c$
- $\Upsilon$ mass is 9.46 GeV/$c^2$
- $\Upsilon$ $v_2$ data from ALICE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, centrality 5-60%, acceptance $2.5 < y < 4$
- $\Upsilon$ $p_T$ spectrum peak maximum at about 4 GeV/$c$, compatible with transverse flow rapidity of about 0.5 in peripheral collisions

## Context
This work extends blast-wave model analyses previously applied to lighter hadrons at LHC energies, such as the ALICE blast-wave fits to $\pi$, K, p spectra (Abelev 2013), and the STAR formula approach for $v_2(p_T)$ fits (Adler 2001, Adamczyk 2015). The model provides a baseline to test whether heavy $b$ quarks thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma, complementing transport calculations (Du 2017, Bhaduri 2018) which predict small regeneration fractions for $\Upsilon$ mesons. The results enable future comparisons with higher-$p_T$ $\Upsilon$ $v_2$ measurements at the LHC to test the thermalization hypothesis, and highlight the need for $\Upsilon$ $p_T$ spectra in narrower centrality classes to fully address the question of $b$ quark thermalization.
