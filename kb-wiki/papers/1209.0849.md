# Octet-baryon masses in finite space

> We report on a recent study of finite-volume effects on the lowest-lying octet baryon masses using the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order by analysing the latest $n_f = 2 + 1$ lattice QCD results from the NPLQCD Collaboration.

# Octet-baryon masses in finite space

## Key claim
Finite-volume corrections to octet baryon masses can be described by next-to-leading-order covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme, but the underlying role of virtual decuplet baryon contributions is smaller in the EOMS approach than in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), even though both approaches fit the NPLQCD lattice data with similar quality.

## Method
The authors fitted the NPLQCD Collaboration's \(n_f = 2 + 1\) lattice QCD results for octet baryon masses using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order with the EOMS renormalization scheme, and compared the fits to those obtained with HBChPT. Finite-volume corrections were computed by replacing spatial momentum integrals with discrete sums over allowed momenta in a finite hypercube, and the low-energy constants were determined by fitting the lattice data.

## Key numbers
- Both EOMS BChPT and HBChPT yield fits with \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 1.6\) to the NPLQCD lattice data.
- The NPLQCD lattice data were obtained with \(n_f = 2 + 1\) flavors (two light quarks and one strange quark).
- The chiral extrapolations from EOMS BChPT agree much better with experimental octet baryon masses than those from HBChPT, as shown in Table III of Geng (2011).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of finite-volume effects on nucleon masses in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory by AliKhan (2003) to the three-flavor octet baryon sector. It provides the first systematic study of finite-volume corrections for all ground-state octet baryons using the EOMS scheme, and it enables more reliable chiral and finite-volume extrapolations of lattice QCD results. The findings contradict the conclusion of Beane (2011) that decuplet contributions must be included, showing instead that their importance depends on the chiral perturbation theory framework used.
