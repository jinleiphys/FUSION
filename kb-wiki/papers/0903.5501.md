# Shape mixing and beta-decay properties of neutron-deficient Kr and Sr isotopes

> Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives in neutron-deficient Kr and Sr isotopes are investigated within a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation. The approach is based on a selfconsistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations and residual separable particle-hole and particle-particle forces. A simple two-level model is used to mix the nuclear shapes into the physical ground state. Good agreement with experiment is found with shape mixing coefficients which are consistent with those obtained phenomenologically from mixing of rotational bands.

# Shape mixing and beta-decay properties of neutron-deficient Kr and Sr isotopes

## Key claim

Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives in neutron-deficient Kr and Sr isotopes can be described by a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation combined with a simple two-level shape mixing model, but the agreement depends on the specific isotope and the mixing coefficients are not always uniquely determined by the data; for example, the experimental monopole strength in 74Kr is inconsistent with the 48% prolate mixing extracted from Coulomb excitation, while the half-lives are reproduced with 50-60% mixing.

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme interactions Sk3 and SLy4, including BCS pairing correlations, to generate single-particle energies and wave functions for 72,74Kr and 76,78Sr. They then applied a quasiparticle random phase approximation with separable residual particle-hole and particle-particle interactions to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives, using a two-level mixing model to combine prolate and oblate (or spherical) intrinsic configurations into the physical ground states.

## Key numbers

- For 72Kr, the experimental squared mixing amplitude for the prolate configuration is λ = 0.10 (mostly oblate), extracted phenomenologically by Bouchez et al.
- For 74Kr, the extracted squared mixing amplitude for the prolate configuration from Coulomb excitation is λ = 0.48 (Clement et al.)
- The coupling strengths used in the QRPA calculations are χ_ph_GT = 0.17 MeV and κ_pp_GT = 0.03 MeV
- A standard quenching factor of 0.77 is applied to the Gamow-Teller strength calculations
- The half-life formula uses D = 6200 s
- For 72Kr, the half-life is reproduced with λ = 0.3 (Sk3) or λ = 0.2 (SLy4), slightly higher than the experimental λ = 0.10
- For 74Kr with Sk3, λ = 0.6 reproduces the half-life
- The Q_EC value for 72Kr is 5.04 MeV; for 78Sr it is 3.76 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous deformed QRPA calculations by Sarriguren et al. and applies them to shape-coexisting nuclei in the A=70-80 mass region, where shape coexistence has been experimentally observed in Kr isotopes by Piercey et al., Chandler et al., Becker et al., and Bouchez et al. The two-level mixing model follows the approach used by Heyde et al. and Clement et al. to interpret Coulomb excitation data and electric monopole strengths. The results are compared with experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions from Piqueras et al. (72Kr), Poirier et al. (74Kr), and Nacher et al. (76Sr), and with calculations from the complex excited VAMPIR approach by Petrovici et al. The study provides complementary evidence for shape coexistence in this mass region and is relevant for nuclear reaction rates in the rapid-proton capture process in X-ray bursts (Schatz et al.).
