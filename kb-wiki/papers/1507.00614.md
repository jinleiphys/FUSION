# A study of charm quark correlations in ultra-relativistic $p$ + $p$ collisions with PYTHIA

> Measurements of heavy flavor quark (charm and bottom) correlations in heavy ion collisions are instrumental to understand the flavor dependence of energy loss mechanisms in hot and dense QCD media. Experimental measurements of these correlations in baseline $p$+$p$ collisions are crucial to understand the contributions of perturbative and non-perturbative QCD processes to the correlation functions and further help in interpreting correlation measurements in heavy ion collisions. In this paper, we investigate $D$-$\bar{D}$ meson correlations and $D$ with one particle from $D$ meson decay daughter correlations using PYTHIA Event Generator in $p$ + $p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200, 500 and 5500 GeV. Charm/bottom events are found to contribute mainly to the away side/near side pattern of $D$-electron correlations, respectively. In the energy region of RHIC, $D$-$\bar{D}$ correlations inherit initial $c$-$\bar{c}$ correlations and $B\rightarrow DX$ decay contribution is insignificant. Furthermore, Bottom quark correlations are suggested to be applicable at LHC energy, as the bottom contributions on $D$ related correlations are relatively large.

## Key claim

The paper uses PYTHIA simulations to show that $D$-$\bar{D}$ meson correlations in $p$+$p$ collisions mostly inherit initial $c$-$\bar{c}$ pair correlations at RHIC energies, but at LHC energies the bottom quark contributions and higher-order pQCD processes become significant enough that charm correlations may be less attractive, and bottom correlations might be a cleaner probe.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8 (version 8.168) to simulate $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200, 500, and 5500 GeV, tuning the strong coupling constant for final parton showers (TimeShower:alphaSvalue) and the minimum invariant transverse momentum threshold for hard QCD processes (PhaseSpace:pTHatMin) to match measured $c\bar{c}$ production cross sections from STAR at 200 and 500 GeV and ALICE at 7000 GeV. They then analyzed azimuthal correlation functions for $D$-$\bar{D}$ pairs, $D$-$K$, $K$-$D$, $D$-$e$, and $e$-$D$ pairs, separating contributions from direct charm production and from bottom decays, and comparing results across collision energies.

## Key numbers

- The tuned PYTHIA parameters for $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV were TimeShower:alphaSvalue = 0.18 and PhaseSpace:pTHatMin = 1.3 GeV/$c$, yielding $\chi^{2}/{\rm ndf}$ = 1.31 to STAR data.
- For $\sqrt{s}$ = 500 GeV, the tuned parameters were TimeShower:alphaSvalue = 0.15 and PhaseSpace:pTHatMin = 1.5 GeV/$c$, with $\chi^{2}/{\rm ndf}$ = 2.13.
- For $\sqrt{s}$ = 7000 GeV, the tuned parameters were TimeShower:alphaSvalue = 0.15 and PhaseSpace:pTHatMin = 2.8 GeV/$c$, with $\chi^{2}/{\rm ndf}$ = 1.95.
- In $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 500 GeV with a trigger $p_T$ cut > 3 GeV/$c$, $D$-$\bar{D}$ pairs from $B$ decays are approximately 4% of the total $D$-$\bar{D}$ pairs.
- Up to trigger $p_T$ cut at 6 GeV/$c$, $D$-$\bar{D}$ pairs from $B$ contributions are less than 5% in 200 GeV collisions, while the $B$ contribution is approximately 15% in 5500 GeV collisions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of heavy flavor correlations studied by Xianglei Zhu and collaborators (2011, 2012), who predicted that radiative and collisional energy loss mechanisms produce distinct modifications to charm pair correlations in heavy ion collisions. The paper provides PYTHIA-based baseline calculations for $p$+$p$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, which are necessary to interpret upcoming measurements of $D$-$\bar{D}$ and $D$-electron correlations in heavy ion collisions at STAR and ALICE. The results enable experimentalists to design correlation measurements that separate charm and bottom contributions, and suggest that at LHC energies, bottom quark correlations may be a more practical probe than charm correlations.
