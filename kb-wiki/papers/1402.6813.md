## Key claim

The study finds that the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave model can describe the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons only for high-multiplicity events in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, not for minimum-bias events, indicating that collective behavior may emerge only in the most central-like collisions.

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave analysis previously applied to nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, and to proton-lead (pPb) collisions at LHC, to proton-proton collisions. The results for high-multiplicity pp events show similar centrality dependence of âŸ¨Î²âŸ© and Tkin as seen in AuAu collisions at RHIC (analyzed by the same group) and PbPb and pPb collisions at LHC (ALICE collaboration). The finding of stronger radial flow in pp compared to pA and AA systems matches predictions from a theoretical work by Shuryak and Zahed (2013) on the applicability of hydrodynamics in small systems. The different response of strange particles versus pions/kaons/protons to the BGBW model echoes observations from SPS heavy-ion collisions and RHIC heavy-ion collisions where multi-strange hadrons appear to freeze out earlier.
