# Indication of transverse radial flow in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We analyze the measured spectra of $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p$($\bar p$) in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt {s}$ = 0.9, 2.76 and 7 TeV, in the light of blast-wave model to extract the transverse radial flow velocity and kinetic temperature at freeze-out for the system formed in $pp$ collisions. The dependency of the blast-wave parameters on average charged particle multiplicity of event sample or the `centrality' of collisions has been studied and compared with results of similar analysis in nucleus-nucleus ($AA$) and proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions. We analyze the spectra of $K_{s}^0$, $Λ$($\bar Λ$) and $Ξ^-$ also to see the dependence of blast-wave description on the species of produced particles. Within the framework of the blast-wave model, the study reveals indication of collective behavior for high-multiplicity events in $pp$ collisions at LHC. Strong transverse radial flow in high multiplicity $pp$ collisions and its comparison with that in $pA$ and $AA$ collisions match with predictions from a very recent theoretical work [Shuryak and Zahed 2013 arXiv:1301.4470] that addresses the conditions for applicability of hydrodynamics in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions.

## Key claim

The study finds that the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave model can describe the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons only for high-multiplicity events in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, not for minimum-bias events, indicating that collective behavior may emerge only in the most central-like collisions.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits of the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave (BGBW) model to published CMS transverse momentum spectra of π±, K±, and p(p̅) for pp collisions at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV, using a fit range of 0.5 < pT < 2 GeV/c. They also separately fitted the BGBW model to CMS spectra of Ks0, Λ(Λ̅), and Ξ− from non-single-diffractive events at √s = 0.9 and 7 TeV. The source radius for each event class was estimated from CMS HBT measurements using the parameterization R = a·⟨Nch⟩^(1/3).

## Key numbers

- For the highest-multiplicity pp events at √s = 7 TeV (⟨Nch⟩ = 131), the average transverse radial flow velocity ⟨β⟩ = 0.809 ± 0.005 and kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin = 104.29 ± 0.15 MeV, with χ²/n.d.f = 0.44
- For pp at √s = 2.76 TeV with ⟨Nch⟩ = 98, ⟨β⟩ = 0.769 ± 0.005 and Tkin = 110.39 ± 0.13 MeV
- For pp at √s = 0.9 TeV with ⟨Nch⟩ = 75, ⟨β⟩ = 0.745 ± 0.004 and Tkin = 106.43 ± 0.10 MeV
- For strange particles (Ks0, Λ, Ξ−) at √s = 7 TeV, Tkin = 149 ± 0.59 MeV and ⟨β⟩ = 0.62 ± 0.006
- For strange particles at √s = 0.9 TeV, Tkin = 140 ± 0.53 MeV and ⟨β⟩ = 0.54 ± 0.01

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave analysis previously applied to nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, and to proton-lead (pPb) collisions at LHC, to proton-proton collisions. The results for high-multiplicity pp events show similar centrality dependence of ⟨β⟩ and Tkin as seen in AuAu collisions at RHIC (analyzed by the same group) and PbPb and pPb collisions at LHC (ALICE collaboration). The finding of stronger radial flow in pp compared to pA and AA systems matches predictions from a theoretical work by Shuryak and Zahed (2013) on the applicability of hydrodynamics in small systems. The different response of strange particles versus pions/kaons/protons to the BGBW model echoes observations from SPS heavy-ion collisions and RHIC heavy-ion collisions where multi-strange hadrons appear to freeze out earlier.
