# Dissipative hydrodynamics coupled to chiral fields

> Using second--order dissipative hydrodynamics coupled self-consistently to the linear $σ$ model we study the 2+1 dimensional evolution of the fireball created in Au+Au relativistic collisions. We analyze the influence of the dynamics of the chiral fields on the charged-hadron elliptic flow $v_2$ and on the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ for a temperature-independent as well as for a temperature-dependent viscosity-to-entropy ratio $η/s$ calculated from the linearized Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. We find that $v_2$ is not very sensitive to the coupling of chiral sources to the hydrodynamic evolution, but the temperature dependence of $η/s$ plays a much bigger role on this observable. On the other hand, the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ turns out to be much more sensitive than $v_2$ to both the coupling of the chiral sources and the temperature dependence of $η/s$.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the charged-hadron elliptic flow $v_2$ is not very sensitive to the coupling of chiral fields to the hydrodynamic evolution, but the temperature dependence of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio $\eta/s$ plays a much bigger role on this observable; however, the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ is much more sensitive than $v_2$ to both the coupling of chiral sources and the temperature dependence of $\eta/s$.

## Method
The authors coupled second-order dissipative hydrodynamics to the linear sigma model (LSM) to simulate the 2+1 dimensional evolution of the fireball created in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV. They solved conservation equations for energy-momentum with source terms from chiral fields, along with classical equations of motion for the sigma and pion fields, using the publicly available code of Luzum and Romatschke with temperature-dependent speed of sound and shear viscosity calculated from the LSM and linearized Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. The initial energy density profile was calculated using Glauber's model with initialization time $\tau_0 = 1$ fm/c and central temperature $T_i = 333$ MeV, and freeze-out was performed using the isothermal Cooper-Frye prescription at $T_f = 130$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature: $T_i = 333$ MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: $T_f = 130$ MeV
- Initialization time: $\tau_0 = 1$ fm/c
- Critical temperature from LSM: $T_c \sim 150$ MeV (smaller than the lattice QCD value of $\sim 170$ MeV)
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 138$ MeV
- Sigma mass: $m_\sigma \sim 600$ MeV (obtained with $\lambda^2 = 20$)
- Cut-off imposed on $\eta/s$: $\eta/s \leq 0.4$ (corresponding to $T \sim 140$ MeV for $g=3, 3.2, 3.4$)
- Average value of $\eta/s$ throughout hydrodynamic evolution: $\sim 0.11$
- Transverse plane size: $13$ fm $\times 13$ fm
- Impact parameter used in Glauber model: Woods-Saxon parameters $R_0=6.4$ fm and $\chi=0.54$ fm

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Mishustin and Scavenius (ideal fluid dynamics with LSM) and Paech et al. (3+1 ideal hydrodynamics coupled to LSM) by incorporating second-order dissipative hydrodynamics with temperature-dependent transport coefficients. It also relates to the work of Plumari et al. (2010), who found a sizable suppression of $v_2$ due to chiral field dynamics using Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equations, and to studies by Niemi, Bozek, and Nagle (2011) showing that $v_2$ at RHIC is dominated by shear viscosity in the hadronic phase. The results enable future extraction of transport coefficients from higher-order flow harmonics like $v_4$, which may provide more stringent constraints on $\eta/s$ and the chiral transition than $v_2$ alone.
