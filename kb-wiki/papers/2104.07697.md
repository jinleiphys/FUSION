# Skyrme pseudopotentials at next-to-next-to-leading order Construction of local densities and first symmetry-breaking calculations

> There is an ongoing quest to improve on the spectroscopic quality of nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) of the Skyrme type through extensions of its traditional form. One direction for such activities is the inclusion of terms of higher order in gradients in the EDF. We report on exploratory symmetry-breaking calculations performed for an extension of the Skyrme EDF that includes central terms with four gradients at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) and for which the high-quality parametrization SN2LO1 has been constructed recently [P. Becker et al, Phys. Rev. C 96, 044330 (2017)]. Up to now, the investigation of such functionals with higher-order terms was limited to infinite matter and spherically symmetric configurations of singly- and doubly-magic nuclei. We address here nuclei and phenomena that require us to consider axial and non-axial deformation, both for reflection-symmetric and also reflection-asymmetric shapes, as well as the breaking of time-reversal invariance. Achieving these calculations demanded a number of formal developments. These all resulted from the formulation of the N2LO EDF requiring the introduction of new local densities with additional gradients that are not present in the EDF at NLO. Their choice is not unique, but can differ in the way the gradients are coupled. While designing a numerical implementation of N2LO EDFs in Cartesian 3d coordinate-space representation, we have developed a novel definition and a new unifying notation for normal and pair densities that contain gradients at arbitrary order. The resulting scheme resolves several issues with some of the choices that have been made for local densities in the past, in particular when breaking time-reversal symmetry. Guided by general practical considerations, we propose an alternative form of the N2LO contribution to the Skyrme EDF that is built from a different set of densities.

## Key claim

The SN2LO1 parametrization of the N2LO Skyrme EDF does not yet present a systematic improvement over standard NLO parametrizations for the deformed and rotating nuclei studied here, calling for improved fit protocols that better exploit the additional degrees of freedom offered by the higher-order terms.

## Method

The authors implemented the N2LO Skyrme EDF (including central terms with four gradients) into a Cartesian 3D coordinate-space code that allows for axial and non-axial deformation, reflection-asymmetric shapes, and time-reversal breaking. They developed a novel unifying notation and definition for local normal and pair densities containing gradients at arbitrary order, and proposed an alternative form of the N2LO contribution built from a different set of densities that reduces computational cost and memory requirements. Calculations were performed for ground states of even-even Kr and Nd isotopes, the fission barrier of 240Pu, and the superdeformed rotational band of 194Hg using the SN2LO1 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The SN2LO1 parametrization was adjusted with a protocol similar to that used for SLy5 and SLy5* parametrizations of the NLO EDF, with additional stability constraints imposed via linear-response calculations
- For masses and radii of spherical nuclei, the overall performance of SN2LO1 is comparable to SLy5* and SLy5 parametrizations
- The N2LO energy density includes four coupling constants C^(4)_t for each isoscalar (t=0) and isovector (t=1) channel
- The study examined the fission barrier of 240Pu and the superdeformed rotational band of 194Hg as representative examples

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of N2LO Skyrme EDFs that were limited to infinite matter and spherically symmetric configurations of singly- and doubly-magic nuclei (Becker 2017). It builds on the formulation of higher-order Skyrme EDFs by Carlsson (2008) and Raimondi (2011), and on the development of the SN2LO1 parametrization by Becker (2017). The results enable future fits that can better discriminate between NLO and N2LO terms, and provide a framework for implementing even higher-order (N3LO) extensions of the Skyrme EDF.
