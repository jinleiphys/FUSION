# The effect of triangular flow on di-hadron azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Using the AMPT model for relativistic heavy ion collisions, we have studied the di-hadron azimuthal angular correlations triggered by emitted jets in Au+Au collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and impact parameter $b=8$ fm. A double-peak structure for the associated particles at the away side of trigger particles is obtained after subtracting background correlations due to the elliptic flow. Both the near-side peak and the away-side double peaks in the azimuthal angular correlations are, however, significantly suppressed (enhanced) in events of small (large) triangular flow, which are present as a result of fluctuations in the initial collision geometry. After subtraction of background correlations due to the triangular flow, the away-side double peaks change into a single peak with broad shoulders on both sides. The away side of the di-hadron correlations becomes essentially a single peak after further subtraction of higher-order flows.

## Key claim

The away-side double-peak structure in di-hadron azimuthal correlations, previously attributed to jet-medium interactions, is dominated by triangular flow from initial geometry fluctuations in mid-central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=8 fm, but this effect is weaker in central collisions where other mechanisms (such as jet deflection or Mach cone shock waves) remain appreciable.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and impact parameter b=8 fm, generating events with initial parton distributions from HIJING, evolving them via the ZPC parton cascade with a 10 mb elastic cross section, and converting partons to hadrons via spatial coalescence followed by hadronic scattering in the ART model. They calculated di-hadron azimuthal correlations between trigger particles (2.5-6 GeV/c) and associated particles (0.15-2.5 GeV/c) in mid-pseudorapidity, then subtracted background correlations from elliptic flow (v2), triangular flow (v3), and higher-order flows up to n=5 using event-averaged flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV; impact parameter: b=8 fm
- Trigger particle transverse momentum window: 2.5-6 GeV/c; associated particle window: 0.15-2.5 GeV/c
- Elastic parton scattering cross section: 10 mb
- Events classified as small triangular flow: v3 less than 0.03; large triangular flow: v3 greater than 0.05
- Background correlations calculated up to n=5 were already very close to those from hadron pairs

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic studies of Alver and Roland (2010) and Alver et al. (2010) who suggested triangular flow could produce the away-side double-peak structure, and directly addresses the STAR Collaboration result (Agarwal et al., 2010) showing the double-peak persists after subtracting estimated triangular flow backgrounds. The paper enables a consistent dynamical treatment of flow backgrounds in jet correlation studies, showing that in mid-central collisions triangular flow dominates the away-side structure while in central collisions (b=0 fm) other mechanisms from references such as Vitev (2005), Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2006), and Betz et al. (2009, 2010) remain important.
