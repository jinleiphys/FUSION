# Nuclear Shadowing in a Parton Recombination Model: Q$^2$ Variation

> Q$^2$ variation of the nuclear-structure-function ratio $F_2^A(x,Q^2)/F_2^D(x,Q^2)$ is investigated in a parton model with Q$^2$-rescaling and parton-recombination effects. Calculated results are compared with the NMC (New Muon Collaboration) and the Fermilab-E665 experimental data. We find that our theoretical results show small Q$^2$ variations and that they are consistent with the data within present experimental accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Q² variation of the nuclear structure function ratio F₂^A(x,Q²)/F₂^D(x,Q²) calculated in a parton model combining Q²-rescaling and parton-recombination effects is small and consistent with existing NMC and Fermilab-E665 data, but the author notes that perturbative QCD would not be applicable in the small-Q² region and that the calculation should be considered a naive estimate.

## Method

The author calculates nuclear parton distributions at a starting scale Q₀² = 0.8 GeV² using Q² rescaling for valence quarks and modifying sea-quark and gluon distributions to satisfy momentum conservation, then applies parton-recombination effects. These distributions are evolved to higher Q² using the ordinary Altarelli-Parisi equation, without including modifications to the Q² evolution due to parton recombinations. The resulting structure function ratios are compared with NMC data for calcium and carbon nuclei and with Fermilab-E665 data for xenon.

## Key numbers

- Starting Q² scale: Q₀² = 0.8 GeV²
- Rescaling parameters: ξ_C^V = 1.60 for carbon, ξ_Ca^V = 1.86 for calcium
- Cutoff for leak-out partons: z₀ = 2.0 fm
- Results shown at x values: 0.0085, 0.035 (shadowing region), 0.125 (anti-shadowing), and 0.55 (EMC effect region) for calcium
- For xenon, theoretical curves calculated at x = 0.01 and 0.025, compared with E665 data in the x range 0.001 < x < 0.025
- The ratio F₂^A(x,Q²)/F₂^D(x,Q²) slightly increases with Q² at small x and is almost constant at medium x

## Context

This work extends the author's previous model (Kumano, 1994) that combined Q² rescaling (used for the EMC effect in the medium-x region) with parton recombination (used for shadowing in the small-x region) by providing a detailed comparison with Q²-variation data from NMC and Fermilab-E665. The model is based on parton-level physics rather than macroscopic nuclear physics approaches such as nuclear binding, Fermi motion, or vector-meson dominance. The author notes that the full Q² evolution including parton-recombination modifications (as in Qiu, 1990) and next-to-leading-order effects (Kumano, Kudo, and Konuma, 1994) remain as future research topics, and that accurate experimental data are needed for testing theoretical details.
