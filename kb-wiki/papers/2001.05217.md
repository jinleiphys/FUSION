# Dirac Eigenvalue spectrum of $N_f$=2+1 QCD towards the chiral limit using HISQ fermions

> We utilize the eigenvalue filtering technique combined with the stochastic estimate of the mode number to determine the eigenvalue spectrum. Simulations of (2 + 1)-flavor QCD are performed using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ/tree) action on $N_τ$ = 8 lattices with aspect ratios $N_σ/N_τ$ ranging from 5 to 7. The strange quark mass is fixed to its physical value $m_{s}^{\rm phy}$, and the light quark masses $m_{l}$ are varied from $m_{s}^{\rm phy}/40$ to $m_{s}^{\rm phy}/160$ which correspond to pion mass $m_π$ ranging from 110 MeV to 55 MeV in the continuum limit. We compute the chiral condensate and $χ_π - χ_δ$ through the eigenvalue spectrum obtained from the the eigenvalue filtering method. We compare these results with those obtained from a direct calculation of the observables which involves inversions of the fermion matrix using the stochastic "noise vector" method. We find that these approaches yield consistent results. Furthermore, we also investigate the quark mass and temperature dependences of the Dirac eigenvalue density at zero eigenvalues to gain more insights about the $U_A(1)$ symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The eigenvalue filtering technique combined with stochastic estimation of the mode number can reliably reproduce the chiral condensate and the U_A(1) susceptibility from the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum, yielding results consistent with direct fermion matrix inversion methods. The study finds that the eigenvalue density at zero eigenvalues appears to vanish near 162 MeV towards the chiral limit, suggesting SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral symmetry restoration, while a linear quark mass dependence of ρ(0,m) at that temperature may indicate persistent U_A(1) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors used the Chebyshev filtering technique with polynomial orders up to 16000-24000 combined with Gaussian random noise vectors to estimate the mode number of the staggered Dirac operator on (2+1)-flavor HISQ/tree gauge configurations. They computed the eigenvalue spectrum from the mode number using binning, then extracted the chiral condensate and χ_π − χ_δ via spectral integrals and compared these with results from direct stochastic noise vector inversions of the fermion matrix. The eigenvalue density at zero was obtained by fitting the low-lying spectrum to a cubic polynomial in λ.

## Key numbers

- Light quark masses varied from m_s^phy/40 to m_s^phy/160, corresponding to pion masses from 110 MeV to 55 MeV in the continuum limit.
- Simulations performed on N_τ = 8 lattices with aspect ratios N_σ/N_τ from 5 to 7.
- Chebyshev polynomial order p=24000 costs 3 minutes per configuration with one noise vector on 56^3×8 lattices at m_π = 55 MeV; calculating 192 low-lying eigenvalues with the Kalkreuter-Simma algorithm costs about 3.5 hours on the same configuration.
- The eigenvalue spectrum at zero ρ(0,m) appears to vanish at approximately 162.2 MeV towards the chiral limit.
- No clear evidence of a gap around λ=0 was observed up to the highest temperature studied, 166 MeV, at the smallest quark mass m_s^phy/160.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of U_A(1) symmetry breaking using N_f=2+1 flavors by Buchoff et al. (2013), Dick et al. (2015), and Bazavov et al. (2019), as well as N_f=2 studies by Brandt et al. (2016) and Suzuki et al. (2017), which generally concluded that U_A(1) remains broken at the pseudocritical temperature. The eigenvalue filtering method provides a computationally cheaper alternative to the Kalkreuter-Simma Ritz algorithm for obtaining the full eigenvalue spectrum, enabling simulations at lighter quark masses and larger volumes. The results enable further investigation of the microscopic mechanism of U_A(1) breaking and the order of the chiral phase transition in QCD.
