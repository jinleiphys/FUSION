# Resumming double logarithms in the QCD evolution of color dipoles

> The higher-order perturbative corrections, beyond leading logarithmic accuracy, to the BFKL evolution in QCD at high energy are well known to suffer from a severe lack-of-convergence problem, due to radiative corrections enhanced by double collinear logarithms. Via an explicit calculation of Feynman graphs in light cone (time-ordered) perturbation theory, we show that the corrections enhanced by double logarithms (either energy-collinear, or double collinear) are associated with soft gluon emissions which are strictly ordered in lifetime. These corrections can be resummed to all orders by solving an evolution equation which is non-local in rapidity. This equation can be equivalently rewritten in local form, but with modified kernel and initial conditions, which resum double collinear logs to all orders. We extend this resummation to the next-to-leading order BFKL and BK equations. The first numerical studies of the collinearly-improved BK equation demonstrate the essential role of the resummation in both stabilizing and slowing down the evolution.

## Key claim

The double-logarithmic corrections (energy-collinear or double collinear) that plague the perturbative expansion of the BFKL and BK equations beyond leading-logarithmic accuracy can be resummed to all orders by enforcing a strict ordering in lifetime for successive soft gluon emissions, which reduces the longitudinal phase-space from Y to Y-ρ.

## Method

The authors performed an explicit calculation of Feynman graphs in light-cone (time-ordered) perturbation theory for two successive gluon emissions from a quark-antiquark dipole, identifying which time-orderings contribute to double-logarithmic accuracy. They showed that the net double-logarithmic contributions come only from graphs with the same topology as leading-logarithmic-approximation graphs but with the additional constraint that successive gluon emissions are strictly ordered in lifetime (τ_p > τ_k). This constraint was then used to derive a non-local evolution equation in rapidity, which was subsequently reformulated into a local form with a modified kernel and initial conditions that resum double collinear logarithms to all orders, and extended to the next-to-leading order BFKL and BK equations.

## Key numbers

- The saturation exponent extracted from numerical solutions of the collinearly-improved BK equation is smaller by roughly a factor of two than in the absence of the resummation.
- The double-logarithmic approximation resums terms of the type (ᾱ Y ρ)^n to all orders, where ᾱ ≡ α_s N_c/π is the fixed and small QCD coupling, Y ≡ ln(s/Q_0^2) is the rapidity, and ρ ≡ ln(1/(r^2 Q_0^2)) measures the logarithmic separation in transverse scales.
- The phase-space reduction from the lifetime-ordering constraint produces a contribution of order ᾱ ρ^2 from the hard gluon integration, matching the double transverse logarithm piece of the full NLO result from Balitsky (2008).

## Context

This work extends the collinear resummation schemes originally developed for the NLO BFKL equation by Salam (1998), Ciafaloni (1999, 2003), and Vera (2005) to the BK equation in transverse coordinate space, enabling compatibility with the non-linear structure of the BK equation. The resummation addresses the severe lack-of-convergence problem in the NLO BFKL and BK equations, which was shown by Lappi (2015) to cause unstable numerical solutions (amplitudes decreasing with energy or turning negative) for physically interesting initial conditions. The results enable stable numerical studies of the BK equation with resummed double logarithms and provide a foundation for adding further NLO corrections including single transverse logarithms that may require additional resummations.
