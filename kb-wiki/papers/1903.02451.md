# A statistical analysis of the nuclear structure uncertainties in $μ$D

> The charge radius of the deuteron (D), was recently determined to three times the precision compared with previous measurements using the measured Lamb shift in muonic deuterium (muD). However, the muD value is 5.6 $σ$ smaller than the world averaged CODATA-2014 value [1]. To shed light on this discrepancy we analyze the uncertainties of the nuclear structure calculations of the Lamb shift in muD and conclude that nuclear theory uncertainty is not likely to be the source of the discrepancy.

## Key claim

The nuclear theory uncertainty in the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium is not likely to be the source of the 5.6 sigma discrepancy between the muonic deuterium charge radius and the CODATA-2014 value, because the dominant uncertainties come from higher-order (Zα) corrections and single-nucleon physics rather than from nuclear model dependence.

## Method

The authors quantified the total theoretical uncertainty of the TPE contribution (δ_TPE) by identifying and estimating eight separate sources of uncertainty: statistical uncertainties from low-energy constants (LECs) in nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, uncertainties from the maximum lab energy used in fitting NN potentials, chiral truncation uncertainties (evaluated using Bayesian posteriors), cut-off variations in NN potentials, η-expansion truncation uncertainties, systematic approximations in electromagnetic operators, single-nucleon physics uncertainties, and higher-order (Zα) corrections. The calculations used chiral effective field theory NN potentials at orders from LO to N⁴LO, and the systematic nuclear physics uncertainty (σ_syst) was combined from the chiral truncation, electromagnetic operator, and maximum lab energy uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The final value for the TPE correction is δ_TPE = -1.715 meV, with a total uncertainty σ_Total ranging from +0.022/-0.023 meV to +0.028/-0.029 meV depending on the single-nucleon uncertainty source used.
- The experimentally determined value from Pohl (2016) is δ_TPE = -1.7638(68) meV, which differs from the theoretical value by less than 2 sigma.
- The η-expansion truncation uncertainty σ_η is 0.3% (0.005 meV).
- The statistical uncertainty σ_stat is 0.06% (0.001 meV).
- The systematic nuclear physics uncertainty σ_syst is +0.5%/-0.6% (+0.008/-0.011 meV).
- The single-nucleon uncertainty σ_N is 0.6% (0.0102 meV) from Krauth (2016) or 1.2% (0.0198 meV) from Hill (2017).
- The higher-order (Zα) uncertainty σ_Zα is 1.0% (0.0172 meV).
- The muonic deuterium charge radius value is 5.6 sigma smaller than the CODATA-2014 world averaged value.

## Context

This work extends the uncertainty quantification framework from Hernandez (2018) and Ji (2018) by updating the chiral truncation uncertainty using explicit 68% confidence intervals from Bayesian posteriors, which slightly increased the lower bound of the total uncertainty from -0.024 meV to -0.023 meV. The analysis contradicts the possibility that nuclear structure uncertainties from NN-force models explain the proton radius puzzle in deuterium, and instead points to higher-order (Zα) corrections and single-nucleon physics (Hill 2017) as the dominant uncertainty sources that need further investigation.
