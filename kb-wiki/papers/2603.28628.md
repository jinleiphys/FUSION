# From $α$ decay to cluster decay: an extreme case of transfer learning

> When training data are limited, data-driven models are especially vulnerable to optimization-related fluctuations from random initialization and to sampling-induced bias from insufficient training data. We address both challenges with transfer learning (TL): deep neural networks (DNNs) are first pretrained on $α$ decay half-lives and then fine-tuned on a small cluster decay dataset. The pretraining stage provides a physically informed initialization that stabilizes optimization, while transferred global decay systematics regularize the fit and reduce sensitivity to training set composition. Despite extreme data sparsity, the resulting models accurately predict cluster decay half-lives for parent nuclei from $^{221}$Fr to $^{242}$Cm. We further quantify how initialization and sample selection affect predictive accuracy and robustness, demonstrating that TL enables stable and reliable learning in the small-sample regime.

# From α decay to cluster decay: an extreme case of transfer learning

## Key claim
Transfer learning from α decay to cluster decay enables accurate prediction of cluster decay half-lives despite extreme data sparsity, but only when the pretrained network provides a physically informed initialization that constrains optimization; direct application of an α-only network to cluster decay fails catastrophically, and training on combined α+cluster data without transfer learning also performs poorly due to data imbalance.

## Method
A deep neural network with five input features (Z, A, Z_c, A_c, Q) was first pretrained on 591 ground-state α decay half-lives using the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm with 10-fold cross-validation. The pretrained parameters were then used to initialize fine-tuning on a small cluster decay dataset of 27 experimentally confirmed data points, using either full fine-tuning (all layers re-optimized) or shallow fine-tuning (only the last two layers re-optimized). The final architectures were a single hidden layer of six neurons for full fine-tuning and two hidden layers of six neurons each for shallow fine-tuning, selected by jointly considering pretraining and transfer performance.

## Key numbers
- 591 ground-state α decay half-lives were used for pretraining, spanning 105 ≤ A ≤ 294 and 52 ≤ Z ≤ 118
- 27 experimentally confirmed cluster decay data points from parent nuclei ²²¹Fr to ²⁴²Cm were used for fine-tuning
- Full fine-tuning reached UDL-level accuracy with only 4 training samples
- Shallow fine-tuning required 7 training samples to reach UDL-level accuracy
- The fully fine-tuned model achieved σ_rms = 1.089 for cluster decay predictions across 50 random initializations
- Cluster decay branching ratios relative to α decay range from 10⁻⁹ to 10⁻¹⁶

## Context
This work extends the universal decay law (UDL) framework of Qi et al. (2009), which unified α decay and cluster decay within an R-matrix approach, and the unified empirical formula of Ni et al. (2008) derived from the WKB approximation. It builds on prior ML applications to α decay half-life prediction by Saxena et al. (2021), Pang et al. (2022), and others, and on transfer learning applications in nuclear physics including neutrino-interaction classification by Chappell et al. (2022) and electron-nucleus cross-section prediction by Graczyk et al. (2025). The results enable prediction of cluster decay half-lives for superheavy elements, where experimental detection is complicated by competing spontaneous fission, and provide a proof of concept for applying transfer learning to other data-limited problems such as neutron capture along the r-process path, double β decay, and fission properties far from stability.
