# Non-perturbative QCD Modeling and Meson Physics

> Using a ladder-rainbow kernel previously established for light quark hadron physics, we explore the extension to masses and electroweak decay constants of ground state pseudoscalar and vector quarkonia and heavy-light mesons in the c- and b-quark regions. We make a systematic study of the effectiveness of a constituent mass concept as a replacement for a heavy quark dressed propagator for such states. The difference between vector and axial vector current correlators is explored within the same model to provide an estimate of the four quark chiral condensate and the leading distance scale for the onset of non-perturbative phenomena in QCD.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow kernel previously fitted to light quark physics can be extended to heavy-light and quarkonia mesons, but a constituent mass approximation for heavy quarks reproduces meson masses while underestimating electroweak decay constants by 30-70%; fully dressed heavy quark propagators are necessary for accurate decay constants, yet for heavy-light states the full kernel fails to produce physical bound states, suggesting the infrared strength of the kernel is too strong for heavy quarks.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for dressed quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using the rainbow-ladder truncation with the Maris-Tandy effective coupling. They compute masses and electroweak decay constants for ground state pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the charm and bottom sectors, comparing results from a constituent mass approximation for heavy quarks against fully dressed propagators. They also calculate the difference between vector and axial vector current correlators to extract the four-quark condensate and the leading non-perturbative distance scale.

## Key numbers

- Constituent masses obtained from fits to the lightest pseudoscalar: \(M_c^{\rm cons} = 2.0\) GeV for the c-quark, \(M_b^{\rm cons} = 5.3\) GeV for the b-quark
- Heavy-light decay constants from constituent mass approximation are 30-50% below experimental values (e.g., calculated \(f_D = 0.154\) GeV vs. experimental 0.222 GeV; calculated \(f_B = 0.105\) GeV vs. experimental 0.176 GeV)
- For quarkonia with fully dressed propagators, decay constants are well reproduced: \(f_{\eta_c} = 0.387\) GeV (expt. 0.340 GeV), \(f_{J/\psi} = 0.415\) GeV (expt. 0.411 GeV), \(f_{\eta_b} = 0.692\) GeV, \(f_\Upsilon = 0.682\) GeV (expt. 0.708 GeV)
- The four-quark condensate extracted at renormalization scale \(\mu = 19\) GeV is 65% greater than the vacuum saturation assumption \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle^2\)
- The first Weinberg sum rule yields \(f_\pi = 0.0728\) GeV (compared to the input \(f_\pi = 0.0924\) GeV convention)
- The Das-Guralnik-Mathur-Low-Young sum rule gives \(m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0} = 4.86\) MeV (experimental value \(4.43 \pm 0.03\) MeV)
- The chirality-flip ratio identifies a scale of \(\sim 0.5\) fm for the onset of non-perturbative dynamics

## Context

This work extends the Maris-Tandy ladder-rainbow kernel (Maris 1997, Maris 1999), previously successful for light quarks, into the heavy quark domain. It systematically tests the constituent mass approximation against fully dressed propagators for charm and bottom quarks, finding that while masses are reproduced, decay constants require full dressing. The results partially confirm the suggestion by Brodsky and Shrock (2008) of a universal maximum wavelength for quarks and gluons in hadrons. The vector-axial vector correlator analysis provides an estimate of the four-quark condensate and a non-perturbative distance scale consistent with lattice QCD and the Instanton Liquid Model (Faccioli 2003). The work enables further refinement of DSE kernels for heavy quarks and improved understanding of non-perturbative QCD scales.
