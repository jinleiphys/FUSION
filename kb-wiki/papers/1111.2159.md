# Elliptic flow of thermal dileptons as a probe of QCD matter

> We study the variation of elliptic flow of thermal dileptons with transverse momentum and invariant mass of the pairs for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The dilepton productions from quark gluon plasma (QGP) and hot hadrons have been considered including the spectral change of light vector mesons in the thermal bath. The space time evolution has been carried out within the frame work of 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with lattice+hadron resonance gas equation of state. We find that a judicious selection of invariant mass(M) and transverse momentum (p_T) windows can be used to extract the collective properties of quark matter, hadronic matter and also get a distinct signature of medium effects on vector mesons. Our results indicate a reduction of elliptic flow (v_2) for M beyond phi mass, which if observed experimentally would give the measure of v_2 of the partonic phase.

## Key claim

A judicious selection of invariant mass (M) and transverse momentum (p_T) windows for thermal dileptons can extract the collective properties of quark matter and hadronic matter separately, and a reduction of elliptic flow (v_2) for M beyond the phi mass, if observed experimentally, would indicate the small momentum anisotropy in the early partonic phase.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice QCD + hadron resonance gas equation of state to simulate the space-time evolution of Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for 30-40% centrality. They calculated thermal dilepton production from both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hot hadronic matter, including medium modifications to the rho meson spectral function, and evaluated the elliptic flow v_2 as a function of p_T and invariant mass M.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_i = 456 MeV at thermalization time tau_i = 0.6 fm/c for 30-40% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Transition temperature T_c = 175 MeV for quark-hadron conversion
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T_F = 130 MeV
- QGP phase lifetime approximately 6 fm/c; hadronic matter phase duration approximately 6-12 fm/c
- For high invariant mass (langle M rangle = 2.5 GeV), v_2 is small for the entire p_T range because these pairs arise dominantly from the QGP epoch when flow is not fully developed
- For langle M rangle = 0.77 GeV (around rho mass), v_2 is large because pairs are emitted when flow is fully developed in the hadronic phase
- The magnitude of elliptic flow at LHC is significantly larger than values obtained at RHIC for all invariant mass windows

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton elliptic flow at RHIC energies by Rupa (2009) and Deng (2010) to LHC collision conditions, and builds on the framework of electromagnetic probes as penetrating signals from hot QCD matter as reviewed by Alam (2001) and Rapp (2009). The results enable experimental tests at LHC where the measured v_2 of dileptons at high invariant mass could directly probe the early-time collective motion of the partonic phase, and the mass dependence of v_2 can serve as a "chronometer" of the space-time evolution of heavy-ion collisions.
