# Time Evolution of Prompt Gamma-Ray Emission in $^{252}$Cf(sf) and $^{233,235}$U($n$,f) Reactions

> We investigate the time evolution of prompt fission $γ$ emission due to the presence of ns to ms isomers in the fragments produced in the neutron-induced fission of $^{233,235}$U and in the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf. Calculations performed with the CGMF fission event generator are compared with recent experimental data on $^{252}$Cf(sf) and $^{235}$U($n$,f) obtained with the DANCE+NEUANCE setup at the Los Alamos Neutron Science Center. Of particular interest are the average $γ$-ray energy spectrum as a function of time since scission, $ϕ(ε_γ,t)$, the increase of the average $γ$-ray multiplicity over time, $N_γ(t)$, and its evolution in time as a function of the fission fragment mass, $N_γ(A,t)$. The time evolution of isomeric ratios in post-neutron emission fission fragments can be defined and used to test and reveal some deficiencies in our knowledge of the low-lying levels of neutron-rich nuclei produced in fission reactions.

## Key claim

The time evolution of prompt fission gamma-ray emission due to ns to ms isomers in fission fragments can be calculated with the CGMF fission event generator and compared to DANCE+NEUANCE experimental data, revealing that deficiencies in the knowledge of low-lying nuclear levels of neutron-rich fission fragments cause discrepancies between predicted and measured time-gated gamma-ray spectra, particularly for specific energy windows and fragment mass regions.

## Method

The CGMF Monte Carlo code, which implements the Hauser-Feshbach statistical formalism to follow fission fragment decay through neutron and gamma emission on an event-by-event basis, was used to calculate time-dependent gamma-ray spectra, multiplicities, and isomeric ratios for 252Cf spontaneous fission and 233,235U neutron-induced fission. The CGMF output was forward-propagated through a GEANT4 simulation of the DANCE+NEUANCE detector setup at LANSCE, and the simulated events were compared to experimental data. Different nuclear structure input files (RIPL-3 from 2015, RIPL-3 from 2020, and ENSDF from 2024) were used to study the sensitivity of the calculated time-gated gamma-ray spectra to the underlying discrete level data.

## Key numbers

- About 4% of total prompt fission gamma rays for 252Cf(sf) and more than 8% for 233,235U(nth,f) are predicted to be emitted past the 10 ns mark, which is commonly used as a time coincidence window in fission experiments.
- A strong peak near 0.55 MeV in the time-gated spectrum (50 ns to 2 microseconds) for 252Cf(sf) was traced to 139Xe, where spurious half-lives of 10 ns each were present in the RIPL-3 discrete level file.
- The 19/2- isomer in 133Te at 1.61 MeV excitation energy has a reported half-life of 100 ns and decays via a 1.151 MeV photon.
- The 6+ isomer in 134Te at 1.69 MeV has a half-life of 164 ns, with the final gamma transition from the first 2+ state to the 0+ ground state having energy 1.279 MeV.
- Significant differences in average gamma multiplicity as a function of fragment mass appear in the [120:140] mass region and to a lesser extent [95:100] when comparing calculations with no time cutoff versus a 10 ns time cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the study of prompt fission gamma rays by Talou (2021) and Stetcu (2014) to include the time evolution of emission due to isomeric states, using the CGMF code that has been applied to multi-chance fission, fission product yields, and neutron-gamma correlations. The calculations are compared to experimental data from Rusev (2020, 2025) obtained with the DANCE+NEUANCE setup at LANSCE, and to earlier experimental results from John (1970). The findings enable improved interpretation of nuclear diagnostic subcritical experiments and inference of fission product yields from gamma coincidence measurements, while also revealing specific deficiencies in the ENSDF and RIPL-3 databases for neutron-rich nuclei such as 139Xe, 133Te, and 134Te.
