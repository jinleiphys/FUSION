# A Matrix Model for Baryons and Nuclear Forces

> We propose a new matrix model describing multi-baryon systems. We derive the action from open string theory on the wrapped baryon vertex D-branes embedded in the D4-D8 model of large N holographic QCD. The positions of k baryons are unified into k x k matrices, with spin/isospin of the baryons encoded in a set of k-vectors. Holographic baryons are known to be very small in the large 't Hooft coupling limit, and our model offers a better systematic approach to dynamics of such baryons at short distances. We compute energetics and spectra (k=1), and also short-distance nuclear force (k=2). In particular, we obtain a new size of the holographic baryon and find a precise form of the repulsive core of nucleons. This matrix model complements the instanton soliton picture of holographic baryons, whose small size turned out to be well below the natural length scale of the approximation involved there. Our results show that, nevertheless, the basic properties of holographic baryons obtained there are robust under stringy corrections within a few percents.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new matrix model for multi-baryon systems derived from open string theory on wrapped baryon vertex D-branes in the D4-D8 holographic QCD model, claiming that the basic properties of holographic baryons obtained from the instanton soliton picture are robust under stringy corrections within a few percent, despite the soliton size being well below the natural length scale of that approximation.

## Method

The authors derive a U(k) matrix quantum mechanics action from the low-energy effective field theory on k D4'-branes (baryon vertex branes) wrapping an S^4 in the Witten background geometry, including the effects of the background Ramond-Ramond flux and the presence of N_f flavor D8-branes. The action is a deformed ADHM matrix model containing a Chern-Simons term, mass deformations for the X^4 and w fields, and a commutator term from dimensional reduction of the Yang-Mills kinetic term. For k=1 and k=2, the authors compute static energy configurations by integrating out auxiliary fields and minimizing the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* The holographic baryon size from the matrix model is larger by a factor of (5/4)^(1/4) compared to the soliton result of approximately 9.6/(M_KK sqrt(lambda)), where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling and M_KK is the vector meson mass scale.
* The overall normalization of the matrix model action is lambda N_c M_KK / (27 pi) for the quadratic part from the DBI action.
* The mass term for the X^4 field is (1/3) M_KK^2 (X^4)^2 in the quadratic Lagrangian.
* The mass term for the w field is (1/6) M_KK^2 times the w kinetic term normalization.
* The Chern-Simons term gives N_c times the integral of tr(A_0) dt.

## Context

This work extends the holographic baryon program in the Sakai-Sugimoto D4-D8 model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2004), where baryons were previously described as instanton solitons on flavor D8-branes (Hong et al., 2007; Hata et al., 2007). The matrix model complements that soliton picture by providing a systematic approach at short distances below the string scale, where the soliton approximation becomes questionable. This enables future study of many-nucleon systems including nuclei and neutron star matter, and provides a framework for computing arbitrary k-body interactions from holographic QCD.
