# Higher twist jet broadening and classical propagation

> The transverse broadening of jets produced in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus is studied in the collinear limit. A class of medium enhanced higher twist corrections are re-summed to calculate the transverse momentum distribution of the produced collinear jet. In contrast to previous approaches, resummation of the leading length enhanced higher twist corrections is shown to lead to a two dimensional diffusion equation for the transverse momentum of the propagating jet. Results for the average transverse momentum obtained from this approach are then compared to the broadening expected from a classical Langevin analysis for the propagation of the jet under the action of the fluctuating color Lorentz force inside the nucleons. The set of approximations that lead to identical results from the two approaches are outlined. The relationship between the momentum diffusion constant $D$ and the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ is explicitly derived.

## Key claim

The paper shows that resumming the leading length-enhanced higher twist corrections to the transverse momentum distribution of a jet produced in deep-inelastic scattering off a large nucleus leads to a two-dimensional diffusion equation, and that this result is equivalent to a classical Langevin analysis of a colored charge moving under a fluctuating color Lorentz force, provided certain approximations are made.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic tensor for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off a large nucleus in the Breit frame, expanding order by order in higher twist corrections that are enhanced by the nuclear length. They isolate the class of diagrams with multiple gluon field insertions on the hard parton line, apply the collinear approximation, and resum the leading length-enhanced contributions to obtain a diffusion equation for the transverse momentum distribution of the final jet. They then compare this result to a classical Langevin analysis of a colored parton propagating through fluctuating color fields.

## Key numbers

* The transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) is related to the momentum diffusion constant \(D\) by the explicit relation derived in the paper (no numerical value given).
* The average transverse momentum \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle\) from the diffusion equation is compared to the broadening from the Langevin analysis, with the result that the two approaches yield identical results under the outlined approximations.
* The Bjorken variable is defined as \(x_B = Q^2 / 2p^+ q^-\), where \(Q^2\) is the virtuality of the exchanged photon and \(p^+\) is the nucleon light-cone momentum.
* The leading twist contribution to the hadronic tensor involves the quark structure function \(f_q(x_B)\) integrated over the light-cone separation \(\int dy^- / (2\pi) e^{-i x_B p^+ y^-}\).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist approach to jet broadening in dense matter (HT scheme, Ref. HT) by deriving a diffusion equation for transverse momentum, which had not been obtained in previous resummations such as those of Fries (2002). It establishes a physical connection between the higher-twist formalism and the finite-temperature field theory approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY), which admits a kinetic theory description. The results enable future work on including radiative energy loss within the same diffusion framework, and the explicit relation between \(\hat{q}\) and the diffusion constant \(D\) provides a bridge between phenomenological transport coefficients and classical stochastic dynamics.
