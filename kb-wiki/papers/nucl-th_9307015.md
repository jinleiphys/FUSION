# The Skyrme model description of the spin-orbit force

> We examine the derivation of the spin-orbit force from the Skyrme model. We find substantial agreement with phenomenological potentials. We show that a systematic and simultaneous treatment of all components of the kinetic energy introduces a minus sign relative to previous calculations for the iso-spin independent part of the interaction. We also explain the smallness of the iso-spin dependent part of the spin-orbit potential.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model yields the correct sign and order of magnitude for the nucleon-nucleon spin-orbit interaction, contrary to previous calculations that obtained the wrong sign, provided one properly inverts the mass matrix to convert velocities to canonical momenta rather than using a naive replacement.

## Method

The authors used the product Ansatz for two B=1 hedgehog Skyrmions to construct the B=2 system, then made all collective parameters (separation R, iso-rotations A and B) time-dependent to derive an effective Lagrangian. They expanded the kinetic energy in tensors of the O(4) group, inverted the mass matrix to obtain the Hamiltonian, and extracted the spin-orbit coupling terms from the off-diagonal components coupling the relative velocity to the sum of the rotational velocities of the two Skyrmions.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: fπ = 6.45 MeV; dimensionless parameter g = 4.48; pion mass mπ = 138 MeV (these three parameters determine all results)
- At large separation R, d1^v → M/4 (where M is the single Skyrmion mass), d1^ω → 2Λ (where Λ is the moment of inertia), and u3^{vω} → 0
- The isospin-independent spin-orbit potential agrees with the BonnR phenomenological potential beyond 1 fm separation
- The isospin-dependent spin-orbit potential is smaller than the isospin-independent part and positive at large R, oscillating at smaller R

## Context

This work extends prior Skyrme model calculations of the NN interaction by Riska and Danbom (1990) and Otofuji et al. (1992), which obtained the correct magnitude but wrong sign for the spin-orbit force. The authors show that a systematic treatment of the kinetic energy inversion reverses that sign, yielding agreement with phenomenology without needing the sixth-order term added by Riska and Schwesinger (1991). This completes the first steps in deriving the NN interaction from non-perturbative QCD, following earlier successful descriptions of central attraction, tensor force, and short-range repulsion in the Skyrme model.
