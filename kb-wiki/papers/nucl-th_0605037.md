# Forward Production With Large p/πRatio and Without Jet Structure at Any p_T

> Particle production in the forward region of heavy-ion collisions is shown to be due to parton recombination without shower partons. The regeneration of soft partons due to momentum degradation through the nuclear medium is considered. The degree of degradation is determined by fitting the $\bar p/p$ ratio. The data at $\sqrt s=62.4$ GeV and $η=3.2$ from BRAHMS on the $p_T$ distribution of average charged particles are well reproduced. Large proton-to-pion ratio is predicted. The particles produced at any $p_T$ should have no associated particles above background to manifest any jet structure.

# Forward Production With Large p/π Ratio and Without Jet Structure at Any p_T

## Key claim

Particles produced in the forward region (x > 0.3) of heavy-ion collisions at √s = 62.4 GeV arise from parton recombination of thermal partons only, without any contribution from shower partons or hard scattering. The authors predict that these particles at any p_T should have no associated jet structure, and that the proton-to-pion ratio exceeds 1 for p_T > 1 GeV/c, reaching values greater than 2 above p_T = 2.5 GeV/c, but these predictions depend on the degradation parameter κ being determined by fitting the preliminary antiproton-to-proton ratio.

## Method

The authors extend the recombination model to include regeneration of soft partons from momentum degradation of valons traversing nuclear matter, parametrized by a degradation parameter κ. They calculate the x-distributions of protons and pions using the recombination model with factorizable parton distributions from wounded nucleons, then assume factorizability between longitudinal and transverse momentum to obtain p_T distributions via thermal-parton recombination (TT for pions, TTT for protons). The value of κ is determined by fitting the calculated antiproton-to-proton ratio to preliminary BRAHMS data at √s = 62.4 GeV and η = 3.2, and the resulting p_T distribution of average charged particles is compared to BRAHMS data.

## Key numbers

- Degradation parameter κ = 0.76 gives antiproton-to-proton ratio R_ṗ/p = 0.031; κ = 0.72 gives R_ṗ/p = 0.058, bracketing the observed value of approximately 0.05 at √s = 62.4 GeV and η = 3.2
- The inverse slope parameter T = 196 MeV describes the p_T dependence of the exponential spectra
- For κ = 0.76, the average p_T is 1.09 GeV/c; for κ = 0.72, the average p_T is 1.07 GeV/c
- At x = 0.6, the pion distribution increases by roughly a factor of 3 due to soft-parton regeneration
- Without regeneration, the antiproton-to-proton ratio would be at the level of 2.5 × 10⁻⁵ for κ = 0.76
- The proton-to-pion ratio is predicted to exceed 1 for p_T > 1 GeV/c and become greater than 2 above p_T = 2.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier recombination model treatment of the fragmentation and transfragmentation regions (Hwa and Yang, 2005) by adding soft-parton regeneration from momentum degradation and including transverse momentum distributions. It contrasts with fragmentation-based approaches, which would predict p/π ≪ 1 due to fragmentation functions, and with models involving hard scattering that would produce power-law p_T behavior and jet structure. The results are compared to BRAHMS data at √s = 62.4 GeV (IA, 2005) and preliminary data on K/π and ṗ/p ratios (IA2, 2005; Hoy, 2005). The work enables experimental tests of whether forward-region particles at any p_T lack associated jet structure, and whether the p/π ratio exceeds unity, a definitive signature of recombination over fragmentation.
