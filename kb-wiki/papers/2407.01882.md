# A sign of three-nucleon short-range correlation from an analysis of nuclear mass and short-range correlation probability

> Three-nucleon short-range correlation ($3N$ SRC) represents a rare and intriguing part of the nuclear dynamics at short distance, beyond the two-nucleon short-range correlation ($2N$ SRC). To search its existence is a hot topic in the ongoing and future high-energy nuclear experiments and the developments of nuclear theory. In this study, we found a positive sign of $3N$ SRC in nuclei, by analyzing the correlation between the per-nucleon nuclear mass and the probability of a nucleon in $2N$ SRC state, with the current experimental measurements of $^2$H, $^3$He, $^4$He, $^9$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{56}$Fe, Cu, $^{197}$Au and $^{208}$Pb from SLAC, CLAS, and JLab Hall C collaborations. The effective masses of the nucleons in $2N$ SRC and $3N$ SRC are also extracted from the analysis, which provide some references for the nuclear medium effect study. The probability of $3N$ SRC is much smaller than that of $2N$ SRC, thus requiring high-luminosity experiments to confirm its existence.

## Key claim

By analyzing the correlation between per-nucleon nuclear mass and the probability of a nucleon being in a two-nucleon short-range correlation (2N SRC) state, the authors find a positive sign of three-nucleon short-range correlation (3N SRC) existence, but note that the analysis is model-dependent and the result should be treated as a preliminary study.

## Method

The authors decomposed nuclear mass per nucleon into contributions from mean-field nucleons, nucleons in 2N SRC, and nucleons in 3N SRC, assuming the 3N SRC probability scales as \(P_{3N} = \kappa P_{2N}^2\) based on a sequential cohesion process. They used experimental 2N SRC scaling factors \(a_2\) from SLAC, CLAS, and JLab Hall C collaborations for ten nuclei (²H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁶Fe, Cu, ¹⁹⁷Au, ²⁰⁸Pb) to derive absolute 2N SRC probabilities via two different estimates of the number of SRC pairs in deuteron, then performed linear and quadratic fits to the mass versus \(P_{2N}\) data.

## Key numbers

- For the fit using \(P_{2N}^{\rm I}\) data (from the authors' previous estimation of deuteron SRC pairs), the \(\chi^2/N\) was 58.37/10 with 3N SRC included versus 160.5/10 without 3N SRC; the sum of residual error squares was 1.90 with 3N SRC versus 2.35 without.
- For the fit using \(P_{2N}^{\rm II}\) data (from CLAS estimation of deuteron SRC pairs), the \(\chi^2/N\) was 58.39/10 with 3N SRC included versus 161.3/10 without 3N SRC.
- From the fit to \(P_{2N}^{\rm I}\) data with 3N SRC, the extracted mass difference for 2N SRC nucleons was \((m_{\rm \bar{N}}^{\rm 2N}-m_{\rm \bar{N}}) = -48.0 \pm 5.0\) MeV, and \(\kappa(m_{\rm \bar{N}}^{\rm 3N}-m_{\rm \bar{N}}) = -367 \pm 68\) MeV.
- The per-nucleon nuclear masses used ranged from 930.18 MeV (⁵⁶Fe) to 937.81 MeV (²H).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of short-range correlations by the same authors (Wang 2020, Ma 2023) that found 2N SRC alone insufficient to reproduce the nuclear EMC effect. It provides a new indirect method to search for 3N SRC, complementing direct experimental searches by CLAS and JLab Hall C collaborations that have yielded inconclusive results on the 3N SRC plateau. The analysis enables future high-luminosity experiments to confirm 3N SRC existence and provides reference values for the effective masses of nucleons in SRC states for nuclear medium effect studies.
