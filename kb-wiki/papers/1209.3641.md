# Octet baryon masses in next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We study the ground-state octet baryon masses and sigma terms using the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO). By adjusting the available 19 low-energy constants (LECs), a reasonable fit of the $n_f=2+1$ lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) results from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD and NPLQCD collaborations is achieved. Finite-volume corrections to the lattice data are calculated self-consistently. Our study shows that N$^3$LO BChPT describes better the light quark mass evolution of the lattice data than the NNLO BChPT does and the various lattice simulations seem to be consistent with each other. We also predict the pion and strangeness sigma terms of the octet baryons using the LECs determined in the fit of their masses. The predicted pion- and strangeness-nucleon sigma terms are $σ_{πN}=43(1)(6)$ MeV and $σ_{s N}=126(24)(54)$ MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme describes the light quark mass evolution of lattice QCD octet baryon masses better than next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) does, but only when finite-volume corrections are calculated self-consistently and all 19 low-energy constants are adjusted simultaneously.

## Method

The authors calculated the ground-state octet baryon self-energies up to N3LO using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme, including tree-level contributions from Lagrangians up to fourth chiral order and one-loop diagrams at third and fourth order. They performed a simultaneous fit of all 19 low-energy constants to publicly available nf=2+1 lattice QCD results from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD, and NPLQCD collaborations, with finite-volume corrections computed self-consistently within the same chiral framework. Virtual decuplet baryon contributions were not explicitly included because their effects could not be disentangled from octet contributions given the large number of unknown low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma_{pi N} = 43(1)(6) MeV, predicted from the low-energy constants determined in the fit of octet baryon masses
* Strangeness-nucleon sigma term: sigma_{s N} = 126(24)(54) MeV, predicted from the same fit
* 19 low-energy constants were adjusted to fit the lattice data: m0, b0, bD, bF, b1-8, and d1-5,7,8
* The fit used nf=2+1 lattice QCD results from five collaborations: PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD, and NPLQCD

## Context

This work extends previous studies of octet baryon masses using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, which had been performed up to NNLO by Ishikawa (2009) using the extended-on-mass-shell scheme, and by Young and Thomas (2010) using the finite range regulator method. It also builds on N3LO studies by Semke and Lutz (2012) using a partial summation scheme, but differs by including finite-volume effects self-consistently and by simultaneously fitting data from multiple lattice collaborations to test their consistency. The results enable improved chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD baryon masses to the physical point and provide predictions for baryon sigma terms that can be compared with future lattice calculations and experimental determinations.
