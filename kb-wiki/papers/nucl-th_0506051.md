# Nuclear halo structure from quasielastic charge-exchange reactions

> Neutron and proton densities in the nuclear periphery are investigated within (p,n) charge-exchange isobar transitions. For this purpose we have developed parameter-free optical potentials with a detailed treatment of the in-medium $t_τ$ part of the effective interaction. Non local coupled-channel Lane equations are solved to obtain the scattering observables. The use of conventional proton and neutron densities significantly underestimates Fermi (forward-angle) cross-sections in agreement with findings by various other groups. However, we have found model-independent densities which provide a remarkable improvement in the description of the quasielastic scattering data.The densities obtained are consistent with recent measurements at CERN in studies of the neutron-to-proton halo factor f(r)=Z$ρ_n/Nρ_p$ with antiprotons. These findings provide an alternative way to investigate the nuclear periphery, and may also help to solve the long-standing puzzle of the underestimated Fermi cross section in (p,n) charge-exchange phenomena.

# Nuclear halo structure from quasielastic charge-exchange reactions

## Key claim

Conventional proton and neutron densities significantly underestimate forward-angle (Fermi) cross-sections in (p,n) charge-exchange reactions, but model-independent densities fitted to the scattering data reveal a neutron halo structure with a peripheral halo factor consistent with antiproton measurements at CERN. The authors claim these findings may help solve the long-standing puzzle of the underestimated Fermi cross section, but note that additional constraints from the elastic channel and asymmetric nuclear matter effects may be required.

## Method

Parameter-free optical potentials were constructed using a full-folding approach with a detailed treatment of the in-medium isovector part of the effective interaction, based on the isospin-symmetric nuclear matter g-matrix treated fully off-shell. Non-local coupled-channel Lane equations were solved to obtain scattering observables for quasielastic (p,n) charge-exchange reactions to the isobaric analog state. Model-independent nuclear densities were parameterized as reference densities multiplied by a dressing function constructed from an N-knot spline, with both proton and neutron densities varied to fit measured cross sections while constraining proton densities to the measured charge r.m.s. radius and volume integrals to the correct N and Z.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca(p,n) at 160 MeV, a peripheral halo factor f(r)=Zρn/Nρp of 2.3 was obtained, in good agreement with values reported by Sch03.
- The nuclear skin (difference between neutron and proton r.m.s. radii) was found to be 0.52 fm, much larger than conventional reported values.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Bau01 and others who found that no microscopic effort had satisfactorily described (p,n) charge-exchange cross sections without phenomenological adjustments. The results are consistent with antiproton measurements at CERN reported by Sch03, and provide an alternative method to investigate nuclear periphery. The approach enables future work incorporating additional constraints from the elastic channel, closer scrutiny of isovector strength, and inclusion of asymmetric nuclear matter effective interactions.
