# Subleading Power Factorization with Radiative Functions

> The study of amplitudes and cross sections in the soft and collinear limits allows for an understanding of their all orders behavior, and the identification of universal structures. At leading power soft emissions are eikonal, and described by Wilson lines. Beyond leading power the eikonal approximation breaks down, soft fermions must be added, and soft radiation resolves the nature of the energetic partons from which they were emitted. For both subleading power soft gluon and quark emissions, we use the soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive an all orders gauge invariant bare factorization, at both amplitude and cross section level. This yields universal multilocal matrix elements, which we refer to as radiative functions. These appear from subleading power Lagrangians inserted along the lightcone which dress the leading power Wilson lines. The use of SCET enables us to determine the complete set of radiative functions that appear to $\mathcal{O}(λ^2)$ in the power expansion, to all orders in $α_s$. For the particular case of event shape observables in $e^+e^-\to$ dijets we derive how the radiative functions contribute to the factorized cross section to $\mathcal{O}(λ^2)$.

## Key claim

The paper derives an all-orders gauge-invariant bare factorization for subleading power soft gluon and quark emissions using soft collinear effective theory (SCET), showing that beyond leading power the eikonal approximation breaks down and universal multilocal matrix elements called radiative functions appear from subleading power Lagrangians inserted along the lightcone that dress the leading power Wilson lines. The factorization is derived to O(λ²) in the power expansion to all orders in αₛ, but the authors note that this is a bare factorization and the renormalization group structure remains to be fully developed.

## Method

The authors use SCET to expand the QCD Lagrangian in powers of the small parameter λ, separating contributions into hard scattering operators and dynamical Lagrangians. They apply the BPS field redefinition to decouple ultrasoft degrees of freedom at leading power, then treat subleading power interactions as finite numbers of operator insertions along the lightcone that decorate the Wilson lines. Gauge invariant ultrasoft quark and gluon fields are defined using Wilson lines, and the subleading power Lagrangians are rewritten in terms of these gauge invariant building blocks to derive factorization formulas at both amplitude and cross section level.

- The propagator for collinear fields is given by 1/(n̄·p̃ n·pᵣ + p̃⊥²), which is independent of n̄·pᵣ and pᵣ⊥

## Context

This work extends the leading power factorization theorems of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985-1989) to subleading power, where the eikonal approximation for soft emissions breaks down and soft fermions must be added. It builds on previous SCET studies of subleading power corrections in B-physics by Beneke (2002), Bosch, Lee, and Neubert (2004), and others, as well as recent work on subleading power soft limits by Feige (2017) and Moult (2017). The results enable systematic resummation of subleading power corrections to event shape observables and improved next-to-next-to-leading order subtraction schemes, as demonstrated recently for thrust in H→gg by Moult (2018) and for threshold resummation by Beneke (2018).
