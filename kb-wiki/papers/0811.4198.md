# Density Matrix Expansion for Low-Momentum Interactions

> A first step toward a universal nuclear energy density functional based on low-momentum interactions is taken using the density matrix expansion (DME) of Negele and Vautherin. The DME is adapted for non-local momentum-space potentials and generalized to include local three-body interactions. Different prescriptions for the three-body DME are compared. Exploratory results are given at the Hartree-Fock level, along with a roadmap for systematic improvements within an effective action framework for Kohn-Sham density functional theory.

## Key claim

The paper takes a first step toward a universal nuclear energy density functional based on low-momentum interactions, using the density matrix expansion (DME) of Negele and Vautherin adapted for non-local momentum-space potentials and generalized to include local three-body interactions, but the authors caution that the DME involves prescriptions for resummations without a corresponding power counting to justify them, and the results are exploratory at the Hartree-Fock level only.

## Method

The authors adapt the Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion (DME) for non-local momentum-space low-momentum (Vlowk) two-body potentials and generalize it to include local three-body interactions of the chiral N²LO form. They derive formulas for the coefficient functions in the resulting Skyrme-like energy density functional in terms of integrals over partial-wave matrix elements of the Vlowk potential, and compare different prescriptions for the three-body DME. The work is restricted to isoscalar (N=Z) functionals at Hartree-Fock level, with spin-orbit and tensor terms deferred.

## Key numbers

* The DME energy functional takes the form E_DME[ρ,τ,J] = ∫ d³R ε_DME(ρ(R),τ(R),J(R)) where the energy density is written as ε_DME = τ/(2M) + A[ρ] + B[ρ]τ + C[ρ]|∇ρ|² + ..., with A, B, C being functions of the isoscalar density ρ rather than constant Skyrme parameters.
* The Skyrme Hartree-Fock energy functional for N=Z nuclei is given as E_SHF[ρ,τ,J] = ∫ d³x { (1/(2M))τ + (3/8)t₀ρ² + (1/16)t₃ρ^(2+α) + (1/16)(3t₁+5t₂)ρτ + (1/64)(9t₁-5t₂)(∇ρ)² - (3/4)W₀ρ∇·J + (1/32)(t₁-t₂)J² }.
* The effective mass in the Kohn-Sham equation is given by 1/(2M*(x)) = 1/(2M) + [(3/16)t₁ + (5/16)t₂]ρ(x).

## Context

This work extends the original DME approach of Negele and Vautherin (1972, 1975), which was based on G-matrix interactions, to modern low-momentum potentials (Vlowk) that do not require G-matrix resummations due to their softened character. The paper provides a roadmap for systematic improvements within an effective action framework for Kohn-Sham density functional theory, enabling future calculations that include second-order and higher many-body contributions, isovector dependence, spin-orbit and tensor terms, and pairing, with the goal of constructing a universal nuclear energy density functional (UNEDF).
