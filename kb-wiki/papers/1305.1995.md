# Stochastic extension of the Lanczos method for nuclear shell-model calculations with variational Monte Carlo method

> We propose a new variational Monte Carlo (VMC) approach based on the Krylov subspace for large-scale shell-model calculations. A random walker in the VMC is formulated with the $M$-scheme representation, and samples a small number of configurations from a whole Hilbert space stochastically. This VMC framework is demonstrated in the shell-model calculations of $^{48}$Cr and $^{60}$Zn, and we discuss its relation to a small number of Lanczos iterations. By utilizing the wave function obtained by the conventional particle-hole-excitation truncation as an initial state, this VMC approach provides us with a sequence of systematically improved results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a variational Monte Carlo (VMC) approach that stochastically samples a Krylov subspace to systematically improve shell-model wave functions obtained from conventional particle-hole truncation, without explicitly handling the full Hilbert space. The method avoids the sign problem of Markov Chain Monte Carlo because the sampling density is defined by the squared wave function, but the power of the Hamiltonian in the Monte Carlo implementation is restricted to rather small values such as p=3 or 4.

## Method

The authors construct a trial wave function as a linear combination of powers of the Hamiltonian acting on a starting vector from a t-particle t-hole truncated subspace, with coefficients treated as variational parameters. A Metropolis random walker samples M-scheme basis states with probability proportional to the squared overlap with the trial wave function, and the energy expectation value is computed stochastically using the local energy. The variational parameters are optimized using the Nelder-Mead downhill simplex method combined with a reweighting technique that allows reuse of a single set of Monte Carlo samples.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Cr in the pf shell with GXPF1A interaction, the M-scheme dimension is 1,963,461; the exact shell-model ground-state energy is -99.578 MeV, which is almost reached at p=3,4 and t≥3.
- For ⁶⁰Zn in the pf shell with GXPF1A interaction, the M-scheme dimension is 2.2×10⁹, about 10³ times larger than that of ⁴⁸Cr.
- The VMC calculations used 48 random walkers, with 1000 burn-in steps followed by 10000 sampling steps per walker.
- The effective charges used for B(E2) transition probabilities are (e_p, e_n) = (1.2, 0.8)e.

## Context

This work extends the Krylov-subspace Lanczos method (introduced in the 1970s by Lanczos and Sebe) and the variational Monte Carlo approach for shell-model calculations (Shimizu et al., 2010) by combining them to overcome the exponential growth of the Hilbert space dimension. The method avoids the sign problem that plagues quantum Monte Carlo approaches (such as those by the Shell Model Monte Carlo group and Rombouts et al.), and provides a systematic improvement over conventional t-particle t-hole truncation. The approach enables estimation of exact energy eigenvalues using truncated wave functions and could be combined with energy-variance extrapolation techniques for future applications.
