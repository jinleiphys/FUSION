# Geometrical analysis of the algebraic cluster model

> Three-body clusters are studied in the algebraic cluster model. Particular attention is paid to the case of three identical particles. It is shown in a geometrical analysis that the harmonic oscillator, the deformed oscillator and the oblate symmetric top are contained as special solutions. An application of the oblate top limit to the nucleus 12C suggests that the low-lying spectrum can be described as a configuration of three identical alpha particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the algebraic cluster model (ACM) for three identical particles contains the six-dimensional spherical harmonic oscillator, the deformed oscillator, and the oblate symmetric top as special dynamical-symmetry limits; an application to the nucleus 12C suggests that its low-lying spectrum can be described as three identical alpha particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle, but only the oblate top limit can account for the qualitative features of the experimental form factors.

## Method

The author introduces a U(7) interacting boson model for the relative motion of three clusters, using vector boson creation and annihilation operators for the Jacobi coordinates plus an additional scalar boson. The geometric properties of the algebraic Hamiltonian are studied via time-dependent mean-field approximations using coherent states, classical limits, and Bohr-Sommerfeld requantization techniques. The energy spectra for the three special solutions (U(6) limit, SO(7) limit, and oblate symmetric top) are derived analytically, and the oblate top limit is fitted to the experimental excitation energies and form factors of 12C.

## Key numbers

- For the six-dimensional harmonic oscillator (U(6) limit): energy spectrum E(n) = epsilon (n+3) with n = 0,1,... and epsilon = sqrt(C/m).
- For the six-dimensional displaced oscillator (SO(7) limit): energy eigenvalues E(v,sigma) approximately epsilon (v+1/2) + (1/(2 m r0^2)) [sigma(sigma+4) + 15/4], with v = 0,1,... and sigma = 0,1,...
- For the oblate symmetric top: vibrational energy E_3,vib = omega_1 (v_1+1/2) + omega_2 (v_2+1) with omega_1 = 4 N R^2 xi_1 and omega_2 = (4 N R^2)/(1+R^2) xi_2; rotational energy E_3,rot = kappa_1 L(L+1) + kappa_2 (K minus or plus 2 ell)^2.
- For the 12C application: the number of bosons is taken as N = 10; the fit to excitation energies below 15 MeV identifies states 0+, 2+, 3-, 4+ of the ground rotational band, the first 0+ state of the stretching vibration, and the first 1- state of the bending vibration.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model approach previously applied to baryons (BIL) and the vibron model for diatomic molecules (vibron) to three-body clusters in nuclear physics. It provides a geometrical interpretation of the U(7) model's dynamical symmetries, connecting them to the six-dimensional spherical oscillator, deformed oscillator, and oblate symmetric top solutions of the Schrodinger equation. The application to 12C as a three-alpha-particle configuration enables future calculations of electromagnetic transition rates and form factors that can be compared with experimental data, with the oblate top limit offering a description that the U(6) and SO(7) limits cannot match for the observed form factor minima.
