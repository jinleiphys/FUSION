# Effect of tensor force on density dependence of symmetry energy within the BHF Framework

> The effect of tensor force on the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy has been investigated within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. It is shown that the tensor force manifests its effect via the tensor $^3SD_1$ channel. The density dependence of symmetry energy $E_{sym}$ turns out to be determined essentially by the tensor force from the $π$ meson and $ρ$ meson exchanges via the $^3SD_1$ coupled channel. Increasing the strength of the tensor component due to the $ρ$-meson exchange tends to enhance the repulsion of the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and leads to reduction of symmetry energy. The present results confirm the dominant role played by the tensor force in determining nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence within the microscopic BHF framework.

# Effect of tensor force on density dependence of symmetry energy within the BHF Framework

## Key claim

The tensor force determines the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy essentially via the $^3SD_1$ coupled channel from $\pi$ and $\rho$ meson exchanges, but the short-range tensor component from the $\rho$ meson exchange is not well constrained and varying its strength produces opposite effects on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter versus symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn-B one-boson-exchange potential, which includes $\pi$, $\eta$, $\sigma$, $\delta$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons. They isolated the tensor force contribution by decomposing the potential energy into isospin $T=0$ and $T=1$ channels and specifically examining the $^3SD_1$ coupled channel. To study the short-range tensor effect, they artificially scaled the $\rho$-meson tensor component by a factor $\alpha$ (where $V_T^{\rho*} = \alpha V_T^{\rho}$) and computed the resulting symmetry energy as the difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter energies.

## Key numbers

- At $\rho = 0.2$ fm$^{-3}$, the $T=0$ channel contribution to potential energy increases from -22.5 MeV to 0 MeV as asymmetry $\beta$ goes from 0 to 1, while the $T=1$ channel changes only by about 2.5 MeV (from -22.5 MeV to -25 MeV).
- At $\rho = 0.5$ fm$^{-3}$, the $T=0$ channel contribution increases from -30 MeV to 0 MeV as $\beta$ goes from 0 to 1, while the $T=1$ channel changes only by about 11 MeV (from -28 MeV to -27 MeV).
- The tensor coupling ratio $f_\rho/g_\rho = 6.1$ is used in the Bonn-B interaction, making the $\rho$ tensor coupling much stronger than its vector coupling.
- The D-state probability $P_d\%$ in various NN potentials is estimated to be between 4% and 8%.

## Context

This work extends previous BHF studies by Bombaci (1991) and Zuo (1999) that used Paris and AV14 interactions, confirming that the $T=0$ channel dominates the isovector part of the equation of state. It also builds on the investigation by Chen (2005) and others who studied tensor contributions to symmetry energy and its slope parameter $L$ at saturation density. The results are consistent with experimental evidence from JLab (Subedi 2008) for enhanced neutron-proton short-range correlations over proton-proton correlations. The paper enables future work to constrain the short-range tensor force by comparing predicted high-density symmetry energy behavior with heavy-ion collision data and neutron star observations.
