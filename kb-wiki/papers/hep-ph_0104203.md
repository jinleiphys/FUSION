# Moments of the neutron charge form factor and the N->Delta quadrupole transition

> Recent data allow a new parametrization of the neutron charge form factor G_En. A parameter-free quark model relation between G_En and the N->Delta quadrupole form factor is used to predict the latter from the G_En data. In particular, the neutron charge radius is related to N->Delta quadrupole moment, while the fourth moment of G_En connects to the N->Delta quadrupole transition radius. From the latter we derive an experimental value for the charge radius of the light constituent quarks. Finally, the C2/M1 ratio in pion electroproduction is predicted from the elastic neutron form factor data.

## Key claim

The paper uses a parameter-free quark-model relation between the neutron charge form factor and the N->Delta quadrupole transition form factor to predict the latter from new neutron form factor data, but the derivation assumes dominance of two-body exchange currents and the relation holds irrespective of the spatial part of the quark wave functions only if one-body contributions and three-body corrections are small (estimated at less than 20% and less than 30% of the two-body currents, respectively).

## Method

The authors performed a downhill simplex fit of the Galster-type parametrization G_En(Q^2) = -mu_n * a * tau / (1 + d * tau) * G_Dipole(Q^2) to four selected quasifree electron-deuteron and electron-He-3 scattering data points for G_En below Q^2 = 1 (GeV/c)^2, including the neutron charge radius from thermal neutron-electron scattering as a constraint. They then applied the quark-model relation G_C2^{N->Delta}(q^2) = -3*sqrt(2)/q^2 * G_E^n(q^2) to extract the N->Delta quadrupole moment and transition radius from the fitted G_En moments, and used the relation C2/M1 = (M_N / (2*sqrt(q^2))) * (G_En / G_Mn) to predict the C2/M1 ratio in pion electroproduction.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius: <r_n^2> = -0.113 fm^2 (statistical error 0.0026 fm^2, total error 0.0060 fm^2) from Kopecky (1995)
- Fourth moment of G_En: <r_n^4> = -0.32(8) fm^4 extracted from the new fit
- N->Delta quadrupole transition radius: <r_{N->Delta}^2> = 0.84(21) fm^2
- Quark core radius: b = 0.60 fm derived from the neutron charge radius and N-Delta mass splitting
- Charge radius of light constituent quarks: r^2_{gamma q} = 0.64 fm^2 (corresponding to r_{gamma q} = 0.8 fm)
- Fit parameters (including rms constraint): a = 0.898(44), d = 2.74(1.99), chi^2 = 0.63
- Ratio of second to first derivative of G_En: R = -7.19 (GeV/c)^{-2}
- Predicted C2/M1 ratio range: -0.03 to -0.08

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model with two-body exchange currents developed by Buchmann, Hernandez, and Yazaki (1991) and Buchmann (1997, 2000), and provides a new parametrization of G_En that supersedes the earlier evaluations by Galster (1971) and Platchkov (1990), which gave significantly larger chi^2 values (10.53 and 62.65 respectively) when compared to the new data. The predicted C2/M1 ratio agrees surprisingly well with direct measurements from pion electroproduction experiments by Bartsch (1999) and earlier work, suggesting a general validity of the relation between elastic neutron form factors and the N->Delta transition, and enables future experiments at higher Q^2 to better determine the parameter d and reduce the uncertainty on the N->Delta quadrupole transition radius.
