# Retarded versus time-nonlocal quantum kinetic equations

> The finite duration of the collisions in Fermionic systems as expressed by the retardation time in non-Markovian Levinson-type kinetic equations is discussed in the quasiclassical limit. We separate individual contributions included in the memory effect resulting in (i) off-shell tails of the Wigner distribution, (ii) renormalization of scattering rates and (iii) of the single-particle energy, (iv) collision delay and (v) related non-local corrections to the scattering integral. In this way we transform the Levinson equation into the Landau-Silin equation extended by the non-local corrections known from the theory of dense gases. The derived nonlocal kinetic equation unifies the Landau theory of quasiparticle transport with the classical kinetic theory of dense gases. The space-time symmetry is discussed versus particle-hole symmetry and a solution is proposed which transforms these two exclusive pictures into each other.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Levinson-type non-Markovian kinetic equation for the Wigner distribution is equivalent to the Landau-Silin equation for the quasiparticle distribution up to second order in the damping (scattering rate), provided that the off-shell contributions and gradient corrections are properly accounted for; this equivalence holds only in the quasiclassical limit and under a linear gradient expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for the correlation functions and derive the Levinson equation in the Wigner representation, then perform a gradient expansion in space and time to linear order. They separate the retardation effects into contributions from off-shell tails of the Wigner distribution, renormalization of scattering rates and single-particle energy, collision delay, and non-local corrections, and show that these can be rearranged to transform the Levinson equation into the Landau-Silin equation with a quasiparticle drift term. The extended quasiparticle approximation is used to relate the Wigner distribution to the quasiparticle distribution via a functional that includes off-shell contributions.

## Key numbers

- The wave-function renormalization factor is given by \( z = 1 + \left. \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial \omega} \right|_{\omega = \varepsilon} \), where \(\sigma\) is the real part of the selfenergy and \(\varepsilon\) is the quasiparticle energy.
- The off-shell contribution to the Wigner distribution is expressed as \( \rho = f + \int \frac{d\omega}{2\pi} \frac{\wp}{\omega - \varepsilon} \frac{\partial}{\partial \omega} \left( (1-f) \sigma^<_\omega - f \sigma^>_\omega \right) \), where \(\wp\) denotes the principal value.
- The non-local corrections to the scattering integral are given by derivatives of the scattering phase shift \(\phi = \text{Im} \ln t^R(\omega, k, p, q, t, r)\), with explicit expressions for \(\Delta_t = \partial \phi / \partial \omega\), \(\Delta_K = \frac{1}{2} \partial \phi / \partial r\), and others.
- The spectral sum rule \(\int \frac{d\omega}{2\pi} a(k, \omega, r, t) = 1\) and the energy-weighted sum rule \(\int \frac{d\omega}{2\pi} \omega a(k, \omega, r, t) = \frac{k^2}{2m} + \sigma^{\text{hf}}(k, r, t)\) are satisfied by the extended quasiparticle approximation.

## Context

This work extends the Levinson-type kinetic equation (Levinson 1965, 1969) and the Landau-Silin equation for quasiparticle transport by unifying them with non-local corrections known from the classical kinetic theory of dense gases, such as the Enskog equation. It builds on the extended quasiparticle approximation developed by Spicka and Lipavsky (1995) and the nonlocal kinetic equation derived by Spicka, Lipavsky, and Morawetz (1996, 1997). The paper enables a consistent treatment of finite collision duration and virial corrections in Fermi systems, linking the BBGKY hierarchy approach to the quasiparticle picture, and provides a framework for numerical solutions of the derived nonlocal kinetic equation.
