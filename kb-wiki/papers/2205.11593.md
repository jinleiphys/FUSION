# Precision Determination of the Neutral Weak Form Factor of $^{48}$Ca

> We report a precise measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry $A_{\rm PV}$ in the elastic scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons from $^{48}{\rm Ca}$. We measure $A_{\rm PV} =2668\pm 106\ {\rm (stat)}\pm 40\ {\rm (syst)}$ parts per billion, leading to an extraction of the neutral weak form factor $F_{\rm W} (q=0.8733$ fm$^{-1}) = 0.1304 \pm 0.0052 \ {\rm (stat)}\pm 0.0020\ {\rm (syst)}$ and the charge minus the weak form factor $F_{\rm ch} - F_{\rm W} = 0.0277\pm 0.0055$. The resulting neutron skin thickness $R_n-R_p=0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)}$~fm is relatively thin yet consistent with many model calculations. The combined CREX and PREX results will have implications for future energy density functional calculations and on the density dependence of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron scattering from $^{48}$Ca is measured to be $A_{\rm PV} = 2668 \pm 106\ {\rm (stat)} \pm 40\ {\rm (syst)}$ parts per billion, yielding a neutron skin thickness $R_n-R_p = 0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)}$ fm that is relatively thin yet consistent with many model calculations, though the abstract's claim of consistency masks significant model dependence in the extraction.

## Method
The CREX Collaboration measured the parity-violating asymmetry $A_{\rm PV}$ by scattering a 2.18 GeV longitudinally polarized electron beam from isotopically enriched $^{48}$Ca targets in Hall A at Jefferson Lab, using the PREX-2 apparatus reoptimized for calcium. Scattered electrons at 4-6 degrees were directed through dipole magnets and collimators into two High-Resolution Spectrometers, with elastically scattered electrons detected by fused-silica Cherenkov detectors. The beam helicity was flipped at 120 Hz in quartet patterns, and 412 Coulombs of production data were acquired with 150 $\mu$A beam current, with corrections applied for beam polarization, helicity-correlated beam fluctuations, isotopic purity, inelastic contributions, and acceptance effects.

## Key numbers
- $A_{\rm PV} = 2668 \pm 106\ {\rm (stat)} \pm 40\ {\rm (syst)}$ parts per billion for $^{48}$Ca at $\langle Q^2\rangle = 0.0297 \pm 0.0002\ {\rm (GeV/c)}^2$ and $\langle \theta \rangle = 4.51^\circ \pm 0.02^\circ$
- Neutral weak form factor $F_{\rm W}(q=0.8733\ {\rm fm}^{-1}) = 0.1304 \pm 0.0052\ {\rm (stat)} \pm 0.0020\ {\rm (syst)}$
- Charge minus weak form factor $F_{\rm ch} - F_{\rm W} = 0.0277 \pm 0.0055$ (experimental)
- Neutron skin thickness $R_n-R_p = 0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)}$ fm
- Weak skin $\RW - \Rch = 0.159 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.023\ {\rm (model)}$ fm
- Beam polarization $P_{\rm b} = 87.10 \pm 0.39\%$
- Total systematic uncertainty on $A_{\rm PV}$: 40 ppb (1.5%); statistical uncertainty: 106 ppb (4.0%)

## Context
This measurement extends the parity-violating electron scattering technique previously applied to $^{208}$Pb by the PREX-2 collaboration (2021) to the lighter $^{48}$Ca nucleus, where microscopic coupled cluster calculations by Hu et al. (2021) and Hagen et al. (2016) are more feasible. The combined CREX and PREX-2 results constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy in nuclear matter and inform energy density functional calculations, with implications for neutron star properties such as radius and tidal deformability, complementing X-ray observations (Riley et al., 2019) and gravitational wave detections.
