# Coulomb effects in four nucleon continuum states

> The Faddeev-Yakubovski equations are solved in configuration space for low energy four-nucleon continuum states. Coulomb interaction was included into the formalism permitting an exact description of the scattering states in p+$^{3}$He and p+$^{3}$H systems.

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space can be extended to include Coulomb interactions, enabling an exact description of low-energy scattering in p+3He and p+3H systems; however, the p-3H system remains challenging because the 4He virtual state is placed between thresholds only when Coulomb is included, and the MT I-III potential fails to reproduce experimental p-3H scattering lengths and excitation functions.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space for four-nucleon continuum states, incorporating the Coulomb interaction into the formalism. They investigated n-3H, p-3He, and p-3H systems using the semi-realistic MT I-III potential and several realistic NN potentials (Av.14, Av.18) in conjunction with the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon force (3NF). Calculations were performed for low-energy scattering states, with partial wave bases enlarged compared to prior work.

## Key numbers

* n-3H scattering length (Jπ=0+): 4.10 fm (MT I-III), 4.28 fm (Av.14), 4.04 fm (Av.18+UIX)
* n-3H scattering length (Jπ=1+): 3.63 fm (MT I-III), 3.81 fm (Av.14), 3.60 fm (Av.18+UIX)
* p-3He scattering length (Jπ=0+): 11.5 fm (MT I-III)
* p-3He scattering length (Jπ=1+): 9.20 fm (MT I-III)
* p-3H scattering length (Jπ=0+): -63.1 fm (MT I-III), -13.9 fm (Av.14), -16.5 fm (Av.18+UIX)
* p-3H scattering length (Jπ=1+): 5.50 fm (MT I-III), 5.77 fm (Av.14), 5.39 fm (Av.18+UIX)
* The n-3H total cross section near the resonance peak was studied at Ecm = 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior Faddeev-Yakubovski calculations by Carbonell and collaborators (2001) that used the MT I-III potential for n-3H scattering, and by the Pisa group (2003) who used Av.18+UIX forces for p-3He. The inclusion of Coulomb interaction is essential for correctly placing the 4He virtual state between the p-3H and n-3He thresholds, resolving a problem where earlier Coulomb-neglected calculations produced a bound state instead. The results enable future detailed analysis of p-3H scattering above the n-3He threshold and provide a stringent test of NN potentials and three-nucleon forces in four-nucleon continuum states.
