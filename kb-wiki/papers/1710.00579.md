# Cluster formation in pre-compound nuclei in the time-dependent framework

> Background: Applications of nuclear time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) are often capable of providing quantitative description of heavy ion reactions. However, the structure of pre-compound states produced in heavy ion reactions are difficult to assess theoretically in TDDFT as the s.p. density alone is a weak indicator of shell structure and cluster states. Purpose: We employ the time-dependent nucleon localization function (NLF) to reveal structure of pre-compound states in nuclear reactions involving light and medium-mass ions. We primarily focus on spin saturated systems with N = Z. Furthermore, we study reactions with oxygen and carbon ions, for which experimental evidence for α clustering in pre-compound states exists. Method: We utilize the symmetry-free TDDFT approach and compute the NLFs to describe $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca + $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca, and $^{16,18}$O + $^{12}$C collisions at energies above the Coulomb barrier. Results: We show that NLFs reveal a variety of time-dependent modes involving cluster structures. For instance, the $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O collision results in a vibrational mode of a quasi-molecular α-$^{12}$C-$^{12}$C-α state. For heavier ions, a variety of cluster configurations are predicted. For the collision of $^{16,18}$O + $^{12}$C, we showed that the pre-compound system has a tendency to form α clusters. This result supports the experimental findings that the presence of cluster structures in the projectile and target nuclei gives rise to strong entrance channel effects and enhanced α emission. Conclusion: The time-dependent NLF is a good indicator of clusters structures in complex pre-compound states formed in heavy-ion fusion reactions. The localization reveals the presence of collective vibrations involving cluster structures, which dominate the initial dynamics of the fusing system.

## Key claim

The time-dependent nucleon localization function (NLF) reveals cluster structures in pre-compound states formed in heavy-ion fusion reactions that are invisible in the single-particle density alone, but the authors cannot directly address experimental alpha-decay cross sections because TDDFT cannot describe quantum tunneling.

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-unrestricted time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional UNEDF1, solving the time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations on an equidistant grid with the Sky3D package. They computed time-dependent nucleon localization functions (NLFs) for central and peripheral collisions of 16O + 16O, 40Ca + 16O, 40Ca + 40Ca, and 16,18O + 12C at energies above the Coulomb barrier, with a time step of 0.2 fm/c in a cubic box of length 32 fm.

## Key numbers

- 16O + 16O central collision at Ecm = 20 MeV: a quasi-molecular alpha-12C-12C-alpha vibrational state is observed, with alpha clusters at the tips and two 12C rings at z = ±4 fm
- 16O + 16O collision at Ecm = 100 MeV: the system fissions after approximately t = 240 fm/c, with 12C clusters at the tips and alpha clusters formed in the neck area
- 40Ca + 40Ca central collision at Ecm = 150 MeV: the pre-compound state of 80Zr exhibits configurations involving 32S clusters, 28Si clusters, 24Mg clusters, and alpha-36Ar-36Ar-alpha molecular states at different times up to t = 900 fm/c
- 40Ca + 40Ca collision at Ecm = 300 MeV: the intermediate state survives for only a very short time before splitting, with 24Mg clusters at the tips, two 12C clusters, and a 8Be region in the center at t = 170 fm/c
- 16O + 40Ca collision at Ecm = 80 MeV: a quasi-molecular 12C-44Ti structure is predicted at t = 170 fm/c
- 16O + 40Ca collision at Ecm = 200 MeV with impact parameter b = 2 fm: the lighter fragment has mass number A ≈ 13.7 and charge Z ≈ 7
- 18O + 12C collision at Ecm = 14 MeV and b = 2 fm: appreciable alpha clustering is apparent, especially at the tips of the pre-compound system, for energies between 8 and 14 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the nucleon localization function, previously used for ground-state nuclei by Reinhard (2011) and for fission by Zhang (2016), to the time-dependent domain of heavy-ion collisions. The results support experimental findings by Vadas (2015) that cluster structures in projectile and target nuclei give rise to strong entrance channel effects and enhanced alpha emission in reactions such as 16O + 12C, 16O + 13C, and 18O + 12C. The work enables future studies of pre-equilibrium alpha-emission probabilities, though significant extensions beyond TDDFT are required to estimate actual emission rates.
