# Searching for minimum in dependence of squared speed-of-sound on collision energy

> Experimental results of the rapidity distributions of negatively charged pions produced in proton-proton ($p$-$p$) and beryllium-beryllium (Be-Be) collisions at different beam momentums, measured by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration at the super proton synchrotron (SPS), are described by a revised (three-source) Landau hydrodynamic model. The squared speed-of-sound parameter $c^2_s$ is then extracted from the width of rapidity distribution. There is a local minimum (knee point) which indicates a softest point in the equation of state (EoS) appearing at about 40$A$ GeV/$c$ (or 8.8 GeV) in $c^2_s$ excitation function [the dependence of $c^2_s$ on incident beam momentum (or center-of-mass energy)]. This knee point should be related to the searching for the onset of quark deconfinement and the critical point of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase transition.

## Key claim

The squared speed-of-sound parameter extracted from rapidity distributions of negatively charged pions in p-p and Be-Be collisions shows a local minimum (knee point) at a center-of-mass energy of 8.8 GeV, which the authors interpret as a "softest point" in the equation of state potentially related to the onset of quark deconfinement and the QGP phase transition, though they note this relation remains an open question.

## Method

The authors applied a revised (three-source) Landau hydrodynamic model, fitting rapidity distributions of negatively charged pions measured by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration at the SPS for p-p collisions at beam momenta of 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c and for Be-Be collisions at 20A, 31A, 40A, 75A, and 150A GeV/c. The squared speed-of-sound parameter was extracted from the Gaussian width of each source using the relation σ_X = sqrt[(8/3) * (c²_s(X) / (1 - c⁴_s(X))) * L], where L = ln(√s_NN / 2m_p). The central source width σ_C and corresponding c²_s(C) were then plotted as excitation functions across collision energies from 6.3 to 17.3 GeV and combined with previous results at higher energies (≥19.6 GeV).

## Key numbers

- The local minimum (knee point) in c²_s(C) appears at √s_NN = 8.8 GeV (corresponding to 40A GeV/c beam momentum)
- For p-p collisions, the five beam momenta studied were 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c, corresponding to √s = 6.3, 7.7, 8.8, 12.3, and 17.3 GeV
- For Be-Be collisions, the five beam momenta studied were 20A, 31A, 40A, 75A, and 150A GeV/c, corresponding to √s_NN = 6.3, 7.7, 8.8, 11.9, and 16.8 GeV
- At higher energies (≥19.6 GeV), most c²_s(C) values lie between 1/3 and 1/2 (in units of c²)
- A jump in c²_s(C) occurs between 17.3 and 19.6 GeV, from ≤0.3 to the range 1/3–1/2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Liu et al., 2015) using the three-source Landau hydrodynamic model to lower SPS energies, and the observed softest point at 8.8 GeV is compatible with prior work using the Landau hydrodynamic model and ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics hybrid approach that located the softest point between 4 and 9 GeV. The results enable further searches for the onset of quark deconfinement and the critical point of the QGP phase transition, though the authors note that the relation between the softest point and deconfinement onset remains an open question requiring analysis of additional experimental data.
