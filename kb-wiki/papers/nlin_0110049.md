# Integrability and exact spectrum of a pairing model for nucleons

> A pairing model for nucleons, introduced by Richardson in 1966, which describes proton-neutron pairing as well as proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing, is re-examined in the context of the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method. Specifically, this shows that the model is integrable by enabling the explicit construction of the conserved operators. We determine the eigenvalues of these operators in terms of the Bethe ansatz, which in turn leads to an expression for the energy eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a coupled pairing model for nucleons, originally introduced by Richardson (1966) and including proton-neutron pairing alongside proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing, is integrable via the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method; the integrability holds for a special case where the energy level spacings for protons and neutrons are identical and the scattering coupling is the same for all three pairing types, giving the Hamiltonian an isospin symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct a solution of the Yang-Baxter equation associated with the fundamental representation of the Lie algebra \(so(5)\), then define a transfer matrix acting on a D-fold tensor product space. By taking a quasi-classical expansion of the transfer matrix, they obtain a set of mutually commuting conserved operators \(\tau_j\). The Hamiltonian is expressed as a function of these operators and the \(so(3)\) Casimir operator \(K\), yielding the pairing Hamiltonian with coupling \(g = 2/\alpha\). The eigenvalues of the conserved operators and the Hamiltonian are determined via the analytic Bethe ansatz, leading to coupled Bethe ansatz equations for the parameters \(v_i\) and \(w_j\).

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian coupling constant is given by \(g = 2/\alpha\), where \(\alpha\) is a free parameter.
- The \(so(5)\) Casimir invariant \(C\) takes the eigenvalue \(\chi_C(1,0) = 8\) for the fundamental representation.
- The \(so(3)\) Casimir operator \(K\) has eigenvalue \(\chi_K(\mu) = \mu(\mu+1)\) on a representation with highest weight \(\mu\).
- The energy eigenvalues are given by \(E = \sum_j^M v_j\), where the \(\{v_j\}\) are roots of the Bethe ansatz equations.
- For the case \(D=2, M=1, N=0\), the Bethe ansatz equation reduces to \(\alpha + 2/(v-\epsilon_1) + 2/(v-\epsilon_2) = 0\), which is quadratic for non-zero \(\alpha\) but linear (with unique finite solution \(v = (\epsilon_1+\epsilon_2)/2\)) when \(\alpha = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method treatment previously applied to the reduced BCS Hamiltonian by Zhou et al. (2000) and von Delft and Poghossian (2000) to the coupled pairing model for nucleons introduced by Richardson (1966). The model was recently studied by Dukelsky et al. (2001). The results enable the possibility of computing form factors and correlation functions for this model using algebraic methods, analogous to the results of Zhou et al. (2000). The Bethe ansatz equations obtained differ from those of Richardson (1966), and the equivalence of the two solutions remains an open problem.
