# Two-photon exchange correction to $2S$-$2P$ splitting in muonic helium-3 ions

> We calculate the two-photon exchange correction to the Lamb shift in muonic helium-3 ions within the dispersion relations framework. Part of the effort entailed making analytic fits to the electron-$^3$He quasielastic scattering data set, for purposes of doing the dispersion integrals. Our result is that the energy of the 2$S$ state is shifted downwards by two-photon exchange effects by 15.14(49) meV, in good accord with the result obtained from a potential model and effective field theory calculation.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange correction shifts the 2S state energy downward by 15.14(49) meV, with an uncertainty small enough to meet the required precision of better than 10% for extracting the nuclear charge radius from muonic helium-3 Lamb shift measurements, and the result agrees well with potential model and effective field theory calculations.

## Method

The authors used a dispersion relations framework to calculate the two-photon exchange correction to the Lamb shift in muonic helium-3 ions. They made analytic fits to electron-helium-3 quasielastic scattering data, including data from the Donal Day web archive and additional 180-degree data sets, using functional forms motivated by a Fermi gas model with modifications for low energy and low momentum transfer regions. The calculation was separated into elastic, inelastic (nuclear and nucleon), and subtraction contributions, with the subtraction function obtained from a sum rule based on the dispersion relation for the transverse Compton amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Total two-photon exchange correction to the 2S state energy: -15.14(49) meV
- Elastic contribution: -10.93(27) meV
- Nuclear polarizability (inelastic) contribution: -5.50(40) meV
- Nucleon polarizability (inelastic) contribution: -0.306(15) meV
- Subtraction contribution: 1.60(12) meV
- Predicted magnetic polarizability of helium-3: 5.7(0.5) x 10^{-3} fm^3
- Charge radius of helium-4 from electron scattering: 1.681(4) fm
- Charge radius of helium-3 from electron scattering analysis: 1.973(14) fm
- Isotope shift measurements yield helium-3 charge radii of 1.9728(36) fm, 1.9748(36) fm, and 1.9631(44) fm from three different measurements
- Bethe-Levinger sum rule test: integral to 30 MeV above threshold gives 1.65, to 60 MeV gives 1.78

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relations approach previously applied to muonic hydrogen by Carlson et al. (2011) and Gorchtein et al. (2013) and to muonic deuterium by Carlson et al. (2013) to the case of muonic helium-3. The calculation corroborates the potential model and effective field theory results of Dinur et al. (2015) and Hernandez et al. (2016), with the total TPE correction agreeing within uncertainties. The result enables extraction of the helium-3 charge radius from ongoing muonic helium Lamb shift experiments at Paul Scherrer Institute, with the TPE uncertainty now smaller than the required 1.5 meV benchmark, though future measurements at the MESA accelerator in Mainz could further reduce the uncertainty by a factor of 2 to 4.
