# Azimuthal Jet Tomography of Quark Gluon Plasmas at RHIC and LHC

> Recent data on the azimuthal and transverse momentum dependence of high-pT>10 GeV pion nuclear modification factors in nuclear collisions at RHIC/BNL and LHC/CERN are analyzed in terms of a wide class of jet-energy loss models and a variety of transverse expanding collective flow backgrounds. RHIC data at 200 AGeV are found to be surprisingly consistent with rather different dE/dx models when coupled to recent 2+1D minimally viscous QGP flow field predictions. However, extrapolations to LHC, with parameters fixed at RHIC, favor running coupling QCD based energy-loss models over fixed coupling QCD, conformal AdS holography, or Tc-dominated jet-energy loss models that tend to overpredict jet quenching at the LHC.

## Key claim

At RHIC, four distinct classes of jet-energy loss models (running-coupling pQCD, fixed-coupling pQCD, conformal AdS holography, and Tc-dominated SLTc) all describe the azimuthal and transverse momentum dependence of the pion nuclear modification factor equally well when coupled to realistic 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic flow fields, but extrapolation to LHC with parameters fixed at RHIC favors running-coupling QCD-based models over the others.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear modification factor R_AA(p_T, phi) for high-p_T > 10 GeV pions using a parametric dE/dx model with exponents (a,b,c) controlling jet energy, path length, and temperature dependence, coupled to three different bulk QGP flow fields: ideal VISH2+1 hydrodynamics, viscous (eta/s=0.08) RL hydrodynamics, and a v_perp=0.6 blast wave model. For each model, the jet-medium coupling kappa was fixed by fitting a single reference point at p_T=7.5 GeV for central 0-5% Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV with R_AA=0.20, then predictions were compared to RHIC data at 200 AGeV and LHC data at 2.76 ATeV for both 0-5% and 20-30% centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- RHIC reference point: R_AA = 0.20 at p_T = 7.5 GeV for central 0-5% Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV, used to constrain all models
- For QCD1 and QCD2 models, the jet-medium coupling kappa was reduced by approximately 30% from RHIC to LHC to simulate running QCD coupling
- SLTc model uses kappa_c/kappa_Q = 3 for the temperature range 113 < T < 173 MeV, with kappa_Q = kappa(T > 173 MeV)
- CUJET free parameter: maximum alpha_s^max = 0.4 for alpha_s(Q^2 < 1 GeV^2)
- All models underpredict R_AA in the intermediate kinematic region 2 < p_T < 8 GeV at both RHIC and LHC

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Bass et al. (2008) and the JET Collaboration by incorporating 2+1D transverse expansion from VISH2+1 (Song 2007, Shen 2010) and RL hydro (Luzum 2008) flow fields, and by testing a wider class of dE/dx models including the SLTc model of Liao (2008). The results contradict the tentative conclusion of Adare (2012) that AdS/CFT models describe RHIC data better than QCD-based models, and instead show that all models perform equally well at RHIC when realistic flow is included. The cross-comparison of RHIC and LHC data enables discrimination between models, favoring running-coupling pQCD as implemented in CUJET (Buzzatti 2012) over conformal AdS holography (Marquet 2009, Gubser 2008) and Tc-dominated models, while identifying the intermediate p_T region 2-8 GeV as a remaining challenge requiring non-equilibrium QGP theory.
