# Towards a model-independent constraint of the high-density dependence of the symmetry energy

> Neutron-proton elliptic flow difference and ratio have been shown to be promising observables in the attempt to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy above the saturation point from heavy-ion collision data. Their dependence on model parameters like microscopic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections, compressibility of nuclear matter, optical potential, and symmetry energy parametrization is thoroughly studied. By using a parametrization of the symmetry energy derived from the momentum dependent Gogny force in conjunction with the Tübingen QMD model and comparing with the experimental FOPI/LAND data for 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, a moderately stiff, x=-1.35 +/- 1.25, symmetry energy is extracted, a result that agrees with that of a similar study that employed the UrQMD transport model and a momentum independent power-law parametrization of the symmetry energy. This contrasts with diverging results extracted from the FOPI $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio available in the literature.

## Key claim

By comparing the Tubingen QMD transport model with FOPI/LAND experimental data for 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, the authors extract a moderately stiff symmetry energy (x = -1.35 +/- 1.25) using a momentum-dependent Gogny force parametrization, but this result carries a hedge: the extracted constraint depends on the choice of optical potential parametrization and on whether one uses the neutron-proton elliptic flow difference or ratio, with the two observables giving slightly different central values that are reconciled only by averaging.

## Method

The authors simulated 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon using the Tubingen quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model with a Gogny-inspired momentum-dependent parametrization of the symmetry energy that includes a stiffness parameter x. They compared the simulated neutron-proton elliptic flow difference (v2^n - v2^p) and ratio (v2^n/v2^p) against reanalyzed FOPI/LAND experimental data across multiple rapidity and transverse momentum windows, systematically varying the compressibility modulus (K), nucleon wave function width (L), optical potential parametrization, and symmetry energy parametrization to assess model dependence.

## Key numbers

- Extracted symmetry energy stiffness parameter: x = -1.50(+1.75/-1.00) from npEFD and x = -1.25(+1.25/-1.00) from npEFR, yielding a combined result of x = -1.35 +/- 1.25
- Slope parameter L_sym = 129(+46/-80) MeV from npEFD and L_sym = 118(+45/-57) MeV from npEFR
- Curvature parameter K_sym = 272(+291/-508) MeV from npEFD and K_sym = 199(+291/-362) MeV from npEFR
- The experimental elliptic flow values for neutrons, protons, and hydrogen are tabulated for impact parameters from 1.9 fm to 7.2 fm at 400 MeV/nucleon, with statistical uncertainties ranging from 0.0022 to 0.0148
- The compressibility modulus was varied in the range K = 190-280 MeV and nucleon wave function width in the range L = 2.5-7.0 fm^2 while keeping simulated elliptic flows within 25% of experimental values

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Russotto et al. (2011) and Cozma et al. (2011) by simultaneously studying both neutron-proton elliptic flow difference and ratio, and by systematically assessing model dependence from optical potential and symmetry energy parametrization choices. The results agree with the UrQMD-based study of Russotto et al. (2011) that used a momentum-independent power-law parametrization and found gamma = 0.9 +/- 0.4, but contrast with diverging results extracted from the FOPI pi-/pi+ ratio by Xiao et al. (2009), Feng and Jin (2009), and Xie et al. (2013), which favored soft, stiff, and super-soft symmetry energy scenarios respectively. The extracted L_sym values are larger by a factor of 2 than those from neutron skin and isospin diffusion analyses at lower densities (Tsang et al., 2012), and the work enables future constraints from the ASY-EOS experiment.
