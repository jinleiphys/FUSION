# The $d^{\ast}$(2380) dibaryon resonance width and decay branching ratios

> Attempts to reproduce theoretically the relatively small width, $Γ_{d^{\ast}}=80\pm 10$ MeV, of the $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$ $d^{\ast}$(2380) dibaryon resonance established by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration are discussed. The validity of associating the $d^{\ast}$(2380) in quark-based models primarily with a tightly bound $ΔΔ$ configuration is questioned. The $d^{\ast}$(2380) width and decay branching ratios into $NNππ$, $NNπ$ and $NN$ final states are studied within the Gal-Garcilazo hadronic model in which the $d^{\ast}$(2380) is a $πNΔ$ resonance embedded in the $NNππ$ continuum some 80 MeV below the $ΔΔ$ threshold. In particular, predictions are made for the branching ratios of the unobserved yet $d^{\ast}(2380)\to NNπ$ decays which are suppressed in a purely-$ΔΔ$ dibaryon model. Comments are also made on a possible connection of the ABC effect observed in the $pn\to d^{\ast}\to dπ^0π^0$ resonance reaction to the $d^{\ast}$(2380) dibaryon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $d^{\ast}$(2380) dibaryon resonance cannot be described exclusively as a compact $\Delta\Delta$ quasibound state, because such a configuration would produce a width much smaller than the observed $\Gamma_{d^{\ast}}=80\pm 10$ MeV; instead, the resonance requires a two-component structure including a $\pi{\cal D}_{12}$ channel within the Gal-Garcilazo hadronic model.

## Method

The author evaluates the $d^{\ast}$(2380) width by computing the in-medium single-$\Delta$ decay width averaged over the bound-state momentum distribution of a $\Delta\Delta$ configuration, using the empirical momentum-dependent decay width formula $\overline{\Gamma}_{\Delta\to N\pi} = \gamma \, \overline{q}^3 / (q_0^2 + \overline{q}^2)$ with $\gamma=0.74$ and $q_0=159$ MeV. For the branching ratio analysis, the author constructs a $\Delta\Delta$--$\pi{\cal D}_{12}$ mixing model with mixing parameter $\alpha$, solving $\alpha \Gamma_{<} + (1-\alpha) \Gamma_{>} = \Gamma_{NN\pi\pi}^{d^{\ast}}$ where $\Gamma_{<}=44$ MeV and $\Gamma_{>}=100$ MeV, and derives partial decay widths by fitting to experimental peak cross sections.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma_{d^{\ast}} = 80 \pm 10$ MeV: total width of the $d^{\ast}$(2380) dibaryon resonance from WASA-at-COSY and SAID
- $\Gamma_{\Delta} \approx 115$ MeV: free-space width of a single $\Delta$ baryon
- $B_{\Delta\Delta} = 84$ MeV: binding energy of two $\Delta$s in a $\Delta\Delta$ configuration ($2 \times 1232 - 2380$)
- $R_{\Delta\Delta} = 0.76$ fm: r.m.s. radius from quark-based RGM calculations (Huang 2015), yielding $\overline{\Gamma}_{\Delta\Delta\to NN\pi\pi} \approx 47$ MeV (upper bound) or 27 MeV (infinite square well)
- $\Gamma_{\rm tot}^{d^{\ast}} = 75$ MeV: adopted total width for branching ratio calculations
- $\Gamma_{NN}^{d^{\ast}} = 9$ to 11 MeV: $NN$ partial decay width, corresponding to BR of 0.12 to 0.15
- $\sigma(pn\to d^{\ast}\to d\pi^0\pi^0) = 240$ $\mu$b: resonance peak cross section assumed for branching ratio determination
- $\alpha = 5/7$: mixing parameter derived from $\Gamma_{<}=44$ MeV and $\Gamma_{>}=100$ MeV
- $\Gamma_{NN\pi}^{d^{\ast}} \approx 6.2^{+1.0}_{-1.9}$ MeV: predicted $NN\pi$ partial decay width, BR $\approx 8.3^{+1.3}_{-2.5}\%$
- $\sigma(pn\to d^{\ast}\to NN\pi) = 178^{+29}_{-55}$ $\mu$b: predicted $NN\pi$ production cross section

## Context

This work extends the Gal-Garcilazo hadronic model (Gal and Garcilazo, 2013, 2014) in which the $d^{\ast}$(2380) is a $\pi N\Delta$ resonance embedded in the $NN\pi\pi$ continuum, and questions the validity of quark-based $\Delta\Delta$ dibaryon models such as those by Huang (2014, 2015) and Dong (2016). The paper provides predictions for the unobserved $d^{\ast}(2380)\to NN\pi$ decays, which are suppressed in purely-$\Delta\Delta$ models, and connects the ABC effect observed in the $pn\to d\pi^0\pi^0$ reaction to the compact $\Delta\Delta$ component of the resonance. The results enable future experimental tests of the $\pi{\cal D}_{12}$--$\Delta\Delta$ mixing parameter through precise measurement of the $NN\pi$ decay width.
