# Effects of inner crusts on $g$-mode oscillations in neutron stars

> In this work we investigate the influence of neutron stars' crusts on the non-radial $g$-mode oscillations and examine their correlations with nuclear matter properties fixed by adopting 10 different relativistic density functionals. At subsaturation densities, neutron star matter takes non-uniform structures and form the crusts. We find that the Brunt-Väisälä (BV) frequency increases significantly at densities slightly above the neutron drip density (i.e., neutron stars' inner crusts), which leads to crust $g$-mode oscillations with their frequencies insensitive to the adopted density functional. At larger densities, BV frequency increases as well due to the core-crust transitions and emergence of muons, which lead to core $g$-mode oscillations. It is found that the obtained core $g$-mode frequencies generally increase with the slope of nuclear symmetry energy $L$, which eventually intersect with that of the crust $g$ modes adopting large enough $L$. This leads to the avoid-crossing phenomenon for the global $g$ modes that encompass contributions from both the crust and core. The correlation between the global $g_1$ mode and $L$ is identified for neutron stars with masses $M\gtrsim 1.4\ M_{\odot}$, which enables the measurements of $L$ based on gravitational wave observations. In our future study, the effects of the discontinuities in density or shear modulus should be considered, while the temperature, rotation, magnetic field, and superfluid neutron gas in neutron stars could also play important roles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Brunt-Väisälä frequency increases significantly at densities just above the neutron drip density (inner crust), producing crust g-mode oscillations whose frequencies are insensitive to the chosen density functional; however, this insensitivity applies only to the crust g-modes, while core g-mode frequencies generally increase with the slope of nuclear symmetry energy L, and the global g₁ mode shows a linear correlation with L for neutron stars with masses greater than about 1.4 solar masses, enabling potential measurements of L from gravitational wave observations.

## Method

The authors adopted 10 relativistic mean-field density functionals (NL3, PK1, TM1, GM1, MTVTC, DD-LZ1, DD-MEX, PKDD, DD-ME2, TW99) to construct unified equations of state for neutron star matter, including non-uniform pasta phases (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) in the inner crust determined via Thomas-Fermi approximation within Wigner-Seitz cells. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for equilibrium stellar structure and then computed non-radial g-mode oscillations (l=2) under the relativistic Cowling approximation, using the difference between adiabatic and equilibrium sound speeds to determine the Brunt-Väisälä frequency.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density lies within the range 0.0002 ≲ n_b ≲ 0.0003 fm⁻³ for the 10 models.
- Crust-core transition densities lie within the range 0.054 ≲ n_b ≲ 0.074 fm⁻³.
- The slope of symmetry energy L ranges from 42.5 MeV (DD-LZ1) to 118.6 MeV (NL3) across the 10 functionals.
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between the global g₁ mode frequency and L ranges from 0.7 to 0.9 for neutron stars with fixed masses.
- The maximum masses of neutron stars predicted by all 10 functionals exceed two solar masses.
- The binding energy B is approximately -16 MeV for all functionals, with values from -16.06 MeV (DD-LZ1) to -16.33 MeV (GM1).
- The incompressibility K ranges from 230.7 MeV (DD-LZ1) to 300.5 MeV (GM1).
- The symmetry energy S ranges from 32.0 MeV (DD-LZ1) to 37.6 MeV (PK1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of g-mode oscillations in neutron stars that typically neglected crust contributions, such as those by Finn (1987) and Reisenegger (1992) who focused on density discontinuities, and more recent work by Jaikumar (2021) and Tran (2023) on composition g-modes in uniform matter. The inclusion of inner crust structure enables identification of avoid-crossing between crust and core g-modes, and the correlation between the global g₁ mode and the slope of symmetry energy L provides a pathway to constrain nuclear matter properties through future gravitational wave observations of binary neutron star mergers, where tidal excitation of g-modes is expected.
