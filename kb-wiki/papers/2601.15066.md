# Combined constraints on dark photons from high-energy collisions, cosmology, and astrophysics

> We investigate a dark sector coupled to the Standard Model (SM) through a kinetically mixed dark photon $U$ associated with a new $U(1)'$ gauge symmetry. Kinetic mixing $\varepsilon$ induces an effective coupling to the electromagnetic current, while $U$ interacts with stable dark matter (DM) $χ$ via a dark gauge coupling $g_χ$. Our analysis is based on the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, extended to include dark photon production and decay into dileptons ($U\!\to e^+e^-$). In PHSD, dark photons are produced in high-energy collisions through Dalitz decays of light mesons ($π^0,η,η',ω$), Delta-resonances ($Δ\!\to N U$), direct vector meson decays ($ρ,ω,ϕ\!\to U$), kaon decays, and $q\bar q\!\to U$ annihilation. Building on previous PHSD benchmarks against dilepton data, we extract upper limits on $\varepsilon^2(m_U,m_χ,α_χ)$ in both the visible regime ($m_U<2m_χ$), where $U\!\to e^+e^-$ dominates, and the invisible regime ($m_U>2m_χ$), where $U\!\toχ\barχ$ is kinematically open. Cosmological and astrophysical constraints are incorporated in two complementary ways. First, we compute the velocity-dependent self-interaction cross section $σ/m_χ$ for Yukawa-mediated SIDM and confront it with bounds from dwarf galaxies, galaxy groups, and clusters. Second, we determine thermal relic target curves by computing the relic abundance and requiring $Ω_{\rm DM}h^2\simeq 0.12$, consistent with \textit{Planck} measurements of the cosmic microwave background. Combining PHSD limits on $\varepsilon^2$ with relic density and self-interaction requirements, we exclude regions of the $(m_χ,m_U)$ plane for each DM realization (Dirac, Majorana, or complex scalar) and identify benchmark scenarios in which heavy-ion, cosmological, and astrophysical constraints are simultaneously satisfied.

## Key claim

The paper derives combined constraints on dark photon kinetic mixing \(\varepsilon^2\) by simultaneously requiring consistency with heavy-ion dilepton data (via the PHSD transport model), thermal relic abundance (\(\Omega_{\rm DM}h^2 \simeq 0.12\)), and velocity-dependent self-interaction cross sections bounded by dwarf galaxies, galaxy groups, and clusters; the limits depend on whether the dark photon decays visibly (\(m_U < 2m_\chi\)) or invisibly (\(m_U > 2m_\chi\)), and on the spin of the dark matter particle (Dirac fermion, Majorana fermion, or complex scalar).

## Method

The authors extended the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to include dark photon production through Dalitz decays of light mesons (\(\pi^0, \eta, \eta', \omega\)), Delta resonances (\(\Delta \to N U\)), direct vector meson decays (\(\rho, \omega, \phi \to U\)), kaon decays (\(K^+ \to \pi^+ U\)), and \(q\bar{q} \to U\) annihilation. They extracted upper limits on \(\varepsilon^2(m_U, m_\chi, \alpha_\chi)\) by comparing the predicted dark-photon-induced dilepton yield to the standard model background in heavy-ion collisions, separately for the visible regime (\(m_U < 2m_\chi\)) and the invisible regime (\(m_U > 2m_\chi\)). Cosmological and astrophysical constraints were incorporated by computing the velocity-dependent self-interaction cross section \(\sigma/m_\chi\) for Yukawa-mediated SIDM using the CLASSICS code and confronting it with bounds from dwarf galaxies, galaxy groups, and clusters, and by determining thermal relic target curves using the ReD-DeLiVeR code requiring \(\Omega_{\rm DM}h^2 \simeq 0.12\).

## Key numbers

- Thermal relic abundance target: \(\Omega_{\rm DM}h^2 \simeq 0.12\), consistent with Planck measurements of the cosmic microwave background.
- Self-interaction cross section range of interest: \(\sigma/m_\chi \sim 0.1\)–\(10~\mathrm{cm^2\,g^{-1}}\) for alleviating small-scale structure tensions.
- Invariant-mass bin width used in PHSD analysis: \(\mathrm{d}M = 10\) MeV.
- Dark photon mass range probed by heavy-ion dilepton measurements: \(m_U > 2m_e\) (kinematic threshold for \(U \to e^+e^-\)) up to a few GeV.
- Kinetic mixing parameter range considered compatible with precision electroweak data: \(\varepsilon\) in the range \(10^{-7}\)–\(10^{-2}\).
- Dark coupling benchmark used for branching fraction illustrations: \(\alpha_\chi = 0.1\) (Figure 3) and \(\alpha_\chi = 0.05\) (Figure 4, right panel).

## Context

This work extends previous PHSD-based studies by Schmidt (2021), Bratkovskaya (2022), and Romero Jorge (2024, 2025) that derived limits on \(\varepsilon^2(m_U)\) in the visible regime only, by now incorporating invisible decays to dark matter and combining with cosmological and astrophysical constraints. It builds on the vector portal framework first proposed by Holdom (1985) and connects heavy-ion observables to thermal freeze-out and self-interacting dark matter dynamics, enabling identification of benchmark scenarios where heavy-ion, cosmological, and astrophysical constraints are simultaneously satisfied.
