# A study of the fine-structure constant dependence of radiative capture in Halo-EFT

> We study the fine-structure constant dependence of the rates of some selected radiative capture reactions within the framework of so-called Halo Effective Field Theory in order to assess the adequacy of some assumptions made on the Coulomb penetrability. We find that this dependence deviates from that implied by a parameterization of the cross sections of this effect via a simple penetration factor. Some features of this fine-structure dependence are discussed, in particular its potential impact on the abundances of the light elements in primordial nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The fine-structure constant dependence of radiative capture cross sections in Halo-EFT deviates from the simple penetration-factor parameterization used in earlier work (Meissner 2023), meaning that the assumptions made about Coulomb penetrability in that prior study are not fully adequate for all reactions in the Big Bang nucleosynthesis network.

## Method

The authors computed the cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for four radiative capture reactions (n + 7Li → 8Li + γ, p + 7Be → 8B + γ, 3H + 4He → 7Li + γ, and 3He + 4He → 7Be + γ) using Halo Effective Field Theory, which treats the nuclear system as a core plus a valence nucleon or cluster. They evaluated the electric dipole and magnetic dipole contributions using effective range expansions with parameters from prior Halo-EFT analyses (Fernando 2011, Higa 2020, Higa 2022, Premarathna 2019), then computed the α-dependence by varying the fine-structure constant in the Coulomb functions and binding momenta while keeping the strong-interaction parameters fixed.

## Key numbers

- The nominal fine-structure constant used is α₀ = 7.2973525693(11) × 10⁻³ = 1/137.035999084(2) (Workman 2022)
- For the p + 7Be → 8B + γ reaction, the 1⁺ resonance position is E_R = 0.630(3) MeV with width Γ_R = 0.0357(6) MeV
- Binding momenta for the final states range from γ = 14.9465 MeV (⁸B ground state) to γ = 88.9099 MeV (⁷Li ground state)
- The s-wave scattering lengths for the 3H + 4He → 7Li + γ reaction are a₀ = 17.0 fm (fit parameter set) and a₀ = 13.0 fm (model A parameter set)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Meissner (2023) on the fine-structure constant dependence of light element abundances in Big Bang nucleosynthesis by providing a more rigorous theoretical treatment of the radiative capture reactions that were previously parameterized using simple penetration factors. The Halo-EFT framework, reviewed by Hammer, Ji and Phillips (2017), allows for an analytic study of the α-dependence that can assess the validity of the earlier assumptions. The results enable more reliable predictions for how variations in the fine-structure constant would affect primordial abundances, which is relevant for tests of fundamental constant variation in cosmology.
