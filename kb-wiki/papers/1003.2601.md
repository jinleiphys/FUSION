# Thermal Conductivity and Sound Attenuation in Dilute Atomic Fermi Gases

> We compute the thermal conductivity and sound attenuation length of a dilute atomic Fermi gas in the framework of kinetic theory. Above the critical temperature for superfluidity, T_c, the quasi-particles are fermions, whereas below T_c, the dominant excitations are phonons. We calculate the thermal conductivity in both cases. We find that at unitarity the thermal conductivity κin the normal phase scales as κ~ T^{3/2}. In the superfluid phase we find κ~ T^{2}. At high temperature the Prandtl number, the ratio of the momentum and thermal diffusion constants, is 2/3. The ratio increases as the temperature is lowered. As a consequence we expect sound attenuation in the normal phase just above T_c to be dominated by shear viscosity. We comment on the possibility of extracting the shear viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity using measurements of the sound absorption length.

## Key claim

The paper computes the thermal conductivity and sound attenuation length of a dilute atomic Fermi gas using kinetic theory, finding that at unitarity the thermal conductivity scales as κ ∝ T^(3/2) in the normal phase and κ ∝ T^2 in the superfluid phase, with the caveat that these results do not match well near the critical temperature T_c, suggesting additional processes or excitations become relevant in that regime.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation using a variational method with orthogonal polynomial trial functions, treating fermionic quasiparticles above T_c and phonon excitations below T_c. For the normal phase, they compute the collision integral using the s-wave scattering cross section σ = a^2/(1 + a^2 q^2) for atom-atom scattering, while for the superfluid phase they use the three-phonon interaction matrix element from the Son-Wingate effective lagrangian for the phonon field.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity in the normal phase, thermal conductivity κ ∝ T^(3/2) for T ≫ T_F
- In the superfluid phase at unitarity, κ ∝ T^2
- At high temperature (T ≫ T_F), the Prandtl number Pr = 2/3, independent of scattering length
- In the BCS limit (|a| → 0) at high temperature, κ = (75/(128√π)) √(T/m) (1/a^2)
- In the unitarity limit (|a| → ∞) at high temperature, κ = (225/(128√π)) m^(1/2) T^(3/2)
- In the BCS limit at high temperature, η = (5√(mT))/(32 a^2 √π)
- In the unitarity limit at high temperature, η = (15 (mT)^(3/2))/(32 √π)
- In the superfluid phase, shear viscosity η_H = 5.06 × 10^(-6) ξ^(7/2) (T_F/T)^5 η_0, where ξ ≃ 0.44
- The critical temperature at unitarity is T_c ≃ 0.15 T_F

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of shear and bulk viscosity in dilute Fermi gases by Bruun (2005) and Rupak and Schaefer (2007), and complements calculations of bulk viscosities in the superfluid phase by Escobedo (2009). The results enable extraction of shear viscosity from sound attenuation measurements in atomic traps, building on first sound observations by Joseph (2006) and proposals for second sound detection by Taylor (2009). The holographic duality results of Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) and Son and Starinets (2007) provide context for the strong-coupling limit of η/s ≈ ℏ/(4π k_B).
