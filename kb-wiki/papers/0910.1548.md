# Classical Strongly Coupled QGP: VII. Energy Loss

> We use linear response analysis and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to derive the energy loss of a heavy quark in the SU(2) classical Coulomb plasma in terms of the $l=1$ monopole and non-static structure factor. The result is valid for all Coulomb couplings $Γ=V/K$, the ratio of the mean potential to kinetic energy. We use the Liouville equation in the collisionless limit to assess the SU(2) non-static structure factor. We find the energy loss to be strongly dependent on $Γ$. In the liquid phase with $Γ\approx 4$, the energy loss is mostly metallic and soundless with neither a Cerenkov nor a Mach cone. Our analytical results compare favorably with the SU(2) molecular dynamics simulations at large momentum and for heavy quark masses.

## Key claim

The energy loss of a heavy quark in the SU(2) classical Coulomb plasma is derived using linear response and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, and is found to be strongly dependent on the Coulomb coupling Γ; in the liquid phase near Γ≈4, the loss is mostly metallic and soundless, with neither a Cerenkov nor a Mach cone, and the analytical results compare favorably with SU(2) molecular dynamics simulations at large momentum and for heavy quark masses.

## Method

The authors use linear response theory and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem in the classical limit to express the heavy quark energy loss in terms of the l=1 monopole and non-static structure factor of the SU(2) colored Coulomb plasma. They solve the Liouville equation in the collisionless limit to obtain the non-static structure factor, and then derive the longitudinal dielectric function ε₁(ω,k) from which the energy loss integral is evaluated. The energy loss is computed for charm and bottom quarks at Coulomb couplings Γ=2, 3, and 4, using the analytic static structure factor S₀₁(k) obtained from the generalized Ornstein-Zernicke equation.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss formula is valid for all Coulomb couplings Γ = V/K, the ratio of mean potential to kinetic energy.
- For the SU(2) colored Coulomb plasma, the Debye wave number squared is k_D², and the plasmon frequency is ω_p = v_T k_D with v_T = √(T/m).
- The SU(2) Casimir for a heavy quark probe is C_F = 3/4, and for thermal constituent gluons C₂ = 2 with mass m ≈ πT.
- The Wigner-Seitz radius a_WS = 0.6883 β for a density dominated by black-body gluon radiation n = 0.244 × 3/β³.
- The energy loss for charm and bottom is shown for Γ = 2, 3, and 4 around the SU(2) liquid point, with the loss exceeding 1 for Γ=4 when normalized to total kinetic energy per length.
- For the comparison with molecular dynamics, Γ ≈ 4.8 is used (with n = 1/λ³ and λ = 1/3T), and analytical results at Γ=4 compare favorably with simulations at large momenta and for heavier quark masses (bottom).

## Context

This work extends the classical strongly coupled QGP (cQGP) model developed by Cho and Zahed (2009) and Gelman et al. (2006), which models the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma using classical colored constituents interacting via strong Coulomb interactions. The analytical framework provides a derivation for the energy loss observed in SU(2) molecular dynamics simulations by Dusling and Zahed (2009). The results contrast with holographic QCD calculations by Herzog et al. (2006) and Chesler and Yaffe (2007) that reported Mach cones, likely because those holographic jets are colorless; the authors note that colorless (mesonic) jets would couple to the sound channel through the S₀₀(k) structure factor and are expected to produce a Mach cone.
