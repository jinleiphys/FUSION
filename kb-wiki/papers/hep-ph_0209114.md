# Reanalysis of Azimuthal Spin Asymmetries of Meson Electroproduction

> The azimuthal spin asymmetries for pion production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of unpolarized charged lepton beams on longitudinally polarized nucleon targets, are reanalyzed by taking into account an important sign correction to previous formulas. It is found that different approaches of distribution functions and fragmentation functions may lead to distinct predictions on the azimuthal asymmetries measured in the HERMES experiments, thus the available data cannot be considered as a direct measurement of quark transversity distributions, although they still can serve to provide useful information on these distributions and on T-odd fragmentation functions. Predictions of the azimuthal spin asymmetries for kaon production are also presented, with different approaches of distribution and fragmentation functions. The unfavored fragmentation functions cannot be neglected for $K^-$ and $K^0_S$ production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic processes.

## Key claim

The azimuthal spin asymmetries for pion production measured by HERMES cannot be considered a direct measurement of quark transversity distributions, because different approaches for distribution and fragmentation functions lead to distinct predictions; however, the data can still provide useful information on transversity distributions and T-odd fragmentation functions, particularly at small x.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the azimuthal spin asymmetry \(A_{UL}^{\sin\phi}\) for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of unpolarized charged leptons on longitudinally polarized nucleon targets, incorporating a sign correction to the \(\Sigma_{2T}\) term in the theoretical formula. They calculated predictions using three approaches for the distribution and fragmentation functions (Leading Approach, Approach 1, and Approach 2) with two model parametrizations for the parton distributions (a quark-diquark model and a pQCD based analysis), and compared results for pion and kaon production on proton, deuteron, and neutron targets.

## Key numbers

- The analyzing power \(A_{UL}^{\sin\phi}\) was calculated for pion and kaon production using target polarizations \(S=0.86\) for the proton and \(S=0.75\) for the deuteron and neutron targets.
- The Collins fragmentation function parametrization used a hadronic scale \(M_C = 0.7\) GeV and a mean-square transverse momentum \(\langle P_{h\perp}^2 \rangle = (0.44)^2\) GeV\(^2\).
- For kaon fragmentation functions, the favored fragmentation function for \(K^{\pm}\) is given by \(D^{K^{\pm}}(z) = 0.31 z^{-0.98}(1-z)^{0.97}\) and the unfavored by \(\hat{D}^{K^{\pm}}(z) = 1.08 z^{-0.82}(1-z)^{2.55}\).
- For \(K_S^0\), the favored fragmentation function is \(D^{K_S^0}(z) = 0.53 z^{-0.57}(1-z)^{1.87}\) and the unfavored is \(\hat{D}^{K_S^0}(z) = 1.45 z^{-0.62}(1-z)^{3.84}\).

## Context

This work corrects a sign error in the formulas used in earlier calculations by Efremov et al. (2000), Ma, Schmidt, and Yang (2001), and others, which significantly changes the predicted azimuthal asymmetries. The analysis extends previous work by providing predictions for kaon production and showing that unfavored fragmentation functions are important for \(K^-\) and \(K_S^0\) production, in contrast to the predictions of Efremov, Goeke, and Schweitzer (2002). The results enable future experimental studies at HERMES to discriminate between different models of transversity distributions and Collins fragmentation functions, and also point to the need to consider alternative mechanisms such as QCD final-state interactions proposed by Brodsky, Hwang, and Schmidt (2002) and Collins (2002).
