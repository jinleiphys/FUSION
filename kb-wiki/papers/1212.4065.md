# The soliton-soliton interaction in the Chiral Dilaton Model

> We study the interaction between two B = 1 states in the Chiral Dilaton Model where baryons are described as non-topological solitons arising from the interaction of chiral mesons and quarks. By using the hedgehog solution for the B = 1 states we construct, via a product ansatz, three possible B = 2 configurations to analyse the role of the relative orientation of the hedgehog quills in the dynamics of the soliton-soliton interaction and investigate the behaviour of these solutions in the range of long/intermediate distance. One of the solutions is quite binding due to the dynamics of the π and σ fields at intermediate distance and should be used for nuclear matter studies. Since the product ansatz breaks down as the two solitons get close, we explore the short range distance regime with a model that describes the interaction via a six-quark bag ansatz. We calculate the interaction energy as a function of the inter-soliton distance and show that for small separations the six quarks bag, assuming a hedgehog structure, provides a stable bound state that at large separations connects with a special configuration coming from the product ansatz.

# The soliton-soliton interaction in the Chiral Dilaton Model

## Key claim

The Chiral Dilaton Model produces a B=2 bound state with approximately 8 MeV binding energy, but only in one specific isospin configuration (configuration C) where the pion and sigma fields provide strong attraction at intermediate distances; the other configurations (A and B) are fundamentally repulsive. The short-range behavior requires a six-quark bag ansatz because the product ansatz breaks down due to Pauli blocking, and only the A configuration connects smoothly to the hedgehog six-quark bag solution at zero separation.

## Method

The authors constructed B=2 configurations from two B=1 hedgehog solitons using a product ansatz for the chiral meson fields, with three different relative isospin orientations (A, B, C) implemented via SU(2) rotation matrices. For the short-distance regime where the product ansatz fails, they employed a variational six-quark bag ansatz with Gaussian trial wavefunctions for the quarks and hyperbolic-tangent profiles for the meson fields, minimizing the energy as a function of the inter-soliton distance. The interaction energy was calculated as the difference between the B=2 total energy and twice the B=1 energy at each separation.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of the B=1 soliton: 1175.6 MeV (for parameters m_sigma=550 MeV, m_pi=139 MeV, g=5, f_pi=93 MeV)
- Quark eigenvalue for B=1: 83.1 MeV
- Quark kinetic energy contribution to B=1: 1138.0 MeV
- Sigma field contribution to B=1: 334.5 MeV
- Pion field contribution to B=1: 486.0 MeV
- Quark-sigma interaction energy in B=1: -101.4 MeV
- Quark-pion interaction energy in B=1: -787.0 MeV
- Six-quark bag total energy at zero separation: 2344 MeV
- Interaction energy V_int for six-quark bag at zero separation: -7.2 MeV
- Quark eigenvalues for six-quark bag: epsilon_0 = -30.6 MeV, epsilon_1 = 344.8 MeV
- Crossing distance between configuration A and six-quark bag: approximately 1 fm
- Attraction in configuration A occurs around d = 1.5 fm (a few MeV)
- Attraction in configuration B occurs around d = 1.2 fm
- Attraction in configuration C occurs above d = 1.4 fm
- Bag radius from sigma field: approximately 0.5 fm
- Variational parameter R_c for six-quark bag: approximately 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to dense matter (Park 2003, Lee 2003, Park 2008, Park 2009) by incorporating explicit quark degrees of freedom coupled to chiral mesons, following the Chiral Dilaton Model developed by Bonanno (2008) and Drago (2011). The product ansatz methodology parallels that used in Skyrme model studies of skyrmion-skyrmion interactions, but the CDM includes quark Pauli repulsion at short distances rather than vector meson repulsion. The six-quark bag variational approach follows the work of Blattel (1987) and Birse (1985). A more complete numerical solution for the B=2 system in a chiral quark-soliton model was performed by Sawado (1998, 2000), but the authors note that approach is difficult to implement at this stage. The configuration C is identified as the one that should be used for nuclear matter studies, analogous to the attractive channel in the Skyrme model that leads to phase transitions in dense matter.
