# Neutrino decoupling including flavour oscillations and primordial nucleosynthesis

> We revisit the decoupling of neutrinos in the early universe with flavour oscillations. We rederive the quantum kinetic equations which determine the neutrino evolution based on a BBGKY-like hierarchy, and include for the first time the full collision term, with both on- and off-diagonal terms for all relevant reactions. We focus on the case of zero chemical potential and solve these equations numerically. We also develop an approximate scheme based on the adiabatic evolution in the matter basis. In fact, the large difference between the oscillations and cosmological time scales allows to consider averaged flavour oscillations which can speed up the numerical integration by two orders of magnitude, when combined with a direct computation of the differential system Jacobian. The approximate numerical scheme is also useful to gain more insight into the physics of neutrino decoupling. Including the most recent results on plasma thermodynamics QED corrections, we update the effective number of neutrinos to $N_{\mathrm{eff}} = 3.0440$. Finally we study the impact of flavour oscillations during neutrino decoupling on the subsequent primordial nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new calculation of the effective number of neutrinos, finding \(N_{\mathrm{eff}} = 3.0440\), which updates the previously standard value of 3.046; this result includes for the first time the full collision term with both on- and off-diagonal contributions for all relevant reactions, though the calculation is restricted to the case of zero chemical potential.

## Method

The authors derive the quantum kinetic equations (QKEs) for neutrino flavour evolution in the early universe using a BBGKY-like hierarchy, extending prior work to include the complete collision integral for neutrino-electron scattering, neutrino-antineutrino annihilation, and neutrino self-interactions with full matrix structure. They solve these equations numerically for the case of zero chemical potential, and also develop an approximate adiabatic scheme that exploits the large separation between oscillation and cosmological timescales to average flavour oscillations, speeding up numerical integration by two orders of magnitude. The calculation incorporates QED corrections to the plasma equation of state and uses comoving variables with the photon temperature as a proxy for the scale factor.

## Key numbers

* Effective number of neutrinos: \(N_{\mathrm{eff}} = 3.0440\), including QED corrections and full flavour oscillation treatment.
* The approximate adiabatic scheme speeds up numerical integration by two orders of magnitude compared to the full solution.
* The baryon-to-photon ratio used is \(\eta \equiv n_b/n_\gamma \simeq 6.1\times 10^{-9}\), from Planck 2018 measurements.
* Neutrino decoupling occurs when the temperature drops below approximately \(2 \, \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The standard ratio for neutrino and photon temperatures after decoupling is \(T_\gamma / T_\nu = (11/4)^{1/3}\) in the instantaneous decoupling approximation.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of neutrino decoupling with flavour oscillations by Mangano (2005), Gariazzo (2019), and Akita (2020), which approximated some collision terms by neglecting off-diagonal components or using damping approximations. The full collision term treatment and the new \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\) value provide more precise initial conditions for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis calculations (building on the authors' prior work Froustey (2019) without oscillations) and for CMB analyses such as Planck 2018, which used \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}=3.046\) and found \(N_{\mathrm{eff}} = 2.99 \pm 0.17\) at 68% confidence.
