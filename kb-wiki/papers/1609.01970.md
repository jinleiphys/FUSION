# Tagged spectator DIS on a polarized spin-1 target

> We discuss the process of deep-inelastic electron scattering (DIS) on the polarized deuteron with detection of a nucleon in the nuclear fragmentation region ("spectator tagging"). We cover (a) the general structure of the semi-inclusive DIS cross section on a spin-1 target; (b) the tagged structure functions in the impulse approximation, where deuteron structure is described by the $NN$ light-front wave function; (c) the extraction of free neutron structure through on-shell extrapolation in the recoil proton momentum. As an application we consider the extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1n}$ through polarized electron scattering on the longitudinally polarized deuteron with proton tagging and on-shell extrapolation. Such measurements would be possible at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) with polarized deuteron beams and forward proton detectors.

# Tagged spectator DIS on a polarized spin-1 target

## Key claim

Tagged spectator deep-inelastic scattering on a polarized deuteron, combined with on-shell extrapolation to the nucleon pole, can provide a model-independent extraction of the free neutron spin structure function \(g_{1n}\) over a wide kinematic range at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), provided that final-state interactions and nuclear shadowing effects are further studied.

## Method

The authors develop the general formalism for semi-inclusive DIS on a spin-1 target with spectator nucleon detection, expressing the cross section in terms of 41 structure functions (5 unpolarized, 13 vector polarized, 23 tensor polarized). They compute observables in the impulse approximation using the light-front deuteron wave function, which includes Melosh rotations and both S-wave and D-wave components, and evaluate the double spin asymmetry \(A_\parallel\) for longitudinally polarized electrons on a longitudinally vector-polarized deuteron target with proton tagging.

## Key numbers

- The spectator nucleon is detected with low momentum of a few 100 MeV in the deuteron rest frame.
- The BONuS experiment measured spectator protons down to momenta of 70 MeV.
- The Deeps experiment measured at proton momenta of 300-600 MeV.
- The D-wave effects on the asymmetry are largest in the tensor-force-dominated region of recoil momenta 300-500 MeV.
- The asymmetry becomes flat for recoil momenta \(\leq 100\) MeV, making the on-shell extrapolation robust in the impulse approximation.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of tagged spectator DIS from unpolarized measurements (pioneered by the Deeps experiment at JLab (Klimenko, 2005) and the BONuS experiment at JLab (Baillie, 2011; Tkachenko, 2014)) to the polarized case, enabling extraction of neutron spin structure. The pole extrapolation method (Sargsian, 2005) is applied to the double spin asymmetry, where the nucleon pole cancels between numerator and denominator, making the extraction robust. The results enable future measurements at a polarized EIC (specifically the JLEIC figure-8 ring design) to determine \(g_{1n}\) over a wide kinematic range, though further theoretical work on final-state interactions and nuclear shadowing is needed.
