# Microscopic study of neutrino trapping in hyperon stars

> Employing the most recent parametrization of the baryon-baryon interaction of the Nijmegen group, we investigate, in the framework of the Brueckner--Bethe--Goldstone many-body theory at zero temperature, the influence of neutrino trapping on the composition, equation of state, and structure of neutron stars, relevant to describe the physical conditions of a neutron star immediately after birth (protoneutron star). We find that the presence of neutrinos changes significantly the composition of matter delaying the appearance of hyperons and making the equation of state stiffer. We explore the consequences of neutrino trapping on the early evolution of a neutron star and on the nature of the final compact remnant left by the supernova explosion.

## Key claim

Neutrino trapping in hyperonic matter delays the onset of hyperon formation and stiffens the equation of state, but the maximum mass of hyperon stars decreases during deleptonization, creating a window of metastable protoneutron stars that may collapse into low-mass black holes after neutrinos diffuse out.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner–Bethe–Goldstone (BBG) many-body theory at zero temperature, using the most recent Nijmegen parametrization of the baryon-baryon interaction for the complete baryon octet. They solved the Bethe–Goldstone equation self-consistently for nucleon-nucleon, hyperon-nucleon, and hyperon-hyperon G-matrices, and incorporated nucleonic three-body forces via the Heiselberg–Hjorth-Jensen parametrization of the Argonne V18 interaction. The composition of beta-stable matter was determined by imposing charge neutrality, chemical equilibrium, and lepton number conservation (Y_Le = 0.4, Y_Lμ = 0) for the trapped case, and the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations were solved to obtain stellar structure.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino-trapped matter, the Σ⁻ hyperon appears at a baryon number density of approximately 0.50 fm⁻³, while the Λ and Σ⁰ appear at about 0.57 fm⁻³ and 1.02 fm⁻³, respectively.
- The maximum gravitational mass for neutrino-trapped hyperon stars is 1.595 M⊙, with a central energy density of 2.30 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³ and a central baryon density of 1.066 fm⁻³.
- The maximum gravitational mass for neutrino-free hyperon stars is 1.283 M⊙, with a central energy density of 3.19 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³ and a central baryon density of 1.537 fm⁻³.
- The radius of the maximum-mass configuration is 11.14 km for the trapped case and 9.86 km for the free case.
- Protoneutron stars with gravitational masses between 1.28 and 1.59 M⊙ (baryonic masses between 1.40 and 1.72 M⊙) are metastable.
- For a constant baryonic mass of 1.34 M⊙, the energy carried away by neutrinos during deleptonization is about 9 × 10⁵² erg.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of cold, deleptonized hyperon stars by Baldo et al. (2000) and Vidaña et al. (2000a) to the protoneutron star stage with trapped neutrinos. It contrasts with earlier studies of neutrino trapping in hyperonic matter that used relativistic mean-field models (Keil and Janka 1994, Prakash et al. 1997). The finding that the maximum mass decreases during deleptonization when hyperons are present is opposite to the behavior in purely nucleonic matter (Bombaci 1996, Prakash et al. 1997), and it enables predictions of metastable neutron stars and delayed black hole formation with masses in the 1–2 M⊙ range.
