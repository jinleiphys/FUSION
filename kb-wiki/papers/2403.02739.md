# Implementation of the microscopic nuclear potential in the coupled channels calculations to study the fusion dynamics of Oxygen based reactions

> In the present work, we have incorporated the microscopic relativistic nuclear potential obtained from recently developed relativistic R3Y NN potential in the coupled channels code CCFULL to study the fusion dynamics. The R3Y NN-potential and the densities of interacting nuclei are obtained for the relativistic mean-field approach for the NL3$^*$ parameter set. It is to be noted that the R3Y NN potential can be expressed in terms of masses of the mesons and their couplings by considering the meson degrees of freedom within the relativistic mean field, which has a form similar to the widely used M3Y potential. We focused on the fusion cross-sections for $Oxygen$-based reactions with targets from different mass regions of the periodic table i.e. $^{16}$O + $^{24}$Mg, $^{18}$O + $^{24}$Mg, $^{16}$O + $^{148}$Sm, $^{16}$O + $^{176}$Hf, $^{16}$O + $^{176}$Yb, $^{16}$O + $^{182}$W, and $^{16}$O + $^{186}$W. A comparison is also made with the ones calculated using the nuclear potential obtained from the traditional Woods-Saxon potential and the widely used M3Y NN potential within CCFULL. The coupled channel calculations are performed with shape and rotational degrees of freedom to examine the fusion enhancement at below-barrier energies. It is observed from the calculations that the fusion cross-sections obtained using R3Y NN potential with rotational degrees of freedom are found to be more consistent with the experimental data than those for the M3Y and Woods-Saxon potentials mainly at below barrier energies.

## Key claim
The authors claim that incorporating the microscopic relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential (derived from the relativistic mean-field approach with the NL3* parameter set) into the coupled channels code CCFULL yields fusion cross-sections that are more consistent with experimental data than those obtained using the traditional Woods-Saxon potential or the non-relativistic M3Y potential, particularly at below-barrier energies. This consistency is conditional on including rotational degrees of freedom (up to the 4+ state) for deformed target nuclei.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the NL3* parameter set to compute both the nuclear densities of the interacting nuclei and the relativistic R3Y NN potential, expressed in terms of meson masses and coupling constants. This microscopic nuclear potential was then used as input to the coupled channels code CCFULL, which solves the coupled channels equations including rotational degrees of freedom (quadrupole β2 and hexadecapole β4 deformations) for the target nuclei. Fusion cross-sections were calculated for seven oxygen-based reactions (16O + 24Mg, 18O + 24Mg, 16O + 148Sm, 16O + 176Hf, 16O + 176Yb, 16O + 182W, and 16O + 186W) and compared with results obtained using the Woods-Saxon and M3Y potentials, as well as with experimental data.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same group (Bhuyan et al., 2018, 2020; Shil et al., 2021) that used the R3Y NN potential with the l-summed Wong formula for fusion and capture cross-sections, but were limited to spherical symmetry. The novelty is the implementation of the microscopic R3Y potential within the coupled channels code CCFULL, allowing inclusion of rotational degrees of freedom for deformed target nuclei. This enables more realistic treatment of fusion dynamics at sub-barrier energies compared to the empirical Woods-Saxon potential (Akyuz-Winther parameterization) and the non-relativistic M3Y potential (Satchler and Love, 1979). The approach can be applied to study fusion reactions involving exotic nuclei far from the beta-stable region.
