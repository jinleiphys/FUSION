# Fluctuations and initial state granularity in heavy ion collisions and their effects on observables from hydrodynamics

> A comparison is made between results obtained using smooth initial conditions and event-by-event initial conditions in the hydrodynamical description of relativistic nuclear collisions. Some new results on directed flow are also included.

# Fluctuations and initial state granularity in heavy ion collisions and their effects on observables from hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions (hot tubes) from NeXus, rather than smooth averaged initial conditions, produce measurable differences in hydrodynamic observables such as the high-pt part of the pt distribution, the flattening of v2(pt), the absence of a shoulder in v2(eta), and the appearance of a ridge in two-particle correlations; however, the effects are presented as comparisons to smooth-initial-condition results, and some results (particularly on directed flow) are described as preliminary.

## Method

The authors used the SPheRIO code, based on Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics, to solve ideal hydrodynamics with initial conditions provided by the NeXus microscopic model, normalized by an eta-dependent factor to reproduce dNch/deta in each centrality window. They compared results from a single run with smooth initial conditions (the standard approach) against event-by-event averages over a set of NeXus initial conditions, using an equation of state with a critical point and a freeze-out temperature fixed by dNch/pt dpt.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pt distribution: the high-pt part is lifted when using event-by-event initial conditions compared to smooth initial conditions (figure 2, left).
- Elliptic flow v2(pt): the pt dependence is flatter for event-by-event initial conditions than for smooth initial conditions (figure 2, center).
- Elliptic flow v2(eta): no shoulder is observed for event-by-event initial conditions, in contrast to smooth initial conditions (figure 2, right).
- Directed flow v1(eta) for charged particles: NeXSPheRIO results are in quantitative agreement with PHOBOS for |eta| < 3 (figure 3, left) and in qualitative agreement with STAR for |eta| < 3, but the turnover occurs at smaller eta than for STAR (figure 3, right).
- Directed flow v1(eta) shows little dependence on system size (Au+Au vs Cu+Cu) in centrality windows from 6-15% to 45-55% at 200 A GeV (figure 4).
- For identified particles, protons show a big wiggle in v1(eta) while pions show a plateau (figure 5, left).
- Directed flow v1(eta) has a plateau for fluctuating initial conditions and a somewhat stronger negative inclination for smooth initial conditions (figure 5, right).

## Context

This work extends the comparison between smooth and event-by-event initial conditions in hydrodynamic descriptions of relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC, building on prior studies by the same group (Andrade et al. 2004, 2006, 2008) and others (Hama et al. 2005) that used the NeXSPheRIO approach. The results show that fluctuating initial conditions can produce the ridge effect in two-particle correlations (observed by STAR) and v2 fluctuations, which do not appear with smooth initial conditions, and provide a framework for further studies of directed flow at RHIC energies, including comparisons with microscopic models such as RQMD and UrQMD.
