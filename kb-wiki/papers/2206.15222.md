# Onset of Electron Captures and Shallow Heating in Magnetars

> The loss of magnetic pressure accompanying the decay of the magnetic field in a magnetar may trigger exothermic electron captures by nuclei in the shallow layers of the stellar crust. Very accurate analytical formulas are obtained for the threshold density and pressure, as well as for the maximum amount of heat that can be possibly released, taking into account the Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion. These formulas are valid for arbitrary magnetic field strengths, from the weakly quantizing regime to the most extreme situation in which electrons are all confined to the lowest level. Numerical results are also presented based on experimental nuclear data supplemented with predictions from the Brussels-Montreal model HFB-24. This same nuclear model has been already employed to calculate the equation of state in all regions of magnetars.

## Key claim
The decay of a magnetar's magnetic field reduces magnetic pressure, which can trigger exothermic electron captures by nuclei in the shallow crustal layers. The paper provides very accurate analytical formulas for the threshold density, threshold pressure, and maximum possible heat release, valid for arbitrary magnetic field strengths from the weakly quantizing regime up to the extreme case where all electrons are confined to the lowest Landau level.

## Method
The authors derived analytical expressions for the threshold density and pressure of electron-capture reactions in a magnetar crust, incorporating the Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion in a strong magnetic field. They then evaluated these formulas numerically using experimental nuclear masses supplemented by predictions from the Brussels-Montreal nuclear model HFB-24, which had previously been used to compute the equation of state for all regions of magnetars.

## Key numbers
- The analytical formulas for threshold density, threshold pressure, and maximum heat release are valid for any magnetic field strength, from the weakly quantizing regime to the extreme case where all electrons occupy the lowest Landau level.
- Numerical results were obtained using experimental nuclear data combined with predictions from the HFB-24 model (the same model used earlier for the magnetar equation of state).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of electron captures in neutron star crusts by explicitly accounting for the quantizing effect of ultra-strong magnetic fields on electron motion, which had been neglected or treated approximately in earlier work (e.g., by Chamel and Fantina in prior studies of magnetar crusts). The derived formulas enable more accurate modeling of shallow heating in magnetars, which is relevant for understanding observed outbursts and cooling behavior. The results can be incorporated into future simulations of magnetar thermal evolution and magnetic field decay.
