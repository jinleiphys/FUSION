# Nuclear matter as a liquid phase of spontaneously broken semi-classical $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ chiral perturbation theory: Static chiral nucleon liquids

> We study effective field theories (EFT) of nuclear structure based on spontaneously broken global $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry of QCD with two massless quarks, i.e. $SU(2)χPT$. For ground-state nuclei, this EFT enables expansion and truncation in inverse powers of $Λ_{χSB}\simeq 1 GeV$, with analytic operators renormalized to all loop orders. We derive the EFT Lagrangian to order $Λ^0_{χSB}$. We show that $SU(2)χPT$ of protons, neutrons and pions admits a semi-classical "Static Chiral Nucleon Liquid" (Static$χ$NL) phase and that "Pion-less" $SU(2)χPT$ emerges in this liquid: far-infrared pions decouple from Static$χ$NL, vastly simplifying the derivation of saturated nuclear matter (the infinite liquid phase) and of finite microscopic liquid drops (ground-state nuclides). Static$χ$NL are made entirely of nucleons with even parity, total spin zero, and even $Z$ and $N$; local expectation values for spin and momenta vanish. They explain the power of pion-less $SU(2)χPT$ to capture experimental ground-state properties of certain nuclides, this explanation following directly from the global symmetries of QCD with two massless quarks. Mean-field Static$χ$NL non-topological solitons are true solutions of $SU(2)χPT$'s semi-classical symmetries: they obey all CVC and PCAC conservation laws and they have zero internal and external pressure. The nuclear liquid-drop model and the semi-empirical mass formula emerge -- with correct nuclear density and saturation and asymmetry energies -- in an explicit Thomas-Fermi construction. We relate our work to compatible and complementary work in pionless and in halo/ cluster EFTs, also composed entirely of nucleons and applied to light ($A\leq 6$) nuclei, which might provide important (<12.5%) corrections to Static$χNL$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within the framework of spontaneously broken \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) of protons, neutrons, and pions, a semi-classical "Static Chiral Nucleon Liquid" (Static\(\chi\)NL) phase exists in which infrared pions decouple from the ground state, provided the ground state is static, homogeneous, isotropic, and composed of fully paired nucleons with vanishing local spin and momentum expectation values.

## Method

The authors derived the \(SU(2)\chi\)PT Lagrangian to order \(\Lambda_{\chi SB}^0\) (i.e., including all analytic operators of order \(\Lambda_{\chi SB}\) and \(\Lambda_{\chi SB}^0\)) in the chiral limit of two massless quarks. They then took expectation values of the pion equations of motion and the nucleon Dirac equation in the hypothesized liquid ground state, showing that the nucleon current expectation values \(\langle J_\mu^\pm \rangle\), \(\langle J_\mu^{\pm,5} \rangle\), \(\langle J_i^3 \rangle\), and \(\langle J_\mu^{3,5} \rangle\) all vanish, leaving only \(\langle J_0^3 \rangle \neq 0\). By enforcing a static condition \(\{\partial_0 \vec{\pi}\}_{IR} = 0\) for the infrared pion field, they demonstrated that the classical pion source terms vanish, yielding a pionless effective Lagrangian composed solely of free nucleons and four-nucleon contact interactions.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_{\chi SB} \simeq 1\) GeV, used as the expansion parameter for operator power counting.
* The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 130.4 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.2\) MeV, with \(f_\pi \equiv F_\pi / \sqrt{2} = 92.2\) MeV.
* The up and down quark masses are given as \(m_u \simeq 6\) MeV and \(m_d \simeq 12\) MeV, and the pion mass as \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV, all compared to \(\Lambda_{\chi SB} \sim 1\) GeV.
* The paper states that pionless EFT corrections to Static\(\chi\)NL might provide "important (<12.5%) corrections" for light nuclei with \(A \leq 6\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of a chiral liquid phase first introduced by Lynn (1993), who proposed that \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) \(\chi\)PT could admit a liquid phase with saturated density and zero external pressure. It contrasts with earlier non-topological soliton models such as those of Lee and co-workers (1974-1975), Chin and Walecka (1974), and Serber (1976), as well as the Quantum Hadrodynamics-1 (QHD-1) model of Chin and Walecka (1977) and Serot (1986), which suffered from uncontrolled higher-loop renormalization as shown by Furnstahl et al. (1989). The paper demonstrates that the nuclear liquid-drop model and the Bethe-von Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula emerge naturally from the \(\chi\)PT framework with correct nuclear density, saturation energy, and asymmetry energy. This work enables a first-principles derivation of saturated nuclear matter and finite ground-state nuclides from QCD's global symmetries, and relates to compatible pionless and halo/cluster EFTs applied to light nuclei (\(A \leq 6\)).
