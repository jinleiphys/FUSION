# Search for the QCD Critical Point by Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions at RHIC

> Higher moments of net-proton multiplicity distributions are applied to search for the QCD critical point. In this paper, we will present measurements for kurtosis ($κ$), skewness ($S$) and variance $σ^2$) of net-proton multiplicity distributions at the mid-rapidity ($|y|<0.5$) and transverse momentum range $0.4<p_{T}<0.8$ {\gevc} for Au+Au collisions at {\sNN} = 7.7, 11.5, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV, Cu+Cu collisions at {\sNN} = 22.4, 62.4 and 200 GeV, $d$+Au collisions at {\sNN} = 200 GeV and p+p collisions at {\sNN} = 62.4 and 200 GeV. The moment products $κσ^2$ and $S σ$ of net-proton distributions, which are related to volume independent baryon number susceptibility ratios, are consistent with Lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations at high energies ({\sNN} = 62.4 and 200 GeV). Deviations of $κσ^2$ and $Sσ$ for the Au+Au collisions at low energies ({\sNN} = 7.7, 11.5 and 39 GeV) from HRG model calculations are also observed.

## Key claim

The moment products kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma of net-proton multiplicity distributions, which are volume-independent proxies for baryon number susceptibility ratios, are consistent with Lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations at high collision energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 and 200 GeV), but show deviations from HRG model predictions at lower energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV), potentially indicating critical point effects.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions (proton number minus antiproton number) at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c using the Time Projection Chamber for particle identification via ionization energy loss. The analysis computed the variance (sigma^2), skewness (S), and kurtosis (kappa) and formed the products kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma, which are related to the ratios of baryon number susceptibilities chi_B^(4)/chi_B^(2) and chi_B^(3)/chi_B^(2), respectively. Centrality bin width correction was applied to eliminate volume fluctuations, and statistical errors were evaluated using the Delta theorem method.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV; Cu+Cu at 22.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV; d+Au at 200 GeV; p+p at 62.4 and 200 GeV were analyzed.
- The transverse momentum range was 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c and rapidity range |y| < 0.5.
- For 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 and 200 GeV, kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma were consistent with Lattice QCD and HRG model calculations.
- For 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV, deviations of kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma from HRG model calculations were observed.
- The mean (M) and sigma of net-proton distributions increased monotonically with number of participants (N_part), while skewness (S) and kurtosis (kappa) decreased.

## Context

This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which aims to map the QCD phase diagram and search for the QCD critical point by varying collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV down to 7.7 GeV. The higher-moment approach connects experimental observables to thermodynamic susceptibilities calculable in Lattice QCD (as in the work of Cheng et al., 2009) and the Hadron Resonance Gas model, providing a quantitative method to probe bulk properties of hot dense nuclear matter. The results enable further searches at intermediate energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 and 27 GeV, data collected in 2011) and motivate theoretical work to explain the observed deviations from HRG model predictions at low energies.
