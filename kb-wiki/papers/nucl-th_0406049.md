# Cluster models of Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-9Be hypernuclei

> Configuration space Faddeev calculations are performed for the binding energy of Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-9Be bound states, here considered as alpha-Lambda-Lambda and alpha-alpha-Lambda clusters respectively, in order to study the dependence of the calculated binding energy on the alpha-Lambda potential input. For Lambda-Lambda-6He, using realistic interactions, the uncertainty in extracting the Lambda-Lambda S=L=0 interaction strength does not exceed 0.1 MeV, which is a fraction of the order of magnitude derived for other theoretical uncertainties. For Lambda-9Be, the dependence of the calculated binding energy on the alpha-Lambda potential is considerably larger, of order 1 MeV. Our results for Lambda-9Be suggest that the odd-state alpha-Lambda interaction is substantially reduced with respect to the even-state component.

# Cluster models of ΛΛ⁶He and Λ⁹Be hypernuclei

## Key claim

The uncertainty in extracting the ΛΛ interaction strength from ΛΛ⁶He binding energy calculations, due to the choice of αΛ potential, does not exceed 0.1 MeV when realistic αΛ potentials with both short-range repulsion and longer-range attraction are used; however, for Λ⁹Be the calculated binding energy depends on the αΛ potential by about 1 MeV, suggesting the odd-state (p-wave) αΛ interaction must be substantially weaker than the even-state (s-wave) component.

## Method

Configuration-space Faddeev equations were solved numerically for the three-body systems αΛΛ (ΛΛ⁶He) and ααΛ (Λ⁹Be), using local pairwise potentials for the αα, αΛ, and ΛΛ interactions. For ΛΛ⁶He, partial waves up to lαΛ=6 and lΛΛ=6 were included; for Λ⁹Be, partial waves up to l=5 were included, with the Coulomb repulsion between the two α clusters treated rigorously. The αΛ potentials tested included purely attractive s-wave forms (Tang-Herndon, Gibson), repulsive-core s-wave forms (Maeda-Schmid, Isle), and for the first time in Λ⁹Be calculations, an l-dependent potential (MSA) with separate s-wave and p-wave components inspired by the NSC97 model.

## Key numbers

- For ΛΛ⁶He, the incremental binding energy ΔBΛΛ from experiment is 1.0 ±0.2 +0.2/-0.1 MeV
- For Λ⁹Be, the dependence of the calculated binding energy on the αΛ potential is of order 1 MeV
- For ΛΛ⁶He, the uncertainty in δBΛΛ due to the αΛ potential type is less than 0.1 MeV
- For ΛΛ⁶He, the high partial-wave contributions of the αΛ potential total no more than about 0.2 MeV
- For Λ⁹Be using the MSA l-dependent potential, the calculated binding energy is reduced by about 0.75 MeV compared to the l-independent s-wave model
- For Λ⁹Be in the s-wave model, calculated binding energies range from 6 MeV to 8 MeV depending on the αΛ potential
- For Λ⁹Be, the αα pairing energy δBαα varies over a range of 3 MeV depending on the αΛ interaction used
- For Λ⁹Be, the excited (3/2+,5/2+) doublet splitting is less than 50 keV experimentally

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev calculations of Filikhin et al. (2003) for ΛΛ hypernuclei by systematically studying the model dependence of the αΛ interaction, and applies the MSA l-dependent αΛ potential of Myint et al. (2003) to Λ⁹Be for the first time. The results confirm the earlier finding of Hiyama et al. (1997) that the p-wave component of the αΛ interaction affects Λ⁹Be binding by up to 1 MeV, and suggest that three-body ααΛ repulsive forces of order 1 MeV may be needed to fully account for the binding energy. The work enables more reliable extraction of the ΛΛ interaction strength from the NAGARA event data of Takahashi et al. (2001), and provides benchmark Faddeev results against which future calculations including channel coupling or three-body forces can be compared.
