# Dispersion Laws for In-medium Fermions and Gluons in the CFL Phase of QCD

> We evaluate several quantities appearing in the effective lagrangian for the color-flavor locked phase of high density QCD using a formalism which exploits the approximate decoupling of fermions with energy negative with respect to the Fermi energy. The effective theory is essentially two-dimensional and exhibits a Fermi velocity superselection rule, similar to the one found in the Heavy Quark Effective Theory. Within the formalism we reproduce, using gradient expansion, the results for the effective parameters of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons. We also determine the dispersion laws for the gluons. By coupling the theory to fermions and integrating over the two-dimensional degrees of freedom we obtain the effective description of in-medium fermions.

## Key claim

The paper derives dispersion laws for gluons and in-medium fermions in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD at high density, using an effective two-dimensional theory on the Fermi surface. The key hedge is that the results are obtained in the limit of very high chemical potential \(\mu\) (i.e., \(\mu \to \infty\)) and at leading and next-to-leading order in \(1/\mu\), with the gap parameter \(\Delta\) treated as a fixed input; higher-order corrections in \(1/\mu\) could modify the numerical values.

## Method

The authors exploit the approximate decoupling of fermion states with energy far below the Fermi energy, projecting the Dirac Hamiltonian onto states near the Fermi surface using velocity-dependent fields \(\psi_+\) and \(\psi_-\). This yields an effective two-dimensional theory with a Fermi velocity superselection rule analogous to Heavy Quark Effective Theory. They then couple this theory to Nambu-Goldstone bosons (NGBs) and gluons in a \(SU(3)_L \otimes SU(3)_R \otimes SU(3)_c\) invariant manner, perform a gradient expansion of the generating functional at one-loop order, and integrate over the Fermi surface to obtain the effective parameters and dispersion laws.

## Key numbers

- Decay constant of the NGBs: \(F^2 = \frac{\mu^2 (21 - 8\ln 2)}{36\pi^2}\).
- NGB velocity: \(v = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
- Debye mass: \(m_D^2 = g_s^2 F^2 = \frac{\mu^2 g_s^2}{36\pi^2} (21 - 8\ln 2)\).
- Meissner mass: \(m_M^2 = \frac{m_D^2}{3}\).
- Rest mass of scalar, longitudinal, and transverse gluons: \(m^R_{A_0} = m^R_{A_L} = m^R_{A_T} \approx 1.70\,\Delta\).
- Inverse penetration length for \(A_0\): \(m^P_{A_0} \approx 2.94\,\Delta\).
- Effective mass for \(A_0\): \(m^*_{A_0} \approx 5.10\,\Delta\).
- Inverse penetration length for longitudinal gluons: \(m^P_{A_L} \approx 3.73\,\Delta\).
- Effective mass for longitudinal gluons: \(m^*_{A_L} \approx 8.19\,\Delta\).
- Effective mass for transverse gluons: \(m^*_{A_T} \approx -18.04\,\Delta\) (negative, indicating a maximum in the spectrum at zero momentum).
- Rest mass of in-medium fermions: \(\Delta\).
- Effective mass of in-medium fermions: \(m^* = 9\,\Delta\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory formalism for high-density QCD developed by Hong (2000) and Beane (2000) to the CFL phase, reproducing earlier results for NGB parameters obtained by Son (1999) using different methods. The paper provides a unified derivation of the Debye and Meissner masses, previously computed by Son (1999) and Zarembo (2000), and derives for the first time the full dispersion laws for scalar, longitudinal, and transverse gluons, as well as the effective description of in-medium fermions. The formalism enables further study of transport properties and the response of the CFL phase to external perturbations, though the authors note that applicability to realistic extreme matter conditions likely requires including additional perturbations.
