# Small damping approach in Fermi-liquid theory

> The validity of small damping approximation (SDA) for the quasi-classical description of the averaged properties of nuclei at high temperatures is studied within the framework of collisional kinetic theory. The isoscalar collective quadrupole vibrations in hot nuclei are considered. We show that the extension of the SDA, by accounting for the damping of the distribution function $δf$ in the collision integral reduces the rate of variation with temperature of the Fermi surface distortion effects. The damping of the $δf$ in the collision integral increases significantly the collisional width of the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) for small enough values of the relaxation time. The temperature dependence of the eigenenergy of the GQR becomes much more weaker than in the corresponding SDA case.

# Small damping approach in Fermi-liquid theory

## Key claim

The small damping approximation (SDA) overestimates the rate at which the eigenenergy of the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) decreases with temperature; extending the SDA by accounting for the damping of the distribution function δf in the collision integral (replacing energy-conserving δ-functions with Lorentzians) yields a much weaker temperature dependence of the GQR eigenenergy and increases the collisional width by up to 20% for small relaxation times.

## Method

The authors start from the linearized collisional kinetic equation for a finite Fermi-liquid drop and derive closed equations of motion for the density, velocity field, and pressure tensor, restricting the dynamic Fermi-surface distortion to multipolarity l=2. They obtain a dispersion equation for the complex eigenfrequency ω = ω_R − iω_I of isoscalar quadrupole vibrations, using a separable displacement field for irrotational flow. The collision integral is treated first in the small damping approximation (SDA) with a real-frequency relaxation time τ_SDA, and then extended by replacing the energy-conserving δ-functions with Lorentzians of width ħω_I, yielding a frequency- and width-dependent relaxation time τ. The resulting dispersion equation is solved numerically for a nucleus with A=224.

## Key numbers

- For A=224 (Z=88, valley of beta-stability), the surface tension coefficient is b_S = 17.2 MeV and the Coulomb energy coefficient is b_C = 0.7 MeV.
- The adopted nuclear radius parameter is r_0 = 1.12 fm.
- The parameter α = 9.2 MeV corresponds to an in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_in ≈ σ_free/2, where σ_free ≈ 40 mb.
- The additional contribution to the collisional width Γ from the damping of δf does not exceed 20% of the SDA value.
- For α = 4.6 MeV, the ratio ΔΓ/Γ shows non-monotonic behavior as a function of temperature because Γ_SDA has a bell-shaped form for moderate temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid theory treatment of nuclear collective vibrations by Landau (1957) and the collisional kinetic theory of Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1959), specifically addressing the zero- to first-sound transition in hot nuclei studied by Lipik et al. (1993) and Bapel et al. (1991). The authors previously developed the formalism for arbitrary multipolarity of Fermi-surface distortion (Kolomietz et al., 1992) and for memory effects in the collision integral (Kolomietz et al., 1995; Kiko et al., 1996). The present work enables more accurate predictions of the GQR properties at high temperatures and clarifies the regime where the SDA is inadequate, particularly for small relaxation times (small α).
