# Possible Production of the Sigma Meson in Nuclei

> It should be a typical subject in this workshop to explore possible change of hadron properties in association with that of QCD vacuum due to an environmental change. In this report, we first summarize what hadrons are expected to show such a change and how they do. Then we propose several experiments including the ones utilizing electron and photon beams for examining possible restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclei and the possible existence of the sigma meson. The experiments are based on the observation that the sigma meson may decrease the mass and the width along with the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the medium.

## Key claim

The sigma meson may decrease its mass and width in nuclear matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, making it potentially observable in nuclei even though it is elusive in vacuum; the paper proposes several experiments to produce the sigma meson in nuclei, but the actual existence of the sigma meson with a low mass (about 600 to 800 MeV) remains controversial.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical predictions from effective theories of QCD (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and the Walecka model, which show that the sigma meson mass decreases in hot and/or dense medium while the pion mass remains unchanged in the Nambu-Goldstone phase. The paper then proposes three types of nuclear reactions to produce the sigma meson: (1) pion absorption on a nucleon in a nucleus, (2) proton or light-ion collisions with a nucleon, and (3) electron scattering with virtual photon conversion, all followed by detection of four gamma rays from two neutral pions or dileptons from scalar-vector mixing.

## Key numbers

- The sigma meson mass in free space is predicted to be about 700 MeV, almost twice the constituent quark mass of about 350 MeV.
- The sigma meson decay width in vacuum is estimated as Gamma = 400 to 1000 MeV.
- Phase shift analysis of pi-pi scattering gives a pole with real part Re m_sigma = 500 to 700 MeV and imaginary part Im m_sigma = 500 to 800 MeV.
- A preliminary experimental result at KEK shows a bump around 600 MeV with a width of about 400 MeV in the reaction pi- p -> n pi0 pi0.
- The Walecka model predicts a decrease of m_sigma at nuclear density rho_0 reaching as large as 20% of the free-space value, even at zero temperature.
- The photon line and sigma dispersion relation overlap between q_1 about 200 MeV/c and q_2 about 600 MeV/c, where real photons can create the sigma meson in nuclei.
- Real photons may be used to create the sigma meson for the kinematical region q = 400 to 700 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies initiated about a decade earlier by Pisarski (1982) and others on hadron property changes in hot and/or dense medium, as reviewed by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994). The paper builds on the Walecka model calculations by Saito, Maruyama, and Soutome (1995) showing sigma meson mass decrease in nuclear matter, and on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as a low-energy effective theory. The proposed experiments aim to confirm the existence of the elusive sigma meson and test predictions of partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei, which could enable future studies of QCD vacuum modification in baryonic matter.
