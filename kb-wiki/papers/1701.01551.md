# The hidden-charm multiquark states

> Since 2003 many charmonium-like states were observed experimentally. Especially those charged charmonium-like $Z_c$ states and bottomonium-like $Z_b$ states cannot be accommodated within the naive quark model, which are good candidates of either the hidden-charm tetraquark states or molecules composed of a pair of charmed mesons. In 2015, the LHCb Collaboration discovered two hidden-charm pentaquark states, which are also beyond the quark model. In this talk, we review the current experimental progress and investigate various theoretical interpretations of these candidates of the multiquark states. We list the puzzles and theoretical challenges of these models when confronted with the experimental data. We also discuss possible future measurements which may distinguish the theoretical schemes on the underlying structures of the hidden-charm multiquark states.

## Key claim

The hidden-charm multiquark states (XYZ states, \(P_c\) pentaquarks) are strong candidates for exotic hadrons beyond the naive quark model, but many of them may not be genuine resonances; they could arise from kinematical effects, threshold openings, cusp effects, final-state interactions, or triangle singularities.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on charmonium-like and bottomonium-like states discovered since 2003, focusing on charged \(Z_c\) and \(Z_b\) states and the LHCb \(P_c\) pentaquarks. It compares theoretical interpretations including hadronic molecules (loosely bound states via meson exchange), compact tetraquarks (tightly bound via color force), hybrid charmonia, and conventional charmonium states distorted by coupled-channel effects. Selected examples (\(P_c\), X(3872), \(Z_b/Z_c\), Y(4260)) are examined in detail against experimental decay patterns, masses, and widths.

## Key numbers

- \(P_c(4380)\): mass 4380 MeV, width ~205 MeV, spin-parity \({3\over 2}^-\); \(P_c(4450)\): mass ~4450 MeV, narrow width, spin-parity \({5\over 2}^+\) (LHCb, 2015).
- X(3872): mass within 0.2 MeV of the \(\bar D D^*\) threshold; total width less than 1 MeV; \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\).
- \(Z_c(3900)\): mass near \(\bar D D^*\) threshold, \(J^P=1^+\); open-charm decay mode strongly suppressed compared to traditional charmonium.
- Y(4260): vector charmonium candidate at 4.26 GeV; not observed in R-value scan or open-charm processes, appearing as a dip rather than a peak.
- Lattice QCD simulation: vector hybrid charmonium lies around 4.26 GeV; no exotic candidates found in the isovector channel for X(3872) or \(Z_c(3900)\).

## Context

This review extends the ongoing search for exotic multiquark states that began with the discovery of X(3872) by Belle (2003) and the charged \(Z_b\) states by Belle (2011). It contrasts the molecular interpretation (analogous to the deuteron, as in the one-meson-exchange formalism applied by the authors and others) with the compact tetraquark and hybrid charmonium models. The paper highlights that lattice QCD simulations (e.g., dynamical lattice QCD using \(c\bar c\), two-meson, and diquark-antidiquark operators) strongly support X(3872) as a mixture of \(c\bar c\) and \(\bar D D^*\) molecule, while disfavoring tetraquark interpretations for \(Z_c(3900)\). Future measurements at Belle2 and LHCb are expected to distinguish between these theoretical schemes.
