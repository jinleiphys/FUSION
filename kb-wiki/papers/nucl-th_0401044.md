# Quantum calculations of Coulomb reorientation for sub-barrier fusion

> Classical mechanics and Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations of heavy ions collisions are performed to study the rotation of a deformed nucleus in the Coulomb field of its partner. This reorientation is shown to be independent on charges and relative energy of the partners. It only depends upon the deformations and inertias. TDHF calculations predict an increase by 30% of the induced rotation due to quantum effects while the nuclear contribution seems negligible. This reorientation modifies strongly the fusion cross-section around the barrier for light deformed nuclei on heavy collision partners. For such nuclei a hindrance of the sub-barrier fusion is predicted.

# Quantum calculations of Coulomb reorientation for sub-barrier fusion

## Key claim

Coulomb reorientation of a deformed projectile during sub-barrier fusion depends only on deformation and inertia (mass ratio), not on charges or incident energy; however, the classical result underestimates the reorientation by about 30% compared to the quantum Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculation, and this reorientation hinders sub-barrier fusion for light deformed projectiles on heavy targets.

## Method

Classical rigid-body dynamics and TDHF calculations with Skyrme effective interactions (SkM* and SLy4 parametrizations) were performed for asymmetric heavy-ion collisions, treating the deformed projectile in the Coulomb field of a spherical target. The TDHF code used a 3D box with 0.8 fm grid spacing and 0.45 fm/c time step, with the projectile and target evolved separately in their center-of-mass frames assuming Rutherford trajectories for the centers of mass. The resulting reorientation angles were then incorporated into the coupled-channels code CCDEF to compute fusion barrier distributions, and compared with full coupled-channels calculations using CCFULL including long-range Coulomb couplings up to 241 fm.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 24Mg + 208Pb at the barrier, classical dynamics gives a maximum reorientation of about 23 degrees for an initial orientation of 45 degrees.
- TDHF with the SkM* force predicts a maximum reorientation of 28.2 degrees for the same system; with SLy4 it gives 33.6 degrees, both about 30% larger than the classical value.
- For the reaction 154Sm + 16O at the barrier, the classical maximum reorientation is less than 2 degrees.
- The analytical expression for the reorientation angle is Delta-phi = 3 epsilon * (A_t / (A_p + A_t)) * sin(2*phi_infinity) * (1/2 + ln 2), where epsilon is related to the deformation parameter beta_2.
- The nuclear contribution to reorientation is negligible: for 24Mg + 16O, the extracted nuclear reorientation at 45 degrees is about -3 degrees.
- The ratio Delta-B(45 deg)/Delta-B(90 deg) from TDHF for 24Mg + 16O is 0.45(5), close to the sin^2 law prediction of 1/2.

## Context

This work extends classical reorientation studies by Holm (1969) and Wilets (1967) and provides a quantum treatment using TDHF, going beyond the isotropic orientation distribution assumption used in earlier analyses of fusion data for 16O + 144-154Sm reactions by Stokstad (1978), DiGregorio (1986), Wei (1991), Leigh (1993), and Morton (1994). The results show that long-range Coulomb couplings must be included in coupled-channels calculations (such as CCFULL by Hagino, 1999) for light deformed projectiles on heavy targets, and that the reorientation effect reduces the sub-barrier fusion enhancement that would otherwise be expected from static deformation alone. The work suggests experiments comparing fusion excitation functions for a light deformed projectile like 24Mg on different spherical targets (16O, 40Ca, 208Pb) to isolate the reorientation effect.
