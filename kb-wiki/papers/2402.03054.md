# The footprint of nuclear saturation properties on the neutron star $f$ mode oscillation frequencies: a machine learning approach

> We investigate the intricate relationships between the non-radial \(f\) mode oscillation frequencies of neutron stars (NS)s and the corresponding nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) using a machine learning (ML) approach within the ambit of the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework for nuclear matter. With two distinct parameterizations of the Walecka model, namely, (1) with non-linear self interactions of the scalar field (NL) and, (2) a density dependent Bayesian model (DDB), we perform a thorough examination of the \(f\) mode frequency in relation to various nuclear saturation properties. The correlations between the \(f\) mode frequencies and nuclear saturation properties reveal, through various analytical and ML methods, the complex nature of NSs and their potential as the cosmic laboratory for studying extreme states of matter. A principal component analysis (PCA) has been performed using mixed datasets from DDB and NL models to discriminate the relative importance of the different components of the EOS on the $f$ mode frequencies. Additionally, a {\it Random forest feature importance} analysis also elucidates the distinct roles of these properties in determining the \(f\) mode frequency across a spectrum of NS masses. Our findings are further supported by symbolic regression searches, yielding high-accuracy relations with strong Pearson coefficients and minimal errors. These relations suggest new methodologies for probing NS core characteristics, such as energy density, pressure, and speed of sound from observations of non-radial \(f\) mode oscillations of NSs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that machine learning methods, particularly symbolic regression, can uncover high-accuracy relations between neutron star \(f\) mode oscillation frequencies and nuclear saturation properties, but this is conditional on using the scaled Cowling approximation (which corrects for the 10-30% overestimation of frequencies in the standard Cowling approximation) and is demonstrated only within two specific families of relativistic mean field models (NL and DDB).

## Method

The authors generated approximately 18,000 equation of state samples each for two relativistic mean field parametrizations (non-linear self interactions and density-dependent Bayesian models) using Bayesian inference constrained by nuclear saturation properties, chiral effective field theory calculations for pure neutron matter, and the observational lower bound of 2 solar masses for neutron star maximum mass. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each equation of state to obtain stellar structure, then computed \(f\) mode oscillation frequencies within the Cowling approximation for quadrupole (\(l=2\)) modes and scaled these frequencies to approximate full general relativity corrections using a linear relation from Yoshida (1997). They applied principal component analysis, random forest feature importance, and symbolic regression via the GPlearn algorithm to identify correlations between nuclear saturation properties and \(f\) mode frequencies across neutron star masses from 1.2 to 2.0 solar masses.

## Key numbers

* The \(f\) mode frequency is overestimated by 30% for compactness of 0.05 and by 15% for compactness of 0.2 in the Cowling approximation relative to full general relativity, based on the scaling relation from Yoshida (1997)
* The analysis covers neutron star masses in the range 1.2 to 2.0 solar masses, with compactness parameter \(C = M/R\) varying from 0.13 to 0.22
* The error in the Cowling approximation for \(f\) modes ranges from approximately 10% to 30%, with error decreasing for higher compactness (higher mass neutron stars)
* The datasets contain approximately 18,000 samples each for the DDB and NL equation of state families
* The Pearson correlation analysis covers baryon number densities up to 1.25 fm\(^{-3}\) (approximately 8 times nuclear saturation density)

## Context

This work extends prior correlation studies between neutron star \(f\) mode frequencies and equation of state properties, such as those by Kumar (2023) and Kunjipurayil (2022), who demonstrated strong correlations (approximately 0.9) between \(f\) mode frequencies for 1.4 to 1.8 solar mass neutron stars and matter pressure at 1.5 to 2.5 times nuclear saturation density. The paper complements the statistical analysis of Manoharan (2023) by applying artificial intelligence techniques including symbolic regression to uncover universal relations. The results enable new methodologies for probing neutron star core characteristics such as energy density, pressure, and speed of sound from future gravitational wave observations of non-radial \(f\) mode oscillations, particularly with advanced detectors like the Einstein Telescope.
