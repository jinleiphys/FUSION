# Constraining the symmetry energy from the neutron skin thickness of Tin isotopes

> We show in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach that unambiguous correlations exist between observables of finite nuclei and nuclear matter properties. Using this correlation analysis to existing data on the neutron skin thickness of Sn isotopes, we find important constraints on the value E_{sym}(rho_0) and density slope L of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density. Combining these constraints with those from recent analyses of isospin diffusion and double neutron/proton ratio in heavy ion collisions leads to a value of L=58\pm 18 MeV approximately independent of E_{sym}(ρ_0).

# Constraining the symmetry energy from the neutron skin thickness of Tin isotopes

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that existing data on the neutron skin thickness of Sn isotopes, analyzed via a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock correlation method, can constrain the density slope L of the nuclear symmetry energy to L = 58 ± 18 MeV, approximately independent of the symmetry energy value at saturation density E_sym(ρ₀). This result depends on combining the Sn skin constraints with those from heavy-ion collision analyses of isospin diffusion and double neutron/proton ratio.

## Method
The authors reparameterize the 9 Skyrme interaction parameters in terms of 9 macroscopic nuclear matter quantities (including ρ₀, E₀(ρ₀), K₀, effective masses, E_sym(ρ₀), L, and gradient coefficients), then vary each macroscopic quantity individually within its known empirical range while keeping others fixed at the MSL0 reference set. They perform a chi-squared analysis comparing calculated neutron skin thicknesses of Sn isotopes to experimental data, and combine the resulting constraints with those from heavy-ion collision analyses of isospin diffusion and double n/p ratio.

## Key numbers
- L = 58 ± 18 MeV: the extracted density slope of the symmetry energy, approximately independent of E_sym(ρ₀), obtained by combining Sn skin constraints with heavy-ion collision analyses.
- L = 54 ± 13 MeV: the value obtained from the Sn neutron skin data alone, within a 2σ uncertainty from the chi-squared analysis.
- The MSL0 reference parameter set uses: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, E₀(ρ₀) = −16 MeV, K₀ = 230 MeV, m*_{s,0} = 0.8m, m*_{v,0} = 0.7m, E_sym(ρ₀) = 30 MeV, L = 60 MeV, G_V = 5 MeV·fm⁵, G_S = 132 MeV·fm⁵, and spin-orbit coupling W₀ = 133.3 MeV·fm⁵.

## Context
This work extends the established correlation between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy parameters found in earlier mean-field calculations (Brown 2000, Horowitz 2001, Furnstahl 2002, Yoshida 2004, Chen 2005, Todd-Rutel 2005, Reinhard 2010) by introducing a reparameterization method that avoids varying the 9 Skyrme parameters directly. The combined constraint on L from Sn skin data and heavy-ion collision analyses (Tsang 2009) yields a more precise value than previous estimates, though the constraint on E_sym(ρ₀) itself is not improved. This enables tighter predictions for neutron star properties that depend on the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
