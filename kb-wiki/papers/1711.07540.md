# Post-prior equivalence for transfer reactions with complex potentials

> In this paper, we address the problem of the post-prior equivalence in the calculation of inclusive breakup and transfer cross sections. For that, we employ the model proposed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C 32, 431 (1985)], conveniently generalized to include the part of the cross section corresponding the transfer to bound states. We pay particular attention to the case in which the unobserved particle is left in a bound state of the residual nucleus, in which case the theory prescribes the use of a complex potential, responsible for the spreading width of the populated single-particle states. We see that the introduction of this complex potential gives rise to an additional term in the prior cross section formula, not present in the usual case of real binding potentials. The equivalence is numerically tested for reaction induced by deuterons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the post-prior equivalence for transfer reactions holds even when complex potentials are used to describe bound states of the residual nucleus, but only if the non-orthogonality term is included in the prior-form expression; without this term, the prior and post formulas give different results when the x-A potential has an imaginary part.

## Method

The authors generalize the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model (1985) to include transfer to bound states by extending the complex optical potential for the x-A system to negative energies. They derive the relationship between the post-form and prior-form inclusive breakup cross sections, showing that the prior-form UT (Udagawa-Tamura) formula must be supplemented with a non-orthogonality term and an interference term to match the IAV post-form result. The formalism is tested numerically for the 58Ni(d,pX) reaction at Ed=80 MeV, comparing post and prior calculations with and without the additional terms.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied is 58Ni(d,pX) at Ed_lab = 80 MeV
- The neutron-58Ni potential is extrapolated to negative energies by fixing its real and imaginary parts to their values at En = 1 MeV for En ≤ 1 MeV
- When the imaginary part of the n-58Ni potential is reduced by a factor of 10, the IAV and UT formulas show almost perfect agreement for En < 0
- For constant Wx, the energy distribution follows a Breit-Wigner (Lorentzian) shape with width Γn = ⟨φn|Wx|φn⟩

## Context

This work extends the IAV model (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent, 1985) for inclusive breakup to negative energies, following the approach of Udagawa, Lee, and Tamura (1986) and more recently Potel et al. (2015) who used dispersive optical potentials. The paper resolves the long-standing discrepancy between the IAV post-form and UT prior-form formulas (Udagawa and Tamura, 1981) by showing that the prior form requires additional non-orthogonality and interference terms when complex potentials are used. This enables a unified description of breakup and transfer reactions using complex potentials, with implications for surrogate reaction methods and dispersive optical model analyses.
