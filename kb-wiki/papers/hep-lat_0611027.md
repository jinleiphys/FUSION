# Towards the QCD phase diagram

> We summarize our recent results on the phase diagram of QCD with N_f=2+1 quark flavors, as a function of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ. Using staggered fermions, lattices with temporal extent N_t=4, and the exact RHMC algorithm, we first determine the critical line in the quark mass plane (m_{u,d},m_s) where the finite temperature transition at μ=0 is second order. We confirm that the physical point lies on the crossover side of this line. Our data are consistent with a tricritical point at (m_{u,d},m_s) = (0,\sim 500) MeV. Then, using an imaginary chemical potential, we determine in which direction this second-order line moves as the chemical potential is turned on. Contrary to standard expectations, we find that the region of first-order transitions shrinks in the presence of a chemical potential, which is inconsistent with the presence of a QCD critical point at small chemical potential. The emphasis is put on clarifying the translation of our results from lattice to physical units, and on discussing the apparent contradiction of our findings with earlier lattice studies.

## Key claim
Contrary to standard expectations, the region of first-order transitions in QCD with 2+1 quark flavors shrinks when a baryon chemical potential is turned on, which is inconsistent with the presence of a QCD critical point at small chemical potential. This result comes with caveats including a coarse lattice spacing of approximately 0.3 fm and small box sizes.

## Method
Using staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extent N_t=4 and the exact RHMC algorithm, the authors first determined the chiral critical line in the quark mass plane (m_{u,d}, m_s) at zero chemical potential by measuring the Binder cumulant B_4 of the light quark condensate on 8^3, 12^3, and 16^3 x 4 lattices. They then applied an imaginary chemical potential to determine the curvature of the critical surface at mu=0, converting results from lattice to physical units using the 2-loop beta-function.

## Key numbers
- Critical mass for N_f=3 at mu=0: a m^c(mu=0) = 0.0260(5) with RHMC algorithm (compared to 0.033(1) with the R algorithm)
- For N_f=3: m^c(mu)/m^c(0) = 1 - 0.7(4) (mu/(pi T))^2 + ...
- Pseudo-critical gauge coupling for N_f=3: beta_0(am, a mu) = 5.1369(3) + 1.94(3)(a m - a m^c_0) + 0.781(7)(a mu)^2
- Tricritical point estimate for N_f=2+1: m_s^{tric} ~ 2.8 T_c at m_{u,d}=0
- Physical point check: m_pi/m_rho = 0.148(2) at lattice parameters a m_{u,d}=0.005, a m_s=0.25, beta=5.1857

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density, particularly the chiral critical line mapping at mu=0. The results contradict findings by Fodor and Katz (2004) and Gavai and Gupta (2005), who concluded for a QCD critical point at small chemical potential. The authors attribute the disagreement to differences in methodology: Fodor and Katz fixed quark masses in lattice units rather than physical units, while Gavai and Gupta studied N_f=2 theory without the strange quark. The work enables future studies on finer lattices to confirm whether the curvature of the critical surface remains negative, which would rule out a critical point at mu/T less than or approximately equal to 1.
