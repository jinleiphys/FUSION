# Pion electroproduction measurements in the nucleon resonance region

> We report new pion electroproduction measurements in the $Δ(1232)$ resonance, utilizing the SHMS - HMS magnetic spectrometers of Hall C at Jefferson Lab. The data focus on a region that exhibits a strong and rapidly changing interplay of the mesonic cloud and quark-gluon dynamics in the nucleon. The results are in reasonable agreement with models that employ pion cloud effects and chiral effective field theory calculations, but at the same time they suggest that an improvement is required to the theoretical calculations and provide valuable input that will allow their refinements. The data illustrate the potential of the magnetic spectrometers setup in Hall C towards the study the $Δ(1232)$ resonance. These first reported results will be followed by a series of measurements in Hall C, that will expand the studies of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance offering a high precision insight within a wide kinematic range from low to high momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The new pion electroproduction measurements at $Q^2 = 0.36$ GeV$^2$ are in reasonable agreement with models that include pion cloud effects and chiral effective field theory calculations, but the data simultaneously indicate that improvements to the theoretical calculations are needed, particularly for the CMR ratio which shows a rapid divergence from experiment above $Q^2 = 0.2$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The experiment measured the $ep \rightarrow ep\pi^0$ reaction using the SHMS and HMS magnetic spectrometers in Hall C at Jefferson Lab, with a 4.56 GeV electron beam incident on a 10 cm liquid-hydrogen target. Scattered electrons were detected in the SHMS and recoil protons in the HMS in coincidence, with the outgoing pion identified through missing-mass reconstruction. Cross sections were measured at $Q^2 = 0.36$ GeV$^2$ over a center-of-mass energy range from 1210 MeV to 1250 MeV, with coverage in the pion polar angle $\theta_{\pi q}^*$ and azimuthal angle $\phi_{\pi q}^*$ extending from in-plane kinematics to 50$^\circ$ out-of-plane angles.

## Key numbers

- $Q^2 = 0.36$ GeV$^2$: the intermediate momentum transfer at which all measurements were performed
- $W$ range from 1210 MeV to 1250 MeV: the center-of-mass energy coverage across the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance
- $CMR = (-5.85 \pm 0.28_{exp} \pm 0.20_{mod})\%$: the extracted Coulomb quadrupole to magnetic dipole ratio at $Q^2 = 0.36$ GeV$^2$
- $EMR = (-1.93 \pm 0.50_{exp} \pm 0.10_{mod})\%$: the extracted electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole ratio at $Q^2 = 0.36$ GeV$^2$
- Beam energy uncertainty of 0.06% and total accumulated beam charge uncertainty of 0.5%
- Target density of $0.0725 \pm 0.0003$ g/cm$^3$ at 19 K, with a density fluctuation of 0.7% from boiling at 20 $\mu$A beam current
- Target length of $100 \pm 0.26$ mm, contributing 0.26% uncertainty to cross section

## Context

This work extends the world data on $N \rightarrow \Delta(1232)$ transition form factors, which have been explored up to $Q^2 = 6$ (GeV/c)$^2$ by previous experiments including CLAS (Aznauryan 2009) and Hall A (Kelly 2005). The results confirm the inadequacy of constituent quark model predictions (Capstick 1993) that underestimate the quadrupole amplitudes by an order of magnitude, and support the dominance of pion cloud effects as described by the DMT model (Kamalov 2001) and chiral effective field theory (Pascalutsa 2007). The measurements enable refinements to theoretical calculations and will be followed by a series of approved Hall C experiments spanning $Q^2$ from 0.01 to 0.7 GeV$^2$, which will test the QCD prediction that the two quadrupole amplitudes converge at $Q^2 \rightarrow 0$ and resolve discrepancies between CLAS and Hall A data at higher $Q^2$.
