# Reduced quasifission competition in fusion reactions forming neutron-rich heavy elements

> Measurements of mass-angle distributions (MADs) for Cr + W reactions, providing a wide range in the neutron-to-proton ratio of the compound system, (N/Z)CN, have allowed for the dependence of quasifission on the (N/Z)CN to be determined in a model-independent way. Previous experimental and theoretical studies had produced conflicting conclusions. The experimental MADs reveal an increase in contact time and mass evolution of the quasifission fragments with increasing (N/Z)CN, which is indicative of an increase in the fusion probability. The experimental results are in agreement with microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations of the quasifission process. The experimental and theoretical results favor the use of the most neutron-rich projectiles and targets for the production of heavy and superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that increasing the neutron-to-proton ratio of the compound nucleus (N/Z)_CN reduces quasifission competition and enhances fusion probability in heavy-ion reactions, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of Cr + W systems where deformation and closed-shell effects are minimized, and the extracted fusion probabilities are upper limits derived from mass distribution widths rather than direct measurements.

## Method

Mass-angle distributions (MADs) were measured for eight Cr + W reactions using the ANU CUBE detector system, with beams of 50,52,54Cr from the 14UD electrostatic accelerator and superconducting LINAC bombarding isotopically enriched targets of 180,182,184,186W. All reactions were measured at a fixed center-of-mass energy relative to the Bass barrier of E_c.m./V_B = 1.13. The experimental MADs were compared with microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for the most neutron-deficient (50Cr + 180W) and most neutron-rich (54Cr + 186W) systems.

## Key numbers

- The charge product Z_p Z_t is 1776 for all Cr + W reactions studied.
- The compound nucleus (N/Z)_CN ranges from 1.35 (50Cr + 180W) to 1.45 (54Cr + 186W), corresponding to a change of 10 neutrons between the most neutron-deficient and most neutron-rich systems.
- The least neutron-rich system (50Cr + 180W) has a maximum fusion probability P_CN of approximately 25 percent, while the most neutron-rich system (54Cr + 186W) shows P_CN of approximately 45 percent.
- The target deformations beta_2 range from 0.254 to 0.225 across the W isotopes used.
- Center-of-mass energies range from 218.3 MeV (54Cr + 186W) to 222.6 MeV (50Cr + 180W), with excitation energies from 56.73 MeV to 71.65 MeV.
- TDHF calculations define fusion as contact times exceeding 35 zeptoseconds (zs).

## Context

This work resolves conflicting conclusions from previous experimental and theoretical studies on the dependence of quasifission on neutron-richness. Prior studies such as Vinodkumar et al. (2008) and Sahm et al. (1985) reported decreased P_CN with increased (N/Z)_CN for Sn + Zr reactions, while Lesko et al. (1986) and Liang et al. (2012) found the opposite trend for Sn + Ni systems. The model-independent MAD approach shows that decreased fissility from increased neutron content outweighs the associated decrease in mass asymmetry, which previous model-dependent analyses had treated as the dominant factor. The results support the use of neutron-rich radioactive ion beams for producing heavy and superheavy nuclei, and encourage further development of TDHF as a microscopic tool for low-energy heavy-ion reaction dynamics.
