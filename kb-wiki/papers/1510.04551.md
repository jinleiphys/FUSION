# Closed analytical solutions of Bohr Hamiltonian with Manning-Rosen potential model

> In the present work, we have obtained closed analytical expressions for eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Manning-Rosen potential for γ-unstable nuclei as well as exactly separable rotational ones with γ=0. Some heavy nuclei with known \b{eta} and γ bandheads have been fitted by using two parameters in the ν-unstable case and three parameters in the axially symmetric prolate deformed one. A good agreement with experimental data has been achieved.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed analytical expressions for the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the Bohr Hamiltonian using the Manning-Rosen potential, but the solutions rely on the Greene-Aldrich approximation for the centrifugal term, which is valid only for small values of the screening parameter α.

## Method

The authors separate the Bohr Hamiltonian into β and γ parts for both γ-unstable nuclei (potential independent of γ) and axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei (γ potential taken as a harmonic oscillator). They apply the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) to solve the radial Schrödinger equation with the Manning-Rosen potential, using the Greene-Aldrich approximation 1/β² ≈ α² e^{-αβ}/(e^{-αβ}−1)² to handle the centrifugal term. The energy eigenvalues are obtained from the quantization condition of AIM, and the radial wave functions are expressed in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers

- Energy eigenvalues for γ-unstable nuclei: ε_{n,τ} = −¼ [ (α²(μ+n)²−(A+2)²)(α²(μ+n)²−A²) / (α²(μ+n)²) ], where A = e^{αβ₀}−1 and Λ = τ(τ+3)
- Energy eigenvalues for prolate axial rotor: same formula with μ = ½ + √(9/4 + (6c)(n_γ+1) + (L(L+1)−K²)/3 + A²/α²)
- The model fits 25 γ-unstable nuclei with mass number A > 100 and E(4⁺_{g.s.})/E(2⁺_{g.s.}) < 2.6 using two parameters (α, β₀)
- The model fits axially symmetric deformed nuclei with A ≥ 150 and E(4⁺_{g.s.})/E(2⁺_{g.s.}) > 2.9 using three parameters (α, β₀, c)
- Root mean square deviation defined as σ_{r.m.s.} = √[ Σ(E_i(exp)−E_i(th))² / ((n−1)E(2₁⁺)²) ]

## Context

This work extends the approach of Iachello (2000) and Iachello (2001), who introduced the E(5) and X(5) critical point symmetries using infinite square well potentials, by replacing the β-part of the nuclear potential with the Manning-Rosen potential. It follows similar studies that used other potentials for the Bohr Hamiltonian, including the Coulomb, Kratzer, harmonic oscillator, Davidson, and Morse potentials. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for energy spectra of γ-unstable and axially deformed nuclei, though the authors note that for some nuclei, other β potentials such as Davidson or sextic type would be more appropriate for better agreement, particularly for β-band predictions.
