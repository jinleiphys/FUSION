# On the stability of super-heavy nuclei

> The potential-energy surfaces of an extended set of heavy and super-heavy even-even nuclei with $92 \le Z \le 126$ and isospins $40 \le N-Z \le 74$ are evaluated within the recently developed Fourier shape parametrization. Ground-state and decay properties are studied for 324 different even-even isotopes in a four-dimensional deformation space, defined by non-axiality, quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecapole degrees of freedom. Nuclear deformation energies are evaluated in the framework of the macroscopic-microscopic approach, with the Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop model and a Yukawa-folded mean-field potential. The evolution of the ground-state equilibrium shape (and possible isomeric, metastable states) is studied as a function of $Z$ and $N$. Alpha-decay $Q$-values and half-lives, as well as fission-barrier heights, are deduced. In order to understand the transition from asymmetric to symmetric fission along the Fm isotopic chain, the properties of all identified fission paths are investigated. Good agreement is found with experimental data wherever available. New interesting features about the population of different fission modes for nuclei beyond Fm are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the next "magic configuration" for super-heavy nuclei is not spherical at Z=114, N=184 as anticipated by other macroscopic-microscopic models, but is instead slightly prolate-deformed and located at Z=114-116 and N=170-174, with the stabilization driven mainly by neutrons.

## Method

The authors evaluated 4D potential-energy surfaces for 324 even-even nuclei with Z=92-126 and isospin 40-74 using a Fourier shape parametrization with non-axiality, quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecapole degrees of freedom. Deformation energies were calculated within the macroscopic-microscopic approach using the Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop (LSD) model for the liquid-drop energy and a Yukawa-folded mean-field potential for the single-particle energies, with Strutinsky shell corrections and BCS pairing correlations. The grid comprised 32,202 points in the (η, q₂, q₃, q₄) space with mesh steps of 0.03 for η, 0.05 for q₂, 0.03 for q₃, and 0.03 for q₄.

## Key numbers

- 324 even-even nuclei were studied with charge numbers 92 ≤ Z ≤ 126 and isospins 40 ≤ N-Z ≤ 74
- The deformation grid consisted of 32,202 points with η = 0 (0.03) 0.12, q₂ = -0.45 (0.05) 2.35, q₃ = 0 (0.03) 0.21, q₄ = -0.21 (0.03) 0.21
- The strongest microscopic stabilization was predicted at Z=116 for the ²⁸⁶Lv and ²⁹⁰Lv isotopes (N=170 and 174)
- A triaxial "window" was predicted across N ≈ 174 for elements from No (Z=102) to Ds (Z=110), with η ≈ 0.07 corresponding to a 15% difference between the longer and shorter half-axis
- Lighter actinides with A ≈ 230 showed hexadecapole ground-state deformation q₄^eq ≈ +0.10, while nuclei between Hs and Cn with A ≈ 270 showed q₄^eq ≈ -0.08
- The deformed shell effect at N=162 was observed from U towards Hs, and a weaker shell effect at N=152 appeared for elements between Cm and Sg

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Pomorski et al., 2017) of preactinides and actinides (78 ≤ Z ≤ 94) using the Fourier shape parametrization into the less-known super-heavy element region. The predictions differ from other macroscopic-microscopic models such as Kowal (2014) and Moller (2009, 2015), which anticipated spherical magicity at Z=114 and N=184, and show consistency with self-consistent approaches such as Cwiok (2005) and Bruyeres (2017) regarding the triaxial "window" around N=174. The results enable experimental guidance for the quest for the super-heavy island of stability and provide predictions for alpha-decay Q-values and half-lives that can be tested against future measurements.
