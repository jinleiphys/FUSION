# Pairing in exotic neutron rich nuclei around the drip line and in the crust of neutron stars

> Exotic and drip-line nuclei as well as nuclei immersed in a low density gas of neutrons in the outer crust of neutron stars are systematically investigated with respect to their neutron pairing properties. This is done using Skyrme density-functional and different pairing forces such as a density-dependent contact interaction and a separable form of a finite-range Gogny interaction. Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and BCS theories are compared. It is found that neutron pairing is reduced towards the drip line while overcast by strong shell effects. Furthermore resonances in the continuum can have an important effect counterbalancing the tendency of reduction and leading to a persistence of pairing at the drip line. It is also shown that in these systems the difference between HFB and BCS approaches can be qualitatively large.

## Key claim

Neutron pairing in exotic nuclei is reduced towards the drip line but can persist due to resonances in the continuum, counterbalancing the reduction; the difference between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and BCS approaches can be qualitatively large in these systems, and the choice of pairing gap definition (lowest canonical state versus average over pairing tensor) matters significantly at and beyond the drip line.

## Method

The authors solved self-consistent HFB equations in a spherical box with Dirichlet-Neumann mixed boundary conditions, using Skyrme density functionals (SLy4, F+, F0, F-, LNS1) for the particle-hole channel and two pairing interactions: a density-dependent contact interaction (DDCI) and a separable finite-range interaction (SFRI) reproducing the Gogny D1S pairing gap. They compared the pairing gap of the lowest canonical state (Δ_LCS) with the average pairing gap over the pairing tensor (Δ_UV) for isotopic chains of calcium, nickel, molybdenum, tin, lead, and zirconium, both for isolated nuclei and for nuclei immersed in a dilute neutron gas simulating the inner crust of neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The DDCI parameters used are V0 = -530.0 MeV fm^3, η = 0.7, α = 0.45, ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with a smooth cutoff at quasiparticle energy E^a ≥ 20 MeV.
- The SFRI strength is γ = -728 MeV fm^3 with form factor parameter b = 0.644 fm, corrected to 0.9γ for use with Skyrme single-particle spectra.
- The momentum cutoff for the HFB basis is k_max = 4 fm^-1, corresponding to an energy cutoff of about 320 MeV.
- A new fit for the asymmetry dependence of the pairing gap is given as Δ = [1 - 2((N-Z)/A)^2] × 6.75/A^(1/3), replacing the earlier fit Δ = [1 - 7.74((N-Z)/A)^2] × 6.75/A^(1/3) from Yamagami (2008).
- For calcium isotopes, the pairing energy at the drip line varies by approximately a factor of 2 between different Skyrme interactions (F+, F0, F- giving larger values; SLy4, LNS1 giving smaller values).
- The drip-line nucleus for zirconium is N=84 with SLy4+SFRI and N=88 with SLy4+DDCI.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing in neutron-rich nuclei beyond the drip line, building on earlier studies of pairing in finite nuclei and neutron star crusts by Pillet (2010), Vinas (2010), and Pastore (2011, 2012). It contradicts the naive expectation that neutron pairing should be enhanced at the surface of exotic nuclei due to low-density neutron matter, showing instead that pairing is reduced toward the drip line due to the long coherence length of Cooper pairs. The results enable improved modeling of the outer-inner crust transition in neutron stars and provide guidance for future experiments on nuclei far from stability, as well as for analogous systems in cold atomic gases studied by Schuck (2011) and Viverit (2001).
