# The evolution equation for the energy-momentum moments of the non-equilibrium density function & The regularized relativistic third order hydrodynamics

> In this work, we first derive the evolution equation for the general energy-momentum moment of $δf$, where $δf$ is the deviation from the local equilibrium phase space density. We then introduce a relativistic extension of regularized hydrodynamics developed in the non-relativistic case by Struchtrup and Torrilhon that combines the method of moments and Chapman-Enskog expansion. Hydrodynamic equations up to the third-order in gradients are then systematially derived within the context of a single species system and the relaxation time approximation. This is followed by a series of linear stability and causality analysis. For the system of massless particles without any charge conservation, the third-order hydrodynamics is shown to be linearly stable and causal.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general evolution equation for energy-momentum moments of the non-equilibrium phase space density and then apply a relativistic extension of the regularized hydrodynamics method (originally developed by Struchtrup and Torrilhon for non-relativistic systems) to systematically obtain third-order hydrodynamic equations; for massless particles without charge conservation, the resulting third-order theory is linearly stable and causal, but this result is conditional on the specific truncation scheme and the relaxation time approximation used.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation with the relaxation time approximation and derive a general evolution equation for the energy-momentum moments \(\rho_r^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_n}\) of the deviation \(\delta f\) from local equilibrium. They then apply a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion directly to these moments (rather than to \(\delta f\) itself) to determine their order in gradients, keeping the hydrodynamic variables \(\Pi\) and \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) as dynamic while expressing higher-rank moments in terms of them. Using this regularization method, they derive hydrodynamic equations up to third order in gradients for a single-species system, followed by a linear stability and causality analysis around thermal equilibrium for the massless case.

## Key numbers

* The bulk pressure is given by \(\Pi = -\frac{m^2}{3}\rho_0\) and the shear tensor by \(\pi^{\mu\nu} = \rho_0^{\mu\nu}\), where \(\rho_0\) and \(\rho_0^{\mu\nu}\) are specific energy-momentum moments of \(\delta f\).
* The first-order constitutive relations are \(\Pi = \frac{m^2}{3}\tau_R\phi_{-1|0}\theta + O(\epsilon^2)\) and \(\pi^{\mu\nu} = -\tau_R\varphi_{-1|0}\sigma^{\mu\nu} + O(\epsilon^2)\), where \(\tau_R\) is the relaxation time and \(\theta = \partial_\mu u^\mu\) is the expansion rate.
* For massless particles (\(m=0\)), the third-order hydrodynamics is shown to be linearly stable and causal; the analysis follows the procedure outlined in de Brito and Denicol (2023).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic regularized hydrodynamics (R13) method of Struchtrup and Torrilhon (2003-2007) to the relativistic regime, providing a systematic framework that combines the method of moments and Chapman-Enskog expansion. It builds on prior derivations of third-order hydrodynamics by Jaiswal (2013-2015) and de Brito and Denicol (2023), but differs by applying the Chapman-Enskog-like expansion to the energy-momentum moments rather than to the phase space density. The general moment equation derived here (Eq. 4.22) agrees with results from Denicol and others (2012) for \(n=0,1,2,3,4\) and enables systematic derivation of higher-order relativistic hydrodynamic theories with controlled truncation.
