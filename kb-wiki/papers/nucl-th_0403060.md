# Properties and evolution of a protoneutron star in the enlarged SU(3) model

> Protoneutron stars are hot and lepton rich objects formed as a result of type II supernovae explosion. This paper describes results of analysis of protoneutron star models constructed under the assumptions that strange particles are present in the core. All calculations have been performed for the neutrino opaque matter when the entropy per baryon is of the order 2. The equation of state which is crucial for the structure and composition of a star has been obtained in the framework of the chiral SU(3) model and compared with the one involving derivative coupling of baryons to mesons. Of special interest in this paper is the comparison of the two protoneutron star models with those formed when neutrinos leak out of the system, described as cool, deleptonized neutron stars. It has been shown that the maximum mass of the hot protoneutron star with trapped neutrinos is close to 1.9 M (the Sun Mass) in case of the SU(3) model. However, deleptonization reduces the value of stable protoneutron star mass significantly to 1.5 M.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a hot protoneutron star with trapped neutrinos is close to 1.9 solar masses when calculated using the chiral SU(3) model, but deleptonization (neutrino loss) reduces the stable mass significantly to approximately 1.5 solar masses, indicating that the evolutionary stage strongly determines the star's observable properties.

## Method

The authors constructed protoneutron star models using the chiral SU(3) model in the relativistic mean-field approximation, including the full baryon octet (nucleons, Lambda, Sigma, Xi hyperons) and treating matter as neutrino-opaque with fixed entropy per baryon of 2 and total lepton number Y_L = 0.4. They solved the coupled Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields and Dirac equations for baryons self-consistently, incorporating nonlinear scalar and vector meson interaction terms, and compared results with the derivative-coupling ZM1 model and standard TM1 and GM3 parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hot protoneutron star with trapped neutrinos: close to 1.9 solar masses (SU(3) model)
- Maximum mass after deleptonization (cold, neutrino-free star): approximately 1.5 solar masses (SU(3) model)
- Entropy per baryon used throughout calculations: of order 2 (in units of Boltzmann's constant)
- Total lepton number for neutrino-trapped matter: Y_L = 0.4
- Hyperon potentials in saturated nuclear matter: U_Lambda^N(rho_0) = -30 MeV, U_Sigma^N(rho_0) = +30 MeV, U_Xi^N(rho_0) = -18 MeV
- Scalar sigma meson mass in SU(3) model: 477.6 MeV
- Scalar delta meson mass in SU(3) model: 1029 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star structure using chiral SU(3) models (Sua 1990s) and quantum hadrodynamics approaches such as the TM1 parameter set and the ZM1 derivative-coupling model (Zimanyi and Moszkowski 1990). The inclusion of hyperons and the delta meson addresses the problem of negative nucleon effective masses at high density seen in standard approaches, and the comparison between neutrino-trapped and deleptonized states enables modeling of protoneutron star evolution from formation to cooling, following the framework of Prakash et al. (1990s) and Burrows (1990s).
