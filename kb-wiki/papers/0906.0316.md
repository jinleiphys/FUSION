# Neutral kaon photoproduction on the deuteron

> Neutral kaon photoproduction on the deuteron has been investigated by including the final state effects and compared with the experimental data. Comparison shows that the models used in this calculation can reproduce the data in the $Σ$ channel regions fairly well but still give over predictions in the $Λ$ channel. It seems that the tensor target asymmetries are more suitable for studying the final state effects. The extractions of the elementary photoproduction amplitude are also demonstrated.

## Key claim

The KAON-MAID model with the missing \(D_{13}(1895)\) resonance (set A) overpredicts the inclusive cross section for neutral kaon photoproduction on the deuteron, while the sets without that resonance (sets B) describe the data fairly well in the \(\Sigma\) channel but remain poor for the \(\Lambda\) channel.

## Method

The authors calculated the inclusive cross section for \(d(\gamma,K^0)YN\) using the KAON-MAID model for the elementary operator, with the deuteron wave function and \(YN\) interaction taken from Nijmegen models. They included final state effects via \(YN\) rescattering, \(KN\) rescattering, and the \(\pi N \rightarrow KY\) intermediate process using separable rank-1 interactions. The tensor target asymmetries \(T_{2M}\) were computed, and the elementary photoproduction amplitude was extracted from the deuteron reaction by separating the deuteron wave function from the squared elementary amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section data for \(d(\gamma,K^0)YN\) were measured at photon energies \(E_{\gamma} = 0.97\) GeV and \(E_{\gamma} = 1.0\) GeV, with forward kaon angle \(\theta_K = 0\).
- For set B, the model describes the \(\Sigma\) channel data up to \(p_K = 0.4\) GeV/c at \(E_{\gamma} = 0.97\) GeV and up to \(p_K = 0.5\) GeV/c at \(E_{\gamma} = 1.0\) GeV.
- Tensor target asymmetries \(T_{2M}\) were calculated as a function of kaon angle \(\theta_K\) for photon energy \(E_{\gamma} = 1.1\) GeV.
- Elementary amplitudes were extracted for photon energies \(E_{\gamma} = 1.1\) GeV and \(E_{\gamma} = 1.3\) GeV, with spectator proton momentum varied at 0, 50, and 100 MeV/c, and with \(\theta_N = 30^\circ\) and \(\phi_N = 180^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon photoproduction on the deuteron, specifically the investigation of \(YN\) final state interaction by Yamamura et al. (1999) using realistic \(YN\) potentials, and the inclusion of \(KN\) rescattering and \(\pi N \rightarrow KY\) processes by Salam et al. (2004) for charged kaon channels. The results enable the extraction of elementary photoproduction amplitudes for neutron channels using quasi-free scattering kinematics on the deuteron, which is directly relevant to the recent experiment at LNS Sendai by Tsukada et al. (2008).
