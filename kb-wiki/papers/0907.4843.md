# A Summary of Bulk Dynamics from Quark Matter 2009

> I review the recent progress in measuring elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions. These measurements show clearly how hydrodynamics starts to develop as the system size is increased from peripheral to central collisions. During this transition, the momentum range described by hydrodynamics increases as the system progresses from a kinetic to a hydrodynamic regime. Many of the systematic deviations from ideal hydrodynamics are reproduced effortlessly once the shear viscosity is included. In order to extract the shear viscosity from the data, kinetic theory can be used to determine which aspects of the elliptic flow reflect the details of the microscopic interactions, and which aspects reflect the underlying transport coefficients. I also review the identified hadron elliptic flow and the predictions of hydrodynamics for the LHC.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic interpretation of elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions is strongly supported by systematic data, but the extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio (\(\eta/s\)) requires careful modeling because the microscopic details of kinetic models cannot be trusted when the transport time scales are close to the quantum time scale \(\tau_R \sim \hbar/(\pi T)\).

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and particle species from RHIC, comparing them to ideal and viscous hydrodynamic calculations as well as kinetic theory simulations (BAMPS, MPC). The analysis uses Monte-Carlo Glauber calculations to estimate the participant plane eccentricity (\(\epsilon_{PP}\)) and accounts for event-by-event fluctuations and non-flow effects to extract the response coefficient \(C = v_2 / \epsilon_{PP}\).

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamics becomes a good approximation for \(\eta/s \lesssim 0.3\), based on the criterion \(0.2 (\eta/s / 0.3) (1\ \mathrm{fm} / \tau_o) (300\ \mathrm{MeV} / T_o) \ll 1\).
- Ideal hydrodynamics reproduces observed elliptic flow for \(p_T < 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in the most central collisions.
- Viscous corrections to ideal hydrodynamics grow as \((p_T/T)^2 (\ell_{\mathrm{mfp}} / L)\), restricting the applicable momentum range.
- The Knudsen number is defined as \(K \equiv \sigma / (\pi R^2) \, dN/dy\) for kinetic theory comparisons.

## Context

This summary extends the work of Voloshin, Poskanzer, and Snellings (2008) on elliptic flow measurements and the hydrodynamic framework reviewed by Teaney (2009) in "Viscous Hydrodynamics and the Quark Gluon Plasma." It builds on the PHOBOS collaboration's recognition of participant eccentricity fluctuations (Alver et al., 2007) and the systematic classification of viscous corrections by Danielewicz and Gyulassy (1985). The results enable extraction of the shear viscosity from heavy ion data and provide predictions for elliptic flow at the LHC, where hydrodynamics predicts a saturation of flow increase compared to naive extrapolations from lower energies.
