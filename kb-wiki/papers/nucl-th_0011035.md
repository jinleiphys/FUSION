# The Principle of Relativity and Modified Connection between the Mass of a Body and its Velocity: a Case of V>C

> Following the basic idea expressed in [1], we assume that for any particle or body with mass M its own time t depends on therelative change \frac{ΔM}{M} of that mass. Based on this assumption, one discusses possible existence of a hypothetical particles and bodies with a velocity V>C

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a particle's proper time depends on the relative change of its mass, and uses this assumption to argue for the possible existence of particles or bodies moving faster than light (V > C), but the parameter μ linking time to mass change is left physically undefined.

## Method
The author assumes a differential relation dt = -μ⁻¹ (ΔM/M) connecting proper time t to relative mass change ΔM/M, where μ is an unknown parameter. To explore conditions for superluminal velocity, a simple model of a potential barrier of height E_b and thickness Δx is introduced, with the transmission probability D given by the standard quantum tunneling formula. The particle energy E is taken from a prior expression involving μ and the relative mass change, and the barrier height is estimated by setting E = E_b so that D = 1.

## Key numbers
- The strong-interaction μ-parameter is estimated as μ_n ≈ 10²³ sec⁻¹.
- The spatial scale ΔS used in the energy expression is ΔS ≈ 10⁻¹³ cm.

## Context
This work extends the speculative framework introduced in an earlier paper [1] (Dermendjiev, 2000) that proposed a mass-dependent proper time. It attempts to clarify the physical meaning of the μ-parameters by comparing them with dimensionless coupling constants for strong, weak, and gravitational interactions, noting a similarity in curves from the prior work. The paper enables further theoretical exploration of superluminal motion under modified relativistic assumptions, but does not provide experimental predictions or falsifiable tests.
