# Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV in a multiphase transport model

> The multiplicity and elliptic flow of charged particles produced in Pb-Pb collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV from the Large Hadron Collider are studied in a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. With the standard parameters in the HIJING model, which is used as initial conditions for subsequent partonic and hadronic scatterings in the AMPT model, the resulting multiplicity of final charged particles at mid-pseudorapidity is consistent with the experimental data measured by the ALICE Collaboration. This value is, however, increased by about 25% if the final-state partonic and hadronic scatterings are turned off. Because of final-state scatterings, particular those among partons, the final elliptic flow of charged hadrons is also consistent with the ALICE data if a smaller but more isotropic parton scattering cross section than previously used in the AMPT model for describing the charged hadron elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider is used. The resulting transverse momentum spectra of charged particles as well as the centrality dependence of their multiplicity density and the elliptic flow are also in reasonable agreement with the ALICE data. Furthermore, the multiplicities, transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows of identified hadrons such as protons, kaons and pions are predicted.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting can simultaneously describe the measured charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, but only if the Lund string fragmentation parameters are set to the default HIJING values (smaller string tension) and a smaller (1.5 mb) but more isotropic parton scattering cross section is used, rather than the larger cross section (10 mb) previously needed for RHIC data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting, where initial hadrons from HIJING 1.383 are converted to valence quarks and antiquarks, evolved via the ZPC parton cascade, then hadronized by spatial coalescence and followed by hadronic scatterings via the ART model. They varied the Lund string fragmentation parameters (a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV^-2) and parton cross section parameters (alpha_s=0.33, mu=3.2 fm^-1 giving 1.5 mb) to fit ALICE data, using about 1,000 events for multiplicity and spectra and 10,000 events for p_T-dependent elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity at mid-pseudorapidity for 0-5% central collisions: 1,536 +/- 4, consistent with ALICE data of 1584 +/- 4 (stat) +/- 76 (sys)
- Turning off final-state partonic and hadronic scatterings increases multiplicity by about 25%, to 1925 +/- 5
- Parton scattering cross section used for LHC: 1.5 mb (compared to 10 mb used for RHIC)
- Initial temperature estimated from energy density: about 468 MeV at LHC, about 378 MeV at RHIC
- Specific viscosity eta_s/s estimated: about 0.273 at LHC, compared to 0.085 or 0.114 at RHIC (depending on screening mass)
- Multiplicity ratio of mid-rapidity protons, kaons, and pions: roughly 1:2:16

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model, previously applied to RHIC energies (Lin 2001, Lin 2002a, Chen 2004), to the new LHC energy of sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV where ALICE data had recently become available (Aamodt 2010a-d). The finding that a smaller parton cross section is needed at LHC than at RHIC, leading to a larger estimated specific viscosity, contrasts with hydrodynamic model results (Luzum 2010) that suggested similar eta_s/s at both energies. The authors note this discrepancy may be resolved by using a temperature-dependent screening mass, and their predictions for identified hadron elliptic flows await future experimental tests.
