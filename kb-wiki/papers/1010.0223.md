# Stability of P11 Resonance Extracted from pi-N Data

> We address a question about how much resonance poles and residues extracted from data depend on a model used for the extraction, and on the precision of data. We focus on the P11 pi-N scattering and use the dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) model developed in Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab. We examine the model-dependence of the poles by varying parameters largely within the EBAC-DCC model. We find that two poles associated with the Roper resonance are fairly stable against the variation. We also study the stability of the Roper poles against different analytic structure of the P11 amplitude below pi-N threshold by using a bare nucleon model. We again find a good stability of the Roper poles.

## Key claim
The two poles associated with the Roper resonance in the P11 pi-N partial wave are fairly stable against large variations of model parameters within the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels framework, and also against changes in the analytic structure of the amplitude below the pi-N threshold introduced by a bare nucleon model.

## Method
The author used the dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) model developed at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab, which includes pi-N, eta-N, and pi-pi-N channels (with pi-Delta, rho-N, and sigma-N components). Parameters for meson-baryon interactions and bare N* states were varied largely while maintaining good reproduction of the SAID single-energy solution. Additionally, a bare nucleon model was constructed by adding a bare nucleon state and removing direct meson-baryon to nucleon interactions, altering the analytic structure below threshold. Pole positions were extracted via analytic continuation.

## Key numbers
- Two Roper poles from the JLMS fit (one EBAC-DCC model) at (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV (Re M_R, -Im M_R).
- Two Roper poles from the 2N*-3p fit at (1368, 82) MeV and (1375, 110) MeV.
- Two Roper poles from the 2N*-4p fit at (1372, 80) MeV and (1385, 114) MeV.
- Two Roper poles from the 1N0-1N*-3p fit (bare nucleon model) at (1363, 81) MeV and (1377, 128) MeV.
- Chi-squared per data point for the 1N0-1N*-3p fit: 2.51.
- An additional pole from the 2N*-4p fit at (1636, 67) MeV, possibly related to oscillatory behavior at higher W.

## Context
This work extends the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels analyses (JLMS model, 2007) by systematically testing the model dependence of extracted resonance parameters, a question raised by the existence of controversial N* states in higher mass regions. The stability of the two-pole structure near the pi-Delta threshold supports the robustness of the Roper resonance extraction from SAID single-energy data, and the bare nucleon model (following Afnan and Pearce) tests sensitivity to analytic structure. This enables more confident use of extracted pole positions for comparisons with QCD-based predictions of baryon resonances.
