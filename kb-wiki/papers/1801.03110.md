# QCD thermodynamics from lattice calculations with non-equilibrium methods: The SU(3) equation of state

> A precise lattice determination of the equation of state in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is carried out by means of a simulation algorithm, based on Jarzynski's theorem, that allows one to compute physical quantities in thermodynamic equilibrium, by driving the field configurations of the system out of equilibrium. The physical results and the computational efficiency of the algorithm are compared with other state-of-the-art lattice calculations, and the extension to full QCD with dynamical fermions and to other observables is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Jarzynski's equality can be used to compute the SU(3) equation of state on the lattice with reduced thermalization costs compared to the standard integral method, but the method still requires subtraction of ultraviolet vacuum contributions, and its computational efficiency depends on the system having a sufficiently narrow distribution of work values.

## Method

The authors discretize SU(3) Yang-Mills theory on a hypercubic lattice using the Wilson action, with the inverse gauge coupling β serving as the control parameter that is varied linearly in Monte Carlo time to drive the system out of equilibrium. They compute the ratio of partition functions at two different temperatures via the exponential average of the work done during non-equilibrium trajectories, using a Markov process that combines one heat-bath step with five to ten over-relaxation steps per sweep. The physical pressure is obtained by subtracting the zero-temperature contribution evaluated on a large symmetric lattice at the same lattice spacing, following the relation p(T)/T⁴ = p(T_in)/T_in⁴ + (N_t/N_s)³ [ln ⟨exp(-ΔS)⟩_{finite T} - γ ln ⟨exp(-ΔS)⟩_{T=0}].

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is set by T_c r_0 = 0.7457(45), where r_0 is the Sommer scale determined in Necco (2001).
- The Sommer scale r_0 is assumed to be of order 0.5 fm, consistent with phenomenological potential models for QCD.
- The critical deconfinement temperature in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is approximately 270 MeV when converted into physical units.
- The lattice spacing a is tuned by varying β, with the scale set using the Sommer parameter r_0 from Necco (2001).

## Context

This work extends the benchmark study of Caselle (2016) in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory to the physically relevant SU(3) case, and provides an alternative to the standard integral method of Engels (1990) and to recent approaches based on the gradient flow (Kitazawa 2016, Asakawa 2013) or moving reference frames (Giusti 2016). The results are compared with state-of-the-art calculations by Borsanyi (2012), Giusti (2016), and Kitazawa (2016), and the method is presented as a versatile technique that can be extended to full QCD with dynamical fermions and to other observables.
