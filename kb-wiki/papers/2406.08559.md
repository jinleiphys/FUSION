# The connection between Nucleon Energy Correlators and Fracture Functions

> We establish a sum rule that connects fracture functions to nucleon energy-energy correlators~(NEECs) in a one-to-one correspondence. Using this sum rule, we study the energy pattern in the target fragmentation region of deep inelastic scatterings. Through investigations up to twist-3, we express all eighteen energy-pattern structure functions in terms of associated NEECs, elucidating various azimuthal and spin asymmetries critical for nucleon tomography. Additionally, we investigate the perturbative matching of the twist-2 quark NEECs. We demonstrate that the Sivers-type and worm-gear-type quark NEECs match onto twist-3 multi-parton distributions. Our work provides a framework for examining energy-weighted observables through hadron production processes in the target fragmentation region, offering new insights into nucleon tomography.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a sum rule that connects fracture functions to nucleon energy-energy correlators (NEECs) in a one-to-one correspondence, but this relationship is conditional on integrating over hadron phase space with an energy-weighting factor and summing over all hadron species, meaning NEECs are derived from fracture functions rather than being independent quantities.

## Method
The authors derived a sum rule relating the correlation matrix of fracture functions to that of NEECs by expressing the energy flow operator in terms of hadronic number operators and comparing with the single-inclusive summation in fracture function definitions. They then decomposed both correlation matrices into chiral-even components up to twist-3, obtaining individual sum rules for each of the twelve quark NEECs in terms of corresponding fracture functions. The perturbative matching of twist-2 quark NEECs onto collinear parton distributions was studied using the sum rule, with particular focus on the Sivers-type and worm-gear-type NEECs that require twist-3 multi-parton correlations.

## Key numbers
- Eighteen energy-pattern structure functions are expressed in terms of associated NEECs, with ten contributed by twist-2 NEECs (four exclusively gluonic) and eight generated by twist-3 NEECs
- Four twist-2 quark NEECs are identified: unpolarized ($f_{1}^q$), helicity ($g_{1L}^q$), Sivers-type ($f_{1T}^{t,q}$), and worm-gear-type ($g_{1T}^{t,q}$)
- Eight twist-3 quark NEECs are identified: $f^{t,q}$, $f_{L}^{t,q}$, $f_{T}^q$, $f_{T}^{t,q}$, $g^{t,q}$, $g_{L}^{t,q}$, $g_{T}^q$, $g_{T}^{t,q}$
- The sum rule involves integration over $\xi_h$ from 0 to $1-x$ with weighting factor $\xi_h$ and transverse momentum constraint $|\bs P_{h\perp}| = \theta \xi_h P^+ / \sqrt{2}$

## Context
This work bridges two previously independent lines of research: SIDIS studies in the target fragmentation region using fracture functions (Trentadue and Veneziano, 1993; Berera, 1995; Grazzini, 1997; Collins, 1997) and DIS energy pattern studies using NEECs (Liu, 2022; Cao, 2023). The sum rule shows that fracture functions are the parent functions of NEECs, enabling the authors to express all eighteen energy-pattern structure functions in terms of NEECs and perform the first perturbative matching of $n_t$-odd NEECs onto twist-3 multi-parton distributions. This framework provides new avenues for nucleon tomography by connecting energy-weighted observables to hadron production processes in the target fragmentation region.
