# Phase transitions in dense 2-colour QCD

> We investigate 2-colour QCD with 2 flavours of Wilson fermion at nonzero temperature T and quark chemical potential mu, with a pion mass of 700 MeV (m_pi/m_rho=0.8). From temperature scans at fixed mu we find that the critical temperature for the superfluid to normal transition depends only very weakly on mu above the onset chemical potential, while the deconfinement crossover temperature is clearly decreasing with mu. We also present results for the Landau-gauge gluon propagator in the hot and dense medium.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition in 2-colour QCD is remarkably constant across a range of chemical potentials above the onset value, while the deconfinement crossover temperature clearly decreases with increasing chemical potential; these conclusions are drawn from simulations with a pion mass of 700 MeV (m_pi/m_rho=0.8) and may depend on the specific quark mass used.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of 2-colour QCD with two flavours of Wilson fermion, using a standard Wilson gauge and fermion action augmented with a diquark source term. Temperature scans at fixed chemical potential were conducted on 16^3 x N_tau lattices with N_tau=4-16, corresponding to temperatures T=47, 70, 94, and 141 MeV, for chemical potentials mu a=0.0-0.9. The superfluid transition temperature was extracted from inflection points of the diquark condensate extrapolated to zero diquark source, while the deconfinement crossover region was determined from the renormalised Polyakov loop using two different renormalisation schemes.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 700 MeV, with m_pi/m_rho=0.8
- Lattice spacing: a=0.178(6) fm, with am_pi=0.645(8)
- Superfluid critical temperature T_s at mu a=0.40: 94(9) MeV
- Superfluid critical temperature T_s at mu a=0.50: 93(6) MeV
- Superfluid critical temperature T_s at mu a=0.60: 93(7) MeV
- Superfluid critical temperature T_s at mu a=0.35: 82(27) MeV
- Onset chemical potential at T=0: mu_o a = m_pi a/2 = 0.32
- Deconfinement crossover region at mu=0: T_d = 217(23) MeV
- Deconfinement crossover region at mu a=0.60: T_d = 67-152 MeV
- Temperature scan range: T=47, 70, 94, and 141 MeV (N_tau=24, 16, 12, and 8 respectively)
- Diquark source values used: ja=0.02, 0.04

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 2-colour QCD by Hands (2006, 2010) and Cotter (2012) that mapped out the phase diagram in the (mu,T) plane, providing a more precise determination of the phase transition lines. The results provide benchmark constraints for functional approaches to dense QCD, such as Dyson-Schwinger equations and the functional renormalisation group, which use the Landau-gauge gluon propagator as a key input. The authors plan to extend these studies to smaller quark masses and finer lattice spacings, and to study the quark propagator response to mu and T in a forthcoming publication.
