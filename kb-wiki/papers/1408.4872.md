# Systematic study of shell gaps in nuclei

> The nucleon separation energies and shell gaps in nuclei over the whole nuclear chart are systematically studied with eight global nuclear mass models. For unmeasured neutron-rich and super-heavy regions, the uncertainty of the predictions from these different mass models is still large. The latest version (WS4) of the Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass formula, in which the isospin dependence of model parameters is introduced into the macroscopic-microscopic approach inspired by the Skyrme energy-density functional, is found to be the most accurate one in the descriptions of nuclear masses, separation energies and shell gaps. Based on the predicted shell gaps in nuclei, the possible magic numbers in super-heavy nuclei region are investigated. In addition to the shell closures at $N=184, Z=114$, the sub-shell closures at around $N=178, Z=120$ could also play a role for the stability of super-heavy nuclei.

# Systematic study of shell gaps in nuclei

## Key claim
The WS4 mass formula achieves the most accurate description of nuclear masses, separation energies, and shell gaps among eight global mass models, with the rms deviation for masses falling to 298 keV, the first time the 0.3 MeV threshold has been crossed within the mean-field framework. However, the paper's claim that shell closures at N=184, Z=114 and sub-shell closures at N=178, Z=120 could play a role for super-heavy nuclei stability is qualified by the observation that for the candidate doubly-magic nucleus 298Fl, the shell correction is actually smaller than that of its neighbor 296120 by about 1 MeV due to the influence of these sub-shell closures.

## Method
The authors systematically compared eight global nuclear mass models (FRDM, HFB17, HFB27, DZ28, WS, WS*, WS3, and WS4) by calculating two-neutron separation energies S2n and empirical shell gaps Δ(N,Z) defined as the sum of neutron and proton shell gaps from binding-energy differences. The WS4 model introduces isospin-dependent parameters and surface diffuseness corrections into the macroscopic-microscopic Weizsäcker-Skyrme approach, with 18 independent parameters fitted to 2353 measured nuclear masses from AME2012. Shell gaps and shell corrections were then used to investigate possible magic numbers in the super-heavy region.

## Key numbers
- WS4 rms deviation for masses: 298 keV (2353 measured masses, N and Z ≥ 8)
- WS4 rms deviation for S2n: 0.276 MeV (2123 measured S2n values)
- WS4 rms deviation for shell gaps Δ: 0.725 MeV (1689 measured shell gaps)
- FRDM rms deviation for S2n: 0.493 MeV; HFB27: 0.425 MeV; DZ28: 0.336 MeV
- FRDM rms deviation for masses: 654 keV; HFB17: 576 keV; HFB27: 512 keV; DZ28: 394 keV
- Shell correction of 296120: 6.2 MeV (larger than that of 298Fl by 1 MeV)
- WS4 rms deviation for Sn: 258 keV (2199 measured); for Sp: 274 keV (2150 measured); for S2p: 322 keV (2032 measured)

## Context
This work extends the series of Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass formulas developed by Wang and collaborators (Wang 2010, Liu 2011, WS4 2014), systematically testing them against other global models including the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (1995), the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov models HFB17 and HFB27 from the Skyrme energy-density functional approach, and the Duflo-Zuker DZ28 model. The study enables improved predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations and for the synthesis of super-heavy nuclei, where the WS4 predictions for shell gaps at N=184, Z=114 and sub-shell closures at N=178, Z=120 provide input for statistical model calculations of survival probabilities. The finding that proton separation energies are systematically less well described than neutron separation energies points to missing physics related to protons in current global mass models.
