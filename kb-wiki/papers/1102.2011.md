# Atomic analysis of the $(n;t)-$reaction of the helium-3 atoms with slow neutrons

> Probabilties of formation of various hydrogenic species during the exothermic nuclear $(n,{}^3$He$;t,p)-$reaction of atomic helium-3 with slow neutrons are determined. In particular, we have found that the probability to form the tritium atom ${}^3$H in its ground state is $\approx$ 55.19287 %, while analogous probability to form the protium atom ${}^1$H is $\approx$ 1.02363 %. Analogous probabilities of formation of the negatively charged hydrogen ions, i.e. the ${}^3$H$^{-}$ and ${}^1$H$^{-}$ ions, in the nuclear $(n,{}^3$He$; t,p)-$reaction with slow neutrons, are $\approx$ 7.8680 % and $\approx$ 0.06583 %, respectively. We also consider bremsstrahlung from fast fission-type reactions in atomic systems. The spectrum of emitted radiation is analyzed.

# Atomic analysis of the $(n;t)-$reaction of the helium-3 atoms with slow neutrons

## Key claim
The probabilities of forming various hydrogenic species (tritium atoms, protium atoms, and their negative ions) during the exothermic nuclear $(n,{}^3\text{He};t,p)-$reaction in the two-electron helium-3 atom are computed using a new method based on direct computation of probability amplitudes under the sudden approximation, with the condition that the nuclear reaction time is significantly shorter than the typical atomic time ($\tau_a \approx 2.418884331 \cdot 10^{-17}$ sec).

## Method
The authors developed a method based on the sudden approximation to compute probability amplitudes for final-state atomic species formed during the nuclear reaction ${}^3\text{He} + n = p + t + 0.764$ MeV in the two-electron helium-3 atom. For one-electron final states (tritium or protium atoms), they computed integrals of the form $M_{in;fi} = \langle \Psi_{fi}({\bf x}_1) \mid \exp(\imath {\bf V} \cdot {\bf r}_1) \mid \Psi_{in}({\bf x}_1, {\bf x}_2) \rangle$, and for two-electron final states (negative hydrogen ions), they computed $M_{in;fi} = \langle \Psi_{fi}({\bf x}_1, {\bf x}_2) \mid \exp(\imath {\bf V} \cdot {\bf r}_1 + \imath {\bf V} \cdot {\bf r}_2) \mid \Psi_{in}({\bf x}_1, {\bf x}_2) \rangle$, using highly accurate variational wave functions with 400 exponential basis functions for the helium atom and H$^{-}$ ion. They also analyzed bremsstrahlung radiation from fast fission-type reactions in atomic systems by computing the spectral resolution of dipole radiation.

## Key numbers
- Probability to form tritium atom ${}^3$H in its ground state: $\approx$ 55.19287 %
- Probability to form protium atom ${}^1$H in its ground state: $\approx$ 1.02363 %
- Probability to form negatively charged tritium ion ${}^3$H$^{-}$: $\approx$ 7.8680 %
- Probability to form negatively charged protium ion ${}^1$H$^{-}$: $\approx$ 0.06583 %
- Cross-section of the nuclear reaction for slow neutrons: $\sigma_{max} \approx 5330 \cdot 10^{-24}$ cm$^2$ (5330 barn)
- Velocities of nuclear fragments: tritium $\approx$ 1.59632 a.u., protium $\approx$ 4.78897 a.u.
- Total energy of ground state ${}^{\infty}$He atom: -2.90372437703405 a.u.
- Total energy of ground state ${}^{\infty}$H$^{-}$ ion: -0.527751016544308 a.u.
- Uncertainties for ground state probabilities: $\approx 0.3 - 1 \cdot 10^{-4}$ %
- Uncertainties for excited state probabilities: $\approx 0.5 - 1 \cdot 10^{-3}$ %

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Frolov and Wardlaw, 2009) which determined final-state probabilities for the nuclear $(n,{}^3\text{He};t,p)-$reaction in the one-electron helium-3 ion (${}^3\text{He}^{+}$), now treating the more experimentally relevant two-electron helium-3 atom. The method improves upon the alternative procedure based on few-electron density matrices described in that prior work, which was numerically complex and prone to accuracy loss. The results enable improved sensitivity in detectors of thermal and slow neutrons (referencing Wilkinson and Worledge, Yampolsky, and Tsoulfanidis), and have applications in Boron Neutron Capture Therapy (BNCT) using the $(n, {}^{10}\text{B}; {}^{7}\text{Li},{}^{4}\text{He})$ reaction (referencing Hawthorne and colleagues), as well as in thermonuclear explosive devices.
