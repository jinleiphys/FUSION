# Electromagnetic form factors of bound nucleons revisited

> We investigate the possible modifications of the nucleons' electromagnetic form factors in the framework of a modified Skyrme model allowing for nucleon deformation and using realistic nuclear mass distributions. We show that such effects are small in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors show that medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in light nuclei like ^4He are small (below 20% for momentum transfers up to 0.6 GeV^2), contradicting earlier studies that assumed uniform nuclear matter density and found larger effects; the modifications are probe-dependent and do not correspond to a uniform swelling or shrinking of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors use a modified Skyrme model that allows for nucleon deformation via variational parameters in the profile function and the angular function, with the density dependence encoded in medium functionals α_p and α_s derived from low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data. They place a deformed Skyrmion at various distances R from the center of a ^4He nucleus described by a realistic Gaussian density distribution, then compute the l=0 partial wave projections of the electric and magnetic form factors for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The proton magnetic moment in free space is μ_p = 1.93 nuclear magnetons; the neutron magnetic moment is μ_n = -1.20 nuclear magnetons; the ratio |μ_p/μ_n| = 1.61 (empirical value is 1.46)
- At the center of ^4He (R = 0 fm), the nucleon mass ratio m_N/m_N^free = 0.817; at R = 2.5 fm, it rises to 0.994
- The variational parameter r_S (Skyrmion size) decreases monotonically from 0.627 fm at R = 0 fm to 0.604 fm at R = 2.5 fm; the free-space value is 0.600 fm
- Magnetic moments change by less than 5.5%: at R = 0 fm, μ_p = 1.883 n.m. and μ_n = -1.137 n.m.; at R = 1.5 fm, μ_p = 1.975 n.m. and μ_n = -1.245 n.m.
- The residual density at R = 0 fm is 0.7 ρ_0 and at R = 1 fm is 0.55 ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} is nuclear matter density
- The ^4He density parameter r_0 = 1.31 fm
- Input parameters: F_π = 108 MeV, e = 5.265, m_N = 938 MeV, m_π from pion mass, b_0 = -0.024 m_π^{-1}, c_0 = 0.21 m_π^{-3}, g_0' = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Yakhshiev, Meißner, Wirzba, 2002) that used realistic nuclear density distributions and allowed nucleon deformation, finding that uniform size modification cannot be maintained. It contradicts earlier studies (Celenza, Meißner, Ruiz, Lu, Musakhanov, and others) that assumed homogeneous nuclear matter and found larger medium modifications. The results are consistent with limits from electron scattering data based on the y-scaling hypothesis (Sick) and comparable to calculations in a quark-meson model with shell-like nuclear densities (Lu). The paper concludes that extraction of the neutron charge form factor from light nuclei at low and intermediate momentum transfer is not sensitive to these medium effects given current experimental accuracy.
