# Approximations in Fusion and Breakup reactions induced by Radioactive Beams

> Some commonly used approximations for complete fusion and breakup transmission coefficients in collisions of weakly bound projectiles at near barrier energies are assessed. We show that they strongly depend on the adopted classical trajectory and can be significantly improved with proper treatment of the incident and emergent currents in the WKB approximation.

## Key claim

The commonly used factorization of the complete fusion transmission coefficient into a tunneling factor times a survival probability is a reasonable approximation only depending on the classical trajectory chosen, but it is always inaccurate near the Coulomb barrier; an improved WKB treatment of incident and emergent currents yields accurate fusion cross sections across all energies.

## Method

The authors tested approximations for complete fusion and breakup transmission coefficients in the specific system of 11Li + 12C, using a standard strong-absorption optical potential and a schematic polarization potential with constant real and imaginary strengths of 2.0 MeV. They compared exact quantum mechanical calculations with several semiclassical approximations: the Hill-Wheeler formula, a WKB tunneling factor times a survival probability evaluated along Rutherford or nuclear-inclusive trajectories, and an improved WKB approach that directly integrates the complex wavenumber over the full radial range without separating classically allowed and forbidden regions.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for 11Li + 12C: V_B = 2.67 MeV
- Optical potential parameters: V0_opt = -60 MeV, W0_opt = 60 MeV, r_r0 = 1.25 fm, r_i0 = 1.00 fm, a_r0 = 0.60 fm, a_i0 = 0.60 fm
- Polarization potential strength: W0_pol = 2.0 MeV, V0_pol = -2.0 MeV
- 11Li breakup threshold: B_bu = 0.2 MeV, giving polarization diffuseness alpha = 6.6 fm
- Fusion cross section converges for partial waves up to l = 10; breakup cross section requires partial waves up to l approximately 80

## Context

This work extends the polarization potential approach of Hussein et al. (1992), Takigawa et al. (1993), and Canto et al. (1995, 1998) by testing the semiclassical approximations for transmission coefficients that those studies employed. The improved WKB method resolves the inaccuracies near the Coulomb barrier that plagued earlier factorized approximations, and the treatment of breakup via both entrance and exit branches of the trajectory corrects the approach of Hussein et al. (1992). The results enable more reliable calculations of complete fusion and breakup cross sections for weakly bound radioactive beams at near-barrier energies, relevant to astrophysical processes and superheavy element searches.
