# Hot Spot model of Nucleon and Double Parton Scattering

> We calculate the rate of double parton scattering (DPS) in proton - proton collisions in the framework of the recently proposed hot spot model of the nucleon structure. The resulting rate, especially for the case of three hot spots, appears to be in tension with the current experimental data on DPS at the LHC.

# Hot Spot model of Nucleon and Double Parton Scattering

## Key claim
The hot spot model of nucleon structure, when used to calculate double parton scattering (DPS) rates, produces effective cross sections that are in tension with experimental LHC data, particularly for the case of three hot spots (N_q=3), even after accounting for the fact that experimental data at higher Q^2 must be evolved downward to the low scales where the model is formulated.

## Method
The authors calculate the DPS effective cross section σ_eff using the hot spot model, where gluons are concentrated around N_q points (hot spots) with Gaussian transverse distributions of width B_q, centered within a proton of size B_p, and with fluctuating strengths p_i following a log-normal distribution. They evaluate the event-by-event cross section by averaging over hot spot positions, impact parameter, and strength fluctuations, separating the calculation into three classes of diagrams depending on whether the two partons in each proton come from the same or different hot spots. The model parameters are taken from fits to γ p → J/ψ + gap + Y data from Mantysaari et al. (2022).

## Key numbers
- For the variable N_q set (N_q ≈ 6.79), σ_eff ≈ 17 mb.
- For the fixed N_q ≡ 3 set, σ_eff ≈ 10.5 mb.
- Experimental data for σ_eff at Q ∼ 20 GeV and higher give approximately 20 mb.
- Inverse DGLAP evolution from experimental scales to the low scales of the hot spot model yields σ_eff of order 25–35 mb.
- When hot spot strength fluctuations are neglected (σ=0), the variable N_q set gives σ_eff ≈ 32 mb, and the N_q ≡ 3 set gives σ_eff ≈ 17 mb.
- Model parameters from Mantysaari et al. (2022): for variable N_q: B_q = 0.474 GeV^{-2}, B_p = 4.02 GeV^{-2}, σ = 0.833; for N_q ≡ 3: B_q = 0.346 GeV^{-2}, B_p = 4.45 GeV^{-2}, σ = 0.563.

## Context
This work extends the hot spot model of nucleon structure developed by Mantysaari et al. (2022) for exclusive J/ψ production to the domain of double parton scattering, which is sensitive to parton-parton correlations in the transverse plane. The results contradict the mean-field approach to multiparton interactions (MPI) and show tension with experimental DPS measurements from ATLAS and CMS collaborations, as reviewed by B. Blok and M. Strikman. The analysis demonstrates that DPS data can serve as a constraint on models of nucleon structure, and suggests that increasing the number of hot spots or including hot spot strength fluctuations can reduce the tension, though pQCD evolution only worsens the discrepancy for the N_q=3 case.
