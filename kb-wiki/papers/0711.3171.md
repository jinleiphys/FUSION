# Deformation or spherical symmetry in 10Be and the inversion of 1/2- - 1/2+ states in 11Be

> For a core plus one neutron system like 11Be we have calculated the energies of the 1/2- and 1/2+ states assuming a deformation of the core deduced from the low energy 2+ state properties or taking into account the coupling of the neutron with this 2+ state interpreted as a spherical one-phonon state. We have shown that the two derivations yield identical results if the phonon energy is neglected in the second derivation and close results in the general case.

# Deformation or spherical symmetry in 10Be and the inversion of 1/2- - 1/2+ states in 11Be

## Key claim

The two apparently conflicting models for the inversion of 1/2- and 1/2+ states in 11Be, one assuming a deformed 10Be core (Nunes et al.) and the other assuming a spherical core with a one-phonon vibrational 2+ state (Vinh Mau), yield identical results for the energy shifts when the phonon energy is neglected, and close results in the general case where the phonon energy is included.

## Method

The author derived analytic expressions for the energy shift of the 1/2- and 1/2+ neutron states in 11Be using second-order perturbation theory for two different core models. In the deformed model, the neutron-core potential is a deformed Woods-Saxon potential expanded to first order in the deformation parameter β₂. In the spherical model, the potential includes a particle-phonon coupling term for a 2+ vibrational phonon. The two resulting energy-shift formulas were compared analytically.

## Key numbers

- The 2+ state in 10Be is at 3.36 MeV, used as the rotational state in the deformed model and as the one-phonon vibrational state in the spherical model.
- The deformation parameter β₂ is deduced from the measured B(E2) transition strength and is interpreted either as a deformation parameter (deformed model) or as a collective transition amplitude (spherical model).
- The phonon energy E₂ appears in the denominators of the spherical-model energy shift formula; when E₂ is set to zero, the two formulas become identical.
- The spherical-model energy shift is smaller than the deformed-model shift because E₂ appears in the denominators (phonon energy is a few MeV, but in the limit of strong collectivity it approaches zero).

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between two prior theoretical approaches to the 1/2-–1/2+ inversion in 11Be: the deformed-core model of Nunes et al. and the spherical-core particle-phonon coupling model of Vinh Mau. The author shows analytically that the two models are equivalent for the 1/2+ and 1/2- states when the phonon energy is neglected, and that they give close results otherwise. This equivalence is supported by large-basis shell model calculations (Elliott) and by a recent analysis of the p(11Be,10Be)d reaction (Pitthan) where wave functions from both models gave equally good agreement with experiment. The work also connects to deformed Hartree-Fock calculations by Li and Heenen and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Girod, which find spherical minima for 10Be and 11Be in mean-field approximation but deformed configurations when correlations are added.
