# Diquark confinement in an extended NJL model

> In a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model supplemented with an infrared cutoff in addition to the ultraviolet cutoff we study the issue whether diquarks are confined when the model is extended beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation. The gap equation, obtained in a truncation scheme motivated via a nontrivial quark-gluon vertex function, is solved to determine the constituent quark mass if chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken. In a second step, the Bethe-Salpeter equations for mesons and diquarks beyond ladder approximation are derived, taking care to preserve Goldstone's theorem in the pion channel. While the obtained masses of pseudoscalar and vector mesons are only moderately shifted compared to the values in ladder approximation, we observe that scalar diquarks disappear from the physical spectrum and therefore are confined. For axialvector diquarks we observe indications, that the same mechanism may also work, but the NJL model allows no conclusive answer in this channel.

## Key claim

In a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model supplemented with an infrared cutoff, scalar diquarks become confined (disappear from the physical spectrum) when the model is extended beyond rainbow-ladder approximation, but the NJL model allows no conclusive answer for axialvector diquarks.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved beyond rainbow approximation by including a vertex correction to first order in the coupling constant G, using a generalized proper-time regularization scheme with both ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs. The Bethe-Salpeter equations for mesons and diquarks were then derived beyond ladder approximation by cutting internal quark lines in the self-energy diagrams, preserving Goldstone's theorem in the pion channel. The resulting two-loop integrals were approximated using dipole parametrizations of the vertex correction.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass in rainbow approximation: 0.3873 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.400 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Constituent quark mass beyond rainbow approximation: 0.4194 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.4295 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Pion mass in ladder approximation: 0 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.207 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Pion mass beyond ladder approximation: 0 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.182 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Scalar diquark mass in ladder approximation: 0.762 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.786 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV); beyond ladder approximation: unbound
- Vector meson mass in ladder approximation: 0.612 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.630 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Vector meson mass beyond ladder approximation: 0.737 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.749 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV)
- Axialvector diquark mass in ladder approximation: 0.612 GeV (chiral limit) and 0.630 GeV (with m0 = 0.017 GeV); beyond ladder approximation: unbound
- Parameters used: ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 0.630 GeV, coupling G = 184.18 GeV^{-2}, current quark mass m0 = 0.017 GeV, infrared cutoff mu = 0.1 GeV

## Context

This work tests a conjecture from Ben96 that diquark confinement can be obtained when the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation are considered beyond rainbow and ladder approximation, previously studied in the Munczek-Nemirovsky model (Mun83). The authors use an NJL model with an infrared cutoff (following Ebe96) to mimic quark confinement, finding that scalar diquarks are confined while vector meson masses shift moderately upward. This enables future baryon calculations where diquarks enter as confined effective constituents, eliminating quark-diquark thresholds in baryon descriptions (Hel97).
