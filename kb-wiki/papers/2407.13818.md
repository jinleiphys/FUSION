# Imaging the Wakes of Jets with Energy-Energy-Energy Correlators

> As the partons in a jet propagate through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in a heavy-ion collision, they lose energy to, kick, and are kicked by the medium. The resulting modifications to the parton shower encode information about the microscopic nature of QGP. The momentum and energy lost by the parton shower are gained by the medium and, since QGP is a strongly coupled liquid, this means that the jet excites a wake in the droplet of QGP. After freezeout, this wake becomes soft hadrons with net momentum in the jet direction meaning that reconstructed jets include hadrons originating from both the modified parton shower and its wake. This makes it challenging to find an unambiguous experimental view of the response of a droplet of QGP to a jet. Recent years have seen significant advances in the understanding of the substructure of jets using correlation functions of the energy flux operator. So far, such studies have focused primarily on the two-point correlator, which serves to identify the angular scale of the underlying dynamics. Higher-point correlators hold the promise of mapping out the dynamics themselves. We perform the first study of the shape-dependent three-point energy-energy-energy correlator in heavy-ion collisions. Using the Hybrid Model to simulate the interactions of high energy jets with QGP, we show that hadrons originating from wakes are the dominant contribution to the three-point correlator in the regime where the three points are well-separated in angle, forming a roughly equilateral triangle. This equilateral region of the correlator is far from the region populated by collinear vacuum emissions, making it a canvas on which jet wakes can be imaged. Our work is a key step towards the systematic use of energy correlators to image and unravel the dynamical response of a droplet of QGP to a passing jet, and motivates many experimental and theoretical studies.

## Key claim

Using the Hybrid Model to simulate jets in heavy-ion collisions, the authors show that the three-point energy-energy-energy correlator (EEEC) has a kinematic region, where the three angular points form a roughly equilateral triangle, in which hadrons originating from the wake excited by the jet in the quark-gluon plasma are the dominant contribution, far from the collinear region populated by vacuum emissions.

## Method

The authors simulate high-energy jets propagating through quark-gluon plasma using the Hybrid Model, which couples a perturbative parton shower to a strongly coupled hydrodynamic medium via holography-inspired energy loss and includes a crude implementation of the wake as soft hadrons from freezeout of the hydrodynamically excited medium. They compute the three-point energy-energy-energy correlator (EEEC) from reconstructed anti-kT jets with R=0.8, both with the wake contribution included and with it turned off, and analyze the correlator as a function of the triangle shape parameters (ξ, φ) and the overall angular scale RL.

## Key numbers

- The study uses jets reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.8, and always chooses RL < R.
- Most results use energy weighting exponent n=1.0.
- The Hybrid Model wake implementation yields too many hadrons with pT < 2 GeV/c and too few with pT between 2 and 4 GeV/c compared to available data.
- The equilateral region of the EEEC is identified as the regime where the three points are well-separated in angle, forming a roughly equilateral triangle, and is far from the region populated by collinear vacuum emissions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the two-point energy-energy correlator (E2C) in heavy-ion collisions by Andres (2022, 2023), Barata (2023, 2024), and others, which found an enhancement at large angles but could not distinguish wake contributions from medium-induced parton shower modifications. The authors introduce the shape-dependent three-point correlator as a tool to isolate wake effects, building on the theoretical framework for energy correlators developed by Basham (1978), Hofman (2008), and Chen (2019, 2020), and on the Hybrid Model for jet-medium interactions introduced by Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2016). This work enables future experimental measurements of the EEEC in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC to directly image the shape of jet wakes, and motivates improved theoretical treatments of the wake beyond the crude Hybrid Model implementation.
