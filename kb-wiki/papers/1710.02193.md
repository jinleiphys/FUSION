# Nuclear spin dependence of time reversal invariance violating effects in neutron scattering

> The spin structure of parity violating and time reversal invariance violating effects in neutron scattering is discussed. The explicit relations between these effects are presented in terms of functions nuclear spins and neutron partial widths of p-wave resonances.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit spin-dependent factors (parameter κ) relating parity violating (PV) and time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects in neutron scattering, correcting misprints in earlier publications. The authors show that while κ has a "natural" value of order unity, it can fluctuate significantly across different p-wave resonances due to unknown spin-dependent partial neutron width amplitudes, meaning that resonances with the largest PV effects may not have the largest TRIV effects.

## Method

The authors used the distorted wave Born approximation to first order in parity and time reversal violating interactions, expressing the forward scattering amplitudes for polarized neutrons on polarized targets in terms of R-matrix elements in the channel spin representation (J = l + S, where S = s + I). They derived explicit formulas for the ratio κ of TRIV to PV cross section differences at p-wave resonances with total spin J = I+1/2 and J = I-1/2, expressing these in terms of the unknown amplitudes of neutron partial widths for different channel spins.

## Key numbers

- The PV asymmetry in the 0.734 eV p-wave resonance in 139La has been measured to be (9.56 ± 0.35)·10^{-2}
- TRIV effects in neutron-nucleus scattering can have a discovery potential 10^2 to 10^4 more sensitive than current limits
- For J=0 p-wave resonances in nuclei with spin I=1/2, the parameter κ = 1 exactly, independent of neutron partial widths
- The suppression factor between the ratio of TRIV to PV matrix elements and the corresponding ratio of nucleon coupling constants is about A^{1/3} (where A is the atomic number)
- The first measurement of κ, x, and y parameters was performed for the 0.74 eV p-wave compound nuclear resonance of 139La

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TRIV searches in neutron transmission developed by Bunakov (1982), Gudkov (1990, 1991), and Bowman (2014), correcting a misprint in Eq.(3) of Gudkov (1990) that propagated through subsequent publications. The results enable the optimal choice of nuclear targets and p-wave resonances for proposed TRIV experiments, and provide the formalism needed to analyze experimental data from measurements of angular correlations in (n,γ) reactions as described by Sushkov (1985) and from pseudomagnetic neutron spin rotation as proposed by Gudkov (2017).
