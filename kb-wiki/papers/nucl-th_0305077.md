# Self-consistent random phase approximation in the Schutte-Da-Providencia fermion-boson model

> Self-consistent random phase approximation (SCRPA) is applied to the exactly solvable model with fermion boson coupling proposed by Schütte and Da-Providencia. Very encouraging results in comparison with the exact solution of the model for various observables are obtained. The transition from the normal phase to the phase with a spontaneously broken symmetry is carefully investigated. The strong reduction of the variance in SCRPA vs HF is pointed out.

## Key claim

Self-consistent random phase approximation (SCRPA) with an extended RPA operator that includes boson pair terms improves results for the Schütte-Da-Providencia model compared to SCRPA with only linear boson terms, but the Goldstone theorem remains violated in the symmetry-broken phase: the low-lying spurious mode is lowered by approximately 30% but still does not decouple from the physical spectrum.

## Method

The author applies SCRPA to the exactly solvable Schütte-Da-Providencia fermion-boson model, using an RPA excitation operator that includes fermion pairs, linear boson terms, and quadratic boson pair terms (SCRPA(6)), extending the earlier SCRPA(4) approach that omitted the boson pairs. The amplitudes are determined via the equation-of-motion method with a cranked Hamiltonian \(H' = H - \mu P\) in the symmetry-broken phase, and the expectation value \(\langle T^0 \rangle\) is expressed as a series in \(T^+\) and \(T^-\) operators to close the self-consistency equations. Results are compared with exact solutions, standard RPA, and the earlier SCRPA(4) for the chemical potential, energy differences, RPA eigenvalues, boson number, and variance of the symmetry operator.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential \(\mu\) at \(x=1.4\): exact = 0.0325, SCRPA(6) = 0.0348, SCRPA(4) = 0.0405, standard RPA = 0.1303 (Table I).
- Chemical potential \(\mu\) at \(x=2.2\): exact = 0.0320, SCRPA(6) = 0.0300, SCRPA(4) = 0.0240, standard RPA = 0.0345 (Table I).
- Chemical potential \(\mu\) at \(x=3.0\): exact = 0.0204, SCRPA(6) = 0.0195, SCRPA(4) = 0.0135, standard RPA = 0.0071 (Table I).
- The low-lying spurious mode in the deformed region is lowered by approximately 30% when boson pair terms are added to the RPA operator (from SCRPA(4) to SCRPA(6)).
- The phase transition point is localized at \(x = 1.0\), where \(x = G\sqrt{N/\alpha}\), with parameters \(\alpha = 3\) and \(N = 30\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier SCRPA study of the Schütte-Da-Providencia model by Bertrand et al. (2001), which used only linear boson terms in the RPA operator and found strong violation of the Goldstone theorem. The present inclusion of boson pair terms improves results for the chemical potential, boson number, and variance of the symmetry operator, and allows calculation of an additional excited state (\(\Omega_3\)) in the spherical region. However, the Goldstone mode problem persists, and the author notes that further ideas are needed to substantially improve the situation in the symmetry-broken region, similar to challenges faced by other beyond-mean-field approaches like coupled cluster theory and correlated basis function methods.
