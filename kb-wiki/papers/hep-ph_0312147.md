# Chiral perturbation theory for pentaquark baryons and its applications

> We construct a chiral lagrangian for pentaquark baryons assuming that the recently found Theta^+ (1540) state belongs to an antidecuplet of SU(3) flavor symmetry with J^P = (1/2)^(+-). We derive the Gell-Mann-Okubo formulae for the antidecuplet baryon masses, and a possible mixing between the antidecuplet and the pentaquark octet. Then we calculate the cross sections for pi^- p -> K^- Theta^+ and gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ using our chiral lagrangian. The resulting amplitudes respect the underlying chiral symmetry of QCD correctly. We also describe how to include the light vector mesons in the chiral lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a chiral Lagrangian for pentaquark baryons assuming the Theta^+ (1540) state belongs to an SU(3) flavor antidecuplet with J^P = (1/2)^(+-), and calculates cross sections for hadroproduction and photoproduction that depend on the unknown parity and couplings; the results are presented rescaled by the factor 1 MeV / Gamma_Theta because the Theta^+ decay width is not precisely known.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian for pentaquark baryons at lowest order in the derivative expansion, treating the Theta^+ as a member of an SU(3) antidecuplet with either positive or negative parity. They derive the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formulae for the antidecuplet and a mixing term between the antidecuplet and a pentaquark octet. Using this Lagrangian, they compute the Feynman diagrams for pi^- p -> K^- Theta^+ (including both t-channel nucleon exchange and s-channel antidecuplet N-tilde exchange) and for gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ (including a contact term required by gauge invariance), then evaluate the cross sections as functions of beam energy.

## Key numbers

* Theta^+ (1540) mass: 1540 MeV, with a very narrow width less than 5 MeV.
* Pion decay constant f = 93 MeV used in the chiral Lagrangian.
* Predicted masses from the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula: m_N-tilde = 1647 MeV, m_Sigma-tilde = 1755 MeV, assuming a state at 1862 +/- 2 MeV is identified as Xi_{3/2}.
* Coupling squared for the pentaquark-nucleon interaction: C^2_{PN}(P=+1) = 2.7 x Gamma_Theta/GeV and C^2_{PN}(P=-1) = 0.90 x Gamma_Theta/GeV.
* Nucleon anomalous magnetic moments used: kappa_p = 1.79, kappa_n = -1.91, giving kappa_D = 2.87 and kappa_F = 0.836.
* The coupling H_{PN} is varied between -4 and 4 in the numerical analysis.
* The pentaquark magnetic moment kappa_Theta is varied between -1 and 1.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework, previously applied to ordinary baryons and mesons (Manohar 1996), to the newly discovered pentaquark states. It provides predictions for pi^- p -> K^- Theta^+ and gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ cross sections that go beyond earlier calculations by including the s-channel antidecuplet exchange diagram and the contact term required by chiral symmetry. The results enable experimental determination of the Theta^+ parity and couplings from hadroproduction and photoproduction data, and the framework can be extended to other processes such as gamma p -> K^0 Theta^+ or K^+ p -> pi^+ Theta^+.
