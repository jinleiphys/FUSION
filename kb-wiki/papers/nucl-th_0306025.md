# Kinetic description of charmonium production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We study the evolution of charmonia as they collide with the constituents of the fireball produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The latter evolves in a manner controlled by the equation of state as given by lattice QCD, and is constructed in such a way that the observed hadronic spectra are correctly reproduced. A kinetic description of charmonium interactions with both quark-gluon and hadronic degrees of freedom allows to study in detail the evolution in different regimes, controlled by collision energy, kinematics and geometry. The data collected at the CERN-SPS accelerator are well described and new estimates for J/psi production at BNL-RHIC are presented.

## Key claim

The authors argue that charmonium (J/ψ) suppression in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described by a kinetic approach without invoking Debye screening, provided the fireball evolution is independently constrained by hadronic observables rather than fitted to J/ψ data. The model reproduces SPS data and gives qualitative predictions for RHIC, but the authors caution that the RHIC extension relies on assumptions about freeze-out parameters and baryon density that have not yet been rigorously determined.

## Method

The authors construct a thermal fireball whose evolution is fixed by lattice QCD thermodynamics (using a quasi-particle model with temperature-dependent masses and a confinement factor) and by reproducing measured hadronic spectra at freeze-out. They then solve a kinetic transport equation for J/ψ mesons interacting with the fireball constituents, using the Bhanot-Peskin cross section for dissociation in the quark-gluon plasma and detailed balance for possible regeneration via c-cbar coalescence. The initial charm and charmonium production is computed within a Glauber framework with nuclear absorption, using leading-order perturbative QCD with a K-factor of 2 and GRV94LO parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the QCD transition: Tc = 170 MeV (for two massless flavors)
- Charm quark mass used: mc = 1.5 GeV; factorization scale: μc = 1.4 GeV
- K-factor for open charm production: K = 2
- Nuclear absorption cross section for J/ψ at SPS energy (√s = 17.3 GeV): σNUC_J/ψN = 4.5 mb
- Energy scaling exponent for nuclear absorption: λ = 0.12
- Thermalization time: 1.0 fm/c at SPS, 0.6 fm/c at RHIC
- Initial temperature for central collisions: T0 = 320 MeV at SPS, T0 = 390 MeV at RHIC
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 100 MeV at SPS, Tf = 130 MeV at RHIC
- Freeze-out proper time: τf = 18 fm/c at SPS, 17 fm/c at RHIC
- Initial and final transverse RMS radii: R0 = 4.8 fm, Rf = 9.0 fm (both SPS and RHIC)
- Number of c-cbar pairs in Au+Au collisions grows by nearly two orders of magnitude from SPS to RHIC energies

## Context

This work extends the kinetic treatment of charmonium in heavy-ion collisions beyond earlier approaches that fitted suppression exclusively to J/ψ data, instead using a fireball model previously applied to dilepton production by Renk, Schneider, and Weise (2002). The quasi-particle description of the QGP equation of state follows Schneider and Weise (2001), incorporating lattice QCD results. The authors contrast their approach with models invoking Debye screening (e.g., DPS01, W01) and with conventional nuclear effect models (e.g., HK98, HKP01, KTH01). The work enables future studies of J/ψ at RHIC and LHC energies where regeneration from the large charm quark population may become significant, as suggested by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2000) and others.
