# Relativistic rank-one separable kernel for helium-3 charge form factor

> Elastic electron-helium-3 scattering is studied in the relativistic impulse approximation. The amplitudes for the three-nucleon system are obtained by solving the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equations with a separable rank-one kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The contribution of partial-wave states with nonzero orbital moment of a nucleon pair is investigated. The static approximation and additional relativistic corrections for the 3He charge form factor are calculated for the momentum transfer squared up to 100 fm^-2. The contribution of the 3D1 partial-wave state and relativistic corrections are found to be sizable at high energies.

## Key claim

The relativistic corrections to the helium-3 charge form factor are sizable at high momentum transfer squared, but the simple rank-one separable kernel considered in the paper does not reproduce the diffraction minima of the form factor, which exist in the experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equations (Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations) with a rank-one separable kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, using relativistic Yamaguchi and Tabakin potentials. They calculated the charge form factor of helium-3 in the relativistic impulse approximation, considering the static approximation and three types of relativistic corrections: the boost contribution from the Lorentz transformation of the propagator arguments, the pole contribution from a simple pole on the complex variable q0, and the expansion contribution from a Taylor series expansion of the final wave function arguments.

## Key numbers

- The charge form factor was calculated for momentum transfer squared up to 100 fm^-2.
- For the SS case (only ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 partial-wave states), relativistic corrections are higher than the static approximation in the region 20-100 fm^-2 by a maximum factor of 1.3 at t=50 fm^-2.
- For the SSD4 case (including ^3D_1 partial-wave state), relativistic corrections are higher than the static approximation in the region 15-100 fm^-2 by a maximum factor of 1.5 at t=50 fm^-2.
- For SS calculations at t=50 fm^-2, the expansion contribution is about 70%, the boost contribution is about 50%, and the pole contribution is about -20%.
- For SS calculations at t=100 fm^-2, the expansion contribution is about 58%, the boost contribution is about 60%, and the pole contribution is about -18%.
- Taking into account the D wave in the function tau (SSD4) leads to a static approximation lower by a factor of 0.7-0.65 at t=20-30 fm^-2 and by a factor of 0.6 at t=50-100 fm^-2.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Bondarenko et al. (2017, 2018, 2019) that solved the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations for rank-one separable kernels but considered only ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 partial-wave states and used the static approximation. The paper adds the contribution of ^3D_1, ^3P_0, ^3P_1, and ^1P_1 partial-wave states and three types of relativistic corrections. The results show that neither the inclusion of the ^3D_1 state nor the relativistic corrections produce the diffraction minimum in the form factor, which was obtained in earlier work by Rupp (1991) using a multirank kernel, though at an incorrect momentum transfer. This enables future investigation of how these relativistic corrections affect the diffraction minimum position when combined with multirank kernels.
