# Multi-particle potentials from light-like Wilson lines in quark-gluon plasmas: a generalized relation of in-medium splitting rates to jet-quenching parameters $\hat q$

> A powerful historical insight about the theory of in-medium showering in QCD backgrounds was that splitting rates can be related to a parameter $\hat q$ that characterizes the rate of transverse-momentum kicks to a high-energy particle from the medium. Another powerful insight was that $\hat q$ can be defined (with caveats) even when the medium is strongly coupled, using long, narrow Wilson loops whose two long edges are light-like Wilson lines. The medium effects for the original calculations of in-medium splitting rates can be formulated in terms of 3-body imaginary-valued `potentials' that are defined with three long, light-like Wilson lines. Corrections due to overlap of two consecutive splittings can be calculated using similarly defined 4-body potentials. I give a simple argument for how such $N$-body potentials can be determined in the appropriate limit just from knowledge of the values of $\hat q$ for different color representations. For $N > 3$, the $N$-body potentials have non-trivial color structure, which will complicate calculations of overlap corrections outside of the large-$N_c$ or soft bremsstrahlung limits.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for any number \(N\) of high-energy particles in a quark-gluon plasma, the imaginary-valued "potential" describing medium effects on splitting processes can be determined in the harmonic oscillator approximation solely from knowledge of the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat q\) for each particle's color representation and for the combined color representations of each pair, but this result holds only in the limit of small transverse separations and with the caveat that \(\hat q\) itself has logarithmic dependence on the separation scale.

## Method

The author generalizes the known relation between the 3-body potential for in-medium splitting rates and the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat q\) to \(N\)-body potentials, using a simple argument based on translation invariance and the reduction of the \(N\)-body problem to effective 2-body and 3-body problems when subsets of particles are placed at coincident transverse positions. The argument is first demonstrated for weakly-coupled plasmas using color generator algebra and then extended to any coupling strength by requiring that the harmonic oscillator form of the potential be fixed by the constraints from these special cases, yielding a result where the \(N\)-body potential is expressed as a sum over pairs with coefficients involving \(\hat q_i\), \(\hat q_j\), and a color operator \(\hat q_{ij}\) representing the combined color representation of particles \(i\) and \(j\).

## Key numbers

- The 2-body potential for small transverse separation \(\Delta b\) is given by \(V(\Delta b) \to -\frac{i}{4} \hat q \, (\Delta b)^2\), where \(\hat q\) is the jet-quenching parameter defined by \(\langle Q_\perp^2 \rangle = \hat q \, \Delta z\).
- The 3-body potential in harmonic approximation is \(V(\mathbf{b}_1,\mathbf{b}_2,\mathbf{b}_3) = -\frac{i}{8} [(\hat q_1+\hat q_2-\hat q_3)(\mathbf{b}_2-\mathbf{b}_1)^2 + (\hat q_2+\hat q_3-\hat q_1)(\mathbf{b}_3-\mathbf{b}_2)^2 + (\hat q_3+\hat q_1-\hat q_2)(\mathbf{b}_1-\mathbf{b}_3)^2]\).
- The general \(N\)-body potential is \(\underline{V}(\mathbf{b}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{b}_N) = -\frac{i}{8} \sum_{i>j} (\hat q_i+\hat q_j-\underline{\hat q}_{ij}) (\mathbf{b}_i-\mathbf{b}_j)^2\), where \(\underline{\hat q}_{ij}\) is a color operator acting on the \(N\)-particle color space.
- For weakly-coupled plasmas, \(\hat q_R = C_R \hat{\bar q}\), with \(C_R\) the quadratic Casimir of representation \(R\), and the potential reduces to \(V(\mathbf{b}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{b}_N) \simeq \frac{i}{4} \sum_{i>j} \mathbf{T}_i\cdot\mathbf{T}_j \, \hat{\bar q} \, (\mathbf{b}_i-\mathbf{b}_j)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the historical insights of Baier et al. (BDMPS, 1997) and Zakharov (1996) on the QCD Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect, who originally related in-medium splitting rates to \(\hat q\) and formulated the problem in terms of 3-body potentials. The paper generalizes the relation to \(N\)-body potentials for \(N>3\), which are needed to compute corrections from overlapping formation times of consecutive splittings, a problem previously addressed only in simplifying limits such as soft emission (Blaizot, Iancu, Wu, 2013) or large-\(N_c\) (Arnold, 2013). The result enables calculations of overlap effects without those assumptions, though the non-trivial color structure for \(N>3\) complicates such calculations outside the large-\(N_c\) or soft bremsstrahlung limits.
