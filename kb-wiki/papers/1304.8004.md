# Stabilizing perturbative Yang-Mills thermodynamics with Gribov quantization

> We evaluate the thermodynamic quantities of Yang-Mills theory using the Gribov quantization, which deals with nonperturbative resummation. The magnetic scale is automatically incorporated into the framework and we find it efficient to stabilize the perturbative expansion of the free energy. In the temperature range $T=T_c \sim 2\,T_c$ the major uncertainty in our results comes from the nonperturbative running coupling that is adopted from the lattice simulation, while the convergence above $2\,T_c$ is impressively robust. We also present the corresponding interaction measure (i.e., trace anomaly) up to close to $T_c$.

## Key claim
The Gribov quantization, which incorporates a nonperturbative magnetic scale via the Gribov mass parameter, stabilizes the perturbative expansion of the Yang-Mills free energy; the major uncertainty in the temperature range T = T_c to 2 T_c comes from the lattice-adopted nonperturbative running coupling, while convergence above 2 T_c is robust.

## Method
The authors evaluate the Yang-Mills free energy using Gribov quantization, which restricts the functional integration to the Gribov region via a no-pole condition. The Gribov mass parameter is determined self-consistently from a gap equation solved numerically with dimensional regularization. The gluon and ghost propagators are then used in a quasiparticle approximation to compute the free energy, with the running coupling adopted from lattice measurements of the heavy-quark free energy.

## Key numbers
- The Gribov mass parameter at high temperature scales as m_G ~ (N_c/(4√2π)) g^2 T, with N_c = 3 for SU(3) Yang-Mills.
- The renormalization scale is fixed at μ = 1.69 GeV to reproduce the T = 0 ghost dressing function from lattice data.
- The uncertainty in the free energy at 2.5 T_c is about 35% that of three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), and about 15% at 5 T_c.
- The ghost dressing function is nearly T-independent, with numerical results shown at T = 200 MeV and T = 400 MeV using the UV running coupling (c = 2.97).

## Context
This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach to finite temperature, building on the earlier attempt by Zwanziger (2006) to use Gribov quasiparticles for the equation of state. It addresses the Linde problem (the breakdown of perturbation theory at the magnetic scale g^2 T) and provides results consistent with lattice data from Borsanyi et al. (2012). The approach enables systematic inclusion of magnetic-scale effects in Yang-Mills thermodynamics, with future extensions including the Polyakov loop for deconfinement and application to full QCD.
