# Planar versus non-planar anti-NN annihilation into mesons in the light of anti-qq operators and the 1/Nc expansion

> It is argued that in the antiproton-proton annihilation into two mesons $\bar pp\longrightarrow m_1 m_2$, the origin of different restrictive angular momentum selection rules commonly obtained for planar annihilation diagrams $A2$ and for non-planar rearrangement diagrams $R2$ lies in the omission of momentum transfer between an annihilated antiquark-quark pair and a remaining quark or antiquark. If it is included, there is no reason for dismissing one type of diagram in favor of another one. Some considerations in the large $N_c$ limit of QCD equally shed light on the planar and non-planar contributions to the total $\bar NN\to m_1 m_2$ annihilation amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the different angular momentum selection rules commonly attributed to planar annihilation diagrams (A2) versus non-planar rearrangement diagrams (R2) in antiproton-proton annihilation into two mesons arise from an unrealistic omission of momentum transfer between the annihilated antiquark-quark pair and the remaining quarks; once momentum transfer is included, both diagram types yield the same selection rules and neither can be dismissed in favor of the other.

## Method

The author analyzes the transition operators for the non-planar R2 diagram in a constituent quark model, deriving explicit expressions for the cases where the annihilated quark-antiquark pair carries vacuum (³P₀) or gluon (³S₁) quantum numbers, including momentum-transfer-dependent terms. The resulting operators are then sandwiched between two-pion and nucleon-antinucleon wave functions to determine the allowed orbital angular momenta ℓ_{ππ} of the final pions. Separately, the large-N_c limit of QCD is used to count the N_c-order of the R2 and A2 diagrams by generalizing the quark-line diagrams to N_c colors and applying combinatorial factors from quark-antiquark pairings and gluon couplings.

## Key numbers

- The large-N_c analysis yields an N_c-order for the R2 diagram generalization of approximately N_c! / N_c^{N_c} → e^{-N_c} for N_c → ∞, using Stirling's approximation.
- The A2 diagram generalization is suppressed by an additional factor 1/N_c² relative to the R2 case.
- The cross section for N̅N annihilation into N_c−k mesons in the large-N_c limit behaves as exp(−4N_c ε₀ r²_m m) × exp{[1/2 − 3/2 ln(3/2)] N_c} (η N_c)^{2k}, where η² ≈ 0.25 is the non-perturbative annihilation/creation probability.
- For annihilation into N_c−k+l mesons (with l created quarks), the cross section differs by an additional factor η^{2l}, with η < 1.

## Context

This work extends the debate between Dover et al. (1980s), who argued for A2 diagram dominance based on SU(3) symmetry breaking and restrictive selection rules, and Hartmann et al., who favored R2 topology from observed branching ratios of π⁺ρ⁻ versus K̅K*. The author's quark-model calculations with momentum transfer (building on Kloet and El-Bennich, early 2000s) show that both R2 and A2 diagrams allow ℓ_{ππ} = 0,1,2,3,... waves, contradicting the earlier restrictive rules. The large-N_c analysis (following 't Hooft, 1974; Witten, 1979; and Pirner, 1990s) confirms that neither topology dominates, and the R2 diagrams alone (with both ³P₀ and ³S₁ mechanisms) suffice to reproduce LEAR data on p̅p → π⁻π⁺ differential cross sections and analyzing powers.
