# Critical Point and Deconfinement from Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We employ the Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark and gluon propagators in order to study QCD with 2+1 flavours at finite temperature and density. In a suitable truncation for these equations, we determine the position of the critical end-point as well as the deconfinement temperature at all chemical potentials. For the latter, the Polyakov-loop potential is obtained from the QCD propagators. This is possible for the first time at finite chemical potential, with implications for effective models.

## Key claim

The authors determine the critical end-point and deconfinement temperature in QCD with 2+1 flavours using Dyson-Schwinger equations, but the critical end-point is only constrained to lie at a ratio of chemical potential to temperature greater than one, not precisely located.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators at finite temperature and density, using a quenched gluon propagator from lattice QCD with full temperature dependence and adding the quark loop to account for unquenching. The quark-gluon vertex is modeled with a phenomenological ansatz. The Polyakov-loop potential is extracted by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation for a constant background field, which shifts the Matsubara frequencies.

## Key numbers

- The chiral restoration and deconfinement temperatures are found to be near-by, with the critical end-point located at a ratio of chemical potential to temperature greater than one (μ/T > 1).
- Including the strange quark reduces the critical temperature Tc by about 50 MeV for all chemical potentials, comparing the Nf=2 and Nf=2+1 cases.
- At zero chemical potential, the Polyakov-loop potential shows a minimum close to the confining value φ = 2/3 below Tc, and above Tc the minimum moves to smaller φ (larger Polyakov loop).
- At T = 115 MeV, the Polyakov-loop potential is shown as a function of chemical potential, with the minimum shifting from the confining value.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to finite chemical potential, where previously the Polyakov-loop potential could only be extracted at zero density. The results are compared to lattice QCD data for the quark condensate at zero chemical potential from Borsanyi (2010). The dual condensate method from Gattringer (2006) is used to obtain the deconfinement line, and the Polyakov-loop potential extraction follows the development in Fister (2013). The truncation scheme enables future improvements to effective models of QCD by providing a first-principles determination of the Polyakov-loop potential at finite density.
