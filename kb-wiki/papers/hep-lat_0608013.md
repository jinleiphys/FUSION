# The transition temperature in QCD

> We present a detailed calculation of the transition temperature in QCD with two light and one heavier (strange) quark mass on lattices with temporal extent N_t =4 and 6. Calculations with improved staggered fermions have been performed for various light to strange quark mass ratios in the range, 0.05 <= m_l/m_s <= 0.5, and with a strange quark mass fixed close to its physical value. From a combined extrapolation to the chiral (m_l -> 0) and continuum (aT = 1/N_t -> 0) limits we find for the transition temperature at the physical point T_c r_0 = 0.457(7) where the scale is set by the Sommer-scale parameter r_0 defined as the distance in the static quark potential at which the slope takes on the value, (dV_qq(r)/dr)_r=r_0 = 1.65/r_0^2. Using the currently best known value for r_0 this translates to a transition temperature T_c = 192(7)(4)MeV. The transition temperature in the chiral limit is about 3% smaller. We discuss current ambiguities in the determination of T_c in physical units and also comment on the universal scaling behavior of thermodynamic quantities in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The transition from hadronic matter to the quark-gluon plasma in QCD with two light and one heavier (strange) quark is a rapid crossover rather than a true phase transition, and the transition temperature at the physical point is determined to be T_c = 192(7)(4) MeV, with the chiral limit value about 3% smaller, contingent on the combined extrapolation to the chiral and continuum limits using improved staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extent N_tau = 4 and 6.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using an improved staggered fermion action (p4fat3 action) with a tree-level O(a^2) improved gauge action, employing the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm to eliminate finite step-size errors. They calculated the chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, and their susceptibilities on lattices of size N_sigma^3 x N_tau with N_tau = 4 and 6 and spatial extents N_sigma = 8, 16, 24, and 32, for light to strange quark mass ratios m_l/m_s ranging from 0.05 to 0.5. Zero-temperature calculations on 16^3 x 32 lattices provided the static quark potential and hadron masses to set the scale via the Sommer parameter r_0.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature at the physical point: T_c r_0 = 0.457(7), where r_0 is the Sommer-scale parameter defined by (dV_qq(r)/dr)_r=r_0 = 1.65/r_0^2
- Using r_0 = 0.469(7) fm, this gives T_c = 192(7)(4) MeV
- Transition temperature in the chiral limit is about 3% smaller than the physical point value
- Light to strange quark mass ratios studied: 0.05 <= m_l/m_s <= 0.5
- Pseudo-scalar (pion) masses ranged from approximately 150 MeV to 500 MeV
- On N_tau = 4 lattices, the difference between critical couplings from Polyakov loop and chiral susceptibilities on 8^3 x 4 lattices was beta_c,L - beta_c,l = 0.0077(9)
- On N_tau = 6 lattices, the largest difference beta_c,L - beta_c,l = 0.019(7) was found for m_l/m_s = 0.2

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the QCD transition temperature performed with improved staggered fermions by Bernard et al. (2004) and with Wilson fermions by Schierholz et al., providing the first combined chiral and continuum limit extrapolation for (2+1)-flavor QCD with almost physical light quark masses. The results enable more precise comparisons with experimental observables from relativistic heavy ion collisions and allow further studies of universal scaling behavior of thermodynamic quantities in the chiral limit, while also highlighting current ambiguities in determining T_c in physical units due to uncertainties in the scale parameter r_0.
