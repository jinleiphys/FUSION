# Causal dissipative hydrodynamics for heavy ion collisions

> We briefly discuss the recent developments in causal dissipative hydrodynamic for relativistic heavy ion collisions. Phenomenological estimate of QGP viscosity over entropy ratio from several experimental data, e.g. STAR's $ϕ$ meson data, centrality dependence of elliptic flow, universal scaling elliptic flow etc. are discussed. QGP viscosity, extracted from hydrodynamical model analysis can have very large systematic uncertainty due to uncertain initial conditions.

## Key claim

Phenomenological estimates of the QGP shear viscosity to entropy ratio (\(\eta/s\)) from multiple experimental observables lie in a narrow range \(\eta/s \approx 0.08\)–\(0.20\), but the systematic uncertainty from uncertain initial conditions in hydrodynamic simulations can be as large as \(\sim 200\%\) or more, preventing a precise determination.

## Method

The paper reviews causal (Israel-Stewart) second-order dissipative hydrodynamics, where shear stress tensor and bulk pressure are treated as extended thermodynamic variables with relaxation equations. The author then describes fits of the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code to STAR \(\phi\) meson data (multiplicity, mean \(p_T\), integrated \(v_2\)), PHENIX charged-particle elliptic flow data, and PHOBOS data on eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow versus multiplicity, extracting \(\eta/s\) via \(\chi^2\) minimization and a Knudsen-number analysis generalized to include entropy generation.

## Key numbers

* \(\eta/s = 0.07 \pm 0.03\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.14\ (\text{sys.})\) from STAR \(\phi\) meson data in 0–5% Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, with systematic uncertainty evaluated over initial time \(\tau_i=0.2\)–\(1.0\) fm, freeze-out temperature \(T_F=140\)–\(160\) MeV, hard-scattering fraction \(f=0\)–\(95\%\), and initial transverse velocity parameter \(\alpha=0\)–\(0.6\).
* \(\eta/s = 0.12 \pm 0.03\) from the minimum of a scaling-violation function \(F\) for \(\pi^-\), \(K^+\), p, \(\phi\), and \(\Omega\) in 0–60% Au+Au collisions, comparing simulated universal scaling to the PHENIX scaling function.
* Best-fit \(\eta/s\) from centrality-dependent elliptic flow (PHENIX charged particles) varies from \(0 \pm 0.03\) (0–10% centrality) to \(0.169 \pm 0.005\) (50–60% centrality) in Au+Au collisions.
* From a generalized Knudsen-number fit to PHOBOS data (Cu+Cu and Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=62\) and \(200\) GeV), \(\eta/s = 0.17 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.20\) after converting the fitted parameter \(\etas = 0.83 \pm 0.51\) using simulation results from Chaudhuri (2009).
* Luzum and Romatschke (2008) obtained \(\eta/s = 0.1 \pm 0.1\ (\text{theory}) \pm 0.08\ (\text{experiment})\) from charged-particle multiplicity, radial flow, and elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This review extends the program of extracting QGP viscosity from relativistic heavy-ion data, following earlier work by Luzum and Romatschke (2008), Song and Heinz (2008), and others who used elliptic flow to bound \(\eta/s\). The paper specifically analyzes STAR \(\phi\) meson data (Abelev et al., 2007) and PHENIX centrality-dependent elliptic flow (Afanasiev et al., 2009) to obtain \(\eta/s\) estimates, and generalizes the Knudsen-number formula of Bhalerao et al. (2005) to account for entropy generation in viscous evolution. The results enable future work to constrain initial conditions and to separate QGP and hadronic contributions to the extracted viscosity, as well as to incorporate bulk viscosity effects (e.g., Song and Heinz, 2009) without significantly altering shear viscosity estimates.
