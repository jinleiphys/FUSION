# Covariant interacting Hadron-Resonance Gas model

> The Hadron-Resonance Gas (HRG) approach - used to model hadronic matter at small baryon potentials $μ_B$ and finite temperature $T$ - is extended to finite and large chemical potentials by introducing interactions between baryons in line with relativistic mean-field theory defining an interacting HRG (IHRG). Using lattice data for $μ_B=0$ as well as information on the nuclear equation of state at $T=0$ we constrain the attractive and repulsive interactions of the IHRG such that it reproduces the lattice equation of state at $μ_B=0$ and the nuclear equation of state at $T=0$ and finite $μ_B$. The formulated covariant approach is thermodynamically consistent and allows us to provide further information on the phase boundary between hadronic and partonic phases of strongly interacting matter by assuming constant thermodynamic potentials.

# Covariant interacting Hadron-Resonance Gas model

## Key claim
The authors extend the standard Hadron-Resonance Gas (HRG) model to finite and large baryon chemical potentials by introducing baryon interactions via relativistic mean-field theory, creating an interacting HRG (IHRG) that simultaneously reproduces lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential and the nuclear equation of state at zero temperature. The model is thermodynamically consistent and can provide information on the phase boundary between hadronic and partonic phases, but only by assuming constant thermodynamic potentials when approaching from the hadronic side.

## Method
The authors combine a relativistic mean-field theory (RMFT) for baryons (nucleons and Delta resonances) with a standard HRG for mesons and resonances, subtracting the non-interacting nucleon contribution to avoid double counting. The attractive and repulsive interactions are constrained by fitting to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration at zero baryon chemical potential and to the nuclear equation of state from Weber (1992) at zero temperature. The model uses density-dependent vector couplings and constant scalar couplings, with self-consistent equations for the meson fields solved simultaneously with the thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon minimum: -16.1 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.164 fm^{-3}
- Normal nuclear density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 550 MeV; Omega meson mass: m_omega = 782 MeV
- Scalar coupling: Gamma_sigma_N = 9.26; Vector coupling: Gamma_omega_N = 10.59
- Cubic sigma self-interaction: B = 5.1 fm^{-1}; Quartic sigma self-interaction: C = 9.8
- The HRG with all PDG hadrons below 2.6 GeV (HRG-1) describes lattice pressure up to T = 180 MeV within error bars
- The basic HRG-0 (without scalar mesons) describes lattice data only up to T = 150 MeV
- Critical energy density of about 0.5 GeV/fm^3 corresponds to a baryon density of 3 rho_0

## Context
This work extends the standard HRG approach, which successfully describes particle ratios in heavy-ion collisions (Cleymans 1990, Braun-Munzinger 2003, Andronic 2005) and lattice QCD thermodynamics at low chemical potential (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014), by incorporating interactions from relativistic mean-field theories (Walecka 1974, Serot 1984) that describe nuclear matter properties. The model bridges the gap between two well-known regimes: the temperature axis at zero chemical potential (accessible via lattice QCD) and the zero-temperature axis at finite chemical potential (described by nuclear physics). The approach enables extrapolations into unknown regions of the QCD phase diagram and can provide a hadronic-side definition of the phase boundary, which is relevant for interpreting results from upcoming facilities such as FAIR and NICA.
