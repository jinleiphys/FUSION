# Mixed-symmetry octupole and hexadecapole excitations in the N=52 isotones

> Background: Excitations with mixed proton-neutron symmetry have been previously observed in the $N=52$ isotones. Besides the well established quadrupole mixed-symmetry states (MSS), octupole and hexadecapole MSS have been recently proposed for the nuclei $^{92}$Zr and $^{94}$Mo. Purpose: The heaviest stable $N=52$ isotone $^{96}$Ru was investigated to study the evolution of octupole and hexadecapole MSS with increasing proton number. Methods: Two inelastic proton-scattering experiments on $^{96}$Ru were performed to extract branching ratios, multipole mixing ratios, and level lifetimes. From the combined data, absolute transition strengths were calculated. Results: Strong $M1$ transitions between the lowest-lying $3^-$ and $4^+$ states were observed, providing evidence for a one-phonon mixed-symmetry character of the $3^{(-)}_2$ and $4^+_2$ states. Conclusions: $sdg$-IBM-2 calculations were performed for $^{96}$Ru. The results are in excellent agreement with the experimental data, pointing out a one-phonon hexadecapole mixed-symmetry character of the $4^+_2$ state. The $\big< 3^-_1||M1||3^{(-)}_2\big>$ matrix element is found to scale with the $<2^+_{\mathrm{s}}||M1||2^+_{\mathrm{ms}}>$ matrix element.

## Key claim

The paper provides experimental evidence for one-phonon mixed-symmetry octupole and hexadecapole components in the wave functions of the \(3^{(-)}_2\) and \(4^+_2\) states of \(^{96}\)Ru, respectively, based on the observation of strong \(M1\) transitions; however, the octupole assignment relies on a tentative negative-parity assignment for the 3076 keV state, and the authors note that fragmentation of the one-phonon mixed-symmetry states cannot be excluded.

## Method

Two inelastic proton-scattering experiments on \(^{96}\)Ru were performed: one at Yale University using an 8.4 MeV proton beam with the YRAST Ball spectrometer to extract branching ratios and multipole mixing ratios via \(p\gamma\) coincidence and \(\gamma\gamma\) angular-correlation techniques, and one at the University of Cologne using a 7.0 MeV proton beam with the SONIC/HORUS setup to measure level lifetimes via the Doppler-shift attenuation method (DSAM). From the combined data, absolute transition strengths were calculated. \(sdg\)-IBM-2 calculations were performed with the code ArbModel using \(^{100}\)Sn as an inert core (\(N_\pi=3\), \(N_\nu=1\)) and a Hamiltonian and transition operators adopted from Casperson et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- \(B(M1; 3^{(-)}_2 \rightarrow 3^-_1) = 0.14(4)~\mu_N^2\) for the 3076 keV state in \(^{96}\)Ru, providing evidence for mixed-symmetry octupole character.
- \(B(E1; 3^{(-)}_2 \rightarrow 2^+_{\mathrm{ms}}) = 0.14(3)~\mathrm{mW.u.}\) and \(B(E1; 3^{(-)}_2 \rightarrow 2^+_{\mathrm{s}}) = 0.0017(3)~\mathrm{mW.u.}\) for the same state.
- Ratio \(\frac{\langle 3^-_1 ||M1|| 3^{(-)}_2 \rangle}{\langle 2^+_{\mathrm{s}} ||M1|| 2^+_{\mathrm{ms}} \rangle} = 0.53(9)\) for \(^{96}\)Ru.
- Lifetime \(\tau = 72(5)~\mathrm{fs}\) for the \(4^+_2\) state at 2462 keV, extracted via DSAM.
- \(B(M1; 4^+_2 \rightarrow 4^+_1) = 0.90(18)~\mu_N^2\) for \(^{96}\)Ru, supporting hexadecapole mixed-symmetry character.
- \(B(E2; 4^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 1.52(19)~\mathrm{W.u.}\) for the same state.
- \(sdg\)-IBM-2 calculation predicts \(B(M1; 4^+_2 \rightarrow 4^+_1) = 1.13~\mu_N^2\) and \(B(E2; 4^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 1.44~\mathrm{W.u.}\), in good agreement with experiment.
- The \(g\)-boson part of the \(M1\) operator contributes 83% of the \(\langle 4^+_1 ||M1|| 4^+_2 \rangle\) matrix element, while the \(d\)-boson part contributes 17%.
- Predicted \(E4\) strengths: \(B(E4; 4^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 1.09~\mathrm{W.u.}\) and \(B(E4; 4^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 0.55~\mathrm{W.u.}\) (with \(e_{\pi 1}\) set to \(1~\sqrt{\mathrm{W.u.}}\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of mixed-symmetry states (MSS) in the \(N=52\) isotones to the heaviest stable member \(^{96}\)Ru, following prior observations of quadrupole MSS in these nuclei (Pietralla et al., 1999-2008; Werner et al., 2002; Klein et al., 2002; Fransen et al., 2003, 2005) and recent proposals of octupole and hexadecapole MSS in \(^{92}\)Zr and \(^{94}\)Mo (Fransen et al., 2003, 2005; Smirnova et al., 2000; Scheit et al., 2010; Casperson et al., 2013). The \(sdg\)-IBM-2 calculations, following the approach of Casperson et al. (2013) for \(^{94}\)Mo, successfully reproduce the strong \(M1\) transition between the lowest-lying \(4^+\) states in \(^{96}\)Ru, suggesting that hexadecapole components in low-lying \(4^+\) states are a general phenomenon in near-spherical nuclei. The results enable future studies of \(E4\) strengths via \((e,e')\) experiments and motivate shell-model or quasiparticle phonon model calculations to investigate the role of microscopic \(g\)-factors in generating \(M1\) strengths between low-lying \(4^+\) states.
