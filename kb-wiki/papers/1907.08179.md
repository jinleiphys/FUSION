# Strangeness neutrality and the QCD phase diagram

> Since the incident nuclei in heavy-ion collisions do not carry strangeness, the global net strangeness of the detected hadrons has to vanish. We show that there is an intimate relation between strangeness neutrality and baryon-strangeness correlations. In the context of heavy-ion collisions, the former is a consequence of quark number conservation of the strong interactions while the latter are sensitive probes of the character of QCD matter. We investigate the sensitivity of baryon-strangeness correlations on the freeze-out conditions of heavy-ion collisions by studying their dependence on temperature, baryon- and strangeness chemical potential. The impact of strangeness neutrality on the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials will also be discussed. We model the low-energy sector of QCD by an effective Polyakov loop enhanced quark-meson model with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors and use the functional renormalization group to account for the non-perturbative quantum fluctuations of hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors show that strangeness neutrality (global net strangeness zero) in heavy-ion collisions is intimately related to baryon-strangeness correlations via a differential equation, and that these correlations carry signatures of the chiral phase transition at finite baryon chemical potential, though this sensitivity may arise from the distinct dynamics of hadronic versus QGP degrees of freedom rather than critical physics.

## Method

The authors model low-energy QCD using a 2+1 flavor Polyakov-loop enhanced quark-meson (PQM) effective theory that includes open strange mesons (kaons), strange baryons (via modified quark distribution functions), and quarks. They solve the non-perturbative functional renormalization group (FRG) flow equations to compute quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation, then extract the thermodynamic potential, strangeness chemical potential at strangeness neutrality, and baryon-strangeness correlations as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- At large temperatures (T >> Tc ≈ 155 MeV), the strangeness chemical potential at strangeness neutrality approaches μS0 = μB/3, the free quark limit
- For μB up to 675 MeV, the pseudocritical temperatures at strangeness neutrality differ by roughly 5% from those at μS = 0
- The relative difference in the chiral condensate between strangeness neutrality and μS = 0 reaches up to about 30%
- The relative difference in the Polyakov loop between strangeness neutrality and μS = 0 reaches up to about 20%
- At μB = 0, the baryon-strangeness correlation CBS computed from the model agrees with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2012)

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon-strangeness correlations introduced by Koch (2005) as a diagnostic for the nature of strongly interacting matter, and generalizes relations used by lattice QCD simulations (Bazavov 2012, Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2017) for determining freeze-out conditions from small μB/T to arbitrary μB and T. The results enable extraction of the strangeness chemical potential from measured baryon-strangeness correlations across beam energies, and demonstrate that strangeness neutrality significantly affects the equation of state and phase boundaries compared to calculations that neglect strangeness conservation.
