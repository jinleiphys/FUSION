# Microscopic self-consistent description of induced fission: dynamical pairing degree of freedom

> The role of dynamical pairing in induced fission dynamics is investigated using the time-dependent generator coordinate method in the Gaussian overlap approximation, based on the microscopic framework of nuclear energy density functionals. A calculation of fragment charge yields for induced fission of $^{228}$Th is performed in a three-dimensional space of collective coordinates that, in addition to the axial quadrupole and octupole intrinsic deformations of the nuclear density, also includes an isoscalar pairing degree of freedom. It is shown that the inclusion of dynamical pairing has a pronounced effect on the collective inertia, the collective flux through the scission hyper-surface, and the resulting fission yields, reducing the asymmetric peaks and enhancing the contribution of symmetric fission, in better agreement with the empirical trend.

## Key claim
The inclusion of dynamical pairing as an explicit collective coordinate in the TDGCM+GOA framework for induced fission of ²²⁸Th reduces the asymmetric fission peaks and enhances symmetric fission yields, bringing the calculated charge distributions into better agreement with empirical data, but the 3D calculation still does not fully reproduce the experimental yields, particularly the symmetric contribution, and the model fails to match the odd-even staggering of the experimental charge yields.

## Method
The authors performed a three-dimensional TDGCM+GOA calculation for induced fission of ²²⁸Th using the relativistic energy density functional DD-PC1 in the particle-hole channel and a separable finite-range pairing force in the BCS approximation. The collective space included axial quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation parameters plus an isoscalar pairing coordinate λ₂, with the collective potential and inertia tensor computed from self-consistent MDC-RMF calculations. The perturbative cranking approximation was used for the shape-related mass tensor elements, while the non-perturbative cranking formula was used for the pairing mass element, and the time evolution was propagated using the FELIX 2.0 code with an initial wave packet built 1 MeV above the fission barrier.

## Key numbers
- The collective space grid spanned β₂ from -1 to 7 with step 0.04, β₃ from 0 to 3.5 with step 0.05, and λ₂ from -0.1 to 2.0 with step 0.1.
- The time step was δt = 5×10⁻⁴ zs (1 zs = 10⁻²¹ s), and charge distributions were calculated after 4×10⁴ time steps (20 zs).
- The absorption rate for the imaginary potential was r = 20×10²² s⁻¹ with absorption band width w = 1.0.
- The Gaussian neck operator used a_N = 1 fm, with the pre-scission domain defined by ⟨Q_N⟩ > 3.
- The collective flux through the scission contour peaked at λ₂ ≈ 0.3, corresponding to an increase of approximately 20% relative to the static proton and neutron pairing gaps.
- Pairing gaps increased by a factor of approximately 2-3 over the range of λ₂ considered, with shell effects vanishing for λ₂ ≥ 0.6.
- The charge yields were convoluted with a Gaussian of width 1.6 units of particle number.

## Context
This work extends previous TDGCM+GOA studies of induced fission that used only two-dimensional shape spaces (quadrupole and octupole), such as the calculations for ²²⁶Th by Tao et al. (2017) which showed that increasing static pairing strength reduces asymmetric peaks. It also builds on the analysis by Zhao et al. (2020) comparing perturbative and non-perturbative collective masses in 2D calculations. The results enable future studies that combine dynamical pairing with finite-temperature effects, since the authors note that their zero-temperature calculation fails to quantitatively match the symmetric contribution, whereas their earlier work on ²²⁶Th (Zhao et al., 2019) showed that finite-temperature potentials and masses improve agreement with the empirical triple-humped structure.
