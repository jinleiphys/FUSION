# Effective action for relativistic hydrodynamics from Crooks fluctuation theorem

> A new effective theory framework for fluctuating hydrodynamics in the relativistic regime is derived using standard thermodynamical principles and general properties of non-equilibrium stochastic dynamics. For the first time, we establish clear and concise conditions for ensuring that the resulting effective theories are causal, stable, and well-posed within general relativity. These properties are independent of spacetime foliation and are valid in the full nonlinear regime. Out-of-equilibrium fluctuations are constrained by a relativistically covariant version of Crooks fluctuation theorem, which determines how the entropy production is distributed even when the system is driven by an external force. This leads to an emerging $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry responsible for imposing fluctuation-dissipation relations for n-point correlation functions, which matches the standard constraints for the Schwinger-Keldysh effective action.

## Key claim
The paper derives a new effective field theory framework for relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics that is causal, stable, and well-posed in the full nonlinear regime for any spacetime foliation, using a covariant version of Crooks fluctuation theorem rather than the standard Schwinger-Keldysh approach. The authors claim this is the first such framework to simultaneously satisfy all these properties, but the construction is limited to systems where the generating current and dissipative potential can be specified and the resulting equations of motion are symmetric hyperbolic.

## Method
The authors construct a path integral for relativistic hydrodynamics using a generating vector current X^μ(φ) and a dissipative potential Ξ(φ,φ̄), where φ are dynamical fields (including equilibrium variables α, β^μ and out-of-equilibrium variables φ^a) and φ̄ are auxiliary fields. Causality and stability are imposed by requiring that the characteristic matrix ∂²X^μ/∂Φ^a∂Φ^b is symmetric and that the vector K^μ defined from the free energy current Ω^μ is timelike future-directed. Crooks fluctuation theorem is enforced via a Z₂ transformation on the fields and auxiliary fields, which yields the standard KMS symmetry and fluctuation-dissipation relations for n-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- The entropy production rate per unit volume σ is given by σ = -Φ^a ∇_μ(∂X^μ/∂Φ^a), which includes both system entropy production ∇_μs^μ and external entropy production σ_ext = (∂X^μ/∂λ^h)∇_μλ^h
- The fluctuation-dissipation theorem in Fourier space takes the form G_S^{ab}(ω,k) = (iT/ω)(G_R^{ab} - G_R^{ab†}) = (2T/ω)Im(G_R^{ab})
- For the diffusion example with an Abelian gauge field, the steady-state solution gives Ohm's law j^μ = κ β_ν F^{μν} where κ is an even function of the dynamical variables

## Context
This work extends the divergence-type theory formalism of Geroch (1991) and the information current approach of Gavassino (2021) to include stochastic fluctuations via Crooks fluctuation theorem. It contrasts with the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory approach of Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015-2017), which cannot guarantee causality and local well-posedness in the nonlinear regime for stochastic systems. The framework enables numerical simulations of relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics using Monte Carlo techniques, particularly relevant for quark-gluon plasma in small collision systems and near the QCD critical point.
