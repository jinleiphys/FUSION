# Causal aspects of effective QCD models

> Here we studied the possible diffusion speed for relativistic fluid in different effective quantum chromodynamics models: Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL), Polyakov loop extended NJL, entangled PNJL (EPNJL) model.

# Causal aspects of effective QCD models

## Key claim
The diffusion speed of relativistic fluid in effective QCD models (NJL, PNJL, EPNJL) remains causal (below the speed of light) for all temperatures, with interacting massive quark matter yielding lower diffusion speeds than the massless non-interacting limit of \(v_T = 1/\sqrt{5}\), provided a finite shear relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) is introduced via the Maxwell-Cattaneo equation.

## Method
The authors computed the shear viscosity \(\eta\) from kinetic theory using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, with constituent quark masses \(M_Q(T)\) obtained from three effective QCD models: Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL), Polyakov loop extended NJL (PNJL), and entangled PNJL (EPNJL). Assuming the shear relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) equals the relaxation time \(\tau_Q\), they then evaluated the maximum causal diffusion speed \(v_T^{\text{max}} = \sqrt{\eta / [(\epsilon + P) \tau_\pi]}\) in the \(k \to \infty\) limit for each model.

## Key numbers
- For massless non-interacting quarks, the normalized shear viscosity \(\eta/(\tau_c T^4)\) is constant (black solid horizontal line in Fig. 2) and the causal diffusion speed is \(v_T = 1/\sqrt{5}\) (black solid horizontal line in Fig. 3).
- The constituent quark masses \(M_Q(T)\) for NJL, PNJL, and EPNJL models are shown as functions of temperature in Fig. 1, with the EPNJL model yielding the largest mass at low temperatures.
- The maximum causal diffusion speed \(v_T^{\text{max}}\) for all three interacting models remains below the massless limit of \(1/\sqrt{5} \approx 0.447\) at low temperatures, approaching this limit at high temperatures.

## Context
This work extends the causal formulation of relativistic fluid dynamics, originally developed by Maxwell and Cattaneo, to interacting quark matter described by effective QCD models. The authors build on prior studies of the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio), the PNJL model, and the EPNJL model, which provide quasi-particle descriptions of the thermodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma. The results demonstrate that these effective models preserve causality, and the investigation maps the temperature-dependent zone of causal diffusion speeds relative to the marginal massless limit, enabling future studies of causal transport in heavy-ion collision simulations.
