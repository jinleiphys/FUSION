# More on heavy tetraquarks in lattice QCD at almost physical pion mass

> We report on our progress in studying exotic, heavy tetraquark states, $qq\prime \bar Q\bar Q\prime$. Using publicly available dynamical $n_f =2+1$ Wilson-Clover gauge configurations, generated by the PACS-CS collaboration, with pion masses $\simeq$164, 299 and 415 MeV, we extend our previous analysis to heavy quark components containing heavier than physical bottom quarks $\bar Q\bar Q\prime=\bar b\prime\bar b\prime$ or $\bar Q\bar Q\prime=\bar b\bar b\prime$, charm and bottom quarks $\bar c\bar b$ and also only charm quarks $\bar c\bar c$. Throughout we employ NRQCD and relativistic heavy quarks for the heavier than bottom, bottom and charm quarks. Using our previously established diquark-antidiquark and meson-meson operator basis we comment in particular on the dependence of the binding energy on the mass of the heavy quark component $\bar Q\bar Q$, with heavy quarks ranging from $m_Q=0.85\ldots 6.3\cdot m_b$. In the heavy flavor non-degenerate case, $\bar Q\bar Q\prime$, and especially for the tetraquark channel $ud\bar c\bar b$, we extend our work to utilize a $3\times 3$ GEVP to study the ground and threshold states thereby enabling a clear identification of possible binding. Finally, we present initial work on the $\bar Q\bar Q\prime=\bar c\bar c$ system where a much larger operator basis is available in comparison to flavor combinations with NRQCD quarks.

## Key claim

The binding energy of heavy tetraquarks of the type \(qq'\bar Q\bar Q'\) increases as \(\sim 1/m_{\tilde Q}\) for heavy quark masses between \(0.846\,m_b\) and infinity, confirming the heavy-quark-symmetry-based binding mechanism; however, the results for the \(ud\bar c\bar b\) channel at \(m_\pi=299\) MeV are not conclusive and permit an interpretation as either bound at the \(\mathcal{O}(25~\text{MeV})\) level or unbound, while at almost physical pion mass there is evidence of binding at the \(\mathcal{O}(50~\text{MeV})\) level.

## Method

The authors used dynamical \(n_f=2+1\) Wilson-Clover gauge configurations from the PACS-CS collaboration at pion masses of 164, 299, and 415 MeV. They computed heavy-quark propagators using NRQCD for bottom and heavier-than-bottom quarks and a relativistic (Tsukuba-type) heavy quark action for charm quarks, and extracted binding energies from a \(2\times 2\) or \(3\times 3\) GEVP (generalized eigenvalue problem) in a diquark-antidiquark and meson-meson operator basis, fitting the lowest eigenvalue to a single exponential.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of \(ud\bar b\bar b\) tetraquark after chiral extrapolation: \(\Delta E = 189(10)(3)\) MeV.
* Binding energy of \(ls\bar b\bar b\) tetraquark after chiral extrapolation: \(\Delta E = 98(7)(3)\) MeV.
* Heavy quark masses studied relative to physical bottom mass: \(m_{b'}/m_b \approx 0.846, 1.463, 1.928, 4.395, 6.287\).
* Pion masses on the three ensembles: 415 MeV, 299 MeV, and 164 MeV.
* Lattice spacing: \(a^{-1} = 2.194(10)\) GeV.
* For \(ud\bar c\bar b\) at \(m_\pi=164\) MeV, evidence of binding at the \(\mathcal{O}(50~\text{MeV})\) level relative to the non-interacting two-meson threshold.
* For \(ud\bar c\bar b\) at \(m_\pi=299\) MeV, binding either at the \(\mathcal{O}(25~\text{MeV})\) level or unbound.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD study of \(ud\bar b\bar b\) and \(ls\bar b\bar b\) tetraquarks (Francis et al., 2016) by mapping the heavy-quark mass dependence from \(0.846\,m_b\) to infinity and by adding channels with charm quarks. It tests the phenomenological binding mechanism based on heavy quark symmetry and good diquarks (Jaffe, 2004), and the results for \(ud\bar c\bar c\) are compared to a contemporaneous study by Cheung et al. (2017) which found no binding at \(m_\pi=391\) MeV. The work enables future studies of the light-quark mass dependence and finite-volume effects needed to determine whether the \(ud\bar c\bar b\) and \(ud\bar c\bar c\) systems are genuinely bound.
