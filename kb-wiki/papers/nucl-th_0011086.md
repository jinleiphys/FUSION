# Electromagnetic K+ production on the deuteron with hyperon recoil polarization

> Photo- and electroproduction processes of K+ on the deuteron are investigated theoretically. Modern hyperon-nucleon forces as well as an updated kaon production operator on the nucleon are used. Sizable effects of the hyperon-nucleon final state interaction are seen in various observables. Especially the photoproduction double polarization observable C_z is shown to provide a handle to distinguish different hyperon-nucleon force models.

## Key claim
The double polarization observable \(C_z\) in \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda)\) and \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Sigma^-)\) photoproduction is shown to be sensitive enough to distinguish between different hyperon-nucleon force models (NSC97f vs. NSC89), but this conclusion is based on preliminary calculations at specific kinematic points (kaon lab momentum 870 MeV/c for \(\Lambda\) and 810 MeV/c for \(\Sigma^-\)) and has not yet been confirmed by experiment.

## Method
The authors performed rigorous calculations of \(K^+\) photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron using a coupled \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) final-state interaction framework. They employed modern hyperon-nucleon potentials from the Nijmegen group (NSC97f and NSC89) and an updated kaon production operator on the nucleon. Cross sections and the double polarization observable \(C_z\) were computed for both inclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+)\) and exclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda)\) and \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Sigma^-)\) reactions, as well as for the electroproduction process \(d(e, e' K^+)\), comparing full final-state interaction results with plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results.

## Key numbers
- Incident photon energy \(E_\gamma = 1.3\) GeV and outgoing kaon angle \(\theta_K = 1^\circ\) for photoproduction calculations.
- Inclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+)\) cross section shows two pronounced quasifree peaks at kaon lab momenta \(P_K \approx 945\) MeV/c and \(P_K \approx 809\) MeV/c.
- Exclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda)n\) cross section and \(C_z\) computed at \(P_K = 870\) MeV/c; exclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Sigma^-)p\) computed at \(P_K = 810\) MeV/c.
- For electroproduction, two kinematic sets are shown: one reproducing Hall C conditions (incident electron energy \(E_e\) and momentum transfer \(Q^2\) unspecified in text) and another with \(Q^2 = 0.20\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 17^\circ\).
- In PWIA, \(C_z\) values are nearly 100%; full FSI results show dramatic deviations that distinguish NSC97f from NSC89.

## Context
This work extends earlier rigorous calculations of light hypernuclei (Miyagawa et al., prior work) and the inclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+)\) and exclusive \(d(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda (\Sigma))\) processes (Miyagawa et al., 1999). It incorporates the Nijmegen NSC97f and NSC89 hyperon-nucleon forces, which were previously found to give reasonable binding energies for the hypertriton. The results enable future experimental tests at TJNAF (Jefferson Lab) for both photoproduction and electroproduction on the deuteron, and suggest that the double polarization observable \(C_z\) can discriminate between hyperon-nucleon interaction models. The electroproduction results also show a prominent enhancement near the \(\Sigma\) threshold attributed to a \(YN\) \(t\)-matrix pole, motivating further systematic studies across a wider kinematic range and extension to the \(A=3\) system.
