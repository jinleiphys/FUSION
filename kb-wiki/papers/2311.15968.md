# Effects of hyperon potentials and symmetry energy in quark deconfinement

> In this letter we discuss how the results of recent nuclear experiments that correspond to measurements at low densities can affect the equation of state at large densities and temperatures, changing the particle composition and ultimately influencing deconfinement to quark matter. In particular, saturation values of the hyperon potentials affect the hyperon content, while the symmetry energy at saturation directly regulates how the stiffness of the equation of state changes with isospin. We make use of a chiral model that describes nucleons, hyperons, and quarks to show how astrophysical conditions, such as the ones in neutron stars, present the ideal ground to study the effects of these two quantities in dense matter. In this case, for small charge fraction/ large isospin asymmetry, the couplings that reproduce different symmetry energy slopes can significantly modify deconfinement, with quantitative changes in the critical chemical potential depending on the deconfining potential. On the other hand, different values of the parameter that controls the hyperon potentials (kept within a range close to experimental data) do not affect deconfinement significantly.

## Key claim

The paper shows that varying hyperon potentials within or near the experimental range (changes of less than 10 MeV each) does not significantly affect quark deconfinement at any temperature, but higher-order vector interactions that modify the symmetry energy slope \(L\) by 7 MeV do produce significant changes, especially for low temperature, non-constrained strangeness, and low charge fraction.

## Method

The authors employ the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a relativistic SU(3) effective chiral model that consistently describes nucleons, hyperons, and quarks with a deconfinement order parameter. They vary the explicit symmetry-breaking coupling (ExpSB) to change hyperon potentials and add mixed vector-isovector \(\omega\rho\) and higher-order \(\omega^4\) interactions to modify the symmetry energy slope, then compute QCD phase diagrams for different temperatures, charge fractions, and strangeness constraints.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy slope \(L\) changes from 88 to 75 MeV when the \(\omega\rho\) interaction is added, a difference of 13 MeV.
- For the original deconfining potential \(U_\Phi\), changes in higher-order couplings push the deconfinement coexistence free energy by up to 15 MeV for \(Y_Q=0\) at \(T=0\), corresponding to a shift in density of approximately 0.5 \(n_{\rm{sat}}\).
- For the alternative deconfining potential \(U'_\Phi\), higher-order vector interactions can increase the deconfinement coexistence free energy by up to 185 MeV for \(Y_Q=0\) at \(T=0\), corresponding to a shift in density of approximately 2 \(n_{\rm{sat}}\).
- The \(\Lambda\) hyperon potential is set to \(U_\Lambda = -28\) MeV for the central ExpSB value of 0.86, with \(U_\Sigma = 5\) MeV and \(U_\Xi = -18\) MeV.
- The first-order phase transition for \(U'_\Phi\) exists only for charge fractions \(Y_Q = 0 - 0.14\) (standard couplings, \(Y_S \neq 0\)) down to \(Y_Q = 0 - 0.04\) (with \(\omega\rho\) and \(\omega^4\) couplings).

## Context

This work extends prior analyses of hyperon potentials and symmetry energy effects (e.g., Fortin (2017), Gal (2016), ALICE (2020)) by considering their combined influence on quark deconfinement at finite temperature and out of weak equilibrium. The results are particularly relevant for neutron star conditions, and the authors note that Lopes (2023) independently found that lower \(L\) values are associated with higher critical baryon chemical potential for the phase transition, in agreement with their findings. The study enables better understanding of how recent experimental controversies (e.g., PREX-II results for \(L\)) affect predictions for hybrid stars and the QCD phase diagram.
