# Low-energy pions in nuclear matter and pi pi photoproduction within a BUU transport model

> In the present paper we investigate a method to describe low-energy scattering events of pions and nuclei within a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model. Implementing different scenarios of medium modifications, we studied the mean free path of pions in nuclear matter at low momenta and compared pion absorption simulations to data. Pursuing these studies we have shown, that also in a regime of a long pionic wave length the semi-classical BUU model still generates reasonable results. We present results on pi-induced events in the regime of 10 MeV < Tkin < 130 MeV and photo-induced pi pi production at incident beam energies of 400-460 MeV.

# Low-energy pions in nuclear matter and pi pi photoproduction within a BUU transport model

## Key claim
The semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model produces reasonable results for low-energy pions (down to kinetic energies of about 20 MeV) despite the long pionic wavelength, but the observed downward shift of the two-pion invariant mass spectrum in photoproduction experiments can be explained by conventional final-state interaction effects without invoking in-medium modification of the sigma meson.

## Method
The authors implemented a BUU transport model with coupled-channel dynamics, including resonance propagation (Delta, N*), pion-nucleon elastic scattering, pion absorption via N Delta -> NN and NN Delta -> NNN processes, and a hadronic potential for the pion based on the model by Nieves et al. below 80 MeV pion momentum and a first-order Delta-hole result above 140 MeV, matched in the intermediate region. They compared simulations of pion-induced reactions (absorption and scattering) on various nuclei against experimental data, and applied the same framework to analyze two-pion photoproduction off 12C and 208Pb at incident photon energies of 400-460 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pion kinetic energy range studied: 10 MeV to 130 MeV for pion-induced events
- Incident photon beam energies for pi pi photoproduction: 400-460 MeV
- Delta potential depth: approximately -30 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0)
- Nucleon potential strength: approximately 50 MeV at rho_0
- The hadronic potential for the pion was implemented below 80 MeV pion momentum (Nieves model) and above 140 MeV (Delta-hole model), matched in the 80-140 MeV region
- Mean free path of pions in the BUU simulation without hadronic potential agrees well with quantum mechanical results by Mehrem, Radi and Rasmussen at very low energies
- The BUU model reproduces pion absorption data down to kinetic energies of approximately 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier transport model studies by Salcedo et al. (with only pions as degrees of freedom) and Engel et al. (with a precursor version of the present model) who investigated pions with kinetic energies of 85-300 MeV. The authors compare their mean free path results to quantum mechanical optical model calculations by Mehrem, Radi and Rasmussen, and their pion absorption results to data from Byfield, Ashery and Nakai as well as optical model results by Oset. For the two-pion photoproduction analysis, they compare to a model by Roca et al. that uses a purely absorptive final-state prescription without elastic scattering or charge exchange. The work enables a critical reassessment of claims that the sigma meson is modified in nuclear matter, showing that conventional final-state effects must be accounted for before invoking partial chiral symmetry restoration.
