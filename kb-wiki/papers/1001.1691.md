# Anomalous Soft Photons in Hadron Production

> Anomalous soft photons in excess of what is expected from electromagnetic bremsstrahlung have been observed in association with the production of hadrons, mostly mesons, in high-energy (K+)p, (pi+)p, (pi-)p, pp, and (e+)(e-) collisions. We propose a model for the simultaneous production of anomalous soft photons and mesons in quantum field theory, in which the meson production arises from the oscillation of color charge densities of the quarks of the underlying vacuum in the flux tube. As a quark carries both a color charge and an electric charge, the oscillation of the color charge densities will be accompanied by the oscillation of electric charge densities, which will in turn lead to the simultaneous production of soft photons during the meson production process. How the production of these soft photons may explain the anomalous soft photon data will be discussed. Further experimental measurements to test the model will be proposed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that anomalous soft photons observed in excess of electromagnetic bremsstrahlung in hadron-producing collisions arise from electric charge density oscillations that accompany the color charge density oscillations responsible for meson production in the flux tube, but the model is limited to quarks with two flavors and relies on an approximate compactification from four-dimensional to two-dimensional space-time.

## Method

The authors model the flux tube environment in high-energy particle production by compactifying the QCD4×QED4 system into a QCD2×QED2 system under conditions of longitudinal dominance and transverse confinement, with coupling constants related by the flux tube radius. They apply non-Abelian bosonization for the color SU(3) subgroup and Abelian bosonization for the flavor degrees of freedom to find stable boson states, restricting the bosonization to only the amplitude degree of freedom while keeping the orientation fixed. The boson masses are obtained by expanding the Hamiltonian density about the potential minimum and evaluating second derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The two-dimensional coupling constant is related to the four-dimensional coupling constant by \(g_{\rm 2D}^2 \sim g_{\rm 4D}^2/(\pi R_T^2)\), where \(R_T\) is the flux tube transverse radius.
- In the massless quark limit, the QCD2 isoscalar meson mass is \(M_{0({\rm QCD2})}^1 = g_{_{\rm QCD2}}\sqrt{2/\pi}\), which matches the massive boson mass from Arm99 for \(N_f=2\).
- The boson mass squared for each isospin state is \((M_{I({\rm 2D})}^\alpha)^2 = \left( (g_u^\alpha+(-1)^I g_d^\alpha)/\sqrt{2\pi} \right)^2 + \frac{2}{3-\alpha} e^\gamma m_T \mu\), where \(\gamma=0.5772\) is the Euler constant.
- The quark electric charges are \(Q_u=2/3\) and \(Q_d=-1/3\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum field theory description of meson production via string fragmentation from Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) to include simultaneous photon production, addressing the fourth feature of DELPHI observations (2006, 2009) that existing models could not explain. The model builds on prior bosonization results for QED2 and QCD2 from Coleman (1976) and others, and generalizes the large-\(N_f\) QCD2 meson solutions of Arm99, Tri02, and Abr04 down to \(N_f=2\). The proposed QED2 photon model enables predictions for future experimental tests in \(Z^0\) hadronic decay and other high-energy collisions.
