# Nuclear structure and decay properties of even-even nuclei in $Z=70-80$ drip-line region

> We study nuclear structure properties for various isotopes of Ytterbium (Yb), Hafnium(Hf), Tungsten(W), Osmium(Os), Platinum(Pt) and Mercury(Hg) in $Z = 70 - 80$ drip-line region starting from $N =80$ to $N=170$ within the formalism of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The pairing correlation is taken care by using BCS approach. We compared our results with Finite Range Droplet Model(FRDM) and experimental data and found that the calculated results are in good agreement. Neutron shell closure are obtained at $N=82$ and $126$ in this region. We have also studied probable decay mechanisms of these elements.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with BCS pairing, using the NL3 parameter set, reproduces bulk properties (binding energy, radii, deformation) of even-even nuclei in the Z=70-80 region from N=80 to N=170 in good agreement with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and experimental data, but this agreement is explicitly demonstrated only for a subset of isotopes (e.g., Hg binding energy per nucleon in Fig. 1) and the formalism is extended to the neutron drip line where experimental data are largely absent.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations with the NL3 force parameter set in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, using BCS pairing with constant gap formulae for protons and neutrons. They calculated ground-state binding energies, quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters, root-mean-square radii, two-neutron separation energies, and single-particle energy levels for isotopic chains of Yb, Hf, W, Os, Pt, and Hg. Decay Q-values and half-lives for alpha decay (Viola-Seaborg formula) and beta decay (Fiset-Nix empirical formula) were computed from the RMF binding energies.

## Key numbers
* Two-neutron drip line for Yb (Z=70) occurs at neutron number N=166; for Hf, W, Os (Z=72,74,76) at approximately N=170; for Hg (Z=80) at N=168.
* Neutron shell closures are identified at N=82 and N=126 from sharp drops in the differential two-neutron separation energy dS2n for all six isotopic chains.
* The basis space was converged at N_F = N_B = 20 oscillator quanta, chosen after test calculations on 220Yb and 230Hg showed physical observables become almost constant beyond N_F = N_B = 14.
* The constant pairing gap parameters used are R=5.72, s=0.118, t=8.12, with B_s=1 and I = (N-Z)/(N+Z).
* The center-of-mass correction is estimated as E_c.m. = (3/4)(41 A^{-1/3}) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier RMF survey of 1315 even-even nuclei by Lalazissis et al. (1999), which focused mostly near stability, to the neutron drip line for Z=70-80. It compares results with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1995, 1997) and experimental data from Audi et al. (2012). The study enables predictions of ground-state shapes, shell closures, and dominant decay modes (beta decay over alpha decay) for neutron-rich isotopes in this region, which are relevant for planning experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities.
