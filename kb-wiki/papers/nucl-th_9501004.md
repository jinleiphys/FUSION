# Short range correlations in the pion s-wave self-energy of pionic atoms

> We evaluate the contribution of second order terms to the pion-nucleus s-wave optical potential of pionic atoms generated by short range nuclear correlation. The corrections are sizeable because they involve the isoscalar s-wave $πN$ amplitude for half off-shell situations where the amplitude is considerably larger than the on-shell one. In addition, the s-wave optical potential is reanalyzed by looking at all the different conventional contributions together lowest order, Pauli corrected rescattering term, second order absorptive effects, terms from the interaction of pions with the virtual pion cloud (chiral corrections) and correlation effects. Different off-shell extrapolations for the $πN$ amplitude are used and it is found that, although some individual terms are sensitive to the extrapolation, the sum of them is rather insensitive. The results are compared with empirical values from best fits to the data and are found to be compatible, within theoretical and empirical uncertainties. The results do not rule out further contributions but they put stringent constraints on their strength.

# Short range correlations in the pion s-wave self-energy of pionic atoms

## Key claim

Short range nuclear correlations produce a sizeable repulsive correction to the s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential, but when this correction is added to the dispersive (absorptive) correction, the sum is small (about 15% of the leading term) and rather insensitive to the off-shell extrapolation of the πN amplitude, contrary to the individual terms. The total theoretical result is compatible with empirical values from pionic atom fits within uncertainties, but does not rule out further contributions while putting stringent constraints on their strength.

## Method

The authors evaluate the second-order contribution to the s-wave pion self-energy in nuclear matter arising from short range correlations, using a correlation function approach where the pion exchange in rescattering terms is modified by simultaneous exchange of other mesons. They combine this with three different off-shell extrapolations of the isoscalar s-wave πN amplitude (Hamilton, LMM, and Jülich models) and three different nuclear correlation functions, and add the result to previously calculated dispersive (absorptive) and chiral (virtual pion cloud) contributions to obtain the total theoretical s-wave parameter b₀.

## Key numbers

- Lowest order b₀ = -0.0130 ± 0.0033 m_π⁻¹ (from experimental πN scattering lengths a₁ = 0.171 ± 0.004 m_π⁻¹ and a₃ = -0.105 ± 0.003 m_π⁻¹)
- Pauli corrected second order rescattering Δb₀ = -0.0140 ± 0.0008 m_π⁻¹
- Dispersion plus correlation correction δb₀,ₐ₊𝒸 = +0.0039 ± 0.0020 m_π⁻¹ (repulsive)
- Chiral correction δb₀,𝒸ₕ = -0.0022 ± 0.0002 m_π⁻¹ (attractive)
- Total theoretical b̄₀ᵗʰ = -0.0253 ± 0.0063 m_π⁻¹
- Empirical b₀ from Seki and Masutani (1993): -0.0285 ± 0.0007 m_π⁻¹
- Empirical b₀ from Meirav et al. (1991): -0.0350 ± 0.0062 m_π⁻¹
- Empirical b₀ from Nieves et al. (1993): -0.0323 ± 0.0007 m_π⁻¹
- Weighted average empirical b₀ᵉᵐᵖ = -0.0304 ± 0.0025 m_π⁻¹ (including systematic error from potential shape assumptions)

## Context

This work extends the original Ericson-Ericson (1966) framework for pionic atoms by adding short range correlation effects to the s-wave self-energy, analogous to the Lorentz-Lorenz effect already known for the p-wave self-energy. It combines and reanalyzes contributions from dispersive corrections (Oset et al., 1982; Nieves et al., 1993), chiral corrections from virtual pion clouds (Weinberg, 1990; Oset et al., 1994), and the new correlation terms, using three off-shell extrapolations (Hamilton, 1973; LMM, 1974; Jülich group, 1994). The results constrain the "missing repulsion" problem in pionic atoms and enable more precise comparisons between theory and empirical fits from Seki and Masutani (1993), Meirav et al. (1991), and Nieves et al. (1993).
