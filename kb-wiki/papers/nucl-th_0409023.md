# Renormalization of $NN$ scattering: contact potential

> The renormalization of the $T$-matrix for $NN$ scattering with a contact potential is reexamined in a nonperturbative regime through rigorous nonperturbative solutions. Based on the underlying theory point of view, it is shown that the UV divergences in the nonperturbative solutions of the $T$-matrix should be subtracted through 'endogenous' counter terms, which in turn leads to a nontrivial prescription dependence. Moreover, employing the effective range expansion, the important procedure for imposing physical boundary conditions to remove the nontrivial prescription dependence is discussed and highlighted, especially before making physical claims. As byproducts, some relations between the effective range expansion parameters are derived. We also discussed the power counting of the couplings for the nucleon-nucleon interactions and other subtle points present within EFT framework beyond perturbative treatment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in nonperturbative solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering with a contact potential, ultraviolet divergences must be subtracted through "endogenous" counter terms (applied before the nonperturbative reorganization) rather than through the usual "exogenous" counter terms added to the Lagrangian, and this procedure leads to a nontrivial prescription dependence that can only be removed by imposing physical boundary conditions such as effective range expansion parameters.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly for contact potentials in the \(^1S_0\) channel at three chiral orders (\(\Delta=0,2,4\)) using a factorized algebraic method following Phillips (1997). They regularized the divergent integrals \([J_n]\) that appear in the closed-form on-shell \(T\)-matrix solutions, then performed effective range expansions to express the scattering length, effective range, and higher shape parameters in terms of both the coupling constants \([C_n]\) and the subtracted finite integrals \([\bar{J}_n]\). The prescription dependence was analyzed by parametrizing the subtracted integrals with a scale \(\widetilde{\mu}\) and dimensionless numbers \([q_n]\).

## Key numbers

- At leading order (\(\Delta=0\)): scattering length \(a^{-1} = \frac{4\pi}{M}(C_0^{-1} + \bar{J}_0)\), effective range \(r_e = 0\), all higher shape parameters \(v_k = 0\) for \(k \geq 2\)
- At next-to-leading order (\(\Delta=2\)): effective range \(r_e = \frac{8\pi}{M} \frac{(2C_2 - C_2^2\bar{J}_3)(1 - C_2\bar{J}_3)^2}{(C_0 + C_2^2\bar{J}_5)^2}\)
- At next-to-leading order: higher shape parameters \(v_k = \frac{4\pi}{M}(-)^{k+1} \frac{(1 - C_2\bar{J}_3)^2 C_2^k (2 - C_2\bar{J}_3)^k}{(C_0 + C_2^2\bar{J}_5)^{k+1}}\) for \(k \geq 2\)
- The subtracted integrals are parametrized as \(J_0 \equiv q_0 M \widetilde{\mu}\), \(J_3 \equiv q_3 M \widetilde{\mu}^3\), \(J_5 \equiv q_5 M \widetilde{\mu}^5\), with \(\widetilde{\mu} \in (f_\pi, \Lambda)\) where \(\Lambda \sim 500\) MeV
- Naturalness bound: \(|\bar{J}_n| \leq M^{n+1}\) for \(n \neq 0\), \(|\bar{J}_0| \leq M^2\)
- At next-to-next-to-leading order (\(\Delta=4\)): five prescription-dependent parameters (\(\bar{J}_0, \bar{J}_3, \bar{J}_5, \bar{J}_7, \bar{J}_9\)) appear in eight compact expressions, requiring at least five boundary conditions (\(a, r_e, v_2, v_3, v_4\)) to fix the prescription

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of nonperturbative renormalization in effective field theory (Yang, 2003) and addresses controversies in the application of chiral perturbation theory to nucleon-nucleon scattering following Weinberg's approach. The paper directly confronts the formal consistency issues raised by Beane, Kaplan, and collaborators regarding power counting in nonperturbative EFT, arguing that the difficulty dissolves once one recognizes that endogenous counter terms (derived from the underlying theory perspective) are required rather than exogenous ones. The analysis enables a systematic procedure for fixing renormalization prescriptions through physical boundary conditions from effective range expansion parameters, and provides relations among higher-order shape parameters (e.g., \(v_k = v_3(-2v_2/r_e)^{k-3}\) for \(k \geq 4\) at next-to-leading order) that hold independently of the prescription.
