# Study of nonlocality effects in direct capture reactions with Lagrange-mesh $R$-matrix method

> We apply the Lagrange-mesh $R$-matrix method to calculate the $S$-factor for the $^{13}$C$(p,γ)^{14}$N and $^{16}$O$(p,γ)^{17}$F direct radiative capture reactions. By comparing the astrophysical $S$-factors calculated with nonlocal and local potentials, we investigate the nonlocality effects coming from the nuclear potentials in the direct capture reactions. Our calculations are in good agreement with the experimental data and indicate a nonnegligible difference in the results of local and nonlocal potentials. The use of small diffuseness narrow potentials also provides a remarkably good fit in the case with multiple broad resonances. Our findings suggest that the nonlocal potential improves the calculated results although the difference between the local and nonlocal potentials is smaller than uncertainties from other sources. We propose the nonlocality potential should be used in the potential model calculation of future astrophysics rates evaluation.

## Key claim
The authors find that using nonlocal potentials in the potential model for direct radiative capture reactions improves agreement with experimental data, but the difference between local and nonlocal potential results is smaller than the typical 10% theoretical uncertainty from other sources, contrary to earlier studies that reported larger nonlocality effects of 20%-25%.

## Method
The authors applied the Lagrange-mesh R-matrix method to solve the Schrodinger equation with both local Woods-Saxon potentials and Perey-Buck-type nonlocal potentials for bound and scattering states. They calculated the astrophysical S-factor for the 13C(p,gamma)14N and 16O(p,gamma)17F reactions by computing the radial overlap integral of scattering and bound wave functions, then fitting spectroscopic factors independently for local and nonlocal cases to match experimental data. For the 13C(p,gamma)14N reaction, they used narrow diffuseness potentials to simultaneously describe two broad resonances, a feat not previously achieved with a pure potential model.

## Key numbers
- For 16O(p,gamma)17F: the nonlocal potential gave S(0) values of 0.39 keV b (ground state, d5/2) and 9.32 keV b (excited state, s1/2), compared to local values of 0.36 keV b and 9.12 keV b; the difference between local and nonlocal total S(0) was 2% (9.48 vs 9.71 keV b).
- For 16O(p,gamma)17F: the single-particle ANC for the ground state was 0.96 fm^{-1/2} (local) and 1.11 fm^{-1/2} (nonlocal); for the excited state it was 81.51 fm^{-1/2} (local) and 88.09 fm^{-1/2} (nonlocal); the spectroscopic factors were 1.07 (local) and 0.85 (nonlocal) for the ground state, and 0.91 (local) and 0.80 (nonlocal) for the excited state.
- For 13C(p,gamma)14N: the two resonances are at E_R=0.518 MeV (width Gamma=0.037 MeV, 1- state) and E_R=1.225 MeV (width Gamma=0.408 MeV, 0- state).
- For 13C(p,gamma)14N: the bound state proton separation energy was -7.550 MeV; the local potential strength was 52.26 MeV and the nonlocal was 61.89 MeV for the ground state, with radius R_c=2.939 fm and diffuseness a_c=0.65 fm.

## Context
This work extends the study of nonlocality effects in direct capture reactions by Tian, Pang, and Ma (2018), who reported 20%-25% nonlocality effects for reactions like 7Li(n,gamma)8Li and 12C(p,gamma)13N, but used the same spectroscopic factor for both local and nonlocal potentials. The authors here show that allowing spectroscopic factors to be fitted independently reduces the apparent nonlocality effect, and they achieve the first simultaneous description of two broad resonances in 13C(p,gamma)14N within a pure potential model. This enables more reliable evaluation of astrophysical reaction rates for the CNO cycle and AGB star nucleosynthesis, particularly for the 13C(p,gamma)14N reaction that depletes the seed for the neutron-producing 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction.
