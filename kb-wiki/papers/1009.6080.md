# Mass independence and asymmetry of the reaction: Multi-fragmentation as an example

> We present our recent results on the fragmentation by varying the mass asymmetry of the reaction between 0.2 and 0.7 at an incident energy of 250 MeV/nucleon. For the present study, the total mass of the system is kept constant (ATOT = 152) and mass asymmetry of the reaction is defined by the asymmetry parameter (? = | (AT - AP)/(AT + AP) |). The measured distributions are shown as a function of the total charge of all projectile fragments, Zbound. We see an interesting outcome for rise and fall in the production of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) for large asymmetric colliding nuclei. This trend, however, is completely missing for large asymmetric nuclei. Therefore, experiments are needed to verify this prediction.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the well-known rise-and-fall behavior of intermediate mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity as a function of bound charge (Zbound) occurs only for nearly symmetric colliding nuclei, and is completely absent for large asymmetric nuclei; the authors state that experiments are needed to verify this prediction.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions at a fixed center-of-mass energy of 250 MeV/nucleon, with the total mass of the system held constant at ATOT = 152. They varied the mass asymmetry parameter η from 0.2 to 0.7 by selecting different projectile-target combinations, and applied the minimum spanning tree (MST) clusterization algorithm to identify fragments from the phase-space output. To isolate asymmetry effects from Coulomb effects, the Coulomb force was switched off in the analysis.

## Key numbers

- Total mass of the system fixed at ATOT = 152 for all reactions.
- Incident energy: 250 MeV/nucleon (center-of-mass energy).
- Asymmetry parameter η varied over six values: 0.2 (56Fe + 96Ru), 0.3 (50Cr + 102Ru), 0.4 (40Ca + 112Sn), 0.5 (32S + 120Sn), 0.6 (28Si + 124Xe), 0.7 (16O + 136Xe).
- Slope values (m) from linear fits of <NIMF> versus η: -1.7 at Zbound = 16, -2.7 at Zbound = 20, -2.6 at Zbound = 27.
- Maximum IMF production observed in the Zbound range from 15 to 20.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of multifragmentation by the ALADiN group (Hubele et al., Jain et al., Schuttauf et al.) and by Tsang et al. (2009), which had not systematically accounted for the mass asymmetry of the reaction. The authors use the IQMD model (Hartnack et al.) to show that the universal rise-and-fall behavior of IMF multiplicity with Zbound, previously reported for symmetric systems, disappears for large asymmetries. The results call for experimental verification using asymmetric projectile-target combinations at 250 MeV/nucleon.
