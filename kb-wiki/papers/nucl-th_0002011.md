# The $F/D$ ratio and meson-baryon couplings from QCD sum rules-II

> Using QCD sum rules, we compute the diagonal meson-baryon couplings, $πNN$, $ηNN$, $πΞΞ$, $ηΞΞ$, $πΣΣ$ and $ηΣΣ$, from the baryon-baryon correlation function with a meson, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} < 0| T J_B(x) {\bar J}_B(0)|M(p)>$. The calculations are performed to leading order in $p_μ$ by considering the two separate Dirac structures, $i γ_5 γ_μp^μ$ and $γ_5 σ_{μν} {q^μp^ν}$ separately. We first improve the previous sum rule calculations on these Dirac structures for the $πNN$ coupling by including three-particle pion wave functions of twist 4 and then extend the formalism to calculate the other couplings, $ηNN$, $πΞΞ$, $ηΞΞ$, $πΣΣ$ and $ηΣΣ$. In the SU(3) symmetric limit, we identify the terms responsible for the $F/D$ ratio in the OPE by matching the obtained couplings with their SU(3) relations. Depending on the Dirac structure considered, we find different identifications for the $F/D$ ratio. The couplings including the SU(3) breaking effects are also discussed within our approach.

## Key claim

The paper computes diagonal meson-baryon couplings using QCD sum rules and finds that the identification of the $F/D$ ratio depends on which Dirac structure of the correlation function is considered, yielding different expressions for $\alpha = F/(F+D)$ depending on whether the $i\gamma_5 \hat{p}$ or $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ structure is used, even in the SU(3) symmetric limit.

## Method

The authors compute the baryon-baryon correlation function with an external meson, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} \langle 0| T J_B(x) \bar{J}_B(0)|M(p)\rangle$, to leading order in the meson momentum $p_\mu$. They consider two separate Dirac structures, $i\gamma_5 \hat{p}$ and $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$, and improve previous calculations by including three-particle pion wave functions of twist 4. The formalism is extended from $\pi NN$ to $\eta NN$, $\pi\Xi\Xi$, $\eta\Xi\Xi$, $\pi\Sigma\Sigma$, and $\eta\Sigma\Sigma$ couplings, and the OPE terms are matched to SU(3) relations to identify contributions responsible for the $F/D$ ratio.

## Key numbers

- $f_\pi = 93$ MeV (pion decay constant)
- Twist-4 parameter $\delta^2 = 0.2$ GeV$^2$
- Quark-gluon mixed condensate parameter $m_0^2 = 0.8$ GeV$^2$
- From the $i\gamma_5$ sum rules beyond the chiral limit (previous work), the $F/D$ ratio obtained is $F/D \sim 0.2$
- The new $\delta^2$ term in the $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ sum rule for $\pi NN$ cancels the $m_0^2$ term and makes the OPE larger, improving agreement with the empirical $\pi NN$ coupling (previously reported as $g_{\pi N} \sim 10$)

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule calculations of meson-baryon couplings by Kim et al. (1999) that used the $i\gamma_5$ Dirac structure beyond the chiral limit, which gave $F/D \sim 0.2$ and showed large SU(3) breaking effects inconsistent with Nijmegen potentials (Rijken et al., 1990s) or common hypernuclei assumptions. The present paper improves the $\pi NN$ calculation by including twist-4 three-particle pion wave functions not properly considered in earlier work (Kim et al., 1997) and extends the analysis to the $i\gamma_5 \hat{p}$ and $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ Dirac structures. The results show Dirac-structure dependence of the $F/D$ ratio identification, which needs to be resolved to provide reliable QCD constraints for baryon-baryon potential models.
