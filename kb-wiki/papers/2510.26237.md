# MC-EKRT: Monte Carlo event generator with saturated minijet production for initializing 3+1 D fluid dynamics in high energy nuclear collisions

> We present a novel saturation and leading order collinear factorization based Monte-Carlo implementation of the EKRT model for computing QCD matter initial states in high-energy nuclear collisions. As new features the MC implementation gives a full 3-dimensional initial state event-by-event, introduces new event-by-event fluctuating spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution functions, includes dynamical fluctuations in minijet production and saturation, and accounts for the energy-momentum and valence-quark number conservation.

# MC-EKRT: Monte Carlo event generator with saturated minijet production for initializing 3+1 D fluid dynamics in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The MC-EKRT model extends the earlier EbyE-EKRT saturation framework to provide full 3-dimensional, event-by-event initial conditions for hydrodynamics, including dynamical fluctuations in minijet production and spatially dependent fluctuating nuclear parton distribution functions, while maintaining agreement with measured pseudorapidity distributions and elliptic flow across LHC and RHIC energies. The authors note that the saturation filter is the dominant mechanism suppressing transverse energy, with energy-momentum conservation and valence-quark number conservation having progressively smaller effects.

## Method
The framework computes minijet production from leading-order collinearly factorized perturbative QCD, then applies a Monte Carlo implementation of the EKRT saturation conjecture: candidate dijet processes are accepted or rejected based on a transverse area non-overlap condition with a free parameter kappa_sat controlling saturation strength. Spatially dependent event-by-event nuclear PDFs are constructed from nucleon configurations using EPS09 nPDFs as the ensemble average. The surviving partonic final state is converted via Gaussian smearing into a 3+1 D energy density profile for viscous hydrodynamical evolution, with final hadron spectra computed via Cooper-Frye freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- Charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta as a function of pseudorapidity is compared for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV (ALICE data) and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (PHOBOS data), for central and semi-peripheral centrality classes.
- Elliptic flow v_2 as a function of pseudorapidity is compared for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (ALICE data) and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (PHOBOS data).
- The saturation condition uses a free parameter kappa_sat in the inequality |s - s_cand| < (1/kappa_sat) * (1/p_T + 1/p_T_cand).
- The average number of dijets in each nucleon-nucleon pair is T_nn(b) * sigma_jet, where T_nn(b) is the nucleon-nucleon overlap function.

## Context
This work extends the EbyE-EKRT model (Niemi 2015, Eskola 2017, Hirvonen 2022) that successfully described midrapidity observables in Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, and Au+Au collisions, by adding dynamical fluctuations and full 3D rapidity-dependent initial conditions. The new framework enables systematic studies of rapidity-dependent observables in heavy-ion collisions, testing whether the same saturation mechanism that explains midrapidity data also works at forward and backward rapidities. The authors note that only at larger rapidities in peripheral collisions at RHIC do larger deviations from data begin to appear.
