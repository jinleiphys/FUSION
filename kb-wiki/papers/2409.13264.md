# Re examination of the effect of Pairing Gaps on Gamow Teller Strength Distributions and beta decay Rates

> Beta decay is one of the key factors to understand the r process and evolution of massive stars. The Gamow Teller (GT) transitions drive the beta decay process. We employ the proton neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (on QRPA) model to calculate terrestrial and stellar beta decay rates for 50 top ranked nuclei possessing astrophysical significance according to a recent survey. The model parameters of the on QRPA model affect the predicted results of beta decay. The current study investigates the effect of nucleon nucleon pairing gaps on charge changing transitions and the associated beta decay rates. Three different values of pairing gaps, namely TF, 3TF and 5TF, were used in our investigation. It was concluded that both GT strength distributions and half lives are sensitive to pairing gap values. The 3TF pairing gap scheme, in our chosen nuclear model, resulted in best prediction with around 80 percent of the calculated half-lives within a factor 10 of the measured ones. The 3TF pairing scheme also led to calculation of biggest beta decay rates in stellar matter.

## Key claim

The study finds that Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives are sensitive to the choice of nucleon-nucleon pairing gap, with the 3TF pairing scheme yielding the best agreement with measured half-lives (about 80% within a factor of 10), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model employed and the set of 50 nuclei selected.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model to calculate terrestrial and stellar beta-decay rates for 50 nuclei identified as astrophysically significant in a recent survey. They systematically varied the nucleon-nucleon pairing gap using three different values: the standard Thomas-Fermi (TF) pairing gap, 3TF, and 5TF. For each pairing scheme, they computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions and corresponding beta-decay half-lives, comparing the latter against experimental measurements.

## Key numbers

* For the 3TF pairing scheme, approximately 80% of the calculated half-lives fell within a factor of 10 of the experimentally measured values.
* Three different pairing gap values were tested: TF, 3TF, and 5TF.
* The study focused on 50 top-ranked nuclei of astrophysical significance.
* The 3TF pairing scheme produced the largest beta-decay rates in stellar matter among the three schemes tested.

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of beta-decay rates for r-process and stellar evolution modeling by systematically examining the sensitivity to pairing gap parameters, a model input often treated as fixed. The finding that the 3TF pairing gap yields best agreement with experiment for this specific set of 50 nuclei provides guidance for future large-scale calculations of beta-decay rates needed for nucleosynthesis simulations, though the authors note the result is model-dependent and may not generalize to all nuclei or other QRPA implementations.
