# Exploring the interplay of helicity and global-frame spin density matrix elements

> A significant $ϕ$-meson global spin alignment ($ρ_{00}$) signal was measured in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\le62$ GeV. Conventional physics mechanisms such as $s(\bar{s})$ spin polarization fail to accommodate this $ρ_{00}$ signal, motivating further investigation into its origin. Recent studies from a general framework utilizing the gauge/gravity duality predict that there could be non-zero helicity-frame spin alignment generated by the relative motion of $s\bar{s}$ pairs to the thermal background in heavy-ion collisions, leading to significant global $ρ_{00}$. In this work, we derive the expected relationships between $ϕ$-meson helicity and global-frame spin density matrix elements and confirm these analytical results using Monte Carlo simulations. The effects on these relationships from finite elliptic flow and various kinematic selections are also examined. Additionally, we extract $ρ_{00}$ simultaneously with the off-diagonal elements from the 2-dimensional $θ^{*}$ and $β$ angular dimensions, as opposed to the standard 1-dimensional extraction of $ρ_{00}$. These angles describe the direction of a daughter kaon's momentum in the $ϕ$-meson's rest frame, where $θ^{*}$ is the polar angle with respect to the chosen spin-quantization axis, and $β$ is the azimuthal angle in the perpendicular plane. This 2-dimensional method allows us to consider possible effects from finite geometric acceptance and detector efficiencies, which could couple to the spin density matrix elements and influence the extraction of these elements within the same frame. The studies presented in this work explore the relationships between helicity and global-frame spin density matrix elements and possible detector effects on the 2-dimensional extraction of spin density matrix elements.

# Exploring the interplay of helicity and global-frame spin density matrix elements

## Key claim

The paper derives and confirms with Monte Carlo simulations that every element of the helicity-frame spin density matrix affects every element of the global-frame spin density matrix, but integration over the azimuthal angle difference $\phi-\Psi$ (as done in typical experimental measurements) removes many of these dependencies, with the remaining contributions modified by elliptic flow, rapidity, and transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors analytically relate the global-frame and helicity-frame spin density matrix elements for vector mesons using a 3-dimensional rotation operator with Euler angles derived from the $\phi$-meson momentum components. They then perform Monte Carlo simulations of $\phi$-meson decays into $K^{+}K^{-}$ pairs using Pythia, applying weights to the helicity-frame angular distributions to simulate non-zero helicity-frame spin density matrix elements, and extract the resulting global-frame elements by fitting the 2-dimensional $\cos\theta^{*}$ and $\beta$ angular distributions. The effects of elliptic flow are incorporated by weighting the $\phi-\Psi$ distribution according to a Fourier expansion with variable $v_{2}$ coefficient.

## Key numbers

- $\phi$-mesons generated with $p_{T,\phi}=2$ GeV/c and $y_{\phi}=1$ for the $\phi-\Psi$ dependence studies
- Twenty evenly spaced discrete values of $\phi-\Psi$ from 0 to $\pi$ used in the angular dependence study
- $v_{2}=0.2$ used as the fixed elliptic flow value in rapidity and $p_{T}$ dependence studies
- Significant $p_{T,\phi}$ dependence of $\langle\rho_{\lambda_{1}\lambda_{2}}^{g}\rangle$ observed for $p_{T,\phi}\leq 2$ GeV/c, with distributions flattening at higher $p_{T,\phi}$

## Context

This work extends the gauge/gravity duality framework of HelicityFrame (2024) that predicts non-zero helicity-frame spin alignment from the relative motion of $s\bar{s}$ pairs to the thermal background in heavy-ion collisions. The paper provides the necessary relationships to connect those helicity-frame predictions to the global-frame measurements reported by the STAR Collaboration (Nature, 2023) for $\phi$-meson global spin alignment in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq 62$ GeV. The results enable future experimental extractions of off-diagonal spin density matrix elements, which are relevant as potential physics backgrounds to chiral magnetic effect observables involving vector meson decay products as discussed in OffDiagonalOrig and OffDiagonal.
