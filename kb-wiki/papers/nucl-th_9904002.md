# $S$-matrix poles near the $ΛN$ and $ΣN$ Thresholds in the Coupled \\$ΛN-ΣN$ System

> We search $t$-matrix poles for $ΛN-ΣN$ coupling interactions using two soft core models of the Nijmegen group which bind the hypertriton at the correct binding energy, and hard core models which are still influential in hypernuclear physics. To treat the hard core potentials, a useful method for calculating the off-shell $t$-matrix is proposed. We find poles close to the $ΣN$ threshold in the second or third quadrant of the complex plane of the $ΣN$ relative momentum. The relation between the poles and the shape of the $ΛN$ elastic total cross section is discussed based on a so-called uniformization by which two-channel $t$-matrices become single-valued on a complex valuable. We also find poles near the $ΛN$ threshold. These are correlated to the $S$-wave $ΛN$ scattering lengths, the values of which have yet to be determined.

## Key claim

The paper searches for poles of the $t$-matrix in the coupled $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ system using several Nijmegen potential models, finding that poles near the $\Sigma N$ threshold can produce either cusps or round resonance peaks in the $\Lambda N$ elastic total cross section depending on which Riemann sheet they occupy, and that these poles are not simple threshold effects but can move between sheets as the coupling strength varies.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space for the $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ system, analytically continuing the $t$-matrix into the complex energy plane. For the hard core potentials (Nijmegen D and F), they developed a two-potential formula that separates the pure hard core part, which is treated analytically, from the remainder, which is solved via a standard integral equation. They used a uniformization mapping introduced by Newton (1966) to transform the four-sheeted Riemann energy surface into a single complex plane, allowing clear identification of pole locations relative to the two thresholds.

## Key numbers

* NSC97f pole near $\Sigma N$ threshold: $q_2 = (-0.35, 0.15)$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to center-of-mass energy $E = (2135.6, -3.89)$ MeV
* NSC89 pole near $\Sigma N$ threshold: $q_2 = (-0.04, -0.39)$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to $E = (2126.3, 1.07)$ MeV
* Nijmegen D pole near $\Sigma N$ threshold: $q_2 = (-0.18, -0.08)$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to $E = (2132.8, 1.07)$ MeV
* Nijmegen F pole near $\Sigma N$ threshold: $q_2 = (-0.28, 0.12)$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to $E = (2134.2, -2.49)$ MeV
* $^1S_0$ scattering length for potentials that reproduce hypertriton binding: $-2.6$ to $-2.4$ fm
* $^3S_1$ scattering length for those potentials: $-1.7$ to $-1.3$ fm
* Corresponding $^1S_0$ antibound-state pole position in $q_1$: approximately $-0.27 i$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends previous few-body analyses of the hypertriton by Miyagawa and Gloeckle (1993) and Miyagawa et al. (1995), which showed that the Nijmegen soft core model NSC89 binds the hypertriton while the Juelich potential does not, and that the $\Lambda-\Sigma$ conversion contributes about 8% of the total potential energy. The paper directly addresses the need to study $\Lambda-\Sigma$ conversion effects near the $\Sigma N$ threshold, where unstable bound states have been predicted by Harada et al. (1990) and searched for experimentally in the $^4He(K^-,\pi^-)$ reaction by Nagae (1997). The results enable future calculations of $\Lambda NN-\Sigma NN$ continuum states and analyses of kaon photoproduction reactions at TJLAB and SPring-8.
