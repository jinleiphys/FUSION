# Studies of the electric dipole transitions of deformed rare-earth nuclei

> Spectrum and electric dipole transition rates and relative intensities in $^{152-154}$Sm, $^{156-160}$Gd, $^{160-162}$Dy are studied in the framework of the interacting boson model with s,p,d,f bosons. It is found that E1 transition data among the low-lying levels are in good agreement with the SU(3) dynamical symmetry of the spdf interacting boson model proposed by Engel and Iachello to describe collective rotation with octupole vibration. These results show that these nuclei have SU(3) dynamic symmetry to a good approximation. Also in this work many algebraic expressions for electric dipole transitions in the SU(3) limit of the spdf-IBM have been obtained. These formulae together with the formulae given previously exhaust nearly all the E1 transitions for low-lying negative parity states. They are useful in analyzing experimental data.

## Key claim

The low-lying negative parity states and electric dipole transitions in the deformed rare-earth nuclei \(^{152-154}\)Sm, \(^{156-160}\)Gd, and \(^{160-162}\)Dy are well described by the SU(3) dynamical symmetry of the spdf interacting boson model, but only to a good approximation; the authors acknowledge that the dynamical symmetry is broken in real nuclei and that some discrepancies remain, particularly for certain transitions in \(^{160}\)Dy and intensity ratios in \(^{152,154}\)Sm.

## Method

The authors applied the SU(3) limit of the spdf interacting boson model (IBM) to calculate energy spectra and electric dipole (E1) transition matrix elements for low-lying positive and negative parity bands (ground state, \(\beta\), \(\gamma\), \(K^\pi=0^-\), and \(K^\pi=1^-\) bands). They derived algebraic expressions for E1 transition matrix elements using SU(3) Wigner coefficients and compared calculated B(E1) values and relative intensities with experimental data for seven deformed rare-earth nuclei, fitting the parameters of a general E1 transition operator (with sp, dp, and df terms) to reproduce the data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{152}\)Sm, the fitted parameters are: \(a_1 = -7.49\) keV, \(\epsilon_- = 4.65\) MeV, \(a_3 = -8.90\) keV, \(a_4 = 16.75\) keV for positive parity states and \(a_4 = 9.67\) keV for negative parity states.
- In \(^{152}\)Sm, the fitted E1 operator parameters are \(\chi_{dp} = 81.669\) and \(\chi_{df} = -4.975\) (with the sp term coefficient fixed to 1).
- In \(^{160}\)Dy, experimental B(E1) values vary over a wide range from \(6.8 \times 10^{-3}\) W.u. to \(3.10 \times 10^{-8}\) W.u., a change of five orders of magnitude.
- In \(^{158}\)Gd, the 7 experimental B(E1) values range from 0.00633 W.u. to \(1.21 \times 10^{-5}\) W.u. and are all well reproduced by the model.
- In \(^{152}\)Sm, the ratio \(B(E1,1^-\rightarrow 2^+_{gs})/B(E1,1^-\rightarrow 0^+_{gs})\) is approximately 2 in the SU(3) octupole vibration limit, consistent with the factor \(\sqrt{L}\) in the matrix elements.
- In \(^{152}\)Sm, the experimental value \(B(E1;4^+_{\gamma}\rightarrow3^-_1) = 3.2 \times 10^{-5}(13)\) is reproduced by introducing a small mixing parameter \(|a_2| = 0.0118\) between \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) bands.

## Context

This work extends the spdf interacting boson model SU(3) limit, originally proposed by Engel and Iachello to describe octupole vibration in deformed nuclei, to a systematic study of rare-earth nuclei where ample E1 transition data exist. It provides algebraic E1 transition formulae that, together with those given in a previous reference, exhaust nearly all E1 transitions for low-lying negative parity states. The results are compared with the sd-f IBM study by Cottle and Zamfir, and the authors note that both models give similar agreement with data, but the spdf IBM is more general and includes the p boson, which is found to be critical in some nuclei such as \(^{152}\)Sm. The work enables future identification of other nuclei with SU(3) octupole vibration dynamical symmetry, particularly in the actinide region.
