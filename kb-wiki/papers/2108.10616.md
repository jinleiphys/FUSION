# Bosonic fluctuations in the $( 1 + 1 )$-dimensional Gross-Neveu(-Yukawa) model at varying $μ$ and $T$ and finite $N$

> Using analogies between flow equations from the Functional Renormalization Group and flow equations from (numerical) fluid dynamics we investigate the effects of bosonic fluctuations in a bosonized Gross-Neveu model -- namely the Gross-Neveu-Yukawa model. We study this model for finite numbers of fermions at varying chemical potential and temperature in the local potential approximation. Thereby we numerically demonstrate that for any finite number of fermions and as long as the temperature is non-zero, there is no $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry breaking for arbitrary chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The authors numerically demonstrate that for any finite number of fermions N and any non-zero temperature T, there is no spontaneous Z2 symmetry breaking in the Gross-Neveu-Yukawa model at any chemical potential μ, supporting the qualitative arguments of Landau and of Dashen, Ma, and Rajaraman (1974) that long-range bosonic fluctuations restore the symmetry in the deep infrared.

## Method

The authors use the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) in the local potential approximation (LPA) to study the bosonized Gross-Neveu-Yukawa model in 1+1 dimensions. They reformulate the LPA flow equation as a conservation equation analogous to fluid dynamics and solve it numerically using a finite-volume method, treating fermionic contributions as a sink/source term that introduces a moving discontinuity at low temperatures and non-zero chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for a phase transition to the symmetry-broken phase is T_C = 0 for all chemical potentials μ and finite N.
- At T = μ = 0 (vacuum), the computations indicate spontaneous symmetry breaking even at finite N.
- The mean-field (infinite-N) limit of the LPA flow equation is shown analytically to reproduce the canonical infinite-N phase diagram from Wolff (1985) and Thies (2006).

## Context

This work extends the qualitative arguments of Landau and Lifshitz (1980) and Dashen, Ma, and Rajaraman (1974) against symmetry breaking in one-dimensional systems at non-zero temperature, providing the first numerical FRG confirmation for the Gross-Neveu model. The results contradict indications from recent lattice Monte-Carlo studies by Pannullo (2019) and Lenz (2020) that some condensation phenomena might persist at finite N, but the authors note those lattice results may suffer from finite volume effects and discretization artifacts. The work enables future studies of the T=0, μ>0 regime where a possible quantum phase transition is suggested but could not be computed due to numerical difficulties from the moving discontinuity.
