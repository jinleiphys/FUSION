# Non-Markovian large amplitude motion and nuclear fission

> The general problem of dissipation in macroscopic large-amplitude collective motion and its relation to energy diffusion of intrinsic degrees of freedom of a nucleus is studied. By applying the cranking approach to the nuclear many body system, a set of coupled dynamical equations for the collective classical variables and the quantum mechanical occupancies of the intrinsic nuclear states is derived. Different dynamical regimes of the intrinsic nuclear motion and its consequences on time properties of collective dissipation are discussed. The approach is applied to the descant of the nucleus from the fission barrier.

# Non-Markovian large amplitude motion and nuclear fission

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-Markovian Langevin equation for nuclear collective motion from a microscopic cranking approach, showing that the random force accelerates the descent from the fission barrier compared to Newtonian dynamics, and that this acceleration grows with memory time. The approach is limited to ensemble-averaged dynamics, losing information about quantum fluctuations of intrinsic degrees of freedom, which are then reintroduced phenomenologically via a stochastic force term satisfying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Method

The authors start from the Liouville equation for the nuclear many-body density matrix and apply Zwanzig's projection technique to derive coupled equations for the diagonal and non-diagonal parts of the density matrix in the basis of adiabatic eigenstates. Using random matrix theory for the coupling matrix elements between collective and intrinsic degrees of freedom, they obtain a diffusion-like equation for ensemble-averaged occupancies of many-body states, then derive the collective mass parameter and energy dissipation rate, leading to a set of non-Markovian Langevin equations for the collective shape parameters. The equations are solved numerically for symmetric fission of 236U using a two-parameter family of Lorentz shapes for the nuclear surface, with 2 × 10^4 trajectories generated by different random realizations of the stochastic force.

## Key numbers

- Initial kinetic energy at saddle-point deformation: E_kin = 1 MeV
- Temperature of the nucleus: T = 2 MeV
- Total number of trajectories for the Langevin calculation: 2 × 10^4
- The most probable and mean value of the saddle-to-scission descent time are significantly smaller than the Newtonian estimation (no specific numerical value given for the Newtonian time)

## Context

This work extends the dissipative diabatic model of Ayik and Nörenberg (1982) and the linear response theory of Hofmann (1997) by deriving non-Markovian dynamics from a microscopic cranking approach with random matrix theory, building on the authors' previous work (Kolomietz and Radionov, 2007). The approach enables the study of memory effects in nuclear fission dynamics, showing that the random force accelerates the descent from the fission barrier and that the difference between Langevin and Newtonian calculations of the mean saddle-to-scission time grows with memory time, which the authors attribute to correlation properties of the non-Markovian random force.
