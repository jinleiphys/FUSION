# Contemporary continuum QCD approaches to excited hadrons

> Amongst the bound states produced by the strong interaction, radially excited meson and nucleon states offer an important phenomenological window into the long-range behavior of the coupling constant in Quantum Chromodynamics. We here report on some technical details related to the computation of the bound state's eigenvalue spectrum in the framework of Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equations.

## Key claim

Radially excited meson and nucleon states provide a phenomenological window into the long-range behavior of QCD's coupling constant, but the authors report that they are unable to consistently and simultaneously describe both ground and excited state observables with the given interaction in the rainbow-ladder truncation, one parameter set reproduces experimental ground state masses and decay constants but not excited state data, while a second parameter set only provides a reasonable description of the excited pseudoscalar mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons and the Faddeev equation for nucleons as eigenvalue problems in the rainbow-ladder truncation of the interaction kernel. They employ the power (von Mises) iteration method for ground states, Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization to extract excited state eigenvectors, and the implicitly restarted Arnoldi method (IRAM) via the ARPACK library for numerical stability with non-symmetric matrices after momentum discretization and Chebyshev expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the pion ground state, Model 1 yields mass 0.138 GeV and decay constant 0.139 GeV; Model 2 yields mass 0.153 GeV and decay constant 0.189 GeV (experimental: mass 0.139 GeV, decay constant 0.1304 GeV)
- For the π(1300) first radial excitation, Model 1 yields mass 0.990 GeV and decay constant -1.1×10^{-3} GeV; Model 2 yields mass 1.414 GeV and decay constant -8.3×10^{-4} GeV (experimental mass: 1.30 ± 0.10 GeV)
- For the kaon ground state, Model 1 yields mass 0.493 GeV and decay constant 0.164 GeV; Model 2 yields mass 0.541 GeV and decay constant 0.214 GeV (experimental: mass 0.493 GeV, decay constant 0.156 GeV)
- For the K(1460) first radial excitation, Model 1 yields mass 1.158 GeV and decay constant -0.018 GeV; Model 2 yields mass 1.580 GeV and decay constant -0.017 GeV (experimental mass: 1.460 GeV)
- For the η_c(1S) ground state, Model 1 yields mass 3.065 GeV and decay constant 0.389 GeV; Model 2 yields mass 3.210 GeV and decay constant 0.464 GeV (experimental: mass 2.984 GeV, decay constant 0.395 GeV)
- For the η_c(2S) first radial excitation, Model 1 yields mass 3.402 GeV and decay constant 0.089 GeV; Model 2 yields mass 3.784 GeV and decay constant 0.105 GeV (experimental mass: 3.639 GeV)
- The preferred inverse effective range ω of the Maris-Tandy interaction in rainbow-ladder truncation is ω_{Q\bar Q} = 0.7 GeV for heavy quarkonia versus ω_{q\bar q} = 0.5 GeV for light quarkonia
- The decay constant of excited pseudoscalar states vanishes identically in the chiral limit: f_{P_n}^{\hat m=0}(μ) ≡ 0 for n ≥ 1

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach to excited hadrons, building on prior studies of ground state mesons by Maris and Tandy (1999) and Qin et al. (2011). The techniques described enable computation of masses, weak decay constants, and electromagnetic form factors for radially excited mesons and nucleons, with the nucleon's first radial excitation recently identified as the Roper resonance by Segovia et al. (2015). The paper reports that the rainbow-ladder truncation with the Maris-Tandy interaction cannot simultaneously describe ground and excited state observables with a single parameter set, pointing to the need for improved truncations beyond the leading approximation.
