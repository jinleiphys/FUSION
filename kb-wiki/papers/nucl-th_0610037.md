# Microscopic three-body forces and kaon condensation in cold neutrino-trapped matter

> We investigate the composition and the equation of state of the kaon condensed phase in neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped star matter within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with three-body forces. We find that neutrino trapping shifts the onset density of kaon condensation to a larger baryon density, and reduces considerably the kaon abundance. As a consequence, when kaons are allowed, the equation of state of neutrino-trapped star matter becomes stiffer than the one of neutrino free matter. The effects of different three-body forces are compared and discussed. Neutrino trapping turns out to weaken the role played by the symmetry energy in determining the composition of stellar matter, and thus reduces the difference between the results obtained by using different three-body forces.

# Microscopic three-body forces and kaon condensation in cold neutrino-trapped matter

## Key claim

Neutrino trapping shifts the onset density of kaon condensation to a larger baryon density and reduces kaon abundance, making the equation of state stiffer than in neutrino-free matter; however, this result depends on the choice of three-body force (phenomenological Urbana versus microscopic) and on the value of the kaon-nucleon interaction parameter \(a_3 m_s\), with the effect of neutrino trapping weakening the role of the symmetry energy in determining stellar composition.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-body potential supplemented by either the phenomenological Urbana three-body force or a microscopic three-body force derived from meson-exchange currents. Kaon condensation was treated using the Kaplan-Nelson chiral effective theory, and the coupled equations for chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and condensate amplitude minimization were solved for both neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped matter at zero temperature, with lepton number fractions fixed at \(Y_{L_e}=0.4\) and \(Y_{L_\mu}=0\) for the trapped case.

## Key numbers

- Onset density for kaon condensation with \(a_3 m_s = -310\) MeV: approximately \(0.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), almost independent of the adopted nucleonic three-body force.
- Symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density: \(30 \pm 4\) MeV, reproduced by both three-body force models.
- Maximum gravitational mass with kaons and microscopic three-body force in neutrino-free matter: about \(2\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum gravitational mass with kaons and phenomenological Urbana three-body force in neutrino-free matter: about \(1.7\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum gravitational mass with kaons and phenomenological Urbana three-body force in neutrino-trapped matter: around \(1.8\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum gravitational mass with kaons and microscopic three-body force in neutrino-trapped matter: slightly larger than \(2\ M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BHF study of kaon condensation in neutrino-free neutron star matter (Zuo 2004) and the investigation of three-body force effects on neutron star maximum mass (Zhou 2004). It builds on systematic studies of protoneutron star structure by Prakash (1997), who used schematic potential models, relativistic field approaches, and effective chiral models, and on work by Vidana (2003) showing that neutrino trapping delays hyperon onset. The results enable predictions for protoneutron star evolution and black hole formation timescales, and the authors note that inclusion of hyperons (which appear at \(2-3\) times saturation density in BHF calculations) may compete with kaon condensation and alter the scenario.
