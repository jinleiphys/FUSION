# 2-group global symmetries, hydrodynamics and holography

> 2-group global symmetries are a particular example of how higher-form and conventional global symmetries can fuse together into a larger structure. We construct a theory of hydrodynamics describing the finite-temperature realization of a 2-group global symmetry composed out of $U(1)$ zero-form and $U(1)$ one-form symmetries. We study aspects of the thermodynamics from a Euclidean partition function and derive constitutive relations for ideal hydrodynamics from various points of view. Novel features of the resulting theory include an analogue of the chiral magnetic effect and a chiral sound mode propagating along magnetic field lines. We also discuss a minimalist holographic description of a theory dual to 2-group global symmetry and verify predictions from hydrodynamic descriptions. Along the way we clarify some aspects of symmetry breaking in higher-form theories at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a hydrodynamic theory for systems with a 2-group global symmetry (combining a U(1) zero-form and a U(1) one-form symmetry) at finite temperature, showing that the constitutive relations acquire novel terms at ideal (zeroth-derivative) order that are analogous to anomaly-induced transport but arise from an anomaly-free symmetry structure.

## Method

The authors derive constitutive relations for the stress-energy tensor, the one-form current, and the two-form current using a Euclidean partition function approach on \(S^1 \times \mathbb{R}^3\) and an effective action formalism for ideal hydrodynamics. They also construct a minimal holographic dual in \(AdS_{d+2}\) with bulk gauge fields \(\mathcal{A}_a\) and \(\mathcal{B}_{ab}\) governed by an action invariant under 2-group gauge transformations, and verify hydrodynamic predictions against quasinormal mode spectra.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium current along the string direction at zero external source is \(\langle j^\mu \rangle h_\mu = -2 \hat{\kappa} \mu_a \rho_b\), where \(\hat{\kappa}\) is the 2-group structure constant, \(\mu_a\) the zero-form chemical potential, and \(\rho_b\) the one-form charge density.
- The equilibrium momentum density along the string direction is \(\langle T^{t \nu} \rangle h_\nu = -\hat{\kappa} \mu_a^2 \rho_b\).
- The transverse collective mode (skewed Alfven wave) has dispersion \(\omega_\perp = \left( - \frac{\hat{\kappa} \mu_a^2 \rho_b}{\varepsilon + p} \pm \sqrt{\mathcal{V}_A^2 + \left( \frac{\hat{\kappa} \mu_a^2 \rho_b}{\varepsilon + p} \right)^2} \right) q_z\), with \(\mathcal{V}_A^2 = \frac{\mu_b \rho_b}{\varepsilon + p}\).
- The longitudinal chiral sound mode has dispersion \(\omega_\parallel = - \frac{\hat{\kappa} \rho_b}{\chi_{aa}} q_z\), where \(\chi_{aa} = \partial \rho_a / \partial \mu_a\) is the zero-form susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework for higher-form symmetries developed by Grozdanov et al. (2016) and Armas et al. (2018) to include the 2-group structure where zero-form and one-form symmetries intertwine. It contrasts with anomalous hydrodynamics (e.g., chiral magnetic effect studied by Son and Surowka (2009) and Landsteiner (2016)) by showing that 2-group effects appear at ideal order rather than at first order in derivatives. The holographic model builds on the gauge/gravity duality and was developed concurrently with related work by DeWolfe and others (2020) on higher-group holography.
