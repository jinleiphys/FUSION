# Bulk Viscosity in Hybrid Stars

> We compute the bulk viscosity of a mixed quark-hadron phase. In the first scenario to be discussed, the mixed phase occurs at large densities and we assume that it is composed of a mixing of hyperonic matter and quarks in the Color Flavor Locked phase. In a second scenario, the mixed phase occurs at lower densities and it is composed of a mixing of nucleons and unpaired quark matter. We have also investigated the effect of a non-vanishing surface tension at the interface between hadronic and quark matter. In both scenarios, the bulk viscosity is large when the surface tension is absent, while the value of the viscosity reduces in the second scenario when a finite value for the surface tension is taken into account. In all cases, the r-mode instabilities of the corresponding hybrid star are suppressed.

# Bulk Viscosity in Hybrid Stars

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of a mixed quark-hadron phase in hybrid stars is large enough to suppress r-mode instabilities in all scenarios considered, but this result depends critically on whether the surface tension at the hadron-quark interface is negligible or finite. When a finite surface tension (10–30 MeV/fm²) is present, the viscosity is reduced, particularly in the nucleon-quark mixed phase scenario, yet the suppression of r-mode instabilities remains.

## Method
The authors construct equations of state for two scenarios: a hyperon-quark mixed phase (hyperonic matter with Color Flavor Locked quark matter) at high densities, and a nucleon-quark mixed phase (nucleons with unpaired quark matter) at lower densities. They generalize the formalism of Lindblom (2001) to compute bulk viscosity in a mixed phase under Gibbs conditions, classifying reactions as fast (strong interactions, including melting processes) or slow (weak non-leptonic reactions), and separately treat cases with negligible surface tension (σ ≲ 1 MeV/fm²) and with finite surface tension (10–30 MeV/fm²) where melting processes are suppressed and baryon number and charge are conserved separately in each phase.

## Key numbers
- Surface tension threshold for negligible case: σ ≲ 1 MeV/fm² (r-mode excitations provide energy per baryon of a few MeV, sufficient to break finite-size structures).
- Surface tension range for finite case: 10 MeV/fm² < σ < 30 MeV/fm² (values above ~30 MeV/fm² would not allow mixed phase formation).
- Relaxation time for mechanical equilibrium via strong interactions: t_s ~ 10⁻²³ s; period of r-mode perturbation: t_p ~ 10⁻³ s.
- Bulk viscosity in the hyperon-quark mixed phase is comparable to that of pure hyperonic matter; in the nucleon-quark mixed phase it is of the same order as bulk viscosity of strange matter (Madsen 1992).
- Critical angular velocities are computed for a star of mass M = 1.46 M⊙; hybrid stars with hyperon-quark mixed phase are as stable as purely hyperonic stars; a small mixed phase region near the edge of the star is sufficient to damp r-modes in the nucleon-quark scenario.
- Modified Urca process viscosity scales as T⁶ and is negligible below T ~ 10¹⁰ K.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of r-mode instabilities in compact stars, previously studied for purely hadronic stars (Lindblom 2001; Jones 2001) and for pure quark stars (Madsen 1999), to the case of hybrid stars containing a mixed quark-hadron phase. The authors show that even when color-superconducting quark matter (which has negligible bulk viscosity) is present, the mixed phase region provides sufficient damping to stabilize the star, contradicting the expectation that pure color-superconducting quark stars are ruled out by pulsar data. The results enable future studies of hybrid star rotation and gravitational wave emission, and highlight the importance of the poorly-constrained surface tension parameter.
