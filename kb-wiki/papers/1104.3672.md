# Evidence for the tensor meson exchange in the kaon photoproduction

> The contribution of the tensor meson $K_2^*(1430)$ exchange in the process $γp\to K^+Λ(Σ^0)$ is investigated within the Regge framework. Inclusion of the $K_2^*$ exchange in the $K(494)+K^*(892)$ exchanges with the coupling constants chosen from the SU(3) symmetry leads to a better description of the production mechanism without referring to any fitting procedure. This shows the significance of the role of the tensor meson exchange to have the Regge theory basically free of parameters with the SU(3) symmetry a good approximation for the meson-baryon couplings.

## Key claim

Including the tensor meson \(K_2^*(1430)\) exchange in the Regge framework for \(\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda(\Sigma^0)\) allows the model to describe the production mechanism using coupling constants from SU(3) symmetry without any fitting procedure, provided the weak exchange degeneracy of the \(K^*\)-\(K_2^*\) pair is a good approximation and the \(K_2^*\) coupling constants are estimated from the known \(a_2NN\) couplings via SU(3) relations.

## Method

The authors constructed a Regge-pole model for kaon photoproduction in the \(s\)-channel helicity amplitude formalism, adding the \(K_2^*(1430)\) tensor meson exchange to the usual \(K(494)+K^*(892)\) exchanges. They determined all meson-baryon coupling constants from SU(3) symmetry relations and known decay widths, without fitting to the photoproduction data. The \(K_2^*\) Regge trajectory was taken with slope \(0.83\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and the rotating phase for the signature factor, and the coupling constants \(g^{(1)}_{K_2^*NY}\) were derived from the \(a_2NN\) coupling using SU(3) with the ratio \(F/D = -1.8 \pm 0.2\).

## Key numbers

* Radiative decay width of \(K_2^* \to K\gamma\): \(\Gamma = 0.24 \pm 0.05\) MeV, yielding the coupling constant \(g_{\gamma K K_2^*} = 0.276\).
* \(K_2^*\) trajectory: \(\alpha_{K_2^*}(t) = 0.83\,(t - m_{K_2^*}^2) + 2\).
* Chosen median SU(3) value for the \(a_2NN\) coupling: \(g^{(1)}_{a_2NN} = 1.4\), with \(g^{(2)}_{a_2NN} \approx 0\).
* Resulting \(K_2^*NY\) couplings from SU(3) with \(\alpha_{(1)} = 2.25\): \(g^{(1)}_{K_2^*p\Lambda} = -4.45\), \(g^{(1)}_{K_2^*p\Sigma^0} = -4.9\) (both with \(g^{(2)} = 0\)).
* \(K^*NY\) couplings from SU(3) in the present work: \(g^v_{K^*p\Lambda} = -4.5\), \(g^t_{K^*p\Lambda} = -16.7\), \(g^v_{K^*p\Sigma^0} = -2.6\), \(g^t_{K^*p\Sigma^0} = 3.2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of pion photoproduction (Yu, 2008), where the tensor meson \(a_2(1320)\) exchange was similarly included, to the kaon photoproduction channels. It directly addresses the long-standing problem that the \(K+K^*\) Regge models of Levy (2005) and Guidal (1997) required anomalously large \(K^*\) coupling constants to fit data, which disagreed with SU(3) predictions and with the Nijmegen soft-core potential (NSC97a). By adding the \(K_2^*\) exchange, the authors demonstrate that the Regge theory can be made essentially parameter-free using SU(3) symmetry, and they show that the previously fitted large \(K^*\) couplings were an artifact of omitting the tensor meson. This enables future parameter-free Regge predictions for other strangeness photoproduction processes.
