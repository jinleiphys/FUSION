# Three-loop Corrections in a Covariant Effective Field Theory

> Chiral effective field theories have been used with success in the study of nuclear structure. It is of interest to systematically improve these energy functionals (particularly that of quantum hadrodynamics) through the inclusion of many-body correlations. One possible source of improvement is the loop expansion. Using the techniques of Infrared Regularization, the short-range, local dynamics at each order in the loops is absorbed into the parameterization of the underlying effective lagrangian. The remaining nonlocal, exchange correlations must be calculated explicitly. Given that the interactions of quantum hadrodynamics are relatively soft, the loop expansion may be manageable or even perturbative in nuclear matter. This work investigates the role played by the three-loop contributions to the loop expansion for quantum hadrodynamics.

## Key claim

The three-loop contributions to the loop expansion for quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) are investigated to determine whether the loop expansion is a viable expansion scheme; the short-range local dynamics are absorbed via Infrared Regularization, and the remaining nonlocal exchange correlations are calculated in the ladder approximation to the Bethe-Saltpeter equation, with the expectation that the loop expansion may be asymptotic due to the relatively soft interactions of QHD.

## Method

The connected generating functional for QHD was expanded to three-loop order, yielding eleven integrals of interest: ten with four nucleon propagators and two meson propagators (scalar, vector, or pion), plus one additional diagram with two baryon and two pion propagators. Infrared Regularization was used to separate and absorb local vacuum-loop contributions into the effective lagrangian parameters, while the remaining nonlocal exchange correlations were evaluated by replacing two of the baryon propagators in each ladder integral with a two-body propagator (Blankenbecler-Sugar, Thompson, or Erkelenz-Holinde forms). The crossed-ladder diagrams required a Fierz transformation for proper antisymmetrization.

## Key numbers

* The three-loop integrals are found to be about third order in the power counting scheme developed for the underlying QHD lagrangian, consistent with the two-loop integrals being about third order as shown in prior work (McIntire 2007).
* The naturalness of the lowest-level couplings is confirmed at the three-loop level, meaning the undetermined coefficients in the lagrangian remain of order unity.
* No specific numerical values for the integrals (e.g., in MeV or dimensionless units) are provided in the text; the paper focuses on the structure and power-counting order of the three-loop contributions rather than reporting computed numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the two-loop analysis of McIntire (2007) and McIntire (2007a) for QHD to the three-loop level, using the same Infrared Regularization techniques developed for Chiral Perturbation Theory by Tang (1996), Ellis (1998), Becher (1999, 2000), and Scherer (2003). The results help determine whether the loop expansion is a good expansion scheme for QHD; future work is left to find the appropriate expansion parameter and to include nonlinear meson self-interactions at three-loop order.
