# Proton radioactivity in deformed nuclei with microscopic optical potential: A novel angular-dependent emission mechanism in the nanosecond-lived $^{149}$Lu

> We present a theoretical description of proton radioactivity in 149Lu, the most oblate deformed proton emitter known, by combining a deformed microscopic optical potential derived from ab initio nuclear matter calculations with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin penetration probabilities and the assault frequency of the emitted proton estimated through a new harmonic-oscillator-inspired scheme. We predict a novel angular-dependent phenomenon unprecedented in spherical proton emitters: the disappearance of classically allowed regions at small polar angles $(θ\leq 21^\circ)$. Our framework yields a half-life $T_{1/2}=467^{+143}_{-108}$ ns for 149Lu, in excellent agreement within uncertainties with the experimental value $450^{+170}_{-100}$ ns. Deformation analysis rigorously excludes configurations with $|β_2|\geq 0.32$. Extensions to 150, 151Lu and their isomers also achieve excellent agreement with experimental half-life data. We further predict 148Lu as another highly oblate $(β_2 = -0.166)$ proton emitter with a half-life $T_{1/2}=4.42$ ns. This work validates deformed microscopic optical potentials as a robust predictive tool for drip-line proton emitters and provides quantitative evidence for deformation effects in exotic decays.

## Key claim
The paper predicts a novel angular-dependent emission mechanism in the deformed proton emitter 149Lu, where the classically allowed region for the proton is completely suppressed at small polar angles (θ ≤ 21°), a phenomenon absent in spherical proton emitters. This prediction depends on using the full Schrödinger equivalent potential rather than the linear approximation (US + U0) used in prior covariant energy density functional studies, which artificially deepens the potential barrier and obscures this effect.

## Method
The authors combine a deformed microscopic optical potential derived from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory in the full Dirac space with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation for penetration probabilities. The nuclear potential is taken as the real part of the optical potential constructed via an improved local density approximation using two-dimensional nucleon densities from deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional. The assault frequency of the emitted proton is estimated through a new harmonic-oscillator-inspired scheme by extracting the oscillator frequency ω(θ) from the potential minimum.

## Key numbers
- 149Lu half-life: T1/2 = 467+143-108 ns (central prediction with Bonn A, Qp = 1920 keV, t = 1.3 fm), in agreement with experimental value 450+170-100 ns
- Classically allowed region disappears at polar angles θ ≤ 21° for 149Lu
- Deformation analysis excludes configurations with |β2| ≥ 0.32 for 149Lu
- 148Lu predicted as highly oblate (β2 = -0.166) proton emitter with T1/2 = 4.42 ns (using Qp = 2334 keV from Bayesian mass model)
- 150Lu ground state: T1/2 = 51.12 ms (experimental: 45(3) ms)
- 150mLu isomer: T1/2 = 18.50 μs (experimental: 40(7) μs)
- 151Lu ground state: T1/2 = 104.33 ms (experimental: 127.1(18) ms)
- 151mLu isomer: T1/2 = 9.57 μs (experimental: 16.0(0.5) μs)
- Nuclear potential depth approximately -56 MeV at r = 0 for 149Lu
- Daughter nucleus 148Yb deformation parameter β2 = -0.168 used for 149Lu calculations

## Context
This work extends prior studies of 149Lu using covariant energy density functional theory by XiaoY (2023) and LuQ (2024), which used only the linear term US + U0 and omitted the quadratic contribution in the Schrödinger equivalent potential, thereby artificially deepening the potential barrier. The paper also contrasts with nonadiabatic quasiparticle calculations by Auranen (2022) and empirical formulas by Chen (2019) and Delion (2021), which fail to reproduce the anomalously short half-life of 149Lu. The framework enables predictions for 148Lu as a candidate shortest-lived proton emitter and validates deformed microscopic optical potentials as a predictive tool for drip-line proton emitters.
