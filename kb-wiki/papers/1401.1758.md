# Jet Quenching and Correlations

> This article reviews recent advances in our understanding of the experimental aspects of jet-quenching and correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Emphasis is put on correlation measurements, namely jet-like correlations with anisotropic flow subtraction in heavy-ion collisions and long-range pseudorapidity correlations in small systems. Future path on correlation studies is envisioned which may elucidate jet-medium interactions and the properties of the hot dense medium in QCD.

## Key claim

This review argues that while jet-quenching and anisotropic flow are well-established phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, the extraction of jet-like correlation signals requires careful subtraction of flow backgrounds that are themselves measured via the same two-particle correlation methods, creating a fundamental ambiguity where the "flow" background and "jet" signal are effectively the same type of correlation measured in different phase-space regions.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of high-pT particle suppression, fully reconstructed jets (using the anti-kT algorithm with cone radius parameters R=0.2-0.5), jet-hadron correlations, gamma-hadron and gamma-jet correlations, and dihadron correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It describes the standard two-particle correlation function formalism with mixed-event normalization and Fourier decomposition into harmonic coefficients Vn, and discusses the ZYAM (Zero Yield At Minimum) procedure for background subtraction. The review also covers anisotropic flow measurements via two-particle correlations, event-plane methods, and multi-particle cumulants.

## Key numbers

- Jet cross-section suppression of a factor of 2 observed in top 10% central Pb+Pb collisions relative to 60-80% peripheral collisions at LHC (ATLAS)
- CMS jet fragmentation measurements used tracks with pT > 4 GeV/c and cone radius parameter R=0.3
- CMS jet-hadron correlations used tracks with pT > 1 GeV/c
- CMS gamma-jet correlations required direct gamma and jet energies of at least 60 GeV and 30 GeV respectively
- High-pT trigger particles in dihadron correlations typically carry mean z = 70% of the jet energy

## Context

This review extends the experimental understanding of jet-quenching established at RHIC (Arsene 2005, Back 2005, Adams 2005, Adcox 2005) and LHC (Muller 2012) into correlation measurements at low to intermediate pT. It builds on the theoretical framework of partonic energy loss via gluon bremsstrahlung and multiple scattering (Gyulassy 1990, Wang 1991) and the discovery of large collective flow (Ollitrault 1992, Heinz 2013). The review specifically addresses the interplay between jet-like correlations and anisotropic flow subtraction, a challenge highlighted by recent realizations that odd harmonic flow (v3) can be large due to event-by-event geometry fluctuations (Alver 2010, Holopainen 2010, Schenke 2010). Future measurements of gamma-hadron and gamma-jet correlations are identified as the "golden probe" to disentangle jet-medium interactions from flow backgrounds.
