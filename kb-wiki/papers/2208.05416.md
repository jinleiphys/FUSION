# Impact of nuclear structure on longitudinal flow decorrelations in high-energy isobar collisions

> Fluctuations of harmonic flow along pseudorapidity, known as flow decorrelations, are an important probe of the initial state geometry of the quark-gluon plasma. The flow decorrelations are shown to be sensitive to the collective structure of the colliding nuclei, as revealed clearly by comparing collisions of isobars, $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr, which have different nuclear structures. The flow decorrelations in central collisions are mostly sensitive to nuclear deformations, while those in the mid-central collisions are primarily sensitive to differences in skin thickness between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr. Longitudinal flow decorrelations in heavy-ion collisions are a new tool to probe the structure of colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

Flow decorrelations in high-energy isobar collisions are sensitive to nuclear structure, but the sensitivity is centrality-dependent: in central collisions (0–10%) the decorrelation is mostly sensitive to nuclear deformations (β₂ and β₃), while in mid-central collisions (10–40%) it is primarily sensitive to differences in nuclear skin thickness a₀ between ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model in string melting mode to simulate ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They computed the flow decorrelation observable r_n(η) for n=2 and n=3 using hadrons with transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 4 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |η| < 1.5, with reference pseudorapidity gaps of 3.1 < |ηref| < 5.1 for r₂ and 2.1 < |ηref| < 5.1 for r₃. To isolate the effect of individual nuclear structure parameters, they introduced three intermediate parameter sets (Case2–Case4) that vary only one Woods-Saxon parameter at a time between the Ru-like (Case1) and Zr-like (Case5) configurations.

## Key numbers

- r₂ values decrease by approximately 10% around η = 1.5 in both systems
- r₂ values in Ru+Ru are approximately 1% larger than in Zr+Zr in the 10–40% centrality interval
- r₃ decorrelation reaches about 30% around η = 1.5
- r₃ values in Ru+Ru are approximately 4% smaller than in Zr+Zr in the 0–10% centrality interval
- Quadrupole deformation: β₂Ru = 0.162, β₂Zr = 0.06
- Octupole deformation: β₃Ru = 0, β₃Zr = 0.2
- Nuclear skin: a₀Ru = 0.46 fm, a₀Zr = 0.52 fm
- Half-height radius: R₀Ru = 5.09 fm, R₀Zr = 5.02 fm
- Approximately 100 million events simulated for each of the five parameter sets

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear structure effects in isobar collisions, following prior measurements of v₂ and v₃ ratios by STAR (2021) and theoretical studies by Jia (2021) and Giacalone (2021) showing that nuclear deformations and radial profiles influence flow harmonics. The results demonstrate that longitudinal flow decorrelations provide a new tool to probe nuclear structure, complementing existing constraints from multiplicity distributions and harmonic flow ratios. The findings enable future experimental measurements at RHIC to extract nuclear deformation and skin thickness parameters from isobar collision data.
