# Hyperfine structure of the metastable 3P2 state of alkaline earth atoms as an accurate probe of nuclear magnetic octupole moments

> Measuring the hyperfine structure (HFS) of long-lived $^3P_2$ states of divalent atoms may offer the opportunity of extracting relatively unexplored nuclear magnetic octupole and electric hexadecapole moments. Here, using relativistic many-body methods of atomic structure and the nuclear shell model, we evaluate the effect of these higher nuclear moments on the hyperfine structure. We find that the sensitivity of HFS interval measurements in $^{87}$Sr needed to reveal the perturbation caused by the nuclear octupole moment is on the order of kHz. Results of similar analyses for $^{9}$Be, $^{25}$Mg, and $^{43}$Ca are also reported.

# Hyperfine structure of the metastable 3P2 state of alkaline earth atoms as an accurate probe of nuclear magnetic octupole moments

## Key claim

Measuring hyperfine intervals in the metastable $^3P_2$ state of $^{87}$Sr with a sensitivity of approximately 1 kHz could reveal the perturbation caused by the nuclear magnetic octupole moment, provided the second-order dipole-quadrupole correction can be computed to sufficient accuracy. The extraction of the octupole constant $C$ from measured intervals is limited by knowledge of the second-order dipole-quadrupole interference term $\zeta$, not by the dipole-dipole term $\eta$.

## Method

The authors employed a combined configuration-interaction and many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) method with Brueckner orbitals and a dual kinetic-balance B-spline basis set to compute electronic wave functions for the $^3P_2$ states of divalent alkaline-earth atoms. They evaluated the electronic matrix elements for magnetic-dipole, electric-quadrupole, magnetic-octupole, and electric-hexadecapole hyperfine interactions, including core-polarization (Brueckner orbital), screening, and random-phase approximation (RPA) corrections. Nuclear magnetic octupole moments were estimated using a single-particle nuclear shell model.

## Key numbers

- For $^{87}$Sr, the predicted magnetic-octupole hyperfine constant is $C(^{87}\mathrm{Sr}) = -201$ Hz, based on the single-particle nuclear shell model estimate of $\Omega = -0.38$ b$\times\mu_N$.
- The second-order dipole-quadrupole correction to $C$ for $^{87}$Sr is $\Delta C = 1.10(2)$ kHz, with an estimated error bar of 20 Hz.
- The sensitivity required in measuring hyperfine intervals in $^{87}$Sr to reveal the octupole perturbation is $\sigma_{\delta W} \approx 1$ kHz, yielding $\sigma_C \approx 100$ Hz.
- For $^{43}$Ca, the predicted octupole constant is $C(^{43}\mathrm{Ca}) = -16.2$ Hz; for $^{25}$Mg, $C(^{25}\mathrm{Mg}) = -2.57$ Hz; for $^{9}$Be, $C(^{9}\mathrm{Be}) = -3.57 \times 10^{-2}$ Hz.
- The CI+MBPT calculation for $^{87}$Sr yields $A = -230.6$ MHz (experiment: $-212.765(1)$ MHz) and $B = 62.2$ MHz (experiment: $67.215(15)$ MHz), representing approximately 8% agreement.
- The $^3P_2$ lifetimes are $1.29 \times 10^2$ s for $^{87}$Sr, $5.13 \times 10^2$ s for $^{43}$Ca, $1.06 \times 10^3$ s for $^{25}$Mg, and not listed for $^{9}$Be.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear magnetic octupole moments beyond the small number of isotopes previously measured (Cl, Ga, Br, In, V, Eu, Lu, Hf, and Cs) by proposing alkaline-earth atoms as clean systems where direct extraction of octupole moments is possible, unlike multi-valent atoms where atomic-structure calculations are inaccurate. The authors note that in the case of $^{133}$Cs, the measured octupole constant differed from the nuclear shell model prediction by a factor of 40 (Gerginov et al., 2003), suggesting that similar deviations may be found for the isotopes considered here. The work enables future microwave spectroscopy experiments on trapped or freely-falling alkaline-earth atoms, building on recent trapping demonstrations for Sr and Ca and ongoing efforts with Mg (Gruber et al., 2002; Nagel et al., 2003; Xu et al., 2003; Yasuda and Katori, 2004).
