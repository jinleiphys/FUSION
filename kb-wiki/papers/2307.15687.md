# Unconventional mechanisms of heavy quark fragmentation

> Heavy and light quarks produced in high-$p_T$ partonic collisions radiate differently. Heavy quarks regenerate their color field, stripped-off in the hard reaction, much faster than the light ones and radiate a significantly smaller fraction of the initial quark energy. This peculiar feature of heavy-quark jets leads to a specific shape of the fragmentation functions observed in $e^+e^-$ annihilation. Differently from light flavors, the heavy quark fragmentation function strongly peaks at large fractional momentum $z$, i.e. the produced heavy-light mesons, $B$ or $D$, carry the main fraction of the jet momentum. This is a clear evidence of the dead-cone effect, and of a short production time of a heavy-light mesons. Contrary to propagation of a small $q\bar q$ dipole, which survives in the medium due to color transparency, a heavy-light $Q\bar q$ dipole promptly expands to a large size. Such a big dipole has no chance to remain intact in a dense medium produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. On the other hand, a breakup of such a dipole does not affect much the production rate of $Q\bar q$ mesons, differently from the case of light $q\bar q$ meson production.

# Unconventional mechanisms of heavy quark fragmentation

## Key claim

Heavy quarks (charm and bottom) radiate only 10-20% of their initial momentum due to the dead-cone effect, which suppresses low-transverse-momentum gluon radiation; this explains the observed fragmentation functions that peak at large fractional momentum z (around 80% for B mesons), but the production length extracted from data is extremely short and shrinks with rising pT, indicating a purely perturbative fragmentation mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculate the radiational energy loss of heavy quarks in vacuum using the perturbative gluon radiation spectrum with a dead-cone suppression factor, then extract the production length distribution directly from experimental fragmentation function data via a differential relation. They model heavy-light Q\bar q dipole propagation through a hot medium using the transport coefficient (broadening rate) \hat q, with the dipole attenuation calculated via an exponential factor depending on the mean squared dipole radius and the path-integrated \hat q. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed by folding the pp production cross section with the medium-modified production length distribution and comparing to ATLAS and CMS data for B and D mesons in lead-lead collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quarks radiate only 10-20% of their initial momentum (Fig. 2, text: "small fraction 10-20%")
- Most B mesons carry z ~ 80% of the b-quark momentum (text: "z ~ 80%")
- Mean squared charge radius of B meson: <r_ch^2>_B = 0.378 fm^2 (from potential model, reference [radius])
- Formation length of B meson: l_f = 0.06 fm * [p_T/1 GeV] (for oscillatory potential with m_B' - m_B = 0.6 GeV)
- Mean free path of B meson in medium: lambda_B = 0.04 fm (at \hat q = 1 GeV^2/fm)
- Critical temperature for string tension: T_c = 200 MeV
- Vacuum string tension: kappa_0 = 1 GeV/fm
- Fitted transport coefficient for b-quarks: \hat q_0 = 0.2-0.25 GeV^2/fm
- Fitted transport coefficient for c-quarks: \hat q_0 = 0.45-0.55 GeV^2/fm
- Fitted transport coefficient for light quarks: \hat q_0 ≈ 2 GeV^2/fm
- Time for equilibrated medium production: t_0 = 1 fm
- Soft cut-off parameter: lambda = 0.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the dead-cone effect formalism of Dokshitzer, Khoze, and Troian (1991) and the radiational energy loss calculations of Kopeliovich, Potashnikova, and Schmidt (2010) to explain heavy quark fragmentation in both vacuum and dense medium. It contradicts the expectation that heavy quarks would have long production lengths, finding instead extremely short perturbative production lengths. The analysis enables extraction of flavor-dependent transport coefficients from heavy-ion data, showing a hierarchy (\hat q_0 for b < c < light quarks) that the authors attribute to the same dead-cone effect suppressing broadening for heavier quarks. The work also identifies a discrepancy at low p_T (below 10 GeV) for D mesons where data show a steep rise in R_AA not captured by the pQCD mechanism, suggesting a hydrodynamic component as previously observed for light hadrons by Nemchik, Karpenko, Kopeliovich, Potashnikova, and Sinyukov (2013).
