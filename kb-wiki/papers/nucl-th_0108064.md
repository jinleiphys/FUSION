# ISI and FSI in NN-->NNX reactions

> 34 pages, no figures; invited contribution to the symposium on "Threshold meson production in pp and pd interaction", extended COSY-11 collaboration meeting, Cracow, Poland, June 20-24, 2001; final correction: two anticommutators replaced by commutators at the end of section 3

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Watson-Migdal Approximation (WMA) for treating Initial and Final State Interactions (ISI/FSI) in nucleon-induced meson production near threshold is insufficient for quantitative covariant descriptions, and that offshell effects parametrized by a function \(P(\kappa)\) must be included to restore unitarity, contrary to the common onshell approximation (OWMA) which neglects principal-value contributions.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical formalism of ISI and FSI in \(NN\rightarrow NNX\) reactions, deriving the Watson-Migdal Approximation from a two-potential scattering formalism with a Hamiltonian split into short-range (\(W\)) and long-range (\(U\)) potentials. The T-matrix is factorized into enhancement factors \(T_{FSI}\) and \(T_{ISI}\) and a short-range production amplitude \(T^{(0)}\), with the offshell information contained in principal-value parts of propagators parametrized by \(P(\kappa)\). A Taylor expansion of the total cross section in the dimensionless variable \(\eta_\phi\) (maximum meson momentum in units of meson mass) is performed to analyze the energy dependence near threshold, treating ISI as a constant multiplicative factor and FSI as dominating the energy dependence through the phase-space integral.

## Key numbers

* The excess energy \(Q_{cm} = \sqrt{s} - m_{1'} - m_{2'} - m_\phi\) and the dimensionless quantity \(\eta_\phi = |\vec{k}_\phi|^{max}_{cm}/m_\phi\) both vanish exactly at threshold.
* For \(pp\rightarrow pp\eta\) close to threshold, assuming \(P(\kappa_0)\simeq 0\) and \(\delta^{(0)}\approx -60^\circ\) from VPI phase-shift analysis, \(|T_{ISI}(s^{thr})|\approx 1/2\), giving a reduction of \(\sigma(s)\) by a factor of about 1/4.
* The threshold cross section for \(NN\rightarrow [[NN]_L \phi]_\ell\) scales as \(\sigma \propto \eta_\phi^{2(\ell+L)+4}\) in the nonrelativistic Watson-Migdal framework.
* The threshold kinetic laboratory energy is given by \(T^{thr}_{Lab} = ((m_{1'}+m_{2'}+m_\phi)^2 - (m_1+m_2)^2)/(2m_2)\).

## Context

This invited review extends the Watson-Migdal formalism originally developed by Brueckner, Chew, and Hart (1951), Watson (1951,1952), Migdal (1955), and Fermi (1955), and commonly applied in Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA). It addresses shortcomings identified in recent work by Hanhart and Nakayama (1999), Baru et al. (2000,2001), and Kerbikov (2000) regarding offshell effects and the non-universality of FSI enhancement factors. The paper enables future quantitative covariant treatments of ISI/FSI in reactions like \(pN\rightarrow pNX\) with \(X=\eta,\pi^0\), and calls for systematic construction of enhancement factors that satisfy unitarity constraints.
