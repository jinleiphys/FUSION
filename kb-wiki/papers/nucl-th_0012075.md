# Non-localities and Fermi motion corrections in $K^-$ atoms

> We evaluate the p-wave $K^-N$ amplitudes from the chiral Lagrangians and from there construct the p-wave part of the $K^-$ nucleus optical potential plus a small s-wave part induced from the elementary p-wave amplitude and the nuclear Fermi motion. Simultaneously, the momentum and energy dependence of the s-wave optical potential, previously developed, are taken into account and shown to generate a small p-wave correction to the optical potential. All the corrections considered are small compared to the leading s-wave potential, and lead to changes in the shifts and widths which are smaller than the experimental errors. A thorough study of the threshold region and low densities is conducted, revealing mathematical problems for which a physical solution is given.

## Key claim

The p-wave and nonlocal corrections to the K- nucleus optical potential, derived from chiral Lagrangians and Fermi motion, are all small compared to the leading s-wave potential and produce changes in the calculated kaonic atom shifts and widths that are smaller than the experimental errors.

## Method

The authors evaluated p-wave K- N amplitudes from chiral Lagrangians and constructed the p-wave part of the K- nucleus optical potential, plus a small s-wave part induced from the elementary p-wave amplitude and nuclear Fermi motion. They also accounted for the momentum and energy dependence of the s-wave optical potential previously developed in Ramos (1999), which generates a small p-wave correction. A thorough study of the threshold region and low densities was conducted, introducing a finite excitation energy gap (1-3 MeV) to resolve mathematical problems with the cusp behavior of the free t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The density of symmetric nuclear matter is taken as rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3, with corresponding Fermi momentum k_F0 = 268.4 MeV.
- For k_F = 100 MeV (rho = 0.05 rho_0), 50 MeV (rho = 0.006 rho_0), and 10 MeV (rho = 0.00005 rho_0), the real part of the kaon rescattering selfenergy divided by rho is shown as a function of k^0 - m.
- The gap energy used to regularize the low-density behavior is 1-3 MeV, with changes in the calculated shifts and widths far smaller than experimental uncertainties.
- The low-density limit constraints give b(rho)/rho at rho=0 equal to - (partial t / partial s) at threshold, and c(rho)/rho at rho=0 equal to (partial t / partial s) at threshold times 2(m_K + M_N).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model approach to K- nucleus interactions developed by Oset (1999) and Ramos (1999), which previously focused on s-wave optical potentials. It addresses nonlocal corrections analogous to those studied for pionic atoms by Garcia-Recio (1997), where s-wave elementary amplitudes induce p-wave terms in the optical potential. The results enable more precise calculations of kaonic atom spectra and inform studies of kaon selfenergies in nuclear matter relevant to kaon condensates in neutron stars, as explored by Kaplan (1993) and Pons (1999).
