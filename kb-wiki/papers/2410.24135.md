# A generalized picture of colour decoherence in dense QCD media

> We revisit the calculation of the soft gluon emission probability off a colour-singlet $q\bar q$ system that evolves in a quark-gluon plasma. The $q\bar q$ antenna is created in the presence of a medium and then emits a soft gluon outside. The gluon emission probability is modified with respect to the vacuum baseline due to interactions with the medium during the formation of the antenna and its propagation. Previous studies disregarded the former effect and found that the medium modification to the interference pattern of the antenna was controlled by the so-called critical angle $θ_c$, that exclusively depends on medium properties. We find that accounting for medium interactions during the antenna formation enhances the total rate of emissions off the $q\bar q$ antenna. Interestingly, it also promotes the notion of a critical angle to a dynamic quantity, denoted $\tildeθ_c$, that depends on both the medium and the antenna properties and is thus different for every splitting. As a consequence, depending on the region of parameter space, colour decoherence can either be delayed or accelerated with respect to previous estimates.

## Key claim

Accounting for medium interactions during the formation of the quark-antiquark antenna enhances the total rate of soft gluon emissions and promotes the critical angle from a static quantity depending only on medium properties to a dynamic quantity, denoted \(\tilde\theta_c\), that depends on both the medium and the antenna properties and can therefore either delay or accelerate colour decoherence relative to previous estimates.

## Method

The authors calculate the squared matrix element for \(\gamma^*\to q\bar q g\) in a dense QCD medium, treating the medium as a classical colour field with Gaussian statistics and static scattering centres. They consider a colour-singlet \(q\bar q\) antenna propagating through a brick of quark-gluon plasma of length \(L\), with the soft gluon emitted outside the medium, and include a non-zero light-cone time difference \(\Delta x^+_A\) between the antenna formation in the amplitude and conjugate amplitude. The quark and antiquark propagators are treated eikonally, and the calculation separates contributions where the antenna forms inside the medium, outside the medium, or in mixed configurations.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum formation time of an emission is given by \(t_f = 2/(k_t\theta)\), with \(k_t = \min(p_{t1}, p_{t2})\theta\).
- The gluon formation time is \(t_g = 2 z_g z_q E_\gamma / (\mathbf{k} - z_g\mathbf{p})^2\) and the antenna formation time is \(t_A = 2 z_q (1-z_q) E_\gamma / [(1-z_q)\mathbf{p} - z_q\bar{\mathbf{p}}]^2\).
- For central PbPb collisions at \(p_t = 400\) GeV and jet cone size \(R=0.4\), a \(30\%\) suppression of the jet cross-section is observed (CMS, 2021).
- The vacuum splitting function is \(P_{\gamma\to q\bar q}(z_q) = n_f[z_q^2 + (1-z_q)^2]\).
- The decoherence factor \(\Delta_{\rm med}\) ranges from 0 in vacuum to 1 in completely opaque media.

## Context

This work extends the series of calculations on colour decoherence in QCD media initiated by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2010-2012) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2011-2012), which considered instantaneous antenna formation (\(\Delta x^+_A = 0\)) and found that medium modification of the interference pattern was controlled by a static critical angle \(\theta_c\) depending only on medium properties. The present calculation incorporates the medium modification to the antenna formation cross-section previously studied by Dominguez et al. (2019) and Isaksen et al. (2023), who showed that the medium modification factor \(F_{\rm med}\) deviates from unity for unbalanced splittings at wide angles. This enables a more complete description of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions, where both antenna formation and propagation effects must be accounted for.
