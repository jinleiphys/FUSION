# Delayed clusters accompanying nonmesonic weak decay of the $Λ$-hypernuclei: a clue to nonleptonic processes

> The nonmesonic decay of $Λ$-hypernuclei provides access to the nonleptonic weak decay process $ΛN \to NN$, which is achievable only through the observation of hypernuclear ground-state decays. We continue the discussion of some specific cases which make it possible to detect a few exclusive transitions, namely, the stripping of nucleon from the ground state results in a resonance state decaying via emission of two clusters. Delayed clusters accompanying weak decay of light hypernuclei give a unique information on spin dependence of the weak decay matrix elements.

# Delayed clusters accompanying nonmesonic weak decay of the Λ-hypernuclei: a clue to nonleptonic processes

## Key claim
The observation of delayed clusters (two light nuclei) emitted from resonance states formed after one-nucleon induced nonmesonic weak decay of light p-shell hypernuclei can provide unique information on the spin dependence of the weak decay matrix elements, but this is conditional on the ability to measure exclusive decay channels and on the availability of spectroscopic factors for the relevant nuclear transitions.

## Method
The authors analyze the one-nucleon induced nonmesonic weak decay mechanism in light p-shell hypernuclei, where stripping of a nucleon from the ground state produces a resonance state that subsequently decays via emission of two clusters (e.g., alpha particles, tritons, helions). They identify candidate hypernuclei for such delayed cluster decays using shell model considerations and fractional parentage coefficients, and they derive expressions for the exclusive decay widths of ⁷ΛLi in terms of the four spin-isospin matrix elements R_{NS} of the phenomenological Block-Dalitz approach. The ratios of these widths are shown to be nearly independent of nuclear structure and can discriminate between different models of the weak ΛN interaction (one-meson exchange, two-pion exchange, direct quark models).

## Key numbers
- The nonmesonic weak decay releases up to 176 MeV from the conversion of a Λ-hyperon into a neutron.
- For ⁷ΛLi, the spin-isospin fractional parentage coefficients are κ(3/2) = 4/5 and κ(1/2) = 1/5 in the simplest case where the ⁶Li ground state wave function is |s⁴ p² : ¹³S₁⟩.
- The ratio Γ_n/Γ_p for ⁴ΛH ranges from 0.693 (DQ+ model) to 27.9 (PKE model), and for ⁴ΛHe from 0.031 (PKE) to 0.417 (ME model).
- For ⁷ΛLi, the calculated ratio κR₁ ranges from 1.360 (PKE) to 8.719 (π+ρ model), κR₂ from 1.063 (PKE) to 5.500 (DQ+), and R₃ from 0.075 (π only) to 0.500 (DQ+).

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis by Block and Dalitz (1963) who first expressed the total nonmesonic width of s-shell hypernuclei as a sum of four spin-isospin rates, and continues the discussion by Majling and Batusov (2000) on alpha-particle emission from ¹⁰ΛB and ¹⁰ΛBe. The proposed method enables the determination of all four weak matrix elements R_{NS} from exclusive cluster decay measurements, which is not possible from total widths alone due to the lack of neutron transition data. The results are expected to be tested with future experiments at the Nuclotron facility.
