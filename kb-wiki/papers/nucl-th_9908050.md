# Gravitational coupling to two-particle bound states and momentum conservation in deep inelastic scattering

> The momentum conservation sum rule for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from composite particles is investigated using the general theory of relativity. For two 1+1 dimensional examples, it shown that covariant theories automatically satisy the DIS momentum conservation sum rule provided the bound state is covariantilly normalized. Therefore, in these cases the two DIS sum rules for baryon conservation and momentum conservation are equivalent.

## Key claim
The paper shows that, for two 1+1 dimensional models, covariant theories automatically satisfy the DIS momentum conservation sum rule provided the bound state is covariantly normalized, making the two DIS sum rules for baryon conservation and momentum conservation equivalent rather than independent constraints.

## Method
The authors use general relativity to study gravitational coupling to two-particle bound states in 1+1 dimensions, deriving Feynman rules for graviton-scalar interactions and gravitational Ward-Takahashi identities. They analyze two specific models: bound states of two scalar particles and bound states of a spin-1/2 fermion with a scalar particle, using the Bethe-Salpeter ladder approximation to construct bound state vertices and verify gauge invariance of the gravitational coupling.

## Key numbers
- The paper works in 1+1 dimensional space-time, with the metric signature diag[1,-1] in the flat space limit
- The gravitational coupling constant is defined as κ = 8πG/c⁴ in four dimensions
- The effective four-point coupling constant is g = λ²/m², where λ is the Yukawa coupling and m is the mass of the exchanged φ particle
- The bound state normalization condition is shown to be sufficient to ensure the gravitational field couples to the total bound state mass

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Batiz and Gross, 1999) showing that the baryon conservation sum rule follows from the covariant bound state normalization condition. It addresses a puzzle in covariant descriptions of DIS: whether the momentum conservation sum rule provides an independent constraint or is also a consequence of normalization. The paper uses gravitational Ward-Takahashi identities following approaches by West (year not given) and techniques from Savkli (year not given) and Peskin (year not given), and demonstrates that the gravitational coupling to composite systems has the same structure as coupling to elementary particles, with mass replaced by total bound state mass.
