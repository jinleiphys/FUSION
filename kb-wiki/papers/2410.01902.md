# Revisiting Single Inclusive Jet Production: Small-$R$ Resummation at Next-to-Leading Logarithm

> The precision description of jet production plays an important role in many aspects of collider physics. In a recent paper we have presented a new factorization theorem for inclusive small radius jet production. The jet function appearing in our factorization theorem exhibits a non-standard renormalization group evolution, which, starting at next-to-leading logarithm (NLL), differs from previous results in the literature. In this paper we perform a first phenomenological study using our newly developed formalism, applying it to compute the spectrum of small radius jets in $e^+e^-\to J+X$ at NLL. We compare our results with previous predictions, highlighting the numerical impact of previously neglected terms throughout phase space. Our approach can be used for a variety of different collider systems, in particular, $ep$ and $pp$ collisions, with broad applications to the jet substructure program. Most importantly, since our factorization theorem is valid to all orders, the approach developed here will enable NNLL resummation of small radius logarithms in inclusive jet production, extending the precision of jet substructure calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first phenomenological application of a new factorization theorem for inclusive small-radius jet production, showing that the jet function's renormalization group evolution at next-to-leading logarithm (NLL) differs from the standard DGLAP evolution assumed in previous work by Kang et al. (2016), and that this difference produces moderate but non-negligible numerical corrections that grow at lower jet energies and smaller jet radii.

## Method

The authors solve the non-standard renormalization group equation for the jet function iteratively to high loop orders (up to 5 loops) in momentum-fraction space, using the known timelike DGLAP splitting kernels. They then convolve the evolved jet function with the standard inclusive hard function for electron-positron annihilation to compute the differential cross section for e+e- → J + X at NLL accuracy, comparing results obtained with the new evolution equation against those from the standard DGLAP convolution structure.

## Key numbers

* For R = 0.2 at LEP energy √s = 91.2 GeV, the ratio between 4-loop and 5-loop iterative solutions shows subpercent deviation for most of the jet energy fraction, with only ~1% difference in the threshold or small-z_J region.
* For R = 0.4 at the same energy, the convergence is faster than for R = 0.2, as expected from the smaller size of the small-R logarithms.
* The difference between the new factorization and the standard DGLAP convolution approach grows at smaller jet energy fractions and becomes more pronounced for R = 0.2 compared to R = 0.4.
* The distribution is normalized to the partonic total cross-section σ_tot = 1 + 3 a_s C_F + O(a_s^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own recent factorization theorem (Lee, Moult, Zhang 2024) for inclusive small-radius jet production, which corrected the earlier factorization approach of Kang et al. (2016) that neglected certain terms arising at NLL. The same issue was also identified by van Beekveld et al. (2024). The new formalism enables NNLL resummation of small-radius logarithms in inclusive jet production, with applications to ep and pp collisions and the broader jet substructure program. The evolution equation is identical to that for the N-point projected energy correlators studied by Dixon et al. (2019) and Chen et al. (2020).
