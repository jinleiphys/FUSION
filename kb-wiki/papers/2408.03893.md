# Investigation of pion-nucleon contributions to nucleon matrix elements

> We investigate contributions of excited states to nucleon matrix elements computed in lattice QCD by employing, in addition to the standard nucleon interpolating operator, pion-nucleon ($π$-$N$) operators. We solve a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) to obtain an optimal interpolating operator that minimizes overlap with the $π$-$N$ states. We derive a variant of the standard application of the GEVP method, which allows for constructing 3-point correlation functions using the optimized interpolating operator without requiring the computationally demanding combination that includes $π$-$N$ operators in both sink and source. We extract nucleon matrix elements using two twisted mass fermion ensembles, one ensemble generated using pion mass of 346 MeV and one ensemble tuned to reproduce the physical value of the pion mass. Especially, we determine the isoscalar and isovector scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial, and tensor matrix elements. We include results obtained using a range of kinematic setups, including momentum in the sink. Our results using this variational approach are compared with previous results obtained using the same ensembles and multi-state fits without GEVP improvement. We find that for the physical mass point ensemble, the improvement, in terms of suppression of excited states using this method, is most significant for the case of the matrix elements of the isovector axial and pseudoscalar currents.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a variational approach using a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) with both standard nucleon and pion-nucleon interpolating operators can suppress excited-state contamination in nucleon matrix elements, but the improvement is most significant for the isovector axial and pseudoscalar currents at the physical pion mass, while other channels show more modest effects.

## Method
The authors solve a GEVP for the 2-point correlation matrix built from standard nucleon and pion-nucleon interpolating operators in two frames (center-of-mass and first moving frame) to obtain an optimized nucleon interpolating operator. They derive a variant of the standard GEVP method that constructs 3-point correlation functions using the optimized operator without requiring the computationally expensive combination with pion-nucleon operators at both sink and source, computing only the 3-point functions with the standard nucleon operator at sink or source combined with the pion-nucleon operator at the other end. Nucleon matrix elements for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial, and tensor currents (both isoscalar and isovector) are extracted using two twisted mass fermion ensembles.

## Key numbers
- Ensemble cA211.53.24: pion mass 346 MeV, lattice spacing 0.0908 fm, spatial extent 2.27 fm, nucleon mass 1193(18) MeV
- Ensemble cA2.09.48: pion mass 131 MeV, lattice spacing 0.0938 fm, spatial extent 4.50 fm, nucleon mass 931(3) MeV
- 2467 configurations used for ensemble cA211.53.24; 1228 configurations for ensemble cA2.09.48
- Gaussian smearing parameters: alpha_G = 4.0, N_G = 50, giving approximately 0.5 fm smearing radius for the nucleon on the physical point ensemble
- APE smearing parameters: n_APE = 50, alpha_APE = 0.5

## Context
This work extends the variational approach first studied by Barca et al. (2022) using an ensemble with pion mass 429 MeV, now applying it to a physical pion mass ensemble and including more operators. The approach addresses excited-state contamination that chiral perturbation theory suggests is amplified in axial (Bar, 2018), pseudoscalar (Bar, 2019), and electromagnetic (Bar, 2021) form factors at physical pion mass. The results are compared with previous multi-state fits without GEVP improvement on the same ensembles, and the method enables future calculations that may further reduce excited-state contamination by including the fully GEVP-improved 3-point functions with pion-nucleon operators at both source and sink.
