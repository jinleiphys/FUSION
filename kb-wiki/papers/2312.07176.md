# Evidence of isospin-symmetry violation in high-energy collisions of atomic nuclei: Theoretical and Phenomenological considerations

> Recently, the NA61/SHINE collaboration at the CERN SPS reported evidence of isospin-symmetry violation in high-energy nuclear collisions [Nature Commun. 16, 2849 (2025)]. The effect was observed in the relative yields of charged and neutral kaons and cannot be explained by known sources of isospin symmetry breaking. In this work, we extend the theoretical and phenomenological aspects of that study. We discuss the historical background and introduce the concepts of isospin transformations and symmetry. Importantly, we relate isospin symmetry to the QCD flavor symmetry, and we present both conceptual and analytical proofs demonstrating the equality of the mean multiplicities of charged and neutral kaons for an initial ensemble of colliding systems that is invariant under charge-symmetry transformation.

## Key claim

The paper provides theoretical and phenomenological proofs that the equality of mean multiplicities of charged and neutral kaons holds for an initial ensemble of colliding systems that is invariant under charge-symmetry transformation, but this equality can be violated by known isospin-breaking effects (quark mass differences and electromagnetic interactions) at the level of several percent at collision energies around 10-20 GeV.

## Method

The authors present conceptual and analytical proofs using the charge-symmetry operator \(\hat{C}=e^{i\pi \hat{I}_{y}}\) and the statistical operator formalism, demonstrating that a charge-uniform initial ensemble (such as nucleus-nucleus collisions with equal numbers of protons and neutrons) evolves under charge-symmetric strong interactions into a charge-uniform final ensemble, forcing equality of mean multiplicities for charge-symmetry partner kaons. They then estimate the magnitude of charge-symmetry violation within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, using the measured kaon mass difference and a temperature parameter of \(T = 150\) MeV to compute the expected ratio of charged to neutral kaon yields.

## Key numbers

- The up quark mass is \(m_u = 2.16 \pm 0.07\) MeV and the down quark mass is \(m_d = 4.70 \pm 0.07\) MeV (Particle Data Group 2024).
- The strange quark mass is \(m_s = 93.5 \pm 0.8\) MeV (Particle Data Group 2024).
- The ratio of kaon masses from QCD is \(m_{K^+}/m_{K^0} = \sqrt{(m_u+m_s)/(m_d+m_s)} = 0.9870 \pm 0.0005\), compared to the experimental value \(0.99209 \pm 0.00004\).
- The estimated ratio of mean multiplicities from the mass difference alone is \(\langle K^+ \rangle / \langle K^0 \rangle \approx e^{-(m_{K^+} - m_{K^0})/T} \approx 1.03\) for a temperature parameter \(T = 150\) MeV.
- The detailed estimate of charge-symmetry violation in kaon production is about several percent at center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 10-20\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical and phenomenological aspects of the experimental discovery by the NA61/SHINE collaboration (Nature Communications 16, 2849, 2025), which reported evidence of isospin-symmetry violation in high-energy nuclear collisions that cannot be explained by known sources. The paper builds on the Shmushkevich method (Shmushkevich, 1955, 1956) for relating cross-sections from isospin invariance, and discusses the connection between isospin symmetry and QCD flavor symmetry, including the role of the chiral anomaly in protecting isospin symmetry for pions as noted by Gross (1979) and Pisarski (1983). The results enable future tests of charge-symmetry invariance in collisions of oxygen-oxygen and pion-carbon systems recently initiated by NA61/SHINE.
