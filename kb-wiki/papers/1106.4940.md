# New flow observables

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the initial transverse density profile result in a collective flow pattern which also fluctuates event by event. We propose a number of new correlation observables to characterize these fluctuations and discuss how they should be analyzed experimentally. We argue that most of these quantities can be measured at RHIC and LHC.

# New flow observables

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations of the initial transverse density profile in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions produce a collective flow pattern that also fluctuates event by event; the paper proposes new correlation observables to characterize these fluctuations, arguing that most can be measured at RHIC and LHC, but the actual feasibility depends on achieving sufficient event statistics and controlling nonflow effects through rapidity gaps and appropriate particle weights.

## Method

The authors define k-particle azimuthal correlations of the form v{n1, n2, ..., nk} = ⟨cos(n1φ1 + ... + nkφk)⟩, where only combinations with n1 + ... + nk = 0 are measurable due to uncontrolled impact parameter orientation. They express these correlations in terms of flow harmonics v_n and their reference angles Ψ_n, showing that they measure moments of the event-by-event flow distribution. They then estimate statistical errors for each observable using the resolution parameter χ_n = v_n{2}√M and compute the number of events needed for a 5% measurement, accounting for nonflow effects from global momentum conservation and collinear particle pairs.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the measured ⟨v₃⁴⟩/⟨v₃²⟩² in 0%-20% central collisions is close to 1.9, while the Monte-Carlo Glauber model prediction is near 2 (corresponding to Gaussian fluctuations)
- Assumed flow harmonic values for central collisions: v₁{2}=0.74%, v₂{2}=2.40%, v₃{2}=1.90%
- Assumed multiplicities: M=2000 for ALICE, M=6000 for CMS/ATLAS, M=900 for STAR/PHENIX
- Number of events needed for 5% measurement of v{2,-2} in 0%-5% centrality: 3×10² (ALICE), 2×10² (ATLAS or CMS), 9×10² (STAR or PHENIX)
- Number of events needed for 5% measurement of v{1,1,1,-3}/(v₁³v₃): 3×10⁶ (ALICE), 1×10⁵ (ATLAS or CMS), 5×10⁷ (STAR or PHENIX)
- Number of events needed for 5% measurement of v{1,1,-1,-1}/v₁⁴: 2×10⁷ (ALICE), 4×10⁵ (ATLAS or CMS), 3×10⁸ (STAR or PHENIX)

## Context

This work extends the standard flow analysis framework based on Fourier harmonics (Voloshin 1994) and cumulant methods (Borghini 2001) by introducing mixed-harmonic correlations that probe angular correlations between the event-plane angles Ψ₁, Ψ₂, and Ψ₃. It builds on earlier studies of initial-state fluctuations (Dumitru 2008) and Monte-Carlo models (Alver 2008, Drescher 2007, Albacete 2010) to provide predictions that can be compared with ALICE data at 2.76 TeV. The proposed observables enable experimental tests of different initial-state models (Monte-Carlo Glauber versus Monte-Carlo KLN) and will constrain the dynamics of thermalization and collective flow in heavy-ion collisions.
