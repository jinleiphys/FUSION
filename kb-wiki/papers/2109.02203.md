# Constituent quark axial current couplings to light vector mesons in the vacuum and with a weak magnetic field

> Unusual constituent quark axial current couplings to light vector mesons, $ρ$ and $ω$, are derived in the vacuum and under weak magnetic field by considering a quark-antiquark interaction mediated by a non perturbative gluon exchange. Similarly, light axial mesons are found to couple anomalously with the constituent quark vector current. These interactions are of the type of the Wess-Zumino-Witten terms, being strongly anisotropic and dependent on the vector (or axial) meson polarization. They also provide axial (vector) form factors for the vector (axial) mesons and are quite small, suppressed nearly by $1/{M^*}^2$ with respect to the vector mesons minimal coupling to the quark vector current. Some three leg meson vertices are also presented: $π-ρ-A_1$ and $V_1V_2 A$ (where $V_1,V_2$ are vector mesons and $A$ an axial meson). A vector and axial-vector mesons mixing is identified at non zero magnetic field which however can contribute only in the presence of a third particle or in a medium. Numerical results are presented for different effective gluon propagators.

## Key claim

The paper derives anomalous constituent quark axial current couplings to light vector mesons (ρ and ω) that are strongly suppressed, by approximately 1/M*² relative to the vector meson minimal coupling to the quark vector current, and are of the Wess-Zumino-Witten type, being strongly anisotropic and dependent on the vector meson polarization; these couplings are presented as small corrections to the vector meson form factors.

## Method

The author starts from a QCD-based global color model (GCM) with a non-perturbative gluon exchange, performs a Fierz transformation to introduce meson auxiliary fields, and then expands the quark determinant in a large constituent quark mass expansion with a zero-order derivative expansion. The resulting effective action yields anomalous couplings of vector mesons (ρ, ω) to the constituent quark axial current and of axial mesons (A₁, f₁) to the quark vector current, as well as three-leg meson vertices (π-ρ-A₁ and V₁V₂A), with numerical results computed for three different effective gluon propagators in the low momentum regime Q² < 1 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The anomalous coupling form factor F_{vja}(K,Q) is suppressed by approximately 1/M*² relative to the vector meson minimal coupling to the quark vector current (no explicit numerical value for the suppression factor is given beyond the functional form).
- The ratio of the anomalous coupling form factor to the pion axial coupling is given by F_{vja}(K,Q)/G_A(K,Q) = 1/(4 M* F), where M* is the constituent quark mass and F is the pion decay constant (no numerical values for M* or F are provided in the text).
- Numerical results are presented for Q² < 1 GeV² for three different effective gluon propagators, with on-shell values for some coupling constants and estimations for the axial averaged quadratic radius (a.q.r.) of vector mesons (no explicit numerical values for these on-shell constants or radii are given in the truncated text).
- The coupling function G_{v-a-π}(0,0) is estimated as G_{v-a-π}(0,0) ~ (g_f^(0) M*)/2 + (1/e) g_{Fρω}, where g_f^(0) is a renormalization parameter and g_{Fρω} is the coupling constant of the neutral rho meson coupling to a photon and the omega meson (no numerical values provided).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies using the same analytical framework (Braghin, PRD 2018a, PRD 2019, PRD 2018b, JPG 2020a, JPG 2020b) to derive anomalous couplings of vector mesons to the axial current, which are of the Wess-Zumino-Witten type and have been obtained in other approaches such as bosonized NJL models (Gomm et al., 1986; Ebert and Reinhardt, 1986) and sigma models. The derived ρ-A₁ mixing under weak magnetic fields can only contribute in the presence of a third particle or in a medium, and the three-leg couplings (π-ρ-A₁ and V₁V₂A) are relevant for understanding meson interactions in experiments at facilities like FAIR/GSI, NICA, J-PARC, and for the μ (g-2) problem via VVA correlators.
