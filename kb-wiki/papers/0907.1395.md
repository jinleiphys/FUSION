# Predictions for the strangeness S=-3 and -4 baryon-baryon interactions in chiral effective field theory

> The leading order strangeness S=-3 and -4 baryon-baryon interactions are analyzed within chiral effective field theory. The chiral potentials consist of contact terms without derivatives and of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. Assuming SU(3) flavor symmetry those contact terms and the couplings of the pseudoscalar mesons to the baryons are related to the corresponding quantities of the S=-1 hyperon-nucleon channels. Specifically, the values of the pertinent five low-energy constants related to the contact terms are already fixed from our preceeding study of the Lambda-N and Sigma-N systems and thus genuine predictions for the Xi-Lambda, Xi-Sigma, and Xi-Xi interactions can be made. Strong attraction is found in some of the S=-3 and -4 channels, suggesting the possible existence of bound states.

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral effective field theory potentials for strangeness S=-3 and -4 baryon-baryon interactions, constructed under the assumption of SU(3) flavor symmetry and with low-energy constants already fixed from fits to Lambda-N and Sigma-N data, predict strong attraction in some channels that may indicate bound states, but the authors caution that the cut-off dependence in the S=-3 singlet channel is so strong that a virtual bound state transforms into a real bound state within the considered cut-off range, limiting predictive power at leading order.

## Method

The authors constructed leading-order chiral potentials consisting of four-baryon contact terms without derivatives and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges (pions, kaons, and eta mesons) for the Xi-Lambda, Xi-Sigma, and Xi-Xi systems. They assumed SU(3) flavor symmetry to relate the five contact-term low-energy constants and the meson-baryon coupling constants to those previously determined from fits to Lambda-N and Sigma-N scattering data. The coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved in momentum space with an exponential regulator function for cut-off values between 550 and 700 MeV, and cross sections and scattering lengths were computed for S-wave partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Xi-Lambda singlet scattering length ranges from -33.5 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 9.07 fm (Lambda=700 MeV), changing sign within the cut-off range and indicating a transition from virtual to real bound state
- Xi-Lambda singlet binding energy for cut-off Lambda=700 MeV is -0.43 MeV
- Xi-Sigma (I=3/2) singlet scattering length ranges from 4.28 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 2.74 fm (Lambda=700 MeV), with binding energies from -2.23 MeV (Lambda=550 MeV) to -6.15 MeV (Lambda=700 MeV)
- Xi-Xi singlet scattering length ranges from 3.92 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 2.47 fm (Lambda=700 MeV), with binding energies from -2.56 MeV (Lambda=550 MeV) to -7.28 MeV (Lambda=700 MeV)
- Xi-Lambda cross section at p_lab=150 MeV/c is 254-269 mb across cut-offs, compared to 38.5 mb (NSC97a) and 29.0 mb (fss2 quark model)
- Xi-Sigma (I=3/2) cross section at p_lab=150 MeV/c is 430-492 mb across cut-offs

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Epelbaum, 2005) and hyperon-nucleon interactions (Polinder, 2006) to the strangeness S=-3 and -4 sectors. It contrasts with the Nijmegen meson-exchange potential (Stoks, 1999), which found bound states in Xi-Sigma and Xi-Xi channels using SU(3) symmetry with explicit breaking, and with the constituent quark model of Fujiwara (2006), which found decreasing attraction from S=0 to S=-4 and no di-baryon bound states except the deuteron. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from J-PARC and FAIR facilities, and provide a framework for analyzing multi-strange baryon systems studied in lattice QCD (Beane, 2009).
