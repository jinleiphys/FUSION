# Dynamical coupled-channels analysis of 1H(e,e'pi)N reactions

> We have performed a dynamical coupled-channels analysis of available p(e,e'pi)N data in the region of W < 1.6 GeV and Q^2 < 1.45 (GeV/c)^2. The channels included are gamma^* N, pi N, eta N, and pi pi N which has pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N components. With the hadronic parameters of the model determined in our previous investigations of pi N --> pi N, pi pi N reactions, we have found that the available data in the considered W < 1.6 GeV region can be fitted well by only adjusting the bare gamma^* N --> N^* helicity amplitudes for the lowest N^* states in P33, P11, S11 and D13 partial waves. The sensitivity of the resulting parameters to the amount of data included in the analysis is investigated. The importance of coupled-channels effect on the p(e,e' pi)N cross sections is demonstrated. The meson cloud effects, as required by the unitarity conditions, on the gamma^* N --> N^* form factors are also examined. Necessary future developments, both experimentally and theoretically, are discussed.

## Key claim

The available $p(e,e'\pi)N$ data up to $W < 1.6$ GeV and $Q^2 < 1.45$ (GeV/c)$^2$ can be fitted well by adjusting only the bare $\gamma^* N \rightarrow N^*$ helicity amplitudes for the lowest $N^*$ states in $P_{33}$, $P_{11}$, $S_{11}$, and $D_{13}$ partial waves, but the extracted parameters show significant sensitivity to the amount of data included, indicating that the current dataset is insufficient to uniquely determine the transition form factors.

## Method

The authors employed a dynamical coupled-channels model including $\gamma^* N$, $\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $\pi\pi N$ (with $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, and $\sigma N$ components) channels. Hadronic parameters were fixed from previous analyses of $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ and $\pi\pi N$ reactions, and only the bare $\gamma^* N \rightarrow N^*$ helicity amplitudes for four $N^*$ states ($S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, $P_{33}$, $D_{13}$) were varied to fit structure function data ($\sigma_T+\epsilon\sigma_L$, $\sigma_{LT}$, $\sigma_{TT}$, $\sigma_{LT'}$) from $p(e,e'\pi^0)p$ and $p(e,e'\pi^+)n$ reactions at fixed $Q^2$ values.

## Key numbers

- Data analyzed cover $W \leq 1.6$ GeV and $Q^2 \leq 1.45$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- At $Q^2 = 0.4$ (GeV/c)$^2$, the bare helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for $S_{11}$ varies from $100.80 \pm 1.46$ (Fit1) to $83.25 \pm 1.21$ (Fit2) to $48.29 \pm 5.46$ (Fit3) depending on which data subsets are included
- At $Q^2 = 0.4$ (GeV/c)$^2$, the bare $P_{33}$ $A_{3/2}$ amplitude ranges from $-146.00 \pm 0.60$ (Fit1) to $-137.50 \pm 0.56$ (Fit2) to $-150.80 \pm 1.03$ (Fit3)
- The coupled-channels effect on $\sigma_T$ at high $W \sim 1.5$ GeV is small (10-20%) already at $Q^2 = 0.4$ (GeV/c)$^2$, compared to about 30-40% at $Q^2 = 0$
- Data are available at $Q^2$ values of 0.3, 0.4, 0.5, 0.525, 0.6, 0.65, 0.75, 0.9, 1.15, and 1.45 (GeV/c)$^2$

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels analysis previously performed by the same group for pion photoproduction (Julia-Diaz et al., 2008) to electroproduction reactions, using the same hadronic model developed in earlier studies of $\pi N$ scattering (Matsuyama et al., 2007; Julia-Diaz et al., 2007; Kamijo et al., 2009). The analysis demonstrates that current data covering only 4 out of 11 independent $p(e,e'\pi)N$ observables are insufficient to uniquely determine the $\gamma^* N \rightarrow N^*$ transition form factors, motivating the need for forthcoming double and triple polarization measurements at Jefferson Lab.
