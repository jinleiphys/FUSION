# Occupation numbers from functional integral

> Occupation numbers for non-relativistic interacting particles are discussed within a functional integral formulation. We concentrate on zero temperature, where the Bogoliubov theory breaks down for strong couplings as well as for low dimensional models. We find that the leading behavior of the occupation numbers for small momentum is governed by a quadratic time derivative in the inverse propagator that is not contained in the Bogoliubov theory. We propose to use a functional renormalization group equation for the occupation numbers in order to implement systematic non-perturbative extensions beyond the Bogoliubov theory.

## Key claim

The leading behavior of occupation numbers for small momentum is governed by a quadratic time derivative in the inverse propagator that is not contained in the Bogoliubov theory, and this resolves the infrared problems of the Bogoliubov theory for low dimensions and strong couplings.

## Method

The paper computes occupation numbers for non-relativistic interacting bosons at zero temperature using a functional integral formulation. It analyzes the frequency and momentum dependence of the full propagator, including terms quadratic in frequency that are absent in the Bogoliubov approximation, and proposes a functional renormalization group equation for the occupation numbers to implement systematic non-perturbative extensions.

## Key numbers

- For the Bogoliubov theory in the small momentum limit, the occupation number diverges as \(n_p(\vec q) = \frac{\sqrt{M\lambda_\phi}\bar\phi_0}{2q}\) (equation 37A).
- For the general dynamic term in one dimension, the occupation number for \(q\to 0\) behaves as \(n(\vec q) = \left(\frac{M}{8\bar A\bar V}\right)^{1/2} q^{-1}\) (equation N8).
- For the general dynamic term, the anomalous dimension \(\eta\) vanishes for \(d=2,3\), while it is in the range \(0<\eta\leq 1/2\) for \(d=1\).
- For \(d=3\), the occupation number scales as \(n(\vec q)\sim \frac{\ln^{\frac12}(k^2_0/q^2)}{q}\) in the far infrared (equation N2).

## Context

This work extends the Bogoliubov theory (Bogoliubov) for non-relativistic bosons, which breaks down for strong couplings and low dimensions, and is incompatible with the Mermin-Wagner theorem (Mermin and Wagner) for \(d=1,2\) at nonzero temperature. The paper builds on a functional renormalization group study of quantum phase transitions (Wetterich 2007) and proposes a renormalization group treatment based on the average action (Wetterich 1993, Wetterich 1991, Berges, Tetradis, and Wetterich) to compute occupation numbers non-perturbatively, enabling systematic improvements for coupled boson-fermion systems relevant to the BEC-BCS crossover.
