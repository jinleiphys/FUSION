# Multiphonon and ``hot''-phonon Isovector Electric-Dipole Excitations

> We argue that a substantial increase in the cross section for Coulomb excitation in the region of the Double Giant Dipole Resonance should be expected from Coulomb excitation of excited states involved in the spreading of the one-phonon resonance, in a manifestation of the Brink-Axel phenomenon. This generates an additional fluctuating amplitude and a corresponding new term to be added incoherently to the usual cross-section. The appropriate extension of an applicable reaction calculation is considered in order to estimate this effect.

# Multiphonon and "hot"-phonon Isovector Electric-Dipole Excitations

## Key claim

The authors argue that a substantial increase in the Coulomb excitation cross section in the region of the Double Giant Dipole Resonance (DGDR) should arise from the Coulomb excitation of background states involved in the spreading of the one-phonon resonance (the Brink-Axel phenomenon), generating an additional fluctuating amplitude that must be added incoherently to the usual cross section. This enhancement is expected to be significant only when the collision time is comparable to or longer than the inverse GDR width, and should diminish at higher bombarding energies.

## Method

The authors develop a statistical model of the target nucleus that couples a collective degree of freedom (the GDR phonon) to a set of complex intrinsic states, using a random-matrix description for the coupling matrix elements. They derive the average (optical) Green's function for the system and calculate the cross sections for populating the first and second collective states through sequential Coulomb excitation, then separately compute the fluctuating contribution from the process where a second collective excitation occurs on top of the "hot" intrinsic background remaining after the first phonon has decayed. The fluctuating cross section is estimated using three approximations: replacing intrinsic excitation energies and widths by their average values, approximating the sum over intrinsic states as a Lorentzian integral, and arguing that the momentum integrals are reduced by a factor of the ratio of widths.

## Key numbers

- The usual DGDR cross section peaks at approximately 2E_GDR with a width of approximately 2Γ_GDR, while the fluctuating Brink-Axel component has a width of approximately Γ_GDR.
- The enhancement factor for the peak cross section is roughly (1 + Γ₁↓/Γ₁), and for the integrated cross section is roughly (1 + ½ Γ₁↓/Γ₁) σ_DGDR.
- For heavy nuclei, Γ₁↓ ≈ Γ₁, giving enhancement factors of approximately 2 for the peak and approximately 3/2 for the integrated cross section.
- Using a phenomenological expression for the compound escape width, Γ_ν ≈ 14 exp(-4.69√(A/E*)) MeV, and taking Sn as an example, the total width saturates at high excitation at Γ_1ν ≈ 4.5 MeV + 14 MeV, which compares with the observed value of 15 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the DGDR excitation cross section at a laboratory energy of about 100A MeV is a factor of two larger than the predicted one, while at 640A MeV it is only about 30% greater than calculated.

## Context

This work addresses the persistent discrepancy between coupled-channel Coulomb excitation calculations and experimental data for the DGDR in ¹³⁶Xe, ¹⁹⁷Au, and ²⁰⁸Pb, where harmonic-approximation calculations underestimate measured cross sections by factors of 2 to 3. Previous explanations considered anharmonicities (Volpe 1995; Bortignon and Dasso 1997) and quenching of the 1⁺ DGDR state (Bertulani 1996). The present mechanism extends the statistical treatment of Ko (1978) for deeply-inelastic heavy-ion collisions to the Coulomb excitation of giant resonances, and enables a semiclassical extension of the model of Canto (1994) to include the Brink-Axel effect. The work also connects to the observed increase of DGDR width with incident energy in ²⁰⁸Pb data (Beene 1993; Boretzky 1996).
