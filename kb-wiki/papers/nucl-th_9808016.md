# Hadronic properties of the S_{11}(1535) studied by electroproduction off the deuteron

> Properties of excited baryonic states are investigated in the context of electroproduction of baryon resonances off the deuteron. In particular, the hadronic radii and the compositeness of baryon resonances are studied for kinematic situations in which their hadronic reinteraction is the dominant contribution. Specifically, we study the reaction $d(e,e'S_{11})N$ at $Q^2\ge 1 GeV^2$ for kinematics in which the produced hadronic state reinteracts predominantly with the spectator nucleon. A comparison of constituent quark model and effective chiral Lagrangian calculations of the $S_{11}$ shows substantial sensitivity to the structure of the produced resonance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that electroproduction of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance off the deuteron, in kinematics where the produced hadronic state rescatters off the spectator nucleon, can distinguish between a constituent quark model description (where the resonance is a larger excited baryonic state) and an effective chiral Lagrangian description (where the resonance is a superposition of meson-baryon channels), a distinction that is not possible from single-nucleon reactions alone.

## Method

The authors calculate the $d(e,e'S_{11})n$ reaction at $Q^2\ge 1~GeV^2$ using the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to treat the rescattering of the produced hadronic system off the spectator nucleon. They incorporate two different models for the $S_{11}(1535)$: a constituent quark model (CQM) where the resonance is an $L=1$ radial excitation with a larger radius than the nucleon, and an effective chiral Lagrangian (ECL) approach where the resonance emerges from a coupled-channel system of $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, and $K\Sigma$ states. The differential cross section is expressed in terms of invariant structure functions $\sigma_T$, $\sigma_L$, $\sigma_{TT}$, and $\sigma_{TL}$, and the ratio $R$ of the full cross section (including rescattering) to the impulse approximation cross section is computed as a function of spectator nucleon kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at $Q^2 = 1~GeV^2$ and $W = 1.54~GeV$ for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance.
- The spectator nucleon transverse momentum is restricted to $p_{st} \lesssim 0.4~GeV/c$ to ensure the dominant contribution arises from the rescattering diagram.
- Within the CQM, the mean-square radius of the $S_{11}$ is $<r_{s11}^2> = 4b_{hosc}^2$, compared to $<r_{nucl}^2> = 3b_{hosc}^2$ for the nucleon, leading to predicted total cross section and slope parameters $(\sigma^{tot}_{hN},b) = ({4\over 3}\sigma^{tot}_{NN},{7\over 6} b_{NN})$.
- At $p_s = 400~MeV/c$, the CQM calculation with the larger $S_{11}$ radius predicts more than $50\%$ greater rescattering compared to the case where the $S_{11}$ radius equals the nucleon radius.
- At $p_s = 200~MeV/c$, the CQM calculation predicts $15\%$ more screening for the larger $S_{11}$.
- The ratio $R_{\sigma}(p_{s1},p_{s2})$ comparing cross sections at $p_s \approx 400~MeV/c$ and $p_s \approx 200~MeV/c$ differs by a factor of 2 between the CQM predictions for the larger-radius $S_{11}$ versus the nucleon-radius $S_{11}$.
- The screening effects dominate for $p_{st} < 300~MeV/c$ (giving $R<1$), while double scattering dominates for $p_{st} \ge 300~MeV/c$ (giving $R>1$).

## Context

This work extends the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) method previously applied to quasielastic $d(e,e'N)N$ reactions by Frankfurt, M. Johnson, Sargsian, and Strikman (1997) to the case of resonance electroproduction. It contrasts two competing descriptions of the $S_{11}(1535)$: the constituent quark model classification as a $[70,1^-]_1$ supermultiplet state (see Burkert (1995) and Stoler (1993) for reviews), and the effective chiral Lagrangian approach developed by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1997) where the resonance arises from coupled-channel dynamics of pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons with octet baryons. The proposed measurement on the deuteron could enable determination of the hadronic radius and compositeness of baryon resonances, properties that are not accessible from free-nucleon experiments.
