# Hydrodynamic scaling from the dynamics of relativistic quantum field theory

> Hydrodynamic behavior is a general feature of interacting systems with many degrees of freedom constrained by conservation laws. To date hydrodynamic scaling in relativistic quantum systems has been observed in many high energy settings, from cosmic ray detections to accelerators, with large particle multiplicity final states. Here we show first evidence for the emergence of hydrodynamic scaling in the dynamics of a relativistic quantum field theory. We consider a simple scalar $λϕ^4$ model in 1+1 dimensions in the Hartree approximation and study the dynamics of two colliding kinks at relativistic speeds as well as the decay of a localized high energy density region. The evolution of the energy-momentum tensor determines the dynamical local equation of state and allows the measurement of the speed of sound. Hydrodynamic scaling emerges at high local energy densities.

## Key claim
This paper provides the first evidence that hydrodynamic scaling (energy density isosurfaces lying on hyperboloids of constant \(\tau^2 = t^2 - x^2\)) emerges from the dynamics of a relativistic quantum field theory, specifically a scalar \(\lambda \phi^4\) model in 1+1 dimensions, but only at sufficiently high local energy densities and within the Hartree approximation.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled equations of motion for the mean field \(\varphi\) and the mode functions \(\psi_k\) of a scalar \(\lambda \phi^4\) quantum field theory in the Hartree approximation, using a spatial lattice with \(N=1024\) points and spacing \(dx=0.125\). They studied two initial conditions: (1) two kinks boosted toward each other at relativistic speed \(v=0.8\) in the broken symmetry phase, and (2) a Gaussian energy overdensity in the unbroken phase with amplitude \(\varphi_0=5\) and width \(A=1\). The energy-momentum tensor was computed from the evolved fields, and the local energy density \(\varepsilon\), pressure \(p\), and fluid velocity were extracted to test for hydrodynamic scaling.

## Key numbers
- Lattice parameters: \(dx=0.125\), \(N=1024\), \(dt=0.025\), with \(m^2=1\), \(\lambda=1\), \(\hbar=1\).
- For the Gaussian initial condition with \(\varphi_0=5\) and \(A=1\), the energy contained in the initial hot region is about two orders of magnitude larger than that deposited by the kink collision.
- The speed of sound \(c_0\) extracted from the decay of energy density at \(x=0\) is \(1 \geq c_0 \gtrsim 0.77\), compatible with an ultrarelativistic equation of state \(c_0=1\) in 1D.
- The energy density scaling follows \(\varepsilon(x,t)/\varepsilon_0 = (\tau/x_0)^{-(1+c_0^2)}\) with fits shown for \(1+c_0^2=2\) (corresponding to \(c_0=1\)) and \(1+c_0^2=1.6\).

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic scaling ideas of Landau (1953) and Bjorken (1983) by demonstrating their emergence from a quantum field theory rather than assuming them phenomenologically. The results show that even in a simple 1+1 dimensional scalar model with mean-field dynamics, the energy isosurfaces follow the predicted hyperboloid form at high energy densities, enabling future studies of the equation of state and speed of sound in more realistic 3D quantum field theories with scattering, which are necessary for direct comparison with heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.
