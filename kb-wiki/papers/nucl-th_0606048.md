# Effects of deformation in the three-body structure of 11Li

> 11Li is studied within a three-body model 9Li+n+n where the core is allowed to be deformed and/or excite. In particular, we include reorientation couplings and couplings between the two bound states of 9Li. Contrary to the other examples studied within this model, we find that core excitation does not affect the structure of 11Li significantly. Reorientation couplings of the deformed 9Li can change the ground state of 11Li from a predominantly two neutron s1/2^2 configuration into a p1/2^2. In addition, we see no evidence for the existence of significant d-wave strength in its ground state, as opposed to the prediction by shell model. A comparison with shell model is presented.

## Key claim

Core deformation in the three-body model of 11Li can change the ground state configuration from predominantly (p1/2)2 to (s1/2)2 depending on the deformation parameter, but core excitation of 9Li does not significantly affect the structure, contrary to findings for other nuclei studied with the same model.

## Method

The authors performed three-body Faddeev calculations in hyperspherical coordinates for the 9Li+n+n system, treating the 9Li core with a rotational model parameterized by quadrupole deformation β2. The n-core Woods-Saxon interaction depths were fitted to reproduce the low-lying continuum structure of 10Li: the s-wave virtual state at +50 keV, the p1/2 resonance at +400 keV, and the d5/2 resonance at +3.4 MeV, with the bound 1p3/2 orbital fixed at -4.1 MeV. The model space included reorientation couplings of the deformed 9Li ground state (3/2−) but excluded the 1/2− excited state after checking its insensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of 11Li is 295 ± 35 keV against three-body breakup (Young).
- The r.m.s. matter radius of 11Li is 3.55 ± 0.10 fm (radius).
- The charged radius predicted with β2=0.3 is Rch=2.49 fm, in agreement with a recent measurement (Sanchez).
- For β2=0.3, the ground state of 11Li has approximately equal (s1/2)2 and (p1/2)2 probabilities; for small β2 it is nearly 60% (p1/2)2; for large β2 it becomes more than 80% (s1/2)2.
- The (d5/2)2 configuration is always less than 7% regardless of deformation.
- Shell model (WBP) predicts 15% d-wave in the 11Li ground state, with 50% (s1/2)2, 29% (d5/2)2, and 18% (d3/2)2 in the 2ħω component.
- The experimental two-neutron separation energy and r.m.s. matter radius constrain β2 ≲ 0.3.
- A recent high-precision mass measurement gives S2n = 376 ± 5 keV (Bachelet).

## Context

This work extends the three-body model with core excitation previously applied to 11Be and 12Be (Brida, Nunes, Brown 2005, 2006) to the 11Li system, finding that unlike those cases, core excitation is unimportant and reorientation couplings dominate. The results contradict shell model predictions using the WBP Hamiltonian (Warburton, Brown 1992) which find significant d-wave strength (≈15%) in the 11Li ground state, whereas the three-body model finds less than 7%. Resolving the location of d-states in 10Li experimentally, whether near 2 MeV as shell model predicts or above 3 MeV as assumed here, would settle the ground state structure of 11Li.
