# Nuclear PDFs After the First Decade of LHC Data

> We present a review of the conceptual basis, present knowledge and recent progress in the field of global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs). After introducing the theoretical foundations and methodological approaches for the extraction of nuclear PDFs from experimental data, we discuss how different measurements in fixed-target and collider experiments provide increasingly precise constraints on various aspects of nuclear PDFs, including shadowing, antishadowing, the EMC effect, Fermi motion, flavor separation, deuteron binding, target-mass and other higher-twist effects. Particular emphasis is given to measurements carried out in proton-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, which have revolutionized the global analysis during the past decade. These measurements include electroweak-boson, jet, light-hadron, and heavy-flavor observables. Finally, we outline the expected impact of the future Electron Ion Collider and discuss the role and interplay of nuclear PDFs with other branches of nuclear, particle and astroparticle physics.

## Key claim
This review paper argues that LHC proton-lead collision data over the past decade have "revolutionized" the global analysis of nuclear PDFs, but the authors hedge that the non-perturbative nature of nuclear interactions and the need for phenomenological models mean that a consensus on the underlying mechanisms (e.g., the EMC effect) "still has to emerge."

## Method
The authors review the theoretical foundations of nuclear PDFs within the operator product expansion and QCD-improved parton model, including target-mass and higher-twist corrections. They then describe the methodological approaches of three major global analysis frameworks (nCTEQ, EPPS, and nNNPDF), which minimize a chi-squared figure-of-merit between experimental data and theoretical calculations using either Hessian or Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation methods. The review focuses on how fixed-target deep-inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan, and collider data from RHIC and LHC (including electroweak-boson, jet, light-hadron, and heavy-flavor observables) constrain nuclear PDFs across different x regions (shadowing, antishadowing, EMC effect, Fermi motion).

## Key numbers
- The EMC collaboration in 1983 observed a suppression of F2^Fe/F2^D at x_N=0.65 of approximately 0.89 in muon DIS.
- The parameterization R(x) = 1.10 - 0.36x - 0.28e^{-21.9x} + 2.77x^{14.4} fits SLAC and NMC data for large-A isoscalar structure function ratios to deuteron, without A or Q^2 dependence.
- The nCTEQ15 NLO analysis uses 16 free parameters for the x-dependence of u_v, d_v, g, and u_bar+d_bar, with 38 error PDFs obtained via Hessian method and a tolerance of Delta chi^2 = 35.
- The EPPS21 analysis parameterizes nuclear modifications through 24 free parameters, with A-dependence encoded via a reference nucleus A_ref = 12 and exponent gamma_i > 0.
- The nCTEQ15HQ fit includes LHC data on D^0, J/psi, B->J/psi, Upsilon, psi', and B->psi' production using a data-driven effective matrix element method.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on global nuclear PDF analyses that began with Eskola (1992) parameterizing structure function ratios, and includes the modern frameworks nCTEQ (Kovarik 2015), EPPS (Eskola 2009, 2016, 2021), and nNNPDF (AbdulKhalek 2022). It contrasts with earlier reviews that focused on proton PDFs (Gao 2017, Kovarik 2019, Ethier 2020) or sketched a larger multi-dimensional picture of the nucleus (Klein 2019). The review enables future work by outlining the expected impact of the Electron Ion Collider and discussing connections to lattice QCD, gluon saturation searches, the quark-gluon plasma, and astrophysical phenomena.
