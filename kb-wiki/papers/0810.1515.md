# Fluctuations of Chromodynamic Fields in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Chromodynamic fluctuations in the collisionless quark-gluon plasma are found as a solution of the initial value linearized problem. The stable and unstable plasmas are discussed.

# Fluctuations of Chromodynamic Fields in Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Chromodynamic fluctuations in both equilibrium and nonequilibrium quark-gluon plasma can be obtained as solutions of the initial value linearized transport problem, but the behavior differs fundamentally: in stable plasmas the initial fluctuations decay exponentially and a stationary spectrum emerges, while in unstable plasmas the memory of initial fluctuations is not lost and unstable modes grow exponentially.

## Method

The author linearizes the collisionless transport equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons together with the Yang-Mills equations around a stationary, homogeneous, colorless state. The resulting system is solved via one-sided Fourier transformation with specified initial conditions for the distribution functions and chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields. Correlation functions of the fields are then obtained by averaging over statistical ensembles, with initial correlations identified from the classical system of free partons.

## Key numbers

- For the two-stream unstable system, the dispersion equation $\varepsilon_L(\omega,{\bf k}) = 0$ yields four roots $\pm \omega_{\pm}({\bf k})$, where $\omega_-({\bf k})$ corresponds to the two-stream electrostatic instability when ${\bf k}^2 ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2 < 2 \mu^2 \big({\bf k}^2 - ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2\big)$, with $\mu^2 \equiv g^2n/(2|{\bf q}|)$ and ${\bf u} \equiv {\bf q}/|{\bf q}|$ the stream velocity.
- For the unstable two-stream system, the growth rate is $\omega_-({\bf k})=i\gamma_{\bf k}$ with $0 \le \gamma_{\bf k} \in R$, and the correlation function of longitudinal chromoelectric fields grows exponentially in both $(t_1 + t_2)$ and $(t_1 - t_2)$.
- In the equilibrium case, the stationary fluctuation spectrum of chromoelectric fields is given by $\langle E_a^i E_b^j\rangle_{\omega \, {\bf k}} = 2 \delta^{ab} T \omega^3 \bigg[ \frac{k^ik^j}{{\bf k}^2} \frac{\Im \varepsilon_L(\omega,{\bf k})}{|\omega^2 \varepsilon_L(\omega,{\bf k})|^2} + \Big(\delta^{ij} - \frac{k^ik^j}{{\bf k}^2}\Big) \frac{\Im \varepsilon_T(\omega,{\bf k})}{|\omega^2 \varepsilon_T(\omega,{\bf k})-{\bf k}^2|^2} \bigg]$.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study (Mrowczynski, 2005) on chromomagnetic instabilities in anisotropic quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and connects to the work of Asakawa (2006) showing that transport coefficients are strongly modified by the fluctuation spectrum of chromomagnetic fields. The method follows the approach exposed in the handbook by Lifshitz and Pitaevskii (1981), applicable to both equilibrium and nonequilibrium plasmas. The results enable calculation of how unstable chromodynamic modes generate growing fields that modify plasma properties such as transport coefficients in the early-stage quark-gluon plasma produced at heavy-ion colliders.
