# Anisotropic hydrodynamics for mixture of quark and gluon fluids

> A system of equations for anisotropic hydrodynamics is derived that describes a mixture of anisotropic quark and gluon fluids. The consistent treatment of the zeroth, first and second moments of the kinetic equations allows us to construct a new framework with more general forms of the anisotropic phase-space distribution functions than those used before. In this way, the main difficiencies of the previous formulations of anisotropic hydrodynamics for mixtures have been overcome and the good agreement with the exact kinetic-theory results is obtained.

## Key claim

The new anisotropic hydrodynamics framework for a mixture of quark and gluon fluids, using the zeroth, first, and second moments of the kinetic equations, eliminates the exponentially damped anisotropy parameters found in prior work (Florkowski 2012) and achieves good agreement with exact kinetic-theory results, but only for the specific choice of the mixing parameter alpha=1 (or alpha=0) in the zeroth-moment equation, with alpha=0.5 giving much worse agreement.

## Method

The authors derived a system of five coupled ordinary differential equations for the proper-time evolution of the transverse-momentum scale parameters Lambda_q and Lambda_g, the anisotropy parameters xi_q and xi_g, and the effective temperature T, for a (0+1)D boost-invariant system. The equations were obtained from the zeroth moment (baryon number conservation plus a linear combination of quark and gluon density equations), the first moment (energy-momentum conservation with Landau matching), and the second moment (using the Tinti 2013 form consistent with Israel-Stewart theory near equilibrium) of the kinetic equations in the relaxation-time approximation, applied separately to quarks and gluons with Romatschke-Strickland distribution functions.

## Key numbers

* Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c, evolution continued to tau = 10 fm/c.
* Initial transverse-momentum parameters Lambda_{0,i} for quarks and gluons set equal to 1 GeV.
* Relaxation time tau_eq = 0.25 fm/c (constant).
* For the oblate-oblate configuration: initial anisotropy parameters xi_{0,q}=1 and xi_{0,g}=10.
* For the prolate-oblate configuration: xi_{0,q}=-0.5 and xi_{0,g}=10.
* For the prolate-prolate configuration: xi_{0,q}=-0.5 and xi_{0,g}=-0.25.
* Finite baryon density case: b_0 = 1 fm^{-3} compared to b_0 = 0.
* Ratio of internal degrees of freedom tilde{r} = g_g/(2g_q) = 2/3.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach for mixtures developed by Florkowski (2012, 2013, 2014), which used only zeroth and first moments and required setting the transverse-momentum scale parameters for quarks and gluons equal to close the underdetermined system. By including second moments, the new framework allows independent parameters for each species and eliminates the unphysical exponentially damped anisotropy solutions found in those earlier studies. The results are compared to exact solutions of the kinetic equations from Florkowski (2014) and show good agreement for total quantities, though the agreement is worse for gluons alone and for the highly non-equilibrium prolate-oblate configuration. The approach enables more realistic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the oblate-oblate initial conditions expected after the decay of coherent color fields.
