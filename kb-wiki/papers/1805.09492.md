# Properties of Massive Rotating Protoneutron Stars with Hyperons: Structure and Universality

> In this work, we study the properties and structure of a massive and rapidly rotating protoneutron star (PNS) with hyperon content. We follow several stages of quasi-stationary evolution in an approximate way at four discrete steps. We use a density-dependent (DD) relativistic mean field theory (RMF) model and calculate different quantities such as mass, equatorial radius, moment of inertia, and quadrupole moment to get different rotating configurations upto the mass-shedding limit. We study the effect of the appearance of $Λ$, the lightest of all hyperons, on each of the evolutionary stages of the PNS. We also check its sensitivity to the inclusion of $ϕ$ vector meson as a mediator of $Λ-Λ$ interaction in detail. Finally, we investigate the universal relations between moment of inertia and compactness in the context of a hot and young compact object.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal relation between normalized moment of inertia and compactness is broken during the early, hot, neutrino-rich stages of protoneutron star evolution, but is restored once the star becomes cold and reaches beta equilibrium; this conclusion is drawn from an approximate quasi-stationary model using four discrete evolutionary stages with constant entropy per baryon, not from a full time-dependent simulation.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state (EOS) for dense matter using a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DD-RMF) model with the DD2 parameter set, including nucleons only (HS(DD2)), nucleons plus Λ hyperons (BHBΛ), and nucleons plus Λ hyperons with ϕ-meson mediated hyperon-hyperon interaction (BHBΛϕ). They computed static and uniformly rotating stellar configurations at four discrete stages of protoneutron star evolution (stage I: s_B=1, Y_L=0.4; stage II: s_B=2, Y_L=0.4; stage III: s_B=2, Y_ν=0; stage IV: T=0, cold catalyzed) using the LORENE code for axisymmetric rotating stars, and compared the normalized moment of inertia (I/M^3 and I/MR^2) against compactness (C=M/R) with the fitting functions of Breu and Rezzolla (2016).

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for static cold NS (stage IV): HS(DD2) = 2.423 M_solar, BHBΛ = 1.955 M_solar, BHBΛϕ = 2.1 M_solar
- Maximum gravitational mass for static stage I (s_B=1, Y_L=0.4): HS(DD2) = 2.375 M_solar, BHBΛ = 2.108 M_solar, BHBΛϕ = 2.172 M_solar
- Kepler frequency for a star with baryonic mass M_B=1.8 M_solar: cold catalyzed = 935 Hz, stage I = 703 Hz, stage II = 577 Hz
- Threshold density for Λ hyperons in BHBΛϕ at T=0: 0.329 fm^{-3}; at stage III (s_B=2, Y_ν=0): 0.114 fm^{-3}
- Ratio of rotational energy to gravitational energy (T/|W|) for M_B=1.8 M_solar: increases from 0.051 (stage I) to 0.115 (stage IV)
- Deviation from the Breu and Rezzolla (2016) universal I-C relation for M_B=1.8 M_solar: stage I ~22%, stage II ~30%, stage III ~12%, stage IV ~2%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of protoneutron star structure by Bednarek (2006) and Mu (2017) using older EOS parametrizations, and incorporates constraints from the ~2 M_solar mass measurements of PSRs J1614-2230 and J0348+0432 and the tidal deformability bounds from GW170817. The universal relation between normalized moment of inertia and compactness was previously studied for cold neutron stars by Breu and Rezzolla (2016) and for nucleon-only PNS by Martinon et al. (2014) who found the I-Love-Q relation broken at early stages; the present work extends this to hyperonic PNS and finds similar deviations. The results caution against applying universal relations to connect pre-merger and post-merger quantities in binary neutron star merger remnants.
