# Explicitly Covariant Light-Front Dynamics and Relativistic Few-Body Systems

> The wave function of a composite system is defined in relativity on a space-time surface. In the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics, reviewed in the present article, the wave functions are defined on the plane $ω\cd x=0$, where $ω$ is an arbitrary four-vector with $ω^2=0$. The standard non-covariant approach is recovered as a particular case for $ω= (1,0,0,-1)$. Using the light-front plane is of crucial importance, while the explicit covariance gives strong advantages emphasized through all the review. The properties of the relativistic few-body wave functions are discussed in detail and are illustrated by examples in a solvable model. The three-dimensional graph technique for the calculation of amplitudes in the covariant light-front perturbation theory is presented. The structure of the electromagnetic amplitudes is studied. We investigate the ambiguities which arise in any approximate light-front calculations, and which lead to a non-physical dependence of the electromagnetic amplitude on the orientation of the light-front plane. The elastic and transition form factors free from these ambiguities are found for spin 0, 1/2 and 1 systems. The formalism is applied to the calculation of the relativistic wave functions of two-nucleon systems (deuteron, scattering state), with particular attention to the role of their new components in the deuteron elastic and electrodisintegration form factors and to their connection with meson exchange currents. Straigthforward applications to the pion and nucleon form factors and the $ρ-π$ transition are also made.

## Key claim

The paper argues that explicitly covariant light-front dynamics, where wave functions are defined on a plane ω·x=0 with ω²=0, generalizes the standard non-covariant light-front approach (recovered for ω=(1,0,0,-1)) and provides strong advantages, but any approximate light-front calculation suffers from ambiguities leading to a non-physical dependence of electromagnetic amplitudes on the orientation of the light-front plane.

## Method

The authors develop a three-dimensional graph technique for calculating amplitudes in covariant light-front perturbation theory, generalizing the Weinberg rules from old-fashioned perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame. They explicitly transform Feynman amplitudes into covariant light-front amplitudes for spinless, spin-1/2, and spin-1 particles, demonstrating the equivalence of the approaches for on-shell S-matrix elements. The formalism is applied to relativistic wave functions of two-nucleon systems (deuteron and scattering states), including calculations of elastic and transition form factors for spin-0, spin-1/2, and spin-1 systems.

## Context

This review extends the work of Weinberg (1966) on rules for old-fashioned perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame, generalizing those rules to a covariant formulation with an arbitrary light-front vector ω. The approach relates to the work of Brodsky, Drell, and Chang (1973) on light-front quantization, and the transformation between Feynman amplitudes and light-front amplitudes was investigated in detail by L. Bakker (referenced as ref. [lbak]) and by Schmidt (referenced as ref. [schmidt]). The formalism enables consistent calculation of relativistic few-body wave functions and electromagnetic form factors for systems including the deuteron, pion, and nucleon, while clarifying the origin of ambiguities that arise in approximate light-front calculations.
