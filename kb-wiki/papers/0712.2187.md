# Investigation of Low-Density Symmetry Energy via Nucleon and Fragment Observables

> With stochastic transport simulations we study in detail central and peripheral collisions at Fermi energies and suggest new observables, sensitive to the symmetry energy below normal density. As such we identify on one hand the isospin imbalance ratio, i.e. the relative amount of isospin equilibration in binary, peripheral reactions of nuclei with different isospin, as a function of the energy loss, which is sensitive to isospin diffusion; on the other hand the isospin asymmetry of an intermediate mass fragment (IMF) in symmetric collisions in ternary reactions, or more particularly, the ratio of the IMF to the residue asymmetry, which is sensitive to isospin migration.

# Investigation of Low-Density Symmetry Energy via Nucleon and Fragment Observables

## Key claim
Stochastic transport simulations suggest that the isospin imbalance ratio as a function of energy loss and the asymmetry ratio of intermediate mass fragments to residues in ternary reactions are sensitive observables for the symmetry energy below normal density, but the authors note that theoretical calculations still disagree with each other and with experimental data, leaving the isovector equation of state as an open question.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio collision simulations using the microscopic Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model, which combines mean field transport theory with hard nucleon-nucleon collisions and stochastic fluctuations. They used a generalized effective interaction with momentum-dependent terms in both isoscalar and isovector channels, based on an asymmetric extension of the Gale-Bertsch-DasGupta force, and tested two behaviors of the symmetry energy around saturation: asy-soft and asy-stiff. Central and peripheral collisions of tin isotopes were simulated at Fermi energies to study pre-equilibrium emission and charge equilibration dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Compressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter: 215 MeV, with isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.67, held fixed across simulations.
- Double ratio of neutron over proton yield in central 124Sn+124Sn over 112Sn+112Sn collisions at 50 AMeV shows asy-soft EOS produces higher ratios than asy-stiff, but both results fall considerably below experimental data from Famiano et al.
- Imbalance ratios for Sn+Sn collisions were studied at incident energies of 35 AMeV and 50 AMeV as a function of impact parameter and relative energy loss.
- The asymmetry ratio of intermediate mass fragments to residues for symmetric Sn+Sn reactions at 50 AMeV and impact parameter b = 6 fm shows an almost 30% effect between asy-stiff and asy-soft EOS for the more realistic momentum-dependent (MD) interaction.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of isospin dynamics at low and intermediate energies (Tsang 1992, Shi 2003, Baran 2005) and addresses the poorly-determined isovector equation of state relevant for exotic nuclei and astrophysics. The authors build on the Stochastic Mean Field model (Fabbri 2004, Chomaz 2004) and the imbalance ratio method (Ramirez 2004) to propose new observables. The results favor an asy-stiff EOS from isospin diffusion and neck fragmentation data but an asy-soft behavior from pre-equilibrium studies, indicating that further work with more asymmetric radioactive beams is needed to resolve the discrepancy.
