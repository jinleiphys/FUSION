# Causal and stable first-order chiral hydrodynamics

> We derive the set of inequalities that is necessary and sufficient for nonlinear causality and linear stability of first-order relativistic hydrodynamics with either a $U(1)_V$ conserved current or a $U(1)_A$ current with a chiral anomaly or both. Our results apply to generic hydrodynamic frames in which no relations among the transport parameters are imposed. Furthermore, our analysis yields, to the best of our knowledge, the first theory of viscous chiral hydrodynamics proven to be causal and stable. We find that causality demands the absence of vorticity-induced heat flux, forcing a departure from the thermodynamic frame in the chiral case. The inequalities for causality and stability define a hypervolume in the space of transport parameters, wherein each point corresponds to a consistent formulation. Notably, causality is determined by just three combinations of transport parameters. We present our results in a form amenable to numerical hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key claim

The authors derive the first complete set of necessary and sufficient inequalities for nonlinear causality and linear stability in first-order relativistic chiral hydrodynamics, applicable to generic hydrodynamic frames without imposed relations among transport parameters. The key hedge is that causality forces the absence of vorticity-induced heat flux, which requires a departure from the thermodynamic frame in the chiral case, and that the causality conditions should also be sufficient for local well-posedness in Gevrey function spaces, though the mathematical proof is beyond the scope of this work.

## Method

The authors start from the BDNK first-order constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and conserved current, including all symmetry-allowed first-order derivative terms. They derive the principal part of the equations of motion and analyze the characteristic polynomial to obtain necessary and sufficient conditions for nonlinear causality, then perform a linear perturbation analysis around homogeneous equilibrium states in Minkowski spacetime to derive conditions for linear stability. The analysis is extended to chiral hydrodynamics by incorporating anomalous axial currents for both \(U(1)_A\) and \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) symmetries, with the transport parameters treated as free functions of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Causality is determined by just three combinations of transport parameters, while stability requires an additional nine combinations.
- The inequalities define a 14-dimensional hypervolume in the space of transport parameters where each point corresponds to a causal and stable formulation.
- The thermodynamic stability conditions require \(c_V > 0\) (volumetric heat capacity positive) and \((\partial(\mu/T)/\partial n)_\varepsilon > 0\) for thermal and diffusive stability.
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\) is frame-invariant, and there exist seven frame-invariant quantities: \(f_i = \pi_i - \varepsilon_i (\partial P/\partial \varepsilon)_n - \nu_i(\partial P/\partial n)_\varepsilon\) for \(i=1,2,3\), \(\ell_i = \gamma_i - n\theta_i/w\) for \(i=1,2,3\), and \(\eta\) itself.

## Context

This work extends the BDNK framework (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun, 2017-2020) for causal and stable first-order hydrodynamics to include chiral anomalies, addressing a gap left by prior work on chiral hydrodynamics (Son and Surowka, 2009) which had causality problems as noted by Speranza (2021). The results enable numerical simulations of viscous chiral hydrodynamics in contexts such as quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and ultradense matter in neutron star mergers, where previous second-order approaches (Gorbar, 2017) lacked causality and stability analyses.
