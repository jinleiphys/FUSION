# $Z_c(3900)$ as a resonance from the $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction

> In this paper it is proposed that the charged charmonium-like state $Z_c(3900)$ is a resonance above the threshold from the $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction. The $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction is described by the one-boson exchange model with light meson exchanges plus a short-range $J/ψ$ exchange. The scattering amplitude is calculated within a Bethe-Salpeter equation approach and the poles near the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold are searched. In the isoscalar sector, two poles found under the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold, i.e., bound states, have the quantum numbers $I^G(J^{PC})=0^-(1^{+-})$ and $0^+(1^{++})$. The latter can be related to the $X(3872)$. In the isovector sector, a bound state with $I^G(J^P)=1^+(1^+)$ is found with a large cutoff at about 3 GeV. If a cutoff at about 2 GeV is adopted with which a pole carrying the quantum number of the $X(3872)$ is produced at an energy of about 3871 MeV, the pole for the bound state with $1^+(1^+)$ runs across the threshold to a second Rienman sheet and becomes a resonance above the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold, which can be identified with the $Z_c(3900)$. With such a cutoff, the $D\bar{D}^{*}$ invariant mass spectrum is also investigated and the experimental results found by BESIII can be reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the charged charmonium-like state \(Z_c(3900)\) is a resonance above the \(D\bar{D}^*\) threshold, not a bound state, and that this interpretation depends on using a specific cutoff value \(\Lambda \approx 2.4\) GeV in the regularization of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which simultaneously reproduces the \(X(3872)\) as a bound state.

## Method

The \(D\bar{D}^*\) interaction was modeled using a one-boson exchange (OBE) potential including light pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges plus a short-range \(J/\psi\) exchange, with coupling constants from heavy quark effective theory. The scattering amplitude was calculated by solving a three-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation in the covariant spectator theory, using an exponential regulator with cutoff \(\Lambda\) to regularize the propagator. Poles of the amplitude were searched in the complex energy plane for both the first and second Riemann sheets to identify bound states and resonances.

## Key numbers

- A bound state with quantum numbers \(I^G(J^{PC}) = 0^+(1^{++})\) was found at an energy of about 3871 MeV with cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.4\) GeV, identified with the \(X(3872)\).
- In the isovector sector, a bound state with \(I^G(J^P) = 1^+(1^+)\) was found with a large cutoff at about 3 GeV.
- With cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.4\) GeV, the pole for the \(1^+(1^+)\) state became a resonance at \(z = 3876 + i5\) MeV (pole mass 3876 MeV, half-width 5 MeV).
- The peak in the \(D\bar{D}^*\) invariant mass spectrum from the model was found at about 3881 MeV, compared to the experimental BESIII values of \(3883.9 \pm 1.5 \pm 4.2\) MeV in the \(D\bar{D}^*\) channel and \(3899 \pm 3.6 \pm 4.9\) MeV in the \(\pi^- J/\psi\) channel.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the \(D\bar{D}^*\) interaction using the Bethe-Salpeter equation (He 2014) and nonrelativistic OBE models (Sun 2011, Sun 2012), which found no bound state for the \(Z_c(3900)\). It contrasts with lattice calculations (Chen 2014, Lee 2014, Prelovsek 2013) that did not support a \(Z_c(3900)\) bound state, and with interpretations of the \(Z_c(3900)\) as a kinematic cusp effect (Guo 2014). The result enables a unified description of the \(X(3872)\) as a bound state and the \(Z_c(3900)\) as a resonance from the same \(D\bar{D}^*\) interaction, and provides a framework for studying other near-threshold XYZ states as resonances rather than bound states.
