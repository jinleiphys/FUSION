# Coulomb Effects on Particle Spectra in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Coulomb effects on $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$ spectra in relativistic nuclear collisions are investigated. At collision energies around 1 GeV the ratio of $π^-$ to $π^+$ is enhanced several times at low transverse momenta but less at ultrarelativistic energies. We describe the ratios at SIS, AGS and SPS energies with simple analytic models as well as more elaborate numerical models incorporating the expansion dynamics. The Coulomb effect depends on the properties of the source after the violent collision phase and provides information on source sizes, freeze-out times, and expansion velocities. Comparison with results from HBT analyses are made. Predictions for $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$ at RHIC and LHC energies are given.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low transverse momenta in relativistic nuclear collisions can be explained by Coulomb interactions between produced pions and the net positive charge of the source, but the magnitude of the effect depends critically on whether the charge source expands slowly (spherically) or rapidly (longitudinally), with the expansion velocity determining whether particle energies are shifted or scaled.

## Method

The authors develop analytic models for Coulomb effects on charged pion and kaon spectra by assuming simple geometrical shapes of the charge distribution at freeze-out: a static spherical charge for slowly expanding systems (SIS energies) and a rapidly longitudinally expanding cylindrical charge for ultrarelativistic energies (AGS, SPS). They also employ a more elaborate numerical dynamical model that includes both longitudinal and transverse flow, retardation effects, and treats the electromagnetic field as a first-order perturbation on particle trajectories after freeze-out. The models are compared to experimental π⁻/π⁺ ratio data from SIS, AGS, and SPS experiments.

## Key numbers

- At SIS energies (1 A·GeV Au+Au), the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low pion momenta is ~3, described with Coulomb potential V_c = 27 ± 2 MeV and temperature T = 75 MeV, corresponding to source radius R ≈ 8.0 fm and baryon density ~0.85 ρ₀
- At AGS energies (11.4 A·GeV), the Coulomb kick Δp_⟂ = 20 MeV/c, with freeze-out radius R_f ≈ 10 fm
- At SPS energies (158 A·GeV Pb+Pb), the Coulomb kick Δp_⟂ = 12 MeV/c, with freeze-out radius R_f ≈ 9 fm
- For S+S collisions at SPS, the Coulomb kick p_c ≈ 4 MeV/c, giving a very small effect
- The average π⁻/π⁺ ratio at SIS is ⟨π⁻/π⁺⟩ = 1.94 from the Δ-resonance model (5N²+NZ)/(5Z²+NZ); at AGS it is ~1.2; at SPS it is ~1.05
- The proton rapidity density at SPS is dN^p/dy = 37 ± 2; at AGS it is ~70
- For kaons at SPS, the Coulomb enhancement of K⁻/K⁺ is smaller by a factor of m_π/m_K ≈ 1/3 compared to pions

## Context

This work extends earlier static-source Coulomb models by Gyulassy, Stock, Bao, and Osada to include source dynamics and expansion, showing that the Coulomb effect provides information on freeze-out parameters complementary to HBT analyses. The authors compare their extracted freeze-out radii (R_f ≈ 8-10 fm) to HBT radii from NA44 data (R_s ≈ 5 fm, R_l ≈ 5.5 fm) and find consistency at SPS energies, though at SIS energies the HBT radius (~15 fm) is considerably larger. The dynamical model is based on an earlier publication by Barz et al. and is compared to transport model results by Teis et al. The work enables extraction of freeze-out times, source sizes, and expansion velocities from charged particle ratios at RHIC and LHC energies.
