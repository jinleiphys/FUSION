# Charm and Hidden Charm Scalar Resonances in Nuclear Matter

> We study the properties of the scalar charm resonances $D_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_0(2400)$, and the theoretical hidden charm state X(3700) in nuclear matter. We find that for the $D_{s0}(2317)$ and X(3700) resonances, with negligible and small width at zero density, respectively, the width becomes about $100 {\rm MeV}$ and $200 {\rm MeV}$ at normal nuclear matter density, accordingly. For $D_0(2400)$ the change in width is relatively less important. We discuss the origin of this new width and trace it to reactions occurring in the nucleus. We also propose a possible experimental test for those modifications in nuclear matter, which will bring valuable information on the nature of those scalar resonances and the interaction of $D$ mesons with nucleons.

## Key claim

The authors find that the dynamically-generated scalar charm resonances \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and the hidden charm state \(X(3700)\) acquire large in-medium widths of about 100 MeV and 200 MeV respectively at normal nuclear matter density, while the \(D_0(2400)\) shows a comparatively weaker medium effect due to its already large free-space width. The claim is hedged by noting that mass shifts are small and model uncertainties (such as the SU(4) symmetry breaking scheme and choice of form factors) prevent clear conclusions on mass changes.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for two pseudoscalars, using a kernel derived from an extrapolation to SU(4) of the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian, with strong SU(4) breaking from explicit vector-meson masses. The in-medium calculation incorporates Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and self-consistent \(\pi\) and \(D\) meson self-energies, plus a \(p\)-wave self-energy from \(Y_c N^{-1}\) excitations with a heavy-meson form factor. The cutoff in the regularization scheme is fixed by reproducing the \(\Lambda_c(2593)\) resonance in vacuum.

## Key numbers

* The \(D_{s0}(2317)\) resonance, with negligible width at zero density, acquires a width of about 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
* The hidden charm state \(X(3700)\), with a small width at zero density, acquires a width of about 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
* The \(D_0(2400)\) resonance shows a relatively less important change in width compared to the other two resonances, due to its already large free-space width.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium modifications of dynamically-generated scalar mesons from the light sector (such as \(\sigma(600)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\)) into the charm sector, using methods previously applied to the \(\Lambda_c(2593)\) resonance by Tolos et al. (2004) and to the \(\Sigma_c(2880)\) by Lutz et al. (2006). The results enable experimental tests via transparency ratios at future facilities like FAIR, providing a probe of the nature of these scalar resonances and the \(D\) meson-nucleon interaction.
