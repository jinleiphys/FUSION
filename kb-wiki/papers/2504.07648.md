# Simultaneous calculation of elastic scattering, transfer, breakup, and other direct cross sections for $d$+$^{197}$Au reaction

> Simultaneous analyses are performed for cross section of elastic scattering, Coulomb breakup, transfer, and other direct yields for the $d$+$^{197}$Au system at all available energies. The data are reproduced well by the optical model that is based on parts related to the Coulomb and nuclear contributions of the direct cross sections. This method of calculation can be successfully applied to the reactions of deuteron with heavy targets.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a modified optical model, incorporating separate dynamical polarization potentials for Coulomb breakup and neutron transfer, can simultaneously reproduce elastic scattering, transfer, breakup, and total reaction cross sections for the \(d+^{197}\)Au system across a wide energy range, with the caveat that the model requires fitting only three parameters (the depths of the real potential, the surface imaginary potential, and the transfer potential) while keeping all other global optical model parameters fixed.

## Method

The authors start from the global deuteron optical potential of An (2006) and replace the standard surface imaginary term with three separate components: a Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP) for Coulomb breakup, an imaginary nuclear dynamical polarization potential (NDPP) for \((d,p)\) transfer, and a surface imaginary potential for nuclear breakup and other direct channels. The CDPP is derived from a formalism treating the deuteron as a proton core plus valence neutron scattering from a heavy target, while the NDPP uses a Woods-Saxon derivative form with geometry fixed by semiclassical theory (strong absorption radius \(R_L = 1.4(A_P^{1/3}+A_T^{1/3})\) and diffuseness \(a_L = 2.167\) fm). The three free parameters (real depth \(V_r\), surface imaginary depth \(W_s\), and transfer depth \(W_L\)) are adjusted to fit elastic scattering and transfer data simultaneously.

## Key numbers

* Elastic scattering data were analyzed at ten incident energies between 5 and 16 MeV from Giudice (2023), plus additional data at 11.8 MeV (Igo, 1961), 13.6 MeV (Korner, 1968), 21.6 MeV (Yntema, 1959), 25.3 MeV (Djaloeis, 1964), and 52 MeV (Hinterberger, 1968).
* The Coulomb barrier for the system is \(V_B \sim 10.5\) MeV.
* The diffuseness parameter for the transfer potential is fixed at \(a_L = 2.167\) fm, derived from the deuteron separation energy \(\varepsilon_0 = -2.225\) MeV.
* At \(E_{lab} = 5\) MeV, the calculated partial cross sections are: \(\sigma_{vol} = 0\) mb, \(\sigma_{sur} = 0.1\) mb, \(\sigma_{d,p} = 0.2\) mb, \(\sigma^C_{bu} = 0.1\) mb, and total \(\sigma_R = 0.4\) mb.
* At \(E_{lab} = 52\) MeV, the calculated partial cross sections are: \(\sigma_{vol} = 300\) mb, \(\sigma_{sur} = 2447\) mb, \(\sigma_{d,p} = 25.1\) mb, \(\sigma^C_{bu} = 192\) mb, and total \(\sigma_R = 2965\) mb.
* The fitted energy-dependent parameters follow a Lorentzian form with coefficients given for \(V_r(E)\), \(W_d(E)\), and \(W_L(E)\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Maridi (2021, 2024) by applying the combined CDPP and NDPP method to the \(d+^{197}\)Au system, which had not been simultaneously analyzed for elastic scattering, transfer, breakup, and total reaction cross sections. The model improves upon global deuteron optical potentials such as those of Daehnick (1980), Bojowald (1988), An (2006), and Han (2006), which use a single surface imaginary term for all direct channels, by separating the contributions from different reaction mechanisms. The results enable future systematic studies of deuteron-induced reactions on heavy targets at near-barrier energies using a minimal set of adjustable parameters.
