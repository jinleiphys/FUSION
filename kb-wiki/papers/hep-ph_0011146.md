# Chiral dynamics in the presence of bound states: kaon-nucleon interactions revisited

> We study the S-wave kaon-nucleon interactions for strangeness S=-1 in a novel relativistic chiral unitary approach based on coupled channels. Dispersion relations are used to perform the necessary resummation of the lowest order relativistic chiral Lagrangian. A good description of the data in the K^- p, πΣand πΛchannels is obtained. We show how this method can be systematically extended to higher orders, emphasizing its applicability to any scenario of strong self-interactions where the perturbative series diverges even at low energies. Discussions about the differences to existing approaches employing pseudo-potentials in a regulated Lippmann-Schwinger equation are included. Finally, we describe the resonance content of our meson-baryon amplitudes and discuss its nature.

## Key claim

The authors present a novel relativistic chiral unitary approach based on coupled-channel subtracted dispersion relations that can resum the non-perturbative right-hand cut contributions in kaon-nucleon interactions, even starting from only the lowest-order tree-level chiral Lagrangian, and they show that this method can be systematically extended to higher orders in chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors constructed S-wave partial wave amplitudes for strangeness S=-1 kaon-nucleon interactions by taking the lowest-order (tree-level) relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian as input and then resumming the unitarity (right-hand cut) contributions using coupled-channel subtracted dispersion relations. The resulting T-matrix is given by T(W) = [I + T₁(W)·g(s)]⁻¹·T₁(W), where T₁(W) are the lowest-order chiral amplitudes and g(s) are scalar loop integrals with subtraction constants. The subtraction constants were either fixed to "natural" values or fitted to scattering data and threshold ratios.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral unitary approach previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering by Oller and Meissner (2000) to the kaon-nucleon system, and provides an alternative to the regulated Lippmann-Schwinger equation methods used by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998). The approach enables systematic inclusion of higher-order chiral corrections and explicit resonance fields, which is necessary to determine whether the Λ(1405) resonance is a pure meson-baryon bound state or has a preexisting quark-model component.
