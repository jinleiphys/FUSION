# Present Status of Single Pion Production in Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

> Some of the recent progress in the physics of pion production induced by neutrinos on nucleons and nuclei is reviewed from a theoretical perspective. The importance of Watson's theorem to reconcile ANL and BNL data with the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation for the $Δ(1232)$ is discussed. The disagreement between MiniBooNE data and theoretical calculations is presented in the light of the new MINERvA data. The coherent pion production data on $^{12}$C obtained by MINERvA are also compared to different microscopic and PCAC models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that respecting Watson's theorem in the weak pion production amplitude is essential to reconcile the axial coupling \(C_5^A(0)\) extracted from ANL and BNL bubble-chamber data with the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation; without imposing unitarity, the extracted value falls about \(2\sigma\) below the GT prediction.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models of single pion production induced by neutrinos on nucleons and nuclei, focusing on the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and non-resonant background amplitudes. They describe how Watson's theorem is restored in the \(P_{33}\) partial wave by introducing energy- and \(q^2\)-dependent phases taken from the SAID \(\pi N\) phase shifts, following the approach of Olsson (1974). For nuclear targets, they compare microscopic models (with in-medium \(\Delta\) broadening and pion final-state interactions) and PCAC-based coherent pion production models to data from MiniBooNE and MINERvA.

## Key numbers

*   \(C_5^A(0) = 1.12 \pm 0.11\) and \(M_{A\Delta} = 0.95 \pm 0.06\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from a fit to original ANL and BNL data (\(W_{\pi N} < 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) with Watson's theorem restored.
*   \(C_5^A(0) = 1.14 \pm 0.07\) and \(M_{A\Delta} = 0.96 \pm 0.07\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from a fit using the reanalyzed ANL and BNL data (flux-normalization independent) with Watson's theorem restored.
*   The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation predicts \(C_5^A(0)\big|_{\mathrm{GT}} = 1.15 - 1.2\).
*   Without imposing Watson's theorem, the revisited ANL+BNL data yield \(C_5^A(0) = 1.05 \pm 0.07\), clearly below the GT relation.
*   The ratio \(R(T_\pi) = (d\sigma/dT_\pi)_{\mathrm{MINERvA,\,CC}\pi^\pm} / (d\sigma/dT_\pi)_{\mathrm{MiniBooNE,\,CC}\pi^+} \approx 2\) is predicted by the NuWro generator but is absent in the data.
*   A value of \(C_5^A(0)\) extracted from ANL/BNL data ignoring Watson's theorem would result in a 30% smaller coherent pion production cross section compared to using the GT relation.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical understanding of weak pion production by addressing long-standing inconsistencies between ANL (Radecky, 1981) and BNL (Kitagaki, 1986) data sets, which were recently reanalyzed by Wilkinson (2014). The paper contrasts the MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010) data, which show an unexplained excess of forward pions, with the newer MINERvA (Eberly, 2014; Higuera, 2014) data. It enables future work by providing a consistent determination of the axial coupling \(C_5^A(0)\) and by identifying the need to resolve the tension between MiniBooNE and MINERvA data sets, as well as the need for improved coherent pion production models beyond the Rein-Sehgal (1982) and Berger-Sehgal (2008) approaches.
