# Predicting the Lambda binding energy in nuclear matter

> The purpose of this note is to report predictions of the binding energy of the $Λ$ hyperon in nuclear matter using the latest version of the Juelich nucleon-nucleon meson-exchange potential. Results from a conventional Brueckner calculation are compared with previously reported values. A calculation including Dirac effects on the $Λ$ single-particle potential is also presented. Issues encountered in Dirac calculations with nucleon-hyperon potentials are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the Jülich NY05 nucleon-hyperon potential predicts a Λ binding energy in nuclear matter of approximately 50 MeV at normal nuclear density, which is considerably larger than the generally accepted empirical value of about 30 MeV, raising questions about the reliability of that empirical value.

## Method

The author performed non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of the Λ single-particle potential in symmetric nuclear matter at a Fermi momentum of 1.35 fm⁻¹, using the Jülich NY05 meson-exchange potential for the nucleon-hyperon interaction and treating the Λ as an impurity (k_F^Λ ≈ 0). A Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculation was also performed by incorporating the Λ effective mass through scalar and vector potentials, with pseudoscalar coupling replaced by pseudovector coupling to avoid unreasonably large relativistic corrections. The Λ binding energy B_Λ was obtained as the negative of the Λ single-particle potential at zero momentum.

## Key numbers

- B_Λ = 51.27 MeV from the non-relativistic BHF calculation with the NY05 potential at k_F^N = 1.35 fm⁻¹ (compared to 29.8 MeV with the older NY94 potential)
- B_Λ = 47.4 MeV from the DBHF calculation with the NY05 potential, showing a reduction of approximately 4 MeV due to Dirac effects
- The largest partial-wave contributions to B_Λ in the BHF calculation come from the ³S₁+³D₁ channel (37.69 MeV) and the ¹S₀ channel (8.73 MeV) for NY05
- The NY94 potential gave 27.2 MeV from ³S₁+³D₁ and 3.6 MeV from ¹S₀
- The NY05 potential predicts a hypertriton binding energy of 2.27 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.354(50) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of hyperon binding in nuclear matter using the Nijmegen and Jülich potentials, specifically comparing with the results of Reuber et al. (1994) who used the older Jülich NY94 potential. The paper presents the first DBHF calculation of the Λ binding energy, finding that Dirac effects reduce the binding by about 4 MeV, roughly half the corresponding effect on nucleon binding. The results raise questions about the empirical Λ binding energy value derived from hypernuclear data by Dover et al., and the author notes that a fully self-consistent DBHF calculation for mixed nucleon-hyperon matter is in progress to address the impact on the equation of state and neutron star predictions.
