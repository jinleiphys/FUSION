# Excited state baryon spectroscopy from lattice QCD

> We present a calculation of the Nucleon and Delta excited state spectrum on dynamical anisotropic clover lattices. A method for operator construction is introduced that allows for the reliable identification of the continuum spins of baryon states, overcoming the reduced symmetry of the cubic lattice. Using this method, we are able to determine a spectrum of single-particle states for spins up to and including J = 7/2, of both parities, the first time this has been achieved in a lattice calculation. We find a spectrum of states identifiable as admixtures of SU(6) x O(3) representations and a counting of levels that is consistent with the non-relativistic $qqq$ constituent quark model. This dense spectrum is incompatible with quark-diquark model solutions to the "missing resonance problem" and shows no signs of parity doubling of states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a sufficiently complete basis of operators, including derivative constructions that resolve higher orbital angular momentum states, reveals a dense spectrum of excited baryon states that is incompatible with quark-diquark model solutions to the "missing resonance problem" and shows no signs of parity doubling. The authors hedge that their work remains incomplete because they have entered a regime of open decay channels requiring the addition of multi-particle operators to the basis.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on dynamical anisotropic clover lattices using the distillation method for quark-field smearing, which replaces the smearing function with an outer product over low-lying eigenmodes of the Laplacian. They constructed baryon operators with up to two covariant derivatives, combined using SU(2) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to obtain total angular momentum up to J=7/2, and applied the variational method to extract the spectrum from correlation matrices. The operators were classified according to flavor (octet, decuplet, singlet), Dirac spin, and spatial symmetry to enable reliable identification of continuum spins despite the reduced symmetry of the cubic lattice.

## Key numbers

- Four nearly degenerate excited states found at approximately 2.2 GeV in the Nucleon J=1/2+ channel at the lightest pion mass studied
- Three nearly degenerate states found near 2.8 GeV in the same channel
- For the singlet flavor representation: 14 operators in G1 irrep, 22 in H irrep, 9 in G2 irrep (summed over 0, 1, and 2 derivatives)
- For the octet flavor representation: 28 operators in G1 irrep, 48 in H irrep, 20 in G2 irrep
- For the decuplet flavor representation: 15 operators in G1 irrep, 26 in H irrep, 11 in G2 irrep
- With two derivatives, at most J=7/2 can be reached in the continuum limit

## Context

This work extends the approach developed by Dudek et al. (2007, 2009, 2010) for excited meson spectroscopy to baryon systems, and builds upon the distillation method of Peardon (2009). The results contradict earlier lattice calculations by Mahbub (2010) and Engel (2010), which found only two excited levels below 2.8 GeV in the Nucleon J=1/2+ channel using only local or quasi-local operators without derivative constructions. The dense spectrum observed is consistent with the non-relativistic qqq constituent quark model and SU(6) x O(3) representations, and the authors argue that the "missing resonance problem" is resolved by using a sufficiently complete operator basis. The work enables future calculations that include multi-particle operators to address the regime of open decay channels.
