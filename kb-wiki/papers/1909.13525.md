# Long-distance Contributions to Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay $π^- \toπ^+ e e$

> Neutrinoless double beta decay, if detected, would prove that neutrinos are Majorana fermions and provide the direct evidence for lepton number violation. If such decay would exist in nature, then $π^-π^-\to ee$ and $π^-\toπ^+ ee$ (or equivalently $π^-e^+\toπ^+ e^-$) are the two simplest processes accessible via first-principle lattice QCD calculations. In this work, we calculate the long-distance contributions to the $π^-\toπ^+ee$ transition amplitude using four ensembles at the physical pion mass with various volumes and lattice spacings. We adopt the infinite-volume reconstruction method to control the finite-volume effects arising from the (almost) massless neutrino. Providing the lattice QCD inputs for chiral perturbation theory, we obtain the low energy constant $g_ν^{ππ}(m_ρ)=-10.89(28)_\text{stat}(74)_\text{sys}$, which is close to $g_ν^{ππ}(m_ρ)=-11.96(31)_\text{stat}$ determined from the crossed-channel $π^-π^-\to ee$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the long-distance contributions to the neutrinoless double beta decay process π⁻ → π⁺ e e using lattice QCD at the physical pion mass, obtaining a low-energy constant g_ν^{ππ}(m_ρ) = -10.89(28)_stat(74)_sys that is close to the value from the crossed-channel π⁻π⁻ → e e decay, but the result carries a systematic uncertainty of approximately 10% due to finite-volume effects and lattice artifacts that are not fully controlled.

## Method

The authors compute the Euclidean-space hadronic function H'(x) for the π⁻ → π⁺ e e transition using four ensembles at physical pion mass with different volumes and lattice spacings generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaborations. They employ the infinite-volume reconstruction (IVR) method to control finite-volume effects from the massless neutrino propagator, and subtract the vacuum intermediate-state contribution to remove exponentially growing terms in the Euclidean time integral. The normalized amplitude A is extracted and inserted into the chiral perturbation theory (χPT) formula to determine the low-energy constant g_ν^{ππ}(μ) at μ = m_ρ.

## Key numbers

- Normalized amplitude A = 0.1045(34)_stat(50)_L(55)_a at the continuum limit, where the systematic uncertainties are for finite-volume effects and lattice artifacts respectively
- Low-energy constant g_ν^{ππ}(m_ρ) = -10.89(28)_stat(33)_L(66)_a, with total systematic uncertainty 0.74
- For comparison, g_ν^{ππ}(m_ρ) from the crossed-channel π⁻π⁻ → e e decay is -11.96(31)_stat (Feng 2018)
- The amplitude ratio A_IVR for ensemble 24D (m_π L = 3.3) is 0.1052(9), and for ensemble 32D (m_π L = 4.5) is 0.0943(6), showing a ~10% discrepancy before finite-volume corrections
- Continuum extrapolation uses ensembles with inverse lattice spacings a⁻¹ = 1.015 GeV (24D) and 1.378 GeV (32D-fine), with additional check at a⁻¹ = 1.73 GeV (48I)

## Context

This work extends the exploratory lattice QCD study of π⁻π⁻ → e e decay by Feng (2018) to the crossed-channel π⁻ → π⁺ e e process, providing a cross-check for chiral perturbation theory predictions. The calculation builds on the infinite-volume reconstruction method developed by Feng (2018) and complements earlier lattice calculations by the CalLat Collaboration (Nicholson 2018) for short-distance contributions and NPLQCD Collaboration (Detmold 2018) for long-distance contributions. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay in the nucleon sector, where a short-range contribution breaking Weinberg's power-counting scheme has been identified by Cirigliano (2018).
