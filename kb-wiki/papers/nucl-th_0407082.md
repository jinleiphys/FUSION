# Onset of intruder ground state in exotic Na isotopes and evolution of the N=20 shell gap

> The onset of intruder ground states in Na isotopes is investigated by comparing experimental data and shell-model calculations. This onset is one of the consequences of the disappearance of the N=20 magic structure, and the Na isotopes are shown to play a special role in clarifying the change of this magic structure. Both the electromagnetic moments and the energy levels clearly indicate an onset of ground state intruder configurations at neutron number N=19 already, which arises only with a narrow N=20 shell gap in Na isotopes resulting from the spin-isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (as compared to a wider gap in stable nuclei like 40Ca). It is shown why the previous report based on the mass led to a wrong conclusion.

## Key claim

The onset of intruder ground-state configurations in Na isotopes occurs at neutron number N=19 (³⁰Na), not at N=20 as previously inferred from mass measurements, and this early onset arises specifically because the N=20 shell gap is narrow (about 3 MeV) in Na isotopes due to the spin-isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, compared to the wider gap (about 6 MeV) in stable nuclei like ⁴⁰Ca.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) with the SDPF-M effective interaction, which includes the full sd-shell orbits and two lower pf-shell orbits. They compared calculated electromagnetic moments (quadrupole and magnetic dipole), energy levels, and B(E2) transition strengths against experimental data for neutron-rich Na isotopes from N=16 to N=20. To probe the role of the shell gap, they artificially varied the monopole interaction between the 0d₅/₂ and 0d₃/₂ orbits to control the N=20 gap width and observed the resulting changes in ground-state properties.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole moment of ²⁹Na: experimental value 8.6(3) e·fm², SDPF-M prediction 9.1 e·fm², indicating ~42% intruder mixing in the ground state at N=18
- Magnetic moment of ³⁰Na: experimental value 2.083(10) μ_N, compared to USD-model value 2.69 μ_N, resolved by MCSM with SDPF-M as consequence of intruder ground state
- B(E2↑) for ³⁰Na from Coulomb excitation: 130^{+90}_{-65} e²·fm⁴, consistent with MCSM calculation giving B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 3⁺₁) = 168 e²·fm⁴
- N=20 shell gap in Na isotopes from SDPF-M: about 3.3 MeV for ³⁰Na, compared to about 4.3 MeV in the USD interaction and about 6 MeV in ⁴⁰Ca
- Transition from intruder to normal ground state in ³⁰Na occurs at shell gap about 4 MeV; in ³¹Na at about 5 MeV; in ²⁹Na at about 3 MeV
- Correlation energy within sd shell for USD interaction: 1.7 MeV for ³¹Na, 3.7 MeV for ³⁰Na, 7.2 MeV for ²⁹Na

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the "island of inversion" concept (first identified from extra binding energies of ³¹,³²Na by Campi and collaborators) and the disappearance of the N=20 magic structure previously established in ³²Mg and ³⁰Ne. The paper directly contradicts the earlier conclusion from the USD interaction (Wildenthal, 1984) and the island-of-inversion model (Caurier and collaborators) that the transition to intruder ground states occurs at N=20, showing instead that electromagnetic moments and B(E2) values reveal the onset already at N=19. The results enable future experimental studies of the predicted rotational bands in ³⁰Na (K=2 and K=1 bands) and negative-parity states, and demonstrate that the shell evolution mechanism (Otsuka and collaborators, 2001) driven by the spin-isospin-dependent monopole interaction explains both the narrow N=20 gap in neutron-rich Na isotopes and the wide gap in stable ⁴⁰Ca.
