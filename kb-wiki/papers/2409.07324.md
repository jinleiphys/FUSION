# $^{61}$Cr as a Doorway to the N = 40 Island of Inversion

> This paper reports on the measurement of the ground-state spin and nuclear magnetic dipole moment of $^{61}$Cr. The radioactive ion beam was produced at the CERN-ISOLDE facility and was probed using high-resolution resonance ionization laser spectroscopy with the CRIS apparatus. The present ground-state spin measurement $I = \frac{1}{2}$, differing from the previously adopted $I =(\frac{5}{2})$, has significant consequences on the interpretation of existing beta decay data and nuclear structure in the region. The structure and shape of $^{61}$Cr is interpreted with state-of-the-art Large-Scale Shell Model and Discrete-Non-Orthogonal Shell Model calculations. From the measured magnetic dipole moment $μ(^{61}$Cr$)=+0.539(7)~μ_N$ and the theoretical findings, its configuration is understood to be driven by 2 particle - 2 hole neutron excitations with an unpaired $1p_{1/2}$ neutron. This establishes the western border of the $N=40$ Island Of Inversion (IoI), characterized by 4 particle - 4 hole neutron components. We discuss the shape evolution along the Cr isotopic chain as a quantum phase transition at the entrance of the $N=40$ IoI.

# $^{61}$Cr as a Doorway to the N = 40 Island of Inversion

## Key claim

The ground-state spin of $^{61}$Cr is measured to be $I = \frac{1}{2}$, contradicting the previously adopted $I = (\frac{5}{2})$ from beta-decay studies; this revision, combined with the measured magnetic dipole moment, establishes $^{61}$Cr as the last chromium isotope with a ground-state dominated by 2 particle - 2 hole neutron configurations, placing it at the western entrance to the N = 40 Island of Inversion where 4 particle - 4 hole configurations dominate.

## Method

Radioactive $^{61}$Cr beams were produced at CERN-ISOLDE via 1.4-GeV proton-induced fission on a uranium-carbide target, mass-separated, and probed using high-resolution resonance ionization laser spectroscopy with the CRIS apparatus. The ground-state spin was determined from the number of hyperfine peaks observed in the 427.48-nm atomic transition, and the magnetic dipole moment was extracted from the hyperfine $A_{S_3}$ parameter using $^{53}$Cr as a reference. The results were interpreted with Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM) diagonalizations and Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell Model (DNO-SM) calculations using the LNPS effective interaction in a valence space comprising the $pf$-shell for protons and the $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$, and $1d_{5/2}$ orbitals for neutrons on an inert $^{48}$Ca core.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state spin of $^{61}$Cr: $I = \frac{1}{2}$, revised from the previously adopted $I = (\frac{5}{2})$.
- Magnetic dipole moment of $^{61}$Cr: $\mu(^{61}\text{Cr}) = +0.541(6)~\mu_N$, measured via the hyperfine $A_{S_3}$ parameter $A_{S_3}(^{61}\text{Cr}) = +282.4(28)~\text{MHz}$.
- Magnetic dipole moment of $^{51}$Cr: $\mu(^{51}\text{Cr}) = -0.933(13)~\mu_N$, consistent with the literature value $-0.934(5)~\mu_N$.
- Hyperfine $A_{S_3}$ parameter for $^{53}$Cr: $A_{S_3}(^{53}\text{Cr}) = -82.64(16)~\text{MHz}$, agreeing with the literature value $-82.5985(15)~\text{MHz}$.
- Reference magnetic dipole moment for $^{53}$Cr: $\mu_{\text{lit}}(^{53}\text{Cr}) = -0.47454(3)~\mu_N$.
- $^{61}$Cr beam intensity: approximately 3000 ions/s, with isobaric contamination of about $2 \times 10^5$ ions/s.
- Maximum total efficiency of the laser ionization scheme: 1 per 300 for even-even chromium isotopes.
- Deformation parameters from DNO-SM: ground-state wavefunction of $^{61}$Cr centered around $(\beta, \gamma) \sim (0.23, 20^\circ)$.
- Neutron intruder orbital occupancy: 1.65 neutrons in $\nu g_{9/2}$ and $\nu d_{5/2}$ orbitals.
- Excited state energies from beta decay: 70.8(3) keV and 97.7(3) keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the N = 40 Island of Inversion, previously characterized in $^{62,64}$Cr by Gade (2021) and Crawford (2013) as dominated by 4 particle - 4 hole neutron excitations, and in $^{64}$Cr by Santamaria (2015) as the most collective chromium isotope. The measurement revises the spin assignment from the tentative $(\frac{5}{2}^-)$ proposed by Gaudefroy (2005) and Caceres (2009) based on beta-decay systematics. The results enable reinterpretation of existing beta-decay data for $^{61}$Cr and $^{61}$V, and establish $^{61}$Cr as a transitional nucleus marking a second-order quantum phase transition at the western border of the N = 40 Island of Inversion, analogous to the shape evolution described by Otsuka (2020) for the N = 60 region.
