# On the Role of Initial Conditions and Final State Interactions in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the rapidity dependence of the elliptical flow in heavy ion collisions at 200 GeV (cms), by employing a three-dimensional hydrodynamic evolution, based on different initial conditions, and different freeze-out scenarios. It will be shown that the form of pseudo-rapidity ($η$) dependence of the elliptical flow is almost identical to space-time-rapidity ($η_{s}$) dependence of the initial energy distribution, independent of the freeze-out prescriptions.

## Key claim

The shape of the pseudorapidity (η) dependence of elliptic flow v₂ is almost identical to the space-time-rapidity (ηₛ) dependence of the initial energy distribution, and this correlation holds independently of the freeze-out prescription used.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV center-of-mass energy, using two different initial conditions: a parameterized initial condition (PAR) and a microscopic string-based initial condition from the EPOS model. For each initial condition, they tested three freeze-out scenarios: pure hydrodynamic freeze-out at 100 MeV, pure hydrodynamic freeze-out at 169 MeV, and hydrodynamic freeze-out at 169 MeV followed by a hadronic cascade using UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for both PAR and EPOS initial conditions
- Central Au+Au collision impact parameter b = 2.2 fm; peripheral collision b = 8.2 fm
- Time step Δτ = 3.9 fm for PAR and 4 fm for EPOS initial conditions
- Freeze-out temperatures tested: 100 MeV (FO_100) and 169 MeV (FO_169); critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV
- Minimum bias events and centrality classes analyzed: 3-15%, 15-25%, 25-50%, and 0-40% of most central collisions

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies by Hirano (2008) which used parameterized initial conditions, and introduces a comparison with the microscopic EPOS initial condition based on string fragmentation rather than partonic degrees of freedom. The finding that the v₂ pseudorapidity shape is strongly correlated with the initial energy density distribution, while rapidity spectra are much less sensitive to initial conditions, enables future work to use v₂ rapidity dependence as a diagnostic tool for discriminating between different models of the initial state in heavy ion collisions.
