# Elliptic flow in ultra-relativistic collisions with polarized deuterons

> Predictions are made for elliptic flow in collisions of polarized deuterons with a heavy nucleus. It is shown that the eccentricity of the initial fireball, evaluated with respect to the deuteron polarization axis perpendicular to the beam direction, has a substantial magnitude for collisions of highest multiplicity. Within the Glauber approach we obtain $\sim 7\%$ for the deuteron states with spin projection 0, and $\sim -3 \%$ for spin projection $\pm 1$. We propose to measure the elliptic flow coefficient as the second order harmonic coefficient in the azimuthal distribution of produced charged hadrons with respect to the fixed polarization axis. Collective expansion yields a value of the order of $1\%$ for this quantity, as compared to zero in the absence of polarization and/or collectivity. Such a vivid rotational symmetry breaking could be measured with the current experimental accuracy of the relativistic heavy-ion experiments. The effect has a fundamental significance for understanding the nature of dynamics in small systems, as its experimental confirmation would prove the presence of the shape-flow transmutation mechanism, typical of hydrodynamic expansion or rescattering in the later stages of the fireball evolution.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that elliptic flow in polarized deuteron-heavy nucleus collisions, measured with respect to the fixed polarization axis, will be nonzero (of order 1%) for high-multiplicity events, whereas it would be zero in the absence of polarization and/or collective expansion. The effect relies on the interference term between the S-wave and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function, which produces a prolate shape for spin projection ±1 and an oblate shape for spin projection 0.

## Method

The authors use the Glauber Monte Carlo code GLISSANDO with a wounded-nucleon model plus a binary-collisions admixture to simulate d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. The initial entropy deposition is proportional to (NW/2 + a Nbin) with a = 0.145, smeared with a Gaussian of width 0.4 fm. The eccentricity of the fireball with respect to the polarization axis is computed from the deuteron wave function (Reid93 potential, 5.7% D-wave probability) and then propagated through the Glauber model to obtain the elliptic flow coefficient v2{ΦP}.

## Key numbers

- Eccentricity of the deuteron wave function itself: ε2 ≈ 0.11 for j3 = 0, ε2 ≈ −0.05 for j3 = ±1 (projected onto the transverse plane).
- For the most central d+Au collisions, the fireball ellipticities are reduced by ~30% relative to the deuteron wave-function values, yielding ε2{ΦP} at the level of a few percent.
- The predicted elliptic flow coefficient v2{ΦP} for most central collisions is of order 1%, specifically up to ~1.5% for maximal tensor polarization Pzz = −2 and down to ~−0.75% for Pzz = 1.
- With experimentally achievable deuteron polarization (−1.5 ≲ Pzz ≲ 0.7), the predicted v2{ΦP} ranges from −0.5% to 1%.
- The proportionality constant k between eccentricity and flow vectors is k ∼ 0.2 for these small systems.

## Context

This work extends the shape-flow transmutation argument previously applied to unpolarized d+Au collisions (Bozek 2011), where the elliptic deformation arises from the geometric configuration of the two nucleons. It directly addresses the ongoing debate between hydrodynamic/transport descriptions and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) explanations of collectivity in small systems (Mace 2018). The proposal enables a clean experimental test: measuring the one-particle azimuthal distribution with respect to a fixed polarization axis avoids the systematic uncertainties from non-flow effects and the need for multiparticle cumulants, and could be performed at RHIC, LHC, or lower-energy facilities such as NA61 or LHCb fixed-target runs.
