# Convergence of hydrodynamic modes: insights from kinetic theory and holography

> We study the mechanisms setting the radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations in kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. This introduces a qualitatively new feature with respect to holography: a nonhydrodynamic sector represented by a branch cut in the retarded Green's function. In contrast with existing holographic examples, we find that the radius of convergence in the shear channel is set by a collision of the hydrodynamic pole with a branch point. In the sound channel it is set by a pole-pole collision on a non-principal sheet of the Green's function. More generally, we examine the consequences of the Implicit Function Theorem in hydrodynamics and give a prescription to determine a set of points that necessarily includes all complex singularities of the dispersion relation. This may be used as a practical tool to assist in determining the radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations.

## Key claim

In relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic theory, the radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations is set by mechanisms qualitatively different from those in holography: in the shear channel it is set by a collision of the hydrodynamic pole with a branch point, while in the sound channel it is set by a pole-pole collision on a non-principal sheet of the retarded Green's function, rather than by a branch point singularity on the principal sheet as in holographic examples.

## Method

The authors analytically studied the retarded two-point functions of the stress tensor in RTA kinetic theory for massless particles, which were previously computed in closed form by Romatschke (2015). They expanded the hydrodynamic shear and sound mode dispersion relations around zero spatial momentum to high order (up to 500 terms for shear, 1000 for sound), applied ratio and root tests to determine convergence radii, and used symmetric Padé approximants to locate singularities in the complex momentum plane. They also derived ordinary differential equations for the dispersion relations from the pole conditions and solved them numerically along rays in the complex momentum plane to confirm the nature of the singularities.

## Key numbers

* Shear channel radius of convergence: \(|k_\perp^*| = 3/(2\tau)\), where \(\tau\) is the relaxation time; this corresponds to a collision between the hydrodynamic pole and a branch point at \(k = \pm 3i/2\), \(\omega = i/2\).
* Sound channel radius of convergence: \(|k_\parallel^*| \simeq 0.7410387/\tau\), determined numerically from a pole-pole collision on a non-principal sheet; the critical momentum on the \(n=1\) sheet is \(k_{c,n=1} = 0.0102873 + 0.7409673 i\) (in units where \(\tau=1\)).
* Shear channel small-\(k\) expansion: \(\omega_\perp(k) = -i k^2/5 + \mathcal{O}(k^4)\).
* Sound channel small-\(k\) expansion: \(\omega_\parallel^\pm(k) = \pm k/\sqrt{3} - 2i k^2/15 + \mathcal{O}(k^3)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic mode convergence from holographic quantum field theories (Withers, 2018; Grozdanov et al., 2019) and MIS-type models (Heller et al., 2020) to weakly-coupled kinetic theory, where a branch cut in the retarded Green's function introduces qualitatively new behavior. The results show that in RTA kinetic theory the convergence-limiting mechanism differs from holography: branch point singularities arise from hydrodynamic pole–branch point collisions (shear) or pole-pole collisions on non-principal sheets (sound), rather than from hydrodynamic–transient pole collisions on the principal sheet. The authors also provide a general prescription based on the Implicit Function Theorem to determine the set of points that necessarily includes all complex singularities of hydrodynamic dispersion relations, which may serve as a practical tool for other theories.
