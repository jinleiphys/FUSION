# Particle production and equilibrium properties within a new hadron transport approach for heavy-ion collisions

> The microscopic description of heavy-ion reactions at low beam energies is achieved within hadronic transport approaches. In this article a new approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) is introduced and applied to study the production of non-strange particles in heavy-ion reactions at $E_{\rm kin}=0.4-2A$ GeV. First, the model is described including details about the collision criterion, the initial conditions and the resonance formation and decays. To validate the approach, equilibrium properties such as detailed balance are presented and the results are compared to experimental data for elementary cross sections. Finally results for pion and proton production in C+C and Au+Au collisions is confronted with HADES and FOPI data. Predictions for particle production in $π+A$ collisions are made.

## Key claim

SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) is introduced as a new hadronic transport approach that reproduces experimental data for pion and proton production in C+C and Au+Au collisions at beam kinetic energies of 0.4-2A GeV, but the model currently neglects electromagnetic potentials and relies on vacuum resonance properties without medium modifications.

## Method

The approach solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation using a geometric collision criterion (UrQMD-type) with point-like test particles, where inelastic interactions are treated via resonance excitations and decays with vacuum properties. The model employs a grid-based nearest-neighbor search for collision finding, implements Woods-Saxon initial conditions with optional Fermi motion and Skyrme mean-field potentials, and uses either fixed time steps or dynamically determined propagation steps. Validation is performed through elastic box tests of scattering rates and comparisons with experimental elementary cross sections and heavy-ion data from HADES and FOPI.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: E_kin = 0.4-2A GeV for heavy-ion reactions
- Nuclear ground state density: rho_0 = 0.168 fm^{-3}
- Default Skyrme potential parameters: a = -209.2 MeV, b = 156.4 MeV, tau = 1.35, S_pot = 18 MeV, corresponding to incompressibility K = 240 MeV
- Minimal grid cell size: (2.5 fm)^3, corresponding to maximum cross section sigma_tot_max = pi d^2 = 200 mb
- Default Woods-Saxon diffusiveness: d = 0.545 fm (with specific values for Au: 0.535 fm, Pb: 0.54 fm, Cu: 0.597 fm, U: 0.556 fm)
- Typical Fermi momentum: p_F = 300 MeV, corresponding to energy p_F^2/(2m_N) = 45 MeV
- Time between consecutive actions in Cu-Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV: average as small as 0.01 fm/c, with outliers reaching 0.0001 fm/c
- Elastic box test temperature: T = 0.13 GeV, with constant isotropic cross sections sigma in {1, 10, 30, 50} mb and test particle numbers N_test in {1, 10, 50, 100}

## Context

This work extends the lineage of hadronic transport approaches such as UrQMD (Bass 1998), JAM (Nara 1999), GiBUU (Buss 2011), and others by providing a modern, flexible open-source code that can serve as a standard reference for purely hadronic systems with vacuum properties. The model is designed to be extensible for future inclusion of medium effects and many-body interactions, and it is intended to improve the description of the hadronic rescattering stage in hybrid approaches for RHIC and LHC energies, as well as to provide a baseline for studying the QCD phase diagram at FAIR and NICA.
