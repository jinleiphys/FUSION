# Nuclear energy density functionals from empirical ground-state densities

> A model is developed, based on the density functional perturbation theory and the inverse Kohn-Sham method, that can be used to improve relativistic nuclear energy density functionals towards an exact but unknown Kohn-Sham exchange-correlation functional. The improved functional is determined by empirical exact ground-state densities of finite systems. A test of the model and an illustrative calculation are performed, starting from two different approximate functionals, to reproduce the parameters and density dependence of a target functional, using exact ground-state densities of symmetric N=Z systems.

## Key claim

The authors develop a model combining density functional perturbation theory (DFPT) and the inverse Kohn-Sham (IKS) method that can iteratively improve a given relativistic nuclear energy density functional toward the exact but unknown Kohn-Sham exchange-correlation functional, provided the difference between the starting functional and the exact functional is small enough to be treated as a first-order perturbation.

## Method

The method assumes the exact interaction functional can be written as the known functional plus a small first-order correction, and expands the Dirac Kohn-Sham single-nucleon equations, densities, and ground-state energy to first order in the perturbation parameter. The IKS method is used to extract exact single-particle energies from empirical ground-state densities, and these energies are inserted into a derived relation that determines the first-order correction to the interaction functional. The improved functional from each iteration becomes the starting functional for the next iteration, continuing until the resulting densities reproduce the exact ground-state densities to the desired accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the DFPT+IKS approach previously applied to noble-gas atoms by Naito (2019) to the nuclear case, specifically to relativistic nuclear energy density functionals. The authors note that while the IKS method was first applied to nuclear EDFs by Accorto (2020), that implementation could not determine the spin-orbit potential from ground-state densities because the spin-orbit term is a phenomenological addition in non-relativistic functionals; relativistic functionals automatically generate the spin-orbit potential from scalar and vector self-energies. The method enables systematic improvement of approximate relativistic functionals using empirical ground-state densities of finite nuclei, with the potential to iteratively approach the exact Kohn-Sham functional.
