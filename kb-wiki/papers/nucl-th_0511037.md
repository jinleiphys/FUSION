# Excitation of Nuclear Collective States by Heavy Ions within the Model of Semi-Microscopic Optical Potential

> The (semi-)microscopic double-folding nucleus-nucleus optical potentials are suggested for consideration of inelastic scattering with excitation of collective nuclear states by using the adiabatic approach and the elastic scattering amplitude in the high-energy approximation. The analytical expression for inelastic scattering amplitude is obtained keeping the first order terms in the deformation parameter of a potential. Calculations of inelastic cross sections for the $^{17}$O heavy ions scattered on different nuclei at about hundred Mev/nucleon are made, and the acceptable qualitative agreement with the experimental data is obtained without introducing free parameters. The prospect of the method for further applications is discussed.

# Excitation of Nuclear Collective States by Heavy Ions within the Model of Semi-Microscopic Optical Potential

## Key claim

The semi-microscopic double-folding optical potential, with only two adjustable normalization parameters (N_r and N_im) fitted to elastic scattering data, can produce acceptable qualitative agreement with experimental inelastic scattering cross sections for 2+ collective state excitations without introducing additional free parameters, provided the deformation parameters of the nuclear and Coulomb potentials are related by the equality of their rms radii times deformation.

## Method

The authors constructed semi-microscopic optical potentials U(r) = N_r V^DF(r) + i N_im V^DF(r) using double-folding (DF) microscopic potentials calculated from nuclear density distributions and effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. They introduced deformation of the nuclear potential through a coordinate transformation r → r + δr, expanded to first order in the deformation parameter β_2, and derived the transition potential as -r (dU/dr) Σ_μ α_{2μ} Y_{2μ}(θ,φ). The inelastic scattering amplitude was obtained in the high-energy approximation (HEA) using the eikonal phase, keeping only first-order terms in β_2, with Coulomb distortion accounted for by replacing the impact parameter b with the turning point distance in the Coulomb field.

## Key numbers

- Normalization coefficients for the semi-microscopic potential U = N_r V^DF + i N_im V^DF at E_lab = 1435 MeV: N_r = 0.6 and N_im = 0.6 for ^60Ni; N_r = 0.6 and N_im = 0.5 for ^90Zr; N_r = 0.5 and N_im = 0.5 for ^120Sn; N_r = 0.5 and N_im = 0.8 for ^208Pb.
- Coulomb deformation parameters β^(c)_2 (set 1): 0.2067 for ^60Ni, 0.091 for ^90Zr, 0.1075 for ^120Sn, 0.0544 for ^208Pb.
- Nuclear deformation parameters β^(n)_2 (set 1, from rms radii relation): 0.2453 for ^60Ni, 0.1072 for ^90Zr, 0.1270 for ^120Sn, 0.0644 for ^208Pb.
- Nuclear deformation parameters β^(n)_2 (set 2, free fit): 0.4 for ^60Ni, 0.16 for ^90Zr, 0.25 for ^120Sn, 0.1 for ^208Pb.
- Beam energy: E_lab = 1435 MeV for ^17O projectiles (approximately 84 MeV/nucleon).

## Context

This work extends the high-energy approximation (HEA) treatment of inelastic scattering previously applied by Lukyanov, Metawei, and Zemlyanaya (2004) using phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials, now replacing those with semi-microscopic double-folding potentials. The approach builds on the double-folding model of Khoa and collaborators (1990, 2000) and the microscopic imaginary potential derived from eikonal theory by Glauber (1959) and Sitenko (1971). The method enables parameter-free or minimally-parameterized calculations of inelastic cross sections for heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies, avoiding the ambiguity problems of phenomenological optical potentials noted by Satchler (1983). Future work could apply these potentials in coupled-channel calculations that include higher-order terms in β_2, or construct microscopic transition potentials directly rather than deriving them from the optical potential.
