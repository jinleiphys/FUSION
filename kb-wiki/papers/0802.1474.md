# Omega meson production in pp collisions with a polarized beam

## Key claim
The paper derives model-independent formulae for the beam analyzing power and beam-to-meson spin transfers in $pp \to pp\omega$ using all six threshold partial wave amplitudes ($Ss$, $Sp$, $Ps$ channels), and shows that the lowest three amplitudes $f_1, f_2, f_3$ can be determined empirically without discrete ambiguities, but only if both vector and tensor polarization measurements of the $\omega$ meson are available, since the vector polarization cannot be obtained from the dominant $3\pi$ decay mode.

## Method
The authors extend a previous irreducible tensor approach (Ramachandran et al.) to include all six threshold partial wave amplitudes for $pp \to pp\omega$, expressed in both $L$-$S$ coupling ($R_k$) and $j$-$j$ coupling ($M_k$) schemes. They derive expressions for the unpolarized differential cross-section, the beam analyzing power $A_y$, and the Fano statistical tensors $t^k_q$ (vector and tensor polarizations of the $\omega$) in terms of bilinears of the partial wave amplitudes $f_k$, then show how measurements with polarized and unpolarized beams can extract the magnitudes and relative phases of $f_1, f_2, f_3$.

## Key numbers
- The total cross-section for $pp \to pp\omega$ was measured at 5 excess energies $\epsilon$ in the range 3.8 MeV to 30 MeV in c.m. (Hibou et al.)
- A strong anisotropic angular distribution was reported at $\epsilon = 173$ MeV (Abd El-Samad et al.)
- The OZI rule estimate is $R_{OZI} = 4.2 \times 10^{-3}$ (Lipkin); the latest experimental estimate is $R_{\phi/\omega} \approx 8 \times R_{OZI}$ (Hartmann et al.)
- The distances probed are estimated as 0.53 fm for $\pi$, 0.21 fm for $\omega$, and 0.18 fm for $\varphi$ production (Nakamura et al.)
- The branching ratio for $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ is 8.9%, which can be used to measure vector polarization

## Context
This work extends the model-independent irreducible tensor approach of Ramachandran et al. (2004, 2005) for $pp \to pp\omega$ from three partial wave amplitudes ($Ss$, $Sp$) to all six threshold amplitudes ($Ss$, $Sp$, $Ps$). It directly addresses the recent first measurement of the beam analyzing power $A_y$ (Abd El-Bary et al., 2007) and the ongoing program to measure beam-to-meson spin transfers (Brinkmann et al.) using the $3\pi$ decay mode. The analysis shows that the earlier measurement of $A_y$ compatible with zero at $\epsilon = 129$ MeV likely indicates a zero relative phase between $f_1$ and $f'$ rather than vanishing $f_2, f_3$, since anisotropy in the unpolarized cross-section already rules out the latter. The results enable complete empirical determination of the three lowest partial wave amplitudes using the upcoming WASA facility at COSY, which can detect the $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ decay needed for vector polarization measurements.
