# Excitation function of femtoscopic Lévy source parameters of pion pairs in EPOS4

> Three-dimensional (3D) femtoscopic source parameters of pions provide a sensitive probe of the space-time structure of particle-emitting sources in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Compared to one-dimensional measurements, three-dimensional femtoscopy not only provides a valuable cross-check but also offers a more complete characterization of the source geometry and its dynamical evolution. Particularly, differences between the out and side directions are sensitive to signals of a strong first-order phase transition, while the collision-energy dependence of Lévy radii may reveal non-monotonic features related to the equation of state. In this work, we systematically investigate the transverse mass (mT) and collision-energy (sqrt(sNN)) dependence of the three-dimensional femtoscopic parameters of pion pairs with Lévy-type sources in the STAR Beam Energy Scan (BES) range from sqrt(sNN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV using the EPOS4 model. The analyzed parameters include the Lévy index alpha, the correlation strength lambda, and the three-dimensional radii Rout, Rside and Rlong, corresponding to the outward, sideward, and longitudinal (beam) directions. Derived quantities such as the out-side squared radius difference and the out/side ratio are also investigated. The results show that the extracted radii Rside and Rlong decrease with increasing transverse mass and increase gradually with collision energy, while Rout shows little energy dependence. The Lévy index alpha exhibits only a mild dependence on mT and collision energy, whereas the correlation strength lambda shows a clear mT dependence and generally decreases with increasing collision energy. A comparison with EPOS3 results indicates general agreement within approximately 2sigma, with the notable exception of Rside, which is systematically smaller in EPOS4.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic, model-based baseline of three-dimensional Lévy femtoscopic source parameters for pion pairs across the STAR Beam Energy Scan range (sqrt(sNN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV) using EPOS4, finding that the extracted radii Rside and Rlong decrease with transverse mass and increase with collision energy, while Rout shows little energy dependence; however, the authors note that EPOS4 is less reliable below sqrt(sNN) = 19.6 GeV, and the results are presented as a non-critical theoretical reference for future experimental searches for critical point signatures.

## Method

The authors generated 4000 minimum-bias Au+Au events per collision energy using EPOS4 version 4.0.3 with the default X3FF equation of state, selected the 0-10% most central events via impact parameter cuts, and analyzed identical charged pion pairs (pi+pi+ and pi-pi-) with transverse momentum 0.15 to 1.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1. The three-dimensional pair distance distribution D(rho) was constructed in the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS) using Bertsch-Pratt coordinates, then fitted simultaneously in all three projections with a single three-dimensional Lévy-stable distribution to extract the Lévy index alpha, the three radii Rout, Rside, Rlong, and the correlation strength lambda. Systematic uncertainties were estimated by varying the number of merged events, the maximum pair momentum difference, the fit range in rho, and the centrality definition.

## Key numbers

- The Lévy index alpha shows only mild dependence on transverse mass and collision energy, with values typically between approximately 1.0 and 1.5 across all energies and mT bins
- The total relative systematic uncertainties for alpha averaged over all mT bins (0.245-0.663 GeV/c^2) range from 1.0% at 200 GeV to 1.5% at 19.6 and 27 GeV
- The total relative systematic uncertainties for Rside averaged over all mT bins range from 0.9% at 27 and 39 GeV to 1.2% at 7.7 and 14.5 GeV
- For Rlong, the average total relative systematic uncertainties range from 2.0% at 39 GeV to 2.6% at 7.7 and 9.2 GeV
- At sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV, the deviation between EPOS4 and EPOS3 for Rside corresponds to N_sigma = 2.5 to 5.8 across mT bins, with the largest deviation (N_sigma = -5.8) at mT = 0.24 GeV/c^2
- The correlation strength lambda generally decreases with increasing collision energy, with average total relative systematic uncertainties of 0.5-0.7% across all energies
- For mT below 0.7 GeV/c^2, systematic uncertainties fluctuate around 0.5-3%; above 0.7 GeV/c^2, uncertainties reach up to 13%

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional Lévy femtoscopy framework developed by Kincses et al. (2024) and the EPOS3-based analysis by Kincses et al. (2025) to the newer EPOS4 event generator, providing a non-critical theoretical baseline for interpreting future experimental data from the STAR Beam Energy Scan. The study is motivated by predictions from Csanád (2025) that the Lévy index and the out-side radius difference may show non-monotonic energy dependence near the QCD critical point, and the results enable comparison with upcoming three-dimensional femtoscopic measurements from STAR and other experiments. The paper also notes that EPOS4 implements a fully self-consistent parallel scattering scheme compared to EPOS3, leading to systematically smaller Rside values with deviations of 2.5-5.8 sigma.
