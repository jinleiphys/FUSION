# How to Measure Squeeze Out

> Squeeze out happen when the expanding central fireball flows around a large surface flux tube in a central Au-Au collision at RHIC. We model such an effect in a flux tube model. Two particle correlations with respect to the $v_2$ axis formed by the soft fireball particles flowing around this large flux tube is a way of measuring the effect.

## Key claim

Squeeze out occurs when the expanding central fireball in a central Au-Au collision at RHIC flows hydrodynamically around a large surface flux tube, and this effect can be measured via two-particle correlations with respect to the $v_2$ axis formed by the soft fireball particles flowing around that tube.

## Method

The author modifies an existing flux tube model for central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200.0$ GeV by manually adding a hydrodynamical squeeze-out flow pattern around the largest flux tube. Soft particle momentum vectors are adjusted to create flow along the Y axis, at $\pm45^\circ$, and a $180^\circ$ particle of roughly twice the momentum for momentum conservation, with final adjustments to conserve momentum in X and Y. The simulation is run over many central events, and two-particle azimuthal correlation functions are computed with respect to the $v_2$ reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Central Au-Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200.0$ GeV is the system simulated.
- The global $v_3$ is pointed in the minus X direction ($180^\circ$ direction) in the simulation.
- The $v_2$ reaction plane lies along the Y axis, at $90^\circ$ to the largest flux tube.
- The correlation $\langle \cos(3\phi_1 - \phi_2(p_t) - 2\Psi_{RP})\rangle$ shows a sign change from lower $p_t$ to higher $p_t$, indicating $v_1$ shifts from aligned with global $v_3$ at low $p_t$ to anti-aligned at high $p_t$.
- The correlation $\langle \cos(\phi_1 + \phi_2(p_t) - 2\Psi_{RP})\rangle$ shows no sign change from lower to higher $p_t$, but a shift toward positive values.

## Context

This work extends the flux tube model of Longacre (2015) for central Au-Au collisions at RHIC, which assumed spherically symmetric blast waves from locally thermalized systems. The squeeze-out effect was previously simulated under the name "flux tube shadowing" by Longacre (2015), which predicted a strong $v_3$ azimuthal correlation. The present paper adds a hydrodynamical squeeze-out flow around the largest flux tube to that model and proposes specific two-particle correlation measurements that could detect this effect experimentally at RHIC.
