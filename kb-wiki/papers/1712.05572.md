# The three-pion decays of the $a_1(1260)$

> We investigate the decay of $a^+_1(1260) \to 蟺^+ 蟺^+ 蟺^-$ with the assumption that the $a_1(1260)$ is dynamically generated from the coupled channel $蟻蟺$ and $\bar{K}K^*$ interactions. In addition to the tree level diagrams that proceed via $a^+_1(1260) \to 蟻^0 蟺^+ \to 蟺^+蟺^+ 蟺^-$, we take into account also the final state interactions of $蟺蟺\to 蟺蟺$ and $K \bar{K} \to 蟺蟺$. We calculate the invariant $蟺^+ 蟺^-$ mass distribution and also the total decay width of $a^+_1(1260) \to 蟺^+ 蟺^+ 蟺^-$ as a function of the mass of $a_1(1260)$. The calculated total decay width of $a_1(1260)$ is significantly different from other model calculations and tied to the dynamical nature of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance. The future experimental observations could test of model calculations and would provide vary valuable information on the relevance of the $蟻蟺$ component in the $a_1(1260)$ wave function.

# The three-pion decays of the $a_1(1260)$

## Key claim

The $a_1(1260)$ resonance, assumed to be dynamically generated from coupled $\rho\pi$ and $\bar{K}K^*$ interactions, decays to three pions predominantly via an intermediate $\rho$ meson; the calculated total decay width of $a_1(1260) \to 3\pi$ differs significantly from other model calculations (e.g., Achasov (2004) obtained 860 MeV at $M_{a_1}=1230$ MeV, while this work finds 166 MeV), and the result depends on the dynamical nature of the resonance rather than a simple quark-model interpretation.

## Method

The authors compute the decay $a^+_1(1260) \to \pi^+ \pi^+ \pi^-$ using tree-level diagrams where $a_1(1260) \to \rho^0 \pi^+$ followed by $\rho^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-$, with the $a_1\rho\pi$ coupling taken from the chiral unitary approach of Roca (2005). They additionally include triangular-loop diagrams for final-state interactions of $\pi\pi \to \pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K} \to \pi\pi$, which dynamically generate the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances, using meson-meson scattering amplitudes from the chiral unitary approach of Oller (1997). The invariant $\pi^+ \pi^-$ mass distribution and the total decay width as a function of $a_1(1260)$ mass are calculated and compared to experimental data from $\tau \to \pi^- \pi^- \pi^+ \nu_\tau$ decays (Albrecht, 1992).

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants from Roca (2005): $g_{a_1 \rho \pi} = (-3795 + i2330)$ MeV and $g_{a_1 \bar{K} K^*} = (1872 - i1468)$ MeV.
- Total decay width $\Gamma = 166$ MeV at $M_{a_1} = 1230$ MeV for $a^+_1(1260) \to \pi^+ \pi^+ \pi^-$.
- Cutoff parameter for $\rho$ form factor fixed at $\Lambda_\rho = 1500$ MeV; results insensitive to its variation.
- $\rho$ meson mass taken as $m_\rho = 775.26$ MeV; $\Gamma_{\rm on} = 149.1$ MeV for $\rho$ decay.
- Pion mass $m_\pi = 139.57$ MeV; cutoff for loop integrals $q_{\rm max} = 630$ MeV.
- PDG values: $M_{a_1(1260)} = 1230 \pm 40$ MeV; COMPASS measurement gives $\Gamma_{a_1(1260)} = 367 \pm 9 ^{+28} _{-25}$ MeV and $M_{a_1(1260)} = 1255 \pm 6^{+7}_{-17}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation picture of the $a_1(1260)$ from coupled-channel $\rho\pi$ and $\bar{K}K^*$ interactions (Roca, 2005) to its three-pion decay, providing a test of the molecular interpretation against the quark-model description. The results differ substantially from the calculation of Achasov (2004), who obtained $\Gamma_{a_1} = 860$ MeV at the same mass using a different approach. The calculated $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant mass distribution agrees fairly with experimental $\tau$ decay data (Albrecht, 1992), supporting the dynamical picture, and the predicted energy dependence of the width can be tested by future precise measurements of the $a_1(1260)$ mass and width, such as those from COMPASS (Alekseev, 2009).
