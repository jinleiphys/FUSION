# Relativistic Theory of Few-Body Systems

> Very significant advances have been made in the relativistic theory of few body systems since I visited Peter Sauer and his group in Hannover in 1983. This talk provides an opportunity to review the progress in this field since then. Different methods for the realtivistic calculation of few nucleon systems are briefly described. As an example, seven relativistic calculations of the deuteron elastic structure functions, $A$, $B$, and $T_{20}$, are compared. The covariant spectator theory, among the more successful and complete of these methods, is described in more detail.

## Key claim

Seven relativistic calculations of deuteron elastic structure functions are compared, and only the VODG (covariant spectator theory) and SPR (instant-form Hamiltonian dynamics without v/c expansion) provide a reasonable description of all three observables (A, B, T20), likely because they are the only calculations valid to all orders in (v/c)^2 that also include complete currents consistent with the dynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares seven relativistic calculations of deuteron elastic structure functions, which fall into two major schools: Greens function dynamics (manifestly covariant approaches) and Hamiltonian dynamics (instant-form, front-form, and point-form). The covariant spectator theory is described in more detail, including its propagator that places one nucleon on its positive energy mass-shell, its method for constructing consistent electromagnetic currents via the Ward-Takahashi identity, and its application to the three-nucleon bound state.

## Key numbers

- The VODG (spectator theory) calculation uses a dipole form factor for F3 with Λ² = 0.71 GeV² as the "standard" case
- A tripole form factor for F3 with Λ² = 5 GeV² gives an excellent description of all elastic deuteron observables
- The ρπγ exchange current uses a dipole form factor with Λ² = 1.5 GeV² in one model comparison
- The spectator theory NN scattering fit to 350 MeV lab kinetic energy achieves χ² ~ 2 with 13 fitting parameters
- The three-body binding energy calculation for ³H shows good agreement with experiment using the same off-shell coupling parameter ν that gives the best fit to two-body data

## Context

This review extends the comparison of relativistic approaches to few-nucleon systems that began with work by Friar and others in the 1970s and 1980s, who found that one-pion exchange corrections for instant-form and spectator theory agreed to lowest order only after all corrections were added together. The paper enables future benchmark calculations comparing different relativistic methods, and shows that the deuteron data can determine the previously unknown form factor F3 in the spectator theory, which can then be tested against other electron scattering observables. The work builds on Dirac's (1949) classification of Hamiltonian dynamics forms and the author's own prior development of the spectator theory (Gross, 1982) and consistent current construction (Gross and Riska, 1987).
