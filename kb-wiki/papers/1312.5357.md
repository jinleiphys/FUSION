# Charged-current quasielastic neutrino cross sections on $^{12}$C with realistic spectral and scaling functions

> Charge-current quasielastic (CCQE) (anti)neutrino scattering cross sections on a $^{12}$C target are analyzed using a spectral function $S(p,{\cal E})$ that gives a scaling function in accordance with the ($e,e'$) scattering data. The spectral function accounts for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, it has a realistic energy dependence and natural orbitals (NO's) from the Jastrow correlation method are used in its construction. In all calculations the standard value of the axial mass $M_A= 1.032$ GeV/c$^2$ is used. The results are compared with those when NN correlations are not included, as in the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, or when harmonic-oscillator (HO) single-particle wave functions are used instead of NO's. The role of the final-state interactions (FSI) on the theoretical spectral and scaling functions, as well as on the cross sections is accounted for. A comparison of the results for the cases with and without FSI, as well as to results from the phenomenological scaling function obtained from the superscaling analysis (SuSA) is carried out. Our calculations based on the impulse approximation (IA) underpredict the MiniBooNE data, but agree with the data from the NOMAD experiment. The possible missing ingredients in the considered theoretical models are discussed.

# Charged-current quasielastic neutrino cross sections on $^{12}$C with realistic spectral and scaling functions

## Key claim
The authors' impulse approximation (IA) calculations using a realistic spectral function that accounts for nucleon-nucleon correlations and final-state interactions under-predict the MiniBooNE CCQE neutrino cross section data on $^{12}$C, but agree with the NOMAD experiment data, using the standard axial mass $M_A = 1.032$ GeV/c$^2$ without requiring an anomalously large value.

## Method
The authors constructed a realistic spectral function $S(p,\mathcal{E})$ for $^{12}$C using natural orbitals from the Jastrow correlation method with a Lorentzian energy dependence having widths $\Gamma_{1p}=6$ MeV and $\Gamma_{1s}=20$ MeV fixed to experimental data. They computed CCQE (anti)neutrino cross sections within the impulse approximation, incorporating final-state interactions through a complex optical potential that replaces the energy-conserving delta function with a Lorentzian form, and compared results from the RFG, harmonic-oscillator, and natural-orbital spectral functions with and without FSI.

## Key numbers
- Standard axial mass used throughout: $M_A = 1.032$ GeV/c$^2$
- Lorentzian widths for spectral function: $\Gamma_{1p} = 6$ MeV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 20$ MeV for $^{12}$C
- Scaling function calculations performed at momentum transfer $q = 1$ GeV/c and initial electron energy $\varepsilon = 1$ GeV
- Total cross sections calculated up to 100 GeV for comparison with NOMAD data
- Fermi constant: $G = 1.16639 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$; Cabibbo angle: $\cos\theta_c = 0.9741$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SuSA-based analysis (Ivanov et al., 2012) by replacing the phenomenological scaling function with a realistic spectral function constructed in earlier work (PhysRevC.83.045504) that accounts for NN correlations beyond the mean-field approximation. The results contradict the need for an enhanced axial mass $M_A \approx 1.35$ GeV/c$^2$ suggested by simple RFG fits to MiniBooNE data, and instead support the interpretation that multi-nucleon excitations (2p-2h meson exchange currents) are the missing ingredient, as proposed by Amaro et al. (2011) and Nieves et al. (2012). The framework enables consistent analysis across the full energy range from MiniBooNE (1 GeV region) to NOMAD (up to 100 GeV).
