# Fractional vorticity, Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield systems and complex structures for the (generalized) spinor Gross-Pitaevskii equations

> The (generalized) Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) for a complex scalar field in two spatial dimensions is analyzed. It is shown that there is an infinite family of self-interaction potentials which admit Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) bounds together with the corresponding first-order BPS systems. For each member of this family, the solutions of the first-order BPS systems are automatically solutions of the corresponding second-order generalized GPE. The simplest topologically non-trivial solutions of these first-order BPS systems describe configurations with quantized fractional vorticity. The corresponding fraction is related to the degree of non-linearity. The case in which the self-interaction potential is of order six (namely $|Ψ|^{6}$, which is a relevant theory both in relativistic quantum field theories in $(2+1)$ dimensions in connection with the quantum Hall effect as well as in the theory of the supersolids) is analyzed in detail. Such formalism can also be extended to the case of quantum mixtures with multi-component GPEs. The relationship between these techniques and supersymmetry will be discussed. In particular, despite several common features, we will show that there are multi-component GPEs that are not supersymmetric (at least, not in the standard sense) and possess a BPS system of the above type.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an infinite family of self-interaction potentials for the (generalized) Gross-Pitaevskii equation in two spatial dimensions admits Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) bounds and first-order BPS systems, with the caveat that the simplest topologically nontrivial solutions describe configurations with quantized fractional vorticity whose fraction is tied to the degree of nonlinearity of the potential.

## Method

The authors construct a first-order elliptic system for two real scalar fields in two spatial dimensions, where the right-hand sides are the real and imaginary parts of an analytic superpotential \(W(Z)\) of the complex field \(Z = \Phi_1 + i\Phi_2\). By imposing that the second-order field equations contain no first derivatives (the Cauchy-Riemann conditions in field space), the system reduces to a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation. The method is applied to superpotentials of the form \(W \propto Z^2\) (quartic interaction), \(W \propto Z^{5/2}\) (fifth-order interaction), and \(W \propto Z^3\) (sixth-order interaction), yielding explicit BPS systems and their fractional-vorticity solutions.

## Key numbers

* For the \(|\Psi|^4\) interaction (quadratic superpotential \(W = \frac{\kappa}{\sqrt{2}} Z^2\)): the simplest topologically nontrivial solution has vorticity \(1/3\), with amplitude \(\rho(r) = \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{2\sqrt{2} A r^{1/3} - 3\kappa r}\).
* For the \(|\Psi|^5\) interaction (superpotential \(W = \frac{\kappa}{\sqrt{5/2}} Z^{5/2}\)): the fractional vorticity is \(2/7\), with amplitude \(\rho(r) = (A r^{3/7} - \frac{21}{4\sqrt{10}} \kappa r)^{-2/3}\).
* For the \(|\Psi|^6\) interaction (superpotential \(W = \frac{\kappa}{\sqrt{3}} Z^3\)): the fractional vorticity is \(1/4\), with amplitude \(\rho(r) = (A r^{1/2} - \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \kappa r)^{-1/2}\) and effective coupling \(g_{eff} = \kappa\).
* For the \(|\Psi|^6\) case, the topological charge \(Q_1\) evaluated on an annulus with outer radius \(R\) and inner radius \(a\) gives \(Q_1 = -\frac{\pi \hbar^2}{4M (A R^{1/2} - \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \kappa R)} + \frac{\pi \hbar^2}{4M (A a^{1/2} - \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \kappa a)}\).

## Context

This work extends the recent discovery of a BPS system for the standard Gross-Pitaevskii equation with quartic interaction (Canfora and Pais, 2024) to a whole family of nonlinear potentials, including the fifth-order potential relevant to supersolids (Lee-Huang-Yang theory) and the sixth-order potential relevant to the Tonks-Girardeau limit and to relativistic Chern-Simons theories in (2+1) dimensions (Jackiw and Pi, 1990; Dunne, 1999). The formalism enables the construction of fractional-vorticity configurations without extra ingredients such as gauge fields, and it can be extended to multi-component (spinor) Gross-Pitaevskii equations for quantum mixtures, including cases that are not supersymmetric in the standard sense.
