# Charmed hadron production via equal-velocity quark combination in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Recent data on the production of $D$ mesons and $Λ_c^+$ baryons in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider exhibit a number of striking characteristics such as enhanced yield ratios $D_s^+/D^0$, $Λ_c^+/D^0$ and their transverse momentum dependences. In this paper, we derive the momentum dependence of open charm mesons and singly charmed baryons produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions via the equal-velocity quark combination. We present analytic expressions and numerical results of yield ratios and compare them with the experimental data available. We make predictions for other charmed hadrons.

## Key claim

The equal-velocity quark combination (EVC) model can describe the transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependence of charmed hadron yield ratios in heavy ion collisions, provided that the observed enhancement of strange-to-non-strange and baryon-to-meson ratios arises not only from the strangeness suppression factor $\lambda_s$ but also from the shape of the light-quark $p_T$ spectra at low $p_T$.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the $p_T$-dependent production of open charm mesons ($D^0$, $D^+$, $D_s^+$) and singly charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c^+$, $\Sigma_c$, $\Xi_c$, $\Omega_c^0$) using the equal-velocity quark combination formalism, where quarks and anti-quarks combine with equal velocity into hadrons. They incorporated strong and electromagnetic decays of short-lived resonances (e.g., $D^*$, $\Sigma_c$, $J^P=(3/2)^+$ baryons) and applied the model to Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC, using light-quark $p_T$ spectra extracted from data on $\Xi^-$ and $\phi$ mesons and charm quark spectra from perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u=m_d=0.33$ GeV, $m_s=0.5$ GeV, $m_c=1.5$ GeV.
- Strangeness suppression factor $\lambda_s$ ranges from 0.49 (0-10% centrality) to 0.44 (60-80% centrality) in Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- Light-quark temperature parameters: $T_d$ from 0.27 GeV (0-10%) to 0.23 GeV (60-80%); $T_s$ from 0.34 GeV (0-10%) to 0.32 GeV (60-80%).
- Charm quark temperature $T_c=0.45$ GeV for 0-10% to 20-40% centrality, 0.44 GeV for 60-80%.
- Vector-to-pseudo-scalar meson production ratio taken as 1.5; $J^P=(1/2)^+$ to $J^P=(3/2)^+$ baryon ratio taken as 2.
- After decay contributions, about 80% of $\Lambda_c^+$ baryons come from decays, only about 20% are directly produced.
- Final-state enhancement factors from decays: $f_{D^0}^{(fin)} \approx 3.516 f_{D^0}$, $f_{D_s^+}^{(fin)} \approx 2.5 f_{D_s^+}$, $f_{\Lambda_c^+}^{(fin)} \approx 5 f_{\Lambda_c^+}$.

## Context

This work extends the equal-velocity quark combination model, previously applied successfully to light and strange hadron production in $pp$ and $p$-Pb collisions by Li et al. (2018) and Song et al. (2018), to charmed hadrons in heavy ion collisions. It addresses the observed enhancement of $D_s^+/D^0$ and $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratios at RHIC and LHC, which fragmentation models (e.g., those in Fragmentation 2018) fail to describe. The results enable predictions for other charmed hadrons ($\Xi_c$, $\Omega_c^0$) and provide a way to probe light-quark $p_T$ spectra in the quark-gluon plasma.
