# On the flow of kaons produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the different contributions to the in-plane flow of K+ mesons observed recently by the FOPI collaboration in the reaction Ni(1.93 AGeV)+Ni. Due to the kinematics of the three body phase space decay the flow of kaons produced in baryon-baryon interactions is smaller than that of the baryons in the entrance channel. On the contrary, in pi N interactions the flow of the sources and of the kaons are identical. Therefore the total kaon flow depends on the relative number of Delta N -> K+ and pi N -> K+ reactions and hence on the lifetime of the Delta, in addition to the already known dependence on the potential interaction interaction of the kaons with the nuclear environment.

# On the flow of kaons produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The small in-plane flow of K+ mesons observed by the FOPI collaboration in Ni(1.93 AGeV)+Ni collisions is primarily a consequence of production kinematics, the three-body phase space decay of baryon-baryon collisions and the reduced flow of nucleons that have passed through the high-density zone, rather than a direct signature of the kaon potential interaction with nuclear matter, contrary to earlier speculation.

## Method
The authors employed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations of the Ni+Ni reaction at 1.93 AGeV, implementing kaon production cross sections for πB → YK and BB → BYK channels (with B = N, Δ and Y = Λ, Σ) and a kaon optical potential with scalar and vector components. They compared the directed in-plane flow (defined as the average p_x weighted by the sign of the center-of-mass rapidity) of kaons, their production sources, and nucleons, and studied the sensitivity to the Δ resonance lifetime prescription and to the scalar and vector parts of the kaon potential.

## Key numbers
- The threshold for kaon production in NN collisions is 1.583 GeV.
- The in-plane flow of all nucleons reaches asymptotically 80 MeV/c at an impact parameter of 2 fm.
- Nucleons that have passed a density of ρ/ρ₀ ≥ 2 have a considerably smaller average flow than the average nucleon.
- 80% of kaons are produced at a density above normal nuclear matter density, and 60% do not rescatter at a density lower than normal nuclear matter density.
- The kaon rescattering cross section is approximately 12 mb.
- The experimental cut limits particles to a transverse momentum larger than 250 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Ko (1997) and collaborators who proposed that kaon in-plane flow is directly related to the relative strength of scalar and vector potentials in nuclear matter. The authors show that kinematic effects from the three-body phase space decay and the reduced flow of high-density nucleons, previously noted by Jae (1997), dominate the observed kaon flow, and that the Δ resonance lifetime (using the formulation of Hirsch (1995) versus the Breit-Wigner approach of Huber (1995)) significantly modifies the flow. The results contradict the earlier interpretation by Ko (1997) and also differ from the findings of Fuchs (1997) regarding the effect of the vector potential. The work enables future disentangling of kaon production mechanisms, in-medium Δ lifetime, and kaon-nucleon interactions from flow data.
