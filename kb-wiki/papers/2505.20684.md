# Integrable Non-Holonomic Constraints and Gauge Fixing in Classical Field Theory

> We show how the usual derivation of the equations of motion for a classical field theory with non-holonomic constraints, constraints that depend on the derivatives of the field, fails. As a result, the usual method for gauge fixing in classical and quantum field theories fails for general gauges that depend on the derivatives of the fields, such as the Coulomb and Lorenz gauges. The point of failure occurs at the use of the transposition rule, $δ(\partial_νA^μ)=\partial_ν(δA^μ)$, in the derivation of the equations of motion from the extremization of the action; we show that the transposition rule does not hold for general non-holonomic constraints. We define the concept of integrability of non-holonomic constraints in field theory and prove that the usual transposition rule holds for theories with these constraints. We are thus able to recover the usual treatment of gauge fixing for gauges of this type. We apply our formalism to the specific examples of the Coulomb and Lorenz gauges.

## Key claim

The usual derivation of equations of motion from Hamilton's principle fails for classical field theories with non-holonomic constraints (constraints involving field derivatives) because the transposition rule δ(∂_ν A^μ) = ∂_ν(δA^μ) does not hold for general such constraints; however, the transposition rule does hold for "integrable" non-holonomic constraints, which include the Coulomb and Lorenz gauges but exclude gauges such as the 't Hooft-Veltman gauge.

## Method

The authors define a non-holonomic constraint g[∂_ν A^μ](x) = 0 as integrable if it can be expressed as the covariant divergence of a vector functional f^ν[A^μ](x) that depends only on the field components (not their derivatives), with one specially singled-out "dependent" field component A^{μ_D} satisfying f^{μ_D} = A^{μ_D} and the remaining f^ν independent of A^{μ_D}. They then develop a functional calculus with Gâteaux derivatives and chain rules for function-valued functionals, and prove the transposition rule separately for the independent field components (μ ≠ μ_D) and for the dependent component (μ = μ_D) under this integrability condition.

## Key numbers

- The paper defines the spacetime manifold as M = [t_i, t_f] × R^{d-1} with flat metric η^{μν} = diag(1, -1, ..., -1), where d is the number of spacetime dimensions.
- The dependent field component A^{μ_D} is expressed as an integral from -∞ to x^{μ_D} of the functional B, with the condition that A^{μ_D} vanishes as x^{μ_D} → -∞.
- The test functions γ_i used in the functional derivative definition are required to vanish at the temporal boundaries: γ_i(x) = 0 for all x ∈ {t_i, t_f}.

## Context

This work extends Flannery's (2011) demonstration that the transposition rule fails for non-holonomic constraints in point-particle mechanics to the context of classical field theory. The authors show that standard gauge-fixing treatments for the Coulomb and Lorenz gauges (used ubiquitously in works by Dirac (1964), Peskin, Weinberg, and others) are justified because these gauges constitute integrable non-holonomic constraints under the authors' definition. The paper identifies the 't Hooft-Veltman gauge as a non-integrable non-holonomic constraint where the standard application of Hamilton's principle is expected to fail, opening the question of how to properly treat such gauges.
