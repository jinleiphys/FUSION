# Comparison of nuclear hamiltonians using spectral function sum rules

> Background: The energy weighted sum rules of the single-particle spectral functions provide a quantitative understanding of the fragmentation of nuclear states due to short-range and tensor correlations. Purpose: The aim of this paper is to compare on a quantitative basis the single-particle spectral function generated by different nuclear hamiltonians in symmetric nuclear matter using the first three energy-weighted moments. Method: The spectral functions are calculated in the framework of the self-consistent Green's function approach at finite temperature within a ladder resummation scheme. We analyze the first three moments of the spectral function and connect these to the correlations induced by the interactions between the nucleons. In particular, the variance of the spectral function is directly linked to the dispersive contribution of the self-energy. The discussion is centred around two- and three-body chiral nuclear interactions, with and without renormalization, but we also provide results obtained with the traditional phase-shift-equivalent CD-Bonn and Av18 potentials. Results: The variance of the spectral function is particularly sensitive to the short-range structure of the force, with hard-core interactions providing large variances. Chiral forces yield variances which are an order of magnitude smaller and, when tamed using the similarity renormalization group, the variance reduces significantly and in proportion to the renormalization scale. The presence of three-body forces does not substantially affect the results. Conclusions: The first three moments of the spectral function are useful tools in analysing the importance of correlations in nuclear ground states. In particular, the second-order moment provides a direct insight into dispersive contributions to the self-energy and its value is indicative of the fragmentation of single-particle states.

## Key claim

The first three energy-weighted moments of the single-particle spectral function in symmetric nuclear matter provide a quantitative tool to compare different nuclear Hamiltonians, with the variance (second moment) being particularly sensitive to the short-range structure of the nuclear force and the degree of fragmentation of single-particle states, though these moments are not observables in the strict sense and depend on the underlying Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors computed single-particle spectral functions in symmetric nuclear matter using the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach at finite temperature (T=5 MeV) within a ladder resummation scheme, employing partial wave decompositions up to J=4 (J=8) in the dispersive (Hartree-Fock) contribution. They analyzed the first three energy-weighted moments of the spectral function for two- and three-body chiral nuclear interactions (N3LO and N3LO+SRG), with and without renormalization, as well as for the traditional phase-shift-equivalent CD-Bonn and Av18 potentials, all at a single density of ρ=0.2 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic energy per particle at ρ=0.20 fm⁻³ and T=5 MeV: free Fermi gas value is 27.1 MeV; N3LO+SRG+3NF yields 29.4 MeV; N3LO+3NF yields 36.7 MeV; CD-Bonn yields 38.9 MeV; Av18 yields 47.1 MeV.
- For the first moment m⁽¹⁾_k at k=0: N3LO 2NF-only gives approximately -70 MeV; N3LO+3NF gives approximately -31 MeV; SRG-evolved N3LO gives approximately -118 MeV; SRG-evolved N3LO+3NF gives approximately -83 MeV.
- The m^>_k contribution below k_F: approximately 20 MeV for N3LO 2NF; approximately 25 MeV for N3LO+3NF; approximately 0 MeV for SRG-evolved N3LO; approximately 35 MeV for CD-Bonn; approximately 82 MeV for Av18.
- For the second moment m⁽²⁾_k near k≈0: approximately (1-2)×10⁴ MeV² for all chiral forces; approximately (0.8-1.5)×10⁵ MeV² for CD-Bonn and Av18.
- The minimum of m⁽²⁾_k around k≈300 MeV: approximately 10⁴ MeV² for unrenormalized chiral interactions; approximately 10³ MeV² for SRG-renormalized interactions.
- The zeroth moment sum rule m⁽⁰⁾_k=1 is fulfilled to better than 0.1% for all interactions and momenta.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of energy-weighted sum rules in infinite nuclear matter by Polls (1994), Frick (2004), and Rios (2006), and in finite nuclei by Duguet (2012, 2015), by systematically comparing chiral effective field theory interactions (Entem-Machleidt N3LO) with traditional phenomenological potentials (CD-Bonn and Av18). The analysis demonstrates that the variance of the spectral function provides a direct link to dispersive contributions in the self-energy and quantifies the fragmentation of single-particle states, enabling future studies to use these sum rules as benchmarks for nuclear interactions and many-body methods beyond bulk saturation properties.
