# Freeze-out conditions from strangeness observables at RHIC

> We determine chemical freeze-out conditions from strangeness observables measured at RHIC beam energies. Based on a combined analysis of lowest-order net-Kaon fluctuations and strange anti-baryon over baryon yield ratios we obtain visibly enhanced freeze-out conditions at high beam energies compared to previous studies which analyzed net-proton and net-charge fluctuations. Our findings are in qualitative agreement with the recent study [1] which utilizes the net-Kaon fluctuation data in combination with information from lattice QCD. Our complimentary approach shows that also strange hadron yield ratios are described by such enhanced freeze-out conditions.

## Key claim
Chemical freeze-out conditions extracted from a combined analysis of net-Kaon fluctuations and strange anti-baryon over baryon yield ratios show visibly enhanced temperatures at high beam energies compared to previous studies using net-proton and net-charge fluctuations, with the enhancement being most pronounced at the largest collision energies.

## Method
The authors performed a combined fit of lowest-order net-Kaon fluctuations (variance over mean, σ²_K/M_K) and strange anti-baryon over baryon yield ratios (Λ̄/Λ, Ξ⁻/Ξ⁺, Ω⁻/Ω⁺) measured by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC beam energies from √s = 11.5 to 200 GeV. They used a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with an updated Particle Data Group (2016) particle list, implementing experimental kinematic acceptance cuts and explicit resonance decay contributions including the probabilistic nature of decay processes. The analysis enforced net-strangeness neutrality and a fixed proton-to-baryon ratio of approximately 0.4 to determine the chemical potentials μ_S and μ_Q as functions of temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperatures at √s = 200 GeV are visibly enhanced compared to the results of Alba (2014), which used net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations; the enhancement is largest at the highest beam energies and diminishes toward lower √s
- The baryon chemical potential μ_B is also visibly enhanced compared to Alba (2014) except at the smallest beam energy √s = 11.5 GeV
- Neglecting the probabilistic nature of resonance decays reduces the determined freeze-out temperature by about 5% and underestimates μ_B by up to 18%
- Resonance decays reduce the lowest-order net-Kaon fluctuation measure σ²_K/M_K by about 15% from its Skellam limit for the same thermal parameters
- The electric charge chemical potential μ_Q is negative and negligibly small compared to μ_B; the strangeness chemical potential μ_S constitutes a non-negligible fraction of μ_B

## Context
This work extends the approach of Alba (2014), which determined freeze-out conditions from net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations, by instead using strangeness observables. The results are in qualitative agreement with the recent study by Bellwied (2018), which used net-Kaon fluctuation data supplemented by lattice QCD information. The authors show that strange hadron yield ratios can be described by the same enhanced freeze-out conditions, providing a complementary determination. The work enables future studies incorporating dynamical effects, critical fluctuations, and canonical ensemble formulations to refine the freeze-out conditions.
