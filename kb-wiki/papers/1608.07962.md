# The first self-consistent calculation of quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei accounting for phonon induced corrections

> The self-consistent model, developed previously to describe phonon coupling (PC) effects in magnetic moments of odd magic and semi-magic nuclei, is extended to quadrupole moments. It is based on the theory of finite Fermi systems with the use of the perturbation theory in $g_L^2$, where $g_L$ is the vertex creating the $L$-phonon. Accounting for the phonon tadpole diagrams is an important ingredient of this model. The calculation scheme is based on the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a and does not contain any adjusted parameters. The odd In and Sb isotopes are considered, which are the proton-odd neighbors of even tin nuclei. The $2^+_1$ phonon is taken into account which quadrupole moment is one ingredient of the calculation scheme. The corresponding values were found by us previously. Two main PC corrections, due to the phonon $Z$-factor and due to the phonon-induced interaction, have opposite signs and cancel strongly each other, leaving room for other `small' corrections, so that the resulting PC correction is much lower than the absolute values of each of two main ones. However, it remains noticeable, making the overall agreement with the data significantly better.

# The first self-consistent calculation of quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei accounting for phonon induced corrections

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed self-consistent model for phonon coupling (PC) corrections in magnetic moments to quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei, finding that while the two main PC corrections (from the phonon Z-factor and phonon-induced interaction) have opposite signs and largely cancel, the remaining net correction is still noticeable and improves agreement with experimental data. The model contains no adjusted parameters beyond those already present in the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a.

## Method

The calculation uses the theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with perturbation theory in g_L^2, where g_L is the vertex creating the L-phonon. The self-consistent Fayans energy density functional DF3-a generates the nuclear potential well and effective NN force. The authors consider only the 2^+_1 phonon and compute four PC corrections: the phonon Z-factor correction, the phonon-induced interaction correction (GGD), the correction from direct action of the external E2 field on the phonon (GDD), and the non-diagonal end correction. The final quadrupole moment is obtained from the ansatz involving the Z-factor renormalization of the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For the 18 nuclei considered, the rms deviation between theoretical predictions and experimental values is 0.15 b with PC corrections, compared to 0.27 b without PC corrections.
- The Z-factor values for In isotopes range from 0.488 (for 111In) to 0.800 (for 127In), with several cases near 0.5 where perturbation theory becomes questionable.
- For 105In, the quadrupole moment changes from +0.833 b (mean-field) to 0.76 b (with PC corrections), compared to experimental value +0.83(5) b.
- For 115Sb, the quadrupole moment changes from -0.882 b (mean-field) to -0.62 b (with PC corrections), compared to experimental value -0.36(6) b.
- The 2^+_1 phonon excitation energies in even Sn isotopes range from 1.130 MeV (112Sn) to 1.485 MeV (128Sn) theoretically, compared to experimental values from 1.132 MeV (124Sn) to 1.300 MeV (114Sn).
- The quadrupole moments of 2^+_1 phonons in even Sn isotopes range from -0.50 b (110Sn) to +0.04 b (120Sn) theoretically.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent TFFS approach for magnetic moments (Saperstein et al., 2015; Kamerdzhiev et al., 2015) to the quadrupole moment case. It builds on mean-field calculations of quadrupole moments in odd In and Sb isotopes (Saperstein et al., 2014; Tolokonnikov et al., 2015) and on self-consistent calculations of 2^+_1 phonon properties in even Sn isotopes (Saperstein et al., 2016). The approach differs from shell-model calculations (for A<90) which use effective charges fitted per nuclear shell, as the present method is fully self-consistent with no adjustable parameters. The results enable future development of a more consistent treatment beyond perturbation theory for cases with small energy denominators, as initiated for single-particle energies (Saperstein et al., 2016).
