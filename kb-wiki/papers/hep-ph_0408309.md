# Landau-gauge condensates from the quark propagator on the lattice

> We compute the dimension-2 condensate, <A^2>, and the dimension-4 mixed condensate, <\bar q A q>, from the recent quenched lattice results for the quark propagator in the Landau gauge. We fit the lattice data to the Operator Product Expansion in the "fiducial" region 1.2 GeV < Q < 3 GeV. Our result for the dynamical gluon mass at the scale of 10 GeV^2 is m_A=600-650 MeV, in agreement with independent determinations. For the mixed Landau gauge condensate of dimension-4 we get alpha_s <\bar q g A q> = (-0.11 +/- 0.03) GeV^4. This value is an order of magnitude larger than the <G^2> gluon condensate.

# Landau-gauge condensates from the quark propagator on the lattice

## Key claim
The dimension-2 condensate ⟨A²⟩ and the dimension-4 mixed condensate ⟨q̄ g A̸ q⟩ can be extracted from quenched lattice data for the quark propagator in Landau gauge by fitting to the Operator Product Expansion in the fiducial region 1.2 GeV < Q < 3 GeV, yielding a dynamical gluon mass m_A = 600–650 MeV at the scale of 10 GeV², but the results carry systematic uncertainties from the choice of fitting function and fiducial region.

## Method
The authors fit the vector quark self-energy A(Q) from quenched lattice data (100 configurations, 16³×32 lattices, a = 0.124 fm, Asqtad improved staggered action) to the OPE expansion A(Q) = 1 + c₂/Q² + c₄/Q⁴ in the momentum range 1.2 GeV ≤ Q ≤ 3 GeV. They extract the condensates by matching the fitted coefficients c₂ and c₄ to the OPE expressions for A(Q) in Landau gauge, using χ² minimization with jackknife error estimation.

## Key numbers
- c₂ = (0.37 ± 0.04) GeV² from the fit to A(Q) in the range 1.2 GeV ≤ Q ≤ 3 GeV
- c₄ = (−0.25 ± 0.06) GeV⁴ from the same fit
- α_s(μ²) ⟨A²⟩_μ = (0.36 ± 0.04) GeV², corresponding to g² ⟨A²⟩ = (2.1 ± 0.1 GeV)²
- α_s ⟨q̄ g A̸ q⟩ = (−0.11 ± 0.03) GeV⁴, an order of magnitude larger than the ⟨G²⟩ gluon condensate (⟨α_s/π G²⟩ ≈ 0.009 GeV⁴)
- Dynamical gluon mass m_A = (625 ± 33) MeV when evolved from 2 to 10 GeV²; m_A = (611 ± 32) MeV from 1 to 10 GeV²; m_A = (635 ± 34) MeV from 4 to 10 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the OPE analysis of the quark propagator previously applied to the mass function M(Q) by Bowman et al. (2004) to also include the wave-function renormalization, enabling extraction of the dimension-2 gluon condensate ⟨A²⟩ and the dimension-4 mixed condensate. The results agree with independent determinations of the gluon mass compiled in the review by Field (2001) and with the tachyonic gluon mass parameter m_A ∼ 700 MeV found by Chetyrkin, Narison, and Zakharov (1998, 2001) from QCD sum rules. The extracted condensates provide a lattice-based input for continuum approaches such as Dyson-Schwinger equations and QCD sum rules, and the mixed condensate determination is claimed as the first such estimate.
