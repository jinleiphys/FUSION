# Antiflow of Nucleons at the Softest Point of the EoS

> We investigate flow in semi-peripheral nuclear collisions at AGS and SPS energies within macroscopic as well as microscopic transport models. The hot and dense zone assumes the shape of an ellipsoid which is tilted by an angle Theta with respect to the beam axis. If matter is close to the softest point of the equation of state, this ellipsoid expands predominantly orthogonal to the direction given by Theta. This antiflow component is responsible for the previously predicted reduction of the directed transverse momentum around the softest point of the equation of state.

## Key claim
The reduction of directed transverse flow around the softest point of the equation of state is caused by an "antiflow" component: the hot and dense zone forms a tilted ellipsoid in coordinate space that expands predominantly orthogonal to the tilt direction, which cancels the normal directed flow around midrapidity. This mechanism operates specifically when the equation of state is softened by a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma or by resonance/string excitations, and the effect is demonstrated in both fluid-dynamical and microscopic transport models.

## Method
The authors employed one-fluid dynamics, three-fluid dynamics with a dynamical local unification procedure, and the microscopic UrQMD transport model to simulate semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at AGS and SPS energies. The one- and three-fluid calculations used an equation of state with a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma (hadronic phase with nucleons interacting via sigma and omega meson exchange plus non-interacting pions; QGP phase as a non-interacting gas of massless u,d quarks and gluons in the MIT Bag model with bag parameter B^{1/4}=235 MeV, critical temperature T_c~170 MeV). The three-fluid model treated projectile, target, and newly produced particles as separate fluids with a local unification criterion based on pressure ratios. The directed flow was computed as the weighted mean transverse in-plane momentum per nucleon, and the flow was decomposed locally into normal flow and antiflow components based on the sign of y(x)*p_x(x).

## Key numbers
- Directed flow p_x^{dir}/N is reduced to approximately 0 MeV at the end of the time evolution for Au+Au at 8 AGeV, b=3 fm, with a phase transition to QGP in one-fluid dynamics (mean net-baryon density drops below ground-state density rho_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3})
- The three-fluid model predicts a local minimum in the excitation function of directed flow at bombarding energies between 10 and 20 AGeV, depending on the fluid unification criterion
- The three-fluid model with unification shows a local maximum in directed flow at E_Lab^{kin} ~ 40 AGeV
- The impact parameter where directed flow is strongest is b_m ~ 4 fm at AGS and b_m ~ 8 fm at SPS
- UrQMD calculations used density-dependent Skyrme potentials with incompressibility K ~ 380 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier one-fluid calculations by Yaris and Staubo (1990s) that predicted a local minimum in directed flow excitation function at ~8 AGeV due to a softened equation of state. The three-fluid model shifts this minimum to higher energies (10-20 AGeV) by accounting for non-equilibrium effects and limited stopping power in the early collision stage, which delay the build-up of transverse pressure. The antiflow mechanism provides a physical explanation for the flow reduction, and the prediction of a subsequent maximum at ~40 AGeV offers an experimental test: if CERN-SPS experiments find larger directed flow than at maximum AGS energies, the existence of a minimum due to intermediate softening of the equation of state would be unambiguously proven. The work also shows that the non-trivial ellipsoidal event shape couples longitudinal to transverse flow, complicating simplified geometrical overlap models used to study elliptic flow by Ollitraut, Sorge, and others.
