# Novel pentaquark picture for singly heavy baryons from chiral symmetry

> We propose a new type of structure for singly heavy baryons of $Qqq\bar{q}q$ in addition to the conventional one of $Qqq$. Based on chiral symmetry of the light quarks, we show that the $Qqq\bar{q}q$ baryon offers a novel picture for heavy quark spin-singlet and flavor-antisymmetric baryons. By making use of the effective Lagrangian approach, we find $Λ_c(2765)$ and $Ξ_c(2967)$ are mostly $Qqq\bar{q}q$ while $Λ_c(2286)$ and $Ξ_c(2470)$ are mostly $Qqq$. The masses of negative-parity baryons are predicted. We also derive a sum rule and the extended Goldberger-Treiman relation that the masses of the baryons satisfy. Furthermore, a mass degeneracy of parity partners of the baryons with the restoration of chiral symmetry is discussed. These are the unique features that the conventional picture of radial excitation in the quark model does not accommodate. Our findings provide useful information not only for future experiments but also for future lattice simulations on diquarks.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the excited singly heavy baryons $\Lambda_c(2765)$ and $\Xi_c(2967)$ are predominantly compact pentaquark states ($Qqq\bar{q}q$) rather than conventional radial excitations ($Qqq$), based on a chiral-symmetry mirror representation for diquarks. The claim is hedged by noting that these baryons are described as superpositions of three-quark and pentaquark components, with the mixing angles depending on two possible parameter sets.

## Method

The authors construct an $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ chiral effective Lagrangian within heavy baryon effective theory (HBET), introducing both conventional diquarks ($d \sim qq$) and mirror diquarks ($d' \sim qq\bar{q}q$) that transform under opposite chiral representations. Mass eigenvalues for positive- and negative-parity baryons are derived from the Lagrangian after spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, using the vacuum expectation values $\sigma_q = 93$ MeV and $\sigma_s$ as inputs. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the masses of four experimentally observed $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$ baryons ($\Lambda_c(2286)$, $\Lambda_c(2765)$, $\Xi_c(2470)$, $\Xi_c(2967)$) and lattice QCD estimates for conventional diquark masses from Bi (2015), together with a quark model prediction for the lightest negative-parity $\Lambda_c$ from Yoshida (2015).

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2286)$ mass: 2286 MeV (input, $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$)
- $\Lambda_c(2765)$ mass: 2765 MeV (input, $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$)
- $\Xi_c(2470)$ mass: 2470 MeV (input, $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$)
- $\Xi_c(2967)$ mass: 2967 MeV (input, $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$)
- Conventional diquark masses from lattice QCD: $M(d_{+,i=1,2}) = 906$ MeV, $M(d_{-,i=1,2}) = 1142$ MeV, $M(d_{+,i=3}) = 725$ MeV, $M(d_{-,i=3}) = 1265$ MeV
- Predicted negative-parity baryon masses: $M(B_{-,i=1,2}^{L}) = 2732$ MeV, $M(B_{-,i=1,2}^{H}) = 3302$ MeV, $M(B_{-,i=3}^{H}) = 3529$ MeV
- Mixing angles for $\Lambda_c$ positive-parity states: $\theta_{B_{+,i=3}} = \pm 21.4^\circ$ (Set I) or $\pm 23.2^\circ$ (Set II)
- Mixing angles for $\Xi_c$ positive-parity states: $\theta_{B_{+,i=1,2}} = \pm 14.8^\circ$ (Set I) or $\pm 29.9^\circ$ (Set II)
- Common heavy mass parameter: $m_B = 2868$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the mirror nucleon concept for $N^*(1535)$ (Detar 1988, Jido 2001) to the heavy baryon sector, proposing that $\Lambda_c(2765)$ and $\Xi_c(2967)$ are analogous to the Roper resonance $N(1440)$ but described by pentaquark configurations rather than radial excitations as in the quark model (Copley 1979, Yoshida 2015). The model predicts a sum rule equating the total mass of the four $\Lambda_c$ states with that of the four $\Xi_c$ states, and an extended Goldberger-Treiman relation linking parity-partner mass differences to pion coupling constants. These predictions can be tested in future experiments and lattice simulations of diquarks (Bi 2015), and the framework enables studies of chiral partner mass degeneracy at finite temperature or density where chiral symmetry is restored.
