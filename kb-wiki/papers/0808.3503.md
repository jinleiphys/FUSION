# Chaotic Scattering in the Regime of Weakly Overlapping Resonances

> We measure the transmission and reflection amplitudes of microwaves in a resonator coupled to two antennas at room temperature in the regime of weakly overlapping resonances and in a frequency range of 3 to 16 GHz. Below 10.1 GHz the resonator simulates a chaotic quantum system. The distribution of the elements of the scattering matrix S is not Gaussian. The Fourier coefficients of S are used for a best fit of the autocorrelation function if S to a theoretical expression based on random--matrix theory. We find very good agreement below but not above 10.1 GHz.

## Key claim

The Verbaarschot-Weidenmüller-Zirnbauer (VWZ) theory of chaotic scattering is compatible with experimental data only when the resonator simulates a fully chaotic two-dimensional quantum system (below 10.1 GHz), but fails when a second vertical mode appears above that frequency, indicating the test is sensitive to additional symmetries.

## Method

The authors measured complex transmission and reflection amplitudes (S-matrix elements) of a microwave cavity shaped as a tilted stadium billiard, coupled to two antennas, over 3 to 16 GHz at room temperature. They constructed S-matrix autocorrelation functions and their Fourier transforms, then performed maximum likelihood fits of the VWZ theoretical expression to the Fourier coefficients, using the mean level spacing from the Weyl formula and treating the absorption parameter as the only free parameter. A goodness-of-fit test based on the distribution of the logarithm of the squared Fourier coefficients was applied to 1 GHz frequency intervals.

## Key numbers

- The resonator height is 14.6 mm, with the two-dimensional regime valid for frequencies f ≤ 10.1 GHz
- Frequency step size was 250 kHz for reflection measurements and 100 kHz for transmission measurements
- The elastic enhancement factor W decreases smoothly from W = 3.5 ± 0.7 for 4 ≤ f ≤ 5 GHz to W = 2.0 ± 0.7 for 9 ≤ f ≤ 10 GHz
- The sum T₁ + T₂ + τ_abs increases from 0.11 in the 3-4 GHz interval to 1.15 in the 9-10 GHz interval
- The ratio Γ/D increases from 0.02 to 0.2 over the same frequency range
- Each 1 GHz frequency interval provides M = 2400 Fourier coefficients (800 from each of three excitation functions)
- The goodness-of-fit test admits a 10% probability for erroneous decision

## Context

This work extends previous tests of chaotic scattering theory that were performed in the regimes of isolated resonances (Γ ≪ D) by Lynn (1968) and the Ericson regime (Γ ≫ D) by Ericson (1960, 1963), as well as earlier microwave resonator studies by Schäfer et al. (2003) and others that did not apply rigorous statistical goodness-of-fit tests. The results enable confident use of VWZ theory for predicting average cross sections and S-matrix correlation functions in fully chaotic systems, and suggest that the analysis method can serve as a tool to detect symmetries or regular motion within chaotic systems in the regime of overlapping resonances.
