# Width of the QCD transition in a Polyakov-loop DSE model

> We consider the pseudocritical temperatures for the chiral and deconfinement transitions within a Polyakov-loop Dyson-Schwinger equation approach which employs a nonlocal rank-2 separable model for the effective gluon propagator. These pseudocritical temperatures differ by a factor of two when the quark and gluon sectors are considered separately, but get synchronized and become coincident when their coupling is switched on. The coupling of the Polyakov-loop to the chiral quark dynamics narrows the temperature region of the QCD transition in which chiral symmetry and deconfinement is established. We investigate the effect of rescaling the parameter T_0 in the Polyakov-loop potential on the QCD transition for both the logarithmic and polynomial forms of the potential. While the critical temperatures vary in a similar way, the width of the transition is stronger affected for the logarithmic potential. For this potential the character of the transition changes from crossover to a first order one when T_0 < 210 MeV, but it remains crossover in the whole range of relevant T_0 values for the polynomial form.

## Key claim

The pseudocritical temperatures for chiral and deconfinement transitions, which differ by more than a factor of two when quark and gluon sectors are treated separately, become synchronized and coincident at 195 MeV when coupling is switched on; this coupling narrows the QCD transition region to a tiny temperature interval, but the width remains much too small compared to lattice QCD results, and for the logarithmic Polyakov-loop potential the transition changes from crossover to first order when the parameter T₀ falls below 210 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a Polyakov-loop Dyson-Schwinger equation (PDSE) approach with a nonlocal rank-2 separable model for the effective gluon propagator, solving the coupled gap equations for quark mass functions and the Polyakov-loop expectation value at finite temperature. They computed pseudocritical temperatures from the peak positions of the temperature derivatives of the order parameters (quark mass functions and Polyakov loop) for both logarithmic and polynomial forms of the Polyakov-loop potential, and evaluated spatial meson screening masses from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and scalar channels.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature for chiral restoration without Polyakov-loop coupling: T_χ = 128 MeV (light quark sector)
- Pseudocritical temperature for deconfinement without coupling: T_d = 270 MeV (from pure gauge PL potential parametrization)
- Synchronized pseudocritical temperature with coupling: T_c = 195 MeV for logarithmic PL potential; 193 MeV for polynomial PL potential
- For the logarithmic PL potential, the transition changes from crossover to first order when T₀ < 210 MeV; for T₀ > 210 MeV the slope is T_c = const + 0.40 T₀, and for T₀ < 210 MeV the slope is T_c = const + 0.30 T₀
- For the polynomial PL potential, the transition remains crossover for all relevant T₀ values, with T_c = const + 0.36 T₀
- Vacuum parameters: m_u⁰ = m_d⁰ = 5.49 MeV, m_s⁰ = 115 MeV, Λ₀ = 0.758 GeV, Λ₁ = 0.961 GeV, p₀ = 0.6 GeV
- The width of the transition region reduces from about 10% without PL coupling to about 1% with PL coupling, as measured by σ-π squared mass difference and decay channel thresholds

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model approach of Ratti (2005) and the nonlocal PNJL models of Blaschke (2007) and Hell (2008) by incorporating a rank-2 separable gluon propagator that reproduces both the dynamical quark mass function and wave function renormalization from lattice QCD simulations of Parappilly (2005). The results contradict the T₀ rescaling prescription of Schaefer (2007) for the logarithmic PL potential, which would predict a first-order transition for N_f=2+1 where lattice QCD (Aoki 2006, Ejiri 2009) finds a crossover; the polynomial PL potential avoids this contradiction. The approach enables predictions for meson properties at finite temperature and the behavior of the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation near T_c, but the narrow transition width (≈1%) is much smaller than that obtained in recent lattice QCD studies such as Cheng (2010).
