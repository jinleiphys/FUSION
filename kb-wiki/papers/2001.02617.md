# Lack of Debye and Meissner screening in strongly magnetized quark matter at intermediate densities

> We study the static responses of cold quark matter in the intermediate baryonic density region (characterized by a chemical potential $μ$) in the presence of a strong magnetic field. We consider in particular, the so-called Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave (MDCDW) phase, which is materialized by an inhomogeneous condensate formed by a particle-hole pair. It is shown, that the MDCDW phase is more stable in the weak-coupling regime than the one considered in the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry braking phenomenon (MC$χ$SB) and even than the chiral symmetric phase that was expected to be realized at sufficiently high baryonic chemical potential. The different components of the photon polarization operator of the MDCDW phase in the one-loop approximation are calculated. We found that in the MDCDW phase there is no Debye screening neither Meissner effect in the lowest-Landau-level approximation. The obtained Debye length depends on the amplitude $m$ and modulation $b$ of the inhomogeneous condensate and it is only different from zero if the relation $| μ-b| > m$ holds. But, we found that in the region of interest this inequality is not satisfied. Thus, no Debye screening takes place under those conditions. On the other hand, since the particle-hole condensate is electrically neutral, the U(1) electromagnetic group is not broken by the ground state and consequently there is no Meissner effect. These results can be of interest for the astrophysics of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave (MDCDW) phase of strongly magnetized cold quark matter at intermediate densities, there is no Debye screening and no Meissner effect under the lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation, but this conclusion is conditional: the Debye length depends on the condensate parameters and is only nonzero if the inequality \(|\mu - b| > m\) holds, and the authors find this inequality is not satisfied in the region of interest.

## Method

The authors used a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with massless quarks in a constant external magnetic field, employing the mean-field approximation to describe the MDCDW phase characterized by spatially modulated scalar and pseudoscalar condensates. They calculated the one-loop photon polarization operator in the LLL approximation, extracted the Debye mass from the 00-component in the infrared limit (\(p_0 = 0, p^3 \rightarrow 0\)), and compared thermodynamic potentials of the MDCDW and magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking (MC\(\chi\)SB) phases to determine stability.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling constant for the weak-coupling regime in the MDCDW phase is \(G_c = 3.27\) (dimensionless, scaled with cutoff \(\Lambda\)).
- For a magnetic field \(\sqrt{eB} = 1.0\) (in units of \(\Lambda\)), the LLL approximation is valid with a percentage deviation of 20% in the density region \(0.2 \lesssim \mu \lesssim 1\) and 10% in the region \(0.5 \lesssim \mu \lesssim 1\) (chemical potential \(\mu\) in units of \(\Lambda\)).
- The MC\(\chi\)SB phase has a critical chemical potential \(\mu_c\) where its chiral condensate vanishes, satisfying \(\mu_c \lesssim m(\mu=0)\), where \(m(\mu=0)\) is the particle mass at zero chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in dense quark matter under magnetic fields, specifically the MDCDW phase introduced by Klimenko and by Ferrer et al. (2014, 2015), and contrasts it with the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking (MC\(\chi\)SB) phenomenon described by Gusynin et al. (1995). The finding that the MDCDW phase is more stable than the MC\(\chi\)SB phase at weak coupling and intermediate densities, and that it lacks Debye and Meissner screening, is relevant for neutron star astrophysics, where the absence of screening affects electric field penetration and magnetic field expulsion in the stellar interior.
