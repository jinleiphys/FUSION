# On Gamov states of $Σ^+$ hyperons

> Both the FINUDA and AMADEUS experiments evidenced a low $Σ^+$ momentum component when investigating $Σ^+ π^-$ pairs produced in K$^-$ nuclear capture. This component is interpreted as a consequence of Gamov state formation, with the hyperon trapped in the Coulomb field of the residual nucleus. Description of such states and their participation in the capture reaction is presented. Some consequences are indicated.

# On Gamov states of Σ⁺ hyperons

## Key claim
The low-momentum enhancement of Σ⁺ hyperons observed in K⁻ nuclear capture experiments by FINUDA and AMADEUS is interpreted as arising from Gamov state formation, where the hyperon becomes trapped in the Coulomb field of the residual nucleus, but the observed widths of these states (∼0.2 MeV) are not due to nuclear absorption but rather to π meson emission kinematics.

## Method
The authors model the K⁻ capture process as a three-step mechanism: hadronic capture K⁻ p → Σ⁺ π⁻, trapping of the Σ⁺ by the Coulomb potential of the residual nucleus into a Gamov state, and subsequent decay of the Gamov state into the residual nucleus and a low-momentum Σ⁺. They construct a Green's function from solutions of the Schrödinger equation with a potential comprising Coulomb, nuclear, and imaginary absorptive parts, and compute transition amplitudes using a zero-range strangeness-transfer operator T. The momentum spectrum is obtained by integrating over three-body phase space with the Wronski determinant of the regular and outgoing wave functions providing the Gamov state singularity.

## Key numbers
- The low-momentum peak appears in the 20 ± 15 MeV/c momentum range
- Gamov state energies lie in the range 0 < E_G < 0.4 MeV
- Gamov state widths are very small: 0.5 < Γ_G < 20 KeV
- The depth of the nuclear potential V_o required for Gamov states is between −19.3 and −18.0 MeV
- The experimental widths are about 0.2 MeV
- The Gamov peak magnitude is about 3% of the large peak
- The peak position observed by AMADEUS corresponds to Q ≃ 15 MeV/c
- The nuclear absorption factor |1 + iW_s G|² ≃ 0.6 for W_s(0) ≃ −15 MeV
- The high-momentum spectrum is centered at ∼200 MeV/c
- The kinematic limit for atomic captures on Carbon is 1416 MeV; for in-flight capture with p_K = 120 MeV/c it is pushed up by about 14 MeV

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of low-momentum Σ⁺ events first observed by FINUDA on a ⁶Li target and later by AMADEUS on ¹²C and ⁹Be targets, where the solid carbon fiber target ruled out energy loss as an explanation. The analysis builds on old emulsion measurements reviewed in EMULS and uses nuclear potential parameters from hyperonic atoms studied by Batt. The authors note that no Σ hypernuclear states have been found in the nuclei of interest, with the only discovered Σ hypernucleus being ⁴He_Σ from Nagae, making Gamov states a substitute for direct hypernuclear spectroscopy. The work enables a new branch of hypernuclear spectroscopy if Gamov states can be formed in heavier nuclei.
