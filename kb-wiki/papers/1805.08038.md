# Vacuum Decay Induced by False Skyrmions

> We consider the Skyrme model modified by the addition of mass terms which explicitly break chiral symmetry and pick out a specific point on the model's target space as the unique true vacuum. However, they also allow the possibility of false vacua, local minima of the potential energy. These false vacuum configurations admit metastable skyrmions, which we call false skyrmions. False skyrmions can decay due to quantum tunnelling, consequently causing the decay of the false vacuum. We compute the rate of decay of the false vacuum due to the existence of false skyrmions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a Skyrme model with a potential that has both a true vacuum and a false vacuum, metastable "false skyrmions" can exist, and these objects can trigger the decay of the false vacuum through quantum tunnelling at a rate that the authors compute, but only for specific parameter choices (here $m_1=0.5$, $m_2=10$) where the false vacuum energy density is $\epsilon=0.25$ and the potential barrier height is approximately 100.

## Method

The authors modify the Skyrme Lagrangian by adding mass terms $-\frac{1}{4}(m_1^2\Tr[\mathbb{1}-\UU]+m_2^2\Tr[\mathbb{1}-\UU^2])$ that explicitly break chiral symmetry, creating a global minimum at $\UU=\mathbb{1}$ (true vacuum) and a local minimum at $\zeta=\pi$ (false vacuum). They use the rational map ansatz with profile function $f(r)$ satisfying boundary conditions $f(0)=2\pi$ (true vacuum at centre) and $f(\infty)=\pi$ (false vacuum at infinity), solve the resulting equation of motion numerically using MATLAB's bvp4c solver for baryon numbers $B=1$ to $B=2000$, and compute the Euclidean action for quantum tunnelling of the skyrmion radius $R$ treated as a collective coordinate in the thin-wall approximation.

## Key numbers

* False vacuum energy density: $\epsilon = 0.25$ (for $m_1=0.5$, $m_2=10$), with the potential barrier height approximately 100.
* Energy of $B=1$ false skyrmion: $E = 2.769$ (in units of $12\pi^2$, with false vacuum energy subtracted).
* Energy of $B=2000$ false skyrmion: $E = 4516.776$ (same units).
* Charge radius $\langle r^2\rangle_Q^{1/2}$ for $B=1$ false skyrmion: $0.370$; for $B=2000$: $13.415$.
* For shell-like false skyrmions, $\langle r^2\rangle \propto B$ (linear scaling), while for ball-like true skyrmions $\langle r^2\rangle \propto B^{2/3}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of metastable solitons in false vacua, including magnetic monopoles, vortices, and cosmic strings (Kumar 2010, Lee 2013), to the Skyrme model which contains no gauge fields. The false skyrmion's energy as a function of radius takes the form $E = \alpha R^2 + \beta/R - (1/9\pi)\epsilon R^3$, analogous to the thin-wall expressions found for those gauge-theory solitons. The results enable future calculations of false vacuum decay rates in models where topological defects are present, potentially affecting cosmological phase transitions or condensed matter systems where skyrmions arise.
