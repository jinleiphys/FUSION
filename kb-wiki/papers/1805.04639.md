# Electron scattering from a deeply bound nucleon on the light-front

> We calculate the cross section of the electron scattering from a bound nucleon within light-front approximation. The advantage of this approximation is the possibility of systematic account for the off-shell effects which become essential in high energy electro-nuclear processes aimed at probing the nuclear structure at small distances. We derive a new dynamical parameter which allows to control the extent of the "off-shellness" of electron - bound-nucleon electromagnetic current for different regions of momentum transfer and initial light-cone momenta of the bound nucleon. The derived cross section is compared with the results of other approaches in treating the off-shell effects in electron-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a light-front treatment of electron scattering from a deeply bound nucleon that allows systematic separation of off-shell effects, introducing a new boost-invariant parameter η that quantifies the "off-shellness" of the electron-bound-nucleon electromagnetic current; the authors claim that by restricting η < 0.1 one can confine off-shell effects below 5% for any realistic bound nucleon momenta at different Q² values.

## Method

The authors calculate the exclusive d(e,e′N)N reaction within plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using effective light-front perturbation theory in the light-cone τ-time ordered representation. They separate the covariant scattering amplitude into propagating and instantaneous contributions, and introduce the off-shell parameter η = (1/Q²)(4(m_N² + p_T²)/(α(2-α)) - m_d²) to quantify the off-shellness of the γ*N_bound scattering. The electron-bound-nucleon cross section σ_eN is expressed through four independent structure functions (V_L, V_TL, V_T, V_TT) which are calculated explicitly in terms of η and the nucleon electromagnetic form factors F₁ and F₂.

## Key numbers

- The off-shell parameter η is defined as η = (1/Q²)(4(m_N² + p_T²)/(α(2-α)) - m_d²), where α is the light-cone momentum fraction of the bound nucleon, p_T is its transverse momentum, and Q² is the virtual photon four-momentum squared
- By restricting η < 0.1, off-shell effects can be confined below 5% for any realistic values of bound nucleon momenta at different Q²
- The instantaneous contribution to the longitudinal structure function V_L_inst^N contains terms proportional to F₁²η_i(τη_i(1+η_q) - 2 - η_q) and other combinations involving η_i and η_q
- The propagating part of the transverse structure function V_T_prop^N = 4m_N²[F₁²(p_T²/m_N² + 2τ(1+η_q)) + 2F₁F₂κτ(2+η_q) + F₂²κ²τ(2 + p_T²/m_N² + 2τη_i(η_i+η_q))]

## Context

This work extends the light-front treatment of nuclear dynamics developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) and Miller (2000) to the specific problem of electron-bound-nucleon scattering, addressing the off-shell ambiguity first recognized in intermediate-energy A(e,e′N) experiments at SACLAY and NIKHEF in the 1980s. The approach contrasts with the de Forest (1983) model, which provided eight different expressions for the eN_bound cross section without preference, by instead deriving a single parameterized expression that quantifies off-shell effects. The results enable systematic extraction of nuclear spectral and decay functions in the high-momentum region relevant to short-range correlation studies, and can be extended beyond PWIA to include final state interactions using the generalized eikonal approximation of Sargsian (2009).
