# A Better Angle on Hadron Transverse Momentum Distributions at the EIC

> We propose an observable $q_*$ sensitive to transverse momentum dependence (TMD) in $e N \to e h X$, with $q_*/E_N$ defined purely by lab-frame angles. In 3D measurements of confinement and hadronization this resolves the crippling issue of accurately reconstructing small transverse momentum $P_{hT}$. We prove factorization for $\mathrm{d} σ_h / \mathrm{d}q_*$ for $q_*\ll Q$ with standard TMD functions, enabling $q_*$ to substitute for $P_{hT}$. A double-angle reconstruction method is given which is exact to all orders in QCD for $q_*\ll Q$. $q_*$ enables an order-of-magnitude improvement in the expected experimental resolution at the EIC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the purely angular observable \(q_*\) can substitute for the standard transverse momentum \(P_{hT}\) in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), achieving an order-of-magnitude improvement in experimental resolution while preserving the same factorization structure in terms of standard transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions, provided \(q_* \ll Q\).

## Method

The authors define \(q_*\) using only lab-frame pseudorapidities of the outgoing electron and hadron, the beam energy, and the acoplanarity angle between the electron and hadron in the target rest frame. They prove a factorization theorem for the differential cross section \(\mathrm{d}\sigma_h / \mathrm{d}q_*\) at leading power in \(P_{hT}/(zQ)\), showing it involves the same TMD functions as the standard \(P_{hT}\) spectrum. A double-angle reconstruction method for \(Q\), \(x\), and \(y\) is given that is exact to all orders in QCD for \(q_* \ll Q\). The experimental sensitivity is evaluated using Pythia 8.306 simulations with EIC detector resolutions, and a Bayesian reweighting analysis is performed to compare statistical sensitivity and systematic bias between \(q_*\) and \(P_{hT}\).

## Key numbers

* For a nominal measurement at \(P_{hT}/z = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with \(Q = 20\ \mathrm{GeV}\), a typical detector resolution of \(\Delta = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) leads to a 50% uncertainty on \(P_{hT}\).
* The \(q_*\) observable improves the resolution over \(P_{hT}/z\) by an order of magnitude across the TMD region \(q_*, P_{hT}/z \lesssim 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the case of a backward electron and central hadron at the EIC.
* The simulated event sample at \(18 \times 275\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) with \(10\ \mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) integrated luminosity yields \(N_{\pi^+} = 4.18 \times 10^8\) after SIDIS cuts.
* The Bayesian reweighting analysis shows more than a factor of 10 improvement in uncertainty on the dominant fragmentation parameter \(w_3\) for both \(q_*\) and \(P_{hT}\) compared to the prior from the MAPTMD22 fit.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the \(\phi^*_\eta\) observable from Drell-Yan production in hadron-hadron collisions (Banfi et al., 2010) to the SIDIS process at the EIC. It addresses the crippling issue of accurately reconstructing small transverse momentum \(P_{hT}\) that has plagued previous TMD measurements at HERMES, COMPASS, RHIC, and JLab. The factorization theorem for \(q_*\) uses the same TMD PDFs and FFs as standard SIDIS factorization (Collins, 2011; Ji, Ma, and Yuan, 2004), enabling direct substitution of \(q_*\) for \(P_{hT}\) in TMD analyses. The double-angle reconstruction method builds on the work of Bentvelsen et al. (1992) but uses the hadron angle rather than the struck quark angle and holds to all orders in \(\alpha_s\). This work enables precise 3D mapping of confinement and hadronization dynamics at the EIC with dramatically improved experimental resolution.
