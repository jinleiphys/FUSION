# Distinguishing Hadronic Cascades from Hydrodynamic Models in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb Reactions by Impact Parameter Variation

> We propose to study the impact parameter dependence of the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio in Pb(160AGeV)+Pb reactions. The anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio is a sensible tool to distinguish between hadronic cascade models and hydrodynamical models, which incorporate a QGP phase transition.

## Key claim

The impact parameter dependence of the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb reactions can distinguish between hadronic cascade models and hydrodynamical models that incorporate a QGP phase transition, with the caveat that the hydrodynamical model predicts a constant ratio across impact parameters while the microscopic UrQMD model predicts a strong dependence.

## Method

The authors compared two theoretical approaches: a 3-fluid hydrodynamical model with a first-order QGP phase transition (using an equation of state with phase transition) and the UrQMD microscopic hadronic transport model. The 3-fluid model calculated entropy per net baryon (S/A) as a function of impact parameter, with hadron ratios computed assuming chemical isochronous freeze-out at net baryon density ρ=ρ0/2 or at temperature T=160 MeV. The UrQMD model treated non-equilibrium dynamics through baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson collisions with resonance and color flux tube formation and decay.

## Key numbers

- In the 3-fluid hydrodynamical approach, the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio stays constant with impact parameter b
- In the UrQMD hadronic model, the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio drops rapidly from 1.3 to 0.5 as impact parameter increases from 0 fm to 13 fm
- For S/A greater than 25 (the relevant range for SPS energies), the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio is practically unchanged when using freeze-out temperature T=160 MeV instead of freeze-out density ρ=ρ0/2
- In peripheral collisions, the anti-Lambda/anti-Proton ratio in pp-like reactions is approximately 0.3-0.5
- The 3-fluid hydrodynamical calculations cover impact parameters from 0 fm to 9 fm; UrQMD covers 0 fm to 13 fm

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether strangeness enhancement signals a QGP phase transition (Rafelski, 1982) or can be explained by hadronic non-equilibrium transport models (RQMD). The authors propose impact parameter variation as a new diagnostic, building on earlier thermal model analyses by Stoecker, Braun-Munzinger, Cleymans, and Rafelski that assumed chemical equilibrium. The results enable experimental tests by the NA49 and CERES collaborations to determine the degree of local chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies.
