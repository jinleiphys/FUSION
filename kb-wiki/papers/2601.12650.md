# QCD-Like Theories with Different Color Numbers

> Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) with a general number of colors, $\Nc$, provides a powerful theoretical laboratory to explore the dynamics of non-Abelian gauge theories. Although $\Nc =3$ does not look a large number, the $1/\Nc$ expansion provides us with a very useful classification and book-keeping scheme for hadronic processes and sharpens conceptions otherwise obscured in real-world QCD with $\Nc = 3$. Important applications are dense QCD matter where the first principle methods for QCD are not available and many conceptual issues remain to be clarified. In this chapter we first review hadrons at large $\Nc$ from the viewpoint of quark-gluon dynamics, and then extend the discussions to hot/dense matter, focusing on confinement-deconfinement aspects. We emphasize how the large-$\Nc$ limit provides a unified organizing principle for hadronic and quark degrees of freedom in regimes where first-principle methods are limited. Two-color and isospin QCD, for which lattice simulations at finite density can be performed for a special reason, is reviewed.

## Key claim

The large-Nc expansion provides a unified organizing principle for hadronic and quark degrees of freedom in regimes where first-principle methods are limited, but the direct application to nuclear physics is subtle because the axial coupling gA scales as Nc, leading to overly strong long-range pion exchange that would produce deeply bound nuclear matter and crystals rather than the liquid-like behavior observed in real-world QCD with Nc=3.

## Method

The author reviews the 't Hooft large-Nc limit (holding lambda = Nc g_s^2 fixed) and uses double-line color representations to count powers of Nc in Feynman diagrams for mesons and baryons. The matching between quark-gluon graphs and hadronic graphs is performed to determine scaling of meson decay widths, meson-meson vertices, baryon-meson couplings, and nuclear forces, with the results then extended to hot matter and cold dense matter at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The ratio Gamma/m for mesons scales as 1/Nc; for rho(770) with m_rho ~ 775 MeV and Gamma_rho ~ 150 MeV, Gamma_rho/m_rho ~ 0.2; for omega(782), Gamma_omega/m_omega ~ 0.011; for K*(892), Gamma_K*/m_K* ~ 0.06
- In meson exchange potentials, the ratio g_omega_NN / g_rho_NN ~ 3.3, consistent with large-Nc counting where omega coupling is ~ Nc^{1/2} and rho coupling is ~ Nc^{-1/2}
- The ratio g_sigma_NN / g_a0_NN ~ 3.8, consistent with large-Nc expectation of g_sigma_NN ~ Nc and g_a0_NN ~ O(1)
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}, approximately the nucleon density in typical nuclei
- The deconfinement temperature at large Nc (neglecting quark contributions) is T_deco ~ 270 MeV, while at Nc=3 the pseudo-critical temperature is T_c ~ 155 MeV
- Around T_c, the entropy density is s ~ 2-3 fm^{-3}

## Context

This chapter reviews and extends the large-Nc framework originally introduced by 't Hooft (1973) and developed by Witten (1979) for hadronic processes, and by Kaplan (1995) for baryon-baryon interactions. It builds on the Skyrme model (Skyrme 1961, 1962) for baryons as chiral solitons and the Hagedorn temperature concept (Hagedorn 1965) for the limiting temperature of hadronic descriptions. The work enables systematic classification of hadronic processes in QCD-like theories and provides qualitative guidance for dense QCD matter where lattice simulations suffer from the sign problem, with two-color QCD (QC2D) and isospin QCD (QCD_I) serving as testbeds for concepts relevant to neutron star physics.
