# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering From Fully-Dynamical Lattice QCD

> We present results of the first fully-dynamical lattice QCD determination of nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths in the 1S0 channel and 3S1-3D1 coupled channels. The calculations are performed with domain-wall valence quarks on the MILC staggered configurations with lattice spacing of b=0.125 fm in the isospin-symmetric limit, and in the absence of electromagnetic interactions.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first fully-dynamical lattice QCD determination of nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths, but only at pion masses of 350 MeV and above, which is at the upper limit of where the effective field theory describing NN interactions is expected to be valid.

## Method
The authors performed a hybrid lattice QCD calculation using domain-wall valence quarks on MILC staggered gauge configurations with lattice spacing b=0.125 fm and spatial extent L=2.5 fm. They computed the energy shift of the lowest-lying two-nucleon state in a finite lattice volume and extracted scattering lengths using Lüscher's relation, with calculations performed at three different pion masses in the isospin-symmetric limit without electromagnetic interactions.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: 353.7 ± 2.1 MeV, 492.5 ± 1.1 MeV, and 593.0 ± 1.6 MeV
- Scattering length in the 1S0 channel at m_pi = 353.7 MeV: a = 0.63 ± 0.50 fm (statistical uncertainty, fitting range 5-10)
- Scattering length in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels at m_pi = 353.7 MeV: a = 0.63 ± 0.74 fm (statistical uncertainty, fitting range 5-9)
- Scattering length in the 1S0 channel at m_pi = 492.5 MeV: a = 0.65 ± 0.18 fm (fitting range 6-9)
- Scattering length in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels at m_pi = 492.5 MeV: a = 0.41 ± 0.28 fm (fitting range 6-9)
- Scattering length in the 1S0 channel at m_pi = 593.0 MeV: a = 0.0 ± 0.5 fm (fitting range 7-12)
- Scattering length in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels at m_pi = 593.0 MeV: a = -0.2 ± 1.3 fm (fitting range 7-12)
- Lattice spacing: b = 0.127 ± 0.001 fm (set via quark-mass dependence of f_pi)
- Systematic error of approximately 0.1 fm on each scattering length from truncation of the effective range expansion

## Context
This work extends the pioneering quenched lattice QCD study of nucleon-nucleon scattering by Fukugita et al. (1994), which was performed at pion masses above 550 MeV, by using fully-dynamical configurations at lighter pion masses. The results enable matching of lattice QCD calculations to many-body methods such as Green's Function Monte Carlo (Pieper, 2004) and the No-Core Shell Model (Forssen, 2004), and constrain the chiral extrapolation of scattering lengths using effective field theory power-counting schemes (Weinberg, 1990; Kaplan, 1998; Beane, 2001) down to the physical pion mass.
