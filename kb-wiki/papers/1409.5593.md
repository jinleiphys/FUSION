# Momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger-Equation, Fourier-transform with Gauss-Expansion-Method

> In these notes we construct the momentum-space potentials from configuration-space using for the Fourier-transformation the Gaussian-Expansion-Method (GEM). This has the advantage that the Fourier-Bessel integrals can be performed analytically, avoiding possible problems with the oscillations in the Bessel functions for large r, in particular for $p_f \neq p_i$. The mass parameters in the exponentials of the Gaussian base-functions are fixed using the geometric progression recipe of Hiyama-Kamimura. The fitting of the expansion coefficients is linearly and very fast. Application to nucleon-nucleon is given in detail for the recent Extended-soft-core model ESC08c. The NN phase shifts obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equations agree very well with those obtained in configuration-space solving the Schrödinger equations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) can be used to perform Fourier transforms of configuration-space nucleon-nucleon potentials into momentum space analytically, avoiding numerical problems with oscillatory Bessel functions at large radii, and that the resulting momentum-space potentials, when used in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, produce phase shifts that agree very well with those obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation in configuration space.

## Method

The author expands configuration-space potentials (central, tensor, spin-orbit, and quadratic-spin-orbit) as sums of Gaussian basis functions with mass parameters fixed by the geometric progression recipe of Hiyama-Kamimura. The Fourier-Bessel integrals are then performed analytically using closed-form expressions for the double-Bessel transforms of Gaussian functions. The linear expansion coefficients are fitted to the potential values at a set of distances and to the volume integrals. The resulting momentum-space potentials are used to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering, and the phase shifts are compared to those from configuration-space calculations.

## Key numbers

* The Gaussian basis functions use mass parameters \(\mu_k\) in the range \(0 < \mu_i < 2\Lambda\), where \(\Lambda\) is the soft-core cutoff parameter from the Extended-soft-core model ESC08c.
* The tensor potential expansion uses coefficients \(a_k\) and \(\mu_k\) such that \(V_T(r) = \frac{4}{3}\sum_{k=1}^N (a_k\mu_k^2) (\mu_k r)^2 \exp(-\mu_k^2 r^2)\).
* The basic radial integral for central potentials is \(I_{n,n}(a,b;\mu^2) = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{4 \mu^3} \exp\left(-\frac{a^2+b^2}{4\mu^2}\right) f_n\left(\frac{ab}{2\mu^2}\right)\), where \(f_n(X)=\sqrt{\pi/2X} I_{n+1/2}(X)\).

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space treatment of the Extended-soft-core model ESC08c, previously developed by Rijken and collaborators (Rijken, 2002; Nagels, Rijken, and Yamamoto, 2013, 2014), by providing an efficient method to compute momentum-space potentials from configuration-space input. The GEM approach avoids the time-consuming numerical integrals required for direct momentum-space evaluation of the ESC potentials. The method can be generalized to hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon systems, where the expansion coefficients become matrices in channel space.
