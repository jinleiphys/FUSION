# Benchmark values for the net proton number fluctuations

> We formulate a baseline model for the net-proton number fluctuations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our model includes total baryon number conservation, fluctuations of the participating nucleon number, limited acceptance, and a possibility that isospin is remembered by the participating wounded nucleons. Most importantly, we formulated the model as consisting of two components: wounded nucleons and the produced nucleon-antinucleon pairs. Those two components have different rapidity distributions. Owing to this feature we obtain also predictions for the rapidity dependence of the cumulants of the net-proton number distribution and their ratios.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a baseline model for net-proton number fluctuations that includes non-critical effects (baryon number conservation, fluctuating participant number, limited acceptance, and isospin memory) and shows that these effects produce strong rapidity and energy dependences in cumulant ratios, but cannot explain the large enhancement of kappa sigma^2 observed at the lowest collision energies.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-component Monte Carlo model: wounded nucleons from a Glauber simulation (GLISSANDO) with a double-Gaussian rapidity distribution, and produced nucleon-antinucleon pairs with a Woods-Saxon rapidity distribution. The number of pairs is Poisson-distributed with mean proportional to the number of wounded nucleons, and the rapidity distributions are tuned to reproduce STAR data for net protons and antiprotons at each collision energy. Central moments up to fourth order and the volume-independent ratios S sigma, kappa sigma^2, and kappa sigma^4 / n_bar are computed as functions of rapidity window size, rapidity window position, centrality, and collision energy.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV with fixed N_w = 338, the scaled skewness S sigma for net baryons follows the binomial formula S sigma = 1 - 2p, where p = mu_1(Delta y)/N_w, and the scaled kurtosis kappa sigma^2 follows kappa sigma^2 = 1 - 6p + 6p^2.
* For net protons at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV with fluctuating N_w in 0-5% centrality, S sigma is increased by about 0.25 over the fixed-N_w result for all Delta y up to 2 units.
* For sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the scaled kurtosis kappa sigma^2 as a function of rapidity window center y starts around 1 and falls to 0 at y = 1.2.
* For sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the scaled kurtosis kappa sigma^2 starts at very low values and grows with increasing collision energy, opposite to the experimental trend.
* The model parameters for 7.7 GeV: y_m = 0.519, N_BBbar = 0.8265; for 200 GeV: y_m = 1.665, N_BBbar = 177.794.

## Context

This work extends baseline studies of net-baryon fluctuations by Braun-Munzinger (2016) and Bzdak (2017) by incorporating energy-dependent rapidity distributions and a two-component (wounded nucleon plus produced pairs) structure. It provides a reference for the proposal by Brewer to search for the critical point using rapidity-dependent fluctuations, and it highlights that the large kappa sigma^2 enhancement at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV observed by the STAR collaboration (STARfluc) is not reproduced by these non-critical effects, indicating the need for additional physics.
