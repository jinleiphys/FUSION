# Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling in Muon Capture and Second-Order Corrections

> The hyperfine effect in muon capture rate is analyzed with taking into account of the second-order terms in 1/M in the non-relativistic Hamiltonian. It is shown that in the situation of the dominance of the squared first-order terms the interference of zero-order and second-order terms has no significant influence. General expression for neutrino (recoil nucleus) angular distribution in muon capture by nucleus with non-zero spin is proposed. High sensitivity to $g_P$ of the term related with alignment of the initial mesic atom is discussed in the approximation of dominating Gamow--Teller matrix element.

# Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling in Muon Capture and Second-Order Corrections

## Key claim
The hyperfine effect in muon capture rates can be described consistently using a second-order (in 1/M) non-relativistic Hamiltonian, but when squared first-order terms dominate, the interference between zero-order and second-order terms has no significant influence on the capture rate. The alignment term in the neutrino angular distribution remains highly sensitive to the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P, even when second-order corrections are included.

## Method
The author performed a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of the relativistic Hamiltonian for muon capture by a nucleus, keeping terms up to second order in 1/M, including for the first time terms associated with the nucleon-nucleus potential. General multipole expansions were developed for all operators in the non-relativistic Hamiltonian, and expressions for the neutrino angular distribution and muon capture rates from different hyperfine states (F± = J_i ± 1/2) were derived. The analysis was applied to the specific case of a 3/2 → 1/2 transition with same parity to study the magnitude of interference effects.

## Key numbers
- The PCAC prediction gives g_P ≃ 7g_A ≃ -8.4, which agrees with measurements on proton, ¹²C, and ²³Na (citing Joh96), but experiments on ⁶Li (citing Deu68) and ²⁸Si (citing Bru95) yielded values for g_P close to zero.
- The ratio Λ₊/Λ₋ for a transition |Jπ⟩ → |J-1 π⟩ with dominating Gamow-Teller matrix element is given by Λ₊/Λ₋ = G_P² / [3(2J+1)/J (G_A - 1/3 G_P)² + (J-1)/(3J) G_P²], where G_P = (E_ν/2M)(g_P - g_A - g_V - g_M).
- For the example transition 3/2 → 1/2 with same parity, the muon capture rates are Λ₊ = (3C_μ/8)(M₁²(2) + (2/√15)M₁(2)M₂(2) + (1/15)M₂²(2) + (16/15)M₂²(-3)) and Λ₋ = (2C_μ/3)(M₁²(-1) + (1/16)M₁²(2) - (√15/8)M₁(2)M₂(2) + (15/16)M₂²(2)).
- The neutrino energy is given by E_ν ≃ Q_μ(1 - Q_μ/(2M_f) + ...), where Q_μ = M_i + E_μ - M_f.
- The second-order terms in the amplitude M₁(2) for the 3/2 → 1/2 transition are estimated not to exceed several percent of the dominant first-order term.

## Context
This work extends the hyperfine effect analysis of muon capture, previously developed by Balashov (1967) and Walecka (1976) using first-order non-relativistic Hamiltonians, by including second-order corrections in 1/M. The author also extends the alignment effect analysis for transitions |Jπ⟩ → |J±1 π⟩ previously studied in Barabanov (1996) under the approximation of dominating Gamow-Teller matrix element. The general expression for neutrino angular distribution enables estimation of non-leading matrix element contributions for any given transition, which is relevant for resolving the discrepancy between PCAC predictions and measurements of g_P in ⁶Li (Deu68) and ²⁸Si (Bru95).
