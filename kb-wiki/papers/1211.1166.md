# Effects of chain decays, radial flow and $U_{A}(1)$ restoration on the low-mass dilepton enhancement in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au reactions

> In sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions PHENIX reported a significant enhancement in the low-mass region (0.1 < m_ee < 0.7 GeV) of the dielectron spectrum, which is still not fully understood. Several theoretical works and an indirect measurement suggest that, due to the possible restoration of the U_A(1) part of the chiral symmetry in a hot and dense medium, the mass of the eta' meson may substantially decrease. This work reports on a statistically acceptable description of the PHENIX low-mass dilepton enhancement using a radial flow dominated meson spectra, chain decays of long-lived resonances and an in-medium eta' mass modification.

# Effects of chain decays, radial flow and $U_{A}(1)$ restoration on the low-mass dilepton enhancement in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au reactions

## Key claim
A statistically acceptable description of the PHENIX low-mass dilepton enhancement in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions can be achieved by combining radial flow dominated meson spectra, chain decays of long-lived resonances, and an in-medium $\eta'$ mass modification, but the analysis remains preliminary with further systematic studies needed.

## Method
The authors used the EXODUS Monte-Carlo event generator, tuned to PHENIX acceptance, to simulate dielectron spectra from $\pi^0$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\eta$, $\eta'$, and $\phi$ decays including all direct and cascade decay channels producing lepton pairs. Meson spectra were generated using a hydrodynamical formula (Eq. 4) fitted simultaneously to $\pi^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ data from Adler (2003), with Core-Halo corrections applied to pion spectra. The in-medium $\eta'$ mass was treated as a parameter governing the overall size of the $\eta'$ excess via a modified Hagedorn formula, while the slope parameter $B^{-1}$ governed the shape of the low-$\pt$ trapped component.

## Key numbers
- Fit to PHENIX data with radial flow and chain decays alone: $\chi^2$/NDF = 24.1/12, CL = 1.9% (mass range 0.12–1.2 GeV), compared to the original PHENIX cocktail with $\chi^2$/NDF = 50.3/12, CL = 0.01%
- Best fit including in-medium $\eta'$ mass modification: $\chi^2$/NDF = 15.2/12, CL = 23%, with $m_{\eta'}^* = 520$ MeV and $B^{-1} = 55$ MeV
- In-medium $\eta'$ masses below 380 MeV excluded at CL > 99.9% regardless of $B^{-1}$
- Hydrodynamical fit parameters: $\langle u_T \rangle^2 = 0.21 \pm 0.01$, $T_0 = 178 \pm 4$ MeV, $\alpha$ fixed to 0, with $\chi^2$/NDF = 63/49, CL = 8.6%
- Vacuum $\eta'$ mass: $m_{\eta'} = 958$ MeV; vacuum $\eta'$ width: $\Gamma_{\eta'} = 1.30 \pm 0.07$ keV
- Characteristic condensate temperature used: $T_\mathrm{cond} = 170$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the indirect observation of in-medium $\eta'$ mass modification from Bose-Einstein correlation measurements by Vertesi (2009, 2010) to the dilepton channel, using the $\eta' \to e^+e^-\gamma$ Dalitz decay. It addresses the PHENIX low-mass dilepton enhancement reported by Adare (2010), which remained unexplained by standard hadronic cocktails. The analysis contrasts with NA60 results at SPS energies (Arnaldi, 2006) where $\rho$ broadening was observed, and with NA44 S+Pb data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.4$ GeV (Kharzeev) which showed no significant $\eta'$ mass drop, suggesting an energy-dependent onset of chiral dynamics between SPS and RHIC. The results enable further investigation of $U_A(1)$ restoration effects and call for more detailed experimental data on transverse momentum and centrality dependence.
