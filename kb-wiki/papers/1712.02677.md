# Some aspects of global Lambda polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> Large orbital angular momentum can be generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions, and part of it is expected to be converted into final particle's polarization due to the spin-orbit coupling. Within the framework of A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, we studied the vorticity-induced polarization of $Λ$ hyperons at the midrapidity region $|η|<1$ in Au-Au collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\sim200$ GeV. Our results show that the global polarization decreases with the collisional energies and is consistent with the recent STAR measurements. This behavior can be understood by less asymmetry of participant matter in the midrapidity region due to faster expansion of fireball at higher energies. As another evidence, we discuss how much the angular momentum is deposited in different rapidity region. The result supports our asymmetry argument.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Λ hyperons in non-central heavy-ion collisions decreases with increasing collision energy, which the authors attribute to less asymmetry of participant matter in the midrapidity region due to faster longitudinal expansion of the fireball at higher energies, rather than to the total orbital angular momentum of the system.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate Au-Au collisions at energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They coarse-grained the parton collective motion to obtain hydrodynamic quantities including fluid velocity, temperature, and thermal vorticity, then calculated the Λ spin four-vector from the thermal vorticity using the Becattini formula. The global polarization was obtained by averaging the spin three-vector over all Λ hyperons in the midrapidity region |η|<1 and projecting onto the direction of the system's orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Global Λ polarization decreases from about 2.1% at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to about 0.2% at √s_NN = 200 GeV in Au-Au collisions at 20%-50% centrality
- Feed-down Λ hyperons from resonance decays suppress the global polarization by about 17% from the primary-only result
- Simulations were performed at eight collision energies: 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Two impact parameters were used: b = 7 fm and b = 9 fm, corresponding to the 20%-50% centrality class
- 10^4 events were generated for each collision energy for the angular momentum analysis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Li et al., 2017) on the energy dependence of Λ polarization in heavy-ion collisions, providing further evidence that the polarization's decrease with energy is due to less asymmetric participant matter at midrapidity at higher energies. The results are consistent with STAR collaboration measurements (STAR, 2017) and with hydrodynamic and transport model calculations by Karpenko et al. (2016), Xie et al. (2017), and Sun et al. (2017). The finding that angular momentum per parton in the midrapidity region decreases with increasing collision energy, contrary to the total angular momentum, supports the asymmetry argument and enables a better understanding of the relationship between initial geometry and final-state polarization in heavy-ion collisions.
