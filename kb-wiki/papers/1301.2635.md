# Pre-Equilibrium Radial Flow from Central Shock-Wave Collisions in AdS5

> Using gauge/gravity duality, central ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions are modelled as collisions of shock waves in five-dimensional asymptotic AdS space. For early times after the collision, it is possible to analytically match the metric from the past to the future light-cone. This allows extraction of the pre-equilibrium energy-momentum tensor of the strongly coupled, large N gauge theory. For central collisions, this allows qualitative statements concerning the build-up of radial flow at mid-rapidity in AA and pA collisions. We find that the early-time radial flow buildup is identical to that expected from ideal hydrodynamics with an entropy density proportional to the square root of the product of the matter densities in the individual "nuclei".

## Key claim

Using gauge/gravity duality, the early-time radial flow buildup in central shock-wave collisions is identical to that expected from ideal hydrodynamics, even though the system as a whole is far from equilibrium; this result holds only at mid-rapidity and close to the AdS boundary where the series expansion converges.

## Method

The authors model central ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions as head-on collisions of gravitational shock waves in five-dimensional asymptotic AdS space, with arbitrary azimuthally symmetric transverse density profiles. They analytically match the metric from the past to the future light-cone by solving Einstein's equations order by order in an early-time power series expansion in Milne coordinates, then transform the resulting line element to Fefferman-Graham form to extract the boundary energy-momentum tensor of the strongly coupled large N gauge theory.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at early times: \(\epsilon = \frac{2}{\kappa} \rho^2(r) \tau^2\) for AA collisions, where \(\rho(r)\) is the transverse charge density and \(\kappa = N_c^2/(2\pi^2)\) for \({\cal N}=4\) SYM
- Radial flow velocity: \(u^r = -\frac{\rho^\prime(r)}{3 \rho(r)}\tau + {\cal O}(\tau^3)\) for AA collisions, giving \(\partial_\tau u^r = -\partial_r \ln(\rho(r))/3\)
- For pA collisions, radial flow: \(u^r = -\frac{\rho_1^\prime(r)\rho_2(r)+\rho_1(r)\rho_2^\prime(r)}{6 \rho_1(r)\rho_2(r)}\tau\)
- Energy-momentum tensor components at leading order: \(T_{\tau\tau} = \frac{2}{\kappa} \rho_1(r) \rho_2(r) \tau^2\), \(\tau^{-2}T_{\xi\xi} = -\frac{6}{\kappa}\rho_1(r) \rho_2(r) \tau^2\), \(T_{rr} = \frac{4}{\kappa} \rho_1(r)\rho_2(r)\tau^2\), \(T_{\phi\phi} = 4\frac{\rho_1(r) \rho_2(r)}{\kappa} r^2 \tau^2\) (all with \({\cal O}(\tau^4)\) corrections)

## Context

This work extends the matching technique of Grumiller and Romatschke (2008) for constant-density shock waves to arbitrary azimuthally symmetric transverse profiles, generalizing previous numerical simulations by Chesler and Yaffe (2010) and Wu and Romatschke (2011) that assumed translational invariance in the transverse plane. The results provide the foundation for incorporating full transverse dynamics into numerical solutions of the Einstein equations in AdS5 (van der Schee, 2012), enabling future studies of equilibration including radial flow effects that could be compared to experimental observations at RHIC and the LHC.
