# Charm-hadron reconstruction through three body decay in hadronic collisions using Machine Learning

> Studies of heavy-quark (charm and beauty) production in hadronic and nuclear collisions provide excellent testing grounds for the theory of strong interaction, quantum chromodynamics. Heavy-quarks are produced predominantly in the initial hard partonic interactions, allowing them to witness the entire evolution process. The charm hadrons are produced in two ways. Firstly, the prompt charm hadrons which are formed from the charm quark hadronization which are produced directly from the initial hard-scatterings or the decay of other excited charm states. Secondly, the nonprompt charm hadrons which are produced from the decay of beauty hadrons. The produced charm hadrons then usually decay to light-flavor hadrons or leptons via two or three body decay. The reconstruction of charm hadrons is challenging due to the large combinatorial background as well as the difficulty of distinguishing between prompt and non-prompt charm hadrons. In this work, we use machine learning models--XGboost and Deep Neural Network--to reconstruct $Λ_c^{+} (udc)$ hadrons via its three body final state decay channel, $Λ_c^{+} \rightarrow pK^0_s$ and $K^0_s \rightarrow π^{+}π^{-}$. Using several experimentally available features of the decay daughters, these models can separate signal from background and identify prompt and nonprompt candidates with nearly 99\% accuracy. This method performs an unbinned track-level reconstruction since the $Λ_c$ candidates are tagged directly from their decay daughters. The necessary data for this study are simulated in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13.6$~TeV using PYTHIA8 (Monash) model.

## Key claim

Machine learning models (XGBoost and Deep Neural Network) can reconstruct Lambda_c+ hadrons via the three-body decay channel Lambda_c+ -> p K0_s (with K0_s -> pi+ pi-) and separate signal from background while identifying prompt and nonprompt candidates with nearly 99% accuracy, using only experimentally available features of the decay daughters in simulated pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13.6 TeV.

## Method

The authors simulated pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13.6 TeV using the PYTHIA8 (Monash) event generator with charm and beauty enhanced production for training data, then applied momentum smearing and three-dimensional primary vertex smearing to mimic experimental conditions. They trained XGBoost and Deep Neural Network models on six input features (invariant mass, pseudoproper decay length, and distance of closest approach for both the Lambda_c and the K0_s) to perform a three-class classification into background, prompt Lambda_c, and nonprompt Lambda_c candidates. The trained models were then evaluated on a separate minimum-bias simulated dataset to reconstruct the transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of prompt and nonprompt Lambda_c hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The XGBoost model identifies background candidates with almost 100% accuracy, prompt candidates with almost 99% accuracy, and nonprompt candidates with almost 96% accuracy; the DNN model reaches 94% accuracy for nonprompt tagging.
- The XGB model has a 3.5% misidentification rate for nonprompt candidates as prompt candidates, while the DNN model has a 6% misidentification rate for the same class.
- The branching ratio for Lambda_c+ -> p K0_s is 1.59 +/- 0.07%, and for K0_s -> pi+ pi- is 69.20 +/- 0.05%.
- The nonprompt Lambda_c yield is almost one order of magnitude smaller than the prompt yield at low transverse momentum, with the difference decreasing and approaching similar values beyond pT >= 16 GeV/c.
- The invariant mass selection window used is 1.8 < M_inv^Lambda_c < 2.8 GeV and 0.45 < M_inv^K0_s < 0.55 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to reconstruct and tag prompt and nonprompt J/psi and D0 mesons using machine learning models via two-body final state decays, as done by Prasad (2023) and Goswami (2024). The method addresses the challenge of reconstructing charm hadrons through three-body decays, where combinatorial background is large and the production fraction of nonprompt charm hadrons is only about 10% of inclusive charm production. This approach enables unbinned track-level reconstruction of Lambda_c hadrons directly from decay daughters, eliminating the need for signal extraction via invariant mass fitting, and can be applied to experimental data from LHC experiments such as ALICE to study charm and beauty hadronization mechanisms.
