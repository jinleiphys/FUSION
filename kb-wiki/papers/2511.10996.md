# Accuracy and Applicability of the Hartle-Thorne and Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu Methods for Modeling Rotating Neutron Stars

> Neutron stars, which are composed of extremely dense nuclear matter, serve as natural laboratories to study nuclear interactions beyond the terrestrial experiments. Recent researches have actively explored how the equation of state (EoS) can be constrained by observed neutron star masses and radii, and how nuclear interactions affect their macroscopic properties. Most of these studies, however, rely on the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, which assumed static, spherically symmetric neutron stars. Since neutron stars are rotating objects and thus axisymmetrically deformed, the TOV calculation may be insufficient to capture their realistic structure. In this work, we investigate the influence of nuclear matter properties on the physical quantities of rotating neutron stars using two approaches: the perturbative Hartle-Thorne (HT) method and fully general relativistic Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method. For nuclear EoS parameter sets, we emamine the OMEG series, in which the slope of the symmetry energy $L$ is systematically varied. We find that rotational effects lead to a noticeable increase in the stellar radius, which depends sensitively on values of $L$. Additionally, focusing on the rotational deformation, we show that the results obtained by these two methods deviate each other even for the slowly rotating case such as $Ω=200$ Hz. These results reveal that, for detailed discussions on the internal structure and stability of rotating neutron stars, the fully general relativistic method such as KEH is indispensable.

## Key claim

The perturbative Hartle-Thorne (HT) method and the fully general relativistic Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method yield results that deviate from each other even for slowly rotating neutron stars at Ω = 200 Hz, and the deviation becomes substantial (6.9–8.4% radius discrepancy for a 1.4 M⊙ star) at 800 Hz, contrary to the common assumption that the HT method is accurate for slow rotation.

## Method

The authors computed rotating neutron star structures using both the perturbative Hartle-Thorne (HT) method (expanding the metric to second order in angular velocity Ω) and the fully general relativistic Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method (solving coupled elliptic field equations for axisymmetric spacetimes via the Cook-Shapiro-Teukolsky approach). They employed the OMEG series of relativistic mean-field equations of state (OMEG1, OMEG2, OMEG3), which share identical saturation density (0.1484 fm⁻³), effective nucleon mass ratio (0.620), and incompressibility (256.0 MeV), while varying only the slope of the symmetry energy L (70.00, 45.00, and 20.00 MeV respectively). The EoSs were matched to the MYN13 crust EoS at low densities under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- For OMEG1 (L = 70.00 MeV) at Ω = 800 Hz, the KEH radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star is 14.91 km, while the HT radius is 13.65 km, giving a 6.9–8.4% discrepancy between methods for 1.4 M⊙ stars at 800 Hz across all OMEG models.
- At Ω = 200 Hz, the radius ratio (Rpol/Req) from KEH is 0.98922 for OMEG1, while HT gives 0.99218, a relative error of 0.30%; for OMEG3 the KEH ratio is 0.98789 and HT is 0.99312, a 0.53% error.
- At Ω = 800 Hz, the radius ratio from KEH is 0.67871 for OMEG1, while HT gives 0.86239, a 27.06% relative error; for OMEG3 the KEH ratio is 0.76349 and HT is 0.87987, a 15.24% error.
- The maximum mass for OMEG1 at Ω = 800 Hz is 2.22 M⊙ (KEH) versus 2.21 M⊙ (HT), with corresponding radii of 12.30 km and 12.09 km (about 2% difference).
- For non-rotating (Ω = 0) configurations, the maximum mass is 2.14 M⊙ for OMEG1 with radius 11.71 km, and the 1.4 M⊙ radius is 12.77 km.

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of perturbative and fully relativistic rotating neutron star models initiated by Kacskovics (2022), who found that HT accuracy deteriorates above 400 Hz, and by Kwon (2025), who studied Skyrme-type EoSs with the KEH method. The use of the OMEG EoS series, where only the slope of the symmetry energy L varies while other nuclear matter properties are fixed, enables isolation of the L effect on rotational deformation, building on the correlation between L and neutron star radii reported by Sun (2024) and Lopes (2024). The results enable future studies to determine the rotation frequency threshold below which the computationally simpler HT method is reliable for given nuclear EoS parameters, and above which fully relativistic methods like KEH are indispensable for accurate modeling of neutron star internal structure and stability.
