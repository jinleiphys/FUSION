# $Υ(1S)$ transverse momentum spectra through dissociation and regeneration in heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the transition between a quarkonium state and an unbound heavy quark-antiquark pair through gluo-dissociation and inelastic parton scattering using a partonic picture that interpolates between the formal limits based on potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at different temperatures. While the thermal width increases with momentum and temperature, the quarkonium regeneration is affected by the heavy quark distribution function which depends on the diffusion constant. By solving the Boltzmann equation with the dissociation and regeneration terms, we investigate the medium modifications of quarkonium momentum spectra. Our numerical results indicate that the $Υ(1S)$ $R_{AA}$ at high transverse momentum are influenced by the regeneration effects depending on the heavy quark diffusion. In this picture, the published CMS data that show an almost transverse momentum independence can be explained by the interplay between the suppression by dissociation and enhancement by regeneration at low and high transverse momenta, respectively. With the same input, we also calculate the transverse momentum dependence of the $Υ(1S)$ $v_2$ and show that it lies within the limits of the available data.

# Wiki Page: Υ(1S) Transverse Momentum Spectra through Dissociation and Regeneration in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The observed near-transverse-momentum-independence of the Υ(1S) nuclear modification factor (R_AA) in CMS data can be explained by the interplay between suppression from dissociation at low transverse momentum and enhancement from regeneration at high transverse momentum, provided the heavy quark diffusion constant takes values D(2πT) ≈ 5–8. The regeneration effects at high transverse momentum depend on the heavy quark diffusion constant, which the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors solved a Boltzmann transport equation for Υ(1S) including collision terms for gluo-dissociation (Υ + g → b + b̅) and inelastic parton scattering (Υ + p → b + b̅ + p, with p = g, q, q̅), together with their inverse reactions for regeneration. The scattering amplitudes were derived using hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory with an effective vertex from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, interpolating between formal limits from potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at different temperatures. The heavy quark distribution functions entering the regeneration term were obtained by solving a Fokker-Planck equation with a diffusion constant, and the medium evolution was modeled as a Bjorken expansion at mid-rapidity with initial temperature T₀ = 550 MeV (for √s_NN = 5.02 TeV) and T₀ = 525 MeV (for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV).

## Key numbers

- Υ(1S) thermal width: at low temperature where binding energy exceeds Debye mass, gluo-dissociation is effective for quarkonium at rest; at high temperature where m_D ≫ E, inelastic parton scattering dominates; the total thermal width grows with momentum, reaching ~100 MeV at q_T = 15 GeV
- Regeneration term magnitude: C_reg / f_Υ ~ 10 MeV at q_T = 15 GeV, compared to Γ_diss ~ 100 MeV, giving regeneration effects at most ~10% relative to dissociation
- Final Υ(1S) number reduced to approximately 40% of initial (R_AA ≈ 0.4 at low momentum, independent of diffusion constant)
- Centrality-integrated CMS R_AA values: 0.453 ± 0.014(stat) ± 0.046(syst) at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV; 0.376 ± 0.013(stat) ± 0.035(syst) at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Heavy quark diffusion constant range consistent with data: D(2πT) ≈ 5–8
- Initial ratio of Υ(1S) to b-b̅ pairs: σ_pp→Υ / σ_pp→b-b̅ = 1.76 × 10⁻³
- Parameters used: α_s = 0.4, a₀ = 0.14 fm, b quark mass m = 4.8 GeV, initial time t₀ = 0.3 fm/c, initial volume V₀ = 60 fm³

## Context

This work extends kinetic approaches to quarkonium suppression by treating both dissociation and regeneration consistently through partonic cross sections that interpolate pNRQCD limits, rather than using separate coalescence models or detailed balance assumptions as in earlier work by Rapp (2017) and Strickland (2017). The authors note that their thermal widths qualitatively agree with Lee-V (2013), and their D-dependence of R_AA is consistent with findings by Chen (2017) based on Langevin equation and Wigner function approaches. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming experimental data and lattice QCD computations of the heavy quark diffusion constant, and suggest that bottomonium regeneration may be more significant than previously assumed despite the low b-quark density.
