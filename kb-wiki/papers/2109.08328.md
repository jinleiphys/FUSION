# Cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach for a mean-field description of nuclear rotations near the drip line

> To describe the yrast states in weakly bound nuclei, I directly solve the coordinate-space cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation on a three-dimensional lattice with the continuum states discretized in a box. After the numerical demonstration for the ground-state band in a medium-mass nucleus, I apply the newly-developed method to neutron-rich even-$N$ Mg isotopes. I find that the appearance of the significantly low $I^π=2^+$ state in $^{40}$Mg is mainly due to the suppression of pairing. The calculation predicts that the $2^+$ state in $^{42}$Mg appears as high in energy as in $^{34\text{-}38}$Mg whereas the triaxial deformation is enhanced in non-zero spin states. The present numerical framework offers a practical approach for investigating the near yrast states systematically and revealing structures unique in drip-line nuclei.

## Key claim

The appearance of the significantly low \(I^\pi=2^+\) state in \(^{40}\)Mg is mainly due to the suppression of pairing, but the calculation still underestimates the measured moment of inertia, indicating that the weak-binding effect reduces the pairing correlation and enhances the moment of inertia, though the observed value lies between the results with and without pairing.

## Method

The author directly solves the coordinate-space cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice with a box boundary condition, discretizing continuum states in the box. The KSB Hamiltonian is diagonalized directly using the ScaLAPACK pdsyev subroutine, with reflection symmetries imposed to reduce computational time. The method is applied to the ground-state band in \(^{172}\)Hf for validation, then to neutron-rich even-\(N\) Mg isotopes using the SkM* and SLy4 functionals combined with the Yamagami-Shimizu-Nakatsukasa pairing energy-density functional.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic moment of inertia \(\mathcal{J}^{(1)}\) for \(^{172}\)Hf at \(\omega_{\rm rot}=0.05\) MeV converges with box size about 11–12 fm; calculated rms radius is 5.37 fm for neutrons and 5.28 fm for protons.
- For \(^{34}\)Mg, the calculated \(R_{4/2}\) value is 2.96, compared to the measured value of 3.06.
- Chemical potential for neutrons in \(^{34,36,38,40}\)Mg: \(-4.17\), \(-3.26\), \(-2.42\), \(-1.61\) MeV respectively.
- For \(^{40}\)Mg without pairing, \(\mathcal{J}^{(1)} = 6.8\) MeV\(^{-1}\); the observed \(\mathcal{J}^{(1)}\) is 6.0 MeV\(^{-1}\); the oblate solution has \(\mathcal{J}^{(1)} = 1.9\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{42}\)Mg, \(\mathcal{J}^{(1)}\) at \(\omega_{\rm rot}=0.1\) MeV changes by about 1.3% when box size is varied; the \(R_{4/2}\) value becomes smaller by 6% when enlarging the box from \(M=10\) to 14.
- Computational time per iteration: 13.0, 52.5, and 91.0 core-hours for mesh sizes \(M=12\), 14, and 16 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the cranked shell model within the nuclear energy-density functional approach to weakly bound nuclei near the drip line, building on the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism of Dobaczewski (1984) and the cranked Kohn-Sham approach of Bender (2003) and others. The study addresses the "island of inversion" for Mg isotopes, where earlier configuration-mixing calculations with the Gogny force by Rodriguez (2002, 2016) and the relativistic approach by Yao (2011) failed to reproduce the low \(2^+\) state in \(^{40}\)Mg. The present framework enables systematic investigation of near-yrast states in drip-line nuclei and reveals that the suppression of pairing, rather than shape fluctuation, drives the low \(2^+\) energy in \(^{40}\)Mg, while predicting triaxial deformation in \(^{42}\)Mg.
