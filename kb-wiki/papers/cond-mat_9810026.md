# Critical Exponents for Nuclear Multifragmentation: dynamical lattice model

> We present a dynamical and dissipative lattice model, designed to mimic nuclear multifragmentation. Monte-Carlo simulations with this model show clear signature of critical behaviour and reproduce experimentally observed correlations. In particular, using techniques devised for finite systems, we could obtain two of its critical exponents, whose values are in agreement with those of the universality class to which nuclear multifragmentation is supposed to belong.

# Critical exponents for nuclear multifragmentation: dynamical lattice model

## Key claim

The authors claim that a simple dynamical lattice model, with a Fermi-gas caloric curve and a specific ratio between surface and bulk relaxation times (parameter a=0.998), reproduces the critical exponents τ and γ of the 3D percolation universality class, which nuclear multifragmentation is believed to belong to. The hedge is that different values of the model parameter produce different exponents, and this sensitivity will require further investigation.

## Method

The authors simulate nuclear multifragmentation using a 6x6x6 cluster of up spins (initial mass 216) on a 32x32x32 cubic lattice, driven by an Ising Hamiltonian with nearest and next-nearest couplings. The system evolves under a Metropolis mass-conserving Kawasaki-like dynamics, where spin flips transfer nucleons from the inner to the outer perimeter; after each flip, connectivity is checked using a burning algorithm, and detached clusters are recorded as fragments. The excitation energy is decreased by a fixed factor a=0.998 every 100 steps to represent bulk radiative decay, and a Fermi-gas caloric curve relates temperature to excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Initial nucleus mass: 216 nucleons (6x6x6 cluster of up spins), chosen for comparison with experimental and theoretical data from Campi (1986) and Gilkes et al.
- Critical temperature: T_c = 8.50 (in units of the Onsager critical temperature for the 2D nearest-neighbor Ising model)
- Critical exponent τ = 2.18, obtained from the slope λ₃/₂ of the log-log plot of the third versus second moments of the fragment distribution
- Critical exponent γ = 1.80, obtained from fitting the second moment M₂(T) to a power law |T - T_c|^γ on both sides of the transition
- Model parameter a = 0.998 (constant decremental factor for excitation energy every 100 steps)
- 2000 events simulated for each initial temperature value
- Typical run time: approximately 8 hours on a 433 DEC Alpha workstation

## Context

This work extends the percolation-based interpretation of nuclear multifragmentation, following the approach of Campi (1986) and the experimental analysis of Gilkes et al., who identified critical exponents in multifragmentation data. The model is similar to lattice gas models used previously for wetting and drop formation, and to earlier work by the same group on evaporation and fragment distribution moments. The results support the hypothesis that nuclear multifragmentation belongs to the 3D percolation universality class, and the model's tunable parameter allows future exploration of different sets of critical exponents.
