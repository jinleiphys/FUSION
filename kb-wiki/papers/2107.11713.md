# QLBT: A linear Boltzmann transport model for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma of quasi-particles

> We develop a new heavy quark transport model, QLBT, to simulate the dynamical propagation of heavy quarks inside the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our QLBT model is based on the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model with the ideal QGP replaced by a collection of quasi-particles to account for the non-perturbative interactions among quarks and gluons of the hot QGP. The thermal masses of quasi-particles are fitted to the equation of state from lattice QCD simulations using the Bayesian statistical analysis method. Combining QLBT with our advanced hybrid fragmentation-coalescence hadronization approach, we calculate the nuclear modification factor $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $D$ mesons at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider. By comparing our QLBT calculation to the experimental data on the $D$ meson $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and $v_2$, we extract the heavy quark transport parameter $\hat{q}$ and diffusion coefficient $D_\mathrm{s}$ in the temperature range of $1-4~T_\mathrm{c}$, and compare them with the lattice QCD results and other phenomenological studies.

## Key claim

The QLBT model improves upon the standard linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) approach by replacing the ideal quark-gluon plasma with a quasi-particle description whose thermal masses are fitted to lattice QCD equations of state, enabling extraction of heavy quark transport coefficients in the temperature range of 1–4 Tc that are consistent with both lattice QCD calculations and other phenomenological studies.

## Method

The authors constructed a quasi-particle model where gluon and light quark thermal masses are temperature-dependent functions of a running coupling g(T), with the coupling parameters fitted via Bayesian Markov Chain Monte Carlo to the entropy density from Wuppertal-Budapest and Hot QCD lattice data. They then implemented this quasi-particle medium into the linear Boltzmann transport framework for heavy quarks, including both elastic scattering (with leading-order pQCD matrix elements evaluated using quasi-particle masses) and inelastic medium-induced gluon emission via the higher-twist formalism. The model was calibrated by comparing calculated D meson R_AA and v_2 at RHIC and LHC to experimental data from STAR and CMS, using a Gaussian process emulator and Bayesian analysis to extract the energy-dependent strong coupling parameters A and B for heavy-quark-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range for transport coefficient extraction: 1–4 Tc, with Tc = 150 MeV for Wuppertal-Budapest data and Tc = 154 MeV for Hot QCD data
- Fitted parameters for g(T) from WB lattice data: a = 2.063 ± 0.1705, b = 0.836 ± 0.1186, c = 7.792 ± 0.9857, d = 0.492 ± 0.054
- Fitted parameters for g(T) from HQ lattice data: a = 6.8899 ± 1.055, b = 0.398 ± 0.462, c = 54.9825 ± 12.76, d = 0.449 ± 0.0432
- Fitted parameters for heavy-quark coupling α_s(E) using WB EoS: A = 0.073 ± 0.004, B = 1.204 ± 0.015
- Fitted parameters for heavy-quark coupling α_s(E) using HQ EoS: A = 0.075 ± 0.0034, B = 1.195 ± 0.0157
- Hydrodynamic starting time: τ0 = 0.6 fm; specific shear viscosity: η/s = 0.08

## Context

This work extends the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model of Wang (2013), He (2015), Cao (2016), and Xing (2019) by incorporating a quasi-particle description of the QGP medium, following approaches by Plumari (2011), Gossiaux (2009), and Berrehrah (2014) for modeling non-perturbative interactions. The extracted spatial diffusion coefficient D_s is compared with lattice QCD results from Ding (2012) and Banerjee (2011), as well as phenomenological studies by Song (2015), Scardina (2017), Riek (2010), Gossiaux (2008), and Xu (2017). The model enables simultaneous description of D meson R_AA and v_2 at both RHIC and LHC energies, and provides temperature-dependent transport coefficients that can be used in future studies of heavy quark dynamics and jet quenching phenomenology.
