# Matching the QCD and Hadron Sectors and Medium Dependent Meson Masses; Hadronization in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The recent developments on the "vector manifestation" of chiral symmetry by Harada and Yamawaki provide a compelling evidence for, and "refine," the in-medium scaling of hadronic properties in dense/hot matter (call it ``BR scaling") proposed by the authors in 1991. We reinterpret the Harada-Yamawaki result obtained in a Wilsonian renormalization-group approach to hidden local symmetry theory matched to QCD at near the chiral scale in terms of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and predict that the vector meson mass in medium should scale $m_ρ^\star/m_ρ\sim (\la\bar{q}q\ra^\star/\la\bar{q}q\ra)^{1/2}$ from $n=0$ up to $\sim n=n_0$ (where $n_0$ is nuclear matter density) and then go over to the linear scaling $m_ρ^\star/m_ρ\sim \la\bar{q}q\ra^\star/\la\bar{q}q\ra$ up to the chiral transition density $n_c$ at which the mass is to vanish (in the chiral limit) according to the vector manifestation fixed point. In the regime of the linear scaling above $n_0$, the (vector) gauge coupling constant should fall linearly in $\la\bar{q}q\ra^\star$, vanishing at the critical point, with the width of the vector meson becoming steeply narrower. We suggest the in-medium vector meson mass, {\em both} parametric and pole, which goes smoothly to zero with increasing density, to be an order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration. Some issues related to recent RHIC observations are qualitatively discussed in the appendices. Our main conclusion that follows from these considerations is that the movement towards chiral restoration can be reliably described in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio mean field, with constituent quarks as variables, although there is probably density discontinuity following chiral restoration.

## Key claim

The authors reinterpret Harada and Yamawaki's vector manifestation of chiral symmetry, obtained via Wilsonian renormalization-group matching of hidden local symmetry to QCD, in terms of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. They argue that the in-medium vector meson mass should follow a square-root scaling with the quark condensate from zero density up to nuclear matter density, then transition to a linear scaling with the condensate up to the chiral transition density, with the vector meson mass serving as an order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors match the vector and axial-vector correlators of hidden local symmetry theory to QCD operator product expansion at a scale near the chiral scale (approximately 4πfπ), then use renormalization-group equations to flow the parameters down to the on-shell scale. They reinterpret this matching procedure within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with constituent quarks as variables, using the NJL gap equation to relate the in-medium quark condensate to the dynamically generated quark mass and the vector meson mass. The medium dependence is incorporated by adding positive-energy nucleon states that contribute negatively to the quark condensate relative to the negative-energy sea.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate in vacuum is ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(250 MeV)³, obtained with a covariant cutoff Λ₄ = 715 MeV for Nf = 2 and Nc = 3
- The critical density for chiral restoration in the NJL model with a cutoff of 700 MeV and bare quark mass m̄ ≈ 5 MeV is approximately 5n₀, where n₀ is normal nuclear matter density
- At nuclear matter density, the quark condensate ratio ⟨q̄q⟩*/⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ 0.64, giving fπ*/fπ ≈ 0.8, using the pion-nucleon sigma term ΣπN = 46 MeV
- The vector meson mass scaling at nuclear matter density is m_V*/m_V(n₀) ≈ 0.78, consistent with the analysis of Song et al.
- The critical temperature in the NJL model is Tc ≳ 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends and refines the "BR scaling" proposed by Brown and Rho (1991), which posited that hadron masses drop in dense/hot medium. The authors reinterpret the vector manifestation of Harada and Yamawaki (reviewed in their Physics Reports) as providing a rigorous foundation for BR scaling through Wilsonian matching to QCD. The paper connects to the Rapp-Wambach configuration mixing approach, arguing that BR scaling and RW mixing "fuse" at lower energies for the ρ-sobar excitation but "defuse" for the elementary ρ meson. The work enables a description of chiral restoration using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio mean field with constituent quarks, and provides a framework for understanding hadronization in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC.
