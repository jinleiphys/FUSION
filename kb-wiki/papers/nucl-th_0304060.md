# On the linear term in the nuclear symmetry energy

> The nuclear symmetry energy calculated in the RPA from the pairing plus symmetry force Hamiltonian with equidistant single-nucleon levels is for mass number A=48 approximately proportional to T(T+1.025), where T is the isospin quantum number. An isovector character of the pair field assumed to produce the observed odd-even mass staggering is essential for this result. The RPA contribution to the symmetry energy cannot be simulated by adding to the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov energy a term proportional to the isospin variance in the Bogolyubov quasiparticle vacuum, and there are significant corrections to the approximation which consist in adding half the isocranking angular velocity. The present calculation employs a smaller single-nucleon level spacing than used in a previous investigation of the model.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy calculated in the RPA from the pairing plus symmetry force Hamiltonian with equidistant single-nucleon levels is for mass number A=48 approximately proportional to T(T+1.025), where T is the isospin quantum number, with the exact value of the linear term coefficient depending on the single-nucleon level spacing (giving x=1.020 for eta=1.1 MeV and x=1.031 for eta=1.4 MeV).

## Method

The author used a Hamiltonian consisting of an independent-nucleon term with equidistant fourfold degenerate single-nucleon levels, an isobarically invariant isovector pairing force, and a symmetry force. Hartree-Bogolyubov optimal states were derived from a Routhian with a nucleon chemical potential placed midway between the lowest and highest single-nucleon energies, and the isocranking angular velocity was varied to produce a range of isospin projections. The RPA calculation employed a perturbative boson expansion of products of nucleon field operators, truncated to second order, with the resulting RPA operators modified to recover the exact commutation relations of the particle number and isospin operators.

## Key numbers

- For single-nucleon level spacing eta=1.1 MeV, the symmetry energy is proportional to T(T+1.020), extracted from the fit E2-E2,T=0 = (eta+kappa)T/2 - 0.033 x T^2
- For single-nucleon level spacing eta=1.4 MeV, the analysis gives x=1.031
- The total coefficient of T^2/2 in the Hartree-Bogolyubov part of the symmetry energy is eta+kappa = 3.3 MeV, derived from 2(134.4A^-1 - 203.6A^-4/3) for A=48
- The pair gap is Delta = 12/sqrt(A) = 1.7 MeV for A=48
- The number of equidistant fourfold degenerate single-nucleon levels is Omega = 24

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigation (Neergard, 2003) by using smaller single-nucleon level spacings (eta=1.1 and 1.4 MeV) than the Fermi gas estimate used previously, following criticism by Satula and Wyss (2002) and Glowacz, Satula and Wyss that the earlier value was unrealistically large. The results are consistent with empirical indications from Glowacz, Satula and Wyss that the linear term coefficient x is slightly larger than 1, and the paper shows that the RPA contribution cannot be simulated by adding a term proportional to the isospin variance to the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov energy as suggested by Satula and Wyss (2002).
