# Recent shell-model calculations of gamma-decay strength functions

> We present recent shell-model calculations of the gamma-decay in sd-pf and pf-shell nuclei. We focus on the M1 part of the dipole strength which was shown to exhibit interesting low-energy effects, in particular a low-energy enhancement which can have a considerable impact on the radiative neutron capture. We discuss the persistence of the shell effects in the nuclear quasi-continuum and the relation between the shape of the strength function at low energy and nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations show that the low-energy enhancement of the M1 gamma-decay strength function is most probable near closed shells and can be shifted to a scissors mode in deformed nuclei, but the simple empirical prescription assuming a deformation-independent low-energy limit is not adequate to describe the complex structure of this contribution.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations in the full sd-pf and pf model spaces using the SDPF-U and LNPS interactions, with a standard quenching factor of 0.75 applied to the spin part of the magnetic dipole operator. For each nucleus, they calculated 60 excited states in the spin range J=0-7 for even and odd-odd nuclei and J=1/2-15/2 for odd nuclei, yielding approximately 2×10^4 M1 matrix elements used in averaging the B(M1) values. The de-excitation strength function was obtained from the Bartholomew definition, combining the average B(M1) with the partial level density.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 2×10^4 M1 matrix elements were used in the averaging of B(M1) values for each nucleus.
- The empirical low-energy contribution to the M1 strength function uses parameters C ≈ 10^(-8) MeV^(-3) and η = 0.8 MeV^(-1), adjusted on shell-model results and experimental data.
- For light sd and pf nuclei, C values lower than 10^(-8) MeV^(-3) are obtained.
- C values between 1 and 6 × 10^(-8) MeV^(-3) are predicted by the shell model for spherical N ≈ 82 nuclei, reaching a maximum for 132Te (Sieja 2018).
- An error bar of 0.2 × 10^(-8) MeV^(-3) was included in all shell-model estimates of C.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of dipole strength functions in sd-pf and pf-shell nuclei (Sieja 2018, Schwengner and collaborators) and the D1M+QRPA approach (Martini 2016, Goriely 2016) which only addressed photoabsorption strength. The results challenge the deformation-dependent prescription proposed by Krticka (2019) and Goriely (2019), which assumed an exponentially decreasing C factor with deformation, showing that this simple formula fails for light vibrational nuclei. The findings enable improved modeling of radiative neutron capture cross sections for exotic neutron-rich nuclei, where the low-energy M1 enhancement can have considerable impact.
