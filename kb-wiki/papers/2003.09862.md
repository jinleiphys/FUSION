# Multi-fermion systems with contact theories

> We address the question of minimal requirements for the existence of quantum bound states. In particular, we demonstrate that a few-body system with zero-range momentum-independent two-body interactions is unstable against decay into clusters, if mixed-symmetry of its wave function is enforced. We claim that any theory in which the two-body scattering length is much larger than any other scale involved exhibits such instability. We exemplify this with the inability of the leading-order pionless effective field theory to describe stable states of $A>4$ nuclei. A finite interaction range is identified as a sufficient condition for a bound mixed-symmetry system. The minimal value of this range depends on the proximity of a system to unitarity, on the number of constituents, and on the particular realization of discrete scale invariance of the three-body spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that any fermionic system governed by zero-range, momentum-independent two-body interactions (i.e., in the contact or unitary limit) cannot support stable bound states with mixed-spatial-symmetry wave functions (P-wave states), regardless of the number of particles. This instability is demonstrated to be a universal feature of the unitary universality class, with the caveat that a finite interaction range is a sufficient condition to produce such bound states, and the minimal range required depends on the system's proximity to unitarity, particle number, and the discrete scale invariance of the three-body spectrum.

## Method

The authors employ the leading-order (LO) pionless effective field theory (EFT), which includes zero-range two- and three-body contact interactions regularized with a Gaussian regulator of cutoff \(\Lambda\) (inversely proportional to the interaction range). They solve the few-body Schrödinger equation using the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM) for systems up to \(A=8\) nucleons and for \((A+1)\)-body systems with a symmetric core of \(A\) distinguishable fermions plus one extra identical fermion. For larger systems up to \(A=150\), they use a single-channel Resonating Group Method (RGM) approximation treating the system as a two-body problem of a frozen symmetric core and the extra particle. The coupling constants are calibrated to reproduce the dimer and trimer binding energies, and the cutoff is varied to probe the contact limit (\(\Lambda \to \infty\)).

## Key numbers

*   For nuclear systems described by SU(4)-symmetric pionless EFT at LO, stable P-shell nuclei (e.g., \(^3\)H, \(^4\)H, \(^5\)He, \(^6\)Li, \(^7\)Li, \(^8\)Be) are found only below a system-dependent critical cutoff \(\Lambda_c < 2\) fm\(^{-1}\); for larger cutoffs (shorter-range interactions), these states become unstable against fragmentation.
*   The critical cutoffs \(\Lambda_c\) for \((A+1)\)-body systems with a symmetric core of \(A\) particles and one extra identical fermion increase approximately linearly with \(A\) for \(A \leq 6\) when the system is tuned to a ratio of trimer to dimer binding energies \(B(3)/B(2) = 3\) or \(4\).
*   At unitarity (realized with \(B(2)=0\) and \(B(3)=1\) MeV), the critical cutoff \(\Lambda_c\) for \((A+1)\)-body systems rises linearly with core size \(A\) up to at least \(A \sim 100\), with no indication of divergence, implying no stable mixed-symmetry states exist in the contact limit.
*   For systems away from unitarity with \(B(2)=1\) MeV and \(B(3)=4\) MeV, the critical cutoff \(\Lambda_c\) increases with \(A\) up to a maximum at some \(A^*\) and then decreases, showing that finite-range effects can stabilize P-wave states only for a limited range of particle numbers.

## Context

This work extends and systematizes earlier findings on the instability of mixed-symmetry states in contact theories, such as the instability of the four-body system of two-component fermions (Petrov, 2005) and the instability of \(^{16}\)O against four-\(\alpha\) decay in pionless EFT (Contessi, 2017). It also builds on the known result that a zero-range theory stabilizes three fermions of unequal mass only for a mass ratio above about 8.2 (Kartavtsev, 2007). The paper contradicts the possibility of describing stable P-wave nuclei (e.g., \(^6\)Li, \(^7\)Li, \(^8\)Be) with LO pionless EFT, implying that such systems belong to a different universality class requiring at least one additional low-energy constraint (e.g., a finite effective range or scattering volume). The results enable future work to identify whether the necessary correction is a perturbative refinement or defines a new universality class via a nonperturbative mechanism.
