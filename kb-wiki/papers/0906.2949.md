# The phase and pole structure of the N*(1535) in piN-->piN and gammaN-->piN

> The nature of some baryonic resonances is still an unresolved issue. The case of the N*(1535) is particularly interesting in this respect due to the nearby etaN threshold and interference with the N*(1650). The N*(1535) has been described as a threshold effect, as a genuine 3-quark resonance, or as dynamically generated from the interaction of the octet of baryons with the octet of mesons. In the scheme of dynamical generation, predictions for the interaction of the N*(1535) with the photon can be made. In this study, we simultaneously analyze the role of the N*(1535) in the piN-->piN and gammaN-->piN reactions and compare to the respective amplitudes from partial wave analyses. This test is very sensitive to the meson-baryon components of the N*(1535).

## Key claim

The N*(1535) resonance can be described as dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions, but a quantitative description of both piN to piN and gammaN to piN reactions requires the inclusion of a genuine (3-quark) resonance component, as indicated by the need for deviations from "natural" values of the subtraction constants in the model.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel model using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the Weinberg-Tomozawa term as the driving interaction for S-wave meson-baryon scattering, including channels piN, etaN, KLambda, and KSigma. They simultaneously analyze the S11 partial wave in piN to piN and gammaN to piN reactions, incorporating both dynamically generated poles and genuine resonance pole terms of the form 1/(z - M_r) for the N*(1535) and N*(1650), with the photoproduction amplitude constructed following the gauge-invariant formalism of Haberzettl (1997).

## Key numbers

- The dynamically generated pole from the "natural" subtraction constant scheme is found at z0 = 1653 - 145i MeV, with large couplings into KSigma and etaN
- The fine-tuned model of Inoue (2001) produces a pole at around 1537 - 37i MeV
- The pion decay constant values used are f_K = 1.22 f_pi and f_eta = 1.3 f_pi, with a more recent evaluation giving f_K = 1.193 f_pi
- The four subtraction constants a_piN, a_etaN, a_KLambda, and a_KSigma are treated as free parameters, with "natural" values around -2

## Context

This work extends the unitary chiral perturbation theory approach of Inoue (2001) and Jido (2007) for the N*(1535) by simultaneously analyzing both hadronic and photoproduction amplitudes at the multipole level, rather than just cross sections or helicity amplitudes. The study tests whether the N*(1535) is purely dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions or requires a genuine 3-quark component, following earlier work by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), and Nieves (2001). The results enable future studies of the interplay between dynamically generated and genuine resonance states in baryon spectroscopy, and provide a framework for testing hadronic models against polarization observables from partial wave analyses by Arndt (2002) and Drechsel (2007).
