# $Λ$$Λ$ interactions in finite-density QCD sum rules

> The properties of $Λ$-hyperons in pure $Λ$ matter are studied with the finite-density QCD sum rule approach. The first order quark and gluon condensates in $Λ$ nuclear matter are deduced from the chiral perturbation theory. The sum rule predictions are sensitive to the four-quark condensates, $<\bar{q}q>^2_ρ$ and $<\bar{q}q>_ρ<\bar{s}s>_ρ$, and the $πN$ sigma term. When $<\bar{q}q>^2_ρ$ is nearly independent of density and $<\bar{q}q>_ρ<\bar{s}s>_ρ$ depends strongly on density, we can obtain weakly attractive $Λ$$Λ$ potentials (about several MeV) in low $Λ$ density region, which agree with the information from the latest double $Λ$ hyper-nucleus experiments. The nearly no density dependence of $<\bar{q}q>^2_ρ$ and strong density dependence of $<\bar{q}q>_ρ<\bar{s}s>_ρ$ can be explained naturally if the properties of $<\bar{q}q>^2_ρ$ and $<\bar{q}q>_ρ<\bar{s}s>_ρ$ are assumed to be similar to those of $ππ$ and $\bar{K} K$ in nuclear medium, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that weakly attractive ΛΛ potentials (about several MeV) in low Λ density regions can be obtained from finite-density QCD sum rules, but only under the specific condition that the four-quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩²_ρ is nearly independent of density while ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ⟨s̄s⟩_ρ depends strongly on density, with the πN sigma term near its lower limit of 56 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the finite-density QCD sum rule approach previously used for ΛN interactions in nucleonic matter to study ΛΛ interactions in pure Λ matter. They derived first-order in-medium quark and gluon condensates from chiral perturbation theory, including the Λ matrix elements for scalar and twist-2 operators, and parameterized the unknown density dependence of the four-quark condensates ⟨q̄q⟩²_ρ and ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ⟨s̄s⟩_ρ using two free parameters f₁ and f₂. The sum rules were solved by minimizing a logarithmic measure to extract the scalar self-energy Σ_s (effective mass M*_Λ) and vector self-energy Σ_v, from which the ΛΛ potential U_Λ = Σ_s + Σ_v was obtained.

## Key numbers

- The Λ vacuum mass predicted by the sum rules ranges from 1036 to 1222 MeV over Borel mass M² = 0.8 to 1.5 GeV², with the optimization region chosen as 1.1 ≤ M² ≤ 1.4 GeV².
- At ρ = ρ₀, with f₁ = 0.25, f₂ = 0.8 and σ_πN = 56 MeV, the effective mass M*_Λ ranges from 0.68 M_Λ to 0.88 M_Λ as f₂ varies from 0.6 to 1.0, and U_Λ ranges from -0.015 M_Λ to -0.227 M_Λ.
- With f₁ = 0.0, f₂ = 1.0 and σ_πN = 56 MeV, the potential reaches an extremum U_Λ ≈ -0.008 M_Λ ≈ -9 MeV around ρ = 0.5ρ₀.
- At ρ = ρ₀, the overall allowed ranges are M*_Λ ≈ (0.73 ± 0.1 ± 0.05) M_Λ, U_Λ ≈ (-0.05 ± 0.05 ± 0.02) M_Λ, and Σ_v ≈ (0.14 ± 0.04) M_Λ, for 0 ≤ f₁ ≤ 0.25, 0.6 ≤ f₂ ≤ 1, and 56 ≤ σ_πN ≤ 76 MeV.
- The optimized residue λ* decreases from 3.27×10⁻² GeV³ to 1.66×10⁻² GeV³, and the continuum threshold s₀* decreases from 2.86 GeV² to 1.94 GeV², as density increases from zero to ρ₀ (with f₁ = 0.25, f₂ = 0.8).

## Context

This work extends the finite-density QCD sum rule method previously applied to ΛN interactions by the same group (sum5) to the study of ΛΛ interactions in pure Λ matter. The results contradict earlier predictions of a much stronger ΛΛ potential U_Λ ≈ -20 MeV at 0.5ρ₀ from Schaffner et al. (pdepth20) based on older double Λ hyper-nucleus data, and instead agree with the weaker potential of about -5 MeV at 0.5ρ₀ extracted from the latest measurements of ⁶He_ΛΛ by expBe and exph. The interpretation that ⟨q̄q⟩²_ρ and ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ⟨s̄s⟩_ρ behave like ππ and K̄K in nuclear medium, respectively, connects to Jaffe's predictions for four-quark states and enables future tests with more accurate double Λ hyper-nucleus experiments at KEK.
