# Solitonic Excitations in Collisions Of Superfluid Nuclei

> We investigate the role of the pairing field dynamics in low-energy heavy ion reactions within the nuclear time-dependent density functional theory extended to superfluid systems. Recently, we have reported on unexpectedly large effects associated with the relative phase of the pairing field of colliding nuclei on the reaction outcomes, such as the total kinetic energy and the fusion cross section [P. Magierski, K. Sekizawa, and G. Wlazłowski, arXiv:1611.10261 [nucl-th]]. We have elucidated that the effects are due to creation of a "domain wall" or a "solitonic structure" of the pairing field in the neck region, which hinders energy dissipation as well as the neck formation, leading to significant changes of the reaction dynamics. The situation nicely mimics the one extensively studied experimentally with ultracold atomic gases, where two clouds of superfluid atoms possessing different phases of the pairing field are forced to merge, creating various topological excitations, quantum vortices and solitons, as well as Josephson currents. In this paper, we present unpublished results for a lighter system, namely, $^{44}$Ca+$^{44}$Ca. It is shown that the pairing effects on the fusion hindrance are rather small in lighter systems, due to a strong tendency towards fusion, which is consistent with an earlier study.

## Key claim

Pairing field phase differences between colliding superfluid nuclei create a "domain wall" or "solitonic structure" in the neck region that hinders energy dissipation and neck formation, but in lighter systems like ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca the effect on fusion hindrance is rather small (up to about 5% of the Coulomb barrier), consistent with an earlier study of ²⁰O+²⁰O by Hashimoto and Sekizawa (2016).

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) calculations, solving the TDSLDA equations for quasiparticle wave functions on a Cartesian spatial lattice of 80 fm × 25 fm × 25 fm with 1.25 fm spacing, using the Fayans FaNDF⁰ energy density functional for the normal part and a local pairing functional, with the spin-orbit term neglected. Head-on collisions of ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca were simulated at energies around the Coulomb barrier, with the relative phase of the pairing field dynamically imprinted using a constant external potential, and the fusion threshold energy was determined by repeating simulations at different collision energies.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological fusion barrier V_Bass for ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca is approximately 48 MeV.
- At collision energy E ≈ 1.09 V_Bass (≈ 52.3 MeV), the Δφ = 0 case resulted in fusion while the Δφ = π case produced binary fragments.
- The change in fusion threshold energy due to phase difference is up to about 5% of the barrier, corresponding to 2.3 MeV.
- The effective barrier increase averaged over phase difference is E_extra ≈ 1.3 MeV.
- The fusion threshold energy B(Δφ) follows a sin²(Δφ/2) pattern as a function of phase difference.
- The uncertainty in fusion threshold energy due to finite collision energy steps is less than 220 keV.
- The time step for evolution was Δt ≈ 0.038 fm/c, and stable time evolution was maintained over intervals exceeding 12,000 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of heavier systems (²⁴⁰Pu+²⁴⁰Pu and ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr) reported in Magierski, Sekizawa, and Wlazłowski (2016), where pairing phase effects changed total kinetic energy by up to 20 MeV and fusion energy by almost 30 MeV. The results for ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca show much smaller effects, consistent with the earlier TDHFB study of ²⁰O+²⁰O by Hashimoto and Sekizawa (2016), and confirm that lighter systems have a stronger tendency towards fusion that suppresses the pairing-induced hindrance. The observed sin²(Δφ/2) dependence of the fusion threshold differs from the sin(Δφ) dependence expected for the nuclear Josephson effect proposed by Dietrich (1970), and the authors note that realistic simulations including spin-orbit coupling are needed for quantitative predictions.
