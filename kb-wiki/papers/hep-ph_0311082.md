# Improved Light-Cone Sum Rules for the Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon within the light-cone sum rule approach. In comparison to previous work (hep-ph/0112085) we suggest to use a pure isospin-1/2 interpolating field for the nucleon, since the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current leads to numerically large, unphysical, isospin violating contributions. The leading-order sum rules are derived for the form factors and the results are confronted with the experimental data. Our approach tends to favor the nucleon distribution amplitudes that are not far from the asymptotic shape.

## Key claim

The use of a pure isospin-1/2 interpolating field for the nucleon (rather than the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current) eliminates large, unphysical isospin-violating contributions of order 20% or more in light-cone sum rule calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and the resulting leading-order sum rules favor nucleon distribution amplitudes that are not far from the asymptotic shape.

## Method

The authors calculate the correlation function of an interpolating nucleon field with the electromagnetic current, then match the hadronic representation (with a nucleon intermediate state) to the QCD calculation at large Euclidean momenta in terms of nucleon distribution amplitudes of twist 3 through 6. A Borel transformation is performed with parameter M_B in the range 1.0-1.5 GeV, and a continuum cutoff at s_0 approximately (1.5 GeV)^2 is introduced. The improved isospin-1/2 current is constructed as a linear combination of the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current terms, and the sum rules for the Dirac and Pauli form factors F_1 and F_2 are derived at leading order in the strong coupling.

## Key numbers

- Isospin relation F_i^{np} = F_i^{pp} - F_i^{nn} is violated by approximately 20% when using the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current with asymptotic distribution amplitudes.
- The non-perturbative parameters used are f_N = (5.3 ± 0.5) × 10^{-3} GeV^2, λ_1 = -(2.7 ± 0.9) × 10^{-2} GeV^2, and λ_2 = (5.1 ± 1.9) × 10^{-2} GeV^2.
- The Borel parameter is chosen as M_B = √2 GeV (approximately 1.41 GeV) for the plots, with variation in the range 1.2-1.6 GeV yielding no sizeable effect.
- The dipole formula parameter is μ_0^2 ≈ 0.71 GeV^2.
- The LCSR prediction for the proton magnetic form factor normalized to the dipole overestimates the data by about 50%.
- The LCSR prediction for the neutron magnetic form factor normalized to the dipole overestimates the data by about 25%.
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p shows differences of up to 50% compared to the previous calculation using the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current (Braun et al., 2002).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone sum rule approach to nucleon form factors developed by Braun et al. (2002), who used the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky current. The authors identify and correct an implicit isospin violation of order 20% in that earlier work by constructing a pure isospin-1/2 interpolating field. The improved sum rules show better stability and better agreement with experimental data using asymptotic distribution amplitudes, enabling more reliable extraction of nucleon distribution amplitudes from form factor data and future application to axial form factors.
