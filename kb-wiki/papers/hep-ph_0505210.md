# Strong Color Field Baryonic Remnants in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at 200A GeV

> The effects of strong color electric fields (SCF) on the baryon production at RHIC are studied in the framework of HIJING/BB (v2.0) model. The particle species dependence of nuclear modification factors (NMF) are analyzed for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV. A doubling of the string tension leading to a modification of the strangeness suppression according to Schwinger mechanism is shown to provide an alternate explanation to coalescence models for the interpretation of the observed baryon and meson production at moderate $p_T$ and results in a predicted enhancement in the (multi)strange (anti)hyperon production.

## Key claim

A doubling of the string tension (from κ₀ ≈ 1 GeV/fm to κ ≈ 2 GeV/fm) within the HIJING/B̅B v2.0 model, which modifies strangeness suppression via the Schwinger mechanism, provides an alternative dynamical explanation to coalescence models for the observed baryon/meson anomaly at moderate transverse momenta (2 < pT < 5 GeV/c) in Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV.

## Method

The authors implemented strong color field (SCF) effects in the HIJING/B̅B v2.0 model by increasing the effective string tension from κ₀ = 1 GeV/fm to κ = 2 GeV/fm, which modifies the di-quark suppression factor (γqq), strangeness suppression factor (γs), and intrinsic transverse momentum broadening parameters according to the Schwinger mechanism. They computed nuclear modification factors RAA(pT) and Rcp(pT) for pions, kaons, protons, Λ hyperons, Ξ hyperons, and Ω hyperons in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, comparing results with and without SCF effects against PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- String tension doubled from κ₀ ≈ 1 GeV/fm (default) to κ ≈ 2 GeV/fm for the SCF scenario
- Di-quark suppression factor γqq changed from 0.02 (κ₀) to 0.12 (κ = 2 GeV/fm)
- Strangeness suppression factor γs changed from 0.30 (κ₀) to 0.55 (κ = 2 GeV/fm)
- Intrinsic transverse momentum broadening for di-quarks increased from σqq' = 1.08 GeV/c (κ₀, f=3) to σqq'' = 1.5 GeV/c (κ = 2 GeV/fm)
- Pion yield modified by less than ≈ 20% at moderate pT when SCF effects are included
- Lambda yield predicted to increase by a factor of approximately ten relative to binary scaling in central collisions with SCF
- Multi-strange hyperon production predicted to increase up to a factor of roughly 2 for Ξ and up to a factor of more than 10 for Ω relative to binary scaling

## Context

This work extends the HIJING/B̅B v2.0 model previously presented by Topor Pop et al. (2004), which used junction-antijunction loops with enhanced intrinsic kT ≈ 1 GeV/c and default string tension κ₀ = 1 GeV/fm to partially explain the baryon/meson anomaly. The SCF approach provides an alternative to recombination models (Muller 2003) and hydrodynamical approaches (Heinz 2003) for interpreting baryon and meson production at moderate pT. The predicted enhancement of (multi)strange hyperon production offers a testable signature that could distinguish between SCF mechanisms and other explanations such as chiral symmetry restoration.
