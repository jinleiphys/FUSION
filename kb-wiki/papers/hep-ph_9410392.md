# Soft Photons in Hadron-Hadron Collisions: Synchrotron Radiation from the QCD Vacuum?

> We discuss the production of soft photons in high energy hadron-hadron collisions. We present a model where quarks and antiquarks in the hadrons emit ``synchrotron light'' when being deflected by the chromomagnetic fields of the QCD vacuum, which we assume to have a nonperturbative structure. This gives a source of prompt soft photons with frequencies $ω<= 300 MeV$ in the c.m. system of the collision in addition to hadronic bremsstrahlung. In comparing the frequency spectrum and rate of ``synchrotron'' photons to experimental results we find some supporting evidence for their existence. We make an exclusive--inclusive connection argument to deduce from the ``synchrotron'' effect a behaviour of the neutron electric formfactor $G_E^n(Q^2)$ proportional to $(Q^2)^{1/6}$ for $Q^2 < 20 fm^{-2}$. We find this to be consistent with available data. In our view, soft photon production in high energy hadron-hadron and lepton-hadron collisions as well as the behaviour of electromagnetic hadron formfactors for low $Q^2$ are thus sensitive probes of the nonperturbative structure of the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that soft photons observed in hadron-hadron collisions, with frequencies up to about 300 MeV in the center-of-mass system, may originate from synchrotron radiation emitted by quarks and antiquarks as they are deflected by nonperturbative chromomagnetic fields of the QCD vacuum, rather than solely from hadronic bremsstrahlung. The authors hedge that this model provides only "some supporting evidence" when compared to experimental data, and that the data from one key experiment (Botz et al. cite experiment 3) are consistent with the presence of such synchrotron photons only if the effective path length parameter \(l_{\rm eff}\) is less than about 40 fm, while the same data do not definitively require an excess over bremsstrahlung.

## Method

The authors model the QCD vacuum as having a nonvanishing gluon condensate \(G_2\) with a finite correlation length \(a \simeq 0.35\) fm, leading to a domain picture where chromomagnetic fields fluctuate. They treat quarks in fast-moving hadrons as undergoing deflection in these background fields and compute the resulting synchrotron photon spectrum using classical synchrotron radiation formulas, averaging incoherently over partons in both initial-state and final-state hadrons. The inclusive photon yield per inelastic collision is obtained by summing contributions from all hadrons, using measured structure functions \(F_2\) for nucleons and pions, and parametrized pion inclusive cross sections, with the effective chromomagnetic field strength encoded in a single free parameter \(l_{\rm eff}\) (the distance a quark travels to acquire transverse momentum \(\sigma = 300\) MeV). The model is compared to data from 450 GeV proton-beryllium collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 29.09\) GeV, and an exclusive-inclusive connection argument is used to relate the synchrotron effect to the neutron electric form factor at low momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

* Gluon condensate value: \(G_2 = (0.95 \pm 0.45)\;{\rm GeV}^4\) (Equation 2.1).
* Vacuum correlation length: \(a \simeq 0.35\;{\rm fm}\) (Equation 2.4).
* Mean transverse momentum of quarks in hadrons: \(\sigma = 300\;{\rm MeV}\) (Equation 2.27), with an estimated uncertainty of \(\pm 100\) MeV.
* Center-of-mass energy for the comparison experiment: \(\sqrt{s} = 29.09\;{\rm GeV}\) (Equation 3.2).
* Inelastic \(pN\) cross section used: \(\sigma_{\rm inel}(pN) = 33.2\;{\rm mb}\) (Equation 3.7).
* Mean pion multiplicity at \(\sqrt{s}=29\) GeV: \((n_{\pi^+}+n_{\pi^-}+n_{\pi^0})/3 = 3.74\) (Equation 3.8).
* Effective path length range consistent with data: \(20\;{\rm fm} \lesssim l_{\rm eff} \lesssim 40\;{\rm fm}\) (Equation 3.13).
* Predicted scaling of synchrotron-to-bremsstrahlung ratio: \(\propto \omega^{2/3}\) (Equation 2.41).
* Estimated breakdown of incoherence assumption for transverse photon momentum: \(|\vec k_T| \lesssim 3.5\;{\rm MeV}\) (Equation 2.42).
* Predicted neutron electric form factor behavior: \(G_E^n(Q^2) \propto (Q^2)^{1/6}\) for \(Q^2 < 20\;{\rm fm}^{-2}\) (from abstract and Section 4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Botz et al. 1992) that nonperturbative QCD vacuum fields can produce soft prompt photons via a synchrotron mechanism, and it provides the first detailed comparison to experimental data, specifically the upper limits from the experiment by Specht et al. (1994). The model offers an alternative to the "soft annihilation model" (proposed by authors cited as 45, 46) and the "glob model" (authors cited as 49, 50), and the authors note that the dependence of anomalous photon yield on hadronic multiplicity \(n_h\) can distinguish these: their model predicts \(n_\gamma^{({\rm syn})} \propto n_h + 2\), whereas the soft annihilation model gives \(\propto n_h^2\) and the glob model gives independence of \(n_h\). The paper also makes an exclusive-inclusive connection to predict a specific power-law behavior for the neutron electric form factor at low \(Q^2\), which the authors claim is consistent with available data, suggesting that soft photon production and low-\(Q^2\) form factors are sensitive probes of nonperturbative QCD vacuum structure.
