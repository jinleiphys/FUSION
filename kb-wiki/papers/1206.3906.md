# Shear viscosity in superfluid neutron stars

> The phonon contribution to the shear viscosity $η$ in superfluid neutron stars is calculated by assuming neutron pairing in a $^1S_0$ channel. The shear viscosity is obtained by means of variational methods for the solution of the Boltzmann equation amended by a collision term which takes into account the binary collisions of phonons. Effective field theory techniques are used to extract the phonon scattering rates in terms of the equation of state (EoS) of the system. We find that $η\propto 1/T^5$, the proportionality factor depending on the EoS of the system. Our results indicate that the phonon contribution to $η$ might have important effects for the different oscillation modes of the star.

## Key claim
The phonon contribution to shear viscosity in superfluid neutron stars scales as η ∝ 1/T⁵, with the proportionality factor depending on the equation of state (EoS) of the system; however, this hydrodynamic description is only valid for temperatures above approximately 0.1 MeV, where the phonon mean free path is smaller than the typical stellar radius.

## Method
The authors used effective field theory (EFT) techniques to derive the phonon self-interactions from the equation of state, then solved the Boltzmann equation with a collision term for binary phonon collisions using variational methods. The shear viscosity was extracted from the linearized transport equation for small deviations from equilibrium, using the APR EoS as parametrized by Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen for β-stable nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity scales as η ∝ 1/T⁵, a universal feature shared with superfluid ⁴He and superfluid cold atoms at unitary.
- For β-stable matter using the APR EoS, ηT⁵ increases by almost three orders of magnitude when density changes from ρ₀ to 2 ρ₀.
- The phonon mean free path exceeds 10 km (typical stellar radius) for temperatures below T ∼ 0.1 MeV, indicating the breakdown of hydrodynamics.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition to the normal phase is T_c ∼ 1 MeV, though this value depends on the EoS.
- The speed of sound c_s reaches relativistic values (c_s/c approaching unity) at densities of 1.5–2 ρ₀ for β-stable nuclear matter.

## Context
This work extends the phonon EFT approach previously applied to superfluid cold atoms (Rupak 2007, Mannarelli 2012) and to shear viscosity calculations in neutron stars (Manuel 2004, Alford 2009) by providing a general formulation that depends only on the equation of state. The results indicate that phonon contributions to shear viscosity may significantly affect oscillation modes of neutron stars, but future studies must compare phonon-phonon scattering with other dissipation processes such as phonon-electron collisions to determine the dominant mechanism.
