# Hadronic Spectral Functions in Nuclear Matter

> We study the in-medium properties of mesons $(π,η,ρ)$ and baryon resonances in cold nuclear matter within a coupled-channel analysis. The meson self energies are generated by particle-hole excitations. Thus multi-peak spectra are obtained for the mesonic spectral functions. In turn this leads to medium-modifications of the baryon resonances. Special care is taken to respect the analyticity of the spectral functions and to take into account effects from short-range correlations both for positive and negative parity states. Our model produces sensible results for pion and $Δ$ dynamics in nuclear matter. We find a strong interplay of the $ρ$ meson and the $D_{13}(1520)$, which moves spectral strength of the $ρ$ spectrum to smaller invariant masses and leads to a broadening of the baryon resonance. The optical potential for the $η$ meson resulting from our model is rather attractive whereas the in-medium properties modifications of the $S_{11}(1535)$ are found to be quite small.

## Key claim

The paper presents a coupled-channel analysis of meson (π, η, ρ) and baryon resonance in-medium properties in cold nuclear matter, but the results depend on the treatment of short-range correlations (SRC) whose strength parameters for negative parity states are not well constrained and are estimated from a correlation function approach with a cutoff parameter q_c = m_ω, making the model essentially parameter free only under the assumption that q_c does not change for different potential types.

## Method

The authors compute meson self-energies from particle-hole excitations and then use these to obtain medium-modified baryon resonance self-energies via a coupled-channel Dyson equation. Short-range correlations are incorporated through contact interactions derived from pion and ρ meson exchange, with a non-relativistic reduction applied; for negative parity states, pion and ρ induced contact interactions have different structures and are treated separately. The strength parameters for SRC are determined from the correlation integral method of Oset and Weise (1982), using a correlation function C(r) = 1 - j_0(q_c r) with q_c = m_ω, and for positive parity states the parameters are fixed by requiring reasonable in-medium properties of the P_33(1232) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The short-range correlation parameter for π-NN coupling is g_π^{p,NN} = 0.6, and for resonance-nucleon coupling g_π^{p,R_N R_M} = 0.45.
- The cutoff parameters used are Λ_π = 1.0 GeV, Λ_η = 1.5 GeV, Λ_ρ = 1.5 GeV, and Λ_g = 1.5 GeV.
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}.
- The coupling constants used include f_{NNπ} = 1.0, f_{NNη} = 2.34, f_{NNρ} = 7.8, and f_{ΔNρ} = 10.5.
- The short-range parameters for negative parity states are given as ranges: g_π^s = (0, 0.1), g_ρ^s = (0, 0.1), g_π^d = (0, 0.4) = g_π^{dp}, and g_η^s = (0, 0.1).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Helgesson et al. (1997, 1998) and Lutz et al. (1998) by including short-range correlations for both positive and negative parity baryon resonances, which were previously treated only for positive parity states. The model enables a consistent description of meson and baryon resonance spectral functions in nuclear matter, particularly the interplay between the ρ meson and the D_13(1520) resonance, and provides a framework for calculating the η meson optical potential and its coupling to the S_11(1535) resonance. The treatment of SRC via contact interactions follows the correlation function method of Oset and Weise (1982), and the results for the P_33(1232) resonance are benchmarked against earlier work by Salcedo et al. (1988) and Helgesson et al. (1997).
