# Neutrino diffusion in the pasta phase matter within the Thomas-Fermi approach

> The behavior and properties of neutrinos in non-uniform nuclear matter, surrounded by electrons and other neutrinos are studied. The nuclear matter itself is modeled by the non-linear Walecka model, where the so-called nuclear pasta phase is described using the Thomas-Fermi approximation, solved in a Wigner-Seitz cell. We obtain the total cross-section and mean-free path for the neutrinos, taking into account scattering and neutrino absorption, and compare the final results for two known kind of model parametrizations: one in which non-linear effects in the strong sector are explicitly written in the model Lagrangian and another one in which the coupling constants are density dependent. The solution for this problem is important for the understanding of neutrino diffusion in a newly born neutron star after a supernova explosion.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean-free path in the pasta phase matter is systematically larger than in homogeneous matter at the same density, primarily due to Pauli blocking effects inside dense clusters and the larger proton fraction in clusterized matter, which suppresses the absorption process that dominates the total cross-section.

## Method

The authors model non-uniform nuclear matter in β-equilibrium with trapped neutrinos using the non-linear Walecka model within the Thomas-Fermi approximation solved in Wigner-Seitz cells of droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble geometries. They calculate the total neutrino cross-section including both neutral-current scattering and charged-current absorption processes, then obtain the neutrino mean-free path by dividing the cross-section by the Wigner-Seitz cell volume. Six different parametrizations are compared: four with constant couplings and non-linear meson terms (GM3, NL3, NL3ωρ, FSUGold) and two with density-dependent couplings (TW, van Dalen et al.).

## Key numbers

- The pasta phase exists for temperatures T ≲ 8 MeV and baryonic densities in the range ~0.0002 fm⁻³ < ρ < ρ_t, where the crust-core transition density ρ_t ~ ρ₀/2 (ρ₀ ≈ 0.148–0.153 fm⁻³ depending on the model)
- At T = 1 MeV and E_ν = μ_ν, the mean-free path for FSUGold parametrization shows clear dominance of the absorption process over elastic scattering contributions from protons, neutrons, electrons, and neutrinos
- For ρ_B = 0.018 fm⁻¹ at T = 1 MeV, the neutrino mean-free path as a function of incident energy differs noticeably between parametrizations, with triangles marking λ at the neutrino chemical potential μ_ν of each model
- At ρ_B = 0.05 fm⁻¹ and T = 1 MeV (slab geometry), the mean-free path decreases and approaches the homogeneous matter result
- Increasing temperature from T = 1 MeV to T = 5 MeV reduces the mean-free path by almost one order of magnitude for both pasta and homogeneous matter calculations
- The three models with smallest proton fraction (GM3, Dalen, NL3) have the smallest symmetry energy for 0.05 < ρ < 0.1 fm⁻³ and produce the lowest mean-free paths
- NL3ωρ (with softer symmetry energy density dependence) gives larger mean-free paths compared to NL3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino transport in neutron star crusts by incorporating the full pasta phase structure within a self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approach, following the formalism developed for homogeneous nuclear matter by Prakash et al. (1997). The results are relevant for understanding neutrino diffusion in newly born neutron stars after supernova collapse, where pasta phases are expected to exist at sub-saturation densities. The paper shows that the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which varies significantly between models such as NL3 and NL3ωρ, has a non-negligible effect on the neutrino mean-free path, and that pasta phase effects cannot be neglected for baryonic densities below 0.1 fm⁻³ and temperatures below 10 MeV.
