# Ferromagnetic condensation in high density hadronic matter

> We investigate the occurrence of a ferromagnetic phase transition in high density hadronic matter (e.g., in the interior of a neutron star). This could be induced by a four fermion interaction analogous to the one which is responsible for chiral symmetry breaking in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, to which it is related through a Fierz transformation. Flavor SU(2) and flavor SU(3) quark matter are considered. A second order phase transition is predicted at densities about 5 times the normal nuclear matter density, a magnetization of the order of $10^{16}$ gauss being expected. It is also found that in flavor SU(3) quark matter, a first order transition from the so-called 2 flavor super-conducting (2SC) phase to the ferromagnetic phase arises. The color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase may be completely hidden by the ferromagnetic phase.

# Ferromagnetic condensation in high density hadronic matter

## Key claim

A second-order ferromagnetic phase transition in high-density hadronic matter is predicted at densities about 5 times normal nuclear matter density, but this result depends on a coupling constant chosen arbitrarily (G = 20 GeV⁻²) and the transition density would decrease to about 3ρ₀ only if a larger coupling (G = 30 GeV⁻²) is used, which may be justified by vacuum polarization renormalization.

## Method

The authors construct a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type model with a chiral-symmetric tensor-tensor four-fermion interaction, related to the standard NJL interaction through a Fierz transformation, and apply mean-field and no-sea approximations to flavor SU(2) and flavor SU(3) quark matter. The thermodynamical potential is minimized as a function of the order parameter F (analogous to the BCS gap) to determine the equilibrium phase, and the magnetization is computed from the effective lowest Landau level contribution. For flavor SU(3) matter, the spin-polarized phase is compared with the 2SC and CFL color-superconducting phases using the Gibbs criterion for first-order transitions.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for the ferromagnetic transition: μ_c = 0.41 GeV (for G = 20 GeV⁻²), given by μ_c = π/√(3G)
- Transition density for symmetric matter: 4.87 ρ₀ ≤ ρ ≤ 9.99 ρ₀ (partial polarization region), with pressure 0.00175 < P < 0.00273 GeV⁴
- Expected magnetization: of order 10¹⁶ gauss
- For G = 30 GeV⁻², the transition density would decrease to approximately 3 ρ₀
- For flavor SU(3) matter, the spin-polarized phase is stable for μ > 0.51 GeV, while the 2SC phase is stable for μ < 0.51 GeV (for G_c = 5 GeV⁻²)
- Critical chemical potential for onset of full polarization in symmetric matter: μ_p = √(2π/G)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ferromagnetic transitions in nuclear matter using Skyrme forces (Navarro, 1996; Skyrme, 1959; Vidana, 2002) and in quark matter from QCD-inspired models (Tatsumi, 2000; Iwazaki, 2005; Ebert, 2006), but differs from Broniowski (1995) where spin polarization arises from pion condensation before chiral symmetry restoration. The model enables future investigation of magnetized phases in compact stars, though the authors note that vacuum contributions and finite quark masses require careful treatment for physical applications. The interplay between color superconductivity and spin polarization is compared with the 2SC and CFL phases described in Bohr (2012).
