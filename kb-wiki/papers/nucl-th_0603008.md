# A new realistic many-body approach for the description of high-energy scattering processes off complex nuclei

> A linked cluster expansion for the calculation of ground state observables of nuclei with realistic interactions has been developed. Using the V8' potential the ground state energy, density and momentum distribution of complex nuclei have been calculated and found to be in good agreement with the results obtained within the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain, and Variational Monte Carlo approaches. Using the same cluster expansion, with wave function and correlations parameters fixed from the calculation of the ground-state observables, various high energy scattering processes off complex nuclei have been calculated taking final state interaction effects into account by means of the Glauber multiple scattering series.

## Key claim

The authors present a linked cluster expansion (eta-expansion) method that can reliably calculate ground-state observables and high-energy scattering processes for complex nuclei using realistic interactions, with the caveat that the method's accuracy depends on the order of the expansion and the choice of single-particle wave functions, particularly for heavier nuclei like calcium-40 where agreement with reference calculations is less satisfactory than for oxygen-16.

## Method

The authors developed a cluster expansion (eta-expansion) technique to evaluate expectation values of quantum mechanical operators using a correlated ground state wave function built from a shell-model mean-field wave function and a symmetrized correlation operator. They applied this method to calculate ground-state energy, density, and momentum distribution for oxygen-16 and calcium-40 using the V8' realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, then used the same wave functions and correlation parameters to compute distorted momentum distributions for the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X reaction with final state interactions treated via the Glauber multiple scattering series.

## Key numbers

- Ground state total energy for oxygen-16 at first order of eta-expansion: -66.87 MeV (compared to FHNC result of -65.12 MeV)
- Ground state energy per particle for oxygen-16: -4.18 MeV (compared to FHNC result of -4.07 MeV)
- Potential energy contribution for oxygen-16: -390.37 MeV (compared to FHNC result of -390.30 MeV)
- Kinetic energy contribution for oxygen-16: 323.50 MeV (compared to FHNC result of 325.18 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain (FHNC) approach of Fabrocini (2001) and Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations of Pieper (2001) by providing a more economical method for calculating ground-state observables that can be readily applied to scattering processes. The approach enables calculation of distorted momentum distributions for semi-inclusive electron scattering reactions on complex nuclei and has been applied to total neutron-nucleus cross sections, showing good agreement with data when inelastic shadowing effects are included.
