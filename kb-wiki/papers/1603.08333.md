# Quark-Jet model for transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions

> In order to describe the hadronization of polarized quarks, we discuss an extension of the quark-jet model to transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions. The description is based on a product ansatz, where each factor in the product represents one of the transverse momentum dependent splitting functions, which can be calculated by using effective quark theories. The resulting integral equations and sum rules are discussed in detail for the case of inclusive pion production. In particular, we demonstrate that the 3-dimensional momentum sum rules are satisfied naturally in this transverse momentum dependent quark-jet model. Our results are well suited for numerical calculations in effective quark theories, and can be implemented in Monte-Carlo simulations of polarized quark hadronization processes.

## Key claim

The paper extends the quark-jet model to transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions using a product ansatz where each factor represents a TMD splitting function, and demonstrates that the three-dimensional momentum sum rules are satisfied naturally in this framework, though the model is limited to effective quark theories without explicit gluon or gauge link degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors formulate integral equations for TMD fragmentation functions based on a product ansatz for quark cascades, where each step in the fragmentation chain is described by elementary TMD splitting functions that can be calculated using effective quark theories. They derive explicit forms of these integral equations for the case of inclusive pion production with quark flavor SU(2), and analytically confirm the validity of the longitudinal momentum, transverse momentum, and isospin sum rules. The elementary splitting functions are obtained from tree diagrams for T-even functions and one-loop graphs for T-odd functions in effective theories involving constituent quark-pion couplings.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark-jet model framework (Bentz et al., 2009) from one-dimensional fragmentation functions to the TMD case, building on the original Field-Feynman jet model (1978). The formalism enables self-consistent Monte-Carlo simulations of polarized quark hadronization, which is needed for studying correlations in polarized single- and dihadron fragmentation functions. The results are applicable to effective quark theories like the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and complement other approaches such as the Lund model (Andersson et al., 1983) and spectator models (Jakob et al., 1997; Bacchetta et al., 2001).
