# QED radiative corrections for accelerator neutrinos

> Neutrino oscillation experiments at accelerator energies aim to establish charge-parity violation in the neutrino sector by measuring the energy-dependent rate of $ν_e$ appearance and $ν_μ$ disappearance in a $ν_μ$ beam. These experiments can precisely measure $ν_μ$ cross sections at near detectors, but $ν_e$ cross sections are poorly constrained and require theoretical inputs. In particular, quantum electrodynamics radiative corrections are different for electrons and muons. These corrections are proportional to the small quantum electrodynamics coupling $α\approx 1/137$; however, the large separation of scales between the neutrino energy and the proton mass ($\sim{\rm GeV}$), and the electron mass and soft-photon detection thresholds ($\sim{\rm MeV}$) introduces large logarithms in the perturbative expansion. The resulting flavor differences exceed the percent-level experimental precision and depend on nonperturbative hadronic structure. We establish a factorization theorem for exclusive charged-current (anti)neutrino scattering cross sections representing them as a product of two factors. The first factor is flavor universal; it depends on hadronic and nuclear structure and can be constrained by high-statistics $ν_μ$ data. The second factor is non-universal and contains logarithmic enhancements, but can be calculated exactly in perturbation theory. For charged-current elastic scattering, we demonstrate the cancellation of uncertainties in the predicted ratio of $ν_e$ and $ν_μ$ cross sections. We point out the potential impact of non-collinear energetic photons and the distortion of the visible lepton spectra and provide precise predictions for inclusive observables.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a factorization theorem for exclusive charged-current (anti)neutrino scattering cross sections that separates flavor-universal hadronic structure from flavor-dependent QED radiative corrections, demonstrating that the ratio of electron-neutrino to muon-neutrino cross sections can be predicted with percent-level precision despite large logarithmic enhancements in the individual cross sections.

## Method

The authors apply soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to factorize charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering cross sections into a hard function containing nonperturbative hadronic physics, a jet function describing collinear photon radiation, and a soft function for low-energy photons. They compute the soft and jet functions through one-loop order in perturbation theory, and evaluate the hard function using nucleon form factors extracted from other data with a gauge-invariant form-factor insertion model for one-loop contributions. The factorization is applied to both exclusive observables (including photons below an energy threshold or within an angular cone around the charged lepton) and inclusive observables (including all photon events), with numerical results for neutrino energies relevant to T2K/HyperK (0.6 GeV) and NOvA/DUNE (2.0 GeV).

## Key numbers

- For T2K/HyperK at E_nu = 0.6 GeV, the inclusive electron-to-muon cross-section ratio for neutrinos is (sigma_e/sigma_mu - 1) = 2.84 +/- 0.06 +/- 0.37 percent, and for antineutrinos is 1.84 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.20 percent
- For NOvA/DUNE at E_nu = 2.0 GeV, the inclusive electron-to-muon cross-section ratio for neutrinos is (sigma_e/sigma_mu - 1) = 0.54 +/- 0.01 +/- 0.22 percent, and for antineutrinos is 0.20 +/- 0.01 +/- 0.19 percent
- The radiative correction to the electron-neutrino exclusive cross section is approximately 15 percent at E_nu = 2 GeV, resulting from a logarithmic enhancement ~ ln(E_nu/m_e) ln(E_nu/Delta E)
- The probability of muon misidentification as an electron due to collinear photon radiation is less than a few x 10^{-4} for NOvA and DUNE, and less than 10^{-4} for T2K/HyperK
- Nuclear corrections to sigma_e/sigma_mu are of order 10^{-4} at E_nu = 2 GeV and of order 10^{-3} at E_nu = 0.6 GeV
- The photon energy cutoff for detector acceptance is estimated as Delta E approximately 12 MeV for argon detector material

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach developed for elastic electron-proton scattering by Hill (2016) to charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering, accounting for the different electric charges, weak interaction vertices, and real collinear photon radiation relevant for neutrino detectors. The results provide precise predictions that replace the previously assumed 2 percent uncertainties on radiative correction extrapolations used by T2K and NOvA, and demonstrate that radiative correction uncertainty can be controlled to the higher precision needed by future DUNE and HyperK experiments. The formalism enables future applications to neutrino energy reconstruction, radiative corrections with pion and resonance production, and inclusion of Coulomb and nuclear effects.
