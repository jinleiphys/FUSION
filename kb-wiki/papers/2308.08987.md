# Excluded Volume Effects on Cold Neutron Star Phenomenology

> Observable properties of neutron stars are studied within a hadronic equation of state derived from the quark level. The effect of short-range repulsion is incorporated within the excluded volume framework. It is found that one can sustain neutron stars with masses as large as 2.2$M_\odot$ even including hyperons in $β$ equilibrium, while producing radii and tidal deformabilities consistent with current constraints.

## Key claim
The excluded volume formalism, applied to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, can sustain neutron stars with masses as large as 2.2 solar masses even when hyperons are present in beta equilibrium, but only for a specific choice of the excluded volume radius (r = 0.5 fm), which is in mild tension with some tidal deformability constraints.

## Method
The authors incorporate short-range repulsion via the excluded volume effect (EVE) into the hadronic QMC equation of state, assigning each baryon a spherical excluded volume parameterized by a hard-core radius r. The QMC couplings are refitted for each value of r (0, 0.4, 0.45, and 0.5 fm) to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties (n0 = 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy -15 MeV, symmetry energy 30 MeV), and the resulting equation of state is used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron stars in beta equilibrium with hyperons included.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star mass reaches 2.22 solar masses for r = 0.5 fm, with central density nc = 0.880 fm^-3, central pressure 393 MeV fm^-3, and central energy density 1093 MeV fm^-3
- For r = 0.45 fm, maximum mass is 2.06 solar masses with radius 11.9 km, central density 0.910 fm^-3, and central pressure 315 MeV fm^-3
- For r = 0.4 fm, maximum mass is 1.93 solar masses with radius 11.7 km
- For r = 0 fm (no excluded volume), maximum mass is 1.65 solar masses
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass star: Lambda_1.4 = 190 (+390/-120) from GW170817; the r = 0.45 fm case lies at the boundary of this constraint, while r = 0.5 fm lies outside
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K increases from 225 MeV at r = 0 fm to 270 MeV at r = 0.5 fm
- Speed of sound squared at maximum mass: cs^2 = 0.36 for r = 0.4 fm, 0.48 for r = 0.45 fm, 0.69 for r = 0.5 fm
- Hyperon binding energies in symmetric matter at saturation: Lambda bound by -32 to -34 MeV, Xi bound by -15 to -16 MeV, depending on r

## Context
This work extends the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and Stone (2007) by incorporating the excluded volume effect, which was previously applied to an earlier sigma-omega QMC model by Panda (2002) but without comparison to neutron star observations. The approach addresses the "hyperon crisis" where hyperon appearance softens the equation of state and limits maximum masses; the authors find that EVE can sustain masses above 2 solar masses without requiring a phase transition to deconfined quark matter as studied by Annala (2019) and others. The results challenge the interpretation that a speed of sound below one-third is a unique signature of deconfined quark matter, as argued by Motta (2020) working with the same QMC framework. Future gravitational wave observations from binary neutron star mergers are expected to further constrain the allowed excluded volume radius.
