# Bayesian Model Selection for Electromagnetic Kaon Production on the Nucleon

> We present the results of a Bayesian analysis of a Regge model for K+ Lambda photoproduction. The model is based on the exchange of K+(494) and K*+(892) trajectories in the t-channel. For different prior widths, we find decisive Bayesian evidence (Δln Z ~ 24) for a K+ Lambda photoproduction Regge model with a positive vector coupling and a negative tensor coupling constant for the K+(892) trajectory, and a rotating phase factor for both trajectories. Using the chi^2 minimization method, one could not draw this conclusion from the same dataset.

## Key claim
The paper claims decisive Bayesian evidence (Δln Z ≈ 24) for a specific Regge model of K+Λ photoproduction with a positive vector coupling and negative tensor coupling for the K*+(892) trajectory and a rotating phase factor for both trajectories, a conclusion that could not be drawn from the same dataset using the chi-squared minimization method.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of a Regge model for K+Λ photoproduction based on exchange of K+(494) and K*+(892) trajectories in the t-channel. They computed the Bayesian evidence (marginal likelihood) using the Nested Sampling algorithm, integrating over model parameters with uniform priors, and compared model variants with different sign factors and coupling constant signs. The analysis used 72 high-energy data points (Eγ ≥ 5 GeV) from previous experiments.

## Key numbers
- Δln Z ≈ 24 between the best model variant and the second-best variant, decisively favoring the model with rotating phase for both trajectories, positive vector coupling, and negative tensor coupling for K*+(892)
- The best model variant has ln Z = 24.30 ± 0.75 above the second-best variant
- Prior width variations by a factor of 10 produced less than 5% difference in computed ln Z values
- The analysis used 72 high-energy data points with Eγ ≥ 5 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Regge model for K+Λ photoproduction previously studied by Corthals (2006), who could not resolve sign and phase ambiguities using chi-squared minimization. The Bayesian approach resolves these ambiguities and is important for the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model, where exact determination of background parameters affects extraction of resonance information. The method enables future work on the missing-resonance problem by calculating probabilities of individual resonance contributions in a Bayesian framework.
