# Combined effect of thermal and quantum fluctuations in superconducting nanostructures: a path integral approach

> We study the combined effect of thermal and quantum fluctuations in a zero dimensional superconductor. By using path integral techniques, we obtain novel expressions for the partition function and the superconducting order parameter which include both types of fluctuations. Our results are valid for any temperature and to leading order in δ/Δ_{0} where δis the mean level spacing and Δ_{0} is the bulk energy gap. We avoid divergences at low temperatures, previously reported in the literature, by identifying and treating non-perturbatively a low-energy collective mode. In the low and high temperature limit our results agrees with those from the random phase (RPA) and the static path approximation (SPA) respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents a path-integral framework that simultaneously incorporates thermal and quantum fluctuations in a zero-dimensional superconductor, yielding a partition function and order parameter free of the low-temperature divergences that plagued prior combined approaches (Argent et al.). The results are valid to leading order in δ/Δ₀ (mean level spacing over bulk gap) and for any temperature, with the key innovation being a non-perturbative treatment of a low-energy collective phase mode.

## Method

The authors start from the BCS Hamiltonian and integrate out fermionic degrees of freedom via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, obtaining a partition function expressed as a functional integral over a complex pairing field Δ(τ). They decompose the field into polar coordinates (amplitude s(τ) and phase φ(τ)), separate the static amplitude s₀ and a winding-number phase a₀τ, and treat the remaining fluctuations δs(τ) and δφ(τ) in a Gaussian (RPA) approximation around each static configuration. The static amplitude s₀ is then integrated exactly (SPA), while the collective zero-mode phase φ₀ is integrated non-perturbatively, avoiding the negative eigenvalues that caused divergences in earlier work.

## Key numbers

- The results are valid to leading order in δ/Δ₀, where δ is the mean level spacing and Δ₀ is the bulk energy gap.
- For a constant spectral density ρ(ε) = 1/δ, the zero-temperature order parameter is Δ_C(0) = Δ₀(1 + α δ/E_D) with α a constant of order unity (RPA result).
- For the shell model with degeneracy N_l, the condition for applicability is δ ≪ Δ₀, where δ = 2E_D/N_l.
- In the shell model at T=0, the first correction to mean-field is Δ_C = Δ₀[1 + (gδ/Δ₀){√(1 + (ε₀/Δ₀)²) – ½[1 + (ε₀/Δ₀)²]}], with Δ₀ = √(E_D²g² – ε₀²).
- For T ≫ T_c in the shell model, to lowest order in δ, the SPA result is Δ_C ≃ √[(δg tanh²(βε₀/2) coth²(β_cε₀/2))/(β(1 – tanh(βε₀/2) coth(β_cε₀/2)))].

## Context

This work extends the static path approximation (SPA) of Scalapino (1970s) and the random phase approximation (RPA) of Argent et al., which individually described thermal or quantum fluctuations but produced divergences when combined at low temperatures. The results provide a unified theoretical framework for pairing in confined geometries (nanoparticles, hot nuclei, trapped cold atomic gases) at finite temperature, enabling quantitative calculations of thermodynamic observables, odd-even effects, and experimental quantities such as differential conductance in STM measurements.
