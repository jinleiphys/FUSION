# Diffusion of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In order to characterize nuclear matter under extreme conditions, we calculate all diffusion transport coefficients related to baryon, electric and strangeness charge for both a hadron resonance gas and a simplified kinetic model of the quark-gluon plasma. We demonstrate that the diffusion currents do not depend only on gradients of their corresponding charge density. Instead, we show that there exists coupling between the different charge currents, in such a way that it is possible for density gradients of a given charge to generate dissipative currents of another charge. Within this scheme, the charge diffusion coefficient is best viewed as a matrix, in which the diagonal terms correspond to the usual charge diffusion coefficients, while the off-diagonal terms describe the coupling between the different currents. In this letter, we calculate for the first time the complete diffusion matrix including the three charges listed above. We find that the baryon diffusion current is strongly affected by baryon charge gradients, but also by its coupling to gradients in strangeness. The electric charge diffusion current is found to be strongly affected by electric and strangeness gradients, whereas strangeness currents depend mostly on strange and baryon gradients.

## Key claim

The paper calculates for the first time the complete diffusion coefficient matrix (including off-diagonal coupling terms) for baryon, electric, and strangeness charge diffusion in both a hadron resonance gas and a simplified quark-gluon plasma model, showing that coupling between different charge currents can be as large as the diagonal terms and should not be neglected in hydrodynamic simulations of low-energy heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation using a first-order Chapman-Enskog expansion, truncating the expansion of the distribution function in powers of energy at the lowest level (M=1). They consider elastic, isotropic 2-to-2 collisions with classical statistics, using tabulated energy-dependent hadronic cross sections from the Particle Data Group for 19 hadron species (pions, kaons, nucleons, lambdas, sigmas) and constant cross sections for missing channels, while for the QGP they use massless quarks and gluons with a total cross section fixed to give shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 1/(4 pi).

## Key numbers

- For the QGP, the total cross section is fixed to sigma_tot approximately 0.72 / T^2, corresponding to eta/s = 1/(4 pi)
- The hadron resonance gas results are shown for temperatures T less than or equal to 160 MeV, and QGP results above this temperature
- Baryon chemical potential values considered: mu_B = 0, 300, and 600 MeV
- At RHIC beam energy scan energies down to sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the baryon chemical potential can reach values up to mu_B approximately 400 MeV
- The off-diagonal coefficient kappa_SB (coupling strangeness gradients to baryon current) is similar in magnitude to the diagonal baryon diffusion coefficient kappa_BB
- The ratio kappa_QB / kappa_QQ indicates baryon chemical potential gradients contribute about an order of magnitude less to electric diffusion than electric gradients
- kappa_SQ is of the same order of magnitude as kappa_QQ for T greater than approximately 100 MeV
- kappa_SB magnitude is about a factor of two smaller than kappa_SS
- kappa_SQ is about an order of magnitude smaller than kappa_SS

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of individual charge diffusion coefficients (such as the electric conductivity studied on the lattice by Aarts (2014), Brandt (2015), and Ding (2016), and in perturbative QCD by Greif (2014) and Arnold (2000)) to include the full diffusion matrix with off-diagonal coupling terms. The results can be used in hydrodynamic simulations of low-energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, NICA, and FAIR, where net-charge diffusion becomes important, and the authors note that holographic results from Rougemont (2015, 2017) match their QGP calculations at high temperature in the conformal limit.
