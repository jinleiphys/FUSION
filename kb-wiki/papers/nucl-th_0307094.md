# Probing the isospin dependent mean field and nucleon nucleon cross section in the medium by the nucleon emissions

> We study the isospin effects of the mean field and two-body collision on the nucleon emissions at the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions by using an isospin dependent transport theory. The calculated results show that the nucleon emission number $N_{n}$ depends sensitively the isospin effect of nucleon nucleon cross section and weakly on the isospin dependent mean field for neutron-poor system in higher beam energy region . In particular, the correlation between the medium correction of two-body collision and the momentum dependent interaction enhances the dependence of nucleon emission number $N_{n}$ on the isospin effect of nucleon nucleon cross section. On the contrary, the ratio of the neutron proton ratio of the gas phase to the neutron proton ratio of the liquid phase, i.e., the degree of isospin fractionation $<(N/Z)_{gas}>_{b}/<(N/Z)_{liq}>_{b}$ depends sensitively on the isospin dependent mean field and weakly on the isospin effect of two-body collision for neutron-rich system in the lower beam energy region. In this case, $N_{n}$ and $<(N/Z)_{gas}>_{b}/<(N/Z)_{liq}>_{b}$ are the probes for extracting the information about the isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section in the medium and the isospin dependent mean field,respectively.

# Probing the isospin dependent mean field and nucleon nucleon cross section in the medium by the nucleon emissions

## Key claim
The nucleon emission number \(N_n\) depends sensitively on the isospin effect of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section but only weakly on the symmetry potential for neutron-poor systems at higher beam energies, while the degree of isospin fractionation \(<(N/Z)_{gas}>_b / <(N/Z)_{liq}>_b\) depends sensitively on the symmetry potential and weakly on the isospin effect of two-body collisions for neutron-rich systems at lower beam energies. The authors emphasize that their calculated results can be compared with experimental data directly, unlike some prior work that relied on indirect definitions of the gas and liquid phases.

## Method
The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which extends the standard QMD model by incorporating isospin-dependent mean field terms (including symmetry potentials \(U_1^{sym}\), \(U_2^{sym}\), \(U_3^{sym}\), and a zero-potential \(U_0^{sym}\)) and an empirical density-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{NN} = (1 + \alpha \rho / \rho_0) \sigma^{free}_{NN}\) with \(\alpha = -0.2\) or \(\alpha = 0.0\). They simulated collisions of neutron-poor systems (\(^{76}\text{Kr} + ^{76}\text{Kr}\) and \(^{112}\text{Sn} + ^{40}\text{Ca}\)) at higher beam energies and a neutron-rich system (\(^{124}\text{Sn} + ^{124}\text{Sn}\)) at lower beam energy, tracking nucleon emission numbers and the neutron-proton ratios of emitted nucleons (gas phase) and intermediate mass fragments (liquid phase).

## Key numbers
- The strength of the symmetry potential \(c\) is taken as 32 MeV.
- The empirical in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section uses \(\alpha \approx -0.2\) to reproduce flow data, with \(\sigma_{NN} = (1 + \alpha \rho / \rho_0) \sigma^{free}_{NN}\).
- For the neutron-poor system \(^{76}\text{Kr} + ^{76}\text{Kr}\) at impact parameter \(b = 4.0\) fm and beam energy 150 MeV/nucleon, the peak density \(\rho / \rho_0\) is larger for \(\sigma^{iso}\) than for \(\sigma^{noiso}\), and the gap between lines for \(\alpha = -0.2\) is larger than that for \(\alpha = 0.0\).
- For the neutron-rich system \(^{124}\text{Sn} + ^{124}\text{Sn}\) at beam energy 50 MeV/nucleon, the degree of isospin fractionation \(<(N/Z)_{gas}>_b / <(N/Z)_{liq}>_b\) shows larger variations between different symmetry potentials (\(U_3^{sym}\) vs \(U_1^{sym}\)) than between \(\sigma^{iso}\) and \(\sigma^{noiso}\).
- The free neutron-proton cross section is about a factor of 3 times larger than the free proton-proton or free neutron-neutron cross section below 400 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Bao-An Li and collaborators (early 2000s) who investigated the isospin dependence of the mean field using the IBUU model, and by R. Pak and colleagues who found that isospin dependence of collective flow and balance energy originates from the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. The authors improve on earlier isospin fractionation calculations by Bao-An Li (which used a density threshold \(\rho_c = 1/8 \rho_0\) to define gas and liquid phases and could not directly compare with experiment) by using the IQMD model to quantify the gas phase via nucleon emission and the liquid phase via fragment emission, enabling direct comparison with experimental data. The results enable future extraction of the isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section from nucleon emission numbers in neutron-poor systems at higher energies, and the symmetry potential from isospin fractionation in neutron-rich systems at lower energies.
