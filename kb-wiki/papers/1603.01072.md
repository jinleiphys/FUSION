# Effect of the $2p2h$ cross-section uncertainties on an analysis of neutrino oscillations

> We report the results of a study aimed at quantifying the impact on the oscillation analysis of the uncertainties associated with the description of the neutrino-nucleus cross section in the two-particle--two-hole sector. The results of our calculations, based on the kinematic method of energy reconstruction and carried out comparing two data-driven approaches, show that the existing discrepancies in the neutrino cross sections have a sizable effect on the extracted oscillation parameters, particularly in the antineutrino channel.

## Key claim

The existing discrepancies between data-driven descriptions of two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) contributions to neutrino-nucleus cross sections have a sizable effect on extracted oscillation parameters, particularly in the antineutrino channel, when using the kinematic method of energy reconstruction and applying a 20% systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compared two data-driven approaches to modeling 2p2h contributions to charged-current quasielastic (CC QE) cross sections on carbon: (i) an effective approach using an increased axial mass of M_A = 1.2 GeV with a realistic spectral function, and (ii) the GENIE 2.8.0 Monte Carlo generator's empirical 2p2h model added to spectral-function calculations with M_A = 1.03 GeV. They simulated a T2K-like off-axis experiment with a 600 MeV peaked flux, applied the kinematic method of energy reconstruction to all event types, and used GLoBES to perform a chi-squared analysis of nu_mu and antinu_mu disappearance, fitting for theta_23 and Delta m^2_31.

## Key numbers

- The effective calculations with M_A = 1.2 GeV reproduce the NOMAD and MINERvA CC QE cross section data for both neutrinos and antineutrinos, while the GENIE-based (vt) calculations overestimate the MiniBooNE antineutrino data.
- The MiniBooNE CC QE cross sections are higher than the effective calculations by a factor of 1.2, consistent with the reported ratio of detected to predicted events of 1.21 +/- 0.24 from the first MiniBooNE analysis.
- The analysis is based on approximately 6000 unoscillated events with reconstructed energies between 0.3 and 1.7 GeV, in both neutrino and antineutrino modes.
- The assumed true oscillation parameters include Delta m^2_31 = 2.46 x 10^-3 eV^2 and theta_23 = 42.30 degrees.
- A 20% systematic uncertainty is applied for shape (bin-to-bin uncorrelated) and normalization (correlated).
- The chi-squared best-fit per degree of freedom values shown in the confidence region plots indicate that the differences between the two approaches are too large to be neglected.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects on oscillation parameters pioneered by Coloma (2013) and addresses the puzzling discrepancy between T2K and SciBooNE inclusive cross section measurements, where T2K data are lower by about 20% than SciBooNE results. The findings are relevant to next-generation long-baseline experiments such as the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment, which aims to determine the charge-parity violating phase, and demonstrate that improving the precision of neutrino and antineutrino cross sections at energies around 1 GeV is essential for future oscillation studies.
