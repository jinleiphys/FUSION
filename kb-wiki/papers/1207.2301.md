# Timelike Compton Scattering from JLAB to RHIC and LHC energies

> Timelike Compton scattering (TCS) i.e. the exclusive photoproduction of a lepton pair with large invariant mass nicely complements the already successful experimental study of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS). The same Generalized Parton Distributions enter both amplitudes, which offer a promissing way to access the quark and gluon nucleon structure. We review recent progress in this domain, emphasizing the fact that analyticity and factorization properties dictate the relation of the NLO corrections to TCS to those of DVCS. We also stress that data on TCS at high energy should be available soon thanks to the proposed experimental program at JLab at 12 GeV, and that, before the future high energy electron ion colliders become reality, the study of ultraperipheral collisions at the RHIC and LHC may open a window on quark and gluon GPDs at very small skewness. .

## Key claim

Timelike Compton scattering (TCS) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) share the same Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), but the NLO corrections to TCS differ dramatically from those to DVCS, especially for the real part of the Compton form factor, which can change by approximately -400% in the valence region at small skewness.

## Method

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) coefficient functions for TCS using analyticity and factorization properties, deriving a relation connecting TCS and DVCS amplitudes via complex conjugation. They then evaluate the Compton form factors using the Goloskokov-Kroll model for GPDs and the GRVGJR2008 PDFs, and calculate cross sections for ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and LHC energies by folding the photon-proton cross section with an equivalent photon flux.

## Key numbers

- NLO correction to the real part of the TCS Compton form factor $\mathcal{H}$ is of the order of -400% at $\mu^2 = Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ and $t=0$ for $\xi$ between $10^{-4}$ and $10^{-2}$
- For ultraperipheral $pp$ collisions at LHC, the Bethe-Heitler contribution integrated over $\theta = [\pi/4, 3\pi/4]$, $\phi = [0, 2\pi]$, $t = [-0.05, -0.25]$ GeV$^2$, $Q'^2 = [4.5, 5.5]$ GeV$^2$, and photon energies $k = [20, 900]$ GeV gives $\sigma_{pp}^{BH} = 2.9$ pb
- The Compton contribution for the same LHC kinematics gives $\sigma_{pp}^{TCS} = 1.9$ pb
- The predicted event rate at LHC with nominal luminosity ($10^{34}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$) is of order $10^5$ events/year
- For ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC with $\sqrt{s_{pp}} = 500$ GeV$^2$ and $t = -0.1$ GeV$^2$, the Bethe-Heitler contribution alone gives $10^3$ events for $10^7$ s

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TCS originally proposed in the TCS paper (2002) and connects it to the well-established DVCS NLO results of Belitsky, Mueller, Niedermeier, and Schafer (2000). The paper reviews progress toward extracting GPDs from TCS data, which complements DVCS measurements at JLab at 12 GeV and enables access to GPDs at very small skewness through ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and LHC before future electron-ion colliders become operational.
