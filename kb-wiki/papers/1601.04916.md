# Quark and pion effective couplings from polarization effects

> A flavor SU(2) effective model for pions and quarks is derived by considering polarization effects departing from the usual quark-quark effective interaction induced by dressed gluon exchange, i.e. a global color model for QCD. For that, the quark field is decomposed into a component that yields light mesons and the quark-antiquark condensate, being integrated out by means of the auxiliary field method, and another component which yields constituent quarks, which is basically a background quark field. Within a longwavelength and weak quark field expansion (or large quark effective mass expansion) of a quark determinant, the leading terms are found up to the second order in a zero order derivative expansion, by neglecting vector mesons that are considerably heavier than the pion. Pions are considered in the structureless limit and, besides the chiral invariant terms that reproduce previously derived expressions, symmetry breaking terms are also presented. The leading chiral quark-quark effective couplings are also found corresponding to a NJL and a vector-NJL couplings. All the resulting effective coupling constants and parameters are expressed in terms of the current and constituent quark masses and of the coupling $g$.

## Key claim

Starting from a global color model for QCD with dressed gluon exchange, the author derives a low-energy effective model for pions and constituent quarks by splitting the quark field into two components and expanding the resulting quark determinant in a long-wavelength and weak quark field (or large effective quark mass) expansion, finding that the leading chiral quark-quark effective couplings correspond to NJL and vector-NJL couplings, with all effective coupling constants expressed in terms of the current and constituent quark masses and the coupling \(g\).

## Method

The author begins with a flavor SU(2) global color model featuring a dressed gluon propagator kernel, performs a Fierz transformation to express the quark-quark interaction in terms of bilocal quark bilinears, then splits the quark field into a component that yields light mesons and the quark-antiquark condensate (integrated out via auxiliary fields) and a background component that yields constituent quarks. After introducing auxiliary fields for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial channels, vector mesons are neglected as heavier than the pion, and a chiral rotation is performed to obtain the non-linear realization of chiral symmetry. The quark determinant is expanded in a zero-order derivative expansion (long-wavelength limit) up to second order in the pion field and quark bilinears, yielding effective couplings for pions and quarks.

## Key numbers

* The Fierz transformation yields a factor \(\alpha = 8/9\) for SU(2) flavor, arising from \((N_c^2-1)/N_c^2\).
* The local limit coupling constant is estimated as \(G \sim g^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2\) for massless gluons or \(G \sim g^2/M_G^2\) for non-zero effective gluon mass, with \(G \sim g^2 \sim 1/N_c\).
* The pion mass satisfies the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation: \(M_\pi^2 F^2 = -\langle \bar{q} q \rangle m_q\), with \(M_\pi^2 = i N_c (1/F) \; Tr' \; S_0 m\).
* The pion kinetic term coefficient is \(k_2 = f_\pi^2\), where \(k_2 g_{\mu\nu} \delta_{ij} = i N_c \; Tr' \; \sigma_i \sigma_j \; \gamma_5 \gamma_\mu \gamma_5 \gamma_\nu S_0^2\).

## Context

This work extends the global color model approach of Refs. [PRC, ERV, E-R1986] by including chiral symmetry breaking terms from the current quark mass and deriving explicit quark-quark effective couplings (NJL and vector-NJL) from polarization effects, going beyond the chiral limit considered in those earlier works. The resulting effective model provides a QCD-derived foundation for the Manohar-Georgi (1984) type expansion of chiral effective field theory, with the leading quark-meson axial coupling being of order \(N_c^0\) and the NJL-type couplings being higher order in \(1/N_c\) in agreement with Witten (1979). This enables further investigation of the convergence of the QCD effective action and the calculation of hadron properties from more fundamental QCD parameters.
