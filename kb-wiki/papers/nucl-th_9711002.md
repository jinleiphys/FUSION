# Kaon production in heavy-ion collisions and maximum mass of neutron stars

> We determine an `empirical' kaon dispersion relation by analysing and fitting recent experimental data on kaon production in heavy-ion collisions. We then investigate its effects on hadronic equation of state at high densities and on neutron star properties. We find that the maximum mass of neutron stars can be lowered by about 0.4$M_\odot$, once kaon condensation as constrained by our empirical dispersion relation is introduced. We emphasize the growing interplay between hadron physics, relativistic heavy-ion physics and the physics of compact objects in astrophysics.

## Key claim

The authors determine an "empirical" kaon dispersion relation by fitting KaoS data on kaon production in heavy-ion collisions, and find that the maximum mass of neutron stars can be lowered by about 0.4 solar masses once kaon condensation as constrained by this empirical relation is introduced, though these values change by about 20% when different nuclear equations of state are used.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on a chiral Lagrangian (Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot) to simulate heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (1-2 AGeV), treating nucleons, delta resonances, and pions with mean-field potentials and stochastic two-body collisions. They treated the scalar attraction parameters a_K and a_anti-K as free parameters, fitting them to reproduce K+ and K- kinetic energy spectra from the KaoS collaboration for Ni+Ni collisions. They then used the resulting antikaon dispersion relation to calculate neutron star properties, combining it with the same nuclear energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- The K+ potential at normal nuclear matter density is about +20 MeV (repulsive), corresponding to a_K ≈ 0.22 GeV^2 fm^3 and Sigma_KN ≈ 400 MeV.
- The K- potential at normal nuclear matter density is about -110 MeV (attractive), corresponding to a_anti-K ≈ 0.45 GeV^2 fm^3.
- K- condensation onset occurs at about 3 times nuclear saturation density (3 rho_0).
- The maximum mass of neutron stars is lowered by about 0.4 solar masses when kaon condensation is introduced.
- The critical density for kaon condensation and the amount of lowering in maximum neutron star mass change by about 20% when different nuclear equations of state are used.
- Without medium effects, the calculated K- yield is about a factor of 3-4 below the experimental data; including medium effects increases the K- yield by about a factor of 3, bringing it into agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of kaon condensation in dense matter, originally proposed by Kaplan and Nelson, by using experimental data from heavy-ion collisions (KaoS collaboration at GSI) to empirically constrain the kaon in-medium properties rather than relying solely on theoretical models. The results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Brown et al., Weise and collaborators) that K+ feels a weak repulsive potential and K- a strong attractive potential, and the authors note that alternative explanations for lowering the maximum neutron star mass, such as the introduction of Sigma- hyperons (Glendenning), are complementary rather than competing scenarios. This work enables more empirically grounded predictions for neutron star structure and demonstrates the growing interplay between hadron physics, relativistic heavy-ion physics, and compact object astrophysics.
