# On the Strongly-Interacting Low-Viscosity Matter Created in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Substantial collective flow is observed in collisions between large nuclei at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) as evidenced by single-particle transverse momentum distributions and by azimuthal correlations among the produced particles. The data are well-reproduced by perfect fluid dynamics. A calculation of the dimensionless ratio of shear viscosity $η$ to entropy density $s$ by Kovtun, Son and Starinets within anti-de Sitter space/conformal field theory yields $η/s = \hbar/4πk_B$ which has been conjectured to be a lower bound for any physical system. Motivated by these results, we show that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons has behavior similar to helium, nitrogen, and water at and near their phase transitions in the ratio $η/s$. We suggest that experimental measurements can pinpoint the location of this transition or rapid crossover in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) in QCD matter must have a minimum at or near the phase transition or rapid crossover between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma, and that sufficiently precise experimental measurements of collective flow in heavy ion collisions can pinpoint the location of this transition.

## Method

The authors compare the temperature dependence of η/s for helium, nitrogen, and water at various pressures using data from the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), showing that η/s has a minimum at the critical point. They then apply this empirical observation to QCD by combining existing calculations of η/s in the low-temperature hadron phase (from Prakash and collaborators, using two-body interactions beyond the chiral approximation including ρ-meson resonances) with perturbative QCD calculations of η/s in the high-temperature quark-gluon phase (from Arnold and collaborators, using full leading-order expression with Coulomb logarithms summed). They also consider the large Nc limit to show that η/s has a discontinuity proportional to Nc² in that limit.

## Key numbers

- The conjectured lower bound for η/s from anti-de Sitter space/conformal field theory is η/s = ħ/(4π kB), which in natural units is 1/(4π).
- Lattice QCD yields an equation of state differing from an ideal gas by about 10% for temperatures above 1.5 Tc, where Tc ≈ 175 MeV is the critical or crossover temperature.
- For a massless gas of N bosonic degrees of freedom, the close-packed limit gives η/s ≈ 2/N^(1/3).
- In the low energy chiral limit for pions, η/s = (15/(16π)) (fπ⁴/T⁴), which diverges as T → 0.
- For three flavors of massless quarks in perturbative QCD, η/s = 5.12 / (g⁴ ln(2.42/g)).
- The two-loop running coupling uses Λ_T = 30 MeV, corresponding to an energy scale of 2πT and Λ_MSbar = 200 MeV.
- The minimum in η/s occurs near Tc ≈ 190 MeV.
- Lattice gauge theory for pure SU(3) without quarks gives η/s ≈ 1/2 in the temperature range 1.6 < T/Tc < 2.2, with uncertainties of order 100%.
- Classical modeling of long wavelength QCD modes from Tc to 1.5 Tc gives η/s ≈ 0.34.

## Context

This work extends the empirical observation from atomic and molecular systems (helium, nitrogen, water) that η/s has a minimum at the critical point, first noted by Kovtun, Son and Starinets (2005) in the context of the conjectured lower bound from anti-de Sitter space/conformal field theory. It applies this insight to QCD by combining the hadron-phase viscosity calculations of Prakash and collaborators with the perturbative QCD calculations of Arnold and collaborators. The paper enables a program of systematic beam energy and projectile/target mass scans from SPS to RHIC to LHC energies, where scaling violations to perfect fluid flow predictions can be used to extract numerical values of η/s and thereby locate the QCD phase transition or rapid crossover.
