# Partial Dynamical Symmetries

> This overview focuses on the notion of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), for which a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by a subset of solvable eigenstates, but is not shared by the Hamiltonian. General algorithms are presented to identify interactions, of a given order, with such intermediate-symmetry structure. Explicit bosonic and fermionic Hamiltonians with PDS are constructed in the framework of models based on spectrum generating algebras. PDSs of various types are shown to be relevant to nuclear spectroscopy, quantum phase transitions and systems with mixed chaotic and regular dynamics.

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) describes a situation where a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by a subset of solvable eigenstates, but the Hamiltonian itself does not share that symmetry; this relaxes the stringent conditions of complete solvability so that only some states retain the dynamical symmetry properties.

## Method

The author presents general algorithms for constructing Hamiltonians with PDS by identifying n-particle annihilation operators that vanish when acting on states in a chosen irreducible representation of the symmetry group G, then forming number-conserving normal-ordered interactions from these operators and their Hermitian conjugates. Explicit bosonic Hamiltonians with PDS are constructed within the interacting boson model (IBM) framework, using U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetry chains as starting points, with the solvable states chosen to be those in physically relevant irreps such as the ground state or ground band.

## Key numbers

- For U(5)-PDS type I: the solvable ground state |[N], n_d=τ=L=0⟩ has eigenvalue E_PDS = 0, and the solvable state |[N], n_d=τ=L=3⟩ has eigenvalue E_PDS = 3ε + 21A + 18B + 12C
- For U(5)-PDS type I with interaction V_0: solvable states with n_d=τ=N have eigenvalue E_PDS' = N[ε_d + A(N+4) + B(N+3)] + CL(L+1), and states with n_d=τ=N-1 have eigenvalue E_PDS' = (N-1)[ε_d + A(N+3) + B(N+2)] + CL(L+1)
- For SU(3)-PDS type I: the Hamiltonian H(h_0,h_2) = h_0 P†_0 P_0 + h_2 P†_2·P̃_2 has the property that P_0 and P_2μ annihilate all states in the SU(3) irrep (2N,0), which describes the ground band of a prolate deformed nucleus
- The SU(3) DS Hamiltonian eigenenergies are E_DS = h_2 [ -f_2(λ,μ) + 2N(2N+3) ] + C L(L+1), where f_2(λ,μ) = λ² + μ² + λμ + 3λ + 3μ

## Context

This review extends the concepts of exact and dynamical symmetries by introducing intermediate symmetry structures where the symmetry is neither exact nor completely broken, generalizing earlier work on accidental degeneracy (Moshinsky, 1983) and quasi-dynamical symmetry (Rowe, 2004-2005). The PDS notion is shown to be relevant to nuclear spectroscopy, quantum phase transitions, and systems with mixed chaotic and regular dynamics, and it differs from spontaneous symmetry breaking (where the Hamiltonian respects the symmetry but the ground state breaks it) and from quasi-exactly solvable Hamiltonians (Turbiner, 1988), where solvable states form complete representations rather than partial ones.
