# Quasiparticle pairing encoding of atomic nuclei for quantum annealing

> Quantum computing is emerging as a promising tool in nuclear physics. However, the cost of encoding fermionic operators hampers the application of algorithms in current noisy quantum devices. In this work, we analyze an encoding scheme based on pairing nucleon modes. This approach significantly reduces the complexity of the encoding, while maintaining a high accuracy for the ground states of semimagic nuclei across the $sd$ and $pf$ shells and for tin isotopes. In addition, we also explore the encoding ability to describe open-shell nuclei within the above configuration spaces. When this scheme is applied to a trotterized quantum adiabatic evolution, our results demonstrate a computational advantage of up to three orders of magnitude in CNOT gate count compared to the standard Jordan-Wigner encoding. Our approach paves the way for efficient quantum simulations of nuclear structure using quantum annealing, with applications to both digital and hybrid quantum computing platforms.

# Quasiparticle pairing encoding of atomic nuclei for quantum annealing

## Key claim
The quasiparticle pairing encoding reduces the computational cost of quantum simulations for nuclear ground states by up to three orders of magnitude in CNOT gate count compared to standard Jordan-Wigner encoding, but this advantage is limited to semimagic nuclei where pairing dominates; for open-shell nuclei with significant deformation, the accuracy degrades substantially, with infidelities reaching 0.78 and relative energy errors up to 22%.

## Method
The authors map pairs of like-nucleon modes with opposite magnetic quantum numbers (time-reversed partners) onto hardcore boson quasiparticles, which are directly encoded as qubits without requiring fermion-to-qubit transformations. They construct a projected quasiparticle Hamiltonian \(H_Q\) by projecting the nuclear-shell-model Hamiltonian onto the subspace of pure quasiparticle states, then benchmark its ground-state properties against full configuration interaction (FCI) results. The computational advantage is assessed by simulating trotterized quantum adiabatic (T-QA) evolution for both the standard fermionic encoding and the quasiparticle encoding, counting the required CNOT gates to reach matched final-state fidelities.

## Key numbers
- Relative energy errors for oxygen isotopes in the \(sd\) shell: \(\Delta_Q E \le 2 \times 10^{-2}\) (2% or better)
- Fidelities for oxygen isotopes: \(F_Q \ge 0.95\) (infidelity \(\le 0.05\))
- For \(^{44}\)Ti in the \(pf\) shell: infidelity \(1-F_Q = 0.76\)
- For calcium isotopes in the \(pf\) shell: relative energy errors \(\Delta_Q E \le 3 \times 10^{-3}\) and infidelities \(1-F_Q \le 0.02\)
- For tin isotopes: fidelities \(F_Q \ge 0.99\)
- CNOT gate reduction: 2–3 orders of magnitude fewer CNOT gates for the quasiparticle encoding compared to standard Jordan-Wigner encoding in T-QA simulations
- For \(^{20}\)O: T-QA evolution time \(\tau\) chosen such that fidelity \(F(\tau) = 0.95\) (the maximum achievable with \(H_Q\))
- Timestep in T-QA: \(\Delta t \, \omega = 0.1\) where \(\omega = 1\) MeV

## Context
This work extends the hardcore boson mapping approach previously applied in condensed matter and quantum chemistry (e.g., by Krompiec (2025) and del (2025)) to nuclear-shell-model calculations, and generalizes the two-valence-neutron study of Yoshida et al. (2024) to medium-mass nuclei across multiple shells. The quasiparticle encoding builds on the seniority model and interacting boson model frameworks (Talmi, Suhonen, Iachello). The projected Hamiltonian \(H_Q\) is treated as the zeroth-order term of a Brillouin-Wigner expansion (Hubac, 2000; Brillouin), with higher-order terms planned to recover physics beyond pure quasiparticle states. The approach enables efficient quantum simulations of semimagic nuclei from oxygen through tin isotopes, with the authors noting a concurrent preprint (arXiv:2509.20642) presenting a similar mapping for variational quantum eigensolver simulations.
