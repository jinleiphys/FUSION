# A relativistic coupled-channel formalism for electromagnetic form factors of 2-body bound states

> We discuss a Poincaré invariant coupled-channel formalism which is based on the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics. Electromagnetic scattering of an electron by a 2-body bound state is treated as a 2-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas-type mass operator. In this way retardation effects in the photon-exchange interaction are fully taken into account. The electromagnetic current of the 2-body bound state is then extracted from the one-photon-exchange optical potential. As an application we calculate electromagnetic pion and deuteron form factors. Wrong cluster properties, inherent in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework, are seen to cause spurious (unphysical) contributions in the current. These are separated and eliminated in an unambiguous way such that one is left with a current that has all the desired properties.

# A relativistic coupled-channel formalism for electromagnetic form factors of 2-body bound states

## Key claim
The paper presents a Poincaré-invariant coupled-channel formalism based on point-form relativistic quantum mechanics that fully accounts for retardation effects in photon-exchange interactions when calculating electromagnetic form factors of two-body bound states. However, the Bakamjian-Thomas construction used in the formalism introduces violations of cluster separability that generate spurious (unphysical) contributions to the electromagnetic current, which must be separated and eliminated in an unambiguous way.

## Method
The authors treat elastic electron-bound-state scattering as a 2-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas-type mass operator in the velocity-state representation, with vertex interactions derived from quantum field theory. After eliminating the electron-constituents-photon channel via a Feshbach reduction, the one-photon-exchange optical potential is obtained, from which the bound-state electromagnetic current is extracted as an integral over bound-state wave functions, constituent currents, and Wigner D functions. The spurious contributions arising from the violation of cluster separability are removed by taking the limit of infinite total momentum (k → ∞) of the bound state.

## Key numbers
- For the pion (spin-0) case, the current requires 2 covariants and 2 form factors (physical f and spurious b) instead of the expected single covariant and single form factor; in the limit s → ∞, the spurious form factor b(Q²,s) vanishes and f(Q²,s) becomes independent of s.
- For the deuteron (spin-1) case, the current decomposes into 11 independent Lorentz-covariants, yielding 3 physical form factors (F₁, F₂, G_M) and 8 spurious form factors.
- The physical form factors are extracted via limits such as F₁(Q²) = lim_{k→∞} (-1/(2k)) [J⁰(k'_D,1; k_D,1) + J⁰(k'_D,1; k_D,-1)] and G_M(Q²) = lim_{k→∞} (-i/Q) J²(k'_D,1; k_D,1).
- Elastic scattering observables A(Q²) and B(Q²) are calculated for a Walecka-type model using different parametrizations of nucleon form factors (GK, MMD, and point-like nucleons).

## Context
This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (BT53) and point-form relativistic quantum mechanics (Kl98) to electromagnetic form factor calculations, addressing the known cluster separability violation (KP91) that plagues such approaches. The pion form factor result in the infinite-momentum limit is equivalent to the standard front-form result of Chung and Coester (1988), while the deuteron results resemble those from covariant light-front approaches (Carbonell, 1998). The formalism is general enough to accommodate n-particle bound states and exchange-current effects from dynamical particle exchange, enabling benchmark calculations to define "relativistic effects" in few-body problems.
