# Quark-Novae, cosmic reionization, and early r-process element production

> We examine the case for Quark-Novae (QNe) as possible sources for the reionization and early metal enrichment of the universe. Quark-Novae are predicted to arise from the explosive collapse (and conversion) of sufficiently massive neutron stars into quark stars. A Quark-Nova (QN) can occur over a range of time scales following the supernova event. For QNe that arise days to weeks after the supernovae, we show that dual-shock that arises as the QN ejecta encounter the supernova ejecta can produce enough photons to reionize hydrogen in most of the Inter-Galactic medium (IGM) by z ~ 6. Such events can explain the large optical depth tau_e ~ 0.1 as measured by WMAP, if the clumping factor, C, of the material being ionized is smaller than 10. We suggest a way in which a normal initial mass function (IMF) for the oldest stars can be reconciled with a large optical depth as well as the mean metallicity of the early IGM post reionization. We find that QN also make a contribution to r-process element abundances for atomic numbers A > 130. We predict that the main cosmological signatures of Quark-Novae are the gamma-ray bursts that announce their birth. These will be clustered at redshifts in the range z ~ 7-8 in our model.

## Key claim

Quark-Novae (QNe) arising from the conversion of neutron stars to quark stars can produce enough ionizing photons via a dual-shock mechanism to reionize hydrogen in most of the IGM by redshift z ~ 6, potentially explaining the large WMAP optical depth τ_e ~ 0.1 without requiring a top-heavy initial mass function for the first stars, provided the clumping factor C of the ionized material is smaller than 10.

## Method

The authors model the frequency of dual-shock Quark-Novae (dsQNe) by assuming a conventional Scalo IMF for massive stars, an average galaxy density n_g(z) = n_g(0)(1+z)^3, and an average Type II supernova rate f_SN(z) ~ 1 yr^{-1} Galaxy^{-1}. They calculate the ionizing photon yield from the luminosity of the reshocked ejecta when QN ejecta collide with supernova ejecta days to weeks after the supernova, using the time-dependent luminosity L_SN(t) and the photospheric temperature T_pk to derive N_ion. The ionization fraction f(z) and Thomson optical depth τ_e are then computed from the ionization balance equation and the Press-Schechter collapse fraction, with the clumping factor C as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- dsQN frequency estimate: f_dsQN = 0.2 yr^{-1} Mpc^{-3} from the Scalo IMF, with an adopted average of ~1 yr^{-1} Mpc^{-3} during the reionization epoch
- Number of ionizing photons per dsQN: N_ion ≈ 5 × 10^60 photons for R_phot = 3 × 10^15 cm and T_pk = 3 × 10^4 K
- Emissivity of UV ionizing photons from QNe: Ṅ_ioni,QNe ≈ 1.5 × 10^53 s^{-1} Mpc^{-3} for f_dsQN = 1 yr^{-1} Mpc^{-3}
- Ionizing photons per baryon from dsQNe: f_re ~ 2 photons per baryon for N_ion = 5 × 10^60, f_dsQN = 1 yr^{-1} Mpc^{-3}, and δt_re = 1 Gyr
- Thomson optical depth: τ_e varies from ~0.13 to ~0.066 for clumping factor C = 5 to C = 15, assuming prompt reionization at z ~ 20
- WMAP 5-year constraint: τ_e = 0.087 ± 0.017 (Dunkley et al. 2009)
- QNe produce r-process elements only for atomic numbers A ≥ 130
- Predicted redshift clustering of QN gamma-ray bursts: z ~ 7-8

## Context

This work extends the Quark-Nova model of Ouyed et al. (2002) and the dual-shock mechanism of Leahy and Ouyed (2008) to cosmology, proposing QNe as an alternative to top-heavy Pop III stars for early reionization. It addresses the "negative feedback" problem identified by Sokasian et al. (2004) and Ricotti and Ostriker (2004), where metal enrichment from Pop III stars can reneutralize the IGM. The paper enables future chemical evolution studies incorporating QN r-process yields for elements above A ~ 130, and suggests observational tests via gamma-ray burst clustering at z ~ 7-8.
