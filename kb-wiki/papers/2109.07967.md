# Charmonium suppression in ultra-relativistic proton-proton collisions at LHC energies: A hint for QGP in small systems

> Proton-proton ($pp$) collision has been considered as a baseline to study the system produced in relativistic heavy-ion (AA) collisions with the basic assumption that no thermal medium is formed in $pp$ collisions. This warrants a cautious analysis of the system produced in $pp$ collisions at relativistic energies.In this work we investigate the charmonium suppression in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02, 7$ and $13$ TeV energies. Further, charmonium suppression has been studied for various event multiplicities and transverse momenta by including the mechanisms of color screening, gluonic dissociation, collisional damping along with regeneration due to correlated $c\bar c$ pairs. Here we obtain a net suppression of charmonia at high-multiplicity events indicating the possibility of the formation of quark-gluon plasma in $pp$ collisions.

# Charmonium suppression in ultra-relativistic proton-proton collisions at LHC energies: A hint for QGP in small systems

## Key claim

The authors claim that net suppression of charmonia at high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies indicates the possibility of quark-gluon plasma formation in small systems, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that cold nuclear matter effects are absent in pp collisions and that the observed suppression arises solely from hot QGP mechanisms (color screening, gluonic dissociation, collisional damping, and correlated c-cbar regeneration).

## Method

The authors employ the Unified Model of Quarkonia Suppression (UMQS), which combines color screening, gluonic dissociation, collisional damping, and regeneration via correlated c-cbar pairs, to calculate charmonium suppression in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV. The space-time evolution of the QGP medium is modeled using second-order (1+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with boost invariance, incorporating transverse expansion as a correction. Initial temperatures are estimated from charged-particle multiplicity using an isentropic expansion assumption, and the transport equation for charmonium evolution is solved analytically under the assumption that the number of J/psi states is much smaller than the number of c-cbar pairs at initial time.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature reaches approximately 370 MeV at high multiplicity in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, comparable to initial temperatures achieved in heavy-ion collisions
- Initial temperature approximately 200 MeV at low multiplicity in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV
- Thermalization times assumed: tau_0 = 0.3 fm for sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, 0.2 fm for sqrt(s) = 7.0 TeV, and 0.1 fm for sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
- QGP lifetime for viscous evolution: 3.8 fm/c, compared to 1.5 fm/c for ideal fluid evolution at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
- Dissociation temperatures: J/psi at 325 MeV, chi_c at 180 MeV, psi(2S) at 173 MeV
- J/psi formation time: 0.89 fm; chi_c formation time: 2.0 fm; psi(2S) formation time: 1.5 fm
- Cross-sections at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: sigma_J/psi = 16.72 microbarn, sigma_chi_c = 5.016 microbarn, sigma_psi = 1.672 microbarn, sigma_c-cbar = 2.942 mb
- Cross-sections at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV: sigma_J/psi = 10.152 microbarn, sigma_chi_c = 3.045 microbarn, sigma_psi = 1.015 microbarn, sigma_c-cbar = 2.097 mb
- Lattice QCD critical temperature T_c approximately 155 MeV

## Context

This work extends the UMQS model previously applied to bottomonia suppression in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions (Singh et al., 2020) to the study of charmonia in pp collisions, motivated by experimental hints of collective behavior in small systems from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations at the LHC. The choice of charmonia over bottomonia is justified because bottomonia dissociation temperatures exceed 650 MeV, making them insensitive to the lower temperatures expected in pp systems. The results suggest that QGP-like medium may be produced even at low multiplicities in pp collisions at LHC energies, challenging the traditional assumption that pp collisions serve as a baseline free of thermal medium effects. Future work could extend this analysis to include bottomonia suppression and compare with experimental data from high-multiplicity pp events.
