# QCD equation of state matched to lattice data and exhibiting a critical point singularity

> We construct a family of equations of state for QCD in the temperature range 30 MeV $\leq T\leq$ 800 MeV and in the chemical potential range $0\leq μ_B \leq$ 450 MeV. These equations of state match available lattice QCD results up to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^4)$ and in each of them we place a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class. The position of this critical point can be chosen in the range of chemical potentials covered by the second Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. We discuss possible choices for the free parameters, which arise from mapping the Ising model onto QCD. Our results for the pressure, entropy density, baryon density, energy density and speed of sound can be used as inputs in the hydrodynamical simulations of the fireball created in heavy ion collisions. We also show our result for the second cumulant of the baryon number in thermal equilibrium, displaying its divergence at the critical point. In the future, comparisons between RHIC data and the output of the hydrodynamic simulations, including calculations of fluctuation observables, built upon the model equations of state that we have constructed may be used to locate the critical point in the QCD phase diagram, if there is one to be found.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a family of QCD equations of state that simultaneously match lattice QCD results up to order mu_B^4 and contain a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class, but the authors emphasize that placing a critical point in each equation of state is entirely by construction and their calculation in isolation is not a path toward determining whether the QCD phase diagram actually features a critical point or where it lies.

## Method

The authors start from the Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure in mu_B/T up to order mu_B^4 from lattice QCD at mu_B=0, then subtract the critical contribution from a 3D Ising model parametrization to obtain "Non-Ising" Taylor coefficients. They use a linear map with six parameters to transform the 3D Ising model variables (r,h) onto QCD coordinates (T,mu_B), with the critical point location as two of the parameters, and numerically invert the map to express the Ising pressure and its derivatives as functions of T and mu_B. The full pressure is then reconstructed by adding the symmetrized critical pressure (ensuring evenness in mu_B) to the Taylor-expanded Non-Ising pressure.

## Key numbers

* Temperature range: 30 MeV <= T <= 800 MeV; chemical potential range: 0 <= mu_B <= 450 MeV
* Lattice QCD matching is performed up to O(mu_B^4) in the Taylor expansion of the pressure
* 3D Ising critical exponents used: beta = 0.326, delta = 4.80, alpha = 0.11
* The parametrization uses normalization constants M_0 = 0.605 and h_0 = 0.394, with theta_0 = 1.154
* The regular function f(T,mu_B) is chosen as the constant T_C^4, where T_C is the critical temperature

## Context

This work extends prior approaches that either used model-based equations of state with a critical point (e.g., Nonaka and Asakawa (2005), Bluhm et al. (2007)) or used Taylor-expanded lattice QCD equations of state describing only a crossover (Bellwied et al. (2015), Monnai et al. (2016), Denicol et al. (2018)). It is the first to incorporate both lattice QCD results and a critical point with correct universal scaling behavior. The family of equations of state is intended for use in hydrodynamic simulations and fluctuation calculations that can be compared to data from the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC, potentially constraining the location of the QCD critical point if one exists.
