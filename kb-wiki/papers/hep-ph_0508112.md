# Estimates for Pion-Photon Transition Distributions

> In the scaling regime, amplitudes for backward virtual Compton scattering and hadron annihilation into two photons depend on structure functions that describe the hadron-photon transition at the partonic level. We construct simple analytical models for the vector and axial-vector pion-photon transition distribution amplitudes based on double distributions. Via a sum rule, these models are tuned to reproduce the pion-photon transition form factors. To obtain reasonable estimates of the distributions at a low scale, we modify the models to saturate the positivity bounds using empirically parametrized parton distributions. We also determine a model-independent contribution to the axial-vector transition distribution using chiral perturbation theory.

# Estimates for Pion-Photon Transition Distributions

## Key claim
Simple analytical models for vector and axial-vector pion-photon transition distribution amplitudes can be constructed based on double distributions, but these models must be modified to saturate positivity bounds using empirically parametrized parton distributions to obtain reasonable estimates at a low scale; a model-independent contribution to the axial-vector transition distribution can also be determined using chiral perturbation theory.

## Method
The author constructs a constituent quark model with a triplet of pions and a doublet of quarks, using the Lagrangian with a pseudoscalar pion-quark coupling. Vector and axial-vector transition double distributions are derived from the impulse approximation to twist-two matrix elements, with the constituent quark mass tuned to reproduce the experimentally parametrized pion-photon transition form factor. To obtain phenomenologically reasonable input distributions, the model is augmented by factorizing realistic parton distributions into the double distributions to saturate positivity bounds.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 132$ MeV
- Dipole mass from experimental parametrization of $F_{\pi\gamma}(t)$: $\Lambda = 776$ MeV
- Constituent quark mass tuned to reproduce form factor at low momentum transfer: $m = 0.18$ GeV
- Pion mass used in model: $M_\pi = 0.14$ GeV
- Ratio $F_A(0)/F_V(0) = 0.98$ from the model with $m = 0.18$ GeV
- Axial transition form factor at zero momentum transfer from model: $F_A(0) = 0.026$

## Context
This work extends the formalism of double distributions, previously applied to generalized parton distributions by Radyushkin (1997, 1998) and others, to the case of pion-photon transition distribution amplitudes introduced by Pire (2004). The transition distributions enter processes such as $\pi^+ \pi^- \to \gamma^* \gamma$ and backward virtual Compton scattering $\gamma^* \pi^+ \to \pi^+ \gamma$ in the scaling regime. The model-independent chiral perturbation theory treatment of the axial-vector contribution follows the approach of Arndt (2001) and Chen (2001) for mapping twist-two operators onto chiral perturbation theory. This work enables phenomenological estimates of transition distribution amplitudes that can be evolved to higher scales for comparison with experimental data.
