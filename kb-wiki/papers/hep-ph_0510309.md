# Have Pentaquark States Been seen?

> The status of the search for pentaquark baryons is reviewed in light of new results from the first two dedicated experiments from CLAS at Jefferson Lab and of new analyses from several laboratories on the $Theta^+(1540)$. Evidence for and against two heavier pentaquark states is also discussed.

# Have Pentaquark States Been Seen?

## Key claim

The evidence for the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark state has significantly eroded with recent CLAS results, but still leaves room for a state with an intrinsic width of $\Gamma < 0.5$ MeV; the evidence against the heavier $\Xi(1862)$ and $\Theta_c^0(3100)$ pentaquark candidates is rapidly increasing, making their existence highly questionable.

## Method

The paper reviews published experimental evidence for pentaquark baryons, comparing positive sightings from ten experiments claiming the $\Theta^+$ (including LEPS, DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR, ZEUS, HERMES, COSY, and SVD) with new high-statistics null results from dedicated CLAS measurements on proton and deuterium targets, BaBar quasi-real photoproduction, Belle $K^+A$ scattering, and searches for the $\Xi_5$ and $\Theta_c^0$ by COMPASS, FOCUS, CDF, and other experiments. The analysis uses the production ratio $R_{\Theta^+,\Lambda^*} \equiv \sigma_{tot}(\Theta^+) / \sigma_{tot}(\Lambda^*)$ and model-dependent cross section limits to constrain the $\Theta^+$ width.

## Key numbers

- CLAS upper limit on $\Theta^+$ photoproduction cross section from protons: 0.85–1.3 nb (95% c.l.) in the mass range 1.525–1.555 GeV
- CLAS upper limit on $\Theta^+$ photoproduction cross section from neutrons: 5 nb (95% c.l.)
- Belle upper limit on $\Theta^+$ width: $\Gamma_{\Theta^+} < 0.64$ MeV (90% c.l.) at mass 1539 MeV; $\Gamma_{\Theta^+} < 1$ MeV (90% c.l.) over mass range 1525–1555 MeV
- Width extracted from $K^+A$ scattering analyses: $\Gamma = 0.9 \pm 0.3$ MeV
- CLAS-2(p) upper limit on $\Theta^+/\Lambda^*$ ratio: $< 0.2\%$ at $E_\gamma = 1.4–3.8$ GeV
- NA49 claimed $\Xi_5(1862)$ with mass $1862 \pm 2$ MeV/$c^2$ and $4\sigma$ significance; FOCUS upper limit on $\Xi_5/\Xi(1530)$ ratio: $< 0.003$
- H1 claimed $\Theta_c^0(3100)$ with mass $3099 \pm 3 \pm 5$ MeV/$c^2$ and $5.4\sigma$ significance
- Predicted $\Theta^+/\Lambda^*$ ratio from low-energy resonance formation for 1 MeV width: $R_{\Theta^+,\Lambda^*} \approx 0.014$ (1.4%)

## Context

This review extends the controversy following the 2003 announcement of the $\Theta^+(1540)$ discovery, which appeared to confirm the chiral soliton model prediction by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) of an anti-decuplet of pentaquark states. The paper directly contradicts the earlier positive sightings by SAPHIR (which claimed 300 nb cross section) and the original CLAS deuterium result (which claimed $5.2 \pm 0.6\sigma$ significance), using new dedicated high-statistics CLAS measurements. The results enable quantitative tests of hadrodynamical models by Roberts (2004), Ko et al. (2004), Nam et al. (2005), and Oh et al. (2004), which predict $\Theta^+$ photoproduction cross sections as functions of its width and spin-parity assignment.
