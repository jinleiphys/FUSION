# Spin waves in spin hydrodynamics

> The propagation properties of spin degrees of freedom are analyzed in the framework of relativistic hydrodynamics with spin based on the de Groot--van Leeuwen--van Weert definitions of the energy-momentum and spin tensors. We derive the analytical expression for the spin wave velocity for arbitrary statistics and show that it goes to half the speed of light in the ultra-relativistic limit. We find that only the transverse degrees of freedom propagate, analogously to electromagnetic waves. Finally, we consider the effect of dissipative corrections and calculate the damping coefficients for the case of Maxwell-Jüttner statistics.

## Key claim

Spin degrees of freedom in relativistic spin hydrodynamics propagate as transverse waves with a speed that approaches half the speed of light in the ultra-relativistic limit, but only when the background fluid is unpolarized and quadratic or higher-order spin terms are neglected.

## Method

The authors linearize the spin conservation equation derived from the de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW) definitions of the energy-momentum and spin tensors, assuming a quiescent unpolarized background fluid. They then perform a wave analysis of the spin polarization tensor components, decomposing them into electric-type (κ) and magnetic-type (ω) parts, and derive the dispersion relation for small perturbations propagating along a single spatial direction.

## Key numbers

- Spin wave velocity in the ultra-relativistic limit (z = m/T → 0): c_spin = 1/2 (in units where c = 1), independent of statistics.
- In the non-relativistic limit (z ≫ 1): c_spin ≃ 1/√(2z).
- For Maxwell-Jüttner statistics at small z: c_spin = 1/2 [1 − z²/16 + O(z⁴)].
- For Fermi-Dirac statistics at small z: c_spin = 1/2 [1 − (15z²/4π²) × (1 + 3ξ²/π²)/(7 + 30ξ²/π² + 15ξ⁴/π⁴) + O(z⁴)], where ξ = μ/T.
- The spin wave speed satisfies 0 < c_spin ≤ 1/2, with the lower bound reached for a cold gas of massive particles.

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid spin hydrodynamics formalism developed by Florkowski et al. (2017, 2018) based on the GLW definitions from de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert (1980). It parallels earlier wave analyses in relativistic hydrodynamics by Cercignani and Kremer (2002), Rezzolla and Zanotti (2013), and Monnai and Hirano (2014), and is analogous to studies of spin waves using effective action approaches by Montenegro et al. (2017, 2020). The results enable future investigations of spin wave propagation in heavy-ion collisions and provide a foundation for incorporating dissipative corrections, which the authors calculate for the Maxwell-Jüttner gas using the dissipative spin tensor from Bhadury et al. (2020).
