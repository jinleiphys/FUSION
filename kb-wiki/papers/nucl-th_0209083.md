# Present status of electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron above pion threshold

> The present status of the theoretical description of electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron above pion threshold is reviewed. Three major topics are considered: (i) retardation effects in $π$-meson exchange contributions to $NN$-interaction and meson exchange currents in deuteron photodisintegration above $π$-threshold in the $Δ$-resonance region, (ii) off-shell effects in the one-body current treated in a simple pion cloud model in deuteron photodisintegration in and above the $Δ$-resonance region, and (iii) final state interaction effects in photoproduction of $π$ and $η$ mesons on the deuteron.

## Key claim

This review argues that a theoretical description of electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron above pion threshold using meson, nucleon, and isobar degrees of freedom can achieve quantitative agreement with experimental data only when retardation effects in the NN-interaction and meson exchange currents, off-shell effects in the one-body current, and complete three-body final state interactions are all properly included, contrary to earlier static approximations that showed significant discrepancies.

## Method

The authors review three major theoretical developments: (i) inclusion of pion retardation in both the NN-potential and meson exchange currents for deuteron photodisintegration in the Delta-resonance region, using a coupled channel approach; (ii) treatment of off-shell effects in the one-body electromagnetic current using a pion cloud model for the nucleon, with the same meson-nucleon model used for the NN-interaction; (iii) calculation of final state interactions in pion and eta photoproduction on the deuteron, including complete rescattering in all two-body subsystems and, for eta production near threshold, a full three-body treatment restricted to s-waves.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for deuteron photodisintegration was underestimated by 20-30% in static calculations (Wilhelm et al., 1993) before inclusion of retardation effects
- For eta photoproduction on the proton, the dressed amplitudes yield A_n = (-114 - i1.7) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} and A_p = (120.9 - i66.1) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- The ratio (sigma_n/sigma_p)_res = |A_n/A_p|^2 = 0.68, approximately 2/3, at the S11(1535) resonance
- The ratio of isoscalar to proton amplitude from the coherent reaction gives A_s/A_p = 0.25 e^{-i 0.969}
- The earlier extraction from coherent eta production gave |A_s/A_p|_coh approximately 0.2, while from incoherent production the value was 0.09

## Context

This review extends the work of Wilhelm et al. (1993) on deuteron photodisintegration by showing that static approximations fail and that retardation effects in the NN-interaction and meson exchange currents are essential for agreement with experimental data from Crawford et al. (1996), Blanpied et al. (1995), and Wartenberg et al. (1999). The off-shell effects studied in the one-body current using a pion cloud model (Schwamb et al., 2001) show sizeable influences on differential cross sections at forward and backward angles. The three-body treatment of final state interactions in incoherent eta photoproduction near threshold (Fix et al., 2002) resolves discrepancies that first-order rescattering calculations (Fix et al., 1997) could not explain, particularly the enhancement of the cross section right above threshold seen in the data of Hejny et al. (2002). The work enables more reliable extraction of neutron amplitudes from reactions on the deuteron and provides a framework for testing effective degrees of freedom against explicit quark-gluon descriptions.
