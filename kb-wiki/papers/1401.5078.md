# QCD Evolution of the Sivers Asymmetry

> We study the QCD evolution of the Sivers effect in both semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan production (DY). We pay close attention to the non-perturbative spin-independent Sudakov factor in the evolution formalism and find a universal form which can describe reasonably well the experimental data on the transverse momentum distributions in SIDIS, DY lepton pair and $W/Z$ production. With this Sudakov factor at hand, we perform a global fitting of all the experimental data on the Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS from HERMES, COMPASS and Jefferson Lab. We then make predictions for the Sivers asymmetry in DY lepton pair and $W$ production that can be compared to the future experimental measurements to test the sign change of the Sivers functions between SIDIS and DY processes and constrain the sea quark Sivers functions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a single universal non-perturbative Sudakov factor, with parameters tuned to describe spin-averaged transverse momentum distributions across SIDIS, Drell-Yan, and W/Z production, can be used to extract quark Sivers functions from SIDIS data and predict the Sivers asymmetry in Drell-Yan processes, conditional on the validity of the sign-change prediction between SIDIS and Drell-Yan.

## Method

The authors applied the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) QCD evolution formalism at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) accuracy to transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs), fragmentation functions (TMDFFs), and the weighted quark Sivers function. They introduced a non-perturbative Sudakov factor with a universal parameter \(g_2\) and process-dependent \(g_1\) parameters, tuned these to describe spin-averaged hadron multiplicity in SIDIS, Drell-Yan dilepton, and W/Z boson production data, then performed a global fit of the Sivers asymmetry data from HERMES, COMPASS, and Jefferson Lab using the MINUIT package to extract the Qiu-Sterman functions.

## Key numbers

* Universal non-perturbative Sudakov parameter: \(g_2 = 0.16\ \text{GeV}^2\), with \(b_{\text{max}} = 1.5\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
* Intrinsic transverse momentum widths at \(Q_0 = \sqrt{2.4}\ \text{GeV}\): \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_{Q_0} = 0.38\ \text{GeV}^2\) for TMDPDFs, \(\langle p_T^2\rangle_{Q_0} = 0.19\ \text{GeV}^2\) for TMDFFs.
* Global fit of Sivers asymmetry data: total \(\chi^2 \approx 300\) for 241 data points, giving \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 1.3\).
* SIDIS data fitted from HERMES at \(\langle Q^2\rangle \approx 2.45\ \text{GeV}^2\), COMPASS at \(\langle Q^2\rangle \approx 3-5\ \text{GeV}^2\), and JLab at \(\langle Q^2\rangle = 1.38-2.68\ \text{GeV}^2\), with transverse momentum cuts \(P_{h\perp} \leq 0.6\), \(0.7\), and \(0.5\ \text{GeV}\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the QCD evolution framework for TMDs developed by Collins (2011), Aybat and Rogers (2011), and Echevarria, Idilbi, and Scimemi (2012) by providing a universal non-perturbative Sudakov factor that simultaneously describes spin-averaged data from low-Q SIDIS (HERMES, COMPASS) to high-Q W/Z production (Tevatron, LHC). It enables predictions for the Sivers asymmetry in Drell-Yan lepton pair and W boson production at future experiments (COMPASS, RHIC, Fermilab) to test the predicted sign change of the Sivers function between SIDIS and Drell-Yan processes, a key prediction of QCD factorization from Brodsky, Collins, and Boer (2002).
