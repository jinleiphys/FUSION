# Redundancy of the off-shell parameters in chiral effective field theory with explicit spin-3/2 degrees of freedom

> In this note we prove to all orders in the small scale expansion that all off-shell parameters which appear in the chiral effective Lagrangian with explicit Delta(1232) isobar degrees of freedom can be absorbed into redefinitions of certain low-energy constants and are therefore redundant.

## Key claim

The paper proves to all orders in the small-scale expansion that all off-shell parameters appearing in the chiral effective Lagrangian with explicit Delta(1232) isobar degrees of freedom can be eliminated by field redefinitions and are therefore redundant, meaning they do not affect S-matrix elements. The proof relies on the equivalence theorem and holds for arbitrary processes, not just at leading order.

## Method

The authors use an inductive proof based on the small-scale expansion (SSE), where the Delta-nucleon mass splitting Delta = 293 MeV is treated as an additional small parameter alongside external momenta and the pion mass. They first establish a lemma showing that interaction terms at a given order in epsilon can be rewritten using equations of motion via field redefinitions that leave the S-matrix invariant. They then prove by induction that all bilinear and linear couplings of the spin-3/2 field that contain off-shell parameters (structures involving gamma_mu or covariant derivatives) can be transformed away order by order, using the property that the transformed terms are shifted to higher orders in the expansion.

## Key numbers

* Delta-nucleon mass splitting: Delta = 293 MeV, approximately 2 M_pi
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV
* The small parameters of the expansion are epsilon in {Q/Lambda_chi, M_pi/Lambda_chi, Delta/Lambda_chi}
* The free Lagrangian parameter A must satisfy A != -1/2 to avoid singularities

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Ellis and Tang (1996) and Pascalutsa (2000) who showed redundancy of off-shell parameters at leading order, and by Fettes and Meissner (2000) who confirmed this in explicit SSE calculations. The paper provides the first rigorous proof to all orders in the SSE for arbitrary processes, building on the authors' previous work (Krebs, Epelbaum, Meissner, 2008) on non-linear field redefinitions for spin-3/2 fields. This enables practitioners to use the standard chiral Lagrangian with naive Feynman rules without worrying about off-shell parameters, simplifying calculations in chiral EFT with explicit Delta isobars.
