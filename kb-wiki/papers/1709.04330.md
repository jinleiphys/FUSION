# Algebraic diagrammatic construction formalism with three-body interactions

> Self-consistent Green's function theory has recently been extended to the basic formalism needed to account for three-body interactions [A. Carbone, A. Cipollone, C. Barbieri, A. Rios, and A. Polls, (Phys. Rev. C 88, 054326 (2013))]. The contribution of three-nucleon forces has so far been included in ab initio calculations on nuclear matter and finite nuclei only as averaged two-nucleon forces. We derive the working equations for all possible two- and three-nucleon terms that enter the expansion of the self-energy up to the third order, thus including the interaction-irreducible (i.e., not averaged) diagrams with three-nucleon forces that have been previously neglected. We employ the algebraic diagrammatic construction up to the third order as an organization scheme for generating a non perturbative self-energy, in which ring (particle-hole) and ladder (particle-particle) diagrams are resummed to all orders. We derive expressions of the static and dynamic self-energy up to the third order, by taking into account the set of diagrams required when either the skeleton or nonskeleton expansions of the single-particle propagator are assumed. A hierarchy of importance among different diagrams is revealed, and a particular emphasis is given to a third-order diagram (see Fig. 2c) that is expected to play a significant role among those featuring an interaction-irreducible three-nucleon force. A consistent formalism to resum at infinite order correlations induced by three-nucleon forces in the self-consistent Green's function theory is now available and ready to be implemented in the many-body solvers.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete set of working equations for the algebraic diagrammatic construction (ADC) up to third order, ADC(3), for the self-energy in self-consistent Green's function theory when three-body interactions are included in an interaction-irreducible (i.e., not averaged) form, which had been previously neglected in favor of averaged two-body effective forces.

## Method

The authors employ the algebraic diagrammatic construction up to third order as an organization scheme to generate a nonperturbative self-energy, in which ring (particle-hole) and ladder (particle-particle) diagrams are resummed to all orders. They derive explicit expressions for the static and dynamic self-energy up to third order by considering both skeleton and nonskeleton expansions of the single-particle propagator, using the effective Hamiltonian formalism of Carbone et al. (2013) to reduce the diagrammatic set to interaction-irreducible terms. The working equations are presented for diagrams with two-particle-one-hole (2p1h) and two-hole-one-particle (2h1p) intermediate states, as well as for a subset of diagrams with 3p2h and 3h2p intermediate states.

## Key numbers

* The paper notes that for nuclear interactions, three-nucleon forces are typically about one-tenth the strength of two-nucleon forces: \(<\widehat{W}>\approx\frac{1}{10}<\widehat{V}>\).
* The diagrammatic expansion includes 17 one-particle irreducible, skeleton, and interaction-irreducible self-energy diagrams at third order in the expansion of the dynamic self-energy.
* The second-order expansion contains exactly two one-particle irreducible, skeleton, and interaction-irreducible diagrams.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Green's function formalism developed by Carbone et al. (2013) (Phys. Rev. C 88, 054326), which introduced the basic diagrammatic rules for three-body interactions but did not derive the nonperturbative ADC equations. Previous SCGF calculations for nuclear matter and finite nuclei by Soma et al. (2008), Hebeler et al. (2010, 2011), Cipollone et al. (2013), and Carbone et al. (2013) included three-nucleon forces only as averaged two-nucleon forces. The derived formalism is now ready for implementation in many-body solvers, enabling consistent infinite-order resummation of correlations induced by three-nucleon forces.
