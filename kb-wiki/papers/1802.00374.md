# Multiparticle azimuthal cumulants in p+Pb collisions from a multiphase transport model

> A new subevent cumulant method was recently developed, which can significantly reduce the non-flow contributions in long-range correlations for small systems compared to the standard cumulant method. In this work, we study multi-particle cumulants in $p$+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV with a multiphase transport model (AMPT), including two- and four-particle cumulants ($c_{2}\{2\}$ and $c_{2}\{4\}$) and symmetric cumulants [SC(2, 3) and SC(2, 4)]. Our numerical results show that $v_{2}\{2\}$ is consistent with the experimental data, while the magnitude of $c_{2}\{4\}$ is smaller than the experimental data, which may indicate either the collectivity is underestimated or some dynamical fluctuations are absent in the AMPT model. For the symmetric cumulants, we find that the results from the standard cumulant method are consistent with the experimental data, but those from the subevent cumulant method show different behaviors. The results indicate that the measurements from the standard cumulant method are contaminated by non-flow effects, especially when the number of produced particles is small. The subevent cumulant method is a better tool to explore the $real$ collectivity in small systems.

# Multiparticle azimuthal cumulants in p+Pb collisions from a multiphase transport model

## Key claim
The AMPT model reproduces the two-particle elliptic flow $v_{2}\{2\}$ in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the four-particle cumulant $c_{2}\{4\}$ magnitude is systematically smaller than the ATLAS data, which may indicate that either the collectivity is underestimated or some non-Gaussian dynamical fluctuations are absent in the AMPT model. The subevent cumulant method reveals that standard cumulant measurements are contaminated by non-flow effects, especially at low multiplicity.

## Method
The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model with an elastic parton-parton cross section of 3 mb to simulate p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV. They calculated two- and four-particle azimuthal cumulants ($c_{2}\{2\}$ and $c_{2}\{4\}$) and symmetric cumulants SC(2,3) and SC(2,4) using both the standard Q-cumulant method and the newly developed subevent cumulant method (two-subevent and three-subevent variants), with kinematic cuts matching the ATLAS detector acceptance ($|\eta| < 2.5$, $0.3 < p_T < 3$ GeV). The evolution of cumulants was also traced at four stages of the AMPT model: initial stage, after parton cascade, after quark coalescence, and after hadronic rescatterings.

## Key numbers
- $v_{2}\{2\}$ from the subevent cumulant method agrees with CMS data (Chatrchyan 2013) and prior AMPT results (Bzdak 2014) across the full $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle$ range in p+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV.
- $c_{2}\{4\}$ from the standard cumulant method becomes positive at $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle < 70$, while subevent methods yield negative $c_{2}\{4\}$ over the full $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle$ range; the AMPT $c_{2}\{4\}$ magnitude is systematically smaller than ATLAS three-subevent data (Aaboud 2017).
- Hadronic rescatterings suppress $c_{2}\{4\}$ by a factor of approximately 8, while suppressing $c_{2}\{2\}$ by a factor of approximately 2.
- SC(2,3) from the standard cumulant method is negative at high multiplicity and positive at low multiplicity, matching CMS data (Sirunyan 2017); subevent methods yield negative SC(2,3) for the entire multiplicity range.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of collectivity in small systems using the AMPT model (Ma 2014, Bzdak 2014) by applying the subevent cumulant method developed by Jia (2017) to suppress non-flow contributions. It directly tests the claim by Huo (2017) that symmetric cumulant measurements in small systems are unreliable without subevent methods, which was based on PYTHIA and HIJING models lacking collective flow. The results enable future comparisons between transport models and hydrodynamic models for small-system collectivity, and suggest that the subevent cumulant method is necessary to isolate genuine collective flow from non-flow contamination in p+Pb collisions.
