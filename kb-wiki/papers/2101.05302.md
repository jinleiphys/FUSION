# Magnetars and Axion-like Particles: Probes with the Hard X-ray Spectrum

> Quiescent hard X-ray and soft gamma-ray emission from neutron stars constitute a promising frontier to explore axion-like-particles (ALPs). ALP production in the core peaks at energies of a few keV to a few hundreds of keV; subsequently, the ALPs escape and convert to photons in the magnetosphere. The emissivity goes as $\sim T^6$ while the conversion probability is enhanced for large magnetic fields, making magnetars, with their high core temperatures and strong magnetic fields, ideal targets for probing ALPs. We compute the energy spectrum of photons resulting from conversion of ALPs in the magnetosphere and then compare it against hard X-ray data from NuSTAR, INTEGRAL, and XMM-Newton, for a set of eight magnetars for which such data exists. Upper limits are placed on the product of the ALP-nucleon and ALP-photon couplings. For the production in the core, we perform a calculation of the ALP emissivity in degenerate nuclear matter modeled by a relativistic mean field theory. The reduction of the emissivity due to improvements to the one-pion exchange approximation is incorporated, as is the suppression of the emissivity due to proton superfluidity in the neutron star core. A range of core temperatures is considered, corresponding to different models of the steady heat transfer from the core to the stellar surface. For the subsequent conversion, we solve the coupled differential equations mixing ALPs and photons in the magnetosphere. The conversion occurs due to a competition between the dipolar magnetic field and the photon refractive index induced by the external magnetic field. Semi-analytic expressions are provided alongside the full numerical results. We also present an analysis of the uncertainty on the axion limits we derive due to the uncertainties in the magnetar masses, nuclear matter equation of state, and the proton superfluid critical temperature.

## Key claim

Magnetars, with their high core temperatures (up to ~10^9 K) and strong magnetic fields (up to 10^14-10^15 G), are ideal targets for probing axion-like particles (ALPs) because the ALP emissivity scales as T^6 and the conversion probability is enhanced by large magnetic fields; however, the derived upper limits on the product of ALP-nucleon and ALP-photon couplings depend significantly on uncertainties in magnetar masses, nuclear matter equation of state, and the proton superfluid critical temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated ALP production from nucleon bremsstrahlung processes (n+n, p+p, n+p) in the degenerate nuclear matter core of magnetars using a relativistic mean field theory (IUF equation of state) with one-pion exchange interactions, including a correction factor of C_pi = 1/4 to account for improvements to the OPE approximation and incorporating suppression from proton superfluidity. They then solved the coupled differential equations mixing ALPs and photons in the dipolar magnetosphere to compute the conversion probability, and compared the resulting theoretical photon energy spectrum against hard X-ray data from NuSTAR, INTEGRAL, and XMM-Newton for a set of eight magnetars.

## Key numbers

- ALP emissivity from n+n bremsstrahlung: Q^0_nn = (31/2835 pi) C_pi (m_n/m_pi)^4 f^4 G_an^2 p_Fn F(c) T^6, where C_pi = 1/4 is the correction factor to the one-pion exchange approximation
- ALP emissivity from n+p bremsstrahlung: Q^0_np = (124/2835 pi) C_pi (m_n/m_pi)^4 f^4 G_an^2 p_Fp G(c,d) T^6
- The magnetar core temperatures considered range from 10^8 K to 5 x 10^9 K (specifically: 10^8 K, 5 x 10^8 K, 10^9 K, and 5 x 10^9 K)
- The magnetar model uses a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with radius 12.6 km, where the inner 11.3 km is uniform nuclear matter core and the outer 1.3 km is crust
- The proton superfluid critical temperature is above 4 x 10^9 K in most of the core (for singlet pairing), while neutron triplet pairing critical temperature is below 3 x 10^8 K throughout the core
- At core temperature 10^8 K, the proton gap is 170 keV in the center of the magnetar, rising to a maximum of 1 MeV near the core-crust boundary

## Context

This work extends the methods developed by Fortin et al. (2018) for calculating ALP-photon conversion in neutron star magnetospheres, applying them systematically to eight magnetars with existing hard X-ray data. The study improves upon earlier work by Morris (1984) and Raffelt and Stodolsky (1987) by properly accounting for the full radial dependence of the conversion probability, which becomes substantial near the "radius of conversion" at approximately 1000 neutron star radii. The results enable future experiments targeting X-ray emission from magnetars to incorporate ALP searches as a component of their science goals, and the analysis of uncertainties in magnetar masses, nuclear equation of state, and proton superfluid critical temperature provides a framework for interpreting constraints from neutron star observations.
