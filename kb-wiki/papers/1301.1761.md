# Study of the behavior of nuclear modification factor in freeze-out state

> One of the latest trends in the advancement of experimental high-energy physics is to identify the quark gluon plasma (QGP) predicted qualitatively by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We discuss whether nuclear transparency effect which is considered an important phenomenon, connected with dynamics of hadron-nuclear and nuclear-nuclear interactions could reflect some particular properties of the medium. FASTMC is used for Au-Au collision at RHIC energies. Critical change in the transparency is considered a signal on the appearance of new phases of strongly interacting matter and the QGP.

# Study of the behavior of nuclear modification factor in freeze-out state

## Key claim
The nuclear modification factor R, defined as the ratio of particle yields in central to peripheral Au-Au collisions, shows a critical change and saturation at a thermal freeze-out temperature of about 150 MeV, which the authors interpret as a signal of a regime change in nuclear transparency and possible formation of new phases of strongly interacting matter including quark-gluon plasma (QGP).

## Method
The authors used the FASTMC (Fast Hadron Freezout Generator) to simulate Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, generating hadrons on the thermal freeze-out hypersurface. They computed the nuclear modification factor R as the ratio of particle yields in central collisions to those in peripheral collisions for octet baryons, octet pseudoscalar mesons, and their corresponding antiparticles, and studied R as a function of thermal freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers
- The critical change in the nuclear modification factor R occurs at a temperature of about 150 MeV, where the behavior transitions from a linearly increasing function of thermal freeze-out temperature to a temperature-independent straight line.
- The saturation temperature of approximately 150 MeV is identified as the point where the critical change in transparency indicates changing properties of the medium.
- The critical temperature for QGP formation is cited as of the order of 170 MeV.
- The saturation scale Lambda_s for RHIC energies is cited as of the order of 1-2 GeV.
- The freeze-out time scale is given as about 15 fm/c, after which particles stream freely without further strong interactions.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear transparency as a signal for phase transitions in strongly interacting matter, building on the concept that different states of heavy ion collisions (QGP phase, hadronization, freeze-out) should exhibit different transparency values. The authors reference STAR data at RHIC BNL (200 GeV Au+Au and p+p collisions) on strange particle nuclear modification factors and PHENIX results on J/psi suppression in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions. The approach enables using the freeze-out state to extract information about earlier stages of the collision, with the critical change in R as a function of temperature potentially serving as a signature for the formation of new phases of nuclear matter.
