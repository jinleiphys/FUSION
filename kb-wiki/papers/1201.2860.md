# Vector-like contributions from Optimized Perturbation in the Abelian Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model for cold and dense quark matter

> Two-loop corrections for the standard Abelian Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model are obtained with the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method. These contributions improve the usual mean-field and Hartree-Fock results by generating a $1/N_c$ suppressed term, which only contributes at finite chemical potential. We take the zero temperature limit observing that, within the OPT, chiral symmetry is restored at a higher chemical potential $μ$, while the resulting equation of state is stiffer than the one obtained when mean-field is applied to the standard version of the model. In order to understand the physical nature of these finite $N_c$ contributions, we perform a numerical analysis to show that the OPT quantum corrections mimic effective repulsive vector-vector interaction contributions. We also derive a simple analytical approximation for the mass gap, accurate at the percent level, matching the mean-field approximation extended by an extra vector channel to OPT. For $μ\gtrsim μ_c$ the effective vector coupling matching OPT is numerically close (for the Abelian model) to the Fierz-induced Hartree-Fock value $G/(2N_c)$, where $G$ is the scalar coupling, and then increases with $μ$ in a well-determined manner.

## Key claim

The Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method applied to the standard Abelian Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model generates effective repulsive vector-vector interaction contributions at next-to-leading order in 1/N_c, which only appear at finite chemical potential; the effective vector coupling that matches OPT results near criticality is numerically close to the Fierz-induced Hartree-Fock value G/(2N_c) for the Abelian model, but this agreement is a numerical accident rather than a generic feature.

## Method

The authors applied the OPT method to the Abelian NJL model at zero temperature, computing the free energy density to two-loop order (first order in the OPT expansion). They compared the OPT results for the quark effective mass, pressure, baryon density, energy density, and equation of state with mean-field approximation (MFA) results obtained by adding an explicit repulsive vector-vector interaction term -G_V (ψ̄ γ^ν ψ)^2 to the Lagrangian. A simple analytical approximation for the mass gap was derived by expanding to first order in the quark mass M, yielding a percent-level accurate expression that allowed matching the OPT and MFA results to determine the effective G_V(μ) relation.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential μ_c = 358.5 MeV for OPT (with G_V=0), compared to μ_c = 350.9 MeV for MFA with G_V=0
- MFA critical chemical potentials for different vector couplings: μ_c = 356.3 MeV for G_V = 3G/(8N_c), μ_c = 358.3 MeV for G_V = G/(2N_c), μ_c = 361.9 MeV for G_V = 3G/(4N_c)
- Model parameters: Λ = 620 MeV, GΛ^2 = 4.44, m_c = 5.0 MeV (OPT) or m_c = 5.36 MeV (MFA), producing f_π = 92.4 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, M = 341 MeV, and -⟨ψ̄ ψ⟩^{1/3} = 191 MeV
- Effective vector coupling from analytical matching: G_V N_c/G ≈ 0.53 at μ ≈ μ_c ≈ 360 MeV, rising to G_V ≈ 0.6 G/N_c at μ ≈ 400 MeV and G_V ≈ 1.13 G/N_c at μ ≈ 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous applications of OPT to the NJL model (Kneur et al., 2011) by explicitly demonstrating that the 1/N_c corrections generated by OPT mimic the effects of an explicit repulsive vector-vector interaction term -G_V (ψ̄ γ^ν ψ)^2, which is known from studies by Koch, Buballa, and others to influence the first-order phase transition region and matter stability at finite density. The results enable a parameter-free way to incorporate vector-like corrections in NJL-type models and suggest that OPT captures physics beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation, with implications for the QCD phase diagram and the location of the critical point, which the authors indicate will be investigated in future work for the SU(2) and SU(N) versions of the model.
