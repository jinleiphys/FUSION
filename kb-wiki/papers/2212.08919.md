# Excited states of zero seniority based on a pair condensate

> We study the excited states of zero seniority for various like-particle systems interacting by pairing forces and by general two-body interactions. We consider two types of excitations, generated from a ground state described by a pair condensate. One type is obtained by breaking a pair from the ground state condensate and replacing it by "excited" collective pairs built on time-reversed single-particle orbits. The second type of zero seniority excited states is described by a condensate of identical excited pairs. The structure of these excited states is analysed for the picked fence model and for the valence neutrons of $^{108}$Sn. For a state-depending pairing interaction, the first type of excited states agree well with the J=0 states which are known in $^{108}$Sn. At the same time, these states can be also associated unambiguously with those exact states which are the closest in energy to the experimental levels. The states corresponding to the excited pair condensate appear at low energies, around the energy of the second excited state of the first type, and they do not have a simple correspondence with exact eigenstates. However, at a much higher excitation energy there is an exact state which is similar in structure to an EPC state. It is shown that this EPC state has the features of a giant pairing vibration.

## Key claim

For a state-dependent pairing interaction, the one-broken-pair excited states agree well with the known J=0 states in $^{108}$Sn, but the excited pair condensate (EPC) states appear at low energies without a simple correspondence to exact eigenstates; only at a much higher excitation energy does an exact state with the features of a giant pairing vibration resemble an EPC state.

## Method

The authors constructed two types of zero-seniority excited states from a particle-number projected BCS (PBCS) pair condensate ground state: one-broken-pair states, formed by replacing one pair in the condensate with an excited collective pair, and excited pair condensate (EPC) states, formed by replacing all pairs with identical excited collective pairs. They compared these variational approximations to exact solutions from the Richardson method for state-independent pairing in a picket fence model and to exact diagonalization results for the valence neutrons of $^{108}$Sn using both a state-dependent pairing interaction and a general shell-model interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the picket fence model with 4 pairs in 8 equidistant levels and pairing strength g=0.7–1.1, the PBCS ground state energy deviates from the exact value by at most 0.26% (at g=1.1).
- For the picket fence model at g=0.7, the EPC state with the highest overlap (0.986) with an exact eigenstate has energy -49.596, compared to the exact eigenstate energy of -49.935.
- For $^{108}$Sn with state-dependent pairing, the 46th exact seniority-zero state (energy -31.854) has an overlap of 0.995 with an EPC state of energy -31.905.
- For $^{108}$Sn with the general shell-model interaction, the PBCS ground state binding energy is very close to the exact result, and the first one-broken-pair state agrees well with the second exact excited state and the first experimental J=0 level.

## Context

This work extends the PBCS approach for ground states (Dietrich, Sandulescu and Bertsch) to excited zero-seniority states, complementing earlier one-broken-pair studies (Talmi, Gambir, Bonsignori, Allaart) by also introducing EPC states. The analysis of the EPC state in $^{108}$Sn connects to the concept of giant pairing vibrations (GPV) previously studied for lead isotopes (Broglia, Liotta). The results enable future experimental searches for low-energy EPC states and clarify which exact J=0 states in $^{108}$Sn correspond to simple one-broken-pair structures.
