# The Role of the Equation of State in Binary Mergers

> Binary mergers involving black holes and neutron stars have been proposed as major sources of gravitational waves, r--process nucleosynthesis, and gamma ray bursters. In addition, they represent an important, and possibly unique, observable that could distinguish between normal and self--bound neutron stars. These two families of stars have distinctly different mass--radius relationships resulting from their equations of state which can be revealed during their mergers if stable mass transfer ensues. We consider two cases of gravitational-radiation induced binary mergers: (i) a black hole and a normal neutron star, and (ii) a black hole and a self-bound strange quark matter star. We extend previous Newtonian analyses to incorporate the pseudo-general relativistic Paczyński-Wiita potential or a potential correct to second--order post-Newtonian order in Arnowitt--Deser--Misner coordinates. These potentials are employed to study both the orbital evolution of the binary and the Roche lobe geometry that determines when and if stable mass transfer between the components is possible. The Roche lobe geometry with pseudo-general relativistic or post-Newtonian potentials has not heretofore been considered. Our analysis shows that differences in the evolution of normal neutron stars and strange quark matter stars are significant and could be detected in gravity waves. Both the amplitude and frequencies of the wave pattern are affected. In addition, details of the equation of state for either normal neutron stars or strange quark stars may be learned. A single merger could reveal one or two individual points of the mass-radius relation, and observations of several mergers could map a significant portion of this relation.

## Key claim

Differences in the evolution of normal neutron stars and strange quark matter stars during binary mergers with a black hole are significant and could be detected in gravitational waves, but only if stable mass transfer occurs; the analysis uses pseudo-general relativistic or post-Newtonian potentials to extend previous Newtonian treatments of Roche lobe geometry and orbital evolution.

## Method

The authors extended previous Newtonian analyses of gravitational-radiation induced binary mergers by incorporating the pseudo-general relativistic Paczyński-Wiita potential or a potential correct to second-order post-Newtonian order in Arnowitt-Deser-Misner coordinates. They employed these potentials to study both the orbital evolution of the binary and the Roche lobe geometry that determines when and if stable mass transfer between the components is possible, using a semi-analytic approach with point-like masses and conservative mass transfer assumptions.

## Key numbers

- The estimated NS-NS merger rate is \(1.8\times 10^{-4}\) yr\(^{-1}\) galaxy\(^{-1}\) with an uncertainty of about a factor of 3 to 10.
- For the advanced LIGO detector, mergers up to distances of 350 Mpc could be observed, with a total detection rate of up to approximately 1 day\(^{-1}\) anticipated.
- The NS-BH merger rate has been estimated to be about \(10^{-5}-10^{-4}\) yr\(^{-1}\) galaxy\(^{-1}\), or up to 6 times larger than the NS-NS merger rate.
- For normal neutron stars, stable mass transfer terminates when the star's mass approaches the minimum neutron star mass of approximately 0.1 M\(_\odot\); for strange quark matter stars, it continues indefinitely.
- For small to moderate mass self-bound stars, \(R \propto M^{1/3}\) so that \(\alpha \cong 1/3\); only for configurations approaching the maximum mass does \(\alpha\) turn negative.
- The maximum masses for prototype EOS's range from 1.38 M\(_\odot\) (GS1) to 2.77 M\(_\odot\) (MS0), with corresponding radii at maximum mass from 8.27 km (GS1) to 13.32 km (MS0).
- The 2PN Roche lobe radius fit uses parameters \(\alpha_{\sss C} = 1.951\) and \(\beta_{\sss C} = 1.812\) in the correction factor \(C(q, z) = 1 + z\,(\alpha_{\sss C}\, q^{1/5} - \beta_{\sss C})\).

## Context

This work extends earlier Newtonian treatments of binary mergers by Clark (1977), Jaranowski (1992), and Portegies Zwart (1998) to include general relativistic corrections via pseudo-potentials and post-Newtonian approximations, and builds on the Roche lobe overflow model for mass transfer. The analysis enables future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish between normal neutron stars and strange quark matter stars, and to map the mass-radius relation of neutron stars through observations of several mergers.
