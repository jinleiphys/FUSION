# Universal tetramer and pentamer in two-dimensional fermionic mixtures

> We study the emergence of universal tetramer and pentamer bound states in the two-dimensional $(N+1)$ system, which consists of $N$ identical heavy fermions interacting with a light atom. We show that the critical heavy-light mass ratio to support a ($3+1$) tetramer below the trimer threshold is $3.38$, and to support a ($4+1$) pentamer below the tetramer threshold is $5.14$. While these ground state tetramer and pentamer are both with zero total angular momentum, they exhibit very different density distributions and correlations in momentum space, due to their distinct angular momentum decompositions in the dimer-fermion frame. These universal bound states can be accessible by a number of Fermi-Fermi mixtures now realized in cold atoms laboratories, which also suggest novel few-body correlations dominant in their corresponding many-body systems.

# Universal tetramer and pentamer in two-dimensional fermionic mixtures

## Key claim

The paper claims that ground-state tetramer (3+1) and pentamer (4+1) bound states emerge in two-dimensional fermionic mixtures at critical heavy-light mass ratios of 3.38 and 5.14 respectively, but only when the total angular momentum is zero, a condition the abstract does not emphasize. The previously reported tetramer at mass ratio 5.0 is actually an excited state, not the ground state.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for the (N+1) system of N identical heavy fermions interacting with a light atom via a contact interaction in two dimensions. They transform the integral equation for the F-function, which describes relative motion between the heavy-light dimer and the remaining fermions, into a matrix equation using coarse-graining of momentum magnitude and angle. For the trimer and tetramer they solve the matrix equation directly; for the pentamer they use an iterative method due to larger matrix size. The total angular momentum is decomposed into contributions from relative angular momenta between the dimer and each individual fermion.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass ratio for the ground state (3+1) tetramer below the trimer threshold: 3.38
- Critical mass ratio for the ground state (4+1) pentamer below the tetramer threshold: 5.14
- The previously reported tetramer (Parish) emerges at mass ratio 5.0 with total angular momentum |m_tot| = 1, and is an excited state
- The trimer emerges at mass ratio 3.34 with total angular momentum |m_tot| = 1, consistent with Pricoupenko
- Binomial fitting parameters for tetramer energy difference: alpha = 7.08, beta = 17.39, eta_c = 3.38
- Binomial fitting parameters for pentamer energy difference: alpha = 0.58, beta = 0.52, eta_c = 5.14

## Context

This work extends prior studies of universal few-body bound states in two-dimensional fermionic systems, where only a trimer at mass ratio 3.34 (Pricoupenko) and an excited tetramer at mass ratio 5.0 (Parish) were previously known. The results contrast with three-dimensional systems, where universal trimers, tetramers, and pentamers require much larger mass ratios (e.g., 8.2 for the 3D trimer from Kartavtsev and Malykh). The predicted bound states are accessible in existing cold-atom Fermi-Fermi mixtures such as 40K-6Li and 53Cr-6Li, and the distinct momentum-space correlation patterns, including spontaneous triangular crystallization in the two-body correlation of the tetramer and centered-triangle patterns in the pentamer, provide experimental signatures measurable via time-of-flight imaging and single-atom resolved detection.
