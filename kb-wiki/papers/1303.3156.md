# Renormalization Group Running of Dimension-Six Sources of Parity and Time-Reversal Violation

> We perform a systematic study of flavor-diagonal parity- and time-reversal-violating operators of dimension six which could arise from physics beyond the SM. We begin at the unknown high-energy scale where these operators originate. At this scale the operators are constrained by gauge invariance which has important consequences for the form of effective operators at lower energies. In particular for the four-quark operators. We calculate one-loop QCD and, when necessary, electroweak corrections to the operators and evolve them down to the electroweak scale and subsequently to hadronic scales. We find that for most operators QCD corrections are not particularly significant. We derive a set of operators at low energy which is expected to dominate hadronic and nuclear EDMs due to physics beyond the SM and obtain quantitative relations between these operators and the original dimension-six operators at the high-energy scale. We use the limit on the neutron EDM to set bounds on the dimension-six operators.

## Key claim

The paper systematically derives the low-energy set of parity- and time-reversal-violating (P/T) operators that dominate hadronic and nuclear electric dipole moments (EDMs) from physics beyond the Standard Model, finding that QCD corrections are not particularly significant for most operators, and that gauge invariance at the high-energy scale restricts the form of low-energy four-quark operators to only two types (color-singlet and color-octet pseudoscalar-scalar) rather than the ten possible operators considered in previous work.

## Method

The authors begin at an unknown high-energy scale (M_T) above the electroweak scale, where they list all gauge-invariant dimension-six P/T operators involving first-generation quarks, gauge fields, and the Higgs boson. They calculate one-loop QCD and, where necessary, electroweak corrections to these operators, then evolve them using renormalization-group equations down to the electroweak scale (M_EW ~ 100 GeV), integrate out heavy SM fields (top quark, W/Z bosons, Higgs), and continue the evolution to the QCD scale (~1 GeV). The resulting low-energy operators are then used with the neutron EDM limit to set bounds on the original dimension-six coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM upper bound forces the QCD theta term to satisfy theta_bar <= 10^{-10} (citing prior bound from dnbound)
- The electroweak scale is taken as M_EW ~ 100 GeV
- The high-energy scale M_T is assumed to lie considerably higher than M_EW
- The anomalous dimension matrix for the four-quark operators (gamma_PS) is given analytically in terms of N (number of colors = 3) and n_f (number of flavors), with specific entries: gamma_PS = -2 * matrix with elements [(3N+4)(N^2-1)/N^2 - beta_0, (N+1)^2(N-2)(N-1)/N^3; -4(N+2)/N, 2(N^2+2)/N^2 - 2C_2(N) - beta_0]
- The mixing of four-quark operators into quark (C)EDMs (gamma_mix) contains factors of m_q'/m_q, e.g., for up quark EDM from O_PS1 the entry is -m_d/m_u
- The quark-Higgs operator anomalous dimension gamma_qH = -6*C_2(N) where C_2(N) = (N^2-1)/(2N)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to P/T violation by systematically enforcing SM gauge symmetries on dimension-six operators, building on the operator classification of Buchmuller and Wyler (1985) and Grzadkowski et al. (2010). It differs from prior studies by An et al. (2009) and Hisano et al. (2012), who considered the most general SU_c(3) x U(1) invariant four-quark operators; the present work shows that gauge invariance restricts the relevant set to only two operators. The derived low-energy operator set enables future calculations of hadronic and nuclear EDMs using chiral perturbation theory (as in de Vries et al., 2011-2012) or lattice QCD, and allows comparison of EDM bounds with direct LHC constraints on new physics models.
