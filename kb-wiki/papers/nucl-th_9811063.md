# Eta, Omega and Charmed Mesic Nuclei

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, we investigate theoretically whether $η$ and $ω$ mesons form meson-nucleus bound states. We study several nuclei from $^{6}$He to $^{208}$Pb, including those which are the final nuclei in the proposed experiment at GSI. Results for the $ω$ are compared with those of the Walecka model. Our results suggest that one should expect to find $η$- and $ω$-nucleus bound states in all these nuclei. Furthermore, we investigate the possibility of charmed mesic nuclei. It is shown that the $D^-$ meson will inevitably form narrow bound states with $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim
Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the paper predicts that η and ω mesons should form bound states in all nuclei studied from ⁶He to ²⁰⁸Pb, and that the D⁻ meson will inevitably form narrow bound states with ²⁰⁸Pb; however, the predicted binding energies depend on phenomenological parameters for in-medium meson widths (γ_η = 0.5, γ_ω = 0.2) and on an uncertain vector coupling strength for the D⁻ case.

## Method
The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation in momentum space for meson-nucleus bound states, using mean-field potentials derived from the QMC model. The QMC model treats mesons as MIT bags containing quarks and antiquarks that interact with scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) mean fields in the nucleus, with the same quark-level coupling constants used for nucleons. For the ω meson, results are compared with the Walecka model (QHD) including σ-ω mixing, and for the D⁻ meson two choices of the vector coupling strength are tested (the standard QMC value and a 1.4² enhancement suggested by K⁺-nucleus scattering).

## Key numbers
- η meson 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −56.3 MeV (width 33.2 MeV) with γ_η = 0.5
- ω meson 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −118 MeV (width 33.1 MeV) with γ_ω = 0.2 in QMC; −157 MeV (width 40.8 MeV) in the Walecka model
- ω meson 1s bound state energy in ⁶He: −55.6 MeV (width 24.7 MeV) in QMC; −97.4 MeV (width 33.5 MeV) in the Walecka model
- D⁻ meson 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −10.6 MeV with enhanced vector coupling (Ṽ_ω); −35.2 MeV with standard QMC vector coupling (V_ω)
- D⁻ meson 1s bound state energy without Coulomb potential: −11.2 MeV (standard V_ω)
- D⁰ meson 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −96.2 MeV (standard V_ω, zero width assumed)
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends the theoretical investigation of meson-nucleus bound states stimulated by the experimental proposal of Hayano et al. (1998) using the (d,³He) reaction at GSI. It contrasts the QMC model predictions for the ω meson with those of the Walecka model (QHD) from earlier work by the same group, finding systematically less binding in QMC. The study of charmed mesic nuclei (D⁻ in ²⁰⁸Pb) is motivated by the absence of strong decay channels for the D⁻, promising cleaner experimental signatures than D⁰ or D⁺ mesons, which couple to open charm channels. The results enable experimental searches at GSI and TJNAF for η- and ω-mesic nuclei, and suggest that D⁻-nucleus bound states could provide direct information on the Lorentz scalar σ field and partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter.
