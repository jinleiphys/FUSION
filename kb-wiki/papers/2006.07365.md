# Acoplanarity of QED pairs accompanied by nuclear dissociation in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions

> This paper investigates the transverse momentum broadening effect for electromagnetic production of dileptons in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions accompanied by nuclear dissociation. The electromagnetic dissociation probability of nuclei for different neutron multiplicities is estimated, which could serve as a centrality definition (i.e. impact parameter estimate) in ultra-peripheral collisions. In the framework of lowest-order QED, the acoplanarity of dilepton pairs is calculated for different neutron emission scenarios in ultra-peripheral collisions, indicating significant impact-parameter dependence. The verification of impact-parameter dependence is crucially important to understand the broadening effect observed in hadronic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the transverse momentum broadening observed in dilepton production in hadronic heavy-ion collisions may originate from impact-parameter dependence of the QED production process itself, rather than from in-medium effects in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, provided that the baseline broadening can be precisely characterized using neutron multiplicity tags in ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate the electromagnetic dissociation probability of nuclei for different neutron multiplicities using the equivalent photon approximation, treating the mean number of photon absorptions as a Poisson distribution. They then compute the acoplanarity of lepton pairs from photon-photon interactions in lowest-order QED using the external field approximation, with the multi-dimensional integration performed via the VEGAS Monte Carlo routine. The cross section for lepton pair production with mutual electromagnetic excitation is obtained by integrating over impact parameter the product of the pair production probability, the nuclear dissociation probabilities, and the probability of no hadronic interaction from the optical Glauber model.

## Key numbers

- The $\langle \alpha \rangle$ and $\sqrt{\langle p_{T}^{2}\rangle}$ for ($>$1n$>$1n) are 20 to 30 percent larger than for (0n0n) in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV.
- The total dissociation cross section integrated over impact parameter is found to be consistent with experimental measurements by the ALICE collaboration (PhysRevLett.109.252302).
- The calculations are performed for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
- The $p_{T}^{2}$ range is limited to less than 0.01 (units not specified but GeV$^2$/c$^2$ implied) to avoid the influence of the Sudakov effect.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lowest-order QED calculation (Zha et al., 2020) that described STAR and ATLAS data without in-medium effects, and contrasts with the approach of Klein et al. (2018) who used effective QED multiple scattering parameters $\langle \hat{q}_{QED} L \rangle$ of order (50 MeV)$^2$ to (100 MeV)$^2$ to describe ATLAS data. The paper enables experimental verification of impact-parameter dependent broadening in ultra-peripheral collisions using neutron multiplicity tags from Zero Degree Calorimeters, which would provide a baseline for extracting possible medium effects in hadronic collisions.
