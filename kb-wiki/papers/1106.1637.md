# Thermodynamics and Instabilities of a Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Plasma

> We extend our analysis of a IIB supergravity solution dual to a spatially anisotropic finite-temperature N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma. The solution is static, possesses an anisotropic horizon, and is completely regular. The full geometry can be viewed as a renormalization group flow from an AdS geometry in the ultraviolet to a Lifshitz-like geometry in the infrared. The anisotropy can be equivalently understood as resulting from a position-dependent theta-term or from a non-zero number density of dissolved D7-branes. The holographic stress tensor is conserved and anisotropic. The presence of a conformal anomaly plays an important role in the thermodynamics. The phase diagram exhibits homogeneous and inhomogeneous (i.e. mixed) phases. In some regions the homogeneous phase displays instabilities reminiscent of those of weakly coupled plasmas. We comment on similarities with QCD at finite baryon density and with the phenomenon of cavitation.

## Key claim

The paper extends a holographic IIB supergravity solution dual to a spatially anisotropic finite-temperature N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma, showing that the phase diagram contains both homogeneous and mixed (inhomogeneous) phases, with the homogeneous phase exhibiting instabilities reminiscent of weakly coupled plasmas in some regions, though the authors explicitly leave open whether these instabilities are the strong-coupling counterpart of weak-coupling instabilities.

## Method

The authors construct a static, regular type IIB supergravity solution with an anisotropic horizon, factorized as a five-dimensional axion-dilaton-gravity system times an S^5, dual to N=4 SYM deformed by a position-dependent theta-term linear in one spatial coordinate (z). They solve the equations of motion numerically for the metric functions and dilaton, compute the holographic stress tensor via holographic renormalization with appropriate counterterms, and determine the phase diagram by analyzing the free energy as a function of temperature T and anisotropy parameter a (proportional to the D7-brane density).

## Key numbers

- Entropy density in the isotropic limit (a << T): s^0(T) = (π^2/2) N_c^2 T^3
- Entropy density in the anisotropic limit (a >> T): s = c_ent N_c^2 a^{1/3} T^{8/3}, with c_ent a numerical coefficient
- The axion parameter a is related to the D7-brane density n by a = λ n / (4π N_c), where λ is the 't Hooft coupling
- The conformal anomaly induces a non-zero trace of the stress tensor, with the logarithmic term coefficient given by \tilde{g}^{(4)}_{ij} = (a^4/48) diag(1, -1, -1, 3)
- The blackening factor cf vanishes at the horizon u = u_H, with temperature given by T = (cf_1 sqrt(cb_H))/(4π)

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature anisotropic solution of Azeyanagi, Li, and Takayanagi (2009) to finite temperature, providing a UV completion of a Lifshitz-like IR geometry. It relates to holographic studies of non-commutative SYM theories at finite temperature (e.g., Aharony et al., 2000), p-wave superconductors (e.g., Gubser, 2008; Hartnoll et al., 2008), and anisotropic plasmas with singular horizons. The results enable future studies of anisotropic fluid dynamics via the fluid/gravity correspondence and provide a framework for investigating whether the identified instabilities correspond to the strong-coupling analog of Weibel-type instabilities known from weakly coupled plasmas (Weibel, 1959; Mrowczynski, 1988).
