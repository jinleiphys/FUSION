# Rigorous asymptotic and moment-preserving diffusion approximations for generalized linear Boltzmann transport in arbitrary dimension

> We derive new diffusion solutions to the monoenergetic generalized linear Boltzmann transport equation (GLBE) for the stationary collision density and scalar flux about an isotropic point source in an infinite $d$-dimensional absorbing medium with isotropic scattering. We consider both classical transport theory with exponentially-distributed free paths in arbitrary dimensions as well as a number of non-classical transport theories (non-exponential random flights) that describe a broader class of transport processes within partially-correlated random media. New rigorous asymptotic diffusion approximations are derived where possible. We also generalize Grosjean's moment-preserving approach of separating the first (or uncollided) distribution from the collided portion and approximating only the latter using diffusion. We find that for any spatial dimension and for many free-path distributions Grosjean's approach produces compact, analytic approximations that are, overall, more accurate for high absorption and for small source-detector separations than either $P_1$ diffusion or rigorous asymptotic diffusion. These diffusion-based approximations are exact in the first two even spatial moments, which we derive explicitly for various non-classical transport types. We also discuss connections between the random-flight-theory derivation of the Green's function and the discrete spectrum of the transport operator and report some new observations regarding the discrete eigenvalues of the transport operator for general dimensions and free-path distributions.

## Key claim

The paper derives new diffusion approximations for the generalized linear Boltzmann transport equation (GLBE) in arbitrary dimensions with non-exponential free-path distributions, finding that Grosjean's moment-preserving approach outperforms both classical \(P_1\) diffusion and rigorous asymptotic diffusion for high absorption and small source-detector separations, though rigorous asymptotic diffusion achieves higher accuracy far from the source where transient terms are negligible.

## Method

The authors consider monoenergetic isotropic point sources in infinite \(d\)-dimensional absorbing media with isotropic scattering, analyzing both classical transport with exponentially-distributed free paths and non-classical transport with beta-prime, Chi, and Gamma (Erlang) free-path distributions. They derive rigorous asymptotic diffusion approximations by identifying poles of the Fourier-transformed total collision density and scalar flux, and generalize Grosjean's approach of separating the uncollided distribution from the collided portion and approximating only the latter using diffusion. The diffusion approximations are obtained as inverse Fourier transforms of frequency-domain Pade approximants, and the authors validate conjectures that both \(P_1\) and Grosjean diffusion lengths depend only on the mean free path, mean square free path, and dimension \(d\).

## Key numbers

- For exponential transport in flatland (\(d=2\)), the rigorous asymptotic diffusion approximation for scalar flux is \(\phi(r) \approx c K_0(r \sqrt{1-c^2})/\pi\), with discrete eigenvalue \(\nu_0 = 1/\sqrt{1-c^2}\)
- For exponential transport in the rod model (\(d=1\)), the exact total collision density is \(\Cpt(r) = e^{-r\sqrt{1-c}}/(2\sqrt{1-c})\)
- For Chi random flights in flatland with \(k=2\), the rigorous asymptotic diffusion approximation for collision density is \(\Cpt(r) \approx K_0(\sqrt{\pi} r \sqrt{\log(1/c)})/(2c)\)
- For Chi random flights in 3D with \(k=3\), the classical \(P_1\) diffusion approximation for collision density is \(\Cpt(r) \approx 4 e^{-4\sqrt{1-c} r/\sqrt{\pi}}/(\pi^2 r)\)
- For Gamma random flights in 3D with \(k=2\), the exact total collision density is \(\Cpt(r) = e^{-2\sqrt{1-c} r}/(\pi r)\), with discrete eigenvalue \(\nu_0 = 1/(2\sqrt{1-c})\)
- For Gamma random flights in flatland with \(k=2\), the discrete eigenvalue is \(\nu_0 = 1/(2\sqrt{1-c^{2/3}})\)
- For beta-prime random flights, the conjectured \(P_1\) diffusion length is \(\nu_0 = 1/(\sqrt{2}\sqrt{(1-c)d(k-2)/k})\)
- For Chi random flights in general dimension, the first two even collision density moments are dimension-independent: \(\int \Omega_d(r) \Cpt(r) dr = 1/(1-c)\) and \(\int \Omega_d(r) r^2 \Cpt(r) dr = k\Gamma(k/2)^2/(2(c-1)^2\Gamma((k+1)/2)^2)\)

## Context

This work extends classical transport theory (exponential free paths) to non-classical transport processes relevant for partially-correlated random media, building on prior work by Stadje (1987), Paasschens (1997), and Zoia et al. (2011) who provided time-resolved solutions in 2D and 4D. The paper generalizes Grosjean's moment-preserving diffusion approach, previously applied to classical 3D transport, to arbitrary dimensions and non-exponential free-path distributions including beta-prime, Chi, and Gamma families. The results enable improved diffusion approximations for transport problems where classical \(P_1\) diffusion fails, particularly for high absorption and small source-detector separations, and establish connections between random-flight theory and the discrete spectrum of the transport operator for general dimensions.
