# Cool quark matter

> We generalize the state-of-the-art perturbative Equation of State of cold quark matter to nonzero temperatures, needed in the description of neutron star mergers and core collapse processes. The new result is accurate to order g^5 in the gauge coupling, and is based on a novel framework for dealing with the infrared sensitive soft field modes of the theory. The zero Matsubara mode sector is treated using a dimensionally reduced effective theory, while the soft non-zero modes are resummed using the Hard Thermal Loop approximation. This combination of known effective descriptions offers unprecedented access to small but nonzero temperatures, both in and out of beta equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the perturbative equation of state (EoS) of cold quark matter to nonzero temperatures, achieving accuracy to order g^5 in the gauge coupling, but the result is restricted to temperatures where the soft non-zero Matsubara modes can be treated using the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation and the zero mode sector via dimensionally reduced Electrostatic QCD (EQCD), meaning the framework is valid only for temperatures small compared to the chemical potential but not vanishing.

## Method

The QCD pressure is decomposed into three parts: a naive loop expansion for hard modes, a dimensionally reduced effective theory (EQCD) for the static (zero Matsubara mode) sector, and a Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation for the soft non-zero Matsubara modes. The naive QCD pressure is taken from existing three-loop (order g^4) results, the EQCD contribution is evaluated analytically to order g^5, and the HTL contribution is computed numerically via a ring-sum integral, with the combination of all three terms rendered UV and IR finite. The final expression smoothly interpolates between the known zero-temperature and high-temperature limits.

## Key numbers

* The pressure is accurate to order g^5 in the gauge coupling, where g^2 = 4π α_s.
* The Debye screening mass squared is m_E^2 = (g^2/3)[(N_c + N_f/2)T^2 + (3/(2π^2)) Σ_f μ_f^2].
* The HTL correction takes the form p_HTL^corr = (d_A m_E^4)/(256π^2) f_HTL(T/m_E), where d_A = N_c^2 - 1.
* In the low-temperature limit, f_HTL(x) → 4 ln x + 11 - 4γ - 2π^2/3 + (14 ln 2)/3 + (16 ln^2 2)/3 + 4 ln π - δ - (64π/3)x + O(x^2), with δ ≈ -0.8563832.
* In the high-temperature limit, f_HTL(x) → -0.006178(1)/x^2 + O(1/x^3).
* The pressure is evaluated for temperatures T = 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV as a function of baryon chemical potential μ_B, with renormalization scale varied by a factor of 2 around the midpoint of the μ_B=0 scale (0.723 × 4πT) and the T=0 scale (2μ_B/3).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature perturbative EoS of Kurkela (2009) and the high-temperature EQCD result of avpres to the intermediate regime of small but nonzero temperatures relevant for neutron star mergers and core collapse. It improves upon the earlier tour-de-force resummation of Ipp (2006), which was limited to three massless quark flavors at equal chemical potentials and produced a cumbersome numerical result. The new EoS enables phenomenological modeling of neutron star mergers by providing a smooth interpolation between the T=0 limit and temperatures up to ~100 MeV, and can be used to constrain the behavior of the EoS at moderate densities following the strategy of Kurkela (2014).
