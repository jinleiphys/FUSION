# Roles of Quark Degrees of Freedom in Hypernuclei

> The quark model description of the hyperon nucleon forces, especially the antisymmetric spin-orbit forces, is studied from the spin-flavor SU(6) and the flavor SU(3) symmetry point of view. It is pointed out that the quark exchange interaction predicts strong antisymmetric spin-orbit force between the hyperon and nucleon.

## Key claim

The quark exchange interaction predicts a strong antisymmetric spin-orbit (ALS) force between hyperons and nucleons, comparable in magnitude to the ordinary symmetric spin-orbit (SLS) force, but only in channels where SU(3) flavor symmetry allows it; in channels where ALS vanishes in the SU(3) limit (such as ΣN with I=3/2), the ALS remains strongly suppressed even when SU(3) breaking is included.

## Method

The author analyzes the antisymmetric spin-orbit (ALS) force in hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions using two complementary approaches: (1) a quark cluster model (QCM) where the short-range baryon-baryon interaction is derived from one-gluon exchange between quarks, with the six-quark wave function constrained by the Pauli principle and spin-flavor SU(6) symmetry; and (2) a one-boson exchange (OBE) potential model where ALS arises from vector meson exchanges (ρ, ω₈, K*) when the F/D ratios for vector and tensor couplings differ. Both approaches are evaluated in the SU(3) symmetric limit and with SU(3) breaking from strange quark mass differences.

## Key numbers

- Quark cluster model ALS matrix element at R=0 for ΛN ← ΛN (I=1/2): 37 MeV in SU(3) limit, 32 MeV with SU(3) breaking
- Quark cluster model ALS matrix element at R=0 for ΣN ← ΣN (I=1/2): -88 MeV in SU(3) limit, -79 MeV with SU(3) breaking
- Quark cluster model ALS matrix element at R=0 for ΣN ← ΣN (I=3/2): 0 MeV in SU(3) limit, 1 MeV with SU(3) breaking
- Quark cluster model ordinary LS matrix element at R=0 for ΛN ← ΛN (I=1/2): -74 MeV in SU(3) limit, -55 MeV with SU(3) breaking
- Quark cluster model ordinary LS matrix element at R=0 for ΣN ← ΣN (I=3/2): -95 MeV in SU(3) limit, -94 MeV with SU(3) breaking
- OBE vector meson exchange ALS coefficient for ⟨8ₐ ¹P₁|V_ALS|8ₛ ³P₁⟩: total 20 (in units of fg(α-β)/√5), from ρ=8, ω₈=4, K*=8
- OBE vector meson exchange ALS coefficient for ⟨ΛN ¹P₁|V_ALS|ΛN ³P₁⟩: total -1 (in same units)
- OBE vector meson exchange ALS coefficient for ⟨ΣN(1/2) ¹P₁|V_ALS|ΣN(1/2) ³P₁⟩: total 3 (in same units)

## Context

This work extends the quark cluster model description of short-range baryon-baryon interactions previously developed by Oka, Yazaki, and collaborators (Oka and Yazaki, 1980s), and applies it specifically to the antisymmetric spin-orbit force in hyperon-nucleon systems. The SU(3) symmetry analysis generalizes the earlier observation from the Nijmegen potential models (Rijken, Nagels, and Yamamoto, 1970s-1990s) that ALS forces in YN systems do not vanish even in the flavor symmetric limit, unlike in NN systems where ALS is a charge symmetry breaking effect. The results enable future experimental tests of the short-range YN interaction through measurements of spin-orbit splittings in hypernuclei, particularly to resolve the question of whether the Λ single-particle spin-orbit force is as small as widely believed or sizable as recent experiments suggest.
