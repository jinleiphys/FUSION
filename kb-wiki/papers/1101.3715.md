# A New Equation of State for Astrophysical Simulations

> We generate a new complete equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter for a wide range of temperatures, densities, and proton fractions ready for use in astrophysical simulations of supernovae and neutron star mergers. Our previous two papers tabulated the EOS at over 180,000 grid points in the temperature range $T$ = 0 to 80 MeV, the density range $n_B$ = 10$^{-8}$ to 1.6 fm$^{-3}$, and the proton fraction range $Y_P$ = 0 to 0.56. In this paper we combine these data points using a suitable interpolation scheme to generate a single equation of state table on a finer grid. This table is thermodynamically consistent and conserves entropy during adiabatic compression tests. We present various thermodynamic quantities and the composition of matter in the new EOS, along with several comparisons with existing EOS tables. Our equation of state table is available for download.

## Key claim

The authors generate a single, thermodynamically consistent equation of state (EOS) table for nuclear matter by interpolating their previously calculated data points (over 180,000 grid points) onto a finer grid, with the caveat that the interpolation scheme does not guarantee monotonicity of entropy and internal energy unless additional smoothing steps are applied.

## Method

The authors combine a relativistic mean field (RMF) model for uniform and non-uniform matter at intermediate to high densities with a Virial expansion for low-density matter, using elastic scattering phase shifts and nuclear masses as input. They then apply a bicubic Hermite interpolation scheme to the free energy, preceded by a smoothing procedure that discards numerically noisy entropy points violating monotonicity constraints, to produce a finer EOS table with 40 points per decade in both temperature and density. The final table is constructed by integrating the smoothed entropy to obtain free energy, then using bicubic interpolation to derive pressure and entropy via thermodynamic derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: T = 0 to 80 MeV (36 points in the original grid, 110 points in the interpolated table)
- Baryon density range: n_B = 10^{-8} to 1.6 fm^{-3} (83 points in the original grid, 329 points in the interpolated table)
- Proton fraction range: Y_P = 0 to 0.56 (53 points in the original grid, 1 + 52 points in the interpolated table)
- Original calculations: 73,840 data points from Virial calculation, 17,021 from nonuniform Hartree calculation, and 90,478 from uniform matter calculations
- Overall calculations took 7,000 CPU days on Indiana University's supercomputer clusters
- Maximum neutron star gravitational mass: 2.77 solar masses with a radius of 13.3 km and central density of about 10^{15} g/cm^3
- The zero-temperature energy per baryon starts near -8.5 MeV at 10^{-8} fm^{-3}
- The proton fraction in beta equilibrium reaches a minimum value of 0.007 around 0.025 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two papers (Shen, Horowitz, Teige, 2010a and 2010b) by combining their RMF and Virial EOS calculations into a single interpolated table suitable for astrophysical simulations. The new EOS is compared with the two existing widely-used EOS tables: the Lattimer-Swesty EOS (using a compressible liquid drop model with a Skyrme force) and the H. Shen, Toki, Oyamatsu, and Sumiyoshi EOS (using Thomas-Fermi and variational approximations with an RMF model). The table enables future work to run astrophysical simulations of supernovae and neutron star mergers with different EOS tables to identify quantities sensitive to the EOS, and the authors plan to present additional EOS tables for softer interactions.
