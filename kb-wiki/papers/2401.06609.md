# Incorporating $DDπ$ effects and left-hand cuts in lattice QCD studies of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$

> We generalize the relativistic field-theoretic three-particle finite-volume scattering formalism to describe generic $DDπ$ systems in the charm $C=2$ sector. This includes the isospin-0 channel, in which the recently discovered doubly-charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ is expected to manifest as a pole in the $DD π\to DD π$ scattering amplitude. The formalism presented here can also be applied to lattice QCD settings in which the $D^*$ is bound and, in particular, remains valid below the left-hand cut in $D D^*$ scattering, thus resolving an issue in previous analyses of lattice-determined finite-volume energies.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the relativistic field-theoretic three-particle finite-volume scattering formalism to describe generic $DD\pi$ systems in the charm $C=2$ sector, including the isospin-0 channel where the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ tetraquark appears as a pole. The formalism remains valid below the left-hand cut in $DD^*$ scattering, resolving an issue in previous lattice QCD analyses that used the two-particle Luscher formalism near or on this cut.

## Method

The authors extend the relativistic field-theoretic (RFT) three-particle formalism, combining methods used for $2+1$ systems (two identical and one distinct particle) with those used for three pions of arbitrary isospin. They derive the quantization condition relating finite-volume energies to the divergence-free three-particle K-matrix $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$, and present integral equations relating this K-matrix to physical $DD\pi$ scattering amplitudes. The formalism incorporates the $D^*$ as a pole in the subthreshold $p$-wave $I=1/2$ $D\pi$ scattering amplitude, so that one-pion exchange diagrams are naturally included in the three-particle framework rather than appearing as a left-hand cut in the two-particle formalism.

## Key numbers

- The left-hand cut in $DD^*$ scattering lies at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{lhc}}} = 3966$ MeV for a lattice setup with $M_D \approx 1925$ MeV, $M_{D^*} \approx 2050$ MeV, and $M_\pi \approx 280$ MeV, only 9 MeV below the $DD^*$ threshold at 3975 MeV
- The two-pion exchange left-hand cut in the $u$-channel occurs at $E_{\mathrm{cm}} = 3937$ MeV for the same lattice parameters, 29 MeV below the one-pion left-hand cut
- The three-particle formalism extends the range of validity to $E_{\mathrm{cm}} = 2M_D + 2M_\pi$ (or $2M_{D^*}$ if lower), compared to the two-particle formalism valid only between $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{lhc}}}$ and $E_{\mathrm{inel}} = 4130$ MeV
- The quantization condition factorizes into isospin blocks of dimension 2 for $I=0$ and $I=2$, and dimension 4 for $I=1$

## Context

This work extends the RFT three-particle formalism of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) to describe $DD\pi$ systems, generalizing previous work on $2+1$ systems by Blanton et al. (2021) and on three pions of arbitrary isospin by Hansen et al. (2020). It addresses the left-hand cut problem identified in lattice studies of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ by Padmanath et al. (2022) and Du et al. (2023), where the two-particle Luscher formalism breaks down near the one-pion exchange cut. The formalism enables future lattice QCD studies of the $T_{cc}^+$ at physical quark masses, where the $D^*$ decays into $D\pi$, and also applies to $KK\pi$ and $BB\pi$ systems where similar tetraquark candidates exist.
