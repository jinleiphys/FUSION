## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moment of the rho meson is extracted from BaBar's final (not preliminary) data on $e^+e^- \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^0 \pi^0$, yielding $\mu_\rho = 2.7 \pm 0.3$ in $e/2 m_\rho$ units, which improves on a previous extraction ($2.1 \pm 0.5$) by using definite data, better-grounded parameters, and an explicit gauge-invariant description of the process.

## Method

The authors model the $\gamma^* \to 4\pi$ vertex using a vector meson dominance (VMD) framework that includes intermediate $\rho$, $\rho'(1450)$, $\omega$, $a_1$, $\sigma(600)$, and $f_0(980)$ resonances. They enforce gauge invariance by combining the $\pi$-exchange, $\rho$-exchange (which contains the $\rho\rho\gamma$ vertex), and contact-term channels, and impose Bose-Einstein symmetry and charge conjugation via pion exchange. All model parameters are fixed by fitting to a set of low-energy observables and the BaBar $\omega$ channel data, leaving only the $\rho$ magnetic dipole moment $\beta_0$ as a free parameter, which is then determined by fitting the total cross section in the $0.9$ to $1.8$ GeV energy range.

## Key numbers

* $\mu_\rho = 2.7 \pm 0.3$ in units of $e/2 m_\rho$, extracted from BaBar $e^+e^- \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^0 \pi^0$ data in the center-of-mass energy range $0.9$ to $1.8$ GeV.
* The previous extraction, using preliminary BaBar data, gave $\mu_\rho = 2.1 \pm 0.5$ in the same units.
* The $\rho$ meson coupling constants from the fit are $g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 5.95 \pm 0.08$ and $g_\rho = 4.96 \pm 0.09$.
* The $\rho'(1450)$ coupling constants are $g_{\rho'\pi\pi} = 5.8 \pm 0.44$ and $g_{\rho'} = 12.85 \pm 0.4$, with a relative phase $\theta = 0.9 \pm 0.04$ (in units of $\pi$).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Garcia Gudino et al. (2013) and David (2015), who used preliminary BaBar data to obtain a first data-driven value of the $\rho$ MDM. The present analysis uses the final BaBar data (BaBar Collaboration, 2017) and a more robust parameter determination from Avalos et al. (2022), including explicit gauge invariance. The result falls within the range predicted by various theoretical approaches (relativistic quark models, Dyson-Schwinger equations, lattice QCD), which span $\mu_\rho = [1.5, 2.7]$ in $e/2 m_\rho$ units, and provides a benchmark for future experimental and theoretical studies of vector meson electromagnetic properties.
