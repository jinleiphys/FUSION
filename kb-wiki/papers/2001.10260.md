# Flow in collisions of light nuclei

> In this talk we discuss several interesting aspects of collective flow in small systems in reference to planned future experiments. First, we bring in the novel possibility of exploring elliptic flow with heavy nuclei collisions on polarized deuteron targets, accessible in AFTER@LHC program after Long Shutdown 3. Then we pass to the Glauber model predictions for the presently considered ${}^{16}{\rm O}-{}^{16}{\rm O}$ program and RHIC and the LHC. Finally, we turn to our previous proposal concerning specific flow signatures of intrinsic correlations (such as the supposed $α$ clusterization) in the structure of light nuclei in collisions with heavy nuclei. In particular, ${}^{12}{\rm C}$--heavy nucleus ultrarelativistic reactions would provide insight into the ground-state ${}^{12}{\rm C}$ structure, complementary to the traditional nuclear structure features.

## Key claim

Collective flow in small systems can be studied via novel experimental configurations, but the observable signals depend on specific conditions: elliptic flow from polarized deuteron targets requires a one-body measurement with respect to a fixed polarization axis, while signatures of intrinsic clustering in light nuclei like 12C require event-by-event shape correlations that are only visible in collisions with heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors used Monte Carlo Glauber simulations (GLISSANDO) to model initial-state deformations in 16O-16O collisions and in 12C-heavy nucleus collisions. For the polarized deuteron case, they derived an analytic formula for the elliptic flow coefficient v2 based on the deuteron's quadrupole moment and mean square radius, combined with a hydrodynamic response coefficient. For the clustering study, they used configurations from Variational Monte Carlo simulations of 12C with realistic nuclear correlations, comparing clustered (three-alpha triangle) versus uniform nucleon distributions.

## Key numbers

- For polarized deuteron targets: the elliptic flow coefficient estimate is v2{ΦP} ≃ -k × (3Q2)/(4Z(⟨r²⟩ + (3/2)⟨b²⟩)) × (3j3² - j(j+1))/(j(2j-1)), where k ∼ 0.1 is the hydrodynamic response coefficient, and ⟨b²⟩ ∼ 1 fm² is the average impact parameter squared in inelastic NN collisions
- The proposed AFTER@LHC experiments would use a 2.76A TeV Pb beam on fixed targets, with polarized targets potentially installed during LHC Long Shutdown 3 (2023-2025)
- For 16O-16O collisions: the normalized symmetric cumulant shows similar behavior to Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe systems but shifted to lower values of wounded nucleon number NW
- For 12C-heavy nucleus collisions: the double ratio [ε3{4}/ε3{2}]/[ε2{4}/ε2{2}] shows a characteristic non-monotonic behavior for clustered 12C (solid line) versus uniform distribution (dotted line)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow in small systems, building on the shape-flow transmutation mechanism proposed by Bozek (2018) and Broniowski (2019) for polarized deuteron collisions. The 16O-16O analysis complements earlier model calculations by Sievert (2019) and Huang (2019). The clustering signature in 12C continues the authors' prior work (Broniowski 2013, Arriola 2014, Bozek 2014, Rybczynski 2017) and uses realistic nuclear correlations from Lonardoni (2017). The proposed measurements would provide independent tests of whether collective flow is generated at late stages in small systems, and could enable new studies of nuclear structure correlations complementary to traditional nuclear structure experiments.
