# Convolution approach to the piNN system

> The unitary NN-piNN model contains a serious theoretical flaw: unitarity is obtained at the price of having to use an effective piNN coupling constant that is smaller than the experimental one. This is but one aspect of a more general renormalization problem whose origin lies in the truncation of Hilbert space used to derive the equations. Here we present a new theoretical approach to the piNN problem where unitary equations are obtained without having to truncate Hilbert space. Indeed, the only approximation made is the neglect of connected three-body forces. As all possible dressings of one-particle propagators and vertices are retained in our model, we overcome the renormalization problems inherent in previous piNN theories. The key element of our derivation is the use of convolution integrals that have enabled us to sum all the possible disconnected time-ordered graphs. We also discuss how the convolution method can be extended to sum all the time orderings of a connected graph. This has enabled us to calculate the fully dressed NN one pion exchange potential. We show how such a calculation can be used to estimate the size of the connected three-body forces neglected in the new piNN equations. Early indications are that such forces may be negligible.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new theoretical approach to the piNN system that obtains unitary equations without truncating Hilbert space, overcoming the renormalization problems inherent in previous unitary NN-piNN models where the effective piNN coupling constant was smaller than the experimental one; the only approximation made is the neglect of connected three-body forces, and early indications suggest such forces may be negligible.

## Method

The authors derive piNN equations using convolution integrals to sum all possible disconnected time-ordered graphs, retaining all possible dressings of one-particle propagators and vertices. They express the Green functions for the piNN system in terms of convolution integrals of dressed single-particle propagators and subsystem t-matrices, then recast the equations into a form that exposes the NN channel explicitly. The method is extended to connected diagrams to calculate the fully dressed NN one-pion exchange potential, allowing estimation of the neglected connected three-body forces.

## Key numbers

- The effective piNN coupling constant in the unitary NN-piNN model is a factor Z^(1/2) times the physical coupling constant, where Z is typically between 0.6 and 0.8
- The half-off-shell OPE potential comparison was performed with p = 0.1 p_0 and x = -0.8, showing essential identity between the fully dressed and convolution-model results

## Context

This work extends the earlier unitary NN-piNN model of Sauer et al. (1985) and the disconnectedness analysis of Stelbovics and Stingl (SS), resolving the renormalization flaw where the effective piNN coupling constant was too small. The convolution approach enables future calculations of piNN observables (such as pp -> pi+ d and pi d scattering) without the renormalization problems that plagued previous formulations, and the method for dressing connected diagrams can be applied to improve one-boson exchange models of the NN interaction.
