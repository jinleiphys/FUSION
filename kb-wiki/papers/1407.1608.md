# ${}^{\ \ \ 6}_{־›־›} He$ in cluster effective field theory

> The hypernucleus $\nuclide[6][־›־›]{He}$ is studied as a three-body ($־›־›־±$) cluster system in cluster effective field theory at leading order. We find that the three-body contact interaction exhibits the limit cycle when the cutoff in the integral equations is sent to the asymptotic limit, and thus it should be promoted to leading order. We also derive a determination equation of the limit cycle which reproduces the numerically obtained limit cycle. We then study the correlations between the double $־›$ separation energy $B_{־›־›}^{}$ of $\nuclide[6][־›־›]{He}$ and the scattering length $a_{־›־›}^{}$ of the $S$-wave $־›־›$ scattering. The role of the scale in this approach is also discussed.

# ${}^{\ \ \ 6}_{־›־›} He$ in cluster effective field theory

## Key claim

The three-body contact interaction in the $־›־›־±$ cluster system exhibits a limit cycle when the cutoff in the integral equations is taken to the asymptotic limit, and therefore must be promoted to leading order in the cluster effective field theory expansion. The authors derive a determination equation for this limit cycle whose solution reproduces the numerically obtained cycle, and they study the correlation between the double $־›$ separation energy $B_{־›־›}$ of ${}^{6}_{־›־›}He$ and the $S$-wave $־›־›$ scattering length $a_{־›־›}$.

## Method

The hypernucleus ${}^{6}_{־›־›}He$ is treated as a three-body ($־›־›־±$) cluster system in cluster effective field theory at leading order, with the $־±$ particle field taken as an elementary field. The authors solve coupled integral equations for $S$-wave elastic $־›$-${}^{5}_{־›}He$ scattering with a sharp cutoff $־›_c$, using dressed dibaryon propagators for the $־›־›$ ($^1S_0$) channel and the $־›־±$ (${}^{5}_{־›}He$) channel. The three-body contact interaction coupling $g(־›_c)$ is studied as a function of the cutoff, and the homogeneous part of the integral equations is analyzed in the asymptotic limit to derive the determination equation for the limit cycle.

## Key numbers

- The two-$־›$ separation energy of ${}^{6}_{־›־›}He$ from the NAGARA event is $B_{־›־›} = 6.93 ֲ± 0.16$ MeV.
- The $־›$ separation energy of ${}^{5}_{־›}He$ is $B_־› ג‰ƒ 3.12$ MeV, giving a binding momentum $־³_{־›־±} ג‰ƒ 73.2$ MeV.
- The large momentum scale of the $־±$-cluster theory is $־›_H ג‰ƒ 170$ MeV, yielding an expansion parameter $Q/־›_H גˆ¼ ־³_{־›־±}/־›_H ג‰ƒ 0.43$.
- The imaginary solution of the determination equation is $s_0 = 1.0496\cdots$, and the numerically extracted value from the limit cycle curves gives $s_0 ג‰ƒ 1.05$.
- Without the three-body contact interaction, the bound state with $B_{־›־›}=6.93$ MeV appears only for cutoff $־›_c ג‰³ 300$ MeV, requiring $a_{־›־›} ג‰ˆ -3.4 ֳ— 10^3$ fm; for $a_{־›־›} = -0.6$ to $-1.8$ fm, the required cutoff is $־›_c = 570$ to $408$ MeV.
- With the three-body contact interaction renormalized at $B_{־›־›}=6.93$ MeV and $1/a_{־›־›} = -2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, the coupling values are $g(־›_c) ג‰ƒ -0.715$, $-0.447$, $-0.254$ for $־›_c = 170$, $300$, $430$ MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the cluster effective field theory approach previously applied by the same authors to ${}^{4}_{־›־›}H$ as a $־›־›d$ system (Ando, Oh, and collaborators, 2013) to the heavier hypernucleus ${}^{6}_{־›־›}He$ as a $־›־›־±$ system. The limit cycle behavior of the three-body contact interaction follows the pattern first reported by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck for neutron-deuteron scattering in pionless EFT, and the determination equation generalizes the earlier work of Danilov (1961). The results enable systematic extraction of the $־›־›$ scattering length $a_{־›־›}$ from the measured $B_{־›־›}$ and provide a framework for comparing with potential model calculations such as those in Filikhin and Gal (2002).
