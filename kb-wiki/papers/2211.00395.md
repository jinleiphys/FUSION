# Efficient few-body calculations in finite volume

> Simulating quantum systems in a finite volume is a powerful theoretical tool to extract information about them. Real-world properties of the system are encoded in how its discrete energy levels change with the size of the volume. This approach is relevant not only for nuclear physics, where lattice methods for few- and many-nucleon states complement phenomenological shell-model descriptions and ab initio calculations of atomic nuclei based on harmonic oscillator expansions, but also for other fields such as simulations of cold atomic systems. This contribution presents recent progress concerning finite-volume simulations of few-body systems. In particular, it discusses details regarding the efficient numerical implementation of separable interactions and it presents eigenvector continuation as a method for performing robust and efficient volume extrapolations.

## Key claim
The paper presents recent progress in finite-volume simulations of few-body systems, specifically detailing efficient numerical implementation of separable interactions within a discrete variable representation (DVR) and introducing finite-volume eigenvector continuation (FVEC) as a method for robust and efficient volume extrapolations. The abstract's claim of "efficient" implementation is qualified by the paper's focus on algorithmic design choices (sorted vectors, binary searches, compressed data structures) that trade memory footprint for speed, particularly beneficial for large-scale or GPU-based calculations.

## Method
The author describes a DVR based on plane-wave states for few-body calculations in periodic cubic boxes, with separable two-body interactions implemented via a mapping of basis states to integer indices and a data structure using sorted vectors and binary searches to efficiently compute matrix elements. For volume extrapolation, the paper introduces FVEC, which uses dilatation operations to map states between different box sizes and constructs a reduced-basis eigenproblem from training data at a few volumes to extrapolate energy levels to target volumes.

## Key numbers
* Neutron-neutron scattering length used to fix the separable potential: \(a_{nn} = -18.9~\mathrm{fm}\).
* Training data for FVEC extrapolation of three-neutron system: three DVR calculations with \(N=22\) at box sizes \(L = 19, 20, 21~\mathrm{fm}\).
* FVEC extrapolation shown for negative-parity \(S_z=1/2\) states of three neutrons, using a total of \(3 \times 8 = 24\) training data points from four cubic group multiplets.
* The DVR basis uses \(N\) plane-wave modes with \(N>2\) and equidistant points \(x_k \in [-L/2, L/2)\) with weights \(w_k = L/n\).

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume methodology pioneered by Luscher (1985, 1986, 1990) for extracting infinite-volume scattering information from discrete energy levels, and builds on prior DVR implementations by Klos (2018), Konig (2020), Dietz (2021), and Yapa (2022). The FVEC technique adapts eigenvector continuation, first introduced by Frame (2017) for parametric extrapolation, to the specific challenge of volume dependence in finite-volume calculations. This enables efficient study of larger few-body systems, such as searches for a tetraneutron resonance state, and complements lattice QCD approaches for hadronic systems and cold atomic gas simulations.
