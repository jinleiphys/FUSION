# Perfect QCD -- a new Universal approach to soft QCD

> The ideas presented in this proceeding aims to be a first step towards a description of hadronic collisions where all soft processes are fundamentally strongly coupled and the same Universal strongly coupled physics drives both initial and final-state interactions. As it is not currently possible to derive such a picture from first principles, instead, an attempt to generalize the perfect liquid observation to a ``perfect QCD'' guiding principle is presented, focusing on implications for particle production in small systems. The first steps towards a microscopic model is taken by arguing that ``perfect QCD'' suggests that the screening in the initial state is so large that multi-parton interactions are of little or no importance. Instead, a target and projectile remnant is coherently excited and particle production is mainly driven by radiation in a qualitative similar manner as $e^+e^- \rightarrow q\bar{q}$. Finally, some of the possible implications of this ``excited remnant model'' are presented. It is argued that the time ordering of soft and hard physics can explain the absence of jet quenching in small systems and that the coherence scale of the projectile and target provides insights into what small systems will exhibit flow.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that soft QCD processes in hadronic collisions are fundamentally strongly coupled and universal, driven by a "perfect QCD" principle that generalizes the perfect liquid observation. The author explicitly states that this is not a proven model but a first step toward a new picture, and that the goal is to demonstrate that surprising insights can be obtained from this principle rather than to present a complete, validated framework.

## Method

The author argues from the observation that the quark-gluon plasma behaves as a perfect liquid with minimal shear viscosity, and extends this to a "perfect QCD" principle requiring strong coupling and minimal entropy production. From this principle, the author deduces that initial-state screening must be so large that multi-parton interactions are negligible, and instead each nucleon remnant is coherently excited as a whole, with particle production driven by final-state radiation similar to e+e- → qqbar. The resulting "excited remnant model" is illustrated qualitatively using UA5 data on charged particle pseudorapidity distributions in pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- UA5 data at √s = 200 GeV/c in pp collisions shows dNch/dη is flat for low-multiplicity events and narrows as multiplicity increases, consistent with the excited remnant model's picture of radiation adding shorter strings
- The paper notes that e+e- collisions produce more particles on average than pp collisions, citing Back (2004) and Back (2006) for the observation that about 50% of the proton energy can escape as a color-neutral object
- The transverse momentum of radiation from excited remnants is argued to be pT < 1/R, where R is the size of the excited remnant

## Context

This work extends the perfect liquid observation from heavy-ion physics (RHIC and LHC, with references to Arsene 2004, Adcox 2004, Back 2004, Adams 2005, Aamodt 2010, ATLAS 2011, Chatrchyan 2012) to a universal principle for soft QCD. It contradicts the standard perturbative picture used in generators like Pythia (Sjostrand 2014, 2006) and the Color-Glass Condensate model (Gelis 2010), both of which treat initial interactions as multiple parton-parton scatterings. The picture is reminiscent of the BGK model (Brodsky 1977) and the Dual Parton Model (Capella 1992), but differs by excluding multi-parton interactions. The paper enables a new framework for understanding jet quenching absence in small systems and predicting which collision systems will exhibit collective flow, though it notes that existing HERA data from ZEUS (2019, 2021) and H1 do not show the expected flow signatures.
