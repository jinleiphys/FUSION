# Contrasting Features of Parton Energy Loss in Heavy-ion Collisions at RHIC and the LHC

> Energetic quarks and gluons lose energy as they traverse the hot and dense medium created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of leading particles quantifies parton energy loss in such collisions, with the particle spectrum in $p+p$ collisions as a reference. Previous $R_{AA}$ measurements at RHIC energies have revealed an approximately constant trend at high transverse momenta ($p_{T}$), implying a scenario where parton energy loss, $Δp_{T}$, scales proportionally with $p_{T}$, a feature naively expected from energy loss dynamics in elastic collisions. In this study, we investigate the LHC $R_{AA}$ measurements which exhibit a pronounced $p_{T}$ dependence of $R_{AA}$ for various particle species, and our analysis attributes this behavior to $Δp_T$ being approximately proportional to $\sqrt{p_{T}}$. These distinct features are consistent with model calculations of dominant radiative energy loss dynamics at the LHC, in contrast to the dominance of collisional energy loss at RHIC. Additionally, the linear increase of fractional energy loss with medium density at different $p_{T}$ magnitudes affirms the previous empirical observation that the magnitude of the energy loss depends mostly on the initial entropy density, with no significant path-length dependence. Implications on the dynamical scenarios of parton energy loss and future experimental investigations will also be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum dependence of parton energy loss shifts from being proportional to \(p_T\) at RHIC to being proportional to \(\sqrt{p_T}\) at the LHC, based on an empirical analysis of \(R_{AA}\) data for leading particles. This claim is conditional on interpreting \(R_{AA}\) as a horizontal shift in the \(p\)+\(p\) spectrum and on the assumption that fragmentation occurs after energy loss.

## Method

The authors fit Tsallis distributions to published \(p\)+\(p\) spectra for various particle species at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) and 5.02 TeV to extract reference spectral parameters. They then fit the corresponding \(R_{AA}\) data in Pb+Pb collisions using a model where the \(p_T\) shift \(S(p_T) = S_0 p_T^\alpha\) is applied to the \(p\)+\(p\) spectrum, with \(S_0\) and \(\alpha\) as free parameters. The extracted \(\alpha\) values are compared across particle species and collision energies, and the fractional energy loss \(\Delta p_T / p_T\) is studied as a function of initial entropy density \(\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN}{dy}\).

## Key numbers

- For charged hadrons in Au+Au at 200 GeV, the \(R_{AA}\) plateau at high \(p_T\) is consistent with \(\alpha = 1\) (proportional energy loss).
- For charged pions in Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV, the rising \(R_{AA}\) trend is consistent with \(\alpha = 0.5\) (square-root energy loss).
- Fits to charged particles in 0-5% Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV yield \(\alpha = 0.42 \pm\) (statistical uncertainty), consistent with 0.5.
- Fits to \(\pi^\pm\) in 0-5% Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV yield \(\alpha = 0.54 \pm\) (statistical uncertainty), consistent with 0.5.
- Fits to prompt \(J/\psi\) in 0-100% Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV yield \(\alpha = 0.54 \pm\) (statistical uncertainty).
- Fits to prompt \(D^0\) in 0-10% Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV yield \(\alpha = 0.27 \pm\) (statistical uncertainty), with a deviation of \(1.6\sigma\) from 0.5.
- The fractional energy loss \(\Delta p_T / p_T\) shows a linear increase with \(\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN}{dy}\) at both 2.76 and 5.02 TeV for various \(p_T\) values.

## Context

This work extends the empirical framework of Wang and Huang (2008), which found \(\Delta p_T \propto p_T\) at RHIC, to LHC energies. The results contrast with the earlier RHIC finding and support a transition from collisional to radiative energy loss dominance, consistent with theoretical predictions by Baier et al. (2001) for medium-induced gluon radiation. The analysis also confirms the earlier observation that energy loss magnitude depends primarily on initial entropy density rather than path length, as seen in Wang and Huang (2008). The findings enable future tests with heavy-quark-tagged jets at sPHENIX and LHC experiments, and raise questions about the path-length dependence of energy loss in light of observed elliptic flow values.
