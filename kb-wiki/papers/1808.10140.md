# Structure of eta' mesonic nuclei in a relativistic mean field theory

> The structure and the energy spectrum of the $Î·^{\prime}$ mesonic nuclei are investigated in a relativistic mean field theory. One expects a substantial attraction for the $Î·^{\prime}$ meson in finite nuclei due to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the nuclear medium. Such a hadronic scale interaction for the $Î·^{\prime}$ mesonic nuclei may provide modification of the nuclear structure. The relativistic mean field theory is a self-contained model for finite nuclei which provides the saturation property within the model, and is good to investigate the structure change of the nucleus induced by the $Î·^{\prime}$ meson. Using the local density approximation for the mean fields, we solve the equations of motion for the nucleons and the $Î·^{\prime}$ meson self-consistently, and obtain the nuclear density distribution and the $Î·^{\prime}$ energy spectrum for the $Î·^{\prime}$ mesonic nuclei. We take $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca for the target nuclei. We find several bound states of the $Î·^{\prime}$ meson for these nuclei thanks to the attraction for $Î·^{\prime}$ in nuclei. We also find a sufficient change of the nuclear structure especially for the $1s$ bound state of $Î·^{\prime}$. This implies that the production of the $1s$ bound state in nuclear reaction may be suppressed.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the $\eta^{\prime}$ meson forms several bound states in $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca due to an attractive scalar potential derived from partial restoration of chiral symmetry, but they also find that the nuclear structure is sufficiently modified for the $1s$ bound state that its production in nuclear reactions may be suppressed.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic mean field theory with $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and photon mean fields to describe the nucleus, and introduced an $\eta^{\prime}$ meson as a matter field coupled only to the $\sigma$ field via a coupling constant $g_{\sigma\eta^{\prime}}$. They solved the Dirac equation for nucleons and the Klein-Gordon equation for the $\eta^{\prime}$ meson self-consistently using a local density approximation, iterating until convergence, for the target nuclei $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca. The $\eta^{\prime}$-$\sigma$ coupling strength was fixed to reproduce an 80 MeV reduction of the $\eta^{\prime}$ effective mass at saturation density, based on results from the linear $\sigma$ model of Sakai (2013).

## Key numbers
- The $\eta^{\prime}$ effective mass reduction at saturation density ($\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$) is set to 80 MeV, based on the linear $\sigma$ model of Sakai (2013).
- The $\sigma$-$\eta^{\prime}$ coupling constants $g_{\sigma\eta^{\prime}}$ range from 2.13 to 2.94 across the nine parameter sets, about one third of the $\sigma N$ coupling constant.
- For normal nuclei without $\eta^{\prime}$, the binding energy per particle $E_{\rm tot}/A - m_N$ is approximately -7.7 MeV for $^{16}$O, -8.6 MeV for $^{40}$Ca, and -5.9 MeV for $^{12}$C (parameter set 2 for O and Ca, set 6 for C).
- The saturation density is $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$, the binding energy per nucleon is $B/A = -16.3$ MeV, and the symmetry energy is $A_{\rm sym} = 32.5$ MeV for all parameter sets.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of $\eta^{\prime}$ bound states by Jido (2011, 2012), which assumed a fixed nuclear density, by using a self-consistent relativistic mean field theory that allows the nuclear structure to change in the presence of the $\eta^{\prime}$ meson. It builds on the linear $\sigma$ model of Sakai (2013) for the $\eta^{\prime}$ mass reduction and on the relativistic mean field framework applied to kaonic nuclei by Gazda (2007, 2008, 2012). The results enable future experimental searches for $\eta^{\prime}$ bound states, such as the ongoing $^{12}$C$(\gamma,p)$ experiment by the LEPS2 collaboration, by predicting that the $1s$ bound state may be difficult to observe due to nuclear structure changes.
