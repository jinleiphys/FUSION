# Nuclear Reaction Rates in a Plasma

> The problem of determining the effects of the surrounding plasma on nuclear reaction rates in stars is formulated ab initio, using the techniques of quantum statistical mechanics. We derive a result that expresses the complete effects of Coulomb barrier penetration and of the influence of the surrounding plasma in terms of matrix elements of well defined operators. We find that possible "dynamical screening" effects that have been discussed in the literature are absent. The form of our results suggests that an approach that relies on numerical calculations of the correlation functions in a classical Coulomb gas, followed by construction of an effective two body potential and a quantum barrier penetration calculation, will miss physics that is as important as the physics that it includes.

# Nuclear Reaction Rates in a Plasma

## Key claim
The authors derive a first-principles quantum statistical mechanical formulation of nuclear reaction rates in a plasma and find that "dynamical screening" effects discussed in the literature are absent. The corrections to the Salpeter screening result do not separate into a product of initial-state and final-state effects, and approaches that combine classical correlation functions with quantum barrier penetration calculations will miss physics as important as what they include.

## Method
The authors formulate the problem using quantum statistical mechanics, treating the Coulomb force between the fusing particles exactly while treating the coupling between the fusing particles and the plasma particles perturbatively, with the plasma itself treated in the one-loop approximation. They derive a general expression for the reaction rate in terms of matrix elements of well-defined operators, separating the plasma interactions with the fusing particles from their internal Coulomb interactions. The approach exploits the dilute nature of the reacting particles (allowing Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics) while making no approximation for the remainder of the plasma, including electrons which may require Fermi statistics.

## Key numbers
- The Salpeter correction factor for reaction rates is exp{β e₁ e₂ κ_D}, where κ_D is the Debye wave number and e₁, e₂ are the charges of the fusing nuclei.
- Under solar conditions, screening effects give about a 5% increase in the rate of proton-proton fusion and a 20% increase in the rate for the reaction p + ⁷Be → γ + ⁸B.
- The correction to the Maxwell-Boltzmann limit for the reacting particles at the solar core is roughly of order 10⁻⁶.
- A bound is placed on remaining corrections at the e⁴ level, limiting their contributions to around the 0.1% level under solar conditions.

## Context
This work extends the foundational results of Gamow (1928) on Coulomb barrier penetration and Salpeter (1954) on Debye screening effects in stellar plasmas. The authors find that the leading correction terms from their complete perturbative approach have a nature essentially different from those produced by the "basically classical" approach used by Salpeter and Van Horne (1969), Dewitt, Graboske and Cooper (1973), Graboske et al. (1973), and Jancovici (1977), which translates classical correlation functions into modifications of the potential between fusing particles. The results conflict with the dynamical screening corrections reported by Carrero, Schäfer, and Koonin (1988), and the authors show in an appendix how such terms arise in a real-time treatment and what terms cancel them. The paper enables a systematic perturbative framework for calculating plasma corrections to nuclear reaction rates without dividing the problem into classical and quantum parts.
