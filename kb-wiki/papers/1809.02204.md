# Splitting of single-particle levels in clusters potentials

> In analogy with the Nilsson model, we calculate the splitting of spherical single-particle levels in a deformed field, but for cluster potentials. We study applications to alpha-cluster nuclei with two, three and four alpha particles, in which the deformation corresponds to the relative distance between the alpha particles. The splitting of the single-particle levels is studied for the cases of a dumbbell, equilateral triangle and a regular tetrahedron. The observed patterns may be used to gain insight into how the single-particle levels evolve with deformation.

# Splitting of single-particle levels in clusters potentials

## Key claim

In analogy with the Nilsson model for deformed quadrupole fields, the splitting of spherical single-particle levels is calculated for cluster potentials where the deformation corresponds to the relative distance between alpha particles; the observed patterns may be used to gain insight into how single-particle levels evolve with deformation, but the study is limited to alpha-cluster nuclei with two, three, and four alpha particles in specific geometric configurations (dumbbell, equilateral triangle, regular tetrahedron).

## Method

The single-particle Schrödinger equation was solved by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian (kinetic term plus central potential, spin-orbit interaction, and Coulomb potential) in a harmonic oscillator basis. The central potential was obtained by convolving a Gaussian alpha-particle density distribution with a Volkov potential, with the deformation parameter β representing the relative distance of alpha particles to the center of mass. Correlation diagrams were computed as a function of β for each geometric configuration, and overlaps between intrinsic states at different deformations were calculated to study mixing between levels.

## Key numbers

- Central potential strength: V₀ = 32 MeV
- Gaussian width parameter: α = 0.0511 fm⁻²
- Spin-orbit strength for dumbbell: V₀,ₛₒ = 27.5 MeV fm²
- Spin-orbit strength for triangle: V₀,ₛₒ = 17 MeV fm²
- Spin-orbit strength for tetrahedron: V₀,ₛₒ = 12.1 MeV fm²
- Wave function crossings and onset of asymptotic degeneracy occur around β = 3.5 to 5 fm, with convergence toward separated-alpha degeneracy after 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the Cluster Shell Model of Della Rocca et al. (2018) and the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) of Bijker (2000, 2014) by calculating single-particle level splitting in cluster potentials, analogous to the Nilsson model for quadrupole-deformed nuclei (Casten, 2000; Bohr, 1998). The results enable extension of the ACM to odd-cluster nuclei such as ⁹Be, ⁹B, ¹³C, ¹³N, ¹⁷O, and ¹⁷F via the Algebraic Cluster-Fermion Model (ACFM) (Santana-Valdés, 2018).
