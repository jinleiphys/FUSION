# Factorization and Resummation for Dijet Invariant Mass Spectra

> Multijet cross sections at the LHC and Tevatron are sensitive to several distinct kinematic energy scales. When measuring the dijet invariant mass m_jj between two signal jets produced in association with other jets or weak bosons, m_jj will typically be much smaller than the total partonic center-of-mass energy Q, but larger than the individual jet masses m, such that there can be a hierarchy of scales m << m_jj << Q. This situation arises in many new-physics analyses at the LHC, where the invariant mass between jets is used to gain access to the masses of new-physics particles in a decay chain. At present, the logarithms arising from such a hierarchy of kinematic scales can only be summed at the leading-logarithmic level provided by parton-shower programs. We construct an effective field theory, SCET+, which is an extension of soft-collinear effective theory that applies to this situation of hierarchical jets. It allows for a rigorous separation of different scales in a multiscale soft function and for a systematic resummation of logarithms of both m_jj/Q and m/Q. As an explicit example, we consider the invariant mass spectrum of the two closest jets in e+e- -> 3 jets. We also give the generalization to pp -> N jets plus leptons relevant for the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new effective field theory, SCET+, that extends soft-collinear effective theory to handle the kinematic situation where the dijet invariant mass between two close jets is parametrically smaller than the total partonic center-of-mass energy but larger than the individual jet masses (m << m_jj << Q), enabling systematic resummation of logarithms of both m_jj/Q and m/Q that previously could only be summed at leading-logarithmic level by parton-shower programs.

## Method

The authors construct SCET+ by introducing a new "collinear-soft" (csoft) mode with momentum scaling p_csoft ~ Q(lambda^2, eta^2, eta*lambda) where lambda = m/Q and eta = m/sqrt(t), which sits between the usual collinear and ultrasoft modes in virtuality. They perform a multi-step matching procedure: first matching QCD onto SCET at the hard scale Q, then matching SCET onto SCET+ at the intermediate scale sqrt(t), and finally integrating out collinear modes at the jet scale m and csoft modes at the scale m^2/sqrt(t). The factorization theorem for e+e- -> 3 jets is derived as an explicit example, with all ingredients computed at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The hierarchy of scales considered is m << m_jj << Q, where m is the individual jet mass, m_jj is the dijet invariant mass between two close jets, and Q is the total partonic center-of-mass energy
- The csoft modes have virtuality p_csoft^2 ~ m^4/t, which is the intermediate soft scale between the jet scale m^2 and the ultrasoft scale m^4/Q^2
- The expansion parameter in both matching steps is lambda = m/Q for the first step and lambda = m/sqrt(t) for the second step
- The CDF W+2 jets measurement shows an excess around m_jj ~ 150 GeV, with subleading jet p_T ~ 40 GeV and total Wjj system invariant mass Q ~ 300 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SCET framework for exclusive jet production (Stewart 2009, Ellis 2009, Ellis 2010, Stewart 2010, Jouttenus 2011) to handle the case where two jets become close, introducing additional kinematic scales. The need to "refactorize" the soft function into more fundamental pieces was first pointed out by Ellis (2010). The two-step matching procedure for the hard coefficient was previously addressed (Bauer 2006, Bauer 2006, Baumgart 2010) but without fully considering the soft sector below sqrt(t). This work enables systematic resummation of logarithms in the soft sector for the first time, which is essential for precise theoretical predictions of dijet invariant mass spectra at the LHC and Tevatron.
