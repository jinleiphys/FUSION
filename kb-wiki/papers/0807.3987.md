# Longitudinal broadening of near side jets due to parton cascade

> Longitudinal broadening along $Δη$ direction on near side in two-dimensional ($Δϕ\times Δη$) di-hadron correlation distribution has been studied for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, within a dynamical multi-phase transport model. It was found that the longitudinal broadening is generated by a longitudinal flow induced by strong parton cascade in central Au+Au collisions, in comparison with p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. The longitudinal broadening may shed light on the information about strongly interacting partonic matter at RHIC.

## Key claim

Longitudinal broadening of the near-side jet in the Δη direction of two-dimensional di-hadron correlations in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV is generated by longitudinal flow induced by strong parton cascade, as shown in a dynamical multi-phase transport model (AMPT) when compared to p+p collisions at the same energy.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT model to simulate central Au+Au collisions (0–10% centrality) at √sNN = 200 GeV, with a partonic cross section of 10 mb, and the default AMPT model for p+p collisions at the same energy. They varied the evolution-time of partonic matter (from 0.5 to 4.0 fm/c) after which partonic interactions ceased and remaining partons coalesced into hadrons, then constructed two-dimensional (Δφ × Δη) di-hadron correlation functions using a mixing-event technique with trigger pT between 2.5 and 6 GeV/c and associated pT between 1.5 and 2.5 GeV/c, within |η| < 1.0.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of root-mean-square widths rms(Δη)/rms(Δφ) ≈ 2.6 in central Au+Au collisions, compared to ≈ 1.0 in p+p collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- Slope of the linear increase of associated near-side yield per trigger particle with partonic evolution-time: 0.085 ± 0.002 fm⁻¹.
- Slope of the linear increase of the rapidity limit ηe0 (amplitude of longitudinal flow) with evolution-time: 0.093 ± 0.003 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical efforts to explain the "ridge" correlation observed at RHIC, including the recombination model of Chiu and Hwa (2005), the longitudinal flow model of Armesto et al. (2004) and Satarov et al. (2005), and the collisional energy loss calculation for heavy quarks by Romatschke (2007). The study provides dynamical evidence from a transport model that strong parton cascade generates longitudinal flow, which in turn produces the observed longitudinal broadening; it also notes consistency with Satarov et al. (2005) that the width scales with the logarithmic formation time of the near-side correlation. The authors indicate that three-particle Δη correlation studies are in progress to further discriminate among proposed mechanisms.
