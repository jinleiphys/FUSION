# Shape and multiple shape coexistence of nuclei within covariant density functional theory

> Shape and multiple shape coexistence of nuclei are investigated throughout the nuclear chart by calculating the low-lying spectra and the quadrupole shape invariants for even-even nuclei with $10\leq Z\leq 104$ from the proton drip line to the neutron one within a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on the covariant density functional PC-PK1. The quadrupole shape invariants are implemented to characterize the quadrupole deformations of low-lying $0^+$ states and predict nuclear mass regions of shape and multiple shape coexistence. The predicted low-lying spectra and the shape or multi-shape coexisting nuclei are overall in good agreement with the available experimental results. In addition, the present work predicts a wealth of nuclei with shape or multiple shape coexistence in the neutron-rich regions. The connection between the strong $E0$ transition strength and the occurrence of shape coexistence is analyzed systemically. It is found that nuclei with pronounced shape coexistence generally have strong $E0$ transition strengths, while the reverse may not be true. The present results can serve as useful guidelines for experimental searches and theoretical studies of shape and multiple shape coexistence, especially in neutron-rich regions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape and multiple shape coexistence can be systematically predicted across the nuclear chart using quadrupole shape invariants from a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on the covariant density functional PC-PK1, but the predictions are limited to even-even nuclei and the model only treats collective degrees of freedom, potentially missing non-collective contributions.

## Method

Deformation-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations were performed for even-even nuclei with atomic numbers 10 to 104 from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line using the PC-PK1 covariant density functional and a finite-range separable pairing force with strength G=728 MeV fm^3. The resulting potential energy surfaces and collective parameters were used to build a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian, which was solved to obtain excitation energies, electromagnetic transition strengths, and quadrupole shape invariants for the four lowest 0+ states. Quadrupole shape invariants were calculated using the Kumar-Cline sum rules with the 30 lowest 2+ states included to ensure convergence.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength adopted was G=728 MeV fm^3.
- For excitation energies of 2_1+ states, the average logarithmic ratio of theory to experiment was R=0.33 with dispersion sigma=0.43, indicating calculated results are about 40% higher than data on average.
- The criterion for shape coexistence between 0_1+ and 0_2+ states was set as d_12 > 0.06, where d_ij is the relative distance in the deformation plane.
- For multiple shape coexistence, the criterion was d_ijk = min{d_ij, d_ik, d_jk} > 0.06 for the three lowest 0+ states.
- Nuclei with pronounced shape coexistence (d_12 > 0.1) generally have E0 transition strengths ranging from 20 to 200 milliunits.
- The triaxial RHB equation was solved in three-dimensional harmonic oscillator bases including 12, 14, and 16 major shells for nuclei with Z<20, 20≤Z<82, and 82≤Z≤104, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence using the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with the PC-PK1 functional by Quan (2017) to a global survey across the entire nuclear chart, whereas earlier work focused on specific regions. The results are compared with experimental data compiled by Garrett (2022) and with beyond-mean-field calculations using the Gogny D1S functional by Delaroche (2010). The predicted mass regions of multiple shape coexistence, particularly in neutron-rich areas, provide guidance for future experiments at rare isotope beam facilities and for theoretical studies extending the approach to odd nuclei.
