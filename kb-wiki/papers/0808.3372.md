# Beyond the rainbow: effects from pion back-coupling

> We investigate hadronic unquenching effects in light quarks and mesons. To this end we take into account the back-coupling of the pion onto the quark propagator within the non-perturbative continuum framework of Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE). We improve on a previous approach by explicitly solving both the coupled system of DSEs and BSEs in the complex plane and the normalisation problem for Bethe-Salpeter kernels depending on the total momentum of the meson. As a result of our study we find considerable unquenching effects in the spectrum of light pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector mesons.

## Key claim

The authors find that including pion back-coupling effects in the quark-gluon interaction produces considerable unquenching effects in the spectrum of light pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons, but the sign and magnitude of these effects depend on the specific model interaction used for the Yang-Mills part, with the Maris-Tandy model showing attraction for the pion mass and the authors' soft-divergence model showing repulsion.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDE) for the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) for meson bound states in the complex plane, incorporating both a Yang-Mills part (either the Maris-Tandy model or a soft-divergence model fitted to quenched lattice results) and a hadronic part approximated by one-pion exchange. The pion back-coupling is implemented using the leading chiral-limit approximation for the pion Bethe-Salpeter vertex, and the normalisation condition for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes is solved including the double-integral term from the momentum-dependent kernel.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 138 MeV (fitted) for both models with pion back-coupling included; without back-coupling, Maris-Tandy gives 140 MeV and the soft-divergence model gives 125 MeV
- Pion decay constant: 93.2 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 93.8 MeV (soft-divergence model) with pion back-coupling; without back-coupling, 104 MeV and 102 MeV respectively
- Rho meson mass: 720 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 703 MeV (soft-divergence model) with pion back-coupling, compared to 821 MeV and 795 MeV without
- Axial-vector a1 mass: 913 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 873 MeV (soft-divergence model) with pion back-coupling, compared to 979 MeV and 941 MeV without; experimental value is 1230 MeV
- Axial-vector b1 mass: 750 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 806 MeV (soft-divergence model) with pion back-coupling, compared to 820 MeV and 879 MeV without; experimental value is 1230 MeV
- Scalar sigma mass: 598 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 485 MeV (soft-divergence model) with pion back-coupling, compared to 746 MeV and 638 MeV without; experimental range is 400-1200 MeV
- Eta mass: 497 MeV and eta-prime mass: 963 MeV (soft-divergence model only, with pion back-coupling)
- Maris-Tandy model parameters: omega = 0.37 GeV, D = 1.45 GeV^2, quark mass = 3.7 MeV at mu = 19 GeV
- Soft-divergence model parameters: d1 = 1.45 GeV^2, d2 = 0.1 GeV^2, d3 = 3.95 GeV^2, quark mass = 2.6 MeV at mu^2 = 170 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder truncation of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations, which had been successful for pseudoscalar and vector mesons (Maris 1997, Maris 1999) but gave masses of 800-900 MeV for scalar mesons and failed for axial-vector mesons. The authors improve on their own previous approach (Fischer 2007) by solving the equations in the complex plane rather than using the real-value approximation, and by solving the normalisation problem for Bethe-Salpeter kernels that depend on the total momentum of the meson. This work is complementary to investigations of hadronic intermediate states in bound-state calculations (Watson 2004) and studies of unquenching effects in the gluon polarization (Fischer 2003, Fischer 2005). The results enable further exploration of whether scalar and axial-vector mesons are simple quark-antiquark bound states or more complicated configurations such as tetraquarks, multi-meson molecules, or hybrids.
