# Efimov physics with $1/2$ spin-isospin fermions

> The structure of few-fermion systems having $1/2$ spin-isospin symmetry is studied using potential models. The strength and range of the two-body potentials are fixed to describe low energy observables in the angular momentum $L=0$ state and spin $S=0,1$ channels of the two-body system. Successively the strength of the potentials are varied in order to explore energy regions in which the two-body scattering lengths are close to the unitary limit. This study is motivated by the fact that in the nuclear system the singlet and triplet scattering lengths are both large with respect to the range of the interaction. Accordingly we expect evidence of universal behavior in the three- and four-nucleon systems that can be observed from the study of correlations between observables. In particular we concentrate in the behavior of the first excited state of the three-nucleon system as the system moves away from the unitary limit. We also analyze the dependence on the range of the three-body force of some low-energy observables in the three- and four-nucleon systems.

# Efimov physics with 1/2 spin-isospin fermions

## Key claim

The three- and four-nucleon systems lie inside the Efimov window where universal behavior is expected, but the first excited state of the three-fermion system disappears into the two-body continuum at the physical point (ratio a0/a1 = -4.31), unlike the bosonic case where both ground and first excited states remain bound; this disappearance depends on the ratio of singlet to triplet scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors constructed spin-dependent Gaussian two-body potentials with ranges and strengths fixed to reproduce experimental low-energy observables (scattering lengths, effective ranges, deuteron binding energy) in the L=0 singlet (S=0) and triplet (S=1) channels. They then varied the potential strengths along planes of constant ratio a0/a1 to explore the unitary limit, solving the Schrödinger equation for the three-fermion Jπ=1/2+ state. A hypercentral Gaussian three-body force was introduced to study correlations between the triton binding energy, doublet n-d scattering length, and 4He ground state energy.

## Key numbers

- Triplet scattering length a1 = 5.419 ± 0.007 fm (experimental)
- Singlet scattering length a0 = -23.740 ± 0.020 fm (experimental)
- Effective ranges: r_eff^0 = 2.77 ± 0.05 fm, r_eff^1 = 1.753 ± 0.008 fm
- Deuteron binding energy: -2.225 MeV
- Ground state three-fermion energy at unitary limit: E3^0 = 3.62 MeV (binding momentum κ0)
- Ratio of consecutive Efimov states in zero-range limit: E3^n/E3^{n+1} ≈ 515.03 (s0 ≈ 1.00624)
- Ratio of ground to first excited state at unitary limit using Gaussian potential: ≈ 528
- First excited state energy at unitary limit: E3^1 = 0.00685 MeV (for r0=r1=1.65 fm)
- Ground state energies at physical point for different ratios: -21.05 MeV (a0/a1=1), -15.12 MeV (a0/a1=2), -11.44 MeV (a0/a1=∞), -10.22 MeV (a0/a1=-4.31)
- Experimental triton binding energy: -8.48 MeV
- Experimental doublet n-d scattering length: 2a_nd ≈ 0.65 fm
- Experimental 4He ground state energy: -28.3 MeV
- Optimum four-body ground state energy with short-range three-body force: -28.4 MeV (using different ranges r0=1.82 fm, r1=1.57 fm)
- Shift parameter Γ0^0 varies from 0.87 (a0/a1=1) to -0.23 (a0/a1=-4.31)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Efimov physics in bosonic systems using Gaussian potentials (Gattobigio 2014, Kievsky 2013) to fermions with 1/2 spin-isospin symmetry, motivated by the fact that nuclear singlet and triplet scattering lengths are both large compared to the interaction range. The study builds on Efimov's zero-range theory (Efimov 1970) and the modified radial law incorporating finite-range corrections (Kievsky 2015, Ji 2015). The analysis of the three-body force dependence on range connects to the Phillips line (Phillips 1968) and to modern chiral effective field theory approaches where three-body contact terms at N2LO are tuned to describe triton binding and doublet scattering length (Kievsky 2010). The results enable a potential model that simultaneously describes 3H and 4He binding energies and the doublet n-d scattering length, which many realistic NN potentials with three-nucleon interactions cannot achieve (Kievsky 2008).
