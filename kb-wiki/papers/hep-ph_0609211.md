# Charge Fluctuations along the QCD phase boundary

> We discuss the properties of the net--quark and isovector fluctuations along the chiral phase transition line in the plane spanned by temperature and baryon chemical potential. Our results are obtained in terms of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model within the mean-field approximation. The model is formulated at the finite temperature and for non-vanishing net--quark and the isospin chemical potentials. The fermion interactions are controlled by the strength of the scalar and vector couplings in the iso-scalar and iso-vector channels of constituent quarks. We explore properties and differences in the behavior of the net--quark number and isovector susceptibilities for different values of thermal parameters near the phase transition. We argue that any non-monotonic behavior of the net--quark number susceptibility along the phase transition boundary is an excellent probe of the existence and the position of the second order endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The net-quark number susceptibility \(\chi_q\) exhibits a non-monotonic behavior along the QCD phase boundary line only when a tricritical point (TCP) exists in the phase diagram; a monotonic \(\chi_q\) along the boundary would indicate the absence of such a critical endpoint. This non-monotonicity is seen only when approaching the phase boundary from the chirally broken phase, not from the symmetric phase.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation at finite temperature and non-zero net-quark and isospin chemical potentials. They solved the gap equations for the dynamical quark mass and shifted chemical potentials, then computed the net-quark number susceptibility \(\chi_q\) and isovector susceptibility \(\chi_I\) as second derivatives of the thermodynamic potential. The susceptibilities were evaluated along the chiral phase transition line for different values of the vector coupling constant \(G_V^{(S)}\) in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) for \(G_V^{(S)} = 0\) is located at \((T, \mu_q) = (65, 275)\) MeV; for \(G_V^{(S)} = 0.3\,G_S\) it shifts to \((T, \mu_q) = (42, 305)\) MeV.
- The transition temperature at \(\mu_q = \mu_I = 0\) is \(T_0 = 177\) MeV in the chiral limit.
- The window of sensitivity to the TCP along the phase boundary is \(\Delta T_c \simeq 30\) MeV above the TCP and \(\Delta \mu_q^c \simeq 10\) MeV around the TCP.
- This \(\Delta T_c \simeq 30\) MeV window corresponds to a center-of-mass energy step of \(\Delta \sqrt{s} \sim 1\) A\(\cdot\)GeV in heavy-ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results from Fodor et al. (2004) and Ejiri et al. (2005) on quark susceptibilities, which showed a cusp-like structure in \(\chi_q\) at the transition temperature but could not distinguish between critical and regular contributions. The authors use the NJL model to demonstrate that a non-monotonic \(\chi_q\) along the phase boundary is a specific signature of a tricritical point, unlike the monotonic behavior seen in the isovector susceptibility. This enables experimental searches for the QCD critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions by mapping the energy dependence of net-proton fluctuations.
