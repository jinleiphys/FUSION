# First lattice study of low-energy charmonium-hadron interaction

> We study the scattering lengths of charmonia (J/psi and eta_c) with light hadrons (pi, rho and N) by the quenched lattice QCD simulations on 24x24x24x48, 32x32x32x48 and 48x48x48x48 lattices with the lattice spacing a = 0.068 fm. The scattering length is extracted by using the Luscher's phase-shift formula together with the measurement of the energy shift Delta E of two hadrons on the lattice. We find that there exist attractive interactions in all channels, J/psi(eta_c)-pi, J/psi(eta_c)-rho and J/psi(eta_c)-N: The s-wave J/psi-pi (eta_c-pi) scattering length is determined as 0.0119+-0.0039 fm (0.0113+-0.0035 fm) and the corresponding elastic cross section at the threshold becomes 0.018+0.013-0.010 mb (0.016+0.011-0.008 mb). Also, the J/psi-N (eta_c-N) spin-averaged scattering length is 0.71+-0.48 fm (0.70+-0.66 fm), which is at least an order of magnitude larger than the charmonium-pion scattering length. The volume dependence of the energy shifts is also investigated to check the expected 1/L^3 behavior of Delta E at a large spatial size L.

# First lattice study of low-energy charmonium-hadron interaction

## Key claim

The authors find attractive interactions in all studied charmonium-light hadron channels (J/ψ-π, J/ψ-ρ, J/ψ-N, ηc-π, ηc-ρ, ηc-N) using quenched lattice QCD, but caution that results for J/ψ-ρ and J/ψ-N channels have large statistical errors and that the L=24 fm lattice (La ≈ 1.6 fm) is too small to yield reliable scattering lengths for these channels, so only L=32 and L=48 data are used for those systems.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations at β=6.2 (a ≈ 0.068 fm) on three lattice volumes (24³×48, 32³×48, 48³×48) with three light quark masses corresponding to Mπ/Mρ = 0.68, 0.83, 0.90 and a fixed charm quark mass (κc=0.1360). They computed four-point correlation functions for two-hadron systems in a finite box, extracted the energy shift ΔE from the ratio of four-point to two-point functions, and applied Lüscher's phase-shift formula to obtain s-wave scattering lengths, using both the full phase-shift formula (PSF) and the leading large-L expansion (LLE) for comparison.

## Key numbers

- s-wave J/ψ-π scattering length: 0.0119 ± 0.0039 fm
- s-wave ηc-π scattering length: 0.0113 ± 0.0035 fm
- Elastic cross section at threshold for J/ψ-π: 0.018 +0.013 -0.010 mb
- Elastic cross section at threshold for ηc-π: 0.016 +0.011 -0.008 mb
- J/ψ-N spin-averaged scattering length: 0.71 ± 0.48 fm (PSF method)
- ηc-N spin-averaged scattering length: 0.70 ± 0.66 fm (PSF method)
- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.068 fm (a⁻¹ ≈ 2.9 GeV)
- Three lattice sizes: La ≈ 1.6 fm (L=24), 2.2 fm (L=32), 3.2 fm (L=48)
- Three light quark masses: Mπ ≈ 0.6 GeV (κ=0.1520), 0.9 GeV (κ=0.1506), 1.2 GeV (κ=0.1489)

## Context

This work provides the first lattice QCD determination of charmonium-light hadron scattering lengths, extending prior theoretical estimates from QCD sum rules by Hayashigaki (2000) (a₀ ≈ 0.1 fm for J/ψ-N), gluonic van der Waals interaction by Brodsky and Miller (1997) (a₀ ≈ 0.25 fm), and QCD multipole expansion by Sibirtsev and Voloshin (2005) (a₀ ≥ 0.37 fm). The results enable future studies of charmonium-nucleus bound states, which were previously explored phenomenologically by Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Téramond (1990) and Wasson (1991), and provide a lattice benchmark for the soft-pion theorem prediction that the J/ψ-π interaction vanishes in the chiral limit.
