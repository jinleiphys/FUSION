# Heavy quarkonium dissociation in the finite space of heavy-ion collisions

> The dissociation of heavy quarkonia in the constrained space is calculated at leading order compared with that in infinitely large medium. To deal with the summation of the discrete spectrum, a modified Euler-Maclaurin formula is developed as our numerical algorithm. We find that with the constraint in space, the dissociation of quarkonia at early time becomes negligible.

## Key claim

The dissociation of heavy quarkonia in the finite spatial volume of a heavy-ion collision fireball is significantly weaker than in an infinitely large medium at the same temperature, and at early times the dissociation becomes negligible even if the medium temperature is high. This conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the fireball is infinitely large in the transverse directions and that the longitudinal size equals \(L = 2t\) at time \(t\) after the collision.

## Method

The authors solve a rate equation for heavy quarkonium dissociation via gluon dissociation (\(g + H \to Q + \bar{Q}\)) at leading order, using Bjorken's hydrodynamics to model the fireball expansion. The gluon spectrum in the finite longitudinal space is obtained by summing over discrete momentum modes \(k_z = n\pi/L\) rather than integrating over a continuum. To evaluate this sum numerically, the authors develop a modified Euler-Maclaurin formula (a 2-2-1 cut) that handles the discrete summation accurately. The loss term \(\alpha_H\) is then computed for \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon(1s)\) as a function of longitudinal size \(L\), time \(t\), and transverse momentum \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

- The gluon number density in a finite fireball of length \(L = 1\) fm at \(T = 0.35\) GeV is never larger than that in an infinite fireball; gluons with energy less than the ground state energy \(\omega_0 = \pi/L\) vanish.
- In the static frame, the ratio \(f_r = f_{\omega}^{L} / f_{\omega}^{\infty}\) satisfies \((1 - \omega_0/\omega) \le f_r \le 1\) for \(\omega \ge \omega_0\), with equality only at \(\omega = n\omega_0\).
- In the fast moving frame, the ratio is \(f_r = (1/L) \sqrt{2\pi B / \omega} \, e^{B\omega(\sqrt{1-(\omega_0/\omega)^2} - 1)} \ll 1\).
- The loss term \(\alpha_{J/\psi}\) at fixed \(T = 0.35\) GeV shows finite volume effects are remarkable when \(L \lesssim \pi / \epsilon_{J/\psi}\).
- With Bjorken hydrodynamics (\(T^* = 0.35\) GeV at \(t^* = 0.6\) fm/\(c\)), the loss term \(\alpha_{J/\psi}\) is negligible at small \(t\) even if formation times are early.
- The effective initial time \(t_0\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon(1s)\) at \(p_T = 0\) satisfies \(t_{0_{J/\psi}} / t_{0_{\Upsilon(1s)}} \approx 1.65\), close to the ratio of threshold energies \(\epsilon_{\Upsilon(1s)} / \epsilon_{J/\psi} = 1.67\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of heavy quarkonium suppression in an infinitely large QGP medium (Matsui and Satz 1986; Blaizot and Ollitrault 1996) by incorporating the finite longitudinal size of the fireball produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with prior models that assume an infinite medium and thus overestimate early-time dissociation. The results enable a more realistic estimate of the effective initial time for suppression, which can be used as an additional cut beyond the usual formation times of the QGP and quarkonia in phenomenological studies of \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) suppression in experiments such as those at RHIC and LHC.
