# The Polyakov loop correlator at NNLO and singlet and octet correlators

> We present the complete next-to-next-to-leading-order calculation of the correlation function of two Polyakov loops for temperatures smaller than the inverse distance between the loops and larger than the Coulomb potential. We discuss the relationship of this correlator with the singlet and octet potentials which we obtain in an Effective Field Theory framework based on finite-temperature potential Non-Relativistic QCD, showing that the Polyakov loop correlator can be re-expressed, at the leading order in a multipole expansion, as a sum of singlet and octet contributions. We also revisit the calculation of the expectation value of the Polyakov loop at next-to-next-to-leading order.

# The Polyakov loop correlator at NNLO and singlet and octet correlators

## Key claim
The paper presents the complete next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO, order g^6) calculation of the Polyakov loop correlator, but only under the specific hierarchy 1/r >> T >> m_D >> g^2/r, meaning the inverse distance between the loops is much larger than the temperature, which is much larger than the Debye mass, which is much larger than the Coulomb scale.

## Method
The calculation is performed in static gauge using dimensional regularization, separating momentum regions by appropriate expansions and resumming the Debye mass in longitudinal propagators when the loop momentum is of that order. The hierarchy 1/r >> T >> m_D >> g^2/r is implemented at the Lagrangian level by integrating out scales sequentially from static QCD, first the inverse distance 1/r to obtain zero-temperature pNRQCD, then the thermal scales T and m_D. The Polyakov loop correlator is then re-expressed at leading order in the multipole expansion as a sum of singlet and octet correlators, with the singlet and octet free energies computed from loop diagrams in pNRQCD.

## Key numbers
- Polyakov loop at NNLO (order g^4): ⟨L_R⟩ = 1 + (C_R α_s/2)(m_D/T) + (C_R α_s^2/2)[C_A(ln(m_D^2/T^2) + 1/2) - n_f ln 2] + O(g^5)
- Debye mass: m_D^2 = g^2 T^2/3 (N_c + n_f/2)
- Polyakov loop correlator C_PL(r,T) leading term: (N_c^2 - 1)/(8 N_c^2) × [α_s(1/r)^2/(rT)^2] at order g^4
- Correlator includes terms at order g^5: -2 α_s^2/(rT) × (m_D/T)
- Correlator includes terms at order g^6: α_s^3/(rT)^3 × (N_c^2 - 2)/(6 N_c) plus logarithmic and constant terms
- The calculation stops at order g^6(rT)^0, omitting powers of g greater than 6 and powers of rT greater than zero at order g^6

## Context
This work extends the leading-order results of McLerran (1981) and Gross (1980) and complements the order g^6 results of Nadkarni (1986), which were valid for r m_D ~ 1 rather than the short-distance regime rT << 1 considered here. The Polyakov loop result differs from the long-time accepted result of Gava and Jengo (1981) in the finite terms at order g^4, agreeing instead with a recent determination by Burnier (2009). The EFT framework based on finite-temperature pNRQCD builds on the work of Brambilla (2008, 2010) and Escobedo (2008), and the singlet and octet free energies obtained here do not fully agree with the real parts of the real-time potentials computed in those works, due to different boundary conditions (cyclic imaginary time versus large real time). This enables future comparisons with lattice measurements and provides input for heavy quarkonium phenomenology in the quark-gluon plasma through potential models.
