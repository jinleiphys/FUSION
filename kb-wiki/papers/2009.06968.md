# Diquark Induced Short-Range Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations \& the EMC Effect

> Diquark formation across a short-range nucleon-nucleon pair is proposed as the underlying QCD physics of short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei. SRC pairs have been proposed as the cause of distorted quark behavior in nuclei; experimentally observed quark momentum distribution distortions termed the EMC effect. The strong spatial overlap of SRC pairs brings nucleon constituents within range of inter-nucleon QCD potentials and any bonds formed - such as the diquark bond - affects their distributions. In this SRC model, diquarks form in the $\rm 3_C \otimes 3_C \rightarrow \bar{3}_C$ channel of $\rm SU(3)_C$ acting on valence quarks from highly overlapping nucleon wavefunctions. The most energetically favorable diquark is a valence $u$ quark from one nucleon with a valence $d$ quark from the other in a spin-0 state bound together via continual single gluon exchange and an attractive quantum chromodynamics short-range potential. Formation of a new scalar isospin-singlet $[ud]$ diquark across a NN pair is proposed as the primary QCD-level theoretical foundation for SRC models of distorted structure functions in $\rm A\geq 3$ nuclei. Contributions from the higher mass spin-1 isospin triplet states $(ud)$, $(uu)$ and $(dd)$ are possible, with the spin-1 $(ud)$ diquark proposed as a higher mass but viable structure function distortion mechanism for the spin-1 ground state deuteron. Predictions are made for lepton scattering experiments on $\rm ^3H$ and $\rm ^3He$ nuclear targets, with implications for the coefficients of the 3-valence quark Fock states in the nucleon wavefunction.

## Key claim

Diquark formation across a short-range nucleon-nucleon pair via the attractive $3_C \otimes 3_C \rightarrow \bar{3}_C$ channel of SU(3)_C is proposed as the underlying QCD-level mechanism for short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei, which in turn cause the distorted quark momentum distributions observed as the EMC effect. The model specifically predicts that the scalar isospin-singlet $[ud]$ diquark is the primary driver for A ≥ 3 nuclei, while the higher-mass spin-1 $(ud)$ diquark may contribute in the spin-1 deuteron, with the caveat that direct calculations of structure function distortions $F_2(x_B)$ are not yet complete.

## Method

The author calculates diquark masses and binding energies using baryon mass spectroscopy with sub-hadronic hyperfine interactions, fitting effective quark masses to measured baryon masses from the $\Lambda(1116)$ and $\Sigma^0(1193)$ baryons. The quark-quark separation distance in the diquark is estimated via a non-relativistic square-well potential model, and compared to nucleon-nucleon separation distances derived from SRC relative momenta. Counting arguments for possible $[ud]$ diquark combinations in A=3 nuclei are performed for both the quark-diquark and 3-valence-quark internal nucleon configurations, yielding predictions for the ratio of same-flavor to neutron-proton SRC pairs.

## Key numbers

- Scalar $[ud]$ diquark binding energy: $148 \pm 9$ MeV, calculated from $m_u^b + m_d^b + m_s^b - M_\Lambda$
- Scalar $[ud]$ diquark mass: $578 \pm 11$ MeV
- Vector $(ud)$, $(uu)$, $(dd)$ diquark masses: $776 \pm 11$ MeV each
- Effective light quark mass in baryons: $m_q^b = 363$ MeV
- Non-relativistic estimate of scalar $[ud]$ diquark radius: $R_{[ud]} \sim 0.6 \times 10^{-15}$ m
- Proposed NN separation distance threshold for diquark formation: $d_{NN} \lesssim d_F = 0.79$ fm, corresponding to Fermi momentum $k_F = 250$ MeV/c
- For A=3 nuclei with 3-valence-quark nucleons, the ratio of same-flavor to neutron-proton SRC satisfies $0 \leq \mathcal{N}_{pp}/\mathcal{N}_{np} \leq 2/5$ for $^3$He and $0 \leq \mathcal{N}_{nn}/\mathcal{N}_{np} \leq 2/5$ for $^3$H

## Context

This work extends the SRC model of the EMC effect, first proposed by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (1990, 1991) and later supported by CLAS collaboration results (2019), by providing a QCD-level foundation through diquark formation. It builds upon decades of diquark research including Jaffe (2004) on diquark masses and the baryon spectroscopy methods of Karliner (2014). The model makes testable predictions for the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab on $^3$H and $^3$He targets, and for the E12-11-112 experiment which has published isospin-dependent SRC results (Li, 2022). The hexadiquark 12-quark color-singlet solution for $^4$He (West, 2020) is cited as a related application of the diquark formation mechanism.
