# Higher-order baryon number susceptibilities: interplay between the chiral and the nuclear liquid-gas transitions

> We use an improved version of the SU(3) flavour parity-doublet quark-hadron model to investigate the higher order baryon number susceptibilities near the chiral and the nuclear liquid-gas transitions. The parity-doublet model has been improved by adding higher-order interaction terms of the scalar fields in the effective mean field Lagrangian, resulting in a much-improved description of nuclear ground-state properties, in particular the nuclear compressibility. The resulting phase diagram of the model agrees qualitatively with expectations from lattice QCD, i.e., it shows a crossover at zero net baryo-chemical potential and a critical point at finite density. Using this model, we investigate the dependence of the higher-order baryon number susceptibilities as function of temperature and chemical potential. We observe a strong interplay between the chiral and liquid-gas transition at intermediate baryo chemical potentials. Due to this interplay between the chiral and the nuclear liquid-gas transitions, the experimentally measured cumulants of the net baryon number may show very different beam energy dependence, subject to the actual freeze-out temperature.

## Key claim

The experimentally measured cumulants of the net baryon number may show very different beam energy dependence depending on the actual freeze-out temperature, due to a strong interplay between the chiral and nuclear liquid-gas transitions at intermediate baryo-chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors used an improved SU(3) flavour parity-doublet quark-hadron model in mean-field approximation, adding higher-order interaction terms of the scalar fields to better reproduce nuclear ground-state properties, particularly the nuclear compressibility. They introduced a de-confinement mechanism via a Polyakov loop potential and excluded volumes for hadrons, then solved the equations of motion self-consistently to compute the grand-canonical potential as a function of temperature and baryo-chemical potential. The second-, third-, and fourth-order baryon number susceptibilities were calculated numerically using a five-point derivative formula across a grid of temperatures from 15 MeV to 180 MeV and baryo-chemical potentials from 0 MeV to 1200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear compressibility: 267.12 MeV, obtained from the improved parity-doublet model.
- Saturation density: 0.142 fm^-3, with a nucleonic binding energy of -16.00 MeV.
- Hyperonic optical potentials in ground-state nuclear matter: U_Λ(ρ_o) = -28 MeV and U_Ξ(ρ_o) = -18 MeV.
- The two crossover lines (liquid-gas and chiral) merge at an intermediate chemical potential μ_B ≈ 400 MeV.
- The freeze-out line parameterization uses T_lim values of 165 MeV and 120 MeV, with μ_B (MeV) = 1303 / (1 + 0.286 √s_NN) and T (MeV) = T_lim / (1 + exp[2.60 - ln(√s_NN)/0.45]).

## Context

This work extends previous parity-doublet model studies by Steinheimer (2011) and Motohiro (2015) by adding higher-order scalar interaction terms to improve nuclear compressibility, and by incorporating a Polyakov loop de-confinement mechanism following Steinheimer (2010) and Ratti (2005). The results show that at high baryo-chemical potentials (μ_B > 400 MeV), nuclear interactions significantly affect baryon number susceptibilities, which is relevant for interpreting fluctuation measurements from the RHIC beam energy scan and the HADES experiment at GSI. The study enables more realistic comparisons between effective model predictions and experimental cumulant measurements by accounting for the interplay between the liquid-gas and chiral transitions along different freeze-out lines.
