# Systematic study of even-even $^{20-32}$Mg isotopes

> A systematic study for 2$^+_1$ and 4$^+_1$ energies for even-even $^{20-32}$Mg by means of large-scale shell model calculations using the effective interaction USDB and USDBPN with SD and SDPN model space respectively. The reduced transition probability {\em B}({\em E}2;$\uparrow$) were also calculated for the chain of Mg isotopes.Very good agreement were obtained by comparing the first 2$^+_1$ and 4$^+_1$ levels for all isotopes with the recently available experimental data and with the previous theoretical work using 3DAMP+GCM model, but studying the transition strengths {\em B}({\em E}2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \rightarrow2^+_1$) for Mg isotopes using constant proton-neutron effective charges prove the limitations of the present large-scale calculations to reproduce the experiment in detail.

# Systematic study of even-even $^{20-32}$Mg isotopes

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations using the USDB and USDBPN effective interactions reproduce the experimental 2$^+_1$ and 4$^+_1$ excitation energies for even-even $^{20-32}$Mg isotopes very well, but the use of constant proton-neutron effective charges (e$_{\pi}$=1.25e, e$_{\nu}$=0.8e) reveals limitations in reproducing the B(E2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \rightarrow$2$^+_1$) transition strengths in detail.

## Method

The author performed unrestricted large-scale shell model calculations using the Nushell@MSU code with a $^{16}$O core and valence nucleons distributed over the 1d$_{5/2}$, 1d$_{3/2}$, and 2s$_{1/2}$ orbitals. The USDB effective interaction in the SD model space was used for $^{20-30}$Mg, while the USDBPN interaction in pn formalism with the SDPN model space was employed for $^{32}$Mg. Calculated 2$^+_1$ and 4$^+_1$ excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities were compared with experimental data and with previous theoretical results from the 3DAMP+GCM model (Yao et al., 2011) and HFB-Gogny calculations (Rodriguez-Guzman et al., 2002).

## Key numbers

- For $^{20-32}$Mg isotopes, the calculated 2$^+_1$ and 4$^+_1$ excitation energies show better agreement with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center (2012) than the previous 3DAMP+GCM calculations of Yao et al. (2011).
- The effective charges used for B(E2) calculations were e$_{\pi}$=1.25e for protons and e$_{\nu}$=0.8e for neutrons.
- The calculated B(E2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \rightarrow$2$^+_1$) values (in e$^{2}$fm$^{4}$) for the chain $^{20-32}$Mg are closer to experimental data from RIKEN, GANIL, and CERN than the predictions of the 3DAMP+GCM model (Yao et al., 2011) and the HFB-Gogny model (Rodriguez-Guzman et al., 2002).
- The valence nucleon counts range from 4 valence protons plus 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, and 16 valence neutrons for $^{20}$Mg through $^{32}$Mg respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous large-scale shell model studies of Mg isotopes (Caurier et al., 1998; Utsuno et al., 1999; Otsuka et al., 2001; Caurier et al., 2005) and provides a systematic comparison with the 3DAMP+GCM approach of Yao et al. (2011) and the HFB-Gogny calculations of Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2002). The results demonstrate that while shell model calculations with the USDB/USDBPN interactions successfully describe excitation energies across the isotopic chain, the constant effective charge approach fails to reproduce the detailed experimental B(E2) systematics, suggesting the need for more sophisticated treatment of effective charges or configuration mixing beyond the sd model space.
