# Quantum-mechanical picture of peripheral chiral dynamics

> The nucleon's peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities are computed in chiral effective field theory. The densities are represented in first-quantized form, as overlap integrals of chiral light-front wave functions describing the transition of the nucleon to soft pion-nucleon intermediate states. The orbital motion of the pion causes a large left-right asymmetry in a transversely polarized nucleon. The effect attests to the relativistic nature of chiral dynamics [pion momenta k = O(M_pi)] and could be observed in form factor measurements at low momentum transfer.

## Key claim
The nucleon's peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities, computed in leading-order chiral effective field theory, exhibit a large left-right asymmetry in a transversely polarized nucleon because the orbital motion of the soft pion is relativistic (pion momenta of order the pion mass), not because of non-relativistic motion; the asymmetry is numerically large and the inequality |ρ̃₂^V(b)| < ρ₁^V(b) is nearly saturated.

## Method
The authors computed the isovector transverse charge and magnetization densities at peripheral distances b = O(M_π⁻¹) using leading-order chiral effective field theory in the light-front formulation. They represented the densities as overlap integrals of chiral light-front wave functions describing the nucleon's transition to soft pion-nucleon intermediate states, with the wave functions derived from the pseudoscalar πNN vertex and the invariant mass difference of the intermediate state. The densities at the special points b = ∓b e_x on the transverse x-axis were expressed in terms of the spin-flip wave function, and an effective contact term (reflecting non-chiral intermediate states) was added, contributing less than 10% to ρ₁^V(b) at b > 1 M_π⁻¹.

## Key numbers
- The ratio ρ^V_left(b)/ρ^V_right(b) is approximately 10 at b = 1 M_π⁻¹ and decreases slowly at larger distances.
- The contact term contributes less than 10% of ρ₁^V(b) at b > 1 M_π⁻¹.
- The left and right densities are of the same parametric order in the heavy-baryon limit, ρ^V_left(b)/ρ^V_right(b) = O(1) for M_π/M_N → 0.
- The densities are computed at peripheral transverse distances b = O(M_π⁻¹), with the pion mass M_π as the natural scale.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic chiral EFT calculations of Granados and Weiss (2013) for nucleon form factors by providing a first-quantized, light-front wave function representation of the peripheral transverse densities. It confirms and explains the numerical inequality |ρ̃₂^V(b)| < ρ₁^V(b) observed in that earlier work. The results enable future extraction of peripheral transverse densities from precise form factor measurements at low momentum transfer using dispersion fits (Belushkin et al., 2006) and could be tested in high-energy proton-proton collisions where scattering occurs on a peripheral pion. The light-front representation can be extended to include Δ isobars, implement large-N_c scaling, and compute peripheral densities of matter and angular momentum.
