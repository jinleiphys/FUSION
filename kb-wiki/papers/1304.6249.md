# 2p2h effects on the weak pion production cross section

> The neutrino-nucleon --> lepton pion QE reaction on the A-target is used as a signal event or/and to reconstruct the neutrino energy, using two-body kinematics. Competition of another processes could lead to misidentification of the arriving neutrinos, being important the fake events coming from the CC1-pion background. A precise knowledge of cross sections is a prerequisite in order to make simulations in event generators to substract the fake ones from the QE countings, and in this contribution we analyze the different nuclear effects on the CC1-pion channel. Our calculations also can be extended for the NC case.

## Key claim

The authors analyze the effect of two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) contributions on the charged-current single-pion (CC1π) production cross section in neutrino-nucleus interactions, finding that the 2p2h contribution is important and comparable to the 1p1h one, but note that in the π⁰ channel the results do not reproduce data well and nonresonant contributions and charge exchange terms should be included.

## Method

The authors compute the 2p2h+1π amplitude for neutrino-induced pion production on nuclear targets, using a relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) of QHD I for binding effects, a perturbative approach in nuclear matter for the momentum distribution, and the Eikonal approach for pion final state interactions. The pion self-energy is treated in the Δ-hole approach, with the Δ width accounting for the final pion-nucleon state through absorptive evaluation. The total and differential cross sections are calculated for the CC1π⁺ channel and compared with MiniBooNE data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects on weak pion production previously performed by Lalakulich (2012) for the 1p1h+1π configuration space, adding the 2p2h+1π contributions. The calculations use the Relativistic Hartree approximation of QHD I from Serot (1986) for binding effects and a perturbative approach in nuclear matter from Mariano (1996) for the momentum distribution. The results are compared with MiniBooNE data, and the authors note that meson exchange currents should be included alongside 2p2h contributions for a more realistic estimation.
