# Gamow vectors and Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics

> Gamow solutions are used to transform self-adjoint energy operators by means of factorization (supersymmetric) techniques. The transformed non-hermitian operators admit a discrete real spectrum which is occasionally extended by a single complex eigenvalue associated to normalized eigensolutions. These new Hamiltonians are not pseudo-hermitian operators and also differ from those obtained by means of complex-scaling transformations. As an example, Coulomb-like potentials are studied.

# Gamow vectors and Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics

## Key claim

Gamow (decaying) eigensolutions can be used as transformation functions in supersymmetric quantum mechanics to construct non-hermitian Hamiltonians that are strictly isospectral to the original self-adjoint Hamiltonian, or that admit a single additional complex eigenvalue with a square-integrable eigenfunction; these new Hamiltonians are not pseudo-hermitian and differ from those obtained via complex-scaling transformations.

## Method

The author factorizes a spherically symmetric self-adjoint Hamiltonian H_l as H_l = AB + epsilon, where epsilon is a complex factorization constant and A, B are first-order differential operators that are not mutual adjoints. Using a Gamow solution u(r) (a solution of the Schrodinger equation with complex eigenvalue and purely outgoing boundary conditions) as the transformation function, the reversed product h_l = BA + epsilon yields a non-hermitian partner Hamiltonian. The method is generalized by relaxing the boundary condition at the origin to include the singular solution r^{-l}, producing a generalized Gamow vector omega(r) that allows a normalizable eigenfunction for the complex eigenvalue.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb potential is treated with units where energy is given in multiples of 13.5 eV (Z times the Rydberg energy) and length in Bohr radii r_B = 0.529 x 10^{-8}/Z cm.
- For the hydrogen-like example with l=1, the Gamow vector u(r) corresponds to the complex eigenvalue epsilon = -0.2604 + i 0.104 (times 13.5 eV).
- The imaginary part of the transformed potential v_l(r) becomes relevant at intermediate distances between 2 and 6 Bohr radii for the parameters considered.
- The generalized Gamow vector omega(r) and its associated square-integrable wavefunction Psi_epsilon are plotted from zero to 20 Bohr radii, with the complex eigenvalue epsilon = -0.2604 + i 0.104 (times 13.5 eV).

## Context

This work extends the complex factorization approach introduced by Rosas-Ortiz (2003) and relates to the refined factorization methods reported in the same period. It contrasts with the complex-scaling method of Aguilar (1971), Simon (1972), and others, where the transformed potential takes the simpler form V_theta = e^{i 2 theta} V(r e^{i theta}). The results differ from pseudo-hermitian transformations (Mostafazadeh, 2003) and PT-symmetric approaches (Bender, 2003). The construction enables the systematic generation of non-hermitian test Hamiltonians with controlled spectral properties, and the author notes that iteration of the method can produce higher-order intertwining operators, with second-order transformations capable of producing non-hermitian operators with real spectrum extended by two complex conjugate eigenvalues.
