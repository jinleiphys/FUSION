# Machine learning-based event generator for electron-proton scattering

> We present a new machine learning-based Monte Carlo event generator using generative adversarial networks (GANs) that can be trained with calibrated detector simulations to construct a vertex-level event generator free of theoretical assumptions about femtometer scale physics. Our framework includes a GAN-based detector folding as a fast-surrogate model that mimics detector simulators. The framework is tested and validated on simulated inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data along with existing parametrizations for detector simulation, with uncertainty quantification based on a statistical bootstrapping technique. Our results provide for the first time a realistic proof-of-concept to mitigate theory bias in inferring vertex-level event distributions needed to reconstruct physical observables.

## Key claim

The authors present a proof-of-concept that a generative adversarial network (GAN)-based Monte Carlo event generator can be trained on simulated detector-level deep-inelastic scattering data and then used to reconstruct vertex-level event distributions without relying on theoretical assumptions about femtometer-scale physics, provided the detector effects are modeled by a separately trained conditional GAN detector surrogate.

## Method

The framework uses two GANs: a primary GAN (the MLEG) that generates vertex-level event features from noise, and a secondary conditional GAN trained as a detector surrogate that maps vertex-level features to detector-level features using the eic-smear detector parametrization. The MLEG generator is concatenated with the detector surrogate, and a discriminator compares the resulting synthetic detector-level events against real detector-level training samples; the generator is updated adversarially until it produces vertex-level events whose detector-level projections are indistinguishable from the training data. The method is validated on simulated inclusive deep-inelastic electron-proton scattering pseudodata generated from the JAM global QCD analysis framework at a center-of-mass energy of 318.2 GeV, with uncertainty quantification via bootstrapping over 10 independent GAN trainings.

## Key numbers

* Center-of-mass energy of 318.2 GeV, compatible with HERA kinematics, used for generating inclusive DIS pseudodata.
* Generator and discriminator each use 5 hidden dense layers with 512 neurons per layer and a 10% dropout rate.
* Training performed for 100,000 epochs using Adam optimizer with learning rate 10^-4, beta1=0.5, beta2=0.9, and a generator-to-discriminator training ratio of 5.
* 10 independent GANs were trained using bootstrapping (random sampling with replacement) to produce uncertainty bands.
* The reduced inclusive ep cross section was compared across 18 x-bins (i=0 for highest x to i=17 for lowest x), with each bin scaled by a factor 2^i for visualization.

## Context

This work extends the application of GANs in high-energy physics, previously used for fast detector simulation (Paganini 2018, CaloGAN 2018, de Oliveira 2017, Musella 2018) and for mapping parton-level to detector-level data (Bellagente et al., 2019), by demonstrating a full closure test that reconstructs vertex-level events from detector-level pseudodata without theoretical assumptions about hadronization or factorization. The approach contrasts with traditional Monte Carlo event generators such as PYTHIA, HERWIG, and SHERPA, which rely on perturbative QCD and phenomenological hadronization models. The results enable future applications to real experimental data from Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provide a method for distributing high-dimensional event data in a compact, model-independent form rather than as binned histograms.
