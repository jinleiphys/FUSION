# Cluster-liquid transition in finite saturated fermionic systems

> The role of saturation for cluster formation in finite systems such as atomic nuclei is analyzed by considering three length-scale ratios, and performing deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations. The effect of clusterisation in deformed light systems is related to the saturation property of the inter-nucleon interaction. The formation of clusters at low nucleon density is illustrated by expanding the radius of $^{16}$O. A phase diagram shows that the formation of clusters can be interpreted as a hybrid state between the crystal and the liquid phases. In the hybrid cluster phase the confining potential attenuates the delocalization generated by the effective nuclear interaction.

## Key claim

The formation of clusters in finite saturated fermionic systems like atomic nuclei can be interpreted as a hybrid phase between a crystal and a quantum liquid, where the confining potential attenuates the delocalization generated by the effective nuclear interaction, but this interpretation is conditional on the system having a nucleon number below a threshold A₀ and a quantality parameter Λ greater than 0.1.

## Method

The authors performed deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model based on the energy density functional DD-ME2. They computed binding energy curves as a function of the axial quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ for ²⁰Ne, and also performed constrained calculations for ¹⁶O by gradually expanding its radius to reduce density. The analysis was complemented by deriving analytical relations between the localization parameter α, the quantality parameter Λ, and the nucleon number A to construct a phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium density of nucleonic matter is ρ_eq ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For ²⁰Ne, the calculated equilibrium shape is a prolate quadrupole ellipsoid with β₂ ≈ 0.55, and characteristic observables (binding energy, charge and matter radii) reproduce available data within 1%.
- The Mott-like phase transition for ¹⁶O occurs at a critical radius r_c = 3.33 fm, when the density is reduced to ρ/ρ_eq ≈ 1/3.
- The ratio of the critical radius to the ground-state radius r_c / r_g.s. ≈ 1.3 for the DD-ME2 functional, compared to ≈ 1.7 obtained with the Gogny effective interaction.
- The typical nucleon number for which clustering effects are likely to occur is A₀ ≈ 10, obtained from Eq. (rel3) with γ ≈ 3/4 and the typical Λ value for nuclear matter.
- The liquid phase corresponds to Λ > 0.1, while the crystalline solid phase is characterized by Λ < 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of cluster formation in finite nuclei previously studied by Ebran et al. (2012, 2013) by explicitly connecting clusterization to the saturation property of the inter-nucleon interaction. The authors relate their findings to the quantality parameter Λ introduced by Mottelson (1996) and to the Mott effect for vanishing binding in nuclear matter studied by Ropke and collaborators. The results enable a unified phase diagram for finite saturated fermionic systems, showing that cluster states occur in light nuclei (A ≈ 10) while medium-heavy and heavy nuclei exhibit quantum liquid behavior due to the A¹/⁶ dependence of the localization parameter.
