# Neutron Stars as Type-I Superconductors

> In a recent paper by Link, it was pointed out that the standard picture of the neutron star core composed of a mixture of a neutron superfluid and a proton type-II superconductor is inconsistent with observations of a long period precession in isolated pulsars. In the following we will show that an appropriate treatment of the interacting two-component superfluid (made of neutron and proton Cooper pairs), when the structure of proton vortices is strongly modified, may dramatically change the standard picture, resulting in a type-I superconductor. In this case the magnetic field is expelled from the superconducting regions of the neutron star leading to the formation of the intermediate state when alternating domains of superconducting matter and normal matter coexist.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when the strong interaction between neutron and proton Cooper pairs is properly accounted for, the proton superconductor in a neutron star core may be type-I rather than type-II, contrary to the standard picture; this conclusion depends on the smallness of the isospin-symmetry-breaking parameter \(\epsilon\), which the authors estimate as \(\epsilon \sim 10^{-2}\).

## Method

The authors construct a two-component Ginzburg-Landau free energy for proton and neutron condensates, with an approximate \(SU(2)\) isospin symmetry in the interaction potential. They solve the linearized field equations far from a single proton vortex to obtain the asymptotic behavior of the condensates and the gauge field, then compute the interaction energy between two widely separated vortices using the method of Speight (1997) and Mack & Schaposnik (2000). They also calculate the critical magnetic fields \(H_c\) and \(H_{c2}\) for a specific polynomial potential to verify the type-I/type-II boundary.

## Key numbers

* The London penetration depth is estimated as \(\lambda \sim 80\) fm and the proton correlation length as \(\xi \sim 30\) fm, giving a naive Landau-Ginzburg parameter \(\kappa = \lambda/\xi \sim 3\).
* The ratio of proton to neutron Cooper pair density in the neutron star core is \(\gamma \equiv n_1/n_2 \sim 5-15\%\).
* The effective Landau-Ginzburg parameter for the two-component system is \(\kappa_{np} = \sqrt{\epsilon}\,\lambda/\xi\); the superconductor is type-I when \(\kappa_{np} < 1/\sqrt{2}\).
* Using the estimate \(\epsilon \sim (m_d - m_u)/\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 10^{-2}\), the authors obtain \(\kappa_{np} \sim 0.3 < 1/\sqrt{2}\), corresponding to a type-I superconductor.

## Context

This work directly addresses the inconsistency raised by Link (2003) between the standard picture of a type-II proton superconductor in neutron stars and the observed long-period precession of isolated pulsars. It extends prior studies of two-component condensates in cosmic strings, high-\(T_c\) superconductors, and Bose-Einstein condensates (Witten 1985; various authors) to the neutron star context. If confirmed, the result would require reconsideration of glitch models that assume a type-II proton superconductor, such as those invoking flux-tube pinning, and would imply that the neutron star core consists of alternating domains of superconducting and normal matter in the intermediate state.
