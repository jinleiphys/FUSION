# Relativistic Calculation of Polarization Observables in NN -> DπProcesses

> A detailed analysis of processes of the type NN -> dπis presented taking into account the exchange graphs of a nucleon and a pion. A large sensitivity of polarization observables to the off-mass shell effects of nucleons inside the deuteron is shown. The influence of the inclusion of the P-wave in the deuteron wave function (DWF) is studied. We have analyzed the sensitivity of all the observables to the form of πNN - current taking into account the off-shellnis of the nucleon and the choice of the DWF relativistic form. From the results one can see the very large sensitivity of all the observables, especially of the polarization characteristics to the choice of the DWF form.

## Key claim

The paper shows that polarization observables in the reaction NN -> dpi are extremely sensitive to the off-mass-shell effects of nucleons inside the deuteron and to the inclusion of P-wave components in the deuteron wave function, with some polarization characteristics even changing sign when these effects are included. The authors qualify that the equivalence theorem between pseudoscalar and pseudovector piNN coupling cannot be applied to this process because of the presence of the bound deuteron state.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic covariant formalism for the NN -> dpi reaction amplitude, expanding it over six independent invariant amplitudes. They calculated both the one-nucleon exchange (pole) graph and the pion rescattering (triangle) graph, using the Gross equation approach for the deuteron wave function with S-wave, D-wave, and P-wave components, and a linear combination of pseudoscalar and pseudovector piNN coupling with a mixing parameter lambda. The triple integrals for the rescattering graph were evaluated numerically using Gaussian quadrature, and the results were compared with experimental data at proton kinetic energy T_p = 578 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton kinetic energy T_p = 578 MeV, corresponding to pion kinetic energy T_pi = 147 MeV
- The relative momentum of the nucleon inside the deuteron increases by approximately sqrt(m*mu) = 360 MeV when a pion is absorbed by an off-shell nucleon
- The intra-deuteron distance corresponding to this momentum is approximately 1/sqrt(m*mu) = 0.6 fm
- The total probability of small components in the deuteron wave function (P_small) increases monotonically with the mixing parameter lambda from approximately 0.03% for lambda = 0 to approximately 1.5% for lambda = 1
- The coupling constant g^2/4pi = 14.7 for the piNN vertex
- The differential cross section dsigma/dOmega is described rather well using the Gross approach for the deuteron wave function at mixing parameter lambda = 0.6-0.8

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic studies of NN -> dpi reactions that used multichannel Schrodinger equations with separable or local potentials, as well as earlier relativistic approaches by Locher (1990s) and others that did not successfully describe all polarization observables, especially the asymmetries A_y0 and iT_11. The paper builds on the deuteron wave function formalism developed by Buck and Gross using a one-boson-exchange model with pi, rho, omega, and sigma exchange. The results enable testing of off-shell effects in the piNN system at small distances and provide constraints on the form of the deuteron wave function and the mixing parameter in the piNN vertex.
