# Beyond the 2-Body Interaction Paradigm: The Case for Extended A-Body Pairing Interaction in Nuclei

> We discuss modeling of nuclear structure beyond the 2-body interaction paradigm. Our first example is related to the need of three nucleon contact interaction terms suggested by chiral perturbation theory. The relationship of the two low-energy effective coupling parameters for the relevant three nucleon contact interaction terms $c_D$ and $c_E$ that reproduce the binding energy of $^3$H and $^3$He has been emphasized and the physically relevant parameter region has been illustrated using the binding energy of $^4$He. Further justification of A-body interaction terms is outlined based on the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki effective interaction method used in solving the nuclear many-body problem within a finite model space. The third example we use is an exactly solvable A-body extended pairing interaction applied to heavy nuclei with a long isotopic chains, in particular using $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb as closed core system illustrates a remarkable relationship between the extended pairing strength $G(A)$ and the size of the valence space $\dim(A)$ for the members of these two isotope chain: $G(A)=α\dim(A)^{-β}$ with $α=259.436$ for Sn and $α=366.77$ for Pb while the parameter $β$ is practically 1. These three cases present evidence for the need of better understanding of the three-nucleon (NNN), four-nucleon (NNNN), and A-body interactions in nuclei either derived from ChPT or from a phenomenological considerations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear structure modeling must move beyond the conventional two-body interaction paradigm, presenting evidence from chiral perturbation theory, the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki effective interaction method, and an exactly solvable A-body extended pairing model that all point to the necessity of three-nucleon (NNN), four-nucleon (NNNN), and higher A-body interactions for accurate description of nuclei across the mass table.

## Method

The author first reviews the failure of high-precision NN-potentials (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, N3LO) to reproduce binding energies of A=3 and A=4 systems, and discusses how chiral perturbation theory NNN-interaction parameters c_D and c_E are constrained by the binding energies of 3H, 3He, and 4He. The Okubo-Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation method is then outlined to show how effective interactions in finite model spaces necessarily generate A-body terms. Finally, the exactly solvable Extended Pairing Interaction (EPI) is applied to long isotopic chains of Sn (A=100-130) and Pb (A=181-202) using Nilsson deformed shell model single-particle energies, where the pairing strength G(A) is determined by matching experimental relative binding energies.

## Key numbers

- The high-precision NN-potentials fail to reproduce the triton binding energy by more than 0.5 MeV and underbind 4He by more than 4 MeV
- The binding energies used to constrain c_D and c_E are 8.482 MeV for 3H and 7.718 MeV for 3He, with the experimental 4He binding energy being 28.296 MeV
- For the Pb isotopes (A=181-202), the extended pairing strength follows G(A) = alpha * dim(A)^(-beta) with alpha = 366.7702 and beta = 0.9972
- For the Sn isotopes relative to 132Sn, the extended pairing strength follows G(A) = alpha * dim(A)^(-beta) with alpha = 259.436 and beta = 0.9985
- The quadratic fits to ln(G) for Pb isotopes give log(G(A)) = 382.3502 - 4.1375 A + 0.0111 A^2 for even A and log(G(A)) = 391.6113 - 4.2374 A + 0.0114 A^2 for odd A

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Epelbaum, Machleidt, and Entem (2000s) that established the need for NNN-interactions from QCD, and the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki effective interaction method (Okubo 1954, Lee and Suzuki 1980) that generates many-body terms from unitary transformations. The exactly solvable Extended Pairing Interaction model, previously developed by Feng, Pan, and collaborators, is here applied to demonstrate that A-body interactions are phenomenologically relevant even for heavy nuclei, with the remarkable result that the pairing strength G depends only on the model space dimension rather than directly on mass number A. This enables future studies using Woods-Saxon potentials or other single-particle bases to test the universality of the log-log relation between G and model space dimension.
