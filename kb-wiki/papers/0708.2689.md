# Nilsson diagrams for light neutron-rich nuclei with weakly-bound neutrons

> Using Woods-Saxon potentials and the eigenphase formalism for one-particle resonances, one-particle bound and resonant levels for neutrons as a function of quadrupole deformation are presented, which are supposed to be useful for the interpretation of spectroscopic properties of some light neutron-rich nuclei with weakly-bound neutrons. Compared with Nilsson diagrams in text books which are constructed using modified oscillator potentials, we point out a systematic change of the shell structure in connection with both weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels related to small orbital angular momenta $\ell$. Then, it is seen that weakly-bound neutrons in nuclei such as $^{15-19}$C and $^{33-37}$Mg may prefer to being deformed as a result of Jahn-Teller effect, due to the near degeneracy of the 1d$_{5/2}$-2s$_{1/2}$ levels and the 1f$_{7/2}$-2p$_{3/2}$ levels in the spherical potential, respectively. Furthermore, the absence of some one-particle resonant levels compared with the Nilsson diagrams in text books is illustrated.

# Nilsson diagrams for light neutron-rich nuclei with weakly-bound neutrons

## Key claim

Using Woods-Saxon potentials rather than the modified oscillator potentials of standard textbook Nilsson diagrams, the shell structure for neutrons changes systematically for weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels with small orbital angular momenta (ℓ=0 and 1). This near degeneracy of the 1d₅/₂-2s₁/₂ and 1f₇/₂-2p₃/₂ levels at spherical shape provides a Jahn-Teller mechanism for deformation in neutron-rich C and Mg isotopes, but only if the proton configuration allows the deformation.

## Method

One-particle bound and resonant neutron levels were calculated as a function of quadrupole deformation using Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters for stable nuclei (diffuseness, spin-orbit strength, radius parameter from Bohr and Mottelson (1969)), with the depth adjusted to give a desired binding energy for a particular one-neutron level in each example. The coupled Schrödinger equations were solved in coordinate space with correct asymptotic behavior for r→∞, and one-particle resonant levels for deformed potentials were identified using the eigenphase formalism, where a resonance is defined as the energy at which one eigenphase increases through π/2 as energy increases.

## Key numbers

- For A=17 parameters with V_WS = −40.0 MeV at β=0: ε(1d₅/₂) = −560 keV, ε(2s₁/₂) = −415 keV, resonant energy of 1d₃/₂ = 5.60 MeV
- For A=31 parameters with V_WS = −40.0 MeV at β=0: 1f₇/₂ resonant level at ε_Ω = 1.59 MeV; 2p₃/₂ and 2p₁/₂ resonant levels are not obtained
- For A=37 parameters with V_WS = −40.0 MeV at β=0: 1f₅/₂ resonance at 5.22 MeV (width 2.08 MeV), 2p₃/₂ resonance at 0.018 MeV (width 0.005 MeV), ε(1f₇/₂) = −0.66 MeV; distance between 1f₇/₂ and 2p₃/₂ levels is 680 keV
- The [200 1/2] resonant level cannot be found for β > 0.46 and 0 < ε_Ω < 1.82 MeV
- The 2p₃/₂ and 2p₁/₂ resonant levels are not obtained for ε > 0.80 MeV and ε > 0.62 MeV, respectively, for the A=31 parameters
- The observed neutron separation energy of ¹⁷C is −730 keV

## Context

This work extends the classic Nilsson diagrams of Nilsson (1955) and Bohr and Mottelson (1975), which were constructed using modified oscillator potentials for stable deformed nuclei with separation energies of 7-10 MeV, to the regime of light neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line where weakly-bound neutrons with small ℓ play a unique role. The results provide a framework for interpreting spectroscopic properties of nuclei such as ¹⁵⁻¹⁹C and ³³⁻³⁷Mg, and enable future studies of how the low density of one-neutron resonant levels (due to the absence of Ω^π = 1/2⁺, 1/2⁻, and 3/2⁻ resonances in several-MeV regions) affects many-body correlations like pair correlation, which must be studied by treating the nearby continuum without discretizing the spectra.
