# Effect of collective neutrino flavor oscillations on vp-process nucleosynthesis

> The vp process is a primary nucleosynthesis process which occurs in core collapse supernovae. An essential role in this process is being played by electron antineutrinos. They generate, by absorption on protons, a supply of neutrons which, by (n,p) reactions, allow to overcome waiting point nuclei with rather long beta-decay and proton-capture lifetimes. The synthesis of heavy elements by the vp process depends sensitively on the \barν_e luminosity and spectrum. As has been shown recently, the latter are affected by collective neutrino flavor oscillations which can swap the \barν_e and \barν_{μ,τ} spectra above a certain split energy. Assuming such a swap scenario, we have studied the impact of collective neutrino flavor oscillations on the vp-process nucleosynthesis. Our results show that the production of light p-nuclei up to mass number A=108 is very sensitive to collective neutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that collective neutrino flavor oscillations, which swap the \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectra above a split energy of about 18 MeV, enhance the neutron production rate in the \(\nu p\) process by a factor \(\Gamma \approx 1.4\), but this enhancement is relatively insensitive to the exact split energy up to 25 MeV; the resulting increase in abundances of light \(p\)-nuclei up to mass number \(A=108\) is very sensitive to the oscillations, while the effect is reduced at early and late times when the \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectra become more similar.

## Method

The authors adopted antineutrino spectra from a 15 \(M_\odot\) supernova simulation (Buras et al., 2006) and approximated them by an \(\alpha\)-distribution. They assumed a complete swap of the \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectra above a split energy \(E_s \approx 18\) MeV, based on normal mass hierarchy calculations by Dasgupta et al. (2009). The effect on nucleosynthesis was studied by scaling the antineutrino absorption rate on protons by a constant factor \(\Gamma\) (defined as the ratio of the modified to the unmodified neutron production rate) and performing network calculations for a trajectory from Pruet et al. (2005).

## Key numbers

- The mean energy of the \(\bar{\nu}_e\) spectrum is \(\langle E_{\bar{\nu}_e} \rangle = 14.56\) MeV and of the \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectrum is \(\langle E_{\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}} \rangle = 15.44\) MeV, both with \(\alpha = 2.3\).
- The split energy for the spectral swap is \(E_s \approx 18\) MeV, based on Dasgupta et al. (2009).
- The neutron production rate is increased by a factor \(\Gamma \approx 1.4\) for the 15 \(M_\odot\) star; \(\Gamma\) is relatively insensitive to \(E_s\) up to 25 MeV.
- For an 8.8 \(M_\odot\) star, \(\Gamma = 1.1\) at about 1 s after explosion; for a 10.8 \(M_\odot\) star, \(\Gamma = 1.3\) at the same time.
- The overproduction factor of \(^{96}\)Ru is 2.3 for \(\Gamma = 1.3\) and nearly 3 for \(\Gamma = 1.4\).
- Abundances of light \(p\)-nuclides (\(^{92,94}\)Mo, \(^{96,98}\)Ru) are enhanced by factors 2–3 due to collective oscillations.

## Context

This work extends studies of the \(\nu p\) process (Froehlich et al., 2006; Pruet et al., 2006; Wanajo, 2006) by incorporating collective neutrino flavor oscillations, which were previously shown to swap \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectra (Dasgupta et al., 2009; Duan and Friedland, 2011). The results indicate that the production of light \(p\)-nuclei up to \(A=108\) (e.g., \(^{108}\)Cd) is very sensitive to these oscillations, while heavier \(p\)-nuclei likely require other processes. The authors note that recent simulations (Huedepohl et al., 2010; Fischer et al., 2010) show \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}\) spectra become increasingly similar after about 10 s, and that collective oscillations are suppressed during the early accretion phase (Chakraborty et al., 2011), implying a reduced impact at early and late times. Future work will include consistent treatment of collective oscillations with neutrino spectra and trajectories from supernova simulations.
