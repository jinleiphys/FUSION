# Bottomonium spectroscopy in the quark-gluon plasma

> The spectroscopic properties of heavy quarkonia are substantially different in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that is created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as compared to the vacuum situation that can be tested in pp collisions at the same center-of-mass energy. In this article, a series of recent works about the dissociation of the Y(nS) and chi_b(nP) states in the hot QGP is summarized. Quarkonia dissociation occurs due to (1) screening of the real quark-antiquark potential, (2) collisional damping through the imaginary part of the potential, and (3) gluon-induced dissociation. In addition, reduced feed-down plays a decisive role for the spin-triplet ground state. Transverse-momentum and centrality-dependent data are well reproduced in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. In the asymmetric p-Pb system, alterations of the parton density functions in the lead nucleus account for the leading fraction of the modifications in cold nuclear matter (CNM), but the hot-medium effects turn out to be relevant in spite of the small initial spatial extent of the fireball, providing additional evidence for the generation of a quark-gluon droplet.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes a theoretical approach (the "Heidelberg model") that reproduces bottomonium suppression data in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies by combining screening of the real potential, collisional damping through the imaginary potential, and gluon-induced dissociation, with the hedge that reduced feed-down plays a decisive role for the spin-triplet ground state and that in p-Pb systems, cold nuclear matter effects account for the leading fraction of modifications but hot-medium effects remain relevant despite the small fireball.

## Method

The authors solve the temperature-dependent radial stationary Schrödinger equation for six bottomonium states (Υ(1S,2S,3S) and χb(1P,2P,3P)) using a complex Cornell-type potential with a screened real part and an imaginary part accounting for collisional damping. They compute gluodissociation widths from singlet-to-octet dipole transition cross sections folded with a Bose-Einstein gluon distribution, then sum damping and dissociation widths incoherently for each spacetime point in a longitudinally boost-invariant, transversely expanding ideal hydrodynamic background, including relativistic Doppler effects due to relative motion between bottomonia and the medium.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of Υ(1S) ground state: about 1.10 GeV (vacuum)
- Binding energy of J/ψ: 0.64 GeV (vacuum)
- Formation time of bottomonia states: τF ≃ 0.3–0.6 fm/c
- Bottom quark production time scale: 0.02 fm/c
- String tension: σ = 0.192 GeV²
- QCD scale: ΛQCD = 276.3 MeV
- Initial medium temperatures in central Pb-Pb at LHC: of the order of 400 MeV or above
- Number of colors Nc = 3, number of flavors Nf = 3
- Reduced feed-down contributes 30–40% to the modification factor for the Υ(1S) state at LHC energies

## Context

This work extends the theoretical approach of the Heidelberg Multiparticle Dynamics group, building on prior treatments of quarkonium dissociation including screening (Matsui and Satz 1986), the complex in-medium potential (Laine et al. 2007), and gluodissociation (Bhanot and Peskin 1979; Brambilla et al. 2011). It compares with results from Rapp et al. (kinetic rate equations), Strickland et al. (viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics), and Vitev et al., among others, and enables quantitative comparison with CMS, ALICE, and LHCb data on Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV.
