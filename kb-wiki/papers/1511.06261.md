# Properties of magnetized neutral mesons within a full RPA evaluation

> We consider the two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model within the RPA framework to evaluate the masses of the $σ$ and $π^0$ mesons and the $π^0$ decay constant in the presence of a magnetic field at vanishing temperatures and baryonic densities. The present work extends other RPA applications by fully considering the external momenta, which enter the integrals representing the magnetized polarization tensor, so that more accurate results can be obtained in the evaluation of physical quantities containing pionic contributions. As we show, this technical improvement generates results which agree well with those produced by lattice simulations and chiral perturbation theory. Our method may also prove to be useful in future evaluations of quantities such as the shear viscosity and the equation of state of magnetized quark matter with mesonic contributions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by fully evaluating the external momentum dependence of the magnetized polarization tensor within the RPA (rather than approximating it as momentum-independent), they obtain more accurate results for neutral meson properties that agree well with lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory, whereas previous RPA evaluations that neglected this momentum dependence produced less accurate results.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model within the random phase approximation (RPA) to compute the pseudoscalar and scalar polarization loops in the presence of a constant external magnetic field at zero temperature and zero baryon density. Unlike earlier RPA applications that approximated the polarization integrals by setting the external momentum to zero, the present work fully retains the external momentum dependence in the integrals, performs the sum over Landau levels analytically using the Hurwitz-Zeta function, and regularizes the divergences with a magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR) scheme using a sharp noncovariant cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters used are: cutoff Lambda = 664.3 MeV, bare quark mass m = 5.0 MeV, and coupling G Lambda^2 = 2.06, which reproduce f_pi^0 = 92.4 MeV, m_pi^0 = 135.0 MeV, and the quark condensate <qbar q>^{1/3} = -250.8 MeV at zero field.
- The full RPA result for the normalized pion decay constant f_pi^0^2(B)/f_pi^0^2(0) agrees with ChPT predictions for eB up to 0.1 GeV^2, while the q-qbar formalism of Simonov predicts an opposite behavior at very weak fields.
- The full RPA result for the normalized neutral pion mass m_pi^0(B)/m_pi^0(0) agrees with LQCD predictions of Hidaka up to about 0.5 GeV^2, with a maximum difference of about 15% at intermediate field values, and the agreement improves again for eB greater than about 2 GeV^2.
- The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation evaluated with the full RPA drops from its B=0 value by only about 1% and remains stable as B increases, in agreement with analytical predictions by Agasian and Shushpanov.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA evaluations of mesonic properties in a magnetic field by Lemmer (using Schwinger's proper time approach with I(k^2) approximately I(0)) and by Sadooghi (using the lowest Landau level approximation, which produced tachyonic instabilities). The results agree well with lattice QCD simulations by Hidaka and chiral perturbation theory by Andersen. The method enables future evaluations of pionic contributions to the equation of state of magnetized quark matter (as studied by Fukushima) and shear viscosity (as studied by Lang), and can be extended to charged mesons and thermal effects.
