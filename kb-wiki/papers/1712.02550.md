# Revisiting the pseudoscalar meson and glueball mixing and key issues in the search for pseudscalar glueball state

> We revisit the mixing mechanism for pesudscalar mesons and glueball which is introduced by the axial vector anomaly. We demonstrate that the physical mass of the pseudoscalar glueball does not favor to be lower than 1.8 GeV if all the parameters are reasonably constrained. This conclusion, on the one hand, can accommodate the pseudoscalar glueball mass calculated by Lattice QCD, and on the other hand, is consistent with the high-statistics analyses at BESIII that all the available measurements do not support the presence of two closely overlapping pseudoscalar states in any exclusive channel. Such a result is in agreement with the recent claim that the slightly shifted peak positions for two possible states $η(1405)$ and $η(1475)$ observed in different channels are actually originated from one single state with the triangle singularity interferences. By resolving this long-standing paradox, one should pay more attention to higher mass region for the purpose of searching for the pseudoscalar glueball candidate.

## Key claim

The physical mass of the pseudoscalar glueball does not favor being lower than 1.8 GeV when all parameters in the axial-vector-anomaly mixing scheme are reasonably constrained, which rules out the possibility of a light pseudoscalar glueball around 1.4 GeV and resolves the contradiction between lattice QCD calculations and earlier experimental assignments.

## Method

The authors construct a 4x4 mixing scheme for the pseudoscalar mesons (η, η′, η_c) and the glueball via the axial vector anomaly, using a unitary mixing matrix with three mixing angles (θ, φ_G, φ_Q). They impose constraints from lattice QCD for the gluonic matrix element G_g, from experimental decay constants f_q, f_s, f_c, and from the η_c → γγ branching ratio, then solve the mass density matrix equations to extract the physical glueball mass M_G as a function of the light-quark–glueball mixing angle φ_G.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar glueball mass M_G is found to be in the range 2.0–2.2 GeV for the central value of φ_G = 12°, with uncertainties from G_g = −(0.054 ± 0.008) GeV³.
- For φ_G = 3°, the extracted glueball mass is about 3.8 GeV, which is ruled out as unphysical.
- The mixing angle between heavy-flavor state and glueball is fixed at φ_Q = 11.6°, derived from BR(η_c → γγ) = (1.59 ± 0.13) × 10⁻⁴.
- The light-flavor mixing angle is fixed at θ = −11°, with f_q = f_π = 131 MeV and f_c = 487.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the mixing formalism of Feldmann (1998) and Tsai (2011) by re-examining the parameter space and showing that the earlier assignment of η(1405) as the pseudoscalar glueball is incompatible with the axial-anomaly mixing mechanism when lattice QCD constraints on G_g are included. The conclusion supports the triangle singularity explanation of Wu (2011) and Aceti (2012) for the η(1405)/η(1475) puzzle, and directs future experimental searches for the pseudoscalar glueball to the higher mass region above 1.8 GeV, consistent with quenched lattice QCD results from Morningstar (1999) and Chowdhury (2014) predicting a mass around 2.4–2.6 GeV.
