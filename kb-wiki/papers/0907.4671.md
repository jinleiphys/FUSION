# Extraction of the strong neutron-proton mass difference from the charge symmetry breaking in pn-->d pi0

> We perform a complete calculation of charge symmetry breaking effects for the reaction pn -> d pi0 at leading order in chiral perturbation theory. A new leading-order operator is included. From our analysis we extract δm_N^{str}, the strong contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference. The value obtained, δm_N^{str} = 1.5 \pm 0.8 (exp.) \pm 0.5 (th.) MeV, is consistent with the result based on the Cottingham sum rule. This agreement provides a non-trivial test of our current understanding of the chiral structure of QCD.

## Key claim

The strong contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference, \(\delta m_N^{\rm str}\), is extracted from the charge symmetry breaking forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn\to d\pi^0\) at leading order in chiral perturbation theory, including a newly identified leading-order operator. The extracted value is consistent with the Cottingham sum rule result, but the uncertainty is dominated by experimental error.

## Method

The authors performed a complete leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation of charge symmetry breaking effects in the reaction \(pn\to d\pi^0\), including a new rescattering operator from the time-dependent Weinberg-Tomozawa term. The forward-backward asymmetry \(A_{fb}\) was computed from the interference of isospin-conserving \(p\)-wave amplitudes (taken from a next-to-next-to-leading order calculation) with the isospin-violating \(s\)-wave amplitude, using realistic nucleon-nucleon wave functions in a hybrid approach. The experimental total cross section for \(nn\to d\pi^-\) was used to fix the isospin-conserving \(s\)-wave contribution, and the resulting \(A_{fb}\) was compared to the TRIUMF measurement to extract \(\delta m_N^{\rm str}\).

## Key numbers

*   Extracted strong neutron-proton mass difference: \(\delta m_N^{\rm str} = 1.5 \pm 0.8\ {\rm (exp.)} \pm 0.5\ {\rm (th.)}\ {\rm MeV}\) from the \(pn\to d\pi^0\) reaction.
*   Experimental forward-backward asymmetry at TRIUMF: \(A_{fb} = [17.2 \pm 8\ {\rm (stat.)} \pm 5.5\ {\rm (sys.)}] \times 10^{-4}\) at \(T_{\rm lab}=279.5\ {\rm MeV}\) (excess energy \(\eta=0.17\)).
*   Leading-order theoretical expression for the asymmetry: \(A_{\rm fb}^{\rm LO} = (11.5 \pm 3.5)\times 10^{-4} \cdot (\delta m_N^{\rm str} / {\rm MeV})\).
*   Comparison value from the Cottingham sum rule (Gasser): \(\delta m_N^{\rm str} = 2.0 \pm 0.3\ {\rm MeV}\).
*   Total cross section for \(nn\to d\pi^-\) used as input: \(\sigma = 252^{+5}_{-11}\ \cdot \eta\ [\mu{\rm b}]\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation of charge symmetry breaking in \(pn\to d\pi^0\) by van Kolck (1990s) and Bolton (2009), correcting a numerical error in the former and including a new leading-order operator from the time-dependent Weinberg-Tomozawa term. The result resolves a factor-of-3 overprediction of the forward-backward asymmetry present in those earlier calculations. The extracted \(\delta m_N^{\rm str}\) is consistent with the value from the Cottingham sum rule (Gasser, 1980s) and with a lattice QCD determination, providing a non-trivial test of the chiral structure of QCD. This enables a new method for extracting the strong component of the neutron-proton mass difference from pion production data, and motivates improved experimental measurements of the asymmetry.
