# Piecing the Solar Neutrino Puzzle Together at SNO

> We perform an oscillation parameter-independent analysis of solar neutrino flux measurements from which we predict the charged-current rate at SNO relative to Standard Solar Model to be $R_{SNO}^{CC}=0.35^{+0.16}_{-0.09} (0.46^{+0.13}_{-0.11})$ for oscillations to active (sterile) neutrinos. By alternately considering the $^8$B flux normalization fixed and free, we find that the flux measured by Super-Kamiokande (SK) not being a result of oscillations is strongly disfavored for oscillations to active neutrinos. SNO will determine the best-fit value of the $^8$B flux normalization $β$ (equal to the neutral-current rate), without recourse to neutral-current measurements, from the derived relation $β=R_{SNO}^{NC}=5.85 R_{SK} - 4.85 R_{SNO}^{CC}$. Using a simple parameterization of the fraction of high, intermediate, and low energy solar neutrinos starting above resonance, we reproduce the results of global analyses to good accuracy; we find that the LMA solution with a normal mass hierarchy is clearly favored. With $β$ free, our analysis for oscillations to active neutrinos gives $R_{SNO}^{NC}=β=1.34 \pm 0.34$, which corresponds to $R_{SNO}^{CC}=0.28\pm 0.07$.

## Key claim

The paper performs an oscillation parameter-independent analysis of solar neutrino flux measurements to predict the SNO charged-current rate, finding that the Super-Kamiokande flux not being a result of oscillations is strongly disfavored for oscillations to active neutrinos, but only when the ⁸B flux normalization is alternately considered fixed and free.

## Method

The authors divide the solar neutrino spectrum into high (⁸B, hep), intermediate (⁷Be, pep, ¹⁵O, ¹³N), and low (pp) energy parts, and use the measured rates from the ³⁷Cl, ⁷¹Ga, and Super-Kamiokande experiments to solve for the average survival probabilities P_H, P_I, and P_L for each energy class. They then apply adiabatic MSW constraints to these probabilities, parameterizing the fraction f_j of neutrinos in each energy class that start above resonance, to test the LMA and LOW solutions and predict the SNO charged-current and neutral-current rates.

## Key numbers

* Predicted SNO charged-current rate relative to Standard Solar Model: R_SNO^CC = 0.35^{+0.16}_{-0.09} for oscillations to active neutrinos (1 sigma).
* Predicted SNO charged-current rate relative to Standard Solar Model: R_SNO^CC = 0.46^{+0.13}_{-0.11} for oscillations to sterile neutrinos (1 sigma).
* Best-fit ⁸B flux normalization (equal to SNO neutral-current rate) for active neutrinos with free ⁸B flux: beta = R_SNO^NC = 1.34 ± 0.34.
* Corresponding best-fit SNO charged-current rate for active neutrinos with free ⁸B flux: R_SNO^CC = 0.28 ± 0.07.
* Best-fit oscillation parameters from adiabatic analysis with free ⁸B flux: delta m^2 = 2 × 10^{-5} eV^2, sin^2 2theta = 0.65.
* Best-fit vacuum mixing angle from adiabatic analysis with SSM ⁸B flux: sin^2 theta = 0.273, corresponding to sin^2 2theta = 0.79.
* Derived relation for SNO neutral-current rate: R_SNO^NC = beta = 5.85 R_SK - 4.85 R_SNO^CC, with R_SK = 0.459.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analysis procedure of Barger, Phillips, and Whisnant (1991) by applying it to the then-upcoming SNO experiment, and reproduces the results of more comprehensive global fits such as those by the authors' previous analysis (2001). The paper enables SNO to determine the ⁸B flux normalization from its charged-current measurement alone, without requiring neutral-current data, and shows that the LMA solution with a normal mass hierarchy is clearly favored over the LOW solution and inverted hierarchy.
