# Flavor Dependence of Charged Pion Fragmentation Functions

> We have measured the flavor dependence of multiplicities for pi^+ and pi^- production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on proton and deuteron targets to explore a possible charge symmetry violation in fragmentation functions. The experiment used an electron beam with energies of 10.2 and 10.6 GeV at Jefferson Lab and the Hall-C spectrometers. The electron kinematics spanned the range 0.3<x<0.6, 2<Q^2<5.5 GeV^2, and 4<W^2<11 GeV^2. The pion fractional momentum range was 0.3< z <0.7, and the transverse momentum range was 0<p_T<0.25 GeV/c. Assuming factorization at low p_T and allowing for isospin breaking, we find that the results can be described by two "favored" and two "un-favored" effective low $p_T$ fragmentation functions that are flavor-dependent. However, they converge to a common flavor-independent value at the lowest x or highest W of this experiment.

## Key claim

The paper reports that charged pion fragmentation functions (FF) extracted from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on proton and deuteron targets are flavor-independent and consistent with charge/isospin symmetry at the highest center-of-mass energy (W = 3.2 GeV), but deviate from this expectation at lower W (higher x), suggesting higher-twist contributions become important rather than fundamental charge symmetry violation in the fragmentation process itself.

## Method

The experiment used 10.2 and 10.6 GeV electron beams at Jefferson Lab Hall C to measure semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) yields of pi+ and pi- from hydrogen and deuterium targets. The four measured multiplicities were used to form sum and difference ratios (R1 and R2) and to extract two favored fragmentation functions (D_u->pi+, D_d->pi-) and two un-favored fragmentation functions (D_u->pi-, D_d->pi+) by solving a system of four equations, assuming factorization at low transverse momentum (p_T < 0.25 GeV/c) and using CTEQ5 next-to-leading-order parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Electron beam energies: 10.2 and 10.6 GeV
- Kinematic range: 0.3 < x < 0.6, 2 < Q^2 < 5.5 GeV^2, 4 < W^2 < 11 GeV^2 (W from 2.2 to 3.2 GeV)
- Pion fractional momentum: 0.3 < z < 0.7
- Pion transverse momentum: 0 < p_T < 0.25 GeV/c with average <p_T> ~ 0.1 GeV/c
- Systematic uncertainty on extracted multiplicities: 2.8%
- Systematic uncertainty on sum and difference ratios: 2.2%
- Detector efficiencies: 97%-99%; trigger efficiency: 98%-99%; live times: 94%-99%
- Beam currents: 2 microA to 70 microA
- Missing mass cut: M_X > 1.6 GeV/c^2 to exclude resonance region

## Context

This work extends previous SIDIS measurements from the JLab 6 GeV experiment (Tigran, 2007) to higher W and Q^2, and provides direct experimental tests of charge/isospin symmetry in fragmentation functions that complement global QCD analyses by the DSS (2007, 2008), JAM, and MAP collaborations. The results enable future extractions of charge symmetry violation in parton distribution functions at Jefferson Lab (proposal by the same collaboration) by demonstrating that CSV in fragmentation functions is negligible at high W, and suggest that spectator tagging techniques on deuterium or helium-4 targets could improve measurements of un-favored down quark fragmentation.
