# Molecular dynamics simulation for baryon-quark phase transition at finite temperature and density

> We study the baryon-quark phase transition in a molecular dynamics (MD) of quark degrees of freedom at finite temperature and density. The baryon state at low density and temperature, and the deconfined quark state at high density and temperature are reproduced. We investigate the equations of state of matters with different $u$-$d$-$s$ compositions. Then we draw phase diagrams in the temperature-density plane by this simulation. It is found that the baryon-quark transition is sensitive to the quark width.

## Key claim

The baryon-quark phase transition is sensitive to the quark width parameter \(L\), with the critical density shifting from around \(3\rho_0\) for \(L=0.43\) fm to around \(5\rho_0\) for \(L=0.33\) fm; the abstract does not specify that the transition density depends strongly on this model parameter and that only the larger-width case gives a low critical density while the smaller-width case yields results consistent with other theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics (MD) simulations of quark many-body systems using Gaussian wave packets with fixed width \(L\) for each quark, a Pauli potential to mimic fermionic statistics, and an effective Hamiltonian including color confinement, one-gluon exchange, and meson exchange potentials (\(\sigma,\omega,\rho,\phi\)). They solved cooling equations to find ground states at zero temperature and used a Nosé-Hoover thermostat to study finite-temperature systems, extracting phase diagrams from the specific-heat maxima for \(ud\), \(udd\), and \(uds\) matter at various baryon densities.

## Key numbers

- Quark widths used: \(L = 0.43\) fm (width parameter I) and \(L = 0.33\) fm (width parameter II); effective widths for kinetic energy were \(L^{\rm eff}_{u,d}=1.0\) fm and \(L^{\rm eff}_{s}=0.8\) fm.
- For width parameter I (\(L=0.43\) fm), the baryon-quark transition in \(ud\) matter occurs around \(3\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear density).
- For width parameter II (\(L=0.33\) fm), the transition occurs around \(5\rho_0\).
- The nucleon mass obtained with width parameter I is \(M_N \approx 1500\) MeV; with width parameter II it is \(M_N \approx 940\) MeV.
- Binding energy of \(ud\) matter was fitted to 16.5 MeV/nucleon at \(1\rho_0\).
- Critical temperatures for \(uds\) matter are 5–15 MeV larger than for \(ud\) matter at the same density.
- Constituent quark masses: \(m_{u,d}=300\) MeV, \(m_s=500\) MeV; string tension \(K=900\) MeV/fm; QCD fine structure constant \(\alpha_s=1.25\).

## Context

This work extends earlier molecular dynamics approaches to quark systems by Bona (1999, 2000), Hofmann (2000), and Maruyama (2000), but introduces a model with less ambiguity in defining the ground state and temperature. It provides phase diagrams in the temperature-density plane for baryon-quark matter, complementing lattice QCD results at high temperature (Kogut, 1983; Stephens, 2004) and bag model predictions at finite density (Satz, 1998). The results enable future studies of neutron star cores and beta-equilibrium matter, though the authors note that the strange \(uds\) matter EOS requires improvement from future experiments and inclusion of color magnetic interactions and chiral symmetry restoration effects.
