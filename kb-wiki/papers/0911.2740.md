# Meson-baryon nature of Lambda(1405) in chiral dynamics

> We present the recent progress in the study on the origin of baryon resonances in chiral dynamics. It is shown that in the chiral coupled-channel approach, the form of the interaction cannot be specified due to the cutoff of the loop integral. To avoid this ambiguity, we propose a natural renormalization scheme, which affords a clue to the origin of the resonance, together with the phenomenological fitting to experimental data. We study the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance in comparison with the N(1535).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Lambda(1405) resonance can be primarily described as a dynamical meson-baryon molecule generated by the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction alone, while the N(1535) requires a substantial Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole contribution beyond the purely dynamical component, but this conclusion depends on adopting the proposed natural renormalization scheme to separate CDD pole contributions from the loop function.

## Method

The authors use the N/D method to construct meson-baryon scattering amplitudes in a chiral coupled-channel approach, where the kernel interaction is given by the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term. They propose a natural renormalization scheme that fixes the subtraction constant by requiring the loop function G to vanish at the baryon mass threshold, thereby excluding CDD pole contributions from the loop function. By comparing phenomenological amplitudes (fitted to data with adjustable subtraction constants) to amplitudes in the natural scheme, they extract an effective interaction kernel whose pole structure reveals the presence of CDD pole contributions.

## Key numbers

- The nearest pole in the effective interaction kernel for the Lambda(1405) channel lies at approximately 7.9 GeV, far above the resonance energy region.
- The nearest pole in the effective interaction kernel for the N(1535) channel lies at 1693 +/- 37i MeV, within the energy region of interest.
- The natural renormalization condition sets G(M_N) = 0 for all channels, where M_N is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001) for describing baryon resonances as meson-baryon scattering states. It provides a method to distinguish between dynamical (meson-baryon molecule) and non-dynamical (CDD pole) components of resonances, which was previously ambiguous due to the cutoff dependence of loop integrals. The conclusion that Lambda(1405) is predominantly a meson-baryon molecule while N(1535) has substantial CDD pole content is qualitatively consistent with analyses of N_c scaling by Hyodo (2007) and Roca (2008), and with electromagnetic size estimates by Sekihara (2008). This enables future studies to systematically separate dynamical and non-dynamical components in other baryon resonances.
