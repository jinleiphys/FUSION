# Isospin breaking decays as a diagnosis of the hadronic molecular structure of the $P_c(4457)$

> The LHCb Collaboration announced the observation of three narrow structures consistent with hidden-charm pentaquark states. They are candidates of hadronic molecules formed of a pair of a charmed baryon and an anticharmed meson. Among them, the $P_c(4457)$ mass is consistent with earlier predictions of a $Σ_c\bar D^*$ molecule with $I=1/2$. We point out that if such a picture were true, one would have $\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)\to J/ψΔ^+)/\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)\to J/ψp)$ at the level ranging from a few percent to about 30%. Such a large isospin breaking decay ratio is two to three orders of magnitude larger than that for normal hadron resonances. It is a unique feature of the $Σ_c\bar D^*$ molecular model, and can be checked by LHCb.

## Key claim

If the $P_c(4457)$ is an $S$-wave $\Sigma_c \bar D^*$ hadronic molecule with isospin $I=1/2$, then the ratio $\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)^+ \to J/\psi \Delta^+)/\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)^+ \to J/\psi p)$ should be at the level of a few percent to about 30%, which is two to three orders of magnitude larger than isospin breaking effects for normal hadron resonances. The large uncertainty in this prediction comes mainly from the uncertainty in the $P_c(4457)$ mass.

## Method

The authors model the $P_c(4457)^+$ as a bound state of $\Sigma_c \bar D^*$ in the $I=1/2$ channel, with two charge components: $\Sigma_c^{++} D^{*-}$ and $\Sigma_c^+ \bar D^{*0}$. Using the two-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation, they derive the ratio of coupling constants for these two components. They then compute the decay amplitudes into $J/\psi p$ and $J/\psi \Delta^+$ via $\Sigma_c \bar D^*$ loops, relating the amplitudes to the wave functions at the origin using a Gaussian regulator with cutoff $\Lambda$ between 0.5 and 1 GeV, and employing spin-flavor matrix elements from Burns (2015) to relate the $J/\psi \Delta^+$ and $J/\psi p$ amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The $P_c(4457)^+$ mass is $4457.3 \pm 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}$ MeV, with width $6.4 \pm 2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9}$ MeV (LHCb, 2019).
- The $\Sigma_c^+ \bar D^{*0}$ threshold is $4459.8 \pm 0.4$ MeV, giving a binding energy of $2.5^{+1.8}_{-4.2}$ MeV for the $P_c(4457)^+$ relative to this threshold.
- The $\Sigma_c^{++} D^{*-}$ threshold is $4464.23 \pm 0.15$ MeV, giving a binding energy of $6.9^{+1.8}_{-4.1}$ MeV relative to this threshold.
- The predicted ratio $\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)^+ \to J/\psi \Delta^+)/\mathcal{B}(P_c(4457)^+ \to J/\psi p)$ ranges from a few percent to about 30%, depending on the cutoff and mass uncertainties.
- The three-momenta for the decays are about 0.83 GeV ($J/\psi p$) and 0.52 GeV ($J/\psi \Delta^+$), much larger than the binding momenta of 73 MeV ($\Sigma_c^+ \bar D^{*0}$) and 124 MeV ($\Sigma_c^{++} D^{*-}$).

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular interpretation of the $P_c(4457)$, previously predicted as an $I=1/2$ $\Sigma_c \bar D^*$ bound state by Wu et al. (2012) and studied by many groups including Roca et al. (2015), He (2015), and Burns (2015). The authors draw an analogy to the $X(3872)$ molecule, which shows large isospin breaking in its decays to $J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-$ and $J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$, and to the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ whose isospin breaking decay width is about 100 keV in the $DK$ molecular picture. The prediction can be tested by LHCb searching for $P_c(4457)^+$ in the $J/\psi \Delta^+$ mode.
