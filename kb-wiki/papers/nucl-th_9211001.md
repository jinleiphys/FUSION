# Pionic effects in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei

> The structure functions calculated in the Chiral bag model reproduce quite well, after appropriate perturbative evolution to large energy scales, the experimental data. We use these results to interpret the structure of the $EMC$ data as a quenching of the pion decay constant due to the in medium behavior of the nucleon. This explanation supports recent proposals of this phenomenon whose origin is the scale invariance of the $QCD$ lagrangian.

## Key claim

The structure functions calculated in the Chiral bag model, after perturbative QCD evolution to high energy scales, reproduce experimental data qualitatively, and the EMC effect can be interpreted as a quenching of the pion decay constant in the nuclear medium, supporting the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon structure functions in the Chiral bag model using chiral expansion to first order in 1/(4π f_π² R²), including photon-quark scattering, pionic final-state interactions, and meson-exchange current contributions. They then evolved these low-energy structure functions to high Q² using leading-order renormalization group equations, assigning a low-energy scale Q₀ to the model calculation. For the nuclear medium, they replaced the free pion decay constant f_π with a scaled in-medium value f_π* = σ f_π, following the Brown-Rho scaling prescription, and recomputed the structure function ratio.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius R = 1 fm, pion decay constant f_π = 95 MeV, yielding nucleon mass M ≈ 1000 MeV
- Ratio Q₀/Λ_QCD = 1.5, with Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV, giving low-energy scale Q₀ = 0.3 GeV
- Evolution performed from Q₀ to Q = 5 GeV (plateau reached from Q = 1.5 GeV onward)
- Gluon content of evolved free-nucleon structure function: about 47% (experimental value is 55%)
- Structure function ratio computed for scaling parameter σ = 0.95 and σ = 0.9, compared to EMC data
- Up to 50 modes used in cavity perturbation theory; up to 35 moments computed in evolution

## Context

This work extends the Jaffe-Ross (1980) approach of using low-energy bag model calculations as boundary conditions for QCD evolution, and follows the Brown-Rho (1991) scaling hypothesis for in-medium modifications of coupling constants. It contrasts with conventional nuclear physics models that include binding energy and density effects (as reviewed by Rondio, 1990) and with QCD rescaling models that invoke nucleon swelling. The results suggest that deep inelastic scattering data can probe scaling properties of effective Lagrangians in the nuclear medium, though the authors note that missing ingredients (support corrections, Fermi motion, gluonic contributions) would be needed for a quantitative fit.
