# Chiral Perturbation Theory on the Lattice and its Applications

> Chiral perturbation theory (CPT), the low-energy effective theory of QCD, can be used to describe QCD observables in the low-energy region in a model-independent way. At any given order in the chiral expansion, CPT introduces a finite number of parameters that encode the short-distance physics and that must be determined from experiment or numerical lattice QCD simulations. In this thesis, we calculate a number of hadronic observables in the quenched and partially quenched versions of CPT: Chiral corrections to B^(*) --> D^(*) at zero recoil are investigated in quenched CPT. We study in detail the charge radii of the meson and baryon octets, electromagnetic properties of the baryon decuplet, and the baryon decuplet to octet electromagnetic transitions in both, quenched and partially quenched CPT. We further show how effects due to the finite size of the lattice can be accounted for in heavy meson CPT and calculate, as explicit examples, neutral B meson mixing and the heavy-light meson decay constants. We also demonstrate how one can account for effects due to finite lattice spacing in the low-energy theories, considering as an example electromagnetic meson and baryon properties. The results of our calculations are crucial to extrapolate quenched and partially quenched lattice data from the heavier light quark masses used on the lattice to the physical values.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the formally dominant ${\mathcal O}(1/M^2)$ corrections to $B^{(*)}\to D^{(*)}$ form factors at zero recoil in quenched chiral perturbation theory (QCPT), finding that these corrections are proportional to $(\Delta_B - \Delta_D)^2$ and contain a $1/m_{qq}^2$ singularity in the chiral limit that is absent in full QCD, revealing a sickness of the quenched approximation.

## Method

The author constructs quenched heavy meson chiral perturbation theory for $B$ and $D$ mesons, including the hyperfine splitting operators that break heavy quark symmetry at ${\mathcal O}(1/M)$. One-loop corrections to the form factors $h_+(1)$, $h_{A_1}(1)$, and $h_1(1)$ at zero recoil are computed from wave function renormalization and vertex correction diagrams, with the $\mu$ dependence canceled by local counterterms $X(\mu)$. The double ratios ${\mathcal R}_+$, ${\mathcal R}_1$, and ${\mathcal R}_{A_1}$ used in lattice QCD calculations are then derived from these form factor corrections.

## Key numbers

- The hyperfine splittings are $\Delta_D \approx 142$ MeV and $\Delta_B \approx 46$ MeV, making the ratios $\Delta_D/m$ and $\Delta_B/m$ of order 1 for physical light quark masses.
- The leading corrections to $h_+(1)$ and $h_1(1)$ are proportional to $(\Delta_B - \Delta_D)^2$, while $h_{A_1}(1)$ receives corrections proportional to $(3\Delta_B^2 + \Delta_D^2 + 2\Delta_B\Delta_D)$.
- The hairpin parameter $\mu_0$ is estimated at $500$-$900$ MeV from the $\eta$-$\eta'$ mass splitting, with $\mu_0 \sim {\mathcal O}(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}})$.
- The axial coupling is taken as $g^2 = 0.4$, with the hairpin coupling $\alpha$ set to either $0$ or $0.7$, and the $\gamma$ coupling constrained to $-g \le \gamma \le g$.
- The $1/m_{qq}^2$ pole in the quenched result dominates over the logarithmic behavior of full CPT, with the double ratio ${\mathcal R}_{A_1}$ containing a term proportional to $2g^2\mu_0^2/(9m^2)$ in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the CPT calculation of Randall and Wise (1993) for $D$ meson hyperfine splitting corrections to the quenched approximation, and complements the partially quenched calculation by Savage (2001). The results are designed to improve the chiral extrapolation of quenched lattice QCD simulations of $B\to D^*l\nu$ decays, such as those by Hashimoto et al. (2001) and Kronfeld et al. (2002), by providing a more reliable estimate of the uncertainties associated with the quenched approximation. The calculation also provides the ${\mathcal O}(1/M^2)$ corrections to the double ratios ${\mathcal R}_+$, ${\mathcal R}_1$, and ${\mathcal R}_{A_1}$ that are central to lattice determinations of the form factors.
