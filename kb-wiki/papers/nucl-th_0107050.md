# Evidence for partial chiral symmetry restoration from pionic atoms

> Extensive data on strong interaction effects in pionic atoms are analyzed with a density-dependent isovector scattering amplitude suggested recently by Weise to result from a density dependence of the pion decay constant. Most of the so-called `missing s-wave repulsion' is removed when adopting this approach, thus indicating a partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter. The resulting potentials describe quite well also elastic scattering of 20 MeV pions on Ca. Further tests with elastic scattering are desirable.

## Key claim

Most of the so-called "missing s-wave repulsion" in pionic atoms can be removed by adopting a density-dependent isovector scattering amplitude suggested by Weise, which results from a density dependence of the pion decay constant; this indicates partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter, though the resulting potentials describe elastic scattering of 20 MeV pions on Ca only "quite well" and further tests with elastic scattering are desirable.

## Method

The author analyzed 60 experimental values of strong interaction level shifts, widths, and upper level yields for pionic atoms from 16O to U, including deeply bound 1s and 2p states in 205Pb, using a phenomenological optical potential. Fits were performed with the p-wave parameters fixed at free pion-nucleon values (with Lorentz-Lorenz parameter ξ=1), varying only the s-wave parameters (b0, b1, ReB0, ImB0) and the quadratic absorptive term C0. The density-dependent isovector amplitude b1(ρ) = b1(0)/(1-2.3ρ) from Weise (2001) was introduced into the potential, using the local nuclear density ρ(r), and fits were repeated; additional fits included a relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) correction with a nucleon mass parameterization M(ρ)/M(0) = 1/(1 + aρ) with a=2.7 fm3.

## Key numbers

- ImB0 was found to be remarkably constant at 0.056 mπ⁻⁴ across fits.
- With the conventional potential, ReB0 was repulsive and large, approximately -3 times ImB0 (ReB0 ≈ -0.168 mπ⁻⁴).
- The parameter b1 was found to be more than 35% larger in absolute value than its free pion-nucleon value.
- For 40Ca, the b1² term contributed as much as 35% of the real potential.
- With the density-dependent b1 prescription (σN=50 MeV), ReB0 became approximately -ImB0 (ReB0 ≈ -0.056 mπ⁻⁴).
- With σN=65 MeV, the empirical b0 and b1 parameters became consistent with free pion-nucleon values.
- All five potentials yielded values between 34 and 39 MeV for the real potential at the center of 208Pb, and between 12.4 and 13.7 MeV at the 50% density point.
- For elastic scattering of 19.5 MeV pions on Ca, adjusting ReB0 by 0.02 mπ⁻⁴ improved agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pionic atom data from the comprehensive review by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997), which identified the "missing s-wave repulsion" as a large repulsive ReB0 term (3 to 5 times larger than ImB0) that contradicted theoretical expectations. The paper implements a density-dependent isovector amplitude proposed by Weise (2001) based on partial chiral symmetry restoration, where the pion decay constant fπ*² = fπ² - (σN/mπ²)ρ. This approach contrasts with earlier attempts using a relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) by Gmitro, Kamalov, and others (1992) and Birbrair (1983, 1991), which modified the nucleon mass in the medium but did not bring b0 and b1 into agreement with free values. The results enable further tests through precision elastic scattering data for very low energy π± on additional nuclei, which are currently lacking beyond the single Ca dataset from Wright (1988).
