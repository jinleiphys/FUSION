# Energy Dependent Isospin Asymmetry in Mean-Field Dynamics

> The Lagrangian density of relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with non-linear derivative (NLD) interactions is applied to isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. We study the symmetry energy and the density and energy dependences of nucleon selfenergies. At high baryon densities a soft symmetry energy is obtained. The energy dependence of the isovector selfenergy suppresses the Lane-type optical potential with increasing energy and predicts a $ρ$-meson induced mass splitting between protons and neutrons in isospin asymmetric matter.

## Key claim

The non-linear derivative (NLD) extension of relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory predicts a soft symmetry energy at high baryon densities and an energy-dependent suppression of the Lane-type optical potential, but these results depend on the specific choice of the cut-off parameter \(\Lambda = 770\) MeV and the exponential form of the non-linear operator.

## Method

The authors applied the NLD Lagrangian density, which introduces exponential momentum-dependent form factors into the standard RMF meson-nucleon vertices, to infinite isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation. They solved the coupled set of meson field equations self-consistently at fixed baryon density and isospin asymmetry, then determined single-particle energies by numerically solving the dispersion relation for protons and neutrons. The cut-off parameter \(\Lambda\) was fixed to 770 MeV by fitting the empirically known energy dependence of the Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential in symmetric matter.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off parameter \(\Lambda = 770\) MeV, fixed from the energy dependence of the Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential in symmetric nuclear matter.
- \(\rho NN\) coupling constant \(g_{\rho} = 3\), close to its universal value from the one-boson-exchange model.
- The NLD model produces a soft symmetry energy at high baryon densities, in contrast to the stiff symmetry energy of the standard Walecka model.
- The energy dependence of the isovector selfenergy suppresses the Lane-type optical potential with increasing energy.

## Context

This work extends the NLD model previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter and proton-nucleus scattering (Gaitanos and Kaskulov, 2011) into the isovector channel, addressing the long-standing controversy over the stiffness of the symmetry energy at high densities. It contrasts with standard RMF models that predict a stiff symmetry energy (Kubis and Kutschera, 1997; Liu et al., 2002) and with density-dependent coupling models that predict a soft symmetry energy (Typel and Wolter, 1999). The model also provides a mechanism for \(\rho\)-meson induced mass splitting between protons and neutrons in asymmetric matter, which has been debated in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Fuchs et al., 2001) and relativistic Hartree-Fock models (Long et al., 2006).
