# Tides in merging neutron stars: Consistency of the GW170817 event with experimental data on finite nuclei

> The agreement of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) deduced from the GW170817 based tidal deformability with the one obtained from empirical data on microscopic nuclei is examined. It is found that suitably chosen experimental data on isoscalar and isovector modes of nuclear excitations together with the observed maximum neutron star mass constrain the EoS which displays a very good congruence with the GW170817 inspired one. The giant resonances in nuclei are found to be instrumental in limiting the tidal deformability parameter and the radius of neutron star in somewhat narrower bounds. At the 1$σ$ level, the values of the canonical tidal deformability $Λ_{1.4}$ and the neutron star radius $R_{1.4}$ come out to be $267\pm144$ and $11.6\pm1.0$ km, respectively.

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state (EoS) constrained by experimental data on isoscalar and isovector giant resonances in finite nuclei, together with the observed maximum neutron star mass, shows very good agreement with the EoS deduced from the GW170817 tidal deformability, but this conclusion is model-dependent as the EoS is taken as a smooth function of density and avoids phase transitions to exotic matter.

## Method

The authors used 28 Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) to calculate neutron star properties and tested them against experimental data for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid energy, isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) peak energy, and dipole polarizability in 208Pb, along with the observed lower limit on the maximum neutron star mass. They then performed a new chi-squared fit to a wider set of data including binding energies and charge radii of spherical magic nuclei, ISGMR energies of 208Pb, 90Zr, and 120Sn, and dipole polarizabilities of 208Pb, 48Ca, 68Ni, and 120Sn, together with the maximum neutron star mass constraint, to produce a new EDF called SkLambda267.

## Key numbers

- Canonical tidal deformability Lambda_{1.4} = 267 +/- 144 at the 1-sigma level (267 +/- 236 at 90% confidence level)
- Canonical neutron star radius R_{1.4} = 11.6 +/- 1.0 km
- Maximum neutron star mass M_NS^max = 2.04 +/- 0.15 solar masses
- Symmetry energy coefficient J_0 = 31.4 +/- 3.1 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L_0 = 41.1 +/- 18.2 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility K_0 = 230.2 +/- 6.4 MeV
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.162 +/- 0.002 fm^{-3}
- Energy per nucleon at saturation e_0 = 16.04 +/- 0.20 MeV
- Neutron-skin thickness for 208Pb = 0.15 +/- 0.05 fm
- Effective mass m_0^*/m = 0.70 +/- 0.05
- Isospin splitting of effective mass Delta m_0^*/delta = -0.25 +/- 0.35

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the connection between laboratory nuclear data and neutron star properties, building on prior studies such as Fattoyev (2018) which used relativistic mean field models, and Annala (2018) which generated generic families of EoSs. The authors show that only three of 28 Skyrme EDFs (SLy2, SLy4, and KDE0-J34) satisfy all constraints from giant resonances and the neutron star mass limit, and their new SkLambda267 EoS yields tidal deformability and radius values consistent with the GW170817 analysis of Abbott (2018b). The results enable tighter constraints on the nuclear matter EoS from combined nuclear physics and gravitational wave data, though the authors note that future LIGO-Virgo detections may help identify possible phase transitions in neutron star cores.
