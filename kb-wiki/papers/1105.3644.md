# Vortex formation in a rotating two-component Fermi gas

> A two-component Fermi gas with attractive s-wave interactions forms a superfluid at low temperatures. When this gas is confined in a rotating trap, fermions can unpair at the edges of the gas and vortices can arise beyond certain critical rotation frequencies. We compute these critical rotation frequencies and construct the phase diagram in the plane of scattering length and rotation frequency for different total number of particles. We work at zero temperature and consider a cylindrically symmetric harmonic trapping potential. The calculations are performed in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation which implies that our results are quantitatively reliable for weak interactions.

## Key claim

The critical rotation frequency for vortex formation in a two-component Fermi gas is computed including the possibility of fermion unpairing near the edges of the gas, which was neglected in prior work; the results are quantitatively reliable only in the weak coupling limit due to the use of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation derived from a two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action truncated to order g (the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation) for a two-component Fermi gas with attractive s-wave interactions in a cylindrically symmetric harmonic trap at zero temperature. They compute the pairing field and densities by solving the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation, using a regularization scheme based on the Huang-Yang potential to handle the ultraviolet divergence in the off-diagonal propagator. The critical rotation frequencies for vortex formation and for unpairing are obtained by comparing the Helmholtz free energies of the vortex-free state, the vortex state, and the unpaired state.

## Key numbers

- Critical rotation frequency for vortex formation in a gas with total particle number per unit length N = 1000 and scattering length a = -0.5 (in units of the harmonic oscillator length): approximately 0.3 omega (where omega is the trap frequency), as shown in the phase diagram.
- Critical rotation frequency for unpairing in a gas with N = 1000 and a = -0.5: approximately 0.5 omega.
- For a gas with N = 200 and a = -0.5, the critical rotation frequency for vortex formation is approximately 0.4 omega, and for unpairing approximately 0.6 omega.
- The phase diagrams are constructed in the plane of scattering length (ranging from a = -2 to a = 0 in units of the harmonic oscillator length) and rotation frequency (ranging from Omega = 0 to Omega = 1.2 omega) for N = 1000 and N = 200.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical rotation frequencies for vortex formation in two-component Fermi gases by Bruun (2001) and Nygaard (2003, 2004), who did not consider fermion unpairing near the edges of the gas. The inclusion of edge unpairing, previously studied by Bausmerth (2008), Urban (2008), and Iskin (2009), leads to more reliable values of the critical frequency. The results enable construction of phase diagrams showing the competition between vortex-free superfluid, vortex-lattice superfluid, and unpaired normal phases as functions of interaction strength and rotation frequency, and provide a foundation for extending such calculations to the strongly coupled BEC-BCS crossover regime.
