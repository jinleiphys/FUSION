# Transverse energy-momentum tensor distributions in polarized nucleons

> We complete our study of the relativistic spatial distributions of the energy-momentum tensor inside polarized nucleons within the quantum phase-space formalism. In the present work, we focus on the components of the energy-momentum tensor involving at least one transverse index. We also explore the multipole structure of the transverse distributions in a moving nucleon. In the infinite-momentum frame, we show that the formalism reproduces the standard light-front distributions, including those with a ``bad'' component, and explains the origin of their structure.

## Key claim
The paper completes the study of relativistic spatial distributions of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) inside polarized nucleons by analyzing the components involving at least one transverse index, showing that in the infinite-momentum frame the quantum phase-space formalism reproduces standard light-front distributions including those with a "bad" component, but only for elastic frames where the nucleon has non-vanishing average longitudinal momentum.

## Method
The authors used the quantum phase-space formalism to define two-dimensional relativistic spatial distributions of the EMT via Fourier transforms of elastic-frame matrix elements, applying Lorentz boosts and Wigner spin rotations to the transverse Breit-frame amplitudes. They classified the normalized EMT distributions into scalar, vector, and tensor components based on transformation properties under rotations about the z-axis, and analyzed the multipole structure of transverse distributions for both longitudinal and transverse nucleon polarizations. The analysis was performed for arbitrary nucleon momentum P_z, interpolating between the Breit frame (P_z=0) and the infinite-momentum frame (P_z to infinity).

## Key numbers
- The total orbital angular momentum about the z-axis is given by L_z = sigma^3_{s's} [J(0) - S(0)], where J(0)=1/2 from Poincare symmetry
- The total orbital energy flux about the z-axis is given by bar L_z = sigma^3_{s's} [J(0) + S(0)]
- The total angular momentum and intrinsic spin about the z-axis are J_z = sigma^3_{s's} J(0) and S_z = sigma^3_{s's} S(0), respectively
- The integrated isotropic stress over the transverse plane equals delta_{s's} F_0(0), and summing over all partons the total isotropic stress vanishes due to the von Laue condition
- The multipole form factor E(0)=1, J(0)=1/2, and F_0(Q^2)+(tau/2)F_2(Q^2)=0 are constraints from Poincare symmetry

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of polarized nucleon EMT distributions (Won 2025dgc), which only considered components without transverse indices, and the unpolarized EMT study (Lorce 2018egm). It builds on the quantum phase-space formalism previously applied to electromagnetic current (Lorce 2020onh, Kim 2021kum, Chen 2022smg), axial-vector current (Chen 2024oxx, Chen 2024ksq), and scalar EMT distributions. The results enable precise mapping of nucleon structure for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and provide a framework for understanding spin-orbit correlations and the chiral structure of hadrons.
