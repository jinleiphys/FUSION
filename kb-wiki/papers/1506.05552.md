# Equation of state for neutron stars with hyperons and quarks in relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation

> We construct the equation of state (EoS) for neutron stars explicitly including hyperons and quarks. Using the quark-meson coupling model with relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, the EoS for hadronic matter is derived by taking into account the strange ($σ^{\ast}$ and $ϕ$) mesons as well as the light non-strange ($σ$, $ω$, $\vecπ$ and $\vecρ$) mesons. Relevant coupling constants are determined to reproduce the experimental data of nuclear matter and hypernuclei in SU(3) flavor symmetry. For quark matter, we employ the MIT bag model with one-gluon-exchange interaction, and Gibbs criteria for chemical equilibrium in the phase transition from hadrons to quarks. We find that the strange vector ($ϕ$) meson and the Fock contribution make the hadronic EoS stiff, and that the maximum mass of a neutron star can be consistent with the observed mass of heavy neutron stars even if the coexistence of hadrons and quarks takes place in the core. However, in the present calculation the transition to pure quark matter does not occur in stable neutron stars. Furthermore, the lower bound of the critical chemical potential of the quark-hadron transition at zero temperature turns out to be around 1.5 GeV in order to be consistent with the recent observed neutron star data.

## Key claim

The authors construct an equation of state for neutron stars that can satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint from recent astrophysical observations even when hyperons and quarks are present in the core, but only if the strange vector (phi) meson and Fock exchange contributions are included to keep the hadronic EoS stiff; the transition to pure quark matter does not occur in stable neutron stars within this calculation.

## Method

The hadronic EoS was derived using the chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) model within relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation, including both strange (sigma* and phi) and non-strange (sigma, omega, pi, rho) mesons with coupling constants determined from nuclear matter and hypernuclei data under SU(3) flavor symmetry. For quark matter, the MIT bag model with one-gluon-exchange interaction was employed, and the hadron-to-quark phase transition was treated using Gibbs criteria for chemical equilibrium with a density-dependent bag constant.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.1 MeV at normal nuclear density n0 = 0.155 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy: E_sym = 32.5 MeV
- Incompressibility in SU(3) case: slightly smaller than SU(6) case, standing closer to the range K0 = 240 +/- 20 MeV
- Hyperon potentials at normal density: U_Lambda^(N) = -28 MeV, U_Sigma^(N) = +30 MeV
- Lower bound of critical chemical potential for quark-hadron transition at zero temperature: around 1.5 GeV
- Observed neutron star masses used as constraints: PSR J1614-2230 with 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses and PSR J0348+0432 with 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Bag constant parameters: B0 = 400 MeV fm^-3, B_infinity = 50 MeV fm^-3
- Current quark masses: m_u(d) = 5 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- Free baryon masses: M_N = 939 MeV, M_Lambda = 1116 MeV, M_Sigma = 1193 MeV, M_Xi = 1318 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where previous EoS with hyperons became too soft to support 2 solar mass neutron stars, extending earlier RHF calculations by Miyatsu (2011) and Katayama (2012) using the QMC model. The approach contrasts with recent calculations by Bonanno (2011), Lenzi (2012), and Orsaria (2013) that required strong vector interactions between quarks to reach 2 solar masses, as the present work uses the MIT bag model without such strong vector interactions. The results enable future studies of hybrid star properties where hadrons and quarks coexist in the core, while establishing that pure quark matter cores are not supported in this framework.
