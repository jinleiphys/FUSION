# In-Medium $π-π$ Correlations in the '$σ$-Meson'-Channel

> The present status of the in-medium invariant $π-π$ mass distribution in the sigma-meson channel around the threshold is reviewed from the theory side and contrasted with recent experiments on $(π,2π)$ knock-out reactions. A preliminary investigation indicates that the strongly target-mass dependent invariant mass enhancement of the two pions can be explained theoretically. A more refined reaction theory is needed to confirm this result. In the theoretical description, based on the linear sigma model, emphasis is put on constraints from chiral symmetry.

# In-Medium π-π Correlations in the 'σ-Meson'-Channel

## Key claim

The strongly target-mass dependent enhancement of the invariant π-π mass distribution near threshold observed in (π,2π) knock-out experiments can be explained theoretically by in-medium modifications of the σ-meson propagator and π-π T-matrix, but only if a more refined reaction theory at finite total three-momentum is developed to confirm this preliminary result.

## Method

The authors use the linear σ-model at leading order in a 1/N expansion to compute the in-medium π-π T-matrix and σ-meson propagator, incorporating chiral symmetry constraints through Ward identities. Pions are coupled to Δ-hole and particle-hole excitations in cold nuclear matter including short-range correlations via Migdal parameters, and the resulting in-medium π-π correlations are inserted as final-state interactions into the existing tree-level reaction model of Oset and Vicente-Vacas for (π,2π) knock-out reactions. A simplified "toy model" with a modified pion dispersion relation (ω_π(q) = √(m_π² + γ q²), γ between 0.4 and 0.8) is used to approximate the in-medium effects at finite pair three-momentum.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum σ-meson mass distribution shows a very broad peak roughly 500 MeV wide.
- The form factor parameters fitted to ππ phase shifts are: g = 0.9, q_d = 1.0 GeV, α = 3.
- The leading-order 1/N expansion gives a scattering length a_0^0 = (9/(32π)) (m_π/f_π²) for N=3 physical charges, compared to the Weinberg value a_0^0 = (7/(32π)) (m_π/f_π²).
- For the phenomenological model including t- and u-channel corrections, the bare σ mass is m_σ = 1.0 GeV and the cutoff parameter is Q_d = 8 m_π.
- The in-medium toy model uses γ values from 0.8 down to 0.4 depending on density.
- The in-medium invariant mass of a pion pair is related to the vacuum invariant mass by: M̃_ππ = M_ππ - (1/γ - 1) P², where P is the total three-momentum of the pair.
- The theoretical curves reproduce experimental data with γ in the range 0.6 to 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological models (Schuck et al., 1995; Aouissat et al., 1995) that coupled a bare σ meson to the ππ decay channel and found attractive downward shifts of the σ mass distribution driving instability near saturation density. It contrasts with earlier approaches by implementing chiral symmetry constraints (Aouissat et al., 1997) that generate repulsion below the 2m_π threshold, preventing unphysical invasion of strength into that region. The paper directly addresses the experimental findings of Grion et al. (1998) and Bonutti et al. (1998) on (π,2π) knock-out reactions at TRIUMF, which show a target-mass dependent threshold enhancement distinct from the shadowing effects observed in higher-energy (p,2π) reactions at SATURNE (Pluta et al., 1996). The next step requires a full reaction theory at finite total three-momentum to replace the toy model and confirm whether the in-medium π-π correlations can quantitatively explain the experimental data.
