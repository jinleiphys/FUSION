# On the Electric Dipole Polarizability of the Three-Hadron Bound System

> A simple analytical expression for the electric dipole polarizability of the three-hadron bound system having only one stable bound state has been derived neglecting by the higher orbital components of the off-shell three-body transition matrix at the energy of the bound state. As a case in point, we have estimated the electric dipole polarizability of the triton, using a cluster triton wave function and the Hulthen potential to describe the related p-n and n-d bound states.

## Key claim

The authors derive a simple analytical expression for the electric dipole polarizability of a three-hadron bound system with only one stable bound state, under the explicit assumption that higher orbital components of the off-shell three-body transition matrix at the bound-state energy can be neglected. This approximation allows the polarizability to be expressed solely in terms of first partial derivatives of the bound-state wave function with respect to Jacobi momentum variables.

## Method

Starting from the Watson-Feshbach formalism for the effective interaction between a charged particle and a three-body complex, the authors express the electric dipole polarizability in terms of the ground-state wave function and the truncated Green's operator. By decomposing the three-body transition matrix into pole and smooth parts and neglecting higher orbital components of the smooth part at the bound-state energy, they reduce the expression to a form involving only the free Green's operator and the bound-state wave function. They then apply this formalism to the triton using a cluster (deuteron + neutron) model wave function, with the Hulthén potential describing both the p-n and n-d interactions, and compute the polarizability by fitting potential parameters to experimental binding energies and scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron electric dipole polarizability from the Hulthén potential fitted to B_d and triplet p-n scattering length: α_E^H(²H) = 0.6442 fm³.
* Deuteron electric dipole polarizability from the Hulthén potential fitted to B_d and asymptotic normalization constant A_S: α_E^H(²H) = 0.6292 fm³.
* Triton electric dipole polarizability from the Hulthén potential fitted to B_d, ³a_pn, B_n, and ²a_nd: α_E^H(³H) = 0.235 fm³, with direct term α_E^0 = 0.109 fm³ and exchange term α_E^ex = 0.126 fm³.
* Triton electric dipole polarizability from the Hulthén potential fitted to B_d and A_S(²H) only: α_E^H(³H) = 0.225 fm³.
* Experimental deuteron binding energy used: B_d = 2.224575(9) MeV (Ref. 27).
* Experimental triton binding energy used: B_t = 8.481855(13) MeV (Ref. 29).
* Experimental doublet n-d scattering length used: ²a_nd = 0.65(4) fm (Ref. 30).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism for two-body bound complexes (Kharchenko and Shadchin, 1993, 1997) to three-body systems, and follows their earlier prediction for the lambda hypertriton polarizability (Kharchenko and Kharchenko, 2008). The derived expression (Eq. 21) provides a simpler alternative to full Faddeev calculations, enabling estimates using cluster wave functions. The result for the triton (0.235 fm³) is compared with calculations for the mirror nucleus ³He (Stetcu et al., 2009; Pachucki and Moro, 2007) and with the experimental ³He values from direct measurement (Goeckner et al., 1991) and photoabsorption data (Rinker, 1976). The authors note that no direct measurement of the triton polarizability exists, and recommend future calculations using more realistic Faddeev wave functions such as those generated with the Reid soft-core potential (Hajduk et al., 1980).
