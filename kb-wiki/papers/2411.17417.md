# Dissociation and regeneration of charmonia within microscopic Langevin simulations

> We present a classical model to study the formation of charmonia, as well as dissociation and regeneration processes of heavy-quark bound states in the quark gluon plasma using Langevin simulations. The charm and anticharm quarks are described as Brownian particles in the background medium of light quarks and gluons and interact among them over a Coulomb-like screened potential to form bound states, which can dissociate again due to interactions with the medium. Box simulations at fixed temperature and volume are used to verify that the system reaches the expected thermal distribution in the equilibrium limit and to test bound state properties. The medium evolution is then parametrized by a boost-invariant fireball. In this configuration, the elliptic flow of charm and anticharm quarks as well as of charmonia is studied at RHIC and LHC energies.

# Dissociation and regeneration of charmonia within microscopic Langevin simulations

## Key claim
The authors present a classical Langevin-based model that can simultaneously describe charmonium dissociation and regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma, but only after tuning the strong coupling constant to alpha_s = 0.7 to match the statistical hadronization model (SHM) predictions for charmonium yields at equilibrium.

## Method
Charm and anticharm quarks are treated as Brownian particles propagating through a quark-gluon plasma background, with their dynamics governed by relativistic Langevin equations. The heavy-quark interaction is modeled via a complex Coulomb-like screened potential (Blaizot formalism), where the real part provides attractive binding and the imaginary part accounts for medium-induced dissociation. Box simulations at fixed temperature and volume verify equilibration, then the medium is evolved using a boost-invariant elliptic fireball parametrization to study elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- Strong coupling constant tuned to alpha_s = 0.7 at T_c = 160 MeV, with running coupling parametrized by C = 0.76
- Drag coefficient cut-off Lambda = 4 GeV used in the calculation of gamma
- Box simulations performed at T = 160 MeV with volume V = (8 fm)^3 and V = (10 fm)^3
- Charm quark elliptic flow studied at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC, 20-40% centrality) and sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (LHC, 0-20% and 20-40% centrality)
- Inclusive J/psi v_2 reaches up to 11.6% for 20-40% centrality at LHC, matching ALICE data within uncertainties

## Context
This work extends the classical Langevin approach to heavy-quark dynamics (Oei 2024) by incorporating bound-state formation and dissociation through a screened potential, building on the formalism of Blaizot. It contrasts with the statistical hadronization model (SHM) of Andronic (2003, 2017, 2021), which treats charmonium production as instantaneous at freeze-out, by providing a dynamical description throughout the QGP evolution. The results enable future calculations of the nuclear modification factor and extension to the bottomonium sector, as well as incorporation of primordial charmonia for more direct comparison with experimental data from ALICE and RHIC.
