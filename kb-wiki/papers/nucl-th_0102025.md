# Non-diffractive mechanisms in the $ϕ$ meson photoproduction on nucleons

> We examine the non-diffractive mechanisms in the $ϕ$ meson photoproduction from threshold up to a few GeV using an effective Lagrangian in a constituent quark model. The new data from CLAS at large angles can be consistently accounted for in terms of {\it s}- and {\it u}-channel processes. Isotopic effects arising from the reactions $γp\to ϕp$ and $γn\to ϕn$, are investigated by comparing the cross sections and polarized beam asymmetries. Our result highlights an experimental means of studying non-diffractive mechanisms in $ϕ$ meson photoproduction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that non-diffractive s- and u-channel processes, described via an effective quark-meson coupling in a constituent quark model, can consistently account for the large-angle phi meson photoproduction data from CLAS, but only up to a few GeV and with the caveat that the Pomeron exchange model used for the diffractive background is extrapolated from higher energies and may not be uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with an effective Lagrangian for the phi-qq vertex, including vector and tensor couplings. They calculate s- and u-channel amplitudes for phi photoproduction on protons and neutrons, including nucleon pole terms and a complete set of low-lying baryon resonances (up to n=2 in the harmonic oscillator basis). The diffractive contribution is modeled via a t-channel Pomeron exchange following Donnachie and Landshoff (1987), and t-channel pion exchange is also included but found to be small. The model parameters a and b for the phi-qq coupling are fitted to differential cross section data at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV and 3.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The fit to data at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV and 3.6 GeV yields a = 0.241 +/- 0.105 and b' = -0.458 +/- 0.091 for the phi-qq vector and tensor couplings.
* The ratio a(phi)/a(omega) = -0.096 to -0.087 is close to the SU(3) symmetry value of -tan(3.7 deg) = -0.065 for the deviation from ideal omega-phi mixing.
* The phi meson decay constant used in the Pomeron exchange is f_phi = 164.76 MeV, determined from Gamma(phi -> e+e-) = 1.32 keV.
* The Pomeron trajectory parameters are epsilon = 0.08 and alpha' = 0.25 GeV^{-2}, with a cutoff mu_0 = 1.2 GeV for the off-shell quark form factor.
* The pion exchange couplings are g^2_{pi NN}/4pi = 14 and g^2_{phi pi gamma} = 0.143.
* The CLAS data cover momentum transfers above 1.5 (GeV/c)^2 with center-of-mass energy W between 2.66 and 2.86 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-diffractive phi photoproduction by Zhao et al. (1999) and addresses the controversy between approaches that invoke a strangeness component in the nucleon (Henley et al., 1992; Titov et al., 1997-1998) and those using an OZI-evading phi NN coupling (Williams, 1998). The paper shows that s- and u-channel processes dominate at large angles and that the u-channel nucleon pole term is particularly important, consistent with findings by Laget (2000). The isotopic comparison between gamma p and gamma n reactions provides a new experimental handle to isolate non-diffractive mechanisms, and the results suggest that forward-angle kinematics may be the cleanest region for studying possible strangeness content in the nucleon.
