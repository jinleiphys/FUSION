# Real-time measurements of solar $pp$ neutrinos using $^{131}$Xe

> Various large-scale experiments for double beta decay or dark matter are based on xenon. Current experiments are on the tonne scale but there are also ideas to aim for even larger sizes in the future. Here we study the potential of the isotope $^{131}$Xe to allow to make real-time measurements of solar $pp$ neutrinos, besides classical neutrino-electron scattering. Improved nuclear models are used to determine the cross-section of neutrinos on $^{131}$Xe. The present calculations deviate significantly from the previous ones due to updated estimates for the excited-state contributions. The updated capture-rate estimate for $pp$ neutrinos is $(4.47\pm 0.09)\ \rm SNU$ and for all solar neutrinos $(80\pm 21)\ \rm SNU$, with neutrino survival probabilities taken into account. Depending on the amount of Xe, solar $pp$ neutrinos might be measured with rates of 100 per day, thus allowing to monitor them in real time for a long period.

## Key claim

The isotope 131Xe could enable real-time measurements of solar pp neutrinos in large-scale xenon detectors, with an updated capture-rate estimate of (4.47 ± 0.09) SNU for pp neutrinos and (80 ± 21) SNU for all solar neutrinos, but the total rate is much larger than the old estimate of 20.0 SNU primarily because the 8B neutrino contribution is now predicted to be over ten times larger than previously thought, highlighting vast uncertainties in excited-state contributions.

## Method

The authors used the Donnelly-Walecka method for semi-leptonic nuclear processes to compute neutrino-nucleus cross sections on 131Xe. For low-energy neutrinos (pp, pep, 7Be, CNO) they performed shell-model calculations using the NuShellX@MSU code with the sn100pn effective Hamiltonian in a model space of orbitals 0g7/2, 1d, 2s, and 0h11/2, adopting experimental energies for the lowest excited states and fixing the ground-state-to-ground-state cross section from the electron-capture half-life of 131Cs. For higher-energy 8B and hep neutrinos they used cross sections from the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM) as calculated by Pirinen et al. (2019), with an effective axial-vector coupling gA = 1.0.

## Key numbers

- pp neutrino capture rate: (4.47 ± 0.09) SNU, about 14% lower than the previous estimate of 5.2 SNU
- Total solar neutrino capture rate: (80 ± 21) SNU, much larger than the old estimate of 20.0 SNU from Georgadze et al.
- 8B neutrino contribution: 62 ± 19 SNU, over ten times the previous estimate of 4.6 SNU
- 7Be neutrino contribution: 10.4 ± 1.4 SNU, compared to the previous estimate of 9.6 SNU
- Threshold for charged-current neutrino capture on 131Xe: 355 ± 5 keV
- Natural abundance of 131Xe: 21.2%
- Half-life of 131Cs: 9.69 days
- Ground-state-to-ground-state reduced transition probability B(GT) = 0.0178, deduced from the electron-capture half-life with an estimated uncertainty of 2%
- Solar pp neutrinos might be measured with rates of 100 per day depending on the amount of xenon

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Georgadze et al. for neutrino capture on 131Xe, using improved nuclear models and updated solar fluxes from the BS05(OP) solar model by Bahcall (2005) and neutrino survival probabilities from Agostini (2018). The results show that large-scale xenon detectors currently operating (Xenon1T, LUX-ZEPLIN) and future detectors like DARWIN (50 tons of xenon) could parasitically measure solar pp neutrinos in real time, complementing the radiochemical measurements from Homestake, GALLEX, GNO, and SAGE, and the real-time neutrino-electron scattering measurements from Borexino.
