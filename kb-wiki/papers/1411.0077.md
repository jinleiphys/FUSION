# Gauge invariance, Lorentz covariance and canonical quantization in nucleon structure studies

> There are different operators of quark and gluon momenta, orbital angular momenta, and gluon spin in the nucleon structure study. The precise meaning of these operators are studied based on gauge invariance, Lorentz covariance and canonical quantization rule. The advantage and disadvantage of different definitions are analyzed. A gauge invariant canonical decomposition of the total momentum and angular momentum into quark and gluon parts is suggested based on the decomposition of the gauge potential into gauge invariant (covariant) physical part and gauge dependent pure gauge part. Challenges to this proposal are answered. \keywords{Physical and pure gauge potentials; Gauge invariant canonical quark and gluon momenta, orbital angular momenta and spins; Homogeneous and non-homogeneous Lorentz transformations; Gauge invariant decomposition and gauge invariant extension; Classical and quantum measurements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a gauge-invariant canonical decomposition of nucleon momentum and angular momentum into quark and gluon parts is possible and physically meaningful, contrary to claims that such operators are nonlocal, not Lorentz covariant, or not measurable. The authors assert that their decomposition, based on separating the gauge potential into a gauge-invariant physical part and a gauge-dependent pure gauge part, resolves these issues and correctly describes the measured quark momentum in deep inelastic scattering.

## Method

The authors analyze existing decompositions of nucleon spin and momentum, including the Jaffe-Manohar (JM) decomposition and Ji's gauge-invariant decomposition, identifying their respective shortcomings in gauge invariance or satisfaction of the canonical angular momentum algebra. They propose a new decomposition that splits the gauge potential \(A_\mu\) into a physical part \(A_{\mu,\text{phys}}\) (satisfying a transverse wave condition) and a pure gauge part \(A_{\mu,\text{pure}}\) (with vanishing field strength), then construct gauge-invariant canonical momentum and orbital angular momentum operators using \(D_{\text{pure}} = \nabla - ieA_{\text{pure}}\). The authors respond to critiques regarding nonlocality, Lorentz covariance, and uniqueness by invoking the Aharonov-Bohm effect, non-homogeneous Lorentz transformations, and the Helmholtz theorem.

## Key numbers

* Quark spin contribution to the nucleon spin is only about one third of its known value, while the contribution from polarized gluons seems to be quite small (no explicit numerical precision given).
* The paper states that the vector potential is \((\alpha)^2_{\text{em}}\) suppressed when compared to the canonical momentum, but argues this does not justify approximate gauge invariance.
* The paper references the NSFC grant numbers 1175215, 11175088, and 11035006 as funding sources.

## Context

This work extends the debate on nucleon spin decomposition initiated by Jaffe and Manohar (1990) and Ji (1997), offering an alternative to Ji's gauge-invariant decomposition which the authors argue does not satisfy the canonical angular momentum algebra. The paper responds to specific objections raised by Ji and Wakamatsu, and aims to enable a consistent theoretical framework for interpreting parton distribution measurements in deep inelastic scattering, particularly the quark momentum distribution. The authors also connect their approach to the description of the Aharonov-Bohm effect and to established results in optics regarding photon spin and orbital angular momentum.
