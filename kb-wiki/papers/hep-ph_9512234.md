# Chiral symmetry and the reaction $γp \to π^0 p$

> We analyze the new threshold data for neutral pion photoproduction off protons in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that large loop corrections are needed to understand the S--wave multipole $E_{0+}$ and that all pertinent low--energy constants can be understood within the framework of resonance exchange saturation. Previous inconsistencies in the description of this reaction in the threshold region are resolved.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the threshold data for neutral pion photoproduction off protons can be consistently described within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, resolving previous inconsistencies, but only when large loop corrections are included and the low-energy constants are constrained by resonance exchange saturation rather than left completely free.

## Method

The authors analyzed the new TAPS collaboration data for the reaction γp → π⁰p using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to order q⁴ for the S-wave multipole E₀₊ and to order q³ for the P-wave multipoles P₁, P₂, P₃. They performed fits to the differential and total cross sections, constraining the low-energy constants a₁, a₂, and bₚ by resonance exchange saturation from Δ and vector meson (ρ⁰ + ω) contributions, with the Δ off-shell parameters X, Y, Z bounded by independent constraints from the proton magnetic polarizability and πN scattering.

## Key numbers

- E₀₊ at π⁰p threshold: -1.16 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ (theory), compared to experimental value -1.31 ± 0.08 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ from Fuchs et al.
- E₀₊ at π⁺n threshold: -0.44 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ (theory), compared to experimental value ≈ -0.4 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ from Fuchs et al.
- Sum of S-wave low-energy constants from resonance fit: (a₁ + a₂)^(V+Δ) = 6.59 GeV⁻⁴, with vector meson contribution 2.67 GeV⁻⁴ and Δ contribution 3.92 GeV⁻⁴
- P-wave low-energy constant from resonance fit: b_P^(reso) = 13.0 GeV⁻³, consistent with resonance saturation estimate b_P^(reso) = 9.7 + 3.1 = 12.8 GeV⁻³
- P-wave slopes at threshold: P₁/|q| = 0.480 GeV⁻², P₂/|q| = -0.512 GeV⁻², P₃/|q| = 0.544 GeV⁻²
- χ²/dof for the resonance fit: 2.21

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework developed in Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) for neutral pion photoproduction, applying it to the new TAPS data from Fuchs et al. which showed discrepancies with the older Mainz data of Beck et al. The paper resolves the apparent inconsistency between free fits and resonance-constrained fits that had been seen with the older data, showing that the earlier mismatch was an artefact. The results enable extension of this formalism to electroproduction for comparison with new data from NIKHEF and MAMI, and the predicted small photon asymmetry Σ near threshold will be testable in polarization measurements at MAMI.
