# Chiral symmetry and the reaction $Оіp \to ПЂ^0 p$

> We analyze the new threshold data for neutral pion photoproduction off protons in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that large loop corrections are needed to understand the S--wave multipole $E_{0+}$ and that all pertinent low--energy constants can be understood within the framework of resonance exchange saturation. Previous inconsistencies in the description of this reaction in the threshold region are resolved.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the threshold data for neutral pion photoproduction off protons can be consistently described within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, resolving previous inconsistencies, but only when large loop corrections are included and the low-energy constants are constrained by resonance exchange saturation rather than left completely free.

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework developed in Bernard, Kaiser, and MeiГџner (1995) for neutral pion photoproduction, applying it to the new TAPS data from Fuchs et al. which showed discrepancies with the older Mainz data of Beck et al. The paper resolves the apparent inconsistency between free fits and resonance-constrained fits that had been seen with the older data, showing that the earlier mismatch was an artefact. The results enable extension of this formalism to electroproduction for comparison with new data from NIKHEF and MAMI, and the predicted small photon asymmetry ОЈ near threshold will be testable in polarization measurements at MAMI.
