# Faddeev fixed center approximation to $π\bar{K} K^*$ system and the $π_1(1600)$

> We investigate the three-body system of $π\bar{K} K^*$ by using the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equation, taking the interaction between $π$ and $\bar{K}$, $π$ and $K^*$, and $\bar{K}$ and $K^*$ from the chiral unitary approach. The study is made assuming scattering of a $π$ on a $\bar{K} K^*$ cluster, which is known to generate the $f_1(1285)$ state. The resonant structure around $1650$ MeV shows up in the modulus squared of the $π$-$(\bar{K} K^*)_{f_1(1285)}$ scattering amplitude and suggests that a $π$-$(\bar{K} K^*)_{f_1(1285)}$ state, with "exotic" quantum numbers $J^{PC} = 1^{-+}$, can be formed. This state can be identified as the observed $π_1(1600)$ resonance. We suggest that this is the origin of the present $π_1(1600)$ resonance and propose to look at the $πf_1(1285)$ mode in future experiments to clarify the issue.

# Faddeev fixed center approximation to πK̅K* system and the π₁(1600)

## Key claim

The π₁(1600) resonance with exotic quantum numbers J^PC = 1^{-+} can be dynamically generated as a π-(K̅K*)_{f₁(1285)} three-body state in the Faddeev fixed-center approximation, provided the K̅K* cluster forms the f₁(1285) resonance. The authors suggest this molecular interpretation as an alternative to hybrid, four-quark, or mixing explanations, but note that the identification depends on the stability of the peak around 1650 MeV against variations of the subtraction constants and the f₁(1285) mass.

## Method

The authors applied the fixed-center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations for the πK̅K* system, treating the K̅K* pair as a bound cluster that generates the f₁(1285) resonance. The two-body scattering amplitudes for πK̅ and πK* were taken from the chiral unitary approach as previously developed by Roca (2005), Geng (2006), and Guo (2005), with the πK̅ and πK* interactions evaluated in the isospin I=1/2 and I=3/2 sectors. The total scattering amplitude T for π-(K̅K*)_{f₁(1285)} was computed including single and double scattering contributions with s-wave projected form factors for the f₁(1285) bound state, and resonant structures were identified from peaks in |T|².

## Key numbers

- The f₁(1285) mass used as the cluster mass M_R = 1281.3 MeV (from PDG)
- The resonant structure appears around √s ≈ 1650 MeV in |T|², identified with the π₁(1600) which has PDG mass 1660 MeV
- The subtraction constants used: a_{πK*} = -1.85 with μ = 1000 MeV for I=1/2; a_{πK} = -1.38 with μ = m_K for I=1/2; a_{πK} = -4.64 with μ = m_K for I=3/2
- The cutoff parameter Λ = 990 MeV was used to regularize loop functions, with tests at Λ = 890 and 1090 MeV showing similar results
- At √s = 1660 MeV, the invariant masses of the subsystems are √s₁ = 792 MeV (πK̅) and √s₂ = 1244 MeV (πK*)
- No bump structure was found around √s ≈ 1400 MeV, suggesting π₁(1400) is not generated from this mechanism

## Context

This work extends the FCA-Faddeev approach previously applied to systems such as NK̅K (Xie, 2010), π-(Δρ) (Xie, 2011), and ρKK̅, ηKK̅, ρDD̅ (Bayar, 2013; Liang, 2013; Durkaya, 2015) to the exotic meson sector. It provides an alternative molecular interpretation for π₁(1600) that contrasts with hybrid meson calculations (Meyer, 2010; Bellantuono, 2014), four-quark state models (Chen, 2008), and molecule/four-quark mixing (Narison, 2009). The authors propose that future experimental searches for the πf₁(1285) decay mode can test this molecular origin hypothesis.
