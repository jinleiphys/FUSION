# A novel scenario for the production of antihyperons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We elaborate on our recent suggestion on antihyperon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions by means of multi-mesonic (fusion-type) reactions. It will be shown that the (rare) antihyperons are driven towards chemical equilibrium with pions, nucleons and kaons on a timescale of 1--3 fm/c in a still moderately baryon-dense hadronic environment.

## Key claim

Antihyperons in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be driven towards chemical equilibrium with pions, nucleons and kaons on a timescale of 1–3 fm/c via multi-mesonic (fusion-type) reactions, even in a moderately baryon-dense hadronic environment, contrary to the earlier claim that binary strangeness-exchange reactions alone cannot explain their observed abundances.

## Method

The author derives a master equation for antihyperon yield from an underlying Boltzmann-type transport equation, incorporating multi-mesonic reactions of the form n₁π + n₂K ↔ Ȳ + N, where n₂ counts the number of anti-strange quarks in the antihyperon and n₁ + n₂ is expected to be around 5–7. The annihilation rate Γ_Ȳ is estimated by assuming cross sections similar to p+p̅ annihilation (≈ 50 mb) and using baryon densities ρ_N ≈ 1–2 ρ₀ from the HSD transport model. The master equation is solved for an isentropic expansion with parameters characteristic of SPS energies, assuming pions, kaons, and nucleons remain in thermal and chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The annihilation timescale (Γ_Ȳ)⁻¹ is approximately 1–3 fm/c, adopting baryon density ρ_N ≈ 1–2 ρ₀ and annihilation cross section σ ≈ 50 mb.
- The net baryon density at midrapidity in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS reaches ρ_B ≈ 2 ρ₀ after about 5 fm/c from the onset of the collision.
- Antihyperons freeze out at a temperature around 150–160 MeV, corresponding to a baryon density of about 0.5–1 ρ₀, at a time of 8–10 fm/c (3–5 fm/c after the onset of the hadronic description).
- The number of pions plus kaons involved in the multi-mesonic reactions (n₁ + n₂) is expected to be around 5–7.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Greiner and Leupold (2000) on multi-mesonic antihyperon production and contradicts the earlier claim by Koch, Müller, and Rafelski (1986) that chemical equilibrium values for antihyperons cannot be explained by successive binary strangeness-exchange reactions. It also builds on the thermal model analyses of Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS by Braun-Munzinger, Heppe, and Stachel (1999) and Redlich (1999), and was partly triggered by the work of Rapp and Shuryak (2000) on pions and kaons going out of chemical equilibrium below freeze-out. The proposed mechanism enables a dynamical explanation for the chemical freeze-out point of antihyperons and suggests that present transport codes need new strategies to include multi-particle channels for direct comparison with experimental data.
