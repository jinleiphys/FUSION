# Revisiting the compatibility problem between the gauge principle and the observability of the canonical orbital angular momentum in the Landau problem

> As is widely-known, the eigen-functions of the Landau problem in the symmetric gauge are specified by two quantum numbers. The first is the familiar Landau quantum number $n$, whereas the second is the magnetic quantum number $m$, which is the eigen-value of the canonical orbital angular momentum (OAM) operator of the electron. The eigen-energies of the system depend only on the first quantum number $n$, and the second quantum number $m$ does not correspond to any direct observables. This seems natural since the canonical OAM is generally believed to be a {\it gauge-variant} quantity, and observation of a gauge-variant quantity would contradict a fundamental principle of physics called the {\it gauge principle}. In recent researches, however, Bliohk et al. analyzed the motion of helical electron beam along the direction of a uniform magnetic field, which was mostly neglected in past analyses of the Landau states. Their analyses revealed highly non-trivial $m$-dependent rotational dynamics of the Landau electron, but the problem is that their papers give an impression that the quantum number $m$ in the Landau eigen-states corresponds to a genuine observable. This compatibility problem between the gauge principle and the observability of the quantum number $m$ in the Landau eigen-states was attacked in our previous letter paper. In the present paper, we try to give more convincing answer to this delicate problem of physics, especially by paying attention not only to the {\it particle-like} aspect but also to the {\it wave-like} aspect of the Landau electron.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent observability of the canonical orbital angular momentum (OAM) quantum number \(m\) in the Landau problem, as suggested by the \(m\)-dependent splitting of helical electron beams observed by Bliokh et al., can be reconciled with the gauge principle by carefully distinguishing between the particle-like and wave-like aspects of the Landau electron, and by recognizing that the observed rotational dynamics depend on the gauge-invariant total probability current rather than on the gauge-variant canonical OAM alone.

## Method

The authors analyze the Landau problem in the symmetric gauge by expressing the Hamiltonian as a sum of a 2-dimensional harmonic oscillator and a Zeeman term, which reveals a symmetry between states with opposite signs of the magnetic quantum number \(m\). They compute the probability density and the separate canonical and gauge contributions to the probability current for Landau eigenstates with various \(n\) and \(m\) values, and compare the resulting total current distributions for states with \(m>0\) and \(m<0\). The analysis is extended to interference phenomena of two helical electron beams with different \(m\) values and to a comparison between nondiffractive Landau beams and diffractive Laguerre-Gauss beams in free space.

## Key numbers

- The eigen-energies of the Landau Hamiltonian are \(E_n = (n + 1/2) \omega_c\), where \(\omega_c = eB/m_e\) is the cyclotron frequency, and the Landau quantum number \(n\) takes non-negative integers.
- The expectation value of the mechanical OAM operator in Landau eigenstates is \(\langle L_z^{\text{mech}} \rangle = 2n + 1\).
- The three-fold splitting of the rotational angular velocity of the Landau electron is given by \(\langle \omega \rangle = 0\) for \(m < 0\), \(\langle \omega \rangle = \omega_L = \omega_c/2\) for \(m = 0\), and \(\langle \omega \rangle = \omega_c\) for \(m > 0\).
- For the state with \(n=0, m=-20\), the energy is \(E = \omega_L\); for the state with \(n=20, m=+20\), the energy is \(E = 41 \omega_L\), despite both having identical probability densities.
- The magnetic length is \(l_B = 1/\sqrt{eB}\), and the harmonic oscillator length parameter is \(b^2 = 2 l_B^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter paper (Wakamatsu et al., 2020) and their earlier studies of the Landau problem (Wakamatsu et al., 2018) and the Aharonov-Bohm effect (Wakamatsu et al., 2018). It directly addresses the observations of Bliokh et al. (2012, 2014) on the \(m\)-dependent rotational dynamics of helical electron beams in a uniform magnetic field, which appeared to challenge the gauge principle. The analysis also connects to the nucleon spin decomposition problem, where distinguishing between canonical and mechanical OAM is a key issue (Ji, 1997; Burkardt, 2013; Wakamatsu, 2015-2016). The paper enables a clearer physical interpretation of the observability of the canonical OAM in the presence of a magnetic field and reconciles it with gauge invariance by emphasizing the role of the total probability current.
