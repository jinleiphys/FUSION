# Some remarks on Coulombic effects in $pp$ and $\bar pp$ scattering and the determination of $ρ$

> We point out a very simple method for calculating the mixed Coulomb-nuclear corrections to the $pp$ and $\bar pp$ scattering amplitudes that has been missed in the extensive past work on this problem. The method expresses the correction in terms of a rapidly convergent integral involving the inverse Fourier-Bessel transform of the nuclear amplitude and a known factor containing the Coulomb phase shift with form-factor corrections. The transform can be calculated analytically for the exponential-type model nuclear amplitudes commonly used in fits to the high-energy data at small momentum transfers, and gives very accurate results for the corrections. We examine the possible effects of the Martin zero in the real part of the nuclear amplitude, and the accuracy of the Bethe-West-Yennie phase approximation for the Coulomb-nuclear corrections. We then apply the method to a redetermination of the ratio $ρ$ of the real to the imaginary parts of the forward scattering amplitude in fits to high-energy ISR data previously analyzed using an approximate version of the correction. The only significant changes relative the accuracy of those fits are at 52.8 GeV. Our method is applicable more generally, and can be used also at lower energies and for proton-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The authors present a simplified method for calculating mixed Coulomb-nuclear corrections to pp and p̅p scattering amplitudes that has been overlooked in previous work, claiming it gives very accurate results for exponential-type nuclear models and that the only significant changes in redetermining the ρ parameter from ISR data occur at 52.8 GeV.

## Method

The method expresses the mixed Coulomb-nuclear correction term as a rapidly convergent integral involving the inverse Fourier-Bessel transform of the nuclear amplitude multiplied by a known factor containing the Coulomb phase shift with form-factor corrections. For exponential-type model nuclear amplitudes commonly used in fits to high-energy data at small momentum transfers, the inverse Fourier-Bessel transform can be calculated analytically, and the remaining integral over impact parameter b converges exponentially. The authors then apply this method to redetermine the ratio ρ from fits to high-energy ISR data previously analyzed using an approximate version of the correction.

## Key numbers

- The standard proton charge form factor used is F_Q(q²) = μ⁴/(q²+μ²)² with μ² = 0.71 GeV²
- At 53 GeV, the Martin zero in the real part of the nuclear amplitude is located at q² = 0.319 GeV²
- At 8 TeV, the Martin zero is located at q² = 0.169 GeV²
- At 13 TeV, the Martin zero is located at q² = 0.156 GeV²
- The Gaussian form-factor phase 2δ_FF^Gauss differs from the standard proton form-factor phase by less than 1% for b = 10⁻⁴ GeV⁻¹ and by approximately 6% for b ≈ 2 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the long line of research on Coulomb-nuclear interference effects in pp and p̅p scattering dating back to Bethe (1958), West and Yennie (1968), and Cahn (1982), with later modifications by Kundrát and Lokajiček. The method simplifies the calculation of mixed Coulomb-nuclear corrections relative to the Cahn-Kundrát-Lokajiček approach, which involved delicate manipulations to avoid singularities from the infinite range of the Coulomb interaction. The authors demonstrate that the Martin zero in the real part of the nuclear amplitude, which Pacetti et al. suggested could account for low ρ values in TOTEM experiments at 8 and 13 TeV, has negligible effect on ρ determinations because the region of maximum sensitivity to Coulomb-nuclear interference occurs at very small q² values well below the zero location. The method is applicable more generally to lower energies and proton-nucleus scattering.
