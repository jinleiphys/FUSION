# QCD Sum Rule for 1/2- Baryons

> The masses of the flavor octet and singlet baryons with negative parity and spin 1/2 are calculated using the QCD sum rule. We find that the chiral symmetry breaking vacuum condensates cause the mass splitting of the positive and negative baryons. The present sum rule reproduces the observed masses of these baryons within 10%, and predicts the mass of the excited Xi baryon with $J^P = {1/2}^{-}$ at 1.63 GeV. We confirm that the negative-parity state, $Λ_S-$, is the ground state in the flavor singlet baryon spectrum.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule reproduces the observed masses of negative-parity flavor octet and singlet baryons within 10%, and predicts the mass of the excited Xi baryon with J^P = 1/2^- at 1.63 GeV; the chiral symmetry breaking vacuum condensates (quark condensate and mixed condensate) are identified as the cause of the mass splitting between positive- and negative-parity baryons, with the caveat that the analysis neglects next-to-leading order alpha_s corrections and higher dimensional terms.

## Method

The authors use the QCD sum rule with an "old-fashioned" correlation function defined in the rest frame (p=0) to separate contributions from positive- and negative-parity baryons, which couple to the same interpolating field. They construct sum rules for the positive- and negative-parity states separately by taking combinations A(p0)+B(p0) and A(p0)-B(p0) of the chiral even and odd parts of the correlation function, then apply a Borel transform to extract masses. The QCD parameters (quark condensate, mixed condensate, strange quark mass, and SU(3) breaking ratios) are determined by requiring the sum rules to reproduce the observed masses of N+, Lambda+, Sigma+, and Xi+ within 5% with Borel stability better than 10%.

## Key numbers

- Observed masses of negative-parity baryons are reproduced within 10% by the sum rule
- Predicted mass of the excited Xi baryon with J^P = 1/2^-: 1.63 GeV
- Up and down quark condensate determined from the sum rule: consistent with the standard value (-0.225 +/- 0.025 GeV)^3 from chiral perturbation theory
- Mixed condensate parameter m0^2 = <q-bar g sigma*G q> / <q-bar q>: consistent with 0.5-1.0 GeV^2 from baryon sum rules
- Gluon condensate fixed to <(alpha_s/pi) GG> = (0.36 GeV)^4
- SU(3) breaking ratio for quark condensates chi = <s-bar s>/<q-bar q> ~ 0.8
- Strange quark mass m_s determined from sum rules (somewhat smaller than updated analyses, but B_- masses are insensitive to m_s)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule technique for separating positive- and negative-parity nucleons (Jido, Oka 1996) to hyperons and flavor singlet baryons. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic quark model (Isgur, Karl) which does not incorporate chiral symmetry, and with earlier QCD sum rule approaches by Ioffe (1981) and by Lee and Kim (1995) that used different interpolating fields or did not include continuum contributions. The results support the interpretation that N(1535) is the chiral partner of the nucleon, analogous to the parity-doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989), and enable predictions for the spin-parity assignment of Xi(1690) as 1/2^- and for the ordering of the flavor singlet Lambda_S spectrum.
