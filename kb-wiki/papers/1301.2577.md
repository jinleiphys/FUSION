# Influence of an inhomogeneous and expanding medium on signals of the QCD phase transition

> According to a fluid dynamic expansion of the fireball we investigate how the inhomogeneity of the system influences the chiral phase transition of QCD. We compare the averaged values of the order parameter in equilibrium with that of a homogeneous system. If the temperature is averaged over a certain region of the fireball the corresponding correlation length does not diverge in an expansion with a critical point.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the temperature of an expanding heavy-ion collision fireball is averaged over an inhomogeneous fluid-dynamic volume, the correlation length does not diverge at the critical point, but instead remains finite and grows only to about 1.5 fm. This smearing effect is a direct consequence of the system's spatial inhomogeneity, which the abstract's phrase "does not diverge in an expansion with a critical point" glosses over by not specifying the averaging procedure.

## Method

The authors used ideal fluid dynamics to simulate the expansion of a fireball created in a heavy-ion collision, with the fluid composed of quarks and antiquarks whose local thermodynamic properties are given by the quark-meson model in mean-field approximation. For a fixed baryochemical potential, the strength of the phase transition was tuned via the quark-meson coupling g: g=3.3 gives a crossover, g=3.63 gives a critical point at T_c ≈ 140 MeV, and g=5.5 gives a first-order phase transition at T_c ≈ 123 MeV. The equilibrium value of the chiral order parameter (sigma field) was computed locally in each fluid cell, and then averaged over an inner volume of about 30 fm³, and this averaged evolution was compared to the case of a homogeneous system with the same averaged temperature.

## Key numbers

- For the critical point scenario (g=3.63), the averaged correlation length in the inhomogeneous system grows up to 1.5 fm, while in a homogeneous system it would diverge at the critical temperature.
- For the first-order phase transition scenario (g=5.5), the discontinuity in the sigma field at the transition temperature (T_c ≈ 123 MeV) is washed out when averaging over the inhomogeneous system.
- The averaging volume used was an inner volume of about 30 fm³.
- The critical point occurs at T_c ≈ 140 MeV for g=3.63.

## Context

This work extends the model of nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (NχFD) developed by Nahrgang et al. (2011), which previously showed that nonequilibrium effects influence the evolution of fluctuations of the chiral order parameter. The paper contradicts the implicit assumption in earlier studies by Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999) that the background medium is homogeneous in thermodynamic quantities like temperature. The results enable future fully dynamic studies of the correlation length growth within NχFD, which the authors note is currently being worked on.
