# Exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation

> This Thesis concentrates on the analysis of coupled RTA (relaxation time approximation) kinetic equations for bosons and fermions. Bosons are treated as massless particles, while fermions have a finite mass. Using analytic and numerical methods we find exact solutions for such a mixture in the case of one-dimensional, boost-invariant expansion (for systems which are transversally homogeneous). In this way, several earlier results obtained for one-component systems and classical particles are generalized.

## Key claim

This thesis presents exact solutions of coupled relativistic Boltzmann equations in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a mixture of massless bosons and massive fermions undergoing one-dimensional, boost-invariant expansion, thereby generalizing earlier results that were limited to one-component systems or classical particles.

## Method

The author solves the coupled RTA kinetic equations analytically and numerically for a system that is transversally homogeneous and expands in a boost-invariant manner along the longitudinal direction. Bosons are treated as massless particles while fermions are assigned a finite mass, and the relaxation time approximation is used to model the collision terms. The solutions are obtained for the time evolution of the distribution functions and the resulting macroscopic quantities such as energy density and particle number density.

## Context

This thesis extends the work of Florkowski, Maksymiuk, and Ryblewski (2018) on coupled kinetic equations for fermions and bosons in the RTA, as well as earlier studies of one-component RTA kinetics. The exact solutions provide a benchmark for testing more sophisticated approaches to non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, such as anisotropic hydrodynamics (Alqahtani, Nopoush, and Strickland, 2018) and full numerical simulations of relativistic fluid dynamics (Romatschke and Romatschke, 2017). The results enable systematic studies of how quantum statistics (Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distributions) affect the thermalization process in a simplified but exactly solvable setting.
