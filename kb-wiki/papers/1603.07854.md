# Update of HKN Nuclear PDFs

> We discuss consistency of the nuclear effects between the electromagnetic and weak interactions. In order to study a possibility of different nuclear effects in the neutrino DIS process, double differential cross section data are compared with these values obtained by the HKN07 nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs). Discrepancies are found in the small and large-$x$ regions, and difference of kinematical value $y$ dependence exists between the $ν$ and $\barν$ data around $x=0.35$. Moreover, we study statistical significance of the neutrino DIS data in each $x$ bin and discuss about the possibility of the different nuclear modifications.

## Key claim

The nuclear effects in charged-current neutrino-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS) may not be quantitatively identical to those in electromagnetic (EM) DIS, but the current neutrino data lack sufficient statistical significance to definitively conclude that probe dependence of nuclear modifications exists.

## Method

The author compared double differential cross section data from the NuTeV (Fe target), CHORUS (Pb target), and CDHSW (Fe target) neutrino DIS experiments with theoretical values calculated using the HKN07 nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), which were determined from EM DIS and Drell-Yan data. The comparison was performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) with kinematic cuts of Q^2 > 4 GeV^2 and W > 3.5 GeV, and the rational difference (Data-Theory)/Theory was plotted for each experiment across x, y, and E_nu bins. Additionally, the chi distribution (chi_i = (D_i - T_i/norm_j)/sigma_i) was studied at each x bin to assess the statistical significance of the neutrino data for modifying the nPDFs.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV (Fe target): 1168 neutrino data points and 966 anti-neutrino data points; chi^2 per number of data = 1.54 without normalization factors, 1.23 with normalization factor 0.96.
- CHORUS (Pb target): 412 neutrino and 412 anti-neutrino data points; chi^2 per number of data = 1.58 without normalization factors, 0.96 with normalization factor 0.94.
- CDHSW (Fe target): 465 neutrino and 464 anti-neutrino data points; chi^2 per number of data = 1.18 without normalization factors, 0.91 with normalization factor 0.96.
- For reference, the charged lepton Fe DIS data chi^2 per number of data for HKN07 is 1.52.
- The uncertainty of the normalization factors obtained by fixing the nPDFs is about 2%.

## Context

This work extends the HKN07 global analysis (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai, 2007) of nuclear PDFs, which was based on EM DIS and Drell-Yan data, by testing its predictions against neutrino DIS data. It addresses the suggestion by Kovarik, Suzuki, et al. (2011) that nuclear effects in the charged-current process may differ from the EM case, and follows the approach of Paukkunen and Salgado (2011) who compared differential cross sections. The analysis enables future global fits using only neutrino DIS data to determine nuclear modifications, and highlights the need for more precise neutrino measurements and comparisons with W production in pA collisions at RHIC and LHC to resolve the question of probe dependence.
