# Hydrodynamization and non-equilibrium Green's functions in kinetic theory

> Non-equilibrium Green's functions provide an efficient way to describe the evolution of the energy-momentum tensor during the early time pre-equilibrium stage of high-energy heavy ion collisions. Besides their practical relevance they also provide a meaningful way to address the question when and to what extent a hydrodynamic description of the system becomes applicable. Within the kinetic theory framework we derive a new method to calculate time dependent non-equilibrium Green's functions describing the evolution of energy and momentum perturbations on top of an evolving far-from-equilibrium background. We discuss the approach towards viscous hydrodynamics along with the emergence of various scaling phenomena for conformal systems. By comparing our results obtained in the relaxation time approximation to previous calculations in Yang-Mills kinetic theory, we further address the question which macroscopic features of the energy momentum tensor are sensitive to the underlying microscopic dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-equilibrium Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor, computed within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to kinetic theory, capture the approach to viscous hydrodynamics and exhibit scaling phenomena for conformal systems; however, the detailed evolution of the energy-momentum tensor shows quantitative differences (at the 10% level) compared to previous calculations in Yang-Mills kinetic theory, indicating that some macroscopic features are sensitive to the underlying microscopic dynamics.

## Method

The authors linearize the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation around a boost-invariant Bjorken flow background, then derive evolution equations for the moments of the perturbed distribution function using a generalization of Grad's method of moments. They solve the resulting infinite hierarchy of coupled moment equations numerically by truncating at a maximum angular momentum \(l_{\text{max}} = 512\) and reconstruct the non-equilibrium Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor from the linearized moments. The background evolution is first solved for both constant relaxation time and conformal (\(\tau_R T = \text{const}\)) cases, and then linear perturbations in the transverse plane are propagated on top of this evolving far-from-equilibrium background.

## Key numbers

* For the conformal RTA case, the asymptotic constant relating late-time energy density to initial conditions is \(C_{\infty} \approx 0.9\), while for Yang-Mills kinetic theory \(C_{\infty} \approx 1\), a difference of about 10%.
* The effective shear viscosity over entropy ratio \((\eta/s)_{\text{eff}}/(\eta/s)\) for the conformal RTA case differs from Yang-Mills kinetic theory results at the 10% level for intermediate times \(\tilde{w} \sim 1\).
* For constant relaxation time, the asymptotic integration constant is \(\lim_{\tau \to \infty} \tau^{4/3} e(\tau) = (\tau^{4/3} e)_{\infty} \simeq 1.2 \, \tau_R^{1/3} (\tau e)_0\).
* Viscous hydrodynamics starts to describe the evolution of the energy-momentum tensor around evolution times \(\tau/\tau_R \sim 2-3\) for the constant relaxation time case.

## Context

This work extends the KøMPøST framework (Kurkela et al., 2018) which calculated non-equilibrium Green's functions in QCD kinetic theory for the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions. By comparing RTA results to Yang-Mills kinetic theory, the authors identify which macroscopic features of the energy-momentum tensor are sensitive to microscopic dynamics versus universal scaling behavior. The method enables systematic calculation of pre-equilibrium Green's functions in simpler kinetic theory setups, facilitating future phenomenological studies of event-by-event initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations.
