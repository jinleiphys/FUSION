# Microscopic description of the torque acting on fission fragments

> When two fragments are created in a fission decay, any torque due to nuclear and Coulomb interaction can change the fragment's angular momentum. This article explores the character and magnitude of the angular momentum as a function of the initial conditions around the scission point using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory. To understand the torque acting on the fragments, the Frozen Hartree-Fock method is also used to determine the collective potential at scission. Two $^{240}$Pu fission channel ( $^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru and $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr ) are studied. These two channels cover different shapes (spherical, quadrupole, and octupole deformation) of the fragments. It is found that the angular momentum generated by the Coulomb interaction after fission is mainly collective, while this is not the case for the angular momentum generated at scission. The competition between rotational modes (bending, wriggling, and twisting) is discussed and shows that the angular momentum is generated mainly perpendicular to the fission axis.

## Key claim

The angular momentum generated by the Coulomb interaction after scission is mainly collective, while the angular momentum generated at scission by the nuclear interaction is less collective; the paper provides only qualitative results because it does not discuss the source and amplitude of the initial fluctuations of the orientation angle of the fragments.

## Method

The author used the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to compute the dynamics of two fission channels of $^{240}$Pu ($^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru and $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr) starting from various initial fragment orientations and separations. The Frozen Hartree-Fock (FHF) method was used to compute the collective potential as a function of fragment orientation angles and separation distance. The calculations used the Sly4d functional without pairing on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with a mesh spacing of 0.8 fm and a time step of 0.3 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru at large distance (D=17 fm) with initial $\theta_{\rm L}$=25$^{\circ}$, the light fragment $^{108}$Ru acquires a final intrinsic spin of 11 $\hbar$ from Coulomb torque alone.
- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru at short distance (D=15 fm) with initial $\theta_{\rm L}$=25$^{\circ}$, the bending mode yields 3.7 $\hbar$ for the heavy fragment and -8.6 $\hbar$ for the light fragment.
- For $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr at large distance (D=17 fm) with $\theta_{\rm H}=-\theta_{\rm L}=25^{\circ}$, the heavy fragment acquires 4 $\hbar$ of angular momentum.
- For $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr at short distance (D=15 fm), the twisting mode parallel to the fission axis reaches a maximum of 2.0 $\hbar$ at $\varphi$=90$^{\circ}$, much smaller than the perpendicular component averaging around 10 $\hbar$.
- The rigid moment of inertia values are: $^{132}$Sn = 50.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{144}$Ba = 63.1 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{108}$Ru = 51.4 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{96}$Sr = 37.1 $\hbar^2$/MeV.
- The $^{108}$Ru fragment has a quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ = 0.82 and an excitation energy of 3.5 MeV above the ground state.

## Context

This work extends microscopic density functional theory treatments of fission fragment spin, specifically the static projection methods of Bertsch (2019) and the dynamical TDHF approaches of Bulgac (2021, 2022). The paper directly addresses the argument of Wilson (2021) that post-scission Coulomb-induced rotation would depend on the product $Z_1Z_2$ and would be incompatible with experimental data; the author finds that the generated angular momentum depends more on fragment deformation than on $Z_1Z_2$, potentially resolving this discrepancy. The results enable future beyond-mean-field treatments, such as stochastic methods, to self-consistently describe Coulomb-induced rotation and the initial fluctuations of fragment orientation angles.
