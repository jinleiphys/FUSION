# Exploiting $ν$-dependence of projected energy correlators in HICs

> We extend the recently derived factorization formula for energy-energy correlators to study the analytic structure of general $ν$-point projected energy correlators in heavy ion collisions. The $ν$-point projected energy correlators (or, $ν$-correlators) are an analytically continued family of the integer $N$-point projected energy correlators, which probe correlations between $N$ final-state particles. By tracking the largest separation ($χ$) between the $N$ particles, in vacuum, their structure is closely related to the DGLAP splitting functions and exhibits a classical scaling behavior $\sim 1/χ$ which is modified by resummation through the anomalous dimensions. We show that, in a thermal medium, the $ν$-correlators display non-trivial angular scaling already at the leading order in perturbation theory. We find that for non-integer values, particularly $ν<1$, medium-induced jet function is enhanced compared to $ν>1$. This is particularly manifested in the ratios of $ν$-correlators with respect to the two-point energy correlator which encode an intrinsic angular information for $ν<1$ when compared to large $ν$ values. Moreover, for small-$ν$ values, the $ν$-correlators appear to saturate at $ν=0.01$. We further confirm our leading-order numerical computations against simulated events from JEWEL for the parton level production cross-section. Finally, we qualitatively discuss the effect of BFKL resummation for various values of $ν$.

## Key claim

The authors extend the factorization formula for two-point energy correlators to general ν-point projected energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions, showing that for non-integer values (particularly ν < 1) the medium-induced jet function is enhanced compared to ν > 1, and that the ν-correlators saturate at ν = 0.01 for small-ν values, though these results are restricted to leading-order perturbation theory in the single-scattering approximation.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization formula for ν-point projected energy correlators (ν-correlators) in the collinear limit, using soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon exchanges to describe jet-medium interactions in a thermal quark-gluon plasma. They evaluate the medium-induced jet function at leading order for the q → qg splitting process, considering two phase-space regions (|k| > |q| and |k| < |q|) and the large formation time limit, then validate their analytic results against parton-level simulations from the JEWEL event generator.

## Key numbers

- The medium-induced jet function in the |k| > |q| phase-space region scales as ∼ 1/χ²ω² at leading order in the large system size limit, with explicit form J^[ν] ≈ (α_s g⁴ T³(5+4ν))/((ν+2)π⁴) × L/(χ²ω²)
- The medium-induced jet function in the |k| < |q| phase-space region scales as ∼ 1/(ω m_D χ^{3/2}) at leading order, with explicit form J^[ν] ≈ (α_s g⁴ T³ ν L)/(π⁴ ω m_D χ^{3/2})
- The ν-correlators saturate at ν = 0.01 for small-ν values
- The L → ∞ approximation holds well for system sizes L ≳ 10 fm at χ = 0.01
- Medium parameters used: T = 0.4 GeV, L = 5 fm, m_D = 0.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the factorization formula for two-point energy correlators in the medium derived by Singh (2024) to general ν-point correlators, building on the vacuum ν-correlator framework of Chen (2020). The ν-correlators provide access to anomalous dimensions of the system and small-x physics through the ν → 0 limit, with the medium-induced modifications potentially probing the structure of medium evolution equations. The authors qualitatively discuss BFKL resummation effects for various ν values, which would require higher-order computations of the jet function beyond the leading-order treatment presented here.
