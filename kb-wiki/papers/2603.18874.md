# Probing the Color-Octet Mechanism via Dihadron Fragmentation in $χ_b$ Decays

> The color-octet (CO) mechanism is a cornerstone of non-relativistic QCD, yet its long-distance matrix elements remain limited, preventing stringent tests of the theory. We demonstrate that the Artru-Collins asymmetry in hadronic decays of the $P$-wave bottomonium state $χ_{b2}$ provides a direct probe of CO dynamics. The asymmetry arises exclusively from the CO decay channel, whereas the color-singlet (CS) contribution affects only the unpolarized rate, so that a nonzero signal constitutes unambiguous evidence of the CO mechanism. This observable provides a novel way to extract the ratio $ρ_8$ between CO and CS matrix elements. Focusing on $e^+e^-\toΥ(2S)\toγ\,χ_{b2}$ at Belle, we show that the asymmetric beam configuration preserves the asymmetry in the laboratory frame and avoids the strong suppression present in the center-of-mass frame. With the Belle II dataset, $ρ_8$ could be determined with sufficient precision to address the long-standing discrepancy between the lattice calculations and phenomenological determinations.

## Key claim

The Artru-Collins asymmetry in hadronic decays of the P-wave bottomonium state χb2 provides a direct probe of color-octet dynamics, because the asymmetry arises exclusively from the color-octet decay channel while the color-singlet contribution affects only the unpolarized rate; a nonzero signal therefore constitutes unambiguous evidence of the color-octet mechanism, and the observable enables extraction of the ratio ρ8 between color-octet and color-singlet matrix elements.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for χb2 → qq̄, gg → (π⁺π⁻) + (π⁺π⁻) + X within the collinear factorization framework, combining the radiative production Υ(2S) → γ χb2 at Belle with the partonic decay in the χb2 rest frame. The hadronization is described by dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs), with the chiral-odd interference DiFF H₁^⧅,q probing transverse quark polarization. The asymmetry A₁₂ ≡ 2⟨cos(φ₁+φ₂)⟩ is extracted from the average value of the azimuthal angle sum, and the authors show that the asymmetric beam configuration at Belle preserves the asymmetry in the laboratory frame by removing angular dependence that would cause cancellations in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- The lattice central value used for the ratio ρ₈(m_b) = 0.044, defined at the scale m_b ≈ 4.6 GeV (Bodwin 2007)
- The asymmetry A₁₂ reaches the percent level in magnitude, smaller than that measured in π⁺π⁻-dihadron pair production at Belle due to dilution from the gluon channel
- The projected precision in the laboratory frame surpasses the current lattice uncertainty of ρ₈(m_b) = 0.044 ± 0.015 (Bodwin 2007) with an integrated luminosity of O(0.1) ab⁻¹
- At L ∼ 10 ab⁻¹, the precision on ρ₈(m_b) could reach the few-percent level
- The CLEO measurement from χbJ(1P) → D⁰ X gave ρ₈(m_b) = 0.16^{+0.071}_{-0.047} (CLEO 2008)
- The asymmetry is evaluated for z_i ∈ [0.19, 0.99] and M_i ∈ [0.28, 2.05] GeV (Cocuzza 2023)
- The Lorentz boost velocity of the Υ(2S) in the laboratory frame is β = 0.3–0.4 at Belle or Belle II

## Context

This work extends the application of Artru-Collins asymmetries, previously measured in e⁺e⁻ annihilation at Belle (Belle 2011), to the study of color-octet dynamics in NRQCD. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between lattice NRQCD calculations of ρ₈(m_b) (Bodwin 2007) and phenomenological extractions from χbJ(1P) → D⁰ X at CLEO (CLEO 2008). The method enables an independent determination of ρ₈(m_b) from χbJ decay, which can test the universal nature of the gluonic correlator E₃ in potential-NRQCD (Brambilla 2001, Brambilla 2020) and can be cross-checked using Υ(3S) → γ χb2(2P) transitions.
