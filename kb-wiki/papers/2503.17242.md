# $β$-Decay Properties of Neutron-rich Yttrium Isotopes

> In this study, we have reported key nuclear properties of weak $β$-decay processes on Yttrium isotopes for the mass number range $A = 101 - 108$. This mass region is important in astrophysical $r$-process abundances. Our study might be helpful in $r$-process simulations. We have computed charge-changing strength distributions, $β$-decay half-lives, $β$-delayed neutron emission probabilities, and $β^{-}$ (EC) weak rates under stellar conditions. We have performed microscopic calculations based on deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) over a wide temperature ($10^7 - 3 \times 10^{10}$) K and density ($10 - 10^{11}$) g/cm$^3$ domain. Unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions have been included in the calculations in addition to the allowed transitions. A significant decrease in calculated half-lives in certain cases, e.g., in $^{107}$Y ($^{108}$Y) by about 67\% (42\%), has been observed because of the contribution from U1F transitions. We have compared present results with measured and theoretical works. A good agreement of our half-lives with experimental data is observed.

## Key claim

The deformed pn-QRPA model, including unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions alongside allowed Gamow-Teller transitions, produces beta-decay half-lives for neutron-rich yttrium isotopes (A=101-108) that agree reasonably with experimental data, but the improvement from including U1F transitions is highly isotope-dependent: half-lives decrease by as much as 67% for 107Y and 42% for 108Y, while other isotopes show reductions of only 2.6% to 38%.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model, using a Nilsson basis with BCS pairing correlations and separable Gamow-Teller residual interactions, to compute charge-changing strength distributions, beta-decay half-lives, beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities, and stellar weak rates (beta-minus and electron capture) for 101-108Y. Unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions were included in addition to allowed transitions, with strength parameters tuned to reproduce experimental half-lives; calculations covered a temperature range of 10^7 to 3x10^10 K and density range of 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3.

## Key numbers

- 107Y half-life: experimental 0.034 s; pn-QRPA with only GT = 0.080 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.026 s (67% reduction from U1F inclusion)
- 108Y half-life: experimental 0.030 s; pn-QRPA with only GT = 0.041 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.023 s (42% reduction from U1F inclusion)
- 105Y half-life: experimental 0.095 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.083 s (38% reduction from GT-only value of 0.136 s)
- 103Y half-life: experimental 0.239 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.209 s (30% reduction from GT-only value of 0.300 s)
- 101Y half-life: experimental 0.426 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.481 s (9.7% reduction from GT-only value of 0.533 s)
- 102Y half-life: experimental 0.298 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.306 s (2.6% reduction)
- 104Y half-life: experimental 0.197 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.194 s (3.75% reduction)
- 106Y half-life: experimental 0.074 s; pn-QRPA with GT+U1F = 0.081 s (21.32% reduction)
- Beta-delayed neutron emission probability for 108Y: pn-QRPA = 65.2%; QRPA+gross theory (Moller 2003) = not reported; KHF (Pfeiffer 2002) = 25.5%; QRPA-1 and QRPA-2 (Pfeiffer 2002) = 36.0%
- Beta-delayed neutron emission probability for 104Y: pn-QRPA = 44.1%; QRPA+gross theory = 4.8%; KHF = 11.6%; QRPA-1 and QRPA-2 = 3.2%

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations by Nabi and collaborators (2004, 2019, 2020, 2021) for neutron-rich nuclei with A <= 100 to the intermediate mass region A=100-110, which is significant for r-process nucleosynthesis. The results are compared with experimental data from Audi (2017) and with theoretical calculations by Moller (2003) using QRPA+gross theory, and by Pfeiffer (2002) using QRPA-1, QRPA-2, and the Kratz-Herrmann formula (KHF); the authors note that their beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities differ notably from other models, which they attribute to different treatments of daughter energy levels and transition matrix elements. The computed half-lives and weak rates are intended for use in r-process simulations, particularly for the neutron-rich yttrium isotopes that lie along the r-process path in this mass region.
