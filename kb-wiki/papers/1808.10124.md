# Nuclear magnetic moments in covariant density functional theory

> The nuclear magnetic moment is an important physical observable and serves as a useful tool for the stringent test of nuclear models. For the past decades, the covariant density functional theory and its extension have been proved to be successful in describing the nuclear ground-states and excited states properties. However, a long-standing problem is its failure to predict magnetic moments. This article reviews the recent progress in the description of the nuclear magnetic moments within the covariant density functional theory. In particular, the magnetic moments of spherical odd-A nuclei with doubly closed shell core plus or minus one nucleon and deformed odd-A nuclei.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that covariant density functional theory (CDFT) has historically failed to predict nuclear magnetic moments, but recent progress incorporating meson exchange currents and first- and second-order configuration mixing corrections has enabled successful reproduction of magnetic moments for spherical odd-A nuclei with doubly closed shell cores plus or minus one nucleon, as well as for deformed odd-A nuclei.

## Method

The authors review the CDFT framework with point-coupling interactions, deriving the Dirac equation for single nucleons and the effective electromagnetic current operator. They then introduce the one-pion exchange current corrections via seagull and in-flight Feynman diagrams, and formulate first-order and second-order configuration mixing corrections using perturbation theory, including 1p-1h, 2p-1h, and 3p-2h intermediate states. The approach is applied to nuclei with LS closed-shell cores (near 16O and 40Ca) and jj closed-shell cores (near 208Pb), as well as to deformed odd-A nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The free anomalous gyromagnetic ratios used are kappa_p = 1.793 and kappa_n = -1.913
- The nucleon effective (scalar) mass M* = M + S is approximately 0.6M
- For isoscalar magnetic moments of nuclei with A = 15, 17, 39, and 41: experimental values are 0.22, 1.41, 0.71, and 1.92 respectively; Schmidt values are 0.19, 1.44, 0.64, and 1.94; spherical CDFT without polarization gives 0.32, 1.57, 0.94, and 2.21
- The azimuthal current in 15N is enhanced by nearly a factor of three when core polarization is included self-consistently

## Context

This review extends the non-relativistic work of Arima and Horie (1954) on first-order configuration mixing (the Arima-Horie effect) and the work of Shimizu, Ichimura and Arima (1974) on second-order tensor correlations, into the relativistic CDFT framework. It addresses the long-standing failure of relativistic models to predict magnetic moments, first noted by Miller (1975) and Serot (1981), by incorporating the "back-flow" effect treated via Landau-Migdal theory by McNeil (1986) and via self-consistent time-odd fields by Hofmann (1988) and Furnstahl (1989). The review enables future systematic calculations of magnetic moments across the nuclear chart within a fully relativistic framework, including for exotic nuclei far from stability.
