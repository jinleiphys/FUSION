# On the critical end point in a two-flavor linear sigma model coupled to quarks

> We use the linear sigma model coupled to quarks to explore the location of the phase transition lines in the QCD phase diagram from the point of view of chiral symmetry restoration at high temperature and baryon chemical potential. We compute analytically the effective potential in the high- and low-temperature approximations up to sixth order, including the contribution of the ring diagrams to account for the plasma screening properties. We determine the model parameters, namely, the couplings and mass-parameter, from conditions valid at the first order phase transition at vanishing temperature and, using the Hagedorn limiting temperature concept applied to finite baryon density, for a critical baryochemical potential of order of the nucleon mass. We show that when using the set of parameters thus determined, the second order phase transition line (our proxy for the crossover transition) that starts at finite temperature and zero baryon chemical potential converges to the line of first order phase transitions that starts at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential to determine the critical end point to lie in the region $5.02<μ_B^{\mbox{CEP}}/T_c<5.18$, $0.14<T^{\mbox{CEP}}/T_c<0.23$, where $T_c$ is the critical transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

Using the linear sigma model with quarks, the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram is located in the region \(5.02 < \mu_B^{\text{CEP}}/T_c < 5.18\) and \(0.14 < T^{\text{CEP}}/T_c < 0.23\), where \(T_c\) is the critical transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential, but this result depends on fixing the model parameters from a first-order phase transition at \(T=0\) with \(\mu_B^c \simeq m_N\) based on the Hagedorn limiting temperature concept.

## Method

The authors computed the effective potential of the two-flavor linear sigma model coupled to quarks analytically in both low-temperature and high-temperature expansions up to sixth order, including ring diagram contributions to account for plasma screening. Model parameters (couplings \(\lambda\), \(g\), and mass parameter \(a\)) were determined from conditions describing a first-order phase transition at vanishing temperature and a critical baryochemical potential of order the nucleon mass, using the lever rule to define an effective pion mass in the mixed phase. The phase diagram was then mapped by finding degenerate minima of the effective potential in the low-temperature expansion (first-order line) and second-order transition points in the high-temperature expansion (proxy for crossover), with the CEP located where these lines converge.

## Key numbers

* The CEP lies in the region \(5.02 < \mu_B^{\text{CEP}}/T_c < 5.18\) and \(0.14 < T^{\text{CEP}}/T_c < 0.23\), where \(T_c\) is the critical temperature at \(\mu_B=0\).
* For \(\mu_q^c = 300\) MeV and \(\lambda = 0.65, 0.75, 0.85\), the corresponding couplings are \(g = 1.40, 1.45, 1.50\) and mass parameters \(a = 68.85, 82.05, 92.72\) MeV, respectively.
* The critical temperature at \(\mu_B=0\) is \(T_c \sim 180\) MeV for the parameter sets used.
* When using \(\mu_B^c \simeq 1\) GeV (\(\mu_q^c \simeq 333\) MeV), \(T_c \gtrsim 190\) MeV, which is above the lattice QCD range of 170 MeV \(< T_c < 186\) MeV for two flavors.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the linear sigma model with quarks (Ayala et al., various years) by determining model parameters from phase transition conditions rather than vacuum conditions, aiming for quantitative accuracy. The results are compared to lattice QCD crossover transition regions obtained from Taylor expansion methods (Bazavov et al., 2018), showing agreement at low \(\mu_B\). The approach enables a prediction for the CEP location at low temperature and high baryon chemical potential, a region where experimental exploration is challenging and where future Beam Energy Scan data from STAR, FAIR, and NICA may provide constraints.
