# Relativistic Dissipative Magnetohydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation for a two-component gas

> We derive the equations of motion of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, as well as microscopic expressions for all of its transport coefficients, from the Boltzmann equation using the method of moments. In contrast to reference Phys. Rev. D 98(7) 2018, where a single component gas was considered, we perform our derivation for a locally neutral fluid composed of two massless particle species with opposite charges. We demonstrate that the magnetohydrodynamical equations of motion become dramatically different for this more realistic system. The shear-stress tensor no longer obeys a single differential equation; it breaks into three non-degenerate components with respect to the magnetic field, each evolving according to different dynamical equations. For large magnetic fields, we further show that the solution of this theory displays oscillatory behaviour that can no longer be described by an Israel-Stewart-like theory. Finally, we investigate the derived equations in a Bjorken flow scenario.

## Key claim

The authors derive relativistic magnetohydrodynamic equations for a locally neutral two-component gas of massless particles with opposite charges, finding that the shear-stress tensor splits into three non-degenerate components relative to the magnetic field direction, each governed by distinct dynamical equations, a qualitative departure from single-component gas results where the magnetic field merely adds extra transport coefficients.

## Method

The derivation starts from the Boltzmann equation for two massless particle species with opposite charges and zero chemical potential, using the method of moments with a 14-moment approximation to close the moment hierarchy. The authors impose Landau matching conditions, assume vanishing electric field and zero net charge, and perform a tensor decomposition of the shear-stress tensor with respect to the magnetic field direction using a basis of projection operators and complex null vectors. They then obtain coupled equations for the total shear-stress tensor and the relative shear-stress tensor between species, which are further decomposed into scalar components parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields reach peaks of approximately 10^19 gauss at RHIC and 10^20 gauss at LHC in heavy-ion collisions (from introduction, citing prior work)
- The inverse relaxation time for the total shear-stress tensor is given by Σ = (3 n̂₀/5)(σ_T^(+-) + σ_T), where n̂₀ is the equilibrium particle density and σ_T are cross sections
- The inverse relaxation time for the relative shear-stress tensor is Σ' = (n̂₀/5)(5σ_T^(+-) + 3σ_T)
- In the absence of a magnetic field, Σ = 1/τ_π = (ε + P₀)/(5η), relating the relaxation time to shear viscosity η

## Context

This work extends the single-component gas derivation of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics from kinetic theory by Denicol (2018) to the more physically realistic case of a two-component neutral plasma. The results contradict the expectation that a magnetic field merely introduces additional transport coefficients, as found in earlier work by Denicol (2018, 2019) and others using single-component gases. The oscillatory behavior of the shear-stress tensor at large magnetic fields, which cannot be described by Israel-Stewart-like theories, opens the door to studying magnetic field effects in quark-gluon plasma dynamics in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical plasmas.
