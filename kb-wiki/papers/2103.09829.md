# On the transition form factors of the axial-vector resonance $f_1(1285)$ and its decay into $e^+e^-$

> Estimating the contribution from axial-vector intermediate states to hadronic light-by-light scattering requires input on their transition form factors (TFFs). Due to the Landau-Yang theorem, any experiment sensitive to these TFFs needs to involve at least one virtual photon, which complicates their measurement. Phenomenologically, the situation is best for the $f_1(1285)$ resonance, for which information is available from $e^+e^-\to e^+e^- f_1$, $f_1\to 4π$, $f_1\to ργ$, $f_1\to ϕγ$, and $f_1\to e^+e^-$. We provide a comprehensive analysis of the $f_1$ TFFs in the framework of vector meson dominance, including short-distance constraints, to determine to which extent the three independent TFFs can be constrained from the available experimental input, a prerequisite for improved calculations of the axial-vector contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering. In particular, we focus on the process $f_1\to e^+e^-$, evidence for which has been reported recently by SND for the first time, and discuss the impact that future improved measurements will have on the determination of the $f_1$ TFFs.

## Key claim
The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the three independent transition form factors (TFFs) of the $f_1(1285)$ axial-vector resonance within a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) framework, including short-distance constraints, to determine how well these TFFs can be constrained from available experimental data, a prerequisite for improved calculations of the axial-vector contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering. The authors emphasize that, while the SND collaboration has recently reported first evidence for $f_1\to e^+e^-$, the current data do not yet allow an unambiguous extraction of all three TFFs, and future improved measurements will be essential.

## Method
The authors construct a VMD model for the $f_1(1285)$ TFFs using the Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) decomposition to ensure the absence of kinematic singularities, incorporating isovector contributions from $\rho(770)$ and $\rho'(1450)$ mesons and isoscalar contributions from $\omega$ and $\phi$ mesons. They impose short-distance constraints from the Brodsky-Lepage limit and fit the model parameters to experimental data from $e^+e^-\to e^+e^-f_1$, $f_1\to 4\pi$, $f_1\to \rho\gamma$, $f_1\to \phi\gamma$, and the recently observed $f_1\to e^+e^-$ decay. The analysis includes energy-dependent widths for the vector mesons and accounts for $f_1$-$f_1'$ mixing with a mixing angle $\theta_A = 62(5)^\circ$.

## Key numbers
- The equivalent two-photon decay width of the $f_1(1285)$ is given by $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{\gamma\gamma} = \frac{\pi\alpha^2}{48} M_{f_1} |\mathcal{F}_{sT}(0,0)|^2$, where $M_{f_1}=1285$ MeV is the $f_1$ mass.
- The ratio of isoscalar to isovector contributions for the $f_1\gamma\gamma$ coupling is $R_{S/V} = -4.7(3.4)\%$, derived from the mixing angle $\theta_A = 62(5)^\circ$.
- The asymptotic behavior of the TFFs in the doubly-virtual limit is $\mathcal{F}_2(q^2,q^2) = \frac{F_{A}^{\text{eff}} M_A^3}{2q^4} + \mathcal{O}(1/q^6)$ and $\mathcal{F}_3(q^2,q^2) = -\frac{F_{A}^{\text{eff}} M_A^3}{2q^4} + \mathcal{O}(1/q^6)$, where $F_{A}^{\text{eff}}$ is the effective decay constant.
- The singly-virtual asymptotic limits are $\mathcal{F}_2(q^2,0) = \frac{3 F_{A}^{\text{eff}} M_A^3}{q^4} + \mathcal{O}(1/q^6)$ and $\mathcal{F}_3(0,q^2) = -\frac{3 F_{A}^{\text{eff}} M_A^3}{q^4} + \mathcal{O}(1/q^6)$.

## Context
This work extends the systematic analysis of axial-vector TFFs initiated in Hoferichter (2020), which provided the BTT decomposition and short-distance constraints, by applying it specifically to the $f_1(1285)$ resonance where the most phenomenological input is available. The study is motivated by the need to improve the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, where axial-vector intermediate states are a potentially sizable uncertainty. The results enable future refinements of axial-vector contributions to HLbL scattering and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming measurements from the SND collaboration and other experiments.
