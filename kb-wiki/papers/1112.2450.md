# Pionic Atom Spectroscopy in the (d,3He) reaction at finite angles

> We study the formation of deeply bound pionic atoms in the (d,3He) reactions theoretically and show the energy spectra of the emitted 3He at finite angles, which are expected to be observed experimentally. We find that the different combinations of the pion-bound and neutron-hole states dominate the spectra at different scattering angles because of the matching condition of the reaction. We conclude that the observation of the (d,3He) reaction at finite angles will provide the systematic information of the pionic bound states in each nucleus and will help to develop the study of the pion properties and the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors theoretically show that observing the (d,3He) reaction at finite scattering angles (≥2°) will reveal contributions from pionic 2p states that are hidden at forward angles, provided the instrumental energy resolution is sufficiently good (300 keV or 100 keV FWHM). The claim is conditional on the kinematical correction factor K being properly included in the effective number approach to obtain realistic angular dependence.

## Method

The authors modify the effective number approach used in prior work (Umemoto, Umemoto2) by introducing a kinematical correction factor K derived from the ratio of laboratory-frame to center-of-mass-frame elementary cross sections for the d + n → 3He + π− reaction. They calculate the (d,3He) spectra for 122Sn targets at an incident deuteron kinetic energy of 500 MeV, using both harmonic oscillator and phenomenological neutron wavefunctions, and include distortion effects via the Eikonal approximation. The elementary cross sections are taken from experimental data on the p + d → t + π+ reaction via isospin symmetry, parameterized as a function of the scattering angle.

## Key numbers

- Elementary cross section in the lab frame at θ=0°: 1.77 × 10³ μb/sr; at θ=3°: 1.22 × 10³ μb/sr
- Elementary cross section in the CM frame at θ=0°: 8.00 μb/sr; at θ=49.5°: 4.02 μb/sr
- The kinematical correction factor K decreases about 20% at θ=2° from its value at θ=0° for Td = 500 MeV
- The K factor varies about 10% within the Q-value range considered at both 0° and 2°
- The largest peak in forward spectra appears at Q = -137.8 MeV; the pion production threshold is at Q = -141.6 MeV
- The neutron separation energies of three relevant neutron levels (3s1/2, 2d3/2, 1h11/2) differ from each other within only 60 keV
- The reaction Q-value constant Mn + Md − MHe = 6.787 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for deeply bound pionic atom spectroscopy developed by Umemoto, Umemoto2, and earlier predictions by nd, d3He. It builds on experimental discoveries of deeply bound pionic atoms in (d,3He) reactions at RIKEN (Yamazaki 1996, Gilg, Itahashi) and precise binding energy determinations in Sn isotopes (KSuzuki 2004). The paper specifically addresses the need for systematic information on multiple pionic bound states (1s, 2s, 2p) in the same nucleus to reduce systematic errors from neutron distribution uncertainties and absolute energy calibration, as noted by KSuzuki (2004) and Ikeno (2010). The results are intended to guide upcoming experiments at RIBF/RIKEN (Itahashi2) aiming to observe pionic 2s and 2p states simultaneously.
