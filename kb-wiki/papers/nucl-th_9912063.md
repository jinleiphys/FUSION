# H-dibaryon

> The quark cluster model studies concerning the H-dibaryon are reviewed. The covered topics are the H-dibaryon itself, the interaction between a nucleon and an H-dibaryon, and the one between two H-dibaryons. A related study on the H-dibaryon in nuclear matter is also reviewed and its implication to double hypernuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon, a six-quark (uuddss) state predicted by Jaffe (1977) to be 81 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold, may not exist as a strongly bound state; quark cluster model calculations show that its mass depends critically on the inclusion of meson-exchange potentials and the treatment of flavor symmetry breaking, with some models finding only a resonance rather than a bound state.

## Method

The authors review non-relativistic quark cluster model (QCM) studies of the H-dibaryon, where the total wave function of a baryon-baryon system is constructed as an antisymmetrized product of two baryon cluster wave functions (harmonic oscillator shell model) plus a relative motion wave function, solved via the resonating group method (RGM). The quark-quark interaction includes the one-gluon-exchange potential (OGEP) of Fermi-Breit type, with some studies also incorporating pseudoscalar-meson exchange at the quark level and phenomenological σ-meson exchange at the baryon level, as well as instanton-induced interactions (III).

## Key numbers

- Jaffe's original MIT bag model prediction: H-dibaryon mass 81 MeV lighter than the ΛΛ threshold (2231 MeV)
- In the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, the color magnetic interaction operator gives Θ = -24 for the H-dibaryon compared to 2Θ = -16 for two Λ's
- Straub et al. (1988) found that with only OGEP and pseudoscalar meson exchange, the H-dibaryon appears as a resonance at E_cm = 26 MeV (not a bound state)
- Lattice QCD result from Wetzorke (1999): 2221 ± 141 MeV for smaller lattice, heavier for larger lattice
- Experimental candidate events: Alekseev et al. (1990) reported M_H = 2217.1 ± 7.1 MeV and 2224.3 ± 8.4 MeV
- Double hypernucleus data: Danysz et al. (1963) reported ¹⁰_ΛΛBe with B_ΛΛ = 17.7 ± 0.4 MeV and ΔB_ΛΛ = 4.3 ± 0.4 MeV; Prowse et al. (1966) reported ⁶_ΛΛHe with B_ΛΛ = 10.9 ± 0.5 MeV and ΔB_ΛΛ = 4.7 ± 1.0 MeV

## Context

This review summarizes two decades of theoretical work on the H-dibaryon following Jaffe's (1977) prediction, covering quark cluster model calculations by Oka et al. (1983), Straub et al. (1988), Takeuchi and Oka (1991), and others, as well as experimental searches from BNL, KEK, CERN, and Fermilab collaborations. The results contrast with the success of QCM in reproducing baryon mass spectra and NN scattering phase shifts, highlighting the H-dibaryon as a touchstone for QCD-inspired models. The review enables future experimental searches by clarifying the mass range where the H-dibaryon might exist (binding energy less than a few tens of MeV) and connects to double hypernucleus studies that provide lower bounds on the H-dibaryon mass.
