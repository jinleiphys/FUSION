# Constraining the nonanalytic terms in the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the nuclear equation of state

> We examine the properties of the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the nuclear equation of state from chiral two- and three-body forces. We focus on extracting the high-order symmetry energy coefficients that consist of both normal terms (occurring with even powers of the isospin asymmetry) as well as terms involving the logarithm of the isospin asymmetry that are formally nonanalytic around the expansion point of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. These coefficients are extracted from numerically precise perturbation theory calculations of the equation of state coupled with a new set of finite difference formulas that achieve stability by explicitly removing the effects of higher-order terms in the expansion. We consider contributions to the symmetry energy coefficients from both two- and three-body interactions. It is found that the coefficients of the logarithmic terms are generically larger in magnitude than those of the normal terms from second-order perturbation theory diagrams, but overall the normal terms give larger contributions to the ground state energy. The high-order isospin-asymmetry terms are especially relevant at large densities where they affect the proton fraction in beta-equilibrium matter.

## Key claim

The isospin-asymmetry expansion of the nuclear equation of state contains both regular even-power terms and nonanalytic logarithmic terms, and while the logarithmic coefficients are generically larger in magnitude than the regular coefficients from second-order perturbation theory, the regular terms still dominate the ground state energy overall.

## Method

The authors performed many-body perturbation theory calculations of the nuclear equation of state using chiral effective field theory two- and three-body forces at Hartree-Fock and second-order levels. They developed a modified finite difference method that explicitly removes the influence of higher-order terms to extract the coefficients of both regular terms (A₂, A₄, A₆) and logarithmic terms (A₄;ₗ, A₆;ₗ) in the isospin-asymmetry expansion up to sixth order in δ.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter is n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The A₂ coefficient from three-body forces at saturation density is on the order of 1–2 MeV, compared to the total empirical value of A₂ ≈ 30 MeV.
- The A₄ and A₆ coefficients from Hartree-Fock calculations are comparable in magnitude in the density range 0 < n < 0.20 fm⁻³.
- The A₈ Maclaurin series coefficient at density n = 0.15 fm⁻³ is much smaller than A₄ and A₆, indicating efficient convergence of the Maclaurin series.
- The logarithmic term δ⁴ ln|δ| peaks at δ = e⁻¹/⁴ ≈ 0.78.

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Kaiser (2015) that perturbation theory beyond Hartree-Fock introduces nonanalytic logarithmic terms in the isospin-asymmetry expansion, which was later verified numerically by Wellenhofer et al. (2016) with realistic chiral forces. The authors extract for the first time the high-order coefficients up to sixth order in δ, enabling more precise predictions of the proton fraction in beta-equilibrium nuclear matter at high densities, which affects neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process.
