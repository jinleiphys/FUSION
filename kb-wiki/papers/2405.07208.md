# Kaon superfluidity in the early universe

> Previously, it was found that pion superfluidity could be realized in the QCD epoch of the early universe, when lepton flavor asymmetry $|l_{\rm e}+l_μ|$ is large enough to generate a charge chemical potential $|μ_{\rm Q}|$ larger than vacuum pion mass. By following the same logic, kaon superfluidity might also be possible when $|l_{\rm e}+l_μ|$ is so large that $|μ_{\rm Q}|$ becomes larger than vacuum kaon mass. Such a possibility is checked by adopting Ginzburg-Landau approximation within the three-flavor Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Consider the case with full chemical balance, though kaon superfluidity could be stable compared to the chiral phases with only $σ$ condensations, it would get killed by the more favored homogeneous pion superfluidity. If we introduce mismatch between $s$ and $d$ quarks, kaon superfluidity would require so large $s$ quark density that such a state is impossible in the early universe.

## Key claim

Kaon superfluidity in the early universe is theoretically possible when the charge chemical potential exceeds the vacuum kaon mass, but under conditions of full chemical balance it is always thermodynamically disfavored compared to homogeneous pion superfluidity, and when a mismatch between strange and down quarks is introduced, the required strange quark density is too large to be realized in the early universe.

## Method

The author uses the three-flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with quarks and gluons as degrees of freedom, applying a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the thermodynamic potential in powers of the kaon condensate around the background of pion superfluidity. The analysis considers two scenarios: full chemical balance between quark flavors, and a scenario with mismatch between strange and down quarks. The thermodynamic potentials of chiral phases, pion superfluidity, and kaon superfluidity are compared to determine the ground state.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum pion mass and vacuum kaon mass serve as thresholds: kaon superfluidity requires the charge chemical potential $|\mu_{\rm Q}|$ to be larger than the vacuum kaon mass.
- The reduced temperature parameter in the Polyakov loop potential is $\tilde{T} \equiv T/T_0$ with $T_0 = 0.27$ GeV.
- The pure gluon potential coefficients are fitted to lattice QCD data: $V(L)/T^4 = -\frac{1}{2}(3.51 - 2.47/\tilde{T} + 15.2/\tilde{T}^2)L^2 - (1.75/\tilde{T}^3) \ln[1 - 6L^2 + 8L^3 - 3L^4]$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion superfluidity in the early universe by Vovchenko (2020), Middeldorf-Wygas (2020), and Cao (2021, 2022), which showed that large lepton flavor asymmetry can generate a charge chemical potential sufficient for pion condensation. It also connects to neutron star studies of kaon condensation by Kaplan (1986) and to three-flavor chiral perturbation theory by Andersen (2022), which found no kaon superfluidity for any isospin chemical potential under full chemical balance. The result rules out kaon superfluidity as a viable phase in the early universe, narrowing the set of possible QCD phases during that epoch.
