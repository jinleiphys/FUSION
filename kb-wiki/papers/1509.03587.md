# Hyperons in Neutron Stars

> In this work I briefly review some of the effects of hyperons on the properties of neutron and proto-neutron stars. In particular, I revise the problem of the strong softening of the EoS, and the consequent reduction of the maximum mass, induced by the presence of hyperons, a puzzle which has become more intringuing and difficult to solve due the recent measurements of the unusually high masses of the millisecond pulsars PSR J1903+0327 ($1.667\pm 0.021 M_\odot$), PSR J1614-2230 ($1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$), and PSR J0348+0432 ($2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$). Some of the solutions proposed to tackle this problem are discussed. Finally, I re-examine also the role of hyperons on the cooling properties of newly born neutron stars and on the so-called r-mode instability.

# Hyperons in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

Hyperons are energetically unavoidable in neutron star interiors above about 2–3 times nuclear saturation density, but their presence softens the equation of state so strongly that most microscopic models predict maximum masses below the observed 2-solar-mass pulsar masses, creating the "hyperon puzzle" that remains unresolved despite proposed solutions involving repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions, hyperonic three-body forces, or early deconfinement to quark matter.

## Method

This is a review paper that surveys the existing theoretical literature on hyperonic neutron star matter, comparing results from both phenomenological approaches (relativistic mean-field models and Skyrme-type interactions) and microscopic many-body methods (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, Vlow-k, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo). The author examines three proposed solutions to the hyperon puzzle and also re-evaluates the role of hyperons in proto-neutron star metastability, cooling via neutrino emission, and the r-mode instability driven by bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1903+0327 mass: 1.667 ± 0.021 solar masses
- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses
- PSR J0348+0432 mass: 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Hyperon onset density: approximately 2–3 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3})
- Central densities of neutron stars: 4–8 times normal nuclear matter saturation density (epsilon_0 ~ 2.7 × 10^{14} g/cm^3)
- Typical neutron star masses: 1–2 solar masses; radii: 10–12 km
- In microscopic calculations, maximum mass reduction can fall below the canonical 1.4–1.5 solar masses
- Most relativistic models including hyperons obtain maximum masses in the range 1.4–1.8 solar masses
- For a specific BHF model, proto-neutron stars with gravitational mass between 1.28 and 1.59 solar masses (baryonic mass 1.40–1.72 solar masses) are metastable and collapse to a black hole after deleptonization
- Hyperonic three-body forces in one calculation (Vidana et al.) yield a maximum mass of only 1.6 solar masses
- Critical proton fraction for direct Urca process: 11% to 15%

## Context

This review extends the long-standing line of work on hyperons in neutron stars that began with Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960). It directly addresses the tension created by the high-mass pulsar measurements of Demorest (2010), Freire (2011), and Antoniadis (2013), which rule out almost all previously proposed equations of state with hyperons. The paper surveys three competing solution families: repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions via vector mesons (explored by Bednarek 2011, Weissenborn, Oertel 2014, Maslov), hyperonic three-body forces (studied by Takatsuka 2000, Vidana, Yamamoto, Lonardoni 2015), and early phase transitions to deconfined quark matter (investigated by Ozel, Weissenborn and Sagert, Klahn 2013, Bonanno, Lastowiecki 2012). The discussion of metastable proto-neutron stars and black hole formation builds on the work of Keil (1995) and Vidana (2011). The r-mode instability analysis follows the framework of Lindblom (1985, 1986) and the Chandrasekhar-Friedman-Schutz mechanism, with hyperonic bulk viscosity calculations from the literature on hyperon bulk viscosity.
