# Lattice QCD in Background Fields

> Electromagnetic properties of hadrons can be computed by lattice simulations of QCD in background fields. We demonstrate new techniques for the investigation of charged hadron properties in electric fields. Our current calculations employ large electric fields, motivating us to analyze chiral dynamics in strong QED backgrounds, and subsequently uncover surprising non-perturbative effects present at finite volume.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate new techniques for computing charged hadron properties in background electric fields on the lattice, but caution that current calculations are limited by systematic errors from large field strengths and finite volume effects, requiring further studies to extract physical results.

## Method

The background field method is applied by computing hadron correlation functions using lattice QCD in classical external electric fields for various field strengths, then matching the calculated correlation functions to the behavior predicted by the relevant single-particle effective action. For the neutral pion, the energy shift as a function of field strength yields the electric polarizability; for the charged pion, the correlation function follows a parabolic cylinder function form rather than a simple exponential. The electric field is implemented on a discrete torus using quantized link variables to avoid non-uniformities from plaquettes wrapping around the time boundary.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of 390 MeV on an ensemble of 200 configurations of size 20^3 x 128 with anisotropy factor a_s / a_t = 3.5
- Field strength quantization: E = 2 pi n / (q_d L T) where q_d = -1/3 e is the down quark charge
- For the 20^3 x 128 lattices, (e E / m_pi^2)^2 = 0.18 n^2, where n is the integer in the quantization condition
- For magnetic fields on the same lattices, (e B / m_pi^2)^2 = T^2/L^2 (e E / m_pi^2)^2 with T^2/L^2 = 3.3
- Pion Compton wavelength lambda_C = 1 / m_pi ~ 3/2 fm

## Context

This work extends the background field method for lattice QCD previously applied to neutral hadrons (Detmold 2006) to charged hadrons in electric fields, using anisotropic dynamical clover lattices generated by the Hadron Spectrum collaboration (Edwards 2008, Lin 2008). The calculations employ strong field chiral perturbation theory (Tiburzi 2008) to handle the large electric fields required by quantization conditions on the torus ('t Hooft 1979, Smit 1986). The finite volume corrections and oscillatory behavior in extracted energies point to the need for non-perturbative treatment of Wilson loop couplings, with ongoing work to incorporate these effects using Mathieu functions.
