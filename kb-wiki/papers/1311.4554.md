# Electromagnetic splitting of quark and pseudoscalar meson masses from dynamical QCD + QED

> Lattice QCD simulations are now reaching a precision where electromagnetic corrections from QED become important. In investigating the effects of SU(3) breaking due to quark mass differences within QCD, a group-theoretical analysis of the mass dependence greatly helped us organize our results. We now do the same with electromagnetic charge effects by extending the calculations to dynamical 1+1+1 flavor QCD + QED.

# Electromagnetic splitting of quark and pseudoscalar meson masses from dynamical QCD + QED

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous group-theoretical analysis of SU(3) breaking from quark mass differences to include electromagnetic charge effects, performing the first fully dynamical 1+1+1 flavor QCD+QED lattice simulation. The electromagnetic coupling is simulated at an artificially large value (α_EM ≈ 1/10) rather than the physical value, with the intention to interpolate between this point and pure QCD to reach the physical coupling α_EM = 1/137.

## Method

The simulation uses tree-level Symanzik improved SU(3) gauge action with a noncompact U(1) photon action, and SLiNC fermions with nonperturbatively determined clover coefficient. The authors simulate at the flavor SU(3) symmetric point where all three sea quark masses are equal, keeping the average sea quark mass fixed, and employ partially quenched (PQ) calculations to determine the expansion coefficients for the pseudoscalar meson mass formula. The Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm is used for the u quark while HMC is used for d and s quarks at the symmetric point.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at the physical SU(3) symmetric point: M_π = M_K = 413 MeV
- Lattice spacing at β=5.50 on 24³×48 lattice: approximately 10% smaller than in pure QCD
- Pseudoscalar meson masses at the flavor symmetric point (κ_PQ = 0.1209): aM(n̄n) = 0.4606(30), aM(d̄d) = 0.5655(16), aM(ūd) = 0.7310(15), aM(ūu) = 0.8283(11)
- Pure QCD pseudoscalar mass at the symmetric point: aM = 0.1779(6)
- Additive quark mass renormalization: Δam_n = 0.036 e², Δam_d = Δam_s = 0.056 e², Δam_u = 0.122 e²
- Pure QCD quark mass at the flavor symmetric point: am_u = am_d = am_s = 0.012
- Expansion coefficients: a²β_0^EM = 1.20 e², a²β_1^EM = 0.44 e², a²β_2^EM = +0.025 e²
- Simulated electromagnetic coupling: e² = 1.25

## Context

This work extends the program of Bietenholz (2011) which systematically investigated flavor symmetry breaking in meson and baryon masses using pure QCD with 2+1 flavors. The authors note that previous lattice investigations of electromagnetic corrections to hadron observables were performed on pure QCD background configurations, while a simulation with dynamical photons including meson-photon mixing effects was still missing. This work enables the determination of electromagnetic contributions to hadron masses and quark mass ratios, though the authors caution that the ratio m_d/m_u becomes scheme and scale dependent once QED is included.
