# Unified triquark equations

> We derive covariant equations describing the three-quark bound state in terms of quark and diquark degrees of freedom. The equations are exact in the approximation where three-body forces are neglected. A feature of these equations is that they unify two often-used but seemingly unrelated approaches that model baryons as quark-diquark systems; namely, (i) the approach using Poincaré covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equations driven by a one-quark-exchange kernel [pioneered by Cahill {\it et al.}, Austral.\ J.\ Phys.\ {\bf 42}, 129 (1989) and Reinhardt, Phys.\ Lett.\ B {\bf 244}, 316 (1990)], and (ii) the approach using the quasipotential quark-diquark bound-state equation where the kernel consists of the lowest-order contribution from an underlying quark-quark potential [pioneered by Ebert {\it et al.}, Z.\ Phys.\ C {\bf 76} 111 (1997)]. In particular, we show that each of these approaches corresponds to the unified equations with its kernel taken in different, non-overlapping, approximations.

# Unified triquark equations

## Key claim

The paper derives covariant three-quark bound-state equations that are exact when three-body forces are neglected, and shows that two widely used but seemingly unrelated quark-diquark models of baryons, the Poincaré covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equations and the quasipotential quark-diquark bound-state equation, are actually non-overlapping approximations of the same unified equations, corresponding to keeping only the first or only the second term, respectively, in an infinite series that forms the kernel.

## Method

The authors start from the Faddeev formulation for three distinguishable quarks with only pairwise interactions, writing the three-body kernel as a sum of two-body kernels. They decompose the two-body t matrix into three mutually exclusive parts: the bare two-body kernel, a pole term corresponding to diquark formation, and a regular remainder. By expressing the Faddeev equations in matrix form and expanding the inverse operator that appears after isolating the pole term, they obtain an infinite series whose successive terms correspond to increasing numbers of quark exchanges between quark-diquark states. The procedure is then extended to the case of three identical quarks by imposing antisymmetry through exchange operators.

## Key numbers

* The paper contains no original numerical results; it is a formal derivation of equations.

## Context

This work unifies two long-standing approaches to modeling baryons as quark-diquark systems: the covariant Faddeev equations pioneered by Cahill et al. (1989) and Reinhardt (1990), and the quasipotential quark-diquark model pioneered by Ebert et al. (1997). The derivation follows a procedure similar to the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) reduction of three-particle Faddeev equations to coupled two-particle equations, but differs by decomposing the two-body t matrix into three parts (including the bare kernel) rather than two. The unified equations enable future calculations of baryon form factors (electromagnetic, axial-vector, pseudoscalar) that combine contributions from both kernels, whereas current form-factor calculations treat the two approaches separately. The authors note that analogous unified equations were derived for the tetraquark in a companion paper.
