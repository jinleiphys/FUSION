# Optimized simulations of 50Ti(p,α) and 49Ti(d,α) reactions for hospital-cyclotron production of 47Sc

> The production of 47Sc, a promising radioisotope for targeted radionuclide therapy, by means of hospital-cyclotron reactions is investigated. Two possible routes are considered: the proton-induced reaction on enriched 50Ti targets and the deuteron-induced reaction on enriched 49Ti targets. The cross-sections of the reactions are calculated using the TALYS code with optimized parameters and compared with the available experimental data. The optimal energy ranges for the production of 47Sc are determined by taking into account the thick-target yields and the purity of the product. The results show that both reactions can provide high yields and high purity of 47Sc. The feasibility of producing 47Sc with a hospital cyclotron is demonstrated by performing realistic simulations of the irradiation for both 50Ti(p,α) and 49Ti(d,α) reactions.

## Key claim

Both the proton-induced reaction on enriched 50Ti targets (8–18 MeV) and the deuteron-induced reaction on enriched 49Ti targets (5–10 MeV) can produce 47Sc with radionuclidic purity above 99% using hospital cyclotrons, but the claim depends on the optimized TALYS calculations using genetic algorithms and on the assumption that older experimental data for the 49Ti(d,α) route are unreliable due to target enrichment issues.

## Method

The authors used the TALYS nuclear reaction code with a statistical ensemble of model parameter combinations to generate cross-section bands for 47Sc, 46Sc, and 48Sc production. They then applied genetic algorithms to optimize the level-density parameters (c and p) for the compound nuclei 47Sc and 46Sc, selecting the best-fit TALYS model combinations (PE3–LD4 for the proton route, PE3–LD6 for the deuteron route) to reproduce available experimental data. Finally, they computed thick-target integral yields and radionuclidic purity as a function of time for both reactions under standard irradiation conditions (1 hour, 1 μA).

## Key numbers

- For 50Ti(p,α) at 18–8 MeV: 47Sc yield = 4.138 MBq/(μA·h), 46Sc yield = 8.35×10⁻⁵ MBq/(μA·h), 48Sc yield = 0.0 MBq/(μA·h)
- For 49Ti(d,α) at 10–5 MeV: 47Sc yield = 1.418 MBq/(μA·h), 46Sc yield = 2.14×10⁻⁴ MBq/(μA·h), 48Sc yield = 0.0 MBq/(μA·h)
- Optimized level-density parameters for 50Ti(p,α) route: c = 0.0 MeV⁻¹/² and p = 0.5 MeV for 47Sc compound nucleus
- Optimized level-density parameters for 49Ti(d,α) route: c = 1.573 MeV⁻¹/² and p = 0.390 MeV for 46Sc compound nucleus; c = -0.029 MeV⁻¹/² and p = 1.327 MeV for 47Sc compound nucleus
- Radionuclidic purity remains above 99% for several weeks (proton route) and for almost 20 days (deuteron route)
- 47Sc half-life: 3.349 days

## Context

This work extends the search for viable 47Sc production routes beyond reactor-based methods (Soliman 2020) and earlier cyclotron approaches using 48Ca targets (Misiak 2017). The authors resolve a discrepancy between older cross-section measurements by Gadioli (1981) and newer data by Dellepiane (2022) and De Dominicis (2023) for the 50Ti(p,α) reaction, finding the older data likely erroneous below 30 MeV. For the 49Ti(d,α) route, the only available experimental data from Chen and Miller (1964) suffer from insufficient target enrichment, and the authors note that deuteron break-up contributions (as discussed by Avrigeanu 2023) are not properly included in current TALYS models. The results enable hospital-cyclotron production of 47Sc for targeted radionuclide therapy, with the deuteron route requiring only a 10 MeV beam compared to 18 MeV for protons, though the proton route offers nearly three times higher yield.
