# Gluon saturation effects on the color singlet J/Psi production in high energy dA and AA collisions

> We derive the formulae for the cross section of J/Psi production in high energy pA and AA collisions taking into account the gluon saturation/color glass condensate effects. We then perform the numerical calculations of the corresponding nuclear modification factors and find a good agreement between our calculations and the experimental data on J/Psi production in dA collisions. We also observe that cold nuclear modification effects alone cannot describe the data on J/Psi production in AA collisions. Additional final state suppression (at RHIC) and enhancement (at LHC) mechanisms are required to explain the experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper derives cross-section formulas for J/Psi production in pA and AA collisions that include gluon saturation/color glass condensate effects, finding that cold nuclear modification effects alone cannot describe the AA data; additional final-state suppression (at RHIC) and enhancement (at LHC) mechanisms are required to explain experimental observations.

## Method

The authors derive the cross section for color-singlet J/Psi production in pA collisions using a factorized expression where the gluon projectile interacts with the nucleus, with the c-cbar pair converting from color octet to color singlet via a last inelastic interaction inside the nucleus. They generalize this to AA collisions by letting the initial gluon be emitted from either nucleus and including c-cbar dipole scattering in both nuclei. The quasi-classical approximation assumes the coherence length for J/Psi production is much larger than the nuclear radius, and they later incorporate low-x evolution effects using the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. Numerical calculations use two models for the dipole scattering amplitude: the DHJ model and the bCGC model.

## Key numbers

* Saturation scale parameterization: Q_s^2 = 0.13 GeV^2 * e^(lambda*y) * N_coll, with lambda = 0.3 (DHJ model)
* bCGC model parameters: gamma_s = 0.628, lambda = 0.28, N_0 = 0.7, c = 9.9
* J/Psi wave function parameters: N_T = 1.23, R_T^2 = 6.5 GeV^{-2}
* Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV used in numerical calculations
* Normalization constant C = 1 provides best description of pp and dA data
* LHC calculations performed at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV and 5.5 TeV; almost no change in nuclear modification factor between these energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous publications (Kharzeev et al. 2005, 2008) where they assumed the c-cbar pair propagates through the nucleus in the color octet state and becomes color singlet only after the last interaction; here they drop that assumption by allowing color conversion inside the nucleus. The paper uses the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the dipole scattering amplitude and incorporates the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation. The results enable predictions for J/Psi production in pA collisions at LHC and demonstrate that cold nuclear matter effects alone cannot describe AA data, requiring additional mechanisms such as Matsui-Satz color screening or gluon-induced dissociation at RHIC and enhancement mechanisms at LHC.
