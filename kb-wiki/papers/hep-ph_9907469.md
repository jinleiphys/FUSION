# Bethe-Salpeter Approach for Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation restores exact elastic unitarity in the $s-$ channel by summing up an infinite set of chiral loops. We use this equation to show how a chiral expansion can be undertaken in the two particle irreducible amplitude and the propagators accomplishing exact elastic unitarity at any step. Renormalizability of the amplitudes can be achieved by allowing for an infinite set of counter-terms as it is the case in ordinary Chiral Perturbation Theory. Crossing constraints can be imposed on the parameters to a given order. Within this framework, we calculate the leading and next-to-leading contributions to the elastic $ππ$ scattering amplitudes, for all isospin channels, and to the vector and scalar pion form factors in several renormalization schemes. A satisfactory description of amplitudes and form factors is obtained. In this latter case, Watson's theorem is automatically satisfied. From such studies we obtain a quite accurate determination of some of the ChPT $SU(2)-$low energy parameters (${\bar l}_1 - {\bar l}_2 = -6.1\er{0.1}{0.3} $ and ${\bar l}_6= 19.14 \pm 0.19$). We also compare the two loop piece of our amplitudes to recent two--loop calculations.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation restores exact elastic unitarity in the s-channel by summing an infinite set of chiral loops, but crossing symmetry is only recovered perturbatively when an exact two-particle irreducible amplitude containing t- and u-channel loop contributions is used; at lowest order, the approach violates crossing symmetry, though this can be approximately restored by imposing constraints on the renormalized parameters.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for elastic ππ scattering by expanding the two-particle irreducible amplitude (potential) and the pion propagator in a chiral power series, using the free meson propagator at lowest order and the O(p²) chiral amplitude as the potential. They propose ansätze for the off-shell scattering amplitudes in each isospin channel (I=0,1,2), reduce the integral equation to algebraic systems, and renormalize the divergent integrals by interpreting them as finite free parameters fitted to data or expressed in terms of the Gasser-Leutwyler low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The unitarity limit in standard ChPT is reached at center-of-mass energy √s ~ 4√π f ~ 670 MeV, with pion mass m = 139.57 MeV and pion decay constant f = 93.2 MeV.
- The difference of low-energy parameters is determined as l̄₁ − l̄₂ = −6.1⁺⁰·¹₋₀·₃.
- The low-energy parameter l̄₆ is determined as 19.14 ± 0.19.
- The isoscalar s-wave inverse amplitude is given by T⁻¹₀₀(s) = −I₀(s) + 2(f² + I₂(4m²))² / [2I₄(4m²) + (m² − 2s)f² + (s − 4m²)I₂(4m²)].
- The isovector p-wave inverse amplitude is T⁻¹₁₁(s) = −I₀(s) + (2I₂(4m²) − 6f²)/(s − 4m²).
- The isotensor s-wave inverse amplitude is T⁻¹₂₀(s) = −I₀(s) + 2(f² + I₂(4m²))² / [2I₄(4m²) + (s − 2m²)f² + (s − 4m²)I₂(4m²)].

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach to chiral perturbation theory, building on earlier studies of bubble resummation in the chiral limit by Bedaque and collaborators (1993) and on the authors' own previous work (1999). It provides a framework that justifies several unitarization methods based on algebraic manipulations of on-shell amplitudes, including the Inverse Amplitude Method (Dobado and Pelaez, 1993) and dispersion relation approaches (Pennington and Bugg, 1997), while also enabling improved determination of low-energy parameters and generating two-loop corrections more precisely than recent two-loop calculations.
