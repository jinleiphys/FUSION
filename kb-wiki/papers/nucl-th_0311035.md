# Sum Rules and Spin-Dependent Polarizabilities of the Deuteron in Effective Field Theory

> We construct sum rules for the forward vector and tensor polarizabilities for any spin-$S$ target and apply them to the spin-1 deuteron. We calculate these polarizabilities of the deuteron to the next-to-leading order in the pionless effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper constructs sum rules for forward vector and tensor polarizabilities applicable to any spin-S target and applies them specifically to the spin-1 deuteron, calculating these polarizabilities to next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory; however, the authors note that extracting these polarizabilities directly from Compton scattering is experimentally difficult because high-intensity polarized photon beams at energies of order 1 MeV or less are not currently available.

## Method

The authors derive dispersion relations and sum rules for forward Compton scattering amplitudes on a general spin-S target, then specialize to the spin-1 deuteron. They calculate the scalar electric and magnetic polarizabilities (alpha_E0, beta_M0), the tensor polarizabilities (alpha_E2, beta_M2), and the forward spin polarizability (gamma) using pionless effective field theory, evaluating Feynman diagrams at leading order and next-to-leading order with counterterms fixed from other processes.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.225 MeV, with gamma = sqrt(M_N B) = 45.703 MeV
- Scalar electric polarizability: alpha_E0 = 0.6339 fm^3 (at N^3LO in pionless EFT)
- Magnetic polarizability: beta_M0 = 0.067 fm^3 (about 10% of alpha_E0)
- Electric tensor polarizability: alpha_E2 = -0.068 fm^3 (at LO+NLO in pionless EFT)
- Magnetic tensor polarizability: beta_M2 = 0.195 fm^3 (at N^2LO)
- Forward spin polarizability at leading order: gamma^LO = 3.596 fm^4
- Forward spin polarizability at next-to-leading order: gamma^NLO = 0.324 fm^4 (10% of leading order)
- Asymptotic D/S ratio of deuteron: eta_sd = 0.0254
- Deuteron wave function renormalization: Z_d = 1.69, with rho_d = 1.764 fm
- Singlet S0 scattering length: a^(1S0) = -23.714 fm
- Counterterm from neutron capture: L_1 = 3.26 fm^4

## Context

This work extends sum rule techniques previously applied to spin-independent polarizabilities (Baldin sum rule) and the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule to the spin-dependent vector and tensor polarizabilities of the deuteron. The calculations in pionless effective field theory build on prior EFT work by Chen et al. (2002) and Phillips et al. (2000) on deuteron polarizabilities, and the sum rules provide a potential experimental extraction method using the HIGS facility at Duke. The results enable future measurements of spin-dependent deuteron polarizabilities through polarized photo-production cross sections, which would test the EFT predictions and probe the underlying nuclear dynamics.
