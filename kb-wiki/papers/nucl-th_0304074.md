# Towards a practical approach for self-consistent large amplitude collective motion

> We investigate the use of an operatorial basis in a self-consistent theory of large amplitude collective motion. For the example of the pairing-plus-quadrupole model, which has been studied previously at equilibrium, we show that a small set of carefully chosen state-dependent basis operators is sufficient to approximate the exact solution of the problem accuratly. This approximation is used to study the interplay of quadrupole and pairing degrees of freedom along the collective path for realistic examples of nuclei. We show how this leads to a viable calculational scheme for studying nuclear structure, and discuss the surprising role of pairing collapse.

## Key claim

The authors show that a small set of carefully chosen, state-dependent operatorial basis functions can approximate the exact solution of the self-consistent local harmonic approximation (LHA) for large amplitude collective motion, but only if the basis includes operators with a suppression factor of the quasi-particle energy to a sufficiently high power to capture pairing-vibrational modes accurately.

## Method

The authors apply the local harmonic approximation (LHA) to the pairing-plus-quadrupole model, solving the constrained mean-field problem together with a local random phase approximation (RPA) that determines the constraining operator. To make the repeated RPA diagonalization tractable, they project the RPA matrices onto a small subspace of state-dependent one-body operators, where each operator is weighted by a power of the two-quasi-particle energy. They then follow the collective path self-consistently and solve the collective Schrodinger equation along that path, including zero modes for pairing rotation.

## Key numbers

* For the beta-vibration in 54Cr, the full RPA energy is 1.216 MeV and the projected RPA energy is 1.246 MeV, with a decoupling measure delta_B of 0.0013.
* For the gamma-vibration in 62Zn, the full RPA energy is 1.284 MeV and the projected RPA energy is 1.298 MeV, with delta_B of 0.0010.
* For the Delta(1)-vibration in 54Cr, the full RPA energy is 3.208 MeV and the projected RPA energy is 3.894 MeV, with delta_B of 0.1591.
* For the Delta(2)-vibration in 62Zn, the full RPA energy is 3.537 MeV and the projected RPA energy is 4.797 MeV, with delta_B of 0.7934.
* The quadrupole strength kappa for 54Cr is 0.201367 MeV, with neutron pairing strength G_n = 0.525586 MeV and proton pairing strength G_p = 0.485390 MeV, giving ground state deformation epsilon = 0.167 and pairing gaps Delta_n = Delta_p = 1.60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the operatorial basis method introduced by Walet (1999) for equilibrium RPA calculations to the full collective path away from equilibrium, using the pairing-plus-quadrupole model of Baranger and Kumar (1968). It enables a practical calculational scheme for self-consistent large amplitude collective motion in nuclei, and reveals that pairing collapse plays a surprising role along the collective path, requiring inclusion of pairing-rotational zero modes in the quantized Hamiltonian.
