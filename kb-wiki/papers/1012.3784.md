# Thermal production of relativistic Majorana neutrinos: Strong enhancement by multiple soft scattering

> The production rate of heavy Majorana neutrinos is relevant for models of thermal leptogenesis in the early Universe. In the high temperature limit the production can proceed via the 1 <-> 2 (inverse) decays which are allowed by the thermal masses. We consider new production mechanisms which are obtained by including additional soft gauge interactions with the plasma. We show that an arbitrary number of such interactions gives leading order contributions, and we sum all of them. The rate turns out to be smooth in the region where the 1 <-> 2 processes are kinematically forbidden. At higher temperature it is enhanced by a factor 3 compared to the 1 <-> 2 rate.

## Key claim

The production rate of heavy Majorana neutrinos in the early Universe receives leading-order contributions from processes involving an arbitrary number of soft gauge interactions with the plasma, which must be summed to all orders; at high temperature this rate is enhanced by a factor of 3 compared to the previously considered 1 ↔ 2 (inverse) decay rate, and the rate remains smooth even in the region where the 1 ↔ 2 processes are kinematically forbidden.

## Method

The authors computed the production rate of heavy Majorana neutrinos in a hot electroweak plasma using thermal field theory, relating the out-of-equilibrium production rate to a real-time equilibrium correlation function via the retarded self-energy. They integrated out hard field modes to obtain asymptotic masses for Standard Model particles, then derived a recursion relation for n-point functions with soft gauge bosons, which led to an integral equation for the self-energy that resums all ladder diagrams with multiple soft SU(2) and U(1) gauge boson exchanges. The resulting integral equation was solved numerically to obtain the production rate including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect from collinear emission.

## Key numbers

* The baryon-to-photon ratio from combined analysis of large-scale structure and CMB data is (6.21 ± 0.16) × 10⁻¹⁰.
* The asymptotic thermal mass for the Higgs boson is m_φ² = (1/16)(3g² + g'² + 4h_t² + 8λ)T².
* The asymptotic thermal mass for the lepton doublet is m_ℓ² = (1/16)(3g² + g'²)T².
* At high temperature, the production rate including multiple soft scattering is enhanced by a factor of 3 compared to the 1 ↔ 2 rate alone.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of Majorana neutrino production via 1 ↔ 2 (inverse) decays that were considered by Giudice (2004) and Kiessig (2010), which used thermal masses to allow decays of Higgs bosons into Majorana neutrinos and leptons. The authors show that additional soft gauge interactions, previously neglected, contribute at leading order and must be resummed, similar to the treatment of photon production in a quark-gluon plasma by Aurenche (2002) and Arnold (2001), and to the general approach for thermal particle production developed by Besak and Bodeker (2010). This result constitutes an important step toward a complete treatment of thermal leptogenesis and enables more accurate calculations of the baryon asymmetry in the early Universe.
