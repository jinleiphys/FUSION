# Electromagnetic structure of the lowest-lying decuplet resonances in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of the leading SU(3)-breaking $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$-corrections to the electromagnetic moments and charge radius (CR) of the lowest-lying decuplet resonances in covariant chiral perturbation theory. In particular, the magnetic dipole moment (MDM) of the members of the decuplet is predicted fixing the only low-energy constant (LEC) present up to this order with the well measured MDM of the $Ω^-$. We predict $μ_Δ^{++}=6.04(13)$ and $μ_Δ^+=2.84(2)$ which agree well with the current experimental information. For the electric quadrupole moment (EQM) and the CR we use state-of-the-art lattice QCD results to determine the corresponding LECs, whereas for the magnetic octupole moment (MOM) there is no unknown LEC up to the order considered here and we obtain a pure prediction. We compare our results with those reported in large $N_c$, lattice QCD, heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory and other models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of the leading SU(3)-breaking corrections at order \( \mathcal{O}(p^3) \) to the electromagnetic moments and charge radius of the lowest-lying decuplet resonances using covariant chiral perturbation theory, with the magnetic dipole moment of the \( \Omega^- \) used to fix the only low-energy constant, and lattice QCD results used to determine the constants for the electric quadrupole moment and charge radius, while the magnetic octupole moment is a pure prediction with no unknown low-energy constant at this order.

## Method

The authors employed covariant chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to compute the leading SU(3)-breaking corrections at \( \mathcal{O}(p^3) \) to the electromagnetic form factors of the spin-3/2 decuplet baryons. They evaluated one-loop diagrams involving octet and decuplet baryons and pseudoscalar mesons, fixing the low-energy constant \( g_d \) for magnetic dipole moments using the experimental \( \Omega^- \) magnetic dipole moment, and determining the constants \( g_q \) and \( g_{er} \) for electric quadrupole moments and charge radii using lattice QCD results for the \( \Omega^- \). The magnetic octupole moment required no new low-energy constant and was obtained purely from the chiral loops.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole moment of \( \Delta^{++} \): \( \mu_{\Delta^{++}} = 6.04(13) \, \mu_N \) (nuclear magnetons), compared to the experimental range \( 3.7 \) to \( 7.5 \, \mu_N \) from the Particle Data Group.
- Magnetic dipole moment of \( \Delta^{+} \): \( \mu_{\Delta^{+}} = 2.84(2) \, \mu_N \), compared to the experimental value \( 2.7^{+1.0}_{-1.3} \, (\text{stat}) \pm 1.5 \, (\text{syst}) \pm 3 \, (\text{theor}) \, \mu_N \) from Kotulla et al. (2002).
- Magnetic dipole moment of \( \Omega^- \): \( \mu_{\Omega^-} = -2.02 \, \mu_N \) (used as input to fix the low-energy constant, with experimental uncertainty \( \pm 0.05 \, \mu_N \)).
- Electric quadrupole moment of \( \Delta^+ \) (using quenched lattice QCD input for \( \Omega^- \)): \( \mathcal{Q}_{\Delta^+} = -2.4(1.5) \times 10^{-2} \, \text{fm}^2 \).
- Electric quadrupole moment of \( \Omega^- \) (from quenched lattice QCD): \( \mathcal{Q}_{\Omega^-} = 0.86(12) \times 10^{-2} \, \text{fm}^2 \) (used to fix the low-energy constant \( g_q \)).

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral perturbation theory formalism previously applied to baryon-octet magnetic moments by Geng et al. (2008, 2009) to the decuplet resonances, improving upon heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Butler et al. (1993) and Banerjee et al. (1995) which overestimated chiral corrections. The results are compared with predictions from large \( N_c \) (Luty et al., 1994), lattice QCD (Leinweber et al., 1992; Lee et al., 2005), and quark models (Hikasa et al., 1992; Schlumpf et al., 1993), and they enable future experimental tests of the \( \Delta(1232) \) magnetic dipole moment and electric quadrupole moment, as well as unquenched lattice QCD calculations of the \( \Omega^- \) electromagnetic properties.
