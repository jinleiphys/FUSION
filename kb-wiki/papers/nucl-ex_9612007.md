# Search for non-Poissonian behavior in nuclear beta-decay

> We performed two independent counting experiments on a beta-emitting source of Sm151 by measuring the gamma-photon emitted in a fraction of the decays. For counting times ranging from 10**-3 to 5.12*10**4 seconds, our measurements show no evidence of deviations from Poissonian behavior and, in particular, no sign of 1/f noise. These measurements put strong limits on non-Poissonian components of the fluctuations for the subset of decays accompanied by gamma, and corresponding limits for the total number of beta-decays. In particular, the magnitude of a hypothetical flicker floor is strongly bounded also for the beta-decay. This result further constrains theories predicting anomalous fluctuations in nuclear decays.

# Search for non-Poissonian behavior in nuclear beta-decay

## Key claim
The authors find no evidence of deviations from Poissonian statistics in the beta-decay of Sm-151 for counting times from 10^-3 to 5.12×10^4 seconds, but this conclusion applies strictly to the subset of decays accompanied by gamma emission; limits on non-Poissonian behavior for the total beta-decay are weaker by a factor of approximately 3000 for most quantities, except for the flicker floor where the bound is equally strong.

## Method
Two independent counting experiments (A and B) were performed on a Sm-151 source (activity 3.7 GBq) by detecting the 21.532 keV gamma photons emitted in 0.91% of beta decays that populate an excited state of Eu-151. The photons were detected with a NaI(Tl) crystal and photomultiplier tube, with counting periods T ranging from 10^-3 s to 5.12×10^4 s (experiment A) or 1.28×10^4 s (experiment B). For each T, 64 consecutive counts were recorded and the Allan variance was computed to search for 1/f noise and other non-Poissonian components.

## Key numbers
- Mean count rate in experiment A: (5.3687 ± 0.00025) × 10^3 counts/s
- Mean count rate in experiment B: (2.4262 ± 0.00022) × 10^4 counts/s
- Measured reduced Allan variance R(T) as low as 6 × 10^-9 (experiment A) and 3 × 10^-9 (experiment B) at the longest counting periods without deviation from Poissonian behavior
- Upper bound on flicker floor F < 3 × 10^-9, valid also for the total beta-decay statistics
- Upper bound on the ratio of 1/f noise to Poissonian noise at the longest time interval: (C/f)/(m T_max) < 1 × 10^-5 for the gamma channel; for total beta decays the limit is weaker: (C_beta/f)/(m_beta T_max) < 3 × 10^-2
- Fit of M(T) × R(T) yields 0.99 ± 0.02, consistent with pure Poissonian statistics
- Mean lifetime of Sm-151: (130 ± 12) years
- Branching ratio for the detected gamma channel: xi = (3.14 ± 0.22) × 10^-4
- Excited state energy: (21.532 ± 0.068) keV; mean lifetime of excited state: 1.38 × 10^-8 seconds
- Upper limit on coherence factor zeta in Handel's model: zeta < 1 × 10^-5 (using average electron energy 13.96 keV)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies on gamma decay statistics of Sn-119m (Boscaino 1994a, 1994b), where deviations from Poissonian behavior were explained by time-dependent statistics rather than exotic effects. The experiment directly addresses conflicting results in the literature: some studies on alpha decays (Am-241 by Prestwich 1986, 1988; Po-210 by Alessio 1989) and beta decays (Cs-137 by Gopala 1988) found Poissonian behavior, while others on alpha sources (Am-241, Pu-239, Cm-244 by Gong 1983, Kolesnik 1986, 1987, Rusov 1992) and beta sources (Tl-204 by Alessio 1989, Y-90 by Alessio 1991, Sr-90/Y-90 by Gopala 1994) reported variances exceeding Poissonian values by more than an order of magnitude. The results constrain Handel's quantum 1/f noise theory (Van Vliet 1982), which predicts a coherence factor zeta that Gopala (1994) measured as 5.2×10^-3 to 8.3×10^-3 in other beta decays, more than two orders of magnitude above the authors' upper limit. The experiment enables further tests of theories predicting anomalous fluctuations in nuclear decays, particularly those distinguishing between beta, gamma, and alpha decay mechanisms.
