# Probing Hadronization with Strangeness

> The Omega-bar/Omega ratio originating from string decays is predicted to be larger than unity in proton-proton interaction at SPS energies. The anti-omega dominance increases with decreasing beam energy. This surprising behavior is caused by the combinatorics of quark-antiquark production in small and low-mass strings. Since this behavior is not found in a statistical description of hadron production in proton-proton collisions, it may serve as a key observable to probe the hadronization mechanism in such collisions.

# Probing Hadronization with Strangeness

## Key claim
The Omega-bar/Omega ratio in proton-proton interactions at SPS energies is predicted to exceed unity within string fragmentation models, with anti-omega dominance increasing at lower beam energies; however, this prediction is conditional on the string mass being small, and preliminary NA49 data with 95% confidence place the ratio below 0.5, which would instead support statistical hadronization.

## Method
The authors compare predictions from three string fragmentation models (UrQMD, NeXuS, PYTHIA) with two canonical statistical model approaches for Omega-bar/Omega ratios in proton-proton collisions at 160 GeV. String models implement color flux tube decay via the Schwinger mechanism, while statistical models enforce exact conservation of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge using canonical ensembles with fitted temperature, volume, and strangeness suppression parameters.

## Key numbers
- Omega-bar/Omega ratio predicted by string models exceeds unity in pp at 160 GeV, with all three string models agreeing within ±20%
- Preliminary NA49 data: roughly 45 Omega- extracted, no anti-Omega observed; 95% confidence level places Omega-bar/Omega ratio below 0.5
- Canonical Model I fit to preliminary NA49 pp data yields T = 183.7 ± 6.7 MeV, VT³ = 6.49 ± 1.33, and <s s-bar> = 0.405 ± 0.026 with χ²/dof = 11.7/9
- Canonical Model II uses T ≈ 158 MeV and μ_B ≈ 238 MeV from NA49 Pb-Pb data, with correlation volume V₀ ≈ 5.8 fm³ (R₀ ≈ 1.12 fm)
- 4π yields in pp at 160 GeV: NeXuS gives Omega = 0.48×10⁻⁴ and anti-Omega = 0.79×10⁻⁴; PYTHIA gives 0.17×10⁻⁴ and 0.30×10⁻⁴; UrQMD gives 0.66×10⁻⁴ and 1.05×10⁻⁴; Canonical Model I gives 0.46×10⁻⁴ and 0.31×10⁻⁴; Canonical Model II gives 0.41×10⁻⁴ and 0.24×10⁻⁴

## Context
This work extends the debate between statistical hadronization models (Hagedorn 1965; Becattini 1997) and color flux tube dynamics for elementary hadron-hadron collisions. The Omega-bar/Omega ratio is proposed as a key observable to distinguish these mechanisms, since statistical models always predict ratios ≤ 1 while string models predict > 1 for small strings. The paper also introduces balance functions as a complementary probe of hadronization time scales, discussed further in other contributions to the same proceedings (Scott, Gary, Marguerite). The results enable experimental tests at SPS energies using NA49 data, with first preliminary results favoring the statistical hadronization hypothesis.
