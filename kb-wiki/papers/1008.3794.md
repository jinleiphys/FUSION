# DN interaction from meson exchange

> A model of the DN interaction is presented which is developed in close analogy to the meson-exchange KbarN potential of the Juelich group utilizing SU(4) symmetry constraints. The main ingredients of the interaction are provided by vector meson (rho, omega) exchange and higher-order box diagrams involving D*N, DΔ, and D*Δintermediate states. The coupling of DN to the pi-Lambda_c and pi-Sigma_c channels is taken into account. The interaction model generates the Lambda_c(2595) resonance dynamically as a DN quasi-bound state. Results for DN total and differential cross sections are presented and compared with predictions of an interaction model that is based on the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term. Some features of the Lambda_c(2595) resonance are discussed and the role of the near-by pi-Sigma_c threshold is emphasized. Selected predictions of the orginal KbarN model are reported too. Specifically, it is pointed out that the model generates two poles in the partial wave corresponding to the Lambda(1405) resonance.

# DN interaction from meson exchange

## Key claim

The paper presents a meson-exchange model for the DN interaction that generates the Λc(2595) resonance dynamically as a DN quasi-bound state, but the model requires fine-tuning of scalar-meson coupling constants (σ from 1 to 2.6 and a0 from -2.6 to -4.8) to reproduce the experimentally observed resonance positions, indicating that SU(4) symmetry alone is insufficient for quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel DN interaction model in close analogy to the Juelich meson-exchange KbarN potential, using SU(4) symmetry constraints and G-parity conservation to derive vector meson (ρ, ω) exchange contributions and higher-order box diagrams with D*N, DΔ, and D*Δ intermediate states. The model includes coupling to πΛc and πΣc channels and solves a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation in time-ordered perturbation theory to obtain scattering amplitudes. The scalar-meson contributions were fine-tuned so that the dynamically generated states in the S01 and S11 partial waves match the Λc(2595) and Σc(2800) resonance positions from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Λc(2595) resonance pole position: 2593.9 + i 2.88 MeV (S01 partial wave)
- Second S01 pole: 2603.2 + i 63.1 MeV
- Σc(2800) resonance pole position: 2797.3 + i 5.86 MeV (S11 partial wave)
- P01 pole: 2804.4 + i 2.04 MeV (tentatively associated with Λc(2765))
- DN scattering length for I=0: -0.41 + i 0.04 fm
- DN scattering length for I=1: -2.07 + i 0.57 fm
- KbarN scattering length for I=0: -1.21 + i 1.18 fm (from the Juelich model)
- KbarN scattering length for I=1: 1.01 + i 0.73 fm
- KbarN S01 pole positions: 1435.8 + i 25.6 MeV and 1334.3 + i 62.3 MeV
- Kaonic hydrogen energy shift ΔE: 217 eV (Juelich model prediction)
- Kaonic hydrogen width Γ: 849 eV (Juelich model prediction)
- DN I=1 cross section at threshold: approximately 600 mb

## Context

This work extends the Juelich group's meson-exchange approach from the KbarN system (Muller-Groeling et al., 1990) to the charm sector via SU(4) symmetry, following earlier work on the DbarN interaction (Haidenbauer et al., 2007). The model is compared with the SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa approach of Mizutani et al. (2006), which generates different pole structures including three S01 and two S11 states. The results enable predictions for DN cross sections needed for detector design at the FAIR facility and provide a framework for studying D-mesic nuclei and J/ψ dissociation in matter. The paper also reports previously unpublished features of the original Juelich KbarN model, including its two-pole structure for the Λ(1405) resonance, which shows quantitative similarity to the chiral SU(3) unitary approach of Borasoy et al. (2005).
