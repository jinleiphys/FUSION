# Soft pions at high energy as an origin of flavor asymmetry of the light sea quarks in the nucleon

> By using the soft pion theorem in the inclusive reactions, soft pions' contribution to the structure function $F_2$ in the nucleon is estimated. It is shown that this contribution produces such a large flavor asymmetry in the light sea quark distributions that it gives about $30\sim 50 %$ of the NMC deficit in the Gottfried sum.

# Soft pions at high energy as an origin of flavor asymmetry of the light sea quarks in the nucleon

## Key claim
Soft pions produced in inclusive reactions at high energy, via the soft pion theorem applied under approximate Feynman scaling, can account for about 30–50% of the NMC deficit in the Gottfried sum, providing a mechanism that complements mesonic models in the medium- and high-energy regions where those models lack predictive power.

## Method
The author applies the soft pion theorem to inclusive virtual-photon–nucleon scattering, taking the soft pion limit in the null-plane formalism to avoid velocity-dependent ambiguities. The hadronic tensor is constructed from the squared amplitude classified into terms from axial-vector current attachments to initial nucleons, final nucleons/antinucleons, and null-plane commutators; contributions from spin-dependent terms and nucleon/antinucleon multiplicity are retained. The resulting expression for the flavor asymmetry in the structure function difference (F₂ᵉᵖ − F₂ᵉⁿ) is converted into an antiquark asymmetry (d̄ − ū) and evaluated numerically using valence quark distributions at Q₀² = 4 GeV², with the pion phase space factor integrated over transverse momentum and Feynman x_F constraints.

## Key numbers
- The soft pion contribution to the Gottfried sum integral J(0,1) = ∫₀¹ (dx/x)(F₂ᵉᵖ − F₂ᵉⁿ)|_soft is estimated to be about −0.04 to −0.02.
- For a=0.20, b=1, c=0.1: J(10⁻⁴, 0.2) = −0.018, J(10⁻⁵, 0.2) = −0.021, J(10⁻⁶, 0.2) = −0.023.
- For a=0.20, b=1, c=0.1: J(10⁻⁹, 1) = −0.030 and J(0.2, 1) = −0.005.
- Reducing c from 0.1 to 0.05 (with a=0.2, b=1) gives J(10⁻⁴, 0.2) = −0.012, a ~25% reduction.
- The NMC deficit is taken as 0.07 following the E866/NuSea experiment; the soft pion contribution reaches about 30–50% of this value.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic approach to the modified Gottfried sum rule, which had already shown that the NMC deficit reflects hadronic vacuum effects from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. It addresses a shortcoming of mesonic models (reviewed in the mesonic models literature) that account for only about half the NMC deficit via πNN and πNΔ processes, with higher resonances and multiparticle states giving uncertain contributions. The soft pion mechanism provides a background contribution that should be added to the meson cloud model, potentially explaining the small excess in E866/NuSea data compared to those models, and enables a dynamical origin for flavor asymmetry in the medium- and small-x region where mesonic models lack predictive power.
