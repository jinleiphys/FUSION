# Mid-Point Technique for Calculating Divergent integrals

> A mid-point technique is suggested to overcome the difficulties in other techniques. The modified effective interaction quark potential which uses to calculate different properties of the NJL model such as the constituent quark mass, the pressure, and the energy density is solved using the present technique. The present method gives good accuracy for the mathematical problem and avoids the physical difficulty in the previous works.

## Key claim

The mid-point technique transforms divergent integrals into finite integrals whose accuracy depends only on a tunable parameter \(n\), avoiding the missing terms and cut-off dependence of prior regularization methods; the author claims this gives "good accuracy" but the method is demonstrated only for the NJL model with a specific running coupling constant \(G(T,\mu)=G_0 e^{-A(T+\mu)}\).

## Method

The author applies an N-midpoint quadrature to the divergent scalar density integral of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, first changing variables to \(y=e^{-p'}\) to map the infinite momentum range onto \([0,1]\). The integral is then discretized using \(n\) equally spaced midpoints \(A_i = i/n + 1/(2n)\), yielding an analytic sum (Eq. 8) that replaces the divergent sea term. The same transformation is applied to the thermodynamic potential (Eq. 10), and the pressure and energy density are obtained via standard thermodynamic relations. The coupling constant is made temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent via \(G(T,\mu)=G_0 e^{-A(T+\mu)}\).

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant used for scaling is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* For \(n=400\), the maximum momentum in the transformed integral is \(621.67\) MeV; for \(n=1000\), it is \(706.88\) MeV.
* The sea term (third term in Eq. 8) is \(2.7407 \times 10^{13}\) MeV at \(n=400\) and \(9.8946 \times 10^{13}\) MeV at \(n=1000\).
* The constituent quark mass is "steady" up to 75 MeV temperature at zero chemical potential, then decreases with increasing temperature.

## Context

This work extends the NJL model calculations of Refs. [18,19] by introducing a running coupling constant \(G(T,\mu)\) and applying the mid-point technique to handle the divergent Dirac-sea integral, which the author argues avoids the cut-off dependence of Pauli-Villars and three-momentum cutoff methods used in prior works (e.g., Refs. [20,21]). The method enables calculation of constituent quark mass, pressure, and energy density without a fixed momentum cutoff, and the author notes the phase transition remains a crossover as in Refs. [18,19]. The approach is intended to improve on the finite-part and summability methods cited in the introduction.
