# $η$-$η'$ mixing in large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory: discussion, phenomenology, and prospects

> A systematic study of the $η$-$η'$ mixing in Large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory is presented with special emphasis on the role of the next-to-next-to-leading-order contributions in the combined momentum, quark-mass, and $1/N_c$ expansions. At this order, loop corrections as well as OZI-rule-violating pieces need to be included. Mixing angles as well as pseudoscalar decay constants are discussed within this framework. The results are compared with recent phenomenological approaches.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic study of η-η' mixing in Large-Nc chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the combined momentum, quark-mass, and 1/Nc expansion, but the numerical results are preliminary because the large number of unknown low-energy constants (LECs), especially those associated with OZI-rule-violating terms, prevents a fully constrained prediction without additional input from phenomenology or lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors construct the effective Lagrangian for U(3) chiral perturbation theory up to order δ² (NNLO) in the simultaneous expansion in momenta p ∼ √δ, quark masses m ∼ δ, and 1/Nc ∼ δ. They compute the η-η' mass matrix including loop corrections and OZI-rule-violating contributions, then diagonalize it through a sequence of field redefinitions (removing higher-derivative terms, normalizing kinetic terms, and rotating to mass eigenstates) to extract the mixing angle θ and the decay constants in the octet-singlet basis. The unknown LECs are handled via two strategies: matching to physical observables (Strategy NLO I) or using SU(3) LECs from Bijnens (2014) with additional physical input for OZI-violating parameters (Strategy NLO II).

## Key numbers

* Leading-order mixing angle: θ⁽⁰⁾ = -19.6°, obtained using M₀² = (0.820 GeV)² from physical masses.
* For FK/Fπ in L NcChPT: LO = 1, NLO = +0.15, NNLO = +0.03 (with NNLO breakdown: loop +0.05, Ci -0.01, LiLj -0.02).
* For MK²/MK,phys² at μ = 0.77 GeV: LO = 1.26, NLO = +0.01, NNLO = -0.27 (with NNLO breakdown: loop -0.15, Ci -0.14, L4,L6 +0.03, LiLj -0.01).
* The η'-loop contributes -0.10 to the kaon mass NNLO correction, compared to -0.05 from π,K,η loops.
* The C19 LEC contributes -0.29 to the kaon mass NNLO correction, while C12,14,17,31 contribute +0.15.

## Context

This work extends the Large-Nc chiral perturbation theory framework for η-η' mixing developed by Herrera-Siklody (1996), Kaiser (1998, 2000), and Leutwyler (1997) by including NNLO loop corrections and OZI-rule-violating terms systematically. The results are compared with phenomenological determinations from Leutwyler (1997), Feldmann (1998), Benayoun (1999), Escribano (2005, 2013), and others. The study enables future one-loop calculations of VPγ transitions and meson-baryon interactions involving η and η' mesons, and provides a framework for matching to lattice QCD results to determine the poorly known OZI-violating LECs.
