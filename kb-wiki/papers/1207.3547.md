# Energy Release Associated with Quark Phase Transition in Neutron Stars: Comparative Analysis of Maxwell and Glendenning Scenarios

> We study the compact stars internal structure and observable characteristics alterations due to the quark deconfinement phase transition. To proceed with, we investigate the properties of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in the improved relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, including a scalar-isovector δ-meson effective field. In order to describe the quark phase, we use the improved version of the MIT bag model, in which the interactions between u, d and s quarks inside the bag are taken into account in the one-gluon exchange approximation. We compute the amount of energy released by the corequake for both cases of deconfinement phase transition scenarios, corresponding to the Maxwellian type ordinary first-order phase transition and the phase transition with formation of a mixed quark-hadron phase (Glendenning scenario).

## Key claim

The energy released by a quark deconfinement phase transition in neutron stars depends on the phase transition scenario: for a fixed baryonic mass, the Glendenning construction (mixed hadron-quark phase) yields more conversion energy than the Maxwell construction (ordinary first-order transition), but the minimum baryonic mass required to trigger the transition is larger in the Maxwell case.

## Method

The authors model isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory including a scalar-isovector δ-meson field, and describe the quark phase with an improved MIT bag model incorporating one-gluon exchange interactions among u, d, and s quarks. They compute equations of state for both the Maxwell construction (first-order phase transition at constant pressure with a density jump) and the Glendenning construction (mixed hadron-quark phase with continuous pressure and density). Using these EOSs, they integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain gravitational mass, radius, and baryonic mass of compact stars, then calculate the energy release from the difference in binding energy between the critical hadronic configuration and the post-transition hybrid star.

## Key numbers

- Maxwell construction phase transition occurs at constant pressure \(P_0 = 2.11\) MeV/fm³, with nucleonic energy density \(\varepsilon_N = 114.5\) MeV/fm³ and quark energy density \(\varepsilon_Q = 271.4\) MeV/fm³.
- Glendenning construction mixed phase boundaries: \(\varepsilon_N = 72.79\) MeV/fm³, \(P_N = 0.43\) MeV/fm³ at the lower boundary; \(\varepsilon_Q = 1280.88\) MeV/fm³, \(P_Q = 327.75\) MeV/fm³ at the upper boundary.
- Density discontinuity parameter for Maxwell construction: \(\lambda = 2.327\).
- Maximum mass of stars containing deconfined quarks: \(M_{max} = 1.853 M_\odot\) for Glendenning construction; \(M_{max} = 1.828 M_\odot\) for Maxwell construction.
- Energy release from the quark deconfinement phase transition is of order \(10^{50} \div 10^{52}\) erg.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous series (Alaverdyan et al., 2010–2012) comparing Maxwell and Glendenning constructions for quark deconfinement in neutron stars, using RMF theory with δ-meson fields. It builds on the stability criterion of Seidov (1971) for first-order phase transitions and on the mixed-phase formalism of Glendenning (1992). The results enable observational tests to distinguish between the two phase transition scenarios, with implications for gamma-ray burst and gravitational wave emission models (Bombaci and Drago, 2000; Marranghello et al., 2002).
