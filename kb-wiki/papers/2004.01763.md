# Many-body Dyson equation approach to the seniority model of pairing

> As is well known, the single level seniority model of pairing has been solved exactly since long using angular momentum algebra. It is shown that it can also be solved using the Dyson equation of standard many-body theory. The formalism shows some interesting many-body aspects.

## Key claim

The single-level seniority model of pairing, which has been solved exactly for decades using angular momentum algebra, can also be solved exactly using the Dyson equation of standard many-body theory together with equation-of-motion techniques, though the procedure is "not completely trivial" and reveals interesting many-body aspects.

## Method

The author constructs generalized single-particle operators that kill the exact ground state, then minimizes an energy-weighted sum rule to obtain a 2x2 eigenvalue equation. This is recast into a Dyson equation for a 2x2 Green's function matrix, whose poles yield the chemical potentials and whose spectral representation gives occupation numbers and the ground-state energy. The same approach is extended to pair-addition and pair-removal modes using extended RPA operators.

## Key numbers

- The exact ground-state energy for the seniority model is given by \( E(N,s) = -\frac{G}{4}(s^2 - 2s(\Omega + 1) + 2N(\Omega+ 1) - N^2) \), where \(N\) is particle number, \(s\) is seniority, and \(\Omega = j+1/2\) is half the shell degeneracy.
- The chemical potentials are \(\mu_+ = G N/2\) for adding a particle and \(\mu_- = -G[2(\Omega+1)-N]/2\) for removing a particle.
- The exact expectation value \(\langle S_+S_-\rangle = \frac{N}{4}(2\Omega - N + 2)\) is reproduced by the Dyson equation approach.
- The occupation number per magnetic substate is \(n_m = N/(2\Omega)\), independent of the azimuthal quantum number \(m\).

## Context

This work extends the standard many-body Dyson equation formalism to a solvable model (the seniority model of Racah and Kerman) that was previously treated only with angular momentum algebra. The approach demonstrates that the Dyson equation combined with equation-of-motion techniques can reproduce the exact solution, and the author suggests this method may generalize to more realistic multi-level problems such as the Lipkin model, where self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) methods have been applied previously by the same author.
