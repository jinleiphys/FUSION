# Testing the hadronic spectrum in the strange sector

> Heavier resonances are continually being added to the hadronic spectrum from the Particle Data Group that follow an exponentially increasing mass spectrum. However, it has been suggested that even further states predicted from Quark Models are needed in the hadronic spectrum in order to improve the agreement between the hadron resonance gas model predictions and lattice QCD data. We find that the inclusion of such states with extrapolated branching ratios slightly decreases the freezeout temperature. To eliminate ambiguities, we introduce a first principle method to extract the freeze-out temperature for charged kaons from experimental data, which yields a lower bound of $T_{\text{fo}} \gtrsim $145 MeV for the highest collision energy at RHIC.

# Testing the hadronic spectrum in the strange sector

## Key claim
Including additional hadronic states predicted by Quark Models (with extrapolated branching ratios) into the hadron resonance gas model produces only a small decrease in the extracted freeze-out temperature. A new first-principles method comparing lattice QCD susceptibilities to experimental charged kaon data yields only a lower bound of T_fo ≳ 145 MeV for the highest RHIC collision energy, rather than a precise value.

## Method
The author compared net-charge and net-proton fluctuation ratios (χ₁/χ₂) from the hadron resonance gas model to STAR Beam Energy Scan data, using both the standard PDG resonance list and an extended list including Quark Model states with extrapolated branching ratios enforcing quantum number and mass conservation. Separately, ratios of charged kaon susceptibilities (χ₂^K/χ₁^K) computed from lattice QCD partial pressures were compared to preliminary STAR data at √s_NN = 200 GeV to extract a freeze-out temperature bound.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature for light matter decreases by a small amount when Quark Model states are added to the hadronic spectrum (no specific numerical shift given in text).
- A lower bound of T_fo ≳ 145 MeV is obtained for charged kaon freeze-out at the highest RHIC collision energy (√s_NN = 200 GeV) from the comparison of lattice QCD susceptibilities to preliminary STAR data.

## Context
This work extends the hadron resonance gas model studies of Bazavov (2014) by including resonance decay effects that were omitted in that prior work, and addresses the tension between light and strange freeze-out temperatures reported by Floris (2014). The new method using charged kaon susceptibilities from lattice QCD (Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration data) enables direct first-principles extraction of strangeness freeze-out parameters, though current experimental uncertainties limit the result to a lower bound; smaller error bars are needed to resolve the light-versus-strange sector discrepancy.
