# Relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions: general aspects and recent developments

> Relativistic hydrodynamics has been quite successful in explaining the collective behaviour of the QCD matter produced in high energy heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We briefly review the latest developments in the hydrodynamical modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Essential ingredients of the model such as the hydrodynamic evolution equations, dissipation, initial conditions, equation of state, and freeze-out process are reviewed. We discuss observable quantities such as particle spectra and anisotropic flow and effect of viscosity on these observables. Recent developments such as event-by-event fluctuations, flow in small systems (proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions), flow in ultra central collisions, longitudinal fluctuations and correlations and flow in intense magnetic field are also discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic hydrodynamics has been successful in explaining collective behavior of QCD matter produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, but the theory of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics is not yet conclusively established, with several inconsistencies and approximations in the Israel-Stewart formulation that prevent proper understanding of thermodynamic and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors review the formulation of relativistic fluid dynamics from a phenomenological perspective, covering ideal hydrodynamics, covariant thermodynamics, and the first-order Navier-Stokes and second-order Israel-Stewart theories of dissipative fluid dynamics. They discuss essential modeling ingredients including initial conditions, pre-equilibrium dynamics, equations of state, the Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure for particle production, and hadronic rescattering after freeze-out. The review also covers extraction of transport coefficients from anisotropic flow data and recent developments such as event-by-event fluctuations, flow in small systems (proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions), ultra-central collisions, longitudinal fluctuations, and flow in intense magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) lower bound for shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is $\eta/s \ge 1/4\pi$ for all fluids in the strong coupling limit of holographic theories
- For a weakly interacting relativistic Bose gas, the entropy per particle is $s/n = 3.6$, leading to $\eta/s \gtrsim 0.09$, which is close to the KSS bound
- The estimated $\eta/s$ of QGP was so close to the KSS bound that it led to the claim that matter formed at RHIC was the most perfect fluid ever observed

## Context

This review extends the work of Romatschke (2007), Song (2010), Luzum (2010), and Qiu (2011) on hydrodynamic analysis of spectra and azimuthal anisotropy at RHIC and LHC, which suggested the matter formed is strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP). It also builds on the foundational work of Eckart (1940), Landau-Lifshitz, and Israel-Stewart (1979) on relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. The review enables future quantification of transport properties of QGP from experimental data and addresses theoretical foundations needed to confirm whether QGP is indeed the most perfect fluid ever created.
