# Gamma-Z box corrections to weak charges of heavy nuclei in atomic parity violation

> We present a new dispersive formulation of the gamma-Z box radiative corrections to weak charges of bound protons and neutrons in atomic parity violation (APV) measurements on heavy nuclei such as 133-Cs and 213-Ra. We evaluate for the first time a small but important additional correction arising from Pauli blocking of nucleons in a heavy nucleus. Overall, we find a significant shift in the gamma-Z correction to the weak charge of 133-Cs, approximately 4 times larger than the current uncertainty on the value of sin^2(theta_W), but with a reduced error compared to earlier estimates.

## Key claim

The gamma-Z box correction to the weak charge of 133-Cs is approximately 4 times larger than the current uncertainty on sin^2(theta_W), but with a reduced error compared to earlier estimates by Marciano and Sirlin (1983). The new corrections are 15% smaller for free nucleons and 20% smaller for nucleons bound in a heavy nucleus due to Pauli blocking.

## Method

The authors used a dispersive formulation of the gamma-Z box radiative correction, writing the imaginary part in terms of inclusive gamma-Z interference structure functions F_{3,gamma-Z}^N obtained from lepton-nucleon scattering. They split the contributions into three kinematic regions: elastic (depending on nucleon elastic magnetic and axial-vector form factors), resonance (using parametrizations from Lalakulich et al. (2007) with modified isospin factors), and deep-inelastic scattering (using parton distribution functions above Q_0^2 between 1 and 2 GeV^2). For bound nucleons in 133-Cs, they introduced a Pauli blocking correction using a Fermi gas model with Fermi momenta derived from Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key numbers

- B^p (free nucleons) = 9.95(40), compared to previous MS estimate of 11.8(1.0)
- B^n (free nucleons) = 9.82(40), compared to previous MS estimate of 11.5(1.0)
- B^p (bound nucleons in 133-Cs) = 9.36(40)
- B^n (bound nucleons in 133-Cs) = 9.32(40)
- Q_W(Cs) with bound nucleons = -73.26(4), compared to previous MS value of -73.14(6)
- Q_W(Ra) with bound nucleons = -117.42(7), compared to previous MS value of -117.22(10)
- Pauli blocking correction factor of 0.61 for proton elastic contribution and 0.59 for neutron elastic contribution
- Fermi momenta for 133-Cs: <k_F> = 0.218 GeV for protons, 0.236 GeV for neutrons

## Context

This work extends the dispersive formulation of gamma-Z box corrections developed by Blunden et al. (2011) for parity-violating electron scattering to atomic parity violation measurements in heavy nuclei. It improves upon the pioneering estimates of Marciano and Sirlin (1983) by using a systematic dispersion relation approach rather than a quark model description, and introduces for the first time a Pauli blocking correction for nucleons bound in a heavy nucleus. The results enable more precise tests of the Standard Model through comparison of the Weinberg angle measured at the Z pole with values extracted from atomic parity violation in 133-Cs and 213-Ra.
