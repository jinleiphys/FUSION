# Calculating few-body resonances using an oscillator trap

> We investigate the possibility of calculating the parameters of few-body resonances using the oscillator trap boundary conditions. We place the few-body system in an oscillator trap and calculate the energy spectrum and the strength function of a suitably chosen transition. Broader resonances are identified as Lorentzian peaks in the strength function. Narrower resonances are identified through the pattern of avoided crossings in the spectrum of the system as function of the trap size. As an example we calculate 0+ resonances in 12C within the 3-alpha model.

## Key claim

The authors propose that few-body resonance parameters can be extracted by placing the system in an artificial oscillator trap and analyzing either the strength function (for broader resonances) or avoided crossings in the energy spectrum as a function of trap size (for narrower resonances), but the method's applicability depends on the resonance width relative to the trap energy scale: broader resonances with width Gamma greater than or approximately equal to hbar^2/(2mb^2) are identified via Lorentzian peaks, while narrower resonances require the avoided-crossing analysis.

## Method

The three-alpha system with J^pi = 0^+ is placed in an oscillator trap V_trap = (hbar^2/(2m)) * (rho^2/b^4) with trap length b varied around 30-40 fm, and the three-body problem is solved using the adiabatic hyperspherical method. For broader resonances, the strength function S(E) of a transition induced by operator F = rho^2 from an initial state with asymptotic form rho^{-5/2} exp(-rho/b_3) (b_3 = 4 fm) is computed and fitted to a Lorentzian. For narrower resonances, the spectrum of the system in the trap is computed as a function of b, and the resonance energy and width are extracted by fitting the avoided-crossing pattern to an arctangent function after subtracting the oscillator contribution proportional to b^{-4}.

## Key numbers

* Broader resonance in 12C (0+): resonance energy E_r = 4.3 MeV, width Gamma = 0.9 MeV, extracted from Lorentzian fit to strength function.
* Narrower resonance in 12C (0+): resonance energy E_r = 0.38435 MeV, extracted from fit E(b) = E_r + K b^{-4} with K = 7.471 MeV fm^4.
* Narrower resonance width: Gamma = 77 eV, extracted from arctangent fit to avoided-crossing data.
* Trap length b varied around 30-40 fm; the threshold separating broad from narrow resonances is Gamma = hbar^2/(2mb^2).
* Three-body force strength: V_3(rho) = -76 MeV exp(-rho^2/(4 fm)^2).

## Context

This work extends the box method (also called the stabilization method) of Maier, Cederbaum, and Domcke (1980) and Zhang et al. (2008) by replacing the hard-wall box boundary condition with an oscillator trap, which has a simpler boundary condition and can be used in stochastic variational calculations with correlated Gaussians as in Thogersen, Fedorov, and Jensen (2008). The results for the 0+ resonances in 12C are consistent with earlier complex scaling calculations by Fedorov, Garrido, and Jensen (2003) and with the three-alpha model of Fedorov and Jensen (1996), while the width of 77 eV for the narrow resonance is consistent with the 10-30 eV range reported by Fedotov et al. (2004) given the slightly different three-body potential used here. The approach enables calculation of few-body resonance parameters using only real energies and real wavefunctions, avoiding complex arithmetic.
