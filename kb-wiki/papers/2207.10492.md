# First row CKM unitarity

> In this talk I briefly review the precision test of the first row CKM unitarity, focusing mainly on $V_{ud}$ and $V_{us}$ extracted from pion, kaon, neutron and superallowed nuclear beta decays. I will discuss the current status of several important Standard Model theory inputs to these processes, and the need for future improvements.

## Key claim

The combination of $|V_{ud}|$ from superallowed nuclear beta decays and $|V_{us}|$ from semileptonic kaon decays yields a deviation from first-row CKM unitarity of $-0.0021(7)$, at a significance level of 3.2$\sigma$, which is not yet sufficient to claim a discovery due to five major sources of uncertainty, primarily from theory.

## Method

The author reviews the extraction of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed nuclear beta decays (0⁺ nuclei), free neutron decay, and pion semileptonic decay, and $V_{us}$ from semileptonic kaon decays ($K_{\ell 3}$) and leptonic kaon/pion decays ($K_{\mu 2}$, $\pi_{\mu 2}$). For each channel, the Standard Model theory inputs are dissected: radiative corrections (including the $\gamma W$-box diagram treated via dispersion relations and lattice QCD), nuclear-structure-dependent corrections, isospin-breaking corrections, lattice QCD form factors, and electromagnetic corrections from Chiral Perturbation Theory and Sirlin's representation.

## Key numbers

- $|V_{ud}|_{0^+}^2 + |V_{us}|_{K_{\ell 3}}^2 - 1 = -0.0021(7)$, significance 3.2$\sigma$
- $|V_{ud}|$ from superallowed decays: 0.97373(31) (Hardy 2020)
- $|V_{us}|$ from $K_{\ell 3}$: 0.22308(55)
- $|V_{us}|$ from $K_{\mu 2}$: 0.2252(5)
- $|V_{us}/V_{ud}|$ from $K_{\mu 2}/\pi_{\mu 2}$ ratio: 0.23131(41)$_\text{lat}$(24)$_\text{exp}$(19)$_\text{RC}$
- $f_{K^+}/f_{\pi^+}$ (lattice $N_f=2+1+1$): 1.1932(21)
- $f_+(0)$ (lattice $N_f=2+1+1$): 0.9698(17)
- Electromagnetic correction $\delta_\text{EM}$ for $K_{\mu 2}/\pi_{\mu 2}$: $-0.0069(17)$
- $\pi_{e3}$ branching ratio: $1.038(6) \times 10^{-8}$ (PIBETA 2004)
- $|V_{us}f_+(0)|$ combined average: 0.21634(38)$_K$(3)$_\text{HO}$

## Context

This talk reviews the status of first-row CKM unitarity tests following the 2018 dispersion-relation treatment of the $\gamma W$-box diagram (Seng 2018), which shifted the central value of $|V_{ud}|$ and unveiled a tension. It extends the earlier work of Marciano and Sirlin (2005) and Hardy and Towner (2014, 2020) on radiative and nuclear-structure corrections. The anomalies motivate improved lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon $\gamma W$-box diagram (Feng 2020 for the pion case), ab-initio nuclear calculations, and next-generation experiments such as PIONEER to improve the $\pi_{e3}$ branching ratio precision by a factor of 3 or more.
