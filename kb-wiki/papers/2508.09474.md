# Spinless glueballs in generalized linear sigma model

> Within the framework of the generalized linear sigma model, a comprehensive analysis of scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs and their mixing with mesons is presented. The Lagrangian of the model contains two chiral nonets, a quark-antiquark type and a two-quark two-antiquark type. The pseudoscalar and scalar glueballs are introduced through their connections with the axial and the trace anomalies of QCD, respectively. It is found that in order to satisfy the axial anomaly and at the same time accurately generate all seven eta masses, it is necessary to include at least two pseudoscalar glueballs, a physical one and an unphysical one that gets integrated out and yields an effective instanton-type term which is needed in generation of the eta masses. At the leading order, which corresponds to keeping effective terms in the Lagrangian that contain no more than eight underlying quarks and antiquarks, the mass spectrum of the model is worked out and shown to be in complete agreement with experiment. The quark and glue contents of the isosinglet scalars below 2 GeV, and of the isosinglet pseudoscalars up to around 2.2 GeV, are analyzed in detail and their correlations with the scalar glueball condensate are examined. Decay widths of isosinglet scalars as well as different self consistencies within this framework are used to probe the glueball condensate and thereby estimate the quark and glue contents of these states. In the pseudoscalar sector, the state that is dominantly made of glue is clearly a state with mass above 2 GeV. In the scalar sector, the identification of glue contents is less certain and in principle the three isosinglets in the 1.5-2.0 GeV can contain substantial glue. These glue contents are determined as functions of the scalar glueball condensate which is the key quantity in this analysis.

## Key claim
The paper claims that to simultaneously satisfy the axial anomaly of QCD and accurately generate all seven eta meson masses within the generalized linear sigma model, it is necessary to include at least two pseudoscalar glueballs: one physical and one unphysical that is integrated out to produce an effective instanton-type term. The analysis is performed at leading order (terms with no more than eight underlying quarks and antiquarks) and the mass spectrum is shown to be in complete agreement with experiment, though the identification of glue contents in the scalar sector is less certain than in the pseudoscalar sector.

## Method
The author extends the generalized linear sigma model, which contains a quark-antiquark chiral nonet (M) and a two-quark two-antiquark chiral nonet (M'), by introducing scalar and pseudoscalar glueball fields (h and g) coupled through terms that model the axial and trace anomalies of QCD. The axial anomaly is implemented using two pseudoscalar glueball fields: a physical one (g) and an unphysical one (g') that is integrated out to generate an instanton-type term; the trace anomaly is modeled with a function f_S involving the scalar glueball condensate h^4 and determinants of the meson fields. The model parameters (approximately 20 free parameters) are determined by fitting to experimental data on meson masses and decay widths, with the analysis performed in the isospin limit and with the simplification that the trace anomaly is saturated by gluons only (lambda_2 = lambda_3 = 0, gamma_m = 2).

## Key numbers
- The scalar isosinglet states f_0(1370), f_0(1500), and f_0(1710) in the 1.5-2.0 GeV range can contain substantial glue, with their glue contents determined as functions of the scalar glueball condensate.
- In the pseudoscalar sector, the state that is dominantly made of glue has a mass above 2 GeV.
- For the broad state f_0(500), a mass of 477 ± 8 MeV and a decay width of 398 ± 107 MeV were found in agreement with PDG (from prior work cited in the paper).
- For K_0*(700), a mass of 670-770 MeV and a decay width of 640-750 MeV were found in agreement with PDG (from prior work cited in the paper).
- For a_0(980), a mass of 984 ± 6 MeV and a decay width of 108 ± 30 MeV were found in close agreement with PDG (from prior work cited in the paper).

## Context
This work extends the generalized linear sigma model framework previously developed by Fariborz and collaborators (2005, 2009) without glueballs, which successfully described isotriplet and isodoublet mesons, to include scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs and their mixing with mesons. It builds on earlier studies of glueballs in the SU(3) flavor limit by Fariborz (2019, 2021) and generalizes the treatment of the axial anomaly beyond the formulations of Rosenzweig, Schechter, and collaborators (1980, 1981) by incorporating both a physical pseudoscalar glueball and an instanton term. The analysis enables a detailed determination of the quark and glue contents of isosinglet scalar states below 2 GeV and pseudoscalar states up to around 2.2 GeV, and provides a framework for probing the scalar glueball condensate through decay widths and self-consistency constraints.
