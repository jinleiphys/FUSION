# Real-time observables from Euclidean thermal correlation functions

> In this work we apply a local quantum field theory approach in order to analyse the connection between real-time observables and Euclidean thermal correlation functions. In particular, using data generated from the functional renormalisation group in the quark-meson model, we demonstrate that in-medium effects can be directly extracted from the spatial momentum dependence of the Euclidean propagators, in contrast to conventional approaches, which rely on the reconstruction from different Matsubara frequencies. As an application, we determine the analytic features that arise from the discrete spectral contribution to the pion correlation function, and calculate the non-perturbative shear viscosity arising from these states.

# Real-time observables from Euclidean thermal correlation functions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that in-medium effects (damping factors) for thermal particle states can be directly extracted from the spatial momentum dependence of Euclidean propagators at zero Matsubara frequency, rather than requiring reconstruction from multiple Matsubara frequencies as in conventional approaches. This extraction is shown to be feasible using non-perturbative functional renormalisation group data in the quark-meson model, provided the gap between the particle mass and the continuum onset is sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors generate Euclidean pion propagator data at zero Matsubara frequency and non-vanishing spatial momenta using a functional renormalisation group (FRG) framework in the quark-meson model, for temperatures in the range 1 to 150 MeV. They compute the N=0 Fourier coefficient w_0(x) from the momentum-space propagator via a Fourier transform, then perform linear fits to ln(|x| w_0(x)) to extract the damping factor parameters alpha and gamma. The shear viscosity from asymptotic pion states is then calculated analytically from the extracted damping factor using a general formula derived in prior work.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature pion mass: m_pi = 105.769 MeV, estimated from the zero-momentum intercept of the pion propagator data at T = 1 MeV
- Temperature range of FRG data: T in [1, 150] MeV
- Ultraviolet cutoff scale of FRG data: Lambda = 1 GeV
- Fits to ln(|x| w_0(x)) yield chi^2/d.o.f. less than or approximately 1 for T in [1, 125] MeV, with slightly larger values for T > 125 MeV
- The pion damping factor has the functional form D_{pi,beta}(x) = alpha * exp(-|x|*gamma), where gamma = B - m_pi and alpha = 4*pi*exp(A) from the fit parameters
- The shear viscosity eta_0^pi is computed as a function of T, with significant uncertainty in the range T in [1, 65] MeV due to the 1/gamma component dominating at low temperatures

## Context

This work extends the formal results of Lowdon (2022), which established that dissipative thermal properties can be extracted from Euclidean two-point functions, by applying them to non-perturbative FRG data in the quark-meson model. The approach contrasts with conventional methods that rely on reconstructing real-time information from multiple Matsubara frequencies, which suffer from an ill-posed inverse problem. The resulting pion shear viscosity shows similar characteristics to calculations from chiral perturbation theory (Chen 2006, Fernandez-Fraile 2006, Lang 2012) and kinetic theory (Davesne 1995, Dobado 2003), including comparable magnitude, a peak-like behaviour at low temperature, and vanishing in the T to 0 limit. This work enables the direct calculation of real-time observables from Euclidean lattice or FRG data without solving the inverse problem, and could ultimately provide new insights into QCD.
