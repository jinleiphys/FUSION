# Radial Oscillations of Neutron Stars with Antikaon Condensate

> In this article, we study the neutron stars with antikaon condensates and their radial oscillations. We incorporate the antikaons $(K^-,\bar{K}^0)$ within a relativistic mean field theory with density dependent couplings. A transition to the kaon condensed phase is treated as a second order phase transition for different possible antikaon optical potentials. Then, we solve the structure equations of stars and calculate the fundamental and higher order frequencies of their radial pulsations. We study the eigenfunctions of different radial modes for a $2M_\odot$ star with equations of state having different potential depths of antikaons. We also study the large frequency separations for the $2M_\odot$ star. We find distinct features of the appearances of antikaons on the eigenfunctions. The shape of the eigenfunctions corresponding to the radial perturbations is affected due to the appearance of the antikaons, but for the pressure perturbation, the shape remains unchanged.

# Radial Oscillations of Neutron Stars with Antikaon Condensate

## Key claim

The appearance of antikaon condensates via a second-order phase transition in neutron star matter produces distinct features in the radial oscillation eigenfunctions and frequencies, with the fundamental frequency decreasing rapidly upon antikaon onset; however, the authors note an ambiguity regarding whether the antikaon condensate appears as a second-order transition, and they caution that off-shell corrections for the antikaon-nucleon interaction (which would make the interaction repulsive at the threshold for antikaon appearance) were not included.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for neutron star matter using a relativistic mean field model with density-dependent couplings (DD2 parameter set) for nucleons, incorporating antikaons (K- and anti-K0) via minimal coupling with constant meson-antikaon couplings. They treated the transition to the kaon condensed phase as a second-order phase transition for various antikaon optical potentials ranging from -100 to -180 MeV, solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure, and then solved the Gondek et al. formulation of the Chandrasekhar radial oscillation equations to obtain eigenfrequencies and eigenfunctions for the fundamental and higher-order radial modes.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for nucleon-only matter: 2.417 solar masses, radius 11.87 km
- Maximum mass for antikaon optical potential -100 MeV: 2.298 solar masses, radius 12.15 km
- Maximum mass for antikaon optical potential -180 MeV: 1.900 solar masses, radius 11.46 km
- Antikaon optical potential deeper than -170 MeV is excluded by the 2 solar mass observational constraint
- For optical potential -160 MeV, antikaons appear at approximately 2.52 times nuclear saturation density
- Fundamental mode frequency for a 2 solar mass star with nucleon-only matter: 2.57528 kHz
- Fundamental mode frequency for a 2 solar mass star with antikaon optical potential -160 MeV: 1.55987 kHz
- Symmetry energy for DD2 model: 31.67 MeV; slope parameter: 55.04 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star radial oscillations (Chandrasekhar 1964; Vaeth and Chanmugam 1992; Gondek 1997) to include antikaon condensates, building on the density-dependent relativistic mean field treatment of antikaon-condensed matter by Char (2014). The study is motivated by observations of 2 solar mass pulsars (Antoniadis 2013; Arzoumanian 2017; Fonseca 2021), the GW170817 neutron star merger event, and NICER mass-radius measurements (Riley 2019; Miller 2019). The results enable future work on proto-neutron star oscillations and the combined effects of antikaons and hyperons, and suggest that radial oscillation measurements could distinguish between different compositions of neutron star interiors.
