# Once more about gauge invariance in ϕ\toγ\pi0\pi0

> It is argued that the realization of gauge invariance condition as a consequent of cancellation between the ϕ\toγf0\toγ\pi0\pi0 resonance contribution and a ϕ\toγ\pi0\pi0 background one, suggested in Ref. [1], is misleading.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the gauge invariance condition for the process φ → γπ⁰π⁰ cannot be realized as a cancellation between the φ → γf₀ → γπ⁰π⁰ resonance contribution and a background term, as suggested in Anisovich (2006), because that model only satisfies the low-energy theorem at the φ meson mass shell (E = m_φ) rather than at every total energy E as gauge invariance requires.

## Method

The author examines the gauge invariance condition for the φ → γπ⁰π⁰ amplitude, which requires the amplitude to vanish at least as O(k) when the photon momentum k → 0 (the low-energy theorem). The analysis shows that the model of Anisovich (2006) imposes the cancellation condition only at M_{ππ} = m_φ, which ensures the ω³ law for the soft photon spectrum only when the total center-of-mass energy E equals m_φ. The author then demonstrates that extending the cancellation condition to arbitrary E would require introducing a new vector resonance with mass near 980 MeV.

## Key numbers

- g_π² = 0.12 GeV², g_K² = 0.27 GeV², M₀ = 0.975 GeV (parameters of the f₀ resonance from Anisovich (2006))
- 1/a = -0.2 GeV², μ = 0.388 GeV (parameters of the background term from Anisovich (2006))
- A new vector resonance with mass E = m_V ≈ 980 MeV and visible width Γ_visible ≈ 70 MeV would be required if the cancellation condition were imposed at every energy E

## Context

This work critiques the gauge invariance implementation in Anisovich (2006), which proposed a cancellation mechanism between the φ → γf₀ resonance and a background to satisfy the low-energy theorem for φ → γπ⁰π⁰. The author argues that proper gauge invariance requires the φ → γf₀ amplitude itself to satisfy the low-energy theorem, as shown in Achasov (prior work), and that the proposed model only works at the φ mass shell. The analysis enables a correct understanding of gauge invariance in radiative decays of vector mesons and references earlier work by Low and Creutz on gauge invariance in particle physics.
