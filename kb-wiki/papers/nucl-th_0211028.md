# Pion-nucleus optical potential valid up to the DELTA-resonance region

> We present in this article an optical potential for the $π$-nucleus interaction that can be used in various studies involving $π$-nucleus channels. Based on earlier treatments of the low energy $π$-nucleus optical potential, we have derived a potential expression applicable from threshold up to the $Δ$-resonance region. We extracted the impulse approximation form for this potential from the $π-N$ scattering amplitude and then added to it kinematical and physical corrections. The kinematic corrections arise from transforming the impulse approximation expression from the $π-N$ center of mass frame to the $π$-nucleus center of mass frame, while the physical corrections arise mostly from the many-body nature of the $π$-nucleus interaction. By taking advantage of the experimental progress in our knowledge of the $π-N$ process, we have updated earlier treatments with parameters calculated from state-of-the-art experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents a pion-nucleus optical potential derived from first principles that is applicable from threshold up to the Delta-resonance region, but the author acknowledges that the potential systematically underestimates low-energy s-wave attraction for heavy nuclei (such as 208Pb), indicating a missing many-body effect not captured by the formalism.

## Method

The author starts from the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in the impulse approximation, then applies kinematic corrections to transform from the pion-nucleon center-of-mass frame to the pion-nucleus center-of-mass frame, and adds physical corrections including multiple scattering (with the Ericson-Ericson Lorentz-Lorenz effect summed to all orders for p-waves), two-nucleon pion absorption, Pauli blocking, and Coulomb corrections. The s- and p-wave parameters are extracted from the state-of-the-art phase-shift analysis of Arndt et al. (VPI/SAID) and kept fixed without refitting to any pion-nucleus data, unlike the earlier work of Stricker, McManus, and Carr (SMC) which had modified parameters to fit data.

## Key numbers

- The optical potential is tested for π+ elastic scattering from 208Pb at pion laboratory kinetic energy of 30.7 MeV, where the unmodified potential underestimates the data (missing s-wave attraction).
- For π+ elastic scattering from 12C at 30.3 MeV, the potential reproduces experimental data well.
- For π+ elastic scattering from 16O at 114.4 MeV, the potential reproduces experimental data.
- For π- elastic scattering from 40Ca at 180 MeV (close to the Delta-resonance peak), the potential reproduces experimental data.
- For π+ elastic scattering from 16O at 240.2 MeV (towards the end of the Delta-resonance region), the potential reproduces experimental data.
- The Ericson-Ericson parameter λ is taken in the range 1.2-1.6, following estimates by Oset and Weise and by Baym and Brown.
- The Coulomb energy correction parameter ECoul is shown as a function of proton number Z, shifting the kinetic energy at which the potential is evaluated.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy pion-nucleus optical potential of Stricker, McManus, and Carr (SMC) (1979-1984) to higher energies covering the Delta-resonance region, by removing low-energy approximations, using fully covariant kinematics including nuclear recoil, and updating parameters from the modern phase-shift analysis of Arndt et al. (VPI/SAID) rather than the older Rowe, Salomon, and Landau (RSL) analysis. The potential is intended for use in analyzing pion photo- and electro-production experiments at Jefferson Lab and Mainz, particularly for studies of coherent pion photoproduction from nuclei in the Delta-resonance region.
