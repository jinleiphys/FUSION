# Partonic Picture of Nuclear Shadowing at Small x

> We find that coherent multiple scatterings of the partonic fluctuations of the virtual photon with the nucleons inside the nucleus result in the large nuclear shadowing effect for parton distributions in nuclei. We predict the gluon shadowing effect at small $x$ and for $Q^2\geq 3 GeV^2$.

# Partonic Picture of Nuclear Shadowing at Small x

## Key claim

Nuclear shadowing for parton distributions arises from coherent multiple scatterings of partonic fluctuations of the virtual photon with nucleons inside the nucleus, and the gluon shadowing effect can be unambiguously predicted by perturbative QCD for Q² ≥ 3 GeV², provided the initial shadowing ratio at Q₀² has minimal influence on the result.

## Method

The authors apply the Glauber-Gribov multiple-scattering theory in the target rest frame, where the virtual photon interacts with nucleons via its quark-antiquark (q\bar q) color-singlet fluctuation. They compute the nuclear structure function F₂^A and the gluon distribution xg_A by integrating the photon wave function over impact parameter, longitudinal momentum fraction, and transverse separation, using the color transparency mechanism for the q\bar q N cross section. The evolution from a low Q₀² = 0.4 GeV², where initial shadowing ratios are parametrized from experimental data, is performed using perturbative QCD evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic range of experimental data used is 10⁻⁵ < x < 0.1 and 0.05 GeV² < Q² < 100 GeV², showing systematic reduction of F₂^A/A relative to F₂^N.
- The coherence length condition l_c > R_A (i.e. x < 10⁻²) is required for the q\bar q configuration to interact coherently with all nucleons.
- The effective number of scatterings for quarks is given by κ_q(x,Q²) = (2Aπ)/(3R_A² Q²) α_s(Q²) xg_N^{DLA}(x,Q²).
- The gluon effective number of scatterings is κ_g = 9κ_q/4, leading to a 12 times faster increase of gluon density with Q² compared to sea quarks at small x.
- The perturbative shadowing mechanism is predicted to dominate for Q² ≥ 3 GeV², where dependence on the initial condition R_g⁰(x) diminishes.

## Context

This work extends the vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model, which describes low-Q² DIS, into the perturbative QCD regime where partonic degrees of freedom become relevant. It builds on the Glauber-Gribov multiple-scattering theory (Glauber, 1959; Gribov, 1969) and the color transparency mechanism for point-like color-singlet configurations (Strikman, 1990). The results enable more reliable calculations of semihard processes in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at √s ≥ 200 GeV, particularly for determining initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation, and emphasize that impact parameter dependence of nuclear structure functions must be incorporated correctly for central nuclear collisions.
