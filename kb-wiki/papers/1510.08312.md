# Properties of superheavy nuclei with Z = 124

> We employ Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with NL3 parametrization to investigate the ground state properties of superheavy nucleus, Z = 124. The nuclei selected (from among complete isotopic series) for detailed investigation show that the nucleon density at the center is very low and therefore, these nuclei can be treated as semi-bubble nuclei. The considerable shell gap appears at neutron numbers N = 172, 184 and 198 showing the magicity corresponding to these numbers. The results are compared with the macro-microscopic Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) wherever possible.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with NL3 parametrization predicts that nuclei with Z = 124 exhibit shell closures at neutron numbers N = 172, 184, and 198, and that many of these isotopes have very low central nucleon density, making them "semi-bubble" nuclei; however, the magic numbers are isotope- and parameter-dependent, and the results are only compared with other theoretical models (FRDM) since no experimental data exist for this region.

## Method

The authors solved the RMF field equations for the isotopic chain of Z = 124 with neutron numbers N = 158 to 220 (mass numbers A = 282 to 384) using the NL3 parameter set, expanding Dirac spinors and boson fields in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 20 oscillator shells. Pairing correlations were included via a constant-gap BCS formalism with pairing gaps taken from the phenomenological Madland-Nix formula, and the center-of-mass correction was estimated using the harmonic oscillator formula E_c.m. = (3/4)(41A^{-1/3}) MeV. Binding energies, quadrupole deformation parameters, two-neutron separation energies, alpha-decay Q-values, and half-lives were calculated and compared with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and available experimental data for lower-Z nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for $^{334}$124: 2284.71 MeV (RMF) vs. 2286.75 MeV (FRDM), with quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ = 0.128 (RMF) vs. 0.335 (FRDM)
- Binding energy for $^{312}$124: 2166.19 MeV (RMF) vs. 2163.84 MeV (FRDM), a discrepancy of 2.35 MeV
- Maximum binding energy difference between RMF and FRDM for Z = 124 isotopes: 7.43 MeV for $^{320}$124 (about 0.3% discrepancy)
- Binding energy for $^{288}$120: 2026.51 MeV (RMF) vs. 2023.06 MeV (FRDM), with $\beta_2$ = -0.049 (RMF) vs. -0.104 (FRDM)
- Two-neutron separation energy decrease at N = 172 and 184 for Z = 124: approximately 3.5 MeV; at N = 198: approximately 2 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy decrease for Z = 120: approximately 5.0 MeV at N = 172, approximately 3.0 MeV at N = 184, and approximately 3.5 MeV at N = 198
- Alpha-decay Q-value for $^{296}$124: 14.11 MeV, with half-life log$_{10}$ T$_\alpha$ = -6.41 seconds
- Alpha-decay Q-value for $^{232}$92 (end of decay chain): 3.41 MeV (RMF) vs. 5.41 MeV (experimental), with half-life log$_{10}$ T$_\alpha$ = 25.66 seconds (RMF) vs. 9.50 seconds (experimental)

## Context

This work extends previous RMF and Skyrme Hartree-Fock studies of superheavy nuclei, particularly the authors' earlier investigation of Z = 122 isotopes (Patra 2009), and theoretical predictions of magic numbers by Rutz (1997), Gupta (1997), Patra (1999), and Cwiok (1996, 2005). The results contradict the appearance of a deformed magic number at N = 162 reported by Rutz (1997), as no sudden change in separation energy was found at that neutron number. The semi-bubble structure predicted for Z = 120 and 124 nuclei, with central density depressions extending 1-2 fm, suggests these nuclei may have fullerene-type structures and provides guidance for experimental searches for the "island of stability" in the superheavy region.
