# Searching for the baryon-to-meson transition region with the MPD at NICA

> In heavy-ion reactions, statistical models predict a rapid change in the baryon-to-meson ratio as a function of the collision energy. This change occurs when the hadronic medium transits from a baryon- to a meson-dominated gas. The transition is expected to take place at a temperature around 140 MeV and a baryon chemical potential around 420 MeV, corresponding to a center-of-mass collision energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 8.2$ GeV. The energy range of the MPD experiment will be suitable for the exploration of this transition region. We present preliminary results of feasibility studies for the measurement of the transverse momentum spectra for mesons and baryons, using Monte Carlo data samples, in order to study the crossing point between these transverse momenta, as a function of the centrality and collision energy.

## Key claim

Statistical models predict a rapid change in the baryon-to-meson ratio at a temperature around 140 MeV and baryon chemical potential around 420 MeV, corresponding to a center-of-mass collision energy of approximately 8.2 GeV; the authors present preliminary Monte Carlo feasibility studies suggesting that the MPD experiment at NICA, operating in the 4 to 11 GeV range, can explore this transition region by measuring the crossing point of meson and baryon transverse momentum spectra.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD event generator to simulate Au+Au collisions at 7.7 and 11.5 GeV and Bi+Bi collisions at 9.2 GeV, then reconstructed the events with GEANT3 (Au+Au) and GEANT4 (Bi+Bi) detector simulations of the MPD. Particle identification was performed using a Bayesian n-sigma method combining energy loss measurements from the Time Projection Chamber and time-of-flight information from the TOF detector, with tracks selected at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum from 0.2 to 2 GeV/c. The crossing point between pion and proton transverse momentum distributions was identified for each centrality class and collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The transition is expected at temperature around 140 MeV and baryon chemical potential around 420 MeV, corresponding to sqrt(s_NN) approximately 8.2 GeV
- MPD covers the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 4 to 11 GeV
- TPC tracks charged particles with transverse momentum larger than 50 MeV/c and covers pseudorapidity |eta| less than 1.2
- TOF time resolution is around 50 ps
- For 0-10% centrality Au+Au collisions, crossing points are at p_T approximately 0.65 GeV/c at 7.7 GeV and 0.85 GeV/c at 11.5 GeV
- For 70-80% centrality Au+Au collisions, crossing points are at p_T approximately 0.75 GeV/c at 7.7 GeV and 1.05 GeV/c at 11.5 GeV
- For Bi+Bi collisions at 9.2 GeV, crossing point for most central collisions is at p_T approximately 0.55 GeV/c, for most peripheral at p_T approximately 0.65 GeV/c
- K+/pi+ integrated yield ratios at Monte Carlo level are approximately 0.229 at 7.7 GeV, 0.232 at 9.2 GeV, and 0.205 at 11.5 GeV
- Reconstruction efficiency for pions is about 66% (Au+Au) and 72% (Bi+Bi); for kaons about 57% (Au+Au) and 64% (Bi+Bi)

## Context

This work extends statistical model predictions by Cleymans and collaborators regarding the baryon-to-meson transition, and complements prior MPD collaboration studies of particle spectra at different energies. The results are too preliminary for definite conclusions, but the authors expect that with MPD commissioning in early 2024, the crossing point analysis can be used to investigate radial flow and particle production mechanisms, and to extract freeze-out parameters to characterize the transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated hadronic matter.
