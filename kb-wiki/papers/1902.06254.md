# Impact of Glasma on heavy quark observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC

> In the pre-thermal equilibrium stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, a strong quasi-classical transverse gluon field emerges at about $τ_0 \simeq 0.1 \, \rm fm/c$ and evolves together with their longitudinal counterparts according to the classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations. Recently it has been shown that these fields induce a diffusion of charm quarks in momentum space resulting in a tilt of their spectrum without a significant drag. We find that in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC such a novel dynamics of charm quarks leads to an initial enhancement of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) at $p_T$ larger than 2 GeV$/c$ contrary to the standard lore. Moreover, the same dynamics leads to a larger final elliptic flow ($v_2$) inducing a relation between $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ that is quite close to the experimental measurements. Our study also shows that such an initial pre-thermal stage is unlikely to be described in terms of a standard drag and diffusion dynamics, because even if one tune such coefficients to reproduce the same $R_{AA}(p_T)$ this would imply a significantly smaller $v_2$.

## Key claim

The pre-thermal Glasma stage in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC induces a diffusion of charm quarks that initially enhances the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) at $p_T > 2$ GeV$/c$, contrary to the standard expectation of suppression; this initial enhancement leads to a larger final elliptic flow ($v_2$) that brings the $R_{AA}$-$v_2$ correlation closer to experimental measurements, but only if the Glasma lifetime is in the range $0.3-0.5$ fm$/c$.

## Method

The authors simulate charm quark dynamics in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV by first solving classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations for the Glasma fields in SU(2) gauge theory on a static 3D lattice (4 fm $\times$ 4 fm transverse plane, lattice spacing 0.04 fm), initialized via the McLerran-Venugopalan model with $g^2\mu_A = 3$ GeV. Charm quarks are evolved through the Glasma using Wong equations from formation time $\tau_{form}=0.1$ fm$/c$ until $\tau_{glasma}=0.3-0.5$ fm$/c$, then the subsequent QGP evolution is modeled via a Fokker-Planck/Langevin approach with drag and diffusion coefficients from a quasi-particle model (QPM) tuned to lattice QCD thermodynamics, with hadronization to $D$ mesons by fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark formation time: $\tau_{form} \simeq m_c^{-1} \approx 0.1$ fm$/c$
- Glasma lifetime range considered: $\tau_{glasma} = 0.3-0.5$ fm$/c$ (corresponding to Glasma duration $0.2-0.4$ fm$/c$)
- For $\tau_{glasma}=0.3$ fm$/c$, $R_{AA}$ of charm quarks increases up to 1.2 at intermediate $p_T$; for $\tau_{glasma}=0.5$ fm$/c$, $R_{AA}$ increases up to 1.5
- Diffusion coefficient parametrization for Glasma stage: $D(p)=D_0(1+cp)^2$ with $D_0=3.6$ GeV$^2$/fm and $c=0.02$ GeV$^{-1}$ for $\tau_{glasma}=0.3$ fm$/c$, $c=0.025$ GeV$^{-1}$ for $\tau_{glasma}=0.5$ fm$/c$
- Inclusion of Glasma increases $v_2$ by about $15-20\%$ at $p_T > 2$ GeV$/c$ compared to standard evolution starting at $\tau_0=0.3$ fm$/c$
- Starting hydrodynamic evolution at $\tau_0=0.1$ fm$/c$ (without Glasma) decreases $v_2$ by about $20\%$ at $p_T>2$ GeV$/c$ relative to the standard case
- Impact parameter: $b=9.25$ fm (simulating $30\%-50\%$ centrality)

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark diffusion in the Glasma from $pA$ collisions (Ruggieri 2018) to $AA$ collisions at LHC, addressing the persistent tension between simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ in heavy quark phenomenology (Cao 2016, Xu 2018, Dong 2019). Previous approaches (Das 2015, Scardina 2017) reduced this tension by including temperature-dependent diffusion and coalescence hadronization, but still struggled at LHC energies. The present work shows that the Glasma stage provides a mechanism to increase $v_2$ without sacrificing $R_{AA}$ agreement, and enables future studies of $D-\bar D$ angular correlations and directed flow $v_1$ as probes of the initial electromagnetic field (Das 2016).
