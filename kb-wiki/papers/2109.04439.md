# Driving chiral phase transition with ring diagram

> We study the dressing of four-quark interaction by the ring diagram, and its feeding back to the quark gap equation, in an effective chiral quark model. Implementing such an in-medium coupling naturally reduces the chiral transition temperature in a class of chiral models, and is capable of generating the inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperatures. We also demonstrate the important role of confining forces, via the Polyakov loop, in a positive feedback mechanism which reinforces the inverse magnetic catalysis.

## Key claim

The in-medium dressing of the four-quark interaction via the ring diagram, when fed back into the quark gap equation, naturally reduces the chiral transition temperature and generates inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperatures, without requiring artificial tuning of the deconfinement temperature parameter or an explicit magnetic-field-dependent coupling, provided the confining force (Polyakov loop) suppresses free quarks in the low-temperature phase.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral quark model with an instantaneous, color-diagonal four-quark interaction derived from Coulomb-gauge QCD. They dress the static four-quark coupling by resumming the ring diagram, i.e., inserting the finite-temperature polarization tensor Π₀₀ in the static, zero-momentum limit into the inverse coupling, and then solve the quark gap equation self-consistently with this dressed coupling. The Polyakov loop is coupled to the quarks via a modified thermal distribution, and its expectation value is determined from a separate gap equation involving a pure-glue potential. Calculations are performed at finite temperature and finite magnetic field (via Landau-level summation) using the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The chiral transition temperature is reduced from >200 MeV (without the ring) to approximately 160 MeV at zero magnetic field when the ring diagram is included with the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme.
* The vacuum parameters used are: Λ = 1.101 GeV, G_NJL Λ² = 3.668, and current quark mass m = 5 MeV, yielding f_π = 92.9 MeV, m_π = 137.8 MeV, and ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ = −(250 MeV)³ per flavor.
* The finite-temperature polarization tensor Π₀₀^T in the static limit approaches −T²/3 in the massless (high-temperature) limit, and the scalar polarization Π_S^T approaches −(1/6) T² in the same limit.
* The constituent quark mass in the Schwinger scheme is approximately 200 MeV, compared to approximately 300 MeV in the previous 4D cutoff scheme for the same chiral condensate value.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lo et al., 2020) which demonstrated that polarization provides a natural mechanism connecting the pure-gauge deconfinement scale (T_d ≈ 270 MeV) to the chiral crossover temperature (T_pc ≈ 156.5 MeV) and drives inverse magnetic catalysis. It contrasts with approaches that introduce an explicit B-dependent coupling (mediumG1, mediumG2) or artificial tuning of the T_d parameter in the gluon sector. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density, including the critical end point, and motivate extending the model to include non-local interactions and dynamical gluons.
