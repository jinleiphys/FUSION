# Neutrino-nucleus interactions in the T2K experiment

> We present a study of neutrino-nucleus interactions at the T2K experiment based on the GiBUU transport model. The aim of T2K is to measure $ν_e$ appearance and $θ_{13}$, but it will also be able to do a precise measurement of $ν_μ$ disappearance. The former requires a good understanding of $π^0$ production while the latter is closely connected with a good understanding of quasielastic scattering. For both processes we investigate the influence of nuclear effects and particular final-state interactions on the expected event rates taking into account the T2K detector setup.

## Key claim

The T2K experiment's ability to measure neutrino oscillation parameters depends critically on understanding nuclear effects and final-state interactions in neutrino-nucleus interactions, with the NCπ⁰ cross section needing to be known to 10% accuracy for the resulting error on oscillation parameters to be comparable to statistical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors applied the GiBUU (Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model, which treats neutrino-nucleus scattering as a two-step process: initial-state interactions of neutrinos with bound nucleons (using a local Thomas-Fermi approximation with mean-field potentials and Pauli blocking), followed by propagation of produced particles through the nucleus with complex final-state interactions including elastic and inelastic collisions and particle decays. The model was used to simulate NCπ⁰ production and CCQE scattering on oxygen targets, averaged over the T2K neutrino flux distribution.

## Key numbers

- Total flux-averaged NC1π⁰ cross section on oxygen: (0.34 – 0.48) × 10⁻³⁸ cm², where the range reflects uncertainty from the Δ axial form factor (ANL vs. BNL data fits)
- Coherent pion production contribution to NCπ⁰: (0.02 – 0.04) × 10⁻³⁸ cm²
- Misidentified π⁰ events contributing to νₑ appearance background: 0.09 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², which is 26% of true pion events
- At a realistic proton kinetic energy detection threshold of 0.2 GeV in the near detector, only about 30% of the total QE cross section is measured directly
- The far detector sees roughly 15% more CCQE-like events than true CCQE events due to fake contributions from Δ excitation
- Standard deviation for reconstructed neutrino energy: 18% for the tracking (near) detector and 23% for the Cherenkov (far) detector at 0.7 GeV neutrino energy, giving a combined error of 29% for oscillation parameter extraction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GiBUU model studies of neutrino-nucleus interactions (Leitner 2006, Leitner 2008) and their applications to MiniBooNE and K2K experiments (Leitner 2008, Leitner 2010). The study addresses the specific needs of the T2K experiment, which has just started operation, by investigating how the conceptually different near detector (tracking) and far detector (Super-Kamiokande water Cherenkov) affect event selection and energy reconstruction. The results enable improved systematic uncertainty estimates for T2K's oscillation parameter extraction and highlight the need for realistic event generators that properly handle in-medium π-N-Δ dynamics.
