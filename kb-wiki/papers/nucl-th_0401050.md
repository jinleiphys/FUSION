# The stability of the relativistic three-body system and in-medium equations

> We present a relativistic three-body equation to study the stability of the isolated three-body system and the correlations in a medium of finite temperatures and densities. Relativity is implemented utilizing the light front form. Using a zero-range force we find the relativistic analog of the Thomas collapse and investigate the possibility that the nucleon exists as a Borromean system. Within a systematic Dyson equation approach we calculate the three-body Mott transition and the critical temperature of the color-superconducting phase.

## Key claim

Using a zero-range force in a light-front relativistic three-body equation with an invariant cut-off, the author finds a relativistic analog of the Thomas collapse (where the three-body mass goes to zero for a finite two-body mass), but also identifies a parameter region where the proton can exist as a Borromean system (bound with no bound two-body subsystems), contingent on the choice of quark mass and cut-off.

## Method

The author formulates a relativistic three-body equation using light-front dynamics with a zero-range (contact) interaction, regularized by an invariant cut-off \(\Lambda\) on the virtual two-body mass. The two-body propagator is solved first, then used as input in a three-body Faddeev-type integral equation. For the in-medium case, the Dyson equation approach is extended to include self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking factors via Fermi distribution functions, and the three-body equation is solved to find the Mott dissociation lines and the critical temperature for color superconductivity via the Thouless criterion.

## Key numbers

- For cut-off parameters \(\Lambda = 4m, 6m, 8m\) and \(\Lambda/m = 10^{15} \rightarrow \infty\), the three-body bound state collapses (\(M_{3B} \rightarrow 0\)) for a finite two-body mass, showing the relativistic Thomas collapse.
- For a quark mass \(m = 315\) MeV, the proton (mass \(m_p = 938\) MeV) is described as a Borromean system for all values of \(\Lambda\).
- For a quark mass \(m = 400\) MeV, the proton is Borromean for cut-off values \(\Lambda\) above approximately \(9.6m\).
- The Mott lines (temperature and chemical potential for three-quark bound state dissociation) are calculated for \(\Lambda = 4m, 6m, 8m\); at zero density the critical temperature depends on the cut-off.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic three-body problem with contact forces (Thomas collapse, Thomas 1935) into the relativistic domain using light-front equations, building on prior work by Frederico (1992) and Carbonell and Karmanov (2002). It also connects to lattice QCD and model simulations of the QCD phase diagram (Karsch 2000, Alford 2001) by providing a framework to study three-quark correlations, the Mott transition (dissociation of bound states), and color superconductivity in medium. The results enable future studies that include spin and chiral restoration to refine the description of baryons in hot, dense matter.
