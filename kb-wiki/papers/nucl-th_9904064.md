# Neutron rich nuclei in density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory with isovector mesons

> Density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory has been extended to describe properties of exotic nuclei. The effects of Fock exchange terms and of pi - and rho - meson contributions are discussed. These effects are found to be more important for neutron rich nuclei than for nuclei near the valley of stability.

## Key claim
The inclusion of Fock exchange terms and isovector (pi and rho) meson contributions produces larger effects in neutron-rich nuclei than in nuclei near the valley of stability, but the authors note that rearrangement terms have been omitted for simplicity and the results are presented as a qualitative demonstration of these effects.

## Method
The authors extended density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (RDHF) theory to include pi and rho mesons, using coupling constants derived from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the Bonn A potential. They applied three approaches, RDH with sigma, omega, and rho mesons; RDHF with sigma and omega only; and RDHF with sigma, omega, pi, and rho mesons, to calculate binding energies, proton and neutron radii, and density distributions for Ca isotopes from A=40 to A=70. Short-range correlations for pi and rho exchanges were handled by removing contact interactions.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per particle for 40Ca: RDH gives -8.21 MeV, RDHF gives -7.76 MeV, experimental value is -8.50 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splitting of d5/2 and d3/2 levels in 48Ca: relativistic mean field theory gives 9.32 MeV, RDHF gives 3.19 MeV, experimental value is 4.3 MeV.
- Meson masses used: m_sigma = 550 MeV (RDHF without isovector mesons) or 450 MeV (RDHF with isovector mesons), m_omega = 782.6 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV.
- Coupling constants for isovector mesons: f_pi^2/4pi = 0.08, g_rho^2/4pi = 0.55, f_rho/g_rho = 3.7.

## Context
This work extends the density dependent relativistic Hartree (RDH) approach of Brockmann and Toki (1992) and the RDHF work of Fritz, Muther, and Machleidt (1993) to include pi and rho mesons for neutron-rich nuclei. It contrasts with relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations by Sharma et al. (1993), Lalazissis et al. (1994), and others, which achieve high precision but neglect Fock exchange terms. The results enable future studies of neutron halos and other exotic nuclear properties, particularly for nuclei far from stability where Fock and isovector contributions become significant.
