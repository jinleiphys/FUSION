# Strangeness in Lattice QCD

> The status of lattice calculations in the light hadron sector is reviewed. Special emphasis is given to recent lattice determinations of the mass of the strange quark. The impact of non-perturbative renormalization and control over lattice artefacts on the attainable precision is discussed in detail. Furthermore the influence of dynamical quark effects is assessed.

# Strangeness in Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The quenched approximation describes the light hadron spectrum at the level of approximately 10%, but for the first time a significant deviation from experiment is detected, with the nucleon-to-rho mass ratio falling 6.7% (2.5σ) below the experimental value. The strange quark mass in the quenched approximation is determined with high precision (97 ± 4 MeV in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV), but the conversion into physical units carries an additional systematic uncertainty of approximately 10% due to the scale ambiguity inherent in the quenched approximation.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of the light hadron spectrum and the strange quark mass, focusing on simulations using the quenched approximation (where the fermionic determinant is set to 1) and, where available, simulations with Nf=2 dynamical quark flavors. The strange quark mass is extracted from the PCAC relation for charged kaons, using non-perturbative renormalization via an intermediate scheme (the Schrodinger Functional or the regularization-independent scheme) to avoid the slow convergence of lattice perturbation theory, followed by continuum extrapolations to remove lattice artefacts of O(a^2).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-to-rho mass ratio in quenched QCD from CP-PACS: m_N/m_ρ = 1.143 ± 0.033, which is 6.7% (2.5σ) below the experimental value of 1.218
- Nucleon-to-rho mass ratio from MILC Collaboration (staggered fermions, continuum limit): m_N/m_ρ = 1.254 ± 0.018 (stat) ± 0.028 (syst)
- Sum of RGI quark masses from ALPHA/UKQCD: M_s + M_hat = 140 ± 5 MeV, using f_K = 160 ± 2 MeV
- Strange quark mass in MSbar scheme at 2 GeV, quenched approximation (ALPHA/UKQCD): m_s^MSbar(2 GeV) = 97 ± 4 MeV
- Strange quark mass in MSbar scheme at 2 GeV from CP-PACS: 88^{+4}_{-6} MeV for Nf=2, and 110^{+3}_{-4} MeV for quenched (Nf=0)
- Light quark mass ratio from Chiral Perturbation Theory: M_s/M_hat = 24.4 ± 1.4
- Non-perturbative renormalization factor mbar_SF/M = 0.864 ± 0.011 at scale μ_0 = 275 MeV, known with 1.5% precision
- Inclusion of dynamical quarks decreases the strange quark mass estimate by roughly 20% compared to the quenched approximation
- Discrepancy with experiment for m_φ reduces from 6.5% (quenched) to 1.4% (Nf=2); for m_K* from 4.4% to 1.0%

## Context

This review extends the benchmark quenched hadron spectrum calculations of CP-PACS (1999), which superseded earlier work by GF11, and compares them with results from MILC (staggered fermions) and UKQCD (O(a) improved Wilson fermions). The non-perturbative renormalization technique using the Schrodinger Functional was developed by the ALPHA Collaboration and applied to quark mass determination by ALPHA/UKQCD. The results enable future work combining lattice QCD with Chiral Perturbation Theory to address the long-standing question of whether the up-quark is massless, as noted by Kaplan, Nelson, and Cohen (1999), with a pilot study already appearing. The review also highlights the need for larger computers and algorithmic improvements to simulate dynamical quarks with m_PS/m_V below 0.5 and odd numbers of flavors.
