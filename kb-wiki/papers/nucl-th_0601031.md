# Full $fp$-shell study of even-even $^{48-56}$Ti isotopes

> The level schemes and transition rates {\em B}({\em E}2;$\uparrow$) of eve-even $^{48-56}$Ti isotopes were studied by performing large-scale shell model calculations with FPD6 and GXPF1 effective interactions. Excellent agreement were obtained by comparing the first 2$^{+}$ level for all isotopes with the recently available experimental data, but studying the transition strengths {\em B}({\em E}2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \to2^+_1$) for all Ti isotopes using constant proton-neutron effective charges prove the limitations of the present large-scale calculations to reproduce the experiment in detail.

# Full $fp$-shell Study of Even-Even $^{48-56}$Ti Isotopes

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations using FPD6 and GXPF1 effective interactions achieve excellent agreement with experimental data for the first 2$^{+}$ level energies across all even-even $^{48-56}$Ti isotopes, but using constant proton-neutron effective charges (e$_p$=1.15$e$, e$_n$=0.8$e$) reveals the limitations of these calculations to reproduce the B(E2) transition strengths in detail, particularly the strong variation across neutron-rich isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the OXBASH code with the D3F7 model space and FPD6 Hamiltonian for $^{48}$Ti, the F7P3 model space with FPD6 for $^{50}$Ti, and the HO model space with FPD6 for $^{52,54,56}$Ti, with cores of $^{32}$S, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{48}$Ca respectively. The GXPF1 effective interaction was also used to calculate level spectra for $^{54}$Ti and $^{56}$Ti for comparison. Reduced transition probabilities B(E2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \rightarrow$2$^+_1$) were computed using constant effective charges e$_p$=1.15$e$ and e$_n$=0.8$e$ as suggested by prior work.

## Key numbers

- First 2$^{+}$ level energies for $^{48-56}$Ti: FPD6 calculations show better agreement than GXPF1 for most isotopes, except for $^{54}$Ti at N=32 where GXPF1 is more successful in reproducing the experimental E$_x$(2${^+_1}$) level.
- For $^{56}$Ti, lowering the single-particle energy of the $f_{5/2}$ orbit by 0.8 MeV improves the prediction of E$_x$(2${^+_1}$) by about 0.2 MeV, but fails completely in describing high spin states.
- B(E2; 0$^+_{g.s.} \rightarrow$2$^+_1$) calculations using FPD6 with e$_p$=1.15$e$ and e$_n$=0.8$e$ produced staggering and showed better agreement with experiment than GXPF1A for $^{52}$Ti at N=30, while KB3G calculations from Poves et al. (2005) were in better agreement for $^{48,50,54,56}$Ti.

## Context

This work extends the full $pf$-shell model studies of A=48 nuclei by Caurier and Zuker (1994) using KB1 and KB3 interactions, and the isobaric chain studies by Poves et al. (2001) using KB3 and FPD6. It directly compares with the experimental and theoretical work of Dinca et al. (2005) on $^{52,54,56}$Ti using GXPF1A, confirming the presence of a sub-shell closure at N=32 in neutron-rich Ti isotopes above $^{48}$Ca. The results enable further refinement of effective interactions and single-particle energies, particularly the $f_{5/2}$ orbit, to improve description of transition rates in semi-magic nuclei, while confirming the limitations noted by Dinca et al. (2005) and Poves et al. (2005) in reproducing B(E2) magnitudes and their variation across neutron-rich Ti isotopes.
