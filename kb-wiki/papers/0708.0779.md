# Real-time gauge theory simulations from stochastic quantization with optimized updating

> We investigate simulations for gauge theories on a Minkowskian space-time lattice. We employ stochastic quantization with optimized updating using stochastic reweighting or gauge fixing, respectively. These procedures do not affect the underlying theory but strongly improve the stability properties of the stochastic dynamics, such that simulations on larger real-time lattices can be performed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that stochastic quantization with optimized updating, specifically using stochastic reweighting or gauge fixing, can produce stable, accurate real-time lattice gauge theory simulations, but only when the reweighting parameter or gauge coupling satisfies specific inequalities (e.g., α ≳ β for the U(1) one-plaquette model) that ensure the Langevin dynamics is governed by an attractive fixed point.

## Method

The authors apply stochastic quantization to U(1) and SU(2) one-plaquette models and to SU(2) gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions on a Minkowskian space-time lattice. They introduce an optimized updating procedure by adding a linear term αφ to the action (stochastic reweighting) or by employing gauge fixing, which modifies the complex Langevin equation without changing the underlying quantum theory. The fixed point structure and convergence properties of the resulting Langevin dynamics are analyzed analytically via the Fokker-Planck equation and numerically by comparing stochastic averages to exact results from direct integration.

## Key numbers

* For the U(1) one-plaquette model at β=1 without reweighting (α=0), the stochastic average ⟨e^{iφ}⟩ gave −0.009(±0.006) − i·0.00006(±0.00007), while the exact value is i·0.575.
* For the same model with reweighting parameter α=1 at β=1, the stochastic average ⟨e^{iφ}⟩ gave −0.001(±0.003) + i·0.575(±0.0004), matching the exact result i·0.575.
* For the U(1) one-plaquette model with β=α=1, the distribution of φ is centered near the real fixed point; for β=α=10 and β=α=100, the distribution becomes increasingly peaked at the classical fixed point, with fluctuations suppressed as β increases.
* For SU(2) gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions, gauge fixing leads to stable physical solutions for not too small β ∼ 1/g², where large β corresponds to approaching the continuum limit.

## Context

This work extends the real-time stochastic quantization approach of Berges (2005, 2006) for scalar and SU(2) gauge field theories, where without optimization no stable physical solution could be observed for SU(2) even at small couplings. The optimized updating using stochastic reweighting or gauge fixing strongly improves stability, enabling simulations on larger real-time lattices than previously possible. The results enable first-principles real-time simulations of gauge theories such as QCD, which are otherwise inaccessible due to the sign problem in Minkowskian path integrals.
