# QGP Theory: Status and Perspectives

> The current status of Quark-Gluon-Plasma Theory is reviewed. Special emphasis is placed on QGP signatures, the interpretation of current data and what to expect from RHIC in the near future.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that while SPS experiments have created a new state of high energy-density matter compatible with deconfinement, no single observable can provide unambiguous proof; the concept of a QGP needs to be rethought, as searching for a fireball of quarks and gluons interacting dominantly according to leading order pQCD is unrealistic, with deconfinement likely occurring as a fast evolving phase of partonic degrees of freedom interacting in the domain of soft non-perturbative QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of the phase diagram, transport theory approaches (Classical Yang-Mills, Parton Cascade Model, Nuclear Fluid Dynamics, and hadronic transport models), and compares these to experimental data from SPS and first RHIC results. It examines multiple QGP signatures including multi-strange baryon enhancement, penetrating probes (dileptons and direct photons), chemical equilibration, hard processes and jet quenching, balance functions and fluctuations, collective flow, and two-particle interferometry.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature from quenched lattice QCD: T_C ≈ 270 MeV
- Critical temperature from simulations with dynamical quarks at μ_B=0: T_C ≈ 165 MeV
- For the most realistic case of QCD with two flavors of light quarks (masses between 5 and 10 MeV) and one flavor with mass around 200 MeV, the order of the phase transition remains unclear
- For systems of finite volume (V ≤ 125 fm^3), deconfinement cannot be complete
- Fraction of produced particles stemming from hard processes at √s=130 GeV: 37% (from eikonal analysis of PHOBOS data)
- Energy loss around 0.25 GeV/fm from newer GLV calculations provides good agreement with RHIC data
- String tension of κ=3 GeV/fm used in enhanced UrQMD calculations for central collisions
- Hadronization temperatures of 225 MeV and 165 MeV used in Bjorken thermal model for balance function calculations
- At SPS, Ω's suffer on average only one hadronic interaction, while N's and Λ's suffer approximately 5-6 collisions before freeze-out

## Context

This 2002 review summarizes the status of QGP theory following the CERN SPS heavy-ion program and the first year of RHIC operations. It extends earlier reviews of QGP signatures and builds on lattice work by Karsch (1996), Laermann (1996), and Fodor (2001), transport theory developments by the author and collaborators (Bass et al., 2000), and the Color Glass Condensate concept from McLerran and Venugopalan (1994). The paper enables future work by identifying open questions including whether hadronic multi-particle collisions can drive chemical equilibrium, whether charge fluctuation observables are masked by hadronization dynamics, and whether saturation or conventional pQCD approaches better describe RHIC initial conditions.
