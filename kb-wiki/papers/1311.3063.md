# Convergent perturbative nuclear effective field theory

> We consider the nuclear effective field theory including pions in the two-nucleon sector in the S waves up to including the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) terms according to the power counting suggested by the Wilsonian renormalization group analysis done in a previous paper. We treat only the leading contact interaction nonperturbatively, and the rest, including the long-distance part of pion exchange, are treated as perturbations. To define the long-distance part, it is important to introduce a separation scale, or a cutoff. We employ a hybrid regularization, in which the loops with only contact interactions are regularized with Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS), while the loops with (long-distance part of) pion exchange are regularized with a Gaussian damping factor (GDF), to simplify the (nonperturbative) leading-order amplitudes. The scale introduced by PDS is identified with the cutoff of GDF up to a numerical factor. We emphasize that the introduction of the GDF requires a careful definition of the coupling constant for the pion exchange. We obtain the analytic expressions for the phase shifts for the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channels. By fitting them to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis data, it is shown that the effective theory expansion with perturbative long-distance part of pion exchange is converging.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a perturbative treatment of the long-distance part of one-pion exchange, combined with a nonperturbative treatment of only the leading contact interaction, yields a convergent effective field theory expansion for nucleon-nucleon scattering in S waves up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), provided a hybrid regularization scheme with a separation scale is employed.

## Method

The authors perform NNLO calculations for the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ scattering channels using a hybrid regularization scheme: Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) regularizes loops containing only contact interactions, while a Gaussian damping factor (GDF) regularizes loops involving the long-distance part of pion exchange. The separation scale $\lambda$ introduced by the GDF is identified with the PDS scale $\mu$ up to a numerical factor ($\mu = \lambda/\sqrt{\pi}$). The calculated phase shifts are fitted to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis data to determine the low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* The physical cutoff of the effective field theory is $\Lambda_0 \approx 350$ MeV, above which the description does not hold.
* Input parameters: nucleon mass $M = 938$ MeV, pion mass $m_\pi = 138$ MeV, pion exchange coupling constant $g_A^2/2f^2 = 4.68 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-2}$.
* The separation scale is chosen as $\lambda = \sqrt{\pi}\mu = 350$ MeV.
* Fitting ranges for center-of-mass momentum: 0--30 MeV for LO, 0--70 MeV for NLO, and 0--250 MeV for NNLO for both $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ phase shifts.
* The NNLO results fit excellently up to approximately 300 MeV.
* Dimensionless coupling constants in the $^1S_0$ channel: LO $\tilde{C}_0 = -0.958$, NLO $\tilde{C}_0 = -0.964$, $\tilde{C}_2 = 0.517$, $\tilde{D}_2 = 0.479$, NNLO $\tilde{C}_0 = -0.967$, $\tilde{C}_2 = 0.205$, $\tilde{D}_2 = 0.099$, $\tilde{C}_4 = -0.055$, $\tilde{D}_4 = 1.180$, $\tilde{E}_4 = 1.203$.
* Dimensionless coupling constants in the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel: LO $\tilde{C}_0 = -1.22$, NLO $\tilde{C}_0 = -1.31$, $\tilde{C}_2 = 1.47$, $\tilde{D}_2 = 0.68$, NNLO $\tilde{C}_0 = -1.42$, $\tilde{C}_2 = 0.89$, $\tilde{D}_2 = -0.11$, $\tilde{C}_4 = -11.72$, $\tilde{D}_4 = 4.28$, $\tilde{E}_4 = 31.1$, $\tilde{C}_2^{(SD)} = -4.80$.

## Context

This work extends the KSW power counting scheme (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998; van Kolck 1998) by incorporating insights from Wilsonian renormalization group analysis (Harada et al. 2005-2010) to resolve the convergence failure at NNLO previously identified by Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (1999). The approach is similar to that of Beane, Kaplan, and Vuorinen (2008) but differs in using the same regularization for both S-wave channels, employing a Gaussian damping factor rather than a Pauli-Villars regulator, and interpreting the separation scale as a floating cutoff not exceeding 350 MeV. The results enable future application to P waves and provide a framework for systematic renormalization group analysis of the coupling constants.
