# Mesons and the Structure of Nucleons

> The role of mesons, particularly the pion, in the structure of nucleons is reviewed and investigated. Since quark-antiquark pairs are likely to ``transform" into mesons at large distances, mesons are expected to contribute to nucleon structure. Their effects on the Gottfried sum rule, on the strangeness content of the nucleon, and on the spin of the nucleon are discussed.

## Key claim

Mesons, particularly the pion and kaon clouds, contribute significantly to nucleon structure properties including the Gottfried sum rule, strangeness content, and spin, but the magnitude of these contributions depends strongly on the spatial extent of the nucleon (e.g., bag radius or oscillator length) and the momentum cutoff used in effective meson-nucleon vertices, with smaller cutoffs yielding much smaller strange quark matrix elements.

## Method

The authors review and extend calculations of meson cloud contributions to nucleon structure using several hadronic models: an SU(3) extended cloudy bag model (CBM), a chiral non-relativistic constituent quark model (CNRQM), and a covariant perturbation theory with point couplings. For the Gottfried sum rule, they compute the pion cloud contribution via convolution of the pion structure function with its momentum distribution in the infinite momentum frame, using the Sullivan process. For strangeness content, they evaluate kaon loop contributions to strange quark vector and axial vector matrix elements, including seagull gauge invariance corrections. For the spin structure, they compute the depolarizing effect of the meson cloud on the valence quark spin contribution.

## Key numbers

- Experimental Gottfried sum rule value: S_G = 0.240 ± 0.016 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 (NMC group), corresponding to Δ\bar q = 0.280 ± 0.048
- The experimental S_G value of 0.24 can be reached for a pion-nucleon form factor cutoff of approximately 1.5 GeV
- EMC measurement of the proton polarized structure function first moment: Γ_1^p = 0.126 ± 0.011 ± 0.014, compared to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule prediction of 0.175 ± 0.007 (assuming Δs = 0)
- In the MIT bag model, the valence quark spin contribution ΔΣ = 0.65 from relativistic effects alone
- For the cloudy bag model with R ≈ 1.1 fm (fitting electromagnetic observables), the meson cloud reduces ΔΣ by about 15%
- Strange magnetic moment from point coupling model: F_2^s(0) ≈ -1.0 μ_N; from cloudy bag model: F_2^s(0) = -0.09 μ_N; from monopole cutoff model (Λ ≈ 1.3 GeV): F_2^s(0) = -0.3 μ_N
- Strangeness radius from cloudy bag model: ⟨r^2⟩_s = -0.006 fm^2; from monopole cutoff model: -0.03 fm^2
- Strange axial vector form factor g_A^s from cloudy bag model: -0.004; from monopole cutoff model: -0.04; from Skyrme model: -0.10
- Experimental g_A^s from BNL: -0.15 ± 0.09; from EMC: -0.19 ± 0.06

## Context

This paper reviews and extends work on meson cloud contributions to nucleon structure, building on the Sullivan process (Sullivan 1972) for pion contributions to structure functions and the cloudy bag model (Miller 1984, Thomas 1984). It addresses the Gottfried sum rule puzzle from the NMC experiment (Amaudruz 1991), the EMC spin crisis (Ashman 1988), and the interpretation of strange quark matrix elements. The calculations extend earlier work by Koepf, Henley, and Pollock (1992) on kaon loops and by Musolf and Burkardt using monopole form factors, and they compare with Jaffe's pole analysis (Jaffe) and Skyrme model calculations. The results enable interpretation of ongoing parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates (SAMPLE) and CEBAF, as well as neutrino scattering experiments at LAMPF (LSND), designed to measure strange quark form factors.
