# $Îł^\ast Îł\to Î·, Î·^\prime$ transition form factors

> Using a continuum approach to the hadron bound-state problem, we calculate $Îł^\ast Îł\to Î·, Î·^\prime$ transition form factors on the entire domain of spacelike momenta, for comparison with existing experiments and in anticipation of new precision data from next-generation $e^+ e^-$ colliders. One novel feature is a model for the contribution to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel deriving from the non-Abelian anomaly, an element which is crucial for any computation of $Î·, Î·^\prime$ properties. The study also delivers predictions for the amplitudes that describe the light- and strange-quark distributions within the $Î·, Î·^\prime$. Our results compare favourably with available data. Important to this at large-$Q^2$ is a sound understanding of QCD evolution, which has a visible impact on the $Î·^\prime$ in particular. Our analysis also provides some insights into the properties of $Î·, Î·^\prime$ mesons and associated observable manifestations of the non-Abelian anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified continuum calculation of the $\gamma^\ast \gamma \to \eta, \eta^\prime$ transition form factors across all spacelike momenta, but the reliability of the predictions depends critically on a model for the non-Abelian anomaly contribution to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, which is introduced because standard rainbow-ladder truncation fails for these mesons and would otherwise yield ideal mixing (unphysical $\eta \sim u\bar{u} + d\bar{d}$ and $\eta^\prime \sim s\bar{s}$ states).

## Method

The authors solve the coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons using a rainbow-ladder kernel for the standard part of the interaction, supplemented by a new model for the non-Abelian anomaly contribution to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The anomaly kernel is parameterized as a momentum-dependent infrared-localized interaction with two tensor structures, controlled by parameters $\theta_\xi$ and ${\mathpzc r}_A$, and its strength is fitted to reproduce the experimental masses of the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons. The resulting Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are then used to compute the transition form factors, including the effects of QCD evolution on the distribution amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: $\hat m_l = 7$ MeV, $\hat m_s = 170$ MeV, corresponding to evolved current-quark masses $m_l^{\zeta_2} = 5.1$ MeV, $m_s^{\zeta_2} = 125$ MeV at renormalization scale $\zeta_2 = 2$ GeV.
- Computed meson masses: $m_\pi = 0.134$ GeV, $m_K = 0.496$ GeV, $m_\eta = 0.56$ GeV, $m_{\eta^\prime} = 0.96$ GeV (experimental values: $m_\eta = 0.55$ GeV, $m_{\eta^\prime} = 0.96$ GeV).
- Computed decay constants: $f_\pi = 0.093$ GeV, $f_K = 0.11$ GeV; for $\eta,\eta^\prime$: $f_\eta^l = 0.072$ GeV, $f_\eta^s = -0.092$ GeV, $f_{\eta^\prime}^l = 0.070$ GeV, $f_{\eta^\prime}^s = 0.104$ GeV.
- Mixing angle from single-angle fit: $\phi = 42.8^\circ$, with ideal decay constants $f^l = 0.101$ GeV ($1.08 f_\pi$) and $f^s = 0.138$ GeV ($1.49 f_\pi$).
- Octet-singlet basis results: $f_8 = 1.34 f_\pi$, $\theta_8 = -21^\circ$, $f_0 = 1.26 f_\pi$, $\theta_0 = -2.8^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the unified continuum description of $\gamma^\ast \gamma \to M$ transition form factors previously developed for $\pi^0$, $\eta_c$, and $\eta_b$ by Raya et al. (2015, 2016) and Chen et al. (2016), which used rainbow-ladder truncation and successfully described data for those mesons. The present paper addresses the missing case of $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons, where the non-Abelian anomaly is essential and standard truncations fail, producing ideal mixing. The results enable comparison with existing experimental data on the domain $Q^2 \in [0, 112]$ GeV$^2$ from Gronberg et al. (1997) and the BaBar collaboration (2006, 2011), and provide predictions for next-generation $e^+ e^-$ colliders. The analysis also delivers predictions for the light- and strange-quark distribution amplitudes within the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons.
