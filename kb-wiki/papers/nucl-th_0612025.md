# Helium-cluster decay widths of molecular states in beryllium and carbon isotopes

> The $α$ particle and $^6$He emissions from possible molecular states in beryllium and carbon isotopes have been studied using a mean-field-type cluster potential. Calculations can reproduce well the $α$-decay widths of excited states in $^{8}$Be, $^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne. For the nucleus $^{10}$Be, we discussed the $α$-decay widths with different shapes or decay modes, in order to understand the very different decay widths of two excited states. The widths of $^{6}$He decay from $^{12}$Be and $α$ decays from $^{13,14}$C are predicted, which could be useful for future experiments.

# Helium-cluster decay widths of molecular states in beryllium and carbon isotopes

## Key claim

The mean-field-type cluster potential, previously successful for ground-state decays in heavy nuclei, can reproduce α-decay widths of excited states in light nuclei (⁸Be, ¹²C, ²⁰Ne) within a factor of 3, but the model's success for ¹⁰Be and ¹³,¹⁴C depends critically on assumed deformations, decay energies, and spin assignments, with some calculated widths deviating from experimental total widths by orders of magnitude when the structure assignment is uncertain.

## Method

The authors construct a cluster potential by folding single-nucleon potentials from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (SLy4 force) with a folding factor λ determined by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition and the Wildermuth rule for the global quantum number G. Partial decay widths are computed via the WKB tunneling formula with a preformation factor P=1 for even-even nuclei and P=0.6 for the odd nucleus ¹³C, and for deformed cases the width is averaged over emission directions using axially deformed single-particle potentials from shape-constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock.

## Key numbers

- ⁸Be ground state (0⁺, 0.00 MeV): calculated α-decay width 7.8 eV, experimental 6.8±1.7 eV
- ⁸Be 2⁺ state (3.04 MeV): calculated 1.6×10³ keV, experimental 1.5×10³ keV
- ²⁰Ne 6⁺ state (8.78 MeV): calculated 0.17 keV, experimental 0.11±0.02 keV
- ¹⁰Be 2⁺ state (7.54 MeV): spherical calculation gives 0.51 eV, deformed calculation gives 0.85 eV, experimental 22±7 eV (Liendo 2002)
- ¹⁰Be 4⁺ state (10.15 MeV): spherical calculation gives 35 keV, deformed calculation gives 97 keV, experimental 130 keV (Freer 2006)
- ¹²Be 0⁺₃ state (10.9 MeV): calculated ⁶He-decay width 410 keV, AMD prediction 700 keV (Kanada-En'yo 2003)
- ¹²C 0⁺₂ state (7.654 MeV): calculated α-width 5.9 eV (linear structure), experimental 8.5 eV
- ¹²C 3⁻₁ state (9.641 MeV): calculated α-width 17 keV (oblate), experimental total width 34 keV
- ¹³C 5/2⁻ state (10.82 MeV): calculated α-width 0.2 eV, experimental total width 24 keV
- ¹⁴C 5⁻ state (14.87 MeV): calculated α-width 0.4 keV, experimental total width 35 keV
- ¹⁴C 3⁻ state (12.58 MeV): calculated α-width 22 eV, experimental total width 95 keV

## Context

This work extends the mean-field-type cluster potential approach previously applied to ground-state cluster radioactivities in heavy nuclei (Pei 2006) to excited molecular states in light beryllium and carbon isotopes, building on the semiclassical cluster model of Buck (1977, 1990, 1995). The calculations address the discrepant α-decay widths of the 7.54 MeV and 10.15 MeV states in ¹⁰Be, which had been interpreted with different channel radii by Liendo (2002) and Freer (2006). The predicted ⁶He-decay widths for ¹²Be and α-decay widths for ¹³,¹⁴C provide guidance for ongoing experiments (Price 2006, Soic 2004) and test the molecular cluster assignments proposed by von Oertzen (2004) and the AMD calculations of Kanada-En'yo (2003, 2006).
