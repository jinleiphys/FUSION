# Observation of strong nuclear suppression in exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction in Au$+$Au ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC

> We report a measurement of exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction in Au$+$Au ultra-peripheral collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV using the STAR detector. For the first time, i) the rapidity correlation between $J/ψ$ photoproduction and neutron emission from nuclear breakups has been experimentally measured; ii) nuclear suppression factors are measured for both the coherent and incoherent $J/ψ$ production. At photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 25.0 GeV, the coherent and incoherent $J/ψ$ cross sections of Au nuclei are found to be $71\pm10\%$ and $36\pm7\%$, respectively, of that of free protons. The stronger suppression observed in the incoherent production provides a new experimental handle to study the initial-state parton density in heavy nuclei. Data are compared with theoretical models quantitatively.

# Observation of strong nuclear suppression in exclusive J/ψ photoproduction in Au+Au ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC

## Key claim
The nuclear suppression factor for incoherent J/ψ photoproduction on Au nuclei (36±7% of the free-proton cross section) is significantly stronger than for coherent production (71±10%), providing a new experimental handle on initial-state parton density in heavy nuclei, though the data do not conclusively support sub-nucleonic parton density fluctuations in the CGC model at RHIC energies.

## Method
The STAR detector measured exclusive J/ψ and ψ(2s) photoproduction in Au+Au ultra-peripheral collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using the electron-positron decay channel. Events were categorized by neutron emission patterns detected in zero-degree calorimeters (0n0n, 0nXn, XnXn), and template fits to invariant mass and pair transverse momentum distributions were used to extract coherent and incoherent yields separately. The photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy ambiguity was resolved using the technique of Guzey et al. (2013) to obtain the γ+Au → J/ψ+Au cross section.

## Key numbers
- Coherent J/ψ cross section on Au nuclei: 71±10% of the free-proton cross section at ⟨WγN⟩ = 25.0 GeV (suppression factor S^Au_coh = 0.71±0.04±0.07±0.07, where errors are model uncertainty, combined statistical/systematic, and luminosity scale)
- Incoherent J/ψ cross section on Au nuclei: 36±7% of the free-proton cross section at ⟨WγN⟩ = 25.0 GeV (suppression factor S^Au_incoh = 0.36^{+0.03}_{-0.04}±0.04±0.04)
- Integrated luminosity: 13.5 nb⁻¹, yielding approximately 2.4×10⁷ UPC J/ψ triggered events
- Branching ratio for J/ψ → e⁺e⁻: 5.97%

## Context
This measurement extends the study of nuclear suppression in vector meson photoproduction from coherent processes (previously measured by PHENIX (2009), ALICE (2012, 2020, 2021, 2023), CMS (2023)) to include the first explicit measurement of incoherent nuclear suppression. The data are compared with the leading twist approximation (LTA) nuclear shadowing model of Guzey et al. (2013) and the color glass condensate (CGC) saturation model of Mantysaari et al. (2022). The stronger suppression in incoherent production provides complementary constraints to hadronic p+A collision data on nuclear parton distribution functions, and establishes an experimental baseline for future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
