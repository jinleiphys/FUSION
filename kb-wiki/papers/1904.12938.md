# Multiplicity dependence of strange and multi-strange hadrons in p$-$p, p$-$Pb and Pb$-$Pb collisions at LHC energies using Tsallis-Weibull Formalism

> The transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) distribution of strange hadrons ($K_{S}^{0}$ and $Λ$) and multi-strange hadrons( $Ξ$ and $Ω$) measured in p$-$p, p$-$Pb, and Pb$-$Pb collisions at LHC energies have been studied for different multiplicity classes using Tsallis-Weibull (or q$-$Weibull)formalism. The distribution describes the measured $p_{T}$ spectra for all multiplicity (or centrality)classes. The multiplicity dependence of the extracted parameters are studied for the mentioned collisions systems. The $λ$ parameter was observed to increase systematically with the collision multiplicity and follows a mass hierarchy for all collision system. This characteristic feature indicates that $λ$ can be associated to the strength of collectivity for heavy ion collisions. It can also be related to strength of dynamic effects such as multi-partonic interactions and color reconnections which mimic collectivity in smaller systems. The non-extensive $q$ parameter is found to be greater than one for all the particles suggesting that the strange particles are emitted from a source which is not fully equilibrated.

## Key claim

The Tsallis-Weibull (q-Weibull) distribution successfully describes the transverse momentum spectra of strange and multi-strange hadrons across all collision systems (p-p, p-Pb, Pb-Pb) and multiplicity classes at LHC energies, with the lambda parameter showing a systematic increase with multiplicity and a mass hierarchy that may indicate collectivity in heavy-ion collisions or mimic collectivity via multi-partonic interactions and color reconnections in smaller systems.

## Method

The authors fitted the q-Weibull distribution function to published ALICE experimental data for the transverse momentum spectra of K_S^0, Lambda, Xi, and Omega hadrons in p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, across multiple multiplicity or centrality classes. The three free parameters (q, lambda, k) were extracted from fits to the invariant yield as a function of p_T, and their evolution was studied as a function of mean charged particle density (dN_ch/d_eta) or number of participating nucleons (N_part).

## Key numbers

- In p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the lambda parameter for K_S^0 ranges from 0.48 +/- 0.006 (lowest multiplicity class, dN_ch/d_eta = 2.26 +/- 0.12) to 0.68 +/- 0.007 (highest multiplicity class, dN_ch/d_eta = 21.3 +/- 0.6)
- In p-p collisions, the lambda parameter for Omega hadrons reaches 1.54 +/- 0.1 at the highest multiplicity class (dN_ch/d_eta = 18.9 +/- 0.6)
- In Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the lambda parameter for K_S^0 ranges from 0.51 +/- 0.01 (80-90% centrality, N_part = 6.4 +/- 4.2) to 0.76 +/- 0.01 (0-5% centrality, N_part = 382.7 +/- 3.0)
- In Pb-Pb collisions, the lambda parameter for Omega hadrons reaches 1.67 +/- 0.03 in the 0-10% centrality class (N_part = 356.1 +/- 3.6)
- The q parameter is greater than one for all particles and collision systems, with values typically between 1.09 and 1.37 across all fitted data sets
- In p-p collisions, the k parameter for K_S^0 ranges from 1.73 +/- 0.03 to 1.84 +/- 0.02 across multiplicity classes

## Context

This work extends the q-Weibull formalism previously applied to charged particle p_T spectra (qweib3) to identified strange and multi-strange hadrons across multiple collision systems. The study builds on observations of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions reported by ALICE (alicepp, alicenature, ppbcas), and on the use of Tsallis statistics to describe p_T distributions in hadronic collisions (cleyman1, wilk, shukla, pptsallis). The results suggest that the lambda parameter can serve as a proxy for collective radial flow in heavy-ion collisions, while in small systems it may reflect dynamic effects such as multi-partonic interactions and color reconnections predicted by fragmentation models (dipsy, rope, pythia8). The work enables further investigation of the physical interpretation of q-Weibull parameters across different particle species and collision energies.
