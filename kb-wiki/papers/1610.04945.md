# Diffractive charmonium spectrum in high energy collisions in the basis light-front quantization approach

> Using the charmonium light-front wavefunctions obtained by diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian with the one-gluon exchange interaction and a confining potential inspired by light-front holography in the basis light-front quantization formalism, we compute production of charmonium states in diffractive deep inelastic scattering and ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions within the dipole picture. Our method allows us to predict yields of all vector charmonium states below the open flavor thresholds in high-energy deep inelastic scattering, proton-nucleus and ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions, without introducing any new parameters in the light-front wavefunctions. The obtained charmonium cross section is in reasonable agreement with experimental data at HERA, RHIC and LHC. We observe that the cross-section ratio $σ_{Ψ(2s)}/σ_{J/Ψ}$ reveals significant independence of model parameters.

## Key claim

Using charmonium light-front wavefunctions from the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, which are obtained by diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian with one-gluon exchange and a confining potential inspired by light-front holography, the authors compute diffractive charmonium production in the dipole picture without introducing new parameters for the wavefunctions. The cross-section ratio σ_Ψ(2s)/σ_J/Ψ shows significant independence of model parameters, though the BLFQ wavefunctions underestimate J/Ψ production at HERA, especially at low Q², where the dipole model itself has large theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive vector charmonium production in diffractive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions (UPC) using the dipole model, where the scattering amplitude is given by the convolution of the dipole-proton cross section with the overlap of the virtual photon light-front wavefunction (LFWF) and the vector meson LFWF. The charmonium LFWFs for J/Ψ and Ψ(2s) are obtained by solving the light-front Schrödinger equation with an effective Hamiltonian that includes kinetic energy, a confining potential from light-front holography, and one-gluon exchange, with parameters m_c = 1.522 GeV and κ_con = 0.938 GeV fitted to the charmonium mass spectrum. The dipole cross section is taken from two impact-parameter-dependent parametrizations, the bSat model (based on Glauber-Mueller formula with LO DGLAP evolution) and the bCGC model (interpolating between BFKL and Balitsky-Kovchegov solutions), with parameters fitted to HERA inclusive DIS data.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass in the BLFQ Hamiltonian fit: m_c = 1.522 GeV; confining strength: κ_con = 0.938 GeV; both fitted to 8 charmonium states below open-flavor thresholds, with root-mean-square deviation in masses of about 50 MeV.
- For coherent J/Ψ production in Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV at RHIC, the BLFQ LFWF with bCGC III dipole parametrization predicts dσ/dy = 60.4 μb, compared to the measured value 45.6 ± 13.3 (stat) ± 5.9 (sys) μb.
- For coherent J/Ψ production in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV at LHC, the BLFQ LFWF prediction (with bCGC III) is within statistical uncertainty of ALICE and CMS data.
- The bSat I dipole model fit to ZEUS data gives χ²/d.o.f. = 193.0/160 = 1.21; the bCGC I fit gives χ²/d.o.f. = 144.0/160 = 0.900.
- The boosted Gaussian II LFWF prediction for J/Ψ cross section at Q² = 0 can differ by as much as 30% using different bSat dipole parametrizations.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to charmonium mass spectra, decay constants, and form factors (Li et al., 2015) to diffractive production cross sections, using the dipole model that has been successful in describing HERA data with phenomenological wavefunctions (Kowalski et al., 2006; Marquet et al., 2007). The BLFQ wavefunctions are compared to boosted Gaussian wavefunctions (Kowalski et al., 2006; Armesto et al., 2014) and show qualitative differences in the ratio σ_L/σ_T at large Q²; the cross-section ratio σ_Ψ(2s)/σ_J/Ψ is found to be significantly less sensitive to dipole model parameters than the individual cross sections, making it a promising observable for discriminating between wavefunction models with future data from the Electron-Ion Collider and LHeC.
