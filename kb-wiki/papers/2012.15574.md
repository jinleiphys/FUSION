# Self-consistent conversion of a one-component bulk viscous fluid to particles

> Comparison of heavy-ion experiments to fluid dynamics simulations requires the conversion of the fluid to particles. Extending the approach in Molnar & Wolff, PRC 95, 024903 (2017), this work presents self-consistent bulk viscous corrections from kinetic theory for a one-component system with isotropic $2\to 2$ interactions. The phase space corrections are contrasted to the Grad ansatz and also to corrections obtained from the relaxation time approximation. In addition, the bulk viscosity of the system is calculated and compared with the Grad result, as well as the $ζ\propto (1 - 3 c_s^2)^2 η$ relation between shear and bulk viscosity near the conformal limit. The possible influence of various bulk correction choices on differential elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ in heavy-ion collisions is also estimated.

# Self-consistent conversion of a one-component bulk viscous fluid to particles

## Key claim

Self-consistent bulk viscous phase space corrections from kinetic theory for a one-component system with isotropic $2\to 2$ interactions exhibit a more complicated momentum dependence than the Grad ansatz or relaxation time approximation, switching sign twice as a function of momentum, and the Grad approximation underestimates the bulk viscosity by more than a factor of 2 for small mass-to-temperature ratios $m/T < 0.1$.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Boltzmann transport equation for a one-component system with isotropic, energy-independent $2\to 2$ interactions, obtaining bulk viscous corrections $\delta f$ by extremizing a variational functional $Q[\chi]$ under constraints that the bulk correction contributes neither to local energy density nor to local particle density. The self-consistent solutions for the scalar function $\chi(|\tilde p|)$ are computed numerically using basis expansions for mass-to-temperature ratios $10^{-2} \le m/T \le 70$, and the bulk viscosity is extracted from the maximum of the variational functional. The results are compared to the Grad 14-moment approximation and the Dusling-Schäfer relaxation time approximation, and their effects on differential elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ are estimated using a four-source model.

## Key numbers

- Self-consistent bulk viscosity exceeds the Grad result by more than a factor of 2 for $m/T < 0.1$ (small mass limit)
- Near the nonrelativistic limit ($m/T > 10$), the Grad approximation is accurate to within 10% for the bulk viscosity
- Near the conformal limit ($m/T \lesssim 0.03$), the Weinberg relation $\zeta \approx 3.75 (1 - 3 c_s^2)^2 \eta$ holds for self-consistent corrections, compared to $\zeta_{Grad} \approx (5/3)(1 - 3 c_s^2)^2 \eta_{Grad}$ in the Grad approximation
- For pions ($m/T = 1$) at high $p_T$ ($1.5 < p_T < 2$ GeV), the Grad approach overestimates bulk viscous effects on $v_2$ by up to 50% compared to self-consistent results
- The four-source model uses parameters $T = 0.14$ GeV, $v_x = 0.6$, $v_y = 0.5$, and $\Pi/e = -0.001$ to approximate mid-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200A$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent approach of Molnar and Wolff (2017, PRC 95, 024903) from shear viscous corrections to bulk viscous corrections for one-component systems with isotropic $2\to 2$ interactions. It contrasts the self-consistent results with the Grad ansatz used in the Hirano-Monnai bulk corrections (Monnai, 2009) and the relaxation time approximation of Dusling and Schäfer (2011). The findings enable more reliable extraction of bulk viscosity from heavy-ion collision data and highlight the need for future calculations in multicomponent hadronic mixtures, as the single-component results are insufficient for realistic hadron gas applications.
