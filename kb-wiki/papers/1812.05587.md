# Polarized baryon production in heavy ion collisions: an analytic hydrodynamical study

> We utilize known exact analytic solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics to analytically calculate the polarization of baryons produced in heavy ion collisions. Assuming local thermodynamical equilibrium also for spin degrees of freedom, baryons get a net polarization at their formation (freeze-out). This polarization depends on the time evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), which can be described as an almost perfect fluid. By using exact analytic solutions, we thus can analyze the necessity of rotation (and vorticity) for non-zero net polarization. In this paper we give the first analytical calculations for the polarization four-vector. We use two hydrodynamical solutions; one is the spherically symmetric Hubble flow (a somewhat oversimplified model, to demonstrate the methodology). The other solution which we use is a somewhat more involved one that corresponds to a rotating and accelerating expansion, and is thus well suited to investigate some main features of the time evolution of the QGP created in peripheral heavy-ion collisions (although there are still many numerous features of a real collision geometry that are beyond the reach of this simple model). Finally we illustrate and discuss our results on the polarization.

## Key claim

Using exact analytic solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics, the authors provide the first analytical calculations of the baryon polarization four-vector in heavy ion collisions, demonstrating that non-zero net polarization requires rotation and vorticity in the expanding QGP, though the models employed are simplified and do not capture all features of real collision geometry.

## Method

The authors assume local thermodynamic equilibrium including spin degrees of freedom and compute the polarization of baryons at freeze-out using two exact analytic solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics: a spherically symmetric Hubble flow (an oversimplified model used to demonstrate the methodology) and a rotating, accelerating expansion solution suited to investigate features of the time evolution of the QGP created in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. The polarization four-vector is derived analytically from the velocity and vorticity fields of these solutions.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon polarization in heavy ion collisions by moving beyond purely numerical or phenomenological approaches to provide the first analytic treatment using exact hydrodynamical solutions. It builds on the understanding that the QGP behaves as an almost perfect fluid and that spin degrees of freedom may reach local thermodynamic equilibrium. The results enable future comparisons with experimental polarization measurements from peripheral collisions and can guide more realistic models that incorporate additional features of collision geometry not captured by these simple analytic solutions.
