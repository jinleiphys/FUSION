# Precision calculation of the quartet-channel p-d scattering length

> We present a fully perturbative calculation of the quartet-channel proton--deuteron scattering length up to next-to-next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory. We use a framework that consistently extracts the Coulomb-modified effective range function for a screened Coulomb potential in momentum space and allows for a clear linear extrapolation back to the physical limit without screening. Our result of (10.9 +/- 0.4) fm agrees with older experimental determinations of this quantity but deviates from potential-model calculations and a more recent result from Black et al., which find larger values around 14 fm. As a possible resolution to this discrepancy, we discuss the scheme dependence of Coulomb subtractions in a three-body system.

## Key claim
The authors present a fully perturbative calculation of the quartet-channel proton-deuteron scattering length up to next-to-next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory, obtaining a value of (10.9 ± 0.4) fm that agrees with older experimental determinations but deviates from potential-model calculations and the more recent result from Black et al. (1999), which found values around 14 fm. The paper suggests that this discrepancy may arise from scheme dependence in how Coulomb subtractions are performed in a three-body system.

## Method
The calculation uses pionless effective field theory with a screened Coulomb potential in momentum space, introducing a small photon mass λ as a regulator. The authors solve the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation for the quartet-channel amplitude, including Coulomb diagrams (bubble, box, and effective-range corrections) up to next-to-next-to-leading order, and extract the Coulomb-modified effective range function with a clear linear extrapolation back to the physical limit λ→0. The calculation is performed strictly perturbatively, expanding the deuteron propagator and interaction kernels in the effective range rd to avoid uncontrolled resummation of higher-order terms.

## Key numbers
- The quartet-channel p-d scattering length is a_pd = (10.9 ± 0.4) fm, obtained at next-to-next-to-leading order with an expected EFT accuracy of about 3%
- The deuteron binding momentum γ_d = 45.7022(1) MeV and effective range r_d = 1.765(4) fm are used as two-body input parameters
- The calculation uses momentum cutoffs Λ = 140, 280, and 560 MeV to check cutoff dependence
- The scattering length is extracted from the Coulomb-modified effective range function in the momentum range from 2 to 4 MeV near threshold

## Context
This work extends the pionless EFT formalism for the n-d system (Bedaque 1997, 1998) and earlier p-d calculations by Rupak and Kong (2001) and the present authors (2011), now performing a strictly perturbative calculation following the approach of Vanasse (2013) for the n-d system. The result agrees with older experimental determinations by Huttel (1983) and Arvieux (1973) but deviates from potential-model calculations and the measurement by Black et al. (1999). The method can be applied to other charged-particle systems, particularly halo nuclei, and enables extraction of near-threshold bound-state properties such as asymptotic normalization constants for astrophysical S-factors.
