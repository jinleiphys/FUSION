# QCD axial anomaly enhances the $Î·Î·^\prime$ decay of the hybrid candidate $Î·_1(1855)$

## Key claim

The QCD axial anomaly enhances the decay width of the $\eta\eta^\prime$ channel for the $\eta_1(1855)$ interpreted as a $\bar s s g$ hybrid meson, even though this mode is strongly suppressed by the small $P$-wave phase space; the authors find that the anomaly-assisted decay processes produce partial widths of 1.80 and 0.74 MeV, which are comparable to or larger than the normal-process width of 0.68 MeV, making this a characteristic signal of hybrid nature.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules and light-cone sum rules to compute three-point correlation functions for the $\bar s s g$ hybrid meson with quantum numbers $I^G J^{PC}=0^+1^{-+}$, evaluating both the normal decay process (one quark-antiquark pair excited from the valence gluon) and the abnormal processes where the $\eta$ or $\eta^\prime$ meson is produced via the QCD axial anomaly through the matrix elements $\langle 0| (\alpha_s/4\pi) G\tilde G |\eta\rangle = m_\eta^2 f_\eta$ and similarly for $\eta^\prime$. They performed operator product expansion and Borel transformation to extract coupling constants and partial decay widths for the $\eta\eta^\prime$, $K^*(892)\bar K$, and $K_1(1270)\bar K$ channels.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\eta_1(1855)$: $M = 1855 \pm 9 ^{+6}_{-1}~\text{MeV}/c^2$; width $\Gamma = 188 \pm 18 ^{+3}_{-8}~\text{MeV}$ (BESIII measurement)
- Partial width for $\eta_1(1855) \to \eta\eta^\prime$ via anomaly process (b): $\Gamma = 1.80^{+0.72}_{-0.44}~\text{MeV}$
- Partial width for $\eta_1(1855) \to \eta\eta^\prime$ via anomaly process (c): $\Gamma = 0.74^{+0.42}_{-0.18}~\text{MeV}$
- Partial width for $\eta_1(1855) \to \eta\eta^\prime$ via normal process (a): $\Gamma = 0.68^{+2.26}_{-0.36}~\text{MeV}$
- Partial width for $\eta_1(1855) \to K^*(892)\bar K + c.c.$: $\Gamma = 98.1^{+82.9}_{-60.4}~\text{MeV}$
- Partial width for $\eta_1(1855) \to K_1(1270)\bar K + c.c.$: $\Gamma = 30.4^{+40.1}_{-19.3}~\text{MeV}$
- Sum of calculated partial widths for $\bar s s g$ hybrid at 1855 MeV: $\Gamma_{\text{sum}} \approx 132^{+126}_{-81}~\text{MeV}$
- Coupling constant $|g^b_{\eta\eta^\prime}| = 1.78$ (anomaly process b); $|g^c_{\eta\eta^\prime}| = 0.53$ (anomaly process c)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule study of $\bar q q g$ hybrid mesons (Chen, Huang, Zhu 2010) by incorporating the QCD axial anomaly contribution to $\eta\eta^\prime$ decays, which was neglected earlier. It directly addresses the decades-long puzzle that the flux-tube model selection rule (Page 1998) forbids $J^{PC}=1^{-+}$ hybrid decays into two $S$-wave ground-state mesons, contradicting the experimental discovery modes of $\pi_1(1400)$, $\pi_1(1600)$, and $\eta_1(1855)$. The results support the interpretation of $\eta_1(1855)$ as a $\bar s s g$ hybrid meson rather than a $K\bar K_1(1400)$ hadronic molecule (Dong 2022), where the $K^*(892)\bar K$ partial width differs by two orders of magnitude (98.1 MeV vs. 0.9 MeV). The work enables future experimental searches at BESIII, Belle-II, GlueX, LHC, and PANDA to differentiate between the $\bar q q g$ and $\bar s s g$ hybrid interpretations and to search for partner states.
