# The Myriad Uses of Instantons

> In quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the role which topologically non-trivial configurations play in splitting the singlet pseudo-Goldstone meson, the $η^\prime$, from the octet is familiar. In addition, such configurations contribute to other processes which violate the axial $U(1)_A$ symmetry. While the nature of topological fluctuations in the confined phase is still unsettled, at temperatures well above that for the chiral phase transition, they can be described by a dilute gas of instantons. We show that instantons of arbitrary topological charge $Q$ generate anomalous interactions between $2 N_f |Q|$ quarks, which for $Q = 1$ make the $η^\prime$ heavy. For two flavors we compute an anomalous quartic meson coupling and discuss its implications for the phenomenology of the chiral phase transition. A dilute instanton gas suggests that for cold, dense quarks, instantons do not evaporate until very high densities, when the baryon chemical potential is $\gtrsim 2$ GeV.

## Key claim

Instantons of arbitrary topological charge \(Q\) generate effective interactions between \(2 N_f |Q|\) quarks, generalizing the familiar 't Hooft vertex for \(Q=1\); for two flavors, the \(Q=2\) contribution produces a quartic meson coupling whose phenomenological impact on the chiral phase transition is small unless the coupling is very large.

## Method

The authors compute the generating functional of QCD in a background of \(Q\)-instantons, using the ADHM construction and expanding in the limit where constituent instantons are well separated (their separations much larger than their sizes). They derive the zero-mode determinant for massless quarks, obtaining a local effective Lagrangian for \(Q=2\) instantons, and then bosonize this into a linear sigma model for two flavors. The meson mass spectrum is studied in a mean-field approximation, with parameters fixed by vacuum masses and \(f_\pi\), and the reduced temperature is varied to mimic the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers

* Instanton density in vacuum for \(N_c=3\), \(N_f=2\): \(n_1(\rho) = \frac{d_{\overline{MS}}}{\rho^5} \left( \frac{8\pi^2}{g^2} \right)^6 \exp\left( -\frac{8\pi^2}{g^2} \right)\) with \(d_{\overline{MS}} \approx 0.00449\).
* Typical instanton size from the density maximum: \(\rho \Lambda_{\overline{MS}} \approx 1/2\), giving \(\rho \approx 1/3\) fm for \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} \approx 300\) MeV.
* Computed couplings from the dilute instanton gas (with \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 0.3\) GeV): \(\kappa_1 = 3886\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\kappa_2 = 1.819 \times 10^8\ \text{GeV}^{-8}\).
* Resulting anomalous meson couplings in vacuum: \(\chi_1 = 0.320\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\chi_2 = 1.852\) (with \(M = 0.0953\) GeV).
* Instanton effects reduced to 10% of vacuum strength at \(T \approx 0.7\, \Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\) for \(\mu=0\), and at \(\mu \approx 2.4\, \Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\) for \(T=0\).
* For cold, dense quarks, instantons evaporate only at baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B \gtrsim 2\) GeV (using \(T_c = 156\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the classic 't Hooft instanton analysis (1976) for \(Q=1\) to arbitrary topological charge \(Q\), providing explicit local effective interactions for \(Q=2\). It builds on the ADHM construction (Atiyah, Drinfeld, Hitchin, Manin, 1978) and prior work on instanton zero modes (Corrigan 1978, Osborn 1978). The results enable studies of axial \(U(1)_A\) violation in higher-point correlation functions and in meson spectra, and suggest that instanton effects persist to very high baryon densities in cold quark matter. The dilute gas approximation used here is supported by lattice QCD results (Bazavov 2012, Buchoff 2013, Bonati 2015, Borsanyi 2015) showing that the topological susceptibility above a few hundred MeV follows the dilute instanton gas prediction.
