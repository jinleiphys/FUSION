# Time reversal and parity breaking by propagation

> The time independent mean field method (TIMF) for the calculation of matrix elements of non relativistic propagators is based on a variational principle whose non linearity induces a multiplicity of variational solutions. Several of them can break any symmetry shared by the Hamiltonian and initial and final states. We describe a soluble model where, in particular, time reversal and parity breakings occur. Such breakings account for important properties of propagation amplitudes.

## Key claim

The time-independent mean field (TIMF) method for calculating propagator matrix elements can produce variational solutions that break time reversal and parity symmetries, even when the Hamiltonian and the initial/final states are symmetric; these symmetry-breaking solutions are not merely artifacts but can provide exact amplitudes and are necessary to account for all singularities of the exact propagator when correlations are limited.

## Method

The authors construct a soluble two-particle model using two single-particle orbitals with energies -1 and 1, giving a Hamiltonian with eigenvalues -2, 0, 0, and 2. They apply the TIMF variational principle to a Schwinger-like functional for the propagator amplitude, using trial states restricted to product forms with a correlation parameter c that interpolates between an uncorrelated mean-field limit (c=1) and the exact solution (c=c_ex). By solving the saddle-point equations, they classify the variational solutions into three branches: fully symmetric (conserving both parity and time reversal), parity-breaking (time reversal conserved), and time-reversal-breaking (parity conserved).

## Key numbers

* Single-particle energies: -1 and 1 for orbitals |a> and |b>, respectively.
* Two-body Hamiltonian eigenvalues: -2, 0, 0, and 2 for states |a^2>, |ab>, |ba>, |b^2>.
* Exact propagator: D_ex(E) = r^4/(E+2) + 2r^2/E + 1/(E-2), with poles at E = -2, 0, and 2.
* For (r,E) = (-1.2, 1.5): c_ex = -1.29 and D_ex = 0.51.
* For uncorrelated trial states (c=1) at E=0: parity-breaking residue = r^2, time-reversal-breaking residue = 2r^2, compared to exact residue 2r^2.
* For fully symmetric estimates at E=2 and E=-2: residues 1 and r^4, respectively, matching exact residues.

## Context

This work extends the TIMF method previously applied to nuclear and atomic processes with 3, 4, 5, and 8 particles (Abe and Giraud 1985; Lemm, Weiguny, and Giraud 1990; Giraud and Nagarajan 1991; Giraud, Kessal, and Liu 1987; Wierling et al. 1994), where symmetry breakings were already observed (Mekideche and Giraud 1993; Giraud et al. 1993). The soluble model provides a complete classification of solution branches and demonstrates that mean-field solutions should be mixed linearly to recover the best approximation of the exact solution over the full energy range, removing the ambiguity of choosing between multiple saddle points.
