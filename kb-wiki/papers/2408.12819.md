# Parton Distribution Function of a Deuteron-like Dibaryon System from Lattice QCD

> We report a lattice QCD calculation of the parton distribution function (PDF) of a deuteron-like dibaryon system using large-momentum effective theory. The calculation is done on three Wilson Clover ensembles with a fixed lattice spacing a=0.105 fm and two pion masses. The lattice matrix elements are computed at proton momenta up to 2.46 GeV with the signal of high momentum modes being improved by applying the momentum smearing technique. The state-of-the-art renormalization, matching and extrapolation are then applied to obtain the final result of the light-cone PDF. A comparison between the result of the dibaryon system and the sum of the proton and neutron PDFs is also given.

## Key claim

The authors report the first lattice QCD calculation of the parton distribution function (PDF) of a deuteron-like dibaryon system using large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), but the result is limited to a single lattice spacing and two pion masses, and the LaMET approach is shown to be unreliable in the small-x region (below approximately x = 0.065).

## Method

The calculation was performed on three Wilson-clover ensembles with fixed lattice spacing a = 0.105 fm and pion masses of 293 MeV and 941 MeV, using proton momenta up to 2.46 GeV with momentum smearing to improve high-momentum signals. Bare matrix elements were computed from two-point and three-point correlation functions with joint fitting to extract ground-state quasi-PDF matrix elements, followed by nonperturbative hybrid-scheme renormalization, leading renormalon resummation (LRR), renormalization group resummation (RGR), and perturbative matching to obtain the light-cone PDF.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.105 fm (fixed across all three ensembles)
- Pion masses: 292.9(1.2) MeV (C32P29 and C24P29 ensembles) and 940.7(1.2) MeV (C24P90 ensemble)
- Maximum proton momentum: 2.46 GeV (achieved on C24P29 and C24P90 ensembles)
- Lattice sizes: 32^3 x 64 (C32P29) and 24^3 x 72 (C24P29 and C24P90)
- The LRR+RGR-improved PDF blows up in the small-x region from approximately -0.065 < x < 0.065, indicating LaMET is unreliable in that region
- Most chi-squared per degree of freedom values for the correlated fits are below 1.5

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach, previously applied to single-nucleon PDFs by groups such as Lattice Parton Collaboration (2022) and others, to a multibaryon system for the first time. It builds on the pioneering work of the NPLQCD collaboration (Detmold 2020, Illa 2021) who calculated moments of proton, diproton, and He-3 systems, and relates to the nuclear EMC effect first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (1983). The calculation enables direct comparison of dibaryon PDFs with the sum of proton and neutron PDFs from lattice QCD, providing a new tool to study nuclear effects in the simplest multibaryon bound system.
