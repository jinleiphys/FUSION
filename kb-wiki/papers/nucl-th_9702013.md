# Constraints on Parity-Even Time Reversal Violation in the Nucleon-Nucleon System and Its Connection to Charge Symmetry Breaking

> Parity-even time reversal violation (TRV) in the nucleon-nucleon interaction is reconsidered. The TRV $ρ$-exchange interaction on which recent analyses of measurements are based is necessarily also charge-symmetry breaking (CSB). Limits on its strength $\bar{g}_ρ$ relative to regular $ρ$-exchange are extracted from recent CSB experiments in neutron-proton scattering. The result $\bar{g}_ρ\le 6.7\times 10^{-3}$ (95% CL) is considerably lower than limits inferred from direct TRV tests in nuclear processes. Properties of $a_1$-exchange and limit imposed by the neutron EDM are briefly discussed.

**Authors:** Simonius, Markus  
**Date:** 1997-02-06

## Key claim

Parity-even time reversal violating (TRV) interactions in the nucleon-nucleon system mediated by charged rho-meson exchange are necessarily also charge-symmetry breaking (CSB), and limits on the TRV coupling strength extracted from existing CSB experiments in neutron-proton scattering yield a bound considerably lower than those inferred from direct TRV tests in nuclear processes, though the result depends on the reliability of theoretical predictions for the regular (TRI) CSB contribution that must be subtracted.

## Method

The author derives the unique form of the parity-even TRV rho-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction from effective Lagrangian and symmetry constraints, then computes the contribution of this interaction to the CSB asymmetry Delta A = A^n - A^p in elastic neutron-proton scattering using helicity amplitude formalism and partial wave decomposition. Limits on the TRV coupling strength gbar_rho are extracted by comparing the calculated TRV+CSB asymmetry with experimental CSB measurements at 183, 347, and 477 MeV after subtracting theoretical predictions for the regular (time-reversal invariant) CSB contribution, with reduction factors alpha_J accounting for short-range correlations estimated for the lowest contributing partial wave.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the TRV rho-exchange coupling strength: |gbar_rho| <= 6.7 x 10^-3 at 95% confidence level (|gbar_rho| <= 4.4 x 10^-3 at 80% CL), obtained from weighted average of three CSB measurements
- At 347 MeV lab energy and 72.8 degree center-of-mass angle: Delta A = (59 +/- 10) x 10^-4, with Delta A^TRI = 53 x 10^-4, yielding Delta = (6 +/- 10) x 10^-4 and gbar_rho = (22 +/- 37) x 10^-4
- At 477 MeV and 69.7 degrees: Delta A = (47 +/- 23) x 10^-4, with Delta A^TRI = 55 x 10^-4, yielding Delta = (-8 +/- 23) x 10^-4 and gbar_rho = (-27 +/- 77) x 10^-4
- At 183 MeV averaged over 82.2-116.1 degrees: Delta A = (34.8 +/- 7.4) x 10^-4, with Delta A^TRI = 33 x 10^-4, yielding Delta = (1.8 +/- 7.4) x 10^-4 and gbar_rho = (20 +/- 83) x 10^-4
- Sensitivity coefficient Kbar(alpha_1=1/2) = 0.269 at 347 MeV, 0.297 at 477 MeV, and 0.089 at 183 MeV
- For axial vector (a1) exchange: limit on TRV a1-nucleon coupling f_T <= 3 x 10^-5 derived from neutron electric dipole moment limit d_n <= 1.1 x 10^-25 e-cm (95% CL)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis of parity-even TRV constraints (Simonius, 1975) and the rho-exchange TRV interaction proposed in Simonius and Wyler (1977), which formed the basis for recent nuclear TRV searches by Haxton and Hoering (1993) and Haxton, Hoering, and Musolf (1994). The derived limit on gbar_rho is considerably lower than those obtained from nuclear process analyses (Haxton and Hoering, 1993; Engel, Gould, and Hnizdo, 1994; Huffman et al., 1996; Vorov, 1996) and slightly better than constraints from atomic electric dipole moments, though the neutron EDM limit from Haxton, Hoering, and Musolf (1994) is numerically lower but depends on the uncertain parity-violating pi-NN coupling constant. The analysis enables future work to focus on unnatural parity exchange (a1 or three-pion exchange) as the only remaining possibility for parity-even TRV beyond the constrained rho-exchange mechanism.
