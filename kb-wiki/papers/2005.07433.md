# Subleading contributions to the nuclear scalar isoscalar currents

> We extend our recent analyses of the nuclear vector, axial-vector and pseudoscalar currents and derive the leading one-loop corrections to the two-nucleon scalar current operator in the framework of chiral effective field theory using the method of unitary transformation. We also show that the scalar current operators at zero momentum transfer are directly related to the quark mass dependence of the nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading one-loop corrections (at chiral order Q^0) to the two-nucleon scalar isoscalar current operator in chiral effective field theory, extending previous work on vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar currents; the scalar current operators at zero momentum transfer are shown to be directly related to the quark mass dependence of nuclear forces, though the one-body current at the same accuracy level is not yet available.

## Method

The derivation uses the method of unitary transformation within the heavy-baryon formulation of SU(2) chiral effective field theory, following the same approach previously applied to nuclear forces and electroweak currents. The authors compute one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and short-range contact contributions to the two-nucleon scalar current operator at order Q^0, employing dimensional regularization and expressing results in terms of physical parameters F_π, M_π, and g_A. The renormalization of ultraviolet divergences is performed by absorbing them into low-energy constants from the effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The dominant two-nucleon scalar current contribution appears at chiral order Q^{-2} from one-pion exchange, with the explicit form S_2N^(Q^{-2}) = - (g_A^2 M_π^2)/(4 F_π^2 m_q) × (q_1·σ_1 q_2·σ_2)/[(M_π^2+q_1^2)(M_π^2+q_2^2)] τ_1·τ_2
- The pion-nucleon σ-term is given as σ_πN = (59.1 ± 3.5) MeV from Roy-Steiner-equation analysis of pion-nucleon scattering
- The scalar functions o_i(k) for the one-pion-exchange contributions at order Q^0 are expressed in terms of the loop function L(k) = [√(k^2+4M_π^2)/k] × ln[(√(k^2+4M_π^2)+k)/(2M_π)]
- The β-functions for the renormalized contact LECs are β_S^NN = (1/2)(1+6g_A^2-15g_A^4+24F^2 g_A^2 C_T) and β_T^NN = (1/4)(1+6g_A^2-15g_A^4+48F^2 g_A^2 C_T)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of nuclear vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar currents (Kolling 2009, Kolling 2011, Krebs 2016, Krebs 2019) to the scalar isoscalar channel, using the same unitary transformation method that ensures off-shell consistency with the corresponding nuclear forces (Epelbaum 2004, Epelbaum 2014, Bernard 2007, Bernard 2011). The derived scalar currents are relevant for dark matter searches involving Higgs-portal and WIMP models, and the relation to quark mass dependence of nuclear forces connects to lattice QCD efforts in the multibaryon sector (Epelbaum 2002, Beane 2002, Barnea 2013, Baru 2015) and studies of temporal variation of light quark masses (Bedaque 2010, Berengut 2013).
