# Symmetry Obstruction to Fermi Liquid Behavior in the Unitary Limit

> We show that a Fermi gas, in three dimensions, at temperatures above the superconducting phase transition but below the Fermi temperature, can not be described by Fermi Liquid Theory (FLT) in the unitary limit where the scattering length diverges. The result follows by showing that the there are no effective field theory descriptions that both behave like a Fermi liquid and properly non-linearly realize the spontaneously broken boost and conformal invariance of the system.

# Symmetry Obstruction to Fermi Liquid Behavior in the Unitary Limit

## Key claim
A three-dimensional Fermi gas at unitarity (where the scattering length diverges) cannot be described by Fermi Liquid Theory for temperatures between the superconducting transition temperature and the Fermi temperature, because no effective field theory can simultaneously behave like a Fermi liquid and non-linearly realize the spontaneously broken boost and conformal symmetries of the system.

## Method
The authors use the coset construction for spontaneously broken space-time symmetries to analyze the non-linear realization of the Schrödinger group, which includes boosts, dilations, and special conformal transformations. They consider three possible symmetry realizations: including all Goldstone modes (framon, dilaton, and the Goldstone for special conformal transformations), imposing inverse Higgs constraints to eliminate some Goldstones, or imposing dynamical inverse Higgs constraints (DIHC) that yield operator constraints without introducing Goldstone modes. The consistency of each realization with Fermi liquid power counting and the existence of long-lived quasiparticles is then tested.

## Key numbers
- The quasiparticle width in a Fermi liquid scales as $\Gamma(E) \sim (E-\mu)^2$, where $\mu$ is the chemical potential.
- If the framon Goldstone mode exists and couples non-derivatively, the quasiparticle width scales as $\Gamma(E) \sim (E-\mu)^{d/3}$, which gives non-Fermi liquid behavior in d=2 and marginal Fermi liquid behavior in d=3.
- In two dimensions, the framon momentum scales as $k_\eta \sim \lambda^2$, where $\lambda$ is the RG rescaling parameter, making it sub-leading to quasiparticle momentum and forcing a multipole expansion.
- In three dimensions, the framon momentum scales as $k_\eta \sim \lambda$, the same as quasiparticle momentum.
- The Landau relation from boost invariance is $1/m^\star = 1/m + G_1/3$, where $m^\star$ is the effective mass, $m$ is the free electron mass, and $G_1 = g_1 D(\mu)$ with $D(\mu)$ the density of states on the Fermi surface.
- In three dimensions at unitarity, the constraint from the dilaton yields $(2-d)g(\vec{p},\mu) = \beta(g)$, which for d=3 gives $\beta(g) = -g$, implying power-law running of the coupling inconsistent with Fermi liquid theory.

## Context
This work addresses the open question of whether cold Fermi gases at unitarity (where the scattering length diverges) behave as Fermi liquids above the superconducting transition temperature. Prior experiments gave conflicting results: Ref. (NatureFLT, 2008) and Ref. (PRLFLT, 2009) found Fermi liquid behavior, while Ref. (Pseudogap1, 2008), Ref. (Pseudogap2, 2009), and Ref. (Levin2, 2010) did not. The authors extend the effective field theory approach to Fermi liquids developed by Benfatto (1990), Shankar (1994), and Polchinski (1992), and the coset construction for spontaneously broken space-time symmetries of Ivanov (1971), Volkov (1973), and Ogievetsky (1974). The result rules out Fermi liquid behavior at unitarity in three dimensions and provides an exact beta function for the coupling function in the unitary limit, which can guide future theoretical and experimental work on the nature of the normal state in strongly interacting Fermi gases.
