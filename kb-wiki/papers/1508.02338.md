# Probing Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=2760$ GeV with spectators

> There is event by event geometric as well as quantum fluctuations in the initial condition of heavy-ion collisions. The standard technique of analysing heavy-ion collisions in bins of centrality obtained from final state multiplicity averages out the various initial configurations and thus restricts the study to only a limited range of initial conditions. In this paper, we propose an additional binning in terms of total spectator neutrons in an event. This offers us a key control parameter to probe events with broader range of initial conditions providing us an opportunity to peep into events with rarer initial conditions which otherwise get masked when analysed by centrality binning alone. We find that the inclusion of spectator binning allows one to vary $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ independently. We observe that the standard scaling relation between $\displaystyle{v_2/\varepsilon_2}$ and $\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN_{\text{ch}}}{dη}$ exhibited by centrality bins is broken by the spectator neutron bins. However, the acoustic scaling relation between $\displaystyle{\ln\left( v_n/\varepsilon_n\right)}$ and transverse system size holds for both centrality as well as spectator bins for central to mid-central collisions. The introduction of the spectator binning allows us to tune over a wide range viscosity driven effects for events with varying initial states but similar final state multiplicity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that binning Pb+Pb collision events by total spectator neutron number (L+R) in addition to standard centrality binning (based on final-state charged-particle multiplicity) allows access to rarer initial-state configurations and enables independent variation of the initial eccentricities ε₂ and ε₃, which are otherwise averaged out within a single centrality bin.

## Method

The authors simulated ~2×10⁶ Pb+Pb events at √s_NN = 2760 GeV using the AMPT model (Default version), which uses HIJING initial conditions followed by parton cascade, Lund string fragmentation, and a hadronic afterburner. Events were first binned by centrality (charged-particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη), then further subdivided by the total number of spectator neutrons (L+R) as measured by zero-degree calorimeters. The initial-state geometry (participant eccentricities ε₂, ε₃, transverse sizes σ_x, σ_y, and number of binary collisions N_coll) and final-state anisotropic flow coefficients (v₂, v₃) were computed for each combined bin.

## Key numbers

- The L+R distribution for the 10–15% centrality bin shows a peak around 85–95 spectator neutrons, with event counts dropping by a factor of 5 when L+R shifts by ~20.
- For the 20–25% centrality bin, v₂ varies by ~130% across different L+R bins, while v₃ changes by only ~10%.
- In AMPT, the average transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ shows ~10–15% variation across L+R bins within a given dN_ch/dη bin; in HIJING, ⟨p_T⟩ is single-valued with N_part and shows no such variation.
- The acoustic scaling relation ln(v_n/ε_n) vs. 1/Λ_T holds approximately for central to mid-central collisions (0–40% centrality) and their L+R bins, with a mild breaking of the scaling.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using spectator asymmetry to trigger specific collision configurations in deformed U+U collisions (Chatterjee 2014, Bairathi 2015) and the correlation between forward-backward asymmetry and spectator asymmetry in Pb+Pb (Jia 2015). It complements event-shape engineering techniques using the flow vector q₂ (Schukraft 2012, Huo 2013) by providing a direct initial-state observable (spectator neutrons) rather than a final-state proxy. The method enables more precise extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s and disentanglement of non-linear mode couplings (e.g., v₂–v₄, v₂,v₃–v₅) in future experimental data.
