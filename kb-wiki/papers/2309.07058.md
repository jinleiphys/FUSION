# QCD phase diagram and equation of state in background electric fields

> The phase diagram and the equation of state of QCD is investigated in the presence of weak background electric fields by means of continuum extrapolated lattice simulations. The complex action problem at nonzero electric field is circumvented by a novel Taylor expansion, enabling the determination of the linear response of the thermal QCD medium to constant electric fields -- in contrast to simulations at imaginary electric fields, which, as we demonstrate, involve an infrared singularity. Besides the electric susceptibility of QCD matter, we determine the dependence of the Polyakov loop on the field strength to leading order. Our results indicate a plasma-type behavior with a negative susceptibility at all temperatures, as well as an increase in the transition temperature as the electric field grows.

# QCD phase diagram and equation of state in background electric fields

## Key claim
The authors present the first lattice QCD determination of the equation of state and phase diagram in weak background electric fields, finding a negative electric susceptibility at all temperatures and an increase in the transition temperature as the electric field grows. This result contradicts existing model predictions (e.g., Tavares (2019)) that had found the opposite trend, and the authors caution that their Taylor expansion method corresponds to the Weldon-type regularization appropriate for finite systems, not the Schwinger propagator approach used in those models.

## Method
The complex action problem at nonzero electric field is circumvented by a leading-order Taylor expansion of the partition function in the electric field strength, enabling measurement of the linear response of thermal QCD matter. The authors perform continuum-extrapolated lattice simulations using rooted staggered fermions with physical quark masses on four lattice spacings (Nt = 6, 8, 10, 12), measuring the zero-momentum projected density-density correlator and its second moment to extract the electric susceptibility, and the correlator of the Polyakov loop with the same density operator to determine the field-dependence of the Polyakov loop. The equilibrium charge profile induced by the electric field is taken into account via a local canonical ensemble, and additive renormalization of the susceptibility is performed by subtracting the zero-temperature contribution.

## Key numbers
- Electric susceptibility curvature of the transition line: κ_E = 0.37(9) GeV^{-3}
- The electric susceptibility ξ is negative at all temperatures, indicating plasma-type behavior with electric permittivity below unity
- The QED renormalization scale determined at T=0 is μ_QED = 115(6) MeV
- The continuum extrapolation of T_c(E) is stable for eE ≲ 0.3 GeV^2
- The high-temperature limit for non-interacting quarks gives ξ^free → -Σ_f (q_f/e)^2 * (N_c/(12π^2)) * [log(T^2π^2/m^2) - 2γ_E - 1] as T → ∞

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD methodology previously developed for background magnetic fields (Bali (2014), Bali (2020)) to the electric field case, using a Taylor expansion approach analogous to that for chemical potentials. The results contradict predictions from the PNJL model (Tavares (2019)) which used the Schwinger propagator and found the opposite phase diagram behavior; the authors note that the PNJL model also fails to correctly describe the phase diagram at B>0 (Andersen (2014)). The study enables future investigations of the QCD phase diagram in electromagnetic fields relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors, and demonstrates that simulations with imaginary electric fields (Detmold (2009), Yang (2022)) cannot be used to directly evaluate the electric susceptibility due to a singular behavior at E=0.
