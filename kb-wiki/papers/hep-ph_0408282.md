# Temperature Dependence of Gluon and Ghost Propagators in Landau-Gauge Yang-Mills Theory below the Phase Transition

> The Dyson-Schwinger equations of Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory for the gluon and ghost propagators are investigated. Numerical results are obtained within a truncation scheme which has proven to be successful at vanishing temperature. For temperatures up to 250 MeV we find only minor quantitative changes in the infrared behaviour of the gluon and ghost propagators. The effective action calculated from these propagators is temperature-independent within the numerical uncertainty.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations for gluon and ghost propagators in Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory show only minor quantitative changes in their infrared behavior for temperatures up to 250 MeV, and the effective action calculated from these propagators is temperature-independent within numerical uncertainty, suggesting that gluons and ghosts do not contribute to pressure or entropy below the phase transition.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory at non-vanishing temperature using the imaginary-time formalism with periodic boundary conditions (Matsubara frequencies). They employed a truncation scheme that has been tested at zero temperature, replacing the full ghost-gluon vertex by the tree-level one and using a constructed ansatz for the three-gluon vertex that provides correct one-loop ultraviolet behavior. The equations were solved on a space-time torus with a 24^3 momentum grid and an ultraviolet cutoff of 4.7 GeV, using a Newton-Raphson iteration starting from zero-temperature results.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: up to 250 MeV for the main results, with additional solutions up to temperatures allowed by the UV cutoff where 2πT ≈ Λ
- Critical temperature for pure SU(3) Yang-Mills phase transition: Tc ≈ 270 MeV (from lattice calculations by Karsch (2003))
- Spatial volume of the torus: approximately 6 fm^3 (for the 24^3 grid with UV cutoff 4.7 GeV)
- Fit to cutoff dependence of the effective action gave a constant term c = (9.8 ± 1.3) × 10^(-10) GeV^4, corresponding to c^(1/4) ≈ 5.6 MeV
- The difference ΔZ = Z_0 - Z_m is of the order of a few percent in the intermediate momentum region compared to the sum Z

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to finite temperature below the deconfinement phase transition, building on zero-temperature studies by von Smekal (1997), Fischer (2002), and others that established the infrared behavior of gluon and ghost propagators as related to confinement. The results are consistent with lattice calculations by Bonnet (2001) and Bowman (2004) at zero temperature, and complement investigations of the high-temperature limit by Maas (2004). The finding that the effective action is temperature-independent up to 250 MeV suggests that gluons and ghosts do not contribute to thermodynamic quantities in the confined phase, which could indicate a first-order phase transition if confirmed by other methods.
