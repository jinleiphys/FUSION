# Nuclear and Hybrid Equations of State in Light of the Low-Mass Compact Star in HESS J1731-347

> We sample over $500$ relativistic mean-field theories constrained by chiral effective field theory and properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and test them against known stellar structure constraints. This includes a recent mass and radius measurement of a compact object in supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, with an unusually low mass of $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}\,M_{\odot}$ and a compact radius of $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km. We show that none of the sampled nuclear models meet all constraints at the $68\,\%$ credibility level, but that hybrid equations of state with a quark matter inner core and nuclear outer core easily can. This indicates a tension between astrophysical constraints and low-energy nuclear theory.

## Key claim

None of the sampled nuclear models (relativistic mean-field theories) can simultaneously satisfy all astrophysical constraints at the 68% credibility level, but hybrid equations of state with a quark matter inner core and nuclear outer core can easily do so, indicating a tension between astrophysical constraints and low-energy nuclear theory.

## Method

The authors sampled over 500 relativistic mean-field theories (RMFTs) constrained by chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) for pure neutron matter between 0.5 and 1.5 times saturation density and by known properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius curves and tested these against multiple astrophysical constraints including the low-mass compact object in HESS J1731-347. For hybrid equations of state, they combined a soft nuclear RMFT (QMC-Soft) with a constant speed of sound model for quark matter, varying the transition density, energy density jump, and quark matter speed of sound.

## Key numbers

* Mass of the compact object in HESS J1731-347: M = 0.77 (+0.20 / -0.17) solar masses
* Radius of the compact object in HESS J1731-347: R = 10.4 (+0.86 / -0.78) km
* Saturation density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
* QMC-Soft nuclear model predicts R = 11.47 km at M = 0.77 solar masses
* Successful hybrid models use transition densities from n_tr = 2 n0 to n_tr = 2.4 n0
* Successful hybrid models use energy density jumps from Delta_epsilon/epsilon_tr = 0.004 to 0.151
* Speed of sound squared in quark matter fixed to c_Q^2 = 0.48 for the main analysis
* Lowest speed of sound squared sustaining a two-solar-mass star: c_Q^2 approximately 0.4
* Dimensionless tidal deformabilities at 1.4 solar masses for successful hybrid models: Lambda = 247 to 391

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous sampling procedure for RMFTs (Alford 2022, Alford 2023) by incorporating the 2022 mass-radius measurement of the central compact object in HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022). The study contrasts with proposals of pure strange quark matter stars (Doroshenko 2022, Di Clemente 2022, Oikonomou 2023, Horvath 2023) by instead investigating hybrid stars with a nuclear outer core and quark matter inner core. The results motivate further improvement of low-energy nuclear theory predictions and astrophysical observations, and show that neglecting the possibility of quark matter cores could lead to falsely excluding soft nuclear models.
