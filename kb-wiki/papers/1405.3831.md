# Prediction of a missing higher charmonium around 4.26 GeV in $J/ψ$ family

> Inspired by the similarity of mass gaps of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ families, the prediction of missing higher charmonium with mass $4263$ MeV and very narrow width is made. In addition, the properties of two charmonium-like states, $X(3940)$ and $X(4160)$, and charmonium $ψ(4415)$ are discussed. Here, $X(3940)$ as $η_c(3S)$ is established while the explanation of $X(4160)$ to be $η_c(4S)$ is fully excluded and $η_c(4S)$ is typically a very narrow state. These predictions can be accessible at BESIII, Belle and BelleII in near future.

# Prediction of a missing higher charmonium around 4.26 GeV in J/ψ family

## Key claim
The authors predict a missing charmonium state ψ(4S) with mass around 4263 MeV and a very narrow total width of approximately 6 MeV, based on the similarity of mass gaps between the J/ψ and Υ families. The prediction is conditional on the assumption that the mass gap relation between n=3 and n=4 states holds for both families, and the state is argued to have escaped detection due to its narrow width making it difficult to identify in open-charm decay analyses and R-value scans.

## Method
The authors apply the quark pair creation (QPC) model to calculate OZI-allowed two-body strong decay widths of charmonia, using simple harmonic oscillator wave functions with the parameter R taken from Godfrey (1986) and the dimensionless pair-creation strength γ fitted to experimental data. They compare mass gaps between the J/ψ and Υ families to predict the ψ(4S) mass, then compute partial and total decay widths for ψ(4S) decaying into D(*)\bar{D}(*), D_s(*)\bar{D}_s(*), and other open-charm channels. The same approach is applied to η_c(3S), η_c(4S), and ψ(4415) as ψ(5S).

## Key numbers
- Predicted mass of missing ψ(4S): 4263 MeV, obtained by adding the mass gap between Υ(4S) and Υ(3S) to the mass of ψ(3S) (ψ(4040))
- Total decay width of predicted ψ(4S): approximately 6 MeV, very narrow compared to other higher charmonia
- ψ(4040) (ψ(3S)) width: 80±10 MeV; ψ(4160) width: 103±8 MeV; ψ(4415) width: 62±20 MeV (from PDG)
- ψ(3770) width: 27.2 MeV, given as comparison for a state just 43 MeV above D\bar{D} threshold
- Y(4260) width: 95±14 MeV; Y(4360) width: 74±18 MeV (PDG average values)
- Mass of η_c(3S) estimated as approximately 3992 MeV, close to X(3940) mass of 3942^{+7}_{-6}±6 MeV
- X(3940) width: 37^{+26}_{-15}±8 MeV
- X(4160) width: 139^{+111}_{-61}±21 MeV, which excludes η_c(4S) assignment
- Narrow structure in e+e- → h_c(1P)π+π- data: mass 4216±18 MeV, width 39±22 MeV (Yuan 2013)
- ψ(4415) as ψ(5S): total width overlaps PDG average (62±20 MeV) for R in range 2.86 to 3.21 GeV^{-1}
- Ratio R1 = Γ_{D\bar D}/Γ_{D*\bar D*} for ψ(4415): 0.004-0.06; R2 = Γ_{D*\bar D+H.c.}/Γ_{D*\bar D*}: 0.19-0.68 (for R=2.86-3.19 GeV^{-1})

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of the charmonium family by exploiting the empirical similarity between J/ψ and Υ mass spectra, following the observation that bottomonia with radial quantum numbers n=1-4 are well established. The prediction of ψ(4S) at 4263 MeV is consistent with earlier theoretical calculations using screening potentials by Li (2009) (mass 4273 MeV) and Dong (1994) (mass 4247 MeV). The authors distinguish their predicted narrow ψ(4S) from the broad charmonium-like states Y(4260) and Y(4360), which have been proposed as non-resonant interference effects by Chen (2010, 2011). The work also addresses the identification of X(3940) as η_c(3S) and excludes X(4160) as η_c(4S), while discussing ψ(4415) as a candidate for ψ(5S). The predictions are testable at BESIII, Belle, and BelleII, and the authors note a possible connection to a narrow structure near 4216 MeV observed in BESIII data analyzed by Yuan (2013).
