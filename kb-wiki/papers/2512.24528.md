# Geometric phase from encircling an exceptional point of a quantum resonance in the complex-scaling method

> Non-Hermitian operators are now routinely used to describe few-mode systems such as optical resonators and superconducting qubits, and exceptional points (EPs) are defective spectral singularities of such non-Hermitian operators. In contrast, the scattering-theoretic formulation of EP physics for unbounded Hamiltonians remains less settled. In this work, we formulate the geometric phase associated with encircling an EP when the underlying eigenstates are quantum resonances within a one-dimensional scattering model. To do this, we employ the complex-scaling method, where resonance poles of the S matrix are realized as discrete eigenvalues of the non-Hermitian dilated Hamiltonian, to construct situations in which resonant and scattering states coalesce into an EP in the complex energy plane, that is, the resonance pole is embedded into the continuum spectrum. We analyze the self-orthogonality in the vicinity of an EP, the Berry phase, and the Chern characteristic. Our results clarify how EP branch structure and geometric holonomy arise directly from resonance poles in scattering theory, thereby connecting non-Hermitian spectral topology with the traditional theory of quantum resonances.

## Key claim

The paper formulates the geometric phase (Berry phase) associated with encircling an exceptional point (EP) where a quantum resonance pole coalesces with the continuum spectrum in a one-dimensional scattering model, using the complex-scaling method (CSM) to treat resonances as discrete eigenstates. The hedge is that this EP is not introduced as a formal analogy but as the branch-point singularity of a resonance pole in a genuine scattering problem, which directly controls the monodromy and holonomy of the resonance eigenstate under parameter cycles.

## Method

The authors employ the complex-scaling method (CSM), performing a nonunitary coordinate rotation \(x \mapsto x e^{i\theta}\) to map the Hamiltonian to a non-Hermitian dilated operator \(H_\theta\), where resonance poles of the S-matrix become discrete eigenvalues. They use an exactly solvable one-dimensional model, the inverted Rosen–Morse (Pöschl–Teller) potential, and vary the potential strength parameter \(\lambda\) to construct situations where a resonance pole and a continuum state coalesce into an EP in the complex energy plane. To make the continuum spectrum tractable, they introduce a momentum-bin discretization of the complex-scaled continuum, normalizing the binned states with a Kronecker delta rather than a Dirac delta, and analyze the self-orthogonality, Berry phase, and Chern characteristic near the EP.

## Key numbers

- The resonance energy spectrum for the inverted Rosen–Morse potential under CSM is given by \(E^{\mathrm{R}}_n = \frac{\hbar^2\beta^2}{8m} \left[\sqrt{\frac{8m\lambda}{\beta^2 \hbar^2} - 1} - i(2n + 1) \right]^2\), where \(n\) is a nonnegative integer.
- The critical scaling angle \(\theta_n\) at which the \(n\)-th resonance pole contacts the complex-scaled continuum is \(\theta_n = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left(- \frac{\mathrm{Im}\,E^{\mathrm{R}}_n}{\mathrm{Re}\,E^{\mathrm{R}}_n}\right)\).
- The branch-point parameter value \(\lambda_{\mathrm{bp}}\) (the EP location) is \(\lambda_{\mathrm{bp}} = \lambda_0^+ = \frac{\beta^2 \hbar^2}{4m}\frac{1 + \cos 2\theta}{\sin^2 2\theta} = \frac{\beta^2 \hbar^2}{4m}\frac{1}{1-\cos2\theta}\).
- Near the EP, the energy follows Moiseyev's ansatz: \(E_{\mathrm{CSM}}^\theta(\lambda) = E_{\mathrm{bp}} + \alpha \sqrt{\lambda - \lambda_{\mathrm{bp}}} + O(\lambda)\), with the square-root dependence indicating branch order 2.

## Context

This work extends the study of exceptional points from finite-dimensional effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonians (routinely used for optical resonators and superconducting qubits, as reviewed by Ashida (2020)) to genuine infinite-dimensional scattering theory with unbounded Hamiltonians. It connects the older functional-analytic machinery of the complex-scaling method (Aguilar, Balslev, and Combes, 1971) and Gamow states with the newer language of non-Hermitian spectral topology, showing how EP branch structure and geometric holonomy arise directly from resonance poles. The results enable future investigation of how such holonomy might be encoded in scattering phase shifts under quasistatic parameter cycles, potentially informing non-Hermitian descriptions of scattering and decay in quantum field theories.
