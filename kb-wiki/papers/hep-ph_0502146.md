# Gapless Hartree-Fock Resummation Scheme for the O(N) Model

> A modified selfconsistent Hartree-Fock approximation to the lambda*phi^4 theory with spontaneously broken O(N) symmetry is proposed. It preserves all the desirable features, like conservation laws and thermodynamic consistency, of the selfconsistent Dyson scheme generated from a 2PI functional, also known as the Phi-derivable scheme, while simultaneously respecting the Nambu-Goldstone theorem in the chiral-symmetry broken phase. Various approximate resummation schemes are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified Hartree-Fock approximation for the O(N) model that is both Phi-derivable (ensuring conservation laws and thermodynamic consistency) and simultaneously respects the Nambu-Goldstone theorem in the spontaneously broken symmetry phase, a condition that standard Phi-derivable Hartree-Fock approximations fail to meet.

## Method

The authors introduce a phenomenological symmetry-restoring correction term Delta-Phi to the standard two-particle-irreducible (2PI) Phi functional of the Hartree-Fock approximation. This correction, which depends only on the Green functions and not on the classical field, is constructed to produce the required RPA correction to the pion self-energy at zero four-momentum. The resulting gapless Hartree-Fock (gHF) scheme is then solved self-consistently through the coupled mean-field and Schwinger-Dyson equations derived from the modified Phi functional, using a naive renormalization where divergent tadpole contributions are simply omitted.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum pion mass M_pi(T=0) = 139 MeV, sigma mass M_sigma(T=0) = 600 MeV, and pion decay constant phi_0 = f_pi = 93 MeV, used as input to fix the parameters m^2 and lambda for the case N=4.
* The external field H is set to H = m_pi^2 * f_pi, the same as at tree level, to make the vacuum equations consistent.
* The correction term Delta-Phi = -(N-1)lambda/(2N) * (Q_pi - Q_sigma)^2, where Q_a are the finite-temperature tadpole integrals, produces the required RPA correction Delta-Sigma_RPA_pi(k=0) = -(2lambda/N)(Q_pi - Q_sigma) to the pion self-energy.

## Context

This work extends the Phi-derivable (or CJT) formalism of Baym (1962) and Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974) by addressing a known deficiency: standard Phi-derivable approximations violate the Nambu-Goldstone theorem in the spontaneously broken phase, as demonstrated by Baym and Grinstein (1977) and others. The paper builds on earlier attempts to reconcile conservation laws with the Goldstone theorem, such as the gapless approximations of Hohenberg and Martin (1965) and the Baym-Grinstein modified Hartree-Fock scheme, but achieves this within a fully self-consistent Phi-derivable framework. The approach enables consistent dynamical and thermodynamic treatments of systems with spontaneously broken O(N) symmetry, such as chiral phase transitions in nuclear matter or Bose-Einstein condensates, without the need for supplementary Bethe-Salpeter or RPA calculations that break self-consistency.
