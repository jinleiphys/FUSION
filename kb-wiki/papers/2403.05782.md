# Symmetric cumulant $sc_{2,4} \left \{ 4 \right \}$ and asymmetric cumulant $ac_{2} \left \{ 3 \right \}$ from transverse momentum conservation and flow

> Multiparticle cumulants method can be used to reveal long-range collectivity in small and large colliding systems. The four-particle symmetric cumulant $sc_{2,4} \left \{ 4 \right \}$, three-particle asymmetric cumulant $ac_{2} \left \{ 3 \right \}$, and the normalized cumulants $nsc_{2,4} \left \{ 4 \right \}$ and $nac_{2} \left \{ 3 \right \}$ from the transverse momentum conservation and flow are calculated. The interplay between the two effects is also investigated. Our results are in a good agreement with the recent ATLAS measurements of multiparticle azimuthal correlations with the subevent cumulant method, which provides insight into the origin of collective flow in small systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the four-particle symmetric cumulant \(sc_{2,4}\{4\}\) and three-particle asymmetric cumulant \(ac_{2}\{3\}\) measured in small systems can be explained by a combination of transverse momentum conservation (dominant at low multiplicity) and collective flow (dominant at high multiplicity), without requiring exotic initial-state mechanisms; the agreement with ATLAS data holds specifically when the experimental charged-particle multiplicity is scaled by a factor of 1.5 to account for the total particle number under transverse momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for \(sc_{2,4}\{4\}\), \(ac_{2}\{3\}\), and their normalized versions \(nsc_{2,4}\{4\}\) and \(nac_{2}\{3\}\) by expanding the multiparticle probability distribution with imposed transverse momentum conservation to leading order in \(1/N\), assuming equal transverse momenta for all particles. They then incorporated collective flow by including Fourier coefficients \(v_2\), \(v_3\), \(v_4\) and event-plane correlations, keeping terms up to \(v_n^4\) for the symmetric cumulant and \(v_n^3\) for the asymmetric cumulant. The results were evaluated as functions of particle number \(N\) and transverse momentum \(p\), and compared to ATLAS data from p+p collisions at 13 TeV using subevent cumulant methods.

## Key numbers

* \(sc_{2,4}\{4\}\) from transverse momentum conservation alone scales as \(\frac{5p^{12}}{16(N-4)^6\langle p^2\rangle_F^6} - \frac{p^{12}}{48(N-2)^6\langle p^2\rangle_F^6}\) for equal transverse momenta \(p\) and \(\langle p^2\rangle_F = 0.25\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* \(ac_{2}\{3\}\) from transverse momentum conservation alone scales as \(\frac{p^8}{4(N-3)^2\langle p^2\rangle_F^4}\) for equal transverse momenta \(p\) and \(\langle p^2\rangle_F = 0.25\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* Flow parameters used in the combined calculation: \(v_2 = 0.08\), \(v_3 = 0.0175\), \(v_4 = 0.08^2\), \(v_{2F} = 0.025\), \(\langle p^2\rangle_F = 0.5^2\ \text{GeV}^2\), with event-plane correlations \(\cos(4(\Psi_4-\Psi_2)) = 0.8\) and \(\cos(2\Psi_2 - 6\Psi_3 + 4\Psi_4) = -0.15\).
* The normalized cumulant \(nsc_{2,4}\{4\}\) approaches approximately 2.0 at very large \(N\), reflecting the hydrodynamic correlation between \(v_2\) and \(v_4\).
* The normalized cumulant \(nac_{2}\{3\}\) approaches approximately 1 at large \(N\), indicating convergence of event planes \(\Psi_2\) and \(\Psi_4\) in the same direction.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Bzdak (2010) and others on transverse momentum conservation effects in multiparticle correlations, and directly addresses the ATLAS measurements (2023) of \(sc_{2,4}\{4\}\) and \(ac_{2}\{3\}\) in p+p collisions at 13 TeV using subevent cumulant methods. The results support the interpretation that collectivity in small systems arises from a combination of transverse momentum conservation at low multiplicity and hydrodynamic flow at high multiplicity, consistent with earlier PYTHIA and HIJING studies by the same group (2022). The approach enables future calculations of higher-order symmetric cumulants \(sc_{k,l,m}\{6\}\) to understand coupling between different flow harmonics in small systems.
