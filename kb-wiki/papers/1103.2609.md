# Conflicting coupling of the unpaired nucleons and the structure of collective bands in odd-odd nuclei

> Conflicting coupling of unpaired nucleons in the odd-odd nuclei is discussed. A very simple explanation is suggested for the damping of the energy spacing of the lowest levels in the rotational bands in the odd-odd nuclei in the case of a "conflicting" coupling of the odd proton and neutron comparative to those of the bands based on the state of the strongly-coupled particle in the neighbouring odd nuclei entering the "conflicting" configuration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reduction of low-lying energy intervals in rotational bands of odd-odd nuclei with "conflicting" coupling (where one odd nucleon is strongly coupled to the deformed core and the other is decoupled) can be explained by a geometric factor arising from the different orientation of the total angular momentum in the odd-odd nucleus compared to the neighboring odd nucleus, rather than requiring detailed treatment of the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors use the quasiclassical limit of the "axial rotor + two quasiparticles" model, treating the rotational Hamiltonian with Coriolis interaction between the odd nucleons and the rotating core. They derive an expression relating the moment of inertia of the odd-odd nucleus to that of the neighboring odd nucleus via a geometric factor involving the sine of the angle between the total angular momentum and the symmetry axis. The model is tested by comparing calculated energy intervals with experimental data from in-beam gamma-spectroscopy for several isotopic chains.

- Ratio of energy intervals (12⁻–11⁻)/(17/2⁺–15/2⁺) in iodine isotopes: 0.971 for ¹¹⁶I, 0.995 for ¹¹⁸I, 1.060 for ¹²⁰I (experimental, Table 1)
- The band head spin for extreme "conflicting" coupling is approximately I_head = √(j_n² + j_p²)
- The alignment α of the decoupled particle ranges from 5.5 to 4.9 across the iodine isotopes studied

## Context

This work extends the concept of "conflicting" coupling introduced by Flaum and Cline (1976) and earlier work by Levon (1992) on enhanced M1 transitions in odd-odd nuclei. The paper provides a simple geometric explanation for the damping of low-lying energy spacings observed in odd-odd nuclei such as ¹⁰²,¹⁰⁴,¹⁰⁶Ag, ¹⁰⁶,¹⁰⁸,¹¹⁰In, ¹¹²–¹²⁰Sb, ¹¹⁶–¹²²I, and ¹²⁰,¹²²Cs (with decoupled h₁₁/₂ neutron and strongly coupled g₉/₂ proton), as well as nuclei in the A~170 and A~200 regions. The approach enables systematic comparison of rotational bands across odd-odd and odd nuclei without requiring detailed knowledge of the residual n-p interaction, and the authors note that the residual interaction gives a signature-dependent contribution with the opposite phase needed to explain staggering, supporting their neglect of it.
