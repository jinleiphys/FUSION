# Heavy quark dynamics in the QGP: R_AA and v_2 from RHIC to LHC

> We study the stochastic dynamics of c and b quarks in the hot plasma produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC, providing results for the nuclear modification factor R_AA and the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 of the single-electron spectra arising from their semi-leptonic decays. The initial QQbar pairs are generated using the POWHEG code, implementing pQCD at NLO. For the propagation in the plasma we develop a relativistic Langevin equation (solved in a medium described by hydrodynamics) whose transport coefficients are evaluated through a first-principle calculation. Finally, at T_c, the heavy quarks are made hadronize and decay into electrons: the resulting spectra are then compared with RHIC results. Predictions for LHC are also attempted.

# Heavy quark dynamics in the QGP: R_AA and v_2 from RHIC to LHC

## Key claim

The stochastic dynamics of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, modeled via a relativistic Langevin equation with transport coefficients computed from first principles (combining hard pQCD and soft HTL-resummed contributions), can reproduce the measured nuclear modification factor R_AA of non-photonic electrons at RHIC, but only when the strong coupling is evaluated at an intermediate scale of 1.5πT; the agreement at lower transverse momentum remains imperfect and might require hadronization via coalescence.

## Method

The authors generated initial ccbar and bbbar pairs using the POWHEG code at NLO pQCD with CTEQ6M PDFs, including nuclear PDF effects via EPS09 for nucleus-nucleus collisions. Heavy quark propagation through the QGP was modeled with a relativistic Langevin equation solved in a hydrodynamically expanding medium (using both ideal and viscous hydro codes), with transport coefficients κ_T and κ_L computed from a first-principles calculation that separates hard scatterings (pQCD) and soft scatterings (HTL resummation) at an intermediate cutoff |t|* ~ m_D^2. At T_c, heavy quarks were hadronized using Peterson fragmentation functions and decayed into electrons via PYTHIA, with the resulting single-electron spectra compared to PHENIX data at RHIC and used for predictions at LHC.

## Key numbers

- Initial sample of 45 × 10^6 ccbar and bbbar pairs generated for each explored case
- At RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): σ_ccbar = 254.14 μb (p-p), 236.11 μb (Au-Au); σ_bbar = 1.769 μb (p-p), 2.033 μb (Au-Au)
- At LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV): σ_ccbar = 3.0146 mb (p-p), 2.2877 mb (Pb-Pb); σ_bbar = 0.1872 mb (p-p), 0.1686 mb (Pb-Pb)
- RHIC hydro initialization: ideal hydro with τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c, s_0 = 110 fm^-3, T_0 = 357 MeV; viscous hydro with τ_0 = 1.0 fm/c, s_0 = 83.8 fm^-3, T_0 = 333 MeV
- LHC hydro initialization: viscous hydro with τ_0 = 0.1 fm/c, s_0 = 1840 fm^-3, T_0 = 854 MeV; viscous hydro with τ_0 = 1.0 fm/c, s_0 = 184 fm^-3, T_0 = 420 MeV
- Best agreement with PHENIX R_AA data at high p_T (≥ 4 GeV/c) obtained with α_s evaluated at scale 1.5πT, giving α_s = 0.32 at T = 300 MeV
- Minimum bias impact parameter: b = 8.44 fm for Au-Au at RHIC, b = 8.58 fm for Pb-Pb at LHC

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based approach to heavy quark energy loss developed by the same authors (Alberico et al., Refs. lange_hot and lange_pra) by providing a first-principles calculation of transport coefficients rather than using phenomenological parameters. It contrasts with earlier studies by Hira, Rapp, Tea, Aic, and Das (all cited in the paper) that employed different modeling of heavy quark interactions with the medium. The results enable predictions for LHC heavy-ion collisions and highlight the need for including coalescence hadronization (as suggested by Rapp, cited in the paper) to improve agreement with RHIC data at low p_T, while also demonstrating that the choice of hydrodynamical scenario (ideal vs. viscous) has only a small effect on the final observables.
