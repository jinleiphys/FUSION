# Liquid-like phases of π^+π^- matter

> To give a common theoretical description of liquid phases of the charged pion matter in a wide temperature interval, the relativistic quantum $ϕ^6$ type model is considered. The liquid states of pion condensate and hot pion matter are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic quantum φ⁶ model can describe both a liquid phase of pion condensate at zero temperature and a hot pion liquid at temperatures above the condensation temperature, provided the model parameter λ is chosen in the range 0.25 < λ < 0.268 to produce a first-order phase transition; the hot liquid phase appears with increasing temperature, contrary to molecular physics, and is predicted to exist at fireball temperatures around 75 MeV if the fitted coupling g ≈ 8 MeV is realized in nature.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic quantum φ⁶ Lagrangian density with attractive φ⁴ and repulsive φ⁶ terms, applying the mean-field approximation in two regimes: at T=0, the pion field operator is replaced by a classical complex field φ to describe the condensate, and the static quasi one-dimensional "bubble" solution of the relativistic Gross-Pitaevskii equation is obtained analytically; for T > T_cond, the field is treated as quantum fluctuations χ, and the thermodynamic potential is computed in the mean-field approximation, leading to gap equations for the scalar density σ and effective mass m_*. The model parameters λ and g are fixed by requiring a first-order phase transition in the condensate and by fitting to dilepton spectra from the DLS Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m = 140 MeV (used throughout the model).
- Critical temperature of the phase transition in hot pion matter is about 42.3 MeV and less than 136 MeV (the latter predicted in prior work cited as KGSG).
- Fireball temperature extracted from DLS dilepton spectra is T = 75 MeV.
- Fitted coupling constant g ≈ 8 MeV from equation F_+(s,T,0)=0 at chemical equilibrium (μ=0).
- Model parameter λ = 0.26 is chosen to achieve liquid-gas phase transition in condensate; the first-order PT occurs for 0.25 < λ < 0.268.
- At λ = 0.25, the maximum charge is limited by q ≈ 5.3 (in dimensionless units Q/g²).
- The pion annihilation threshold from the fit is approximately 2ms = 260 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic φ⁶ model used in molecular physics (cited as GFTC, KT) to a relativistic quantum φ⁶ model for charged pion matter, aiming to describe liquid phases relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior chiral perturbation theory approaches (Skyrme, Weinberg, Gasser-Leutwyler) and earlier predictions of a hot pion liquid (KGSG). The results enable further investigation of pion condensate signatures in heavy-ion collisions and the role of pion liquid in nucleonic equation of state softening, with the authors noting that calculations with non-vanishing chemical potential will be published elsewhere.
