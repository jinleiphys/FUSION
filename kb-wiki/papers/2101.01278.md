# Fast flavor oscillations in dense neutrino media with collisions

> We investigate the impact of the nonzero neutrino splitting and elastic neutrino-nucleon collisions on fast neutrino oscillations. Our calculations confirm that a small neutrino mass splitting and the neutrino mass hierarchy have very little effect on fast oscillation waves. We also demonstrate explicitly that fast oscillations remain largely unaffected for the time/distance scales that are much smaller than the neutrino mean free path but are damped on larger scales. This damping originates from both the direct modification of the dispersion relation of the oscillation waves in the neutrino medium and the flattening of the neutrino angular distributions over time. Our work suggests that fast neutrino oscillation waves produced near the neutrino sphere can propagate essentially unimpeded which may have ramifications in various aspects of the supernova physics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that elastic neutrino-nucleon collisions damp fast flavor oscillations on time or distance scales larger than the neutrino mean free path, but fast oscillation waves produced near the neutrino sphere can propagate essentially unimpeded on shorter scales; the damping arises from both direct modification of the dispersion relation and the flattening of the neutrino angular distributions over time.

## Method

The authors solve the quantum kinetic equation for a homogeneous, axially symmetric neutrino gas in the mean-field limit, including only isoenergetic elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering. They perform a linear stability analysis by computing the dispersion relation of collective oscillation modes as a function of time, and verify the analytic predictions with numerical simulations in both the linear and nonlinear regimes using a periodic spatial box with 64 angular bins and up to 48000 spatial bins.

## Key numbers

- Collision strength parameters: $\kappa_0 = 2 \times 10^{-3}$ (strong collision scenario) and $\kappa_0 = 2 \times 10^{-4}$ (weak collision scenario), both in units where $\mu = 1$
- Effective vacuum oscillation frequency: $\omega = \pm 10^{-5}$ (plus for normal hierarchy, minus for inverted hierarchy)
- Ratio of collision constants: $\kappa_1 / \kappa_0 \approx 0.5$ for electron fraction $Y_e = 0.3$
- Simulation box sizes: $L = 1200$ (linear regime) and $L = 2400$ (nonlinear regime), corresponding to about 1 m for typical $\mu \sim 10^5$ km$^{-1}$ on the proto-neutron star surface
- Initial perturbation amplitude: $\epsilon_0 = 10^{-7}$ (linear regime) and $\epsilon_0 = 0.1$ (nonlinear regime)
- Number of angle bins: 64 (all numerical calculations); dispersion relation calculations also used 256 bins for comparison

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier collisionless study (Martin 2019) which showed that fast oscillation waves can spontaneously appear in dense neutrino media and redistribute the electron lepton number. It also builds on the stability analysis framework introduced by Izaguirre (2016) and the treatment of neutrino-nucleon collisions by Cirigliano (2017). The results confirm that the zero mass-splitting approximation widely used in fast oscillation studies is valid when $|\omega|/\mu \ll 1$, addressing a question raised by Shalgar (2020). The finding that fast oscillations can propagate unimpeded near the neutrino sphere has implications for supernova dynamics, nucleosynthesis, and neutrino signals.
