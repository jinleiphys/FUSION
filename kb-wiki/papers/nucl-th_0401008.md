# Statistical and Dynamic Models of Charge Balance Functions

> Charge balance functions, which identify balancing particle-antiparticle pairs on a statistical basis, have been shown to be sensitive to whether hadronization is delayed by several fm/c in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Results from two classes of models are presented here, microscopic hadronic models and thermal models. The microscopic models give results which are contrary to recently published pi+pi- balance functions from the STAR collaboration, whereas the thermal model roughly reproduce the experimental results. This suggests that charge conservation is local at breakup, which is in line with expectations for a delayed hadronization. Predictions are also presented for balance functions binned as a function of Q_inv.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the narrowing of charge balance functions with increasing collision centrality observed by STAR is qualitatively consistent with a delayed hadronization scenario, but only if charge conservation is enforced locally within small spatial domains at breakup; the microscopic hadronic models (RQMD and HIJING/GROMIT) fail to reproduce this narrowing, instead showing a slight broadening.

## Method

The authors compare two classes of models against STAR's published $\pi^+\pi^-$ balance functions. First, they run microscopic hadronic simulations using RQMD and HIJING/GROMIT, which create hadrons early (within ~1 fm/c) via string fragmentation and then model rescattering via hadronic cascades. Second, they construct a canonical blast-wave model in which particles are generated from independent thermal domains of volume 64 fm$^3$ at chemical freeze-out temperature $T_\mu = 175$ MeV, then have momenta reassigned according to a blast-wave prescription with kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_k = 120$ MeV and maximum transverse flow velocity $0.7c$; inter-domain Coulomb and strong-interaction correlations are added using full Coulomb wave functions and phase-shift-modified wave functions for pions.

## Key numbers

- RQMD half-width for Au+Au central collisions (b = 0-3 fm): $1.56 \pm 0.11$ units of rapidity, compared to $1.48 \pm 0.11$ for pp collisions, showing a slight broadening rather than the ~15% narrowing seen in STAR data.
- HIJING half-width for Au+Au central (b = 0-3 fm): $1.14 \pm 0.11$ units of rapidity, essentially unchanged from the pp value of $1.18 \pm 0.02$.
- The thermal blast-wave model uses a chemical freeze-out temperature $T_\mu = 175$ MeV, a kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_k = 120$ MeV, a maximum transverse velocity of $0.7c$, a transverse radius of 13 fm, and a breakup time $\tau = 10$ fm/c.
- The domain volume for the canonical calculation is chosen as 64 fm$^3$, implying charge conservation enforced on a length scale of ~4 fm.

## Context

This work directly tests the STAR collaboration's (2003) measurement of $\pi^+\pi^-$ balance functions in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, which showed narrowing with centrality. The microscopic models (RQMD, HIJING) represent the standard string-fragmentation approach used in earlier work by Bass and Danielewicz (1999) and by the HIJING collaboration; their failure to reproduce the narrowing suggests that early hadronization is inconsistent with data. The thermal blast-wave model extends the successful blast-wave description of spectra (Retiere, 2003; Tomasik, 2003) by incorporating canonical charge conservation within domains, following earlier work on charge fluctuations by Jeon and Pratt (2002) and on balance function distortions by Pratt (2003). The results enable future comparisons with $K^+K^-$ and $p\bar{p}$ balance functions to further test the delayed-hadronization hypothesis.
