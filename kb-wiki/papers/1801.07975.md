# Removal Energies and Final State Interaction in Lepton Nucleus Scattering

> We investigate the binding energy parameters that should be used in modeling electron and neutrino scattering from nucleons bound in a nucleus within the framework of the impulse approximation. We discuss the relation between binding energy, missing energy, removal energy ($ε$), spectral functions and shell model energy levels and extract updated removal energy parameters from ee$^{\prime}$p spectral function data. We address the difference in parameters for scattering from bound protons and neutrons. We also use inclusive e-A data to extract an empirical parameter $U_{FSI}( (\vec q_3+\vec k)^2)$ to account for the interaction of final state nucleons (FSI) with the optical potential of the nucleus. Similarly we use $V_{eff}$ to account for the Coulomb potential of the nucleus. With three parameters $ε$, $U_{FSI}( (\vec q_3+\vec k)^2)$ and $V_{eff}$ we can describe the energy of final state electrons for all available electron QE scattering data. The use of the updated parameters in neutrino Monte Carlo generators reduces the systematic uncertainty in the combined removal energy (with FSI corrections) from $\pm$ 20 MeV to $\pm$ 5 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that using updated removal energy parameters (epsilon) and empirical final state interaction (FSI) and Coulomb corrections (U_FSI and V_eff) can reduce the systematic uncertainty in the combined removal energy for neutrino Monte Carlo generators from ±20 MeV to ±5 MeV, but this improvement is conditional on the impulse approximation framework and applies specifically to quasielastic (QE) scattering.

## Method

The authors extract updated average removal energy parameters (epsilon^P, epsilon^N) from exclusive ee'p spectral function measurements on several nuclei, using published Koltun sum rule data from Jefferson Lab Hall A and Saclay experiments. They also extract an empirical parameter U_FSI((q_3+k)^2) from inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data to account for final state nucleon interactions with the nuclear optical potential, and use V_eff to account for the Coulomb potential. These three parameters are then applied to describe the energy of final state electrons across all available electron QE scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Systematic uncertainty in combined removal energy (with FSI corrections) reduced from ±20 MeV to ±5 MeV
- For carbon-12: average missing energy ⟨E_m^P⟩ = 26.1 ± 0.4 MeV (JLab average) and 23.4 ± 0.5 MeV (Saclay)
- For oxygen-16: proton separation energy S^P = 12.1 MeV, neutron separation energy S^N = 15.7 MeV
- For iron-56: average missing energy ⟨E_m^P⟩ = 29.2 ± 1.1 MeV (JLab average)
- For lead-208: k_F^P = 0.275 GeV (from authors' own fits)
- A +20 MeV change in removal energy yields a change in Δm^2_32 of +0.03 × 10^{-3} eV^2 for T2K experiment parameters (L=295 km, E_nu ~ 0.6 GeV)
- A +20 MeV/c change in Fermi momentum k_F yields a much smaller change of +0.005 × 10^{-3} eV^2 in extracted Δm^2_32

## Context

This work extends the Smith-Moniz analysis of removal energies (Smith and Moniz, 1972) and the Moniz analysis of Fermi momenta from e-A data, updating parameters using modern spectral function measurements from Jefferson Lab (C12 collaboration) and Saclay. The paper addresses the need for improved binding energy parameters in neutrino Monte Carlo generators such as GENIE, NEUT, NUWRO, and GiBUU, which currently use a one-dimensional momentum distribution with an average removal energy parameter. The results enable more accurate reconstruction of neutrino energy in oscillation experiments like T2K, where the uncertainty in removal energy is identified as the largest contribution to the systematic error in Δm^2_32.
