# Delays Associated with Elementary Processes in Nuclear Reaction Simulations

> Scatterings, particularly those involving resonances, and other elementary processes do not happen instantaneously. In the context of semiclassical nuclear reaction simulations, we consider delays associated with an interaction for incident quantum wave-packets. As a consequence, we express delays associated with elementary processes in terms of elements of the scattering matrix and phase shifts for elastic scattering. We show that, to within the second order in density, the simulation must account for delays in scattering consistently with the mean field in order to properly model thermodynamic properties such as pressure and free-energy density. The delays associated with nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering in free space are analysed with their nontrivial energy dependence. Finally, an example of s-channel scattering of massless partons is studied, and scattering schemes in nuclear reaction simulations are investigated in the context of scattering delays.

## Key claim

The paper shows that semiclassical nuclear reaction simulations must account for both the forward-wave delay (consistent with a mean field) and the scattered-wave delay to properly model thermodynamic properties like pressure and free-energy density to second order in density; however, if one relaxes the requirement of correct single-particle energies, the mean field can be manipulated to absorb all delay effects, or vice versa.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for time delays of incident quantum wave-packets interacting with a scatterer, expressing delays in terms of elements of the scattering matrix and phase shifts for elastic scattering. They then demonstrate consistency with an ergodic constraint linking the extra time spent near a scatterer to the change in the density of states, and express the free-energy density and pressure in terms of these delays. Numerical calculations of spin-isospin averaged time delays are performed for nucleon-nucleon (NN) and pion-nucleon scattering using free-space amplitudes, and an example of s-channel scattering of massless partons is studied.

## Key numbers

* For nucleon-nucleon scattering near threshold, scattering lengths approach 20 fm, which limits the strict validity of the authors' considerations because the volume containing a single scatterer must be large compared to the scattering length.
* The correction to pressure from two-particle interactions is given by an integral over phase-space occupancies \( f = e^{(\mu - E)/T} \), involving the real part of the forward T-matrix and a term proportional to the product of cross section, relative velocity, and angular-dependent delay time \(\Delta \tau_s(\Omega)\).
* For scattering in only one partial wave, the extra time delay due to scattering is \( d\delta_\ell / dE \), not \( 2 d\delta_\ell / dE \) as one might naively guess from a partial wave reflecting off a potential.

## Context

This work extends prior semiclassical transport simulations of heavy-ion reactions (e.g., Yariv and Fraenkel 1979, Cugnon 1980, Wong 1982, Bertsch and Das Gupta 1988) by providing a formal connection between time delays of elementary processes and thermodynamic quantities. It addresses sensitivity of simulation results to space-time details of scattering noted by Halbert (1981), Gyulassy (1982), and others. The results enable consistent inclusion of delay effects in simulations up to arbitrary order in density, which is especially important for high-energy collisions at AGS (14 GeV/nucleon), SPS (200 GeV/nucleon), RHIC, and LHC where Lorentz dilation amplifies space-time effects.
