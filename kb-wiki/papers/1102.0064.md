# QCD Origin of Strong Magnetic Field in Compact Stars

> Some magnetic properties of quark matter and a microscopic origin of the strong magnetic field in compact stars are discussed; ferromagnetic order is discussed with the Fermi liquid theory and possible appearance of spin density wave is suggested within the NJL model. Implications of these magnetic properties are briefly discussed for compact stars.

# QCD Origin of Strong Magnetic Field in Compact Stars

## Key claim
Ferromagnetic order in quark matter, arising from spontaneous spin polarization via the one-gluon-exchange interaction, can produce magnetic fields of order 10^13–10^17 G in compact stars, but the screening effect from hard-dense-loop resummation qualitatively changes the magnetic susceptibility and can suppress spontaneous magnetization for large numbers of flavors.

## Method
The author applies Fermi-liquid theory to compute the magnetic susceptibility of quark matter using the one-gluon-exchange interaction, incorporating hard-dense-loop (HDL) resummation to handle infrared divergences from longitudinal and transverse gluons. For the non-uniform phase, the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used to study the dual chiral density wave (DCDW), where the chiral angle oscillates spatially, leading to a spin density wave (SDW) pattern. The analysis includes both zero-temperature and finite-temperature cases, with the latter revealing non-Fermi-liquid behavior from transverse gluon contributions.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field estimate from ferromagnetic quark matter in a solar-mass star with 10 km radius: O(10^13–10^17 G).
- Magnetic susceptibility at T=0 includes a screening term proportional to g^4 ln(g^2), with κ = m_D^2 / (2 k_F^2) and Debye mass m_D^2 = g^2 μ k_F / (2 π^2).
- QCD coupling constant used: α_s = g^2/(4π) = 2.2; strange quark mass m_s = 300 MeV from the MIT bag model.
- Curie temperature estimated at several tens of MeV, with specific points at k_F = 1.1 fm^−1 and k_F = 1.6 fm^−1 shown in the phase diagram.
- Non-Fermi-liquid term in magnetic susceptibility: (C_f g^2 u_F / 72) * (2 k_F^4 + k_F^2 m^2 + m^4) / (k_F^4 E_F^2) * T^2 ln(Λ/T).
- DCDW wavevector q satisfies q/2 > M in the stable phase, with q ~ 2 p_F near the critical end point.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of ferromagnetism in quark matter using the Bloch mechanism for itinerant electrons (Herring, 1966) and Fermi-liquid theory (Baym and Pethick, 1976). It builds on prior calculations of magnetic susceptibility in quark matter (Tatsumi, 2000) and the dual chiral density wave phase (Nakano and Tatsumi, 2004). The results enable future studies of magnetic field evolution in compact stars, including magnetars, and suggest connections to color superconductivity and neutrino emission processes in dense matter.
