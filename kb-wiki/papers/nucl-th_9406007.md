# Single-Particle Nondegeneracy and SU(3) Fermion Dynamical Symmetry

> It is shown that the SU(3) symmetry of the fermion dynamical symmetry model is essentially preserved even for highly nondegenerate spherical single-particle energies. The breaking of SU(3) symmetry by single-particle energy terms for either normal deformation or superdeformation occurs only through an indirect Pauli effect and is significant only when the spherical single-particle splitting within shells is artificially large relative to that observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The SU(3) symmetry of the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) is essentially preserved even for highly nondegenerate spherical single-particle energies; the breaking of SU(3) symmetry by single-particle energy terms occurs only through an indirect Pauli effect and is significant only when the spherical single-particle splitting within shells is artificially large relative to that observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors decomposed the single-particle energy operator in the FDSM k-i basis and computed its commutation with the SU(3) Casimir operator to identify which components can mix SU(3) irreducible representations in the u=0 (zero heritage) space. They then derived analytic expressions for the mixing matrix elements and used second-order perturbation theory to estimate upper limits on the energy shift caused by single-particle splitting, employing experimental single-particle energies from the 126-184 shell and scaling them by a factor ρ.

## Key numbers

- For the 126-184 shell with experimental single-particle splitting (ρ=1), the upper limit on the symmetry-breaking energy shift ranges from a fraction of an eV to several keV.
- The mixing matrix element Δ takes two extreme forms: Δ_< = -(0.0787/Ω₁) Σᵢ e¹ᵢ Ωᵢ [9-j symbol] and Δ_> = -(1.412/Ω₁) Σᵢ e¹ᵢ Ωᵢ [9-j symbol], where Ω₁ is the pair degeneracy of the shell responsible for SU(3) symmetry.
- The lower limit for the energy denominator in the perturbation expression is estimated as 2 MeV for normally deformed nuclei.
- Only when the single-particle splitting is scaled to be ten times larger than the experimental splitting (ρ=10) does the symmetry-breaking become significant enough to question perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the FDSM framework developed by Wu, Feng, Guidry, and collaborators in the late 1980s (Wu 1986, Wu 1987b), which built on the schematic fermion model of Ginocchio (1980). The paper initiates a systematic analysis of symmetry-breaking terms in the Sp(6) ⊃ SU(3) limit of the FDSM, complementing earlier work by Kirson (1984) on vibrational symmetries. The results enable quantitative shell model calculations in heavy nuclei using the FDSM truncation scheme, and suggest that the SU(3) wavefunctions remain essentially pure for both normal deformation and superdeformation, in contrast to other fermion SU(3) symmetries such as the Elliott model (Elliott 1958) and the pseudo-SU(3) model (Arima 1969, Hecht 1969), where single-particle effects are expected to have larger influence.
