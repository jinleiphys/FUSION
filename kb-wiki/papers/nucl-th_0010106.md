# Double-beta decay matrix elements for Ge-76

> Double-beta decay matrix elements (ME) for $^{76}Ge$ are calculated with different quasi random phase approximation (QRPA)-based methods. First, the ME for the two-neutrino mode are computed using two choices for the single particle (s.p.) basis: i) $2-4\hbarω$ full shells and ii) $3-4\hbarω$ full shells. When calculated with the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) and full-RQRPA their values are rather dependent on the size of the single particle basis used, while calculated with proton-neutron QRPA (pnQRPA) and second-QRPA approaches such a dependence was found to be small. The Ikeda sum rule was well fulfilled within pnQRPA for both choices of the s.p. basis and with a good approximation within second-QRPA, while the RQRPA and full-RQRPA methods give deviations up to 21%. Further, the ME for the neutrinoless mode are calculated with the pnQRPA, RQRPA and full-RQRPA methods. They all give close results for the calculation with the smaller basis (i), while for the larger basis (ii), the results differ significantly either from one method to another or within the same method. Finally, using the most recent experimental limit for the $0νββ$ decay half-life of $^{76}Ge$ a critical discussion on the upper limits for the neutrino mass parameter obtained with different theoretical approaches is given.

## Key claim

The matrix elements for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-beta decay of Ge-76 show a significant dependence on the size of the single-particle basis when calculated with renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) and full-RQRPA methods, while pnQRPA and second-QRPA methods exhibit much smaller basis-size dependence; however, the RQRPA-type methods violate the Ikeda sum rule by up to 21%, which may lead to systematically too small matrix elements and thus too large neutrino mass limits.

## Method

The authors calculated double-beta decay matrix elements for Ge-76 using four QRPA-based methods: pnQRPA, second-QRPA, RQRPA, and full-RQRPA, with two choices of single-particle basis (9 levels from pf and sdg shells with a Ca-40 core, and 12 levels from sd, pf, and sdg shells with an O-16 core). Single-particle energies were obtained from a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential, the residual interaction was a Brueckner G-matrix from Bonn-OBEP, and the quasiparticle energies and BCS occupation amplitudes were derived by solving HFB equations with and without proton-neutron pairing. The Ikeda sum rule was checked for each method, and the neutrinoless mode matrix elements were used with the experimental half-life limit to extract upper limits for the effective neutrino mass.

## Key numbers

- For the two-neutrino mode, the experimental half-life used for fitting was T^(2ν)_(1/2) = 1.55 × 10^21 yr from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment.
- The g_pp values that best fit the experimental two-neutrino matrix element were 0.94 for both pnQRPA calculations, and 0.99 (12-level basis) and 1.01 (9-level basis) for second-QRPA.
- For RQRPA and full-RQRPA, the two-neutrino matrix elements differed by up to 40-50% between the two basis choices at the crossing point with the experimental value.
- Deviations from the Ikeda sum rule were: pnQRPA 0.23-0.48%, second-QRPA 2.7-4.18%, RQRPA 19.66-21.34%, and full-RQRPA 16.91-17.68%.
- For the neutrinoless mode with the smaller (9-level) basis, all three methods gave matrix elements close to each other (3.9-4.1 in non-dimensional units).
- Using the experimental limit T^(0ν)_(1/2) > 1.9 × 10^25 yr (90% C.L.), the extracted upper limits for the neutrino mass parameter with full-RQRPA were 0.407 eV (9-level basis) and 0.625 eV (12-level basis).
- The phase space factor used was F_1^(0ν) = 6.31 × 10^(-15) yr^(-1).

## Context

This work extends earlier QRPA-based calculations of double-beta decay matrix elements for Ge-76, particularly comparing the newer RQRPA and full-RQRPA methods (Toivanen and Suhonen, 1995; Schweiger et al., 1996) with the established pnQRPA (Halbleib and Sorensen, 1967; Grotz and Klapdor, 1986) and second-QRPA (Raduta et al., 1991; Stoica et al., 1994) approaches. The study enables a critical assessment of the reliability of different theoretical methods for extracting neutrino mass limits from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment's half-life limits, and highlights the need for improved numerical techniques in RQRPA-type calculations and for including corrections from weak magnetism and pseudoscalar couplings as done by Simkovic et al. (1999).
