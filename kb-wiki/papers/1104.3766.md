# Integrable two-channel p_x+ip_y-wave superfluid model

> We present a new two-channel integrable model describing a system of spinless fermions interacting through a p-wave Feshbach resonance. Unlike the BCS-BEC crossover of the s-wave case, the p-wave model has a third order quantum phase transition. The critical point coincides with the deconfinement of a single molecule within a BEC of bound dipolar molecules. The exact many-body wavefunction provides a unique perspective of the quantum critical region suggesting that the size of the condensate wavefunction, that diverges logarithmically with the chemical potential, could be used as an experimental indicator of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new two-channel integrable model for spinless fermions with a p-wave Feshbach resonance that exhibits a third-order quantum phase transition, unlike the BCS-BEC crossover in the s-wave case. The critical point coincides with the deconfinement of a single molecule within a BEC of bound dipolar molecules, and the size of the condensate wavefunction diverges logarithmically with the chemical potential, potentially serving as an experimental indicator of the phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct an exactly solvable two-channel p_x+ip_y-wave superfluid model by bosonizing the large-spin limit of a hyperbolic Richardson-Gaudin integrable model, using a Holstein-Primakoff mapping to obtain a spin-boson Hamiltonian. They then derive the exact many-body wavefunction in terms of pair energies (pairons) that satisfy Richardson equations, and analyze the thermodynamic limit to obtain coupled BCS-like equations for the bosonic density and chemical potential. The model is connected to the physical p-wave pairing Hamiltonian by representing the SU(2) algebra in terms of fermionic pair operators with momentum-dependent coupling.

## Key numbers

- The critical point occurs at x_cr = 1/2 - ρ, where x = δ/(2g²L_c) and ρ = M/L_c is the fermion density; for ρ=1/4, λ=1/2, ω=1, this gives x_cr = 1/4.
- The Moore-Read line corresponds to x_MR = (1-ρ)/2, where the energy density ε = 0.
- The root mean square size of the condensate wavefunction diverges as r_rms² → -ln|μ/√(2ωλρ₀)| / [2λρ₀ ln(1+ω/(2λρ₀))] at the QPT point where μ → 0.
- The fidelity susceptibility χ_f/L_c at the critical point scales as -(dμ/dx)² ln(Δx)/(ωλρ₀), showing logarithmic divergence as Δx → 0.

## Context

This work extends the one-channel p_x+ip_y pairing model studied by Rombouts et al. (2010) and Dunning et al. (2010) to include a Feshbach resonance with an additional bosonic degree of freedom and an extra control parameter λ. The model enables experimental detection of the topological quantum phase transition via density-density correlation measurements in cold atomic gases, and provides exact wavefunction insights into the confinement-deconfinement nature of the transition, contrasting with the power-law behavior seen in Ising chain models studied by Damski (2011).
