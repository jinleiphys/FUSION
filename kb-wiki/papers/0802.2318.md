# Tables of Hyperonic Matter Equation of State for Core-Collapse Supernovae

> We present sets of equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter including hyperons using an SU_f(3) extended relativistic mean field (RMF) model with a wide coverage of density, temperature, and charge fraction for numerical simulations of core collapse supernovae. Coupling constants of Sigma and Xi hyperons with the sigma meson are determined to fit the hyperon potential depths in nuclear matter, U_Sigma(rho_0) ~ +30 MeV and U_Xi(rho_0) ~ -15 MeV, which are suggested from recent analyses of hyperon production reactions. At low densities, the EOS of uniform matter is connected with the EOS by Shen et al., in which formation of finite nuclei is included in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. In the present EOS, the maximum mass of neutron stars decreases from 2.17 M_sun (Ne mu) to 1.63 M_sun (NYe mu) when hyperons are included. In a spherical, adiabatic collapse of a 15$M_\odot$ star by the hydrodynamics without neutrino transfer, hyperon effects are found to be small, since the temperature and density do not reach the region of hyperon mixture, where the hyperon fraction is above 1 % (T > 40 MeV or rho_B > 0.4 fm^{-3}).

## Key claim

The paper presents new equation of state (EOS) tables for supernova simulations that include hyperons, but the authors find that hyperon effects are small in a spherical, adiabatic collapse of a 15 solar mass star because the temperature and density do not reach the region where the hyperon fraction exceeds 1% (T > 40 MeV or baryon density > 0.4 fm^{-3}).

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with parameter set TM1 to include the full baryon octet using flavor SU(3) symmetry, determining coupling constants for Sigma and Xi hyperons to fit recent experimental hyperon potential depths (U_Sigma = +30 MeV, U_Xi = -15 MeV). They connected the uniform matter EOS at low densities to the Shen EOS table, which includes finite nuclei via the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and tabulated thermodynamic quantities as functions of baryon mass density, charge ratio, and temperature over wide ranges suitable for supernova simulations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass decreases from 2.17 solar masses (nucleonic EOS without hyperons) to 1.63 solar masses (with hyperons using repulsive Sigma potential)
- Maximum neutron star mass with attractive hyperon potentials (EOSY(SM)) is 1.55 solar masses
- Threshold density for Lambda hyperon appearance in neutron star matter: 0.32 fm^{-3}
- Threshold density for Sigma- hyperon appearance with repulsive potential: 1.14 fm^{-3}
- Threshold density for Xi- hyperon appearance: 0.40 fm^{-3}
- Hyperon fraction exceeds 1% only when temperature exceeds 40 MeV or baryon density exceeds 0.4 fm^{-3}
- Incompressibility of symmetric uniform matter with TM1: 281 MeV
- Symmetry energy parameter: 36.9 MeV
- EOS table covers baryon mass density from 10^{5.1} to 10^{15.4} g/cm^3 (104 points), charge ratio from 0 to 0.56 (72 points), and temperature from 0 to 100 MeV (32 points)

## Context

This work extends the widely used Shen EOS (Shen et al., 1998) and the TM1 RMF parameter set (Sugahara and Toki, 1994) by including hyperons based on updated hyperon potential depths from recent experimental analyses (Noumi et al., 2002; Harada et al., 2006; Kohno et al., 2006; Maekawa et al., 2007). The tables enable numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron star cooling, and black hole formation with strangeness degrees of freedom, which previously had no publicly available EOS tables including hyperons. The results contradict earlier works with attractive Sigma potentials (Schaffner and Mishustin, 1996) by finding that Sigma hyperons appear at much higher densities when repulsive potentials are used.
