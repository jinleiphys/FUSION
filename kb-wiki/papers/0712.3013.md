# Recent Developments in Chiral Unitary Dynamics of Resonances

> In this talk I summarize recent findings made on the description of axial vector mesons as dynamically generated states from the interaction of peseudoscalar mesons and vector mesons, dedicating some attention to the two $K_1(1270)$ states. Then I review the generation of open and hidden charm scalar and axial states. Finally, I present recent results showing that the low lying $1/2^+$ baryon resonances for S=-1 can be obtained as bound states or resonances of two mesons and one baryon in coupled channels dynamics.

# Recent Developments in Chiral Unitary Dynamics of Resonances

## Key claim
Axial vector mesons, open and hidden charm scalar and axial states, and low-lying 1/2^+ baryon resonances with strangeness S=-1 can be described as dynamically generated states arising from meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions in coupled channels, without being introduced as elementary degrees of freedom. The two K1(1270) states are claimed to be distinct resonances with masses near 1197 MeV and 1284 MeV, rather than a single state, and the X(3872) is generated as a positive C-parity state with a nearly degenerate negative C-parity partner.

## Method
The authors use chiral Lagrangians for pseudoscalar and vector meson interactions, unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, to generate scattering amplitudes whose poles correspond to resonances. For the three-body baryon sector, Faddeev equations are solved with on-shell two-body t-matrices from chiral unitary dynamics, including cancellations between off-shell contributions and three-body forces. SU(4) extensions with vector meson exchange and mass-ratio corrections are employed for charm systems.

## Key numbers
- Two K1 states found at 1197 MeV (width ~240 MeV) and 1284 MeV (width ~140 MeV), with the first coupling most strongly to K* pi and the second to K rho.
- D_s0*(2317) generated at 2317.25 MeV with zero width.
- D_0*(2400) generated at 2129.26 MeV with width 157.00 MeV (imaginary part).
- Hidden charm scalar state X(3700) predicted at 3718.93 MeV with width 0.06 MeV.
- X(3872) generated at 3837.57 MeV with zero width and positive C-parity; a negative C-parity partner at 3840.69 MeV with width 1.60 MeV.
- D_s1(2460) generated at 2455.91 MeV with zero width; D_1(2430) at 2311.24 MeV with width 115.68 MeV.
- D_s1(2536) generated at 2573.62 MeV with width 0.07 MeV; D_1(2420) at 2526.47 MeV with width 0.08 MeV.
- Branching fraction ratio B(X -> pi+ pi- pi0 J/psi) / B(X -> pi+ pi- J/psi) = 1.0 ± 0.4 ± 0.3.
- Three-body forces from off-shell and chiral sources cancel to within 5% of the on-shell contribution in the SU(3) limit.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to scalar mesons like sigma(600), f0(980), a0(980), and kappa(800) (Gasser 1984, Oller 1997) and to 1/2^- baryon resonances (Weise 1998, Jido 2003) into the axial vector meson sector and charm sector. It builds on prior studies of dynamically generated axial mesons (Lutz 2003, Roca 2005) and charm scalars (Kolomeitsev 2003, Hofmann 2004, Chiang 2004, Daniel 2005). The three-body baryon calculation is the first coupled-channel treatment of two meson-one baryon systems for S=-1 resonances, addressing the poorly understood low-lying 1/2^+ states such as Sigma(1660), Sigma(1770), Lambda(1600), and Lambda(1810). The predicted hidden charm states enable experimental searches in e+ e- collisions and B-factory data.
