# Equation-of-motion and Lorentz-invariance relations for tensor-polarized parton distribution functions of spin-1 hadrons

> Structure functions of polarized spin-1 hadrons will be measured at various accelerator facilities in the near future. Recently, transverse-momentum-dependent and collinear parton distribution functions were theoretically proposed at twist 3 and twist 4 in addition to the twist-2 ones, so that full investigations became possible for structure functions of spin-1 hadrons in the same level with those of the spin-1/2 nucleons. Furthermore, twist-3 tensor-polarized multiparton distribution functions were also recently found for spin-1 hadrons. In this work, we show relations among the collinear parton- and multiparton-distribution functions for spin-1 hadrons by using equation of motion for quarks. These relations are valuable in constraining the distribution functions and learning about multiparton correlations in spin-1 hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives equation-of-motion and Lorentz-invariance relations that connect twist-3 tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) to twist-3 multiparton distribution functions and transverse-momentum-weighted twist-2 PDFs for spin-1 hadrons, under the condition that the lightcone gauge and specific boundary conditions for the gluon field are used.

## Method

The authors start from the quark equation of motion \((i\slashed{D} - m)\psi = 0\) and express it in lightcone coordinates, then take traces with the collinear correlation functions \(\Phi_D^\mu(x)\) and \(\Phi(x)\) for spin-1 hadrons. The correlation functions are expanded in terms of tensor-polarized PDFs (twist-2 and twist-3) and multiparton distribution functions defined with the gluon field tensor \(G^{+\mu}\) and the covariant derivative \(D^\mu\). By integrating the equation-of-motion constraints over the momentum-fraction variable \(y\) and using relations between the \(k_T\)-weighted correlation function and the multiparton correlation functions, the authors obtain integral equations linking the PDFs and multiparton distributions.

## Key numbers

* The twist-3 PDF \(f_{LT}(x)\) is related to the \(k_T\)-weighted function \(f_{1LT}^{(1)}(x)\) and multiparton distributions \(F_{G,LT}\) and \(G_{G,LT}\) by: \(x f_{LT}(x) - f_{1LT}^{(1)}(x) - \mathcal{P} \int_{-1}^1 dy \, [F_{G,LT}(x,y) + G_{G,LT}(x,y)]/(x-y) = 0\).
* The Lorentz-invariance relation is: \(d f_{1LT}^{(1)}(x)/dx - f_{LT}(x) + (3/2) f_{1LL}(x) - 2 \mathcal{P} \int_{-1}^1 dy \, F_{G,LT}(x,y)/(x-y)^2 = 0\).
* The twist-3 PDF \(e_{LL}(x)\) satisfies: \(x e_{LL}(x) - 2 \mathcal{P} \int_{-1}^1 dy \, H_{G,LL}^\perp(x,y)/(x-y) - (m/M) f_{1LL}(x) = 0\), where \(m/M \ll 1\) so the mass term is practically negligible.
* The multiparton distribution \(F_{D,LT}(x,y)\) is expressed as: \(F_{D,LT}(x,y) = \delta(x-y) f_{1LT}^{(1)}(x) + \mathcal{P}[1/(x-y)] F_{G,LT}(x,y)\).

## Context

This work extends to spin-1 hadrons the equation-of-motion and Lorentz-invariance relations previously derived for spin-1/2 nucleons by Goeke et al. (2003) and Metz et al. (2009). It builds on the recent theoretical framework for twist-3 and twist-4 tensor-polarized PDFs and multiparton distributions developed by Kumano and Song (2021) and on earlier twist-2 studies by Bacchetta (2000). The derived relations will help constrain the tensor-polarized PDFs and multiparton correlations in spin-1 hadrons, which are expected to be measured at JLab, Fermilab, NICA, LHC-spin, and future electron-ion colliders.
