# Excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter II: Three-flavor shell-like distribution of baryons in phase space

> We extend the excluded-volume model of isospin symmetric two-flavor dense quarkyonic matter [Phys. Rev. C 101, 035201 (2020)] including strange particles and address its implications for neutron stars. The effective sizes of baryons are defined from the diverging hard-core potentials in the short interdistance regime. Around the hard-core density, the repulsive core between baryons at short distances leads to a saturation in the number density of baryons and generates perturbative quarks from the lower phase space, which leads to the shell-like distribution of baryons by the Pauli exclusion principle. The strange-quark Fermi sea always appears at high densities but the $Λ$ hyperon shell only appears when the effective size of the $Λ$ hyperon is smaller than the effective size of nucleons. We find that the pressure of strange quarkyonic matter can be large enough to support neutron stars with two times solar mass and can have a large sound speed, $c_s^2 \simeq 0.7$. The fraction of the baryon number carried by perturbative quarks is about 30% at the inner core of most massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

The excluded-volume model for three-flavor quarkyonic matter, which includes strange particles and an explicit shell-like distribution of baryons in phase space, can produce an equation of state stiff enough to support neutron stars with two solar masses, but only if the effective size of the $\Lambda$ hyperon is smaller than that of nucleons; otherwise the $\Lambda$ hyperon shell does not appear.

## Method

The authors extend a previous two-flavor excluded-volume model (Duarte et al., 2020) to include strange quarks and $\Lambda$ hyperons, defining baryon effective sizes from hard-core repulsive potentials. They impose a shell-like momentum distribution for baryons, where the lower boundary is set by the saturated quark Fermi sea via the Pauli exclusion principle, and introduce an enhanced phase-space measure for quarks to avoid unphysical divergences. The equation of state is then computed under $\beta$-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The sound speed squared reaches $c_s^2 \simeq 0.7$ in strange quarkyonic matter.
- The pressure of strange quarkyonic matter can support neutron stars with two times solar mass ($2 M_\odot$).
- The fraction of the baryon number carried by perturbative quarks is about 30% at the inner core of the most massive neutron stars.
- The hard-core density considered is $n_0 > 0.65~\text{fm}^{-3} \simeq 4\rho_0$, where $\rho_0$ is normal nuclear density.
- The effective hard-core radius for baryons is around $r_c \simeq 0.6~\text{fm}$.
- The variation range for the $\Lambda$ hyperon hard-core size parameter is $\vert \alpha \vert < 0.2$.
- The regulator for the quark phase-space measure is set to $\delta = 0.15~\text{GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter (Jeong et al., 2019; Duarte et al., 2020) to include strange particles, addressing the need for an equation of state that is soft at low densities and hard at high densities to simultaneously satisfy the $R_{1.4} \leq 13.5~\text{km}$ constraint from GW170817 and the requirement of supporting $2 M_\odot$ neutron stars (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013; Cromartie et al., 2019). The model provides an alternative to first-order phase transitions to quark matter, generating the required stiff-to-soft evolution naturally through the quarkyonic matter concept (McLerran et al., 2007; McLerran et al., 2018). The results enable future work on the role of multi-flavor hard-core repulsion and the possible emergence of heavier baryons like $\Delta(1232)$ in dense matter.
