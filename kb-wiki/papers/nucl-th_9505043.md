# Chiral Dynamics and the Low Energy Kaon-Nucleon Interaction

> We examine the meson-baryon interaction in the strangeness S=-1 sector using an effective chiral Lagrangian. Potentials are derived from this Lagrangian and used in a coupled-channel calculation of the low energy observables. The potentials are constructed such that in the Born approximation the s-wave scattering amplitude is the same as that given by the effective chiral Lagrangian, up to order $q^2$. Comparison is made with the available low energy hadronic data of the coupled $K^-p, Σπ, Λπ$ system, which includes the $Λ(1405)$ resonance, $K^-p$ elastic and inelastic scattering, and the threshold branching ratios of the $K^-p$ decay. Good fits to the experimental data and estimates of previously unknown Lagrangian parameters are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective chiral Lagrangian, when used to construct a potential that is iterated to infinite order via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, can successfully reproduce the low-energy kaon-nucleon interaction data including the Λ(1405) resonance, but only when terms up to order q² are included; the current algebra term alone fails to reproduce the threshold branching ratio γ = Γ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻)/Γ(K⁻p → π⁻Σ⁺).

## Method

The authors construct meson-baryon pseudo-potentials from the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian such that in the Born approximation the s-wave scattering amplitude matches the chiral Lagrangian up to order q². These potentials, in both local Yukawa and separable forms, are iterated in a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the six channels (π⁺Σ⁻, π⁰Σ⁰, π⁻Σ⁺, π⁰Λ, K⁻p, K̄⁰n). The free Lagrangian parameters (b₀, d₀, d_D, d_F, d₁, d₂) and range parameters α are fitted to experimental data including K⁻p scattering cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and constraints from πN and K⁺N scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1405) resonance is produced just below the K⁻p threshold at 1405 MeV
- Best-fit local potential uses a single common range α = 412 MeV for all channels
- Best-fit parameters for the local potential: b₀ = -0.493 GeV⁻¹, d₀ = -0.66 GeV⁻¹, d_D = -0.02 GeV⁻¹, d_F = -0.29 GeV⁻¹, d₁ = +0.23 GeV⁻¹, d₂ = -0.39 GeV⁻¹
- The K⁻p scattering length obtained is (-0.97 + 1.1 i) fm; in the isospin limit this becomes (-0.51 + 0.89 i) fm
- The root mean square radius of the K̄N bound state is 1.3 fm
- The πN sigma term from the best fit is σ_πN = 44 MeV
- The b_D and b_F parameters are fixed from baryon mass splittings: b_D = +0.066 GeV⁻¹ and b_F = -0.213 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory from the πN and K⁺N systems, where the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term works well, into the strongly interacting S = -1 sector where a resonance dominates. It builds on the potential model approach of Dalitz (1960s) for the K̄N system and the heavy baryon chiral Lagrangian formalism developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and Bernard et al. (1992). The results provide the first estimates of the double-derivative "d" parameters of the order q² chiral Lagrangian, and enable future tests of chiral dynamics against improved K⁻p atomic level shift measurements and additional scattering data.
