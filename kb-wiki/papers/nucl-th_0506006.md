# A dynamical model for pion electroproduction on the nucleon

> We develop a Lorenz- and gauge-invariant dynamical model for pion electroproduction in the resonance region. The model is based on solving of the Salpeter (instantaneous) equation for the pion-nucleon interaction with a hadron-exchange potential. We find that the one-particle-exchange kernel of the Salpeter equation for pion electroproduction develops an unphysical singularity for a finite value of $Q^{2}$. We analyse two methods of dealing with this problem. Results of our model are compared with recent single-polarization data for pion electroproduction.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Lorentz- and gauge-invariant dynamical model for pion electroproduction in the resonance region, but the authors acknowledge that the one-particle-exchange kernel of the Salpeter equation develops an unphysical singularity for a finite value of the photon virtuality \(Q^{2}\), requiring an ad-hoc regularization method.

## Method

The model solves the Salpeter (instantaneous) equation for the pion-nucleon interaction using a hadron-exchange potential that includes Born terms (nucleon direct and crossed, t-channel pion, rho, omega exchanges, Kroll-Ruderman term) and direct and crossed Delta-isobar contributions. To avoid singularities in the u- and t-channel exchange propagators that appear at finite \(Q^{2}\), the authors compare two regularization methods: (1) fixing the relative energy variable to \(\pm Q^{2}/2W\) with subsequent restoration of current conservation via analytic correction terms, and (2) the spectator approximation where the spectator particle is placed on its mass shell. The electroproduction amplitude is then computed to first order in the electromagnetic coupling, including pion rescattering as final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic coupling of the Delta, \(g_{M}\), is 2.67 in the \(Q^{2}/2W\) approximation and 3.10 in the spectator approximation.
- The electric coupling of the Delta, \(g_{E}\), is 0.11 in the \(Q^{2}/2W\) approximation and 0.05 in the spectator approximation.
- The Coulomb coupling of the Delta, \(g_{C}\), is -0.38 in the \(Q^{2}/2W\) approximation and -0.18 in the spectator approximation.
- The dipole form factor parameter \(\Lambda\) used for comparison is 0.71 GeV\(^2\).
- The model fits Delta electromagnetic form factors up to \(Q^{2}=4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- Predictions are shown for differential cross sections at \(Q^{2}=5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and \(Q^{2}=6\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) at \(W=1.23\) GeV.
- The longitudinal-transverse polarized structure function \(\sigma_{LT'}\) is compared with CLAS data at \(Q^{2}=0.4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and \(W=1.22\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier OHIO dynamical model (Caia, Wright, Pascalutsa, 2004) for pion electroproduction, which itself builds on a pion-nucleon scattering model (piNscatt) that satisfies unitarity and Watson's theorem. The paper addresses a technical singularity problem that arises when extending the Salpeter equation from real photons (\(Q^{2}=0\)) to virtual photons (\(Q^{2}>0\)), a difficulty not present in other approaches such as SAID or MAID partial-wave analyses, or in the dynamical models of Sato and Lee (Sato2) and the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) group. The two regularization methods yield moderate differences in the extracted \(N\to\Delta\) transition form factors, which the authors treat as a measure of theoretical uncertainty. The model enables predictions for higher-\(Q^{2}\) data being analyzed at Jefferson Lab, and provides a framework for studying both pion photoproduction and electroproduction within the same dynamical model.
