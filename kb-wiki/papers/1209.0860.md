# Jet modification in hot and dense QCD matter

> High energy quarks and gluons play essential roles in the tomographic study of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and of the quark-gluon plasma. They interact with the traversed dense matter through elastic and inelastic collisions, and lose some of their initial energy in the process. Experimental observables for jet modification include single inclusive hadron production, hadron emission opposite to a triggered hadron or photon, and full jet observables. Here a brief review of parton energy loss and jet quenching in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions is presented.

## Key claim

This review argues that while pQCD-based parton energy loss models are qualitatively consistent with jet quenching data from RHIC and the LHC, significant quantitative differences between models persist (with extracted transport parameters varying by factors of two to five) due to soft, collinear, and eikonal approximations, motivating the development of full next-to-leading order (NLO) jet quenching schemes.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for jet modification in hot QCD matter, covering single inclusive hadron suppression via medium-modified fragmentation functions, full jet observables including dijet energy asymmetry, and photon-tagged jet correlations. It compares existing pQCD-based formalisms (AMY, ASW, BDMPS-Z, GLV, HT) for radiative energy loss using the QGP "brick" problem and discusses phenomenological calculations that embed jet energy loss models into hydrodynamic backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for single inclusive hadrons at the LHC shows a rapid rise as a function of transverse momentum, attributed to a much flatter leading parton spectrum compared to RHIC.
- Jet transport parameters (e.g., hat-q) from different models differ by a factor of two up to five when describing the same RHIC data within the same hydrodynamic background (Bass 2008).
- Dijet energy asymmetry A_J = (p_T,1 - p_T,2)/(p_T,1 + p_T,2) increases from peripheral to central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, while no strong change is observed for the dijet azimuth separation distribution.
- The LHC provides jets with transverse energies in excess of 100 GeV for full jet reconstruction studies.

## Context

This review extends the systematic comparison of parton energy loss models initiated by Bass (2008) and Armesto (2011), which revealed that different formalisms (AMY, ASW, BDMPS-Z, GLV, HT) produce transport coefficients differing by factors of 2-5 even for the simplified QGP "brick" problem. The work enables future development of unified NLO jet quenching schemes that incorporate both soft collinear and hard large angle radiations, and motivates simultaneous modeling of jet shower evolution and hydrodynamic medium response to deposited energy.
