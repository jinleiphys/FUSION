# Evaluation of the curvature-correction term from the equation of state of nuclear matter

> Based on the nuclear equation of state, the curvature correction term to the surface tension coefficient is calculated. Tolman $δ$ correction is shown to be sensitive to the Skyrme force parametrization. The temperature dependence of the Tolman length, important in heavy ion collisions experiments, is derived. In suggested approach the curvature term is related to the bulk properties of the nuclear matter through the equation of state. The obtained results are compared with the existing theoretical calculations based on the Gibbs-Tolman formalism and with the theoretical predictions concerning its dependence on the interparticle distance.

## Key claim

The curvature correction (Tolman length δ) to the nuclear surface tension can be evaluated from the nuclear equation of state alone, but the obtained values are sensitive to the chosen Skyrme force parametrization and are negative for all parametrizations tested, meaning the surface of tension lies closer to the liquid phase.

## Method

The authors perform a gedanken experiment in which a spherical volume of infinite nuclear matter is isolated, allowed to shrink under surface tension, and the resulting pressure difference is expanded in powers of 1/R. By equating this expansion to the Laplace equation with the Tolman-corrected surface tension, an expression for δ is derived in terms of derivatives of the equation of state. The nuclear equation of state is taken in a Skyrme-force form at zero temperature and normal density, and δ is evaluated numerically for three Skyrme parametrizations (SkM*, SLy6, SV-min) using a planar surface tension σ∞ = 1.1 MeV/fm².

## Key numbers

- Tolman length δ for SkM*: -0.8869 fm
- Tolman length δ for SLy6: -1.5600 fm
- Tolman length δ for SV-min: -0.5512 fm
- For comparison, δ from the Gibbs-Tolman approach (Kolomietz 2013) for SkM*: -0.3703 fm
- The average interparticle distance in nuclear matter at normal density ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³ is r₀ ≈ 0.7 fm
- The planar surface tension used: σ∞ = 1.1 MeV/fm²

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs-Tolman formalism (Tolman 1949) for curvature corrections to surface tension, previously applied to liquid droplets, to nuclear matter by connecting δ to bulk properties through the equation of state. The results are compared with calculations from Kolomietz (2012, 2013) using the Gibbs-Tolman approach for charged Fermi-liquid droplets; the present values are larger in magnitude but consistent in sign and order of magnitude. The temperature dependence of δ, which increases with temperature for all parametrizations, follows the same qualitative behavior as ordinary liquids (Bulavin 1998) and is relevant for interpreting multifragmentation experiments and light-nuclei yields in heavy-ion collisions.
