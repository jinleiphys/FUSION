# Electromagnetic Radiation from QCD Matter: Theory Overview

> Recent theory developments in electromagnetic radiation from relativistic heavy-ion collisions are reviewed. Electromagnetic observables can serve as a thermometer, a viscometer, and tomographic probes to the collision system. The current status of the "direct photon flow puzzle" is highlighted.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (direct photons and dileptons) can serve as a thermometer, viscometer, and tomographic probe of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the "direct photon flow puzzle" remains unresolved because theoretical calculations still underestimate the measured large yields and momentum anisotropies for photons with transverse momentum below 4 GeV.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments in electromagnetic radiation from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, focusing on photon and dilepton production rates, hydrodynamic modeling of the bulk medium evolution, and comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC. It examines how viscous hydrodynamic evolution (including shear and bulk viscosity), viscous corrections to photon emission rates, and event-by-event fluctuations affect the calculated direct photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX Collaboration reported effective temperature T_eff = (239 ± 25_stat ± 7_sys) MeV for 0-20% Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- ALICE Collaboration found T_eff = (304 ± 11_stat ± 40_sys) MeV in 0-20% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Thermal photons emitted from temperatures below 250 MeV receive significant blue-shift from hydrodynamic flow
- Extracted average temperature from dilepton spectra in the intermediate mass region (1.5-2.5 GeV) is about 30% lower than the initial temperature at top RHIC energy
- Inclusion of bulk viscosity increases the space-time volume in the late hadronic phase by about 50% and reduces hydrodynamic radial flow by about 10%
- PHSD model produces about 4 times more photons from pi-pi bremsstrahlung than hydrodynamic calculations
- Thermal photon emission shows a two-wave structure: first wave from high temperature regions during early evolution, second wave near the cross-over region at proper time 3-8 fm/c
- About 50% thermal enhancement over prompt pQCD contribution found for p_T < 3 GeV in minimum bias small collision systems

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for electromagnetic probes developed by Shuryak (1978) and Hwa (1985) for measuring QGP temperature, and addresses the "direct photon flow puzzle" first reported by the PHENIX Collaboration (2011). The work builds on viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Schenke (2006), Dusling (2009), and Dion (2011), and compares with transport approaches such as PHSD by Linnyk (2015) and coarse-grained UrQMD by Endres (2014, 2015). The results enable future measurements at RHIC Beam Energy Scan phase II and FAIR experiments to extract quantitative information about QGP properties and test the existence of thermalized QGP in small collision systems.
