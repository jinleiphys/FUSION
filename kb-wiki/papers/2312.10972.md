# Acausality in truncated 'MIS'-type theory

> The causal-stable Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory is known to have a finite number of out of equilibrium derivative order corrections but requires treating the viscosity tensor as a separate degree of freedom with its own equations of motion, apart from the fundamental fluid degrees of freedom like velocity and temperature. In this work, I will show that it is possible to rewrite the MIS theory only in terms of velocity and temperature, but the resultant constitutive relation for dissipation must include all orders of gradient corrections. In this work I will argue that an all-order resummation of gradient contributions is equivalent to introducing new `non-fluid' degrees of freedom in the MIS theory. It will also be shown, using the relativistic quantum causality condition, that any finitely truncated order of derivative correction, however high it is, leads to a theory that is acausal, unless the corrections are infinitely summed up to all orders.

## Key claim
Any finite-order truncation of the derivative expansion in relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics leads to acausal signal propagation, regardless of how high the truncation order is; causality is only preserved when the gradient corrections are summed to all orders, which is equivalent to introducing new non-fluid degrees of freedom as in the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory.

## Method
The author rewrites the MIS theory's shear-stress tensor as an infinite sum of order-by-order gradient corrections in terms of velocity and temperature only, without treating the viscosity tensor as an independent degree of freedom. Linearizing the conservation equations around hydrostatic equilibrium, the shear and sound channel dispersion relations are derived for both finite-order truncations and the all-order resummed case. The relativistic quantum causality condition Im(ω(k)) ≤ |Im(k)| is then applied to test whether the dispersion relation solutions for various truncation orders satisfy causal propagation.

## Key numbers
- For truncation at N=0 (Navier-Stokes), the shear channel solution ω = -iη̃k² gives Im(ω) = η̃p² for imaginary k = ±ip, which violates Im(ω) ≤ |Im(k)| since Im(ω) ∼ p² while |Im(k)| ∼ p.
- For truncation at N=1, at k² = (1±ε)/(η̃τπ) with ε << 1, Im(ω±) = ±(1/τπ)(1/ε), which is a large positive number while |Im(k)| is finite, violating causality.
- For truncation at N ≥ 2, at small k the solution gives Im(ω) ∼ [1/(η̃|τπ|^N|k|²)]^{1/(N-1)} cos{...}, which can be made a large positive number while |Im(k)| remains finite.
- The all-order theory satisfies Im(ω) ≤ |Im(k)| for all k when τπ > η̃, which is the asymptotic causality condition for the MIS shear channel.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory (Muller, 1967; Israel, 1976; Israel and Stewart, 1979) by showing that its causal and stable behavior requires an infinite resummation of gradient corrections, not merely a finite-order truncation. The result contrasts with the recently proposed first-order stable and causal BDNK theory (Bemfica, 2017; Bemfica, 2019; Bemfica, 2020; Kovtun, 2019; Hoult, 2020; Hoult, 2021), which avoids additional degrees of freedom but operates away from the Landau frame where velocity and temperature lack a first-principles definition in terms of the stress tensor operator. The work enables a clearer understanding of why dissipative fluid theories must either include all orders of gradients, introduce non-fluid degrees of freedom (MIS), or accept ambiguous definitions of fluid variables (BDNK).
