# Detecting high-energy neutrinos from microquasars with the ANTARES telescope

> The ANTARES project aims at the construction of a neutrino telescope 2500 m below the surface of the Mediterranean sea, close to the southern French coast. The apparatus will consist of a 3D array of photomultiplier tubes, which detects the Cherenkov light emitted by upward going neutrino-induced muons. High-energy neutrinos may be produced in powerful cosmic accelerators, such as, gamma-ray bursters, active galactic nuclei, supernova remnants, and microquasars. We have estimated the event rate in ANTARES of neutrinos coming from these sources, and particularly for a microquasar model, and found that for some of these sources the detection rate can be up to several events per year.

## Key claim

The ANTARES neutrino telescope may detect up to several high-energy neutrino events per year from microquasars, but only for a few specific sources; the detection rate is highly dependent on the source model parameters, with most sources yielding less than one event per year.

## Method

The authors convolved the predicted neutrino fluxes from microquasars, taken from the theoretical model of Distefano et al. (2002), with the simulated effective area of the ANTARES detector to calculate expected event rates. The effective area was determined from Monte Carlo simulations of the detector response to neutrino-induced muons, including reconstruction efficiency and quality cuts. Event rates were calculated for 14 visible microquasar sources using the integral of the effective area times the differential neutrino flux over energy.

## Key numbers

- For the persistent sources SS433 and GX339-4, about 6-8 events per year are expected in ANTARES
- The atmospheric neutrino background is approximately 0.5 events per year within 1 degree around a source
- For the source V4641 Sgr with a larger distance (9.6 kpc) and jet directed toward the line of sight (9 degrees), the neutrino detection rate is about 100 events per year
- The effective area for ANTARES increases rapidly up to about 1 PeV, above which it saturates for a uniform event distribution
- The intrinsic angular resolution of ANTARES reaches a limiting value of 0.15 degrees at high energies
- The energy resolution for muon energy reconstruction is about a factor of 2 above 1 TeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of neutrino production in microquasars by Levinson and Waxman (2001) and Distefano et al. (2002) by calculating the specific detection rates for the ANTARES telescope, which was under construction at the time. The paper enables comparison between the ANTARES and AMANDA detectors, noting that SS433 is visible to both (11.5 hours per day for ANTARES and 24 hours per day for AMANDA) allowing cross-checks, while GX339-4 is always visible to ANTARES but never seen by AMANDA. The results inform the observational strategy for the first generation of underwater neutrino telescopes.
