# Effective field theory of 3He

> 3He and the triton are studied as three-body bound states in the effective field theory without pions. We study 3He using the set of integral equations developed by Kok et al. which includes the full off-shell T-matrix for the Coulomb interaction between the protons. To leading order, the theory contains: two-body contact interactions whose renormalized strengths are set by the NN scattering lengths, the Coulomb potential, and a three-body contact interaction. We solve the three coupled integral equations with a sharp momentum cutoff, Lambda, and find that a three-body interaction is required in 3He at leading order, as in the triton. It also exhibits the same limit-cycle behavior as a function of Lambda, showing that the Efimov effect remains in the presence of the Coulomb interaction. We also obtain the difference between the strengths of the three-body forces in 3He and the triton.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction does not alter the leading-order renormalization structure of the three-body force in ³He: a three-body contact interaction is required at leading order, and it exhibits the same limit-cycle behavior as a function of cutoff as in the triton, confirming that the Efimov effect survives in the presence of the Coulomb interaction. The difference between the three-body force strengths in ³He and the triton is of the expected size for an electromagnetic effect.

## Method

The authors solved the three coupled integral equations for nucleon-dibaryon scattering in the S-wave, total-spin-1/2, total-isospin-1/2 channel, using the formalism of Kok et al. (1975) which incorporates the full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix between the two protons. The equations were discretized with Gauss-Legendre quadrature and a sharp momentum cutoff Λ, and the homogeneous parts were treated as nonlinear eigenvalue problems to find bound-state energies. The three-body coupling H(Λ) was adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energies of the triton and ³He for various cutoff values.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy: B₃(³H) = 8.48182 MeV, corresponding to momentum scale α = 89.239 MeV.
- ³He binding energy: B₃(³He) = 7.71804 MeV, corresponding to α = 85.127 MeV.
- For the triton with H(Λ)=0, the cutoff that reproduces the experimental binding energy is Λ₀ = 380.689 MeV.
- For ³He with the same isospin-symmetric three-body force (H(Λ₀) from the triton), the calculated binding energy is 7.66038 MeV, within 1% of the experimental value and differing from the triton energy by 0.82 MeV.
- The scale parameter Λ₀ for ³He is 382.46 MeV, differing from the triton value by 0.46%; this contributes about 50 keV to the triton-³He binding energy difference.
- Two-body inputs: γ = 45.703 MeV (deuteron binding), a_np = -23.749 ± 0.008 fm, a_nn = -18.5 ± 0.4 fm, a_C = -7.8063 ± 0.0026 fm (pp Coulomb scattering length).

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory treatment of three-nucleon systems, previously applied to the triton by Bedaque et al. (2000), to include the Coulomb interaction nonperturbatively for ³He. It uses the integral equation approach of Kok et al. (1975) originally developed for separable potentials, and confirms the expectation from Hammer and Higa (2008) that the Efimov effect survives in the presence of Coulomb interactions. The results enable future calculations of low-energy pd scattering and electromagnetic properties of ³He, and provide the framework for consistent higher-order calculations including isospin-breaking effects beyond leading order.
