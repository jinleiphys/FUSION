# Regularity and Chaos in low-lying 2+ states of even-even nuclei

> Using all the available empirical information, we analyse the spacing distributions of low-lying 2+ levels in even-even nuclei by comparing them with a theoretical distribution characterized by a single parameter (the chaoticity parameter f). We use the method of Bayesian inference. We show that the necessary unfolding procedure generally leads to an overestimate of f. We find that f varies strongly with the ratio of the excitation energies of the first 4+ and 2+ levels and assumes particularly small values in nuclei that have one of the dynamical symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model.

## Key claim
The chaoticity parameter \(f\) for low-lying \(2^{+}\) states in even-even nuclei varies strongly with the ratio \(R_{4/2}=E(4_{1}^{+})/E(2_{1}^{+})\) and assumes particularly small values at \(R_{4/2}=2.0\), \(2.5\), and \(3.3\), corresponding to the dynamical symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model; however, the unfolding procedure used on short sequences of levels systematically overestimates \(f\), so only relative values of \(f\) are reliable.

## Method
The authors compiled 1306 \(2^{+}\) levels from 169 even-even nuclei using published nuclear data compilations, grouping nuclei into classes defined by intervals of the ratio \(R_{4/2}\). Each sequence of levels was unfolded using the constant-temperature formula \(N(E)=N_{0}+\exp[(E-E_{0})/T]\), and the nearest-neighbor spacing distributions were analyzed with Bayesian inference to extract the mean chaoticity parameter \(\overline{f}\) and its standard deviation for each class. The analysis used an approximate single-parameter spacing distribution \(p(s,f)\) derived from the Rosenzweig-Porter superposition of GOE subspectra.

## Key numbers
- 1306 levels of spin-parity \(2^{+}\) from 169 even-even nuclei were analyzed.
- The effective temperature parameter from unfolding follows \(T = (15 \pm 4) A^{-(0.62 \pm 0.05)}\) MeV.
- Deep minima of \(\overline{f}\) occur at \(R_{4/2}=2.0\), \(2.5\), and \(3.3\), corresponding to the IBM dynamical symmetries \(U(5)\), \(O(6)\), and \(SU(3)\) respectively.
- A further minimum of statistical significance occurs for \(2.25 \leq R_{4/2} \leq 2.35\), associated with the critical \(E(5)\) symmetry at \(R_{4/2}=2.2\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis of Abul-Magd and Weidenmueller (1995) by using a much larger data set and Bayesian inference to quantify the chaoticity parameter \(f\) for low-lying \(2^{+}\) states. The results confirm the theoretical predictions of Alhassid et al. (1990) that the IBM dynamical symmetries (\(U(5)\), \(SU(3)\), \(O(6)\)) correspond to increased regularity, and they connect to the empirical systematics of Casten et al. (1995) relating \(E(4_{1}^{+})\) and \(E(2_{1}^{+})\). The finding of a minimum near \(R_{4/2}=2.2\) supports the existence of the \(E(5)\) critical-point symmetry proposed by Iachello (2001) and observed by Casten and Zamfir (2001).
