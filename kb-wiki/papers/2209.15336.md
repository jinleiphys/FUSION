# Hadron productions and jet substructures associated with $Z^0/γ$ in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We carry out a detailed study of medium modifications on $Z^0$/$γ$+hadron correlations as well as jet substructures in association with $Z^0/γ$ in Pb+Pb collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV at the LHC. We utilize the Linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to simulate the jet-medium interactions and medium response, and an extended cluster hadronization model to investigate the non-perturbative transition of quarks and gluons into final hadrons in heavy-ion collisions. Including hadronization effect, we can well describe $Z^0/γ$+hadrons correlations and $Z^0/γ$-tagged jet substructures in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions simultaneously. Medium modification on jet profile and jet fragmentation functions indicate that particles carrying a large fraction of the jet momentum are generally closely aligned with the jet axis, whereas low-momentum particles are observed to have a much broader angular distribution relative to jet axis in Pb+Pb collisions due to jet-medium interactions. In particular, we find that $Z^0/γ$-tagged hadron correlations are sensitive to the soft particles from the dense medium and medium response, while jet-substructures show weak dependence on those soft hadrons with only a fraction of them falling inside the jet area

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including a cluster hadronization model extended to heavy-ion collisions, alongside the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet-medium interactions, can simultaneously describe both \(Z^0/\gamma\)+hadron correlations and \(Z^0/\gamma\)-tagged jet substructures in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, but only when contributions from negative (thermal) partons are included for the hadron correlations, while jet substructures show weak dependence on those soft hadrons.

## Method

The authors simulate \(Z^0/\gamma\)-tagged jet events in p+p collisions using the SHERPA event generator with NLO matrix elements matched to parton showers. These events are then embedded into the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate jet-medium interactions and medium response in Pb+Pb collisions, with a 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamic background. An extended cluster hadronization model is developed, generalizing the SHERPA cluster model to heavy-ion collisions by separately combining positive (shower and recoil) partons and negative (thermal) partons into color-neutral clusters, with a fission threshold parameter \(M_f = 3.0\) GeV for positive partons and \(M_f = 0.25\) GeV for negative partons.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Cluster fission threshold parameter: \(M_f = 3.0\) GeV for positive partons and \(M_f = 0.25\) GeV for negative partons.
- Quark masses used in cluster hadronization: \(M_{u,d} = 0.3\) GeV/\(c^2\), \(M_s = 0.45\) GeV/\(c^2\), \(M_c = 1.5\) GeV/\(c^2\), \(M_b = 5\) GeV/\(c^2\).
- Strong coupling constant in LBT: \(\alpha_s = 0.20\).
- Jet shape normalization radius: \(r_f = \Delta R = 0.3\) to match CMS measurement setup.
- \(Z^0\) candidate invariant mass window: \(60-110\) GeV/\(c^2\) with \(p_T^Z > 30\) GeV/\(c\).
- Charged hadron kinematic cuts: \(|\eta^{ch,h}| < 2.4\) and \(p_T^{ch,h} > 1\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the cluster hadronization model originally developed for p+p collisions by Gottschalk (1982-1986) and Webber (1983) to heavy-ion collisions, interfacing it with the LBT model previously used to describe hadron suppression (He 2015, Cao 2016), inclusive jets (He 2018), and \(\gamma\)+hadron/jet correlations (Chen 2017, Luo 2018). The study addresses the challenge posed by different jet radius dependence observed in charged jet measurements by ALICE, \(\gamma\)-tagged charged jets by STAR, and inclusive jets by CMS and ATLAS, and enables simultaneous description of both \(Z^0/\gamma\)-hadron correlations and jet substructures in heavy-ion collisions.
