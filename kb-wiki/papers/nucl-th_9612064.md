# Extended shell-model calculation for even N=82 isotones with realistic effective interactions

> The shell model within the $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2}$ shell is applied to calculate nuclear structure properties of the even Z=52 - 62, N=82 isotones. The results are compared with experimental data and with the results of a quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculation. The interaction used in these calculations is a realistic two-body G-matrix interaction derived from modern meson-exchange potential models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the shell model all the two-body matrix elements are renormalized by the $\hat{Q}$-box method whereas for the QRPA the effective interaction is defined by the G-matrix.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a full shell-model calculation without truncations, using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A meson-exchange potential via the G-matrix and Q-box renormalization, can reproduce the low-lying energy levels and E2 transitions of even N=82 isotones (Z=52-62) to within 0.1-0.3 MeV of experimental data, but the agreement depends on the specific nucleus and state, with deviations for high-spin states in 144Sm and a less sharp shell closure at Z=64 than observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed a full shell-model calculation within the 2s1d0g7/2 0h11/2 model space for valence protons outside a 132Sn core, using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The G-matrix was first calculated using a double-partitioning scheme with harmonic oscillator wave functions (ħΩ = 7.87 MeV for A=132), then the effective two-body interaction was obtained by renormalizing the G-matrix through the Q-box method, summing all non-folded diagrams through third order in the G-matrix and iterating the folded-diagram expansion to convergence. The single-particle energies were taken from the experimental 133Sb spectrum except for the 2s1/2 orbital, which was taken from Sagawa et al. (1987). For comparison, a quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculation was performed using the same G-matrix but with a larger single-particle space including the 1p0f shell for protons and the 2s1d0g7/2 and 2p1f0h shells for neutrons, with Woods-Saxon single-particle energies for orbitals outside the model space.

## Key numbers

- The 0+1 – 2+1 spacing in 146Gd is calculated by the shell model as 1.864 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.972 MeV.
- The deviation of calculated energy levels from experimental ones is generally within 0.1 – 0.3 MeV for both shell model and QRPA across the isotone sequence.
- The 8+1 state in 144Sm is calculated 0.58 MeV too high in energy, and the 10+1 state is 0.97 MeV too high.
- The 2+1 state energy in 134Te is calculated as 1.405 MeV (shell model) and 1.506 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.279 MeV.
- The 2+1 state energy in 136Xe is calculated as 1.510 MeV (shell model) and 1.499 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.313 MeV.
- The 2+1 state energy in 138Ba is calculated as 1.532 MeV (shell model) and 1.498 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.436 MeV.
- The 2+1 state energy in 140Ce is calculated as 1.515 MeV (shell model) and 1.481 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.596 MeV.
- The 2+1 state energy in 142Nd is calculated as 1.537 MeV (shell model) and 1.465 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.576 MeV.
- The 2+1 state energy in 144Sm is calculated as 1.753 MeV (shell model) and 1.482 MeV (QRPA), compared to the experimental 1.660 MeV.
- The shell model dimension for 148Dy is 16,010,204 basis states, the largest in the calculation.
- The oscillator energy used for the G-matrix calculation is ħΩ = 7.87 MeV for A=132.
- The starting energy for the effective interaction is ω = -20 MeV.
- An effective proton charge of e_peff(SM) = 1.4e is used for shell model E2 transitions, and e_peff(QRPA) = 1.0e for QRPA.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of Sn isotopes (Holt et al., 1995; Engeland et al., 1995) to the proton-rich N=82 isotones, providing a full shell-model calculation without truncations for the first time in this region. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological shell-model studies by Wildenthal (1990) and others who used schematic interactions and truncations, and with the generalized seniority approach of Scholten et al. (1983, 1987, 1989). The comparison with QRPA, using the same G-matrix but a larger single-particle space, tests whether the perturbative renormalization in the shell model adequately accounts for core excitations. The results enable future studies of octupole correlations and high-spin states in this mass region, and provide a benchmark for testing other many-body methods.
