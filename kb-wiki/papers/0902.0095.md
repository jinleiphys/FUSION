# Hyperdeformation in the Cd isotopes: a microscopic analysis

> A systematics search for the nuclei in which the observation of discrete hyperdeformed (HD) bands may be feasible with existing detector facilities has been performed in the Cd isotopes within the framework of cranked relativistic mean field theory. It was found that the $^{96}$Cd nucleus is a doubly magic HD nucleus due to large proton Z=48 and neutron N=48 HD shell gaps. The best candidate for experimental search of discrete HD bands is $^{107}$Cd nucleus characterized by the large energy gap between the yrast and excited HD bands, the size of which is only 15% smaller than the one in doubly magic HD $^{96}$Cd nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^{107}$Cd is the best candidate for experimental search of discrete hyperdeformed (HD) bands with existing detector facilities, based on a fully self-consistent analysis that includes polarization effects neglected in simpler analyses. The hedge is that the energy gap between the yrast and excited HD bands in $^{107}$Cd is only 15% smaller than the one in the doubly magic HD $^{96}$Cd nucleus, and that theoretical predictions are subject to errors in single-particle energy descriptions that are not fully quantified.

## Method

The authors performed cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory calculations without pairing, using the NL1 parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian (with NL3, NLSH, NLZ, and NL3* used for cross-checks). Excited HD configurations were built from yrast HD configurations by exciting one proton, one neutron, or both, with the constraint that the energy gap between the excited and target orbitals did not exceed 2.5 MeV in the routhian diagram; all configurations were then calculated in a fully self-consistent manner to obtain total energies as a function of spin.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap between the yrast HD configuration and lowest excited HD configurations in $^{96}$Cd is around 1.5 MeV.
- The energy gap between the yrast and lowest excited HD configurations in $^{107}$Cd is around 1.3 MeV.
- The combined size of the $N=59$ and $N=61$ HD shell gaps in $^{108}$Cd is 2.50 MeV (NL1 parametrization), ranging from 2.44 to 2.70 MeV across parametrizations.
- The $Z=48$ HD shell gap in $^{96}$Cd is 1.75 MeV (NL1), ranging from 1.75 to 2.71 MeV across parametrizations.
- The $N=48$ HD shell gap in $^{96}$Cd is 2.00 MeV (NL1), ranging from 2.00 to 3.03 MeV across parametrizations.
- The energy gaps between yrast and excited HD configurations in $^{108}$Cd and $^{109}$Cd are around 0.9 and 1.1 MeV, respectively.
- The energy gap between the yrast HD and lowest excited configurations is larger by approximately 0.2 MeV in calculations without time-odd mean fields (NM) than with NM at spins where HD configurations become yrast (I approximately 67 hbar).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior systematic CRMF study of HD in the Z=40-58 region (Afanasjev and Abusara, 2008) by performing a fully self-consistent analysis of HD band density in Cd isotopes, which was previously only done for $^{124}$Xe. The results contradict the simpler analysis based solely on routhian gaps, which overestimates the energy gap between yrast and excited HD configurations by up to approximately 0.5 MeV due to neglected polarization effects. The paper enables experimental searches for discrete HD bands in $^{107}$Cd using existing facilities like GAMMASPHERE, and suggests that the HD minimum is sufficiently stable against fission based on macroscopic-microscopic calculations by other groups (Dudek and collaborators, 2007).
