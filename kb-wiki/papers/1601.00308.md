# The subtle interplay of elastic and inelastic collisions in the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma

> Using kinetic theory, we analyze the interplay of elastic and inelastic collisions in the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma. The main focus is the dynamics and equilibration of long wavelength modes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma, inelastic collisions (soft gluon radiation) dominate the dynamics at very low momenta even when elastic collisions are present, and that this fast radiation actually hinders Bose condensation by rapidly eliminating excess particles, contrary to the expectation that a \(1/p\) distribution would facilitate condensation.

## Method

The authors use kinetic theory with a Fokker-Planck equation (small-angle approximation) for elastic scattering and a generalized kinetic equation with a simplified splitting kernel for inelastic processes, both applied to a spatially uniform, isotropic gluon system. They analyze the evolution of the phase-space distribution function starting from a color-glass-condensate-inspired initial condition \(f(p) = f_0 \, \theta(1 - p/Q_s)\) and compare the behavior of elastic-only, inelastic-only, and combined dynamics, focusing on the small-momentum region.

## Key numbers

* The initial energy density is \(\epsilon_{\rm in} = f_0 Q_s^4 / (8\pi^2)\), which matches the equilibrium value \(\epsilon_{\rm eq} = T_{\rm eq}^4 \pi^2 / 30\) for \(T_{\rm eq} \approx 0.44 f_0^{1/4} Q_s\).
* The initial particle number is \(n_{\rm in} = f_0 Q_s^3 / (6\pi^2)\), and the ratio \(n_{\rm in}/n_{\rm eq}\) equals unity when \(f_0 = f_c \approx 0.154\).
* For elastic scattering, the dimensionless time is \(\tau \equiv 4 \pi \alpha^2 N_c^2 t \mathcal{L} Q_s = 4\pi^3 \bar\alpha^2 t \mathcal{L} Q_s\), where \(\mathcal{L}\) is the Coulomb logarithm.
* For inelastic scattering, the solution at small momenta gives \(f(p,\tau) = T^*/p + (f_0 - T^*/p) e^{-P_*^2(\tau)/p^2}\) with \(P_*^2(\tau) = R I_a \tau\), where \(R\) is a free parameter of order unity controlling the relative strength of inelastic versus elastic collisions.

## Context

This work extends parametric analyses of weak-coupling thermalization (Baier 2000, Kurkela 2011) and prior kinetic theory studies of Bose condensation (Blaizot 2013, Blaizot 2014) by explicitly comparing the roles of elastic and inelastic collisions. It builds on recent analytical progress in medium-induced gluon cascades (Blaizot 2015) and addresses the question of whether inelastic processes facilitate or hinder condensation, a point raised by Huang (2013). The results enable a more complete understanding of how the quark-gluon plasma reaches equilibrium, with a forthcoming publication planned to detail the dependence on the relative strength parameter \(R\).
