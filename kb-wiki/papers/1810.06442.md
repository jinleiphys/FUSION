# Ab initio Folding Potentials for Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering based on NCSM One-Body Densities

> Calculating microscopic optical potentials for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering has already led to large body of work in the past. For folding first-order calculations the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction and the one-body density of the nucleus were taken as input to rigorous calculations in a spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series. Based on the Watson expansion of the multiple scattering series we employ a nonlocal translationally invariant nuclear density derived from a chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and the very same interaction for consistent full-folding calculation of the effective (optical) potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering for light nuclei. We calculate scattering observables, such as total, reaction, and differential cross sections as well as the analyzing power and the spin-rotation parameter, for elastic scattering of protons and neutrons from $^4$He, $^{6}$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O, in the energy regime between 100 and 200~MeV projectile kinetic energy, and compare to available data. Our calculations show that the effective nucleon-nucleus potential obtained from the first-order term in the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering expansion describes experiments very well to about 60 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, which coincides roughly with the validity of the NNLO chiral interaction used to calculate both the NN amplitudes and the one-body nuclear density.

## Key claim

The first-order term in the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series, using a consistent chiral NNLO interaction for both the NN amplitudes and the nuclear one-body density, describes elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering observables well up to about 60 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, which coincides roughly with the momentum transfer corresponding to 125 MeV laboratory energy where the NNLO interaction was fitted.

## Method

The authors compute a nonlocal translationally invariant one-body density from no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using the chiral NNLO_opt interaction, then perform a full-folding convolution of this density with fully off-shell NN t-matrices derived from the same interaction, including the Møller factor for frame transformation. The resulting first-order optical potential is used to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for elastic scattering observables, with the Coulomb interaction included exactly for proton scattering. The calculations are carried out in momentum space in three dimensions without partial wave decomposition, using Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections and analyzing powers are calculated for proton and neutron elastic scattering from 4He, 6He, 12C, and 16O at projectile kinetic energies between 100 and 200 MeV
- For 4He at Nmax=18, cross sections show no dependence on oscillator parameter hbar-omega over the range 16-24 MeV
- For 16O at Nmax=10, differential cross sections agree with data up to about 40 degrees (momentum transfer 1.5-2 fm^-1)
- The vertical dashed line marker at q=2.45 fm^-1 corresponds to 125 MeV laboratory kinetic energy in the np system, the upper limit of the NNLO_opt fit
- For 12C at 122, 160, and 200 MeV, calculations agree well with differential cross section data in the forward direction, with reasonable agreement for 160 and 200 MeV past 2.45 fm^-1 to about 3.5 fm^-1
- For 6He at 200 MeV/nucleon, calculations at Nmax=18 with hbar-omega between 16-24 MeV show good agreement with experimental differential cross section data
- Total cross section for neutron-16O scattering between 100-200 MeV using consistent NNLO_opt input shows very good agreement with experiment
- At 65 MeV, the neutron-16O total cross section calculation significantly underestimates the data
- For proton scattering at 230 MeV, calculated total and reaction cross sections for 16O, 12C, and 4He agree with experimental data within error bars
- For 12C, the extracted ground-state point-proton rms radius of 2.31(13) fm yields an estimated reaction cross section of 222(9) mb at 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of microscopic optical potential calculations from the 1990s (Crespo, Elster, Arellano, Chinn) by using modern chiral NN interactions and ab initio NCSM densities, enabling consistent treatment of the NN interaction in both structure and reaction calculations. The results improve upon earlier full-folding calculations by Chinn (1995) and show better agreement with data than the contemporary work of Gennari (2017), particularly for analyzing powers, likely due to the treatment of the projection operator Q and the choice of NN interaction. The strong correlation found between calculated reaction cross sections and point-proton rms radii enables uncertainty quantification of scattering observables based on structure calculation uncertainties.
