# Model study of the energy dependence of the correlation between anisotropic flow and the mean transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions

> A hybrid model that employs the hadron-string transport model UrQMD and the (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE, is used to investigate the beam energy dependence of the correlation coefficient $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ between the average transverse momentum $[p_{T}]$ of hadrons emitted in an event and the square of the anisotropic flow coefficient $v_2^2$. For Au+Au collisions, the model predicts characteristic patterns for the energy and event-shape dependence of the variances for $[p_{T}]$ and $v_n^2$ (${\rm Var}([p_T])$ and ${\rm Var}(v_2^2)$), and the covariance of $v_n^2$ and $[p_{T}]$ (${\rm cov}(v_{2}^{2},[p_T])$), consistent with the attenuation effects of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$. In contrast, $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ is predicted to be insensitive to the beam energy but sensitive to the initial-state geometry of the collisions. These observations suggest that a precise set of measurements for ${\rm Var}([p_T])$, ${\rm Var}(v_2^2)$, ${\rm cov}(v_{2}^{2},[p_T])$ and $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ as a function of beam-energy and event-shape, could aid precision extraction of the temperature and baryon chemical-potential dependence of $η/s$ from the wealth of Au+Au data obtained in the RHIC beam energy scan.

## Key claim

The hybrid model predicts that the correlation coefficient ρ(v²₂,[pT]) is insensitive to the specific shear viscosity η/s and to beam energy, but sensitive to the initial-state geometry of the collisions; this contrasts with the variances Var([pT]) and Var(v²₂) and the covariance cov(v²₂,[pT]), which show characteristic patterns consistent with η/s attenuation effects.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining the hadron-string transport model UrQMD for the early and late non-equilibrium stages with the (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE for the quark-gluon plasma phase, employing a Chiral model equation of state. Simulated events were generated for minimum bias Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies √sNN = 19.5, 27, 62.4, and 200 GeV, with observables evaluated for charged hadrons with 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |η| < 1.0 to mimic STAR detector acceptance. The correlation coefficient and its components were computed using two- and three-subevent cumulant methods to minimize non-flow effects.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: √sNN = 19.5, 27, 62.4, and 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Specific shear viscosity values used in the hybrid model: η/s = 0.08 (for √sNN = 200 and 62.5 GeV), η/s = 0.12 (for √sNN = 27.0 GeV), and η/s = 0.15 (for √sNN = 19.5 GeV)
- Transverse momentum range for charged hadrons: 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity acceptance: |η| < 1.0
- Fluid to particle transition switching energy density: εSW = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Comparison models: AMPT with η/s = 0.1 at √sNN = 200 GeV; EPOS with η/s = 0.08 at √sNN = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of ρ(v²₂,[pT]) in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using AMPT and EPOS models (Magdy 2021) by charting the beam-energy dependence across the RHIC beam energy scan range. The results complement prior hydrodynamic and transport model investigations of the correlation coefficient by Bozek (2016, 2020), Giacalone (2017, 2020), Schenke (2020), and Lim (2021), and provide predictions that could be tested against experimental measurements from the STAR collaboration to enable precision extraction of the temperature and baryon chemical-potential dependence of η/s.
