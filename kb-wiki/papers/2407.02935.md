# Properties of the QCD Matter -- An Experimental Review of Selected Results from RHIC BES Program

> In the paper, we discuss the development of the multi-gap resistive plate chamber Time-of-Flight (TOF) technology and the production of the STAR TOF detector in China at the beginning of the 21st century. Then we review recent experimental results from the first beam energy scan program (BES-I) at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Topics cover measurements of collectivity, chirality, criticality, global polarization, strangeness, heavy-flavor, di-lepton and light nuclei productions.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes selected experimental results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan phase-I (BES-I) program, but the authors explicitly state that the nature of the QCD phase transition and the exact temperature and density conditions remain unclear, despite the experimental evidence for a transition provided by RHIC.

## Method

The paper reviews measurements from the STAR experiment at RHIC using data from Au+Au collisions collected during the BES-I program across a range of collision energies. The analysis relies on particle identification via the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) for energy loss measurements and the Multi-gap Resistive Plate Chamber Time-of-Flight (TOF) detector for velocity measurements, enabling identification of pions, kaons, protons, hyperons, phi mesons, and light nuclei. The review covers measurements of particle spectra, yields, chemical freeze-out parameters, baryon stopping, and strangeness production.

## Key numbers

- Systematic time resolution of the STAR TOF detector: approximately 80 ps (MRPC intrinsic resolution approximately 60 ps)
- With 2-sigma separation using TOF, protons/(pions + kaons) identified up to 3 GeV/c and kaons/pions up to 1.6 GeV/c; without TOF these limits were 1.0 GeV/c and 0.7 GeV/c respectively
- 16 candidates of anti-helium-4 (4He-bar) identified using combined TPC and TOF measurements
- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tch becomes constant at approximately 160 MeV after sqrt(sNN) = 11.5 GeV in central Au+Au collisions
- MRPC intrinsic time resolution reached 16.8 ps with 32 gas gaps of 0.104 mm width
- Final yield of MRPC module production reached 95 percent with good stability and consistency
- Sealed MRPC time resolution better than 60 ps and efficiency larger than 97 percent

## Context

This review extends the experimental program initiated by the STAR collaboration at RHIC (STAR 2010) and builds upon earlier measurements from AGS and CERN experiments (Ahmad 1991, Ahmad 1998, E917 2001, Albergo 2002, NA49 2008, NA57 2006). The results provide input for thermal models (Andronic 2005, Cleymans 2006) and transport models (UrQMD, HSD) to constrain the QCD phase diagram. The paper enables future comparisons with the RHIC BES phase-II program, the STAR fixed-target program, and next-generation facilities such as CBM and CEE.
