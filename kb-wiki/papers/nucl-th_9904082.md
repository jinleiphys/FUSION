# Elliptic Flow Based on a Relativistic Hydrodynamic Model

> Based on the (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model, the space-time evolution of hot and dense nuclear matter produced in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions is discussed. The elliptic flow parameter v_2 is obtained by Fourier analysis of the azimuthal distribution of pions and protons which are emitted from the freeze-out hypersurface. As a function of rapidity, the pion and proton elliptic flow parameters both have a peak at midrapidity.

# Elliptic Flow Based on a Relativistic Hydrodynamic Model

## Key claim

Elliptic flow parameter v_2 for both pions and protons has a peak at midrapidity in non-central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy, based on a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model. However, the author explicitly states these results are only preliminary because the initial parameters were chosen "by hand" rather than fitted to experimental data, and cautions readers not to take the results quantitatively.

## Method

The author numerically solves the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a perfect fluid without assuming cylindrical symmetry or Bjorken's boost-invariant solution, using an extended version of the Piecewise Parabolic Method (PPM). The equation of state features a first-order phase transition between a hadronic phase (Hagedorn statistical bootstrap model with excluded volume approximation) and a QGP phase (massless free u, d, s-quarks and gluon gas), matched by Gibbs' condition. The momentum distribution of pions and protons is obtained via the Cooper-Frye formula with freeze-out temperature 140 MeV, and the elliptic flow parameter v_2 is extracted from Fourier analysis of the azimuthal distribution.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter: b = 7 fm for non-central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy
- Initial energy density at z=0: E_0 = 2500 MeV/fm^3
- Hagedorn temperature: T_H = 155 MeV
- Bag constant: B^(1/4) = 230 MeV
- Critical temperature: T_C = 159 MeV at zero baryon density
- Mixed phase pressure: P_mix = 70.9 MeV/fm^3 at zero baryon density
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Initial time: t_0 = 1.4 fm
- Diffuseness parameter: delta_r = 0.3 fm
- Numerical error in v_2 for central collisions (b=0 fm): less than 10^(-1) percent
- Numerical error in energy and baryon density conservation: less than one percent

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic approaches by Ollitrault (1992) and Shuryak (1990s) that assumed Bjorken's boost-invariant solution and solved only in the transverse plane, by performing a full (3+1)-dimensional simulation. The results appear to contrast with experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration, which showed elliptic flow slightly peaked at medium-high rapidity rather than at midrapidity. The author suggests future work should fix initial parameters using experimental rapidity distributions or a parton cascade model such as VNI (Geiger, 1990s) to make the hydrodynamic model predictive.
