# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Light Nuclei

> Accurate quantum Monte Carlo calculations of ground and low-lying excited states of light p-shell nuclei are now possible for realistic nuclear Hamiltonians that fit nucleon-nucleon scattering data. At present, results for more than 30 different (J^pi;T) states, plus isobaric analogs, in A \leq 8 nuclei have been obtained with an excellent reproduction of the experimental energy spectrum. These microscopic calculations show that nuclear structure, including both single-particle and clustering aspects, can be explained starting from elementary two- and three-nucleon interactions. Various density and momentum distributions, electromagnetic form factors, and spectroscopic factors have also been computed, as well as electroweak capture reactions of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo methods can now compute ground and low-lying excited states of light p-shell nuclei (A ≤ 8) with an accuracy that reproduces the experimental energy spectrum, but the methods are limited to systems of A ≤ 12 because computational cost grows rapidly with each added nucleon.

## Method
The authors use Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) to construct trial wave functions with two- and three-body correlation operators acting on antisymmetrized basis states, then apply Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) to project out the exact ground state via Euclidean propagation. The Hamiltonians include the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential (fit to 4301 pp and np scattering data points with χ² per datum of 1.09) plus either the Urbana IX or Illinois series of three-nucleon potentials. Expectation values are evaluated by Metropolis Monte Carlo integration over configuration space and operator order, with wave functions represented as arrays of complex numbers whose size grows as 2^A × (A choose Z).

## Key numbers
- More than 30 different (J^π; T) states plus isobaric analogs in A ≤ 8 nuclei have been computed, with excellent reproduction of the experimental energy spectrum.
- The Argonne v18 potential was fit to 1787 pp and 2514 np scattering data points in the range 0–350 MeV, plus the nn scattering length and deuteron binding energy, achieving χ² per datum of 1.09.
- GFMC binding energies can be calculated with an accuracy of better than 2%.
- The wave function vector for ⁸Li has 14,336 complex numbers in the charge-conserving basis, reduced to 7,168 in the isospin-conserving basis.
- The VMC wave function for ⁸Li requires diagonalization of a 7×7 matrix of LS components.

## Context
This work extends the exact few-nucleon methods (Faddeev for A=3, Green's function Monte Carlo for ⁴He) to the p-shell, where previous descriptions relied on the shell model of Cohen and Kurath (1960s) or the G-matrix no-core shell model of Navratil, Vary, and Barrett (2000). The calculations demonstrate that nuclear structure, including clustering aspects like α+d in ⁶Li, can be predicted from elementary two- and three-nucleon interactions without effective charges. The results enable first-principles studies of electroweak capture reactions of astrophysical interest and provide reference points for developing Skyrme interactions for neutron-rich nuclei.
