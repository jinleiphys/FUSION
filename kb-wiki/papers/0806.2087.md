# Probing the low-x structure of nuclear matter with diffractive hadron production in pA collisions

> We argue that hadron production in coherent diffraction of proton on a heavy nucleus provides a very sensitive probe of the low-x QCD dynamics. This process probes the BFKL dynamics in proton and the non-linear gluon evolution in nucleus. We calculate the diffractive hadron production cross sections in the RHIC and LHC kinematic regions. To study the nuclear effects we introduce the diffractive nuclear modification factor. We show that unlike the nuclear modification factor for inclusive hadron production that has a very interesting dynamics at RHIC but is expected to be almost completely saturated at the LHC, the nuclear modification factor for diffractive production exhibits a non-trivial behavior both at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the nuclear modification factor for diffractive hadron production in pA collisions exhibits a non-trivial behavior at both RHIC and LHC energies, unlike the inclusive nuclear modification factor which is expected to be almost completely saturated at the LHC; this claim is conditional on the approximation that the proton light-cone wavefunction can be modeled by a system of color dipoles and that the pA cross section can be approximated by a quarkonium-nucleus cross section with an effective dipole size of twice the proton radius.

## Method

The authors model diffractive gluon production in pA collisions by approximating the proton as a color dipole system and using the Color Glass Condensate framework, relating the pA cross section to the quarkonium-nucleus cross section via the relation dσ^{qqqA}/d²k_T dy ≈ (3/2) dσ^{q\bar qA}/d²k_T dy. They calculate the diffractive cross section using the BFKL equation for the dipole density in the proton and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for the forward elastic dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude, parameterizing the latter with the KKT model that incorporates the anomalous dimension in different kinematic regions. The diffractive nuclear modification factor R^{pA}_diff is then evaluated by normalizing the pA cross section to A times the pp cross section, with the pp case obtained by taking the A→1 limit.

## Key numbers

- The diffractive nuclear modification factor in the quasi-classical approximation at high transverse momenta approaches R^{pA}_diff(k_T,0) → A^{1/3} exp(-(1/4)A^{1/3} ln A^{1/3}) as k_T → ∞, with Q_{s0}² = A^{1/3} Λ² and Λ a non-perturbative momentum scale.
- In the double logarithmic approximation for hard gluons with k_T ≫ Q_s, the nuclear modification factor scales as R^{pA}_diff(k_T,y) ≈ A^{1/3} [1 - √(2\bar α_s y) (ln(Q_{s0}/Λ))/√(ln(k_T/Λ))] for k_T ≫ Q_geom.
- The saturation scale is given by Q_s²(y) = Λ² A^{1/3} e^{λ y} (√s/200 GeV)^λ with parameters Λ = 0.6 GeV and λ = 0.3 fixed by DIS data, and the initial saturation scale is defined at y_0 = 0.5.
- In the extended geometric scaling region Q_s < k_T < Q_geom, the nuclear modification factor approximately satisfies R^{pA}_diff(k_T,y) ∼ A^{1/3} (R^{pA}_incl(k_T,y))².
- On the critical line k_T = Q_s(y) at forward rapidities, R^{pA}_diff(Q_s(y),y) ∼ exp(-4√(\bar α_s λ) y), implying strong suppression in the forward direction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical analysis of coherent diffractive gluon production in onium-heavy nucleus collisions (Li, 2008) and applies it to proton-nucleus collisions by modeling the proton as a color dipole system, following the phenomenological approach of Avsar (2005-2007) for approximating the proton light-cone wavefunction. The study contrasts with the inclusive nuclear modification factor R^{pA}_incl discussed by Kharzeev (2003), which shows Cronin enhancement at RHIC but is expected to saturate at LHC; the diffractive factor instead exhibits strong rapidity dependence at both energies due to higher twist contributions. The results enable discrimination between different models of low-x dynamics by comparing inclusive and diffractive hadron production across the wide kinematic range of RHIC and LHC, particularly regarding the A-dependence and energy-dependence of higher twist contributions.
