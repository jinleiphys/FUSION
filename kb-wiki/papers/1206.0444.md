# Influence of Fractal Embedding in Three-Dimensional Euclidean Space on Wave Propagation in Electro- Chromodynamics

> In this paper two zero-dimensional compact sets with equal topological and fractal dimensions but embedded in Euclidean space by different ways are under study. Diffraction of plane electromagnetic wave propagated and reflected by fractal surfaces is considered for each of these compact sets placed in vacuum. It is obtained, that the embedding of compact influences on characteristics of wave in final state. Thus, the embedding of Cantor set in Euclidean space is additional property of a fractal which can be important both for applications of fractal electrodynamics and for physics of strong interactions.

## Key claim
The embedding of a zero-dimensional fractal compact set in three-dimensional Euclidean space (wild versus tame) can influence the physical characteristics of wave propagation and intensity distribution, even when the topological and Hausdorff dimensions are identical. This is a qualitative hypothesis; the paper does not provide quantitative verification.

## Method
Two zero-dimensional compact sets with equal topological and Hausdorff dimensions were constructed: the wild Antoine compact set (A) and a tame compact set (C) homeomorphic to A. Diffraction of a plane electromagnetic wave (wavelength λ much smaller than the initial torus diameter) was considered qualitatively for each fractal surface placed in vacuum, with the wave propagating through and reflecting from the fractal structures. The analysis compared diffraction from obstacles (in the wild case) versus diffraction from splits (in the tame case) at successive construction steps.

## Key numbers
- Average linear size of instantons/sphalerons in QCD: ~1/3 fm.
- Volume of matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions: V_f ~ 2.5 × 10^3 fm^3 at RHIC energy √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- Volume of matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions: V_f ~ 5.0 × 10^3 fm^3 at LHC energy √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.
- Estimated number of sphaleron-type clusters in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: about 400.
- The construction step number k̃ is defined by the condition diam T_{k̃...1} < (k̃+1)^{-1} < λ, where λ is the wavelength.

## Context
This work extends the study of fractal electrodynamics by considering not only fractal dimension but also the embedding type (wild vs. tame) of zero-dimensional fractals in three-dimensional Euclidean space. It builds on the construction of the Antoine compact set (Antoine, 1921) and the tame compact set (Sandrakova, 1971), and on the theorem relating Lipschitz homeomorphisms to equal fractal dimensions (Crownover, 1999; Okorokov, 2005). The paper suggests that embedding properties could be relevant for understanding wave interactions with topologically non-trivial QCD vacuum structures (instantons, merons, fractons) and for applications in fractal electrodynamics and strong interaction physics. No experimental study of embedding effects on wave propagation exists; the paper calls for future quantitative investigations.
