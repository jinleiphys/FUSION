# Lambda Lambda-XiN Coupling Effects in Light Hypernuclei

> The significance of $ΛΛ$-$Ξ$N coupling in double-$Λ$ hypernuclei has been studied. The Pauli suppression effect due to this coupling in $^6_{ΛΛ}$He has been found to be 0.43 MeV for the coupling strength of the NSC97e potential. This indicates that the free-space $ΛΛ$ interaction is stronger by about $5^{\circ}$ phase shift than that deduced from the empirical data of $^6_{ΛΛ}$He without including the Pauli suppression effect. In $^5_{ΛΛ}$He and $^5_{ΛΛ}$H, an attractive term arising from $ΛΛ$-$Ξ$N conversion is enhanced by the formation of an alpha particle in intermediate $Ξ$ states. According to this enhancement, we have found that the $ΛΛ$ binding energy ($ΔB_{ΛΛ}$) of $^5_{ΛΛ}$He is about 0.27 MeV larger than that of $^6_{ΛΛ}$He for the NSC97e coupling strength. This finding deviates from a general picture that the heavier is the core nucleus, the larger is $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$.

# Lambda Lambda-XiN Coupling Effects in Light Hypernuclei

## Key claim

The free-space Lambda-Lambda interaction is stronger than previously deduced from the Nagara event data for 6_Lambda_Lambda_He, because the Pauli suppression effect from Lambda-Lambda to Xi-N coupling reduces the apparent binding energy by 0.43 MeV for the NSC97e coupling strength; consequently, the Lambda-Lambda binding energy of 5_Lambda_Lambda_He is about 0.27 MeV larger than that of 6_Lambda_Lambda_He, contradicting the general expectation that heavier core nuclei produce larger Delta B_Lambda_Lambda.

## Method

The authors treat double-Lambda hypernuclei as three-body systems (Lambda + Lambda + core nucleus) and construct single-channel Lambda-Lambda interactions from the diagonal and off-diagonal terms of strangeness S = -2 coupled interactions, using Shinmura's potentials that are phase-shift equivalents to the Nijmegen soft-core NSC97e and hard-core NHC-D and NHC-F potentials. They perform three-body calculations with Gaussian basis functions expanded over rearrangement channels in Jacobi coordinates, incorporating the Pauli suppression effect via projection operators on 0s nucleon states in the alpha particle, and separately evaluating an enhancement effect from alpha particle formation in intermediate Xi states for five-body systems.

## Key numbers

- Pauli suppression effect in 6_Lambda_Lambda_He: 0.43 MeV for the NSC97e coupling strength, 0.09 MeV for NHC-D, and 0.88 MeV for NHC-F
- Free-space Lambda-Lambda interaction (V_Lambda_Lambda_e2) gives Delta B_Lambda_Lambda(6_Lambda_Lambda_He) = 1.44 MeV, which reduces to the empirical value of 1.01 MeV when Pauli suppression is included
- Delta B_Lambda_Lambda of 5_Lambda_Lambda_He with NSC97e coupling: 1.28 MeV, which is 0.27 MeV larger than the 1.01 MeV of 6_Lambda_Lambda_He
- Lambda-Lambda scattering lengths: a_Lambda_Lambda_e1 = -0.73 fm, a_Lambda_Lambda_e2 = -1.03 fm, a_Lambda_Lambda_F1 = -0.67 fm, a_Lambda_Lambda_F2 = -1.32 fm
- Maximum Lambda-Lambda phase shifts: 12 degrees for V_Lambda_Lambda_e1, 17 degrees for V_Lambda_Lambda_e2, and 22 degrees for V_Lambda_Lambda_F2
- Enhancement effect Delta V_alpha_e in 5_Lambda_Lambda_He: -0.41 MeV (attractive) for NSC97e
- Pauli suppression effect Delta V_Pauli_e in 5_Lambda_Lambda_He: 0.23 MeV for NSC97e
- Net coupling effect in 5_Lambda_Lambda_He: 0.18 MeV attraction for NSC97e; in 5_Lambda_Lambda_H: 0.07 MeV repulsion for NSC97e

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lambda-Lambda interactions in double-Lambda hypernuclei following the unambiguous observation of 6_Lambda_Lambda_He (the "Nagara" event) in the KEK E373 experiment. The authors address the omission of Pauli suppression effects in prior Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2000), who predicted that heavier core nuclei yield larger Delta B_Lambda_Lambda. The results enable experimental determination of the Lambda-Lambda to Xi-N coupling strength through future observations of 5_Lambda_Lambda_He and 5_Lambda_Lambda_H, which could be produced via the (K-,K+) reaction on a 7Li target as proposed by Kumagai-Fuse and Akaishi.
