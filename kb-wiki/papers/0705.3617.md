# Final State Interaction Effects in Semi-inclusive Deep Inelastic processes A(e,e'p)X off the deuteron and complex nuclei

> The effects of the final state interaction (FSI) in semi inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering processes $A(e,e'p)X$ off nuclei are investigated in details. Proton production is described within the spectator and the target fragmentation mechanisms whose relevance to the experimental study of the deep inelastic structure functions of bound nucleons and the non perturbative hadronization process is analyzed. Particular attention is paid to the deuteron target within kinematical conditions corresponding to the available and forthcoming experimental data at Jlab. We argue that there are kinematical regions where FSI effects are minimized, allowing for a reliable investigation of the DIS structure functions, and regions where the interaction of the quark-gluon debris with nucleons is maximized, which makes it possible to study hadronization mechanisms. Nuclear structure has been described by means of realistic wave functions and spectral functions and the final state interaction has been treated within an eikonal approximation approach which takes into account the rescattering of the quark-gluon debris with the residual nucleus and, in the case of complex nuclei, within an optical potential approach to account for the FSI of the struck proton.

## Key claim

The paper argues that there are specific kinematical regions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclei where final state interaction effects are minimized, allowing reliable extraction of bound nucleon structure functions, and other regions where FSI effects are maximized, enabling study of hadronization mechanisms; this claim is conditional on the validity of the eikonal approximation for the debris-nucleon interaction and on the Bjorken limit kinematics.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section for the A(e,e'p)X process using the spectator mechanism and the target fragmentation mechanism, with nuclear structure described by realistic wave functions and spectral functions. For the deuteron, final state interaction was treated within an eikonal approximation that accounts for rescattering of the quark-gluon debris off the spectator proton using an effective cross section sigma_eff(z,Q^2,x) that depends on the distance traveled by the hadronizing system. For complex nuclei, the FSI of the debris with the residual A-2 system was treated with the same eikonal approach, while the FSI of the recoiling proton with the A-2 system was treated within an optical potential approach (Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation).

## Key numbers

- The analysis was performed at Bjorken x = 0.6, with kinematics corresponding to JLab experiments at 12 GeV incident electron energy.
- For the deuteron target, the ratio of PWIA cross section to cross section including FSI was calculated as a function of spectator momentum and emission angle, showing that at low momenta and parallel kinematics FSI effects are minimized.
- The target fragmentation contribution was quantified by the ratio R_tf = (dσ_tf + dσ_sp^PWIA)/dσ_sp^PWIA, which was found to contribute only in a very narrow forward direction and for large values of the spectator momentum.
- First experimental data from JLab at initial electron energy E = 5.765 GeV are available (Kuhn, 2007), but the authors note their approach corresponds to the Bjorken limit and requires generalization to finite Q^2 and nu for direct comparison.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical treatments of the spectator mechanism in SIDIS off the deuteron by Ciofi degli Atti, Kaptari, and collaborators (2004) that considered FSI effects, now also including the target fragmentation mechanism and generalizing to complex nuclei. The paper builds on the two-nucleon correlation model for nuclear spectral functions developed by Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996) and the hadronization model for effective cross sections that successfully described grey track production in DIS off nuclei (Ciofi degli Atti, Kopeliovich, and Kaptari, 2003). The results enable experimental programs at JLab (the BONUS experiment) to extract bound nucleon structure functions in kinematics where FSI are minimized, and to study nonperturbative hadronization mechanisms in kinematics where FSI are maximized.
