# Continuum Effects and the Trojan Horse Mechanism in Halo Nuclei-Induced Reactions: Implications for Heavy Isotope Synthesis

> Nonelastic breakup (NEB) reactions induced by the halo nucleus $^{11}$Be on $^{64}$Zn at 28.7 MeV are investigated using the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model combined with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method. NEB cross sections calculated with full CDCC wave functions (including continuum states), ground-state-only CDCC wave functions, and single-channel calculations are compared. The results indicate that continuum effects are negligible and that NEB cross sections are dominated by the ground-state contribution. This validates the use of simpler models like the distorted wave Born approximation for such reactions. Additionally, by varying the binding energy in a toy model, the feasibility of using halo nuclei in the Trojan Horse Method (THM) for synthesizing heavy isotopes is explored. It is demonstrated that THM significantly enhances sub-barrier fusion cross sections due to the weak binding of halo nuclei, offering a promising approach for the synthesis of new elements.

## Key claim

Continuum effects are negligible for nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections in the $^{11}$Be + $^{64}$Zn reaction at 28.7 MeV, with the ground-state contribution dominating; however, the Trojan Horse Method using halo nuclei can enhance sub-barrier fusion cross sections by at least three orders of magnitude, contingent on the weak binding of the projectile.

## Method

The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model was combined with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method to calculate NEB cross sections for the $^{64}$Zn($^{11}$Be, $^{10}$Be$X$) and $^{64}$Zn($^{11}$Be, $nX$) reactions at 28.7 MeV laboratory energy. Three formulations of the three-body scattering wave function were compared: full CDCC wave functions including continuum states, CDCC wave functions using only the ground state component, and single-channel calculations considering only the projectile ground state. A toy model was employed by manually adjusting the binding energy of $^{11}$Be from 0.504 MeV to 2.504 MeV to explore the Trojan Horse Method enhancement.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 28.7 MeV in the laboratory frame for the $^{11}$Be + $^{64}$Zn reaction
- Actual binding energy of $^{11}$Be: $E_b = 0.504$ MeV; toy model increased to $E_b = 2.504$ MeV
- Integrating the NEB cross section over the energy range 5 to 10 MeV yields 26 mb
- Direct reaction cross section at 7 MeV is on the order of $10^{-6}$ mb, indicating at least three orders of magnitude enhancement via the Trojan Horse Method
- CDCC calculations included neutron-$^{10}$Be states up to partial waves $\ell = 0-3$, covering excitation energies up to 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the $^{11}$Be + $^{64}$Zn reaction by Di Pietro (2019), Druet (2012), Keeley (2010), and Di Pietro (2012), which highlighted strong continuum coupling effects on elastic scattering and substantial inclusive breakup cross sections. The present results contrast with the model of Rangel (2020), where continuum effects play a different role. The validation of simpler DWBA approaches for NEB cross sections simplifies future modeling, while the demonstrated Trojan Horse Method enhancement opens pathways for synthesizing neutron-rich superheavy isotopes, potentially using giant halo nuclei as proposed by Meng (1998, 2002) and Terasaki (2006).
