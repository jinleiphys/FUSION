# Double Beta Decay in pn-QRPA Model with Isospin and SU(4) Symmetry Constraints

> The transition matrix elements for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ double beta decays are calculated for $^{48}Ca$, $^{76}Ge $, $^{82}Se$, $^{100}Mo$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$ nuclei, using a $δ$-interaction. As a guide, to fix the particle-particle interaction strengths, we exploit the fact that the missing symmetries of the mean field approximation are restored in the random phase approximation by the residual interaction. Thus, the T=1, S=0 and T=0, S=1 coupling strengths have been estimated by invoking the partial restoration of the isospin and Wigner SU(4) symmetries, respectively. When this recipe is strictly applied, the calculation is consistent with the experimental limit for the $2ν$ lifetime of $^{48}Ca$ and it also correctly reproduces the $2ν$ lifetime of $^{82}Se$. In this way, however, the two-neutrino matrix elements for the remaining nuclei are either underestimated (for $^{76}Ge$ and $^{100}Mo$) or overestimated (for $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$) approximately by a factor of 3. With a comparatively small variation ($<10%$) of the spin-triplet parameter, near the value suggested by the SU(4) symmetry, it is possible to reproduce the measured $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ in all the cases. The upper limit for the effective neutrino mass, as obtained from the theoretical estimates of $0ν$ matrix elements, is $<m_ν>\cong 1$ eV. The dependence of the nuclear matrix elements on the size of the configuration space has been also analyzed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate double beta decay matrix elements using the pn-QRPA model with a delta interaction, fixing the particle-particle coupling strengths by requiring partial restoration of isospin and Wigner SU(4) symmetries. When this symmetry-based recipe is strictly applied, only the two-neutrino lifetimes of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁸²Se are correctly reproduced, while the matrix elements for the other four nuclei are off by approximately a factor of three; however, a small variation (less than 10%) of the spin-triplet parameter near the SU(4)-suggested value can reproduce all measured two-neutrino half-lives.

## Method

The authors performed pn-QRPA calculations for 0⁺→0⁺ double beta decays in ⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹²⁸Te, and ¹³⁰Te using a delta interaction. The spin-singlet (T=1, S=0) particle-particle strength was fixed at s=1 by invoking isospin symmetry restoration, and the spin-triplet (T=0, S=1) strength was set near the value t_sym suggested by partial SU(4) symmetry restoration, with the minimum of the Gamow-Teller β⁺ strength S₋(1⁺) used as the criterion. For ⁴⁸Ca, three different configuration spaces (2ħω–3ħω, 0ħω–3ħω, and 0ħω–4ħω) were compared, while for the other nuclei an eleven-dimensional space covering the 3ħω and 4ħω shells plus the 0h₉/₂ and 0h₁₁/₂ orbitals was used.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca at t = t_sym, the calculated 2ν matrix element M₂ν is consistent with the experimental limit; the unperturbed 0ν matrix element M₀ν⁰ is dominated by m₀ν⁰(1⁺) ≈ 0.9 MeV⁻¹, giving total M₀ν⁰ ≈ -0.45 MeV⁻¹.
- For ⁷⁶Ge and ¹⁰⁰Mo at t = t_sym, the calculated M₂ν values are underestimated by approximately a factor of 3 compared to experiment.
- For ¹²⁸Te and ¹³⁰Te at t = t_sym, the calculated M₂ν values are overestimated by approximately a factor of 3 compared to experiment.
- The upper limit for the effective neutrino mass from the 0ν matrix elements is ⟨m_ν⟩ ≅ 1 eV.
- The variation of the spin-triplet parameter t needed to fit all measured 2ν half-lives is less than 10% relative to t_sym.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA approach of Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) and the Pasadena group (Engel et al., 1988) by introducing a symmetry-based method to fix the particle-particle interaction strengths, rather than calibrating them against individual β⁺ decay data. The results contrast with those of the Tübingen and Heidelberg groups (Tomoda et al., 1987; Muto et al., 1989, 1991), who used realistic G-matrix interactions and found less sensitivity of the 0ν matrix elements to the particle-particle channel. The study enables improved neutrino mass limits from 0νββ decay by demonstrating that the symmetry-constrained QRPA yields ⟨m_ν⟩ ≅ 1 eV, and it highlights the need to better understand the role of forbidden virtual states and configuration space completeness in future calculations.
