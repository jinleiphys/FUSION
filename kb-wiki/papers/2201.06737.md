# $K^- d\rightarrow πΛN$ reaction for studying charge symmetry breaking in the $ΛN$ interaction

> We discuss charge symmetry breaking in the $ΛN$ interaction. In order to investigate the $Λp$ and $Λn$ interactions at low energies, we propose to utilize the $K^-d\to π^-Λp$ and $K^-d\to π^0Λn$ reactions. They are symmetric under the exchange of both the pion and nucleon isospin partners in the final states. This advantage allows us to study charge symmetry breaking in the $ΛN$ interaction. We calculate the differential cross sections of these reactions with stopped kaons theoretically and discuss the possibility to extract the scattering length and effective range of the $ΛN$ scattering. With stopped kaons, the $ΛN$ interaction takes place dominantly in the spin-triplet state thanks to the deuteron spin and $s$-wave dominance of the scattering amplitudes at the low energy. We find that the ratio of the differential cross sections as a function of the $ΛN$ invariant mass between the two reactions is useful for revealing how charge symmetry breaking, or isospin symmetry breaking appears in the low-energy $ΛN$ scattering.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the ratio of differential cross sections between the \(K^- d \to \pi^- \Lambda p\) and \(K^- d \to \pi^0 \Lambda n\) reactions, measured with stopped kaons and with the \(\Lambda N\) system in the spin-triplet state, can reveal charge symmetry breaking in the low-energy \(\Lambda N\) interaction, provided the background contributions are suppressed by restricting the angular integration of the final \(\Lambda\) to \(\theta^*_\Lambda < \pi/2\).

## Method

The authors calculated the \(\Lambda N\) invariant mass spectra for both reactions using a theoretical framework based on Feynman diagrams that include \(\Lambda\) exchange (foreground), \(\Sigma\) exchange, impulse approximation, kaon exchange, and pion exchange. The \(\Lambda N\) scattering amplitude was parametrized by the scattering length and effective range, the \(\bar K N \to \pi Y\) amplitudes were taken from a chiral unitary approach, and the deuteron wavefunction was taken from the CD-Bonn potential. The cross sections were evaluated for stopped kaons (laboratory momentum \(0 \ \mathrm{MeV}/c\)) and the angular integration of the final \(\Lambda\) was restricted to \(\theta^*_\Lambda \in [0, \pi/2]\) to suppress background.

## Key numbers

*   The spin-triplet \(\Lambda p\) scattering length used is \(a^t_{\Lambda p} = -1.56\ \mathrm{fm}\) and effective range is \(r^t_{\Lambda p} = 3.7\ \mathrm{fm}\), taken from the experimental analysis of the \(p+p\rightarrow K^+ +(\Lambda+p)\) reaction by Budzanowski (2010).
*   The \(\Sigma N\) scattering length from the Nijmegen NSC97f potential used is \(a_{\Sigma N} = 1.68 - i 2.35\ \mathrm{fm}\); the alternative value from the Julich '04 potential is \(a_{\Sigma N} = -3.83 - i 3.01\ \mathrm{fm}\).
*   The ratio of the \(\bar K N \to \pi \Lambda\) amplitudes, \(\mathcal{R}_V(W)\), deviates significantly from unity (the isospin-symmetric value) in the c.m. energy range \(1420\ \mathrm{MeV} < W < 1450\ \mathrm{MeV}\), indicating large isospin breaking in that region.
*   The excitation energy of the first \(1^-\) state differs by \(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) between \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H}\) and \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He}\), compared to a difference of \(0.071\ \mathrm{MeV}\) between \(^3\mathrm{H}\) and \(^3\mathrm{He}\) after electromagnetic corrections (Miller, 1994).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of the \(K^- d \to \pi \Lambda N\) reaction, which were historically used to investigate \(\Lambda p\) scattering and \(\Sigma N\) interactions (e.g., Dalitz (1979), Toker (1981)), by applying modern chiral unitary amplitudes for the \(\bar K N\) interaction (Oset, 2001) and explicitly introducing isospin breaking in the \(\Lambda N\) interaction. The proposal directly addresses the need to measure the unconstrained \(\Lambda n\) scattering parameters, motivated by the large charge symmetry breaking observed in \(A=4\) mirror hypernuclei (J-PARC E13, 2015; A1 Collaboration, 2015). The work enables a dedicated experimental extraction of the \(\Lambda n\) scattering length and effective range, and a test of theoretical predictions for charge symmetry breaking in the \(\Lambda N\) system, such as the \(\Delta a^{\rm CSB}\) values calculated by Haidenbauer (2021).
