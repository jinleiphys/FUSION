# Chiral and angular momentum content of mesons

> First, we overview the present status of the effective chiral restoration in excited hadrons and an alternative explanation of the symmetry observed in the highly excited hadrons. Then we discuss a method how to define and measure in a gauge invariant manner the chiral and angular momentum content of mesons at different resolution scales, including the infrared scale, where mass is generated. We illustrate this method by presenting results on chiral and angular momentum content of rho and rho' mesons obtained in dynamical lattice simulations. The chiral symmetry is strongly broken in the rho(770) and neither the a_1(1260) nor the h_1(1170) can be considered as its chiral partners. Its angular momentum content in the infrared is approximately the 3S1 partial wave, in agreement with the quark model language. However, in its first excitation, rho(1450), the chiral symmetry breaking is much weaker and in the infrared this state belongs predominantly to the (1/2,1/2) chiral representation. This state is dominated in the infrared by the 3D1 partial wave and cannot be considered as the first radial excitation of the rho(770)-meson, in contrast to the quark model.

# Chiral and angular momentum content of mesons

## Key claim
The paper argues that effective chiral restoration is observed in the first excited rho meson, ρ(1450), but not in the ground state ρ(770); however, the claim is hedged by noting that the precise value of the ratio a_V/a_T for ρ(1450) at large smearing widths R ~ 0.8–1 fm is not available, and the observed trend is only "indicative" of a smooth onset of effective chiral restoration.

## Method
The authors performed dynamical lattice QCD simulations using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator with two mass-degenerate light sea quarks. They computed a cross-correlation matrix from four interpolators (vector and pseudotensor operators with two different Gaussian smearing widths, R ≈ 0.34 fm and 0.67 fm) and extracted the masses and eigenvector coefficients for the ground state and first excited state of the rho meson. The ratio of vector to pseudotensor couplings, a_V/a_T, was used to determine the chiral representation content, and a unitary transformation mapped this onto the ²S⁺¹L_J partial wave basis in a gauge-invariant manner.

## Key numbers
- The ratio a_V/a_T for the ground state ρ(770) at the smallest resolution scale R ≈ 0.67 fm is approximately 1.2, indicating a strong mixture of chiral representations.
- The ground state ρ(770) in the infrared is approximately a ³S₁ partial wave, consistent with the quark model.
- The first excited state ρ(1450) shows a strong dependence of a_V/a_T on the resolution scale, with a very small value at large R, implying dominance of the (1/2,1/2)_b chiral representation.
- The ρ(1450) in the infrared is dominated by the ³D₁ partial wave, contradicting the quark model prediction of a radial ³S₁ excitation.
- Two smearing widths were used: R ≈ 0.34 fm (narrow) and 0.67 fm (wide).

## Context
This work extends the study of effective chiral restoration in excited hadrons, previously observed in baryon and meson spectra (Glozman, 2007; Glozman, 2009), by providing a first-principles lattice determination of chiral and angular momentum content. It contradicts the quark model interpretation that ρ(1450) is a radial excitation of ρ(770) and instead supports the effective chiral restoration scenario. The method enables future lattice studies of the chiral and angular momentum decomposition of other mesons at infrared scales, and addresses the open question of whether the missing chiral partners for high-spin states are suppressed by centrifugal effects in scattering experiments (Glozman and Sarantsev, 2008; Klempt, 2009).
