# new type of pion interferometry formula

> New type of pion interferometry formulas is derived, in which not only the correlator but also the pion multiplicity is taken consideration. The method to get information about initial emission probability of the unsymmetrized Bosons is given.

# New Type of Pion Interferometry Formula

## Key claim
A new multipion interferometry formula is derived that depends not only on the two-particle correlator but also explicitly on the pion multiplicity distribution, enabling extraction of the initial emission probability of unsymmetrized bosons. The formula reproduces standard results only when pions are emitted independently; for non-independent emission, the correlation function deviates from the standard form.

## Method
The author constructs a multipion state with coefficients a_n connected to the pion multiplicity distribution, assuming a totally chaotic source with random phase factors. Using recurrence relations for the source distribution (modeled as a Gaussian in space and momentum with parameters R and Δ), the one-pion and two-pion inclusive distributions are computed, and the two-pion correlation function C₂(q) is derived from the ratio of the two-pion to the square of the one-pion inclusive distributions. Three different pion states (D1, D2, D3) corresponding to different choices of a_n are compared to study the effects of multipion Bose-Einstein symmetrization.

## Key numbers
- Source size parameter R = 5.3 fm and momentum width Δ = 0.18 GeV used for multiplicity distribution calculations (Fig. 1)
- Source size parameter R = 5 fm and momentum width Δ = 0.16 GeV used for momentum distribution and two-pion interferometry calculations (Figs. 2 and 3)
- Mean unsymmetrized pion multiplicity ⟨n⟩ = 18 for D1, D2, and D3 in Fig. 1, achieved with input n₀ values of 18, 4.183, and 37.38 respectively
- Mean pion multiplicity ⟨n⟩ = 19 for momentum distribution calculations (Fig. 2)
- Mean pion multiplicity ⟨n⟩ = 17.7 for two-pion interferometry calculations (Fig. 3)
- For D1 state: C₂(q=0) = 2, C₂(q→∞) = 1
- For D2 state: C₂(q=0) = 2.19, C₂(q→∞) = 1.07
- For D3 state: C₂(q=0) = 1.97, C₂(q→∞) = 0.987
- Experimental heavy-ion results indicate C₂(q) ≈ 1.01–1.03 at q ∼ 300–400 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of multipion Bose-Einstein symmetrization effects by Pratt (1993), Csörgő and collaborators, and Zhang's own prior work, addressing the shortcoming that standard two-pion interferometry formulas ignore multipion symmetrization. The new formula enables distinguishing between different pion emission states (independent vs. correlated emission) through the behavior of the two-pion correlation function at both small and large relative momentum. The author notes that present heavy-ion experimental data favor the D1 state (independent emission) as the best model for analysis, and suggests that information about pion states can be obtained through the interferometry method.
