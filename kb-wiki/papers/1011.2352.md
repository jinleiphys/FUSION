# Potential for measurement of the tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron in storage-ring experiments with polarized beams

> Measurement of the tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron is of great interest, especially in connection with the possibilities of COSY and GSI. These polarizabilities can be measured in storage rings by the frozen spin method providing a disappearance of g-2 precession. This method will be used in the planned deuteron electric-dipole-moment experiment in storage rings. The tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron significantly influences the buildup of the vertical polarization in the above experiment. The spin interactions depending on the electric dipole moment, the tensor electric polarizability, and main systematical errors caused by field misalignments have very different symmetries. For the considered experimental conditions, the sensitivity to the deuteron EDM of $1\times10^{-29} e\cdot$cm corresponds to measuring the both of tensor polarizabilities with an accuracy of $δα_T\approxδβ_T\approx5\times10^{-42}$ cm$^3$. This conservative estimate can be improved by excluding the systematical error caused by the field instability which is negligible for the measurement of the tensor polarizabilities. To find the tensor magnetic polarizability, the horizontal components of the polarization vector should be measured.

## Key claim

The tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron can be measured in storage rings using the frozen spin method, with the sensitivity to the deuteron electric dipole moment (EDM) of \(1\times10^{-29}~e\cdot\)cm corresponding to measuring both tensor polarizabilities with an accuracy of \(\delta\alpha_T\approx\delta\beta_T\approx5\times10^{-42}\) cm\(^3\), a conservative estimate that can be improved by excluding systematic errors from field instability.

## Method

The authors derive a matrix Hamiltonian for spin-1 deuteron dynamics in storage rings under the frozen spin method, where the \(g-2\) precession is suppressed. They compute the evolution of the vector and tensor polarization components using cylindrical coordinates and a rotating frame, accounting for the tensor electric polarizability \(\alpha_T\) and tensor magnetic polarizability \(\beta_T\) via spin interactions quadratic in the spin. The analysis distinguishes the symmetry signatures of the EDM, tensor electric polarizability, and systematic errors from field misalignments by reversing the initial polarization direction and the beam direction.

## Key numbers

- Sensitivity to the deuteron EDM of \(1\times10^{-29}~e\cdot\)cm corresponds to measuring both tensor polarizabilities with accuracy \(\delta\alpha_T\approx\delta\beta_T\approx5\times10^{-42}\) cm\(^3\) under the considered experimental conditions.
- Theoretical predictions for the tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron are \(\alpha_T = -6.2\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (CGS), \(-6.8\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (JL), and \(3.2\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (FP).
- Theoretical estimate for the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron is \(\beta_T = 1.95\times10^{-40}\) cm\(^3\) (CGS, JL).
- With a tensor-polarized initial beam, the preliminary estimate of experimental accuracy improves to \(\delta\alpha_T\sim 10^{-43}\) cm\(^3\).
- For the tensor magnetic polarizability, achievable absolute precision is \(\delta\beta_T\sim 10^{-43}\) cm\(^3\), giving relative precision \(\delta\beta_T/\beta_T\sim 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical proposals by Baryshevsky and co-workers (2000s) for measuring tensor polarizabilities via spin oscillations and vertical polarization buildup, and provides detailed spin-dynamics formulas for the frozen spin method planned in the deuteron EDM experiment at storage rings (EDM Collaboration, 2010). The results enable high-precision measurements of deuteron tensor polarizabilities at facilities like COSY and GSI, and are applicable to any spin-1 nucleus, with implications for testing theories of spin-dependent nuclear forces.
