# Level-anticrossing in B(E2) anomaly (I)

> Recently, a new mechanism for explaining the B(E2) anomaly was given by F. Pan \emph{et al.} (PRC, 110, 054324, 2024), which is realized in the parameter region from the SU(3) symmetry limit to the O(6) symmetry limit, and seems to be not related to the SU(3) symmetry. However, through SU(3) analysis, a new technique proposed recently, we found that it is not so. The new mechanism is related to level-anticrossing phenomenon, which is related to level-crossing phenomenon in the SU(3) symmetry limit. By incorporating previous ideas, we have a more general explanatory framework for the B(E2) anomaly, which is important for understanding some higher-order interactions in the interacting boson model. Through analysis, it is shown that level-anticrossing in this mechanism mainly results from the third-order interaction $[\hat{L}\times \hat{Q}_χ \times \hat{L}]^{(0)}$. Finally, the B(E2) anomaly in $^{170}$Os is also discussed within this general framework.

## Key claim

The new mechanism for the B(E2) anomaly proposed by Pan et al. (2024), which appears unrelated to SU(3) symmetry because it occurs between the SU(3) and O(6) symmetry limits, is actually related to SU(3) symmetry in a hidden way: level-anticrossing in this parameter region is connected to level-crossing that occurs within the SU(3) symmetry limit when the third-order interaction parameter is varied.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3) analysis on the Hamiltonian used by Pan et al. (2024), which includes the third-order interaction $[\hat{L}\times \hat{Q}_{\chi} \times \hat{L}]^{(0)}$ alongside the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and the $d$-boson number operator. They varied the parameter $\chi$ from the SU(3) symmetry limit ($\chi=-\sqrt{7}/2$) to the O(6) symmetry limit ($\chi=0$) for boson number $N=9$, and also varied the strength $\eta$ of the third-order interaction, tracking the evolution of low-lying energy levels and E2 transition rates. The B(E2) anomaly in $^{170}$Os was discussed within this framework.

## Key numbers

- For $^{170}$Os parameters ($\kappa=30.0$ keV, $f=15.5$ keV, $\eta=-14.87$ keV), the $B_{4/2}$ value becomes $\leq 0.38$ (the experimental value for $^{170}$Os) in a parameter region around $\chi \approx -0.35$ between the SU(3) and O(6) symmetry limits.
- In the SU(3) symmetry limit with $\eta$ varying from 0 to -29.74 keV, level-crossing between the $4_1^+$ state and another $4^+$ state occurs at $\eta = -27.15$ keV, and between the $6_1^+$ state and another $6^+$ state at $\eta = -17.9$ keV.
- When $\eta = -29.74$ keV, the B(E2) anomaly occurs from the SU(3) symmetry limit and exists in a broader parameter region, with the left side of the dashed line corresponding to $B_{4/2} \leq 0.38$.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism proposed by Pan et al. (2024) for explaining the B(E2) anomaly, which was found in the parameter region from the SU(3) symmetry limit to the O(6) symmetry limit. The authors use the SU(3) analysis technique introduced by Cheng et al. (2025) to show that this new mechanism is actually related to level-crossing in the SU(3) symmetry limit, previously studied by Wang et al. (2020). The finding merges two previously distinct mechanisms (Wang et al., 2020 and Pan et al., 2024) into a more general explanatory framework, which is important for understanding higher-order interactions in the interacting boson model and for interpreting the B(E2) anomaly in nuclei such as $^{170}$Os.
