# Nuclear PDF constraints from p+Pb collisions at the LHC

> As the current nuclear PDF analyses are mainly constrained by fixed-target Drell-Yan and deeply inelastic scattering data only the quark nuclear modifications at fairly large $x$ values are in a good control. Inclusive pion production in d+Au collisions at RHIC provides some constraints for gluons but due to the limited kinematic reach of the data the gluon modifications remain uncertain especially at small values of $x$. In this talk, we discuss how the existing data from p+Pb collisions at the LHC can improve the nuclear PDF fits and which measurements would be sensitive to the small-$x$ gluons. In particular, we consider inclusive hadron production, compare this to direct photons, and show estimates of the effect of CMS dijet measurements to the uncertainty of nuclear gluon distributions.

# Nuclear PDF constraints from p+Pb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
Existing LHC p+Pb data on inclusive charged hadron production show a surprising 40% enhancement at high transverse momentum that cannot be explained by current nuclear PDF models, while CMS dijet data can reduce the uncertainty of gluon nuclear modifications at x > 0.01, and forward direct photon measurements would be needed to constrain small-x gluon shadowing.

## Method
The authors performed NLO pQCD calculations using CT10 proton PDFs with EPS09 nuclear modifications and multiple fragmentation functions to compute nuclear modification factors R_pPb for inclusive charged hadrons and pions in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV. They also computed direct photon cross sections at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV, comparing prompt and fragmentation components, and applied a Hessian reweighting analysis to CMS dijet data to estimate the impact on gluon nuclear modification uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- CMS inclusive charged hadron data show a 40% enhancement at p_T greater than approximately 20 GeV/c, well above any nPDF-based prediction
- ALICE charged hadron data at low p_T around 3 GeV/c overshoot EPS09-based predictions by about 20%
- For inclusive pion production at p_T = 5 GeV/c and eta = 0, 2, 4 at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV, the cross section receives contributions from a broad range of x_Pb values with suppression (shadowing) turning to enhancement (antishadowing) around x approximately 0.01
- In the Hessian reweighting analysis of CMS dijet data, applying a weight factor of 10 (compared to the factor of 20 used for RHIC pion data in EPS09) clearly reduces the estimated uncertainty of the gluon modification
- For isolated direct photons at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV, the fragmentation component is suppressed by more than a factor of 2 compared to the inclusive case, increasing sensitivity to small x_Pb

## Context
This work extends the EPS09 nuclear PDF analysis (Eskola, Paukkunen, Salgado 2009) and addresses the limited kinematic reach of fixed-target DIS and Drell-Yan data that constrain quark modifications mainly at large x, as well as the limited constraints from RHIC d+Au inclusive pion data (Eskola et al. 2012). The results show that CMS dijet data (Chatrchyan et al. 2014) can reduce gluon uncertainty at mid/large x, while forward direct photon measurements would be needed to constrain small-x gluon shadowing, complementing the inclusive hadron measurements from ALICE (Abelev et al. 2014), CMS (Khachatryan et al. 2015), and ATLAS (ATLAS Collaboration 2014) that show puzzling high-p_T enhancements requiring further study before inclusion in nPDF fits.
