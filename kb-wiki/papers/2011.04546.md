# A first estimate of $η/s$ in Au+Au reactions at E$_{\rm lab}=1.23$ $A$GeV

> The HADES experiment at GSI has recently provided data on the flow coefficients $v_1,...,v_4$ for protons in Au+Au reactions at $E_{\rm lab} = 1.23$~$A$GeV (or $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4$ GeV). This data allows to estimate the shear viscosity over entropy ratio, $η/s$ at low energies via a coarse graining analysis of the UrQMD transport simulations of the flow harmonics in comparison to the experimental data. By this we can provide for the first time an estimate of $η/s\approx0.65\pm0.15$ (or $(8\pm2)\,(4π)^{-1}$) at such low energies.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in Au+Au collisions at E_lab = 1.23 AGeV is estimated as η/s ≈ 0.65 ± 0.15 (or (8 ± 2)(4π)^(-1)), but this value is space- and time-dependent, with the quoted number representing the volume average at the time of full nuclear overlap.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD transport model (v3.4) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV beam energy, generating 10^7 events with an impact parameter cut b ≤ 3.4 fm. They applied a coarse-graining procedure on a spatial grid with cells of 1 fm side length and time steps of 0.25 fm, extracting local temperature T and baryo-chemical potential μ_B from the energy-momentum tensor and net-baryon current via a hadron resonance gas equation of state. The shear viscosity was then computed using the kinetic theory relation η(T) = (5√π/16)(√(mT)/σ_tr), with the average value ⟨√m/σ_tot⟩ = 0.0256 √GeV/mb extracted from the simulation, and the entropy density s obtained from the thermodynamic relation s = (ε + P - μ_B ρ_B)/T.

## Key numbers
- η/s ≈ 0.65 ± 0.15 (or (8 ± 2)(4π)^(-1)) for Au+Au at √s_NN = 2.4 GeV (E_lab = 1.23 AGeV), extracted at the time of full nuclear overlap.
- The average value ⟨√m/σ_tot⟩ = 0.0256 √GeV/mb was extracted from central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV.
- During the most dense phase (5-10 fm) with ρ_B/ρ_0 ≈ 2, η/s remained approximately constant at 0.4-0.6 (or (5-7)(4π)^(-1)).
- The temperature during the relevant overlap time stayed approximately constant at 60-80 MeV.
- The coarse-grained data was based on an ensemble of 10^7 events with impact parameter b ≤ 3.4 fm.

## Context
This work provides the first estimate of η/s at the low beam energy of 1.23 AGeV, extending the energy dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from higher-energy measurements at RHIC and LHC. It builds on previous UrQMD studies that demonstrated the model's ability to describe HADES flow data (Hillmann 2018, 2019) and on earlier coarse-graining approaches (Steinheimer 2016) that showed local equilibrium is achieved at similar energies. The result enables future comparisons with viscous multi-fluid hydrodynamics and suggests that different flow components (v_1 vs v_2) receive different viscous contributions because they are generated at different stages of the collision.
