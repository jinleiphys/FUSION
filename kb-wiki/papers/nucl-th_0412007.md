# Net Charge and Isospin Fluctuations in the World of Elementary Particles

> Charge and isospin event-by-event fluctuations in high-energy pp-collisions are predicted within the Unitary Eikonal Model ({\bf UEM}), in particular the fluctuation patterns of the ratios of charged-to-charged and neutral-to-charged pions. These fluctuations are found to be sensitive to the presence of unstable resonances, such as $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons.We predict that the charge-fluctuation observable $D_{UEM}$ should be restricted to the interval $8/3\le D_{UEM}\le 4$ depending on the $ρ/π$ production ratio.This result is shown to be compatible with the formation of a hadron gas at RHIC and SPS energies.

## Key claim

The charge-fluctuation observable \(D_{UEM}\) is predicted to lie in the interval \(8/3 \le D_{UEM} \le 4\) depending on the \(\rho/\pi\) production ratio, with the upper bound of 4 corresponding to no \(\rho\) production and the lower bound of \(8/3\) corresponding to no direct pion production.

## Method

The authors used the Unitary Eikonal Model (UEM) to compute event-by-event charge and isospin fluctuations in high-energy pp-collisions. The model assumes pions are produced in the baryon-free central region via coherent production from classical source functions, with global isospin conservation enforced. The calculation considered a cloud of \(N\) pions consisting of directly produced pions and pions from \(\rho\)-type cluster decays, and the observable \(D\) was derived from the fluctuations of the ratio \(R = N_{+}/N_{-}\).

## Key numbers

* The charge-fluctuation observable \(D_{UEM}\) is bounded to the interval \(8/3 \le D_{UEM} \le 4\) for pp-collisions, depending on the \(\rho/\pi\) production ratio.
* The upper bound \(D_{UEM} = 4\) is obtained when \(\overline{n}_{\rho} = 0\) (no \(\rho\) production).
* The lower bound \(D_{UEM} = 8/3\) is obtained when \(\overline{n}_{\pi} = 0\) (no direct pion production).
* The UrQMD model predicts \(\tilde{D} = 1\) for a quark gluon gas, \(\tilde{D} = 2.8\) for a resonance gas, and \(\tilde{D} = 4\) for an uncorrelated pion gas.

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Eikonal Model previously developed by Martinis et al. (1994, 1995) to predict charge fluctuations in pp-collisions, and compares these predictions with the thermal model, the UrQMD model of Bleicher et al. (2000), and the quark-gluon gas model. The results are shown to be compatible with hadron gas formation observed at RHIC and SPS energies by Adcox (2002), Blume (2002), and Reid (2002), and differ noticeably from expectations for a Quark-Gluon Plasma, leading the author to conclude there is no indication for QGP yet.
