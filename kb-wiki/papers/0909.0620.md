# Relativistic models for electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Relativistic models developed for the exclusive and inclusive quasielastic (QE) electron scattering have been extended to charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino-nucleus scattering. Different descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) are compared. For the inclusive electron scattering the relativistic Green's function approach is compared with calculations based on the use of relativistic purely real mean field potentials in the final state. Both approaches lead to a redistribution of the strength but conserving the total flux. Results for the differential cross section at different energies are presented. Scaling properties are also analyzed and discussed

## Key claim

Relativistic models for quasielastic electron scattering can be extended to charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, but the treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) introduces significant model-dependent uncertainties, particularly at higher momentum transfers where the Green's function approach may incorporate non-nucleonic contributions that contaminate the purely nucleonic response.

## Method

The authors compare relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) for exclusive one-nucleon knockout, a semi-inclusive approach using RDWIA amplitudes summed over shell-model states with complex optical potentials, and a relativistic Green's function (GF) approach for inclusive scattering that uses a spectral representation of the single-particle optical model Green's function. For inclusive electron scattering, the GF results are compared with calculations using relativistic purely real mean field potentials (RMF) and with relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) where FSI are neglected.

## Key numbers

- For CC and NC neutrino scattering on 12C at incident neutrino energies of 500 and 1000 MeV, FSI reduce the cross sections by approximately 50% compared to RPWIA calculations.
- At q = 500 MeV/c for 12C(e,e'), the RMF and GF models produce similar results, but at q = 1000 MeV/c the GF cross sections are higher than RMF in the maximum region, with the enhancement differing between two optical potential parameterizations (EDAD1 and EDA2).
- For 16O(nu_mu,mu-) at E_nu = 500 and 1000 MeV with theta_mu = 30 degrees, the GF results differ significantly from both RPWIA and the approximation using only the real part of the optical potential (rROP).
- The imaginary part of the optical potential has its maximum strength around 500 MeV, while the real part decreases with increasing energy.

## Context

This work extends relativistic models previously developed and successfully tested for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions (Meucci, 2003; Udias, 1993) to inclusive electron and neutrino scattering, comparing the Pavia group's Green's function approach with the Madrid-Sevilla group's relativistic mean field model. The comparison is important because neutrino oscillation experiments require precise knowledge of nuclear effects, and the analysis shows that at higher momentum transfers (around 1000 MeV/c) the GF approach may incorporate Delta excitation contributions that modify the quasielastic response, while the RMF model appears more suited to estimate purely nucleonic contributions. The scaling function analysis connects to the extensive superscaling studies of Donnelly (1999) and the phenomenological scaling function extracted from longitudinal response data.
