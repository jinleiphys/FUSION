# Neutron skin impurity from Coulomb core polarization in $^{208}\text{Pb}$: Insights from PREX-II and validation via the $(^{3}\text{He}, t)$ isobaric analog state reaction

> I investigate the effect of Coulomb core-polarization in $^{208}\text{Pb}$, highlighting its impact on uncertainties in neutron distribution measurements due to the discrepancy between neutron excess and neutron skin regions at the surface. This effect is validated using the $(^{3}\text{He}, t)$ isobaric analog state (IAS) reaction at 420 MeV. My findings reveal that the Coulomb boundary radius, where core-polarization becomes negligible, is critical for accurately probing the neutron skin region in the target nucleus. Consequently, I propose extending the $^{208}\text{Pb}(^{3}\text{He}, t)$IAS reaction experiment conducted by Zegers et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 99, 202501 (2007)] to measure cross sections up to 3$^\circ$ angles to more precisely determine the neutron skin thickness in $^{208}\text{Pb}$ from theoretical reaction analysis. Additionally, I observe that, under the assumption of a large neutron skin in $^{208}\text{Pb}$, as suggested by the PREX-II result, the core-polarization effect introduces impurity into the neutron skin region.

# Neutron skin impurity from Coulomb core polarization in $^{208}\text{Pb}$: Insights from PREX-II and validation via the $(^{3}\text{He}, t)$ isobaric analog state reaction

## Key claim
Under the assumption of a large neutron skin in $^{208}\text{Pb}$ as suggested by the PREX-II result (0.283 ± 0.071 fm), the Coulomb core-polarization effect introduces impurity into the neutron skin region by pushing protons outward into the neutron-dominated area. The $(^{3}\text{He}, t)$ isobaric analog state reaction at 420 MeV primarily probes the neutron excess region rather than the neutron skin at forward scattering angles, with the Coulomb boundary radius (where core-polarization becomes negligible) being critical for accurate neutron skin determination.

## Method
The author employed the Lane model and Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with a double-folding model incorporating Chiral three-nucleon forces G-matrix effective interactions from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and Skyrme Hartree-Fock densities from the SAMi-J energy density functional family. Two transition density options were compared: the conventional isovector density ($\rho_n - \rho_p$) and the neutron excess density ($\rho_{nexc}$), with target nuclear densities obtained from SHF calculations using SAMi-J interactions that simulate different neutron skin values in $^{208}\text{Pb}$.

## Key numbers
- Neutron skin thickness from PREX-II: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- Reanalyzed PREX-II using relativistic and non-relativistic EDFs: 0.19 ± 0.02 fm, with symmetry energy slope 54 ± 8 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness from RCNP experiment: 0.211^{+0.054}_{-0.063} fm
- SAMi-J29: $\Delta R_{np}$ = 0.162 fm, $\Delta R_{nexc}$ = 0.687 fm, $\Delta R_{core}$ = -0.142 fm
- SAMi-J30: $\Delta R_{np}$ = 0.180 fm, $\Delta R_{nexc}$ = 0.706 fm, $\Delta R_{core}$ = -0.123 fm
- SAMi-J31: $\Delta R_{np}$ = 0.199 fm, $\Delta R_{nexc}$ = 0.724 fm, $\Delta R_{core}$ = -0.103 fm
- SAMi-J35: $\Delta R_{np}$ = 0.266 fm, $\Delta R_{nexc}$ = 0.787 fm, $\Delta R_{core}$ = -0.034 fm
- Coulomb boundary radii: 5.8 fm (SAMi-J29), 5.7 fm (SAMi-J31), 5.4 fm (SAMi-J35)
- Overlap region between differential cross sections for both transition potentials at first minimum: approximately between 1.8° and 2.1°

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Coulomb core-polarization originally investigated by Auerbach and Van Giai (1981) and applies it to the $(^{3}\text{He}, t)$ charge-exchange reaction at 420 MeV previously studied by Zegers et al. (2007) and Loc et al. (2014). The author's prior work (Huan, 2021) using the G-matrix double-folding model did not account for core-polarization. The study validates that the reaction probes neutron excess rather than neutron skin at forward angles, and proposes extending the Zegers et al. (2007) experiment to measure cross sections up to 3° at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB) to determine neutron skin thickness without core-polarization interference, enabling direct comparison with PREX-II results.
