# Irradiation Induced Dimensional Changes in Bulk Graphite; The theory

> Basing on experimental data on irradiation-induced deformation of graphite we introduced a concept of diffuse domain structure developed in reactor graphite produced by extrusion. Such domains are considered as random continuous deviations of local graphite texture from the global one. We elucidate the origin of domain structure and estimate the size and the degree of orientational ordering of its domains. Using this concept we explain the well known radiation-induced size effect observed in reactor graphite. We also propose a method for converting the experimental data on shape-change of finite-size samples to bulk graphite. This method gives a more accurate evaluation of corresponding data used in estimations of reactor graphite components lifetime under irradiation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the well-known radiation-induced dimensional changes in bulk reactor graphite can be explained by a "diffuse domain structure", regions of size approximately 0.6 cm where the local axis of symmetry randomly deviates from the global extrusion direction, and that this model allows conversion of data from small finite-size samples to bulk graphite behavior, though the authors note this is a lower bound because they employ a purely elastic rather than elastoplastic approach.

## Method

The authors introduce a concept of diffuse domains as regions of size approximately 0.6 cm with transversely isotropic symmetry, where the local anisotropy direction varies continuously and randomly in space. They formulate the elastic energy of this domain structure using a stiffness matrix in a local coordinate system that rotates with the domain orientation, then derive macroscopic elasticity equations by averaging over the random domain orientations. The model is used to relate experimentally measured longitudinal and transverse length changes and cross-sectional flattening of cylindrical samples to the free-domain irradiation-induced deformations and the mean-square angular deviation of domain axes.

## Key numbers

- Domain size is estimated as approximately 0.6 cm, based on the scale over which elliptical cross-section orientations vary randomly along irradiated cylindrical samples.
- The relative volume change formula (ΔV/V)_T = (ΔL/L)^∥ + 2(ΔL/L)^⊥ increasingly diverges from direct volume measurements with increasing neutron fluence for samples irradiated at 460 ± 25 °C.
- Cylindrical samples with diameters of 6 mm and 8 mm were used, with the 6 mm diameter being comparable to the domain size of approximately 0.6 cm.
- Micro-crystallites in the graphite have dimensions of approximately 10^2 nm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Panyukov et al., 2012) that first identified the mismatch between traditional volume change calculations and direct measurements in irradiated graphite samples. The model addresses a known problem in reactor graphite lifetime assessment: data from materials testing reactors (higher neutron flux, finite samples) must be converted to conditions of bulk graphite in power reactors. The approach contrasts with standard methods that use the simple combination of longitudinal and transverse strains (ΔL/L)^∥ + 2(ΔL/L)^⊥, which neglects cross-sectional area changes. The paper explicitly notes that a rigorous solution would require elastoplastic theory, but insufficient understanding of irradiation creep mechanisms (Problem II in the paper) forces the use of a purely elastic approach, giving a lower bound for the influence of domain structure on bulk shape changes.
