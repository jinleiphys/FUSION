# Predicting magic numbers of nuclei with semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions

> Magic numbers are predicted in wide range of the nuclear chart by the self-consistent mean-field calculations with the M3Y-P6 and P7 semi-realistic $NN$ interactions. The magic numbers are identified by vanishing pair correlations in the spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov regime. We also identify submagic numbers when energy gain due to the pairing is sufficiently small. It is found that the results with M3Y-P6 well correspond to the known data, apart from a few exceptions. For some of the magic or submagic numbers the prediction differs from that with the Gogny-D1S or D1M interaction. Roles of the tensor force and the spin-isospin channel originating from the one-pion exchange potential are investigated in the $Z$- or $N$-dependence of the shell gap.

## Key claim

Magic numbers across the nuclear chart are predicted by spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations using the M3Y-P6 and P7 semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, with magic numbers identified by vanishing pair correlations and submagic numbers identified when the energy gain from pairing is below 0.5 or 0.8 MeV; the results with M3Y-P6 correspond well to known data apart from a few exceptions, but for some magic or submagic numbers the prediction differs from that with the Gogny-D1S or D1M interaction.

## Method

Self-consistent spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for even-even nuclei with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 126 and N ≤ 200 using the M3Y-P6 and P7 semi-realistic interactions, which explicitly include the tensor force with realistic origin and the central spin-isospin channel from the one-pion exchange potential. Magic numbers were identified when the proton or neutron pair energy (E_pair) vanished in the HFB state, while submagic numbers were identified when the energy difference E_HF - E_HFB was smaller than threshold values of 0.5 MeV or 0.8 MeV for nuclei where the other nucleon species was magic. The results were compared to those obtained with the Gogny-D1S and D1M interactions, and contributions of the tensor force and the OPEP central channel to single-particle energies were extracted to analyze their roles in shell gap evolution.

## Key numbers

- N=16 is magic at 24O with D1M (E_n_pair vanishes) but submagic with M3Y-P6 (E_HF - E_HFB < 0.8 MeV)
- N=28 magicity is lost for Z ≤ 14 with M3Y-P6 and P7, while only for Z ≤ 12 with D1S and D1M
- N=32 is submagic at 52Ca (owing to n1p_3/2 closure) but not at 60Ni with M3Y-P6 and P7, though kept with D1S and D1M
- N=40 is submagic at 68Ni with all interactions, but lost at 60Ca with M3Y-P6 and P7 while preserved with D1S and D1M
- N=50 is submagic at 70Ca with M3Y-P6 and P7 (rather than magic), while 70Ca is unbound with D1S and D1M
- N=56 is submagic at 96Zr with M3Y-P6 and P7 (due to n1d_5/2 closure) but not with D1M
- N=184 is a good magic number for all bound nuclei with any of the four interactions
- Z=14 is magic at 34Si with all four interactions, but not magic in N ≥ 26 with M3Y-P6 and P7
- Z=40 is magic in 38 ≤ N ≤ 46 with D1M and in 38 ≤ N ≤ 48 with D1S, but this magicity is broken with M3Y-P6 and P7 (consistent with experimental data on 80Zr)
- Z=120 is magic in N ≤ 178 with all four interactions, extending to N=200 with M3Y-P7
- Z=126 is a good magic number with D1S, M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P7, but not with D1M
- Threshold values for submagic identification: λ_sub = 0.5 MeV and 0.8 MeV, independent of A

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of M3Y-type semi-realistic interactions (Nakada 2003, 2008, 2010, 2013) and their application to shell structure in neutron-rich Ca and Ni nuclei (Nakada 2010). The results are compared to predictions from the Gogny-D1S (Berger 1984) and D1M (Goriely 2009) interactions, which lack explicit tensor force and realistic OPEP central channels. The study enables systematic prediction of magic numbers across the nuclear chart including nuclei far from beta stability, and clarifies the roles of the tensor force and spin-isospin channel in shell evolution, though the authors note that quadrupole deformation degrees of freedom should be included in future work for more precise investigation.
