# Pionic Content of Rho-N-N and Rho-N-Delta Vertex Functions

> The dynamical content of rho-N-N and rho-N-Delta vertex functions is studied with a mesonic model. A set of coupled integral equations satisfied by these vertex functions were solved self-consistently. These soulutions indicate that the dominant mesonic content arises from di-pion dynamics. With the experimentally determined pion-baryon-baryon coupling constants and ranges as input, the model predicts a g_{ρNN} that agrees with the meson-exchange-potential results. On the other hand, it predicts a smaller f_{ρNΔ} and much softer form factors. Implications of the findings on the use of phenomenological coupling constants in nuclear reaction studies are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dominant mesonic content of the rho-N-N and rho-N-Delta vertex functions arises from di-pion dynamics, and that the model predicts a g_rhoNN consistent with meson-exchange-potential results but a smaller f_rhoNDelta and much softer form factors, with the caveat that these predictions depend on the specific input parameters for pion-baryon-baryon coupling constants and ranges.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical mesonic model where the rho-N-N and rho-N-Delta vertex functions are described by a set of four coupled integral equations, solved self-consistently by iteration. The driving terms are triangle diagrams involving pion exchange, with loop diagrams treated as corrections; the equations were solved in the p-wave channel only, using experimentally determined pion-baryon-baryon coupling constants and dipole form factor ranges as input. The resulting vertex functions were then related to the standard Lagrangian coupling constants g_rhoNN and f_rhoNDelta via partial-wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- g_rhoNN from the di-pion model: 1.025 (set b) and 1.033 (set c), compared to MEP values ranging from 1.28 to 2.27 (i.e., g^2/4pi = 0.13 to 0.41).
- f_rhoNDelta from the di-pion model: 3.50 - 2.71i (set b) and 5.25 - 2.70i (set c), compared to MEP values ranging from 4.91 to 7.81 (i.e., f^2/4pi = 1.92 to 4.86).
- Form factor ranges Lambda_rhoNN = 430-435 MeV and Lambda_rhoNDelta = 490-495 MeV, much smaller than the MEP values of 1.2 GeV.
- Input parameters: g_rhoPiPi = 0.6684 M_pi^{-1/2}, Lambda_rhoPiPi = 2.336 fm^{-1}, f_piNN = 1, Lambda_piNN = Lambda_piNDelta = 950 MeV (corresponding to a monopole range of 612 MeV).
- f_piNDelta = 1.69 (set a) or 2.19 (sets b and c); f_piDeltaDelta = 0.8 (set a) or 1.03 (sets b and c).

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange approach by providing a dynamical, self-consistent calculation of the rho-N-N and rho-N-Delta vertices, in contrast to earlier work by Gari (1990) which treated the triangle and loop diagrams as perturbative corrections to a phenomenological vertex. The results contradict the large form-factor ranges (1.2 GeV) used in standard meson-exchange potentials (Machleidt 1987, Arenhoevel 1984) and instead support the smaller pion ranges (monopole range less than 650 MeV) inferred from deep inelastic scattering data (Frankfurt 1991, Kumar 1993). The findings enable future studies of meson production reactions where the energy-dependent, complex-valued nature of the vertex functions may produce new interference effects.
