# The phi NN coupling from chiral loops

> Starting from effective Lagrangians which combine a gauge formulation of Vector Meson Dominance with Chiral Lagrangians, the coupling of the phi to the nucleon, which is zero at tree level due to the OZI rule, is calculated perturbatively considering loop contributions to the electric and magnetic form factors. We obtain reasonably smaller values for both form factors than those for rho NN and consistent with the expected order of magnitude of the OZI rule violation.

## Key claim

The $\phi NN$ coupling, which is zero at tree level due to the OZI rule, receives non-zero contributions from one-loop diagrams involving kaons and hyperons; the resulting electric and magnetic form factors are small compared to the $\rho NN$ coupling, consistent with a weak OZI rule violation, but the magnetic form factor is dominated by intermediate $\Sigma^{*}(1385)$ states and the total electric form factor vanishes at zero momentum transfer due to gauge symmetry.

## Method

The authors used effective Lagrangians combining chiral SU(3) dynamics with vector meson dominance in a gauge-invariant framework to compute one-loop contributions to the $\phi NN$ electric and magnetic form factors. They evaluated diagrams with intermediate $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma^{*}(1385)$ hyperons and kaon loops, regularized with monopole form factors (cutoff $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV for pseudoscalar couplings, $\Lambda_\phi = 2.5$ GeV for vector couplings), and also considered the effect of $\phi$-$\omega$ mixing.

## Key numbers

- $G^{M}_{\phi NN}(\vec{q}=\vec{0}) = 1.10$ (total magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer, dimensionless)
- $G^{E}_{\phi NN}(\vec{q}=\vec{0}) = 0$ (total electric form factor at zero momentum transfer, exact cancellation from gauge invariance)
- $G^{M}_{\phi NN}$ is a factor of about 20 smaller than the experimental $\rho NN$ magnetic coupling $G^{M,exp}_{\rho NN} \sim 21$
- The loop contribution to $G^{M}_{\rho NN}$ from Jido (2002) is 6.05, only a factor of about 6 larger than the $\phi NN$ result of 1.10
- Changing the kaon decay constant from $f$ to $f_K = 1.22 f$ reduces all results by a factor of $(1.22)^{-2} = 0.67$ (33% reduction)
- Using alternative $F$ and $D$ parameters ($D=0.85$, $F=0.52$) gives $G^{M}(q=0)=1.29$; using ($D=0.80$, $F=0.50$) gives $G^{M}(q=0)=1.16$
- Total estimated uncertainty in $G^{M}(q=0)$ is about 40% when combining form factor uncertainties (20-30%) and parameter variations in quadrature
- For the strong $\phi$-$\omega$ mixing scenario, $G^{V}_{\phi NN}(q=0) \sim -0.6$ from mixing alone, about 1/5 of $G^{V}_{\rho NN}$

## Context

This work extends the loop-calculation approach previously applied to the $\rho NN$ coupling by Jido (2002) to the $\phi NN$ case, where the tree-level coupling vanishes due to the OZI rule. It contradicts earlier dispersion-relation studies by Hoehler and Hammer (1995, 1999) that gave large $\phi NN$ couplings implying strong OZI violation, and supports the reanalysis by Meissner (1997) that found small couplings after including correlated $\rho\pi$ exchange. The results enable more reliable calculations of $\phi$ meson exchange in nuclear interactions and $\phi$ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, with the caveat that the momentum range of validity is limited to about 500 MeV/c due to gauge invariance constraints from the form factors.
