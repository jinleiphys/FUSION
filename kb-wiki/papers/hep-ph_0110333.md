# Chiral unitary approach to S-wave meson baryon scattering in the strangeness S=0 sector

> We study the S-wave interaction of mesons with baryons in the strangeness S=0 sector in a coupled channel unitary approach. The basic dynamics is drawn from the lowest order meson baryon chiral Lagrangians. Small modifications inspired by models with explicit vector meson exchange in the t-channel are also considered. In addition the pi pi N channel is included and shown to have an important repercussion in the results, particularly in the isospin 3/2 sector.

## Key claim

The $N^*(1535)$ resonance is dynamically generated as a pole in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude, with its mass, width, and branching ratios in fair agreement with experiment, while a $\Delta(1620)$ resonance also appears as a pole at the right position but with a very large width, coming essentially from the coupling to the $\pi \pi N$ channel, in qualitative agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for S-wave meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness $S=0$ sector, using the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian as the kernel. They include six two-body channels ($\pi^- p$, $\pi^0 n$, $\eta n$, $K^+ \Sigma^-$, $K^0 \Sigma^0$, $K^0 \Lambda$) and also incorporate the $\pi \pi N$ channel as an additional coupled channel. Small modifications inspired by vector meson dominance (VMD) are applied to the chiral coefficients to account for the momentum-transfer dependence of the $\rho$ and $K^*$ propagators.

## Key numbers

- $f_{\pi}=93$ MeV, $f_K = 1.22 f_{\pi}$, $f_{\eta}=1.3 f_{\pi}$ (weak decay constants used in the potential)
- Subtraction constants at $\mu=1200$ MeV: $a_{\pi N}(\mu)=2.0$, $a_{\eta N}(\mu)=0.1$, $a_{K \Lambda}(\mu)=1.5$, $a_{K \Sigma}(\mu)=-2.8$ (final fit including $\pi \pi N$ channel)
- $\rho$ meson mass $m_{\rho}=770$ MeV; $K^*$ meson mass $m_{K^*}=892$ MeV (used in VMD corrections)
- The $\rho$ meson tail reduces the chiral coefficient by about 25% at energies around 1500 MeV
- The $\pi \pi N$ channel threshold is at $\sqrt{s_{th}} \sim 1213$ MeV
- The $\eta n$ threshold appears at 1487 MeV; the $K \Sigma$ threshold appears at 1690 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998) for meson-baryon scattering in the $S=0$ sector by including the $\pi \pi N$ channel, which was omitted in prior studies by Hosaka (1997) and by Oset and collaborators (2000). The inclusion of the $\pi \pi N$ channel is shown to be crucial for reproducing the inelasticities in the $I=3/2$ sector and for generating the $\Delta(1620)$ resonance. The approach enables a unified description of $\pi N$ elastic scattering and $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ cross sections up to center-of-mass energies around 1600 MeV, and provides revised determinations of the S-wave $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ transition amplitudes that differ from those of Manley (1984) and Burkhardt (1999).
