# $^3_ηHe$ nucleus modeling in the frame optical potential model

> The conditions, at which quasi-bound $η-^{3}$He state is possible, have been investigated and compared with the available findings about $η$N-scattering length and the information about $^{3}$He nucleus from references. We conclud that the existence of quasi-bound $η-^{3}$He state within the framework of the optical potential model, which doesn`t contradict all collected findings, is not possible, but the observing anomaly of $η^{3}$He-interaction at low energies is a virtual state.

# $^{3}_{\eta}$He nucleus modeling in the frame optical potential model

## Key claim
The authors conclude that a quasi-bound $\eta-^{3}$He state (a mesic nucleus) is not possible within the optical potential model without contradicting existing data on the $\eta$N-scattering length and $^{3}$He nuclear structure; the observed low-energy anomaly in $\eta-^{3}$He interaction is instead a virtual state near the sum mass of free particles.

## Method
The authors constructed an optical potential for $\eta$-nucleus interaction using a Fermi-form nucleon density distribution for $^{3}$He, with the half-density radius $R_c$ and diffusion thickness $a$ constrained by the experimental root-mean-square radius of $^{3}$He (taken as 1.9 Fm). They calculated the formation boundary for a quasi-bound state (binding energy $\epsilon \approx 0$) in the complex plane of the $\eta$N-scattering length $a_0$, varying the diffuseness parameter $a/R_c$, and compared the required $a_0$ values with published $\eta$N-scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- The root-mean-square radius of $^{3}$He was taken as 1.9 Fm, averaged from several measurements.
- For diffuseness $a/R_c = 0.25$, quasi-bound state formation requires $|\text{Re}(a_0)| \geq 0.75$ Fm.
- Published values of $|\text{Re}(a_0)|$ lie in the interval $(0.27 \div 1.0)$ Fm (from work [5]).
- Four nucleon density parameter sets are given: for $a/R_c = 0.10$, $R_c = 2.210$ Fm and $\rho_0 = 0.060$ nucleon/Fm$^3$; for $a/R_c = 0.15$, $R_c = 1.991$ Fm and $\rho_0 = 0.074$ nucleon/Fm$^3$; for $a/R_c = 0.20$, $R_c = 1.770$ Fm and $\rho_0 = 0.093$ nucleon/Fm$^3$; for $a/R_c = 0.25$, $R_c = 1.571$ Fm and $\rho_0 = 0.114$ nucleon/Fm$^3$.

## Context
This work extends earlier experimental and theoretical studies of $\eta$-mesic nuclei, specifically following the precise experiment [1] and the analysis in [2] that suggested either a quasi-bound or virtual $\eta-^{3}$He state. The authors compare their results with published $\eta$N-scattering lengths from works [5, 6, 7, 8] and find that the imaginary parts of those scattering lengths preclude a quasi-bound state. Their conclusion agrees with work [9], which also found that quasi-bound $\eta$-states with three nucleons are impossible but that a virtual $\eta-^{3}$He state is possible. The result enables future studies to focus on the virtual-state interpretation of low-energy $\eta-^{3}$He interactions rather than searching for a bound mesic nucleus.
