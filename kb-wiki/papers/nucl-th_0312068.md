# The relation between the photonuclear E1 sum rule and the effective orbital g-factor

> The connection between the enhancement factor (1+kappa) of the photonuclear E1 sum rule and the orbital angular momentum g-factor (gl) of a bound nucleon is investigated in the framework of the Landau-Migdal theory for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Special emphasis is put on the role of gauge invariance to establish the kappa-gl relation. By identifying the physical processes which are taken into account in kappa and gl, the validity and limitations of this relation are discussed. The connections to the collective excitations and to nuclear Compton scattering are also shown.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the relation \(1+\kappa = 2\,g_{\ell,\text{IV}}\) between the photonuclear E1 sum rule enhancement factor and the isovector orbital g-factor holds generally in the Landau-Migdal quasiparticle gas approximation, but only a part of the total \(\kappa\) is related to \(g_{\ell,\text{IV}}\) when nucleon correlations beyond the quasiparticle gas are included, contrary to earlier claims that the relation involves the total \(\kappa\).

## Method

The authors use the Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter to derive expressions for the proton and neutron orbital g-factors and the E1 sum rule enhancement factor, employing gauge invariance and Ward-Takahashi identities to relate the quasiparticle current to the Landau-Migdal interaction parameters. They separate the current-current correlation function into a part involving particle-hole cuts (\(\Pi_A\)) and a part involving other cuts (\(\Pi_B\)), then identify which physical processes (diagrams) contribute to \(\kappa\) and \(g_\ell\) in terms of Feynman and time-ordered Bethe-Goldstone diagrams, and connect the results to nuclear Compton scattering.

## Key numbers

* The orbital g-factor for a proton is \(g_\ell(p) = \frac{M}{\mu_p} - \frac{M v_F}{3 p_F} F_1(pn) \frac{\mu_n}{\mu_p} \frac{N}{A} \beta\), where \(\beta = \left[1 - \left(\frac{N-Z}{A}\right)^2\right]^{-1/3}\), \(M\) is the nucleon mass, \(v_F\) the Fermi velocity, \(p_F\) the Fermi momentum, and \(F_1(pn)\) the \(\ell=1\) Landau-Migdal parameter for proton-neutron interactions.
* The orbital g-factor for a neutron is \(g_\ell(n) = \frac{M v_F}{3 p_F} F_1(pn) \frac{Z}{A} \beta\).
* The effective charges for E1 transitions are \(q_p = \frac{N}{A} e_p\) and \(q_n = -\frac{Z}{A} e_p\), where \(e_p\) is the proton electric charge.
* The relation \(\frac{Z}{A} \frac{\mu_p}{M} g_\ell(p) + \frac{N}{A} \frac{\mu_n}{M} g_\ell(n) = \frac{Z}{A}\) holds for the orbital g-factors in asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation derived by Fujita and Hirata (1970s) for symmetric Fermi gas in first-order perturbation theory, and the later work by Migdal and collaborators (1965) using quasiparticle gas, to the case of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter (\(N \neq Z\)). It clarifies that the Landau-Migdal approach gives the total \(\kappa\) in the relation, while earlier work by Arima and collaborators showed that only part of \(\kappa\) is related to \(g_{\ell,\text{IV}}\) when correlations are included. The results enable more detailed analyses of photo-neutron experiments and photon scattering experiments, particularly for nuclei in the lead region, and connect to studies of giant dipole resonances and nuclear Compton scattering.
