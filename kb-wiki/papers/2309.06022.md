# Impact of modified gravity theory on neutron star and nuclear matter properties

> New observational data, measured with a high degree of accuracy, of compact isolated neutron stars and binary stars in gravitational wave remnants have the potential to explore the strong field gravity. Within the framework of energy-momentum squared gravity (EMSG) theory we study its impact on several properties of neutron stars and plausible modifications from the predictions of general relativity. Based on a representative set of relativistic nuclear mean field models, non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models and microscopic calculations, we show deviations of neutron star mass-radius sequence in EMSG theory as compared to general relativity. The variation in the effective nuclear equation of state in EMSG, results in distinct magnitudes in the reduced pressure, speed of sound, and maximum compactness at the center of neutron stars. We perform extensive correlation analysis of the nuclear model parameters with the neutron star observables in light of the new observational bounds. Perceptible modifications in the correlations are found in the models of gravity that provide different estimates of the slope and curvature of nuclear matter symmetry energy. The available neutron star data however do not impose stringent enough constraints for clear evidence of deviations from general relativity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that energy-momentum squared gravity (EMSG) modifies neutron star mass-radius sequences and nuclear matter properties compared to general relativity, but the available neutron star observational data do not impose stringent enough constraints to provide clear evidence for deviations from general relativity.

## Method

The authors solved the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations in EMSG theory using 18 relativistic mean field (RMF) models, 24 non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models, and 2 microscopic calculations (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with Argonne V18 plus Urbana-type potentials, and the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall variational EoS). They computed neutron star mass, radius, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability, then performed correlation analysis between nuclear matter parameters (symmetry energy slope L, curvature K_sym, incompressibility K_0) and neutron star observables in both EMSG and GR frameworks.

## Key numbers

- The EMSG coupling parameter α is constrained to the range -10^-38 ≲ α ≲ 10^-37 cm^3/erg from the 2 M⊙ maximum mass neutron star constraint and physicality of effective EoSs at the center of neutron stars
- Binary pulsar observations yield a compatible value of -6 × 10^-38 ≲ α ≲ 10^-36 cm^3/erg
- Solar system tests give a much weaker constraint: -4 × 10^-27 < α < 10^-26 cm^3/erg
- The minimum α values for stable configurations are -(1.9, 1.2, 0.8) × 10^-37 cm^3/erg for NL3, BSR2, and Sly4 EoSs respectively
- The maximum α value used is α_max = 10^-37 cm^3/erg
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K_0 is constrained to 210 ≤ K_0 ≤ 260 MeV from experimental data
- Symmetry energy at saturation is constrained to 28 ≤ e_sym(ρ_0) ≤ 34 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L is constrained to 46 ≤ L ≤ 106 MeV at saturation density ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star properties in modified gravity theories, particularly building on the EMSG framework developed by Katirci (2014), Roshan (2016), and Akarsu (2022). It contrasts with f(R) gravity approaches by Capozziello (2011) and f(R,T) gravity by Harko (2011), while using the same nuclear EoS models previously employed in GR-based correlation analyses by Alam (2016), Malik (2018), and Carson (2019). The results enable future tests of EMSG against upcoming high-precision neutron star observations from NICER and gravitational wave events.
