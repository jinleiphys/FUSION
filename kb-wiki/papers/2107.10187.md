# Light-Front Transverse Momentum Distributions for ${\cal J}$=1/2 Hadronic Systems in Valence Approximation

> The semi-inclusive correlator for a ${\cal J}$=1/2 bound-system, composed by A spin-1/2 fermions, is linearly expressed in terms of the light-front Poincaré covariant spin-dependent spectral function, in valence approximation. The light-front spin-dependent spectral function is fully determined by six scalar functions that allow for a complete description of the six T-even transverse-momentum distributions, suitable for a detailed investigation of the dynamics inside the bound system. The application of the developed formalism to a case with a sophisticated dynamical content, like $^3$He, reaches two goals: (i) to illustrate a prototype of an investigation path for gathering a rich wealth of information on the dynamics and also finding valuable constraints to be exploited from the phenomenological standpoint; (ii) to support for the three-nucleon system a dedicated experimental effort for obtaining a detailed 3D picture in momentum space. In particular, the orbital-angular momentum decomposition of the bound state can be studied through the assessment of relations among the transverse-momentum distributions, as well as the relevance of the relativistic effect generated by the implementation of macrocausality. A fresh evaluation of the longitudinal and transverse polarizations of the neutron and proton is also provided, confirming essentially the values used in the standard procedure for extracting the neutron structure functions from both deep-inelastic scattering and semi-inclusive reactions, in the same kinematical regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a spin-1/2 bound system of A spin-1/2 fermions in valence approximation, the six T-even transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs) can be linearly expressed in terms of a light-front Poincaré-covariant spin-dependent spectral function, which is fully determined by six scalar functions; the authors apply this formalism to 3He as a prototype, noting that the approximate relations among TMDs investigated in prior work can be tested using 3He as a playground since such tests are much more challenging for the proton due to quark confinement and hadron fragmentation.

## Method

The authors construct the light-front spin-dependent spectral function for a generic spin-1/2 bound system using light-front Hamiltonian dynamics (LFHD) with the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of Poincaré generators, obtaining the spectral function from overlaps between the LF wave function and tensor products of a plane wave with an interacting (A-1)-particle spectator state. They then derive a linear relation between this spectral function and the semi-inclusive fermion correlator in valence approximation, which allows them to express the six T-even twist-two TMDs in terms of six scalar functions (B_i and b_i) that are evaluated numerically for 3He using realistic nuclear interactions and non-relativistic wave functions transformed via Melosh rotation matrices.

## Key numbers

* The LF spin-dependent spectral function is normalized such that for each isospin channel tau = p(n), the trace integral yields 1 (baryon number sum rule).
* The momentum sum rule gives integral of x times the momentum distribution n^tau(x,k_perp) over x and k_perp equals 1/3.
* For the (A-1)=2 case, the energy density rho(epsilon) equals 1 for bound states (e.g., deuteron) and rho(epsilon) = m sqrt(m epsilon)/2 for the continuum, with m the constituent mass.
* The free mass of the [1,(A-1)] cluster is given by M_0^2[1,(A-1)] = (m^2 + kappa_perp^2)/xi_1 + (M_S^2 + kappa_perp^2)/(1 - xi_1), where M_S^2 = 4m^2 + 4m epsilon for (A-1)=2.

## Context

This work extends the light-front spectral function formalism developed by Del Dotto et al. (2016) for three-nucleon systems to generic spin-1/2 hadronic systems in valence approximation, and provides the first calculation of TMDs for 3He from a Poincaré-covariant framework with realistic nuclear interactions. The results enable future experimental efforts at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider to extract neutron structure functions from polarized 3He targets using semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and allow testing of approximate TMD relations previously investigated by Jaffe and Manohar (1990) and Lorce and Pasquini (2011) in a nuclear system where confinement does not prevent direct comparison with theory.
