# Spin polarized states in strongly asymmetric nuclear matter

> In the framework of a Fermi liquid theory it is considered the possibility of appearance of spin polarized states in strongly asymmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme effective interaction. The zero temperature dependence of neutron and proton spin polarization parameters as functions of density is found for SLy4, SLy5 effective forces. It is shown that at some critical density it will be formed the state with the oppositely directed spins of neutrons and protons, while the state with the same direction of spins does not appear. In comparison with neutron matter, even small admixture of protons strongly decreases the threshold density of spin instability. It is clarified that protons become totally polarized within very narrow density domain while in the density profile of neutron spin polarization parameter their appear long tails near the transition density.

## Key claim

Strongly asymmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme effective forces (SLy4 and SLy5) undergoes a phase transition to a spin polarized state where neutron and proton spins are oppositely directed, but only if the density exceeds a critical threshold; the state with spins aligned in the same direction does not appear for these force parameterizations.

## Method

The authors used a Fermi liquid theory framework with Skyrme effective interactions (SLy4 and SLy5 potentials) to derive self-consistent equations for the ferromagnetic (FM) and antiferromagnetic (AFM) spin order parameters at zero temperature. They solved these equations numerically for strongly asymmetric nuclear matter (isospin asymmetry parameters α = 0.95, 0.9, 0.8) and for pure neutron matter (α = 1), obtaining the density dependence of the neutron and proton spin polarization parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for spin instability in neutron matter: approximately 0.59 fm⁻³ for SLy4 and 0.58 fm⁻³ for SLy5.
- Critical density for spin instability in asymmetric nuclear matter with α = 0.95: approximately 0.38 fm⁻³ for both SLy4 and SLy5.
- Density at which protons become fully polarized (α = 0.95): approximately 0.41 fm⁻³ for SLy4 and 0.40 fm⁻³ for SLy5.
- Density at which neutrons become fully polarized (α = 0.95): approximately 1.05 fm⁻³ for SLy4 and 1.02 fm⁻³ for SLy5.
- The critical density shift from neutron matter (≈ 3.7 ρ₀) to asymmetric matter with 2.5% protons (≈ 2.4 ρ₀) corresponds to a displacement from approximately 0.59 fm⁻³ to 0.38 fm⁻³ (using ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin polarization in nuclear matter that relied on calculating magnetic susceptibility to find the pole structure (Vidana and Bombaci, 2002; Rios and Polls, 2002), by instead directly solving self-consistent equations for the order parameters. It contrasts with microscopic calculations using realistic NN interactions (such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with Nijmegen potentials by Vidana and Polls, 2002) which find no evidence of ferromagnetic instability. The results are relevant for modeling the thermal and magnetic evolution of pulsars, whose cores consist of strongly asymmetric nuclear matter.
