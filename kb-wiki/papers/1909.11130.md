# Simple Woods-Saxon type form for $Ωα$ and $Ξα$ interactions using Folding Model

> We derive a simple Woods-Saxon type form for the potentials between $Y=Ξ, Ω$ and $α$ by using a single-folding potential method, based on a separable $Y$-nucleon potential. Accordingly, the potentials $Ξ+α$ and $Ω+α$ are obtained using the ESC08c Nijmegens $ΞN$ potential (in $^{3}S_{1}$ channel) and HAL QCD Collaboration $ΩN$ interactions (in lattice QCD), respectively. In deriving the potential between $Y$ and $α$, the same potential between $Y$ and $N$ is used. Binding energy, scattering length and effective range of $Y$ particle on the alpha particle are approximated by the resultant potentials. The depths of the potentials in $Ωα$ and $Ξα$ systems are obtained $-61$ and $-24.4$ MeV, respectively. In the case of $Ξα$ potential, a fairly good agreement is observed between the single-folding potential method and the phenomenological potential of Dover-Gal model. These potentials can be used in 3-,4- and 5-body cluster structures of $ Ω$ and $Ξ$ hypernuclei.

# Simple Woods-Saxon type form for Ωα and Ξα interactions using Folding Model

## Key claim

The authors derive Woods-Saxon type potentials for Ξ+α and Ω+α systems using a single-folding method, but the resulting binding energies should be considered upper bounds because the coupling of ΩN to higher- and lower-mass channels and the Coulomb force are not included; a coupled-channel analysis would be needed for definitive conclusions.

## Method

The authors apply a single-folding potential method, convolving the nucleon density distribution of the alpha particle (with rms radius 1.47 fm) with a separable Y-nucleon potential. For the ΞN interaction they use the ESC08c Nijmegen potential in the ³S₁ channel, and for the ΩN interaction they use the HAL QCD Collaboration potential in the ⁵S₂ channel from lattice QCD. The resulting Yα potentials are fitted to a Woods-Saxon form, and the Schrödinger equation is solved to extract scattering observables from the asymptotic wave function.

## Key numbers

- Depth of the Ωα potential: -61 MeV (same for all four parameter sets P₁ through P₄)
- Depth of the Ξα potential: -24.4 MeV (from the single-folding potential fit)
- Binding energy of Ξ+α system: -1.54 MeV (SFP model) compared to -2.1 MeV (Dover-Gal model)
- Binding energy of Ω+α system: ranges from -22.4 MeV to -23.0 MeV across parameter sets P₁-P₄ (using experimental masses); with HAL QCD masses, ranges from -22.8 MeV to -23.4 MeV
- Scattering length for Ξα: -6.6 fm (SFP model) versus -4.9 fm (Dover-Gal model)
- Effective range for Ξα: 1.9 fm (both SFP and Dover-Gal models)
- Scattering length for Ωα: ranges from -0.98 fm to -1.05 fm across parameter sets (experimental masses)
- Effective range for Ωα: 0.67 fm for all parameter sets (experimental masses)
- ΩN binding energy: 1.54 MeV (⁵S₂ channel)
- ΞN binding energy: 1.67 MeV (³S₁ channel)
- ΩN scattering length: 5.30 fm; effective range: 1.26 fm
- ΞN scattering length: 4.91 fm; effective range: 0.527 fm

## Context

This work extends the single-folding potential method to hyperon-alpha systems, testing it against the phenomenological Dover-Gal model (Dover and Gal, 1983) for the Ξα case and finding good agreement. The results enable studies of 3-, 4-, and 5-body cluster structures of Ω and Ξ hypernuclei, particularly in few-alpha cluster models as developed by Hiyama (2000, 2014) and in variational four-body calculations by Garcilazo and Valcarce (2020). The Ωα binding energy of approximately 23 MeV is larger than the Ωd binding energy of approximately 17 MeV found by Garcilazo and Valcarce (2018), which the authors note is physically reasonable.
