# Proceedings of the second International PrimeNet Workshop

> These are the proceedings of the second PrimeNet Workshop, held in September 26-28, 2011, at the campus of Forschungszentrum Jülich, Germany. This workshop is part of the activities in the project "Study of Strongly Interacting Matter" (acronym HadronPhysics2), which is an integrating activity of the Seventh Framework Program of EU. This HP2 project contains several activities, one of them being the network PrimeNet having the focus on Meson Physics in Low-Energy QCD. This network is created to exchange information on experimental and theoretical ongoing activities on mainly eta and eta-prime physics at different European accelerator facilities and institutes.

## Key claim
The WASA-at-COSY experiment aims to improve the upper limit on the branching ratio of the C-violating decay eta -> pi0 e+ e- and to search for the rare decay eta -> e+ e- as a probe of physics beyond the Standard Model, where the latter decay's branching ratio in the Standard Model is predicted at the level of ~10^-9 but current experimental limits are five orders of magnitude larger.

## Method
For the eta -> pi0 e+ e- analysis, the WASA-at-COSY detector collected eta mesons via the reaction pd -> 3He X at 1 GeV beam energy in 2008 and 2009, with 10^7 and 2x10^7 eta events respectively. Events were preselected requiring 3He identification in the forward detector and two photons plus an e+ e- pair in the central detector; background from multipion production and other eta decays was simulated and subtracted using a Monte Carlo cocktail fit to the data, with optimal cuts determined by maximizing an evaluation function G = S_R * (S_R / B_R). For the eta -> e+ e- search, eta mesons were produced in pp -> pp eta at 1.4 GeV beam energy using a special trigger demanding high energy deposit in both sides of the electromagnetic calorimeter, with the number of eta mesons estimated from the eta -> gamma gamma decay channel.

## Key numbers
- For the eta -> pi0 e+ e- analysis: 9920 out of 10^6 simulated signal events survive after optimal cuts, with background reduced by a factor of 1.7 x 10^8; one event candidate remains in the 2008 data after all cuts.
- The previous upper limit for eta -> pi0 e+ e- is BR < 4 x 10^-5 (from earlier experiments).
- For the eta -> e+ e- search: the current best experimental limit is BR < 2.7 x 10^-5 at 90% confidence level from the CELSIUS/WASA experiment.
- The number of eta mesons collected in the 2-week data sample for the eta -> e+ e- analysis is estimated as ~4.4 x 10^7, approximately forty times larger than the previous analysis.
- The Standard Model prediction for eta -> e+ e- branching ratio is ~10^-9.
- The measured branching ratio for pi0 -> e+ e- from KTeV is (7.49 +/- 0.29 +/- 0.25) x 10^-8.

## Context
This work extends the search for C-parity violation in eta decays and for rare eta decays that could indicate new physics, building on previous upper limits from CELSIUS/WASA (2008) and other experiments. The eta -> pi0 e+ e- analysis uses data from WASA-at-COSY to improve sensitivity to C-violating processes, while the eta -> e+ e- search is motivated by the discrepancy between the KTeV measurement of pi0 -> e+ e- and theoretical calculations by Dorokhov and collaborators (2007), which has been suggested by Kahn et al. (2007) as possible evidence for a light neutral U boson in dark matter models proposed by Boehm et al. (2003). The results will enable more stringent tests of C-parity conservation and searches for light dark matter mediators.
