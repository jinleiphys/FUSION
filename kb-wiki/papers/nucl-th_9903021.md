# Can We Extract Lambda-Lambda Interaction from Two-Particle Momentum Correlation ?

> We analyze the invariant mass spectrum of Lambda-Lambda in $^{12}C(K^-,K^+ Lambda Lambda)$ reaction at P(K^+)=1.65 GeV/c by using a combined framework of IntraNuclear Cascade (INC) model and the correlation function technique. The observed enhancement at low-invariant masses can be well reproduced with attractive Lambda-Lambda interactions with the scattering length either in the range a = -6 \sim -4 fm (no bound state) or a = 7 \sim 12 fm (with bound state). We also discuss Lambda-Lambda correlation functions in central relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a possible way to eliminate this discrete ambiguity.

# Can We Extract Lambda-Lambda Interaction from Two-Particle Momentum Correlation?

## Key claim

The observed enhancement at low invariant masses in the $\Lambda$-$\Lambda$ spectrum from $^{12}C(K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda)$ at $P_{K^+}=1.65$ GeV/c can be well reproduced by attractive $\Lambda$-$\Lambda$ interactions, but this yields a discrete ambiguity: the scattering length is either in the range $a = -6 \sim -4$ fm (no bound state) or $a = 7 \sim 12$ fm (with bound state), and the two solutions cannot be distinguished from the $(K^-,K^+)$ data alone.

## Method

The authors combined an IntraNuclear Cascade (INC) model, which generates the hyperon source function, with the correlation function technique using a single-channel description of $\Lambda\Lambda$ in the spin-singlet state. They parametrized the $\Lambda$-$\Lambda$ interaction with a two-range Gaussian potential $V_{\Lambda\Lambda}(r) = v_l \exp(-r^2/\mu_l^2) + v_s \exp(-r^2/\mu_s^2)$, fixed the shorter range at $\mu_s = 0.45$ fm, and searched for $\chi^2$ local minima in the $(v_l, v_s)$ plane for longer range parameters $\mu_l = 0.6 \sim 1.0$ fm. They then calculated $\Lambda$-$\Lambda$ correlation functions in central relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the JAM cascade model to test whether the two solutions could be distinguished.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length range for no bound state: $a = -6 \sim -4$ fm (from fits with $\mu_l = 0.6 \sim 1.0$ fm)
- Scattering length range with bound state: $a = 7 \sim 12$ fm (from fits with $\mu_l = 0.6 \sim 1.0$ fm)
- Effective range for no bound state solutions: $r_{\rm eff} = 1.6 \sim 2.0$ fm
- Effective range for bound state solutions: $r_{\rm eff} = 1.2 \sim 1.6$ fm
- Binding energies for bound state solutions: $0.29 \sim 0.72$ MeV
- Reduced chi-squared values for all fits: $\tilde{\chi}^2 = 0.34 \sim 0.43$ (DOF = 9)
- Source spreading radius in $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction: $r_{12} \simeq 5$ fm (beyond which correlation effects wash out)

## Context

This work extends the correlation function technique, previously used to extract source sizes when final-state interactions are well-known, to instead extract unknown hyperon-hyperon interactions from the invariant mass spectrum measured by Ahn et al. (KEK-E224 collaboration). The INC model used was previously shown by Ohnishi, Nara, and Hirata (1997) to reproduce inclusive $(K^-,K^+)$ spectra. The extracted scattering parameters are compared with Nijmegen models (ND, NF, and NSC89), and the authors propose that relativistic heavy-ion collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies could resolve the discrete ambiguity between attractive interactions with and without a bound state, because the larger source size in those collisions breaks the long-wave approximation that produces the double-well structure.
