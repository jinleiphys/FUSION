# Lattice QCD noise reduction for bosonic correlators through blocking

> We propose a method to substantially improve the signal-to-noise ratio of lattice correlation functions for bosonic operators or other operator combinations with disconnected contributions. The technique is applicable for correlations between operators on two planes (zero momentum correlators) when the dimension of the plane is larger than the separation between the two planes which are correlated. In this case, the correlation arises primarily from points whose in-plane coordinates are close, but noise arises from all pairs of points. By breaking each plane into bins and computing bin-bin correlations, it is possible to capture these short-distance correlators exactly while replacing (small) correlators at large spatial extent with a fit, with smaller uncertainty than the data. The cost is only marginally larger than averaging each plane before correlating, but the improvement in signal-to-noise can be substantial. We test the method on correlators of the gradient-flowed topological charge density and squared field strength, finding noise reductions by a factor of $\sim$ 3$-$7 compared to the conventional approach on the same ensemble of configurations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a blocking method that improves the signal-to-noise ratio of lattice correlation functions for bosonic operators by exploiting the fact that the signal arises primarily from small transverse separations between operator positions, while noise arises from all pairs of points; the method works specifically when the dimension of the plane is larger than the separation between the two correlated planes.

## Method

The authors split each spatial plane into cubic bins of size \(n_b \times n_b \times n_b\) lattice sites, sum the operator values within each bin, and compute bin-bin correlators as a function of transverse separation \(s\). They then divide the \(s\) range into three regions: a short-distance region where data is used directly (signal-to-noise better than 10), a middle region where data and a fitted function are linearly combined (signal-to-noise between 10 and 2), and a long-distance tail where only the fitted function is used (signal-to-noise worse than 2). The method is tested on gradient-flowed topological charge density and squared field strength correlators using quenched lattice configurations.

## Key numbers

* Noise reduction by a factor of \(\sim 3-7\) compared to the conventional approach on the same ensemble of configurations.
* Test performed on a \(64^3 \times 16\) quenched lattice with \(10\,000\) configurations, using a bin size of \(4 \times 4 \times 4\).
* For the energy-momentum tensor correlator at temporal separation \(\tau/a = 8\) and flow depth \(\tau_f/a^2 = 1.28\), the correlator \(G(s,\tau)\) falls off rapidly such that only the first few data points (\(s/a \lesssim 15\)) contribute significantly to the integrated correlator.
* The cut point \(s_0\) is chosen as the largest \(s\) value where the signal-to-noise is better than 10; the cut point \(s_{\mathrm{cut}}\) is chosen where the estimated signal-to-noise ratio is consistently worse than 2.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of noise in lattice correlators first explored by Parisi (1983), who showed that disconnected contributions cause the signal-to-noise ratio to not improve with increasing transverse volume. The method is related to the Fourier-based approach of Liu (2017) but differs in how the differential transverse-separation information is used to extract the integrated correlator. The technique is compatible with gradient flow and with unquenched lattice QCD simulations, unlike the multilevel algorithm of Luscher (2001) which is restricted to pure-glue theories.
