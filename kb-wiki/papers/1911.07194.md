# $Îž^- t$ quasibound state instead of $Î›Î›nn$ bound state

> We study the coupled $Î›Î›nn-Îž^- pnn$ system to check whether the inclusion of channel coupling is able to bind the $Î›Î›nn$ system. We use a separable potential three-body model of the coupled $Î›Î›nn - Îž^- pnn$ system as well as a variational four-body calculation with realistic interactions. Our results exclude the possibility of a $Î›Î›nn$ bound state by a large margin. However, we have found a $Îž^- t$ quasibound state above the $Î›Î›nn$ threshold.

## Key claim
The paper excludes the possibility of a bound $^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}$n (i.e., $\Lambda\Lambda nn$) state by a large margin, even when coupling to the $\Xi^- pnn$ channel is included; instead, a narrow $\Xi^- t$ quasibound state exists above the $\Lambda\Lambda nn$ threshold, decaying into that channel.

## Method
The authors used two complementary approaches: (1) a separable-potential three-body model treating the dineutron as an elementary particle, solving coupled Faddeev-type equations for the $\Lambda\Lambda (nn) - \Xi^- p (nn)$ system in the complex energy plane; and (2) a generalized Gaussian variational (GGV) four-body calculation with local central Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon potentials fitted to low-energy parameters and phase shifts from the Nijmegen ESC08c and HAL QCD potentials for the $NN$, $\Lambda N$, $\Lambda\Lambda$, and $\Xi N$ subsystems.

## Key numbers
- The $\Xi^- t$ quasibound state energy (real part) relative to the $\Xi^- pnn$ threshold ranges from $-10.89$ MeV to $-13.52$ MeV across models, with an imaginary part (width) of only $0.04$ to $0.07$ MeV (Table III).
- The $\Xi^- t$ scattering length ranges from $0.957 - i\,0.003$ fm to $1.551 - i\,0.015$ fm across models (Table IIIp).
- In the four-body variational calculation, the $\Xi^- pnn$ bound state energy is $14.43$ MeV (HAL QCD) or $10.78$ MeV (Nijmegen ESC08c) below the $\Xi^- pnn$ threshold; including Coulomb increases these to $15.18$ MeV and $11.31$ MeV, respectively.
- To artificially bind the $\Lambda\Lambda nn$ system, the $\Lambda\Lambda$ attractive strength must be multiplied by a factor $\ge 1.8$ (Nijmegen) or $\ge 1.6$ (HAL QCD), which would produce an almost-bound $\Lambda\Lambda$ state in free space ($a_{^1S_0}^{\Lambda\Lambda} = -29.15$ fm).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior study of the uncoupled $\Lambda\Lambda nn$ system (Garcilazo 2017) and directly contradicts the suggestion by Bleser et al. (2019) that a $^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}$n double hypernucleus could explain structures in the BNL AGS-E906 experiment. It also builds on the three-body model of Garcilazo (2016) for the $\Lambda\Lambda N - \Xi NN$ system and uses the latest $\Lambda\Lambda - \Xi N$ transition potentials from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Sasaki 2018) and the Nijmegen ESC08c potential (Nagels 2015). The result enables future searches for a $\Xi^- t$ quasibound state in experiments, with the calculated scattering lengths useful for predicting energy shifts in $\Xi^- t$ atomic levels.
