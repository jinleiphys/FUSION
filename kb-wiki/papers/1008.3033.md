# Particle transfer reactions with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory using a particle number projection technique

> A particle-number projection technique is used to calculate transfer probabilities in the $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb reaction below the fusion barrier. The time evolution of the many-body wave function is obtained with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) mean-field theory. The agreement with experimental data for the sum of the proton-transfer channels is good, considering that TDHF has no parameter adjusted on reaction mechanism. Some perspectives for extensions beyond TDHF to include cluster-transfers are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a particle-number projection technique applied to TDHF wave functions can compute transfer probabilities in heavy-ion collisions, achieving good agreement with experimental data for the sum of one- and two-proton transfer channels in the 16O+208Pb reaction below the fusion barrier, despite TDHF having no parameter adjusted on reaction mechanism.

## Method

The time evolution of the many-body wave function was obtained using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional, solved on a 3D spatial grid with the tdhf3d code. A particle-number projection operator was applied to the fragments of the final Slater determinant state to extract the probabilities for specific numbers of protons and neutrons in the light fragment, with the projection integral discretized using M=300 gauge angles for convergence. Central collisions of 16O+208Pb were simulated at center-of-mass energies below the fusion barrier, and the resulting transfer probabilities were compared to experimental data converted from angular cross-sections using Rutherford trajectory assumptions.

## Key numbers

- At E_c.m. = 74.44 MeV (just below the fusion barrier), the average proton number in the light fragment was ~6.1 and the average neutron number was ~8.1, indicating two protons transferred on average.
- At E_c.m. = 70 MeV, the average proton and neutron numbers in the light fragment were both ~8.0, showing no net change in average particle numbers.
- The variance of the proton number in the light fragment at E_c.m. = 74.44 MeV was sigma_R^p ~ 0.5, and for neutrons sigma_R^n ~ 0.3.
- At E_c.m. = 70 MeV, both proton and neutron variances were sigma_R^p ~ sigma_R^n ~ 0.2.
- The time step used was Delta t = 1.5 x 10^-24 s, with a spatial grid of 84 x 28 x 14 points and lattice spacing Delta x = 0.8 fm.
- The initial distance between nuclei was 44.8 fm.
- The two-proton transfer probability at the barrier (R_min ~ 12.7 fm) was the most probable channel, while at 65 MeV (~13% below the barrier) transfer probabilities were one or several orders of magnitude lower.

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of nucleon transfer in heavy-ion collisions (Umar 2008, Simenel 2008, Golabek 2009, Washiyama 2009, Kedziora 2010) by introducing a particle-number projection technique to compute individual transfer channel probabilities, rather than just average values. The method is compared to experimental data from Videbaek (1977) for the 16O+208Pb system, and the paper discusses how the approach could be generalized to other many-body systems such as electron transfer in atomic clusters or nuclear fission. The results enable future extensions beyond TDHF to include cluster transfers (e.g., pair or alpha transfer) using TDHF-Bogolyubov theory, time-dependent density-matrix theory, or the time-dependent Generator Coordinate Method.
