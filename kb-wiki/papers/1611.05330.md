# Phases of dense matter with holographic instantons

> We discuss nuclear matter and the transition to quark matter in the decompactified limit of the Sakai-Sugimoto model. Nuclear matter is included through instantons on the flavor branes of the model. Our approximation is based on the flat-space solution, but we allow for a dynamical instanton width and deformation and compute the energetically preferred number of instanton layers in the bulk as a function of the baryon chemical potential. We determine the regions in parameter space where the binding energy of nuclear matter is like in QCD, and compute the phase diagram in the plane of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim
The Sakai-Sugimoto model, in its decompactified limit with a phenomenological constraint on instanton width and deformation, can produce a first-order baryon onset and chiral restoration at high chemical potential, but only within a narrow parameter range; the full unconstrained solution yields only a single instanton layer and no chiral restoration, which is unphysical compared to QCD.

## Method
The authors use the decompactified limit of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, where the asymptotic separation of D8- and anti-D8-branes is small compared to the compactified extra dimension. They approximate nuclear matter by flat-space instanton solutions on the flavor branes, allowing for a dynamical instanton width and deformation parameter, and compute the free energy by solving the equations of motion for the abelian gauge field and the brane embedding. The energetically preferred number of instanton layers in the holographic direction is determined by minimizing the free energy with respect to the instanton density, width, deformation, and layer positions at given temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter in QCD is about 16 MeV, with a vacuum baryon mass M0 = 939 MeV, giving a relative binding energy μ0/M0 ≈ 98%.
- The model reproduces the QCD relative binding energy μ0/M0 = 98% along a specific curve in the ρ0-γ0 parameter space (Fig. 5).
- For comparison, curves with unrealistically large relative binding energies μ0/M0 = 73% and 75% are also shown (Fig. 5).
- The maximum number of energetically preferred instanton layers is Nz = 2 for all checked values of ρ0 and γ0 and all densities.
- The zero-temperature baryon onset is second-order in the unconstrained approximation (instantons become pointlike, ρ → 0, as nI → 0), but becomes first-order in the constrained approach for certain parameter ranges.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter in the Sakai-Sugimoto model that used pointlike instanton approximations (Bergman 2007, Preis 2011) and finite-size instantons (Ghoroku 2012, Li 2015). It contrasts with purely numerical single-baryon solutions (Cherman 2009, Bolognesi 2013, Rozali 2013) by sacrificing some sophistication to handle many-instanton systems. The results enable future matching of the model's five parameters to nuclear saturation properties, potentially providing a unified holographic description of nuclear and quark matter for compact star applications, though the authors note that including instanton interactions in position space (currently in preparation) may modify the phase structure.
