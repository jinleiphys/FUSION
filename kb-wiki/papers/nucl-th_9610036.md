# Effects of state dependent correlations on nucleon density and momentum distributions

> The proton momentum and density distributions of closed shell nuclei are calculated within a model treating short--range correlations up to first order in the cluster expansion. The validity of the model is verified by comparing the results obtained with purely scalar correlations with those produced by finite nuclei Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations. State dependent correlations are used to calculate momentum and density distributions of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca, and the effects of their tensor components are studied.

# Effects of state dependent correlations on nucleon density and momentum distributions

## Key claim

The authors show that a simplified model retaining only first-order cluster expansion diagrams (which conserve particle number) reproduces finite-nuclei Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) results for density and momentum distributions, provided the same scalar correlation functions are used. The state-dependent (especially tensor) components of the correlation produce effects that are localized in momentum space, primarily around 2.0 fm⁻¹, and act in opposite direction to scalar correlations on the density distribution.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-body density matrix (OBDM) for doubly-closed shell nuclei using a Jastrow-type correlated wave function, expanding the correlation operator up to first order in the cluster expansion and retaining only diagrams that conserve particle number (the lowest-order set). Single-particle wave functions are generated from a spherical Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit coupling, and the correlation functions are taken either from finite-nuclei FHNC variational calculations (using the Afnan-Tang S3 interaction) or from nuclear matter FHNC calculations with six active channels (scalar, spin, isospin, spin-isospin, tensor, and tensor-isospin). The proton density distribution is the diagonal of the OBDM, and the momentum distribution is its Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- For all four nuclei studied (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca), correlated and uncorrelated momentum distributions are practically identical up to momentum values of about 1.8–2.0 fm⁻¹, but separate at higher values where uncorrelated distributions descend very rapidly while correlated distributions are orders of magnitude larger.
- The tensor correlation effects in the momentum distribution are localized in a small region around 2.0 fm⁻¹, as shown by the comparison of full correlation results with those omitting tensor channels.
- The scalar channel is the dominant term of the correlation; the tensor term (f₅) is described as "extremely small" and the tensor-isospin term (f₆) peaks at an inter-particle distance of about 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the simplified correlation model developed in Co (1995) to calculate both density and momentum distributions, and validates it against finite-nuclei FHNC calculations by Arias de Saavedra et al. (1996) that were limited to central interactions and scalar correlations. The model enables systematic study of state-dependent correlation effects in medium-mass nuclei (¹²C through ⁴⁸Ca) without the full complexity of FHNC calculations, though the authors note that the numerical effort grows rapidly with the number of single-particle states, making ²⁰⁸Pb calculations extremely heavy. The approach contrasts with Variational Monte Carlo calculations of ¹⁶O by Pieper et al. (1992) and with the more complete but computationally intensive FHNC theory.
