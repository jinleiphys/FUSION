# Baryon and Meson Excited States

> The masses of fifteen baryon sets and twenty-four meson sets of three or more equal-quantum excited states are fitted by a simple two-parameter logarithm function, $M_n = αLn(n) + β$, where $n$ is the level of radial excitation. The conjecture is made that accurately measured masses using Breat-Wigner PDG2024 data at fixed $J^P$ for baryons and $J^{PC}$ for mesons of all equal-quantum baryons (including LHCb exotic $P_{c\bar{c}}^+$s) and meson (including $s\bar{s}$, $s\bar{c}$, $c\bar{c}$, $c\bar{b}$, and $b\bar{b}$) excited states are related by the logarithm function used here; at least for the mass range of currently known excited states. Thus, a universal mass equation for equal-quantum excited-states sets is presented. The masses of twelve baryon sets and sixteen meson sets, with only two equal-quantum excited states in each set, using Breit-Wigner PDG2024 masses and their uncertainties, are fitted by thr universal mass equation.

## Key claim
The masses of baryon and meson excited-state sets with equal quantum numbers (fixed \(J^P\) for baryons, fixed \(J^{PC}\) for mesons) can be described by a universal mass equation \(M_n = \alpha \ln(n) + \beta\), at least for the mass range of currently known excited states; the authors present this as a conjecture rather than a proven law, noting that the fit works for sets with three or more states and also for "duo sets" with only two known states.

## Method
The authors collected Breit-Wigner masses from the PDG2024 listings for baryon sets at fixed \(J^P\) and meson sets at fixed \(J^{PC}\). They performed \(\chi^2\) minimization fits of the two-parameter logarithm function \(M_n = \alpha \ln(n) + \beta\) to each set, where \(n\) is the radial excitation level. For sets with only two known excited states (duo sets), they fixed \(\beta = M_1\) and determined \(\alpha\) from the two masses. They then used the fits to predict masses of missing or higher excited states and evaluated measurability by calculating Breit-Wigner overlap curves.

## Key numbers
- For the \(N1/2^+\) baryon set, the universal mass equation fit predicted one missing excited state and four higher excited states of the proton/neutron.
- For the baryon systematics, a power-equation fit of \(\alpha\) versus \(M_1\) gave \(\alpha = 1.688 \times 10^7 \, M_1^{-1.446}~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(c\bar{c}\) meson sets, the linear fit of \(\alpha\) versus \(M_1\) gave \(\alpha = -(0.7539 \pm 0.0045) M_1 + (3190.7 \pm 14.1)~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(b\bar{b}\) meson sets, the linear fit gave \(\alpha = -(0.6359 \pm 0.0028) M_1 + (6809.3 \pm 27.4)~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The de Broglie wavelength for a mass is \(\lambda_n = 2\pi (197.327)/M_n~\mathrm{fm}\), and the wavefunction radius is taken as \(r_n = (\pi/2)(\hbar c / M_n)\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Roper 2024, Roper 2025) that began with excited states of the proton/neutron and then generalized to other equal-quantum combinations. It uses the PDG2024 data (Particle Data Group 2024) as the experimental basis. The universal mass equation is presented as an empirical alternative to more complex theoretical models; the authors connect it to the Cornell potential (Eichten 1974) by plotting excited-state masses versus an estimated wavefunction radius. The work enables experimentalists to predict masses of missing or higher excited states in baryon and meson spectra, and it highlights the challenge of "bump hunting" due to overlapping resonances, as shown by Breit-Wigner overlap calculations.
