# The pasta phase and its consequences on neutrino opacities

> In this paper, we calculate the diffusion coefficients that are related to the neutrino opacities considering the formation of nuclear pasta and homogeneous matter at low densities. Our results show that the mean free paths are significantly altered by the presence of nuclear pasta in stellar matter when compared with the results obtained with homogeneous matter. These differences in neutrino opacities certainly influence the Kelvin-Helmholtz phase of protoneutron stars and consequently the results of supernova explosion simulations.

# The pasta phase and its consequences on neutrino opacities

## Key claim

The presence of nuclear pasta structures (droplets, rods, slabs) at low baryonic densities in protoneutron star matter significantly reduces the neutrino diffusion coefficients compared to homogeneous matter, which will alter the Kelvin-Helmholtz cooling phase; however, the pasta phase only exists up to temperatures around 12 MeV and densities below 0.1 fm⁻³ for the NL3 parametrization, and the diffusion coefficients show a gap at the transition density where homogeneous matter becomes the ground state.

## Method

The authors used the nonlinear Walecka model (NL3 parametrization) in a mean-field approximation to describe nucleons and leptons in β-equilibrium with trapped electron neutrinos. The pasta phase was constructed using the coexistence phases (CP) method, solving Gibbs conditions for two coexisting phases with surface and Coulomb energy contributions, for three geometries: droplets (3D), rods (2D), and slabs (1D). Neutrino opacities were computed from neutral-current scattering on neutrons and protons and charged-current absorption on neutrons, with cross sections taken from Burrows (1986) and others, and the diffusion coefficients D₂, D₃, and D₄ were evaluated from the Rosseland mean free paths.

## Key numbers

- The pasta phase exists at densities of 0.006–0.1 fm⁻³ in neutral nuclear matter and 0.029–0.065 fm⁻³ in β-equilibrium stellar matter, as cited from prior work.
- The NL3 parametrization gives saturation density 0.148 fm⁻³, binding energy 16.3 MeV, compressibility 272 MeV, and effective mass 0.6 M at saturation.
- Diffusion coefficients were calculated for baryonic densities above 0.005 fm⁻³, with temperatures from 0 to 50 MeV, and lepton fractions Y_L = 0.2 and 0.4.
- For D₂ at T = 5 MeV and Y_L = 0.4, the pasta and homogeneous curves cross at ρ = 0.12 fm⁻³.
- The pasta phase diffusion coefficients are always lower than the homogeneous matter coefficients; D₂ and D₄ are one order of magnitude larger than results from Pons (1998) at low densities, but coincide at higher densities.
- The reference cross section σ₀ = 1.76 × 10⁻⁴⁴ cm² and axial coupling g_A = 1.254 are used.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of neutrino opacities in protoneutron stars by Pons (1999, 2001) and Horowitz (2004), who studied the linear response of nuclear pasta to neutrinos using semi-classical simulations, and Sonoda (2007), who found that pasta occupies 10–20% of the supernova core mass. The authors use the coexistence phases method previously applied by the same group (pasta1, pasta2) rather than the Thomas-Fermi or quantum molecular dynamics approaches. The results enable future simulations of the Kelvin-Helmholtz phase of protoneutron star evolution with realistic pasta-phase opacities, and the authors note that a planned improvement is to include α-particles and use a relativistic surface tension as in pasta_alpha.
