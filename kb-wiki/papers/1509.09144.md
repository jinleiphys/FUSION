# Projectile deformation effects in the breakup of $^{37}$Mg

> We study the breakup of $^{37}$Mg on Pb at 244MeV/u with the recently developed extended theory of Coulomb breakup within the post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation that includes deformation of the projectile. Comparing our calculated cross section with the available Coulomb breakup data we determine the possible ground state configuration of $^{37}$Mg.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{37}$Mg most likely has spin-parity $J^\pi = 3/2^-$ (from the $2p_{3/2}$ orbital) with a one-neutron separation energy of $0.10 \pm 0.02$ MeV, but this conclusion depends on the chosen spectroscopic factor; a $J^\pi = 1/2^+$ assignment cannot be fully excluded based on the present data due to uncertainty in the spectroscopic factor for the $2s_{1/2}$ configuration.

## Method

The authors used the post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation extended to include projectile deformation via an axially symmetric quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potential for the fragment-fragment interaction. They calculated pure Coulomb total one-neutron removal cross sections ($\sigma_{-1n}$) for the breakup of $^{37}$Mg on a Pb target at 244 MeV/nucleon, considering neutron removal from $2p_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$, and $1f_{7/2}$ orbitals with shell-model spectroscopic factors ($C^2S$). The calculated cross sections were compared to experimental data from Kobayashi et al. (2014) as a function of one-neutron separation energy ($S_{-1n}$) and quadrupole deformation parameter ($\beta_2$).

## Key numbers

- For the $^{36}$Mg$(0^+)\otimes 2p_{3/2}\nu$ configuration with shell-model $C^2S = 0.31$, calculated cross sections overlap with the experimental data band in the $S_{-1n}$ region of $0.10 \pm 0.02$ MeV.
- For the $^{36}$Mg$(0^+)\otimes 2s_{1/2}\nu$ configuration with $C^2S = 0.40$ (as extracted by Kobayashi et al. (2014)), the extracted $S_{-1n}$ is $0.26 \pm 0.04$ MeV, compared to $0.40^{+0.19}_{-0.13}$ MeV deduced in that reference.
- For the $^{36}$Mg$(0^+)\otimes 2p_{3/2}\nu$ configuration with $S_{-1n} = 0.22$ MeV and $C^2S = 0.42$, overlap with data occurs for $0.35 < \beta_2 < 0.68$; with $C^2S = 0.31$, overlap occurs for $0.62 < \beta_2 < 0.94$.
- For the $^{36}$Mg$(0^+)\otimes 2s_{1/2}\nu$ configuration with $C^2S = 0.40$ and $S_{-1n} = 0.40$ MeV, there is no overlap between calculated cross sections and the data band for any $\beta_2$ in the range $0$ to $1.0$ (or beyond).

## Context

This work extends the study of Coulomb breakup from light exotic nuclei into the deformed medium-mass region of the "island of inversion," following prior work on $^{23}$O and $^{31}$Ne. It addresses controversies regarding the ground state configuration of $^{37}$Mg, where Kobayashi et al. (2014) argued for a $3/2^-$ assignment from $2p_{3/2}$ and Takechi et al. suggested a halo structure from reaction cross sections. The results support a $J^\pi = 3/2^-$ ground state but note that a $1/2^+$ assignment cannot be fully excluded; the authors call for future measurements of exclusive observables such as core-valence neutron relative energy spectra and angular distributions to provide unambiguous confirmation.
