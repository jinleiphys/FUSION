# 3N Scattering in a Three-Dimensional Operator Formulation

> A recently developed formulation for a direct treatment of the equations for two- and three-nucleon bound states as set of coupled equations of scalar functions depending only on vector momenta is extended to three-nucleon scattering. Starting from the spin-momentum dependence occurring as scalar products in two- and three-nucleon forces together with other scalar functions, we present the Faddeev multiple scattering series in which order by order the spin-degrees can be treated analytically leading to 3D integrations over scalar functions depending on momentum vectors only. Such formulation is especially important in view of awaiting extension of 3N Faddeev calculations to projectile energies above the pion production threshold and applications of chiral perturbation theory 3N forces, which are to be most efficiently treated directly in such three-dimensional formulation without having to expand these forces into a partial wave basis.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed three-dimensional (3D) operator formulation for two- and three-nucleon bound states to three-nucleon scattering, showing that the Faddeev multiple scattering series can be evaluated order by order by treating spin degrees of freedom analytically, resulting in 3D integrations over scalar functions of momentum vectors only. This avoids the need for partial-wave decomposition, which becomes impractical at projectile energies above the pion production threshold.

## Method

The authors start from the spin-momentum operator structure of the NN t-operator, which consists of six scalar spin-momentum operators multiplied by scalar functions of momenta. They apply this operator representation to the Faddeev multiple scattering series for three-nucleon scattering, working out the first-order term (one NN t-operator) and the second-order term (two NN t-operators) explicitly. The spin operators are combined analytically into scalar functions of the vector momenta, leaving only 3D momentum integrations over scalar functions. The method handles the moving logarithmic singularities that appear in the second order by replacing them with single poles, similar to the treatment of poles in the 2N Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

* The NN t-operator expansion requires exactly 6 scalar spin-momentum operators due to parity and time reversal invariance.
* The initial deuteron state is expressed in terms of 2 scalar functions phi_1(p) and phi_2(p), proportional to the standard s- and d-wave components of the deuteron wave function.
* The free 3N propagator G_0 depends on the total c.m. energy E and the Jacobi momenta via E_q = E - (3/(4m)) q^2.
* The np t-matrix for t=0 has a deuteron pole at E_q = E_d, expressed as t_{np}^{00} = hat{t}_{np}^{00} / (E_q + i epsilon - E_d).

## Context

This work extends the 3D operator formulation previously developed for 2N and 3N bound states by Gloeckle, Fachruddin, Elster, Golak, Skibinski, and Witala (2009) to the scattering regime. It builds on prior three-boson scattering calculations in a Faddeev scheme using momentum vectors directly by Liu et al. (2004), and on Poincare invariant formulations for three-boson scattering by Lin et al. (2008). The approach is motivated by the need to go beyond partial-wave decomposition for projectile energies above the pion production threshold and to efficiently incorporate chiral perturbation theory 3N forces without expanding them into partial waves.
