# Orbital Angular Momentum at Small $x$

> We revisit the problem of the small Bjorken-$x$ asymptotics of the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions in the proton utilizing the revised formalism for small-$x$ helicity evolution derived recently in a paper by Cougoulic, Kovchegov, Tarasov, and Tawabutr. We relate the quark and gluon OAM distributions at small $x$ to the polarized dipole amplitudes and their (first) impact-parameter moments. To obtain the $x$-dependence of the OAM distributions, we derive novel small-$x$ evolution equations for the impact-parameter moments of the polarized dipole amplitudes in the double-logarithmic approximation (summing powers of $α_s \ln^2(1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant). We solve these evolution equations numerically and extract the large-$N_c$, small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark and gluon OAM distributions, which we determine to be \[ L_{q+\bar{q}}(x, Q^2) \sim L_{G}(x,Q^2) \sim ΔΣ(x, Q^2) \sim ΔG(x,Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{3.66 \, \sqrt{\frac{α_s N_c}{2π}}},\] in agreement with an earlier work by Boussarie, Hatta, and Yuan within the precision of our numerical evaluation (here $N_c$ is the number of quark colors). We also investigate the ratios of the quark and gluon OAM distributions to their helicity distribution counterparts in the small-$x$ region.

## Key claim

The quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions at small Bjorken-\(x\) have the same asymptotic behavior as the helicity parton distribution functions, scaling as \((1/x)^{3.66\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)}}\), but the numerical ratios of OAM to helicity distributions differ from the predictions of Boussarie, Hatta, and Yuan (2019) and require further analytic study to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The author relates the quark and gluon OAM distributions at small \(x\) to polarized dipole amplitudes and their first impact-parameter moments, using the revised small-\(x\) helicity evolution formalism of Cougoulic, Kovchegov, Tarasov, and Tawabutr (2022). Novel double-logarithmic-approximation evolution equations for the impact-parameter moments of the polarized dipole amplitudes are derived in the large-\(N_c\) limit, summing powers of \(\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)\). These equations are solved numerically with a finite step-size discretization, and the continuum limit is extracted by fitting a polynomial model in step size and inverse maximum rapidity.

## Key numbers

* The small-\(x\), large-\(N_c\) asymptotic intercept for all OAM distributions and helicity PDFs is \(3.66 \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)}\), extracted from numerical solutions of the evolution equations.
* The quark OAM distribution \(L_{q+\bar{q}}(x, Q^2)\) and gluon OAM distribution \(L_G(x, Q^2)\) both scale as \((1/x)^{3.66\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)}}\).
* The constant term \(A_q(Q^2)\) in the ratio \(L_{q+\bar{q}}/\Delta\Sigma\) and \(A_G(Q^2)\) in \(L_G/\Delta G\) are computed as functions of \(Q^2\) (in GeV\(^2\)) and shown in Figure 2 of the paper; numerical values are close to but not exactly \(-1\) and \(-2\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Kovchegov (2019) by including contributions from the gluon polarized dipole amplitude \(G^i_{10}\) that were previously omitted, and it derives the first evolution equations for the impact-parameter moments of polarized dipole amplitudes. The result that OAM distributions share the same small-\(x\) asymptotics as helicity PDFs agrees with Boussarie, Hatta, and Yuan (2019), but the numerical ratios of OAM to helicity distributions differ from that work's predictions, motivating future analytic solutions of the evolution equations. The formalism enables improved theoretical input for extrapolating OAM measurements to the experimentally inaccessible \(x \to 0\) region.
