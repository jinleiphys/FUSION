# On the similarity of the parton distributions in nuclei with more than three nucleons

> It is shown that the latest results from NMC and E665 on the $F_2^A(x)/F_2^{D}(x)$ obtained in the shadowing region, bring new evidence of the universal $A$ -- dependence of distorsions of a free nucleon structure function by nuclear medium. The universality holds in the entire $x$ -range and can be explained as a saturation of the distortions of the parton distributions in a four-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the distortions of the free-nucleon structure function in nuclei, as measured by the ratio \(F_2^A(x)/F_2^{\rm D}(x)\), exhibit a universal \(A\)-dependence for all nuclei with \(A \geq 4\), with the qualification that this universality may be explained by a saturation of the distortions already in a four-nucleon system (or possibly even in a three-nucleon system, pending data on \(^3\)He).

## Method

The author performed a global fit of the ratio \(r^A(x) = F_2^A(x)/F_2^{\rm D}(x)\) using a three-parameter factorization (Eq. 1) for data in the range \(10^{-3} < x < 0.7\) from NMC and E665, and a four-parameter extension (Eq. 2) for SLAC and BCDMS data in the range \(0.2 < x < 0.9\). The parameters \(m_i\) (distortion magnitudes) were extracted for seven nuclear targets (He, Li, C, Ca, Xe, Cu, Pb) and their \(A\)-dependence was fitted with a function proportional to \((1 - A_{\rm S}/A)\), where \(A_{\rm S}\) is the number of surface nucleons from a Woods–Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(r^A(x) = F_2^A(x)/F_2^{\rm D}(x)\) was fitted over the range \(10^{-3} < x < 0.7\) for seven nuclei, with \(\chi^2 / \text{d.o.f.} \leq 1\) for each.
* The distortion parameters \(m_i\) increase from their minimum value \(m_i(\text{He})\) at \(A = 4\) to approximately \(3\,m_i(\text{He})\) for nuclei with \(A > 40\).
* The relative distortion scale \(s_{\rm h} = m_i(A)/m_i(\text{He})\) varies from 1 at \(A=4\) to about 3 for \(^{207}\text{Pb}\).
* The data cover a \(Q^2\) range from 0.2 GeV\(^2\) to 200 GeV\(^2\), over which \(r^A(x)\) is claimed to be independent of \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier factorization approach of Smirnov (1994, 1995) and the nuclear-density-based model of Barshay (1985) to include new data from NMC and E665 in the shadowing region. It contradicts the notion that the EMC effect shape changes significantly with \(A\) beyond \(A=4\), instead proposing that the shape is frozen after saturation in the four-nucleon system. The results enable a two-stage model of nuclear parton distortions (hard distortions for \(A \leq 4\), soft density-driven distortions for \(A > 4\)) and call for future measurements on \(^3\)He to test whether saturation occurs at \(A=3\) or \(A=4\).
