# Equivalence of channel-corrected T-matrix and anomalous propagator approach

> Any many-body approximation corrected for unphysical repeated collisions in a given condensation channel is shown to provide the same set of equations as they appear by using anomalous propagators. The ad-hoc assumption in the latter theory about non-conservation of particle numbers can be released. In this way the widespread used anomalous propagator approach is given another physical interpretation. A generalized Soven equation follows which improves any approximation in the same way as the coherent potential approximation (CPA) improves the averaged T-matrix for impurity scattering.

# Equivalence of channel-corrected T-matrix and anomalous propagator approach

## Key claim

The Dyson equation can be rewritten exactly into a form containing anomalous propagators and selfenergies if the repeated collisions in one singular channel are subtracted, showing that the anomalous propagator approach is an exact rewriting rather than requiring an ad-hoc assumption about particle number non-conservation. The channel-corrected selfenergy obeys a generalized Soven equation that improves any chosen approximation in the same way as the coherent potential approximation (CPA) improves the averaged T-matrix for impurity scattering.

## Method

The author starts from the standard Dyson equation and splits the selfenergy into channels, subtracting the singular channel where condensation occurs to remove unphysical repeated collisions with the same state. By rewriting the propagator with the subtracted channel and using the separability of the singular-channel T-matrix, the author shows that the resulting equations take the matrix form of the Beliaev equations for bosons or the Nambu-Gorkov equations for fermions. A generalized Soven equation is then derived in operator form by introducing a concentration operator D and an effective potential, which becomes an algebraic equation in the appropriate representation.

## Key numbers

- The free propagator is given as $G_0^{-1} = \omega - \epsilon_k$ and $\bar G_0^{-1} = -\omega - \epsilon_{-k}$.
- The singular-channel T-matrix is separable and written $T_i(p) = \mp \Delta(p) \bar \Delta(p)$ for bosons/fermions respectively.
- The resulting propagator takes the form $G_{11} = (\omega + \epsilon_{-k} + \bar \Sigma_{11}) / [(\omega + \epsilon_{-k} + \bar \Sigma_{11})(\omega - \epsilon_k - \Sigma_{11}) \pm \Sigma_{12}^2]$.
- The anomalous Green function is defined as $G_{12} \equiv \mp \Sigma_{12} / [(\omega + \epsilon_{-k} + \bar \Sigma_{11})(\omega - \epsilon_k - \Sigma_{11}) \pm \Sigma_{12}^2]$.
- For the averaged T-matrix approximation with $K = G_\s$ and closing by concentration $c$, the standard Soven equation becomes $(1-c) \Sigma_i(p) / [1 + \Sigma_i(p) G(p)] = c [V(p) - \Sigma_i(p)] / [1 + (\Sigma_i(p) - V(p)) G(p)]$.
- For the two-particle T-matrix with $K = G \cdot G_\s$, the concentration operator is $D(p) = \sum_{\bar{\bf p}, \bar \omega} V(({\bf \bar p - p})/2) G(-\bar p) / \tilde V_i(p) = \Sigma_H({\bf p}) / \tilde V_i(p)$, where $\Sigma_H$ is the Hartree selfenergy.

## Context

This work resolves the Prange paradox (Prange, 1960), where a seemingly worse approximation (asymmetric selfconsistency) gave better results than the full self-consistent T-matrix, by showing that removing repeated collisions with the same state (as proposed by Lipavsky (2008) and Sopik et al. (2009)) yields the correct gap equation. The paper establishes the equivalence between this channel-corrected T-matrix approach and the well-established anomalous propagator formalism of Beliaev (1958) for bosons and Nambu-Gorkov (1958) for fermions, reviewed by Shi and Griffin (1998). The generalized Soven equation extends the coherent potential approximation (CPA) developed by Soven (1967), Velicky et al. (1968), and others, from impurity scattering to two-particle scattering in any approximation scheme, enabling systematic improvement of approximations for pairing and condensation phenomena in nuclear, solid-state, and plasma physics.
