# Lambda (1405) photoproduction based on chiral unitary model

> Recent CLAS data for the pi Sigma invariant mass distributions (line-shapes) in the gamma p -> K^+ pi Sigma reaction are theoretically investigated. The line-shapes have peaks associated with the Lambda(1405) excitation. Our model consists of gauge invariant photo-production mechanisms, and the chiral unitary model that gives the rescattering amplitudes where Lambda(1405) is contained. It is found that, while the pi Sigma line-shape data in the Lambda(1405) region are successfully reproduced by our model for all the charge states, the production mechanism is not so simple that we need to introduce parameters associated with short-range dynamics to fit the data. Our detailed analysis suggests that the nonresonant background contribution is not negligible, and its sizable effect shifts the Lambda(1405) peak position by several MeV. We also analyze the data using a Breit-Wigner amplitudes instead of those from the chiral unitary model. We find that the fitted Breit-Wigner parameters are closer to the higher pole position for Lambda(1405) of the chiral unitary model. This work sets a starting point for a fuller analysis in which line-shape as well as K^+ angular distribution data are simultaneously analyzed for extracting Lambda(1405) pole(s).

## Key claim

The CLAS $\pi\Sigma$ line-shape data for $\gamma p \to K^+ \pi \Sigma$ are successfully reproduced by a model combining gauge-invariant photoproduction mechanisms with chiral unitary rescattering amplitudes, but only after introducing adjustable short-range parameters (contact terms and modified subtraction constants) to fit the data, implying the production mechanism is not simple.

## Method

The authors constructed a gauge-invariant tree-level model for $\gamma p \to K^+ M_j B_j$ photoproduction using gauged Weinberg-Tomozawa terms, Born terms, and vector-meson exchange mechanisms, followed by final-state rescattering described by chiral unitary model amplitudes for $(J,L)=(1/2,0)$ and $(1/2,1)$ partial waves. They introduced phenomenological contact terms to simulate short-range dynamics (e.g., baryon resonances) and allowed the subtraction constants in the meson-baryon Green functions to vary per production mechanism group, fitting the resulting $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass distributions to CLAS data at $W=2.0$, 2.1, 2.2, and 2.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The chiral unitary model gives two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles at $1426-16i$ MeV and $1390-66i$ MeV, with the higher pole coupling more strongly to $\bar K N$ and the lower to $\pi\Sigma$.
- The single Breit-Wigner fit to the CLAS line-shape data yields $M_{BW}=1412$ MeV and $\Gamma_{BW}=67$ MeV, closer to the higher pole position.
- The nonresonant background contribution shifts the $\Lambda(1405)$ peak position by "several MeV" (no exact number given).
- Data were fitted at four center-of-mass energies: $W=2.0$, 2.1, 2.2, and 2.3 GeV.
- The CLAS experiment covered center-of-mass energies $1.95 < W < 2.85$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Nacher et al. (1999) by including more gauge-invariant production mechanisms and fitting the high-statistics CLAS data from Moriya et al. (2013), which provided the first simultaneous measurement of all three $\pi\Sigma$ charge states in photoproduction. The analysis sets the stage for a fuller extraction of the $\Lambda(1405)$ pole(s) by simultaneously fitting both the $\pi\Sigma$ line-shape data and the $K^+$ angular distribution data from CLAS (Moriya et al., 2013), which will help constrain the production mechanism and reduce model dependence.
