# Nuclear Zemach Moments and Finite-Size Corrections to Allowed Beta Decay

> The finite-size correction to $β$-decay plays an important role in determining the expected antineutrino spectra from reactors at a level that is important for the reactor-neutrino anomaly. Here we express the leading-order finite-size correction to allowed $β$-decay in terms of Zemach moments. We calculate the Zemach moments within a Hartree-Fock model using a Skyrme-like energy density functional. We find that the Zemach moments are increased relative to predictions based on the simple assumption of identical uniform nuclear-charge and weak-transition densities. However, for allowed ground-state to ground-state transitions in medium and heavy nuclei, the detailed nuclear structure calculations do not change the finite-size corrections significantly from the simple model predictions, and are only 10-15% larger than the latter even though the densities differ significantly.

## Key claim

The leading-order finite-size correction to allowed beta decay can be expressed in terms of nuclear Zemach moments; although the detailed Hartree-Fock charge and weak transition densities differ significantly from the simple assumption of identical uniform densities, the resulting Zemach moments for medium and heavy nuclei are only 10-15% larger than the uniform-density predictions, so the simple model remains reasonably accurate for reactor antineutrino spectra.

## Method

The authors expressed the finite-size correction to allowed Gamow-Teller and Fermi beta decay in terms of two Zemach moments, ⟨r⟩_(2) and ⟨r⟩^r_(2), derived from Holstein's form-factor integrals. They calculated these moments using a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with the SLy4 energy density functional and the code HFBRAD, assuming spherical symmetry, for nine ground-state-to-ground-state beta-decay transitions spanning light to heavy nuclei. The convoluted Zemach densities were computed from the normalized charge density and the weak transition density obtained from the product of neutron and proton single-particle radial wave functions.

## Key numbers

* For the uniform-density approximation with R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm, the Zemach moment ⟨r⟩_(2) = (36/35)R.
* The Hartree-Fock ⟨r⟩_(2) for heavy nuclei is about 10% higher than the uniform-density estimate; for the transition ¹⁰⁰Nb → ¹⁰⁰Mo, the uniform value is 5.73 fm and the mixed (Hartree-Fock) value is 6.28 fm.
* The smaller Zemach-like moment ⟨r⟩^r_(2) from Hartree-Fock is about 30% higher than the uniform-density value; for ¹⁰⁰Nb → ¹⁰⁰Mo, the uniform value is 2.86 fm and the mixed value is 3.76 fm.
* The ratio ⟨r⟩_(2)/⟨r⟩^r_(2) for the mixed case is approximately 1.7, compared to the equal-density prediction of 2.0.
* For ¹⁰⁰Nb → ¹⁰⁰Mo, the improved treatment changes the slope of the finite-size correction by 12%, which translates into a maximum change of 0.5% in the shape of the beta-decay spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of finite-size corrections to beta decay by Hayes et al. (2014), which used the simple assumption of equal uniform charge and weak transition densities with R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm. The authors connect the correction to Zemach moments, which are also central to atomic hyperfine splitting (Zemach, 1956) and the Lamb shift (Friar, 1979). The results enable improved estimates of the reactor neutrino anomaly by quantifying the uncertainty in the finite-size correction; the authors note that about 30% of reactor antineutrino spectra come from forbidden transitions, which require more complicated treatment not addressed here.
