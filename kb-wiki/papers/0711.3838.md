# The Δcontribution to the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force

> Because the nucleon may be excited and transformed into a virtual $Δ$ resonance easily, we consider the decuplet contribution to the parity-violating (PV) nucleon-nucleon interaction in the chiral effective field theory. The effective PV nucleon-nucleon potential is derived without introducing any unknown coupling constants.

## Key claim

The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential receives a contribution from virtual \(\Delta\) baryon intermediate states, calculable in chiral effective field theory without introducing any unknown coupling constants beyond the known strong coupling \(g_{\pi N\Delta}\) and the weak coupling \(h_\pi^1\); the abstract omits the condition that this result holds only to next-next-leading order (\(\mathcal{O}(Q)\)) in the small-scale expansion.

## Method

The authors employ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom, using the small-scale expansion (SSE) where both the pion mass and the nucleon-\(\Delta\) mass difference \(\delta = m_\Delta - m_N\) are counted as \(\mathcal{O}(Q)\). They compute two-particle-irreducible (2PI) cross and box diagrams with one parity-violating \(\pi N\) vertex and one parity-conserving \(\pi N\Delta\) vertex, retaining only non-analytic terms after dimensional regularization. The resulting momentum-space potential is expressed in terms of the loop functions \(L(Q)\), \(A(Q)\), \(B(Q)\), and \(C(Q)\), which depend on the pion mass \(m_\pi\), the mass splitting \(\delta\), and the momentum transfer \(Q\).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nucleon axial coupling: \(g_A \simeq 1.27\)
- Pion mass used in numerical comparison: \(m_\pi = 135\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nucleon-\(\Delta\) mass difference: \(\delta = 294\ \text{MeV}\)
- Strong coupling relation from quark model: \(g_{\pi N\Delta} = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{5} g_A\)
- Weak coupling scale: \(h_\pi^1 \sim 10^{-7}\)
- The coefficient \(\tilde{C}_2^\Delta(Q)\) and the two-pion-exchange coefficient \(\tilde{C}_2^{2\pi}(Q)\) are comparable in magnitude but have opposite signs at small momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the effective-field-theory treatment of the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon force by Zhu, Maekawa, Holstein, Ramsey-Musolf, and van Kolck (2005), which did not include explicit \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom. The \(\Delta\) contribution was noted earlier in meson-exchange models by various authors (e.g., roleDelta, roleDelta2, roleDelta3) and in the DDH framework extended to \(\Delta\) (DeltaPVN). The present result provides the \(\Delta\)-induced medium-range potential that can be incorporated into future calculations of parity-violating observables such as the longitudinal asymmetry in \(pp\to pp\) or the photon asymmetry in \(\vec{n}p\to d\gamma\), complementing the two-pion exchange contributions already derived in the EFT framework.
