# Constraining the initial state granularity with bulk observables in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV

> In this paper we conduct a systematic study of the granularity of the initial state of hot and dense QCD matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and its influence on bulk observables like particle yields, $m_T$ spectra and elliptic flow. For our investigation we use a hybrid transport model, based on (3+1)d hydrodynamics and a microscopic Boltzmann transport approach. The initial conditions are generated by a non-equilibrium hadronic transport approach and the size of their fluctuations can be adjusted by defining a Gaussian smoothing parameter $σ$. The dependence of the hydrodynamic evolution on the choices of $σ$ and $t_{start}$ is explored by means of a Gaussian emulator. To generate particle yields and elliptic flow that are compatible with experimental data the initial state parameters are constrained to be $σ=1$ fm and $t_{\rm start}=0.5$ fm. In addition, the influence of changes in the equation of state is studied and the results of our event-by-event calculations are compared to a calculation with averaged initial conditions. We conclude that even though the initial state parameters can be constrained by yields and elliptic flow, the granularity needs to be constrained by other correlation and fluctuation observables.

## Key claim
The initial state parameters (Gaussian smoothing width σ and hydrodynamic starting time t_start) in a hybrid transport+hydrodynamic model for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV can be constrained by bulk observables like particle yields and elliptic flow, but the granularity of the initial state itself cannot be determined from these observables alone and requires correlation and fluctuation observables.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid model combining (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with a hadronic cascade (UrQMD) for initial non-equilibrium dynamics and final-state rescattering. Initial conditions were generated from UrQMD events, with point particles smeared into Gaussian distributions of width σ to control granularity; the transition time from transport to hydrodynamics (t_start) was also varied. A Gaussian process emulator was trained on 30 model evaluations across the two-dimensional parameter space (σ, t_start) to efficiently explore parameter combinations that reproduce experimental data for particle yields, transverse mass spectra, and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers
- Default parameters constrained by data: σ = 1 fm and t_start = 0.5 fm
- σ was varied between 0.8 fm (lower limit for numerical stability) and 2.0 fm (smooth profiles)
- Parameter pairs yielding same pion yield: (σ=0.8 fm, t_start=0.3 fm), (σ=1.0 fm, t_start=0.5 fm), (σ=1.5 fm, t_start=1.2 fm), (σ=2.0 fm, t_start=1.8 fm)
- Central collisions defined by impact parameter b < 3.4 fm; mid-central collisions by b = 5-9 fm
- Midrapidity range used: |y| < 0.5
- Freeze-out criterion: energy density ≈ 713 MeV/fm³
- Each model evaluation took approximately 3 CPU-hours

## Context
This work extends earlier hybrid model studies by Petersen et al. (2008) at lower energies and addresses the growing interest in initial-state fluctuations for multi-particle correlations, including elliptic flow, triangular flow, and ridge correlations (Agakishiev 2010, Alver 2010, Schenke 2010). The study demonstrates that bulk observables are insensitive to initial-state granularity, confirming earlier findings by Petersen et al. (2010) at lower energies, and that averaging over initial states yields similar results to event-by-event calculations. The results enable future multi-parameter fits using statistical emulators to constrain transport coefficients like shear viscosity, and highlight the need for correlation and fluctuation observables to determine granularity.
