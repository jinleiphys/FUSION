# Can $J/ψ$ suppression and $p_T$ broadening signal the deconfinement transition at RHIC?

> We have analyzed the latest NA50 data on $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS. It is shown that a QCD based nuclear absorption model, where $J/ψ$'s are absorbed in nuclear medium could explain the latest NA50 data on the centrality dependence of the $J/ψ$ over Drell-Yan ratio. The model also explains the NA50 data on $J/ψ$ over minimum bias ratio and the $p_T$ broadening of $J/ψ$'s. A QGP based threshold model where all the $J/ψ$'s are suppressed above a threshold density, also explains the data sets with smeared threshold density. Even at RHIC energy, centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression or $p_T$ broadening could not distinguish between the two models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that even at RHIC energy, the centrality dependence of J/psi suppression and p_T broadening cannot distinguish between a QCD-based nuclear absorption model (without deconfinement) and a QGP-based threshold model (with deconfinement), because both models produce nearly identical predictions for these observables.

## Method

The author analyzed the final NA50 data from the 2000 run of 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, fitting the centrality dependence of the J/psi over Drell-Yan ratio, the J/psi over minimum bias ratio, and the p_T broadening of J/psi. Two models were compared: a QCD-based nuclear absorption model where J/psi absorption in nuclear medium is parameterized via a square momentum gain factor per unit length, and a QGP-based threshold model where all J/psi are suppressed above a critical transverse density, with parameters fixed from high-statistics pA data. The models were then extrapolated to predict observables at RHIC energy for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- J/psi-nucleon absorption cross section from high statistics 450 GeV pA data: sigma_abs = 4.4 +/- 1.0 mb
- Common sigma_abs from pA and NA38 200 GeV/c S+U data: 4.4 +/- 0.5 mb
- Square momentum gain factor from pA data fit: epsilon^2 = 0.187 GeV^2/fm
- Threshold density for one-parameter threshold model fit to latest NA50 data: n_c = 3.78 fm^-2
- Two-parameter threshold model fit: n_c = 3.98 fm^-2 and lambda = 0.82 fm^2
- Mean squared transverse momentum in NN collisions at SPS (sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV): <p_T^2>_NN = 1.15 GeV^2
- Mean squared transverse momentum in NN collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV): <p_T^2>_NN = 2.45 GeV^2 (extrapolated)
- Gluon-nucleon cross section times average broadening from S+U data: sigma_gN * delta_0 = 0.442 +/- 0.056

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analyses of preliminary NA50 data (Chaudhuri 2003) and responds to the final NA50 2000 run results (Alessandro 2005), which showed flatter suppression than earlier runs. The paper directly compares the QCD-based nuclear absorption model (Chaudhuri 2002; Qiu, Vary, Zhang 2002) with the threshold model of Blaizot, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000), finding that both can fit the SPS data when the threshold density is smeared. The conclusion that J/psi suppression and p_T broadening cannot serve as a "smoking gun" for deconfinement at RHIC challenges the proposal by Kharzeev et al. (1997) that decreasing p_T broadening would signal deconfinement. The work enables future studies to focus on combined analysis of multiple observables rather than relying on J/psi suppression alone.
