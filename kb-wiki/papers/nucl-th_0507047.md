# Dynamic Pairing Effects on Low-Frequency Modes of Excitation in Deformed Mg Isotopes close to the Neutron Drip Line

> Low-frequency quadrupole vibrations in deformed ${}^{36,38,40}$Mg are studied by means of the deformed Quasiparticle-RPA based on the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism. Strongly collective $K^π=0^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ excitation modes (carrying 10-20 W.u.) are obtained at about 3 MeV. It is found that dynamical pairing effects play an essential role in generating these modes. It implies that the lowest $K^π=0^{+}$ excitation modes are particularly sensitive indicators of dynamical pairing correlations in deformed nuclei near the neutron drip line.

# Dynamic Pairing Effects on Low-Frequency Modes of Excitation in Deformed Mg Isotopes close to the Neutron Drip Line

## Key claim

The soft Kπ=0+ excitation modes in deformed 36,38,40Mg near the neutron drip line are particularly sensitive indicators of dynamical pairing correlations, but this sensitivity depends on the coherent superposition of particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole excitations; when the residual particle-particle interactions are switched off, the prominent Kπ=0+ peak disappears entirely, while the Kπ=2+ mode remains but with reduced strength.

## Method

The authors solved the deformed Quasiparticle-RPA (QRPA) equation in the standard matrix formulation using a quasiparticle basis obtained from the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) formalism on a two-dimensional lattice discretizing cylindrical coordinates (ρ, z). The mean-field Hamiltonian employed a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with parameters from Yoshida (2005) except for a slightly smaller isovector potential strength of 30 MeV, while the pairing field was treated self-consistently using a surface-type density-dependent contact interaction with V0 = -450 MeV·fm3 and ρ0 = 0.16 fm-3. Residual interactions used a Skyrme-type interaction for the particle-hole channel and the density-dependent contact interaction for the particle-particle channel, with renormalization factors fph and fpp determined by requiring the spurious Kπ=1+ rotation mode and Kπ=0+ number fluctuation mode to appear at zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Strongly collective Kπ=0+ and 2+ excitation modes carrying 10-20 W.u. are obtained at about 3 MeV in 36,38,40Mg.
- In 36Mg: prominent peaks at 2.9 MeV (Kπ=2+) and 3.3 MeV (Kπ=0+).
- In 38Mg: prominent peak at 2.8 MeV (Kπ=2+).
- In 40Mg: prominent peaks at 2.8 MeV (Kπ=2+) and 2.9 MeV (Kπ=0+).
- For the soft Kπ=0+ mode at 2.9 MeV in 40Mg: B(E2) = 2.4 e2fm4, B(Qν2) = 112 fm4, and B(QIS2) = 148 fm4.
- Neutron threshold energies: 4.01 MeV (1qp continuum) and 4.1 MeV (2qp continuum) for 36Mg, 2.14 MeV for 38Mg, and 0.60 MeV for 40Mg.
- Lattice mesh size: Δρ = Δz = 0.8 fm; box boundary at ρmax = 10.0 fm and zmax = 12.8 fm; quasiparticle energy cutoff at 50 MeV; QRPA model space cutoff at Eα + Eβ ≤ 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of octupole excitations in superdeformed neutron-rich sulfur isotopes using RPA based on a deformed Woods-Saxon potential (Yoshida, 2005), now incorporating pairing correlations self-consistently through the coordinate-space HFB formalism. The calculation contradicts the restriction of most prior QRPA studies to spherical nuclei, as noted by Matsuo (2001), Hagino (2001), and Terasaki (2005), and addresses the largely unexplored territory of low-frequency RPA modes in deformed unstable nuclei, with only recent attempts by Nakatsukasa (2005) and Hagino (2004a). The finding that the coupling between quadrupole shape vibration and neutron pairing vibration (known from Bohr and Mottelson for stable deformed nuclei) occurs among loosely bound and resonant two-quasiparticle excitations enables future experimental searches for this soft Kπ=0+ mode as a direct indicator of dynamical pairing correlations in deformed drip-line nuclei.
