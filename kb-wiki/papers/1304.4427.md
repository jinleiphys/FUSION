# Lambda Polarization in Peripheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> We predict the polarization of Lambda and antiLambda hyperons in peripheral heavy ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energy, based on the assumption of local thermodynamical equilibrium at freeze-out. The polarization vector is proportional to the curl of the inverse temperature four-vector field and its length, of the order of percents, is maximal for particle with moderately high momentum lying on the reaction plane. A selective measurement of these particles could make Lambda polarization detectable.

## Key claim

The authors predict that Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons produced in peripheral heavy ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energy will have a polarization of the order of a few percent, arising from local thermodynamical equilibrium at freeze-out. The abstract's claim of "detectable" polarization is qualified by the condition that the signal is maximal only for hyperons with moderately high momentum lying in the reaction plane, and that a selective measurement of these specific particles is required to observe it.

## Method

The polarization vector is calculated from the thermal vorticity (the curl of the inverse temperature four-vector field) at freeze-out, using a Particle-in-Cell (PIC) fluid dynamic model of the quark-gluon plasma. The freeze-out is enforced via the Cooper-Frye prescription at a fixed laboratory time when the energy-density-weighted average temperature is approximately 180 MeV. The polarization is then evaluated by weighting the thermal vorticity with the Fermi-Juttner distribution for the Lambda hyperons.

## Key numbers

- The average polarization of Lambda hyperons is of the order of 1-2%, consistent with the experimental upper bound of |P| ≤ 0.02 from STAR (2007).
- The maximum polarization reaches around 5% for Lambda hyperons with transverse momentum p_T > 3 GeV/c lying in the reaction plane.
- At p_y ≈ 3 GeV/c, the polarization is about 2%; in the reaction plane at p_x ≈ 3 GeV/c, the polarization is about 5%.
- The energy-density-weighted average thermal vorticity ⟨Ω_zx⟩ is 0.0453 for a collision at √s_NN = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 0.7 b_max.
- The magnetic field at freeze-out is estimated at 2 × 10^10 T, yielding a polarization from electromagnetic effects of order μ_N g_Λ B/T ≈ 10^-6, at least four orders of magnitude smaller than the predicted signal.
- The fraction of Lambda hyperons from single nucleon-nucleon collisions (corona effect) is estimated at about 4.2% for peripheral collisions with N_P ≈ 100 participants, contributing at most 0.002 to the polarization.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Becattini (2013) and Csernai et al. (2013) on vorticity in heavy ion collisions, providing the first quantitative prediction of Lambda polarization from a full hydrodynamical calculation. It contradicts the earlier null result from STAR (2007) by showing that a finite polarization can be observed with selective momentum cuts. The prediction enables experimental searches for local thermodynamical equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom and indirect evidence of vorticous flow at freeze-out, and the authors plan similar calculations for LHC energies.
