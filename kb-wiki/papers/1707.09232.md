# The $α+ d \rightarrow ~ ^6\mathrm{Li} + γ$ astrophysical $S$-factor and its implications for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> The $α+d\rightarrow\, ^6{\rm Li}+γ$ radiative capture is studied in order to predict the $^6$Li primordial abundance. Within a two-body framework, the $α$ particle and the deuteron are considered the structureless constituents of $^6$Li. Five $α+d$ potentials are used to solve the two-body problem: four of them are taken from the literature, only one having also a tensor component. A fifth model is here constructed in order to reproduce, besides the $^6$Li static properties as binding energy, magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments, also the $S$-state asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). The two-body bound and scattering problem is solved with different techniques, in order to minimize the numerical uncertainty of the present results. The long-wavelength approximation is used, and therefore only the electric dipole and quadrupole operators are retained. The astrophysical $S$-factor is found to be significantly sensitive to the ANC, but in all the cases in good agreement with the available experimental data. The theoretical uncertainty has been estimated of the order of few % when the potentials which reproduce the ANC are considered, but increases up to $\simeq 20$ % when all the five potential models are retained. The effect of this $S$-factor prediction on the $^6$Li primordial abundance is studied, using the public code PArthENoPE. For the five models considered here we find $^6{\rm Li}/$H$ = (0.9 - 1.8) \times 10^{-14}$, with the baryon density parameter in the 3-$σ$ range of Planck 2015 analysis, $Ω_b h^2= 0.02226 \pm 0.00023$.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for the α + d → ⁶Li + γ reaction is significantly sensitive to the S-state asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of ⁶Li, and the theoretical uncertainty in the S-factor is only a few percent when potentials that reproduce the experimental ANC are used, but increases to about 20% when all five potential models are considered.

## Method

The authors treat ⁶Li as a two-body α + d system and solve the bound and scattering Schrödinger equation using five different α + d potentials: four taken from the literature (V_H, V_T, V_M, V_D) and one newly constructed (V_G) that reproduces the experimental ANC. The long-wavelength approximation is used, retaining only electric dipole and quadrupole operators, and the two-body problem is solved with both Numerov and variational methods to minimize numerical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- ⁶Li binding energy B = 1.474 MeV (experimental value 1.474 MeV) for V_H and V_M; 1.475 MeV for V_T; 1.4735 MeV for V_D and V_G
- S-state ANC values: V_H = 2.70 fm¹/², V_T = 2.31 fm¹/², V_M = 2.30 fm¹/², V_D = 2.50 fm¹/², V_G = 2.30 fm¹/² (experimental ANC = 2.30 ± 0.12 fm¹/²)
- ⁶Li magnetic dipole moment: V_H, V_T, V_M = 0.857 μ_N; V_D, V_G = 0.848 μ_N (experimental = 0.822 μ_N)
- ⁶Li electric quadrupole moment: V_H, V_T, V_M = 0.286 fm²; V_D = -0.066 fm²; V_G = -0.051 fm² (experimental = -0.082 fm²)
- Predicted primordial ⁶Li/H abundance range: (0.9 - 1.8) × 10⁻¹⁴ for baryon density parameter Ω_b h² = 0.02226 ± 0.00023 (Planck 2015 3-σ range)

## Context

This work addresses the "second lithium problem", the discrepancy between observed ⁶Li in old halo stars and Big Bang Nucleosynthesis predictions, which has been weakened by recent three-dimensional stellar atmosphere models (Cayrel et al., Perez et al., Steffen et al., Lind et al.). The study extends previous two-body potential model calculations (Mukhamedzhanov et al., 2011; Dubovichenko, 1998) by constructing a new potential that reproduces the experimental ANC, and improves upon the ab-initio variational Monte Carlo approach of Nollett et al. (2001) which had 10-20% uncertainties. The results enable more precise BBN predictions for ⁶Li abundance using the public code PArthENoPE.
