# Treatment of broken symmetry in the Faddeev approach to the strange baryon spectra

> By solving the Faddeev equations we calculate the mass of the strange baryons in the framework of a relativistic constituent quark model. The Goldstone-boson-exchange quark-quark interaction is derived from $SU(3)_F$ symmetry, which is explicitly broken as the strange quark is much heavier. This broken symmetry can nicely be accounted for in the Faddeev framework.

# Treatment of broken symmetry in the Faddeev approach to the strange baryon spectra

## Key claim
The Faddeev method can account for explicit SU(3)_F symmetry breaking in strange baryon spectra by treating light quarks as identical particles with exact exchange symmetry while treating the strange quark as a heavier particle with broken symmetry, all while retaining the SU(3)_F symmetric form of the Goldstone-boson-exchange interaction derived in the chiral limit.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic three-quark Faddeev equations using a two-component reduction: one component for the light-light or strange-strange pair and one for the light-strange pair. The quark-quark interaction combines a linear confining potential with a Goldstone-boson-exchange chiral potential (pion, kaon, eta, eta-prime exchanges), where the confining part is incorporated into a reference Hamiltonian and the short-range chiral part is treated in a separable approximation using Coulomb-Sturmian basis functions. The symmetry between identical light quarks is enforced exactly, while the strange quark is treated with its physical mass of 500 MeV versus 340 MeV for up/down quarks.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 340 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV
- Meson masses used: mu_pi = 139 MeV, mu_K = 494 MeV, mu_eta = 547 MeV, mu_eta' = 958 MeV
- Meson octet-quark coupling constant: g_8^2 / 4pi = 0.67
- Confinement parameters: V_0 = 416 MeV, C = 2.33 fm^{-2}
- Cutoff parameters: Lambda_0 = 2.86 fm^{-1}, kappa = 0.81, (g_0/g_8)^2 = 1.34
- Lambda(1116) ground state: Faddeev result 1133 MeV (experiment 1116 MeV)
- Sigma(1193) ground state: Faddeev result 1163 MeV (experiment 1189-1197 MeV)
- Xi(1318) ground state: Faddeev result 1345 MeV (experiment 1315-1321 MeV)
- Omega(1672) ground state: Faddeev result 1657 MeV (experiment 1672.45 +/- 0.29 MeV)
- Faddeev results are slightly lower than variational calculations of Melde et al. (2008), mostly by a few MeV

## Context
This work extends the relativistic constituent quark model with Goldstone-boson-exchange interaction, previously applied to light baryons by the Graz group (Glozman and Riska 1996; Glozman, Plessas and collaborators 1996-1997; Melde et al. 2008), to the strange baryon sector. The Faddeev approach improves upon the variational calculations of Melde et al. (2008) by properly treating the broken exchange symmetry between light and strange quarks, rather than imposing full wave function symmetry irrespective of mass differences. This enables more accurate calculations of the full strange baryon spectrum including Lambda, Sigma, Xi, and Omega states.
