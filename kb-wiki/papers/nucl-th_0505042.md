# Space-Time Evolution of Hadronization in DIS: Semi-Exclusive Processes and Grey Track Production

## Key claim
Semi-exclusive DIS processes (A(e,e'B)X) and grey track production in DIS off nuclear targets can serve as complementary tools to investigate the space-time evolution of hadronization, provided that a time-dependent effective debris-nucleon cross section incorporating both non-perturbative string dynamics and perturbative gluon radiation is used to describe the final state interactions.

## Method
The author reviews a hadronization model that combines soft dynamics (color string model, with string tension κ ≈ 1 GeV/fm) with hard dynamics (pQCD gluon radiation) to construct a time-dependent effective debris-nucleon cross section σ_eff(t). This cross section is then applied to two processes: (1) semi-exclusive DIS on deuterium, where the distorted momentum distribution of the spectator proton is calculated including final state interactions via a Glauber-like approach; and (2) grey track production in μ-Xe DIS at 490 GeV beam energy, where the mean number of grey tracks is obtained from the calculated mean number of collisions using an empirical relation from the E665 experiment.

## Key numbers
- String tension: κ ≈ 1 GeV/fm
- Mass scale separating soft and hard production: λ = 0.65 GeV
- Time scale for first string breaking: Δt ≈ 1 fm
- Time parameter t_0 = (m_N x_Bj)^{-1} = 0.2 fm / x_Bj
- Inelastic meson-nucleon cross section used: σ_in^{MN} = 17.7 mb
- Grey track momentum range: 200–600 MeV/c (protons from E665 experiment)
- Beam energy for grey track data: 490 GeV (μ-Xe and μ-D DIS at Fermilab E665)
- Empirical relation for mean grey tracks: ⟨n_g⟩ = [⟨ν_c⟩ − (2.08 ± 0.13)] / (3.72 ± 0.14)
- Fixed kinematics for grey track data: x_Bj = 0.07 (for Q² dependence) and Q² = 14.3 GeV² (for x_Bj dependence)

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Kopeliovich et al. (2004) for hadronization in nuclear media, which was developed to explain HERMES data on leading hadron production. The author reviews two complementary approaches: semi-exclusive DIS (A(e,e'B)X) as proposed by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (2003, 2004), and grey track production as analyzed by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (2004) using E665 data. The key advance is that both processes are sensitive to the bulk of final state interactions rather than the rare leading hadron production, and the observed Q² dependence of grey track production provides a discriminating test between different hadronization models. Preliminary semi-exclusive data from JLab (Kuhn, 2004) are compared with calculations including final state interactions.
