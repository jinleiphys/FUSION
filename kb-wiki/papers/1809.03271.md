# On the contribution of the P and D partial-wave states to the binding energy of the triton in the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev approach

> The influence of the partial-wave states with nonzero orbital moment of the nucleon pair on the binding energy of the triton in the relativistic case is considered. The relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equation in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism is applied. Two-nucleon t matrix is obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation with separable kernel of nucleon-nucleon interaction of the rank one. The kernel form factors are the relativistic type of the Yamaguchi functions. The following two-nucleon partial-wave states are considered: $^1S_0$, $^3S_1$, $^3D_1$, $^3P_0$, $^1P_1$, $^3P_1$. The system of the integral equations are solved by using the iteration method. The binding energy of the triton and three-nucleon amplitudes are found. The contribution of the P and D states to the binding energy of triton is given.

## Key claim

The contribution of two-nucleon P- and D-partial-wave states (with nonzero orbital angular momentum) to the triton binding energy is found to be small (0.5% to 1% total) within the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev approach using rank-one separable Yamaguchi-type interactions, with the D-state contribution being positive and the P-state contributions partially canceling each other.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation for the three-nucleon bound state (triton) using a separable rank-one kernel for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with form factors of the relativistic Yamaguchi type. They performed a partial-wave decomposition including six two-nucleon states (¹S₀, ³S₁, ³D₁, ³P₀, ¹P₁, ³P₁) and solved the resulting system of 12 integral equations for the real and imaginary parts of the amplitudes using an iteration method after Wick rotation. The spin-isospin structure was incorporated via a recoupling coefficient matrix, and the binding energy was determined by the condition that the ratio of successive iterations approaches unity at the bound-state mass.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with only ¹S₀ and ³S₁ states included: 9.221 MeV (for deuteron D-state probability p_D = 4%)
- Triton binding energy with ³D₁ state added: 9.294 MeV (p_D = 4%), giving a D-state contribution of +0.073 MeV (0.8%)
- Triton binding energy with ³P₀ state added: 9.314 MeV (p_D = 4%)
- Triton binding energy with ¹P₁ state added: 9.287 MeV (p_D = 4%)
- Triton binding energy with ³P₁ state added: 9.271 MeV (p_D = 4%)
- For p_D = 5%: binding energy with only S-states = 8.819 MeV; with D-state = 8.909 MeV
- For p_D = 6%: binding energy with only S-states = 8.442 MeV; with D-state = 8.545 MeV
- Experimental triton binding energy quoted: 8.48 MeV
- Total contribution of P- and D-states to binding energy: 0.5% to 1% depending on p_D
- Relativistic calculation with only S-states exceeds nonrelativistic result by 0.44 MeV (citing Rupp and Tjon (1988))

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev calculations by Rupp and Tjon (1988, 1992) that considered only S-states or S- and D-states, by now including P-wave contributions (³P₀, ¹P₁, ³P₁) alongside the D-wave. The authors' prior work (Bondarenko, Burov, Yurev, 2015, 2017) had incorporated the D-wave in both the t-matrix and the three-body amplitudes; the present paper generalizes to nonzero orbital angular momentum of the nucleon pair. The obtained amplitudes are intended for future calculation of electromagnetic form factors of the triton, relevant for experiments such as Jefferson Lab E1210103 with electron energies up to 12 GeV.
