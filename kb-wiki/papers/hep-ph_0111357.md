# Scattering of Gluons from the Color Glass Condensate

> We prove that the inclusive single-gluon production cross section for a hadron colliding with a high-density target factorizes into the gluon distribution function of the projectile, defined as usual within the DGLAP collinear approximation, times the cross section for scattering of a single gluon on the strong classical color field of the target. We then derive the gluon-proton (nucleus) inelastic cross section and show that it is (up to logarithms) infrared safe and that it grows slowly with center of mass energy. Furthermore, we discuss jet transverse momentum broadening for the case of nuclear targets. We show that in the saturation regime, in contrast to the perturbative regime, the width of the transverse momentum distribution is infrared finite and grows rapidly with energy and rapidity. In both regimes, however, transverse momentum broadening exibits the same dependence on atomic number A.

## Key claim

The inclusive single-gluon production cross section for a dilute hadron colliding with a high-density target factorizes into the projectile's DGLAP gluon distribution function times the cross section for scattering a single gluon on the target's strong classical color field, but this factorization is proven only in the weak-projectile limit where the projectile's field is much weaker than the target's field (realized when the rapidity separation between projectile and target is large).

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the presence of random color charges, using a Gaussian weight to average over the gauge potentials of the projectile and target. They derive the number distribution of produced gluons at asymptotic times in the weak-projectile limit, then integrate over impact parameter and divide by the flux of incoming collinear gluons to obtain the gluon-proton inclusive cross section. For the saturation regime (transverse momentum \(p_\perp\) below the target saturation scale \(Q_t\)), they expand to all orders in the target's strong field but leading order in the projectile's weak field.

## Key numbers

- In the saturation regime, the differential gluon-proton cross section is \(d\sigma^{\rm sat}_{gp}/d^2p_\perp = (1/4\pi)(1/p_\perp^2) \pi R^2\), valid for \(p_\perp \gg \Lambda_{QCD}\).
- The total gluon-proton inclusive cross section in the saturation regime is \(\sigma^{\rm sat}_{gp} \simeq (1/4) \pi R^2 \log(Q_t^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\).
- For a nuclear target, the saturation scale \(Q_A^2\) is \(A^{1/3}\) times larger than that for a proton.
- Transverse momentum broadening in the saturation regime is \(\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle \simeq Q_A^2/4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\), scaling as \(A^{1/3}\).
- In the perturbative regime (\(p_\perp \gg Q_t\)), the differential cross section is \(d\sigma^{\rm pert}_{gp}/d^2p_\perp = 4\alpha_s^2 [N_c^2/(N_c^2-1)] (1/p_\perp^4) \int_{x_0}^1 dx\, g_t(x,p_\perp^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the classical field method of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Kovchegov (1996) for high-density QCD to prove collinear factorization for inclusive gluon production in asymmetric hadron-nucleus collisions. It derives the gluon-proton inelastic cross section in the saturation regime, showing that its growth with energy is only logarithmic (using HERA-compatible parameterizations of the saturation momentum from Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff) rather than power-like as in perturbative QCD. The results enable predictions for jet transverse momentum broadening at forward rapidity in nuclear collisions, which should be larger than in central rapidity and increase with energy, contrary to standard perturbative expectations from BDMPS (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff).
