# Jet Quenching and Its Azimuthal Anisotropy in AA and possibly High Multiplicity pA and dA Collisions

> We report our calculation of jet quenching and its azimuthal anisotropy in the high energy AA and high multiplicity pA and dA collisions. The purpose of this study is twofold. First, we improve our previous event-by-event studies, by properly implementing $p_t$ dependence in the modeling. We show that, within the jet "monography" scenario featuring a strong near-Tc-enhancement of jet energy loss, the computed high-$p_t$ nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the harmonic coefficients in its azimuthal anisotropy $v_{2,\,3,\,4}$, agree well with the available data from both Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Second, in light of current discussions on possible final state collective behavior in the high-multiplicity pPb and dAu collisions, we examine the implication of final state jet attenuation in such collisions, by applying the same model used in AA collisions and quantifying the corresponding $R_{pA}$ and $R_{dA}$ and their azimuthal anisotropy. The high-$p_t$ $V_n$ provide a set of clean indicators of final state energy loss. In particular we find in the most central pPb (5.02 TeV) and dAu (200 GeV) collisions, $V_2$ is on the order of $0.01$ and $0.1$ respectively, measurable with current experimental accuracy. In addition, our high-$p_t$ $R_{dA}$ is around $0.6$ which is compatible with preliminary dAu results from RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that within the jet "monography" scenario featuring a strong near-Tc-enhancement of jet energy loss, the computed high-p_t nuclear modification factor R_AA and the harmonic coefficients V_2, V_3, V_4 agree well with available data from both RHIC and LHC, but only after introducing p_t dependence into the model (via parameter delta approximately 0.19) which was absent in their previous studies.

## Method

The authors used a geometric model scheme where differential energy loss dE/dl is expressed as a function of local entropy density along the jet path, with a Gaussian enhancement near T_c (parameters xi=6, s_c=7/fm^3, s_w=2/fm^3). They introduced p_t dependence via a parameter delta in the energy loss formula, fixed by chi-squared analysis of central R_AA data at both RHIC and LHC. For AA collisions they used optical Glauber for centrality dependence and Monte-Carlo Glauber for event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions to compute azimuthal anisotropy. For pPb and dAu collisions they applied the same NTcE model with two different initial state implementations ("size a" using participant density and "size b" using collision density) to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Optimal parameters from chi-squared analysis: delta approximately 0.19 and kappa approximately 0.0065 (energy unit GeV)
- R_AA reaches approximately 0.2 in most central AA collisions at both RHIC and LHC
- Reduction of jet-medium interaction strength from RHIC to LHC is approximately 30%
- In most central pPb (5.02 TeV) collisions, V_2 is on the order of 0.01
- In most central dAu (200 GeV) collisions, V_2 is on the order of 0.1
- High-p_t R_dA is around 0.6, compatible with preliminary dAu results from RHIC
- NN cross sections used: 42 mb at RHIC (200 GeV), 62 mb at LHC (2.76 TeV), 68 mb for pPb (5.02 TeV)
- Entropy density to participant density ratio s_0: approximately 90/fm for pPb (5.02 TeV), approximately 49/fm for dAu (200 GeV)
- Mid-rapidity multiplicity per participant: dN_ch/(d eta N_p) = 1.16 at RHIC, 2.14 at LHC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous event-by-event studies (Liao 2008, Liao 2011, Zhang 2012) by properly implementing p_t dependence, which was absent in their earlier fractional energy loss scenario. The NTcE model builds on the "magnetic scenario" for strongly coupled QGP (Liao 2006, Liao 2008) and was first developed to explain the large jet quenching anisotropy at RHIC. The paper also applies the same model to high-multiplicity pPb and dAu collisions, motivated by recent observations of long-range "ridge" correlations in these small systems (CMS 2012, ALICE 2012, ATLAS 2013, PHENIX 2013), suggesting that high-p_t azimuthal anisotropy can serve as a clean indicator of final state jet-medium interaction distinct from initial state effects. The work enables future combined hard-soft correlation studies and provides predictions testable by current experimental capabilities at RHIC and LHC.
