# Effective Field Theory and $χ$pt

> A brief introduction to the subject of chiral perturbation theory ($χ$pt) is given, including a discussion of effective field theory and application to the upcoming Bates virtual Compton scattering measurement.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (χpt) provides a systematic low-energy expansion of QCD that is predictive up to energies of about 500 MeV, but the expansion must be truncated at finite order (typically four-derivative level) and relies on phenomenological constants (the Gasser-Leutwyler L_i coefficients) that are not determined from chiral symmetry alone.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of an effective field theory for QCD based on chiral symmetry, starting from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian (two-derivative order) and extending to the one-loop, four-derivative level using the Gasser-Leutwyler counterterm Lagrangian with ten phenomenological constants L_i. For the nucleon sector, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχpt) is employed, incorporating a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to maintain proper power counting, and applied to processes such as threshold pion photoproduction and virtual Compton scattering.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: F_π = 92.4 MeV
- Goldberger-Treiman relation: 92.4 MeV × 13.05 = 1206 MeV vs. 939 MeV × 1.266 = 1189 MeV (agreement to better than 2%)
- Threshold charged pion photoproduction E_0+ multipole predictions: π^+n = +26.3 × 10^{-3}/m_π, π^-p = -31.3 × 10^{-3}/m_π
- Experimental E_0+(γp→π^+n): (+27.9 ± 0.5) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Burg1), (+28.8 ± 0.7) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Adam), (+27.6 ± 0.3) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Sal)
- Experimental E_0+(γn→π^-p): (-31.4 ± 1.3) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Burg1), (-32.2 ± 1.2) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Gold1), (-31.5 ± 0.8) × 10^{-3}/m_π (Tri)
- Neutral pion photoproduction E_0+(π^0p): theory = -1.2 × 10^{-3}/m_π, experiment = -1.31 ± 0.08 × 10^{-3}/m_π (Mai)
- Proton electric polarizability (real photon): α_E^{exp} = (12.1 ± 0.8 ± 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3, β_M^{exp} = (2.1 ∓ 0.8 ∓ 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Chiral one-loop prediction for proton polarizabilities: α_E^{theo} = 10 β_M^{theo} = 12.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Chiral scale: Λ_χ ∼ 4π F_π ∼ 1 GeV
- Gasser-Leutwyler counterterm values: L_1^r = 0.65 ± 0.28, L_2^r = 1.89 ± 0.26, L_3^r = -3.06 ± 0.92, L_5^r = 2.3 ± 0.2, L_9^r = 7.1 ± 0.3, L_10^r = -5.6 ± 0.3

## Context

This paper extends the effective field theory approach from quantum mechanical Rayleigh scattering (where the ω^4 dependence of the cross section explains why the sky is blue) to QCD via chiral perturbation theory, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). It enables predictions for low-energy hadronic processes including threshold pion photoproduction and virtual Compton scattering, with the upcoming Bates measurement at q̅ = 240 MeV using the OOPS detector system expected to test the chiral predictions for generalized proton polarizabilities, complementing the MAMI measurement at q̅ = 600 MeV.
