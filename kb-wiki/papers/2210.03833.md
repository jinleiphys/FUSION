# Fast rotation of nuclei with extreme isospin in the vicinity of neutron and proton drip lines

> The analysis of the present understanding of collective rotation in very neutron-rich nuclei is presented. It is shown that collective rotation can lead to the increase of stability of rotational states with increasing spin. The detailed investigation of rotational excitations in very proton-rich nuclei confirms this conclusion and indicate that experimental studies of such features are more feasible in the nuclei near proton drip line. They also show that rotational bands which are proton quasi-bound at zero or low spins can be transformed into proton bound ones at high spin by collective rotation of nuclear systems. This is due to strong Coriolis interaction which acts on high-$j$ or strongly mixed M orbitals and drives the highest in energy occupied single-particle states into negative energy domain. These physical mechanisms lead to a substantial extension of the nuclear landscape beyond the spin zero proton drip line. In addition, a new phenomenon of the formation of giant proton halos in rotating nuclei emerges: it is triggered by the occupation of strongly mixed M intruder orbitals.

# Fast rotation of nuclei with extreme isospin in the vicinity of neutron and proton drip lines

## Key claim
Collective rotation can transform proton quasi-bound rotational bands into proton bound ones at high spin, thereby extending the nuclear landscape beyond the spin-zero proton drip line; this occurs because the Coriolis interaction drives high-j or strongly mixed M orbitals into negative energy domain. The authors argue that this mechanism, combined with increased centrifugal barrier from high-l orbitals, leads to increased stability of rotational states with increasing spin, but note that this conclusion is drawn from self-consistent cranked relativistic mean field calculations and has not yet been experimentally confirmed for most of the predicted cases.

## Method
The authors performed cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) calculations using the NL3* covariant energy density functional, with no pairing correlations, in a one-dimensional cranking approximation. They solved the CRMF equations in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with up to 16 fermionic shells and 20 bosonic shells, restricting to reflection-symmetric shapes. They systematically surveyed nucleonic configurations in very proton-rich nuclei from ¹⁴Ne to ⁶⁷Kr, tracking the evolution of single-particle routhians and deformation parameters as functions of rotational frequency and angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- In ¹⁴Ne, the transition from quasi-bound to proton-bound rotational band occurs at rotational frequency Ω ≈ 3.2 MeV for the [1,1,1,1] ⊗ [2,3,3,2] occupation block, corresponding to spin I_trans.
- The Coulomb barrier in N=Z nuclei increases almost linearly from 2 to 6 MeV when Z changes from 10 to 36 (at spherical shape).
- The experimental window for proton emission half-lives in medium mass nuclei ranges from 4.5 × 10⁻⁷ s to a few seconds, corresponding to resonance widths Γ ≈ 10⁻²² to 10⁻¹⁵ MeV.
- In ¹¹Be, the favored signature branch of the ground state 1/2⁺ rotational band has neutron decay width around 200 keV, while unfavored signature states have widths around 0.7 MeV.
- In ³⁹Mg, the rotational band based on the deformed 1/2[321] Nilsson state shows drastic decrease of decay width from very broad to very narrow resonances with increasing angular momentum, due to transition from l=1 p₃/₂ to l=3 f₇/₂ dominance.
- The maximum angular momentum in the ground state rotational band of ³⁸Ne is I_max = 4⁺, while a two particle-two hole excited configuration reaches I_max = 14⁺.
- Total binding energies E(I) calculated with N_F=16 and N_F=20 differ by no more than several hundred keV across the studied nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line by Afanasjev et al. (2019) using CRMF, and by other authors using particle-plus-core models for ¹¹Be (Fossez et al., 2016) and ³⁹Mg (Fossez et al., 2016). It also builds on the exploratory cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock study of neutron-rich Ne and Mg isotopes by Neergard et al. (2001). The paper provides the first systematic CRMF investigation of rotational structures in very proton-rich nuclei at and beyond the spin-zero proton drip line, and predicts a new phenomenon of giant proton halos triggered by occupation of strongly mixed M intruder orbitals. The results suggest that experimental studies of such rotational features are more feasible near the proton drip line than near the neutron drip line, and that future facilities such as FRIB may enable their observation.
