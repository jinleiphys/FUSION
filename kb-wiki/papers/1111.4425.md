# Spin Polarizabilities on the Lattice

> Spin polarizabilities provide information on the internal structure of hadrons in the presence of weak external electromagnetic fields, and are actively studied by Compton scattering experiments. They provide finer detail than the regular polarizabilities since they require space and time-varying fields. Using an effective action in the weak field limit, we have identified methods to isolate each of the physical quantities ($μ, α, β, γ_{E1}, γ_{M1}, γ_{E2}, γ_{M2}$) for spin-1/2 hadrons, both neutral and charged. We also perform a lattice QCD simulation to investigate the feasibility of the effective action approach.

# Spin Polarizabilities on the Lattice

## Key claim
Using an effective quantum mechanical action in the weak field limit, the authors identify methods to isolate all seven physical quantities (magnetic moment, electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities, and four spin polarizabilities) for spin-1/2 hadrons on the lattice, but a preliminary lattice QCD study of the electric dipole spin polarizability γE1 shows that Monte Carlo noise must be reduced by at least a factor of 10 before reliable values can be extracted.

## Method
The authors discretize a non-relativistic effective quantum mechanical action on a finite lattice and compute the two-point correlation function numerically using a double-precision BiCGSTAB solver. By designing specific background electromagnetic field configurations (constant electric fields, constant magnetic fields, and time- and space-varying fields) and manipulating ratios of polarized correlators, they isolate each polarizability term individually. They test the method by recovering known input values from the effective correlator alone, then perform a preliminary lattice QCD simulation with Wilson actions on a 24^3 x 48 lattice at a=0.093 fm to study γE1.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size for most effective theory tests: 24^3 x 64 with spacing a=0.1 fm
- Lattice QCD simulation: 24^3 x 48 lattice at a=0.093 fm, with 700 configurations analyzed
- Pion masses studied in QCD simulation: 893 to 404 MeV
- Test input values for proton: μ=2.79 μN, α=12 x 10^{-4} fm^3, β=2 x 10^{-4} fm^3, γE1=-3.4 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γM1=2.7 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γE2=1.9 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γM2=0.3 x 10^{-4} fm^4
- Test input values for neutron: μ=-1.91 μN, α=12 x 10^{-4} fm^3, β=2 x 10^{-4} fm^3, γE1=-5.6 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γM1=3.8 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γE2=2.9 x 10^{-4} fm^4, γM2=-0.7 x 10^{-4} fm^4
- Field values used in γE1 QCD study: (e1, e2) = (0.23, 0.46) fm^{-2}
- QCD data for γE1 suggests a range anywhere between 0 and -200 x 10^{-4} fm^4
- Error bars need reduction by at least a factor of 10 for a definitive γE1 value

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD study of hadron polarizabilities, where magnetic moments, electric dipole polarizability α, and magnetic dipole polarizability β have been previously studied by Tiburzi (2011), while spin polarizabilities are just beginning to be explored by Engelhardt (2011). The effective action approach builds on the formalism of Detmold (2006) and uses the same polarizabilities that appear in low-energy Compton scattering amplitudes as studied by Babusci (1998). The methods enable future determination of all spin polarizabilities by matching effective quantum mechanical correlators with lattice QCD correlators on the same lattice with the same boundary conditions, provided Monte Carlo noise can be sufficiently reduced.
