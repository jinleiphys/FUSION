# Hydrodynamic modeling of 3He-Au collisions at sqrt(sNN)=200 GeV

> Collective flow and femtoscopy in ultrarelativistic 3He-Au collisions are investigated within the 3+1-dimensional (3+1D) viscous event-by-event hydrodynamics. We evaluate elliptic and triangular flow coefficients as functions of the transverse momentum. We find the typical long-range ridge structures in the two-particle correlations in the relative azimuth and pseudorapidity, in the pseudorapidity directions of both Au and 3He. We also make predictions for the pionic interferometric radii, which decrease with the transverse momentum of the pion pair. All features found hint on collectivity of the dynamics of the system formed in 3He-Au collisions, with hydrodynamics leading to quantitative agreement with the up-to-now released data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that 3+1D viscous event-by-event hydrodynamics yields quantitative agreement with preliminary PHENIX data for collective flow and femtoscopy in 3He-Au collisions at 200 GeV, but the hedge is that the agreement is with "up-to-now released data" and the results are described as "hinting" on collectivity rather than proving it definitively.

## Method

The authors use a three-phase approach: (1) Glauber Monte Carlo simulations of the initial state with GLISSANDO, using three-nucleon configurations of 3He from Green's function Monte Carlo calculations; (2) 3+1D viscous event-by-event hydrodynamics with shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s=0.04 (for T<170 MeV), starting at τ=0.6 fm/c and ending at freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV; (3) statistical hadronization at freeze-out simulated with THERMINATOR. The initial entropy density uses a mixed model of wounded nucleons and binary collisions with relative weight α=0.125 and σ_NN^inel=42 mb. The analysis uses 500 hydrodynamic events with 5000 THERMINATOR events generated on top of each.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0.04 (for T < 170 MeV) used in the hydrodynamic evolution
- Initial entropy density constructed with relative weight α = 0.125 and σ_NN^inel = 42 mb with Gaussian wounding profile of width 0.4 fm
- Evolution starts at τ = 0.6 fm/c and ends at freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV
- Centrality class c = 0-5% selected using events with number of wounded nucleons N_w ≥ 25
- Flow coefficients evaluated for charged hadrons with |η| < 0.5, using reference particles from Au-side bin 3.1 < η < 3.9 and 0.3 < p_T < 5.0 GeV
- Ratio of rms radii of 3He and deuteron is 1.14, similar to the corresponding ratio for the R_side radii
- Transverse momentum conservation acceptance window limited to |∑_i p_T,i| < 5 GeV, accepting about 10% of all events

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach previously applied to d-Au collisions by Bozek (2011) and others, and specifically follows up on the authors' earlier paper (Bozek 2014) that proposed tests of collectivity using ratios of flow coefficients from 4- and 2-particle cumulants. The paper confirms recent findings by Romatschke (2015) for flow coefficients and interferometric radii in the same system. The results enable predictions for 3He-Au collisions currently analyzed at RHIC, and the approach can be extended to other small-system collisions such as 12C-Au as proposed by Broniowski (2013).
