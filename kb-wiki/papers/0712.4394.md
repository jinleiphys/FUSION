# Real and imaginary-time $Q\bar{Q}$ correlators in a thermal medium

> We investigate the behavior of a pair of heavy fermions, denoted by $Q$ and $\bar{Q}$, in a hot/dense medium. Although we have in mind the situation where $Q$ and $\bar{Q}$ denote heavy quarks, our treatment will be limited to simplified models, which bear only some general similarities with QCD. We study in particular the limiting case where the mass of the heavy fermions is infinite. Then a number of results can be derived exactly: a Schrödinger equation can be established for the correlator of the heavy quarks; the interaction effects exponentiate, leading to a simple instantaneous effective potential for this Schrödinger equation. We consider simple models for the medium in which the $Q\bar Q$ pair propagates. In the case where the medium is a plasma of photons and light charged fermions, an imaginary part develops in this effective potential. We discuss the physical interpretation of this imaginary part in terms of the collisions between the heavy particles and the light fermions of the medium; the same collisions also determine the damping rate of the heavy fermions. Finally we study the connection between the real-time propagator of the heavy fermion pair and its Euclidean counterpart, and show that the real part of the potential entering the Schrödinger equation for the real-time propagator is the free energy calculated in the imaginary-time formalism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, in the limit of infinite heavy-fermion mass, the real-time correlator of a heavy quark-antiquark pair obeys a Schrödinger equation with an effective potential that can develop an imaginary part, and that the real part of this potential equals the free energy calculated in the imaginary-time formalism, but only for simplified models that bear "only some general similarities with QCD," not for full QCD itself.

## Method

The authors study the propagator of a heavy fermion pair in a thermal medium using a non-relativistic field theory approach, taking the limit of infinite heavy-fermion mass to decouple the equation of motion. They analyze three model media: a non-relativistic cold Fermi gas, a thermal bath of mesons, and a plasma of photons and light charged fermions (QED-like). In each case, they derive the effective potential from resummed ladder diagrams and examine the analytic continuation between real-time and Euclidean (imaginary-time) correlators.

## Key numbers

- The heavy-fermion propagator in the infinite-mass limit becomes \( S^0_R(t,\mathbf{x}) = i\theta(t)\delta(\mathbf{x}) \) (Eq. 3.10 in the text).
- The large-time behavior of the correlator yields \( \overline{G}(t,\mathbf{r}_1-\mathbf{r}_2) \sim \exp(-it V_\infty(\mathbf{r}_1-\mathbf{r}_2)) \) (Eq. 4.27), where \( V_\infty \) is given by an integral over momentum.
- The free energy extracted from the Euclidean correlator at \( \tau = \beta \) is \( \Delta F_{Q\bar{Q}} = \int \frac{d\mathbf{q}}{(2\pi)^3} e^{i\mathbf{q}\cdot(\mathbf{r}_1-\mathbf{r}_2)} \left( \mathcal{U}^{Q\bar{Q}}(\omega=0,\mathbf{q}) + \mathcal{U}^{QQ}(\omega=0,\mathbf{q}) \right) \) (Eq. 4.32).

## Context

This work extends the line of investigation initiated by Laine et al. (2006-2007) on real-time heavy-quark propagators and their effective potentials, and connects to lattice studies of Polyakov loop correlators by McLerran and Svetitsky (1981). It also relates to the broader effort to understand quarkonium dissociation in a quark-gluon plasma, where earlier work by Matsui and Satz (1986) proposed screening as the dominant mechanism, and subsequent studies by Rapp et al. (1999-2001) and others debated whether the effective potential should be identified with the free energy or the internal energy. The paper enables a clearer separation between real-time dynamics (where an imaginary potential arises from collisions with light fermions) and equilibrium free energies (which remain real), and provides a framework for interpreting lattice data on Euclidean correlators in terms of spectral functions.
