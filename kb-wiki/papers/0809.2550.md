# Inclusive results for electron and photon scattering in the resonance region

> We present a model for electron scattering off nuclei and photon absorption in the resonance energy region (W <= 2 GeV). The elementary gamma/gamma* N-vertex is described using in-medium kinematics and up-to-date form factors for QE-scattering, pion-production form-factors and resonance helicity amplitudes of the MAID analysis. We find good agreement with inclusive data on electron scattering off Oxygen. For photon absorption in Carbon we find a large impact of the momentum dependent mean-field acting on initial- and final-state baryons.

## Key claim

The model achieves good agreement with inclusive electron scattering data on Oxygen and shows that momentum-dependent mean-field potentials acting on initial- and final-state baryons have a large impact on photon absorption in Carbon, but the agreement in the Delta region worsens when in-medium collisional broadening and dispersive contributions to the real parts are included.

## Method

The authors employ an impulse-approximation model for inclusive electron and photon scattering off nuclei, using in-medium kinematics and up-to-date form factors for quasi-elastic scattering, pion-production form factors, and resonance helicity amplitudes from the MAID analysis. The nuclear ground state is treated with a local Thomas-Fermi momentum distribution, and the baryon mean-field potentials are taken from the GiBUU transport model, which includes a momentum-dependent potential parametrized by Welke et al. The in-medium modifications include Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb forces, collisional broadening of the Delta resonance width, and dispersive contributions to the real parts of self-energies.

## Key numbers

* Electron scattering on Oxygen-16 at beam energy 0.7 GeV and scattering angle 32 degrees: the quasi-elastic peak is broadened toward higher energy transfer when a momentum-dependent mean field is included, compared to the no-potential case.
* Electron scattering on Oxygen-16 at beam energies 0.88, 1.08, 1.2, and 1.5 GeV and scattering angle 32 degrees: the full calculation including momentum-dependent potential and collisional broadening agrees well with data from Anghinolfi et al. (1996), especially in the dip region between quasi-elastic and single-pion peaks.
* Photon absorption cross section on Carbon-12: the calculation with momentum-dependent potential (EQS 5) and no in-medium width modification describes the Delta region properly but overestimates the second resonance region; including collisional broadening and dispersive contributions lowers the Delta region below the data.
* For photon absorption, the momentum-dependent mean field alone smears the second resonance peak enough to dissolve it, without requiring additional in-medium width effects.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous results for electron scattering (Buss et al., 2007) by including more baryon resonances and addressing real photon absorption. It contrasts with the nuclear many-body theory (NMBT) approach of Benhar et al. (2005), which achieves better agreement in the quasi-elastic region at 0.7 GeV but comparable results at 1.08 GeV. The model also differs from the earlier precursor by Effenberger et al. (1996), which failed to describe the second resonance region in photon absorption; the present work shows that a momentum-dependent mean field alone can dissolve the second resonance peak, whereas Effenberger's model still showed prominent structure. The results enable future extraction of exclusive cross sections within the GiBUU transport framework and serve as a benchmark for neutrino-nucleus interactions.
