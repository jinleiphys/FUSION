# Variance Reduction and Cluster Decomposition

> It is a common problem in lattice QCD calculation of the mass of the hadron with an annihilation channel that the signal falls off in time while the noise remains constant. In addition, the disconnected insertion calculation of the three-point function and the calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment with the $θ$ term suffer from a noise problem due to the $\sqrt{V}$ fluctuation. We identify these problems to have the same origin and the $\sqrt{V}$ problem can be overcome by utilizing the cluster decomposition principle. We demonstrate this by considering the calculations of the glueball mass, the strangeness content in the nucleon, and the CP violation angle in the nucleon due to the $θ$ term. It is found that for lattices with physical sizes of 4.5 - 5.5 fm, the statistical errors of these quantities can be reduced by a factor of 3 to 4. The systematic errors can be estimated from the Akaike information criterion. For the strangeness content, we find that the systematic error is of the same size as that of the statistical one when the cluster decomposition principle is utilized. This results in a 2 to 3 times reduction in the overall error.

## Key claim

The signal-to-noise problem in disconnected insertion lattice QCD calculations, where the noise remains constant while the signal decays exponentially, can be overcome by applying the cluster decomposition principle to restrict the volume sum over relative coordinates to a saturation radius \(R_s\), yielding a variance reduction factor of \(\sqrt{V/V_{R_s}}\) without compromising the signal.

## Method

The authors computed disconnected insertion correlators for glueball masses, the strangeness content in the nucleon, and the CP-violation phase for the neutron electric dipole moment using valence overlap fermions on RBC-UKQCD \(2+1\) flavor domain-wall configurations. They applied a spatial or four-dimensional cutoff \(R\) on the relative coordinate between operator clusters in the volume sum, using fast Fourier transforms to implement the convolution efficiently, and compared results at the saturation radius \(R_s \sim 8/M\) to results summing over the full lattice volume.

## Key numbers

- For lattices with physical sizes of 4.5–5.5 fm, statistical errors of the glueball mass, strangeness content, and CP-violation angle were reduced by a factor of 3 to 4.
- For the scalar glueball correlator at \(t=4\) on a 5.5 fm lattice, the error at cutoff \(R=9\) was reduced by a factor of \(\sim 4\) compared to \(R=24\) (full volume), consistent with the predicted factor \(\sim (24/9)^{3/2} = 4.4\).
- For the pseudoscalar glueball correlator at \(t=4\), the error improvement was a factor of \(\sim 3\), consistent with the estimate \(\sim (24/12)^{3/2} = 2.8\).
- For the strange scalar matrix element in the nucleon on a 4.6 fm lattice, cutting off the spatial sum at \(R_s = 12\) (1.7 fm) reduced errors by a factor of 4 compared to \(R_s = 27\) (full volume), equivalent to gaining \(\sim 16\) times more statistics.
- For the CP-violation phase \(\alpha^1\) on the 48I lattice (5.5 fm), cutting off the sum of topological charge density at \(R \sim 16\) reduced the error by a factor of \(\sim 3.6\) compared to using the total topological charge.
- Using the Akaike information criterion, the strange matrix element was determined as 0.160 (15) (15) (statistical and systematic errors equal), compared to the original value of 0.143(45) without the cluster decomposition cutoff, resulting in a 2 to 3 times reduction in the overall error.

## Context

This work extends the Parisi-Lepage analysis of signal-to-noise ratios in lattice QCD (Parisi, 1983; Lepage, 1989) and addresses the \(\sqrt{V}\) noise problem in disconnected insertion calculations that has limited calculations of the strangeness content in the nucleon (Bali, 2009), the \(\rho\) meson mass (Chen, 2015), the light-by-light contribution to muonic \(g-2\) (Blum, 2015), and the neutron electric dipole moment from the \(\theta\) term (Shintani, 2015). The method enables variance reduction by a factor of \(V/V_{R_s}\) for disconnected insertions on large-volume lattices, making calculations of glueball masses, nucleon strangeness, and CP-violating observables feasible with significantly reduced computational cost.
