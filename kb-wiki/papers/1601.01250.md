# The mass of heavy-light mesons in a constituent quark picture with partially restored chiral symmetry

> We probe effects of the partial chiral symmetry restoration to the mass of heavy-light mesons in a constituent quark model by changing the constituent quark mass of the light quark. Due to the competing effect between the quark mass and the linearly rising potential, whose contribution to the energy increases as the quark mass decreases, the heavy-light meson mass has a minimum value near the constituent quark mass typically used in the vacuum. Hence, the meson mass increases as one decreases the constituent quark mass consistent with recent QCD sum rule analyses, which show an increasing $D$ meson mass as the chiral order parameter decreases.

## Key claim

The mass of heavy-light mesons (such as D and B mesons) increases when the constituent quark mass of the light quark decreases, contrary to the naive expectation that a lighter constituent would make the meson lighter. This occurs because the decreasing quark mass allows the light quark to probe larger distances, increasing the confining potential energy and creating a competing effect that produces a minimum meson mass near the vacuum constituent quark mass of about 0.3 GeV.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, a Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential, and a hyperfine spin-spin interaction. They solved the Schrodinger equation variationally with a single Gaussian trial wave function, fitting parameters to reproduce experimental light-light and heavy-light meson masses. They then systematically varied the light constituent quark mass while keeping the string tension and other confinement parameters fixed, computing the resulting D, D*, B, and B* meson masses.

## Key numbers

- The fitted vacuum constituent light quark mass is 0.324 GeV, with charm quark mass 1.83 GeV and bottom quark mass 5.21 GeV.
- The string tension used is sigma = 0.2 (GeV)^2.
- The naive minimization formula gives a minimum meson energy near m_q approximately 0.3 GeV.
- For constituent quark masses below about 0.3 GeV, the D and B meson masses increase as the quark mass decreases.
- The gluon condensate (and by extension confinement) is expected to change by less than 5% at normal nuclear matter density, while the chiral order parameter is reduced by more than 20%.

## Context

This work extends and is consistent with recent QCD sum rule analyses by Suzuki et al. (2015) and Hilger et al., which show an increasing D meson mass as the chiral order parameter decreases. It also agrees with effective chiral model calculations by Sasaki et al. (2014) and Suenaga et al. based on the chiral partner structure. The results contradict the naive heavy quark limit expectation that a lighter constituent quark would make the meson lighter. The paper enables future QCD sum rule checks for the D* and B* meson channels, where the sign of the quark condensate term suggests similar behavior but more OPE terms could modify the prediction.
