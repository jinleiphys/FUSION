# BCS-BEC Crossover in 2D Fermi Gases with Rashba Spin-Orbit Coupling

> We present a systematic theoretical study of the BCS-BEC crossover in two-dimensional Fermi gases with Rashba spin-orbit coupling (SOC). By solving the exact two-body problem in the presence of an attractive short-range interaction we show that the SOC enhances the formation of the bound state: the binding energy $E_{\text B}$ and effective mass $m_{\text B}$ of the bound state grows along with the increase of the SOC. For the many-body problem, even at weak attraction, a dilute Fermi gas can evolve from a BCS superfluid state to a Bose condensation of molecules when the SOC becomes comparable to the Fermi momentum. The ground-state properties and the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transition temperature are studied, and analytical results are obtained in various limits. For large SOC, the BKT transition temperature recovers that for a Bose gas with an effective mass $m_{\text B}$. We find that the condensate and superfluid densities have distinct behaviors in the presence of SOC: the condensate density is generally enhanced by the SOC due to the increase of the molecule binding, the superfluid density is suppressed because of the non-trivial molecule effective mass $m_{\text B}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a two-dimensional Fermi gas with Rashba spin-orbit coupling, even when the intrinsic attractive interaction is weak (small binding energy \(\epsilon_{\text B}\) relative to Fermi energy \(\epsilon_{\text F}\)), the system can evolve from a BCS superfluid to a Bose-Einstein condensate of molecules as the spin-orbit coupling strength \(\lambda\) becomes comparable to the Fermi momentum \(k_{\text F}\). This crossover is driven by the enhancement of the two-body bound state (the "rashbon") by the spin-orbit coupling, not by tuning the interaction strength via a Feshbach resonance.

## Method

The authors solve the exact two-body problem for an attractive short-range interaction in the presence of Rashba spin-orbit coupling to obtain the binding energy \(E_{\text B}\) and effective mass \(m_{\text B}\) of the bound state. For the many-body problem at zero temperature, they use a self-consistent mean-field (BCS) theory to compute the pairing gap \(\Delta\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and condensate density as functions of \(\lambda/k_{\text F}\). The Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transition temperature is determined by deriving an effective XY-model action for the phase fluctuations and computing the superfluid density \(\rho_s\) from a derivative expansion of the fermionic path integral.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy enhancement: For \(\eta \ll \epsilon_{\text B}\) (where \(\eta = \lambda^2/2\)), the binding energy is \(E_{\text B} \simeq \epsilon_{\text B} + 2\lambda^2\); for \(\eta/\epsilon_{\text B} \rightarrow \infty\), \(E_{\text B} \simeq \lambda^2\).
* Molecule effective mass: For \(\lambda \rightarrow 0\), \(m_{\text B} \rightarrow 2m\); for \(\lambda \rightarrow \infty\), \(m_{\text B} \rightarrow 4m\).
* Pairing gap in the BEC limit (large SOC): \(\Delta \simeq \sqrt{2E_{\text B} \epsilon_{\text F} \zeta(\kappa)}\), where \(\kappa = E_{\text B}/\lambda^2\) and \(\zeta(\kappa)\) is a function defined in the text.
* BKT transition temperature in the rashbon limit: \(T_{\text{BKT}} = (2m/m_{\text B}) \epsilon_{\text F}/8\).
* Superfluid density at \(T=0\) in the large-SOC limit: \(\rho_s \simeq (2m/m_{\text B}) n\).

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover in two-dimensional Fermi gases (previously investigated without spin-orbit coupling by Randeria et al. (1990) and others) to include Rashba spin-orbit coupling. It builds on theoretical observations that novel bound states in three dimensions can be induced by a non-Abelian gauge field (Vyasanakere and Shenoy, 2010) and on studies of anisotropic superfluidity in 3D Fermi gases with Rashba SOC (Zhou and Zhang, 2011). The results enable experimental detection of the crossover in quasi-2D cold atom systems by observing the shrinking of the atom cloud in a trap with increased SOC, and they provide a new mechanism for pseudogap formation in 2D fermionic systems.
