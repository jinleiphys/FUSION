# A schematic model for the direct cross-section in reactions induced by exotic and stable projectiles

> A geometric model for the direct contribution of the reaction cross section induced by light ions on different targets is presented. The model separates the total reaction cross section into two components, one for total fusion and another for direct reactions. We show that the direct part scales as $2 πRa$, where $R$ is related to the nuclear radius and $a$ is the width of a ring, which is related to the nuclear diffuseness. A simple expression is presented to calculate the radius $R$ and the width parameter $a$ in terms of the masses and charges of the system. The method is applied to experimental data of exotic, weakly bound, and strongly bound projectiles on several targets. Different diffuseness parameters were obtained for different types of projectiles: exotic n-rich, stable weakly bound, stable strongly bound and exotic p-rich exotic projectiles.

## Key claim

The direct part of the reaction cross section scales as \(2\pi R a\), where \(R\) is related to the nuclear radius and \(a\) is a width parameter related to nuclear diffuseness, but the model requires different diffuseness parameters for different projectile types (exotic neutron-rich, stable weakly bound, stable strongly bound, and exotic proton-rich) to fit the universal curve, and the model works best for energies well above the Coulomb barrier (\(x \ge 1.8\)).

## Method

The authors separate the total reaction cross section into fusion and direct components using a geometric picture where direct reactions occur in a peripheral ring of radius \(R_x\) and width \(a\). They relate the Coulomb radius \(R_b\) to the nuclear radius \(R\) using a Woods-Saxon potential model from prior work, obtaining expressions for \(R_x\) and \(a\) in terms of projectile and target masses and charges. The direct cross section is then reduced by dividing by \(2\pi R_x a\) and plotted against \(x = E/V_b\) to test a universal curve, with the nuclear diffuseness parameter \(a_n\) adjusted separately for each projectile class.

## Key numbers

- Exotic neutron-rich projectiles: \(\hat{a}_n^{n-rich} = 1.32(06)\) fm (diffuseness parameter)
- Stable weakly bound projectiles: \(\hat{a}_n^{WB} = 0.66^{+0.16}_{-0.12}\) fm
- Stable strongly bound projectiles: \(\hat{a}_n^{SB} = 0.68^{+0.16}_{-0.04}\) fm
- Exotic proton-rich projectiles: \(a_n^{p-rich} = 0.55\) fm (no error estimation possible)
- The reduced variance between data and the universal curve decreases by a factor of approximately 5 in the \(x > 1.8\) range
- For the \(^8\)B + \(^{28}\)Si system, the model gives \(R_x = 7.16\) fm and \(a = 1.23\) fm, compared to values of 7.5 fm and 1.02 fm from Ref. [8B58Ni]
- The standard diffuseness value for stable ions is \(a_n = 0.65\) fm, with reduced nuclear radius \(r_0 = 1.3\) fm

## Context

This work extends the geometric reduction methods for total reaction cross sections developed by Canto (2009), Canto (2015), and Gomes (2005) by explicitly separating the direct component from fusion. The model enables systematic comparison of direct reaction cross sections across different projectile types (exotic neutron-rich, weakly bound, strongly bound, and proton-rich) using a single universal curve with projectile-class-dependent diffuseness parameters. The results contrast with prior analyses by Vivi (2022) and Uiran (2022) which found large total reaction cross sections for exotic proton-rich projectiles, but the authors caution that too few data points exist for proton-rich systems above the barrier to draw definite conclusions.
