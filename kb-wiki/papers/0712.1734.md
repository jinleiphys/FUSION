# Chiral Extrapolation of light resonances from Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Both scalar and vector light resonances can be generated from the unitarization of one-loop chiral perturbation theory. This amounts to using in a dispersion relation the chiral expansion, which incorporates the correct QCD quark mass dependence. We can thus predict the quark mass dependence of the poles associated to those light resonances. Our results compare well with some recent lattice results for the rho(770) mass and can be used as a benchmark for future lattice results on the rho(770) or the f0(600) also known as the sigma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the quark mass dependence of the poles of the light scalar (f0(600) or sigma) and vector (rho(770)) resonances can be predicted from unitarized one-loop chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) without introducing resonances by hand, but the prediction is limited to pion masses up to approximately 500 MeV to avoid spoiling the chiral expansion and to maintain an elastic pi-pi regime below the K-Kbar threshold.

## Method

The authors use the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to unitarize one-loop SU(2) ChPT partial waves for elastic pi-pi scattering, deriving the amplitude from a dispersion relation that uses ChPT input only where it is well justified. They then track the movement of the rho(770) and f0(600) poles in the second Riemann sheet as the pion mass is increased from the chiral limit up to 500 MeV, using low-energy constants (LECs) determined partly from standard ChPT and partly from an IAM fit to data.

## Key numbers

- The rho(770) and f0(600) pole positions are studied for pion masses up to m_pi ≤ 500 MeV, where the kaon mass becomes approximately 600 MeV and pi-pi scattering remains elastic for 200 MeV before the two-kaon threshold.
- The LECs used are l3^r = 0.8 ± 3.8 and l4^r = 6.2 ± 5.7 from standard ChPT, and l1^r = -3.7 ± 0.2 and l2^r = 5.0 ± 0.4 from an IAM fit, all evaluated at mu = 770 MeV.
- The rho pole moves toward the real axis and becomes a bound state when its mass reaches the two-pion threshold, while the sigma pole splits into two virtual states on the real axis below threshold for sufficiently high pion masses.

## Context

This work extends the unitarized ChPT approach of Dobado (1996) and Pelaez (2004) to predict the quark mass dependence of light resonances, providing a benchmark for lattice QCD results that are typically obtained at relatively high quark masses. The results compare qualitatively with lattice results from Aoki (1999) for the rho(770) mass, though the IAM dependence appears somewhat steeper; the authors note that taking the large N_c limit of ChPT (reducing the rho width) reproduces the lattice results even better. The approach enables future lattice studies of the rho(770) and f0(600) to have a model-independent chiral extrapolation formula, and the authors indicate that extensions to SU(3) coupled-channel IAM and uncertainty estimates are in progress.
