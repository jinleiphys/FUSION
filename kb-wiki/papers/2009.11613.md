# Complete experiments in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction

> The problem of extracting photoproduction amplitudes uniquely from so called complete experiments is discussed. This problem can be considered either for the extraction of full production amplitudes, or for the determination of multipoles. Both cases are treated briefly. Preliminary results for the fitting of multipoles, as well as the determination of their error, from recent polarization measurements in the $Δ$-region are described in more detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that mathematically complete experiments for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction require a minimum of 8 observables for extracting full spin amplitudes, but only 5 observables for a truncated partial wave analysis (TPWA), provided one works in the mathematically precise case without measurement uncertainties.

## Method

The author reviews the formalism of CGLN amplitudes and transversity amplitudes for photoproduction, then applies the Chiang-Tabakin method using bilinear products and Fierz identities to identify complete sets of observables. For the TPWA case, the author performs fits to actual data in the Delta-region using a truncation at ℓmax=1 (S- and P-waves), extracting Legendre coefficients from angular distributions and then minimizing a chi-squared functional to determine multipoles. A bootstrapping method with 250 resampled datasets is used to estimate multipole uncertainties and check for ambiguities.

## Key numbers

- A mathematically complete set for spin amplitude extraction requires 8 observables, including the unpolarized cross section and the three single polarization observables (Σ, T, P)
- For a TPWA with ℓmax=1, a complete set requires only 5 observables, e.g. {σ0, Σ, T, P, F}
- The fit uses NMC = 1000 Monte Carlo start configurations for the 7-dimensional multipole space (8ℓmax - 1 = 7)
- Bootstrapping was performed with 250 additional datasets generated by resampling the original data
- The analysis was performed at EγLAB ≈ 338 MeV in the Delta-region
- Additional fits replaced Kharkov P data with SAID predictions endowed with a 5% error at each datapoint

## Context

This work extends the theoretical complete experiment framework of Chiang and Tabakin (1997) and the earlier work by Omelaenko (1981) on discrete ambiguities in truncated partial wave analyses. The paper applies these mathematical results to actual polarization measurements from MAMI (Schumann-Otte data for T and F), MAMI data for σ0 and Σ, and Kharkov data for P (Belyaev data). The bootstrapping method provides a model-independent error estimation for multipole extraction, and the results show that the uncertainty of the M1- multipole is particularly sensitive to the precision of the recoil polarization observable P.
