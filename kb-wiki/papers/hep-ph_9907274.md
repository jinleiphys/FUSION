# No Spontaneous Breakdown of Chiral Symmetry in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> We argue that the spontaneous breakdown of symmetry in the chirally symmetric Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model which was supposed to illustrate the origin of the low mass of pions in hadron physics does not occur due to strong fluctuations in the sigma - pi field space. Although quarks acquire a constituent mass, sigma and pi turn out to have equal heavy masses of the order of the constituent quark mass.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, contrary to the long-standing interpretation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry does not occur in the model when strong fluctuations in the sigma-pi field space are taken into account nonperturbatively. Although quarks still acquire a constituent mass, the sigma and pi mesons end up with equal heavy masses of the order of the constituent quark mass, meaning the chiral symmetry is restored by directional fluctuations.

## Method

The authors start from the chirally symmetric Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian with SU(2) flavor and N_c colors, convert it via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation into a theory of collective scalar and pseudoscalar fields sigma and pi, and integrate out the quark fields. They then expand the effective action to second order in the fluctuating fields around the stationary point, obtaining the inverse propagators for sigma and pi, and extract the stiffness of directional fluctuations in the sigma-pi field space. Using a cutoff regularization in momentum space, they compare this stiffness to a critical stiffness derived from a second gap equation for the Lagrange multiplier field lambda, which determines whether the system is in an ordered (broken symmetry) or disordered (symmetric) phase.

## Key numbers

* The stiffness of directional fluctuations in D=4 dimensions with a cutoff Lambda is given by beta = (N_c)/(2pi)^2 { ln[1 + (Lambda/M)^2] - Lambda^2/(M^2 + Lambda^2) }, where M is the constituent quark mass.
* The critical stiffness for N_n=4 directional field components is beta_c = (4/(16 pi^2)) (Lambda_pi^2 / rho^2), where Lambda_pi is a cutoff on pion momenta and rho is the magnitude of the sigma-pi field.
* The critical number of colors required to prevent restoration of chiral symmetry is N_c = 5 when Lambda_pi = Lambda, which is incompatible with color SU(3).
* To obtain a critical number N_c = 3 would require a pionic cutoff Lambda_pi less than or approximately 0.8 Lambda.
* The ratio Lambda^2/M^2 must lie in the range (3.3, 7.3) to reproduce the experimental pion decay constant f_pi approximately 0.093 GeV for constituent quark masses M in (300, 400) MeV.
* The phase with broken symmetry for three colors would require Lambda^2 greater than or approximately 11 M^2, which lies outside the admissible range from f_pi.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative analysis of fluctuations previously applied to the Gross-Neveu model in 2+epsilon dimensions by the same authors (1998), where two separate phase transitions were identified (one for quark mass generation and one for chiral symmetry breaking). It also builds on earlier studies of directional fluctuations in Ginzburg-Landau theories with spontaneously broken O(N) symmetry by Kleinert (1998). The paper directly contradicts the standard interpretation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as a laboratory for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, as formulated by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) and extended by one of the authors (Kleinert, 1976), which had been widely used in nuclear physics. The result suggests that the model cannot describe the chiral symmetry breakdown of quark physics unless at least five colors are introduced, making it incompatible with QCD's three colors.
