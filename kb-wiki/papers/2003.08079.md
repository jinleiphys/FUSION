# Cooling of hybrid neutron stars with microscopic equations of state

> We model the cooling of hybrid neutron stars combining a microscopic nuclear equation of state in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with different quark models. We then analyze the neutron star cooling curves predicted by the different models and single out the preferred ones. We find that the possibility of neutron p-wave pairing can be excluded in our scenario.

## Key claim

The paper models the cooling of hybrid neutron stars that combine a microscopic nuclear equation of state with various quark models, and finds that neutron p-wave pairing can be excluded in their scenario, but only under the specific condition that quark superfluidity is disregarded and the Gibbs construction for the hadron-quark phase transition is used.

## Method

The authors combined a nuclear equation of state derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach using the Argonne V18 potential with three different quark models: the Dyson-Schwinger model (with two parameter sets, DS1.0 and DS1.5), the field correlator model, and the MIT bag model. They constructed hybrid star equations of state using the Gibbs phase transition construction, then computed stellar structure and cooling curves for various neutron star masses, incorporating nucleonic pairing gaps (p1S0 channel only) and quark direct Urca processes.

## Key numbers

- The purely hadronic V18 equation of state yields a maximum neutron star mass of 2.34 solar masses
- All hybrid models were adjusted to give a maximum mass of 2.10 solar masses, consistent with the observational constraint from MSP J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses, 68.3% credibility interval)
- The direct Urca process in nucleonic matter sets in at a baryon density of 0.37 fm^-3, corresponding to a neutron star mass of 1.01 solar masses
- The proton 1S0 pairing gap vanishes at a baryon density of 0.599 fm^-3 for the V18 model, corresponding to a mass of 1.92 solar masses
- The quark matter onset density ranges from 0.498 fm^-3 (DS1.0 model, mass 1.59 solar masses) to 0.629 fm^-3 (MIT bag model, mass 2.00 solar masses)
- For the V18 equation of state, the radius of a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star is 12.33 km, compatible with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (radius 13.02 km, 68% credibility interval)

## Context

This work extends previous cooling studies by the same group (Wei et al., 2016, 2018, 2019) that used purely nucleonic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations of state and found compatibility with all current cooling data. It contrasts with earlier hybrid star cooling studies using the MIT bag model (Negreiros et al., 2012) or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Carvalho et al., 2015), and with studies that included quark superfluidity (Noda et al., 2013; Sedrakian, 2016). The results enable future investigations of whether unique observational signatures could confirm or reject the presence of a hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars, and suggest that including quark superfluidity might change the cooling predictions by orders of magnitude.
