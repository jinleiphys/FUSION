# Nonperturbative Corrections to Soft Drop Jet Mass

> We provide a quantum field theory based description of the nonperturbative effects from hadronization for soft drop groomed jet mass distributions using the soft-collinear effective theory and the coherent branching formalism. There are two distinct regions of jet mass $m_J$ where grooming modifies hadronization effects. In a region with intermediate $m_J$ an operator expansion can be used, and the leading power corrections are given by three universal nonperturbative parameters that are independent of all kinematic variables and grooming parameters, and only depend on whether the parton initiating the jet is a quark or gluon. The leading power corrections in this region cannot be described by a standard normalized shape function. These power corrections depend on the kinematics of the subjet that stops soft drop through short distance coefficients, which encode a perturbatively calculable dependence on the jet transverse momentum, jet rapidity, and on the soft drop grooming parameters $z_{\rm cut}$ and $β$. Determining this dependence requires a resummation of large logarithms, which we carry out at LL order. For smaller $m_J$ there is a nonperturbative region described by a one-dimensional shape function that is unusual because it is not normalized to unity, and has a non-trivial dependence on $β$.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantum field theory based description of nonperturbative hadronization corrections for soft drop groomed jet mass distributions, showing that in an intermediate jet mass region the leading power corrections are given by three universal nonperturbative parameters (independent of kinematics and grooming parameters except for parton flavor), while in a smaller jet mass region the corrections are described by a one-dimensional shape function that is not normalized to unity and has a nontrivial dependence on the grooming parameter β.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and the coherent branching formalism to derive factorization theorems for the groomed jet mass distribution. They identify two distinct regions of jet mass m_J where grooming modifies hadronization effects: the soft drop operator expansion (SDOE) region and the soft drop nonperturbative (SDNP) region. In the SDOE region, they perform an operator product expansion to extract leading power corrections as universal nonperturbative matrix elements, and calculate the perturbative Wilson coefficients with leading logarithmic (LL) resummation accounting for running coupling effects. In the SDNP region, they derive a novel shape function description using EFT tools.

## Context

This work extends the partonic factorization theorem for soft drop groomed jet mass derived by Frye et al. (2016) to include nonperturbative hadronization corrections from QCD. It provides a rigorous operator-based alternative to Monte Carlo hadronization models (such as those in Pythia and Herwig) and to earlier scaling estimates based on single gluon emission by Dasgupta et al. (2013) and Marzani et al. (2017). The results enable field-theoretically controlled predictions for groomed jet observables at hadron colliders, applicable to both e+e- and pp collisions, and can be extended to other observables not sensitive to soft recoil effects such as angularities away from the broadening limit for a < 1.
