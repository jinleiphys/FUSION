# Charged bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ and the $Υ(5S)\to Υ(2S)π^+π^-$ decay

> Inspired by the newly observed two charged bottomonium-like states, we consider the possible contribution from the intermediate $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ states to the $Υ(5S)\to Υ(2S)π^+π^-$ decay process, which naturally explains Belle's previous observation of the anomalous $Υ(2S)π^+π^-$ production near the peak of $Υ(5S)$ at $\sqrt s=10.87$ GeV [K.F. Chen {\it et al}. (Belle Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 100}, 112001 (2008)]. The resulting $dΓ(Υ(5S)\to Υ(2S)π^+π^-)/dm_{π^+π^-}$ and $dΓ(Υ(5S)\to Υ(2S)π^+π^-)/d\cosθ$ distributions agree with Belle's measurement after inclusion of these $Z_b$ states. This formalism also reproduces the Belle observation of the double-peak structure and its reflection in $Υ(2S)π^+$ invariant mass spectrum of $Υ(5S)\to Υ(2S)π^+π^-$ decay.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the newly observed charged bottomonium-like states \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) as intermediate resonances in the \(\Upsilon(5S)\to \Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-\) decay naturally explains the previously puzzling \(\cos\theta\) distribution of the final-state pions, which could not be reproduced by direct production and hadronic-loop mechanisms alone.

## Method

The authors constructed a total decay amplitude for \(\Upsilon(5S)\to \Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-\) by summing four contributions: (a) a direct S-wave and D-wave transition described by an effective Lagrangian following Novikov and Shifman; (b) intermediate \(\sigma(600)\) and \(f_0(980)\) scalar mesons produced via \(B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}\) hadronic loops; and (c) the two charged \(Z_b\) states as intermediate resonances coupling to \(\Upsilon(5S)\pi^\mp\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\pi^\pm\). The free parameters (coupling strengths and relative phases) were determined by global fits to Belle data on the dipion invariant mass spectrum, the \(\Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\) invariant mass spectrum, and the \(\cos\theta\) angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- Mass and width of \(Z_b(10610)\): \(M = 10608.4 \pm 2.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Gamma = 15.6 \pm 2.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Mass and width of \(Z_b(10650)\): \(M = 10653.2 \pm 1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Gamma = 14.4 \pm 3.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Partial decay width of \(\Upsilon(5S) \to \Upsilon(2S) \pi^+ \pi^-\) with \(Z_b\) states included: \(\Gamma = 0.915\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with the Belle measurement \(\Gamma = 0.85 \pm 0.07(\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.16(\mathrm{syst})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Fitted coupling parameter for \(Z_b(10610)\): \(F_{Z_b(10610)} = 3.412 \pm 0.385\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (with \(f_0(980)\) included).
- Fitted coupling parameter for \(Z_b(10650)\): \(F_{Z_b(10650)} = 2.994 \pm 0.261\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (with \(f_0(980)\) included).
- Mass of \(\Upsilon(5S)\): \(10.870\ \mathrm{GeV}\); mass of \(\Upsilon(2S)\): \(10.023\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the anomalous \(\Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-\) production near the \(\Upsilon(5S)\) peak reported by Belle (K.F. Chen et al., 2008). Previous calculations by Chen et al. (2011) and Ali et al. (2009) could reproduce the dipion invariant mass spectrum but failed to describe the \(\cos\theta\) angular distribution. The inclusion of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) states, first observed by Belle in 2011, resolves this puzzle and also reproduces the double-peak structure in the \(\Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\) invariant mass spectrum. The results support the interpretation of these states as \(B\bar{B}^*\) and \(B^*\bar{B}^*\) S-wave molecular states, as previously proposed by Liu et al. (2008). The formalism can be extended to other \(\Upsilon(5S)\) hidden-bottom decay channels such as \(\Upsilon(5S)\to \Upsilon(1S,3S)\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(\Upsilon(5S)\to h_b(1P,2P)\pi^+\pi^-\).
