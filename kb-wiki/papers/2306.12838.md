# Detailed study of the astrophysical direct capture reaction $^{6}{\rm Li}(p, Îł)^{7}{\rm Be}$ in a potential model approach

> The astrophysical $S$ factor and reaction rates of the direct capture process $^{6}$Li(p,$Îł)^{7}$Be are estimated within a two-body single-channel potential model approach. Central potentials of the Gaussian-form in the $^2P_{3/2}$ and $^2P_{1/2}$ waves are adjusted to reproduce the binding energies and the empirical values of the asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) for the $^7$Be(3/2$^-$) ground and $^7$Be(1/2$^-$) excited bound states, respectively. The parameters of the potential in the most important $^2S_{1/2}$ scattering channel were fitted to reproduce the empirical phase shifts from the literature and the low-energy astrophysical $S$ factor of the LUNA collaboration. The obtained results for the astrophysical $S$ factor and the reaction rates are in a very good agreement with available experimental data sets. The numerical estimates reproduce not only the absolute values, but also the energy and temperature dependence of the $S$ factor and reaction rates of the LUNA collaboration, respectively. The estimated $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ primordial abundance ratio $(4.67\pm 0.04 )\times 10^{-10}$ is well consistent with recent BBN result of $(4.72\pm 0.72) \times 10^{-10}$ after the Planck observation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a single-channel potential model with Gaussian-form central potentials can simultaneously reproduce both the absolute values and the energy/temperature dependence of the astrophysical S factor and reaction rates for the $^{6}{\rm Li}(p,\gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ reaction as measured by the LUNA collaboration, without requiring additional free parameters for the reaction rates.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrodinger equation for the $p+^{6}{\rm Li}$ system using Gaussian-form central potentials with point-like Coulomb terms. Potential parameters in the $^{2}P_{3/2}$ and $^{2}P_{1/2}$ partial waves are fitted to reproduce the binding energies and empirical asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) for the $^{7}{\rm Be}(3/2^{-})$ ground state and $^{7}{\rm Be}(1/2^{-})$ excited state, while the $^{2}S_{1/2}$ scattering channel parameters are fitted to empirical phase shifts from the literature and the low-energy astrophysical S factor from the LUNA collaboration. The astrophysical S factor and reaction rates are then calculated from the resulting wave functions using electric and magnetic transition operators.

## Key numbers

- Zero-energy astrophysical S factor: total S(0) = 90.31 eV b (60.0 eV b for ground state $^{7}{\rm Be}(3/2^{-})$, 30.31 eV b for excited state $^{7}{\rm Be}(1/2^{-})$ at $E^{*}=0.429$ MeV)
- Astrophysical S factor at Gamow energy E=15.1 keV: total S(15.1 keV) = 89.29 eV b
- Astrophysical S factor at E=25 keV: total S(25 keV) = 73.21 eV b
- Squared ANC values used for fitting: 4.81 $\pm$ 0.38 fm$^{-1}$ for $^{7}{\rm Be}(3/2^{-})$ ground state and 4.29 $\pm$ 0.27 fm$^{-1}$ for $^{7}{\rm Be}(1/2^{-})$ excited state, from Kiss (2021)
- Estimated $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ primordial abundance ratio: $(4.67 \pm 0.04) \times 10^{-10}$, consistent with BBN result of $(4.72 \pm 0.72) \times 10^{-10}$ after Planck observation
- Reaction rate at $T_{9}=0.01$: $2.53 \times 10^{-10}$ cm$^{3}$ mol$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$; at $T_{9}=1$: $1.95 \times 10^{2}$ cm$^{3}$ mol$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach previously applied to the $^{6}{\rm Li}(p,\gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ reaction by Dubovichenko (2022), which could not simultaneously reproduce the astrophysical S factor and the temperature dependence of the LUNA reaction rates. The authors incorporate empirical ANC values from the transfer reaction analysis of Kiss (2021) to constrain the bound-state potentials. The model enables improved predictions for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis calculations of $^{7}{\rm Li}$ primordial abundance, addressing the longstanding discrepancy between observed and predicted $^{7}{\rm Li}$ abundances.
