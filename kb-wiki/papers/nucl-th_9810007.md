# Enhancement of $πA \to ππA$ Threshold Cross Sections by In-Medium $ππ$ Final State Interactions

> We address the problem of pion production in low energy $π$-nucleus collisions. For the production mechanism we assume a simple model consisting of a coherent sum of single pion exchange and the excitation---followed by the decay into two pions and a nucleon---of the $N^*(1440)$ resonance. The production amplitude is modified by the final state interaction between the pions calculated using the chirally improved Jülich meson exchange model including the polarization of the nuclear medium by the pions. The model reproduces well the experimentally observed $πA \to ππA$ cross sections, especially the enhancement with increasing $A$ of the $π^+π^-$ mass distribution in the threshold region.

# Enhancement of πA → ππA Threshold Cross Sections by In-Medium ππ Final State Interactions

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of π⁺π⁻ mass distributions near threshold in πA → ππA reactions on nuclear targets can be reproduced by medium modifications of the scalar-isoscalar S-wave ππ interaction, provided the production mechanism is modeled as a coherent sum of one-pion exchange and N*(1440) resonance decay, and the in-medium ππ amplitude is computed using the chirally improved Jülich meson exchange model with nucleon-hole and Δ-hole polarization of the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors model ππ production on a single nucleon via two mechanisms: one-pion exchange (OPE) and N*(1440) resonance formation followed by decay into a scalar-isoscalar ε resonance (around 1400 MeV) which then decays to two pions. The ππ final state interaction is computed using the chirally improved Jülich meson exchange model, which incorporates ρ exchange and contact interactions in a Blankenbecler-Sugar scattering equation, with medium polarization included through nucleon-hole and Δ-hole excitations that renormalize the single-pion propagator. The differential cross sections are folded with a Monte Carlo simulation of the CHAOS spectrometer acceptance, and Fermi motion in finite nuclei is treated by averaging over the longitudinal component of the nucleon momentum within the Fermi sphere at a fixed local density.

## Key numbers

- Incoming pion kinetic energy: Tπ = 282.7 MeV (nominal beam energy at TRIUMF)
- For deuterium target, theoretical π⁺π⁻ cross sections overestimate data for Mππ ≤ 320 MeV and underestimate for Mππ ≥ 350 MeV; π⁺π⁺ channel agreement is "quite satisfactory"
- The average nuclear density that best reproduces ⁴⁰Ca data is ρ = 0.5–0.7 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density)
- The ratio of the threshold peak to the main maximum at Mππ ≈ 350 MeV increases with small increases in density, consistent with trends from ¹²C to ⁴⁰Ca to ²⁰⁸Pb
- The N*(1440) branching ratio to N(ππ)ₛ₋wₐᵥₑᴵ⁼⁰ is 5–10%; branching ratio to Nπ is 60–70%
- The ππ coupling constant fπNN = 1.01; isospin factor IF = 2 for charged pion exchange

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Schuck et al. (1998) and the CHAOS collaboration (1996, 1998) that the scalar-isoscalar ππ channel in nuclear matter shows strong threshold enhancement. It uses the chirally improved Jülich model developed by Rapp, Durso, and Wambach (1996), which respects chiral symmetry constraints on the S-wave scattering length while fitting free ππ scattering data up to 1.5 GeV. The in-medium ππ amplitudes are the same ones used in studies of nuclear saturation by Rapp et al. (1997) and Rapp, Machleidt, and Durso (1998), where they were incorporated into the Bonn NN potential. The results enable further experimental and theoretical investigation of subthreshold ππ correlations at higher densities, which are relevant for understanding nuclear binding and the possibility of pion pair condensation.
