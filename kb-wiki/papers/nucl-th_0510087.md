# Search for the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Solutions for Chiral Rotation in N=75 Isotones

> A search for the self-consistent solutions for the chiral rotational bands in the N=75 isotones, 130Cs, 132La, 134Pr and 136Pm is performed within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock cranking approach using SKM* and SLy4 parametrizations. The dependence of the solutions on the time-odd contributions in the energy functional is studied. From among the considered four isotones, self-consistent chiral solutions are obtained only in 132La. The microscopic calculations are compared with the 132La experimental data and with results of a classical model that contains all the mechanisms underlying the chirality of the collective rotational motion. Strong similarities between the HF and classical model results are found. The suggestion formulated earlier by the authors that the chiral rotation cannot exist below a certain critical frequency is further illustrated and discussed, together with the microscopic origin of a transition from the planar to chiral rotation in nuclei. We also formulate the separability rule by which the Tilted-Axis-Cranking solutions can be inferred from three independent Principal-Axis-Cranking solutions corresponding to three different axes of rotation.

## Key claim

Self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock chiral rotational solutions are obtained only in 132La among the four N=75 isotones studied (130Cs, 132La, 134Pr, 136Pm), and the existence of chiral rotation is found to require a critical rotational frequency below which such solutions do not exist.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Tilted-Axis-Cranking (TAC) calculations using the code HFODD (v2.05c) with SKM* and SLy4 parametrizations, expanding single-particle wavefunctions onto 12 entire spherical harmonic-oscillator shells. They examined three variants of time-odd contributions in the energy functional: variant N (all time-odd coupling constants set to zero), variant G (only C_t^j and C_t^{∇j} included), and variant T (all time-odd terms except C_t^T included). The parity was conserved and pairing correlations were not included.

## Key numbers

- For 132La with SLy4 force and variant G: critical frequency ω_crit^HF = 0.60 MeV/ℏ and critical spin I_crit^HF = 20.3 ℏ
- For 132La with SkM* force and variant G: critical frequency ω_crit^HF = 0.54 MeV/ℏ and critical spin I_crit^HF = 17.8 ℏ
- For 132La with SLy4 force and variant N: critical frequency ω_crit^HF = 0.68 MeV/ℏ and critical spin I_crit^HF = 18.8 ℏ
- For 132La with SkM* force and variant N: critical frequency ω_crit^HF = 0.62 MeV/ℏ and critical spin I_crit^HF = 17.8 ℏ
- For 132La with SkM* force and variant T: critical frequency ω_crit^HF = 0.58 MeV/ℏ and critical spin I_crit^HF = 18.5 ℏ
- Deformation parameters for 132La: β = 0.26 and γ = 46° (SLy4) or β = 0.25 and γ = 45° (SkM*), nearly independent of time-odd terms
- For 134Pr, phenomenological TAC calculations gave ω_crit = 0.3 MeV/ℏ (Dim00a, Hec01a)
- For 136Pm, phenomenological TAC calculations gave ω_crit < 0.2 MeV/ℏ (Dim00a, Hec01a)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Tilted-Axis-Cranking calculations for chiral rotation (Frauendorf 1993, Dim00a, Hec01a, Sta01b) by implementing a fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach, which tests the stability of chiral configurations against core polarization. The study focuses on the N=75 isotones where candidate chiral bands were first systematically studied (Sta01b), and where lifetime measurements in 132La (Grodner 2004, 2005; Srebrny 2005) revealed deviations from earlier Particle-Rotor Model predictions. The results enable future self-consistent studies of chiral rotation in other mass regions and provide a microscopic foundation for the critical frequency concept previously suggested by the authors.
