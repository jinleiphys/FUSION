# A unified description for nuclear equation of state and fragmentation in heavy ion collisions

> We propose a model that provides a unified description of nuclear equation of state and fragmentations. The equation of state is evaluated in Bragg-Williams as well as in Bethe-Peierls approximations and compared with that in the mean field theory with Skyrme interactions. The model shows a liquid-gas type phase transition. The nuclear fragment distributions are studied for different densities at finite temperatures. Power law behavior for fragments is observed at critical point. The study of fragment distribution and the second moment $S_2$ shows that the thermal critical point coincides with the percolation point at the critical density. High temperature behavior of the model shows characteristics of chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

The lattice gas model can simultaneously describe both the nuclear equation of state (showing liquid-gas phase transition) and nuclear fragmentation, with the thermal critical point coinciding with the percolation point at the critical density of 0.5ρ₀, but only if the freeze-out density is close to that value; at other densities the percolation point occurs at different temperatures.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter using a lattice gas on a simple cubic lattice, where each site can hold at most one nucleon and nearest-neighbor interactions are given by energy -ε. The equation of state is computed in both the Bragg-Williams and Bethe-Peierls approximations, and compared with mean-field theory using a Skyrme interaction. Fragment distributions are generated by Monte Carlo sampling of lattice configurations (weighted by Boltzmann factors) and Maxwell-Boltzmann momenta, with cluster formation determined by a relative kinetic energy criterion (p_r²/2μ - ε < 0).

## Key numbers

- Critical density: ρ_C = 0.5 ρ₀ (from Bragg-Williams approximation)
- Critical temperature: kT_C = γε/4 = 1.5ε (Bragg-Williams); more exact value T_C = 1.1275ε (from Stinchcombe)
- Lattice gas critical compressibility: P_C V_C / RT_C = 2 ln 2 - 1 = 0.386 (Bragg-Williams); Van der Waals value is 0.375
- Skyrme interaction parameters: A = -356 MeV, B = 303 MeV, σ = 7/6, producing saturation density 0.16 fm⁻³ and binding energy 16 MeV per nucleon
- Lattice spacing: a³ = 1/ρ₀ = 6.25 fm³
- At density ρ/ρ₀ = 1, percolation occurs at T = 1.47 T_C in this model and at T = 1.55 T_C in the Coniglio-Klein model
- Minimum freeze-out density for percolation: ≈ 0.3 ρ₀
- Best fit to experimental data (using phenomenological temperature mapping) obtained with freeze-out density 0.39 ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the percolation models of Bauer (1988) and Campi (1986) by adding thermodynamic content via the lattice gas model from Huang (1963), enabling simultaneous calculation of the equation of state and fragment distributions. It contrasts with mean-field theories using Skyrme interactions (Jaquaman, 1984) which cannot predict fragment distributions, and with purely thermal models (Gross, 1990; Siemens, 1983; Lopez, 1989). The model enables determination of freeze-out density from experimental fragment observables, and shows that the thermal critical point coincides with the percolation point at ρ = 0.5ρ₀, a feature not imposed a priori.
