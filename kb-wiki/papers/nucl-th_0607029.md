# Spin polarized neutron matter within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> The relation between energy and density (known as the nuclear equation of state) plays a major role in a variety of nuclear and astrophysical systems. Spin and isospin asymmetries can have a dramatic impact on the equation of state and possibly alter its stability conditions. An example is the possible manifestation of ferromagnetic instabilities, which would indicate the existence, at a certain density, of a spin-polarized state with lower energy than the unpolarized one. This issue is being discussed extensively in the literature and the conclusions are presently very model dependent. We will report and discuss our recent progress in the study of spin-polarized neutron matter. The approach we take is microscopic and relativistic. The calculated neutron matter properties are derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. This makes it possible to understand the nature of the EOS properties in terms of specific features of the nuclear force model.

## Key claim

Using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with the Bonn B potential, the energy difference between fully polarized and unpolarized neutron matter continues to grow with density but at a decreasing rate, and no ferromagnetic phase transition is observed up to at least 10 times normal nuclear density; however, the curvature of the spin-symmetry energy suggests such instabilities are in principle possible within the Dirac model, even though they do not actually occur in the density range studied.

## Method

The authors solve the DBHF self-consistency problem for spin-polarized neutron matter by separating the G-matrix interaction into spin-up and spin-down components, using partial wave decomposition and a three-dimensional integration over momentum to obtain the single-particle potential for each spin state. The Pauli operator for scattering of two particles with unequal Fermi momenta is defined analogously to the isospin-asymmetric case, and the angle-average procedure is applied to the Pauli operator and two-particle propagator. The average energy per particle is then computed as a function of spin asymmetry parameter beta, and the spin-symmetry energy S(rho) is extracted from the parabolic dependence of the energy on beta squared.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between fully polarized and unpolarized neutron matter (the spin-symmetry energy) is calculated up to densities of 10 times normal nuclear density (rho_0).
- No ferromagnetic transition is observed up to at least 10 rho_0.
- The DBHF and BHF predictions for the energy difference between polarized and unpolarized states cross over just above 3 rho_0.
- The spin-symmetry energy S(rho) shows a tendency to saturate at high density, with the growth rate slowing down.
- The ratio chi_F / chi (free Fermi gas magnetic susceptibility to calculated susceptibility) is shown as a function of density up to 10 rho_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized neutron matter using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with realistic NN potentials (Vidana, Polls, and Ramos, 2002) and Monte Carlo simulations (Fantoni, Sarsa, and Schmidt, 2001), which also excluded ferromagnetic instabilities at densities up to several times normal nuclear density. It contrasts with predictions from Skyrme-type interactions (Kutshera and Wojcik, 1994) that show ferromagnetic transitions between 1.1 rho_0 and 3.5 rho_0, and with relativistic calculations based on effective meson-nucleon Lagrangians (Marcos et al., 1991) that predict transitions at several times nuclear matter density. The results enable future work combining both spin and isospin asymmetries, and investigating temperature dependence of these effects.
