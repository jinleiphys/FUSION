# NJL-model description of Goldstone boson condensation in the color-flavor locked phase

> A schematic NJL-type model is employed to investigate kaon and pion condensation in deconfined quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, explicitly referring to quark degrees of freedom. To that end we allow for non-vanishing pseudoscalar diquark condensates in addition to the scalar ones which constitute the CFL phase. Color neutrality is ensured by the appropriate choice of color chemical potentials. The dependence of the free energy in the Goldstone condensed phases on quark masses and charge chemical potentials is found to be in good qualitative -- in most cases also quantitative -- agreement with the predictions obtained within the effective Lagrangian approach.

# NJL-model description of Goldstone boson condensation in the color-flavor locked phase

## Key claim

The NJL-type model reproduces the qualitative and mostly quantitative predictions of the effective Lagrangian approach for kaon and pion condensation in the CFL phase, but the coefficient relating Goldstone boson masses to quark masses is only about one-third of the leading-order High Density Effective Theory value at strong coupling (gap ~100 MeV), indicating that the leading-order result may only be reached at very weak couplings.

## Method

The author employs a schematic NJL-type model with a point-like color-current interaction, allowing for non-vanishing pseudoscalar diquark condensates in addition to the scalar diquark condensates that define the CFL phase. The mean-field thermodynamic potential is evaluated numerically at zero temperature and fixed chemical potential mu = 400 MeV, with color neutrality enforced by adjusting color chemical potentials mu_3 and mu_8. The analysis restricts the minimization to condensates obtainable from the CFL solution via chiral rotations, comparing the free energy of the rotated states to the predictions of chiral perturbation theory in the CFL phase.

## Key numbers

- CFL gap parameters in the chiral limit (m_q = m_s = 0) with equal chemical potentials: Delta^s_22 = Delta^s_55 = Delta^s_77 = 104.2 MeV
- With m_s = 120 MeV and massless up/down quarks, before color neutrality: Delta^s_22 = 105.4 MeV, Delta^s_55 = Delta^s_77 = 100.7 MeV (5% splitting)
- Color chemical potential needed for neutrality with m_s = 120 MeV: mu_8 = -10.12 MeV
- After color neutrality with m_s = 120 MeV: Delta^s_22 = 103.4 MeV, Delta^s_55 = Delta^s_77 = 101.8 MeV
- Fitted pion decay constant from NJL model: f_pi = 80 MeV, compared to leading-order result f_pi = 83.4 MeV
- Fitted coefficient a = 0.169 for meson mass formula, about 1/3 of leading-order value 3*Delta^2/(pi^2*f_pi^2) with Delta ~ 100 MeV
- With reduced coupling g*Lambda^2 = 1.5 (gaps ~30 MeV), fitted a is about 2/3 of leading-order prediction
- Threshold for kaon condensation disappears below m_s = 31 MeV with original parameters (g*Lambda^2 = 2.6)
- Model parameters: 3-momentum cutoff Lambda = 600 MeV, coupling g*Lambda^2 = 2.6

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity in NJL-type models, which have been used to investigate various color superconducting phases (Buballa, review) but had not previously incorporated Goldstone boson condensation. The results are compared to the effective Lagrangian approach developed by Schafer (2000), Bedaque and Schafer (2002), and Kryjevski and collaborators (2002), which predicts meson condensation when effective chemical potentials exceed meson masses. The paper lays the groundwork for more complete studies of the phase structure of dense quark matter, including the recently proposed gapless phases at densities below the CFL regime (Alford, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal, 2004; Ruster, Shovkovy, and Rischke, 2004; Forbes and Zhitnitsky, 2004), and calls for future extensions to include quark-antiquark condensates and instanton effects.
