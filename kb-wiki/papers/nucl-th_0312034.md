# Improved Unitarized Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory for pion-nucleon Scattering to fourth order

> We extend our previous analysis of the unitarized pion-nucleon scattering amplitude including up to fourth order terms in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. We pay special attention to the stability of the generated Delta(1232 resonance, the convergence problems and the power counting of the chiral parameters.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous unitarized pion-nucleon scattering amplitude to include fourth-order terms in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT), demonstrating that the Delta(1232) resonance is dynamically generated with stability, but only if the low-energy constants (LEC) are properly separated according to the F^{-2} and M^{-1} power counting scheme; without this reordering, the third-order description of the P33 channel worsens significantly.

## Method

The authors re-expand the HBChPT amplitudes of Fettes and Meissner (2001) to fourth order, separating terms by powers of F^{-2} and M^{-1}, then apply an improved Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) that enforces exact unitarity. They perform Monte Carlo sampling of the perturbative LEC without fitting, then carry out constrained and unconstrained fits to the partial wave analysis data of Arndt et al. (1995) for the S and P waves, using two strategies for handling the fourth-order constants.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1232) mass from the unfitted fourth-order calculation with reordered LEC: 1238 +10/-9 MeV
- Delta(1232) width from the unfitted fourth-order calculation with reordered LEC: 125.2 +20.4/-16.4 MeV
- Delta(1232) mass from the fourth-order fit (strategy 1): 1232 +35/-29 MeV
- Delta(1232) width from the fourth-order fit (strategy 1): 107.3 +45.1/-31.0 MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the fourth-order fit (strategy 1): 0.17
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the third-order fit: 0.3
- The third-order LEC bd_18 from the fourth-order fit (strategy 1): -17.49 +/- 1.31 GeV^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous third-order unitarized analysis (Gomez Nicola et al., 2000) and the perturbative fourth-order HBChPT calculation of Fettes and Meissner (2001). It contrasts with the standard Inverse Amplitude Method of Gomez Nicola and Pelaez (1999), which required unnatural LEC values, by showing that proper LEC reordering yields natural constants. The results enable future application of unitarization methods within the Lorentz-invariant formalism of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), which promises better convergence properties.
