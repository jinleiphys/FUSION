# Statistical properties of neutron-induced reaction cross sections using random-matrix approach

> We investigate the statistical properties of neutron-induced nuclear reactions on $^{238}$U using the GOE-$S$-matrix model, in which the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) is embedded into the scattering ($S$) matrix. The GOE-$S$-matrix model does not require any experimental values of the average level spacing $D$ and average decay width $Γ$ with their statistical distributions, but the model is fully characterized by the channel transmission coefficients used in the Hauser-Feshbach theory. We demonstrate that the obtained compound nucleus decay width distribution resembles the $χ$-squared distribution with the degree of freedom greater than unity. This approach enables us to generate fluctuating cross sections while preserving requisite unitarity and accounting for interference between resonances. By comparing the calculated cross section distribution with that from $R$-matrix theory, we demonstrate a smooth transition from the resolved resonance region to the continuum region.

# Statistical properties of neutron-induced reaction cross sections using random-matrix approach

## Key claim
The GOE-S-matrix model, characterized solely by channel transmission coefficients without requiring experimental average level spacing or decay width, produces compound nucleus decay width distributions that resemble a chi-squared distribution with degrees of freedom greater than unity, and enables a smooth transition from the resolved resonance region to the continuum region while preserving unitarity and accounting for resonance interference.

## Method
The authors embedded the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) into the scattering (S) matrix and applied the resulting GOE-S-matrix model to neutron-induced reactions on 238U. They used transmission coefficients calculated by the CoH3 code that reproduce the experimental average cross section, and introduced phase shifts of incoming and outgoing channels to remap the GOE eigenchannel energy scale onto the actual laboratory energy. The distribution of decay widths was calculated using the K-matrix representation and pole expansion of the S matrix without assuming any statistical distributions, and the fluctuating and average cross sections were compared with those from the single-level Breit-Wigner formula using average resonance parameters from ENDF/B-VIII.0 and with the Reich-Moore formalism.

## Key numbers
- For the elastic scattering channel on 238U, the decay width distribution resembled a chi-squared distribution with degrees of freedom slightly greater than 1, consistent with existing theoretical prescriptions (Moldauer 1980, Kawano 2015).
- For the neutron capture channel on 238U, the decay width distribution approached a Gaussian distribution with a mean of 1, corresponding to a chi-squared distribution with larger degrees of freedom.
- The ratio of average width to average resonance spacing, ⟨Γ⟩/D, is small at very low energies but becomes greater than unity in the keV energy region for actinides, marking the transition from resolved to unresolved resonances.

## Context
This work extends the GOE-S-matrix approach of Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller, and Zirnbauer (1985) and the Monte Carlo implementation by Kawano, Talou, and Weidenmuller (2015) to a realistic case with 238U. It addresses the lack of a solid theoretical foundation for statistical properties in the unresolved resonance region, where previous approaches such as the single-level Breit-Wigner formula (Breit 1936) suffer from broken unitarity and neglect of resonance interference, and even the Reich-Moore R-matrix approximation (Reich 1958) requires knowledge of statistical distributions of resonances. The model enables consistent treatment across the resolved resonance region, unresolved resonance region, and Hauser-Feshbach region using common transmission coefficients, eliminating the need for experimental average resonance parameters and their assumed statistical distributions.
