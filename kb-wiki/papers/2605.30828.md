# Dynamical selection of fragment shell effects in spontaneous fission of $^{240}$Pu, $^{232}$Th, and $^{264}$Fm

> Understanding how fragment shell effects influence spontaneous fission mass yields remains a central challenge in nuclear fission theory. This work investigates the role of shell effects in the spontaneous fission of $^{240}$Pu, $^{232}$Th, and $^{264}$Fm by combining microscopic collective dynamics with fragment-level shell analysis. A two-step framework is employed: first, the tunneling from the inner to outer turning points is described using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin approximation along the least-action path on a potential energy surface calculated from constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Second, the dissipative descent from the outer turning points to scission is simulated via Langevin dynamics in a large collective space of quadrupole and octupole deformations. Fragment shell effects are quantified using smoothed level density indicators for representative even-even fragment pairs extracted from Langevin scission configurations. The analysis reveals that enhanced yields arise from a coherent overlap among dynamically populated scission configurations, low-energy regions on the fragment potential energy surfaces, and low neutron and/or proton level densities near the Fermi surface. Proton shell effects provide persistent microscopic selectivity in both light and heavy fragments across asymmetric channels, while neutron shell effects offer additional stabilization. Deformed shell effects at finite quadrupole and octupole deformations play a crucial role in stabilizing asymmetric fission channels. This work demonstrates that fission fragment yields reflect shell-favored configurations that are made accessible by the potential energy surface topology and populated by stochastic dynamics, with the largest yields corresponding to configurations where shell gaps provide maximal binding.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spontaneous fission yields reflect shell-favored fragment configurations that are made accessible by the potential energy surface topology and populated by stochastic Langevin dynamics, with the largest yields corresponding to configurations where shell gaps provide maximal binding, rather than simply reflecting static shell closures.

## Method

The authors employed a two-step microscopic framework combining constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the SkM* Skyrme energy density functional. First, the tunneling from inner to outer turning points was described using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation along the least-action path on the potential energy surface. Second, the dissipative descent from the outer turning points to scission was simulated via Langevin dynamics in a collective space of quadrupole and octupole deformations, with fragment shell effects quantified using smoothed level density indicators for representative even-even fragment pairs extracted from the Langevin scission configurations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{240}$Pu, the heavy fragment mass yield is dominated by a peak at $A_H\approx134\text{--}136$, with a weaker subdominant component extending to $A_H\approx140\text{--}145$
- For $^{232}$Th, the heavy fragment mass yield shows a broad peak at $A_H\approx134\text{--}136$ with a weak shoulder near $A_H\approx144$
- For $^{264}$Fm, narrow symmetric mass and charge yields are centered at $A\approx132$ and $Z\approx50$, corresponding to a symmetric split near the $^{132}$Sn configuration
- The potential energy surface was mapped on a uniform $(Q_{20}, Q_{30})$ mesh with steps of $1~\mathrm{b}$ and $1~\mathrm{b}^{3/2}$
- The smoothed level density indicator used a window width of $\Delta E=2.5~\mathrm{MeV}$
- The scission condition was defined by a neck operator value of $Q_N=2$ with $a_N=1~\mathrm{fm}$
- Raw yields were folded with a Gaussian of width $\sigma_A=3$ for mass yields and $\sigma_Z=2$ for charge yields
- 80,000 Langevin trajectories were calculated from 200 outer turning points for $^{240}$Pu

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of spontaneous fission by combining density functional theory with Langevin dynamics, building on the framework developed by Sadhukhan et al. (2016) for microscopic fission studies. The analysis of fragment shell effects using smoothed level density indicators follows the approach of Bernard et al. (2023) and Bernard (2024) for studying shell stabilization in fission fragments. The results for $^{240}$Pu are compared with experimental data from Thierens (1981) and Laidler (1962), as well as with GEF predictions, and the mass components are discussed in relation to the Brosa (1990) phenomenological picture of standard I (S1) and standard II (S2) fission modes. The work enables future studies of how potential energy surface topology and stochastic dynamics jointly determine which shell-favored configurations appear as yield peaks across the nuclear chart.
