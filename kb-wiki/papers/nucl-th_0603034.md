# From non-Hermitian effective operators to large-scale no-core shell model calculations for light nuclei

> No-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using ab initio effective interactions are very successful in reproducing experimental nuclear spectra. The main theoretical approach is the use of effective operators, which include correlations left out by the truncation of the model space to a numerically tractable size. We review recent applications of the effective operator approach, within a NCSM framework, to the renormalization of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, as well as scalar and tensor operators.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how effective operators constructed via a unitary transformation approach can, in principle, reproduce full-space observables in a truncated model space, but the practical two-body cluster approximation yields strong renormalization only for short-range operators, while long-range operators like the quadrupole transition operator are only weakly renormalized at that level, contrary to earlier expectations from calculations that included up to six-body correlations.

## Method

The authors apply the Okubo unitary transformation method to construct energy-independent effective Hamiltonians and general effective operators within the no-core shell model (NCSM) framework. They use a cluster approximation (two-body or three-body) to compute the transformation operator ω from exact eigenvectors of the a-body subsystem, then solve the A-body problem in a harmonic-oscillator basis truncated by NmaxℏΩ excitations. The method is applied to the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential for ⁴He, ⁶Li, ¹⁰C, ¹²C, and ¹⁴C, computing ground-state energies, B(E2) transition strengths, and the longitudinal-longitudinal distribution function.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He ground-state energy converges to the exact Faddeev-Yakubovski result for the CD Bonn potential when using the three-body cluster approximation; the exact result misses the experimental value by a few MeV because three-body forces are neglected.
- For ⁴He, the ground-state energy converges faster at ℏΩ = 28 MeV than at ℏΩ = 19 MeV, while the first 0⁺ excited state converges faster at ℏΩ = 19 MeV.
- ⁶Li B(E2, 1⁺0 → 3⁺0) in a 2ℏΩ model space: 2.647 e²fm⁴ with bare operator, 2.784 e²fm⁴ with effective operator; in a 10ℏΩ model space: 10.221 e²fm⁴ with bare operator.
- ⁶Li B(E2, 2⁺0 → 1⁺0) in a 2ℏΩ model space: 2.183 e²fm⁴ with bare operator, 2.269 e²fm⁴ with effective operator; in a 10ℏΩ model space: 4.502 e²fm⁴ with bare operator.
- ¹⁰C B(E2, 2₁⁺0 → 0⁺0) in a 4ℏΩ model space: 3.05 e²fm⁴ with bare operator, 3.08 e²fm⁴ with effective operator.
- ¹²C B(E2, 2₁⁺0 → 0⁺0) in a 4ℏΩ model space: 4.03 e²fm⁴ with bare operator, 4.05 e²fm⁴ with effective operator.
- For ⁴He, the longitudinal-longitudinal distribution function computed with effective operators becomes model-space invariant at high momentum transfer (short range), while at intermediate q ≈ 2.5 fm⁻¹ even the effective operator results vary.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM effective-operator formalism of Navratil et al. (1993, 2000) and Okubo (1954) to general scalar and tensor operators beyond the Hamiltonian. It contrasts with the earlier NCSM calculation by Navratil et al. (1996) that obtained the large empirical effective charges (1.5 e for protons, 0.5 e for neutrons) by including up to six-body correlations in a 0ℏΩ space; the present two-body cluster results show only weak renormalization of the quadrupole operator, indicating that higher-order clusters are essential for long-range operators. The method enables consistent calculation of effective operators for electromagnetic transitions and response functions in light nuclei up to A ≤ 16, and provides a path to test chiral nuclear interactions and three-nucleon forces.
