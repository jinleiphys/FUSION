# Mass from Nothing

> We study the Abelian Higgs model with multiple scalar fields, but without mass terms. Solving the model non-perturbatively order-by-order in the number of scalar fields, we find that radiative corrections generate masses for the scalar and gauge boson, without spontaneous symmetry breaking. The mass scales are set by the $Λ$-parameter of the electroweak running coupling, thereby naturally avoiding the hierarchy problem. No part of our calculation employs a weak-coupling expansion, and we find that the perturbative vacuum is metastable, and hence must decay to the stable non-perturbative vacuum of the theory, which we identify. Although the field content of our Lagrangian is standard, our results predict the existence of two heavy scalar resonances in addition to the Higgs. We believe that these predicted resonances will ultimately allow experimentalists to discriminate between our method and standard solutions of the Higgs model.

## Key claim

The authors propose a mechanism for mass generation in the Abelian Higgs model that does not rely on spontaneous symmetry breaking or tachyonic mass terms, instead generating scalar and gauge boson masses purely from radiative corrections in a large N expansion. The claim is conditional on the large N limit being physically realized, and the authors acknowledge that their results strictly hold only in this limit, though they note consistency with expectations about metastability of the perturbative vacuum in the full Standard Model.

## Method

The authors study the Abelian Higgs model with N complex scalar fields and no mass terms in the Lagrangian, using a large N expansion (order-by-order in 1/N) as a non-perturbative solution technique rather than weak-coupling perturbation theory. They gauge-fix by imposing a condition on the scalar fields (making them real), introduce an auxiliary field to rewrite the quartic scalar interaction, integrate out the scalar fields, and evaluate the effective potential in dimensional regularization to next-to-leading order in 1/N. The renormalized effective potential is then minimized to find the vacuum state and determine the generated masses.

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative saddle point gives a scalar (Higgs) mass squared of \( \bar m_{\rm Higgs}^2 = \Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}}^2 e^1 + \mathcal{O}(N^{-1}) \), where \( \Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} \) is the QCD-like scale parameter of the electroweak running coupling
- The gauge boson mass is given by \( \bar m_A^2 = \bar m_{\rm Higgs}^2 u^2 + \mathcal{O}(N^{-1}) \), where \( u^2 = e_R^2 / \lambda_R \) is a constant ratio of renormalized couplings
- The effective potential at the non-perturbative saddle is \( \frac{\mathcal{V}(m_H=\bar m_H)}{\text{vol}} = -\frac{1+\frac{8+3u^4}{N}}{128\pi^2} \exp\left[2 - \frac{4(10.42+u^4+3u^4\ln u)}{N}\right] \), which is negative (lower free energy) compared to the perturbative saddle at zero
- The theory predicts two additional scalar resonances at masses approximately 1.84 and 2.63 times the Higgs boson mass

## Context

This work extends the symmetric mass generation approach previously discussed for scalar and fermionic theories by Frohlich (1981), Kondo (2018), Tong (2021), and Butt (2021), combining it with the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism (1973) for radiative mass generation and large N expansion techniques from Abbott (1975) and Linde (1976). The authors build on recent developments showing that scalar quantum field theory may be non-trivial in four dimensions (Romatschke 2023), exploiting a loophole in the Aizenman (2019) proof of quantum triviality. The results enable a non-perturbative solution of the Abelian Higgs model that avoids the hierarchy problem and predicts experimentally testable heavy scalar resonances, with a follow-up work planned to generalize to non-Abelian Higgs and full electroweak theory.
