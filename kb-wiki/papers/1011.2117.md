# Numerical treatment of long-range Coulomb potential with Berggren bases

> The Schrodinger equation incorporating the long-range Coulomb potential takes the form of a Fredholm equation whose kernel is singular on its diagonal when represented by a basis bearing a continuum of states, such as in a Fourier-Bessel transform. Several methods have been devised to tackle this difficulty, from simply removing the infinite-range of the Coulomb potential with a screening or cut function to using discretizing schemes which take advantage of the integrable character of Coulomb kernel singularities. However, they have never been tested in the context of Berggren bases, which allow many-body nuclear wave functions to be expanded, with halo or resonant properties within a shell model framework. It is thus the object of this paper to test different discretization schemes of the Coulomb potential kernel in the framework of complex-energy nuclear physics. For that, the Berggren basis expansion of proton states pertaining to the sd-shell arising in the A ~ 20 region, being typically resonant, will be effected. Apart from standard frameworks involving a cut function or analytical integration of singularities, a new method will be presented, which replaces diagonal singularities by finite off-diagonal terms. It will be shown that this methodology surpasses in precision the two former techniques.

# Numerical treatment of long-range Coulomb potential with Berggren bases

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a new "off-diagonal method" for discretizing the Coulomb potential kernel in Berggren bases surpasses both the cut method and the subtraction method in precision, but only for the specific case of diagonalizing a one-body Hamiltonian where the basis-generating potential and the diagonalized potential differ only through their charge parameter ΔZc.

## Method
The author tests three discretization schemes for treating the singular diagonal kernel of the Coulomb potential when expanding proton states in Berggren bases: (1) a cut method that removes improper integrals by truncating the Coulomb potential at a finite radius R; (2) a subtraction method that separates the kernel into an analytically integrable singular part and a regular remainder; and (3) a new off-diagonal method that replaces diverging diagonal matrix elements with finite off-diagonal terms evaluated at shifted momenta k± = k ± w/(4π). The methods are applied to diagonalize a one-body Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon and Coulomb potentials for proton states in the sd-shell (A ∼ 20 region), using Gauss-Legendre quadrature to discretize the Berggren contour.

## Key numbers
- The off-diagonal method reproduces the integral I(k,kmax) = ∫⟨s_k'|Vc(ΔZc,r)|s_k⟩ dk' with relative differences of 10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁴ for NGL = 50 Gauss-Legendre points, decreasing by an order of magnitude when NGL is doubled.
- Three values of α are tested for the Coulomb potential: α = 0.25, 0.45, and 0.65 fm⁻¹, corresponding to nuclei with approximately 70, 10, and 5 nucleons respectively.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used are: diffuseness d = 0.65 fm, radius R₀ = 3 fm, central depth V₀ = 52 MeV, spin-orbit depth Vₛₒ = 5 MeV.
- Basis charge Zc⁽ᵇ⁾ = 10 and diagonalized charge Zc⁽ᵈ⁾ = 8, giving ΔZc = -2.
- The Coulomb potential parameter α is set to α = 3√π/(4R₀) to match the uniformly charged-sphere value at r=0.

## Context
This work extends previous treatments of Coulomb singularities in momentum-space representations (PRD_sub, PRC_sub, PRA_sub) to the Berggren basis framework, which is essential for the Gamow shell model (Mic09). The Berggren basis completeness for charged particles was established only recently (JMP), and previous numerical tests of completeness relations (Mic03, Mic04, Cocoyoc, Hagen_Vaagen) excluded the Coulomb potential from the diagonalized kernel. The off-diagonal method enables future calculations of observables connecting nuclei with different proton numbers and isospin operator expectation values in the Gamow shell model, particularly for light nuclei where Berggren bases are widely used (Mic02, Mic03, Mic04, Gaute_Morten, Gaute_Morten_2, Gaute_Morten_3, Mic07, Mic07a, Mic10).
