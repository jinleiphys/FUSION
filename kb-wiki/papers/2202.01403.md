# Quasi-normal g-modes of neutron stars with quarks

> Quasi-normal oscillation modes of neutron stars provide a means to probe their interior composition using gravitational wave astronomy. We compute the frequencies and damping times of composition-dependent core g-modes of neutron stars containing quark matter employing linearized perturbative equations of general relativity. We find that ignoring background metric perturbations due to the oscillating fluid, as in the Cowling approximation, underestimates the g-mode frequency by up to 10% for higher mass stars, depending on the parameters of the nuclear equation of state and how the mixed phase is constructed. The g-mode frequencies are well-described by a linear scaling with the central lepton (or combined lepton and quark) fraction for nucleonic (hybrid) stars. Our findings suggest that neutron stars with and without quarks are manifestly different with regards to their quasi-normal g-mode spectrum, and may thus be distinguished from one another in future observations of gravitational waves from merging neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper computes the frequencies and damping times of composition-dependent core g-modes of neutron stars containing quark matter using fully general relativistic perturbative equations, finding that the Cowling approximation (which ignores background metric perturbations) underestimates the g-mode frequency by up to 10% for higher mass stars, depending on the nuclear equation of state parameters and how the mixed phase is constructed.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Einstein equations for polar (even-parity) perturbations of a self-gravitating fluid in general relativity, using the Lindblom-Detweiler formalism with four coupled first-order ODEs for the perturbation amplitudes H1, K, W, and X. They employed the Zhao-Lattimer (ZL) model for nucleonic matter, the vMIT bag model for quark matter, and both the Gibbs construction (for a soft first-order transition) and the Kapusta-Welle (KW) crossover framework to describe the hadron-quark mixed phase. The g-mode frequencies were obtained as eigenvalues satisfying the boundary condition of vanishing Lagrangian pressure variation at the stellar surface, and results were compared against the relativistic Cowling approximation where metric perturbations are set to zero.

## Key numbers

* For the ZL nucleonic model with L=40 MeV, the maximum neutron star mass is 2.09 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 10.3 km; for L=70 MeV, the maximum mass is 2.10 solar masses with radius 11.2 km.
* For the KW crossover model with L=40 MeV, the maximum mass is 2.03 solar masses with radius 10.6 km; for L=70 MeV, the maximum mass is 2.01 solar masses with radius 11.7 km.
* For the Gibbs construction model with L=40 MeV, the maximum mass is 2.04 solar masses with radius 10.6 km.
* The Cowling approximation underestimates the g-mode frequency by up to 10% for higher mass stars compared to the full general relativistic calculation.
* The g-mode frequencies follow a linear scaling with the central lepton fraction for nucleonic stars and with the combined lepton and quark fraction for hybrid stars.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of composition g-modes in neutron stars (Jaikumar 2021, 2021PhRvD.104.l23032C) which used only the relativistic Cowling approximation, by providing the first fully general relativistic treatment of such modes for realistic neutron and hybrid stars. Prior work by Reisenegger and Goldreich (RG, RG2) pioneered composition g-modes in Newtonian gravity, while subsequent studies by Kantor (2014) and Dommes (2015) used the relativistic Cowling approximation. The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish neutron stars with and without quark cores through their quasi-normal g-mode spectrum, particularly in merger signals from next-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope or Cosmic Explorer.
