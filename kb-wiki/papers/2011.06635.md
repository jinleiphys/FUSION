# Phase transitions and critical behavior in hadronic transport with a relativistic density functional equation of state

> We develop a flexible, relativistically covariant parameterization of dense nuclear matter equation of state suited for inclusion in computationally demanding hadronic transport simulations. Within an implementation in the hadronic transport code SMASH, we show that effects due to bulk thermodynamic behavior are reproduced in dynamic hadronic systems, demonstrating that hadronic transport can be used to study critical behavior in dense nuclear matter, both at and away from equilibrium. We also show that two-particle correlations calculated from hadronic transport simulation data follow theoretical expectations based on the second order cumulant ratio, and constitute a clear signature of crossing the phase diagram above the critical point.

## Key claim

The authors develop a flexible, relativistically covariant parameterization of dense nuclear matter equation of state for hadronic transport simulations, and demonstrate that hadronic transport can reproduce bulk thermodynamic behavior and critical phenomena in dense nuclear matter. The hedge is that the high-density phase transition modeled is fundamentally different from QCD because the degrees of freedom do not change across the transition within Landau Fermi-liquid theory, unlike in QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic vector density functional (VDF) model based on Landau Fermi-liquid theory, where the energy density is postulated with vector-current interactions parameterized by coefficients \(C_n\) and powers \(b_n\). They implement this mean-field model in the hadronic transport code SMASH, using four interaction terms to produce two first-order phase transitions (nuclear liquid-gas and a postulated QGP-like transition), with the eight free parameters fixed by constraints including saturation density, binding energy, and critical point positions. The equations of motion are derived from the quasiparticle energy and solved dynamically in the transport simulation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.160\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Binding energy at saturation: \(E_0 = -16.3\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nuclear liquid-gas critical point: \(T_c^{(N)} = 18\ \text{MeV}\), \(n_c^{(N)} = 0.06\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Example QGP-like critical temperatures: \(T_c^{(Q)} = 50\ \text{MeV}\) (sets I-III), \(100\ \text{MeV}\) (sets IV-V), and \(125\ \text{MeV}\) (set VI)
- Example QGP-like critical densities: \(n_c^{(Q)} = 3.0\ n_0\) (sets I, II, IV) and \(4.0\ n_0\) (sets III, V, VI)
- Incompressibility at saturation for example sets: ranges from \(K_0 = 260\ \text{MeV}\) (set I) to \(280\ \text{MeV}\) (set III)

## Context

This work extends the use of hadronic transport beyond simple afterburner stages by incorporating mean-field interactions that reproduce a chosen equation of state with critical behavior, addressing a gap noted in prior approaches such as Nara (2016) where hadronic potentials were typically neglected. The VDF model is designed to enable systematic Bayesian analyses (as in Novak 2013 and Bernhard 2016) comparing different dense matter equations of state against experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. The approach enables future studies of critical point signatures in dynamical simulations, including two-particle correlations and multiplicity fluctuations, which are otherwise inaccessible to purely theoretical predictions.
