# Collective states from random interactions

> The anharmonic vibrator and rotor regions in nuclei are investigated in the framework of the interacting boson model using an ensemble of random one- and two-body interactions. Despite the randomness of the interactions (in sign and size) we find a predominance of L(P)=0(+) ground states and strong evidence for the occurrence of both vibrational and rotational band structure.

# Collective states from random interactions

## Key claim
Despite using completely random one- and two-body interactions (in sign and size) within the interacting boson model, the authors find that vibrational and rotational band structures emerge in a significant fraction of cases, but only for sufficiently large boson number relative to the rank of the interaction; the bands appear gradually as a function of N/k, with little or no evidence for such bands when N is comparable to k.

## Method
The authors used the interacting boson model (IBM) with N=16 bosons and an ensemble of random one- and two-body Hamiltonians, where the two one-body and seven two-body matrix elements were chosen independently from a Gaussian distribution with zero mean and variance v^2. For each of 1000 random runs, they calculated the entire energy spectrum and B(E2) values between yrast states, then analyzed the probability distribution of the energy ratio R = [E(4+)-E(0+)]/[E(2+)-E(0+)] and the correlation between B(E2) ratios and energy ratios.

## Key numbers
- 63.4% of runs produced a ground state with L^P = 0^+
- 16.7% of runs produced a ground state with L^P = 32^+ (the maximum angular momentum for N=16)
- 13.8% of runs produced a ground state with L^P = 2^+
- The probability distribution P(R) showed a peak at R ~ 1.95, close to the harmonic vibrator value of 2
- A second, narrower peak appeared at R ~ 3.35, close to the rotor value of 10/3 (~3.33)
- About 50% of all cases with a 0^+ ground state corresponded to the vibrator region in the correlation plot of B(E2) ratios versus energy ratios
- About 25% of all cases corresponded to the rotor region

## Context
This work extends the observation from the nuclear shell model with random interactions, where a predominance of 0^+ ground states was found (Johnson, 1998; Bijker and Frank, 1999), into the interacting boson model framework. The study builds on the empirical tripartite classification of nuclear structure into seniority, anharmonic vibrator, and rotor regions (Zamfir, 1999). The results enable further investigation into how collective structure emerges from random interactions as a function of boson number N and interaction rank k, and emphasize that regular features arise from a much wider class of Hamiltonians than those usually considered realistic.
