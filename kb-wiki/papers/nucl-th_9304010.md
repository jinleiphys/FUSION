# Model Analysis of Time Reversal Symmetry Test in the Caltech Fe-57 Gamma-Transition Experiment

> The CALTECH gamma-transition experiment testing time reversal symmetry via the E2/M1 mulipole mixing ratio of the 122 keV gamma-line in Fe-57 has already been performed in 1977. Extending an earlier analysis in terms of an effective one-body potential, this experiment is now analyzed in terms of effective one boson exchange T-odd P-even nucleon nucleon potentials. Within the model space considered for the Fe-57 nucleus no contribution from isovector rho-type exchange is possible. The bound on the coupling strength phi_A from effective short range axial-vector type exchange induced by the experimental bound on sin(eta) leads to phi_A < 10^{-2}.

## Key claim

The experimental bound on the T-odd mixing ratio in the 122 keV gamma-transition of Fe-57, when analyzed in terms of effective one-boson-exchange T-odd P-even nucleon-nucleon potentials, yields a bound on the axial-vector coupling strength phi_A of about 10^{-2}, but only if short-range correlations are included; without them the bound would be artificially stronger by roughly two orders of magnitude.

## Method

The author evaluated the T-odd admixture to the E2/M1 mixing ratio of the 122 keV transition in Fe-57 using first-order perturbation theory, with the T-violating potential taken as either rho-meson vector exchange or axial-vector meson exchange. Nuclear wave functions were computed in a restricted shell-model space (three valence neutron particles in the 2p1/2, 2p3/2, 1f5/2 shells and two valence proton holes in the 1f7/2 shell above a closed Ni-56 core) using a surface delta interaction, and short-range correlations were included via a correlation function f(r) = 1 - exp(-a r^2)(1 - b r^2).

## Key numbers

* The experimental bound on the T-odd part of the mixing ratio is |sin(eta)| = (3.1 +/- 6.5) x 10^{-4} for the 122.1 keV gamma line in Fe-57.
* The calculated relation between sin(eta) and the axial-vector coupling strength is sin(eta) = 3.14 x 10^{-2} phi_A.
* The resulting bound on the axial-vector coupling strength is phi_A = (1 +/- 2) x 10^{-2}.
* The earlier bound on the effective one-body coupling constant G_T from the same experiment was |G_T| <= 5 x 10^{-6}.
* The short-range correlation function uses parameters a = 1.1 fm^{-2} and b = 0.68 fm^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends an earlier analysis by Beyer (1989) that used an effective one-body potential to bound T-violation in the Caltech Fe-57 experiment, and parallels the approach of Grimlet and Vogel (1991) who investigated T- and P-violating pion exchange potentials. The analysis shows that within the chosen model space, isovector rho-type exchange gives no contribution, and the bound on axial-vector exchange (phi_A ~ 10^{-2}) is about four orders of magnitude weaker than the bound on the effective one-body potential, indicating that the origin of the one-body potential requires clarification through more complex mechanisms such as two-boson exchange.
