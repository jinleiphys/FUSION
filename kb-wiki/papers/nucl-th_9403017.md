# Ground-state Correlation Effects in Extended RPA Calculations

> We study normalization problems associated with use of perturbatively correlated ground-states in extended RPA schemes in the context of a specific but typical example. The sensitivity of the results to the amount of $2p2h$ admixtures to the correlated ground state is also investigated in terms of a modification of the standard perturbative approach.

# Ground-state Correlation Effects in Extended RPA Calculations

## Key claim
The paper shows that the standard perturbative treatment of ground-state correlations in extended second random phase approximation (ESRPA) leads to inflated strength distributions due to normalization errors, and that even after correcting the normalization, significant excess strength remains compared to the simpler second random phase approximation (SRPA). This excess is sensitive to the amount of 2p2h admixtures in the ground state, and using Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory to reduce these admixtures brings the results close to the SRPA.

## Method
The authors compute the Gamow-Teller (GT) response for ⁴⁸Ca using the MY3 force in a 0ħω–3ħω oscillator space, comparing four treatments: plain SRPA (Hartree-Fock ground state), ESRPA (Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation with c₀=1), normalized ESRPA (NESRPA, with c₀ adjusted for unit norm), and modified ESRPA (MESRPA, using Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory for the ground-state amplitudes). The linear response function R(E) is evaluated via projection operators onto 1p1h and 2p2h subspaces, with complex energy shifts Δ=1 MeV for 1p1h and Δ=3 MeV for 2p2h subspaces to smooth the strength function.

## Key numbers
- The ground-state energy from the Brillouin-Wigner dispersion equation is E₀ = −29 MeV, about 8% of the experimental ground-state binding energy of ⁴⁸Ca.
- The percentage of 2p2h admixtures in the ground state is reduced from 62% in ESRPA to 31% in MESRPA.
- In SRPA, 30% of the low-energy GT strength is shifted to the high-energy region (E > 20 MeV) compared to RPA.
- In ESRPA, the low-energy GT strength S₊< increases to 82% of the sum rule.
- The positive branch of the GT sum rule S₊ − S₋ = 3(N−Z) is divided at E = 20 MeV into low-energy (S₊<) and high-energy (S₊>) parts.

## Context
This work extends the critique by Van Neck et al. (1993) of perturbative normalization in nuclear many-body calculations, specifically addressing the ESRPA formalism of Arima (1980s). The authors show that the normalization problem inflates strength distributions and that even after correction, the results remain sensitive to the amount of 2p2h ground-state correlations. The MESRPA using Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory, which treats 0p0h–2p2h coupling consistently with 1p1h–2p2h coupling, yields results close to the simpler SRPA. The authors argue these trends should apply to other response functions, including longitudinal and transverse inclusive responses in quasi-free electron scattering as studied by Alberico et al. (1988), Takayanagi et al. (1990), and Mariano et al. (1993).
