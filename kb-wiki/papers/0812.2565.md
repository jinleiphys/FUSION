# Laser-induced nonresonant nuclear excitation in muonic atoms

> Coherent nuclear excitation in strongly laser-driven muonic atoms is calculated. The nuclear transition is caused by the time-dependent Coulomb field of the oscillating charge density of the bound muon. A closed-form analytical expression for electric multipole transitions is derived and applied to various isotopes; the excitation probabilities are in general very small. We compare the process with other nuclear excitation mechanisms through coupling with atomic shells and discuss the prospects to observe it in experiment.

## Key claim

Coherent nuclear excitation in strongly laser-driven muonic atoms is possible via the time-dependent Coulomb field of the oscillating muon charge density, but the excitation probabilities are always very small because the driving laser frequency is far off resonance with the nuclear level spacing; the process might come into experimental reach by powerful XFEL facilities in the near future.

## Method

The authors model a hydrogenlike muonic atom in the ground state exposed to a superintense laser field, restricting consideration to nuclear charges Z ≤ 10. The nuclear Coulomb field is approximated by a spherical harmonic oscillator potential to allow an analytical, nonperturbative treatment of the laser-driven muon dynamics. A closed-form analytical expression for electric multipole transition probabilities is derived within first-order perturbation theory, using the nuclear long-wavelength limit and a multipole expansion of the Coulomb interaction between muonic and nuclear charge densities.

## Key numbers

- For muonic ¹⁹F (E1 transition at 110 keV), the nuclear excitation probability reaches about 10⁻¹⁴ at 10²⁶ W/cm² laser intensity with 10 keV XFEL radiation.
- For muonic ¹⁶N (E2 transition at 120 keV), the excitation probability is substantially smaller than for ¹⁹F at the same laser parameters.
- The probability for an E1 transition in a hydrogenlike muonic atom is larger by 9 orders of magnitude than in the corresponding electronic system, when the laser field strength is scaled so that the ratio u₀/a₀ is identical.
- The muonic Bohr radius for ¹⁹F is 28.4 fm; for ¹⁶N it is 36.5 fm; for electronic ²³⁵U (treated nonrelativistically) it is 575 fm.
- The atomic binding energy (½ħω₀) is 228 keV for muonic ¹⁹F and 138 keV for muonic ¹⁶N.
- The barrier-suppression field strength E_OBI corresponds to u₀ = a₀/16.
- The free muon lifetime is 2.2 μs.
- The Coulomb field in the ground state of muonic hydrogen corresponds to approximately 4 × 10²⁵ W/cm² and scales as Z⁶ with atomic number.
- The nuclear γ-ray wavelength ħ̸ = c/ω_N ≈ 10³ fm for low-lying transitions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear excitation by coherent electron motion (NECEM), previously studied for electronic atoms such as ²³⁵U by Becker, Goreslavsky, and Hartmann (1991) and by Sokolik and Belyanin (1988), to muonic atoms where the smaller Bohr radius provides a substantial enhancement. The process is compared with resonant nuclear excitation by electron transition (NEET), which was first measured in ¹⁸⁹Os in the mid-1970s and more recently in ¹⁹⁷Au, and with muonic NEET observed as early as 1960. The results indicate that while muonic atoms are favorable candidates, the nonresonant nature of the process makes experimental observation extremely challenging; the authors suggest that relativistic ion beam collisions at GSI or future XFEL facilities might enable detection.
