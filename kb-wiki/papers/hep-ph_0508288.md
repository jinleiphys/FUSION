# X(1835) as the Lowest Mass Pseudoscalar Glueball and Proton Spin Problem

> We consider the parity doublet structure observed in high hadronic excitations within the instanton model for the QCD vacuum. In the conventional approach this doubling phenomenon is treated as a manifestation of the partial restoration of chiral or $U(1)_A$ symmetry. We demonstrate that the suppression of direct instanton contribution to the masses of excited hadrons leads to the partial $U(1)_A$ symmetry restoration in hadron spectrum. The origin of X(1835) resonance observed by BES Collaboration is studied upon the doublet structure. We argue also how X(1835) be interpreted as the lowest pseudoscalar glueball state, and derive its coupling constant to proton. It turns out that this coupling is large and negative. Demonstrated is how this large coupling affects the gluonic contribution to the proton spin.

# X(1835) as the Lowest Mass Pseudoscalar Glueball and Proton Spin Problem

## Key claim
The X(1835) resonance observed by BES Collaboration can be interpreted as the lowest mass pseudoscalar glueball state, forming a parity doublet with the scalar glueball candidate f0(1710). This interpretation is conditional on the instanton mechanism of partial U(1)A symmetry restoration in highly excited hadrons, where the suppression of direct instanton contributions to the correlators of opposite-parity currents leads to parity degeneracy.

## Method
The authors use the instanton model of the QCD vacuum to compute the direct instanton contribution to the difference of scalar and pseudoscalar glueball current correlators. They match this theoretical expression with a phenomenological sum over parity doublet states, fitting the residues of the [X(1835), f0(1710)] doublet while fixing the [eta'(958), f0(980)] doublet residue from the known eta' coupling to gluons. The coupling of X(1835) to protons is then estimated using an effective instanton-induced Lagrangian for two-gluon interaction with the flavor-singlet pseudoscalar quark current, and the resulting contribution to the flavor-singlet axial coupling of the proton is evaluated via a generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key numbers
- Residue of X(1835) coupling to gluons: lambda_X = 2.95 GeV^3, obtained from fitting the direct instanton contribution to the correlator difference in the interval Q > 1 GeV
- Residue of eta'(958) coupling to gluons: lambda_eta' = 0.88 GeV^3, derived from F_eta' = sqrt(3) * f_pi with f_pi = 132 MeV
- Gluon contribution to the flavor-singlet axial coupling: g_A^{glue} ≈ -0.39
- Total flavor-singlet axial coupling: g_A^0 ≈ 0.4, in agreement with experimental data g_A^0 ≈ 0.3 to 0.4
- Coupling constant of X(1835) to proton: |g_{XPP}| ≈ 3.5, with negative sign, from experimental branching ratio of J/Psi → gamma p pbar
- Valence quark contribution to proton spin: g_A^v = 0.79, using SU(6) value g_{eta' NN} = 6.5
- Ratio of glueball to eta' coupling to proton: g_{XPP} / g_{eta' PP} ≈ -2, estimated from instanton-induced interaction

## Context
This work extends the instanton-based explanation of parity doubling in excited hadrons, previously discussed by Glozman (2000) and Shifman (2000) in terms of chiral or U(1)A symmetry restoration, by providing a specific dynamical mechanism via suppression of direct instanton contributions. The interpretation of X(1835) as a pseudoscalar glueball challenges the baryonium interpretation proposed by Zhu (2005) and others, and offers a new resolution to the proton spin problem by attributing the small observed value of g_A^0 to a large negative gluonic contribution from the light glueball. The approach connects to earlier work on the QCD topological susceptibility by Narison (1996), Ioffe (1995), and Dorokhov (2003), and to instanton-based calculations of the proton spin by Kochelev (1998), Schafer (1995), and Shuryak and Forte (1994).
