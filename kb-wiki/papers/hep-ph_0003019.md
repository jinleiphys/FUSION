# Chiral symmetry breaking, color superconductivity and quark matter phase diagram: a variational approach

> We discuss in this note simultaneous existence of chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity at finite temperature and density in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type model. The methodology involves an explicit construction of a variational ground state and minimisation of the thermodynamic potential. There exists solutions to the gap equations at finite densities with both quark antiquark as well as diquark condensates for the "ground" state. However, such a phase is thermodynamically unstable with the pressure being negative in this region. We also compute the equation of state, and obtain the structure of the phase diagram in the model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while solutions to the coupled gap equations exist at finite densities with both quark-antiquark and diquark condensates present simultaneously, this mixed phase is thermodynamically unstable because the pressure becomes negative in that region.

## Method

The authors constructed a variational ground state with both quark-antiquark and diquark condensates in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with a four-fermion point interaction. They included finite temperature and density effects using thermofield dynamics through a thermal Bogoliubov transformation, then minimized the thermodynamic potential to obtain coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass M and the superconducting gap Delta.

## Key numbers

- Dynamically generated quark mass at zero temperature and density: approximately 490 MeV (with coupling g^2 = 55.98 GeV^{-2} and cutoff Lambda = 0.67 GeV)
- Critical fermi momentum for chiral symmetry restoration: k_f^c = 0.39 GeV, corresponding to a quark number density of 1.7 per fm^3
- Maximum superconducting gap (without chiral condensate): about 90 MeV at fermi momentum around 550 MeV
- Superconducting gap vanishes at around k_f = 600 MeV due to the cutoff
- Superconducting transition temperature: approximately 55 MeV
- Chiral transition temperature at zero density: approximately 270 MeV
- Tricritical point for the chiral phase transition: approximately 84 MeV
- Bag constant (energy density at zero quark number density and zero temperature): approximately -(0.2 GeV)^4

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry breaking in Coulomb gauge QCD models (Finger 1981, Davis 1988, Alkofer 1989) and color superconductivity studies using NJL models (Wilczek 1998, Sarah 1999) by considering both condensates simultaneously. The variational approach differs from mean-field approximations used by Asakawa (1989) and others. The resulting phase diagram structure is qualitatively similar to that obtained by Berges and Rajagopal (1999) using an instanton-induced four-fermion interaction model. The work enables further study of the quark matter phase diagram, particularly the mixed phase region where chiral symmetry is restored and matter becomes superconducting, with possible connections to bag model descriptions of nucleons.
