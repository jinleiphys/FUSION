# Can one see the number of colors in eta, eta-prime --> pi^+ pi^- gamma?

> We investigate the decays eta, eta-prime --> pi^+ pi^- gamma up to next-to-leading order in the framework of the combined 1/N_c and chiral expansions. Counter terms of unnatural parity at next-to-leading order with unknown couplings are important to acommodate the results both to the experimental decay width and the photon spectrum. The presence of these coefficients does not allow for a determination of the number of colors from these decays.

## Key claim

The decays η, η' → π⁺ π⁻ γ cannot be used to determine the number of colors N_c, because next-to-leading order counter terms with unknown couplings are essential to fit both the experimental decay width and photon spectrum, and these couplings can absorb the N_c dependence.

## Method

The authors performed a combined 1/N_c and chiral expansion up to next-to-leading order (NLO) for the decays η, η' → π⁺ π⁻ γ. They included the Wess-Zumino-Witten term at leading order, then added gauge-invariant unnatural-parity counter terms at NLO (a p⁴ term with coupling L̃₁ and p⁶ terms with couplings K̃₁–K̃₄), together with η-η' mixing and meson wave-function renormalization. The unknown counter-term coefficients were fitted to the experimental η decay width and photon spectrum for different assumed values of N_c.

## Key numbers

- Experimental decay width Γ(η → π⁺ π⁻ γ) = 56.1 ± 5.4 eV (from PDG)
- Experimental decay width Γ(η' → π⁺ π⁻ γ) = 59.6 ± 5.2 keV (from PDG)
- At leading order without mixing, fitting the η decay gives N_c = 7; fitting the η' decay gives N_c = 10
- At leading order with η-η' mixing, the experimental values allow N_c = 4 or N_c = 5, ruling out N_c = 3
- For N_c = 3 at NLO, a perfect fit to the η decay width and photon spectrum is obtained with fitted parameters β_η₈^(1) = 1.3 × 10⁻³ and β_η₈^(0) = 28.4 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻²
- For N_c = 2 at NLO, an equally good fit is achieved with β_η₈^(1) = −3.2 × 10⁻³ and β_η₈^(0) = 22.0 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the discussion of whether anomalous decays can confirm N_c = 3, following the observation by Abdel-Rehim and Black (2004) and Black and Wise (2004) that the π⁰ → γγ decay cannot support N_c = 3 due to cancellation by a Goldstone-Wilczek term. The authors show that η, η' → π⁺ π⁻ γ also fail to determine N_c, contradicting the tree-level suggestion by Black and Wise (2004) that the η decay width is proportional to N_c² and could replace π⁰ → γγ as a textbook example. The results enable future next-to-next-to-leading order calculations (including one-loop corrections) to test whether N_c = 5 could be brought into agreement with experiment.
