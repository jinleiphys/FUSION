# Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass term for the Davidson potential

> Analytical expressions for spectra and wave functions are derived for a Bohr Hamiltonian, describing the collective motion of deformed nuclei, in which the mass is allowed to depend on the nuclear deformation. Solutions are obtained for separable potentials consisting of a Davidson potential in the beta variable, in the cases of gamma-unstable nuclei, axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, and triaxial nuclei, implementing the usual approximations in each case. The solution, called the Deformation Dependent Mass (DDM) Davidson model, is achieved by using techniques of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM), involving a deformed shape invariance condition. Spectra and B(E2) transition rates are compared to experimental data. The dependence of the mass on the deformation, dictated by SUSYQM for the potential used, reduces the rate of increase of the moment of inertia with deformation, removing a main drawback of the model.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical solutions for the Bohr Hamiltonian with a deformation-dependent mass of the form \(B(\beta)=B_0/(1+a\beta^2)^2\) for the Davidson potential, using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) techniques; the mass dependence on deformation is not arbitrary but is dictated by the deformed shape invariance condition required for exact solvability, and the model reduces the rate of increase of the moment of inertia with deformation compared to the constant-mass case.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr Hamiltonian and introduce a position-dependent mass term \(B(\beta)=B_0/(1+a\beta^2)^2\) using the von Roos two-parameter formalism. They consider separable potentials consisting of a Davidson potential \(u(\beta)=\beta^2 + \beta_0^4/\beta^2\) in the \(\beta\) variable, treating three cases: \(\gamma\)-unstable nuclei, axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, and triaxial nuclei with \(\gamma=\pi/6\). Exact separation of variables is achieved in each case, and the resulting radial equation is solved by imposing a deformed shape invariance condition within the SUSYQM framework, yielding analytical expressions for energy spectra and wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The deformation-dependent mass is taken as \(B(\beta)=B_0/(1+a\beta^2)^2\), with \(a \ll 1\).
* The Davidson potential is \(u(\beta)=\beta^2 + \beta_0^4/\beta^2\), where \(\beta_0\) indicates the position of the minimum.
* For \(\gamma\)-unstable nuclei, the angular eigenvalue is \(\Lambda = \tau(\tau+3)\), with \(\tau\) the seniority quantum number; within the ground state band \(L=2\tau\).
* For axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, \(\tilde\Lambda = [L(L+1)-K^2]/3 + (6c)(n_\gamma+1)\), with \(n_\gamma=0,1,2,\dots\).
* For triaxial nuclei, \(\bar\Lambda = [L(L+4)+3 n_w(2L-n_w)]/4 + \sqrt{2c}(n_\gamma+1/2)\), where \(n_w = L-\alpha\) is the wobbling quantum number.
* The coefficients in the effective radial potential are \(k_1 = 2 + a^2[5(1-\delta-\lambda)+(1-2\delta)(1-2\lambda)+6+\Lambda]\), \(k_0 = a[5(1-\delta-\lambda)+8+2\Lambda]\), and \(k_{-1} = 2+\Lambda+2\beta_0^4\).

## Context

This work extends the Bohr Hamiltonian framework by allowing the mass parameter to depend on deformation, addressing a known discrepancy where constant-mass models predict moments of inertia increasing proportionally to \(\beta^2\) while experimental data show a more moderate increase, as noted by Ring and others. The approach builds on earlier numerical solutions of a generalized Bohr Hamiltonian by Kumar and by Libert, but provides analytical solutions using SUSYQM techniques, keeping the number of free parameters small (two or three). The results enable analytical comparisons to experimental spectra and \(B(E2)\) transition rates for \(\gamma\)-unstable and axially symmetric deformed nuclei, and can be extended to triaxial nuclei as well.
