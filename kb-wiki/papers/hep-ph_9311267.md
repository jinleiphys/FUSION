# Longevity of the quark-gluon plasma and the mixed phase from intensity interferometry of high energy photons

> Two-photon intensity interferometry is shown to provide an accurate measurement of lifetime of quark-gluon plasma created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions via the difference of outward and sidewardcorrelation radii. Under the assumption of a longitudinal, boost invariant expansion of the plasma, we obtain analytical expressions for the correlations from the quark-gluon plasma phase. A $3+1$ dimensional expansion of the plasma along with a first order phase transition to hadrons is next considered, and, leads to a source with two characteristic lifetimes, one for the quark-gluon plasma phase, and the other for the longer lived mixed phase. This may even help us to {\em experimentally} determine the order of the phase transition.

## Key claim
Two-photon intensity interferometry can measure the lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma and the mixed phase separately via the difference of outward and sideward correlation radii, potentially allowing experimental determination of the order of the phase transition. The method relies on the assumption that high-energy photons are produced throughout the space-time evolution and suffer essentially no interactions with the surrounding medium.

## Method
The authors derived analytical expressions for the two-photon correlation function under Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, approximating the source time dependence as exponential with a time scale τ₀. They then performed full 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic calculations with a first-order phase transition for lead-lead collisions at RHIC energies, extracting outward and sideward correlation radii from the computed correlation functions for photons with transverse momenta around 1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T_i = 532 MeV at proper time τ_i = 0.124 fm/c for RHIC lead-lead collisions, with critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV and freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV
- Sideward correlation radius R_side = 8.6 fm from the 3+1 dimensional calculation
- Two outward correlation radii extracted: R_out = 22 fm and R_out = 9.8 fm
- Two time scales derived from the radii difference: τ₀ ≈ 10 fm/c (mixed phase) and τ₀ ≈ 2.4 fm/c (QGP phase)
- Photon transverse momenta of 1, 2, and 3 GeV were analyzed, with similar results across these values

## Context
This work extends earlier photon interferometry studies by Srivastava and collaborators (1992-1993) and builds on Bjorken hydrodynamics (Bjorken 1983) and 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic models (Jane 1993). The two-scale behavior in outward correlations had appeared in earlier calculations but its significance was not fully realized. The approach contrasts with pion interferometry from the NA35 collaboration, which found R_out - R_side consistent with zero, indicating pions do not probe the early evolution. The method could distinguish first-order from second-order phase transitions, since a second-order transition would lack a mixed phase.
