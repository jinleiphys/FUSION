# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions and the Nucleon Vector Current

> Using flavor twisted boundary conditions, we study nucleon matrix elements of the vector current. We twist only the active quarks that couple to the current. Finite volume corrections due to twisted boundary conditions are determined using partially twisted, partially quenched, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, which we develop for the graded group SU(7|5). Asymptotically these corrections are exponentially small in the volume, but can become pronounced for small twist angles. Utilizing the Breit frame does not mitigate volume corrections to nucleon vector current matrix elements. The derived expressions will allow for better controlled extractions of the isovector magnetic moment and the electromagnetic radii from simulations at zero lattice momentum. Our formalism, moreover, can be applied to any nucleon matrix elements.

## Key claim

Using flavor twisted boundary conditions on only the active quarks that couple to the vector current, the finite volume corrections to nucleon form factors can be sizable especially for the magnetic contribution and for small twist angles, and the Breit frame does not reduce these corrections; the corrections depend on an unphysical parameter \(g_1\) that must be determined from lattice data or eliminated by using isospin-twisted boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors develop a partially twisted, partially quenched heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for the graded group \(SU(7|5)\) to incorporate flavor twisted boundary conditions on the active valence quarks in the baryon sector. They compute finite volume corrections to the nucleon mass and to the vector current form factors (Sachs electric and magnetic form factors) for both rest frame and Breit frame kinematics, using mode sums over discrete lattice momenta shifted by the twist angles. The formalism is applied to the connected contributions of the electromagnetic current, allowing access to isovector quantities but not isoscalar.

## Key numbers

- The smallest available lattice momentum with periodic boundary conditions is about \(400-500\ \text{MeV}\) on typical lattices.
- The chiral limit value of the pion decay constant is \(f = 0.13\ \text{GeV}\).
- The nucleon mass splittings induced by twisted boundary conditions are estimated to be less than five percent for a lattice pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.25\ \text{GeV}\) and maximal twist angles \(\bm{\theta} = \pi(1,1,1)\), using \(g_A = 1.25\), \(g_{\Delta N} = 1.5\), \(\Delta = 0.29\ \text{GeV}\), \(M_N = 0.94\ \text{GeV}\), and varying \(g_1\) in the range \(-2 \leq g_1 \leq 2\).

## Context

This work extends the use of flavor twisted boundary conditions from meson systems (Jiang 2008) to nucleon vector current matrix elements, and is qualitatively similar to the isospin-twisted boundary conditions approach for nucleon isovector form factors (Tiburzi 2006). The derived expressions enable better controlled extractions of the isovector magnetic moment and electromagnetic radii from lattice simulations at zero lattice momentum, and the formalism can be applied to any nucleon matrix elements. The method relies on partially twisted boundary conditions where only valence quarks are twisted, avoiding the need for new gauge configurations.
