# Resonant relativistic corrections and the A_y problem

> We study relativistic corrections to nuclear interactions caused by boosting the two-nucleon interaction to a frame in which their total momentum does not vanish. These corrections induce a change in the computed value of the neutron-deuteron analyzing power A_y that is estimated using the plane-wave impulse approximation. This allows a transparent analytical calculation that demonstrates the significance of relativistic corrections. Faddeev calculations are however needed to conclude on the A_y puzzle.

# Resonant relativistic corrections and the A_y problem

## Key claim

Relativistic boost corrections to the two-nucleon interaction, specifically spin-violating terms at order (Q/m)^2, can produce a resonant enhancement of the neutron-deuteron analyzing power A_y at low energies (E_n ≈ 3 MeV) due to interference between large S-wave scattering lengths and ^3P_j waves, but a full Faddeev calculation including distortions is needed to determine whether this resolves the A_y puzzle.

## Method

The authors compute relativistic boost corrections to the two-nucleon interaction using the Poincaré group commutation relations formalism of Ref. [rel1,rel2,rel3] to leading order in (Q/m)^2, keeping only central parts of the NN interaction. They evaluate the spin-violating contributions to the nd scattering amplitude using the plane-wave impulse approximation, with the NN interaction described by leading-order pionless effective field theory contact interactions and benchmarked nd phase shifts from Kievsky et al. (2000, 2001) for the unperturbed scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The nd analyzing power A_y at E_n = 3 MeV increases by approximately 10% at the peak (θ ≈ 100°) when spin-violating relativistic boost corrections are included, based on the plane-wave impulse approximation estimate.
- The S-wave scattering lengths used are a_0 = a_{^1S_0} = -23.768 ± 0.006 fm and a_1 = a_{^3S_1} = 5.420 ± 0.001 fm from Ref. [as].
- The relevant average momentum from prescattering contributions is estimated as κ ≈ 0.6 fm^{-1}, about three times larger than |q + q'|/4 ≈ 0.1 fm^{-1} for E_n = 3 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy is E_d = -2.22 MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing A_y puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering, where all modern NN interactions under-predict A_y by 30% for laboratory energies E_N ≲ 30 MeV (Gloeckle, 1996), while existing three-nucleon interactions give small contributions at low energies (Kievsky, 1997; chiral 3NF). The authors extend the relativistic boost correction formalism of Ref. [rel1,rel2,rel3] to spin observables, showing that contrary to naive expectations these corrections can be significant at low energies due to resonant enhancement from large S-wave scattering lengths. The results motivate future Faddeev calculations including distortions to fully assess whether relativistic effects resolve the puzzle, and the approach can be extended to larger systems such as n-^3He scattering where the discrepancy reaches 100% (Fisher et al., 2006).
