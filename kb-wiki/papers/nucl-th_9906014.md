# Neutron charge radius and the Dirac equation

> We consider the Dirac equation for a finite-size neutron in an external electric field. We explicitly incorporate Dirac-Pauli form factors into the Dirac equation. After a non-relativistic reduction, the Darwin-Foldy term is cancelled by a contribution from the Dirac form factor, so that the only coefficient of the external field charge density is $e/6 r^2_{En}$, i. e. the root mean square radius associated with the electric Sachs form factor . Our result is similar to a recent result of Isgur, and reconciles two apparently conflicting viewpoints about the use of the Dirac equation for the description of nucleons.

# Neutron charge radius and the Dirac equation

## Key claim

The Darwin-Foldy term in the non-relativistic reduction of the Dirac equation for a finite-size neutron is cancelled by a contribution from the Dirac form factor, leaving only the root mean square radius associated with the electric Sachs form factor as the coefficient of the external field charge density. This result is independent of any specific quark substructure model and follows solely from the assumption that the neutron is described by the Dirac equation with form factors describing its non-zero size.

## Method

The authors incorporated Dirac-Pauli form factors F₁(t) and F₂(t) into the Dirac Hamiltonian for a neutron in an external electric field, then performed a non-relativistic reduction using both a squaring procedure (following Bethe and Salpeter) and a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation. They expanded the form factors at low momentum transfer and used the relation between the Dirac form factor slope and the neutron charge radius to show that the Foldy term (proportional to κ/4m²) cancels against a contribution from F₁'(0), leaving only the Sachs charge radius r²_En.

## Key numbers

- The neutron charge radius r²_En is defined through G_E(-q²) = -1/6 r²_En q² + ... for the neutron, with the analogous expression G_E(-q²) = 1 - 1/6 r²_Ep q² + ... for the proton.
- The relation F₁'(0) = -1/6 r²_En + κ/4m² connects the Dirac form factor slope to the neutron charge radius and the anomalous magnetic moment κ.
- After cancellation, the coefficient of the external field charge density in the non-relativistic Hamiltonian is e/6 r²_En.

## Context

This work reconciles two conflicting viewpoints in the literature about the use of the Dirac equation for nucleons, specifically the dispute between Byrne (who accepted the Foldy term) and Alex (who denied it) regarding consistency with chiral bag models. The result is similar to a recent result of Isgar from the valence quark model, but the authors show it follows from any form factor description without reference to internal structure. The analysis agrees with the low-energy Compton scattering analysis of L'vov and extends the authors' previous work on neutron polarizability (Bawin and Coon, 1997; Musinn, 1998).
