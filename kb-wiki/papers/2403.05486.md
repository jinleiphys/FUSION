# Confronting the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and the $N/D$ method for coupled-wave separable potentials

> We study a family of separable potentials with and without added contact interactions by solving the associated Lippmann-Schwinger equation with two coupled partial waves. The matching of the resulting amplitude matrix with the effective-range expansion is studied in detail. When a counterterm is included in the potential we also carefully discuss its renormalization. Next, we use the matrix $N/D$ method and study whether the amplitude matrices from the potentials considered admit an $N/D$ representation in matrix form. As a novel result we show that it is typically not possible to find such matrix representation for the coupled partial-wave case. However, a separate $N/D$ representation for each coupled partial wave, a valid option known in the literature, is explicitly implemented and numerically solved in cases where the matrix $N/D$ method is unavailable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a wide family of regular separable potentials coupling two partial waves (specifically S- and D-waves), a matrix $N/D$ representation of the scattering amplitude is typically impossible to construct, contrary to what had been assumed in the literature; however, a separate $N/D$ representation for each coupled partial wave remains valid and can be implemented numerically.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly for a family of separable potentials with vertex functions $g_\ell(Q) = (Q-L)^{1/4} Q^{\ell/2} / (Q+\Lambda_0^2)^{1+\ell/2}$, where $\ell = 0,2$ and $L \leq 0$ sets the left-hand cut onset. They then attempt to construct a matrix $N/D$ representation $\boldsymbol{T} = \boldsymbol{D}^{-1}\boldsymbol{N}$ for the resulting on-shell amplitude, examining two normalization choices ($\boldsymbol{N}(0)=\boldsymbol{1}$ and $\boldsymbol{D}(0)=\boldsymbol{1}$). When the matrix $N/D$ method fails, they implement the alternative approach of applying the $N/D$ method to each partial-wave amplitude separately, solving the resulting dispersive integral equations iteratively until convergence.

## Key numbers

- The loop integral $I_\ell(A)$ is evaluated analytically, with coefficients $\mathfrak{a}_{\ell m}$ and $\mathfrak{b}_{\ell m}$ given explicitly for $\ell=0$ (e.g., $\mathfrak{a}_{00} = -1$, $\mathfrak{a}_{01} = -1$) and $\ell=2$ (e.g., $\mathfrak{a}_{20} = -\frac{1}{3}-\frac{7}{12}x-\frac{1}{8}x^2$, where $x \equiv L/\Lambda_0^2$ with $-1 < x \leq 0$)
- The scattering-length matrix elements are expressed in terms of potential parameters, e.g., $a_{00} = \frac{\sqrt{-x}}{4\pi \Lambda_0^3} \left[ \mathfrak{c}_0^{[0]} - \frac{1-\mathfrak{c}_2^{[0]}\gamma_{22}}{\gamma_{00} - |\boldsymbol{\gamma}| \mathfrak{c}_2^{[0]}} \right]^{-1}$
- The effective-range matrix elements include $r_{02} = -\frac{4\pi \Lambda_0^3}{\sqrt{-x}} \left(6+\frac{1}{x}\right) \frac{\gamma_{02}}{|\boldsymbol{\gamma}|} = r_{20}$
- The matrix $\boldsymbol{D}(0)$ for the $\boldsymbol{N}(0)=\boldsymbol{1}$ normalization is non-invertible: $\boldsymbol{D}(0) = \Lambda_0^8 \left[ \frac{\gamma_{00}}{|\boldsymbol{\gamma}|} - \mathfrak{c}_2^{[0]} \right] \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$

## Context

This work extends the exact $N/D$ method developed by Entem, Oller, and collaborators (2016, 2018) for uncoupled partial waves to the coupled-wave case, where previous efforts (Oller, 2018) had failed to generate extra solutions beyond those from the regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The finding that matrix $N/D$ representations are typically unavailable for coupled-wave separable potentials explains this failure and motivates the use of separate $N/D$ representations for each partial wave, a method previously employed by Albaladejo (2012), Guo (2013), and Oller (2014) for chiral potentials. This enables future dispersive studies of coupled-channel systems where the matrix $N/D$ method cannot be applied.
