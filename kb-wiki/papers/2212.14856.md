# Effect of color superconductivity on the mass of hybrid neutron stars in an effective model with pQCD asymptotics

> The effective cold quark matter model by Alford, Braby, Paris and Reddy (ABPR) is used as a tool for discussing the effect of the size of the pairing gap in three-flavor (CFL) quark matter on the maximum mass of hybrid neutron stars (NSs). This equation of state (EOS) has three parameters which we suggest to determine by comparison with a nonlocal NJL model of quark matter in the nonperturbative domain. We show that due to the momentum dependence of the pairing which is induced by the nonlocality of the interaction, the effective gap parameter in the EOS model is well approximated by a constant value depending on the diquark coupling strength in the NJL model Lagrangian. For the parameter $a_4=1-2α_s/π$ a constant value below about \num{0.4} is needed to explain hybrid stars with ${\rm M}_{\rm max} \gtrsim 2.0~{\rm M}_\odot$, which would translate to an effective constant $α_s\sim 1$. The matching point with a running coupling at the 1-loop $β$ function level is found to lie outside the range of chemical potentials accessible in NS interiors. A dictionary is provided for translating the free parameters of the nlNJL model to those of the ABPR model. Both models are shown to be equivalent in the nonperturbative domain but the latter one allows to quantify the transition to the asymptotic behaviour in accordance with perturbative QCD. We provide constraints on parameter sets that fulfill the $2~{\rm M}_\odot$ mass constraint for hybrid NSs, as well as the low tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 by a softening of the EOS on the hybrid NS branch with an early onset of deconfinement at ${\rm M}_{\rm onset}<1.4~{\rm M}_\odot$. We find that the effective constant pairing gap should be around 100 MeV but not exceed values of about 130 MeV because a further increase of the gap would entail a softening of the EOS and contradict the $2~{\rm M}_\odot$ mass constraint.

## Key claim
The effective constant pairing gap in the ABPR model, which approximates the momentum-dependent gap from a nonlocal NJL model, should be around 100 MeV but must not exceed about 130 MeV; a larger gap would soften the equation of state and violate the 2 solar mass maximum mass constraint for hybrid neutron stars.

## Method
The authors use the three-parameter ABPR model for color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter and determine its parameters by fitting to the pressure and diquark gap of a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model in the nonperturbative domain. They provide a dictionary translating the nlNJL diquark and vector coupling strengths ($\eta_D$, $\eta_V$) to the ABPR parameters (effective gap $\Delta^*$, bag pressure $B$). The resulting hybrid neutron star equations of state are then tested against the $2~{\rm M}_\odot$ mass constraint and the low tidal deformability constraint from GW170817.

## Key numbers
- The effective constant pairing gap $\Delta^*$ should be around 100 MeV but not exceed about 130 MeV.
- For the parameter $a_4 = 1 - 2\alpha_s/\pi$, a constant value below about 0.4 is needed to explain hybrid stars with ${\rm M}_{\rm max} \gtrsim 2.0~{\rm M}_\odot$, translating to an effective constant $\alpha_s \sim 1$.
- The matching point between a constant $\alpha_s$ and a running 1-loop coupling is at $\mu = 635$ MeV.
- For the nlNJL model, the critical diquark coupling for vacuum stability is $\eta_D^* = 0.761$, and the analysis is limited to $\eta_D \le 0.75$.
- The average effective gap $\Delta^*$ is fitted as $\Delta^*[{\rm MeV}] = 77.65 - 521\, \eta_D + 1233.3\, \eta_D^2$.
- The bag pressure $B$ is fitted as $B[{\rm MeV/fm}^3] = 288 - 1070\, \eta_D + 1400\, \eta_D^2$.
- For parameter set I a ($\eta_D=0.45$, $\eta_V=1.5$), the maximum mass is $2.19~{\rm M}_\odot$ and the onset mass is $1.37~{\rm M}_\odot$.
- For parameter set II a ($\eta_D=0.5$, $\eta_V=1.25$), the maximum mass is $2.03~{\rm M}_\odot$ and the onset mass is $1.37~{\rm M}_\odot$.
- For parameter set III a ($\eta_D=0.6$, $\eta_V=0.75$), the maximum mass is only $1.64~{\rm M}_\odot$, failing the $2~{\rm M}_\odot$ constraint.

## Context
This work extends the effective ABPR model (Alford, Braby, Paris, and Reddy, 2004) by linking its free parameters to the microscopic couplings of a nonlocal NJL model (Contrera et al., 2022). It provides a direct mapping between the phenomenological bag pressure and pairing gap and the underlying diquark and vector coupling strengths. This enables future studies to use the simple ABPR form while being anchored to a more fundamental chiral quark model, allowing for systematic scans of hybrid neutron star phenomenology constrained by multi-messenger observations like GW170817.
