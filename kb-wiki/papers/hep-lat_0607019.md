# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions and Isovector Form Factors

> We use vector flavor symmetry to relate form factors of isospin changing operators to isovector form factors. Flavor twisted boundary conditions in lattice QCD thus allow isovector form factors of twist-two operators, e.g, to be computed at continuous values of the momentum transfer. These twisted boundary conditions, moreover, are implemented only in the valence sector. Effects of the finite volume must be addressed to extract isovector moments and radii at zero lattice momentum. As an example, we use chiral perturbation theory to assess the volume effects in extracting the isovector magnetic moment of the nucleon from simulations with twisted boundary conditions.

## Key claim

Flavor twisted boundary conditions, implemented only in the valence sector, allow isovector form factors of twist-two operators to be computed at continuous values of the momentum transfer, but the finite volume effects must be addressed to extract isovector moments and radii at zero lattice momentum.

## Method

The author uses vector flavor symmetry SU(2)_V to relate matrix elements of isospin-changing operators to isovector matrix elements, then applies partially twisted boundary conditions in lattice QCD where only valence quarks are twisted while sea quarks remain periodic. Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) is employed at finite volume to assess the volume effects in extracting the isovector magnetic moment of the nucleon from simulations with twisted boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The lowest non-zero momentum mode on current dynamical lattices is approximately 500 MeV.
- For lattice pion masses around 0.25 to 0.35 GeV, there is a 5-10% deviation from linear q^2 behavior in the isovector magnetic form factor.
- At a pion mass of 0.35 GeV, the chiral expansion receives approximately 30% corrections from higher-order terms scaling as m_pi / Lambda_chi.
- Beyond q^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2, there are approximately 30% corrections from recoil q^2 / M^2 terms in loop graphs.
- In a 2.5 fm box at twist angle theta = pi and physical pion mass, the volume-induced isospin splittings are approximately 15% for pions and approximately 5% for nucleons.
- When the pion mass is twice as large, the isospin splittings are approximately 1% for both pions and nucleons.
- The momentum transfer from twisting is B = theta x 0.079 GeV for the plotted finite volume effects.

## Context

This work extends the use of twisted boundary conditions for flavor-changing operators (Tiburzi 2005) to isovector form factors via SU(2)_V symmetry relations, building on earlier twisted boundary condition studies by Bedaque (2004), de Divitiis (2004), and Sachrajda (2004). The finite volume corrections calculated here for the isovector magnetic moment using twisted boundary conditions are compared with the results of Beane (2004), who derived finite volume effects under the assumption of momentum extrapolation or background magnetic fields. This enables lattice practitioners to extract isovector moments and radii without model-dependent assumptions about momentum dependence, using controlled EFT corrections rather than ad hoc fitting functions.
