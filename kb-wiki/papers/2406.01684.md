# Color Glass Condensate meets High Twist Expansion

> We establish the correspondence between two well-known frameworks for QCD multiple scattering in nuclear media: the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and the High-Twist (HT) expansion formalism. We argue that a consistent matching between both frameworks, in their common domain of validity, is achieved by incorporating the sub-eikonal longitudinal momentum phase in the CGC formalism, which mediates the transition between coherent and incoherent scattering. We perform a detailed calculation and analysis of direct photon production in proton-nucleus scattering as a concrete example to establish the matching between HT and CGC up to twist-4, including initial- and final-state interactions, as well as their interferences. The techniques developed in this work can be adapted to other processes in electron-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions, and they provide a potential avenue for a unified picture of dilute-dense dynamics in nuclear media.

## Key claim

The authors establish a formal correspondence between the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and High-Twist (HT) expansion formalisms for QCD multiple scattering in nuclear media, but only after incorporating sub-eikonal longitudinal momentum phases into the CGC framework; without these phases, the naive twist expansion of CGC results does not match the HT expansion, demonstrating that the collinear and eikonal limits do not commute.

## Method

The authors performed a detailed calculation of direct photon production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions as a concrete test case, computing the cross-section at twist-4 level in the HT expansion formalism including initial-state, final-state, and interference contributions. They then computed the same process in the CGC formalism, first using the standard eikonal approximation and then with sub-eikonal phases added, and expanded both CGC results to twist-4 order to compare with the HT expansion. The matching required the inclusion of longitudinal sub-eikonal phases in the CGC to mediate the transition between coherent and incoherent scattering, corresponding to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effect.

## Key numbers

- The twist-4 contribution is suppressed by an additional power of \(1/p_{\gamma\perp}^2\) relative to the leading twist, where \(p_{\gamma\perp}\) is the transverse momentum of the produced photon, but enhanced by a factor of \(A^{1/3}\) for large nuclei.
- The partonic Mandelstam variables are defined as \(\hat{s} = (x_q P_p + x_g P_A)^2\), \(\hat{t} = (x_q P_p - p_\gamma)^2\), \(\hat{u} = (x_g P_A - p_\gamma)^2\).
- The leading twist cross-section for direct photon production is proportional to \(\xi^2[1+(1-\xi)^2]/p_{\gamma\perp}^4\), where \(\xi\) is the longitudinal momentum fraction of the incoming quark carried by the photon.
- The on-shell condition for the initial-state central cut diagram gives \(\delta(x_1 + x_2 - x - x_c)\), where \(x_c = (x/-\hat{t})(2p_\gamma \cdot l_T - l_T^2)\) and \(x = p_{\gamma\perp}^2/[\xi(1-\xi)x_q s]\).
- The color factor for the double scattering contribution is \(C = 1/(4N_c^2)\), where \(N_c\) is the number of colors.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to connect the CGC and collinear factorization frameworks, including sub-eikonal corrections to parton propagators by Altinoluk (2014-2022) and Chirilli (2018-2021), and rapidity evolution studies by Balitsky (2015-2019). The paper provides a unified picture of dilute-dense dynamics in nuclear media that can be adapted to other processes in electron-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions, and it clarifies the role of the LPM interference effect in mediating the transition between coherent CGC scattering and incoherent HT scattering.
