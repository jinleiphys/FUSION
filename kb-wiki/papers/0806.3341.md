# Heavy Flavor in the sQGP

> We attempt a unified treatment of heavy quarkonia and heavy-quark diffusion in the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Our approach is based on finite-temperature T-matrices with interaction potentials estimated from the heavy-quark internal energy computed in thermal lattice QCD (lQCD). In the charmonium sector S-wave bound states (J/ψ, η_c) survive up to temperatures of ~2 T_c, not inconsistent with constraints from euclidean correlation functions in lQCD. In the open-heavy flavor sector, the T-matrix interaction reduces heavy-quark diffusion substantially, leading to fair agreement with single-electron spectra at RHIC and suggestive for a small viscosity-to-entropy ratio close to T_c.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified T-matrix approach, using interaction potentials estimated from the heavy-quark internal energy computed in thermal lattice QCD, can simultaneously describe charmonium bound states surviving up to approximately 2 T_c and heavy-quark diffusion coefficients small enough to reproduce single-electron spectra at RHIC, but the precise relation between the heavy-quark potential and the free energy computed in lattice QCD remains an open problem.

## Method

The authors solve a two-body scattering equation (ladder resummation) for the in-medium T-matrix of heavy quark-antiquark pairs and heavy-light quark scattering, using the heavy-quark internal energy extracted from thermal lattice QCD as the interaction potential. For charmonia, they compute spectral functions and Euclidean correlators from the T-matrix, while for open heavy flavor they compute thermal relaxation rates and implement them into relativistic Langevin simulations of Au-Au collisions at RHIC, hadronizing the heavy quarks via a combined coalescence and fragmentation framework.

## Key numbers

- S-wave charmonium bound states (J/psi, eta_c) survive up to temperatures of approximately 2 T_c
- Different extractions of the heavy-quark internal energy from lattice QCD imply up to approximately 40 percent uncertainty in the potential
- Charm- and bottom-quark thermalization times are reduced by a factor of approximately 3 compared to elastic perturbative QCD scattering when using effective meson resonance exchange
- Heavy-light T-matrices exhibit resonance-like D-meson correlations up to temperatures of approximately 1.5 T_c and diquark correlations up to approximately 1.2 T_c
- Uncertainties in heavy-quark spectra due to different extractions of the lattice QCD internal energy amount to approximately 30 percent
- The crossing point of charm and bottom contributions to single-electron spectra is at approximately p_t^e = 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the effective resonance model of van Hees and Rapp (2004, 2005) by replacing phenomenological meson-exchange interactions with a T-matrix approach using potentials from thermal lattice QCD, consistent with the lattice QCD correlator studies of Datta (2003) and Morrin (2005). The results enable a unified description of both quarkonium binding and heavy-quark diffusion, and provide estimates of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio near T_c that are suggestive of a strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma, with open problems including a proper definition of the in-medium potential and corrections from radiative processes.
