# Electrically charged compact stars

> We review here the classical argument used to justify the electrical neutrality of stars and show that if the pressure and density of the matter and gravitational field inside the star are large, then a charge and a strong electric field can be present. For a neutron star with high pressure (~ 10^{33} to 10^{35} dynes /cm^2) and strong gravitational field (~ 10^{14} cm/s^2), these conditions are satisfied. The hydrostatic equation which arises from general relativity, is modified considerably to meet the requirements of the inclusion of the charge. In order to see any appreciable effect on the phenomenology of the neutron stars, the charge and the electrical fields have to be huge (~ 10^{21} Volts/cm). These stars are not however stable from the viewpoint that each charged particle is unbound to the uncharged particles, and thus the system collapses one step further to a charged black hole

# Electrically charged compact stars

## Key claim
The paper argues that while stars are classically assumed to be electrically neutral, neutron stars with high internal pressure (~10^33 to 10^35 dynes/cm^2) and strong gravitational fields (~10^14 cm/s^2) could in principle hold a net charge and produce electric fields up to ~10^21 V/cm, but such charged configurations are unstable because individual charged particles become unbound and the system collapses further to a charged black hole.

## Method
The authors modify the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation from general relativity to include the Maxwell-Einstein stress tensor, adding electrostatic field energy density and charge density terms. They solve this modified TOV equation for a polytropic equation of state with index n=1.5 and constant kappa=0.05 [fm]^(8/3), assuming the charge density is proportional to the mass density via a charge fraction parameter f (rho_ch = f * epsilon, where epsilon = rho c^2 in MeV/fm^3). Trapped protons are taken as the charge carriers, and the sign of the charge does not affect the formulation.

## Key numbers
- Neutron star internal pressure range: ~10^33 to 10^35 dynes/cm^2
- Gravitational field strength: ~10^14 cm/s^2
- Electric field required for appreciable phenomenological effect: ~10^21 V/cm
- Maximum allowed surface charge: Q = sqrt(G) * M_sun * (M/M_sun) ~ 10^20 Coulomb * (M/M_sun)
- For charge fraction f = 0.0001, no departure from chargeless stellar structure is observed
- For the maximum allowed charge fraction f = 0.0011, the total mass increases up to 12 times the value of the chargeless case
- Polytropic index n = 1.5 and kappa = 0.05 [fm]^(8/3) used for the equation of state

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of charged fluid spheres by Bekenstein (1971), Bonnor (1980), and Zhang et al. (1982), who indirectly verified that stellar structure is significantly affected by electric charge only when charge density approaches mass density. The paper builds on the Majumdar (1947) and Papapetrou (1947) treatments of charged dust in general relativity, and on the detailed formalism of Ray et al. (2003). The results suggest that charged neutron stars could serve as intermediate states between supernova collapse and charged black holes, though their short lifetime due to particle expulsion makes them observationally unlikely.
