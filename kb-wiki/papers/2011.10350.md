# Investigating the transverse momentum dependent gluon Sivers function in quarkonium production at $pp$ colliders

> In this contribution, we will present a short overview of the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) approach as a tool for studying the 3-dimensional structure of hadrons in high-energy (un)polarized hadron collisions. We will then summarize the present status of a running research programme that aims at constraining the poorly known transverse momentum dependent gluon Sivers function, through the study of single spin asymmetries in quarkonium (mainly $J/ψ$), pion, and $D$-meson production in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC. Finally, we will shortly discuss perspectives for this field of research, emphasizing in particular its role in the physics programme of LHC in the fixed-target setup and NICA.

## Key claim

The authors summarize an ongoing research programme that aims to constrain the poorly known transverse momentum dependent gluon Sivers function through phenomenological analysis of single spin asymmetries in quarkonium (mainly J/psi), pion, and D-meson production in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC, using the generalized parton model and its colour gauge invariant extension as a working Ansatz since no TMD factorization theorem has been proven for inclusive hadron production in hadronic collisions.

## Method

The authors adopt the generalized parton model (GPM), which assumes factorization as a phenomenological starting Ansatz and extends collinear QCD via inclusion of spin and parton intrinsic motion effects using the helicity formalism, together with its colour gauge invariant extension (CGI-GPM) that accounts for initial- and final-state interactions via single eikonalized gluon exchange. For quarkonium production, they combine the TMD approach with the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism, considering contributions from the 3S1(1,8), 1S0(8), and 3PJ(8) Q-Qbar states for J/psi production, and include both 2-to-1 and 2-to-2 partonic processes.

## Key numbers

- Single spin asymmetry A_N data for p^up p -> J/psi + X at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV over rapidity ranges 0.12 < |y| < 2.2 and |y| < 0.35 for transverse momenta up to 6 GeV (Adare 2010, Aidala 2018)
- Unpolarized cross section data for J/psi production in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for p_T up to 8.5 GeV (Adare 2009) and at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV for p_T up to 20 GeV (Adam 2019)
- A_N data for neutral pion production at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV in central rapidity region for p_T approximately 1-5 GeV (Adare 2013)
- A_N data for inclusive jet production at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for p_T approximately 10-30 GeV (Adamczyk 2012)
- A_N for muons from open heavy-flavour decays in polarized p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for -0.1 < x_F < 0.1 and p_T in range 1-5 GeV (Aidala 2017)

## Context

This work extends the TMD approach to gluon Sivers function studies, which have been poorly constrained because gluons decouple at leading order from SIDIS, Drell-Yan, and e+e- annihilation processes that form the pillars of TMD factorization. The analysis builds on prior phenomenological work using the GPM (Anselmino 2005) and CGI-GPM (Gamberg 2010, D'Alesio 2011) frameworks, and aims to exploit data from RHIC experiments (PHENIX and STAR) as well as future measurements at LHC fixed-target setup and NICA to provide unique constraints on the gluon Sivers function that are not accessible through other processes.
