# SU(N) Wigner-Racah algebra for the matrix of second moments of embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble of random matrices

> Recently Pluhar and Weidenmueller [Ann. Phys. (N.Y.) Vol 297, 344 (2002)] showed that the eigenvectors of the matrix of second moments of embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble of random matrices generated by k-body interactions (EGUE(k)) for m fermions in N single particle states are SU(N) Wigner coefficients and derived also an expression for the eigenvalues. Going beyond this work, we will show that the eigenvalues of this matrix are square of a SU(N) Racah coefficient and thus the matrix of second moments of EGUE(k) is solved completely by SU(N) Wigner-Racah algebra.

# SU(N) Wigner-Racah algebra for the matrix of second moments of embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble of random matrices

## Key claim

The eigenvalues of the matrix of second moments of the embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble generated by k-body interactions (EGUE(k)) for m fermions in N single particle states are squares of SU(N) Racah coefficients, not merely the expression derived by Pluhar and Weidenmueller (2002); this completes the solution of the matrix of second moments via SU(N) Wigner-Racah algebra.

## Method

The author starts from the Pluhar-Weidenmueller result that the eigenvectors of the covariance matrix are SU(N) Wigner (Clebsch-Gordan) coefficients. Using the decomposition of the k-body Hamiltonian into SU(N) irreducible tensors B_k(g_nu omega_nu), the reduced matrix elements of these tensors are expressed as SU(N) Racah coefficients via Eqs. (55-56) of Hecht (1975). A Racah transform (Eq. 20) and the Biedenharn-Elliott sum rule extended to SU(N) are then applied to convert the covariance expression into a generalized eigenvalue expansion, yielding eigenvalues that are explicitly squares of SU(N) Racah coefficients (Eq. 24). The eigenvalues are then evaluated in closed form using known formulas for the Racah coefficients, reproducing the Pluhar-Weidenmueller result (Eq. 26) as a special case.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalues of the matrix of second moments are E_mu = (lambda^2 / N_k) * Lambda^mu(k) for mu = 0, 1, ..., m-k, where Lambda^mu(k) = C(m-mu, k) * C(N-m+k-mu, k) (Eqs. 17, 26).
- The degeneracy of each eigenvalue E_mu is d(g_mu) = (N_nu)^2 - (N_{nu-1})^2 = (N!)^2 (N+1)(N-2nu+1) / [(nu!)^2 (N-nu+1!)^2] (Eq. 16).
- The ensemble-averaged variance of H in m-particle space is Lambda^0(k) (Eq. 27), in units of lambda^2/N_k.
- The excess parameter gamma_2 of the density of eigenvalues is given by Eq. 29: gamma_2 = [ (1/N_m) * sum_{nu=0}^{min(k,m-k)} Lambda^nu(m-k) Lambda^nu(k) d(nu) / (Lambda^0(k))^2 ] - 1.
- The variance of the distribution of centroids over the ensemble is hat{Sigma}_{11} = (1/N_m) * Lambda^0(m-k) / Lambda^0(k) (Eq. 31).
- The variance of the distribution of variances over the ensemble is hat{Sigma}_{22} = (2/(N_m)^2) * sum_{nu=0}^{m-k} [Lambda^nu(k)/Lambda^0(k)]^2 d(nu) (Eq. 33).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous analytical results of Pluhar and Weidenmueller (2002), who showed that the eigenvectors of the second-moment matrix are SU(N) Wigner coefficients and derived an eigenvalue expression using a duality relation. The present paper provides an alternative derivation showing the eigenvalues are squares of SU(N) Racah coefficients, thereby solving the matrix completely within SU(N) Wigner-Racah algebra. This approach enables explicit calculation of low-order moments (state density and bivariate moments) for EGUE(k), previously obtained by Benet et al. (2001) without using Racah coefficients. The results also translate to bosonic EGUE(k) via the N to -N symmetry, connecting to work by Asaga et al. (2002), and open the door to analyzing embedded ensembles with more general group symmetries, such as U(2N) containing U(N) x SU(2) for fermions with spin.
