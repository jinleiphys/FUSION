# From Microscales to Macroscales in 3D: Selfconsistent Equation of State for Supernova and Neutron Star Models

> First results from a fully self-consistent, temperature-dependent equation of state that spans the whole density range of neutron stars and supernova cores are presented. The equation of state (EoS) is calculated using a mean-field Hartree-Fock method in three dimensions (3D). The nuclear interaction is represented by the phenomenological Skyrme model in this work, but the EoS can be obtained in our framework for any suitable form of the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction. The scheme we employ naturally allows effects such as (i) neutron drip, which results in an external neutron gas, (ii) the variety of exotic nuclear shapes expected for extremely neutron heavy nuclei, and (iii) the subsequent dissolution of these nuclei into nuclear matter. In this way, the equation of state is calculated across phase transitions without recourse to interpolation techniques between density regimes described by different physical models. EoS tables are calculated in the wide range of densities, temperature and proton/neutron ratios on the ORNL NCCS XT3, using up to 2000 processors simultaneously.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully self-consistent, temperature-dependent equation of state for neutron stars and supernova cores calculated using a mean-field Hartree-Fock method in three dimensions, removing the constraint of spherical symmetry that limited all previous models. The key hedge is that the nuclear interaction is represented by the phenomenological Skyrme SkM* force in this work, but the framework can accommodate any suitable form of the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction.

## Method

The equation of state is constructed by modeling nuclear matter as an infinite sequence of cubic unit cells, each containing a certain number of neutrons and protons, and solving the Hartree-Fock equations in three dimensions using a variational principle. The calculation iteratively minimizes the total energy per particle starting from trial single-particle wave functions (Gaussian times polynomial, harmonic oscillator, or plane waves), using a combination of Imaginary Time Step and Damped Gradient Step iteration schemes to achieve convergence. The EoS tables are computed on the ORNL NCCS XT3 supercomputer using up to 2000 processors simultaneously, covering baryon number densities from 0.001 to 0.16 fm⁻³, temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV, and proton fractions from 0 to 0.5.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number density range covered: 0.001 fm⁻³ to 0.16 fm⁻³
- Temperature range covered: 0 to 10 MeV
- Proton fraction range covered: 0 to 0.5

- Lower density limit for spherical nucleus arrangement: n_b < 0.0001 fm⁻³
- Supercomputer used: ORNL NCCS XT3 with up to 2000 processors

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent Hartree-Fock equation of state calculations by Bonche and Vautherin (1981) and Hillebrant and Wolff (1985), which were limited to spherical symmetry, and by Magierski and Heenen (2002), which considered only zero temperature. The approach contrasts with the widely used non-relativistic EoS by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and the relativistic mean-field model by Shen et al (1998), which both assume spherical symmetry and use different models for different density and temperature regions requiring interpolation. The results enable future investigation of weak interaction processes such as neutrino transport and beta-decay in the self-consistently determined continuous medium, which may have profound consequences for macroscopic modeling of core-collapse supernovae and neutron stars.
