# Matrix elements of one-body and two-body operators between arbitrary HFB multi-quasiparticle states

> We present new formulae for the matrix elements of one-body and two-body physical operators in compact forms, which are applicable to arbitrary Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions, including those for multi-quasiparticle excitations. The test calculations show that our formulae may substantially accelerate the process of symmetry restoration when applied to the heavy nuclear system.

## Key claim

The paper presents new compact formulae for matrix elements of one-body and two-body operators between arbitrary Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) multi-quasiparticle states that may substantially reduce computational time by several orders of magnitude when applied to symmetry restoration in heavy nuclear systems, but the efficiency gain depends on the availability of precomputed intermediate quantities and the choice of interaction form.

## Method

The authors derive compact expressions for matrix elements of one-body and two-body operators between arbitrary HFB multi-quasiparticle states using Pfaffian algebra and a generalization of the Wick theorem. They express the matrix elements in terms of Pfaffians of skew-symmetric matrices constructed from contractions of single-fermion operators, and then apply Pfaffian expansion formulas and the Pfaffian version of the Lewis Carroll formula to obtain closed-form expressions that avoid the combinatorial explosion of the conventional generalized Wick theorem approach. The method handles both the case where the overlap between HFB vacua is nonzero and the special case of zero overlap (the Egido pole).

## Key numbers

* For a model space dimension M=80 and up to 6-quasiparticle states (2n=12), the ratio of CPU time for the conventional method to the new formula (Eq. ib4) can exceed 10^6.
* The computational cost of the new formula (Eq. ib4) scales as O((2n)^4), independent of the model space dimension M, while the conventional method scales as O(M^4(2n)^3).
* For M_I=100 (dimension of the matrix of matrix elements) and M=80, the global efficiency ratio r of the new method relative to the conventional method can be on the order of 10^5.
* The CPU time t_V for precomputing the intermediate quantities V_0, V^(1), and V^(2) is within several seconds for M ≤ 80 on a 2.4 GHz Intel CPU.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian-based approach for HFB overlaps developed by Robledo (2009), Bertsch and Robledo (2012), and Mizusaki and Oi (2012) to the calculation of operator matrix elements, which had not been previously treated in compact form. The new formulae enable post-HFB calculations such as symmetry restoration and configuration interaction for heavy nuclei in large Fock spaces, where conventional methods become intractable. The authors note that for very large model spaces, the method may still require separable interactions (as used in the Projected Shell Model) rather than full two-body interactions to keep the precomputation time manageable.
