# 3D Jet Tomography of Twisted Strongly Coupled Quark Gluon Plasmas

> The triangular enhancement of the rapidity distribution of hadrons produced in p+A reactions relative to p+p is a leading order in A^{1/3}/log(s) violation of longitudinal boost invariance at high energies. In A+A reactions this leads to a trapezoidal enhancement of the local rapidity density of produced gluons. The local rapidity gradient is proportional to the local participant number asymmetry, and leads to an effective rotation in the reaction plane. We propose that three dimensional jet tomography, correlating the long range rapidity and azimuthal dependences of the nuclear modification factor, R_{AA}(η,ϕ,p_\perp; b>0), can be used to look for this intrinsic longitudinal boost violating structure of $A+A$ collisions to image the produced twisted strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (sQGP). In addition to dipole and elliptic azimuthal moments of R_{AA}, a significant high p_\perp octupole moment is predicted away from midrapidity. The azimuthal angles of maximal opacity and hence minima of R_{AA} are rotated away from the normal to the reaction plane by an `Octupole Twist' angle, θ_3(η), at forward rapidities.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the intrinsic violation of longitudinal boost invariance in non-central A+A collisions, controlled by the parameter δ ≈ (A^(1/3)-1)/(2Y), creates a "twisted" initial sQGP density that can be imaged via three-dimensional jet tomography. The key hedge is that this effect relies on the assumption that at high enough p_T, initial-state nuclear effects like shadowing and Cronin enhancement can be neglected.

## Method
The authors model the initial sQGP density using a BGK-inspired trapezoidal rapidity distribution proportional to the local participant nucleon densities, with a Gaussian envelope to fit BRAHMS central Au+Au data. They compute the line-integrated opacity χ(η,φ,b) for jets produced at points distributed according to the binary collision number density, then convert opacity to a nuclear modification factor R_AA using a simple exponential survival probability e^(-κχ) calibrated to reproduce central R_AA ≈ 0.2. The azimuthal Fourier moments v_n of R_AA and the octupole twist angle θ_3 are extracted as functions of rapidity and impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- δ ≈ 0.45 for Au+Au at √s = 200 AGeV (Y ≈ 5, A^(1/3) ≈ 6)
- δ ≈ 0.28 for Pb+Pb at LHC √s = 5500 AGeV
- The central opacity for b=0 is χ_0 = 11/fm^2, with κ ≈ 0.25 used to fit central R_AA ≈ 0.2
- The octupole twist angle θ_3(η) reaches approximately 10-20 degrees at |η| ≈ 2-3 for b = 6-9 fm
- The predicted high p_T |v_1| peaks at η ≈ 2 with opposite sign relative to spectator v_1

## Context
This work extends 2D jet tomography (Gyulassy 2003) to full 3D by incorporating the rapidity dimension, building on the BGK trapezoidal model (Brodsky 1977) and HIJING phenomenology (Wang 1991) that successfully describe the triangular rapidity distributions observed in p+A and D+Au collisions at RHIC (PHOBOS 2004, BRAHMS data). The predicted octupole moment v_3 and the rapidity-anti-correlated rotation of R_AA minima provide a new observable that could be tested at a future RHIC II upgrade, complementing existing elliptic flow v_2 measurements (STAR, PHOBOS) and hydrodynamic calculations (Hirano 2001, 2004; Magas 2002) that have not yet reproduced the observed rapidity dependence of elliptic flow.
