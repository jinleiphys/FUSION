# The Extending for Composite Skyrme Model

> In this paper, we have extended the composite Skyrme model proposed by H. Y. Cheung, F. Gursey to some respects of theoretical physics such as supersymmetry, gravitation.

## Key claim

The paper extends the composite Skyrme model of Cheung and Gursey (1990) to include pion mass, supersymmetry, and gravitation, claiming that including the pion mass term improves predictions for static nucleon properties compared to the original massless version, though the authors acknowledge that the original model's predictions were "not really exact."

## Method

The authors introduce a pion mass term into the composite Skyrme Lagrangian density for general winding number n, then apply the hedgehog ansatz and collective coordinate quantization to obtain nucleon and delta masses. For the supersymmetric extension, they replace ordinary derivatives with covariant derivatives and extend the chiral fields to supermultiplets. For the gravitational extension, they couple the composite Skyrme model to Einstein gravity using a static spherically symmetric metric and derive the coupled field equations for the profile function f(r), metric function N(r), and mass function m(r).

## Key numbers

- For n=4, the pion mass parameter beta is set to 0.0025
- e = 2.87 (dimensionless parameter, no experimental value given)
- F_pi = 446.56 MeV (experimental: 186 MeV)
- Isoscalar charge radius sqrt(<r^2>_{E,I=0}) = 0.21 fm (experimental: 0.71 fm)
- Isovector charge radius sqrt(<r^2>_{E,I=1}) = 2.26 fm (experimental: 0.88 fm)
- Isoscalar magnetic radius sqrt(<r^2>_{M,I=0}) = 1.0 fm (experimental: 0.81 fm)
- Isovector magnetic radius sqrt(<r^2>_{M,I=1}) = 2.26 fm (experimental: 0.8 fm)
- Proton magnetic moment mu_pro = 1.64 mag (experimental: 2.79 mag)
- Neutron magnetic moment mu_neu = -1.57 mag (experimental: -1.91 mag)
- Ratio |mu_pro/mu_neu| = 1.04 (experimental: 1.46)
- Axial coupling g_A = 1.22 (experimental: 1.23)

## Context

This work extends the composite Skyrme model of Cheung and Gursey (1990) by adding a pion mass term, which was omitted in the original formulation due to spontaneous symmetry breaking. The authors compare their numerical results for static nucleon properties with those from Cheung and Gursey (1990), as well as earlier Skyrme model calculations, noting improvements in the axial coupling g_A (1.22 versus 1.3, 0.61, 0.65, and 1.27 from prior works) and neutron magnetic moment (-1.57 versus -1.17, -1.31, and -1.24). The supersymmetric extension builds on the four-dimensional supersymmetric nonlinear sigma model of Bergshoeff, Nepomachie, and Schnitzer (1984), while the gravitational coupling follows work on the Einstein-Skyrme system by several authors. The formalism is intended to enable calculation of gravitating skyrmion properties and potential applications in cosmology.
