# Transverse spin and transverse momenta in hard scattering processes

> Inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic leptoproduction offers possibilities to study details of the quark and gluon structure of hadrons. In many of these experiments polarization is an essential ingredient. We also emphasize the dependence on transverse momenta of the quarks, which leads to azimuthal asymmetries in the produced hadrons.

## Key claim

Inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic leptoproduction can reveal details of quark and gluon structure in hadrons, but the full picture requires including both polarization and the dependence on transverse momenta of quarks, which generates azimuthal asymmetries in the produced hadrons that are not captured by collinear approximations.

## Method

The author performs a tree-level QCD calculation of hard scattering amplitudes for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), separating the process into hard and soft parts. The soft parts are parametrized as quark-quark correlation functions (distribution functions for the target hadron and fragmentation functions for the detected hadron) including their dependence on transverse momentum, and the analysis is extended to subleading order in 1/Q by including quark-quark-gluon correlation functions. The resulting cross sections are expressed in terms of twist-two and twist-three distribution and fragmentation functions, with Gaussian transverse momentum dependence assumed for the quark distributions and fragmentation functions to evaluate convolutions.

## Key numbers

- The typical hadronic scale Λ is of order 1 GeV, and deep inelastic processes require Q considerably larger than this scale.
- In the inclusive cross section up to order 1/Q, the twist-3 function g_T appears multiplied by a factor M/Q, where M is the target mass.
- For semi-inclusive scattering with a gaussian transverse momentum dependence, the distribution functions are parametrized as f(x, p_T^2) = f(x) * (R_H^2/π) * exp(-R_H^2 p_T^2), and fragmentation functions as D(z, z^2 k_T^2) = D(z) * (R_h^2/(π z^2)) * exp(-R_h^2 k_T^2).
- The cross section for unpolarized targets at order 1/Q contains a cos(φ_h) asymmetry with a factor Q_T/Q, where Q_T is the transverse momentum of the virtual photon.

## Context

This work extends the framework of Jaffe and Ji (1992) for k_T-integrated distribution functions to include transverse momentum dependence, building on earlier work by Ralston and Soper (1979) and Tangerman and Mulders (1995). The analysis shows that all six twist-two x- and p_T-dependent quark distribution functions for a spin-1/2 hadron can be accessed in leading-order asymmetries, including the transverse spin distribution h_1 identified by Collins (1993). The results enable experimental extraction of these distribution and fragmentation functions from azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, particularly at facilities like HERA.
