# Faddeev approach to confined three-quark problems

> We propose a method that allows for the efficient solution of the three-body Faddeev equations in the presence of infinitely rising confinement interactions. Such a method is useful in calculations of nonrelativistic and especially semirelativistic constituent quark models. The convergence of the partial wave series is accelerated and possible spurious contributions in the Faddeev components are avoided. We demonstrate how the method works with the example of the Goldstone-boson-exchange chiral quark model for baryons.

## Key claim

The authors propose a modified Faddeev approach that can efficiently solve three-quark problems with infinitely rising confinement potentials, provided that the long-range parts of the confinement are moved into a modified channel resolvent and an auxiliary short-range Gaussian potential is introduced to shift bound states of the modified Hamiltonian far above the energy region of physical interest (above approximately 2 GeV).

## Method

The total Hamiltonian is split differently than usual: the long-range parts of the confinement interactions from all three two-body subsystems are included in a modified Hamiltonian H^c, leaving only short-range residual interactions (hyperfine potentials and short-range confinement parts) in the modified Faddeev equations. The modified Faddeev equations are solved using a Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion approach, where the basis is truncated at finite N and the inverse of the channel resolvent matrix is approximated by the matrix of the inverse operator. An auxiliary Gaussian potential with parameters a0 = 3 fm^{-1} and r0 = 1 fm is introduced to cut off the confinement at short and intermediate distances, ensuring no bound states of H^c below approximately 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon ground state mass: 939 MeV (semirelativistic) and 942 MeV (nonrelativistic), converged with 10 channels and N=20 separable terms
- Delta(1232) ground state mass: 1239 MeV (semirelativistic) and 1237 MeV (nonrelativistic), converged with only 5 channels
- N*(1440) excitation mass: 1459 MeV (semirelativistic) and 1467 MeV (nonrelativistic)
- N*(1535)-N*(1520) negative-parity excitation mass: 1516-1518 MeV (semirelativistic) and 1532 MeV (nonrelativistic)
- Auxiliary Gaussian potential parameters: a0 = 3 fm^{-1} and r0 = 1 fm, chosen to avoid bound states of H^c below approximately 2 GeV
- Approximately 20 separable expansion terms and at most 10 angular-momentum channels are sufficient for convergence

## Context

This work extends the modified Faddeev formalism previously suggested by Papp (1999) for nonrelativistic confined three-body problems to semirelativistic constituent quark models, where the confinement is considerably stronger (consistent with the string tension of quantum chromodynamics). The method is demonstrated on the Goldstone-boson-exchange chiral quark model, for which nonrelativistic solutions had been obtained by both the standard Faddeev approach and the stochastic variational method (SVM) of Suzuki and Varga (1998), while semirelativistic solutions had only existed from SVM. The results confirm that the two distinct solution methods produce identical light baryon spectra, enabling reliable Faddeev calculations for semirelativistic quark models where the partial-wave convergence was previously too slow and spurious contributions contaminated the Faddeev components.
