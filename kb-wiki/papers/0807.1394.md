# String theory implications on causal hydrodynamics

> 12 pages, PTPTeX; v2: minor corrections & added references; Invited talk presented at NFQCD2008 Symposium (March 3-6, 2008, YITP); To appear in Prog. Theor. Phys. Suppl

## Key claim

String theory via the AdS/CFT duality provides the first explicit computations of second-order transport coefficients (relaxation times) for strongly coupled gauge theory plasmas, but the Israel-Stewart theory should not be taken literally because third-order terms contaminate the shear-mode extraction, making only the sound-mode result for the relaxation time τπ reliable.

## Method

The author uses the AdS/CFT duality to map finite-temperature strongly coupled gauge theories to Schwarzschild-AdS black holes in various dimensions (SAdS4, SAdS5, SAdS7). By studying linear perturbations of the black hole horizons and computing the dispersion relations for the shear mode and sound mode of the dual plasma, the relaxation time τπ is extracted from the O(q⁴) term in the shear mode and the O(q³) term in the sound mode. The consistency of the Israel-Stewart framework is checked by comparing τπ obtained independently from these two channels.

## Key numbers

* For SAdS5 (dual to N=4 SYM), the relaxation time from the shear mode is τπ = (1 − ln 2)/(2π T) and from the sound mode is τπ = (2 − ln 2)/(2π T) ≈ 0.21 fm when T⁻¹ = 1 fm.
* For SAdS4 (dual to M2-brane theory), the shear-mode result is τπ = [9 − (9 ln 3 − √3 π)]/(24π T) and the sound-mode result is τπ = [18 − (9 ln 3 − √3 π)]/(24π T) ≈ 0.18 fm.
* For SAdS7 (dual to M5-brane theory), the shear-mode result is τπ = [18 − (9 ln 3 + √3 π)]/(24π T) and the sound-mode result is τπ = [36 − (9 ln 3 + √3 π)]/(24π T) ≈ 0.27 fm.
* The ratio τπ/η for N=4 SYM at strong coupling is τπ/η = 2(2 − ln 2)/(T s) ≈ 3/(T s); the weak-coupling Boltzmann gas estimate from Israel-Stewart is τπ/η = 6/(T s).
* The signal propagation speed v_front (defined as √(Dη/τπ)) is 0.62 for SAdS5, compared to the speed of sound vs = 0.58.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT computation of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/s = 1/(4π) (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2003) to second-order hydrodynamics, addressing the fact that heavy-ion simulations (Romatschke 2007, Song 2008) used the AdS/CFT value for η/s but weak-coupling results for causal hydrodynamics. The results contradict the naive expectation that the Israel-Stewart theory is universally applicable, showing that third-order terms are not small. The findings enable future work to compute finite-coupling corrections (Buchel 2008) and to test whether the ratio τπ/η is approximately coupling-independent, in contrast to η/s.
