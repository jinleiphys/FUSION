# Variation after Particle-Number Projection for the HFB Method with the Skyrme Energy Density Functional

> Variation after particle-number restoration is incorporated for the first time into the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework employing the Skyrme energy density functional with zero-range pairing. The resulting projected HFB equations can be expressed in terms of the local gauge-angle-dependent densities. Results of projected calculations are compared with those obtained within the Lipkin-Nogami method in the standard version and with the Lipkin-Nogami method followed by exact particle-number projection.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first implementation of variation after particle-number projection (VAPNP) within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework using the Skyrme energy density functional with zero-range pairing, but the practical feasibility relies on expressing the projected HFB equations in terms of local gauge-angle-dependent densities and on reintroducing an artificial chemical potential to stabilize the iterative solution.

## Method

The authors derived the Skyrme HFB+VAPNP equations by expressing the projected energy as a functional of the unprojected HFB density matrix and pairing tensor, then performing the variation to obtain modified self-consistent fields that depend on gauge-angle-dependent local densities (particle density, kinetic energy density, spin-current density, and their pairing counterparts). The resulting equations were solved in a harmonic oscillator basis using Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature for the two-dimensional gauge-angle integrals (one for neutrons, one for protons), with the canonical representation used to simplify calculations of the projected fields. To maintain numerical stability, a Lagrange multiplier mu was artificially reintroduced into the HFB+VAPNP equations to keep the average particle number close to the desired value during iterations, with mu converging to zero at the self-consistent solution.

## Key numbers

- The projected energy E^N is computed as a double integral over gauge angles phi_n and phi_p: E^N = integral dphi_n dphi_p y_n(phi_n) y_p(phi_p) E(phi_n, phi_p), where the integration is performed numerically using L_n x L_p Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature points.
- The canonical representation yields diagonal gauge-angle-dependent matrices: C_mu(phi_q) = exp(2i phi_q) / (u_mu_q^2 + exp(2i phi_q) v_mu_q^2), where v_mu^2 are occupation probabilities with 0 <= v_mu^2 <= 1.
- The effective pairing strength G_eff used in the Lipkin-Nogami method is defined as G_eff = - (bar_Delta)^2 / E_pair, where bar_Delta is the average pairing gap and E_pair is the HFB pairing energy.
- The Lipkin-Nogami curvature lambda_2 is estimated from a seniority-pairing expression involving traces of density matrix rho and pairing tensor kappa, with the effective pairing strength G_eff determined from the self-consistent HFB solution.

## Context

This work extends the VAPNP method, previously implemented for schematic models (Sheikh 2000) and for the finite-range Gogny force (Anguiano 2001, 2002), to the widely used Skyrme energy density functional with zero-range pairing forces. The results are compared with the Lipkin-Nogami method (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964) and the projected Lipkin-Nogami method (Dobaczewski 1993, Stoitsov 2003), enabling systematic studies of pairing correlations in nuclei where particle-number symmetry restoration is important, such as weakly bound exotic nuclei near the drip lines.
