# Directed flow from C-odd gluon correlations at small $x$

> It is shown that odd harmonic azimuthal correlations, including the directed flow $v_1$, in forward two-particle production in peripheral proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions can arise simply from the radial nuclear profile of a large nucleus. This requires consideration of the C-odd part of the gluonic generalized transverse momentum dependent (GTMD) correlator of nucleons in the nucleus. The gluonic GTMD correlator is the Fourier transform of an off-forward hadronic matrix element containing gluonic field strength tensors that are connected by gauge links. It is parametrized in terms of various gluon GTMD distribution functions (GTMDs). We show (in a gauge invariant way) that for the relevant dipole-type gauge link structure in the small-$x$ limit the GTMD correlator reduces to a generalized Wilson loop correlator. The Wilson loop correlator is parametrized in terms of a single function, implying that in the region of small $x$ there is only one independent dipole-type GTMD, which can have a C-odd part. We show that the odderon Wigner distribution, which is related to this C-odd dipole GTMD by a Fourier transform, generates odd harmonics in the two-particle azimuthal correlations in peripheral $pA$ collisions. We calculate the first odd harmonic $v_1$ for forward production within the color glass condensate framework in the limit of a large number of colors. We find that nonzero odd harmonics are present without breaking the rotational symmetry of the nucleus, arising just from its inhomogeneity in the radial direction. Using a CGC model with a cubic action, we illustrate that percent level $v_1$ can arise from this C-odd mechanism. In contrast, we show that only even harmonics arise in diffractive dijet production in ultra-peripheral $pA$ collisions where this gluon dipole GTMD also appears.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nonzero odd harmonic azimuthal correlations, including directed flow \(v_1\), in forward two-particle production in peripheral proton-nucleus collisions can arise from the radial inhomogeneity of the nuclear profile alone, without breaking rotational symmetry, provided one includes the C-odd (odderon) part of the gluonic dipole GTMD correlator.

## Method

The authors parametrize the gluon-gluon GTMD correlator for unpolarized hadrons and show that in the small-\(x\) and small-\(\xi\) limit the dipole-type GTMD correlator reduces to a single generalized Wilson loop correlator, parametrized by one function \(\mathcal{E}\). They then compute the first odd harmonic \(v_1\) for forward dihadron production in peripheral \(pA\) collisions within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework, using the large-\(N_c\) limit and a Gaussian ansatz for the proton double parton distribution, with the nuclear density described by a transverse impact parameter profile \(T(\bm{b})\).

## Key numbers

* Percent-level \(v_1\) can arise from the C-odd mechanism, as illustrated using a CGC model with a cubic action.
* The relation between the GTMDs in the small-\(x\) limit is given by \(\lim_{x,\xi \to 0} x\mathcal{F}_1 = \lim_{x,\xi \to 0} x\mathcal{F}_2^{(1)} = -4 \lim_{x,\xi \to 0} x\mathcal{F}_3^{(1)} = -2 \lim_{x,\xi \to 0} x\mathcal{F}_4^{(1)} = \mathcal{E}^{(1)}\), where \(\mathcal{F}_i^{(n)} \equiv [(\bm{k}^2 - \bm{\Delta}^2/4)/(2M^2)]^n \,\mathcal{F}_i\).
* The forward limit of the Wilson loop GTMD gives \(\lim_{\Delta \to 0} \mathcal{E}(\bm{k}^2,\bm{\Delta}^2,\bm{k} \cd \bm{\Delta}) = e(\bm{k}^2)\), where \(e\) is the Wilson loop TMD defined in Boer (2016).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hagiwara (2017) on even harmonics from the gluon Wigner distribution to include odd harmonics from the odderon Wigner distribution. It contrasts with earlier work by Dumitru (2014) where odd harmonics required C-odd fluctuations in an anisotropic, rotationally noninvariant target; here odd harmonics arise from C-odd correlations without breaking rotational symmetry, due to the radial inhomogeneity of the nucleus. The result enables the interpretation of directed flow \(v_1\) in peripheral \(pA\) collisions as an initial-state effect from the odderon, and it shows that the same gluon dipole GTMD produces only even harmonics in diffractive dijet production in ultra-peripheral \(pA\) collisions.
