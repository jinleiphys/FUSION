# Neutrino interactions with nucleons and nuclei at intermediate energies

> We investigate neutrino-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies incorporating quasielastic scattering and Delta(1232) excitation as elementary processes, together with Fermi motion, Pauli blocking and mean-field potentials in the nuclear medium. A full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions is achieved with a semiclassical BUU transport model. Results for inclusive reactions and nucleon knockout are presented.

## Key claim

The authors model neutrino-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies (0.5 to 2 GeV) using quasielastic scattering and Delta(1232) excitation as elementary processes, but the results depend on the specific parameterizations of form factors and the semiclassical BUU transport model for final state interactions; the model is limited to heavy nuclei (from carbon onward) and the presented numerical results are for iron targets only.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical model of neutrino-nucleus collisions using a fully relativistic formalism for neutrino-nucleon interactions, incorporating quasielastic scattering and Delta(1232) resonance excitation with state-of-the-art parameterizations of vector and axial form factors. Nuclear medium effects were included via Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and mean-field potentials, while final state interactions were treated with a semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model in coupled channels, allowing particles to undergo elastic and inelastic collisions, decay, or absorption.

## Key numbers

- The model covers neutrino energies between 0.5 and 2 GeV, where quasielastic scattering and Delta(1232) resonance excitation dominate.
- For the axial form factor in quasielastic scattering, the axial mass parameter is M_A = 1 GeV with g_A = -1.267.
- For the N-Delta(1232) transition, the vector form factor parameters are C_3^V(0) = 1.95 and M_V = 0.84 GeV.
- For the axial N-Delta(1232) transition, the parameters are C_5^A(0) = 1.2 and M_A = 1.05 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical treatments of neutrino-nucleus interactions by incorporating a full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions via the BUU transport model, building on earlier work by Singh (1998) on Delta decay broadening and Effenberger (1999) on transport model development. The model is intended to support interpretation of data from experiments such as MINERvA and FINeSSE, which will measure neutrino interactions on nuclear targets to extract nucleon and N-Delta axial form factors and the strange spin of the nucleon.
