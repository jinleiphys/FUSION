# What are the early degrees of freedom in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions?

> The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model is used to study the impact on the choice of initial degrees of freedom on the final hadronic and electromagnetic observables in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. We find that a non-perturbative system of massive gluons (scenario I) and a system dominated by quarks and antiquarks (scenario II) lead to different hadronic observables when imposing the same initial energy-momentum tensor $T_{μν}(x)$ just after the passage of the impinging nuclei. In case of the gluonic initial condition the formation of $s,{\bar s}$ pairs in the QGP proceeds rather slow such that the anti-strange quarks and accordingly the $K^+$ mesons do not achieve chemical equilibrium even in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Accordingly, the $K^+$ rapidity distribution is suppressed in the gluonic scenario and in conflict with the data from the BRAHMS Collaboration. The proton and antiproton rapidity distributions also disfavor the scenario I. Furthermore, a clear suppression of direct photon and dilepton production is found for the pure gluonic initial conditions which is not so clearly seen in the present photon and dilepton spectra from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV due to a large contribution from other channels. It is argued that dilepton spectra in the invariant mass range 1.2 GeV $< M <$ 3 GeV will provide a definitive answer once the background from correlated $D$-meson decays is subtracted experimentally.

## Key claim

The choice of initial degrees of freedom (pure gluons vs. quarks and antiquarks) in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) significantly affects hadronic and electromagnetic observables in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, even when the initial energy-momentum tensor Tμν(x) is kept identical; however, the present photon and dilepton data do not yet provide a definitive discrimination, and the authors argue that dilepton spectra in the invariant mass range 1.2 GeV < M < 3 GeV will give a conclusive answer once the D-meson decay background is subtracted.

## Method

The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport model, based on Kadanoff-Baym equations with dressed propagators from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), was used to simulate 5% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Two extreme initial scenarios were imposed: scenario I with only massive gluons (no quarks/antiquarks) and scenario II with only quarks and antiquarks (no gluons), while keeping the local energy-momentum tensor Tμν(x) identical in both cases. The time evolution of parton abundances, interaction rates, hadronic spectra, elliptic flow, and electromagnetic observables (photons and dileptons) were computed and compared to experimental data from BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The gluon decay time in scenario I is τg ≈ 6–8 fm/c, roughly in line with BAMPS calculations.
- The critical energy density for the phase transition is εc ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³.
- The strangeness equilibration time for partonic energies in PHSD is on the order of 20–30 fm/c.
- The gluon spectral function width in PHSD is 100–150 MeV, implying a decay rate of 1.5–2 fm/c.
- The ratio of strange to light quarks from gluon decay is about 1/3.
- The average formation time of partons in scenario II is 0.2–0.3 fm/c.
- The partonic photon contribution is about an order of magnitude larger in scenario II than in scenario I.
- The QGP di-electron yield shows a large difference between scenarios, increasing with invariant mass M in the range 1.2 GeV < M < 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous PHSD studies of p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (Konchakovski 2014) and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (Volo 2015), which found that hadronic spectra and collective flow were not sensitive to initial state fluctuations. The study directly tests the gluon-dominated initial state scenario proposed by Horst (2015) and the quark/antiquark scenario, building on earlier work by the Catania group (Greco 2015) and the BAMPS model. The results enable future experimental discrimination using dilepton measurements in the invariant mass range 1.2–3 GeV, motivating the STAR detector upgrade with a muon telescope (Ruan 2009).
