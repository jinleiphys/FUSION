# A similarity renormalization group approach to Green's function methods

> The family of Green's function methods based on the $GW$ approximation has gained popularity in the electronic structure theory thanks to its accuracy in weakly correlated systems combined with its cost-effectiveness. Despite this, self-consistent versions still pose challenges in terms of convergence. A recent study \href{https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0089317}{[J. Chem. Phys. 156, 231101 (2022)]} has linked these convergence issues to the intruder-state problem. In this work, a perturbative analysis of the similarity renormalization group (SRG) approach is performed on Green's function methods. The SRG formalism enables us to derive, from first principles, the expression of a naturally static and Hermitian form of the self-energy that can be employed in quasiparticle self-consistent $GW$ (qs$GW$) calculations. The resulting SRG-based regularized self-energy significantly accelerates the convergence of qs$GW$ calculations, slightly improves the overall accuracy, and is straightforward to implement in existing code.

## Key claim

The paper derives a static, Hermitian form of the self-energy for quasiparticle self-consistent GW (qsGW) calculations from first principles using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) formalism, which accelerates convergence and slightly improves accuracy, but only when the flow parameter s is chosen to be large enough to include many states yet small enough to avoid intruder states.

## Method

The authors apply Wegner's canonical SRG generator to the upfolded (linearized) form of the GW quasiparticle equation, solving the flow equation perturbatively to second order. This yields renormalized screened two-electron integrals and a static self-energy correction that depends on the flow parameter s, which is then used in place of the standard symmetrized qsGW self-energy in self-consistent field calculations.

## Key numbers

- The first-order renormalized coupling elements evolve as \(W_{pq}^{\nu(1)}(s) = W_{pq}^{\nu} e^{-(\Delta_{pq}^{\nu})^2 s}\), where \(\Delta_{pr}^{\nu} = \epsilon_p - \epsilon_r - \text{sgn}(\epsilon_r-\epsilon_F)\Omega_\nu\).
- The static SRG-qsGW self-energy matrix elements are given by \(\Sigma_{pq}^{\text{SRG-qsGW}}(s) = \sum_{r\nu} \frac{\Delta_{pr}^{\nu}+ \Delta_{qr}^{\nu}}{(\Delta_{pr}^{\nu})^2 + (\Delta_{qr}^{\nu})^2} W_{pr}^{\nu} W_{qr}^{\nu} \times \left[1 - e^{-\left[(\Delta_{pr}^{\nu})^2 + (\Delta_{qr}^{\nu})^2\right] s}\right]\).
- The energy cutoff is related to the flow parameter as \(\Lambda = s^{-1/2}\); states with energy difference greater than \(\Lambda\) are decoupled from the reference space.

## Context

This work extends the SRG regularization approach that Monino and Loos (2022) applied to remove discontinuities in GW quasiparticle equations, and builds on the SRG formalism developed by Wegner (1994) and Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994) and later applied in quantum chemistry by Evangelista and coworkers (2014–2023). The resulting static self-energy provides a principled alternative to the ad hoc imaginary-shift regularizer commonly used in qsGW calculations, and the formalism can be applied to other self-energies such as second-order Green's function or T-matrix approximations.
