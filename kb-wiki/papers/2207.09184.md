# Chiral effects in astrophysics and cosmology

> The microscopic quantum nature of elementary particles, chirality, leads to macroscopic phenomena like the chiral anomaly, chiral magnetic effect, and chiral plasma instability. We review recent progress of the studies of these chiral effects in high-energy astrophysics, such as pulsar kicks, magnetars, and core-collapse supernovae, and early Universe cosmology, such as the primordial magnetic field, baryogenesis, and chiral gravitational waves. We also provide a pedagogical introduction to the chiral effects and low-energy effective theories to describe them in and out of equilibrium -- the chiral (magneto)hydrodynamics, chiral kinetic theory, and chiral radiation transport theory for neutrinos.

## Key claim

This review argues that chirality of elementary particles (neutrinos, electrons, quarks) leads to macroscopic chiral effects, such as the chiral anomaly, chiral magnetic effect, and chiral plasma instability, that can significantly modify the non-equilibrium evolution of high-energy astrophysical systems and early Universe cosmology, but the applicability depends on the specific system's energy scales and the perturbative treatment of fermion masses that cause chirality flipping.

## Method

The authors provide a pedagogical review of chiral effects (chiral anomaly, chiral magnetic effect, chiral vortical effect, chiral plasma instability) and the low-energy effective theories to describe them: chiral (magneto)hydrodynamics, chiral kinetic theory, and chiral radiation transport theory for neutrinos. They then apply these frameworks to specific astrophysical puzzles (pulsar kicks, magnetars, core-collapse supernovae) and cosmological phenomena (primordial magnetic fields, baryogenesis, chiral gravitational waves), summarizing recent theoretical progress in each area.

## Key numbers

- Neutron stars typically have velocities ~100 km/s after formation (pulsar kicks)
- Magnetars have surface magnetic fields ~10^15 G
- Core-collapse supernova explosion energy is ~10^51 erg for stars with mass >~10 solar masses
- The chiral magnetic effect current for Dirac fermions is j = (e mu_5)/(2 pi^2) B, where mu_5 is the chiral chemical potential
- The chiral plasma instability growth rate is Gamma_CPI = (1/sigma) (e^2 mu_5/(4 pi^2))^2, with maximal wavenumber k_CPI = e^2 |mu_5|/(4 pi^2)
- The chiral vortical effect particle number current for right-handed fermions is j_R^z = (mu_R^2/(4 pi^2) + T^2/12) Omega
- The chiral anomaly in 3+1 dimensions gives partial_mu j^{mu5} = (e^2/(2 pi^2)) E·B

## Context

This review extends the study of chiral effects from heavy-ion collisions and Weyl/Dirac semimetals (reviewed by Kharzeev (2015) and Armitage (2017) respectively) to astrophysics and cosmology. It addresses long-standing puzzles including pulsar kicks (reviewed by Lai (2001)), magnetars (reviewed by Harding (2006), Spruit (2007), Enoto (2019)), and core-collapse supernovae (reviewed by Kotake (2012), Burrows (2012), Janka (2016)), as well as cosmological mysteries of primordial magnetic fields (reviewed by Durrer (2013), Subramanian (2015)), baryogenesis (reviewed by Riotto (1999)), and chiral gravitational waves. The work enables future theoretical and observational studies connecting microscopic chirality to macroscopic astrophysical and cosmological phenomena.
