# The pentaquark in K-plus-d total cross section data

> An analysis of $K^+$-d total cross section data is undertaken to explore possible effects of the recently observed resonance in the S=+1 hadronic system with mass around 1.55 GeV. It is found that a structure corresponding to the resonance is visible in the data. The width consistent with the observed deviation from background is found to be $0.9\pm 0.3$ MeV and the mass is $1.559\pm 0.003$ GeV/c$^2$ for spin-parity $\h^+$ and $1.547\pm0.002$\ GeV/c$^2$ for $\h^-$. The errors are one standard deviation and statistical only. \

## Key claim

A structure corresponding to the S=+1 pentaquark resonance is visible in existing K⁺-d total cross section data after applying double-scattering and Fermi-motion corrections, but the extracted mass depends on the assumed spin-parity assignment because the background phase shift shifts the observed peak differently for the S₀₁ (Jᴾ = ½⁻) and P₀₁ (Jᴾ = ½⁺) partial waves.

## Method

The author analyzed eight K⁺-d total cross section data points from Bowen et al. (1973) by first constructing a background fit using scattering-length and scattering-volume parameterizations for I=0 and I=1 s- and p-wave phase shifts, with double-scattering corrections and Fermi-momentum averaging applied. A resonant S-matrix term (product form with background) was then added in either the S₀₁ or P₀₁ partial wave, and χ² contours were computed as a function of mass and width to extract best-fit parameters.

## Key numbers

- Width of the resonance: Γ = 0.9 ± 0.3 MeV (combining statistical and background-uncertainty contributions)
- Mass for spin-parity ½⁺ (P₀₁ partial wave): 1.559 ± 0.003 GeV/c²
- Mass for spin-parity ½⁻ (S₀₁ partial wave): 1.547 ± 0.002 GeV/c²
- I=1 s-wave scattering length: −0.328 fm
- I=0 s-wave scattering length: −0.06 fm (s-wave fit) or 0.00 fm (p-wave fit)
- I=0 P₀₁ scattering volume: 0.123 fm³ (s-wave fit) or 0.127 fm³ (p-wave fit)
- Bowen et al. data renormalized by factor 1.06 to match Krauss et al. normalization
- Background fit falls 5 σ below the data in the resonance region

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cahn and Trilling (2003), who calculated a width of 0.9 ± 0.3 MeV from the charge-exchange channel but used only a linear background and a Hulthén deuteron wave function without double-scattering corrections. The present analysis includes double scattering (known to be important at low energies from work by Garcilazo and others) and a measured Fermi-momentum distribution, enabling the resonance to be seen in older K⁺-d total cross section data. The results enable a potential parity determination by comparing the mass extracted from the S₀₁ versus P₀₁ partial wave with masses from discovery experiments (Nakano et al., Barmin et al., Asratyan et al., Barth et al., JLab1, JLab2), though the author cautions that systematic errors and the product-form S-matrix assumption require further study.
