# Deformed Woods-Saxon Potential in the Frame of Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics for Any l-State

> A novel analytically solvable deformed Woods-Saxon potential is investigated by means of the Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics. Hamiltonian hierarchy method and the shape invariance property are used in the calculations. The energy levels are obtained for any l-state. The interrelations for some nuclear scattering processes are also discussed

# Deformed Woods-Saxon Potential in the Frame of Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics for Any l-State

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deformed Woods-Saxon potential can be solved analytically for any l-state using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SQM) and shape invariance, but only after introducing an effective potential that approximates the centrifugal barrier term under the condition that the parameter α = (1+q)/R₀ is small. The exact solution for l ≠ 0 is therefore approximate, relying on a first-order approximation of the centrifugal term.

## Method

The authors apply the Hamiltonian hierarchy method and shape invariance property of supersymmetric quantum mechanics to the radial Schrödinger equation with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential. For l = 0, they construct a superpotential that satisfies the Riccati equation and derive partner potentials that exhibit shape invariance, yielding exact analytical energy eigenvalues. For l ≠ 0, they replace the centrifugal barrier term ℏ²l(l+1)/(2mr²) with an approximate term ℏ²l(l+1)α²/[2m(q+e^{(r-R₀)/a})²] that has the same functional form as the deformed Woods-Saxon potential, enabling the same SQM factorization method to be applied.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter q satisfies q ≥ 1, with specific calculations performed for q = 1 and q = 2.
- The surface diffuseness parameter a ranges between 0.8 and 1.4 fm for nuclear scattering processes.
- The radius parameter r₀ = 0.90 fm is used empirically.
- For q = 1, the mass number A is in the domain 6 ≤ A ≤ 30; for q = 2, A is in the domain 19 ≤ A ≤ 101.
- The parameter α is defined as α = 1/a for the general case, and α = (1+q)/R₀ for nuclear scattering processes.
- The energy eigenvalue formula for l ≠ 0 is given by Eq. (45): E_{nl} = -(ℏ²/(2ma²)) × [((2mV₀a²/(qℏ²) - l(l+1)/q²) / (1+2n+√(1+4l(l+1)/q²)))² + ½(2mV₀a²/(qℏ²) - l(l+1)/q²) + (1/16)(1+2n+√(1+4l(l+1)/q²))²].

## Context

This work extends prior applications of supersymmetric quantum mechanics to exponential-type potentials, following the shape invariance framework introduced by Gendenshtein (1983) and the Hamiltonian hierarchy method developed by Cooper and collaborators. The deformed Woods-Saxon potential generalizes the standard Woods-Saxon form used in nuclear physics for describing nucleon-nucleus scattering, and the authors compare their results with earlier numerical calculations by Berkdemir and Sever (2004). The analytical expressions enable rapid computation of bound-state energies for nuclear systems without full numerical integration, and the authors suggest that further testing on other effective Woods-Saxon-like potentials is needed to validate the method's credibility.
