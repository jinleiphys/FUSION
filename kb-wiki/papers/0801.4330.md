# Proton-proton fusion in pionless effective theory

> The proton-proton fusion reaction, $pp\to de^+ν$, is studied in pionless effective field theory (EFT) with di-baryon fields up to next-to leading order. With the aid of the di-baryon fields, the effective range corrections are naturally resummed up to the infinite order and thus the calculation is greatly simplified. Furthermore, the low-energy constant which appears in the axial-current-di-baryon-di-baryon contact vertex is fixed through the ratio of two- and one-body matrix elements which reproduces the tritium lifetime very precisely. As a result we can perform a parameter free calculation for the process. We compare our numerical result with those from the accurate potential model and previous pionless EFT calculations, and find a good agreement within the accuracy better than 1%.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their pionless effective field theory (EFT) calculation with di-baryon fields, performed only up to next-to-leading order (NLO), reproduces the proton-proton fusion amplitude to within better than 1% accuracy compared to accurate potential model results, but this agreement relies on fixing the unknown low-energy constant \(l_{1A}\) indirectly from the tritium lifetime via a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) calculation, rather than from direct experimental data on the two-body system.

## Method

The authors used pionless EFT with di-baryon fields, including the Coulomb interaction, to calculate the amplitude for \(pp\to de^+\nu_e\) up to NLO. The effective range corrections were resummed to infinite order via the di-baryon fields, and the unknown low-energy constant \(l_{1A}\) in the axial-current-di-baryon-di-baryon contact vertex was fixed using the relative strength of the two-body to one-body matrix element, \(\delta_{2B} = (0.86 \pm 0.05)\%\), which was previously determined from the tritium lifetime in HB\(\chi\)PT. The amplitude was evaluated in the zero-proton-momentum limit using two sets of effective range parameters from the literature.

## Key numbers

*   Squared reduced matrix element \(\Lambda^2(0) = 7.09 \pm 0.02\) (dimensionless) for the \(pp\to de^+\nu_e\) process at zero proton momentum, averaged over two sets of effective range parameters.
*   Leading-order (LO) reduced matrix element \(\Lambda_{\rm LO}(0) = 2.641\) (dimensionless).
*   Next-to-leading-order (NLO) reduced matrix element \(\Lambda_{\rm NLO}(0) = 2.663 \pm 0.004\) (dimensionless), averaged over the two parameter sets.
*   Low-energy constant \(l_{1A} = -0.50 \pm 0.03\) (dimensionless), fixed from \(\delta_{2B} = (0.86 \pm 0.05)\%\).
*   Effective range parameters used: \(a_C = -7.8063 \pm 0.0026\) fm, \(r_0 = 2.794 \pm 0.014\) fm, \(\rho_d = 1.760 \pm 0.005\) fm (Set 1); \(a_C = -7.8149 \pm 0.0029\) fm, \(r_0 = 2.769 \pm 0.014\) fm, \(\rho_d = 1.753 \pm 0.008\) fm (Set 2).
*   Deuteron binding energy \(B = 2.224575\) MeV, axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.2695\), proton mass \(m_p = 938.272\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of \(pp\) fusion by Kong and Ravndal (NLO) and Butler and Chen (N\(^4\)LO), which suffered from large uncertainties due to an unfixed low-energy constant \(L_{1A}\). By using di-baryon fields to resum effective range corrections and fixing the analogous constant \(l_{1A}\) from the tritium lifetime (as done in HB\(\chi\)PT by Park et al. (2003)), the authors achieve a parameter-free calculation with improved precision. The result enables future work to include higher-order corrections (e.g., vacuum polarization and \(1/m_N\) terms) to reach a few tenths of a percent accuracy, and it suggests that the same low-energy constant can be used to predict other processes such as \(nn\) fusion, neutrino-deuteron reactions, and muon capture on the deuteron.
