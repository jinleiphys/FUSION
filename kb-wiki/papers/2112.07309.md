# Pairing patterns in polarized unitary Fermi gases above the superfluid transition

> We non-perturbatively study pairing in the high-temperature regime of polarized unitary two-component Fermi gases by extracting the pair-momentum distribution and shot-noise correlations. Whereas the pair-momentum distribution allows us to analyze the propagation of pairs composed of one spin-up and one spin-down fermion, shot-noise correlations provide us with a tomographic insight into pairing correlations around the Fermi surfaces associated with the two species. Assuming that the dominant pairing patterns right above the superfluid transition also govern the formation of condensates in the low-temperature regime, our analysis suggests that the superfluid ground state is homogeneous and of the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer-type over a wide range of polarizations.

## Key claim

Assuming that the dominant pairing patterns right above the superfluid transition also govern the formation of condensates at low temperatures, the analysis suggests that the superfluid ground state is homogeneous and of the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) type over a wide range of polarizations, but the authors do not exclude the existence of an inhomogeneous phase at larger polarizations and lower temperatures.

## Method

The authors used the complex Langevin (CL) method, a non-perturbative numerical approach that circumvents the sign problem arising in conventional Monte Carlo methods for spin-polarized systems. They computed the pair-momentum distribution and the shot-noise (density-density) correlation function in momentum space for a three-dimensional unitary Fermi gas on a spacetime lattice of size 11^3 x 160. The calculations were performed at fixed inverse temperature times chemical potential βμ = 2.0 (above the superfluid transition) for Zeeman fields βh from 0 to 2.0, corresponding to polarizations up to p ≈ 0.5 at T/T_F^↑ ≈ 0.29.

## Key numbers

- The superfluid phase transition temperature of the balanced system is (βμ)_c ≈ 2.5, corresponding to T_c/T_F^↑ ≈ 0.16.
- The largest polarization considered is p ≈ 0.5 at T/T_F^↑ ≈ 0.29.
- The spacetime lattice used is V × N_τ = 11^3 × 160.
- The pair-momentum distribution ρ_pair shows a maximum only at q = 0 for all temperatures and polarizations considered (βh < 2.0, βμ = 2.0).
- The zero-momentum component ρ_pair(q = 0) decreases as the chemical potential mismatch βh increases from 0 to 2.0 at fixed βμ = 2.0.

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative studies of the finite-temperature phase diagram of the spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gas, following earlier continuum methods such as Luttinger-Ward studies (Pini, 2021) that predicted dominant FFLO-type pairing fluctuations in certain regions. The results place ab initio bounds on the possible locations of exotic pairing phases such as FFLO, showing that for polarizations up to p ≈ 0.5 at T/T_F^↑ ≈ 0.29, only conventional BCS-type pairing is indicated. The shot-noise correlations, recently measured experimentally in two-dimensional Fermi gases (Holten, 2021), provide experimentally accessible predictions for three-dimensional systems and enable future experimental tests of pair formation in strongly correlated systems under extreme conditions.
