# A Two Potential Formula and its Application to Proton-Proton Scattering

> Within the framework of potential scattering theory we derive an analytical two-potential formula for the on-shell partial wave scattering amplitude. This formula embodies a large number of possible applications, including long range Coulomb forces as well as short distance singular potentials. As an example illustrating the use of the formula we analyze the determination of the strong proton-proton scattering s-wave phase shift from the experimentally determined Coulomb phase when the one pion exchange and two pion exchange chiral potentials are taken into account and analyze the relevant scales of the problem.

# A Two Potential Formula and its Application to Proton-Proton Scattering

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical two-potential formula for on-shell partial wave scattering amplitudes that relates the full phase shift to the short-range phase shift via a Moebius transformation of Wronskians, applicable when the short-range potential dominates at short distances. The formula is then applied to extract strong proton-proton s-wave phase shifts from Coulomb phase shifts using chiral one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials, with the hedge that the separation between strong and electromagnetic effects is not fully model-independent, particularly when short-distance electromagnetic corrections are included.

## Method

The authors start from the reduced Schrodinger equation for s-wave scattering and decompose the potential into long-range (V_L) and short-range (V_S) components, assuming V_S dominates at short distances r_c. They match logarithmic derivatives of the full and short-range reduced wave functions at r_c, then use the superposition principle to express both wave functions as linear combinations of sine-like and cosine-like solutions, yielding a bilinear relation between cot(delta) and cot(delta_S) involving Wronskians of the regular and irregular solutions evaluated at r_c. For the Coulomb case, the asymptotic boundary conditions are replaced by Coulomb wave functions F_0 and G_0, and the same matching procedure gives an analogous formula. The method is applied to proton-proton scattering by treating the strong interaction as V_S and Coulomb repulsion as V_L, using chiral potentials up to N^2 LO (including OPE and TPE) and the Nijmegen partial wave analysis for Coulomb phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Strong pp effective range from Nijmegen II potential: r_S = 2.84 fm; Coulomb pp effective range: r_C = 2.76 fm, giving a 3% relative difference
- Expected relative error from pion range: R_pi0 / a_B = M_p alpha / 2 m_pi0 ~ 2.5%
- Strong scattering length from Nijmegen II: alpha_S = -17.25 fm; Coulomb scattering length: alpha_C = -7.81 fm
- Extracted strong effective ranges at r_c = 0.1 fm: r_S,contact = 2.78 fm, r_S,OPE = 2.63 fm, r_S,TPE = 2.87 fm (compared to Nijmegen II value 2.84 fm)
- Cut-off needed to distinguish chiral TPE contributions within phase shift accuracy: r_c below 0.8 fm
- Two-pion exchange Compton wavelength scale: lambda_2pi = 1/(2 m_pi) ~ 0.7 fm
- Chiral couplings from pp data: c_1 = -0.76(7) GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.78(11) GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.92(52) GeV^{-1}
- For cut-off above r_c = 1.2 fm, lower and higher order computations cannot be distinguished within uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the two-potential formalism originally developed by Watson (1953) and Gell-Mann and Goldberger (1953) by providing an explicit on-shell relation between phase shifts for local potentials, which the standard Lippmann-Schwinger approach cannot directly produce. The coordinate-space renormalization analysis connects to momentum-space effective field theory treatments of proton-proton scattering by Kong and Ravndal (1998, 1999) using power divergence subtraction, and addresses disagreements with the next-to-next-to-leading order results of Ando et al. (2007) regarding Coulomb corrections to the effective range. The results enable model-independent extraction of strong phase shifts from experimentally measured Coulomb phase shifts using chiral potentials, with the observation that cut-offs below 0.8 fm are needed to resolve TPE contributions, and that the singular nature of chiral TPE potentials (diverging as 1/r^6) does not cause problems in the two-potential framework.
