# Finite-temperature equation of state with hyperons

> We present the novel finite-temperature FSU2H$^*$ equation-of-state model that covers a wide range of temperatures and lepton fractions for the conditions in proto-neutron stars, neutron star mergers and supernovae. The temperature effects on the thermodynamical observables and the composition of the neutron star core are stronger when the hyperonic degrees of freedom are considered. We pay a special attention to the temperature and density dependence of the thermal index in the presence of hyperons and conclude that the true thermal effects cannot be reproduced with the use of a constant $Γ$ law.

# Finite-temperature equation of state with hyperons

## Key claim

The FSU2H$^*$ model provides a finite-temperature equation of state for proto-neutron stars, neutron star mergers, and supernovae that includes hyperonic degrees of freedom; however, the thermal index $\Gamma$ depends non-negligibly on temperature and density when hyperons are present, meaning that the common approximation of a constant $\Gamma$ law (typically between 1.5 and 2.0) cannot accurately reproduce the true thermal effects.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model (FSU2H$^*$) that describes baryonic matter composed of nucleons and hyperons interacting through $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\phi$, and $\sigma^{*}$ meson exchange, solved at finite temperature under conditions of $\beta$-stability and charge neutrality. The Dirac equations for baryons and leptons and the Euler-Lagrange equations for meson fields are solved simultaneously for a grid of temperatures ($T = 0-100$ MeV), baryon densities ($\rho_B = \rho_0/2$ to $8\rho_0$), and lepton fractions ($Y_l = 0-0.4$), for both neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped matter.

## Key numbers

- The FSU2H$^*$ model yields a dimensionless tidal deformability $\Lambda = 526.3$ for a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star, compatible with GW170817 observations.
- The model satisfies the $2 M_\odot$ neutron star mass constraint and produces stellar radii below 13 km.
- The $\Xi$ hyperon nuclear potential is set to $U_{\Xi} = -24$ MeV, based on recent experimental data from Friedman (2021).
- The equation of state covers temperatures from $T = 0$ to $100$ MeV, baryon densities from $\rho_0/2 \sim 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$ to $8\rho_0 \sim 1.0$ fm$^{-3}$, and lepton fractions $Y_l = 0$ to $0.4$.
- Nuclear saturation density is $\rho_0 \sim 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the cold FSU2H model of Tolos (2016, 2017) to finite temperature, improving the $\sigma$-$\Xi$ coupling to match the recent $\Xi$ nuclear potential measurement and incorporating the $\sigma^*$ meson to reproduce the $\Lambda\Lambda$ bond energy observed in hypernuclear experiments by Takahashi (2001) and Ahn (2013). The model enables more realistic simulations of proto-neutron star cooling, core-collapse supernovae, and binary neutron star mergers, and directly challenges the common practice in groups such as Hotokezaka (2013), Endrizzi (2018), and Camelio (2020) of using a constant $\Gamma$ law (typically between 1.5 and 2.0) to approximate finite-temperature corrections.
