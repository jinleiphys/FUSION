# Probing QCD (media) with prompt photons

> The QCD phenomenology of prompt photon production from e-p to p-p/p-pbar and A-A collisions is reviewed. The use of prompt photons as a probe of (i) parton distribution functions (in a proton or in nuclei) as well as (ii) fragmentation functions (into photons and hadrons) and their medium-modifications is highlighted.

# Probing QCD (media) with prompt photons

## Key claim
Prompt photons can serve as a sensitive probe of parton distribution functions (in protons and nuclei) and fragmentation functions (into photons and hadrons) including their medium modifications, but the distinction between direct and fragmentation photons becomes meaningless beyond Born level, and only the sum of these contributions is physical and much less dependent on the arbitrary fragmentation scale than the individual components.

## Method
The paper reviews the perturbative QCD framework for prompt photon production, which proceeds either through direct hard subprocesses or via collinear fragmentation of hard partons, with collinear singularities absorbed into fragmentation functions D_{γ/k}(z, M_F) defined in a given factorization scheme. The author compares NLO QCD predictions to experimental data from e-p collisions at HERA (H1 and ZEUS), p-p and p-pbar collisions at fixed-target experiments (FNAL, SPS) and colliders (ISR, RHIC, Tevatron), and A-A collisions at RHIC, examining single-photon spectra, photon-jet correlations, and photon-hadron correlations.

## Key numbers
- World data from fixed-target to collider experiments are reproduced by NLO calculations within 20-30%, covering a range in x_T ≡ 2p_T/√s from 10^{-2} to 1 and almost 10 orders of magnitude in invariant production cross section.
- At Tevatron, data and theory agree from p_T = 50 GeV up to p_T = 400 GeV (CDF preliminary at √s = 1.96 TeV), but at low p_T ≲ 40 GeV CDF data lie significantly above NLO calculations.
- In DIS γ*–p reactions at HERA, good description of data is obtained at large Q^2 ≳ 40 GeV^2 while discrepancy is seen at low Q^2.
- In central Au–Au collisions at RHIC at √(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the nuclear modification factor R_AA^γ for single photons is of order 1, while pions show significant suppression.
- The photon–hadron momentum imbalance distributions in pp and Au–Au collisions at √(s_NN) = 200 GeV have been measured by PHENIX with rather large error bars.

## Context
This review extends the QCD phenomenology of prompt photon production from earlier work by Aurenche (2006) and Fontannaz (2001, 2003), and discusses constraints on nuclear PDFs that are crucial for interpreting quark-gluon plasma signatures such as jet quenching, where current precision is poor compared to pp collisions (Eskola, 2009). The paper enables future measurements at RHIC (forward photons in d–Au collisions) and LHC (photon–hadron correlations by ALICE and CMS) to constrain gluon nuclear densities and medium-modified fragmentation functions, and notes that a new Soft-Collinear Effective Theory approach by Becher (2009) achieves next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy with reduced scale sensitivity compared to present NLO calculations.
