# Violation of pseudospin symmetry in nucleon-nucleus scattering: exact relations

> An exact determination of the size of the pseudospin symmetry violating part of the nucleon-nucleus scattering amplitude from scattering observables is presented. The approximation recently used by Ginocchio turns out to underestimate the violation of pseudospin symmetry. Nevertheless the conclusion of a modestly broken pseudospin symmetry in proton-208Pb scattering at EL=800MeV remains valid.

## Key claim

The exact relation for the pseudospin symmetry violating part of the nucleon-nucleus scattering amplitude shows that the first-order approximation used by Ginocchio (1999) systematically underestimates the violation, but the conclusion of a modestly broken pseudospin symmetry in proton-208Pb scattering at 800 MeV remains valid.

## Method

The authors derived closed-form expressions relating the scattering amplitude to the observables (differential cross section, analyzing power P, and spin rotation function Q) for nucleon-nucleus scattering on a spin-zero target. They then applied a unitary transformation to the standard spin-dependent scattering amplitudes A and B to obtain the pseudospin representation amplitudes Ã and B̃, and derived exact expressions for the ratio R_ps = B̃/Ã in terms of the measured observables P and Q. These exact relations were evaluated using experimental proton-208Pb scattering data at 800 MeV from Hoffmann (1978) and Fergerson (1986).

## Key numbers

- The correction factor C_s in the exact expression for |R_s|^2 is >= 1, showing the first-order approximation systematically underestimates the spin-dependent part.
- At theta = 15 degrees, the first-order approximation underestimates |R_s|^2 by a factor of 2.
- The increment in |R_ps|^2 from the exact relation compared to the first-order approximation is up to 40%.
- |R_ps|^2 reaches values of about 0.15 at theta = 15 degrees.
- At theta = 15 degrees, |R_ps|^2 / |R_s|^2 is approximately 1/2.4 from the exact relation, compared to approximately 1/2 from the first-order approximation of Ginocchio (1999).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pseudospin symmetry in nucleon-nucleus scattering by Ginocchio (1999), who used a first-order approximation to extract the pseudospin symmetry breaking part of the scattering amplitude from proton-208Pb data at 800 MeV. The exact relations derived here show that the first-order approximation underestimates the violation, but the pseudospin symmetry remains a relevant concept at intermediate energies. The results enable future studies of the energy dependence of |R_ps|^2 for several nuclei to determine whether increased violation occurs at lower energies, as suggested by Bowlin (1988).
