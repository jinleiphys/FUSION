# Gauge fields renormalization groups and thermofractals

> The perturbative approach to QCD has been shown to be limited, and the difficulties to obtain accurate calculations in the low-energy region seems to be insurmountable. A recent approach uses the fractal structures of Yang-Mills Field Theory to circumvent those difficulties, allowing for the determination of an analytic expression for the running coupling. The results obtained are in agreement with several experimental findings, and explain many of the observed phenomena at high-energy collisions. In this work, we address some of the conceptual aspects of the fractal approach, which are expressed in terms of the renormalization group equation and the self-energy corrections to the parton mass. We associate these well-known concepts with the origins of the fractal structure in the quantum field theory.

## Key claim

The fractal structure in Yang-Mills fields arises from the combination of the scaling invariance described by the Callan-Symanzik equation and the complex internal structure of the effective parton due to self-energy interactions; this connection is expressed in terms of the renormalization group equation and self-energy corrections, not merely as an analogy.

## Method

The authors analyze the Dyson-Schwinger expansion for Yang-Mills fields, separating the self-energy contributions (which define the effective parton) from the proper-vertex interactions. They then show that the effective parton satisfies the Callan-Symanzik renormalization group equation, establishing both scale-free symmetry and internal structure, which together produce self-similarity. The resulting recursive equations for the probability density of energy ratios among components are solved, yielding q-exponential functions that are identified with the effective coupling in a non-perturbative analytical form.

## Key numbers

* The entropic index q for QCD is given by (q-1)^{-1} = (1/3)[11 n_c - (4/2) n_f], where n_c is the number of colours and n_f the number of flavours.
* The fractal dimension calculated from the theory is D = 0.69.
* The power-law distributions of particle energy or momenta are observed to be practically independent of particle species and collision energy above approximately 1 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermofractal approach introduced by Deppman (2016) and Deppman (2019) to explain the fractal origin of Tsallis statistics in high-energy collisions, building on the Callan-Symanzik equation (Callan 1972, Symanzik 1971) and Dyson-Schwinger formalism (Dyson 1949). It reconciles Hagedorn's Self-Consistent Thermodynamics (Hagedorn 1965) with experimental data by showing that the effective parton is a thermofractal of type-II, and it enables applications to hadron states, cosmic rays, and neutron-star physics. The theory also provides a non-perturbative analytical expression for the QCD running coupling with only one free parameter (the overall strength G_o), and predicts a constant value of q that has been tested against experimental data analyses (Cleymans 2011, Marques 2012).
