# Clusterization and deformation of multi-$Λ$ hypernuclei within relativistic mean-field model

> Deformed multi-$Λ$ hypernuclei are studied within a relativistic mean-field model. In this paper, we take some $N=Z$ "hyper isotope" chains, i.e., $^{8+n}_{\ \ nΛ}{\rm Be}$, $^{20+n}_{\ \ \ nΛ}{\rm Ne}$, and $^{28+n}_{\ \ \ nΛ}{\rm Si}$ systems where $n = 2$, $4$ for Be, and $n = 2$, $8$ for Ne and Si. A sign of two-$^6_{2Λ}$He cluster structure is observed in the two-body correlation in $^{12}_{4Λ}$Be. In the Ne hyper isotopes, the deformation is slightly reduced by addition of $Λ$ hyperons whereas it is significantly reduced or even disappears in the Si hyper isotopes.

# Clusterization and deformation of multi-Λ hypernuclei within relativistic mean-field model

## Key claim

Deformed multi-Λ hypernuclei in N=Z hyper isotope chains (Be, Ne, Si) show that Λ hyperons can either reduce deformation or induce cluster structures, but the observed sign of two-⁶₂ΛHe cluster structure in ¹²₄ΛBe is based on two-body correlation analysis at the mean-field level, and a beyond-mean-field calculation is needed to draw a firm conclusion on cluster correlation.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with meson-exchange interactions, using the PK1 parameter set for nucleon-meson couplings and a parameter set fitted to the binding energy of ⁴⁰ΛCa for Λ-meson couplings, with the tensor ω-Λ coupling fixed by a quark model. The mean-field equations were solved self-consistently on a 3-dimensional lattice with 24³ mesh points and 0.8 fm lattice spacing, without any assumption on spatial symmetry or cluster structure, using the damped gradient iteration technique and fast Fourier transform for derivatives.

## Key numbers

- ¹²₄ΛBe: neutron quadrupole deformation β₂N = 0.68, Λ hyperon β₂Λ = 0.57; neutron rms radius RN = 2.42 fm, Λ rms radius RΛ = 3.49 fm; α-α distance Dαα = 2.8 fm
- ¹⁰₂ΛsBe (Λ's in s orbital): β₂N = 0.64, β₂Λ = 0.18; RN = 2.36 fm, RΛ = 2.59 fm; Dαα = 2.6 fm
- ¹⁰₂ΛpBe (Λ's in p orbital): β₂N = 0.72, β₂Λ = 0.71; RN = 2.49 fm, RΛ = 4.27 fm; Dαα = 3.0 fm
- ⁸Be (normal isotope): β₂N = 0.67, RN = 2.43 fm, Dαα = 2.8 fm
- ²⁰Ne: β₂N = 0.43, RN = 2.82 fm; ²²₂ΛNe: β₂N = 0.40, β₂Λ = 0.16; ²⁸₈ΛNe: β₂N = 0.38, β₂Λ = 0.09
- ²⁸Si: β₂N = -0.31, RN = 3.14 fm; ³⁰₂ΛSi: β₂N = -0.22, β₂Λ = -0.12; ³⁶₈ΛSi: β₂N = 0.00, β₂Λ = 0.00
- RMF calculation gives BΛΛ = 4.24 MeV and ΔBΛΛ = 1.08 MeV for ⁶₂ΛHe; BΛΛ = 13.23 MeV and ΔBΛΛ = 0.59 MeV for ¹⁰₂ΛBe
- Last occupied Λ orbital in ¹²₄ΛBe is bound by only 1.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multi-strangeness nuclei, particularly the cluster model calculations by Bando, Ikeda, and Motoba (1983-1985) that demonstrated Λ hyperons in ¹²₄ΛBe favor localization around two α clusters, and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics study by Isaka (2011) on single-Λ hypernuclei. The results on deformation reduction in Si hyper isotopes are consistent with earlier RMF studies of single-Λ hypernuclei by Lu (2011) and Mei (2018). The paper enables future systematic investigations of ground-state structure of multi-Λ nuclei and suggests the need to include Λ-Λ and nucleon-Λ pairing correlations in beyond-mean-field calculations.
