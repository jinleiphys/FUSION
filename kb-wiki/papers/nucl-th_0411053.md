# Predicting the single-proton/neutron potentials in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We discuss the one-body potentials for protons and neutrons obtained from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of neutron-rich matter, in particular their dependence upon the degree of proton/neutron asymmetry. The closely related symmetry potential is compared with empirical information from the isovector component of the nuclear optical potential.

# Predicting the single-proton/neutron potentials in asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

The single-neutron and single-proton potentials in asymmetric nuclear matter, calculated from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) theory, show a linear dependence on the asymmetry parameter and yield a symmetry potential whose energy dependence is consistent with empirical optical potential data, but the model dependence of predictions for observables sensitive to the difference between neutron and proton properties remains large.

## Method

The authors performed Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations of neutron-rich nuclear matter using the Bonn-B, Bonn-A, and Bonn-C nucleon-nucleon potentials. They computed the single-neutron and single-proton potentials as functions of momentum, asymmetry parameter, and density, and extracted the symmetry potential from the difference between neutron and proton potentials divided by twice the asymmetry. The results were compared with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations and with the empirical Lane potential from nuclear optical potential data.

## Key numbers

- Total nucleon density of 0.185 fm$^{-3}$, corresponding to a Fermi momentum of 1.4 fm$^{-1}$, used for most calculations
- Asymmetry parameter values $\alpha = (\rho_n - \rho_p)/(\rho_n + \rho_p)$ of 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6 used in momentum-dependence studies
- Empirical Lane potential parameters: $a \approx 22-34$ MeV, $b \approx 0.1-0.2$ MeV for the relation $U_{Lane} = a - bT$ where $T$ is kinetic energy
- Fixed asymmetry parameter of 0.4 used for comparison between DBHF and BHF predictions at Fermi momentum 1.4 fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the isospin-asymmetric equation of state (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2003) and neutron radii and neutron skins (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2003). It compares DBHF predictions with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Bombaci and Lombardo, 1991; Hassaneen and Muther, 2004) and with the empirical Lane potential (Lane, 1962). The results are intended to provide microscopic input for isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport models of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for experiments at the Rare Isotope Accelerator, and to constrain the isospin-dependent properties of the nuclear equation of state relevant for neutron star structure and supernova dynamics.
