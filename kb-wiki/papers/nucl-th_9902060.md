# $ρ$-mass Modification in $He^3$ - a Signal of Restoration of Chiral Symmetry or Test for Nuclear Matter Models ?

> Two recent experiments have demonstrated that the effective $ρ$-mass in nuclear medium, as extracted from the $^3He(γ, π^+ π^-)$ reaction, is substantially reduced. This has been advocated as an indication of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter. We show that even in the absence of chiral symmetry, effective mean field nuclear matter models can explain these findings quantitatively.

## Key claim

The authors show that the substantial reduction of the effective rho-meson mass in He-3, previously argued as a signature of partial restoration of chiral symmetry, can be explained quantitatively by mean field nuclear matter models that properly include the tensor coupling of the rho meson to the nucleon.

## Method

The authors used a mean field approach based on the Walecka model Lagrangian, including both vector and tensor couplings of the rho meson to the nucleon. They employed a Gaussian density distribution for He-3 fitted to reproduce the rms charge radius of 1.88 fm, and calculated the effective rho mass at the one-loop level as a function of radius. The average rho mass was then obtained by weighting the radial mass distribution with the baryon density. Three parameter sets were used: Bonn potential, QCD sum rule, and Walecka model values.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally observed drop in rho mass from the first photoproduction experiment (E_gamma = 800-1120 MeV) was 160 ± 35 MeV, giving an effective rho mass in the range 575-645 MeV
- The second experiment (E_gamma = 380-700 MeV) found an effective rho mass in the range 450-530 MeV
- The Bonn potential parameter set (g_rho = 2.63, c_rho = 6.1) yielded an average rho mass of 536 MeV
- The QCD sum rule parameter set (g_rho = 2.5 ± 0.2, c_rho = 8.0 ± 2.0) gave an average rho mass in the range 449-565 MeV
- The Walecka model parameter set (g_rho = 8.912, c_rho = 6.1) gave an average rho mass of 304 MeV

## Context

This work directly addresses the interpretation of the rho mass reduction observed by Lolos (1998) and Huber (1998) in photoproduction on He-3, which those authors had argued could not be explained by mean field models and therefore indicated chiral symmetry restoration. The authors show that previous mean field calculations by Saito (1997) had neglected the tensor coupling of the rho meson, and that including this coupling brings mean field predictions into agreement with experiment. This enables further work on full photoproduction calculations to obtain quantitative comparisons with experimental data.
