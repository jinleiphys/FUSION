# Progress in Understanding the Nuclear Equation of State at the Quark Level

> At the present time there is a lively debate within the nuclear community concerning the relevance of quark degrees of freedom in understanding nuclear structure. We outline the key issues and review the impressive progress made recently within the framework of the quark-meson coupling model. In particular, we explain in quite general terms how the modification of the internal structure of hadrons in-medium leads naturally to three- and four-body forces, or equivalently, to density dependent effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the internal quark structure of nucleons must be considered in nuclear structure because the scalar potential experienced by bound nucleons (approximately 400 MeV at saturation density) is comparable to the typical internal excitation energy of the nucleon (300-500 MeV), leading naturally to many-body forces and density-dependent effective interactions through the scalar polarizability of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors review the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which uses the MIT bag model to describe the quark structure of nucleons and calculates how the nucleon's effective mass depends non-linearly on the applied scalar field via a scalar polarizability parameter d. They then show how expanding the QMC energy functional around zero scalar field yields an effective Skyrme-type force with three- and four-body terms, and later derive a density-dependent effective interaction without expansion that can be used in non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations for finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The scalar field strength in QHD at saturation density ρ₀ is approximately 400 MeV
- The typical internal excitation energy of the nucleon is 300 MeV (spin excitations) to 500 MeV (orbital excitations)
- The scalar polarizability d in the MIT bag model is d = +0.22R, where R is the bag radius
- The coefficients of the SkIII force derived from QMC agree at the 10% level with the phenomenological SkIII force
- The SkM force fitted to the QMC form over density range (0, ρ₀) shows agreement at the 10-20% level
- The QMC model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of about 2.1 M⊙ with a central density of approximately 6ρ₀
- The non-relativistic approximation leads to an error of almost 50% in the velocity of sound at just 2ρ₀
- Two versions of the QMC model give incompressibilities of 340 MeV (QMC700) and 256 MeV (QMCπ4)
- The spin-orbit splitting for ¹⁶O 1p₁/₂-1p₃/₂ neutrons: experimental 6.10 MeV, QMC 6.01 MeV
- The spin-orbit splitting for ⁴⁰Ca 1d₃/₂-1d₅/₂ neutrons: experimental 6.15 MeV, QMC 6.41 MeV
- The spin-orbit splitting for ⁴⁰Ca 1d₃/₂-1d₅/₂ protons: experimental 6.00 MeV, QMC 6.24 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model originally developed by Guichon (1987) and reviewed by Saito (2005), providing a microscopic connection between quark degrees of freedom and commonly used effective nuclear forces like the Skyrme force (Vautherin 1971). It contrasts with traditional approaches based on nucleon-nucleon potentials such as the Paris potential (Cottingham 1973), Bonn potential (Machleidt 1987), Nijmegen potential (de Swart 1995), and Argonne 18 potential (Wiringa 1994), as well as chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2004), none of which incorporate the modification of nucleon internal structure in-medium. The results enable calculations of the nuclear equation of state for neutron stars, including predictions for hyperon thresholds and maximum neutron star mass, and connect to the EMC effect (Aubert 1983) through covariant quark models like the NJL model (Bentz 2001).
