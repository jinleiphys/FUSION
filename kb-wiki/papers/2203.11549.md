# Multicomponent relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation

> We derive multicomponent relativistic second-order dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equations for a reactive mixture of $N_{\text{spec}}$ particle species with $N_q$ intrinsic quantum numbers (e.g. electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness) using the method of moments. We obtain the continuity equations for multiple conserved charges as well as the conservation equations for the total energy and momentum in the single-fluid approximation. These $4+N_q$ conservation laws are closed by deriving the second-order equations of motion for the dissipative quantities in the $(10+4N_q)$-moment approximation. The resulting fluid-dynamical equations are formally similar to those of a single-component system, but feature different thermodynamic relations and transport coefficients. We derive general relations for all transport coefficients and compute them explicitly in the ultrarelativistic limit.

## Key claim

The authors derive multicomponent relativistic second-order dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation for a reactive mixture of particle species with multiple conserved quantum numbers, using the method of moments; the resulting equations are formally similar to single-component systems but feature different thermodynamic relations and transport coefficients that contain the microscopic interactions of all components.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a reactive mixture of \(N_{\text{spec}}\) particle species with binary inelastic collisions, where the chemical potential of each species is expressed in terms of \(N_q\) conserved quantum number chemical potentials (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness). They apply the method of moments, generalizing the single-component approach of Denicol et al. (2012), and sum the dynamical equations for individual species to obtain a "single-fluid" description. The \(4+N_q\) conservation laws (energy-momentum plus \(N_q\) conserved charges) are closed by deriving second-order equations of motion for dissipative quantities in the \((10+4N_q)\)-moment approximation, with transport coefficients computed explicitly in the ultrarelativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- The conservation laws consist of \(4+N_q\) equations, where \(N_q\) is the number of conserved quantum numbers (e.g., 3 for baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness).
- The dissipative equations are derived in the \((10+4N_q)\)-moment approximation, which for a single-component system (\(N_{\text{spec}} = N_q = 1\)) reduces to the 14-moment approximation of Denicol et al. (2012).
- The thermodynamic response matrix \(\mathcal{T}^{-1}\) has dimensions \((1+N_q) \times (1+N_q)\), with explicit entries such as \(-J_{30}\) for the \(\partial e/\partial\beta\) component and \(J_{10}^{qq'}\) for \(\partial n_q/\partial\alpha_{q'}\).
- In the ultrarelativistic limit, all transport coefficients are computed explicitly (detailed expressions provided in appendices).

## Context

This work extends the single-component relativistic second-order fluid dynamics of Israel and Stewart (1979) and the method-of-moments derivation by Denicol et al. (2012) to multicomponent mixtures with multiple conserved charges. It generalizes earlier multicomponent approaches by Prakash et al. (1993), Monnai and Hirano (2010), and Kikuchi et al. (2015), incorporating additional second-order terms and providing explicit transport coefficients. The results enable more realistic fluid-dynamical modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where hadronic matter carries multiple conserved quantum numbers and the coupling between diffusion currents for different charges is important, as shown in prior work by Greif et al. (2017), Fotakis et al. (2019, 2021), and Rose et al. (2020).
