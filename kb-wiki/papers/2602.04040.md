# Emergent equilibrium-like yields from nonequilibrium cascade dynamics

> We study nonequilibrium cascades in which fragile bound or coherent structures are formed through intermediate states rather than by direct equilibration. Motivated by light-nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and by Bose--Einstein condensation in cosmological settings, we analyze such processes within the Schwinger--Keldysh real-time formalism. We show that commonly used rate equations can be understood as a controlled Markovian approximation obtained by integrating out intermediate reservoirs in an underlying multi-component nonequilibrium dynamics. When the finite lifetime of these reservoirs is retained, non-Markovian memory effects naturally appear, leading to delayed and history-dependent formation dynamics. The associated memory time provides a quantitative criterion for the validity of reduced, rate-based descriptions far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that equilibrium-like final yields in nonequilibrium cascade dynamics arise as a consequence of finite-lifetime intermediate reservoirs and memory effects, rather than from genuine thermal equilibration, and that commonly used rate equations are valid only when the reservoir lifetime is short compared to macroscopic evolution timescales.

## Method

The author embeds the cascade picture within the Schwinger-Keldysh real-time field-theoretic framework, deriving Kadanoff-Baym equations that contain explicit memory kernels through time-convolution integrals. By performing a Wigner transformation, gradient expansion, and quasiparticle approximation, the author shows that the Markovian limit corresponds to approximating the self-energy as local in time, yielding reduced rate equations. The intermediate reservoir is then formally eliminated, producing integro-differential equations whose memory kernels encode the finite reservoir lifetime, and the Markovian limit is recovered when the correlation time is much shorter than the macroscopic evolution time.

## Key numbers

- The intermediate reservoir lifetime in heavy-ion collisions is identified as the Delta resonance lifetime, tau_Delta ~ Gamma_Delta^{-1}, providing a natural criterion for Markovian validity in terms of the ratio tau/tau_exp
- The memory kernel from Delta decay takes the exponential form K_Delta(t-t') = Gamma_Delta * exp(-Gamma_Delta (t-t')) * theta(t-t')
- The Markovian limit corresponds to Gamma_Delta -> infinity, where K_Delta(t-t') approaches a delta function

## Context

This work extends the author's previous rate-equation descriptions of light-nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions (Fukuyama, prior work) and cosmological Bose-Einstein condensation (FMT2008, FM2009) by placing them on a systematic Schwinger-Keldysh foundation. The paper connects two seemingly disparate systems, Delta-mediated deuteron formation in heavy-ion collisions and condensate formation in scalar-field dark matter cosmology, showing they share the same structural feature of an intermediate nonequilibrium reservoir. The analysis enables quantitative assessment of when reduced rate equations are reliable and identifies non-Markovian signatures such as delayed formation and non-monotonic behavior of the coalescence parameter B_2 as targets for future experimental studies.
