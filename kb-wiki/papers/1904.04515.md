# Polarization observables for elastic electron scattering off a moving nucleon

> General expressions for all parity-conserving polarization observables of elastic electron-nucleon scattering in the one-photon exchange approximation are derived for a general frame of reference, i.e.\ not assumming scattering off a nucleon at rest and not specializing to a specific system of coordinates. Essentially, the given expressions are also valid for the inverse process, i.e.\ nucleon scattering off electrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for all parity-conserving polarization observables in elastic electron-nucleon scattering within the one-photon exchange approximation, without assuming the nucleon is at rest and without specializing to a particular coordinate system, making the results valid for any reference frame and also applicable to the inverse process of nucleon scattering off electrons.

## Method

The author defines any observable as a trace over spin degrees of freedom of a quadratic form in the T-matrix elements with associated density matrices, then separates the trace into a contraction of a lepton tensor and a hadron tensor. Explicit forms for the lepton and hadron tensors are derived for four cases: both initial and final particles unpolarized, one initial or final particle polarized, and both particles polarized, using the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M for the nucleon current. The resulting expressions are then applied to compute the unpolarized cross section, beam-target asymmetries, polarization transfer from initial to final particles, and spin correlations between outgoing particles.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the ratios of transverse and longitudinal polarization components of the emitted proton due to initial nucleon motion was up to 20% at high missing momentum, although the correction in the double ratio was only a few percent (from the Introduction, citing experiments on quasi-elastic scattering).
- The kinematic factor F_kin is given by an expression involving m_e, m_N, k', Q^2, and k·p, with a high-energy limit where g(p,k,k') tends to zero (Eq. 5 and 6).
- The unpolarized differential cross section in the high-energy limit for a nucleon at rest reduces to the standard form: dσ_0/dΩ_e = (α^2 k'^2)/(Q^2 k^2) [ (G_E^2 + τ G_M^2)/(1+τ) cot^2(θ_e/2) + 2τ G_M^2 ] (Eq. 27).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of polarization observables in elastic electron-proton scattering, such as those by Bincer (1957), Ritus (1957), and Akhiezer (1968), which considered specific reference frames and coordinate systems. It was motivated by recent experiments on quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering (Ya17, Pa19) that measured polarization transfer from longitudinally polarized electrons to emitted protons, where the bound proton's Fermi motion caused corrections up to 20%. The general frame-independent expressions enable easy evaluation of any observable for arbitrary reference frames and coordinate systems, and can be implemented into a computer code for comparison with experimental data.
