# Phase transitions in the $sdg$ interacting boson model

> A geometric analysis of the $sdg$ interacting boson model is performed. A coherent-state is used in terms of three types of deformation: axial quadrupole ($β_2$), axial hexadecapole ($β_4$) and triaxial ($γ_2$). The phase-transitional structure is established for a schematic $sdg$ hamiltonian which is intermediate between four dynamical symmetries of U(15), namely the spherical ${\rm U}(5)\otimes{\rm U}(9)$, the (prolate and oblate) deformed ${\rm SU}_\pm(3)$ and the $γ_2$-soft SO(15) limits. For realistic choices of the hamiltonian parameters the resulting phase diagram has properties close to what is obtained in the $sd$ version of the model and, in particular, no transition towards a stable triaxial shape is found.

## Key claim
For realistic choices of the Hamiltonian parameters, the phase diagram of the $sdg$ interacting boson model has properties close to those obtained in the simpler $sd$ version of the model, and in particular no transition towards a stable triaxial shape is found, despite the inclusion of hexadecapole degrees of freedom.

## Method
A geometric analysis of the $sdg$ interacting boson model was performed using a coherent-state formalism with three deformation parameters: axial quadrupole ($\beta_2$), axial hexadecapole ($\beta_4$), and triaxial ($\gamma_2$). A schematic Hamiltonian was constructed that interpolates between four dynamical symmetries of U(15): the spherical ${\rm U}(5)\otimes{\rm U}(9)$, the prolate and oblate deformed ${\rm SU}_\pm(3)$, and the $\gamma_2$-soft SO(15) limits. The phase-transitional structure was established by finding degenerate critical points of the potential energy surface, where the determinant of the stability matrix vanishes, as functions of the control parameters $\xi$, $\chi$, and $\zeta$.

## Key numbers
- The spherical minimum at $\beta_2^*=\beta_4^*=0$ continuously evolves into quadrupole-deformed minima at $\beta_2^*=\pm\sqrt{2\xi-1}$, independent of $\zeta$, with the phase transition occurring at $\xi=1/2$ (or $\eta=1/4$).
- For $\chi=0$, the spherical saddle point at $\beta_2^*=\beta_4^*=0$ evolves into hexadecapole-deformed critical points at $\beta_4^*=\pm\sqrt{[\xi(2-\zeta)-1]/(1-\xi\zeta)}$ at $\xi=1/(2-\zeta)$ (or $\eta=r/4$).
- The potential has a minimum for $\xi<1/2$, a saddle point for $1/2<\xi<1/(2-\zeta)$, and a maximum for $\xi>1/(2-\zeta)$, all at $\beta_2^*=\beta_4^*=0$, for the U(5)$\otimes$U(9) to SO(15) transition with $\chi=0$.

## Context
This work extends the phase-transitional analysis of the interacting boson model from the $sd$-IBM, where the phase diagram was established by Lopez-Moreno and Castanos (1996) and earlier by Feng et al. (1981), to the $sdg$-IBM which includes hexadecapole deformations. The study builds on the geometric interpretation of the IBM developed by Dieperink et al. (1980), Ginocchio (1980), and Bohr (1980), and on the coherent-state method proposed by Gilmore (1979). The results enable future investigations of whether the inclusion of $g$ bosons can produce phase transitions to stable triaxial shapes, which the present work finds absent for realistic parameter choices.
