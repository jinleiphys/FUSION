# Chiral dynamics of few-nucleon systems

> 8 pages, 6 figures, invited talk, International Nuclear Physics Conference (INPC 2007), Tokyo, Japan, June 3-8, 2007

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematically improvable framework for nuclear forces, but at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) the theoretical uncertainty in three-nucleon scattering is already rather large at moderate energies, and certain observables (e.g., the symmetric space-star breakup configuration) show large discrepancies with data even at very low energy.

## Method
The author derives nuclear potentials from the most general effective Lagrangian consistent with QCD's chiral symmetry, using the chiral expansion parameter \(Q/\Lambda\) to organize contributions. Two-nucleon forces (2NF) are worked out to N³LO, three-nucleon forces (3NF) to N²LO, and the leading four-nucleon force (4NF) at N³LO is derived via unitary transformation. Lattice simulations are performed at lowest chiral order using Monte Carlo sampling on a Euclidean lattice with hadronic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- The \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation energy is \(\Delta m = 293\) MeV.
- In the delta-less EFT, the low-energy constants are \(c_3 = -3.87\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(c_4 = 2.89\) GeV\(^{-1}\); with explicit \(\Delta\)'s (using \(h_A = 1.34\)), they become \(c_3 = -0.79\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(c_4 = 1.33\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The triton binding energy from lattice simulations at leading order is \(E_{^3H} = -8.9(2)\) MeV, within 5% of the experimental value.
- The \(\alpha\)-particle binding energy from the same lattice simulations is \(E_{^4He} = -21.5(2)\) MeV, about 25% smaller in magnitude than the experimental value of \(-28.296\) MeV.
- The leading 4NF expectation values are of the order of a few hundred keV, with strong cancellations between individual contributions.

## Context
This talk reviews the status of chiral EFT for few-nucleon systems, extending the foundational work of Weinberg (1990) and building on N³LO 2NF calculations by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2004). The N²LO 3NF results are compared with phenomenological potentials such as CD Bonn 2000 (Machleidt, 2000) combined with the TM99 3NF (Coon, 2001) and coupled-channel calculations including explicit \(\Delta\) and Coulomb (Deltuva, 2005). The lattice simulations extend the pionless EFT lattice work of Borasoy et al. (2005) to chiral EFT. The next steps include extending 3NF studies to N³LO, incorporating \(\Delta\)-isobar effects into 3NF and isospin-breaking interactions, and generalizing lattice simulations to higher orders and scattering observables.
