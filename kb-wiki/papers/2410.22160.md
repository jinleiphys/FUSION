# A Gaussian Process Generative Model for QCD Equation of State

> We develop a generative model for the nuclear matter equation of state at zero net baryon density using the Gaussian Process Regression method. We impose first-principles theoretical constraints from lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas at high- and low-temperature regions, respectively. By allowing the trained Gaussian Process Regression model to vary freely near the phase transition region, we generate random smooth cross-over equations of state with different speeds of sound that do not rely on specific parameterizations. We explore a collection of experimental observable dependencies on the generated equations of state, which paves the groundwork for future Bayesian inference studies to use experimental measurements from relativistic heavy-ion collisions to constrain the nuclear matter equation of state.

# A Gaussian Process Generative Model for QCD Equation of State

## Key claim

The authors develop a Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) generative model that produces random smooth cross-over equations of state at zero net baryon density without relying on specific parameterizations, but the generated EOS sets are constrained by first-principles lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas results at high and low temperatures respectively, with free variation only permitted in the intermediate phase transition region.

## Method

The authors train a Gaussian Process Regression model on scaled pressure ln(P/T⁴) as a function of ln(T/T₀) using low-temperature hadron resonance gas data (T ≤ 0.13 GeV) and high-temperature lattice QCD data (T ≥ 0.7 GeV) from HotQCD (2014). They sample random instances from the trained GPR, compute entropy and speed of sound via thermodynamic derivatives, and apply filters enforcing positivity of pressure derivatives, compressibility, and causality (0 ≤ c_s² < 1/2). Two extreme EOS sets are selected and deployed in the iEBE-MUSIC framework for (2+1)D boost-invariant simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV with IP-Glasma initial conditions and UrQMD hadronic afterburner.

## Key numbers

- The optimized GPR kernel length scale is l = 0.487, corresponding to a correlation length of approximately 120 MeV at T = 200 MeV
- Anchor points for selected EOS sets: (T, P/T⁴) = (0.25 GeV, 0.75) for EOS 1 and (0.25 GeV, 3.5) for EOS 2
- The switching energy density for particlization is e_sw = 0.18 GeV/fm³
- The hydrodynamic starting time is τ_hydro = 0.4 fm/c
- The linearized causality condition with C_η = 5 and C_ζ = 5 constrains c_s² ≤ 8/15 (approximately 0.533)
- The maximum speed of sound squared allowed in the generator is c_s,max² = 1/2
- Initial total entropies from the three EOS differ by approximately 15%
- EOS 1 produces a factor of 3 more thermal photons than the other two EOS sets in 0-20% centrality Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference approach to QCD equation of state determination pioneered by Pratt (2015) and the use of Gaussian Processes for neutron star EOS modeling by Landry (2018) and Mroczek (2023). The method provides a non-parametric alternative to the Taylor expansion schemes used in most phenomenological studies at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, such as those by Monnai (2019) and Noronha-Hostler (2019). The results demonstrate that hadronic observables (mean pT, anisotropic flow, HBT radii) are sensitive to the speed of sound in the crossover temperature region T ∈ [0.15, 0.25] GeV, while thermal photon yields directly probe the temperature profile of the equation of state. This work enables future Bayesian inference studies to constrain the QCD equation of state using experimental measurements from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the formulation extendable to finite baryon density.
