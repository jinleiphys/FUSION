# Sensitivity of Neutron Star Observables to Transition Density in Hybrid Equation-of-State Models

> We investigate how the transition density \(ρ_{tr}\) affects hybrid constructions of the neutron-star equation of state (EoS) in which a nucleonic description at low densities is matched to a model-agnostic high-density extension based on a speed-of-sound parametrization. Using four representative nucleonic models--Taylor expansion, \(\frac{n}{3}\) expansion, Skyrme, and relativistic mean-field--built from identical nuclear matter parameters, we isolate the impact of the low-density EoS and the transition density on neutron star observables. We find that, within the present smooth-matching prescription, neutron star properties such as radii and tidal deformabilities retain significant sensitivity to the choice of low-density EoS for commonly adopted transition densities around \(ρ_{tr} \approx 2ρ_0\), even when the same high-density parametrization is employed. This residual dependence arises from differences in the matching conditions at \(ρ_{tr}\), which propagate into the high-density extension, so different low-density inputs lead to different effective high-density EoSs. These findings are robust across two distinct speed-of-sound parametrizations. Quantitatively, the model spread in radius and tidal deformability at $1.4\,M_\odot$ exceeds the current observational uncertainty by factors of $\sim 1.8$ and $\sim 1.4$ at $ρ_{\mathrm{tr}} \approx 2ρ_0$, whereas these factors reduce to $\sim 1.05$ and $\sim 0.4$ at $ρ_{\mathrm{tr}} = ρ_0$. Lowering the transition density, therefore, systematically diminishes the spread among models and leads to more consistent predictions. Our results demonstrate that the widely used choice \(ρ_{tr} \approx 2ρ_0\) does not guarantee model independence in hybrid EoS constructions, and should be treated as an explicit source of systematic uncertainty when inferring dense matter properties from neutron star observations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the widely used choice of transition density \(\rho_{tr} \approx 2\rho_0\) in hybrid neutron-star equation-of-state constructions does not guarantee model independence; the residual sensitivity of neutron star observables to the low-density equation of state at this transition density can exceed current observational uncertainties by factors of \(\sim 1.8\) for radius and \(\sim 1.4\) for tidal deformability at \(1.4\,M_\odot\).

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by matching four nucleonic models (Taylor expansion, \(n/3\) expansion, Skyrme, and relativistic mean-field), all built from identical nuclear matter parameters, to a model-agnostic high-density extension based on a speed-of-sound parametrization at systematically varied transition densities \(\rho_{tr} = \rho_0,\; 1.5\rho_0,\; 2\rho_0\). The high-density equation of state was built iteratively from the matching point using the speed-of-sound parametrization, and neutron star properties were computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The robustness of results was tested across two distinct speed-of-sound parametrization sets differing in peak height, location, and width.

## Key numbers

- At \(\rho_{tr} \approx 2\rho_0\), the model spread in radius at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) exceeds current observational uncertainty by a factor of \(\sim 1.8\); at \(\rho_{tr} = \rho_0\), this factor reduces to \(\sim 1.05\).
- At \(\rho_{tr} \approx 2\rho_0\), the model spread in tidal deformability at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) exceeds current observational uncertainty by a factor of \(\sim 1.4\); at \(\rho_{tr} = \rho_0\), this factor reduces to \(\sim 0.4\).
- For \(\rho_{tr} = 2\rho_0\) with Set1 parameters, the radius at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) ranges from 11.08 km (Taylor) to 12.52 km (Skyrme), and the tidal deformability at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) ranges from 240.2 (Taylor) to 512.54 (Skyrme).
- For \(\rho_{tr} = \rho_0\) with Set1 parameters, the radius at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) ranges from 11.58 km (Taylor) to 12.42 km (RMF), and the tidal deformability at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) ranges from 350.94 (\(n/3\)) to 435.94 (RMF).
- The maximum mass ranges from \(1.92\,M_\odot\) (Taylor at \(\rho_{tr} = 2\rho_0\)) to \(2.33\,M_\odot\) (Skyrme at \(\rho_{tr} = 2\rho_0\)).

## Context

This work extends the speed-of-sound parametrization approach of Tews (2018) by isolating the transition density as an independent source of systematic uncertainty, which previous Bayesian analyses by Capano (2019), Dietrich (2020), and Venneti (2026) had entangled with simultaneous variations in nuclear matter parameters and observational inputs. The findings enable future inference frameworks that treat \(\rho_{tr}\) as a free parameter to be marginalized over, rather than fixed a priori, and suggest that transition densities in the range \(\rho_0 \lesssim \rho_{tr} \lesssim 1.5\rho_0\) offer a better balance between model independence and physical reliability than the commonly adopted \(\rho_{tr} \approx 2\rho_0\).
