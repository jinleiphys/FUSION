# Bose-Einstein Correlations from Random Walk Models

> We argue that strong final state rescattering among the secondary particles created in relativistic heavy ion collisions is essential to understand the measured Bose-Einstein correlations. The recently suggested ``random walk models'' which contain only initial state scattering are unable to reproduce the measured magnitude and K_\perp-dependence of R_\perp in Pb+Pb collisions and the increase of R_l with increasing size of the collision system.

## Key claim

The Random Walk Model (RWM) and LEXUS models, which attempt to describe nucleus-nucleus collisions as a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, fail to reproduce the measured Bose-Einstein correlation data because they lack final state rescattering among produced hadrons; the authors argue that strong final state rescattering is essential, not just initial state scattering.

## Method

The authors reconstructed the emission function for the RWM from its momentum-space formulation, then calculated the transverse HBT radius R_perp using a simplified model that fixed fireball longitudinal rapidity to zero in the longitudinally co-moving system and introduced an r-dependent transverse broadening parameter. They also derived an expression for the longitudinal radius parameter R_l in terms of the distribution of nucleon-nucleon collision points, using a geometric approximation where nuclei are replaced by cylinders. The calculated R_perp and R_l values were compared to experimental data from NA44, NA49, and NA35 collaborations for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at 160-200 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions, the calculated R_perp values underpredicted the experimental data which give R_perp approximately 6 fm at small M_perp (NA49 Collaboration); the model gave values consistent with a transversally non-expanding source.
- For S+S collisions, the experimental data give R_perp approximately 4 fm (NA35, NA44 Collaborations), again underpredicted by the model.
- The longitudinal radius parameter from nucleon-nucleon collisions was estimated as R_{l,s_c} approximately 1.7 fm, based on data from NA22 and other experiments.
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the geometric approximation gave R_{l,sigma}^2 = (1/20)*(1/gamma^2)*(R_A^2 + R_B^2) with gamma approximately 10 at CERN/SPS energies, underpredicting the experimental R_l^2 by a factor of 10 after adding the R_{l,s_c}^2 contribution of about 3 fm^2.
- The parameter delta for the random walk was recalculated as delta = 0.217, using delta_{pW} = 0.3 from Leonidov, Nardi, Satz (1997).

## Context

This work extends the critique of the RWM and LEXUS models beyond single-particle spectra, which had been tested against data by Esumi, Heinz, Xu (1997) and Alam, Cleymans, Redlich, Satz (1997), to two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations. The authors show that these models, proposed by Leonidov, Nardi, Satz (1997) and Jeon, Kapusta (1997), cannot reproduce the transverse momentum dependence of R_perp or the system-size dependence of R_l, in contrast to hydrodynamic parametrizations by Chapman, Scotto, Heinz (1995) and Chapman, Nix, Heinz (1995) which can accommodate the data through collective expansion and longer fireball lifetimes (tau_0 approximately 8 fm/c for Pb+Pb). The results enable future work to focus on models with extensive final state rescattering, such as Monte Carlo transport models and hydrodynamic source parametrizations.
