# Explanation of more effective effective field theory

> We show that the so-called more effective effective field theory (MEEFT) is essentially equivalent to the proper nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) in describing low-energy electroweak processes in nuclei. A key to understanding their equivalence is the relation between a NEFT-based NN-potential and a phenomenological potential through the Wilsonian renormalization group equation.We also discuss an important advantage of MEEFT.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the so-called "more effective effective field theory" (MEEFT) is essentially equivalent to the proper nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) for describing low-energy electroweak processes in nuclei, with the equivalence relying on the relation between a NEFT-based NN-potential and a phenomenological potential through the Wilsonian renormalization group equation.

## Method

The author demonstrates the equivalence by comparing nuclear matrix elements evaluated in proper NEFT (using a NEFT-derived potential and current operators) with those evaluated in MEEFT (using a phenomenological potential and NEFT-derived current operators with a projection operator). The key step is using the Wilsonian renormalization group equation to relate the NEFT-based potential to the model-space potential obtained from the phenomenological potential, establishing that the wave functions satisfy |Ψ_EFT⟩ ≈ η|Ψ_ph⟩, where η projects onto the model space with cutoff Λ.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff Λ for the model space is set to 200 MeV in the deuteron wave function comparison (Figure 1).
- The deuteron wave function comparison shows that the S-wave and D-wave radial components from V_M (model-space potential) and V_EFT (NEFT-based potential) are in close agreement at Λ = 200 MeV, with V_EFT including one-pion-exchange potential plus contact interactions with zero and two derivatives (dashed curve) and additionally with four derivatives (dotted curve).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Nakamura, 2004) on the relation between NEFT-based potentials and phenomenological potentials via the Wilsonian renormalization group equation. It provides a formal foundation for MEEFT, which was developed by Park et al. (various references) and has been applied to reactions such as pp → de⁺ν_e, ³He p → ⁴He e⁺ν_e, ν_e d → e⁻ pp, and ν d → ν pn. The paper enables a rigorous understanding of why the hybrid MEEFT procedure, which uses phenomenological wave functions with NEFT current operators, yields results equivalent to proper NEFT calculations.
