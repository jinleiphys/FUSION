# Observation of Narrow $N^+(1685)$ and $N^0(1685)$ Resonances in $γN \to ηπN$ Reactions

> Observation of a narrow structure at $W\sim 1.68$ GeV in the excitation functions of some photon- and pion-induced reactions may signal a new narrow isospin-1/2 $N(1685)$ resonance. New data on the $γN \to πηN$ reactions from GRAAL seems to reveal the signals of both $N^+(1685)$ and $N^0(1685)$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper reports the observation of narrow peak-like structures in the $\eta p$ and $\eta n$ invariant mass spectra from $\gamma N \to \pi \eta N$ reactions, which the authors interpret as evidence for both the charged $N^+(1685)$ and neutral $N^0(1685)$ members of a narrow isospin-1/2 resonance, though they note that alternative explanations (interference of known wide resonances or cusp effects) cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The GRAAL facility used a back-scattered laser photon beam on 6.04 GeV electrons to produce photons in the energy range 0.55–1.5 GeV, with data selected from 1.4–1.5 GeV near the $\gamma N \to \pi N(1685)$ threshold. Events from $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$, $\gamma p \to \pi^+ \eta n$, $\gamma n \to \pi^0 \eta n$, and $\gamma n \to \pi^- \eta p$ were identified by reconstructing $\eta$ and $\pi^0$ from two-photon decays in the BGO Ball, detecting recoil nucleons in forward detectors with time-of-flight resolution $\sigma_{TOF}\sim 250$ psec, and applying kinematic cuts on coplanarity and missing/invariant masses to isolate the $\gamma N \to \pi R_{(\eta N)}$ channel.

## Key numbers

- Peak position of the structure: $M = 1678 \pm 0.8_{\text{stat}} \pm 10_{\text{syst}}$ MeV
- Statistical significance of the peak over background: $4.6\sigma$ (log likelihood ratio of Gaussian+polynomial fit versus polynomial-only fit)
- Width of the structure: $\Gamma \sim 10$ MeV (noted as possibly affected by corrections)
- Photon energy range selected for analysis: $E_\gamma = 1.4 - 1.5$ GeV
- Photon energy resolution: $\frac{3\%}{E_\gamma(\text{GeV})}$ in the BGO Ball
- Time-of-flight resolution for recoil nucleons: $\sigma_{TOF} \sim 250$ psec
- Systematic uncertainty in peak position from calibration: $\sim 10$ MeV (from a 20 psec time-of-flight error causing $\sim 10$ MeV shift)

## Context

This work extends previous observations of a narrow structure at $W\sim 1.68$ GeV in the $\gamma n \to \eta n$ excitation function (the "neutron anomaly") reported by GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, LNS, and A2@MAMI (Kuznetsov et al. 2007, Krusche et al. 2007, Kashevarov et al. 2011), and in Compton scattering on the neutron (Kuznetsov et al. 2011). The results support the Chiral Soliton Model prediction of a narrow isospin-1/2 resonance as the second member of the exotic anti-decuplet (Diakonov et al. 1997), but the authors note that alternative interpretations in terms of interference of known wide resonances (Anisovich et al. 2012) or cusp effects (Doring et al. 2011) remain possible. The observation enables future dedicated experiments at other facilities to confirm the resonance and to search for a second predicted structure at $W\sim 1.726$ GeV.
