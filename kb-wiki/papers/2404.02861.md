# Pre-equilibrium Photon and Dilepton Production

> We use QCD kinetic theory to compute photon and dilepton production in the chemically equilibrating out-of-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma created in the early stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We derive universal scaling functions for the pre-equilibrium spectra of photons and dileptons. These scaling functions can be used to make realistic predictions for the pre-equilibrium emission and consequently establish the significance of the pre-equilibrium phase for the production of electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive universal scaling functions for pre-equilibrium photon and dilepton spectra that depend only on the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) and a local entropy scale, but caution that the scaling loses accuracy at large momentum/mass because those particles are produced at very early times (\(\tilde{w} \lesssim 0.15\)) when the system is sensitive to the initial quark-gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors use QCD kinetic theory with a longitudinally boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous system to compute all leading-order processes for quarks, gluons, photons, and dileptons, including infrared regularization and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. They derive dimensionless scaling functions \(\mathcal{N}_\gamma\) and \(\mathcal{N}_\text{dilepton}\) by expressing the time-integrated spectra in terms of the equilibrium time \(\tau_\text{eq}\) and equilibrium temperature \(T_\text{eq}\), which are related to the specific shear viscosity and the local entropy density. The scaling is validated by performing QCD kinetic theory calculations for different coupling strengths \(\lambda = 4\pi N_c \alpha_s\) and integrating the spectra up to \(\tilde{w}=1.0\), where an effective hydrodynamic description becomes applicable.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium time and temperature are related by \(\tau_\text{eq} T_\text{eq} \approx 4\pi \eta/s\).
- The equilibrium time scales as \(\tau_\text{eq} \approx (T\tau^{1/3})_\infty^{-3/2} (4\pi\eta/s)^{3/2}\).
- The equilibrium temperature scales as \(T_\text{eq} \approx (T\tau^{1/3})_\infty^{3/2} (4\pi\eta/s)^{-1/2}\).
- The effective number of bosonic degrees of freedom of the QGP is \(\nu_\text{eff} \approx 32\).
- The scaling for dileptons is given by \(dN_\text{dilepton}/(d^2\mathbf{x}_T M dM dy_q) = (\eta/s)^2 \mathcal{N}_\text{dilepton}(\bar{M})\), where the rescaled mass is \(\bar{M} = \sqrt{\eta/s} M / (T\tau^{1/3})_\infty^{3/2}\).
- The scaling for photons is given by \(dN_\gamma/(d^2\mathbf{x}_T d^2\mathbf{p}_T dy_p) = (\eta/s)^2 \tilde{C}_\gamma^\text{ideal} \mathcal{N}_\gamma(\bar{\mathbf{p}}_T)\), where the rescaled momentum is \(\bar{\mathbf{p}}_T = \sqrt{\eta/s} \mathbf{p}_T / (T\tau^{1/3})_\infty^{3/2}\).
- The scaling holds for spectra integrated up to \(\tilde{w}=1.0\), but breaks down for \(\tilde{w} \lesssim 0.15\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pre-equilibrium photon production by Garcia-Montero et al. (2023) to include dileptons, and builds on the QCD kinetic theory framework of Arnold et al. (2002) and the hydrodynamization description of Kurkela and collaborators (2018). The universal scaling functions enable event-by-event calculations of pre-equilibrium electromagnetic emission in heavy-ion collisions, allowing direct comparison with other sources of photons and dileptons. The authors note that the formalism could be extended to other pre-equilibrium processes such as heavy-flavor production, heavy-quark diffusion, or jet energy loss.
