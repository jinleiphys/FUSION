# Single-channel fits and K-matrix constraints

> A K-matrix formalism is used to relate single-channel and multi-channel fits. We show how the single-channel formalism changes as new hadronic channels become accessible. These relations are compared to those commonly used to fit pseudoscalar meson photoproduction data.

# Single-channel fits and K-matrix constraints

## Key claim

The paper shows that single-channel fits to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction data can be related to multi-channel K-matrix analyses through a set of derived formulas, but the simple phase constraint of Watson's theorem (which applies only below the two-pion production threshold) must be replaced by more complicated expressions involving additional hadronic T-matrix terms once inelastic channels open. The derived forms remain finite at K-matrix poles even when individual terms diverge, provided the K-matrix residues are factorizable.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the photoproduction T-matrix element Tγπ by starting from a 2×2 hadronic K-matrix (πN and πΔ channels) and then extending to a 3×3 K-matrix that includes the photon channel, using the relation T = K(1 − iK)−1. The derivation is generalized to an arbitrary number of hadronic channels, showing that each new channel mN adds a term proportional to i(Kγm − (KγΔ/KπΔ)Kπm) Tπm. The resulting formulas are compared to the single-channel parametrizations used in the SAID and MAID analyses of pion photoproduction data.

## Key numbers

- The paper discusses the Δ(1232) resonance region as the domain where Watson's theorem provides a strong constraint on single-channel fits.
- For many established resonances, the πN branching fractions are only 10–30%, making the background term A in Eq. (24) potentially large.

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation by Workman (VPI90) that generalized Watson's theorem (Watson, 1954) beyond the two-pion production threshold by assuming dominance of a single additional πΔ channel. The paper provides a framework for comparing single-channel fits (such as the SAID analysis of Arndt et al. and the MAID parametrization) with multi-channel K-matrix analyses, enabling more direct comparisons between results from different fitting approaches. The formalism is also applied to eta photoproduction in the N(1535) channel, where the author notes that early single-channel fits (Krusche, 1995) assumed resonance dominance with negligible background, while multi-channel analyses (Penner, 2002) gave different results.
