# Bimodality - a general feature of heavy ion reactions

> Recently, is has been observed that events with the {\it same} total transverse energy of light charged particles (LCP) in the quasi target region, $E_{\perp 12}^{QT}$, show two quite distinct reaction scenarios in the projectile domain: multifragmentation and residue production. This phenomenon has been dubbed "bimodality". Using Quantum Molecular Dynamics calculations we demonstrate that this observation is very general. It appears in collisions of all symmetric systems larger than Ca and at beam energies between 50 A.MeV and 600 A.MeV and is due to large fluctuations of the impact parameter for a given $E_{\perp 12}^{QT}$. Investigating in detail the $E_{\perp 12}^{QT}$ bin in which both scenarios are present, we find that neither the average fragment momenta nor the average transverse and longitudinal energies of fragments show the behavior expected from a system in statistical equilibrium, in experiment as well as in QMD simulations. On the contrary, the experimental as well as the theoretical results point towards a fast process. This observation questions the conjecture that the observed bimodality is due to the coexistence of 2 phases at a given temperature in finite systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that bimodality (the observation of two distinct reaction scenarios, multifragmentation and residue production, for the same total transverse energy of light charged particles) is a general feature of heavy ion collisions for symmetric systems larger than Ca at beam energies between 50 A.MeV and 600 A.MeV, but argues that this phenomenon is caused by large fluctuations of the impact parameter for a given transverse energy rather than by the coexistence of two phases at a given temperature in a finite system.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data from the INDRA-ALADIN collaboration for Au+Au collisions at 60 A.MeV and compared them with Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations for various symmetric systems (Au+Au, Xe+Sn, Kr+Kr, Ca+Ca) at beam energies from 60 to 150 A.MeV. They studied the properties of fragments and light charged particles in the bimodality interval of total transverse energy, examining the dependence of fragment velocities, transverse energies, and momentum tensor components on the asymmetry parameter a2, which characterizes whether events produce a single large residue or multiple fragments.

## Key numbers

- Bimodality appears in collisions of all symmetric systems larger than Ca at beam energies between 50 A.MeV and 600 A.MeV
- For Au+Au at 60 A.MeV, the bimodality interval in reduced transverse energy is 12 < E_perp_12^QT / E_0cm < 17
- The average transverse kinetic energy of fragments in the bimodality interval is approximately 35 MeV, independent of fragment mass and charge
- The transverse energy of 13.5 MeV extracted from fitting experimental data is consistent with the Goldhaber model prediction of approximately 14 MeV, not with thermal expectations
- The average transverse velocity of the heaviest fragment shows an almost constant transverse kinetic energy of 35 MeV, too large to be compatible with a thermal heat bath

## Context

This work extends the initial observation of bimodality by the INDRA and ALADIN collaboration (Tamain et al., 2006) and by other groups (Bruno et al., 2007), which had been interpreted as evidence for a first-order phase transition in finite nuclear systems as predicted by Gross (1997), Fai and collaborators (1998), and Chomaz (2004). The paper contradicts the thermal equilibrium interpretation by showing that fragment velocities follow the Morrissey systematics (Morrissey, 1989) and the Goldhaber model (Goldhaber, 1974), indicating a fast, non-equilibrium process. This finding enables future work to focus on dynamical reaction mechanisms rather than phase coexistence as the explanation for bimodality in heavy ion reactions.
