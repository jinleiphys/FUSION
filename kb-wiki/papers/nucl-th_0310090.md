# Proton-Neutron Interaction near Closed Shells

> Odd-odd nuclei around double shell closures are a direct source of information on the proton-neutron interaction between valence nucleons. We have performed shell-model calculations for doubly odd nuclei close to $^{208}$Pb, $^{132}$Sn and $^{100}$Sn using realistic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The calculated results are compared with the available experimental data, attention being focused on particle-hole and particle-particle multiplets. While a good agreement is obtained for all the nuclei considered, a detailed analysis of the matrix elements of the effective interaction shows that a stronger core-polarization contribution seems to be needed in the particle-particle case.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using realistic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn potential reproduce experimental proton-neutron multiplets in odd-odd nuclei near double shell closures, but a detailed analysis of the matrix elements shows that a stronger core-polarization contribution (by a factor of 2.6) is needed in the particle-particle case to achieve full agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for six odd-odd nuclei (²⁰⁸Bi, ²¹⁰Bi, ¹³²Sb, ¹³⁴Sb, ⁹⁸Ag, and ¹⁰²In) using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. They first constructed a low-momentum potential V_low-k with a cutoff Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹, then calculated the Q-box including all one-body and two-body diagrams up to second order in V_low-k, and finally summed the folded-diagram series using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. The calculations were performed with the OXBASH shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Bi particle-hole multiplets, discrepancies between calculated and experimental energies are well below 100 keV for almost all states.
- For ²¹⁰Bi, the calculation predicts J^π = 0⁻ as ground state with the 1⁻ state at about 150 keV excitation energy, while experimentally the 1⁻ state is the ground state with the 0⁻ state at about 50 keV.
- Multiplying the diagonal matrix elements of the core-polarization contribution V_ph by a factor of 2.6 for the πh_9/2 νg_9/2 configuration in ²¹⁰Bi produces results that "practically overlap the experimental ones."
- For ⁹⁸Ag, the largest discrepancy between calculation and experiment is 130 keV for the 5⁺ state.
- The cutoff momentum used for V_low-k is Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies by the same group (Covello, 2000) that focused on nuclei with identical valence particles or holes, now turning to nuclei with unlike valence nucleons. It revisits the classic particle-hole multiplet calculations for ²⁰⁸Bi by Kuo (1968) using the Hamada-Johnston potential, finding better agreement with the CD-Bonn potential and modern many-body methods. The results enable predictions for unstable nuclei in the ¹³²Sn and ¹⁰⁰Sn regions where experimental data remain scarce, and suggest that coupling to octupole excitations in ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹³²Sn may explain the need for enhanced core polarization in particle-particle multiplets.
