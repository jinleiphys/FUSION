# Bjorken flow in one-dimensional relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with magnetization

> We study the one-dimensional, longitudinally boost-invariant motion of an ideal fluid with infinite conductivity in the presence of a transverse magnetic field, i.e., in the ideal transverse magnetohydrodynamical limit. In an extension of our previous work Roy et al., [Phys. Lett. B 750, 45 (2015)], we consider the fluid to have a non-zero magnetization. First, we assume a constant magnetic susceptibility $χ_{m}$ and consider an ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state. For a paramagnetic fluid (i.e., with $χ_{m}>0$), the decay of the energy density slows down since the fluid gains energy from the magnetic field. For a diamagnetic fluid (i.e., with $χ_{m}<0$), the energy density decays faster because it feeds energy into the magnetic field. Furthermore, when the magnetic field is taken to be external and to decay in proper time $τ$ with a power law $\simτ^{-a}$, two distinct solutions can be found depending on the values of $a$ and $χ_m$. Finally, we also solve the ideal magnetohydrodynamical equations for one-dimensional Bjorken flow with a temperature-dependent magnetic susceptibility and a realistic equation of state given by lattice-QCD data. We find that the temperature and energy density decay more slowly because of the non-vanishing magnetization. For values of the magnetic field typical for heavy-ion collisions, this effect is, however, rather small. Only for magnetic fields which are about an order of magnitude larger than expected for heavy-ion collisions, the system is substantially reheated and the lifetime of the quark phase might be extended.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' previous work (Roy et al., 2015) on ideal transverse magnetohydrodynamic Bjorken flow by including nonzero magnetization, finding that the energy density decay slows for paramagnetic fluids (positive magnetic susceptibility) and accelerates for diamagnetic fluids (negative magnetic susceptibility); however, for magnetic field strengths typical of heavy-ion collisions the effect is small, and only for fields about an order of magnitude larger than expected does the system become substantially reheated and the quark-phase lifetime potentially extended.

## Method

The authors solve the ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamic equations for one-dimensional, longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken flow with infinite electrical conductivity and a transverse magnetic field, incorporating magnetization via a linear magnetic susceptibility. They first assume constant magnetic susceptibility with an ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state, then consider an external magnetic field decaying as a power law in proper time, and finally solve numerically with a temperature-dependent susceptibility and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state.

- The frozen-flux theorem gives B/B_0 = s/s_0 = tau_0/tau, where tau is proper time, for the Bjorken flow with transverse magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Roy et al., 2015) which considered ideal MHD Bjorken flow without magnetization and found that the energy density decay is unchanged from the non-magnetic case. The inclusion of magnetization modifies the energy density evolution through the magnetic susceptibility, with paramagnetic (chi_m > 0) and diamagnetic (chi_m < 0) cases producing opposite effects. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy-ion collisions where magnetic fields may influence the quark-gluon plasma evolution, and connect to lattice-QCD studies of magnetic susceptibility (Bali et al., 2013; Bonati et al., 2013) and the chiral magnetic effect.
