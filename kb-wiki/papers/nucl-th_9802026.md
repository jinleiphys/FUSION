# Global Transmission Coefficients in Hauser-Feshbach Calculations for Astrophysics

> The current status of optical potentials employed in the prediction of thermonuclear reaction rates for astrophysics in the Hauser-Feshbach formalism is discussed. Special emphasis is put on $α$+nucleus potentials. A novel approach for the prediction of $α$+nucleus potentials is proposed. Further experimental efforts are motivated.

# Global Transmission Coefficients in Hauser-Feshbach Calculations for Astrophysics

## Key claim
The paper proposes a novel global α+nucleus optical potential for Hauser-Feshbach calculations, but emphasizes that this potential is intended to achieve a low *average* deviation from experiment across many nuclei, not high accuracy for any specific reaction; for individual cases, fine-tuning to a particular nucleus would improve results but would sacrifice predictive power.

## Method
The author constructs a global α+nucleus optical potential using a double-folding procedure (DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction) for the real part, with a strength parameter λ determined from systematics of the volume integral per interacting particle pair. The imaginary part is parametrized using a threshold-dependent form with parameters J₀ and Δ, where the threshold E₀ is set by the energy of the first excited state and Δ is related to the level density derivative dρ/dE, thereby implicitly incorporating nuclear structure and deformation effects.

## Key numbers
- The volume integral of the real part J_R is given by two energy regimes: for E_c.m. ≤ 26 MeV, J_R = f(A) + 0.67 E_c.m.; for 26 < E_c.m. < 120 MeV, J_R = [0.011f(A) − 2.847]E_c.m. − 1.277f(A) + 626.591, where f(A) = 311.0132 exp(A_T^(−2/3)) and E_c.m. is in MeV.
- The imaginary part volume integral J_I(E_c.m.) is zero for E_c.m. ≤ E₀ and follows J_I = J₀ · (E_c.m. − E₀)² / [(E_c.m. − E₀)² + Δ²] for E_c.m. > E₀, where E₀ is the threshold energy for inelastic channels, J₀ is a saturation parameter, and Δ is the rise parameter.
- At energies E_c.m. > 100 MeV, the imaginary depth W should reach its saturation value, which determines J₀.

## Context
This work extends earlier astrophysical statistical model calculations that used simplified equivalent square well potentials and the black nucleus approximation (Arnould 1972, Woosley 1978) or a phenomenological Saxon-Woods potential (Mann 1978, Thielemann 1987). It is motivated by recent experimental folding-potential descriptions (Mohr 1994, Atzrott 1996) that found systematic mass- and energy-dependence but did not provide a global parametrization. The proposed potential, combined with improved level density treatments (Rauscher 1997), aims to reduce the two major remaining uncertainties in global astrophysical reaction rate calculations and enable more reliable predictions of low-energy cross sections for unstable nuclei.
