# Jet-Triggered Photons from Back-Scattering Kinematics for Quark Gluon Plasma Tomography

> High energy photons created from back-scattering of jets in quark gluon plasma are a valuable probe of the temperature of the plasma, and of the energy loss mechanism of quarks in the plasma. An unambiguous identification of these photons through single inclusive photon measurements and photon azimuthal anisotropies has so far been elusive. We estimate the spectra of back-scattering photons in coincidence with trigger jets for typical kinematic situations at the Large Hadron Collider and the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We find that the separation of back-scattering photons from other photon sources using trigger jets depends crucially on our ability to reliably estimate the initial trigger jet energy. We estimate that jet reconstruction techniques in heavy ion experiments need to be able to get to jet $R_{AA}\gtrsim 0.7$ in central collisions for viable back-scattering signals.

## Key claim

Back-scattering photons from jets in quark gluon plasma can be identified via coincidence with trigger jets, but the signal is only viable if jet reconstruction techniques achieve jet \(R_{AA}\gtrsim 0.7\) in central collisions; with currently used small jet cone radii the signal would be too small to be seen experimentally.

## Method

The authors calculated the rate of Compton and annihilation back-scattering processes between fast quarks (subject to energy loss) and thermal partons from a fireball with a temperature profile, using the backward peak approximation. They used the code JETPHOX (version 1.2.2) at leading order (default) or next-to-leading order for prompt hard and fragmentation photon backgrounds, and the code package PPM to compute parton energy loss (via a simple LPM-inspired model with \(\hat q \approx 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at RHIC), jet energy loss (parameterized as \(dE_T/d\tau = - \hat r \ln(E_T/\Lambda)\)), and the back-scattering photon rate. The fireball model assumed a longitudinally expanding, boost-invariant QGP phase with transverse entropy density from a Glauber calculation, normalized to RHIC and LHC multiplicity data.

## Key numbers

- Initial value of \(\hat q \approx 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm in the center of Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, consistent with JET collaboration findings.
- For RHIC: trigger window of 30–35 GeV in \(E_T\) for jets; back-scattering signal develops a shoulder of about 4 GeV width when parton energy loss is included.
- For LHC: trigger window of 60–65 GeV in \(E_T\) for jets; parton and jet energy loss create shoulders up to 15–20 GeV wide on both sides of the trigger window for raa 0.7 and 0.5 scenarios.
- The value of \(\hat r\) needed to reproduce jet raa of 0.7 at RHIC is about 0.24 GeV/fm initially in the center of head-on Au+Au collisions, corresponding to an initial energy loss of \(\sim 1.2\) GeV/fm for 30 GeV jets.
- Single inclusive jet \(R_{AA}\) of 0.7 or larger in central collisions will likely be necessary to carry out this measurement.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of jet-medium photons in two limits: as electromagnetic bremsstrahlung to jet quenching (Zakharov 2004, Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe 2001) and as an elastic back-scattering process (Fries 2002). It proposes a new observable, photon-jet correlations, to separate back-scattering photons from thermal, pre-equilibrium, and prompt hard photon sources, which had been difficult using single inclusive photon spectra or elliptic flow measurements (Turbide 2005, Turbide 2007, Adare 2011, Lohner 2012). The results enable future experimental searches for back-scattering photons at RHIC and LHC, contingent on improved jet reconstruction achieving larger jet \(R_{AA}\) values.
