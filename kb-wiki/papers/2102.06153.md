# New insights into nuclear physics and weak mixing angle using electroweak probes

> Using the new results on coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data in cesium-iodide provided by the COHERENT experiment, we determine a new measurement of the average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$. In combination with the atomic parity violation (APV) experimental result, we derive the most precise measurement of the neutron rms radii of $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$, disentangling for the first time the contributions of the two nuclei. By exploiting these measurements we determine the corresponding neutron skin values for $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$. These results suggest a preference for models which predict large neutron skin values, as corroborated by the only other electroweak measurements of the neutron skin of $^{208}\text{Pb}$ performed by PREX experiments. Moreover, for the first time, we obtain a data-driven APV+COHERENT measurement of the low-energy weak mixing angle with a percent uncertainty, independent of the value of the average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\text{Cs}$ and $^{127}\text{I}$, that is allowed to vary freely in the fit. The value of the low-energy weak mixing angle that we found is slightly larger than the standard model prediction.

## Key claim

Using updated COHERENT coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) data on a cesium-iodide target combined with atomic parity violation (APV) measurements on cesium, the authors obtain the most precise determination of the neutron rms radii of 133Cs and 127I separately for the first time, and derive a data-driven measurement of the low-energy weak mixing angle with percent-level uncertainty that is independent of the assumed neutron radius. The central value of the weak mixing angle is slightly larger than the Standard Model prediction, though compatible within 1 sigma.

## Method

The authors performed a combined least-squares fit of the COHERENT CsI CEvNS data (using 8 energy bins and 11 time intervals) with the APV measurement of the weak charge of 133Cs. The COHERENT analysis used the symmetrized Fermi, Helm, and Klein-Nystrand form factor parameterizations with fixed proton radii from muonic atom spectroscopy (4.821 fm for 133Cs, 4.766 fm for 127I) and allowed the neutron rms radii to vary. The APV constraint was incorporated via the theoretical weak charge expression that includes a neutron-skin correction term, enabling simultaneous extraction of the two nuclear radii and the weak mixing angle.

## Key numbers

- Average neutron rms radius of CsI from COHERENT data alone: R_n(CsI) = 5.55 ± 0.44 fm (factor of 2.5 more precise than previous determinations using 2017 COHERENT data)
- Separate neutron rms radii from combined COHERENT+APV fit (assuming SM weak mixing angle): R_n(133Cs) = 5.27 +0.33/-0.33 fm, R_n(127I) = 5.9 +1.0/-0.9 fm
- Neutron skin of 133Cs: ΔR_np(133Cs) = 0.45 +0.33/-0.33 fm
- Neutron skin of 127I: ΔR_np(127I) = 1.1 +1.0/-0.9 fm
- Low-energy weak mixing angle from combined fit with R_n(CsI) free: sin^2θ_W = 0.2406 ± 0.0035
- Lower limit on the symmetry energy slope parameter L from combined result: L > 38.5 MeV
- Total predicted CEvNS events using SM inputs and NSM neutron radii: N_CEvNS = 311.8

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of COHERENT 2017 data (Cadeddu 2017, Papoulias 2019) by using the updated higher-statistics CsI dataset with improved quenching factor determination. The combination with APV data (Wood 1997, Guena 2004) allows disentangling the 133Cs and 127I contributions for the first time, whereas earlier COHERENT-only analyses could only constrain their average. The results favor nuclear models predicting large neutron skins, consistent with the PREX experiments on 208Pb (Abrahamyan 2012, Adhikari 2021), and provide a model-independent electroweak benchmark for calibrating hadronic probe measurements. Future measurements from the P2, MOLLER, DUNE near detector, and additional COHERENT detectors (argon, germanium, NaI) will further constrain the weak mixing angle and neutron radii.
