# $A=2,3,4$ nuclear contact coefficients in the generalized contact formalism

> We present a theoretical calculation for the $A = 2, 3$ and 4 nuclear contact coefficients within the generalized contact formalism, using both local and non-local chiral potentials. The Hyperspherical Harmonics method is employed to calculate the nuclear wave functions, from which we derive two-body momentum distributions and density functions to extract the contact coefficients. We have extracted the contact coefficients from two-body momentum distributions or from density functions, for a given nucleus and potential, and we have found that the generalized contact formalism predictions are verified in the triplet spin channel for local and non-local potentials. On the other hand, some significant tensions exist for the singlet channels, especially when studied with non-local potentials. We have also analyzed the model-independence of the ratios between the contact coefficients, and we have found to be quite satisfied. This study extends previous works based on local interaction models only.

## Key claim

The generalized contact formalism (GCF) predictions are verified in the triplet spin channel for both local and non-local chiral potentials, but significant tensions exist for the singlet channels, especially when studied with non-local potentials, contrary to the abstract's implication that the formalism is broadly validated.

## Method

The authors used the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) method to solve the A-body bound states for A=2, 3, and 4 nuclei, employing both local chiral potentials (Norfolk NV2+3 classes I and II) and non-local chiral potentials (N2LO through N4LO with cutoffs 450, 500, 550 MeV). They extracted contact coefficients from two-body momentum distributions (2BMDs) in k-space and two-body density functions (2BDFs) in r-space by identifying plateau regions in the ratios of these distributions to universal two-body wave functions, averaging over 1 fm⁻¹ intervals in k-space and 1 fm intervals in r-space.

## Key numbers

- For ³H with the N3LO500 potential (no three-nucleon interaction), the contact coefficient C_{np}^{S=1} extracted from r-space is 4.198 ± 0.026, while from k-space it is 4.085 ± 0.157 (converged at N_{Lag}=28)
- For ³H with the same potential, C_{nn/pp}^{S=0} from r-space is 0.441 ± 0.027 and from k-space is 0.390 ± 0.009
- For ⁴He with the N3LO500 potential (including three-nucleon interaction), C_{np}^{S=1} from r-space is 7.462 ± 0.050 and from k-space is 8.713 ± 1.06
- The plateau region for local potentials in k-space occurs beyond 3-3.5 fm⁻¹, while for non-local potentials it occurs between 2 and 3 fm⁻¹
- The contact coefficient C_{np}^{S=1} is approximately an order of magnitude larger than the S=0 coefficients across all analyzed nuclei

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cruz-Torres (2019), which studied contact coefficients using only local interaction models, by incorporating both local and non-local chiral potentials from chiral effective field theory. The study enables testing whether the GCF predictions and the model-independence of contact coefficient ratios hold across a much larger variety of nuclear potentials, which is important for calculating contact coefficients in heavier nuclei using softer potentials and then scaling them to harder potentials.
