# Delta mass shift as a thermometer of kinetic decoupling in Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV

> The HADES experiment at GSI will soon provide data on the production and properties of $Δ$(1232) baryons from Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV. Using the UrQMD model, we predict the yield and spectra of $Δ$(1232) resonances. In addition we show that one expects to observe a mass shift of the $Δ$(1232) resonance on the order of 50 MeV in the reconstructable $Δ$(1232) mass distribution. This mass shift can be understood in terms of late stage $Δ$(1232) formation with limited kinetic energy. We show how the mass shift can be used to constrain the kinetic decoupling temperature of the system.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD transport model, the authors predict a downward mass shift of the Delta(1232) resonance on the order of 50 MeV in the reconstructable mass distribution for Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV, and propose that this shift can be used as a thermometer to extract the kinetic decoupling temperature of the system. The mass shift is presented as a late-stage regeneration effect, not a conventional in-medium modification.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model version 3.4 to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV beam energy. They reconstructed Delta(1232) resonances by following the decay products (pion and nucleon) of each individual resonance after its decay, classifying daughter particle interactions into four scenarios: no rescattering, one elastic rescattering, both elastic rescattering, or any inelastic rescattering. The invariant mass distributions of reconstructable Delta resonances were then analyzed as functions of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The predicted downward mass shift of the Delta(1232) resonance is on the order of 50 MeV in the reconstructable mass distribution for central Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV.
- The pole mass of the Delta(1232) is given as m_delta^0 = 1.232 GeV/c^2, and the width is Gamma_delta^0 = 0.118 GeV/c^2.
- A simple Breit-Wigner distribution multiplied by a phase space factor with temperature T = 81 MeV reproduces the mean mass as a function of transverse momentum observed in the UrQMD simulation.
- The mass distribution is analyzed for transverse momentum bins ranging from p_perp = 0-0.15 GeV to p_perp = 1.2-1.35 GeV, and for rapidity bins across the full range.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium effects on resonances in heavy-ion collisions, which has traditionally focused on the rho meson via dilepton decays at RHIC (Torrieri 2001, Bleicher 2002, Broniowski 2003, Kolb 2003, Geurts 2012, Makek 2016) and SPS (Arnaldi 2006, Damjanovic 2007, vanHees 2007, Endres 2014) energies, as well as at the LHC (Albuquerque 2018, ALICE 2018, Tripathy 2018). The paper is motivated by upcoming data from the HADES collaboration on Delta(1232) production in Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV, and the authors propose that the observed mass shift at low transverse momentum can be used to constrain the kinetic decoupling temperature of the system, enabling a new method to probe the freeze-out stage of the reaction.
