# Nuclear Pairing in the T=0 channel revisited

> Recent published data on the isoscalar gap in symmetric nuclear matter using the Paris force and the corresponding BHF single particle dispersion are corrected leading to an extremely high proton-neutron gap of $Δ\sim 8$ MeV at $ρ\sim 0.5ρ_0$. Arguments whether this value can be reduced due to screening effects are discussed. A density dependent delta interaction with cut off is adjusted so as to approximately reproduce the nuclear matter values with the Paris force.

## Key claim

The previously published isoscalar (T=0) pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter obtained with the Paris force was underestimated by about 20% due to numerical inaccuracies; the corrected calculation yields a giant gap of approximately 8 MeV at about half saturation density, though the authors discuss whether screening effects in the medium might reduce this value.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equation for symmetric nuclear matter in the T=0 channel using the Paris NN force together with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock single-particle dispersions, verifying their results with two independent numerical codes and checking that the Argonne V14 force gives practically the same result. They then adjusted a density-dependent zero-range delta interaction with an energy cutoff to reproduce the corrected nuclear matter gap values, fitting the parameters for both bare mass and effective mass cases.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar pairing gap maximum: Δ ∼ 8 MeV at ρ ∼ 0.5 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Gap value in finite nuclei (e.g., N=Z=35) estimated via local density approximation: Δ ∼ 3 MeV
- For the effective delta force fit with bare mass: parameters α = 0.90, η = 0.40, cutoff energy ε_C = 60 MeV, and v₀ = -480 MeV fm³
- For the effective delta force fit with effective mass: parameters α = 0.20, η = -0.10, cutoff energy ε_C = 60 MeV, and v₀ = -480 MeV fm³
- The fit with bare mass reproduces the microscopic calculation up to k_F ∼ 1.7 fm⁻¹; the fit with effective mass breaks down at k_F ∼ 1.35 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work corrects earlier numerical results from Garrido et al. (1999) and Baldo, Lombardo, and Schuck (1995) for the T=0 pairing gap, bringing them into quantitative agreement with earlier calculations of the critical temperature by Alm, Ropke, and Schmidt (1990). The corrected giant gap values suggest that T=0 proton-neutron pairing may be responsible for the Wigner energy in the nuclear mass formula for N=Z nuclei, and the authors note that screening of the tensor force in the medium remains a controversial subject (Zheng and Zamick, 1991). The effective density-dependent delta force adjusted here is intended for use in finite nucleus calculations of T=0 pairing, though the authors caution that detailed surface dependence may differ from results obtained with the full Paris force.
