# Internal conversion coefficients for superheavy elements

> The internal conversion coefficients (ICC) were calculated for all atomic subshells of the elements with 104<=Z<=126, the E1...E4, M1...M4 multipolarities and the transition energies between 10 and 1000 keV. The atomic screening was treated in the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater model. The Tables comprising almost 90000 subshell and total ICC were recently deposited at LANL preprint server.

## Key claim

The authors calculated internal conversion coefficients (ICC) for superheavy elements with Z from 104 to 126, extending existing tables that previously covered only up to Z=104; the calculations assume the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater atomic model and treat the nucleus as a finite Fermi distribution of charge, differing from the homogeneous sphere model used by Roesel et al. (1978).

## Method

The ICC were computed using the program NICC, solving the Dirac equation for bound and free electron wavefunctions with the atomic potential of Lu et al. (1971) and evaluating conversion matrix elements by direct integration in first-order perturbation theory. The atomic screening was treated in the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater model, and the nucleus was described with a Fermi charge distribution rather than the homogeneous sphere used by Roesel et al. (1978). Calculations covered all atomic subshells for elements Z=104 through Z=126, multipolarities E1 through E4 and M1 through M4, and 16 transition energies from 10 keV to 1 MeV, producing almost 90000 subshell and total ICC.

## Key numbers

- For Z=105, total ICC at 30 keV: E1 = 2.36(0), E2 = 8.04(3), M1 = 1.81(2), M4 = 5.59(8) (notation: number in parentheses is power of 10 multiplier)
- For Z=110, total ICC at 30 keV: E1 = 2.58(0), E2 = 1.21(4), M1 = 2.97(2), M4 = 9.30(8)
- For Z=115, total ICC at 30 keV: E1 = 2.81(0), E2 = 1.89(4), M1 = 5.59(2), M4 = 1.56(9)
- For Z=120, total ICC at 30 keV: E1 = 1.33(0), E2 = 1.68(4), M1 = 1.04(3), M4 = 1.64(9)
- For Z=125, total ICC at 30 keV: E1 = 1.40(0), E2 = 2.87(4), M1 = 2.03(3), M4 = 2.82(9)
- At Z=104, the ICC of this work agree with those of Roesel et al. (1978) typically within 1-2% based on 100 comparisons across various subshells, transition energies, and multipolarities
- Monte Carlo simulation for 600 nm thick lead target with 10 keV initial electron energy showed that convolution with Gaussian spectrometer response of FWHM = 0.5 keV and 1.0 keV still allows observation of conversion electron lines

## Context

This work extends the ICC tables of Roesel et al. (1978), which covered Z up to 104, and Band et al. (1978), which covered K, L, and M shells for Z up to 104, into the superheavy region up to Z=126. The calculations were motivated by recent experiments on Z=102 isotope 254No by Reiter et al. (1999) and the indication of Z=118 formation by Ninov et al. (1999), enabling future gamma-ray and conversion electron spectroscopy of superheavy nuclei. The Monte Carlo simulations demonstrate that electron scattering in typical reaction targets does not prevent conversion electron spectroscopy down to 10 keV, supporting the planning of heavy ion experiments and interpretation of future electron spectra.
