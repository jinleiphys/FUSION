# Spatially improved operators for excited hadrons on the lattice

> We present a new approach for determining spatially optimized operators that can be used for lattice spectroscopy of excited hadrons. Jacobi smeared quark sources with different widths are combined to construct hadron operators with different spatial wave functions. We use the variational method to determine those linear combinations of operators that have optimal overlap with ground and excited states. The details of the new approach are discussed and we demonstrate the power of the method using examples from quenched baryon and meson spectroscopy. In particular we study the Roper state and rho(1450) and discuss some physical implications of our tests.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining Jacobi-smeared quark sources with two different widths (narrow and wide) in a variational analysis of correlation matrices provides a powerful tool for extracting excited hadron masses on the lattice, but the method's success depends on careful operator selection and the results for excited states carry larger statistical uncertainties than ground state results.

## Method

The authors constructed hadron operators by combining narrow and wide Jacobi-smeared quark sources (with widths of approximately 0.27 fm and 0.41 fm respectively) to form basis operators with different spatial wave functions. They then computed full correlation matrices of these operators and used the variational method to diagonalize them, extracting eigenvalues whose exponential decay gives the masses of ground and excited states. The method was tested on quenched gauge configurations (12^3 x 24 lattice, Luscher-Weisz action, beta = 7.9, lattice spacing a = 0.148 fm) using the chirally improved Dirac operator with 100 configurations and 10 quark masses from am = 0.02 to am = 0.20.

## Key numbers

- Narrow source parameters: N = 18, kappa = 0.210, giving Gaussian width sigma ~ 0.27 fm
- Wide source parameters: N = 41, kappa = 0.191, giving Gaussian width sigma ~ 0.41 fm
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.148(2) fm as determined from the Sommer parameter
- Lattice volume: 12^3 x 24
- Statistics: 100 gauge configurations
- Quark mass range: am = 0.02 to am = 0.20
- Smallest quark mass for excited nucleon fits: am = 0.04, corresponding to pion mass of 450 MeV
- Fit range for ground state nucleon: t = 4 to t = 9
- Fit range for first excited nucleon: t = 3 to t = 9 (for am = 0.20) shrinking to t = 4 to t = 7 (for am = 0.05)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of excited hadrons that used only two interpolating fields (chi_1 and chi_2) for nucleons, which failed to capture the Roper state reliably as shown by Broemmel et al. (2004). The method addresses the need for spatially optimized operators with different radial wave functions, building on the variational method of Michael (1985) and Jacobi smearing techniques. The results enable future large-scale studies of the nucleon spectrum to investigate the level crossing between the Roper resonance N(1440) and the negative parity N(1535) as a function of quark mass, and contradict the claim by Sasaki (2001) of level crossing at 600 MeV pion mass.
