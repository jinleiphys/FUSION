# Electromagnetic break-up of nuclei with A = 3 - 7

> This talk contains a short review of some of the progresses made in the last three years in the calculations of electromagnetic cross sections of light nuclei up to A=7. Since many of them have been possible thanks to the use of the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, both for inclusive and exclusive reactions, I will first make a few remarks on the method, stressing its essential points and then show results for different nuclei. One of the interesting outcomes is e.g. the appearing of typical collective motion features from ab initio six-body calculations. When a comparison with available experimental data is attempted, it is rather disappointing to realize that low-energy data are old, incomplete and not accurate enough to disantangle interesting effects, showing the need of a major experimental program in this direction, together with more theoretical efforts to implement modern realistic forces in continuum calculations of $A\geq 4$ systems.

# Electromagnetic break-up of nuclei with A = 3 - 7

## Key claim
The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method enables ab initio calculations of electromagnetic cross sections for light nuclei up to A=7 without requiring explicit continuum wave functions, but the available low-energy experimental data are too old, incomplete, and inaccurate to disentangle interesting effects such as those from three-body forces or collective motion features.

## Method
The LIT method replaces the calculation of continuum wave functions by solving Schrödinger-like equations with external sources and a Lorentzian kernel, using bound-state methods to obtain finite-norm solutions. For inclusive reactions, one solves a single equation involving the nuclear ground state and the electromagnetic operator; for exclusive reactions, an additional equation for the fragment interaction potential is required. The resulting integral transforms are then inverted, with the Lorentzian kernel providing stable inversion unlike Laplace kernels. The equations were solved using correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) expansions for A=3,4 and the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method combined with a Lanczos algorithm reformulation for A=6,7.

## Key numbers
- Triton photonuclear cross section: LIT results agree with Faddeev equation solutions at the level of a few percent, using unretarded E1 approximation and the AV18 potential.
- $^4$He($\gamma$,n) cross section: first result with full final-state interaction beyond the three-body break-up threshold, using the central MTI-III potential.
- $^6$He total dipole photodisintegration: two resonance peaks appear, interpreted as a soft dipole mode (neutron halo against alpha core) and a Gamow-Teller mode (protons against neutrons), persisting independently of the potential used.
- $^6$Li total photodisintegration: a single peak is observed, with good agreement to data at lower energy using the AV4' potential (which includes P-wave interactions); sizeable disagreement remains at higher energies.
- $^7$Li total photodisintegration: preliminary seven-body calculation shows a unique peak at about the same energy as experimental data, with the height well reproduced; a shoulder below 15 MeV arises from different thresholds for T=3/2 and T=1/2 channels.

## Context
This work extends the LIT method, developed by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1994) and Lapiana (2000), from three-body systems to larger nuclei up to A=7, where explicit continuum calculations are not feasible. The results for $^6$He and $^6$Li show collective motion features emerging from ab initio six-body calculations, a new capability. The paper calls for a major experimental program to produce accurate low-energy data, and for further theoretical work to implement modern realistic forces in continuum calculations for A≥4 systems, building on benchmark comparisons with Faddeev results for the triton (benchmark paper, 2002) and earlier calculations with MTI-III and MN potentials (Malfliet and Tjon, 1977).
