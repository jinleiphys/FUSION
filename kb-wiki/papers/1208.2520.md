# Democratic decay of 6Be exposed by correlations

> The interaction of an E/A=70 - MeV 7Be beam with a Be target was used to populate levels in 6Be following neutron knockout reactions. The three-body decay of the ground and first excited states into the alpha+p+p exit channel were detected in the High Resolution Array. Precise three-body correlations extracted from the experimental data allowed us to obtain insight into the mechanism of the three-body democratic decay. The correlation data are in a good agreement with a three-cluster-model calculation and thus validate this theoretical approach over a broad energy range.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the three-body decay of 6Be remains "democratic" (essentially three-body in nature) over a much broader energy range than previously expected, with sequential decay through the 5Li ground-state resonance only becoming visible at decay energies above approximately 2 Er(5Li) + Gamma(5Li), and even then the mechanism is a complex mixture that cannot be disentangled into pure sequential decay.

## Method

The authors populated levels in 6Be via neutron knockout reactions using a 70-MeV/nucleon 7Be beam on a 9Be target at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The alpha+p+p decay products were detected in the High Resolution Array (14 Si-CsI telescopes), and three-body correlations were extracted from the invariant mass of each event. The experimental correlations were compared to a three-cluster model calculation solving the inhomogeneous three-body Schrodinger equation with source functions constructed in the sudden neutron-removal approximation (Serber model), with Monte Carlo simulations accounting for apparatus bias and resolution.

## Key numbers

- The 7Be secondary beam had an intensity of 4 x 10^7 s^-1 with approximately 90% purity
- The High Resolution Array telescopes were located 90 cm downstream from the target, subtending zenith angles from 1.4 degrees to 13 degrees
- The experimental 6Be decay energy spectrum was measured up to ET approximately 10 MeV (energy above the alpha+p+p threshold)
- The source function coefficients obtained from fitting the experimental ET distribution were {alpha, beta, gamma, delta} = {0.42, 0.3, 0.49, 0.7}
- Energy bins of 0.5 MeV width were used for the 0+ state and 1 MeV width for the 2+ state resonance peaks
- The high-energy correlation bin covered ET = 5.8 to 9.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 6Be by Grigorenko (2009), Charity (2010), and Fomichev (2012), and provides the highest statistical significance data to date for validating three-body decay models. The results confirm and extend the concept of "democratic decay" proposed by Bochkarev (1989) and Pfutzner (2012), showing that the transition from three-body to sequential decay dynamics occurs at much higher energies than the simple energetic accessibility of intermediate states would suggest. The data enable future benchmark studies of two-proton emission in heavier nuclei like 45Fe, and because of isospin symmetry, the two-proton correlations can shed light on the structure of the mirror neutron-halo nucleus 6He.
