# Disentangling covariant Wigner functions for chiral fermions

> We develop a general formalism for the quantum kinetics of chiral fermions in a background electromagnetic field based on a semiclassical expansion of covariant Wigner functions in the Planck constant $\hbar$. We demonstrate to any order of $\hbar$ that only the time-component of the Wigner function is independent while other components are explicit derivative. We further demonstrate to any order of $\hbar$ that a system of quantum kinetic equations for multiple-components of Wigner functions can be reduced to one chiral kinetic equation involving only the single-component distribution function. These are remarkable properties of the quantum kinetics of chiral fermions and will significantly simplify the description and simulation of chiral effects in heavy ion collisions and Dirac/Weyl semimetals. We present the unintegrated chiral kinetic equations in four-momenta up to $O(\hbar ^2)$ and the integrated ones in three-momenta up to $O(\hbar)$. We find that some singular terms emerge in the integration over the time component of the four-momentum, which result in a new source term contributing to the chiral anomaly, in contrast to the well-known scenario of the Berry phase term. Finally we rewrite our results in any Lorentz frame with a reference four-velocity and show how the non-trivial transformation of the distribution function in different frames emerges in a natural way.

## Key claim

The authors prove to any order in the Planck constant ℏ that the system of quantum kinetic equations for multiple components of the covariant Wigner function for chiral fermions can be reduced to a single chiral kinetic equation involving only the time-component distribution function, with the spatial components being explicitly derivative.

## Method

The authors perform a semiclassical expansion of the covariant Wigner function in powers of ℏ for massless fermions in a background electromagnetic field, using the vector component of the Wigner function with chirality (VWC). They expand the operators Π^μ and G^μ in even powers of ℏ and derive the quantum kinetic equations order by order, then show recursively that the evolution equations for the spatial components are redundant and that only the time-component is independent. The formalism is applied to obtain explicit results up to O(ℏ^2) in four-momenta and O(ℏ) in three-momenta.

## Key numbers

- The unintegrated chiral kinetic equation is presented up to O(ℏ^2) in four-momenta and the integrated one up to O(ℏ) in three-momenta.
- The first-order energy pole shift is given by E_p^(±) = ±E_p (1 ∓ ℏ s Q B·Ω_p), where Ω_p ≡ p/(2|p|^3) is the Berry curvature and E_p = |p|.
- The chiral kinetic equation in three-momentum to O(ℏ) contains a term (1 + ℏ s Q Ω_p·B) multiplying the time derivative of the distribution function.
- New singular terms emerge in the integration over p_0, including a term proportional to (E·B)(∇_p·Ω_p) f and a term with an infrared cutoff Λ: -(2ℏ s/Λ)(E·p)(B·p) δ'(Λ^2 - p^2) f.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical expansion approach first proposed in Vasak (1987) but without concrete calculations, and builds on recent O(ℏ) results by Son (2012), Manuel (2013, 2014), Hidaka (2016), and Huang (2018). The formalism provides a general framework that does not require quasi-equilibrium conditions, unlike earlier expansions in space-time gradients and field strengths near equilibrium by Gao (2012, 2015, 2017). The results enable simplified description and simulation of chiral effects in heavy ion collisions and Dirac/Weyl semimetals, and the authors find a new source term contributing to the chiral anomaly distinct from the well-known Berry phase term.
