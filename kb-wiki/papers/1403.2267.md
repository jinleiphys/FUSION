# A chiral effective field theory study of hadronic parity violation in few-nucleon systems

> We reconsider the derivation of the nucleon-nucleon parity-violating (PV) potential within a chiral effective field theory framework. We construct the potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order by including one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, contact, and 1/M (M being the nucleon mass) terms, and use dimensional regularization to renormalize the pion-loop corrections. A detailed analysis of the number of independent low-energy constants (LEC's) entering the potential is carried out. We find that it depends on six LEC's: the pion-nucleon PV coupling constant $h^1_π$ and five parameters multiplying contact interactions. We investigate PV effects induced by this potential on several few-nucleon observables, including the $\vec{p}$-$p$ longitudinal asymmetry, the neutron spin rotation in $\vec{n}$-$p$ and $\vec{n}$-$d$ scattering, and the longitudinal asymmetry in the $^3$He$(\vec{n},p)^3$H charge-exchange reaction. An estimate for the range of values of the various LEC's is provided by using available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper derives the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, finding that it depends on six independent low-energy constants (the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h^1_\pi\) and five contact-term parameters), but the authors provide only estimates for the range of these constants from available experimental data rather than definitive determinations.

## Method

The authors constructed the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential within chiral effective field theory by including one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, contact, and \(1/M\) (nucleon mass) terms up to next-to-next-to-leading order, using dimensional regularization to renormalize pion-loop corrections. They performed a detailed analysis of the independent low-energy constants entering the Lagrangian at order \(Q^2\) for the \(\Delta I=0\), \(\Delta I=1\), and \(\Delta I=2\) sectors, then computed parity-violating observables including the \(\vec{p}\)-\(p\) longitudinal asymmetry, neutron spin rotation in \(\vec{n}\)-\(p\) and \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) scattering, and the longitudinal asymmetry in the \(^3\)He\((\vec{n},p)^3\)H charge-exchange reaction.

## Key numbers

* The pion-nucleon parity-violating coupling constant \(h^1_\pi\) and five contact-term parameters constitute the six independent low-energy constants entering the potential at next-to-next-to-leading order.
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 1\) GeV specifies the expansion parameter \(Q/\Lambda_\chi\).
* The bare pion decay constant is \(f_\pi \simeq 92\) MeV.
* The order of magnitude of all low-energy constants is \(h \sim G_F f_\pi^2 \approx 10^{-7}\), which sets the scale of parity-violating effects in few-nucleon systems.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering chiral effective field theory study of Kaplan and Savage (1993) and the next-to-next-to-leading order derivation by Zhu et al. (2005), who included ten four-nucleon contact terms that were later shown by Girlanda (2008) to reduce to five independent terms. The paper enables future determinations of the six low-energy constants by fitting to planned experiments at cold neutron facilities including LANSCE, NIST, and the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge, specifically measurements of neutron spin rotation in \(\vec{n}\)-\(p\) and \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) scattering and the longitudinal asymmetry in the \(^3\)He\((\vec{n},p)^3\)H reaction.
