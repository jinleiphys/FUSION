# Beyond-mean-field approaches for nuclear neutrinoless double beta decay in the standard mechanism

> Nuclear weak decays provide important probes to fundamental symmetries in nature. A precise description of these processes in atomic nuclei requires comprehensive knowledge on both the strong and weak interactions in the nuclear medium and on the dynamics of quantum many-body systems. In particular, an observation of the hypothetical double beta decay without emission of neutrinos ($0νββ$) would unambiguously demonstrate the Majorana nature of neutrinos and the existence of the lepton-number-violation process. It would also provide unique information on the ordering and absolute scale of neutrino masses. The next-generation tonne-scale experiments with sensitivity up to $10^{28}$ years after a few years of running will probably provide a definite answer to these fundamental questions based on our current knowledge on the nuclear matrix element (NME), the precise determination of which is a challenge to nuclear theory. Beyond-mean-field approaches have been frequently adapted for the study of nuclear structure and decay throughout the nuclear chart for several decades. In this review, we summarize the status of beyond-mean-field calculations of the NMEs of $0νββ$ decay assuming the standard mechanism of an exchange of light Majorana neutrinos. The challenges and prospects in the extension and application of beyond-mean-field approaches for $0νββ$ decay are discussed.

## Key claim

Beyond-mean-field (BMF) approaches, particularly the symmetry-projected generator coordinate method (PGCM), can compute nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay in medium-mass and heavy deformed nuclei, but the resulting NMEs from different BMF and shell-model calculations still disagree by a factor of about three, causing an order-of-magnitude uncertainty in the predicted half-life for a given effective neutrino mass.

## Method

The authors review BMF methods starting from a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) reference state, then restoring broken symmetries (particle number, angular momentum, parity) and incorporating quantum fluctuations via the PGCM, where the nuclear wave function is a linear combination of nonorthogonal mean-field wave functions projected onto correct quantum numbers. They apply these techniques to compute NMEs for the standard mechanism of light Majorana neutrino exchange, using either phenomenological Hamiltonians, energy density functionals (EDFs), or realistic nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- The best experimental half-life lower limits are: $^{76}$Ge $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}>1.8\times 10^{26}$ yr, $^{130}$Te $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}>3.2\times 10^{25}$ yr, $^{136}$Xe $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}>1.07\times 10^{26}$ yr.
- These translate to effective neutrino mass constraints: $^{76}$Ge $|\langle m_{\beta\beta}\rangle| < 0.079-0.180$ eV (90% C.L.), $^{130}$Te $|\langle m_{\beta\beta}\rangle| < 0.075-0.350$ eV (90% C.L.), $^{136}$Xe $|\langle m_{\beta\beta}\rangle| < 0.061-0.165$ eV (90% C.L.).
- The next-generation tonne-scale experiments aim for a discovery potential half-life of about $10^{28}$ years.
- The free-space axial-vector coupling strength $g_A(0)$ is around 1.27.
- The phase-space factor $G_{0\nu}\approx 10^{-14}$ yr$^{-1}$.
- The electron mass $m_e\simeq 0.511$ MeV.
- The energy difference $\Delta_{npe}=(m_n-m_p-m_e)c^2=0.782$ MeV.
- The $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay half-lives range from $10^{18}$ to $10^{21}$ years.
- The black-box induced Majorana mass correction is $\delta m_\nu={\cal O}(10^{-28})$ eV.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of NME calculations for $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay beyond earlier reviews by Engel (2017) and Vergados et al. (2012), focusing specifically on BMF approaches which are not comprehensively covered elsewhere. It contrasts BMF methods with valence-space interacting shell model calculations (Caurier et al., 2008; Menendez et al., 2009; Horoi, 2016) and with quasiparticle random-phase approximation and interacting boson model approaches (Simkovic et al., 2009; Barea et al., 2013; Kotila, 2021), highlighting the persistent factor-of-three discrepancy between model predictions. The work enables future development of ab initio methods for heavy nuclei by marrying BMF techniques with realistic nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory (Cirigliano et al., 2018, 2019; Yao et al., 2020).
