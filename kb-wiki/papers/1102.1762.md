# $α$-particle condensate states in $^{16}$O

> The existence of a rotational band with the $α$+$^{12}$C($0_2^+$) cluster structure, in which three $α$ particles in $^{12}$C($0_2^+$) are locally condensed, is demonstrated near the four-$α$ threshold of $^{16}$O in agreement with experiment. This is achieved by studying structure and scattering for the $α$+$^{12}$C($0_2^+$) system in a unified way. A drastic reduction (quenching) of the moment of the inertia of the $0^+$ state at 15.1 MeV just above the four-$α$ threshold in $^{16}$O suggests that it could be a candidate for the superfluid state in $α$-particle condensation.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a rotational band with an α+¹²C(0₂⁺) cluster structure exists near the four-α threshold in ¹⁶O, and that the 0⁺ state at 15.1 MeV shows a drastically reduced moment of inertia, suggesting it could be a candidate for the superfluid state in α-particle condensation, but the authors hedge that this interpretation relies on the state being fragmented from a broader band head, and that the condensation probability is only roughly conjectured from the moment of inertia reduction.

## Method
The authors performed a unified description of structure and scattering for the α+¹²C system using a double folding (DF) model with density-dependent M3Y effective interaction (DDM3Y) in coupled channel calculations. They calculated diagonal and coupling potentials from nucleon densities of ¹²C obtained via the resonating group method, and solved coupled channel scattering equations to locate resonant states. The potential was normalized to reproduce elastic and inelastic α+¹²C rainbow scattering data at high energies (139, 166, 172.5 MeV) and low energy (18.5 MeV), then applied to predict bound and resonant states near the four-α threshold.

## Key numbers
- The calculated rotational constant for the K=0₂⁺ band is k=80 keV, close to the experimental centroid value of k=86 keV from observed 2⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ states.
- The intercluster distance between the orbiting α and the ¹²C(0₂⁺) core is estimated as R=5.9 fm from the relative motion contribution to the moment of inertia; when considering only the superfluid part of ¹²C(0₂⁺), the effective distance increases to R=9.2 fm.
- The 0⁺ state at Ex=15.1 MeV has a moment of inertia reduced to about one quarter of that of the 2⁺ and 4⁺ states of the K=0₂⁺ band.
- The calculated band head 0⁺ state of the K=0₂⁺ band lies at Ex=16.61 MeV with a width Γ=1.14 MeV; the centroid of three experimentally observed fragmented 0⁺ states is 16.9 MeV.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair for the real potential is JV=427.3 MeV·fm³ for the ground state channel at Eα=18.5 MeV, and JV=409.6 MeV·fm³ for the ground state channel and 510.3 MeV·fm³ for the α-¹²C(0₂⁺) channel at near-zero energy.

## Context
This work extends the study of α-particle condensation beyond the Hoyle state in ¹²C (Tohsaki 2001, Funaki 2003) to ¹⁶O, where previous studies focused only on 0⁺ states (Tohsaki 2001, Wakasa 2007, Funaki 2008). It contradicts the long-standing interpretation by Chevallier (1967) that the observed high-spin states form a linear chain of four α particles, instead showing they form a rotational band with α+¹²C(0₂⁺) cluster structure. The unified description of structure and scattering, previously successful for α+⁴⁰Ca (Michel 1986, Yamaya 1998), enables identification of the 15.1 MeV 0⁺ state as a candidate for the superfluid state in α-particle condensation, and opens the possibility of searching for analogous local α-particle condensation in heavier nuclei up to the A=212 region.
