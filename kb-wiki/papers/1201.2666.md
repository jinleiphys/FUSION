# General Form of the Color Potential Produced by Color Charges of the Quark

> Constant electric charge $e$ satisfies the continuity equation $\partial_μj^μ(x)= 0$ where $j^μ(x)$ is the current density of the electron. However, the Yang-Mills color current density $j^{μa}(x)$ of the quark satisfies the equation $D_μ[A] j^{μa}(x)= 0$ which is not a continuity equation ($\partial_μj^{μa}(x)\neq 0$) which implies that a color charge $q^a(t)$ of the quark is not constant but it is time dependent where $a=1,2,...8$ are color indices. In this paper we derive general form of color potential produced by color charges of the quark. We find that the general form of the color potential produced by the color charges of the quark at rest is given by $Φ^a(x) =A_0^a(t,{\bf x}) =\frac{q^b(t-\frac{r}{c})}{r}\[\frac{{\rm exp}[g\int dr \frac{Q(t-\frac{r}{c})}{r}] -1}{g \int dr \frac{Q(t-\frac{r}{c})}{r}}\]_{ab}$ where $dr$ integration is an indefinite integration, ~~ $Q_{ab}(τ_0)=f^{abd}q^d(τ_0)$, ~~$r=|{\vec x}-{\vec X}(τ_0)|$, ~~$τ_0=t-\frac{r}{c}$ is the retarded time, ~~$c$ is the speed of light, ~~${\vec X}(τ_0)$ is the position of the quark at the retarded time and the repeated color indices $b,d$(=1,2,...8) are summed. For constant color charge $q^a$ we reproduce the Coulomb-like potential $Φ^a(x)=\frac{q^a}{r}$ which is consistent with the Maxwell theory where constant electric charge $e$ produces the Coulomb potential $Φ(x)=\frac{e}{r}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general form for the color potential produced by the time-dependent color charges of a quark, showing that unlike the constant electric charge in Maxwell theory, the Yang-Mills color current density satisfies a covariant derivative equation rather than a continuity equation, which forces the color charge \(q^a(t)\) to be time dependent. The claimed result is conditional on the quark being at rest and on the use of retarded time, and the potential reduces to a Coulomb-like form only when the color charge is constant.

## Method

The author starts from the SU(3) pure gauge potential expressed as \(A^{\mu a}(x) = [\partial^\mu \omega^b(x)] [(e^{gM(x)}-1)/(gM(x))]_{ab}\) with \(M_{ab}(x)=f^{abc}\omega^c(x)\), derived by summing infinite non-commuting terms in the group element. By analogy with the U(1) case, where an electron at speed \(v\sim c\) produces an approximate pure gauge potential, the author constructs the SU(3) approximate pure gauge potential for a quark moving at \(v\sim c\) and then obtains the physical color potential for a quark at rest by setting \(v=0\) in that expression. The derivation uses the retarded time \(\tau_0 = t - r/c\) and the structure constants \(f^{abd}\) of SU(3).

## Key numbers

* The general color potential for a quark at rest is \(\Phi^a(x) = A_0^a(t,\mathbf{x}) = \frac{q^b(t-r/c)}{r} \left[ \frac{\exp\left(g\int dr \frac{Q(t-r/c)}{r}\right) - 1}{g\int dr \frac{Q(t-r/c)}{r}} \right]_{ab}\), where \(Q_{ab}(\tau_0)=f^{abd}q^d(\tau_0)\) and \(r = |\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{X}(\tau_0)|\).
* For constant color charge \(q^a\), the potential reduces to the Coulomb-like form \(\Phi^a(x) = q^a / r\).
* In the short-distance limit where the coupling \(g \to 0\), the potential approaches \(\Phi^a(x) \to q^a(\tau_0)/r\).
* The non-zero structure constants \(f^{abd}\) are tabulated: e.g., \(f^{123}=1\), \(f^{147}=1/2\), \(f^{156}=-1/2\), \(f^{246}=1/2\), \(f^{257}=1/2\), \(f^{345}=1/2\), \(f^{367}=-1/2\), \(f^{458}=\sqrt{3}/2\), \(f^{678}=\sqrt{3}/2\).

## Context

This work extends the classical Yang-Mills theory approach to color charges, building on the analogy between U(1) electromagnetism and SU(3) non-abelian gauge theory originally formulated by Yang and Mills (1954). It contrasts with lattice QCD methods (Wilson, 1974), which cannot predict the explicit form of the color potential \(A^{\mu a}(x)\) with color indices, and instead provides an analytic expression that reduces to a Coulomb-like potential at short distances, consistent with asymptotic freedom. The result is intended to enable further study of quark confinement by providing the exact color potential needed to understand why quarks are confined inside a stable proton.
