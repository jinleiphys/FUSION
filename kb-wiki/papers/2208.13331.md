# Effects of the formation time of parton shower on jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions

> Jet quenching has successfully served as a hard probe to study the properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). As a multi-particle system, jets take time to develop from a highly virtual parton to a group of partons close to mass shells. In this study, we present a systematical study on the effects of this formation time on jet quenching in relativistic nuclear collisions. Jets from initial hard scatterings were simulated with Pythia, and their interactions with QGP were described using a Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model that incorporates both elastic and inelastic scatterings between jet partons and the thermal medium. Three different estimations of the jet formation time were implemented and compared, including instantaneous formation, formation from single splitting, and formation from sequential splittings, before which no jet-medium interaction was assumed. We found that deferring the jet-medium interaction with a longer formation time not only affects the overall magnitude of the nuclear modification factor of jets, but also its dependence on the jet transverse momentum.

## Key claim

Deferring the start of jet-medium interactions by using a realistic parton formation time (from single or sequential splittings) not only reduces the overall magnitude of jet quenching (increases $R_\mathrm{AA}$) but also changes the $p_\mathrm{T}$ dependence of the nuclear modification factor from a decreasing trend to a slightly rising trend, an effect that is qualitatively consistent with STAR data but which the abstract does not specify is dependent on the specific formation-time model used.

## Method

Jets from p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV were generated with Pythia 8, and the full parton-level events were fed into the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate elastic and inelastic scatterings with a QGP medium evolved via viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNew). Three different prescriptions for the formation time of each final-state parton were implemented: instantaneous formation (setup-1), formation from a single splitting using the ancestor parton's energy (setup-2), and formation from the full sequence of splittings in the Pythia shower (setup-3); no jet-medium interaction was allowed before both the parton formation time and the QGP formation time ($\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$) had elapsed. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-$k_\perp$ algorithm using Fastjet, and the nuclear modification factor $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and central-to-peripheral ratio $R_\mathrm{cp}$ were computed for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

* For a 50 GeV quark initiating the shower, only about 50% of final-state partons (setup-2) and about 20% (setup-3) are formed before $\sim 1$ fm/$c$ (the QGP formation time scale); by 5 fm/$c$ these fractions rise to about 83% and 76%, respectively.
* The average formation time from sequential splittings (setup-3) shows an approximately linear increase with the initial $p_\mathrm{T}$ of the ancestor parton for p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV.
* For a fixed $\alpha_\mathrm{s}$ and jet cone size $R=0.2$, the jet $R_\mathrm{AA}$ in 0-10% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV is larger (less quenching) when going from setup-1 (instantaneous) to setup-2 (single splitting) to setup-3 (sequential splittings), reflecting the hierarchy $t_\mathrm{init}^\mathrm{setup-3} > t_\mathrm{init}^\mathrm{setup-2} > t_\mathrm{init}^\mathrm{setup-1}$.
* The jet $R_\mathrm{AA}$ decreases with $p_\mathrm{T}$ for setup-1 but increases with $p_\mathrm{T}$ for setup-2 and setup-3, a trend that is qualitatively consistent with STAR data for $R_\mathrm{cp}$ at cone sizes $R=0.2$, 0.3, and 0.4.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet quenching by explicitly modeling the finite formation time of the parton shower, an effect previously investigated in terms of its influence on azimuthal dependence (Adhya, 2021) and on weaker quenching for longer formation times using a time reclustering algorithm (Apolinario, 2020). The results demonstrate that the choice of formation-time prescription introduces significant systematic uncertainty in extracting transport coefficients (such as $\alpha_\mathrm{s}$ and $\hat{q}$) from jet $R_\mathrm{AA}$ data, and motivate future improvements including medium modification at high virtuality and a hadronization scheme for direct comparison with charged-hadron jet measurements.
