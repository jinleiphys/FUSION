# Self weakening of the tensor interaction in a nucleus

> We examine several ``landmarks'' for the effects of the tensor interaction on the properties of light nuclei. These were usually discussed in the context of small-space shell-model calculations. We show, using $G$ matrices derived from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, that when the model space is small (e.g., $0\hbarω$), these effects are overestimated, indicating that the tensor interaction is too strong. However, when larger spaces are used, there is a diminishing of these effects, which, in general, leads to better agreement with experiment.

# Self weakening of the tensor interaction in a nucleus

## Key claim
The tensor interaction appears too strong when evaluated in small (0ℏω) shell-model spaces, but its effects diminish when larger model spaces (2ℏω, 4ℏω) are used, leading to better agreement with experiment. This self-weakening mechanism means that the effective tensor interaction does not need to be modified in the nuclear medium for nuclear structure calculations, contrary to what is required for nucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations using Brueckner G matrices derived from the Nijmegen II realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, with the Pauli operator Q defined differently for each model space to avoid double counting. They examined three landmark cases: the isospin splitting of Jπ=0− states in 16O, the beta decay of 14C to 14N, and the electromagnetic moments of 6Li, comparing results from 0ℏω spaces with those from larger spaces including 2ℏω, 3ℏω, and 4ℏω excitations. No phenomenological single-particle energies were used; these were generated implicitly from the two-body G matrix elements and one-body kinetic energy.

## Key numbers
- Isospin splitting ΔE between Jπ=0−, T=1 and T=0 states in 16O: experimental value 1.845 MeV; 1ℏω 1p-1h space gives 2.809 MeV; adding 3ℏω 3p-3h gives 2.678 MeV; adding 3ℏω 2p-2h gives 1.641 MeV; full (1+3)ℏω calculation gives 1.931 MeV.
- For the A=14 beta decay, B(GT) in 0ℏω space is 3.967; in full 2ℏω space it decreases by about 55% to 1.795, compared to the experimental value of nearly zero.
- M1 moment μ of 14N ground state: 0.768 μN in 0ℏω; 0.554 μN in 2ℏω; experimental value is 0.404 μN (using bare g factors).
- Electric quadrupole moment Q of 6Li: −0.360 e fm² in 0ℏω; −0.251 e fm² in 2ℏω; −0.0085 e fm² in 4ℏω; experimental value is −0.082 e fm².
- M1 moment μ of 6Li: 0.866 μN in 0ℏω; 0.848 μN in 2ℏω; experimental value is 0.822 μN.
- For the schematic interaction with full tensor strength (y=1), Q of 6Li is −0.358 e fm²; with half tensor strength (y=0.5) it is −0.135 e fm²; with no tensor (y=0) it is +0.106 e fm².

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Blomquist and Molinari and by Millener and Kurath on the role of the tensor interaction in light nuclei, and directly addresses the overestimation of tensor effects in small-space shell-model calculations noted by Zamick, Zheng, and Fayache in prior work. The results show that higher-shell admixtures naturally weaken the effective tensor interaction, contradicting the need for medium modifications of the tensor force that have been proposed from nucleon-nucleus scattering analyses by Hintz and Stephenson and from the Brown-Rho universal scaling idea. The paper enables systematic shell-model calculations in larger spaces without requiring ad hoc adjustments to the tensor interaction strength for nuclear structure applications, while leaving open the question of why scattering data appear to require such modifications.
