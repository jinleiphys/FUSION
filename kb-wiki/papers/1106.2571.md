# Thermal Upsilon(1s) and chi_b1 suppression in sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> I compute the thermal suppression of the Upsilon(1s) and chi_b1 states in sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Using the suppression of each of these states I estimate the total R_AA for the Upsilon(1s) state as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum. I find less suppression of the chi_b1 state than would be traditionally assumed; however, my final results for the total Upsilon(1s) suppression are in good agreement with recent preliminary CMS data.

## Key claim

The author computes thermal suppression of the Upsilon(1s) and chi_b1 states in sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions and finds less suppression of the chi_b1 state than traditionally assumed, but the final results for total Upsilon(1s) suppression agree with preliminary CMS data only under the condition that the chi_b1's finite formation time and the transverse temperature profile are properly accounted for.

## Method

The author combines anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) temporal evolution with a complex-valued heavy quark potential that includes both real and imaginary parts, solving the Schrodinger equation via the finite difference time domain method. The system is evolved from tau_0=0.3 fm/c until the local energy density drops below that of an ideal gas at T=192 MeV, using a Glauber model for transverse participant density and assuming initial anisotropy xi_0=0. The suppression factor R_AA is computed by integrating the instantaneous thermal decay rate over proper time, accounting for formation time dilation and feed-down from excited states assuming all excited states have the same R_AA as the chi_b1.

## Key numbers

- Central temperatures for zero impact parameter collisions: T_0 = {520, 504, 494} MeV for 4 pi eta/S = {1, 2, 3} respectively
- Formation times in local rest frame: tau_form^0 = 0.2 fm/c for Upsilon(1s) and 0.4 fm/c for chi_b1
- In an isotropic plasma, the medium-induced width of the Upsilon(1s) is approximately Im[E_bind] ~ 0.211 T
- String tension sigma = 0.223 GeV and alpha = 0.385 used in the potential model
- Percentage of directly produced Upsilon(1s) states from CDF: 50.9 +/- 8.2 (stat) +/- 9.0 (syst) percent
- Termination temperature for evolution: T = 192 MeV
- Initial evolution time: tau_0 = 0.3 fm/c

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quarkonium suppression in isotropic plasmas by Laine et al. (2006) and Brambilla et al. (2008) to the case of momentum-space anisotropic plasmas, using the anisotropic hydrodynamics formalism developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Ryblewski (2010). The results are compared to preliminary CMS data from 2011 and enable future inclusion of other excited states (Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s)) and transverse expansion effects.
