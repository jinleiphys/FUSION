# Dielectric properties of interacting storage ring plasmas

> A dielectric function including collisional correlations is derived by linearizing the self consistent Vlasov equation with a Fokker-Planck collision integral. The calculation yields the same type of dielectric function as in the standard-theory of Schottky-noise in storage rings. This dielectric function is compared with the Mermin-dielectric function derived from a kinetic equation with relaxation time approximation. We observe that these functions are identical, however the Mermin-DF is computationally advantageous. The limits of both dielectric functions are given and the sum rules are proven. We apply these dielectric functions for typical storage ring plasmas and calculate the stopping power and the plasmon excitation spectrum.

## Key claim

The dielectric function derived from the Vlasov-Fokker-Planck equation with a Fokker-Planck collision integral is numerically identical to the Mermin dielectric function derived from a relaxation-time approximation, but the Mermin form is computationally much simpler; this identity holds for the isotropic, three-dimensional storage ring plasmas considered, within the limits of weak collisions (low q-values) where the Fokker-Planck expansion is valid.

## Method

The authors linearized the self-consistent Vlasov equation with a Fokker-Planck collision integral (including dynamical friction and diffusion linked by the Einstein relation) to derive a dielectric function for an isotropic three-dimensional plasma. Separately, they re-derived the Mermin dielectric function from a kinetic equation with a relaxation-time approximation that conserves particle number by relaxing to a local equilibrium distribution. They compared the two dielectric functions numerically for typical storage ring parameters and analytically in the static, long-wavelength, and strong-friction limits, then applied the Mermin form to compute stopping power and Schottky noise spectra.

*   For \(^{12}\)C\(^{6+}\) ions: density \(n = 1.6 \times 10^{13}\,\text{m}^{-3}\), longitudinal temperature \(T_\parallel = 8000\,\text{K}\), plasma parameter \(\Gamma = 0.0031\), plasma frequency \(\omega_{pl} = 9.2 \times 10^{6}\,\text{s}^{-1}\), collision frequency \(\lambda = 4.0 \times 10^{5}\,\text{s}^{-1}\).
*   For electrons in the cooler: density \(n = 2.9 \times 10^{13}\,\text{m}^{-3}\), temperature \(3.5\,\text{K}\), plasma parameter \(\Gamma = 0.23\), plasma frequency \(\omega_{pl} = 3.0 \times 10^{8}\,\text{s}^{-1}\), collision frequency \(\lambda = 3.4 \times 10^{12}\,\text{s}^{-1}\).
*   The Mermin and VFP dielectric functions are identical within numerical accuracy for the tested parameters (temperatures \(0.6\)–\(60\,\text{K}\), wave numbers below and above the inverse Debye length).
*   For \(^9\)Be\(^+\) at \(\Gamma = 0.77\) (lower temperature), the Mermin stopping power becomes smaller than the Lindhard result; at \(\Gamma = 0.13\) almost no difference is observed.
*   The modified Mermin dielectric function fits the measured Schottky spectrum for a \(^{12}\)C\(^{6+}\) beam at \(73.3\,\text{MeV}\) with revolution frequency \(617\,\text{kHz}\) and harmonic number \(M=5\) (density \(n = 8.3 \times 10^{13}\,\text{m}^{-3}\), \(T_\parallel = 11000\,\text{K}\)).

## Context

This work extends the standard Schottky-noise theory of Park (1980) by showing that the dielectric function derived from the Vlasov-Fokker-Planck equation is identical to the Mermin dielectric function (Mermin, 1970), enabling easier computation of collective excitations and stopping power in storage ring plasmas. The results are applied to experiments at the Heidelberg TSR with \(^9\)Be\(^+\) and \(^{12}\)C\(^{6+}\) beams (Habs, 1995; Sram, 1997). The inclusion of collisions via the Mermin dielectric function modifies the stopping power in the direction of binary collision T-matrix results (Morawetz and Röpke, 1996) and simulation data (Zwicknagel, 1996), but still overestimates the friction force compared to those benchmarks, indicating the need for further strong-coupling corrections.
