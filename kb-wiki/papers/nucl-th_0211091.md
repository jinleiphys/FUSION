# Consequences of kinetic non-equilibrium for the nuclear equation-of-state in heavy ion collision

> Highly compressed nuclear matter created in relativistic heavy collisions is to large extent governed by local non-equilibrium. As an idealized scenario colliding nuclear matter configurations are studied within both, relativistic mean field theory and using more realistic in-medium interactions based on the Dirac-Brueckner T-matrix. The equation of state in anisotropic matter is thereby governed by two competing effects: The enlarged phase space volume in colliding matter tends to soften the internal potential energy of the subsystems whereas the relative motion of the two currents leads to a strong additional repulsion in the system. An effective EOS constructed for anisotropic momentum configurations shows a significant net softening compared to ground state nuclear matter. This effect is found to be to large extend independent on the particular choice of the nuclear interaction. A critical discussion of standard transport approaches with respect to the considered non-equilibrium effects is given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear equation-of-state (EOS) extracted from heavy ion collisions is significantly softened by kinetic non-equilibrium effects compared to ground state nuclear matter, and this net softening is largely independent of the particular choice of nuclear interaction model.

## Method

The authors study idealized colliding nuclear matter (CNM) configurations consisting of two counter-streaming Fermi ellipsoids, using both relativistic mean field theory (Walecka model QHD-I) and the more microscopic Dirac-Brueckner (DB) T-matrix approach. They compute the energy-momentum tensor for these anisotropic momentum configurations and derive an effective EOS by subtracting the kinetic energy of the relative motion between the two currents from the total energy density.

## Key numbers

- The Walecka model QHD-I yields a binding energy of -15.75 MeV, a saturation Fermi momentum of k_F_sat = 1.42 fm^-1, an effective mass ratio M*/M = 0.556, and a compression modulus of K = 540 MeV
- At subsystem densities rho_0 = rho_sat, the effective mass in CNM drops and reaches an asymptotic value around streaming velocity u/c = 0.7 (corresponding to E_lab = 1.8 GeV)
- The effective mass in CNM at zero velocity gives the nuclear matter result at twice saturation density (rho = 2 rho_sat)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of colliding nuclear matter by Sehn (1996) and others who treated CNM within the Walecka model, and by Gmat2 (1992) and Elsenhans (1992) who used non-relativistic Brueckner G-matrix forces. The paper provides a critical discussion of standard transport approaches (BUU and QMD) which typically use phenomenological density-dependent mean fields that do not fully account for the non-equilibrium phase space configurations present during the high-density compression phase of heavy ion collisions. The findings enable more accurate extraction of the ground state EOS from heavy ion reaction data by properly accounting for the separation of compression energy from relative motion and thermal contributions.
