# Low Energy alpha-alpha Semimicroscopic Potentials

> The $\alp-\alp$ interaction potential is obtained within the double folding model with density-dependent Gogny effective interactions as input. The one nucleon knock-on exchange kernel including recoil effects is localized using the Perey-Saxon prescription at zero energy. The Pauli forbidden states are removed thanks to successive supersymmetric transformations. Low energy experimental phase shifts, calculated from the variable phase approach, as well as the energy and width of the first $0^+$ resonance in $^8$Be are reproduced with high accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the alpha-alpha interaction potential derived from the double folding model with density-dependent Gogny effective interactions, after removing Pauli-forbidden bound states via supersymmetric transformations, reproduces low-energy experimental phase shifts and the first 0+ resonance in 8Be with high accuracy, but only after applying a renormalization factor λ close to unity (within 9% of 1) and using the Perey-Saxon localization prescription at zero energy.

## Method

The alpha-alpha interaction potential was calculated using the double folding model with Gaussian one-body densities for the alpha particle (oscillator parameter b adjusted to the root mean square radius) and three parametrizations of the Gogny effective interaction (D1, D1S, D1N) as input. The one-nucleon knock-on exchange kernel including recoil effects was localized using the lowest-order Perey-Saxon prescription at zero energy, producing both sub-barrier and over-barrier branches for the real part of the local exchange potential. Pauli-forbidden 0S and 1S bound states were removed via successive supersymmetric transformations following the formalism of Baye, and the resulting phase shifts were calculated using the variable phase method of Calogero with proper boundary conditions for singular potentials.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator parameter b for the alpha-particle density is adjusted to reproduce the root mean square radius of 1.58 ± 0.002 fm, extracted from a Glauber analysis of experimental interaction cross sections
- The deep folding potentials have an average strength of 78 ± 7 MeV, close to the RGM calculation value of Schmid and Wildermuth
- The BFW potential has two ℓ=0 bound states located at -72.79 MeV and -25.88 MeV respectively
- The renormalization factor λ values are: 0.9920 (BFW), 0.9891 (D1), 0.9873 (D1N), 0.9906 (D1S)
- The fitted SUSY potentials have a long-range attractive component with μa = 0.442 ± 0.005 fm⁻¹
- The resonance energy is 0.092 MeV (the first 0+ resonance in ⁸Be)
- Varying the third decimal of λ varies the position of the resonance by 5×10⁻⁴
- The phase shift integration was performed up to 500 fm in steps of 0.001 fm
- The Gaussian fit was restricted to the radial range r = 1.5-10 fm
- The potentials are considered physically meaningful in the energy range Elab = 0-5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the double folding model approach to the alpha-alpha system using Gogny effective interactions, building on prior RGM calculations by Schmid and Wildermuth and the phenomenological potentials of Buck, Friedrich and Wheatley (BFW) and Ali and Bodmer. The paper also references earlier work by Sofianos et al. who derived the alpha-alpha potential using the energy density formalism based on Skyrme effective interactions. The resulting SUSY potentials enable calculations of alpha matter ground state properties and alpha-cluster models of nuclei, with the Gaussian expansion facilitating practical applications in the energy range up to 5 MeV.
