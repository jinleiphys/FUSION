# Dissipative dynamics of fission in the framework of asymptotic expansion of Fokker-Planck equation

> The dynamics of fission has been formulated by generalising the asymptotic expansion of the Fokker-Planck equation in terms of the strength of the fluctuations where the diffusion coefficients depend on the stochastic variables explicitly. The prescission neutron multiplicities and the mean kinetic energies of the evaporated neutrons have been calculated and compared with the respective experimental data over a wide range of excitation energy and compound nuclear mass. The mean and variance of the total kinetic energies of the fission fragments have been calculated and compared with the experimental values.

# Dissipative dynamics of fission in the framework of asymptotic expansion of Fokker-Planck equation

## Key claim
The authors present a generalized asymptotic expansion of the Fokker-Planck equation for systems where diffusion coefficients depend explicitly on stochastic variables, and apply it to fission dynamics under the condition that the relative fluctuation of the total kinetic energy is small compared to its mean value (typically the ratio of root-mean-square deviation to mean kinetic energy is ~0.1). The formalism reduces the dynamics to a set of linear ordinary differential equations for the moments, avoiding the need to solve the full Fokker-Planck or Langevin equations.

## Method
The authors generalize van Kampen's asymptotic expansion method (van Kampen, 1965) for the Fokker-Planck equation to cases where diffusion coefficients depend on the stochastic variables, as occurs in fission when friction depends on instantaneous nuclear shape. They apply this formalism to a one-dimensional elongation degree of freedom (the centre-to-centre distance between fission fragments), solving the Euler-Lagrange equations for deterministic motion and coupled first-order differential equations for the variances. The calculation uses a combination of one-body wall friction (attenuated to 10% of the original wall value) in the ground-state-to-saddle region and two-body friction (viscosity coefficient 4 × 10⁻²³ MeV·s·fm⁻³, corresponding to 0.06 TP) in the saddle-to-scission region, with nuclear shapes described by a parameterization that includes elongation and mass asymmetry.

## Key numbers
- The viscosity coefficient used in the saddle-to-scission region is μ₀ = 4 × 10⁻²³ MeV·s·fm⁻³, corresponding to 0.06 TP (1 TP = 6.24 × 10⁻²² MeV·s·fm⁻³).
- The constant K in the friction form factor h(α) = exp(-K α²) was found to be K = 161 ± 3, independent of the compound system mass.
- The ratio of root-mean-square deviation to mean kinetic energy is typically ~0.1, justifying the asymptotic expansion approach.
- The scission elongation parameter c_sc depends on asymmetry α as c_sc = -2.0 α² + 0.032 α + 2.0917.
- The level density parameter is a = A/10, where A is the compound nucleus mass number.
- The initial velocity is given by ṙ₀ = (E*₀ R_N / 2μ)^(1/2), where R_N is a random number between 0 and 1.
- The initial position dispersion is δr₀ = [ħ/(2ωμ) coth(ħω/(2T(r=r_min)))]^(1/2).

## Context
This work extends the asymptotic expansion method of van Kampen (1965) for stochastic processes with constant diffusion coefficients to the case of fission, where diffusion coefficients depend explicitly on the stochastic variables (shape-dependent friction). It builds on the authors' previous work (Dhara, 1999) and the shape parameterization of Brack (1972), and provides an alternative to solving the full Langevin equation (as done by Abe, Fröbrich, and Pomorski) or multidimensional Fokker-Planck equations (as done by FP1-FP4). The formalism enables calculation of prescission neutron multiplicities, mean neutron kinetic energies, and the mean and variance of total kinetic energies of fission fragments without solving the full stochastic differential equations, and can be extended to include the neck degree of freedom in future work.
