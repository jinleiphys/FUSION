# Comment on ``Validity of certain soft-photon amplitudes''

> The criteria suggested by Welsh and Fearing (nucl-th/9606040) to judge the validity of certain soft-photon amplitudes are examined. We comment on aspects of their analysis which lead to incorrect conclusions about published amplitudes and point out important criteria which were omitted from their analysis.

# Comment on "Validity of certain soft-photon amplitudes"

## Key claim
The criteria proposed by Welsh and Fearing (1996) for judging the validity of soft-photon amplitudes lead to incorrect conclusions about published amplitudes, because they omitted important criteria and used improper procedures for constructing and antisymmetrizing the amplitudes. Specifically, the claim that the TuTts amplitude cannot be antisymmetrized while being written in terms of the measurable pp elastic amplitude is incorrect; the properly antisymmetrized TuTts amplitude exists and satisfies the Pauli principle.

## Method
The authors examine the procedures used by Welsh and Fearing (1996) to construct soft-photon amplitudes for nuclear bremsstrahlung, focusing on the phase space problem and the symmetrization problem. They compare the Welsh-Fearing approach with the derivation methods of Liou et al. (1993, 1996), showing that the Welsh-Fearing three-step procedure (choose an unsymmetrized amplitude, exchange outgoing momenta, then symmetrize) lacks a guiding prescription for the initial step, which can lead to incorrect amplitudes. The authors demonstrate that the TuTts amplitude $M^{TuTts}_{\mu}$ from Liou et al. (1996) is properly antisymmetrized and expressed in terms of the standard GGMW representation for pp elastic scattering, while the alternative amplitude $M^{TuTts}_{2\mu}$ violates the Pauli principle at order O(K), not O(K/K) as claimed by Welsh and Fearing.

## Key numbers
- The violation of the Pauli principle in the $M^{TuTts}_{2\mu}$ amplitude occurs at order O(K), not at O(K/K) as stated by Welsh and Fearing (1996).
- Coplanar $pp\gamma$ cross sections at 157 MeV for symmetric proton scattering angles of 10 degrees are almost identical for the two amplitudes $M^{TuTts}_{\mu}$ and $M^{TuTts}_{2\mu}$, except when the photon angle $\psi_{\gamma}$ is near 180 degrees.
- TRIUMF cross section data at 280 MeV, which disagree with some predictions calculated using the Low amplitude (Michaelian et al., 1990; Liou et al., 1995), can be described satisfactorily by the $M^{TuTts}_{\mu}$ amplitude.

## Context
This comment extends the debate on soft-photon amplitudes for nuclear bremsstrahlung, specifically addressing the work of Welsh and Fearing (1996) on validity criteria. It builds on the two-class framework of Liou et al. (1993, 1995, 1996), where TuTts amplitudes (evaluated using Mandelstam variables $u_1, u_2, t_p, t_q$) are optimal for processes with strong u-channel exchange effects, and TsTts/TETAS amplitudes (evaluated at $s_i, s_f, t_p, t_q$) are optimal for processes with strong s-channel resonance effects. The comment clarifies that the TETAS amplitudes, which successfully describe bremsstrahlung near resonances such as $\pi^+p\gamma$ (Nefkens et al., 1979; Lin et al., 1991) and $p^{12}C\gamma$ (Yan et al., 1992), satisfy an important criterion neglected by Welsh and Fearing: a valid amplitude must predict the correct energy position and width of the resonant peak. The conventional Low amplitude (Low, 1958) fails to describe these resonance data correctly.
