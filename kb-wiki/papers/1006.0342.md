# Neural Network Parameterizations of Electromagnetic Nucleon Form Factors

> The electromagnetic nucleon form-factors data are studied with artificial feed forward neural networks. As a result the unbiased model-independent form-factor parametrizations are evaluated together with uncertainties. The Bayesian approach for the neural networks is adapted for chi2 error-like function and applied to the data analysis. The sequence of the feed forward neural networks with one hidden layer of units is considered. The given neural network represents a particular form-factor parametrization. The so-called evidence (the measure of how much the data favor given statistical model) is computed with the Bayesian framework and it is used to determine the best form factor parametrization.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Bayesian neural networks provide an unbiased, model-independent parametrization of electromagnetic nucleon form factors with quantified uncertainties, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the network architecture selected by the highest Bayesian evidence (rather than by cross-validation or ad-hoc functional forms) objectively resolves the bias-variance trade-off for the limited experimental datasets (26 to 57 points per form factor).

## Method

The authors trained feed-forward multi-layer perceptron networks with one hidden layer (type 1-M-1, where M is the number of hidden neurons) to fit experimental data for the four nucleon electromagnetic form factors (G_Ep, G_Mp, G_En, G_Mn) as functions of Q^2. They applied a Bayesian framework adapted for chi-squared error minimization, using a weight-decay regularization term with the alpha parameter optimized online, and selected the best network architecture by computing the Bayesian evidence for networks with M=1 to 5 hidden units. For each architecture, a large sample of networks (150 to 1300, depending on the dataset) with randomized initial weights was trained using the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm, and the ten fits with the lowest total error were retained; among these, the fit with the highest evidence was chosen as the best parametrization.

## Key numbers

* The proton electric form factor G_Ep data were analyzed with 1-3-1 network type, yielding a distribution of total error S/N from the network sample.
* The proton magnetic form factor G_Mp data were analyzed with 1-3-1 network type, yielding a distribution of total error S/N from the network sample.
* For the neutron magnetic form factor G_Mn data, networks with M=1 to 5 hidden units were compared; the 1-3-1 network was indicated by the maximal evidence as the best fit.
* For the neutron electric form factor G_En data, networks with M=1 to 5 hidden units were compared; the 1-2-1 network was indicated by the maximal evidence as the best fit.
* The sample sizes required to determine a clear S_cut value were: 150 for G_En data, 250 for G_Mp data, 700 for G_Mn data, and 1300 for G_Ep data.
* The best fits were characterized by chi-squared per degree of freedom less than 1: chi^2(w_MP, D)/(N-W) < 1.

## Context

This work extends the neural-network approach previously applied by the NNPDF collaboration (Forte et al., 2002; Del Debbio et al., 2004; Ball et al., 2008) to nucleon structure functions, but adapts it to the smaller datasets available for electromagnetic form factors (26-57 points) where cross-validation is impractical. The method provides model-independent parametrizations with uncertainties that can replace ad-hoc functional forms (such as those of Kelly, 2004; Arrington, 2006; Bodek, 2007) in applications like neutrino-nucleus cross-section predictions for long-baseline oscillation experiments (K2K, T2K) and extraction of nucleon strangeness from elastic neutral-current scattering.
