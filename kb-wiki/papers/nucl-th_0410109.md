# Sensitivity of 8B breakup cross section to projectile structure in CDCC calculations

> Given the Astrophysical interest of $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B, there have been several experiments applying the Coulomb dissociation method for extracting the capture rate. Measurements at Michigan State are dominated by $E1$ contributions but have a small $E2$ component. On the other hand, a lower energy measurement at Notre Dame has a much stronger $E2$ contribution. The expectation was that the two measurements would tie down the $E2$ and thus allow for an accurate extraction of the $E1$ relevant for the capture process. The aim of this brief report is to show that the $E2$ factor in breakup reactions does not translate into a scaling of the $E2$ contribution in the corresponding capture reaction. We show that changes to the $^8$B single particle parameters, which are directly related to the $E2$ component in the capture reaction, do not effect the corresponding breakup reactions, using the present reaction theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the quadrupole (E2) strength in breakup reactions, as computed within the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, does not directly translate into the E2 strength of the corresponding astrophysical capture reaction \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B; modifications to the \(^8\)B single-particle parameters that drastically change the E2 capture cross section have negligible effects on the breakup cross sections for both the Michigan State University (MSU) and Notre Dame experiments.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations for the breakup of \(^8\)B on Pb targets at 44 and 81 MeV/A (MSU data) and on a \(^{58}\)Ni target at 25.6 MeV (Notre Dame data), using the code FRESCO. They modified the \(p\)-\(^7\)Be interaction by reducing the depth of the potential for p- and f-wave scattering states to one half of its original value, which produced a large change in the E2 capture cross section, and compared the resulting breakup observables to those from the original interaction and from calculations with an artificial 1.6 scaling of the quadrupole excitation couplings.

## Key numbers

- The E2 capture cross section for \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B was computed with the original interaction (solid line) and with the p- and f-wave potential depth reduced to one half (dashed line), showing a large modification (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given in text).
- For the MSU data at 44 MeV/A on Pb with \(\theta_{\mathrm{max}}=1.5^\circ\), the modified interaction produced virtually no effect on the \(^7\)Be momentum distribution (Fig. 2).
- For the MSU data at 81 MeV/A on Pb with \(\theta_{\mathrm{max}}=1.5^\circ\), the modified interaction again produced virtually no effect (Fig. 3).
- For the MSU data at 44 MeV/A on Pb with \(\theta_{\mathrm{max}}=3.5^\circ\), a small effect was seen (Fig. 4).
- For the Notre Dame data, the ratio of the E2-only breakup cross sections at the peak of the energy distribution for \(20^\circ\) between the scaled (1.6 factor) and original models was 2.7; when E1 and nuclear contributions were included, the ratio became 1.7.
- The Notre Dame data could not be described satisfactorily when the quadrupole strength was multiplied by a factor of 1.6.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC calculations of Nunes (1999) and Tostevin (2001) for \(^8\)B breakup, and directly addresses the findings of Mortimer (2002) that an artificial 1.6 factor in the quadrupole excitation couplings was needed to fit MSU data. The paper contradicts the expectation that combining the MSU and Notre Dame measurements would tie down the E2 component for the capture reaction, showing instead that the quadrupole strength in breakup reactions is not simply related to the E2 capture strength. The results suggest that additional physics, such as the 15% core-excited component of \(^8\)B (GSI), not included in the single-particle CDCC model, may be needed to consistently describe both experiments.
