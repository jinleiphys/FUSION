# Functional renormalization group study of the jet quenching parameter near the QCD critical end point

> We investigate the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ in the QCD phase diagram within a QCD-assisted low-energy effective theory using the functional renormalization group (fRG). Following the formalism that relates $\hat{q}$ to the spectral functions of the chiral order-parameter field, we compute the $σ$ and $π$ meson contributions to $\hat{q}$ at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential from analytically continued mesonic two-point functions. We find that $\hat{q}$ receives appreciable contributions mainly above the chiral phase boundary and exhibits a pronounced enhancement at large baryon chemical potential as the chiral crossover sharpens toward the critical end point (CEP), a behavior consistent with the picture of partonic critical opalescence (PCO), a pronounced enhancement of jet transverse momentum broadening induced by the critical $σ$ field fluctuations.

## Key claim

The jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ shows a pronounced enhancement at large baryon chemical potential as the chiral crossover sharpens toward the critical end point (CEP), consistent with partonic critical opalescence, but the authors note that a definitive identification of genuine CEP-driven critical enhancement versus gradual enhancement from the sharpening crossover requires extending calculations to the immediate vicinity of and beyond the CEP.

## Method

The authors compute the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ within a two-flavor Polyakov-loop quark-meson (PQM) model using the functional renormalization group (fRG). They obtain the $\sigma$ and $\pi$ meson spectral functions from analytically continued retarded two-point correlation functions, which are computed directly from the Wetterich flow equation using 3D regulators that allow explicit analytic continuation in external frequency. The running Yukawa coupling between quarks and mesons is determined by matching its RG flow to that obtained from first-principles fRG calculations of 2+1-flavor QCD at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The CEP is located at $(T_\mathrm{CEP}, \mu_{B_\mathrm{CEP}}) = (98, 643)$ MeV in the QCD-assisted low-energy effective theory
- The $\sigma$ and $\pi$ spectral functions are shown at $\mu_B = 636$ MeV and $|\bm{p}| = 0.3$ GeV for temperatures $T = 100$ MeV, $T = 125$ MeV, and $T = 185$ MeV
- The jet quenching parameter is computed for a jet quark with initial momentum $|\bm{q}| = 3$ GeV
- At $\mu_B = 636$ MeV and $T = 185$ MeV, the $\sigma$ and $\pi$ spectral functions are nearly degenerate, consistent with approximate chiral symmetry restoration
- The $\hat{q}_\pi$ contribution is approximately three times as large as $\hat{q}_\sigma$ across the phase diagram, consistent with the pion degeneracy factor $d_\pi = 3$

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Wu et al. (2022) that relates $\hat{q}$ to spectral functions of the chiral order-parameter field, moving beyond their mean-field treatment which required external mapping to the Ising model to capture critical behavior. The fRG approach employed here generates the enhancement of $\hat{q}$ near the CEP directly through the Wetterich flow equation, without resorting to an external universality-class mapping. The results are consistent with the partonic critical opalescence (PCO) scenario and suggest that jet observables may serve as a valuable tool for probing the QCD phase structure, with the enhancement extending along the phase boundary toward smaller $\mu_B$ rather than being confined to a narrow window near the CEP.
