# Second-Order Eikonal Corrections for A(e,e'p)

> The first-order eikonal approximation is frequently adopted in interpreting the results of $A(e,e'p)$ measurements. Glauber calculations, for example, typically adopt the first-order eikonal approximation. We present an extension of the relativistic eikonal approach to $A(e,e'p)$ which accounts for second-order eikonal corrections. The numerical calculations are performed within the relativistic optical model eikonal approximation. The nuclear transparency results indicate that the effect of the second-order eikonal corrections is rather modest, even at $Q^{2} \approx 0.2$ (GeV/c)$^2$. The same applies to polarization observables, left-right asymmetries, and differential cross sections at low missing momenta. At high missing momenta, however, the second-order eikonal corrections are significant and bring the calculations in closer agreement with the data and/or the exact results from models adopting partial-wave expansions.

## Key claim

The second-order eikonal corrections to the relativistic optical model eikonal approximation (ROMEA) for A(e,e'p) reactions are modest for most observables at Q² ≥ 0.2 (GeV/c)², but become significant (up to 30%) at high missing momenta, where they bring calculations into closer agreement with exact partial-wave results and data.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic optical model eikonal approximation (ROMEA) by deriving a second-order eikonal factor from Baker's (1972) integral equation for potential scattering, including both central and spin-orbit optical potentials. The formalism was applied to compute nuclear transparencies, induced normal polarization P_n, left-right asymmetry A_LT, and differential cross sections for A(e,e'p) reactions on ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁵⁶Fe, using the EDAD1 and EDAI parametrizations of the optical potential from Cooper et al. (1993). Results were compared to data and to relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations from the Madrid-Sevilla group.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 1.7 (GeV/c)², ROMEA and SOROMEA transparencies agree to within 1%; at Q² = 0.3 (GeV/c)², the difference rises to 3% for ⁵⁶Fe and 5% for ¹²C.
- For ¹²C at Q² ≈ 0.5 (GeV/c)², second-order corrections reduce the induced normal polarization P_n for the 1s₁/₂ level by roughly 20% at missing momenta p_m > 125 MeV/c, while for 1p₃/₂ knockout the effect is smaller than 5%.
- For ¹⁶O(e,e'p) at Q² ≈ 0.8 (GeV/c)², second-order eikonal corrections affect differential cross sections by up to 30% at high missing momenta, but are almost negligible for p_m below the Fermi momentum.
- Spectroscopic factors extracted from ¹⁶O data: ROMEA gives 0.84 (1p₃/₂) and 0.75 (1p₁/₂); SOROMEA gives 0.83 (1p₃/₂) and 0.74 (1p₁/₂); RDWIA gives 0.92 (1p₃/₂) and 0.78 (1p₁/₂).

## Context

This work extends the ROMEA framework of Debruyne et al. (2000) by incorporating second-order eikonal corrections following Baker (1972), and applies it to A(e,e'p) reactions for the first time. The results show that the first-order eikonal approximation (used in standard Glauber calculations) is robust down to proton kinetic energies of about 200 MeV, explaining the success of Glauber approaches in this regime. The second-order corrections enable more accurate comparisons with exact RDWIA calculations (Udias et al., 1993, 2000) and with data at high missing momenta, where meson-exchange and Δ-isobar currents also become important (Ryckebusch et al., 1999).
