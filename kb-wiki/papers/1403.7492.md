# Finite-size effects at the hadron-quark transition and heavy hybrid stars

> We study the role of finite-size effects at the hadron-quark phase transition in a new hybrid equation of state constructed from an ab-initio Brückner-Hartree-Fock equation of state with the realistic Bonn-B potential for the hadronic phase and a covariant non-local Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model for the quark phase. We construct static hybrid star sequences and find that our model can support stable hybrid stars with an onset of quark matter below $2 M_\odot$ and a maximum mass above $2.17 M_\odot$ in agreement with recent observations. If the finite-size effects are taken into account the core is composed of pure quark matter. Provided that the quark vector channel interaction is small, and the finite size effects are taken into account, quark matter appears at densities 2-3 times the nuclear saturation density. In that case the proton fraction in the hadronic phase remains below the value required by the onset of the direct URCA process, so that the early onset of quark matter shall affect on the rapid cooling of the star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including finite-size effects (surface tension and charge screening) at the hadron-quark phase transition allows stable hybrid stars with a pure quark matter core and a maximum mass above 2.17 solar masses, provided the quark vector channel interaction is small (eta_V = 0.10). The abstract's claim of agreement with observations is qualified by the condition that the vector coupling must be weak; for stronger coupling (eta_V = 0.20) the hybrid star branch lies on the borderline of stability and quark matter appears only at higher densities.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by combining a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation with the Bonn-B potential and three-body forces for the hadronic phase, and a two-flavor covariant non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model with vector interactions for the quark phase. They apply the Gibbs conditions for the mixed phase using a Wigner-Seitz approximation with sharp boundaries, including surface tension (sigma = 10 and 40 MeV fm^-2) and Coulomb energy, and solve the Poisson equation self-consistently to obtain density profiles and the equation of state for pasta structures (droplets, rods, slabs). Static hybrid star sequences are then computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for eta_V = 0.10 and sigma = 10 MeV fm^-2: 2.17 solar masses (M_sun).
- Maximum mass for eta_V = 0.20 and sigma = 10 MeV fm^-2: 2.49 M_sun.
- Maximum mass for bulk Gibbs construction (vanishing surface tension) at eta_V = 0.10: 2.19 M_sun.
- Maximum mass for Maxwell construction (strong surface tension limit) at eta_V = 0.10: 2.17 M_sun.
- Onset of quark matter at densities 2-3 times nuclear saturation density (for eta_V = 0.10 with finite-size effects).
- Optimal droplet radius at n_B = 0.70 fm^-3 and sigma = 10 MeV fm^-2: R = 4.50 fm, with cell size R_W = 8.98 fm.
- Optimal droplet radius at n_B = 0.70 fm^-3 and sigma = 40 MeV fm^-2: R = 9.14 fm, with cell size R_W = 18.3 fm.
- Surface tension values used: sigma = 10 and 40 MeV fm^-2.
- Current quark mass set to m = 2.37 MeV for u and d quarks.
- Threshold proton fraction for direct URCA process: Y_p = 1/9.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase with finite-size effects (Voskresensky 2001, Endo 2005, Maruyama 2007) by replacing the simple bag model for quark matter with a more sophisticated covariant non-local NJL model that is calibrated to lattice QCD data. The results are consistent with the high-mass pulsar observations of Demorest (2010) for PSR J1614-2230 and Antoniadis (2013) for PSR J0348+0432. The paper enables future studies of rapid cooling in hybrid stars, as the early onset of quark matter with weak vector coupling keeps the proton fraction below the direct URCA threshold, potentially resolving the cooling puzzle.
