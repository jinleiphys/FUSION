# Identification of significant $E0$ strength in the $2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1$ transitions of $^{58, 60, 62}$Ni

> The $E0$ transition strength in the $2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1$ transitions of $^{58,60,62}$Ni have been determined for the first time following a series of measurements at the Australian National University (ANU) and the University of Kentucky (UK). The CAESAR Compton-suppressed HPGe array and the Super-e solenoid at ANU were used to measure the $őī(E2/M1)$ mixing ratio and internal conversion coefficient of each transition following inelastic proton scattering. Level half-lives, $őī(E2/M1)$ mixing ratios and $ő≥$-ray branching ratios were measured at UK following inelastic neutron scattering. The new spectroscopic information was used to determine the $E0$ strengths. These are the first $2^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ $E0$ transition strengths measured in nuclei with spherical ground states and the $E0$ component is found to be unexpectedly large; in fact, these are amongst the largest $E0$ transition strengths in medium and heavy nuclei reported to date.

## Context

This work provides the first measurements of E0 transition strengths between 2‚Āļ states in nuclei with spherical ground states, extending the very limited data set of J‚ÜíJ (J>0) E0 transitions previously reported only in deformed nuclei, mostly in the lanthanide region (Wood, 1999). The results contradict predictions of simple models such as the spherical-quadrupole-vibrator model, where E0 transitions are forbidden if the change in phonon number is one. The microscopic model of Brown (1995), which successfully reproduces 0‚Āļ‚Üí0‚Āļ E0 strengths, fails to reproduce these new 2‚Āļ‚Üí2‚Āļ results, suggesting that second-order corrections involving coupling to the giant quadrupole excitation of the nucleus are needed.
