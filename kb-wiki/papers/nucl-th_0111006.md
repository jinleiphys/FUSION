# $Q^2$-dependence of backward pion multiplicity in neutrino-nucleus interactions

> The production of pions emitted backward in inelastic neutrino-nucleus interactions is analyzed within the impulse approximation in the framework of the dual parton model. We focus on the $Q^2$-dependence of the multiplicity of negative pions, normalized to the total cross section of the reaction $ν+ A \to μ+ X$. The inclusion of planar (one-Reggeon exchange) and cylindrical (one-Pomeron exchange) graphs leads to a multiplicity that decreases as $Q^2$ increases, in agreement with recent measurements carried out at CERN by the NOMAD collaboration. A realistic treatment of the high momentum tail of the nucleon distribution in a nucleus also allows for a satisfactory description of the semi-inclusive spectrum of backward pions.

## Key claim

The multiplicity of backward-emitted negative pions in neutrino-nucleus interactions, normalized to the total cross section, decreases with increasing \(Q^2\) only when both planar (one-Reggeon exchange) and cylindrical (one-Pomeron exchange) graphs are included; the planar contribution falls as \(\sqrt{1/Q^2}\) while the cylindrical contribution rises as \(Q^{2\Delta}\) with \(\Delta \approx 0.1\)–\(0.15\), and the net decrease agrees with NOMAD data.

## Method

The authors compute the relativistic invariant semi-inclusive pion spectrum for the reaction \(\nu + A \to \mu + \pi + X\) within the impulse approximation, using the Quark Gluon String Model (similar to the Dual Parton Model) to describe the elementary \(\nu\)-nucleon vertex. They include both planar (one-Reggeon exchange) and cylindrical (one-Pomeron exchange) topological graphs, and fold the elementary spectra with a nucleon distribution function that incorporates a high-momentum tail beyond the range of nonrelativistic spectral functions. The calculation is applied to the kinematics of the NOMAD experiment on \(\nu + C\) for pions with momenta \(0.35 < p_\pi < 0.8\) GeV/c emitted in the backward hemisphere.

## Key numbers

- The planar graph contribution to the normalized multiplicity \(\langle \tilde{n}_\pi \rangle\) decreases as \(\sqrt{1/Q^2}\) with increasing \(Q^2\) and \(W_X\).
- The cylindrical graph contribution increases as \(Q^{2\Delta}\) with \(\Delta\) in the range \(0.1\)–\(0.15\).
- The cylindrical graph dominates for \(Q^2 > 10\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The average squared nucleon transverse momentum used is \(\langle k_t^2 \rangle = 0.12\)–\(0.14\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), corresponding to an average nucleon momentum of about 0.4 GeV/c.
- The pion momentum range analyzed is \(0.35 < p_\pi < 0.8\) GeV/c.
- The gluon fraction in the nucleon is taken as \(\langle y_g \rangle \simeq 0.15\)–\(0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Benhar et al., 2001, referred to as I) which considered only the cylindrical graph and focused on strictly backward pions; here both planar and cylindrical graphs are included and the full backward hemisphere is treated. The results are compared to data from the NOMAD collaboration at CERN, and the approach builds on the Quark Gluon String Model of Kaidalov (1990s) and the Dual Parton Model of Capella et al. (1990s). The inclusion of the high-momentum tail of the nucleon distribution, described via overlaps of three-quark colorless objects, enables a satisfactory description of the semi-inclusive pion spectrum and the \(Q^2\)-dependence of the multiplicity, while the remaining discrepancy at \(Q^2 > 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) suggests a role for secondary rescattering effects not included in the model.
