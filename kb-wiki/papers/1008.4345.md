# Third Zemach Moment of the Proton

> Modern electron scattering experiments have determined the proton electric form factor, G_{Ep}(Q^2), to high precision. We utilize this data, represented by the different form factor parametrizations, to compute the third Zemach moment of the proton charge distribution. We find that existing data rule out a value of the third Zemach moment large enough to explain the current puzzle with the proton charge radius determined from the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen. This is in contrast with the recent claim of De Rujula [arXiv:1008.3861].

# Third Zemach Moment of the Proton

## Key claim
Existing electron scattering data, as represented by empirical form factor parametrizations, rule out a value of the third Zemach moment large enough to resolve the proton radius puzzle from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements, contrary to the claim of De Rujula (2010). The authors demonstrate that a large third Zemach moment would require an unrealistically large fourth moment of the charge distribution, which is incompatible with measured form factor data.

## Method
The authors compute the third Zemach moment ⟨r_p³⟩_(2) using three different parametrizations of the proton electric form factor G_Ep(Q²): the standard dipole form, the Kelly (2004) parametrization, and fit II of Alberico et al. (2009). They evaluate the Zemach moment integral in momentum space and derive a rigorous upper bound relating the third Zemach moment to the mean-square charge radius ⟨r_p²⟩ and the fourth moment ⟨r_p⁴⟩ of the charge distribution.

## Key numbers
- The third Zemach moment from the dipole form factor: 2.023 fm³
- The third Zemach moment from the Kelly (2004) parametrization: 2.524 fm³
- The third Zemach moment from the Alberico et al. (2009) parametrization: 2.545 fm³
- All three values are approximately a factor of 15 smaller than the De Rujula (2010) claim of 36.6 ± 6.9 fm³
- The De Rujula (2010) toy model gives ⟨r_p⁴⟩ = 1849 fm⁴, compared to empirical values of 1.083 fm⁴ (dipole), 1.619 fm⁴ (Kelly), and 1.623 fm⁴ (Alberico et al.)
- 80% of the strength for the Zemach moment comes from Q² less than 1 GeV², and 50% from Q² less than 0.25 GeV²
- The upper bound on the third Zemach moment with μ = 1 GeV is 3.029 fm³ (dipole), 4.067 fm³ (Kelly), and 4.097 fm³ (Alberico et al.)

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle arising from the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement by Pohl et al. (2010), giving a proton radius of 0.84184 ± 0.00067 fm, and the CODATA compilation value of 0.8768 ± 0.0069 fm. The authors directly contradict the proposal of De Rujula (2010) that a large third Zemach moment could reconcile these values, and instead support the earlier experimental extraction by Friar and Sick (2005) who found ⟨r_p³⟩_(2) = 2.71 ± 0.13 fm³. The analysis enables future investigations of low Q² data to improve G_Ep parametrizations, though the authors conclude that significantly enhancing the Zemach moment above the dipole result is extremely unlikely.
