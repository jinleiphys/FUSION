# Strange and heavy mesons in hadronic matter

> We present selected results on the properties of strange and heavy-flavoured mesons in a hot and dense nuclear medium, with emphasis in selfconsistent coupled-channel approaches based on the chiral Lagrangian. In the strangeness sector, we discuss how the enhanced reactivity of light strange vectors at FAIR conditions can be tied to in-medium effects on their predominant decay modes (e.g. $\bar K^*\to\bar K π$) and to the excitation of strange baryons in vector-meson nucleon interactions. In the heavy-flavour sector, we focus on recent determinations of the transport coefficients of charmed and bottomed mesons in a hadron gas at vanishing baryonic chemical potential. We comment on the role of microscopic transport simulations to establish a connection between theoretical models and experimental observables from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents selected results on strange and heavy-flavored meson properties in hot and dense nuclear matter, emphasizing that selfconsistent coupled-channel approaches based on the chiral Lagrangian can describe in-medium modifications, but the results are conditional on the specific theoretical framework and require further validation through microscopic transport simulations to connect with experimental observables from heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors employed selfconsistent coupled-channel approaches based on the chiral Lagrangian, solving Bethe-Salpeter equations for scattering amplitudes and evaluating meson selfenergies in a hot and dense nuclear medium. For strange mesons, they extended a coupled-channel model to finite temperature to match FAIR conditions, accounting for Pauli-blocking effects, finite-temperature baryonic mean-field potentials, and meson selfenergies of pions and kaons. For heavy-flavor mesons, they used a Fokker-Planck equation approach with scattering amplitudes from an effective field theory constrained by chiral and heavy-quark symmetries, with exact unitarity imposed in partial waves to extend applicability up to temperatures around the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Attractive $\bar K$ potential of about 40-60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ($\rho_0$), obtained from selfconsistent coupled-channel calculations
- Repulsive $K$ mass shift of about 30 MeV in cold matter at normal density, close to the low-density $t\rho$ approximation result
- $\bar K^*$ width enlarged beyond 200 MeV at $\rho=\rho_0$, with the in-medium $\bar K\pi$ cloud contributing about 100 MeV
- Positive $K^*$ mass shift of about 50 MeV at $\rho=\rho_0$ from tree-level amplitudes, reduced to about 40 MeV when full unitarized scattering amplitudes are implemented
- Relaxation time of bottomed mesons at 1 GeV/c momentum: $\lambda_B\simeq 80$ fm at $T=150$ MeV
- Relaxation time of charmed mesons at 1 GeV/c momentum: $\lambda_D\simeq 40$ fm at the same conditions ($T=150$ MeV)
- Total elastic $B\pi$ cross section exhibits a $B_0$ resonance at $\sqrt{s}\simeq 5.53$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous selfconsistent coupled-channel studies of kaon properties in nuclear matter by Ramos (1999) and Tolos (2000) to finite temperature conditions relevant for the FAIR facility, and builds upon heavy-flavor transport coefficient calculations by He (2011), Ghosh (2011), Abreu (2011, 2012), and Das (2011) that used effective field theory approaches. The results enable improved microscopic transport simulations (such as HSD and IQMD approaches) for heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding strangeness production mechanisms and the equilibration of heavy mesons in hadronic matter, with the finding that bottomed mesons may serve as optimal carriers of information about the phase transition due to their long relaxation times compared to typical hadron gas lifetimes.
