# Evolution of Nuclear Spectra with Nuclear Forces

> We first define a series of NN interaction models ranging from very simple to fully realistic. We then present Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei to show how nuclear spectra evolve as the nuclear forces are made increasingly sophisticated. We find that the absence of stable five- and eight-body nuclei depends crucially on the spin, isospin, and tensor components of the nuclear force.

## Key claim

The absence of stable five- and eight-body nuclei depends crucially on the spin, isospin, and tensor components of the nuclear force, but obtaining both the mass gaps at A=5 and 8 *and* stable A=6,7 nuclei is a very sensitive issue that may require both tensor and spin-orbit forces, while a truly good fit to ground state binding energies, spin-orbit splittings, and spin ordering additionally requires multinucleon forces.

## Method

The authors defined a series of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction models ranging from a pure central force (AV1') through progressively more sophisticated potentials (AV2', AVX', AV4', AV6', AV8') to the fully realistic Argonne v18 (AV18) potential, combined with the Illinois 2 (IL2) three-nucleon potential. They performed Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations for 26 states in nuclei from 4He to 10B, evaluating how the energy spectra evolve as the nuclear forces become increasingly sophisticated.

## Key numbers

- The AV18/IL2 combination reproduces 27 narrow states in A ≤ 10 nuclei with an rms deviation of only 600 keV
- The AV8' potential (without Coulomb) gives a deuteron binding energy of 2.24 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.22 MeV
- The AV1' and AVX' potentials produce a deuteron bound by only 0.43 MeV
- The AV2' potential preserves the correct deuteron binding of 2.24 MeV
- The expectation value of the one-pion-exchange potential is typically 80% of ⟨v_ij⟩ for AV6' and above
- The OPE-based weight estimate for relative binding gives values of 18 for 4He and 5He, 21 for 6He and 6Li, 27 for 7Li, 24 for 8He, 36 for 8Be, and 39 for 10B ground states

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of nuclear forces from simple to realistic models, building on the Argonne v18 potential (Wiringa, 1995) and Illinois three-nucleon potentials (Pieper, 2001). It demonstrates that purely central nuclear forces are inadequate beyond the s-shell, and that the spin-isospin and tensor components from one-pion-exchange are vital for reproducing nuclear spectra. The findings support a connection between nuclear structure and QCD, particularly the role of the pion as a Goldstone boson and the dominance of spin-isospin and tensor forces in 1/N_c expansions (Kaplan, 1997). This enables future work to make closer connections between nuclear structure and underlying QCD features.
