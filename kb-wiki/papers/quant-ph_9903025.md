# Quantum Mechanics of Extended Objects

> We propose a quantum mechanics of extended objects that accounts for the finite extent of a particle defined via its Compton wavelength. The Hilbert space representation theory of such a quantum mechanics is presented and this representation is used to demonstrate the quantization of spacetime. The quantum mechanics of extended objects is then applied to two paradigm examples, namely, the fuzzy (extended object) harmonic oscillator and the Yukawa potential. In the second example, we theoretically predict the phenomenological coupling constant of the $ω$ meson, which mediates the short range and repulsive nucleon force, as well as the repulsive core radius.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a quantum mechanics of extended objects that accounts for a particle's finite size via its Compton wavelength, and claims that this formalism can theoretically predict the phenomenological coupling constant of the omega meson and the repulsive core radius in nucleon-nucleon interactions, though the full numerical derivation of these predictions is not presented in the truncated text.

## Method

The author introduces a fuzzy position operator \(X_f = (X e^{-P^2/m^2})\) and a fuzzy time operator \(T_f = (T e^{-H^2/m^2})\), where the Gaussian smearing width is set by the particle's Compton wavelength \(1/m\) (in units where \(\hbar = c = 1\)). The Hilbert space representation theory of these operators is developed, yielding modified commutation relations and uncertainty principles. The formalism is then applied to two systems: the fuzzy harmonic oscillator, solved approximately in the large-wavelength regime, and the Yukawa potential for the deuteron bound state, where a variational method with trial wavefunction \(\psi(p) = e^{-\alpha p r_0}\) is used to obtain a range-depth relation.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding energy is \(E_0 = -2.226\) MeV, used as the variational target for the Yukawa potential analysis.
* The omega meson mass is 782 MeV, and the sigma meson (effective boson) mass is 550 MeV, with phenomenological coupling constants \(g_\omega^2/4\pi = 10.83\) and \(g_\sigma^2/4\pi = 7.303\) from the literature.
* The repulsive hard core radius in nucleon-nucleon interactions is approximately 0.6 fm, as proposed by Jastrow (1951).
* For the fuzzy harmonic oscillator in the large-wavelength approximation, the energy spectrum is \(E_n = (n + 1/2)\omega - \omega^2/(2m)\) up to \(o(\hbar^2)\), and with anharmonic corrections \(E_n = (n + 1/2)\omega - \omega^2/(2m) + (3\omega^2/(4m))(1 + 2n + 2n^2)\).
* The fuzzy position eigenfunctions are shown at ranges \(r_0 = 1.43\) fm and \(r_0 = 0.3596\) fm in figure captions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of minimal length uncertainties previously examined by Kempf, and connects to Snyder's (1947) demonstration of Lorentz-covariant quantized spacetime. The nucleon-nucleon interaction analysis builds on the meson-exchange framework described by Weise and Walecka, and aims to provide a theoretical basis for the phenomenological omega meson coupling constant and repulsive core radius that are normally fitted to scattering data. The formalism could enable descriptions of compound particles such as baryons or mesons in regimes where their nonzero size matters but internal structure details do not contribute.
