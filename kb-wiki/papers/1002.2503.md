# Cold Compressed Baryonic Matter with Hidden Local Symmetry and Holography

> I describe a novel phase structure of cold dense baryonic matter predicted in a hidden local symmetry approach anchored on gauge theory and in a holographic dual approach based on the Sakai-Sugimoto model of string theory. This new phase is populated with baryons with half-instanton quantum number in the gravity sector which is dual to half-skyrmion in gauge sector in which chiral symmetry is restored while light-quark hadrons are in the color-confined phase. It is suggested that such a phase that aries at a density above that of normal nuclear matter and below or at the chiral restoration point can have a drastic influence on the properties of hadrons at high density, in particular on short-distance interactions between nucleons, e.g., multi-body forces at short distance and hadrons -- in particular kaons -- propagating in a dense medium. Potentially important consequences on the structure of compact stars will be predicted.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a novel phase of cold dense baryonic matter, existing at densities above normal nuclear matter density but below or at the chiral restoration point, where chiral symmetry is restored while quarks remain confined in color-singlet hadrons. This phase is populated by half-skyrmions in the gauge sector (or half-instantons in the holographic dual), and the author emphasizes that the predictions are qualitative rather than quantitatively accurate due to the truncated Lagrangian and unknown higher-order parameters.

## Method

The author uses two complementary theoretical approaches: (1) a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian with vector mesons and a dilaton field, treated via skyrmion crystal calculations on an FCC lattice using the Atiyah-Manton ansatz, and (2) the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model in 5D, where baryons arise as instantons stabilized by a Chern-Simons term. In both approaches, dense matter is studied by compressing the crystal lattice (skyrmion case) or by analyzing instanton splitting into half-instanton dyons (holographic case), with the dilaton field in HLS introduced to enforce the vector manifestation fixed point where the gauge coupling vanishes at chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Vector meson mass scale in vacuum: \(m_V \sim 800\) MeV
- Half-skyrmion transition density range: between \(\sim 1.3\) times and \(\sim 3\) times nuclear matter density, depending on Lagrangian parameters
- Binding energy between two half-instanton dyons in the holographic model: roughly 180 MeV
- In the FCC crystal, baryon number density given by \(n = 1/2L^3\), where \(L\) is the lattice size

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach with vector manifestation (HLS/VM) developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model (2005) to cold dense baryonic matter. It builds on prior skyrmion crystal calculations by Park and Vento (2009) and the Atiyah-Manton ansatz (1989), and on the discovery of half-skyrmion phases in condensed matter physics. The predictions enable future experimental tests at facilities like FAIR/GSI, and have potentially important consequences for the equation of state of compact stars, particularly regarding short-distance multi-body forces and kaon propagation in dense medium.
