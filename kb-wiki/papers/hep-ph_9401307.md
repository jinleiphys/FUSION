# Quark cluster signatures in deuteron electromagnetic interactions

> A suggestion is made for distinguishing 2N and 6q short range correlations within the deuteron. The suggestion depends upon observing high momentum backward nucleons emerging from inelastic electromagnetic scattering from a deuteron target. A simple model is worked out to see the size of effects that may be expected.

# Quark cluster signatures in deuteron electromagnetic interactions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the ratio of backward nucleon production cross sections in deep inelastic electromagnetic scattering from deuterons can distinguish between 2N (two-nucleon) and 6q (six-quark cluster) short-range correlations, provided the backward nucleon momentum is above about 300 MeV/c. The signature is that the ratio R1 should be independent of Bjorken x for a pure 2N correlation model, but should show significant x-dependence for a 6q cluster model.

## Method

The authors derive differential cross section formulas for backward proton production from deuterons in deep inelastic lepton scattering, treating separately the 2N correlation case (where the struck neutron's Fermi motion determines the backward proton momentum) and the 6q cluster case (where a quark is struck from a six-quark object and the remaining five quarks fragment into a backward proton). They then compute the ratio R1 = (sigma_meas / K) / F2n(xi) for both models, using quark distribution functions from Carlson and Havens (1983) and CTEQ1L (1993) for the neutron structure function, and counting-rule-based fragmentation functions for the 6q cluster decay.

## Key numbers

- Backward proton momentum threshold of 300 MeV/c is suggested as a starting point for isolating 6q cluster effects, based on existing neutrino scattering data
- For a 4 GeV electron beam with Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the maximum directly backward proton momentum is 564 MeV; for a 6 GeV beam at the same Q^2, the maximum is 609 MeV
- The maximum possible backward proton momentum at infinite energy is 704 MeV (3/4 of the nucleon mass)
- For the 6q model with alpha = 1.4, the ratio R1 shows factors of two difference from maximum to minimum across the accessible x range, while the 2N model predicts no such variation
- For backward neutron versus proton production in the 6q model, the ratio is predicted to be 3/2 independent of x; in the 2N model, the ratio varies from about 1 at low xi to about 4 at high xi

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using neutrino production of backward protons (Carlson, Lassila, and Sukhatme, 1991; Carlson and Lassila, 1993) to electromagnetic interactions, providing an alternative experimental test. The paper contrasts with the 2N correlation model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1979) and the enhanced high-momentum component approach of Atti (1991), which can also reproduce backward proton spectra. The proposed measurement could be performed at CEBAF with a 6 GeV beam or at Fermilab experiment E665 with 490 GeV muons, and would help determine whether the short-range part of the deuteron wave function contains a significant 6q cluster component (estimated at about 5% of the overall deuteron wave function from overlap calculations).
