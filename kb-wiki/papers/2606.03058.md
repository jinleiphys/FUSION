# Chemical Equilibration and Thermalization of Quark-Gluon Plasma in a Parton Cascade Model with 2-to-3 Quark Interactions

> We investigate the thermalization, chemical equilibration, and hydrodynamization behavior of the far-from-equilibrium, gluon-dominated quark gluon plasma (QGP) produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200$ GeV using the hadronic transport model SMASH extended to simulate partonic interactions. The initial conditions are prepared using the mini-jet model with nuclear parton distribution functions. We first validate the model in a box simulation with the periodic boundary condition to establish indicators for thermalization, chemical equilibration, and hydrodynamization by analyzing energy spectrum and momentum anisotropy. We observe that the additional inelastic channels accelerate thermalization and chemical equilibration compared to the gluon-only scheme. Applying the same framework to the expanding medium, we find that the energy spectrum converges toward the Boltzmann distribution at $t \sim 0.2$ fm while momentum isotropization is achieved at $t \sim 2$ fm, but chemical equilibration is not clearly established even after 5 fm. The Knudsen number rises above unity after $\sim 4$ fm, indicating a breakdown of the hydrodynamic regime at later times consistent with other kinetic theory approaches.

## Key claim

The addition of inelastic 2-to-3 channels involving quarks (qg → qgg and qq → qqg) accelerates thermalization and chemical equilibration compared to a gluon-only scheme in a parton cascade model, but in an expanding medium chemical equilibration is not clearly established even after 5 fm, contrary to the faster equilibration suggested by the box simulation.

## Method

The authors extended the hadronic transport model SMASH to simulate partonic interactions by adding partonic particles and cross-sections calculated from perturbative QCD at tree level, including 2-to-2 and 2-to-3 processes with dynamic screening mass regularization. Initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV were prepared using a mini-jet model with nuclear parton distribution functions (nNNPDF), and the system was evolved via Monte-Carlo solution of the Boltzmann transport equation in full 3D with stochastic collision criteria to satisfy detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV; initial conditions yield 250 nucleon-nucleon collisions producing 500 initial partons (all gluons) with average parton energy ⟨Eparton⟩ = 3.25 GeV and total energy ⟨ETotal⟩ = 1629 GeV
- In box simulation at T = 450 MeV (energy density e ∼ 65 GeV/fm³), the system with extra channels reaches gluon number density Boltzmann limit around 30 fm, while the case without extra channels has not reached it even after 50 fm
- In expanding medium, energy spectrum converges toward Boltzmann distribution at t ∼ 0.2 fm, momentum isotropization is achieved at t ∼ 2 fm, but chemical equilibration is not clearly established even after 5 fm
- Knudsen number rises above unity after ∼ 4 fm, indicating breakdown of the hydrodynamic regime at later times

## Context

This work extends earlier kinetic theory studies using BAMPS with 2-to-3 interactions exclusive to gluons (Xu, 2004), which still resulted in longer equilibration and hydrodynamization times than expected. The inclusion of quark-involved 2-to-3 channels addresses the strong quark production at early times and the necessary transition from a gluon-dominated to a quark-dominated system for chemical equilibrium. The results enable comparison of equilibration timescales between box simulations and expanding medium, and the finding that the Knudsen number exceeds unity after ∼4 fm is consistent with other kinetic theory approaches, informing the validity range of hydrodynamic modeling for QGP.
