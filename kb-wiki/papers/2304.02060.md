# Spin-Dependent Interactions and Heavy-Quark Transport in the QGP

> We extend a previously constructed T-matrix approach to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to include the effects of spin-dependent interactions between partons. Following earlier work within the relativistic quark model, the spin-dependent interactions figure as relativistic corrections to the Cornell potential. When applied to the vacuum spectroscopy of quarkonia, in particular their mass splittings in S- and P-wave states, the issue of the Lorentz structure of the confining potential arises. We confirm that a significant admixture of a vector interaction (to the previously assumed scalar interaction) improves the description of the experimental mass splittings. The temperature corrections to the in-medium potential are constrained by results from thermal lattice-QCD for the equation of state (EoS) and heavy-quark (HQ) free energy in a selfconsistent set-up for heavy- and light-parton spectral functions in the QGP. We then deploy the refined in-medium heavy-light T-matrix to compute the charm-quark transport coefficients in the QGP. The vector component of the confining potential, through its relativistic corrections, enhances the friction coefficient for charm quarks in the QGP over previous calculations by tens of percent at low momenta and temperatures, and more at higher momenta. Our results are promising for improving the current phenomenology of open heavy-flavor observables at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously constructed T-matrix approach to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by including spin-dependent interactions as relativistic corrections to the Cornell potential, and they confirm that a significant admixture of a vector interaction (to the previously assumed scalar interaction) in the confining potential improves the description of experimental quarkonium mass splittings, with the vector component enhancing the friction coefficient for charm quarks by tens of percent at low momenta and temperatures.

## Method

The authors implement spin-dependent interactions (spin-orbit, spin-spin, and tensor terms) as relativistic corrections to the Cornell potential within a thermodynamic T-matrix approach, using a mixing parameter χ to control the vector/scalar Lorentz structure of the confining potential. They compute heavy-quarkonium spectral functions in vacuum from the T-matrix to determine bound-state masses, then constrain in-medium temperature corrections using thermal lattice-QCD results for the heavy-quark free energy and equation of state in a selfconsistent setup for heavy- and light-parton spectral functions. Finally, they deploy the refined in-medium heavy-light T-matrix to compute charm-quark transport coefficients in the QGP.

- String breaking scale r_SB ≈ 1 fm, used to enforce a flat potential above that distance
- Smearing parameter b = 10 for the Gaussian used to regularize the Dirac delta in the spin-spin interaction, with saturation checked for b > 10
- Cutoff r_c = 0.01 fm introduced in Fourier transforms for spin-dependent potentials, with results insensitive to ±50% variation
- Pseudocritical temperature T_pc ≈ 160 MeV, cited as the temperature above which effective QGP constituent masses are typically large

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix approach previously developed by Mannarelli (2005), Riek (2010), and Liu (2017) for studying heavy-flavor particles in a strongly coupled QGP, which had not included spin-orbit and spin-spin interactions. The authors follow the relativistic quark model approach of Ebert (1997, 2002) that found mixed scalar and vector structures in the confining potential yield good quarkonium spectroscopy, and they note that studies of the Wilson loop by Brambilla (1996, 1997) suggest the confining potential cannot be purely scalar. The results are promising for improving the phenomenology of open heavy-flavor observables at RHIC and the LHC.
