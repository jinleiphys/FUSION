# Universal bound states and resonances with Coulomb plus short-range potentials

> We study charged particles in three dimensions interacting via a short-range potential in addition to the Coulomb potential. When the Bohr radius and the scattering length are much larger than the potential range, low-energy physics of the system becomes independent from details of the short-range potential. We develop the zero-range theory to describe such universal physics in terms of the Bohr radius and the scattering length by generalizing the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition, which is then applied to two charged particles to reveal their bound states and resonances. Infinite resonances are found for a repulsive Coulomb potential, one of which turns into a bound state with increasing inverse scattering length, whereas infinite bound states exist for an attractive Coulomb potential with no resonances at any scattering length. The zero-range theory is also applied to three equally charged particles at infinite scattering length under the variational Born-Oppenheimer approximation. We find that the effective potential between two heavy particles induced by a light particle is an inverse-square attraction at distances shorter than the Bohr radius, leading to infinite deep bound states, whereas shallow ones successively turn into resonances with increasing Coulomb repulsion.

# Universal bound states and resonances with Coulomb plus short-range potentials

## Key claim

The paper develops a zero-range theory for charged particles interacting via both Coulomb and short-range potentials, claiming that when the Bohr radius and scattering length are both much larger than the potential range, the low-energy physics becomes universal and independent of short-range details. However, the authors acknowledge that realizing such conditions may require "accidental fine-tuning," and that the theory's predictions deviate from experimental data by factors of 2–20 when the Bohr radius is not sufficiently large compared to the potential range.

## Method

The authors generalize the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition for the s-wave radial wave function using self-adjoint extension of the Hamiltonian, parametrizing the zero-range interaction by a scattering length \(\tilde{a}\) in the presence of a Coulomb potential. They solve the resulting transcendental equations for poles of the S-matrix in the complex energy plane to find bound states and resonances for two charged particles, and apply a variational Born-Oppenheimer approximation with a trial wave function superposing two bound states to study three equally charged particles at infinite scattering length.

## Key numbers

- For two protons: \(a_0 \approx 57.6\) fm, \(\tilde{a} \approx -7.83 \pm 0.01\) fm, ratio \(a_0/\tilde{a} \approx -7.36\) predicts no observable resonance
- For two \(\alpha\) particles: \(a_0 \approx 3.63\) fm, \(\tilde{a} \approx (-1.92 \pm 0.09) \times 10^3\) fm, ratio \(a_0/\tilde{a} \approx -0.0019\); zero-range theory predicts resonance at \(E \approx 9.0\) keV, while measured \(^8\)Be ground state is at \(E \approx 184.1 \pm 0.1\) keV (factor of 20 deviation)
- For two \(\alpha\) particles with effective range \(\tilde{r} \approx 1.1\) fm included: resonance energy shifts to \(E \approx 88\) keV (factor of 2 deviation)
- For triply charmed baryons from lattice QCD: \(a_0 \approx 2.8\) fm, \(\tilde{a} \approx -19\) fm, \(\tilde{r} \approx 0.45\) fm, ratio \(a_0/\tilde{a} \approx -0.15\); zero-range theory predicts resonance at \(E \approx (0.81 - 0.03i)\) MeV without effective range, or \(E \approx (1.3 - 0.2i)\) MeV with effective range included
- For three equally charged particles at infinite scattering length: the effective potential between two heavy particles induced by a light particle becomes an inverse-square attraction \(-\hbar^2 c^2/(2mR^2)\) at short distances, with constant \(c = 0.567143\ldots\) solving \(c = e^{-c}\)

## Context

This work extends the zero-range theory (Bethe-Peierls boundary condition, Bethe 1935) to include Coulomb potentials, generalizing prior treatments that assumed only short-range interactions. The results are relevant to nuclear physics (proton-proton scattering, alpha-alpha scattering in \(^8\)Be), hadron physics (triply charmed baryons from lattice QCD by Lyu 2021), and dark-matter astrophysics (Hisano 2003-2005, Arkani-Hamed 2009, Braaten 2017-2018). The three-body analysis builds on the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970, 1973) and the Born-Oppenheimer approximation (Fonseca 1979), showing that Coulomb repulsion causes shallow bound states to successively turn into resonances. The framework enables future studies of universal few- and many-body physics with charged particles, though the authors note that the required conditions of large Bohr radius and scattering length may require accidental fine-tuning.
