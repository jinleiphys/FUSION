# Correlations within the Non-Equilibrium Green's Function Method

> Non-equilibrium Green's Function (NGF) method is a powerful tool for studying the evolution of quantum many-body systems. Different types of correlations can be systematically incorporated within the formalism. The time evolution of the single-particle Green's functions is described in terms of the Kadanoff-Baym equations. The current work initially focuses on introducing the correlations within infinite nuclear matter in one dimension and then in a finite system in the NGF approach. Starting from the harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian, by switching on adiabatically the mean-field and correlations simultaneously, a correlated state with ground-state characteristics is arrived at within the NGF method. Furthermore the use of cooling to for improving the adiabatic switching is explored.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlated ground states for nuclear systems can be prepared within the Non-equilibrium Green's Function (NGF) method by adiabatically switching on mean-field and residual interactions simultaneously, but the resulting states are not perfectly cold and static; residual oscillations and a slight non-zero temperature remain as artifacts that must be tolerated in subsequent collision simulations.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations for one-dimensional infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclear slabs, using a Skyrme-type interaction for the Hartree-Fock mean-field and a self-consistent Born approximation for the correlation self-energies. They start from an uncorrelated system trapped in a harmonic oscillator potential and adiabatically transform the Hamiltonian by switching off the trap while ramping up the mean-field and a Gaussian residual interaction, with the switching function \( f(t) = 1/(1+e^{t/\tau}) \). The use of a cooling friction term proportional to the time derivative of the density is explored to improve the stationarity of the final state.

- Spatial grid separation in production calculations: \( \text{d}x = 0.67 \, \text{fm} \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field studies using KB equations for nuclear collisions (Rios 2011) by incorporating correlations beyond the mean-field level. It builds on earlier adiabatic switching techniques for correlated states (Tohyama 1994, Pfitzner 1994) and on imaginary-time evolution methods (Danielewicz 1984). The results enable future investigations of collective oscillations, particularly isospin dynamics, in correlated nuclear systems within the NGF framework.
