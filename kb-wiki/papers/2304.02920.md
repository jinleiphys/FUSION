# Possible molecular dibaryons with $csssqq$ quarks and their baryon-antibaryon partners

> In this work, we systematically investigate the charmed-strange dibaryon systems with $csssqq$ quarks and their baryon-antibaryon partners from the interactions $Ξ^{(',*)}_{c}Ξ^{(*)}$, $Ω^{(*)}_cΛ$, $Ω^{(*)}_cΣ^{(*)}$, $Λ_cΩ$ and $Σ^{(*)}_cΩ$ and their baryon-antibaryon partners from interactions $Ξ^{(',*)}_{c}\barΞ^{(*)}$, $Ω^{(*)}_c\barΛ$, $Ω^{(*)}_c\barΣ^{(*)}$, $Λ_c\barΩ$ and $Σ^{(*)}_c\barΩ$. The potential kernels are constructed with the help of effective Lagrangians under SU(3), heavy quark, and chiral symmetries to describe these interactions. To search for possible molecular states, the kernels are inserted into the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation, which is solved to find poles from scattering amplitude. The results suggest that 36 and 24 bound states can be found in the baryon-baryon and baryon-antibaryon interactions, respectively. However, much large values of parameter $α$ are required to produce the bound states from the baryon-antibaryon interactions, which questions the existence of these bound states. Possible coupled-channel effect are considered in the current work to estimate the couplings of the molecular states to the channels considered.

## Key claim
The paper predicts 36 bound states in baryon-baryon interactions and 24 bound states in baryon-antibaryon interactions for charmed-strange dibaryon systems with $csssqq$ quarks, but the baryon-antibaryon bound states require much larger values of the cutoff parameter $\alpha$ to appear, which "questions the existence of these bound states."

## Method
The authors constructed potential kernels using effective Lagrangians under SU(3), heavy quark, and chiral symmetries for light meson exchanges (pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons). These kernels were inserted into the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation (qBSE), which was solved to find poles in the scattering amplitude for S-wave bound states with binding energy smaller than 30 MeV. Single-channel calculations were performed for $\Xi^{(',*)}_{c}\Xi^{(*)}$, $\Omega^{(*)}_c\Lambda$, and $\Omega^{(*)}_c\Sigma^{(*)}$ systems and their baryon-antibaryon partners, followed by coupled-channel calculations to estimate couplings between molecular states and decay channels.

## Key numbers
- 36 bound states found in baryon-baryon interactions and 24 bound states in baryon-antibaryon interactions from the systems studied
- For $\Xi_c\Xi^{(*)}$ interactions: eight bound states appear at $\alpha$ values less than 1; binding energies of isovector $\Xi_c\Xi$ states with $(0,1)^+$ and isoscalar/isovector $\Xi_c\Xi^{*}$ states with $(1,2)^+$ reach 30 MeV at $\alpha$ values of about 1.5
- For $\Xi^{'}_c\Xi^{(*)}$ interactions: eight bound states from baryon-baryon interactions appear at $\alpha$ values less than 1; two isoscalar bound states from $\Xi^{'}_c\Xi$ with $(0,1)^+$ increase to 20 MeV at $\alpha$ values of about 3.5 and 5.0
- For $\Xi^{*}_c\Xi^{(*)}$ interactions: bound states from baryon-baryon systems appear at $\alpha$ values below 1.5; ten states increase to 30 MeV at $\alpha$ values of about 2.5
- For $\Omega_c\Lambda/\Omega_c\Sigma^{(*)}$ interactions: only seven states produced; $\Omega_c\Sigma^{*}$ states with $(1,2)^+$ appear at $\alpha$ values of about 3.0 and 1.5
- For $\Omega^{*}_c\Lambda/\Omega^{*}_c\Sigma^{(*)}$ interactions: nine bound states produced from sixteen interactions; $\Omega^{*}_c\Sigma^{*}$ states with $(1,2,3)^+$ appear at cutoff about 2.0
- Baryon-antibaryon bound states generally appear at $\alpha$ values more than 3, with some requiring $\alpha$ values around 4.6 to 4.8

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of charmed-strange dibaryons with $csqqqq$ quarks (Kong 2022) by replacing one $u/d$ quark in each constituent baryon with a strange quark to reach $csssqq$ systems, which were "scarcely studied in the literature." The study is motivated by experimental observations of charmed-strange molecular candidates such as $D^{*}_{s0}(2317)$ near the $DK$ threshold (BaBar 2003) and $X_0(2900)$/$X_1(2900)$ near the $\bar{D}^{*}K^{*}$ threshold (LHCb 2020), as well as hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_c$ (LHCb 2015-2022). The results enable future experimental searches for these predicted dibaryon molecular states and provide a framework for estimating their couplings to decay channels through coupled-channel effects.
