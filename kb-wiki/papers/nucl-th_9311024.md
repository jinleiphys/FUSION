# Chaotic Dynamics in the Framework of the Vlasov-Nordheim Equation

> In the framework of the time-dependent mean field theory, we solve the Vlasov-Nordheim equation and study the sensitive dependence of this highly non-linear equation on the initial conditions. We find that, when nuclear matter is inside the spinodal region, initially small differences between similar trajectories grow exponentially and produce strongly divergent paths resulting in a chaotic evolution. We focus on the fact that chaos spontaneously arises without putting any additional noise. We calculate the largest Lyapunov exponent for various initial densities. This analysis is performed for nuclear matter in two dimensions. It is argued that the mean field chaoticity can cause nuclear multifragmentation observed in intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim
The Vlasov-Nordheim equation exhibits chaotic dynamics inside the nuclear matter spinodal region, with initially small differences between trajectories growing exponentially and producing strongly divergent paths, even without adding any external noise or stochastic term.

## Method
The authors solved the Vlasov-Nordheim equation on a two-dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a simplified Skyrme interaction for the effective one-body field. Nuclear matter was initialized with a sinusoidal density profile along the x-direction at various densities, and the largest Lyapunov exponent was extracted by tracking the time evolution of the distance between two initially close density profiles. The analysis was performed both with and without the two-body collision integral.

## Key numbers
- Side lengths of the torus: L_x = 51 fm and L_y = 15 fm; cell size: δx = 1/3 fm, δp = 40 MeV/c; time integration step: 0.5 fm/c
- Fermi momentum at saturation: P_F = 260 MeV/c, giving a two-dimensional saturation density ρ_0 = 0.55 fm^{-2}
- Initial sinusoidal perturbation: wave number k = 2π (n_k/L_x) with n_k = 5, amplitude 1% of local density; temperature T = 3 MeV
- The largest Lyapunov exponent λ̅ increases from zero at ρ = 0.66 ρ_0 to a flat maximum around ρ = 0.35 ρ_0
- Inclusion of two-body collisions changes λ̅ by about 10%
- For densities ρ ≤ 0.4 ρ_0, the mean field divergence time τ_MF = ħ/λ̅ is smaller than the single particle time τ_sp = ħ/E_F

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear matter instability in the spinodal region, including linear response analyses by Heiselberg (1993) and Langevin-type noise approaches by Burgio et al. (1992). It provides quantitative confirmation of the chaotic scenario suggested by Knoll et al. (1984) using two-dimensional TDHF calculations, and by Idier et al. (1993). The results support the idea that mean field chaoticity can cause nuclear multifragmentation observed in intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions, and suggest that event-by-event descriptions are necessary rather than averaging over trajectories.
