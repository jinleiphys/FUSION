# Exploring Spin Polarization of Heavy Quarks in Magnetic Fields and Hot Medium

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions give rise to the formation of both deconfined QCD matter and a strong magnetic field. The spin of heavy quarks is influenced by interactions with the external magnetic field as well as by random scatterings with thermal light partons. The presence of QCD matter comprising charged quarks can extend the lifetime and strength of the magnetic field, thereby enhancing the degree of heavy quark polarization. However, the random scatterings with QCD matter tend to diminish heavy quark polarization. In this study, we utilize the Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert (LLG) equation to investigate both these contributions. Taking into account the realistic evolutions of medium temperatures and the in-medium magnetic fields at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), we observe that heavy quark polarization is limited by the short lifetime of the magnetic field and the high temperatures of the medium. Furthermore, we explore the mass dependence of quark polarization, revealing that the polarization degree of strange quarks is much larger than that of charm quarks.

# Exploring Spin Polarization of Heavy Quarks in Magnetic Fields and Hot Medium

## Key claim
Heavy quark spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions is limited to at most a few percent even when in-medium magnetic field effects are included, because the magnetic field decays too quickly (lifetime < 1.0 fm/c) and the medium temperatures are too high for significant polarization to develop before random scatterings wash it out. The polarization of strange quarks is much larger than that of charm quarks, but still only reaches approximately 7% in peripheral collisions.

## Method
The authors coupled the Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert (LLG) equation for spin evolution with the Langevin equation for momentum evolution of heavy quarks, using realistic time-dependent magnetic fields and temperature profiles from hydrodynamic simulations of RHIC Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and LHC Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Two parameterizations of the in-medium magnetic field were used: a uniform field decaying as 1/(1+(t/t_B)^2) and a spatially-dependent field decaying as 1/(1+t/t_B) with Gaussian transverse profile. The damping factor alpha in the LLG equation was varied between 0.1 and 1.0, and the thermal fluctuating magnetic field was treated as white noise with strength proportional to temperature.

## Key numbers
- Initial magnetic field strength at LHC: eB_0 = 128 m_pi^2 for centrality 30-40% in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions; eB_0 = 145 m_pi^2 for centrality 60-70%
- Initial magnetic field strength at RHIC: eB_0 = 4.55 m_pi^2 for centrality 30-40% in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions
- Magnetic field lifetime parameter: t_B = 0.4 fm/c
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Decoupling temperature for hadronization: T = 170 MeV
- Spatial diffusion coefficient from deep learning: D_s * 2*pi*T = 4.87 + 4.16*(T/T_c - 1)
- Final spin polarization of strange quarks in LHC peripheral collisions (cent. 60-70%): approximately 7%
- Final spin polarization of charm quarks in LHC central collisions (cent. 30-40%): nearly zero

## Context
This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions that focused on light hadrons such as Lambda hyperons measured by STAR (2017) and modeled via hydrodynamic and transport approaches by Karpenko (2016) and Li (2017). It introduces the LLG equation from condensed matter physics to heavy quark spin dynamics, complementing earlier work on heavy quark momentum diffusion by Cao (2013), He (2013), and lattice QCD calculations of the spatial diffusion coefficient by Altenkort (2023). The results suggest that charmonium spin polarization observed in experiments likely originates from vortical fields rather than magnetic fields, and the authors note that their non-equilibrated spin and momentum distributions of charm quarks provide essential input for spin-coalescence models of J/psi production.
