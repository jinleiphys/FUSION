# Variation in displacement energies due to isospin nonconserving forces

> For mirror nuclei with masses A=42-95, the effects of isospin nonconserving nuclear forces are studied with nuclear shell model using the Coulomb displacement energy and triplet displacement energy as probes. It is shown that the characteristic behavior of the displacement energies can be well reproduced if the isovector and isotensor nuclear interactions with J=0 and T=1 are introduced into the f7/2 shell. These forces, with their strengths being found consistent with the nucleon-nucleon scattering data, tend to modify nuclear binding energies near the N=Z line. At present, no evidence is found that these forces are needed for the upper fp-shell. Theoretical one- and two-proton separation energies are predicted accordingly, and locations of the proton drip-line are thereby suggested.

## Key claim

The characteristic odd-even staggering in Coulomb displacement energies and the enhanced triplet displacement energies for mirror nuclei in the mass region A=42-54 can be reproduced only by including isovector and isotensor nuclear interactions with J=0 and T=1 in the f7/2 shell, but no evidence is found that these forces are needed for the upper fp-shell.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A interaction for the full fp shell and the JUN45 interaction for the pf5/2g9/2 model space, adding isospin nonconserving (INC) nuclear interactions (isovector and isotensor components) plus Coulomb and electromagnetic spin-orbit terms. They calculated Coulomb displacement energies (CDE) and triplet displacement energies (TDE) for odd-mass nuclei with isospin T=1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 and even-mass nuclei with T=1, 2, and 3, comparing results with and without the INC interactions against experimental data from Audi (2003) and Tu (2011).

## Key numbers

- The INC interaction parameters for the f7/2 shell were chosen as βpp = -22.5 keV, βnn = 77.5 keV, and βpn = -55.0 keV to reproduce experimental CDE and TDE data.
- Without the INC nuclear interaction, calculated TDE values were smaller by about 150 keV than the experimental values.
- The nn interaction was found to be approximately 0.8% more attractive than pp, and pn approximately 2.5% stronger than the average of nn and pp, consistent with nucleon-nucleon scattering data from Machleidt (2001).
- The rms deviation between calculated and experimental binding energies was about 100 keV for the predicted proton-rich nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to resolve the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen 1969) by incorporating isospin nonconserving nuclear forces, building on shell-model calculations by Zuker (2002) and earlier work by Kaneko (2010, 2012) on specific mirror pairs. The results enable predictions of one- and two-proton separation energies up to A=95, mapping the proton drip-line relevant to the rp-process nucleosynthesis path discussed by Schatz (2001, 2006), and suggest candidates for future proton-emitter experiments.
