# Meson - nucleon vertex form factors at finite temperature

> In this paper the dependence of meson-nucleon-nucleon vertex form factors is studied as a function of termperature. The results are obtained starting from a zero temperature Bonn potential. The temperature dependence of the vertex form factors and radii is studied in the thermofield dynamics, a real-time operator formalism of finite temperature field theory. It is anticipated that these results will have an impact on the study of relativistic heavy-ion collisions as the critical temperature for the phase transition from hadronic to quark-gluon system is approached.

## Key claim

The paper claims that meson-nucleon vertex form factors and coupling constants are nearly independent of temperature below a critical temperature, then change rapidly above it, based on calculations using the Bonn potential as a zero-temperature input and the thermofield dynamics formalism.

## Method

The authors start from the zero-temperature Bonn potential and use thermofield dynamics, a real-time operator formalism of finite-temperature field theory, to compute the temperature dependence of meson-nucleon vertex form factors. They calculate three-line vertex corrections for pion, sigma, omega, and rho meson-nucleon interactions by evaluating loop integrals that include thermal propagators for nucleons and mesons, separating the temperature-dependent contributions from the zero-temperature parts. The form factors are then obtained as ratios relative to their zero-temperature values.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant squared divided by 4 pi at zero temperature is 14.09, with a cutoff parameter Lambda_piNN = 1005 MeV (Bonn A potential parameters).
- The coupling constants g_piNN(T), g_sigmaNN(T), g_omegaNN(T), and g_rhoNN(T) are nearly independent of temperature below a critical temperature T_c^B, then change rapidly for T greater than T_c^B.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of temperature-dependent coupling constants by Zhang et al. (1990s) and Dominguez et al. (1990s) to include the full momentum dependence of meson-nucleon form factors, which were previously unknown at finite temperature. The results are intended to inform studies of relativistic heavy-ion collisions as the system approaches the critical temperature for the phase transition from hadronic matter to a quark-gluon plasma, and may help in determining the equation of state of hot and dense nuclear matter relevant to nuclear physics, astrophysics, and cosmology.
