# An Inconsistency in the Simulation of Bose-Einstein Correlations

> We show that the formalism commonly used to implement Bose-Einstein correlations in Monte-Carlo simulations can lead to values of the two-particle correlator significantly smaller than unity, in the case of sources with strong position-momentum correlations. This is more pronounced when the phase space of the emitted particles is strongly reduced by experimental acceptance or kinematic analysis selections. It is inconsistent with general principles according to which the Bose-Einstein correlator is larger than unity. This inconsistency seems to be rooted in the fact that quantum mechanical localization properties are not taken into account properly.

# An Inconsistency in the Simulation of Bose-Einstein Correlations

## Key claim

The commonly used "cos-prescription" for implementing Bose-Einstein correlations in Monte-Carlo simulations can produce two-particle correlator values significantly below unity for sources with strong position-momentum correlations, particularly when experimental acceptance or kinematic cuts are applied. This violates the general principle that the Bose-Einstein correlator must always be larger than unity, and the inconsistency appears to originate from an incorrect treatment of quantum mechanical localization properties.

## Method

The authors developed a simple Monte-Carlo phase-space model with analytically given distributions, generating pions from a Gaussian source of radius R = 6 fm with transverse momentum and rapidity distributions chosen to match CERN 158 GeV/n Pb+Pb data. Bose-Einstein correlations were added by weighting each identical pion pair with the Born probability of a symmetrized two-pion plane wave, 1 + cos[(r1 - r2)·(p1 - p2)], assuming instantaneous emission. The model incorporated radial flow via a parameterized flow profile β(r) = 1 - exp(-r/f), and the correlator C(q3) was fitted to a one-dimensional Gaussian form in the longitudinal comoving system (LCMS) frame.

## Key numbers

- Source Gaussian radius: R = 6 fm, motivated by the hard sphere radius of 208Pb
- Inverse slope parameter of pT-distribution: B = 200 MeV
- Flow strength parameter fitting Venus data: f = 9 fm
- Mean flow velocity extracted: approximately 0.35c
- Bins for relative pair momentum differences: 5 MeV, checked to be insensitive to bin sizes in the range 5-20 MeV
- Each Monte-Carlo event contained typically 100 identical pions
- For the linear source with complete position-momentum correlation (D = 0.02), the correlator oscillates between 2 and 0
- The unphysical dip in the correlator appears around q3 = 0.08 GeV/c when pT is restricted to 0.1-0.3 GeV/c with a 45-degree emission cone cut

## Context

This work extends studies of Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry in heavy-ion collisions, where source parameters derived from correlation functions are sensitive to dynamical expansion (Herrmann 1993, Csorgo 1994, Chapman 1995), flow effects (Xu 1995, Alber 1995), and experimental acceptance. The paper identifies a fundamental flaw in the standard Monte-Carlo weighting procedure used by many groups (Pratt 1986, Sullivan 1993, Humanic 1994, Fialkowski 1995), showing it can produce unphysical results that contradict the exact quantum-field-theoretic formalism of Heinz (1996) and Wiedemann (1996), where the correlator is always larger than unity. This finding calls into question the quantitative reliability of existing Monte-Carlo simulations of Bose-Einstein correlations and motivates the development of a proper quantum mechanical event generator.
