# Possible molecular states from the $NΔ$ interaction

> Recently, a hint for dibaryon $NΔ(D_{21})$ was observed at WASA-AT-COSY with a mass about $30\pm10$ MeV below the $NΔ$ threshold. It has a relatively small binding energy compared with the $d^*(2380)$ and a width close to the width of the $Δ$ baryon, which suggests that it may be a dibaryon in a molecular state picture. In this work, we study the possible $S$-wave molecular states from the $NΔ$ interaction within the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. The interaction is described by exchanging $π$, $ρ$, and $ω$ mesons. With reasonable parameters, a $D_{21}$ bound state can be produced from the interaction. The results also suggest that there may exist two more possible $D_{12}$ and $D_{22}$ states with smaller binding energies. The $π$ exchange is found to play the most important role to bind two baryons to form the molecular states. An experimental search for possible $NΔ(D_{12})$ and $NΔ(D_{22})$ states will be helpful for understanding the hint of the dibaryon $NΔ(D_{21})$.

## Key claim

Within a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach using one-boson-exchange potentials (π, ρ, ω), a bound state with quantum numbers \(IJ^P = 21^+\) (\(D_{21}\)) can be produced from the \(N\Delta\) interaction, consistent with the experimental hint from WASA-at-COSY; the same interaction also predicts two additional, more shallowly bound states (\(D_{12}\) and \(D_{22}\)), but their existence depends on the choice of the cutoff parameter \(\alpha\) and model uncertainties.

## Method

The \(N\Delta\) interaction was constructed in a one-boson-exchange model with π, ρ, and ω meson exchanges, using effective Lagrangians with coupling constants fixed by experiment and SU(3) symmetry. The potential was inserted into a three-dimensional quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation (qBSE) under the spectator approximation (heavier Δ on-shell), and bound states were searched for as poles on the real axis below the \(N\Delta\) threshold after partial-wave decomposition for fixed spin-parity \(J^P\).

## Key numbers

- The experimental hint for the \(N\Delta(D_{21})\) state has a mass of \(2140(10)\) MeV and a width of \(110(10)\) MeV, about \(30\pm10\) MeV below the \(N\Delta\) threshold (WASA-at-COSY, Adlarson 2018).
- With the form factor \(f_2\) and the cutoff parameter \(\alpha\) tuned to reproduce the experimental \(D_{21}\) binding energy, the predicted binding energies of the \(D_{12}\) and \(D_{22}\) states are about 2 MeV and 8 MeV, respectively; after considering uncertainties, these become several MeV and ten MeV, respectively.
- The deuteron (\(NN(D_{01})\)) is reproduced in the same framework at \(\alpha \approx 2.7\), while the \(D_{21}\) state requires \(\alpha \approx 3.2\).
- The π exchange alone produces a binding energy close to the full model for all three states; the ω exchange provides a repulsive force, and without π exchange no bound state appears.

## Context

This work extends the study of dibaryons beyond the deuteron and the well-known \(d^*(2380)\) (a compact hexaquark observed at WASA-at-COSY, Adlarson 2011). It directly addresses the recent experimental hint for an isotensor \(N\Delta(D_{21})\) state (Adlarson 2018), which had not been supported by earlier constituent quark model calculations (Valcarce 2005, Ping 2000). The results suggest that, if the \(D_{21}\) state is confirmed, two additional molecular \(N\Delta\) states (\(D_{12}\) and \(D_{22}\)) with smaller binding energies should exist, providing targets for future experimental searches.
