# Splitting of proton-antiproton directed flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The rapidity dependent directed flow of particles produced in a relativistic heavy ion collision can be generated in the hydrodynamic expansion of a tilted source. The asymmetry of the pressure leads to a build up of a directed flow of matter with respect to the collision axis. The experimentally observed ordering of the directed flow of baryons, pions and antibaryons can be described as resulting from the expansion of a baryon inhomogeneous fireball. An uneven distribution of baryons in the transverse plane leads to an asymmetry in the collective push for protons and antiprotons. Precise measurements of the collective flow as a function of rapidity could serve as a strong constraint on mechanism of baryon stopping in the early phase of the collision.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed splitting of directed flow between protons and antiprotons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be explained by a hydrodynamic model only if the initial baryon density distribution is tilted more strongly than the bulk entropy density, creating an inhomogeneous baryon distribution in the transverse plane that generates an asymmetric collective push for baryons versus antibaryons.

## Method

The author constructs a three-dimensional tilted fireball initial condition using a Glauber model for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, with separate parametrizations for the entropy density (Eq. 6) and the net baryon density (Eq. 9). The initial entropy density uses asymmetric longitudinal profiles f±(η||) with tilt parameter ηT = 2.4, while the baryon density uses a larger tilt with parameters ηB = 4.4 and σB = 2.2. The subsequent hydrodynamic expansion is performed using the three-dimensional MUSIC code with shear viscosity η/s = 0.08, an equation of state at finite baryon density, and freeze-out at energy density εfr = 0.5 GeV/fm³, starting from an early initialization time of 0.2 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with nuclear parameters A = 197, σ = 42 mb, R = 6.37 fm, a = 0.54 fm
- Tilt parameters for entropy density: ηT = 2.4, α = 0.1, ηS⁰ = 1, ση = 1.3
- Baryon density tilt parameters: ηB = 4.4, σB = 2.2
- Shear viscosity η/s = 0.08
- Freeze-out energy density εfr = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Hydrodynamic initialization time: 0.2 fm/c
- Centrality range for directed flow analysis: 10-40% (primary results), with centrality dependence studied from 0% to 70%
- Slope of directed flow dv₁(y)/dy fitted in central rapidity region y ∈ [-1, 1]

## Context

This work extends the tilted fireball model of Bozek (2010) which successfully described charged particle directed flow but could not explain the observed splitting of directed flow for identified particles measured by the STAR Collaboration (2011, 2014). The mechanism proposed here resolves a long-standing problem where dynamical and hybrid models (Chen 2009, Steinheimer 2014, Konchakovski 2014, Nara 2016) could not reproduce the baryon-antibaryon flow splitting at top RHIC energy. The results suggest that precise measurements of identified particle directed flow can serve as a strong constraint on dynamical models of the initial state involving stopped baryons, and the mechanism could also apply at lower energies where additional effects from phase transitions, spectator shadowing, or mean fields may play a role.
