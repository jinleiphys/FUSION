# Theoretical description of nuclear collective excitations

> Density functional theory is a preferred microscopic method for calculation of nuclear properties over the whole nuclear chart. Besides ground-state properties, which are calculated by Hartree-Fock theory, nuclear excitations can be described by means of Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The main objective of the present work is to give Skyrme RPA formalism for spherically symmetric nuclei, using the techniques of angular-momentum coupling. Various auxiliary topics, such as Hartree-Fock theory, Coulomb integral, center-of-mass corrections and pairing, are treated as well. RPA method is derived also for axially deformed nuclei. The derived formulae are then implemented in the computer code and utilized for calculation of some physical results. After thorough investigation of the precision aspects of the calculation, the following topics are treated as examples: toroidal nature of the low-energy (pygmy) part of the E1 resonance, giant resonances of various multipolarities in deformed nucleus Sm-154, and magnetic dipole (M1) transitions in deformed Cr-50. Keywords: Random Phase Approximation, Skyrme functional, giant resonances in nuclei, reduced matrix elements I thank Jiri Mares and F. ## Key claim

The work provides a complete derivation and implementation of Skyrme Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for both spherically symmetric and axially deformed nuclei, including time-even and time-odd terms of the Skyrme functional, with the hedge that the separable RPA approach is presented as a numerically efficient alternative to full RPA, and that the spherical harmonic oscillator basis allows extension of the model space to over 100 major shells for near-complete elimination of spurious center-of-mass contributions in E1 transitions.

## Method

Skyrme Hartree-Fock ground states were computed using either Reinhard's grid-based codes (haforpa for spherical, skyax for deformed nuclei) or a newly written spherical harmonic oscillator basis code, then RPA matrix elements were derived using angular-momentum coupling techniques for spherical symmetry and explicit expressions for axial symmetry. The residual interaction was derived fully self-consistently from the same Skyrme density functional, and the resulting RPA codes (sph_qrpa, sph_srpa, skyax_qrpa) were vectorized and parallelized for multi-processor workstations with 12 CPU cores and over 32 GB of RAM.

## Key numbers

- Model space extended to over 100 major shells in the spherical harmonic oscillator basis, compared to 22-23 major shells in Reinhard's haforpa code
- Skyax_qrpa code designed for workstations with 12 CPU cores and more than 32 GB of RAM
- Calculations performed for deformed nucleus Sm-154 (giant resonances of various multipolarities) and deformed Cr-50 (magnetic dipole M1 transitions)

## Context

This work extends the separable RPA approach developed by Nesterenko (2002, 2006) for Skyrme functionals in axially deformed nuclei, and the spherical RPA formalism of Reinhard (1992), Terasaki (2005), and Colo (2013). The new spherical codes enable treatment of both electric and magnetic multipolarities with full inclusion of spin and tensor terms of the Skyrme functional, which are shown to be important for M1 and toroidal E1 resonances. The results enable future systematic studies of pygmy resonances and giant resonances across the nuclear chart, building on the author's published work with Reinhard (2014) and Repko (2013).
