# Non-Equilibrium Dynamics in QCD and Holography

> The plasma generated in heavy ion collisions goes through different phases in its time evolution. While early times right after the collision are governed by far-from equilibrium dynamics, later times are believed to be well described by near-equilibrium dynamics. While the regimes of non-equilibrium are prohibitively complicated to describe within QCD, effective descriptions such as hydrodynamics provide a viable approach. In addition, holographic descriptions allow access to the full non-equilibrium dynamics at strong coupling. In this presentation, we review three examples of such hydrodynamic approaches and corresponding holographic descriptions: 1) non-equilibrium shear viscosity, 2) propagation of non-equilibrium sound waves, and 3) the non-equilibrium chiral magnetic effect.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma generated in heavy ion collisions may be applicable even in anisotropic systems with large gradients and far from global equilibrium, but the specific values of transport coefficients such as shear viscosity and speed of sound depend strongly on both the direction of anisotropy and the degree of non-equilibrium, requiring an anisotropic non-equilibrium effective description rather than standard isotropic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The author reviews three holographic calculations in which generalized Einstein's equations are solved numerically to obtain the time-dependent spacetime metric dual to N=4 Super-Yang-Mills plasma in various states. The numerical data are holographically mapped to the time-dependent energy-momentum tensor and charge currents, then analyzed by constructing anisotropic hydrodynamic constitutive equations from all tensor structures transforming under the Lorentz group, restricted by field theoretic or thermodynamic requirements. Three specific quantities are computed as functions of time: the shear viscosity in anisotropic equilibrium and isotropic non-equilibrium states, the speed of sound in Bjorken-expanding plasma, and the chiral magnetic conductivity in Bjorken-expanding plasma with a magnetic field and axial charge density.

## Key numbers

* The specific shear viscosity in an isotropic non-equilibrium state, normalized to the KSS value 1/(4pi), decreases from 1 to approximately 0.5 as temperature rises from 155 MeV to 310 MeV, then increases to approximately 1.2 before returning to 1 at later times.
* The longitudinal specific shear viscosity in anisotropic equilibrium with a strong magnetic field decreases rapidly towards large dimensionless magnetic field B/T^2, while the transverse shear viscosity is independent of magnetic field, temperature, chemical potential, and chiral anomaly coefficient.
* Thermodynamically defined speeds of sound in Bjorken-expanding plasma are not reliable before proper times tau approximately 1/T, but perturbatively computed speeds of sound begin to qualitatively agree with thermodynamic results as early as tau approximately 2/T.
* The accumulated charge from the chiral magnetic effect depends strongly on initial conditions, with a more recent extension favoring the strongest signature around 10 GeV collision energy, roughly agreeing with STAR experiment favoring maximal CME at 15 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on applying hydrodynamics to heavy ion collisions, where simulations using nearly perfect fluid dynamics approximate experimental observables already at times as early as 1 fm/c (Romatschke 2017, Noronha-Hostler 2016). It builds on holographic studies showing hydrodynamic behavior at very early times (Chesler 2008, 2009, 2010) and on the development of anisotropic hydrodynamics based on kinetic theory (Martinez 2010, Florkowski 2010, Strickland 2014). The results enable future Bayesian analyses extending Bernhard (2019) to incorporate anisotropic non-equilibrium effective descriptions, and suggest that the KSS bound eta/s >= 1/(4pi) (Kovtun 2004) is broken specifically for longitudinal shear viscosity in anisotropic systems (Rebhan 2009, Erdmenger 2010, Garbiso 2020, Ammon 2020).
