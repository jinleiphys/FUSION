# Equation of state of hypernuclear matter: impact of hyperon--scalar-meson couplings

> We study the equation of state and composition of hypernuclear matter within a relativistic density functional theory with density-dependent couplings. The parameter space of hyperon--scalar-meson couplings is explored by allowing for mixing and breaking of SU(6) symmetry, while keeping the nucleonic couplings constant fixed. The subset of equations of state, which corresponds to small values of hyperon--scalar-meson couplings allows for massive M < 2.25 M_solar compact stars; the radii of hypernuclear stars are within the range 12--14 km. We also study the equation of state of hot neutrino-rich and neutrinoless hypernuclear matter and confirm that neutrinos stiffen the equation of state and dramatically change the composition of matter by keeping the fractions of charged leptons nearly independent of the density prior to the onset of neutrino transparency. We provide piecewise polytropic fits to six representative equations of state of hypernuclear matter, which are suitable for applications in numerical astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hypernuclear equations of state with small hyperon--scalar-meson couplings can produce compact stars with masses up to 2.25 solar masses, potentially resolving the "hyperonization puzzle" where hyperon softening was thought to prevent massive neutron stars, but only for a subset of the explored coupling parameter space.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic density functional theory with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (DD-ME2 parametrization) and extended it to include the full baryon octet. They systematically varied the hyperon--scalar-meson coupling constants within constraints from SU(6) symmetry breaking and hypernuclear potentials, while keeping hyperon--vector-meson couplings fixed, and computed the equation of state and composition of hypernuclear matter at both zero and finite temperatures with and without trapped neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Maximum compact star mass: M ≤ 2.25 M_solar for the subset of equations of state with small hyperon--scalar-meson couplings
- Radii of hypernuclear stars: 12--14 km
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3} in the DD-ME2 parametrization
- Range of hyperon--sigma coupling ratios explored: x_Σσ = g_Σσ/g_Nσ from 0.26 to 0.66; x_Λσ = g_Λσ/g_Nσ from 0.52 to 0.66
- Λ-hyperon potential in nuclear matter at saturation: U_Λ = -28 MeV
- Σ-hyperon potential in nuclear matter at saturation: U_Σ = 30 MeV
- Lepton fraction range for proto-neutron star matter: 0.1 ≤ Y_L ≤ 0.4
- Temperature for hot matter calculations: T = 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic density functional studies of hypernuclear matter (Glendenning 1985, Schaffner-Bielich 1996) and addresses the "hyperonization puzzle" raised by the discovery of 2 solar mass pulsars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The parameter space exploration is motivated by external field QCD sum rules involving SU(6) symmetry breaking and mixing effects (Timmermans). The results provide piecewise polytropic fits suitable for numerical astrophysics applications and confirm previous findings on neutrino stiffening of the equation of state (Vartanyan 1977, Pons 1999).
