# Conformal higher-order viscoelastic fluid mechanics

> We present a generally covariant formulation of conformal higher-order viscoelastic fluid mechanics with strain allowed to take arbitrarily large values. We give a general prescription to determine the dynamics of a relativistic viscoelastic fluid in a way consistent with the hypothesis of local thermodynamic equilibrium and the second law of thermodynamics. We then elaborately study the transient time scales at which the strain almost relaxes and becomes proportional to the gradients of velocity. We particularly show that a conformal second-order fluid with all possible parameters in the constitutive equations can be obtained without breaking the hypothesis of local thermodynamic equilibrium, if the conformal fluid is defined as the long time limit of a conformal second-order viscoelastic system. We also discuss how local thermodynamic equilibrium could be understood in the context of the fluid/gravity correspondence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a conformal second-order fluid with all possible parameters in the constitutive equations can be obtained without breaking the hypothesis of local thermodynamic equilibrium, provided the conformal fluid is defined as the long time limit of a conformal second-order viscoelastic system.

## Method

The authors construct a generally covariant formulation of relativistic viscoelastic fluid mechanics by introducing the elastic strain tensor as a local thermodynamic variable alongside the energy-momentum vector, charge density, and metric. They use the manifestly Weyl-covariant formalism developed by Loganayagam (2008) to enforce conformal invariance, and determine the dynamics from the conservation law of the energy-momentum tensor together with rheology equations for the strain evolution, all constrained by the second law of thermodynamics and the hypothesis of local thermodynamic equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The strain tensor \(\varepsilon_{\mu\nu}\) is assigned the same Weyl weight as the metric: \(w = -2\).
- The energy-momentum tensor has weight \(w = d+2\) in \(d\)-dimensional spacetime.
- The proper energy density scales as \(e = T^d \, \bar{e}(h,\varepsilon)\) and the entropy density as \(s = T^{d-1} \, \bar{s}(h,\varepsilon)\) for a conformal viscoelastic fluid.
- The Euler relation for a conformal fluid with zero chemical potential gives \(\bar{s} = \frac{d}{d-1} \bar{e}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic viscoelastic fluid mechanics (Fukuma and Sakatani, 2011a, 2011b), which was based on Onsager's linear nonequilibrium thermodynamics and gave a causal completion of first-order relativistic fluid mechanics. It directly builds on the manifestly Weyl-covariant formulation of conformal second-order fluid mechanics developed by Loganayagam (2008) and the conformal fluid model of Baier et al. (2008). The paper enables a consistent interpretation of second-order fluid mechanics within local thermodynamic equilibrium, and suggests a possible viscoelasticity/quantum gravity correspondence in the context of the fluid/gravity duality.
