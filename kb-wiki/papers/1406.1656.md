# Further explorations of the alpha-particle optical model potential at low energies for the mass range A=45-209

> The recent high-precision measurements of alpha-particle induced reaction data below the Coulomb barrier (B) make possible the understanding of limits and possible improvement of a previous optical model potential (OMP) for alpha-particles on nuclei within the mass number range 45<A<209 [M. Avrigeanu et al., Phys. Rev. C 82, 014606 (2010)]. An updated version of this potential is given in the present work concerning mainly an increased surface imaginary potential depth well below B for A>130. Moreover, underestimation of reaction cross sections for well-deformed nuclei is removed by using ~7% larger radius for the surface imaginary part of this spherical OMP. Improved input parameters based on recent independent data, particularly gamma-ray strength functions, but no empirical rescaling factor of the gamma and/or neutron widths have been involved within statistical model calculation of the corresponding (alpha,x) reaction cross sections.

# Further explorations of the alpha-particle optical model potential at low energies for the mass range A=45-209

## Key claim
The previous alpha-particle optical model potential (Avrigeanu et al., Phys. Rev. C 82, 014606, 2010) can be improved for heavier nuclei (A>130) by increasing the surface imaginary potential depth below the Coulomb barrier, and for well-deformed nuclei (152<A<190) by using an approximately 7% larger radius for the surface imaginary part of this spherical potential, while still using improved gamma-ray strength functions without empirical rescaling factors for gamma or neutron widths.

## Method
The authors performed statistical model calculations of (alpha,x) reaction cross sections using an updated version of the STAPRE-H95 code, comparing with recent high-precision measurements of alpha-induced reactions below the Coulomb barrier for target nuclei from A=45 to A=209. They employed back-shifted Fermi gas nuclear level densities, nucleon transmission coefficients from the KD03 potential, and enhanced generalized Lorentzian (EGLO) model gamma-ray strength functions with fitted parameters k0 and Tf to reproduce radiative strength function data and s-wave neutron-resonance average radiation widths. For well-deformed nuclei, they also considered direct-interaction distorted-wave Born approximation calculations for collective inelastic scattering using the DWUCK4 code.

## Key numbers
- Surface imaginary potential depth WD = 4 MeV for A<130 at energies below E1 (replacing previous value of 3.5 MeV)
- Surface imaginary potential depth WD = 4 MeV for A>130 at energies below E1 (replacing previous value of approximately 1.5 MeV)
- Approximately 7% larger radius for the surface imaginary potential component (rD = 1.52 fm) for well-deformed nuclei with 152<A<190
- Real potential volume integral JR values for local fits range from 316 to 547 MeV·fm^3 across target nuclei from 132Ba to 209Bi at energies 15-43 MeV
- Surface imaginary volume integral JD values for local fits range from 0 to 44.6 MeV·fm^3 across the same nuclei
- Direct inelastic scattering to total reaction cross section ratio for 162Er increases from approximately 0.1% at threshold to 7% at 16.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the global alpha-particle optical model potential previously developed by Avrigeanu et al. (2010) for the mass range A=45-209, addressing underestimations found when comparing with newer high-precision measurements for heavier nuclei (A>130) and well-deformed nuclei. The authors follow the approach of Vonach et al. (1983) in treating the total reaction cross section as corresponding to compound-nucleus formation, and note that Lanier et al. (1990) previously demonstrated enhanced charged-particle reaction cross sections for deformed nuclei using larger radii in spherical optical model calculations. The updated potential enables improved statistical model calculations of alpha-induced reaction cross sections for applications in nuclear astrophysics and reaction data evaluation, while the authors note that coupled-channels calculations handling Coulomb excitation processes are beyond the scope of this revision.
