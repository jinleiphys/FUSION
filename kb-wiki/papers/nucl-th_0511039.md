# How To Classify 3-Body Forces -- And Why

> For systems with only short-range forces and shallow 2-body bound states, the typical strength of any 3-body force in all partial-waves, including external currents, is systematically estimated by renormalisation-group arguments in the Effective Field Theory of Point-Like Interactions. The underlying principle and some consequences in particular in Nuclear Physics are discussed.

# How To Classify 3-Body Forces -- And Why

## Key claim

Three-body forces in systems with short-range interactions and shallow two-body bound states can be systematically classified by renormalisation-group arguments, and their actual strength in many partial-wave channels deviates significantly from simplistic dimensional estimates, some are "promoted" to lower order and some "demoted" to higher order than naive power-counting would suggest.

## Method

The author solves the Faddeev equation for particle-dimer scattering at leading order, then uses a Mellin transformation to extract the asymptotic off-shell behaviour of the scattering amplitude at large momenta. The superficial degree of divergence of higher-order corrections is computed by comparing the asymptotic exponent \(s_l(\lambda)\) from the homogeneous equation against the naive expectation \(l+1\); a three-body force is required as a counter-term whenever the correction becomes cut-off dependent, i.e. when \(\Re[n - s_l(\lambda) - s_{l'}(\lambda')] \geq 0\). The analysis is performed for both spin-less bosons and nucleons in various spin and partial-wave channels.

## Key numbers

- In the pion-less EFT for nuclear physics, the expansion parameter is \(Q \approx 1/3\) (deuteron scattering length \(a \approx 4.5\;\mathrm{fm}\), range \(R \approx 1.5\;\mathrm{fm}\)).
- The quartet-S wave neutron-deuteron scattering length at N2LO is \([5.09(\mathrm{LO}) + 1.32(\mathrm{NLO}) - 0.06(\mathrm{N2LO})]\;\mathrm{fm} = [6.35 \pm 0.02]\;\mathrm{fm}\), agreeing with experiment \([6.35 \pm 0.02]\;\mathrm{fm}\).
- The total cross-section for triton radiative capture \(nd \to t\gamma\) at thermal energies is \([0.485(\mathrm{LO}) + 0.011(\mathrm{NLO}) + 0.007(\mathrm{N2LO})]\;\mathrm{mb} = [0.503 \pm 0.003]\;\mathrm{mb}\), compared to the measured value \([0.509 \pm 0.015]\;\mathrm{mb}\).
- In the quartet-S channel, the first three-body force is predicted to enter at N6LO (demoted relative to the simplistic N4LO estimate), giving a theoretical uncertainty of \(\pm(1/3)^3 \times 0.02\;\mathrm{fm} \lesssim \pm 0.001\;\mathrm{fm}\).
- For the \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}_2\) molecule, the expansion parameter is \(Q \approx 1/100\) (scattering length \(a \approx 104\;\mathrm{\AA}\), range \(R \approx 1\;\mathrm{\AA}\)).

## Context

This work extends the renormalisation-group analysis of three-body forces in the Effective Field Theory of Point-Like Interactions, building on the earlier recognition by Minlos and Faddeev that the centrifugal term for three bosons is attractive rather than repulsive, and on the hyper-spherical approach of Gasaneo and Macek. The classification provides a systematic power-counting for three-body forces in all partial waves, applicable to atomic trimers, nucleon-deuteron scattering, and reactions relevant for big-bang nucleo-synthesis. The method is also applicable to Chiral EFT, where only those local N-body forces are added at each order that are necessary as counter-terms to cancel short-distance divergences.
