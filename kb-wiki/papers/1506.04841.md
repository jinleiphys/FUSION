# Isoscalar and neutron modes in the E1 spectra of Ni isotopes and the relevance of shell effects and the continuum

> We study theoretically the electric dipole transitions of even Ni isotopes at low energies, using the self-consistent quasi-particle random-phase approximation (RPA) with the D1S Gogny interaction and a continuum-RPA model with the SLy4 Skyrme force. We analyze isoscalar states, isovector states, and the dipole polarizability. We define a reference value for the polarizability, to remove a trivial dependence on the mass number. We compare our results with data and other calculations, with a focus on collective states, shell effects, and threshold transitions. Our results support the presence of a strong isoscalar transition, with little or moderate E1 strength, as a universal feature of ordinary nuclei. In moderately neutron-rich Ni isotopes, namely 68Ni and neighboring isotopes, this transition is found bimodal due to couplings with surface neutrons. An adequate treatment of the continuum states appears essential for describing suprathreshold E1 strength, especially beyond 68Ni. Very exotic isotopes (N>50) are found highly polarizable, with practically all their E1 strength in the continuum. The dipole polarizability and the neutron skin thickness are influenced by shell structure in different ways, so they can appear anticorrelated. A comparison with existing results for lighter (Ca) and heavier (Sn) nuclei suggests that the so-called pygmy dipole strength is influenced strongly by shell effects and that, partly for that reason, its isospin structure depends on the mass region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a strong isoscalar (IS) low-energy dipole transition, carrying little or moderate E1 strength, is a universal feature of ordinary nuclei, but in moderately neutron-rich Ni isotopes (especially 68Ni and neighbors) this transition becomes bimodal due to couplings with surface neutrons, and the treatment of continuum states is essential for describing suprathreshold E1 strength beyond 68Ni.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary theoretical approaches: (1) self-consistent quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with the Gogny D1S interaction, using a harmonic oscillator basis of 15 shells, and (2) continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) with the SLy4 Skyrme force, using Green's functions in coordinate space with appropriate boundary conditions to account for particle emission. The QRPA model lacks proper continuum treatment, while the CRPA model does not include pairing and was used only for closed-shell configurations.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon separation energies for N=20, 28, 40, 50 Ni isotopes calculated with the D1S model are 0.41, 7.31, 8.59, and 5.59 MeV, respectively; for N=28 and 40 the AME2012 evaluation gives 7.167 and 7.793 MeV.
- The GDR energy formula used is E_GDR = (31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6}) MeV, and the reference polarizability a_D^coll is defined as (hbar c / 2 pi^2) * (60 NZ MeV mb) / (A E_GDR^2).
- The TRK enhancement factor is 0.6 for D1S and 0.25 for SLy4.
- In 58Ni, IS dipole strength was detected below and above particle emission threshold, with a high-lying peak observed at 34 MeV (calculated response peaks around 30 MeV) and a structure at 17.4 MeV.
- The single-neutron unit for E1 transitions is B_s.n.(E1) = 0.06445 A^{2/3} (Z/A)^2 e^2 fm^2.
- For 80,82,84,86Ni, the nucleon separation energies calculated with D1S are 2.05, 1.88, 1.84, and 0.71 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of low-energy dipole strength in Ca and Sn isotopic chains (Papakonstantinou et al., 2011; 2012; 2014; 2015), where they attributed IS strength to a collective surface dipole mode and IV strength largely to single-particle transitions. The Ni isotopic chain represents an intermediate mass region between lighter Ca and heavier Sn nuclei, and the results are compared with existing data for stable Ni isotopes and 68Ni from Rossi et al. (2013) and other experiments. The findings suggest that the pygmy dipole strength is strongly influenced by shell effects and that its isospin structure depends on the mass region, enabling future constraints on the nuclear equation of state from data on 68Ni and other neutron-rich nuclei.
