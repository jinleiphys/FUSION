# Running coupling effects in the evolution of jet quenching

> We study the consequences of including the running of the QCD coupling in the equation describing the evolution of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ in the double logarithmic approximation. To start with, we revisit the case of a fixed coupling, for which we obtain exact solutions valid for generic values of the transverse momentum (above the medium saturation scale) and corresponding to various initial conditions. In the case of a running coupling, we construct approximate solutions in the form of truncated series obtained via successive iterations, whose convergence is well under control. We thus deduce the dominant asymptotic behavior of the renormalized $\hat q$ in the limit of a large evolution time $Y\equiv\ln(L/λ)$, with $L$ the size of the medium and $λ$ the typical wavelength of a medium constituent. We show that the asymptotic expansion is universal with respect to the choice of the initial condition at $Y=0$ and, moreover, it is remarkably similar to the corresponding expansion for the saturation momentum of a shockwave (a large nucleus). As expected, the running of the coupling significantly slows down the increase of $\hat q$ with $Y$ in the asymptotic regime at $Y\gg 1$. For the phenomenologically interesting value $Y\simeq 3$, we find an enhancement factor close to 3, independently of the initial condition and for both fixed and running coupling.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the running of the QCD coupling in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) evolution equation for the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) significantly slows its growth with evolution time \(Y\equiv\ln(L/\lambda)\), and that the asymptotic expansion of \(\ln\hat q_s(Y)\) is universal with respect to initial conditions and remarkably similar to that of the saturation momentum for a shockwave target.

## Method

The authors solve the DLA integral equation for \(\hat q(\tau,\bp^2)\) with a saturation boundary, first for fixed coupling (obtaining exact analytic solutions) and then for running coupling using successive iterations to construct truncated series whose convergence is controlled. The running coupling is implemented via the one-loop QCD beta function, with the coupling evaluated at the scale set by the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon. The asymptotic behavior at large \(Y\) is extracted numerically from the iteration series.

## Key numbers

* For the phenomenologically interesting value \(Y\simeq 3\), the enhancement factor \(\hat q_s(Y)/\hat q^{(0)}\) is close to 3, independently of the initial condition and for both fixed and running coupling.
* The dominant asymptotic term for \(\ln\hat q_s(Y)\) with running coupling grows like \(\sqrt{Y}\), slower than the fixed-coupling behavior \(\ln\hat q_s(Y)\propto Y\).
* The running coupling parameter is \(b = 12 N_c/(11 N_c - 2 N_f)\); for \(N_f=N_c=3\), \(b = 4/3\).
* The DLA is strictly trustworthy for \(Y\) in the window \(1/\sqrt{\bar\alpha} \lesssim Y < 1/\bar\alpha\), which is parametrically large when \(\bar\alpha \ll 1\).

## Context

This work extends the DLA evolution of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) previously developed at fixed coupling by Liou (2013), Iancu (2014), and Blaizot (2014) to include running coupling effects, which are known to be essential for realistic phenomenology. The asymptotic expansion for \(\ln\hat q_s(Y)\) is remarkably similar to that of \(\ln Q_s^2(Y)\) for a shockwave target computed by Mueller (2002) and Munier (2003) using the BFKL equation with a saturation boundary, suggesting universal features in high-energy evolution towards saturation. The results enable more realistic predictions for jet quenching phenomenology at RHIC and the LHC, where \(Y\simeq 2\div 3\) is physically relevant.
