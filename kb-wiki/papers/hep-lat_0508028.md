# Ghost condensation on the lattice

> We perform a numerical study of ghost condensation -- in the so-called Overhauser channel -- for SU(2) lattice gauge theory in minimal Landau gauge. The off-diagonal components of the momentum-space ghost propagator G^{cd}(p) are evaluated for lattice volumes V = 8^4, 12^4, 16^4, 20^4, 24^4 and for three values of the lattice coupling: β= 2.2, 2.3, 2.4. Our data show that the quantity ϕ^b(p) = ε^{bcd} G^{cd}(p) / 2 is zero within error bars, being characterized by very large statistical fluctuations. On the contrary, |ϕ^b(p)| has relatively small error bars and behaves at small momenta as L^{-2} p^{-z}, where L is the lattice side in physical units and z \approx 4. We argue that the large fluctuations for ϕ^b(p) come from spontaneous breaking of a global symmetry and are associated with ghost condensation. It may thus be necessary (in numerical simulations at finite volume) to consider |ϕ^b(p)| instead of ϕ^b(p), to avoid a null average due to tunneling between different broken vacua. Also, we show that ϕ^b(p) is proportional to the Fourier-transformed gluon field components {\widetilde A}_μ^b(q). This explains the L^{-2} dependence of |ϕ^b(p)|, as induced by the behavior of | {\widetilde A}_μ^b(q) |. We fit our data for |ϕ^b(p)| to the theoretical prediction (r / L^2 + v) / (p^4 + v^2), obtaining for the ghost condensate v an upper bound of about 0.058 GeV^2. In order to check if v is nonzero in the continuum limit, one probably needs numerical simulations at much larger physical volumes than the ones we consider. As a by-product of our analysis, we perform a careful study of the color structure of the inverse Faddeev-Popov matrix in momentum space.

## Key claim

The authors find that the quantity \(\phi^b(p) = \epsilon^{bcd} G^{cd}(p) / 2\) is zero within error bars with very large statistical fluctuations, while its absolute value \(|\phi^b(p)|\) has relatively small error bars and behaves at small momenta as \(L^{-2} p^{-z}\) with \(z \approx 4\); they argue this pattern indicates spontaneous breaking of a global \(SL(2,R)\) symmetry associated with ghost condensation, but caution that to check if the ghost condensate \(v\) is nonzero in the continuum limit one probably needs numerical simulations at much larger physical volumes than those considered.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of pure \(SU(2)\) lattice gauge theory in minimal Landau gauge, evaluating the off-diagonal components of the momentum-space ghost propagator \(G^{cd}(p)\) for lattice volumes \(V = 8^4, 12^4, 16^4, 20^4, 24^4\) at three values of the lattice coupling \(\beta = 2.2, 2.3, 2.4\). They computed \(\phi^b(p)\) and its absolute value, and fitted the data for \(|\phi^b(p)|\) to the theoretical prediction \((r / L^2 + v) / (p^4 + v^2)\). As a by-product, they performed a careful study of the color structure of the inverse Faddeev-Popov matrix in momentum space.

## Key numbers

* For \(|\phi^b(p)|\) at small momenta, the behavior is \(L^{-2} p^{-z}\) with \(z \approx 4\), where \(L\) is the lattice side in physical units.
* Fitting \(|\phi^b(p)|\) to \((r / L^2 + v) / (p^4 + v^2)\) yields an upper bound for the ghost condensate \(v\) of about \(0.058\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* Lattice volumes used: \(8^4\), \(12^4\), \(16^4\), \(20^4\), \(24^4\).
* Lattice coupling values: \(\beta = 2.2\), \(2.3\), \(2.4\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of ghost condensation in the Overhauser channel, previously considered only in Furui (2003) for minimal Landau gauge in both \(SU(2)\) and \(SU(3)\) cases, where the off-diagonal anti-symmetric components of the ghost propagator were found to be identically zero. The present results support the theoretical prediction by Dudal (2003) that off-diagonal components of the ghost propagator are proportional to the ghost condensate \(v\) and that a nonzero expectation value is related to spontaneous breaking of a global \(SL(2,R)\) symmetry. The findings enable future lattice studies at larger physical volumes to determine whether the ghost condensate \(v\) is nonzero in the continuum limit.
