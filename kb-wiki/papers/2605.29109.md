# First steps towards gauge-independent vortex identification through machine learning

> As a first step towards machine identification of confining objects in thermalized lattice gauge configurations, we present our 2dVoId model for center vortex identification on pure SU(2) lattices in $D = 2$ dimensions. We create a training set by inserting thin Z2 vortices at various locations on a zero action lattice, and then distort those configurations by applying random SU(2) gauge transformations, noise, and by thickening the vortices via cooling. For moderate vortex visibility, our model is able to reliably identify the location of center vortices. We additionally demonstrate scalability through tiling strategies, which will enable generalization to higher dimensions while reducing training costs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a convolutional neural network can identify center vortices in pure SU(2) lattice gauge configurations without gauge fixing, but only for configurations where the vortex signal is moderately visible above noise; the method is not yet tested on thermalized Monte Carlo configurations in the physically relevant D=3 or D=4 dimensions.

## Method

The authors generate training configurations on 24x24 periodic lattices by inserting thin Z2 vortices at known positions on a cold (all links = identity) lattice, then applying random SU(2) gauge transformations, random link noise, and iterative cooling to thicken the vortices. They train a convolutional neural network (2dVoId) with three hidden convolutional layers using 3x3 kernels and circular padding, taking as input the 16 real components of the two SU(2) link variables per site, and outputting a probability map for vortex presence at each plaquette. They also test a tiling strategy where a model trained on 12x12 lattices with zero padding is applied to larger lattices by dividing them into overlapping patches.

## Key numbers

- Training configurations span a grid of gauge noise scales and link noise scales each in [0, 0.3] with step size 0.1, giving 16 combinations, with 400 training and 40 validation configurations per combination.
- At high visibility (V > 0.6), all density models achieve sub-1% per-plaquette error on 24x24 lattices.
- The model was trained for 30 epochs with batches of 64 configurations, using an initial learning rate of 10^-3 reduced by factor 0.7 when validation loss fails to improve for 5 epochs.
- The tiling test uses a model trained on 12x12 lattices, applied to 24x24 test lattices via four non-overlapping quadrants with a halo of 5 border sites per quadrant.

## Context

This work extends the center vortex confinement mechanism literature (t Hooft 1977, Cornwall 1979, Nielsen and Olesen 1979) by addressing the long-standing criticism that standard vortex identification relies on gauge fixing to maximal center gauge (Del Debbio et al. 1998). It builds on machine learning applications to lattice gauge theory (Carrasquilla and Melko 2017, Wetzel and Scherzer 2017) and symmetry-aware neural architectures (Favoni et al. 2020, Nagai et al. 2021). The D=2 proof-of-principle enables future extension to D=3 and D=4 where center vortices are physically relevant for confinement, and where the tiling strategy would allow training on small patches while deploying on large lattice volumes.
