# Small-$x$ Factorization from Effective Field Theory

> We derive a factorization theorem that allows for resummation of small-$x$ logarithms by exploiting Glauber operators in the soft collinear effective field theory. Our analysis is carried out for the hadronic tensor $W^{μν}$ in deep inelastic scattering, and leads to the definition of a new gauge invariant soft function $S^{μν}$ that describes quark and gluon emission in the central region. This soft function provides a new framework for extending resummed calculations for coefficient functions to higher logarithmic orders. Our factorization also defines impact factors by universal collinear functions that are process independent, for instance being identical in small-$x$ DIS and Drell-Yan.

## Key claim

The paper derives a factorization theorem for the hadronic tensor \(W^{\mu\nu}\) in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) that resums small-\(x\) logarithms using Glauber operators in soft collinear effective field theory (SCET), but this result is explicitly valid only at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) order and relies on the specific kinematic regime where the momentum transfer \(Q^2\) is fixed while the center-of-mass energy \(s\) becomes asymptotically large.

## Method

The authors analyze DIS in the electron-proton center-of-mass frame, using light-cone coordinates and SCET with Glauber operators to separate the dynamics into soft and collinear modes. They derive a factorization formula \(W^{\mu\nu}(q,P) = \int d^{d-2}k_\perp S^{\mu\nu}(q,k_\perp,\frac{\nu}{xP^-},\epsilon) \, C(k_\perp,P,\frac{\nu}{P^-},\epsilon)\), where \(S^{\mu\nu}\) is a gauge-invariant soft function describing central-region quark and gluon emission and \(C\) is a collinear function. Fixed-order calculations of \(S^{\mu\nu}\) for the \(F_2\) and \(F_L\) structure functions verify that this soft function replaces the off-shell cross sections used in earlier approaches, while the NLO calculation of \(C\) confirms it satisfies the \(d\)-dimensional BFKL equation.

## Key numbers

* The factorization theorem is valid at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) order, enabling computation of DIS coefficient functions at this order.
* The leading-order calculation of the soft function \(S^{\mu\nu}\) for the \(F_2\) and \(F_L\) structure functions directly verifies that this function provides a proper replacement of the off-shell cross sections used in earlier approaches.
* The next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of the collinear function \(C\) enables direct verification that it satisfies the \(d\)-dimensional BFKL equation.
* The leading logarithmic (LL) series for the coefficient function and anomalous dimensions shows each power of \(\alpha_s\) brings at most one additional logarithm, with the coefficient function beginning at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) due to the presence of an intermediate soft sector.
* The resummation of the glue-to-glue anomalous dimension at LL level follows from the implicit equation \(1 = \frac{\alpha_s C_A}{\pi n} \chi(\gamma_{gg}(n,\alpha_s))\) with \(\chi(\gamma) = 2\psi(1) - \psi(\gamma) - \psi(1-\gamma)\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Catani and Hautmann (1994) who first resummed leading small-\(x\) logarithms in coefficient functions and anomalous dimensions using dimensional regularization, and builds on the SCET-based description of the forward scattering limit with Glauber operators from Rothstein (2016). The factorization defines a single gauge-invariant soft function \(S^{\mu\nu}\) that replaces the multiple off-shell cross sections required in earlier approaches, and the collinear function is universal across processes (identical in small-\(x\) DIS and Drell-Yan), enabling future extension to higher logarithmic orders and other processes beyond inclusive DIS.
