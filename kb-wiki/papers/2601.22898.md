# $f_2(1270)\toπ+π$ as a probe of spin and vorticity in heavy-ion collisions

> The correlation between vorticity and spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions can be probed through polarization measurements of hadrons, whose total spin originates from both constituent-quark spins and orbital angular momentum in the quark-model framework. To motivate such experimental studies, we calculate the general angular distribution of produced pion in $f_2(1270)\toπ+π$ using interaction Lagrangian and helicity formalism and check that both methods yield the same result. The distribution is given as a function of angle between pion and initial quantization axis of $f_2$ and the spin density matrix element of $f_2$. Its diagonal entries and $ρ_{20}$ component were computed assuming local thermal equilibrium and blast wave model for different centrality classes, hence given as a function of azimuthal angle with respect to the impact parameter.

## Key claim

The angular distribution of pions from the decay \(f_2(1270)\to\pi+\pi\) can be used to probe the relationship between vorticity and spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions, but the interpretation depends on whether the spin density matrix follows a simple thermal equilibrium form or a more complex coalescence model that accounts for imperfect equilibration between spin and vorticity.

## Method

The authors calculated the general angular distribution of pions from \(f_2(1270)\to\pi+\pi\) using both an effective interaction Lagrangian and the helicity formalism, verifying that both methods yield identical results. They then computed the diagonal spin density matrix elements and the \(\rho_{20}\) component of the \(f_2\) meson assuming local thermal equilibrium within a blast-wave model that incorporates elliptic flow and polarization, using parameters for three centrality classes (0-15%, 15-30%, and 30-60%) and averaging over a space-time rapidity range of \(-4\leq\eta\leq4\).

## Key numbers

* The decay width of \(f_2(1270)\to\pi+\pi\) is \(185.8\times(0.843)\;\text{MeV}\), with the branching ratio given in parentheses.
* The momentum of the final pion in the \(f_2\) rest frame is \(|\boldsymbol{p}_1|=623\;\text{MeV}\).
* The dimensionless coupling constant for the \(f_2\pi\pi\) interaction is \(g_{f_{2}\pi\pi}=5.89\).
* The global vorticity parameter used is \(\Omega_{\text{global}}/T = 0.024\), derived from a numerical estimate of \(0.02\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) and a temperature \(T=0.165\;\text{GeV}\).
* The blast-wave model parameters for the three centrality classes include: for 0-15%, \(\epsilon=0.055\), \(\delta=0.12\), \(\tau_f=7.666\;\text{fm}\), \(r_{\text{max}}=6.540\;\text{fm}\); for 15-30%, \(\epsilon=0.097\), \(\delta=0.26\), \(\tau_f=6.258\;\text{fm}\), \(r_{\text{max}}=5.417\;\text{fm}\); for 30-60%, \(\epsilon=0.137\), \(\delta=0.37\), \(\tau_f=4.266\;\text{fm}\), \(r_{\text{max}}=3.779\;\text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions beyond vector mesons (such as \(\phi\), \(K^*\), and \(D^*\) measured by STAR and ALICE) to the tensor meson \(f_2(1270)\), which carries more spin information due to its \(5\times5\) density matrix. The calculations build on the blast-wave model with elliptic flow and polarization developed by Florkowski and collaborators, and incorporate global vorticity as modeled by Karpenko. The results enable future experimental comparisons that could distinguish between the thermal equilibrium picture of spin and vorticity and non-equilibrium scenarios described by a Fert-Valet type equation with a finite relaxation time, as proposed by Montena and collaborators.
