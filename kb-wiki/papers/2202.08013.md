# Elastic pion-proton and pion-pion scattering at high energies in holographic QCD

> The total and differential cross sections of high energy pion-proton and pion-pion scattering are investigated in a holographic QCD model, focusing on the Regge regime in which the Pomeron exchange gives the dominant contribution to those cross sections. The Pomeron is described by the Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator, and the hadron-Pomeron couplings are given by gravitational form factors of the hadrons, which are obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD models. Since the parameters, which characterize the Pomeron trajectory, have been determined in a preceding study of the proton-proton scattering, the pion-proton cross sections can be expressed with a single adjustable parameter. Once this parameter is determined by the experimental data of the pion-proton total cross section, its differential cross section and the pion-pion cross sections can be calculated without any additional parameter. Although the currently available data are limited, it is found that our calculation is consistent with those. Our predictions are explicitly shown, and the present model can be tested at the future experimental facilities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic pion-proton and pion-pion scattering cross sections at high energies can be described by a holographic QCD model with only a single adjustable parameter (the pion-Pomeron coupling constant), once the Pomeron trajectory parameters and proton-Pomeron coupling are fixed from a preceding proton-proton study (Xie 2019). The hedge is that the currently available experimental data are limited and have large uncertainties in the TeV region, so the absolute magnitudes of the predicted cross sections may have uncertainties, especially because the fit is dominated by lower-energy data.

## Method

The authors model the Pomeron as a Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator and obtain the hadron-Pomeron couplings from the gravitational form factors of the proton and pion, which are computed using bottom-up AdS/QCD models (hard-wall model). The proton gravitational form factor is derived from a five-dimensional Dirac equation, and the pion gravitational form factor from an effective action for meson fields in the chiral limit. The total and differential cross sections for pion-proton and pion-pion scattering are then expressed analytically, with the Pomeron trajectory parameters (intercept and slope) and proton-Pomeron coupling taken from the preceding proton-proton study (Xie 2019). The single remaining parameter, the pion-Pomeron coupling constant, is determined by fitting the model to pion-proton total cross section data from the Particle Data Group (2020) for laboratory momenta above 100 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

*   The best-fit value for the pion-Pomeron coupling constant is \(\lambda_\pi = 3.950 \pm 0.016\) GeV\(^{-1}\), determined from pion-proton total cross section data with \(P_{\mathrm{lab}} \ge 100\) GeV/c (corresponding to \(\sqrt{s} \ge 13.73\) GeV).
*   The Pomeron trajectory parameters taken from the preceding proton-proton study (Xie 2019) are: intercept \(\alpha_c(0) = 1.086\), slope \(\alpha_c' = 0.377\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and proton-Pomeron coupling \(\lambda_p = 9.70\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
*   The predicted ratio of total cross sections is \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}^{\pi p} / \sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}^{p p} = 0.73\) and \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}^{\pi \pi} / \sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}^{p p} = 0.39\).
*   The cutoff parameter for the pion in the hard-wall AdS/QCD model is \(z_0^\pi = 1/(322~\mathrm{MeV})\), determined from the \(\rho\)-meson mass \(m_\rho\).
*   The cutoff parameter for the proton is \(z_0^p = 1/(245~\mathrm{MeV})\), determined from the proton mass.

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to elastic proton-proton scattering developed by Xie (2019) to pion-involved processes, using the universality of the Pomeron to fix most parameters. It builds on the formalism of Domokos (2009) for holographic description of hadron scattering in the Regge regime and uses gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models by Abidin (2008, 2009). The model provides predictions for pion-pion total and differential cross sections that can be tested at future experimental facilities, and it highlights the sensitivity of the differential cross section slope to the internal structure (gravitational form factor) of the hadrons.
