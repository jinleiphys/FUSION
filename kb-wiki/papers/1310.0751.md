# Off-shell effects in the relativistic mean field model and their role in CC (anti)neutrino scattering at MiniBooNE kinematics

> The relativistic mean field (RMF) model is used to describe nucleons in the nucleus and thereby to evaluate the effects of having dynamically off-shell spinors. Compared with free, on-shell nucleons as employed in some other models, within the RMF nucleons are described by relativistic spinors with strongly enhanced lower components. In this work it is seen that for MiniBooNE kinematics, neutrino charged-current quasielastic cross sections show some sensitivity to these off-shell effects, while for the antineutrino-nucleus case the total cross sections are seen to be essentially independent of the enhancement of the lower components. As was found to be the case when comparing the RMF results with the neutrino-nucleus data, the present impulse approximation predictions within the RMF also fall short of the MiniBooNE antineutrino-nucleus data.

## Key claim

For MiniBooNE kinematics, neutrino charged-current quasielastic cross sections show some sensitivity to dynamical off-shell effects (enhanced lower components of nucleon spinors in the RMF model), while for antineutrino-nucleus scattering the total cross sections are essentially independent of these effects due to cancellation between the transverse T and T' contributions.

## Method

The authors employed the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model to describe bound and continuum nucleon states as self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions derived from a Lagrangian with sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They compared these fully relativistic results, which include dynamically off-shell spinors with strongly enhanced lower components, against the Effective Momentum Approach (EMA), where spinors are placed on the mass shell by enforcing the same upper-to-lower component relationship as for free spinors. The flux-integrated double-differential and total cross sections for charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino and antineutrino scattering on carbon-12 were computed and compared to MiniBooNE experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mean beam energy for neutrinos: ⟨E_ν⟩ = 788 MeV; for antineutrinos: ⟨E_ν̄⟩ = 665 MeV
- The standard axial mass value used is M_A = 1.03 GeV/c² (world average from deuteron data), compared to the MiniBooNE-fitted value of 1.35 GeV/c²
- The difference between RMF and EMA results for neutrino total cross sections is about a few percent
- The cancellation of T and T' contributions for antineutrinos breaks above 2 GeV of incoming lepton energy
- Integration over muon kinetic energies for differential cross sections: 0.2 GeV < T_μ < 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering triggered by MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010), where the Relativistic Fermi Gas model required an enlarged axial mass (1.35 GeV/c²) to fit data, while NOMAD (Lyubushkin, 2008) and MINERνA (2012) favor the standard value (1.03 GeV/c²). The RMF approach, previously validated against inclusive electron scattering data, is compared to factorized approaches like the SuSA model (Amaro, 2004) and the Spectral Function approach (Benhar, 2010; Ankowski, 2012). The results show that dynamical off-shell effects are relatively small for neutrinos and negligible for antineutrinos at MiniBooNE kinematics, and that RMF predictions still fall short of MiniBooNE data, suggesting additional contributions from two-body currents and multi-nucleon processes are needed, as explored by Martini (2009, 2010), Nieves (2011, 2013), and Amaro (2010, 2011).
