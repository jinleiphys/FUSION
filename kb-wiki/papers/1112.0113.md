# Muon Capture on Deuteron and 3He: A Personal Review

> The present status of theoretical and experimental studies of muon capture reactions on light nuclei is reviewed. In particular, the recent results for the two reactions 2H(μ^-,ν_μ)nn and 3He(μ^-,ν_μ)3H are presented, and the unresolved discrepancies among different measurements and calculations, open problems, and future developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current theoretical and experimental status of muon capture on deuteron and ³He, highlighting unresolved discrepancies between different measurements and calculations, particularly for the deuteron capture rate where experimental errors remain in the 6–10% range and theoretical predictions vary between 386 and 423 s⁻¹.

## Method

The author presents the formalism for calculating muon capture rates using the weak interaction Hamiltonian with multipole expansion of the nuclear weak current operators. For muon capture on deuteron, the final nn state is expanded in partial waves up to J≤2 and L≤3, considering ¹S₀, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂–³F₂, and ¹D₂ channels. For muon capture on ³He, the contributing reduced matrix elements are limited to C₀(V), C₁(A), L₀(V), L₁(A), E₁(A), and M₁(V). The nuclear wave functions are obtained using the hyperspherical-harmonics method with two representative Hamiltonian models: AV18/UIX (phenomenological) and N3LO/N2LO (chiral effective field theory).

## Key numbers

- Muon capture on deuteron doublet rate Γᴰ: 392 ± 2.3 s⁻¹ (Marcucci et al. 2011), 386 s⁻¹ (Ando et al. 2002), 423 ± 7 s⁻¹ (Ricci et al. 2010)
- Muon capture on ³He total rate Γ₀: 1484 ± 13 s⁻¹ (Marcucci et al. 2011), 1484 ± 8 s⁻¹ (Marcucci et al. 2002), 1499 ± 16 s⁻¹ (Gazit 2008)
- Experimental Γ₀ for ³He: 1496 ± 4 s⁻¹ (Ackerbauer et al. 1998)
- Experimental Γᴰ for deuteron: 470 ± 29 s⁻¹ (Bardin et al. 1986), 409 ± 40 s⁻¹ (Cargnelli et al. 1986)
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor theoretical value at q₀² = -0.88 m_μ²: G_PS^TH(q₀²) = 8.2 ± 0.2
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor experimental value: G_PS^EXP(q₀²) = 7.3 ± 1.2
- MuCap collaboration singlet capture rate on hydrogen: 725.0 ± 13.7 (stat) ± 10.7 (syst) s⁻¹
- Vector asymmetry A_v for ³He: 0.63 ± 0.09 (stat)⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·₁₄ (syst) (Souder et al. 1998)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical work on muon capture reactions using modern nuclear potentials and currents, comparing results from the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach and chiral effective field theory. The calculations are relevant for extracting the induced pseudoscalar form factor and for testing nuclear models used in astrophysical reactions such as the pp and hep reactions studied by Schiavilla et al. (1998), Marcucci et al. (2000), and Park et al. (2003). The on-going MuSun experiment at PSI aims to resolve the discrepancies in the deuteron capture rate by achieving 1.5% precision, building on techniques developed for the MuCap experiment.
