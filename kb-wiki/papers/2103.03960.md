# Distribution amplitudes of light diquarks

> Accumulating evidence indicates that soft quark+quark (diquark) correlations play an important role in the structure and interactions of hadrons constituted from three or more valence-quarks; so, it is worth developing insights into diquark structure. Using a leading-order truncation of those equations needed to solve continuum two-valence-body bound-state problems, the leading-twist two-parton distribution amplitudes (DAs) of light-quark scalar and pseudovector diquarks are calculated. The diquark DAs are narrower and taller than the asymptotic profile that characterises mesons. Consequently, the valence quasiparticles in a diquark are less likely to carry a large light-front fraction of the system's total momentum than those in a meson. These features may both influence the form of baryon DAs and be transmitted to diquark distribution functions (DFs), in which case their impact will be felt, e.g. in the proton's $u$ and $d$ valence-quark DFs.

## Key claim

Using a leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of continuum bound-state equations, the leading-twist two-parton distribution amplitudes (DAs) of light-quark scalar and pseudovector diquarks are narrower and taller than both the asymptotic QCD profile and the DAs of their counterpart mesons; this implies valence quasiparticles in a diquark are less likely to carry a large light-front fraction of the system's total momentum than those in a meson.

## Method

The authors solved the quark gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equations for mesons and diquarks using a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation with a QCD-connected interaction kernel that preserves one-loop renormalisation group behaviour. They computed Mellin moments of the leading-twist two-parton DAs via light-front projection of the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, using a mass-independent momentum subtraction scheme at renormalisation scale ζ_H = 0.33 GeV, and reconstructed the pointwise DAs from the first four moments using physically motivated functional forms.

## Context

This work provides the first predictions for the x-dependence of light diquark DAs, extending prior studies of meson DAs (Chang 2013) and proton structure (Mezrag 2017). The results are consistent with the pattern that diquark electromagnetic radii are about 1.1 times those of corresponding mesons (Maris 2004). The narrower diquark DAs imply that valence quarks inside diquark correlations within a proton will be less likely to participate in hard interactions than the bystander valence quark, which should manifest in the ratio of d and u quark distribution functions in the proton. The authors note that corrections to RL truncation may affect certain aspects of diquark correlations due to confinement, and suggest replicating the analysis with more sophisticated bound-state kernels (Williams 2015, Binosi 2016, Qin 2020).
