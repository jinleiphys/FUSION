# Padé expansion and nucleon-nucleon scattering in coupled channels

> We extend our Padé-aided analysis of the nonperturbative renormalization of nucleon-nucleon scattering to the case of coupled channels.

## Key claim

The authors extend their Padé-aided nonperturbative renormalization scheme for nucleon-nucleon scattering from uncoupled to coupled channels, demonstrating that the prescription dependence in the T-matrix can be parametrized via Padé approximants of the nonperturbative factor Re(G) while preserving unitarity automatically, but only for laboratory energies below about 100 MeV when using the simplest constant-Padé approximation.

## Method

The authors transform the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix into an inverse form T^{-1} = V^{-1} - G, where G is a 2x2 matrix factor containing all loop processes. They apply a constant Padé approximant (energy-independent) to the real part of G, parametrizing the nonperturbative renormalization prescription through three fitted parameters g^0_{2,2}, g^0_{2,4}, and g^0_{4,4}. These parameters are determined by fitting to partial-wave analysis (PWA) data for the phase shifts and mixing angle of the ^3D_3-^3G_3 coupled channels at laboratory energies from 0 to 50 MeV, using potentials from Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000) at LO, NLO, and NNLO.

## Key numbers

* The fitted Padé parameter g^0_{2,2} (diagonal, ^3D_3 channel) takes values -1300, -1500, and -770 MeV^2 at LO, NLO, and NNLO respectively, with corresponding scale sqrt(|g^0_{2,2}|) = 36, 39, and 28 MeV.
* The off-diagonal parameter g^0_{2,4} takes values 4200, 5200, and 210 MeV^2 at LO, NLO, and NNLO, with sqrt(|g^0_{2,4}|) = 65, 72, and 14 MeV.
* The diagonal parameter g^0_{4,4} (^3G_3 channel) takes values -25000, -33000, and 5200 MeV^2 at LO, NLO, and NNLO, with sqrt(|g^0_{4,4}|) = 160, 180, and 72 MeV.
* The extracted scales from all Padé parameters lie between 10 and 200 MeV, corresponding to alpha values in the range 0.26 to 0.91, within the expected EFT scale range (10 to 500 MeV).
* The constant-Padé approximation reproduces PWA phase shifts for ^3D_3 and ^3G_3 up to laboratory energies of about 100 MeV, but fails above this energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Padé-aided renormalization analysis for uncoupled channels (Yang and Huang, 2007; Yang, Huang, and Liu, 2006) to the coupled-channel case, addressing the ongoing controversy about consistent renormalization and power counting in nonperturbative nucleon-nucleon effective field theory (Bedaque and van Kolck, 2002; Epelbaum, 2006; Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck, 2005). The method provides an alternative to other nonperturbative parametrization schemes (Yang, Elster, and Phillips, 2007; Entem, Ruiz Arriola, Pavon Valderrama, and Machleidt, 2007) and enables systematic investigation of whether nonperturbative renormalization scales remain within the EFT domain. Future work requires more sophisticated (energy-dependent) Padé approximants to improve predictions at higher energies and to observe order-by-order convergence.
