# Actinide and lanthanide molecules to search for strong CP-violation

> The existence of the fundamental CP-violating interactions inside the nucleus leads to the existence of the nuclear Schiff moment. The Schiff moment potential corresponds to the electric field localized inside the nucleus and directed along its spin. This field can interact with electrons of an atom and induce the permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) of the whole system. The Schiff moment and corresponding electric field are enhanced in the nuclei with the octupole deformation leading to the enhanced atomic EDM. There is also a few-order enhancement of the T,P-violating effects in molecules due to the existence of energetically close levels of opposite parity. We study the Schiff moment enhancement in the class of diatomic molecules with octupole-deformed lanthanide and actinide nuclei: $^{227}$AcF, $^{227}$AcN, $^{227}$AcO$^+$, $^{229}$ThO, $^{153}$EuO$^+$ and $^{153}$EuN. Projecting the existing experimental achievements to measure EDM in diamagnetic molecules with spherical nucleus ($^{205}$TlF) to the considered systems one can expect a very high sensitivity to the quantum chromodynamics parameter ${\bar θ}$ and other hadronic CP-violation parameters surpassing the current best limits by several orders of magnitude. It can have a dramatic impact on the modern understanding of the nature of CP-violating fundamental interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that diatomic molecules containing octupole-deformed lanthanide and actinide nuclei (specifically \(^{227}\)AcF, \(^{227}\)AcN, \(^{227}\)AcO\(^+\), \(^{229}\)ThO, \(^{153}\)EuO\(^+\), and \(^{153}\)EuN) can provide sensitivity to the quantum chromodynamics parameter \(\bar{\theta}\) and other hadronic CP-violation parameters that surpasses current best limits by several orders of magnitude, but this projection depends on explicit molecular electronic structure calculations rather than simple atomic-based rescaling, which the authors show can have large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled-cluster calculations at the CCSD(T) level (including single, double, and perturbative triple excitations) using generalized relativistic effective core potentials (GRECP) to compute the molecular constants \(X\) and \(W_S^{(2)}\) that describe the interaction between the nuclear Schiff moment and electrons in each diatomic molecule. The wavefunction behavior inside the heavy nucleus was restored using a two-step procedure that accounts for finite nuclear size corrections, and the resulting molecular constants were combined with nuclear Schiff moment estimates from prior work to predict the energy shifts for a fully polarized molecule at \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{227}\)AcF, the molecular constant \(X = -1593\) \(e/a_B^4\) (CCSD(T) level), yielding \(W_S^{(2)} = -8240\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 0.4\) mHz.
* For \(^{227}\)AcN, \(X = -8950\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = -46295\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 2.5\) mHz.
* For \(^{227}\)AcO\(^+\), \(X = -11302\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = -58461\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 3.1\) mHz.
* For \(^{229}\)ThO, \(X = -3332\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = -17085\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| \leq 0.3\) mHz.
* For \(^{153}\)EuO\(^+\), \(X = -2114\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = -11677\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 0.4\) mHz.
* For \(^{153}\)EuN, \(X = -1890\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = -10419\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 0.3\) mHz.
* For comparison, \(^{205}\)TlF has \(X = 7004\) \(e/a_B^4\) and \(W_S^{(2)} = 37192\) \(e/a_B^4\); the estimated energy shift for \(\bar{\theta} = 10^{-10}\) is \(|\Delta E| = 0.007\) mHz, and the previous experiment (Cho, 1991) measured \((-0.14 \pm 0.24)\) mHz.
* The nuclear Schiff moment for \(^{227}\)Ac is \(S = 6\) \(e\) fm\(^3\) \(\bar{\theta}\); for \(^{229}\)Th, \(S \leq 2\) \(e\) fm\(^3\) \(\bar{\theta}\); for \(^{153}\)Eu, \(S = -3.7\) \(e\) fm\(^3\) \(\bar{\theta}\); for \(^{205}\)Tl, \(S = 0.02\) \(e\) fm\(^3\) \(\bar{\theta}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Schiff moment enhancement in molecules, which had been limited to molecules containing \(s\)- and \(p\)-elements (e.g., TlF studied by Cho in 1991 and Petrov in 2002), to the class of \(f\)-element molecules with octupole-deformed nuclei. The authors show that simple atomic-based rescaling, as used in Flambaum (2019), can be unreliable for these systems, and that explicit molecular calculations are necessary. The results enable experimental searches for CP-violation using molecules with stable or long-lived isotopes (\(^{153}\)Eu is stable; \(^{227}\)Ac has a 21.8-year half-life; \(^{229}\)Th has a 7900-year half-life), which are more practical than short-lived isotopes like \(^{225}\)Ra (14.9-day half-life) studied in Kudashov (2013). The different structure of contributions from \(\bar{\theta}\) and quark chromo-EDMs in these deformed-nucleus systems compared to spherical-nucleus TlF means that combined experiments could disentangle different sources of CP-violation.
