# Theory of Neutrino Physics -- Snowmass TF11 (aka NF08) Topical Group Report

> This is the report for the topical group Theory of Neutrino Physics (TF11/NF08) for Snowmass 2021. This report summarizes the progress in the field of theoretical neutrino physics in the past decade, the current status of the field, and the prospects for the upcoming decade.

## Key claim

This Snowmass topical group report summarizes the progress, current status, and future prospects for theoretical neutrino physics, emphasizing that a robust and expanded theory effort is necessary to interpret the upcoming "deluge of neutrino-related data" from next-generation experiments, but that the domestic neutrino theory community remains "very small" and requires a "qualitative increase" in support to meet experimental schedules.

## Method

The report synthesizes contributions from dozens of community white papers submitted during the Snowmass 2021 process, covering neutrino mass mechanisms, flavor model-building, phenomenology of oscillations and scattering, nuclear physics inputs for cross-section calculations, and connections to astrophysics and cosmology. It identifies key theoretical challenges across energy scales from the cosmic neutrino background (meV) to ultrahigh-energy cosmic neutrinos (EeV), and outlines the required interplay between lattice gauge theory, many-body nuclear theory, perturbative QCD, and event-generator development.

## Key numbers

* The dimension-five operator scale for neutrino masses is \(\Lambda \sim 10^{15}\) GeV, derived from \(m_\nu = v^2/\Lambda\) with current neutrino mass information.
* In the Type I seesaw, new heavy neutral fermions can be as light as a few eV, with the lower bound derived from oscillation experiments.
* The Cabibbo angle \(\theta_c\) appears in a flavor-model sum rule: \(\sin^2\theta_{13} = \sin^2\theta_c/\sqrt{2}\).
* The quark CP phase \(\delta_{\rm CKM}\) is known at the 6.5% level: \(\delta_{\rm CKM} = 68.8^\circ \pm 4.5^\circ\).
* Cosmic neutrinos have been detected with energies between 1 TeV and 10 PeV.
* The cosmic neutrino background decoupled at temperatures around 1 MeV (10 billion kelvin), compared to the cosmic microwave background which decoupled at 3000 K.

## Context

This report extends the Snowmass 2021 process and builds on decades of theoretical neutrino physics, including the Weinberg (1979) dimension-five operator and the seesaw mechanisms (Type I, II, III). It complements other Snowmass topical group reports (EF08, EF09, NF02, NF03, RF5) and cites dozens of community white papers submitted during the process. The report identifies open questions including the mechanism behind nonzero neutrino masses, the flavor puzzle, the short-baseline anomalies (referencing MicroBooNE results from 2021), and the need for improved cross-section predictions for DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande. It enables future work by defining precision targets for oscillation parameters (e.g., \(\cos\delta\) and \(\theta_{23}\)) and by calling for increased investment in neutrino theory, lattice QCD, nuclear many-body methods, and event-generator development.
