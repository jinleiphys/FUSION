# Modeling Backward-Angle ($u$-channel) Virtual Compton Scattering at an Electron-Ion Collider

> High-energy backward ($u$-channel) reactions can involve very large momentum transfers to the target baryons, shifting them by many units of rapidity. These reactions are difficult to understand in conventional models in which baryon number is carried by the valence quarks. Backward Compton scattering is an especially attractive experimental target, because of its simple final state. There is currently limited data on this process, and that data is at low center-of-mass energies. In this paper, we examine the prospects for studying backward Compton scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). We model the cross-section and kinematics using the limited data on backward Compton scattering and backward meson production, and then simulate Compton scattering at EIC energies, in a simple model of the ePIC detector. Generally, the proton is scattered toward mid-rapidity, while the produced photon is in the far-forward region, visible in a Zero Degree Calorimeter (ZDC). We show that the background from backward $π^0$ production can be rejected using a high-resolution, well-segmented ZDC.

## Key claim

The paper models backward (u-channel) virtual Compton scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider and shows that, despite large uncertainties in the cross-section magnitude, the process may be measurable with event rates of order 10^4 to 10^5 per 10 fb^-1 of integrated luminosity, provided that the dominant background from backward π^0 production can be rejected using a high-resolution, well-segmented Zero Degree Calorimeter.

## Method

The authors constructed two phenomenological models of backward VCS using an exponential form dσ/du ∼ exp(-D|u-u0|), with slope parameters D = 2.4 GeV^-2 (Model 1, from Hall C ω electroproduction data) and D = 21.8 GeV^-2 (Model 2, from NINA ω photoproduction data). They anchored the cross-section amplitude to Jefferson Lab Hall A VCS data at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 and cosθ = -0.975, and modeled the W-dependence as (W^2 - m_p^2)^-2 and the Q^2-dependence as (Q^2 + 2.77 GeV^2)^-4. A similar model was constructed for backward π^0 production using SLAC photoproduction data. These models were implemented in a modified version of the eSTARlight Monte Carlo event generator to simulate events at three EIC collision energies (5×41 GeV, 10×100 GeV, and 18×275 GeV) and passed through a simple model of the ePIC detector.

## Key numbers

- Model 1 normalization amplitude A = 32 μb/GeV^2 with 7% uncertainty from the fit to VCS data at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 and cosθ = -0.975
- Model 2 normalization amplitude A = 65 μb/GeV^2 with 7% uncertainty from the same VCS data
- Slope parameter D = 2.4 ± 1.8 GeV^-2 for Model 1 (from ω electroproduction at W = 2.5 GeV, 0.03 < -u < 0.28 GeV^2)
- Slope parameter D = 21.8 ± 1.2 GeV^-2 for Model 2 (from ω photoproduction at W = 3.1 GeV, 0.01 < -u < 0.12 GeV^2)
- Total cross section for Model 1 at 18×275 GeV: 14 pb (integrated over Q^2 = 0-5 GeV^2, W > 2 GeV), yielding 1.4×10^5 events per 10 fb^-1
- Total cross section for Model 2 at 18×275 GeV: 3.1 pb, yielding 3.1×10^4 events per 10 fb^-1
- Total cross section for π^0 production at 18×275 GeV: 89 pb, yielding 8.9×10^5 events per 10 fb^-1
- π^0 model slope parameter D = 4.2 ± 0.4 GeV^-2 and amplitude A = 1.26 ± 0.07 mb/GeV^2 from SLAC photoproduction data
- π^0 model W-scaling exponent α = 2.8 ± 0.1 from SLAC data at photon energies 6-18 GeV
- Geometric acceptance for p' + γ detection at 275 GeV proton beam with B0 EMCal: 81% for Q^2 = 10^-3 to 1 GeV^2, 71% for Q^2 = 1-2 GeV^2, 29% for Q^2 = 2-5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the study of backward (u-channel) reactions to the future Electron-Ion Collider, building on limited existing data from Jefferson Lab (Laveissiere 2009) and Daresbury Laboratory (Clifft 1977) for backward meson production, and from SLAC (Anderson 1969) for backward π^0 photoproduction. The paper addresses the need identified in the EIC White Paper for DVCS measurements up to large momentum transfers, and connects to recent theoretical work by Pire (2021) on baryon-to-photon/meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) that propose a u-channel factorization scheme. The results enable future experimental studies of baryon-number transfer mechanisms and may help constrain parton distributions at small impact parameters, complementing forward DVCS measurements that map the proton at large impact parameters.
