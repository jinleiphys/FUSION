# Valence-quark distribution functions in the kaon and pion

> We describe expressions for pion and kaon dressed-quark distribution functions that incorporate contributions from gluons which bind quarks into these mesons and hence overcome a flaw of the commonly used handbag approximation. The distributions therewith obtained are purely valence in character, ensuring that dressed-quarks carry all a meson's momentum at a characteristic hadronic scale and vanishing as $(1-x)^2$ when Bjorken-$x\to 1$. Comparing such distributions within the pion and kaon, it is apparent that the size of SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking in meson parton distribution functions is modulated by the flavour dependence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Corrections to these leading-order formulae may be divided into two classes, responsible for shifting dressed-quark momentum into glue and sea-quarks. Working with available empirical information, we build an algebraic framework that is capable of expressing the principal impact of both classes of corrections. This enables a realistic comparison with experiment which allows us to identify and highlight basic features of measurable pion and kaon valence-quark distributions. We find that whereas roughly two-thirds of the pion's light-front momentum is carried by valence dressed-quarks at a characteristic hadronic scale, this fraction rises to 95% in the kaon; evolving distributions with these features to a scale typical of available Drell-Yan data produces a kaon-to-pion ratio of u-quark distributions that is in agreement with the single existing data set; and predict a u-quark distribution within the pion that agrees with a modern reappraisal of $πN$ Drell-Yan data. Precise new data are essential in order to validate this reappraisal and because a single modest-quality measurement of the kaon-to-pion ratio cannot be considered definitive.

## Key claim

The paper derives valence-quark distribution functions for the pion and kaon that incorporate gluon binding contributions omitted by the standard handbag approximation, finding that at a characteristic hadronic scale roughly two-thirds of the pion's light-front momentum is carried by valence dressed-quarks, whereas this fraction rises to 95% in the kaon; the authors caution that the single existing kaon-to-pion ratio measurement is of modest quality and cannot be considered definitive.

## Method

The authors compute valence dressed-quark distribution functions using corrected impulse-approximation formulae derived from QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, employing an algebraic framework with dressed-quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes parameterized to reproduce the pion and kaon leptonic decay constants. They then incorporate two classes of corrections: meson-cloud contributions that shift momentum into sea-quarks, and gluonic corrections that shift momentum into the gluon distribution, using empirical constraints from pion-nucleon Drell-Yan data to fix the pion's sea and glue parameters and fitting the kaon's gluon parameter to the existing kaon-to-pion Drell-Yan ratio data.

## Key numbers

- At the hadronic scale ζ_H, roughly two-thirds (≈67%) of the pion's light-front momentum is carried by valence dressed-quarks, while this fraction rises to 95% in the kaon.
- The ratio u_V^K(x)/u_V^π(x) as x→1 is 0.37; the ratio u_V^π(x)/s̄_V^K(x) as x→1 is 0.29.
- The shift in the peak of u_V^K(x) away from x=0.5 is 17%, nearly identical to the 16% shift in the leading-twist s-quark parton distribution amplitude in the kaon.
- With M_u = 0.4 GeV and M_s = 1.2 M_u, the parameters Λ_π = 0.52 M_u and Λ_K = 0.93 M_u yield f_π = 0.092 GeV and f_K = 0.11 GeV, in agreement with experiment.
- The pion's sea-quark distribution at ζ_H = 0.51 GeV is parameterized with ᾱ = 0.16, β̄ = 5.20, γ̄ = 3.243, δ̄ = 5.206; 29% of the pion's momentum is carried by glue at that scale.
- The pion's chiral-limit valence-quark distribution behaves as (1-x)^2 when x→1, consistent with the QCD parton model prediction.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Chang (2014) for the pion to the kaon, providing a systematic treatment of SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking in meson parton distribution functions. The results contradict the leading-order analysis of the E615 experiment (Conway, 1989) which reported u_V^π(x) ∼ (1-x), but are consistent with the next-to-leading-order reanalysis including soft-gluon resummation by Aicher (2010). The predictions enable future tests with tagged deep inelastic scattering at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab 12) and with proposed electron-ion collider measurements, while highlighting the need for precise new Drell-Yan data to validate the kaon-to-pion ratio.
