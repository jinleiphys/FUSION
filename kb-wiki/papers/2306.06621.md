# Continuum-discretized coupled-channel calculations for $^{6}$Li fusion reactions with closed channels

> Fusion reactions induced by the weakly bound nucleus $^{6}$Li with targets $^{28}$Si, $^{64}$Ni, $^{144}$Sm and $^{209}$Bi at energies around the Coulomb barrier are investigated within a three-body model where $^{6}$Li is described with an $α+ d$ cluster model. The total fusion (TF) cross sections are calculated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method and the complete fusion (CF) cross sections are extracted through the sum-rule model. The calculations demonstrate that (i) for the TF cross section calculations, the continuum states up to 40 MeV are found to be necessary, which corresponds to the inclusion of closed channels for light and medium mass targets, such as $^{28}$Si, $^{59}$Co and $^{144}$Sm, (ii) the converged CDCC results for TF cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier are almost the same as single channel results in which the continuum coupling effect is neglected, and (iii) the continuum coupling strongly influences partial wave fusion cross sections and the closed channels play a significant role in the improvement of the description of the CF cross sections at energies below the Coulomb barrier for the $^6$Li+$^{28}$Si, $^{59}$Co and $^{144}$Sm systems.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for light and medium-mass targets (²⁸Si, ⁵⁹Co, ¹⁴⁴Sm), continuum states of ⁶Li up to 40 MeV, well above the threshold energy for closed channels, are necessary for converged total fusion cross sections, contradicting earlier CDCC studies that used maximum continuum energies of only 6–8 MeV. For the heavy target ²⁰⁹Bi, closed-channel coupling effects on total fusion are weak (0.3% difference).

## Method

The authors describe ⁶Li as an α + d cluster and discretize its continuum states using the pseudo-state method with a regularized Lagrange–Laguerre mesh (N=35, h=0.5 fm). Total fusion cross sections are calculated with the CDCC method using short-range Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials (radius factor 0.8 fm, diffuseness 0.1 fm, depths 20–80 MeV) for α-target and d-target interactions, with real parts from the São Paulo potential version 2. Complete fusion cross sections are extracted via the sum-rule model, which separates CF and ICF by a cut-off angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The maximum continuum energy ε_max = 40 MeV is sufficient for convergence; changes in total fusion cross sections are less than 2% when the imaginary potential depth varies from 20 to 80 MeV.
- For ⁶Li+²⁰⁹Bi at E_cm = V_B = 30.40 MeV, σ_TF calculated with ε_max = ε_th differs from that with ε_max = 40.0 MeV by only 0.3% (W₀ = 50 MeV).
- Coulomb barriers: V_B = 6.87 MeV (²⁸Si), 12.41 MeV (⁶⁴Ni), 25.15 MeV (¹⁴⁴Sm), 30.40 MeV (²⁰⁹Bi).
- The ⁶Li binding energy for α + d breakup is 1.47 MeV.
- Calculated ⁶Li root-mean-square matter and charge radii are both 2.54 fm, agreeing with the experimental value 2.54 ± 0.03 fm (Tanihata 1985).
- Resonance energies (calculated vs. experimental): 3⁺ state 0.710 MeV (exp. 0.716 MeV), 2⁺ state 3.00 MeV (exp. 2.84 MeV), 1⁺ state 4.24 MeV (exp. 4.18 MeV).

## Context

This work extends CDCC studies of ⁶Li fusion by Diaz-Torres (2003), Lubian (2022), and others, who used ε_max of only 6–8 MeV and neglected imaginary parts of off-diagonal couplings. It follows the authors' prior CDCC analysis for ⁶Li+⁵⁹Co (Chen 2022), which found ε_max should be at least 50 MeV for converged elastic breakup cross sections. The results enable improved calculations of complete fusion for ⁶Li with light and medium targets, where previous theoretical studies were rare compared to heavy-target systems.
