# Universal Effective Charges in the $sd$ and $fp$ Shells

> The 247-keV state in $^{54}$Sc, populated in the $β$ decay of $^{54}$Ca, is reported here as a nanosecond isomer with a half-life of 26.0(22) ns. The state is interpreted as the $1^+$ member of the $πf_{7/2}\otimesνf_{5/2}$ spin-coupled multiplet, which decays to the $3^+,πf_{7/2} \otimes νp_{1/2}$ ground state. The new half-life corresponds to a pure $E2$ transition with a strength of 1.93(16) W.u., providing the most precise, unambiguous $B(E2)$ value in the neutron-rich $fp$ region to date for a nucleus with valence protons above $Z=20$. Notably, it is roughly four times larger than the $B(E2; 1/2^{-} \rightarrow 5/2^{-})$ value in $^{55}$Ca. The results, as compared to semi-empirical and ab initio shell-model calculations, indicate (1) a weak $N=34$ sub-shell gap relative to $N = 32$, (2) a large $E2$ enhancement in Sc as compared to Ca due to $1p-1h$ proton excitations across $Z=28$, and (3) empirical effective proton and neutron charges, $e_π$ = 1.30(8)$e$ and $e_ν$ = 0.452(7)$e$, respectively, that are in contrast to reports of $e_π\approx 1.1-1.15e$ and $e_ν\approx 0.6-0.8e$ for $fp$-shell nuclei near $N = Z$. We demonstrate that these reports are erroneous and that, in fact, a universal set of effective charges can be used across the $sd$ and $fp$ shells.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a universal set of effective proton and neutron charges, e_π = 1.33e and e_ν = 0.45e, can be used consistently across both the sd and fp nuclear shells, contradicting previous claims of isospin or orbital dependence that had led to reported values of e_π ≈ 1.1-1.15e and e_ν ≈ 0.6-0.8e for fp-shell nuclei near N = Z.

## Method

The 247-keV state in 54Sc was populated via β decay of 54Ca at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB) using the FRIB Decay Station initiator (FDSi), with γ-rays detected by LaBr₃ scintillators and HPGe detectors. The half-life of the state was determined from a maximum-likelihood fit to the β-γ time-difference distribution, and the resulting B(E2) value was compared to semi-empirical (KB3G, GXPF1A, UFP-CA) and ab initio (SM*, VS-IMSRG) shell-model calculations to extract effective charges and evaluate the N=34 sub-shell gap.

## Key numbers

- The 247-keV state in 54Sc has a half-life of 26.0(22) ns, corresponding to a pure E2 transition strength of 1.93(16) W.u.
- The empirical effective charges extracted are e_π = 1.30(8)e and e_ν = 0.452(7)e, with a ratio e_π/e_ν = 2.88(18)
- The B(E2; 1/2⁻ → 5/2⁻) value in 55Ca is 0.42(18) W.u., roughly four times smaller than the 54Sc value
- The B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) in 50Ca is 0.68(2) W.u., and the B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) in 54Ti is 6.0(12) W.u.
- The microsecond isomer in 54Sc has a weighted average half-life of 2.75(3) μs
- The reduced chi-squared for E2 data improves from 18.1 (with standard charges e_π=1.5e, e_ν=0.5e) to 1.1 (with the new fitted charges)

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron-rich fp-shell nuclei near the N=32 and N=34 sub-shell closures, building on prior measurements of B(E2) values in Ca isotopes by Valiente-Dobon (2009) and Koiwai (2022), and quadrupole moments of 49,51Ca by Garcia Ruiz (2015). The results contradict earlier claims of orbital-dependent effective charges in the fp shell (du Rietz, 2004; Allmond, 2014) and instead demonstrate equivalence with the universal sd-shell effective charges established by Richter (2008) and the microscopic derivations of Dufour and Zuker (1996), enabling more consistent electric-quadrupole calculations across both shells.
