# Structure of the exotic nucleus $^{14}$B in the ground state

> We investigate the structure of the neutron rich nucleus $^{14}$B through studies of its breakup in the Coulomb field of a heavy target. The breakup amplitude is calculated within an adiabatic as well as finite range DWBA theories of breakup reactions. Both these formalisms allow the use of realistic wave functions for the relative motion between the fragments in the ground state of the projectile. The longitudinal momentum distributions of $^{13}$B (ground state) following the breakup of $^{14}$B on a heavy target at beam energy of 60 MeV/nucleon, calculated using two possible ground state configurations of $^{14}$B, have been compared with the recent data. The data seem to favour $^{13}$B(${3\over 2}^-)\otimes2s_{1/2}$ as the possible ground state configuration of $^{14}$B with a spectroscopic factor close to unity. We give our predictions for the neutron angular distributions and the relative energy spectra in the one-neutron removal reaction of $^{14}$B.

## Key claim

The data favor a ground state configuration of 14B as 13B(3/2−) ⊗ 2s1/2 with a spectroscopic factor close to unity, but the authors note that the theoretical cross section is less than the experimental one, and the small discrepancy could be attributed to nuclear breakup contributions present in the kinematical domain covered by the data.

## Method

The authors performed Coulomb breakup calculations for 14B on a 197Au target at 60 MeV/nucleon beam energy using two quantum mechanical formalisms: the adiabatic (AD) model and the finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA). They compared calculated parallel momentum distributions of the 13B core in its ground state with experimental data from Guimaraes et al. (2000), using a single-particle potential model with Woods-Saxon parameters and considering pure 2s1/2 and pure 1d5/2 valence neutron configurations, as well as a coherent superposition weighted by spectroscopic factors from Warburton and Brown (1992).

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of 14B: 0.97 MeV
- Measured width of parallel momentum distribution of 13B core: 59 ± 3 MeV/c
- Calculated width for pure s-state configuration: about 95% of the measured width (approximately 56 MeV/c)
- Pure s-state configuration gives about 80% of the total one-neutron removal cross section: 510.4 mb
- Experimental total one-neutron removal cross section: 638 ± 45 mb
- rms size of 14B with 2s1/2 configuration: 2.79 fm; with 1d5/2 configuration: 2.57 fm
- rms size of valence neutron with 2s1/2 configuration: 5.46 fm; with 1d5/2 configuration: 3.43 fm
- Grazing angle for the reaction on Au target: 4.4 degrees
- Spectroscopic factors from shell model calculations: 0.306 for 1d5/2 and 0.662 for 2s1/2 (Warburton and Brown, 1992)

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron halo nuclei to the odd-odd nucleus 14B, which is the lowest mass bound system among the N=9 isotones. The authors compare their calculations with experimental data from Guimaraes et al. (2000) at MSU and with earlier measurements on a carbon target at GANIL by Sauvan et al. (2000) that gave a narrow width of 56.5 ± 0.5 MeV/c. The results support the existence of a one-neutron halo structure in 14B, similar to the well-established halo nuclei 11Be and 19C, and the authors provide predictions for neutron angular distributions and relative energy spectra to guide future measurements at extreme forward angles below the grazing angle.
