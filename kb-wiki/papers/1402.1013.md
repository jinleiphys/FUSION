# Beyond Rainbow-Ladder in a covariant three-body Bethe-Salpeter approach: Baryons

> We report on recent results of a calculation of the nucleon and delta masses in a covariant bound-state approach, where to the simple rainbow-ladder gluon-exchange interaction kernel we add a pion-exchange contribution to account for pion cloud effects. We observe good agreement with lattice data at large pion masses. At the physical point our masses are too large by about five percent, signaling the need for more structure in the gluon part of the interaction.

## Key claim

The authors find that adding a pion-exchange contribution to the rainbow-ladder kernel improves agreement with lattice data for nucleon and delta masses at large pion masses, but at the physical point the calculated masses remain too large by about five percent, indicating that further structure in the gluon part of the interaction is still needed.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant three-body Bethe-Salpeter (Faddeev) equation for nucleon and delta baryons, using quark propagators obtained from their Dyson-Schwinger equation. They extend the standard rainbow-ladder truncation by including a pion-exchange contribution to the two-body interaction kernel, where the pion couples to quarks via a dressed gamma-5 vertex derived from the leading component of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The interaction parameter Lambda is set to 0.74 GeV for the pure rainbow-ladder case (RL1) and 0.84 GeV when pion exchange is included (RL2).
- The current-quark mass is m_{u/d}(19 GeV) = 3.7 MeV for both cases.
- The interaction parameter eta is varied between 1.6 and 2.0, with eta=1.6 corresponding to the upper limit of the mass bands shown in the results.
- At the physical point, the calculated nucleon and delta masses are too large by about five percent.

## Context

This work extends the standard rainbow-ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger equations, which has been extensively used for ground-state meson and baryon calculations (Eichmann 2013, Bashir 2012). The approach parametrizes unquenching effects by including hadronic degrees of freedom, following earlier work on pion-exchange contributions (Fischer 2007, Fischer 2008). The results are compared with lattice data from Ali Khan (2003) through Gattringer (2008), and the authors note that the curvature of M(m_pi^2) below the lowest lattice points is too small, suggesting that further structure in the quark-gluon interaction beyond the present scheme is needed.
