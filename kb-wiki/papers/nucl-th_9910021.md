# Solar proton burning, neutrino disintegration of the deuteron and pep process in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

> The astrophysical factor S_pp(0) for the solar proton burning, p + p -> D + positron + neutrino, is recalculated in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD). We obtain S_pp(0) = 4.08 x 10^{-25} MeV b which agrees good with the recommended value S_pp(0) = 4.00 x 10^{-25} MeV b. The amplitude of low-energy elastic proton-proton (pp) scattering in the singlet S-wave state with the Coulomb repulsion contributing to the amplitude of the solar proton burning is described in terms of the S-wave scattering length and the effective range. This takes away the problem pointed out by Bahcall and Kamionkowski (Nucl. Phys. A625 (1997) 893) that in the RFMD one cannot describe low-energy elastic pp scattering with the Coulomb repulsion in agreement with low-energy nuclear phenomenology. The cross section for the neutrino disintegration of the deuteron, neutrino + D -> electron + p + p, is calculated with respect to S_pp(0) for neutrino energies from threshold to 10 MeV. The results can be used for the analysis of the data which will be obtained in the experiments planned by SNO. The astrophysical factor S_pep(0) for the pep process, p + electron + p -> neutrino + D, is calculated relative to S_pp(0) in complete agreement with the result obtained by Bahcall and May (ApJ. 155 (1969) 501).

## Key claim

The authors recalculate the astrophysical factor S_pp(0) for solar proton burning in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) and obtain a value of 4.08 x 10^{-25} MeV b, which agrees with the recommended value of 4.00 x 10^{-25} MeV b, but does not confirm the enhancement by a factor of 1.4 reported in an earlier modified version of the RFMD.

## Method

The authors apply the RFMD, which describes the deuteron as a neutron-proton Cooper pair induced by a local four-nucleon interaction, and evaluate the amplitude of the pp fusion reaction by summing an infinite series of one-nucleon loop diagrams in leading order of the large N_C expansion. The contribution of low-energy elastic pp scattering with Coulomb repulsion is incorporated using the S-wave scattering length and effective range from experimental data, and the overlap factor from Coulomb and strong interactions inside one-nucleon loop diagrams is evaluated perturbatively. The cross section for neutrino disintegration of the deuteron is then expressed in terms of S_pp(0), and the astrophysical factor for the pep process is calculated relative to S_pp(0).

## Key numbers

- S_pp(0) = 4.08 x 10^{-25} MeV b for the solar proton burning reaction p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e, compared to the recommended value of 4.00 x 10^{-25} MeV b
- The effective coupling constant G_piNN = 3.27 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-2}, derived from the pion-nucleon coupling constant g_piNN = 13.4, pion mass M_pi = 135 MeV, and np scattering length a_np = (-23.75 +/- 0.01) fm
- The S-wave scattering length for pp scattering with Coulomb repulsion a^e_pp = (-7.8196 +/- 0.0026) fm and effective range r^e_pp = 2.790 +/- 0.014 fm
- The overlap factor |F^e_pp|^2 = 1 + (4.25 x 10^{-3}) approximately 1, showing the Coulomb contribution inside one-nucleon loop diagrams is small
- The function f(xi) = 0.222 at T_pp = 0, where xi = m_e/(W + T_pp) and W = 0.932 MeV

## Context

This work extends the RFMD approach previously developed by the same authors (Ivanov et al., 1997-1999) and addresses a problem raised by Bahcall and Kamionkowski (1997) that the RFMD could not describe low-energy elastic pp scattering with Coulomb repulsion in agreement with nuclear phenomenology. The results for the neutrino disintegration of the deuteron can be used for analysis of data from experiments planned by the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO). The astrophysical factor for the pep process is calculated in complete agreement with the result obtained by Bahcall and May (1969).
