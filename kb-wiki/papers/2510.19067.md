# Connecting ground-state properties of ${}^6$Li to each other and to scattering data

> We examine the relationship between the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) of $^6$Li and other low-energy observables in the $α$-deuteron system. Our analysis uses a set of calculations carried out within the {\it ab initio} No Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) using a variety of inter-nucleon interactions and basis sizes, and yielding ${}^6$Li deuteron separation energies between 1.3 and 1.8 MeV [Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 042503 (2022)]. These NCSMC calculations show that the square of the ANC is strongly correlated with the separation energy over this range. In this work, we investigate the origin of this correlation using the phenomenological $R$-matrix, a single-channel potential and a perturbative approach. We show that this correlation occurs because the depth of the $α$-deuteron central potential changes by only a small relative amount as the separation energy varies. We then investigate if the ANC can be accurately extracted from $α$-deuteron phase shifts in an ideal case in which low-energy data are available and there are no experimental errors. We find that both $R$-matrix and Coulomb-modified effective-range theory (CM-ERE) yield extracted ANCs close to, although not exactly equal to, the true value, provided the extrapolation is constrained by the known position of the bound-state pole and at least three terms are included in the fit function. The $R$-matrix approach converges faster than the CM-ERE as the number of parameters increases and is also more robust against the inclusion of low-energy and high-energy phase shift data. Finally, our study also shows that a naive quantification of uncertainties by comparing different truncations used in both theories is not accurate, and suggests the accuracy of ANCs extracted from phase shift data needs further investigation.

## Key claim
The strong correlation between the $^6$Li ANC$^2$ and its deuteron separation energy arises because the depth of the $\alpha$-deuteron central potential changes by only a small relative amount as the separation energy varies, and this correlation is not a manifestation of leading-order Halo EFT universality in $^6$Li.

## Method
The authors analyzed twelve NCSMC calculations of $^6$Li from Hebborn et al. (2022) that used various chiral EFT interactions and basis sizes, yielding separation energies between 1.39 and 1.86 MeV. They investigated the ANC-separation energy correlation using phenomenological $R$-matrix theory, a single-channel Woods-Saxon potential model, and a perturbative approach. They then tested the accuracy of ANC extraction from $\alpha$-deuteron phase shifts by fitting $R$-matrix and Coulomb-modified effective-range theory (CM-ERE) amplitudes to synthetic NCSMC phase shift data between 0.01 MeV and 3 MeV, using the known bound-state pole position as a constraint.

## Key numbers
- The twelve NCSMC calculations produced $^6$Li deuteron separation energies ranging from 1.39 MeV to 1.86 MeV.
- The reduced width $\gamma^2$ from six selected NCSMC calculations varied by only 13% across different binding energies.
- In the square well toy model, the potential depth changed only from approximately 93.5 MeV to 95 MeV to shift the bound state from -1.9 MeV to -1.3 MeV, while $\gamma^2$ changed from 7.35 to 7.30.
- The Woods-Saxon potential used a fixed diffuseness $a_R = 0.65$ fm and radii $r_R$ of 1.0, 1.2, and 1.4 fm to reproduce the correlation.
- The asymptotic ratio $\mathcal{R}_{\text{as}}(\kappa)$ became independent of radius $\tilde{R}$ for $\tilde{R} \geq 4$ fm in all NCSMC calculations.
- Leading-order Halo EFT predicted ANC$^2$ values a factor of ten smaller than the NCSMC predictions for the relevant $\kappa$ and $\eta_b$ values.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Hebborn et al. (2022) which found a precise NCSMC ANC$^2$ value of $6.86 \pm 0.21$ fm$^{-1}$ for $^6$Li, discrepant with the accepted value $5.3 \pm 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$ from phase-shift analysis by PhysRevC.43.822. The correlation between binding energy and ANC has been observed in three-nucleon systems by PhysRevC.37.2859 and explained by pionless EFT, but the authors show this $^6$Li correlation is not a leading-order universal effect. The study enables improved understanding of how ANCs extracted from phase shift data should be interpreted, with implications for reevaluating $\alpha$-induced reaction rates such as $^{13}$C$(\alpha,n)^{16}$O and $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O that rely on the $^6$Li ANC.
