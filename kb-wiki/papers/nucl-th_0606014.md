# Deuteron photodisintegration with polarized photons at astrophysical energies

> Following precise experimental studies at the Duke Free-Electron Laser Laboratory, we discuss photodisintegration of deuterons with 100% linearly polarized photons using a model independent theoretical approach taking together $M1$ and $E1$ amplitudes simultaneously. The isoscalar $M1_s$ contribution is also taken exactly into account. From the existing experimental measurement on doubly polarized thermal neutron capture, it is seen that the isoscalar $M1_s$ contribution could be of the same order of magnitude as the experimentally measured cross sections at energies relevant to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN). Therefore appropriate measurements on deuteron photodisintegration are suggested to empirically determine the $M1_s$ contribution at astrophysical energies.

## Key claim

The isoscalar M1_s contribution to deuteron photodisintegration, traditionally neglected at astrophysical energies, could be of the same order of magnitude as the experimentally measured cross sections at energies relevant to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), based on an estimate from thermal neutron capture data. The paper argues that this contribution should be empirically determined through measurements with linearly polarized photons, rather than assumed negligible.

## Method

The authors develop a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism for the reaction d(γ,n)p with 100% linearly polarized photons, considering only dipole transitions (M1 and E1) with l=0,1 partial waves in the final state. They derive explicit expressions for the differential cross section and beam analyzing power in terms of the isovector M1_v, isoscalar M1_s, isovector E1_v (three amplitudes for final ³P_j states), and isoscalar E1_s amplitudes. The formalism shows that a term proportional to cosθ in the differential cross section arises specifically from interference between M1_s and E1_v amplitudes, providing a direct experimental signature for the isoscalar contribution.

## Key numbers

- The thermal neutron capture cross section is 334.2 ± 0.5 mb (Cox, Wynchank and Collie).
- The ratio R of triplet to singlet capture cross sections at thermal energies is estimated as 1.202 × 10⁻³, giving a ³S₁ contribution of 401.7 μb to the thermal cross section.
- The photodisintegration cross section at 2.62 MeV is 1.30 ± 0.029 mb, increasing to 2.430 ± 0.17 mb at 4.45 MeV.
- The measured circular polarization P_γ from polarized neutron capture is -(1.5 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³, compared to a theoretical prediction of order -1.1 × 10⁻³ with 25% estimated accuracy.
- The measured γ anisotropy η from polarized thermal neutron capture is (1.0 ± 2.5) × 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework used by Schreiber et al. (2000) and Tornow et al. (2002) for analyzing deuteron photodisintegration with polarized photons at the Duke Free-Electron Laser Laboratory, but removes their simplifying assumptions that set the isoscalar M1_s amplitude to zero. The paper builds on the influential BBN uncertainty analysis by Burles, Nollett, Truran and Turner (1999), and addresses the 15% discrepancy between theory and experiment noted by Nagai et al. (1995) for the d(γ,n)p cross section at neutron energies of 550 and 1080 keV. The formalism enables empirical determination of the energy-dependent ratio of isoscalar to isovector M1 contributions, which could sharpen BBN predictions.
