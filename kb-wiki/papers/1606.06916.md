# Charged current quasi elastic scattering of muon neutrino with nuclei

> We present a study on the charge current quasi elastic scattering of $ν_μ$ from nucleon and nuclei which gives a charged muon in the final state. To describe nuclei, the Fermi Gas model has been used with proposed Pauli suppression factor. The diffuseness parameter of the Fermi distribution has been obtained using experimental data. We also investigate different parametrizations for electric and magnetic Sach's form factors of nucleons. Calculations have been made for CCQES total and differential cross sections for the cases of $ν_μ-N$, $ν_μ-^{12}C$ and $ν_μ-^{56}Fe$ scatterings and are compared with the data for different values of the axial mass. The present model gives excellent description of measured differential cross section for all the systems.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Fermi Gas model with a proposed Pauli suppression factor (including a diffuseness parameter) provides an "excellent description" of measured differential cross sections for muon-neutrino charged-current quasi-elastic scattering on carbon and iron, but only for certain values of the axial mass \(M_A\); the MiniBooNE data require a larger value (\(M_A = 1.23\) GeV) than most other experiments, which are well described by \(M_A\) between 1.05 and 1.12 GeV.

## Method

The authors compute neutrino-nucleon charged-current quasi-elastic scattering cross sections using the Llewellyn Smith model with a dipole axial form factor and Galster (or Alberico) parametrizations for the nucleon electric and magnetic Sachs form factors. For nuclear targets (carbon-12 and iron-56), they embed the free-nucleon cross section in a Fermi Gas model with a smooth Fermi distribution (diffuseness parameter \(a = 0.020\) GeV) and a Pauli suppression factor that depends on the energy transfer and a binding energy of 10 MeV. They then compare their calculated differential and total cross sections to experimental data from MiniBooNE, Gargamelle, MINERvA, NOMAD, and other collaborations for various values of the axial mass \(M_A\).

## Key numbers

*   The Fermi momentum for carbon is taken as \(k_F = 0.221\) GeV; for iron, \(k_F = 0.260\) GeV.
*   The binding energy \(E_B\) is set to 10 MeV for both carbon and iron.
*   The diffuseness parameter \(a\) in the Fermi distribution is 0.020 GeV.
*   The axial vector constant \(g_A\) is -1.267.
*   The dipole vector mass squared \(M_v^2\) is 0.71 GeV\(^2\).
*   The Fermi coupling constant \(G_F\) is \(1.16 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and the Cabibbo angle cosine is 0.97425.
*   Nuclear effects reduce the total cross section by 10% even at neutrino energies above 1 GeV.
*   The calculations with \(M_A = 1.05\) and 1.12 GeV give good description of most experimental data; MiniBooNE data require \(M_A = 1.23\) GeV for an excellent fit.

## Context

This work extends the standard Llewellyn Smith (1971) and Fermi Gas Model (Smith, 1972) approach for charged-current quasi-elastic scattering by introducing a smooth Pauli suppression factor with a diffuseness parameter, fitted to experimental data. It compares its results to global analyses (Kuzmin, 2007) that find \(M_A = 0.977 \pm 0.016\) GeV and to the NOMAD (Lyubushkin, 2008) analysis yielding \(M_A = 1.05 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.06\) GeV, as well as to the MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010) results that require a larger \(M_A\) (1.35 GeV). The model does not include two-nucleon (2p2h) contributions, which are considered in more sophisticated approaches such as the superscaling (SuSA) model (Megias, 2016) and relativistic mean-field theory. The results enable future neutrino oscillation experiments to use a simple, computationally efficient nuclear model with a well-constrained axial mass range for carbon and iron targets.
