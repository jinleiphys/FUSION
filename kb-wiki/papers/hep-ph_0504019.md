# The High-Temperature Phase of Landau-Gauge Yang-Mills Theory

> The properties of the high-temperature phase of Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge are investigated by extending an earlier study on the infinite-temperature limit to finite temperatures. To this end the Dyson-Schwinger equations for the propagators of the gluon and the Faddeev-Popov ghost are solved analytically in the infrared and numerically at non-vanishing momenta. Gluons, polarized transversely with respect to the heat bath are found to comply with the Gribov-Zwanziger and the Kugo-Ojima scenario, while longitudinally polarized gluons are screened. Therefore the high-temperature phase is strongly interacting. It is furthermore conjectured that Yang-Mills theory undergoes a first-order phase transition. Indications are found that at high temperatures the thermodynamic properties are nearly those of an ideal gas, although long-range interactions prevail.

## Key claim

The high-temperature phase of Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory is strongly interacting, with transversely polarized gluons remaining confined according to the Gribov-Zwanziger and Kugo-Ojima scenarios, while longitudinally polarized gluons become screened; the authors conjecture a first-order phase transition, but note that thermodynamic properties are nearly those of an ideal gas despite long-range interactions persisting.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the gluon and Faddeev-Popov ghost propagators in Landau gauge at finite temperature, extending a prior infinite-temperature study. They performed an analytical infrared analysis using power-law ansatze and numerical solutions at non-vanishing momenta, employing a truncated Matsubara sum with up to 13 frequencies, a bare ghost-gluon vertex, and a modified soft three-gluon vertex. Renormalization was implemented via a momentum subtraction scheme with independent counter-terms for each Matsubara mode.

## Key numbers

- The infrared exponent for the ghost dressing function is \(\kappa = 0.39760\) at \(\zeta = 1\) (the preferred solution), with an alternative solution \(\kappa = 1/2\).
- The longitudinal gluon dressing function \(H(p)\) behaves mass-like with exponent \(l = 1\).
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) is implicitly referenced via the first non-zero Matsubara frequency \(p_0 = 2\pi T_c \approx 1.7\) GeV.
- Numerical solutions were obtained at \(T = 1.5\) GeV with \(N = 12\) Matsubara frequencies, showing hard-mode dressing functions deviate from unity by up to about 30%.
- The renormalized mass for the soft longitudinal mode is set to \(m_{3d}\), given by \(m_{3d}^2 = r^2 g^4 T^2 + g^2 T C_A \frac{r g^2 T}{4\pi}\), with \(r = m_h / g_3^2\) taken from prior work (Maas 2004).

## Context

This work extends the infinite-temperature study of Maas (2004) to finite temperatures, complementing studies below the phase transition by Gruter (2004). It confirms that the Gribov-Zwanziger and Kugo-Ojima confinement scenarios persist for transverse gluons above \(T_c\), while longitudinal gluons acquire a Debye screening mass. The results enable further investigation of the phase transition order and the equation of state, suggesting that macroscopic ideal-gas behavior emerges from a microscopically nonperturbative theory.
