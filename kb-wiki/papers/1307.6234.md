# Equilibrium and equilibration in a gluon plasma with improved matrix elements

> The hot and dense matter created in the early stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision is composed mainly of gluons. Radiative processes can play an important role for the thermalization of such partonic systems. The simplest parton number changing processes are commonly described by the Gunion-Bertsch formula. We show that the cross section from the exact matrix element for the lowest order radiative process could be significantly smaller than that based on the Gunion-Bertsch formula. In light of this, we discuss the role of radiative processes on the equilibrium and equilibration of a gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The exact matrix element for the lowest-order gluon radiative process (2-to-3) yields a cross section significantly smaller than that predicted by the commonly used Gunion-Bertsch formula, which implies that chemical equilibration in a gluon plasma may be slower than previously expected.

## Method

The authors computed the exact matrix element modulus squared for the process gg → ggg using inner-products of particle four-momenta, regulated propagator singularities with a Debye screening mass, and calculated cross sections for a typical gluon plasma at fixed temperature and coupling. They compared these results to the Gunion-Bertsch formula across phase space using a dimensionless differential cross section quantity f-bar, and implemented the exact matrix element into a radiative transport model to study equilibrium cross sections and rates.

## Key numbers

- For α_s = 0.47, μ² = 10 fm⁻², and s = 4 GeV²: σ₂₂ = 0.312 fm², σ₂₃ = 0.0523 fm², ratio σ₂₃/σ₂₂ = 16.8%
- For α_s = 0.3, μ² = 6.38 fm⁻²: σ₂₂ = 0.199 fm², σ₂₃ = 0.0504 fm², ratio much smaller than 50%
- In equilibrium at T = 0.524 GeV and α_s = 0.4: ⟨σ₂₃⟩₂₃/⟨σ₂₂⟩₂₂ ∼ 57% (biased ratio), but ⟨σ₂₃⟩/⟨σ₂₂⟩ ∼ 17% (unbiased ratio)
- Ratio of rates per unit volume w₂₃/w₂₂ ∼ 12%

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of parton number changing processes in gluon plasmas (Xu 2004, Chen 2009, Zhang 2010, Fochler 2013) by replacing the widely used Gunion-Bertsch formula (Gunion 1981, Wang 1994) with the exact matrix element for the lowest-order radiative process. The results contradict the expectation from the Gunion-Bertsch formula that radiative cross sections are about 50% of elastic cross sections, and cannot be accounted for by the recently proposed correction factor of Fochler (2013). This enables more accurate simulations of chemical equilibration, kinetic equilibration, and shear viscosity in the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
