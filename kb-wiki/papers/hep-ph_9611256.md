# On the calculation of hadron form factors from Euclidean Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We apply Euclidean time methods to phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger models of hadrons. By performing a Fourier transform of the momentum space correlation function to Euclidean time and by taking the large Euclidean time limit, we project onto the lightest on-mass-shell hadron for given quantum numbers. The procedure, which actually resembles lattice gauge theory methods, allows the extraction of moments of structure functions, moments of light-cone wave functions and form factors without `ad hoc' extrapolations to the on-mass-shell points. We demonstrate the practicality of the procedure with the example of the pion form factor.

# On the calculation of hadron form factors from Euclidean Dyson-Schwinger equations

## Key claim

Euclidean time projection methods, analogous to those used in lattice gauge theory, can be applied to phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger equation models to extract on-mass-shell hadron form factors without ad hoc extrapolations into the complex plane, provided that no additional poles lie in the immediate vicinity of the ground-state pion pole of interest.

## Method

The authors Fourier transform the Euclidean momentum-space three-point correlation function (the inhomogeneous pseudoscalar-vector vertex) to Euclidean time, then take the large Euclidean time limit to project onto the lightest on-mass-shell hadron for given quantum numbers. They demonstrate the procedure using a toy model with a constituent quark mass, where the quark propagator is taken as a free fermion propagator, the pseudoscalar vertex as iγ5/P², and the vector vertex as -iγμ, allowing direct comparison with the exact result obtained by fixing the mass-shell condition explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass of 300 MeV used in the toy model, with a cutoff of 1 GeV applied on the relative momentum at the quark-photon vertex to regulate the integration.
- Fourier transform performed with 4096 equally-spaced points at a grid spacing of 0.02 GeV for the 300 MeV quark mass case.
- Time separations Δt ≈ 2 fm used for the extracted form factor comparison.
- For the 1 GeV constituent quark mass test, 1024 Fourier transform points at a grid spacing of 0.03 GeV were used.
- External momenta constrained such that P² = K² = 0 in the toy model demonstration.

## Context

This work extends Euclidean time projection methods previously applied to two-point functions by Hollenberg, Roberts and McKellar (1992) to three-point functions for form factor calculations. It provides an alternative to earlier Dyson-Schwinger form factor calculations by Roberts (1996), Frank, Mitchell, Roberts and Tandy (1995), and Burden, Roberts and Thomson (1996) that required entire-function fits and extrapolations into the complex plane. The method enables calculation of elastic form factors, moments of parton distribution functions, and transition form factors using Euclidean Dyson-Schwinger equations without ad hoc analytic continuation, though it is practically limited to the lightest hadrons for given quantum numbers and requires increasing computational resources at large momentum transfers.
