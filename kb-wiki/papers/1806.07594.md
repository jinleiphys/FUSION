# Shear viscosity of ultrarelativistic Boson systems in the presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate

> We calculate for the first time the shear viscosity of ultrarelativistic Boson systems in the presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC). Two different methods are used. One is the Grad's method of moments and another is the Green-Kubo relation within a kinetic transport approach. In this work we consider a Boson system with isotropic elastic collisions and a gluon system with elastic scatterings described by perturbation QCD (pQCD). The results show that the presence of a BEC lowers the shear viscosity. This effect becomes stronger for the increasing proportion of the BEC in the Boson system and is insensitive to the detail of interactions.

## Key claim

The presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) lowers the shear viscosity of ultrarelativistic Boson systems, and this effect becomes stronger with increasing BEC proportion but is insensitive to the details of interactions. The counterbalance between infinite collision rates and zero collision angles in processes involving the BEC yields a finite and nonzero contribution to the shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors calculated the shear viscosity using two independent methods: Grad's method of moments (second-order kinetic theory) and the Green-Kubo relation within the Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) kinetic transport framework. They considered a massless Boson system with isotropic elastic collisions and constant cross sections, and also a gluon system with elastic scatterings described by perturbative QCD (pQCD) matrix elements using Hard-Thermal-Loop treatment. For the Green-Kubo calculations, fluctuations in a static box at thermal equilibrium were simulated with test particles, and the shear viscosity was extracted from the correlation function of the shear tensor.

## Key numbers

- For a massless Boson system without BEC and isotropic collisions, the shear viscosity coefficient is η = k T/σ₂₂ with k = 1.05 (compared to k = 1.2 for Boltzmann statistics neglecting Bose factors).
- For a gluon system at T = 0.443 GeV with 37% BEC proportion (from initial f₀ = 1.0 and Q_s = 1 GeV), η/s = 0.438 with BEC and η/s = 0.531 without BEC, giving a ratio of 0.825.
- For isotropic scatterings with 37% BEC proportion, the ratio η(37% BEC)/η(No BEC) = 0.846.
- The shear viscosity for isotropic collisions is given by η = k T/σ₂₂ where k = (48ζ[5]²/π⁴) × [8ζ[5]/45 − 8π²ζ[3]/63 + 1.827 + (4n_c/(5π²T³))(12ζ[3]² − 1.504)]⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of gluon BEC formation in the glasma phase of heavy-ion collisions (Blaizot 2011, Blaizot 2013) and provides the first calculation of shear viscosity in the presence of a BEC. The results are relevant for understanding the small η/s ratio of the quark-gluon plasma extracted from flow measurements at RHIC and LHC (Schenke 2011). The analytical formula for isotropic scatterings can be used to estimate BEC effects on gluon shear viscosity, and the BAMPS framework can be extended to include number-changing processes and massive particles in future work.
