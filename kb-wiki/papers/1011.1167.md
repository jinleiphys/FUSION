# The Sphaleron Rate in SU(N) Gauge Theory

> The sphaleron rate is defined as the diffusion constant for topological number NCS = int g^2 F Fdual/32 pi^2. It establishes the rate of equilibration of axial light quark number in QCD and is of interest both in electroweak baryogenesis and possibly in heavy ion collisions. We calculate the weak-coupling behavior of the SU(3) sphaleron rate, as well as making the most sensible extrapolation towards intermediate coupling which we can. We also study the behavior of the sphaleron rate at weak coupling at large Nc.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the weak-coupling behavior of the SU(3) sphaleron rate and its large-Nc scaling, but note that the formal range of validity is very narrow: not only the coupling αs, but even the inverse log of the coupling 1/ln(1/αs) must be small, and at larger coupling their extrapolation amounts to a crude estimate.

## Method

The authors implement Bodeker's effective theory, in which the infrared gauge dynamics are described by a Langevin equation with a constant color conductivity σ, derived from hard thermal loops. They discretize this Langevin dynamics on a lattice using short Hamiltonian trajectories with randomly drawn electric fields, measure Chern-Simons number evolution, and extrapolate to zero lattice spacing, infinite volume, and zero refresh time to obtain the continuum sphaleron rate in the effective theory.

## Key numbers

- SU(3) sphaleron rate: Γ_sphal = (132 ± 4) (g²T²/mD²) (ln(mD/γ) + 3.0410) αs⁵ T⁴, where mD² = (6+nf)/6 g²T² and γ = (nc g²T/4π) (ln(mD/γ) + 3.041)
- Large-Nc scaling: Γ_sphal = (0.21 ± 0.01) (nc g²T/mD²) (ln(mD/γ) + 3.0410) ((nc²-1)/nc²) (nc α)⁵ T⁴, with no further Nc corrections visible within error bars for Nc = 2,3,5,8
- Volume dependence saturates at L = 15/(nc g²T); simulations use L ≥ 18/(nc g²T)
- Lattice spacing a < 1/(nc g²T) gives O(1%) unknown corrections to effective lattice spacing, but the scaling involves a⁵ so a 1% error in a gives a 5% error in the diffusion rate

## Context

This work extends the understanding of topology change from the SU(2) electroweak sector (studied by Arnold, Son, Yaffe (1997); Bodeker (1998); Moore (2000)) to SU(3) for QCD, where only two parametric estimates (Giudice (1998); Moore (2000)) and one crude calculation (Moore (2000)) existed previously. The results enable quantitative estimates of axial quark number equilibration in heavy ion collisions and electroweak baryogenesis, and the authors note that extracting the sphaleron rate from Euclidean lattice data is even harder than for other transport coefficients like electrical conductivity or shear viscosity (Meyer (2007)).
