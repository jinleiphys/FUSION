# On E0 Transitions in even-even nuclei

> The reanimation of the investigations dedicated to 0^{+} states energies and E0 transitions between them is provoked by new and more precise experimental techniques that not only made revision of the previous data but also gave a possibility to obtain a great amount of new 0^{+} states energies and conversion electrons data. We suggest one phenomenological model for estimation of the E0 transition nuclear matrix elements. Recently theoretical calculations [1] predicted existence of a 0^{+} state with energy 0.68 MeV in ^{160}Dy nucleus. Powerful enough arguments in favor of existence of 681.3 keV state in ^{160}Dy nucleus are presented.

# On E0 Transitions in even-even nuclei

## Key claim

The authors present a phenomenological model for estimating E0 transition nuclear matrix elements between excited 0+ states in even-even nuclei, and provide experimental evidence supporting the existence of a previously predicted 0+ state at 681.3 keV in 160Dy, though the model relies on an approximation that neglects mutual influence of electronic and nuclear wave functions.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian using Holstein-Primakoff boson operators derived from fermion pairs in a single j-subshell, yielding a parabolic energy spectrum En = An - Bn2 + C where n is the number of monopole bosons. They calculate E0 transition matrix elements ρmn by integrating the nuclear-electron factor Fnuc,el(k,R) with a boson transition amplitude f(m,n,p,w) that depends on the nuclear size R0 and a surface diffuseness parameter w. Experimental β-spectrograms from photographic plates of Er and Ho fractions were analyzed using the MAC-1 installation at ITEP to search for transitions in 160Dy.

## Key numbers

- The predicted 0+ state in 160Dy has energy 0.68 MeV from theoretical calculations, and the authors identify a state at 681.3 keV
- A transition from the 2+ state at 2503.8 keV to the 681.3 keV 0+ state was found with energy 1822.5 keV [1822.4(3) keV] and intensity I = 0.24
- A transition from the 681.3 keV state to the 2+ state was found with energy 86.8 keV (594.5 keV and I < 0.3)
- Known excited states in 160Dy include energies 1280.0, 1456.7, 1708.2, and 1952.3 keV
- In 158Gd, a 0+ state at 0.2548 MeV (n=20) is more collective than the 0+ state at 0.5811 MeV (n=1)

## Context

This work extends prior phenomenological approaches to 0+ states and E0 transitions by Kantele, Passoja, Toivonen, and Kumpulainen, who noted that ρ2(0+2 → 0+1) is very small compared to ρ2(0+3 → 0+1), indicating differing collectivity. The model enables classification of 0+ states by monopole boson number n and provides a framework for estimating E0 matrix elements between states with different boson numbers, which could help determine half-lives and clarify the structure of excited 0+ states in deformed nuclei.
