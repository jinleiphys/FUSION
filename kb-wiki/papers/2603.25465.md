# Bayesian analysis of proton-proton fusion in chiral effective field theory

> The astrophysical $S$-factor for the proton-proton fusion is calculated in the low-energy regime for a variety of nuclear interactions and consistent nuclear currents, derived within chiral effective field theory. We estimate, for the first time, the theoretical uncertainty on the $S$-factor due to the truncation of the chiral expansion of the currents using a Bayesian analysis. In order to reach an accuracy at the percent level in the calculation, the electromagnetic potential includes contributions beyond the leading Coulomb interaction, such as two-photon exchange and vacuum polarization. The initial proton-proton state is expanded in partial waves and only the ${}^1S_0$ contribution is included, as it is known that the other partial-waves effects are negligible. The low-energy constant entering the contact term in the weak axial current operator is calibrated to reproduce the Gamow-Teller matrix element in Tritium $β$-decay. The value $S(0)$ is found to be $S(0)=(4.068 \pm 0.025)\times 10^{-25} \: \text{MeV}\: \text{b}$.

# Bayesian analysis of proton-proton fusion in chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton fusion at zero energy is calculated as S(0) = (4.068 ± 0.025) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b, with a 0.6% relative uncertainty that is derived from a Bayesian analysis of chiral expansion truncation errors combined with a statistical estimate of model dependence across both local and non-local chiral potentials. This uncertainty is smaller than the 1.5% error quoted in the SFIII review, and the central value is slightly lower due to the inclusion of local chiral potentials alongside non-local ones.

## Method

The authors calculate the pp → d e⁺ νₑ matrix element using chiral effective field theory interactions and consistent axial currents at orders up to N³LO, employing both local Norfolk (NV) potentials and non-local Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (EMN) potentials. The electromagnetic potential includes two-photon exchange and vacuum polarization beyond leading Coulomb, and the low-energy constant z₀ in the axial current contact term is calibrated to reproduce the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β-decay. Theoretical uncertainties from truncation of the chiral expansion are quantified using a Gaussian Process Bayesian analysis following Melendez (2019), treating the interaction and current expansions independently, while model dependence is estimated by averaging over six EMN and four NV potential models with equal probability weights per family.

## Key numbers

- S(0) = (4.068 ± 0.025) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b, corresponding to a 0.6% relative uncertainty
- S′(0)/S(0) = 10.60 ± 0.01 MeV⁻¹
- S″(0)/S(0) = 347.9 ± 0.9 MeV⁻²
- For the EMN500 potential at N³LO with current at N³LO: S(0) = 4.091 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b
- For the NVIa potential at N³LO with current at N³LO: S(0) = 4.062 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b
- For the NVIIb potential at N³LO with current at N³LO: S(0) = 4.030 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b
- The total truncation uncertainty band is approximately 0.6% over the energy range 0–30 keV
- The relative error per datum in the Taylor polynomial fit is approximately 0.013%

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the pp S-factor summarized in the Solar Fusion III review (SFIII, 2023), which recommended S(0) = 4.09(1 ± 0.015) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b based on averaging results from the phenomenological AV18 potential (Wiringa, 1995), non-local χEFT calculations (Acharya, 2023), and pionless EFT (De León, 2023). For the first time, the authors apply a Bayesian truncation-error analysis (Melendez, 2019) to the pp reaction, following the methodology developed for muon capture on deuteron (Gnech, 2024). The inclusion of both local NV potentials (Baroni, 2018) and non-local EMN potentials (Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk, 2017) resolves the previously noted discrepancy between the lower AV18 result (4.05 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b) and higher non-local χEFT results, tracing it to differences in deuteron wave function shapes. The results enable more precise predictions of solar neutrino fluxes from the pp chain.
