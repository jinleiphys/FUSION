# Universality of nucleon short-range behavior with chiral forces

> Modern advanced nuclear ab initio approaches with the similarity renormalization group (SRG) softened interactions miss high-momentum information, thus rendering them less suitable for characterizing nucleon-nucleon short-range physics. We introduce a novel framework to construct SRG-independent nuclear wave functions from No-Core Shell Model calculations. Applying our method to densities obtained with semilocal momentum-space-regularized chiral NN and NNN forces, we show key universalities of short-range behavior: (1) The two-body density ratio in the np S=1 channel, relative to the deuteron (d), is remarkably insensitive to interaction details. (2) More strikingly, while the ratio of total two-body densities to the deuteron exhibits cutoff dependence, the same ratio to the $α$-particle (4-He) is almost independent of the interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that short-range correlations (SRC) in light nuclei, when expressed as ratios of two-body densities relative to the deuteron in the neutron-proton spin S=1 channel, are insensitive to the specific chiral interaction details and regularization scheme; more strikingly, when total two-body density ratios are taken relative to the alpha particle (⁴He) rather than the deuteron, they become almost independent of the interaction cutoff, whereas deuteron-referenced total ratios retain cutoff dependence.

## Method

The authors perform Jacobi No-Core Shell Model (J-NCSM) calculations using semilocal momentum-space-regularized (SMS) chiral NN potentials at N⁴LO⁺ with momentum cutoffs Λ_N = 400, 450, 500, and 550 MeV, combined with consistently adjusted N²LO three-nucleon forces. They apply the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to soften the interactions, then construct SRG-independent nuclear wave functions by applying the inverse unitary transformation corresponding to the SRG evolution in the NN partial wave basis, thereby restoring high-momentum information lost during SRG softening. Two-body relative densities are computed in both momentum space and coordinate space for light nuclei including ⁴He, ⁶He, and ⁶Li, and ratios are taken relative to the deuteron and to the alpha particle.

## Key numbers

- The two-body density ratio in the np S=1 channel relative to the deuteron is independent of chiral interaction details for momenta up to approximately 4.5 fm⁻¹.
- For ⁴He, the ratio of total two-body densities to the deuteron in coordinate space varies from 3.2 to 4.0 across SMS interactions with Λ_N = 400 to 550 MeV, consistent with experimental values ranging from 3.02 ± 0.17 to 3.80 ± 0.34.
- For the pp S=0 channel in ⁶Li and ⁶He relative to ⁴He, variations across Λ_N values are less than or equal to 0.05.
- For the nn S=0 channel in ⁶He relative to ⁴He, the ratio varies from 1.3 to 1.4 at high momentum and short range across different Λ_N values.
- SRG flow parameters of 1.88 fm⁻¹ and 2.24 fm⁻¹ were used, with harmonic oscillator frequencies ω = 12, 16, 20, and 24 MeV tested for convergence.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlation universality, previously established for local phenomenological and chiral interactions using Quantum Monte Carlo methods (Cruz-Torres 2019, Chen 2016, Lynn 2019), to non-local chiral forces (SMS N⁴LO⁺ + N²LO) that are standard in ab initio methods requiring SRG softening. The finding that density ratios relative to the alpha particle are interaction-independent, while deuteron-referenced total ratios are not, highlights the role of three-nucleon correlations inherent in the alpha particle reference. The framework enables extension of SRC studies to heavier nuclei using other SRG-dependent ab initio methods such as in-medium SRG (IMSRG) and coupled-cluster (CC) theory.
