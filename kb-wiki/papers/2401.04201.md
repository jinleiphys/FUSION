# Measuring jet quenching with a Bayesian inference analysis of hadron and jet data by JETSCAPE

> The JETSCAPE Collaboration reports the first multi-messenger study of the QGP jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ using Bayesian inference, incorporating all available hadron and jet inclusive yield and jet substructure data from RHIC and the LHC. The theoretical model utilizes virtuality-dependent in-medium partonic energy loss coupled to a detailed dynamical model of QGP evolution. Tension is observed when constraining $\hat{q}$ for different kinematic cuts of the inclusive hadron data. The addition of substructure data is shown to improve the constraint on $\hat{q}$, without inducing tension with the constraint due to inclusive observables. These studies provide new insight into the mechanisms of jet interactions in matter, and point to next steps in the field for comprehensive understanding of jet quenching as a probe of the QGP.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE Collaboration reports the first multi-messenger Bayesian inference extraction of the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ that simultaneously incorporates inclusive hadron yields, inclusive jet yields, and jet substructure data from both RHIC and the LHC; however, tension is observed between the $\hat{q}$ values preferred by low-$\pT{}$ hadrons and high-$\pT{}$ hadrons, indicating missing theoretical uncertainties in the model.

## Method

The analysis uses a multi-stage parton energy-loss model (MATTER+LBT) implemented in the JETSCAPE simulation framework, with partons propagated through a 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic medium calibrated to soft-sector observables. Jet energy loss is parametrized via a hard thermal loop calculation of $\hat{q}$, modulated by a virtuality-dependent term that reduces energy loss for highly virtual partons. Bayesian inference is performed using inclusive charged hadron $R_{\text{AA}}$ and jet $R_{\text{AA}}$ from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, PHENIX, and STAR in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV, and a separate proof-of-concept study adds hadron-in-jet fragmentation $D(z)$ and groomed substructure observables $z_{\text{g}}$ and $R_{\text{g}}$.

## Key numbers

- The analysis includes inclusive charged hadron $R_{\text{AA}}$ and jet $R_{\text{AA}}$ from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200$ GeV at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV and 5.02 TeV at the LHC, for centralities within 0--50%.
- The calculations required 10 million core-hours on high performance computing facilities provided by XSEDE.
- The posterior distribution of $\hat{q}$ extracted from inclusive hadron and jet $R_{\text{AA}}$ is consistent within uncertainties with previous extractions by the JET collaboration and the previous JETSCAPE analysis based on inclusive hadron $R_{\text{AA}}$ only.
- For the substructure study, the 90% confidence interval of the $\hat{q}$ posterior is shown for inclusive jet $R_{\text{AA}}$ alone and for inclusive jet $R_{\text{AA}}$ plus substructure observables, using measurements from 0--10% central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200$ GeV, 2.76 TeV, and 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the previous JETSCAPE proof-of-principle Bayesian analysis (JETSCAPE, 2021) that constrained $\hat{q}$ using only inclusive charged hadron $R_{\text{AA}}$, and is consistent with earlier extractions by the JET collaboration (JET, 2013). The addition of jet substructure observables is shown to improve the constraint on $\hat{q}$ without inducing tension with inclusive observables, pointing to next steps for comprehensive understanding of jet quenching as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma. Future analyses will investigate further hadron and jet observables and additional jet quenching models.
