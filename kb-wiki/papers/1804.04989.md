# Deconfinement Transition Effects on Cosmological Parameters and Primordial Gravitational Wave Spectrum

> The cosmological evolution can be described in terms of directly measurable cosmological scalar parameters (deceleration $q$, jerk $j$, snap $s$, etc...) constructed out of high order derivatives of the scale factor. Their behavior at the critical temperature of the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase transition in early universe could be a specific tool to study the transition, analogously to the fluctuations of conserved charges in QCD. We analyze the effect of the crossover transition from quarks and gluons to hadrons in early universe on the cosmological scalars and on the gravitational wave spectrum, by using the recent lattice QCD equation of state and including the electroweak degrees of freedom and different models of dark matter. Near the transition the cosmological parameters follow the behavior of QCD trace anomaly and of the speed of sound of the entire system. The effects of deconfinement turn out to be more relevant for the modification of the primordial spectrum of gravitational waves and our complete analysis, based on lattice QCD simulations and on the hadron resonance gas below the critical temperature, refines previous results.

## Key claim

The QCD crossover transition from quarks and gluons to hadrons in the early universe produces measurable fluctuations in higher-order cosmological parameters (jerk, snap, etc.) and a modification of the primordial gravitational wave spectrum, but the cosmological parameters return to radiation-dominated values within about 100 microseconds, making the gravitational wave signature the more promising observable.

## Method

The authors used the recent HotQCD lattice QCD equation of state (2014) for temperatures above the critical temperature and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model below it, interpolating at T_l ≈ 130 MeV. They included the electroweak sector as a relativistic gas of massless particles with g_ew = 14.45 degrees of freedom and evaluated three dark matter models (cold dark matter, barotropic with w_b = 1/3, and polytropic/Chaplygin gas) to compute the total equation of state. The cosmological parameters (deceleration q, jerk j, snap s, A_5, A_6) were derived from the time evolution of the scale factor via the Friedmann equations, and the primordial gravitational wave spectrum was computed by numerically integrating the tensor perturbation equations with the full temperature-dependent equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of QCD transition: T_c = 154 MeV (from HotQCD lattice data)
- Transition temperature (minimum of speed of sound) for strong sector only: T_t^s = 147 MeV; adding electroweak sector: T_t^ew = 158 MeV; including massless dark matter: T_t^d = 157 MeV
- Transition time for strong sector only: t_t^s = 36.39 μs; adding electroweak sector: t_t^ew = 18.71 μs; including massless dark matter: t_t^d = 16.98 μs
- Cold dark matter energy density relative to non-dark-matter energy density: ε_cdm ≈ 2 × 10^{-10} ε̄ (negligible)
- Step size in fractional gravitational wave energy density: about 38%, compared to about 30% in Schwarz (1998)
- Typical frequency of gravitational waves from QCD transition: f_* ≈ 10^{-7} Hz

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Castorina (2015) and Greco (2014) that considered only the first cosmological parameters (q, j) and used a parametrized QCD equation of state without dark matter. It also refines the gravitational wave spectrum calculations of Schwarz (1998, 2003) and Watanabe (2006) by using recent lattice QCD simulations and including electroweak and dark matter contributions. The authors find a 10% difference in the gravitational wave energy density step compared to Schwarz (1998), and conclude that the QCD transition effects on cosmological parameters are too short-lived to be observable, while the gravitational wave signature remains the most promising probe.
