# Unfolding the Effects of the T=0 and T=1 Parts of the Two-Body Interaction on Nuclear Collectivity in the f-p Shell

> Calculations of the spectra of various even-even nuclei in the fp shell ({44}Ti, {46}Ti, {48}Ti, {48}Cr and {50}Cr) are performed with two sets of two-body interaction matrix elements. The first set consists of the matrix elements of the FPD6 interaction. The second set has the same T=1 two-body matrix elements as the FPD6 interaction, but all the T=0 two-body matrix elements are set equal to zero (T0FPD6). Surprisingly, the T0FPD6 interaction gives a semi-reasonable spectrum (or else this method would make no sense). A consistent feature for even-even nuclei, e.g. {44,46,48}Ti and {48,50}Cr, is that the reintroduction of T=0 matrix elements makes the spectrum look more rotational than when the T=0 matrix elements are set equal to zero. A common characteristic of the results is that, for high spin states, the excitation energies are too high for the full FPD6 interaction and too low for T0FPD6, as compared with experiment. The odd-even nucleus {43}Ti and the odd-odd nucleus {46}V are also discussed. For {43}Sc the T=0 matrix elements are responsible for staggering of the high spin states. In general, but not always, the inclusion of T=0 two-body matrix elements enhances the B(E2) rates.

## Key claim

The T=1 part of the FPD6 interaction alone provides the "spine" of the yrast spectrum in even-even fp-shell nuclei, yielding a semi-reasonable spectrum; reintroducing the T=0 matrix elements makes the spectrum more rotational, but for high-spin states the full FPD6 interaction gives excitation energies that are too high while T0FPD6 gives energies that are too low compared to experiment.

## Method

Full fp-shell model calculations using the code ANTOINE were performed for even-even nuclei (44Ti, 46Ti, 48Ti, 48Cr, 50Cr) and for 43Ti and 46V, using two sets of two-body interaction matrix elements: the full FPD6 interaction, and a modified version (T0FPD6) in which all T=0 two-body matrix elements were set to zero while keeping the T=1 matrix elements unchanged. Yrast spectra and B(E2) transition rates were computed for both interactions and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 44Ti, the B(E2) ratio (T0FPD6/FPD6) for the 0→2 transition is 0.618, with FPD6 giving 607.24 e²fm⁴ and T0FPD6 giving 375.09 e²fm⁴.
- For 46Ti, the B(E2) ratio for the 0→2 transition is 0.635 (FPD6: 682.06 e²fm⁴, T0FPD6: 432.81 e²fm⁴); for the 4→6 transition the ratio is 0.339 (FPD6: 273.85 e²fm⁴, T0FPD6: 92.867 e²fm⁴).
- For 48Cr, the B(E2) ratio for the 0→2 transition is 0.590 (FPD6: 1378.4 e²fm⁴, T0FPD6: 813.06 e²fm⁴); the 14→16 transition is the only one where T0FPD6 gives a larger value, with a ratio of 1.029.
- For 50Cr, the 8→10 B(E2) ratio is 2.174 (FPD6: 36.549 e²fm⁴, T0FPD6: 79.449 e²fm⁴), the only case where T0FPD6 gives a substantially larger B(E2) than FPD6.
- In 48Ti, the 4→6 B(E2) ratio is 1.185 (FPD6: 64.147 e²fm⁴, T0FPD6: 76.029 e²fm⁴), an exception to the general enhancement from T=0 matrix elements.
- In 42Sc, the total spread of T=1 states (E(6)-E(0)) is 3.122 MeV, more than three times the spread of T=0 states (E(5)-E(1)) of 0.899 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of partial dynamical symmetries and T=0 effects in 44Ti and 43Sc (Robinson et al., 2004) and addresses the debate on T=0 pairing raised by Macchiavelli et al. (2000), who argued that apparent T=0 pairing indicators could be explained by symmetry energies. The finding that T=0 matrix elements enhance collectivity and B(E2) rates, but that T=1 matrix elements alone give a reasonable spectral "spine," enables future experimental searches for odd-J states in even-even nuclei, which are currently very sparse and could constrain effective interactions in the fp shell.
