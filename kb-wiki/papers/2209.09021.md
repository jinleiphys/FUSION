# Vector dark boson mediated feeble interaction between fermionic dark matter and strange quark matter in quark stars

> We study the structural properties like the gravitational mass, radius and tidal deformability of dark matter (DM) admixed strange quark stars (SQSs). For the purpose we consider the vector MIT Bag model to describe the strange quark matter (SQM) and investigate the possible presence of accreted DM in the SQSs consequently forming DM admixed SQSs. We introduce feeble interaction between SQM and the accreted fermionic DM via a vector dark boson mediator. Considering the present literature, in the context of possible presence of DM in SQSs, this work is the first to consider interaction between DM and SQM in the DM admixed SQSs. The mass of the DM fermion ($m_χ$) and the vector mediator ($m_ξ$) and the coupling ($y_ξ$) between them are determined in accordance with the constraint from Bullet cluster and the present day relic abundance, respectively. We find that the presence of DM reduces both the mass and radius of the star compared to the no-DM case. The massive the DM fermion, the lower the values of maximum mass and radius of the DM admixed SQSs. For the chosen values of $m_χ$ and corresponding values of $m_ξ$ and $y_ξ$, the computed structural properties of the DM admixed SQSs satisfy all the various present day astrophysical constraints.We obtain massive DM admixed SQSs configurations consistent with the GW190814 observational data. Hence the secondary compact object associated with this event may be a DM admixed SQS.

## Key claim

This work is the first to consider a feeble interaction between dark matter (DM) and strange quark matter (SQM) in DM-admixed strange quark stars (SQSs), mediated by a vector dark boson. The authors find that DM-admixed SQS configurations can be consistent with the GW190814 observational data, suggesting the secondary compact object in that event may be a DM-admixed SQS, but only for certain choices of the vector coupling parameter G_V and DM fermion mass m_chi.

## Method

The authors adopt the vector MIT Bag (vBag) model to describe SQM composed of u, d, and s quarks with electrons, using three values of the vector coupling G_V (0.3, 0.5, 0.7) and corresponding average bag constants B from the stability window. They introduce fermionic DM with constant number density (Fermi momentum k_F_chi = 0.03 GeV) that interacts with quarks via a vector dark boson mediator xi with coupling g_xi ~ 10^-4, while the DM fermion couples to xi with coupling y_xi. The DM parameters (m_chi, m_xi, y_xi) are chosen to satisfy Bullet cluster self-interaction constraints and present-day relic abundance. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability of the stars.

## Key numbers

* For G_V = 0.3, the bag constant stability window gives B^{1/4}_min = 138 MeV and B^{1/4}_max = 148 MeV.
* For G_V = 0.5, the bag constant stability window gives B^{1/4}_min = 134 MeV and B^{1/4}_max = 143 MeV.
* For G_V = 0.7, the bag constant stability window gives B^{1/4}_min = 131 MeV and B^{1/4}_max = 139 MeV.
* DM fermion masses m_chi considered: 25, 50, 75, 100, 150, and 200 GeV, with corresponding mediator masses m_xi ranging from 4 to 60 MeV and couplings y_xi from 0.27 to 0.52.
* The DM fermion Fermi momentum is fixed at k_F_chi = 0.03 GeV, giving a constant DM number density approximately 1000 times smaller than the average baryon number density of SQM.
* For G_V = 0.7, the pure SQS maximum mass exceeds 3 solar masses, while DM-admixed SQSs with all chosen m_chi values reduce the maximum mass to an acceptable range.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of DM-admixed compact stars that assumed no interaction between DM and standard model matter, such as those by Panotopoulos (2017), Guha (2021), and Sen (2021). It also builds on the vBag model for SQSs developed by Lopes (2020) and Kumar (2022). The authors previously introduced similar feeble interactions for neutron stars using both scalar and vector dark mediators (Guha 2021, Sen 2021), and here apply only the vector mediator to maintain consistency with the vector omega meson in the SQM sector. The results enable future constraints on DM parameters from compact star observations and suggest that the GW190814 secondary object could be a DM-admixed SQS rather than a black hole.
