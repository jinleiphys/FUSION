# Away-side azimuthal distribution in a Markovian parton scattering model

> An event generator is constructed on the basis of a model of multiple scattering of partons so that the trajectory of a parton traversing a dense and expanding medium can be tracked. The parameters in the code are adjusted to fit the Δϕazimuthal distribution on the far side when the trigger momentum is in the non-perturbative region, p_T(trigger)<4 GeV/c. The dip-bump structure for 1<p_T(assoc)<2.5 GeV/c is reproduced by averaging over the exit tracks of deflected jets. An essential characteristic of the model, called Markovian Parton Scattering (MPS) model, is that the scattering angle is randomly selected in the forward cone at every step of a trajectory that is divided into many discrete steps in a semi-classical approximation of the non-perturbative scattering process. Energy loss to the medium is converted to thermal partons which hadronize by recombination to give rise to the pedestal under the bumps. When extended to high trigger momentum with \pt(trigger) >8 GeV/c, the model reproduces the single-peak structure observed by STAR without invoking any new dynamical mechanism.

# Away-side azimuthal distribution in a Markovian parton scattering model

## Key claim
The dip-bump structure observed in the away-side azimuthal distribution at moderate trigger momentum (2.5 < p_T(trigger) < 4.0 GeV/c) can be reproduced by a Markovian Parton Scattering (MPS) model where recoil partons undergo multiple random angular deflections in a dense expanding medium, without invoking collective medium responses such as Mach cones or Cherenkov radiation. The model unifies the double-bump structure at low trigger momentum and the single-peak structure at high trigger momentum (p_T(trigger) > 8 GeV/c) using the same dynamical mechanism, provided that hadronization on the away side is dominated by fragmentation rather than recombination at high p_T.

## Method
The authors constructed a Monte Carlo event generator (MPS) in which a recoil parton traverses a dense, expanding medium in discrete steps; at each step the scattering angle is randomly selected from a forward-cone Gaussian distribution whose width depends on local medium density and parton energy, with no memory of previous steps (Markovian property). Energy loss per step follows the BDMPS form (dE/dx proportional to -sqrt(E)), and partons falling below 0.3 GeV are considered absorbed and thermalized; the deposited energy raises the local temperature and creates a pedestal under the bumps. Exit partons hadronize either by recombination with thermal partons (for p_T(assoc) < 2.5 GeV/c) or by fragmentation (for p_T(assoc) > 4 GeV/c), and the resulting hadron multiplicity is averaged over many trajectories with initial hard scattering points near the near-side surface.

## Key numbers
- Trigger momentum range for dip-bump structure: 2.5 < p_T(trigger) < 4.0 GeV/c; associated particle range: 1.0 < p_T(assoc) < 2.5 GeV/c
- Initial recoil parton energy for low-p_T simulation: E_1 = 4.5 GeV
- Threshold energy for parton absorption: 0.3 GeV
- Reference energy for scattering parameters: E_s = 5 GeV; reference width sigma_s = 0.88; reference step size Delta_s = 1.9 fm
- Energy loss parameter: kappa = 0.17
- Hubble expansion parameter: H = 0.07; initial medium radius: R_0 = 6 fm
- Background thermal parameters: C_0 = 10 GeV^{-1}, T_0 = 0.17 GeV; enhanced temperature after energy deposition: T = 0.20 GeV (increase of 0.03 GeV)
- Pedestal height from thermalization: dN/d(Delta-phi) = 0.0165
- Hadron-to-exit-parton multiplicity factor: 1.7
- For high trigger momentum (8 < p_T(trigger) < 15 GeV/c): recoil parton momenta k_1 = 14.5 GeV/c for 4 < p_T(assoc) < 6 GeV/c, k_2 = 16 GeV/c for p_T(assoc) > 6 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the study of jet quenching and di-hadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions, specifically addressing the away-side azimuthal distribution measured by PHENIX (for 2.5 < p_T(trigger) < 4 GeV/c) and STAR (for 8 < p_T(trigger) < 15 GeV/c). It offers an alternative to collective-response explanations such as Mach cones (Casalderrey-Solana, 2005), Cherenkov radiation (Dremin, 2005), and color wakes (Ruppert, 2005), by showing that multiple Markovian scattering of partons can reproduce the same data. The model enables future discrimination between single-jet and double-jet event structures via three-particle correlation measurements, and predicts a small proton-to-pion ratio among associated particles on the away side even at p_T ~ 4 GeV/c, in contrast to the near-side behavior where recombination dominates.
