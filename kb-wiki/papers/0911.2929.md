# Hamiltonian light-front field theory within an AdS/QCD basis

> Non-perturbative Hamiltonian light-front quantum field theory presents opportunities and challenges that bridge particle physics and nuclear physics. Fundamental theories, such as Quantum Chromodynmamics (QCD) and Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) offer the promise of great predictive power spanning phenomena on all scales from the microscopic to cosmic scales, but new tools that do not rely exclusively on perturbation theory are required to make connection from one scale to the next. We outline recent theoretical and computational progress to build these bridges and provide illustrative results for nuclear structure and quantum field theory. As our framework we choose light-front gauge and a basis function representation with two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis for transverse modes that corresponds with eigensolutions of the soft-wall AdS/QCD model obtained from light-front holography.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach that uses a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis for transverse modes, matching the eigensolutions of the soft-wall AdS/QCD model, as a framework for non-perturbative Hamiltonian light-front field theory; the authors present illustrative results for non-interacting QED in a cavity and outline how the method may be extended to QCD, but emphasize that interactions and renormalization are required before predictive results for physical systems can be obtained.

## Method

The authors adopt light-front gauge and construct single-particle basis states as products of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for transverse modes (characterized by principal quantum number n, orbital quantum number m, and oscillator energy Ω) and discretized momentum-space plane waves for longitudinal modes (with periodic or antiperiodic boundary conditions over a finite interval). Many-parton Fock states are built from these single-particle states subject to cutoffs on total longitudinal momentum K and total transverse oscillator quanta Nmax, with symmetry constraints on total charge, azimuthal quantum number, and spin projection. For non-interacting QED in a transverse harmonic trap, the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in this basis; for QCD, two methods for implementing the global color-singlet constraint are compared.

## Key numbers

- For the ground state of ¹²C calculated with the JISP16 NN interaction and NCFC method, the extrapolated ground state energy has an assessed uncertainty of about 2 MeV, with good agreement with experiment within that uncertainty.
- The largest NCFC calculations for ¹²C at Nmax=8 reach a basis dimension near 600 million; Nmax=10 calculations are in progress.
- Low-lying solutions for A=14 systems are obtained with matrices of dimension one to three billion on 8000 to 50000 processors within a few hours of wallclock time.
- For non-interacting QED in a trap with K=Nmax, there is approximately a factor of 20 increase in total many-parton basis states with each increase of 2 units in the cutoff.
- For the global color-singlet constraint in QCD, the multiplicity of color space states is reduced by a typical factor of 30-40 compared to the zero-color-projection method at the upper ends of the curves shown.
- In the cavity mode QED eigenvalue calculation, the lepton mass is set to 0.511 MeV, the harmonic oscillator parameters are Ω=0.1 MeV and M0=0.511 MeV, and the basis parameters are K=3, Nmax=2, and Mj=1/2.

## Context

This work extends the basis-function methods of the No Core Shell Model (NCSM) and No Core Full Configuration (NCFC) approach, which have been successful in ab initio nuclear structure calculations for nuclei up to A=10-13 using chiral effective field theory interactions, to light-front quantum field theory. The choice of a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis is motivated by the phenomenological success of the soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Karch et al. (2006) and Erlich et al. (2005), together with light-front holography developed by de Teramond et al. (2008), Brodsky et al. (2003), and Polchinski et al. (2001), which provides a semiclassical first approximation to strongly coupled QCD. The BLFQ approach aims to provide a natural extension of AdS/QCD light-front wavefunctions to multiquark and multi-gluonic Fock states, incorporating higher-order corrections corresponding to full QCD theory, and enabling future calculations of observables such as the electron anomalous magnetic moment as a precision test.
