# Interaction model for the gap equation

> We explain a form for the rainbow-ladder kernel whose momentum-dependence is consonant with modern DSE- and lattice-QCD results, and assess its capability as a tool in hadron physics. In every respect tested, this form produces results for observables that are at least equal to the best otherwise obtained in a comparable approach. Moreover, it enables the natural extraction of a monotonic running-coupling and -gluon-mass.

# Interaction model for the gap equation

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new form for the rainbow-ladder kernel of the QCD gap equation whose momentum-dependence is a bounded, regular function that achieves its maximum at zero momentum, in contrast to earlier models that were chosen for numerical expediency but conflict with modern DSE and lattice results. The authors claim that in every respect tested, this form produces results for hadron observables that are at least equal to the best otherwise obtained in a comparable approach, while also enabling the natural extraction of a monotonic running-coupling and gluon-mass.

## Method
The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger gap equation using a rainbow-ladder truncation with a new interaction kernel given by Eq. (CalGQC), which replaces the infrared part of the kernel with a Gaussian form proportional to exp(-s/omega^2) rather than the earlier form proportional to s exp(-s/omega^2). They then solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson bound states and compute ground-state masses and decay constants for pseudoscalar and vector mesons, as well as masses for selected radial excitations and exotic states, using improved numerical methods that handle DSE solutions at complex arguments.

## Key numbers
- For the new interaction with omega=0.5 GeV and (D omega)^(1/3)=0.8 GeV: pion mass m_pi=0.134 GeV, pion decay constant f_pi=0.093 GeV, rho mass m_rho=0.74 GeV, rho decay constant f_rho=0.15 GeV
- For the new interaction with omega=0.6 GeV and (D omega)^(1/3)=0.8 GeV: pion mass m_pi=0.136 GeV, kaon mass m_K=0.497 GeV, phi mass m_phi=1.07 GeV
- Infrared gluon mass scale: M_g=0.67 GeV for omega=0.5 GeV; M_g=0.81 GeV for omega=0.6 GeV
- Infrared running coupling values: alpha_RL(0)/pi=15 for omega=0.5 GeV; alpha_RL(0)/pi=9 for omega=0.6 GeV
- For the new interaction with D omega=(1.1 GeV)^3 and omega=0.6 GeV: first radial excitation of the rho has f_rho1 = -0.45 f_rho

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to hadron physics by replacing the widely-used interaction model of Maris and Tandy (1999), whose kernel Eq. (CalGMT) produced unphysical behaviour at timelike momenta including a zero at s=-(0.046 GeV)^2 and rapid growth to large negative values. The new form is consonant with modern DSE and lattice studies of the gluon propagator by Bowman et al. (2004) and Aguilar et al. (2009, 2010), and with studies of the dressed-quark-gluon vertex by Skullerud et al. (2003) and Bhagwat et al. (2004). The work enables future studies of the phase transition at nonzero temperature and chemical potential, and provides a foundation for moving beyond rainbow-ladder truncation to address outstanding questions in hadron spectroscopy, particularly for axial-vector mesons, radial excitations, and exotic states.
