# Chiral perturbation theory at O(a^2) for lattice QCD

> We construct the chiral effective Lagrangian for two lattice theories: one with Wilson fermions and the other with Wilson sea fermions and Ginsparg-Wilson valence fermions. For each of these theories we construct the Symanzik action through order $a^2$. The chiral Lagrangian is then derived, including terms of order $a^2$, which have not been calculated before. We find that there are only few new terms at this order. Corrections to existing coefficients in the continuum chiral Lagrangian are proportional to $a^2$, and appear in the Lagrangian at order $a^2 p^2$ or higher. Similarly, O(4) symmetry breaking terms enter the Symanzik action at order $a^2$, but contribute to the chiral Lagrangian at order $a^2 p^4$ or higher. We calculate the light meson masses in chiral perturbation theory for both lattice theories. At next-to-leading order, we find that there are no order $a^2$ corrections to the valence-valence meson mass in the mixed theory due to the enhanced chiral symmetry of the valence sector.

## Key claim

The authors construct the chiral effective Lagrangian including terms of order \(a^2\) for lattice QCD with Wilson fermions and for a mixed theory with Wilson sea fermions and Ginsparg-Wilson valence fermions, finding that only a small number of new operators appear at this order and that, at next-to-leading order, there are no \(a^2\) corrections to the valence-valence meson mass in the mixed theory due to the enhanced chiral symmetry of the valence sector.

## Method

The authors first construct the Symanzik effective action through order \(a^2\) for both lattice theories, listing all allowed operators organized by dimension and symmetry properties. They then perform a spurion analysis to identify the transformation properties of symmetry-breaking operators under chiral flavor rotations, and use these spurions to construct the chiral Lagrangian for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons. Finally, they compute the light meson masses in chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order for both the Wilson fermion theory and the mixed-action theory.

## Key numbers

- The chiral Lagrangian at order \(a^2\) for the Wilson action contains three new terms: \(-\hat a^2 W_6'\langle\Sigma^\dag+\Sigma\rangle^2\), \(-\hat a^2 W_7'\langle\Sigma^\dag-\Sigma\rangle^2\), and \(-\hat a^2 W_8'\langle\Sigma^\dag\Sigma^\dag+\Sigma\Sigma\rangle\), where \(\hat a \equiv 2 W_0 a\) and \(W_i'\) are new low-energy constants.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\ \text{GeV}\), used as the reference scale for the power counting in \(m/\Lambda_\chi\) and \(a\Lambda_\chi\).
- The critical quark mass \(m_c\) is of order \(1/a\), defining the renormalized quark mass as \(m_q = m_0 - m_c\).
- For the mixed theory, only 4 of the 14 four-quark operators at order \(a^2\) are allowed in the valence sector: \(O_7^{(6)}\), \(O_8^{(6)}\), \(O_{12}^{(6)}\), and \(O_{13}^{(6)}\), which are the ones invariant under the valence chiral symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the results of Rupak (2002) and Bar (2002) by calculating the chiral Lagrangian including \(a^2\) lattice effects, which had not been computed before. The authors note that current unquenched simulations use lattice spacings that are not very small, and non-perturbatively improved Wilson fermions have leading corrections of order \(a^2\), making these calculations necessary for proper continuum extrapolations. The results enable more accurate chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data by incorporating discretization effects systematically, and the small number of new low-energy constants (3 for Wilson, 4 for mixed theory) limits the number of free parameters in practical applications.
