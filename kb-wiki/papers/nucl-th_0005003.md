# The Sigma Commutator from Lattice QCD

> As a direct source of information on chiral symmetry breaking within QCD, the sigma commutator is of considerable importance. Since hadron structure is a non-perturbative problem, numerical calculations on a space-time lattice are currently the only rigorous approach. With recent advances in the calculation of hadron masses within full QCD, it is of interest to see whether the sigma commutator can be calculated directly from the dependence of the nucleon mass on the input quark mass. We show that, provided the correct chiral behaviour of QCD is respected in the extrapolation to realistic quark masses, one can indeed obtain a fairly reliable determination of the sigma commutator using present lattice data. For two-flavour dynamical fermion QCD the sigma commutator lies between 45 and 55 MeV based on recent data from CP-PACS and UKQCD.

# The Sigma Commutator from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

Provided the correct chiral behaviour of QCD is respected in the extrapolation to realistic quark masses, one can obtain a fairly reliable determination of the sigma commutator using present lattice data; for two-flavour dynamical fermion QCD the sigma commutator lies between 45 and 55 MeV based on recent data from CP-PACS and UKQCD.

## Method

The authors extracted the sigma commutator from the quark mass dependence of the nucleon mass using the relation sigma_N = m_pi^2 * dM_N / d(m_pi^2) evaluated at the physical pion mass. They fitted lattice data for the nucleon mass as a function of m_pi^2 using an extrapolation function that incorporates the correct leading non-analytic (LNA) behaviour proportional to m_pi^3 from chiral perturbation theory, including pion-loop self-energy contributions from both nucleon and Delta intermediate states with a finite-range regulator.

## Key numbers

- The sigma commutator for two-flavour dynamical fermion QCD lies between 45 and 55 MeV, based on CP-PACS and UKQCD lattice data.
- A phenomenological cubic fit (unconstrained by chiral symmetry) gave sigma_N = 29.7 MeV, with a fitted coefficient gamma = -0.761 GeV^{-2} compared to the model-independent LNA coefficient gamma^{LNA} = -5.60 GeV^{-2}.
- The fit using the chirally-motivated extrapolation function including the lowest data point near m_pi^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2 gave sigma_N = 54.6 MeV and a physical nucleon mass of 870 MeV.
- Omitting the lowest data point from the chirally-motivated fit gave sigma_N = 65.8 MeV.
- The experimental value inferred from pion-nucleon scattering data is 45 +/- 8 MeV (Sainio).
- The SESAM collaboration obtained sigma_N = 18 +/- 5 MeV through a direct calculation of the scalar matrix element.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract the sigma commutator from lattice QCD, which had produced values ranging from 15 to 25 MeV in quenched QCD with naive extrapolations (Cabasino) and 40 to 60 MeV from scalar matrix element calculations (Liu, Fukugita). The authors improve upon the SESAM collaboration's direct scalar matrix element approach by using only renormalisation group invariant quantities and by incorporating the correct chiral behaviour from chiral perturbation theory, in contrast to the phenomenological cubic fit used by CP-PACS (Aoki 1999). This enables a stable determination of the sigma commutator from existing two-flavour dynamical fermion data, with the caveat that lattice simulations at lighter quark masses are needed to reduce the extrapolation uncertainty.
