# Production of $ρ^{0}$ meson with large $p_T$ at NLO in heavy-ion collisions

> Production of large transverse momentum $ρ^{0}$ meson in high-energy nuclear collisions is investigated for the first time at the next-leading-order in the QCD improved parton model. The $ρ^0$ fragmentation functions (FFs) in vacuum at any scale $Q$ are obtained, by evolving a newly developed initial parametrization of $ρ^0$ FFs at a scale $\rm Q_{0}^2=1.5\ GeV^2$ from a broken SU(3) model through NLO DGLAP equations. The numerical simulations of $p_{\rm T}$ spectra of $ρ^{0}$ meson in the elementary $\rm p+p$ collisions at NLO give a decent description of STAR $\rm p+p$ data. In $\rm A+A$ reactions the jet quenching effect is taken into account with the higher-twist approach by the medium-modified parton FFs due to gluon radiation in the quark-gluon plasma, whose space-time evolution is described by a (3+1D) hydrodynamical model. The nuclear modification factors for $ρ^{0}$ meson and its double ratio with $π^\pm$ nuclear modification in central $\rm Au+Au$ collisions at the RHIC are calculated and found to be in good agreement with STAR measurement. Predictions of $ρ^{0}$ nuclear modification and the yield ratio $ρ^0/π^0$ in central Pb+Pb at the LHC are also presented. It is shown that the ratio $ρ^0/π^0$ in central Pb+Pb will approach to that in p+p reactions when $p_{\rm T}>12$~GeV.

## Key claim

The authors present the first next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation of large-transverse-momentum ρ⁰ meson production in heavy-ion collisions, using newly developed ρ⁰ fragmentation functions (FFs) derived from a broken SU(3) model. The nuclear modification factor R_AA for ρ⁰ is found to be similar to that of π⁰, and the ρ⁰/π⁰ ratio in central Pb+Pb at the LHC is predicted to approach the p+p value when p_T > 12 GeV.

## Method

The authors obtain ρ⁰ fragmentation functions in vacuum at any scale Q by evolving an initial parametrization at Q₀² = 1.5 GeV² from a broken SU(3) model through NLO DGLAP evolution equations. They compute ρ⁰ production in p+p collisions at NLO in the pQCD improved parton model using CTEQ6M parton distribution functions. For A+A collisions, jet quenching is incorporated via the higher-twist approach, where medium-modified parton fragmentation functions account for gluon radiation in the quark-gluon plasma, with the medium's space-time evolution described by a (3+1D) ideal hydrodynamical model.

## Key numbers

- Initial parametrization scale for ρ⁰ FFs: Q₀² = 1.5 GeV²
- Jet transport parameter at RHIC: q̂₀ = 1.2 GeV²/fm with τ₀ = 0.6 fm for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Jet transport parameter at LHC: q̂₀ = 2.2 ± 0.4 GeV²/fm with τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- The ρ⁰/π⁰ ratio in central Pb+Pb at the LHC approaches the p+p value when p_T > 12 GeV
- Best fit to PHENIX π⁰ data in 0-5% Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV gives q̂₀ = 1.20 ± 0.30 GeV²/fm

## Context

This work extends prior NLO calculations of π⁰ and η meson production in heavy-ion collisions by the same group (Dai et al., 2015) to the ρ⁰ meson, which is heavier than π⁰ and η but composed of similar constituent quarks. The study enables comparison of the suppression pattern across different identified hadrons and provides predictions for ρ⁰ production at the LHC. The broken SU(3) model for vector meson fragmentation functions was previously developed by Saveetha and Indumathi (2013) and Indumathi and collaborators (2011), and the higher-twist approach for jet quenching follows the formalism of Guo and Wang (2000) and Zhang and Wang (2003).
