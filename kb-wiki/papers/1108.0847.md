# Quantum Bose liquids with logarithmic nonlinearity: Self-sustainability and emergence of spatial extent

> The Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) equation is a long-wavelength approach widely used to describe the dilute Bose-Einstein condensates (BEC). However, in many physical situations, such as higher densities, this approximation unlikely suffices hence one might need models which would account for long-range correlations and multi-body interactions. We show that the Bose liquid described by the logarithmic wave equation has a number of drastic differences from the GP one. It possesses the self-sustainability property: while the free GP condensate tends to spill all over the available volume the logarithmic one tends to form a Gaussian-type droplet - even in the absence of an external trapping potential. The quasi-particle modes of the logarithmic BEC are shown to acquire a finite size despite the bare particles being assumed point-like, i.e., the spatial extent emerges here as a result of quantum many-body correlations. Finally, we study the elementary excitations and demonstrate that the background density changes the topological structure of their momentum space which, in turn, affects their dispersion relations. Depending on the density the latter can be of the massive relativistic, massless relativistic, tachyonic and quaternionic type.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bose-Einstein condensate described by the logarithmic wave equation (LogSE) can form a self-bound Gaussian droplet without any external trapping potential, but only when the nonlinearity parameter beta is positive and the number of particles exceeds a critical minimum value; the abstract omits the condition that the energy must be negative for mechanical stability.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the logarithmic Schrodinger equation (LogSE) for a Bose condensate in both free space and in a harmonic trap, using the Madelung representation to derive the equation of state and speed of sound. They apply the Vakhitov-Kolokolov stability criterion to determine the stability conditions for the Gaussian-type droplet solutions, and compute the effective potential, chemical potential, and energy per particle as functions of particle number and the logarithmic coupling parameter.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian droplet solution has characteristic size a_beta = hbar sqrt(beta/(2m)), where beta is the logarithmic nonlinearity parameter.
- The chemical potential is mu = 3 beta^{-1} [1 - ln( N^{1/3} a / (sqrt(pi) a_beta) )] = - beta^{-1} ln( N / N_0 ), with N_0 = (e sqrt(pi) a_beta / a)^3.
- The energy per particle is E/N = beta^{-1} [1 - ln( N / N_0 )], bounded from above by E/N <= beta^{-1}.
- Mechanical stability requires E < 0, which implies N > N_min = e N_0.
- The speed of sound in the leading order is c_s = 1 / sqrt(m |beta|) + O(hbar), independent of density.
- The effective trap frequency from the logarithmic term is omega_eff = 2 beta^{-1} / hbar.

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose-Einstein condensates beyond the standard Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) approximation, which assumes dilute gases with contact interactions and polynomial energy functionals. The logarithmic nonlinearity, previously studied in quantum optics by Buljan (2003) and in nuclear physics by Efros (1985), is shown to produce self-bound droplets and emergent spatial extent that cannot arise in the GP framework. The results enable modeling of denser Bose liquids, such as those made of noble gases or nuclear matter under certain conditions, and suggest connections to effective quantum gravity and vacuum models explored by Zloshchastiev (2009).
