# Electromagnetic radiation from relativistic nuclear collisions

> We review some of the results obtained in the study of the production of electromagnetic radiation in relativistic nuclear collisions. We concentrate on the emission of real photons and dileptons from the hot and dense strongly interacting phases of the reaction. We examine the contributions from the partonic sector, as well as those from the nonperturbative hadronic sector. We examine the current data, some of the predictions for future measurements, and comment on what has been learnt so far.

## Key claim

This review argues that electromagnetic radiation (real photons and dileptons) from relativistic nuclear collisions provides a privileged probe of the hot and dense strongly interacting phases of the reaction, but that certainty in identifying quark-gluon plasma signatures will require simultaneous analysis of complementary observables rather than relying on any single signal.

## Method

The authors review the formalism relating electromagnetic emission rates to the imaginary part of the retarded in-medium photon self-energy at finite temperature and density. They examine contributions from both the partonic sector and the nonperturbative hadronic sector, using effective Lagrangians with parameters fitted to radiative decay measurements via vector meson dominance, and compare theoretical predictions with experimental data from the SPS program (CERES, HELIOS-3, NA38/NA50 collaborations).

## Key numbers

- Low dilepton invariant mass data from Pb-Au collisions at 40 AGeV/c show that calculations with a medium-modified rho spectral density (thick solid line) and with a dropping in-medium rho mass (dash-dotted line) both reproduce the data, but cannot empirically exclude other interpretations
- The intermediate mass dilepton region (m_phi < M < m_J/psi) shows significant enhancement in central S+W and S+U collisions compared to proton-induced reactions, normalized to charged-particle multiplicity
- Thermal hadronic sources in the intermediate mass region (1 GeV < M < 3 GeV) include channels: pi pi, pi rho, pi omega, eta rho, rho rho, pi a1, K Kbar, K Kbar* + c.c.
- The hydrodynamic model uses a relation linking temperature T_0 and formation time tau_0: (2 pi^4)/(45 zeta(3)) * (1/A_T) * dN/dy = 4 a T_0^3 tau_0, where a = 42.25 pi^2/90 for a plasma of massless u, d, s, g partons
- The calculated dimuon invariant mass distribution and transverse momentum spectrum show good agreement with NA50 data after correcting for detector acceptance and resolution

## Context

This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of hot QCD matter, building on earlier reviews and the formalism developed by Feinberg (1976), McLerran and Toimela (1985), and Gale and Kapusta (1991). It discusses the low-mass dilepton excess observed by the CERES collaboration and the intermediate-mass enhancement seen by HELIOS-3 and NA38/NA50, comparing multiple theoretical interpretations including in-medium vector meson spectral density modifications (Rapp and Wambach, 2000), dropping rho mass scenarios (Brown and Rho), and thermal hadronic sources. The work enables future high-statistics measurements at RHIC and LHC to discriminate between competing models of in-medium modifications.
