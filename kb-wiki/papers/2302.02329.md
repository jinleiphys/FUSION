# Probing the jet transport coefficient of cold nuclear matter in electron-ion collisions

> We present a study of the nuclear-medium induced transverse momentum broadening of particle production in future electron-ion-collision~(EIC) experiments. By considering the multiple scattering between hard partons and cold nuclear medium within the higher-twist factorization framework in perturbative QCD, we calculate the transverse momentum broadening of single hadron production in semi-inclusive measurements, as well as the nuclear enhancement of the transverse momentum imbalance for di-hadron and heavy-meson pair productions. In particular, a kinematics dependent non-perturbative jet transport coefficient $\hat q=\hat q(x,Q^2)$ extracted in a global analysis of the current data, together with its uncertainty determined with a Hessian method, are input into our calculations and are available for the community. Significant kinematic and color-state dependences of the nuclear induced broadening/imbalance are predicted. Our results indicate that the future EIC measurements are able to provide powerful constraints on the kinematic dependence of the transport coefficient $\hat q$ and thus greatly facilitate the jet tomography of cold nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that future electron-ion collider (EIC) measurements can provide powerful constraints on the kinematic dependence of the jet transport coefficient $\hat q(x,Q^2)$ for cold nuclear matter, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the higher-twist factorization framework correctly describes multiple scattering effects and that the extracted $\hat q$ with its Hessian uncertainties is a reliable input for these predictions.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of existing world data on transverse momentum broadening from electron-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions (215 data points, later expanded to 227) within the higher-twist expansion formalism of perturbative QCD, extracting a kinematics-dependent jet transport coefficient $\hat q(x,Q^2)$ parameterized as $\hat q_0 \,\alpha_s(\mu^2) \,x^{\alpha}(1-x)^{\beta} \left[\ln(\mu^2/\mu_0^2)\right]^{\gamma}$. They determined the uncertainties of $\hat q$ using the Hessian matrix method with the MINUIT package, then used this extracted $\hat q$ as input to calculate transverse momentum broadening for single hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and transverse momentum imbalance for di-hadron and heavy-meson pair productions at three proposed EIC facilities (US-EIC, EicC, and JLab).

## Key numbers

* Optimal parameters for $\hat q(x,Q^2)$: $\hat{q}_0=0.0191\pm 0.0061~\textrm{GeV}^2/\textrm{fm}$, $\alpha=-0.182\pm 0.050$, $\beta=-2.85\pm 1.87$, $\gamma=0.264\pm 0.169$ at 90% confidence level
* Global minimum $\chi_0^2=275$ for 227 data points in the updated analysis including new ALICE $J/\psi$ data
* For the constant $\hat q$ assumption, minimum total $\chi^2=388$ ($\chi^2$/NDP$=1.8$) compared to $\chi^2=260$ ($\chi^2$/NDP$=1.21$) for the kinematics-dependent $\hat q$ with 215 data points
* The constant $\hat q$ extracted from data is $\hat q= \hat q_0=0.0150^{+0.0023}_{-0.0025}$~GeV$^2$/fm
* For $J/\psi$ broadening at LHC, the kinematics-dependent $\hat q$ gave $\chi^2=4.8$ ($\chi^2$/NDP$=0.4$) while the constant $\hat q$ gave $\chi^2=87.3$ ($\chi^2$/NDP$=7.3$) with 12 data points

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous global extraction of $\hat q$ in cold nuclear matter (Ru et al., 2019) by adding Hessian uncertainty determination and including new ALICE $J/\psi$ data from 2021. The study enables future EIC experiments to test the universality and kinematic dependence of $\hat q$, which has been an iconic quantity in heavy-ion collision studies (Burke et al., 2013; JETSCAPE, 2021) and is related to the gluon saturation scale in small-$x$ physics (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and QCD power corrections at large $x$ (Dokshitzer et al., 1995; Brodsky et al., 2000).
