# Charm jets as a probe for strangeness at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> We explore the feasibility of the measurement of charm-jet cross sections in charged-current deep-inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider. This channel provides clean sensitivity to the strangeness content of the nucleon in the high-$x$ region. We estimate charm-jet tagging performance with parametrized detector simulations. We show the expected sensitivity to various scenarios for strange parton distribution functions. We argue that this measurement will be key to future QCD global analyses, so it should inform EIC detector designs and luminosity requirements.

## Key claim

Charm-jet cross sections in charged-current deep-inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider can provide clean sensitivity to the strangeness content of the nucleon in the high-$x$ region, avoiding the fragmentation-function and nuclear-correction complications that affect other approaches. The authors demonstrate feasibility through parametrized detector simulations, but note that detailed studies of higher-order QCD corrections and QED radiative effects are reserved for future work.

## Method

The authors generated 20 million charged-current deep-inelastic scattering events using Pythia8 with 10 GeV electron and 275 GeV proton beams, then applied a parametrized detector simulation using Delphes with baseline EIC detector parameters. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with radius parameter R=1.0, and charm jets were identified using a displaced-track-counting algorithm based on three-dimensional impact parameter significance, requiring at least two tracks with pT above 0.5 GeV and signed impact parameter significance above 3.00. The sensitivity to strangeness was evaluated by comparing event yields under two extreme scenarios for the strange suppression ratio Rs taken from the CT18 global PDF analysis.

## Key numbers

- Total t-channel CC DIS cross section for Q2 > 100 GeV2 reported by Pythia8: 14.8 pb, similar to the NLO calculation in Aschenauer (2013)
- Charm-to-inclusive jet cross section ratio: about 3.5% at jet pT = 10 GeV, decreasing to less than 0.5% at 40 GeV
- Baseline charm-jet tagging efficiency: 20% for jets with pT > 10 GeV, with light-jet misidentification rate of 0.4%
- Degrading z0 impact parameter resolution from 20 micrometers to 100 micrometers reduces charm tagging efficiency to 14% (30% loss in tagged yield)
- Improving impact parameter resolution to 10 micrometers increases charm tagging efficiency to 26% (30% gain)
- Combining single-track PID approaches (kaon, electron, muon identification) with the track-counting method brings total charm-jet tagging efficiency to 31%
- Bin survival probability for event kinematics reconstruction: about 70% or better for a large region at high Q2 and x

## Context

This work extends feasibility studies of jet measurements at the EIC that previously focused on neutral-current DIS (Arratia 2020, Page 2019) and photoproduction (Aschenauer 2019), as well as inclusive CC DIS studies (Aschenauer 2013). It differs from work by Abdolmaleki (2019) that emphasized the low-x region for the LHeC, instead focusing on the poorly constrained valence region at high x. The measurement would help resolve tensions between dimuon measurements in neutrino DIS (which prefer low Rs values around 0.5) and electroweak boson measurements at the LHC (which prefer Rs consistent with unity), and should inform EIC detector designs and luminosity requirements.
