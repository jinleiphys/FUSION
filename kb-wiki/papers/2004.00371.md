# Comment on "Observation of annual modulation by $γ$ rays from ($α$,$γ$) reactions at the Soudan Underground Laboratory"

> Tiwari {\it et al.}\ have identified an annual modulation of the $γ$-ray flux at the Soudan Underground Laboratory which is strongly correlated to the radon concentration. The $γ$-ray flux results from ($α$,$γ$) reactions which are induced by the $α$ activity of radon and its daughters. Unfortunately, the quantitative analysis of the $γ$-ray flux is based on unrealistic ($α$,$γ$) cross sections, and thus the calculated $γ$-ray fluxes are not reliable.

# Comment on "Observation of annual modulation by γ rays from (α,γ) reactions at the Soudan Underground Laboratory"

## Key claim

The annual modulation of the γ-ray flux at the Soudan Underground Laboratory is strongly correlated to radon concentration, but the quantitative analysis by Tiwari et al. is based on unrealistic (α,γ) cross sections from the TALYS code, making the calculated γ-ray fluxes unreliable even though the temporal correlation itself is well established.

## Method

Mohr examines the TALYS code calculations used by Tiwari et al. for (α,γ) reactions on 16O, 27Al, and 28Si, identifying two critical errors: the TALYS parameter "elwidth" (a Gaussian spreading width set to 0.5 MeV by default) produces non-physical γ-ray energies above the kinematic maximum, and the statistical model is inappropriate for light nuclei like 16O where cross sections are governed by individual resonances rather than smooth averages. The author then calculates realistic thick-target γ-ray spectra for the 16O(α,γ)20Ne reaction using experimentally known resonance properties and direct capture contributions, showing discrete γ-ray lines instead of the continuous spectra presented by Tiwari et al.

## Key numbers

- The maximum γ-ray energy in 16O(α,γ)20Ne for Eα = 4 MeV is Eγ,max = (16/20) × 4 MeV + 4730 keV = 7930 keV, yet Tiwari et al. show γ-ray energies above 14 MeV in their Figure 5
- The highest α energy in the 220Rn chain is 8784 keV from 212Po decay; for 222Rn it is 7687 keV from 214Po decay
- For 16O(α,γ)20Ne at Eα = 8784 keV, the true maximum γ-ray energy is Eγ,max = 11757 keV, while Tiwari et al. show continuous spectra up to approximately 18 MeV
- The maximum γ-ray energies for Eα = 4 MeV are 13.15 MeV for 27Al(α,γ)31P and 10.45 MeV for 28Si(α,γ)32S, yet Tiwari et al. show γ-ray energies above 20 MeV
- Even for low-abundant 17,18O with larger Q-values, Eγ,max remains below 16854 keV

## Context

This comment directly critiques the quantitative analysis of Tiwari et al. (2017), who studied γ-ray fluxes from (α,γ) reactions induced by radon decay at the Soudan Underground Laboratory. The work extends earlier discussions by Mohr (2017) on the limits of statistical model applicability for light nuclei and by Rauscher (2000) on isospin suppression in N=Z nuclei. The author notes that the final integrated γ-ray fluxes in the 4-10 MeV energy window from Tiwari et al. may have the correct order of magnitude despite the inconsistent analysis, and recommends an improved analysis using experimentally known resonance properties rather than TALYS calculations for these light nuclei.
