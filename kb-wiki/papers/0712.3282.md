# Parton Momentum Distribution at the Moment of Jet-Parton Collision

> We extract the early parton momentum distribution using the STAR Collaboration data of ridge particles associated with a near-side jet in central AuAu collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV. The ridge particles are identified as medium partons kicked by the jet near the surface and they carry direct information on the parton momentum distribution at the moment of jet-parton collisions. The extracted parton momentum distribution has a thermal-like transverse momentum distribution but a rapidity plateau structure with a relatively flat rapidity distribution at mid-rapidities with sharp kinematic boundaries at large rapidities that depend on the transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The early parton momentum distribution in central AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV can be extracted from STAR ridge particle data under the assumption that ridge particles are medium partons kicked by a near-side jet near the surface, with the extracted distribution showing a thermal-like transverse component but a rapidity plateau structure whose width decreases as transverse momentum increases.

## Method

The author applies a momentum kick model in which a near-side jet near the medium surface kicks medium partons, loses energy, and fragments into the trigger particle and other products; the kicked medium partons materialize as ridge particles via parton-hadron duality. The normalized ridge particle momentum distribution is related to the initial parton momentum distribution at a shifted momentum p_i = p - q, where q is the momentum kick taken to lie along the trigger particle direction. The initial parton momentum distribution is parametrized with a thermal factor, a modified transverse mass denominator, and a light-cone variable (1-x)^a fall-off that respects kinematic boundaries, and the parameters are fit to STAR data for 0.15 < p_t < 4 GeV and 0 < |η| < 3.9.

## Key numbers

- The momentum kick magnitude |q| = 1.0 GeV
- The product f_R ⟨N⟩ = 4 (ridge particle transmission factor times average number of kicked partons per trigger)
- The fall-off parameter a = 0.5
- The temperature parameter T = 0.50 GeV
- The effective mass parameter m_d = 1 GeV
- The jet fragment transmission factor f_J = 0.632 (derived from absorption length argument)
- The pp near-side jet fit parameters: σ_φ0 = 0.5, m_a = 1.1 GeV, N_jet = 0.75, T_jet = 0.55 GeV, with m = m_π
- The theoretical ridge dN_ch/N_trig p_t dp_t has a peak at p_t ∼ |q| ∼ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model previously proposed by Wong (2007, 2008) for the ridge phenomenon in heavy-ion collisions, and uses STAR Collaboration data from Adams et al. (2005), Putschke (2007), and Wang (2007). The extracted parton momentum distribution provides initial conditions for studying the evolution toward quark-gluon plasma and tests the inside-outside cascade picture of parton production from Casher et al. (1974) and Wong (1991, 1994). After this manuscript was completed, predictions from the model were found to agree with PHOBOS Collaboration measurements by Wenger (2008).
