# KNO scaling from a nearly Gaussian action for small-x gluons

> Transverse momentum integrated multiplicities in the central region of pp collisions at LHC energies satisfy Koba-Nielsen-Olesen scaling. We attempt to relate this finding to multiplicity distributions of soft gluons. KNO scaling emerges if the effective theory describing color charge fluctuations at a scale on the order of the saturation momentum is approximately Gaussian. From an evolution equation for quantum corrections which includes both saturation as well as fluctuations we find that evolution with the QCD β-function satisfies KNO scaling while fixed-coupling evolution does not. Thus, non-linear saturation effects and running-coupling evolution are both required in order to reproduce geometric scaling of the DIS cross section and KNO scaling of virtual dipoles in a hadron wave function.

## Key claim

KNO scaling of transverse-momentum-integrated multiplicities in pp collisions emerges if the effective theory describing color charge fluctuations at the saturation scale is approximately Gaussian; both non-linear saturation effects and running-coupling evolution are required to reproduce this scaling, while fixed-coupling evolution does not satisfy it.

## Method

The authors compute the multiplicity distribution of small-x gluons from a Gaussian effective action (McLerran-Venugopalan model) for valence color charge densities, obtaining a negative binomial distribution. They then introduce a quartic perturbation to the action and compute its effect on the fluctuation parameter k. Separately, they solve a stochastic evolution equation for the dipole size distribution in a hadron wave function via Monte Carlo techniques, evolving 10^5 initial configurations to obtain the multiplicity distribution of virtual dipoles with size below 1/⟨Q_s^2⟩.

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian action, the mean multiplicity scales as \(\bar{n} \sim \frac{N_c (N_c^2-1)}{\alpha_s} Q_s^2 S_\perp\) and the fluctuation parameter as \(k \sim (N_c^2-1) Q_s^2 S_\perp\), giving \(\bar{n}/k \sim N_c/\alpha_s\).
- With the quartic operator, \(\bar{n}/k\) decreases by a factor of \(1 - 3\beta(N_c^2+1)\); for protons at x=10^{-2}, \(\beta \simeq 0.01\) would reduce \(\bar{n}/k\) by a factor of 1.43 relative to the Gaussian theory.
- NBD fits to data show \(\bar{n}/k \simeq 6-7\) at 2360 GeV; the observed increase from \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV to 7 TeV is a factor of about 3.
- For the stochastic evolution, running-coupling evolution (with QCD β-function) satisfies KNO scaling while fixed-coupling evolution (\(\alpha_s\)=const) does not.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (MV) and the approach of Gelis, Lappi, and Venugopalan (2009) who derived the negative binomial distribution for gluon multiplicities. It also builds on the stochastic evolution equation studied by Iancu, Mueller, and Munier (2006) for fixed coupling and by Dumitru, Gelis, and Jalilian-Marian (2007) for running coupling. The finding that running-coupling evolution restores KNO scaling connects to the observation of geometric scaling in DIS by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2000). The results enable a theoretical understanding of why KNO scaling holds at LHC energies (ALICE 2010, CMS 2010) but shows deviations at lower energies (UA5 200 GeV data).
