# Intruder level and deformation in the SD-pair shell model

> The influence of the intruder level on nuclear deformation is studied within the framework of the nucleon-pair shell model truncated to an SD-pair subspace. The results suggest that the intruder level has a tendency to soften the deformation and plays an important role in determining the onset of rotational behavior.

# Intruder level and deformation in the SD-pair shell model

## Key claim
The intruder (unique parity) level tends to soften nuclear deformation and reduce rotational collectivity, contrary to earlier claims that intruder levels play a dominant role in enhancing deformation. The effect is sensitive to the single-particle energy of the intruder level relative to normal parity levels, with rotational spectra (E4+/E2+ = 3.3) realized only when the intruder level lies above the normal parity levels.

## Method
The authors performed SD-pair shell model (SDPM) calculations using a Hamiltonian with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction term and a BCS-determined basis of collective S and D pairs. They varied the single-particle energy epsilon of the intruder level from -0.15 MeV to 0.15 MeV while keeping normal parity levels degenerate, with pairing strength fixed at 0.01 MeV and quadrupole strength kappa at 0.1 MeV/r0^4, for systems with N_pi = N_nu = 3 nucleon pairs in each major shell.

## Key numbers
- For the ds-shell (8-20 region), rotational structure with E4+/E2+ = 3.30 is realized when the intruder level is at epsilon = 0.07 MeV
- For the 20-50 shell, rotational E4+/E2+ = 3.30 occurs at epsilon = 0.06 MeV
- For the 50-82 shell, rotational E4+/E2+ = 3.30 occurs at epsilon = 0.05 MeV
- For the 82-126 shell, rotational E4+/E2+ = 3.30 occurs at epsilon = 0.02 MeV
- For the 126-184 shell, rotational E4+/E2+ = 3.30 occurs at epsilon = 0.00 MeV (degenerate with normal parity levels)
- When the intruder level is at epsilon = -0.15 MeV (lowest), the E4+/E2+ ratio ranges from 1.65 (ds-shell) to 2.69 (50-82 shell), far from rotational
- The B(E2) ratio for 4+ to 2+ transition over 2+ to 0+ transition reaches 1.350 for degenerate cases, close to the IBM SU(3) limit value of 1.349
- Effective charges used: e_pi = 1.5e and e_nu = 0.75e

## Context
This work extends the SD-pair shell model (SDPM) studies of Luo, Zhao, and collaborators on O(6)-limit nuclei, and directly tests the assumption from the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) and pseudo-SU(3) model that intruder levels can be constrained to seniority zero. The results contradict claims from mean-field theories (Ref. 19 in the paper) that intruder levels play a dominant role in determining deformation. The findings enable systematic predictions for where rotational bands should appear across major shells, and provide guidance for incorporating intruder levels into truncated shell model calculations. The authors note that for mixed-shell systems such as protons in 50-82 with neutrons in 82-126, rotational behavior is predicted even with intruder levels close to normal parity levels, consistent with experimental observations in nuclei like 160Gd.
