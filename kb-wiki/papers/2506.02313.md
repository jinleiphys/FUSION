# Euclidean-Monte-Carlo-informed ground-state preparation for quantum simulation of scalar field theory

> Quantum simulators offer great potential for investigating dynamical properties of quantum field theories. However, preparing accurate non-trivial initial states for these simulations is challenging. Classical Euclidean-time Monte-Carlo methods provide a wealth of information about states of interest to quantum simulations. Thus, it is desirable to facilitate state preparation on quantum simulators using this information. To this end, we present a fully classical pipeline for generating efficient quantum circuits for preparing the ground state of an interacting scalar field theory in 1+1 dimensions. The first element of this pipeline is a variational ansatz family based on the stellar hierarchy for bosonic quantum systems. The second element of this pipeline is the classical moment-optimization procedure that augments the standard variational energy minimization by penalizing deviations in selected sets of ground-state correlation functions (i.e., moments). The values of ground-state moments are sourced from classical Euclidean methods. The resulting states yield comparable ground-state energy estimates but exhibit distinct correlations and local non-Gaussianity. The third element of this pipeline is translating the moment-optimized ansatz into an efficient quantum circuit with an asymptotic cost that is polynomial in system size. This work opens the way to systematically applying classically obtained knowledge of states to prepare accurate initial states in quantum field theories of interest in nature.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully classical pipeline for generating efficient quantum circuits to prepare the ground state of an interacting scalar field theory in 1+1 dimensions, using Euclidean Monte-Carlo data to inform a variational ansatz optimization; the resulting states yield comparable ground-state energy estimates but exhibit distinct correlations and local non-Gaussianity compared to standard energy-minimized variational states.

## Method

The authors construct a variational ansatz based on the stellar hierarchy for bosonic quantum systems, representing the ground state as a Gaussian unitary (squeezing operator) acting on a finite-rank core state composed of even Fock states. They perform a classical moment-optimization procedure that augments standard variational energy minimization by adding penalty terms for deviations between the ansatz's expectation values of selected ground-state correlation functions (moments) and those sourced from classical Euclidean path-integral Monte Carlo computations. The optimized ansatz is then translated into an efficient quantum circuit with asymptotic cost polynomial in system size, using a classical algorithm that maps the bosonic degrees of freedom to qubit-based quantum simulator degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The single-mode (0+1)D Hamiltonian is given by $\hat H^{(0+1)}_\sigma = \frac{\pih^2}{2} + \sigma\frac{\phih^2}{2} + \lambda \phih^4$, where $\lambda \geq 0$ and $\sigma = \pm 1$, with the double-well case $\sigma=-1$ having minima at $\phi = \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\lambda}}$ and barrier height $V(\phi=0)=\frac{1}{16\lambda}$.
* The single-mode finite-rank ansatz $\ket{\psi}_R = \hat S(r) (c_0\ket{0} + c_2\ket{2} + \ldots + c_R\ket{R})$ is parametrized by $2+\frac{R}{2}$ real parameters $(r,c_0,c_2,\ldots,c_{R})$, where $R$ is even.
* The fidelity lower bound is given by $F(\vec{\Lambda}) \geq 1 - \delta_E(\vec{\Lambda})$, where $\delta_E(\vec{\Lambda})$ is the ratio of energy discrepancy to the spectral gap $\Delta E$.

## Context

This work extends the variational quantum eigensolver approach to ground-state preparation by incorporating classical Euclidean Monte-Carlo data, addressing a key bottleneck in quantum simulation of field theories identified by Jordan et al. (2011, 2012, 2018) and Marshall et al. (2015). The stellar hierarchy ansatz builds on the work of Chabaud et al. (2020, 2022) on classifying bosonic states by stellar rank, and the moment-optimization procedure provides a systematic way to use classically computed correlation functions to improve variational ansatzes beyond simple energy minimization. This enables the preparation of initial states for quantum simulations of real-time dynamics in scalar field theories, which are otherwise hindered by sign problems in classical Monte-Carlo approaches.
