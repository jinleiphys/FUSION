# Particle production by $γ$-$γ$ interactions in future electron-ion colliders

> The particle production in photon-photon ($γγ$) interactions present in electron-ion collisions is investigated. We present calculations for the total cross sections and event rates related to the production of light mesons [$η, η^\prime, f_0$ and $f_2$], charmonium [$η_c$ and $χ_c$] and charmoniumlike [$X(3915), X(3940), X(4140)$ and $X(6900)$] states, considering the EIC, EicC, LHeC and FCC-eh energies. Our predictions demonstrate that experimental studies of these processes are feasible and useful to constrain the properties of light mesons and quarkonium states and shed some light on the configuration of the considered charmoniumlike states.

## Key claim

The authors claim that experimental studies of particle production via photon-photon interactions in future electron-ion colliders are feasible and useful to constrain the properties of light mesons, charmonium states, and charmoniumlike exotic states, but the feasibility depends strongly on the specific particle mass, the collider energy, and the assumed two-photon decay widths (especially for the exotic X states, where theoretical values are used).

## Method

The authors used the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to factorize the cross section for particle production in electron-nucleus collisions into a product of the photon fluxes from the electron and the nucleus, multiplied by the photon-photon production cross section. The photon flux from the electron was calculated using the standard EPA formula with a maximum momentum transfer of 1.0 GeV^2, while the nuclear photon flux was estimated using a realistic form factor derived from a Wood-Saxon charge density distribution. The photon-photon production cross section was estimated at the Born level using Low's formula, which expresses the cross section as a function of the two-photon decay width and the mass and spin of the produced particle.

## Key numbers

* Light meson production cross sections at the EIC (eAu, 18 GeV on 100 GeV): eta(547) = 2126.88 nb, eta'(958) = 2126.32 nb, f0(980) = 125.59 nb, f2(1270) = 2096.30 nb.
* Charmonium production cross sections at the EIC: eta_c(1S) = 23.41 nb, chi_c0(1P) = 85.20 nb, chi_c2(1P) = 6.16 nb, eta_c(2S) = 2.44 nb.
* Charmoniumlike state production cross sections at the EIC: X(3915) = 0.27 nb, X(3940) = 0.44 nb, X(4140) = 0.65 nb, X(6900) = 5.37 nb.
* After applying kinematical cuts (photon pseudorapidities |eta| <= 3.5 and photon energies >= 1.0 GeV) and decay to two photons, event rates per year at the EIC for light mesons range from 0.83e9 (eta') to 8.86e9 (eta), while for charmonium states they range from 0.16e6 (chi_c2) to 24.00e6 (chi_c0).
* For the charmoniumlike states after cuts at the EIC, event rates per year are: X(3915) = 0.38e3, X(3940) = 0.34e3, X(4140) = 0.46e3, X(6900) = 0.21e6.
* The light-by-light (LbL) scattering background, estimated within an invariant mass window of M_P +/- 2.4% M_P, is smaller than the signal for light mesons but larger than the signal for most charmoniumlike states, except X(6900).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of particle production via photon-photon interactions at the LHC (e.g., Goncalves (2018), Goncalves (2019), Xie (2020), Biloshytskyi (2022)) to the future electron-ion colliders EIC (BNL), EicC (China), LHeC (CERN), and FCC-eh (CERN). The calculations enable future experimental searches for exotic charmoniumlike states and provide a baseline for separating the gamma-gamma production channel from the odderon production channel in pseudoscalar charmonium production, which is relevant for testing the odderon prediction of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) as discussed by Dumitru (2019) and Benic (2023).
