# Isospin non-equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> We study the equilibration of isospin degree of freedom in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions using an isospin-dependent BUU model. It is found that there exists a transition from the isospin equilibration at low energies to non-equilibration at high energies as the beam energy varies across the Fermi energy in central, asymmetric heavy-ion collisions. At beam energies around 55 MeV/nucleon, the composite system in thermal equilibrium but isospin non-equilibrium breaks up into two primary hot residues with N/Z ratios closely related to those of the target and projectile respectively. The decay of these forward-backward moving residues results in the strong isospin asymmetry in space and the dependence of the isotopic composition of fragments on the N/Z ratios of the target and projectile. These features are in good agreement with those found recently in experiments at NSCL/MSU and TAMU, implications of these findings are discussed.

# Isospin non-equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

## Key claim
Using an isospin-dependent BUU model, the authors find a transition from isospin equilibration to non-equilibration as beam energy increases across the Fermi energy in central, asymmetric heavy-ion collisions. The transition is conditional on the beam energy: at 55 MeV/nucleon the composite system reaches thermal equilibrium but remains in isospin non-equilibrium, breaking into two primary residues with N/Z ratios tied to the target and projectile respectively.

## Method
The authors employed an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model incorporating isospin dependence through both the elementary nucleon-nucleon cross sections (using experimental cross sections where neutron-proton collisions are about three times more frequent than neutron-neutron or proton-proton collisions) and the nuclear mean field with a symmetry term of strength C = 32 MeV and a soft equation of state (compressibility K = 200 MeV). They simulated head-on collisions of Ar+Ni at beam energies of 25, 35, 45, and 55 MeV/nucleon, tracking density contours, quadrupole moments, and neutron/proton numbers on each side of the reaction plane over time up to 300 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- At E_beam/A = 55 MeV, the forward-moving residue has an average N/Z ratio of about 7.2/6.0 and an excitation energy of about 8.6 MeV/nucleon.
- At E_beam/A = 55 MeV, the backward-moving residue has an average N/Z ratio of about 21/18 and an excitation energy of about 6.8 MeV/nucleon.
- The symmetry term strength used in the mean field is C = 32 MeV.
- The nuclear compressibility used is K = 200 MeV (soft equation of state).
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section for neutron-proton collisions is about three times that of neutron-neutron or proton-proton collisions at the energies of interest.

## Context
This work extends experimental observations at NSCL/MSU and TAMU (Yennello et al., 1995) showing that at E_beam/A = 25 and 35 MeV isotopic ratios depend only on the combined system N/Z, indicating isospin equilibration, while at 53 MeV the ratios depend separately on target and projectile N/Z. The study contradicts predictions from statistical models assuming isospin equilibrium and from the intranuclear cascade code ISABEL (Yariv, 1979), which both failed to reproduce the entrance-channel effects seen experimentally. The results enable hybrid model studies coupling BUU with statistical decay codes to quantitatively compare with fragment data, and suggest that assuming isospin equilibrium within each primary residue (but not globally) may be a reasonable approximation at energies around 55 MeV/nucleon.
