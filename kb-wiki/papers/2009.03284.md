# Chiral phase transition and kaon-to-pion ratios in the entanglement SU(3) PNJL model

> Within the three-flavor PNJL and EPNJL chiral quark models we have obtained pseudoscalar meson properties in quark matter at finite temperature $T$ and baryochemical potential $μ_B$. We compare the meson pole (Breit-Wigner) approximation with the Beth-Uhlenbeck (BU) approach that takes into account the continuum of quark-antiquark scattering states when determining the partial densities of pions and kaons. We evaluate the kaon-to-pion ratios along the (pseudo-)critical line in the $T-μ_B$ plane as a proxy for the chemical freezeout line, whereby the variable $x=T/μ_B$ is introduced that corresponds to the conserved entropy per baryon as initial condition for the heavy-ion collision experiments. We present a comparison with the experimental pattern of kaon-to-pion ratios within the BU approach and using $x$-dependent pion and strange quark potentials. A sharp "horn" effect in the energy dependence $K^+/π^+$ ratio is explained by the enhanced pion production at energies above $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8$ GeV, when the system enters the regime of meson dominance. This effect is in line with the enhancement of low-momentum pion spectra that is discussed as a precursor of the pion Bose condensation and entails the occurrence of a nonequilibrium pion chemical potential of the order of the pion mass. We elucidate that the horn effect is not related to the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The sharp "horn" effect in the energy dependence of the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio is explained by enhanced pion production at energies above $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8$ GeV when the system enters a regime of meson dominance, and the authors explicitly state that this effect is not related to the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

Within the three-flavor PNJL and EPNJL chiral quark models, the authors obtained pseudoscalar meson properties in quark matter at finite temperature $T$ and baryochemical potential $\mu_B$ by comparing the meson pole (Breit-Wigner) approximation with the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach that accounts for the continuum of quark-antiquark scattering states when determining partial densities of pions and kaons. They evaluated kaon-to-pion ratios along the (pseudo-)critical line in the $T-\mu_B$ plane using the variable $x=T/\mu_B$ as a proxy for the chemical freezeout line, and introduced $x$-dependent pion and strange quark potentials within the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature of the chiral crossover transition at $\mu_B = 0$ GeV in the PNJL model: $T_c = 0.218$ GeV, compared to the lattice QCD result of $T_c = 156.5 \pm 1.5$ MeV
* Pseudocritical temperature in the EPNJL model at low density: $T_c \sim 0.179$ GeV
* Mott transition temperatures: $T^\pi_{\rm Mott} = 0.232$ GeV for pions and $T^K_{\rm Mott} = 0.230$ GeV for kaons
* Critical baryon density for first-order phase transition joining quark matter phase to vacuum: $n_c = 2.9\, n_0$, where nuclear saturation density $n_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
* Model parameters: current quark masses $m_{0 u} = m_{0 d} = 5.5$ MeV, $m_{0 s} = 0.131$ GeV, cutoff $\Lambda = 0.652$ GeV, couplings $g_D \Lambda^5 = 10.592$, $g_S \Lambda^2 = 1.828$
* Vector coupling used: $g_V = 0.6\, g_S$
* Strange quark chemical potential set as $\mu_s = 0.55\mu_u$ with $\mu_u = \mu_d$

## Context

This work extends the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach developed for the two-flavor case by Hufner (1994), Wergieluk (2012), and Blaschke (2013) to the SU(3) PNJL model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction, building on the authors' prior work (Dubinin 2016). The study addresses the long-standing puzzle of the "horn" effect in $K^+/\pi^+$ ratios observed by the NA49 experiment at CERN SPS, which was originally predicted by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1998) based on a first-order phase transition assumption. The authors' explanation via enhanced pion production and a nonequilibrium pion chemical potential offers an alternative to the parton-hadron-string-dynamics (PHSD) transport approach by Palmese (2016) and Moreau (2017), and enables further investigation of the system-size dependence recently observed by the NA61 experiment in Ar+Sc collisions (Gazdzicki 2020).
