# Thermodynamic Geometry of Strongly Interacting Matter

> The thermodynamic geometry formalism is applied to strongly interacting matter to estimate the deconfinement temperature. The curved thermodynamic metric for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is evaluated on the basis of lattice data, whereas the hadron resonance gas model is used for the hadronic sector. Since the deconfinement transition is a crossover, the geometric criterion used to define the \mbox{(pseudo-)critical} temperature, as a function of the baryonchemical potential $μ_B$, is $R(T,μ_B)=0$, where $R$ is the scalar curvature. The (pseudo-)critical temperature, $T_c$, resulting from QCD thermodynamic geometry is in good agreement with lattice and phenomenological freeze-out temperature estimates. The crossing temperature, $T_h$, evaluated by the hadron resonance gas, which suffers of some model dependence, is larger than $T_c$ (about $20\%$) signaling remnants of confinement above the transition.

## Key claim

The deconfinement (pseudo-)critical temperature in QCD can be estimated by applying the thermodynamic geometry criterion $R(T,\mu_B)=0$, where $R$ is the scalar curvature, and the results agree with lattice and freeze-out estimates within about 10%; however, the crossing temperature $T_h$ from the hadron resonance gas model is about 20% larger than $T_c$, which may signal remnants of confinement above the transition but is also model-dependent.

## Method

The thermodynamic metric for QCD was constructed from lattice data for the pressure expanded as a power series in $\gamma^2 = (\mu_B/T)^2$ up to order $\gamma^6$, and the scalar curvature $R$ was computed via the two-dimensional formula involving derivatives of the thermodynamic potential $\phi = P/T$. For the hadronic sector, the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in the Boltzmann approximation was used, with the pressure expressed as a sum over meson and baryon contributions. The (pseudo-)critical temperature was determined by solving $R(T,\mu_B)=0$ for both the QCD and HRG branches.

## Key numbers

- At $\mu_B=0$, the lattice-based (pseudo-)critical temperature is $T_c = 154 \pm 9$ MeV, from chiral susceptibility results cited in the paper.
- The crossing temperature $T_h$ from the HRG model is about 20% larger than $T_c$ at the same baryochemical potential.
- The scalar curvature $R$ was evaluated for $\mu_B = 0$ MeV, $\mu_B = 80$ MeV, and $\mu_B = 135$ MeV, for both the QCD and HRG sectors.
- The QCD-based $T_c(\mu_B)$ results are shown for $r=0$ (or $r=0.5$) and for $n_S=0$, $n_Q/n_B=0.4$, compared with lattice data and freeze-out temperatures from ALICE and STAR collaborations.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic geometry formalism, previously applied to fluids and black holes, to the QCD deconfinement crossover, using the $R=0$ criterion rather than the $R$-crossing method used for first-order transitions. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Bazavov et al. (2017) and with freeze-out temperatures from the statistical hadronization model (SHM) as used by the ALICE and STAR collaborations. The finding that $T_h > T_c$ supports the interpretation that remnants of confinement persist above $T_c$, consistent with lattice simulations by Karsch and by Cea, quasiparticle approaches by Mannarelli et al., and confinement mechanism studies.
