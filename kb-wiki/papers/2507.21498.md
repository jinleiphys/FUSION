# Heavy flavored hydrogen molecule systems

> This study provides a comprehensive analysis of $S$-wave exotic hydrogen-like three-body systems ($ppμ^-$, $ppτ^-$, $μ^-μ^-p$, $τ^-τ^-p$, $pμ^-τ^-$) with spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$, and four-body systems ($ppμ^-μ^-$, $ppτ^-τ^-$) with $J^P = 0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$. We use complex scaling and Gaussian expansion methods to solve the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation and obtain possible bound and quasi-bound states. The resulting binding energies range from $-33.8$~keV to $-340$~eV. Notably, we present the first theoretical estimation of the bound-state energy levels of $ppμ^-μ^-$ and $ppτ^-τ^-$, which is of significant importance for understanding exotic few-body Coulomb systems. We further analyze spin configurations and root-mean-square radii to elucidate the spatial structure of these bound and quasi-bound states. Our results reveal that $K$-type spatial configurations play a crucial role in accurately describing bound and quasi-bound states in the hydrogen-molecule-like systems $ppμ^-μ^-$ and $ppτ^-τ^-$. Incorporating $K$-type configurations significantly alters the mass spectra of these states. Future muon colliders and muon facilities may offer promising platforms for the possible copious production of such heavy flavored hydrogen molecules and molecular ions. For instance, scattering processes such as $2μ^- + \mathrm{H_2} \to \mathrm{H_{2μ}} + 2e^-$, $μ^- + \mathrm{H_2} \to \mathrm{H_{μe}} + e^-$, and $μ^- + \mathrm{H_2^+} \to \mathrm{H_{2μ}^+} + e^-$ could be utilized, facilitating detailed studies of intriguing states such as $\mathrm{H_{2μ}}$, $\mathrm{H_{μe}}$, and $\mathrm{H_{2μ}^+}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that bound and quasi-bound states exist in a wide range of exotic hydrogen-like three- and four-body systems composed of protons, muons, and tau leptons, with the sole exception of the $p\mu^-\tau^-$ system where no bound state is found. The authors present the first theoretical estimation of bound-state energy levels for the four-body systems $pp\mu^-\mu^-$ and $pp\tau^-\tau^-$, but note that these results depend on including $K$-type spatial configurations which significantly alter the mass spectra.

## Method

The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation using the complex scaling method (CSM) combined with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for $S$-wave three-body systems ($pp\mu^-$, $pp\tau^-$, $\mu^-\mu^-p$, $\tau^-\tau^-p$, $p\mu^-\tau^-$) with $J^P = 1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$, and four-body systems ($pp\mu^-\mu^-$, $pp\tau^-\tau^-$) with $J^P = 0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$. Wave functions were constructed using complete spin configurations and spatial components with various Jacobi coordinate structures, including $K$-type configurations for the four-body systems. The interaction was modeled as a pure Coulomb potential with the fine-structure constant.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for all systems range from $-33.8$ keV to $-340$ eV relative to the three-body or four-body thresholds.
- $pp\mu^-$ bound state: binding energy $-2780$ eV, with $r_{pp}^{\rm rms} = 0.90$ pm and $r_{p\mu^-}^{\rm rms} = 0.71$ pm.
- $pp\tau^-$ bound state: binding energy $-17078$ eV, with $r_{pp}^{\rm rms} = 0.22$ pm and $r_{p\tau^-}^{\rm rms} = 0.16$ pm.
- $\mu^-\mu^-p$ bound state: binding energy $-2655$ eV, with $r_{\mu^-\mu^-}^{\rm rms} = 1.46$ pm and $r_{\mu^-p}^{\rm rms} = 1.02$ pm.
- $\tau^-\tau^-p$ bound state: binding energy $-17272$ eV, with $r_{\tau^-\tau^-}^{\rm rms} = 0.19$ pm and $r_{\tau^-p}^{\rm rms} = 0.14$ pm.
- $pp\mu^-\mu^-$ bound state ($J^P = 0^+$): binding energy $-33.8$ keV.
- $pp\tau^-\tau^-$ bound state ($J^P = 0^+$): binding energy $-33.8$ keV.
- $p\mu^-(1S)$ atomic binding energy: $-2.53$ keV; $p\tau^-(1S)$ atomic binding energy: $-16.35$ keV.
- $pp\mu^-$ first quasi-bound state: binding energy $-793$ eV (zero width); $pp\tau^-$ first quasi-bound state: $-4807-8.45i$ eV.
- $\mu^-\mu^-p$ first quasi-bound state ($J^P=1/2^+$): $-749-3.60i$ eV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of exotic hydrogen-like systems, particularly the $pp\mu^-$ molecular ion first predicted by Vesman (1967) and later refined by variational methods and adiabatic approximations. The results for $pp\mu^-$ and $\mu^-\mu^-p$ bound states are consistent with calculations by Liverts (2013), Kilic (2004), and Frolov (1993). The study is motivated by the proton radius puzzle from the CREMA collaboration's muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl, 2013) and draws methodological inspiration from studies of the $T_{cc}(3875)$ tetraquark (LHCb, 2021). The findings enable future experimental searches at muon colliders and muon facilities, where scattering processes such as $2\mu^- + \mathrm{H_2} \to \mathrm{H_{2\mu}} + 2e^-$ could produce these exotic molecules.
