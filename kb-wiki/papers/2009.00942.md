# GW190814: Circumstantial Evidence for Up-Down Quark Star

> Within a confining quark matter model which considers phenomenologically the quark confinement and asymptotic freedom as well as the chiral symmetry restoration and quark deconfinement at high baryon density, we find that if the up-down quark matter ($ud$QM) is more stable than nuclear matter and strange quark matter (SQM), the maximum mass of static quark stars with $ud$QM is $2.87M_{\odot}$ under agreement with both the constraints on star tidal deformability from gravitational wave signal GW170817 and the mass-radius of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 measured by NICER. In contrast, the conventional strange quark star with the SQM that is more stable than nuclear matter while the nuclear matter is more stable than $ud$QM, has a maximum static mass of only $1.87M_{\odot}$ and its radius significantly deviates from NICER's constraint. Our results thus provide circumstantial evidence suggesting the recently reported GW190814's secondary component with a mass of $2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09}M_\odot$ could be an up-down quark star.

# GW190814: Circumstantial Evidence for Up-Down Quark Star

## Key claim
Within a confining quark matter model, the secondary component of GW190814 (mass 2.59 solar masses) could be an up-down quark star (udQS) rather than a conventional strange quark star, provided that up-down quark matter is more stable than both nuclear matter and strange quark matter. The claim is circumstantial because it depends on the specific stability hierarchy of quark matter phases and on satisfying tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and mass-radius constraints from NICER simultaneously.

## Method
The authors employ a confining quark matter (CQM) model that incorporates quark confinement, asymptotic freedom, chiral symmetry restoration, and quark deconfinement at high baryon density via a modified screened Richardson potential. They vary two free parameters (ν_ud and ν_s) controlling the density dependence of quark masses to generate equations of state for both conventional strange quark stars (SQS) and up-down quark stars (udQS). The resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities are compared against constraints from GW170817, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the mass of GW190814's secondary component.

## Key numbers
- Maximum static mass of up-down quark star (udQS) consistent with all constraints: 2.87 solar masses (parameter set udQS2.87)
- Maximum static mass of conventional strange quark star (SQS): 1.87 solar masses (parameter set SQS1.87)
- GW190814 secondary component mass: 2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09} solar masses (90% confidence level)
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Λ̃ = 300^{+420}_{-230} (90% highest posterior density interval) for binary mass ratio q = 0.73-1.00 and chirp mass M_c = 1.186 solar masses
- udQS2.87 predicts Λ̃(q=0.73) = 720 for M_c = 1.186 solar masses and Λ(1.4 solar masses) = 677
- udQS3.30 (maximum mass 3.30 solar masses, without tidal deformability constraint) predicts Λ̃(q=0.73) = 1260, strongly violating GW170817 constraint
- Center baryon density for maximum mass configuration: 0.81 fm^{-3} (udQS2.87), 0.67 fm^{-3} (udQS3.30), 1.60 fm^{-3} (SQS1.87)
- Surface baryon density for maximum mass configuration: 0.33 fm^{-3} (udQS2.87), 0.28 fm^{-3} (udQS3.30), 0.59 fm^{-3} (SQS1.87)

## Context
This work extends the hypothesis of absolutely stable up-down quark matter proposed by Holdom et al. (2018) and subsequent studies of up-down quark stars by Wang et al. (2019), Zhang et al. (2019, 2020), and Ren et al. (2020). It directly addresses the nature of the GW190814 secondary, which falls in the mass gap between neutron stars and black holes. Previous work by Zhou et al. (2019) found a maximum neutron star mass of approximately 2.3 solar masses from combined constraints including heavy-ion collision flow data, while studies by Fatemi et al. (2020), Essick et al. (2020), and Tews et al. (2020) suggested the secondary should be a black hole. The present results enable future searches for up-down quark matter nuggets (udlets) in nuclear reactions, supernova nucleosynthesis, and binary quark star mergers, and motivate self-consistent calculations of surface tension within the CQM model.
