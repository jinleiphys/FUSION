# Particles Associated with Omega Produced at Intermediate p_T

> The dual observation of the Omega production in central Au+Au collisions having both an exponential p_T distribution and also associated particles above the background has been referred to as the Omega puzzle. We give a quantitative description of how that puzzle can be understood in terms of phantom jets, where only ridges without peaks are produced to give rise to both the Omega trigger and its associated particles. In the framework of recombination of thermal partons we are able to reproduce both the Δϕdistribution and the trigger-momentum dependence of the yield of the associated particles. We make predictions on other observables that can be checked by further analyses of the data.

## Key claim
The "Omega puzzle" (the dual observation of an exponential p_T distribution for Omega and the presence of associated particles above background) can be resolved by the concept of phantom jets, where only ridges without peaks are produced, provided that the trigger momentum is low (2.5 < p_T^trig < 4.5 GeV/c) and the associated particle momentum is even lower (1.5 < p_T^assoc < p_T^trig), making the ridge component dominate over any jet peak.

## Method
The authors used the recombination model for thermal partons to calculate the background and ridge contributions to the associated particle distribution in Omega-triggered events. They computed the background from thermal parton recombination using previously determined parameters (C_0 = 23.2 (GeV/c)^{-1}, T_0 = 0.317 GeV/c) and then calculated the ridge contribution using an enhanced thermal source with temperature T = 0.332 GeV/c and a Gaussian function H(Δφ) to describe the azimuthal dependence. The formalism was applied to central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV with trigger momentum window 2.5 < p_T^trig < 4.5 GeV/c and associated particle range 1.5 < p_T^assoc < p_T^trig.

## Key numbers
- Background height: dN^bg/dΔφ = 6.9, matching the experimental value from STAR data.
- Thermal parton parameters: C_0 = 23.2 (GeV/c)^{-1}, T_0 = 0.317 GeV/c.
- Omega spectrum temperature: T_s = 0.33 GeV/c.
- Enhanced thermal source temperature: T = 0.332 GeV/c (15 MeV above T_0).
- Two parameter sets for the ridge calculation: (a) H_0 = 0.795, σ = 3.5; (b) H_0 = 0.790, σ = 3.7.
- Predicted ridge height in Δη: dN^R/dΔη = 0.065 (case a) and 0.054 (case b) for I = 1 in Δφ.
- Effective trigger momentum for the background calculation: ⟨p_1⟩ = 2.8 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously applied to pion, kaon, and proton production by Hwa and collaborators (2004-2005) to address the Omega puzzle identified by the STAR collaboration. The phantom jet concept was suggested earlier by Hwa (2006) as a solution. The paper builds on the ridge formalism developed for non-strange triggers by Chiu and Hwa (2005) and applies it to Omega-triggered events, where strange shower partons are suppressed. The results enable predictions for the Δη distribution of associated particles and for phi-meson triggered events, which can be tested by further STAR data analysis.
