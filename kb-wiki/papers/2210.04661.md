# Charmonium transport in the high-$μ_B$ medium

> We employ the transport model coupled with hydrodynamic equations to study the charmonium dissociation and regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with the large baryon chemical potential in Au-Au collisions at the energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ ($39$, $14.5$, $7.7$) GeV. The baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ is encoded in both Debye mass characterizing the heavy-quark potential and also the equation of state (EoS) of the bulk medium respectively. After considering $μ_B$-corrections in both heavy quarkonium and the QGP medium, we calculate the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of charmonium. And find the $μ_B$ influence on charmonium production at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 and 14.5 GeV is negligible, while the $R_{AA}$ of charmonium is reduced considering $μ_B$ influence at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV Au-Au collisions. It is crucial for studying charmonium production in low energy and also fixed-target heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find that the baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$ has a negligible effect on charmonium production at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 and 14.5 GeV, but reduces the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of charmonium by almost 30% in central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV, with the condition that this reduction only becomes significant when the ratio $\mu_B/T$ is large, specifically at low temperatures and high baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors couple a transport model (Boltzmann equation) with hydrodynamic equations (MUSIC model) to study charmonium dissociation and regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39, 14.5, and 7.7 GeV. The baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$ is encoded in both the Debye mass (which modifies the heavy-quark potential via the Cornell potential parametrization) and the equation of state (NEOS-B) of the bulk medium, with the in-medium binding energies of charmonium states ($J/\psi$, $\chi_c$, $\psi^\prime$) calculated via the two-body Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV, the baryon chemical potential reaches $\mu_B\sim420$ MeV in the central region of Au-Au collisions.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ for deconfinement phase transition at finite $\mu_B$ is determined to be around 0.165, 0.156, and 0.140 GeV for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39, 14.5, and 7.7 GeV respectively.
- The initial time $\tau_0$ of the hot medium is taken as 1.3, 2.2, and 3.6 fm/c for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39, 14.5, and 7.7 GeV respectively.
- The Debye mass can be increased by around 50% at $T_c$ after including the $\mu_B/T$-correction at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of charmonium is reduced by almost 30% in most central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV when considering $\mu_B$ influence.
- The mean transverse momentum square of charmonium at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39, 14.5, and 7.7 GeV is taken as $\langle p_T^2\rangle=$ 1.46, 0.77, and 0.45 (GeV)$^2$ respectively.
- The differential cross section of $J/\psi$ is taken as $d\sigma^{J/\psi}_{pp}/dy=$ 150.7, 37.6, and 17.2 nb in central rapidity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39, 14.5, and 7.7 GeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions (Matsui 1986, Satz 2005) to the high baryon density regime relevant for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC (Bzdak 2019), NA60+ at SPS (Dahms), and CBM at FAIR (CBM 2016). It builds on prior theoretical work using Hard Thermal and Dense Loop theory (Lafferty 2019, Huang 2021, Schneider 2003) and time-dependent Schrodinger equation studies (Tong 2022) by incorporating a more realistic transport model coupled with hydrodynamics that includes $\mu_B$ corrections in both the heavy quark potential and the equation of state. The results enable quantitative predictions for charmonium production in low-energy and fixed-target heavy-ion collisions where baryon chemical potential effects become significant.
