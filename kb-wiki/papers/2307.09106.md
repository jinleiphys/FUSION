# Theoretical study on $Λ_c^+ \to ΛK^+\bar{K}^0$ decay and $Ξ^*(1690)$ resonance

> We present a theoretical study of $Ξ^*(1690)$ resonance in the $Λ_c^+ \to ΛK^+ \bar{K}^0$ decay, where the weak interaction part proceeds through the Cabibbo-favored process $c \to s + u\bar{d}$. Next, the intermediate two mesons and one baryon state can be constructed with a pair of $q\bar{q}$ with the vacuum quantum numbers. Finally, the $Ξ^*(1690)$ is mainly produced from the final state interactions of $\bar{K}Λ$ in coupled channels, and it is shown in the $\bar{K}Λ$ invariant mass distribution. Besides, the scalar meson $a_0(980)$ and nucleon excited state $N^*(1535)$ are also taken into account in the decaying channels $K^+\bar{K}^0$ and $K^+Λ$, respectively. Within model parameters, the $K^+ \bar{K}^0$, $\bar{K}^0 Λ$ and $K^+ Λ$ invariant mass distributions are calculated, and it is found that our theoretical results can reproduce well the experimental measurements, especially for the clear peak around $1690$ MeV in the $\bar{K}Λ$ spectrum. The proposed weak decay process $Λ_c^+ \to ΛK^+ \bar{K}^0$ and the interaction mechanism can provide valuable information on the nature of the $Ξ^*(1690)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The $\Xi^*(1690)$ resonance observed in the $\Lambda_c^+ \to \Lambda K^+ \bar{K}^0$ decay can be described either as a dynamically generated state from meson-baryon final state interactions in coupled channels (Model I) or as a Breit-Wigner resonance (Model II), with both models reproducing the experimental data reasonably well, though the dynamically generated picture yields a narrower width (5.52 MeV) than the PDG average (20 MeV) and better matches the Belle branching fraction ratio.

## Method

The authors model the Cabibbo-favored weak decay $c \to s + u\bar{d}$ followed by hadronization with a $q\bar{q}$ pair from the vacuum, producing intermediate pseudoscalar-meson–baryon ($PB$) and pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar ($PP$) pairs. The final state interactions of $\bar{K}\Lambda$, $K^+\bar{K}^0$, and $K^+\Lambda$ in coupled channels are treated within the chiral unitary approach via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, dynamically generating the $\Xi^*(1690)$, $a_0(980)$, and $N^*(1535)$ resonances. Two models are fitted to the BABAR invariant mass distributions: Model I uses the chiral unitary approach for $\Xi^*(1690)$ with four coupled channels ($\pi\Xi$, $\bar{K}\Lambda$, $\bar{K}\Sigma$, $\eta\Xi$), while Model II treats $\Xi^*(1690)$ as a Breit-Wigner resonance with fixed mass 1690 MeV and width 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of $\Xi^*(1690)$ from Model I: $M_R = 1685.83 + i2.76$ MeV, corresponding to mass $M_{\Xi^*(1690)} = 1685.83$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Xi^*(1690)} = 5.52$ MeV (output from fit)
- Model II uses input $M_{\Xi^*(1690)} = 1690$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\Xi^*(1690)} = 20$ MeV (from PDG)
- $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.51$ for Model I and $1.56$ for Model II, each with 5 free parameters fitted to 79 data points
- Branching fraction ratio $\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c^+ \to K^+ \Xi^*(1690) \to K^+ \bar{K}^0 \Lambda) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c^+ \to \Lambda K^+ \bar{K}^0) = 0.28$ for Model I and $0.07$ for Model II; the Model I value agrees with the Belle collaboration measurement of $0.26 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.03$
- Fitted parameters include $V_1 = 3.60 \pm 0.55$ MeV$^{-1}$ (Model I) and $V_2 = -0.89 \pm 0.67$ MeV$^{-1}$ (Model I); subtraction constants $a_{\bar{K}\Sigma} = -1.99 \pm 0.09$ and $a_{\eta\Xi} = -3.53 \pm 0.29$ (Model I)

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach studies of $\Xi^*(1690)$ as a dynamically generated meson-baryon molecular state (Garcia-Recio 2003, Gamermann 2011, Sekihara 2015, Khemchandani 2016) to the weak decay $\Lambda_c^+ \to \Lambda K^+ \bar{K}^0$, following the methodology used for $\Xi_c$ decays by Miyahara (2016). It directly confronts the theoretical predictions with the BABAR (2006) and Belle (2001) experimental data on this decay, and demonstrates that the dynamically generated $\Xi^*(1690)$ picture reproduces the branching fraction ratio measured by Belle, while the Breit-Wigner model does not. The results enable future precise measurements by BESIII, BelleII, and LHCb to discriminate between the two production mechanisms and clarify the nature of the $\Xi^*(1690)$ resonance.
