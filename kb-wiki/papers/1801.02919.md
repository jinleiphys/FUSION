# Three-neutron resonance study using transition operators

> Existing bound-state type calculations of three-neutron resonances yield contradicting results. A direct study of the three-neutron continuum using rigorous scattering equations with realistic potentials and search for possible resonances is aimed. Faddeev-type integral equations for three-neutron transition operators are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. The evolution of resonances is studied by enhancing the strength of the two-neutron interaction in partial waves with nonzero orbital momentum. Calculated three-neutron transition operators exhibit resonant behavior for sufficiently large enhancement factors; pole trajectories in the complex-energy energy plane are extracted from their energy dependence. However, the resonant behavior completely disappears for the physical interaction strength. There are no physically observable three-neutron resonant states consistent with presently accepted interaction models.

## Key claim

Using rigorous Faddeev-type scattering equations solved directly in the three-neutron continuum, no physically observable three-neutron resonant states exist for any tested realistic interaction model at physical interaction strength; resonant behavior only appears when the two-neutron interaction in higher partial waves is artificially enhanced by a factor of 3 or 4 above the physical value.

## Method

Faddeev-type integral equations for three-neutron transition operators (Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formulation) were solved numerically in momentum-space partial-wave framework, including two-neutron states with total angular momentum j < 3. The evolution of possible resonances was studied by enhancing the strength of the two-neutron interaction in all partial waves with nonzero orbital momentum (L >= 1) while keeping the physical strength in the 1S0 partial wave, and pole trajectories in the complex-energy plane were extracted from the energy dependence of transition operator matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For the SRG potential with higher-wave enhancement factor f = 6.2, a J^Pi = 3/2- resonance was found at (1.41 - 0.22i) MeV
- For the SRG potential with f = 5.6, the J^Pi = 3/2- resonance moved to (4.36 - 2.24i) MeV
- Bound dineutron in 3P2-3F2 partial wave appears at f = 7.24 (SRG) and f = 4.0 (Reid93)
- Bound dineutron in 3P0 partial wave appears at f = 7.97 (SRG) and f = 5.95 (Reid93)
- Bound trineutron for SRG potential occurs at f = 6.42 (J^Pi = 3/2-)
- For the SRG potential, trineutrons in J^Pi = 3/2+, 5/2+, and 5/2- states become bound at f = 6.71, 6.02, and 6.94 respectively
- For the attractive 3P1 enhancement scheme with SRG, 3P1 dineutron becomes bound at f = 6.47 while J^Pi = 1/2+, 1/2-, and 3/2- trineutrons become bound at f = 5.28, 5.48, and 5.44 respectively

## Context

This work directly contradicts the prediction by PhysRevLett.118.232501 (2017) of a three-neutron resonance about 1 MeV above threshold, which relied on bound-state type calculations with extrapolation to the continuum. The results are fully consistent with earlier complex-scaled Faddeev equation studies by PhysRevC.66.054001 (2002) and Lazauskas (2003) that found no physically observable three-neutron resonances. The extension of this transition operator approach to the four-neutron system is identified as the next important step to resolve the ongoing controversy regarding multineutron resonances.
