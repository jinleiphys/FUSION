# Microscopic approach of fission dynamics applied to fragment kinetic energy and mass distributions in 238U

> The collective dynamics of low energy fission in 238U is described within a time-dependent formalism based on the Gaussian Overlap Approximation of the time-dependent Generator Coordinate Method. The intrinsic deformed configurations of the nucleus are determined from the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov procedure employing the effective force D1S with constraints on the quadrupole and octupole moments. Fragment kinetic energy and mass distributions are calculated and compared with experimental evaluations. The effect of the collective dynamics along the fission paths and the influence of initial conditions on these distributions are analyzed and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully microscopic, time-dependent collective model based on the Gaussian Overlap Approximation of the Generator Coordinate Method, using only the D1S effective force without parameter readjustment, can reproduce the main features of fragment kinetic energy and mass distributions in low-energy fission of 238U, but the agreement is limited by the neglect of coupling between collective and intrinsic degrees of freedom and by the assumption of adiabaticity.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1S finite-range effective force to generate a two-dimensional potential energy surface and collective inertia tensor as functions of quadrupole (q20) and octupole (q30) deformation moments. They then solved a time-dependent Schrodinger-like equation derived from the Gaussian Overlap Approximation of the time-dependent Generator Coordinate Method, propagating collective wave functions from quasi-stationary states in the first well through the fission barrier to scission. Fragment mass distributions were obtained by time-integrating the flux of the collective wave function through a scission line defined by a neck density criterion of less than 0.01 nucleon/fm3.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 238U is deformed at q20 approximately 30 b, with a super-deformed minimum near q20 = 80 b
- Scission elongations range from q20max = 320 b for the most asymmetric fission (q30 = 44 b3/2) up to q20max = 550 b for symmetric fragmentation (q30 = 0 b3/2)
- The distance between fragment centers of charge ranges from d = 15.88 fm for AH = 134 to d = 20.27 fm for AH = 119
- The proton pairing gap at the top of the second barrier is 2Δp = 2.3 MeV, decreasing to 2Δp = 1.4 MeV at q20 = 180 b
- The neutron pairing gap at the top of the second barrier is 2Δn = 1.6 MeV
- Theoretical total kinetic energy values overestimate experimental data by approximately 6 percent in the region of most probable asymmetric fission
- The time step used for numerical propagation was Δt = 1.3 × 10-24 s
- The absorption region for eliminating wave function reflections extended from q20 = 800 b to q20 = 1300 b

## Context

This work extends the pioneering time-dependent Generator Coordinate Method calculations of Berger et al. (1984) for 240Pu to a full two-dimensional treatment of 238U fission, incorporating both quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom. It contrasts with purely static approaches such as the multi-dimensional potential energy surfaces of Moller et al. (2000) and the classical three-dimensional Langevin calculations of Karpov et al. (2001), by providing a fully microscopic quantum dynamical treatment. The results enable future studies of dissipation effects by quantifying where the adiabatic approximation breaks down, particularly through the calculated pairing gaps that indicate thresholds for intrinsic excitation.
