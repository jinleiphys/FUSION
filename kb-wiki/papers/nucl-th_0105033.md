# Offshell effects in electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron

> Offshell contributions to the electromagnetic nuclear current are evaluated within a nonrelativistic approach by incorporation one-pion loop contributions in time-ordered perturbation theory. By construction, the correct experimental onshell properties of the nucleon current are ensured so that only the genuine offshell effects appear as model dependent. For a qualitative assessment of such offshell effects, this model is applied to photodisintegration of the deuteron for photon energies up to 500 MeV. While at low energies offshell contributions are small, above 300 MeV they lead to sizeable effects in observables up to about 30 percent pointing to the necessity of incorporating such effects if one aims at theoretical predictions of high precision.

# Offshell effects in electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron

## Key claim

Offshell contributions to the electromagnetic nucleon current in deuteron photodisintegration, evaluated via one-pion loop diagrams within a nonrelativistic time-ordered perturbation framework, produce sizeable effects of up to about 30% in observables above 300 MeV photon energy, but remain small at low energies; the model preserves correct experimental onshell properties by construction, so only the genuine offshell continuation is model-dependent.

## Method

The authors evaluate one-pion loop contributions to the electromagnetic nucleon one-body current within a nonrelativistic time-ordered perturbation theory that includes explicit meson, nucleon, and Delta isobar degrees of freedom. The model uses a three-body Hilbert space with renormalized interactions, ensuring that the experimental onshell nucleon form factors are reproduced, and applies the resulting effective current operator to deuteron photodisintegration for photon energies up to 500 MeV. The loop integrals are regularized with a dipole pion-nucleon form factor (cutoff mass 1700 MeV) and yield complex scalar functions above pion threshold.

## Key numbers

- Offshell effects in observables reach up to about 30% for photon energies above 300 MeV.
- The hadronic form factor cutoff is Lambda_pi = 1700 MeV.
- The isoscalar and isovector anomalous magnetic moments at the photon point are kappa^s_phys = -0.12 and kappa^v_phys = 3.70.
- The study covers photon energies up to k_lab = 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of offshell effects in electromagnetic reactions on nuclei by Naus and Koch (1987), Tiemeijer and Tjon (1990), and Song et al. (1992), but improves upon them by using a consistent nonrelativistic framework that preserves experimental onshell nucleon properties through renormalization, unlike the earlier approaches which did not reproduce experimental onshell values. The deuteron is chosen as the simplest nucleus to study these effects reliably, and the results are relevant for using the deuteron as a neutron target to extract neutron form factors and for measurements of the GDH sum rule on the deuteron at MAMI and ELSA. The work enables future studies of offshell effects in electrodisintegration and pion production on the deuteron.
