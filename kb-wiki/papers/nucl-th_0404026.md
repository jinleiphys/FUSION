# Antibaryons bound in nuclei

> We study the possibility of producing a new kind of nuclear systems which in addition to ordinary nucleons contain a few antibaryons (antiproton, antilambda, etc.). The properties of such systems are described within the relativistic mean-field model by employing G-parity transformed interactions for antibaryons. Calculations are first done for infinite systems and then for finite nuclei from He to Pb. It is demonstrated that the presence of a real antibaryon leads to a strong rearrangement of a target nucleus resulting in a significant increase of its binding energy and local compression. Noticeable effects remain even after the antibaryon coupling constants are reduced by factor 3-4 compared to G-parity motivated values. We have performed detailed calculations of the antibaryon annihilation rates in the nuclear environment by applying a kinetic approach. It is shown that due to significant reduction of the reaction Q-values, the in-medium annihilation rates should be strongly suppressed leading to relatively long-lived antibaryon-nucleus systems. Multi-nucleon annihilation channels are analyzed too. We have also estimated formation probabilities of bound antibaryon-nucleus systems in antiproton-nucleus reactions and have found that their observation will be feasible at the future GSI antiproton facility. Several observable signatures are proposed. The possibility of producing multi-quark-antiquark clusters is discussed.

# Antibaryons bound in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that inserting a real antibaryon (antiproton or antilambda) into a nucleus can produce strongly bound nuclear systems with significantly increased binding energy and local compression, provided that the antibaryon-meson coupling constants are not reduced by more than a factor of 3-4 compared to G-parity motivated values; the authors acknowledge that G-parity symmetry may be violated in dense baryon-rich matter and that the RMF model's applicability is uncertain at the ~2 GeV excitation energy delivered by an antibaryon.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with G-parity transformed interactions for antibaryons, solving the Dirac equation coupled to scalar and vector meson fields self-consistently for both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei from 4He to 208Pb. They perform calculations with four different RMF parameter sets (NL3, NLZ, NLZ2, TM1) and vary the antibaryon coupling strength using a reduction parameter ξ between 0 and 1. Antibaryon annihilation rates in the nuclear environment are calculated using a kinetic approach that accounts for in-medium reduction of reaction Q-values.

## Key numbers

- For infinite NbN matter with χ=1/16 (one antinucleon per 16 nucleons), the TM1 model predicts a minimum energy per particle of 875 MeV at ρ_N/ρ_0=1.75, while the NLZ model predicts 860 MeV at ρ_N/ρ_0=1.84
- For symmetric NbN matter (χ=1), the TM1 model gives a minimum energy per particle of 449 MeV at ρ_N/ρ_0=4.99, and the NLZ model gives 412 MeV at ρ_N/ρ_0=4.04
- The effective nucleon mass m_N* in NbN matter with χ=1/16 is 393 MeV (TM1) and 224 MeV (NLZ), compared to 593 MeV and 544 MeV respectively for pure nucleonic matter
- For a finite antiproton-16O system, the infinite matter estimate gives binding energies of 1070 MeV (TM1) and 1330 MeV (NLZ), while finite-system calculations give 1159 MeV (TM1) and 828 MeV (NLZ2)
- The binding energy per quark-antiquark pair in baryon-symmetric NbN matter is approximately 326 MeV (TM1) or 350 MeV (NLZ), about five times larger than the ~60 MeV binding energy predicted by the NJL model for deconfined q-qbar matter

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent calculations (Bue02) for antiprotons in nuclei to a wider range of target nuclei and to antilambdas, and adds detailed annihilation rate calculations. It builds on earlier predictions of deeply bound antinucleon states using relativistic nuclear models (Aue86, Mao99) and on studies of antiproton optical potentials from atomic data (Won84, Bat97). The paper also connects to work on in-medium antibaryon mass reduction and subthreshold production in heavy-ion collisions (Tei94, Sib98). The results suggest that bound antibaryon-nucleus systems could be observed at the future GSI antiproton facility, and the authors propose that such systems might induce inertial compression of nuclei and possibly a deconfinement phase transition to multi-quark-antiquark clusters (mesoballs) as previously studied in the NJL model (Mis99, Mis00).
