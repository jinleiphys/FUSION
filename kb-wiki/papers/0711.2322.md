# Relativistic corrections to the static potential at O(1/m) and O(1/m^2)

> We investigate the relativistic corrections to the static potential, i.e. the O(1/m) potential and the O(1/m^2) velocity-dependent potentials, in SU(3) lattice gauge theory. They are important ingredients of potential nonrelativistic QCD for heavy quarkonium. Utilizing the multi-level algorithm, we obtain remarkably clean signals of these potentials up to r=0.9 fm. We observe long range nonperturbative contributions to these corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents a nonperturbative lattice determination of the relativistic corrections to the static quark-antiquark potential at order 1/m and 1/m^2, obtaining clean signals up to distances of 0.9 fm. The abstract's claim of "long range nonperturbative contributions" is qualified by the observation that the O(1/m) potential requires a linear term to fit data beyond 0.6 fm, while the perturbative 1/r^2 form alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors compute color-electric field strength correlators on Polyakov loop correlation functions in SU(3) lattice gauge theory using the multi-level algorithm. They extract matrix elements and energy gaps from the spectral representation of these correlators via fits truncated at n_max=3, then insert these quantities into the pNRQCD expressions for the O(1/m) potential V^(1)(r) and the O(1/m^2) velocity-dependent potentials V_b, V_c, V_d, V_e. Simulations are performed at two lattice spacings (beta=5.85, a=0.123 fm and beta=6.00, a=0.093 fm) with the Wilson gauge action.

## Key numbers

- The O(1/m) potential at beta=6.00 is fit to V_fit^(1)(r) = -c'/r^2 + sigma' r + mu' with c' = 0.090(5), sigma' a^3 = 0.0024(1), chi^2/N_df = 0.68 (r/a=2 omitted)
- The force of the O(1/m) potential gives consistent parameters: c' = 0.095(5), sigma' = 0.0024(1), chi^2/N_df = 0.62
- For charmonium (m_c = 1.3 GeV), the O(1/m) correction to the string tension is (2/m_c)sigma' = 0.179 GeV/fm, compared to sigma = 1.07(1) GeV/fm (about 17 percent correction)
- For bottomonium (m_b = 4.7 GeV), the correction is (2/m_b)sigma' = 0.049 GeV/fm (about 5 percent)
- The static potential at beta=6.00 is fit to V^(0)(r) = -c/r + sigma r + mu with c = 0.2808(5), sigma a^2 = 0.0468(1), mu a = 0.7301(4), chi^2/N_df = 3.5
- Velocity-dependent potentials at beta=6.00 fit to -c/r + sigma r + mu: V_b: c = -0.25(2), sigma a^2 = -0.003(1); V_c: c = 0.61(4), sigma a^2 = -0.019(2); V_d: c = -0.042(4), sigma a^2 = -0.0076(3); V_e: c = -0.156(4), sigma a^2 = -0.0069(3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of the O(1/m) potential (Koma 2006) and O(1/m^2) spin-dependent potentials (Koma 2005, Koma 2006) to longer distances (up to 0.9 fm) and to the velocity-dependent potentials. It improves upon an earlier attempt by Bali (1997) where the signal for velocity-dependent potentials was lost due to large statistical errors. The results enable systematic studies of heavy quarkonium spectra by solving the Schrodinger equation with the pNRQCD hamiltonian, and allow tests of the BBMP relations (Barchielli 1986, Barchielli 1988) which connect velocity-dependent potentials to the static potential via Poincare invariance.
