# Electromagnetic field effects on $Υ$-meson dissociation in PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We investigate the effect of the electromagnetic field generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions on the dissociation of $Υ$ mesons. The electromagnetic field is calculated using a simple model which characterizes the emerging quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by its conductivity only. A numerical estimate of the field strength experienced by $Υ$ mesons embedded in the expanding QGP and its consequences on the $Υ$ dissociation is made. The electromagnetic field effects prove to be negligible compared to the established strong-interaction suppression mechanisms.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic field generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies has a negligible effect on Υ-meson dissociation compared to established strong-interaction suppression mechanisms, particularly for the ground state, but the authors examine whether it could provide the additional suppression needed for the excited Υ(2S) state in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The electromagnetic field of two colliding lead nuclei is calculated using Tuchin's (2013) model that characterizes the quark-gluon plasma solely by its electrical conductivity, neglecting polarization and magnetization. The Υ-meson wave functions and energies are obtained by solving the radial Schrödinger equation with a complex potential in the pNRQCD formalism, where the electromagnetic field contributes electric dipole and magnetic dipole terms treated as perturbations. The total in-medium decay width combines Landau damping from the imaginary potential, gluo-dissociation from thermal gluons, and screening effects when the meson energy exceeds the continuum threshold.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths of up to 5×10¹⁵ T (or 15 m_π²/e) are estimated at LHC energies, following Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev (2009)
- The electric dipole term relative to the Cornell string term is ≤ 0.04, using string tension σ = 0.192 GeV²
- The magnetic energy shift relative to the Cornell string term is ≤ 0.008
- The initial central temperature T₀ ≈ 480 MeV is obtained from a fit to Υ(1S) data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, with formation time τ_F = 0.4 fm
- The QGP lifetime implied by the model is approximately 2 fm, about one-fifth of the value experiment suggests
- The root-mean-square displacement of Υ states ranges from 0.2 fm to 1.0 fm, depending on temperature and excitation level

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Hoelck, Nendzig, and Wolschin, 2017) that explained Υ(1S) suppression through screening, collisional damping, gluo-dissociation, and reduced feed-down, but underpredicted Υ(2S) suppression in peripheral collisions. The electromagnetic field effects are found to be too weak to resolve this discrepancy, suggesting that other mechanisms must be responsible. The calculation uses the same hydrodynamic evolution and parameters as the prior work, with the electromagnetic field computed following Tuchin (2013).
