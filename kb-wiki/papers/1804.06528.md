# Workshop on Pion-Kaon Interactions (PKI2018) Mini-Proceedings. Editors: M. Amaryan, Ulf-G. Meißner, C. Meyer, J. Ritman, and I. Strakovsky

> This volume is a short summary of talks given at the PKI2018 Workshop organized to discuss current status and future prospects of pi-K interactions. The precise data on pi-K interaction will have a strong impact on strange meson spectroscopy and form factors that are important ingredients in the Dalitz plot analysis of a decays of heavy mesons as well as precision measurement of Vus matrix element and therefore on a test of unitarity in the first raw of the CKM matrix. The workshop has combined the efforts of experimentalists, Lattice QCD, and phenomenology communities. Experimental data relevant to the topic of the workshop were presented from the broad range of different collaborations like CLAS, GlueX, COMPASS, BaBar, BELLE, BESIII, VEPP-2000, and LHCb. One of the main goals of this workshop was to outline a need for a new high intensity and high precision secondary KL beam facility at JLab produced with the 12 GeV electron beam of CEBAF accelerator. This workshop is a successor of the workshops Physics with Neutral Kaon Beam at JLab [1] held at JLab, February, 2016; Excited Hyperons in QCD Thermodynamics at Freeze-Out [2] held at JLab, November, 2016; New Opportunities with High-Intensity Photon Sources [3] held at CUA, February, 2017. Further details about the PKI2018 Workshop can be found on the web page of the conference: http://www.jlab.org/conferences/pki2018/ .

## Key claim
The workshop aimed to outline the need for a new high-intensity, high-precision secondary \(K_L\) beam facility at Jefferson Lab, using the 12 GeV CEBAF electron beam, to enable pion-kaon scattering experiments that would address the current unsatisfactory knowledge of strange hadron states, contingent on the successful design and funding of such a facility.

## Method
The workshop combined invited plenary talks from experimentalists, lattice QCD practitioners, and phenomenologists across eight sessions, covering topics such as the proposed KL Facility at JLab, lattice QCD approaches to \(\pi\)-\(K\) interactions, chiral effective theories, dispersion relations, \(\pi\)-\(K\) form factors in heavy meson and tau decays, and hadron spectroscopy from collaborations including CLAS, GlueX, COMPASS, BaBar, BELLE, BESIII, VEPP-2000, and LHCb. The program included discussions of a conceptual beamline design using photoproduction from a beryllium target, with flux and background simulations based on GEANT4 and known cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Expected \(K_L^0\) flux on the GlueX physics target: \(\sim 10^4\) \(K_L^0\)/s for momenta \(P = 0.3\)–\(10\) GeV/c, assuming a 10% radiator, a 40 cm Be target, and a 24 m distance between Be and LH\(_2\) targets.
- The ratio of neutrons to \(K_L^0\) from primary electromagnetic interactions is on the order of \(10^3\), lower than from primary proton beams.
- For the proposed experiment with \(10^4\) \(K_L\)/sec, expected yields are about \(2 \times 10^5\) \(\Xi^\ast\) and \(4 \times 10^3\) \(\Omega^\ast\) per month.
- Lattice QCD results for elastic \(I = 1/2\) \(S\)- and \(P\)-wave kaon-pion scattering amplitudes were obtained using an ensemble with \(N_f = 2 + 1\) flavors of dynamical Wilson-clover fermions at \(m_\pi = 240\) MeV.

## Context
This workshop was the fourth in a series following the workshops "Physics with Neutral Kaon Beam at JLab" (2016), "Excited Hyperons in QCD Thermodynamics at Freeze-Out" (2016), and "New Opportunities with High-Intensity Photon Sources" (2017). It extends previous discussions on missing hyperons and strange meson spectroscopy by focusing specifically on \(\pi\)-\(K\) interactions and their impact on strange meson spectroscopy, Dalitz plot analyses of heavy meson decays, and precision measurement of the CKM matrix element \(V_{us}\). The proposed KLF at JLab would complement a similar program at J-PARC with charged kaon beams, which currently has a maximum beam momentum of 2 GeV/c, and would enable studies of dozens of predicted \(\Lambda^\ast\), \(\Sigma^\ast\), \(\Xi^\ast\), and \(\Omega^\ast\) resonances up to 2.5 GeV.
