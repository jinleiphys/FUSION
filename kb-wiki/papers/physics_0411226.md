# A suggested search for 207Pb nuclear Schiff moment in PbTiO3 ferroelectric

> We suggest two types of experiments, NMR and macroscopic magnetometry, with solid PbTiO3 to search for the nuclear Schiff moment of 207Pb. Both kinds of experiments promise substantial improvement over the presently achieved sensitivities. Statistical considerations show that the improvement of the current sensitivity can be up to 10 orders of magnitude for the magnetometry experiment and up to 6 orders of magnitude for the NMR experiment. Such significant enhancement is due to the strong internal electric field of the ferroelectric, as well as due to the possibility to cool the nuclear-spin subsystem in the compound down to nanokelvin temperatures.

# A suggested search for 207Pb nuclear Schiff moment in PbTiO3 ferroelectric

## Key claim

The authors propose that experiments using the ferroelectric compound PbTiO3 could improve sensitivity to the nuclear Schiff moment of 207Pb by up to 10 orders of magnitude (magnetometry) or 6 orders of magnitude (NMR) compared to the current best limit from 199Hg, but these improvements depend on achieving nanokelvin temperatures for the magnetometry approach and on the validity of the cluster-model estimate for the energy shift.

## Method

The authors calculate the energy shift from the interaction of the 207Pb nuclear Schiff moment with the electric field gradient at the lead nucleus in PbTiO3, using a PbO12 cluster model adapted from earlier work on gadolinium garnets (Simon). They then estimate the NMR line shift and linewidth from dipolar broadening, and compute the macroscopic magnetization induced by the Schiff moment for a magnetometry experiment, assuming the nuclear-spin subsystem can be cooled to its freezing temperature of approximately 10 nK.

## Key numbers

- Current upper limit on 199Hg Schiff moment: S(199Hg) < 0.75 × 10⁻⁵⁰ e cm³ = 0.5 × 10⁻²⁵ e a_B³ (from Romalis experiment)
- Estimated energy shift for 207Pb in PbTiO3: Δε ≈ −1.1 × 10⁶ (S / e a_B³) eV
- Corresponding NMR line shift: Δν ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁵ Hz (assuming S at the 199Hg limit)
- NMR line half-width: Δν_Γ = 230 Hz (natural isotopic abundance, Gaussian shape)
- Number density of 207Pb in natural PbTiO3: n ≈ 0.33 × 10²² cm⁻³
- Freezing temperature of nuclear spins: T_f ≈ 10⁻⁸ K = 10 nK
- Projected magnetometry sensitivity to Schiff moment: S ∼ 1.7 × 10⁻³⁵ e a_B³ = 2.5 × 10⁻⁶⁰ e cm³ (10 orders of magnitude improvement over 199Hg)
- Standard model prediction for nuclear Schiff moment: S ∼ 10⁻⁵⁶ e cm³

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lamoreaux (2002), who suggested using gadolinium garnets for electron EDM measurements via macroscopic magnetometry, and applies it to the nuclear Schiff moment of 207Pb in the ferroelectric PbTiO3. The authors adapt a cluster-model formula from Simon for the energy shift. The proposed magnetometry experiment could reach sensitivities 4 orders of magnitude below the standard model prediction, enabling a search for CP-violation beyond current limits from the 199Hg experiment (Romalis) and neutron EDM measurements. The NMR approach offers a complementary method that does not require nanokelvin temperatures.
