# Lattice regularized chiral perturbation theory

> Chiral perturbation theory can be defined and regularized on a spacetime lattice. A few motivations are discussed here, and an explicit lattice Lagrangian is reviewed. A particular aspect of the connection between lattice chiral perturbation theory and lattice QCD is explored through a study of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term.

## Key claim

Lattice regularization can be applied to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as an alternative to dimensional regularization, but the resulting lattice ChPT Lagrangian is not unique; the paper presents one minimal example that has the correct continuum limit, with all extra irrelevant operators (explicit powers of the lattice spacing) set to zero for simplicity.

## Method

The authors construct a lattice ChPT Lagrangian for mesons and heavy baryons by replacing continuum derivatives with finite-difference operators that preserve chiral symmetry and avoid doublers or ghost states. They then compute meson masses at next-to-leading order and octet baryon magnetic moments at O(p³) as functions of the lattice spacing, and examine the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term by attempting to derive it from the underlying Wilson fermion action without taking the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- The chiral scale is given as Λ_χ ∼ 4πF_π ∼ 4πF_K ∼ 1 GeV.
- The meson mass formula is m_M = (2/a) arcsinh(a x_M / 2), where x_π = √(2B m_l), x_K = √(B(m_l + m_s)), x_η = √(2/3 B(m_l + 2m_s)).
- For the kaon mass at next-to-leading order, the expression includes the integral W_4(ε) ≡ ∫₀^∞ dx I₀⁴(x) exp[-x(4 + ε/2)].
- The renormalization group running of low-energy constants for small lattice spacings gives ΔL₄ − 2ΔL₆ = −1/[36(4π)²] ln(a₂/a₁), ΔL₅ − 2ΔL₈ = 1/[6(4π)²] ln(a₂/a₁), and 3ΔL₇ + ΔL₈ = 5/[48(4π)²] ln(a₂/a₁).
- For octet baryon magnetic moments at a = 0.1 fm relative to continuum: Σ⁺ = 1.11, Σ⁰ = 1.94, Σ⁻ = 0.97, Ξ⁰ = 2.58, Ξ⁻ = 1.01, Λ = 1.95, ΛΣ⁰ = 1.05 (ratios of μ_B(a=0.1 fm) / μ_B(a=0)).
- The continuum WZW coefficient is N_c/(240π²).

## Context

This work extends the approach of lattice regularized ChPT previously discussed by Levi (1993), Shushpanov (1997), and Borasoy, Lewis, and Ouimet (2001), and contrasts with the standard method of adding a-dependent operators to continuum ChPT as done by Baer (1997), Aoki (1999), and Beane (2000). The paper also connects to cut-off regularization studies by the UMass group (long-distance regularization) and the Adelaide group (finite range regulators), and to Aoki's derivation of the continuum WZW term from the lattice Wilson action (2000). The results enable systematic studies of discretization effects in lattice QCD simulations and provide a framework for quantifying scheme dependence in truncated ChPT calculations.
