# Spin-orbit splitting and the tensor component of the Skyrme interaction

> We study the role of the tensor term of the Skyrme effective interactions on the spin-orbit splittings in the N=82 isotones and Z=50 isotopes. The different role of the triplet-even and triplet-odd tensor forces is pointed out by analyzing the spin-orbit splittings in these nuclei. The experimental isospin dependence of these splittings cannot be described by Hartree-Fock calculations employing the usual Skyrme parametrizations, but is very well accounted for when the tensor interaction is introduced. The capability of the Skyrme forces to reproduce binding energies and charge radii in heavy nuclei is not destroyed by the introduction of the tensor term. Finally, we also discuss the effect of the tensor force on the centroid of the Gamow-Teller states.

## Key claim

The experimental isospin dependence of spin-orbit splittings in N=82 isotones and Z=50 isotopes cannot be described by Hartree-Fock calculations using standard Skyrme parametrizations, but is very well accounted for when the tensor interaction is introduced, provided the triplet-even and triplet-odd tensor strengths are treated as free parameters and fitted to reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF-BCS) calculations using the SLy5 Skyrme parameter set augmented with zero-range triplet-even and triplet-odd tensor terms, treating the coupling constants T and U (or equivalently the combined parameters α_T and β_T) as free parameters. They fitted α_T and β_T to reproduce experimental single-particle energy differences in the Z=50 isotopes and N=82 isotones, then evaluated the effect of the tensor force on binding energies, charge radii, and the centroid of Gamow-Teller states using sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The fitted tensor parameters are α_T = -170 MeV·fm^5 and β_T = 100 MeV·fm^5; for comparison, the central exchange contributions for SLy5 are α_C = 80.2 MeV·fm^5 and β_C = -48.9 MeV·fm^5.
- Including the tensor force changes the binding energy of 132Sn by +0.65% and the charge radius by -0.17%; for 208Pb the changes are +0.46% and -0.11%, respectively.
- For 132Sn, the 1h11/2 neutron orbital is less bound than the 2d3/2 by 1.76 MeV with SLy5 alone; adding the tensor force reduces this difference to 0.67 MeV (experimentally the 2d3/2 is less bound by about 240 keV).
- For 100Sn, the 2d3/2 level is more bound than 1h11/2 by 2.13 MeV with SLy5 alone; the tensor force reduces this difference to 1.33 MeV (experimentally 0.9 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Stancu et al. (1977) on tensor interactions in the Skyrme framework, using the more modern SLy5 parameter set rather than SIII. It contradicts the common practice of neglecting tensor contributions in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, and shows that the tensor force is essential to reproduce the isospin dependence of spin-orbit splittings measured by Schiffer et al. The results enable future refitting of Skyrme forces including tensor terms, and motivate fully self-consistent RPA or QRPA calculations of vibrational modes such as Gamow-Teller resonances and low-lying 2+ states.
