# Rate Equation Network for Baryon Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We develop and solve a network of rate equations for the production of baryons and anti-baryons in high energy nuclear collisions. We include all members of the baryon octet and decuplet and allow for transformations among them. This network is solved during a relativistic 2+1 hydrodynamical expansion of the of the hot matter created in the collision. As an application we compare to the number of protons, lambdas, negative cascades, and omega baryons measured at mid-rapidity in central collisions of gold nuclei at 65 GeV per nucleon at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that baryon production rates calculated from a network of rate equations are too small to reproduce measured yields of protons, lambdas, cascades, and omegas at RHIC if no baryons are present at the beginning of the hadronic phase; however, if baryons are assumed to be in chemical equilibrium at the critical temperature, the observed abundances of protons, lambdas, and negative cascades can be reproduced, but the omega abundance is overproduced by about 50%.

## Method

The authors developed and solved a coupled network of rate equations for all members of the baryon octet and decuplet, including strong decays and transformations among species, during a 2+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic expansion of the hot matter created in central gold-gold collisions at RHIC. The production rates for octet-octet pairs were taken from Kapusta and Shovkovy, while rates for octet-decuplet and decuplet-decuplet pairs were estimated using SU(3) flavor symmetry and parametrized with coefficients that were tested for sensitivity by varying them by a factor of two. The rate equations were solved within each co-moving fluid cell, with initial conditions of either all baryons in chemical equilibrium or no baryons present at the critical temperature of an assumed first-order quark-gluon to hadron phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The measured yields of protons, lambdas, negative cascades, and omegas at mid-rapidity in central gold-gold collisions at 65 GeV per nucleon at RHIC were compared to calculations.
- The critical temperature was varied among three values: 165, 180, and 200 MeV.
- The strong interaction coupling used was alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.35 plus or minus 0.03.
- The annihilation form factor parametrization used Lambda = 1.63 GeV.
- The calculated omega abundance was about 50% more than observed when baryons were initially in chemical equilibrium at T_c = 180 MeV.
- The baryon chemical potential was taken to be exactly zero in the calculation.
- The freeze-out temperature range considered was 120 MeV to T_c.

## Context

This work extends the rate equation approach of Kapusta and Shovkovy for octet baryon production to include all members of the baryon decuplet, and solves the network within a hydrodynamic expansion model that had been tuned to reproduce measured pion multiplicities at RHIC. The results contradict purely statistical equilibrium models that fit data with a temperature of 170 plus or minus 10 MeV and a baryon chemical potential of tens of MeV, showing instead that dynamical evolution and initial conditions at the phase transition are critical. The paper enables future studies of transverse momentum distributions of baryon species and tests of different initial abundances, and highlights the omega baryon as a remaining puzzle requiring further investigation.
