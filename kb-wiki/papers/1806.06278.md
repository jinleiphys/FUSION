# Four-body eikonal approach to three-body halo nuclei scattering

> Halo nuclei projectiles can undergo into a fragmentation process when scattered by a nucleus target. The corresponding core from exotic nuclei is usually observed while the others fragments of the reaction are not. We use the recently proposed theory by [Phys. Lett. B767, 53 (2017)] with a four-body description of the inclusive breakup reaction where the projectile is 20C described like a two-neutron halo nucleus. The momentum of both neutrons are integrated out giving a generic description of the core angular distribution. In this preliminary study we perform an analysis of the inclusive inelastic breakup cross-section using the eikonal approximation for the distorted wave function of the projectile. A study of the inclusive inelastic cross-sections of 20C from the collision with different targets are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a preliminary four-body eikonal treatment of inclusive inelastic breakup for three-body halo projectiles, showing that the core scattering process is most sensitive to the target surface and that the absorption effect of one fragment damps the difference between four-body and three-body descriptions.

## Method

The authors apply the four-body inclusive breakup formalism of Carlson et al. (2017) to the two-neutron halo nucleus 20C, using the eikonal approximation for the core-target distorted waves and the Sao Paulo potential for the core-target interaction. The three-body projectile wave function is obtained from a renormalized three-body model, and the neutron-target interaction is represented by a Woods-Saxon potential. The inclusive inelastic breakup cross-section is computed by integrating out the momenta of both halo neutrons, yielding the core angular distribution.

## Key numbers

* The scattering T-matrix for 20C off 12C at 50 MeV/nucleon is computed as a function of impact parameter b_b, with most absorption occurring around the target nucleus radius.
* The inclusive four-body fragment cross-section is shown for 20C on 12C and 208Pb targets at incoming energy 50 MeV/nucleon with outgoing core energy E'_b = 0.8 E_b.
* The inelastic effect is demonstrated by comparing angular distributions for outgoing core energies E'_b = 0.8 E_b and E'_b = 0.5 E_b at fixed incoming energy 50 MeV/nucleon.
* Woods-Saxon parameters for the n-12C interaction: W_{0_R} = 49.9395 MeV, W_{0_I} = 1.8256 MeV, a_R = a_I = 0.676 fm, R_I = R_R = 2.5798 fm.

## Context

This work extends the inclusive breakup formalism of Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (1985), Udagawa and Tamura (1981), and Hussein and McVoy (1985) from three-body (two-fragment projectile) to four-body (three-fragment projectile) reactions, following the theoretical framework of Carlson et al. (2017). The preliminary results for 20C on 12C and 208Pb targets demonstrate the role of the three-body projectile wave function and the two-fragment correlation through a three-body absorptive interaction. The next planned steps include adding the two-fragment distorted waves and Coulomb scattering, as well as computing the double-fragment inclusive cross-section to complete the inelastic cross-section.
