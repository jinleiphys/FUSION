# Rotation alignment in neutron-rich Cr isotopes: A probe of deformed single-particle levels across N=40

> Recent experiments have reached the neutron-rich Cr isotope with N=40 and confirmed enhanced collectivity near this sub-shell. The current data focus on low-spin spectroscopy only, with little information on the states where high-j particles align their spins with the system rotation. By applying the Projected Shell Model, we show that rotation alignment occurs in neutron-rich even-even Cr nuclei as early as spin 8 and, due to shell filling, the aligning particles differ in different isotopes. It is suggested that observation of irregularities in moments of inertia is a direct probe of the deformed single-particle scheme in this exotic mass region.

# Rotation alignment in neutron-rich Cr isotopes: A probe of deformed single-particle levels across N=40

## Key claim

The authors predict that rotation alignment of high-j particles occurs in neutron-rich even-even Cr nuclei as early as spin 8ℏ, with the specific aligning particles differing between isotopes depending on neutron shell filling; however, they caution that the standard Nilsson parameters used in their model may require modification for neutron-rich nuclei, and that current experimental data stop at the spins where irregularities are predicted, so experimental confirmation is needed.

## Method

The authors performed Projected Shell Model (PSM) calculations for neutron-rich even-even Cr isotopes with neutron numbers from 30 to 44, using deformed Nilsson single-particle states to build the model basis and a quadrupole plus pairing Hamiltonian with quadrupole-pairing term. The wavefunction is a superposition of angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle states (0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle configurations for even-even nuclei), with particles activated in three major shells (N=2,3,4 for both neutrons and protons). The calculated moments of inertia and band diagrams were analyzed to identify band crossings and rotation alignment effects.

## Key numbers

- Rotation alignment is predicted to occur as early as spin 8ℏ in neutron-rich even-even Cr nuclei
- For ⁵⁴Cr and ⁵⁶Cr, a peak in moment of inertia is predicted at spin 16ℏ for both isotopes
- For ⁵⁸,⁶⁰,⁶²Cr, the first irregularity in moment of inertia is predicted at spin 6 or 8, with peaks at spin 10 and 16
- For ⁶⁴,⁶⁶,⁶⁸Cr (N≥40), a gradual increase in moment of inertia is predicted until a peak forms at spin 14 or 16
- Input quadrupole deformation parameters (ε₂) used: 0.210 for ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Cr; 0.220 for ⁵⁸Cr; 0.240 for ⁶⁰Cr; 0.250 for ⁶²Cr; 0.235 for ⁶⁴,⁶⁶Cr; 0.230 for ⁶⁸Cr
- Monopole pairing strengths: G_M = [G₁ ∓ G₂(N−Z)/A]/A with G₁=18.72 and G₂=10.74 (plus sign for protons, minus for neutrons); quadrupole pairing strength proportional to G_M with constant 0.30

## Context

This work extends previous Projected Shell Model studies on neutron-rich Fe isotopes (Sun et al., 2008) and Ge nuclei, and builds on experimental data for Cr isotopes from Devlin (1999), Zhu (2006), Aoi (2009), and Gade (2010) for ⁶⁴Cr at N=40. The calculations predict high-spin behavior beyond the current low-spin data, enabling future experiments (such as fragmentation of a ⁷⁶Ge beam) to test the deformed single-particle scheme in this mass region. The authors note that large-scale shell-model calculations (Caurier, 2002; Honma, 2005) have successfully described low-spin spectroscopy but may not be appropriate for high-spin physics, while the PSM approach can handle the necessary excited quasiparticle configurations.
