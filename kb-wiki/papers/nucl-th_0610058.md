# Isospin mode splitting and mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We estimate exclusive density and asymmetry parameter dependent dispersion relations of various charged states of pions in asymmetric nuclear matter. The possibility of matter induced mixing of $π^0$ with $η$ is clearly exposed with the further mass modification of $π^0$ meson due to mixing. Asymmetry driven mass splitting and mixing amplitude are of the same order as the corresponding values in vacuum. Closed form analytic results for the mass shifts and dispersion relations with and without mixing are presented. Furthermore, we discuss the sensitivity of our results on the scalar mean field within the framework of Quantum Hadrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM), the difference between proton and neutron Fermi momenta drives both a splitting of the charged pion masses and a matter-induced mixing of π⁰ with the η meson, with these effects being of the same order as their vacuum counterparts even at normal nuclear density. The authors explicitly neglect the vacuum n-p mass difference to isolate the purely density-driven mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium pion self-energies for π⁺, π⁻, and π⁰ using pseudovector coupling in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD), evaluating one-loop diagrams with in-medium nucleon propagators that include a dense part via the Fermi sea. They work in the long-wavelength limit (q ≪ k_F) and obtain closed-form analytic expressions for the self-energies, then solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation to derive dispersion relations and effective masses. The π⁰-η mixing amplitude is computed from the same loop formalism, exploiting the opposite sign of the π⁰ coupling to protons versus neutrons compared to the η coupling.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density with asymmetry parameter α = 0.7 and scalar mean field (M* = 685.0 MeV): Δm_π⁺ = -1.15 MeV, Δm_π⁰ = 4.85 MeV, Δm_π⁻ = 11.19 MeV.
- At α = 0.2 with mean field: Δm_π⁺ = 3.39 MeV, Δm_π⁰ = 5.01 MeV, Δm_π⁻ = 6.98 MeV.
- Matter-induced π⁰-η mixing amplitude at normal nuclear density: Σ^{matt}_{πη} = -1217.475 MeV² at α = 0.2, and -1661.11 MeV² at α = 0.3.
- Vacuum π⁰-η mixing amplitude quoted for comparison: Σ^{vac}_{πη} = -4200 MeV² (Piekarewicz 1993).
- The mass splitting is an O(k_{p,n}⁵/ε*_{p,n}⁵) effect, which is higher order than the leading linear density term.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of pion propagation in symmetric nuclear matter (Korpa 1995, Liu 1997) to the asymmetric case, and introduces a matter-induced π⁰-η mixing mechanism first suggested for ρ-ω mixing by one of the authors (Dutt-Mazumder 1997). The results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory calculations (Weise 2001) in finding that π⁻ experiences more repulsion than π⁰ and π⁺ in neutron-rich matter. The formalism enables future calculations of pion-nucleus optical potentials (Batty 1997, Weise 2001) and low-mass dilepton yields in relativistic heavy ion collisions (Helgesson 1995, Rapp 2000), and can be extended to include the explicit n-p mass difference.
