# Proton induced deuteron knockout reaction as a probe of an isoscalar proton-neutron pair in nuclei

> The isoscalar $pn$ pair is expected to emerge in nuclei having the similar proton and neutron numbers but there is no clear experimental evidence for it. We aim to clarify the correspondence between the $pn$ pairing strength in many-body calculation and the triple differential cross section (TDX) of proton-induced deuteron knockout ($p,pd$) reaction on $^{16}$O. The radial wave function of the isoscalar $pn$ pair with respect to the center of $^{16}$O is calculated with the energy density functional (EDF) approach and is implemented in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework. The $pn$ pairing strength $V_0$ in the EDF calculation is varied and the corresponding change in the TDX is investigated. A clear $V_0$ dependence of the TDX is found for the $^{16}$O($p,pd$)$^{14}$N($1_2^+$) at $101.3$ MeV. The nuclear distortion is found to make the $V_0$ dependence stronger. Because of the clear $V_0$-TDX correspondence, the ($p,pd$) reaction will be a promising probe for the isoscalar $pn$ pair in nuclei. For quantitative discussion, further modification of the description of the reaction process will be necessary.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a clear correspondence between the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength \(V_0\) in an energy density functional (EDF) calculation and the triple differential cross section (TDX) of the \(^{16}\text{O}(p,pd)^{14}\text{N}(1_2^+)\) reaction at 101.3 MeV, but the authors caution that for a quantitative comparison with experimental data, further modification of the reaction description (including deuteron breakup and a coupled-channel treatment of the elementary process) is necessary.

## Method

The radial wave function of the isoscalar (\(T=0\), \(S=1\)) proton-neutron pair relative to the center of \(^{16}\text{O}\) was calculated using the nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) approach with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) equation and a proton-neutron hole-hole Random-Phase Approximation (pn-hhRPA). This wave function was then used as the bound-state input in a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework to compute the triple differential cross section for the \(^{16}\text{O}(p,pd)^{14}\text{N}(1_2^+)\) reaction at 101.3 MeV. The pairing strength \(V_0\) in the density-dependent contact interaction was varied among three values (\(-100\), \(-490\), and \(-600\) MeV fm\(^3\)) to study the sensitivity of the TDX.

## Key numbers

* The TDX for \(^{16}\text{O}(p,pd)^{14}\text{N}(1_2^+)\) was calculated at a proton incident energy of 101.3 MeV, with outgoing particles detected at \((\theta_1^\text{L}, \phi_1^\text{L}) = (40.1^\circ, 0^\circ)\) and \((\theta_2^\text{L}, \phi_2^\text{L}) = (40.0^\circ, 180^\circ)\).
* The recoilless condition occurs at \(T_1^\text{L} \sim 52\) MeV, corresponding to \(E_{pd} \sim 56\) MeV and \(\theta_{pd} \sim 68^\circ\) for the \(p\)-\(d\) scattering.
* The calculated energy difference \(\Delta E = E_{1_2^+} - E_{1_1^+}\) for \(V_0 = -100\), \(-490\), and \(-600\) MeV fm\(^3\) is 5.41, 4.12, and 3.48 MeV, respectively; the experimental value is 3.95 MeV.
* The SHF equation was solved on a mesh of \(\Delta r = \Delta z = 0.6\) fm with a box boundary at \((r_\text{max}, z_\text{max}) = (14.7, 14.4)\) fm; the saturation density \(\rho_0\) used in the pairing interaction is 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\).
* In the DWIA calculation, the ratio of the TDX height (at \(T_1^\text{L} = 52\) MeV) for \(V_0 = -600\) MeV fm\(^3\) relative to that for \(V_0 = -100\) MeV fm\(^3\) is larger than the corresponding ratio in the PWIA limit, indicating distortion enhances the sensitivity.
* An artificial 25% outward widening of the radial wave function (for \(V_0 = -490\) MeV fm\(^3\)) increased the TDX by about a factor of 20.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing in \(N \sim Z\) nuclei, previously suggested by KenYoshi (2014) and ELitivi (2018) using EDF approaches, by connecting the pairing strength to a measurable reaction observable. It builds on an earlier experimental measurement of the \(^{16}\text{O}(p,pd)\) reaction at 101.3 MeV by CSam (1982), which used a simpler single-particle model for the bound deuteron. The present results indicate that the \((p,pd)\) reaction is a promising probe for the \(T=0\) \(pn\) pair, but the authors note that a quantitative extraction of pairing collectivity will require a more accurate description of the reaction process, including deuteron breakup and a coupled-channel treatment of the \(p(pn,d)p\) elementary process, following the approach of REnt (1994) for \((e,e'd)\) reactions.
