# Electrical conductivity and shear viscosity of a pion gas in a thermo-magnetic medium

> We evaluate the electrical conductivity and shear viscosity of a interacting pion gas in a thermo-magnetic medium using the kinetic theory. The collision term of the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in presence of background magnetic field is solved using the relaxation time approximation. The medium modified relaxation time is obtained from the corresponding in-medium $ππ\rightarrow ππ$ scattering cross-section calculated using the thermo-magnetic $ρ$ propagator. It is observed that the average relaxation time shows a $1/T^4$ variation with temperature for a fixed value of magnetic field. The relaxation time shows a mild oscillatory variation with respect to the magnetic field. It is also observed that the medium dependent scattering cross-section causes a considerable amount of influence on the electrical conductivity and shear viscosity compared to its vacuum counterpart.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electrical conductivity and shear viscosity of a pion gas in a thermo-magnetic medium are significantly influenced by medium-dependent scattering cross-sections compared to vacuum cross-sections, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific magnetic field strengths and temperature ranges explored (T = 100-160 MeV, eB = 0.005-0.05 GeV²), and the relaxation time approximation used to solve the Boltzmann transport equation.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in a background magnetic field using the relaxation time approximation, where the collision term is replaced by -δf/τ. The medium-modified relaxation time τ is obtained from the in-medium ππ → ππ scattering cross-section, which is calculated using the thermo-magnetic ρ meson propagator within the real-time formulation of thermal field theory. The ρ meson self-energies are computed at one-loop order for both neutral (ρ⁰) and charged (ρ±) mesons, incorporating Landau level quantization for charged pions in the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_{ρππ} = 20.72 GeV⁻² is estimated from the experimental vacuum decay width of ρ → ππ, which is about Γ_{ρ→ππ} ≈ 150 MeV.
- The average relaxation time ⟨τ⟩ shows a 1/T⁴ variation with temperature for a fixed magnetic field value of eB = 0.01 GeV².
- The magnetic field causes nearly 10% increase in magnitude of the ππ cross-section compared to the vacuum cross-section.
- The thermal bath causes approximately 25-30% decrease in the thermal medium dependent cross-section compared to the vacuum cross-section.
- The effective masses of ρ⁰ and ρ± are calculated at eB = 0.05 GeV², showing marginal variation with temperature.
- The cross-section is computed at T = 130 MeV for three magnetic field values: eB = 0.005 GeV², 0.01 GeV², and 0.05 GeV².

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients in a pion gas by Kalikotay (2020), which estimated electrical conductivity of a static pion gas to be roughly 1-3 MeV, sustaining a magnetic field of order 10⁻⁵ GeV² for about 10 fm/c. The paper builds on prior studies of transport coefficients in magnetized hadronic matter by Harutyunyan (2016) and others, but introduces the novel element of incorporating both thermal and magnetic field effects simultaneously into the scattering cross-section via the ρ meson propagator. This enables more realistic modeling of the hadronic phase in heavy ion collisions, where temperatures range from 100-150 MeV and residual magnetic fields may persist.
