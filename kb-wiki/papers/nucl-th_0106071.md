# Microscopic derivation of pion-nucleon and pion-Delta scattering lengths

> A general expression for the $π-N$ and $π-Δ$ scattering lengths is derived in the framework of a microscopic calculation. Annihilation, negative energy wave-functions and spontaneous chiral symmetry are included consistently. The point-like limit is used to calculate the scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for the pion-nucleon and pion-Delta scattering lengths that are formally independent of the specific microscopic quark-quark interaction kernel, provided the kernel supports spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking; the actual numerical results are obtained only in the point-like limit, which avoids computing the full one-hadron exchange diagrams.

## Method

The authors use the Resonating Group Method (RGM) within an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (eNJL) framework derived from the Gaussian approximation of QCD. They compute the effective pion-baryon potential by summing contact diagrams (energy, kinetic, and potential overlaps) that include quark exchange, quark-antiquark annihilation, and negative-energy wave-function components, all constrained by chiral symmetry. The scattering length is then obtained from the Born approximation to the RGM effective potential, with the point-like limit taken to evaluate the contact term without explicit hadron exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

* The pion-baryon scattering length in the Born approximation is given by \(a_{\pi-B} = -M_\pi \mathcal{O}^{\text{RGM}}_{\pi-B} / (2\pi)\), where \(M_\pi\) is the pion mass and \(\mathcal{O}^{\text{RGM}}_{\pi-B}\) is the RGM overlap kernel.
* The exact scattering length expression is \(a_{\pi-B} = -\mu_{\text{red}} \mathcal{O}^{\text{RGM}}_{\pi-B} / (2\pi + 2\mu_{\text{red}} \mathcal{O}^{\text{RGM}}_{\pi-B} N^{2/3})\), with \(\mu_{\text{red}} = M_\pi - M_\pi^2/M_B + \cdots\) the reduced mass of the pion-baryon system.
* The reduced mass expansion yields \(\mu_{\text{red}} = M_\pi - M_\pi^2/M_B + \cdots\) (no explicit numerical values for masses or scattering lengths are given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the RGM formalism previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Ribeiro, 1990s) and meson-meson scattering (Bicudo et al., 1990s) to the pion-nucleon and pion-Delta systems. It improves upon earlier RGM calculations by Lemaire and Ceuleneer (1990s) by consistently including quark-antiquark annihilation, negative-energy wave-function components, and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The derived kernel-independent formulas enable future calculations of these scattering lengths using any chiral-invariant quark model, with the point-like limit providing a first numerical estimate.
