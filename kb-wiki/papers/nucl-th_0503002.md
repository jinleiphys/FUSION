# The sigma - nucleon form factor in a chiral topological model with anomalous dimension

> The chiral soliton model including pi, rho, omega mesons is extended for the case when the scalar - isoscalar sigma meson has an anomalous dimension - d. The form factor of the sigma - nucleon interaction calculated and to be found not highly sensitive to the value of d.

## Key claim

The sigma-nucleon form factor calculated in a chiral soliton model that includes pi, rho, omega, and sigma mesons is not highly sensitive to the anomalous scale dimension d of the sigma meson, meaning that using d=2.4 instead of the normal value d=1 leads to qualitatively similar results that would produce the same nucleon-nucleon potential in one-boson-exchange models.

## Method

The author extends the pi-rho-omega chiral soliton model by including a scalar-isoscalar sigma meson with an explicit anomalous scale dimension d, following the prescription of Schechter (1980) to restore scale invariance. Classical soliton solutions with baryon number one are obtained by minimizing the classical mass functional using spherical symmetric ansatze for the pion, rho, omega, and sigma profile functions, with boundary conditions theta(0)=pi, G(0)=-2, omega'(0)=sigma'(0)=0, and all fields vanishing at infinity. The sigma-nucleon form factor is then derived using Cohen's (1986) procedure, which involves taking matrix elements of the Klein-Gordon equation between nucleon states in the Breit frame, after redefining the sigma field to obtain a canonical kinetic term with flat metric.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV
- Rho and omega meson masses: m_rho = m_omega = 770 MeV
- Rho-pi-pi coupling constant: g = m/(sqrt(2)*f_pi) = 5.85
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 550 MeV (set to best describe static nucleon properties)
- Vacuum expectation value of scalar field: S_0 = 93 MeV (set equal to f_pi)
- Two values of anomalous dimension considered: d = 1 (normal) and d = 2.4 (anomalous)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous pi-rho-omega-sigma soliton model (Meissner, Rakhimov, Yakhshiev, 2000) which used the ordinary scale dimension d=1, by incorporating an anomalous scale dimension for the sigma meson as suggested in chiral effective Lagrangians for finite nuclear matter by Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot (1995). The resulting form factor is intended for use in one-boson-exchange models of nucleon-nucleon interaction, such as those reviewed by Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster (1987), and could be applied to calculations of nucleon-nucleon observables including phase shifts and deuteron properties.
