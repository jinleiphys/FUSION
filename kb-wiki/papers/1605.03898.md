# Description of light nuclei in pionless effective field theory using the stochastic variational method

> We construct a coordinate-space potential based on pionless effective field theory with a Gaussian regulator. Charge-symmetry breaking is included through the Coulomb potential and through two- and three-body contact interactions. Starting with the effective field theory potential, we apply the stochastic variational method to determine the ground states of nuclei with mass number $A\leq 4$. At next-to-next-to-leading order, two out of three independent three-body parameters can be fitted to the three-body binding energies. To fix the remaining one, we look for a simultaneous description of the binding energy of $^4$He and the charge radii of $^3$He and $^4$He. We show that at the order considered we can find an acceptable solution, within the uncertainty of the expansion. We find that the EFT expansion shows good agreement with empirical data within the estimated uncertainty, even for a system as dense as $^4$He.

## Key claim

The authors show that a coordinate-space potential derived from pionless effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), combined with the stochastic variational method, can simultaneously describe the binding energies and charge radii of nuclei up to A=4 within the estimated uncertainty of the EFT expansion, provided that one of the three independent three-body parameters is left free and fixed by requiring a simultaneous description of the ⁴He binding energy and the charge radii of ³He and ⁴He.

## Method

The authors constructed a coordinate-space potential from pionless EFT at NNLO using a Gaussian regulator, including charge-symmetry breaking through the Coulomb potential and two- and three-body contact interactions. They applied the stochastic variational method (SVM) to solve the Schrödinger equation for the ground states of nuclei with mass number A ≤ 4, fitting two-body parameters to low-energy nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and three-body parameters to the binding energies of ³H and ³He, with the remaining three-body parameter determined by simultaneously describing the ⁴He binding energy and charge radii of ³He and ⁴He.

## Key numbers

* The Gaussian regulator width σ was varied in the range 0.6 to 1.2 fm to explore cut-off dependence and estimate uncertainties.
* A lower bound of R = 1.3 fm was found for a sharp radial cut-off in prior work (Scaldeferri, 1996), which informed the choice of regulator scale.
* The Coulomb potential strength is given by α_em, the fine structure constant, with α_em M (M being the nucleon mass) treated as either order Q or order Q² depending on the power-counting scheme.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach of Kirscher et al. (2009), who used a similar Gaussian-regulated pionless EFT potential with the resonating-group method, by exploring a consistent description of both energies and charge radii at the same truncation order and examining ambiguities in parameter fits. The approach contrasts with Lagrangian-based perturbative treatments such as that of König et al. (2015) and with the Yakubovsky equation approach of Platter et al. (2004), while the SVM itself follows the method of Suzuki (1998). The results enable future extension of the variational method to describe scattering states, which would allow fixing the remaining three-body parameter from three-nucleon strong-interaction observables.
