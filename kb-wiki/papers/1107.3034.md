# A New Path-Integral Representation of the $T$-Matrix in Potential Scattering

> We employ the method used by Barbashov and collaborators in Quantum Field Theory to derive a path-integral representation of the $T$-matrix in nonrelativistic potential scattering which is free of functional integration over fictitious variables as was necessary before. The resulting expression serves as a starting point for a variational approximation applied to high-energy scattering from a Gaussian potential. Good agreement with exact partial-wave calculations is found even at large scattering angles. A novel path-integral representation of the scattering length is obtained in the low-energy limit.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new path-integral representation of the nonrelativistic T-matrix that eliminates the need for "phantom" or "anti-velocity" functional integrations required in prior formulations, using a method from quantum field theory by Barbashov and collaborators. The representation is validated through a variational approximation that shows good agreement with exact partial-wave calculations for high-energy scattering from a Gaussian potential, though the authors note that basic properties like unitarity are less evident in this path-integral formulation.

## Method

The authors start from a nonrelativistic Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann (LSZ) reduction formula that expresses the on-shell T-matrix as a limit of the full Green function, then employ a Schwinger representation and velocity path integral for the time evolution operator. Following the Barbashov method, they manipulate the resulting expression by subtracting a term that vanishes on-shell, introducing an auxiliary time integration and a parameter lambda integration, then performing a series of variable shifts and truncations to eliminate both off-shell factors. The final result is a path-integral representation where the particle propagates along a "ray" path (initial momentum for negative times, final momentum for positive times) with quantum fluctuations described by a velocity path integral and a single lambda integration over the potential.

## Key numbers

* Scattering from a Gaussian potential V(x) = V0 exp(-x^2/R^2) at kR = 4 and 2m V0 R^2 = -4: the variational approximation (B30) outperforms both first and second Born approximations across all scattering angles
* The relative deviation |Delta f/f| between the variational amplitude and exact partial-wave calculation is shown as a function of scattering angle theta, remaining small even at large angles where the cross section is down by many orders of magnitude
* The second Born approximation is explicitly reproduced by the variational approximation when expanded to order V^2, with the factor 1/2 from the lambda-integration canceling to yield the standard second Born term

## Context

This work extends the path-integral approach to nonrelativistic potential scattering previously developed by Rosenfelder (2008), which required functional integration over fictitious "anti-velocity" variables in two versions (3-dimensional and 1-dimensional). The new representation belongs to a "level 1" class (counting powers of the potential in front of the velocity path integral), compared to the "level 0" representations of the earlier work. The method is adapted from Barbashov and collaborators (1970) who developed the amputation technique for Green functions in quantum field theory, later used by Fabbrichesi, Han and others in gravitational scattering. The variational approximation follows the approach of Carron and Rosenfelder (2010) and enables both high-energy scattering calculations and a novel path-integral representation of the scattering length in the low-energy limit.
