# Hidden Local Field Theory and Dileptons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The notion of "hadronic freedom" is introduced based on the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry and is used to suggest that the dileptons measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions do not provide {\em direct} information on the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry and hence on the mechanism for mass generation of light-quark hadrons. We give arguments how the dileptons emitted from those vector mesons whose masses are shifted à la Brown-Rho (BR) scaling by the vacuum change in temperature -- as in heavy-ion collisions -- and/or density -- as in cold compressed matter -- could be strongly suppressed in the hadronic free region between the chiral restoration point and the "flash point" at which the vector mesons recover $\gsim 90%$ of their free-space on-shell masses and the full strong coupling strength. It may seem ironical that the very mechanism i.e., the vector manifestation in hidden local symmetry, that is to make the mass drop and modify the spectral function is in turn responsible for the dilepton suppression. A possible falsification of this drastic prediction will be indicated. We also briefly discuss the potential role played by the holographic dimension intrinsic in gravity-gauge duality that provides a unified field theory description of hadrons -- both mesons and baryons -- under extreme conditions. Baryons arise as coherent states of pions and vector mesons of holographic QCD which define the ground state or the "vacuum" on which mesonic excitations could undergo BR scaling.

## Key claim

The authors argue that dileptons measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions do not provide direct information on chiral symmetry restoration or the mechanism of light-quark hadron mass generation, because vector mesons whose masses are shifted by Brown-Rho scaling are strongly suppressed in the "hadronic freedom" region between the chiral restoration temperature and a lower "flash temperature" where mesons recover approximately 90% of their free-space on-shell masses.

## Method

The paper develops a theoretical framework based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) and its vector manifestation (VM) fixed point, where the gauge coupling vanishes and the vector meson mass goes to zero at the chiral phase transition. The authors introduce the concept of "hadronic freedom" as a regime between the critical temperature \(T_c\) and a flash temperature \(T_f \approx 120\) MeV where all hadronic interactions become weak, and they analyze how this suppresses dilepton emission from BR-scaled vector mesons. They also discuss the infinite tower structure of holographic QCD from the Sakai-Sugimoto model as a potential unified description.

## Key numbers

- Flash temperature: \(T_f \approx 120\) MeV, extracted from Miller's lattice calculation of the gluon condensate, where soft glue begins to melt and vector mesons recover about 90% of their free-space mass (700 MeV for the \(\rho\) meson).
- STAR \(\rho^0/\pi^-\) ratio in Au+Au peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV: \(0.169 \pm 0.003\) (stat) \(\pm 0.037\) (syst), nearly equal to the proton-proton value of \(0.183 \pm 0.001\) (stat) \(\pm 0.027\) (syst).
- In the VM fixed point, the parametric vector meson mass scales as \(M^{*2}_\rho \sim \langle \bar{q}q \rangle^{*2}\), going to zero at the chiral transition.
- The charge sum rule for the pion form factor in the Sakai-Sugimoto model is saturated by the lowest four \(\rho\) mesons: \(F(0) = 1.31 - 0.35 + 0.05 - 0.01 = 1.00\).

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling prediction (Brown and Rho, 1991) that hadron masses drop proportionally to the quark condensate at high temperature and density, and addresses the apparent absence of such mass shifts in dilepton measurements from experiments like NA60 and CERES. The authors propose that the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003) naturally leads to suppression of dileptons from BR-scaled mesons, contradicting the conventional expectation that dileptons directly probe in-medium spectral functions. The framework enables future work on a holographic hidden local symmetry description that treats mesons and baryons on the same footing in hot and dense matter.
