# Hadron modification in a dense baryonic matter

> Starting with the Strongly Correlated Quark Model of a hadron structure, SCQM, we demonstrate how the properties of mesons and baryons are modified in a hot and dense nuclear environment. These in-medium modifications can lead to the observable effects in heavy ion collisions, such as enhancement of strangeness and dropping vector meson masses.

# Hadron modification in a dense baryonic matter

## Key claim
The Strongly Correlated Quark Model (SCQM) can explain the "horn" effect in the energy dependence of the K+/pi+ ratio and the enhancement of dilepton invariant mass spectra as consequences of hadron modification in dense baryonic matter, but only under the condition that the crossing time of colliding nuclei is larger than the formation time of produced particles for the strangeness enhancement mechanism to operate.

## Method
The author applies the Strongly Correlated Quark Model (SCQM), in which valence quarks in hadrons oscillate between a constituent mass state at maximum displacement (0.64 fm) and a current mass state at the origin, with the potential energy of each quark equal to its dynamical mass. The model treats mesons as quark-antiquark systems and baryons as three-quark systems oscillating synchronously, with the hadronic matter distribution around valence quarks taken in a Gaussian form. The in-medium modifications are derived by considering the reduction of available volume and vacuum condensate around valence quarks under compression in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- Maximum quark displacement in hadrons: x_max = 0.64 fm
- Gaussian width parameters for hadronic matter distribution: sigma_x,y = 0.24 fm, sigma_z = 0.12 fm
- Constituent quark mass at maximum displacement: M_Q(x_max) = 360 MeV (one third of the average of delta isobar and nucleon masses)
- Current mass of valence quark: 5 MeV
- The in-medium vector meson mass follows m* = m_0 (1 - alpha rho/rho_0)^beta, where alpha and beta are adjustable parameters
- The resonance decay width follows Gamma_R ~ Gamma_R^0 (rho/rho_0)^gamma

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier development of the Strongly Correlated Quark Model (Musulmanbekov, prior work) to address the "horn" effect observed by the NA49 collaboration at SPS and confirmed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC, as well as the dilepton enhancement measured in various nuclear reactions (references di1, di2, di3). The model offers an alternative to interpretations involving a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, such as the prediction by Rafelski (1982) that strangeness signals deconfinement, and the approach of Ref. gasd which predicted a horn-like structure from a hadronic-to-partonic phase transition. The mechanism proposed here enables a unified explanation of both the strangeness enhancement and vector meson mass dropping without requiring deconfinement, though the author notes that the satisfactory understanding of the horn structure remains incomplete despite many statistical and kinetic models.
