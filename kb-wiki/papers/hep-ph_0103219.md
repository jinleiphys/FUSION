# Explosive Decomposition in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collision

> Recent results from Au+Au collisions at BNL-RHIC energy hint at explosive hadron production at the QCD transition rather than soft hydrodynamic evolution. We speculate that this is due to a rapid variation of the effective potential for QCD close to Tc. Performing real-time lattice simulations of an effective theory we show that the fast evolution of the potential leads to ``explosive'' spinodal decomposition rather than bubble nucleation or critical slowing down.

## Key claim

The authors speculate that the rapid variation of the QCD effective potential near the critical temperature, rather than the order of the phase transition itself, drives explosive spinodal decomposition in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions; this mechanism is presented as an alternative to bubble nucleation or critical slowing down, and the results are not driven by the order of the phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed real-time classical lattice simulations on a 64^3 lattice using an effective theory where the free energy near T_c is dominated by a condensate of Polyakov loops, with coefficients chosen to reproduce lattice QCD data for the pressure and energy density of pure glue theory. They solved the classical Euler-Lagrange equations in a Robertson-Walker metric with a scale factor a(τ)=τ/τ_0, varying the expansion rate by setting the initial proper time τ_0 to either 2000 fm/c (slow expansion) or 100 fm/c (fast expansion), and initialized the Polyakov loop field in the deconfined minimum with Gaussian fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is T_c ~ 0.63√σ, where σ ~ 1 GeV/fm is the string tension at T=0.
- For the effective Polyakov loop potential, the coefficients are b_3 ≈ 0.9 and b_4 ≈ 15.0 for pure glue; for N_f=3, b_4 is rescaled by a factor 47.5/16.
- The coupling between the chiral field and the Polyakov loop is h^2 ≈ 22.
- The lattice spacing used is a_L = 0.25 fm.
- The initial temperature for simulations is T(τ_0) = 1.01 T_c.
- The initial energy density is ≈ 4 T_c^4.
- For fast expansion (τ_0 = 100 fm/c), supercooling by ~1% suffices for the soft modes to roll down to the confined minimum rather than nucleation to occur.
- The threshold for phase coexistence not being established is τ_0 ≲ 100 fm/c, based on the estimate Δτ T_c ≃ 1.
- Observed HBT radii at RHIC are ~5-7 fm, with average transverse boost velocities ~0.6c.
- The outward HBT radius R_o is expected to be larger than the sideward radius R_s by as much as 50%-100% in an equilibrium first-order transition.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) regarding quench-induced decomposition in chiral symmetry breaking, and by Randrup (1996) and Mishustin and Scavenius (1997) who found that chiral effective potentials change too slowly for such effects in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The paper builds on the effective Polyakov loop model of Pisarski (2000) and Dumitru and Pisarski (2000), and uses lattice QCD data from Kaczmarek, Karsch, Laermann and Lutgemeier (2000) for the string tension and equation of state. The results enable predictions for event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse momentum and the near-equality of outward and sideward HBT radii as signatures of explosive decomposition at RHIC.
