# $\bar{B}^0$ and $\bar{B}^0_s$ decays into $J/ψ$ and $f_0(1370),~f_0(1710),~f_2(1270),~f'_2(1525),~K^*_2(1430)$

> We make predictions for the ratios of branching fractions of ${\bar B}^0$ and ${\bar B}^0_s$ decays into $J/ψ$ and the scalar mesons $f_0(1370),~f_0(1710)$ or tensor mesons $f_2(1270),~f'_2(1525),~K^*_2(1430)$. The theoretical approach is based on results of chiral unitary theory where these resonances are shown to be generated from the vector meson-vector meson interaction. Eight independent ratios can be predicted and comparison is made with the recent data on $\bar{B}^0_s$ decay into $J/ψf'_2(1525)$ versus the $\bar{B}^0_s$ decay into $J/ψf_2(1270)$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts eight independent ratios of branching fractions for $\bar{B}^0$ and $\bar{B}^0_s$ decays into $J/\psi$ plus scalar or tensor mesons, based on the assumption that these mesons are dynamically generated from vector meson-vector meson interactions in chiral unitary theory. The predictions are parameter-free except for two overall normalization constants that cancel in the ratios, and the only experimental comparison possible (ratio $R_7$) shows agreement within errors, though the theoretical value $(8.4 \pm 4.6) \times 10^{-2}$ overlaps only the upper edge of the experimental band $(1.0 \sim 3.8) \times 10^{-2}$.

## Method

The authors take the dominant weak decay mechanism for $\bar{B}^0$ ($\bar{B}^0_s$) into $J/\psi$ plus a $d\bar{d}$ ($s\bar{s}$) pair, then hadronize that pair by inserting a $\bar{u}u + \bar{d}d + \bar{s}s$ vacuum fluctuation to produce two vector mesons. These vector-vector pairs are allowed to rescatter in coupled channels using the $G_{VV}$ loop functions and resonance couplings $g_{VV,R}$ from the chiral unitary approach of Geng and Oset (2009), which generates the scalar and tensor resonances as vector-vector molecules. The amplitudes for $J/\psi$ plus a specific resonance are constructed from the weighted sum over vector-vector channels, and the decay widths are computed; the two unknown normalization constants $\tilde{V}_P$ (for scalars) and $\tilde{V}'_P$ (for tensors) cancel in the eight independent ratios.

## Key numbers

*   $R_1 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_0(1370)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_0(1710)] = 6.2 \pm 1.6$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_2 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f'_2(1525)] = 13.4 \pm 6.7$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_3 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi \bar{K}^*_2(1430)] = (3.0 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-2}$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_4 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_0(1710)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_0(1710)] = (7.7 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-3}$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_5 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] = (6.4 \pm 3.2) \times 10^{-1}$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_6 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_0(1370)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_0(1710)] = (1.1 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-2}$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   $R_7 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f'_2(1525)] = (8.4 \pm 4.6) \times 10^{-2}$ (theory), compared with experimental band $(1.0 \sim 3.8) \times 10^{-2}$.
*   $R_8 = \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_2(1270)] / \Gamma[\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi K^*_2(1430)] = (8.2 \pm 4.1) \times 10^{-1}$ (theory, no experimental value).
*   Experimental branching fraction $\mathcal{B}(\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f'_2(1525)) = (2.6^{+0.9}_{-0.6}) \times 10^{-4}$ (PDG).
*   Updated experimental branching fraction $\mathcal{B}(\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi f_2(1270)) = (3.0^{+1.2}_{-1.0}) \times 10^{-6}$ (from LHCb Aaij 2014, submitted to PDG).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Geng and Oset (2009), which dynamically generates the scalar and tensor resonances $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1710)$, $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $K^*_2(1430)$ from vector meson-vector meson interactions, to weak $B$ decays. It follows the same hadronization-plus-rescattering method used by Liang and Oset (2014) for low-lying scalar mesons in $B$ decays, but adapted to the vector-vector sector. The predictions provide eight testable ratios that can discriminate between the vector-molecule interpretation and alternative models (e.g., $q\bar{q}$ states, tetraquarks, or glueball admixtures as discussed by Klempt and Zaitsev (2007), Crede and Meyer (2008), and others); the only currently measurable ratio $R_7$ shows marginal agreement, and the authors encourage further experimental measurements and predictions from other theoretical groups.
