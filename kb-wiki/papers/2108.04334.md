# Holographic charm and bottom pentaquarks I: Mass spectra with spin effects

> We revisit the three non-strange pentaquarks $[\frac 12\frac 12^-]_{S=0,1}$ and $[\frac 12\frac 32^-]_{S=1}$ predicted using the holographic dual description, where chiral and heavy quark symmetry are manifest in the triple limit of a large number of colors, large quark mass and strong $^\prime$t Hooft gauge coupling. In the heavy quark limit, the pentaquarks with internal heavy quark spin $S$ are all degenerate. The holographic pentaquarks are dual to an instanton bound to heavy mesons in bulk, without the shortcomings related to the nature of the interaction and the choice of the hard core inherent to the molecular constructions. We explicitly derive the spin-spin and spin-orbit couplings arising from next to leading order in the heavy quark mass, and lift totally the internal spin degeneray, in fair agreement with the newly reported charmed pentaquarks from LHCb. New charm and bottom pentaquark states are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three non-strange pentaquark states predicted by holography, with spin-isospin assignments [1/2 1/2-]_{S=0,1} and [1/2 3/2-]_{S=1}, match the newly reported LHCb charmed pentaquarks, but only after including next-to-leading-order spin-orbit and spin-spin couplings that lift the degeneracy in internal heavy quark spin S=1, with the hedge that the agreement is "fair" and the computed masses are slightly smaller than observed (approximately 80 MeV difference).

## Method

The authors use the D4-D8-Dbar8 holographic setup with an extra heavy probe brane, where light baryons are dual to instantons and heavy-light baryons to instanton-heavy meson bound states in bulk. They derive the next-to-leading-order Lagrangian in 1/m_H (inverse heavy quark mass) from the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Chern-Simons actions, compute spin-orbit and spin-spin couplings, and solve the resulting Schroedinger equation numerically for the radial wavefunctions. The Kaluza-Klein scale M_KK is fixed to 0.475 GeV by fitting the Lambda_c mass to 2.286 GeV, and the heavy meson mass parameter m_H is determined from the D-meson mass of 1.87 GeV.

## Key numbers

- M_KK = 0.475 GeV (Kaluza-Klein scale fixed to reproduce Lambda_c mass)
- m_H = 3.66 M_KK (heavy meson mass parameter, from M_D = 1.87 GeV)
- Lambda_c mass: computed 2286 MeV, experimental 2286 MeV (fitted)
- Lambda_b mass: computed 5608 MeV, experimental 5620 MeV (predicted)
- Sigma_c (1/2+): computed 2557 MeV, experimental 2453 MeV
- Sigma_c (3/2+): computed 2596 MeV, experimental 2520 MeV
- P_c pentaquark masses: computed [4340/4360/4374] MeV, experimental [4312/4440/4457] MeV
- P_b pentaquark masses predicted: [11155/11163/11167] MeV
- Lambda_c* (1/2-): computed 2683 MeV, experimental 2595 MeV

## Context

This work extends the holographic heavy-light effective action developed by Liu, Nowak, and Zahed (2016-2017) by adding spin-orbit and spin-spin couplings at next-to-leading order in the heavy quark mass, lifting the degeneracy that exists in the heavy quark limit. It contrasts with molecular constructions (Wu 2010, Karliner 2015) by avoiding ambiguities in meson exchange interactions and hard-core choices, and with compact diquark models (Maiani 2015, Lebed 2015) and hadro-charmonium approaches (Eides 2019). The results enable predictions for bottom pentaquark states and excited charm pentaquarks that can be tested by future LHCb data.
