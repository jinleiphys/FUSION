# Chiral Effective Field Theory's Impact on Advancing Quantum Monte Carlo Methods

> Thirty years ago, Steven Weinberg published his seminal paper on "Nuclear Forces from chiral Lagrangians" which has revolutionized the field of theoretical nuclear physics. Nowadays, interactions derived from chiral effective field theory are routinely used to describe nuclear systems ranging from atomic nuclei to the dense matter explored in the core of neutron stars with theoretical uncertainty estimates. In our contribution to the special issue "Celebrating 30 years of Steven Weinberg's paper Nuclear Forces from Chiral Lagrangians", we focus on the impact that chiral effective field theory interactions have played in advancing microscopic studies of atomic nuclei and the nuclear-matter equation of state using quantum Monte Carlo methods.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions, when reformulated as local coordinate-space potentials, enable quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods to provide quasi-exact solutions of the nuclear many-body problem with quantified theoretical uncertainties, a capability not possible with traditional phenomenological interactions.

## Method

The authors review how local chiral EFT interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) are constructed by using Fierz-rearrangement freedom to replace nonlocal momentum-space terms with local coordinate-space operators, making them suitable for Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) methods. These QMC methods then solve the many-body Schrödinger equation via imaginary-time projection, using a transient estimation procedure to eliminate systematic biases from the trial wave function. The same Hamiltonians and computational tools are applied simultaneously to atomic nuclei, nuclear matter, and neutron-star matter.

## Key numbers

- AFDMC calculations with local chiral EFT interactions reproduce ground-state properties of nuclei up to A=16 (oxygen-16), including binding energies and charge radii.
- The study of short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei from deuterium to calcium-40 (A=40) demonstrates universal factorization of the many-body wave function at short distance.
- QMC calculations of pure neutron matter and beta-equilibrated matter produce pressures that agree with the extraction from gravitational-wave signal GW170817 by the LIGO collaboration.
- The analysis of neutron-star multimessenger observations in Dietrich et al. (2020) starts from an EOS set constrained by QMC calculations below 1.5 times nuclear saturation density rho_0.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering chiral EFT framework of Weinberg (1990) by adapting it for use with QMC methods, which were previously limited to phenomenological interactions such as the Argonne two-nucleon potentials (Wiringa 1995, 2002) and Urbana/Illinois three-nucleon forces (Pudliner 1995, Pieper 2001). The combination enables systematic studies connecting atomic nuclei to neutron-star matter with uncertainty estimates, as demonstrated in the multimessenger analysis of Dietrich et al. (2020) published in Science. Open questions include the consistent derivation of three- and many-body forces at higher orders and understanding differences between local and non-local regulator choices.
