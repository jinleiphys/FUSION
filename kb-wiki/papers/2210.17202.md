# Directed flow of $Λ$ from heavy-ion collisions and hyperon puzzle of neutron stars

> We examine the $Λ$ potential from the chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) via the $Λ$ directed flow from heavy-ion collisions. We implement the $Λ$ potential obtained from the $χ$EFT in a vector potential version of relativistic quantum molecular dynamics. We find that the $Λ$ potentials obtained from the $χ$EFT assuming weak momentum dependence reproduce the $Λ$ directed flow measured by the STAR collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan program. While the $Λ$ directed flow is not very sensitive to the density dependence of the potential, the directed flow at large rapidities is susceptible to the momentum dependence. Thus understanding the directed flow of hyperons in a wide range of beam energy and rapidity is helpful in understanding hyperon potentials in dense matter.

## Key claim
The authors find that the Λ potential from chiral effective field theory (χEFT) assuming weak momentum dependence reproduces the Λ directed flow measured by the STAR collaboration, but the directed flow cannot discriminate between χEFT potentials with 2-body versus 2+3-body interactions, and the flow at large rapidities is sensitive to the momentum dependence rather than the density dependence of the potential.

## Method
The authors implemented the Λ single-particle potential from χEFT, parameterized as a function of baryon density and momentum, into the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics with Lorentz-vector potential (RQMDv) mode of the JAM event generator. They simulated mid-central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from √s_NN = 3.0 to 19.6 GeV and compared the calculated Λ directed flow v1 and its slope dv1/dy with STAR collaboration data from the Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key numbers
- The Λ potential from χEFT was fitted in the density range ρ/ρ0 ≤ 3, using the saturation density ρ0 = 0.168 fm⁻³
- The directed flow was calculated in the impact parameter range 4.6 fm < b < 9.4 fm for mid-central Au+Au collisions
- At √s_NN = 4.5 GeV, the calculated Λ directed flow with the GKW3 potential (2+3-body interactions) reproduces the STAR data
- The directed flow slope was compared with STAR data across the beam energy range √s_NN = 3.0 to 19.6 GeV
- The transverse momentum cut used was 0.4 GeV ≤ pT ≤ 2.0 GeV, matching the experimental analysis

## Context
This work extends the approach of Nara et al. (JAMRQMDv, 2020) who explained proton directed flow using a purely hadronic equation of state, and applies it to hyperon flow for the first time. The Λ potentials from χEFT were taken from Gerstung, Kaiser, and Weise (GKW, 2020) for density dependence and Kohno (2018) for momentum dependence. The results provide the first experimental support from heavy-ion collisions for a strongly repulsive Λ potential at high densities, which is relevant to the hyperon puzzle of neutron stars. The work enables future studies of Ξ and Σ flows and their relation to dense matter equations of state.
