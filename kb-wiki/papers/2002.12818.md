# Charge-dependent flow induced by electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

> The colliding heavy ions create extremely strong magnetic and electric fields that significantly affect the evolution of the produced quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The knowledge of these fields is essential for establishing the role of topological fluctuations in the QGP through the chiral magnetic effect and related anomaly-induced phenomena. In this talk, we describe our work on the evolution of the QGP in electric and magnetic fields in the framework of hydrodynamics supplemented, in a perturbative fashion, by the dynamical electromagnetism. The evolution of the QGP fluid is described within the iEBE-VISHNU framework. We find that the electromagnetically induced currents result in a charge-odd directed flow $Δv_1$ and a charge-odd $Δv_3$ flow both of which are odd in rapidity. While the predicted magnitude of these charge-odd flows agrees with the data from RHIC and LHC, the sign of the predicted asymmetry between the flows of positive and negative hadrons is opposite to the data.

## Key claim

The electromagnetically induced currents in heavy ion collisions produce charge-odd directed flow Δv₁ and charge-odd Δv₃ flow that are odd in rapidity; while the predicted magnitude of these flows agrees with RHIC and LHC data, the sign of the predicted asymmetry between positive and negative hadron flows is opposite to the data, indicating that the electromagnetic fields of expected magnitude are present but the magnetohydrodynamic treatment requires improvement.

## Method

The authors describe the evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fluid within the iEBE-VISHNU framework, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance and beginning at τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, with an equation of state based upon lattice QCD calculations. They solve Maxwell equations on top of this hydrodynamical background in a perturbative fashion (no back-reaction included), assuming a constant conductivity, to evaluate the currents induced by electromagnetism. The calculation yields momentum distributions for hadrons with different charge, from which the difference between flow for positively and negatively charged hadrons Δvₙ ≡ vₙ(h⁺) − vₙ(h⁻) is evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic calculation begins at proper time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- Electric and magnetic fields are computed in the transverse plane at z=0 at proper time τ=1 fm/c after a Pb+Pb collision with 20-30% centrality (impact parameters in the range 6.24 fm < b < 9.05 fm) and collision energy √s = 2.76 ATeV
- The charge-odd flow predictions are compared to data from RHIC at 200 GeV and LHC at two collision energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Gursoy 2014, Gursoy 2018) on the effects of electromagnetic fields on collective flow of charged hadrons, using a perturbative treatment of electromagnetism on top of conventional hydrodynamical evolution. The results are compared to data from RHIC (Adam 2019) and LHC (Acharya 2019) showing that the predicted magnitude of charge-odd flows agrees with experiment but the sign is opposite, motivating future improvements including time-dependent conductivity and full magnetohydrodynamical simulations, with a recent pilot MHD simulation (Inghirami 2019) showing the same sign as the perturbative result.
