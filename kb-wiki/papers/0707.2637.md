# Holographic Nuclear Matter in AdS/QCD

> We study the physics with finite nuclear density in the framework of AdS/QCD with holographic baryon field included. Based on a mean field type approach, we introduce the nucleon density as a bi-fermion condensate of the lowest mode of the baryon field and calculate the density dependence of the chiral condensate and the nucleon mass. We observe that the chiral condensate as well as the mass of nucleon decrease with increasing nuclear density. We also consider the mass splitting of charged vector mesons in iso-spin asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors introduce nuclear density in AdS/QCD via a mean-field bi-fermion condensate of the lowest baryon mode, rather than through a baryon chemical potential, and find that both the chiral condensate and nucleon mass decrease with density, though the decrease is smaller than in conventional QCD effective theories.

## Method

The authors model finite nuclear density by turning on a non-zero 5D bi-nucleon condensate \(\langle \bar N_1 N_2 + \bar N_2 N_1 \rangle\) proportional to the 4D scalar density \(\rho_s\), which modifies the equation of motion for the bulk scalar field \(X\) and thus the chiral condensate. They solve the coupled equations for the scalar profile and the nucleon wave functions iteratively to obtain the density-dependent nucleon mass, and separately solve for the time component of the iso-spin vector field \(V_0^3\) to compute the mass splitting of charged \(\rho\)-mesons in iso-spin asymmetric matter.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and nucleon mass \(m_N\) both decrease with nuclear density; at \(c = \rho_B/\rho_0 = 1\) (normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 \sim 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)), the reduction is smaller than the \(\sim 20\%\) drop predicted by conventional effective theories (e.g., BR91, Weise, KKL, qC).
* The vacuum parameters used are: current quark mass \(m_q = 2.34\ \text{MeV}\), chiral condensate \(\sigma_0 = (311\ \text{MeV})^3\), IR cutoff \(z_m = (330\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\), and coupling \(g = 9.3\) to reproduce the vacuum nucleon mass \(m_N = 0.94\ \text{GeV}\).
* The mass of charged \(\rho\)-mesons splits in iso-spin asymmetric matter; for a given proton fraction \(x = \rho_p/\rho_B\), the mass decreases with increasing \(|\mu_I|\) (negative iso-spin chemical potential relevant for neutron stars), but the results become invalid at small \(x\) due to pion condensation.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up AdS/QCD hard-wall model with holographic baryons (Ho, 2007) to finite nuclear density, contrasting with earlier approaches that introduced baryon chemical potential via the boundary value of the \(U(1)_B\) gauge field (Kim, 2006; Nakamura, 2006; Kobayashi, 2006; Domokos, 2007). The results enable future studies of dense matter in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, and the authors suggest that incorporating metric back-reaction (GMTY, Sin, 2007) or using density-dependent chiral condensates from other effective theories (qC) could improve the model.
