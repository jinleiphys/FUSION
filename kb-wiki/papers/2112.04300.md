# Tetraquarks: relativistic corrections and other issues

> We discuss the effect of relativistic kinematics on the binding energy of multiquark states. For a given potential, the use of relativistic kinematics lowers the energy by a larger amount for the threshold made of two mesons than for a tetraquark, so that its binding is weakened. Some other issues associated with exotic hadrons are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic kinematics lowers the energy of the two-meson threshold by a larger amount than the energy of a tetraquark, so that the binding energy of the tetraquark is weakened compared to non-relativistic calculations; a larger quark-to-antiquark mass ratio is required to achieve stability when relativistic effects are included.

## Method

The authors compare non-relativistic (NR) and semi-relativistic (SR) kinetic energy operators in a Hamiltonian describing mesons and tetraquarks, using a linear confining potential \(v(r)=b\,r\) and a Coulomb-plus-linear potential. They solve the four-body problem for \(QQ\bar q\bar q\) tetraquarks using variational calculations with correlated Gaussian wave functions, with the color part of the wave function frozen to \(\bar 3 3\), and also consider mixing of color \(\bar 3 3\) and \(6\bar 6\).

## Key numbers

- For a \(q\bar q\) meson with a pure linear potential (\(a=0\), \(b=1\)), the NR and SR energies are compared as a function of the quark mass \(m\) (Fig. 1).
- For a \(QQ\bar q\bar q\) tetraquark with \(v(r)=r\), the NR and SR energies are compared as a function of the mass ratio \(M/m\), with \(M^{-1}+m^{-1}\) fixed so that the NR threshold remains constant (Fig. 2).
- For the positronium molecule Ps\(_2\) (\(e^+e^+e^-e^-\)), the excess of binding relative to the threshold is about \(3.2\%\).
- For the hydrogen molecule H\(_2\) (\(ppe^-e^-\)), the excess of binding relative to the threshold is about \(17.4\%\).
- For Ps\(_2\) in atomic units (\(m=\hbar=\alpha=1\)), the best variational energy with a simple wave function is \(E\simeq -0.367\), demonstrating stability against dissociation into an electron and a positronium ion.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tetraquark stability in the chromoelectric limit, starting with Ader (1981) and Carlson (1987), which showed that \(QQ\bar q\bar q\) becomes bound if the mass ratio \(M/m\) is large enough. The authors note that in actual quark model calculations, the stability of \(cc\bar u\bar d\) (the \(T_{cc}^+\) tetraquark discovered by LHCb in 2021) arises from a cooperative effect of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic contributions, as shown by Janc (2004) and Barnea (2006). The paper also draws an analogy with hydrogen-like molecules in atomic physics, comparing the stability of Ps\(_2\) and H\(_2\) as studied by Adamowski (1971, 1993) and Hylleraas and Ore (1947). The results enable more refined quark model calculations that account for relativistic kinematics, and the authors stress that kinematics is just one facet of relativistic corrections to be applied to the quark model.
