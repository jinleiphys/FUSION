# Analytic parametrizations of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1440)$ form factors inspired by Light-Front holography

> We present analytic parametrizations for the $γ^\ast N \to N(1440)$ form factors derived from light-front holography in leading twist approximation. The new parametrizations describe the electromagnetic form factors using analytic functions dependent on the masses of the $ρ$ meson and respective radial excitations, as well as the masses of the nucleon and the resonance $N(1440)$. The free parameters of the model, associated with three independent couplings, are interpreted as bare couplings, and are fixed by the nucleon data for large $Q^2$. The proposed parametrizations compare remarkably well with the empirical data for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, corroborating the dominant role of the valence quark degrees of freedom in the $γ^\ast N \to N(1440)$ transition.

# Analytic parametrizations of the γ*N → N(1440) form factors inspired by Light-Front holography

## Key claim

The paper proposes that analytic parametrizations of the γ*N → N(1440) transition form factors, derived from light-front holography in leading twist (three-valence-quark) approximation and expressed using empirical hadron masses, describe the experimental data remarkably well for Q² > 2 GeV², but the abstract's claim of "remarkably well" comparison is qualified by the fact that the parametrizations are not expected to be accurate at low Q² where meson cloud effects (omitted in leading twist) become significant.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the Dirac (F*₁N) and Pauli (F*₂N) transition form factors from light-front holography in leading twist (τ=3) approximation, where the nucleon and Roper are treated as three-valence-quark systems. The form factors are expressed as functions of Q² with poles at the empirical masses of the ρ meson, its radial excitations (mρ, mρ1, mρ2), the nucleon mass (MN), and the Roper mass (MN1 ≈ MR ≈ 1.44 GeV). The three free parameters (gV, ηp, ηn) are interpreted as bare couplings and fixed by fitting nucleon elastic form factor data for Q² > 1.5 GeV², then applied without further adjustment to the γ*N → N(1440) transition.

## Key numbers

- The bare couplings are fixed as gV = 1.42 ± 0.15, ηp = 0.39 ± 0.03, and ηn = −(0.34 ± 0.02) from fits to nucleon data for Q² > 1.5 GeV²
- The parametrizations agree with empirical data for Q² > 2 GeV² for the γ*N → N(1440) transition form factors
- The Roper Pauli form factor F*₂p is described in the region 0.2–3.5 GeV²
- For the nucleon elastic form factors, the model agrees with data for Q² > 1.5 GeV² except for GEp where agreement holds only for Q² > 4 GeV²
- The highest datapoint for the proton ratio μpGEp/GMp at Q² ≈ 8.5 GeV² differs from the model estimate by only 1.3 standard deviations
- The holographic mass scale κ = 0.385 GeV reproduces ρ meson masses within 10% error
- The holographic estimate MR ≈ 4κ ≈ 1.52 GeV deviates from the empirical Roper mass (≈ 1.44 GeV) by 5%

## Context

This work extends the light-front holographic approach of Brodsky and Teramond (2015, 2011, 2012), who showed that accurate descriptions of nucleon form factors and the γ*N → N(1440) Dirac form factor could be obtained using analytic expressions based on ρ meson masses in leading twist approximation. The present paper provides the first simple analytic expression for the Roper Pauli form factor F*₂p, which previous work had not addressed. The parametrizations enable future extension to higher mass nucleon excitations and can be tested by JLab 12-GeV upgrade data for Q² > 6 GeV², where the valence quark estimates are expected to be very accurate.
