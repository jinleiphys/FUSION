# Finite-Temperature Gluon Condensate with Renormalization Group Flow Equations

> Within a self-consistent proper-time Renormalization Group (RG) approach we investigate an effective QCD trace anomaly realization with dilatons and determine the finite-temperature behavior of the gluon condensate. Fixing the effective model at vanishing temperature to the glueball mass and the bag constant a possible gluonic phase transition is explored in detail. Within the RG framework the full non-truncated dilaton potential analysis is compared with a truncated potential version.

## Key claim

Within a self-consistent proper-time Renormalization Group approach, the finite-temperature behavior of the gluon condensate is determined from an effective QCD trace anomaly realization with dilatons, but the analysis is restricted to the local potential approximation and the predictive power for temperatures above approximately 250 MeV is limited because the temperature dependence of the initial values at the ultraviolet cutoff scale is neglected.

## Method

The authors employ a proper-time Renormalization Group flow equation in the local potential approximation, regularized by a Schwinger proper-time representation with a blocking function that introduces an infrared cutoff scale k. They solve the resulting flow equation numerically by discretizing the dilaton field on a grid, starting from a logarithmic dilaton potential at an ultraviolet cutoff scale Λ = 2.0 GeV and evolving to the infrared, with initial parameters fixed to reproduce a chosen glueball mass and bag constant at zero temperature. Finite-temperature effects are incorporated via the Matsubara formalism, and the full non-truncated dilaton potential analysis is compared with a truncated polynomial expansion around the scale-dependent minimum.

## Key numbers

- Glueball mass input value: m_χ = 1.5 GeV, motivated by lattice results.
- Bag constant input range: B^{1/4} = 0.14 to 0.24 GeV.
- For initial parameters h^{1/4} = 0.51 GeV, χ_0 = 0.137 GeV, and Λ = 2.0 GeV, the evolved infrared values are B^{1/4} = 0.276 GeV and m_χ = 1.502 GeV.
- For the smaller bag constant B^{1/4} = 0.149 GeV (obtained from h^{1/4} = 0.34 GeV and χ_0 = 0.068 GeV at Λ = 2.0 GeV), the condensate starts to decrease at T ~ 190 MeV.
- For the larger bag constant B^{1/4} = 0.276 GeV, the onset of a significant shift in the minimum occurs above T ~ 0.4 GeV.
- The UV cutoff dependence of the bag constant is small: for Λ = 2.0, 2.1, 2.2, and 2.3 GeV, B^{1/4} = 0.276, 0.270, 0.264, and 0.258 GeV respectively, while the glueball mass decreases from 1.502 to 1.337 GeV over the same range.

## Context

This work extends the effective dilaton model of Campbell et al. (1990) by using a non-perturbative Renormalization Group method to study the gluon condensate at finite temperature, circumventing the perturbative difficulties that prevented a determination of the phase transition order in that earlier work. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Karsch (1995) and with the finite-temperature dilaton analysis of Sollfrank et al. (1999), confirming that the critical temperature is dominated by the bag constant value. The approach enables a direct calculation of the full effective dilaton potential at any field value, not just at the minimum, and the fixed-point analysis suggests no non-trivial non-singular fixed point exists within the local potential approximation.
