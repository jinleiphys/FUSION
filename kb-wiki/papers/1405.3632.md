# Onset of the ridge structure in AA, pA and pp collisions

> It is shown that the anomalous sharp increasing of the strength of the near-side ridge structures observed in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 62 GeV and $\sqrt{s}=$ 200 GeV and the onset of the ridge structure in pPb and in pp collisions can be naturally explained in the framework of string percolation. In all the cases the near-side strength reflects the collision area covered by the strings stretched between the colliding objects and therefore it is related to the shape of their profile functions. The dependence of the pseudorapidty and azimuthal widths on multiplicty and energy is qualitatively explained.

# Onset of the ridge structure in AA, pA and pp collisions

## Key claim

The anomalous sharp increase in near-side ridge strength observed in Au-Au collisions at 62 and 200 GeV, and the onset of ridge structure in pPb and pp collisions, can be explained by string percolation when the string density approaches a universal critical density, but only if the collision area profile (homogeneous versus Gaussian/Wood-Saxon) is accounted for via a sigmoid function A(ρ) rather than the simple 1−e^{−ρ} form.

## Method

The authors use the dual parton model (DPM) to compute the number and rapidity distribution of strings in pp, pPb, and Au-Au collisions. They then apply string percolation theory: strings overlap into clusters as density ρ increases, with cluster properties determined by the color field sum (scaling as √n) and the fraction of collision area covered by strings. The near-side ridge strength A₁ is modeled as c·A(ρ)^{3/2}, where A(ρ) = 1/[1 + a·e^{−(ρ−ρ_c)/b}] with critical density ρ_c = 1.5, and fitted to experimental data from STAR (Au-Au at 62 and 200 GeV), CMS (pp at 7 TeV), and ALICE (pPb at 5.02 TeV). The pseudorapidity width σ_{Δη} and azimuthal width σ_{Δφ} are derived from the rapidity length of effective clusters and the transverse correlation length, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Critical string density for percolation: ρ_c = 1.5 for Gaussian/Wood-Saxon profiles (ρ_c = 1.2 for homogeneous profile)
- Single string transverse radius: r₀ ≈ 0.2 fm (from lattice and field correlator studies)
- For Au-Au at 200 GeV, string density ρ ranges from 0.6 to 3.0 across centralities
- For pPb at 5.02 TeV, ρ ranges from 0.8 (at N_ch = 50) to 2.3 (at N_ch = 330)
- For pp at 7 TeV, ρ ranges from 0.25 (N_ch = 18, minimum bias) to 0.7 (N_ch = 110)
- Ridge onset in pp occurs at N_ch > 110 (ρ ≈ 0.7); in pPb at N_ch ≳ 50 (ρ ≈ 0.8)
- Fitted parameters for Au-Au at 200 and 62 GeV: a = 1.5, b = 0.75, c = 0.93 (200 GeV) and c = 0.80 (62 GeV)
- Fitted parameters for pPb and pp: a = 1.5, b = 0.35, c = 0.21 (pPb) and c = 0.57 (pp)
- Pseudorapidity width constant: c₁ = 0.23
- Azimuthal width constants: c₂ = 0.866 (Au-Au 200 GeV), c₂ = 0.890 (Au-Au 62 GeV)
- Minimum energy for single string creation: s₀ = 1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the string percolation framework previously applied to multiplicity and transverse momentum distributions (refs. 24, 25, 28, 29) to the specific observable of near-side ridge correlations. It directly addresses the anomalous centrality dependence observed by STAR in Au-Au collisions at 62 and 200 GeV (ref. 22), where the ridge strength slope changes at N_ch ≈ 120 and N_ch ≈ 130 respectively. The model provides a complementary picture to the Color Glass Condensate/glasma approach (refs. 13-18), which explains ridge correlations via saturation of color flux tubes with length 1/Q_s. The percolation framework naturally describes the transition from low to high density and predicts the onset of ridge structure in pp and pPb collisions, which was later confirmed experimentally. The results enable future Monte Carlo simulations (ref. 44) for more detailed comparison with azimuthal and pseudorapidity width data.
