# The Nc dependencies of baryon masses: Analysis with Lattice QCD and Effective Theory

> Baryon masses at varying values of Nc and light quark masses are studied with Lattice QCD and the results are analyzed in a low energy effective theory based on a combined framework of the 1/Nc and Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory expansions. Lattice QCD results for Nc = 3, 5 and 7 obtained in quenched calculations, as well as results for unquenched calculations for Nc = 3, are used for the analysis. The results are consistent with a previous analysis of Nc = 3 Lattice QCD results, and in addition permit the determination of sub-leading in 1/Nc effects in the spin-flavor singlet component of the baryon masses as well as in the hyperfine splittings.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that baryon masses from quenched lattice QCD at \(N_c = 3, 5, 7\) and unquenched results at \(N_c = 3\) are consistent with a combined \(1/N_c\) and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory expansion, but the analysis is limited by the range of pion masses (350 MeV to 1400 MeV) and the need to combine quenched and unquenched data, which is justified only because quenching artifacts are subleading in \(1/N_c\).

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations for \(N_c = 3, 5, 7\) using Wilson plaquette gauge action with clover fermions on \(16^3 \times 32\) volumes, matching bare gauge couplings via the Sommer parameter \(r_1\) to keep discretization effects comparable. Baryon masses and hyperfine splittings were computed for multiple quark masses, and the results were fitted to a mass formula derived from the combined \(1/N_c\) and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory expansions at order \(\xi^3\) (where \(\xi\) links \(1/N_c\) and chiral orders). The analysis also incorporated unquenched \(N_c = 3\) data from other collaborations (PACS-CS, LHPC, ETMC, MILC) to extend the range of pion masses.

## Key numbers

*   For \(N_c = 5\), the rotor spectrum formula predicts the ratio of hyperfine splittings between \(S = 3/2, 1/2\) and \(S = 5/2, 3/2\) states to be 1.66, while lattice QCD gives values between 1.5 and 1.6 across all pion masses.
*   For \(N_c = 7\), the rotor spectrum formula predicts the ratio of hyperfine splittings between \(S = 7/2, 5/2\) and \(S = 3/2, 1/2\) states to be 2.33, while lattice QCD gives values between 2.0 and 2.2.
*   The lattice spacing was approximately 0.08 fm, determined from the Sommer parameter \(r_1 = 0.31\) fm, with \(r_1/a = 3.90(3)\) for \(N_c = 3\), 3.77(3) for \(N_c = 5\), and 3.91(2) for \(N_c = 7\).
*   The chiral limit value of the pion decay constant (scaled by \(\sqrt{3/N_c}\)) from FLAG is \(\mathring{F}_\pi = 86 \pm 1\) MeV, used to define dimensionless ratios for comparing quenched and unquenched data.

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice QCD study by DeGrand (2012) on baryon masses at varying \(N_c\) and applies the combined \(1/N_c\) and chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Jenkins (1995), Flores-Mendieta (1998, 2006), and Calle Cordon (2012, 2013). The analysis enables the determination of subleading \(1/N_c\) effects in the spin-flavor singlet component of baryon masses and hyperfine splittings, and it tests the \(\xi\)-expansion power counting scheme. Future work requires dynamical quark simulations at varying \(N_c\) with lower pion masses to improve the precision of the low-energy constants.
