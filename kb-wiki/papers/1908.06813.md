# Faddeev calculations on lambda hypertriton with potentials from Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko theory

> Effective lambda-proton and lambda-neutron potentials, restored from theoretical scattering phases through Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko theory, are tested on a lambda hypertriton through three-body calculations. The lambda hypertriton is treated as a three-body system consisting of lambda-proton, lambda-neutron and proton-neutron subsystems. Binding energy and root-mean-square radius are computed for the ground state of lambda hypertriton ($J^π=1/2^+$). In coordinate space, the dynamics of the system is described using a set of coupled hyperradial equations obtained from the Differential Faddeev Equations. By solving the eigenvalue problem derived from this set of coupled hyperradial equations, the binding energy and root-mean-square matter radius computed are found to be -2.462 MeV and 7.00 fm, respectively. The potentials are also shown to display a satisfactory convergence behaviour.

## Key claim

The lambda-nucleon potentials derived from Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko inverse scattering theory (GLM-YN0) produce a lambda hypertriton binding energy of -2.462 MeV and a root-mean-square matter radius of 7.00 fm, but these potentials do not include lambda-sigma conversion, which may limit their applicability to heavy hypernuclei.

## Method

The lambda hypertriton was treated as a three-body system (proton, neutron, lambda) and solved using the Differential Faddeev Equations in hyperspherical coordinates. The lambda-proton and lambda-neutron potentials (GLM-YN0) were fitted as sums of three Gaussians from data restored via Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko theory on theoretical scattering phases, while the neutron-proton interaction used the spin-averaged Malfliet-Tjon-V potential. The coupled hyperradial equations were solved by expanding the wavefunctions on a basis of normalised associated Laguerre polynomials, with the eigenvalue problem solved via inverse iteration.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of lambda hypertriton ground state (J^π = 1/2+): -2.462 MeV
- Root-mean-square matter radius of lambda hypertriton: 7.00 fm
- Mass parameter used in calculations: ℏ²/2m = 20.7281 MeV·fm²
- Maximum quantum numbers used: K_max = 8, S_xmax = 1.0, ℓ_xmax = 2, ℓ_ymax = 2
- Lambda hyperon mass: 1.198 u (computed from 1115.683 / 931.5)

## Context

This work extends the application of inverse scattering theory (Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko) to hypernuclear physics, complementing traditional approaches such as Meson-Exchange SU(3) theory (deSwart 1971, Rijken 1993), Meson-Exchange SU(6) theory (Fujiwara 1996), and Chiral Effective Field theory (Polinder 2006, Haidenbauer 2013). The computed binding energy of -2.462 MeV falls within the range of experimental values (-2.35 ± 0.05 MeV from emulsion experiments and -2.47 ± 0.31 MeV from helium bubble chambers) and is comparable to results from other theoretical potentials such as fss2 (-2.514 MeV) and NSC97f (-2.41 MeV). The larger r.m.s. radius (7.00 fm versus 4.9 fm from Nemura 2000) is attributed to underbinding of the deuteron by the Malfliet-Tjon-V potential. The absence of lambda-sigma conversion in the GLM-YN0 potentials is identified as a limitation requiring future work, and further computations are needed to test Charge Symmetry Breaking.
