# Spin-orbit induced backflow in neutron matter with auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo

> The energy per particle of zero-temperature neutron matter is investigated, with particular emphasis on the role of the $\vec L\cdot\vec S$ interaction. An analysis of the importance of explicit spin--orbit correlations in the description of the system is carried out by the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method. The improved nodal structure of the guiding function, constructed by explicitly considering these correlations, lowers the energy. The proposed spin--backflow orbitals can conveniently be used also in Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei.

## Key claim

The spin-backflow orbitals lower the AFDMC energy per particle of neutron matter by about 5%, but this improvement is too small to resolve the discrepancy between AFDMC and variational chain summation (VCS) results for the spin-orbit contribution, particularly at higher densities.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of neutron matter using the Argonne \(v_{8'}\) two-body potential plus the Urbana IX three-body interaction (AU8' Hamiltonian). They modified the guiding function by replacing plane-wave orbitals with spin-backflow orbitals that incorporate explicit spin-orbit correlations, parameterized by a strength parameter \(\beta\) derived from the FHNC/SOC spin-orbit correlation function \(f_b(r)\).

## Key numbers

- At density \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) for 14 neutrons with the AU8' interaction, the JSB guiding function (with \(\beta=1\)) gives an energy per particle of 18.76(5) MeV, compared to 19.76(6) MeV for the JS guiding function.
- At density \(\rho = 2\rho_0\) for 14 neutrons with the AU8' interaction, the JSB guiding function gives an energy per particle of 46.8(1) MeV, compared to 48.4(1) MeV for the JS guiding function.
- The spin-orbit contribution \(\Delta E_{LS}\) for 14 neutrons at \(\rho_0\) with the JSB guiding function is \(-1.2(1)\) MeV, compared to \(-0.14(6)\) MeV with the JS guiding function.
- For 66 neutrons at \(\rho_0\) with the AU8' interaction, the JSB guiding function gives an energy per particle of 52.9(2) MeV, compared to 54.4(6) MeV for the JS guiding function.

## Context

This work extends previous AFDMC calculations by Sarsa et al. (2003) and Fantoni et al. (2001) on neutron matter ground state and magnetic properties, which showed a discrepancy with VCS calculations regarding the spin-orbit energy contribution. The spin-backflow orbitals introduced here can also be used in Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of light nuclei, as noted by Pudliner et al. (1997), Pieper et al. (2002), and Carlson et al. (2003). The AFDMC results with JSB guiding function agree reasonably well with constrained GFMC simulations, but the VCS estimate of \(\Delta E_{LS} = -3.8\) MeV at \(\rho_0\) remains significantly larger than the AFDMC value of \(-1.2(1)\) MeV.
