# Effective Lagrangian Study of $γp \to K^+ Λ$ (spin 3/2 resonances and their off-shell effects)

> The purpose of the present discussion is to supplement the talk, by B.~Saghai at this workshop, on the study of the electromagnetic production of strangeness on the nucleon based upon effective Lagrangian methods. Here we focus on the proper treatment of the spin 3/2 resonances and their associated effects due to the spin 1/2 component of the corresponding fields when they are {\it off the mass shell}.

# Effective Lagrangian Study of γp → K⁺Λ (Spin 3/2 Resonances and Their Off-Shell Effects)

## Key claim

The proper treatment of spin 3/2 resonances using the Rarita-Schwinger formalism, including their off-shell spin 1/2 components via a free off-shell parameter V, improves the fit to existing data for γp → K⁺Λ. The undesirable increase in the integrated cross section above Eγ = 1.5 GeV seen in previous models is considerably tempered, and the large-angle differential cross section at 2.0 GeV changes drastically in favor of preliminary Bonn data.

## Method

The author constructed a tree-level (isobar) amplitude for γ(*)p → K⁺Λ including s-channel nucleon and spin 3/2 resonance exchanges, u-channel hyperonic resonance exchanges, and t-channel strange meson exchanges. The spin 3/2 resonances were treated with the full Rarita-Schwinger propagator containing spin 1/2 components off-shell, and interaction Lagrangians were built with off-shell parameters (W, X, Y, Z) that modify only the spin 1/2 part of the Rarita-Schwinger field. A χ² fit to existing data was performed varying the coupling strengths and off-shell parameters.

## Key numbers

- Integrated cross section improvement: the undesirable increase for EγLab > 1.5 GeV in previous models (e.g., SL) was considerably tempered by the correct spin 3/2 treatment.
- Differential cross section at EγLab = 2.0 GeV: the large-angle behavior changed drastically in favor of yet preliminary data from Bonn.
- The off-shell parameter V enters to modify only the spin 1/2 part of the Rarita-Schwinger field; the spin 3/2 resonance pole part of the amplitudes is independent of V.
- The third electromagnetic coupling term ℒγpR(3) contributes only to electroproduction, with preliminary results indicating a rather minor effect.

## Context

This work extends the proper spin 3/2 resonance treatment developed by the Rensselaer Polytechnic (RPI) group for pion photoproduction in the Δ(1232) region to kaon production. It corrects the earlier approach of Renard and Renard (early 1970s) which did not recognize the propagation of spin 1/2 components off-shell, and addresses the unwanted u-channel singularity in the approach of SL which included spin 3/2 and 5/2 resonances only in the s-channel. The results enable future investigation of resonances with spin higher than 3/2 within covariant schemes and provide predictions for polarization observables in both photo- and electroproduction that distinguish between treatments with and without off-shell considerations.
