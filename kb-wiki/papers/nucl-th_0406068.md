# Effective-range approach and scaling laws for electromagnetic strength in neutron-halo nuclei

> We study low-lying multipole strength in neutron-halo nuclei. The strength depends only on a few low-energy constants: the neutron separation energy, the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the bound state wave function, and the scattering length that contains the information on the interaction in the continuum. The shape of the transition probability shows a characteristic dependence on few scaling parameters and the angular momenta. The total E1 strength is related to the root-mean-square radius of the neutron wave function in the ground state and shows corresponding scaling properties. We apply our approach to the E1 strength distribution of 11Be.

# Effective-range approach and scaling laws for electromagnetic strength in neutron-halo nuclei

## Key claim

The low-lying electric multipole strength in one-neutron halo nuclei can be described using only a few low-energy constants (neutron separation energy, asymptotic normalization coefficient, and scattering length), with the continuum interaction effects entering as corrections that are small for s→p transitions but significant for p→s transitions, provided the scattering lengths are of "natural" size (c_l ~ O(1)) and there are no resonances or subthreshold states in the low-lying continuum.

## Method

The authors apply the effective-range (ER) approximation to the phase shifts of the neutron-core continuum states, truncating at the lowest-order term tan(δ_l) = -(x c_l γ)^(2l+1) with reduced scattering length c_l and scaling parameters x = k/q and γ = qR. They derive analytical expressions for the reduced transition probability dB(Eλ)/dE and the total transition strength B(Eλ) in terms of dimensionless shape functions S and T, then fit the reduced scattering lengths and asymptotic normalization constant to experimental Coulomb dissociation data for ¹¹Be using a χ² minimization.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of ¹¹Be: 504 keV, corresponding to γ = 0.4132 for a nuclear radius R = 2.78 fm
- Asymptotic normalization constant from ER fit: C₀ = 0.724(8) fm⁻¹/²
- Spectroscopic factor from ER approach: C²S = 0.704(15)
- Ground state rms radius: 7.172 fm, corresponding to total B(E1) = 1.645 e²fm² from the sum rule
- Total strength after applying spectroscopic factor: 1.159(36) e²fm²
- Experimental continuum strength exhausts 78(5)% of the sum rule
- Ground state transition probability to first excited state: B(E1) = 0.116(12) e²fm² (10(1)% of sum rule)
- Reduced scattering lengths from ER fit: c₁^(3/2) = -0.41(86,-20), c₁^(1/2) = 2.77(13,-14)
- Spectroscopic factor from Woods-Saxon fit: C²S = 0.696(15)

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach previously applied to the deuteron (Blatt and Weisskopf, 1979) and to ground state properties of halo nuclei (Esbensen, 1997) to the full electromagnetic strength distribution in one-neutron halo nuclei. It generalizes earlier treatments that neglected continuum interactions (Nakamura et al., 1994; Otsuka et al., 1994) or considered only s-wave interactions with zero-range potentials (Kalbermann, 1996). The approach enables extraction of asymptotic normalization constants and spectroscopic factors from Coulomb dissociation data without requiring optical potential parameters from elastic scattering, and can be extended to higher multipolarities and proton halo nuclei.
