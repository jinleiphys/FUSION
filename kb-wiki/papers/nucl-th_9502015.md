# Comment on "Role of heavy meson exchange in near threshold N N --> d pi"

> In a recent paper by C. J. Horowitz (Phys. Rev. C {\bf 48}, 2920 (1993)) a heavy meson exchange is incorporated into threshold NN --> d pi to enhance the grossly underestimated cross section. However, that calculation uses an unjustified assumption on the initial and final momenta, which causes an overestimate of this effect by a factor of 3--4. I point out that the inclusion of the Delta(1232) isobar increases the cross section significantly even at threshold.

# Comment on "Role of heavy meson exchange in near threshold NN → dπ"

## Key claim

The heavy meson exchange (HME) mechanism proposed by Horowitz (1993) to enhance the underestimated threshold cross section for NN → dπ uses an unjustified approximation for the initial and final momenta, overestimating the HME effect by a factor of 3–4; when the approximation is corrected, the HME contribution becomes too small to explain the missing cross section, while inclusion of the Δ(1232) isobar increases the cross section significantly even at threshold.

## Method

The author recalculates the HME radial integrals for σ and ω meson exchange using the correct momentum operator (p' + p) instead of the approximation 2p used by Horowitz (1993), which is invalid because the HME potential does not commute with the momentum operator. The calculation employs Bonn potential A(R) σ and ω couplings and form factors with Reid soft core wave functions, and also includes the Δ(1232) isobar via coupled Schrödinger equations for a modified Reid soft core potential with NΔ admixtures in the initial NN state.

## Key numbers

- The HME contribution to the amplitude is decreased by a factor of 4 when the momentum approximation is corrected, changing the increase in the conventional α from 86 μb (reported by Horowitz 1993) to 18 μb.
- For the deuteron S-state, the corrected integral for σ exchange is -0.0284 fm^{-1/2} compared to -0.0700 fm^{-1/2} using the 2p approximation (at η = qπ/mπ = 0.1424).
- The total HME integral using the correct momentum operator is -0.0287 fm^{-1/2}, versus -0.1223 fm^{-1/2} with the 2p approximation.
- Including the Δ(1232) isobar triples the threshold cross section, leading to an overestimation of the data even without HME.

## Context

This Comment corrects the calculation of Horowitz (1993), which had applied the Koltun and Reitan (1966) approximation for the momentum operator to the HME mechanism in threshold NN → dπ. The corrected result shows that HME cannot resolve the discrepancy between theory and the data of Hutcheon et al. (1991). The work extends the coupled-channels model of Niskanen (1978, 1984) for Δ isobar excitation, which had been successful in describing higher-energy observables such as the analyzing power Ay between 500 and 600 MeV. The finding that the full model overestimates the threshold cross section indicates that either the energy dependence of off-shell pion rescattering is not properly incorporated or some physical mechanism is still missing, but HME does not appear important for this reaction.
