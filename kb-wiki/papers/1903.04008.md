# Multiparticle production and initial quasi-temperature from proton induced carbon collisions at $p_{Lab}=31$ GeV/$c$

> The momentum spectra of charged pions ($π^+$ and $π^-$) and kaons ($K^+$ and $K^-$), as well as protons ($p$), produced in the beam protons induced collisions in a 90-cm-long graphite target [proton-carbon ($p$-C) collisions] at the beam momentum $p_{Lab}=31$ GeV/$c$ are studied in the framework of a multisource thermal model by using Boltzmann distribution and Monte Carlo method. The theoretical model results are approximately in agreement with the experimental data measured by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration. The related free parameters (effective temperature, rapidity shifts, and fraction of non-leading protons) and derived quantities (average transverse momentum and initial quasi-temperature) under given experimental conditions are obtained. It is shown that the considered free parameters and derived quantities to be strongly dependent on emission angle over a range from 0 to 380 mrad and weakly dependent on longitudinal position (graphite target thickness) over a range from 0 to 90 cm.

## Key claim

The multisource thermal model using a single Boltzmann distribution and Monte Carlo method can approximately describe the momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons produced in proton-carbon collisions at 31 GeV/c, but the effective temperature and rapidity shifts are found to be strongly dependent on emission angle (0 to 380 mrad) and only weakly dependent on longitudinal position in the graphite target (0 to 90 cm), contrary to the authors' earlier assumption that effective temperature is independent of rapidity.

## Method

The authors applied a multisource thermal model in which particles are emitted isotropically from local sources in their rest frames, with momenta following a Boltzmann distribution. Using Monte Carlo methods, they generated particle momenta in the source rest frame and then transformed to the laboratory frame by adding rapidity shifts distributed uniformly over a range [y_min, y_max] for pions and kaons, and with an additional leading-proton component for protons. The model was fitted to experimental momentum spectra measured by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration across six longitudinal segments (0-90 cm) and multiple angular bins (0-380 mrad) for five particle species.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: p_Lab = 31 GeV/c for proton-carbon collisions
- Graphite target length: 90 cm, divided into six segments (0-18, 18-36, 36-54, 54-72, 72-90, and 90 cm)
- Emission angle range studied: 0 to 380 mrad
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T_0 from blast-wave model: 0.080 to 0.135 GeV
- Transverse flow velocity beta_T from blast-wave model: 0.21c to 0.42c
- For pions and kaons, effective temperature T and rapidity shift Delta y decrease slightly with increasing emission angle theta
- Fraction k of non-leading protons shows a slight increase with increasing longitudinal position z

## Context

This work extends the multisource thermal model previously developed by the same group (Liu et al.) to proton-carbon collisions at SPS energies, where the authors had previously treated effective temperature as an integral quantity over rapidity. The paper provides a differential analysis in emission angle and target depth, complementing the NA61/SHINE Collaboration's experimental measurements (2016) of particle production from a 90-cm graphite target. The results enable comparison of flow-like effects in small collision systems with those in larger systems such as gold-gold collisions studied by the afterburner model (Xu and Ko, 2011), and the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperatures and flow velocities can be compared with blast-wave model analyses from other experiments.
