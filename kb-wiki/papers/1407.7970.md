# On a new approach to meson phenomenology with the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We investigate capabilities of the effective interaction in a rainbow-ladder truncated meson model of QCD within a covariant Landau-gauge Bethe-Salpeter-equation approach. Based upon past success for the light- as well as heavy-quark domains, we discuss the range of applicability and features of an effort with comprehensive phenomenological claim and goals.

## Key claim

The authors challenge the prevailing view that rainbow-ladder truncation is insufficient for a generally satisfying meson phenomenology, and attempt a counterexample by anchoring the effective quark-gluon interaction in the heavy-quark domain and allowing independent variation of the model parameters \(\omega\) and \(D\), rather than fixing their product as in prior light-quark studies.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow-truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and the ladder-truncated homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states in a covariant Landau-gauge setup, using the well-established Maris-Tandy parameterization of the effective interaction. They compute mass splittings among bottomonium ground and radially excited states on a grid of \(\omega\) and \(D\) values for a fixed bottom-quark mass, then perform a least-squares fit of the ground-state masses to adjust the bottom current-quark mass.

## Key numbers

- The product \(D \times \omega = 0.372\) GeV\(^3\) was fixed in the original light-quark setup, with \(\omega\) varied in the range \([0.3, 0.5]\) GeV.
- In the previous bottomonium study (Blank 2011), \(\omega = 0.61\) GeV was used while keeping \(D \times \omega = 0.372\) GeV\(^3\).
- The best reproduction of splittings on the grid was obtained with \(\omega = 0.7\) GeV and \(D = 1.3\) GeV\(^2\).
- The bottom current-quark mass was initially set to \(m_b = 3.71\) GeV at a renormalization point \(\mu = 19\) GeV, then adjusted to \(m_b = 3.635\) GeV after the ground-state mass fit.

## Context

This work extends the previous bottomonium ground-state study by Blank (2011) to include radially excited states, and generalizes the approach of Maris and Tandy (1999) which was anchored in the light-quark domain. The authors aim to provide a comprehensive description of meson spectra across all quark masses from bottomonium to the chiral limit, possibly allowing the effective interaction to depend on current-quark mass to mimic effects beyond rainbow-ladder truncation, as suggested by recent work by Williams (2014).
