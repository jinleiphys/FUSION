# The Gamow-Teller States in Relativistic Nuclear Models

> The Gamow-Teller(GT) states are investigated in relativistic models. The Landau-Migdal(LM) parameter is introduced in the Lagrangian as a contact term with the pseudo-vector coupling. In the relativistic model the total GT strength in the nucleon space is quenched by about 12% in nuclear matter and by about 6% in finite nuclei, compared with the one of the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum rule. The quenched amount is taken by nucleon-antinucleon excitations in the time-like region. Because of the quenching, the relativistic model requires a larger value of the LM parameter than non-relativistic models in describing the excitation energy of the GT state. The Pauli blocking terms are not important for the description of the GT states.

## Key claim

The relativistic model predicts that the total Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in the nucleon space is quenched by about 12% in nuclear matter and by about 6% in finite nuclei relative to the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum rule, with the missing strength taken by nucleon-antinucleon excitations in the time-like region; consequently, the relativistic model requires a larger value of the Landau-Migdal (LM) parameter than non-relativistic models to reproduce the GT excitation energy.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic Lagrangian with a pseudo-vector pion-nucleon coupling and a contact term for the LM parameter, then computed the RPA correlation function in nuclear matter and for finite nuclei. Analytic expressions for the transverse dimesic function were derived at zero momentum transfer, and the GT excitation energy and strength were obtained from the poles and residues of the RPA response. For finite nuclei, the GT strength was evaluated numerically using the NL-SH relativistic mean-field model for bound-state transitions in 48Ca.

## Key numbers

- The total GT strength in nuclear matter is quenched by about 12% compared to the non-relativistic sum rule value of 2(N-Z).
- The total GT strength in finite nuclei (48Ca) is quenched by about 6% compared to the non-relativistic sum rule value of 16.
- The relativistic factor quenching the strength is (1 - 2/3 v_F^2), where v_F = 0.43 for k_F = 1.36 fm^-1 and effective mass M* = 0.6 M.
- The relativistic model requires a 14% larger value of g' (0.68) compared to the non-relativistic value of about 0.6 to reproduce the GT excitation energy.
- Effects of Pauli blocking terms on the GT excitation energy are less than 0.5%.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of giant resonances previously applied to monopole and quadrupole states (Kurasawa, Suzuki, Van Giai, 1980s) to the GT resonance, which had not been studied in detail in relativistic models. The results contradict the common practice of inserting the LM parameter into the pion propagator (used in high-momentum transfer reactions by Horowitz and collaborators), showing that this approach cannot describe GT states. The findings enable future studies of GT strength distributions in finite nuclei and clarify the role of nucleon-antinucleon excitations in quenching sum rules.
