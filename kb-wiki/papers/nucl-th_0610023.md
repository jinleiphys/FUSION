# Reply to comment "On the test of the modified BCS at finite temperature"

## Key claim

The modified BCS (MBCS) model is thermodynamically inconsistent and has no predictive power for pairing gaps or thermodynamic quantities except in a single fine-tuned example; its range of applicability, if any, is strictly limited to temperatures far below the critical temperature \(T \ll T_c\).

## Method

The authors compare MBCS predictions for the pairing gap \(\bar{\Delta}\), specific heat \(C_{\nu}\), and entropy \(S\) against exact finite-temperature BCS (FT-BCS) results in a picket-fence model (PFM) with varying numbers of single-particle levels \(\Omega\) and fixed particle number \(N=10\), as well as in a realistic single-particle spectrum for the neutron system of \(^{120}\)Sn. They compute entropy via two independent definitions, the thermodynamic integral \(S_1 = \int_0^T (1/t)(\partial \mathcal{E}/\partial t)\,dt\) and the statistical-mechanical expression \(S_2 = -\sum_j (2j+1)[n_j \ln n_j + (1-n_j)\ln(1-n_j)]\), and check their equality as a test of thermodynamic consistency.

## Key numbers

- For the PFM with \(N=10\) and \(\Omega=8,9,10,12,13,14\) at pairing strength \(G = 0.4\) MeV, the MBCS yields either negative heat capacity \(C_{\nu}\) or a negative pairing gap \(\bar{\Delta}\) at moderate temperatures.
- For the PFM with \(N=10\) and \(\Omega=11\) at \(G = 0.4\) MeV, the MBCS mimics a macroscopic theory (the only example that does not break down).
- In the neutron system of \(^{120}\)Sn with a realistic single-particle spectrum, the MBCS entropy \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) differ by orders of magnitude even at temperatures as low as \(T \sim 200\) keV.
- In the same \(^{120}\)Sn calculation, the MBCS entropy \(S_1\) decreases with increasing temperature (shown in the insert of Fig. 2), violating the second law of thermodynamics.

## Context

This Reply addresses the comment by Dang and Arima (2006) on the authors' earlier analysis (Ponomarev and Vdovin, 2005), which had concluded that MBCS is only applicable for \(T \ll T_c\). The present work shows that the MBCS fails thermodynamic consistency tests that standard FT-BCS passes, and that the single successful example cited by Dang and Arima (PFM with \(N=10, \Omega=11\)) is an isolated case among many physically similar configurations that produce unphysical results. The paper reinforces earlier criticism by Civitarese (1989) and demonstrates that the MBCS cannot serve as a reliable theory for heated nuclear systems.
