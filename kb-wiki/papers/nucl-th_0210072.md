# Effects of surface vibrations on quadrupole response of nuclei

> The effect of quadrupole-type surface vibrations on the quadrupole response function of heavy nuclei is studied by using a model based on the solution of the linearized Vlasov equation with moving-surface boundary conditions. By using a separable approximation for the residual interaction, an analytical expression is obtained for the moving-surface response function. Comparison of the fixed- and moving-surface strength functions shows that surface vibrations are essential in order to achieve a unified description of the two characteristic features of the quadrupole response: the giant resonance and the low-lying states. Calculations performed by setting the surface tension equal to zero shows that the low-lying strength is strongly affected by the surface tension.

## Key claim
Surface vibrations of quadrupole type are essential for a unified description of both the giant quadrupole resonance and low-lying quadrupole states, but the model only achieves a qualitative description; quantum effects modify the low-energy region substantially, yielding a discrete state instead of the semiclassical broad bump.

## Method
The authors solve the linearized Vlasov kinetic equation with moving-surface boundary conditions for a spherical nucleus modeled as a cavity of radius R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm. A separable multipole-multipole residual interaction is assumed, allowing an analytical expression for the moving-surface quadrupole response function to be derived. The strength functions are computed for A=208 and compared with the fixed-surface solution.

## Key numbers
- The giant quadrupole resonance energy follows the systematics ħω ≈ 63 A^(-1/3) MeV.
- For A=208, the fixed-surface collective response shifts the main strength from about 16 MeV to lower excitation energy and narrows the giant quadrupole peak.
- The interaction strength used is κ₂ = -1.0 × 10^(-3) MeV fm^(-4), chosen to reproduce the experimental giant quadrupole resonance energy in ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The moving-surface response function exhausts about 98% of the energy-weighted sum rule when integrated up to E = 30 MeV, while the fixed-surface and zero-order functions exhaust only 80% in the same interval.
- The surface tension parameter is σ ≈ 1 MeV fm^(-2), obtained from the mass formula.

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical Vlasov approach of Briere and Dellafiore (1999) and Abrosimov, Dellafiore, and Matera (2001) by including moving-surface boundary conditions to couple nucleon motion with quadrupole surface vibrations. The results are compared with recent random-phase approximation calculations of Hamamoto (2002), which show a discrete low-energy state rather than the broad bump found here. The model enables future inclusion of coupling to surface vibrations of different multipolarity and nucleon-nucleon collisions, as considered by Bortignon and Lacroix (2002).
