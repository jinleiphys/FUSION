# What's in the core of massive neutron stars?

> When hadron-quark continuity is formulated in terms of a topology change at a density higher than twice the nuclear matter densiy $n_0$ the core of massive compact stars can be described in terms of quasiparticles of fractional baryon charges, behaving neither like pure baryons nor deconfined quarks. Hidden symmetries, both local gauge and pseudo-conformal (or broken scale), emerge and give rise to the long-standing quenched $g_A$ in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions at $\sim n_0$ and to the pseudo-conformal sound velocity $v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ at $\gsim 3n_0$. These properties are confronted with the recent observations in superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions and in astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

When hadron-quark continuity is formulated via a topology change at a density above twice nuclear matter density, the core of massive compact stars can be described by confined quasiparticles of fractional baryon charge that behave neither like pure baryons nor deconfined quarks, with the pseudo-conformal sound velocity emerging at densities above about three times nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized effective field theory (GenEFT) based on a scale-symmetric hidden local symmetry Lagrangian (chi-bHLS) that includes baryons, pions, vector mesons (rho, omega), and a scalar dilaton identified with the f0(500). The parameters of this Lagrangian are scaled with density according to constraints from a topology change at a density n1/2, where skyrmion matter transforms to half-skyrmion matter, as determined from a bosonic version of the theory (chi-mHLS). The many-body problem is treated via a Wilsonian renormalization-group approach that reduces to Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid theory at the mean-field level, with corrections beyond the Fermi-liquid fixed point included via Vlowk-RG.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: n0 = 0.161 fm^-3 (empirical: 0.16 ± 0.01 fm^-3)
- Binding energy at saturation: 16.7 MeV (empirical: 16.0 ± 1.0 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at n0: 30.2 MeV (empirical: 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at 2n0: 56.4 MeV (empirical: 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV; 40.2 ± 12.8 MeV)
- Slope parameter L(n0): 67.8 MeV (empirical: 58.9 ± 16 MeV; 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV)
- Incompressibility K0: 250.0 MeV (empirical: 230 ± 20 MeV)
- Topology change density n1/2 = 2.5 n0 (chosen for illustration from the range 2 < n1/2/n0 < 4)
- Maximum star mass Mmax ~ 2.0 solar masses with central density ~ 5 n0
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 ≈ 656, radius R_1.4 ≈ 12.8 km
- Pseudo-conformal sound velocity v_pcs^2/c^2 ≈ 1/3 for densities above ~ 3 n0
- Vector manifestation density n_vm ≥ 25 n0
- Effective axial coupling in nuclear matter: g_A^Landau ≈ 1.0 (quenched from free-neutron value g_A = 1.27)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of a generalized effective field theory (GenEFT) incorporating hidden local symmetry and scale symmetry (Ma and Rho, 2020 review). It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory (s-chiEFT) approaches that do not include explicit vector meson and dilaton degrees of freedom, and with quarkyonic models (McLerran and collaborators) that exhibit more complex structures at the hadron-quark transition density. The prediction of precocious pseudo-conformal sound velocity at ~3n0 is compared with the analysis of Annala et al. (2020), who interpreted similar behavior in the sound velocity as evidence for deconfined quarks in the core of massive stars; the present work argues instead that the core consists of confined half-skyrmion quasiparticles with fractional baryon charge, with true deconfinement occurring only at much higher densities (≥25n0).
