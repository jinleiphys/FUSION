# Constraining compact star properties with nuclear saturation parameters

> A set of hadronic equations of state (EoSs) derived from relativistic density functional theory and constrained by terrestrial experiments, astrophysical observations, in particular by the GW170817 event, and chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) of neutron matter is used to explore the sensitivity of the EoS parameterization on the few nuclear matter characteristics defined at the saturation density. We find that the gross properties of compact stars are most sensitive to the isoscalar skewness coefficient $Q_{\text{sat}}$ and the isovector slope coefficient $L_{\text{sym}}$ around saturation density, since the higher order coefficients, such as $K_{\text{sym}}$, are fixed by our model. More specifically, (i) among these $Q_{\text{sat}}$ is the dominant parameter controlling both the maximum mass and the radii of compact stars while $L_{\rm sym}$ is constrained somewhat by $χ$EFT of neutron matter; (ii) massive enough ($M\sim 2.0~M_{\odot})$ compact stars featuring both hyperons and $Δ$ resonances can be obtained if the value of $Q_{\text{sat}}$ is large enough; (iii) the emergence of $Δ$'s reduces the radius of a canonical mass ($M\sim 1.4~M_{\odot})$ compact star thus easing the tension between the predictions of the relativistic density functionals and the inferences from the X-ray observation of nearby isolated neutron stars.

## Key claim

The gross properties of compact stars are most sensitive to the isoscalar skewness coefficient \(Q_{\text{sat}}\) and the isovector slope coefficient \(L_{\text{sym}}\) around saturation density, but this conclusion depends on the fact that higher-order coefficients such as \(K_{\text{sym}}\) are fixed by the model rather than varied freely.

## Method

The authors constructed a set of hadronic equations of state (EoSs) from relativistic density functional theory, using the DD-ME2 parametrization as a reference and varying seven nuclear matter characteristics (saturation density, Dirac mass, saturation energy, incompressibility, skewness, symmetry energy, and symmetry energy slope) one at a time. They imposed constraints from terrestrial experiments, the GW170817 gravitational wave event, and chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) calculations of neutron matter up to \(\sim 1.3\rho_{\text{sat}}\), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute compact star masses and radii for compositions including nucleons, hyperons, and \(\Delta\) resonances.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass \(M_{\text{max}} \gtrsim 2.0 M_\odot\) and canonical radius \(R_{M_{1.4}} \lesssim 13.8\) km are the two key astrophysical constraints used; these favor small values of \(L_{\text{sym}}\).
- The \(\chi\)EFT band constrains neutron matter EoS up to \(\sim 1.3\rho_{\text{sat}}\); for \(L_{\text{sym}} = 80\) MeV, considerable deviation from \(\chi\)EFT is observed in the very low-density regime.
- Variations in the maximum mass due to isovector characteristics \(E_{\text{sym}}\) and \(L_{\text{sym}}\) are of order \(0.05 M_\odot\); variations in the radius of a canonical neutron star are of order 0.4 km.
- The spread in values of \(Q_{\text{sat}}\) covers the range \(\sim -500 < Q_{\text{sat}} < 500\) MeV across different relativistic density functionals.
- The hyperon potentials used are \(U^{(N)}_\Lambda = -U^{(N)}_\Sigma = -30\) MeV and \(U^{(N)}_\Xi = -14\) MeV at saturation density; the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) bond energy is \(U^{(\Lambda)}_\Lambda (\rho_{\text{sat}}/5) = -0.67\) MeV.
- The \(\Delta\) isoscalar potential at saturation density is constrained by scattering studies to \(-30\) MeV \(+ V_N(\rho_{\text{sat}}) \lesssim V_\Delta(\rho_{\text{sat}}) \lesssim V_N(\rho_{\text{sat}})\) and by heavy-ion collision studies to \(V_N(\rho_{\text{sat}}) \lesssim V_\Delta(\rho_{\text{sat}}) \lesssim 2/3 V_N(\rho_{\text{sat}})\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used Skyrme density functionals to constrain symmetry energy via neutron skin (Chen 2010), giant monopole resonances (Chen 2012), and electric dipole polarizability (Zhang 2014), as well as studies of correlations between \(\Delta^-\) formation density and maximum mass within nonlinear density functional theory (Cai 2015, 2017). It also builds on nucleonic EoS based on Taylor expansions around saturation density (Margueron 2018a, 2018b; Zhang 2018a, 2018b). The key new feature is the mapping of density-functional based EoS onto a generic parametrization in terms of nuclear matter characteristics at saturation, enabling systematic exploration of how uncertainties in these characteristics propagate to compact star properties. This enables future work to use observational constraints on masses and radii to narrow the allowed ranges of higher-order nuclear matter parameters such as \(Q_{\text{sat}}\) and \(L_{\text{sym}}\).
