# On the Applicability of Weak-Coupling Results in High Density QCD

> Quark matter at asymptotically high baryon chemical potential is in a color superconducting state characterized by a gap Delta. We demonstrate that although present weak-coupling calculations of Delta are formally correct for mu -> Infinity, the contributions which have to this point been neglected are large enough that present results can only be trusted for mu >> mu_c ~ 10^8 MeV. We make this argument by using the gauge dependence of the present calculation as a diagnostic tool. It is known that the present calculation yields a gauge invariant result for mu -> Infinity; we show, however, that the gauge dependence of this result only begins to decrease for mu > mu_c, and conclude that the result can certainly not be trusted for mu < mu_c. In an appendix, we set up the calculation of the influence of the Meissner effect on the magnitude of the gap. This contribution to Delta is, however, much smaller than the neglected contributions whose absence we detect via the resulting gauge dependence.

## Key claim

The weak-coupling calculations of the color superconducting gap Delta in high-density QCD, while formally correct in the limit mu approaching infinity, can only be trusted for mu much greater than mu_c approximately 10^8 MeV because neglected contributions (such as vertex corrections) become large for couplings g greater than g_c approximately 0.8, as diagnosed by the gauge dependence of the present results.

## Method

The authors derived and numerically solved the one-loop Schwinger-Dyson gap equation for the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of three-flavor QCD, using a medium-modified gluon propagator with Debye screening and Landau damping but without vertex corrections or the Meissner effect. They solved the resulting integral equation for the gap Delta(k_0) in several different covariant gauges (parameterized by gauge parameter xi) and tracked the gauge dependence of the result as a function of the coupling g to determine at what coupling the neglected contributions become important.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling above which the gauge dependence does not decrease is g_c approximately 0.8, corresponding to mu_c approximately 10^8 MeV when using the perturbative QCD beta-function with Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV.
- The asymptotic parametric form of the gap is ln(Delta/mu) = -3 pi^2/(sqrt(2) g) - 5 ln g + f(g), where the constant term lim_{g to 0} f(g) is estimated as 8.88 by Schaefer and Wilczek and smaller by (pi^2 + 4)/8 - ln 2 = 1.04 by Brown, Liu, and Ren.
- The Debye screening mass for N_f = 3 is m^2 = 3 g^2 mu^2/(2 pi^2).

## Context

This work extends the weak-coupling calculations of the color superconducting gap by Son (1999), Schaefer and Wilczek (1999), and Brown, Liu, and Ren (2000) by using gauge dependence as a diagnostic tool to assess the reliability of these calculations. The authors show that the neglected vertex corrections and other contributions, which are formally subleading at asymptotically large mu, become significant for couplings above g approximately 0.8, meaning that the apparent agreement between weak-coupling results and model-based estimates (e.g., from instanton liquid models or four-fermion interactions) at mu approximately 500 MeV must be seen as coincidental. The paper sets up but does not complete the calculation of Meissner effect contributions, finding them much smaller than the neglected contributions detected via gauge dependence.
