# Chemical relaxation time of pions in hot hadronic matter

> We calculate characteristic time scales for chemical equilibration of pions in hot hadronic matter using an effective chiral Lagrangian. We find that inelastic processes involving the vector and axial vector mesons reduce the chemical equilibration time by a factor of $\sim 10$ compared to the result previously calculated in chiral perturbation theory. For a temperature of $T\sim 150 MeV$ we obtain a chemical relaxation time of $τ_{ch} \simeq 10 fm/c$, which is comparable with typical time scales for a hadronic system generated in SPS-energy heavy ion collisions. The effect of baryons is also estimated and found to be negligible for SPS-energies but important for AGS-energies. We predict, that chemical freeze-out should take place at considerable higher temperatures $ΔT \simeq 20 MeV$ than thermal freeze-out and that the hadronic phase would not sustain a pion chemical potential larger than $100 MeV$.

## Key claim

The chemical equilibration time for pions in hot hadronic matter is reduced by a factor of about 10 compared to previous chiral perturbation theory results when inelastic processes involving vector and axial vector mesons are included, yielding a relaxation time of approximately 10 fm/c at T ≈ 150 MeV that is comparable to typical time scales in SPS-energy heavy ion collisions; however, this result assumes resonances are formed instantaneously and the actual time may be slightly larger.

## Method

The authors calculated chemical relaxation times for pions using an effective chiral Lagrangian with vector and axial vector mesons (ρ, ω, a₁) to describe hadronic interactions. They considered both true number-changing processes (ππ ⇌ ππππ) and apparent number-changing processes involving resonance decays and inelastic scattering (ππ ⇌ ρρ, πa₁ ⇌ ππ, πω ⇌ ππ), evaluating collision integrals with monopole form factors at vertices. The effect of baryons (N and Δ) was also estimated using a phenomenological parameterization of the NΔ → NN cross section.

## Key numbers

- Chemical relaxation time τ_ch ≈ 10 fm/c at T ≈ 150 MeV (including resonances)
- Chemical relaxation time τ_ch ≈ 1.5 fm/c at T ≈ 180 MeV (including resonances)
- Previous chiral perturbation theory result gave τ_ch ≈ 26 fm/c at T = 180 MeV (about 40 fm/c with m_π = 138 MeV)
- Relative chemical equilibrium time between pions and ρ-mesons: τ_rel_ch = 0.4–0.6 fm/c
- Thermal relaxation time τ_th ≈ 2 fm/c at T = 150 MeV (from current algebra result)
- Baryon-induced relaxation time τ_ch ∼ 5 fm/c at T ≈ 150 MeV for AGS experiments (μ_B = 516 MeV)
- Baryon-induced relaxation time τ_ch ∼ 10–100 fm/c at T = 180 MeV for SPS experiments
- With finite pion chemical potential μ_π = 100 MeV at T = 150 MeV, τ_ch ≈ 5 fm/c
- Predicted chemical freeze-out occurs at ΔT ≈ 20 MeV higher temperature than thermal freeze-out
- Pion chemical potential should not exceed approximately 50 MeV at freeze-out temperature of about 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Goity (1992) in chiral perturbation theory, which found much longer chemical equilibration times for pions in a pure pion gas. The inclusion of vector and axial vector mesons (ρ, ω, a₁) dramatically shortens the relaxation time, making chemical equilibration plausible within the lifetimes of heavy-ion collision systems at SPS energies. The results enable more realistic modeling of chemical freeze-out conditions in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that the hadronic phase cannot sustain a pion chemical potential larger than about 100 MeV, consistent with analyses of hadron abundances by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995) and others.
