# Dependence of nuclear binding on hadronic mass variation

> We examine how the binding of light ($A\leq 8$) nuclei depends on possible variations of hadronic masses, including meson, nucleon, and nucleon-resonance masses. Small variations in hadronic masses may have occurred over time; the present results can help evaluate the consequences for big bang nucleosynthesis. Larger variations may be relevant to current attempts to extrapolate properties of nucleon-nucleon interactions from lattice QCD calculations. Results are presented as derivatives of the energy with respect to the different masses so they can be combined with different predictions of the hadronic mass-dependence on the underlying current-quark mass $m_q$. As an example, we employ a particular set of relations obtained from a study of hadron masses and sigma terms based on Dyson-Schwinger equations and a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for confined quarks and diquarks. We find that nuclear binding decreases moderately rapidly as the quark mass increases, with the deuteron becoming unbound when the pion mass is increased by $\sim$60% (corresponding to an increase in $X_q=m_q/Λ_{QCD}$ of 2.5). In the other direction, the dineutron becomes bound if the pion mass is decreased by $\sim$15% (corresponding to a reduction of $X_q$ by $\sim$30%). If we interpret the disagreement between big bang nucleosynthesis calculations and measurements to be the result of variation in $X_q$, we obtain an estimate $δX_q/X_q=K \cdot (0.013 \pm 0.002)$ where $K \sim 1$ (the expected accuracy in $K$ is about a factor of 2). The result is dominated by $^7$Li data.

## Key claim

Nuclear binding decreases moderately rapidly as the quark mass increases, with the deuteron becoming unbound when the pion mass is increased by approximately 60% (corresponding to an increase in \(X_q = m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) of 2.5). The authors present derivatives of nuclear binding energies with respect to hadronic mass changes, which can be combined with different predictions of how hadronic masses depend on the underlying current-quark mass \(m_q\).

## Method

The authors used three Argonne nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV14, AV28, and AV18) and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential to calculate binding energies of light nuclei (\(A \leq 8\)). Two-body energies for the deuteron and the \(^1\)S\(_0\) virtual bound state were solved exactly, while energies for \(^3\)H, \(^{3,4,5}\)He, \(^{6,7}\)Li, and \(^{7,8}\)Be were computed using variational Monte Carlo methods. The hadron masses (nucleon, \(\Delta\)-resonance, pion, and heavy meson masses) were varied individually by \(\pm 0.1\%\) to obtain dimensionless derivatives \(\Delta\mathcal{E}(m_H) = (\delta E/E)/(\delta m_H/m_H)\).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron becomes unbound when the pion mass is increased by \(\sim 60\%\), corresponding to an increase in \(X_q = m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) of 2.5.
- Dineutron becomes bound if the pion mass is decreased by \(\sim 15\%\), corresponding to a reduction of \(X_q\) by \(\sim 30\%\).
- From big bang nucleosynthesis data, the estimated variation is \(\delta X_q/X_q = K \cdot (0.013 \pm 0.002)\) where \(K \sim 1\) (expected accuracy in \(K\) is about a factor of 2), dominated by \(^7\)Li data.
- For the deuteron with AV14, changing the nucleon mass gives \(\Delta Q(m_N) = 0.24\) (dimensionless derivative); changing the pion mass (OPE only) gives \(\Delta Q(m_\pi) = -0.10\); changing the pion mass (including static TPE) gives \(\Delta Q(m_\pi) = -0.25\).
- For the deuteron with AV18, changing the nucleon mass gives \(\Delta Q(m_N) = 0.24\); changing the pion mass (OPE only) gives \(\Delta Q(m_\pi) = -0.09\); changing the pion mass (including static TPE) gives \(\Delta Q(m_\pi) = -0.36\).
- For the deuteron with AV28, changing the nucleon mass gives \(\Delta Q(m_N) = 0.62\); changing the \(\Delta\) mass gives \(\Delta Q(m_\Delta) = -0.38\); changing the pion mass (OPE only) gives \(\Delta Q(m_\pi) = -0.16\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Flambaum and Shuryak (2002) for relating quark mass variations to nuclear properties, and provides quantitative derivatives that can be combined with hadron mass predictions from Dyson-Schwinger equations (as in the work of Holl, Roberts, and collaborators, 2006) or chiral perturbation theory (as in Beane and Savage, 2003). The results enable evaluation of how variations in the fundamental constant \(X_q = m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) would affect big bang nucleosynthesis yields, potentially explaining the discrepancy between calculated and observed \(^7\)Li abundances. The calculations also provide guidance for extrapolating lattice QCD results on nucleon-nucleon interactions to physical quark masses.
