# Exclusive heavy vector meson photoproduction in hadronic collisions at the LHC: predictions of the Color Glass Condensate model for Run 2 energies

> In this letter we update our predictions for exclusive $J/Ψ$ and $Υ$ photoproduction in proton-proton and nucleus - nucleus collisions at the Run 2 LHC energies obtained with the color dipole formalism and considering the impact parameter Color Glass Condensate model (bCGC) for the forward dipole - target scattering amplitude. A comparison with the LHCb data on rapidity distributions and photon - hadron cross sections is presented. Our results demonstrate that the current data can be quite well described by the bCGC model, which takes into account nonlinear effects in the QCD dynamics and reproduces the very precise HERA data, without introducing any additional effect or free parameter.

## Key claim
The bCGC model, without any additional effects or free parameters, can describe the Run 1 and preliminary Run 2 LHCb data on exclusive J/Ψ and Υ photoproduction in pp collisions, provided that the rapidity gap survival probability is set to unity and the dipole-nucleus amplitude is constructed from the dipole-proton cross section via a Glauber-like expression.

## Method
The authors compute exclusive J/Ψ and Υ photoproduction cross sections in pp and PbPb collisions using the color dipole formalism, with the forward dipole-proton scattering amplitude given by the impact parameter Color Glass Condensate (bCGC) model. For nuclear targets, the dipole-nucleus amplitude is obtained from the dipole-proton cross section via a Glauber-like expression using a 3-parameter Fermi nuclear density. The photon flux for protons is described by the Drees-Zeppenfeld model and for nuclei by the relativistic point-like charge model, and corrections for the real part of the amplitude and the skewness factor are included.

## Key numbers
- Exclusive J/Ψ photoproduction total cross section in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 72.4 nb
- Exclusive Υ photoproduction total cross section in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 189.5 pb
- Exclusive J/Ψ photoproduction total cross section in PbPb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV: 21.6 mb
- Exclusive Υ photoproduction total cross section in PbPb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV: 26.02 μb
- The bCGC predictions for pp and PbPb collisions are approximately 10% smaller than the authors' previous predictions from earlier work (Goncalves et al., 2015)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Goncalves et al., 2015) by updating predictions to Run 2 LHC energies and providing the first comparison with LHCb data on the energy dependence of the γp → Vp cross section extracted from hadronic collisions. The bCGC model, which successfully describes inclusive and exclusive HERA data (as shown by Amir et al., 2014), is shown to also describe the LHCb data without modification, suggesting that QCD nonlinear effects are essential for understanding γh interactions at high energies. The results enable future discrimination between different approaches to QCD dynamics through comprehensive analysis of exclusive light and heavy vector meson photoproduction at Run 2.
