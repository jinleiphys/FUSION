# Universality of the Majorana Double Charge Exchange

> The Majorana Double Charge Exchange nuclear reaction mechanism represents a powerful and novel scenario to probe the dynamics of the neutrinoless double beta decay. Recent studies presented in this manuscript have highlighted a key feature of this mechanism, namely its independence with respect to the nuclei involved in the reaction.

## Key claim
The Majorana Double Charge Exchange (MDCE) reaction mechanism exhibits a universal behavior that is nearly independent of the nuclear masses involved in the reaction, particularly for medium and heavy nuclei, with only a mild dependence that becomes slightly more pronounced at higher momenta of the exchanged charged pion.

## Method
The authors performed numerical calculations of pion potentials for the MDCE mechanism using a closure-form evaluation of the pion-nucleon T-matrix, which includes S-wave and P-wave components. Calculations were carried out at a laboratory energy of 270 MeV using 18O projectiles on various target nuclei ranging from 9Be to 116Cd, selected as isotope candidates for neutrinoless double beta decay. The pion potentials were computed for collinear kinematics at charged pion momenta of 400 MeV/c and 800 MeV/c, and the mass dependence of the potential components was analyzed across the target nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Pion rest mass used as separation scale: m_pi ~ 139 MeV
- Laboratory energy for calculations: T_lab = 270 MeV
- Charged pion momenta explored: 400 MeV/c and 800 MeV/c
- Target nuclei range: from 9Be to 116Cd
- The P-wave diagonal components U_11 and U_22 dominate over S-wave components at both 400 MeV/c and 800 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of the MDCE mechanism developed by Lenske (2024) and is part of the NUMEN project proposed by Cappuzzello (2018, 2023) to investigate heavy-ion-induced Double Charge Exchange reactions for constraining neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements. The universality finding provides evidence for the short-range character of the MDCE process and enables future experimental studies to probe 0nu-beta-beta decay dynamics across different nuclear systems without strong dependence on the specific target nucleus.
