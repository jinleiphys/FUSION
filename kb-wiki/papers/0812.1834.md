# Poincare Invariant Three-Body Scattering

> Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering are solved at arbitrary energies in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is incorporated withing the framework of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon interaction, observables for elastic and breakup scattering are calculated and compared to non-relativistic ones.

## Key claim

Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering can be solved at arbitrary energies using momentum vectors without partial wave decomposition, and the resulting relativistic effects in three-body observables arise solely from the different embedding of two-body dynamics in the three-body problem, not from differences in two-body phase shifts.

## Method

The authors solved Poincaré-invariant Faddeev equations formulated with the mass Casimir operator (rest Hamiltonian) in a representation where interactions are invariant under kinematic translations and rotations. Two-body interactions were embedded in the three-body dynamical generators using the Coester-Piper-Serduke (CPS) method to construct phase-shift-equivalent relativistic interactions from a non-relativistic Malfliet-Tjon potential. The relativistic two-body t-matrix was obtained from the non-relativistic one via an analytic relation, and the Faddeev equations were solved directly in terms of vector momentum variables without partial wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The Malfliet-Tjon interaction was parameterized to support a bound state at E_d = -2.23 MeV
- Total cross sections for elastic and breakup scattering were calculated as functions of projectile kinetic laboratory energy up to 1.5 GeV
- Exclusive differential cross sections for the reaction ^2H(p,2p)n were calculated at 508 MeV laboratory projectile energy for symmetric proton angle pairs
- For energies below 300 MeV, rescattering contributions to the total cross section are described as "huge"
- In first-order calculations, there is essentially no relativistic effect in the total elastic cross section
- Relativistic effects increase the total elastic cross section with increasing energy and slightly reduce the total breakup cross section

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Faddeev approach solved with vector variables (Liu 2004) to include exact Poincaré invariance, and builds on the formulation of Poincaré-invariant Faddeev equations detailed in Lin (2007). The method enables calculations at intermediate energies where partial wave decomposition becomes impractical due to the proliferation of partial waves, and provides a framework for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic three-body calculations using phase-shift-equivalent interactions. The exclusive breakup calculations are compared to experimental data from Punjabi (1988) at 508 MeV.
