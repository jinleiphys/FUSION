# Pairing in Nuclei: Exact Solutions

> Pairing plays an essential role in describing nuclear spectra and attempts to describe it has a long history in nuclear physics. Many theoretical tools were developed to treat the pairing problem either exactly or at various levels of approximation. In this contribution to the proceedings of a conference honoring the career of Taka Otsuka, recent developments in the exact solutions of the pairing problem are described.

## Key claim

Exact solutions to the pairing Hamiltonian can be obtained via the Bethe ansatz for several special cases (degenerate single-particle energies with separable pairing strength, or non-degenerate energies with constant pairing strength), but the most general solution remains intractable; the paper presents an algebraic algorithm to solve the Bethe ansatz equations efficiently for systems with multiple SU(2) algebras.

## Method

The paper derives exact solutions for the separable pairing Hamiltonian with two non-degenerate orbitals using a Bethe ansatz parametrization of eigenstates, where step operators are constructed from single-particle energies and pairing strengths. It then generalizes the Bethe ansatz equations to collective neutrino oscillations and introduces a differential-equation-based method to convert the nonlinear Bethe ansatz equations into algebraic equations by evaluating the function Λ(λ) and its derivatives at the single-particle energies ω_p, eliminating the need for direct numerical root-finding.

## Key numbers

- The maximum number of pairs that can occupy a shell-model level j is Ω_j = j + 1/2.
- For the two-orbital separable pairing Hamiltonian, energy eigenvalues are given by E_N = –∑_{n=1}^N (δ x_n)/(β – x_n) = δN – ∑_{n=1}^N 1/(β – x_n), where β and δ are defined in terms of single-particle energies ε_j and coupling constants c_j.
- The Bethe ansatz equation for two SU(2) algebras with ω_1 and ω_2 reduces to ∑_{k≠i} 1/(x_i – x_k) – j_2/x_i + j_1/(1 – x_i) = 0, whose roots satisfy a hypergeometric differential equation.
- For j_q = 1/2, the derivative term in the algebraic formulation vanishes, yielding a set of algebraic equations with unknowns Λ(ω_p); for j_q = 1, the second derivative term vanishes, giving equations for Λ(ω_p) and Λ'(ω_p).

## Context

This work extends the exact-solution program for pairing Hamiltonians initiated by Richardson (1966) and later generalized by Pan (1997) and Balantekin (2007) for separable interactions, and connects to the Bethe ansatz solution of the one-dimensional Heisenberg model (Bethe, 1931). The algebraic algorithm for solving Bethe ansatz equations, following Babelon (2007), enables practical computation of exact eigenstates for nuclear pairing and collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae and neutron-star mergers, where the neutrino transport Hamiltonian (Balantekin, 2006) is mathematically analogous to the pairing Hamiltonian.
