# Initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss effects on inclusive jet production in p+A collisions at RHIC and LHC

> Recent measurements of the centrality and rapidity dependence of single inclusive jet production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC have revealed large and non-trivial nuclear modification of the production cross section for this process. In this paper, we explore to what extent such nuclear modification can be understood by the framework of standard cold nuclear matter effects, in particular initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss. We demonstrate quantitatively that theoretical calculations which include medium-induced radiative corrections can describe rather reasonably the attenuation of the jet production yields in the large transverse momentum region in d+Au collisions at RHIC and p+Pb collisions at the LHC for central to semi-central collisions. We further show that the observed scaling behavior of the nuclear modification factor as a function of the total jet energy $p_T\, {\cosh} (y)$ for various rapidity intervals has a natural explanation in the picture of cold nuclear matter energy loss. On the other hand, the observed enhancement in peripheral collisions is not described in this picture and could have a different origin.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that initial-state cold nuclear matter (CNM) energy loss, implemented via medium-induced radiative corrections, can describe the attenuation of inclusive jet production yields in central to semi-central p+A collisions at both RHIC and LHC, but the observed enhancement in peripheral collisions is not captured by this picture and may have a different origin.

## Method

The authors implement initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss in a leading-order perturbative QCD framework for single inclusive jet production. The medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum is computed to first order in opacity using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) reaction operator approach, and the resulting fractional energy loss is applied as a shift in the parton momentum fraction of the projectile proton's parton distribution functions. The effective path length L for each centrality class is determined via a Glauber model, and the interaction strength between the propagating parton and the nuclear medium is varied by changing the typical momentum transfer xi in the range 0.175 to 0.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

- In d+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the effective path length L ranges from 10.2 fm (0-20% centrality) to 1.5 fm (60-88% centrality), with average number of binary collisions <N_coll> from 15.1 to 3.2
- In p+Pb collisions at LHC at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the effective path length L ranges from 10.6 fm (0-10% centrality) to 1.6 fm (60-90% centrality), with <N_coll> from 14.6 to 3.0
- The gluon mean free path lambda_g is set to 1 fm
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is 42 mb at RHIC energy of sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 70 mb at LHC energy of sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV
- In the highest p_T bins of the most central collisions at the most forward rapidity region, the nuclear suppression R_cp can be as low as 0.2
- The effective fractional energy loss used in the PDF shift is epsilon_q,g,eff = 0.7 * <epsilon_q,g>, where <epsilon_q,g> is the mean energy loss fraction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of cold nuclear matter effects (Kang 2012) and the GLV reaction operator approach (Vitev 2007) to inclusive jet production in p+A collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. The results challenge the standard nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) approach (Eskola 2009), which fails to describe the centrality dependence observed by ATLAS (2014) and PHENIX (2013, 2015). The paper enables future work to go beyond the soft-gluon energy loss approximation and obtain full medium-induced splitting kernels, as well as to understand the dynamical origin of the centrality selection bias that may explain the peripheral enhancement.
