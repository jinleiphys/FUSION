# Effects of nuclear correlations on the $^{16}$O$(e,e'pN)$ reactions to discrete final states

> Calculations of the $^{16}$O$(e,e'pN)$ cross sections to the ground state and first excited levels of the $^{14}$C and $^{14}$N nuclei are presented. The effects of nuclear fragmentation have been obtained in a self-consistent approach and are accounted for in the determination of the two-nucleon removal amplitudes. The Hilbert space is partitioned in order to compute the contribution of both long- and short-range effects in a separate way. Both the two-proton and the proton-neutron emission cross sections have been computed within the same models for the reaction mechanism and the contribution from nuclear structure, with the aim of better comparing the differences between the two physical processes. The $^{16}$O$(e,e'pp)$ reaction is found to be sensitive to short-range correlations, in agreement with previous results. The $^{16}$O$(e,e'pn)$ cross section to $1^+$ final states is dominated by the $Δ$ current and tensor correlations. For both reactions, the interplay between collective (long-range) effects and short-range and tensor correlations plays an important role. This suggests that the selectivity of $(e,e'pN)$ reactions to the final state can be used to probe correlations also beyond short-range effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the selectivity of $(e,e'pN)$ reactions to specific final states of the residual nucleus can be used to probe both short-range and tensor correlations, but the relative importance of these correlations versus long-range collective effects depends strongly on the chosen final state and on whether a proton-proton or proton-neutron pair is emitted.

## Method

The authors computed $^{16}$O$(e,e'pp)$ and $^{16}$O$(e,e'pn)$ cross sections to discrete final states of $^{14}$C and $^{14}$N using a self-consistent many-body approach that partitions the Hilbert space to treat long-range correlations (via a dressed random phase approximation for two-hole states) and short-range correlations (via defect functions from the Bethe-Goldstone equation) separately. The reaction mechanism included one-body currents and two-body currents from seagull, pion-in-flight, and intermediate $\Delta$-isobar diagrams, with final-state interactions described by optical potentials for each outgoing nucleon.

## Key numbers

* The two-proton removal amplitudes for $^{16}$O$(e,e'pp)^{14}$C$_{g.s.}$ in the $^1S_0$ channel with relative quantum numbers $n=0$, $N=1$, $\rho=2$ give a coefficient of $-0.410$ (this work) compared to $-0.416$ in prior work (Geurts et al., 1996); the $^3P_1$ channel with $n=0$, $N=0$, $\rho=2$ gives $+0.513$ compared to $+0.507$.
* The spectroscopic factors for one-nucleon removal from the $p_{1/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ orbitals of $^{16}$O are reduced by factors of 0.72 and 0.76, respectively, relative to the independent-particle shell model, about 10% larger than the experimental factor of approximately 0.65.
* The sum of squared proton-neutron removal amplitudes for transitions to the $1^+_1$ and $1^+_2$ states of $^{14}$N is 0.61 and 0.67, respectively, in the present approach, compared to 0.58 for both states in the coupled-cluster calculation of Ref. [PRC60-pn].
* The harmonic oscillator parameter used is $b = 1.76$ fm, corresponding to $\hbar\omega = 13.4$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of $^{16}$O$(e,e'pp)$ reactions by Geurts et al. (1996) and of $^{16}$O$(e,e'pn)$ reactions by the coupled-cluster approach of Ref. [PRC60-pn], now treating both channels within the same self-consistent hh-DRPA framework with improved treatment of single-particle fragmentation via Faddeev techniques (Barbieri et al., 2001, 2002). The results enable future experimental studies at facilities like MAMI to disentangle short-range correlations, tensor correlations, and long-range collective effects by selecting specific final-state quantum numbers in two-nucleon knockout reactions.
