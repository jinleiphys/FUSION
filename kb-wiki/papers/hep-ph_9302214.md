# E2/M1 Mixing Ratio of $Δ\to Nγ$ and Hyperon Resonance Radiative Decay

> We compute the leading contribution to the E2/M1 mixing ratio of the decay $Δ\rightarrow Nγ$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We find the mixing ratio to be $4\% \ltap |δ_{\rm E2/M1}| \ltap 11\%$, much larger than estimates based on the quark model and other hadronic models. We also compute the mixing ratio for the radiative decay of the hyperon resonances. The decays $Σ^{*+}\rightarrowΣ^+γ$ and $Ξ^{*-}\rightarrowΞ^-γ$ provide a particularly sensitive probe of deviations from heavy baryon spin-flavour SU(6).

## Key claim

The leading contribution to the E2/M1 mixing ratio for Δ → Nγ is computed in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and found to be 4% ≲ |δ_E2/M1| ≲ 11%, which is much larger than quark model estimates but consistent with lattice results; the prediction relies on the assumption that the local E2 counterterm is subleading compared to the pion-loop contribution enhanced by a chiral logarithm.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop pion and kaon contributions to the E2 and M1 amplitudes for decuplet-to-octet baryon radiative decays using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). The M1 amplitude is dominated by a local dimension-five counterterm whose coefficient is fixed by fitting to the measured Δ → Nγ branching ratio, while the E2 amplitude is dominated by the long-distance pion loops that are enhanced by a factor of log(m_π²/Λ_χ²). The mixing ratio δ_E2/M1 is then obtained from the ratio of the E2 and M1 reduced matrix elements, with the strong coupling constants F, D, C, and H varied over their experimentally allowed ranges.

## Key numbers

- The E2/M1 mixing ratio for Δ → Nγ is predicted to lie in the range 4% ≲ |δ_E2/M1| ≲ 11%.
- Previous quark model predictions give δ_E2/M1 ≈ −0.4%, and relativistic wavefunction calculations give essentially zero.
- Lattice computations constrain the mixing ratio to −5% < δ_E2/M1 < 11%.
- Pion-photoproduction analyses indicate −3% < δ_E2/M1 < +4% (from local counterterms only, yielding a real ratio).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier HBChPT calculation of decuplet baryon electromagnetic branching fractions (Butler, Savage, Springer 1992) to focus specifically on the E2/M1 mixing ratio. It contradicts the small values predicted by the constituent quark model (Capstick 1992) and supports the suggestion by Kumano (1988) that pion cloud contributions dominate this ratio. The results enable experimental tests of heavy baryon spin-flavour SU(6) symmetry, particularly through measurements of the decays Σ*⁺ → Σ⁺γ and Ξ*⁻ → Ξ⁻γ at facilities such as CEBAF or with polarized hyperon beams at FNAL (Lipkin and Moinester 1992).
