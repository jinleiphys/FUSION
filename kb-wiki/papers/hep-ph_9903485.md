# $π- π$ Scattering Lengths and Chiral Condensate in NJL Model

> Within the general framework of SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model beyond mean-field approximation, the S-wave $π- π$ scattering lengths $a_0^0$ and $a_0^2$ are calculated. The numerical results are in agreement with that analyzed from recent experiment $πN \to ππN$ data by using Chew-Low-Goebel technique, and the corresponding chiral condensate $<{\bar q}q>$ is around $-(250 {\rm MeV})^3$, which is close to the ones obtained from lattice and sum rules.

# π–π Scattering Lengths and Chiral Condensate in NJL Model

## Key claim
Within the SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model beyond mean-field approximation, the calculated S-wave π–π scattering lengths a₀⁰ and a₀² agree with recent experimental data from πN → ππN reactions analyzed via the Chew-Low-Goebel technique, but only when the free parameter z (ratio of meson to quark momentum cutoffs) lies between 1 and 1.5; the corresponding chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ is around −(250 MeV)³, close to lattice and sum rule results.

## Method
The authors extended the SU(2) NJL model beyond mean-field approximation by including subleading 1/Nc corrections to the quark self-energy and meson polarization functions, using a self-consistent set of Feynman diagrams. They calculated the S-wave π–π scattering lengths a₀⁰ and a₀² at threshold from box and σ-exchange diagrams, replacing mean-field quantities (quark mass m_H, RPA meson propagator) with the beyond-mean-field values. The model parameters, current quark mass m₀, coupling constant G, and two momentum cutoffs Λ_f and Λ_b, were constrained by inputting m_π = 139 MeV and f_π = 92.4 MeV, with the additional free parameter z = Λ_b/Λ_f characterizing meson cloud contributions.

## Key numbers
- m_π = 139 MeV and f_π = 92.4 MeV used as experimental input observables.
- For z = 1.5, a₀⁰ = 0.221 m_π⁻¹ and a₀² = −0.057 m_π⁻¹, compared to recent experimental values a₀⁰ = 0.206 ± 0.013 m_π⁻¹ and a₀² = −0.055 ± 0.021 m_π⁻¹ (starred entries from CHAOS and LAMPF E1179 data).
- The scattering length difference a₀⁰ − a₀² = 0.278 m_π⁻¹ at z = 1.5, compared to the experimental value 0.29 ± 0.04 m_π⁻¹.
- At z = 0 (mean-field limit), the results reproduce Weinberg values: a₀⁰ = 0.16 m_π⁻¹ and a₀² = −0.044 m_π⁻¹.
- The chiral condensate for 1 < z < 1.5 lies between (237 MeV)³ and (256 MeV)³, with corresponding constituent quark mass m ≈ 333 MeV and current quark mass m₀ in the range 5–7 MeV.
- The quark condensate from lattice simulation is (245 ± 4 ± 9 ± 7 MeV)³ and from sum rules is (242 ± 9 MeV)³.

## Context
This work extends earlier NJL model calculations of π–π scattering lengths (NJL1–Schu) that used only mean-field quark mass and RPA meson polarization, by incorporating subleading 1/Nc corrections. The results support the standard scenario of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking driven by a large quark condensate, in contrast to the generalized chiral perturbation theory of the Orsay group (Stern, 1993–1994) which allows a small condensate around (−100 MeV)³. The calculations show that the free parameter z can be fixed by scattering length data, enabling future determination of all four NJL model parameters once precise experimental results from KLOE, E865, and DIRAC become available.
