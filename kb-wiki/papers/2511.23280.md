# Analytical Soft Functions for Heavy-Quark Final States at Hadron Colliders

> We present the first computation of the complete two-loop, fully-differential soft function describing the production of a heavy-quark pair in association with a color-singlet system at hadron colliders. This result constitutes one of the most complex soft functions known to date and it is obtained in closed analytic form for generic multi-dimensional kinematics. This allows us to obtain novel analytic results for the transverse-momentum-dependent and threshold soft functions in this class of processes. We further obtain a decomposition of the soft function into dipole and tripole color correlators, thereby supplying essential building blocks for processes involving a heavy-quark pair produced together with additional light jets at both hadron and lepton colliders. These results represent a key ingredient for advancing precision predictions for heavy-quark physics at the LHC.

# Analytical Soft Functions for Heavy-Quark Final States at Hadron Colliders

## Key claim
The authors present the first complete analytic computation of the two-loop, fully-differential soft function for heavy-quark pair production in association with a color-singlet system at hadron colliders, obtained in closed analytic form for generic multi-dimensional kinematics. The result is expressed in terms of Goncharov polylogarithms and is valid for both transverse-momentum-dependent and threshold kinematics, though the tripole contributions require careful analytic continuation for the spacelike (TMD) case due to branch cuts.

## Method
The soft function is computed using reverse unitarity to replace the momentum-conserving delta function with a Cutkosky cut, followed by integration-by-parts reduction with FIRE6 and Kira to express the result as a linear combination of master integrals. The master integrals are evaluated analytically by solving canonical-form differential equations obtained via Magnus and Dyson series expansions, with boundary conditions set at a phase-space point where all kinematic variables equal unity. The calculation involves 9, 30, and 51 master integrals for the virtual-real corrections to different color correlator types, and 20 master integrals for the double-real corrections.

## Key numbers
- The two-loop soft function is expressed in terms of over 8000 Goncharov polylogarithms encoding the full color and kinematic dependence.
- The differential equation systems for the n_j-v_I, n_1-n_2-v_I, and n_j-v_3-v_4 correlations involve 7, 22, and 39 symbol letters, respectively.
- The tripole correlator T_{12I} involves 2 square roots (R^A_{1,2}) in its differential equations, while T_{j34} involves 4 square roots (R^B_{1,...,4}).
- The soft anomalous dimension includes the angle-dependent cusp anomalous dimension gamma_cusp(rho,alpha_s) and the function g(rho) = [-rho/sqrt(rho^2-1)] ln(4(rho^2-1)) + 2 ln(rho + sqrt(rho^2-1)).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous two-loop calculation of the fully-differential soft function for heavy-quark production at lepton colliders (Liu 2024) to the hadron-collider case, which is considerably more complex due to two additional light-like Wilson lines representing initial-state radiation. The result provides the missing two-loop ingredient for N^3LL resummation of the transverse-momentum spectrum of Q-Qbar-V systems at the LHC, and supplies building blocks for processes where the heavy-quark pair is produced together with additional light jets. The analytic results are made publicly available and enable both fixed-order and resummed predictions for heavy-quark processes, as well as NNLO-accurate Monte Carlo simulations.
