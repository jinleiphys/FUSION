# Probing Surface Quantum Flows in Deformed Pygmy Dipole Modes

> In order to explore the nature of collective modes in weakly bound nuclei, we have investigated deformation effects and surface flow patterns of isovector dipole modes in a shape-coexisting nucleus $^{40}$Mg. The calculations were done in a fully self-consistent continuum finite-amplitude Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) in a large deformed spatial mesh. An unexpected result of pygmy and giant dipole modes having disproportionate deformation splittings in strength functions was obtained. Furthermore, the transition current densities demonstrate that the long-sought core-halo oscillation in pygmy resonances is collective and compressional, corresponding to the lowest excitation energy and the simplest quantum flow topology. Our calculations show that surface flow patterns become more complicated as excitation energies increase.

## Key claim
The paper claims that pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) in the weakly bound, shape-coexisting nucleus \(^{40}\)Mg exhibit deformation splittings that are *disproportionate* to those of the giant dipole resonance (GDR), and that the long-sought core-halo oscillation in the PDR is collective and compressional, but only for the lowest-energy \(K=0\) mode of the prolate shape; higher-energy modes show more complex flow topologies.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent continuum finite-amplitude quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FAM-QRPA) calculations on a large axially symmetric coordinate-space mesh, based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground states obtained with the code HFB-AX. They used an extended SLy4 force for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent pairing interaction, and solved the multipole FAM-QRPA equation iteratively with a hybrid OpenMP/MPI parallel scheme, using a smoothing imaginary part of the excitation frequency of 0.25 MeV and a maximum angular momentum projection \(\Omega_{\rm max}=39/2\).

## Key numbers
- The prolate ground state of \(^{40}\)Mg has a quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.39\) and a neutron Fermi energy \(\lambda_n = -0.33\) MeV; the oblate minimum (\(\beta_2 = -0.32\)) lies 1.9 MeV higher in energy with \(\lambda_n = -0.79\) MeV.
- The prolate shape has a neutron pairing gap \(\Delta_n = 1.23\) MeV (proton pairing gap \(\Delta_p = 0\)); the oblate shape has \(\Delta_p = 0.98\) MeV (\(\Delta_n = 0\)).
- The GDR splitting is \(\delta_E \sim 5\) MeV for both prolate and oblate shapes; the PDR splitting is \(\delta_E = 1.4\) MeV for the prolate shape and \(\delta_E = 0.45\) MeV for the oblate shape, compared to expected values of 0.95 MeV and 1.05 MeV from a hydrodynamic estimate.
- The lowest \(K=0\) PDR mode of the prolate shape has an excitation energy of 2.5 MeV; the other PDR modes (\(K=0\) oblate, \(K=1\) prolate, \(K=1\) oblate) have excitation energies between 4 and 4.5 MeV.
- The calculations used box sizes up to 27.6 fm; the storage of all wave functions for the 27.6 fm box took about 17 Gigabytes.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pygmy resonances in weakly bound nuclei, which were often limited to spherical symmetry (Matsuo, Khan) or to smaller spatial meshes (Yoshida for \(^{40}\)Mg). It contrasts with the hydrodynamic model of Bertsch and the linear deformation-splitting dependence found for GDR by Arteaga, showing that PDR splitting is not simply proportional to deformation. The results enable future high-resolution experimental searches for PDR deformation splitting in deformed neutron-rich nuclei and provide a microscopic visualization of surface quantum flows, which may be relevant to studies of toroidal modes (Vretenar, Nesterenko, Papakonstantinou) and to flows in cold atomic gases (Bulgac) and quark matter.
