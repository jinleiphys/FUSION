# Fluctuations in slope parameter in event-by-event hydrodynamics and momentum anisotropy in heavy ion collisions

> In event by event hydrodynamic model, we have simulated 30-40\% Au+Au collisions at RHIC and computed the slope parameter from the invariant pion distribution. In each event, the slope parameter fluctuates azimuthally. Fourier expansion coefficients $T_n$ for the slope parameter and the Fourier expansion coefficients $v_n$ for the azimuthal distribution $\frac{dN}{dϕ}$ are found to be strongly correlated. Strong correlation between the two expansion coefficients suggests that in addition to azimuthal distribution, fluctuations in the slope parameter of the invariant distribution can as well be used to study the final state momentum anisotropy in relativistic energy heavy ion collisions. If measured experimentally, they can serve as additional constraint for hydrodynamical modeling.

## Key claim

In event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of 30-40% Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the Fourier expansion coefficients of the azimuthally fluctuating slope parameter ($T_n$) and the standard flow coefficients ($v_n$) are strongly correlated, suggesting that slope parameter fluctuations can serve as an additional observable for studying final-state momentum anisotropy, provided they are measured experimentally to constrain hydrodynamic modeling.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code with boost invariance, employing Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions for 500 events of 30-40% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV. The initial energy density was constructed by smearing participant nucleon positions with a Gaussian width $\sigma=0.25$ fm, and the equation of state combined Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD results with a hadronic resonance gas at $T_c=174$ MeV. For each event, the invariant $\pi^-$ spectra were computed at freeze-out temperature $T_F=130$ MeV, and the slope parameter $T(\phi)$ was extracted from an exponential fit to the $p_T$ distribution in the range 1-2 GeV, then Fourier expanded to obtain temperature flow coefficients $T_n$.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: 30-40% Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV (RHIC)
- Initial time $\tau_i = 0.6$ fm; freeze-out temperature $T_F = 130$ MeV
- Slope parameter extracted from $p_T$ range 1-2 GeV
- Gaussian smearing width $\sigma = 0.25$ fm
- Correlation measure $C_{measure}$ between $T_n$ and $v_n$: $n=1$: 0.897, $n=2$: 0.824, $n=3$: 0.940, $n=4$: 0.926, $n=5$: 0.909
- Correlation measure $C_{measure}$ between $v_n$ and $\epsilon_n$: $n=1$: 0.656, $n=2$: 0.974, $n=3$: 0.846, $n=4$: 0.682, $n=5$: 0.715
- Correlation measure $C_{measure}$ between $T_n$ and $\epsilon_n$: $n=1$: 0.572, $n=2$: 0.797, $n=3$: 0.796, $n=4$: 0.651, $n=5$: 0.651

## Context

This work extends event-by-event hydrodynamic studies of flow harmonics, where previous work by Gardim et al. (2011) and others showed that elliptic flow ($v_2$) is strongly correlated with initial eccentricity ($\epsilon_2$) while higher harmonics exhibit weaker correlations due to nonlinear mode mixing. The paper introduces the novel concept of "temperature flow coefficients" $T_n$ from the azimuthal dependence of the slope parameter, demonstrating that $T_n$ and $v_n$ are more strongly correlated than either is with initial eccentricity measures. This suggests that experimental measurement of azimuthal slope parameter fluctuations could provide complementary constraints for hydrodynamic modeling beyond the standard flow coefficients.
