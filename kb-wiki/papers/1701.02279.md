# Charmonium spectrum and diffractive production in a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We study exclusive charmonium production in diffractive deep inelastic scattering and ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions within the dipole picture. The mass spectrum and light-front wavefunctions of charmonium are obtained from the basis light-front quantization approach, using the one-gluon exchange interaction plus a confining potential inspired by light-front holography. We apply these light-front wavefunctions to exclusive charmonium production. The resulting cross sections are in reasonable agreement with electron-proton collision data at HERA and ultra-peripheral nucleus collision measurements at RHIC and LHC. The charmonium cross-section has model dependence on the dipole model. We observe that the cross-section ratio of excited states to the ground state has a weaker dependence than the cross-section itself. We suggest that measurements of excited states of heavy quarkonium production in future electron-ion collision experiments will impose rigorous constraints on heavy quarkonium light-front wavefunctions, thus improving our understanding of meson structure, which eventually will help us develop a precise description of the gluon distribution function in the small-$x$ regime.

## Key claim
The charmonium light-front wavefunctions obtained from the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, which combines one-gluon exchange with a holographic confining potential, produce cross sections for exclusive charmonium production that are in reasonable agreement with HERA, RHIC, and LHC data, though the cross-section itself has model dependence on the dipole model while the cross-section ratio of excited states to the ground state shows weaker dependence.

## Method
The authors solved the eigenvalue equation of an effective light-front Hamiltonian combining holographic QCD confinement and one-gluon exchange dynamics to obtain charmonium mass spectra and light-front wavefunctions. They then applied these wavefunctions within the dipole picture to compute exclusive charmonium production in diffractive deep inelastic scattering and ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions, using both the impact parameter dependent saturation (bSat) model and the impact parameter dependent Color Glass Condensate (bCGC) model with multiple parameter sets.

## Key numbers
- The calculated charmonium spectra agree with experimental values within a root-mean-square deviation of around 50 MeV for states below the open flavor thresholds
- For coherent J/Psi production at mid-rapidity in gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV at RHIC, the bSat I dipole parametrization yields d sigma/d y = 59.9 microbarns and the bCGC I parametrization yields d sigma/d y = 52.6 microbarns, consistent with the measured value of 45.6 +/- 13.3 (stat) +/- 5.9 (sys) microbarns
- The strong coupling constant alpha_s is fixed at alpha_s(M_c cbar) approximately 0.36 for charmonium and alpha_s(M_b bbar) approximately 0.25 for bottomonium

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to the electron anomalous magnetic moment (Honkanen 2010, Zhao 2014) and positronium (Wiecki 2014, Adhikari 2016) to heavy quarkonium systems, building on the mass spectra calculations of Li (2015, 2016). It improves upon phenomenological vector meson light-front wavefunctions used in earlier dipole model studies (Kowalski 2003, 2006) by providing wavefunctions constrained by mass spectroscopy and other observables. The results enable future electron-ion collision experiments to extract gluon distribution functions in the small-x regime through measurements of excited state to ground state cross-section ratios.
