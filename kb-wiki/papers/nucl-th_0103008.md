# Quark mean field model with density dependent couplings for finite nuclei

## Key claim

The quark mean field (QMF) model, which uses the constituent quark model for the nucleon, can reproduce the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) results for nuclear matter while simultaneously providing satisfactory descriptions of spherical finite nuclei, provided the quark-meson couplings are made density dependent through direct coupling to the scalar field. The model predicts an increasing nucleon size and a reduction of the nucleon mass in the nuclear environment, though the exact magnitude of the radius increase depends on the choice of confinement potential (scalar versus scalar-vector).

## Method

The authors start from the QMF model, where each nucleon is described by three constituent quarks confined by a potential (either scalar confinement χ_c = ½kr² or scalar-vector confinement χ_c = ½kr²(1+γ⁰)/2 with k = 1000 MeV/fm²). They introduce density dependent couplings g^q_σ(σ) and g^q_ω(σ) for the scalar σ and vector ω mesons with quarks, parameterized as linear functions of σ^{1/3}, while keeping the ρ-meson coupling constant (g^q_ρ = 4). The parameters a_σ, b_σ, a_ω, b_ω are fitted to reproduce the scalar and vector potentials from RBHF theory (potential A) for symmetric nuclear matter. The nuclear many-body system is then solved in the relativistic mean field approximation, with the effective nucleon mass M_n^* obtained from the quark-level calculation and the meson fields determined from Klein-Gordon equations that include rearrangement terms arising from the density dependent couplings.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -15.5 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: 159 MeV
- Meson masses used: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV
- Symmetry energy: approximately 35 MeV
- Nucleon radius increase at the center of nuclei: approximately 5% for scalar confinement (χ_c = ½kr²) and near 10% for scalar-vector confinement (χ_c = ½kr²(1+γ⁰)/2)
- Effective nucleon mass reduction at the center of nuclei: approximately 40% for both confinement types

## Context

This work extends the QMF model of Shen and collaborators (2000) by introducing density dependent couplings, following the same philosophy as the density dependent relativistic mean field (RMF) approach developed by Typel and Wolter (1999) and others, which reproduced RBHF results for nuclear matter while describing finite nuclei. The key advance is that the present model operates at the quark level rather than the hadronic level, allowing study of nucleon structure modifications (swelling and mass reduction) inside nuclei, which is inaccessible to purely hadronic approaches like RBHF or standard RMF. The results enable future investigations of medium modifications of hadron properties in finite nuclei, connecting the EMC effect observations to nuclear structure calculations.
