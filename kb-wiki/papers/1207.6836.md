# Suppression of high $p_T$ hadron spectra in $p+A$ collisions

> Multiple hard and semi-hard parton scatterings in high-energy $p+A$ collisions involve multi-parton correlation in both momentum and flavor inside the projectile proton which will lead to modification of the final hadron spectra relative to that in $p+p$ collisions. Such modification of final hadron transverse momentum spectra in $p+A$ collisions is studied within HIJING 2.1 Monte Carlo model which includes nuclear shadowing of the initial parton distributions and transverse momentum broadening. Multi-parton flavor and momentum correlation inside the projectile are incorporated through flavor and momentum conservation which are shown to modify the flavor content and momentum spectra of final partons and most importantly lead to suppression of large $p_{T}$ hadron spectra in $p+A$ collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

Multiple hard and semi-hard parton scatterings in high-energy p+A collisions, combined with multi-parton flavor and momentum correlations inside the projectile proton, lead to suppression of high-pT hadron spectra relative to p+p collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, contrary to the naive expectation that only enhancement (Cronin effect) would occur.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING 2.1 Monte Carlo model, which incorporates nuclear shadowing of initial parton distributions and transverse momentum broadening via a kT-kick in each binary nucleon-nucleon scattering. They introduced flavor and momentum conservation through valence quark number conservation and energy-momentum reshuffling, and compared two simulation sets: the default HIJING with hard-soft coupling, and a "de-coherent hard scattering" (DHC) set where hard scatterings are simulated before soft interactions. Final hadron spectra were obtained via Lund string fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The kT-broadening width is given by ⟨k²_T⟩ = 0.14 log(√s/GeV) - 0.43 GeV².
- Nuclear modification factor R_pA for charged hadrons in d+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV shows suppression at high pT relative to p+p, compared to PHENIX and STAR data.
- For p+Pb collisions at √s = 4.4 TeV, the model predicts a suppression band for R_pA at high pT, with the default and DHC results approaching the DHC-without-shadowing scenario at large pT.
- The quark-to-gluon ratio in p+A collisions is suppressed relative to p+p collisions, especially at high pT, due to valence quark number conservation.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang 1991) to include multi-parton correlations beyond the independent nucleon-nucleon collision picture, building on earlier studies of parton shadowing (Li 2001) and the Cronin effect (Cronin 1974). It contrasts with the expectation that only enhancement (Cronin effect) occurs in p+A collisions, showing that flavor conservation and correlated hadronization of multiple jets can produce suppression. The results enable experimental tests in proposed p+Pb collisions at LHC to disentangle cold nuclear effects from jet quenching in hot quark-gluon plasma.
