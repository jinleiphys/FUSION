# Cold Kaons from hot fireballs

> The E814-collaboration has found a component of very low $m_t$ $K^+$ mesons with a slope parameter of $T \sim 15 \, \rm MeV$. We will present a scenario which explains the observed slope parameter and which allows us to predict the expected slope parameter for kaons produced in heavier systems such as Au+Au. We also will discuss the effect of the coulomb interaction on the structure of the spectrum.

## Key claim
The E814 collaboration's observation of a very low transverse-mass (m_t) slope parameter T ~ 15 MeV for K+ mesons can be explained by a scenario involving kaon production from a disoriented chiral condensate, and this scenario allows prediction of the slope parameter for heavier systems such as Au+Au, though the Coulomb interaction modifies the spectrum structure.

## Method
The author develops a theoretical scenario based on the decay of a disoriented chiral condensate to explain the anomalously low transverse-mass slope of K+ mesons observed by the E814 collaboration. The model is then used to predict the expected slope parameter for kaons produced in heavier collision systems, and the effect of the Coulomb interaction on the observed spectrum is discussed.

## Key numbers
- The E814 collaboration observed a component of very low m_t K+ mesons with a slope parameter of T ~ 15 MeV.
- The scenario allows prediction of the expected slope parameter for kaons produced in heavier systems such as Au+Au.

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of the E814 collaboration's experimental finding of low-m_t kaons in heavy-ion collisions. The scenario based on disoriented chiral condensate decay provides a framework to predict results for heavier systems like Au+Au, which would enable further experimental tests. The discussion of Coulomb effects on the spectrum structure connects to ongoing work on final-state interactions in heavy-ion collisions.
