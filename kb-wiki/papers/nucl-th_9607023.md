# Exotic Properties of Light Nuclei and their Neutron Capture Cross Sections

> We have investigated the implications of the neutron halo configuration, observed in the ground-state of some neutron-rich light nuclei, on neutron radiative transition processes. In particular, we have studied the influence of the neutron halo on the direct radiative capture (DRC) process. The energy dependence as well as the strength of E1 emission due to incident p-wave neutrons is strongly influenced by the halo configuration of the residual nucleus capturing state. We have compared the calculated 10Be(n,gamma)11Be DRC cross section with that derived from the experiment in the inverse kinematics (Coulomb dissociation of 11Be). We show from the comparison that some important information on the structure of the halo nucleus 11Be can be derived.

# Exotic Properties of Light Nuclei and their Neutron Capture Cross Sections

## Key claim
The neutron halo configuration of the 11Be ground state (a 2s1/2 state bound by only 0.505 MeV) strongly influences the energy dependence and strength of E1 emission from incident p-wave neutrons in the direct radiative capture (DRC) process, and comparison of calculated 10Be(n,gamma)11Be cross sections with Coulomb dissociation data confirms the halo structure, but only the low-energy part of the data, where experimental uncertainty is lowest, is well reproduced.

## Method
The authors performed direct radiative capture (DRC) model calculations for the 10Be(n,gamma)11Be reaction, using a Woods-Saxon potential wave function for the 2s1/2 bound state of 11Be to evaluate electric dipole matrix elements. They compared these calculated cross sections with experimental values derived from a Coulomb dissociation experiment of 11Be performed at the RIKEN RIPS radioactive beam line, using the relation between dB(E1)/dEx and the neutron capture cross section. The DRC cross section was decomposed into a product of spectroscopic factor, geometrical factor, and radial overlap integral, with the latter being a Fourier-Bessel transform of the bound state radial wave function.

## Key numbers
- The 11Be ground state is bound by only 0.505 MeV, which builds up the neutron halo structure.
- The root-mean-square radius of 11Be is 2.86 ± 0.04 fm (from Nax94).
- The first excited state of 11Be at Ex = 0.320 MeV (Jπ = 1/2−) does not participate in the Coulomb dissociation experiment because all beam nuclei are in the ground state.
- The first state above the neutron emission threshold in 11Be is at Ex = 1.778 MeV (corresponding to Ecm = 1.273 MeV) with Jπ = 3/2+ or 5/2+.
- The 1p1/2 orbit in 11Be is 320 keV above the ground state.

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations of neutron skin effects on low-energy excitation modes (Fux93, Yox95) and the authors' own prior DRC calculations for 12C(n,gamma) and 16O(n,gamma) reactions (Otx94, Mex95). The comparison with the Coulomb dissociation experiment of Nax94 provides a method to study exotic nuclear structure properties and obtain neutron capture cross sections for unstable nuclei where direct measurements are impossible, with applications to nuclear astrophysics (Atami96) for primordial and stellar nucleosynthesis.
