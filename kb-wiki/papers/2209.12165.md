# An Indirect Measurement of $^6$Li(n,$γ$) Cross Sections

> The $^6$Li(n,$γ$)$^7$Li cross sections in the neutron energy range of 0.6 to 4 MeV have been measured by the experimental implementation of the direct capture formalism. This was done by measuring the $γ$ transition probability experimentally and accounting for the spin factor by theoretical calculation. The electromagnetic transition probabilities from $^7$Li$^*$ analogous to the initial neutron capture states of $^6$Li$+n$ were measured by populating the J$_i$ states of $^7$Li through $^7$Li($p,p'$)$^7$Li$^*$ reaction. The impact of coupling of resonant states, above neutron separation threshold of $^7$Li, in the neutron capture, is observed from the capture $γ$ spectrum. The measured cross sections were reproduced through {\sc fresco} and Talys-1.95 Direct Capture Calculations.

## Key claim

The authors report the first measurement of the $^6$Li(n,$\gamma$) cross section in the neutron energy range of 0.6 to 4 MeV, using an indirect method that populates the initial neutron capture states via inelastic proton scattering on $^7$Li. The measurement is limited to the direct capture and compound nuclear contributions, and the authors note that preequilibrium contributions are not addressed by their methodology.

## Method

The experiment used a 16 MeV proton beam from the BARC-TIFR Pelletron facility on a $^{nat}$Li target of 1.4 mg/cm$^2$. Two silicon detector telescopes at $\pm 55^\circ$ recorded inelastically scattered protons, while a 3" diameter by 7" length LaBr$_3$ scintillator at $90^\circ$ measured $\gamma$ rays. The $\gamma$ transition probability $\Gamma(E_x)$ from the initial neutron capture states of $^7$Li to the bound $3/2^-$ ground state and $1/2^-$ 477 keV state was extracted from the unfolded $E_\gamma$-$E_x$ correlation matrix. The cross sections were then deduced as $\sigma = g_j \Gamma(E_x)$, where the spin factor $g_j$ was calculated theoretically using the direct capture module in Talys-1.95 with optical potentials from Chiba et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

* Measured $^6$Li(n,$\gamma$) cross sections in the $\mu$b range for neutron energies from 0.6 to 4 MeV, covering this energy range for the first time.
* The only prior energy-dependent measurement, by Ohsaki et al., covered the neutron energy range of 20 to 80 keV.
* The $^6$Li(n,$\gamma$) Q-value is 7.25 MeV, while the $^7$Li $\rightarrow \alpha + t$ breakup threshold is 2.47 MeV.
* The $\alpha + t$ breakup continuum was discretized with a relative energy bin of 0.5 MeV in the Fresco calculations.
* An optimized average spectroscopic factor of 0.75 was used for the $\alpha + t$ continuum in the Fresco calculations.

## Context

This work extends the only prior energy-dependent measurement of $^6$Li(n,$\gamma$) by Ohsaki et al. (2004), which covered 20-80 keV, into the MeV region for the first time. The method is analogous to the established Oslo method (Schiller et al., 2000; Guttormsen et al., 1987) but uses a single LaBr$_3$ scintillator rather than a $\gamma$ calorimeter, which is feasible because the $\gamma$ multiplicity is near unity. The results show that existing evaluations in ENDF/B-VIII.0, JEFF-3.2, and JENDL-5 do not reproduce the measured cross sections above 0.1 MeV, indicating that the R-matrix fitting used to derive these evaluations from $^6$Li(n,$\alpha$) data may be inappropriate for parametrizing the direct capture component.
