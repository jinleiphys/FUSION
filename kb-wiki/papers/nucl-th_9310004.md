# Spectroscopic Factors in $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb from $(e,e'p)$: Fully Relativistic Analysis

> We present results for spectroscopic factors of the outermost shells in $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, which have been derived from the comparison between the available quasielastic ($e,e'p$) data from NIKHEF-K and the corresponding calculated cross-sections obtained within a fully relativistic formalism. We include exactly the effect of Coulomb distortion on the electron wave functions and discuss its role in the extraction of the spectroscopic factors from experiment. Without any adjustable parameter, we find spectroscopic factors of about 70\%, consistent with theoretical predictions. We compare our results with previous relativistic and nonrelativistic analyses of ($e,e'p$) data. In addition to Coulomb distortion effects we discuss different choices of the nucleon current operator and also analyze the effects due to the relativistic treatment of the outgoing-distorted and bound nucleon wave functions.

# Spectroscopic Factors in $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb from $(e,e'p)$: Fully Relativistic Analysis

## Key claim
Without any adjustable parameter, the authors find spectroscopic factors of about 70% for the outermost shells in $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, consistent with theoretical predictions, but only when using exact relativistic DWBA treatment of electron Coulomb distortion; previous nonrelativistic analyses gave values that were "clearly too low" (e.g., $0.49\pm 0.05$ for the $3s_{1/2}$ shell in $^{208}$Pb) due to overestimated focussing and underestimated absorption.

## Method
The authors performed fully relativistic DWBA calculations of quasielastic $(e,e'p)$ cross-sections for proton knockout from the $3s_{1/2}$ and $2d_{3/2}$ shells in $^{208}$Pb and the $2s_{1/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ shells in $^{40}$Ca, using exact partial-wave treatment of electron Coulomb distortion. Bound proton wave functions were obtained from the relativistic Hartree formalism of Horowitz and Serot (TIMORA code), outgoing proton wave functions from relativistic S-V optical potentials fitted to elastic proton scattering data, and both the $cc1$ and $cc2$ nucleon current operators were compared. Spectroscopic factors were extracted by scaling the calculated reduced cross-sections $\rho(P_m)$ to experimental data from NIKHEF-K (Quint, 1987; Kramer, 1989) using an error-weighted least-squares procedure.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factor for $3s_{1/2}$ shell in $^{208}$Pb: $S=0.71$ (with $cc2$ operator), in agreement with Jin et al. (1992) who obtained $0.71$; previous nonrelativistic analysis gave $0.49\pm 0.05$.
- Spectroscopic factor for $2d_{3/2}$ shell in $^{208}$Pb: $S=0.68$ (with $cc1$) and $S=0.74$ (with $cc2$).
- Spectroscopic factor for $2s_{1/2}$ shell in $^{40}$Ca: $S=0.44$ (with $cc1$), with poor fit quality ($Q>7$).
- Spectroscopic factor for $1d_{3/2}$ shell in $^{40}$Ca: $S=0.76$ (with $cc2$), with good fit quality ($Q<2$).
- Incoming electron energy: $\epsilon_i=412$ MeV for $^{208}$Pb, $375$ MeV for $^{40}$Ca; outgoing proton kinetic energy fixed at $T_F=100$ MeV.
- Ratio of DWBA to PWBA reduced cross-sections at the first maximum for $^{208}$Pb: $1.30$ (this work), compared to $1.31$ (Jin et al., 1992), $1.18$ (McDermott, 1990, HCA), $1.49$ (DWEEPY1), and $1.38$ (DWEEPY2).
- Numerical accuracy estimated at less than 2% in spectroscopic factors.

## Context
This work extends the fully relativistic DWBA treatment of $(e,e'p)$ reactions pioneered by McDermott (1990) and Jin et al. (1992), resolving discrepancies between those earlier calculations by identifying that different bound-state wave functions caused the disagreement in PWBA limits. The authors confirm the result of Jin et al. (1992) that exact Coulomb distortion treatment yields spectroscopic factors near 70% for $^{208}$Pb, consistent with theoretical predictions from dispersion-relation approaches (Mahaux and Sartor, 1991) and variational nuclear matter calculations (Pandharipande, 1984), and significantly higher than the values from nonrelativistic eikonal analyses (DWEEPY) used at NIKHEF-K. The work enables reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors from $(e,e'p)$ data in heavy nuclei, and shows that for medium-mass nuclei like $^{40}$Ca the focussing effect is negligible but exact DWBA is still needed to determine the precise magnitude of the displacement effect.
