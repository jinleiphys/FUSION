# Fermion and gluon spectral functions far from equilibrium

> Motivated by the quark-gluon plasma, we develop a simulation method to obtain the spectral function of (Wilson) fermions non-perturbatively in a non-Abelian gauge theory with large gluon occupation numbers [arXiv:2106.11319]. We apply our method to a non-Abelian plasma close to its non-thermal fixed point, i.e., in a far-from-equilibrium self-similar regime, and find mostly very good agreement with perturbative hard loop (HTL) calculations. For the first time, we extract the full momentum dependence of the damping rate of fermionic collective excitations and compare our results to recent non-perturbative extractions of gluonic spectral functions in two and three spatial dimensions [arXiv:2101.02715, arXiv:1804.01966].

## Key claim

The authors develop a non-perturbative simulation method to extract the fermion spectral function in a non-Abelian gauge theory with large gluon occupation numbers, and find that in a far-from-equilibrium self-similar regime the spectral function mostly agrees with perturbative hard-loop (HTL) calculations, but the damping rate at zero momentum scales with the fermion mass rather than with the effective temperature as HTL would predict.

## Method

The method combines classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge fields with the Dirac equation for Wilson fermions, neglecting fermion backreaction on the gauge fields. The fermion spectral function is computed by evolving plane-wave initial conditions for the fermionic mode functions on a 256^3 lattice through the classical gauge field configurations, then Fourier transforming the resulting correlation functions to obtain the spectral function in frequency-momentum space. The gluonic spectral function is extracted using a similar classical-statistical approach combined with linear response theory.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 256^3 with Q a_s = 0.75 for fermion simulations; also 64^3 with Q a_s = 1 for comparison
- Fermion mass: m = 0.003125 Q (nearly vanishing)
- Reference time for spectral function extraction: Q t_perp = 1500
- Self-similar scaling exponents in 3+1D: beta = -1/7, alpha = 4 beta
- Self-similar scaling exponents in 2+1D: beta = -1/5, alpha = 3 beta
- Damping rate at zero momentum scales as gamma(t,p=0) ~ m_F(t) ~ Q (Q t)^{-1/7}, not as gamma^HTL ~ Q (Q t)^{-3/7}
- Gluonic damping rate in 3+1D: gamma_T(t,p)/omega(t,p=0) ~ (Q t)^{-2/7}, decreasing with time
- In 2+1D, damping rate gamma(t,p) ~ omega_plas(t) (the plasmon mass), remaining of order the dispersion

## Context

This work extends previous non-perturbative extractions of gluonic spectral functions in two and three spatial dimensions (Boguslavski 2018, Boguslavski 2021) to fermionic degrees of freedom, and provides the first full momentum dependence of the fermion damping rate in a far-from-equilibrium plasma. The results enable future studies of transport coefficients (such as photon production rates) and applications to Bjorken-expanding systems relevant for heavy-ion collisions, as well as extension to heavy quarks.
