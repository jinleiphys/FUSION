# Recent Developments of A Multi-Phase Transport Model

> After the public release of A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model in 2004 and detailed descriptions of its physics in a 2005 paper, the model has been constantly updated and developed to make it more versatile and to include more physical processes. This an overview of recent developments of the AMPT model. Ongoing work to fix the violation of charge conservation in the code as well as possible directions for future work are also discussed.

# Recent Developments of A Multi-Phase Transport Model

## Key claim
The AMPT model has been continuously updated after its 2004 public release and 2005 physics description to include more physical processes and greater versatility, but the code currently violates electric charge conservation due to incomplete treatment of kaon charge states and isospin-averaged cross sections, which must be fixed before the model can reliably study charge-dependent observables.

## Method
The author summarizes modifications made to the AMPT model after the 2008 version v1.21/v2.21, including the addition of deuteron interactions in the hadron cascade, user-requested features such as arbitrary hadron insertion and event triggering, and the inclusion of deformed Uranium-238 as a projectile/target nucleus. The ongoing work to restore charge conservation is described, requiring the addition of explicit K0 and anti-K0 particles in the hadron cascade and the implementation of isospin-dependent cross sections for all hadronic channels.

## Key numbers
- The 2004 official version was AMPT v1.11 (default) / v2.11 (string melting); the 2008 version was v1.21 (default) / v2.21 (string melting).
- The quark coalescence in the string melting version starts at energy densities much less than 1 GeV/fm3, which is too low compared to the equation of state from lattice QCD.
- The snapshot figures show a 200 AGeV Au+Au event at impact parameter b=0 fm (central) and b=10 fm (peripheral), with the full box length being 30 fm along each direction.

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model originally developed by Zhang (1999) for RHIC predictions and the string melting version introduced by Lin (2001) to account for partonic flow effects. The discovery of triangular flow from initial geometrical fluctuations using AMPT was reported by Alver (2010). The author identifies two future directions: developing key ingredients within the existing parton-hadron framework (including dynamical parton recombination based on local energy density, as studied by Zhang (2008)) or coupling AMPT with a 3+1D hydrodynamic model to create a hybrid model with self-consistent initial conditions across different collision systems and energies.
