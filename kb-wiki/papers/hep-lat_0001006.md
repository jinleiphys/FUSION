# Domain wall fermions in vector theories

> Applications of Domain Wall fermions to various vector-like lattice theories are reviewed with an emphasis on QCD thermodynamics. Methods for improving their chiral properties at strong coupling are discussed and results from implementing them are presented.

# Domain wall fermions in vector theories

## Key claim

Domain Wall Fermions (DWF) can separate the chiral and Lorentz symmetry limits in lattice QCD, allowing the chiral limit to be approached exponentially in the extra dimension length $L_s$ even at finite lattice spacing, but the required $L_s$ may be as large as 64–96 to achieve a physical pion mass at the couplings accessible in thermodynamic simulations.

## Method

The paper reviews numerical lattice simulations using Domain Wall Fermions with the action of Furman and Shamir (1995) as modified by Vranas (1998), where the extra dimension has $L_s$ sites, $m_0$ is the domain wall height, and $m_f$ is an explicit fermion mass coupling the two walls. Simulations were performed on the Columbia QCDSP computer for quenched and dynamical QCD with two and three flavors, using Wilson plaquette, Iwasaki improved, restricted plaquette, and enhanced rectangle gauge actions, measuring chiral condensates, pion and delta screening masses, and residual masses.

## Key numbers

- Free theory effective mass: $m_{\text{eff}} = m_0(2-m_0)[m_f + (1-m_0)^{L_s}]$ for $0 < m_0 < 2$
- In the Schwinger model, the chiral condensate $\langle \bar{\Psi}\Psi \rangle$ at $m_f=0$ decays exponentially with $L_s$, with two distinct decay rates and an inflection near $L_s=10$
- For quenched QCD above the transition on a $16^3 \times 4$ lattice at $\beta=5.71$, $L_s=32$, $m_0=1.9$, the chiral condensate shows a $1/m_f$ divergence for $m_f < 0.005$, with extrapolated $c_0 = 9.0(4) \times 10^{-4}$
- For two-flavor QCD on $16^3 \times 4$ at $\beta=5.40$ with $L_s=16$, the $m_f=0$ extrapolated $\delta-\pi$ mass difference is $0.087(17)$; at $\beta=5.45$ it is $0.031(9)$, compared to $m_\delta$ and $m_\pi \approx 1.3$
- Critical temperature for two-flavor QCD with Wilson plaquette action: $T_c = 163(4)$ MeV, pion mass $m_\pi = 427(11)$ MeV at the transition
- Critical temperature with Iwasaki improved gauge action: $T_c = 166(3)$ MeV, $m_\pi = 400(7)$ MeV
- Residual mass at the two-flavor transition with Wilson plaquette action ($\beta_c=5.325$): $m_{\text{res}} = 0.028(2)$, inverse lattice spacing $a^{-1} \approx 700$ MeV
- Residual mass with Iwasaki action at transition ($\beta_c=1.9$): $m_{\text{res}} = 0.030(3)$
- Residual mass with restricted plaquette action ($c=0.8$): $m_{\text{res}} = 0.027(1)$
- Residual mass with enhanced rectangle action ($x=3.0$): $m_{\text{res}} = 0.0050(4)$ at $\beta \approx 15$
- Minimum decay rate from large N for $Z_2 \times Z_2$ model: $-\ln(2-\sqrt{2}) = 0.535$

## Context

This review extends the development of Domain Wall Fermions from Kaplan (1992) and the overlap formalism of Narayanan and Neuberger (1993–1995), applying them to vector-like theories including the Schwinger model, four-Fermi models, and QCD thermodynamics. The work addresses the Aoki phase (Aoki, 1984) observed in DWF, and the unresolved question of whether the two-flavor QCD phase transition is second order, which requires $U_A(1)$ breaking (Pisarski, 1984). The results enable future large-scale simulations with improved gauge actions to reduce the required $L_s$ for chiral symmetry restoration, and direct comparisons with staggered fermion results (Kogut and Lagae, 1998) at the same physical scale.
