# Comment on E2 Contribution to the 8B ---> p + 7Be Coulomb Dissociation Cross Section [K. Langanke and T.D. Shoppa, Phys. Rev. C49(1994)R1771, ER(1994)]

> The E2 cross section calculated by Langanke and Shoppa for the Riken experiment on the Coulomb Dissociation of 8B uses E2 nuclear matrix elements from one specific model. Other nuclear models predict a considerably smaller E2 cross section (by a pproximately a factor of 3 to 4), and Langanke and Shoppa appear to assume the most optimistic scenario predicting large E2 cross section. We also note that Barker has already criticised the nuclear model used by Langanke and Shoppa. A MODEL INDEPENDENT Chi-Square analysis of the Riken data, suggest the best fit for the current Riken data is obtained with E1 amplitudes only. The upper limit (90% confidence) on the E2 component derived from our Chi-Square analysis is considerably smaller than used by Langanke and Shoppa. The MODEL DEPENDENT analysis of Langanke-Shoppa should not be considered as a correction to the Riken result, as claimed, and their quoted S17(0) is not substantiated.

# Comment on E2 Contribution to the 8B → p + 7Be Coulomb Dissociation Cross Section

## Key claim

The E2 cross section calculated by Langanke and Shoppa (1994) for the Riken Coulomb dissociation experiment uses nuclear matrix elements from one specific model (Kim et al., 1987) that predicts the largest E2 contribution among available models; a model-independent chi-square analysis of the same Riken data yields a best fit with E1 amplitudes only, and the 90% confidence upper limit on the E2 component is considerably smaller than assumed by Langanke and Shoppa, invalidating their quoted S17(0) value.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent chi-square fit of the measured Coulomb dissociation cross section at 600 keV (and similarly at 800 and 1000 keV) using σ_CD(E1) + σ_CD(E2), where the S-factors S_E1 and S_E2 were treated as free fit parameters. The angular distributions of E1 and E2 cross sections for the Riken data at 46.5 MeV/u are sufficiently different (E1 dominant at approximately 1-2 degrees, E2 at 4-5 degrees) to allow extraction of both components from the data.

## Key numbers

- Best fit at 600 keV: S_E1 = 18 ± 3 eV-b, S_E2 = 0 ± 6 meV-b
- 90% confidence upper limit: S_E2 < 12 meV-b, corresponding to S_E2/S_E1 < 6 × 10^(-4)
- For the 632 keV resonance, Kim et al. (1987) predict S_E2/S_E1 = 1.8 × 10^(-3), while Typel and Baur (1994) predict 5 × 10^(-4) (a factor of 4 smaller)
- Descouvemont and Baye (1994) predict B(E2: 2+ → 1+) = 13 W.u. for the 632 keV resonance, a factor of 2.4 smaller than Kim et al. (1987)
- The LS "correction factors" are given as 1 - α = 0.27 ± 0.1, 0.18 ± 0.16, and 0.23 ± 0.17 at 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 MeV respectively

## Context

This comment directly challenges the analysis of Langanke and Shoppa (1994) regarding the E2 contribution to the 8B Coulomb dissociation cross section measured by Motobayashi et al. (1994). The authors note that Barker (1980, 1988) had already criticized the theoretical foundation of the Kim et al. (1987) model used by Langanke and Shoppa. The work enables a more cautious interpretation of the Riken data, suggesting that model-dependent corrections should not be applied and that the original assumption of E1-only analysis may be justified, with implications for the extracted astrophysical S17(0) factor relevant to solar neutrino physics.
