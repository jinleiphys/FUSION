# A New Parametrization of the Neutrino Mixing Matrix

> The neutrino mixing matrix is expanded in powers of a small parameter $λ$, which approximately equals to 0.1. The meaning of every order of the expansion is discussed respectively, and the range of $λ$ is carefully calculated. We also present some applications of this new parametrization, such as to the expression of the Jarlskog parameter $J$, in which the simplicities and advantages of this parametrization are shown.

# A New Parametrization of the Neutrino Mixing Matrix

## Key claim
The neutrino mixing matrix can be expanded in powers of a small parameter λ (approximately 0.1) around a bi-maximal mixing pattern, rather than around the unit matrix as in the Wolfenstein parametrization of the quark mixing matrix. The expansion is valid only for λ in the range 0.08 < λ < 0.17, which is derived from experimental constraints on the solar mixing angle.

## Method
The authors expand the Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata (MNS) neutrino mixing matrix in powers of a small parameter λ, defined by V_{e1} = √2/2 + λ, which measures the deviation from the bi-maximal mixing pattern. They express all three mixing angles (θ₁, θ₂, θ₃) and all matrix elements as power series in λ up to order λ⁴, introducing two additional parameters a and b of order 1 to describe V_{μ3} = √2/2 + aλ² and V_{e3} = bλ². The allowed ranges of λ, a, and b are then determined from the 3σ experimental constraints on sinθ_atm, sinθ_chz, and sinθ_sol.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter λ is constrained to the range 0.08 < λ < 0.17, consistent with the initial estimate λ ≈ 0.1.
- The parameter b, defined by sinθ_chz = bλ², lies in the range 1.56 < b < 7.03, with the best-fit point sinθ_chz = 0.045.
- The parameter a, defined by V_{μ3} = √2/2 + aλ², lies in the range 0.35 < a < 1.56, with the best-fit point V_{μ3} = 0.72.
- The Jarlskog parameter J is predicted to be in the range J ≈ 0.00996 to 0.01096 (taking sinδ ≈ 1), expressed as J = (√2/2)⁴ bλ² sinδ (1 − 4λ²) to order λ⁴.
- The experimental 3σ ranges used are: 0.58 < sinθ_atm < 0.81, 0 < sinθ_chz < 0.16, and 0.48 < sinθ_sol < 0.61.
- The mass-squared differences (3σ ranges) are: 1.4×10⁻³ eV² < Δm²_atm < 3.3×10⁻³ eV² and 7.3×10⁻⁵ eV² < Δm²_sol < 9.1×10⁻⁵ eV².

## Context
This work extends the approach of the Wolfenstein parametrization (Wolfenstein, 1983) for the quark CKM matrix to the neutrino sector, but expands around the bi-maximal mixing pattern rather than the unit matrix. It contrasts with the Wolfenstein-like parametrization of Xing (Xing, 2002), which required higher orders of small non-diagonal elements. The parametrization enables simple expressions for the Jarlskog parameter J, the effective Majorana mass term ⟨m⟩_{ee} relevant for neutrinoless double beta decay, and the effective neutrino mass term ⟨m⟩²_e, linking these observable quantities to the mass-squared differences Δm²_sol and Δm²_atm.
