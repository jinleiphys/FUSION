# Present status of light flavoured scalar resonances

> This talk is a brief account of recent developments on light scalar meson spectroscopy. I mostly focus on the major revision of the $σ$ or $f_0(500)$ meson in the Review of Particle Physics, driven by new data and the accummulation of rigorous dispersive studies, but I also comment on recent updates of other scalars and future progress.

# Present status of light flavoured scalar resonances

## Key claim
The 2012 Review of Particle Physics (RPP) made a major revision of the σ/f₀(500) meson parameters, reducing the mass range by more than a factor of five and the width range by almost a factor of two, driven by the consistency of rigorous dispersive analyses and new precise NA48/2 data near ππ threshold; however, the author argues that the RPP criteria remain conservative and that only pole extractions based on rigorous analytic methods should be relied upon.

## Method
The author reviews the application of partial-wave dispersion relations, specifically Roy equations and the once-subtracted GKPY (Garcia-Martin–Kaminski–Pelaez–Yndurain) equations, to ππ scattering data, using these relations to check data consistency, constrain fits, and analytically continue amplitudes into the complex plane to extract resonance poles. For the σ meson, the author's group starts from Unconstrained Fits to Data (UFD), then slightly modifies parameters to satisfy dispersion relations while preserving data description, producing Constrained Fits to Data (CFD) that are used for pole determination.

## Key numbers
- σ/f₀(500) pole from GKPY equations: √s_σ = (457⁺¹⁴₋₁₃) − i(279⁺¹¹₋₇) MeV (Garcia-Martin et al., 2011)
- σ/f₀(500) pole from Roy equations: √s_σ = (445 ± 25) − i(278⁺²²₋₁₈) MeV (Garcia-Martin et al., 2011)
- RPP "more radical" average of four most advanced dispersive analyses: √s_σ = (446 ± 6) − i(276 ± 5) MeV
- 2012 RPP σ mass range: 400 to 550 MeV (reduced from 400–1200 MeV in 2010)
- 2012 RPP σ width range: 400 to 700 MeV (reduced from 500–1000 MeV in 2010)
- f₀(980) pole from GKPY equations: √s_f₀(980) = (996 ± 7) − i(25⁺¹⁰₋₆) MeV
- f₀(980) pole from Roy equations: √s_f₀(980) = (1003⁺⁵₋₂₇) − i(21⁺¹⁰₋₈) MeV
- 2012 RPP f₀(980) mass update: 990 ± 20 MeV
- K₀*(800) pole from dispersive approaches: (658 ± 13) − i(278.5 ± 12) MeV (Descotes-Genon et al., 2006)
- K₀*(800) t-matrix pole from BES2 J/Ψ decays: 764⁺⁷¹₋₅₄ − i(306 ± 149⁺¹⁴³₋₈₅) MeV

## Context
This talk reviews developments since the 2009 Chiral Dynamics Workshop, focusing on the 2012 RPP revisions that finally acknowledged what the "scalar community" had known for years: that dispersive analyses (Caprini et al., 2005; Garcia-Martin et al., 2011; Moussallam, 2011) consistently place the σ pole near 450 − i275 MeV. The work extends earlier Roy equation studies (Ananthanarayan et al., 2001; Colangelo et al., 2001; Kaminski et al., 2002) and resolves a longstanding conflict between "dip" and "no-dip" scenarios for the f₀(980) inelasticity. These results enable future RPP revisions for other light scalars, particularly the K₀*(800) which the author argues should be elevated from "needs confirmation" to "well established" status.
