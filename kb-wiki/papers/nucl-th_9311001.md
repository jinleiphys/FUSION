# Thermo Field Dynamics for Quantum Fields with Continuous Mass Spectrum applied to Nuclear Physics

> Transport coefficients are obtained by incorporating a gauge principle into thermo field dynamics of inhomogeneous systems. In contrast to previous derivations, neither imaginary time arguments nor perturbation theory in powers of a coupling constant are used in the calculation. Numerical values are calculated for the pion component in hot nuclear matter.

## Key claim
Transport coefficients for the pion component in hot nuclear matter are obtained by gauging the Bogoliubov symmetry of thermo field dynamics, without using imaginary time arguments or perturbation theory in powers of a coupling constant; the derivation relies on a gradient expansion and neglects higher-order correlations beyond the commutator function of generalized free fields.

## Method
The author gauges the global Bogoliubov (Sp(2) = SU(1,1)) symmetry of the time evolution generator in Liouville space, introducing a gauge potential that couples physical particle operators to gradients in external parameters. For an interacting charged scalar field expanded into momentum eigenmodes, the generator of the local symmetry is constructed and used to compute particle and energy currents to first order in gradients via a commutator with the gauge generator. Transport coefficients (diffusion coefficient and thermal conductivity) are then extracted from the linear response of these currents to gradients of temperature and chemical potential, using a spectral function for pions in a homogeneous gas of nucleons and Delta-33 resonances.

## Key numbers
- Thermal conductivity at a density of 1.59 times nuclear matter density: lambda approximately 9897 GeV/fm^2 times [T/GeV]^3.70.
- High-temperature asymptotic limit of thermal conductivity for a simple spectral function: lambda_0 proportional to (A/gamma_bar) T^3, where gamma_bar is a momentum-averaged width.
- Diffusion coefficient and thermal conductivity diverge as the particle width (interaction strength) goes to zero.
- The Poisson bracket contributes a factor of hbar to the current, while the inverse width contributes a factor of 1/hbar to the transport coefficient.

## Context
This work extends the thermo field dynamics approach of Umezawa (1993) and the generalized free field expansion of Low (1988) to inhomogeneous systems by incorporating a local gauge principle. It contrasts with earlier derivations of transport coefficients that relied on imaginary-time methods or perturbative expansions in the coupling constant, such as the fluid-dynamical calculation of Hosoya, Sakagami, and Takao (1984) and the pion scattering data fits of Weldon (1993) and Gasser (1985). The method enables calculation of transport coefficients for systems with continuous mass spectra, such as pions in hot nuclear matter, and shows that off-shell effects can substantially increase relaxation times compared to fluid-dynamical models.
