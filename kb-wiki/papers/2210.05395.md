# Lattice QCD studies on decuplet baryons as meson-baryon bound states in the HAL QCD method

> We study decuplet baryons from meson-baryon interactions in lattice QCD, in particular, $Δ$ and $Ω$ baryons from P-wave $I=3/2$ $Nπ$ and $I=0$ $Ξ\bar{K}$ interactions, respectively. Interaction potentials are calculated in the HAL QCD method using 3-quark-type source operators at $m_π \approx 410~\textrm{MeV}$ and $m_{K} \approx 635~\textrm{MeV}$, where $Δ$ as well as $Ω$ baryons are stable. We use the conventional stochastic estimate of all-to-all propagators combined with the all-mode averaging to reduce statistical fluctuations. We have found that the $Ξ\bar K$ system has a weaker attraction than the $Nπ$ system while the binding energy from the threshold is larger for $Ω$ than $Δ$. This suggests that an inequality $m_{N}+m_π-m_Δ<m_Ξ+m_{\bar K}-m_Ω$ comes mainly from a smaller spatial size of a $Ξ\bar K$ bound state due to a larger reduced mass, rather than its interaction. Root-mean-square distances of bound states in both systems are small, indicating that $Δ$ and $Ω$ are tightly bound states and thus can be regarded qualitatively as composite states of 3 quarks. Results of binding energies agree with those obtained from temporal 2-point functions within large systematic errors, which arise dominantly from the lattice artifact at short distances.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the larger binding energy of the Ω baryon compared to the Δ baryon arises from stronger interactions or from kinematic effects, finding that the ΞK̅ system has a weaker attraction than the Nπ system, yet the Ω binding energy is larger, suggesting the hierarchy comes mainly from the smaller spatial size of the ΞK̅ bound state due to its larger reduced mass, not from the interaction strength.

## Method

The authors employed the time-dependent HAL QCD method on (2+1)-flavor gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS Collaboration at a lattice spacing of 0.0907(13) fm, with pion mass mπ ≈ 410 MeV and kaon mass mK ≈ 635 MeV. They extracted leading-order (LO) potentials for P-wave I=3/2 Nπ and I=0 ΞK̅ systems from 3-point correlation functions using 3-quark-type source operators for Δ and Ω baryons, combined with stochastic all-to-all propagators and all-mode averaging. The potentials were fitted with three-Gaussian functions, and scattering phase shifts, binding energies, and root-mean-square distances were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation via the Gaussian Expansion Method.

## Key numbers

*   Binding energy of the Δ baryon from the Nπ potential: EbindNπ = 115.6 (17.2) (+54.0 / -69.3) MeV.
*   Binding energy of the Ω baryon from the ΞK̅ potential: EbindΞK̅ = 256.6 (5.5) (+88.2 / -82.2) MeV.
*   Binding energy from baryon masses measured from 2-point functions: mN + mπ - mΔ = 105.5(5.2) MeV and mΞ + mK̅ - mΩ = 293.5(2.8) MeV.
*   Hadron masses at the simulation point: mπ = 411.2(1.7) MeV, mK = 635.1(1.5) MeV, mN = 1217.2(4.7) MeV, mΞ = 1505.3(4.5) MeV, mΔ = 1522.9(7.8) MeV, mΩ = 1847.0(6.5) MeV.
*   Root-mean-square distance for the Nπ bound state: √⟨r²⟩Nπ = 0.70(3) (+0.09 / -0.08) fm.
*   Root-mean-square distance for the ΞK̅ bound state: √⟨r²⟩ΞK̅ = 0.44(1) (+0.05 / -0.05) fm.

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method to decuplet baryons for the first time, analyzing Δ and Ω baryons as meson-baryon bound states. It contrasts with the traditional 3-quark picture and with finite-volume method studies of the Δ resonance at lighter quark masses (Andersen 2017, Paul 2018, Bulava 2022). The results enable future applications of the HAL QCD method to exotic hadrons such as pentaquarks, and the finding that the binding hierarchy is kinematic rather than interaction-driven provides a new perspective on the stability of the Ω baryon.
