# Conformal transformations of conservation equations in spin hydrodynamics

> We study and analyse the conformal transformations of different conservation laws in the spin hydrodynamics framework.

## Key claim

The paper derives the conformal transformation properties of the conservation laws in spin hydrodynamics and identifies the condition required for conformal invariance of the spin conservation law, namely that the spin tensor must be traceless in its first two indices. The GLW and HW spin tensor definitions explicitly break this condition, meaning the spin conservation law is not conformally invariant in those formulations.

## Method

The author applies Weyl rescaling transformations to the conservation equations for net baryon number, energy-momentum, and spin angular momentum in a 4D conformal fluid dynamics framework. Using the known conformal weights of the relevant quantities (net baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor) and the transformation of Christoffel symbols under Weyl rescaling, the transformed conservation equations are derived. The conditions for conformal invariance are then extracted from the transformed equations.

## Key numbers

* Net baryon number current has conformal weight Delta_N = 4, energy-momentum tensor has conformal weight Delta_T = 6, and spin tensor has conformal weight Delta_S = 6 (from Eq. (ConfN)).
* Energy density and pressure have mass dimension 4 and conformal weight Delta_E = Delta_P = 4.
* Net baryon density has conformal weight Delta_N = 3.
* Temperature and baryon chemical potential have conformal weight Delta_T = Delta_mu = 1.
* The spinor field psi and its dual have conformal weight Delta_psi = 3/2, and gamma matrices have conformal weight Delta_gamma^mu = 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of conformal invariance in relativistic fluid dynamics, previously developed for non-spinning fluids by Gubser (2010) and others (Baier 2007, Bhattacharyya 2007, Loganayagam 2008), to the spin hydrodynamics framework. The spin hydrodynamic formalism was first formulated by Florkowski (2017) and has been developed in subsequent works (Florkowski 2018, 2019, Bhadury 2020). The paper shows that while the net baryon number conservation equation is conformally invariant, the spin conservation law requires the spin tensor to satisfy a tracelessness condition that is not met by the GLW spin tensor (DeGroot 1980) or the HW spin tensor (Speranza 2020, Weickgenannt 2020), thus breaking conformal invariance for those formulations. This result enables further investigation into whether alternative spin tensor definitions can restore conformal symmetry in spin hydrodynamics.
