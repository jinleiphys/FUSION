# Moat hyperons in cold neutron stars

> We investigate the hyperonic equation of state within the non-linear derivative model that incorporates a momentum dependence on the interactions, with a special emphasis on properly establishing the conditions for hyperon appearance in neutron star matter. We demonstrate that hyperons can appear at finite momentum, forming a so-called ``moat" region, even when they are absent at lower momenta. Our study shows that this phenomenon significantly alters the composition and equation of state of hyperonic matter as compared to the cases when it is disregarded, highlighting the importance of accurately treating the momentum dependence of the baryon fields in dense matter.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that hyperons can appear in neutron star matter at finite momentum, forming a "moat" region, even when they are absent at lower momenta, but only if the momentum dependence of baryon interactions is treated consistently; this phenomenon significantly alters the composition and equation of state compared to conventional treatments that ignore the fragmentation of the Fermi sphere.

## Method

The authors employ the Non-Linear Derivative (NLD) model, a relativistic mean-field framework that introduces momentum-dependent form factors into the baryon-meson couplings, making the scalar and vector self-energies functions of momentum. They solve the beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions at zero temperature for three cases: nucleons only, hyperons treated with the conventional (monotonic) Fermi sphere assumption, and hyperons treated with full accounting for the non-monotonic single-particle energy spectrum that can produce multiple occupied momentum intervals. The model parameters (SET I) are tuned to chiral effective field theory results from Petschauer (2016), and only Λ and Σ− hyperons are included.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density ρ₀ is approximately 0.16 fm⁻³ (particle density) or 2.7×10¹⁴ g/cm³ (mass density).
- In the consistent "NY" calculation, Σ− hyperons first appear at a baryon density ρ_B ≈ 0.75 fm⁻³.
- At ρ_B = 1.2 fm⁻³, the Σ− hyperon moat region has an energy spread of only about 0.2 MeV, with a momentum width of less than 50 MeV.
- At ρ_B = 1.2 fm⁻³, Σ− hyperons reach an abundance of 20% of the baryon population.
- The maximum mass for the consistent "NY" calculation is M ≈ 1.98 M⊙, while the nucleonic-only model reaches M ≈ 2.03 M⊙.
- The hyperonic models deviate from the nucleonic mass-radius relation only above M ∼ 1.9 M⊙, corresponding to a central density ρ_B,c ≈ 0.7 fm⁻³.
- Λ hyperons appear in stars with masses M ≳ 1.5 M⊙.

## Context

This work extends the NLD model previously applied to nucleonic matter by Gaitanos (2009, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2015) and to hyperonic matter by Chorozidou (2024), correcting an inconsistency in the treatment of the Fermi sphere fragmentation that led to an abrupt disappearance of Λ hyperons in that earlier work. The "moat" phenomenon was previously discussed in the context of heavy-ion collisions by Pisarski (2021) and Rennecke. The results show that cold neutron star observables (mass-radius relations) are largely degenerate with respect to the hyperonic composition, motivating future work on finite-temperature extensions that could be probed by binary neutron star merger observations, as suggested by Kochankovski (2022) and Raduta (2022).
