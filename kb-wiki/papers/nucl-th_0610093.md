# Neutrino-nucleus reactions in the delta resonance region

> Reliable estimates of neutrino-nucleus reactions in the resonance-excitation region play an important role in many of the on-going and planned neutrino oscillation experiments. We study here neutrino-nucleus reactions in the delta-particle excitation region with the use of neutrino pion-production amplitudes calculated in a formalism in which the resonance contributions and the background amplitudes are treated on the same footing. Our approach leads to the neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections that are significantly different from those obtained in the conventional approach wherein only the pure resonance amplitudes are taken into account. To assess the reliability of our formalism, we calculate the electron-nucleus scattering cross sections in the same theoretical framework; the calculated cross sections agree reasonably well with the existing data.

## Key claim

The neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections in the delta resonance region calculated using the Sato-Lee (SL) model, which treats resonance and non-resonant (background) amplitudes on the same footing, are significantly different from those obtained in the conventional approach that includes only pure resonance amplitudes; the non-resonant contributions cause the full calculation to give about 20% more strength than the resonance-only approach even in the resonance region.

## Method

The authors used the Sato-Lee (SL) dynamical model for pion electroweak production on a nucleon, which solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with an effective Hamiltonian containing both non-resonant meson-baryon interactions and bare delta-resonance creation/annihilation vertices. For the nuclear target (carbon-12), they employed a modified Fermi gas model incorporating nuclear correlation effects via the spectral function of Benhar et al. (2005), with Pauli blocking for the final nucleon, and transformed the single-nucleon pion-production amplitudes from the pion-nucleon center-of-mass frame to the laboratory frame. The same framework was applied to electron-nucleus scattering to validate the approach against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The inclusion of Pauli blocking for the final nucleon reduces the forward cross section for $\nu_e ^{12}\mathrm{C}\rightarrow e^- \pi X$ at $\theta=10^\circ$ by about 20%.
- Nuclear correlation effects from the spectral function further broaden the peak width and reduce the peak height by about 20% relative to the Fermi-gas model with Pauli blocking.
- The full SL-model calculation gives cross sections for $\nu_e ^{12}\mathrm{C}\rightarrow e^- \pi X$ that are about 20% larger than the resonance-only approach, even in the resonance region.
- For the $\nu_e N \rightarrow e^- \pi N$ reaction at $E_\nu=1$ GeV, the neutron target cross section is about 20% larger than the value expected from pure delta dominance (which would give the neutron cross section as 1/3 of the proton cross section).
- The electron-nucleus scattering calculation at $E_e=1.1$ GeV and $\theta_e=37.5^\circ$ reproduces the magnitude of the cross section in the delta-resonance region reasonably well compared to the data of Sealock (1989).

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee model, previously tested extensively against JLab data for electromagnetic pion production on single nucleons (Sato and Lee, 2001; 2002), to neutrino-nucleus reactions on carbon-12. It contrasts with the conventional approach of Paschos and collaborators (2004; 2006) and the quark-model calculations of Hemmert et al. (1995), which treat only resonance amplitudes; the SL model resolves a known discrepancy where the axial-vector N-delta transition strength from the constituent quark model is about 35% lower than the empirical value. The calculation enables more reliable estimates of neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments such as K2K, and the authors note that future work must incorporate final-state interactions, pion absorption, and medium effects on delta propagation, particularly for heavier targets like iron-56.
