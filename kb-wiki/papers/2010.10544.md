# Vector dominance, one flavored baryons, and QCD domain walls from the "hidden" Wess-Zumino term

> We further explore a recent proposal that the vector mesons in QCD have a special role as Chern-Simons fields on various QCD objects such as domain walls and the one flavored baryons. We compute contributions to domain wall theories and to the baryon current coming from a generalized Wess-Zumino term including vector mesons. The conditions that lead to the expected Chern-Simons terms and the correct spectrum of baryons, coincide with the conditions for vector meson dominance. This observation provides a theoretical explanation to the phenomenological principle of vector dominance, as well as an experimental evidence for the identification of vector mesons as the Chern-Simons fields. By deriving the Chern-Simons theories directly from an action, we obtain new results about QCD domain walls. One conclusion is the existence of a first order phase transition between domain walls as a function of the quarks' masses. We also discuss applications of our results to Seiberg duality between gluons and vector mesons and provide new evidence supporting the duality.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the phenomenological principle of vector meson dominance (VD) in QCD can be theoretically explained by identifying vector mesons as Chern-Simons fields on QCD domain walls and one-flavored baryons, provided specific conditions on the hidden Wess-Zumino (hWZ) action parameters are satisfied; the claim is hedged by the large N limit and the assumption that the hidden local symmetry (HLS) framework is valid.

## Method

The author computes contributions to the 3d effective theory on QCD domain walls and to the baryon current from a generalized Wess-Zumino term that includes U(N_f) vector mesons (rho and omega), using the hidden local symmetry formalism. The hWZ action is parametrized by three real coefficients c1, c2, c3 (plus c4 when coupling to a U(1) gauge field), and the conditions for vector dominance (vanishing of direct gamma-gamma-pi, gamma-V-pi, and V-pi-pi-pi vertices) are compared with conditions derived from requiring correct Chern-Simons theories on domain walls and correct baryon charge quantization for N_f=1 baryons.

## Key numbers

* The hWZ action is parametrized by three real numbers c1, c2, c3 in the large N limit, plus c4 when coupling to a U(1) gauge field; the proposed values are c1 = 2/3, c2 = -1/3, c3 = 1, c4 = 1.
* The eta' meson mass scales as m_eta'^2 ~ 1/N in the large N limit.
* The cusp tension for the eta' = pi domain wall is T_cusp ~ N Lambda^3, while the tension of the domain wall going through eta' = 0 is at most ~ N m^(1/2) Lambda^(5/2).
* For N_f=1, the baryon current derived from the hWZ action is B^(N_f=1) = -(c3+c4)/(8 pi^2) d omega d eta'; with c3=c4=1 this becomes B^(N_f=1) = -1/(4 pi^2) d omega d eta', giving integer baryon charge for configurations with contour integrals of eta' and omega equal to 2 pi Z.
* The three conditions for vector dominance are c4 = 1, c3 = c4, and c1 - c2 = c3, which together fix c1 - c2 = c3 = c4 = 1.

## Context

This work extends the conjecture of Kan and Karasik (2019, 2020) that vector mesons act as Chern-Simons fields on QCD objects, and provides a theoretical foundation for the 50-year-old vector dominance principle of Sakurai (1969) and Fujiwara (1984). The results contradict the proposal of Gaiotto (2017) that there is no phase transition between Yang-Mills domain walls and QCD domain walls at small quark masses, instead predicting a first-order phase transition. The paper also provides new evidence for a conjectured Seiberg-like duality between gluons and vector mesons, and enables future study of how the eta' cusp potential is generated from integrating out vector mesons rather than gluonic topological density.
