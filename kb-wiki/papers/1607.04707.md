# Properties of strongly magnetized ultradense matter and their imprints on magnetar pulsations

> We investigate the effect of strong magnetic fields on the adiabatic radial oscillations of hadronic stars. We describe magnetized hadronic matter within the framework of the relativistic nonlinear Walecka model and integrate the equations of relativistic radial oscillations to determine the fundamental pulsation mode. We consider that the magnetic field increases, in a density dependent way, from the surface, where it has a typical magnetar value of $10^{15}$ G, to the interior of the star where it can be as large as $3 \times 10^{18}$ G. We show that magnetic fields of the order of $10^{18}$ G at the stellar core produce a significant change in the frequency of neutron star pulsations with respect to unmagnetized objects. If radial pulsations are excited in magnetar flares, they can leave an imprint in the flare lightcurves and open a new window for the study of highly magnetized ultradense matter.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields of order $10^{18}$ G at the stellar core produce a significant change in the frequency of neutron star radial pulsations compared to unmagnetized objects, but only when the central field reaches $3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G; fields of $10^{17}$ G produce very small changes. The authors assume an isotropic effective magnetic pressure based on a mixed poloidal-toroidal field configuration to justify using spherically symmetric 1D equations.

## Method

The authors describe magnetized hadronic matter using the relativistic nonlinear Walecka model (GM1 parametrization) with baryons interacting via sigma-omega-rho meson exchange, including the full baryon octet and leptons (electrons and muons). They adopt a density-dependent magnetic field profile that increases from $10^{15}$ G at the surface to either $10^{17}$ G or $3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G in the interior, with two decay profiles (fast: gamma=3.00, beta=0.02; slow: gamma=2.00, beta=0.05). They then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for equilibrium configurations and solve the Gondek et al. (1997) radial oscillation equations via a shooting method to determine the fundamental mode frequency.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic field: $10^{15}$ G (typical magnetar value)
- Maximum interior magnetic field values considered: $10^{17}$ G and $3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G
- For a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ star with $B_0 = 3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G: oscillation period is about 20% smaller than unmagnetized case
- For a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ star with $B_0 = 3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G: oscillation frequency is about 20% larger than unmagnetized case
- For a $1.7 M_{\odot}$ star with $B_0 = 3.1 \times 10^{18}$ G: oscillation frequency is about 10% larger than unmagnetized case
- Saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$
- Compressibility: $K = 300$ MeV
- Maximum masses consistent with PSR J1614-2230 ($M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}$) and PSR J0348-0432 ($M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}$)

## Context

This work extends studies of radial oscillations in compact stars, following the foundational work of Chandrasekhar (1964) and the oscillation formalism of Gondek et al. (1997), to include ultra-strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars. It builds on prior studies of magnetized equations of state using the nonlinear Walecka model (Glendenning, 2000) and density-dependent magnetic field profiles (Bandyopadhyay et al., 1997; Rabhi et al., 2009). The results enable future comparison with quasi-periodic oscillations observed in giant flares from Soft Gamma Repeaters such as SGR 0526-66, SGR 1900+14, and SGR 1806-20 (Watts and Strohmayer, 2005; Israel et al., 2005), and suggest that radial pulsations, if excited in magnetar flares, could leave imprints in flare lightcurves, though the damping times depend on uncertain bulk viscosity.
