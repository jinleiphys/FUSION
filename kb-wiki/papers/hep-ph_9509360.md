# Contrasting Real-time Dynamics with Screening Phenomena at Finite Temperature

> We discuss the interpretation of Euclidean correlation functions at finite temperature ($T$) and their relationship with the corresponding real-time Green's functions. The soluble 2+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model in the large-$N$ limit is used throughout as a working example. First, the real-time bound state, identified as an elementary excitation at finite $T$, is solved. The bound state mass, the dispersion relation at low momenta, the coupling constant and decay constant are calculated. To characterize the structure of the bound state the on-shell form factor is carefully introduced and calculated. Then we examine the corresponding screening state and contrast the screening mass, coupling constant, decay constant and the screening Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with the real-time quantities. We find that, although they can be used as qualitative indicators in the low-$T$ regime, the screening states at finite $T$ in general do not reflect the properties of the corresponding real-time bound states. Besides, other relevant issues, such as the subtlety of the real-time manifestation of conservation laws due to some internal symmetries at $T\ne 0$, the temperature dependence of the pseudoscalar spectral function and its sum rule, and the high-$T$ limit of the screening state and its implications to the dimensional reduction, are also discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that screening states (static, Euclidean quantities) at finite temperature do not generally reflect the properties of the corresponding real-time bound states (dynamic, Minkowski quantities), except as qualitative indicators in the low-temperature regime, using the exactly soluble 2+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model in the large-N limit as a working example.

## Method

The authors solve the pseudoscalar bound state in the 2+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature in the large-N limit by finding poles of the retarded two-point function for the pseudoscalar current. They calculate the bound state mass, dispersion relation, coupling constant, decay constant, and on-shell form factor in real-time, then compute the corresponding screening mass, coupling, decay constant, and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from the static (Euclidean) limit of the same correlation function, and systematically compare the two sets of quantities.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: \( T_c = \mu / \ln 4 \), where \(\mu\) is the dynamical quark mass at \(T=0\)
- Dynamically generated quark mass: \( m(T) = \sigma_m(T) = \mu\left(1 - T/T_c\right) + 2T \ln\left[1 + \sqrt{1 - 4\exp(-\mu/T)}\right] \) for \(T < T_c\)
- Pion velocity squared: \( v^2(T) \) ranges from \( \lim_{T\to 0} v^2(T) = 1 \) to \( \lim_{T\to T_c} v^2(T) = 1/2 \)
- Pion coupling constant: \( g_\pi^2(T) = 4\pi m / N \left( \int_1^\infty dx \, x^{-2} \tanh(m x / 2T) \right)^{-1} \), with zero-temperature limit \( g_\pi^2 \to 4\pi\mu/N \)
- Goldberger-Treiman relation at finite temperature: \( g_\pi(T) f_\pi(T) = 2m(T) \), where \( f_\pi(T) = g_\pi(T) (N/2\pi) \int_1^\infty dx \, x^{-2} \tanh(m x / 2T) \)

## Context

This work extends the study of real-time versus Euclidean dynamics at finite temperature beyond perturbative contexts, using the Gross-Neveu model which has been employed by Kocic and Gatto for exact results due to its chiral phase transition similar to QCD. The paper contrasts with lattice QCD calculations of screening masses (Mscreen, Latbs) and addresses the subtlety that Euclidean Ward identities do not automatically apply to on-shell real-time quantities, as assumed by Shen (1994). The results enable a clearer understanding of how to interpret Euclidean correlation functions from lattice simulations in terms of real-time physics, and demonstrate the failure of dimensional reduction in this model at high temperature.
