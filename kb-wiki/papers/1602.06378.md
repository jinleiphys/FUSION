# Understanding the negative binomial multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> By deriving a general expression for multiplicity distribution (a conditional probability distribution) in statistical model, we demonstrate the mismatches between experimental measurements and previous theoretical calculations on multiplicity fluctuations. From the corrected formula, we develop an improved baseline measure for multiplicity distribution under Poisson approximation in statistical model to replace the traditional Poisson expectations. We find that the ratio of the mean multiplicity to the corresponding reference multiplicity are crucial to systemically explaining the measured scale variances of total charge distributions in different experiments, as well as understanding the centrality resolution effect observed in experiment. The improved statistical expectations, albeit simple, work well in describing the negative binomial multiplicity distribution measured in experiments, e.g. the cumulants (cumulant products) of total (net) electric charge distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that previous theoretical calculations of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions are incomplete because they use the unconditional probability distribution \( \mathscr{P}_B(q) \) rather than the correct conditional probability distribution \( \mathscr{P}_{B|A}(q|k) \), which accounts for the correlation between the reference multiplicity (sub-event A, used for centrality definition) and the moment-analysis particles (sub-event B). The corrected formula shows that even under Poisson approximation in a specific statistical ensemble, the measured multiplicity distribution mimics a negative binomial distribution rather than a Poisson distribution.

## Method

The author derives a general expression for the multiplicity distribution as a conditional probability \( \mathscr{P}_{B|A}(q|k) = \int d\mathbf{\Omega} F(\mathbf{\Omega}) P_B(q;\mathbf{\Omega}) P_A(k;\mathbf{\Omega}) / \mathscr{P}_A(k) \), where \( F(\mathbf{\Omega}) \) is the distribution of principal thermodynamic variables (PTVs, e.g., volume) and \( P_A \) and \( P_B \) are the distributions in a specific statistical ensemble. Under the Poisson approximation for both sub-events, the author calculates the cumulants of the conditional distribution and develops an improved baseline measure using the experimentally measured reference multiplicity distribution \( \mathscr{P}_A(k) \) and mean multiplicity distribution \( \mathscr{M}(k) \). Approximate analytic solutions are obtained by assuming \( \mu = b\lambda \) (linear relation between Poisson parameters) and \( \mathscr{P}_A(k+m)/\mathscr{P}_A(k) \simeq 1 \) for moderate \( m \).

## Key numbers

- The scale variance of the reference multiplicity distribution is \( \omega_A = 1 + \frac{\int d\lambda f(\lambda)(\lambda - M_A)^2}{M_A} \), which is always greater than 1 except for the delta-function case \( f(\lambda) = \delta(M) \).
- The scale variance of the conditional multiplicity distribution is \( \omega_B = 1 + \frac{\langle (\mu - \langle\mu\rangle)^2 \rangle}{\langle\mu\rangle} \geq 1 \).
- The approximate solution for the variance of total charge multiplicity is \( c_2 = M^2/(k+1) + M \), where \( M \equiv \mathscr{M}(k) \) is the mean multiplicity and \( k \) is the reference multiplicity.
- The approximate NBD parameters are \( r = k+1 \) and \( p = M/(M+k+1) \).
- For net-conserved charges, the approximate cumulant products are \( S\sigma = 2\beta(1-\alpha) + \frac{\beta(1-\alpha^2)+1-\alpha}{\beta(1-\alpha)^2+1+\alpha} \) and \( \kappa\sigma^2 = 6\beta(\gamma - 2\alpha/\gamma) + 1 \), where \( \alpha = M_-/M_+ \), \( \beta = M_+/(k+1) \), and \( \gamma = \beta(1-\alpha)^2 + 1 + \alpha \).
- For net-charge distributions where \( (M_+ + M_-) \simeq k \gg (M_+ - M_-) \), the approximate solutions give \( S\sigma \simeq 4(1-\alpha)/(1+\alpha) \) and \( \kappa\sigma^2 \simeq 4 \), which are about four times the Skellam expectations.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to multiplicity fluctuations by correcting the omission of conditional probability from reference multiplicity in previous studies by Cleymans (2004), Begun (2004, 2006), Garg (2013), Karsch (2010), Alba (2014), Fu (2013), Bhattacharyya (2015), Braun-Munzinger (2011), Bzdak (2012), and Gorenstein (2008, 2011, 2013). It also incorporates the volume fluctuation framework of Skokov (2012). The improved baseline measure enables systematic explanation of the negative binomial multiplicity distributions observed by the NA49 Collaboration (2006), PHENIX Collaboration (2008), and STAR Collaboration (2014), including the centrality resolution effect reported by Luo (2013) and the differences between net-charge and net-kaon cumulants reported by STAR (2014).
