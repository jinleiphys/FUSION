# Collision Broadening of the Phi Meson in Baryon Rich Hadronic Matter

> Phi meson-baryon cross sections, estimated within a one-boson-exchange model, serve as input for a calculation of the collision rates in hot hadronic matter. We find that the width of the ϕmeson is modified through collisions with baryons by 1-10 MeV at 160 MeV temperature depending on the baryon fugacity. Thermalization of the ϕin high energy heavy ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The phi meson's width is broadened by collisions with baryons by 1-10 MeV at 160 MeV temperature depending on the baryon fugacity, but this result assumes the bare phi meson mass throughout and does not consider mass dropping effects from chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors used a one-boson-exchange model with effective Lagrangians to compute tree-level cross sections for seven phi meson-baryon reactions, including phi N to K Lambda, phi N to pi N, and phi Delta to rho N. These cross sections were then thermally averaged over Boltzmann distributions and combined with particle densities to calculate collision rates and mean free paths in a fireball model of hot hadronic matter at temperatures typical of AGS and SPS heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Free space decay width of the phi meson: 4.43 MeV, corresponding to a lifetime of 45 fm/c
- Collision broadening from phi + baryon reactions alone: approximately 10 MeV at 170 MeV temperature and 400 MeV baryon chemical potential
- Total collision broadening from phi + meson and phi + baryon reactions combined: approximately 24 MeV at 170 MeV temperature and 400 MeV baryon chemical potential
- Mean free path of the phi meson: approximately 5 fm at 170 MeV temperature and 400 MeV baryon chemical potential; approximately 7 fm at the same temperature with zero chemical potential
- The reaction phi N to K Lambda with kaon exchange dominates the cross section below threshold energy plus 1.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson in-medium properties that focused on mass modifications from chiral symmetry restoration (Brown and Rho, 1996; Kuwabara and Hatsuda, 1995; Song, 1996; Klingl, Waas, and Weise, 1997) and kaon mass dropping effects (Bhattacharyya et al., 1997; Shuryak and Thorsson, 1992; Lissauer and Shuryak, 1991). Unlike those earlier works, this paper calculates collision broadening rather than decay width modifications. The results complement a similar collision broadening study for the rho meson by Haglin (1996) and build on previous calculations of phi + meson reactions by Haglin (1995). The findings suggest that the phi meson can kinetically thermalize in heavy ion collisions, enabling its use as an in-medium probe in next-generation collider experiments with higher luminosities.
