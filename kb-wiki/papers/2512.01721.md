# Electron-induced single-pion production to constrain the neutron structure in $^{40}$Ar. A proof of concept

> We study electron-induced single-pion production as a way to constrain the neutron structure of $^{40}$Ar, information that is necessary for neutrino experiments using argon detectors. The proposed experimental signal consists in detecting in coincidence the scattered electron, a proton and a $π^-$. We performed simulations compatible with the experimental conditions of the MAMI (University of Mainz) and CLAS (Jefferson Lab) facilities. We have computed cross sections and evaluated the main backgrounds. MAMI is a three-spectrometer system with extremely good energy resolution and small acceptances. We found that, by choosing specific values of the final particle's momenta, the shell structure can be well resolved, with negligible background contributions. CLAS is a large solid angle detector with poorer energy resolution. In both cases, the background can be kept under control by performing cuts in missing energy and missing momentum; however, in CLAS, the shell structure cannot be resolved due to the energy resolution. We conclude that MAMI is particularly appropriate for the proposed experiment, whereas CLAS is better suited for other studies.

## Key claim

Electron-induced triple coincidence ($e,e'p\pi^-$) on $^{40}$Ar can directly probe the neutron spectral function, avoiding the need for a proxy nucleus like $^{48}$Ti, but only if the experiment is performed at a facility with sufficiently good energy resolution (such as MAMI) and with careful kinematic cuts; at CLAS, the shell structure cannot be resolved due to poorer energy resolution.

## Method

The authors simulated the $^{40}$Ar$(e,e'p\pi^-)$ reaction using the Ghent-Hybrid model for single-pion production (based on the Delta resonance, higher resonances, and a chiral perturbation theory background) combined with a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation nuclear model using an independent-particle shell model with relativistic mean-field binding energies. Hadronic final-state interactions (rescattering, pion absorption, charge exchange) were modeled using the NuWro intranuclear cascade. The signal and backgrounds from all single-pion production channels and quasielastic scattering were evaluated for two detector configurations: the three-spectrometer system at MAMI (Mainz) and the large-acceptance CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

* MAMI spectrometer momentum resolution: $\leq 10^{-4}$; angular resolution at target: $\leq 3$ mrad (Table I).
* Beam energy used in MAMI simulation: $E_i = 855$ MeV; selected final electron energy: $E_l = 505$ MeV $\pm 7.5\%$; electron scattering angle: $\theta_l = 30^\circ \pm 1.15^\circ$.
* After cuts in missing energy ($E_m < 70$ MeV) and missing momentum ($p_m < 350$ MeV), unscattered $p\pi^-$ events contribute more than 75% of the full $1p1\pi^-$ cross section around the Delta peak (right panel of Fig. 4).
* The most external neutron shell in $^{40}$Ar has a binding energy of 9.87 MeV (from nuclear mass data).
* Quasielastic scattering contributes less than 1% to the $1p1\pi^-$ sample even before $E_m$ and $p_m$ cuts.

## Context

This work extends the approach of the E12-14-012 experiment at Jefferson Lab (Gu 2021, Jiang 2022), which measured the proton spectral function of $^{40}$Ar via $(e,e'p)$ and used $^{48}$Ti as a proxy for the neutron spectral function (Jiang 2023). The proposed $(e,e'p\pi^-)$ reaction directly probes neutron knockout without neutron detection, similar to the $^{12}$C$(\gamma,\pi^- n)$ study by MacKenzie (1996) and the $^{12}$C$(e,e'p\pi^-)$ measurement at MAMI by Blomqvist (1998). If realized, this experiment would provide the first electron-induced exclusive incoherent pion-production data on argon, enabling improved modeling of neutrino-argon interactions for experiments such as DUNE and MicroBooNE.
