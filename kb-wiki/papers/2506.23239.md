# Half-life of $^{136}$Xe for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay calculated with effective axial-vector current coupling unified for two-neurtino and neutrinoless double-$β$ decay modes

> The upper limit on the mass of the Majorana neutrino, extracted from the limits on the nonobservation of the neutrinoless double-$β$ ($0νββ$) decay, is hampered by uncertainties in the matrix elements of the transition operators. Recently, we have shown that the values of the effective axial-vector current coupling constants ($g_A^\textrm{eff}$) for the $0νββ$ and the two-neutrino double-$β$ decays are close. This striking result was obtained for the first time by including vertex corrections and two-body currents in these matrix elements. In this letter, we calculate the half-life for the $0νββ$ decay ($T_{1/2}^{0ν}$) of $^{136}$Xe using this closeness and show the convergence of the half-life with respect to the variation of the method to determine $g_A^\textrm{eff}$. The closeness of the $g_A^\textrm{eff}$ of the two decay modes plays a decisive role in predicting $T_{1/2}^{0ν}$. The appropriate value of $g_A^\textrm{eff}$ depends on the assumptions made for the sectors of the nuclear structure and transition operators of the calculations within the perturbation scheme. The value $g_A^\textrm{eff}\approx 1$ is obtained when the SkM$^\ast$ is used to describe the nuclear structure component, while a smaller value of $g_A^\textrm{eff}$ is obtained by applying a less realistic interaction like the SGII one.

## Key claim

The effective axial-vector coupling constants ($g_A^\mathrm{eff}$) for the neutrinoless and two-neutrino double-beta decay modes of $^{136}$Xe are close to each other when vertex corrections and two-body currents are included in the nuclear matrix elements, but the appropriate value of $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$ depends on the assumptions made for the nuclear structure and transition operators within the perturbation scheme.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for both the $0\nu\beta\beta$ and $2\nu\beta\beta$ decays of $^{136}$Xe using the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme interactions SkM$^\ast$ and SGII, including the Coulomb interaction and contact-like pairing interactions. The transition operator was perturbed in lowest order by the nucleon-nucleon potential, incorporating vertex corrections and two-body current terms. The effective $g_A$ values were determined by matching calculated half-lives to the experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ half-life and to the half-life obtained with the perturbed NMEs and bare $g_A$, then used to predict the $0\nu\beta\beta$ half-life.

## Key numbers

- Experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ half-life of $^{136}$Xe: $T_{1/2}^{2\nu(\mathrm{exp})} = 2.18 \times 10^{21}$ y (Bar19)
- $g_{A,0\nu}^\mathrm{eff}$(ld;pt) = 0.796 (SkM$^\ast$) and 0.454 (SGII); $g_{A,2\nu}^\mathrm{eff}$(ld;pt) = 0.696 (SkM$^\ast$) and 0.847 (SGII)
- $g_{A,2\nu}^\mathrm{eff}$(pt;exp) = 0.806 (SkM$^\ast$) and 0.833 (SGII)
- Most reliable $0\nu\beta\beta$ half-life prediction for $^{136}$Xe with assumed $\langle m_\nu\rangle$ = 1 meV: (1.3$-$3.0) $\times 10^{31}$ y (SkM$^\ast$)
- Leading-order $0\nu\beta\beta$ GT NME (SkM$^\ast$): 3.095; perturbed sum GT NME (SkM$^\ast$): 1.696
- Leading-order $0\nu\beta\beta$ GT NME (SGII): 2.947; perturbed sum GT NME (SGII): 0.906

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Terasaki and Civitarese, 2025) that first showed the closeness of $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$ for the two double-beta decay modes by including vertex corrections and two-body currents. The result supports the notion of a universal $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$ near unity, consistent with findings by Rho (2023, 2025) from a nuclear matter context and with shell-model calculations for light-mass nuclei (Suhonen, 2017). The predicted half-life is significantly longer than the range (3.0$-$30.0)$\times 10^{29}$ y from the compilation by Engel and Menendez (2017), but overlaps with more recent calculations by Coraggio et al. (2020), Brase et al. (2022), and Jokiniemi et al. (2023). This work enables more reliable extraction of the Majorana neutrino mass from future $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay experiments.
