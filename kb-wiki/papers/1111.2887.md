# Dark Atoms of Dark Matter and their Stable Charged Constituents

> Direct searches for dark matter lead to serious problems for simple models with stable neutral Weakly Interacting Massive Particles (WIMPs) as candidates for dark matter. A possibility is discussed that new stable quarks and charged leptons exist and are hidden from detection, being bound in neutral dark atoms of composite dark matter. Stable -2 charged particles $O^{--}$ are bound with primordial helium in O-helium (OHe) atoms, being specific nuclear interacting form of composite dark matter. The positive results of DAMA experiments can be explained as annual modulation of radiative capture of O-helium by nuclei. In the framework of this approach test of DAMA results in detectors with other chemical content becomes a nontrivial task, while the experimental search of stable charged particles at LHC or in cosmic rays acquires a meaning of direct test for composite dark matter scenario.

## Key claim
The positive results of DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA dark matter experiments can be explained by annual modulation of radiative capture of O-helium (OHe) atoms by sodium nuclei, but only within a specific range of nuclear physics parameters that produce a binding energy of 2–4 keV; the explanation is presented as an illustration rather than a definitive solution due to high sensitivity to nuclear parameter values.

## Method
The paper models OHe as a neutral atom composed of a stable doubly-charged particle O−− bound to primordial helium-4, which interacts with nuclei in underground detectors. The interaction potential between OHe and a nucleus is approximated by a rectangular well, and the Schrödinger equation is solved to find conditions for low-energy bound states. The radiative capture rate is calculated using an analogy with neutron-proton capture, accounting for isospin nonconservation via a factor f = (m_n − m_p)/m_N ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³, and applied specifically to sodium nuclei in the DAMA detector.

## Key numbers
- Bohr radius of OHe atom: R_o ≈ 2 × 10⁻¹³ cm, nearly equal to the radius of a helium nucleus.
- Decoupling temperature of OHe gas from plasma: T_od ≈ 200 S³/³ eV.
- O-helium mass: m_o = S₃ TeV (where S₃ is a dimensionless parameter).
- Drift velocity of OHe near Earth’s surface: V ∼ 400 S₃ cm/s.
- Drift timescale at depth L ∼ 10⁵ cm: t_dr ≈ 2.5 × 10² S₃⁻¹ s.
- Isospin nonconservation factor for E1 transition: f = (m_n − m_p)/m_N ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³.
- Binding energy of Na-OHe system required to explain DAMA signal: E_Na = 3 keV, within the range 2–4 keV.
- Annual modulation signal observed in DAMA experiments is in the energy range 2–6 keV, with no modulation above 6 keV.

## Context
This work extends the composite dark matter scenario proposed by Khlopov and collaborators (2007, 2008), where stable doubly-charged particles O−− bind with primordial helium to form OHe atoms. It directly addresses the long-standing tension between positive DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA results (Bernabei 2003, 2008) and null results from other experiments such as XENON100, CDMS, and CoGeNT. The paper enables future tests by predicting that detectors with different chemical content (e.g., xenon or germanium) should show ionization signals predominantly outside the 2–6 keV range, and that searches for stable doubly-charged particles at the LHC or in cosmic rays can directly probe the composite dark matter scenario.
