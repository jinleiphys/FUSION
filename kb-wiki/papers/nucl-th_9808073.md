# Nonlinear Enhancement of the Multiphonon Coulomb Excitation in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We propose a soluble model to incorporate the nonlinear effects in the transition probabilities of the multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances based on the SU(1,1) algebra. Analytical expressions for the multi-phonon transition probabilities are derived. Enhancement of the Double Giant Resonance excitation probabilities in relativistic ion collisions scales as $(2 k +1)(2k)^{-1}$ for the degree of nonlinearity $(2k)^{-1}$ and is able to reach values $1.5-2$ compatible with experimental data. The enhancement factor is found to decrease with increasing bombarding energy. [KEYWORDS: Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions,Double Giant Resonance]

# Nonlinear Enhancement of the Multiphonon Coulomb Excitation in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nonlinear effects in the transition operators for multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances, parameterized by a single parameter $k$, can explain the observed enhancement of Double Giant Dipole Resonance excitation cross sections by factors of 1.3–2 relative to harmonic phonon predictions, with the enhancement factor decreasing as bombarding energy increases. The model is presented as a soluble phenomenological framework rather than a microscopic derivation, and the authors explicitly postpone detailed numerical comparison with data to future publications.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical coupled-channels model where the relative motion of colliding nuclei follows a straight-line classical trajectory, while the intrinsic excitation of Giant Dipole Resonances is treated quantum mechanically. They generalize the harmonic phonon transition operators $E1_m^\dagger = d_m^\dagger$ to nonlinear operators $E1_m^\dagger = d_m^\dagger \sqrt{1 + N_d/(2k)}$, where $k$ controls the nonlinearity strength. Using the SU(1,1) algebra satisfied by these generalized operators, they derive analytical expressions for multiphonon excitation probabilities by solving a Riccati-type differential equation for a complex amplitude $\alpha(t)$, obtaining results that reduce to the standard Poisson distribution in the harmonic limit $k \rightarrow \infty$.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement of Double Giant Resonance excitation probabilities scales as $(2k+1)(2k)^{-1}$ for nonlinearity parameter $(2k)^{-1}$, reaching values 1.5–2 compatible with experimental data.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the experimental enhancement factor $r_2 \simeq 1.33$ corresponds to nonlinearity parameter $k \simeq 1.5$ (with numerical factor $\psi \simeq 0.88$ giving $1/k \simeq 1$).
- The enhancement factor $r_2$ drops from 2–2.5 at bombarding energies $\varepsilon \sim 100$ MeV per nucleon to 1.2–1.3 at $\varepsilon \sim 640$–700 MeV per nucleon, for fixed $k = 1.3$ in the $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb system.
- The double GDR energy systematics show $E_2 \simeq (1.75-2)\omega$, supporting weak anharmonicity in the spectrum.

## Context

This work addresses the persisting discrepancy between harmonic phonon predictions and experimental data for double GDR excitation cross sections observed in multiple experiments (Schmidt et al., Ritman et al., and the Au-target experiment). It extends prior anharmonic studies by Chomaz and Frattini, and by Volpe et al., which focused on spectral anharmonicity, while also connecting to the "hot phonon" Brink-Axel mechanism proposed by Carlson et al. The model enables analytical treatment of nonlinear transition probabilities while preserving the solvability and unitarity of the harmonic phonon model, and suggests that the enhancement factor's insensitivity to projectile properties (as observed in $^{208}$Pb experiments with various targets) follows naturally from the nonlinearity being an intrinsic property of the excited nucleus.
