# Bayesian uncertainty quantification of perturbative QCD input to the neutron-star equation of state

> The equation of state of neutron-star cores can be constrained by requiring a consistent connection to the perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) calculations at high densities. The constraining power of the QCD input depends on uncertainties from missing higher-order terms, the choice of the unphysical renormalization scale, and the reference density where QCD calculations are performed. Within a Bayesian approach, we discuss the convergence of the perturbative QCD series, quantify its uncertainties at high densities, and present a framework to systematically propagate the uncertainties down to neutron-star densities. We find that the effect of the QCD input on the neutron-star inference is insensitive to the various unphysical choices made in the uncertainty estimation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the effect of perturbative QCD input on neutron-star equation-of-state inference is insensitive to the various unphysical choices made in uncertainty estimation (renormalization scale, missing-higher-order model, and matching chemical potential), but this conclusion depends on the specific Bayesian framework and approximations used to propagate uncertainties from high densities down to neutron-star densities.

## Method

The authors use the Bayesian machine-learning framework MiHO to estimate missing-higher-order uncertainties of the perturbative QCD series for the pressure and number density at high baryon chemical potentials, employing both geometric and abc models for the perturbative coefficients. They then apply robust equation-of-state constraints based on mechanical stability, causality, and thermodynamic consistency to propagate the high-density probability distributions down to neutron-star densities, marginalizing over both the unphysical renormalization scale parameter X and the matching chemical potential μ_H.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative QCD calculations are performed at chemical potentials μ = {2.2, 2.6, 3.0} GeV, corresponding roughly to densities n ≈ {23, 40, 63} n_s, where n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3} is the nuclear saturation density.
- The central renormalization scale is set to X = 1, corresponding to Λ̅ = 2μ_q = (2/3)μ, with conventional scale variation by a factor of two giving X ∈ [1/2, 2].
- The non-perturbative scale Λ_QCD = 378 MeV is used, which fixes α_s(2 GeV) = 0.2994.
- The 68% credible intervals from the scale-marginalization prescription lie at the upper edge of the naive scale-variation estimate, while 95% credible intervals are larger than scale variation, especially for large μ values.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used a fixed chemical potential for applying perturbative QCD results to neutron-star constraints (Komoltsev and Kurkela 2021; Gorda et al. 2022) by introducing marginalization over the matching chemical potential. It builds on Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods developed for high-energy physics (Cacciari 2011; Forte 2013; Bonvini 2020; Duhr 2021) and applies them to the neutron-star equation of state, complementing similar statistical uncertainty quantification efforts in the low-density nuclear regime (Furnstahl 2014; Drischler 2017; Melendez 2019). The framework enables statistically interpretable error estimates for perturbative QCD input in neutron-star inference, which can be combined with astrophysical observations to constrain the equation of state.
