# Quarkonium radiative decays from the hadronic Paschen-Back effect

> We study the radiative (E1 and M1) decays of P-wave quarkonia in a strong magnetic field based on the Lagrangian of potential nonrelativistic QCD. To investigate their properties, we implement a polarized wave function basis justified in the Paschen-Back limit. In a magnetic field stronger than the spin-orbit coupling, the wave functions of the P-wave quarkonia are drastically deformed by the Hadronic Paschen-Back effect. Such deformation leads to the anisotropy of the direction of decays from the P-wave quarkonia. The analytic formulas for the radiative decay widths in the nonrelativistic limit are shown, and the qualitative decay properties are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a magnetic field stronger than the spin-orbit coupling (the Paschen-Back regime), the wave functions of P-wave quarkonia are drastically deformed by the Hadronic Paschen-Back effect, leading to anisotropic radiative decay widths; however, the analytic formulas derived are justified only in the region where the magnetic field is weaker than the scale for other magnetic effects such as Landau level deformation, i.e., \(V_{LS} \ll \sqrt{|eB|} \ll V_{other}\).

## Method

The authors apply the potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) framework to study E1 and M1 radiative decays of P-wave quarkonia in a strong magnetic field. They construct polarized wave function bases justified in the Paschen-Back limit, where the good quantum numbers are \(J_z\), \(L_z\), and \(S_z\), and derive analytic formulas for the decay widths as functions of the emission angle \(\alpha\) relative to the magnetic field direction. The formalism separates the cases where only the HPBE deformation is considered (using spherical coordinates) and where more general deformation including quark Landau levels is included (using cylindrical coordinates).

## Key numbers

- The maxima of magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions reach \(|eB| \sim 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at SPS, \(0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at RHIC, and \(\sim 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at LHC.
- For E1 decays from the \(L_z=0\) basis into S-wave states, the decay width is proportional to \(\sin^2 \alpha\), with a factor of \(2/3\) for \(\alpha = \pi/2\) (perpendicular to the magnetic field).
- For E1 decays from the \(L_z=\pm1\) basis into S-wave states, the decay width is proportional to \((\cos^2 \alpha + 1)\), with a factor of \(2/3\) for \(\alpha = 0,\pi\) (parallel to the magnetic field) and a factor of \(1/3\) for \(\alpha = \pi/2\).
- The E1 decay factor for the \(J_z=0\) eigenstate \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[Y_{10}\chi_{00} \pm Y_{10}\chi_{10}]\) going to S-wave states is \(\frac{3}{4} \sin^2 \alpha\).
- The E1 decay factor for the \(J_z=+2\) eigenstate \(Y_{11}\chi_{11}\) going to \(J/\psi (S_z=+1)\) is \(\frac{3}{16}(\cos^2 \alpha + 1)\), giving \(\frac{3}{8}\) at \(\alpha=0,\pi\) and \(\frac{3}{16}\) at \(\alpha=\pi/2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the Hadronic Paschen-Back effect in P-wave charmonia (Iwasaki and Suzuki, 2018) by providing analytic formulas for radiative decay widths, which were previously only suggested as possible experimental observables. The study builds on the pNRQCD framework developed by Pineda (1997) and Brambilla (1999) for quarkonium decays, and is related to prior work on quarkonium polarization in vacuum by Faccioli (2011) and Shao (2012, 2014), which is distinguished by \(J_z\) rather than the \(L_z\) and \(S_z\) basis used here. The results enable future experimental searches for anisotropic decays of P-wave quarkonia as probes of strong magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC.
