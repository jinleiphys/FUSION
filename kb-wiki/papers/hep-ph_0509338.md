# Photon $+$ Hadron Production in High Energy Deuteron (Proton)-Nucleus Collisions

> We apply the Color Glass Condensate formalism to photon $+$ hadron production cross section in high energy deuteron (proton)-gold collisions at RHIC. We investigate the dependence of the production cross section on the angle between the produced hadron and photon for various rapidities and transverse momenta. It is shown that the angular correlation between the produced hadron and photon is a sensitive probe of the saturation dynamics.

## Key claim

The angular correlation between a produced hadron and photon in deuteron-nucleus collisions is a sensitive probe of gluon saturation dynamics, but the "away side" peak (near θ = π) is only suppressed when the transverse momenta are comparable to the saturation scale Q_s ~ 1.5 GeV; at higher transverse momenta (e.g., 5 GeV) the away side correlation returns, indicating weaker saturation effects.

## Method

The partonic cross section for quark + photon production in quark-nucleus scattering, derived in the Color Glass Condensate formalism by Gelis and Jalilian-Marian (2005), is convoluted with the GRV98 parton distribution functions for the deuteron and the KKP quark-hadron fragmentation functions. The dipole cross section N(b_t, r_t, x_g) is modeled using the KKT parameterization with anomalous dimension γ(y, r_t) and saturation scale Q_s(y) = Q_0 exp[λ(y - y_0)/2] with λ = 0.3 and y_0 = 0.6. The angular dependence I(θ) is isolated analytically for the case γ = 1/2 and computed numerically for the full model at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at RHIC: Q_s ~ 1.5 GeV for the target nucleus at relevant x values.
- The anomalous dimension parameterization uses λ = 0.3 and y_0 = 0.6 for the saturation scale evolution Q_s(y) = Q_0 exp[λ(y - y_0)/2].
- At mid rapidity with l_t = k_t = 1 GeV, the away side (θ → π) correlation is flat; at l_t = k_t = 3 GeV, the away side peak returns.
- For forward rapidity y_l = y_γ = 4, the kinematic limit restricts transverse momenta to ≤ 2 GeV; calculations are shown for l_t = k_t = 1 GeV and l_t = k_t = 1.7 GeV.
- The minimum momentum fraction z_min for the hadron fragmentation is given by z_min ≡ (q_t/√s)[1/(1 - k_t/√s)] e^{y_h - y_γ}.
- The values of x probed in the target wave function are O(10^{-3} - 10^{-4}).

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism applied to single hadron production in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, which was qualitatively predicted by Kharzeev, Kovchegov, and Tuchin (2004) and quantitatively analyzed by Albacete, Armesto, and Jalilian-Marian (2005). It contrasts with recombination models that do not predict suppression for prompt photons. The two-hadron correlation approach of Kovchegov, Levin, and McLerran (2002) is noted to be theoretically more challenging due to 3- and 4-point Wilson line functions, making the photon+hadron channel a cleaner alternative. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC (e.g., with STAR detector upgrades) and at LHC proton-lead runs, where larger rapidity coverage (5-7 units) and higher saturation scales will allow more detailed study of saturation dynamics.
