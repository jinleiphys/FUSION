# Exploring the Lattice Gas Model for isoscaling

## Key claim
The isotopic spin dependent lattice gas model (LGM) reproduces qualitative features of isoscaling seen in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, but quantitative agreement with experiments is lacking; the model systematically underestimates the isoscaling parameter α by about a factor of two compared to experimental data.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of the lattice gas model with nearest-neighbor bond strengths set to ε_np = -5.33 MeV (unlike particles) and ε_pp = ε_nn = 0 (like particles), including Coulomb energy between protons. They calculated average fragment multiplicities from 100,000 Monte-Carlo events using the Metropolis algorithm, for dissociating systems corresponding to ¹¹²Sn+¹¹²Sn (A=168, Z=75) and ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn (A=186, Z=75) central collisions, as well as ⁵⁸Ni and ⁶⁴Ni on ⁹Be (A₁=67, Z₁=32; A₂=73, Z₂=32). The symmetry energy coefficient c_s was extracted from ground state energies of isolated nuclei at zero temperature via a liquid-drop parametrization.

## Key numbers
- Bond strength between unlike particles ε_np = -5.33 MeV (to produce binding energy of 16 MeV per particle)
- For ¹¹²Sn+¹¹²Sn vs ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn: experimental α = 0.361; LGM with 8³ lattice at T=5 MeV gives α ≈ 0.20
- For ⁵⁸Ni vs ⁶⁴Ni on ⁹Be: experimental α ≈ 0.6; LGM calculated α ≈ 0.22
- Extracted symmetry energy coefficient from LGM ground states: c_s = 23.4 MeV (from least squares fit)
- Using eq.(2) with c_s=23.4 MeV would predict α = 0.86 for Sn systems, while LGM calculation gives α ≈ 0.22; using α=0.22 in eq.(2) would imply c_s ≈ 6 MeV
- For Ni on Be, experimental Y₂/Y₁ data available for Z from 1 to 28

## Context
This work extends prior investigations of isoscaling in the lattice gas model, differing in perspective from Su (2008). The authors compare with experimental data from Tsang (2002) for Sn+Sn collisions and Mocko (2006) for Ni+Be collisions. The canonical model (Chaudhuri, 2008) successfully fits the same data, while the grand canonical model cannot explain the Z-dependent variation of α. The LGM reproduces the increase of α from low-Z to high-Z isotopes seen in experiments, a feature the grand canonical model cannot explain. The results suggest that the standard relation between α and symmetry energy (eq.2) may not be valid for the lattice gas model, as the extracted c_s from isolated nuclei (23.4 MeV) would predict α values far larger than those actually computed.
