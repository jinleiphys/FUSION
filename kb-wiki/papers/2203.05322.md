# Semi-empirical model to determine pre- and post-neutron fission product yields and neutron multiplicity

> Post-neutron emission fission product mass distributions are calculated by using pre-neutron emission fission product yields (FPYs) and neutron multiplicity. A semi-empirical model is used to calculate the pre-neutron FPY, first. Then the neutron multiplicity for each fission fragment mass is used to convert the pre-neutron FPY to the post-neutron FPY. In doing so, assumptions are made for the probability for a pre-emission fission fragment with a mass number $A^*$ to decay to a post-emission fragment with a mass number $A$. The resulting post-neutron FPYs are compared with the data available. The systems where the experimental data of not only the pre- and post-neutron FPY but also neutron multiplicity are available are the thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{233}$U, $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu. Thus, we applied the model calculations to these systems and compared the calculation results with those from the GEF and the data from the ENDF and the EXFOR libraries. Both the pre- and post-neutron fission product mass distributions calculated by using the semi-empirical model and the neutron multiplicity reproduce the overall features of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present a semi-empirical model that can consistently reproduce both pre-neutron and post-neutron fission product yields for thermal neutron-induced fission of 233U, 235U, and 239Pu, but only for these three systems where experimental data for both yield types and neutron multiplicity per fragment mass are simultaneously available.

## Method

The authors first fit six adjustable parameters of a semi-empirical fission model (based on Bohr-Wheeler theory with a macroscopic-microscopic fission barrier) to reproduce experimental pre-neutron emission fission product yields from the 2E-1V and 2E-2V methods. They then convert pre-neutron yields to post-neutron yields using experimental neutron multiplicity data for each fission fragment mass, applying three different assumptions for the probability distribution of the number of emitted neutrons: a 2-point distribution, an averaged Poisson distribution, and an optimized Poisson distribution where the Poisson parameter is varied to minimize a combined chi-squared of yields and multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- For 233U(nth,f): 87 pre-neutron data points used; model gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.055 vs GEF gives 0.205
- For 235U(nth,f): 83 pre-neutron data points used; model gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.049 vs GEF gives 0.134
- For 239Pu(nth,f): 95 pre-neutron data points used; model gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.006 vs GEF gives 0.073
- Average ⟨ΔY²⟩ for pre-neutron FPY: model 0.037 vs GEF 0.137
- For post-neutron FPY of 233U(nth,f) (85 data points): optimized Poisson gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.0337 and χ²Y = 1.02; GEF gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.1217 and χ²Y = 1.17
- For post-neutron FPY of 235U(nth,f) (86 data points): optimized Poisson gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.0831 and χ²Y = 5.52; GEF gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.0361 and χ²Y = 4.09
- For post-neutron FPY of 239Pu(nth,f) (88 data points): optimized Poisson gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.0562 and χ²Y = 1.24; GEF gives ⟨ΔY²⟩ = 0.0335 and χ²Y = 0.40
- Model parameters include: V0 = 5.0 MeV, γ = 0.06 MeV−1, Nin = 82, Nout = 91.36 for uranium and 93.02 for plutonium

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-empirical model (Lee 2018, Lee 2020) which was applied directly to post-neutron FPY data using only the average neutron multiplicity per compound nucleus; here the model is instead fitted to pre-neutron FPY data and then converted using fragment-specific neutron multiplicities. The approach is contrasted with the GEF model (Schmidt 2016), which uses about 50 parameters and includes even-odd effects, whereas the present model uses only six adjustable parameters. The work enables a consistent description of both pre- and post-neutron yields for the three systems where complete data exist, and the authors note that more experimental FPY and neutron multiplicity data are needed to extend the approach to other fissioning systems.
