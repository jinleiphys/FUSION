# Single-particle properties of the near-threshold proton-emitting resonance in $^{11}$B

> The excitation function of proton elastic scattering from $^{10}$Be at keV energy is calculated using the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock in the continuum method. The calculation successfully reproduces the narrow near-threshold proton-emitting resonance ($E_x = 11.4$ MeV, $Γ= 6$ keV, and quantum number $J^π = 1/2^+$) in $^{11}$B relevant to the $β$-delayed proton emission of $^{11}$Be. This supports the recent experimental result of Y. Ayyad \textit{et al.} at the ReA3 re-accelerator facility of the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL) at the Michigan State University. The resonance is interpreted as the $s_{1/2}$ single-proton resonance state in the Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field theory.

## Key claim

The narrow near-threshold proton-emitting resonance in \(^{11}\)B (excitation energy 11.4 MeV, width 6 keV, \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\)) is successfully reproduced by self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock in the continuum calculations, but only after applying a slight scaling factor \(\lambda\) (between 0.984 and 1.028) to the nuclear central part of the optical potential to correct the resonance position to 182 keV; without this adjustment, some Skyrme interactions (SLy4, SGII) do not show the resonance in the energy region at all.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations for the ground state of \(^{10}\)Be using four Skyrme interactions (SkM\(^*\), SGII, SLy4, SAMi) and constructed a local equivalent optical potential following the method of Dover (1971). They solved the partial scattering equations for the \(^{10}\)Be(\(p,p\)) reaction at keV energies using the ECIS06 code, extracting the \(s\)-wave phase shift and resonance width from the energy derivative of the phase shift. The nuclear central part of the Skyrme HF optical potential was slightly scaled by a factor \(\lambda\) close to unity to match the experimentally observed resonance position at 182 keV.

## Key numbers

- Resonance in \(^{11}\)B: excitation energy \(E_x = 11.4\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 6\) keV, spin-parity \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\)
- Experimentally observed resonance energy: 182 keV above the proton separation energy (from Ayyad et al., 2022)
- Calculated resonance widths for different Skyrme interactions: SkM\(^*\) gives 5.43 keV, SGII gives 5.97 keV, SLy4 gives 6.09 keV, SAMi gives 5.99 keV
- Scaling factors \(\lambda\) used: SkM\(^*\) = 1.004, SGII = 1.028, SLy4 = 0.984, SAMi = 0.998
- Without adjustment, SAMi interaction gives resonance energy \(E_r = 166\) keV and width \(\Gamma = 4.05\) keV

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme Hartree-Fock in the continuum method, previously applied to keV-nucleon radiative capture reactions in nuclear astrophysics by Anh et al. (2021), to the problem of near-threshold proton-emitting resonances. The calculation supports the direct experimental observation of \(\beta\)-delayed proton decay in \(^{11}\)Be by Ayyad et al. (2019) and the dedicated \(^{10}\)Be(\(p,p\)) experiment by Ayyad et al. (2022), and provides a theoretical interpretation of the resonance as an \(s_{1/2}\) single-proton state. The results enable further studies of the proposed neutron dark decay mode in \(^{11}\)Be, as discussed by Fornal (2018) and Pfutzner (2018), and are relevant to planned FRIB experiments on cluster decay of \(^{11}\)Be into \(^{7}\)Li and an alpha particle.
