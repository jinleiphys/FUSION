# Pion Polarizabilities

> Recent experimental pion polarizability data are reviewed and analyzed. Data on the radiative π^+ meson photoproduction on the proton at MAMI has been used to determine the difference of the electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the charged pion. Fits of the experimental data to the total cross sections of the processes γγ->π^0π^0 and γγ->π^\pmπ^\pm in the energy region from threshold to 2250 MeV and 2500 MeV, respectively, have been carried out using dispersion relations with subtractions for the invariant helicity amplitudes, where the dipole and the quadrupole polarizabilities of π^0 and π^\pm are free parameters. These fits have allowed ones to find the sum and the difference of the electric and magnetic dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the pions. These data are compared with the dispersion sum rules and ChPT calculations. The value of the difference of the charged pion dipole polarizabilities found from the fit is in a good agreement with the results obtained at MAMI (2005), in Serpukhov (1983) and Lebedev Physical Institute (1984). However, all these values deviate from the recent calculations in the framework of ChPT.

# Pion Polarizabilities

## Key claim

The difference of the electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the charged pion, determined from fits to the total cross section of γγ→π⁺π⁻ and from radiative π⁺ photoproduction on the proton, agrees with earlier experimental results from Serpukhov (1983) and Lebedev Physical Institute (1984) but deviates substantially from chiral perturbation theory calculations, possibly because ChPT neglects the σ meson contribution and because two-loop calculations for the quadrupole polarizabilities may receive large corrections at higher orders.

## Method

The authors performed dispersion-relation fits with subtractions to the total cross sections of γγ→π⁰π⁰ (from threshold to 2250 MeV) and γγ→π⁺π⁻ (from threshold to 2500 MeV), treating the dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities as free parameters and saturating the dispersion relations with meson exchanges (ρ, ω, φ in the s-channel; σ, f₀, f₂ in the t-channel). For the charged pion, they also analyzed radiative π⁺ photoproduction on the proton at MAMI (537–817 MeV beam energy) using two different theoretical models, integrating over kinematic regions where the model dependence was below 3% and where the polarizability contribution was largest.

## Key numbers

- Difference of charged pion dipole polarizabilities from γγ→π⁺π⁻ fit: (α₁−β₁)π± = 13.0^{+2.6}_{-1.9} × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Difference of charged pion dipole polarizabilities from MAMI radiative photoproduction: (α₁−β₁)π± = 11.6 ± 1.5_stat ± 3.0_syst ± 0.5_mod × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Sum of charged pion dipole polarizabilities from γγ→π⁺π⁻ fit: (α₁+β₁)π± = 0.18^{+0.11}_{-0.02} × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Difference of π⁰ dipole polarizabilities from γγ→π⁰π⁰ fit: (α₁−β₁)π⁰ = −1.6 ± 2.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Sum of π⁰ dipole polarizabilities from γγ→π⁰π⁰ fit: (α₁+β₁)π⁰ = 0.98 ± 0.03 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Difference of charged pion quadrupole polarizabilities: (α₂−β₂)π± = 25.0^{+0.8}_{-0.3} × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
- Sum of charged pion quadrupole polarizabilities: (α₂+β₂)π± = 0.133 ± 0.015 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
- σ meson parameters from γγ→π⁰π⁰ fit: m_σ = 547 ± 45 MeV, Γ_σ = 1204 ± 362 MeV, Γ_σ→γγ = 0.62 ± 0.19 keV

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-relation analysis of Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005, 2006) to simultaneously determine dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities for both neutral and charged pions, and compares results with the MAMI (2005) radiative photoproduction experiment and with earlier Serpukhov (1983) and Lebedev Physical Institute (1984) measurements. The results contradict two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bellucci et al. (1996) and Ivanov et al. (2005), which predict a smaller difference of charged pion dipole polarizabilities (around 5–6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³) and a positive sum of π⁰ quadrupole polarizabilities; the authors suggest that higher-order ChPT contributions and the σ meson contribution may resolve the discrepancy. The analysis enables improved constraints on pion structure parameters and tests of low-energy QCD models.
