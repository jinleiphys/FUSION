# Understanding Chaos via Nuclei

> We use two models of nuclear collective dynamics - the geometric collective model and the interacting boson model - to illustrate principles of classical and quantum chaos. We propose these models as a suitable testing ground for further elaborations of the general theory of chaos in both classical and quantum domains.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nuclear collective models, the Geometric Collective Model (GCM) and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), serve as a rich testing ground for studying classical and quantum chaos, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis is mostly restricted to the non-rotational case (fixed angular momentum zero), reducing the system to two vibrational degrees of freedom and thereby enabling a direct link to other well-studied two-dimensional chaotic systems.

## Method

The authors analyze classical chaos by numerically integrating Hamilton's equations for the GCM and IBM Hamiltonians using fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration, constructing Poincaré sections to visualize the transition from integrability to chaos. Quantum chaos is studied by numerically diagonalizing the quantized GCM Hamiltonian (in three different quantization schemes: 2D-even, 2D-odd, and 5D) and the finite-dimensional IBM Hamiltonian, then computing spectral statistics such as the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution and the Brody parameter. The GCM Hamiltonian includes a kinetic term with deformation-dependent effective mass extensions and a potential with quadratic, cubic, and quartic terms in the deformation variable β, while the IBM Hamiltonian is built from s- and d-bosons with one- and two-body interactions.

## Key numbers

* The GCM Hamiltonian has an essential parameter τ = B²/(AC) that is invariant under scale transformations and represents the only essential parameter of the bare vibrational model in the classical case.
* The IBM Hamiltonian parameters include χ varying within the interval [−√7/2, 0) for prolate shapes and (0, +√7/2] for oblate shapes.
* The GCM quantum calculations used truncation dimensions of N = 39905 and 59080 for the Hamiltonian matrix in the 2D oscillator basis, with converged results only below a vertical dashed line corresponding to less than half the smaller dimension.
* The classical IBM coordinates and momenta are restricted to finite domains: β ∈ [0, √2], pβ ∈ [0, √2], and pγ ∈ [0, 1].

## Context

This work extends the classic Hénon-Heiles Hamiltonian (Hénon and Heiles, 1964) by including a confining β⁴ term in the GCM potential, which generates richer chaotic structures than the original C=0 case. The paper builds on prior studies of chaos in nuclear systems (Weidenmüller, 2009; Mitchell, 2010) and on the O(5) seniority integral of motion identified by Chacon et al. (1976). The results enable further exploration of the KAM theorem (Kolmogorov, 1954; Arnold, 1963; Moser, 1962) and the Poincaré-Birkhoff fixed-point theorem in the context of nuclear collective dynamics, and allow testing how different quantization schemes affect signatures of quantum chaos.
