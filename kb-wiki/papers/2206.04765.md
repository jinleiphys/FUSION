# On the Gravitational Wave to Matter Coupling of Superfluid Fermi Gases Near Unitarity

> It is well known that gravitational waves distort equilibrium matter globally, making them amenable to detection with laser interferometers. Less well known is the fact that gravitational waves create local non-equilibrium stresses inside matter, which could conceivably lead to alternative detection methods. The gravitational wave to matter coupling $κ$ is a transport coefficient depending on the material, and is poorly known for most substances. In the present work, we calculate $κ$ for a superfluid Fermi gas near unitarity using large-$N$ techniques, finding $κ= \frac{n}{12m}$, with $n$ the number density and $m$ the mass of the fermion, matching the result for free Dirac fermions at zero temperature. Our prediction is amenable to non-perturbative theoretical as well as experimental tests.

## Key claim

The gravitational-wave to matter coupling coefficient \(\kappa\) for a superfluid Fermi gas near unitarity is found to be \(\kappa = \frac{n}{12m}\) (with \(n\) the number density and \(m\) the fermion mass), matching the result for free Dirac fermions at zero temperature in the non-relativistic limit; this result is derived within the R0 (leading large-\(N\) or mean-field) approximation and is predicted to hold for the exact theory up to the value of the Bertsch parameter \(\xi\).

## Method

The authors calculate \(\kappa\) for a non-relativistic two-component Fermi gas with s-wave contact interactions by using the path-integral formulation and introducing a complex auxiliary field to resolve the four-fermion interaction. They employ the R0 approximation (leading large-\(N\) or mean-field approximation), which retains only the global zero mode of the auxiliary field, and solve the resulting gap equation at zero temperature to obtain the superfluid gap \(\Delta\). The transport coefficient \(\kappa\) is then extracted from the zero-frequency, small-momentum expansion of the Euclidean stress-tensor two-point correlator \(C_E(\omega_n=0,k)\) via \(\kappa = \left.\frac{\partial^2}{\partial k^2} C_E(\omega_n=0,k)\right|_{k=0}\), evaluating the momentum integrals analytically using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

* For the unitary Fermi gas (\(a_s \rightarrow -\infty\)), the superfluid gap in the R0 approximation is \(\Delta \simeq 1.1622 \mu\) (from the numerical solution of the gap equation) and \(\Delta/\mu = e^{-2+3\ln 2} \simeq 1.083\) from the analytic approximation.
* The Bertsch parameter in the R0 approximation is \(\xi_{R0} \simeq 0.59\), compared to the established value \(\xi \simeq 0.38\).
* The main result for the gravitational-wave to matter coupling near unitarity is \(\kappa = \frac{n}{12m}\) for spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) fermions.
* For a binary black hole system with two solar-mass black holes orbiting at \(R = 10 r_s\) (with \(r_s \simeq 3\) km), the estimated ratio of gravitational wave stress to thermodynamic pressure is \(\sigma \simeq 10^{-24}\).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the second-order transport coefficient \(\kappa\) (the gravitational-wave to matter coupling) from relativistic theories such as \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super-Yang-Mills (Baier 2007, Finazzo 2014, Grozdanov 2014, Grieninger 2021) and the large-\(N\) O(N) model (Romatschke 2019) to a non-relativistic superfluid Fermi gas near unitarity. The result matches the non-relativistic limit of the free Dirac fermion result (Shukla 2019), suggesting universality for spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) fermions in both the free and unitarity limits. The calculation provides a benchmark for non-perturbative methods such as Monte Carlo and density functional theory (Bulgac 2017, Richie-Halford 2020, Lu 2019) and opens the possibility of testing the result experimentally with ultracold Fermi gases, though the estimated signal-to-noise ratio for gravitational wave detection is currently unrealistic.
