# Thermalization at the femtoscale seen in high-energy Pb+Pb collisions

> A collision between two atomic nuclei accelerated at a speed close to that of light creates a dense system of quarks and gluons. Interactions among them are so strong that they behave collectively like a droplet of fluid of ten-femtometer size, which expands into the vacuum and eventually fragments into thousands of particles. We report a new manifestation of thermalization in recent data from the Large Hadron Collider. Our analysis is based on results from the ATLAS Collaboration, which has measured the variance of the momentum per particle across Pb+Pb collision events with the same particle multiplicity. This variance decreases steeply over a narrow multiplicity range corresponding to central collisions. We provide a simple explanation of this newly-observed phenomenon: For a given multiplicity, the momentum per particle increases with increasing impact parameter. Since a larger impact parameter goes along with a smaller collision volume, this in turn implies that the momentum per particle increases as a function of density, which is a generic consequence of thermalization. Our analysis provides the first direct evidence of this phenomenon at the femtoscale.

## Key claim

The steep decrease of the variance of transverse momentum per particle in ultracentral Pb+Pb collisions provides the first direct evidence of thermalization at the femtoscale, specifically because for a fixed multiplicity the momentum per particle increases with impact parameter (and thus with density), which is a generic consequence of thermalization.

## Method

The authors analyzed ATLAS data on the variance of transverse momentum per particle [p_t] as a function of charged particle multiplicity N_ch and transverse energy E_T in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair. They modeled the joint distribution of [p_t] and N_ch (or E_T) as a two-dimensional Gaussian at fixed impact parameter, using Bayesian reconstruction to extract the probability distribution of impact parameter at fixed multiplicity from the measured multiplicity distribution. The model separates the variance into two contributions: one from impact parameter fluctuations at fixed multiplicity and one from intrinsic fluctuations at fixed impact parameter and multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- The variance of [p_t] decreases by a factor of approximately 2 over a narrow interval of N_ch around 3700 charged particles
- The knee of the multiplicity distribution (mean N_ch for b=0 collisions) is reconstructed as 3683 ± 4 charged particles
- Only 0.35% of events lie above the knee for N_ch-based centrality, and 0.26% for E_T-based centrality
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between [p_t] and N_ch at fixed impact parameter is r_Nch = 0.676 from the fit to ATLAS data
- The standard deviation of [p_t] fluctuations for b=0 is σ_δpt = 9.357 MeV/c from ATLAS data
- The power-law exponent α describing the impact parameter dependence of Var(p_t|b) is approximately 1.2
- The quantum uncertainty in impact parameter is δb ≈ 4 × 10^(-7) fm, negligible compared to the range of b of order 15 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of nucleus-nucleus collisions, which previously explained collective flow phenomena such as elliptic flow (STAR 2000, ALICE 2010), to a new observable involving only particle momenta rather than their directions. The observed effect is not reproduced by models treating the collision as a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, such as HIJING (Wang 1991, Gyulassy 1994), where the variance decreases as 1/N_ch for all multiplicities. The analysis enables a new way to study thermalization in ultracentral collisions and predicts a small increase in average transverse momentum in these events, which has recently been observed by the CMS collaboration (Bernardes 2023).
