# Deformation parameter for diffuse density

> In extracting deformation parameters from multipole moments for deformed nuclei, one commonly uses the formulas which are based on a sharp-cut density distribution. We discuss a possible ambiguity for this procedure and clarify the role of diffuseness parameter of the density distribution. For this purpose, we use a deformed Woods-Saxon density as well as a density distribution obtained from the self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. We show that the formula using a root-mean-square radius instead of a sharp-cut radius requires a large correction due to a finite surface diffuseness parameter even for stable nuclei. An implication to neutron-rich nuclei is also discussed.

## Key claim

The commonly used formula that replaces the sharp-cut radius with a root-mean-square radius to extract the deformation parameter from the quadrupole moment significantly underestimates the deformation parameter, even for stable nuclei, unless a correction for the finite surface diffuseness parameter is included.

## Method

The authors used a deformed Woods-Saxon density distribution with a known input deformation parameter (β=0.3) to test how well three different formulas recover the correct value as a function of the diffuseness parameter a. They then applied the same formulas to self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for Mg isotopes using the NL3 parameter set and the RMFAXIAL code, extracting the diffuseness parameter by fitting the monopole density to a Woods-Saxon shape.

## Key numbers

- For a deformed Woods-Saxon density with β=0.3 and A=40, the root-mean-square formula (Eq. eq:rms) significantly underestimates the deformation parameter except when the diffuseness parameter a is close to zero.
- For stable nuclei with a ≈ 0.55 fm, the sharp-cut formula (Eq. eq:constR) with R0=1.2 A^(1/3) fm provides a reasonable value of the deformation parameter.
- The surface diffuseness correction formula (Eq. eq:diffu) yields Q = sqrt(16π/5) * (3/(4π)) * A * β * ( (5/3)⟨r^2⟩ - (4/3)π^2 a^2 ).
- The root-mean-square radius for a Woods-Saxon density is ⟨r^2⟩ ≈ (3/5) R0^2 * (1 + (7π^2/3) * (a^2/R0^2)).
- The quadrupole moment including diffuseness correction is Q ≈ sqrt(16π/5) * (3/(4π)) * A * R0^2 * β * (1 + π^2 * a^2/R0^2).

## Context

This work extends the standard sharp-cut formulas for extracting deformation parameters from quadrupole moments, as found in textbooks such as Ring and Schuck (1980) and Rowe and Wood (2010). It directly addresses the ambiguity noted in earlier work by Suda et al. (2003), Möller et al. (2000), and Yamagami et al. (2000), who proposed using the root-mean-square radius instead of the sharp-cut radius. The paper shows that the root-mean-square formula requires a large diffuseness correction, and that the sharp-cut formula with R0=1.2 A^(1/3) fm fortuitously works well for stable nuclei. The results enable more reliable extraction of deformation parameters for neutron-rich nuclei where the surface diffuseness is expected to be large, and the authors note that future work should extend the formula to include higher-order terms in β.
