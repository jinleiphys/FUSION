# PHENIX Overview

> These proceedings present highlighted results from PHENIX shown at the Quark Matter 2017 conference.

## Key claim

These proceedings present highlighted results from PHENIX shown at the Quark Matter 2017 conference, covering 8 collision systems and 5 collision energies, but the paper is a conference summary of selected results rather than a single self-contained study with a unified claim.

## Method

The paper summarizes selected PHENIX experimental results presented at the Quark Matter 2017 conference across four physics topics: collective dynamics in small collision systems (using two- and four-particle cumulants and event-plane methods), electromagnetic probes (direct photon production), high-transverse-momentum hadrons (neutral pion nuclear modification factors), and open heavy flavor (dielectron spectra, J/psi from B-hadron decays, and separated charm/bottom electron measurements). Results are compared to hydrodynamic models (SONIC, superSONIC), the AMPT event generator, and theoretical calculations from transport and energy loss models.

## Key numbers

- v2 in p/d/3He+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV shows ordering: v2^{3He+Au} ~ v2^{d+Au} > v2^{p+Au} ~ v2^{p+Al}
- v3 in 3He+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV is larger than v3 in d+Au at the same energy
- v2 measured in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200, 62.4, 39, and 19.6 GeV shows a clear signal rising with pT at all four energies
- Real four-particle cumulant (v2{4}) observed in d+Au collisions at all four energies (200, 62.4, 39, 19.6 GeV)
- Direct photon inverse slope T_eff measured in Cu+Cu at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 and 39 GeV, showing increasing trend with collision energy
- At pT~5 GeV/c in minimum bias p/d/3He+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, peaked enhancement observed in p+Au, smaller enhancement in d+Au, little enhancement in 3He+Au for neutral pions
- Fraction of J/psi from B-hadron decays (F_B->J/psi) measured in pp at sqrt(s_NN)=510 GeV for 1.2<|y|<2.2 and pT>0, and in pp and Cu+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- R_CuAu for J/psi from B-hadron decays in Cu+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV consistent with binary scaling, unlike prompt J/psi which are suppressed by more than a factor of three
- Electrons from charm hadron decays in 0-10% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV show large suppression roughly constant for pT>3 GeV/c
- Electrons from bottom hadron decays in 0-10% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV are less suppressed than charm for pT<5 GeV/c

## Context

This conference proceedings extends PHENIX's long-running program at RHIC investigating the strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma, building on prior flow measurements in small systems at both LHC (CMS 2012, ALICE 2012, ATLAS 2012) and RHIC (Adare 2013, 2014, 2015; Aidala 2016). The results on flow in small systems test hydrodynamic predictions from SONIC (Habich 2014) and AMPT (Lin 2004), while the heavy flavor measurements provide new baselines for understanding energy loss mechanisms. The paper enables future comparisons with the full statistics from the 2014 and 2016 Au+Au data samples, which should provide definitive measurements of heavy flavor energy loss using the VTX and FVTX silicon vertex detectors.
