# Inclusive Breakup Cross Sections in Reactions Induced by the Nuclides $^6$He and $^{6,7}$Li in the Two-Body Cluster Model

> Calculations of inclusive $α$ particle production are performed to interpret reaction experimental data induced by the two-neutron halo $^6$He and the isotopes $^{6,7}$Li on different target nuclei. We have implemented the zero-range post-form DWBA to compute the elastic and nonelastic breakup cross sections. Integrated cross sections, angular distributions and spectra are presented. Projectiles are approximated as two-body clusters, namely $^6$He as $α$+dineutron and $^{6}$Li ($^{7}$Li) interpreted as $α$+deuteron(triton). The São Paulo optical potential is employed in the calculation of the distorted wave functions for the incident channels, while standard phenomenological interactions are used for the fragment-target interactions. Calculations for $^6$Li and $^7$Li furnish a good description of the data, while the reaction involving $^6$He is found to be a complicated case, where data interpretation is still considered a major obstacle. The present analysis identifies an overall large contribution from inclusive breakup emissions for all the cases studied.

## Key claim

The post-form zero-range DWBA inclusive breakup formalism, combined with the São Paulo optical potential and a two-body cluster approximation for the projectile, provides a good description of experimental α-particle production data for reactions induced by stable ⁶Li and ⁷Li, but the description for the halo nucleus ⁶He is more complicated and data interpretation remains incomplete.

## Method

The authors implemented the zero-range post-form DWBA (IAV formalism) to compute elastic and nonelastic breakup cross sections for inclusive α-particle production. Projectiles were approximated as two-body clusters: ⁶He as α+dineutron, ⁶Li as α+deuteron, and ⁷Li as α+triton. The São Paulo optical potential was used for the incident channel distorted waves, with a reduced diffuseness of 0.65 fm, while standard phenomenological optical potentials (Koning-Delaroche for nucleons, Han-Shi-Shen for deuterons, Becchetti-Greenlees for tritons, Avrigeanu et al. for α particles) were used for fragment-target interactions. Compound nucleus contributions were computed using the EMPIRE code with the Hauser-Feshbach model.

## Key numbers

* ⁶He binding energy in the two-body α+dineutron model: ε_b = -1.6 MeV (modified effective value from Moro (2007)) compared to the experimental two-neutron separation energy S_2n = 0.975 MeV
* Zero-range constant for ⁶He: D_0 = 70 MeV·fm^(3/2), adjusted to reproduce data for both ⁶He+⁶⁴Zn and ⁶He+²⁰⁹Bi simultaneously
* Zero-range constant for ⁶Li: D_0 = 45 MeV·fm^(3/2), adjusted to data for both ⁶Li+²⁰⁸Pb and ⁶Li+⁹⁰Zr simultaneously
* Zero-range constant for ⁷Li: D_0 = 42 MeV·fm^(3/2)
* ⁶Li α+deuteron separation energy: ε_b = -1.474 MeV
* ⁷Li α+triton separation energy: ε_b = -2.468 MeV
* Coulomb barrier for ⁶He+¹²⁰Sn: V_B ≈ 13.3 MeV
* São Paulo potential diffuseness parameter used: a_0 = 0.65 fm (reduced from the SPP default of ≈1 fm)
* Imaginary-to-real potential depth ratio: W_0 = 0.78 V_0

## Context

This work extends the post-form DWBA inclusive breakup formalism, previously applied successfully to deuteron breakup by Carlson (2016, 2017), Hussein (2017), and Potel (2017), to reactions induced by the weakly bound nuclei ⁶He, ⁶Li, and ⁷Li. The two-body cluster approximation for ⁶He (α+dineutron) is motivated by experimental evidence for dineutron correlations in halo nuclei, including measurements by Spyrou (2012) for ¹⁶Be, Kubota (2020) for ¹¹Li, and Sun (2021) for ⁶He. The calculations are compared to experimental data from Di Pietro (2004), Scuderi (2011), Signorini (2001, 2003, 2004), de Faria (2010), Appannababu (2019), Ost (1972), and Kumawat (2010). The results enable future extension to four-body reaction models within the DWBA framework, as discussed by Carlson (2017), and highlight the need for additional experimental data at forward angles to clarify reaction mechanisms for ⁶He-induced reactions.
