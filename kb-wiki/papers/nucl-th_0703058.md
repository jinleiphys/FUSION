# Light Nuclei in the Framework of the Symplectic No-core Shell Model

> A symplectic no-core shell model (Sp-NCSM) is constructed with the goal of extending the {\it ab-initio} NCSM to include strongly deformed higher-oscillator-shell configurations and to reach heavier nuclei that cannot be studied currently because the spaces encountered are too large to handle, even with the best of modern-day computers. This goal is achieved by integrating two powerful concepts: the {\it ab-initio} NCSM with that of the $\mathrm{Sp}(3,\mathbb{R})\supset\mathrm{SU}(3)$ group-theoretical approach. The NCSM uses modern realistic nuclear interactions in model spaces that consists of many-body configurations up to a given number of $\hbarΩ$ excitations together with modern high-performance parallel computing techniques. The symplectic theory extends this picture by recognizing that when deformed configurations dominate, which they often do, the model space can be better selected so less relevant low-lying $\hbarΩ$ configurations yield to more relevant high-lying $\hbarΩ$ configurations, ones that respect a near symplectic symmetry found in the Hamiltonian. Results from an application of the Sp-NCSM to light nuclei are compared with those for the NCSM and with experiment.

## Key claim

The symplectic no-core shell model (Sp-NCSM) can reproduce approximately 80% of the NCSM wavefunction for low-lying states in light nuclei using only a tiny fraction (0.001%) of the full NCSM basis space, but this result is restricted to 0ħΩ Sp(3,R) irreps and the authors note that including 2ħΩ 2p-2h configurations would likely improve overlaps further.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations for ¹²C and ¹⁶O using the JISP16 realistic NN interaction in model spaces up to 6ħΩ excitations. They then projected the resulting NCSM eigenstates onto basis states of the symplectic Sp(3,R)⊃SU(3) algebraic model, constructed from 0ħΩ Sp(3,R) lowest-weight states (bandheads) and their symplectic raising operator excitations. Overlaps between the NCSM and Sp-NCSM wavefunctions were computed to quantify the symplectic symmetry content of the realistic eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C at ħΩ=15 MeV, the three leading 0ħΩ Sp(3,R) irreps account for 80.95% of the NCSM 0⁺_gs wavefunction, 80.11% of the 2⁺_1 state, and 77.81% of the 4⁺_1 state (Table 1).
- The most deformed irrep (0 4)S=0 alone contributes 64.84% to the 0⁺_gs, 64.95% to the 2⁺_1, and 68.78% to the 4⁺_1 NCSM eigenstates in ¹²C at ħΩ=15 MeV.
- For ¹²C at ħΩ=11 MeV, the three Sp(3,R) irreps account for 85% of the NCSM 0⁺_gs wavefunction, with the (0 4)S=0 irrep growing to 91% of the Sp(3,R)-symmetric part.
- The symplectic model space for ¹²C with three irreps has total dimension 514, which is 0.001% of the NCSM space dimension (~10⁷).
- For ¹⁶O, the single 0ħΩ (0 0) Sp(3,R) irrep accounts for more than 70% of the NCSM ground state, with the 0ħΩ SU(3) component alone being ≈60%.
- The B(E2:2⁺₁→0⁺_gs) value from the three Sp(3,R) irreps is ≈110% of the corresponding NCSM value for ħΩ=15 MeV and N_max=4.

## Context

This work extends the ab-initio no-core shell model (NCSM) approach by integrating it with the symplectic Sp(3,R) algebraic model, which generalizes Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and provides a microscopic realization of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model. Prior phenomenological studies using symplectic approaches (Escher and Leviatan, 2002; Arickx et al., 1982; Avancini and Pato, 1993) had achieved good reproduction of low-lying energies in ¹²C, but this work establishes for the first time the dominance of Sp(3,R) symmetry in ab-initio NCSM calculations with realistic interactions. The results enable a path toward extending no-core calculations to higher ħΩ configurations and heavier nuclei that are currently computationally unreachable, potentially allowing convergence to measured B(E2) values without effective charges and modeling α-cluster phenomena.
