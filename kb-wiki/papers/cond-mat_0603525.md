# Level Repulsion in Constrained Gaussian Random-Matrix Ensembles

> Introducing sets of constraints, we define new classes of random-matrix ensembles, the constrained Gaussian unitary (CGUE) and the deformed Gaussian unitary (DGUE) ensembles. The latter interpolate between the GUE and the CGUE. We derive a sufficient condition for GUE-type level repulsion to persist in the presence of constraints. For special classes of constraints, we extend this approach to the orthogonal and to the symplectic ensembles. A generalized Fourier theorem relates the spectral properties of the constraining ensembles with those of the constrained ones. We find that in the DGUEs, level repulsion always prevails at a sufficiently short distance and may be lifted only in the limit of strictly enforced constraints.

## Key claim

The paper derives a sufficient condition for GUE-type quadratic level repulsion to persist in constrained Gaussian random-matrix ensembles, but this condition is not necessary: level repulsion may still occur even when the inequality is violated, as demonstrated by the example of traceless Hermitean matrices.

## Method

The authors define constrained Gaussian unitary ensembles (CGUE) by imposing that certain linear combinations of matrix elements vanish, and construct unitarily invariant "superensembles" by averaging over unitary transformations of the constraints. They express the joint eigenvalue probability distribution using the Harish-Chandra Itzykson Zuber (HCIZ) integral, then analyze the convergence of the resulting radial integral to determine when the Vandermonde factor Δ²(x) (which encodes level repulsion) survives. For the orthogonal and symplectic cases, they use an asymptotic expansion of the HCIZ integral to derive analogous conditions.

## Key numbers

- The sufficient condition for GUE-type level repulsion is: N_Q + Σⱼ Lⱼ(Lⱼ−1)/2 < N(N−1)/2, where N_Q is the number of constraints, Lⱼ are degeneracy multiplicities, and N is the matrix dimension.
- For the orthogonal (β=1) and symplectic (β=4) cases, the sufficient condition is: N_Q < β N(N−1)/4.
- The spectral width of the CGUE is reduced relative to the GUE by a factor √(1 − N_Q/N²): (1/N) Trace H²_CGUE = λ² [1 − N_Q/N²].
- The variance of (1/N) Trace H² for the CGUE is V_CGUE = 2λ⁴/N² (1 − N_Q/N²), which differs from the Gaussian prediction 2λ⁴/N² (1 − N_Q/N²)², showing non-Gaussian matrix-element distributions.

## Context

This work provides a theoretical framework for understanding spectral fluctuations in constrained random-matrix ensembles, which include the embedded Gaussian k-body ensembles (Mon75; Kota01; Ben03), the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) (FreWon; BohFlo; Pap06), and random band matrices (Fyo91). The results enable analytical treatment of level repulsion in these physically important ensembles, though the authors note that for the EGUE the sufficient condition is only met for the trivial case k=m. The paper extends prior work on the HCIZ integral (HC57; IZ80; zinn) and on degeneracies in that integral (Sch03; Fyo03), and the complementary distribution function relation generalizes a Fourier theorem connecting constrained and constraining ensembles.
