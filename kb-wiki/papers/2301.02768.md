# Hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star by combining the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and Dyson-Schwinger equation approach

> Starting from the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory for nuclear matter and the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach for quark matter, the possible hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of a neutron star is explored. The first-order phase transition and crossover are studied by performing the Maxwell construction and three-window construction respectively. The mass-radius relation and the tidal deformability of the hybrid star are calculated and compared to the joint mass-radius observation of a neutron star and the constraints from gravitational wave detection. For the Maxwell construction, no stable quark core is found in the interior of a neutron star. For the three-window construction, the parameters of the smooth interpolation function are chosen in such a way to keep the thermodynamic stability and lead to a moderate crossover density region. To support a two-solar-mass neutron star under the three-window construction, the effective width of medium screening effects in quark matter should be around $0.35$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper finds that under the Maxwell construction for a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, no stable quark core is formed inside a neutron star; however, under the three-window construction for a smooth crossover, a two-solar-mass hybrid star can be supported only if the effective width of medium screening effects in quark matter is around 0.35 GeV.

## Method

The authors combine the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory for nuclear matter with the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach for quark matter, both within a relativistic framework. They apply the Maxwell construction to model a first-order phase transition and the three-window construction with a hyperbolic tangent interpolation function to model a smooth crossover between hadron and quark phases. The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for the mass-radius relation and to compute the tidal deformability of hybrid neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_{\text{sat}} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Current-quark mass for strange quark: $m_s = 115\ \text{MeV}$ (obtained by fitting the $K$ meson mass in vacuum)
- Interaction model parameters in vacuum: $\omega = 0.5\ \text{GeV}$ and $D = 1.0\ \text{GeV}^2$
- Vacuum pressure for up and down quarks: $P_u(\mu_{u,0}) = P_d(\mu_{d,0}) = -45\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}^{-3}$
- To support a two-solar-mass neutron star under the three-window construction, the effective width of medium screening effects $\omega_{\text{eff}}$ should be around $0.35\ \text{GeV}$
- The RBHF calculation is limited to densities up to $\rho = 0.57\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ when using the full Dirac space method

## Context

This work extends previous studies that combined the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with the DSE approach (Chen et al., 2011; Chen et al., 2012; Chen et al., 2015; Yasutake et al., 2016) by instead using the relativistic RBHF theory, ensuring consistency with the relativistic DSE framework. The results enable future comparisons with neutron star observations from NICER and gravitational wave detections, and provide constraints on the medium screening effects in quark matter needed to support massive hybrid stars.
