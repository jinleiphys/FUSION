# Deformed shell effects in $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk quasifission fragments

> Background: Quasifission is the main reaction channel hindering the formation of superheavy nuclei (SHN). Its understanding will help to optimize entrance channels for SHN studies. Quasifission also provides a probe to understand the influence of shell effects in the formation of the fragments. Purpose: Investigate the role of shell effects in quasifission and their interplay with the orientation of the deformed target in the entrance channel. Methods: $^{48}$Ca$+^{249}$Bk collisions are studied with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach for a range of angular momenta and orientations. Results: Unlike similar reactions with a $^{238}$U target, no significant shell effects which could be attributed to $^{208}$Pb "doubly-magic" nucleus are found. However, the octupole deformed shell gap at $N=56$ seems to strongly influence quasifission in the most central collisions. Conclusions: Shell effects similar to those observed in fission affect the formation of quasifission fragments. Mass-angle correlations could be used to experimentally isolate the fragments influenced by $N=56$ octupole shell gaps.

## Key claim
Unlike previous TDHF studies of quasifission with a $^{238}$U target, no significant shell effects attributable to the $^{208}$Pb "doubly-magic" nucleus are found in $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk collisions; however, the octupole deformed shell gap at $N=56$ appears to strongly influence quasifission in the most central collisions.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using Skyrme SLy4d energy density functionals for $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 234 MeV. They systematically varied both the orientation angle $\beta$ of the deformed $^{249}$Bk target (12 angles from 0 to 165 degrees) and the orbital angular momentum $L$ (values from 0 to 120-130 $\hbar$), totaling 148 collisions. The calculations tracked fragment masses, proton and neutron numbers, total kinetic energy, and scattering angles to identify shell effects in the quasifission fragments.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: 234 MeV
- Range of orbital angular momenta studied: $L=0$ to $L=120-130\hbar$
- Contact time for fast quasifission: approximately 6 zeptoseconds (zs)
- Mass ratio of quasifission fragments: $0.28 < M_R < 0.72$
- Peak mass ratio yields at $M_R \approx 0.33$ and $0.67$
- Full width half maximum of mass yield: FWHM $\approx 0.1$, corresponding to standard deviation $\sigma_{M_R} \approx 0.042$
- Gated neutron number peak centered at $\overline{N}_{gated} \approx 124$ for heavy fragments
- Peripheral quasifission events centered around $\overline{N}_{periph} \approx 60$ and $\overline{Z}_{periph} \approx 40$ (the $^{100}$Zr region)
- $N=56$ fragments correspond to $^{94}$Sr ($Z=38$, $N=56$)

## Context
This work extends previous TDHF studies of quasifission with actinide targets, particularly $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{238}$U (Wakhle 2014, Oberacker 2014, Umar 2016), which found strong $Z=82$ shell effects in tip collisions. Unlike those studies, the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system shows no significant $^{208}$Pb shell effects but instead reveals the influence of octupole deformed shell gaps at $N=56$, recently identified as drivers of asymmetric fission (Scamps 2018, 2019). The systematic inclusion of a full range of target orientations enables prediction of mass yields and mass-angle correlations that can be directly compared with experiment, and suggests that mass-angle correlation measurements could experimentally isolate fragments influenced by the $N=56$ octupole shell gap.
