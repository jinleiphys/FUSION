# Fermi motion and nuclear modification factor

> It has been argued recently that the so-called nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) is an observable useful for identifying the quark-gluon plasma. We discuss the effect of Fermi motion in nuclei on $R_{AA}$ at CERN SPS and BNL RHIC energies. Contrary to the simple intuition, rather large effects are found for CERN SPS. The Fermi motion in nuclei contributes significantly to the Cronin effect. The effect found is qualitatively similar to the one observed experimentally at CERN energies and similar to the one obtained in the models of multiple scattering of initial partons. We predict different size of the effect for different types of hadrons, especially at low energies.

# Fermi motion and nuclear modification factor

## Key claim

Contrary to simple intuition, the Fermi motion of nucleons in colliding nuclei produces a sizeable enhancement of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) at CERN SPS energies (W ≈ 17–20 GeV), even though at RHIC energies the effect almost disappears. The authors argue that this effect must be included before reliable conclusions about other nuclear effects (such as parton multiple scattering or jet quenching) can be drawn from phenomenological studies.

## Method

The authors model nucleus–nucleus collisions as a superposition of elementary nucleon–nucleon collisions, folding the elementary production cross section for \(\pi^0\) (and other hadrons) over the Fermi momentum distributions of nucleons in the two colliding nuclei. They use a Fermi-gas model with \(p_F = 1.37\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and a Gaussian parametrization for \(^{12}\text{C}\), and employ a parametrization of ISR data for the elementary \(pp \to \pi^0 X\) cross section. The main effect arises because the true nucleon–nucleon center-of-mass energy \(W^*\) differs from the nominal energy \(W\) when averaged over the Fermi distributions, leading to an enhancement of the cross section at fixed transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum taken as \(p_F = 1.37\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) in the Fermi-gas model.
- At SPS energies (W = 17.4 GeV), the Fermi motion causes a sizeable enhancement of \(R_{AA}^{\pi^0}\) for \(p_t > 4\ \text{GeV}\).
- The effect of Fermi motion on \(R_{AA}\) is still present up to W = 50 GeV, but almost disappears at RHIC energies.
- The binding correction (effective nucleon mass reduced by 10–20%) changes the result only quantitatively, not qualitatively; a \(\pm 150\ \text{MeV}\) shift in nucleon mass at W = 20 GeV does not alter the main trend.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) (previously used as a quark-gluon plasma diagnostic by Wang (2000), Wang and Wang (2001), and others) by quantifying a previously neglected nuclear effect. It shows that the Fermi motion alone produces a Cronin-like enhancement, qualitatively similar to that observed by the WA98 collaboration at CERN SPS and to the predictions of parton multiple-scattering models (e.g., parton rescattering models). The authors note that the effect must be included in future analyses of jet quenching and that collisions of light nuclei (e.g., \(d-d\) or \(\alpha-\alpha\)) could help isolate the Fermi-motion contribution from other nuclear effects.
