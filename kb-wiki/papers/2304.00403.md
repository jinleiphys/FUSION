# On statistical fluctuations in collective flows

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, event-by-event fluctuations are known to have non-trivial implications. Even though the probability distribution is geometrically isotropic for the initial conditions, the anisotropic $\varepsilon_n$ still differs from zero owing to the statistical fluctuations in the energy profile. On the other hand, the flow harmonics extracted from the hadron spectrum using the multi-particle correlators are inevitably subjected to non-vanishing variance due to the finite number of hadrons emitted in individual events. As one aims to extract information on the fluctuations in the initial conditions via flow harmonics and their fluctuations, finite multiplicity may play a role in interfering with such an effort. In this study, we explore the properties and impacts of such fluctuations in the initial and final states, which both notably appear to be statistical ones originating from the finite number of quanta of the underlying system. We elaborate on the properties of the initial-state eccentricities for the smooth and event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions and their distinct impacts on the resulting flow harmonics. Numerical simulations are performed. The possible implications of the present study are also addressed.

## Key claim

Even when the initial probability distribution is geometrically isotropic, the anisotropic eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) are non-zero due to statistical fluctuations from finite numbers of sources; simultaneously, flow harmonics extracted from hadron spectra using multi-particle correlators have non-vanishing variance due to finite hadron multiplicity per event, and these two types of statistical fluctuations (initial-state geometric and final-state multiplicity) are mixed and potentially indistinguishable in realistic event-by-event analyses.

## Method

The authors employed the identical point-source model (Ollitrault, 1992) with an isotropic Gaussian average density profile, where \(N\) point-like sources are distributed according to \(p(z_i) = \frac{1}{\pi R_0^2} \exp(-|z_i|^2/R_0^2)\). They derived analytic expressions for the eccentricity distribution and its cumulants, then performed Monte Carlo simulations where flow harmonics were assumed proportional to eccentricities (\(v_n = \mathcal{C}_\mathrm{v} \epsilon_n\) with \(\mathcal{C}_\mathrm{v}=1\)) and hadrons were generated with finite multiplicity \(M\) per event. The flow harmonics were estimated using the two-particle correlation estimator \(\widehat{v_n^2} = \frac{1}{M(M-1)}\sum_{i \ne j} \cos n(\varphi_i - \varphi_j)\), and the resulting variances were compared between smooth and fluctuating initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- For the identical point-source model with \(N\) sources: \(\mathrm{E}[|\epsilon_2|^2] = 2/N\) and \(\mathrm{E}[|\epsilon_2|^4] = 8/(2N + N^2)\)
- Eccentricity cumulants: \(\epsilon_2\{2\} = \sqrt{2/N}\) and \(\epsilon_2\{4\} = \left(16/(N^2(N+2))\right)^{1/4}\)
- For \(N=10\) with fluctuating initial conditions and multiplicity \(M=500\): \(\mathrm{E}[v_2] = 0.282\) with \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 1.696 \times 10^{-2}\); for smooth initial conditions at same \(M\): \(\mathrm{E}[v_2] = 0.295\) with \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 2.410 \times 10^{-3}\)
- For \(N=10\) with fluctuating initial conditions and multiplicity \(M=1000\): \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 1.769 \times 10^{-2}\); for smooth initial conditions at same \(M\): \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 2.041 \times 10^{-3}\)
- For \(N=50\) with fluctuating initial conditions and multiplicity \(M=1000\): \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 7.396 \times 10^{-3}\); for smooth initial conditions at same \(M\): \(\mathrm{Var}[v_2] = 5.576 \times 10^{-4}\)
- In the limit \(M \to \infty\) for fluctuating initial conditions: \(\lim_{M\to\infty} \mathrm{Var}[\widehat{2}_{\{2,-2\}}] = \frac{8\mathcal{C}_\mathrm{v}^4}{2N + N^2} - \frac{4\mathcal{C}_\mathrm{v}^4}{N^2}\), which remains non-zero for finite \(N\)

## Context

This work extends the identical point-source model of Ollitrault (1992) and the cumulant formalism for eccentricities (Ollitrault, 1992; various authors) by simultaneously treating two sources of flow fluctuations: initial-state geometric fluctuations from finite participant number and final-state statistical fluctuations from finite hadron multiplicity. The study distinguishes between the physical fluctuations (from initial conditions, which carry information about the microscopic model) and the purely statistical fluctuations (from finite multiplicity in estimators), showing that the latter do not vanish even for well-defined flow harmonics. This enables more careful interpretation of experimental flow fluctuation measurements, where the two types of variance are mixed and must be disentangled to extract information about initial conditions from event generators such as MC Glauber, CGC MC-KLN, NeXUS, and EPOS.
