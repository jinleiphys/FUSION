# Extracting transition generalized parton distributions from hard exclusive pion-nucleon scattering

> We study the extraction of transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from production of two back-to-back high transverse momentum photons ($γγ$) and a massive pair of leptons ($\ell^+\ell^-$) in hard exclusive pion-nucleon scattering. We argue that the exclusive scattering amplitude of both processes could be factorized into nonperturbative pion distribution amplitude and nucleon transition GPDs that are convoluted with perturbatively calculable short-distance matching coefficients. We demonstrate that the exclusive diphoton production not only is complementary to the Drell-Yan-type dilepton production for extracting the GPDs, but also provides enhanced sensitivities for extracting the parton momentum fraction $x$ dependence of the GPDs. We show that both exclusive observables are physically measurable at the J-PARC and AMBER experiment energies. If the target nucleon can be polarized, corresponding spin asymmetries can offer additional sensitivities for extracting transition GPDs.

## Key claim
The paper argues that exclusive diphoton production in hard pion-nucleon scattering provides enhanced sensitivity to the parton momentum fraction \(x\) dependence of transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) compared to the Drell-Yan dilepton process, because the hard coefficients for diphoton production entangle the measured transverse momentum \(q_T\) with the \(x\) dependence, whereas for dilepton production the \(q_T\) dependence factorizes out leaving only GPD moments.

## Method
The authors factorize the exclusive scattering amplitude for both \(\pi N \to N' \gamma\gamma\) and \(\pi N \to N' \ell^+\ell^-\) into a convolution of a nonperturbative pion distribution amplitude, nucleon transition GPDs, and perturbatively calculable short-distance matching coefficients, valid when the transverse momentum \(q_T\) is much larger than \(\sqrt{|t|}\). They evaluate the unpolarized cross sections and single transverse spin asymmetries (SSAs) for both processes at fixed-target energies corresponding to J-PARC (20 GeV) and AMBER (100 GeV), using the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model for transition GPDs and adding shadow GPDs to demonstrate sensitivity to the \(x\) dependence.

## Key numbers
- Pion beam energies considered: \(E_\pi = 20\) GeV (J-PARC) and \(E_\pi = 100\) GeV (AMBER).
- Kinematic point used for numerical studies: \((t, \xi) = (-0.2~\text{GeV}^2, 0.2)\).
- Minimum transverse momentum cut: \(q_T \geq 1\) GeV to suppress power corrections from \(\sqrt{|t|}/q_T\).
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 0.13\) GeV, used in the asymptotic pion distribution amplitude \(\phi(z) = 6z(1-z)\).

## Context
This work extends the study of hard exclusive processes with pion beams, building on the factorization framework for single diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs) developed by Qiu and collaborators (Qiu, 2022; Qiu, 2023). It contrasts with the Drell-Yan dilepton process (Berger, 2001; Goloskokov, 2015; Sawada, 2016), which at leading order yields only GPD moments and is thus susceptible to shadow GPD ambiguities (Bertone, 2021). The diphoton process was first introduced by Qiu and Yu (2022) and is shown here to provide complementary constraints that can reduce shadow GPD ambiguities, enabling future extractions of the \(x\) dependence of transition GPDs at J-PARC and AMBER.
