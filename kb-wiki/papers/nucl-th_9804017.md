# Two Phase Simulation of Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> A two phase cascade is presented for the treatment of ultra-high energy ion-ion collisions at energies from 17-200 GeV. First a high energy, fast cascade is performed, in which original baryons and freed hard partons, if any, collide. This stage ignores energy loss from soft (slow) processes. In this first version no hard processes, aside from Drell-Yan production, are included. The space-time history of the hard phase is then used as input to reconstruct soft energy-loss, which occurs over a longer time scale. Soft meson production is therefore treated as coherent over groups of interacting nucleons. Two body data, though, are used extensively to guide the reinitialisation of the cascade, which satisfies conservation laws for charge, strangeness and of course, energy-momentum. A second phase, low energy cascade is finally carried out among the products of the hard cascade. The model selected to describe elementary hadron-hadron collisions in the soft cascade incorporates generic mesons and baryons, which are the agents for rescattering. We imagine a constituent quark model applies, with generic mesons consisting of an excited quark-antiquark pair, and generic baryons constructed from three quarks. The chief result is a reconciliation of the important Drell-Yan measurements, indicating high-mass lepton pairs are produced as if no energy is lost from the nucleons, with the apparent success of a purely hadronic, soft cascade in describing nucleon stopping and meson production in heavy ion experiments at the CERN SPS.

## Key claim

The paper presents a two-phase cascade model that reconciles the apparent contradiction between Drell-Yan measurements (which indicate that nucleons lose no energy in high-energy collisions) and the success of purely hadronic soft cascades in describing nucleon stopping and meson production at CERN SPS energies, by separating hard and soft processes based on their different time scales.

## Method

The simulation runs in two phases: first, a high-energy fast cascade records the space-time geometry of nucleon-nucleon collisions while allowing only rapid processes (Drell-Yan and charmonium production) to occur, ignoring soft energy loss. The space-time history from this phase is then used to reconstruct groups of nucleons that have mutually collided, and energy loss is computed for each nucleon using its collision history and measured nucleon-nucleon inelasticity. A second low-energy soft hadronic cascade is then carried out among the products, using generic meson and baryon resonances (constituent quark model with masses 0.3-1 GeV for mesons and 1-2 GeV for baryons) as the agents for rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range: sqrt(s) = 17-200 GeV for ion-ion collisions
- Drell-Yan cross-section in pA collisions scales as sigma_DY ~ A (linear A-dependence)
- Generic meson resonance masses: 0.3-1 GeV for non-strange mesons, centered near 600-700 MeV
- Generic baryon resonance masses: 1-2 GeV
- Generic resonance widths: Gamma ~ 125 MeV
- At RHIC energy (sqrt(s)=200 GeV/A), pion dN/dy differs by no more than 10-15% between lab frame (gamma ~ 20,000) and center-of-mass frame (gamma ~ 100)
- In central Pb+Pb at SPS: approximately 46 groups produced, with largest group containing ~25 nucleons
- Average rapidity drift per collision in pp: Delta y ~ 0.6-0.7
- Drell-Yan pair mass threshold: M_mumu > 4 GeV for hard process classification

## Context

This work extends the earlier hadronic cascade ARC (used at AGS energies) and the LUCIFERI code by introducing a time-scale-based separation of hard and soft processes, building on Gottfried's (1970s) mechanism for leading particle behavior in pA collisions. The approach addresses the puzzle that Drell-Yan production shows linear A-dependence (suggesting no energy loss) while soft meson production shows significant energy loss, a contradiction that pure two-body cascades cannot resolve. The model enables unified treatment of both hard and soft processes in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, and can be used to test whether charmonium suppression at SPS has a purely hadronic explanation.
