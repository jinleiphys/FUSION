# Isospin violation in J/Psi to phi pi0 eta decay and the f0-a0 mixing

> The isospin violating $J/Ψ\to ϕπ^0 η$ decay is thought to be dominated by the mixing of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ scalar resonances. We make a theoretical evaluation of the $J/Ψ\to ϕπ^0 η$ decay extending our own previous model for other $J/Ψ$ decays into one vector meson and two pseudoscalars using the techniques of the chiral unitary approach. The scalar resonances are dynamically generated through the final state interaction of the pseudoscalar mesons implementing unitarity from the lowest order ChPT amplitudes. Besides the direct $J/ΨϕPP$ vertex, other mechanisms like the sequential exchange of vector and axial-vector mesons are shown to be important in order to obtain the actual strength of the mixing. We get a very good agreement with the $π^0 η$ invariant mass distribution and branching ratio with recent BESIII data. Quantification of the $f_0(980)-a_0(980)$ mixing is done and compared with results from several experiments.

## Key claim

The isospin-violating $J/\Psi\to \phi \pi^0 \eta$ decay is dominated by $f_0(980)$-$a_0(980)$ mixing, but the sequential exchange of vector ($K^*(892)$) and axial-vector ($K_1(1270)$, $K_1(1400)$) mesons is crucial to obtain the correct strength; without these mechanisms, the direct production alone overestimates the peak strength by about a factor of two.

## Method

The author extends a previous chiral unitary approach model for $J/\Psi$ decays into one vector meson and two pseudoscalars to the isospin-violating $J/\Psi\to \phi \pi^0 \eta$ channel. The scalar resonances $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ are dynamically generated through the final-state interaction of pseudoscalar mesons, using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with tree-level amplitudes from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian including isospin-breaking and electromagnetic effects. Isospin violation enters via the mass differences between charged and neutral kaons and pions in the two-meson loop functions and in the scattering potentials. In addition to the direct $J/\Psi \phi PP$ vertex with final-state rescattering, the model includes sequential mechanisms where the $J/\Psi$ decays into a vector or axial-vector meson plus a pseudoscalar, and that intermediate meson subsequently decays into the final $\phi$ and another pseudoscalar.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio $BR(J/\Psi\to \phi \pi^0 \eta) = (4.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-6}$, compared to the BESIII result $(5.0 \pm 2.7(\text{stat}) \pm 1.7(\text{sys})) \times 10^{-6}$.
* Mixing intensity $\xi_{fa}(M=991.4\ \text{MeV}) = 0.020^{+0.003}_{-0.011}$, compared to experimental values from SND (0.088), KLOE (0.034), BNL (0.019), and CB (0.027).
* The $f_0$-$a_0$ mixing is enhanced only in the narrow $8\ \text{MeV}$ region between the $K^+K^-$ threshold ($987.4\ \text{MeV}$) and $K^0\bar K^0$ threshold ($995.2\ \text{MeV}$).
* The direct mechanism alone overestimates the experimental peak strength by about a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous model for $J/\Psi$ decays into a vector meson and two pseudoscalars (Roca 2004) to the isospin-violating $J/\Psi\to \phi \pi^0 \eta$ channel, which was measured by BESIII (Ablikim 2010). It improves upon an earlier theoretical calculation by Hanhart (2007) that considered only the direct $J/\Psi\phi PP$ vertex with final-state rescattering, by adding sequential vector and axial-vector exchange mechanisms that are essential for reproducing the observed strength. The results enable quantitative comparison of the $f_0$-$a_0$ mixing intensity across different production reactions, including radiative $\phi$ decays studied by SND, KLOE, BNL, and CB.
