# Meson-baryon couplings and the $F/D$ ratio from QCD sum rules

> Motivated by the recent work on the calculation of the $πNN$ coupling constant using QCD sum rule beyond the chiral limit, we construct the corresponding sum rules for the couplings, $ηNN$, $πΞΞ$, $ηΞΞ$, $πΣΣ$ and $ηΣΣ$. In constructing the $η$-baryon sum rules, we use the second moment of the $η$ wave function, which we obtain from the pion wave function after SU(3) rotation. In the SU(3) symmetric limit, we can identify the term responsible for the $F/D$ ratio in the OPE, which after the sum rule analysis gives $F/D\sim 0.2$, a factor of 3 smaller than from other studies. We also present a qualitative analysis including the SU(3) breaking terms.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis in the SU(3) symmetric limit yields an \(F/D\) ratio of approximately 0.2, which is a factor of 3 smaller than values obtained from other studies; this result is derived from identifying the term responsible for the \(F/D\) ratio in the operator product expansion (OPE) and is subject to the assumption that the second moment of the \(\eta\) wave function equals that of the pion after SU(3) rotation.

## Method

The authors construct two-point QCD correlation functions with a meson, using Ioffe-type baryon interpolating fields for the nucleon and \(\Xi\) hyperon, and evaluate the OPE up to dimension 8 including quark-mass terms beyond the chiral limit. They match the OPE to a phenomenological side parametrized by the meson-baryon coupling and baryon pole, apply a Borel transformation, and extract the couplings for \(\pi NN\), \(\eta NN\), \(\pi\Xi\Xi\), \(\eta\Xi\Xi\), \(\pi\Sigma\Sigma\), and \(\eta\Sigma\Sigma\); the \(F/D\) ratio is then determined by comparing the OPE structures of the \(\pi NN\) and \(\eta NN\) sum rules in the SU(3) limit.

## Key numbers

* \(F/D \sim 0.2\) from the SU(3) symmetric limit sum rule analysis, a factor of 3 smaller than other studies.
* The \(\eta\) meson mass squared \(m_\eta^2\) is approximately 15.6 times larger than \(m_\pi^2\), providing an enhancing factor for the \(\eta\) matrix element relative to the pion.
* The quark-gluon mixed condensate parameter \(m_0^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\) is used in the OPE.
* The three-particle wave function parameter \(f_{3\pi} = 0.003\) GeV\(^2\) is used for the pion.
* The strange quark mass \(m_s \sim 150\) MeV is identified as a large SU(3) breaking source in the \(\pi\Xi\Xi\) sum rule.
* The form factor correction for the \(\eta NN\) coupling from \(p^2=0\) to \(p^2=m_\eta^2\) is estimated as \(m_\eta^2/\Lambda^2 \sim 0.13\) with \(\Lambda \sim 1.5\) GeV from the Bonn potential.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method for the \(\pi NN\) coupling beyond the chiral limit, previously developed by one of the authors (Kim, 1999), to other meson-baryon couplings in the SU(3) sector, including \(\eta NN\), \(\pi\Xi\Xi\), \(\eta\Xi\Xi\), \(\pi\Sigma\Sigma\), and \(\eta\Sigma\Sigma\). The extracted \(F/D\) ratio of about 0.2 is significantly smaller than the value from SU(6) considerations or other phenomenological analyses, and the sum rules provide a framework to qualitatively study SU(3) breaking effects in the couplings, which can constrain hyperon-baryon interaction models such as the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1989) and the Nijmegen model (Rijken, 1991).
