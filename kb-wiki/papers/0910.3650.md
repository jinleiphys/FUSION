# Pade-resummed high-order perturbation theory for nuclear structure calculations

> We apply high-order many-body perturbation theory for the calculation of ground-state energies of closed-shell nuclei using realistic nuclear interactions. Using a simple recursive formulation, we compute the perturbative energy contributions up to 30th order and compare to exact no-core shell model calculations for the same model space and Hamiltonian. Generally, finite partial sums of this perturbation series do not show convergence with increasing order, but tend to diverge exponentially. Nevertheless, through a simple resummation via Pade approximants it is possible to extract rapidly converging and highly accurate results for the ground state energy.

## Key claim

High-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) for nuclear ground-state energies produces exponentially diverging partial sums, but Padé resummation of these divergent series yields results that are numerically identical to exact no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the same Hamiltonian and model space, provided the summed order of the approximant is sufficiently large (typically M+N greater than about 10).

## Method

The authors computed perturbative energy contributions up to 30th order using a recursive formulation based on the Møller-Plesset partitioning of the Hamiltonian, with a harmonic oscillator single-particle basis. They constructed diagonal, super-diagonal, and sub-diagonal Padé approximants from the power-series coefficients and evaluated them at the physical coupling λ=1 to obtain resummed ground-state energy estimates. The results were compared to exact NCSM calculations performed with the Antoine shell-model code for the same model spaces and the same SRG-evolved chiral N3LO interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He in a 12ħΩ model space with ħΩ=20 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -26.561 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.000 MeV from this value.
- For ⁴He in a 12ħΩ model space with ħΩ=32 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -27.194 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.001 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O in a 6ħΩ model space with ħΩ=20 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -108.33 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.01 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O in a 6ħΩ model space with ħΩ=24 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -109.81 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.01 MeV.
- For ⁴⁰Ca in a 4ħΩ model space with ħΩ=20 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -320.37 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.00 MeV.
- For ⁴⁰Ca in a 4ħΩ model space with ħΩ=24 MeV, the NCSM ground-state energy is -294.19 MeV; the Padé [10/10] approximant deviates by +0.01 MeV.
- At 30th order, individual perturbative contributions reach approximately 10⁴ MeV for ¹⁶O and 10⁹ MeV for ⁴⁰Ca, far exceeding any physical energy scale.

## Context

This work extends the study of many-body perturbation theory for nuclear ground states beyond the typical low-order calculations (e.g., Roth et al. (2006), Coraggio et al. (2006, 2007)) by demonstrating that the perturbation series diverges exponentially for realistic interactions, contrary to the assumption of systematic improvability. The use of Padé approximants as a resummation technique enables extraction of exact eigenvalues from the divergent series, establishing a direct equivalence between Padé-resummed MBPT and full configuration-interaction solutions. This enables future applications where Padé approximants could provide accurate ground-state energies from high-order perturbative coefficients without requiring the full diagonalization of the Hamiltonian matrix.
