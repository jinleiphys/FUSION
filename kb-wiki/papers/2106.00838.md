# Gibbs entropy from entanglement in electric quenches

> In quantum electrodynamics with charged fermions, a background electric field is the source of the chiral anomaly which creates a chirally imbalanced state of fermions. This chiral state is realized through the production of entangled pairs of right-moving fermions and left-moving antifermions (or vice versa, depending on the orientation of the electric field). Here we show that the statistical Gibbs entropy associated with these pairs is equal to the entropy of entanglement between the right-moving particles and left-moving antiparticles. We then derive an asymptotic expansion for the entanglement entropy in terms of the cumulants of the multiplicity distribution of produced particles and explain how to re-sum this asymptotic expansion. Finally, we study the time dependence of the entanglement entropy in a specific time-dependent pulsed background electric field, the so-called "Sauter pulse", and illustrate how our resummation method works in this specific case. We also find that short pulses (such as the ones created by high energy collisions) result in an approximately thermal distribution for the produced particles.

## Key claim

The statistical Gibbs entropy associated with fermion-antifermion pairs created by a background electric field is equal to the entanglement entropy between right-moving particles and left-moving antiparticles, providing a microscopic quantum origin for the thermodynamic entropy of the produced state. This equality holds for both fermionic and scalar particles, as the authors note from prior work.

## Method

The authors use Bogoliubov transformations to relate creation and annihilation operators at different times in 1+1 dimensional quantum electrodynamics with a time-dependent background electric field, constructing the Fock space explicitly. They compute the reduced density matrix by tracing over antiparticle states and derive the entanglement entropy from first principles, then compare it to the Gibbs entropy computed from the occupation probabilities given by the Bogoliubov coefficients. They further derive an asymptotic expansion relating the entanglement entropy to the cumulants of the multiplicity distribution of produced particles, using techniques from full counting statistics, and apply this to the specific case of a Sauter pulse electric field.

## Key numbers

- The entanglement entropy per momentum mode is given by \( S_k = -[|\alpha_{k_1,t^*}|^2 \log(|\alpha_{k_1,t^*}|^2) + |\beta_{k_1,t^*}|^2 \log(|\beta_{k_1,t^*}|^2)] \), where \( |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \)
- The asymptotic expansion relating entropy to cumulants is \( S_E = 2\sum_{l=1}^\infty \zeta(2l) C_{2l} \), where \( \zeta(2l) \) are Riemann zeta values and \( C_{2l} \) are even cumulants of the multiplicity distribution
- For the Sauter pulse with small \( \gamma = \mathcal{E}\tau/m \), the asymptotic momentum distribution scales as \( |\beta_{k_1,\infty}|^2 \sim \gamma^2 m^2\tau^2 / [\lambda^2 \sinh^2(\pi m\tau\lambda)] \) with \( \lambda = \sqrt{1 + (k_1/m)^2} \)
- In the short pulse limit \( m\tau \ll 1 \), the spectrum decays as \( 1/(m^2 + k_1^2)^2 \); in the long pulse limit \( m\tau \gtrsim 1 \), the distribution resembles a thermal distribution at temperature \( T = 1/(2\pi\tau) \)

## Context

This work extends the computation of entanglement entropy in pair creation by Ebadi (2014) by explicitly proving its equality to Gibbs entropy and connecting it to the multiplicity distribution of produced particles. The asymptotic expansion relating entropy to cumulants was previously derived by Klich (2008) in the context of quantum shot noise in point contacts. The results enable reconstruction of entanglement entropy from experimentally measurable multiplicity distributions in particle production, with potential applications to understanding thermalization in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, building on ideas from Kharzeev (2005) about thermalization from chromoelectric fields.
