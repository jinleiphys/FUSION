# Neutron beta decay and exact conservation of charged weak hadronic vector current in the Standard Model

> We investigate a reliability of the hypothesis of exact conservation of the charged weak hadronic vector current in the neutron $β^-$-decay with polarized neutron and unpolarized proton and electron. We calculate the contributions of the phenomenological term, responsible for Exact Conservation of the charged weak hadronic Vector Current (or the ECVC effect) in the neutron $β^-$-decay even for different masses of the neutron and proton (see Leitner et al. Phys. Rev. C73, 065502 (2006)), to the correlation coefficients together with a complete set of contributions of scalar and tensor interactions beyond the Standard Model (SM). We argue if total contributions of scalar and tensor interactions beyond the SM (Phys. Rev. D88, 073002 (2013); arXiv: 1212.0332v4 [hep-ph]) will not be able to reconcile the experimental data on the correlation coefficients with the contributions of the ECVC effect, one may conclude that the charged weak hadronic vector current is not conserved in the hadronic transitions of weak processes with different masses of incoming and outgoing hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper tests the hypothesis that the charged weak hadronic vector current is exactly conserved in neutron beta decay, even though the neutron and proton have different masses. The authors argue that if the combined contributions of the Exact Conservation of the charged weak hadronic Vector Current (ECVC) effect and scalar/tensor interactions beyond the Standard Model cannot explain experimental correlation coefficient data, then the charged weak hadronic vector current is not conserved in hadronic transitions with different incoming and outgoing hadron masses.

## Method

The authors calculated the contributions of the phenomenological ECVC term, as introduced by Leitner et al. (2006), to the correlation coefficients in neutron beta decay with a polarized neutron and unpolarized proton and electron. They simultaneously included a complete set of contributions from scalar and tensor interactions beyond the Standard Model, following the formalism of earlier work (2013). The analysis compares these combined theoretical contributions against experimental data on correlation coefficients to test the conservation hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- The paper investigates neutron beta decay (n -> p + e- + antineutrino) with a polarized neutron and unpolarized proton and electron.
- The ECVC effect is calculated for the neutron beta decay system, where the neutron and proton have different masses (neutron mass approximately 939.6 MeV/c^2, proton mass approximately 938.3 MeV/c^2).
- No specific numerical values for correlation coefficients or coupling constants are provided in the available text.

## Context

This work extends the earlier ECVC framework developed by Leitner et al. (2006) for weak processes with different incoming and outgoing hadron masses, and incorporates the beyond-Standard-Model scalar and tensor interaction formalism from work published in 2013. The paper enables experimental tests of whether the charged weak hadronic vector current is conserved in neutron beta decay, which would have implications for the Standard Model description of weak interactions. If the combined ECVC and beyond-Standard-Model contributions cannot match experimental correlation coefficient data, it would suggest a fundamental violation of vector current conservation in hadronic weak transitions.
