# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering, Direct Reaction, and Fusion Cross Sections for the 9Be + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies

> Based on the extended optical model approach in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed for elastic scattering, DR, and fusion cross section data for the $^{9}$Be+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. Similar $χ^{2}$ analyses are also performed by only taking into account the elastic scattering and fusion data as was previously done by the present authors, and the results are compared with those of the full analysis including the DR cross section data as well. We find that the analyses using only elastic scattering and fusion data can produce very consistent and reliable predictions of cross sections particularly when the DR cross section data are not complete. Discussions are also given on the results obtained from similar analyses made earlier for the $^{9}$Be+$^{209}$Bi system.

# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering, Direct Reaction, and Fusion Cross Sections for the 9Be + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that simultaneous χ² analyses using only elastic scattering and fusion data (the EF approach) can produce very consistent and reliable predictions of cross sections for the ⁹Be+²⁰⁸Pb system, particularly when direct reaction (DR) cross section data are not complete. However, the EF approach relies on a semi-experimental DR cross section extracted from the elastic scattering fit, and the overall fit quality depends on the assumption that missing reaction channels (not included in the experimental DR data) are accounted for.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous χ² analyses within the extended optical model framework, where the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts. Two types of analyses were carried out: the EDF approach using experimental elastic scattering, DR, and fusion cross section data, and the EF approach using only elastic scattering and fusion data together with a semi-experimental DR cross section extracted from the elastic scattering fit. The optical potential included a bare nuclear potential, a volume-type fusion potential, and a surface-derivative-type DR potential, with energy-dependent strength parameters constrained by the dispersion relation. The χ² fitting was done in two steps: first varying all four strength parameters (V_D, W_D, V_F, W_F), then fixing V_D and W_D from the first step and performing a two-parameter search for V_F and W_F.

## Key numbers

- Experimental threshold energies: E_{0,D}^{exp} = 30.0 MeV for direct reaction, E_{0,F}^{exp} = 35.0 MeV for fusion, extracted from linear fits to S_i(E) = sqrt(E σ_i)
- Semi-experimental DR threshold energy: E_{0,D}^{semi-exp} = 32.5 MeV
- Fusion cross section at E_cm = 36.4 MeV: σ_F^{exp} = 10 mb; at E_cm = 47.9 MeV: σ_F^{exp} = 580 mb
- Experimental DR cross section at E_cm = 36.4 MeV: σ_D^{exp} = 109 mb; at E_cm = 47.9 MeV: σ_D^{exp} = 410 mb
- Semi-experimental DR cross section at E_cm = 36.4 MeV: σ_D^{semi-exp} = 71 mb; at E_cm = 47.9 MeV: σ_D^{semi-exp} = 597 mb
- Theoretical suppression factor R^{th} = 0.82 (EF case) and 0.87 (EDF case) in the above-barrier region, compared with experimental R^{exp} = 0.79 (EF case) and 0.77 (EDF case)
- Revised suppression factors for ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi: R^{th} = 0.81, R^{exp} = 0.79
- Strong absorption radius: R_{sa} = 12.3 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extended optical model analyses (So et al., 2004) of the ⁶Li+²⁰⁸Pb and ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi systems, where only elastic scattering and fusion data were available. The present study takes advantage of newly available experimental DR cross section data for ⁹Be+²⁰⁸Pb (Woolliscroft et al., 2004) to validate the EF approach. The results confirm that the fusion potential exhibits the threshold anomaly characteristic of tightly bound projectiles (Baeza et al., 1998; Lilley et al., 1999; Fulton et al., 2000), while the DR potential does not show a pronounced threshold anomaly, explaining why the total polarization potential for loosely bound projectiles appears smooth. The work enables future measurements of inelastic scattering and other missing reaction channels to test whether the EF method remains valid when comprehensive DR data become available.
