# A netron halo in 8He

> The structure of $^8$He is investigated within a three-cluster microscopic model. The three-cluster configuration $α+^2n+^2n$ was used to describe the properties of the ground state of the nucleus. The obtained results evidently indicate the existence of a neutron halo in $^8$He.

## Key claim
The three-cluster microscopic model α+²n+²n, using the Algebraic Version of the Resonating Group Method with the Volkov potential, indicates the existence of a neutron halo in ⁸He, though the calculated binding energy is slightly less than the experimental value and the theoretical root-mean-square radii are a little larger than experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors used the Algebraic Version of the Resonating Group Method (AV RGM) with an oscillator basis expansion to solve the many-particle Schrödinger equation for ⁸He as a three-cluster system of an alpha particle and two dineutrons. They employed both a bioscillator basis (BO) and an SU(3) basis to classify basis functions, eliminated Pauli-forbidden states by diagonalizing the antisymmetrization operator matrix, and used the Volkov potential with the oscillator radius as the only free parameter, set to 1.51 fm to minimize the threshold energy of ⁸He decay into ⁴He and two dineutrons.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of ⁸He counted from the ⁴He+²n+²n threshold: -2.065 MeV (with the total basis of 399 Pauli-allowed states)
- Oscillator radius parameter: 1.51 fm
- Energy of the ⁴He+²n+²n threshold: -22.15 MeV
- Bound state energy of the alpha particle: -26.84 MeV
- Mass RMS radius (theory): 2.73 fm; experimental values: 2.37 ± 0.18 fm (Alkhazov 1991) and 2.52 ± 0.03 fm (Tanihata 1988)
- Neutron RMS radius (theory): 2.91 fm; experimental values: 2.50 ± 0.19 fm (Alkhazov 1991) and 2.64 ± 0.03 fm (Tanihata 1988)
- Proton RMS radius (theory): 2.08 fm; experimental values: 1.89 ± 0.17 fm (Alkhazov 1991) and 2.15 ± 0.02 fm (Tanihata 1988)
- Difference between neutron and proton RMS radii (theory): 0.84 fm
- Mean distance between two dineutrons: 2.33 fm
- Mean distance between alpha particle and center of mass of two dineutrons: 1.42 fm
- Contribution to wave function from basis states with μ=2: approximately 80%; from basis states with μ=0: 9%

## Context
This work extends earlier microscopic studies of neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, particularly ⁶He studied by Vasilevsky (1996, 1997) using similar AV RGM methods, and addresses the suggestion by Ogloblin (1991) that the neutron halo in ⁸He consists of four neutrons rather than a ⁶He core. The results enable comparison with other theoretical approaches such as the Refined Resonating Group Method (Hofman 1997) and Multi-configuration Shell Model (Navratil and Barrett 1998), and provide a basis for further three-cluster studies of halo nuclei.
