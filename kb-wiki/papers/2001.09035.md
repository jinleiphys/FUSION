# Shape coexistence in the neutron-deficient $^{188}$Hg investigated via lifetime measurements

> Shape coexistence in the $Z \approx 82$ region has been established in mercury, lead and polonium isotopes. Even-even mercury isotopes with $100 \leq N \leq 106$ present multiple fingerprints of this phenomenon, which seems to be no longer present for $N \geq 110$. According to a number of theoretical calculations, shape coexistence is predicted in the $^{188}$Hg isotope. The $^{188}$Hg nucleus was populated using two different fusion-evaporation reactions with two targets, $^{158}$Gd and $^{160}$Gd, and a beam of $^{34}$S, provided by the Tandem-ALPI accelerators complex at the Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro. The channels of interest were selected using the information from the Neutron Wall array, while the $γ$ rays were detected using the GALILEO $γ$-ray array. The lifetimes of the excited states were determined using the Recoil Distance Doppler-Shift method, employing the dedicated GALILEO plunger device. Using the two-bands mixing and rotational models, the deformation of the pure configurations was obtained from the experimental results. The extracted transition strengths were compared with those calculated with the state-of-the-art symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing (SCCM) and five-dimentional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) approaches in order to shed light on the nature of the observed structures in the $^{188}$Hg nucleus. An oblate, a normal- and a super-deformed prolate bands were predicted and their underlying shell structure was also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first lifetime measurements for states with spin >= 6 in 188Hg, confirming shape coexistence with an oblate ground-state band and a prolate intruder band, but the experimental uncertainties do not permit firm conclusions on the degree of band mixing, and the two beyond-mean-field calculations (SCCM and 5DCH) predict strongly different relative positions and mixing of the bands.

## Method

The 188Hg nucleus was populated via two fusion-evaporation reactions: 34S beam on 160Gd target at 185 MeV and on 158Gd target at 165 MeV, using the Tandem-ALPI accelerators at LNL. The channels of interest were selected using the Neutron Wall array for neutron detection, while gamma rays were detected with the GALILEO spectrometer. Lifetimes of excited states up to spin 16 hbar were measured using the Recoil Distance Doppler-Shift (RDDS) method with the GALILEO plunger device, and the Differential Decay Curve Method (DDCM) was applied to gamma-gamma coincidence data gated on in-flight components of feeding transitions.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the 2_1+ state: 25(2) ps from Exp.1 and 24(1) ps from Exp.2, yielding a weighted average consistent with literature values.
- Lifetime of the 4_1+ state: 1.9(8) ps, determined only from the Exp.2 dataset.
- Average velocity of the 188Hg evaporation residue: beta = 1.71(8)% for Exp.1 and beta = 1.59(1)% for Exp.2.
- Upper limit of 4 ps for the lifetime of the 12_2+ state, with an estimated value of approximately 2 ps from two data points.
- Target-stopper distances used: 20-600 micrometers in Exp.1 and 7-2000 micrometers in Exp.2.

## Context

This work extends lifetime measurements in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes beyond the low-spin yrast states previously studied via RDDS in 178Hg (PhysRevC.99.054325), 180,182Hg (Grahn 2009, Scheck 2010), and 184,186Hg (Rud 1973, Proetel 1974, Gaffney 2014), as well as via fast-timing in 186,188Hg (Joshi 1994, Olaizola 2019). The results are compared with state-of-the-art symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing (SCCM) and five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) calculations, and with the two-band mixing model previously applied to level energies in 180-188Hg by Gaffney (2014). The findings enable a more precise determination of deformation parameters and transition strengths for both the ground-state and intruder bands, and highlight discrepancies between theoretical predictions that need to be resolved by future measurements.
