# BRST Treatment of the Bohr Collective Hamiltonian at High Spins

> The BRST treatment of triaxial systems rotating at high spins is used to solve perturbatively the $γ$-independent Bohr collective hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The BRST treatment of the cranking model, which explicitly introduces collective coordinates and requires constraints and gauge conditions, is used to solve perturbatively the gamma-independent Bohr collective hamiltonian at high spins, with the aim of verifying that infrared divergencies have been properly solved and comparing perturbative results in the cranked basis with those in the laboratory system.

## Method

The authors formulate the Bohr collective hamiltonian in an overcomplete space consisting of five intrinsic variables plus three collective Euler angles, three Lagrange multipliers, and six ghost fermion variables. They construct the BRST hamiltonian, minimize it to obtain the cranking solution (deformation parameters and moment of inertia), and then construct normal modes from the quadratic terms. Perturbative corrections are calculated from the third- and fourth-order terms of the BRST hamiltonian, with diagrammatic contributions to the energies of the yrast state and the one-beta-phonon state.

## Key numbers

- The gamma-independent minimization yields gamma_o = pi/6, moment of inertia Im = 4 beta_o^2, and <p_x> = 2 Omega beta_o
- The wobbling motion frequency is omega_w = 3 Omega
- The gamma-vibration frequency is omega_gamma = 2 Omega
- The beta-vibration frequency is omega_beta = sqrt(8 V_1 + Im V_2)
- The dynamical moment of inertia Im_x = Im + 16 Omega^2 / V_2
- The perpendicular moments of inertia are Im_y = Im_z = beta_o^2
- The total correction to the yrast state energy is given by a formula involving terms proportional to 4/Im, 18I/(omega_beta Im^2), and various combinations of V_1, V_2, V_3, V_4, Im, and omega_beta
- The total correction to the one-beta-phonon state energy is given by a formula involving terms proportional to 36I/(omega_beta Im^2) and various combinations of V_1, V_2, V_3, V_4, Im, and omega_beta

## Context

This work extends the BRST treatment of triaxial systems at high spins developed by Kerman, Bohr, and Casten (1990) and the formalism for transition probabilities by Garrahan and Bes (1994). It provides the first perturbative calculation within the cranked basis for the Bohr collective hamiltonian, which was originally introduced by Bohr (1952). The results can be reproduced analytically in the laboratory frame using the conventional method of separation of variables from Bes (1958), and the approach enables future calculations with realistic systems, particularly for studying the problem of identical bands by measuring differences between moments of inertia of even and odd systems.
