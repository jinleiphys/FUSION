# The effects of strong magnetic fields on the neutron star structure: lowest order constrained variational calculations

> We investigate the effects of strong magnetic fields upon the gross properties of neutron and protoneutron stars. In our calculations, the neutron star matter was approximated by the pure neutron matter. Using the lowest order constrained variational approach at zero and finite temperatures, and employing $AV_{18}$ potential, we present the effects of strong magnetic fields on the gravitational mass, radius, and gravitational redshift of the neutron and protoneutron stars. It is found that the equation of state of neutron star becomes stiffer with increase of the magnetic field and temperature. This leads to larger values of the maximum mass and radius for the neutron stars.

## Key claim
The equation of state of neutron star matter becomes stiffer with increasing magnetic field and temperature, leading to larger maximum mass and radius; however, the effects of magnetic fields with magnitude \(B \leq 10^{18}\ G\) are almost negligible, and the calculations approximate neutron star matter by pure neutron matter.

## Method
The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method at zero and finite temperatures, employing the \(AV_{18}\) nuclear potential to compute the equation of state of pure neutron matter in the presence of strong magnetic fields. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations of general relativistic hydrostatic equilibrium to obtain the gravitational mass, radius, and gravitational redshift of neutron stars and protoneutron stars. For densities below \(0.05\ fm^{-3}\), they used the equation of state from Baym et al. (1971) for all magnetic fields and temperatures.

## Key numbers
- For a cold neutron star at \(B = 10^{19}\ G\), the maximum mass is about \(1.17\%\) larger than the cold non-magnetized one.
- For a protoneutron star at \(T = 15\ MeV\) and \(B = 10^{19}\ G\), the maximum mass increases about \(2.36\%\) compared to a cold non-magnetized one.
- For a cold neutron star, the maximum mass varies between \(1.69 M_{\odot}\) and \(1.71 M_{\odot}\) depending on the interior magnetic field; for a protoneutron star with \(T = 15\ MeV\), this variation is between \(1.70 M_{\odot}\) and \(1.73 M_{\odot}\).
- For the maximum magnetic field considered (\(10^{19}\ G\)), the maximum mass of a cold neutron star is \(1.71 M_{\odot}\) with radius \(9.16\ km\); for a protoneutron star at \(T = 15\ MeV\), the maximum mass is \(1.73 M_{\odot}\) with radius \(9.22\ km\).
- For a cold neutron star at \(B = 10^{19}\ G\), the maximum gravitational redshift is \(z_s^{max}=0.49\); for a hot neutron star at \(T = 15\ MeV\) with the same field, \(z_s^{max}=0.47\).
- For a cold neutron star, the maximum redshift values are \(0.56\), \(0.53\), and \(0.49\) for magnetic fields \(B = 0\), \(5\times10^{18}\), and \(10^{19}\ G\), respectively.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations of spin-polarized neutron matter in strong magnetic fields at zero temperature (Bordbar et al. 2010) and finite temperature (Bordbar and Rezaei 2011) to the structure of neutron stars and protoneutron stars. It contrasts with earlier relativistic mean-field studies such as Chakrabarty et al. (1997), who found decreasing maximum mass with increasing field, and Yuan and Zhang (1999), who found a softening of the equation of state with magnetic field. The results enable comparisons with observational mass-radius constraints from X-ray binaries and binary pulsar systems, and suggest that magnetic field effects are more important for protoneutron star maximum mass than for cold neutron stars.
