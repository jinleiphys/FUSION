# Parity-Violating $Δ$(1232) Electroweak Production: Axial Structure and New Physics

> We consider prospects for studying the parity-violating electroweak excitation of the $Δ(1232) $ resonance with polarized electron scattering (see nucl-th/9801025). We discuss the experimental feasibility and theoretical interpretability of such a measurement as well as the prospective implications for hadron structure theory. We also analyze the extent to which a PV $N\toΔ$ measurement could constrain various extensions of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in electroproduction of the Δ(1232) resonance can provide a clean probe of both hadron structure and physics beyond the Standard Model, but only if non-resonant backgrounds and axial contributions are understood at roughly 25-30% levels or better; a 1% measurement of the dominant term would constrain new physics comparably to atomic parity violation experiments.

## Method

The authors analyze the parity-violating asymmetry A_LR for N→Δ(1232) transition using longitudinally polarized electrons on unpolarized nucleons, where resonant multipoles cancel in the ratio. They decompose the asymmetry into three contributions: a vector-current term Δ^π_(1) containing no hadronic form factor dependence, a non-resonant isoscalar background term Δ^π_(2), and an axial-vector coupling term Δ^π_(3) proportional to the ratio C^A_5/C^V_3. Numerical estimates are made assuming a 1000-hour run with 100% polarized beam at luminosity 2×10^38 cm^−2 s^−1, 20 msr solid angle, and 0.2 GeV outgoing electron energy range.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry structure is given by A_LR = -(G_μ/√2)(|q^2|/(4πα))[Δ^π_(1) + Δ^π_(2) + Δ^π_(3)], where Δ^π_(1) = 2(1-2sin^2θ_W) contains no hadronic form factor dependence
- At θ=10° and beam energy ε=1 GeV, the background term Δ^π_(2)/Δ^π_(3) ≈ 6%, making background uncertainties negligible for axial term extraction
- The vector-current background contributes about 4-6% of the total asymmetry at kinematics suited for sin^2θ_W determination
- A 1% determination of Δ^π_(1) would provide constraints on new physics roughly comparable to atomic parity violation experiments
- A ~25% determination of the hadronic axial vector response could be carried out with realistic running times

## Context

This work extends the analysis of parity-violating electron scattering from nucleons to the Δ(1232) resonance, building on earlier studies of the PV asymmetry structure by the same authors (Pollock et al., 1998) and others. The pure isovector nature of the N→Δ transition eliminates uncertainties from nucleon strangeness content that complicate other PV measurements. The measurement would test the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation and its chiral corrections (Hammer, Hammer, and Meissner, 1996), and could provide constraints on leptoquarks, additional neutral gauge bosons, and fermion compositeness beyond the Standard Model.
