# Production and Thermal Equilibration of Partons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions: $\sqrt{s}= 20 vs. 200 A GeV$

> The production and thermalization of partons liberated in relativistic heavy ioncollisions is investigated within the parton cascade model. The momentum distribution of the partons near z=0 is found to become isotropic beyond about 1 fm/c after the overlap for the collision of lead nuclei at $\sqrt{s}=20 A GeV$ indicating a thermalization. At $\sqrt{s}=200 A GeV$ this is attained by about 0.5 fm/c after the complete overlap.

## Key claim
The parton cascade model suggests that partonic matter produced in lead-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s}=20$ A GeV becomes isotropic (indicating thermalization) at about 1 fm/c after complete nuclear overlap, while at $\sqrt{s}=200$ A GeV this isotropy is attained by about 0.5 fm/c after complete overlap. The claim is hedged by the model's dependence on the cutoff parameters $p_0$ and $Q_0$, and by the fact that only "real" partons (not space-like ones) are included in the analysis.

## Method
The study uses the parton cascade model (Geiger and Muller, 1992) with Monte Carlo transport simulations of quark and gluon interactions in six-dimensional phase space, based on perturbative QCD. The author simulates central collisions of sulfur and lead nuclei at two energies, tracking the longitudinal and rapidity distributions of real partons, and examines the time evolution of the average absolute momenta $|p_x|$, $|p_y|$, and $|p_z|$ in a central slice $|z| \leq 0.5$ fm to assess isotropy. The cutoff $p_0$ for dividing soft and (semi)hard scatterings is set to 1.12 GeV at 20 A GeV and 2.09 GeV at 200 A GeV, with structure function initialization scales $Q_0$ of 1.32 and 2.35 GeV respectively.

## Key numbers
- At $\sqrt{s}=20$ A GeV for Pb+Pb collisions: isotropy of momentum distributions near $z=0$ is attained at about 1 fm/c after complete overlap.
- At $\sqrt{s}=200$ A GeV for Pb+Pb collisions: isotropy is attained by about 0.5 fm/c after complete overlap.
- At $\sqrt{s}=20$ A GeV for Pb+Pb collisions: up to 4 partons per participating nucleon are produced, spread over $|y| \leq 2$.
- At $\sqrt{s}=200$ A GeV for S+S collisions: up to 6 partons per participating nucleon are produced, spread over $|y| \leq 2.5$.
- At $\sqrt{s}=200$ A GeV for Pb+Pb collisions: up to 9 partons per nucleon may be produced.
- At $\sqrt{s}=20$ A GeV for S+S collisions: about 2 partons per participating nucleon are produced, spread over $|y| \leq 2$.
- The cutoff $p_0$ is 1.12 GeV for 20 A GeV and 2.09 GeV for 200 A GeV.
- The structure function initialization scale $Q_0$ is 1.32 GeV at 20 A GeV and 2.35 GeV at 200 A GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier parton cascade model studies by Geiger (1995) on thermalization at RHIC and LHC energies, providing a direct comparison between CERN SPS (20 A GeV) and BNL RHIC (200 A GeV) energies with improved statistics. The results enable a practical interface to hydrodynamical evolution models, as suggested by the author, by providing a thermalized initial condition at about 0.5-1 fm/c. The approach contrasts with earlier estimates by Kari (1995) that considered only first-scattering gluon radiation without multiple scatterings, which the present work shows are essential for achieving and maintaining thermal equilibrium.
