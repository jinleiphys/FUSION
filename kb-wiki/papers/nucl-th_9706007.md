# Deformed Strangelets at Finite Temperature

> Considering the confinement of massless $u$ and $d$ quarks and massive $s$ quarks in an axially symmetric quadrupole shaped deformable bag, the stability properties of color-singlet strangelets at finite temperatures are studied for the baryon number $A \leq$ 100. It is found that, in general, at each $A$ the energy of the strangelet is the lowest for the spherical shape. However, it is interesting to find shell structures for the deformed shapes at some new $A$ values, not found for the spherical shape. This should be of relevance for the experimental search of strangelets. The color projected (singlet) calculation shows that even for the deformed shapes the shell structures vanish only for the temperature, $T \ga$ 30 MeV. On the other hand, from a somewhat crude estimate we also find that at a fixed entropy per baryon (instead of a fixed temperature) of the order of unity or higher these shell structures disappear.

# Deformed Strangelets at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

For baryon numbers A ≤ 100, the spherical shape always yields the lowest energy per baryon at both zero and finite temperature, but deformed shapes (prolate and oblate) exhibit shell structures at new baryon numbers not seen for spherical shapes, which may be relevant for experimental searches of strangelets. The color-singlet constraint preserves these shell structures up to temperatures T ≥ 30 MeV, while at fixed entropy per baryon of order unity or higher the shell structures disappear.

## Method

The authors use an axially symmetric quadrupole-deformable MIT bag model, computing single-particle quark eigenmodes in a spheroidal cavity parameterized by a deformation parameter D (D=1 for spherical, D>1 for prolate, D<1 for oblate). They construct color-singlet and color-unprojected partition functions at T=1 MeV and T=20 MeV, then minimize the free energy with respect to the bag radius R₀ at fixed baryon number A and deformation D, using B¹/⁴=145 MeV, massless u and d quarks, and massive s quarks (m_s=150 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For spherical shape (D=1.0) at T=1 MeV, shell closures occur at A=6 (pocket depth 6.30 MeV), A=14 (0.17 MeV), A=18 (4.37 MeV), A=36 (2.17 MeV), A=44 (0.8 MeV), and A=70 (2.36 MeV)
- For prolate shape D=2.0 at T=1 MeV, shell closures appear at A=14 (5.00 MeV), A=36 (1.34 MeV), and A=72 (0.40 MeV)
- For oblate shape D=0.6 at T=1 MeV, shell closures appear at A=14 (3.40 MeV), A=40 (0.43 MeV), and A=76 (0.45 MeV)
- At T=20 MeV with color projection (CP), shell structure appears at A=4 for D=1.0 (pocket depth 6.0 MeV), D=1.2 (4.7 MeV), and D=1.6 (0.2 MeV); for D=2.0 a shell appears at A=15 (0.6 MeV) with n_s ≈ 7
- For massless s quarks (m_s=0) at T=20 MeV with CP, shell at A=6 has depth 27.11 MeV (D=1.0), 22.34 MeV (D=1.4), and 16.31 MeV (D=2.0)
- Shell structures vanish for temperatures T ≥ 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the spherical strangelet calculations of Gilson and Jaffe (1993) and the finite-temperature spherical studies by Mustafa and Ansari (1996, 1997) to include quadrupole deformation degrees of freedom. The single-particle energies in deformed cavities follow the formalism of Viollier, Chin, and Kerman (1983). The results enable experimental guidance for strangelet searches in heavy-ion collisions, particularly regarding which baryon numbers might yield enhanced stability through shell effects in deformed configurations, and suggest that fixed-entropy conditions (relevant to heavy-ion collision environments) may suppress shell structures compared to fixed-temperature scenarios.
