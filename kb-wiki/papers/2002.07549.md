# Vorticity and Spin Polarization --- A Theoretical Perspective

> We give a theoretical perspective on the vorticity and spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions. We discuss the recent progress in spin hydrodynamics and spin kinetic theory. We also discuss other effects caused by vorticity including the chiral vortical effect and rotation-induced phase transitions.

## Key claim

This paper provides a theoretical perspective on vorticity and spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, arguing that while the global Lambda polarization data strongly support the vorticity interpretation, the differential polarization measurements (particularly the azimuthal-angle dependence) show features inconsistent with the equilibrium vorticity picture, creating a "spin sign problem" that requires going beyond the primitive equilibrium treatment.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical frameworks for describing vorticity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including kinematic vorticity and thermal vorticity definitions, and surveys numerical simulations using UrQMD, HIJING, AMPT, PICR hydrodynamic, and UrQMD+vHLLE hybrid models to compute Lambda polarization. It then presents the formal development of spin hydrodynamics (first-order and Israel-Stewart-type theories treating spin chemical potential as a dynamical variable) and spin kinetic theory (based on Wigner function formalism for Dirac fermions with a systematic expansion in Planck's constant), along with discussions of the chiral vortical effect and rotation-induced phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- Global angular momentum estimate: J ~ sqrt(s) * A * b / 2 ~ 10^6 hbar for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 10 fm
- Vorticity strength: typical values ~ 10^21 - 10^22 s^-1, consistent with experimental extraction from STAR (2017)
- Feed-down suppression of primary Lambda polarization: approximately 10% from higher-lying hadron decays
- The initial y-component of kinematic vorticity at mid-rapidity first increases with sqrt(s) above approximately 2 * m_N (nucleon mass), then decreases at higher energy

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Liang and Wang (2004), Becattini (2013), and others who first proposed the spin-orbit coupling mechanism for vorticity-induced polarization in heavy-ion collisions. It directly addresses the discrepancy between theoretical calculations (which predict f_{2z} < 0 and g_{2y} < 0) and the STAR Collaboration measurements (2018, 2019) showing f_{2z} > 0 and g_{2y} > 0. The paper enables future numerical applications of spin hydrodynamics to heavy-ion collisions and motivates further development of collision terms in spin kinetic theory, as well as experimental searches for chiral vortical waves in the RHIC beam energy scan phase II program.
