# System-size dependence of $γ$-jet modifications in heavy-ion collisions

> Medium modifications of the $γ$-triggered jets are investigated with the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model in heavy-ion collisions with varying system sizes, focusing on centrality dependence in Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC. Our numerical results reveal that jets produced in central collisions exhibit a wider transverse asymmetry ($A_N^y$) distribution, a broader jet shape, and a more pronounced $γ$-jet transverse momentum imbalance ($X_{Jγ}=p_T^{\rm jet}/p_T^γ$) compared to peripheral collisions. These effects arise from the longer path length and stronger jet-medium interactions in central collisions, leading to enhanced jet quenching and medium response. Our findings demonstrate that the magnitude of $γ$-jet modifications is sensitive to the size and centrality of the collision system, with larger systems inducing more significant alterations due to increased energy loss and medium feedback.

## Key claim
Using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, the authors show that gamma-jet modifications in heavy-ion collisions depend systematically on both collision centrality and nuclear species, with central Pb+Pb collisions producing the strongest jet quenching and medium response, while peripheral Xe+Xe collisions show the weakest effects. The abstract's claim of "sensitivity to the size and centrality" is conditioned on the specific choice of jet cone size R=0.3, trigger photon pT above 60 GeV/c, and jet pT above 30 GeV/c.

## Method
The authors simulate gamma-jet events using PYTHIA8 for initial parton showers, the TRENTo model for initial energy density distributions, and (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (CLVisc) for QGP medium evolution. Jet propagation through the medium is modeled with the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic scattering and medium-induced gluon radiation via the high-twist approach, as well as medium response through recoil and "negative" partons. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-kt algorithm using FastJet, and observables are computed for four centrality intervals (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-90%) in both Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.44 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Trigger photon pT threshold: pT_gamma > 60 GeV/c for both collision systems
- Jet cone size: R=0.3, with jet pT threshold: pT_jet > 30 GeV/c
- Associated parton pT threshold: pT_assoc > 1 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity cuts: |eta_gamma| < 1.44, |eta_jet| < 1.6
- Azimuthal angle difference cut: |Delta_phi_jgamma| > (7/8)pi
- Hydrodynamic initial time: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c; freeze-out temperature: T_f = 137 MeV
- Collision energies: Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, Xe+Xe at sqrt(s_NN)=5.44 TeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous 2D jet tomography study (Xiao 2024) which focused on 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions, by systematically comparing centrality-dependent gamma-jet modifications across two different collision systems. The study uses the same LBT framework previously applied to single hadron suppression (Cao 2016), gamma-hadron correlations (Chen 2017), and gamma-jet correlations (Luo 2018). The results enable future experimental comparisons at the LHC to constrain the jet transport coefficient hat-q and medium response dynamics across different system sizes, and complement prior work on system-size dependence of jet quenching (ATLAS 2010, ALICE 2010, Zakharov 2018).
