# Collectivity in the light Xenon isotopes: A shell model study

> The lightest Xenon isotopes are studied in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model (ISM). The valence space comprises all the orbits lying between the magic closures N=Z=50 and N=Z=82. The calculations produce collective deformed structures of triaxial nature that encompass nicely the known experimental data. Predictions are made for the (still unknown) N=Z nucleus 108-Xe. The results are interpreted in terms of the competition between the quadrupole correlations enhanced by the pseudo-SU(3) structure of the positive parity orbits and the pairing correlations brought in by the 0h11/2 orbit. We have studied as well the effect of the excitations from the 100-Sn core on our predictions. We show that the backbending in this region is due to the alignment of two particles in the 0h11/2 orbit. In the N=Z case, one neutron and one proton align to J=11 and T=0. In 110-Xe and 112-Xe the alignment begins in the J=10 T=1 channel and it is dominantly of neutron neutron type. Approaching the band termination the alignment of a neutron and a proton to J=11 and T=0 takes over. In a more academic mood, we have explored the role of the isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations on the structure on the yrast bands of 108-Xe and 110-Xe and examined the role of the isovector and isoscalar pairing condensates in these N~Z nuclei.

## Key claim
The light Xenon isotopes (108-112Xe) exhibit collective deformed structures of triaxial nature that can be explained microscopically within the Interacting Shell Model using a valence space between N=Z=50 and N=Z=82, without requiring extensive excitations of the 100Sn core, contrary to suggestions in prior experimental work by Sandzelius et al. (2007).

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the effective interaction GCN50:82, derived from a realistic G-matrix based on the Bonn-C potential and fitted to approximately 400 experimental excitation energies from 80 nuclei in the region, achieving a root mean square deviation of 110 keV. The valence space (r4h) includes all orbits between the magic closures at N=Z=50 and N=Z=82, with basis dimensions reaching up to approximately 10^10 Slater determinants. The calculations were extended to include 1p-1h and 2p-2h excitations from the 0g9/2 orbit to test the effect of core excitations from 100Sn.

## Key numbers
- The effective interaction GCN50:82 achieves a root mean square deviation of 110 keV after corrections to the monopole, pairing, and multipole Hamiltonians.
- For 110Xe, the calculated intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 is approximately 200 e fm^2, corresponding to beta = 0.16, with B(E2)(2+ -> 0+) = 1005 e^2 fm^4.
- For 108Xe, the predicted B(E2)(2+ -> 0+) is 888 e^2 fm^4, with Q0 = 200 e fm^2 and beta = 0.16.
- For 112Xe, the calculated B(E2)(2+ -> 0+) is 1063 e^2 fm^4, with Q0 = 217 e fm^2 and beta = 0.17.
- The pseudo-SU3 model predicts Q0(108Xe) = 210 e fm^2 and Q0(110-116Xe) = 225 e fm^2, corresponding to B(E2)(2+ -> 0+) of 870 e^2 fm^4 and 1000 e^2 fm^4 respectively.
- Including 1p-1h core excitations from the 0g9/2 orbit increases intrinsic quadrupole moments by 15% for 108Xe, 17% for 110Xe, and 20% for 112Xe; adding 2p-2h excitations raises these to 19% and 21% for 108Xe and 110Xe respectively.
- The triaxiality parameter gamma is 20 degrees for 110Xe and 24 degrees for 108Xe, derived from B(E2) ratios.
- Removing the intruder 0h11/2 orbit from the valence space reduces the moment of inertia by 30% and reduces triaxiality to gamma = 12 degrees for 110Xe.
- Removing the isovector J=0 pairing increases the moment of inertia by a factor of two and delays backbending by two units of angular momentum in 108Xe.

## Context
This work extends the Interacting Shell Model description of collective behavior previously applied to nuclei such as 48Cr and 50Cr in the pf-shell (Caurier et al., 1995; Caurier et al., 1996; Lenzi et al., 1996) and to 24Mg and 26Mg in the sd-shell, where Elliott's SU(3) symmetry explains the onset of collectivity. The calculations contradict the suggestion by Sandzelius et al. (2007) that large depletion of the doubly magic 100Sn core is needed to explain the rotational features observed in 110Xe. The results enable predictions for the unmeasured N=Z nucleus 108Xe and provide a framework for understanding the competition between quadrupole correlations (enhanced by pseudo-SU3 structure) and pairing correlations (from the 0h11/2 orbit) in this region, as well as clarifying the role of isovector versus isoscalar pairing in N approximately equal to Z nuclei.
