# Pion masses in quasiconformal gauge field theories

> We study modifications to Weinberg-like sum rules in quasiconformal gauge field theories. Beyond the two Weinberg sum rules and the oblique S parameter we study the pion mass and the X parameter. Especially, we evaluate the pion mass for walking technicolour theories, in particular also minimal walking technicolour, and find contributions of the order of up to several hundred GeV.

# Pion masses in quasiconformal gauge field theories

## Key claim
The electroweak contributions to pion masses in walking technicolour theories are enhanced compared to running theories, with contributions of up to several hundred GeV, but this enhancement depends on the scale parameter lambda lying between the continuum threshold and the conformal scale, and on the specific values of the vector and axial-vector resonance masses.

## Method
The authors modify the second Weinberg sum rule for quasiconformal (walking) gauge theories by adding a continuum contribution Delta(s) to the spectral function, parameterized by a positive constant alpha of order one and the dimension d_R of the fermion representation. They then use the first and modified second sum rules to eliminate the decay constants F_V and F_A from expressions for the S parameter, X parameter, and the overall magnitude m^2 of the pion mass matrix, expressing these quantities in terms of the resonance masses M_V and M_A, the pion decay constant f_pi, and the continuum parameters.

## Key numbers
- For minimal walking technicolour (MWT) in the light mass window (M_A < M_V less than or approximately equal to 1 TeV), pion masses range from 50 to 300 GeV.
- For the heavy mass window of MWT (M_A > M_V greater than or approximately equal to 2 TeV), all pion masses are at least 150 GeV.
- The continuum threshold for MWT is estimated as 2*Sigma(0) approximately equal to 4*pi*f_pi/d_R, which evaluates to circa 1 TeV.
- Reference values for the scale lambda are chosen as 6 TeV and 20 TeV in the contour plots.
- The electroweak scale Lambda_ew = 246 GeV sets f_pi^2 = 2*Lambda_ew^2/N_f^g where N_f^g is the number of techniflavours gauged under electroweak interactions.
- For MWT with y=0, the mass factors hat_m^2 are 1.0 for Pi_UU and Pi_DD, and 0.53 for Pi_UD; with y=+1 they are 2.87, 1.0, and 1.47 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Appelquist (1998) on modified Weinberg sum rules in quasiconformal theories, who showed that the second sum rule is modified while the first remains unchanged, and that the S parameter is reduced. The authors study the X parameter from Barbieri (2004) and the electroweak contributions to pion masses from Das (1967) and Peskin (1980). For MWT, they use the generators from Dietrich (2005) and Foadi (2007). The results enable phenomenological constraints on walking technicolour models by showing that technipion masses can exceed present experimental bounds without additional extended technicolour interactions, except in the region of small M_A and g.
