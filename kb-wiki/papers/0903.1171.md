# Energy weighted sum rules for mesons in hot and dense matter

> We study energy weighted sum rules of the pion and kaon propagator in nuclear matter at finite temperature. The sum rules are obtained from matching the Dyson form of the meson propagator with its spectral Lehmann representation at low and high energies. We calculate the sum rules for specific models of the kaon and pion self-energy. The in-medium spectral densities of the K and anti-K mesons are obtained from a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels which incorporates the S- and P-waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction. The pion self-energy is determined from the P-wave coupling to particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations, modified by short range correlations. The sum rules for the lower energy weights are fulfilled satisfactorily and reflect the contributions from the different quasi-particle and collective modes of the meson spectral function. We discuss the sensitivity of the sum rules to the distribution of spectral strength and their usefulness as quality tests of model calculations.

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum rules (EWSRs) for kaon and pion propagators in hot and dense nuclear matter are derived and tested against specific chiral unitary models; the sum rules are fulfilled satisfactorily for lower energy weights, but the violation of crossing symmetry in the chiral model for kaon-nucleon interactions causes the zero-mode propagator values for K and anti-K to differ, indicating a model limitation that is less severe for the antikaon.

## Method

The authors derived EWSRs by matching the Dyson form of the meson propagator with its spectral Lehmann representation at high energies, order by order in 1/q^0, for both zero and finite temperature. They then computed the left-hand sides of the sum rules by integrating the in-medium spectral functions of K, anti-K, and pions obtained from specific self-energy models: a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels for kaons (including S- and P-waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction) and a P-wave coupling to particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations with short-range correlations for pions. The saturation of each sum rule was examined as a function of the upper integration limit up to 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The m_0^{(+)} sum rule (integral of omega times sum of K and anti-K spectral functions) saturates to exactly 1, independent of meson momentum, nuclear density, or temperature.
- The anti-K effective mass is lowered by about 50 MeV in cold nuclear matter at saturation density (rho_0); at T=100 MeV, this attraction is reduced to 50% of its zero-temperature value.
- The P-wave contribution to the anti-K optical potential becomes significant for momenta larger than 200 MeV/c.
- The loop integrals for the kaon self-energy are regularized by a cut-off momentum of q_max = 630 MeV/c, limiting the model's reliability to center-of-mass energies up to about 2 GeV (q^0 ~ 1 GeV).
- The m_{-1} sum rule for anti-K at zero temperature and rho_0 is well satisfied for momenta of 0, 150, and 450 MeV/c; at T=100 MeV, the saturation behavior changes due to thermal broadening.

## Context

This work extends the use of energy-weighted sum rules, previously applied to nucleon spectral functions in nuclear matter by Polls (1994) and Frick (2004) and to omega mesons by Dutt-Mazumder (2002), to the case of kaons and pions in hot and dense matter. The sum rules provide a consistency test for the chiral unitary approach to kaon-nucleon interactions developed by Tolos (2006, 2008) and the pion self-energy model of Oset (1989). The results enable future applications of EWSRs to test other meson-nucleon interaction models and to constrain the behavior of meson propagators in heavy-ion collision simulations.
