# Chiral symmetry breaking and chemical equilibrium in a heavy-ion collisions

> We examine the thermalization of an ensemble of the octet of pseudoscalar mesons, in the isospin symmetric limit, whose interactions are constrained through chiral symmetry, unitarity, and measurements. The reaction amplitudes generate all resonances up to masses of about 2 GeV, with twelve input parameters, namely f_pi, three masses, and eight low energy constants (LECs) of chiral perturbation theory. In linear response theory, we find that matter takes an extremely long time to thermalize. These long relaxation times are directly related to the fact that these mesons are pseudo-Goldstone bosons of chiral symmetry breaking. This result indicates that fireballs created with zero baryon number in heavy-ion collisions will drop out of chemical equilibrium once they enter the chiral symmetry broken phase.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a gas of pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, and eta) constrained by chiral symmetry, the chemical relaxation times are extremely long (tens to hundreds of fm), implying that fireballs created in heavy-ion collisions with zero baryon number will drop out of chemical equilibrium once they enter the chiral symmetry broken phase; this conclusion is conditional on the neglect of baryons and the assumption that phase space densities remain much less than unity.

## Method

The authors constructed a linear-response transport theory for the SU(3) octet of pseudoscalar mesons in the isospin symmetric limit, using unitarized two-to-two scattering amplitudes from chiral perturbation theory (GomezNicola 2001). The reaction matrix was reduced from 4x4 to 2x2 by enforcing conservation of net strangeness and total particle number, and its eigenvalues were computed to obtain the chemical relaxation times as a function of temperature. The input parameters were the three meson masses (m_pi, m_K, m_eta), the pion decay constant f_pi, and eight low-energy constants (LECs) of chiral perturbation theory, with uncertainties propagated via Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

* The slowest relaxation time (tau_s) is about 100 fm at a temperature of 150 MeV, increasing to about 1000 fm at 100 MeV.
* The fast relaxation time (tau_f) is about 10 fm at 150 MeV, increasing to about 100 fm at 100 MeV.
* The freeze-out temperature from resonance gas models is T_fo = 158.4 +/- 1.4 MeV (Andronic 2017).
* The chiral crossover temperature from lattice QCD is T_co = 156.5 +/- 1.5 MeV (Aoki 2009, Bazavov 2018).
* The dimensionless ratio Pi_s = tau_s(T) * n_eq(m_pi,T) * m_pi^2 / f_pi^4 ranges from 4 to 8 over the temperature range studied.
* The dimensionless ratio Pi_f = tau_f(T) * n_eq(m_eta,T) * m_pi^2 / f_pi^4 ranges from 0.5 to 1 over the same temperature range.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Prakash (1993) to use chiral perturbation theory for transport in hadron gas, but improves upon that work by using unitarized amplitudes (GomezNicola 2001) that capture the full resonance spectrum up to 2 GeV without ad-hoc parametrizations. The results contradict the common assumption that hadronic matter quickly reaches chemical equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions, and instead suggest that the chiral symmetry breaking mechanism itself causes chemical freezeout to occur near the crossover temperature. The authors note that baryons are neglected and plan to include them in future work, and that the formalism assumes phase space densities much less than unity.
