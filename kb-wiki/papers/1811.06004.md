# QCD at nonzero isospin asymmetry

> We study the phase diagram and the thermodynamic properties of QCD at nonzero isospin asymmetry at physical quark masses with staggered quarks. In particular, continuum results for the phase boundary between the normal and the pion condensation phases and the chiral/deconfinement transition are presented. Our findings indicate that the pion condensation phase is restricted to $T\lesssim170$~MeV for isospin chemical potentials up to 325~MeV. We also use the data to test the range of validity of the Taylor expansion method and show first results for the equation of state.

## Key claim

The pion condensation phase in QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential is restricted to temperatures below approximately 170 MeV for isospin chemical potentials up to 325 MeV, with the phase boundary determined via continuum-extrapolated lattice simulations using a novel regulator extrapolation method.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 dynamical quark flavors at physical quark masses using staggered quarks with a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action and two steps of stout smearing. They introduced a pionic source term as an infrared regulator and developed a two-step improvement program based on singular value decomposition of the Dirac operator to extrapolate observables to the vanishing regulator limit. The phase boundaries were determined from the renormalized pion and quark condensates, with continuum extrapolations performed using data from temporal extents Nt = 8, 10, and 12.

## Key numbers

- The pion condensation phase is restricted to T less than or approximately 170 MeV for isospin chemical potentials up to 325 MeV
- The pseudo-triple point where the BEC phase boundary and chiral crossover transition meet occurs at mu_I = mu_pt and T = T_pt (specific numerical values not given in the text)
- For T = 124 MeV, the BEC phase boundary occurs at mu_I,c approximately equal to m_pi/2
- For T = 176 MeV, the data lies above the BEC phase boundary and Taylor expansion agrees with lattice data up to mu_I/m_pi approximately 0.6 at leading order and approximately 1.6 at next-to-leading order
- The continuum extrapolation of the BEC phase boundary for mu_I > 120 MeV used a fit of the form d_1 + d_2/mu_I^2 with a^2-dependent coefficients

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous continuum phase diagram results (Brandt 2017) from mu_I ≤ 120 MeV to mu_I ≤ 325 MeV, building on initial studies of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential by Kogut (2002, 2004), de Forcrand (2007), Detmold (2012), and Endrodi (2014). The results provide a testbed for the Taylor expansion method commonly used to overcome the complex action problem at nonzero baryon chemical potential, and enable first calculations of the equation of state at pure isospin chemical potential relevant for neutron stars and the early universe.
