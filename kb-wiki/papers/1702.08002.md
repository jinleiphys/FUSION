# Fully coupled-channel complex scaling method for the $K^-pp$ system

> We have developed a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) for the study of the most essential kaonic nucleus, $``K^-pp,"$ which is a resonant state of a $\bar{K}NN$-$πΣN$-$πΛN$ coupled-channel system based on a theoretical viewpoint. By employing the ccCSM and imposing the correct boundary condition of resonance, the coupled-channel problem is completely solved using a phenomenological energy-independent potential. As a result of the ccCSM calculation of $``K^-pp,"$ in which all three channels are treated explicitly, we have obtained three-body resonance as a Gamow state. The resonance pole indicates that the binding energy of $``K^-pp"$ and the half value of its mesonic decay width are 51 MeV and 16 MeV, respectively. In the analysis of the resonant wave function obtained using the ccCSM, we clarify the spatial configuration and channel compositions of $``K^-pp."$ Compared with past studies of single-channel calculations based on effective $\bar{K}N$ potentials, the current study provides a guideline for the determination of the $\bar{K}N$ energy to be used in effective potentials.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) calculation, treating all three channels ($\bar{K}NN$, $\pi\Sigma N$, $\pi\Lambda N$) explicitly with an energy-independent phenomenological potential, yields a genuine three-body resonance pole for the $K^-pp$ system, with the caveat that this result is specific to the AY potential employed and may differ when using chiral SU(3)-based energy-dependent potentials.

## Method

The authors developed a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) for the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi\Sigma N$-$\pi\Lambda N$ three-body system with quantum numbers $J^\pi=0^-$, $T=1/2$, $T_z=1/2$. The wave function was expanded in correlated Gaussian basis functions across 8 channels (2 $\bar{K}NN$, 4 $\pi\Sigma N$, 2 $\pi\Lambda N$), and the complex-scaled Hamiltonian matrix was diagonalized to obtain resonance eigenvalues that are independent of the scaling angle $\theta$. The Hamiltonian included the Argonne v18 $NN$ potential and the AY phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potential, with $YN$ and $\pi N$ potentials neglected as minor contributions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^-pp$: $B_{K^-pp} = 51$ MeV, measured from the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold
- Half mesonic decay width: $\Gamma_{\pi YN}/2 = 16$ MeV
- Mean $NN$ distance in the $\bar{K}NN$ channel: $R_{NN} = 1.86 + i0.14$ fm, corresponding to a density estimated at $1.7\rho_0$ (15% decrease from normal nuclear matter)
- Mean $\bar{K}N$ distance for $I=0$ component: $R_{\bar{K}N(I=0)} = 1.40 - i0.01$ fm
- Mean $\bar{K}N$ distance for $I=1$ component: $R_{\bar{K}N(I=1)} = 1.95 + i0.14$ fm
- Norm of $\bar{K}NN$ component: $1.004 - i0.286$; norm of $\pi\Sigma N$ component: $-0.002 + i0.276$; norm of $\pi\Lambda N$ component: $-0.002 + i0.010$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ccCSM+Feshbach method (Dote et al., 2016), which reduced the coupled-channel problem to a single $\bar{K}NN$ channel via Feshbach projection, by treating all three channels explicitly for the first time. The results contradict single-channel calculations using effective $\bar{K}N$ potentials with the Particle-picture ansatz (Dote et al., 2016) and support the Field-picture ansatz instead. The fully coupled-channel calculation provides a benchmark for determining the $\bar{K}N$ energy in effective-potential approaches and enables future studies with chiral SU(3)-based potentials, which may reveal a double-pole structure of $K^-pp$ as suggested by earlier Faddeev-AGS calculations (Ikeda et al., 2013).
