# Recombination of shower partons in fragmentation processes

> We develop the notion of shower partons and determine their distributions in jets in the framework of the recombination model. The shower parton distributions obtained render a good fit of the fragmentation functions. We then illustrate the usefulness of the distributions in a problem where a jet is produced in the environment of thermal partons as in heavy-ion collisions. The recombination of shower and thermal partons is shown to be more important than fragmentation. Application to the study of two-particle correlation in a jet is also carried out.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the notion of shower parton distributions (SPDs) and shows that fragmentation functions can be reproduced by the recombination of two shower partons, but the real utility of SPDs emerges only when shower partons recombine with thermal partons from the surrounding medium in heavy-ion collisions, where this thermal-shower recombination is claimed to be more important than fragmentation for transverse momenta below 8 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors treat fragmentation as a recombination process, writing the fragmentation function as an integral over the joint distribution of a quark and antiquark (assumed factorizable into two shower parton distributions) times a recombination function. They determine five shower parton distributions (valence, sea, and gluon-initiated for light quarks, plus two strange-quark distributions) by solving integral equations that relate them to five phenomenological fragmentation functions from the BKK parameterization at fixed \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\). The recombination functions for pions and kaons are taken from the valon model, with a delta function enforcing momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- Fragmentation functions are fitted at \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\) using the BKK parameterization; parameters for \(D^{\pi}_S\), \(D^{\pi}_V\), \(D^{\pi}_G\), \(D^K_S\), and \(D^K_G\) are given in Table I (e.g., \(D^{\pi}_S\) has \(N = 2.7236\), \(\alpha = -0.734\), \(\beta = 3.384\), \(\gamma = -5.471\)).
- Shower parton distribution parameters for \(K_{NS}\), \(L\), \(G\), \(L_s\), and \(G_s\) are given in Table II (e.g., \(K_{NS}\) has \(A = 0.333\), \(a = 0.45\), \(b = 2.1\), \(c = 5.0\), \(d = 0.5\)).
- For the application to heavy-ion collisions, the thermal parton distribution is taken as \({\cal T}(k_T) = C k_T e^{-k_T/T}\) with \(C = 23.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(T = 0.317\) GeV, and the hard parton is produced at \(k_T = 10\) GeV/c.
- Thermal-shower recombination dominates over fragmentation for all \(p_T < 8\) GeV/c in the illustrative example.

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa (1980) and Hwa and Yang (2002) to describe fragmentation as recombination of shower partons, in contrast to the traditional perturbative QCD fragmentation approach. It provides a framework to treat hadronization in heavy-ion collisions where soft thermal partons coexist with hard jets, a situation where fragmentation functions alone are insufficient. The shower parton distributions enable calculations of two-particle correlations within a jet (Fig. 5) and will be applied to heavy-ion collisions in a subsequent paper by Hwa and Yang (2004). The approach differs from the parton cascade models of Marchesini and Webber (1988) and Geiger (1995), which track QCD branching processes down to cluster formation but do not yield the same shower parton distributions.
