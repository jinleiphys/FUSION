# Negative-Parity Baryon Spectroscopy

> Results are reported for the first calculation of the low-lying spin-1/2 odd-parity octet baryon masses using ${\cal O}(a^2)$ improved gluon and fermion actions. Methods for removing even-parity ground-state contaminations from the two-point correlation functions at zero and finite momenta are outlined. We investigate the properties of two odd-parity interpolating fields based on the established interpolating fields for the nucleon ground state. Isolation of the lowest-lying odd-parity state appears to be sufficient to begin exploring odd-parity $N \to N^*$ electromagnetic transition form factors.

# Negative-Parity Baryon Spectroscopy

## Key claim
This paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of low-lying spin-1/2 odd-parity octet baryon masses using O(a²) improved actions, but the results are exploratory and based on a single lattice volume with 100 configurations at a coarse lattice spacing of approximately 0.24 fm, requiring chiral extrapolation to compare with experiment.

## Method
The authors compute two-point correlation functions on a 10³ × 16 lattice at β = 7.0 using O(a²) tree-level tadpole-improved Wilson gauge action and the O(a²) tree-level tadpole-improved Dχ34 fermion action (Hamber and Wu, 1983). Two odd-parity interpolating fields are constructed by left-multiplying the standard even-parity nucleon interpolating fields by γ₅, and parity projection is performed by taking appropriate traces of the correlation function matrix to remove even-parity ground-state contamination at both zero and finite momentum.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 10³ × 16, β = 7.0, lattice spacing a ≈ 0.24 fm
- 100 configurations analyzed
- Five quark masses considered, corresponding to three pion-to-rho mass ratios: π/ρ = 0.82, 0.78, and 0.73 (quark masses approximately 210, 180, and 90 MeV)
- Mass ratio N/N(1535) from χ₁⁻ interpolator: 0.89(4) at π/ρ = 0.82, 0.85(5) at π/ρ = 0.78, 0.84(6) at π/ρ = 0.73, extrapolating to 0.71(10) at the chiral limit (experimental value 0.61)
- Mass ratio N/N(1650) from χ₂⁻ interpolator: 0.77(5) at π/ρ = 0.82, 0.71(6) at π/ρ = 0.78, 0.62(9) at π/ρ = 0.73, extrapolating to 0.42(11) at the chiral limit (experimental value 0.57)
- Mass ratio N/Λ(1405) from χΛC₁⁻ interpolator: 0.87(5) at π/ρ = 0.82, 0.83(6) at π/ρ = 0.78, 0.80(7) at π/ρ = 0.73, extrapolating to 0.66(10) at the chiral limit (experimental value 0.67)
- Off-diagonal correlation function (χ₁⁻ χ₂⁻ + χ₂⁻ χ₁⁻)/2 is smaller by more than a factor of two compared to diagonal correlation functions

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD methods for baryon spectroscopy beyond ground-state nucleon calculations to the odd-parity excited states, which had not been previously investigated on the lattice. The use of two distinct interpolating fields (scalar diquark χ₁⁻ and vector diquark χ₂⁻) follows the quark-model expectation that these couple preferentially to different N* states (N(1535) and N(1650) respectively), and the parity projection technique addresses the challenge that odd-parity interpolating fields also couple to lower-lying even-parity states. The results enable future exploration of N → N* electromagnetic transition form factors, which are of direct relevance to planned experiments at Jefferson Lab.
