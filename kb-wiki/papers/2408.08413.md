# Towards quantitative precision in functional QCD I

> Functional approaches are the only first principle QCD setup that allow for direct computations at finite density. Predictive power and quantitative reliability of the respective results can only be obtained within a systematic expansion scheme with controlled systematic error estimates. Here we set up such a scheme within the functional renormalisation group (fRG) approach to QCD, aiming for full apparent convergence. In the current work we test this setup, using correlation functions and observables in 2+1 flavour vacuum QCD as a natural benchmark case. While the current work includes many evolutionary improvements collected over the past two decades, we also report on three novel important developments: (i) A comprehensive systematic error analysis based on the modular nature of the fRG approach. (ii) The introduction of a fully automated computational framework, allowing for unprecedented access and improvement of the fRG approach to QCD. (iii) The inclusion of the full effective potential of the chiral order parameter. This also gives access to all-order scattering events of pions and to the full momentum dependence of correlation functions, which is a first application of the automated computational framework (ii). The results compare very well to other state-of-the-art results both from functional approaches and lattice simulations, and provide data on general multi-scattering events of pions and the sigma mode for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors set up a systematic expansion scheme within the functional renormalisation group (fRG) approach to QCD that aims for full apparent convergence, but the current work tests this setup only in 2+1 flavour vacuum QCD as a benchmark case, with the explicit caveat that quantitative reliability for finite density is bounded to \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 4\) within the present approximation level.

## Method

The authors use the functional renormalisation group (fRG) with emergent composite fields (dynamical hadronisation) to include scalar-pseudoscalar mesonic degrees of freedom, combining a vertex expansion in the glue subsystem with a derivative expansion in the quark-meson sector while retaining full propagators. The pure glue sector uses gluon and ghost two-point functions from two-flavour QCD as external input, and the full effective potential of the chiral order parameter is included using finite element methods. The numerical setup is fully automated, allowing systematic scans over different regulator functions for error estimation.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is found at \(k_\chi \approx \qty{400}{\MeV}\), below which mesonic fluctuations contribute to the off-shell dynamics.
* The three physical pions have masses \(m_\pi \approx \qty{140}{\MeV}\), while the lightest scalar resonance (the \(\sigma\)-mode) has a pole mass of about \(\qty{500}{\MeV}\).
* The estimated location of the critical end point from prior functional QCD studies is \((\mu, T)_\textrm{CEP} \approx (\qty{600}{\MeV}, \qty{115}{\MeV}) - (\qty{650}{\MeV}, \qty{105}{\MeV})\).
* The quantitative reliability bound for the present approximation at finite density is \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 4\).

## Context

This work extends the fRG approach to QCD developed over the past two decades, building on prior studies by Fu (2019), Cyrol (2016, 2017), and Braun (2014, 2020) that established benchmark results at vanishing chemical potential. It introduces a comprehensive systematic error analysis based on the modular (LEGO-expansion) nature of the fRG, a fully automated computational framework, and the inclusion of the full effective potential of the chiral order parameter. The results enable future systematic improvement of functional QCD predictions for the phase structure at higher densities, where lattice simulations are obstructed by the sign problem.
