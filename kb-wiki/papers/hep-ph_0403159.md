# Photoproduction of $Θ^+$ and other pentaquark states

> We present constraints on the {\it relative} photoproduction cross sections of positive parity pentaquark states, $Σ_5$, $Λ_5$, and $N_5$, based on a minimum phenomenology gained in $γN \to K Θ^+$ and their baryon-meson couplings as in the work of Close and Dudek. The possibility of anomalous signals in $γp \to K^0_S (Θ^+;Σ_d^+)$ is discussed. We emphasise the importance of comparing $γN \to K Θ^+$ with "conventional" states such as $γN \to K Σ(1660)$.

# Photoproduction of $\Theta^+$ and other pentaquark states

## Key claim

The relative photoproduction cross sections of positive parity pentaquark states $\Sigma_5$, $\Lambda_5$, and $N_5$ can be constrained from the phenomenology of $\gamma N \to K \Theta^+$ and the fall-apart decay mechanism, but the absolute cross sections are unreliable above $E_{\gamma} > 3$ GeV due to the neglect of Reggeisation. Continued observation of the $\Theta^+$ without seeing $\Sigma_5$ and $\Lambda_5$ at the predicted rates would imply the pentaquark dynamics and masses are outside the currently assumed range, or that these states do not exist.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for the $\Theta N K$ and $\Theta N K^*$ couplings based on pseudovector interactions, with coupling strengths determined by the fall-apart decay dynamics of Close and Dudek (2003). They compute Born approximation cross sections for photoproduction of $\Theta^+$, $\Sigma_5$, $\Lambda_5$, and $N_5$ on both proton and neutron targets, including contact terms, $t$-channel kaon and $K^*$ exchanges, and $s$- and $u$-channel baryon exchanges. The relative cross sections are scaled by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients from the flavor wavefunctions, with kinematic factors from mass differences.

## Key numbers

- For $\Theta^+$ with total width $\Gamma_T \sim 1$ MeV, the cross sections can be rescaled by $\sigma \to \sigma \times \Gamma_T / (1 \text{ MeV})$.
- For an ideally mixed $\Sigma_d$ with mass equal to $\Theta^+$, the relative production rate $\sigma(\gamma n \to K^+ \Sigma^-_d) / \sigma(\gamma n \to K^- \Theta^+) \sim 1/2$.
- For $\Sigma_5(\overline{\mathbf{10}})$ and $\Sigma_5(\mathbf{8}_5)$ with mass equal to $\Theta^+$, the ratios are $1/3$ and $1/6$, respectively.
- For non-strange pentaquark $p_d$ and $n_u$ with mass assumed at 1.44 GeV, the cross sections for $\gamma p \to n_u \pi^+$ and $\gamma n \to \pi^- p_d$ peak at around 70 nb when $E_{\gamma} \sim 1.2$ GeV.
- The $K^*$ coupling is constrained as $g^2_{\Theta N K^*} = 3 g^2_{\Theta N K}$ from fall-apart dynamics.
- The $K^{*0}K^0\gamma$ coupling $g_{K^{*0}K^0\gamma} = 1.13$ is determined by $\Gamma_{K^{*0} \to K^0\gamma} = 117$ keV.
- The $K^{*\pm}K^\pm\gamma$ coupling $g_{K^{*\pm}K^\pm\gamma} = 0.744$ is determined by $\Gamma_{K^{*\pm} \to K^\pm\gamma} = 50$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the flavor wavefunction construction of Close and Dudek (2003) for pentaquark states in the $\overline{\mathbf{10}} \oplus \mathbf{8}$ representation, providing quantitative photoproduction predictions for partner states of the $\Theta^+(1540)$. It enables experimental searches for $\Sigma_5$, $\Lambda_5$, and $N_5$ pentaquarks in photoproduction, and proposes that failure to observe these states at the predicted rates would challenge the pentaquark interpretation of the $\Theta^+$ signal seen by experiments such as LEPS (2003), DIANA (2003), CLAS (2003), and SAPHIR (2003). The work also suggests that conventional states like $\Lambda(1600)$ and $\Sigma(1660)$ should be produced at levels at least as large as the pentaquark predictions if they contain any pentaquark component.
