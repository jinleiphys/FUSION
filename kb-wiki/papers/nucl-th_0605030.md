# Shell model test of quadrupole properties predicted by the rotational formula--the degenerate SDI interaction and non-degenerate FPD6

> In the rotational model for a K=0 band in an even-even nucleus, there is a single parameter--Q_0, the intrinsic quadrupole moment. All B(E2)'s in the band and all static quadrupole moments are expressed in terms of this one parameter. In shell-model calculations, this does not have to be the case. In this work, we consider ground-state bands in {44}Ti, {46}Ti, {48}Ti, {48}Cr, and {50}Cr. We have two models. First, we use a Surface Delta Interaction with degenerate single-particle energies (SDI-deg). We compare this with results of a shell-model calculation using the standard interaction FPD6 and include the single-particle energy splitting. Neither model yields a perfect rotational I(I+1) spectrum, although the SDI-deg model comes somewhat closer. Overall, the simple rotational formula for B(E2)'s and static quadrupole moments hangs together very nicely.

# Shell model test of quadrupole properties predicted by the rotational formula, the degenerate SDI interaction and non-degenerate FPD6

## Key claim

The simple rotational formula for B(E2) values and static quadrupole moments holds together very nicely in shell-model calculations for ground-state bands of 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Ti, 48Cr, and 50Cr, even though neither the degenerate Surface Delta Interaction (SDI-deg) nor the more realistic FPD6 interaction yields a perfect I(I+1) rotational spectrum. The ratio R_SB = Q_0(S)/Q_0(B) remains close to unity across both models, with SDI-deg giving values from 0.964 to 1.009 and FPD6 giving values from 0.826 to 0.989, despite the spectra being non-rotational.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the full fp shell using the code ANTOINE for the ground-state bands of 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Ti, 48Cr, and 50Cr. Two interactions were compared: a Surface Delta Interaction with degenerate single-particle energies (SDI-deg), with its strength fitted to the experimental 2+1 excitation energy, and the standard FPD6 interaction which includes realistic single-particle energy splittings. The authors computed the ratio R_SB = Q_0(S)/Q_0(B) from static quadrupole moments and B(E2) values, and extended the analysis to higher angular momenta using the ratios M(Q)_I and M(B)_I→I-2, which compare intrinsic quadrupole moments at different spins to the I=2 reference.

## Key numbers

- R_SB for SDI-deg ranges from 0.964 (48Cr) to 1.009 (44Ti, 46Ti); for FPD6 ranges from 0.826 (48Ti) to 0.989 (48Cr)
- For 48Ti with SDI-deg: Q(2+) = -32.337 e fm^2, B(E2)2→0 = 247.38 e^2 fm^4, R_SB = 1.0149
- For 48Ti with FPD6: Q(2+) = -17.714 e fm^2, B(E2)2→0 = 112.16 e^2 fm^4, R_SB = 0.8257
- For 48Cr with SDI-deg: Q(2+) = -37.936 e fm^2, B(E2)2→0 = 377.60 e^2 fm^4, R_SB = 0.9638
- For 48Cr with FPD6: Q(2+) = -33.271 e fm^2, B(E2)2→0 = 275.68 e^2 fm^4, R_SB = 0.9892
- Experimental B(E2) values for 48Cr: 2→0 = 272 e^2 fm^4, 4→2 = 329(110) e^2 fm^4, 6→4 = 301(78) e^2 fm^4, 8→6 = 230(69) e^2 fm^4, 10→8 = 195(54) e^2 fm^4, 12→10 = 167(25) e^2 fm^4, 14→12 = 105(18) e^2 fm^4, 16→14 = 37(8) e^2 fm^4
- For 48Ti: experimental 12+1 energy = 8.09 MeV, FPD6 gives 8.31 MeV, SDI-deg gives 15.98 MeV, rotational I(I+1) would give 25.57 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Robinson et al. (2006) which examined the ratio R_SB for 2+ states across many nuclei, finding it close to unity except for 40Ar. The present paper extends the analysis to higher angular momenta and compares the schematic SDI-deg interaction with the realistic FPD6 interaction, complementing the SU(3) model studies by Elliott (1958) and the more recent work by Lenzi et al. (2006) on N=Z nuclei. The results show that the rotational formula for electromagnetic properties holds even when the energy spectrum is not rotational, which contrasts with the harmonic vibrator model where static quadrupole moments vanish and B(E2) values increase with spin. The findings enable further testing of rotational collectivity in shell-model calculations and connect to random interaction studies by Velazquez et al. (2003) and Zelevinsky and Volya (2004) that found high probabilities for the Alaga ratio at the rotational limit.
