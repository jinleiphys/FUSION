# Twin stars and the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state: ruling out strong phase transitions below $1.7n_0$ with the new NICER radius measurements

> We explore the connection between the stiffness of an hadronic equation of state (EoS) with a sharp phase transition to quark matter to its tidal deformability. For this we employ a hadronic relativistic mean field model with a parameterized effective nucleon mass to vary the stiffness in conjunction with a constant speed of sound EoS for quark matter. We compute multiple scenarios with phase transitions according to the four possible cases of a hybrid star EoS with a stable second branch. We demonstrate at the example of GW170817 how the effective nucleon mass can be constrained by using gravitational wave data. We find, that certain values of the effective nucleon mass are incompatible with GW170817 and a phase transition simultaneously. By using the recent NICER measurements of J0030+0451 at the $1σ$ level we constrain our results further and find that strong phase transitions with a visible jump in the mass-radius relation are ruled out at densities below 1.7 times saturation density.

## Key claim

Strong first-order phase transitions from hadronic to quark matter that produce a visible jump in the mass-radius relation (twin stars) are ruled out at densities below 1.7 times nuclear saturation density (n0) when the 1σ NICER radius measurements of J0030+0451 are applied, but this conclusion weakens at the 2σ level.

## Method

The authors use a parameterized relativistic mean field model for hadronic matter, varying the effective nucleon mass m*/m from 0.55 to 0.75 to control stiffness, combined with a constant speed of sound (c_QM = 1) model for quark matter. They construct hybrid star equations of state with a Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition, classifying the resulting twin star solutions into four categories based on the masses of the first and second branches. They compute tidal deformabilities and compare with GW170817 constraints, then overlay the NICER mass-radius measurements of J0030+0451 from both Riley et al. (2019) and Miller et al. (2019).

## Key numbers

- Phase transitions with Δε ≥ 350 MeV/fm³ and transition density below n ≤ 1.7 n0 are ruled out by NICER 1σ data
- Riley et al. (2019) report J0030+0451 mass = 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M⊙, radius = 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km
- Miller et al. (2019) report J0030+0451 mass = 1.44^{+0.15}_{-0.14} M⊙, radius = 13.02^{+1.24}_{-1.06} km
- Both NICER analyses give compactness MG/Rc² = 0.16 ± 0.01
- GW170817 chirp mass = 1.186^{+0.001}_{-0.001} M⊙
- Pure hadronic cases with m*/m ≥ 0.65 are compatible with GW170817 data
- Category IV phase transitions produce hybrid-hybrid combinations within the 50% credibility limit for all m*/m values examined
- The visible jump in mass at the phase transition is ΔM ≥ 0.1 M⊙ for strong transitions

## Context

This work extends the classification of twin stars from Christian (2017) and the tidal deformability analysis of hybrid stars from Christian (2018) by incorporating the new NICER radius measurements of J0030+0451 from Riley et al. (2019) and Miller et al. (2019). The parameterized hadronic model follows Hornick et al. (2018), and the constant speed of sound approach for quark matter follows Alford et al. (2014). The results contradict the possibility of strong early phase transitions that would produce low-mass hybrid stars, and they enable future gravitational wave observations to further constrain the stiffness of the hadronic equation of state and the location of any phase transition.
