# Towards a microscopic description of nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We present the first results of a comprehensive microscopic approach to describe nucleus-nucleus elastic collisions by means of an optical potential derived at first order in multiple-scattering theory and computed by folding the projectile and target nuclear densities with the nucleon-nucleon $t$ matrix, which describes the interaction between each nucleon of the projectile and each nucleon of the target. Chiral interactions are consistently used in the calculation of the $t$ matrix and of the nonlocal nuclear densities, which are computed within the ab initio no-core shell model. Cross sections calculated for $α$ collisions on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O at projectile energies in the range 100-300 MeV are presented and compared with available data. For momentum transfer $q$ up to about $1.0$ fm$^{-1}$ our results are in good agreement with the experimental data, whereas for higher momenta a reduction of the imaginary contributions is needed.

## Key claim

The authors present a first-principles optical potential for nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering that uses chiral interactions consistently in both the nucleon-nucleon t matrix and the ab initio nuclear densities, but the model requires an ad hoc reduction of the imaginary part by a factor of 0.5 to reproduce experimental data at momentum transfers above about 1.0 fm^-1.

## Method

The optical potential is derived at first order in multiple-scattering theory (single-scattering approximation) using a double-folding integral that convolves the projectile and target nonlocal nuclear densities with the free nucleon-nucleon t matrix. The nuclear densities are computed within the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) using chiral NN interactions at N^4LO with a 500 MeV cutoff and three-nucleon forces at N^2LO, with the same chiral interaction used consistently for the t matrix. Cross sections are calculated for alpha-particle elastic scattering on 12C and 16O at projectile energies of 104, 130, and 240 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer threshold for agreement: results match experimental data for q up to about 1.0 fm^-1; disagreement grows for larger q values
- NCSM calculations used hbar omega = 18 MeV, SRG cutoff lambda = 1.8 fm^-1, with Nmax = 8 for 12C and 16O, and Nmax = 16 for 4He
- Three projectile energies studied: 104, 130, and 240 MeV for both 12C(4He,4He)12C and 16O(4He,4He)16O reactions
- Imaginary part reduction factor: rescaling the imaginary component by 0.5 substantially improves agreement at high momentum transfer
- Reaction cross section data for 12C(4He, reac) span 10-1100 MeV; the halved-imaginary-part results fall close to experimental data in the 100-300 MeV range

## Context

This work extends the microscopic optical potential approach previously applied to proton-nucleus elastic scattering by Vorabbi (2017) to the more complex nucleus-nucleus case, using consistent chiral interactions throughout. The results are benchmarked against the double-folding calculations of Durant and Capel (2020), who used chiral NN interactions at N^2LO with either a proportionality constant or Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations to obtain the imaginary part. The approach enables future application to exotic nuclei where experimental data are scarce, though the excess absorption at high momentum transfer requires further investigation of medium effects and multiple-scattering contributions.
