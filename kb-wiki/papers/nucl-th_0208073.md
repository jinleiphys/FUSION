# Enhancement of nuclear Schiff moments and time reversal violation in atoms due to soft nuclear octupole vibrations

> Parity and time invariance violating (${\cal P},{\cal T}$-odd) nuclear forces produce ${\cal P},{\cal T}$-odd nuclear moments, for example, the nuclear Schiff moment. In turn, this moment can induce electric dipole moments (EDMs) in atoms. We estimate the contribution to the Schiff moment from the soft collective octupole vibrations existing in many heavy nuclei. Two possible mechanisms are considered, mixing of the ground state of an odd-$A$ nucleus with the octupole phonon state, and ${\cal P},{\cal T}$-odd admixture to the single-particle wave function of the valence nucleon. We found practically the same contribution to the Schiff moment as that of the static octupole deformation calculated earlier. This confirms a suggestion that the soft octupole vibrations can replace the (controversial) static octupoles in development of the collective Schiff moments. The values of atomic EDM predicted for $^{223,225}$Ra and $^{223}$Rn are enhanced by factors up to $10^{3}$ compared to experimentally studied spherical nuclei $^{199}$Hg and $^{129}$Xe.

## Key claim

Soft octupole vibrations in heavy nuclei can produce nuclear Schiff moments of the same magnitude as those from static octupole deformation, confirming that the dynamical octupole mode can replace the controversial static octupole in generating collective Schiff moments, with the caveat that the result depends on the r.m.s. octupole deformation parameter being comparable to the static one.

## Method

The authors analytically estimate the Schiff moment from two mechanisms in odd-\(A\) nuclei: core excitation, where the valence nucleon's \({\cal P},{\cal T}\)-odd field excites the soft octupole phonon in the even-even core; and particle excitation, where the dynamic octupole deformation mixes opposite-parity single-particle orbitals of the valence nucleon. The intrinsic Schiff moment is expressed in terms of the quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\) and the r.m.s. octupole deformation \(\beta_3^{\rm rms}\), extracted from measured B(E3) transition probabilities, and the mixing with opposite-parity states is treated via the energy denominator \(\Delta E\) between the ground state and the octupole phonon state.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{225}\)Ra (\(I=1/2\), \(\Delta E = 55\) keV): Schiff moment \(S \sim 500\) in units of \(10^{-8}\eta e\,{\rm fm}^3\) from analytical estimate; numerical calculation gives \(S = 300\).
- For \(^{223}\)Ra (\(I=3/2\), \(\Delta E = 50\) keV): analytical estimate \(S \sim 1000\); numerical calculation gives \(S = 400\).
- For \(^{223}\)Rn: numerical calculation gives \(S = 1000\) in the same units.
- For \(^{199}\)Hg: Schiff moment \(S = -1.4\) in the same units (spherical nucleus).
- Atomic EDM predictions: \(d = 2500 \times 10^{-25} e\cdot{\rm cm}\cdot\eta_n\) for \(^{225}\)Ra, \(d = 3400\) for \(^{223}\)Ra, and \(d = 3300\) for \(^{223}\)Rn, compared to \(d = 4\) for \(^{199}\)Hg and \(d = 0.7\) for \(^{129}\)Xe.
- The ratio of the dynamical (vibrational) to static octupole contribution is \(S_{\rm soft}/S_{\rm stat} \approx (\beta_3^{\rm rms})^2/(\beta_3^{\rm stat})^2\), which is near unity when both deformations are \(\sim 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Schiff moments in nuclei with static octupole deformation by Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak (1996) and Spevak, Auerbach, and Flambaum (1997), and responds to the suggestion by Hayes (1999) that soft octupole vibrations could produce similar enhancement. The results enable experimental searches for atomic EDMs in \(^{223,225}\)Ra and \(^{223}\)Rn, which are predicted to have EDMs enhanced by up to \(10^3\) compared to the spherical nuclei \(^{199}\)Hg and \(^{129}\)Xe already studied. The authors also note that future work should explore nuclei soft in both quadrupole and octupole modes, such as light Rn and Ra isotopes with long quasivibrational bands.
