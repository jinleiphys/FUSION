# On the $x_F$ Distribution of $J/ψ$'s Produced in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Thermal production of $J/ψ$ in the quark gluon plasma is reconsidered. We show that if screening effects are not strong enough, the ``in-plasma born'' $J/ψ$'s would generate a peak in the Feynman momentum distribution at $x_F \simeq 0$.

## Key claim

If color screening in the quark-gluon plasma is not strong enough to destroy all charmonium bound states, the small fraction of "in-plasma born" J/ψ mesons that survive would produce a peak in the Feynman momentum distribution at x_F ≈ 0, which could serve as a new signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors compute J/ψ production in heavy ion collisions from three mechanisms: direct perturbative QCD production via parton fusion (using the color evaporation model), thermal statistical coalescence at hadronization, and in-plasma parton fusion (gluon-gluon and quark-antiquark annihilation) within a thermalized quark-gluon plasma. The in-plasma production rate is derived from thermal field theory, integrated over a Bjorken hydrodynamical expansion of the fireball, and multiplied by a screening factor f_s (between 10⁻³ and 10⁻²) to account for the survival probability of bound states against plasma screening.

## Key numbers

- The number of directly produced J/ψ mesons in central AA collisions at RHIC is estimated as N^{J/ψ}_{QCD} ≈ 0.1, based on PHENIX p-p data.
- The number of in-plasma produced c̄c pairs varies by a factor of 30 depending on input choices (charm quark mass, coupling constant, κ factor), ranging from N^{c̄c}_{QGP} = 0.02 (Wang) up to 15 (Rafelski).
- The screening factor f_s is taken in the interval 10⁻³ ≤ f_s ≤ 10⁻²; if f_s < 10⁻³, no visible effect from in-plasma production is observed.
- The initial plasma temperature used in calculations is T₀ = 550 MeV.
- The charm quark mass is varied between 1.2 GeV and 1.5 GeV, changing results by a factor of 4.
- Inclusion of shadowing reduces the number of charm quark pairs and J/ψ mesons by about 10%.

## Context

This work extends the two-component model of Rapp (2001) and the analysis of Hüfner (2001) by including J/ψ production within the plasma itself, which was previously neglected because screening was believed to destroy all bound states. The authors challenge the standard screening picture from Matsui and Satz (1986) and lattice calculations by Karsch (2002), noting that recent calculations for moving charges (Sidi, 2002) show weaker screening, and that lattice results at up to 1.10 T_c allow J/ψ survival (Digal, 2001). The predicted peak at x_F ≈ 0 would fill the dip predicted by Hüfner (2001) and provide a new, distinguishable signal for quark-gluon plasma formation, though it relies on accurate knowledge of pQCD, thermal, and QGP abundances.
