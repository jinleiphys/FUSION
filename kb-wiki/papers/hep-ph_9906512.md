# Superconductivity from perturbative one-gluon exchange in high density quark matter

> We study color superconductivity in QCD at asymptotically large chemical potential. In this limit, pairing is dominated by perturbative one-gluon exchange. We derive the Eliashberg equation for the pairing gap and solve this equation numerically. Taking into account both magnetic and electric gluon exchanges, we find $Δ\sim g^{-5}\exp(-c/g)$ with $c=3π^2/\sqrt{2}$, verifying a recent result by Son. For chemical potentials that are of physical interest, $μ< 1$ GeV, the calculation ceases to be reliable quantitatively, but our results suggest that the gap can be as large as 100 MeV.

# Superconductivity from perturbative one-gluon exchange in high density quark matter

## Key claim

The superconducting gap in QCD at asymptotically large chemical potential scales as Δ ∼ g⁻⁵ exp(-c/g) with c = 3π²/√2, verifying a recent result by Son (1998); however, for chemical potentials below 1 GeV the calculation ceases to be reliable quantitatively, though the results suggest the gap can be as large as 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors derive the Eliashberg equation for the pairing gap in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, starting from the Dyson-Schwinger equation with one-gluon exchange as the dominant interaction. They solve this integral equation numerically for two massless quark flavors, taking into account both magnetic and electric gluon exchanges with hard dense loop screening, and using the one-loop running coupling evaluated at the Fermi momentum p_F = μ.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic scaling of the gap: Δ₀ ≈ 256π⁴ μ g⁻⁵ exp(-3π²/(√2 g)) for N_f = 2, with the overall coefficient correct up to a factor of order one
- For μ = 10¹⁰ MeV (g ≈ 0.67), the numerical solution agrees with Son's approximate analytic solution up to an overall factor c ≈ 2
- For μ = 400 MeV (g ≈ 3.43), the approximate solution remains qualitatively correct
- For chemical potentials μ < 1000 MeV, the gap reaches Δ₀ ≈ 100 MeV
- The Debye screening mass is m_D² = 2m² with m² = N_f g² μ²/(4π²)
- The magnetic screening scale is q_M ≈ (π/4 · m_D² Δ)^(1/3)
- The electric screening scale is q_E ≈ m_D^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity in QCD at high density, following the earlier result by Son (1998) who argued that magnetic gluon exchanges dominate and obtained the exponential form Δ ∼ μ g⁻⁵ exp(-3π²/(√2 g)). The authors strengthen this result by deriving the Eliashberg equation from the Dyson-Schwinger equation, showing gauge independence, and determining the overall numerical coefficient. The calculation supersedes earlier attempts by Bailin and Love (1984) who used a schematic IR cutoff and obtained Δ ∼ μ exp(-c/g²). The results agree in order of magnitude with previous calculations by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schäfer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) based on phenomenological effective interactions normalized to chiral symmetry breaking at zero density. This work enables systematic perturbative calculation of other hadronic properties, such as the chiral condensate, in the high-density color-flavor-locked phase.
