# Shell Model Monte Carlo Investigation of Rare Earth Nuclei

> We utilize the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to study the structure of rare earth nuclei. This work demonstrates the first systematic ``full oscillator shell plus intruder'' calculations in such heavy nuclei. Exact solutions of a pairing plus quadrupole hamiltonian are compared with mean field and SPA approximations in several Dysprosium isotopes from A=152-162, including the odd mass A=153. Basic properties of these nuclei at various temperatures and spin are explored. These include energy, deformation, moments of inertia, pairing channel strengths, band crossing, and evolution of shell model occupation numbers. Exact level densities are also calculated and, in the case of 162 Dy, compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first systematic "full oscillator shell plus intruder" Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in rare earth nuclei, demonstrating that exact solutions of a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian are feasible for A~150, but the accuracy of the static path approximation (SPA) relative to the exact solution depends strongly on the observable and the nuclear deformation.

## Method

The authors used the SMMC method to solve a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian (Kumar-Baranger interaction) in a model space of 32 proton levels (sdg shell plus h11/2 intruder) and 44 neutron levels (pfh shell plus i13/2 intruder) for Dysprosium isotopes (Z=66, A=152-162). They computed thermal observables including energy, spin, quadrupole moments, pairing strengths, moments of inertia, and level densities, and compared the exact canonical results with both the static path approximation (SPA) and mean field approximations.

## Key numbers

- The difference between SPA and exact canonical ground state energies is 2.39±0.15 MeV for 152Dy and 2.59±0.18 MeV for 156Dy
- The total quadrupole moment at β=6 MeV−1 is 1221 b4 for 162Dy versus 834 b4 for 156Dy, a 46% increase
- The moment of inertia for 156Dy is 44.6±7 ħ2/MeV in the exact canonical ensemble at J≈4 with β=8 MeV−1, matching the experimental value of 40 ħ2/MeV; the SPA gives 73.0±2 ħ2/MeV, which equals the rigid body value
- The Monte Carlo sign for 153Dy at β=10 MeV−1 is 0.82 for the uncranked case, dropping to 0.69 at ω=0.05 MeV, 0.52 at ω=0.1 MeV, and 0.10 at ω=0.2 MeV
- The moment of inertia for 153Dy is 49.6±3 ħ2/MeV in the limit ω→0 for the canonical calculation
- The energy difference between full canonical and SPA calculations at T=100 keV for 153Dy is 1.97±0.4 MeV
- Canonical cranking was limited to ω≤0.3 MeV due to sign degradation

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method from lighter nuclei (A~50, where direct diagonalization was previously limited) to the rare earth region (A~150), building on a prior test case for 170Dy (White, Koonin, and Dean, 1997). The results enable systematic microscopic calculations of thermal properties, level densities, and shape evolution in heavy deformed nuclei, and provide benchmarks for simpler approximations like SPA (previously studied by Alhassid and Bush for the Lipkin model) and mean field methods. The authors note that Kumar and Baranger did not calculate 152Dy or 144Ba, and that their model was not claimed valid for nuclei at extreme deformation.
