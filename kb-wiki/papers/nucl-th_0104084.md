# Quark Model Study of The eta Photoproduction: Evidence for a New S11 Resonance?

> An extensive and systematic study of the recent eta photoproduction data up to 1.2 GeV is presented within a chiral constituent quark model. A model embodying all known nucleonic resonances shows clear need for a yet undiscovered third S11 resonance in the second resonance region, for which we determine the mass (1.729 GeV) and the total width (183 MeV). Furthermore, we extract the configuration mixing angles, an important property of the quark-quark interaction in the quark model, for the resonances S11(1535) and S11(1650), as well as for the resonances D13(1520) and D13(1700). Our results agree well with the quark model predictions. In addition, the partial eta N decay widths and/or the photo-excitation helicity amplitudes for the nucleonic resonances S11(1535), S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720), D13(1520), D13(1700), D15(1675), and F15(1680) are also obtained in this approach.

## Key claim
The authors find that a chiral constituent quark model embodying all known nucleonic resonances cannot reproduce the Graal eta photoproduction data above 1.0 GeV, and that a third, previously undiscovered S11 resonance in the second resonance region is required, with the caveat that such a resonance may indicate a molecular-type structure rather than a conventional three-quark state.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic fit of differential cross-section and beam asymmetry data for the reaction gamma p -> eta p up to a laboratory photon energy of 1.2 GeV using a chiral constituent quark model based on SU(6) x O(3) symmetry. They introduced symmetry-breaking coefficients C_N* for each resonance and related these coefficients to configuration mixing angles between the two S11 states and the two D13 states in the 1.5-1.7 GeV region. Three models were compared: Model A with free resonance strengths, Model B with mixing angle constraints, and Model C with an additional third S11 resonance.

## Key numbers
- Mass of the proposed third S11 resonance: 1.729 GeV (with total width 183 MeV)
- Extracted mixing angle for S11 resonances in Model C: -26.6 degrees (compared to Isgur-Karl model prediction of -32 degrees)
- Extracted mixing angle for D13 resonances in Model C: 5.1 degrees (compared to Isgur-Karl model prediction of 6 degrees)
- Mass of S11(1535) in Model C: 1.542 GeV; width: 0.162 GeV
- Reduced chi-squared for Model C: 1.6, compared to 3.2 for Model A and 3.8 for Model B
- Harmonic oscillator strength alpha_ho^2: 0.090 GeV^2 in all three models
- eta NN coupling constant in Model C: 1.467

## Context
This work extends earlier quark model studies of eta photoproduction by Saghai and Li (2000) and the relativized pair-creation model of Capstick and Roberts (1992), using new Graal data (Graal2000) that cover both the first and second resonance regions. The need for a third S11 resonance was previously suggested by Zhang and Riska (1996) as a quasi-bound K-Lambda or K-Sigma state. The extracted configuration mixing angles agree with the Isgur-Karl model predictions (Isgur and Karl, 1977) and with large-N_c effective field theory approaches (Carlson, 1998; Pirjol, 1998). The results enable extraction of partial eta N decay widths and photo-excitation helicity amplitudes for eight nucleonic resonances, and motivate searches for the proposed resonance in higher-energy data.
