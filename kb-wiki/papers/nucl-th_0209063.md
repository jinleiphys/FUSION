# Large mass dileptons from the passage of jets through quark gluon plasma

> We calculate the emission of large mass dileptons originating from the annihilation of quark jets passing through quark gluon plasma. Considering central collisions of heavy nuclei at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies, we find that the yield due to the jet-plasma interaction gets progressively larger as the collision energy increases. We find it to be negligible at SPS energies, of the order of the Drell-Yan contribution and much larger than the normal thermal yield at RHIC energies and up to a factor of ten larger than the Drell-Yan contribution at LHC energies. An observation of this new dilepton source would confirm the occurrence of jet-plasma interactions and of conditions suitable for jet-quenching to take place.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that large-mass dileptons from quark-jet annihilation with thermal antiquarks in quark-gluon plasma become a significant source at RHIC and LHC energies, but the yield is highly sensitive to the initial temperature and formation time of the plasma, with the jet-plasma contribution at LHC being up to a factor of ten larger than the Drell-Yan contribution only under specific assumed initial conditions.

## Method

The authors compute the dilepton emission rate using kinetic theory, folding the perturbative QCD cross section for quark-antiquark annihilation into dileptons with phase-space distributions that are decomposed into a thermal component (characterized by temperature T) and a jet component (from minijet production calculated at leading order with CTEQ5L parton distributions and EKS98 nuclear modifications). The rates are then integrated over the space-time history of a longitudinally expanding, chemically equilibrated or chemically equilibrating QGP, using Bjorken cooling and accounting for the finite path length of jets through the plasma before they reach the surface or the plasma hadronizes.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=17.4 GeV): jet-plasma dilepton yield is negligible compared to Drell-Yan; thermal yield drops drastically when formation time increases from 0.20 fm/c (T0=0.345 GeV) to 0.50 fm/c (T0=0.254 GeV), while jet-plasma yield reduces by a factor of about 2.
- At RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): jet-plasma yield is about one third of the Drell-Yan contribution and much larger than the normal thermal yield; initial conditions from Bjorken formula give T0=0.447 GeV at tau0=0.15 fm/c or T0=0.297 GeV at tau0=0.50 fm/c.
- At LHC (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV): jet-plasma yield at M=10 GeV is up to a factor of ten larger than the Drell-Yan contribution; initial conditions from Bjorken formula give T0=0.897 GeV at tau0=0.073 fm/c or T0=0.473 GeV at tau0=0.50 fm/c.
- For the chemically equilibrating plasma scenario at LHC (self-screened parton cascade initial conditions: T0=1.02 GeV, tau0=0.25 fm/c, lambda_g=0.43, lambda_q=0.082), the large mass yield is completely dominated by jet-plasma interactions, even when only jets with pT > 6 GeV are considered.
- The average distance a jet travels through the plasma before reaching the surface is approximately 0.94 times the nuclear radius R_perp.

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton sources in heavy-ion collisions beyond the established Drell-Yan process (from primary parton annihilation), thermal dileptons from QGP (Kapusta et al., 1991), and hadronic gas contributions (Gale, 1991). It is analogous to the earlier proposal for high-energy photon production from jet-plasma interactions by Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2002). The calculation uses a similar formalism to the parton cascade model of Geiger and Kapusta (1993) for secondary-secondary interactions, but improves upon it by accounting for the finite path length of jets and the space-time evolution of the plasma. The results enable experimental searches for this new dilepton source as a direct confirmation of jet-plasma interactions and jet-quenching, particularly at RHIC and LHC energies.
