# Intermediate mass dileptons in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We consider the production of lepton pairs with intermediate invariant masses, in relativistic nuclear collisions. Thermal sources are briefly discussed, as well as a newer production mechanism which involves jet-plasma interactions. Estimates for high p_T lepton pairs are provided.

## Key claim

Thermal sources of dileptons are large enough to explain the intermediate-mass excess observed at SPS energies without requiring a large quark-gluon plasma contribution or a large enhancement of charm-anticharm pairs, but this conclusion depends on the details of the space-time modeling; at RHIC energies, jet-plasma interactions produce a dilepton source that can exceed the purely thermal source by roughly an order of magnitude and must be included in realistic estimates.

## Method

The authors calculate thermal dilepton production rates using effective Lagrangians and electron-positron annihilation data integrated over a space-time evolution scenario for the colliding system. For jet-plasma interactions, they use the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed photon self-energy in high-temperature field theory, with the dressed fermion propagator, and incorporate a non-thermal phase-space distribution for hard jets via relativistic kinetic theory, including Drell-Yan emission to order alpha_s and virtual photon bremsstrahlung from jets.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, for intermediate masses, the jet-thermal contribution can exceed the purely thermal source by roughly an order of magnitude, as was the case for real photons.
- In the low invariant mass window (0.5 < M < 1 GeV) at RHIC, the absence of any jet-thermal interactions would reduce the yield by a factor of approximately 2 at p_T = 4 GeV.
- The production rate calculation uses T = 0.3 GeV and alpha_s = 0.3 for comparison with Thoma and Traxler (2000), with momentum p = 4 GeV.
- The effective quark mass induced by the thermal medium is given by m_F = g_s T / sqrt(6).

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the Helios/3 data by Li and Gale (1998) and of NA50 data by Kvasnikova et al. (2001), which showed that thermal sources could explain the intermediate-mass dilepton excess without requiring charm enhancement. The recent NA60 measurement confirmed the excess and ruled out open charm enhancement as its source, as reported by Arnaldi et al. (2006). The jet-plasma interaction mechanism was previously shown to contribute sizeably to the intermediate-to-high p_T photon spectrum by Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2003) and by Turbide, Gale, and Jeon (2004). This work enables quantitative inclusion of jet-plasma interactions in future dilepton analyses at RHIC and LHC energies, where heavy quark decays dominate most of the kinematical range and where direct measurements like those of NA60 at SPS would be valuable.
