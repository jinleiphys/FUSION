# Particle number fluctuations and correlations in transfer reactions obtained using the Balian-Vénéroni variational principle

> The Balian-Vénéroni (BV) variational principle, which optimizes the evolution of the state according to the relevant observable in a given variational space, is used at the mean-field level to determine the particle number fluctuations in fragments of many-body systems. For fermions, the numerical evaluation of such fluctuations requires the use of a time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code. Proton, neutron and total nucleon number fluctuations in fragments produced in collisions of two 40Ca are computed for a large range of angular momenta at a center of mass energy E_cm=128 MeV, well above the fusion barrier. For deep-inelastic collisions, the fluctuations calculated from the BV variational principle are much larger than standard TDHF results, and closer to mass and charge experimental fluctuations. For the first time, correlations between proton and neutron numbers are determined within a quantum microscopic approach. These correlations are shown to be larger with exotic systems where charge equilibration occurs.

## Key claim

The Balian-Vénéroni variational principle, applied at the mean-field level, yields particle number fluctuations in fragments from heavy-ion collisions that are significantly larger than standard time-dependent Hartree-Fock predictions and closer to experimental data, but the quantitative agreement is limited by the omission of fusion-fission events and the fact that the calculation applies to primary (excited) fragments before nucleon emission.

## Method

The authors used a three-dimensional TDHF code with the SLy4d Skyrme energy-density functional to simulate collisions of two ⁴⁰Ca nuclei at a center-of-mass energy of 128 MeV. To compute fluctuations via the BV variational principle, they performed forward TDHF evolution from initial time t₀ to final time t₁, then applied a boost to the occupied single-particle wave functions at t₁ according to Eq. (4) (exponential of the relevant one-body operator), and finally evolved these boosted states backward in time to t₀ to evaluate the trace in Eq. (3). The limit as ε → 0 was taken using several values between 10⁻⁴ and 10⁻².

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: E_cm = 128 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca collisions
- Time step: Δt = 1.5 × 10⁻²⁴ s (with Δt = 10⁻²⁴ s used for convergence checks)
- Cartesian grid: 56 × 56 × 28/2 points with mesh size Δx = 0.8 fm
- Initial distance between collision partners: 22.4 fm
- Minimum separation distance for convergence of fluctuations: 22.4 fm
- Damped events defined by total kinetic energy loss (TKEL) ≥ 30 MeV, corresponding to angular momentum L < 82 ħ
- Capture (fusion) occurs for L ≤ 66 ħ, giving a fusion cross section of approximately 1140 mb
- Average mass fluctuation σ_AA ≈ 9.7 in the orbiting region, compared to experimental value σ_AA ≈ 11
- Fusion-fission cross section estimated at approximately 280 mb, compared to damped binary cross section of approximately 570 mb

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative applications of the BV variational principle to heavy-ion collisions by Marston (1985) and Bonche (1985), which used simple effective interactions and geometry restrictions, to realistic three-dimensional TDHF calculations with a full Skyrme energy-density functional including spin-orbit interaction. The results enable first microscopic quantum predictions of proton-neutron number correlations in fragments, which are shown to be larger in exotic systems where charge equilibration occurs, and provide a benchmark for future comparisons with data from exotic beam facilities.
