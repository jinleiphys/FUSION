# Chiral Lagrangians for mesons with a single heavy quark

> We construct the relativistic chiral Lagrangians for heavy-light mesons $(Q\bar{q})$ to the $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ order. From $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$, there are 17, 67, and 404 independent terms in the flavor $SU(2)$ case and 20, 84, and 655 independent terms in the flavor $SU(3)$ case. The Lagrangians in the heavy quark limit are also obtained. From $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$, there are 7, 25, and 136 independent terms in the flavor $SU(2)$ case and 8, 33, and 212 independent terms in the flavor $SU(3)$ case. The relations between low-energy constants based on the heavy quark symmetry are also given up to the $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ order.

# Chiral Lagrangians for mesons with a single heavy quark

## Key claim

The paper systematically constructs relativistic chiral Lagrangians for heavy-light mesons (Q\bar{q}) up to O(p^4) order, providing the complete sets of independent terms for both SU(2) and SU(3) flavor cases. The heavy quark symmetry relations between low-energy constants are given only up to O(p^3) order; at O(p^4) the authors cannot give strict relations because Schouten identity introduces O(p^1) order equations of motion, requiring inversion of a large symbolic matrix.

## Method

The authors construct chiral Lagrangians for heavy-light mesons (pseudoscalar P and vector P*) by enumerating all possible chiral-invariant combinations of building blocks (u^mu, chi_plus, f_plus^munu, h^munu, covariant derivatives) up to O(p^4). They reduce linearly dependent terms using partial integration, Schouten identity, equations of motion, Bianchi identity, Cayley-Hamilton relations, and contact term analysis, performed by computer. They also construct Lagrangians in the heavy quark limit using the superfield H containing both P and P*, then compare the relativistic and heavy-quark-limit forms to obtain relations between low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* At O(p^2), O(p^3), and O(p^4): 17, 67, and 404 independent relativistic terms in SU(2); 20, 84, and 655 independent relativistic terms in SU(3)
* In the heavy quark limit at O(p^2), O(p^3), and O(p^4): 7, 25, and 136 independent terms in SU(2); 8, 33, and 212 independent terms in SU(3)
* At O(p^1): 1 independent term in both relativistic and heavy-quark-limit cases for both SU(2) and SU(3)
* The leading-order coupling constants satisfy f_Q = 2 g_Q M = -2 g M, where M is the heavy-light meson mass in the heavy quark limit

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory constructions for heavy-light mesons, which previously existed only at leading order (Burdman 1992, Yan 1992, Wise 1992) or in partial higher-order forms for specific problems (Guo 2008, Guo 2009, Altenbuchinger 2013, Liu 2012, Yao 2015, and others). The systematic construction up to O(p^4) enables one-loop calculations of heavy-light meson properties, mass differences within doublets, Goldstone boson interactions, and open-flavor particle properties. The heavy quark symmetry relations between low-energy constants provide constraints that can guide future determinations of these constants from lattice QCD or other approaches.
