# Alpha clustering and flow in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show how ultra-relativistic collisions of light nuclei with heavy targets may be used to record snap-shots of the ground-state configurations and reveal information on cluster correlations. The development of collective flow in the formed fireball, which reflects the geometric correlations in the initial state, is essential for the method. As an illustration we analyze the 12C-208Pb collisions.

## Key claim

Ultra-relativistic collisions of light nuclei with heavy targets can serve as an instant snapshot of the ground-state configuration of the projectile, revealing alpha cluster correlations through the collective flow pattern of the fireball, provided that the fireball is large enough to develop collective behavior.

## Method

The authors model 12C-208Pb collisions using the GLISSANDO Glauber Monte Carlo approach, generating random nucleon positions in 12C either with alpha-clustered configurations (three alpha clusters arranged in an equilateral triangle) or uniform distributions, both reproducing the same one-body density from BEC or FMD calculations. The eccentricity parameters epsilon_n (ellipticity for n=2, triangularity for n=3) are computed event-by-event as Fourier coefficients of the transverse-plane source distribution, and their correlation with the number of wounded nucleons N_w is analyzed as a proxy for the harmonic flow coefficients v_n.

## Key numbers

- The short-distance NN repulsion is incorporated by precluding nucleon centers closer than 0.9 fm
- The radial distribution 4πr²ρ₂(r) peaks at the triangle side length l ≃ 3 fm
- The Lorentz contraction factor for colliding nuclei reaches 100 at RHIC
- SPS energies use σ_NN^inel = 32 mb for the Glauber model calculations
- The event-averaged triangularity ⟨ε₃⟩ increases with N_w for clustered 12C, while ⟨ε₂⟩ decreases, in contrast to the uniform case where both are non-zero only due to fluctuations

## Context

This work extends the approach of flow studies in d-A collisions (Bozek 2011) and ³H/³He-A collisions (Sickles 2013) to a larger system where collective effects are expected to be much stronger, making the shape-flow transmutation more visible. The method provides a novel bridge between low-energy nuclear structure physics of alpha clustering (Gamow 1931, Brink 1965, Freer 2007) and high-energy heavy-ion collision phenomenology, enabling experimental tests of ground-state correlations in light nuclei through transverse-momentum asymmetries in hadron spectra.
