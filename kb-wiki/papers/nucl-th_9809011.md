# Simple analytic solution of fireball hydrodynamics

> A new family of simple analytic solutions of hydrodynamics is found for non-relativistic, rotationally symmetric fireballs assuming an ideal gas equation of state. The solution features linear flow profile and a non-trivial transverse temperature profile. The radial temperature gradient vanishes only in the collisionless gas limit. The Zimanyi-Bondorf-Garpman solution and the Buda-Lund parameterization of expanding hydrodynamical sources are recovered as special cases. The results are applied to predict new features of proton-proton correlations and spectra data at 1.93 AGeV Ni + Ni reactions.

## Key claim

A new family of exact, analytic solutions to non-relativistic, rotationally symmetric fireball hydrodynamics is found for an ideal gas equation of state, but only if a non-vanishing radial temperature gradient is present; the collisionless (Knudsen) gas limit with a constant temperature profile is the only solution without such a gradient and lacks dynamical evolution.

## Method

The continuity, Euler, and energy equations for a non-relativistic ideal gas are solved using a self-similar ansatz where density, flow, and temperature depend on space and time through a scaling variable \(x = \mathbf{r}^2 / R^2(t)\). The flow profile is forced to be linear (\(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{r} R'/R\)) by the continuity equation, and the Euler and energy equations yield a time-dependent radial scale \(R(t)\) and a density profile expressed in terms of a freely chosen scaling function for the temperature profile \(\mathcal{T}(x)\). The solution is generalized to expansions in \(d\) spatial dimensions.

## Key numbers

* The radial scale evolves as \(R^2(t) = R_0^2 + \langle u_t \rangle^2 (t - t_0)^2\), where \(\langle u_t \rangle\) is the asymptotic expansion speed and \(R_0\) is the minimum radius at the turning point \(t_0\).
* In the collisionless (Knudsen) gas limit (\(\mathcal{T}(x)=1\)), the density profile is Gaussian with radius \(R_G^2 = R^2 T_0 / (m \langle u_t \rangle^2)\).
* The Zimanyi-Bondorf-Garpman solution is recovered with exponent \(\alpha = m \langle u_t \rangle^2 / (2 T_0) - 1\).
* The Buda-Lund Gaussian radius is given by \(1/R_G^2 = (1/R_0^2) (m \langle u_t \rangle^2 / T_0 - \langle \Delta T/T \rangle_r) \ge 0\).
* For a 3D expanding spherical shell example, parameters are \(R_0 = 5\) fm and \(m \langle u_t \rangle^2 / T_0 = 0.125\); the density develops two maxima when \(\langle \Delta T/T \rangle_r = 0.2 > m \langle u_t \rangle^2 / T_0\).
* The solution is applied to 1.93 AGeV Ni + Ni collisions measured by the FOPI Collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the Zimanyi-Bondorf-Garpman solution (Zimanyi, Bondorf, and Garpman, 1978) by providing a more general family of analytic hydro solutions that includes arbitrary initial temperature profiles. It also recovers the Buda-Lund parameterization (Csorgo and collaborators, 1995-1996) as a Gaussian approximation to an exact solution, constraining its cooling parameters. The results enable predictions of momentum-dependent effective source radii and deviations from exponential proton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, to be tested against FOPI data.
