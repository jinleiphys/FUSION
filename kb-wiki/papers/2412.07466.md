# Implementation and investigation of electron-nucleus scattering in the \textsc{NEUT} neutrino event generator

> Understanding nuclear effects is essential for improving the sensitivity of neutrino oscillation measurements. Validating nuclear models solely through neutrino scattering data is challenging due to limited statistics and the broad energy spectrum of neutrinos. In contrast, electron scattering experiments provide abundant high-precision data with various monochromatic energies and angles. Since both neutrinos and electrons interact via electroweak interactions, the same nuclear models can be applied to simulate both interactions. Thus, high-precision electron scattering data is essential for validating the nuclear models used in neutrino experiments. To enable this, the author has introduced a new electron scattering framework in the \textsc{NEUT} neutrino event generator, covering two interaction modes: quasielastic (QE) and single pion production. \textsc{NEUT} predictions of QE agree well with numerical calculations, supporting the validity of this implementation. From comparisons with \textsc{NEUT} predictions and inclusive electron scattering data, the momentum-dependent removal energy correction is derived, addressing effects beyond the plane wave impulse approximation. This correction is applied to neutrino interactions, observing significant changes in charged lepton kinematics. Notably, the reconstructed neutrino energy distribution shows a peak shift of approximately 20--30\,MeV, which is crucial for accurately measuring neutrino oscillation parameters.

## Key claim

The author claims that implementing electron-nucleus scattering in the NEUT neutrino event generator and deriving a momentum-dependent removal energy correction from comparisons with inclusive electron scattering data can address effects beyond the plane wave impulse approximation, with the caveat that the correction is empirically derived from a linear fit to peak shifts and may have bias from neglecting multinucleon interactions.

## Method

The author implemented quasielastic (QE) and single pion production electron-nucleus scattering modes in the NEUT event generator, extending the existing spectral function model for QE and the dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model for single pion production. The implementation was validated by comparing NEUT predictions with numerical calculations by Ankowski et al., and then compared against inclusive electron scattering data on carbon-12 and oxygen-16 from multiple experiments. A momentum-dependent removal energy correction was extracted by fitting Gaussian functions to the QE peak positions in both NEUT simulations and experimental data, parametrizing the peak shift as a linear function of three-momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-dependent removal energy correction for carbon-12 yields best-fit parameters a = 0.0556 ± 0.0021 and b = -0.0386 ± 0.0008 GeV for the linear function f(q₃) = aq₃ + b
- The reconstructed neutrino energy distribution shows a peak shift of approximately 20–30 MeV when the correction is applied
- NEUT uses a default Fermi momentum of p_F = 209 MeV for carbon-12 and oxygen-16
- The Coulomb potential is set to |V_eff| = 3.1 MeV for carbon-12 and 3.4 MeV for oxygen-16
- The axial mass is M_A = 1.21 GeV by default, with axial coupling constant g_A = -1.2673
- The vector mass is M_V = 0.84 GeV for the dipole parametrization
- The correlated term of the spectral function contributes approximately 20% to the total cross section
- Approximately 90 ± 10% of correlated nucleon pairs are pn, with pp and nn each contributing 5 ± 1.5%
- The pseudoscalar form factor contributes approximately 3% to the neutral-current cross section for bound nucleons
- The EM cross section increases approximately 1.6 times when the Q² cutoff is changed from 0.01 GeV² to 0.005 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the electron scattering capabilities of the NEUT event generator, following similar efforts in GENIE (since version 2), NuWro (as eWro), ACHILLES, and GiBUU. A prior implementation of electron scattering in NEUT by McElwee et al. was limited to QE based on the spectral function by Benhar et al. and was not incorporated into the standard distribution. The derived momentum-dependent removal energy correction addresses limitations of the plane wave impulse approximation, similar to the approach in Bodek (2019), and enables more accurate neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments such as T2K and NOvA. Future work requires implementing multinucleon interactions and accounting for removal energy in single pion production to enable more comprehensive validation.
