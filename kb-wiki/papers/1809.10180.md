# Local chiral interactions and magnetic structure of few-nucleon systems

> The magnetic form factors of $^2$H, $^3$H, and $^3$He, deuteron photodisintegration cross sections at low energies, and deuteron threshold electrodisintegration cross sections at backward angles in a wide range of momentum transfers, are calculated with the chiral two-nucleon (and three-nucleon) interactions including $Δ$ intermediate states that have recently been constructed in configuration space. The $A\,$=$\,$3 wave functions are obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics solutions of the Schrödinger equation. The electromagnetic current includes one- and two-body terms, the latter induced by one- and two-pion exchange (OPE and TPE, respectively) mechanisms and contact interactions. The contributions associated with $Δ$ intermediate states are only retained at the OPE level, and are neglected in TPE loop (tree-level) corrections to two-body (three-body) current operators. Expressions for these currents are derived and regularized in configuration space for consistency with the interactions. The low-energy constants that enter the contact few-nucleon systems. The predicted form factors and deuteron electrodisintegration cross section are in excellent agreement with experiment for momentum transfers up to 2--3 fm$^{-1}$. However, the experimental values for the deuteron photodisintegration cross section are consistently underestimated by theory, unless use is made of the Siegert form of the electric dipole transition operator. A complete analysis of the results is provided, including the clarification of the origin of the aforementioned discrepancy.

## Key claim
The chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions with Delta intermediate states, combined with electromagnetic currents up to N3LO, reproduce experimental magnetic form factors and deuteron electrodisintegration cross sections for momentum transfers up to 2-3 fm^-1, but the deuteron photodisintegration cross section is consistently underestimated unless the Siegert form of the electric dipole transition operator is used.

## Method
The authors calculated magnetic form factors of 2H, 3H, and 3He, deuteron photodisintegration cross sections at low energies, and deuteron threshold electrodisintegration cross sections at backward angles using chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions with Delta intermediate states constructed in configuration space. Wave functions for A=3 systems were obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics solutions of the Schrodinger equation, while the electromagnetic current included one- and two-body terms from one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange, and contact interactions, with Delta contributions retained only at the OPE level. The low-energy constants in the contact currents were determined by fitting the magnetic moments of 2H, 3H, and 3He for each Hamiltonian model considered.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron magnetic moment: experimental value 0.8574 n.m., reproduced by summing contributions from LO (0.8498 n.m. for Ia*), N2LO(RC) (-0.0062 n.m.), N3LO(MIN) (0.0002 n.m.), N3LO(NM) (0.0093 n.m.), and N3LO(OPE) (0.0042 n.m.) for model Ia*
- Trinucleon isoscalar magnetic moment: experimental value 0.4257 n.m., with LO contribution 0.4089 n.m. for Ia* and total reproduced by summing all contributions
- Trinucleon isovector magnetic moment: experimental value -2.553 n.m., with LO contribution -2.1823 n.m. for Ia* and total reproduced by summing all contributions
- Momentum transfer range for agreement with experiment: up to 2-3 fm^-1 for form factors and electrodisintegration cross sections
- Two classes of interactions fitted to laboratory energies: 0-125 MeV (class I) and 0-200 MeV (class II), with chi-squared per datum less than 1.1 and less than 1.4 respectively
- Cutoff radii sets: (0.8,1.2) fm (set a), (0.7,1.0) fm (set b), and (0.6,0.8) fm (set c)

## Context
This work extends the local chiral interactions developed by the Norfolk group (Piarulli 2015, 2016, 2018) by constructing consistent electromagnetic current operators up to N3LO and applying them to few-nucleon systems. The approach differs from earlier derivations by Park (1993, 1996) using covariant perturbation theory and by Koelling (2009, 2011) using time-ordered perturbation theory with unitary transformations, though the formal expressions for the current operator up to N3LO are the same. The results enable testing of the NV2+3 Hamiltonian models against electromagnetic observables and provide constraints on the low-energy constants that enter the three-nucleon interaction and two-nucleon axial current, which is relevant for understanding the collapse problem in neutron matter and the equation of state of neutron stars.
