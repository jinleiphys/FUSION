# Spectral functions at small energies and the electrical conductivity in hot, quenched lattice QCD

> In lattice QCD, the Maximum Entropy Method can be used to reconstruct spectral functions from euclidean correlators obtained in numerical simulations. We show that at finite temperature the most commonly used algorithm, employing Bryan's method, is inherently unstable at small energies and give a modification that avoids this. We demonstrate this approach using the vector current-current correlator obtained in quenched QCD at finite temperature. Our first results indicate a small electrical conductivity above the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The standard Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) using Bryan's algorithm is numerically unstable at small energies in finite-temperature lattice QCD because the kernel diverges as 1/ω; the authors propose a modification that redefines the kernel and spectral function to remove this divergence, enabling stable extraction of transport coefficients such as electrical conductivity.

## Method

The authors modified Bryan's MEM algorithm by multiplying the kernel K(ω,τ) by ω/2T and redefining the spectral function as ρ̄(ω) = (2T/ω)ρ(ω), so that the new kernel K̄(ω,τ) remains finite at ω=0. They applied this modified method to the vector current-current correlator obtained from quenched lattice QCD simulations using staggered quarks on lattices with Nτ=24 and Nτ=16 at temperatures above and below the deconfinement transition. The spectral function was reconstructed from even and odd time slices separately to handle staggered fermion doubling, then combined to obtain the full vector spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Electrical conductivity above the deconfinement transition: σ/T = 0.4 ± 0.1 (normalized to a single flavour)
- Lattice parameters: β=7.192, a⁻¹=9.72 GeV for hot (T/Tc=1.5, Nτ=24) and very hot (T/Tc=2.25, Nτ=16) ensembles; β=6.5, a⁻¹=4.04 GeV for cold ensemble (T/Tc=0.62, Nτ=24)
- Bare staggered quark mass: am=0.01
- Number of configurations: 100 for cold and hot ensembles, 50 for very hot ensemble
- MEM parameters: Nω=1000, aωmax=5, default model parameter b=1.0 (varied to 0.5 and 0.1 for systematic check)

## Context

This work extends prior MEM-based spectral function reconstructions in lattice QCD (Asakawa 2000, Bryan algorithm) by identifying and fixing a numerical instability specific to finite-temperature calculations. The results provide a first-principle lattice QCD estimate of electrical conductivity in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma above Tc, supporting the notion of a strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) suggested by RHIC phenomenology and the conjectured lower bound on viscosity (Kovtun 2004). The method enables future calculations of shear and bulk viscosities, and can be extended to dynamical quark simulations on anisotropic lattices (Aarts 2007).
