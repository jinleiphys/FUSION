# Momentum Space Integral Equations for Three Charged Particles: Diagonal Kernels

> It has been a long-standing question whether momentum space integral equations of the Faddeev type are applicable to reactions of three charged particles, in particular above the three-body threshold. For, the presence of long-range Coulomb forces has been thought to give rise to such severe singularities in their kernels that the latter may lack the compactness property known to exist in the case of purely short-range interactions. Employing the rigorously equivalent formulation in terms of an effective-two-body theory we have proved in a preceding paper [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 61}, 064006 (2000)] that, for all energies, the nondiagonal kernels occurring in the integral equations which determine the transition amplitudes for all binary collision processes, possess on and off the energy shell only integrable singularities, provided all three particles have charges of the same sign, i.e., all Coulomb interactions are repulsive. In the present paper we prove that, for particles with charges of equal sign, the diagonal kernels, in contrast, possess one, but only one, nonintegrable singularity. The latter can, however, be isolated explicitly and dealt with in a well-defined manner. Taken together these results imply that modified integral equations can be formulated, with kernels that become compact after a few iterations. This concludes the proof that standard solution methods can be used for the calculation of all binary (i.e., (in-)elastic and rearrangement) amplitudes by means of momentum space integral equations of the effective-two-body type.

## Key claim

The diagonal kernels of the effective-two-body AGS integral equations for three charged particles with repulsive Coulomb interactions (all charges of the same sign) possess exactly one nonintegrable singularity, which occurs on the energy shell and can be isolated explicitly and inverted, thereby proving that modified integral equations with compact kernels can be formulated for all energies, including above the three-body threshold.

## Method

The authors analyze the singularity structure of the diagonal effective potentials \( \mathcal{V}_{\alpha\alpha}(\mathbf{q}_\alpha', \mathbf{q}_\alpha; z) \) by decomposing them into two parts: a term \( \mathcal{V}^{(a)}_{\alpha\alpha} \) describing single Coulomb rescattering between the projectile and each target particle, and a term \( \tilde{\mathcal{V}}_{\alpha\alpha} \) containing full three-body Coulomb rescattering in the intermediate state. They then extract the leading singularities in the momentum transfer \( \Delta_\alpha = \mathbf{q}_\alpha' - \mathbf{q}_\alpha \) and apply a two-potential splitting procedure to separate the long-range center-of-mass Coulomb potential \( \mathfrak{v}_\alpha^C \) from the shorter-range remainder, leading to modified integral equations with kernels that become compact after iteration.

## Context

This paper completes the proof begun in Mukhamedzhanov, Alt, and Avakov (2000) (Phys. Rev. C 61, 064006), which showed that nondiagonal kernels of the effective-two-body AGS equations possess only integrable singularities for three charged particles with repulsive Coulomb interactions. The present work extends that analysis to diagonal kernels, confirming the earlier findings of Veselova (1970) for energies below breakup and of Alt and Sandhas (1980) for all energies. The results enable the use of standard solution methods for calculating all binary (elastic, inelastic, and rearrangement) amplitudes via momentum space integral equations, and they clarify that opposite-sign charges would introduce severe singularities precluding such standard methods, consistent with the integro-differential approach of Fonseca and Mathelitsch (1985).
