# Statistical hadronization with exclusive channels in e+e- annihilation

> We perform a systematic analysis of exclusive hadronic channels in e+e- collisions at centre-of-mass energies between 2.1 and 2.6 GeV within the statistical hadronization model. Because of the low multiplicities involved, calculations have been carried out in the full microcanonical ensemble, including conservation of energy-momentum, angular momentum, parity, isospin, and all relevant charges. We show that the data is in an overall good agreement with the model for an energy density of about 0.5 GeV/fm^3 and an extra strangeness suppression parameter gamma_S ~ 0.7, essentially the same values found with fits to inclusive multiplicities at higher energy.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model (SHM) in the full microcanonical ensemble, including conservation of energy-momentum, angular momentum, parity, isospin, and all relevant charges, can reproduce exclusive hadronic channel data in e+e- collisions at center-of-mass energies between 2.1 and 2.6 GeV with the same energy density and strangeness suppression parameter found in fits to inclusive multiplicities at higher energy, though the hadron-resonance gas decomposition used for exclusive channels is an approximation that goes beyond the strict validity of the Dashen-Ma-Bernstein theorem.

## Method

The authors calculated microcanonical channel weights for exclusive hadronic final states using a formalism that enforces conservation of the maximal set of observables from space-time symmetries (energy-momentum, spin, parity) and internal symmetries (isospin, C-parity, abelian charges) for a single spherical hadronizing cluster at rest. They extended previous microcanonical partition function calculations to include the hadron-resonance gas with resonance decays, then compared the predicted exclusive channel production rates to experimental data from e+e- collisions at low center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

- Energy density of about 0.5 GeV/fm^3, extracted from fits to exclusive channel data
- Strangeness suppression parameter gamma_S ~ 0.7, extracted from fits to exclusive channel data
- Center-of-mass energy range analyzed: between 2.1 and 2.6 GeV
- Cluster volume radius R appears in the Fourier integrals F_V^(s) via spherical Bessel functions j_1, with the volume comparable to the third power of the pion Compton wavelength (order 1 fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model, previously applied successfully to inclusive multiplicities in elementary collisions (Becattini, 2000s) and relativistic heavy ion collisions, to the more sensitive test of exclusive channel production rates at low energy where a single cluster is expected. The analysis uses the microcanonical formalism developed by the same authors (Ferroni and Becattini, 2010) and addresses the open question of whether the apparent statistical equilibrium in elementary collisions is genuine or merely phase space dominance, as debated in earlier work by Hagedorn and others.
