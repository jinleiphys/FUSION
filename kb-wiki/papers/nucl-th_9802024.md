# Low-energy photodisintegration of 9Be and alpha + alpha + n <-> 9Be + gamma reactions at astrophysical conditions

> A semi-microscopic model for the low-energy photodisintegration of the 9Be nucleus is constructed, and the experimental data are analyzed with its help. The older radioactive isotope data are supported by this analysis. The theoretical photodisintegration cross section is derived. The astrophysical rates for the reaction alpha + alpha + n -> 9Be + gamma and the reverse photodisintegration of 9Be are calculated. The new reaction rate for alpha + alpha + n -> 9Be + gamma is compared with previous estimations.

# Low-energy photodisintegration of 9Be and alpha + alpha + n <-> 9Be + gamma reactions at astrophysical conditions

## Key claim
The older radioactive isotope data for the low-energy photodisintegration of 9Be are supported by the semi-microscopic model, but the theoretical cross section depends on fitting a Woods-Saxon potential to the data, and the model cannot reproduce the experimental binding energy without reducing the n-alpha potential strength by 8%.

## Method
A semi-microscopic three-body model (alpha + alpha + n) was constructed for the 9Be ground state, using Faddeev differential equations expanded over hyperspherical harmonics. The continuum wave function was represented as a product of the 8Be intrinsic wave function (treated as a stable particle due to its 7 keV width) and an s-wave n-8Be relative motion function obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential whose parameters were fitted to the experimental photodisintegration cross section data. The dipole transition matrix element between the ground and continuum states was then calculated to derive the photodisintegration cross section.

## Key numbers
- The 9Be binding energy obtained after reducing the n-alpha potential strength by 8% is 1.50 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.5736 MeV.
- The charge radius of 9Be obtained is 2.48 fm, compared to the experimental value of (2.51 ± 0.01) fm.
- The 8Be resonance width is 7 keV, justifying its treatment as a stable particle.
- The 9Be photodisintegration threshold for the 8Be + n channel is 1.6654 MeV; the alpha + alpha + n threshold is 1.5736 MeV.
- The effective bound-state wave function normalization integral is 0.43.
- For the potential fit to the older radioactive isotope data, the scattering length a = -27.6 fm and effective range r0 = 8.79 fm (set 1); alternatively a = -28.4 fm and r0 = 9.77 fm (set 2).
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom values for the two main fits are 0.62 and 0.525.
- The astrophysical reaction rate fit for N_A^2 <alpha alpha n> is given by 6.59 x 10^(-6) T_9^(-3/2) exp(-1.0653/T_9) times a polynomial denominator, valid for 0.1 ≤ T_9 ≤ 8 with better than 1% accuracy.

## Context
This work extends earlier estimates by Fowler (1975) and Gorres (1995) of the reaction rates for alpha + alpha + n -> 9Be + gamma, which is a key reaction bridging the mass 5 and 8 gaps in the alpha-process during r-process nucleosynthesis in type II supernovae. The model improves upon the single-particle potential approach of Corman (1963) and the two-channel model of Blair (1961) by using a three-body description of the 9Be ground state. The new reaction rates agree with Fowler (1975) at T_9 = 2.0 but differ at lower and higher temperatures, and they agree with Gorres (1995) only under specific assumptions about interference between resonant and non-resonant contributions. The results enable more reliable calculations of nucleosynthesis yields in the high-entropy bubble of supernovae.
