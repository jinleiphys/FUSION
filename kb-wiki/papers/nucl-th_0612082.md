# Differential Cross Sections for Electromagnetic Dissociation

> Differential cross sections for electromagnetic dissociation in nucleus-nucleus collisions are calculated. The kinetic energy distribution is parameterized with a Boltzmann distribution and the angular distribution is assumed isotropic in the projectile frame. In order to be useful for three-dimensional transport codes, these cross sections are available in both the projectile and lab frames. Comparison between theory and experiment is good. The formalism applies to single and multiple nucleon removal, alpha particle removal, and fission in electromagnetic reactions of nuclei.

## Key claim

Differential cross sections for electromagnetic dissociation in nucleus-nucleus collisions are calculated using a Boltzmann distribution for kinetic energy and an isotropic angular distribution in the projectile frame, with the claim that comparison between theory and experiment is good only after modifying the nuclear temperature formula from \(k\Theta = \sqrt{10 E_\gamma / A_P}\) to \(k\Theta = \sqrt{20 E_\gamma / A_P}\) and fitting the absolute peak cross section to experimental data in arbitrary units.

## Method

The authors compute nucleus-nucleus differential cross sections by integrating photonuclear differential cross sections over the Weiszacker-Williams virtual photon spectrum. The photonuclear angular distribution is assumed isotropic, and the spectral distribution uses a Boltzmann form with nuclear temperature \(k\Theta = \sqrt{10 E_\gamma / A_P}\) (later modified to \(\sqrt{20 E_\gamma / A_P}\) for fitting). The resulting cross sections are evaluated in the projectile frame and then Lorentz transformed to the lab frame for use in three-dimensional transport codes.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear temperature parameterization is given as \(k\Theta = \sqrt{10 E_\gamma / A_P}\) (equation 4), later modified to \(k\Theta = \sqrt{20 E_\gamma / A_P}\) for best fit to experiment.
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn cross section is \(\sigma_{\rm TRK} = 60 N_P Z_P / A_P\) MeV mb.
- The GDR energy parameters include \(r_0 = 1.18\) fm, \(J = 36.8\) MeV, \(Q^\prime = 17\) MeV, \(\epsilon = 0.0768\), and \(m^* = 0.7 m_{\rm nucleon}\).
- The example reaction is \({}^{28}{\rm Si} + {}^{208}{\rm Pb} \rightarrow n + {}^{27}{\rm Si} + {}^{208}{\rm Pb}\) at 14.6 A GeV.
- The double differential cross section in the projectile frame shows a single peak near 3 MeV kinetic energy (Fig. 1), which becomes a double peak in the lab frame (Fig. 2).

## Context

This work extends prior parameterizations of total EMD cross sections (Norbury 2000; Norbury 2003; Norbury 2004) to differential cross sections needed for fully three-dimensional transport codes such as HZETRN and FLUKA. The isotropic angular distribution assumption agrees with experimental angular distributions measured by Barrette (1992, 1995), and the spectral distribution comparison uses data from Barrette (1995) for proton and neutron kinetic energy distributions. The formalism enables inclusion of single and multiple nucleon removal, alpha particle removal, and fission in transport codes by using appropriate branching ratios and fission product masses.
