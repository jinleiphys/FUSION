# On the Evolution of Jet Energy and Opening Angle in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> We calculate how the energy and the opening angle of jets in ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory evolve as they propagate through the strongly coupled plasma of that theory. We define the rate of energy loss $dE_{\rm jet}/dx$ and the jet opening angle in a straightforward fashion directly in the gauge theory before calculating both holographically, in the dual gravitational description. In this way, we rederive the previously known result for $dE_{\rm jet}/dx$ without the need to introduce a finite slab of plasma. We obtain a striking relationship between the initial opening angle of the jet, which is to say the opening angle that it would have had if it had found itself in vacuum instead of in plasma, and the thermalization distance of the jet. Via this relationship, we show that ${\cal N}=4$ SYM jets with any initial energy that have the same initial opening angle and the same trajectory through the plasma experience the same fractional energy loss. We also provide an expansion that describes how the opening angle of the ${\cal N}=4$ SYM jets increases slowly as they lose energy, over the fraction of their lifetime when their fractional energy loss is not yet large. We close by looking ahead toward potential qualitative lessons from our results for QCD jets produced in heavy collisions and propagating through quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for jets in strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory, the thermalization distance and fractional energy loss are entirely determined by the initial opening angle and plasma temperature, independent of the initial jet energy, provided the jet has vanishingly small initial size and the thermalization distance is much larger than $1/T$.

## Method

The authors define the jet opening angle via the half-width-at-half-maximum of the energy flux $\Phi(\bm x) = \int dt \langle T^{0x}(t,\bm x) \rangle$ and the energy loss rate via the flux of energy through a sphere surrounding the jet. They compute these quantities holographically using the dual gravitational description of ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory, where jets correspond to arcs of falling open strings in an asymptotically AdS$_5$ spacetime. The calculations are performed in the "steady-state region" where $x \gg 1/T$ and $x_{\rm therm} - x \gg 1/T$, allowing for clean definitions of instantaneous jet properties.

## Key numbers

- Initial opening angle: $\theta_{\rm jet}^{\rm init} = \kappa (E_{\rm init}/E_0)^{-2/3}$ with $\kappa \approx 0.204157$ for the half-width-at-half-maximum definition
- Thermalization distance: $x_{\rm therm} = (1/T) \sqrt{\kappa/\theta^{\rm init}_{\rm jet}}$, independent of initial energy
- Energy loss rate in SSR: $(1/E_{\rm init}) \, dE_{\rm jet}/dx = -4x^2/(\pi x_{\rm therm}^2 \sqrt{x_{\rm therm}^2 - x^2})$
- Opening angle expansion at small $x/x_{\rm therm}$: $\theta_{\rm jet}/\theta_{\rm jet}^{\rm init} \approx 1 + 2(x/(a x_{\rm therm}))^4 + \ldots$ with $a \approx 0.763$
- As $x \to x_{\rm therm}$: $\theta_{\rm jet} \sim \theta_{\rm jet}^{\rm init} / [1 - x/x_{\rm therm}]^2$
- At $x/x_{\rm therm} = 0.8$: jet energy decreased by 30%; at $x/x_{\rm therm} = 0.99$: decreased by 83%
- Maximum value of constant $\mathcal C$ (where $E_0 = T\sqrt{\lambda}/\mathcal C^3$) estimated around 0.5 to 1

## Context

This work extends the holographic jet studies of Chesler and Rajagopal (2014) by dispensing with the finite slab of plasma and deriving results directly from the gauge theory stress tensor. The relationship between initial opening angle and thermalization distance resolves discrepancies among different authors (Ficnar 2013, Arnold 2010-2012) who predicted qualitatively different energy loss rates using different jet models. The results enable the hybrid model for jet quenching introduced by Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2015), which applies the ${\cal N}=4$ SYM energy loss rate parton-by-parton to QCD jets, and provide qualitative insights into how real jets in heavy ion collisions change shape as they lose energy.
