# Propagation of QCD Color through Strongly Interacting Systems

> The propagation of QCD color through atomic nuclei is studied via a new analysis using a geometric model of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. The experimental data were previously published by the HERMES Collaboration and consisted of the multiplicity ratio observable (2007) and the transverse momentum broadening observable (2010). We perform a simultaneous fit of these two observables to estimate (1) the color lifetime of the quark, (2) quark energy loss, (3) the $\hat{q}$ transport coefficient, and (4) the cross section for hadronic interaction with the medium. We present preliminary results for this fit.

# Propagation of QCD Color through Strongly Interacting Systems

## Key claim
The authors perform a simultaneous fit of two HERMES observables using a geometric model of nuclear densities and parton trajectories, but present these results as **preliminary** estimates of the color lifetime, quark energy loss, the $\hat{q}$ transport coefficient, and the hadronic interaction cross section, rather than as definitive measurements.

## Method
The authors constructed a geometric Monte Carlo model that uses known nuclear density distributions to simulate parton trajectories through nuclei. They performed a simultaneous fit to two previously published HERMES observables, the multiplicity ratio (2007) and transverse momentum broadening (2010), for positive pions produced in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on neon, krypton, and xenon targets compared to light nuclei. The fit extracted four parameters: the color lifetime $L_c$, the $\hat{q}$-related parameter $q_0$, the effective prehadron-nucleon cross section $\sigma$, and the quark energy loss, with the latter studied in a model variant labeled BLE30 where the hadronic cross section was fixed at 30 mb.

## Key numbers
- Color lifetime decreases from approximately **8 fm/c** to approximately **2 fm/c** as $z$ ranges from **0.3 to 0.9**, with a functional form similar to the Lund String model.
- The effective hadronic cross section results for $z>0.5$ are smaller than the reference line of **40 mb** from pion-nucleon scattering, consistent with expectations for a forming hadron.
- Quark energy loss shows essentially no $z$ dependence, with an average value shown for $z>0.5$ (exact numerical value not provided in text).
- The $\hat{q}$ values are found to be nearly independent of $z$ (exact numerical value not provided in text).

## Context
This work extends the experimental program of the HERMES Collaboration (2007, 2010) on nuclear effects in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, providing a unified geometric framework to simultaneously describe both the multiplicity ratio and transverse momentum broadening. The approach enables extraction of dynamical quantities as functions of the light-cone momentum fraction $z$, and the results are consistent with the Lund String model for color lifetime. The method is relevant for future cold matter studies at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and builds on theoretical ideas about parton energy loss and hadron formation in nuclear media.
