# Isospin singlet (pn) pairing and quartetting contribution to the binding energy of nuclei

> Isospin singlet (pn) pairing as well as quartetting in nuclei is expected to arise near the symmetry line $N=Z$. Empirical values can be deduced from the nuclear binding energies applying special filters. Within the local density approximation, theoretical estimates for finite nuclei are obtained from results for the condensation energy of asymmetric nuclear matter. It is shown that the isospin singlet condensation energy drops down abruptly for |N-Z|~4 for medium nuclei in the region A=40. Furthermore, alpha-like quartetting and the influence of excitations are discussed.

# Isospin singlet (pn) pairing and quartetting contribution to the binding energy of nuclei

## Key claim

Isospin-singlet proton-neutron pairing and alpha-like quartetting contribute to the binding energy of nuclei near the N=Z line, but this contribution drops abruptly for |N-Z| approximately 4 in medium-mass nuclei around A=40, and the effect is strongest for lighter nuclei (Z<20).

## Method

The authors extracted empirical contributions to binding energy from proton-neutron correlations by applying numerical filters (horizontal filter h, vertical filter v, and combinations w and c) to experimental nuclear binding energies, isolating the asymmetry-dependent term b_i(Z). They then performed theoretical calculations using the local density approximation (LDA), computing the condensation energy density from the solution of the BCS gap equation for asymmetric nuclear matter with a separable Yamaguchi-type interaction, and integrating over density profiles obtained from a Thomas-Fermi model with a Shlomo parameterization of the nuclear mean field.

## Key numbers

- The isospin singlet condensation energy drops abruptly for |N-Z| approximately 4 for medium nuclei in the region A=40.
- The critical temperature for isospin singlet pairing in symmetric nuclear matter reaches up to approximately 5 MeV at one third of nuclear matter density.
- The transition to quartetting beats the transition to isospin singlet pairing at densities below 0.03 fm^{-3}.
- The coupling strength was taken as lambda=92.35 MeV fm^3 in the ^1S_0 channel and lambda=131.50 MeV fm^3 in the ^3S_1 channel to reproduce the average Wigner energy of 2.62 MeV for mass numbers 20 <= A <= 100.
- The additional contribution to condensation energy from quartetting compared to isospin singlet pairing alone is 10.3 percent for ^12C and 9.6 percent for ^16O.
- The ratio of the w-filter for 2^+ excited states to ground states, averaged from Z=10 to Z=26, is 0.64 plus or minus 0.19.
- The average 2^+ excitation energy for even-even nuclei with 10 <= Z <= 30 is 1750 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin singlet pairing in nuclear matter by BLS (1995) and AFRS (1995) and of the Wigner energy by Satula (1997) and Satula (1997a), who developed methods to extract the Wigner term from experimental data. The authors use the local density approximation previously applied successfully to pp and nn pairing by ST (1995). The results enable a microscopic interpretation of the Wigner term in the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula and suggest that isoscalar pairing and quartetting provide constraints on the effective neutron-proton interaction in nuclear medium, with implications for clustering and condensation phenomena in N approximately Z nuclei.
