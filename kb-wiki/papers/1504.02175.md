# Observation of charge asymmetry dependence of pion elliptic flow and the possible chiral magnetic wave in heavy-ion collisions

> We present measurements of $π^-$ and $π^+$ elliptic flow, $v_2$, at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} =$ 200, 62.4, 39, 27, 19.6, 11.5 and 7.7 GeV, as a function of event-by-event charge asymmetry, $A_{ch}$, based on data from the STAR experiment at RHIC. We find that $π^-$ ($π^+$) elliptic flow linearly increases (decreases) with charge asymmetry for most centrality bins at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = \text{27 GeV}$ and higher. At $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = \text{200 GeV}$, the slope of the difference of $v_2$ between $π^-$ and $π^+$ as a function of $A_{ch}$ exhibits a centrality dependence, which is qualitatively similar to calculations that incorporate a chiral magnetic wave effect. Similar centrality dependence is also observed at lower energies.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports that the difference in elliptic flow between negative and positive pions increases linearly with event-by-event charge asymmetry in Au+Au collisions, with a centrality dependence that is qualitatively similar to calculations incorporating a chiral magnetic wave (CMW) effect. The authors hedge that while the data reproduce the CMW expectation, conventional models such as local charge conservation, UrQMD, and AMPT cannot explain the observation, and that quantitative comparison with theory requires further work.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured the elliptic flow (v2) of π⁻ and π⁺ at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, using the Time Projection Chamber for track reconstruction and particle identification via energy loss. The observed charge asymmetry Ach was determined from charged particles with pT > 0.15 GeV/c and |η| < 1, and events were divided into five Ach bins of roughly equal population. The slope parameter r of the linear fit to v2(π⁻) – v2(π⁺) versus Ach was extracted for each centrality bin, using the v2{η sub} method for energies ≤ 62.4 GeV and the v2{2} method for 200 GeV, with an η gap of 0.3 units to suppress short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the slope parameter r for the v2 difference between π⁻ and π⁺ as a function of Ach reaches a maximum in mid-central/mid-peripheral collisions (approximately 30-40% centrality), with a positive value that is qualitatively consistent with CMW calculations.
- The analysis used Au+Au event samples of 2×10⁸ events at 200 GeV, 6×10⁷ at 62.4 GeV, 10⁸ at 39 GeV, 4.6×10⁷ at 27 GeV, 2×10⁷ at 19.6 GeV, 1×10⁷ at 11.5 GeV, and 4×10⁶ at 7.7 GeV.
- Only pions with 0.15 < pT < 0.5 GeV/c were used for the pT-integrated v2, covering 65-70% of all produced pions.
- For 30-40% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the mean charge asymmetry ⟨Ach⟩ from HIJING events is about 0.004.
- The slope parameter r remains significantly positive for 10-60% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 27-200 GeV, while at 11.5 and 7.7 GeV the slopes are consistent with zero within large statistical uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) beyond previous charge separation fluctuation measurements by STAR (STAR Collaboration, 2009-2014) and PHENIX (PHENIX Collaboration, 2010-2011) that provided possible evidence for the chiral magnetic effect (CME). The new approach uses the Ach dependence of pion elliptic flow to isolate the CMW signal, which is predicted to create an electric quadrupole moment in the collision system (Kharzeev and Yee, 2011; Burnier et al., 2011). The results enable quantitative tests of CMW calculations (Yee, 2013; Hirano et al., 2013; Wiedemann and others, 2013) and motivate further theoretical work to match kinematic regions, as well as future measurements of K± v2 slopes to distinguish CMW from mean-field potential effects.
