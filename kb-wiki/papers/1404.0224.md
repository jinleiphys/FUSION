# Nilsson-SU3 selfconsistency in heavy N=Z nuclei

> It is argued that there exist natural shell model spaces optimally adapted to the operation of two variants of Elliott' SU3 symmetry that provide accurate predictions of quadrupole moments of deformed states. A selfconsistent Nilsson-like calculation describes the competition between the realistic quadrupole force and the central field, indicating a {\em remarkable stability of the quadruplole moments}---which remain close to their quasi and pseudo SU3 values---as the single particle splittings increase. A detailed study of the $N=Z$ even nuclei from $^{56}$Ni to $^{96}$Cd reveals that the region of prolate deformation is bounded by a pair of transitional nuclei $^{72}$Kr and $^{84}$Mo in which prolate ground state bands are predicted to dominate, though coexisting with oblate ones,

## Key claim

The paper argues that quadrupole moments of deformed states in heavy N=Z nuclei remain remarkably stable and close to their quasi and pseudo SU3 values even as single particle splittings increase, with the region of prolate deformation bounded by the transitional nuclei 72Kr and 84Mo where prolate ground state bands are predicted to dominate but coexist with oblate ones.

## Method

The authors define Extended Extruder-Intruder (EEI) model spaces (specifically r3gds) that combine pseudo-SU3 symmetry in the r3 subspace with quasi-SU3 symmetry in the gds subspace. They implement a selfconsistent Nilsson-like calculation (the Martinez-Zuker or MZ equations) that linearizes the quadrupole force in the presence of a central field, iterating until input and output quadrupole moments converge. The method is applied to even N=Z nuclei from 56Ni to 96Cd, using the JUN45 interaction for exact diagonalizations in the r3g space and the selfconsistent approach for the full r3gds space where exact diagonalizations are not feasible.

## Key numbers

- For 72Kr with 12 particles in r3 and 4 in gds: oblate Q0/b2 = -(30.2+3+16) = -49.2; prolate Q0/b2 = 30.2+3+27.32 = 60.52
- For A=72, b2 = 4.42 e fm2, giving Q0 ≈ -217 e fm2 (oblate) and 267 e fm2 (prolate)
- B(E2:2+→0+) for 72Kr: approximately 936 e2 fm4 (oblate) and 1422 e2 fm4 (prolate) using e0=2; or 725 e2 fm4 (oblate) and 1101 e2 fm4 (prolate) using e0=1.77
- The normalization constants for the quadrupole force: N2q,3 = sqrt(90×2.5) ≈ 15; N2q,4 = sqrt(210×2.5) ≈ 23
- The universal quadrupole coupling constant κ ≈ 0.22-0.25, fixed at κ=0.3 in the calculations
- For 68Se (r3^12): intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0/b2 = 41.21, B(E2) ≈ 413.64 e2 fm4 from monopole-free q·q calculations
- For (gds)^8 with single particle energies [0,0,0] MeV: B(E2) = 857.959 e2 fm4; with [0,1,2] MeV: B(E2) = 795.566 e2 fm4
- For (gds)^12 with single particle energies [0,0,0] MeV: B(E2) = 1161.574 e2 fm4; with [0,1,2] MeV: B(E2) = 1065.721 e2 fm4

## Context

This work extends the Elliott SU3 symmetry framework (Elliott, 1958) by developing pseudo-SU3 and quasi-SU3 variants adapted to realistic shell model spaces, building on the Extruder-Intruder (EI) model spaces previously introduced by Zuker (1994). The selfconsistent approach revives and completes the Martinez-Zuker formalism (Martinez-Pinedo and Zuker, 1997) which was left unfinished due to a subtlety in the normalization of the quadrupole operator. The results enable quantitative predictions for quadrupole moments and B(E2) transition strengths in heavy N=Z nuclei where full large-scale shell model diagonalizations are not feasible, particularly in the region from 72Kr to 84Mo where prolate-oblate shape coexistence is expected.
