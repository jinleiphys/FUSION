# |Delta I|=3/2 Decays of the Omega^- in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study the decays Omega^- -> Cascade+pion using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to quantify the |Delta I|=1/2 rule in these decay modes. The ratio of |Delta I|=3/2 to |Delta I|=1/2 amplitudes is somewhat larger in these decays than it is in other hyperon decays. At leading order there are two operators responsible for the |Delta I|=3/2 parts of the Omega^- decays which also contribute at one loop to other hyperon decays. These one-loop contributions are sufficiently large to indicate (albeit not definitely) that the measured width ratio of Omega^-->Cascade^0+pion^- to Omega^-->Cascade^-+pion^0 = 2.7 may be too large.

## Key claim

The measured ratio of Omega^- decay widths, Gamma(Omega^- -> Xi^0 pi^-)/Gamma(Omega^- -> Xi^- pi^0) ≈ 2.7, implies a |Delta I|=3/2 amplitude that appears large enough to conflict with measured |Delta I|=3/2 amplitudes in octet-hyperon non-leptonic decays, but within current errors and without additional assumptions about the relative size of the two coupling constants, the two sets of measurements are not in definite conflict.

## Method

The authors construct the next-to-leading-order chiral Lagrangian (O(p)) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory that generates |Delta I|=3/2, |Delta S|=1 transitions involving the Omega^- baryon (decuplet) and Xi baryon (octet) with a pion. They extract the combination of coupling constants (3C_27 + C'_27) from the measured Omega^- -> Xi pi decay rates, then compute the one-loop contributions of this Lagrangian to the |Delta I|=3/2 amplitudes in octet-hyperon non-leptonic decays (Sigma, Lambda, Xi decays) and compare these contributions to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- Measured ratio Gamma(Omega^- -> Xi^0 pi^-)/Gamma(Omega^- -> Xi^- pi^0) ≈ 2.7
- Extracted |Delta I|=3/2 to |Delta I|=1/2 amplitude ratio for Omega^- decays: alpha_3^(Omega)/alpha_1^(Omega) = -0.072 ± 0.013
- Extracted P-wave amplitudes: A^(P)_(Omega^- Xi^- pi^0) = (3.31 ± 0.08) GeV^{-1}, A^(P)_(Omega^- Xi^0 pi^-) = (5.48 ± 0.09) GeV^{-1}
- Fitted coupling combination: 3C_27 + C'_27 = (8.7 ± 1.6) × 10^{-3} G_F m_pi^2
- Kaon-pole contribution to alpha_3^(Omega) is less than 10% of the experimental value
- The most sensitive linear combination of octet-hyperon amplitudes gives (S_3^(Sigma) - 4.2 P_3^(Xi))_Exp = -0.2 ± 0.1, with the new theoretical contribution ≈ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the study of the |Delta I|=1/2 rule from octet-hyperon non-leptonic decays (previously analyzed by He and Valencia (1995) and Jenkins (1992)) to the decuplet Omega^- decays. The measured Omega^- decay ratio of 2.7 (compared to the pure |Delta I|=1/2 prediction of 2) had been claimed in the literature to signal a violation of the |Delta I|=1/2 rule. The authors find that the one-loop contributions from the Omega^- sector to octet-hyperon decays are sufficiently large to suggest the measured Omega^- rates may be too large, but the different coupling combinations involved prevent a definitive conclusion. This enables future experimental checks of the Omega^- decay widths and refined chiral perturbation theory calculations.
