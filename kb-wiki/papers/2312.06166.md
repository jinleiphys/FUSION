# Rotating gluon system and confinement

> In this work the non-abelian gauge theory is reformulated in a local inertial frame with the presence of a background rotation. With this new formalism the influence of the background rotation on the color deconfinement transition for a SU(2) pure gluon system. The KvBLL caloron, which is a color neutral and asymptotically nontrivial solution of Yang-Mills equation at finite temperature, is adopted to confine the color charges. With new solutions of the caloron's constituent particles, i.e. dyons, the semi-classical potential, which confines color charges, and the perturbative potential, induced by the Gaussian fluctuation, have been obtained for this system under rotation. By solving the critical temperature of confinement-deconfinement phase transition in different computation schemes, it is found that neither the rotational semi-classical potential nor Gaussian fluctuations can confine color charges more tightly when the rotation becomes faster. While only a stronger coupling constant is able to make the critical temperature increasing with angular velocity, as that indicated in lattice simulations. And it is also found with some particular sets of parameters, a non-monotonic dependence of the critical temperature will be obtained in the most physically realistic case, in which all the three factors are taken into account.

# Rotating gluon system and confinement

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a SU(2) pure gluon system under rotation, neither the rotational semi-classical potential nor Gaussian fluctuations can confine color charges more tightly when rotation becomes faster; only a stronger coupling constant can make the critical temperature increase with angular velocity as indicated in lattice simulations, and with particular parameter sets a non-monotonic dependence of the critical temperature can be obtained when all three factors are considered.

## Method

The authors reformulate non-abelian gauge theory in a local inertial frame with background rotation using vierbein fields, then adopt the KvBLL caloron (constructed from M and L dyons) as the confining vacuum. They solve the rotation-modified Yang-Mills equation to obtain new dyon solutions where the temporal component acquires a shift proportional to the angular velocity, compute the semi-classical free energy from the dyon actions, and combine it with the perturbative free energy from Gaussian fluctuations around the background caloron. The critical temperature of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition is determined by minimizing the total free energy as a function of the caloron asymptotic profile parameter.

- The critical temperature decreases with real angular velocity when using constant coupling and rotational perturbative free energy (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given for the decrease)
- The critical temperature increases with imaginary angular velocity under the same scheme (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given)
- With a rotation-dependent coupling constant of the form $g(\omega) = (1 + 0.1 |\omega|/\Lambda) g$ and static perturbative free energy, the critical temperature increases with real angular velocity (Fig. 2)
- The system radius is set to $R = 2(\pi T)^{-1}$ and angular velocity to $\omega = 0.9 R^{-1}$ for exhibiting rotation effects
- The dyon action is given by $8\pi^2/g^2 \cdot \rho_D/(2\pi T)$ for a dyon with parameter $\rho_D$

## Context

This work extends the KvBLL caloron approach to confinement (Kraan 1998, Lee 1998, Diakonov 2005) by incorporating background rotation, and addresses the unexpected critical temperature behavior suggested by lattice QCD simulations (Braguta 2021, Yang 2023). It differs from prior studies (Fujimoto 2021, Chen 2020, Braga 2022, Chen 2022) by taking into account all three factors: rotational effects on the background gauge field, Gaussian fluctuations with finite-size and angular momentum polarization effects, and the correlation between coupling constant and rotation speed. The work enables future studies of the full ensemble of calorons and anti-calorons with arbitrary ratios, and suggests that the competition between coupling strengthening and rotational deconfinement effects may produce non-monotonic critical temperature behavior.
