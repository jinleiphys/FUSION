# The $T_{bc}$ tetraquarks near the $B\bar{D}$ threshold

> We study the doubly heavy open-flavor tetraquarks $T_{bc}^{(0)}$ ($J^{P}=0^{+}$) and $T_{bc}^{(1)}$ ($J^{P}=1^{+}$) in the dynamical diquark model, describing the system as a heavy antidiquark--light diquark pair interacting through the lattice-QCD $Σ_g^+(1S)$ Born--Oppenheimer potential. Solving the radial Schrödinger equation yields $M(T_{bc}^{(0)}) = 7.143$--$7.158$ GeV and $M(T_{bc}^{(1)}) = 7.217$--$7.222$ GeV, with hyperfine splittings of $Δ_{HF}\simeq 59$--$79$ MeV. The splitting is driven mainly by the mass difference between symmetric and antisymmetric heavy-antidiquark configurations, while the chromomagnetic interaction contributes linearly with $\partialΔ_{HF}/\partialκ_{\bar b\bar c}=2$, consistent with heavy-antidiquark spin algebra. The mean separation, $\langle r\rangle\simeq 0.45$--$0.46$ fm, and inverse radius, $\langle 1/r\rangle^{-1}\simeq 0.33$--$0.34$ fm, exhibit weak parameter dependence and support a compact diquark--antidiquark interpretation. Relative to open-flavor thresholds, the scalar state lies essentially at the $B\bar D$ threshold and may appear either as a weakly decaying bound tetraquark or as a narrow near-threshold resonance. In contrast, the axial-vector state is consistently predicted as an $S$-wave resonance located $23$--$28$ MeV above $B^{*}\bar D$ and about $70$ MeV below $B\bar D^{*}$, implying a line shape strongly influenced by the nearby $B^{*}\bar D$ threshold.

## Key claim

The scalar $T_{bc}^{(0)}$ state lies essentially at the $B\bar{D}$ threshold and may appear either as a weakly decaying bound tetraquark or as a narrow near-threshold resonance, while the axial-vector $T_{bc}^{(1)}$ state is consistently predicted as an $S$-wave resonance located 23–28 MeV above $B^{*}\bar{D}$, with the scalar state's nature depending on the precise parameter values within the model.

## Method

The author employs the dynamical diquark model within the Born–Oppenheimer approximation, describing the $T_{bc}$ system as a heavy antidiquark $[\bar{b}\bar{c}]$ and a light diquark $[ud]$ interacting through the lattice-QCD $\Sigma_g^+(1S)$ static potential. The radial Schrödinger equation is solved numerically for $L=0$ states, and the effective Hamiltonian includes a spin-independent term $H_0$ (kinetic energy plus the BO potential) plus a chromomagnetic spin-spin term $H_\kappa$ acting separately within the light and heavy clusters. The isospin-spin lock for the light diquark restricts the physical isoscalar states to those with $s_\delta=0$, yielding exactly two $I=0$ states: $T_{bc}^{(0)}$ ($J^P=0^+$) and $T_{bc}^{(1)}$ ($J^P=1^+$).

## Key numbers

- $M(T_{bc}^{(0)}) = 7.143$–$7.158$ GeV across three parameter sets (varying $\kappa_{\bar{b}\bar{c}}$ from 10 to 20 MeV)
- $M(T_{bc}^{(1)}) = 7.217$–$7.222$ GeV across the same three parameter sets
- Hyperfine splitting $\Delta_{HF} \simeq 59$–$79$ MeV between the scalar and axial-vector states
- Mean separation $\langle r\rangle \simeq 0.45$–$0.46$ fm for both states
- Inverse radius $\langle 1/r\rangle^{-1} \simeq 0.33$–$0.34$ fm for both states
- The axial-vector state lies 23–28 MeV above the $B^{*}\bar{D}$ threshold and about 70 MeV below the $B\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold
- The slope $\partial\Delta_{HF}/\partial\kappa_{\bar{b}\bar{c}} = 2$, consistent with heavy-antidiquark spin algebra

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark model previously applied to hidden-charm and charm-strange tetraquarks by Giron, Lebed, and collaborators (2019–2020) and by Mutuk (2025) to the mixed bottom-charm $T_{bc}$ system. It addresses the inconclusive theoretical situation for $T_{bc}$, where lattice QCD studies by Francis et al. (2018), Hudspith et al. (2020), and Meinel et al. (2022) found no clear evidence of binding, while more recent calculations by Padmanath et al. (2023) and Alexandrou et al. (2023) found near-threshold poles with binding energies ranging from a few MeV to about 43 MeV. The present results support a compact diquark–antidiquark interpretation with weak parameter dependence and predict that the scalar state straddles the $B\bar{D}$ threshold, making it a prime candidate for experimental search at LHCb and Belle II.
