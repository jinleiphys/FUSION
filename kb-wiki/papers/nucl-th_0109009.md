# Charge Screening at First Order Phase Transitions

> Possibility of structured mixed phases at first order phase transitions is examined with taking into account of charge screening and surface effects. Hadron-quark phase transition in dense neutron star interior is considered, as concrete example.

## Key claim

The paper argues that structured mixed phases at first order phase transitions in multi-component charged systems, such as the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star interiors, may be prohibited or significantly modified when charge screening and surface effects are consistently included, contrary to earlier treatments that predicted a wide mixed-phase region based on unscreened Coulomb solutions.

## Method

The authors construct a thermodynamic potential for a Wigner-Seitz cell containing a droplet of quark matter (phase I) surrounded by hadronic matter (phase II), with a sharp boundary between phases. They solve the nonlinear Poisson equation for the electric potential in both phases, including Debye screening, and compute the total effective energy per droplet volume as the sum of electric field energy, correlation terms from density-dependent screening, and surface energy. The hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star matter is used as a concrete example, with quark matter described by a bag model and hadronic matter by a nuclear energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- Debye screening lengths: \(\lambda_D^{\rm I} \equiv 1/\kappa^{\rm I} \simeq 3.4/m_\pi\) for quark phase, \(\lambda_D^{\rm II} \equiv 1/\kappa^{\rm II} \simeq 4.2/m_\pi\) for hadron phase (with proton screening), compared to \(\lambda_D^{\rm II} \simeq 8.5/m_\pi\) without proton screening, for \(\rho_B^{\rm II}=1.5\rho_0\), \(\mu_e \simeq 170\) MeV, \(\mu_B \simeq 1020\) MeV, \(\alpha_c \simeq 0.4\)
- Estimated coefficient \(\beta_0 \simeq 1.6 m_\pi^4\) for the same parameters
- For surface tension \(\sigma \simeq 1.3 m_\pi^3\), coefficient \(\beta_1 \simeq 0.7\); for \(\sigma \simeq 10\) MeV/fm\(^2 \simeq 0.14 m_\pi^3\), \(\beta_1 \simeq 0.08\)
- Contribution of electric field effects to surface tension for large drops: \(\sigma_V \simeq -70\) MeV/fm\(^2\) for \(\mu_e=170\) MeV, \(\mu_n=1020\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, \(\alpha_c=0.4\)
- Critical value \(\beta_{1{\rm c}} \simeq 0.5\) above which the structured mixed phase is prohibited (no minimum in droplet size)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg, Pethick, and Staubo (1993) on mixed phases in neutron star interiors, but demonstrates that the pure Coulomb solution assumed in those treatments is never realized within the mixed phase due to screening effects. The paper provides a consistent treatment of charge screening and correlation terms that were disregarded in prior work, showing that the structured mixed phase exists only for sufficiently small surface tension (\(\beta_1 < \beta_{1{\rm c}}\)), and that large-size drops can appear only when the effective surface tension from electric effects nearly cancels the strong-interaction surface tension. The results have implications for the equation of state, neutrino emissivities, glitch phenomena, and r-modes in neutron stars.
