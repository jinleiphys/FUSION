# Estimates of hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 4--40 GeV

> Global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 4-40 GeV in the midrapidity region and total polarization, i.e. averaged over all rapidities, are studied within the scope of the thermodynamical approach. The relevant vorticity is simulated within the model of the three-fluid dynamics (3FD). It is found that the performed rough estimate of the global midrapidity polarization quite satisfactorily reproduces the experimental STAR data on the polarization, especially its collision-energy dependence. The total polarization increases with the collision energy rise, which is in contrast to the decrease of the midrapidity polarization. This suggests that at high collision energies the polarization reaches high values in fragmentation regions.

## Key claim

The authors find that a rough estimate of global midrapidity Λ hyperon polarization, based on the thermodynamical approach and vorticity from the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, "quite satisfactorily reproduces the experimental STAR data on the polarization, especially its collision-energy dependence," but they emphasize this is an estimate with several approximations, not a precise calculation.

## Method

The authors simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 4–40 GeV using the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball fluids with hydrodynamic equations coupled by friction terms. They compute the thermal vorticity from the fluid velocities and temperatures, then apply the thermodynamical approach of Becattini et al. (2013, 2016) to relate this vorticity to the mean spin vector of Λ hyperons emitted from an isochronous freeze-out hypersurface. The global midrapidity polarization is estimated from vorticity averaged over a central slab, while total polarization is estimated from vorticity averaged over the whole participant region with a temperature cut T > 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Global midrapidity Λ polarization reaches values of 5% at √sNN = 3.8 GeV (prediction for NICA/FAIR energies).
- The total angular momentum in semi-central (b = 8 fm) Au+Au collisions rapidly rises with collision energy; 25–30% of the total angular momentum is deposited into participant matter.
- Variation of the freeze-out energy density from εfrz = 0.3 to 0.5 GeV/fm³ changes the central-slab polarization by 30% at 4.3 GeV and by 5% at 39 GeV.
- The difference between results from the first-order-phase-transition and crossover equations of state is negligible.
- The global midrapidity polarization of Λ̄ hyperons quantitatively does not exceed 5% of that for Λ hyperons.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic and kinetic model calculations of hyperon polarization (Karpenko et al., 2016; Xie et al., 2017; Li et al., 2017; Sun et al., 2017; Kolomeitsev et al., 2018; Wei et al., 2018; Shi et al., 2017) by using the 3FD model, which incorporates early-stage nonequilibrium via counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids. The results enable predictions for the FAIR and NICA energy ranges and suggest that at high collision energies, the polarization reaches high values in fragmentation regions, explaining why midrapidity polarization decreases while total polarization increases.
