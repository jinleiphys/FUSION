# 4-momentum conservation as the principal framework for mesonic decay: The case of helium-5-lambda

> Negative-pion and neutral-pion mesonic decays of the hypernucleus $ _Λ^5$He are investigated within two-body and three-body relativistic kinematics in the rest-frame of the hypernucleus. 4-momentum conservation fully constrains the two-body decay, giving rise to a monochromatic pion momentum that is determined through the Newton-Raphson root-finding algorithm. In the case of three-body decay, where 4-momentum conservation is insufficient, the final-state momenta are parametrised by variables whose variations account for all kinematically allowed momenta. A Monte Carlo sampling method is then employed to generate 50 000 decay events per channel that simultaneously satisfy both energy and momentum conservation. The resulting pion momentum distributions exhibit clear peaks at 103.0 MeV/c for neutral pion decay and 97.3 MeV/c for negative pion decay. In addition, only 0.27\% of neutral pion decay events and 0.03\% of negative pion decay events produce nucleons with momenta exceeding the Fermi momentum of $ ^4$He, and are therefore allowed by the Pauli exclusion principle.

## Key claim

Relativistic kinematics based solely on 4-momentum conservation, without dynamical assumptions beyond conservation laws, is sufficient to determine the final-state momentum distributions in mesonic decays of the hypernucleus \(^{5}_{\Lambda}\text{He}\), with the resulting pion momentum peaks closely matching experimental and other theoretical values.

## Method

The authors computed effective Q-values for negative-pion and neutral-pion decays of \(^{5}_{\Lambda}\text{He}\) using the lambda separation energy \(B_\Lambda = 3.12\) MeV and the separation energies \(S_p(^{5}\text{Li}) = -1.960\) MeV and \(S_n(^{5}\text{He}) = -0.735\) MeV. For two-body decays, the Newton-Raphson root-finding algorithm solved the relativistic energy-momentum conservation equations to obtain monochromatic pion momenta. For three-body decays (where the daughter nuclei \(^{5}\text{Li}\) and \(^{5}\text{He}\) break up into \(^{4}\text{He} + p\) or \(^{4}\text{He} + n\)), the authors parametrized final-state momenta as fractions of a total momentum \(p\) with random directional unit vectors, then generated 50,000 decay events per channel via Monte Carlo sampling that simultaneously satisfied both energy and momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- Effective Q-value for negative-pion decay: \(Q_{\pi^-}^{\text{eff}} = 32.76\) MeV
- Effective Q-value for neutral-pion decay: \(Q_{\pi^0}^{\text{eff}} = 37.29\) MeV
- Two-body decay monochromatic pion momentum: \(p_{\pi^-} = 99.27\) MeV/c for negative-pion decay; \(p_{\pi^0} = 105.12\) MeV/c for neutral-pion decay
- Three-body decay pion momentum distribution peaks: 97.3 MeV/c for negative-pion decay; 103.0 MeV/c for neutral-pion decay
- Three-body decay nucleon momentum distribution peaks: 58.6 MeV/c for protons (negative-pion decay); 61.9 MeV/c for neutrons (neutral-pion decay)
- Three-body decay \(^{4}\text{He}\) momentum distribution peaks: 58.0 MeV/c (negative-pion decay); 61.4 MeV/c (neutral-pion decay)
- Pauli-allowed fraction: 0.27% of neutral-pion decay events and 0.03% of negative-pion decay events produce nucleons with momenta exceeding the Fermi momentum of \(^{4}\text{He}\) (220 MeV/c)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of mesonic weak decay in light hypernuclei, particularly the experimental and theoretical investigations of \(^{5}_{\Lambda}\text{He}\) decay by Stra1993, Mot1991, Mot1992, and Chr2013. The authors demonstrate that 4-momentum conservation alone reproduces pion momentum peaks close to those reported in those prior works, without requiring detailed final-state interaction models. The results provide a quantitative basis for identifying which final-state nuclear reactions (such as pion absorption, elastic scattering, and charge exchange) are energetically possible in the decay, and can guide detector acceptance and spectrometer settings for planned experiments.
