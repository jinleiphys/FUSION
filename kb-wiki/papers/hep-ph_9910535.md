# Quantum Transport Equations for a Scalar Field

> We derive quantum Boltzmann equations from Schwinger-Dyson equations in gradient expansion for a weakly coupled scalar field theory with a spatially varying mass. We find that at higher order in gradients a full description of the system requires specifying not only an on shell distribution function but also a finite number of its derivatives, or equivalently its higher moments. These derivatives describe quantum coherence arising as a consequence of localization in position space. We then show that in the limit of frequent scatterings coherent quantum effects are suppressed, and the transport equations reduce to the single Boltzmann equation for particle density, in which particles flow along modified semiclassical trajectories in phase space.

## Key claim

For a weakly coupled scalar field theory with a spatially varying mass, a full description of transport beyond leading order in gradients requires specifying not only an on-shell distribution function but also a finite number of its derivatives (higher moments), which encode quantum coherence from localization in position space; however, in the limit of frequent scatterings where the product of the damping rate and the background variation length scale satisfies \(\Gamma_{\! qc} L \gg 1\), these coherent quantum effects are suppressed and the system reduces to a single semiclassical Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors start from the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the two-point Green function in the closed time contour (CTC) formalism, truncating the hierarchy at order \(\lambda^2\) in the quartic coupling. They transform to the Wigner representation and perform a gradient expansion, deriving the Kadanoff-Baym equations for the propagator and the quantum Boltzmann equation (QBE). The on-shell projection is performed by taking moments of the QBE weighted by powers of momentum, which yields a closed set of equations for the first four moments \(f_0, f_1, f_2, f_3\) at second order in gradients; these moments are then re-expressed in terms of a semiclassical distribution function \(f\) and three coherent quantum densities \(f_{\rm qc1}, f_{\rm qc2}, f_{\rm qc3}\).

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical momentum to second order in gradients is \(k_{\rm sc} = k_0 + \frac{1}{8}\frac{m^{2 \prime \prime}}{k_0^3} + \frac{5}{32}\frac{(m^{2 \prime})^2}{k_0^5}\), where \(k_0^2 = \omega^2 - \vec k_\parallel^2 - m^2(x)\).
* The coherent quantum densities are damped at the rate \(\Gamma_{\! qc} = (\Gamma^> - \Gamma^<)/\omega\), which is the out-of-equilibrium generalization of the on-shell damping rate.
* In the frequent scattering limit, the coherent quantum densities are suppressed by a factor \(1/(\Gamma_{\! qc} L)\) relative to third-order gradient terms, where \(L\) is the scale of variation of the background mass.
* The validity of the gradient approximation requires \(\partial_t f \ll \omega f\) and \(\partial_x f \ll k_0 f\).
* The electroweak bubble wall thickness is estimated as \(L \sim (10-20) T^{-1}\), and the phase boundary speed is smaller than about 0.3 of the speed of light.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the scalar field propagator at non-trivial order in gradients (Joyce, Kainulainen, Prokopec, 1999) and provides a systematic formalism for transport in a slowly varying background, motivated by electroweak baryogenesis at a first-order phase transition. The derived quantum transport equations generalize the standard Kadanoff-Baym approach (Kadanoff and Baym, 1962) and the semiclassical Boltzmann equation to include coherent quantum effects from localization. The results enable a controlled computation of CP-violating sources for baryogenesis, which appear at higher order in gradients; the semiclassical force obtained in the frequent scattering limit is analogous to that introduced in studies of two Higgs doublet models (Joyce, Prokopec, Turok, 1996) and later applied to the MSSM (Cline, Joyce, Kainulainen, 1998).
