# Extension of random-phase approximation preserving energy weighted sum rules: an application to a 3-level Lipkin model

> A limitation common to all extensions of random-phase approximation including only particle-hole configurations is that they violate to some extent the energy weighted sum rules. Considering one such extension, the improved RPA (IRPA), already used to study the electronic properties of metallic clusters, we show how it can be generalized in order to eliminate this drawback. This is achieved by enlarging the configuration space, including also elementary excitations corresponding to the annihilation of a particle (hole) and the creation of another particle (hole) on the correlated ground state. The approach is tested within a solvable 3-level model.

# Extension of Random-Phase Approximation Preserving Energy Weighted Sum Rules: an application to a 3-level Lipkin model

## Key claim
The improved RPA (IRPA) can be generalized to exactly satisfy the Energy Weighted Sum Rules (EWSR) by enlarging the configuration space to include particle-particle and hole-hole excitations, but this introduces spurious states that are not easily recognized because they do not have zero or very small energies.

## Method
The authors start from the IRPA formalism, which linearizes the equations of motion by contracting two-body terms with respect to the correlated ground state rather than the Hartree-Fock state. They then generalize the excitation operator to include not only particle-hole (ph) but also particle-particle (pp) and hole-hole (hh) configurations, deriving new A and B matrices for the RPA-like eigenvalue equations. The approach is tested on a solvable 3-level Lipkin model with SU(3) symmetry, where the nonlinear equations are solved iteratively and compared with exact diagonalization results.

## Key numbers
- The collapse point of standard RPA (where the first excitation energy becomes imaginary) occurs at χ = 0.024 for the chosen parameters.
- The violation of the EWSR in IRPA is about 30% for χ = 0.03.
- The model uses N = 10 particles (2Ω = 10), with level energies ε₀ = 0, ε₁ = ε, ε₂ = 2.5ε, and interaction strengths V₀ = -χ, V₁ = χ, V₂ = -χ/2, V₃ = χ/10.
- The exact model has 66 eigenstates; standard RPA and IRPA produce 2 excited states, while the enlarged IRPA produces 3 states.

## Context
This work extends the IRPA approach previously applied to metallic clusters by Catara et al. (2000) and addresses a known limitation of all RPA extensions that include only particle-hole configurations: they violate EWSR. The authors explicitly show that including pp and hh configurations (similar in spirit to the fully renormalized RPA of Toivanen and Suhonen, but with different implementation) restores the sum rule. The approach is tested on a 3-level Lipkin model previously studied by several authors. The main open question is how to identify and eliminate the spurious states that appear in the enlarged space, since they do not have zero energy and their transition probabilities become appreciable near the collapse point.
