# Hadronic and hybrid stars subject to density dependent magnetic fields

> In this work we present a study of magnetars composed of hadronic and quark matter, under the influence of strong magnetic fields. We present our results for hadronic matter taking into account the effects that the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moment corrections may cause on the equations of state (EOS). Particle abundances are also presented and discussed. Additionally, the case of hybrid stars under the influence of strong magnetic fields is considered. We study the structure of hybrid stars based on the Maxwell condition (without a mixed phase), where the hadron phase is described by the non-linear Walecka model (NLW) and the quark phase by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL). The EOS and the mass-radius relation for each case are calculated and discussed. We show that the recently observed high mass neutron stars can be described by both hadronic and hybrid equations of state subject to strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both purely hadronic and hybrid (hadron-quark) equations of state subject to strong, density-dependent magnetic fields can produce neutron stars with masses high enough to match the recently observed ~2 solar mass pulsars, provided the magnetic field is treated with a density-dependent profile and the maximum field is limited to about 3.1 × 10^18 G to avoid significant anisotropic effects.

## Method

The authors model hadronic matter using the non-linear Walecka model (NLW) with the GM1 parametrization, including the full baryonic octet and anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) corrections for all baryons. Quark matter is described by the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two parameter sets (HK and RKH). Hybrid stars are constructed using the Maxwell condition (no mixed phase), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved with a density-dependent magnetic field profile that increases from 10^15 G at the surface to either 10^17 G or 3.1 × 10^18 G in the interior, using two decay parameter sets (fast: γ=3.00, β=0.02; slow: γ=2.00, β=0.05).

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field considered: 3.1 × 10^18 G (≈ 10^18.5 G), chosen as the upper limit where anisotropic effects can be neglected.
- Surface magnetic field: 10^15 G, taken from observational values for magnetars.
- Saturation density ρ0 = 0.153 fm^-3, with binding energy B/A = -16.3 MeV, symmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV, and compressibility K = 300 MeV (GM1 parametrization).
- Meson masses used: m_σ = 512 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV; nucleon-meson couplings: g_σN = 8.910, g_ωN = 10.610, g_ρN = 8.196.
- Hyperon coupling fractions: X_σH = 0.700, X_ωH = X_ρH = 0.783.
- NJL parameter sets: Λ = 631.4 MeV (HK) and Λ = 602.3 MeV (RKH), with current quark masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 135.7 MeV (HK) or 140.7 MeV (RKH).
- Error in stellar mass from using TOV equations: ~10^-4 to 10^-3 M_⊙ for B = 10^18 G, and ~10^-2 to 10^-1 M_⊙ for B = 10^19 G.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of magnetars by Casali et al. (2013) and others (Bandyopadhyay et al., 1997; Broderick et al., 2000; Lopes et al., 2012) by incorporating a density-dependent magnetic field profile and anomalous magnetic moments for all baryons in the octet, rather than only nucleons. It contrasts with earlier results by Bandyopadhyay et al. (1997) who found that strong magnetic fields reduce maximum masses; here, the authors find that strong fields can stiffen the equation of state and support higher masses. The work enables future studies of hybrid star structure with more realistic magnetic field profiles and comparisons between different quark models (NJL vs. MIT bag model).
