# Angular momentum projection in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The angular momentum projection (AMP) method is implemented in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the point-coupling density functional. The wave functions of angular momentum projected states are expanded in terms of the Dirac Woods-Saxon (WS) basis, providing a proper description of the asymptotic behavior of the wave functions for weakly bound nuclei. The contribution of continuum induced by the pairing is considered by treating the pairing correlation with the Bogoliubov transformation. We present the formulae and numerical checks for the DRHBc+AMP approach and use it to study low-lying excited states of weakly bound deformed nuclei. Our calculations show that neutron-rich magnesium isotopes $^{36,38,40}$Mg are all well deformed nuclei. The low-lying excited states of these three nuclei are obtained by performing the AMP on the mean-field ground-states. The ground-state rotational bands of $^{36,38,40}$Mg are reproduced reasonably well by using this new DRHBc+AMP approach with the density functional PC-F1.

## Key claim

The angular momentum projection (AMP) method is implemented in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with point-coupling density functionals, enabling the study of low-lying excited states of weakly bound deformed nuclei; the ground-state rotational bands of neutron-rich magnesium isotopes \(^{36,38,40}\)Mg are reproduced reasonably well using this new DRHBc+AMP approach with the density functional PC-F1, but the work focuses on projection after variation (PAV) rather than the more technically complicated projection before variation (PBV).

## Method

The deformed RHB equation is solved in the Dirac Woods-Saxon (WS) basis, which provides proper asymptotic behavior for weakly bound nuclei. After obtaining the mean-field wave function for a given quadrupole deformation, angular momentum projection is performed using the projection operator \(\hat{P}^J_{MK}\) and the Hill-Wheeler equation is solved to obtain energies and wave functions of states with good angular momentum. The mixed densities and currents for each Euler angle \(\theta\) are expanded in spherical harmonics, and the Hamiltonian overlap kernel is computed using the generalized Wick's theorem.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state rotational bands of \(^{36}\)Mg, \(^{38}\)Mg, and \(^{40}\)Mg are reproduced reasonably well using the DRHBc+AMP approach with the PC-F1 density functional.
- The charge radius is calculated as \(R_{\mathrm{ch}} = \sqrt{R_p^2 + 0.64\ \mathrm{fm}^2}\).
- The quadrupole deformation parameters are defined as \(\beta_{\tau}= \frac{\sqrt{5 \pi} Q_{\tau}}{3 N_{\tau}\left\langle R_{\tau}^{2}\right\rangle}\).
- A truncation \(\xi\) on the occupation probability \(v^2\) is introduced to reduce numerical computational efforts in the AMP calculations.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc theory, previously developed for deformed halo nuclei (Zhou 2010, Li 2012, Sun 2018), by adding angular momentum projection to describe low-lying excited states. It builds on prior AMP implementations in relativistic mean field models with harmonic oscillator bases (Niksic 2006, Yao 2009) and in multidimensionally-constrained CDFTs (Wang 2021), but uses the Dirac WS basis to properly describe the asymptotic behavior of wave functions for weakly bound nuclei. This enables future microscopic studies of rotational bands in deformed halo nuclei, where shape decoupling effects and halo structures can appear in excited states (Sun 2021).
