# Negative-Parity States and beta-decays in odd Ho and Dy Nuclei with A=151,153

> We have investigated the negative-parity states and electromagnetic transitions in $^{151,153}$Ho and $^{151,153}$Dy within the framework of the interacting boson fermion model 2 (IBFM-2). Spin assignments for some states with uncertain spin are made based on this calculation. Calculated excitation energies, electromagnetic transitions and branching ratios are compared with available experimental data and a good agreement is obtained. The model wave functions have been used to study $β$-decays from Ho to Dy isotones, and the calculated $\log ft$ values are close to the experimental data.

# Negative-Parity States and β-decays in odd Ho and Dy Nuclei with A=151,153

## Key claim

The interacting boson fermion model 2 (IBFM-2) can reproduce the negative-parity states, electromagnetic transitions, and β-decay log ft values for ¹⁵¹,¹⁵³Ho and ¹⁵¹,¹⁵³Dy, but the agreement is qualified by the need to adjust the exchange force strength in the boson-fermion interaction to lower the first 9/2⁻ state in ¹⁵³Ho, and by the restriction of the limited single-particle space in the h₁₁/₂ orbital for Ho isotopes.

## Method

The authors coupled an odd fermion (proton for Ho, neutron for Dy) to an even-even boson core (¹⁵⁰,¹⁵²Dy for Dy isotopes; ¹⁵²,¹⁵⁴Er for Ho isotopes) within the IBFM-2 framework. For Dy isotopes, the odd neutron was placed in the f₇/₂, h₉/₂, p₃/₂, and f₅/₂ orbitals; for Ho isotopes, the odd proton was placed in the h₁₁/₂ orbital only. The boson-boson interaction parameters were fixed by fitting the core nuclei, and the boson-fermion quadrupole and exchange interaction strengths were varied to reproduce experimental energy levels and electromagnetic transitions. β-decay rates from Ho to Dy were then calculated using the resulting wave functions without further adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵¹Ho, the calculated (13/2⁻)₁ state at 0.826 MeV and (11/2⁻)₂ state at 0.878 MeV are close to experimental levels at 0.869 and 0.910 MeV, respectively.
- For ¹⁵³Ho, the calculated (15/2⁻)₁ state at 0.534 MeV is close to the experimental level at 0.576 MeV; the calculated (25/2⁻) and (27/2⁻) states at 2.490 and 2.201 MeV are close to experimental levels at 2.358 and 2.297 MeV, respectively.
- For ¹⁵¹Dy, the calculated (9/2⁻)₂ state at 0.886 MeV is close to an unassigned experimental level at 0.984 MeV; the calculated (9/2⁻)₃ state at 1.523 MeV reproduces an experimental level at 1.549 MeV with uncertain spin.
- For ¹⁵³Dy, the calculated (3/2⁻)₁ state at 0.104 MeV reproduces the experimental level at 0.108 MeV.
- The log₁₀ft values for β-decay from the ¹⁵¹Ho ground state (11/2⁻) to the ¹⁵¹Dy (9/2⁻)₁ state are 4.6 (experiment) and 3.511 (model); for ¹⁵³Ho to ¹⁵³Dy, the corresponding values are 4.7 (experiment) and 3.612 (model).
- For ¹⁵³Dy, calculated log₁₀ft values for branches to predicted (11/2⁻) states at 1.092 and 1.189 MeV are 6.707 and 7.358, respectively, compared to an experimental value of 6.3 for both branches.

## Context

This work extends the application of IBFM-2 to odd-mass rare-earth nuclei in the A ≈ 150 region, following prior IBFM calculations for β-decay by Yoshida (2002), Zuffi (2003), and Barea (2004). The study provides spin assignments for states with uncertain experimental assignments in ¹⁵¹,¹⁵³Dy and ¹⁵¹,¹⁵³Ho, and demonstrates that IBFM-2 can describe β-decay rates without adjustable parameters once the structure is fixed. The predictions enable future experimental tests of the assigned spins and the calculated higher-lying states.
