# Phase structure of mass- and spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases

> We study the phase diagram of mass- and spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases, in search for the emergence of spatially inhomogeneous phases. To account for fluctuation effects beyond the mean-field approximation, we employ renormalization group techniques. We thus obtain estimates for critical values of the temperature, mass and spin imbalance, above which the system is in the normal phase. In the unpolarized, equal-mass limit, our result for the critical temperature is in accordance with state-of-the-art Monte Carlo calculations. In addition, we estimate the location of regions in the phase diagram where inhomogeneous phases are likely to exist. We show that an intriguing relation exists between the general structure of the many-body phase diagram and the binding energies of the underlying two-body bound-state problem, which further supports our findings. Our results suggest that inhomogeneous condensates form for mass ratios of the spin-down and spin-up fermions greater than three. The extent of the inhomogeneous phase in parameter space increases with increasing mass imbalance.

## Key claim

The paper uses functional renormalization group (fRG) techniques to include fluctuation effects beyond mean-field approximation, finding that inhomogeneous condensates of the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) type are likely to form for mass ratios \(m_\downarrow/m_\uparrow \gtrsim 3\), with the extent of the inhomogeneous phase increasing with mass imbalance.

## Method

The authors study a two-component unitary Fermi gas with contact interaction using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a bosonic order-parameter field. They employ functional renormalization group methods to account for order-parameter fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation, and complement this with a two-body bound-state analysis in the presence of Fermi surfaces to identify parameter regions where finite center-of-mass momentum pairing is favored. The mean-field analysis uses a Fulde-Ferrell ansatz \(\bar{\varphi}(\vec{x}) = \bar{\varphi} e^{i2\vec{Q}\cdot\vec{x}}\) for the order parameter.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the unpolarized, equal-mass limit is in accordance with state-of-the-art Monte Carlo calculations (no specific numerical value given in the truncated text)
- Inhomogeneous condensates form for mass imbalances \(m_\downarrow/m_\uparrow \gtrsim 3\) (corresponding to \(\bar{m} \gtrsim 0.5\))
- In the mean-field analysis, the extent of the homogeneous phase superseded by an inhomogeneous phase is as narrow as \(\delta\bar{h} \approx 0.006\) for the largest considered \(\bar{m}=0.95\)
- The zero-temperature critical endpoint CP\(^0\) of the inhomogeneous phase is approximately at \((\bar{h}, \bar{m}) \approx (0.09, 0.65)\)
- Two-body bound states with finite center-of-mass momentum are favored for \(\bar{m} \gtrsim 0.46\) at sufficiently small spin imbalances

## Context

This work extends previous functional renormalization group studies of balanced and imbalanced Fermi gases (Diehl et al., 2007-2012; Braun et al., 2014) to simultaneously include mass and spin imbalance. It addresses the open question of whether FFLO-type inhomogeneous phases survive beyond mean-field approximation in three dimensions, where earlier mean-field studies (Sheehy and Radzihovsky, 2006; Hu and Liu, 2006) disagreed on the existence of such phases. The results suggest that mass-imbalanced mixtures provide a larger parameter space for inhomogeneous pairing, as previously indicated by mean-field studies (Wang et al., 2014), and enable future experimental searches using mixtures of different fermion species such as \(^6\)Li, \(^{40}\)K, \(^{161}\)Dy, \(^{163}\)Dy, and \(^{167}\)Er.
