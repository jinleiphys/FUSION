# Bayesian inference of neutron-skin thickness and neutron-star observables based on effective nuclear interactions

> We have obtained the constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from neutron-skin thickness data by parity-violating electron scatterings and neutron-star observables using a Bayesian approach, based on the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model and its extension as well as the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. While the neutron-skin thickness data (neutron-star observables) mostly constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation (suprasaturation) densities, they may more or less constrain the behavior of the symmetry energy at suprasaturation (subsaturation) densities, depending on the energy-density functional form. Besides showing the final posterior density dependence of the symmetry energy, we also compare the slope parameters of the symmetry energy at 0.10 fm$^{-3}$ as well as the values of the symmetry energy at twice saturation density from three effective nuclear interactions. The present work serves as a comparison study based on relativistic and non-relativistic energy-density functionals, for constraining the nuclear symmetry energy from low to high densities using a Bayesian approach.

# Bayesian inference of neutron-skin thickness and neutron-star observables based on effective nuclear interactions

## Key claim
While neutron-skin thickness data (neutron-star observables) mostly constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation (suprasaturation) densities, they may also constrain the behavior at suprasaturation (subsaturation) densities depending on the energy-density functional form; the constraints on the symmetry energy from both data sets are similar across the three adopted models, but model dependencies exist due to higher-order EOS parameters and the difference between relativistic and non-relativistic models.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian inference using Markov-Chain Monte Carlo with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, employing three energy-density functionals: the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model, its extension the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) model, and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. Model parameters were expressed as macroscopic physics quantities (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy parameters, effective masses) to enable efficient sampling. The likelihood function combined constraints from neutron-skin thickness data of 208Pb (PREX) and 48Ca (CREX) with neutron-star observables from GW170817, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0740+6620, using two-dimensional probability density functions in the mass-radius plane.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-skin thickness data used: 208Pb Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm from PREX; 48Ca Δr_np = 0.121 ± 0.035 fm from CREX
- Prior ranges: symmetry energy at saturation E_sym^0 = 28.5–34.9 MeV; slope parameter L = 30–90 MeV; incompressibility K_0 = 220–260 MeV
- From both data sets at 68% confidence: slope parameter at ρ=0.10 fm^-3 constrained to 47^{+4}_{-5} MeV (SHF), 41^{+9}_{-8} MeV (KIDS), 43^{+13}_{-12} MeV (RMF)
- Symmetry energy at twice saturation density E_sym(2ρ_0) constrained to 49^{+5}_{-7} MeV (SHF), 46^{+13}_{-9} MeV (KIDS), 51^{+8}_{-7} MeV (RMF) at 68% confidence
- Neutron-star data: GW170817 tidal deformability Λ_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120} at 90% confidence; PSR J0030+0451 mass 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M_⊙ and radius 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km; PSR J0740+6620 mass 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_⊙ and radius 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km (68% confidence)

## Context
This work extends Bayesian analyses of the nuclear symmetry energy by comparing relativistic and non-relativistic energy-density functionals simultaneously, building on prior studies using the SHF model (Chen 2010), KIDS model (PhysRevC.105.044305), and RMF model (PhysRevC.90.044305). It combines constraints from parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX 2021, CREX 2022) with neutron-star observations from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817, 2018) and NICER (Riley 2019, Riley 2021). The results enable more robust constraints on the symmetry energy from subsaturation to suprasaturation densities, while highlighting model-dependent uncertainties that future nuclear structure data (isotope binding energy differences, nucleus resonances) may help resolve.
