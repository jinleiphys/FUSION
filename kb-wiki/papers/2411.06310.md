# Mass measurements of neutron-rich nuclides using the Canadian Penning Trap to inform predictions in the $r$-process rare-earth peak region

> Studies aiming to determine the astrophysical origins of nuclei produced by the rapid neutron capture process ($r$ process) rely on nuclear properties as inputs for simulations. The solar abundances can be used as a benchmark for such calculations, with the $r$-process rare-earth peak (REP) around mass number ($A$) 164 being of special interest due to its presently unknown origin. With the advancement of rare isotope beam production over the last decade and improvement in experimental sensitivities, many of these REP nuclides have become accessible for measurement. Masses are one of the most critical inputs as they impact multiple nuclear properties, namely the neutron-separation energies, neutron capture rates, $β$-decay rates, and $β$-delayed neutron emission probabilities. In this work, we report masses of 20 neutron-rich nuclides (along the Ba, La, Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm, Gd, Dy and Ho isotopic chains) produced at the CAlifornium Rare Isotope Breeder Upgrade (CARIBU) facility at Argonne National Laboratory. The masses were measured with the Canadian Penning trap (CPT) mass spectrometer using the Phase-Imaging Ion-Cyclotron-Resonance (PI-ICR) technique. We then use these new masses along with previously published CPT masses to inform predictions for a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) procedure aiming to identify the astrophysical conditions consistent with both solar data and mass measurements. We show that the MCMC responds to this updated mass information, producing refined results for both mass predictions and REP abundances.

## Key claim

The paper shows that incorporating the full suite of Canadian Penning Trap mass measurements for neutron-rich rare-earth nuclides into a Markov Chain Monte Carlo procedure refines both mass predictions in regions beyond current measurements and the resulting r-process rare-earth peak abundances, but the authors note that the calculations still depend on the Duflo-Zuker mass model for unmeasured nuclei and therefore do not yet produce a rare-earth peak fully consistent with solar data.

## Method

Masses of 20 neutron-rich nuclides (isotopes of Ba, La, Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm, Gd, Dy, and Ho) were measured using the Phase-Imaging Ion-Cyclotron-Resonance technique with the Canadian Penning Trap mass spectrometer at the CARIBU facility, which uses a 0.5 Ci Cf-252 source. The measured masses were then incorporated into a Markov Chain Monte Carlo procedure, previously outlined by Vassh et al., to update neutron capture rates, beta-decay rates, beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities, and separation energies used in r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.

## Key numbers

* First reported mass of La-152: mass excess of -49194.7(58) keV, measured with a precision of about 4e-8, limited by its 287(16) ms half-life and production from a 1e-4% branch of Cf-252 fission.
* First reported mass of Gd-168: mass excess of -48171.2(56) keV, produced from a 1e-5% branch of Cf-252 fission.
* First direct mass measurement of an isomer of Ho-168: mass excess of -59954.2(33) keV, with an excitation energy of 58.6(36) keV, agreeing with the NUBASE2020 value.
* For La-151, the CPT measurement achieved a mass excess of -53529.1(17) keV, which is 250 times more precise than the AME2020 value of -53310(440) keV.
* For Ce-155, the CPT measured a mass excess of -47616(6) keV, compared to the AME2020 extrapolated value of -47780(300) keV.
* The systematic uncertainty from magnetic field drift, ion-ion interactions, and other effects was determined to be less than 3.2 ppb, added in quadrature to measurement uncertainties.
* The WS4 mass model showed the best agreement with the 20 measured masses, with an average absolute deviation of 89 keV and a root mean square deviation of 109 keV.
* The KTUY mass model showed the worst agreement, with an average absolute deviation of 580 keV and a root mean square deviation of 712 keV.

## Context

This work extends the decade-long campaign of Canadian Penning Trap mass measurements of neutron-rich rare-earth isotopes (Orford et al., 2018-2022) and incorporates them into the Markov Chain Monte Carlo framework developed by Vassh et al. The new measurements, including first direct masses of La-152 and Gd-168, begin to probe the region of the nuclear chart that most influences rare-earth peak formation, though the authors note that the calculations still rely on the Duflo-Zuker mass model for unmeasured nuclei. The results enable future measurements at next-generation rare isotope beam facilities to further constrain mass model predictions and refine r-process abundance calculations.
