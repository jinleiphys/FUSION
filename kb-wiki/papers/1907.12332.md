# Chiral susceptibility in Nambu Jona Lasinio model: a Wigner function approach

> We estimate here chiral susceptibility at finite temperature within the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL) using the Wigner function approach. We also estimate it in the presence of chiral chemical potential ($μ_5$) as well as a non vanishing magnetic field ($B$). We use medium separation regularization scheme (MSS) to calculate the chiral condensate and corresponding susceptibility. It is observed that for a fixed value of chiral chemical potential ($μ_5$), transition temperature increases with the magnetic field. While for the fixed value of the magnetic field, transition temperature decreases with chiral chemical potential. For a strong magnetic field, we observe non degeneracy in susceptibility for up and down type quarks.

## Key claim

The paper estimates chiral susceptibility at finite temperature within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model using a Wigner function approach, finding that for a fixed chiral chemical potential the transition temperature increases with magnetic field, while for a fixed magnetic field the transition temperature decreases with chiral chemical potential; these results are obtained within the mean-field approximation and using the medium separation regularization scheme.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility for a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by first constructing the gauge-invariant Wigner function for fermions in a background magnetic field with finite chiral chemical potential, using explicit solutions of the Dirac equation in a magnetic field. The scalar Dirac-Heisenberg-Wigner function is then integrated over momentum to obtain the chiral condensate, and the chiral susceptibility is derived as the derivative of the condensate with respect to the current quark mass. The medium separation regularization scheme is employed to handle ultraviolet divergences, and the calculation is performed at the mean-field level for a system in global thermal equilibrium at constant temperature and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- For a fixed chiral chemical potential of mu_5 = 0.1 GeV, the transition temperature increases from approximately 0.19 GeV at B = 0 to approximately 0.21 GeV at B = 0.2 GeV^2.
- For a fixed magnetic field of B = 0.2 GeV^2, the transition temperature decreases from approximately 0.21 GeV at mu_5 = 0 to approximately 0.19 GeV at mu_5 = 0.2 GeV.
- The constituent quark mass at zero temperature and zero magnetic field is approximately 0.335 GeV, obtained using the model parameters: current quark mass m = 0.005 GeV, coupling G_1 = 5.01 GeV^{-2}, G_2 = -2.0 GeV^{-2}, and a three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.65 GeV.
- For a strong magnetic field of B = 0.6 GeV^2, the chiral susceptibility for up and down quarks shows non-degeneracy, with the peak of the up-quark susceptibility occurring at a higher temperature than that of the down-quark susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral susceptibility in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Buballa 2005) and complements lattice QCD calculations (Lattice 1-6) by using a Wigner function formalism that can be generalized to non-equilibrium systems. The results are consistent with earlier Nambu-Jona-Lasinio studies (ChiralNJL1-6) showing that chiral transition temperature decreases with chiral chemical potential, while also demonstrating magnetic catalysis effects seen in prior work (Fourfermi1-17). The approach enables future studies of chiral susceptibility in out-of-equilibrium conditions relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
