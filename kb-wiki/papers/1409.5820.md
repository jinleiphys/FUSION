# Nearly Perfect Fluidity in a High Temperature Superconductor

> Perfect fluids are characterized as having the smallest ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, η/s, consistent with quantum uncertainty and causality. So far, nearly perfect fluids have only been observed in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and in unitary atomic Fermi gases (UFG), exotic systems that are amongst the hottest and coldest objects in the known universe, respectively. We use Angle Resolve Photoemission Spectroscopy (ARPES) to measure the temperature dependence of an electronic analogue of η/s in an optimally doped cuprate high temperature superconductor, finding it too is a nearly perfect fluid around, and above, its superconducting transition temperature Tc.

## Key claim
Using ARPES to measure an electronic analogue of the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio (η/s) in optimally doped Bi2212, the authors find that the cuprate electron fluid is a nearly perfect fluid around and above its superconducting transition temperature Tc, approaching the holographic KSS bound η/s ≥ ħ/(4πkB). The claim is hedged by noting that the measurement is a "rudimentary estimate" of η/s from a kinematic perspective and "not a true measure of the viscosity."

## Method
The authors used Angle Resolved Photoemission Spectroscopy (ARPES) to measure the temperature-dependent density of states (DOS) and single-particle scattering rates in optimally doped Bi2212 single crystals. They extracted an effective η/s by computing the ratio of kinetic energy density to entropy density (Tη = ε/s) from the ARPES-derived DOS and multiplying by the momentum transport scattering time τp, obtained from ARPES lineshape analysis after removing the impurity scattering contribution. The thermodynamic quantities were evaluated using Fermi-Dirac statistics applied to the experimental occupied DOS, with the ground state energy referenced via a symmetrization procedure.

## Key numbers
- Tc = 91 K for the optimally doped Bi2212 samples studied.
- Total instrumental resolution: 12.5 meV (Gaussian FWHM) with angular resolution of 0.1°.
- The impurity scattering rate ħ/τ0 = 17.7 meV, taken as the minimum of ħ/τk(T→0).
- For an ω-independent DOS (α=0), the analytical approximation gives Tη/T = 1/2; for a Dirac cone-like dispersion (α=1), Tη/T = 2/3.
- The holographic KSS bound is η/s ≥ ħ/(4πkB), which corresponds to ħ/τp ≤ 4πkBTη.

## Context
This work extends the concept of nearly perfect fluidity, previously observed only in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and unitary atomic Fermi gases (UFG), to condensed matter systems, specifically, the cuprate high-temperature superconductor Bi2212. It builds on prior theoretical work by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) on the holographic bound from AdS/CFT correspondence, and on hydrodynamic descriptions of strange metal transport near quantum critical points by Sachdev (2011) and Zaanen (2004). The results enable future studies of viscous hydrodynamics in strongly correlated electron systems and suggest that the cuprate strange metal phase may be understood as a quantum critical fluid rather than through conventional quasiparticle approaches.
