# Revealing Nuclear Pions Using Electron Scattering

> A model for the pionic components of nuclear wave functions is obtained from light front dynamical calculations of binding energies and densities. The pionic effects are small enough to be consistent with measured nuclear di-muon production data and with the nucleon sea. But the pion effects are large enough to predict substantial nuclear enhancement of the cross section for longitudinally polarized virtual photons for the kinematics accessible at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key claim

A light-front dynamical model of nuclear matter that yields a small excess of pions (0.05 per nucleon) can simultaneously be consistent with the null result of Drell-Yan experiments searching for nuclear pionic enhancement, yet still predict a measurable 15-30% enhancement of the longitudinal virtual photon cross section on nuclei at Jefferson Laboratory kinematics, provided the excess pion number is tuned to 0.04 for iron via a specific choice of the short-range correlation parameter g' = 0.795.

## Method

The author uses a light-front one-boson-exchange potential in a Brueckner theory calculation of infinite nuclear matter to obtain binding energy, density, and compressibility, from which the excess pion number per nucleon is extracted. The pion distribution functions f_pi/A(y) for nuclei are then constructed by adjusting the parameter g' (representing short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations) to reproduce the 0.05 excess pion number from the light-front calculation, and the Fermi momentum is reduced from 1.37 fm^-1 (nuclear matter) to 1.3 fm^-1 (iron) to obtain the nuclear distribution. These distribution functions are used to compute the pionic contribution to the longitudinal structure function via a convolution formalism, yielding the enhancement of sigma_L/sigma_L(D) for iron targets.

## Key numbers

- Excess pions per nucleon in nuclear matter: 0.05, from the light-front Brueckner calculation.
- Excess pions per nucleon in iron (Fe): 0.04, obtained by reducing Fermi momentum from 1.37 fm^-1 to 1.3 fm^-1.
- Plus-momentum fraction carried by excess pions: 0.016 for nuclear matter, 0.0135 for iron.
- Parameter g' = 0.795 yields the desired 0.05 excess pion number.
- Predicted enhancement of sigma_L(A)/sigma_L(D) for iron: 15-30% for x ~ 0.2-0.3 at Q^2 values accessible at Jefferson Laboratory.
- Pionic contribution to R = sigma_L/sigma_T for the proton at x=0.2, Q^2=0.7 GeV^2: about 0.04.
- Lowest Q^2 considered: 0.3 GeV^2, corresponding to nu ~ 0.4 GeV.

## Context

This work addresses the "crisis" in nuclear theory identified by Bertsch, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1993), where earlier models by Ericson and Thomas (1983) and Friman, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1983) predicted large nuclear pion excesses (about 0.18 per nucleon) that were ruled out by Drell-Yan experiments (Alde et al., 1990). The author extends his own light-front Brueckner calculation (Miller and Machleidt, 1999) and the earlier work of Jung and Miller (1990) to show that a smaller pion excess can coexist with the Drell-Yan data while still producing a detectable signal in longitudinal cross section measurements. The predicted enhancement offers a way to resolve the puzzle of "missing" nuclear pions through experiments at Jefferson Laboratory.
