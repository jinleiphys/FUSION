# Systematic Properties of the Tsallis Distribution: Energy Dependence of Parameters

> Changes in the transverse momentum distributions with beam energy are studied using the Tsallis distribution as a parameterization. The dependence of the Tsallis parameters q, T and the volume on beam energy is determined. The Tsallis parameter q shows a weak but clear increase with beam energy with the highest value being approximately 1.15. The Tsallis temperature and volume are consistent with being independent of beam energy within experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

The Tsallis parameter \(q\) shows a weak but clear increase with beam energy in \(p-p\) collisions, with the highest value being approximately 1.15, while the Tsallis temperature \(T\) and volume \(V\) are consistent with being independent of beam energy within experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The author performed fits of a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution to charged-particle transverse momentum spectra from \(p-p\) collisions at beam energies of 0.54, 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV, using data from the UA1, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations. The fit function summed three Tsallis distributions for \(\pi^+\), \(K^+\), and protons (with antiparticles included via a factor of 2), at mid-rapidity and zero chemical potential, with degeneracy factors of 1 for pions and kaons and 2 for protons. The parameters \(q\), \(T\), and the normalization (related to volume) were extracted from the fits.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis parameter \(q\) reaches a highest value of approximately 1.15 in the fits to charged-particle spectra.
- Beam energies analyzed include 0.54 TeV (UA1), 0.9 TeV, 2.36 TeV, and 7 TeV (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS).
- The fit function used a sum of three Tsallis distributions with degeneracy factors \(g_{\pi^+}=1\), \(g_{K^+}=1\), and \(g_{p}=2\).

## Context

This work extends the use of the Tsallis distribution to describe transverse momentum spectra across a wide range of beam energies, following fits performed by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC and by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at the LHC. The author proposes a thermodynamically consistent parameterization that differs from the form used by those collaborations (e.g., the ALICE parameterization in terms of \(n\) and \(C\)), notably by including an explicit \(m_T\) factor that preserves \(m_T\) scaling. The results enable systematic comparisons of the energy dependence of \(q\), \(T\), and volume, and show that at lower energies the distributions become more Boltzmann-like (with \(q\) closer to 1), consistent with earlier work by Becattini (2000) on resonance decays.
