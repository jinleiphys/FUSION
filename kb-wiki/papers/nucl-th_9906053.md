# Study of the He3(He4,gamma)Be7 and H3(He4,gamma)Li7 reactions in an extended two-cluster model

> The He3(He4,gamma)Be7 and H3(He4,gamma)Li7 reactions are studied in an extended two-cluster model which contains alpha+h/t and Li6+p/n clusterizations. We show that the inclusion of the Li6+p/n channels can significantly change the zero-energy reaction cross sections, S(0), and other properties of the Be7 and Li7 nuclei, like the quadrupole moments Q. However, the results agree with the known correlation trend between S(0) and Q. Moreover, we demonstrate that the value of the zero-energy derivatives of the astrophysical S-factors are more uncertain than currently believed.

## Key claim

Including the Li6+p/n clusterization in the model space can significantly change the zero-energy astrophysical S-factors S(0) and quadrupole moments Q of Be7 and Li7, but the results still agree with the known correlation trend between S(0) and Q; however, the zero-energy derivatives of the astrophysical S-factors are more uncertain than currently believed.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic cluster-model calculations for the He3(He4,gamma)Be7 and H3(He4,gamma)Li7 reactions using an extended two-cluster model that includes both the dominant alpha+h/t clusterization and the additional Li6+p/n configuration. They solved the seven-nucleon Schrodinger equation for the intercluster relative motion functions, using the variational Kohn-Hulthén method for scattering states and a Siegert variational method for bound states, then calculated E1 transition cross sections. Calculations were performed with both the Minnesota (MN) and MHN effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, and results were compared between the single-channel alpha+h/t model and the extended model space.

## Key numbers

- For the He3(He4,gamma)Be7 reaction with the MN interaction at beta=0.4 fm^-2, S(0) = 0.83 keVb in the extended model versus 0.70 keVb in the single-channel model
- For the H3(He4,gamma)Li7 reaction with the MN interaction at beta=0.4 fm^-2, S(0) = 0.148 keVb in the extended model versus 0.131 keVb in the single-channel model
- The derivative S^{-1}dS/dE(0) for He3+He4 fusion changes from -0.53 MeV^-1 (single-channel MN) to -0.56 MeV^-1 (extended MN), and from -0.56 MeV^-1 to -0.63 MeV^-1 for the MHN interaction
- The Be7 quadrupole moment increases by roughly 10% (from -6.61 to -7.23 e fm^2) when adding the Li6+p channel at beta=0.4 fm^-2 with the MN interaction
- The Li7 quadrupole moment increases by about 1.6% (from -3.77 to -3.83 e fm^2) under the same conditions
- The Li6 binding energy calculated with the MN interaction is 1.43 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.475 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of these reactions by Walliser, Langanke, Kajino, and others (all 1980s-1990s) that used only a single alpha+h/t clusterization, and follows the multichannel RGM approach of Mertelmeier and Hofmann who included a Li6+p configuration. The results show that the common strategy of adopting the energy dependence from a potential model and normalizing to data becomes questionable because the reaction cannot be treated as a purely external capture process. The paper also revisits the correlation between S(0) and the quadrupole moment of Li7 proposed by Kajino, finding that the currently favored larger Li7 quadrupole moment (-4.0 e fm^2) would imply an S(0) value about twice that from precision data by Brune et al. (1994), suggesting the need for further studies in larger model spaces.
