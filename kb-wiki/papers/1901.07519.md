# Lattice QCD determination of neutron-antineutron matrix elements with physical quark masses

> Matrix elements of six-quark operators are needed to extract new physics constraints from experimental searches for neutron-antineutron oscillations. This work presents in detail the first lattice quantum chromodynamics calculations of the necessary neutron-antineutron transition matrix elements including calculation methods and discussions of systematic uncertainties. Implications of isospin and chiral symmetry on the matrix elements, power counting in the isospin limit, and renormalization of a chiral basis of six-quark operators are discussed. Calculations are performed with a chiral-symmetric discretization of the quark action and physical light quark masses in order to avoid the need for chiral extrapolation. Non-perturbative renormalization is performed, including a study of lattice cutoff effects. Excited-state effects are studied using two nucleon operators and multiple values of source-sink separation. Results for the dominant matrix elements are found to be significantly larger compared to previous results from the MIT bag model. Future calculations are needed to fully account for systematic uncertainties associated with discretization and finite-volume effects but are not expected to significantly affect this conclusion.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of neutron-antineutron transition matrix elements using physical light quark masses, finding that the dominant matrix elements are 4-8 times larger than previous MIT bag model predictions, though future calculations are needed to fully account for discretization and finite-volume systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using a chiral-symmetric Mobius domain wall fermion action on gauge configurations generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaborations with physical pion mass m_pi = 139.2(4) MeV. They computed six-quark operator matrix elements for neutron-antineutron transitions using two nucleon operators and multiple source-sink separations to study excited-state effects, performed non-perturbative renormalization in an RI-MOM scheme with one-loop matching to MSbar, and used shrinkage estimation of covariance matrices with correlated two-state fits to extract ground-state matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 139.2(4) MeV, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolation
- Meson decay constants on the ensemble: f_pi = 131.1(4) GeV, f_K = 156.4(4) GeV, within 0.6% of physical PDG values (f_pi = 130.4(2), f_K = 156.2(7) GeV)
- Ground-state energy from two-point fits: E_0 = 0.565(24)(8) in lattice units
- Excited-state energy from two-point fits: E_1 = 1.21(15)(65) in lattice units
- m_pi L approximately 3.9 for the lattice volume used
- Non-perturbatively computed operator mixing matrix diagonal up to O(10^-3) corrections
- Final precision of 15-30% for the electroweak-singlet matrix elements M_1, M_2, and M_3
- Lattice QCD results 4-8 times larger than MIT bag model predictions for dominant electroweak-singlet operators

## Context

This work extends earlier MIT bag model calculations of neutron-antineutron matrix elements by providing the first ab initio lattice QCD determination with physical quark masses, finding significantly larger values that increase predicted event rates by factors of 16-64 for planned oscillation experiments. The results enable more precise constraints on baryon-number violating theories and baryogenesis models, and the chiral symmetry of the domain wall fermion action ensures that the 14 distinct |Delta B| = 2 operators do not mix, unlike in previous lattice studies with broken chiral symmetry. The calculation uses gauge configurations from the RBC/UKQCD collaboration (Blum 2014) and renormalization matching from Buchoff (2015), while future work with finer lattice spacings and multiple volumes is needed to fully control discretization and finite-volume systematic uncertainties.
