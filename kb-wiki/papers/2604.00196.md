# An Asymptotically Causal Metamodel for Neutron Star Equations of State

> Nuclear metamodels - phenomenological parametrizations of the energy of nuclear matter - are convenient tools to explore the space of realistic neutron star configurations constrained by astrophysical and nuclear data. While much recent work has focused on composition-agnostic barotropic models, the metamodel approach is designed to describe the composition dependence of the relevant thermodynamic potential. We revise a previously proposed non-relativistic metamodel by introducing a more controlled high-density behaviour, improving both its causal properties and its accuracy in reproducing the pressure and the beta-equilibrium composition of microscopically motivated equations of state. Since causality is automatically enforced at high density, the fraction of discarded models due to superluminal sound speeds is substantially reduced, facilitating metamodel-based explorations of equilibrium neutron star configurations. We further assess our framework by performing a Bayesian inference of neutron star properties beyond standard observables such as masses and radii, exploiting the metamodel's ability to probe composition-dependent quantities including the dUrca threshold and the Ledoux criterion for g-mode stability.

## Key claim

The authors revise a previously proposed non-relativistic nuclear metamodel by introducing a new functional form for the density-dependent interaction terms that enforces asymptotic causality (sound speed approaching \(1/\sqrt{3}\) rather than exceeding unity), thereby substantially reducing the fraction of models discarded due to superluminal sound speeds while improving accuracy in reproducing the pressure and beta-equilibrium composition of microscopically motivated equations of state.

## Method

The authors construct a nucleonic metamodel for the energy density of homogeneous nuclear matter as a function of baryon density and isospin asymmetry, using an ansatz that separates the non-interacting Fermi gas contribution from interaction terms expanded in even powers of the isospin asymmetry \(\delta\) (up to \(\delta^4\)). The interaction terms \(u_i(n)\) are parametrized with a rational function form whose numerator grows as \(n^3\) and denominator ensures at most linear growth in \(n\) at high density, guaranteeing asymptotic causality; the parameters are mapped to nuclear matter parameters (NMPs) at saturation density via Taylor expansion, and the remaining free parameters are fitted to reproduce the symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) energy slices of five selected realistic equations of state (SLy4, BSk24, DD2, FSU2, TM1e). The metamodel is then extended to include electrons and muons for neutron star applications, and the beta-equilibrated equation of state is computed.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic sound speed of the metamodel (including the Fermi gas term) approaches \(v = 1/\sqrt{3}\) at high density, rather than the superluminal values produced by the original polynomial parametrization.
* The metamodel is tested up to central neutron star densities of approximately \(1.2\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* The fit procedure uses the saturation density \(n_0\) and nuclear matter parameters up to second order from the CompOSE tables for each target model.
* The quartic correction \(u_4(n)\) is parametrized as \(u_4(n) = A\,\frac{n/n_0}{1 + (n/n_0)^B}\) with two additional parameters \(A\) and \(B>0\).
* The total number of free parameters in the metamodel is 21 (19 from the \(u_i\) expressions plus \(n_0\) and two from the quartic correction), of which 6 are fixed by the mapping to NMPs at saturation.

## Context

This work extends the nucleonic metamodel approach of Margueron (2018) by replacing the polynomial density dependence that caused superluminal sound speeds at high densities with an asymptotically causal rational function form, filling a gap between the algebraic simplicity of the original metamodel and the robustness of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. The improved metamodel enables Bayesian inference of composition-dependent neutron star properties such as the direct Urca threshold and the Ledoux criterion for g-mode stability, which are inaccessible to composition-agnostic barotropic models (e.g., piecewise polytropes, spectral representations, Gaussian processes). The approach is complementary to RMF-based metamodels (e.g., Li and Sedrakian, 2019; Malik, 2022) which are more computationally expensive for large ensembles.
