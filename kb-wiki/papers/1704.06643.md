# Hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions

> This contribution presents a theoretical overview of hydrodynamic modelling of heavy-ion collisions, with highlights on some recent developments. In particular, the formulation of anisotropic hydrodynamics, the role of hydrodynamic fluctuations, and the non-linear coupling of flow coefficients will be discussed.

## Key claim
This paper presents a theoretical overview of hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, arguing that the fluidity of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is essential for understanding observed phenomena, while acknowledging that the applicability of hydrodynamics is constrained by the convergence of gradient expansion and challenged by large local pressure anisotropy at initial stages.

## Method
The paper reviews the standard relativistic hydrodynamic framework for heavy-ion collisions, where the evolution of the QGP medium is described by solving conservation of energy-momentum with viscous corrections (shear and bulk viscosity) and an equation of state from lattice QCD. It discusses three recent developments: anisotropic hydrodynamics (ahydro), which absorbs pressure anisotropy into an anisotropic background distribution to improve applicability; hydrodynamic fluctuations formulated stochastically through fluctuation-dissipation relations; and nonlinear mode couplings in harmonic flow, where higher-order flow harmonics receive dominant contributions from nonlinear couplings of lower-order eccentricities.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity over entropy ratio for QGP is close to the theoretical expectation for a strongly coupled system: $\eta/s \sim O(1/4\pi)$
- Hydrodynamics is switched on around time $\tau_0 \sim O(1)$ fm/c in current modeling
- For a Bjorken flow with relaxation time approximation, vahydro (viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics) applies to cases when $4\pi\eta/s$ approaches order of $10^3$, far beyond local thermal equilibrium
- In ultra-central proton-proton, proton-lead and lead-lead systems, hydrodynamic fluctuations produce an additional increase of flow velocity correlations that is smaller in lead-lead and larger in proton-proton
- The ratio of fourth order moment to square of rms value of $v_2$ magnitude shows significant deviation from linear eccentricity scaling when centrality becomes larger than 30% in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy

## Context
This review extends the established hydrodynamic paradigm for heavy-ion collisions, which relates harmonic flow $V_n$ to initial state geometric anisotropies $\mathcal{E}_n$, building on foundational work by Ollitrault (1992) on elliptic flow and the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound (2004) on viscosity. The paper discusses anisotropic hydrodynamics developed by Martinez (2010) and Florkowski (2010), hydrodynamic fluctuations building on Landau's fluctuation-dissipation relations, and nonlinear mode coupling analysis by Yan (2015) that enables extraction of medium-dependent response coefficients $\chi_{422}$ and $\chi_{523}$ from experimental data. These developments enable more precise extraction of QGP transport coefficients and extend hydrodynamic modeling to small colliding systems and the QCD critical point search in beam energy scan programs.
