# Electromagnetic probes of the QGP

> We investigate the properties of the QCD matter across the deconfinement phase transition in the scope of the parton-hadron string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. We present here in particular the results on the electromagnetic radiation, i.e. photon and dilepton production, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. By comparing our calculations for the heavy-ion collisions to the available data, we determine the relative importance of the various production sources and address the possible origin of the observed strong elliptic flow $v_2$ of direct photons. We argue that the different centrality dependence of the hadronic and partonic sources for direct photon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions can be employed to shed some more light on the origin of the photon $v_2$ "puzzle". While the dilepton spectra at low invariant mass show in-medium effects like an enhancement from multiple baryonic resonance formation or a collisional broadening of the vector meson spectral functions, the dilepton yield at high invariant masses (above 1.1 GeV) is dominated by QGP contributions for central heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies. This allows to have an independent view on the parton dynamics via their electromagnetic massive radiation.

# Electromagnetic probes of the QGP

## Key claim

The strong elliptic flow $v_2$ of direct photons observed in heavy-ion collisions, which is comparable to that of pions and constitutes the "photon $v_2$ puzzle," can be attributed primarily to hadronic channels (meson-meson and meson-baryon bremsstrahlung) rather than to QGP radiation, provided the hadronic bremsstrahlung contributions are included as an upper estimate; the QGP itself contributes only a small $v_2$ of about 2%.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on generalized off-shell transport equations derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equations and incorporates the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) to reproduce lattice QCD results for the quark-gluon plasma. Photon and dilepton production rates were calculated from non-equilibrium kinetic theory using matrix elements for hadronic and partonic channels, including meson-meson and meson-baryon bremsstrahlung in the soft-photon approximation, and compared to experimental data from PHENIX, STAR, HADES, and NA60 for nucleus-nucleus collisions from SIS to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Direct photon elliptic flow $v_2$ from QGP channels in PHSD is about 2% for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, while hadronic sources show $v_2$ up to 10%.
- The centrality scaling exponent for thermal photon yield is $\alpha = 1.48 \pm 0.08$ from PHENIX data; PHSD gives $\alpha(\text{total}) \approx 1.5$ and $\alpha(\text{QGP}) \sim 1.7$.
- QGP contributes up to about 50% of the direct photon yield between 1 and 2 GeV/$c$ for the most central bin (0-20%) in Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- Dilepton spectra at low invariant mass (0.2-0.6 GeV/$c^2$) show enhancement from multiple $\Delta$-resonance formation at SIS energies (1-2 $A$GeV) and collisional broadening of the $\rho$-meson spectral function at SPS and RHIC energies.
- The dilepton yield at high invariant masses above 1.1 GeV is dominated by QGP contributions for central heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHSD and HSD transport studies (Bratkovskaya 1996, Cassing 2008, Linnyk 2011) and addresses the photon $v_2$ puzzle first reported by the PHENIX Collaboration (2010), which contradicted expectations from hydrodynamic models. The results contrast with state-of-the-art hydro models (VISH2+1, MUSIC) that underestimate photon $v_2$ and spectra at RHIC and LHC energies, as reviewed by Heinz (2013). The findings enable future experimental tests via centrality dependence of photon $v_2$ and dilepton flow coefficients $v_n$, and provide predictions for upcoming ALICE data at LHC and BES-II RHIC data.
