# Diffusion of charm and beauty in the Glasma

> Relativistic nuclear collisions offer a unique way to study strong interactions at very high energy. The collision process can be described within the gluon saturation framework as the interaction of two colored glasses, and because of this interaction strong longitudinal gluon fields, namely the Glasma, are produced immediately after the collision. Besides, heavy quarks are also produced in the very early stage and because of their large mass and small concentration, their motion does not affect the evolution of the Glasma, thus behaving as ideal probes of the Glasma itself. We study the evolution of the heavy quarks in the Glasma allegedly produced in high energy p-Pb collisions by solving consistently the equations of motion of the quarks in the evolving Glasma fields. We find that this motion can be understood in terms of diffusion in momentum space, similarly to the random motion of a heavy probe in a hot thermalized medium. We show how the diffusion of heavy probes affects the nuclear modification factor of D and B mesons in p-Pb collisions.

## Key claim
The motion of charm and beauty quarks in the Glasma fields produced in high-energy p-Pb collisions can be understood as diffusion in momentum space, similar to Brownian motion in a thermal medium, but the authors do not attempt a direct quantitative comparison with experimental data because their calculation lacks longitudinal expansion.

## Method
The authors solve the Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations for the evolving Glasma fields and the Wong equations for the heavy quark dynamics simultaneously, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the initial color charge distributions. Heavy quarks are initialized with a FONLL-based transverse momentum distribution and zero longitudinal momentum, then propagated through the dynamically evolving gluon fields for charm (mass 1.5 GeV) and beauty (mass 4.5 GeV) in a static box without longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass set to 1.5 GeV; beauty quark mass set to 4.5 GeV.
- Color charge density parameter for Pb nucleus: \(g^2\mu_\mathrm{Pb} = 3.4\) GeV; for proton: \(g^2\mu_\mathrm{p} = 1.41\) GeV.
- Lattice spacing used in the simulation: \(\delta x = 0.04\) fm.
- Transverse fields are produced within approximately \(t \approx 0.1\) fm/c from the initial purely longitudinal fields.
- For large \(p_T\), the nuclear modification factor \(R_\mathrm{pPb}\) remains consistent with perturbative FONLL within 20%.
- The calculation is stopped at \(t = 1\) fm/c for both charm and beauty.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Ruggieri 2018) on charm diffusion in p-Pb collisions to include beauty quarks for the first time. It builds on the Fokker-Planck approach of Mrowczynski (2017) but differs by not relying on the small transferred momentum expansion and by including the dynamical evolution of the gluon medium. The results enable future studies that incorporate longitudinal expansion to allow direct comparison with experimental data from ALICE (Abelev 2014) and LHCb (Aaij 2017) on D-meson and B-meson nuclear modification factors.
