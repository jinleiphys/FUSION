# Analytical E1 strength functions of two-neutron halo nuclei: the 6-He example

> An analytical model is developed to study the spectra of electromagnetic dissociation of two-neutron halo nuclei without precise knowledge about initial and final states. Phenomenological three-cluster bound state wave functions, reproducing the most relevant features of these nuclei, are used along with no interaction final states. The 6-He nucleus is considered as a test case, and a good agreement with experimental data concerning the shape of the spectrum and the magnitude of the strength function is found.

## Key claim

An analytical three-body model using phenomenological bound-state wave functions that reproduce both the correct asymptotic behavior (determined by binding energy) and the correct nuclear size can reproduce the shape and magnitude of the E1 strength function for two-neutron halo nuclei, even when final-state interactions are completely neglected. The agreement with experimental data for 6He is good but the model gives approximately 35% lower cross sections than experiment for both beam energies tested.

## Method

The authors constructed phenomenological three-cluster bound-state wave functions for 6He using an expansion over hyperspherical harmonics, with hyperradial functions of the form χ(ρ) = c[exp(-κ₀ρ) - exp(-κ₁ρ)] that reproduce both the two-neutron separation energy (0.97 MeV) and the rms matter radius (2.50 fm). For the final states, they used a complete set of no-interaction three-body wave functions (free-space solutions to the six-dimensional Schrodinger equation) expressed as products of Bessel functions and hyperspherical harmonics. The E1 strength function was then calculated analytically by evaluating transition matrix elements between the initial bound state and the continuum final states, with selection rules ΔK = ±1, Δl_x = 0, Δl_y = ±1, and ΔL = 0, ±1.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ = 0.97 MeV for 6He, used to fix the asymptotic parameter κ₀
- rms matter radius R_rms = 2.50 fm for 6He, used to fix the second parameter κ₁
- The one-parameter model Ψ₁ gave R_rms = 1.79 fm, which is too small compared to the experimental value
- The ratio c²/(2κ₀) ≈ 4 describes the increase in strength when going from the one-parameter to the two-parameter hyperradial function
- The model gives approximately 35% lower cross sections than experiment for both beam energies (37 MeV/A at NSCL and 240 MeV/A at GSI)
- Integrated strength up to E = 4 MeV, 9 MeV, and infinity was calculated and compared with experimental data and microscopic three-cluster calculations

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pushkin et al. (1996) who used a single K=0 hyperspherical harmonic with a one-parameter hyperradial function for 11Li. The present model improves upon that by adding a second hyperradial term to simultaneously reproduce binding energy and nuclear size, and by including multiple hyperspherical harmonics (K=0 and K=2) appropriate for 6He where the K=0 contribution is small due to Pauli suppression. The results are compared with experimental data from Aumann et al. (1999) at GSI and from Warner et al. (2000) at NSCL, and with microscopic three-cluster calculations by Danilin et al. (1998) and Cobis et al. (1998). The model enables quick analytical predictions of E1 strength functions for other two-neutron halo nuclei such as 11Li and 14Be, which the authors state will be treated in a subsequent paper.
