# On Relevance Of Matrix Coordinates For The Inside Of Baryons

> It is argued that one natural choice for coordinates of constituents of a baryonic state in a SU(N) gauge theory are N x N hermitian matrices. It is discussed that the relevance of matrix coordinates is supported at least by the restricted form of the color symmetry. Based on the previous investigations in this direction, the consequences of the idea are shortly reviewed. The model has been considered is originated by the D0-branes of String Theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the natural coordinates for the constituents of a baryonic state in an SU(N) gauge theory are N x N hermitian matrices, supported at least by the restricted form of color symmetry. The claim is hedged by the recognition that this matrix coordinate construction is presented as a heuristic argument and a formal derivation from first principles of QCD is the stated motivation rather than a completed proof.

## Method

The author constructs N x N hermitian matrix coordinates from the wave functions of N quarks in a baryon, defining matrix elements as overlap integrals of position operator expectation values between different color states. The resulting matrix model action is then shown to be identical to the action of N D0-branes in string theory, with gauge symmetry transformations that mix matrix elements and render individual quark positions unobservable. The paper reviews consequences including the derivation of linear inter-quark potentials from one-loop effective calculations and the whiteness of baryons with respect to the SU(N) sector of external fields.

## Key numbers

- The size of the bound state is estimated as ℓ ∼ m^{-1/3} l^{2/3}, where m is the D0-brane mass and l is the string length scale.
- The one-loop effective potential between D0-branes (quarks) is V_{one-loop} ∼ 4π (d-1)/2 Σ_{a>b=1}^N |x_a - x_b| / l^2, showing linear confinement.
- The center-of-mass charge is N(q+q') after modification, where Nq is the original charge from the D0-brane action.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigations (Fatollahi 2002, Fatollahi 2001) and the D0-brane matrix model approach originating from string theory (Witten 1996, Polchinski 1995). The paper connects the matrix coordinate formalism to the M(atrix) model conjecture and DLCQ framework, and cites lattice results (Bali 2001, Cornwall 1997) and the field theory correlator method (Simonov 1996) as supporting the linear potential behavior. The approach enables a framework for studying baryon substructure, scattering amplitudes, and the regime where non-commutativity becomes probed by short-wavelength external fields.
