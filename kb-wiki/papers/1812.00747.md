# Application of resonant decay method for compound-systems at analysis inclusive spectra in high-energy nuclear reactions

> It is shown, that the exponential decrease of the energy spectra of the fragments with growing its energy, which does not depend from the fragment type, targets, projectiles and projectile energies, and which sometimes accompanied slight oscillations, can be explained by the phenomenon of time resonances. These time resonances correspond to decay of intermediate excited nuclear composite system. For the first time, expressions for the decay rate and the probability of survival of such system as a function of time are obtained. Inclusive spectra for two real high-energy nuclear reactions are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the exponential decrease (sometimes with slight oscillations) of inclusive energy spectra of fragments in high-energy nuclear reactions, which is independent of fragment type, target, projectile, and projectile energy, can be explained by "time resonances" corresponding to the decay of intermediate excited nuclear composite systems, rather than by standard energy resonances.

## Method

The authors develop a phenomenological "time resonance" method based on a correspondence between eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian and the time operator, where the reaction amplitude is parameterized with time parameters \(t_n\) and \(\tau_n\) instead of energy parameters. They use a multi-component exponential form for the reaction amplitude (equation 9) to calculate inclusive energy spectra and derive expressions for the decay rate and survival probability of the intermediate composite system. The model is applied to fit experimental inclusive spectra for two reactions: \(^{20}\text{Ne} + ^{238}\text{U} \rightarrow p + X\) and \(^{20}\text{Ne} + ^{238}\text{U} \rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} + X'\) at 2.1 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction \(^{20}\text{Ne} + ^{238}\text{U} \rightarrow p + X\) at 2.1 GeV/nucleon, the time parameters \(\tau_1 = 0.1 \times 10^{-23}\) sec and \(\tau_2 = 0.1 \times 10^{-23}\) sec, with \(t_2 - t_1 = 1.2 \times 10^{-23}\) sec, were used at emission angles \(30^\circ\), \(60^\circ\), and \(90^\circ\).
* For the reaction \(^{20}\text{Ne} + ^{238}\text{U} \rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} + X'\) at 2.1 GeV/nucleon, the time parameters \(\tau_1 = 0.1 \times 10^{-23}\) sec and \(\tau_2 = 0.2 \times 10^{-23}\) sec, with \(t_2 - t_1 = 0.3 \times 10^{-23}\) sec, were used at emission angles \(30^\circ\), \(60^\circ\), and \(90^\circ\).
* The time resonance parameters \(t_1\) were set to approximately \(3.4 \times 10^{-23}\) sec for the proton reaction and \(3.5 \times 10^{-23}\) sec for the helium-3 reaction (at \(\theta = 30^\circ\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the time resonance method (Omelchenko and Olkhovsky, earlier works) to explain the anomalous exponential behavior of inclusive spectra observed in high-energy nuclear reactions, which had been noted in prior studies for bombarding energies of 0.1-10 GeV/nucleon. The method is positioned as an intermediate approach between fireball models (used at lower energies) and nuclear hydrodynamic models (used at higher energies). The results enable the extraction of time parameters characterizing the decay of highly excited intermediate nuclear systems and suggest that the angular dependence of the coefficients \(C_{\alpha\beta}^n\) is a promising direction for further theoretical development.
