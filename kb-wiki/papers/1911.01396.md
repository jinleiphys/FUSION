# D meson sensitivity to a system size scan at LHC

> Experimental measurements in pA collisions indicate no D meson suppression ($R_{\rm pPb} \sim 1$) but a surprisingly large $v_2$. To better understand these results we propose a system size scan at the LHC involving $^{16}$OO, $^{40}$ArAr, $^{129}$XeXe and $^{208}$PbPb collisions. Using Trento+ v-USPhydro+DAB-MOD to make predictions, we find that the $R_{\rm AA}$ tends towards unity when the system size is decreased, but nonetheless, in the most central collisions $v_2\{2\}$ is almost independent of the colliding system. These results are analyzed in light of path length and initial eccentricity variations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in central collisions, the D meson elliptic flow \(v_2\{2\}\) is roughly independent of the colliding system across all transverse momenta, because a suppression effect from decreasing system radius is counterbalanced by an enhancement from increasing initial eccentricity; this compensation does not occur in mid-central collisions, where a clear hierarchy of \(v_2\) between systems is observed.

## Method

The authors use the Trento initial-condition model coupled to the 2D+1 event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics code v-USPhydro and the heavy-quark Monte Carlo simulation DAB-MOD. Heavy quarks are sampled from FONLL distributions, propagated via a relativistic Langevin model with a spatial diffusion coefficient fixed to \(D/(2\pi T)=2.2\) (the value calibrated to PbPb data), and hadronized using a hybrid fragmentation/coalescence model. Predictions are made for \({}^{16}\)OO, \({}^{40}\)ArAr, \({}^{129}\)XeXe, and \({}^{208}\)PbPb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* In 0–10% central collisions, the D meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) tends toward unity as system size decreases, with \((1-R_{\rm AA})\) approximately proportional to the initial medium radius \(\sim A^{1/3}\).
* In 30–50% mid-central collisions, the \(v_2\{2\}\) of smaller systems is significantly suppressed across all \(p_{\rm T}\) compared to larger systems.
* In 0–10% central collisions, the \(v_2\{2\}\) is roughly independent of the colliding system across all \(p_{\rm T}\), due to competing effects of radius and eccentricity.
* The triangular flow \(v_3\{2\}\) shows a consistent suppression in smaller systems regardless of centrality class, even when \(\varepsilon_3\) changes significantly.
* The deformation of the \({}^{129}\)Xe nucleus (spherical vs. prolate) has no influence on the \(R_{\rm AA}\) but does affect the \(v_2\{2\}\) in central collisions.

## Context

This work extends predictions for a system size scan at the LHC, originally proposed by Citron et al. (2018), to D meson observables. It addresses the puzzle from CMS measurements of large D meson \(v_2\) in pPb collisions (Sirunyan et al., 2018) despite \(R_{\rm pPb}\sim 1\), which previous studies (Xu et al., 2015) could not fully explain. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from OO, ArAr, and XeXe runs to test whether hydrodynamic flow can coexist with minimal jet quenching in small systems, and to disentangle the roles of system size and initial geometry.
