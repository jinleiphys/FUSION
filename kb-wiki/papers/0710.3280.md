# Generalized Entropy and Transport Coefficients of Hadronic Matter

> We use the generalized entropy four-current of the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory of relativistic dissipative fluids to obtain information about fluctuations around equilibrium. This allows one to compute the non-classical coefficients of the entropy 4-flux in terms of the equilibrium distribution functions. The Green-Kubo formulae are used to compute the classical or standard transport coefficients from the fluctuations of entropy due to dissipative fluxes.

## Key claim

The generalized entropy four-current of Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory, combined with fluctuation theory and Green-Kubo formulas, allows one to compute both the standard transport coefficients (bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, thermal conductivity) and the non-classical coefficients of the entropy 4-flux from equilibrium distribution functions, reducing the number of independent parameters in relativistic causal fluid dynamics from eight to three (the relaxation times).

## Method

The author derives the second moments of equilibrium fluctuations for the dissipative fluxes (bulk viscous pressure, heat flux, shear viscous pressure tensor) from the generalized entropy density using the Einstein formula for fluctuation probability. These fluctuations are then related to the non-classical coefficients β_i of the entropy 4-current, which are in turn connected to the standard transport coefficients through the relations β_0 = τ_Π/ζ, β_1 = τ_q/κT, and β_2 = τ_π/2η. The Green-Kubo formulas are used to compute the transport coefficients from time correlations of the dissipative fluxes, assuming Maxwell-Cattaneo relaxation equations for their evolution. For hadronic matter, the author performs molecular-dynamical simulations using the UrQMD microscopic transport model for a meson gas in a box.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity of a meson gas (composed of π, η, ω, ρ, φ) computed via UrQMD using Kubo relations grows with temperature; UrQMD results are about twice those from the variational method
- Shear relaxation time for a hot pion gas calculated from UrQMD is about a factor of two less than results from the variational method
- The non-classical coefficients β_0, β_1, β_2 and coupling coefficients α_0, α_1 are shown as functions of the ratio z = m/T (pion mass to temperature) for a pion gas with m_π = 140 MeV
- Temperature range studied for hadronic matter: 100 MeV < T < 200 MeV at zero baryon number density

## Context

This work extends the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory of relativistic dissipative fluids by providing a fluctuation-based method to compute transport coefficients, building on earlier work by Muronga (2004, 2007) and the Green-Kubo formalism. The approach enables the use of microscopic transport models like UrQMD to extract transport coefficients for hadronic matter, which is important for relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Prior calculations of hadronic matter transport coefficients used the variational method (Prakash, Davesne) and relaxation time approximation (Gavin); the UrQMD results here are about twice the variational values for shear viscosity, attributed to the inclusion of multiple meson resonances and different cross section parameterizations.
