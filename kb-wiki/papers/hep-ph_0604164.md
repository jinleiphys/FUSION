# Probing the convergence of perturbative series in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> Using the examples of pion-nucleon scattering and the nucleon mass we analyze the convergence of perturbative series in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory. For both cases we sum up sets of an infinite number of diagrams by solving equations exactly and compare the solutions with the perturbative contributions.

## Key claim

The perturbative series in baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) converges very well for the nucleon mass, but for pion-nucleon scattering the tree-plus-one-loop perturbative result approximates the re-summed non-perturbative expression very poorly already for center-of-mass energies near the Delta resonance mass squared.

## Method

The authors solved integral equations for the pion-nucleon vertex function exactly, summing infinite sets of diagrams for a potential consisting of the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and the s-channel nucleon-pole diagram. They then compared the exact re-summed expressions with the perturbative tree-plus-one-loop contributions for elastic pion-nucleon scattering cross sections and for the nucleon self-energy. The calculations used dimensional regularization with the infrared renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

* For the nucleon mass, the non-perturbative contribution from the summed diagrams is delta m = -0.00233530 MeV, while the perturbative expansion gives (-0.00230219 - 0.00003305 - 0.00000007 + ...) MeV, showing the leading term reproduces the non-perturbative result well.
* The leading non-analytic correction to the nucleon mass is delta m_3 = -3 g_A^2 M^3 / (32 pi F^2), and the re-summed higher-order contributions become larger than this term for pion masses M greater than about 600 MeV.
* Up to M ~ 500 MeV, the non-perturbative sum of higher-order corrections is suppressed compared to the delta m_3 term.
* The input values used are: nucleon mass in chiral limit m = 882.8 MeV, physical nucleon mass m_N = 938.3 MeV, pion decay constant F = 92.4 MeV, axial-vector coupling in chiral limit g_A = 1.267, and pion mass M = 139.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of convergence issues in baryon chiral perturbation theory first raised by Gasser (1988) and addresses the problem of chiral extrapolation of lattice data, as pursued by Beane (2004), Procura (2003), and Leinweber (2005). The authors find that for pion-nucleon scattering the poor convergence near the Delta resonance cannot be cured simply by including the Delta as an explicit degree of freedom, contrary to the approach of Hacker (2005); instead, the Delta must be included simultaneously with solving the scattering equations. For the nucleon mass, the analysis provides an estimate of an upper limit for the radius of convergence of BChPT, suggesting that extrapolations of lattice data for pion masses above about 600 MeV cannot be trusted.
