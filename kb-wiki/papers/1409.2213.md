# Constraint on K Kbar compositeness of the a0(980) and f0(980) resonances from their mixing intensity

> Structure of the $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$ resonances is investigated with the $a_{0}(980)$-$f_{0}(980)$ mixing intensity from the viewpoint of compositeness, which corresponds to the amount of two-body states composing resonances as well as bound states. For this purpose we first formulate the $a_{0}(980)$-$f_{0}(980)$ mixing intensity as the ratio of two partial decay widths of a parent particle, in the same manner as the recent analysis in BES experiments. Calculating the $a_{0}(980)$-$f_{0}(980)$ mixing intensity with the existing Flatte parameters from experiments, we find that many combinations of the $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$ Flatte parameters can reproduce the experimental value of the $a_{0}(980)$-$f_{0}(980)$ mixing intensity by BES. Next, from the same Flatte parameters we also calculate the $K \bar{K}$ compositeness for $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$. Although the compositeness with the correct normalization becomes complex in general for resonance states, we find that the Flatte parameters for $f_{0}(980)$ imply large absolute value of the $K \bar{K}$ compositeness and the parameters for $a_{0}(980)$ lead to small but nonnegligible absolute value of the $K \bar{K}$ compositeness. Then, connecting the mixing intensity and the $K \bar{K}$ compositeness via the $a_{0}(980)$- and $f_{0}(980)$-$K \bar{K}$ coupling constants, we establish a relation between them. As a result, a small mixing intensity indicates a small value of the product of the $K \bar{K}$ compositeness for the $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$ resonances. Moreover, the experimental value of the $a_{0}(980)$-$f_{0}(980)$ mixing intensity implies that the $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$ resonances cannot be simultaneously $K \bar{K}$ molecular states.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the experimental value of the a0(980)-f0(980) mixing intensity, when combined with the relation between mixing intensity and K Kbar compositeness, implies that the a0(980) and f0(980) resonances cannot both be simultaneously K Kbar molecular states, although many combinations of their Flatte parameters can reproduce the measured mixing intensity.

## Method

The authors formulate the a0(980)-f0(980) mixing intensity as the ratio of two partial decay widths of a parent particle, following the BES experimental analysis. They calculate the mixing intensity using existing Flatte parameter sets from multiple experiments (CLEO, KLOE, CB, SND, E852 for a0(980); CDF, KLOE, Belle, BES, FOCUS, SND for f0(980)). From the same Flatte parameters, they compute the K Kbar compositeness for both resonances using the chiral unitary approach, and then connect the mixing intensity to the product of the K Kbar compositeness values via the coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The experimental mixing intensity from BES is given as a ratio of branching fractions; the paper reports central values of calculated mixing intensities ranging from 0.18% to 2.12% across different Flatte parameter combinations
- The K Kbar threshold difference is approximately 8 MeV, given by (m_{K0} + m_{anti-K0}) - (m_{K+} + m_{K-})
- The charged kaon mass is 493.68 MeV and the neutral kaon mass is 497.61 MeV
- The isospin symmetric kaon mass used is (m_{K+} + m_{K-} + m_{K0} + m_{anti-K0})/4 = 495.65 MeV
- The a0(980) masses from different experiments range from 982.5 MeV (KLOE) to 1001 MeV (E852)
- The f0(980) masses from different experiments range from 950 MeV (Belle) to 989.6 MeV (CDF)

## Context

This work extends the compositeness framework previously applied to the Lambda(1405) resonance (Sekihara, 2013) to the scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980). It builds on the chiral unitary approach studies of compositeness (Hyodo, 2011; Aceti, 2012; Xiao, 2012) and the observation of a0(980)-f0(980) mixing by the BES collaboration (Ablikim, 2010). The paper enables future experimental determination of the K Kbar compositeness of these resonances through precise measurements of their mixing intensity, and provides a constraint that rules out the simultaneous K Kbar molecular interpretation of both states.
