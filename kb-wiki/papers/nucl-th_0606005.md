# Quasiclassical description of bremsstrahlung accompanying alpha decay including quadrupole radiation

> We present a quasiclassical theory of alpha decay accompanied by bremsstrahlung with a special emphasis on the case of 210Po, with the aim of finding a unified description that incorporates both the radiation during the tunneling through the Coulomb wall and the finite energy E_gamma of the radiated photon up to E_gamma on the order of Q_alpha/sqrt(eta), where Q_alpha is the alpha-decay Q-value and eta is the Sommerfeld parameter. The corrections with respect to previous quasiclassical investigations are found to be substantial, and excellent agreement with a full quantum mechanical treatment is achieved. Furthermore, we find that a dipole-quadrupole interference significantly changes the alpha-gamma angular correlation. We obtain good agreement between our theoretical predictions and experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors present a quasiclassical theory of bremsstrahlung accompanying alpha decay that unifies previous treatments by Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and Dyakonov (1999), incorporating both radiation during tunneling and finite photon energy effects, with the condition that the result is valid up to x ~ 0.3 for 210Po rather than the more restrictive x << 1/√η required by earlier quasiclassical approaches.

## Method

The authors calculated the dipole transition matrix element for bremsstrahlung in alpha decay using quasiclassical wave functions with a correction of order O(η̄⁻¹), where η̄ is the mean Sommerfeld parameter, and included the classically forbidden tunneling region by shifting the integration contour into the complex plane. They also computed the quadrupole matrix element within the same quasiclassical approximation and evaluated the dipole-quadrupole interference term in the angular distribution. The results were compared to the full quantum mechanical treatment of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and the semiclassical result of Dyakonov (1999) for the specific case of 210Po.

## Key numbers

- Sommerfeld parameter η_i = 22.0 for 210Po decay, with Q-value Q_α = 5.40746 MeV
- Dipole effective charge Z_eff^(1) = 0.399 for 210Po (using accurate masses); approximate value Z_eff^(1) ≈ 2/5
- Quadrupole effective charge Z_eff^(2) = 1.954 for 210Po, roughly five times larger than the dipole effective charge
- The quasiclassical result with the O(η̄⁻¹) correction deviates from the exact quantum mechanical calculation by less than 1% at x = 0.3 (where x = E_γ/Q_α)
- At E_γ = 600 keV (x ≈ 0.11), the unified quasiclassical result agrees with the exact quantum mechanical treatment within about 2%
- The interference coefficient χ(x) ≈ 8.64 x for 210Po, giving χ ≈ 0.1 at E_γ ≈ 0.06 MeV
- The quadrupole contribution to the total differential emission probability after angular integration is less than 1.5% for photon energies up to 600 keV
- The alpha particle final velocity v ≈ 0.05 for 210Po

## Context

This work extends the quasiclassical treatment of Dyakonov (1999), which was restricted to x << 1/√η, and the treatment of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998), which neglected the imaginary part of the matrix element corresponding to tunneling contributions. The unified result enables accurate analysis of experimental bremsstrahlung data from 210Po decay, as demonstrated in the companion experimental paper by the same group, where the dipole-quadrupole interference term proved essential for interpreting angular correlation measurements due to limited detector solid-angle coverage.
