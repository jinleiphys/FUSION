# Investigating $^{238}$U Deformation via Dilepton Production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Due to their weak coupling to the strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, dileptons serve as a sensitive probe of the initial geometry of the colliding nuclei. In this study, we investigate the influence of initial nuclear quadrupole deformation, characterized by the parameter $β_2$, on dilepton production in $U+U$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=196$ GeV. The analysis is varried out using a modified multiphase transport model in which partonic interactions are described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We observe a clear linear dependence of dilepton yields on $β_2^2$ in both the low-mass region (LMR, $<1 GeV/c^2$) and intermediate-mass region (IMR, $1-3 GeV/c^2$) of the dilepton spectrum for the most central collisions. Also, dilepton production in the IMR region exhibits a stronger sensitivity to nuclear deformation than in the LMR, reflecting the dominance of earlier partonic processes in this mass range. These results suggest that precise measurements of dilepton yields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can provide a viable means to determine the deformation parameter $β_2$ of $^{238}$U.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ratio of dilepton yields (normalized by charged-particle multiplicity) in U+U collisions relative to Au+Au collisions shows a clear linear dependence on the square of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂², with the sensitivity being stronger in the intermediate-mass region (IMR, 1-3 GeV/c²) than in the low-mass region (LMR, <1 GeV/c²). This claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis uses a specific model (NJL-extended AMPT) and focuses on the most central collisions, and the authors note that the LMR subregion (0.4-0.75 GeV/c²) is dominated by ρ⁰ meson decays which are relatively insensitive to deformation.

## Method

The authors used a modified multiphase transport (AMPT) model where partonic interactions are described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, replacing the default parton cascade. Initial nuclear density distributions for deformed ²³⁸U and ¹⁹⁷Au were implemented using a Woods-Saxon distribution with quadrupole deformation parameter β₂. Dilepton production was included perturbatively via two channels: quark-antiquark annihilation (q q̅ → e⁺e⁻) in the partonic phase and π⁺π⁻ → ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻ in the hadronic phase, with in-medium modifications of the ρ⁰ meson mass and width. The analysis was performed for U+U collisions at √s_NN = 193 GeV, and the double yield ratio R_U-Au was constructed using Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV as a reference to reduce volume effects and model dependence.

## Key numbers

- The reported experimental value of β₂ for ²³⁸U from STAR is 0.286 ± 0.025, consistent with earlier low-energy measurements of 0.287 ± 0.007 (Pritychenko 2013).
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for ²³⁸U are R_WS = 6.8054 fm, a_WS = 0.605 fm, and β₂ = 0.2863; for ¹⁹⁷Au: R_WS = 6.38 fm, a_WS = 0.535 fm, and β₂ = -0.131 (Raman 2001).
- The fitted slope |k₂| for the LMR_sub (0.4 < M_ll < 0.75 GeV/c²) is 0.174, and for the IMR (1.0 < M_ll < 3.0 GeV/c²) is 0.225, indicating stronger sensitivity in the IMR.
- The NJL model parameters used: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, G_SΛ² = 3.67, KΛ⁵ = 12.36, momentum cutoff Λ = 602.3 MeV/c.
- The isotropic elastic parton scattering cross section is fixed at 3 mb.
- Using β₂ = 0 for Au instead of β₂ = -0.131 yields less than 1% difference in the dilepton spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the program of imaging nuclear shapes via relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which previously used collective flow and transverse momentum fluctuations (Jia 2021, STAR 2024) to extract β₂ of ²³⁸U. It also builds on earlier dilepton studies that used invariant mass spectra to extract QGP temperature (STAR 2015, STAR 2023). The authors note that unlike flow observables, which can be affected by non-flow effects such as resonance decays and hadronic rescattering (Bierlich 2021), dileptons provide a cleaner probe of initial geometry due to their weak final-state interactions. The approach can be extended to isobar collisions (⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr) and combined with experimental data to extract both nuclear deformation and QGP thermal properties.
