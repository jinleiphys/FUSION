# The effect of $^{22}$Ne diffusion in the evolution and pulsational properties of white dwarfs with solar metallicity progenitors

> Because of the large neutron excess of $^{22}$Ne, this isotope rapidly sediments in the interior of the white dwarfs. This process releases an additional amount of energy, thus delaying the cooling times of the white dwarf. This influences the ages of different stellar populations derived using white dwarf cosmochronology. Furthermore, the overabundance of $^{22}$Ne in the inner regions of the star, modifies the Brunt-Väisälä frequency, thus altering the pulsational properties of these stars. In this work, we discuss the impact of $^{22}$Ne sedimentation in white dwarfs resulting from Solar metallicity progenitors ($Z=0.02$). We performed evolutionary calculations of white dwarfs of masses $0.528$, $0.576$, $0.657$ and $0.833$ M$_{\sun}$, derived from full evolutionary computations of their progenitor stars, starting at the Zero Age Main Sequence all the way through central hydrogen and helium burning, thermally-pulsing AGB and post-AGB phases. Our computations show that at low luminosities ($\log(L/L_{\sun})\la -4.25$), $^{22}$Ne sedimentation delays the cooling of white dwarfs with Solar metallicity progenitors by about 1~Gyr. Additionally, we studied the consequences of $^{22}$Ne sedimentation on the pulsational properties of ZZ~Ceti white dwarfs. We find that $^{22}$Ne sedimentation induces differences in the periods of these stars larger than the present observational uncertainties, particularly in more massive white dwarfs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for white dwarfs with Solar metallicity progenitors (Z=0.02), the gravitational settling of 22Ne delays cooling by about 1 Gyr at low luminosities and induces period differences in ZZ Ceti stars larger than observational uncertainties, particularly for more massive white dwarfs, a process previously considered relevant only for higher-metallicity progenitors.

## Method

The authors computed full evolutionary sequences of 0.528, 0.576, 0.657, and 0.833 solar-mass white dwarfs starting from Zero Age Main Sequence progenitors through hydrogen and helium burning, thermally-pulsing AGB, and post-AGB phases using the LPCODE stellar evolution code. They incorporated 22Ne diffusion using molecular dynamics diffusion coefficients from Hughto et al. (2010) and performed adiabatic nonradial pulsation analysis of g-modes with the LP-PUL code to assess asteroseismological consequences for ZZ Ceti stars.

## Key numbers

- White dwarf masses modeled: 0.528, 0.576, 0.657, and 0.833 solar masses, derived from progenitor masses of 1.0, 1.5, 3.0, and 4.0 solar masses respectively
- At log(L/L_sun) approximately -4.5, time delays from 22Ne sedimentation range from 0.7 to 1.2 Gyr depending on stellar mass
- For the 0.576 solar-mass white dwarf model at log(T_eff)=4.01, period differences between models with and without 22Ne sedimentation are on average about 3 seconds, reaching up to about 11 seconds in the 100-1500 second range
- For the 0.833 solar-mass white dwarf model at log(T_eff)=4.04, period differences average about 15 seconds, reaching up to about 47 seconds
- For the 0.833 solar-mass model at log(T_eff)=4.04, temporal rates of period change are on average about 3 x 10^{-15} s/s larger for sequences neglecting 22Ne diffusion
- Initial 22Ne abundance by mass is about 0.02 throughout the innermost region of the white dwarf

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Garcia-Berro et al. (2008) and Althaus et al. (2010) that showed 22Ne sedimentation strongly affects cooling of white dwarfs from high-metallicity progenitors (Z greater than 0.03), by demonstrating the effect is also significant for Solar metallicity (Z=0.02) progenitors. The paper provides the first grid of Solar-metallicity white dwarf cooling sequences incorporating 22Ne sedimentation consistently, and the first analysis of its impact on pulsation periods of ZZ Ceti stars, enabling more accurate white dwarf cosmochronology and asteroseismological fits, particularly for stars like G117-B15A where period change measurements constrain axion mass.
