# Exploring Current Constraints on Antineutrino Production by $^{241}$Pu and Paths Towards the Precision Reactor Flux Era

> By performing global fits to inverse beta decay (IBD) yield measurements from existing neutrino experiments based at highly $^{235}$U enriched reactor cores and conventional low-enriched cores, we explore current direct bounds on neutrino production by the sub-dominant fission isotope $^{241}$Pu. For this nuclide, we determine an IBD yield of $σ_{241}$ = 8.16 $\pm$ 3.47 cm$^2$/fission, a value (135 $\pm$ 58)% that of current beta conversion models. This constraint is shown to derive from the non-linear relationship between burn-in of $^{241}$Pu and $^{239}$Pu in conventional reactor fuel. By considering new hypothetical neutrino measurements at high-enriched, low-enriched, mixed-oxide, and fast reactor facilities, we investigate the feasible limits of future knowledge of IBD yields for $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{239}$Pu, $^{241}$Pu, and $^{240}$Pu. We find that the first direct measurement of the $^{240}$Pu IBD yield can be performed at plutonium-burning fast reactors, while a suite of correlated measurements at multiple reactor types can achieve precision in direct $^{238}$U, $^{239}$Pu, and $^{241}$Pu yield knowledge that meets or exceeds that of current theoretical predictions.

# Exploring Current Constraints on Antineutrino Production by $^{241}$Pu and Paths Towards the Precision Reactor Flux Era

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that existing global inverse beta decay (IBD) yield datasets provide a direct constraint on $^{241}$Pu antineutrino production of $\sigma_{241} = 8.16 \pm 3.47$ cm$^2$/fission, but this constraint carries a 43% relative uncertainty and derives specifically from the non-linear (curvature) relationship between $^{241}$Pu and $^{239}$Pu fission fractions in conventional low-enriched uranium reactor fuel, not from independent sensitivity to $^{241}$Pu alone.

## Method

The authors performed global least-squares fits to time-integrated IBD yield measurements from existing HEU-based experiments (ILL, Savannah River, Krasnoyarsk, Nucifer, STEREO) and LEU-based experiments (Goesgen, Bugey-3, Bugey-4, Rovno, Palo Verde, CHOOZ, Double Chooz, Daya Bay, RENO), using fission fractions and covariance matrices from Ref. GiuntiGlobal. They applied loose 75% external theoretical constraints on sub-dominant isotopes ($^{238}$U, $^{241}$Pu, $^{240}$Pu) to examine weakly-constrained simultaneous fits of all five fission isotopes' IBD yields. For future projections, they generated Asimov datasets for hypothetical experiments at HEU, LEU, mixed-oxide (MOX), and fast reactor facilities using a PROSPECT-like 4-ton detector model with assumed reactor and detector systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- $^{241}$Pu IBD yield: $\sigma_{241} = 8.16 \pm 3.47$ cm$^2$/fission, which is (135 $\pm$ 58)% of current beta conversion model predictions
- $^{235}$U IBD yield from loose fit: $\sigma_{235} = 6.37 \pm 0.08$ cm$^2$/fission, more than 3$\sigma$ (5%) below Huber-predicted value
- $^{238}$U IBD yield from loose fit: $\sigma_{238} = 7.37 \pm 1.95$ cm$^2$/fission, deviating from predicted central value by (36 $\pm$ 20)%
- $^{239}$Pu IBD yield from loose fit: $\sigma_{239} = 4.00 \pm 1.01$ cm$^2$/fission, consistent with Huber predictions within 5%
- Best-fit $\chi^2_{min} = 26.2$ for 38 degrees of freedom (41 data points, 3 fit parameters) for the loose constraint fit
- $^{240}$Pu IBD yield estimated via 3Z-A scaling: $\sigma_{240} = 4.96 \times 10^{-43}$ cm$^2$/fission (no direct measurement exists)
- Assumed "true" IBD yields for future projections: ($\sigma_{235}, \sigma_{238}, \sigma_{239}, \sigma_{241}$) = (6.05, 10.10, 4.40, 6.03) $\times 10^{-43}$ cm$^2$/fission

## Context

This work extends previous global IBD yield fits by Surukuchi et al. (2021) and Giunti et al. (2022) that determined $\sigma_{235}$, $\sigma_{238}$, and $\sigma_{239}$ but either tightly constrained or ignored $^{241}$Pu. The authors show that the Daya Bay and RENO evolution datasets provide the modest breaking of degeneracy between $^{239}$Pu and $^{241}$Pu yields through curvature in the $\sigma_f$ versus burn-up relationship. The paper enables future experimental planning by demonstrating that a suite of correlated measurements at HEU, LEU, MOX, and fast reactors can achieve precision in direct $^{238}$U, $^{239}$Pu, and $^{241}$Pu yield knowledge meeting or exceeding current theoretical predictions, and that the first direct measurement of $^{240}$Pu IBD yield can be performed at plutonium-burning fast reactors such as PFBR or VTR.
