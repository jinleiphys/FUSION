# The Separable Kernel of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> The dispersion relations for nucleon-nucleon (NN) T-matrix in the framework of Bethe-Salpeter equation for two spin one-half particle system and with separable kernel of interaction are considered in the paper. The developed expressions are applied for construction of the separable kernel of interaction for S partial-waves in singlet and triplet channels. We calculate the low energy scattering parameters and the phase shifts and also the deuteron binding energy with the separable interaction. The approach can be easily extended to higher partial-waves for NN-scattering and other reactions (anti N N-, pi N-scattering).

# Separable Kernel of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering can be solved analytically using a rank-I separable kernel of the Yamaguchi type, yielding explicit dispersion relations that connect the kernel parameters (λ and β) to experimental observables, but only after omitting all partial waves containing negative-energy nucleons and neglecting the coupled ³D₁ wave in the triplet channel, retaining only the dominant ¹S₀⁺ and ³S₁⁺ components.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave decomposition of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two spin-one-half particles in the center-of-mass frame, retaining only the positive-energy ¹S₀⁺ wave in the singlet channel and the ³S₁⁺ wave in the triplet channel. They introduced a rank-I separable kernel of the Yamaguchi form g(p₀,p) = (p₀² − p² − β² + iε)⁻¹, solved the resulting equation to obtain the T-matrix in N/D form, and derived dispersion relations with two spectral functions (elastic and inelastic). The analytic expressions for the real and imaginary parts of the function h(s) were then used to fit the kernel parameters λ and β to reproduce experimental low-energy scattering parameters and the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹S₀ channel: λ = −0.29425404 GeV⁻², β = 0.22412880 GeV
- For the ³S₁ channel: λ = −0.79271213 GeV⁻², β = 0.27160579 GeV
- Calculated singlet scattering length a₀ₛ = −23.748 fm (experiment: −23.748 ± 0.010 fm)
- Calculated singlet effective range r₀ₛ = 2.75 fm (experiment: 2.75 ± 0.05 fm)
- Calculated triplet scattering length a₀ₜ = 5.424 fm (experiment: 5.424 ± 0.004 fm)
- Calculated triplet effective range r₀ₜ = 1.774 fm (experiment: 1.759 ± 0.005 fm)
- Calculated deuteron binding energy E_d = 2.224644 MeV (experiment: 2.224644 ± 0.000046 MeV)
- The separable kernel reproduces phase shifts up to T_lab = 100–150 MeV
- Connection to one-boson exchange: for scalar meson σ′ (μ = 0.550 GeV, g²/4π = 7.07), the equivalent λ = −0.2166930823 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic separable-potential approach of Yamaguchi (1954) to the fully relativistic Bethe-Salpeter framework, providing analytic dispersion relations that were previously unavailable for the spin-one-half case. The method connects to the one-boson exchange model of the Bonn group (Machleidt, 1989) by showing that the separable kernel includes the ladder approximation for scalar and vector meson exchange. The approach enables future extension to higher partial waves, multi-rank separable kernels, and other reactions such as antinucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering, as well as applications to electron-deuteron scattering where relativistic pair currents are important.
