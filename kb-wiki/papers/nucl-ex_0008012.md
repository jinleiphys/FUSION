# Study of Residual Product Nuclide Yields from 0.1, 0.2, 0.8, and 2.6 GeV Proton-Irradiated nat-Hg Targets

> The direct gamma-spectrometry method is used to measure more than 350 residual product nuclide yields from 0.1, 0.2, 0.8, and 2.6 GeV proton irradiated HgO targets. The gamma-spectrometer resolution is of 1.8 keV at the 1332 keV gamma-line. The resultant gamma-spectra are processed by the GENIE-2000 code. The gamma-lines are identified, and the cross-sections calculated, by the ITEP-designed SIGMA code using the PCNUDAT radioactive database. The 27-Al(p,x)Na-22 reaction is used as monitor. The experimental results are compared with calculations by the LAHET, CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, and YIELDX codes.

## Key claim

The authors measured more than 350 residual product nuclide yields from proton-irradiated natural mercury targets at four energies, but the abstract's claim of "measurement" glosses over the fact that yields for ground and metastable states individually had to be excluded from comparison with simulations because the codes do not calculate them separately; the comparison was therefore limited to cases where both states could be summed or where only cumulative yields were relevant.

## Method

Thin discs of pressed nat-HgO powder (impurities below 0.16%) were irradiated at the ITEP U-2 synchrotron at 0.1, 0.2, 0.8, and 2.6 GeV proton energies, using the 27Al(p,x)22Na reaction as a monitor. Gamma-spectra were acquired with a detector having 1.8 keV resolution at 1332 keV and processed with GENIE-2000; the SIGMA code using the PCNUDAT database identified gamma lines and calculated cross sections. Experimental yields were compared nuclide-by-nuclide with simulations from LAHET, CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, and YIELDX codes.

## Key numbers

- More than 350 residual product nuclide yields measured from 22Na to 203Hg across four proton energies (0.1, 0.2, 0.8, 2.6 GeV)
- Monitor reaction 27Al(p,x)22Na cross sections used: 19.1 ± 1.3 mb at 0.1 GeV, 15.1 ± 0.9 mb at 0.2 GeV, 15.5 ± 0.9 mb at 0.8 GeV, 11.7 ± 0.9 mb at 2.6 GeV
- At 0.1 GeV, 48 total yields measured (35 selected for comparison); at 0.2 GeV, 66 total (49 selected); at 0.8 GeV, 106 total (88 selected); at 2.6 GeV, 142 total (121 selected)
- Best agreement in spallation region: CEM2k gave mean factor <F> = 1.96 at 0.1 GeV, 1.66 at 0.2 GeV, 1.65 at 0.8 GeV; at 2.6 GeV CEM2k overestimates fission cross section (expected ~75 mb) by a factor of 4
- At 0.8 GeV, YIELDX gave best agreement for A > 130; INUCL for A < 130
- At 2.6 GeV, CEM95 and CASCADE agreed with data for A > 100; none of the codes described A < 100 well

## Context

This work provides the first comprehensive set of residual nuclide yields from proton-irradiated natural mercury at multiple energies, directly motivated by the need for nuclear data for Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) target designs (referenced as bib1-bib3). The experimental data enable benchmarking of spallation and fission models in codes such as LAHET, CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, and YIELDX; the authors note that CEM95 and CEM2k lack fission fragment production models and that CEM2k overestimates fission cross sections above 1 GeV, requiring further development. The results highlight that all codes perform poorly in fission and fragmentation mass regions (e.g., 80 < A < 103 at 0.1-0.2 GeV, 48 < A < 130 at 0.8 GeV, 28 < A < 100 at 2.6 GeV), indicating a need for improved hadron-nucleus interaction models.
