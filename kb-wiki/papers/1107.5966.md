# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

> An improved density-matrix expansion is used to calculate the nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. The two-body interaction comprises long-range one- and two-pion exchange contributions and a set of contact terms contributing up to fourth power in momenta. In addition we employ the leading order chiral three-nucleon interaction with its parameters $c_E, c_D$ and $c_{1,3,4}$ fixed in calculations of nuclear few-body systems. With this input the nuclear energy density functional is derived to first order in the two- and three-nucleon interaction. We find that the strength functions $F_\nabla(ρ)$ and $F_{so}(ρ)$ of the surface and spin-orbit terms compare in the relevant density range reasonably with results of phenomenological Skyrme forces. However, an improved description requires (at least) the treatment of the two-body interaction to second order. This observation is in line with the deficiencies in the nuclear matter equation of state $\bar E(ρ)$ that remain in the Hartree-Fock approximation with low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions.

# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

## Key claim

The nuclear energy density functional derived from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions at first order (Hartree-Fock level) yields strength functions for surface and spin-orbit terms that compare reasonably with phenomenological Skyrme forces in the relevant density range, but an improved description requires treatment of the two-body interaction at least to second order, consistent with deficiencies remaining in the nuclear matter equation of state at the Hartree-Fock level.

## Method

The authors used an improved density-matrix expansion (phase-space averaged, following Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner) to derive a nuclear energy density functional from the N$^3$LOW chiral nucleon-nucleon potential (with cutoff scale $\Lambda = 414$ MeV, matching the low-momentum potential $V_{\rm low-k}$) combined with the leading-order chiral three-nucleon interaction. The two-body interaction includes long-range one- and two-pion exchange and contact terms up to fourth power in momenta, with 24 low-energy constants fixed to NN phase shifts and deuteron properties. The three-nucleon interaction parameters $c_E$, $c_D$, and $c_{1,3,4}$ were taken from calculations of nuclear few-body systems. The functional was derived to first order in the two- and three-nucleon interaction for isospin-symmetric ($N=Z$) systems, producing density-dependent strength functions $\bar E(\rho)$, $F_\tau(\rho)$, $F_\nabla(\rho)$, $F_{so}(\rho)$, and $F_J(\rho)$.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff scale for the N$^3$LOW chiral NN-potential is $\Lambda = 414$ MeV, which coincides with the resolution scale of the universal low-momentum NN-potential $V_{\rm low-k}$ at $\Lambda = 2.1$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The constant contribution to $F_d(\rho)$ from the finite-range two-body interaction is $-27.1$ MeV fm$^5$.
- The low-energy constants for the contact terms include: $C_S = -117.5$ GeV$^{-2}$, $C_T = 2.937$ GeV$^{-2}$, and for quadratic terms $C_5 = 717.4$ GeV$^{-4}$ (relevant for spin-orbit).
- The three-body contact parameter is $c_E = -0.625$ (with $\Lambda_\chi = 700$ MeV), and the $1\pi$-exchange parameter is $c_D = -2.06$.
- The $2\pi$-exchange three-body parameters are $c_1 = -0.76$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -4.78$ GeV$^{-1}$, and $c_4 = 3.96$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The effective nucleon mass at nuclear matter saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is $M^*(\rho_0) \simeq 0.67 M$, compatible with the range $0.7 < M^*(\rho_0)/M < 1$ spanned by phenomenological Skyrme forces.
- The three-body contribution to $F_\tau(\rho)$ at $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ adds a correction of about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2010) by applying their improved density-matrix expansion to chiral interactions at N$^3$LO, and builds on earlier calculations by Holt, Kaiser and Weise (2010) that treated only pion-exchange contributions without short-range contact terms. The results are compared to phenomenological Skyrme forces (such as those by Chabanat et al. and Pearson et al.) and show that first-order Hartree-Fock calculations with low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions still yield an insufficiently deep nuclear matter saturation minimum, consistent with findings by Hebeler et al. (2011) and Achim et al. (2011). The work enables future second-order perturbative calculations that should improve the surface and spin-orbit strength functions, and provides a microscopically derived functional that can be tested in finite-nucleus calculations.
