# Exclusive production of pion pairs with large invariant mass in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The cross section for exclusive $π^+π^-$ and $π^0π^0$ meson pairs production in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is calculated at the energy available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider, i.e., $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.5$ TeV. The cross section for elementary $γγ\toππ$ process is calculated with the help of the pQCD Brodsky-Lepage approach with the distribution amplitude used recently to describe the pion transition form factor measured by the BaBar Collaboration.

# Exclusive production of pion pairs with large invariant mass in nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The cross section for exclusive $\pi^+\pi^-$ and $\pi^0\pi^0$ pair production in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies is calculated using the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD approach with a pion distribution amplitude tuned to BaBar data, but the authors note that other mechanisms (pion exchange, resonances, and high-energy $\pi\pi$ rescatterings) are necessary to correctly describe the elementary cross section, as the pQCD results do not fully match experimental data.

## Method

The elementary $\gamma\gamma \to \pi\pi$ cross section was computed using the leading-order pQCD Brodsky-Lepage formalism with helicity-dependent hard scattering amplitudes, employing a Wu-Huang pion distribution amplitude (parameter B $\approx$ 0.6) evolved via ERBL pQCD evolution from an initial scale $\mu_0^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$. The nuclear cross section for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ TeV was obtained using the b-space equivalent photon approximation (EPA), with a form factor $F^{pQCD}_{reg}(t,u)$ to exclude the nonperturbative small-Mandelstam region, and realistic nuclear charge densities.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ TeV (LHC Pb-Pb collisions)
- Nuclear impact parameter cutoff: $b > R_1 + R_2 \approx 14$ fm for peripheral collisions
- Enhancement of nuclear cross section over elementary cross section: factor of approximately $10^4$, less than the naive $Z_1^2 Z_2^2$ expectation
- Ratio of $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\pi^0$ to $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-$ cross section: about 0.12 for $|\cos \theta| < 0.6$, averaged over $z$
- Above $\sqrt{s_{\gamma\gamma}} > 3$ GeV, the results with and without the regularization form factor coincide

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of exclusive $\rho^0\rho^0$, $\mu^+\mu^-$, heavy-quark pairs, and $D\bar{D}$ production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, various years). The pion distribution amplitude used is the Wu-Huang form (Wu and Huang, recent), which was found to describe BaBar data on the pion transition form factor, and is compared to the earlier Chernyak-Zhitnitsky distribution amplitude (Chernyak and Zhitnitsky). The results show that the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD approach alone does not fully reproduce experimental data points, which lie between the pQCD and hand-bag model predictions, motivating future inclusion of pion exchange, resonances, and high-energy $\pi\pi$ rescatterings.
