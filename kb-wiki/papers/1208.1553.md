# Coherent Neutrinoproduction of Photons and Pions in a Chiral Effective Field Theory for Nuclei

> In this paper we study coherent neutrinoproduction of photons [neutral current (NC)] and pions [charged current (CC) and NC] with $E_ν \leqslant 0.5 $ GeV. The production from nucleons and incoherent production with $E_ν \leqslant 0.5 \mathrm{GeV}$ have been studied in [B. D. Serot and X. Zhang, Phys. Rev. C 86, (2012) 015501; and X. Zhang and B. D. Serot, Phys. Rev. C 86, (2012) 035502]. These processes are relevant to the background analysis in neutrino-oscillation experiments, for example MiniBooNE [A. A. Aquilar-Arevalo et al. (MiniBooNE Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. 100, 032301 (2008)]. We work in the framework of a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory (EFT), which contains nucleons, pions, the Delta (1232) ($Δ$s), isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) fields, and isovector vector ($ρ$) fields. The Lagrangian exhibits a nonlinear realization of (approximate) $SU(2)_{L} \otimes SU(2)_{R}$ chiral symmetry and incorporates vector meson dominance. In the calculation, a revised version of the so-called "optimal approximation" is applied, in which one-nucleon interaction amplitude is factorized out and its medium-modifications are included. The distortion of final pion wave function is calculated using the Eikonal approximation. In addition, we briefly mention the ambiguity of off-shell interaction amplitude. To benchmark the approximation scheme, we study coherent pion photoproduction. Our scheme is then applied to study the neutrinoproductions. In the pion photoproduction and NC photon production, we are able to address the contributions of two contact terms that are partially related to the proposed anomalous interactions involving $ω(ρ)$ $Z$ boson and photon. The calculation is focused on ${}^{12}C$ target, and can be applied to other medium-heavy nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors compute coherent neutrinoproduction of photons and pions from nuclei using a chiral effective field theory, finding that the contact term increases neutral-current photon production by approximately 10% for a reasonable range of the contact coupling, but this enhancement appears insufficient to explain the MiniBooNE low-energy excess events, though a high-energy computation is needed for a firm conclusion.

## Method

The authors work in a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory containing nucleons, pions, Delta (1232) resonances, and isoscalar scalar (sigma), isoscalar vector (omega), and isovector vector (rho) fields, with a nonlinear realization of approximate SU(2)L x SU(2)R chiral symmetry and vector meson dominance. A revised "optimal approximation" is applied where the one-nucleon interaction amplitude is factorized out with medium modifications included, and the final pion wave function distortion is calculated using the Eikonal approximation. The nuclear ground state is computed using the mean-field approximation, and the calculation is benchmarked against coherent pion photoproduction data before being applied to neutrinoproduction on a carbon-12 target.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range studied: E_nu <= 0.5 GeV
- For the Delta width below pion threshold in photon production: Gamma_sp ~ V_0 x rho(r)/rho(0) with V_0 = 80 MeV
- The contact term c_1 = 1.5 is used, with one calculation doubling it to c_1 = 3
- The contact term increases NC photon production by approximately 10% assuming a reasonable range of the contact coupling
- For pion photoproduction at E_gamma = 0.173, 0.235, and 0.29 GeV, angular distributions are compared with data from Gothe (1995) and Arends (1983)
- The pion optical potential uses g' = 0.63

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutrinoproduction from free nucleons (Serot and Zhang, 2012) and incoherent production from nuclei (Zhang and Serot, 2012) to coherent production processes relevant to background analysis in neutrino-oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2008). The calculation differs from other microscopic approaches (Singh, 2006; Alvarez-Ruso, 2007; Amaro, 2009; Hernandez, 2009; Leitner, 2009; Nakamura, 2010) by using a Lorentz-covariant EFT with consistent medium modifications, and from macroscopic approaches based on PCAC (Adler, 1964; Rein, 1983; Paschos, 2006; Berger, 2009) by providing a microscopic treatment valid at low energies. The results enable comparison with the NUANCE event generator output and assessment of whether anomalous interactions involving omega, rho, Z boson, and photon (Harvey, 2007; Hill, 2010) could explain the MiniBooNE excess.
