# Three-potential formalism for the three-body Coulomb scattering problem

> We propose a three-potential formalism for the three-body Coulomb scattering problem. The corresponding integral equations are mathematically well-behaved and can succesfully be solved by the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method. The results show perfect agreements with existing low-energy $n-d$ and $p-d$ scattering calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a three-potential formalism for the three-body Coulomb scattering problem that yields mathematically well-behaved integral equations, which can be solved numerically by the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method; the claim is qualified by the fact that the method is demonstrated only for energies below the breakup threshold, and the author notes that above-breakup calculations require further development due to computational limitations.

## Method

The author derives a set of Faddeev-Noble integral equations combined with a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, linking the three-body Coulomb Green's operator to the channel-distorted Coulomb Green's operator via an auxiliary potential that compensates the Coulomb tail. The short-range part of the interaction is expanded in a separable form using a Coulomb-Sturmian basis, while the long-range Coulomb part is treated exactly through a contour integral representation of the channel-distorted Coulomb Green's operator. The resulting linear system of equations for the coefficients of the Faddeev components is solved algebraically.

## Key numbers

- Convergence to 4 significant digits is achieved with N=30 terms in the separable expansion for both n-d and p-d doublet cases.
- The b parameter of the Coulomb-Sturmian basis is fixed at b=1 fm^{-1} for all calculations.
- Scattering lengths are extrapolated from phase shifts at energies down to 0.01 MeV (doublet case) and 0.001 MeV (quartet case).
- Coulombic calculations require roughly 30% more computer time than short-range-only calculations.
- For above-breakup energies just above threshold, acceptable convergence is reached with terms up to N=34, but the method fails to converge for higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the two-potential formalism of Noble (1967) and the channel-distorted formalism of Bencze (1972) by linking the three-body Coulomb Green's operator to the channel-distorted Coulomb Green's operator via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, without neglecting the intermediate-range polarization potential. The results show perfect agreement with the configuration-space Faddeev calculations of Chen, Payne, Friar, and Gibson (1989) for low-energy n-d and p-d scattering. The method enables future above-breakup Coulomb scattering calculations, though the author notes that further refinement is needed for higher energies.
