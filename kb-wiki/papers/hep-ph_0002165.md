# The eta' and the topological charge density

> We compare two different frameworks which have been proposed to include the eta' in chiral perturbation theory. The equivalence of these two approaches is shown both for the purely mesonic case and in the presence of the ground state baryon octet. The relation between the different sets of parameters in both Lagrangians is clarified.

# The $\eta'$ and the topological charge density

## Key claim
The paper proves the equivalence of two previously proposed frameworks for including the $\eta'$ meson in chiral perturbation theory, both for the purely mesonic sector and when the ground state baryon octet is included. The equivalence holds only when the topological charge density $Q$ is eliminated via its classical equation of motion, not its quantum-corrected equation of motion, which would destroy the equivalence by introducing nonanalytic terms in the quark masses.

## Method
The author compares two approaches: (1) a $U(3)_R \times U(3)_L$ invariant effective Lagrangian where the vacuum angle $\theta(x)$ is treated as an external field that transforms under axial $U(1)$ to compensate the anomaly, and (2) a framework where the topological charge density $Q(x)$ is kept as an explicit background field in the effective theory and then eliminated via its classical equation of motion. The comparison is performed by expanding both Lagrangians to the same order in derivatives, quark masses, and powers of $Q$, then matching the resulting expressions term-by-term. The analysis is extended to higher orders in $Q$ and to the inclusion of baryons, where the equation of motion for $Q$ is solved perturbatively in the $1/N_c$ expansion.

## Key numbers
- The $\eta'$ mass is 958 MeV, considerably heavier than the octet pseudoscalar mesons.
- The pion decay constant $F_\pi \simeq 92.4$ MeV is used to normalize the meson field $U(x)$.
- The singlet $\eta_0$ couples to the singlet axial current with strength $F_0$, distinguished from $F_\pi$ because $U(3)_R \times U(3)_L$ does not exhibit nonet symmetry.
- The low-energy constants $V_1(0) = V_2(0) = F_\pi^2/4$ give the correct normalization for the quadratic terms of the Goldstone boson octet.
- The topological charge density $Q$ scales as $Q \propto g^2 \propto 1/N_c$ in the large $N_c$ limit, motivating the truncation of higher powers of $Q$.

## Context
This work extends and reconciles two lines of research on including the $\eta'$ in chiral perturbation theory: the $U(3)_R \times U(3)_L$ invariant approach of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) and Leutwyler (1996), and the approach using explicit gluonic terms by Di Vecchia and Veneziano (1980). The equivalence had been evident only to lowest order in the chiral and $1/N_c$ expansions; this paper proves it systematically at higher orders. The baryonic extension builds on recent work by Borasoy (1999) and Steininger (1999), which had used the two different frameworks without clarifying their connection. The result enables practitioners to use either formalism interchangeably and clarifies the relation between the different sets of low-energy constants in both Lagrangians.
