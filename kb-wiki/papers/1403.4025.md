# Particle production in relativistic $pp$($\overline{p}$) and $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies with Tsallis statistics using the two-cylindrical multisource thermal model

> An improved Tsallis statistics is implemented in a multisource thermal model to describe systematically pseudorapidity spectra of charged particles produced in relativistic nucleon-nucleon ($pp$ or $p\overline{p}$) collisions at various collision energies and in relativistic nucleus-nucleus ($AA$) collisions at different energies with different centralities. The results with Tsallis statistics using the two-cylindrical multisource thermal model are in good agreement with the experimental data measured at RHIC and LHC energies. It is found that the rapidity shifts of longitudinal sources increase linearly with collision energies and centralities in the framework. According to the laws, we also give a prediction of the pseudorapidity distributions in $pp$($\overline{p}$) collisions at higher energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an improved Tsallis statistics embedded in a two-cylindrical multisource thermal model can systematically describe pseudorapidity spectra of charged particles in both nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus collisions across RHIC and LHC energies, with the hedge that the model uses three free rapidity-shift parameters that must be extracted from fits to experimental data.

## Method

The authors embed an improved, thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution into a multisource thermal model where projectile and target cylinders (ranges of emission sources in rapidity space) are produced in high-energy collisions. For each collision system and centrality, the transverse momentum spectra are first fitted with the Tsallis distribution to extract temperature \(T\) and nonequilibrium parameter \(q\); then the pseudorapidity distributions are reproduced using Monte Carlo sampling of emission sources distributed within the two cylinders and two leading-particle sources, with three free rapidity-shift parameters (\(y_{pmin}\), \(y_{pmax}\), \(y_P\)) determined by fitting the pseudorapidity data.

## Key numbers

* In \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the Tsallis fit to \(P_T\) spectra yields \(\chi^2/{\rm dof}\) values ranging from 0.324 to 1.210.
* For \(pp\)(\(p\bar{p}\)) collisions from 53 to 900 GeV, the rapidity-shift parameters follow linear dependences on \(\ln\sqrt{s_{NN}}\): \(y_{pmax} = (0.506\pm0.041)\ln\sqrt{s_{NN}} + (0.642\pm0.061)\) and \(y_P = (0.211\pm0.047)\ln\sqrt{s_{NN}} + (2.855\pm0.645)\).
* For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the rapidity-shift parameters decrease linearly with centrality percentage \(C\): \(y_{pmax} = -(0.008\pm0.001)C + (3.574\pm0.042)\) and \(y_P = -(0.016\pm0.002)C + (4.604\pm0.049)\).
* For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, the centrality dependence gives \(y_{pmax} = -(0.009\pm0.001)C + (3.982\pm0.045)\) and \(y_P = -(0.010\pm0.002)C + (5.080\pm0.057)\).
* Predictions for \(pp\)(\(p\bar{p}\)) collisions at 10 TeV use parameters \(y_{pmin}=0.260\), \(y_{pmax}=5.297\), \(y_P=4.798\), and normalization \(N_c=51.280\); at 14 TeV, \(y_{pmin}=0.271\), \(y_{pmax}=5.471\), \(y_P=4.862\), and \(N_c=54.204\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own multisource thermal model (Li, Liu, and collaborators, prior to 2014) by replacing the classical Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution with an improved Tsallis distribution that satisfies thermodynamic consistency, as proposed by Cleymans (2012). The model successfully describes pseudorapidity data from PHOBOS, PHENIX, UA5, ALICE, CMS, CDF, and P238 collaborations across energies from 53 GeV to 7 TeV, and provides a predictive framework for higher LHC energies (10 and 14 TeV) and for other centralities in Au+Au, Cu+Cu, and Pb+Pb collisions.
