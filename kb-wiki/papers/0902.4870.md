# Topology studies of hydrodynamics using two particle correlation analysis

> The effects of fluctuating initial conditions are studied in the context of relativistic heavy ion collisions where a rapidly evolving system is formed. Two particle correlation analysis is applied to events generated with the NEXSPHERIO hydrodynamic code, starting with fluctuating non-smooth initial conditions (IC). Results show that the non-smoothness in the IC survives the hydro-evolution and can be seen as topological features of the angular correlation function of the particles emerging from the evolving system. A long range correlation is observed in the longitudinal direction and in the azimuthal direction a double peak structure is observed in the opposite direction to the trigger particle. This analysis provides clear evidence that these are signatures of the combined effect of tubular structures present in the IC and the proceeding collective dynamics of the hot and dense medium.

## Key claim
The non-smoothness of fluctuating initial conditions (IC) in relativistic heavy ion collisions survives hydrodynamic evolution and manifests as topological features in the two-particle angular correlation function, specifically a long-range longitudinal correlation ("Ridge") and a double-peak structure in the away-side azimuthal direction. These features arise only when fluctuating IC are coupled with collective hydrodynamic expansion on an event-by-event basis, not from smooth IC or from the IC alone.

## Method
The authors used the NEXSPHERIO hydrodynamic code, which combines the NEXUS event generator (based on the Gribov-Regge model) to provide fluctuating three-dimensional initial conditions with the SPHERIO code, which solves relativistic hydrodynamic equations in 3D using the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) method. Approximately 200,000 events of Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV were generated for two centrality classes (top 10% and 40-60%). Two-particle correlation analysis in azimuthal angle Δφ and pseudorapidity Δη was applied to charged particles, with trigger particles having pT > 2.5 GeV/c and associated particles pT > 1.0 GeV/c, after subtracting mixed-event backgrounds and elliptic flow (v2) contributions using the ZYAM method.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, with centrality classes of top 10% (central) and 40-60% (peripheral).
- Trigger particle threshold: pT > 2.5 GeV/c; associated particle threshold: pT > 1.0 GeV/c.
- Trigger particle η acceptance limited to ±1.5 to match experimental methods.
- The near-side correlation extends over several units of Δη, up to at least Δη ≈ 2 within the detector acceptance.
- The away-side double-peak structure appears at Δφ ≈ π ± 2 (radians) after v2 subtraction for central events, while peripheral events show no clear away-side correlation.
- Smooth IC (averaged over several NEXUS events) produce no excess beyond v2, whereas fluctuating IC produce the observed topological features.

## Context
This work extends the study of the "Ridge" phenomenon observed by STAR (2005) and PHENIX in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which had been previously explained by models such as Glasma flux tubes (Larry McLerran and collaborators, 2006) and other approaches (Voloshin, 2006; Shuryak, 2007). The authors show that the combination of fluctuating initial conditions from NEXUS with full 3D hydrodynamic evolution in SPHERIO reproduces both the long-range longitudinal correlation and the away-side double-peak structure without requiring explicit jet quenching or conical emission mechanisms. This enables future studies of sub-fermi-scale particle production mechanisms and suggests that initial-state fluctuations can be imprinted on final-state observables through collective dynamics, analogous to super-horizon fluctuations in cosmic microwave background studies (Smoot, 1992).
