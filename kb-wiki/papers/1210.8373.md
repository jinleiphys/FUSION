# Convergence Properties of the Effective Theory for Trapped Bosons

> We investigate few-boson systems with resonant interactions in a narrow harmonic trap within an effective theory framework. The size of the model space is identified with the effective theory cutoff. In the universal regime, the interactions of the bosons can be approximated by contact interactions. We investigate the convergence properties of genuine and smeared contact interactions as the size of the model space is increased and present a detailed error analysis. The spectra for few-boson systems with up to 6 identical particles are calculated by combining extrapolations in the cutoff and in the smearing parameter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for few-boson systems in a harmonic trap, the convergence of effective theory calculations as the model-space cutoff is increased can be dramatically improved by replacing genuine contact interactions with smeared (Gaussian) contact interactions, changing the leading error from a power-law scaling in the cutoff to an exponential scaling, but only if the smearing parameter and cutoff limits are taken in the correct order.

## Method

The authors implement a leading-order effective theory for spinless bosons with resonant two-body interactions in an isotropic harmonic trap, using a finite oscillator-basis model space whose size is identified with the ultraviolet cutoff N. They renormalize the two-body and three-body coupling constants at each N by fixing one two-body energy and one three-body energy, then compute spectra for systems of up to six identical bosons by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix. For smeared interactions, the delta-function contact potentials are replaced by narrow Gaussians of width epsilon, and the convergence is studied as a function of both N and epsilon, with extrapolations to the zero-range limit.

## Key numbers

* For contact interactions, the running coupling constant g^(2)(N) vanishes as -pi^2/(sqrt(2) sqrt(N)) + O(1/N) for large N in the unitary limit with renormalization energy E^(2)=0.5.
* For smeared contact interactions, the two-body coupling constant in the full Hilbert space (N to infinity) expands as g^(2)_infinity(epsilon) = -2 pi^(3/2) epsilon + O(epsilon^2), showing linear dependence on the smearing parameter.
* For finite N, the two-body coupling constant expansion has no linear term: g^(2)_N(epsilon) = c_0(N,E^(2)) + c_2(N,E^(2)) epsilon^2 + O(epsilon^4), where c_0 vanishes as 1/sqrt(N).
* The deviation of three-body energy eigenvalues from exact values for contact interactions shows a power-law dependence on N+3 with slope s approx -0.6 for the first four Efimov states above the renormalization state (E^(2)=0.5, E^(3)=-1).
* For smeared interactions, the energy convergence with N follows an exponential form E_N approx E_infinity + c_1 exp(-c_2 sqrt(N)) in the two-body sector.

## Context

This work extends the effective-theory approach of Stetcu et al. (2006, 2009) and Rotureau et al. (2011), who formulated an effective theory for short-range nuclear forces in the no-core shell model, to systems of up to six trapped bosons. It builds on the authors' previous study (Tolle et al., 2010) of three- and four-body systems and provides a systematic comparison of convergence properties between genuine and smeared contact interactions. The results enable controlled extrapolations to the zero-range limit for larger few-body systems, and the exponential convergence observed for smeared interactions mirrors findings by Coon et al. (2012) for the Idaho N3LO potential, while differing from the Gaussian dependence reported by Furnstahl et al. (2012) for SRG-evolved chiral interactions.
