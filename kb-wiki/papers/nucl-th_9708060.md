# Chiral Symmetry and Meson-Exchange Currents

> The meson-exchange current in nuclei, a long-standing problem in nuclear physics, is described in modern theory of strong interactions, namely, QCD expressed in terms of effective chiral Lagrangian field theory. Some old results are given a modern interpretation and some new results are predicted. Among the topics treated are an accurate calculation of the radiative np capture process at thermal energy, the enhanced axial charge transition in heavy nuclei, Brown-Rho (BR) scaling in dense medium induced by vacuum changes, dropping meson masses and "mended symmetry" in relativistic heavy-ion processes and the link between the physics of dilute and dense hadronic systems through a mapping of effective chiral Lagrangians to Landau Fermi-liquid theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that meson-exchange currents in nuclei, a long-standing problem in nuclear physics, can be fully described using effective chiral Lagrangian field theory derived from QCD, with the key hedge being that this description works systematically only for processes where the "chiral filter" suppresses short-distance physics, leaving long-range pion-exchange as the dominant contribution.

## Method

The author applies chiral perturbation theory to nuclear processes, using a systematic expansion in small momentum scale Q relative to the chiral scale Λ (~1 GeV). For the radiative np capture process, the calculation combines realistic wave functions from the Argonne v18 potential with irreducible graphs computed to next-to-next-to-leading (NNL) order in the chiral expansion. For dense matter, the approach uses Brown-Rho (BR) scaling where hadron masses and couplings scale with the quark condensate in medium, and maps the effective chiral Lagrangian to Landau Fermi-liquid theory at mean-field level.

## Key numbers

- Radiative np capture cross section: experimental value σ_exp = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb, single-particle prediction σ_imp = 305.6 mb, chiral theory prediction σ_theory = 334 ± 3 mb
- Thermal neutron-proton relative momentum: p ≃ 3.4451 × 10⁻⁵ MeV
- ¹S₀ scattering length: a_s = -23.75 fm
- Axial charge enhancement in heavy nuclei: ε_MEC = 1.8–2.0 in the lead region (Warburton analysis)
- BR scaling parameter at nuclear matter density: Φ(ρ₀) = m_V*/m_V = 0.78 ± 0.08
- Predicted nucleon effective mass at ρ₀: m_N*/m_N ≈ 0.69, consistent with QCD sum rule result 0.69⁺⁰·¹⁴₋₀·₀₇
- Orbital gyromagnetic ratio correction: δg_l = 0.23 τ₃, compared with δg_l^proton = 0.23 ± 0.03 from ²⁰⁹Bi dipole sum rule
- Predicted axial coupling constant in medium: g_A*(ρ₀) ≈ 1
- Nuclear matter properties from BR-scaled mean-field model: binding energy B = 16.0 MeV, Fermi momentum k_F = 257 MeV, compression modulus K = 296 MeV, effective mass m_N* = 0.62 m_N

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange current program initiated by Austern (1953) and Riska and Brown (1974) by placing it on the modern foundation of effective chiral Lagrangians. It connects the "chiral filter" mechanism formulated by Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho to Brown-Rho scaling in dense matter, and establishes a mapping to Landau Fermi-liquid theory as developed by Migdal. The approach enables predictions for dense matter phenomena including kaon condensation at ρ ~ 3ρ₀ in compact stars and explains the CERES dilepton data through dropping meson masses in hot dense matter.
