# Absence of decoherence in the complex potential approach to nuclear scattering

> Time-dependent density-matrix propagation is used to demonstrate, in a schematic model of an open quantum system, that the complex potential approach and the Lindblad dissipative dynamics are \emph{not} equivalent. While the former preserves coherence, it is destroyed in the Lindblad dissipative dynamics. Quantum decoherence is the key aspect that makes the difference between the two approaches, indicating that the complex potential model is inadequate for a consistent description of open quantum-system dynamics. It is suggested that quantum decoherence should always be explicitly included when modelling low-energy nuclear collision dynamics within a truncated model space of reaction channels.

## Key claim
Using a schematic model of an open quantum system, the complex potential approach and the Lindblad dissipative dynamics are shown to be *not* equivalent: the complex potential approach preserves coherence, while the Lindblad dynamics destroys it, meaning the complex potential model is inadequate for a consistent description of open quantum-system dynamics.

## Method
Time-dependent density-matrix propagation was performed for a wave-packet scattering off an Eckart potential barrier, comparing two descriptions: (i) a complex potential approach using an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian, and (ii) the Lindblad master equation with a spontaneous emission Lindblad operator. The density-matrix was represented in a grid-basis and propagated using the Faber polynomial expansion of the time-evolution operator and the Fourier method for the kinetic energy commutator.

## Key numbers
- The Eckart potential parameters: \(V_0 = 61.1\) MeV, \(a_0 = 1.2\) fm; the Gaussian absorption potential parameters: \(W_0 = 5\) MeV, \(\sigma_{0w} = 1.5\) fm.
- The wave-packet described relative motion of two objects with mass numbers \(A_1 = 16\) and \(A_2 = 144\), initially centered at \(x_0 = 25\) fm with width \(\sigma_0 = 5\) fm, and initial total energy \(E_0 = 55\) MeV.
- The time step for density-matrix propagation was \(\Delta t = 10^{-22}\) s; typically 130 time steps were required for the wave-packet centroid to reach 25 fm.
- Decoherence changes the quantum tunnelling probability by up to a factor of ten at the lowest incident energies.

## Context
This work extends the debate on open quantum system descriptions in nuclear physics, directly challenging the common view that the complex potential approach (Wigner, Feshbach, Volya) is equivalent to Lindblad dissipative dynamics (Lindblad, Sandulescu). The results suggest that quantum decoherence should be explicitly included in models of low-energy nuclear collision dynamics, potentially resolving inconsistencies in coherent coupled-channels calculations (Balantekin) that fail to simultaneously describe elastic scattering, quasi-elastic scattering, and fusion (Newton, Nanda). This has implications for continuum shell models (Volya) and approaches to spectroscopic factor quenching (Barbieri, Timofeyuk).
