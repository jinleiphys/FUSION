# Pseudospin and spin symmetry in the relativistic generalized Woods-Saxon potential including Coulomb-like tensor potential

> The Dirac Equation is solved approximately for relativistic generalized Woods-Saxon potential including Coulomb-like tensor potential in exact pseudospin and spin symmetry limits. The bound states energy eigenvalues are found by using wavefunction boundary conditions, and corresponding radial wavefunctions are obtained in terms of hypergeometric function. Some numerical examples are given for the dependence of bound states energy eigenvalues on quantum numbers and potential parameters.

## Key claim

The Dirac equation for the generalized Woods-Saxon potential including a Coulomb-like tensor potential can be solved approximately under exact pseudospin and spin symmetry limits, but only by using wavefunction boundary conditions rather than the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, which the authors argue is incorrect because it does not account for wavefunction behavior near the nuclear surface radius \(r=R\).

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation with a generalized Woods-Saxon potential (Woods-Saxon plus Woods-Saxon surface term) and a Coulomb-like tensor potential \(U(r)=-H/r\) under the conditions of exact pseudospin symmetry (\(\Sigma(r)=C_{ps}=\) constant) and exact spin symmetry (\(\Delta(r)=C_s=\) constant). They applied the Pekeris approximation to handle the centrifugal/pseudospin-orbit coupling term, transformed the equation into a hypergeometric-type equation using the variable \(z=(1+e^{(r-R)/a})^{-1}\), and imposed boundary conditions at \(r\rightarrow\infty\) and \(r\rightarrow 0\) to derive energy eigenvalue equations and radial wavefunctions in terms of hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers

* Pseudospin symmetry binding energies (\(\tilde{E}=\mathcal{E}-M\)) for the \(1s_{1/2}\) state: \(-42.7642\) MeV (with \(H=0\)) and \(-40.3580\) MeV (with \(H=-1.5\)), using \(M=939.5654\) MeV/\(c^2\), \(a=0.65\) fm, \(R=7\) fm, \(\Delta_0=750\) MeV, \(\Delta_1=50\) MeV, \(C_{ps}=-50\) MeV, and \(\hbar c=197.3269\) MeV fm.
* Pseudospin symmetry binding energy for the \(0p_{1/2}\) state: \(-42.8961\) MeV (\(H=0\)) and \(-42.8335\) MeV (\(H=-1.5\)).
* Spin symmetry binding energies (\(E=\mathcal{E}+M\)) for the \(0s_{1/2}\) state: \(49.1470\) MeV (\(H=0\)) and \(47.7877\) MeV (\(H=-1.5\)), using \(\Sigma_0=-750\) MeV, \(\Sigma_1=-50\) MeV, \(C_s=50\) MeV, with the same \(M\), \(a\), \(R\), and \(\hbar c\).
* Spin symmetry binding energy for the \(0p_{3/2}\) state: \(48.5261\) MeV (\(H=0\)) and \(45.6458\) MeV (\(H=-1.5\)); for the \(0p_{1/2}\) state: \(48.5261\) MeV (\(H=0\)) and \(49.1711\) MeV (\(H=-1.5\)).

## Context

This work extends prior analytical solutions of the Dirac equation for the Woods-Saxon potential by Candemir (2014) and Guo (2005), who solved the \(s\)-wave case and noted that the Nikiforov-Uvarov method yields incorrect energy eigenvalues because it neglects wavefunction behavior at \(r=R\). The authors also build on studies of tensor coupling under pseudospin and spin symmetry by Akcay (2009), Alberto (2005), and Lisboa (2004), who found that tensor interactions remove degeneracy in pseudospin and spin doublets. The results enable further investigation of bound states in nuclei using generalized Woods-Saxon potentials with surface and tensor terms, and the derived energy eigenvalue equations reduce to the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation form of Pahlavani (2012) when the spin-orbit parameter is set to zero.
