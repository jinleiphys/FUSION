# Effects of Parton Intrinsic Transverse Momentum on Photon Production in Hard-Scattering Processes

> We calculate the photon production cross section arising from the hard scattering of partons in nucleon-nucleon collisions by taking into account the intrinsic parton transverse momentum distribution and the next-to-leading-order contributions. As first pointed out by Owens, the inclusion of the intrinsic transverse momentum distribution of partons leads to an enhancement of photon production cross section in the region of photon transverse momenta of a few GeV/c for nucleon-nucleon collisions at a center-of-mass energy of a few tens of GeV. The enhancement increases as $\sqrt{s}$ decreases. Such an enhancement is an important consideration in the region of photon momenta under investigation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of parton intrinsic transverse momentum leads to an enhancement of the photon production cross section in nucleon-nucleon collisions for photon transverse momenta of a few GeV/c at center-of-mass energies of a few tens of GeV, with the enhancement increasing as \(\sqrt{s}\) decreases; this effect is important for interpreting direct photon measurements in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon production cross section from hard parton scattering in nucleon-nucleon collisions by convolving parton distribution functions (with factorized Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distributions) with elementary cross sections for the Compton process (\(gq \rightarrow \gamma q\)) and annihilation process (\(q\bar{q} \rightarrow \gamma g\)). They perform leading-order (LO) calculations with intrinsic transverse momentum and separately compute next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections using the code of Aurenche et al. (1987, 1988), then combine the two effects via a multiplicative \(K\)-factor approximation, assuming the \(K\)-factor is approximately independent of parton transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The standard deviation \(\sigma\) of the parton intrinsic transverse momentum distribution is estimated as \(\sigma \sim 0.4\) GeV/c, corresponding to a confinement radius of 0.5 fm, giving a width \(\sqrt{2} \times 0.4\) GeV/c = 0.65 GeV/c.
- For \(p_{\gamma T} = 6\) GeV/c at \(\sqrt{s}=20\) GeV, the analytical enhancement factors are \(\kappa_1 = 2.2\) and \(\kappa_2 = 1.27\), yielding a combined enhancement \(\kappa = 2.8\), in rough agreement with the numerical enhancement factor of about 3.8.
- For \(p_{\gamma T} = 6\) GeV/c at \(\sqrt{s}=63\) GeV, the enhancement factor \(\kappa\) decreases to 1.07.
- The \(K\)-factor from NLO corrections is about 2 to 3.5 and is a slowly varying function of photon transverse momentum.
- The authors use a Gaussian distribution for low \(p_T\) with \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle = 0.9\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) following Owens (1987).

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Owens (1987) that parton intrinsic transverse momentum enhances photon production cross sections in hadron-nucleon collisions at energies 19.4 GeV \(< \sqrt{s} < 63\) GeV, providing both analytical and numerical support. It addresses discrepancies between NLO calculations by Cleymans et al. (1995) and experimental data from UA6 at \(\sqrt{s}=24.3\) GeV (lower by a factor of about 2) and E706 at \(\sqrt{s}=30.6\) GeV (lower by a factor of about 1.7), which the authors attribute to the neglect of intrinsic transverse momentum. The results enable more accurate subtraction of hard-scattering photon backgrounds in heavy-ion collisions at energies around \(\sqrt{s} \sim 20\) GeV, relevant for WA80 and WA98 experiments searching for quark-gluon plasma signals.
