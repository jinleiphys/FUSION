# The Ideal Liquid Discovered by RHIC, Infrared Slavery Above and Hadronic Freedom Below $T_c$

> We construct the nature of the matter found in RHIC when its temperature has dropped down close to, and below, $T_c$. Just above $T_c$ it is composed of extremely strongly bound quark-antiquark pairs forming chirally restored mesons of the quantum numbers of the $π, S, ρ$ and $a_1$ with very small size and zero energy and just below $T_c$, it is composed of mesons of the same quantum numbers with zero mass. We invoke infrared slavery for the former and the vector manifestation (VM) of hidden local symmetry for the latter. As the temperature drops below $T_c$, the strongly bound quark-antiquark pairs are ejected into what is basically a region of "hadronic freedom" in which the interactions are zero. Experimental evidences for this are seen in the STAR data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the matter produced at RHIC, when the temperature drops close to and below the critical temperature \(T_c\), is not a gas of free quarks and gluons but rather a system of strongly bound, chirally restored mesons (the \(\pi, S, \rho\) and \(a_1\)) just above \(T_c\), which transition into non-interacting, massless mesons just below \(T_c\). The authors claim that the 32 degrees of freedom from these mesons precisely match the entropy calculated in lattice gauge simulations, resolving a long-standing puzzle, but this picture relies on the specific interpretation of lattice data and the theoretical frameworks of infrared slavery and the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct a theoretical model combining the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the vector manifestation (VM) of hidden local symmetry (HLS) for the region below \(T_c\), and a strong-coupling Coulomb potential derived from lattice gauge simulations (LGS) for the region above \(T_c\). They use the Bielefeld LGS results for the gluon condensate and the running coupling \(\alpha_s(T)\) from Polyakov loop evolution, and apply a relativistic two-body equation to show that the color Coulomb potential, with a doubled interaction strength from Ampère's law, binds quark-antiquark pairs into massless mesons just above \(T_c\).

## Key numbers

*   The NJL model cutoff used is \(\Lambda = 660\) MeV, with a coupling \(G\Lambda^2 = 4.3\), giving a critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 170\) MeV.
*   The soft glue condensate at \(T=0\) is \(B(\text{glue}) = 0.012\) GeV\(^4\), and it drops to \(G^2(T) \sim 0.005\) GeV\(^4\) at \(T_c\) (unquenched).
*   The effective strong coupling constant at \(T_c\) is \(\alpha_s(T_c) = 16/3\), corresponding to \(g = 8\), due to the inclusion of the Casimir factor and the Ampère's law interaction.
*   The rms radius of the chirally restored meson molecule just above \(T_c\) is 0.2 fm.
*   The width of the \(\rho\) meson at chiral restoration scales as \(\Gamma_\rho^\star(T_c) / \Gamma_\rho \sim (\bar m_\rho / m_\rho)^5\), making it completely negligible.

## Context

This work extends the earlier scenario of Brown, Jackson, Bethe and Pizzochero (1993), who argued that mesons are the correct degrees of freedom up to well beyond \(T_c\). It also builds on the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry by Harada and Yamawaki, which predicts that hadron masses and interactions go to zero as \(T\) approaches \(T_c\) from below (Brown-Rho scaling). The paper uses lattice gauge simulation data from the Bielefeld group (Kaczmarek et al., 2004) and the gluon condensate results of Miller (2000) to support its claims. It enables a new understanding of the RHIC data as showing a continuous transition from a strongly interacting "infrared slavery" regime above \(T_c\) to a non-interacting "hadronic freedom" regime below \(T_c\), rather than a simple quark-gluon plasma.
