# Analysis of (K^-,K^+) inclusive spectrum with semiclassical distorted wave model

> The inclusive K^+ momentum spectrum in the 12C(K^-,K^+) reaction is calculated by the semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model, including the transition to the Ξ^- bound state. The calculated spectra with the strength of the Ξ^--nucleus potential -50, -20, and +10 MeV are compared with the experimental data measured at KEK with p_{K^-}=1.65 GeV/c. The shape of the spectrum is reproduced by the calculation. Though the inclusive spectrum changes systematically depending on the potential strength, it is not possible to obtain a constraint on the potential from the present data. The calculated spectrum is found to have strong emission-angle dependence. We also investigate the incident K^- momentum dependence of the spectrum to see the effect of the Fermi motion of the target nucleons which is explicitly treated in the SCDW method.

## Key claim

The semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model reproduces the shape of the inclusive K⁺ momentum spectrum from the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺) reaction, but the calculated absolute cross section is underestimated by about a factor of 2, and the present data cannot constrain the Ξ⁻-nucleus potential strength despite systematic spectral changes with potential depth.

## Method

The SCDW model was applied to calculate the inclusive K⁺ momentum spectrum for the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺) reaction at an incident K⁻ momentum of 1.65 GeV/c, including transitions to both continuum and bound Ξ⁻ states. The calculation used a Woods-Saxon potential for the Ξ⁻-nucleus interaction with depths of -50, -20, and +10 MeV, employed tρ-approximated distorted waves for the kaons, and incorporated the elementary K⁻+p→K⁺+Ξ⁻ cross section with full angular and energy dependence from a parameterization by Nara et al. The Fermi motion of target nucleons was explicitly treated, and the calculated spectra were averaged over emission angles between 1.7° and 13.6° for comparison with KEK experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Incident K⁻ momentum: p_K⁻ = 1.65 GeV/c (KEK experiment)
- Ξ⁻-nucleus potential depths tested: V_Ξ = -50, -20, and +10 MeV
- For V_Ξ = -20 MeV: Ξ⁻ bound state energies in ¹¹B are -8.3 MeV (0s-state) and -0.6 MeV (0p-state)
- For V_Ξ = -50 MeV: Ξ⁻ bound state energies are -28.7 MeV (0s-state), -13.0 MeV (0p-state), and -2.6 MeV (1s-state)
- Calculated spectra averaged over θ_K⁺ = 1.7° to 13.6°
- Lorentzian convolution half-width Γ/2 = 2 MeV used for bound-state region (1 MeV from Ξ⁻p→ΛΛ decay plus 1 MeV detector resolution)
- Calculated total cross sections for K⁺ and K⁻ on ¹²C using tρ potentials agree with those from optical potentials Û^± within about 10%

## Context

This work extends the SCDW model previously applied to (π⁻,K⁺) Σ formation reactions by Kohno et al. and to (p,p'x) and (p,nx) inclusive reactions by Luo, Watanabe, and Ogata. The analysis follows earlier DWIA studies by Fukuda and Khaustov that suggested a Ξ-nucleus well depth of about 14 MeV, but with limited statistics. The results enable future J-PARC experiments to use the predicted strong emission-angle dependence and bound-state peak structures to extract the Ξ⁻-nucleus potential, which is crucial for understanding the strangeness S=-2 sector of baryon interactions.
