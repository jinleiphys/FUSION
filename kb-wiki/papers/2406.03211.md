# Study of hybrid stars with nonstrange quark matter cores

> In this work, under the hypothesis that quark matter may not be strange (Holdom et al. 2018), we adopt a modification of the coupling constant of the four-quark scalar interaction $G\rightarrow G_1+G_2\langle\barψψ\rangle$ in the 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to study nonstrange hybrid stars, where $G_1$ and $G_2$ are two parameters constrained by using the lattice QCD simulation results at the critical temperature and zero chemical potential. The Maxwell construction is used to describe the first-order confinement-deconfinement phase transition in hybrid stars. With recent measurements on neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability, the hybrid equation of states are constrained. It is found that pure nonstrange quark matter cores can exist in hybrid stars, possessing $0.026-0.04$ solar mass. The maximum hybrid star mass in the framework of the modified NJL model is about 0.1 solar mass lighter than that in the conventional 2-flavor NJL model. It is argued that the binary neutron stars in GW170817 should be hadron stars.

## Key claim

Under the hypothesis that quark matter may not be strange (Holdom et al. 2018), pure nonstrange quark matter cores can exist in hybrid stars, but only with small masses of 0.026-0.04 solar masses, and the binary neutron stars in GW170817 should be hadron stars rather than hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors used the Maxwell construction to describe a first-order confinement-deconfinement phase transition in hybrid stars, combining the APR hadronic equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall 1998) with a modified 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model where the four-quark scalar coupling constant is replaced by G1 + G2 times the quark condensate. The parameters G1 and G2 were constrained using lattice QCD simulation results at the critical temperature and zero chemical potential, and the resulting hybrid equations of state were further constrained by recent measurements of neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Pure nonstrange quark matter cores in hybrid stars possess 0.026-0.04 solar masses
- The maximum hybrid star mass in the modified NJL model is about 0.1 solar mass lighter than in the conventional 2-flavor NJL model
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral phase transition at zero chemical potential is constrained to Tpc = 173 +/- 8 MeV from lattice QCD simulations in the 2-flavor case
- The bag constant B^(1/4) in the modified NJL model ranges from 120.9 to 125.4 MeV for the constrained parameter sets
- The critical baryon chemical potential for the hadron-quark phase transition is around 1.5 GeV in the modified NJL model and around 1.6 GeV in the normal NJL model
- The most massive hybrid stars for parameter sets with m = 5.8, 7.5, 9.5 MeV reach about 1.892 solar masses, not satisfying the 2 solar mass constraint
- The critical point (Ppt, epsilon_pt) for the representative hybrid EOS with m = 7.5 MeV and G1 = 4.552 GeV^-2 is (299.9 MeV fm^-3, 856.5 MeV fm^-3)

## Context

This work extends studies of hybrid stars using NJL-type models (Ayriyan et al. 2021, Alvarez-Castillo et al. 2016, PhysRevC.105.035802) by incorporating the hypothesis that quark matter may not be strange (Holdom et al. 2018) and by modifying the four-quark coupling constant to depend on the quark condensate. The results contradict earlier claims that 2 solar mass neutron stars rule out soft equations of state and quark matter in massive neutron stars (Ozel 2006, PhysRevLett.117.032501), and instead support the argument from PhysRevLett.122.061102 and Miao et al. (2020) that GW170817 supports a first-order hadron-quark phase transition at supranuclear densities. The findings enable future Bayesian analyses to further constrain the parameter space of the modified NJL model and the current quark mass.
