# Three-dimensional QCD phase diagram in the pNJL model

> Based on the three-flavor Polyakov-looped Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio (pNJL) model, we have studied the structure of the three-dimensional QCD phase diagram with respect to the temperature, the baryon chemical potential, and the isospin chemical potential, by investigating the interplay among the chiral quark condensate, the pion condensate, and the Polyakov loop. While the pNJL model leads to qualitatively similar structure of the normal quark phase, the pion superfluid phase, and the Sarma phase as well as their phase boundaries, when compared to the NJL model, the inclusion of the Polyakov loop enlarges considerably the areas of the pion superfluid phase and the Sarma phase, and leads to critical end points at higher temperatures. With the contribution of the gluon dynamics effectively included, the present study is expected to give a more reliable prediction of the three-dimensional QCD phase diagram compared to that in the NJL model.

# Three-dimensional QCD phase diagram in the pNJL model

## Key claim

The inclusion of the Polyakov loop in the three-flavor NJL model (producing the pNJL model) enlarges the areas of the pion superfluid phase and the Sarma phase in the three-dimensional QCD phase diagram and leads to critical end points at higher temperatures compared to the NJL model, while the qualitative phase structure remains similar. The authors claim this gives a "more reliable prediction" because gluon dynamics are effectively included, but note that the Polyakov-loop potential parameters are fixed by pure-gluodynamics lattice data and the simultaneous crossover condition, not by direct QCD data at finite density.

## Method

The authors start from the three-flavor pNJL Lagrangian with scalar-isoscalar, vector-isoscalar, scalar-isovector, vector-isovector, and Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) interactions, plus a temperature-dependent Polyakov-loop effective potential. They apply the mean-field approximation, compute the thermodynamic potential from the quark propagator eigenvalues, and solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensates, the pion condensate, the net-quark densities, and the Polyakov loop fields by minimizing the grand potential. The phase boundaries are mapped by tracking the order parameters as functions of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential, and compared with results from the NJL model (Polyakov-loop potential turned off and covariant derivative reduced to partial derivative).

## Key numbers

- The scalar-isovector coupling constant is set to \(G_\mathrm{IS} = -0.002 G_\mathrm{S}\) and the vector-isovector coupling constant to \(G_\mathrm{IV} = 0.25 G_\mathrm{S}\), determined by fitting the physical pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 140.9\) MeV and the reduced isospin density from lattice QCD calculations at zero temperature.
- The Polyakov-loop potential parameters are \(a = 664\) MeV and \(b = 0.028 \Lambda^3\) (with \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV/c), fixed so that the first-order phase transition in pure gluodynamics occurs at \(T = 270\) MeV and the simultaneous crossover of chiral restoration and deconfinement occurs around \(T \approx 212\) MeV.
- The momentum cutoff in the integrals is \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV/c; current quark masses are \(m_u = m_d = 3.6\) MeV and \(m_s = 87\) MeV; scalar-isoscalar coupling \(G_\mathrm{S}\Lambda^2 = 3.6\); KMT coupling \(K\Lambda^5 = 8.9\).
- The pion condensate appears around \(\mu_\mathrm{I} \sim m_\pi\) and disappears at very large \(\mu_\mathrm{I}\) or high temperatures; the CEP temperature increases with \(\mu_\mathrm{I}\) from 200 MeV to 400 MeV and saturates above \(\mu_\mathrm{I} = 400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-dimensional QCD phase diagram study using the three-flavor NJL model (Liu, 2021) by incorporating the Polyakov loop to account for gluon dynamics and deconfinement effects. The pNJL model was originally developed by Fukushima (2003, 2008) and Ratti (2005) to overcome the NJL model's inability to describe deconfinement. The present results show that the pNJL model gives larger pion superfluid and Sarma phase regions and higher CEP temperatures than the NJL model, bringing the predictions closer to those from the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach and the functional renormalization group method. The study enables future extensions to include kaon condensates, color superconductivity, chiral imbalance, or electromagnetic fields, and suggests that the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme may improve the behavior at large chemical potentials.
