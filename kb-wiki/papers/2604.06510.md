# Distribution amplitudes and functions of ground-state scalar and pseudoscalar charmonia

> Charmonia are often supposed to provide simple hydrogen-like ``atomic'' systems that can be used to obtain insights into heavier-quark QCD. We use continuum Schwinger function methods to analyse this hypothesis in connection with ground-state scalar and pseudoscalar charmonia and find that a more complex picture of these states may be necessary. For instance, considering orbital angular momentum, the $χ_{c0}$ is not a simple $P$-wave system; similarly, the $η_c$ wave function contains more than merely $S$-wave contributions. The distribution amplitudes (DAs) and distribution functions (DFs) of these mesons are also nontrivial. For instance, the $χ_{c0}$ DA is not positive definite: owing to QCD symmetries, it possesses domains of balanced negative and positive support. This feature is also expressed in the $χ_{c0}$ DF, but differences between $χ_{c0}$ and $η_c$ DFs diminish under scale evolution. Notably, the light-front momentum fraction carried by glue is the same in both states: it is 10\% less than the in-pion glue momentum fraction. Whilst experimental confirmation of the predictions herein is unlikely, our results should serve as benchmarks for complementary theory attempts to understand local and global structural features of heavier-quark hadrons.

## Key claim

Using continuum Schwinger function methods, the authors find that ground-state scalar and pseudoscalar charmonia are not simple hydrogen-like systems: the χc0 is not a simple P-wave state and the ηc wave function contains more than merely S-wave contributions, contradicting the common nonrelativistic quark model picture even for charm quarks.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for c-quark propagators and meson bound-state wave functions using rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, with a specific effective charge parameterization (ω = 0.8 GeV, ςQ = 0.6 GeV) and a renormalization-group-invariant c current mass m̂c = 1.61 GeV. From the Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, they extracted rest-frame orbital angular momentum decompositions via projection operators, and computed leading-twist distribution amplitudes (DAs) and distribution functions (DFs) at the hadron scale ζH, subsequently evolving DF results to higher scales using the all-orders (AO) scheme.

## Key numbers

- χc0 mass: 3.33 GeV (PDG value 3.41 GeV); ηc mass: 2.98 GeV (PDG value 2.98 GeV)
- ηc decay constant: 0.28 GeV (PDG extracted value 0.24 GeV)
- χc0 DA reconstruction parameter α = 7.79; ηc DA reconstruction parameter α = 2.20
- χc0 DA Gegenbauer coefficients: a1 = 3.279, a3 = -2.921, a5 = 1.802, a7 = -0.803, a9 = 0.254, a11 = -0.054, a13 = 0.0068, a15 ≈ 0
- ηc DA Gegenbauer coefficients: a0 = 1, a2 = -0.189, a4 = 0.0635, a6 = -0.0008, a8 through a14 ≈ 0
- Light-front momentum fraction carried by glue is the same in both χc0 and ηc: 10% less than the in-pion glue momentum fraction
- χc0 rest-frame OAM decomposition: S⊗S = 142%, S⊗P = -172%, P⊗P = 130%
- ηc rest-frame OAM decomposition: S⊗S = 141%, S⊗P = -92%, P⊗P = 51%

## Context

This work extends prior continuum Schwinger function method studies of charmonia by Hilger (2014), Fischer (2014), Yin (2021), Raya (2016), and Chen (2016), delivering for the first time the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions of both ground-state scalar and pseudoscalar charmonia and their rest-frame OAM content. The results contradict the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) picture formalized by Brambilla (1999) and challenge the common assumption that charmonia provide simple hydrogen-like atomic systems for studying heavier-quark QCD. The predictions serve as benchmarks for complementary theoretical approaches to understand structural features of heavier-quark hadrons, though experimental confirmation is unlikely.
