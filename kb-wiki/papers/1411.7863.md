# Wilson coefficients and four-quark condensates in QCD sum rules for medium modifications of $D$ mesons

> Wilson coefficients of light four-quark condensates in QCD sum rules are evaluated for pseudo-scalar $D$ mesons, thus, pushing the sum rules toward mass dimension six. Contrary to the situation for $\bar{q}q$ mesons the impact of the four-quark condensates for vacuum as well as in-medium situations is found to be rather small within the Borel window used in previous analyses. The complete four-quark condensate contributions enable to identify candidates for an order parameter of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking/restoration as well as to evaluate stability criteria of operator product expansions.

## Key claim

The Wilson coefficients of light four-quark condensates in QCD sum rules for pseudo-scalar $D$ mesons are evaluated for the first time, pushing the operator product expansion to mass dimension six; contrary to the situation for light $\bar{q}q$ mesons like the $\rho$, the impact of these four-quark condensates on the sum rule is found to be rather small within the Borel window used in previous analyses, both in vacuum and in-medium.

## Method

The authors compute the current-current correlator for the pseudo-scalar $D$ meson current using the background field method in Fock-Schwinger gauge, evaluating the operator product expansion up to mass dimension six at leading order in the strong coupling $\alpha_s^0$ for soft-gluon contributions. They derive the Wilson coefficients for all light four-quark condensates entering the in-medium sum rule, including both chirally even and chirally odd structures, and then estimate the numerical impact using the factorization hypothesis (ground state saturation) to relate four-quark condensates to products of two-quark condensates.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum four-quark condensate contribution to the Borel-transformed correlator is $\widehat{\Pi}_\text{4q}^\text{vac} (M) = - \frac{16\pi}{27} \frac{e^{-m_Q^2/M^2}}{M^2} \bigg\{ 1 - \frac{1}{4} \frac{m_Q^2}{M^2} - \frac{1}{12}\frac{m_Q^ 4}{M^4} \bigg\} \alpha_\text{s} \kappa_1 \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_0^2$, recovering the result of Aliev and Eletsky.
- For the $\rho$ meson, using $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = (-\unit{0.245}{GeV})^3$, $\langle (\alpha_\text{s}/\pi) G^2 \rangle = \unit{0.012}{GeV^4}$, and $\alpha_\text{s}=0.35$, the numerical OPE at $M\sim\unit{1}{GeV}$ shows the four-quark condensate term contributes $-0.026\,\kappa\, \frac{\text{GeV}^{\,6}}{M^6}$ compared to the gluon condensate term $+0.039\, \frac{\text{GeV}^{\,4}}{M^4}$.
- The chiral condensate in vacuum is $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle_{0} \approx (\unit{-245}{MeV})^{3}$.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of $D$ mesons by Hilger (2009) and Zschocke (2011) which included condensates only up to mass dimension five, and provides the first complete evaluation of light four-quark condensate contributions for open charm mesons in medium. The results enable identification of the chirally odd four-quark condensate $\langle : O_9 : \rangle$ as a candidate order parameter for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking/restoration, and allow evaluation of stability criteria for the operator product expansion. The numerical estimates using factorization provide a basis for future studies of $D$ meson spectral modifications in nuclear matter relevant for experiments at FAIR (CBM and PANDA collaborations).
