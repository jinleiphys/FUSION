# Ab Initio Many-Body Calculations of n-3H, n-4He, p-{3,4}He, and n-10Be Scattering

> We develop a new ab initio many-body approach capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei, by combining the resonating-group method with the use of realistic interactions, and a microscopic and consistent description of the nucleon clusters. This approach preserves translational symmetry and Pauli principle. We present phase shifts for neutron scattering on 3H, 4He and 10Be and proton scattering on {3,4}He, using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. Our A=4 scattering results are compared to earlier ab initio calculations. We demonstrate that a proper treatment of the coupling to the n-10Be continuum is essential to explain the parity-inverted ground state in 11Be.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new ab initio many-body approach (NCSM/RGM) that can describe both bound and scattering states in light nuclei simultaneously, and demonstrate that a proper treatment of the coupling to the n-10Be continuum is essential to explain the parity-inverted ground state in 11Be, a result that previous ab initio calculations had failed to achieve.

## Method

The authors combine the resonating-group method (RGM) with the no-core shell model (NCSM) to construct a microscopic theory that treats bound and scattering states of light nuclei starting from realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. They expand the scattering wave function over binary-cluster channel states, where the cluster eigenstates are obtained by diagonalizing the intrinsic Hamiltonians in the NCSM harmonic oscillator basis. The resulting coupled-channel equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, with calculations performed for neutron scattering on 3H, 4He, and 10Be, and proton scattering on 3He and 4He.

- 3H ground state energy: -8.16 MeV at Nmax=19 with Vlowk NN potential at hbarOmega=18 MeV
- 4He ground state energy: -27.77 MeV at Nmax=17 with Vlowk NN potential at hbarOmega=18 MeV
- n-3H total cross section at Ekin=0.75 MeV: 1.44 barns at Nmax=19 with Vlowk NN potential
- n-4He total cross section at Ekin=5.0 MeV: 1.97 barns at Nmax=17 with Vlowk NN potential
- n-4He 2P3/2 phase shift at Ekin=5.0 MeV: 84.8 degrees at Nmax=17 with Vlowk NN potential
- p-4He 2P3/2 phase shift at Ekin=5.0 MeV: 79.9 degrees at Nmax=17 with Vlowk NN potential
- Less than 2 degrees absolute difference between largest and next-to-largest model spaces for N3LO NN potential phase shifts, except for n-alpha 2P3/2 where difference rises to 5 degrees in the range 1 MeV < Ekin < 4 MeV
- For n-3H and p-3He with N3LO NN potential, differences up to 17 degrees (3P2) at Ekin=2.6 MeV compared to AGS calculations
- 11Be 1/2- state energy: -57.59 MeV from NCSM/RGM with CD-Bonn NN potential at hbarOmega=13 MeV
- 11Be 1/2+ state energy: -57.85 MeV from NCSM/RGM with CD-Bonn NN potential at hbarOmega=13 MeV (parity inverted ground state)
- 10Be ground state energy: -57.06 MeV from NCSM at Nmax=8/9 with CD-Bonn NN potential

## Context

This work extends ab initio scattering calculations beyond the four-nucleon systems previously accessible, building on the nearly-total lack of ab initio calculations for scattering processes involving more than four nucleons overall (GFMC_nHe4, 2004). The approach combines the resonating-group method (RGM) with the no-core shell model (NCSM) into a new microscopic theory (NCSM/RGM). The A=4 scattering results are compared to earlier ab initio calculations using the Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) equations by Deltuva (2007), showing reasonable agreement at low energies but discrepancies increasing with energy due to omitted closed channels. The parity inversion in 11Be, which previous NCSM calculations by the same group (2007) could not explain, is successfully reproduced when the n-10Be continuum coupling is properly treated, demonstrating the importance of including scattering configurations for weakly-bound halo nuclei.
