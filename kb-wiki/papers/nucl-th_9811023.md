# Mean--Field and Nonlinear Dynamics in Many--Body Quantum Systems

> In this paper we discuss in detail the nonlinear equations of the mean--field approximation and their connection to the exact many--body Schrödinger equation. Then we analyze the mean--field approach and the nonlinear dynamics of a trapped condensate of weakly--interacting bosons.

# Mean–Field and Nonlinear Dynamics in Many–Body Quantum Systems

## Key claim

The paper argues that chaotic dynamics in quantum many–body systems is possible only as a transient phenomenon with a finite lifetime (the Heisenberg time), but that this transient can be long enough to be experimentally observable in systems with many particles; the mean–field approximation, which yields nonlinear equations, can be used to estimate this transient chaotic behaviour.

## Method

The authors derive the time-dependent Hartree–Fock equations from the quantum variational principle applied to the Dirac action, showing that the resulting one-body operator is nonlinear. They then apply this mean–field framework to a trapped Bose–Einstein condensate of weakly-interacting bosons, reducing the Hartree–Fock equations to the Gross–Pitaevskii equation. Using a Gaussian trial wavefunction and a hydrodynamical approach, they obtain three coupled ordinary differential equations for the condensate widths, whose Hamiltonian is shown to be nonintegrable for nonzero coupling.

## Key numbers

- Heisenberg time scaling: \( t_H \sim \hbar^{1-N} \), where \( N \) is the number of degrees of freedom.
- Bose–Einstein condensation occurs at temperatures about \( 10^{-7} \) Kelvin.
- The coupling constant for the condensate is defined as \( \tilde{g} = (2/\pi)^{1/2} (N-1) a_s / a_0 \), where \( a_s \) is the scattering length and \( a_0 = (\hbar / m \omega_0)^{1/2} \) is the harmonic oscillator length.
- The eigenfrequencies \( \omega \) (in units of \( \omega_0 \)) for collective excitations satisfy: \( \omega^6 - 3(\lambda_1^2 + \lambda_2^2 + \lambda_3^2) \omega^4 + 8(\lambda_1^2 \lambda_2^2 + \lambda_1^2 \lambda_3^2 + \lambda_2^2 \lambda_3^2) \omega^2 - 20 \lambda_1^2 \lambda_2^2 \lambda_3^2 = 0 \).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of quantum chaos (or quantum chaology) as studied by Berry (1980s) and others, focusing on the mean–field approximation as a bridge between linear quantum dynamics and classical nonlinear chaos. The specific application to Bose–Einstein condensates builds on the Gross–Pitaevskii equation and the hydrodynamical approach of Dalfovo et al. (1997) for axially symmetric traps, and extends it to triaxially asymmetric traps as studied by Salasnich (1998). The paper enables future experimental investigation of transient chaotic dynamics in many–body quantum systems, particularly in trapped Bose–Einstein condensates with asymmetric potentials.
