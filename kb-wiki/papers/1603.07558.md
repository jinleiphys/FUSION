# Can we obtain a "new femtoscopy" on the basis of electromagnetic effects?

> We review our studies of spectator-induced electromagnetic (EM) effects on charged pion emission in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. These effects are found to consist in the electromagnetic {charge splitting} of pion directed flow as well as very large distortions in spectra and ratios of produced charged particles. As it emerges from our analysis, they offer sensitivity to the actual distance $d_E$ between the pion formation zone at freeze-out and the spectator matter. As a result, this gives a new possibility of studying the space-time evolution of dense and hot matter created in the course of the collision. Having established that $d_E$ traces the longitudinal evolution of the system and therefore rapidly decreases as a function of pion rapidity, we investigate the latter finding in view of pion feed-over from intermediate resonance production. As a result we obtain a first estimate of the pion decoupling time from EM effects which we compare to existing HBT data. We conclude that spectator-induced EM interactions can serve as a new tool for studying the space-time characteristics and longitudinal evolution of the system.

# Can we obtain a "new femtoscopy" on the basis of electromagnetic effects?

## Key claim
Spectator-induced electromagnetic effects on charged pions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can serve as a new, largely model-independent tool for studying the space-time evolution of hot and dense matter, provided that the distance \(d_E\) between the pion formation zone and the spectator matter can be extracted from data. The authors obtain a first estimate of the pion decoupling time from these EM effects, which they compare to existing HBT data, but emphasize that this estimate is preliminary with sizeable uncertainties.

## Method
The authors interpret charged pion ratio data from NA49 (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), positive pion directed flow data from WA98 (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), and charge-dependent pion directed flow from STAR (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV) using a simplified one-point pion emission model. In this model, charged pion propagation through the spectator electromagnetic field is computed numerically via classical relativistic equations of motion, yielding sensitivity to the "pion emission distance" \(d_E\). A Monte Carlo simulation assuming resonance dominance (e.g., \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(\rho(770)\)) is then used to interpret the rapidity dependence of \(d_E\), including the possibility of an intermediate system prior to resonance formation with a proper lifetime \(\tau\).

## Key numbers
- The proper lifetime of the intermediate system prior to resonance formation is estimated as \(\tau \approx 2.4\) fm/c for the considered range of collision energies.
- The final time of pion emission from EM effects is estimated as \(5.3 \pm 2.2\) fm/c (first and preliminary estimate).
- For comparison, the ALICE Collaboration HBT analysis gives a pion decoupling time of approximately \(\tau_f \approx 6\) fm/c for the same range of collision energies.
- The analysis uses data from Pb+Pb reactions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV (NA49 and WA98) and Au+Au reactions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV (STAR).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Rybicki et al., several works) on spectator-induced electromagnetic effects on charge-dependent pion yields and directed flow, proposing these effects as a "new femtoscopy" complementary to standard HBT interferometry. The analysis provides independent evidence for an intermediate system of hot and dense matter prior to resonance formation, with the extracted decoupling time comparable to HBT results from the ALICE Collaboration (ALICE HBT analysis, 2015). The approach enables future studies of longitudinal evolution of the system up to high rapidities, with potential improvements from new experimental programs at NA61/SHINE, STAR, and ALICE.
