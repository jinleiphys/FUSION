# Quantifying the Quark Gluon Plasma

> The study of heavy-ion collisions presents a challenge to both theoretical and experimental nuclear physics. Due to the extremely short lifetime and small size of the collision system, disentangling information provided by experimental observables and progress in physical insight requires the careful application of plausible reasoning. I apply a program of statistical methodologies, primarily Bayesian, to quantify properties of the medium in specific models, as well as compare and criticize differing models of the system. Of particular interest are estimations of the specific shear and bulk viscosities, where we find that information carried by the experimental data is still limited. In particular we find a large sensitivity to prior assumptions at high temperatures. Moreover, sensitivities to model assumptions are present at low temperatures, and this source of model uncertainty is propagated with model averaging and model mixing.

## Key claim

The paper finds that Bayesian parameter estimation of the specific shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma remains strongly limited by experimental data, with large sensitivity to prior assumptions at high temperatures and additional model uncertainties at low temperatures that are propagated through model averaging and model mixing.

## Method

The author applied a Bayesian statistical framework to a multistage hybrid model of heavy-ion collisions, combining free-streaming pre-hydrodynamic evolution, second-order viscous hydrodynamics (using the MUSIC code), particlization with viscous corrections, and hadronic afterburners (SMASH or URQMD). Principal component analysis was used for dimensionality reduction of the model emulator, and the posterior distributions for specific shear and bulk viscosities were estimated by comparing model predictions to experimental data from ALICE (Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV and Xe-Xe at 5.44 TeV) and STAR (Au-Au at 200 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Five principal components explain about 94% of the variance for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV; three principal components explain about 91% of the variance for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- Reducing experimental uncertainty by a factor of two still leaves credible intervals for specific bulk and shear viscosities "quite large at high temperatures."
- For the bulk relaxation time study, increasing the factor b_Pi from 1/14.55 to 2/14.55 produced larger mean transverse momenta and transverse energy in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV.
- Including a sigma meson with mass 475 MeV increased the pion yield by 7% compared to excluding the sigma meson (579 vs 531 charged particles per dN_ch/deta).
- The SMASH and URQMD afterburners agreed to within 5% for hadron spectra with p_T below 5 GeV in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian parameter estimation approach of Bernhard (2018, 2019) by using a different equation of state (excluding the sigma meson and using on-mass-shell resonances), allowing the free-streaming time to depend on collision energy, and including additional collision systems (Xe-Xe at 5.44 TeV) while omitting Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV. The study enables future work to treat experimental correlation lengths and magnitudes as uncertain nuisance parameters marginalized over in Bayesian inference, and to include additional observables and systems more sensitive to viscosities at high temperatures.
