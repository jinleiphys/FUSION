# Particle transfer and fusion cross-section for Super-heavy nuclei in dinuclear system

> Within the dinuclear system (DNS) conception, instead of solving Fokker-Planck Equation (FPE) analytically, the Master equation is solved numerically to calculate the fusion probability of super-heavy nuclei, so that the harmonic oscillator approximation to the potential energy of the DNS is avoided. The relative motion concerning the energy, the angular momentum, and the fragment deformation relaxations is explicitly treated to couple with the diffusion process, so that the nucleon transition probabilities, which are derived microscopically, are time-dependent. Comparing with the analytical solution of FPE, our results preserve more dynamical effects. The calculated evaporation residue cross sections for one-neutron emission channel of Pb-based reactions are basically in agreement with the known experimental data within one order of magnitude.

## Key claim

By solving the Master equation numerically instead of using the analytical Fokker-Planck Equation (FPE) solution, the authors avoid the harmonic oscillator approximation to the potential energy surface of the dinuclear system (DNS), preserving more dynamical effects; however, the calculated evaporation residue cross sections for Pb-based one-neutron emission reactions agree with experimental data only within one order of magnitude, not precisely.

## Method

The authors solved the Master equation numerically to describe nucleon transfer in the DNS, coupling the diffusion process with the relaxation of relative kinetic energy, angular momentum, and fragment deformation as time-dependent processes. The nucleon transition probabilities were derived microscopically using single-particle states and a valence space determined by the local excitation energy. The fusion probability was obtained by summing the distribution function over mass asymmetries up to the inner fusion barrier, and the survival probability was calculated using a statistical model with fission and neutron emission widths.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{70}Zn + ^{208}Pb \rightarrow ^{278}112$, about 95% of the fusion probability remains at $J=10\hbar$ and about 68% at $J=20\hbar$ relative to $J=0$.
- The survival probability at $J\sim 15\hbar$ decreases about one order of magnitude compared to central collisions.
- The relaxation times used are: $\tau_{R}\simeq 0.3 \times 10^{-21}s$ for radial kinetic energy, $\tau_{\mathcal{J}}\simeq 1.5 \times 10^{-21}s$ for angular momentum, and $\tau_{\epsilon}\simeq 4 \times 10^{-21}s$ for spheroidal deformation.
- The extracted average values for the quasi-fission barrier are $\hbar\omega^{B_{qf}} \sim 2.0 MeV$ and $\hbar\omega \sim 4.0 MeV$.
- The fusion probability including quasi-fission decreases by about four orders of magnitude as Z increases from 106 to 118 for Pb-based reactions.
- Considering quasi-fission diminishes the fusion probability by one order of magnitude for $Kr+Pb \rightarrow 118$.
- The time step interval $\Delta \tau$ was taken from 0.05 to $0.1\times10^{-22}$sec.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model of Adamian et al. (1997, 1998) by replacing the analytical FPE solution with a numerical Master equation solution, thereby avoiding the harmonic oscillator approximation that weakens shell structure effects. It also couples the nucleon transfer process with relative motion, which was not considered in the approach of Adamian et al. (2003). The calculated evaporation residue cross sections for Pb-based reactions producing compound nuclei from Z=106 to 118 enable comparison with experimental data from Hofmann et al. (1988, 2000) and provide predictions for elements 114, 116, and 118 that can be tested by future experiments.
