# Superscaling predictions for neutrino-induced charged-current charged pion production at MiniBooNE

> Superscaling approximation (SuSA) predictions to neutrino-induced charged-current charged pion production in the Δ-resonance region are explored under MiniBooNE experimental conditions. The results obtained within SuSA for the flux-averaged double-differential cross sections of the π+ production for the ν_μ+CH_2 reaction as a function of the muon kinetic energy and of the scattering angle, the cross sections averaged over the angle, the total cross section for the π+ production, as well as CC1π+ to CCQE cross section ratio are compared with the corresponding MiniBooNE experimental data. The SuSA predictions are in good agreement with data on neutrino flux average cross-sections, but a somewhat different dependence on the neutrino energy is predicted than the one resulting from the experimental analysis.

## Key claim

The SuSA predictions for flux-averaged double-differential cross sections of charged-current pion production agree well with MiniBooNE data, but the predicted dependence on neutrino energy differs somewhat from the experimental analysis, and the unfolded total cross section data show poorer agreement than the flux-averaged results.

## Method

The authors apply the superscaling approximation (SuSA) framework, which uses a phenomenological scaling function extracted from world electron-nucleus scattering data in the Δ-resonance region, to compute neutrino-induced charged-current π⁺ production on a CH₂ target. They evaluate the nuclear response using a generalized Rosenbluth decomposition with vector and axial form factors, employing two parameterizations (PR1 and PR2) for the axial form factors, and compare the results with MiniBooNE data for flux-averaged double-differential cross sections, angle-integrated cross sections, total cross sections, and the CC1π⁺ to CCQE ratio.

## Key numbers

- The total integrated νμ flux factor for the MiniBooNE experiment is Φtot = 5.19 × 10⁻¹⁰ [νμ/cm²/POT].
- Results with the PR1 parameterization are about 5% higher than those with PR2, indicating the uncertainty from the choice of single-nucleon response.
- The SuSA predictions for the total π⁺ production cross section show poorer agreement with data than the flux-averaged cross sections; the data appear to follow a more linear dependence with neutrino energy up to 2 GeV than the theory.
- The CC1π⁺ to CCQE cross section ratio from SuSA shows too much π production strength below 1.2 GeV and too little beyond that, compared to MiniBooNE data corrected for final state interactions.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA approach, previously applied to quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (Amaro 2010, Amaro 2011), to the more challenging channel of charged-current pion production in the Δ-resonance region. The SuSA predictions incorporate nuclear effects such as final state interactions, Δ resonance properties in the nuclear medium, and both coherent and incoherent pion production, all phenomenologically extracted from electron scattering data. The results enable comparison with MiniBooNE data and highlight the need for further theoretical work on the neutrino energy dependence and the role of final state interactions, as discussed in recent publications by Martini (2012), Leitner (2010), and Benhar (2009).
