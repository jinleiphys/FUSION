# Application of the extended P+QQ force model to $N \approx Z$ fp shell nuclei

> To study collective motion, the extended pairing plus $QQ$ force model proposed recently is applied to $A$=46, 48 and 50 nuclei in the $fp$ shell region. Exact shell model calculations in the truncated model space $(f_{7/2},p_{3/2},p_{1/2})$ prove the usefulness of the interaction. The simple model with the pairing plus quadrupole pairing plus $QQ$ force and $J$-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force reproduces unexpectedly well observed binding energies, energy levels of collective (yrast) states and reduced $E2$ transition probabilities in $^{46}$Ti, $^{46}$V, $^{48}$V, $^{48}$Cr, $^{50}$Cr and $^{50}$Mn. The correspondence between theory and experiment is almost comparable to that attained by the full $fp$ shell model calculations with realistic effective interactions. Some predictions are made for energy levels and variations of $B(E2)$ in the yrast bands, in these nuclei. Characteristics of the interaction are investigated by comparing with the realistic effective interactions.

## Key claim

The extended pairing plus quadrupole (P+QQ) force model, which includes pairing, quadrupole pairing, QQ force, and a J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force, reproduces binding energies, yrast energy levels, and E2 transition probabilities in N≈Z fp-shell nuclei almost as well as full fp-shell calculations with realistic effective interactions, but only within a truncated model space (f7/2, p3/2, p1/2) and with the caveat that the interaction fails to give enough binding as N separates from Z.

## Method

Exact shell model calculations were performed in the truncated model space (f7/2, p3/2, p1/2) for A=46, 48, and 50 nuclei using the Hamiltonian composed of four forces: pairing (P0), quadrupole pairing (P2), quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ), and J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force (Vτ=0πν). The four force strengths were determined with A-dependent parameters (g0=0.48(42/A), g2=0.36(42/A)5/3, χ′=0.30(42/A)5/3, k0=2.23(42/A) in MeV) and single-particle energies were taken from the experimental spectrum of 41Ca, with no readjustment of parameters per nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 46Ti: calculated -20.17 MeV vs. experimental -20.28 MeV
- Ground-state energy of 48Cr: calculated -32.38 MeV vs. experimental -32.38 MeV (exact agreement)
- Ground-state energy of 50Mn: calculated -38.72 MeV vs. experimental -38.71 MeV
- B(E2:2→0) in 46Ti: calculated 119 e²fm⁴ vs. experimental 197±32 e²fm⁴
- B(E2:2→0) in 48Cr: calculated 217 e²fm⁴ vs. experimental 321(41) e²fm⁴
- B(E2:4→2) in 48Cr: calculated 305 e²fm⁴ vs. experimental 329(110) e²fm⁴
- B(E2:12→10) in 48Cr: calculated 130 e²fm⁴ vs. experimental 167(25) e²fm⁴
- Single-particle energies (in MeV): ε7/2=0.0, ε3/2=1.94, ε1/2=3.61

## Context

This work extends the pairing plus quadrupole force model approach of Kisslinger, Baranger, and Bohr by adding a J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force, building on the authors' previous single-j shell calculations (Hasegawa, 1999). The results are compared directly with full fp-shell model calculations using the KB3 interaction by Caurier et al. (1995) and the FPD6 interaction by Richter et al. (1991), showing nearly comparable agreement for N≈Z nuclei. The model enables predictions for high-spin yrast states and B(E2) variations in 46Ti, 46V, 48V, 48Cr, 50Cr, and 50Mn that can be tested by future experiments, though the interaction's tendency to overbind non-collective states and its failure for nuclei far from N=Z indicate the need for further refinement.
