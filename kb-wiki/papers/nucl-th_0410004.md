# Light-front field theory of hot and dense quark matter

> Extending the concepts of light-front field theory to quantum statistics provides a novel approach towards nuclear matter under extreme conditions. Such conditions exist, e.g., in neutron stars or in the early stage of our universe. They are experimentally expected to occur in heavy ion collisions, e.g., at RHIC and accelerators to be build at GSI and CERN. Light-front field theory is particularly suited, since it is based on a relativistic Hamiltonian approach. It allows us to treat the perturbative as well as the nonperturbative regime of QCD and also correlations that emerge as a field of few-body physics and is important for hadronization. Last but not least the Hamiltonian approach is useful for nonequilibrium processes by utilizing, e.g., the formalism of nonequilibrium statistical operators.

## Key claim

Extending the concepts of light-front field theory to quantum statistics provides a novel approach towards nuclear matter under extreme conditions, but the temperature must be defined with respect to the medium frame rather than the observer's rest frame, which is formally clarified by the use of a time-like medium velocity vector \(u\).

## Method

The author generalizes many-body Green functions to the light-front by defining a grand canonical partition operator \(Z_G = \exp\{-(u\cdot P - \mu N)/T\}\) with a specific choice of the medium velocity \(u^\nu = (1,1,0,0)\) in light-front coordinates. The Dyson equation approach is then applied to derive single-particle light-cone time-ordered Green functions for an ideal gas, including the proper Pauli blocking factors and Matsubara frequencies for fermions and bosons in a hot and dense medium.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi distribution on the light-front is given by \(f(k) = [\exp((k^-_{\mathrm{on}} + k^+)/(2T)) + 1]^{-1}\), where \(k^-_{\mathrm{on}} = (m^2 + \vec k^2_\perp)/k^+\).
- The Matsubara frequencies for fermions are \(\frac{1}{2}k^-_n = i(2n+1)\pi T + \mu - \frac{1}{2}k^+\) and for bosons \(\frac{1}{2}k^-_n = i2n\pi T + \mu - \frac{1}{2}k^+\).
- The single-particle light-front Green function for the ideal gas (equation 31.38 of Fetter and Walecka (1971) generalized) is given by equation (18) in the text, with the ensemble averages \(\langle b^\dagger b\rangle = f^+\), \(\langle b b^\dagger\rangle = 1 - f^+\), \(\langle d^\dagger d\rangle = f^-\), \(\langle d d^\dagger\rangle = 1 - f^-\).

## Context

This work extends the many-body Green function formalism of Fetter and Walecka (1971) and the cluster expansion approach reviewed by Dukelsky et al. (1998) to the light-front dynamics, building on the light-front quantization framework of Brodsky, Pauli, and Pinsky (1997). It enables the treatment of few-body correlations in a hot and dense relativistic plasma, providing a foundation for studying hadron formation in the early universe, color superconducting phases, and QCD phase transitions, as well as nonequilibrium transport via Kadanov-Baym or Boltzmann equations for heavy ion collisions.
