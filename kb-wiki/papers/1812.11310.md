# Magneto-transport in an anomalous fluid with weakly broken symmetries, in weak and strong regime

> We consider a general system with weakly broken time and translation symmetries. We assume the system also possesses a $U(1)$ symmetry which is not only weakly broken, but is anomalous. We use the second order chiral quasi-hydrodynamics to compute the magneto-conductivities in the system in the presence of a weak magnetic field. Analogous to electrical and thermoelectric conductivities, it turns out that the thermal conductivity is identified with a coefficient which depends on the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly. By applying our general formulas to a free system of Weyl fermions at low temperature limit $T\ll μ$, we find that our system is Onsager reciprocal if the relaxation in all energy, momentum and charge channels occurs at the same rate. In the high temperature limit $T\gg μ$, we consider a strongly coupled $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory with $N_c\gg1$ in the hydrodynamic limit. Its gravity dual is a magnetized charged brane to which, we apply our formulas and compute the conductivities. On the way, we show that analogous to the weak regime, an energy cut-off emerges to regulate the thermodynamic quantities. From this gravity background we also find the coefficients of chiral magnetic effect in agreement with the well-known result of the Son-Surowka.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous magneto-transport calculations for systems with weakly broken time translation and U(1) symmetry to also include weakly broken translation symmetry, showing that the magneto-conductivities depend quadratically on the magnetic field through both chiral and mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly coefficients, and that Onsager reciprocity is only restored when the relaxation rates for charge, energy, and momentum are all equal.

## Method

The authors use second-order chiral quasi-hydrodynamics with relaxation time terms added to the conservation equations for energy, momentum, and charge, each with potentially different relaxation times (τ_e, τ_m, τ_c). They linearize the hydrodynamic equations around equilibrium in the presence of a weak magnetic field B, turn on small electric field and thermal gradient sources, and solve for the response of the hydrodynamic fields (temperature, chemical potential, fluid velocity) in the k→0 limit. The resulting electric and heat currents are then read off to obtain the longitudinal magneto-conductivities σ, α₁, α₂, and κ. The general hydrodynamic results are then applied to two specific systems: a free gas of Weyl fermions at low temperature (T ≪ μ) and a strongly coupled SU(N_c) gauge theory at high temperature (T ≫ μ) using holographic duality.

## Key numbers

- The magneto-conductivities take the form ∼ B² (c² A + c c_g B + c_g² C) where c and c_g are the chiral and mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly coefficients, and A, B, C contain thermodynamic information.
- For the free Weyl fermion system at low temperature limit T ≪ μ, Onsager reciprocity is restored when τ_c = τ_e = τ_m, i.e., when relaxation in all channels occurs at the same rate.
- For the strongly coupled SU(N_c) gauge theory with N_c ≫ 1, the holographic computation is performed in the limit B ≪ μ² ≪ T², using a double expansion over B and μ/T.
- An energy cutoff Δ emerges in the holographic computation to regulate thermodynamic quantities, analogous to an IR cutoff in the momentum space of Weyl particles.
- The coefficients of the chiral magnetic effect obtained from the gravity background agree with the well-known result of Son-Surowka.

## Context

This work extends the magneto-transport calculations of Gooth et al. (2017) for systems with weakly broken time translation and U(1) symmetry to also include weakly broken translation symmetry, and generalizes the electrical DC conductivity results of Landsteiner et al. (2014) to include thermoelectric and thermal conductivities. The hydrodynamic results are applied to a free Weyl fermion system previously studied via chiral kinetic theory by Abbasi et al. (2018), and to a strongly coupled gauge theory using the magnetized charged brane solution in AdS5 from DHoker and Kraus (2009). The paper enables systematic computation of magneto-transport coefficients for any system whose thermodynamics is known, in both weak and strong coupling regimes.
