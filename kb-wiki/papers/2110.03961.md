# Study of the jet transport coefficient at the Large Hadron Collider energies using Color String Percolation Model

> In order to have a better understanding of the matter formed in ultra-relativistic collisions, we estimate the jet transport coefficient, $\hat q$, within the Color Sting Percolation Model (CSPM) for various multiplicity classes in proton-proton collisions and centrality classes in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies. We study $\hat q$ as a function of final state charged-particle multiplicity and initial percolation temperature. Finally, we compare our obtained results with those calculated from the JET collaboration.

## Key claim

The jet transport coefficient estimated within the Color String Percolation Model shows a universal scaling behavior across different collision systems and energies at the LHC, and the scaled coefficient \(\hat q/T^{3}\) exhibits a trend similar to results from the JET collaboration, with a critical behavior observed near the hadronization temperature.

## Method

The authors used the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) to extract the color suppression factor \(F(\xi)\) by fitting the soft part of the transverse momentum spectra (0.15–1.0 GeV/c) for pp, Xe-Xe, and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The initial percolation temperature was derived from \(F(\xi)\) using the single-string average transverse momentum, and the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q\) was obtained from the relation \(\hat q \approx (15/2) T^{2} (1 - e^{-\xi}) / L\), where \(L\) is the longitudinal string extension of about 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature \(T_{c} = 167.7 \pm 2.8\) MeV and critical percolation density \(\xi_{c} \sim 1.2\) were used to obtain the single-string average transverse momentum \(\sqrt{\langle p^{2}_{\rm T}\rangle_{1}} = 207.2 \pm 3.3\) MeV.
- The longitudinal extension of the string \(L\) was taken as approximately 1 fm.
- The analysis covered pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) and 13 TeV, Xe-Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.44\) TeV, and Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) and 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Color String Percolation Model to estimate the jet transport coefficient, a key quantity for quantifying parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The results are compared with those from the JET collaboration, which used five different theoretical models (including Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev, high-twist, and Arnold-Moore-Yaffe approaches) to incorporate particle energy loss. The observed universal scaling of \(\hat q/T^{3}\) across collision systems enables further study of the transition from hadronic to deconfined matter at LHC energies.
