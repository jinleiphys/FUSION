# Color Magnetic Corrections to Quark Model Valence Distributions

> We calculate order $α_s$ color magnetic corrections to the valence quark distributions of the proton using the Los Alamos Model Potential wavefunctions. The spin-spin interaction breaks the model SU(4) symmetry, providing a natural mechanism for the difference between the up and down distributions. For a value of $α_s$ sufficient to produce the $N-Δ$ mass splitting, we find up and down quark distributions in reasonable agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The color magnetic interaction (spin-spin interaction) breaks the SU(4) symmetry of the naive quark model wavefunction, providing a natural mechanism for the observed difference between up and down valence quark distributions in the proton, but the gauge correction is comparable to the two- and three-body corrections, making the d quark distribution quite sensitive to the choice of gauge used to define the quark model wavefunctions.

## Method

The authors calculated order α_s color magnetic corrections to the valence quark distributions of the proton using the Los Alamos Model Potential (LAMP) wavefunctions, which consist of three massless quarks bound by a linear scalar potential. They evaluated the two-body, three-body, and gauge corrections to the quark distributions using a Peierls-Yoccoz momentum eigenstate to describe the nucleon in its rest frame, employing a closure approximation with an effective gluon mass of 400 MeV. The resulting distributions were then evolved from the quark model scale μ_0^2 to 15 GeV^2 using a finite element procedure to reconstruct the evolved distributions from their moments.

## Key numbers

- The LAMP potential parameters are V_0 = 0.9 GeV/fm and r_0 = 0.57 fm, chosen to generate the average of the nucleon and delta masses.
- The effective gluon mass μ is taken to be 400 MeV in the closure approximation.
- The quark model scale μ_0^2 is found to lie between 0.2 and 0.4 GeV^2 when comparing evolved distributions to BEBC neutrino data at Q^2 = 15 GeV^2.
- The spin sum for the two-body correction yields δu^{2b}(x) = 2f^{2b}(x) - 2T^{2b}_{ii}(x) and δd^{2b}(x) = f^{2b}(x) - 4T^{2b}_{ii}(x), making the d quark distribution twice as sensitive to color magnetic effects as the u distribution.
- The spin sum for the three-body correction yields δu^{3b}(x) = 2δq_E^{3b}(x) - 4δq_M^{3b}(x) and δd^{3b}(x) = δq_E^{3b}(x) + δq_M^{3b}(x).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jaffe and Ross (1980), who argued that quark models represent QCD at a low renormalization scale and provided a prescription for testing models against high energy data by evolving quark distributions. The calculation uses the Los Alamos Model Potential (LAMP) and builds on previous evaluations of quark distributions for the MIT bag model and soliton bag model. The results enable further study of color magnetic effects in systems of more than one nucleon and provide a framework for testing quark models against experimental data on valence quark distributions.
