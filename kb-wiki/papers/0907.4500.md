# Third-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics

> Following the procedure introduced by Israel and Stewart, we expand the entropy current up to the third order in the shear stress tensor $π^{αβ}$ and derive a novel third-order evolution equation for $π^{αβ}$. This equation is solved for the one-dimensional Bjorken boost-invariant expansion. The scaling solutions for various values of the shear viscosity to the entropy density ratio $η/s$ are shown to be in very good agreement with those obtained from kinetic transport calculations. For the pressure isotropy starting with 1 at $τ_0=0.4 fm/c$, the third-order corrections to Israel-Stewart theory are approximately 10\% for $η/s=0.2$ and more than a factor of 2 for $η/s=3$. We also estimate all higher-order corrections to Israel-Stewart theory and demonstrate their importance in describing highly viscous matters.

## Key claim
The paper derives a novel third-order evolution equation for the shear stress tensor in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by expanding the entropy current to third order in the shear stress. The third-order corrections are shown to be essential for preventing unphysical negative longitudinal pressure that appears in second-order Israel-Stewart theory for large shear viscosity to entropy density ratios, with the magnitude of corrections depending strongly on the specific η/s value and the initial time.

## Method
The authors follow the Israel-Stewart entropy principle, expanding the entropy current to third order in the shear stress tensor π^{αβ} using a Grad 14-moment ansatz for the off-equilibrium distribution function of massless particles. They derive a third-order evolution equation for π^{αβ} and solve it for one-dimensional Bjorken boost-invariant expansion, comparing the scaling solutions with kinetic transport calculations from the parton cascade BAMPS for various constant η/s values.

## Key numbers
- For pressure isotropy starting at 1 with τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, third-order corrections to Israel-Stewart theory are approximately 10% for η/s = 0.2 and more than a factor of 2 for η/s = 3
- For η/s = 1, third-order and approximative all-orders treatments bring corrections of 200% and 100%, respectively, to the Israel-Stewart result
- For η/s = 3, third-order and approximative all-orders treatments bring corrections of 300% and 200%, respectively, to the Israel-Stewart result
- For η/s = 0.4, the difference between Israel-Stewart theory and BAMPS calculations is 30%; for η/s = 3, the difference is almost 200%
- The initial temperature is T₀ = 500 MeV at initial time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- The shear viscosity coefficient is given by η = (6/5)(T/σ₂₂) for isotropic elastic collisions

## Context
This work extends the second-order Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1976) to third order in the shear stress tensor, addressing the breakdown of second-order theory found at η/s > 0.5 in comparisons with kinetic transport calculations (Huovinen and Molnar, 2009; El et al., 2009). The third-order corrections prevent unphysical reheating and negative pressure phenomena observed in second-order theory for large viscosities (Muronga, 2004; Martinez and Strickland, 2009). The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic descriptions of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems with large η/s ratios or at LHC energies where smaller initial times lead to faster out-of-equilibrium evolution.
