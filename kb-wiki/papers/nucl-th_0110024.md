# Structure Functions of Unstable Lithium Isotopes

> We study both the spin-average and spin-dependent structure functions of the lithium isotopes, $^{6-11}$Li, which could be measured at RIKEN and other nuclear facilities in the future. It is found that the light-cone momentum distribution of the valence neutron in the halo of $^{11}$Li is very sharp and symmetric around y = 1, because of the weak binding. The EMC ratios for Li isotopes are then calculated. We study the possibility of extracting the neutron structure function from data for the nuclear structure functions of the Li isotopes. Next we calculate the spin-dependent structure functions of $^{7,9,11}$Li isotopes, which have spin of 3/2. The effect of the nuclear binding and Fermi motion on the multipole spin structure function, $^{3/2}_{~1}g_1$, is about 10% in the region x < 0.7, but it becomes quite important at large x. The spin structure function of $^{3/2}_{~3}g_1$ is also investigated. Finally, we discuss the modification of the Gottfried and Bjorken integrals in a nuclear medium and point out several candidates for a pair of mirror nuclei to study the flavor-nonsinglet quark distributions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The valence neutrons in the halo of \(^{11}\)Li are so weakly bound that their light-cone momentum distribution is very sharp and symmetric around \(y = 1\), implying these neutrons are in an environment very close to that of a free neutron; this makes the lithium isotopes, particularly \(^{9}\)Li and \(^{11}\)Li, attractive candidates for extracting the free neutron structure function from nuclear data, provided the nuclear binding and Fermi motion effects are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors used a simple shell model with Hartree approximation to calculate single-particle wave functions for \(^{6-11}\)Li isotopes, fitting the potential parameters (central depth, spin-orbit strength, radius, and diffuseness) to reproduce experimental rms radii, one-neutron and one-proton separation energies, and the energy difference between the first excited \(1/2^-\) and ground \(3/2^-\) states. They then computed the light-cone momentum distributions of nucleons in each isotope and convolved them with free nucleon structure functions to obtain the nuclear spin-average structure functions \(F_2^A\) and the multipole spin-dependent structure functions \(^{3/2}_{1}g_1\) and \(^{3/2}_{3}g_1\) for the spin-3/2 isotopes \(^{7,9,11}\)Li.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Li is about 300 keV (specifically 0.301 ± 0.029 MeV), and the one-neutron separation energy is 0.326 ± 0.042 MeV; the authors adopt \(S_n = 301\) or 350 keV for their calculations.
* The rms radius of \(^{11}\)Li is about 3.1 fm, significantly larger than \(A^{1/3} \sim 2.2\) fm.
* The effect of nuclear binding and Fermi motion on the multipole spin structure function \(^{3/2}_{1}g_1\) is about 10% in the region \(x < 0.7\), but becomes quite important at large \(x\).
* The spin structure function \(^{3/2}_{3}g_1\) is negative at small \(x\), becomes positive in the region \(0.2 < x < 0.5\) (though with very small magnitude), and becomes negative again at large \(x\) with large absolute value due to Fermi motion.
* The valence neutron in the halo of \(^{11}\)Li has a momentum distribution sharply peaked around \(k \sim 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear structure functions from stable nuclei to unstable, neutron-rich lithium isotopes, building on the EMC effect literature (European Muon Collaboration, early 1980s) and prior analyses of the deuteron by the Adelaide group (Mel, 1990s) and the \(A=3\) system by Bissey and Pace (1990s). The authors previously studied nonsinglet structure functions in the lightest mirror nuclei (\(^3\)He and \(^3\)H) (Saito, 2001) and suggested using unstable nuclei for flavor-nonsinglet studies. The paper enables future measurements at RIKEN (MUSES project) and other radioactive beam facilities to extract the free neutron structure function and to test the Gottfried and Bjorken sum rules in mirror nuclei such as (\(^7\)Li, \(^7\)Be).
