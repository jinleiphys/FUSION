# Probing Parton Orbital Angular Momentum in Longitudinally Polarized Nucleon

> While the total orbital angular momentum (OAM) of a definite quark flavor in a longitudinally-polarized nucleon can be obtained through a sum rule involving twist-two generalized parton distribution (GPDs), its distribution as a function of parton momentum in light-front coordinates is more complicated to define and measure because it involves intrinsically twist-three effects. In this paper, we consider two different parton OAM distributions. The first is manifestly gauge invariant, and its moments are local operators and calculable in lattice QCD. We show that it can potentially be measured through twist-three GPDs. The second is the much-debated canonical OAM distribution natural in free-field theory and light-cone gauge. We show the latter in light-cone gauge can also be related to twist-three GPDs as well as quantum phase-space Wigner distributions, both being measurable in high-energy experiments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both the gauge-invariant parton orbital angular momentum (OAM) distribution and the canonical OAM distribution (defined in light-cone gauge) can be related to twist-three generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and Wigner distributions, making them potentially measurable in high-energy experiments, though the authors hedge that such measurements are "more difficult to achieve" than leading-twist observables.

## Method

The authors define two distinct quark OAM distributions: a manifestly gauge-invariant distribution \(L_q(x)\) whose moments are local operators, and a canonical distribution \(l_q(x)\) natural in light-cone gauge. They relate these to twist-three GPDs of both D-type (involving covariant derivatives) and F-type (involving field strengths), and to quantum phase-space Wigner distributions with different gauge-link choices. For gluons, they show that while no gauge-invariant decomposition into spin and OAM exists, the canonical gluon OAM distribution \(l_g(x)\) can be defined in light-cone gauge and extended to a gauge-invariant form via the gauge-invariant extension (GIE) procedure.

## Key numbers

- The total quark angular momentum distribution is given by \(J_q(x) = \frac{x}{2} [q(x) + E_q(x)]\), where \(q(x)\) is the quark momentum distribution and \(E_q(x)\) is a twist-two GPD.
- The gauge-invariant quark OAM distribution is defined via moments: \(\int dx L_q(x) x^{n-1} = L_{qn}\), where \(L_{qn}\) are matrix elements of the operator in Eq. (12).
- The canonical quark OAM distribution \(l_q(x)\) satisfies \(\int dx l_q(x) = l_q\), the total canonical quark OAM.
- The gluon helicity distribution \(\Delta g(x)\) and canonical gluon OAM distribution \(l_g(x)\) satisfy \(\int dx \Delta g(x) = \Delta G\) and \(\int dx l_g(x) = l_g\).
- The relation between the two OAM distributions is \(L_q(x) = l_q(x) + l_{q,\text{pot}}(x)\), where \(l_{q,\text{pot}}(x)\) is the potential angular momentum term.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon spin decomposition framework of Ji (1996) and the parton OAM definitions of Hoodbhoy, Ji, and Lu (1998) by clarifying the twist-three nature of OAM distributions in longitudinally polarized nucleons. It connects to the canonical OAM approach of Jaffe and Manohar (1989), Hagler and Schafer (1998), Harindranath and Kundu (1998), and Bashinsky and Jaffe (1998), and shows how these definitions relate to twist-three GPDs and Wigner distributions as discussed by Hatta (2011) and Lorce and Pasquini (2011). The results enable future experimental searches for OAM distributions through hard exclusive processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and through Wigner distribution measurements at facilities like the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
