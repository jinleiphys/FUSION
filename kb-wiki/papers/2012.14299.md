# Relativistic levels of mesic atoms

> We revisit the derivation of the covariant two-body scalar-fermion equation with a Coulomb interaction, presented in a previous paper. We show that it can be given the formal aspect of a Dirac equation, but for the fact that the eigenvalue is also contained in one of the coefficients and thus it is not linearly included. The discussion of the boundary value problem is therefore different, although some properties of the Dirac equation can be recovered in an approximation bringing back to the concept of reduced mass. We discuss a mixed analytic-numerical method of solution which allows to obtain very accurate results and we calculate the lowest levels, states and QED corrections for pionic and kaonic atoms.

## Key claim

The scalar-fermion two-body equation with Coulomb interaction can be cast into the formal aspect of a Dirac equation, but the eigenvalue is also contained in one of the coefficients and thus is not linearly included, which forbids the application of most results holding for the Dirac equation and removes the degeneracy of states with opposite sign of the angular momentum parameter kappa.

## Method

The authors revisit the derivation of a covariant two-body scalar-fermion wave equation with a Coulomb potential, reducing it to a second-order system formally identical to a Dirac equation in a central potential but with eigenvalue-dependent coefficients. They solve the resulting boundary value problem using a mixed analytic-numerical method: series expansions at the origin and at infinity with recurrence relations, holomorphic series at regular points, and Pade approximants for the asymptotic series, then match these solutions at a crossing point to obtain eigenvalues. The method is applied to compute the pure Coulomb levels and the lowest-order QED corrections (one-loop and two-loop vacuum polarization) for pionic and kaonic atoms.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program on covariant two-body wave equations (Giachetti and Sorace, earlier papers) from fermion-fermion systems to scalar-fermion systems, specifically pionic and kaonic atoms. Prior calculations of mesic atom levels used the Schrodinger equation with perturbative relativistic and QED corrections (SBIP, earlier work) or phenomenological finite-size corrections (Guy, Pato, XGG, earlier works). The present fully covariant treatment aims to provide more accurate pure Coulomb levels and QED corrections, enabling improved determination of strong-interaction shifts in the fundamental state when compared with experimental X-ray spectroscopy measurements (Bazzi, earlier work).
