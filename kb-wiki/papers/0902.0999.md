# Full and Partial Thermalization of Nucleons in Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We propose a mechanism of thermalization of nucleons in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. Our model belongs, to a certain degree, to the transport ones; we consider the evolution of the system, but we parametrize this development by the number of collisions of every particle in the system rather than by the time variable. We based on the assumption that the nucleon momentum transfer after several nucleon-nucleon (-hadron) collisions becomes a random quantity driven by a proper distribution.

# Full and Partial Thermalization of Nucleons in Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose that nucleon momentum transfer becomes fully randomized after three successive elastic collisions, and that the nucleon spectrum can be decomposed into contributions from ensembles with different collision numbers plus a possible thermal source from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP); however, they acknowledge that the presence of the thermal source cannot be resolved unambiguously because the partial expansion without the thermal contribution yields the same chi-squared fit to the data.

## Method

The model parametrizes the evolution of the nucleon system by the number of collisions each particle experiences rather than by time, using a "multi-scattering form-factor" \( I_M(\bs Q) \) derived from a microcanonical-like distribution with a saddle-point approximation for large collision number \( M \). The total nucleon spectrum is written as a sum over ensembles with different collision numbers \( M \) (from 1 to \( M_{\rm max} \)) plus a thermal term, where the coefficients \( C(M) \) are determined by fitting to experimental rapidity and transverse mass distributions using the maximum entropy method. The maximum collision number \( M_{\rm max} = 13 \) and the form-factor parameter \( q_{\rm max} = 0.8 \) GeV/c are taken from UrQMD transport model calculations for Au+Au collisions at AGS energies.

## Key numbers

- Maximum number of collisions: \( M_{\rm max} = 13 \) for Au+Au collisions at 11.6 A GeV/c with 0-3% centrality (AGS conditions), determined from UrQMD calculations.
- Form-factor momentum cutoff: \( q_{\rm max} = 0.8 \) GeV/c, chosen so that \( \frac{3}{5} q_{\rm max}^2 = \langle \bs q^2 \rangle = \langle \bs q^2 \rangle_{\rm UrQMD} \).
- Thermal slope parameter extracted from experimental proton \( m_\perp \)-spectra: \( T = 280 \) MeV.
- "Nucleon power" of the QGP under AGS conditions: \( P_{\rm QGP}^{(N)} \approx 11\% \), meaning about 11% of registered nucleons come from the thermalized QGP source.
- Coefficients \( C(M) \) from the fit (absolute number of protons in each collision ensemble): \( C(1)=1.3 \), \( C(2)=23.2 \), \( C(3)=4.7 \), \( C(4)=5.3 \), \( C(5)=6.6 \), \( C(6)=8.2 \), \( C(7)=9.5 \), \( C(8)=10.6 \), \( C(9)=11.6 \), \( C(10)=12.3 \), \( C(11)=12.9 \), \( C(12)=13.5 \), \( C(13)=13.9 \), and \( C_{\rm therm}=16.6 \).

## Context

This work extends transport-model approaches by replacing the time variable with the number of collisions per particle, building on the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999) to determine the maximum collision number. The model is applied to AGS data from the E802 collaboration (Ahle et al., 1999) on Au+Au collisions at 11.6 A GeV/c, and the authors claim that the QGP as a nucleon source may be created not only at SPS energies (Heinz, 2000) but also in central collisions at AGS energies. The approach enables a quantitative estimate of the "nucleon power" of the QGP, though the authors note that more detailed experimental data in the central rapidity region are needed to unambiguously resolve the thermal source contribution.
