# Hadron multiplicity in pp and AA collisions at LHC from the Color Glass Condensate

> We provide quantitative predictions for the rapidity, centrality and energy dependencies of inclusive charged-hadron productions for the forthcoming LHC measurements in nucleus-nucleus collisions based on the idea of gluon saturation in the color-glass condensate framework. Our formulation gives very good descriptions of the first data from the LHC for the inclusive charged-hadron production in proton-proton collisions, the deep inelastic scattering at HERA at small Bjorken-x, and the hadron multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors provide quantitative predictions for charged-hadron multiplicity in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC based on the Color Glass Condensate framework, with the hedge that the kT-factorization used for dense-dense parton systems has not been rigorously proven, but is justified by its success at RHIC.

## Method

The authors employ kT-factorization to compute gluon mini-jet production in AA collisions, using an impact-parameter dependent dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude that incorporates the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation. The nuclear saturation scale is obtained by convolving the proton saturation scale (fitted to HERA DIS data) with the nuclear thickness function via a Wood-Saxon parametrization. Hadronization is modeled using the Local Parton-Hadron Duality principle with a mini-jet mass that depends on the saturation scale, and the two free parameters (overall normalization factor and soft-scale mu) are fixed using RHIC Au-Au data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV for 0-6% centrality.

## Key numbers

- Charged-hadron multiplicity dN_AA/deta at midrapidity eta=0 for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.75 TeV: 1152 +/- 81
- Charged-hadron multiplicity dN_AA/deta at midrapidity eta=0 for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV: 1314 +/- 92
- Theoretical uncertainty estimated at less than 7% at LHC energies, with the band in figures showing about 3% theoretical error
- The (1-x)^4 correction to the unintegrated gluon density at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV for AA collisions is less than 4% for eta <= 4.5 and less than 13% for 4.5 < eta < 6
- A factor of 2 difference in the definition of nuclear saturation scale (Q_A -> 2 Q_A) changes results by less than 5% at the LHC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for pp collisions (Levin and Rezaeian, 2010) to AA collisions at the LHC, improving upon the KLN approach (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi, 2001-2005) by incorporating the unintegrated gluon density that describes HERA DIS data and using the proton saturation scale from DIS to determine the nuclear saturation scale, rather than extrapolating the RHIC saturation scale via BFKL evolution. The predictions enable a crucial test of the CGC framework at LHC energies, with the key difference from KLN being that the KLN predictions underestimated pp multiplicity at sqrt(s)=7 TeV while overestimating AA multiplicity compared to the present results.
