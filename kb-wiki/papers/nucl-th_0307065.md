# QCD-view on hadron form factors in space-like and time-like momentum transfer regions

> QCD gives definite predictions for hadron electromagnetic form factors in space-like and time-like momentum transfer regions, such as the quark counting rules, the hypothesis of hadron helicity conservation, and the relations between nucleon and deuteron form factors in the formalism of reduced nuclear matrix elements. Recent precise data about these form factors, obtained in polarization experiments at the Jefferson Laboratory, have essentially changed our view on this subject. QCD-predictions do not apply to these data up to $Q^2$=5-6 GeV$^2$ for deuteron and for the electric form factor of proton. An analysis of these data suggests that the asymptotic region will more probably start at $Q^2$=20-25 GeV$^2$. We show that the separation of magnetic and electric proton form factors in the time-like region represents the most stringent test of the asymptotic regime and QCD-predictions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the asymptotic regime where perturbative QCD predictions for hadron electromagnetic form factors become valid likely starts at momentum transfers of Q² = 20-25 GeV², not at the 5-6 GeV² range where recent Jefferson Lab data show clear deviations from QCD expectations, particularly for the proton electric form factor and the deuteron.

## Method

The authors analyze recent polarization transfer data from Jefferson Lab for elastic electron-proton scattering in the space-like region, comparing them to the traditional Rosenbluth separation method and to perturbative QCD predictions including quark counting rules and hadron helicity conservation. They also examine deuteron form factor data using the reduced nuclear matrix element formalism and compare space-like and time-like region data for the proton using the Phragmen-Lindelöf theorem, which requires asymptotic equality of form factors in both regions.

## Key numbers

- The dipole approximation parameter a = 0.71 GeV², corresponding to a root mean squared radius of 0.81 fm for the proton
- The linear deviation from dipole behavior for the proton electric form factor is parametrized as μ G_Ep / G_Mp = 1.0 - 0.130 (Q²/GeV² - 0.04) based on data up to Q² = 5.6 GeV²
- The reduced deuteron form factor scaling parameter m₀² = 0.28 GeV², related to the pion form factor behavior
- The QCD scale parameter Λ ≈ 100 MeV gives reasonable fits for deuteron data at Q² ≥ 2 GeV²
- The time-like region magnetic form factor parametrization includes m_nd² = 3.6 ± 0.9 GeV² characterizing deviation from dipole s-dependence
- The asymptotic region is predicted to start at Q² = 20-25 GeV² based on extrapolation of the Phragmen-Lindelöf theorem

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nucleon form factors following the surprising Jefferson Lab polarization transfer measurements by Jones (2000) and Gayou (2002), which contradicted the long-standing dipole approximation and showed that electric and magnetic proton distributions are different. The paper builds on the quark counting rules of Brodsky (1976) and the reduced deuteron form factor formalism, while noting that vector meson dominance models (such as Lomon's recent work and the earlier work by Zichichi (1966)) can reproduce the data trends. The analysis enables future tests of asymptotic QCD through precise measurements of the angular asymmetry in electron-positron annihilation to proton-antiproton in the time-like region, which would also allow testing for two-photon exchange contributions.
