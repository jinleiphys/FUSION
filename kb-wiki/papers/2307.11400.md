# The role of the pion in the lineshape of the $X(3872)$

> We determine the contribution of long-range pion interactions to the $X(3872)$ dynamics, assuming it is a loosely bound $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0}$ molecule. Our result is based on the distorted wave Born approximation in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. Despite their long-range nature, we find that pion interactions cannot produce a large and negative effective range. Nonetheless, they introduce imaginary parts. In particular, they contribute to the total decay width of the $X(3872)$ with a term associated with, but not precisely corresponding to, the $D^*$ width. Our approach can also be applied to the recently discovered $T_{cc}^+$ states.

## Key claim

Despite the long-range nature of pion interactions in the X(3872) system, they cannot produce a large and negative effective range, but they do introduce imaginary parts that contribute to the total decay width of the X(3872) with a term associated with, but not precisely corresponding to, the D* width.

## Method

The authors used the distorted wave Born approximation in non-relativistic quantum mechanics to compute the contribution of long-range pion interactions to the X(3872) dynamics, assuming it is a loosely bound D0 D̅*0 molecule. They constructed an effective Hamiltonian on a two-component Hilbert space describing the D0 D̅*0 and D0 D̅0 π0 systems, then integrated out the D D π component to obtain an effective potential for the D D* subspace. The scattering amplitude and bound state energy were computed perturbatively in the small coupling α, which measures the weakness of the pion-induced interaction.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy B of the X(3872) relative to the D0 D̅*0 threshold has an upper bound: B ≲ 120 keV
- The mass difference δ ≡ m_D* - m_D - m_π ≃ 7 MeV
- The parameter μ ≡ √(2 m_π δ) ≃ 43 MeV
- The pion coupling g^2 ≃ 0.34, extracted from the charged D-meson decay width
- The pion interaction strength α ≡ g^2 μ^2 / (24π f_π^2) ≃ 5 × 10^{-4}
- The D* → D π decay width Γ_* = 2α μ
- For a molecular X(3872) with 0 keV < B ≲ 100 keV, the real part of the effective range from pion exchange is constrained to -0.20 fm ≲ Re r_0 ≲ -0.16 fm
- The imaginary part of the effective range is constrained to 0 fm ≲ Im r_0 ≲ 0.17 fm
- The pion-induced correction to the total decay width is 0 keV ≲ ΔΓ_X ≲ 1.9 keV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Esposito (2021) and Mikhasenko (2022) on using the effective range as a discriminator between molecular and compact tetraquark interpretations of the X(3872). It confirms and simplifies results previously obtained by Braaten (2020) using a non-relativistic effective theory, but with a technically simpler approach based on non-relativistic scattering theory and exact solution of three-dimensional δ-function potentials. The analysis can also be applied to the recently discovered T_cc^+ state, which shares several features with the X(3872).
