# Mass generation in coalescence - effects on hadron spectra

> Different scenarios for the creation of constituent mass in the hadron formation process are discussed. Effects of these may be observable in hadron momentum spectra.

# Mass generation in coalescence - effects on hadron spectra

## Key claim
If hadron mass is generated only at the phase transition (rather than quarks already carrying constituent mass before coalescence), heavier mesons should be suppressed relative to lighter ones in intermediate transverse momentum spectra; however, present data do not allow distinguishing between the two scenarios.

## Method
Two extreme scenarios for mass generation in coalescence are compared analytically: (1) quarks obtain constituent masses before confinement and form hadrons from collinear partons, and (2) quarks remain massless until coalescence, with mass generated at hadron creation. The resulting transverse momentum spectra are derived using boosted thermal distributions, with a Boltzmann penalty factor for the massless-parton scenario. The parameterizations are then fitted together with a high-\(p_T\) power law to neutral pion spectra from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and the \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratio is predicted for each scenario.

## Key numbers
- Temperature \(T = 175 \, \mathrm{MeV}\) and flow velocity \(\eta_T = 0.3\) are used for the model calculations.
- The intermediate \(p_T\) regime considered is \(2 - 6 \, \mathrm{GeV}/c\).
- The \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratio for the power-law component is taken as \(0.45\).
- Below \(p_T = 3 \, \mathrm{GeV}/c\) the two scenarios yield considerably different estimates for the \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratio.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence/recombination models proposed by Fries (2003) and Greco (2003) for particle production in heavy-ion collisions, which typically assume collinear partons with constituent masses. It contrasts with the approach of Schnedermann (1993) for thermal hadron spectra. The paper enables future realistic coalescence calculations that incorporate mass generation dynamics, and notes that PHENIX preliminary data on \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratios cannot yet distinguish the scenarios.
