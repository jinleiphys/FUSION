# Lattice QCD Study of Positive Parity Dibaryons with Maximal Charm and Strangeness

> We present the ground-state energy spectra of dibaryons composed of single-flavor quarks, specifically systems with strangeness $\mathcal{S} = -6$ and charm $\mathcal{C} = 6$. Our lattice QCD study is based on $N_f=2+1+1$ MILC ensembles with highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) sea quarks, spanning four lattice spacings and two spatial volumes. We employ valence quark propagators realized using a relativistic overlap action, evaluate correlation matrices with carefully designed operator bases, and extract reliable ground-state energy estimates in the $S = 0$ and $S = 2$ spin channels. We explore their binding characteristics and interaction dynamics by examining the energy separation between the dibaryon states and the corresponding two-baryon thresholds. These results contribute to a deeper understanding of single-flavor dibaryon states as a function of the quark masses. In the $S=0$ channel, the $Ω_{ccc}$-$Ω_{ccc}$ system exhibits a clear signal of a bound state, while the $Ω$-$Ω$ system lies very close to the threshold, making it difficult to draw definitive conclusions. For $S=2$, both systems are found to be unbound.

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that the $\Omega_{ccc}$-$\Omega_{ccc}$ system in the $S=0$ channel exhibits a clear signal of a bound state, while the $\Omega$-$\Omega$ system in the same channel lies very close to threshold, making definitive conclusions difficult. For the $S=2$ channel, both systems are found to be unbound.

## Method
The authors use $N_f=2+1+1$ MILC ensembles with highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) sea quarks across four lattice spacings and two spatial volumes. Valence quark propagators are realized using a relativistic overlap action, and correlation matrices are constructed from carefully designed operator bases including both nonrelativistic and relativistic embeddings of the $H$ irreducible representation. Ground-state energies are extracted via generalized eigenvalue problems and effective mass analysis, with binding assessed by comparing dibaryon masses to twice the corresponding single-baryon masses.

## Key numbers
- The $\Omega_{ccc}$-$\Omega_{ccc}$ system in the $S=0$ channel shows a clear bound state signal (binding energy not explicitly quoted in the truncated text)
- The $\Omega$-$\Omega$ system in the $S=0$ channel lies very close to the two-baryon threshold, preventing definitive conclusions about binding
- For $S=2$, both $\Omega$-$\Omega$ and $\Omega_{ccc}$-$\Omega_{ccc}$ systems are found to be unbound
- The study uses five ensembles with lattice spacings ranging from $a = 0.042$ fm to $a = 0.1207$ fm, and spatial volumes from $24^3$ to $64^3$
- Sea pion masses range from 217 MeV to 319 MeV across ensembles

## Context
This work extends prior lattice QCD studies of single-flavor dibaryons, including the $\Omega$-$\Omega$ system investigated by Buchoff (2012) using anisotropic Wilson-clover fermions at $m_\pi \approx 400$ MeV, and by HALQCD (2015, 2017) at $m_\pi \approx 700$ MeV and near-physical $m_\pi \approx 146$ MeV. It also builds on the $\Omega_{ccc}$-$\Omega_{ccc}$ study by Lyu (2021) and the authors' own previous work on $\Omega_{bbb}$-$\Omega_{bbb}$ (Mathur, 2022), which found binding of approximately 80 MeV. The results contribute to understanding how single-flavor dibaryon binding properties evolve as a function of quark mass, and provide first-principles input for effective field theories and models of baryon-baryon interactions in the heavy quark sector.
