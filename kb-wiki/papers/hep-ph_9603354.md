# Why is a nucleon bound?

> In a style of popular article, we discuss models of hadronic structure and their relation with models of the QCD vacuum and lattice simulations. Borrowing two main characters from G.Gamow, Mr.Thompson and Professor, we make a travel in the QCD vacuum. Instanton-generated interaction between quarks appear to be major player, they create quark condensate, constituent quark masses and their bound states with properties very close to those observed. Direct removal of perturbative and confining forces (possible on the lattice by ``cooling'') result in very small modification of hadrons. Phase transitions at high T and large number of quarks in the theory are discussed.

# Why is a nucleon bound?

## Key claim

Instanton-generated interactions between quarks are the dominant binding mechanism for light-quark hadrons like the nucleon, while Coulomb and confining forces play a secondary role; this claim is hedged by the author's explicit warning that it is "far from being the universally accepted" statement.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares four historical models of nucleon structure (nonrelativistic quark model, MIT bag model, Skyrmion, and chiral bag) against results from instanton liquid model calculations and lattice QCD simulations with "cooling" that removes perturbative and confining fields. The author presents correlation functions for nucleon and Delta channels computed in the instanton liquid model and compares them with lattice data from the MIT group, showing that hadronic properties survive cooling.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark effective masses are 300-400 MeV in the nonrelativistic quark model.
- Instanton size is approximately ρ ≈ 1/3 fm.
- Nucleon mass calculated in the instanton liquid model (without confinement) is 960 ± 30 MeV.
- Instanton-induced vacuum energy density is Δε ≈ -1 GeV/fm³, about 20 times larger than the MIT bag constant and about 6 times larger than the mass density of nuclear matter.
- Critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is T_c ≈ 150 MeV.
- Pion mass in the instanton liquid model was found to be 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (1961) by replacing its hypothetical four-fermion interaction with the instanton-induced interaction derived by 't Hooft, and it directly contradicts the MIT bag model's assumption that confinement dominates hadron binding. The paper enables future lattice investigations of QCD with larger numbers of quark flavors and studies of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, as well as further work on the "hard glue" or "epoxy" component of the gluon condensate that does not melt at the phase transition, as noted by Brown and Koch (1993).
