# Nuclear Matter Properties of the Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model

> We explore in more detail the modified quark meson coupling (MQMC) model in nuclear matter. Based on previous studies two different functional forms for the density dependence of the bag constant are discussed. For uniform matter distributions the MQMC model can be cast in a form identical to QHD by a redefinition of the sigma meson field. It is then clear that modifications similar to those introduced in QHD will permit the reproduction of all nuclear matter properties including the compressibility. After calibrating the model parameters at equilibrium nuclear matter density, the model and parameter dependence of the resulting equation of state is examined. Nucleon properties and scaling relations between the bag constant and the effective nucleon mass are discussed.

# Nuclear Matter Properties of the Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim

The modified quark meson coupling (MQMC) model can be made formally equivalent to quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) through a redefinition of the scalar meson field, and with sufficiently flexible parametrizations of the density-dependent bag constant, the MQMC model reproduces all nuclear matter properties, including the compressibility, with the same quality as established relativistic mean-field models, provided the bag constant at equilibrium is significantly smaller than its free-space value.

## Method

The authors generalized two previously proposed forms for the density dependence of the bag constant: a direct coupling model (MQMC_A) where the bag constant depends on the scalar field via a polynomial parametrization, and a scaling model (MQMC_B) where the bag constant depends on the effective nucleon mass via a Pade parametrization. They calibrated the free parameters (kappa, quark-meson coupling g_sigma^q, and the coefficients in the polynomial or Pade forms) to reproduce four equilibrium properties of symmetric nuclear matter: saturation density, binding energy, effective nucleon mass at saturation, and compression modulus. The resulting equations of state were compared with QHD mean-field calculations using cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions.

## Key numbers

- Free-space bag constant: B_0^(1/4) = 188.1 MeV (with bag radius R_0 = 0.6 fm and Z_0 = 2.03)
- Compression modulus for the original QMC model: K_0 = 223 MeV
- Compression modulus for the calibrated MQMC models: K_0 = 250 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation in the original QMC model: M_N^* = 0.89 M_N
- Saturation point in the transformed scalar field for MQMC models: g_0 phi / M_N = 0.4
- Saturation point in the transformed scalar field for the original QMC model: g_0 phi / M_N = 0.11
- Range of kappa explored for MQMC_A: 3 < kappa < 7
- Range of kappa explored for MQMC_B: 3 < kappa < 5
- Range of g_sigma^q explored for MQMC_A: 0 <= g_sigma^q < 2
- Range of g_sigma^q explored for MQMC_B: 0.8 < g_sigma^q < 1.5
- Values of B/B_0 at saturation fall between (M_N^*/M_N)^3 and (M_N^*/M_N)^4

## Context

This work extends the modified quark-meson coupling approach introduced by Jin and collaborators (1996a, 1996b), who showed that a density-dependent bag constant could produce the large scalar and vector potentials needed to recover relativistic nuclear phenomenology. The authors demonstrate that the MQMC model is formally equivalent to QHD with a general nonlinear scalar potential, as developed by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and Serot and Walecka (1997). The study enables a systematic connection between quark-level bag parameters and hadronic mean-field potentials, and it shows that the bag constant at equilibrium is model-independent despite the freedom in parametrization, though the exact scaling relation between the bag constant and effective nucleon mass, such as the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991; Adami and Brown, 1993), cannot be uniquely determined from nuclear matter phenomenology alone.
