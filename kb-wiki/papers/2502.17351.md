# Three-body calculation of deuteron-nucleus scattering using microscopic global optical potential

> We test microscopic global optical potential in three-body calculations of deuteron-nucleus scattering. We solve Faddeev-type equations for three-body transition operators. We calculate differential cross section and analyzing power for the deuteron elastic scattering and breakup in collisions with ${}^{12}$C, ${}^{16}$O and ${}^{24}$Mg nuclei, and find a reasonable agreement with available experimental data. Comparison with respective predictions using phenomenological optical potentials reveals systematic deviations in particular kinematic regimes.

## Key claim

The microscopic global optical potential (MGOP) provides a reasonable description of deuteron-nucleus elastic scattering and breakup for light nuclei, but systematic deviations from phenomenological optical potentials appear in particular kinematic regimes, especially at forward angles.

## Method

The authors solved Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) three-body Faddeev-type equations for transition operators in momentum space, using the MGOP potentials (MGOP1 and MGOP2 based on relativistic-mean-field and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS models, respectively) as well as the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche (KD) and Weppner optical potentials for the nucleon-nucleus interactions. The neutron-proton interaction was described by the high-precision CD Bonn potential, and the proton-nucleus Coulomb force was included via screening and renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for deuteron elastic scattering on \({}^{12}\)C were calculated at deuteron beam energies \(E_d = 110\), 140, 170, and 200 MeV.
- Deuteron vector analyzing power for \(d + {}^{12}\)C elastic scattering was calculated at \(E_d = 170\) and 200 MeV.
- Differential cross section for \(d + {}^{16}\)O elastic scattering was calculated at \(E_d = 171\) MeV.
- Differential cross section for \(d + {}^{24}\)Mg elastic scattering was calculated at \(E_d = 170\) MeV.
- Semi-inclusive differential cross section for deuteron breakup on \({}^{12}\)C was calculated at \(E_d = 140\) MeV as a function of the \({}^{12}\)C scattering angle and energy in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context

This work extends the testing of the microscopic global optical potential developed by Furumoto et al. (2013) from nucleon-nucleus scattering to the more complicated few-body reactions of deuteron-nucleus scattering and breakup. It provides a comparison with predictions using phenomenological optical potentials by Koning and Delaroche (2003) and Weppner et al. (2009), and demonstrates that the MGOP approach is applicable to three-body reaction calculations, enabling future studies of deuteron-induced reactions on a wider range of nuclei.
