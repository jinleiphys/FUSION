# $ΛNN$ and $ΣNN$ systems at threshold

> We calculate the hypertriton binding energy and the $Λd$ and $Σd$ scattering lengths using baryon-baryon interactions obtained from a chiral constituent quark model. We study consistently the $ΛNN$ and $ΣNN$ systems analyzing the effect of the $Σ\leftrightarrow Λ$ conversion. Our interactions correctly predict the hypertriton binding energy. The $(I,J)=(0,3/2)$ $ΛNN$ channel is also attractive and it might have a bound state. From the condition of nonexistence of a (0,3/2) $ΛNN$ bound state, an upper limit for the spin-triplet $ΛN$ scattering length is obtained. We also present results for the elastic and inelastic $ΣN$ and $ΛN$ cross sections. The consistent description of the $ΣN$ scattering cross sections imposes a lower limit for the corresponding spin-triplet scattering lengths. In the $ΣNN$ system the only attractive channels are $(I,J)=(1,1/2)$ and $(0,1/2)$, the $(1,1/2)$ state being the most attractive one.

# ΛNN and ΣNN systems at threshold

## Key claim

The hypertriton binding energy is correctly predicted by a chiral constituent quark model, but the existence of a bound state in the (I,J)=(0,3/2) ΛNN channel is ruled out by the absence of experimental evidence, which imposes an upper limit on the spin-triplet ΛN scattering length of 1.68 fm; a lower limit of 1.41 fm is derived from the fit to hyperon-nucleon cross sections.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the coupled ΛNN–ΣNN system using baryon-baryon interactions derived from a chiral constituent quark model, including full Λ↔Σ conversion and the SU(3) scalar meson octet. Two-body t-matrices were constructed with S- and D-wave contributions and expanded in Legendre polynomials, and the integral equations were solved at threshold to obtain Λd and Σd scattering lengths and the hypertriton binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton binding energy: 0.124 MeV (experimental value 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV)
- ΛN spin-triplet scattering length upper limit: a_{1/2,1} ≤ 1.68 fm (from nonexistence of (0,3/2) ΛNN bound state)
- ΛN spin-triplet scattering length lower limit: a_{1/2,1} ≥ 1.41 fm (from fit to hyperon-nucleon cross sections)
- Λd scattering length for (I,J)=(0,1/2) channel: A_{0,1/2} = −17.2 fm
- Effective range for (I,J)=(0,1/2) channel: R_{0,1/2} = 3.82 fm
- Σd scattering length for (I,J)=(1,3/2) channel: A′_{1,3/2} = 0.36 + i 0.29 fm
- ΣN spin-triplet scattering lengths from smallest values tested: a′_{1/2,1} = 2.74 + i 1.22 fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model, previously successful in the nonstrange sector (Valcarce et al., 2005), to the strangeness S=−1 three-baryon systems. It improves upon earlier meson-exchange models (Rijken et al., 1999) and quark-model forces (Fujiwara et al., 2006) by simultaneously constraining the ΛNN and ΣNN systems with almost all available two- and three-body observables. The results enable refined determination of spin-singlet and spin-triplet hyperon-nucleon interaction strengths and provide predictions for Σd scattering lengths that can be tested at facilities such as KEK and FINUDA.
