# A Pionic Hadron Explains the Muon Magnetic Moment Anomaly

> A significant discrepancy exists between experiment and calculations of the muon's magnetic moment. We find that standard formulas for the hadronic vacuum polarization term have overlooked pionic states known to exist. Coulomb binding alone guarantees $π^+ π^-$ states that quantum mechanically mix with the $ρ$ meson. A simple 2-state mixing model explains the magnetic moment discrepancy for a mixing angle of order $α\sim 10^{-2}$. The relevant physical state is predicted to give a tiny observable bump in the ratio R(s) of $e^+ e^-$ annihilation at a low energy not previously searched. The burden of proof is reversed for claims that conventional physics cannot explain the muon's anomalous moment.

# A Pionic Hadron Explains the Muon Magnetic Moment Anomaly

## Key claim

A previously overlooked spin-1, isovector bound state of pions (called pi-rhonium, symbol π₂/ρ) that mixes with the ρ meson can explain the full discrepancy between experimental and theoretical values of the muon anomalous magnetic moment, provided the mixing angle is of order the fine structure constant α ∼ 10⁻². The claim is conditional on the existence of this state and its detection in e⁺e⁻ annihilation at energies just below 2mπ, a region not previously searched.

## Method

The authors identify that standard dispersion relation calculations for the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to aμ use a lower integration limit of s_min = 4mπ², which neglects bound pionic states below the π⁺π⁻ threshold. They construct a simple two-state mixing model between a bare P-wave Coulomb-bound π⁺π⁻ state and the bare ρ meson, diagonalizing the mass matrix to obtain physical states. The required e⁺e⁻ partial width of the mixed state is determined model-independently from the observed aμ discrepancy, and the mixing angle is then fixed by requiring consistency with the ρ meson's known leptonic width.

## Key numbers

- Experimental aμ = (11659208.0 ± 6.3) × 10⁻¹⁰; theoretical aμ = (11659180.4 ± 5.1) × 10⁻¹⁰; discrepancy of approximately 28 × 10⁻¹⁰ units
- Required partial width Γ(π₂/ρ → e⁺e⁻) = 28 eV to account for the full discrepancy
- Predicted physical mass m(π₂/ρ) = 279.11 MeV, with binding energy approximately 32 keV (65 times the Bohr-atom estimate)
- Mixing angle β = 0.46 degrees (sin β = 0.81 percent), with mixing interaction energy m_ix = 3.96 MeV
- Predicted ratio Γ(π₂/ρ → μ⁺μ⁻) ∼ 0.6 × Γ(π₂/ρ → e⁺e⁻) based on phase space
- ρ meson leptonic width Γ(ρ → e⁺e⁻) = 6.8 keV used as reference

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to resolve the muon g-2 discrepancy, which has been a precision test of QED and the Standard Model, with implications for Higgs mass constraints and new physics such as supersymmetry. The authors challenge the standard treatment of hadronic vacuum polarization used by Davier (2002) and others, which imposes the threshold at 4mπ². They argue that the DIRAC experiment's observation of S-wave pionium does not constrain the P-wave state they discuss. The paper reverses the burden of proof: rather than requiring new physics to explain the full anomaly, it demands that conventional physics calculations first account for the overlooked pi-rhonium contribution. If confirmed, this would open the door for a wider range of new physics contributions that need only explain part of the discrepancy.
