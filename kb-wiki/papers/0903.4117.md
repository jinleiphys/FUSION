# Isovector neutron-proton pairing with particle number projected BCS

> The particle number projected BCS (PBCS) approximation is tested against the exact solution of the SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin model for isovector pairing in a system of non-degenerate single particle orbits. Two isovector PBCS wave functions are considered. One is constructed as a single proton-neutron pair condensate, while the other corresponds to a product of a neutron pair condensate and a proton pair condensate. The PBCS equations are solved using a recurrence method and the analysis is performed for systems with an equal number of neutrons and protons distributed in a sequence of equally spaced 4-fold (spin-isospin) degenerate levels. The results show that although PBCS improves significantly over BCS, the agreement of PBCS with the exact solution is less satisfactory than in the case of the SU(2) Richardson model for pairing between like particles.

# Isovector neutron-proton pairing with particle number projected BCS

## Key claim
Particle number projected BCS (PBCS) improves significantly over standard BCS for isovector pairing in N=Z systems, but its agreement with the exact SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin solution is less satisfactory than the analogous PBCS treatment of like-particle pairing in the SU(2) model, because PBCS cannot correctly treat the part of the isovector force describing interactions among pairs not included in the condensate.

## Method
The authors test two PBCS trial wave functions against the exact solution of the SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin model for isovector pairing: one condensate formed by isovector proton-neutron pairs (PBCS0) and one product of separate neutron-neutron and proton-proton condensates (PBCS1). The PBCS equations are solved using recurrence relations for systems with equal numbers of neutrons and protons distributed in a sequence of equally spaced 4-fold degenerate levels (j=1/2) at quarter filling, with the pairing strength varied from weak to strong coupling.

## Key numbers
- Systems studied range from N=2 to N=12 proton-neutron pairs, corresponding to typical open-shell N=Z nuclei.
- For a system with 8 pairs at interaction strength g=0.4, PBCS1 gives an energy of -25.71 for the T_z=±1 part of the Hamiltonian compared to -0.84 for the T_z=0 part.
- For a system with 7 pairs at g=0.4, PBCS0 gives an interaction energy of -18.04 for the T_z=0 component compared to about -1.42 for T_z=±1.
- For a system of two proton-neutron pairs in four levels, mixing PBCS0 and PBCS1 yields correlation energies within 0.0002 of the exact result (e.g., exact 0.05587 vs. mixed 0.0557 at g=0.1; exact 3.66946 vs. mixed 3.6689 at g=1.0).
- All energies are given in units of the single-particle level spacing.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of isovector pairing in the one-level degenerate SO(5) model by Hecht and others, and tests PBCS against the exact non-degenerate SO(5) Richardson-Gaudin solution given by Links et al. and generalized by the authors' group. The results show that while PBCS captures the staggering of odd-even mass differences along the N=Z line that BCS misses (because BCS solutions A and B are degenerate), the accuracy is inferior to the SU(2) like-particle case studied by Sandulescu et al. The authors suggest that going beyond PBCS requires isospin projection or inclusion of quartet correlations, which they report as ongoing work.
