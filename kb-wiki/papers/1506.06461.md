# Reassessing nuclear matter incompressibility and its density dependence

> Experimental giant monopole resonance energies are now known to constrain nuclear incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter $K$ and its density slope $M$ at a particular value of sub-saturation density, the crossing density $ρ_c$. Consistent with these constraints, we propose a reasonable way to construct a plausible equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter in a broad density region around the saturation density $ρ_0$. Help of two additional empirical inputs, the value of $ρ_0$ and that of the energy per nucleon $e(ρ_0)$ are needed. The value of $K(ρ_0)$ comes out to be $211.9\pm 24.5$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear incompressibility at saturation density \(K(\rho_0)\) is \(211.9 \pm 24.5\) MeV, which is somewhat lower than the then-current accepted value of \(230 \pm 20\) MeV, and that this value can be derived from constraints at a sub-saturation crossing density \(\rho_c\) combined with empirical nuclear constants, without assuming a linear correlation between \(K_0\) and giant monopole resonance energies.

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using a non-relativistic framework with a momentum- and density-dependent interaction. They assume a linear density dependence for the effective nucleon mass and a power-law form for the rearrangement potential, then solve coupled equations at the crossing density \(\rho_c\) and saturation density \(\rho_0\) using empirical inputs for \(\rho_0\), the energy per nucleon \(e(\rho_0)\), and the effective mass \(m^*/m\). The incompressibility \(K(\rho_0)\) and its higher density derivatives are obtained from the resulting parameters.

## Key numbers

- \(K(\rho_0) = 211.9 \pm 24.5\) MeV, derived from the method using constraints at the crossing density.
- Crossing density \(\rho_c = 0.110 \pm 0.0008\) fm\(^{-3}\) (corresponding to \(0.71 \pm 0.005\) times the saturation density \(\rho_0\)).
- Input values: \(K_c = 35\) MeV and \(M_c = 1050\) MeV at the crossing density; the output values are \(K_c = 35.46\) MeV and \(M_c = 1051.8\) MeV.
- Empirical constants used: saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.155 \pm 0.008\) fm\(^{-3}\), energy per nucleon \(e(\rho_0) = -16.0 \pm 0.1\) MeV, and effective mass \(m^*/m \sim 0.8 \pm 0.2\).
- From a linear Taylor expansion using \(K_c\) and \(M_c\), the paper notes a simpler estimate \(K_0 \simeq 185 \pm 14.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Khan et al. (2012, 2013), who identified a universal crossing density \(\rho_c\) and showed that the giant monopole resonance centroid correlates strongly with the density slope \(M_c\) rather than directly with \(K_0\). The present paper resolves the apparent discrepancy between the lower \(K_0 \sim 200\) MeV favored by Sn and Cd isotope data and the higher \(K_0 \sim 230\) MeV from earlier analyses of \({}^{208}\)Pb, \({}^{90}\)Zr, and \({}^{144}\)Sm. The method enables construction of the equation of state over a broad density range (\(\rho_0/4 < \rho < 2.0\rho_0\)) and provides estimates of higher-order density derivatives of the energy, such as \(Q_0\) and \(N_0\).
