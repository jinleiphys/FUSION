# Statistical hadronization: successes and some open issues

> Hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions is well described in the framework of the statistical hadronization model, over a broad range of collision energies. We outline this for hadrons composed of light (u, d, s) and heavy (charm and beauty) quarks, discuss recent findings relevant for understanding the phase structure of QCD and formulate some open issues.

# Statistical hadronization: successes and some open issues

## Key claim
The statistical hadronization model (SHM) quantitatively describes hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions from sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV into the multi-TeV range, with chemical freeze-out parameters that agree with lattice QCD predictions for the chiral crossover transition at high energies; however, the detailed production mechanism for composite objects such as light nuclei and hyper-nuclei has not yet been fully established, and the degree of equilibration reached in small collision systems (pp, pA) has not been well determined.

## Method
The authors perform grand-canonical statistical hadronization model fits to measured hadron yields from central heavy-ion collisions across a range of collision energies, extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature T_CF, baryochemical potential mu_B, and fireball volume V. For charm quarks, the model is extended (SHMc) by introducing a charm fugacity g_c fixed by the measured total charm cross section, with charm quark number conserved during fireball evolution. Interactions among hadrons are treated via an S-matrix formulation using measured pion-nucleon phase shifts including repulsive and non-resonant components.

## Key numbers
- For central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: T_CF = 156.6 ± 1.7 MeV, mu_B = 0.7 ± 3.8 MeV, V = 4175 ± 380 fm^3 (per unit rapidity at midrapidity)
- Improved precision on mu_B at LHC: mu_B = 0.7 ± 0.45 MeV
- Fit quality with S-matrix treatment: chi^2_red = 16.9/19
- Limiting temperature from energy dependence parametrization: T_CF^lim = 158.4 ± 1.4 MeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD: 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (Bazavov 2018) and 158.0 ± 0.6 MeV (Borsanyi 2020)
- Freeze-out temperature from light nuclei: T_nuclei = 159 ± 5 MeV
- Charm fugacity for central Pb-Pb at LHC: g_c ≈ 30
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2 MeV, much less than T_CF ≈ T_c ≈ 157 MeV

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization approach developed by Braun-Munzinger (2003), Andronic (2005, 2017) and collaborators, showing that chemical freeze-out parameters at high energies coincide with the QCD phase boundary from lattice QCD calculations (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020). The SHMc extension for charm quarks, first proposed by Braun-Munzinger (2000), demonstrates that charm quarks thermalize in the QGP and that charmonia are unbound inside the QGP, contrary to the original suppression predictions of Matsui (1986). Open issues include the production mechanism for loosely bound light nuclei and hypernuclei, where the SHM description works for heavy systems but coalescence models better describe pp collisions, and the unexpected suppression of D_s^0 meson yields in small systems by about a factor of 2 compared to SHMc predictions.
