# Effect of revised $R_n$ measurements on extended Gari-Krumpelmann model fits to nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> The extended Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) model of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, in which the $ρ$, $ρ'$, $ω$, $ω'$ and $ϕ$ vector meson pole contributions evolve at high momentum transfer to conform to the predictions of perturbative QCD (pQCD), was recently shown to provide a very good overall fit to all the nucleon electromagnetic form factor (emff) data, including the preliminary $R_p$ and $R_n$ polarization data available in 2002, but excluding the older $G_{Ep}$ and $G_{En}$ differential cross section data that was inconsistent with the $R_p$ and $R_n$ data. The recently published final version of the polarization data of the electric to magnetic ratio $R_p$ differs little from the preliminary values for the former, but the new values of $R_n$ are midway between the preliminary values and those inferred from the differential cross section data and the Rosenbluth separation. A new fit of the parameters of the same model has been made with the final $R_p$ and $R_n$ data replacing the preliminary values and the addition of some new $R_n$ and $G_{Mn}$ data. Again there is a good fit to all the data when excluding the differentialcross section data that is inconsistent with the polarization data. This includes a very good fit of the $R_p$ data, which was not possible when the differential cross section $G_(En)$ data was used in place of the polarization $R_n$ data. Thus the change between the preliminary and final $R_n$ data, while substantial, has not impeded the good simultaneous fit to the neutron and proton data. The parameters, fit to the data and predictions of the new model are compared to those of the previous models. Low momentum structures that appear in some data analyses are partially reproduced by the model.

## Key claim

The extended Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) model can still provide a good simultaneous fit to both proton and neutron electromagnetic form factor data even after the final published values of the neutron electric-to-magnetic ratio \(R_n\) are substituted for the preliminary 2002 values, despite the fact that the new \(R_n\) values are midway between the preliminary polarization values and those inferred from older differential cross section data.

## Method

The author refitted the 12 free parameters of the extended GK model (GKex(02S)) using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, replacing the preliminary 2002 \(R_p\) and \(R_n\) polarization data with the final published versions from Perdrisat (2005) and Madey (2003), and adding new \(R_n\) data from Warren (2004) and \(G_{Mn}\) data from Kubon (2002). The model incorporates \(\rho\), \(\rho'\), \(\omega\), \(\omega'\), and \(\phi\) vector meson poles with a QCD cutoff \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) fixed at 0.15 GeV, and excludes older differential cross section data for \(G_{Ep}\) and \(G_{En}\) that are inconsistent with the polarization data.

## Key numbers

- Total \(\chi^2\) for the new GKex(05) fit is 241.3 for 185 data points, compared to 248.0 for GKex(02S) and 361.0 for GKex(01).
- \(R_n\) \(\chi^2\) improved from 5.6 (GKex(02S)) to 1.1 (GKex(05)) for 5 data points.
- \(R_p\) \(\chi^2\) remained nearly unchanged: 10.4 (GKex(02S)) vs. 10.3 (GKex(05)) for 22 data points.
- The \(\rho'\) coupling product \(g_{\rho'}/f_{\rho'}\) changed from 0.0401 (GKex(02S)) to 0.007208 (GKex(05)), with \(\kappa_{\rho'}\) fixed at 12.0.
- The \(\omega'\) coupling \(g_{\omega'}/f_{\omega'}\) changed from 0.2552 (GKex(02S)) to 0.164 (GKex(05)), with \(\kappa_{\omega'}\) changing from 1.4916 to -2.973.
- The QCD cutoff \(\Lambda_2\) (helicity flip suppression) changed from 2.7891 GeV (GKex(02S)) to 2.6115 GeV (GKex(05)).
- Low-momentum structure appears near \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) (GeV/\(c^2\)) in both \(G_{Mp}\) and \(G_{Mn}\) data and is partially reproduced by the model.

## Context

This work extends the earlier GKex(02S) fit of Lomon (2002) by incorporating the final published polarization data from Perdrisat (2005) and Madey (2003), which replaced the preliminary values used previously. The new fit maintains the good simultaneous description of proton and neutron data that was not possible when older differential cross section data for \(G_{En}\) were used instead of polarization \(R_n\) data. The results enable future tests against upcoming Jefferson Lab data (E02-013) at higher momentum transfers up to 3.5 GeV\(^2/c^2\), and may help clarify low-momentum structures near \(Q^2 \approx 0.2\) (GeV/\(c^2\)) that have been associated with pion cloud effects by Friedrich and Walcher (2003).
