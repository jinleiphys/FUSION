# A Resonance Model for pi N -> Y K and pi Delta -> Y K Reactions for Kaon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> In a resonance model the reactions pi N -> Y K and pi Delta -> Y K are studied. For the reactions pi N -> Lambda K and pi Delta -> Lambda K, the resonances N(1650)(J^P=1/2^-), N(1710)(1/2^+) and N(1720)(3/2^+) are included as intermediate states. For the reactions pi N -> Sigma K, the resonances N(1710)(1/2^+), N(1720)(3/2^+) and Delta(1920)(3/2^+) are considered, while for the pi Delta -> Sigma K reactions the intermediate resonances are N(1710)(1/2^+) and N(1720)(3/2^+). Besides these resonances in the s-channel, the t-channel K^*(892) exchanges are also taken into account as a smooth background. The relevant coupling constants for the meson-baryon vertices are obtained (except for Delta(1920)) from the experimental decay branching ratios of the relevant resonances. All isospin channels of the pi N -> Y K and pi Delta -> Y K cross sections are calculated. By comparing the calculated results with the available experimental data, we find that the total cross sections of the pi N -> Y K reactions can be explained by the resonance model. The pi Delta -> Y K cross sections, for which no experimental data are available, are predicted theoretically. Parametrizations of the calculated total cross sections for all different isospin channels are given for the use of kaon productions in heavy ion collisions. The differential cross sections are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the total cross sections for the reactions pi N -> Y K (where Y is Lambda or Sigma) can be explained by a resonance model that includes specific intermediate baryon resonances and t-channel K*(892) exchange, while the cross sections for pi Delta -> Y K are predicted theoretically with no experimental data available for comparison.

## Method

The authors constructed a resonance model where pi N and pi Delta reactions proceed through intermediate baryon resonances (N(1650), N(1710), N(1720) for Lambda K channels; N(1710), N(1720), Delta(1920) for Sigma K channels) plus t-channel K*(892) exchange as a smooth background. Coupling constants for meson-baryon vertices were determined from experimental decay branching ratios of the relevant resonances, using SU(2) isospin symmetry. All isospin channels were calculated coherently from the square of the sum of resonance amplitudes, with form factors included at each vertex to account for hadron finite size.

## Key numbers

- N(1650) resonance: mass approximately 1650 MeV, full width approximately 150 MeV, with branching fractions used of 70% to N pi, 7% to Lambda K, and 5% to Delta pi
- N(1710) resonance: mass approximately 1710 MeV, full width approximately 100 MeV, with branching fractions used of 15% to N pi, 15% to Lambda K, 17.5% to Delta pi, and 6% to Sigma K
- N(1720) resonance: mass approximately 1720 MeV, full width approximately 150 MeV, with branching fractions used of 15% to N pi, 6.5% to Lambda K, 10% to Delta pi, and 3.5% to Sigma K
- Delta(1920) resonance: mass approximately 1920 MeV, full width approximately 200 MeV, with branching fractions used of 12.5% to N pi and 2% to Sigma K
- K*(892) meson: mass approximately 892 MeV, full width 49.8 MeV, with 100% branching to K pi
- The invariant mass range studied is from the threshold for YK production to about 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Randrup and Ko (1987) for kaon production in heavy ion collisions by providing a resonance-based calculation of pi B -> Y K cross sections rather than relying on symmetry assumptions or simple parametrizations. The study improves upon the isospin-averaged parametrization of Cugnon and Lombard (1984) by calculating all isospin channels separately, which is necessary for isospin-dependent transport models like BUU/VUU and QMD. The results enable more reliable calculation of kaon yields in heavy ion collisions and provide the foundation for studying the N Delta -> N Y K channel, which was identified as the main contributor to kaon production in heavy ion collisions.
