# Two, three, many body systems involving mesons

> In this talk we show recent developments on few body systems involving mesons. We report on an approach to Faddeev equations using chiral unitary dynamics, where an explicit cancellation of the two body off shell amplitude with three body forces stemming from the same chiral Lagrangians takes place. This removal of the unphysical off shell part of the amplitudes is most welcome and renders the approach unambiguous, showing that only on shell two body amplitudes need to be used. Within this approach, systems of two mesons and one baryon are studied, reproducing properties of the low lying $1/2^+$ states. On the other hand we also report on multirho and $K^*$ multirho states which can be associated to known meson resonances of high spin.

## Key claim

The authors show that in Faddeev equations using chiral unitary dynamics, the off-shell part of two-body amplitudes cancels with three-body contact terms from the same chiral Lagrangians, leaving only on-shell two-body amplitudes as input; this renders the approach unambiguous, but the cancellation is proven in the SU(3) limit.

## Method

The authors solve Faddeev equations for systems of two mesons and one baryon using chiral unitary dynamics, where the two-body t-matrices for meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions are calculated from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian with dimensional regularization. For multi-meson systems, they use the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, studying successive additions of rho mesons or K* mesons with aligned spins. The three-body T-matrix is evaluated as a function of the invariant mass of a two-body subsystem and the total three-body energy, with resonances identified as peaks in |T|^2.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to two-body meson-baryon systems (Jido 2003, Oset 2001) and three-nucleon problems (Epelbaum) to three-body systems of two mesons and one baryon, reproducing low-lying 1/2+ baryon states. The multi-rho and K* multi-rho studies build on the finding that ρρ interaction is strong enough to bind into the f2(1270) (Molina 2008, Geng 2008), and the N*(1924) state predicted here was suggested to explain the peak around 1920 MeV in γp → K+Λ reactions (SAPHIR, Jefferson Lab). The approach enables systematic study of multihadron states that challenge the conventional three-quark picture of baryons, with predictions testable at BELLE, COMPASS, and WASA/COSY.
