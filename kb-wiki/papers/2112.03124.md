# First Glimpse into the Kaon Gluon Parton Distribution Using Lattice QCD

> In this work, we present the first results on the gluon parton distribution of the kaon from lattice quantum chromodynamics. We carry out the lattice calculation at pion mass around 310~MeV and two lattice spacings, 0.15 and 0.12~fm, using $2+1+1$-flavor HISQ ensembles generated by MILC Collaboration. The kaon correlators are calculated using clover fermions and momentum-smearing sources with maximum boost momentum around 2~GeV and high statistics (up to 324,000 measurements). We study the dependence of the resulting reduced Ioffe-time pseudo-distributions at multiple boost momenta and lattice spacings. We then extract the kaon gluon distribution function in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $μ= 2$~GeV, neglecting the mixing between the gluon and singlet-quark sectors. Our results at the smaller lattice spacing are consistent with phenomenological determinations.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the x-dependent kaon gluon parton distribution, using the pseudo-PDF method at a single pion mass of 310 MeV and two lattice spacings, while neglecting the mixing between the gluon and singlet-quark sectors; the results at the finer lattice spacing (0.12 fm) are consistent with Dyson-Schwinger equation determinations within statistical uncertainties for x > 0.15.

## Method

The authors used clover valence fermions on 2+1+1-flavor HISQ ensembles generated by the MILC Collaboration at two lattice spacings (0.12 and 0.15 fm) with pion mass around 310 MeV. They calculated kaon two-point and three-point gluon correlators using momentum-smearing sources with maximum boost momentum around 2 GeV and high statistics (up to 324,000 measurements), then extracted ground-state matrix elements via two-state simultaneous fits. The reduced Ioffe-time pseudo-distributions were constructed and fitted using a phenomenologically motivated parametrization matched to the gluon PDF in the MSbar scheme at mu = 2 GeV through the pseudo-PDF matching kernel.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 311.1(6) MeV (a12m310 ensemble) and 319.1(31) MeV (a15m310 ensemble)
- Kaon mass: 528.0(5) MeV (a12m310) and 531.7(23) MeV (a15m310)
- Lattice spacings: 0.1207(11) fm (a12m310) and 0.1510(20) fm (a15m310)
- Maximum boost momentum: 2.14 GeV (a12m310) and 2.05 GeV (a15m310)
- Total two-point correlator measurements: 324,160 (a12m310) and 21,600 (a15m310)
- Kaon <x^2>_g/<x>_g = 0.0779(94) and <x^3>_g/<x>_g = 0.0187(42) at mu = 2 GeV in the MSbar scheme
- Pion <x^2>_g/<x>_g = 0.092(15) and <x^3>_g/<x>_g = 0.0250(75) at mu = 2 GeV in the MSbar scheme

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-PDF approach previously applied to nucleon gluon PDFs by Fan et al. (2020) and pion gluon PDFs by Fan et al. (2021) to the kaon for the first time, where prior lattice calculations were limited to valence-quark distributions (Lin et al., 2020). The results are compared with Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations by Cui et al. (2020) and show consistency within uncertainties, enabling future comparisons with experimental measurements planned at the Electron-Ion Collider, COMPASS++/AMBER, and the Electron-Ion Collider in China.
