# Production and absorption of exotic bottomonium-like states in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the production and absorption of $Z_b (10610)$ and $Z_b ^{\prime}(10650)$ states in a hadronic medium, via the processes $\bar{B}^{(*)} B^{(*)} \rightarrow πZ_b ^{(\prime)} $ and the corresponding inverses reactions. We use effective field Lagrangians based on an $SU(4)$-extension of the hidden gauge formalism to account for the couplings between light and heavy mesons, and a phenomenological Lagrangian involving the $ B^{\ast} B^{(\ast)} Z_b ^{(\prime)}$ vertices. The absorption cross sections are found to be much larger than the production ones.

## Key claim

The absorption cross sections for the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b'(10650)$ states in a hadronic medium are found to be much larger than the production cross sections, but still comparable to them; this contrasts with the case of the $X(3872)$, where absorption cross sections are about two orders of magnitude larger than production ones, suggesting that the $Z$ states have slightly better survival chances in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors compute leading-order cross sections for the processes $\bar{B}^{(*)} B^{(*)} \rightarrow \pi Z_b^{(\prime)}$ and their inverse reactions using effective field Lagrangians. The couplings between light and heavy mesons are described by an $SU(4)$-extension of the hidden gauge formalism, while the $B^* B^{(*)} Z_b^{(\prime)}$ vertices are described by phenomenological Lagrangians assuming the $Z_b$ and $Z_b'$ are $S$-wave molecular states of $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$, respectively. The isospin-spin-averaged cross sections are evaluated as functions of center-of-mass energy, with and without the inclusion of monopole form factors.

## Key numbers

- $Z_b^+$ production cross sections: $\sim 3 \times 10^{-3}$ to $5 \times 10^{-2}$ mb for $10.80 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 11.05$ GeV, with the $\bar{B} B \rightarrow \pi Z_b$ channel largest by a factor of 2-3.
- $Z_b^{\prime +}$ production cross sections: $\sim 6 \times 10^{-4}$ to $2 \times 10^{-2}$ mb for $10.82 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 11.05$ GeV, with the $\bar{B}^* B^* \rightarrow \pi Z_b'$ channel largest by a factor of 2-3.
- $Z_b^+$ absorption cross sections: $\sim 8 \times 10^{-2}$ to $6 \times 10^{-1}$ mb for $10.80 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 11.05$ GeV, with the $\pi Z_b \rightarrow \bar{B}^* B^*$ channel largest by a factor of 3-4.
- $Z_b^{\prime +}$ absorption cross sections: $4 \times 10^{-2}$ to $3 \times 10^{-1}$ mb for $10.82 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 11.05$ GeV, with the $\pi Z_b' \rightarrow \bar{B}^* B^*$ channel largest by a factor of 2-3.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{ZBB^*} = 13.10^{+0.83}_{-0.88}$ GeV, $g_{Z'B^*B^*} = 1.04 \pm 0.1$.
- $Z_b$ production cross sections are smaller than absorption cross sections by a factor of 2-10; $Z_b'$ production cross sections are smaller than absorption cross sections by a factor of 8-10.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the $X(3872)$ state (Abreu et al., 2016) to the charged bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b'(10650)$, which were discovered by the Belle Collaboration in $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays. The results are compared to those from Heavy-Meson Effective Theory (HMET) calculations (Guo et al., 2011), where the present cross sections are smaller by about a factor of 10, attributed to differences in the coupling constants. The finding that absorption dominates over production, but less dramatically than for the $X(3872)$, enables future estimates of the survival probability of these exotic states in heavy ion collisions, which could help discriminate between molecular and tetraquark interpretations.
