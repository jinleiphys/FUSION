# Thermalization of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Magnetic Field at Strong Coupling

> We study thermalization of strongly coupled gauge theory plasma in the presence of magnetic field using the AdS/CFT correspondence. We utilize the falling energy-shell model as a holographic description of gauge theory plasma undergoing thermalization, and find the effect of magnetic field on thermalization time in various space-time dimensions. Our results demonstrate that magnetic field universally hastens thermalization of strongly coupled gauge theory plasma.

# Thermalization of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Magnetic Field at Strong Coupling

## Key claim
Magnetic field universally hastens thermalization of strongly coupled gauge theory plasma, but the effect is less significant for the magnetic field values relevant at RHIC and LHC. The conclusion is drawn from a simplified holographic model using the falling thin-shell approximation in AdS/CFT, with the caveat that the magnetic field is modeled via R-symmetry of N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory rather than QCD's actual magnetic field.

## Method
The authors model thermalization as the gravitational collapse of a thin, neutral, homogeneous mass shell falling from the UV boundary of AdS space toward the IR region, eventually forming a black hole. They join two static AdS solutions with magnetic field, one at zero temperature (representing the vacuum) and one at finite temperature (representing thermalized plasma), across the shell's trajectory using the Israel junction condition. For the 5-dimensional AdS case (dual to 4-dimensional QGP), they use an exact magnetically charged AdS5 black hole solution with three orthogonal U(1) magnetic fields of equal magnitude to preserve isotropy and homogeneity. The shell's equation of motion is derived and solved numerically, with the thermalization time defined as the Eddington-Finkelstein time when the shell passes through its black hole horizon.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC with late-time temperature T=300 MeV and B=0: horizon radius z_H=0.209 fm, mass parameter m=525.5 fm^{-4}, constant of motion C=263.7 fm^{-4}
- For RHIC with T=300 MeV and B=2.06 fm^{-2} (0.08 GeV^2): z_H=0.209 fm, m=527.6 fm^{-4}, C=262.6 fm^{-4}
- For RHIC with T=300 MeV and B=51.61 fm^{-2} (2 GeV^2): z_H=0.162 fm, m=1469.1 fm^{-4}, C=331.0 fm^{-4}
- Initial shell position set by saturation scale: for RHIC Q_s=0.87 GeV=4.42 fm^{-1}, for LHC Q_s=1.23 GeV=6.24 fm^{-1}
- Late-time temperatures fixed at T=300 MeV for RHIC and T=400 MeV for LHC

## Context
This work extends the falling energy-shell model of thermalization developed by Lin (2008, 2013) to include external magnetic fields, using magnetically charged AdS black hole solutions from Donos (2011) for the 5-dimensional case and Romans (1991) for the 4-dimensional case. The results are in qualitative agreement with the contemporaneous work of Fuini (2015), who addressed a similar question using a different holographic setup. The study enables future comparison between strong-coupling and weak-coupling thermalization in magnetic fields, where at weak coupling a strong magnetic field causes dimensional reduction to one-dimensional dynamics via lowest Landau levels.
