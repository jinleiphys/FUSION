# Electron exchange model potential: Application to positronium-helium scattering

> The formulation of a suitable nonlocal model potential for electron exchange is presented, checked with electron-hydrogen and electron-helium scattering, and applied to the study of elastic and inelastic scattering and ionization of ortho positronium (Ps) by helium. The elastic scattering and the $n = 2$ excitations of Ps are investigated using a three-Ps-state close-coupling approximation. The higher ($n\ge 3$) excitations and ionization of Ps atom are treated in the framework of Born approximation with present exchange. Calculations are reported of phase shifts, and elastic, Ps-excitation, and total cross sections. The present target elastic total cross section agrees well with experimental results at thermal to medium energies.

# Electron exchange model potential: Application to positronium-helium scattering

## Key claim

The authors present a nonlocal model potential for electron exchange that removes the nonorthogonality defect from the close-coupling equations, and when applied to positronium-helium scattering with a small empirical adjustment of the parameter alpha in the prefactor, yields total cross sections that agree with experimental measurements at thermal to medium energies, unlike previous static-exchange calculations which severely overestimated the cross sections at low energies.

## Method

The authors derive a model exchange potential by applying an average-value prescription to the momentum-space exchange amplitude, factorizing it into a product of projectile and target form factors with a denominator containing averaged kinetic energies. They test this potential on electron-hydrogen and electron-helium scattering, then apply it to ortho-positronium-helium scattering using a three-Ps-state (1s, 2s, 2p) close-coupling approximation in momentum space, with higher Ps excitations (n ≥ 3) and ionization treated in the first Born approximation with the same exchange model.

## Key numbers

- Present three-Ps-state elastic cross section at 0.068 eV: 2.70 π a₀², compared to previous static-exchange result of 14.4 π a₀² from Sarkar and Ghosh (1997)
- Present first Born elastic cross section at 0.068 eV: 13.73 π a₀², compared to 131.9 π a₀² from Sarkar and Ghosh (1997)
- Present first Born elastic cross section at 1 eV: 3.7 π a₀², compared to 12.5 π a₀² from Sarkar and Ghosh (1997)
- Ps-ionization cross section at 20 eV: 4.10 π a₀² (largest partial cross section at medium energies)
- Ps-ionization cross section at 100 eV: 1.74 π a₀²
- Total cross section shows a minimum near the Ps(2s) excitation threshold (5.1 eV), with a sharp increase between 5.1 and 6.8 eV as inelastic channels open
- Modified prefactor uses (0.88 α_κj)² in the denominator to achieve better agreement with experiment

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between previous static-exchange calculations for Ps-He scattering (Fraser 1968; Barker and Bransden 1968; Sarkar and Ghosh 1997) which gave cross sections far exceeding experimental measurements (Garner, Laricchia, and Ozen 1996; Skalsey et al. 1998), and the 22-coupled-pseudo-state calculation (McAlinden, MacDonald, and Walters 1996) which did not reproduce the low-energy minimum. The model exchange potential extends the Ochkur-Rudge approach for electron scattering to positronium impact, and the resulting cross sections bridge the experimental data from thermal energies (around 1 eV) to medium energies (up to 100 eV), predicting a minimum in the total cross section near the Ps(2s) threshold that had been suggested by unpublished work of Peach. The method enables practical coupled-channel calculations for many-electron targets by avoiding the nonorthogonality and overcompleteness problems of full antisymmetrization.
