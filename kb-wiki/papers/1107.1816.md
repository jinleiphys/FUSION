# Elastic Scattering of $^6$He based on a Cluster Description

> Elastic scattering observables (differential cross section and analyzing power) are calculated for the reaction $^6$He(p,p)$^6$He at projectile energies starting at 71 MeV/nucleon. The optical potential needed to describe the reaction is derived describing $^6$He in terms of a $^4$He-core and two neutrons. The Watson first order multiple scattering ansatz is extended to accommodate the internal dynamics of a composite cluster model for the $^6$He nucleus scattering from a nucleon projectile. The calculations are compared with the recent experiments at the projectile energy of 71 MeV/nucleon. In addition, differential cross sections and analyzing powers are calculated at selected higher energies.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Watson first-order multiple scattering formalism to incorporate a cluster description of \(^6\)He (an \(\alpha\)-core plus two valence neutrons) into the optical potential for elastic proton scattering, and find that this cluster model alone does not resolve the "\(A_y\) problem" at 71 MeV/nucleon; only when the free nucleon-nucleon t-matrix is modified by a mean-field potential for the \(\alpha\)-core does the analyzing power become negative at larger angles, partially matching the data.

## Method

The optical potential for \(p+^6\)He elastic scattering is derived from the Watson multiple scattering expansion in first order, treating \(^6\)He as a three-body cluster (an \(\alpha\)-core and two neutrons). The cluster optical potential is constructed by summing separate optical potentials for the \(\alpha\)-core and for each valence neutron, linked by a correlation density \(\rho_{corr}\) that constrains the relative Jacobi momenta between clusters. The resulting six-dimensional folding integral is evaluated via Monte Carlo integration, using the COSMA density for \(^6\)He and the nonlocal Nijm-I nucleon-nucleon potential; for the \(\alpha\)-core, the free NN t-matrix is optionally modified by a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field.

## Key numbers

- The COSMA density for \(^6\)He is fitted to a charge radius of 1.77 fm and a matter radius of 2.57 fm.
- At 71 MeV/nucleon, the cluster model with a mean-field-modified NN t-matrix for the \(\alpha\)-core produces an analyzing power that turns negative at \(60^\circ\).
- The shift in the minima of the analyzing power between \(^4\)He and \(^6\)He is approximately 0.25 fm\(^{-1}\) at both 71 MeV and 200 MeV.
- For \(p+^4\)He at 200 MeV, the optical potential description is adequate up to a momentum transfer of 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\) for both differential cross section and analyzing power.
- At 71 MeV and 100 MeV, the single-scattering optical potential describes the \(p+^4\)He differential cross section only up to a momentum transfer of about 1.75 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model approach for \(p+^6\)He scattering pioneered by Crespo (2006) and Crespo (2007), who used the KMT formulation and Born approximation at higher energies (717 and 297 MeV/nucleon). It directly addresses the "\(A_y\) problem" observed by Uesaka (2010) at 71 MeV/nucleon, where simple folding models (e.g., Weppner (2000), Gupta (2000)) failed to predict the negative analyzing power. The cluster formulation enables future extension to four- or five-body halo nuclei such as \(^8\)He, and provides predictions at 100 and 200 MeV/nucleon for upcoming measurements at the upgraded RIKEN facility.
