# Nucleon-deuteron capture with chiral potentials

> Present day chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to N3LO and three nucleon forces at N2LO are used to analyze nucleon-deuteron radiative capture at deuteron lab energies below E_d= 100 MeV. The differential cross section and the deuteron analyzing powers A_y(d) and A_{yy} are presented and compared to data. The theoretical predictions are obtained in the momentum-space Faddeev approach using the nuclear electromagnetic current operator with exchange currents introduced via the Siegert theorem. The chiral forces provide the same quality of data description as a combination of the two-nucleon AV18 and the three-nucleon Urbana IX interactions. However, the different parametrizations of the chiral potentials lead to broad bands of predictions.

## Key claim

Chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to N3LO and three-nucleon forces at N2LO provide a description of nucleon-deuteron radiative capture data that is comparable in quality to the combination of the AV18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, but the different parametrizations of the chiral potentials produce broad bands of predictions that in some cases prevent a decisive conclusion about the quality of the data description.

## Method

The authors solved the momentum-space Faddeev equations for the nucleon-deuteron scattering state and computed the radiative capture transition amplitude using the nuclear electromagnetic current operator with exchange currents introduced via the Siegert theorem. They used chiral NN potentials at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO, and at N2LO included the consistent chiral three-nucleon force, performing calculations for several combinations of the regularization cut-off parameters (Lambda, tilde-Lambda) covering the possible ranges.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section for neutron-deuteron capture calculated at neutron lab energies of 9.0 MeV and 10.8 MeV.
- Differential cross section for proton-deuteron capture calculated at deuteron lab energies of 29 MeV and 95 MeV.
- Deuteron vector analyzing power A_y(d) calculated at deuteron lab energies of 17.5 MeV, 29 MeV, 45 MeV, and 95 MeV.
- Deuteron tensor analyzing power A_yy calculated at deuteron lab energies of 17.5 MeV, 29 MeV, 45 MeV, and 95 MeV.
- Cut-off parameter combinations used: (450,500), (600,500), (550,600), (450,700), (600,700) in units of MeV/c, with NLO using (400,500) and (550,500) and N3LO using (600,600) instead of (600,500).

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory, previously used for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering by Epelbaum et al. (2002) and deuteron breakup by Kistryn et al., to electromagnetic processes with three nucleons for the first time. It provides the first chiral predictions for the n+d -> gamma + 3H and p+d -> gamma + 3He reactions, and the results are compared to the standard AV18+Urbana IX combination of nuclear forces. The study indicates that future work requires a consistent chiral electromagnetic current operator and extension to N3LO with three-nucleon forces to reduce the theoretical uncertainty bands.
