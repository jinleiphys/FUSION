# An electroweak basis for neutrinoless double $β$ decay

> A discovery of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay would be profound, providing the first direct experimental evidence of $ΔL=2$ lepton number violating processes. While a natural explanation is provided by an effective Majorana neutrino mass, other new physics interpretations should be carefully evaluated. At low--energies such new physics could manifest itself in the form of color and $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_{Y}$ invariant higher dimension operators. Here we determine a complete set of electroweak invariant dimension--9 operators, and our analysis supersedes those that only impose $U(1)_{em}$ invariance. Imposing electroweak invariance implies: 1) a significantly reduced set of leading order operators compared to only imposing $U(1)_{em}$ invariance; and 2) other collider signatures. Prior to imposing electroweak invariance we find a minimal basis of 24 dimension-9 operators, which is reduced to 11 electroweak invariant operators at leading order in the expansion in the Higgs vacuum expectation value. We set up a systematic analysis of the hadronic realization of the 4-quark operators using chiral perturbation theory, and apply it to determine which of these operators have long-distance pion enhancements at leading order in the chiral expansion. We also find at dimension--11 and dimension--13 the electroweak invariant operators that after electroweak symmetry breaking produce the remaining $ΔL=2$ operators that would appear at dimension--9 if only $U(1)_{em}$ is imposed.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that only 11 of the 24 possible dimension-9 operators contributing to neutrinoless double beta decay are invariant under the full electroweak symmetry at leading order in the Higgs vacuum expectation value expansion, contradicting previous analyses that only imposed electromagnetic gauge invariance and found a larger set of operators.

## Method

The author constructs a complete set of SU(3)c × SU(2)L × U(1)Y invariant dimension-9, -11, and -13 operators that violate lepton number by two units, starting from a minimal basis of 24 color and electromagnetic invariant operators. The electroweak invariant operators are then matched to chiral perturbation theory to determine which operators produce long-distance pion enhancements at leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

- 24 dimension-9 operators found in the SU(3)c × U(1)em invariant basis prior to imposing electroweak invariance
- 11 electroweak invariant operators at leading order in the expansion in the Higgs vacuum expectation value (v ≈ 247 GeV)
- 8 scalar 4-quark operators and 8 vector 4-quark operators identified in the complete electromagnetic and color invariant basis
- 15 electroweak invariant operators at dimension-9 (LM1 through LM11, with LM2, LM4, LM6, LM9, LM11 being color-octet variants)
- Additional operators appear at dimension-11 (requiring two Higgs insertions) and dimension-13 (requiring four Higgs insertions) to produce the remaining low-energy dimension-9 operators

## Context

This work extends previous classifications by Babu (2001) that omitted Lorentz structure classification, and by Pas (2000) and Prezeau (2003) that only imposed SU(3)c × U(1)em invariance rather than full electroweak gauge invariance. The paper corrects the literature by including color-octet operators missing from Prezeau (2003) and the "super-formula" of Pas (2000), and by eliminating redundant tensor operators. The electroweak invariant basis enables systematic comparisons between neutrinoless double beta decay experiments and LHC searches for same-sign dilepton, lepton plus missing energy, and dijet plus missing energy signatures, as well as proper treatment of operator mixing under QCD and electroweak renormalization.
