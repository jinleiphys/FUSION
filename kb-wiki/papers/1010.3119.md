# Minimum Shear Viscosity over Entropy Density at Phase Transition? --- A Counterexample

> The ratio eta/s, shear viscosity (eta) to entropy density (s), reaches its local minimum at the (second order) phase transition temperature in a wide class of systems. It was suspected that this behavior might be universal. However, a counterexample is found in a system of two weakly self-interacting real scalar fields with one of them condensing at low temperatures while the other remains in the symmetric phase. There is no interaction between the two fields. The resulting eta/s is monotonically decreasing in temperature despite the phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper presents a counterexample to the suspected universal behavior that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) reaches a local minimum at a second-order phase transition temperature, by constructing a system of two non-interacting weakly self-interacting real scalar fields where eta/s is monotonically decreasing in temperature despite the phase transition.

## Method
The authors study a system (case III) composed of two independent real scalar fields with Lagrangian L1 + L2, where one field (phi1) has positive mass squared and remains in the symmetric phase, while the other (phi2) has negative mass squared and undergoes spontaneous symmetry breaking below a critical temperature Tc. Because there is no interaction between the two fields, the total entropy density s_III = s_I + s_II and the total shear viscosity eta_III = eta_I + eta_II are additive, allowing reliable computation to leading order in the small coupling lambda using kinetic theory and the Hartree approximation.

## Key numbers
- The high-temperature expansion of eta/s for case III gives eta_III/s_III → (c1'/lambda^2)(1 + c2'(mu1^2 + mu2^2)/(2T^2) + O(T^{-3})), where c1' = c1 > 0 and c2' = c2 > 0, implying monotonic decrease at large T when mu1^2 + mu2^2 > 0.
- The effective coupling lambda_eff decreases smoothly from 2lambda at T=0 to lambda at Tc and stays constant above Tc, varying only by a factor of 2.
- The Hartree approximation is reliable when T >> lambda^(1/2) Tc, and quantum fluctuations affect only the region |T - Tc|/Tc ≲ O(lambda), which can be made arbitrarily small by reducing lambda.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Chen 2007) where eta/s develops a cusp at Tc for second-order phase transitions in weakly interacting real scalar field theories, a behavior also seen in QCD at zero baryon chemical potential (Csernai 2006, Chen 2006) and in nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions (Chen 2007). The counterexample contradicts the speculation that this feature is universal, and differs from holographic models (Buchel 2010) where eta/s becomes monotonically increasing below Tc with 1/N corrections, and from sigma model calculations (Dobado 2009) that found a local minimum below Tc using large couplings. The result enables further investigation into whether eta/s can serve as a probe for phase transitions, particularly for locating the QCD critical point.
