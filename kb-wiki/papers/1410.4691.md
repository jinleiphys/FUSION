# Dispersive analysis of the pion transition form factor

> We analyze the pion transition form factor using dispersion theory. We calculate the singly-virtual form factor in the time-like region based on data for the $e^+e^-\to 3蟺$ cross section, generalizing previous studies on $蠅,蠒\to3蟺$ decays and $纬蟺\to蟺蟺$ scattering, and verify our result by comparing to $e^+e^-\to蟺^0纬$ data. We perform the analytic continuation to the space-like region, predicting the poorly-constrained space-like transition form factor below 1 GeV, and extract the slope of the form factor at vanishing momentum transfer $a_蟺=(30.7\pm0.6)\times 10^{-3}$. We derive the dispersive formalism necessary for the extension of these results to the doubly-virtual case, as required for the pion-pole contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.

## Key claim

The authors use dispersion theory to predict the singly-virtual pion transition form factor in both time-like and space-like regions, extracting the slope at zero momentum transfer with a precision that depends on the consistency of the underlying $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ cross-section data, which show tensions between different experiments.

## Method

The authors reconstruct the $\gamma^*\to 3\pi$ amplitude using a once-subtracted dispersive representation with an Omn猫s function that resums $P$-wave $\pi\pi$ rescattering, fixing the subtraction function $a(q^2)$ via a dispersively improved Breit-Wigner parametrization fitted to $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ cross-section data. They then insert this amplitude into an unsubtracted dispersion relation for the pion transition form factor, using the charged pion vector form factor and the sum rule at $q^2=0$ to enforce the chiral anomaly normalization, and analytically continue the result to space-like kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Slope of the form factor at vanishing momentum transfer: $a_\pi = (30.7 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3}$
- Chiral anomaly normalization for $\gamma\to 3\pi$: $\alpha = \frac{F_{3\pi}}{3} \times (1.066 \pm 0.010)$, where $F_{3\pi} = e/(4\pi^2 F_\pi^3)$ with $F_\pi = 92.2\ \text{MeV}$
- Fit to SND+BaBar $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ data below $1.1\ \text{GeV}$ yields reduced $\chi^2$ of $1.01$ to $1.04$; fit to the HLMNT compilation yields reduced $\chi^2$ of $6.33$ to $6.36$
- Pion vector form factor radius: $\langle r^2\rangle_\pi^V \sim 0.435\ \text{fm}^2$

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework previously applied to $\omega,\phi\to 3\pi$ decays and $\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi$ scattering (Kubis and collaborators, 2004-2012) to the general pion transition form factor, providing input for the dispersive description of hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon $g-2$ initiative (Bern group, 2012-2014). The results enable improved predictions for the pion-pole contribution to HLbL and can be tested against upcoming space-like data from BESIII, while the formalism is designed for future extension to the doubly-virtual case needed for the full HLbL calculation.
