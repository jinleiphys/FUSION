# Exposing strangeness: projections for kaon electromagnetic form factors

> A continuum approach to the kaon and pion bound-state problems is used to reveal their electromagnetic structure. For both systems, when used with parton distribution amplitudes appropriate to the scale of the experiment, Standard Model hard-scattering formulae are accurate to within 25% at momentum transfers $Q^2 \approx 8\,$GeV$^2$. There are measurable differences between the distribution of strange and normal matter within the kaons, e.g. the ratio of their separate contributions reaches a peak value of $1.5$ at $Q^2 \approx 6\,$GeV$^2$. Its subsequent $Q^2$-evolution is accurately described by the hard scattering formulae. Projections for kaon and pion form factors at timelike momenta beyond the resonance region are also presented. These results and projections should prove useful in planning next-generation experiments.

## Key claim

Standard Model hard-scattering formulae for pion and kaon elastic form factors are accurate to within 25% at momentum transfers Q² ≈ 8 GeV², but only when used with parton distribution amplitudes computed at the probe scale rather than the conformal-limit form; the domain of reliability for these formulae begins around Q² ≈ 8 GeV² and improves with increasing ln Q².

## Method

The authors use a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation to compute kaon and pion bound-state properties and electromagnetic form factors. The calculation employs a single interaction kernel with one parameter, generalised Nakanishi spectral representations for quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and consistent unamputated dressed-quark-photon vertices. The resulting form factors are compared with leading-order, leading-twist hard-scattering formulae using parton distribution amplitudes determined from the same framework.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of strange-quark to up-quark contributions to the K⁺ form factor reaches a peak value of 1.5 at Q² ≈ 6 GeV², then returns logarithmically to unity at larger Q².
- The kaon parton distribution amplitude is parameterised as φ_K(x) = n_{αβ} x^α (1-x)^β with α = 0.39(4), β = 0.31(4), giving ⟨(2x-1)²⟩_K = 0.271(5) and ⟨(2x-1)⟩_K = 0.0296(9).
- The charged kaon-to-pion form factor ratio rises logarithmically to f_K²/f_π² ≈ 1.42 as Q² → ∞.
- The neutral kaon charge radius squared is r_{K⁰}² = -(0.21 fm)², compared to the experimental value -(0.24 ± 0.08 fm)² from Molzon (1978).
- The kaon mass m_K = 0.49 GeV and decay constant f_K = 0.11 GeV are obtained with current-quark masses m_u = 4.7 MeV and m_s = 112 MeV at ζ = 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the continuum bound-state method previously applied to the pion by Chang (2013) to produce unified predictions for both pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors across the entire spacelike domain. The results enable the first realistic comparison with the hard-scattering formulae of Lepage and Brodsky (1979, 1980), establishing the domain where those formulae become reliable. The projections for timelike form factors beyond the resonance region should guide next-generation experiments at facilities such as those proposed by Horn (2016) and the JLab 12 GeV program, and can be validated by lattice-QCD simulations such as those of Koponen (2017) and Chambers (2017).
