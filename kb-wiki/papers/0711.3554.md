# Heavy Ion Dynamics and Neutron Stars

> Some considerations are reported, freely inspired from the presentations and discussions during the Beijing Normal University Workshop on the above Subject, held in July 2007. Of course this cannot be a complete summary but just a collection of personal thougths aroused during the meeting.

## Key claim

This paper is a personal summary of discussions at the 2007 Beijing Normal University Workshop on Heavy Ion Dynamics and Neutron Stars, not a complete review; the author emphasizes that a unique equation of state (EOS) describing the hadron-quark transition has not yet been reached, and that the link between heavy-ion collision studies and neutron star properties is underappreciated by the radioactive ion beam community.

## Method

The author reports on presentations and discussions from the workshop, covering theoretical and experimental approaches to the nuclear equation of state (EOS) in both hadronic and partonic phases. Topics include non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations for neutron star structure, transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions (e.g., isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics), and analyses of observables such as nucleon ratios, collective flows, and isoscaling to constrain the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses up to more than 2 solar masses (\(M_\odot\)) are possible in a pure hadronic picture with large symmetry repulsion.
- Hyperon matter formation leads to maximum neutron star masses between a lower limit around \(1.3 M_\odot\) and an upper limit below \(1.5 M_\odot\).
- The direct URCA fast neutrino cooling process (proton fraction \(Z/A \simeq 0.11\)) can be reached at densities around \(2-3\rho_0\).
- The symmetry energy density dependence is constrained to \(E_{sym} \approx (\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma\) with \(0.7 < \gamma < 1.1\) around saturation.
- The effect of symmetry stiffness on collective radial flow thresholds at Fermi energies is negligible, of the order of \(1\%\) for a large variation of stiffness.
- The Lanzhou Cooling-Storage-Ring facility extracted, stored, cooled, and accelerated beams up to 1 AGeV: \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) (\(2\times10^9\) pps), \(^{36}\mathrm{Ar}\) (\(1.4\times10^9\) pps), and \(^{129}\mathrm{Xe}\) (\(10^8\) pps).

## Context

This summary extends the discussion of the connection between heavy-ion collision dynamics and neutron star properties, building on prior work by Lombardo (2004) on 3-body forces in BHF, by Page (2006) on high-mass neutron star observations, and by Haensel (2007) on neutron star evolutionary scenarios. It enables future cross-checks between terrestrial heavy-ion experiments and astrophysical observations to constrain the symmetry energy at both sub-saturation and supra-saturation densities, and highlights open questions about the hadron-quark transition, hyperon interactions, and the need for more data on mass-radius relations for neutron stars.
