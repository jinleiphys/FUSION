# Present Status of Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The basic ideas and methods of chiral perturbation theory are briefly reviewed. I discuss the recent attempts to build an effective Lagrangian in the resonance region and summarize the known large-N_C constraints on the low-energy chiral couplings.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) provides a rigorous low-energy effective field theory of QCD, but its predictive power at next-to-next-to-leading order (O(p^6)) is severely limited by the large number (90 even-parity + 23 odd-parity) of unknown low-energy constants, making further progress dependent on estimating these couplings from QCD dynamics rather than from symmetry alone.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of the ChPT Lagrangian order by order in momentum and quark-mass expansions, starting from the O(p^2) Lagrangian with two constants (f and B0) and proceeding to O(p^4) with ten couplings L_i. It then introduces Resonance Chiral Theory (RChT), which couples nonets of vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar resonances to Goldstone bosons, and uses short-distance QCD constraints (such as form-factor vanishing at infinite momentum transfer and Weinberg sum rules) to fix resonance couplings in the large-N_C limit.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant at lowest order: f = f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- O(p^4) coupling L_2 = (1.4 ± 0.3) × 10^{-3} (from K_e4 and pi-pi scattering)
- O(p^4) coupling L_9 = (6.9 ± 0.7) × 10^{-3} (from pion vector charge radius)
- O(p^4) coupling L_10 = (-5.5 ± 0.7) × 10^{-3} (from pi → e nu gamma)
- Single-resonance approximation predictions: L_2 = 1.8 × 10^{-3}, L_9 = 7.1 × 10^{-3}, L_10 = -5.4 × 10^{-3} (using M_V = 0.77 GeV, M_S = 1.0 GeV, f = 92 MeV)
- Short-distance constraint: sum_i F_{Vi} G_{Vi} = f^2 (vector form factor vanishing at infinity)
- Weinberg conditions: sum_i (F_{Vi}^2 - F_{Ai}^2) = f^2 and sum_i (M_{Vi}^2 F_{Vi}^2 - M_{Ai}^2 F_{Ai}^2) = 0

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) who established the O(p^4) ChPT Lagrangian, and the resonance saturation approach of Ecker, Gasser, Pich, and de Rafael (1989) which first linked resonance exchanges to the L_i couplings. The paper enables systematic inclusion of O(p^6) effects by showing how short-distance QCD constraints from two-point and three-point functions can determine the many unknown higher-order couplings, with recent two-loop calculations by Colangelo, Bijnens, and others already providing the non-local chiral logarithm contributions.
