# Color-Neutral Superconducting Quark Matter

> We investigate the consequences of enforcing local color neutrality on the color superconducting phases of quark matter by utilizing the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model supplemented by diquark and the t'Hooft six-fermion interactions. In neutrino free matter at zero temperature, color neutrality guarantees that the number densities of u, d, and s quarks in the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase will be equal even with physical current quark masses. Electric charge neutrality follows as a consequence and without the presence of electrons. In contrast, electric charge neutrality in the less symmetric 2-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase with ud pairing requires more electrons than the normal quark phase. The free energy density cost of enforcing color and electric charge neutrality in the CFL phase is lower than that in the 2SC phase, which favors the formation of the CFL phase. With increasing temperature and neutrino content, an unlocking transition occurs from the CFL phase to the 2SC phase with the order of the transition depending on the temperature, the quark and lepton number chemical potentials. The astrophysical implications of this rich structure in the phase diagram, including estimates of the effects from Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that enforcing local color neutrality in color-superconducting quark matter, using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, leads to equal number densities of u, d, and s quarks in the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase even with physical strange quark masses, and that the free energy cost of neutrality is lower in the CFL phase than in the 2-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase; however, these results depend on the specific NJL model parameters and a three-momentum cutoff, and the authors caution that quantitative results near the cutoff should be viewed with caution.

## Method

The authors employed the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model supplemented by diquark and t'Hooft six-fermion interactions, with a three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 600 MeV and current quark masses m0u = m0d = 5 MeV and m0s = 140 MeV. They solved self-consistently for the thermodynamic potential, dynamical quark masses, and pairing gaps in the CFL and 2SC phases, imposing color and electric charge neutrality by adjusting the chemical potentials mu_3, mu_8, and mu_Q. They also performed an analytic expansion to leading order in m_s^2/mu and Delta^2/mu to understand the free energy costs, and extended the calculation to finite temperature and finite neutrino chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The strange current quark mass used is m0s = 140 MeV, with light quark masses m0u = m0d = 5 MeV, and the ultraviolet cutoff is Lambda = 600 MeV.
- The NJL coupling constants are G_S * Lambda^2 = 1.84, G_D * Lambda^5 = 12.4, and G_DIQ = 3 G_S / 4.
- In the CFL phase at zero temperature, the leading-order analytic result for the color chemical potential is mu_8(CFL) = -(1/(2*sqrt(3))) * (m_s^2 / mu) + O[m_s^4/mu^3, m_s^2 Delta^2/mu^3].
- The free energy density cost of enforcing color and electric charge neutrality in the CFL phase is Delta_Omega_CFL = (5 m_s^4) / (72 pi^2) + O[m^6/mu^2, m^4 Delta^2/mu^2].
- In the 2SC phase at zero temperature, the leading-order analytic result for the electric charge chemical potential is mu_Q(2SC) = -(1/2) * (m_s^2 / mu) - (1/3) * (Delta^2 / mu) + O[m_s^4/mu^3, Delta^2 m_s^2/mu^3].
- The free energy density cost of neutrality in the 2SC phase is Delta_Omega_2SC = (1/(8 pi^2)) * (m_s^4 + (4/3) Delta^2 m_s^2 + (4/3) Delta^4) + O[m_s^6/mu^2, Delta^6/mu^2, Delta^2 m_s^4/mu^2].
- At fixed mu = 460 MeV and zero neutrino chemical potential, the CFL-to-2SC transition occurs at T approximately 17 MeV, with baryon densities n_B = 1.06 fm^{-3} in the CFL phase and n_B = 0.94 fm^{-3} in the 2SC phase.
- At T = 0, the CFL-to-2SC transition occurs at a lepton chemical potential mu_Le approximately 150 MeV.
- The latent heat density along the first-order phase boundary lies in the range 2-15 MeV/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analysis of color neutrality in superconducting quark matter by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and the earlier work by Iida and Baym (2000), but goes further by using a self-consistent NJL model that retains terms to all orders in m_s and by establishing the phase diagram at finite temperature and lepton content relevant for proto-neutron stars. The results complement the analytic findings of Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and provide quantitative predictions for the free energy costs of neutrality, the CFL-to-2SC unlocking transition, and the role of kaon condensation as discussed by Bedaque and Schafer (2001). The phase diagram at finite temperature and neutrino chemical potential enables future studies of quark matter in proto-neutron star interiors, though the authors note that the low-density region likely requires a hadronic phase description.
