# Time scales for fission at finite temperature

> The concept of the "transient effect" is examined in respect of a "mean first passage time". It is demonstrated that the time the fissioning system stays inside the barrier is much larger than suggested by the transient time, and that no enhancement of emission of neutrons over that given by Kramers' rate formula ought to be considered.

# Time scales for fission at finite temperature

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "transient effect", an apparent delay in fission that has been taken to imply additional neutron emission beyond Kramers' rate, is not a genuine physical effect but an artifact of how the initial time is chosen; the mean first passage time (MFPT) shows that the system spends much longer inside the barrier than the transient time suggests, and therefore no enhancement of neutron emission over Kramers' rate formula should be considered.

## Method

The authors examine the time-dependent current across the fission barrier by solving the Fokker-Planck equation (and its overdamped limit, the Smoluchowski equation) for different initial conditions, and compare these results to the MFPT calculated from the adjoint Smoluchowski equation. They perform Langevin simulations for a locally harmonic oscillator potential constructed from two oscillators joined with a smooth first derivative, and compute the MFPT analytically for a cubic potential using the standard formula for overdamped motion.

## Key numbers

- Temperature \(T = 3\) MeV, barrier height \(E_b = 8\) MeV, oscillator frequency \(\hbar\varpi_a = 1\) MeV, and dissipation strength \(\eta_a = 1\) used in the Langevin simulations.
- The MFPT to reach the saddle point \(Q_b\) is exactly half the asymptotic value of the MFPT, which equals the mean fission lifetime \(\tau_f\).
- The Kramers rate formula is given as \(\Gamma_{\rm{K}} = \frac{\hbar\varpi_a}{2\pi} \exp\left(-\frac{E_b}{T}\right) \left(\sqrt{1+\eta_b^2} - \eta_b\right) = \frac{\hbar}{\tau_{\rm{K}}}\).
- For the cubic potential, the MFPT is shown for ratios \(T/E_b = 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1.0\), demonstrating insensitivity to the exit point for small and large \(Q_{\rm{ex}}\).

## Context

This work extends the critique of the Bohr-Wheeler formula originally raised by Kramers (1940), and directly addresses the "transient effect" proposed by Grangé et al. (1980s) and others (e.g., Bhatt, Grangé, and Weidenmüller) as an explanation for excess neutron emission observed in fission experiments. The paper contradicts the interpretation that a transient delay justifies additional neutron emission beyond the ratio \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_{\rm{K}}\), and argues that statistical codes based on time-independent reaction theory should use Kramers' rate without transient corrections. The findings also question the separate concept of a saddle-to-scission time \(\tau_{\rm{ssc}}\) introduced by Hofmann and Nix, suggesting that the MFPT framework provides a more consistent description.
