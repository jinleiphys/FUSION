# Realistic Calculation of the hep Astrophysical Factor

> The astrophysical factor for the proton weak capture on 3He is calculated with correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics bound and continuum wave functions corresponding to a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions. The nuclear weak charge and current operators have vector and axial-vector components, that include one- and many-body terms. All possible multipole transitions connecting any of the p-3He S- and P-wave channels to the 4He bound state are considered. # Realistic Calculation of the hep Astrophysical Factor

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for the proton weak capture on 3He is predicted to be 10.1 × 10^{-20} keV b at a center-of-mass energy of 10 keV, a factor of five larger than the standard-solar-model value, but far smaller than the factor of 20-30 enhancement that would be required to fit the observed Super-Kamiokande excess of high-energy recoil electrons. The result is inherently model dependent, but the authors argue it is unlikely that the model dependence is large enough to accommodate a drastic increase in the prediction.

## Method

The authors used correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) bound and continuum wave functions for the four-nucleon system, corresponding to a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions (AV18/UIX). The nuclear weak charge and current operators included one-body terms (with 1/m^2 relativistic corrections) and many-body terms, with the axial current treated via the transition-correlation-operator (TCO) scheme for Δ-isobar excitation. All multipole transitions connecting the p-3He S-wave (1S0, 3S1) and P-wave (3P0, 1P1, 3P1, 3P2) channels to the 4He bound state were included, and the matrix elements were computed with Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- S-factor at 10 keV center-of-mass energy: 10.1 × 10^{-20} keV b (full calculation including one- and many-body currents, S+P waves)
- S-factor at 0 keV: 9.64 × 10^{-20} keV b (full calculation, S+P waves)
- S-factor at 5 keV: 9.70 × 10^{-20} keV b (full calculation, S+P waves)
- P-wave transitions contribute about 40% of the calculated S-factor
- One-body only S-factor at 10 keV: 29.3 × 10^{-20} keV b (S+P waves)
- Many-body axial currents from Δ excitation reduce the 3S1 capture S-factor by more than a factor of four
- The 3P0 channel S-factor calculated with only the C0 multipole (long-wavelength limit) is 2.2 × 10^{-20} keV b, but including the L0 multipole reduces it to 0.82 × 10^{-20} keV b due to destructive interference
- The standard-solar-model value is 2.3 × 10^{-20} keV b (Schiavilla 1992)
- The 4He binding energy calculated with the CHH method is within 1% of Green's function Monte Carlo results
- The AV18/UIX Hamiltonian predicts p-3He singlet scattering length a_s = 11.5 fm and triplet scattering length a_t = 9.13 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier ab initio microscopic calculations of Schiavilla (1992) and Carlson (1991) by using more accurate CHH wave functions corresponding to the latest generation of realistic interactions (AV18/UIX), and by including the axial charge operator, vector charge and current operators, and 1/m^2 relativistic corrections that were previously neglected. The predicted S-factor of 10.1 × 10^{-20} keV b is a factor of five larger than the standard-solar-model value used in Bahcall and Krastev (1998), but far smaller than the factor of 20-30 enhancement that would be needed to explain the Super-Kamiokande excess of recoil electrons above 14 MeV. The calculation demonstrates that the long-wavelength approximation (q=0 limit) used in previous studies leads to inaccurate results, and that P-wave capture channels contribute significantly (about 40%) to the S-factor, a fact not sufficiently appreciated in earlier work except by Horowitz (1999).
