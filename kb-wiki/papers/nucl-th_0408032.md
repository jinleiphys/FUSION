# The two-proton shell gap in Sn isotopes

> We present an analysis of two-proton shell gaps in Sn isotopes. As the theoretical tool we use self-consistent mean-field models, namely the relativistic mean-field model and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach, both with two different pairing forces, a delta interaction (DI) model and a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI). We investigate the influence of nuclear deformation as well as collective correlations and find that both effects contribute significantly. Moreover, we find a further significant dependence on the pairing force used. The inclusion of deformation plus correlation effects and the use of DDDI pairing provides agreement with the data.

# The two-proton shell gap in Sn isotopes

## Key claim

The two-proton shell gap in Sn isotopes is extremely sensitive to deformation effects, collective ground-state correlations, and the choice of pairing force; only when all three effects are included together with a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) pairing do self-consistent mean-field calculations agree with experimental data, whereas deformation alone (which was sufficient for Pb isotopes) is insufficient for Sn.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using both the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, employing multiple parameterizations (SkM*, SLy6, SkI3, SkO for SHF; NL-Z2, NL3, PC-F1 for RMF). Pairing was added at the BCS level with Lipkin-Nogami correction, using either a zero-range delta interaction (DI) or a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI). For selected Skyrme forces, collective ground-state correlations from quadrupole vibrations were included via the generator-coordinate method with Gaussian overlap approximation, solving a collective Bohr-Hamiltonian to obtain correlated ground-state energies.

## Key numbers

- The experimental neutron pairing gaps used for fitting were: Δn(112Sn) = 1.41 MeV, Δn(120Sn) = 1.39 MeV, Δn(124Sn) = 1.31 MeV, and for protons: Δn(136Xe) = 0.98 MeV, Δn(144Sa) = 1.25 MeV; gaps were fitted to better than 1% in average, with trends reproduced within at least 10% precision.
- Switching from spherical to deformed mean-field calculations lowered the two-proton shell gap by about 1-2 MeV in the transitional region.
- Including collective ground-state correlations further lowered the two-proton shell gap by about 1-2 MeV in the critical region.
- Switching from DI to DDDI pairing reduced the two-proton shell gap by about 1 MeV, which together with correlations brought results for SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3 into agreement with experimental data.
- The effective masses for the Skyrme forces spanned from m*/m = 0.58 (SkI3) to 0.90 (SkO), which influenced the onset of deformation effects.

## Context

This work extends a previous study by Ben02 on two-proton shell gaps in Pb isotopes, where deformation effects alone were sufficient to explain the experimental data. The present study finds that for Sn isotopes, deformation is insufficient and collective ground-state correlations plus the choice of pairing force (DDDI versus DI) are also required. The results enable systematic testing of mean-field models and pairing prescriptions against subtle observables, and the authors note that much more evidence is needed to support the conclusion that DDDI pairing is more realistic, referencing discussions by Avd99, Dob02a, and Dug04a.
