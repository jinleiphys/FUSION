# Connecting scaling with short-range correlations

> We reexamine several issues related to the physics of scaling in electron scattering from nuclei. A basic model is presented in which an assumed form for the momentum distribution having both long- and short-range contributions is incorporated in the single-particle Green function. From this one can obtain saturation of nuclear matter for an NN interaction with medium-range attraction and short-range repulsion, and can obtain the density-density polarization propagator and hence the electromagnetic response and scaling function. For the latter, the shape of the scaling function and how it approaches scaling as a function of momentum transfer are both explored.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple model incorporating both long- and short-range contributions to the nucleon momentum distribution can simultaneously reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and yield a scaling function that approaches scaling from above at large momentum transfers, a feature most models fail to explain.

## Method

The authors assume a parametrized form for the momentum distribution n(k) with a high-momentum tail and incorporate it into the single-particle Green function for infinite, homogeneous, non-relativistic nuclear matter. From this Green function they compute the density-density polarization propagator, the longitudinal electromagnetic response, and the scaling function, using a schematic nucleon-nucleon potential with short-range repulsion and medium-range attraction. The seven parameters of the model (three for the momentum distribution, four for the potential) are adjusted to reproduce the experimental saturation density, binding energy, and compression modulus of nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy: (E/A)_min = -15.68 MeV at k_F = 1.23 fm^-1
- Compression modulus at saturation: kappa_min = 13.8 MeV
- Experimental nuclear matter density: 0.17 fm^-3
- Momentum distribution parameters: alpha = 0.2, beta_1 = 0.4, beta_2 = 4
- Potential parameters: U_0 = 2.5 GeV (repulsive core), V_0 = 53 MeV (attractive part), a = 0.465 fm (core radius), b = 2.10 fm (attractive range)
- Hole effective mass: m*_(h) = 0.50 m, with A_(h) = -77.16 MeV and B_(h) = 41.10 MeV fm^2
- Particle effective mass: m*_(p) = 0.91 m, with A_(p) = -43.09 MeV and B_(p) = 22.72 MeV fm^2
- Hole energy at k=0: epsilon_0^(h) = -77.90 MeV
- Coulomb Sum Rule asymptotic value reached at q approximately 4.6 fm^-1 (correlated) versus 2.46 fm^-1 (Fermi gas)

## Context

This work extends the scaling analysis of Barbaro (2009) by explicitly connecting the momentum distribution that yields nuclear matter saturation to the scaling function obtained from electron scattering. It contrasts with the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation approach of Caballero (2010), which extracts the momentum distribution from experimental scaling data, whereas the present model derives both the momentum distribution and scaling function from a common theoretical framework. The results enable future studies of how short-range correlations influence scaling violations, particularly the asymmetry and approach-to-scaling behavior observed experimentally but not captured by simpler Fermi gas models.
