# Hidden Symmetry and Georgi's Vector Limit

> We study the generation of vector meson masses in the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model of low energy QCD. After demoting the HLS to a hidden global symmetry (HGS), the rho is explicitly shown to be massless for any value of the HS parameter "a" and the chiral partner of the pion appears as the Goldstone boson associated with the spontaneous breaking of the HGS, closely resembling the predictions of Georgi's vector limit.

## Key claim

By demoting the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) to a hidden global symmetry (HGS), the rho meson becomes massless for any value of the HLS parameter \(a\) and without requiring the rho coupling \(g\) to vanish, contradicting Georgi's assumption that the vanishing mass implies a vanishing coupling.

## Method

The authors start from the HLS model of low-energy QCD, where vector mesons are gauge bosons of a local hidden symmetry and acquire mass via the Higgs-Kibble mechanism. They then demote the hidden local symmetry to a hidden global symmetry (HGS), making the scalar compensator field \(S\) a physical Goldstone boson (the chiral partner of the pion, called \(\sigma\)). They compute the rho meson's physical mass by including the tree-level contribution from the Lagrangian mass term and the dressing of the rho propagator by the \(\sigma\) meson, finding a cancellation in the BKY limit \(f_S^2 = a f_P^2\).

## Key numbers

* The Lagrangian mass term for the vector mesons is \(M_{\cal L}^2 = a f_P^2 g^2\) (Eq. Lm).
* The BKY limit is defined by \(f_S^2 = a f_P^2\) (Eq. f_S).
* Georgi's vector limit corresponds to \(a = 1\), giving \(f_\sigma = f_P\).
* The physical rho mass squared is \((m^{\rm phys}_\rho)^2 = a g^2 f_P^2 - a g^2 f_P^2 \frac{a f_P^2}{f_S^2}\) (Eq. vanish), which vanishes in the BKY limit for any value of \(a\).

## Context

This work examines Georgi's (1990s) "vector limit" assumption that the rho mass vanishes along with its coupling, using the HLS model of Bando et al. (1980s). It extends prior studies of the rho mass behavior under certain conditions (e.g., HarShi, BR_MRS_LG_others) and the Farhi-Susskind (1981) and Peyranere (1980s) analyses of scalar dressing effects. The result enables a scenario where the rho is massless without requiring a vanishing coupling, and it clarifies that previous calculations using gauge freedom to remove the rho-sigma interaction (e.g., HY, HarShi, RCW) miss this cancellation.
