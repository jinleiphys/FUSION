# Pentaquark $Θ^+$ in nuclear matter and $Θ^+$ hypernuclei

> We study the properties of the $Θ^+$ in nuclear matter and $Θ^+$ hypernuclei within the quark mean-field (QMF) model, which has been successfully used for the description of ordinary nuclei and $Λ$ hypernuclei. With the assumption that the non-strange mesons couple only to the $u$ and $d$ quarks inside baryons, a sizable attractive potential of the $Θ^+$ in nuclear matter is achieved as a consequence of the cancellation between the attractive scalar potential and the repulsive vector potential. We investigate the $Θ^+$ single-particle energies in light, medium, and heavy nuclei. More bound states are obtained in $Θ^+$ hypernuclei in comparison with those in $Λ$ hypernuclei.

# Pentaquark $\Theta^+$ in nuclear matter and $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei

## Key claim

Within the quark mean-field (QMF) model, the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark experiences a net attractive potential in nuclear matter of about -50 MeV at normal density, arising from a large cancellation between an attractive scalar potential (-420 MeV) and a repulsive vector potential (+370 MeV). This result depends on the assumption that non-strange mesons couple only to the $u$ and $d$ quarks inside baryons, and that the $\Theta^+$ has isospin $I=0$; the parity of the $\Theta^+$ remains ambiguous experimentally and is not determined by this calculation.

## Method

The authors use the quark mean-field (QMF) model, in which constituent quarks satisfy a Dirac equation with scalar confinement and couple directly to $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ meson fields. The $\Theta^+$ is treated as a five-quark state ($uudd\bar{s}$) with the strange antiquark not coupling to non-strange mesons; its effective mass in medium is computed from the quark energies and spin correlations, with parameters fixed by fitting nuclear matter properties and free baryon masses ($M_\Theta = 1540$ MeV, $M_N = 939$ MeV). Self-consistent mean-field equations are then solved for $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei, yielding single-particle energies for the $\Theta^+$ in various nuclei without introducing any new adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- $\Theta^+$ potential in nuclear matter at normal density $\rho_0 = 0.145\;\mathrm{fm^{-3}}$: $U^\Theta = U^\Theta_S + U^\Theta_V = -50\;\mathrm{MeV}$, with $U^\Theta_S = -420\;\mathrm{MeV}$ and $U^\Theta_V = +370\;\mathrm{MeV}$
- For comparison, the $\Lambda$ potential at the same density is $U^\Lambda = -24\;\mathrm{MeV}$
- Reduction of effective $\Theta^+$ mass at normal matter density: $\sim 420\;\mathrm{MeV}$
- Increase of $\Theta^+$ radius at normal matter density: $\sim 5\%$ (all baryon radii increase by $4\%$ to $6\%$)
- In $^{41}_{\Theta}\mathrm{Ca}$, the $1s_{1/2}$ $\Theta^+$ single-particle energy is $-28.1\;\mathrm{MeV}$; at the center of this hypernucleus, the scalar potential is $U_S^\Theta \sim -559\;\mathrm{MeV}$, vector potential $U_V^\Theta \sim +515\;\mathrm{MeV}$, and Coulomb potential $U_C \sim +11\;\mathrm{MeV}$, yielding a net $U^\Theta \sim -33\;\mathrm{MeV}$
- A $3\%$ reduction of $g_\omega^\Theta$ changes the $1s_{1/2}$ $\Theta^+$ single-particle energy in $^{41}_{\Theta}\mathrm{Ca}$ from $-28.1\;\mathrm{MeV}$ to $-38.7\;\mathrm{MeV}$ (a $\sim 10.6\;\mathrm{MeV}$ increase in binding)
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = 313\;\mathrm{MeV}$, $m_s = m_{\bar{s}} = 490\;\mathrm{MeV}$; confining potential strength $k = 700\;\mathrm{MeV/fm^2}$
- Nuclear matter properties used to fix parameters: equilibrium density $\rho_0 = 0.145\;\mathrm{fm^{-3}}$, binding energy $E/A = -16.3\;\mathrm{MeV}$, incompressibility $k = 280\;\mathrm{MeV}$, effective nucleon mass $M_N^* = 0.63 M_N$, symmetry energy $a_{\mathrm{sym}} = 35\;\mathrm{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the quark mean-field model previously applied to ordinary nuclei and $\Lambda$ hypernuclei by Shen and Toki (2002, 2003) to the then-newly discovered pentaquark $\Theta^+$. The predicted $\Theta^+$ potential of $-50$ MeV at normal density is compatible with results from other approaches: Oset et al. (2004) obtained $-60$ to $-120$ MeV using meson-exchange self-energy calculations, and Tsushima (2004) predicted $-40$ to $-90$ MeV via QCD sum rules. The QMF model yields less deeply bound $\Theta^+$ single-particle energies than those obtained in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with simple quark-model scaling of couplings (Zhong et al., 2004), because the effective $\sigma$-$\Theta^+$ coupling in QMF is not a constant but depends self-consistently on the $\sigma$ field and is smaller than the naive $4/3$ ratio. The results motivate experimental searches for $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei via $K^+$-induced reactions, as proposed by Oset et al. (2004), where bound states with high angular momentum might produce observable peaks.
