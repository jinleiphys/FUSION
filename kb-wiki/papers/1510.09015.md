# Testing the $SU(4)$ degeneracy after low-mode removal with $J=2$ mesons

> Recent lattice results on Dirac low-mode removed $J=1,2$ mesons and $J=\frac{1}{2}$ baryons reveal the appearance of a new $SU(4)$ symmetry of confinement. Here the degeneracy of all $J=2$ iso-vector states within an irreducible representation of $SU(4)$ after low-mode removal is demonstrated. The $SU(4)$ symmetry contains $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ and $U(1)_A$ symmetries as subgroups and mixes all compontents $u_L,u_R,d_L,d_R$ of the two-flavor Dirac field. It implies the vanishing of the interaction of quarks with the color-magnetic field, which is shown by using the QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that after removing the low-lying Dirac eigenmodes from valence quark propagators, all J=2 iso-vector meson states become mass degenerate, indicating the emergence of an SU(4) symmetry of confinement that goes beyond the SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_A symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD with dynamical Overlap fermion gauge configurations on a 16^3 × 32 lattice with spacing a=0.118 fm and pion mass 289(2) MeV, removing up to k=30 low-lying Dirac eigenmodes from valence quark propagators via spectral truncation. They computed correlation matrices for J=2 tensor mesons using the variational method and analyzed the eigenvalues and masses as functions of the number of removed modes. The degeneracy patterns were compared to predictions from SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_A restoration versus full SU(4) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 16^3 × 32, lattice spacing a = 0.118 fm
- Pion mass: 289(2) MeV
- Ensemble: 83 gauge field configurations
- Up to k = 30 low-lying Dirac eigenmodes removed, corresponding to eigenvalue cutoff up to 225 MeV
- After k = 20 modes removed (energy cutoff 125 MeV), SU(4) symmetry becomes visible in the spectrum

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies by Denissenya et al. (2014) on J=1 mesons and J=1/2 baryons after low-mode removal, which first revealed the appearance of SU(4) symmetry. The results are consistent with the theoretical identification of SU(4) as the symmetry of confinement by Glozman (2014, 2015), which implies that the interaction of quarks with the color-magnetic field vanishes in SU(4)-symmetric hadrons, leaving only the confining color-Coulomb interaction. The paper enables further investigation of whether even higher symmetries connecting states of different spin J might exist, which would require analysis of finite volume effects.
