# Rapidity dependence of the photon to pion production ratio in high energy collisions

> We investigate rapidity dependence of the ratio of photon and pion production cross sections in high energy proton (deuteron) - nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC. This ratio, and its rapidity dependence can be a sensitive probe of high density QCD (Color Glass Condensate) dynamics and shed further light on the role of saturation physics at RHIC and LHC.

# Rapidity dependence of the photon to pion production ratio in high energy collisions

## Key claim

The ratio of fragmentation photon to neutral pion production cross sections in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions shows a rise at forward rapidities, which can serve as a probe of Color Glass Condensate (saturation) dynamics; however, the CGC-based results are most reliable at very forward rapidities where quark production dominates over gluon production, and at mid-rapidity the approach is questionable because the proton saturation scale is too small and gluon contributions to photon production (formally a higher-order effect) are numerically large.

## Method

The authors compute single inclusive photon and pion production cross sections using the hybrid approach of the Color Glass Condensate formalism, where the target is described as a dense gluon system evolving via JIMWLK equations and the projectile as a collection of partons evolving via DGLAP evolution. The photon production cross section is expressed in terms of quark distributions, the quark-to-photon fragmentation function, and the fundamental dipole scattering probability; the pion production cross section additionally includes gluon distributions, the adjoint dipole scattering probability, and hadron fragmentation functions. A parameterization of the adjoint dipole cross section previously used to fit minimum bias deuteron-gold data at RHIC is employed, and the ratio of photon to pion production is evaluated at different rapidities for proton-proton, deuteron-gold (RHIC), and proton-lead (LHC) collisions.

## Key numbers

- In proton-proton collisions at RHIC, the CGC result for the photon-to-pion ratio is below the NLO pQCD result by a factor of approximately 2 at mid-rapidity.
- Direct photons contribute about 20-30% at transverse momentum p_t = 1.25 GeV in the NLO pQCD calculation.
- For proton-lead collisions at LHC, the center-of-mass energy is taken as sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV (the text states "8.8 GeV" but context and standard LHC energies indicate this is a typo for TeV).
- At LHC, quark production dominates gluon production only after 5-6 units of rapidity away from mid-rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism previously applied to hadron production in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC (DhJ, 2006) and photon production in the CGC framework (FGJJM, BMDS). The ratio approach is motivated by the expectation that K-factors from higher-order corrections may cancel between photon and pion channels, addressing a known limitation of leading-order CGC calculations which require a p_t-independent K-factor to match pion production data. The results are compared to NLO pQCD predictions (NLO, 2006) and enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to test the kinematic range of validity of saturation physics, particularly at forward rapidities where the approach is most robust.
