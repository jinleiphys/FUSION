# Multi-scale evolution of charmed particles in a nuclear medium

> Parton energy-momentum exchange with the quark gluon plasma (QGP) is a multi-scale problem. In this work, we calculate the interaction of charm quarks with the QGP within the higher twist formalism at high virtuality and high energy using the MATTER model, while the low virtuality and high energy portion is treated via a (linearized) Boltzmann Transport (LBT) formalism. Coherence effect that reduces the medium-induced emission rate in the MATTER model is also taken into account. The interplay between these two formalisms is studied in detail and used to produce a good description of the D-meson and charged hadron nuclear modification factor RAA across multiple centralities. All calculations were carried out utilizing the JETSCAPE framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multi-scale approach combining the MATTER model (for high-virtuality, high-energy charm quarks) with the Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) formalism (for low-virtuality, high-energy charm quarks) can describe D-meson and charged hadron nuclear modification factor RAA across multiple centralities, but the virtuality-dependent transverse momentum broadening parameter $\hat{q}(t)$ used for heavy quarks is an approximation that neglects the simultaneous dependence on heavy quark mass, which the authors acknowledge should be addressed in future work.

## Method

The authors simulated charm quark interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using the JETSCAPE framework, where high-virtuality and high-energy charm quarks were evolved using the MATTER model based on the higher-twist formalism with a virtuality-dependent $\hat{q}(t)$, while low-virtuality and high-energy charm quarks were treated via the Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) formalism. The QGP medium evolution was modeled using a Bayesian-tuned 2+1-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation with initial conditions from the TRENTo model, free-streaming for 1.2 fm/c, and second-order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics until the crossover temperature of 154 MeV. The switching between MATTER and LBT occurred at a switching virtuality $t_s$, and hadronization was handled via PYTHIA fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The QGP medium evolution used a cross-over temperature of $T_c = 154$ MeV for converting fluid fields into particles
- The pre-hydrodynamic free-streaming stage lasted for a proper time of $\tau_{FS} = 1.2$ fm/c
- 400 TRENTo initial Pb-Pb configurations were generated in the 0-10% centrality class at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- The effective strong coupling constant used was $\alpha_s^{(\rm eff)} = 0.3$, calibrated to light flavor experimental observables
- The lowest scale for MATTER evolution was taken as $t_0 = 1$ GeV$^2$
- The Debye mass was calculated as $m_D^2 = 6\pi \alpha_s^{(\rm eff)} T^2$ with $N_f = 3$ flavors
- The running coupling $\alpha_s(\mu^2)$ was set to switch from $\alpha_s^{(\rm eff)}$ to the perturbative running form at $\mu_0^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE collaboration's previous multi-scale studies of light flavor energy loss (JETSCAPE, 2022) to heavy flavor, specifically charm quarks, using the same virtuality-dependent $\hat{q}(t)$ formalism developed by Kumar et al. (2019). The approach combines the higher-twist formalism for heavy quarks derived using soft collinear effective theory by Abir et al. (2015) with the linearized Boltzmann transport model, building on earlier single-formalism approaches such as those by Burke et al. (2013). This enables future studies of the simultaneous dependence of $\hat{q}$ on heavy quark mass and virtuality, which the authors identify as a necessary next step.
