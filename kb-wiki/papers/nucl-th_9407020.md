# Resonance Model of $πΔ\rightarrow Y K$ for Kaon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The elementary production cross sections $πΔ\rightarrow Y K$ $(Y=Σ,\,\, Λ)$ and $πN \rightarrow Y K$ are needed to describe kaon production in heavy ion collisions. The $πN \rightarrow Y K$ reactions were studied previously by a resonance model. The model can explain the experimental data quite well \cite{tsu}. In this article, the total cross sections $πΔ\rightarrow Y K$ at intermediate energies (from the kaon production threshold to3 GeV of $πΔ$ center-of-mass energy) are calculated for the first time using the same resonance model. The resonances, $N(1710)\,I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}^+)$ and $N(1720)\, \frac{1}{2} (\frac{3}{2}^+)$ for the $πΔ\rightarrow ΣK$ reactions, and $N(1650)\, \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{2}^-)$, $N(1710)\, \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{2}^+)$ and $N(1720)\, \frac{1}{2} (\frac{3}{2}^+)$ for the $πΔ\rightarrow ΛK$ reactions are taken into account coherently as the intermediate states in the calculations. Also t-channel $K^*(892) \frac{1}{2}(1^-)$ vector meson exchange is included. The results show that $K^*(892)$ exchange is neglegible for the $πΔ\rightarrow ΣK$ reactions, whereas this meson does not contribute to the $πΔ\rightarrow ΛK$ reactions. Furthemore, the $πΔ\rightarrow Y K$ contributions to kaon production in heavy ion collisions are not only non-neglegible but also very different from the $πN \rightarrow Y K$ reactions. ## Key claim

The total cross sections for the elementary reactions πΔ → YK (Y = Σ, Λ) are calculated for the first time using a resonance model, and the results show that these contributions to kaon production in heavy ion collisions are non-negligible and very different from the previously studied πN → YK reactions, meaning that isospin arguments valid for πN → YK cannot be extended to πΔ → YK.

## Method

The authors calculated total cross sections for πΔ → YK reactions at intermediate energies (from threshold to 3 GeV center-of-mass energy) using a resonance model with intermediate N(1650), N(1710), and N(1720) resonances taken into account coherently, plus t-channel K*(892) vector meson exchange. Coupling constants were determined from experimental branching ratios of the resonances, and form factors with a cut-off parameter ΛC = 0.8 GeV (1.2 GeV for the K*ΣΔ vertex) were applied. The total cross sections were then parametrized as analytic functions of the center-of-mass energy √s for use in heavy ion collision simulation codes.

## Key numbers

- The total cross sections for πΔ → ΣK have peaks of the same order as those for πN → ΣK, which are about 0.2 to 0.4 mb (except π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ at about 0.75 mb).
- The total cross section for π⁻Δ⁺⁺ → ΛK⁺ at its peak is about 30% of the largest channel for πN → ΛK (π⁺n → ΛK⁺).
- The K*(892) exchange amplitude |M_d|² for πΔ → ΣK is more than one order of magnitude smaller than other contributions.
- Parametrizations are given for five channels, with thresholds at √s = 1.688 GeV for πΔ → ΣK and √s = 1.613 GeV for πΔ → ΛK.

## Context

This work extends the resonance model previously applied by Tsushima et al. (1994) to πN → YK reactions, which could explain experimental data quite well. The study was motivated by the finding of Ehehalt et al. that Δ baryons make up about 25% of baryons in the central cell of heavy ion collisions due to Pauli blocking of Δ → πN decay, making πΔ → YK processes necessary for accurate kaon production simulations. The parametrizations provided enable inclusion of these reactions in Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulation codes, where previously only parametrizations by Randrup and Ko (1980) and Cugnon and Lombard (1984) were used.
