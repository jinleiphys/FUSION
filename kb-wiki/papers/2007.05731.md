# Vacuum and thermal fluctuation energies of a soliton at finite temperatures

> We have studied the vacuum and thermal fluctuation energies of a soliton at finite temperatures. First the vacuum energy coming from the Dirac sea is calculated by a summation of the discrete and continuum energy spectrum of the Dirac equation in the background field of a soliton. And all the divergences are removed by the same renormalization scheme at zero temperature. Then the vacuum energy and thermal fluctuation energy at finite temperatures in a temperature dependent soliton background are calculated. And the numerical results are analyzed and discussed.

# Vacuum and thermal fluctuation energies of a soliton at finite temperatures

## Key claim
The vacuum and thermal fluctuation energies of a soliton in the Friedberg-Lee (FL) model are calculated at finite temperatures using a phase-shift-based level summation scheme, but the calculation is restricted to one-loop fermion fluctuations only (the sigma field is treated as a classical background and its quantum corrections are ignored), and the soliton solution itself disappears above approximately 121 MeV due to deconfinement in this model.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation in a spherically symmetric soliton background field at finite temperatures, obtaining discrete and continuum eigenvalues. The vacuum energy is computed via a level summation over the Dirac sea, where the continuum contribution is expressed through the momentum derivative of the scattering phase shift for each partial wave (s, p, d, f). Divergences are removed by Born subtraction of the phase shift (first and second Born approximations) combined with on-shell renormalization counterterms fixed at the sigma meson mass. The thermal fluctuation energy is obtained using the imaginary time formalism, yielding a finite expression involving the same subtracted phase shifts weighted by Fermi-Dirac thermal distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters: a = 17.7 fm^{-2}, b = -1457.4 fm^{-1}, c = 20000, g = 12.16; sigma mass m_sigma = 550 MeV; degeneracy factor nu_q = 12.
- Classical soliton energy E_cl decreases with temperature: 6.39 fm^{-1} at T=50 MeV, 6.07 fm^{-1} at T=90 MeV, 5.48 fm^{-1} at T=110 MeV, 4.80 fm^{-1} at T=120 MeV.
- Renormalized correction energy G_2 (negative): -2.37 fm^{-1} at T=50 MeV, -2.57 fm^{-1} at T=90 MeV, -3.23 fm^{-1} at T=110 MeV, -4.81 fm^{-1} at T=120 MeV.
- s-wave vacuum energy term E_ren^{(0)}: -4.24 fm^{-1} at T=50 MeV, -4.05 fm^{-1} at T=90 MeV, -3.72 fm^{-1} at T=110 MeV, -3.36 fm^{-1} at T=120 MeV.
- p-wave vacuum energy term E_ren^{(1)}: -0.54 fm^{-1} at T=50 MeV, -0.57 fm^{-1} at T=90 MeV, -4.8 fm^{-1} at T=110 MeV, -4.29 fm^{-1} at T=120 MeV.
- Thermal fluctuation energy terms are 1 to 6 orders of magnitude smaller than the vacuum energy terms; e.g., s-wave E_T^{(0)}: -4.18 x 10^{-4} fm^{-1} at T=50 MeV, -1.62 x 10^{-1} fm^{-1} at T=120 MeV.
- The soliton solution disappears when temperature exceeds approximately 121 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the phase-shift-based calculation of vacuum energy previously applied to the FL model at zero temperature by Shu et al. (ref23) to the finite temperature case, where the soliton is embedded in a thermal quark medium. The method builds on the level summation scheme developed by Jaffe and collaborators (ref2, ref22, ref22a) for calculating quantum fluctuations in soliton backgrounds, and on Schwinger's earlier treatment of Casimir energy via energy level summation (ref21). The results enable future extension to more realistic chiral soliton models (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio soliton model or chiral quark soliton model) to study nucleon properties in hot or dense medium, relevant to understanding hadron properties in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and for future electron-ion collider experiments.
