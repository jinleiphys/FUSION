# Enforcing causality in nonrelativistic equations of state at finite temperature

> We present a thermodynamically consistent method by which equations of state based on nonrelativistic potential models can be modified so that they respect causality at high densities, both at zero and finite temperature (entropy). We illustrate the application of the method using the high density phase parametrization of the well known APR model in its pure neutron matter configuration as an example. We also show that, for models with only contact interactions, the adiabatic speed of sound is independent of the temperature in the limit of very large temperature. This feature is approximately valid for models with finite-range interactions as well, insofar as the temperature dependence they introduce to the Landau effective mass is weak. In addition, our study reveals that in first principle nonrelativistic models of hot and dense matter, contributions from higher than two-body interactions must be screened at high density to preserve causality.

## Key claim

The authors present a thermodynamically consistent method to modify nonrelativistic equations of state so that they respect causality at high densities at both zero and finite temperature, correcting an inadvertent error in their earlier work (Prakash 1988) where the chemical potential definition lacked a term involving entropy times temperature.

## Method

The method enforces causality by imposing that the squared adiabatic speed of sound (c_s/c)^2 = beta_f remains less than or equal to 1 for densities above a causality-fixing density n_f(S), which depends on entropy. Starting from the thermodynamic definition of the speed of sound, the authors derive a second-order differential equation for the internal energy density as a function of baryon density at fixed entropy, then solve it analytically for both density-independent and density-dependent choices of beta_f. The procedure is applied to the APR, LS, and SLy4 models of pure neutron matter using the Johns-Ellis-Lattimer scheme to evaluate Fermi-Dirac integrals and maintain thermodynamic consistency.

## Key numbers

- For the APR model in pure neutron matter, causality is violated at n = 0.870 fm^{-3} at S=0, at n = 0.914 fm^{-3} at S=3, and at n = 2.710 fm^{-3} at S=5
- For the LS model in pure neutron matter, causality is violated at n = 1.112 fm^{-3} at S=0, at n = 1.232 fm^{-3} at S=3, and at n = 1.774 fm^{-3} at S=5
- For the SLy4 model in pure neutron matter, causality is violated at n = 1.181 fm^{-3} at S=0, at n = 0.608 fm^{-3} at S=3, and at n = 0.307 fm^{-3} at S=5
- The intersection density n_X where all entropy curves cross for APR is 0.809 fm^{-3} in pure neutron matter and 0.830 fm^{-3} in symmetric nuclear matter
- The intersection density n_X for LS is 0.671 fm^{-3} in pure neutron matter and 0.708 fm^{-3} in symmetric nuclear matter
- Nuclear saturation density n_s is approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the causality enforcement method previously available only at zero temperature (Nauenberg 1973, Lattimer 1990, APR 1998) to finite temperature, correcting an error in the finite-temperature treatment of Prakash (1988). The method is relevant to modern microscopic calculations of the equation of state from quantum Monte Carlo (Gandolfi 2011, Wlazlowski 2014) and chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2010, Tews 2013) approaches, which have been limited to about 2n_s and require extrapolation to higher densities. The results enable thermodynamically consistent extrapolations of nonrelativistic equations of state to the high densities and temperatures encountered in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, where entropy per baryon can reach up to 200 and densities up to 10 times saturation density.
