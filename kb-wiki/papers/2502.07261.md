# $öÝ+öÝ+{}^{3}$He cluster structure in ${}^{11}$C

> We study the $öÝ+ öÝ+ {}^{3}$He cluster structure of ${}^{11}$C within the microscopic cluster model. The calculations essentially reproduce the energy spectra for both negative and positive parity states, particularly the $3/2_3^-$ state near the $öÝ+öÝ$+${}^{3}$He threshold. We also calculate the isoscalar monopole, electric quadrupole transition strengths, and root-mean-square radii for the low-lying states. These results suggest that the $3/2_3^-$, $1/2_2^-$, and $5/2_3^-$ states have a well-developed $öÝ+ öÝ$ + ${}^{3}$He cluster structure. The analysis of the generator coordinate method wave functions indicates the dilute gaslike nature for the $3/2_3^-$, $1/2_2^-$, and $5/2_3^-$ states, suggesting that they could be candidates for the Hoyle-analog state. Furthermore, it is found that the $5/2_2^+$ and $5/2_3^+$ states may possess a linear chain structure.

## Key claim
The $3/2_3^-$, $1/2_2^-$, and $5/2_3^-$ states of ${}^{11}$C are identified as candidates for Hoyle-analog states based on their dilute gaslike nature and enhanced isoscalar monopole transition strengths, though the calculated excitation energy of the $3/2_3^-$ state (8.51 MeV) lies slightly above the $\alpha+\alpha+{}^{3}$He threshold (8.46 MeV) rather than below it as in the mirror nucleus ${}^{11}$B.

## Method
The authors employed a microscopic three-cluster generator coordinate method (GCM) using Brink wave functions with an $\alpha+\alpha+{}^{3}$He cluster configuration. They superposed 500 spatial basis wave functions generated from both random sampling and systematic grid-based methods, with the Volkov No.2 interaction combined with the G3RS potential as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Hill-Wheeler equation was solved after diagonalizing the norm kernel and discarding eigenvectors with eigenvalues smaller than 0.001 to ensure numerical stability.

## Key numbers
- Calculated excitation energy of the $3/2_3^-$ state: 8.51 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 8.10 MeV and the $\alpha+\alpha+{}^{3}$He threshold at 8.46 MeV
- Nuclear r.m.s. radius for the $3/2_1^-$ ground state: 2.42 fm; for the $3/2_3^-$ state: 3.54 fm
- Isoscalar monopole transition strengths: $B(\mathrm{ISM}; 3/2_1^- \to 3/2_3^-) = 12.15$ fm$^4$, $B(\mathrm{ISM}; 1/2_1^- \to 1/2_2^-) = 15.03$ fm$^4$, $B(\mathrm{ISM}; 5/2_1^- \to 5/2_3^-) = 12.38$ fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 5/2_1^- \to 3/2_1^-) = 9.1$ $e^2$ fm$^4$, in close agreement with the AMD model value of 6.8 $e^2$ fm$^4$ from Kanada (1997)
- Maximum overlap for the $3/2_3^-$ state: 0.30 at $R_{\alpha-\alpha}=6.1$ fm and $R_{(2\alpha-{}^{3}\mathrm{He})z}=2.2$ fm, indicating a dilute gaslike nature

## Context
This work extends the search for Hoyle-analog states to ${}^{11}$C, the mirror nucleus of ${}^{11}$B, where the $3/2_3^-$ state at 8.56 MeV has been studied using $\alpha+\alpha+t$ GCM and AMD by Yamada (2010) and Kanada (2015). The results build on prior RGM calculations by Vasilevsky (2018) that identified narrow resonance states in ${}^{11}$B and ${}^{11}$C as Hoyle-analog candidates, and on experimental observations by Li (2023) of a resonance at 8.10 MeV assigned as the head of the $K^{\pi}=3/2^-$ rotational band. The findings enable future experimental searches for the predicted $1/2_2^-$ and $5/2_3^-$ states and further investigation of the linear chain structure suggested for the $5/2_2^+$ and $5/2_3^+$ states.
