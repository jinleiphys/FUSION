# Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for light Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei

> New Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations are reported for Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-Lambda-10Be in terms of clusters of alpha's and Lambda's, using Lambda-Lambda s-wave potentials motivated by several of the Nijmegen model interactions. The self consistency of the Lambda-Lambda hypernuclear world data for these species is discussed. The newly reported Lambda-Lambda-6He event is found to be compatible with Lambda-Lambda interaction strengths provided by the Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange model NSC97. Faddeev calculations for Lambda-Lambda-5H and Lambda-Lambda-5He suggest that these Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei are stable against emitting Lambda's for any (essentially attractive) Lambda-Lambda interaction, whereas calcualtions for Lambda-Lambda-4H do not allow a clear-cut conclusion whether or not it is particle stable.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the newly observed Lambda-Lambda-6He event is compatible with the Lambda-Lambda interaction strengths provided by the Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange model NSC97, but only under the condition that the older, more strongly bound event for this species is disregarded. The authors find that the older Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-Lambda-10Be events are inconsistent with each other when using any single Lambda-Lambda interaction model.

## Method

The authors solved s-wave Faddeev equations for the three-body systems alpha-Lambda-Lambda (Lambda-Lambda-6He), H-3-Lambda-Lambda (Lambda-Lambda-5H), and He-3-Lambda-Lambda (Lambda-Lambda-5He), and s-wave Yakubovsky equations for the four-body system alpha-alpha-Lambda-Lambda (Lambda-Lambda-10Be), using the cluster reduction method. The Lambda-Lambda interactions were simulated by three-range Gaussian potentials fitted to the low-energy scattering parameters of several Nijmegen one-boson-exchange models, including ND, NSC97, and ESC00, while the alpha-alpha and Lambda-alpha interactions were taken from established phenomenological potentials.

## Key numbers

- For Lambda-Lambda-6He, the calculated binding energy B_LambdaLambda using the ESC00 model simulation is 10.9 MeV, reproducing the older event reported by Prowse (1966).
- For Lambda-Lambda-6He, the calculated B_LambdaLambda using the NSC97 model simulations ranges from approximately 6.5 to 7.5 MeV, close to the new KEK E373 event reported by Takahashi et al. (2001).
- For Lambda-Lambda-10Be, the calculated B_LambdaLambda using the ND model simulation is 17.7 MeV, reproducing the event reported by Danysz et al. (1963) and Dalitz et al. (1989).
- The correlation between B_LambdaLambda for Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-Lambda-10Be implies that if B_LambdaLambda(Lambda-Lambda-10Be) = 17.7 +/- 0.4 MeV, then the theoretically implied B_LambdaLambda for Lambda-Lambda-6He is about 9.1 +/- 0.4 MeV.
- The r.m.s. distance between the two alpha clusters in Lambda-Lambda-10Be is 3.4 fm for the ND model, in agreement with the calculation of Hiyama et al. (1997).

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations of Filikhin and Gal (2000) and provides a systematic comparison with the variational calculations of Hiyama et al. (1997) and Yamada et al. (2000). The results enable discrimination between different Nijmegen baryon-baryon interaction models (NSC97, ND, ESC00) using the new KEK E373 data, and they show that the older Lambda-Lambda-6He and Lambda-Lambda-10Be events cannot be simultaneously explained by any single Lambda-Lambda interaction. The calculations also demonstrate that a three-body core-plus-two-Lambda model is insufficient for Lambda-Lambda-10Be, as the four-body alpha-alpha-Lambda-Lambda treatment yields about 1.7 MeV more binding due to alpha-alpha correlations.
