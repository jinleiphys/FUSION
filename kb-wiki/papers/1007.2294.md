# Hydrodynamic predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

> Using the newest data for pp scattering at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) combined with the Glauber model, we make hydrodynamic predictions for the soft hadronic observables planned to be measured in the forthcoming Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.

## Key claim

Using the newest LHC pp data combined with a Glauber model and perfect-fluid hydrodynamics, the authors predict soft hadronic observables for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but note that the predictions depend on the assumed value of the parameter α (the minijet contribution) and on the extrapolation of pp multiplicity from the measured 2.36 TeV data point.

## Method

The authors use a 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model with a lattice-based equation of state and a single-freeze-out scenario. The initial entropy density is determined by scaling the measured pp charged-particle multiplicity at 2.36 TeV (extrapolated to 2.76 TeV) by the number of sources (wounded nucleons and binary collisions) from the Glauber model, implemented via the GLISSANDO code. The initial central temperature is then derived by matching the ratio of total entropies at LHC and RHIC energies to the ratio of measured multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated pp charged-particle pseudorapidity density at 2.76 TeV: dN^pp/dη|_(η=0) = 4.82, based on the weighted average of ALICE and CMS data at 2.36 TeV (4.65) scaled by (2.76/2.36)^0.23
- Central source densities: ρ_sr^PbPb(0,0) = 6.76 fm^{-2}, ρ_sr^AuAu(0,0) = 4.71 fm^{-2}
- Ratio of total entropies (LHC 2.76 TeV to RHIC 200 GeV): 3.2
- Initial central temperature at τ_i = 0.25 fm: 735 MeV for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV (compared to 520 MeV for Au+Au at 200 GeV); or 480 MeV if τ_i = 1 fm
- Transverse fireball size: S_⊥ = 23.2 fm^2 for central Pb+Pb at LHC, compared to 22.9 fm^2 for central Au+Au at RHIC
- Predicted charged-particle pseudorapidity density: dN/dη = 2161 for centrality 0-5%, and 905 for centrality 20-30%
- Predicted mean transverse momenta for centrality 0-5%: ⟨p_⊥^pions⟩ = 656 MeV, ⟨p_⊥^kaons⟩ = 1028 MeV, ⟨p_⊥^protons⟩ = 1460 MeV
- Predicted elliptic flow coefficient: v_2 = 0.088 for charged particles in centrality 20-30%
- Scaling density: (1/S_⊥) dN/dy ≃ 105 fm^{-2} at LHC, compared to 35 fm^{-2} at RHIC
- Baryon chemical potential: μ_B ≃ 2.2 MeV, estimated from pp p̄/p ratio of 0.97

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic predictions of Chojnacki et al. (2007) from the originally planned LHC energy of 5.5 TeV down to the actual collision energy of 2.76 TeV, incorporating the newly available LHC pp data. The predictions show larger multiplicity increases than earlier estimates, primarily due to the large observed pp multiplicity and the assumed α = 0.16 for the minijet contribution. The results enable direct comparison with upcoming LHC heavy-ion data and provide a baseline for studying shear viscosity effects, which are expected to be reduced at LHC temperatures compared to RHIC energies as noted by Luzum and Romatschke (2009).
