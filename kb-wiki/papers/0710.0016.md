# Fluid turbulence and eddy viscosity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The eddy viscosity for a turbulent compressible fluid with a relativistic equation of state is derived. Compressibility allows for sound modes, but the eddy viscosity in the shear mode is found to be the same as for incompressible fluids. For two space dimensions (which is the relevant case for the dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions) the eddy viscosity in the shear mode is negative, reducing the effective viscosity below its microscopic value. This could explain the tiny viscosity found at RHIC. Implications for the experimentally accessible elliptic flow coefficient at the LHC are speculated on.

# Fluid turbulence and eddy viscosity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The eddy viscosity in the shear mode for a relativistic compressible fluid in two space dimensions is negative, reducing the effective viscosity below its microscopic value, which could explain the tiny viscosity extracted from RHIC data; however, the calculation is qualitative and the result cannot be trusted below a critical viscosity where the effective viscosity would become negative.

## Method

The author starts from the relativistic fluid dynamic equations with shear viscosity and a relativistic equation of state with constant speed of sound squared. Following the Yakhot-Orszag renormalization group approach to turbulence, the fluid velocity field is split into low and high wavenumber parts, and the effect of small-scale turbulent fluctuations on the large-scale flow is integrated out to derive an effective turbulent (eddy) viscosity. The propagator is decomposed into shear and sound modes (Kovasznay modes), and the eddy viscosity is extracted in the small wavenumber limit.

## Key numbers

- The Reynolds number for gold collisions at RHIC is estimated as Re_RHIC ~ 48 pi, using L ~ 6 fm, T ~ 200 MeV, and eta/s ~ 1/(8 pi) from Romatschke (2007).
- The eddy viscosity in d=2 space dimensions is negative, with the effective viscosity given by nu_eff = nu (1 - lambda/(64 pi)), where lambda = alpha/(nu^3 Lambda^4) is a dimensionless coupling parameter.
- After renormalization group improvement, the effective eta/s behaves as (eta/s)_eff = (eta/s) [1 - bar-lambda (eta/s)^{-3}]^{1/3}, where bar-lambda ~ (T/Lambda)^4.
- Assuming Lambda ~ 1 GeV and T ~ 200 MeV gives bar-lambda ~ 10^{-3}.
- The critical viscosity where the effective viscosity becomes very small is eta/s = (bar-lambda)^{1/3}.

## Context

This work extends the Yakhot-Orszag (1986) approach to turbulence to relativistic compressible fluids with a relativistic equation of state, finding the same eddy viscosity in the shear mode as for incompressible fluids. The negative eddy viscosity in two dimensions was known from the work of Starr (1968) and Kraichnan (1967). The paper builds on the observation that RHIC data favor eta/s ~ 1/(8 pi) (Romatschke, 2007), possibly violating the conjectured bound eta/s >= 1/(4 pi) from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004). The result suggests that if fluid turbulence is operative, elliptic flow at the LHC should increase beyond RHIC values, possibly exceeding the ideal hydrodynamic limit, in contrast to more conventional approaches by Krieg (2007) and Molnar (2007) that predict a decrease. The concept of anomalous turbulent viscosity in heavy-ion collisions was previously suggested by Asakawa (2006), but based on plasma turbulence rather than fluid turbulence.
