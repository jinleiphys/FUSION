# The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at large $N_c$ in the Van der Waals approximation

> We examine the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at large number of colors ($N_c$) within the framework of the Van Der Waals (VdW) model. We argue that the VdW equation is appropriate at describing inter-nucleon forces, and discuss how each parameter scales with $N_c$. We demonstrate that $N_c=3$ (our world) is not large with respect to the other dimensionless scale relevant to baryonic matter, the number of neighbors in a dense system $N_N$. Consequently, we show that the liquid-gas phase transition looks dramatically different at $N_c \to \infty$ with respect of our world: The critical point temperature becomes of the order of $\lqcd$ rather than below it. The critical point density becomes of the order of the baryonic density, rather than an order of magnitude below it. These are precisely the characteristics usually associated with the "Quarkyonic phase". We therefore conjecture that quarkyonic matter is simply the large $N_c$ limit of the nuclear liquid, and the interplay between $N_c$ and $N_N$ is the reason why the nuclear liquid in our world is so different from quarkyonic matter. We conclude by suggesting ways our conjecture can be tested in future lattice measurements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at large number of colors \(N_c\) becomes the "quarkyonic phase" conjectured in prior work, but only if the inter-nucleon interaction strength scales as \(N_c^0\) rather than \(N_c\); the authors hedge that the Van der Waals model can only provide a qualitative estimate and cannot probe the dense liquid phase where pressure scaling with \(N_c\) would be tested.

## Method

The authors apply the Van der Waals (VdW) equation of state to nuclear matter, parametrizing the excluded volume parameter \(\alpha\) as \(\alpha \sim 1 + N_N/N_c\) where \(N_N\) is the number of nearest neighbors (taken as 10 for three dimensions), and the interaction parameter \(\beta\) as \(\beta \sim N_c^\nu\) with \(\nu = 0\) or \(\nu = 1\). They solve the VdW equation analytically for the critical point temperature \(T_c\) and density \(\rho_c\) in both the \(\gamma=0\) and \(\gamma\neq0\) cases, and compute the phase diagram in the \(T-\rho\) and \(T-\mu\) planes using a parametric solution for the coexistence line.

## Key numbers

* Critical point density \(\rho_c \sim (N_c/(N_N + N_c))\) in units of \(\Lambda_{QCD}^3\), ranging from \(\ll \Lambda_{QCD}^3\) at \(N_c=3\) to \(\sim \Lambda_{QCD}^3\) as \(N_c \to \infty\).
* Critical point temperature \(T_c \sim (8/27)(\beta/\alpha)\); for \(\nu=0\) (interaction \(\sim N_c^0\)), \(T_c\) saturates to \(\sim \Lambda_{QCD}\) at large \(N_c\); for \(\nu=1\) (interaction \(\sim N_c\)), \(T_c \sim N_c \Lambda_{QCD}\) and exceeds the deconfinement temperature by \(N_c \sim 10\).
* The nuclear liquid-gas mixed phase at large \(N_c\) occupies the density range from \(\rho \ll \Lambda_{QCD}^3\) to \(\rho \sim \Lambda_{QCD}^3/3\), while in our world (\(N_c=3\)) the mixed phase occupies a small area of the \(T-\rho\) plane.
* The baryon mass is \(\sim N_c \Lambda_{QCD}\) with \(\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 200-300\) MeV, and the constituent quark mass \(m_B/N_c \simeq 310\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of the QCD phase diagram from 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979), and directly addresses the "quarkyonic matter" conjecture of McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The authors contrast their results with the Sakai-Sugimoto model calculations of Lippert (2009) and Rozali (2008), which found that the new phase at large \(N_c\) is the nuclear liquid-gas transition rather than a distinct quarkyonic phase. The paper enables future lattice tests of whether the nuclear liquid-gas critical point evolves toward the quarkyonic regime as \(N_c\) increases, and clarifies that the smallness of the critical temperature and density in our world (\(N_c=3\)) arises because \(N_c\) is not large compared to the number of nearest neighbors \(N_N \sim 10\).
