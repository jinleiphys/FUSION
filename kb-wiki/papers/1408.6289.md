# Quark-Gluon Plasma and Topological Quantum Fields Theory

> Based on an analogy with topologically ordered new state of matter in condensed matter systems, we propose a low energy effective field theory for a parity conserving liquid-like quark-gluon plasma (QGP) around critical temperature in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) system. It shows that below a QCD gap which is expected several times of the critical temperature, the QGP behaves like topological fluid. Many exotic phenomenon of QGP near the critical temperature discovered at RHIC are more readily understood by the suggestion that QGP is a topologically ordered state.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that near the critical temperature, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be described as a topologically ordered state of matter analogous to topological insulators in condensed matter physics, with the key condition that the effective theory is parity-conserving and the topological theta parameter is fixed to a non-trivial value of θ = π, which serves as a topological order parameter rather than a dynamical field.

## Method

The author constructs a low-energy effective field theory by bosonizing the gauge-invariant QCD action, replacing fermionic quarks with two types of gauge fields: a 1-form field aμ and a 2-form field bμν. The effective action includes a topological BF term, a θ-term with θ = π, and a Maxwell term with coupling constant g, with the topological terms dominating at strong coupling and low energies below a QCD gap Λ. The classical equations of motion and hydrodynamic equations are derived from this action, and transport properties such as conductivity, charge susceptibility, and shear viscosity are computed from the effective action after integrating out the a and b fields.

## Key numbers

- The effective theory is valid in the temperature range Tc < T ≲ 2Tc, where Tc is the critical temperature of QCD.
- The QCD gap Λ is expected to be at least several times larger than Tc, based on lattice data from Karsch (2009) and other sources.
- The charge diffusion constant D on self-dual configurations at the lowest Matsubara frequency ω = 2πT takes the value D = 1/(2πT).
- The conductivity is σ = Nc²T/(16π) and the charge susceptibility is χ = Nc²|k|²T/(16πω), for Nc = 3 and with the lowest Matsubara frequency ω = 2πT.
- The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio is derived at the Bogomol'nyi bound, though no explicit numerical value for the ratio is given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between QGP and topological field theories previously discussed by Efraty and Nair (1992, 1993) for pure gluon plasma in the hard thermal loop limit, and generalizes it to a full bosonized effective theory. The approach is contrasted with the chiral superfluid model of QGP by Kalaydzhyan (2012, 2014), where θ is a dynamical field leading to chirality imbalance, whereas here θ = π is a constant topological order parameter preserving parity. The paper enables a hydrodynamic interpretation of QGP near Tc without invoking symmetry breaking, and predicts small transport coefficients consistent with the near-perfect liquid behavior observed at RHIC.
