# "To renormalize or not to renormalize ?'' in the proton-deuteron scattering calculations

> We discuss two approaches which, by applying the screening method, permit one to include the long range proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force in proton-deuteron (pd) momentum-space scattering calculations. In the first one, based on Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation, presented in Phys. Rev. C{\bf{71}}, 054005 (2005) and {\bf{73}}, 057001 (2006), one needs to renormalize elastic scattering amplitude before calculating observables. In the second treatment, proposed by us in Eur. Phys. Journal A {\bf{41}}, 369 (2009), {\bf{41}}, 385 (2009), and arXiv:2310.03433 [nucl.th], this renormalization is avoided. For the proton induced deuteron breakup reaction both approaches require renormalization of the corresponding transition amplitudes. We derive the basic equations underlying both methods under the assumption that all contributing partial wave states are included and explain why in our approach renormalization of the elastic scattering amplitude is superfluous. We show that in order to take into account in the screening limit all partial waves it is required that four additional terms, based on the 3-dimensional and partial-wave projected pp Coulomb t-matrices, identical for both approaches, must appear in transition amplitudes. We investigate importance of these terms for elastic pd scattering below the breakup threshold.

# To renormalize or not to renormalize? in the proton-deuteron scattering calculations

## Key claim

The authors show that two approaches for including the long-range proton-proton Coulomb force in momentum-space proton-deuteron scattering calculations, one based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation requiring renormalization of elastic scattering amplitudes, and their own approach based on the Faddeev equation avoiding such renormalization, must yield identical results for all observables, provided that four additional terms containing both three-dimensional and partial-wave projected Coulomb t-matrices are included in the transition amplitudes.

## Method

The authors derive coupled integral equations for both the AGS-based approach (Eqs. 12-16) and their Faddeev-based approach (Eqs. 7-11) in momentum-space partial-wave basis, separating low angular momentum states (j ≤ j_max) where both nuclear and screened Coulomb interactions act from high angular momentum states (j > j_max) where only the screened Coulomb force is present. They show that by extending the set of low partial-wave states, the four terms containing three-dimensional Coulomb t-matrices cancel against partial-wave projected terms, reducing both approaches to equations structurally identical to standard neutron-deuteron scattering equations (Eqs. 10 and 15). They then compute elastic scattering observables at 3.0 MeV using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and compare results with and without the additional terms.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering calculations performed at E = 3.0 MeV (below breakup threshold) and E = 3.5 MeV (slightly above breakup threshold)
- AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential used with j_s3j7 basis (j_s = largest angular momentum with 2N interaction)
- Tensor analyzing powers show magnitudes of approximately 1%
- At energies above approximately 10 MeV, the fifth term contribution to elastic scattering observables becomes negligible
- At 10 MeV, the fifth term starts to influence some spin observables

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Witała et al., Eur. Phys. Journal A 41, 369 (2009) and 41, 385 (2009); arXiv:2310.03433) for including the Coulomb force in proton-deuteron scattering without renormalization, and compares it to the established AGS-based screening method of Deltuva et al. (Phys. Rev. C 71, 054005 (2005) and 73, 057001 (2006)) which requires renormalization. The paper resolves the apparent contradiction between the two methods by demonstrating their equivalence when all partial waves are properly included. The results enable simplified calculation of Coulomb effects in pd scattering nearly as easily as neutron-deuteron scattering calculations, and confirm the well-known "low energy analyzing power puzzle" where vector analyzing powers A_y and iT_11 are underestimated by theory.
