# Nuclear symmetry energy and proton-rich reactions at intermediate energies

> Based on an isospin dependent transport model IBUU, effects of high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy in the proton-rich reaction $^{22}$Si+$^{22}$Si at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon are studied. It is found that the symmetry energy affects $π^{+}$ production more than $π^{-}$. More interestingly, comparing with neutron-rich reactions, for $π^{-}/π^{+}$ and dense matter's N/Z ratios, effects of symmetry energy in the proton-rich reaction both show contrary behaviors. The practical experiment by using the proton-rich reaction $^{22}$Si+$^{40}$Ca to study nuclear symmetry energy are also provided.

# Nuclear symmetry energy and proton-rich reactions at intermediate energies

## Key claim
Using the IBUU transport model, the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy affects pion production in proton-rich reactions, with a contrary behavior compared to neutron-rich reactions: the soft symmetry energy (x=1) leads to a lower (N/Z) ratio in the high-density region and a lower π⁻/π⁺ ratio than the stiff symmetry energy (x=0), opposite to what is seen in neutron-rich systems.

## Method
The study uses the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean field potential that incorporates a variable parameter x to mimic different forms of the symmetry energy (x=1 for soft, x=0 for stiff). Central collisions (impact parameter 0 fm) of ²²Si+²²Si and ²²Si+⁴⁰Ca at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy are simulated, tracking pion production through Δ(1232) resonance formation and decay, with in-medium NN elastic cross sections scaled by effective mass and free-space NN inelastic cross sections used for simplicity.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for all reactions studied.
- Maximum central baryon density reached: approximately 2 times normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀).
- High-density phase duration: about 11 fm/c (from 2 to 13 fm/c) for ²²Si+²²Si.
- Initial (N/Z) ratio of the high-density region: about 0.7, larger than the average n/p ratio of 0.57 for the ²²Si+²²Si system.
- Sensitivity of π⁻/π⁺ ratio to symmetry energy around the Coulomb peak (E_kin ~ 45 MeV): about 20%.
- For the symmetric reaction ²²Na+²²Na, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio is approximately 1, consistent with (N/Z)² = (44/44)² = 1.

## Context
This work extends prior studies using π⁻/π⁺ ratios to probe high-density symmetry energy (LiBA02, Yong 2006, Gai04, LiQF05b) by examining proton-rich reactions instead of the usual neutron-rich systems. It contradicts the finding of Li, Yong, and Zuo (LYZ) regarding which pion charge state is more sensitive to symmetry energy in proton-rich matter. The results suggest that experiments using proton-rich beams (e.g., ²²Si+⁴⁰Ca) at facilities like the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory, RIKEN, or the Cooler Storage Ring in Lanzhou could provide complementary constraints on the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, and also test theories of nuclear matter under conditions possibly relevant to neutron stars with strong magnetic fields.
