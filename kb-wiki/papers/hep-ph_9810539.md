# The Proton Spin in the Chiral Bag Model : Casimir Contribution and Cheshire Cat Principle

> The flavor singlet axial charge has been a source of study in the last years due to its relation to the so called {\it Proton Spin Problem}. The relevant flavor singlet axial current is anomalous, i.e., its divergence contains a piece which is the celebrated $U_A(1)$ anomaly. This anomaly is intimately associated with the $η^\prime$ meson, which gets its mass from it. When the gauge degrees of freedom of QCD are confined within a volume as is presently understood, the $U_A(1)$ anomaly is known to induce color anomaly leading to "leakage" of the color out of the confined volume (or bag). For consistency of the theory, this anomaly should be canceled by a boundary term. This ``color boundary term" inherits part or most of the dynamics of the volume (i.e., QCD). In this paper, we exploit this mapping of the volume to the surafce via the color boundary condition to perform a complete analysis of the flavor singlet axial charge in the chiral bag model using the Cheshire Cat Principle. This enables us to obtain the hitherto missing piece in the axial charge associated with the gluon Casimir energies. The result is that the flavor singlet axial charge is small independent of the confinement (bag) size ranging from the skyrmion picture to the MIT bag picture, thereby confirming the (albeit approximate) Cheshire Cat phenomenon.

## Key claim

The flavor singlet axial charge of the proton is small and approximately independent of the confinement (bag) radius, ranging from the skyrmion picture to the MIT bag picture, thereby confirming the Cheshire Cat Principle, but only after including the previously missing gluon Casimir contribution.

## Method

The authors calculate the flavor singlet axial charge in the chiral bag model by dividing space into an interior region (bag) described by QCD quarks and gluons and an exterior region described by meson fields including the eta-prime meson. They compute the quark contribution from bagged quark fields, the eta-prime contribution from the meson field, and the gluon contribution split into a static part from quark and eta sources and a vacuum part from gluon Casimir effects. The gluon Casimir contribution is evaluated by assuming the Cheshire Cat Principle to relate the anomaly operator to the gluon condensate, then performing a mode sum over MIT bag eigenmodes with heat kernel regularization to extract the finite part.

## Key numbers

* The finite part of the regularized mode sum for the gluon Casimir contribution is 0.0478 (from cubic fit) or 0.0456 (from quadratic fit).
* The gluon vacuum contribution to the flavor singlet axial charge is given by a formula involving the factor (0.0478) and the eta-prime mass m_eta = 958 MeV.
* The eta-prime field at the boundary is given by eta(r) = -(g_NN_eta / (4 pi M)) (S dot r-hat) (1 + m_eta r) / r^2 exp(-m_eta r), where m_eta = 958 MeV.
* The experimental value of the flavor singlet axial charge at infinite momentum transfer is a^0(infinity) = 0.10 (+0.17 / -0.10).
* The naive OZI approximation gives a^0 approximately 0.69 +/- 0.06.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts by the same authors (Lee, Min, Park, Rho, Vento, 1995-1997) where the Cheshire Cat Principle was only partially realized for the proton spin problem, failing at zero bag radius. The calculation completes the program by including the gluon Casimir contribution that was previously missing, using the color boundary conditions derived from the U_A(1) anomaly cancellation mechanism of Nielsen, Rothe, and Zahed. The result enables a consistent description of the proton spin across the full range of bag radii, connecting the skyrmion picture at small radii to the MIT bag picture at large radii.
