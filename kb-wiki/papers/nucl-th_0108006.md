# Manifestations of high density QCD in the first RHIC data

> We derive a simple analytical scaling function which embodies the predictions of high density QCD on the energy, centrality, rapidity, and atomic number dependences of hadron multiplicities in nuclear collisions. Both centrality and rapidity dependences of hadron multiplicity in $Au-Au$ collisions as measured at RHIC at $\sqrt{s}= 130 {GeV}$ are well described in this approach. The centrality and rapidity dependences of hadron multiplicity at $\sqrt{s}= 200 \ {GeV}$ run at RHIC are predicted; the variation of these dependences with energy appear different from other approaches, and can be used as an important test of the ideas based on parton saturation and classical Chromo-Dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a single analytical scaling function that embodies the predictions of high-density QCD (parton saturation and classical chromodynamics) for hadron multiplicities in nuclear collisions, and shows that this function describes the centrality and rapidity dependences of the first RHIC data at √s = 130 GeV, but the authors hedge that the clear discrimination between this and other approaches may require the study of more "microscopic" observables.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical expression for the rapidity density of produced gluons by evaluating the leading tree diagram for classical gluon radiation, using unintegrated gluon distributions that are constant in the saturation region (k_t^2 ≤ Q_s^2) and fall as 1/k_t^2 above it. They integrated the differential cross section for gluon production over transverse momentum, splitting the integration into regions where both nuclear wave functions are in the saturation regime versus where only one is, and then converted the result from rapidity to pseudorapidity using a Jacobian that accounts for the typical mini-jet mass and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* The "parton liberation" coefficient c was determined from experimental multiplicity to be c = 1.23 ± 0.20.
* The saturation scale Q_s^2 is predicted to vary with center-of-mass energy as Q_s^2 ∝ W^λ, where λ ≃ 0.25 ÷ 0.3, inferred from HERA data.
* The predicted charged hadron multiplicity at √s = 200 GeV for the 6% centrality cut is dN/dη = 616 ÷ 634, based on the PHOBOS measurement of dN/dη = 555 ± 12(stat) ± 35(syst) at √s = 130 GeV.
* The ratio of multiplicities in central events at √s = 200 and √s = 130 GeV was reported by PHOBOS as R(200/130) = 1.14 ± 0.06, consistent with the saturation prediction of R(200/130) = 1.10 ÷ 1.14.
* The exponent λ extracted from the energy dependence of hadron multiplicity between √s = 56 GeV and √s = 130 GeV is λ ≃ 0.37, larger than the HERA value.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Kharzeev and Nardi (2001) of multiparticle production in the quasi-classical picture to include pseudorapidity dependence, and provides predictions for the √s = 200 GeV run at RHIC. The approach is contrasted with the "soft plus hard" mini-jet picture, where the hard component of multiplicity is proportional to the square of the gluon structure function rather than its first power; the authors show that the two approaches yield different predictions for the centrality and rapidity dependences at √s = 200 GeV, which can serve as a test of parton saturation and classical chromodynamics.
