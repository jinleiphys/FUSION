# Collective T=0 pairing in N=Z nuclei? Pairing vibrations around 56Ni revisited

> We present a new analysis of the pairing vibrations around 56Ni, with emphasis on odd-odd nuclei. This analysis of the experimental excitation energies is based on the subtraction of average properties that include the full symmetry energy together with volume, surface and Coulomb terms. The results clearly indicate a collective behavior of the isovector pairing vibrations and do not support any appreciable collectivity in the isoscalar channel.

# Collective T=0 pairing in N=Z nuclei? Pairing vibrations around 56Ni revisited

## Key claim

The experimental excitation energies around 56Ni, after subtracting average properties including the full symmetry energy, show strong collectivity in the isovector (T=1) pairing channel but do not support any appreciable collectivity in the isoscalar (T=0) channel; the T=0 excitations can be accounted for by a residual np diagonal interaction rather than collective effects.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental binding energies and excitation energies of nuclei around the doubly magic N=Z nucleus 56Ni, focusing on odd-odd nuclei. They subtracted average nuclear properties (volume, surface, Coulomb, and full symmetry energy terms) from the experimental binding energies to isolate pairing and shell-structure effects, then compared the resulting excitation spectrum to a schematic two-level model with isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions treated in the random-phase approximation (RPA). The method was benchmarked against the known pairing vibrations around 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- The isovector (T=1) pairing phonon frequency around 56Ni is approximately 1.5 MeV, giving G1/Gcrit ≈ 0.9, indicating strong collective character.
- The isoscalar (T=0) pairing phonon frequency around 56Ni is approximately 4.0 MeV, giving G0/Gcrit ≤ 0.2, indicating very weak collectivity.
- For 40Ca, the T=1 phonon energy is 0.8 MeV with G/Gcrit ∼ 1, and the T=0 phonon energy is 4.9 MeV with G/Gcrit ∼ 0.2.
- For 208Pb, the neutron pairing vibrational phonon energy is 2.3 MeV with G/Gcrit ∼ 0.55.
- The residual np diagonal interaction that can account for the T=0 state energy is approximately 20 MeV/A.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of binding energy differences along the N=Z line by Macchiavelli et al. (1999) and the foundational studies of pairing vibrations by Bes and Broglia (1966) and the review by Bes et al. (1977). It also builds on the general framework for T=1 pairing phonons discussed by Bohr (1969). The results confirm the collective behavior of isovector pairing vibrations established in those earlier works while providing a quantitative argument against isoscalar pair collectivity. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of the competition between T=0 and T=1 pairing in N=Z nuclei and suggests that the ground states of even-even nuclei are correlated states of T=1 pairs, with odd-odd nuclei exhibiting either a T=1 np pair state (collective) or a T=0 np state (single-particle type).
