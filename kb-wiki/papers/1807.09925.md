# Measurements of the chiral magnetic effect with background isolation in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR

> Using two novel methods, pair invariant mass ($m_{inv}$) and comparative measurements with respect to reaction plane ($Ψ_{\rm RP}$) and participant plane ($Ψ_{\rm PP}$), we isolate the possible chiral magnetic effect (CME) from backgrounds in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR. The invariant mass method identifies the resonance background contributions, coupled with the elliptic flow ($v_{2}$), to the charge correlator CME observable ($Δγ$). At high mass ($m_{inv}>1.5$ GeV/$c^{2}$) where resonance contribution is small, we obtain the average $Δγ$ magnitude. In the low mass region ($m_{inv}<1.5$ GeV/$c^{2}$), resonance peaks are observed in $Δγ$($m_{inv}$). An event shape engineering (ESE) method is used to model the background shape in $m_{inv}$ to extract the potential CME signal at low $m_{inv}$. In the comparative method, the $Ψ_{\rm RP}$ is assessed by spectator neutrons measured by ZDC, and the $Ψ_{\rm PP}$ by the 2$^{nd}$-harmonic event plane measured by the TPC. The $v_{2}$ is stronger along $Ψ_{\rm PP}$ and weaker along $Ψ_{\rm RP}$; in contrast, the magnetic field, mainly from spectator protons, is weaker along $Ψ_{\rm PP}$ and stronger along $Ψ_{\rm RP}$. As a result, the $Δγ$ measured with respect to $Ψ_{\rm RP}$ and $Ψ_{\rm PP}$ contain different amounts of CME and background, and can thus determine these two contributions. It is found that the possible CME signals with background isolation by these two novel methods are small, on the order of a few percent of the inclusive $Δγ$ measurements.

## Key claim

Using two novel methods, pair invariant mass analysis and comparative measurements with respect to reaction plane and participant plane, the possible chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR is found to be small, on the order of a few percent of the inclusive charge correlator measurements, though the results are within 1-2 sigma from zero.

## Method

The analysis used the three-point azimuthal correlator difference (Δγ = γ_OS - γ_SS) as the CME observable. The invariant mass method identified resonance background contributions coupled with elliptic flow (v₂) by measuring Δγ as a function of π-π pair invariant mass (m_inv), applying a high-mass cut (m_inv > 1.5 GeV/c²) to suppress resonances, and using event shape engineering (ESE) to model the background shape at low mass. The comparative method measured Δγ with respect to the reaction plane (Ψ_RP) assessed by spectator neutrons in the Zero-Degree Calorimeter (ZDC) and with respect to the participant plane (Ψ_PP) assessed by the second-harmonic event plane in the Time Projection Chamber (TPC), exploiting the fact that v₂ is stronger along Ψ_PP while the magnetic field is stronger along Ψ_RP.

## Key numbers

- In 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the Δγ at m_inv > 1.5 GeV/c² is (5 ± 2 ± 4)% of the inclusive Δγ measurement, combining data from Run-11 (~0.5 billion events), Run-14 (~0.8 billion events), and Run-16 (~1.2 billion events)
- Using the ESE method with a linear fit, the extracted Δγ_CME is (2 ± 4 ± 6)% of inclusive Δγ in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions
- From the comparative Ψ_RP/Ψ_PP method using TPC sub-event method, the extracted CME fraction (f^EP_CME) is (9 ± 4 ± 7)% in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions
- From the comparative method using TPC full-event method, the extracted CME fraction is (12 ± 4 ± 11)% in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends previous CME searches in heavy-ion collisions by STAR and others (Kharzeev 1998, Fukushima 2008, Voloshin 2004) that had observed significant Δγ but could not separate CME from background contributions arising from resonance decay correlations coupled with elliptic flow (Wang 2009, Bzdak 2009, Schlichting 2010, Adamczyk 2013, Wang 2016). The invariant mass method was proposed by Zhao (2017) and the comparative reaction plane/participant plane method by Xu (2017). These data-driven background isolation techniques enable more definitive CME searches, with precision expected to improve with future Au+Au data and new isobar collision data from STAR.
