# Fluctuations of Temperature in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> We investigate temperature fluctuations in hot QCD matter using a 3-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The high-order cumulant ratios $R_{n2}$ ($n>2$) exhibit non-monotonic variations across the chiral phase transition, characterized by slight fluctuations in the chiral crossover region and significant oscillations around the critical point. In contrast, distinct peak and dip structures are observed in the cumulant ratios at low baryon chemical potential. These structures gradually weaken and eventually vanish at high chemical potential as they compete with the sharpening of the chiral phase transition, particularly near the critical point and the first-order phase transition. Our results indicate that these non-monotonic peak and dip structures in high-order cumulant ratios are associated with the deconfinement phase transition. This study quantitatively analyzes temperature fluctuation behavior across different phase transition regions, and the findings are expected to be observed and validated in heavy-ion collision experiments through measurements of event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations.

## Key claim

Temperature fluctuation cumulant ratios $R_{n2}$ ($n>2$) in the 3-flavor PNJL model exhibit non-monotonic variations across the chiral phase transition, but the distinct peak and dip structures observed at low baryon chemical potential are associated with the deconfinement phase transition, not the chiral transition. These structures gradually weaken and vanish at high chemical potential as they compete with the sharpening of the chiral phase transition near the critical point and first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors use a 3-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, solving the stationary conditions for the quark condensates $\sigma_u$, $\sigma_d$, $\sigma_s$, and the Polyakov loops $\Phi$, $\bar{\Phi}$. Temperature fluctuation cumulants $c_n$ are derived from a thermodynamic function $w = \Omega + Ts$, expressed through $n$-th order derivatives of pressure with respect to temperature (entropy susceptibilities $\chi_n$). Dimensionless cumulant ratios $R_{n2} = c_n / c_2^{n-1}$ are computed across the $\mu_B$-$T$ phase diagram to eliminate unknown proportionality factors between temperature and mean transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint (CEP) located at $\mu_B = 943$ MeV and $T = 106$ MeV in the PNJL model.
- Polyakov-loop effective potential parameters: $a_0 = 3.51$, $a_1 = -2.47$, $a_2 = 15.2$, $b_3 = -1.75$, with $T_0 = 210$ MeV.
- Quark current masses: $m_u = m_d = 5.5$ MeV, $m_s = 135.7$ MeV; scalar coupling $G_S \Lambda^2 = 3.67$; KMT coupling $K \Lambda^5 = 9.29$; momentum cutoff $\Lambda = 631.4$ MeV.
- Entropy fluctuations (oscillations) occur predominantly around three characteristic temperatures: near 200 MeV (chiral crossover), near 170 MeV (deconfinement phase transition), and near 110 MeV (critical point).
- Peak/dip structures in $R_{n2}$ are observed around $T \approx 170$ MeV at low baryon chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for temperature fluctuations developed by Chen (2025) to the PNJL model, connecting cumulant ratios to entropy susceptibilities. It builds on lattice QCD results (Aoki 2006, Gupta 2011, Borsanyi 2014) showing a smooth crossover at low $\mu_B$, and on effective model studies (Schaefer 1999, Zhuang 2000, Fu 2008, Fukushima 2008, Qin 2011, Fischer 2012, Chen 2016, Liu 2016, Fu 2020, Liu 2021) suggesting a first-order transition at high $\mu_B$ with a critical endpoint. The results are expected to be testable via event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, particularly through the Beam Energy Scan II program, and complement recent measurements by the STAR Collaboration (Adam 2021, Abdallah 2021, Abdallah 2022, Aboona 2025) of net-proton fluctuations showing non-monotonic energy dependence.
