# n alpha Resonating-Group Calculation with a Quark-Model G-Matrix NN Interaction

> We calculate n alpha phase-shifts and scattering observables in the resonating-group method, using the nuclear-matter G-matrix of an SU_6 quark-model NN interaction. The G-matrix is generated in the recent energy-independent procedure of the quark-model NN interaction with the continuous prescription for intermediate spectra, by assuming an appropriate Fermi momentum k_F=1.2 fm^-1. The n alpha RGM interaction kernels are evaluated with explicit treatments of the nonlocality and momentum dependence of partial-wave G-matrix components. The momentum dependence of the G-matrix components is different for each of the nucleon-exchange and interaction types. Without introducing any artificial parameters except for k_F, the central and spin-orbit components of the n alpha Born kernel are found to have reasonable strengths under the assumption of a rigid translationally invariant shell-model wave function of the alpha-cluster. The characteristic behaviors of three different exchange terms, corresponding to knockout, heavy-particle pickup and nucleon-rearrangement processes, are essentially the same between the case of previous local effective NN forces and the case of nonlocal G-matrix NN interactions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate n-alpha phase shifts and scattering observables using a quark-model G-matrix NN interaction without introducing any artificial parameters except for the Fermi momentum k_F = 1.2 fm^-1, finding that the central and spin-orbit components of the n-alpha Born kernel have reasonable strengths under the assumption of a rigid translationally invariant shell-model wave function of the alpha cluster.

## Method

The authors used the resonating-group method (RGM) in momentum representation (Lippmann-Schwinger RGM) with a G-matrix derived from an SU_6 quark-model NN interaction (models fss2 and FSS). The G-matrix was generated using an energy-independent procedure with the continuous prescription for intermediate spectra in symmetric nuclear matter, assuming a constant Fermi momentum k_F = 1.2 fm^-1. The n-alpha RGM interaction kernels were evaluated with explicit treatments of nonlocality and momentum dependence of partial-wave G-matrix components, without making any local approximation for the G-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum assumed: k_F = 1.2 fm^-1
- Neutron incident energy range for phase shift and cross section comparison: E_n less than 30 MeV
- The S-wave and P-wave phase shifts and low-energy differential cross sections and polarizations were reproduced reasonably well below E_n approximately 30 MeV
- The alpha cluster internal energy expression includes a Coulomb term: 2 e^2 sqrt(nu/pi)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous efforts to construct a unified set of quark-model B_8-B_8 potentials and apply them to few-body systems, linking bare and effective interactions through the G-matrix formalism. It builds on earlier n-alpha RGM studies by Sugie et al. (1955), Kanada et al. (1963), Omojola (1970), and Thompson and Tang (1971), who analyzed exchange kernels for effective central NN forces and classified them into knockout, heavy-particle pickup, and nucleon-rearrangement terms. The present work enables future application to hyperon-nucleus systems, to be reported in a successive paper, and addresses the claim by Nollett et al. (2007) that correct P-wave spin-orbit splitting requires three-nucleon interactions.
