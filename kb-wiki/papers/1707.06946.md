# Fermionic bound states in Minkowski-space: Light-cone singularities and structure

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-body bound system with spin $1/2$ constituent is addressed directly in the Minkowski space. In order to accomplish this aim we use the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and exploit the formal tool represented by the exact projection onto the null-plane. This formal step allows one i) to deal with end-point singularities one meets and ii) to find stable results, up to strongly relativistic regimes, that settles in strongly bound systems. We apply this technique to obtain the numerical dependence of the binding energies upon the coupling constants and the light-front amplitudes for a fermion-fermion $0^+$ state with interaction kernels, in ladder approximation, corresponding to scalar-, pseudoscalar- and vector boson exchanges, respectively. After completing the numerical survey of the previous cases, we extend our approach to a quark-antiquark system in $0^-$ state, taking both constituent-fermion and exchanged boson masses, from lattice calculations. Interestingly, the calculated light-front amplitudes for such a mock pion show peculiar signatures of the spin degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-fermion bound states can be solved directly in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation combined with light-front projection, provided that the end-point singularities arising from spin degrees of freedom are formally identified and integrated out, rather than circumvented through numerical smoothing functions as in prior work by Carbonell and Karmanov (2010).

## Method

The authors apply the Nakanishi integral representation to the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for a two-fermion system with spin 1/2 constituents, then perform an exact projection onto the light-front null-plane by integrating over the light-front component k^- of the relative momentum. This transforms the Bethe-Salpeter equation into a coupled system of integral equations for the Nakanishi weight functions, where the kernel is separated into non-singular contributions (k^+_D not equal to zero) and singular contributions arising from the case k^+_D = 0, which are treated analytically. The numerical solution is obtained as a generalized eigenvalue problem for the coupling constant as a function of binding energy, applied to fermion-fermion 0^+ states with scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector boson exchanges in ladder approximation, and extended to a quark-antiquark 0^- state with parameters from lattice calculations.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies B/m in the range [0.01-0.5] (where m is the constituent mass) were explored for the 0^+ two-fermion system, and the eigenvalues (coupling constants) fully agree with the results of Carbonell and Karmanov (2010) for all three interaction kernels considered.
* The light-front amplitudes for a mock pion (quark-antiquark 0^- state) were computed using constituent-fermion and exchanged-boson masses taken from lattice calculations, showing peculiar signatures of the spin degrees of freedom.
* The binding energies for the 0^+ two-fermion system with scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector boson exchanges were obtained as a function of the coupling constant g^2, with the numerical dependence illustrated for each interaction type.

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi integral representation approach previously applied to two-scalar bound states by Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2014, 2016) and to two-fermion ground states by de Paula, Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2016), now providing the full formal treatment of light-cone singularities that arise from spin degrees of freedom. The method differs from the explicitly covariant light-front approach of Carbonell and Karmanov (2010), which required a smoothing function to achieve stable eigenvalues; the present work formally identifies the singularities at k^+_D = 0 and integrates them out analytically. The results enable future calculation of transverse-momentum distributions for pions and other hadrons directly from Minkowski-space solutions, providing a non-perturbative tool for interpreting semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering experiments at CERN, JLab, and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
