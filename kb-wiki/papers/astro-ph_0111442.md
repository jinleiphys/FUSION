# The observational appearance of strange stars

> Strange quark matter with the density of ~ 5\times 10^{14} g cm^{-3} might exist up to the surface of a strange star. This differs qualitatively from the case of a neutron star and opens observational possibilities to distinguish strange stars from neutron stars. The thermal emission of photons and e^+e^- pairs from the bare (or nearly bare) quark surface of hot strange stars is reviewed. Bare strange stars are reasonable candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters. The bursting activity of a soft gamma-ray repeater may be explained by fast heating of the bare quark surface of a strange star up to the temperature of ~ (1-2)\times 10^9 K and its subsequent thermal emission.

## Key claim

Bare strange stars, whose quark matter surface extends to the stellar photosphere, can be distinguished from neutron stars by their thermal emission of photons and electron-positron pairs, and they are reasonable candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters (SGRs) if the surface temperature reaches approximately (1-2) × 10⁹ K.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical emission mechanisms from the bare quark surface of strange stars, calculating the flux of equilibrium photons (with energies above the plasma frequency of ∼20-25 MeV) and the flux of electron-positron pairs created by the strong electric field (∼5 × 10¹⁷ V cm⁻¹) at the quark surface. It also examines the effects of a normal-matter atmosphere or envelope on the thermal emission, and applies the model to explain the light curves and spectra of SGR giant flares.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark matter density at the surface: ∼5 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³
- Plasma frequency energy at the surface: ℏωp ∼ 20-25 MeV (for baryon density nb ∼ (1.5-2)n0, where n0 = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- Electric field at the quark surface: ∼5 × 10¹⁷ V cm⁻¹, which is ∼38 times the critical field Ecr = 1.3 × 10¹⁶ V cm⁻¹
- Pair creation rate in vacuum at that field: W± ∼ 2.5 × 10⁵³ cm⁻³ s⁻¹
- Flux of e⁺e⁻ pairs from unit surface: f± ∼ 10³⁹ (TS/10⁹ K)³ exp[-11.9 (TS/10⁹ K)⁻¹] J(ζ) cm⁻² s⁻¹
- Temperature range where e⁺e⁻ pair emission dominates: 8 × 10⁸ < TS < 5 × 10¹⁰ K
- Maximum luminosity in e⁺e⁻ pairs far from the star: L±max ∼ 10³⁶ ergs s⁻¹
- Upper limit on non-equilibrium photon emissivity: ξneq ≤ 10⁻⁴
- Critical mass for atmosphere formation: ΔM₁ ∼ 7 × 10¹¹ (A/Z²) (TS/10⁸ K)³/² (R/10⁶ cm)² g
- Peak luminosity of giant SGR flares: ∼10⁴⁵ ergs s⁻¹
- Total thermal energy accumulated in the 5 March 1979 event model: Q = 9.2 × 10⁴⁴ ergs with Δt = 370 s
- Total thermal energy accumulated in the 27 August 1998 event model: Q = 6.4 × 10⁴⁴ ergs with Δt = 270 s
- Rise time for giant outbursts (Linput ∼ 10⁴⁵ ergs s⁻¹): < 10⁻³ s
- Rise time for typical outbursts with superconducting SQM (Δ₀ ≥ 1 MeV): ∼10⁻¹-10⁻³ s

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for strange stars originally proposed by Witten (1984) and Alcock, Farhi, and Olinto (1986), and builds on earlier work by the author on pair creation from bare quark surfaces (Usov 1998) and thermal emission mechanisms (Usov 2001a). The paper enables observational tests to distinguish strange stars from neutron stars, particularly through super-Eddington luminosities and hard photon spectra, and provides a specific model for SGRs that can be tested against future observations of giant and typical flares.
