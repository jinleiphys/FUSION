# Heavy ions and string theory

> We review a selection of recent developments in the application of ideas of string theory to heavy ion physics. Our topics divide naturally into equilibrium and non-equilibrium phenomena. On the non-equilibrium side, we discuss generalizations of Bjorken flow, numerical simulations of black hole formation in asymptotically anti-de Sitter geometries, equilibration in the dual field theory, and hard probes. On the equilibrium side, we summarize improved holographic QCD, extraction of transport coefficients, inclusion of chemical potentials, and approaches to the phase diagram. We close with some possible directions for future research.

## Key claim

This review argues that gauge-gravity duality (AdS/CFT) provides a useful theoretical framework for studying strongly coupled aspects of heavy ion collisions, particularly non-equilibrium dynamics and finite-density QCD, despite the fact that the most tractable dual theories (such as N=4 super-Yang-Mills) are not QCD itself. The authors qualify that the utility of the approach depends on the degree to which universal features of strongly coupled gauge theories can be extracted and applied to QCD, or on the success of "bottom-up" holographic models engineered to match QCD properties.

## Method

The paper reviews a selection of recent developments in applying string theory and the AdS/CFT correspondence to heavy ion physics, dividing the topics into equilibrium and non-equilibrium phenomena. ## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity over entropy density: eta/s = 1/(4 pi) is found across a wide swath of gravity theories, cited from Policastro (2001), Buchel (2003), and Kovtun (2004).
* For top-energy RHIC collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the trapped surface entropy estimate gives N_charged >= 4700, compared to observed N_charged approx 5060 from Back (2002).
* At LHC energies sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the same formula gives N_charged >= 27000, while data indicates N_charged approx 17000.
* With an ultraviolet cutoff Lambda_UV = 2 GeV, the trapped surface model predicts N_charged >= 16800 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, and N_charged >= 3820 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
* The ratio epsilon/T^4 approx 11 for 1.2 T_c less than T less than 2 T_c from lattice results, recovered for black holes in AdS_5 when L^3/G_5 approx 1.9.
* The hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions requires eta/s in the range 0.08 less than eta/s less than 0.4 to reproduce observed harmonic flow coefficients.

## Context

This review extends and complements earlier reviews by Casalderrey-Solana (2011) and Adams (2012), which provide more discussion of heavy quark and quarkonia topics and ultracold gases respectively, while the present work devotes more attention to non-equilibrium dynamics and physics at nonzero baryon density. The trapped surface calculations build on the work of Gubser (2008) and Gubser (2009) on conformal soliton collisions, and the review discusses extensions by Kiritsis (2011) that incorporate a running saturation scale. The review enables future work on understanding the transition to hydrodynamics from arbitrary initial states, the effect of pre-equilibrium phases on observables such as photon emission and heavy quark diffusion, and the construction of realistic holographic models of the QCD phase diagram including a critical point.
