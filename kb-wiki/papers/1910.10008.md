# Extraction of gluon distributions from structure functions at small x in holographic QCD

> We investigate the nucleon and pion gluon distribution functions in the framework of holographic QCD, focusing on the small Bjorken x region. Based on an approximate relation, the gluon distributions are extracted from structure functions of the unpolarized deep inelastic scattering which can be calculated with a holographic QCD model, assuming the Pomeron exchange. All the adjustable parameters of the model are determined with the HERA data of the proton structure functions. We explicitly show that the extracted proton gluon distribution is consistent with results of the recent global QCD analysis. The structure functions of the pion can be computed without any additional parameter, which enables us to predict its gluon distribution also. We find that the resulting pion gluon density is smaller than the proton's, and agrees with the recent global QCD analysis result within the uncertainties.

# Extraction of gluon distributions from structure functions at small x in holographic QCD

## Key claim

The gluon distributions of both the proton and pion at small Bjorken x can be extracted from deep inelastic scattering structure functions calculated in a holographic QCD model, assuming Pomeron exchange, and the resulting proton gluon distribution is consistent with recent global QCD analysis results, while the pion gluon density is smaller than the proton's and agrees with the JAM collaboration analysis within uncertainties, but the extraction relies on an approximate relation whose validity at very small x (down to 10^{-6}) has not been explicitly verified.

## Method

The authors calculate the unpolarized deep inelastic scattering structure functions F2 and FL for the proton and pion in a holographic QCD framework using the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) kernel for Pomeron exchange in five-dimensional AdS space, with confinement effects included via a modified kernel. The four adjustable model parameters (g0^2, rho, z0, and a weight w on the longitudinal photon component) are determined by a simultaneous fit to HERA data for proton F2 and FL in the kinematic region x ≤ 10^{-2} and Q^2 ≤ 10 GeV^2. The gluon distributions are then extracted from the calculated structure functions using an approximate NLO QCD relation that expresses the gluon distribution in terms of F2 and FL.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters from MINUIT fit to HERA data: g0^2 = 162.15 ± 6.71, rho = 0.7798 ± 0.0021, z0 = 3.059 ± 0.079, w = 0.6198 ± 0.0923
- Chi-square per degree of freedom: χ²_d.o.f. = 1.295 (240.8/186)
- Kinematic region of fit: x ≤ 10^{-2} and Q^2 ≤ 10 GeV^2
- Gluon distributions extracted at reference scale Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- Pion gluon distribution predicted without any additional parameter beyond those fit to proton data
- Pion decay constant used: f_π; chiral limit sigma = (332 MeV)^3

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to DIS at small x, building on the BPST Pomeron kernel (Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan, 2007) and previous applications to proton structure functions (Brower et al., 2010; Watanabe et al., 2012, 2013, 2015). The approximate relation used to extract gluon distributions from structure functions was proposed by Cooper-Sarkar et al. (1987). The results are compared with recent global QCD analyses from the NNPDF collaboration (Ball et al., 2017), CTEQ-TEA collaboration (Hou et al., 2019), and the first Monte Carlo global analysis of pion PDFs by the JAM collaboration (Barry et al., 2018). This work enables predictions for pion gluon distributions, which are poorly constrained experimentally, and demonstrates that holographic QCD can provide an alternative nonperturbative approach to parton distribution functions at small x.
