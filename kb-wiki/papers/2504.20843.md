# A nuclear mass model rooted in chiral effective field theory

> We develop a nuclear mass model that is based on chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to leading order. Nuclear binding energies are computed via the Hartree-Fock method using a Hamiltonian from delta-full chiral effective field theory. We employ Hartree-Fock emulators to adjust $11$ low-energy constants in the chiral interaction to binding energies of $18$ even-even nuclei. When applied to $107$ even-even nuclei with mass numbers $16\leq A\leq 56$ the chiral mass model exhibits an overall root-mean-square deviation of $3.5$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors develop a nuclear mass model based on chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to leading order, but the model achieves only a root-mean-square deviation of 3.5 MeV for 107 even-even nuclei, which is less accurate than existing phenomenological mass models and comparable to the semi-empirical Bethe-Weizsacker formula (2.07 MeV RMS on the same set). The key hedge is that the Hartree-Fock approximation with renormalized low-energy constants is intended to demonstrate feasibility rather than competitive accuracy.

## Method

The authors use a Hamiltonian from delta-full chiral effective field theory at NNLO (the ΔNNLO_GO(394) interaction) and solve it via the Hartree-Fock method in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis, including three-nucleon forces in the normal-ordered two-body approximation. They construct projection-based Hartree-Fock emulators using eigenvector continuation (reduced basis method) with 68 training points per nucleus to enable rapid evaluation of the objective function. They calibrate 11 low-energy constants (short-range two-body contacts and three-body contacts c_D and c_E) by solving a nonlinear least-squares problem against binding energies of 18 even-even nuclei, then evaluate the model on 107 even-even nuclei with mass numbers 16 ≤ A ≤ 56.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of 3.5 MeV for the full set of 107 even-even nuclei with mass numbers 16 ≤ A ≤ 56
- RMS deviation of 2.17 MeV for the 18 nuclei used in calibration
- Largest residuals: 11.4 MeV for Si-28, 15.8 MeV for S-32, and 12.0 MeV for S-48
- The semi-empirical Bethe-Weizsacker formula yields RMS deviation of 2.07 MeV on the same 107 nuclei
- Ab initio computations by Stroberg (2021) on about 700 nuclei with 2 ≤ A ≤ 56 found RMS deviation of 3.3 MeV
- The original ΔNNLO_GO(394) interaction yields binding energies of about 50% of experimental values at the central parameter point
- The emulator reproduces ground-state energies with absolute percentage error below 10% for 220 validation points, and frequently better than 1% near experimentally relevant energies
- The three-body contact c_D was optimized to -2.0000 (at the boundary allowed) and c_E to -0.6198, compared to original values of +0.0814 and -0.0024 respectively

## Context

This work extends efforts to build nuclear mass models rooted in chiral effective field theory, following the density-functional approach of Zurek (2024) who achieved 1.5 MeV RMS at NNLO including Delta excitations, and the demonstration by Sun (2022) that three-particle-three-hole correlations can be approximately removed via renormalization of three-body contacts. The model is less accurate than phenomenological approaches such as the Finite-Range Liquid Drop Model by Moller (2016) with 0.56 MeV RMS, the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model by Kortelainen (2010) with 0.97 MeV RMS, and the model by Goriely (2013) with 0.50 MeV RMS. The work enables future development of mass models where short-range contacts are systematically renormalized to absorb beyond-mean-field correlations, potentially improving accuracy while maintaining a foundation in chiral effective field theory.
