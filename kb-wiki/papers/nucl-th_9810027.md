# Rescattering effects in coherent eta-photoproduction on the deuteron

> Rescattering effects in coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron are studied in the region of the S_11(1535) resonance. For the elementary reaction mechanism on the nucleon, an effective isobar model with inclusion of resonances and Born terms is used. The resonance parameters are fixed by considering pion photoproduction only. For the coherent eta production on the deuteron we have considered the impulse approximation taking into account the P_11(1440), D_13(1520), and S_11(1535) resonances, rescattering, and meson exchange currents. For the rescattering mechanism a coupled channel model including the dominant S_11(1535) resonance is used.

## Key claim

Rescattering effects in coherent eta-photoproduction on the deuteron reduce the total cross section by up to about 50%, but this is considered an upper limit due to uncertainties in the NS11 transition potential; the actual reduction is likely smaller with a more realistic choice of parameters.

## Method

The authors used an effective isobar model with resonance parameters fixed solely by fitting pion photoproduction data, then applied it to predict eta photoproduction on the proton without additional fitting. For the deuteron, they included impulse approximation with P11(1440), D13(1520), and S11(1535) resonances, rescattering via a coupled channel model for NN and NS11 channels using static Bonn OBEPQ(ABC) meson exchange potentials, and meson exchange current contributions.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section reduction through rescattering: up to about 50% (considered an upper limit)
- Meson exchange current contributions: about 3% of the total cross section
- Ratio |g^s_gammaNS11 / g^p_gammaNS11| extracted from the model: approximately 0.2, somewhat larger near threshold
- Cutoff parameter for pionic Born background: Lambda_piN = 600 MeV
- Cutoff for piNS11 and etaNS11 transition potentials: Lambda = 1 GeV
- Complete calculation underestimates experimental data by about 30 to 40% for photon energies below 750 MeV
- First order rescattering (one-loop approximation) breaks down above E_gamma = 750 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Bennhold and Tanabe (1991) separable resonance model for coupled piN and etaN channels to coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron, treating rescattering for the first time in a coupled channel model. It contradicts the much larger rescattering effects found by Hoshi et al. (1979) and the smaller effects found by Kamalov, Tiator, and Bennhold (1997) using the Kerman-McManus-Thaler formalism. The results enable future incorporation of the effective couplings into calculations of the incoherent process gamma d -> eta NN to test whether a discrepancy between coherent and incoherent production exists, as claimed by Hoffmann-Rothe et al. (1997).
