# Nucleon self-energy in the relativistic Brueckner approach

> The formalism of the relativistic (or Dirac-) Brueckner approach in infinite nuclear matter is described. As nucleon-nucleon interaction the one-boson exchange potentials Bonn A,B,C and for comparison the Walecka model are used. The T-matrix is determined from the Thompson equation and is projected onto five covariant amplitudes. By the restriction to positive energy states an ambiguity arises in the relativistic Brueckner approach which is discussed here in terms of the pseudo-scalar and the pseudo-vector projection. The influence of the coupling of the nucleon via the T-matrix as an effective two-nucleon interaction to the nuclear medium is expressed by the self-energy. In particular we investigate the scalar and vector components of the self-energy for the different one-boson exchange potentials and discuss their density and momentum dependence. We estimate the uncertainty of the self-energy due to the pseudo-scalar and the pseudo-vector choice. Usually the momentum dependence of the self-energy is thought to be weak, however, we find that this depends on the one-boson exchange potentials. For the Bonn potentials, in contrast to the $σω$-potential, the momentum dependence is strikingly strong above as well as below the Fermi surface. We compare with the results of other groups and study the effects on the equation of state and the nucleon optical potential.

# Nucleon self-energy in the relativistic Brueckner approach

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the nucleon self-energy in the relativistic Brueckner approach is not universally weak as commonly assumed; for the Bonn A, B, and C potentials the momentum dependence is strikingly strong both below and above the Fermi surface, in contrast to the σω-potential where it remains weak.

## Method

The authors solve the Thompson equation for the T-matrix in infinite nuclear matter using the Bonn A, B, C one-boson exchange potentials and, for comparison, the σω-potential from the Walecka model. The on-shell T-matrix is projected onto five covariant amplitudes using both pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector representations, and the nucleon self-energy components (scalar Σ_s, time-like Σ_0, and space-like Σ_v) are computed by integrating the effective two-body interaction over the Fermi sea in the nuclear matter rest frame, with self-consistency achieved through iteration of the effective mass.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass M* for the σω-potential with pseudo-scalar decomposition reproduces values from Horowitz and Serot (1987), with slight deviations above saturation density (k_F = 1.42 fm⁻¹) attributed to different propagator choices.
- For the Bonn potentials at saturation density (k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹), the scalar and vector self-energy components differ by about 5 MeV between the three parametrizations, and by less than 10 MeV at k_F = 1.90 fm⁻¹.
- The space-like vector self-energy component -k_F Σ_v is about 7% of Σ_0 at saturation density for the Bonn potentials.
- The momentum dependence of the self-energy for Bonn A, B, C potentials is of the order of several 100 MeV over its full range below and above the Fermi surface.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Brueckner formalism developed by Horowitz and Serot (1987) and Brockmann and Machleidt (1984) by systematically comparing the Bonn A, B, C potentials with the σω-model and by examining both pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector representations of the T-matrix. The results enable improved treatment of nuclear self-energies in applications to finite nuclei via density-dependent relativistic mean field theory and to heavy ion collisions, where the scalar and vector components become unbalanced due to their different Lorentz transformation properties.
