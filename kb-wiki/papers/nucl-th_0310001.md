# Complex Energy Method in 4-Body Faddeev-Yakubovsky Equations

> The Complex Energy Method [Prog. Theor. Phys. {\bf 109}, 869L (2003)] is applied to the 4-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in the 4-nucleon system. We obtain a well converged solution in all energy regions below and above the 4-nucleon break-up threshold.

## Key claim

The Complex Energy Method (CEM) yields well-converged solutions of the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for the four-nucleon system in all energy regions, including above the four-body break-up threshold where neither the principal value prescription nor contour deformation techniques had previously succeeded.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for four identical nucleons in the \(J^\pi = 0^+\), \(T=0\) state, using a separable Yamaguchi potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the Energy Dependent Pole Expansion (EDPE) to represent three-body and [2+2] subamplitudes as rank-4 separable forms. Instead of taking the limit \(\varepsilon \to 0\) analytically, they solved the equations at finite complex energies \(E + i\varepsilon + \zeta\) (with \(\varepsilon\) values of 0.5 or 0.75 MeV and \(\zeta = 0, \pm 0.125\) MeV) and then numerically extrapolated to the real-energy limit using the point method for analytical continuation.

## Key numbers

- Phase shift \(\delta = 44.34016^\circ\) and inelasticity \(\eta = 1.000000\) for case (I) at 1.5 MeV above the 3N+N threshold, agreeing perfectly with the principal value prescription (PVR) result.
- Phase shift \(\delta = 12.38198^\circ\) and inelasticity \(\eta = 0.948069\) for case (II) at 1 MeV above the 2N+2N threshold, also matching the PVR result.
- Phase shift \(\delta = -5.5095^\circ\) and inelasticity \(\eta = 0.898657\) for case (III) at 1 MeV below the four-body break-up threshold.
- Phase shift \(\delta = -61.669^\circ\) and inelasticity \(\eta = 0.74582\) for case (IV) at 12 MeV above the four-body break-up threshold, converged to within 4 digits.
- Integration cutoffs: 200 fm\(^{-1}\) for two-body momentum \(k\), 40 fm\(^{-1}\) for three-body Jacobi momenta \(p\), and 16 fm\(^{-1}\) for four-body Jacobi momenta \(q\).

## Context

This work extends the Complex Energy Method, previously revived for two- and three-nucleon systems by the same group (Uzu et al., 2003), to the four-nucleon system where neither the principal value prescription (used below three-body threshold by Uzu et al. and others) nor contour deformation techniques (used between three- and four-body thresholds by Fonseca et al.) had been successfully applied above the four-body break-up threshold. The method enables future calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon forces and higher partial waves, where evidence for three-nucleon forces is expected to be more pronounced at high energies.
