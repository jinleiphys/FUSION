# Entropy Production in Collisions of Relativistic Heavy Ions -- a signal for Quark-Gluon Plasma phase transition?

> Entropy production in the compression stage of heavy ion collisions is discussed within three distinct macroscopic models (i.e. generalized RHTA, geometrical overlap model and three-fluid hydrodynamics). We find that within these models \sim 80% or more of the experimentally observed final-state entropy is created in the early stage. It is thus likely followed by a nearly isentropic expansion. We employ an equation of state with a first-order phase transition. For low net baryon density, the entropy density exhibits a jump at the phase boundary. However, the excitation function of the specific entropy per net baryon, S/A, does not reflect this jump. This is due to the fact that for final states (of the compression) in the mixed phase, the baryon density ρ_B increases with \sqrt{s}, but not the temperature T. Calculations within the three-fluid model show that a large fraction of the entropy is produced by nuclear shockwaves in the projectile and target. With increasing beam energy, this fraction of S/A decreases. At \sqrt{s}=20 AGeV it is on the order of the entropy of the newly produced particles around midrapidity. Hadron ratios are calculated for the entropy values produced initially at beam energies from 2 to 200 AGeV.

## Key claim

The excitation function of specific entropy per net baryon (S/A) does not exhibit a jump at the phase transition boundary, even though the equation of state includes a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma; this is because for final states in the mixed phase, baryon density increases with collision energy while temperature remains roughly constant.

## Method

Three macroscopic models were used to calculate entropy production in the compression stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions: a geometrical overlap model, a generalized one-dimensional Rankine-Hugoniot-Taub Adiabate (RHTA) shock model, and a three-fluid hydrodynamical model. All models employed the same equation of state, combining a sigma-omega model for nucleons with an ideal pion gas and an MIT bag model for the quark-gluon plasma phase (bag parameter B^(1/4)=235 MeV), matched by Gibbs conditions. The three-fluid model allowed for finite mean free path effects by treating projectile, target, and produced particles as separate fluids that interact locally.

## Key numbers

- In the geometrical overlap model, compressed matter enters the mixed phase at sqrt(s) ≈ 2.4 AGeV with S/A = 4.5, and pure QGP matter is produced with S/A = 12 at sqrt(s) ≈ 4.6 AGeV.
- In the generalized RHTA shock model, the participant matter reaches the pure QGP phase at T = 65 MeV at sqrt(s) ≈ 3.3 AGeV; at AGS energies S/A ≈ 10, with energy density ε = 5 GeV/fm^3, baryon density ρ_B = 2.0 fm^(-3), and entropy density s = 19 fm^(-3).
- For Pb+Pb at SPS in the RHTA model, ε ≈ 50 GeV/fm^3, ρ_B ≈ 6 fm^(-3), s ≈ 150 fm^(-3), and S/A = 25.
- In the three-fluid model, S/A = 11 for AGS and S/A = 38 for SPS energies (sqrt(s)=20 AGeV); at SPS, 50% of the total entropy is due to shockwaves in projectile and target fluids.
- The three-fluid model yields S/A = 35 at sqrt(s)=18 AGeV and S/A = 38 at sqrt(s)=20 AGeV.
- The critical temperature for vanishing baryon density is T_C ≈ 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of entropy production in the compression stage of heavy ion collisions (Stoecker, Bertsch, Csernai) and compares with experimental data on hadron ratios at AGS and SPS energies (PBMAGS, CSCG, Sollfrank, Letessier). The three-fluid model results are consistent with S/A values extracted from data (S/A=10±2.5 at AGS, S/A=40±10 at SPS) and with the microscopic transport model UrQMD (S/A=12 at AGS). The finding that the S/A excitation function shows no phase transition signature enables future work to search for the QGP transition in other observables such as directed in-plane flow, and to study rapidity and transverse momentum spectra at freeze-out.
