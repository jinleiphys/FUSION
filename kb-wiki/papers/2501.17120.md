# Goldstone bosons at non-zero temperature

> Spontaneous symmetry breaking in quantum field theories at non-zero temperature still holds fundamental open questions, in particular what happens to vacuum Goldstone bosons when the temperature is increased. By investigating a complex scalar field theory on the lattice we demonstrate that Goldstone bosons at non-zero temperature behave like screened massless particle-like excitations, so-called thermoparticles, which continue to exist even in the symmetry-restored phase of the theory. We provide non-perturbative evidence for the functional form of the Goldstone mode's dissipative behaviour, and determine its corresponding spectral properties. Since the persistence of thermal Goldstone modes within symmetry-restored phases is predicted to be a model-independent characteristic, this has fundamental consequences for systems in which continuous symmetries are restored at high temperatures.

# Goldstone bosons at non-zero temperature

## Key claim

Goldstone bosons at non-zero temperature behave as screened massless particle-like excitations (thermoparticles) that persist even in the symmetry-restored phase above the critical temperature, provided the system satisfies local causality, translation and rotation covariance, and thermal equilibrium conditions. The authors provide non-perturbative lattice evidence for this claim in a U(1) complex scalar field theory, but note that the persistence is predicted to be model-independent.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of a U(1) complex scalar field theory with action discretized on a lattice of spacing a, using bare parameters (am0, g0) = (0.297 i, 0.85). They analyzed Euclidean two-point correlation functions and spatial screening correlators on spatially symmetric volumes L^3 x L_tau, with N_tau = 32 (broken phase, T/|v| ≈ 0.35) and N_tau = 2 (symmetry-restored phase, T/|v| ≈ 5.65), performing infinite-volume extrapolations and fitting the data to functional forms derived from the non-perturbative spectral representation of Bros and collaborators.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum expectation value: a^2|v|^2 = 0.00782(4), extracted from infinite-volume extrapolation of C_L(0, L/2) in the N_tau = 32 broken phase
- Temperature ratio in broken phase: T/|v| ≈ 0.35 for N_tau = 32
- Temperature ratio in symmetry-restored phase: T/|v| ≈ 5.65 for N_tau = 2
- UV cutoff: Lambda/|v| = pi/(a|v|) ≈ 36, indicating small cutoff effects beyond a few lattice spacings
- Short-distance range: |x| ≲ 11 a for the chosen parameters
- Screening correlator fits on N_tau = 2 data gave chi^2/d.o.f. ≲ 1 for all volumes (N_s = 64, 96, 128) using single-exponential ansatz
- The damping exponent gamma_L from two-point function fits and screening mass m_L from screening correlator fits converge toward each other in the infinite-volume limit, with fit errors less than 1% and chi^2/d.o.f. ≲ 1

## Context

This work provides the first non-perturbative lattice confirmation of the theoretical prediction by Bros and collaborators (1996) that Goldstone bosons persist as massless thermoparticles above the critical temperature in systems with continuous symmetry breaking. It extends earlier lattice studies of massive thermoparticles in scalar theories (Lowdon, 2024) and in QCD (Lowdon, 2022; Bala, 2023), and builds on the non-perturbative spectral representation framework developed by Bros and collaborators (1992, 1996). The results suggest that the high-temperature phase transitions in chiral and physical QCD may reflect changes in dissipative effects on (pseudo-)Goldstone bosons rather than a change in fundamental degrees of freedom, and that similar signatures should be observable in condensed matter systems such as spin waves in ferromagnets or Landau phonons in superfluids above their critical temperatures.
