# Vertex renormalization of weak interactions in compact stars: beyond leading order

> Neutrino emission rate from baryonic matter in neutron stars via weak neutral vector interaction is computed up to order O(v_F^6), where v_F is the Fermi velocity in units of speed of light. The vector current polarization tensors are evaluated with full vertices which include resummed series in the particle-hole channel. The neutrino emissivity is enhanced compared to the O(v_F^4) order up of 10% for values v_F< 0.4 characteristic to baryons in compact stars.

## Key claim

The neutrino emissivity from baryonic matter in neutron stars via weak neutral vector currents is computed up to order O(v_F^6), where v_F is the Fermi velocity in units of speed of light; the correction beyond the leading non-zero term is below 10% for values v_F ≤ 0.4 characteristic of baryons in compact stars, providing evidence for convergence of the series expansion.

## Method

The vector current polarization tensors were evaluated using full vertices that include resummed series in the particle-hole channel, employing Nambu-Gor'kov propagators within a non-zero temperature formalism. The neutrino emissivity was computed via a double expansion in small parameters arising in the limit of small momentum transfer, with the polarization tensors expanded to order O(η^6) and O(δ^6) where η = q v_F / ξ and δ = |q| v_F / ω. The calculation corrected the next-to-leading order (NLO) contribution from prior work and extended the calculation to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Key numbers

- Neutrino emissivity enhancement compared to O(v_F^4) order: up to 10% for v_F ≤ 0.4, and up to about 20% for v_F = 0.6 in the temperature range 0 ≤ τ ≤ 0.92, where τ = T/T_c with T_c being the critical temperature of the pairing phase transition.
- The scalar polarization tensor Π_00/(2ν(0)) expands as - (4δ^4/45)(1 + 25δ^2/21)(F*F^+)_0 - (4δ^6/45)(F*F^+)_2 + O(δ^8).
- The spatial polarization tensor Π_ii/(2v_F^2ν(0)) expands as -(2δ^2/9 + 22δ^4/135 + 74δ^6/567)(F*F^+)_0 - (14δ^4/135 + 286δ^6/2835)(F*F^+)_2 + O(δ^8).
- The neutrino emissivity formula: ε = (16 G^2 c_V^2 ν(0) v_F^4)/(1215π^3) I(z) T^7, where z = Δ/T and c_V = 1 for neutrons and c_V = 0.08 for protons, with N_f = 3 neutrino flavors.

## Context

This work extends and corrects the prior calculation by Sedrakian (2006) for neutrino emission via neutral vector currents in superfluid baryonic matter, and reconciles it with results from Leinson (2006), Kolomeitsev (2008), and Steiner (2008). The O(v_F^4) term is now in agreement with those references, resolving a previous disagreement where Sedrakian (2006) had obtained a mathematically spurious NLO contribution linear in nucleon recoil. The computation confirms that the nonvanishing contribution to vector-current emissivity arises at order O(v_F^4), and that this contribution remains negligible compared to the axial vector neutrino emissivity computed by Flowers (1976), Kaminker (1999), and Kolomeitsev (2008), which is unaffected by vertex corrections. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino cooling in neutron stars and revise the density response functions of cold neutron matter from Sedrakian (2010).
