# Transport and Connection to Heavy-ion Collisions via Heavy Flavor Probes

> The heavy ion experiments in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are going through upgrade in the next five years, shifting their focus more on the hard processes in the new runs. One of the main goals is to draw a finer image for the quark gluon plasma (QGP). The heavy flavor probes , which witness the whole history of heavy ion collision are particularly sensitive to test the properties of QGP formed in such collisions. The lattice results for heavy flavor probes provide transport and phenomenological models crucial inputs to describe the experimental observations like the strong suppression of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the non-zero azimuthal anisotropy at low $p_T$. In the last two years we have seen significant advances in the lattice QCD studies of heavy flavor probes, including the in-medium quarkonium properties, the complex static quark-antiquark potential and the heavy quark diffusion from lattice simulations at nonzero temperature. These achievements substantially deepen our understanding of the fate of quarkonium, the screening/unscreening of the complex potential and the temperature and quark mass dependence of the heavy quark diffusion in thermal medium. In these proceedings, we review recent results and briefly discuss possible directions in these studies.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of heavy flavor probes now provide non-perturbative inputs for transport and phenomenological models, but the extraction of spectral functions from Euclidean correlators remains ill-posed and relies on physically-motivated model Ansätze whose applicability must be carefully validated for each observable.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of three heavy flavor observables: (1) in-medium quarkonium properties from pseudo-scalar and vector current-current correlators, using relativistic heavy quarks and NRQCD with extended source operators; (2) the complex static quark-antiquark potential from thermal Wilson loops and Wilson line correlators, with spectral reconstruction using cut Lorentzian, Gaussian, and HTL-inspired Ansätze; (3) heavy quark diffusion coefficients from vector current-current correlators and from color-electric and color-magnetic field correlators, the latter employing gradient flow for noise reduction and a three-step matching procedure for renormalization.

## Key numbers
- For charmonium, no resonance peaks are needed at and above 1.1 \(T_c\); for bottomonium, a resonance peak is still needed up to 1.5 \(T_c\) (quenched study on large and fine lattices, Ding 2021).
- In full QCD with (2+1)-flavor HISQ at \(m_\pi \simeq 320\) MeV, pseudo-scalar charmonium shows no resonance peak down to 220 MeV, while bottomonium resonance persists up to 251 MeV (Ali 2023).
- NRQCD calculations at physical pion mass show no mass shift for \(\Upsilon\) and \(\chi_{b0}\) ground and excited states up to 333 MeV; thermal width increases with temperature and is larger for higher excited states (Larsen 2019).
- The complex potential from HotQCD (Bazavov 2023) in the temperature range 153 MeV \(\leq T \leq\) 352 MeV shows no screening of the real part up to \(r \approx 1.0\) fm; the imaginary part increases with \(r\) and temperature, and for \(T > 180\) MeV at \(rT \approx 1\) the imaginary potential exceeds the temperature.
- Full QCD results for \(2\pi T D_s\) from color-electric and color-magnetic correlators (Altenkort 2023) show weak dependence on quark mass and slow increase with temperature; at crossover temperature the value is small, consistent with AdS/CFT and ALICE phenomenological estimates.

## Context
This proceedings paper reviews lattice QCD advances from 2022-2023 that extend earlier quenched studies (Ding 2012, Aarts 2011, Kim 2018) to full QCD with dynamical quarks. The in-medium quarkonium results confirm and extend the quenched findings of Ding (2021) on dissociation temperatures. The complex potential calculations (Bazavov 2023, Bala 2021) replace the original static screening picture of Matsui and Satz (1986) with a dynamical melting mechanism involving Landau damping and singlet-to-octet transitions. The heavy quark diffusion results (Altenkort 2023) provide the first full QCD determination of \(\kappa_E\) and \(\kappa_B\), enabling phenomenological modeling of heavy flavor observables at RHIC and LHC and supporting the nearly-perfect fluid nature of QGP.
