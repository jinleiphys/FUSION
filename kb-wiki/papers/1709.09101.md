# Note on X(3872) production at hadron colliders and its molecular structure

> The production of the X(3872) as a hadronic molecule in hadron colliders is clarified. We show that the conclusion of Bignamini et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 103 (2009) 162001, that the production of the X(3872) at high $p_T$ implies a non-molecular structure, does not hold. In particular, using the well understood properties of the deuteron wave function as an example, we identify the relevant scales in the production process.

# Note on X(3872) production at hadron colliders and its molecular structure

## Key claim

The conclusion of Bignamini et al. (2009) that the observed production rate of X(3872) at high transverse momentum at hadron colliders rules out a hadronic molecule interpretation does not hold, because the momentum-space region over which the bound-state wave function is integrated must be taken much larger than the binding momentum, approximately 300 MeV rather than 35 MeV, to properly account for the short-range part of the wave function.

## Method

The authors analyze the inequality used by Bignamini et al. (2009) that relates the production cross section to an integral over the bound-state wave function in momentum space, and identify the integration region R as the critical parameter. They compute the integrated wave function for the deuteron, a well-established hadronic molecule with similar binding momentum and range of forces, using wave functions from potentials with and without one-pion exchange (OPE) and with different regulator cutoffs. They then estimate inclusive X(3872) production cross sections at Tevatron and LHC-7 by combining short-distance D0D*0 production from Monte Carlo event generators (Pythia and Herwig) with long-distance hadronic-molecule dynamics from effective field theory, using Gaussian regulators with cutoffs from 0.1 to 1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The binding momentum of the deuteron is approximately 45 MeV, similar to that of X(3872).
- Bignamini et al. (2009) used R approximately 35 MeV (of order the binding momentum) and obtained an upper bound of 0.071 nb for the X(3872) production cross section at Tevatron.
- The CDF measurement of the X(3872) production cross section at Tevatron is 37–115 nb; the CMS measurement at LHC-7 is 13–39 nb.
- With a cutoff Lambda = 0.1 GeV (corresponding to R approximately 0.06 GeV), the estimated cross section is 0.07 nb (Herwig) or 0.05 nb (Pythia) at Tevatron, and 0.12 nb (Herwig) or 0.04 nb (Pythia) at LHC-7.
- With a cutoff Lambda = 0.5 GeV (R approximately 0.3 GeV), the estimated cross section becomes 7 nb (Herwig) or 5 nb (Pythia) at Tevatron, and 13 nb (Herwig) or 4 nb (Pythia) at LHC-7.
- With a cutoff Lambda = 1.0 GeV (R approximately 0.6 GeV), the estimated cross section becomes 29 nb (Herwig) or 20 nb (Pythia) at Tevatron, and 55 nb (Herwig) or 15 nb (Pythia) at LHC-7.
- The D+D*- channel, with a binding momentum of 126 MeV, becomes dynamical for large cutoffs and contributes an enhancement factor of about three.

## Context

This work directly challenges the argument of Bignamini et al. (2009) that copious X(3872) production at hadron colliders rules out a molecular D0D*0 structure. It extends the rescattering argument of Artoisenet et al. (2009) by using the deuteron as an explicit example to demonstrate that the relevant momentum scale for saturating the wave-function integral is approximately 2M_pi (about 300 MeV), not the binding momentum. The results enable future calculations of X(3872) production to use consistent regulator choices and show that, while production reactions are not predictive within hadronic theory due to regulator dependence of short-distance wave functions, order-of-magnitude estimates are consistent with a molecular interpretation. The authors note that reactions sensitive to the long-distance tail of the wave function, such as Y(4260) to gamma X(3872), are more directly predictive for molecular structure.
