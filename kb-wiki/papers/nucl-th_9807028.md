# omega-nucleus bound states in the Walecka model

> Using the Walecka model, we investigate theoretically whether an omega meson is bound to finite nuclei. We study several nuclei from ^{6}He to ^{208}Pb, and compare the results with those in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Our calculation shows that deeper omega-nucleus bound states are predicted in the Walecka model than in QMC. One can expect to detect such bound states in the proposed experiment involving the (d,^3He) reaction at GSI.

## Key claim

The Walecka model (QHD) predicts deeper omega-nucleus bound states than the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, but the specific form for the in-medium meson width could not be calculated self-consistently within the model, so a phenomenological parameter is used to cover the estimated range of 30-40 MeV for the width at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors used the Walecka model (QHD-I) in the relativistic Hartree approximation to calculate the "invariant" mass of the omega meson as a function of nuclear density, including sigma-omega mixing effects. They parametrized this mass as a polynomial in density and applied a local density approximation using nuclear density distributions from the Horowitz program to construct an effective potential for the omega meson in finite nuclei. The Klein-Gordon equation for the omega field was then solved in momentum space, including a phenomenological imaginary part to account for the in-medium meson width.

## Key numbers

- Free omega meson mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Free omega meson width: Gamma_omega = 8.43 MeV
- Estimated in-medium omega width at normal nuclear matter density: Gamma*_omega ~ 30-40 MeV
- Parametrization of invariant mass: m_omega* = m_omega - 312.45 x + 199.40 x^2 - 59.277 x^3 + 8.8427 x^4 - 0.52 x^5, where x = rho_B/rho_0 (all numbers in MeV)
- In QHD, even light nuclei give single-particle levels of at least 100 MeV depth
- Phenomenological parameter gamma_omega was varied as 0, 0.2, and 0.4 to cover the estimated width range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of omega meson propagation in infinite nuclear matter within QHD-I (Saito, 1998) and their earlier calculations using the quark-meson coupling model (Saito, 1997). It directly compares with the QMC predictions for omega-mesic nuclei and with the experimental proposal by Hayano (1997) to produce omega mesons via the (d,^3He) reaction at GSI. The results suggest that observation of omega-nucleus bound states could distinguish between the QHD and QMC models.
