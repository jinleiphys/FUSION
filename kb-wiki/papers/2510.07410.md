# Superfluidity in Fermi systems within the framework of Density Functional Theory

> This review is based on lectures given by the author at the Enrico Fermi Summer School in Varenna. It presents the basics of Density Functional Theory (DFT) for Fermi superfluids, with particular emphasis on nuclear systems. Special attention is given to the foundations of both DFT and time-dependent DFT (TDDFT). The review explores the advantages and challenges involved in the practical application of TDDFT to superfluid systems, as well as the typical approximations employed. Various applications of the TDDFT framework to the description of phenomena related to nonequilibrium superfluidity in atomic nuclei, neutron stars, and ultracold atoms are discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that Density Functional Theory (DFT) and its time-dependent extension (TDDFT) provide the most versatile and computationally tractable framework for studying nonequilibrium superfluid dynamics in Fermi systems, despite the fact that the energy density functional is merely proved to exist by the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and must be constructed phenomenologically for non-perturbative regimes such as nuclear systems and unitary Fermi gases.

## Method

The paper reviews the formal foundations of DFT, including the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem establishing one-to-one correspondence between external potential and ground-state density, and the Kohn-Sham procedure that replaces the interacting system with an equivalent non-interacting one. It then extends this framework to superfluid Fermi systems by introducing the anomalous density (pairing field) as an additional fundamental variable, and discusses the superfluid local density approximation as the most useful implementation of TDDFT for nonequilibrium dynamics. The review also addresses the special challenges of self-bound systems like atomic nuclei, where center-of-mass motion must be separated from intrinsic degrees of freedom.

- For a non-interacting Fermi gas at T=0, the pair correlation function $g_2(r)$ decays as $\left(\frac{k_F^3}{8\pi^2}\frac{j_1(k_F r)}{k_F r}\right)^2$, where $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum and $\mu=\epsilon_F=\frac{\hbar^2 k_F^2}{2m}$

## Context

This review extends the DFT framework originally developed for electronic systems (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964; Kohn and Sham, 1965) to superfluid Fermi systems, following the formal extension to superconductors triggered by the discovery of high-temperature superconductivity (Oliveira, Gross, and Kohn, 1988). The work discusses applications to atomic nuclei, neutron stars, and ultracold atoms, and addresses the challenge of constructing energy density functionals for self-bound systems where center-of-mass corrections are required (as developed in works from 2008-2010). The review enables further development of TDDFT for nonequilibrium superfluid dynamics, particularly through the superfluid local density approximation.
