# Complete 0 hbar omega calculations of Gamow-Teller strengths for nuclei in the iron region

> Gamow-Teller strengths for selected nuclei in the iron region (A~56) have been investigated via shell-model Monte Carlo calculations with realistic interactions in the complete fp basis. Results for all cases show significant quenching relative to single-particle estimates, in quantitative agreement with (n,p) data. The J=1,T=0 residual interaction and the f_{7/2}-f_{5/2} spin-orbit splitting are shown to play major roles in the quenching mechanism. Calculated B(E2, 2^+_1 -> 0^+_1) values are in fair agreement with experiment using effective charges of e_p=1.1e and e_n=0.1e.

# Complete 0ℏω calculations of Gamow-Teller strengths for nuclei in the iron region

## Key claim

Complete 0ℏω shell-model Monte Carlo calculations in the full fp-basis for nuclei near A≈56 show significant quenching of Gamow-Teller strengths relative to single-particle estimates, in quantitative agreement with (n,p) data; however, the authors caution that the Brown-Richter interaction used for most calculations has systematic shortcomings (overbinding and too-low GT centroids), and the strong sensitivity of GT strength to the effective interaction means it is premature to draw definite conclusions about whether the quenching is fully recovered in the complete fp-basis until improved effective interactions are available.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model Monte Carlo calculations in the complete set of 0f7/2,5/2–1p3/2,1/2 configurations for ⁵⁴Cr, ⁵⁵Mn, ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe, and ⁵⁶,⁵⁸Ni, using the Brown-Richter (BR) Hamiltonian for most cases and the Kuo-Brown (KB) interaction for ⁵⁴Fe. Calculations were performed at β=2 MeV⁻¹ with 32 time slices (except ⁵⁵Mn at β=1 MeV⁻¹ with 16 time slices), using approximately 3300 Monte Carlo samples at each of six values of the coupling constant g equally spaced between -1 and 0, with extrapolation to the physical case g=1.

## Key numbers

- ⁵⁴Fe (BR): B(GT₊) = 4.3 ± 0.2 (single-particle value 10.3); experimental value 3.5 ± 0.7 (summed to 9 MeV excitation)
- ⁵⁴Fe (KB): B(GT₊) = 2.2 ± 0.3 (single-particle value 10.3); about a factor of two smaller than BR result
- ⁵⁶Ni (BR): B(GT₊) = 7.4 ± 0.3 (single-particle value 13.7)
- ⁵⁴Cr (BR): B(GT₊) = 1.4 ± 0.1 (single-particle value 6.9)
- ⁵⁵Mn (BR): B(GT₊) = 2.2 ± 0.2 (single-particle value 8.6); experimental value 1.72 ± 0.2 (summed to 8 MeV)
- ⁵⁶Fe (BR): B(GT₊) = 2.73 ± 0.04 (single-particle value 10.3); experimental value 2.85 ± 0.3 (summed to 8 MeV)
- ⁵⁸Ni (BR): B(GT₊) = 5.6 ± 0.3 (single-particle value 13.7); experimental value 3.76 ± 0.4 (summed to 8 MeV)
- B(E2, 2⁺₁→0⁺₁) for ⁵⁴Fe (BR) with bare charges: 96 ± 1 e²fm⁴; with effective charges eₚ=1.1e, eₙ=0.1e: 129 ± 1 e²fm⁴; experimental: 124 ± 10 e²fm⁴
- Reducing the f₇/₂–f₅/₂ spin-orbit splitting from 6.49 MeV to 4.49 MeV (MBR interaction) lowered B(GT₊) for ⁵⁴Fe from 4.3 ± 0.2 to 2.40 ± 0.03
- Effective spin-orbit splitting for ⁵⁴Fe (BR): 8.31 ± 0.11 MeV; for ⁵⁴Fe (MBR): 6.82 ± 0.08 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier truncated shell-model calculations by Auerbach et al. (1993) that were limited to 2p–2h excitations and recovered only about half the observed GT quenching, and follows a first complete fp-basis calculation for ⁵⁴Fe by Alhassid et al. (1994). The results demonstrate that neutron-proton correlations in the ground state, particularly the T=0, J=1 residual interaction as suggested by Engel, Vogel, and Zirnbauer (1988), are a major contributor to GT quenching, and that the f₇/₂–f₅/₂ spin-orbit splitting plays a significant role. The calculations enable future determination of improved effective interactions in the complete fp or even fpg₉/₂ basis using the same Monte Carlo techniques, and highlight an odd-even dependence of GT excitation energy centroids that should be incorporated into astrophysical pre-supernova calculations.
