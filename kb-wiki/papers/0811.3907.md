# Extraction of nucleus-nucleus potential and energy dissipation from dynamical mean-field theory

> Nucleus-nucleus interaction potentials in heavy-ion fusion reactions are extracted from the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory. When the center-of-mass energy is much higher than the Coulomb barrier energy, extracted potentials identify with the frozen density approximation. As the center-of-mass energy decreases to the Coulomb barrier energy, potentials become energy dependent. This dependence indicates dynamical reorganization of internal degrees of freedom and leads to a reduction of the "apparent" barrier. Including this effect leads to the Coulomb barrier energy very close to experimental one. Aspects of one-body energy dissipation extracted from the mean-field theory are discussed.

## Key claim

The nucleus-nucleus potential extracted from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory becomes energy-dependent as the center-of-mass energy decreases toward the Coulomb barrier energy, due to dynamical reorganization of internal degrees of freedom; this energy dependence reduces the "apparent" barrier height and brings it close to experimental values, but only when the macroscopic reduction to one-dimensional dissipative dynamics is assumed.

## Method

The authors solve the TDHF equation for head-on collisions to obtain the time evolution of the total density of colliding nuclei. They divide the total density at a separation plane, compute macroscopic variables (relative distance R, momentum P, reduced mass μ) at each time, and assume the dynamics obey a classical equation of motion with a friction term γ(R) depending on velocity. By using two TDHF evolutions with slightly different energies, they extract both the potential dV/dR and the friction coefficient γ(R), then integrate the potential using its asymptotic Coulomb form at large distances.

## Key numbers

- For the 16O+16O reaction at center-of-mass energy E_cm = 34 MeV, the Coulomb barrier predicted by TDHF differs from the frozen-density approximation by less than 0.1 MeV.
- For the 40Ca+40Ca reaction, potentials were extracted at center-of-mass energies ranging from E_cm = 55 MeV to 100 MeV.
- Mesh sizes used in TDHF calculations: 0.8 fm in space and 0.45 fm/c in time.
- The reduced friction parameter β(R) = γ(R)/μ(R) is shown as a function of R scaled by the Coulomb barrier radius R_B for several reactions, with magnitude and radial dependence nearly independent of system size and asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach to heavy-ion fusion reactions by introducing a method (DD-TDHF) that simultaneously extracts nucleus-nucleus potentials and friction coefficients. It compares extracted potentials with the density-constrained TDHF method (Umar, 2006) and the frozen-density approximation (Denisov, 2002), finding good agreement at high energies. The friction coefficients are compared with a microscopic model based on linear response theory (Adamian, 1997), showing good agreement and suggesting universal behavior. This enables systematic extraction of dynamical effects and energy dissipation from microscopic theory for fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier.
