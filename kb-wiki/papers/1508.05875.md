# MCNP6 simulation of light and medium nuclei fragmentation at intermediate energies

> Fragmentation reactions induced on light and medium nuclei by protons and light nuclei of energies around 1 GeV/nucleon and below are studied with the Los Alamos transport code MCNP6 and with its CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators. CEM and LAQGSM assume that intermediate-energy fragmentation reactions on light nuclei occur generally in two stages. The first stage is the intranuclear cascade (INC), followed by the second, Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei produced after the INC. CEM and LAQGSM account also for coalescence of light fragments (complex particles) up to 4He from energetic nucleons emitted during INC. We investigate the validity and performance of MCNP6, CEM, and LAQGSM in simulating fragmentation reactions at intermediate energies and discuss possible ways of further improving these codes

# MCNP6 simulation of light and medium nuclei fragmentation at intermediate energies

## Key claim
MCNP6 using CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 simulates fragmentation reactions on light and medium-light nuclei at intermediate energies in satisfactory agreement with experimental data, but the codes are not yet ready to predict well heavy fragments from reactions with heavier nuclei having mass numbers around 100.

## Method
The authors used the Los Alamos transport code MCNP6 with its CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators to simulate fragmentation reactions induced by protons and light nuclei at energies around 1 GeV/nucleon and below. Both CEM and LAQGSM assume a two-stage process for light targets (atomic number A < 13): an intranuclear cascade (INC) stage followed by Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei, with coalescence of light fragments up to 4He from emitted cascade nucleons. The newer "F" versions of the codes extend preequilibrium emission to light fragments heavier than 4He up to 28Mg and coalescence up to A=7 (CEM03.03F) or A=12 (LAQGSM03.03F).

## Key numbers
- Excitation function for 14C production from p + 16O calculated with CEM03.03 using the standard Fermi breakup model (AFermi = 12) and with cut-off values for AFermi of 16 and 14, compared with experimental data from the T16 Lib compilation.
- Triton spectra at 45, 60, 90, and 130 degrees from 800 MeV/nucleon 20Ne + 20Ne calculated with LAQGSM03.03 using standard coalescence (p0 = 0.108 GeV/c for t and 3He) and modified p0 values labeled "coal1" and "coal2".
- 4He spectra at 35 degrees from 1.2, 1.9, and 2.5 GeV p + 12C measured by the PISA collaboration and calculated by MCNP6 using CEM03.03.
- Cross sections for 48Ca fragmentation on 9Be at 140 MeV/nucleon compared with LAQGSM03.03 predictions.
- Production of 6Li at 60 degrees from 480 MeV p + natAg compared with standard CEM03.03 and CEM03.03F with and without coalescence expansion.

## Context
This work extends the validation of MCNP6 and its event generators CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 specifically for fragmentation of light and medium nuclei at intermediate energies, building on previous validation and verification studies (Mashnik et al., 2014). The paper identifies a gap in current models for producing heavy fragments from nuclei with mass numbers around 100, which are too light for conventional fission but too heavy for standard evaporation, preequilibrium, or coalescence models. The authors propose future improvements by merging CEM and LAQGSM with the GEMINI sequential-binary-decay model (Charity et al.) or implementing the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (Botvina et al.) as competitive modes to evaporation, similar to earlier "G" and "S" versions of the codes developed more than a decade ago.
