# A new N* resonance as a hadronic molecular state

> We report our recent work on a hadronic molecule state of the KbarKN system with I=1/2 and J^P=1/2^+. We assume that the Lambda(1405) resonance and the scalar mesons, f_{0}(980), a_{0}(980), are reproduced as quasi-bound states of KbarN and KbarK, respectively. Performing non-relativistic three-body calculations with a variational method for this system, we find a quasibound state of the KbarKN system around 1910 MeV below the three-body breakup threshold. This state corresponds to a new baryon resonance of N* with J^P=1/2^+. We find also that this resonance has the cluster structure of the two-body bound states keeping their properties as in the isolated two-particle systems. We also briefly discuss another hadronic molecular state composed by two Kbar and one N, which corresponds to a Xi^* resonance.

# A new N* resonance as a hadronic molecular state

## Key claim

The authors find a quasibound state of the KbarKN system around 1910 MeV that corresponds to a new N* resonance with J^P=1/2^+, but only under the assumption that the Lambda(1405) resonance and the scalar mesons f0(980) and a0(980) are themselves quasibound states of KbarN and KbarK, respectively. The state is weakly bound, appearing about 10 MeV below the lowest two-body threshold (Lambda(1405)+K) in both parameter sets tested.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic three-body calculations for the KbarKN system with I=1/2 and J^P=1/2^+ using a variational method with Gaussian expansion. They employed effective two-body interactions parametrized as one-range Gaussian potentials, with the KbarN interaction taken from two different phenomenological potentials (HW-HNJH and AY), the KbarK interaction adjusted to reproduce the masses and widths of f0(980) and a0(980), and the KN interaction fixed by experimental scattering lengths. The bound state energies were obtained as expectation values of the total Hamiltonian, and widths were evaluated from the imaginary parts of the complex energies.

## Key numbers

- The KbarKN quasibound state is found at 1910 MeV (19 MeV below the three-body K+Kbar+N threshold at 1930 MeV) with the HW-HNJH potential, and at 1889 MeV (41 MeV below threshold) with the AY potential.
- The total width of the state is approximately 88 MeV (ImE = -44 MeV) for the HW-HNJH potential and 98 MeV (ImE = -49 MeV) for the AY potential.
- The root-mean-square radius of the KbarKN system is 1.7 fm (HW-HNJH) and 1.4 fm (AY), with inter-hadron distances of 2.1-2.8 fm.
- The KbarN(I=0) subsystem contributes about 34-38 MeV to the total width, and the KbarK(I=1) subsystem contributes about 50 MeV.
- The probability for the KbarN subsystem to be in I=0 is 0.93 (HW-HNJH) and 0.99 (AY); the probability for the KbarK subsystem to be in I=1 is 0.91 (HW-HNJH) and 0.83 (AY).

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular picture previously applied to the Lambda(1405) resonance by Dalitz (1959) and to the f0(980) and a0(980) scalar mesons by Weinstein (1982), as well as the deeply bound kaonic states in light nuclei proposed by Akaishi (2002). The calculation is consistent with independent Faddeev calculations by Martinez Torres (2008) that found a generated resonance at the same energy. The results enable experimental searches for this N* resonance in reactions such as gamma p to K+ Lambda, where a bump structure has been observed by Bradford (2005), and could explain the strong energy dependence of Lambda(1405) production seen in the gamma p to K+ pi+/- Sigma-/+ reaction at SPring8 by Niiyama (2008).
