# The Meissner Effect in a Two Flavor LOFF Color Superconductor

> We calculate the magnetic polarization tensor of the photon and of the gluons in a two flavor color superconductor with a LOFF pairing that consists of a single plane wave. We show that at zero temperature and within the range of the values of the displacement of the Fermi sea that favors the LOFF state, all the eigenvalues of the magnetic polarization tensor are positive. Therefore the chromomagnetic instabilities pertaining to a gapless color superconductor disappear.

## Key claim

The authors show that at zero temperature and within the LOFF window (the range of Fermi surface displacement where the LOFF state is energetically favored), all eigenvalues of the magnetic polarization tensor for both photons and gluons are non-negative, thereby eliminating the chromomagnetic instabilities that plague the gapless two-flavor color superconductor (g2SC) phase.

## Method

The authors calculate the Meissner masses (the zero-momentum limit of the magnetic polarization tensor) for all gluonic excitations and the photon in a two-flavor LOFF color superconductor with a single-plane-wave order parameter. They work within an NJL effective model at zero temperature, using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism and weak-coupling approximation to derive analytic expressions for the free energy and the Meissner tensor components, which are then evaluated analytically for small gap parameter and numerically for arbitrary gap.

## Key numbers

- The critical displacement for the LOFF state at zero temperature is \(\delta_c \simeq 0.754 \Delta_0\), where \(\Delta_0\) is the zero-temperature gap at zero displacement.
- The transverse Meissner mass for the 4-7th gluons is given by \(A = \frac{g^2 \bar\mu^2}{6\pi^2}[1 - \frac{2\delta^2}{\Delta^2}(1 + \frac{1}{5}\rho^2) + \ldots]\) with \(\rho = q/\delta\).
- The longitudinal Meissner mass for the 8th gluon is \(D = \frac{g^2 \bar\mu^2}{9\pi^2}(1 + \frac{e^2}{3g^2})\{1 + \frac{\rho+1}{4\rho^3}[3(\rho+1)(1-x_1^2)\ln\frac{1+x_1}{1-x_1} + 4(\rho+1)^2 x_1^3 - 6(\rho^2+\rho+1)x_1] + \ldots\}\).
- The transverse Meissner mass for the 8th gluon is \(C = \frac{g^2 \bar\mu^2}{9\pi^2}(1 + \frac{e^2}{3g^2})\{1 - \frac{(\rho+1)^2}{8\rho^3}[3(1-x_1^2)\ln\frac{1+x_1}{1-x_1} + 4(\rho+1)x_1^3 - 6x_1] - \ldots\}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Giannakis and Ren, 2005) which showed that the chromomagnetic instability of the 8th gluon in the g2SC phase implies a LOFF state with lower free energy and positive Meissner mass for that gluon. The present paper completes the analysis by demonstrating that the Meissner masses for the 4-7th gluons are also positive in the LOFF window, resolving the instability problem identified by Huang and Shovkovy (2003) and Casalbuoni et al. (2004) for the gapless color superconductor. The result enables further investigation of the LOFF state as a viable candidate for the ground state of quark matter at moderately high baryon density, relevant for compact star physics.
