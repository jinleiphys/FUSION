# Amplifying muon-to-positron conversion in nuclei with ultralight dark matter

> We present an analysis of the lepton-number and lepton-flavour-violating process of muon-to-positron conversion $μ^- + N \rightarrow e^+ + N'$, in the presence of an ultralight scalar dark matter (ULSDM) field which couples to neutrinos. The ULSDM contributes to the effective off-diagonal Majorana mass $ m_{μe}$, therefore amplifying the rate of muon-to-positron conversion to experimentally observable levels. Using existing bounds from SINDRUM II, COMET, and Mu2e experiments, we derive novel constraints on the flavour-off-diagonal couplings of neutrinos to ULSDM. Our work reveals that upcoming experiments can provide stronger sensitivity to these new couplings than bounds arising from cosmological surveys and terrestrial experiments.

## Key claim

The presence of an ultralight scalar dark matter (ULSDM) field with a lepton-flavour-violating coupling to neutrinos can amplify the rate of muon-to-positron conversion in nuclei to experimentally observable levels, even if the underlying neutrino masses are too small to produce a detectable signal; the enhancement arises from a time-averaged contribution to the effective off-diagonal Majorana mass, and the process can occur even in the absence of intrinsic lepton-number violation.

## Method

The authors compute the branching ratio for the process μ⁻ + N → e⁺ + N' in the presence of a coherent ULSDM field φ that couples to ν_e and ν_μ via an effective dimension-six operator. The ULSDM field is treated as a classical oscillating background, and its contribution to the effective Majorana mass m_{μe} is time-averaged over experimental integration times. The resulting enhancement to the conversion rate is compared with existing upper limits from SINDRUM II and projected sensitivities from COMET and Mu2e to derive exclusion contours in the g_{μe}–m_φ parameter space.

## Key numbers

- SINDRUM II bound on the conversion rate: Br(μ⁻ + Ti → e⁺ + Ca) < 1.7 × 10⁻¹² at 90% confidence level, corresponding to |m_{μe}| < 4 × 10¹¹ eV.
- Projected COMET Phase-I sensitivity: R_{μe} < 10⁻¹⁴, corresponding to |m_{μe}| < 3 × 10¹⁰ eV.
- Projected Mu2e sensitivity: R_{μe} < 10⁻¹⁶, corresponding to |m_{μe}| < 3 × 10⁹ eV.
- Projected COMET Phase-II sensitivity: R_{μe} < 6 × 10⁻¹⁷, corresponding to |m_{μe}| < 2 × 10⁹ eV.
- In the Standard Model with Majorana neutrinos, the branching ratio is R_{μ⁻→e⁺} ≈ 10⁻³⁵ × (|m_{μe}|/eV)² for a nuclear matrix element |M_n| = 0.1.
- The vacuum effective Majorana mass |m_{μe}| lies in the range (3–8) × 10⁻³ eV for normal ordering in the hierarchical regime, and as low as 10⁻⁵ eV for inverted ordering.
- Cosmological and astrophysical bounds on the coupling g_{μe} are approximately: g_{μe} ≲ 10⁻⁵ from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, g_{μe} ≲ 10⁻⁷ from CMB neutrino free-streaming, and g_{μe} ≲ 10⁻⁶ from SN1987A cooling.
- The ULSDM-induced contribution to the effective mass squared is ⟨|m_{μe}|²⟩ = (g_{μe} φ₀)²/2, with φ₀ = √(2ρ_φ)/m_φ and local DM density ρ_φ = 0.3 GeV/cm³.
- The oscillation bound from solar neutrino data requires g_{μe} φ₀ ≲ 0.008 eV for m_φ ≲ (10⁻¹⁸–10⁻¹⁹) eV.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism previously applied to neutrinoless double-beta decay by Huang (2021) and Graf (2023), where ULSDM carrying lepton number can enhance or induce lepton-number-violating processes. Here, for the first time, the focus is on flavour off-diagonal couplings probed by μ⁻ → e⁺ conversion, which is complementary to the flavour-diagonal constraints from 0νββ. The results show that upcoming experiments like COMET Phase-II and Mu2e can probe couplings g_{μe} as low as 10⁻¹⁰ for scalar masses m_φ ≲ 10⁻¹¹ eV, providing direct laboratory constraints that can surpass indirect cosmological and astrophysical bounds from BBN, CMB, and SN1987A.
