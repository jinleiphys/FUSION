# Velocity Laws for Bound States in Asymptotically AdS Geometries

> We study the behavior of heavy quark bound states in moving plasmas that are dual to theories with generic non-trivial renormalization group flows interpolating between an AdS geometry in the ultraviolet and infrared fixed points with broken symmetries. We investigate analytically the observables associated with the bound state and find their scaling exponents with respect to the Lorentz factor for ultrarelativistic motion. Despite having asymptotically an AdS geometry, the scaling is not universal and depends on geometric conditions of the Fefferman-Graham expansion in the near boundary regime, or equivalently on the order of the asymptotic background expansion that provides the leading contributions to the Wilson loops.

## Key claim

The scaling exponents of heavy quark bound state observables with respect to the Lorentz factor in ultrarelativistic motion are not universal for asymptotically AdS geometries, but instead depend on specific geometric conditions of the Fefferman-Graham expansion in the near-boundary regime, specifically on the order of the asymptotic background expansion that provides the leading contributions to the Wilson loops.

## Method

The author considers holographic theories dual to moving plasmas with non-trivial renormalization group flows that interpolate between an AdS ultraviolet fixed point and an infrared fixed point with broken rotational symmetries. The system is set up by applying a rotation and boost transformation to place the dipole bound state in the rest frame with the plasma wind at an angle theta relative to the anisotropic direction. The string worldsheet action is derived, and the ultrarelativistic limit is analyzed by solving the worldsheet horizon equation perturbatively in the near-boundary asymptotic expansion of the metric.

## Key numbers

- For motion with angle theta not equal to pi/2, when the asymptotic condition A_5^(2) is non-zero, the screening length scales as L_max ~ (1-v^2)^(1/2), corresponding to the action scaling as S ~ (1-v^2)^(-1/2)
- For motion with angle theta not equal to pi/2, when the asymptotic condition A_5^(2) = 0 holds, the screening length scales as L_max ~ (1-v^2)^(1/4), corresponding to the action scaling as S ~ (1-v^2)^(-1/4)
- The critical worldsheet horizon for the generic case scales as u_c ~ sqrt(1-v^2) when A_5^(2) is non-zero, and as u_c ~ (1-v^2)^(1/4) when A_5^(2) = 0

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of heavy quark bound states in moving plasmas beyond the standard AdS result where the screening length scales as L_max ~ (1-v^2)^(1/4), as found by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006). The author shows that different scaling powers are possible even for asymptotically AdS geometries, depending on the asymptotic expansion conditions. This formalism is applicable to holographic theories with non-trivial renormalization group flows such as those studied by Mateos, Giataganas, and Hoyos (2011-2020), and is motivated by recent experimental results on spin polarization in noncentral collisions from the STAR collaboration (2017) and J/Psi suppression from NA50 (2004).
