# Charmed baryons in nuclear matter

> We study the temperature and baryon density dependence of the masses of the lightest charmed baryons $螞_c$, $危_c$ and $危^*_c$. We also look at the effects of the temperature and baryon density on the binding energies of the $螞_c N$ and $螞_c 螞_c$ systems. Baryon masses and baryon-baryon interactions are evaluated within a chiral constituent quark model. Medium effects are incorporated in those parameters of the model related to the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry, which are the masses of the constituent quarks, the $蟽$ and $蟺$ meson masses, and quark-meson couplings. We find that while the in-medium $螞_c$ mass decreases monotonically with temperature, those of $危_c$ and $危^*_c$ have a nonmonotonic dependence. These features can be understood in terms of a simple group theory analysis regarding the one-gluon exchange interaction in those hadrons. The in-medium $螞_c N$ and $螞_c 螞_c$ interactions are governed by a delicate balance involving a stronger attraction due to the decrease of the $蟽$ meson mass, suppression of coupled-channel effects and lower thresholds, leading to shallow bound states with binding energies of a few~MeV. The $螞_c$ baryon could possibly be bound to a large nucleus, in qualitative agreement with results based on relativistic mean field models or QCD sum rules. Ongoing experiments at RHIC or LHCb or the planned ones at FAIR and J-PARC may take advantage of the present results.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of charmed baryons and their binding energies with nucleons and other charmed baryons are evaluated using a chiral constituent quark model whose parameters are made temperature- and baryon-density-dependent via the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model; the abstract's claim of "shallow bound states with binding energies of a few MeV" is conditioned on a delicate balance between increased attraction from a decreasing sigma meson mass, suppression of coupled-channel effects, and lower thresholds, and the results are specifically applicable to a medium similar to that formed in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used a chiral constituent quark model with one-gluon-exchange, confinement, and pseudoscalar (pion) and scalar (sigma) meson-exchange potentials to describe charmed baryons as three-quark clusters. Medium effects were incorporated by making the constituent quark masses, sigma and pion masses, and quark-meson couplings temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent as predicted by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The in-medium baryon masses were obtained by solving the three-quark bound-state problem with these modified parameters, and the two-baryon interactions were derived via a Born-Oppenheimer approximation and used in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation to search for bound states via the Fredholm determinant.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density \(\rho_B/\rho_0 = 1\) and \(T \simeq 0.12\) GeV, the \(\Lambda_c\) mass decreases by \(\Delta M_{\Lambda_c} = 72\) MeV; this is smaller than the 122 MeV decrease from the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima (2004) and much larger than the approximately 10 MeV decrease from QCD sum rules calculations of Azizi (2017) and Ohtani (2017).
- The vacuum mass difference between \(\Sigma_c\) and \(\Lambda_c\) is 168 MeV, compared to 73 MeV in the strange sector.
- The vacuum mass difference between \(\Sigma_c\Sigma_c\) and \(\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) thresholds is 338 MeV.
- The largest binding energy obtained for the \((I)J^P=(1/2)1^+\) \(N\Lambda_c\) state is of the order of 10 MeV.
- The binding energy of the \((I)J^P=(0)0^+\) \(\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) system is much smaller than that of the \(\Lambda_c N\) system.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to in-medium properties of a \(\Delta\bar{D}^*\) molecule by Carames (2016) to charmed baryons and their interactions. It contrasts with relativistic mean field models such as the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima (2004) and the nonlinear meson-exchange model of Fontoura (2017), which predict \(\Lambda_c\) binding to nuclei, and with QCD sum rules calculations by Wang (2011, 2012), Azizi (2017, 2018), and Ohtani (2017) that give contradictory signs and magnitudes for the \(\Lambda_c\) mass shift depending on the treatment of the four-quark condensate. The results enable predictions for the production of charmed baryon bound states in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHCb and in planned experiments at FAIR and J-PARC.
