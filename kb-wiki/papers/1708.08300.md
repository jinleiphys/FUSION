# Contribution of Kaon component in viscosity and conductivity of hadronic medium

> The two-point correlation functions of kaonic viscous stress tensors and electro-magnetic currents have been calculated respectively to estimate the contributions of the strange sector in the shear viscosity and electrical conductivity of hadronic medium. In the one-loop correlators, kaon propagators contain a non-zero thermal width that leads to non-divergent values of transport coefficients. With the help of effective Lagrangian densities of strange hadrons, we have calculated in-medium self-energy of kaon for different possible mesonic loops, whose imaginary part provide the estimation of kaon thermal width. It is observed that near the quark-hadron transition temperature, the contribution of kaons to shear viscosity is larger and increases faster with temperature than pionic contribution. In case of electrical conductivity the trend due kaon component appears to be opposite in nature with respect to pion component.

## Key claim

The paper claims that near the quark-hadron transition temperature (around 150 MeV), the kaon contribution to shear viscosity becomes larger than the pionic contribution and increases faster with temperature, while for electrical conductivity the kaon component shows an opposite trend compared to the pion component; however, this is conditional on the kaon relaxation time being shorter than the fireball lifetime, which only occurs at high temperatures close to the transition.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermal width (inverse relaxation time) of kaons from two sources: (1) one-loop self-energy diagrams for \(\pi K^*\) and \(K\phi\) loops using effective Lagrangian densities with coupling constants fixed from experimental decay widths, and (2) tree-level elastic scattering diagrams for \(KK\), \(K\pi\), and \(K\bar{K}\) channels. Using the total relaxation time, they computed shear viscosity and electrical conductivity of the kaon component via the Kubo formalism (one-loop approximation) or equivalently the relaxation time approximation, and compared these with the pion component using pion parameters from prior work (Ghosh et al., 2016).

## Key numbers

- Kaon mass \(m_K = 0.500\) GeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.140\) GeV, \(K^*\) mass \(m_{K^*} = 0.890\) GeV, \(\phi\) mass \(m_\phi = 1.020\) GeV.
- Effective coupling constants: \(g_{K\pi K^*}/(4\pi) = 0.86\) and \(g_{KK\phi}/(4\pi) = 1.82\), fixed from experimental decay widths of \(K^* \rightarrow K\pi\) and \(\phi \rightarrow K\bar{K}\).
- Isospin-averaged \(KN\) scattering cross section \(\sigma_{KN} \approx 4.7\) mb, obtained from scattering lengths \(a_{KN}^{I=0} = -0.007\) fm and \(a_{KN}^{I=1} = -0.225\) fm.
- The Landau cut for the \(K\phi\) loop ends at invariant mass \(M = |m_\phi - m_K| = 0.51\) GeV; for the \(K^*\pi\) loop at \(M = |m_{K^*} - m_\pi| = 0.745\) GeV.
- At temperature \(T = 0.150\) GeV, the on-shell thermal width from loop diagrams was evaluated for kaon momenta \(\mathbf{k} = 0.300\) GeV and \(\mathbf{k} = 0.500\) GeV.
- The fireball lifetime is taken as approximately 10 fm; kaon relaxation time becomes shorter than this only at temperatures near \(T \sim 150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of shear viscosity and electrical conductivity in hadronic matter that focused on the \(u,d\) quark sector (pions, sigma, rho resonances) by authors such as Itakura (2003), Dobado (2002), Nicola (2005), and Ghosh (2016). It also complements hadron resonance gas model calculations that included strangeness (Gorenstein, 2008; Hostler, 2008) by isolating the kaon component separately. The results enable more complete inputs for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly near the quark-hadron transition temperature where strange sector contributions become significant.
