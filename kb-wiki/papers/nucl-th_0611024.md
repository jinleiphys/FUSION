# Dependence of the BCS 1S0 superfluid pairing gap on nuclear interactions

> We study in detail the dependence of the 1S0 superfluid pairing gap on nuclear interactions and on charge-independence breaking at the BCS level. Starting from chiral effective-field theory and conventional nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions, we use the renormalization group to generate low-momentum interactions V_{low k} with sharp and smooth regulators. The resulting BCS gaps are well constrained by the NN scattering phase shifts, and the cutoff dependence is very weak for sharp or sufficiently narrow smooth regulators with cutoffs Lambda > 1.6 fm^{-1}. It is therefore likely that the effect of three-nucleon interactions on 1S0 superfluidity is small at the BCS level. The charge dependence of nuclear interactions has a 10% effect on the pairing gap.

## Key claim

The BCS 1S0 superfluid pairing gap in low-density neutron matter is practically independent of the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction and therefore well constrained by NN scattering phase shifts, but the authors emphasize that their work is a theoretical benchmark and not a prediction because polarization, dispersion, and three-nucleon interaction effects beyond the BCS level are not included.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for the 1S0 channel using a free single-particle spectrum, starting from chiral effective-field theory interactions at N3LO and from conventional NN potentials (Nijmegen II, Argonne v18, CD-Bonn). They used the renormalization group to generate low-momentum interactions V_low_k with both sharp and smooth regulators, and solved the gap equation via the method of Khodel et al., iterating between a linearized equation for the momentum-dependent shape function and a Newton-method equation for the gap on the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- Maximal BCS-level gap: Δ ≈ 2.9–3.0 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F ≈ 0.8–0.9 fm^{-1} for neutron-neutron pairing.
- Charge dependence (neutron-proton vs. neutron-neutron) produces a 10% effect on the pairing gap, with neutron-proton gaps ≈ 0.3 MeV larger at maximum.
- Cutoff independence holds for sharp or sufficiently narrow smooth regulators with cutoff Λ > 1.6 fm^{-1}; for low densities, cutoffs as low as Λ > 1.2 k_F are possible.
- BCS gaps for bare interactions are within 2% of the V_low_k results for k_F ≲ 1.0 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 1S0 superfluidity (e.g., Lombardo and Schulze, without charge dependence) by systematically comparing chiral EFT and conventional NN interactions, and by using the renormalization group to control cutoff dependence. The weak cutoff dependence suggests that three-nucleon interaction effects on 1S0 superfluidity are small at the BCS level, in contrast to their role in A=3,4 nuclei and nuclear matter studied by Nogga et al. (2004) and Bogner et al. (2005). The results enable the use of low-momentum interactions in density-functional calculations and provide a benchmark for future work including polarization effects, such as the RG approach of Schwenk et al. (2003) and the fixed-node AFDMC calculations of Gezerlis and Carlson (2006).
