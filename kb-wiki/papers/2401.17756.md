# Electromagnetic form factors of the transition from the Delta to the nucleon

> The low-energy electromagnetic form factors of the $Δ$(1232)-to-nucleon transition are derived combining dispersion theory techniques and chiral perturbation theory. The form factors are expressed in terms of the well-understood pion vector form factor and pion-baryon scattering amplitudes. Nucleon and Delta exchange terms and contact terms constitute the input for these pion-baryon amplitudes. The framework is formulated for all form factors. When comparing to experimental data in the spacelike region of $e^- N \to e^- Δ$ scattering, the focus lies on the numerically dominant magnetic dipole transition form factor. Fitting two subtraction constants (one for the scattering amplitude, one for the form factor) yields a very good description of this dominant form factor up to photon virtualities of about 0.6 GeV. After determining the subtraction constants in the spacelike region and at the photon point, respectively, predictions for the timelike region of Dalitz decays $Δ\to N \, e^+ e^-$ are presented.

## Key claim

The authors derive the low-energy electromagnetic transition form factors of the Delta(1232)-to-nucleon transition using a combination of dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory, but they treat the Delta as if it were an asymptotic state with the peak mass of the resonance rather than as a pole in the complex plane, which limits the accuracy for the smaller electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors.

## Method

The transition form factors are expressed in terms of the well-known pion vector form factor and pion-baryon scattering amplitudes, where the latter are obtained from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order and unitarized in the Muskhelishvili-Omnes framework. Nucleon and Delta exchange terms and contact terms constitute the input for these pion-baryon amplitudes, and the formalism uses dispersion relations for the Jones-Scadron form factors, focusing on the magnetic dipole transition form factor. Two subtraction constants (one for the scattering amplitude, one for the form factor) are fitted to experimental data in the spacelike region of electron-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers

- The framework describes the magnetic dipole transition form factor up to photon virtualities of about 0.6 GeV in the spacelike region.
- The Delta decay width used is approximately 0.117 GeV.
- The momentum of the pion produced in the Delta rest frame is approximately 0.229 GeV.
- The pion vector form factor is described using a parameter alpha_V = 0.12 GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work is the third in a series by the Uppsala group, following studies of the Sigma0-Lambda transition form factors (Granados 2017) and the Sigma*0-Lambda transition form factors (Junker 2019). It extends the dispersion theory approach from hyperon transitions to the nucleon-Delta system, where spacelike experimental data exist for comparison, enabling predictions for the timelike region of Dalitz decays Delta to N e+ e- and testing the validity of the methods developed for hyperon studies.
