# Strong decay widths of S- and P-wave singly-, doubly- and triply-heavy charm and bottom baryons

> We present a study of two-body decay widths of heavy baryons into another heavy baryon and a light pseudoscalar meson in the framework of the non-relativistic quark model in combination with the elementary emission model for the strong couplings. The present study includes the decays of 1S- and 1P-wave baryons with one, two or three heavy quarks, either charm (c) or bottom (b). The relative partial widths are given by the appropriate flavor SU(3) isoscalar factors. The flavor, spin and orbital contributions are discussed explicitly as well as selection rules for forbidden decays. The total widths are compared with the available experimental data as well as with other theoretical studies. The calculated widths of singly-heavy baryons are found to be in reasonable agreement with the observed widths. The decay widths of doubly-heavy baryons are suppressed with respect to those of the singly-heavy baryons by the ratios of the quark masses appearing in the orbital contributions.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic quark model combined with the elementary emission model can simultaneously describe strong decay widths of all 1S- and 1P-wave heavy baryons with one, two, or three charm or bottom quarks, with the calculated widths of singly-heavy baryons showing reasonable agreement with experimental data, while doubly-heavy baryon decay widths are suppressed relative to singly-heavy ones by quark mass ratios appearing in orbital contributions.

## Method

The authors computed two-body strong decay widths of heavy baryons decaying into another heavy baryon and a light pseudoscalar meson using the non-relativistic quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions, combined with the elementary emission model for strong couplings. The flavor SU(3) isoscalar factors were used to determine relative partial widths, and the two coupling constants g and h were determined by a simultaneous fit to available experimental decay widths. The helicity amplitudes were evaluated in the rest frame of the initial baryon, with the z-axis chosen along the meson momentum direction.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants determined from fit: g = 3.32 GeV⁻¹ and h = -0.16 GeV⁻¹
- The η-η' mixing angle used: θ_P = -23°
- The Σ_c(2455) and Σ_c(2520) baryons decay approximately 100% via the Λ_cπ channel, consistent with ground state S-wave interpretation
- The Σ_c(2800) resonance was assigned as the P-wave state ²λ(Σ_c)_{1/2⁻}

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group on Ω_Q baryons (Santopinto 2019, Ortiz-Pacheco 2020), Ξ'_Q and Ξ_Q baryons (Ortiz-Pacheco 2022), and a simultaneous study of mass spectra and electromagnetic couplings of all 1S- and 1P-wave heavy baryons (Ortiz-Pacheco 2023). The results are compared with the ³P₀ model calculations (García-Tecocoatzi 2023) and the chiral quark model (Wang 2017), both of which present comprehensive studies of S- and P-wave heavy baryons of the sextet and anti-triplet. The work enables systematic predictions for yet-unobserved doubly-heavy and triply-heavy baryon decays, particularly relevant given that only one doubly-heavy baryon candidate (Ξ^{++}_{cc}(3621)) has been identified experimentally by LHCb, and no triply-heavy baryons have been observed.
