# Mean transverse mass of hadrons in proton-proton reactions

> An energy dependence of the mean transverse mass $\langle m_T\rangle$ at mid-rapidity in proton-proton ($p+p$) reactions is studied within the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD). The UrQMD model predicts a nonmonotonous dependence of $\langle m_T\rangle$ on collision energy for several hadron species: for $π^+$, $p$, $K^+$, and $Λ$ the mean transverse mass has a maximum at the center of mass energy region $5\le \sqrt{s}\le 8$ GeV. These results are a consequence of an interplay of two contributions: 1) excitations and decays of the baryonic resonances $N^*$ and $Δ$; 2) excitations and decays of the baryonic strings. The UrQMD results do not show any nonmonotonous dependence of $\langle m_T\rangle$ on $\sqrt{s}$ for $π^-$, $K^{-}$, and antiprotons. Whether a nonmonotonous dependence of $\langle m_T\rangle$ at mid-rapidity on the collision energy for $π^+$, $p$, $K^+$, and $Λ$ is relevant for real $p+p$ interactions will be soon checked experimentally by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model predicts a nonmonotonous dependence of the mean transverse mass on collision energy for π⁺, p, K⁺, and Λ at mid-rapidity in p+p reactions, with a maximum in the center-of-mass energy region 5 ≤ √s ≤ 8 GeV. This prediction is a model-specific feature arising from the interplay of baryonic resonance decays and string decays, and its relevance to real p+p interactions awaits experimental verification by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD-3.4 transport model to simulate inelastic p+p collisions and calculated the mean transverse mass ⟨m_T⟩ at mid-rapidity (rapidity window 0 < y < 0.2 in the center-of-mass system) for π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, K⁻, p, antiprotons, and Λ as a function of collision energy √s. They decomposed the total ⟨m_T⟩ into contributions from baryonic resonance decays (N* and Δ states) and from string decays, and compared results with UrQMD-3.3 and the HSD-2.5 model.

## Key numbers

- For π⁺, the mean transverse mass has a maximum at √s ≈ 4.5 GeV and then decreases notably in the region √s = 5–8 GeV.
- For π⁺, a drop in ⟨m_T⟩ occurs at 5 ≤ √s ≤ 8 GeV.
- For K⁺, a drop in ⟨m_T⟩ occurs at 4 ≤ √s ≤ 7 GeV.
- For protons, a decrease in ⟨m_T⟩ occurs at 5 ≤ √s ≤ 17.3 GeV.
- For Λ, a decrease in ⟨m_T⟩ occurs at 8 ≤ √s ≤ 17.3 GeV.
- The nonmonotonous dependence is absent for π⁻, K⁻, and antiprotons in UrQMD.
- The HSD-2.5 model shows a monotonous increase of ⟨m_T⟩ with collision energy for both π⁺ and protons.

## Context

This work extends the study of mean transverse mass from central Pb+Pb collisions (NA49 Collaboration), where a step-like behavior of ⟨m_T⟩ was observed at SPS energies (√s_NN = 7.6–17.3 GeV) and interpreted as evidence for a first-order phase transition to deconfined matter (van Hove, Shuryak, Gorenstein et al. 2003). The UrQMD prediction of a nonmonotonous ⟨m_T⟩ in p+p reactions, if confirmed by NA61/SHINE, would provide a new test for transport models and could help constrain the decay parameters of heavy baryonic resonances, which are currently poorly known experimentally.
