# On the Quark-Gluon Vertex and Quark-Ghost Kernel: combining Lattice Simulations with Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We investigate the dressed quark-gluon vertex combining two established non-perturbative approaches to QCD: the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the quark propagator and lattice-regularized simulations for the quark, gluon and ghost propagators. The vertex is modeled using a generalized Ball-Chiu ansatz parameterized by a single form factor $\tilde X_0$ which effectively represents the quark-ghost scattering kernel. The solution space of the DSE inversion for $\tilde X_0$ is highly degenerate, which can be dealt with by a numerical regularization scheme. We consider two possibilities: (i) linear regularization and (ii) the Maximum Entropy Method. These two numerical approaches yield compatible $\tilde X_0$ functions for the range of momenta where lattice data is available and feature a strong enhancement of the generalized Ball-Chiu vertex for momenta below 1 GeV. Our ansatz for the quark-gluon vertex is then used to solve the quark DSE which yields a mass function in good agreement with lattice simulations and thus provides adequate dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

# On the Quark-Gluon Vertex and Quark-Ghost Kernel: combining Lattice Simulations with Dyson-Schwinger equations

## Key claim

The dressed quark-gluon vertex can be effectively modeled using a generalized Ball-Chiu ansatz parameterized by a single form factor $\tilde X_0$ that represents the quark-ghost scattering kernel, but this form factor should be interpreted as an effective quantity $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff}$ that absorbs contributions from the omitted transverse components and other form factors, rather than the true $X_0$ of the quark-ghost scattering kernel.

## Method

The authors combined lattice-regularized QCD simulations for the quark, gluon and ghost propagators with Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator to extract the effective form factor $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff}(q^2)$ that parameterizes the longitudinal part of the quark-gluon vertex. The DSE inversion problem is ill-posed due to non-invertible kernels with small or vanishing eigenvalues, so two independent regularization schemes were applied: (i) linear regularization solved by iteration, and (ii) the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) using a functional basis expansion with $\chi^2$-fitting to lattice data. The extracted $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff}$ was then reinserted into the quark DSE to compute the quark mass function $M(p^2)$ and wave function $Z(p^2)$ for comparison with lattice results.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass at the renormalization point $\mu = 4.3$ GeV is set to $m(\mu) = 25.2$ MeV from lattice data
- The strong coupling constant is taken as $\alpha_s(\mu^2) = 0.295$ at $\mu = 4.3$ GeV
- The self-consistent DSE solution yields $M(0) \simeq 280$ MeV for the constituent quark mass
- The renormalization constants obtained are $Z_4 = 0.75$ (LRS) and $Z_4 = 0.72$ (MEM), and $Z_2 = 0.938$ (LRS) and $Z_2 = 0.936$ (MEM)
- The effective form factor $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff}$ reaches $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff} \simeq 17$ in the deep infrared region at $p^2 \approx 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$ for the linear regularization solution
- The form factor shows structure around $p^2 \simeq 0.6$ GeV$^2$ with $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff} \approx 2$, and a local minimum at $p^2 \simeq 1.1$ GeV$^2$ with $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff} \simeq 0.5$
- The lattice data covers momenta from $\sim 120$ MeV up to $\sim 4$ GeV for the quark propagator
- The MEM fit used 47 parameters fitted to $N=138$ data points, yielding $\chi^2 = 139.4$
- For the linear regularization with $\Lambda = 5$ GeV, the minimal $\chi^2/{\rm dof} = 1.536$ was achieved with $\epsilon_A = 70.1$, $\epsilon_B = 2.1$

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative study of the quark-gluon vertex by combining DSE methods with lattice QCD data from Bowman (2005), building on earlier work by Aguilar (2010) who computed the form factor $X_0$ using a one-loop dressed approximation that barely deviated from unity. The authors find that their extracted $\tilde X_0^{\rm eff}$ has a much richer structure than the one-loop dressed approximation of Aguilar (2010), and that the standard Ball-Chiu ansatz (equivalent to $\tilde X_0 = 1$) does not produce sufficient dynamical chiral symmetry breaking when using a lattice-regularized dressed gluon propagator. The effective form factor provides a practical tool for quark DSE applications in hadron physics, though the authors note that a full calculation of all form factors of the quark-ghost scattering kernel, augmented by the minimal ansatz for the transverse vertex recently put forward by Qin (2013), is needed for a complete description.
