# Box diagram contribution to the axial two-nucleon current

> Recently, we have worked out the axial two-nucleon current operator to leading one-loop order in chiral effective field theory using the method of unitary transformation. Our final expressions, however, differ from the ones derived by the JLab-Pisa group using time-ordered perturbation theory (Phys. Rev. C 93, no. 1, 015501 (2016) Erratum: [Phys. Rev. C 93, no. 4, 049902 (2016)] Erratum: [Phys. Rev. C 95, no. 5, 059901 (2017)]). In this paper we consider the box diagram contribution to the axial current and demonstrate that the results obtained using the two methods are unitary equivalent at the Fock-space level. We adjust the unitary phases by matching the corresponding two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials and rederive the box diagram contribution to the axial current operator following the approach of the JLab-Pisa group, thereby reproducing our original result. We provide a detailed information on the calculation including the relevant intermediate steps in order to facilitate a clarification of this disagreement.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the previously reported disagreement between the Bochum-Bonn group's axial two-nucleon current derived via unitary transformation and the JLab-Pisa group's result from time-ordered perturbation theory is resolved: the two formulations are unitarily equivalent at the Fock-space level, provided the unitary phases are fixed by matching the corresponding two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors re-derive the box diagram contribution (diagram e8 in the JLab-Pisa notation) to the axial-vector current using the JLab-Pisa group's inversion method for the transfer matrix, applying the same Fock-space expressions for the effective potential. They fix the unitary phases α₁ = -1/2 and α₂ = 1/4 by matching the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential to the JLab-Pisa result, then compute the six time-ordered box diagrams proportional to g_A⁵, perform Passarino-Veltman reduction of tensor integrals in d dimensions, and reduce all scalar integrals to the basis functions s(2,0) = -M_π/(4π) and s(2,2) = A(q₂)/(2π) with A(q₂) = (1/(2q₂)) arctan(q₂/(2M_π)).

## Key numbers

- The unitary phases are fixed to α₁ = -1/2 and α₂ = 1/4, matching the JLab-Pisa group's convention.
- The scalar integral s(2,0) = -M_π/(4π) in three dimensions.
- The scalar integral s(2,2) = A(q₂)/(2π), where A(q₂) = (1/(2q₂)) arctan(q₂/(2M_π)).
- The pion decay constant F_π and nucleon axial coupling constant g_A appear in the prefactor g_A⁵/(512 π F_π⁴) for the final box diagram expressions.
- The pion mass M_π appears explicitly in the reduced expressions, e.g., terms proportional to 3M_π and M_π³/(4M_π² + q₂²).

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between the Bochum-Bonn group's derivation using the method of unitary transformation (Krebs, 2016) and the JLab-Pisa group's time-ordered perturbation theory approach (Baroni, 2015; Baroni, 2016). The demonstration of unitary equivalence enables consistent use of either formalism for nuclear axial-vector current calculations in chiral effective field theory, and the detailed intermediate expressions facilitate error diagnostics and cross-checks between the two approaches.
