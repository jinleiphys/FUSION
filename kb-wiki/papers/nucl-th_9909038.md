# Hyperon-nucleon interactions in the $p p \to K^+ Λp$ reaction

> We present calculations of the invariant mass spectra of the $Λ$p system for the exclusive $p p \to K^+ Λp$ reaction with the aim of studying the final state interaction between the $Λ$-hyperon and the proton. The reaction is described within a meson exchange framework and the final state $Λp$ interaction is incorporated through an off-shell t-matrix for the $Λp \to Λp$ scattering, constructed using the available hyperon-nucleon (YN) potentials. The cross sections are found to be sensitive to the type of YN potential used especially at the $Λ$ and $Σ$ production thresholds. Hence, data on this exclusive reaction, which can be used to constrain the YN potentials are desirable.

## Key claim

The calculated cross sections for the $p p \to K^+ \Lambda p$ reaction are sensitive to the type of hyperon-nucleon (YN) potential used, especially at the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ production thresholds, but the extent of agreement with existing inclusive data depends on which specific YN potential is chosen, and exclusive data are needed to further constrain the potentials.

## Method

The authors calculated the invariant mass spectra of the $\Lambda p$ system for the exclusive $p p \to K^+ \Lambda p$ reaction at a proton beam energy of 2.3 GeV using a meson exchange framework that includes pion exchange, kaon exchange, and direct kaon emission diagrams. The final state $\Lambda p$ interaction was incorporated through an off-shell t-matrix for $\Lambda p \to \Lambda p$ scattering, constructed by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the Nijmegen soft-core and Juelich (versions A, B, tilde-A, tilde-B) YN potentials, including the coupling to the $\Sigma N$ channel.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam energy: 2.3 GeV; kaon laboratory angle fixed at 10 degrees.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{K\Lambda N} = -14.1$ and $g_{\pi NN} = 13.3$.
- Cut-off masses in monopole form factors: $\Lambda_K = 1.2$ GeV for kaon exchange and $\Lambda_{\pi} = 1.3$ GeV for pion exchange.
- The $\Sigma$ production threshold in the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass is at W = 2128 MeV.
- The on-shell approximation multiplied by a form factor with $\beta = 1.36$ fm$^{-1}$ overestimates the correct off-shell t-matrix results.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the $p p \to K^+ Y N$ reaction using resonance models (Tsushima) and meson exchange models (Laget) by explicitly incorporating the off-shell final state interaction between the $\Lambda$ and proton through realistic YN potentials. The calculations show that approximating the final state interaction by an on-shell t-matrix with a form factor, as done in Laget's work, can lead to misleading conclusions about reaction parameters. The results enable future exclusive measurements of the $p p \to K^+ \Lambda p$ reaction to differentiate among the Nijmegen and Juelich YN potentials, and the authors plan to extend the calculations to $\Sigma$ producing channels.
