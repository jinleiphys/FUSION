# Thermalization and isotropization of heavy quarks in a non-Markovian medium in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We study the isotropization and thermalization of heavy quarks in a non-Markovian medium in high energy nuclear collisions. In particular, we analyze the case of a non-stationary medium with a noise whose time-correlator decays as a power law (heavy tailed noise). We assume the correlations decay with an exponent $β-1$, $0\leqβ<1$; we treat $β$ as a free parameter. We analyze the effect of memory on the thermalization and isotropization of heavy quarks in the medium via a generalized Langevin equation. In general, we find that memory slows down the dynamics of heavy quarks; moreover, thermalization and isotropization happen on the same time scale once a realistic initialization is considered. We also find that while the effect on charm quarks can be relevant, beauty quarks are hardly affected by memory in the quark-gluon plasma phase. Finally, we comment on the effect of memory on the estimate of $D_s$ of charm and beauty.

## Key claim

Memory effects from a noise whose time-correlator decays as a power law (heavy-tailed noise) slow down the thermalization and isotropization of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, with charm quarks significantly affected while beauty quarks are hardly affected; the specific form of the noise correlations affects the late-time evolution, unlike the exponential memory case studied in Ruggieri (2022) where memory effects vanish at late times.

## Method

The authors couple heavy quarks to a non-Markovian bath via a generalized Langevin equation with a dissipative kernel and a noise term whose time correlations decay as a power law with exponent β-1 (0 ≤ β < 1). The noise is implemented as a Riemann-Liouville fractional integral of a Gaussian white noise, and the dissipative kernel is defined via a fluctuation-dissipation relation. The generalized Langevin equation is solved numerically for charm and beauty quarks in a QGP bath, using diffusion coefficients from pQCD and a quasi-particle model (QPM), with initial momenta sampled from the FONLL distribution.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks at T = 0.3 GeV with D = 0.13 GeV²/fm (QPM value at p=0), the thermalization time is τ_therm = 6.9 fm/c without memory and τ_therm = 11.4 fm/c with memory (β = 0.5)
- For the bath with memory (β = 0.5), τ_therm ∝ 1/D^α with α ≈ 0.5, while for the memoryless bath τ_therm ≈ ⟨E⟩T/D
- At T = 0.25 GeV with D = 0.1 GeV²/fm, the equilibrium kinetic energy for charm (m = 1.5 GeV) is K_eq = 0.44 GeV
- The parameter κ = 1/8.44 is introduced to reproduce the momentum spreading of memoryless processes in the β → 0 limit

## Context

This work extends the study of Ruggieri (2022), which examined exponential memory kernels, to the case of power-law decaying correlations that appear in strongly correlated systems. The authors find that unlike the exponential case, power-law memory affects heavy quark dynamics even at late times. The results contradict the common assumption in heavy quark studies (Uphoff 2011, Song 2015, Cao 2016, Plumari 2017, and many others) that memory effects can be ignored. The work enables future studies of how memory affects observables like the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow in realistic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
