# Violation of the $Δ$I=1/2 rule in the nonmesonic weak decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> Violations of the $Δ$I=1/2 rule are investigated in the nonmesonic weak hypernuclear decay using a weak $Λ$N$\to$NN transition potential based on meson exchange. While the weak $Δ$I=3/2 matrix elements of baryons with pseudoscalar mesons are known to be very small, the same matrix elements of baryons with vector mesons, evaluated in the factorization approximation, are found to be significant. Within the uncertainties of the factorization approximation we find that the total rate increases by at most 6% lying within the error bars of the more recent experimental result. The neutron- to proton-induced rate, on the other hand, can change by up to a factor of two, while the asymmetry parameter is strongly affected as well.

## Key claim

Violations of the ΔI=1/2 rule in the nonmesonic weak decay of Λ hypernuclei are investigated using a meson-exchange model; while the total decay rate changes by at most 6% and remains within experimental error bars, the neutron-to-proton-induced rate can change by up to a factor of two and the asymmetry parameter is strongly affected, but these results depend on the factorization approximation whose reliability is estimated to be within a factor of 3-4.

## Method

The authors computed weak ΔI=3/2 coupling constants for the ΛNρ and NNK* vertices using the factorization approximation, evaluating baryon-to-baryon and vacuum-to-meson matrix elements of quark currents from the QCD-enhanced effective weak Hamiltonian. These ΔI=3/2 amplitudes were incorporated into a nonrelativistic meson-exchange potential for the ΛN→NN transition, including pseudoscalar (π, η, K) and vector (ρ, ω, K*) meson exchange, and applied to the decay of 12ΛC using standard nuclear structure methods with short-range ΛN correlations from the Nijmegen interaction.

## Key numbers

- Total nonmesonic decay rate of 12ΛC changes by at most 6%, ranging from 0.753 to 0.796 in units of the free Λ decay rate ΓΛ = 3.8 × 10^9 s^-1
- Neutron-to-proton-induced ratio Γn/Γp varies from 0.034 to 0.136 depending on the scaling factors (sρ, sK*) applied to the ΔI=3/2 couplings
- Proton-induced rate Γp/ΓΛ is barely affected, ranging from 0.700 to 0.758
- Neutron-induced rate Γn/ΓΛ changes from 0.026 to 0.095
- Intrinsic asymmetry parameter aΛ varies from -0.522 to -0.072, a factor of 7 difference between extreme cases
- Weak ΔI=3/2 coupling constants for ρ meson: f1 = -0.93, f2 = -1.52, g1 = 0.67 (in units of GF mπ^2 = 2.21 × 10^-7)
- Weak ΔI=3/2 coupling constants for K* meson: f1 = 0.77, f2 = 2.83, g1 = 0.94 (in same units)

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model of Parreño, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997) by adding ΔI=3/2 amplitudes that were previously omitted under the assumption of the ΔI=1/2 rule. It builds on the suggestion by Maltman and Shmatikov (1994, 1995) that significant ΔI=3/2 contributions may exist in the weak vector meson-baryon couplings, in contrast to the well-established ΔI=1/2 dominance of pseudoscalar meson couplings. The results show that improved measurements of the neutron-induced rate and asymmetry parameter, particularly from facilities like FINUDA at DAPHNE, could help assess the validity of the ΔI=1/2 rule in the weak vector meson sector.
