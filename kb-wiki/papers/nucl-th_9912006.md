# Quadrupole Pairing Interaction and Signature Inversion

> The signature inversion in the πh11/2 \otimes νh11/2 rotational bands of odd-odd Cs and La isotopes and the πh11/2 \otimes νi13/2 bands of odd-odd Tb, Ho and Tm nuclei is investigated using pairing and deformation self consistent mean field calculations. The model can rather satisfactorily account for the anomalous signature splitting, provided that spin assignments in som of the bands are revised. Our calculations show that signature inversioncan appear already at axially symmetric shapes. It is found that this is due to the contribution of the λμ=22 component of the quadrupole pairing interaction to the mean field potential.

## Key claim

Signature inversion in odd-odd nuclei can appear already at axially symmetric shapes, contrary to the previous consensus that required positive triaxial deformation (γ > 0). The inversion is caused specifically by the (λμ)=(22) component of the quadrupole pairing interaction contributing to the mean-field potential, provided that spin assignments in some of the bands are revised.

## Method

The authors performed deformation and pairing self-consistent Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations using a macroscopic-microscopic approach with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential and liquid-drop model. Pairing was treated with a separable interaction combining seniority (monopole) and doubly-stretched quadrupole (QQ) pairing, with the Lipkin-Nogami approximate number projection to avoid pairing collapse. The cranked-Lipkin-Nogami equation was solved self-consistently at each rotational frequency and each grid point in (β₂, γ, β₄) deformation space, with blocking performed separately for each signature.

## Key numbers

- Monopole pairing strength G₀₀ ≈ 165 keV (protons) and 145 keV (neutrons) in the A∼130 mass region, with QQ-pairing strengths G₂μ ≈ 4.4 keV/fm⁴ (protons) and 3.3 keV/fm⁴ (neutrons)
- Monopole pairing strength G₀₀ ≈ 140 keV (protons) and 105 keV (neutrons) in the A∼160 mass region, with QQ-pairing strengths G₂μ ≈ 3.0 keV/fm⁴ (protons) and 1.6 keV/fm⁴ (neutrons)
- In ¹²⁰Cs, the signature splitting of the πh₁₁/₂ low-Ω levels is calculated to be more than 0.7 MeV at ħω = 0.2 MeV
- The Γ⁽²²⁾ potential creates signature inversion of the order of a few tens of keV already at axial shape
- The QQ-pairing force increases the anomalous splitting by approximately 40 keV at fixed triaxial shape of γ = 15° and ħω = 0.2 MeV
- For Cs and La isotopes, the agreement between calculated and experimental Routhians is generally within 50 keV
- The signature inversion magnitude is generally less than 30 keV for the Rare Earth nuclei considered
- The critical frequency ħωc (where Coriolis force restores normal signature ordering) shows a significant increase due to QQ-pairing

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TRS calculations on ¹²⁰Cs (Satula 1996) to a systematic study of signature inversion across multiple isotopic chains. It contradicts the earlier interpretation that triaxiality (γ > 0) was the primary mechanism for signature inversion, as proposed by Frauendorf (1983), Hamamoto (1983), and Bengtsson (1984). The results also contrast with the residual proton-neutron contact force approach of Semmes and Ragnarsson (1991), which required different strength parameters for different mass regions. The calculations enable future work on electromagnetic transition rates (BM1/BE2 ratios) and suggest that coupling between rotation and γ-vibration, as well as more elaborate valence neutron-proton interactions, may further improve agreement with data.
