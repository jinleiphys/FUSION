# Electrostatic energy of Coulomb crystals with polarized electron background

> Outer crusts of neutron stars and interiors of cool white dwarfs consist of bare atomic nuclei, arranged in a crystal lattice and immersed in a Fermi gas of degenerate electrons. We study electrostatic properties of such Coulomb crystals, taking into account the polarizability of the electron gas and considering different lattice structures, which can form the ground state. To take the electron background polarization into account, we use the linear response theory with the electron dielectric function given either by the Thomas-Fermi approximation or by the random-phase approximation (RPA). We compare the widely used nonrelativistic (Lindhard) version of the RPA approximation with the more general, relativistic (Jancovici) version. The results of the different approximations are compared to assess the importance of going beyond the Thomas-Fermi or Lindhard approximations. We also include contribution of zero-point vibrations of ions into the ground-state energy. We show that the bcc lattice forms the ground state for any charge number $Z$ of the atomic nuclei at the densities where the electrons are relativistic ($ρ\gtrsim10^6$ g cm$^{-3}$), while at lower densities the fcc and hcp lattices can form the ground state. The MgB$_2$-like lattice never forms the ground state at realistic densities in the crystallized regions of degenerate stars. The RPA corrections strongly affect the boundaries between the phases. As a result, transitions between different ground-state structures depend on $Z$ in a nontrivial way. The relativistic and quantum corrections produce less dramatic effects, moderately shifting the phase boundaries.

## Key claim

The bcc lattice forms the ground state for any nuclear charge number $Z$ at densities where electrons are relativistic ($\rho \gtrsim 10^6$ g cm$^{-3}$), while at lower nonrelativistic densities the fcc and hcp lattices can form the ground state; however, the RPA corrections strongly affect the phase boundaries, making transitions between different ground-state structures depend on $Z$ in a nontrivial way, and the MgB$_2$-like lattice never forms the ground state at realistic densities in degenerate stars.

## Method

The authors calculated the electrostatic energy of Coulomb crystals (bcc, fcc, hcp, and MgB$_2$ lattices) with a polarizable degenerate electron background using linear response theory with three dielectric function approximations: Thomas-Fermi, nonrelativistic random-phase approximation (Lindhard), and relativistic random-phase approximation (Jancovici). They computed the total ground-state energy by adding the electrostatic energy to the zero-point vibrational energy of ions, obtained from phonon spectra calculated in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and determined which lattice structure minimizes the total energy for different values of charge number $Z$, relativity parameter $x$, and density parameter $r_s$.

## Key numbers

- The bcc lattice forms the ground state for any $Z$ at densities $\rho \gtrsim 10^6$ g cm$^{-3}$ (relativistic regime), while at lower densities fcc and hcp can form the ground state
- In the Thomas-Fermi model, the bcc-fcc transition occurs at $\kappa_\mathrm{TF} a = 1.065714$; using only the quadratic approximation shifts this to $\kappa_\mathrm{TF} a \approx 0.93715$
- The Madelung constants for the four lattices: bcc = $-0.895929255682$, fcc = $-0.895873615195$, hcp = $-0.895838120459$, MgB$_2$ = $-0.89450562823$
- The quadratic Thomas-Fermi coefficients $\eta_\mathrm{TF}$: bcc = $-0.103732333707$, fcc = $-0.103795687531$, hcp = $-0.103809851801$, MgB$_2$ = $-0.104080616256$
- The minimum Thomas-Fermi parameter for relativistic electrons is $(\kappa_\mathrm{TF} a)_{\min} = 0.185 Z^{1/3}$
- The unconditional bcc stability range extends to relativity parameters $x \gtrsim 0.8$ (mass densities $\rho \gtrsim 10^6$ g cm$^{-3}$) in the most advanced model
- Zero-point vibrations shift phase boundary densities by less than 1.5% for $x \gtrsim 0.1$
- The hcp lattice height correction shifts transition densities by $\lesssim 0.7\%$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb crystals in neutron star crusts and white dwarf interiors, which had considered only bcc and fcc lattices with polarized electron background (PH73, PC00, B02, K182). It builds on the approach of Chamel and Fantina (2016) for determining ground-state structure and the review by Kozhberov (2020) on Madelung constants. The results enable more accurate modeling of structural transitions in degenerate stars, showing that the bcc lattice is the ground state at high densities while fcc and hcp can appear at lower densities, with the MgB$_2$ lattice never being energetically preferred.
