# Extended Skyrme effective interactions for transport model and neutron stars

> It is important to develop a unified theoretical framework to describe the nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations based on the same effective nuclear interactions. Based on the so-called Skyrme pseudopotential up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, we construct a series of extended Skyrme interactions by modifying the density-dependent term and fitting the empirical nucleon optical potential up to above $1$ GeV, the empirical properties of isospin symmetric nuclear matter, the microscopic calculations of pure neutron matter and the properties of neutron stars from astrophysical observations. The modification of the density-dependent term in the extended Skyrme interactions follows the idea of Fermi momentum expansion and this leads to a highly flexible density behavior of the symmetry energy. In particular, the values of the density slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy for the new extended Skyrme interactions range from $L = -5$ MeV to $L = 125$ MeV by construction, to cover the large uncertainty of the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Furthermore, in order to consider the effects of isoscalar and isovector nucleon effective masses, we adjust the momentum dependency of the single-nucleon optical potential and the symmetry potential of these new extended Skyrme interactions and construct a parameter set family, by which we systematically study the impacts of the symmetry energy and the nucleon effective masses on the properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars. The new extended Skyrme interactions constructed in the present work will be useful to determine the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, especially the symmetry energy, as well as the nucleon effective masses and their isospin splitting, in transport model simulations for heavy-ion collisions, nuclear structure calculations and neutron star studies.

## Key claim

The authors construct extended Skyrme interactions that can simultaneously describe finite nuclei, heavy-ion collisions up to 1 GeV/nucleon, and neutron star properties, but only by modifying the density-dependent term to allow flexible adjustment of higher-order symmetry energy coefficients; the claim that these interactions are "useful" for transport model simulations is conditional on the specific parameter sets fitting empirical nucleon optical potentials, microscopic pure neutron matter calculations, and astrophysical observations.

## Method

The authors start from the Skyrme pseudopotential up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and replace the conventional single density-dependent term (proportional to ρ^α) with three terms proportional to ρ^{1/3}, ρ^{3/3}, and ρ^{5/3}, which allows the equation of state to be expressed as a power series in the Fermi momentum. They then fit the resulting 20 Skyrme parameters to the empirical nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV, selected empirical properties of isospin symmetric nuclear matter, microscopic many-body calculations of pure neutron matter, and neutron star observations including mass-radius constraints from GW170817, NICER, and the central compact object in HESS J1731-347.

## Key numbers

- The density slope parameter L of the symmetry energy for the eight main parameter sets ranges from L = 5 MeV to L = 75 MeV, with four additional sets at L = -5, 85, 105, and 125 MeV
- To simultaneously satisfy constraints from microscopic calculations on pure neutron matter and astrophysical observations of neutron stars with masses of 1.4 M⊙ and 2.0 M⊙, L must lie within 5 MeV ≤ L ≤ 75 MeV
- A range of -5 MeV ≤ L ≤ 35 MeV is necessary to describe the central compact object in HESS J1731-347 (mass M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M⊙, radius R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km)
- The nucleon optical potential is fitted up to energy of 1 GeV
- The EOS is expressed as a power series in ρ^{1/3} from ρ^{2/3} (kinetic energy) to ρ^{9/3}

## Context

This work extends the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential approach of Wang (2018) and the earlier quasilocal energy-density functional construction by Carlsson (2008) and Raimondi (2011), which could describe finite nuclei and heavy-ion collisions but could not simultaneously describe neutron star properties, particularly the small mass and radius of the CCO in HESS J1731-347. The modification follows the idea of Fermi momentum expansion used in the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (eSHF) model of Zhang (2015) and the KIDS model of Papakonstantinou (2016), but introduces three higher-order coefficients (J0, Ksym, Jsym) that can be adjusted to describe neutron stars. The resulting parameter set family of 144 sets enables systematic study of how symmetry energy and nucleon effective masses affect nuclear matter and neutron star properties, and can be applied in transport model simulations for heavy-ion collisions, nuclear structure calculations, and neutron star studies.
