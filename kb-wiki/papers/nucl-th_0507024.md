# Low-energy etaN interactions: scattering lengths and resonance parameters

> We consider the impact of two recent pi- p-->eta n measurements on the etaN scattering length and etaN branching fractions for the N(1535) and N(1520) resonances within a coupled-channel analysis of piN elastic scattering and etaN production data. The sensitivity of these results to model input is also explored.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $\eta N$ scattering length and the $\eta N$ branching fractions for the $N(1535)$ and $N(1520)$ resonances are sensitive to the choice of model used in the coupled-channel analysis, and that improvements in the fitted data do not necessarily reduce the spread in extracted values, particularly for the real part of the scattering length.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix analysis of $\pi N$ elastic scattering data up to 2.1 GeV in pion lab kinetic energy and $\pi^-p\to\eta n$ production data from threshold to 0.8 GeV, including two recent BNL measurements (E909 and E913/914 with the Crystal Ball spectrometer). They extracted resonance parameters for the $N(1535)$ and $N(1520)$ by fitting the $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ partial waves with a K-matrix resonance form allowing two poles plus background, and searched for poles in the complex energy plane; they also compared fits with and without the Crystal Ball data and with different assumptions for the $\rho N$ and $\pi\Delta$ branching fractions of the $N(1520)$.

## Key numbers

- For the $N(1535)$ resonance, the $\eta N$ branching fraction was found to exceed the $\pi N$ fraction in all fits, consistent with recent determinations of about 50% for the $\eta N$ channel.
- For the $N(1520)$ resonance, the $\eta N$ branching fraction was found to be very small, with the authors' range spanning previous determinations of $0.0023\pm0.0004$ (Penner and Mosel) and $0.0008\pm0.0001$ (Mainz analysis).
- From the E909 threshold data, the linear fit gave $\frac{1}{p_{\eta}^\ast}\sigma_{\pi ^- p\to\eta n} = 15.2 \pm 0.8~{\rm \mu b/MeV}$, leading to a lower bound $ImA_{\eta N}\ge 0.172 \pm 0.009~{\rm fm}$.
- This compares with an older result (Binnie et al., 1973) of $\frac{1}{p_{\eta}^\ast}\sigma_{\pi^-p\to\eta n} = 21.2 \pm 1.8 ~{\rm \mu b/MeV}$ and $ImA_{\eta N}\ge 0.24 \pm 0.02~{\rm fm}$.
- The Particle Data Group estimate for the $N(1520)$ $\eta N$ branching ratio is $0.0023\pm0.0004$.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analyses of $\pi N$ elastic scattering and $\eta N$ production by incorporating two recent BNL measurements (E909 and Crystal Ball E913/914), improving on older and often conflicting data reviewed by Clajus and Nefkens. The results are compared with previous determinations by Penner and Mosel, Green and Wycech, and the Mainz group (Tiator et al.), and the authors find that the real part of the $\eta N$ scattering length is particularly model-dependent, with values ranging from negative to 1 fm in earlier work. The analysis enables more precise determinations of the $N(1535)$ and $N(1520)$ resonance parameters and highlights the need for future multi-channel fits including $\pi N\to\pi\pi N$ data and improved eta-photoproduction measurements.
