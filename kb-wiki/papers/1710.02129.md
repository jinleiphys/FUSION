# Coherent $μ-e$ Conversion at Next-to-Leading Order

> We analyze next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections and uncertainties for coherent $μ-e$ conversion . The analysis is general but numerical results focus on ${}^{27}\textrm{Al}$, which will be used in the Mu2E experiment. We obtain a simple expression for the branching ratio in terms of Wilson coefficients associated with possible physics beyond the Standard Model and a set of model-independent parameters determined solely by Standard Model dynamics. For scalar-mediated conversion, we find that NLO two-nucleon contributions can significantly decrease the branching ratio, potentially reducing the rate by as much as 50%. The pion-nucleon $σ$-term and quark masses give the dominant sources of parametric uncertainty in this case. For vector-mediated conversion, the impact of NLO contributions is considerably less severe, while the present theoretical uncertainties are comparable to parametric uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper finds that next-to-leading order (NLO) two-nucleon contributions can reduce the branching ratio for scalar-mediated coherent muon-to-electron conversion by as much as 50%, significantly degrading experimental sensitivity, whereas vector-mediated conversion is much less affected at this order.

## Method

The authors use SU(2) chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to match dimension-six effective CLFV operators at the quark level onto hadronic degrees of freedom (nucleons and pions), treating strange quark contributions as flavor singlets. They compute leading-order (LO) tree-level, NLO one-loop, and NLO two-nucleon (pion-exchange) diagrams for scalar-mediated conversion, and evaluate the corresponding model-independent parameters for aluminum-27, the target nucleus for the Mu2e experiment. For vector-mediated conversion, they note that no NLO contributions arise because the leading corrections to the charge operator appear at NNLO.

## Key numbers

* For scalar-mediated conversion on Al-27, the LO contribution from u- and d-quarks is α_{S,ud}^{(1)}(LO) = 65 ± 11.
* The NLO one-loop contribution for u- and d-quarks is −α_{S,ud}^{(1)}(NLO loop) = 2.71 ± 0.30.
* The NLO two-nucleon contribution for u- and d-quarks is −α_{S,ud}^{(1)}(NLO NN) = 18.8^{+1.6}_{-9.5}.
* The common prefactor for scalar amplitudes is sqrt(m_μ/ω_capt) × (m_μ/(4π v))^2 = 0.5563 ± 0.0005.
* For vector-mediated conversion on Al-27, the model-independent parameters are α_{V,u}^{(1)} = 12.25 ± 0.13 and α_{V,d}^{(1)} = 12.23 ± 0.27.
* The current experimental limit from SINDRUM II is BR(μ−e) < 7 × 10^{-13}, while Mu2e and COMET are expected to reach BR(μ−e) ≲ 5 × 10^{-17}.

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order analyses of coherent muon-to-electron conversion by Kitano (2002), Cirigliano (2009), and Crivellin (2014) to include NLO corrections and their uncertainties. The results enable more accurate predictions for the Mu2e and COMET experiments, which will improve sensitivity by roughly four orders of magnitude over the SINDRUM II bound. The dominant parametric uncertainties for scalar-mediated conversion come from the pion-nucleon sigma-term and quark masses, while for vector-mediated conversion the theoretical and parametric uncertainties are comparable.
