# S-matrix analysis of the baryon electric charge correlation

> We compute the correlation of the net baryon number with the electric charge ($χ_{BQ}$) for an interacting hadron gas using the S-matrix formulation of statistical mechanics. The observable $χ_{BQ}$ is particularly sensitive to the details of the pion-nucleon interaction, which are consistently incorporated in the current scheme via the empirical scattering phase shifts. Comparing to the recent lattice QCD studies in the $(2+1)$-flavor system, we find that the natural implementation of interactions and the proper treatment of resonances in the S-matrix approach lead to an improved description of the lattice data over that obtained in the hadron resonance gas model.

# S-matrix analysis of the baryon electric charge correlation

## Key claim

The S-matrix formalism, using empirical pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts, yields an improved description of the lattice QCD results for the baryon-electric charge correlation χ_BQ compared to the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, but only up to temperatures around T ~ 160 MeV, beyond which hadronic descriptions break down.

## Method

The authors compute the baryon-electric charge susceptibility χ_BQ for an interacting hadron gas using the S-matrix formulation of statistical mechanics, incorporating two-body πN interactions via empirical scattering phase shifts from the SAID partial-wave analysis (15 partial waves for each isospin channel I=1/2 and I=3/2). The effective density of states is constructed from the phase shifts and folded with Fermi-Dirac distribution functions, with free nucleons and |S|=2,3 baryons treated as a non-interacting gas. Inelastic effects from the ηN channel are included via a coupled-channel S-matrix approach using an effective range expansion for the S11 partial wave.

## Key numbers

- At T = 154 MeV, the overall suppression of the interaction contribution in the S-matrix approach compared to the HRG model is around 30%
- For the I=1/2 sector at T = 154 MeV, the ratio of χ_BQ from S-matrix to HRG summed over all channels is 0.700
- For the I=3/2 sector at T = 154 MeV, the ratio of χ_BQ from S-matrix to HRG summed over all channels is 0.657
- The dominant I=3/2 channel P33 (Δ resonance) contributes 1.149 in the S-matrix approach versus 0.829 in HRG at T = 154 MeV
- Up to T ≈ 0.16 GeV, over 90% of the χ_BQ value comes from the elastic part of the spectrum (M ≤ m_η + m_N ≈ 1.5 GeV)
- The ηN scattering length parameters from effective range expansion: a = 0.91(6) + i 0.27(2) fm, r_0 = -1.33(15) - i 0.30(2) fm, s = -0.15(1) - i 0.04(1) fm³

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach previously applied to broad resonances like the σ- and κ-mesons (Broniowski 2015, Weinhold 1997) to the baryon-electric charge correlation, which is particularly sensitive to πN interactions. The observable χ_BQ avoids large mesonic contributions and cancels for |S|=1 hyperons in isospin-symmetric systems, making it an ideal probe of nucleon-pion dynamics. The results enable future S-matrix studies of other lattice observables such as χ_BQ/χ_BS and χ_BQ/χ_BB, though these require more extensive partial-wave data including strange baryon channels.
