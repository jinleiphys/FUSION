# DEEP INELASTIC SCATTERING FROM POLARIZED DEUTERONS

> The spin-dependent structure function of the deuteron, g_1D, is calculated within a covariant framework. The off-shell structure of the bound nucleon gives corrections to the convolution model at a level of half a percent for x below 0.7, increasing to more than five percent at larger x. Overall, the dominant source of error comes from the lack of knowledge associated with the deuteron D-state, which may introduce an uncertainty in the neutron spin structure function, g_1n, extracted from deuterium data of up to ten percent for x around 0.2.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent structure function of the deuteron, g_1D, is calculated within a covariant framework, and the conventional convolution model is shown to break down due to off-shell effects of bound nucleons. However, for x below 0.7 these off-shell corrections are only at the level of half a percent, and the dominant source of uncertainty in extracting the neutron spin structure function g_1n from deuterium data is the lack of knowledge of the deuteron D-state wavefunction, which may introduce an uncertainty of up to ten percent for x around 0.2.

## Method

The authors calculate the polarized deuteron structure function g_1D within a covariant impulse approximation, expanding the truncated nucleon tensor in terms of basis tensors and using relativistic deuteron-nucleon vertex functions. They decompose the full result into a convolution component plus three non-factorizable off-shell corrections arising from the deuteron-nucleon vertex, the nucleon structure function, and an additional term involving the scalar function G_(p). The off-shell nucleon structure function is modeled using relativistic quark-nucleon vertex functions with parameters fitted to unpolarized and polarized nucleon data at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, using SU(4) spin-flavor symmetric relations as a starting point but allowing for symmetry breaking at large x.

## Key numbers

- Off-shell corrections to the convolution model are at the level of half a percent for x below 0.7, increasing to more than five percent at larger x.
- The uncertainty in the neutron spin structure function g_1n extracted from deuterium data due to lack of knowledge of the deuteron D-state may be up to ten percent for x around 0.2.
- The total nuclear effect (including Fermi motion and binding) produces a dip of approximately 7-8% in the ratio g_1D/g_1N at x ≈ 0.6.
- The D-state probability ω_D used in the calculation is 4.7% from the model of Buck and Gross (1979), compared to 5.8% used in the SMC analysis.
- The first moments of the polarized valence distributions in the proton are ∫_0^1 dx Δu_V(x) = 0.99 and ∫_0^1 dx Δd_V(x) = -0.27, saturating the Bjorken sum rule.
- The absolute uncertainty in g_1n from neglecting off-shell corrections is |Δg_1n| ∼ 0.002, while the uncertainty from the D-state depolarization factor can be |Δg_1n| ∼ 0.01.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of nuclear effects in polarized deuteron structure functions by Woloshyn (1990), Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1993), and Toki et al. (1993), which all adopted the convolution approach. It also builds on the authors' previous study of off-shell corrections to the spin-averaged deuteron structure function F_2D (Melnitchouk et al., 1994), where those corrections were found to be 1-2% for x < 0.9. The paper enables more accurate extraction of the neutron spin structure function g_1n from upcoming high-statistics experiments such as SLAC E154 and HERMES, and is relevant for verifying the Bjorken sum rule when combined with proton data from SLAC, EMC, and SMC.
