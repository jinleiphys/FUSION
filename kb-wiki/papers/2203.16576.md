# Speed of Sound for Hadronic and Quark Phases in a Magnetic Field

> In this paper we calculate the speed of sound for three phases that may exist inside a magnetized hybrid neutron star at different density regions: A hadronic phase at low densities, quark-matter in the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase at intermediate densities and a free-quark phase modeled by the MIT bag model at higher densities. It is found that the speed of sound exhibits a non-monotonic behavior, that goes from values smaller than the conformal limit ($c_s^2 < 1/3$) in the hadronic phase, to peak ($c_s^2 > 1/3$) in the MDCDW phase, to finally reach the conformal limit ($c_s^2 \sim 1/3$) at higher densities for quarks in the MIT bag model. Also, the anisotropic speed of sound in the presence of a magnetic field is derived from first principles. This is a consequence of the anisotropy in the system's pressures produced by the breaking of the rotational symmetry in the presence of a magnetic field. The role played by the lowest Landau level contribution in affecting the speed of sound in the magnetized phases is discussed.

## Key claim
The speed of sound in a magnetized hybrid neutron star exhibits a non-monotonic profile that can exceed the conformal limit ($c_s^2 > 1/3$) in the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) quark phase at intermediate densities, provided the inner magnetic field reaches approximately $10^{18}$ G, before approaching the conformal limit from below at higher densities in the free-quark phase.

## Method
The authors derived anisotropic speed of sound formulas from first principles by perturbing the hydrodynamic equations of the magnetized stress-energy tensor, obtaining separate expressions for sound propagation parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field. They then calculated the speed of sound for three phases using their respective equations of state: the hadronic phase modeled by the non-linear Walecka model in the mean-field approximation, the MDCDW quark phase at intermediate densities, and a free-quark phase described by the MIT bag model at high densities.

## Key numbers
- The conformal limit for the speed of sound squared is $c_s^2 = 1/3$; the hadronic phase yields $c_s^2 < 1/3$.
- In the MDCDW phase at magnetic fields $\sim 10^{18}$ G and densities $\sim 3\rho_0$ (where $\rho_0 = 2.3 \times 10^{17}$ kg/m$^3$ is nuclear density), the speed of sound squared peaks at values significantly surpassing $1/3$.
- In the lowest Landau level (LLL) limit for a generic charged fermion, the parallel speed of sound squared approaches $c_s^{\parallel 2} \simeq 1$ (the causal limit) while the perpendicular component vanishes, $c_s^{\perp 2} = 0$.
- The LLL condition requires $m_i^2 < \mu_i^2 \lesssim B$; for hadrons this would need $B \sim 10^{20}$ G, which would make the neutron star unstable.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of speed of sound exceeding the conformal limit, previously reported in QCD at large isospin density (Beyond-Conformal-1, 2020), two-color QCD (Beyond-Conformal-2, 2020), holographic approaches (Beyond-Conformal-3, 2020), and quarkyonic matter (Beyond-Conformal-6, 2020), by adding the effect of strong magnetic fields. The non-monotonic profile found here satisfies the requirements pointed out by Bedaque and Steiner (2015) for reaching stellar masses $\sim 2M_\odot$ in hybrid stars. The MDCDW phase was previously shown to avoid the Landau-Peierls instability (Ferrer and Incera, 2019) and to satisfy maximum mass constraints (InhStars, 2021), making it a viable candidate for neutron star interiors.
