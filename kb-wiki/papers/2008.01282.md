# Coupled reaction channel study of the 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction, and the 8Be+8Be optical potential

> Given the established 2 alpha structure of 8Be, a realistic model of 4 interacting alpha clusters must be used to obtain a 8Be+8Be interaction potential. Such a four-body problem poses a challenge for the determination of the 8Be+8Be optical potential (OP) that is still unknown due to the lack of the elastic 8Be+8Be scattering data. The main goal of the present study is to probe the complex 8Be+8Be optical potential in the coupled reaction channel (CRC) study of the alpha transfer 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction measured at 65 MeV, and to obtain the spectroscopic information on the alpha+8Be cluster configuration of 12C. The 3- and 4-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) methods are used to calculate the elastic alpha+8Be and 8Be+8Be scattering at the energy around 16 MeV/nucleon, with the breakup effect taken into account explicitly. Using the CDCC-based OP and alpha spectroscopic factors given by the cluster model calculation, a good CRC description of the alpha transfer data without any adjustment of the (complex) potential strength.

# Coupled reaction channel study of the 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction, and the 8Be+8Be optical potential

## Key claim

The 8Be+8Be optical potential, which cannot be determined from elastic scattering data due to the lack of such measurements, can be reliably derived from a 4-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculation using a shallow alpha-alpha interaction potential (the Ali-Bodmer potential), and this potential then successfully describes the alpha transfer reaction 12C(alpha,8Be) without any adjustment of the potential strength, provided the alpha spectroscopic factors from the complex scaling method are used.

## Method

The 3-body and 4-body CDCC methods were used to calculate elastic alpha+8Be and 8Be+8Be scattering at energies around 16 MeV/nucleon, with breakup effects included explicitly via discretized continuum states of the unbound 8Be nucleus. Local equivalent optical potentials for these systems were then deduced from the CDCC elastic scattering wave functions using two approaches: a quantum-mechanical matrix inversion method yielding U_LEP, and a standard optical model fit to the CDCC elastic cross sections using a Woods-Saxon form yielding U_WSD. These CDCC-based optical potentials were used as inputs in a coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculation of the alpha transfer 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction measured at E_alpha = 65 MeV, with alpha spectroscopic factors taken from the complex scaling method (CSM) of Kurokawa and Kato.

## Key numbers

- The 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction was measured at E_alpha = 65 MeV (Wozniak et al.)
- Elastic 8Be+8Be scattering was calculated at E_c.m. = 41.3 MeV; elastic alpha+8Be scattering was calculated at E_c.m. = 43.3 MeV
- The CDCC calculation used a maximum pseudostate energy cutoff of E_max = 10 MeV and maximum 8Be angular momentum l_max = 2
- The volume integral of the real U_WSD for 8Be+8Be at E_c.m. = 41.3 MeV is J_V = 95.88 MeV fm^3; for the imaginary part J_W = 46.87 MeV fm^3
- The volume integral of the real U_LEP for 8Be+8Be at E_c.m. = 41.3 MeV is J_V_LEP = 100.0 MeV fm^3; for the imaginary part J_W_LEP = 29.71 MeV fm^3
- The alpha spectroscopic factor for the alpha+8Be ground state configuration in 12C is S_alpha(g.s.) = 0.36; for the 2+ state (E_x = 2.94 MeV) it is S_alpha(2+) = 0.38, both from the CSM calculation of Kurokawa and Kato
- The 8Be ground state is unbound by 92 keV; a quasi-bound approximation with binding energy 0.01 MeV was used for the alpha cluster in 8Be

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method to determine the 8Be+8Be optical potential, which was previously unknown due to the impossibility of measuring elastic 8Be+8Be scattering. The approach builds on earlier 4-body CDCC calculations by Descouvemont (2018) for 11Be+d scattering and on the 3-body CDCC framework developed for deuteron and exotic nuclei. The alpha spectroscopic factors used are from the complex scaling method of Kurokawa and Kato (2007), and the results contradict the practical approach of interpolating the 8Be+8Be optical potential from neighboring systems like 7,8Be+9Be (Romanyshyn et al., 2009), which was found to fail completely in describing the alpha transfer data. The study enables future investigations of the 4-alpha interaction and cluster structure of 12C through the 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction.
