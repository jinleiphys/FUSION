# Derivation of the nonlocal collision term in the relativistic Boltzmann equation for massive spin-1/2 particles from quantum field theory

> We derive the Boltzmann equation and the collision kernel for massive spin-1/2 particles, using the Wigner-function formalism and employing an expansion in powers of $\hbar$. The phase space is enlarged to include a variable related to the spin degrees of freedom. This allows to reduce the transport equations of the independent components of the Wigner function to one scalar equation. To next-to-leading order in $\hbar$, we find that the collision kernel contains both local and nonlocal terms. We show that off-shell contributions cancel in the Boltzmann equation. Our framework can be used to study spin-polarization phenomena induced by vorticity as recently observed in heavy-ion collisions and in condensed-matter systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic Boltzmann equation for massive spin-1/2 particles from quantum field theory, showing that the collision kernel contains both local and nonlocal terms at next-to-leading order in an expansion in powers of $\hbar$, with the nonlocal terms being essential for describing spin-vorticity coupling. The derivation assumes that spin effects enter at first order in $\hbar$ and that the system is dilute enough to neglect initial correlations and higher-order density corrections.

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner-function formalism for Dirac fields, expand the Wigner function in powers of $\hbar$, and decompose it into Clifford-algebra components. They enlarge the phase space by introducing a spin variable $\ms$ to reduce the transport equations to a single scalar Boltzmann equation for the distribution function $f(x,p,\ms)$. The collision kernel is computed by evaluating ensemble averages over two-particle in-states, using a gradient expansion to separate local and nonlocal contributions, and applying the quasi-particle approximation with a modified on-shell condition.

## Key numbers

- The collision kernel is expanded to first order in $\hbar$: $\mathfrak{C} = \mathfrak{C}^{(0)}_{l} + \hbar \{ \mathfrak{C}^{(1)}_{l} + \mathfrak{C}^{(1)}_{nl} \}$, where the zeroth-order term is purely local and the first-order term contains both local and nonlocal parts.
- The spin variable $\ms$ satisfies the constraints $\ms\cdot\ms = -3$ and $p\cdot\ms = 0$, with integration measure $\int dS(p) = 2$.
- The modified on-shell condition for the scalar distribution is $(p^2 - m^2) f(x,p,\ms) = \hbar \, \mathfrak{M}(x,p,\ms) + \mathcal{O}(\hbar^2)$.
- The axial-vector component $\A^\mu$ remains on the mass shell at first order in $\hbar$ even with interactions, as shown by $(p^2 - m^2) \A^\mu = \mathcal{O}(\hbar^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of a Boltzmann equation with nonlocal collisions for spin-1/2 particles (Weickgenannt 2020) by providing full details of the calculation. It builds on the Wigner-function formalism for relativistic kinetic theory (DeGroot 1980) and addresses the "polarization sign problem" observed in STAR Collaboration data (Adam 2019), where theoretical models based on local thermodynamic equilibrium predict the wrong sign for longitudinal Lambda polarization. The framework enables the development of relativistic spin hydrodynamics and can be used to study spin-polarization phenomena induced by vorticity in heavy-ion collisions and condensed-matter systems.
