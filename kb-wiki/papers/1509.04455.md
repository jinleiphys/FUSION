# (Anti-)strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions

> The production and dynamics of strange and antistrange hadrons in heavy-ion reactions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx$ 3 GeV to 200 GeV is analyzed within the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model. The PHSD results for strange baryon and antibaryon production are roughly consistent with the experimental data starting from upper SPS energies. Nevertheless, hadronic final state flavor-exchange reactions are important for the actual abundances, in particular at large rapidities where hadronic dynamics, parton fragmentation and string decay dominate. A striking disagreement between the PHSD results and the available data persists, however, for bombarding energies below $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx$ 8 GeV where the strangeness production is significantly underestimated as in earlier HSD studies. This finding implies that the strangeness enhancement seen experimentally at FAIR/NICA energies cannot be attributed to a deconfinement phase transition or crossover but probably involves the approximate restoration of chiral symmetry in the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model, which includes a crossover transition to a quark-gluon plasma, systematically underestimates strangeness production at center-of-mass energies below about 8 GeV, implying that the experimentally observed strangeness enhancement at FAIR/NICA energies is not due to deconfinement but likely involves the approximate restoration of chiral symmetry in the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model, an off-shell covariant approach based on Kadanoff-Baym equations, to simulate central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx\) 3 GeV to 200 GeV. They extended the hadronic sector of PHSD to include all strangeness exchange reactions such as \(\pi+N \leftrightarrow K+Y\), \(N+N \leftrightarrow N+K+Y\), \(\bar{K}+N \leftrightarrow \pi+Y\), \(\pi+\Xi \leftrightarrow \bar{K}+Y\), \(Y+Y \leftrightarrow N+\Xi\), \(\bar{K}+\Xi \leftrightarrow \Omega+\pi\), and \(\Xi+\Xi \leftrightarrow \Omega+Y\) in meson-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions. The model incorporates hadronic string decay to partons above a critical energy density of about 0.5 GeV/fm\(^3\) and quark fusion for hadronization, simulating a crossover transition.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV for 5% central Au+Au collisions, baryons and antibaryons differ only at low transverse momentum \(p_T < 0.5\) GeV/c due to net absorption of antibaryons.
- The PHSD model underestimates \(K^+\) meson production at low invariant energy, consistent with earlier HSD studies.
- Hyperons (\(\Lambda + \Sigma^0\)) and cascade baryons (\(\Xi^-\)) are substantially underestimated at low invariant energy, while antihyperons (\(\bar{\Lambda} + \bar{\Sigma}^0\)) and \(\bar{\Xi}^+\) are reasonably described.
- The disagreement between PHSD results and experimental data persists for bombarding energies below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 8\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) studies by Bratkovskaya (2004) that also underestimated strangeness at lower SPS energies, and addresses the long-standing puzzle of strangeness enhancement originally suggested as a quark-gluon plasma signature by Koch and others. The finding that PHSD (which includes a QGP phase via crossover) still fails at low energies redirects the explanation toward chiral symmetry restoration in the hadronic phase, which would modify hadron properties and enhance multistrange baryon production. This enables future experimental programs at FAIR and NICA to test chiral symmetry restoration effects rather than deconfinement as the source of strangeness enhancement.
