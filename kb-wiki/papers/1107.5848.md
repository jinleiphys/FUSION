# Auxiliary Field quantum Monte Carlo for Strongly Paired Fermions

> We demonstrate that the inclusion of a BCS importance function dramatically increases the efficiency of the auxiliary field method for strong pairing. We calculate the ground-state energy of an unpolarized fermi gas at unitarity with up to 66 particles and lattices of up to $27^3$ sites. The method has no fermion sign problem, and an accurate result is obtained for the universal parameter $ξ$. Several different forms of the kinetic energy adjusted to the unitary limit but with different effective ranges extrapolate to the same continuum limit within error bars. The finite effective range results for different interactions are consistent with a linear term proportional to the Fermi momentum times the effective range. The new method described herein will have many applications in superfluid cold atom systems and in both electronic and nuclear structures when pairing is important.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a BCS (Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer) importance function in auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) calculations dramatically increases efficiency for strongly paired fermions, and obtains an exact (not variational upper bound) result for the universal parameter ξ of the unitary Fermi gas, finding ξ = 0.372(5), which is below previous experimental values but compatible with the most recent experiments.

## Method

The authors perform AFQMC calculations on lattices of up to 27³ sites with up to 66 particles, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to break up the two-body interaction. They incorporate a BCS trial wave function as an importance function to guide the random walk, and use three different forms of the kinetic energy (quadratic, quartic with zero effective range, and Hubbard hopping) all tuned to the unitary limit where the scattering length diverges. The ground-state energy is extracted from branching random walks in imaginary time, with time-step errors extrapolated to zero and walker population biases checked to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- Universal parameter ξ = 0.372(5) from extrapolation to the continuum limit, combining results from all three kinetic energy forms
- For N=4 particles, BCS importance functions reduced statistical error by a factor of 10 (100× reduction in computer time) compared to free Fermi gas importance functions
- For N=38 particles on a 12³ lattice, the improvement increased to 1500× in computer time
- Slope S of ξ with respect to k_F r_e (effective range): S = 0.11(3) for the quadratic dispersion with N=66, and S = 0.12(3) for the Hubbard dispersion with N=66
- For the quadratic dispersion with N=66: ξ = 0.374(5)
- For the quartic dispersion (zero effective range) with N=66: ξ = 0.372(3)
- For the Hubbard dispersion with N=66: ξ = 0.375(5)

## Context

This work extends the AFQMC methods developed by Zhang and coworkers (1995, 1997, 2003) by incorporating BCS importance functions, which were previously limited to Hartree-Fock or free Fermi gas types. It provides exact (not variational) results for the unitary Fermi gas, improving upon fixed-node Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations by Carlson (2003), Chang (2004), Astrakharchik (2004), Forbes (2011), and Gandolfi (2011) which gave ξ = 0.383(1) as an upper bound. The result ξ = 0.372(5) is below earlier experimental values of 0.39(2) by Luo (2009) and 0.41(1) by Navon (2010), but compatible with the most recent experiments by Zwierlein (2011). The method can be applied to the entire BCS-BEC crossover and to other strongly correlated fermion systems in cold atoms, condensed matter, quantum chemistry, and nuclear physics.
