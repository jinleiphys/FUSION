# Non-local high-$p_t$ transport in anisotropic QCD matter

> We perform a numerical study of non-local partonic transport in anisotropic QCD matter, relevant to the evolution of hard probes in the aftermath of high-energy nuclear scattering events. The recently derived master equation, obtained from QFT considerations, differs from Boltzmann transport by incorporating a non-local elastic scattering kernel arising from density gradients. After rewriting the master equation in a form suitable for numerical implementation and assuming a static density profile, we compare the non-local evolution to Boltzmann transport, demonstrating that the new interaction kernel is essential for accurately describing the azimuthal structure of the final-state momentum distribution. We further study the non-local partonic transport in the case of a matter profile governed by two-dimensional hydrodynamics, accounting for its flow and generalizing the evolution equation. Our results demonstrate the necessity of going beyond classical transport at high-$p_t$ to accurately capture the structure of jets propagating through structured QCD matter. The master equation used in the numerical simulations can be seamlessly integrated into state-of-the-art transport codes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-local elastic scattering kernel derived from QFT, which goes beyond standard Boltzmann transport, is essential for accurately describing the azimuthal structure of the final-state momentum distribution of hard partons propagating through anisotropic QCD matter, with the caveat that the analysis assumes a static density profile or a profile governed by two-dimensional hydrodynamics and relies on a small-angle scattering (diffusion) approximation.

## Method

The authors start from a recently derived master equation for the Wigner function of a single hard parton, which incorporates a non-local collisional kernel arising from density gradients at second order in a gradient expansion. They rewrite this equation in a localized form suitable for numerical implementation by performing a power expansion in the exchanged momentum (diffusion approximation) and assuming a Gyulassy-Wang model for the interaction potential. The resulting evolution equation is then solved numerically on a discretized momentum and position space lattice for two scenarios: a static inhomogeneous medium with an exponentially decaying density profile, and a flowing medium governed by two-dimensional hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* The gradient correction terms in the diffusion coefficient for a static medium with an isotropic Gaussian profile (A_ab = delta_ab mu_a^2) are given by D_xx = hat q(Y)/4 + (alpha_s^2 pi C_F)/(3 m_D^2(Y)) rho(Y) (4 mu_x^4 Y_x^2 + 2(mu_x^4 Y_x^2 + mu_y^4 Y_y^2) - 3 mu_x^2 - mu_y^2) and D_xy = (4 pi alpha_s^2 C_F)/(3 m_D^2(Y)) rho(Y) mu_x^2 mu_y^2 Y_x Y_y.
* The modifications induced by the new collisional kernel lead to an O(20%-60%) deviation in the pressure ratio P_y/P_x at late times compared to the case where only space-dependent hat q is used.
* The parton energy used in the numerical simulations is E = 1000 (in units of the saturation scale Q_s), and the initial momentum dispersion parameter is b = 1.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Barata et al. (2022), which derived a master equation for single parton evolution in a QCD medium with spatial gradients, going beyond the standard AMY/Boltzmann transport approach used in codes like JEWEL, LBT, and LIDO. The numerical results show that gradient corrections cannot be fully captured by making the jet quenching parameter hat q space-dependent, as is commonly done in phenomenological models. The findings enable future integration of the non-local transport equation into state-of-the-art jet quenching simulations, which is necessary for using jets as tomographic probes of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
