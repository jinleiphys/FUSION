# Giant dipole resonance in highly excited nuclei

> The evolution of the giant dipole resonance's (GDR) width and shape at finite temperature $T$ and angular momentum $J$ is described within the framework of the phonon damping model (PDM). The PDM description is compared with the established experimental systematics obtained from heavy-ion fusion and inelastic scattering of light particles on heavy target nuclei, as well as with predictions by other theoretical approaches. Extended to include the effect of angular momentum $J$, its strength functions have been averaged over the probability distributions of $T$ and $J$ for the heavy-ion fusion-evaporation reaction, which forms the compound nucleus $^{88}$Mo at high $T$ and $J$. The results of theoretical predictions are found in excellent agreement with the experimental data. The predictions by PDM and the heavy-ion fusion data are also employed to predict the viscosity of hot medium and heavy nuclei.

# Giant dipole resonance in highly excited nuclei

## Key claim
The phonon damping model (PDM) successfully describes the evolution of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) width and shape at finite temperature and angular momentum, including the saturation of the width at high temperature, which the author argues is a physical necessity because the damping width of an oscillator must be smaller than its frequency in the classical picture.

## Method
The PDM Hamiltonian couples independent single-particle (quasiparticle) fields with the GDR phonon field using Woods-Saxon potentials at zero temperature. The total GDR width is decomposed into a quantal width from couplings to particle-hole configurations and a thermal width from couplings to particle-particle and hole-hole configurations, with the strength functions averaged over probability distributions of temperature and angular momentum for the heavy-ion fusion-evaporation reaction forming the compound nucleus 88Mo.

## Key numbers
- The GDR width increases sharply with temperature within 1 ≤ T ≤ 2.5–3 MeV and appears to saturate at T ≥ 4 MeV
- A significant increase in the GDR width as a function of angular momentum is seen only at J ≥ 25–27 ħ
- For 88Mo at excitation energy E* ≤ 80 MeV the width increase is strong, but at E* > 80 MeV the increase weakens due to saturation of J_max
- The ratio η/s of shear viscosity to entropy density for medium and heavy nuclei at T = 5 MeV is between (1.3–4.0) KSS units, where the KSS bound is ħ/(4πk_B)
- The Fermi liquid drop model (FLDM) predicts η/s at T = 5 MeV within (3.7–6.5) KSS units, roughly 1.5–2 times larger than the PDM predictions

## Context
This work extends the phonon damping model previously developed by Dang (PDM) to include angular momentum effects, and compares its predictions with experimental systematics from heavy-ion fusion reactions and inelastic scattering of light particles on heavy targets. The results are compared with the thermal shape fluctuation model (TSFM) in its phenomenological and adiabatic versions, as well as the Fermi liquid drop model (FLDM) used by Auerbach and collaborators. The PDM predictions for shear viscosity are found to be comparable to values for quark-gluon plasma-like matter at T > 170 MeV discovered at RHIC and LHC.
