# Effect of Jet Quenching on High-$p_T$ Hadron Spectra in High-energy Nuclear Collisions

> Since large $p_T$ particles in high-energy hadronic or nuclear collisions come from jet fragmentation, jet quenching due to parton energy loss in dense matter will cause the suppression of large $p_T$ hadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Assuming an effective energy loss $dE/dx$ for the high $E_T$ partons, effective jet fragmentation functions are constructed in which leading hadrons will be suppressed. Using such effective fragmentation functions, high $p_T$ hadron spectra and particle suppression factors relative to $pp$ collisions are estimated in central high-energy nuclear collisions with a given range of the assumed $dE/dx$. It is found that the suppression factors are very sensitive to the value of the effective energy loss. Systematic nuclear and flavor dependence of the hadron spectra are also studied.

## Key claim

Jet quenching due to parton energy loss in dense matter will cause suppression of large \(p_T\) hadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but the suppression factors are very sensitive to the assumed value of the effective energy loss \(dE/dx\) and the mean-free-path \(\lambda_q\), making it difficult to extract both parameters simultaneously from measured spectra in a model-independent way.

## Method

The author constructs effective jet fragmentation functions modified by parton energy loss, assuming a Poisson distribution for the number of inelastic scatterings a parton undergoes while traversing a dense medium of transverse size \(R_A\). These modified fragmentation functions are then convolved with perturbative QCD jet cross sections using MRS D\(-\prime\) parton distributions and nuclear parton shadowing from the HIJING model to calculate single-particle \(p_T\) spectra and suppression factors \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) for central \(A+A\) collisions relative to \(pp\) collisions.

## Key numbers

- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with constant \(dE_q/dx=1\) GeV/fm and \(\lambda_q=1\) fm, the suppression factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) at large \(p_T\) is smaller than one and increases with \(p_T\).
- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with constant \(dE_q/dx=2\) GeV/fm and \(\lambda_q=0.5\) fm, the suppression factor is further reduced.
- The low-\(p_T\) ratio \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) approaches \(A^{\alpha_h}/\sigma_{pp}T_{AA}(0)=0.149\) to \(0.253\) for central \(Au+Au\) at RHIC energy, assuming \(\alpha_h=1.0\) to \(1.1\).
- The averaged distance a produced jet travels through a hard-sphere nucleus is \(\langle L\rangle_A=1.09 A^{1/3}\).
- For \(pp\) collisions, the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio decreases with \(p_T\) and saturates at about 0.5 at very high \(p_T\), reflecting the valence \(d\) to \(u\)-quark ratio in a proton.
- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions, the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio increases with \(p_T\) and approaches about 1.14, the valence \(d\) to \(u\)-quark ratio in a gold nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic (1996) for studying jet quenching via tagged direct photons to the simpler case of single inclusive particle spectra, which have much larger production rates at RHIC. It builds on theoretical studies of parton energy loss with Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal coherence effects by Baier et al. (1997) and others, and uses the HIJING model parameterization of nuclear shadowing from Wang and Gyulassy (1991). The results enable experimental extraction of the average parton energy loss from high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression at RHIC and LHC, and suggest that the \(p_T\)-dependence of the suppression factor can distinguish between constant and energy-dependent energy loss scenarios.
