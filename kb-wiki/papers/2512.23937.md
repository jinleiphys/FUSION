# Origin of hadron mass from gravitational D-form factor and neutron star measurements

> Clarifying the origin of hadron mass is one of the fundamental problems in particle physics, relevant from hadronic scales to astrophysical observations. At low energies, this issue is reflected in the decomposition of the hadron mass into chiral-variant and -invariant components. In this letter, we propose a method to extract the chiral invariant mass from the gravitational $D$-form factor under the assumption of the lightest-sigma meson dominance. Focusing on the nucleon, we show that a sizable chiral invariant mass is required to reproduce lattice QCD data, consistent with neutron star constraints.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of lightest-sigma meson dominance, the gravitational D-form factor of a hadron is directly sensitive to the chiral invariant component of that hadron's mass; a sizable chiral invariant mass (around 800 MeV) is required to bring the nucleon D-form factor into agreement with lattice QCD data, and this value is consistent with neutron star constraints from parity-doublet model analyses.

## Method

The authors construct a linear sigma model with only a positive-parity nucleon (where mass arises entirely from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking) and compute the nucleon D-form factor under lightest-sigma meson exchange. They then extend this to a parity-doublet nucleon model that includes a chiral invariant mass term m0, which mixes positive- and negative-parity nucleon fields, and recompute the D-form factor. The results are compared with lattice QCD data for the proton D-form factor at mπ = 170 MeV near the physical point.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass taken as mN = 939 MeV; sigma meson mass range mσ = 400–550 MeV (from Particle Data Group for f0(500))
- In the linear sigma model without chiral invariant mass, the forward-limit D-form factor yields DN(0) = −3.89 to −7.35, which is larger in magnitude than other theoretical estimates (continuum Schwinger method: DN(0) = −3.11(1); dispersion-relation analysis: DN(0) = −3.38+0.34−0.35)
- In the parity-doublet model with chiral invariant mass m0 = 800 MeV, the forward-limit becomes DN(0) = −1.75 to −3.30, in closer agreement with other estimates
- The chiral invariant mass range from neutron star analyses is m0 = 500–900 MeV; the authors test m0 = 500 MeV and m0 = 800 MeV as representative values
- The negative-parity nucleon mass is m− = 1535 MeV (N*(1535))

## Context

This work extends the parity-doublet nucleon model approach of Detar (1988) and Jido (2001) to connect gravitational form factors with the chiral invariant mass, building on recent meson-dominance analyses of GFFs by Broniowski (2024, 2025) and Stegeman (2025). It bridges hadron mass decomposition studies from lattice QCD (Aarts 2015, 2017, 2018) and QCD sum rules (Kim 2020, 2021; Lee 2023) with neutron star equation-of-state constraints from parity-doublet models (Minamikawa 2020, 2023; Gao 2024). The work enables future Electron-Ion Collider measurements of GFFs to independently constrain the chiral invariant mass of nucleons and other hadrons, with implications for both hadron physics and neutron star astrophysics.
