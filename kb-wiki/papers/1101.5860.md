# Effects of running couplings on jet conversion photons

> We calculate photons from jet-plasma interaction considering collisional and radiative energy loss of jet parton. The phase space distribution of the participating jet is dynamically evolved by solving Fokker-Planck equation. We treat the strong coupling constant ($α_s$) as function of momentum and temperature while calculating the drag and diffusion coefficients. It is observed that the quenching factor is substantially modified as compared to the case when $α_s$ is taken as constant. It is shown that the Phenix data is reasonably well reproduced when contributions from all the relevant sources are taken into account.

# Effects of running couplings on jet conversion photons

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a running strong coupling constant (α_s as a function of both momentum and temperature) substantially modifies the quenching factor for jet conversion photons compared to the constant-α_s case, and that the PHENIX data are "reasonably well reproduced" in the low p_T domain (4–14 GeV/c) when contributions from all relevant sources are included, but only when both collisional and radiative energy losses are taken into account.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon production rate from jet-plasma interaction using lowest-order Compton scattering and annihilation processes, with one collision partner treated as a thermal bath and the other (the jet) evolved dynamically via a Fokker-Planck equation. The drag and diffusion coefficients in the Fokker-Planck equation are computed using a running strong coupling α_s(k,T) parametrized to match perturbative ultraviolet behavior and the 3D infrared fixed point of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, with the Debye mass solved self-consistently. Both collisional and radiative energy losses are included in the drag coefficient, and the space-time integrated photon yield is obtained by integrating over the jet phase space distribution and the medium evolution.

## Key numbers

- The depletion in the photon p_T spectra with running coupling is a factor of 2–2.5 more compared to the constant coupling case for RHIC energies.
- The initial temperature used for RHIC calculations is T_i = 446 MeV, with an initial time t_i = 0.147 fm/c.
- The p_T range where the calculation is compared to PHENIX data is 4 < p_T < 14 GeV/c.
- The running coupling parametrization uses parameters a = 9.07, b = 5.90, and λ_s = 0.263 GeV, with additional fit parameters u_2 = 5.47, u_3 = 6.01, v_2 = 10.13, and v_3 = 9.27.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of jet conversion photons (DKS) by incorporating a running coupling constant, which had previously been treated as constant in such calculations. The approach builds on the Fokker-Planck evolution method of Rapp (2009) and uses the running coupling parametrization from Braun and Gies (2007). The results enable more realistic comparisons with PHENIX photon data and suggest that collisional energy loss plays an important role in the 4–14 GeV/c p_T range, while also showing that including radiative energy loss improves the description of the data.
