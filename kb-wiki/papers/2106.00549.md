# Z3 gauge theory coupled to fermions and quantum computing

> We study the Z3 gauge theory with fermions on the quantum computer using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) algorithm with IBM QISKit software. Using up to 9 qubits we are able to obtain accurate results for the ground state energy. Introducing nonzero chemical potential we are able to determine the Equation of State (EOS) for finite density on the quantum computer. We discuss possible realizations of quantum advantage for this system over classical computers with regards to finite density simulations and the fermion sign problem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) algorithm, implemented on IBM QISKit with up to 9 qubits, can produce ground state energies for the Z3 gauge theory coupled to fermions that are close to exact diagonalization results, but the accuracy degrades as the system size increases (from 4 to 8 qubits), and the method provides an upper bound rather than the exact value.

## Method

The authors map the Z3 gauge theory Hamiltonian (with staggered fermions, gauge links, and electric field operators) onto qubit operators using tensor products of Pauli matrices and identity matrices. They apply the hybrid classical-quantum VQE algorithm with an Ry Rz variational form and fully entangled circuit of depth 3, using the Sequential Least Squares Programming (SLSQP) optimizer on a statevector simulator in IBM QISKit. They study systems with one, two, and three Z3 boson links coupled to two or three fermion vertices, and also introduce a nonzero chemical potential to compute the equation of state at finite density.

## Key numbers

- For one Z3 boson link and two fermion vertices (4 qubits, 20 Pauli terms, gauge coupling 0.15): exact ground state energy = -0.30901699, VQE result = -0.30900573
- For two Z3 boson links and three fermion vertices (7 qubits, 196 Pauli terms, gauge coupling 0.15): exact ground state energy = -0.69930137, VQE result = -0.68434208
- For three Z3 boson links and three fermion vertices (8 qubits, 547 Pauli terms, gauge coupling 0.15, mass 0): exact ground state energy = -0.86375772, VQE result = -0.83185930
- The equation of state for the 8-qubit system (gauge coupling 0.15, mass 1.0) shows energy rising linearly with chemical potential, with VQE results consistently above exact values

## Context

This work extends the study of discrete gauge theories on quantum computers, following earlier work on Z3 gauge models by Banuls (2016) and others, and builds on staggered fermion formulations used in studies of the Schwinger model by Banks (1975) and Klco (2018). The paper identifies the Z3 gauge theory coupled to fermions as a candidate system for demonstrating quantum advantage over classical computers, particularly for finite-density simulations where the fermion sign problem arises, and suggests future work on finite-temperature equation of state using thermo-double approaches and extension to higher-dimensional QCD using variational methods.
