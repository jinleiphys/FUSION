# Domain Wall in the Linear Sigma Model

> We discuss the role of the axial $U(1)_A$ symmetry in the chiral phase transition using the $U(N_f)_R\times U(N_f)_L$ linear sigma model with two massless quark flavors. We expect that above a certain temperature the axial $U(1)_A$ symmetry will be effectively restored as well as $SU(N_f)_R\times SU(N_f)_L$. Then we can construct a string-like static solution the $η$ string and a kink-like classical solution the domain wall during the chiral phase transition. We give out the possible signals for detecting the domain wall in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Domain Wall in the Linear Sigma Model

## Key claim

If the axial U(1)_A symmetry is effectively restored at a temperature near the chiral phase transition, a domain wall (rather than an eta string) is expected to be produced as a topologically stable kink solution in the U(2)_R x U(2)_L linear sigma model with two massless quark flavors, and this domain wall may be detectable via its decay into sigma mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The authors emphasize that this scenario depends on the U(1)_A symmetry being restored at a temperature comparable to the SU(2)_R x SU(2)_L restoration temperature, which remains an open question given conflicting lattice and instanton liquid model results.

## Method

The authors use the U(N_f)_R x U(N_f)_L linear sigma model with N_f=2 massless quark flavors, truncating the field content to only the sigma and eta mesons (the fields specified by the U(1)_A phase). They construct static kink solutions of the classical equations of motion for the case with a nonzero determinant term (c ≠ 0) that breaks U(1)_A explicitly, and analyze the topological stability and dynamical stability of the resulting domain wall. They then estimate the observable consequences for pion interferometry in heavy-ion collisions by computing the suppression factor beta in the effective intercept parameter of the Bose-Einstein correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Domain wall thickness: delta ~ 0.7 fm, derived from delta ~ (m sqrt(alpha-1))^{-1} with m = 263.83 MeV and alpha = c/m^2 where c = (386.79 MeV)^2
- Domain wall mass per unit area: omega ~ (129.273 MeV)^3, from omega = (2 sqrt(2) m^3)/(3 lambda) (alpha-1)^{3/2}
- Model parameters: m^2 = (263.83 MeV)^2, c = (386.79 MeV)^2, lambda_1 = -31.51, lambda_2 = 82.77, corresponding to m_sigma = 400 MeV and m_eta = 547 MeV
- For RHIC Pb-Pb collisions with QGP radius R ~ 6 fm: domain wall bubble energy E_domainwall ~ 25 GeV, leading to approximately 40 pi+ (or pi-) mesons from the bubble wall and a suppression factor beta ~ 0.85
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions with QGP radius R ~ 10 fm: suppression factor beta ~ 0.7-0.8
- Total pion multiplicity at RHIC: approximately 1500 pions per event

## Context

This work extends the study of topological defects in QCD phase transitions, building on earlier work on eta strings by Zhang, Huang and Brandenberger (1998) and on non-abelian strings by Balachandran and Digal (2002). The paper addresses the ongoing debate about whether U(1)_A symmetry is restored simultaneously with chiral symmetry at T_c, citing lattice results by Alles, D'Elia and Di Giacomo (2000) suggesting simultaneous restoration, and contrasting results from the instanton liquid model by Schafer (1996) and lattice calculations by Alles, D'Elia and Di Giacomo (1997) showing topological susceptibility persists up to ~1.2 T_c. The proposed detection method using HBT interferometry follows the approach of Shuryak and Zhitnitsky (2002) for long-lived bubble walls and the core-halo model of Vance et al. (1998). The work enables experimental searches for U(1)_A restoration signals via enhanced sigma meson production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
