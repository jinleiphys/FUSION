# Few- and many-nucleon systems with semilocal coordinate-space regularized chiral nucleon-nucleon forces

> We employ a variety of ab initio methods including Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, No-Core Configuration Interaction Approach, Coupled-Cluster Theory and In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group to perform a comprehensive analysis of the nucleon-deuteron elastic and breakup reactions and selected properties of light and medium-mass nuclei up to 48Ca using the recently constructed semilocal coordinate-space regularized chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials. We compare the results with those based on selected phenomenological and chiral EFT two-nucleon potentials, discuss the convergence pattern of the chiral expansion and estimate the achievable theoretical accuracy at various chiral orders using the novel approach to quantify truncation errors of the chiral expansion without relying on cutoff variation. We also address the robustness of this method and explore alternative ways to estimate the theoretical uncertainty from the truncation of the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the newly constructed semilocal coordinate-space regularized (SCS) chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials yield improved convergence with chiral order compared to older nonlocal potentials, but the calculations of three- and more-nucleon observables are incomplete starting from N2LO due to missing three-nucleon forces, so the theoretical uncertainty estimates shown assume that the corresponding three-nucleon force contributions will be included.

## Method

The authors employed multiple ab initio methods including Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for A=3 and A=4 systems, No-Core Configuration Interaction (NCCI) approach, Coupled-Cluster (CC) theory, and In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IM-SRG) to analyze nucleon-deuteron elastic and breakup scattering and properties of nuclei up to 48Ca. The SCS chiral potentials with coordinate-space cutoff parameter R=0.9 fm were used, and theoretical uncertainties from truncation of the chiral expansion were estimated using a modified algorithm based on the known chiral expansion of observables rather than cutoff variation.

## Key numbers

- The SCS chiral potentials are available for R = 0.8, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, and 1.2 fm
- For the breakdown scale Lambda_b, values of 600 MeV for R=0.8, 0.9, 1.0 fm, 500 MeV for R=1.1 fm, and 400 MeV for R=1.2 fm were adopted
- Faddeev calculations included NN force components with total two-nucleon angular momenta j <= 5 in 3N partial-wave states with total angular momentum J <= 25/2, sufficient for convergence up to neutron laboratory energies E_lab,n <= 200 MeV
- The nd elastic scattering analyzing power A_y at low energies shows predictions using standard NN potentials (AV18, CDBonn, Nijm1, Nijm2) underestimate experimental data by approximately 30% at the A_y maximum near Theta_c.m. ~ 125 degrees
- For the nn quasi-free scattering (QFS) configuration, theory underestimates data by approximately 20%
- The SCS chiral potentials show that NLO results for the 3P2 phase shift are already very close to Nijmegen partial wave analysis values at energies below approximately 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Binder et al. (2015) that analyzed elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and selected observables in 3H, 4He, and 6Li using the same SCS chiral potentials. The paper presents the first applications of SCS chiral NN potentials to light- and medium-mass nuclei beyond 6Li, and addresses limitations of the uncertainty quantification approach. The results enable future studies of chiral three-nucleon forces, which are needed to resolve discrepancies such as the A_y puzzle in elastic nd scattering and the underestimation of nn QFS cross sections.
