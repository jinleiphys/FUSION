# Systematic reduction of the proton-removal cross section in neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei

> Single neutron- and proton-removal cross sections have been systematically measured for 72 medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei around Z=50 and energies around 900A MeV using the FRagment Separator (FRS) at GSI. Neutron-removal cross sections are described by considering the knock-out process together with initial- and final-state interactions. Proton-removal cross sections are, however, significantly smaller than predicted by the same calculations. The observed difference can be explained as due to the knockout of short-correlated protons in neutron-proton dominating pairs.

## Key claim
The observed systematic reduction of single-proton removal cross sections relative to model predictions for neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei around Z=50 can be explained by the knockout of short-range correlated (SRC) protons in neutron-proton dominating pairs, which depopulates the 1p0n channel in favor of 1p1n channels.

## Method
The experiment used two beams (950A MeV 238U fission and 1200A MeV 132Xe fragmentation) impinging on a beryllium target at GSI to produce neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei. The FRagment Separator (FRS) was operated as two independent magnetic spectrometers: the first section separated the incoming cocktail beam, and a second beryllium target at the intermediate image plane induced nucleon-removal reactions, with residues identified via Bρ-ToF-ΔE measurements. Model calculations used an intra-nuclear cascade (INC) model with realistic radial nucleon profiles and shell-model spectroscopic factors, then were modified to include 20% SRC neutron-proton pairs.

## Key numbers
- 72 medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei around Z=50 were measured for single-neutron removal cross sections
- 13 isotopes of tellurium, antimony, tin, and indium were measured for single-proton removal cross sections
- Energies around 900A MeV for the secondary reactions
- Resolution achieved: ΔZ/Z ≈ 2.6×10⁻³ and ΔA/A ≈ 1.2×10⁻³ in the first FRS section; ΔZ/Z ≈ 3.0×10⁻³ and ΔA/A ≈ 7.8×10⁻⁴ in the second section
- Single-proton removal cross sections are about an order of magnitude smaller than single-neutron removal cross sections
- In 132Sn, 13 protons and 13 neutrons belong to SRC pairs, representing 26% of the protons and 16% of the neutrons

## Context
This work extends systematic nucleon-removal measurements from lighter nuclei (Gade 2008) and from stable medium-mass and heavy nuclei (Rodriguez-Sanchez 2017 for 112,124Sn; Benlliure 2008 for 136Xe; Rejmund 2001 for 197Au; Audouin 2006 for 208Pb; Taieb 2003 for 238U) to long isotopic chains of unstable neutron-rich nuclei around Z=50. The results confirm that the reduction in proton-removal cross sections is a general feature across nuclear masses, and the explanation via SRC neutron-proton pairs (Subedi 2008; Duer 2018) resolves the previously puzzling dependence of spectroscopic strength quenching on neutron excess. This work enables future studies of short-range correlations in neutron-rich systems and their impact on single-nucleon knockout reactions.
