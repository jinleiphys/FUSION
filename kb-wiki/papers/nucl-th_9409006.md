# Perturbative versus Lattice QCD Energy Density Correlators at High Temperatures

> Correlators of magnetic and electric field energy density are investigated for SU($N_c$) gauge theory at high temperatures $T$. At separations $z\leq 2/T$ the correlators are shown to be dominated by a power--law behavior even for finite gluon screening masses. This continuum behavior is well approximated on current $4\times 16^3$--lattices in the perturbative limit and leads to a considerable overestimate of screening masses deduced from fitting the lattice correlators with conventional exponential forms. The use of extended sources and sinks to enhance the signal improves the situation for screening masses $m\gg T$ but leads to a largely uncontrolled error for masses less than $T$. In fact, we show that recent lattice QCD data of Grossmann et al., from which a magnetic screening mass $m_M \approx 2.9 \, T$ was deduced, may even be consistent with a vanishing actual magnetic screening mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the magnetic screening mass \(m_M \approx 2.9\,T\) reported by Grossmann et al. from lattice QCD plaquette correlators may be an artifact: the data may actually be consistent with a vanishing magnetic screening mass once the power-law behavior of the correlators at distances \(z \leq 2/T\) and the effect of fuzzed (smeared) sources are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors compute spatial correlators of magnetic and electric field energy density in SU(\(N_c\)) gauge theory at high temperature using a one-loop, two-gluon exchange diagram in the perturbative limit, assuming the gluon propagator acquires a constant self-energy (screening mass). They then transcribe these continuum results onto a \(4 \times 16^3\) lattice and introduce a model for fuzzed links to mimic the smearing used in the lattice study of Grossmann et al., in order to assess how the extracted screening masses are affected by the power-law enhancement at small separations.

## Key numbers

- For \(zT \geq 1/2\), the free magnetic correlator decays as \((zT)^{-4}\) (polynomial), not exponentially; the zero-Matsubara-mode contribution gives a constant \({\cal C}_2(0)=6\) in the massless case.
- At \(T \approx 1.5\,T_c\), Grossmann et al. reported \(m_M/T \approx 2.9 \pm 0.2\) and \(m_E/T \approx 1.4 \pm 0.2\).
- The power-law behavior dominates for separations \(z \leq 2/T\) even when a finite gluon screening mass is present.
- For screening masses \(m \gg T\), fuzzed sources reduce the error in mass extraction, but for masses less than \(T\) the error becomes "largely uncontrolled."

## Context

This work extends the perturbative analysis of finite-temperature gauge theory (Weldon, 1982; Pisarski, 1989) to energy density correlators and directly challenges the lattice QCD results of Grossmann et al. (1994), who interpreted plaquette correlators as measuring twice the electric and magnetic screening masses. The paper shows that the power-law tail of the correlator, combined with the smearing from fuzzed links, can mimic an exponential decay and produce an apparent finite mass even when the true magnetic screening mass is zero. This calls into question the interpretation of screening masses from energy density correlators and suggests that independent measurements (e.g., of the gluon propagator) are needed for confirmation.
