# Fission fragment mass distributions in reactions populating 200Pb

> The fission fragment mass distributions have been measured in the reactions 16O + 184W and 19F+ 181Ta populating the same compound nucleus 200Pb? at similar excitation energies. It is found that the widths of the mass distribution increases monotonically with excitation energy, indicating the absence of quasi-fission for both reactions. This is contrary to two recent claims of the presence of quasi-fission in the above mentioned reactions.

## Key claim

The fission fragment mass distributions for the reactions 16O + 184W and 19F + 181Ta, both populating the compound nucleus 200Pb*, show a monotonic increase in width with excitation energy and no significant difference between the two entrance channels, indicating the absence of quasi-fission in either reaction, contrary to two recent claims of quasi-fission presence.

## Method

Fission fragment mass distributions were measured using two large-area position-sensitive multi-wire proportional counters (MWPCs) at the 15UD Pelletron accelerator facility. The reactions used pulsed beams of 19F and 16O on enriched 181Ta (200 microgram/cm2 with carbon backing) and self-supporting 184W (100 microgram/cm2) targets, respectively. Masses of correlated fission events were extracted from time-of-flight differences, X-Y coordinates, and energy loss measurements, with a mass resolution of approximately 4 u. The standard deviation of the mass distributions was compared to predictions from statistical theory and two-dimensional Langevin calculations using elongation and mass-asymmetry as collective coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Mass resolution achieved: approximately 4 u.
- The inverse stiffness parameter 1/k = (98.1 ± 15.1) u^2/MeV was used to fit the data, consistent with a comprehensive compilation.
- The constant beta in the angular momentum dependence of the mass variance was approximately 0.05.
- The Coulomb barriers for the reactions: 77.8 MeV for 19F + 181Ta and 71 MeV for 16O + 184W.
- The ratio of experimental to theoretically calculated standard deviation sigma_m was close to 1.0 for all measured energies, indicating absence of quasi-fission.

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion dynamics in the 200Pb compound nucleus, following earlier measurements of evaporation residue cross sections and gamma multiplicity distributions by Shidling et al. (2010, 2011), which suggested entrance channel effects. It directly contradicts the theoretical claims of Nasirov et al. (2012) that quasi-fission and fast fission were significant in the 19F + 181Ta reaction, and also contrasts with the dynamical cluster-decay model calculations by Rajni (2015) that suggested possible quasi-fission in the 16O + 184W reaction. The results enable future theoretical work to distinguish between quasi-fission, fast fission, and pre-equilibrium fission in pre-actinide nuclei.
