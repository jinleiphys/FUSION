# Generalized Gluon Distribution for Quarkonium Dynamics in Strongly Coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Yang-Mills Theory

> We study the generalized gluon distribution that governs the dynamics of quarkonium inside a non-Abelian thermal plasma characterizing its dissociation and recombination rates. This gluon distribution can be written in terms of a correlation function of two chromoelectric fields connected by an adjoint Wilson line. We formulate and calculate this object in $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling using the AdS/CFT correspondence, allowing for a nonzero center-of-mass velocity $v$ of the heavy quark pair relative to the medium. The effect of a moving medium on the dynamics of the heavy quark pair is described by the simple substitution $T \to \sqrtγ \, T$ in agreement with previous calculations of other observables at strong coupling, where $T$ is the temperature of the plasma in its rest frame, and $γ= (1 - v^2)^{-1/2}$ is the Lorentz boost factor. Such a velocity dependence can be important when the quarkonium momentum is larger than its mass. Contrary to general expectations for open quantum systems weakly coupled with large thermal environments, the contributions to the transition rates that are usually thought of as the leading ones in Markovian descriptions vanish in this strongly coupled plasma. This calls for new theoretical developments to assess the effects of strongly coupled non-Abelian plasmas on in-medium quarkonium dynamics. Finally, we compare our results with those from weakly coupled QCD, and find that the QCD result moves toward the $\mathcal{N}=4$ strongly coupled result as the coupling constant is increased within the regime of applicability of perturbation theory. This behavior makes it even more pressing to develop a non-Markovian description of quarkonium in-medium dynamics.

## Key claim

The generalized gluon distribution (GGD) that governs quarkonium dissociation and recombination in a strongly coupled plasma vanishes at negative frequencies, meaning the contributions usually treated as the leading ones in Markovian descriptions are absent; this calls for a non-Markovian treatment of quarkonium in-medium dynamics.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the chromoelectric field correlator defining the GGD in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory. They express the correlator in terms of variations of a fundamental Wilson loop, then evaluate the Wilson loop expectation value holographically via the Nambu-Goto action for a string worldsheet in an AdS5-Schwarzschild spacetime, including the case of a boosted medium. The result is obtained by solving a differential equation for the regular solution F-Ω in the bulk.

## Key numbers

- The moving medium effect on the GGD is described by the substitution T → √γ T, where γ = (1 - v²)^{-1/2} is the Lorentz boost factor and T is the plasma temperature in its rest frame.
- The GGD in strongly coupled N=4 SYM is given by [g_adj^T]_{ij}(ω) = √λ T_F δ_{ij} (π T √γ)³/(12π) × (-i/F⁻_{|Ω|}(0) ∂³F⁻_{|Ω|}/∂ξ³(0)), where Ω = ω/(π T √γ) and λ = g² N_c is the 't Hooft coupling.
- The spectral function for quarkonium ρ_adj^{++}(ω) = (1 - e^{-ω/T}) [g_adj^{++}]^>(ω) vanishes at negative frequencies in the strongly coupled limit.
- At weak coupling, increasing the coupling constant within the regime of applicability of perturbation theory moves the QCD result toward the N=4 strongly coupled result.

## Context

This work extends the framework of Nijs (2023) to a Q\bar{Q} pair moving in a thermal plasma, and generalizes earlier perturbative studies of the GGD by Binder (2021). The velocity dependence T → √γ T agrees with previous AdS/CFT calculations of the heavy quark potential by Liu (2006) and the heavy quark diffusion coefficient by Gubser (2006) and Casalderrey-Solana (2007). The finding that Markovian descriptions break down at strong coupling motivates developing non-Markovian transport formalisms for quarkonium dynamics, which would enable phenomenological studies using GGDs at different couplings to compare with experimental data from heavy ion collisions.
