# A relativistic treatment of pion wave functions in the annihilation antiproton-proton -> pi^-pi^+

> Quark model intrinsic wave functions of highly energetic pions in the reaction \bar pp->π^-π^+ are subjected to a relativistic treatment. The annihilation is described in a constituent quark model with A2 and R2 flavor-flux topology and the annihilated quark-antiquark pairs are in 3P_0 and 3S_1 states. We study the effects of pure Lorentz transformations on the antiquark and quark spatial wave functions and their respective spinors in the pion. The modified quark geometry of the pion has considerable impact on the angular dependence of the annihilation mechanisms.

## Key claim

Lorentz-contracting the intrinsic Gaussian wave functions of the final-state pions in the reaction \(\bar p p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+\) introduces new angle-dependent terms in the transition operators that enhance contributions from higher partial waves (\(J \geq 2\)), which is necessary to reproduce the double-dip structure of the analyzing power \(A_{0n}\) observed in LEAR experiments; the effect is energy-dependent via the boost factor \(\gamma = E_{\mathrm{cm}}/2m_\pi\) and is most pronounced for large \(\gamma\) (e.g., \(\gamma \approx 7\) at a beam momentum of 800 MeV/\(c\)).

## Method

The authors describe the annihilation in a constituent quark model using two flavor-flux topologies: rearrangement (\(R2\)) and annihilation (\(A2\)) diagrams, with the annihilated quark-antiquark pairs in either a \(^3P_0\) (vacuum) or \(^3S_1\) (gluon) state. They Lorentz-transform the spatial part of the pion intrinsic wave function from the pion rest frame to the center-of-mass frame, turning the spherical Gaussian into an ellipsoid contracted along the boost direction, and also consider the effect of Wick helicity rotations on the quark and antiquark spinors. The transition operators are then evaluated in \(r\)-space, and the coefficients of the resulting spin-momentum terms are expressed analytically as functions of the size parameters \(\alpha\) (for the nucleon) and \(\beta\) (for the pion) and the boost factor \(\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* At an antiproton beam momentum \(p_{\mathrm{lab}} = 800\) MeV/\(c\), the total center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 2020\) MeV, giving a relativistic boost factor \(\gamma = E_{\mathrm{cm}}/2m_\pi = 7.2\).
* For the \(R2\) diagram with \(\gamma \simeq 7\), the exponential coefficient \(D\) is about fifteen times larger than the coefficient \(B\), strongly suppressing the overlap when the relative \(\pi\pi\) coordinate \(\mathbf{R}'\) is parallel or antiparallel to the relative \(\bar p p\) coordinate \(\mathbf{R}\).
* In the non-relativistic limit \(\gamma \rightarrow 1\), the new coefficients \(D\), \(C_V\), \(C_L\), and \(C_T\) vanish, recovering the results of Kloet et al. (2002).
* The \(A2\) amplitudes contain a delta function \(\delta(3\mathbf{R}/2 - \mathbf{R}')\) and act in partial waves \(J^\pi = 0^+, 1^-, 2^+, 3^-, 4^+, 5^-, \dots\).

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model calculations of Kloet et al. (2002) and El-Bennich et al. (2003) by explicitly including Lorentz contraction of the final-state pions, which was previously neglected. It builds on the observation by Maruyama et al. (1994) that Lorentz contraction enhances two-pion production in \(\bar p p\) annihilation at rest, but goes beyond that work by analyzing the angular dependence at finite energies and by treating both the Gaussian and spinor parts of the wave function. The results enable a more accurate reproduction of the LEAR differential cross section and analyzing power data, particularly the double-dip structure of \(A_{0n}\), and motivate a subsequent numerical partial-wave analysis (El-Bennich et al., 2004).
