# Polarization Phenomena in Hadronic and Nuclear Processes in Threshold Regime

> The threshold region for processes of hadronic and nuclear interactions is very interesting for a theoretical as well as an experimental point of view. In this region one can apply different physical methods, starting from classical current algebra methods - for processes invloving soft pions, through effective Lagrangian approaches - or perturbative chiral symmetry theory (ChPT). The essential simplification of the spin structure of matrix elements for threshold regime results in better understanding of the underlying mechanisms and allows the analysis of polarization phenomena. A dedicated formalism is developed here for a wide class of processes including even such complicated processes as production of pseudoscalar and vector mesons in nucleon-nucleon collisions. Many interesting physical problems are discussed: hidden strangeness of nucleons and OZI-violation, $ηN$ -and $ωN$ interactions in S-state, the determination of P-parity of strange particles. Special attention is devoted to the analysis of the spin structure and polarization phenomena for nuclear processes with light nuclei which have important applications in fundamental astrophysics and in nuclear fusion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the threshold regime for hadronic and nuclear processes allows a dedicated formalism for polarization phenomena that is not simply a limiting case of the general binary-collision formalism, because the presence of a single independent physical direction (the initial momentum) creates axial symmetry and eliminates the scattering plane, leading to rigorous simplifications and selection rules for polarization observables.

## Method

The authors develop a nonrelativistic spin formalism for threshold processes by reducing relativistic descriptions of spin-1/2, spin-1, and spin-3/2 particles to two-component spinors and three-vectors, using only symmetry principles (conservation of total angular momentum, P-invariance, gauge invariance, Pauli principle, isotopic invariance, and C-invariance). They construct general matrix elements for specific processes such as p+d→³He+π⁰, π+N→N+V, and γ+N→N+V by enumerating allowed partial-wave and multipole transitions at threshold, then derive the resulting polarization observables and their properties.

## Key numbers

- For the process p+d→³He+π⁰ at threshold, two partial amplitudes f₁ and f₂ describe the matrix element, with linear combinations giving the amplitudes for initial total spin S_i=1/2 and S_i=3/2: f₁ = f_{1/2} - 2f_{3/2}, f₂ = f_{1/2} + 2f_{3/2}.
- For γ+N→N+V at threshold, three multipole amplitudes contribute: E1→J^P=1/2⁻, E1→J^P=3/2⁻, and M2→J^P=3/2⁻, with relations 3e₁ = 2f₂ - f₁, 3e₃ = f₁ + f₂, m₃ = f₃.
- The tensor polarization of deuterons produced in unpolarized collisions is characterized by a single real quantity Q: Q_{ab} = (k̂_a k̂_b - (1/3)δ_{ab})Q.
- For vector meson decay V→P+P, the angular distribution is W(θ) ≃ 1 + a cos²θ, with a = -3 + 1/ρ, where ρ is a real dynamical parameter.
- The cross section for polarized nucleon-nucleon collisions at threshold depends on three spin-correlation coefficients: σ(P₁,P₂) = σ₀(1 + A₁P₁·P₂ + A₂k̂·P₁ k̂·P₂), with relations to singlet and triplet cross sections: σ₀A₁ = (-σ_s + σ_{t,0})/4, σ₀A₂ = (-2σ_{t,0} + 2σ_{t,1})/4, σ₀ = (σ_s + σ_{t,0} + 2σ_{t,1})/4.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of polarization phenomena in binary reactions, as developed by Ohlsen (1974) for general kinematics, to the threshold regime where axial symmetry replaces the scattering plane. The paper applies the formalism to a wide class of processes including meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, photoproduction, and nuclear reactions with light nuclei, with applications to hidden strangeness of nucleons, OZI-violation, ηN and ωN interactions in S-state, and determination of P-parity of strange particles. The results enable complete experiments for threshold processes by specifying which polarization measurements are needed (three different polarization measurements plus differential cross section for γ+N→N+V) and which observables vanish identically (all T-odd one-spin polarization observables).
