# Longitudinal and transverse structure functions of proton and deuteron at large x

> Higher-twist effects in the low-order moments of the longitudinal and transverse structure functions of proton and deuteron have been analyzed using available phenomenological fits of existing data in the Q**2 range between 1 and 20 (GeV/c)**2. Both twist-4 and twist-6 contributions have been determined adopting the Natchmann definition of moments, which allows to disentangle properly target-mass effects. The extraction of the matrix elements of the relevant twist-4 operators, describing quark-quark and quark-gluon correlations, is carried out in case of the second moment. The need of transverse data with better quality for x > 0.5 and Q**2 < 10 (GeV/c)**2 as well as more precise and systematic determinations of the L/T separation make JLab @ 12 GeV a good place to improve our understanding of the non-perturbative structure of hadrons.

## Key claim

Higher-twist (twist-4 and twist-6) contributions to the low-order moments of the longitudinal and transverse structure functions of the proton and deuteron can be extracted from existing phenomenological fits in the Q² range 1–20 (GeV/c)², but the analysis is limited by the poor quality of transverse data for x > 0.5 and Q² < 10 (GeV/c)² and by large uncertainties in the longitudinal channel.

## Method

The authors used the Nachtmann definition of moments to remove target-mass corrections, then performed a twist expansion including twist-2, twist-4, and twist-6 terms to fit the Q² dependence of the experimental moments M_n^T(Q²) and M_n^L(Q²) for both proton and deuteron targets. For the transverse moments, the leading-twist contribution was determined simultaneously with the higher-twist parameters from the fit; for the longitudinal moments, parton densities from GRV (1995) were adopted for the leading-twist term. The analysis used a renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV and applied non-singlet evolution for moments with n ≥ 4.

## Key numbers

- The twist-4 mass scale extracted from the transverse moments is μ^(4) ≈ 1 GeV, significantly higher than the naive expectation of √⟨k²_⊥⟩ ≈ 0.3 GeV and higher than results from other twist-4 analyses such as Jaffe and U (1995).
- The twist-6 mass scale from the transverse moments is μ^(6) ≈ 0.6 GeV.
- For the non-singlet twist-4 operators contributing to the second moment, the extracted matrix elements are: A^NS = -9.0 ± 4.5 and B^NS = -2.0 ± 0.4 for quark-gluon correlation operators, and C^NS = 5.2 ± 2.8 for the quark-quark correlation operator.
- For the singlet case, the constraint A^S + 6 B^S = 12.5 ± 1.8 was obtained, along with 8 C^S + 5 A^S - 2 B^S = 0.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist analysis approach of Jaffe and U (1995) by applying it to both transverse and longitudinal structure functions of the proton and deuteron, using the Nachtmann moment definition to properly handle target-mass corrections. The results indicate that twist-4 and twist-6 contributions are significant for moments of order n ≥ 4 in the transverse channel, while the longitudinal second moment is dominated by higher-twist effects up to Q² of several (GeV/c)². The analysis enables future extractions of multiparton correlation matrix elements from Jefferson Lab data at 12 GeV, where better quality transverse data for x > 0.5 and Q² < 10 (GeV/c)² and more precise longitudinal/transverse separations are expected.
