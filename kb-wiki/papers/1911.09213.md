# Masses of Light and Heavy Mesons and Baryons: A Unified Picture

> We compute masses of positive parity spin-$1/2$ and $3/2$ baryons composed of $u$, $d$, $s$, $c$ and $b$ quarks in a quark-diaquark picture. The mathematical foundation for this analysis is implemented through a symmetry-preserving Schwinger-Dyson equations treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction, which preserves key features of quantum chromodynamics, such as confinement, chiral symmetry breaking and low energy Goldberger-Treiman relations. This study requires a computation of diquark correlations containing these quarks which in turn are readily inferred from solving the Bethe-Salpeter equations of the corresponding mesons. Therefore, it serves as a unified formalism for a multitude of mesons and baryons. It builds on our previous works on the study of masses, decay constants and form factors of quarkonia and light mesons, employing the same model. We use two sets of parameters, one which remains exactly the same for both the light and heavy sector hadrons, and another where the coupling strength is allowed to evolve according to the available mass scales of quarks. Our results are in very good agreement with the existing experimental data as well as predictions of other theoretical approaches whenever comparison is possible.

## Key claim
The paper computes masses of positive-parity spin-1/2 and 3/2 baryons containing u, d, s, c, and b quarks using a quark-diquark picture within a symmetry-preserving contact interaction model, but the accuracy of the results depends on using two parameter sets: one fixed for all quarks (CI-LP) and one where the coupling strength evolves with quark mass scale (CI-HP), with the latter yielding closer agreement to experiment for systems with disparate quark masses.

## Method
The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons and diquarks using a vector-vector contact interaction (CI) model, where the gluon propagator is taken as momentum-independent with a dynamically generated gluon mass scale of 500 MeV. Diquark correlations (scalar 0+ and axial-vector 1+) are inferred from the corresponding meson solutions, and baryon masses are obtained by solving the Faddeev equation in a quark-diquark picture. Two parameter sets are employed: CI-LP with fixed coupling and ultraviolet cutoff for all quarks, and CI-HP where the dimensionless coupling follows an inverse logarithmic running with the ultraviolet regulator.

## Context
This work extends the contact interaction model previously applied to light mesons (Gutierrez-Guerrero, 2010; Roberts, 2010, 2011; Chen, 2012) and heavy-light mesons (Bedolla, 2015; Serna, 2017; Raya, 2017; Yin, 2019) to a unified treatment of mesons and baryons containing up to three heavy quarks. It builds on earlier Faddeev equation calculations for light and strange baryons (Chen, 2012; Lu, 2017) and provides a continuum QCD alternative to lattice QCD studies of heavy baryons (Brown, 2014; Padmanath, 2015; Mathur, 2018). The results enable systematic predictions for doubly and triply heavy baryon masses, including states like the Ξ_cc⁺⁺ observed by LHCb at 3621 MeV (Aaij, 2017), and provide a framework for computing decay constants, form factors, and charge radii of heavy hadrons.
