# Properties of nuclei up to $A=16$ using local chiral interactions

> We report accurate quantum Monte Carlo calculations of nuclei up to $A=16$ based on local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order. We examine the theoretical uncertainties associated with the chiral expansion and the cutoff in the theory, as well as the associated operator choices in the three-nucleon interactions. While in light nuclei the cutoff variation and systematic uncertainties are rather small, in $^{16}$O these can be significant for large coordinate-space cutoffs. Overall, we show that chiral interactions constructed to reproduce properties of very light systems and nucleon-nucleon scattering give an excellent description of binding energies, charge radii, and form factors for all these nuclei, including open-shell systems in $A=6$ and 12.

## Key claim

Local chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order, fitted only to nucleon-nucleon scattering and few-body data, can give an excellent description of binding energies, charge radii, and form factors for nuclei up to A=16, but only for the harder cutoff (R0=1.0 fm); the softer cutoff (R0=1.2 fm) produces significant overbinding and compactness in oxygen-16 that is not captured by the abstract's general claim of good agreement.

## Method

The authors used auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations with local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). Three-body interactions containing cubic spin-isospin operators that cannot be propagated exactly in AFDMC were treated by constructing an approximate Hamiltonian H' that can be fully propagated, then computing the difference ⟨H'-H⟩ perturbatively. Calculations were performed for two coordinate-space cutoffs (R0=1.0 fm and R0=1.2 fm) and two alternative forms of the three-body contact term (Eτ and E𝟙).

## Key numbers

- Oxygen-16 ground-state energy at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): -117(5)(16) MeV, compared to experimental -127.6 MeV
- Oxygen-16 ground-state energy at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.2 fm): -263(26)(56) MeV, severely overbound relative to experiment
- Oxygen-16 charge radius at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): 2.71(5)(13) fm, compared to experimental 2.730(25) fm
- Oxygen-16 charge radius at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.2 fm): 2.17(5)(11) fm, significantly too compact
- Carbon-12 ground-state energy at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): -78(3)(9) MeV, compared to experimental -92.2 MeV
- Carbon-12 charge radius at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): 2.48(4)(18) fm, compared to experimental 2.471(6) fm
- Helium-6 ground-state energy at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): -28.4(4)(2.0) MeV, compared to experimental -29.3 MeV
- Lithium-6 charge radius at N2LO (Eτ, R0=1.0 fm): 2.33(4)(10) fm, compared to experimental 2.589(39) fm
- Truncation uncertainty estimated as ΔX^{N2LO} = max(Q^4×|X^LO|, Q^2×|X^NLO-X^LO|, Q×|X^{N2LO}-X^NLO|) with Q=m_π/600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the local chiral interaction program of Gezerlis (2014) and Lynn (2016, 2017) from light nuclei (A≤4) to medium-mass nuclei up to A=16 using AFDMC, which had previously been limited to phenomenological Argonne v18 plus Illinois three-body potentials (Carlson 2015). The results show that chiral Hamiltonians can replace phenomenological models while providing systematic uncertainty estimates, though the softer cutoff (R0=1.2 fm) produces pathological overbinding in oxygen-16 that requires further investigation. The calculations enable future studies of neutron-rich systems and provide benchmarks for other many-body methods that rely on normal-ordering approximations for three-body forces.
