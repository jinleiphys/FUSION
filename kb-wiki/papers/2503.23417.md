# Bayesian method for quantifying the non-Gaussian fluctuations in low-intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> In this work, we present a model-independent method to quantify the non-Gaussian fluctuations in the observable distributions, which are assessed by the difference between the measured observable distributions and reconstructed observable distributions via the Bayesian method. Our results indicate that the strength of non-Gaussian fluctuation increases with the beam energy, and is primarily driven by non-central collision mechanisms. The experimental measurement of the strength of non-Gaussian fluctuation of the observable distributions will provide valuable insights into understanding the nonequilibrium effects in heavy ion collisions and the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent Bayesian method to quantify non-Gaussian fluctuations in observable distributions from heavy ion collisions, where the strength of these fluctuations is measured by the deviation between the measured observable distribution and the reconstructed distribution assuming a Gaussian fluctuation kernel. The authors hedge that the Gaussian form of the fluctuation kernel is an approximation, and the method's validity depends on the assumption that the system's dynamical processes do not dramatically distort the shape of fluctuations.

## Method

The authors generated "measured" observable distributions using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD-Sky version) for the reaction system 112Sn+112Sn at beam energies from 50 MeV/u to 200 MeV/u, applying experimental angle and energy cuts (theta_lab from 14 to 80 degrees and 100 to 147 degrees, kinetic energy from 10 MeV to 100 MeV). They reconstructed the observable distributions by fitting the measured data with a Gaussian fluctuation kernel P_G(X|c_b) using the Bayesian method, where the mean values and covariance matrix elements were expressed as exponential functions of centrality c_b with parameters determined by minimizing the reduced chi-square. The strength of non-Gaussian fluctuations was quantified by the mean-square-error (MSE) between the measured observable distribution P(X) and the reconstructed distribution Q(X).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 50 MeV/u to 200 MeV/u for the 112Sn+112Sn system
- Maximum multiplicity of charged particles M_max = 45 at 50 MeV/u and M_max = 62 at 120 MeV/u under experimental filters
- Maximum total transverse momentum p_t,max^tot = 13 GeV/c at 50 MeV/u and 24 GeV/c at 120 MeV/u
- Reduced chi-square values for reconstruction at 50 MeV/u: chi^2_r,11 = 1.53, chi^2_r,22 = 1.21, chi^2_r,33 = 1.10, chi^2_r,44 = 1.06
- Reduced chi-square values for reconstruction at 120 MeV/u: chi^2_r,11 = 8.49, chi^2_r,22 = 2.09, chi^2_r,33 = 2.15, chi^2_r,44 = 4.55
- Total events generated: 1,000,000 with impact parameter b randomly distributed from 0 to b_max = 1.2 (A_p^1/3 + A_t^1/3) fm

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian method for reconstructing impact parameter distributions previously applied to high-energy heavy ion collisions by Yousefnia et al. and to low-intermediate energies by Chen et al. (2023), who assumed a two-dimensional Gaussian fluctuation kernel. The method enables quantitative analysis of non-Gaussian fluctuations that may arise from nonequilibrium dynamical processes or large correlation lengths near the critical point of nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions, and the authors suggest that experimental measurement of the non-Gaussian fluctuation strength could provide insights into understanding nonequilibrium effects and phase transitions in finite nuclei.
