# The microcanonical ensemble of the ideal relativistic quantum gas

> We derive the microcanonical partition function of the ideal relativistic quantum gas of spinless bosons in a quantum field framework as an expansion over fixed multiplicities. Our calculation generalizes well known expressions in literature in that it does not introduce any large volume approximation and it is valid at any volume. We discuss the issues concerned with the definition of the microcanonical ensemble for a free quantum field at volumes comparable with the Compton wavelength and provide a consistent prescription of calculating the microcanonical partition function, which is finite at finite volume and yielding the correct thermodynamic limit. Besides an immaterial overall factor, the obtained expression turns out to be the same as in the non-relativistic multi-particle approach. This work is introductory to derive the most general expression of the microcanonical partition function fixing the maximal set of observables of the Poincare' group.

## Key claim

The authors derive a microcanonical partition function for the ideal relativistic quantum gas of spinless bosons that is valid at any finite volume, without the large-volume approximation used in all prior work; however, the expression obtained turns out to be the same as in the non-relativistic multi-particle approach up to an immaterial overall factor, provided a consistent prescription for subtracting terms from field degrees of freedom outside the considered volume is introduced.

## Method

The microcanonical partition function is defined as the trace of the projector onto an irreducible representation of the Poincaré group (here, the four-momentum projector) multiplied by the projector onto the Hilbert subspace of states localized within a finite volume V. The authors expand this trace as a sum over fixed particle multiplicities (channels) and calculate the channel weights using a quantum field theoretical framework, starting from the Bogoliubov transformation that maps annihilation and creation operators of the localized problem onto those of the free field over all space. The projector onto localized states is expressed as a functional integral over field degrees of freedom inside V, and spurious contributions from field degrees of freedom outside V are subtracted by hand.

## Key numbers

- The expression for the single-species microcanonical channel weight (equation labeled "singlespecies") involves a sum over permutations of products of Fourier integrals \(F_V({\bf p} - {\bf p}') = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \int_V d^3 x \, e^{i ({\bf p} - {\bf p}')\cdot {\bf x}}\) over the finite volume V.
- In the large-volume limit, the Fourier integral approximates a delta function: \(F_V({\bf p} - {\bf p}') \simeq \delta^3({\bf p} - {\bf p}')\), leading to the resummed expression \(\Omega = \frac{\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}}{(2 \pi)^4} \int_{-\infty-i\varepsilon}^{+\infty-i\varepsilon} d y^0 \int d^3 {\rm y} \; e^{i P \cdot y} \exp \left[ \sum_j \frac{V}{(2 \pi)^3} \int d^3 p \; \log (1 - e^{-i p \cdot y})^{-1} \right]\).
- The Bogoliubov relation for the localized annihilation operator is \(a_{{\bf k}} = \int d^3 p \, F({\bf k},{\bf p}) \frac{\varepsilon_{{\bf k}} + \varepsilon_{{\bf p}}}{2\sqrt{\varepsilon_{{\bf k}}\varepsilon_{{\bf p}}}} a_{{\bf p}} + F({\bf k},-{\bf p}) \frac{\varepsilon_{{\bf k}} - \varepsilon_{{\bf p}}}{2\sqrt{\varepsilon_{{\bf k}}\varepsilon_{{\bf p}}}} a_{{\bf p}}^\dagger\), where \(\varepsilon_{{\bf p}} = \sqrt{{\bf p}^2+m^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of the microcanonical partition function with finite-volume corrections (Becattini and Ferroni, 2004) from a multi-particle first-quantization framework into a full relativistic quantum field theory treatment. It addresses the inadequacy of the first-quantization approach at volumes comparable with the Compton wavelength, where pair creation due to localization becomes unavoidable, as noted by Landau and Lifshitz. The result enables future derivation of the most general microcanonical partition function fixing the maximal set of observables of the Poincaré group, and provides a foundation for studying microcanonical corrections in small-volume systems such as those encountered in statistical approaches to hadron production and the bag model.
