# Production and polarization of $S$-wave quarkonia in potential nonrelativistic QCD

> Based on the potential nonrelativistic QCD formalism, we compute the nonrelativistic QCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for inclusive production of $S$-wave heavy quarkonia. This greatly reduces the number of nonperturbative unknowns and brings in a substantial enhancement in the predictive power of the nonrelativistic QCD factorization formalism. We obtain improved determinations of the LDMEs and find cross sections and polarizations of $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and excited $Υ$ states that agree well with LHC data. Our results may have important implications in pinning down the heavy quarkonium production mechanism.

# Production and polarization of $S$-wave quarkonia in potential nonrelativistic QCD

## Key claim
The authors compute NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for inclusive production of $S$-wave heavy quarkonia using the potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) formalism, working in the strongly coupled regime where $v \gtrsim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}/m$. This reduces the number of nonperturbative unknowns and yields cross sections and polarizations that agree well with LHC data, but the results depend on the choice of $p_T$ cut and assume that corrections of order $v^2$ and $1/N_c^2$ are small.

## Method
The authors express the NRQCD color-octet LDMEs for $S$-wave quarkonia in terms of universal gluonic correlators (${\cal E}_{10;10}$, ${\cal B}_{00}$, ${\cal E}_{00}$) and quarkonium wave functions at the origin, using pNRQCD at leading nonvanishing order in quantum-mechanical perturbation theory. They determine the three gluonic correlators by fitting the NRQCD factorization formula at NLO in $\alpha_s$ to measured LHC cross sections for prompt $J/\psi$, $\psi(2S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, and $\Upsilon(3S)$ production, using two different $p_T$ cuts ($p_T/(2m) > 5$ and $p_T/(2m) > 3$). The resulting LDMEs are then used to compute cross sections and polarization parameters, with feeddown contributions from $P$-wave decays included.

## Key numbers
- For $p_T/(2m) > 5$: ${\cal E}_{10;10} = 0.860 \pm 0.277$ GeV$^2$, $c_F^2 {\cal B}_{00} = -2.25 \pm 7.06$ GeV$^2$, ${\cal E}_{00} = 13.4 \pm 4.6$ GeV$^2$ (in $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at scale $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV)
- For $p_T/(2m) > 3$: ${\cal E}_{10;10} = 1.17 \pm 0.13$ GeV$^2$, $c_F^2 {\cal B}_{00} = -9.79 \pm 3.08$ GeV$^2$, ${\cal E}_{00} = 18.5 \pm 2.1$ GeV$^2$
- $J/\psi$ CO LDMEs for $p_T/(2m) > 5$: $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^3S_1^{[8]}) \rangle = (1.25 \pm 0.40) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^1S_0^{[8]}) \rangle = (-1.10 \pm 3.43) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^3P_0^{[8]}) \rangle/m^2 = (2.18 \pm 0.75) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- $J/\psi$ CO LDMEs for $p_T/(2m) > 3$: $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^3S_1^{[8]}) \rangle = (1.70 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^1S_0^{[8]}) \rangle = (-4.76 \pm 1.50) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle {\cal O}^{J/\psi} (^3P_0^{[8]}) \rangle/m^2 = (3.00 \pm 0.34) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- Fit quality: $\chi^2_{\rm min}/{\rm d.o.f.} = 6.30/41$ for $p_T/(2m) > 5$ and $14.0/71$ for $p_T/(2m) > 3$
- Ratio of $\psi(2S)$ to $J/\psi$ production: $(B_{\psi(2S) \to \mu^+ \mu^-} \times \sigma_{\psi(2S)+X})/(B_{J/\psi \to \mu^+ \mu^-} \times \sigma_{J/\psi+X}) \approx 0.04$ at large $p_T$
- Ratio of $\Upsilon(3S)$ to $\Upsilon(2S)$ production: $(B_{\Upsilon(3S) \to \mu^+ \mu^-} \times \sigma_{\Upsilon(3S)+X})/(B_{\Upsilon(2S) \to \mu^+ \mu^-} \times \sigma_{\Upsilon(2S)+X}) \approx 0.8$ at large $p_T$

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD formalism previously applied to $P$-wave quarkonia by Brambilla et al. (2020, 2021) to $S$-wave states, addressing the longstanding puzzle of heavy quarkonium production where previous NRQCD LDME determinations from hadroproduction data (e.g., Chao et al. 2012, Butenschoen et al. 2012, Bodwin et al. 2014) gave inconsistent and contradictory predictions. The reduction of nonperturbative unknowns through universal gluonic correlators enables simultaneous description of charmonium and bottomonium data, and the results may help resolve tensions with measurements at $ep$ and lepton colliders that were noted by Butenschoen et al. (2012, 2014). Future lattice QCD calculations of the gluonic correlators could provide first-principles determinations, and more measurements of excited $\Upsilon$ states at large $p_T$ would further reduce theoretical uncertainties.
