# Ab initio calculation of the Hoyle state and a new look at clustering in nuclei

> I present an ab initio calculation of the spectrum of carbon-12, including also the famous Hoyle state. Its structure is discussed and a new interpretation of clustering in nuclear physics is given.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first *ab initio* calculation of the Hoyle state in carbon-12, finding a resonance with energy close to the ⁴He+⁸Be threshold, but the results are obtained at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective field theory and still carry systematic uncertainties from omitted higher-order interactions.

## Method
Nuclear lattice simulations were performed using chiral effective field theory forces at N²LO, with space-time discretized on a torus of volume Lₛ³ × Lₜ and lattice spacing a ≈ 2 fm. A multi-channel projection Monte Carlo technique was developed to extract excited states from combinations of initial standing waves with zero total momentum and even parity, and the action was improved to minimize finite lattice spacing effects and unphysical partial-wave mixing from rotational symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers
- Ground state (0₁⁺) energy at NNLO: −91(3) MeV, compared to experimental −92.16 MeV
- Hoyle state (0₂⁺) energy at NNLO: −85(3) MeV, compared to experimental −84.51 MeV
- ⁸Be-alpha threshold from lattice at NNLO: −86(2) MeV, compared to experimental −84.80 MeV
- Spin-2 state (2₁⁺, Jz=0) energy at NNLO: −88(3) MeV; (Jz=2): −90(4) MeV, compared to experimental −87.72 MeV
- Binding energy difference between ³H and ³He: 0.78(5) MeV, in agreement with experimental 0.76 MeV
- Lattice spacing a ≈ 2 fm, corresponding to momentum cut-off ≈ 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends *ab initio* no-core-shell-model calculations (Navratil 2007, Roth 2011) that successfully describe p-shell nuclei but fail for the Hoyle state, while alpha cluster models (Freer 2011, Yamada 2011) describe the Hoyle state well but have little connection to few-nucleon forces. The calculation enables future studies of the quark mass dependence of the ¹²C spectrum and paves the way for model-independent calculation of the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction at stellar energies.
