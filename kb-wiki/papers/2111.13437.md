# Effects of rotation and valence nucleons in molecular-like $α$-chain nuclei

> Effects of rotation and valence nucleons in molecular-like linear $α$-chain nuclei are analyzed using a three-dimensional lattice cranking model based on covariant density functional theory. The structure of $^{16}$C and $^{16}$Ne is investigated as a function of rotational frequency. The valence nucleons, with respect to the 3$α$ linear chain core of $^{12}$C, at low frequency occupy the $π$ molecular orbital. With increasing rotational frequency these nucleons transition from the $π$ orbital to the $σ$ molecular orbital, thus stabilizing the 3$α$ linear chain structure. It is predicted that the valence protons in $^{16}$Ne change occupation from the $π$ to the $σ$ molecular orbital at $\hbarω\approx 1.3$ MeV, a lower rotational frequency compared to $\hbarω\approx 1.7$ MeV for the valence neutrons in $^{16}$C. The same effects of valence protons are found in $^{20}$Mg, compared to the four valence neutrons in $^{20}$O. The model is also used to examine the effect of alignment of valence nucleons on the relative positions and size of the three $α$-clusters in $^{16}$C and $^{16}$Ne.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that valence protons in the mirror nuclei \(^{16}\)Ne and \(^{20}\)Mg transition from the \(\pi\) molecular orbital to the \(\sigma\) molecular orbital at lower rotational frequencies than the corresponding valence neutrons in \(^{16}\)C and \(^{20}\)O, thereby stabilizing the linear \(\alpha\)-chain configuration at lower rotational frequencies in proton-rich systems.

## Method

The authors employed a three-dimensional lattice cranking model based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the DD-ME2 density functional. The cranking Dirac equation was solved self-consistently in 3D lattice space using the inverse Hamiltonian method and spectral method to avoid variational collapse and fermion doubling, with a damping function applied to remove unphysical continuum effects. Starting from a constrained 3\(\alpha\) linear chain configuration in \(^{12}\)C, they calculated the structure of \(^{16}\)C, \(^{16}\)Ne, \(^{20}\)O, and \(^{20}\)Mg as functions of rotational frequency, analyzing single-particle Routhians, nucleon localization functions, density distributions, angular momenta, and quadrupole deformations.

## Key numbers

- The valence protons in \(^{16}\)Ne change occupation from the \(\pi\) to the \(\sigma\) molecular orbital at \(\hbar\omega \approx 1.3\) MeV, compared to \(\hbar\omega \approx 1.7\) MeV for the valence neutrons in \(^{16}\)C.
- The 3\(\alpha\) linear chain proton configuration in \(^{16}\)C stabilizes at rotational frequency \(\hbar\omega \approx 2.2\) MeV.
- The 3\(\alpha\) linear chain neutron configuration in \(^{16}\)Ne stabilizes at rotational frequency \(\hbar\omega \approx 1.7\) MeV.
- For the 4\(\alpha\) systems, the linear chain neutron configuration in \(^{20}\)O stabilizes at \(\hbar\omega \approx 2.4\) MeV, while the proton configuration in \(^{20}\)Mg stabilizes at \(\hbar\omega \approx 2.2\) MeV.
- The angular momentum slope changes at \(\hbar\omega \approx 1.5\) MeV for \(^{16}\)Ne and \(\hbar\omega \approx 2.0\) MeV for \(^{16}\)C, indicating structural change.
- The quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\) of \(^{16}\)Ne shows a sharp rise at \(\hbar\omega \approx 1.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zhao et al. (2015) on the rotational stabilization of linear \(\alpha\)-chain configurations in neutron-rich carbon isotopes \(^{15-18}\)C, by now investigating the role of valence protons in proton-rich mirror nuclei. The results enable future studies of cluster structures in \(N=Z\) nuclei and molecular-like nuclei without axial symmetry constraints, particularly the analysis of bending motion in rotating linear chain configurations.
