# Chromo-polarizability and pipi final state interaction

> The chromo-polarizability of a quarkonium state is a measure of the amplitude of the $E1$-$E1$ chromo-electric interaction of the quarkonium with soft gluon fields and can be measured in the heavy quarkonium decays. Based on the chiral unitary approach, formulas with modification caused by the $S$ wave $ππ$ final state interaction (FSI) for measuring the chromo-polarizabilities are given. It is shown that the effect of the $S$ wave $ππ$ FSI is very important in extracting chromo-polarizabilities from the experimental data. The resultant values with the FSI are reduced to about 1/3 of those determined without the FSI. The consequences of the FSI correction in the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering near the threshold are also discussed. The estimated lower bound of the total cross section is reduced from about 17 mb to 2.9 mb, which agrees with the experimental data point and is compatible with the previously estimated values in the literature. In order to understand the interaction of heavy quarkonia with light hadrons at low energies better and to obtain the chromo-polarizabilities of quarkonia accurately, more data should be accumulated. This can be done in the $J/ψ\to π^+π^-l^+l^-$ decay at BES-III and CLEO-c and in the $Υ\to π^+π^-l^+l^-$ decay at B factories.

## Key claim

The S-wave ππ final state interaction (FSI), accounted for via the chiral unitary approach, reduces the extracted values of the chromo-polarizabilities |α_{ψ'J/ψ}| and |α_{Υ'Υ}| to about 1/3 of those determined without the FSI, and correspondingly lowers the estimated lower bound of the J/ψ-nucleon total cross section at threshold from about 17 mb to 2.9 mb, bringing it into agreement with the experimental data point.

## Method

The authors employed the chiral unitary approach (ChUA) to incorporate the S-wave ππ final state interaction into the decay amplitudes for ψ' → J/ψ π⁺π⁻ and Υ' → Υ π⁺π⁻. They fitted the resulting differential width formula, which includes a two-pion loop integral and the coupled-channel ππ → ππ amplitude for I=0, to the experimental π⁺π⁻ invariant mass spectra from BES and CLEO data, adjusting the chromo-polarizability α_{AB} and the constant C as free parameters. The same FSI factor was then applied to modify the formula for measuring the diagonal chromo-polarizability α_{1S} in decays J/ψ → π⁺π⁻ l⁺l⁻ and Υ → π⁺π⁻ l⁺l⁻, and the corrected α_{J/ψ} was used to recalculate J/ψ-nucleon scattering quantities.

## Key numbers

- |α_{ψ'J/ψ}| = 0.83 ± 0.01 GeV⁻³ with ππ FSI, compared to 2.24 ± 0.02 GeV⁻³ without FSI (fitted to BES ψ' → J/ψ π⁺π⁻ data).
- |α_{Υ'Υ}| = 0.24 ± 0.01 GeV⁻³ with ππ FSI, compared to 0.70 ± 0.01 GeV⁻³ without FSI (fitted to CLEO Υ' → Υ π⁺π⁻ data).
- The FSI factor |1 + 2G_π(q²)T^{I=0}_{ππ,ππ}(q²)| reaches a maximum of about 2.8 at m_{ππ} ≈ 0.48 GeV.
- Estimated lower bound of J/ψ-nucleon total cross section at threshold: 2.9 mb with ππ FSI, reduced from 17 mb without FSI (Sibirtsev and Voloshin, 2005).
- J/ψ-nucleon scattering length: 0.15 fm with FSI, versus 0.37 fm without FSI.
- J/ψ in-medium mass shift: -8.7 MeV with FSI, versus -21 MeV without FSI.
- Experimental J/ψ-nucleon total cross section: σ_{J/ψ N} = 3.8 ± 0.8 ± 0.5 mb at √s ≈ 5.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the multipole expansion and low-energy theorem approach of Sibirtsev and Voloshin (2005) for J/ψ-nucleon scattering, which used a chromo-polarizability of 2 GeV⁻³, by incorporating the S-wave ππ final state interaction via the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Oset (1997). The corrected cross section lower bound of 2.9 mb now agrees with the experimental data point and is compatible with earlier theoretical estimates by Brodsky and Miller (1997) and Teramond et al. (1998). The results enable more accurate extraction of chromo-polarizabilities from future data at BES-III, CLEO-c, and B factories, which is important for understanding J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions.
