# Probing the QGP Phase Boundary with Thermal Properties of $ϕ$ Mesons

> A novel attempt has been made to probe the QCD phase boundary by using the experimental data for transverse momenta of ϕ mesons produced in nuclear collisions at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. The data are confronted with simple thermodynamic expectations and lattice QCD results. The experimental data indicate a first-order phase transition, with a mixed phase stretching the energy density between \sim1 and 3.2 GeV/fm3 corresponding to SPS energies.

## Key claim

The experimental data for the transverse momentum of phi mesons from AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies indicate a first-order phase transition, with a mixed phase stretching the energy density between approximately 1 and 3.2 GeV/fm³ corresponding to SPS energies; this claim is hedged by the use of the Bjorken energy density, which the authors note is a lower bound on the actual initial energy density.

## Method

The authors compiled published experimental data on the transverse momentum spectra of phi mesons from central Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. They fitted these spectra with a blast-wave model to extract the effective temperature (inverse slope parameter), the thermal freeze-out temperature, and the average radial flow velocity. The extracted thermal temperatures and effective temperatures were then plotted as functions of collision energy and of the Bjorken energy density multiplied by formation time to search for a plateau-like structure indicative of a first-order phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase is observed to stretch over an energy density range of approximately 1 to 3.2 GeV/fm³ at SPS energies.
- The Bjorken energy density for Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV is 2.9 GeV/fm³; for Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV it is 4.9 GeV/fm³ (STAR) and 5.4 GeV/fm³ (PHENIX).
- The effective temperature T_eff for phi mesons at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV is 0.299 ± 0.07 ± 0.01 GeV; at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV it is 0.359 ± 0.05 ± 0.02 GeV (STAR) and 0.376 ± 0.024 ± 0.02 GeV (PHENIX).
- The thermal temperature T_th extracted from blast-wave fits at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV is 0.171 GeV; at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV it is 0.157 GeV (STAR) and 0.174 GeV (PHENIX).
- The radial flow velocity v_r at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV is 0.5; at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV it is 0.63.
- The lattice QCD prediction for the critical energy density for deconfinement is approximately 0.7 GeV/fm³.

## Context

This work extends the earlier step-like behavior observed in the mean transverse mass of pions, kaons, and protons as a function of charged-particle multiplicity (van Hove, 1982; Bass and Dumitru, 2000) to the phi meson, which is argued to be a cleaner probe due to its small hadronic re-scattering cross-section. The observed plateau in the effective temperature of phi mesons at SPS energies supports the onset of deconfinement in that energy regime, consistent with anomalies in strangeness production and the failure of hadronic transport models to reproduce kaon inverse slopes (El, 2000; Wagner, 2005). The results enable future beam energy scans at RHIC and SPS to map the QCD phase boundary and search for the critical point.
