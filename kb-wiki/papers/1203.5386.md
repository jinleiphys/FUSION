# Matrix Theory for Baryons: An Overview of Holographic QCD for Nuclear Physics

> We provide, for non-experts, a brief overview of holographic QCD and a review of a recent proposal of matrix-description of multi-baryon systems in holographic QCD. Based on the matrix model, we derive the baryon interaction at short distances in multi-flavor holographic QCD. We show that there is a very universal repulsive core of inter-baryon forces for generic number of flavors. This is consistent with a recent lattice QCD analysis for N_f = 2, 3 where repulsive core looks universal. We also provide a comparison of our results with the lattice QCD and the operator product expansion (OPE) analysis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that holographic QCD, via a matrix model derived from the AdS/CFT correspondence, predicts a universal repulsive core in inter-baryon forces for any number of flavors \(N_f\), including \(N_f > 2\). This universality is presented as consistent with lattice QCD results for \(N_f = 2, 3\), but the holographic derivation relies on the large \(N_c\) and large 't Hooft coupling limits, which are approximations that differ from real QCD.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto (D4D8) holographic model of QCD, where baryons are represented as D4-branes wrapped on an \(S^4\) and flavor is introduced via D8-branes. The multi-baryon system is described by a matrix effective action derived from the ADHM construction for instantons, with an \(A \times A\) hermitian matrix \(X^M\) for baryon positions and an \(A \times N_f\) complex matrix \(w\) for flavor degrees of freedom. The short-distance inter-baryon force is extracted by analyzing the potential between eigenvalues of \(X^M\) after integrating out off-diagonal matrix entries, and this calculation is extended to generic \(N_f > 2\).

## Key numbers

* The baryon mass is of order \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\) in the large \(N_c\) expansion, consistent with the D-brane description.
* The repulsive core is found to be universal for generic number of flavors \(N_f\), with explicit results shown for \(N_f > 2\).
* The lattice QCD analysis cited in the paper covers \(N_f = 2\) and \(N_f = 3\), where the repulsive core appears universal across most channels.

## Context

This work extends the matrix model approach to multi-baryon systems introduced by Hashimoto, Iizuka, and Yi (2010), which derived an action for baryon interactions from holographic QCD. The new result on universal repulsive core for \(N_f > 2\) is compared with lattice QCD calculations by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009) for \(N_f = 2, 3\), and with operator product expansion (OPE) analysis. The paper also reviews prior holographic results including baryon spectroscopy, three-body nuclear forces, and formation of atomic nuclei, all within the same D4D8 model framework. The work aims to bridge QCD and nuclear physics by providing an analytic, non-perturbative tool for deriving nuclear forces, though it acknowledges limitations from the large \(N_c\) and large \(\lambda\) approximations and the presence of unphysical degrees of freedom at high energy scales.
