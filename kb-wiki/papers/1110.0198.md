# Specific viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter from a relaxation time approach

> The specific viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter is studied from the relaxation time approach using an isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction and the nucleon-nucleon cross sections taken as those from the experimental data modified by the in-medium effective masses as used in the IBUU transport model calculations. The relaxation time of neutrons is larger while that of protons is smaller in neutron-rich nuclear matter compared with that in symmetric nuclear matter, and this leads to a larger specific viscosity in neutron-rich nuclear matter. In addition, the specific viscosity decreases with increasing temperature because of more frequent collisions and weaker Pauli blocking effect at higher temperatures. At lower temperatures the specific viscosity increases with increasing density due to the Pauli blocking effect, while at higher temperatures it slightly decreases with increasing density as a result of smaller in-medium effective masses at higher densities.

## Key claim

The specific viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter is larger than that of symmetric nuclear matter, primarily because the relaxation time of neutrons increases while that of protons decreases in neutron-rich matter; this isospin effect is most pronounced at lower temperatures.

## Method

The shear viscosity of uniform, static neutron and proton matter was calculated using the relaxation time approximation to the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, with the isospin- and momentum-dependent (MDI) interaction providing the single-particle potentials and effective masses. The nucleon-nucleon cross sections were taken from free-space parameterized forms (based on experimental data) and then modified by the in-medium effective masses as done in the IBUU transport model. The relaxation time for each isospin species was computed from the collision integrals including Pauli blocking, and the shear viscosity was obtained by integrating the momentum-dependent relaxation time over the equilibrium distribution.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and T = 50 MeV, the total relaxation times for neutrons and protons in neutron-rich matter are of magnitude only a few fm/c.
- At T = 50 MeV, the specific viscosity from in-medium cross sections is about 4–5 ℏ/(4π), close to the value of the quark-gluon plasma extracted from viscous hydrodynamic models.
- The specific viscosity obtained from the relaxation time approach is similar to that extracted from BUU model calculations using Green-Kubo formulas, which is around 20–30 ℏ/(4π) at lower energies and reduces to as low as 6 ℏ/(4π) at higher energies.
- The binding energy of the MDI interaction is E₀ = -16 MeV, the incompressibility is K₀ = 211 MeV, and the symmetry energy is about 31 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the few existing studies of viscosity in nuclear matter formed in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions (Dan84, Shi03, Che07b, Pal10b, Li11) and is one of even fewer studies addressing the isospin dependence of viscosity (Zha10). The results are compared with those from the BUU model using Green-Kubo formulas (Li11) and with Brueckner theory calculations (Zha10). The findings enable future studies of how in-medium modifications of the transition matrix, inelastic collisions producing Δ resonances and pions, and cluster formation at lower densities affect the viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter.
