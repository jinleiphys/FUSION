# Fermions in Two Dimensions: Scattering and Many-Body Properties

> Ultracold atomic Fermi gases in two-dimensions (2D) are an increasingly popular topic of research. The interaction strength between spin-up and spin-down particles in two-component Fermi gases can be tuned in experiments, allowing for a strongly interacting regime where the gas properties are yet to be fully understood. We have probed this regime for 2D Fermi gases by performing T=0 ab initio diffusion Monte Carlo calculations. The many-body dynamics are largely dependent on the two-body interactions, therefore we start with an in-depth look at scattering theory in 2D. We show the partial-wave expansion and its relation to the scattering length and effective range. Then we discuss our numerical methods for determining these scattering parameters. We close out this discussion by illustrating the details of bound states in 2D. Transitioning to the many-body system, we use variationally optimized wave functions to calculate ground-state properties of the gas over a range of interaction strengths. We show results for the energy per particle and parametrize an equation of state. We then proceed to determine the chemical potential for the strongly interacting gas.

## Key claim

The paper presents T=0 diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations for two-dimensional Fermi gases across the BEC-BCS crossover, finding significantly lower ground-state energies than prior DMC results (Bertaina 2011) in the crossover regime, while achieving excellent agreement with Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo (Shi 2015) and a more recent DMC study (Madeira 2017). The authors hedge that their results are for a fixed effective range of \(k_F r_e = 0.006\) and use variationally optimized Jastrow-BCS wave functions with parameters tuned independently for each interaction strength.

## Method

The authors first solve the two-body scattering problem in 2D using the partial-wave expansion, extracting the 2D scattering length \(a_{2D}\) and effective range \(r_e\) from numerical solutions of the reduced radial Schrödinger equation for a modified Pöschl-Teller potential. For the many-body system, they use diffusion Monte Carlo with a Jastrow-BCS trial wave function containing variational parameters \(\alpha_n\), \(b\), \(c\), and \(d\) that are optimized independently for each interaction strength \(\eta = \log(k_F a_{2D})\). The ground-state energy per particle is computed via mixed estimates from DMC, and the results are finite-size corrected to the thermodynamic limit and parametrized as an equation of state using a 7th-order polynomial in the crossover region joined to analytic forms in the BEC and BCS limits.

## Key numbers

- The effective range is held constant at \(k_F r_e = 0.006\) throughout the many-body calculations.
- The finite-size correction to the thermodynamic-limit energy ranges from zero on the BEC side to approximately \(0.041\,E_{\rm FG}\) in the BCS regime.
- The matching points for the equation of state are selected at \(\eta = -0.25\) and \(\eta = 2.5\).
- The dimer scattering length is given by \(a_d \approx 0.557 a_{2D}\) (Petrov 2003).
- The binding energy in 2D is \(\epsilon_b = -4\hbar^2 / (m a_{2D}^2 e^{2\gamma})\) where \(\gamma \approx 0.577215\) is Euler's constant.

## Context

This work extends prior DMC calculations for 2D Fermi gases (Bertaina 2011) by using more sophisticated Jastrow-BCS wave functions with multiple variational parameters, yielding lower energies in the crossover regime. The results agree with Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo (Shi 2015) and a later DMC study (Madeira 2017), and provide more data points on the BCS side than previously available (Galea 2016). The parametrized equation of state enables calculation of the chemical potential and other ground-state properties for strongly interacting 2D Fermi gases, which are relevant to ongoing experiments with ultracold atomic gases in quasi-2D traps and box potentials (Hueck 2017).
