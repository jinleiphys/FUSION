# Heavy Quark Production from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the production of heavy quarks, charm at BNL-RHIC ($\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV/nucleon) and CERN-LHC ($\sqrt{s}$=5.5 TeV/nucleon) and bottom at CERN-LHC from heavy ions colliding at relativistic energies. We consider initial fusion of gluons (and quark- anti-quark annihilation), pre-thermal parton interactions and interactions in thermalized quark gluon plasma. We also consider free-streaming partons as another extreme and compare the results with those from a thermalized plasma of partons. The pre-thermal contribution is calculated by considering interaction among partons having large transverse momenta (jet-partons) after the initial interaction, and from passage of these partons through a thermalized quark gluon plasma. Charm production from pre-thermal processes is found to be comparable to that from prompt (initial) interactions at LHC. It is suggested that this may have important implications for the study of nuclear modification factor, R$_{\rm {AA}}$ as well as for back-to-back correlation of heavy quarks and production of dileptons having a large mass.

## Key claim

The authors find that pre-thermal charm production from jet-jet interactions at LHC energies can exceed the prompt (initial fusion) contribution for intermediate transverse momenta, which would significantly alter the expected back-to-back correlations of heavy quarks and the nuclear modification factor R_AA, but this conclusion depends on the assumed thermalization time and initial conditions.

## Method

The authors calculate heavy quark (charm and bottom) production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV/nucleon) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV/nucleon) using leading-order perturbative QCD with CTEQ5L parton distribution functions, EKS98 nuclear shadowing, and a K-factor of approximately 2.5. They evaluate four production mechanisms: initial gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation (prompt), jet-jet interactions among high-pT partons (pre-thermal), interactions between jets and thermalized quark-gluon plasma (jet-thermal), and thermal production from equilibrated partons, with free-streaming partons considered as an alternative to thermal evolution. The calculations use Bjorken hydrodynamics for the thermal evolution with two different thermalization times (0.15 fm/c and 0.5 fm/c at RHIC; 0.07 fm/c and 0.5 fm/c at LHC).

## Key numbers

- Charm production at RHIC: initial fusion dominates at all pT; jet-jet and jet-thermal contributions are at about 4-5% of initial fusion for large pT with tau_0=0.15 fm/c and T_0=447 MeV
- Charm production at LHC: jet-jet contribution exceeds initial fusion for pT > 2 GeV with tau_0=0.07 fm/c; thermal production reaches about 40% of initial fusion at low pT and drops to 5-10% at pT approximately 5 GeV
- Charm at LHC with tau_0=0.5 fm/c: jet-jet contribution comparable to initial fusion; at pT approximately 8 GeV, jet-jet contributes at 10% of initial fusion; jet-thermal starts at about 20% of initial fusion at lowest pT and rises to about one-third for larger pT
- Bottom production at LHC: jet-jet contribution at 2-6% of initial fusion; thermal and jet-thermal contributions are negligible
- Nuclear overlap factors: T_AA=286 fm^-2 for Au+Au at RHIC; T_AA=292 fm^-2 for Pb+Pb at LHC
- Particle rapidity densities: 1260 at RHIC (experimental); assumed 5625 at LHC
- Initial temperatures: T_0=447 MeV at RHIC for tau_0=0.15 fm/c; larger values at LHC for tau_0=0.07 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark production in heavy ion collisions by Lin and Gyulassy (1995) on jet-jet interactions and by Liu and Fries (2008) on jet conversion in thermalized medium, while also incorporating the pre-thermal production framework of Levai et al. The results suggest that at LHC energies, the substantial non-prompt contributions to charm production will require revision of earlier back-to-back correlation studies by Molnar and others (2004, 2005) that considered only initial fusion processes. The findings have implications for interpreting nuclear modification factor R_AA measurements and dilepton production from correlated heavy quark decays as studied by Rapp and others.
