# Effective description of QCD at high density

> After an introduction to the phases of QCD at high density and zero temperature, we introduce the effective lagrangian for the CFL phase and discuss the perturbative evaluation of its parameters

# Effective description of QCD at high density

## Key claim

The paper argues that at asymptotically high densities, QCD becomes weakly coupled and color superconductivity can be described by an effective low-energy theory, but the quantitative gap calculations from first principles are unlikely to be extrapolated below a chemical potential of order 10^8 GeV, meaning that for the phenomenologically interesting regime of neutron stars and heavy ions (mu less than about 500 MeV) one must rely on phenomenological interactions rather than perturbative QCD.

## Method

The author introduces a toy model with two Fermi oscillators to illustrate the gap equation and Bogoliubov transformation, then extends the analysis to QCD using the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the self-energy in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, ignoring vertex corrections. For the CFL phase with three massless flavors, an effective lagrangian is constructed for the Goldstone bosons arising from the symmetry breaking pattern SU(3)_c x SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R x U(1)_B x U(1)_A to SU(3)_{c+L+R} x Z_2 x Z_2. The parameters of this effective lagrangian are evaluated perturbatively by coupling quasi-particles near the Fermi surface to the Goldstone bosons and computing one-loop self-energies, using a formalism analogous to heavy quark effective theory.

## Key numbers

* For two massless flavors (2SC phase) with cutoff Lambda = 800 MeV and mu = 400 MeV, the gap Delta = 106 MeV; for mu = 500 MeV, Delta = 145 MeV.
* For three massless flavors (CFL phase) with Lambda = 800 MeV and mu = 400 MeV, the gap in the octet channel Delta_8 = 80 MeV and in the singlet channel Delta_1 = -176 MeV.
* The decay constant for the Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase is F_T^2 = mu^2 (21 - 8 ln 2) / (36 pi^2).
* The pion velocity v^2 = 1/3, obtained from integration over the Fermi velocity.
* The Debye mass m_D^2 = g_s^2 F_T^2 and the Meissner mass m_M^2 = (1/3) m_D^2 for the gluons.
* The wave function renormalization coefficients for gluons are alpha_1 = mu^2 g_s^2 / (216 Delta^2 pi^2) (7 + (16/3) ln 2), with similar expressions for alpha_2 and alpha_3.
* The physical gluon masses extrapolate to about 1.70 Delta.
* In the 2SC phase, the dielectric constant epsilon = 1 + g_s^2 mu^2 / (18 pi^2 Delta^2) and lambda = 1.
* The in-medium dielectric constant for electromagnetism is modified as epsilon_tilde = 1 + (r / (18 pi^2)) (e_tilde^2 mu^2 / Delta^2), with r = 4 in CFL and r = 1 in 2SC.

## Context

This work extends the earlier ideas of color superconductivity going back to Barrois (1977) and the more recent developments reviewed by Wilczek (2000) and Hsu (2000). The paper builds on the calculation of the gap in QCD at asymptotically high density by Son (1999) and on the formulation of the effective theory for the CFL phase by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) and Hong (2000). The results enable a systematic low-energy description of the CFL phase, showing quark-hadron continuity with nuclear matter as argued by Schafer and Wilczek (1999), and provide a framework for computing transport properties and responses of dense QCD matter relevant for neutron star physics.
