# Dense Nuclear Matter with Baryon Overlap

> The possibility of new short-distance physics applicable inside the cores of NS is incorporated into the equation of state generated by the quark-meson coupling model. The contribution of this new physics to the energy density is taken to be proportional to the amount of overlap between the quark cores of the baryons involved. With no change to the properties of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, including an incompressibility compatible with data on giant monopole resonances, one can sustain neutron stars with a maximum mass $M_{max}>2.1$ M$_\odot$, even when hyperons are included.

## Key claim

By adding a phenomenological repulsive term proportional to the overlap of baryon quark cores, the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can produce neutron stars with maximum mass above 2.1 solar masses even when hyperons are present, without altering the properties of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density or the incompressibility required to match giant monopole resonance data.

## Method

The authors extended the QMC model by adding a Gaussian-shaped overlap term to the energy density, parameterized by a strength E0 and a range b, which only becomes appreciable at supra-nuclear densities. They solved the mean-field equations self-consistently at beta-equilibrium for baryons including nucleons, Lambda, and Xi hyperons, with the overlap term added to the total energy density before solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass M_max > 2.1 M_sun achieved with hyperons included, using the overlap term
- Radii of 1.4 M_sun neutron stars lie just below 13 km, at the upper end of the range preferred by GW170817 analysis
- Speed of sound squared c_s^2 reaches values as large as 0.6 with the overlap term, compared to values below 1/3 found without it
- The overlap term uses a Gaussian form with range b expected to be of order 0.5 fm (characteristic of baryon quark core size)
- For the case without the isovector scalar delta meson (G_delta=0), the slope of the symmetry energy L decreases to 53 MeV (52 MeV for lambda_3 = 0.00 fm^{-1})

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, which previously predicted neutron stars up to 2 M_sun with hyperons (Stone 2007), three years before the first 2 M_sun neutron star observation (Demorest 2010). The paper addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperons soften the equation of state and lower maximum masses, similar to proposals by Berryman and Gardner (2021) who used a gauged baryon number interaction. The overlap term allows the model to simultaneously satisfy constraints from giant monopole resonance data (which required a cubic sigma term that lowered maximum masses) and the observed 2 M_sun neutron stars, while remaining consistent with GW170817 tidal deformability bounds.
