# Correlating charge radius with quadrupole deformation and $B(E2)$ in atomic nuclei

> A very good linear correlation is found between the four-point charge radius relation $δR_{2p-2n}(Z,N)$ with that of quadrupole deformation data in even-even nuclei. This results in a further improved charge radius relation that holds in a precision of about 5$\times 10^{-3}$ fm. Such correlations are also seen in global nuclear models, their precisions, however, are not enough to be consistent with the experimental data. The new relation between charge radii and deformation of even-even nuclei can be generalized to the reduced electric quadrupole transition probability $B(E2)$ between the first $2^+$ state and the $0^+$ ground state, and the mean lifetime $τ$ of the first 2$^+$ state.

## Key claim

A linear correlation exists between the four-point charge radius relation δR(Z,N) and the corresponding quadrupole deformation term δR(Z,N)_def, with a slope coefficient of 0.29(6); this correlation holds within about 5×10^{-3} fm precision, but the paper notes that current global nuclear models are not accurate enough to reproduce this experimental correlation.

## Method

The authors computed the four-point charge radius relation δR(Z,N) = R(Z,N) - R(Z,N-2) - [R(Z-2,N) - R(Z-2,N-2)] for 149 even-even nuclei from Ne to Cm using experimental charge radii from Angeli (2013) and quadrupole deformation data from beta (2016). They then compared δR(Z,N) with the deformation contribution δR(Z,N)_def derived from the liquid-drop model expression R = R_0[1 + (5/8π)β_2^2], and performed a linear fit to determine the correlation coefficient. The same analysis was repeated using theoretical predictions from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB-24) model and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model.

## Key numbers

- Linear correlation coefficient C = 0.29(6) between δR(Z,N) and δR(Z,N)_def, with reduced χ^2 = 0.8
- Weighted mean of corrected δR(Z,N)^corr = -8×10^{-4} fm with weighted standard deviation of 5×10^{-3} fm
- δR(Z,N)_def for ^98Sr at N=60 shape transition region = 0.088(14) fm
- Deformation parameters for the four nuclei in the Sr chain: ^98Sr β_2 = 0.40(1), ^96Sr β_2 = 0.175(6), ^96Kr β_2 = 0.25(3), ^94Kr β_2 = 0.19(1)
- B(E2) values can be predicted from the four-point relation within ±25% accuracy
- 8 of 10 cases with A < 60 show deviations from the linear trend by more than 1σ (where 1σ = 0.0040 fm)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous charge radius relations δR_{ip-jn}(Z,N) (Sun 2014, Sun 2015) by quantifying the contribution of quadrupole deformation to the four-point charge radius difference. The correlation provides a consistency check between charge radius and deformation data, analogous to how the Garvey-Kelson relations (Garvey and Kelson 1966) work for nuclear binding energies. The improved charge radius formula enables reliable short-range extrapolations of charge radii across the nuclear chart, and the generalization to B(E2) and mean lifetime τ of the first 2^+ state connects to independent work on four-point B(E2) relations (PhysRevC.12.2038). The authors note that a consistent description of radius and transition probabilities is important for interpreting experimental results, citing recent work on ^111-129Cd (Yordanov 2016).
