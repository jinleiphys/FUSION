# Three-body continuum states on a Lagrange mesh

> Three-body continuum states are investigated with the hyperspherical method on a Lagrange mesh. The $R$-matrix theory is used to treat the asymptotic behaviour of scattering wave functions. The formalism is developed for neutral as well as for charged systems. We point out some specificities of continuum states in the hyperspherical method. The collision matrix can be determined with a good accuracy by using propagation techniques. The method is applied to the $^6$He (=$α$+n+n) and $^6$Be (=$α$+p+p) systems, as well as to $^{14}$Be (=$^{12}$Be+n+n). For $^6$He, we essentially recover results of the literature. Application to $^{14}$Be suggests the existence of an excited $2^+$ state below threshold. The calculated B(E2) value should make this state observable with Coulomb excitation experiments.

## Key claim
The paper presents a method for computing three-body continuum states by combining the hyperspherical harmonic method with a Lagrange-mesh basis and R-matrix propagation, but the accuracy of the collision matrix depends on using propagation techniques to handle the very long range of the coupling potentials (up to thousands of femtometers) that arise in the hyperspherical formalism.

## Method
The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using a hyperspherical harmonic expansion, where the hyperradial wave functions are expanded over a Lagrange-mesh basis in an internal region (radius a₀ ≈ 20–40 fm). The R-matrix is computed at a₀ and then propagated numerically using the Numerov algorithm to a larger channel radius a (≈ 250 fm) where the asymptotic Coulomb and centrifugal forms are valid, using an analytical asymptotic expansion of the nuclear potential. The collision matrix is extracted from the propagated R-matrix, and for charged systems the nuclear contribution is separated by subtracting a pure Coulomb calculation.

## Key numbers
- For ⁶He, the α–n potential is renormalized by a factor λ = 1.051 to reproduce the experimental ground-state energy of –0.97 MeV and an r.m.s. radius of 2.44 fm.
- The 2⁺ resonance in ⁶He is found at a theoretical energy of about 0.2 MeV (experimental value 0.82 MeV); after readjusting λ to 1.020, the correct energy is reproduced.
- For ⁶Be, the ground state is found at E = 1.26 MeV with a width Γ = 65 keV (experiment: E = 1.37 MeV, Γ = 92 ± 6 keV).
- The 2⁺ state in ⁶Be is predicted near E ≈ 4 MeV with a broad structure, consistent with the experimental state near 3.0 MeV (Γ = 1.16 ± 0.06 MeV).
- For ⁶He with J = 0⁺, the channel radius ρ_max for K = 0, γ = (0,0) is 4370 fm; for K = 4, γ = (0,0) it is 160 fm; for K = 8, γ = (0,0) it is 125 fm.
- Propagation from a₀ = 20 fm to a = 250 fm yields phase-shift stability better than 0.1° at all energies.
- For ⁶He J = 0⁺, K_max = 20 is sufficient for an accuracy of 2° above 4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Lagrange-mesh + hyperspherical harmonic method previously applied to three-body bound states by Descouvemont, Baye, and collaborators (2003) to unbound continuum states. It addresses the long-range coupling problem noted by Thompson et al. (2000) in three-body R-matrix calculations, using propagation techniques adapted from atomic physics (Baluja and Noble, 1995). The method enables calculation of resonances and non-resonant continuum for Borromean nuclei like ⁶He and ¹⁴Be, and predicts a 2⁺ excited state in ¹⁴Be below threshold with a B(E2) value observable in Coulomb excitation experiments.
