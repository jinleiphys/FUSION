# Structure Factors of Neutron Matter at Finite Temperature

> We compute continuum and infinite volume limit extrapolations of the structure factors of neutron matter at finite temperature and density. Using a lattice formulation of leading-order pionless effective field theory, we compute the momentum dependence of the structure factors at finite temperature and at densities beyond the reach of the virial expansion. The Tan contact parameter is computed and the result agrees with the high momentum tail of the vector structure factor. All errors, statistical and systematic, are controlled for. This calculation is a first step towards a model-independent understanding of the linear response of neutron matter at finite temperature, a realm until now little explored.

## Key claim

The authors compute continuum and infinite volume limit extrapolations of the static vector and axial structure factors of neutron matter at finite temperature using leading-order pionless effective field theory, but only at two specific fugacities (z=1.0 and 1.5) and with the approximation that the effective range expansion is truncated at leading order, which likely deviates noticeably from reality at the center-of-mass energies probed in large regions of supernovae.

## Method

The authors use a lattice formulation of leading-order pionless effective field theory with a spatial cubic lattice, employing Monte Carlo methods with auxiliary-field (Hubbard-Stratonovich) transformations to compute the grand-canonical partition function. They take the "hamiltonian limit" where the temporal lattice spacing Δt is taken to zero before the spatial lattice spacing Δx, and perform continuum and infinite volume limit extrapolations at fugacities z=1.0 and 1.5. The coupling constant is tuned to reproduce the neutron-neutron scattering length a=-18.9 fm.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a = -18.9 fm (central value used in calculations)
- Neutron mass: M = 938 MeV
- Fugacities studied: z = 1.0 and 1.5
- Effective range expansion breakdown energy: E* ≈ 1.6 MeV (center-of-mass energy where the leading-order approximation likely deviates noticeably)
- Effective range parameter: r = 2.75 fm
- Constant c1 in coupling tuning relation: c1^(-1) = 5.14435...
- All errors (statistical and systematic) add up to a few percent

## Context

This work extends beyond the virial expansion regime for neutron matter structure factors, which had previously been limited to lower densities. The calculation provides a first step toward model-independent understanding of linear response of neutron matter at finite temperature, a regime relevant for supernova and neutron star merger physics. The authors note that prior approximations in supernova simulations treated matter as a unitary gas or used virial expansions, and this work enables a path toward definitive calculations with more realistic nuclear forces encompassing the temperatures and densities relevant to supernova physics, as reviewed by Burrows (2004).
