# Note on Many-Quark Model with su(4) Algebraic Structure

> In a many-quark model developed in our previous paper where two-body color pairing and particle-hole type interactions are active, the exact energy eigenstates are re-formed with physically clearer expressions than those derived in our previous paper. By using the re-formed energy eigenstates, two types of the eigenstates in which the pairing correlation and the quark triplet formation separately appear definitely, are unified and this model can be treated for both the strong color correlations and the quark triplet formations.

## Key claim

The paper re-forms the exact energy eigenstates of a previously developed many-quark model (the modified Bonn model) with color pairing and particle-hole interactions into physically clearer expressions, and shows that two previously separate types of eigenstates, those dominated by pairing correlation and those dominated by quark triplet formation, can be unified into a single orthogonal set, provided the condition \(l+s+r \leq \omega\) is satisfied.

## Method

The authors work within a Schwinger boson representation of the \(su(4)\) algebraic structure underlying the Bonn model, using eight kinds of bosons. They construct a new orthogonal set \(\{\dket{lsr\omega}\}\) by acting with quark-triplet creation operators \(\hat{B}^*\), quark-pair creation operators \(\hat{S}^3\), single-quark creation operators \(\hat{q}^1\), and the boson operator \(\hat{b}^*\) on the vacuum, and then extend this to a complete set using raising operators of the \(su(3)\) and \(su(1,1)\) sub-algebras. The energy eigenvalues for the Hamiltonians \(\hat{H}\) and \(\hat{H}_m = \hat{H} + \chi \hat{\mathbf{Q}}^2\) are derived from the Casimir operators of the \(su(4)\), \(su(3)\), and \(su(1,1)\) algebras.

## Key numbers

* The energy eigenvalue for the Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}\) is \(E_{lsr\omega} = -[2l(2(\omega - s - 2r - l) + 1) + 2r(2(\omega - r) + 3)]\), where \(l\), \(s\), \(r\), and \(\omega\) are quantum numbers.
* The additional term from the modification \(\chi \hat{\mathbf{Q}}^2\) is \(F_{ls} = 2l(l+1) + \frac{2}{3}(l+2s)(l+2s+3)\), with \(\chi\) a real parameter.
* The state \(\dket{lsr\omega}\) is an eigenstate of \(\hat{T}_0\) with eigenvalue \(\omega + 2\).
* The quantum numbers obey the condition \(l + s + r \leq \omega\).
* The operator \(\hat{B}^*\) changes the total quark number by 3, \(\hat{S}^3\) by 2, and \(\hat{q}^1\) by 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Tsue et al., 2009, referred to as Paper I) of the Bonn model, which has an \(su(4)\) algebraic structure and was originally developed to describe quark triplet formation from two-body interactions (Bonn model, known from earlier literature). The re-formed orthogonal set unifies the two separate classification schemes (pairing-type and triplet-type eigenstates) presented in Paper I, and the method enables future treatment of both strong color correlations and quark triplet formations within a single framework. The authors also note that the degeneracy with respect to energy eigenvalues could be dissociated using techniques from nuclear collective rotational motion theory.
