# Charged pion electroproduction above the resonance region

> [Background] Above the nucleon resonance region, the $N(e,e'π^\pm)N'$ data cannot be explained by conventional hadronic models. For example, the observed magnitude of the transverse cross section is significantly underestimated in a framework with Reggeized background amplitudes. [Purpose] Develop a phenomenological framework for the $N(e,e'π^\pm)N'$ reaction at high invariant mass $W$ and deep photon virtuality $Q^2$. [Method] Building on the work of Kaskulov and Mosel, a gauged pion-exchange current is introduced with a running cutoff energy for the proton electromagnetic transition form factor. A new transition form factor is proposed. It respects the correct on-shell limit, has a simple physical interpretation and reduces the number of free parameters by one. [Results] A study of the $W$ dependence of the $N(e,e'π^\pm)N'$ lends support for the newly proposed transition form factor. In addition, an improved description of the separated and unseparated cross sections at $-t \lesssim 0.5 \;\text{GeV}^2$ is obtained. The predictions overshoot the measured unseparated cross sections for $-t > 0.5 \;\text{GeV}^2$. Introducing a strong hadronic form factor in the Reggeized background amplitudes brings the calculations considerably closer to the high $-t$ data. [Conclusions] Hadronic models corrected for resonance/parton duality describe the separated pion electroproduction cross sections above the resonance region reasonably well at low $-t$. In order to validate the applicability of these models at high $-t$, separated cross sections are needed. These are expected to provide a more profound insight into the relevant reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

Hadronic models that incorporate resonance/parton duality can describe separated pion electroproduction cross sections above the resonance region reasonably well at low momentum transfer (-t less than about 0.5 GeV^2), but the model's predictions overshoot measured unseparated cross sections at higher -t, requiring additional hadronic form factors in the Reggeized background amplitudes to bring calculations closer to data.

## Method

The authors build on the work of Kaskulov and Mosel by introducing a gauged pion-exchange current with a running cutoff energy for the proton electromagnetic transition form factor, and propose a new transition form factor that respects the correct on-shell limit while reducing the number of free parameters by one. They study the W dependence of the N(e,e'pi+-)N' reaction and compare model calculations (KM model, cKM model, and their own VR model) against separated and unseparated cross section data from CEA and DESY experiments. A strong hadronic form factor is introduced in the Reggeized background amplitudes to improve agreement at high -t.

## Key numbers

- The model provides an improved description of separated and unseparated cross sections at -t less than or equal to 0.5 GeV^2
- Predictions overshoot measured unseparated cross sections for -t greater than 0.5 GeV^2
- Data shown at W = 2.15 GeV and theta_pi = 3.10 degrees for the CEA dataset
- DESY data for n(e,e'pi-)p and p(e,e'pi+)n reactions are compared across -t values up to approximately 0.4 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Reggeized background amplitude framework of Kaskulov and Mosel for charged pion electroproduction above the nucleon resonance region, where conventional hadronic models significantly underestimate the transverse cross section. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between hadronic model predictions and data at high invariant mass W and deep photon virtuality Q^2. The results enable future validation of these models at high -t, but the authors note that separated cross sections at high -t are needed to provide more profound insight into the relevant reaction mechanisms.
