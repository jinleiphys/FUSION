# Tidal Deformability Doppelgangers: Implications of a low-density phase transition in the neutron star equation of state

> Studying the properties of ultra-dense matter is one of the key goals of modern neutron star research. The measurement of the tidal deformability from the inspiral of a binary neutron star merger offers one promising method for constraining the equation of state (EoS) of cold, dense matter. In this work, we report on a new class of EoSs which have significantly different pressures at nuclear densities and large differences in stellar radii, but that predict surprisingly similar tidal deformabilities across the entire range of astrophysically-observed neutron star masses. Using a survey of 5 million piecewise polytropic EoSs, subject to five different sets of nuclear priors, we demonstrate that these "tidal deformability doppelgangers" occur generically. We find that they can differ substantially in the pressure (by up to a factor of 3 at nuclear densities) and in the radius of intermediate-mass neutron stars (by up to 0.5 km), but are observationally indistinguishable in their tidal deformabilities ($ΔΛ< 30$) with the sensitivity of current gravitational wave detectors. We demonstrate that this near-degeneracy in the tidal deformability is a result of allowing for a phase transition at low densities. We show that a combination of input from nuclear theory (e.g., from chiral effective field theory), X-ray observations of neutron star radii, and/or the next generation of gravitational wave detectors will be able to significantly constrain these tidal deformability doppelgangers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that equations of state (EoSs) with significantly different pressures at nuclear densities (by up to a factor of 3) and large differences in stellar radii (up to 0.5 km) can produce nearly identical tidal deformabilities across the entire astrophysically-observed neutron star mass range, making them observationally indistinguishable with current gravitational wave detectors; this degeneracy arises specifically when a phase transition is allowed at low densities between 1 and 2 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors performed a survey of 5 million piecewise polytropic EoSs using five polytropic segments spaced uniformly in log density between nuclear saturation density and 7.4 times saturation density, subject to five different sets of nuclear priors including variations in crust EoS (ap3, ms1b), starting density of the parametrization (1, 1.2, and 1.5 times saturation density), and strength of a Gaussian regularizer on the second logarithmic derivative of pressure. They defined a "doppelganger score" based on the maximum difference in tidal deformability and minimum difference in radius across the astrophysical mass range (1.17 to 2.01 solar masses), and identified pairs of EoSs with scores within 90% of the highest score found.

## Key numbers

- Pressure at nuclear densities differs by up to a factor of 3 between doppelganger EoS pairs
- Radius of intermediate-mass neutron stars differs by up to 0.5 km between doppelganger pairs
- Tidal deformability difference ΔΛ < 30 for doppelganger pairs, making them indistinguishable with current gravitational wave detectors
- For the ap3/ms1b crust pair: fractional pressure difference of 170% and radius difference of 0.72 km at 1.4 solar masses
- For the wff1/ms1 crust pair: fractional pressure difference of 365% and radius difference of 0.48 km at 1.4 solar masses
- Doppelganger populations have average differences in tidal deformability of less than 10
- Doppelganger populations have average radius differences of 0.1-0.4 km
- When crust EoS is enforced to 1.5 times saturation density, doppelgangers have ΔR at 1.4 solar masses less than 0.1 km
- Doppelganger stars have radii of approximately 11-12 km for the baseline sample using ap3 crust

## Context

This work extends the understanding of tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2017) and challenges the standard paradigm that tidal deformability maps robustly to the EoS at supranuclear densities. The finding that EoSs with different pressures at supranuclear densities can produce nearly identical tidal deformabilities is consistent with earlier work by Gamba (2019) showing that changing the crust EoS below 10^14 g/cm^3 can affect radius without significantly changing tidal deformabilities, but extends this to show the effect persists with differences at supranuclear densities. The results imply that next-generation detectors such as Cosmic Explorer, combined with nuclear theory input from chiral effective field theory and X-ray observations from NICER (Miller, 2019; Riley, 2019), will be needed to resolve these degeneracies.
