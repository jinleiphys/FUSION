# Anomalous Transport from Kubo Formulae

> Chiral anomalies have profound impact on the transport properties of relativistic fluids. In four dimensions there are different types of anomalies, pure gauge and mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies. They give rise to two new non-dissipative transport coefficients, the chiral magnetic conductivity and the chiral vortical conductivity. They can be calculated from the microscopic degrees of freedom with the help of Kubo formulae. We review the calculation of the anomalous transport coefficients via Kubo formulae with a particular emphasis on the contribution of the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how Kubo formulae can be used to calculate anomalous transport coefficients (chiral magnetic and chiral vortical conductivities) from microscopic degrees of freedom, with the specific claim that the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly contributes a purely temperature-dependent term to the chiral vortical conductivity that had previously been considered irrelevant for first-order hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors derive Kubo formulae for the chiral magnetic and chiral vortical conductivities from two-point correlators of currents and energy-momentum tensors in relativistic quantum field theory. They then apply these Kubo formulae to two complementary models: a weakly coupled theory of free Weyl fermions (one-loop calculation) and a strongly coupled holographic model using the AdS/CFT correspondence with a mixed gauge-gravitational Chern-Simons term. The calculations are performed using the covariant current formalism with chemical potentials introduced via twisted boundary conditions along the thermal circle.

## Key numbers

* Chiral magnetic conductivity from pure gauge anomaly: proportional to the axial chemical potential, with coefficient fixed by the anomaly coefficient d_{abc}/(2π²) for a single chiral U(1) symmetry
* Chiral vortical conductivity from pure gauge anomaly: proportional to μ², with coefficient fixed by the anomaly polynomial
* Chiral vortical conductivity from mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly: proportional to T², with coefficient determined by b_a/12 where b_a = tr(T_a)_L - tr(T_a)_R
* The mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly contribution to the chiral vortical conductivity gives the same numerical value at weak coupling (free Weyl fermions) and at strong coupling (holographic AdS/CFT calculation)

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on anomalous hydrodynamics by Vilenkin (1979-1980) and the more recent developments by Son and Surowka (2009) who used entropy current arguments to fix pure gauge anomaly contributions. The paper specifically addresses the finding by Landsteiner, Megias, and Pena-Benitez (2011) that the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly contributes to the chiral vortical effect, which had been missed in earlier hydrodynamic treatments. The Kubo formula approach enables calculation of these coefficients from first principles, though it requires a specific microscopic model, unlike the entropy current method which provides a non-renormalization theorem only for pure gauge anomalies.
