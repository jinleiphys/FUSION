# Off shell behaviour of the in medium nucleon-nucleon cross section

> The properties of nucleon-nucleon scattering inside dense nuclear matter are investigated. We use the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model to determine on-shell and half off-shell in-medium transition amplitudes and cross sections. At finite densities the on-shell cross sections are generally suppressed. This reduction is, however, less pronounced than found in previous works. In the case that the outgoing momenta are allowed to be off energy shell the amplitudes show a strong variation with momentum. This description allows to determine in-medium cross sections beyond the quasi-particle approximation accounting thereby for the finite width which nucleons acquire in the dense nuclear medium. For reasonable choices of the in-medium nuclear spectral width, i.e. $Γ\leq 40$ MeV, the resulting total cross sections are, however, reduced by not more than about 25% compared to the on-shell values. Off-shell effect are generally more pronounced at large nuclear matter densities.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is less suppressed at finite densities than found in previous relativistic calculations by Li and Machleidt (1993), and when outgoing momenta are allowed off energy shell, the resulting total cross sections are reduced by not more than about 25% compared to on-shell values for spectral widths up to 40 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model with the Bonn A potential to solve the Thompson equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) in the two-nucleon center-of-mass frame. They computed on-shell and half-off-shell in-medium transition amplitudes and cross sections for neutron-proton and proton-proton channels at various nuclear matter densities, applying an angle-averaged Pauli operator and a refined projection scheme to minimize on-shell ambiguities in the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- At a relative center-of-mass momentum of 342 MeV (equivalent to 250 MeV laboratory energy in vacuum), the differential np cross section at 3 times saturation density (rho_0 = 0.1625 fm^-3) becomes highly anisotropic and dominated by p-wave contributions, with partial waves up to J <= 1 sufficient to approximate the full result.
- The in-medium effective masses used were M* = 766.6, 646.7, 433.6, and 310.1 MeV at Fermi momenta k_F = 1.1, 1.34, 1.7, and 1.9 fm^-1, corresponding to densities of approximately 0.5, 1, 2, and 3 times saturation density.
- For spectral widths Gamma <= 40 MeV, the total cross sections are reduced by not more than about 25% compared to on-shell values.
- At high laboratory energies (>= 200 MeV), both np and pp in-medium cross sections reach asymptotic values around 15-20 mb across the density range studied.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic in-medium NN scattering studies by Li and Machleidt (1993) and the Tubingen group (1992), finding that the suppression of on-shell cross sections at low energies is less pronounced than those earlier calculations indicated due to proper treatment of the Pauli operator in the optical theorem. The half-off-shell results provide necessary input for generalized transport equations beyond the quasi-particle approximation, such as those recently formulated by Leupold and Cassing (2000), enabling more realistic simulations of heavy ion collisions where nucleons acquire finite spectral widths in dense matter.
