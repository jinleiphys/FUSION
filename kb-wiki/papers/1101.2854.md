# Universal Relations for Identical Bosons from 3-Body Physics

> Systems consisting of identical bosons with a large scattering length satisfy universal relations determined by 2-body physics that are similar to those for fermions with two spin states. They require the momentum distribution to have a large-momentum 1/k^4 tail and the radio-frequency transition rate to have a high-frequency 1/omega^{3/2} tail, both of which are proportional to the 2-body contact. Identical bosons also satisfy additional universal relations that are determined by 3-body physics and involve the 3-body contact, which measures the probability of 3 particles being very close together. The coefficients of the 3-body contact in the 1/k^5 tail of the momentum distribution and in the 1/omega^2 tail of the radio-frequency transition rate are log-periodic functions of k and omega that depend on the Efimov parameter.

## Key claim

Identical bosons with a large scattering length satisfy universal relations determined by 3-body physics, in addition to the 2-body relations known for fermions; the coefficients of the 3-body contact in the momentum distribution tail and the radio-frequency transition rate are log-periodic functions of momentum and frequency that depend on the Efimov parameter.

## Method

The authors used quantum field theory with a zero-range interaction, renormalizing the bare coupling constants to produce the scattering length \(a\) and the Efimov parameter \(\kappa_*\). They derived the operator product expansion for the momentum distribution and the radio-frequency transition rate, matching Wilson coefficients to few-body Green functions calculated by solving the Skorniakov-ter-Martyrosian integral equation numerically. The 2-body and 3-body contacts were defined operationally via derivatives of the total energy with respect to \(a\) and \(\kappa_*\).

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\), where \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\).
- The log-periodic function \(F(k)\) in the \(1/k^5\) tail of the momentum distribution has amplitude \(A = 89.2626\) and phase \(\phi = -0.669064\).
- The 2-body contact for an Efimov trimer in the unitary limit with binding momentum \(\kappa_*\) is \(C_2 = 53.0972~\kappa_*\).
- The universal constant \(f_0\) in the Energy Relation is \(f_0 = -8.42427\).
- The log-periodic function \(G_\textrm{rf}(\omega)\) in the \(1/\omega^2\) tail of the rf transition rate has parameters \(B_1 = 9.23\), \(B_2 = -13.6\), and \(\phi_{\rm rf} = 1.33\).
- The renormalization scale \(\Lambda_*\) differs from \(\kappa_*\) by \(s_0 \ln (\Lambda_*/\kappa_*) = 0.971\) mod \(\pi\).
- The analytic approximation for the function \(H\) in the 3-body coupling is multiplied by the numerical constant \(h_0 = 0.879\).

## Context

This work extends the universal relations originally derived by Shina Tan (2005, 2008) for fermions with two spin states to identical bosons, where the Efimov effect introduces a 3-body contact. It builds on the demonstration by Werner and Castin (2010) that Tan's Energy Relation fails for an Efimov trimer, and provides the correct form including the 3-body contact. The results enable experimental tests of Efimov physics in ultracold bosonic gases using short-time probes such as momentum distribution measurements and radio-frequency spectroscopy, similar to those already applied to fermions with two spin states.
