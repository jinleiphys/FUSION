# Tetraquark mixing framework to explain two light-meson nonets

> In this talk, we summarize our recent works on the tetraquark mixing framework for the two light-meson nonets in the $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ channel, the light nonet [$a_0(980)$, $K_0^*(700)$, $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$] and the heavy nonet [$a_0(1450)$, $K_0^*(1430)$, $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$]. We briefly explain this mixing framework and present various phenomenological signatures to support this picture.

## Key claim

The two light-meson nonets in the \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) channel (the light nonet and the heavy nonet) can be identified as the eigenstates resulting from mixing between two tetraquark types, one constructed from a spin-0 diquark and the other from a spin-1 diquark, rather than being separate, unmixed tetraquark configurations.

## Method

The author reexamined the diquark-antidiquark model and proposed two tetraquark types: \(|000\rangle\) from a spin-0 diquark with color and flavor structure \((\bar{\bm{3}}_c, \bar{\bm{3}}_f)\), and \(|011\rangle\) from a spin-1 diquark with \((\bm{6}_c, \bar{\bm{3}}_f)\). The two types mix through the color-spin interaction \(V_{CS} = v_0 \sum_{i < j} \frac{\lambda_i \cdot \lambda_j J_i\cdot J_j}{m_i m_j}\), and the physical resonances (the two PDG nonets) are identified as the eigenstates that diagonalize the resulting \(2\times 2\) hyperfine mass matrix in each isospin channel. The mixing parameters \(\alpha, \beta\) were extracted from the diagonalization, and predictions for fall-apart decay widths into two pseudoscalars (PP mode) and two vectors (VV mode) were computed.

## Key numbers

- Hyperfine mass for \(a_0(980)\) (light nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -488.5\) MeV; for \(a_0(1450)\) (heavy nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -16.8\) MeV.
- Hyperfine mass for \(K_0^*(700)\) (light nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -592.7\) MeV; for \(K_0^*(1430)\) (heavy nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -26.9\) MeV.
- Hyperfine mass for \(f_0(500)\) (light nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -667.5\) MeV; for \(f_0(1370)\) (heavy nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -29.2\) MeV.
- Hyperfine mass for \(f_0(980)\) (light nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -535.1\) MeV; for \(f_0(1500)\) (heavy nonet): \(\langle V_{CS} \rangle = -20.1\) MeV.
- Mixing parameters for the isovector channel: \(\alpha = 0.8167\), \(\beta = 0.5770\).
- Off-diagonal mixing matrix element in the isovector channel: \(\langle 000| V_{CS} |011 \rangle = -222.3\) MeV.
- Experimental mass gap between \(a_0(1450)\) and \(a_0(980)\): 494 MeV; eigenvalue separation from mixing: 471.7 MeV.
- Ratio of partial widths \(\Gamma[a_0(980)\rightarrow \pi\eta] / \Gamma[a_0(1450)\rightarrow \pi\eta]\): predicted 2.51-2.54, compared to Bugg analysis 2.53 and PDG 2.93-3.9.
- Ratio of partial widths \(\Gamma[a_0(980)\rightarrow K\bar{K}] / \Gamma[a_0(1450)\rightarrow K\bar{K}]\): predicted 0.52-0.89, compared to Bugg analysis 0.62 and PDG 0.61-0.81.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-antidiquark model of Jaffe (1977) by introducing a second tetraquark type from a spin-1 diquark and a mixing framework to explain both the light and heavy scalar nonets listed in the Particle Data Group. The framework provides a unified explanation for the large mass gap (around 500 MeV) between the two nonets, the mass ordering within each nonet, and the fall-apart decay patterns. It enables future tests through QCD sum rule calculations (as already performed for \(a_0(980)\) by Lee and Kim, 2019) and through further experimental measurements of decay widths, particularly for the VV mode in the heavy nonet.
