# A novel formulation of the unintegrated gluon distribution for DIS

> We provide a semi-classical description of the inclusive gluon induced Deep Inelastic Scattering cross section in a way that accounts for the leading powers in both the Regge and Bjorken limits. Our approach thus allows a systematic matching of small and moderate $x_{\rm Bj}$ regimes of gluon proton structure functions. We find a new unintegrated gluon distribution with an explicit dependence on the longitudinal momentum fraction $x$ which entirely spans both the dipole operator and the gluonic Parton Distribution Function. Computing this gauge invariant gluon operator on the lattice could allow to probe the energy dependence of the saturation scale from first principles.

## Key claim

The authors derive a semi-classical description of inclusive gluon-induced Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) that accounts for leading powers in both the Regge and Bjorken limits, yielding a new unintegrated gluon distribution with explicit dependence on the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\). This distribution interpolates between the dipole operator at small \(x\) and the gluonic Parton Distribution Function (PDF) at large \(x\), but the derivation is exact only up to corrections of relative order \(p_\perp/\sqrt{s}\), where \(p_\perp\) is an intrinsic transverse momentum in the proton.

## Method

The authors use a semi-classical approach similar to the shock wave approximation but refrain from assuming zero target longitudinal extent. They perform a gradient expansion around the transverse position of quantum fluctuations, using \(k^-\) as a factorization variable between target fields and quantum fluctuations. The DIS cross section is derived via the optical theorem, expressing the hadronic tensor in terms of scalar propagators that satisfy a Schrodinger equation in the background field \(A^+\), and then expanding around the classical limit where transverse positions are approximately conserved.

## Context

This work extends the shock wave approach of Balitsky (1996) and Kovchegov (1999) by removing the assumption of zero target longitudinal extent, which previously led to large collinear logarithms in NLO corrections to the BFKL, BK, and JIMWLK equations (as noted by Beuf 2014 and Edmond 2015). The new unintegrated gluon distribution, which involves finite-length Wilson lines rather than infinite-length ones, could enable lattice QCD calculations to probe the energy dependence of the saturation scale from first principles, and may provide an alternative to the BK equation that naturally resolves the inconsistency between small-\(x\) and collinear regimes.
