# Toward extracting scattering phase shift from integrated correlation function

> In present work, a relation that connects the integrated correlation function of a trapped two-particle system to infinite volume particles scattering phase shift is derived. It has the potential to provide an alternative approach for extracting two-particle scattering phase shift from integrated correlation function in lattice simulation at small Euclidean time region. Both (i) perturbation calculation of 1+1 dimensional lattice Euclidean field theory model of fermions interacting with a contact interaction and (ii) Monte Carlo simulation of a 1D exactly solvable quantum mechanics model are carried out to test the proposed relation. In contrast to conventional two-step approach of extracting energy levels from temporal correlation function in lattice simulation at large Euclidean time first and then applying Lüscher formula to convert energy levels into scattering phase shifts, we show that the difference of integrated correlation functions between interacting and noninteracting trapped systems converges rapidly to infinite volume limit that is given in terms of scattering phase shifts at small Euclidean time region.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relation connecting the integrated correlation function of a trapped two-particle system to the infinite-volume scattering phase shift, showing that the difference between interacting and noninteracting trapped correlation functions converges rapidly to the infinite-volume limit at small Euclidean time, even with a modest trap size. This provides a potential alternative to the conventional two-step approach of extracting energy levels at large Euclidean time and then applying the Luscher or Busch-Englert-Rzazewski-Wilkens (BERW) formula.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic relation between the integrated two-particle correlation function difference and the scattering phase shift using the quantization condition for trapped systems and the infinite-volume limit. They test this relation through (i) perturbation calculation of a 1+1 dimensional lattice Euclidean field theory model of fermions interacting via a contact interaction, and (ii) Monte Carlo simulation of a 1D exactly solvable quantum mechanics model with a square well potential in a harmonic oscillator trap.

## Key numbers

- The main result is given by Eq. (1): \( C(t) - C_0(t) \xrightarrow[t=-i\tau]{\text{trap}\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1}{\pi} \int_0^\infty d\epsilon \frac{d\delta(\epsilon)}{d\epsilon} e^{-\epsilon\tau} + \frac{\delta(0)}{\pi} \), where \(\tau\) is Euclidean time.
- For a periodic box of size \(L\), the quantization condition function is \(\phi(\epsilon) = \sqrt{2\mu\epsilon} \frac{L}{2}\).
- For a harmonic oscillator trap with angular frequency \(\omega\), the quantization condition function is \(\phi(\epsilon) = -\cot^{-1}\left[ \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon}{2\omega}} \frac{\Gamma(\frac{1}{4} - \frac{\epsilon}{2\omega})}{\Gamma(\frac{3}{4} - \frac{\epsilon}{2\omega})} \right]\).
- For 1D contact interaction, the phase shift at zero energy has the nontrivial value \(\delta(0) = -\frac{\pi}{2}\).

## Context

This work extends the conventional two-step approach (Luscher formula for periodic boxes and BERW formula for harmonic oscillator traps) used in lattice QCD and nuclear physics, which requires extracting discrete energy levels from temporal correlation functions at large Euclidean time. The proposed method is analogous to the virial expansion approach in quantum statistical mechanics, where at high temperature (small \(\beta\)) the second virial coefficient is related to scattering phase shifts. The approach aims to address difficulties in extracting energy levels in large spatial volumes and in baryon sectors where plateau signals are noisy, as noted by Bulava (2019).
