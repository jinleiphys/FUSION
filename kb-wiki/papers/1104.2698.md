# Decay of a scalar $σ$-meson near the critical end-point in the PNJL model

> Properties of a scalar $σ$-meson are investigated in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Polyakov loop. Model analysis of the phase diagram of strong interacting matter is performed. The temperature dependence of the $σ\rightarrowππ$ decay width is studied at the zero chemical potential and near the critical end-point. The calculated strong coupling constant $g_{σππ}$ and the decay width are compared with available experimental data and other model results. Nonthermal enhancement of the total decay width is noted for the $σ$ meson near the critical end-point when the condition $m_σ\geq2m_π$ is broken.

## Key claim

The paper claims that near the critical end-point (CEP) in the PNJL model, the total decay width of the sigma meson into two pions shows a nonthermal enhancement, with maximal values about three times larger than at zero chemical potential, specifically when the kinematic condition \(m_\sigma \geq 2m_\pi\) is broken.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Polyakov loop (PNJL) to compute the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the sigma and pion masses, the coupling strength \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi}\), and the decay width \(\Gamma_{\sigma\rightarrow\pi\pi}\). The decay width is calculated from the triangle diagram for \(\sigma\rightarrow\pi\pi\) to lowest order in \(1/N_c\), including a Bose-Einstein factor \(F_{\pi\pi}\) for the final-state pions, and the phase diagram is obtained from the grand canonical potential in the Hartree approximation.

## Key numbers

- The critical end-point is located at \(T_{CEP} \simeq 0.095\) GeV and \(\mu_{CEP} \simeq 0.32\) GeV.
- At zero temperature and chemical potential, the coupling constant \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi} = 2.1\) GeV and the total decay width \(\Gamma_{\sigma\rightarrow\pi\pi} \approx 190\) MeV.
- The experimental sigma mass from BES is \(m_\sigma = 390^{+60}_{-36}\) MeV with width \(\Gamma_{\sigma\to\pi\pi} = 282_{-50}^{+77}\) MeV; from E791 it is \(m_\sigma = 478^{+24}_{-23}\pm17\) MeV with width \(\Gamma_{\sigma\to\pi\pi} = 324_{-42}^{+40}\pm 21\) MeV.
- At \(\mu = 0\), the sigma-to-pion dissociation temperature is \(T_d^\sigma \approx 210\) MeV, and near the Mott temperature the width reaches \(\Gamma_{\sigma\to\pi\pi} \approx 250\) MeV.
- Near the critical end-point, the maximal decay width is larger than at \(\mu=0\) by a factor of about 3.
- If \(\mu\) changes by \(\Delta\mu \sim 10\) MeV relative to \(\mu_{CEP}\), the temperature at which the width drops to zero changes by about 30 MeV from \(T_{CEP}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in the PNJL model (Pedro et al., Mesons et al.) and earlier NJL model calculations of sigma decay at zero density (Hadron et al.). The results are compared with experimental data from the BES Collaboration (2001) and E791 Collaboration (2003), as well as with other quark-meson model predictions (FGILW03, HZH02) and the linear sigma model (DR01). The findings suggest that nonthermal enhancement of pions from sigma decay near the CEP could serve as a signature of chiral phase transition, though experimental separation of resonance decay pions remains challenging.
