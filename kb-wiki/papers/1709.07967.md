# Isospin Effect in Three-Body Kaonic Clusters

> The kaonic clusters $K^{-}K^{-}p$ and $ppK^{-}$ are described based on the configuration space Faddeev equations for $AAB$ system. The $AB$ interaction is given by isospin-dependent potentials. For this isospin model, we show that the relation $\left\vert E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)\right\vert~<~2\left\vert E_{2}\right\vert$ is satisfied when $E_{2}$ is the binding energy of the $AB$ subsystem and $E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)$ is the three-body binding energy when interaction between identical particles is omitted, $V_{AA}=0$. For the $NN{\bar K}$ system, taking into account weak attraction of $NN$ interaction the relation leads to the evaluation $|E_3|\le 2|E_2|$. The "isospinless model" for the kaonic clusters based on the isospin averaged $N{\bar K}$ potential demonstrates the opposite relation $\left\vert E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)\right\vert~>~2\left\vert E_{2}\right\vert$. The isospin "given charge formalism" is presented for $NN{\bar K}$ cluster. This formalism is related to isospin model by unitary transformation of the isospin basis. An interpretation of the "particle representation" for $NN{\bar K}$ system is proposed.

## Key claim

For kaonic clusters with strongly isospin-dependent $N\bar{K}$ interactions, the three-body binding energy $|E_3|$ is bounded by $|E_3| \le 2|E_2|$ when the $AB$ subsystem has a deep bound state, contradicting the opposite relation found in bosonic-like systems and in models using isospin-averaged potentials.

## Method

The authors solved configuration-space Faddeev equations for $AAB$ three-body systems (two identical particles) using $s$-wave spin/isospin-dependent potentials. They employed the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) and simulating Hyodo-Weise (sHW) effective $N\bar{K}$ potentials, the Malfliet-Tjon MT I-III potential for $NN$ interaction, and a repulsive $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ potential. The isospin formalism was compared with an "isospinless" averaged-potential model, and a "given charge formalism" was developed via unitary transformation of the isospin basis.

## Key numbers

- For $ppK^-$ with AY potential and $V_{NN}=0$: $E_3 = -42.9$ MeV, $E_2 = -30.30$ MeV, giving $\delta = 2E_2 - E_3 = -17.6$ MeV (satisfying $|E_3| < 2|E_2|$)
- For $ppK^-$ with AY potential and MT I-III $NN$ potential: $E_3 = -46.0$ MeV, $\delta = -14.6$ MeV
- For $ppK^-$ with sHW potential and $V_{NN}=0$: $E_3 = -17.1$ MeV, $E_2 = -11.16$ MeV, $\delta = -5.20$ MeV
- For $ppK^-$ with sHW potential and MT I-III: $E_3 = -21.0$ MeV, $\delta = -1.3$ MeV
- For $K^-K^-p$ with AY potential and $V_{\bar{K}\bar{K}}=0$: $E_3 = -35.2$ MeV, $E_2 = -30.30$ MeV, $\delta = -25.3$ MeV
- For $K^-K^-p$ with sHW potential and $V_{\bar{K}\bar{K}}=0$: $E_3 = -12.2$ MeV, $E_2 = -11.16$ MeV, $\delta = -10.1$ MeV
- AY $N\bar{K}$ potential parameters: $V_0^s = -595.0$ MeV, $V_0^t = -175.0$ MeV, $b = 0.66$ fm
- sHW $N\bar{K}$ potential parameters: $V_0^s = -908.0$ MeV, $V_0^t = -415.0$ MeV, $b = 0.47$ fm

## Context

This work extends the study of kaonic clusters $NN\bar{K}$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$, which have been debated due to significant disagreement between theoretical binding energies and experimental data (G 2016). The authors show that "AY-like" models (Akaishi and Yamazaki 2002, 2007) cannot produce the "deeply bound" $ppK^-$ state above 60 MeV, contrary to experimental claims (A 2005, Y 2010, I 2015). The isospin-dependent relation $|E_3| < 2|E_2|$ contrasts with the bosonic-like relation $|E_3| > 2|E_2|$ found for systems with isospin-averaged potentials used in prior work (O, Hyodo and Weise 2008, 2009, W 2009). The "given charge formalism" provides a connection to the "particle representation" approach (R 2016, D 2015, S 2017).
