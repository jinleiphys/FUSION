# On the tensor formulation of effective vector Lagrangians and duality transformations

> Using two different methods inspired by duality transformations we present the equivalence between effective Lagrangians for massive vector mesons using a vector field and an antisymmetric tensor field. This completes the list of explicit field transformations between the various effective Lagrangian methods to describe massive vector and axial vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper proves that effective Lagrangians for massive vector mesons written in terms of a vector field and those written in terms of an antisymmetric tensor field are equivalent at the classical level in the sense of the path integral, provided that the vector field satisfies a transversality constraint and that the equivalence holds only for massive vector fields where the mass acts as an infrared cutoff.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral-invariant Lagrangian for a massive vector field \(V_\mu\) interacting with pseudoscalar mesons and external sources. They introduce a duality transformation \(V_\mu = \frac{1}{m} D^\lambda H_{\lambda\mu}\) (or an alternative with a Levi-Civita tensor) and rewrite the path integral over \(V_\mu\) as one over the tensor field \(H_{\mu\nu}\). By adding an auxiliary tensor field and performing two successive orthogonal transformations (first to diagonalize the kinetic term, then to diagonalize the mass term), they eliminate unphysical degrees of freedom and obtain a Lagrangian for a physical massive tensor field. An alternative first-order formalism is also presented, treating \(V_{\mu\nu}\) and \(V_\mu\) as independent fields and integrating out \(V_\mu\) via a Gaussian integral.

## Key numbers

* The equivalence relation between the vector Lagrangian \({\cal L}_V\) and the tensor Lagrangian \({\cal L}_T\) is given by \({\cal L}_T \equiv {\cal L}_V - \frac{f_V^2}{8}\langle f^{\mu\nu}_+ f_{\mu\nu}^+ \rangle + \frac{g_V^2}{8}\langle [u^\mu, u^\nu][u_\mu, u_\nu] \rangle - i\frac{f_V g_V}{4}\langle f_{\mu\nu}^+ [u^\mu, u^\nu] \rangle\), where \(f_V\) and \(g_V\) are couplings from the original vector Lagrangian.
* The couplings in the tensor Lagrangian relate to the vector couplings as \(F_V = -m f_V\) and \(G_V = -m g_V\), where \(m\) is the vector meson mass.
* The vector meson contributions to the low-energy constants of \(O(p^4)\) chiral perturbation theory are: \(H_1^V = -f_V^2/8\), \(L_{10}^V = -f_V^2/4\), \(L_1^V = g_V^2/8\), \(L_2^V = g_V^2/4\), \(L_3^V = -3g_V^2/4\), and \(L_9^V = f_V g_V/2\).

## Context

This work extends the program of establishing equivalence between different effective Lagrangian formulations for massive vector mesons, following earlier attempts in Ecker et al. (1989) (cited as EQUIV) for the non-anomalous sector and in Prades and Pallante (cited as PP) for the anomalous sector. The results automatically reproduce the constraints on local terms that were previously derived from QCD dispersion relations in Ecker et al. (1989). The paper enables a systematic translation between vector and tensor representations for any interaction term, including those with higher derivatives or quark masses, and can be extended to axial vector mesons and the anomalous parity sector.
