# Relativistic effect of spin and pseudospin symmetries

> Dirac Hamiltonian is scaled in the atomic units $\hbar =m=1$, which allows us to take the non-relativistic limit by setting the Compton wavelength $% λ\rightarrow 0 $. The evolutions of the spin and pseudospin symmetries towards the non-relativistic limit are investigated by solving the Dirac equation with the parameter $λ$. With $λ$ transformation from the original Compton wavelength to 0, the spin splittings decrease monotonously in all spin doublets, and the pseudospin splittings increase in several pseudospin doublets, no change, or even reduce in several other pseudospin doublets. The various energy splitting behaviors of both the spin and pseudospin doublets with $λ$ are well explained by the perturbation calculations of Dirac Hamiltonian in the present units. It indicates that the origin of spin symmetry is entirely due to the relativistic effect, while the origin of pseudospin symmetry cannot be uniquely attributed to the relativistic effect.

## Key claim
The origin of spin symmetry is entirely due to the relativistic effect, while the origin of pseudospin symmetry cannot be uniquely attributed to the relativistic effect; the quantum number of single-particle states and the shape of potential make important influence on pseudospin symmetry.

## Method
The authors scaled the Dirac Hamiltonian in atomic units (ħ=m=1) and treated the Compton wavelength λ as a continuous parameter, solving the Dirac equation for a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters fitted to relativistic mean-field calculations for ²⁰⁸Pb. They computed single-particle energies and energy splittings for spin and pseudospin doublets as λ was varied from its original value λ₀ down to zero (the non-relativistic limit), and performed perturbation expansions of the Dirac Hamiltonian up to order 1/m³ to decompose the contributions of individual terms.

## Key numbers
- Spin-orbit splittings for all spin doublets are "almost reduced to zero" when λ/λ₀ is reduced to 0.1.
- For λ/λ₀=0.001, the energies of spin unaligned and aligned states degenerate to the non-relativistic result (e.g., 1p₃/₂ and 1p₁/₂ both reach -56.332 MeV, matching the Schrödinger result).
- The 1g₉/₂ state energy is -27.921 MeV at λ/λ₀=1 and -36.347 MeV at λ/λ₀=0.001; the 1g₇/₂ state energy is -24.701 MeV at λ/λ₀=1 and -36.347 MeV at λ/λ₀=0.001.
- The 1i₁₃/₂ state energy is -8.942 MeV at λ/λ₀=1 and -19.263 MeV at λ/λ₀=0.001; the 1i₁₁/₂ state energy is -3.361 MeV at λ/λ₀=1 and -19.263 MeV at λ/λ₀=0.001.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of spin and pseudospin symmetries in nuclear physics by directly probing their relativistic origins through a continuous parameter λ that interpolates between relativistic and non-relativistic regimes. It builds on the recognition by Ginocchio (1997) of the relativistic feature of pseudospin symmetry and the condition for exact pseudospin symmetry by Meng et al. (1998), and it supports the claim by Liang et al. (2011) that spin splitting can be treated as a perturbation while pseudospin splitting cannot. The results enable future studies to separate relativistic from non-relativistic contributions in nuclear structure phenomena such as deformation, superdeformation, and identical bands.
