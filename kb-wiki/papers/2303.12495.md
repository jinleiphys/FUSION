# Precise determination of quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters of the $sd$-shell nucleus, $^{28}$Si

> Quasi-elastic (QEL) scattering measurements have been performed using $^{28}$Si projectile off a $^{90}$Zr target at energies around the Coulomb barrier. A Bayesian analysis within the framework of coupled channels (CC) calculations is performed in a large parameter space of quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations ($β_{2}$ and $β_{4}$) of $^{28}$Si. Our results clearly show that $^{28}$Si is an oblate shaped nucleus with $β_{2}$=-$0.38 \pm 0.01$ which is in excellent agreement with electromagnetic probes. A precise value of hexadecapole deformation for $^{28}$Si, $β_{4}$=+$0.03 \pm 0.01$, along with a consistent value of quadrupole deformation has now been determined for the first time using QEL scattering. A remarkable agreement between the experimental $β_{4}$ value of $^{28}$Si and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock based calculations is obtained. The QEL results obtained previously for $^{24}$Mg (prolate) and the present result for $^{28}$Si (oblate) hereby affirm the strong sensitivity of the quasi-elastic scattering to ground state deformations, thus reinforcing its suitability as a potential probe for rare exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that quasi-elastic scattering measurements, combined with a Bayesian analysis of coupled channels calculations, can determine both the quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters of the sd-shell nucleus ²⁸Si with high precision. The key hedge is that the extracted hexadecapole deformation value of β₄ = +0.03 ± 0.01 is the first precise determination using this method, and the result depends on the specific choice of including rotational couplings of ²⁸Si within the coupled channels framework.

## Method

Quasi-elastic scattering measurements were performed using a ²⁸Si beam on a ⁹⁰Zr target at beam energies from 70 to 102 MeV in 2-MeV steps, using four silicon surface barrier detectors placed at backward angles (140.0°, 149.9°, 159.8°, and 170.0°). Coupled channels calculations were carried out using a modified version of the CCFULL code, including vibrational couplings of the ⁹⁰Zr target (2⁺ state at 2.18 MeV and 3⁻ state at 2.75 MeV) and rotational couplings of the ²⁸Si projectile (0⁺, 2⁺, and 4⁺ states) within a rigid rotor model. A Bayesian analysis with a Markov-Chain Monte Carlo framework was applied to the χ² distribution obtained by comparing the experimental quasi-elastic excitation function with calculations across a parameter space of β₂ from -0.5 to +0.5 and β₄ from -0.4 to +0.4, each with a step size of 0.01.

## Key numbers

- β₂ = -0.38 ± 0.01 for ²⁸Si (oblate shape), determined from Bayesian analysis of quasi-elastic scattering data
- β₄ = +0.03 ± 0.01 for ²⁸Si, determined from the same analysis
- The correlation between β₂ and β₄ was found to be approximately +0.138
- The χ² value for the oblate shape minimum was approximately four times smaller than that for the prolate shape minimum
- Beam energies were used in the range of 70 to 102 MeV in steps of 2 MeV
- The effective energy range used for χ² calculation was 64 to 75 MeV

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to ²⁴Mg (a prolate sd-shell nucleus) by Gupta et al. (2020) to determine hexadecapole deformation via quasi-elastic scattering. The results contradict many earlier measurements that showed a wide variation in β₂ for ²⁸Si (from -0.34 to -0.55 for oblate, and some reporting prolate values from +0.36 to +0.45), and a large range for β₄ (from +0.08 to +0.33). The β₂ value shows excellent agreement with electromagnetic probes such as Coulomb excitation and electron scattering, while the β₄ value shows remarkable agreement with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the SLy4, SV-min, and SV-bas parameter sets. This work enables the use of quasi-elastic scattering as a potential probe for determining ground state deformations of rare exotic nuclei at radioactive ion beam facilities, where beam intensity is a primary concern.
