# Heavy Quark Energy Loss in Nuclear Medium

> Multiple scattering, modified fragmentation functions and radiative energy loss of a heavy quark propagating in a nuclear medium are investigated in perturbative QCD. Because of the quark mass dependence of the gluon formation time, the medium size dependence of heavy quark energy loss is found to change from a linear to a quadratic form when the initial energy and momentum scale are increased relative to the quark mass. The radiative energy loss is also significantly suppressed relative to a light quark due to the suppression of collinear gluon emission by a heavy quark.

# Heavy Quark Energy Loss in Nuclear Medium

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss of a heavy quark propagating in a nuclear medium is significantly suppressed relative to a light quark, and its dependence on nuclear medium size changes from linear to quadratic as the heavy quark's initial energy or momentum scale increases relative to its mass, due to the quark mass dependence of the gluon formation time and the "dead-cone" effect suppressing collinear gluon emission.

## Method

The authors used perturbative QCD with generalized factorization of twist-four parton matrix elements to compute the medium-modified fragmentation function and radiative energy loss of a heavy quark in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus. They evaluated double parton scattering contributions from 23 cut diagrams, with the dominant contribution coming from the central cut diagram, and assumed a factorized form of the twist-four quark-gluon correction function to obtain analytic expressions for the energy loss.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set at M = 1.5 GeV in the numerical calculation.
- For small quark energy (large x_B) or small Q^2, the heavy quark energy loss is linear in nuclear size R_A: ⟨Δz_g^Q⟩ ∼ C_A (C̃ α_s^2 / N_c) (x_B / x_A Q^2).
- For large quark energy (small x_B) or large Q^2, the heavy quark energy loss has a quadratic dependence on nuclear size: ⟨Δz_g^Q⟩ ∼ C_A (C̃ α_s^2 / N_c) (x_B / x_A^2 Q^2).
- The suppression factor for gluon radiation from a heavy quark relative to a light quark due to the "dead-cone" effect is f_{Q/q} = [ℓ_T^2 / (ℓ_T^2 + z^2 M^2)]^4 = [1 + θ_0^2/θ^2]^{-4}, where θ_0 = M/q^- and θ = ℓ_T/q^- z.

## Context

This work extends the twist-expansion approach for light quark energy loss in nuclear media (Guo and Wang, 2000) to heavy quarks, and is consistent with results reported by Gyulassy and collaborators (2003) during the completion of this study. The findings explain the lack of significant heavy quark energy loss observed in PHENIX measurements of single electron spectra from charm production in Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 GeV, and enable predictions for heavy quark suppression in heavy ion collisions that can be tested at RHIC and LHC.
