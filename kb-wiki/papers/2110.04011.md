# Bayesian parameter estimation in $χ$EFT using Hamiltonian Monte Carlo

> The number of low-energy constants (LECs) in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) grows rapidly with increasing chiral order, necessitating the use of Markov chain Monte Carlo techniques for sampling their posterior probability density function. For this we introduce a Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm and sample the LEC posterior up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the two-nucleon sector of $χ$EFT. We find that the sampling efficiency of HMC is three to six times higher compared to an affine-invariant sampling algorithm. We analyze the empirical coverage probability and validate that the NNLO model yields predictions for two-nucleon scattering data with largely reliable credible intervals, provided that one ignores the leading order EFT expansion parameter when inferring the variance of the truncation error. We also find that the NNLO truncation error dominates the error budget.

## Key claim

The authors introduce Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC) to sample the posterior probability density function of low-energy constants (LECs) in chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the two-nucleon sector, finding that the NNLO truncation error dominates the error budget and that the model yields reliable credible intervals for two-nucleon scattering data only when one ignores the leading order EFT expansion parameter when inferring the variance of the truncation error.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian parameter estimation for LECs in chiral effective field theory up to NNLO, using a Hamiltonian Monte Carlo algorithm to sample the posterior distribution conditioned on two-thirds of the Granada 2013 nucleon-nucleon scattering database (4366 training data points). They employed a statistical model for the EFT truncation error following Wesolowski (2019), with a Gaussian prior for the expansion coefficients and a diagonal covariance matrix for the truncation error, and validated their results against 2018 held-out data points.

## Key numbers

- HMC sampling efficiency is 3.6 to 6.4 times higher than the affine-invariant emcee algorithm: speedup factor S = 6.0 at LO, 6.4 at NLO, and greater than 3.6 at NNLO
- Integrated autocorrelation time tau for HMC chains: 0.58 at LO, 0.78 at NLO, and 1.2 at NNLO, compared to 34, 158, and greater than 126 for emcee respectively
- RMS values of EFT expansion coefficients: cbar_nu=0 = 1.17 at LO, cbar_nu=2 = 4.95 at NLO, cbar_nu=3 = 2.84 at NNLO; cumulative RMS up to NNLO is cbar_0...3 = 2.72
- HMC acceptance rates of 99% at all chiral orders, with step lengths epsilon* of 0.1 (LO), 0.09 (NLO), and 0.08 (NNLO)
- Training data set contains 4366 data points; validation data set contains 2018 data points

## Context

This work extends Bayesian parameter estimation in chiral effective field theory by introducing HMC as a more efficient alternative to the affine-invariant sampling algorithm emcee, which was previously used for LEC inference. The authors build on the statistical model for EFT truncation error developed by Furnstahl (2015) and Wesolowski (2019), and incorporate prior information on pion-nucleon LECs from a Roy-Steiner analysis by Siemens (2017). The approach enables reliable uncertainty quantification for nucleon-nucleon scattering predictions up to NNLO and provides a framework for future applications to higher chiral orders and more complex nuclear systems.
