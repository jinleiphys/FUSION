# Nucleon form factors from a covariant quark core: limits in their description

> In treating the relativistic three-quark problem, a dressed-quark propagator parameterization is used which is compatible with recent lattice data and pion observables. Furthermore two-quark correlations are modeled as a series of quark loops in the scalar and axialvector channel. The resulting reduced Faddeev equations are solved for nucleon and delta. Nucleon electromagnetic form factors are calculated in a fully covariant and gauge--invariant scheme. Whereas the proton electric form factor $G_E$ and the nucleon magnetic moments are described correctly, the neutron electric form factor and the ratio $G_E/G_M$ for the proton appear to be quenched. The influence of vector mesons on the form factors is investigated which amounts to a 25 percent modification of the electromagnetic proton radii within this framework.

## Key claim

The covariant quark-diquark core model, while correctly describing the proton electric form factor and nucleon magnetic moments, produces a quenched neutron electric form factor and a quenched ratio \(G_E/G_M\) for the proton, indicating that a pure quark core description reaches its limits and that additional contributions (such as from the pion cloud) are necessary.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic Faddeev equations for the nucleon and delta using a dressed-quark propagator parameterization compatible with lattice data and pion observables, with two-quark correlations modeled as a series of quark loops in the scalar and axialvector channels. The resulting separable \(t\)-matrix was used to construct quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations, and nucleon electromagnetic form factors were calculated in a fully covariant and gauge-invariant scheme by coupling the photon to all elements of the Faddeev kernel. Five parameter sets were tuned to reproduce the physical nucleon and delta masses (940 MeV and 1230 MeV), leaving the axialvector diquark width as the single free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass \(M_n = 0.94\) GeV and delta mass \(M_\Delta = 1.23\) GeV were used to fix the model parameters.
- Diquark masses across the five parameter sets: scalar diquark masses \(m_{0^+}\) range from 0.75 GeV to 0.86 GeV; axialvector diquark masses \(m_{1^+}\) range from 0.87 GeV to 0.92 GeV.
- The influence of vector mesons on the form factors amounts to a 25% modification of the electromagnetic proton radii.
- Form factors were calculated up to momentum transfers of \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The quark propagator mass scale is \(\lambda = 0.566\) GeV, with parameters \(\bar m = 0.00897\), \(b_0 = 0.131\), \(b_1 = 2.90\), \(b_2 = 0.603\), \(b_3 = 0.185\).

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-diquark models by Oettel et al. (2000) and Bloch et al. (1999) by incorporating a dressed-quark propagator constrained by lattice data and pion observables, and by enforcing manifest electromagnetic gauge invariance through the gauging formalism of Kvinikhidze and Oettel (1999, 2000). The results show that a pure quark core cannot fully describe the neutron electric form factor or the proton \(G_E/G_M\) ratio, suggesting that pionic corrections (estimated to give at least a \(-200\) MeV nucleon mass shift by Hecht et al. (2002) and Oettel et al. (2002)) are essential for a complete description.
