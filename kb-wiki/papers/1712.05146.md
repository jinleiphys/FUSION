# On the spreading width of the Isobaric Analog Resonances

> A description of the spreading width of the Isobaric Analog Resonances in medium-heavy mass spherical nuclei is proposed within a combined approach. This latter consists in incorporating the "Coulomb description" of isospin-forbidden processes into the newly developed particle-hole dispersive optical model. Within this approach, the observed spreading width of the resonances based on the 208,209Pb parent-nuclei ground state is quantitatively estimated without the use of specific adjustable parameters.

# On the spreading width of the Isobaric Analog Resonances

## Key claim
The observed spreading width of Isobaric Analog Resonances (IARs) in medium-heavy mass spherical nuclei can be quantitatively estimated without specific adjustable parameters by incorporating the "Coulomb description" of isospin-forbidden processes into the particle-hole dispersive optical model (PHDOM), provided the distant low-energy tail of the Isovector Giant Monopole Resonance in the beta-minus channel is correctly described.

## Method
The authors combine the "Coulomb description" of isospin-forbidden processes, which relates the IAR total width to the Coulomb strength function at the IAR energy, with the particle-hole dispersive optical model (PHDOM) for describing high-energy charge-exchange monopole excitations. The PHDOM extends continuum-RPA by including a phenomenological energy-averaged particle-hole self-energy term with imaginary and real (dispersive) parts to account for the spreading effect. The IAR spreading width is obtained by subtracting the calculated proton-escape width from the total width, both derived from the corresponding components of the Coulomb strength function evaluated at the IAR energy.

## Context
This work extends the earlier approach of GRU (2010), which used a "Coulomb description" of IAR properties combined with a semi-microscopic model for giant resonances of normal isospin from Urin (2008), but suffered from an incorrect description of giant resonances at their distant tails. The present approach applies the newly developed particle-hole dispersive optical model from Urin (2013), which correctly describes the low-energy tail of the IVGMR(-) where the IAR is located. The method enables future quantitative estimation of IAR partial proton-escape widths, description of IAR asymmetry in proton-induced reaction excitation functions, and estimation of branching ratios for direct nucleon decay of the Isovector Giant Monopole Resonance.
