# On "Sub-Threshold" Reactions Involving Nuclear Fission

> We analyze reactions of several types that are naively below threshold but can proceed because of the release of binding energy from nuclear fission and occasionally the formation of Coulombic bound states. These reactions include (i) photofission with pion production and (ii) charged current neutrino-nucleus reactions that lead to fission and/or formation of a Coulomb bound state of a $μ^-$ with the nucleus of a fission fragment. We comment on the possible experimental observation of these reactions.

# On "Sub-Threshold" Reactions Involving Nuclear Fission

## Key claim
Reactions such as photofission with pion production and charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions that are naively below threshold can proceed because the release of fission energy (approximately 150 MeV) and, in some cases, the formation of Coulomb bound states of muons with fission fragments can provide the additional energy needed to exceed the true threshold. The authors note that near-threshold phase space suppression (three-body final state scaling as Q^2) makes experimental observation quite difficult, though the cross section for neutrino-induced fission with muon binding could be as large as a few percent of the corresponding weak charged-current reaction.

## Method
The authors analyze the kinematics of photofission with pion production (gamma + nucleus -> fission fragments + pi^0 or pi^pm) and charged-current neutrino reactions (nu_mu + nucleus -> mu^- + fission, and the antineutrino analog) on heavy targets such as 238U and 232Th. They estimate cross sections by considering phase space factors near threshold, the transfer of fission energy via strong and Coulombic interactions, and the formation of muonic Coulomb bound states with fission fragments, using a factor f_NIFM ~ Z^2 Z_f^3 alpha^5 to account for the combined effects of energy transfer and bound-state formation.

## Key numbers
- Fission energy release: E_fiss ~ 150 MeV
- Typical fission energy barrier: approximately 5 MeV
- Photofission cross section on 238U at approximately 14 MeV: about 100 mb (giant dipole resonance peak)
- Photofission cross section on 238U at photon energies slightly below 140 MeV: approximately 150 mb
- Photofission cross section on 232Th at photon energies slightly below 140 MeV: approximately 45 mb
- Time scale for fission: t_fiss ~ 10^{-14} sec
- Coulomb binding energy for a muon bound to a fission fragment with Z ~ 60: E_B ~ 5 MeV
- Fermi momentum of struck nucleon: p_F ~ 270 MeV
- Estimated cross section for neutrino-induced fission with muon bound-state formation: of order 10^{-43} to 10^{-42} cm^2 (for 238U target, with E_nu 30 MeV below naive threshold, 80 MeV transferred from fission, and 5 MeV from muon binding)
- Cross section for 12C(nu_mu,mu^-)12N: varies from less than 10^{-42} cm^2 slightly above threshold (~120 MeV) to ~0.7 x 10^{-40} cm^2 for E_nu >= 150 MeV
- Ternary fission with alpha emission (e.g., 235U): frequency relative to binary fission of a few parts in 10^3; typical alpha energy 20-30 MeV
- Supernova nu_mu and antinu_mu average energies: about 25 MeV, with flux extending up to about 50 MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of "sub-threshold" nuclear reactions by considering fission as an energy source, building on prior photofission studies (e.g., Hyde, Wilets) and neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations for light and heavy nuclei (e.g., the 12C system and calculations for 56Fe and 208Pb by Kolbe and Langanke (2000)). The authors draw an analogy to ternary fission with long-range alpha particles (equatorial and polar emission) as a measure of phase space and Coulombic suppression. The results suggest that charged-current neutrino reactions on heavy nuclei could serve as a means to detect supernova nu_mu and antinu_mu, distinguishing them from other neutrino flavors, complementing neutral-current detection methods (e.g., Langanke, Vogel, and Kolbe).
