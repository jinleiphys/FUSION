# QCD at high density: Equation of state for nuclear collisions and neutron stars

> A unified chiral mean field approach is presented for QCD thermodynamics in a wide range of temperatures and densities. The model simultaneously gives a satisfactory description of lattice QCD thermodynamics and fulfills nuclear matter and astrophysical constraints. The resulting equation of state can be incorporated in relativistic fluid-dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars mergers. Access to different regions of the QCD phase diagram can be obtained in simulations of heavy-ion data and observations of neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
The paper presents a unified chiral mean field (CMF) model that simultaneously reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryochemical potential and satisfies nuclear matter and astrophysical constraints, but the model predicts no first-order deconfinement phase transition or critical point at finite density; instead, the transition to quarks is smooth, with only the nuclear liquid-gas transition present.

## Method
The authors extend the SU(3) flavor parity-doublet quark-hadron chiral mean field (CMF) model, which includes a complete list of known hadrons (masses below 2.6 GeV) and three light quark flavors. Baryons interact via mesonic mean fields (sigma, omega, rho, phi, zeta) in a nonlinear sigma-omega model, with parity doubling introducing heavy parity partners that become mass-degenerate as chiral symmetry is restored. Quarks are treated in a PNJL-like approach with a Polyakov loop potential, and excluded volume corrections (1 fm^3 for baryons, 1/8 fm^3 for mesons) prevent reconfinement at high densities. The model parameters are constrained by fitting lattice QCD data on the interaction measure at zero baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Polyakov loop potential parameters: T0 = 180 MeV, a1 = -11.67, a2 = 9.33, b3 = -0.53, and quark couplings g_q_sigma = g_s_zeta = -1.0
- Excluded volume parameter: v_B = 1 fm^3 for baryons, v_M = 1/8 fm^3 for mesons
- At FAIR energies, the system probes temperatures from 10 < T < 270 MeV and chemical potentials from 500 < mu_B < 1500 MeV
- The nuclear liquid-gas transition occurs at T approximately 10 MeV
- Stars with a total quark fraction of more than 30% are unstable according to the TOV equation solution

## Context
This work extends the CMF model approach of Steinheimer (2011) and Mukherjee (2016) by simultaneously constraining the model with lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014, Bazavov 2017, Borsanyi 2018) and with nuclear matter properties. The resulting equation of state is designed for use in fluid-dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers, enabling connections between laboratory experiments and astrophysical observations such as GW170817. The model predicts no critical point associated with deconfinement, contrasting with approaches that predict a first-order transition at high density.
