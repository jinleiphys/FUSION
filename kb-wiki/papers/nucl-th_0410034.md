# Bound States of Heavy Flavor Hyperons

> Several realistic phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interaction models are employed to investigate the possibility of bound deuteron-like states of such heavy flavor hyperons and nucleons, for which the interaction between the light flavor quark components is expected to be the most significant interaction. The results indicate that deuteron-like bound states are likely to form between nucleons and the $Ξ_c^{'}$ and $Ξ_{cc}$ charm hyperons as well as between $Ξ$ hyperons and double-charm hyperons. Bound states between two $Σ_c$ hyperons are also likely. In the case of beauty hyperons the corresponding states are likely to be deeply bound.

## Key claim

Deuteron-like bound states are likely to form between nucleons and the charm hyperons Xi_c' and Xi_cc, as well as between Xi hyperons and double-charm hyperons, but the calculated binding energies depend strongly on which phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interaction model is used and on whether mass-dependent scaling factors are applied to angular-momentum-dependent interaction components.

## Method

The authors took realistic phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interaction models (Nijmegen potentials Nijm93, NijmI, NijmII and the Argonne AV18 potential, plus simplified AVn' versions) and rescaled each operator component using quark-model scaling factors derived from the number of light-flavor quarks in the hyperons. They solved the Schrodinger equation for two-baryon systems with isospin 0 and isospin 1, and also tested the effect of adding baryon-mass-dependent scaling factors to the angular-momentum-dependent terms.

## Context

This work extends the quark-model rescaling approach previously applied to hyperon-nucleon interactions (Rijken, 1999) by applying it systematically to charm and beauty hyperon systems using multiple realistic NN potentials (Nijmegen potentials from 1993 and 1994, AV18 from Wiringa et al., 1995). The results show that the short-range components of NN potentials, which are poorly constrained by NN scattering data, cause large theoretical uncertainties when rescaled for hyperon systems. The Paris potential (Lacombe et al., 1980) gives much smaller binding energies, and the authors conclude that the NijmII potential, with its weaker angular momentum dependence, likely provides the most realistic estimates. The predicted bound states could be searched for experimentally, though charm-exchange couplings may make some states only metastable.
