# Remarks concerning bulk viscosity of hadron matter in relaxation time ansatz

> The bulk viscosity is calculated for hadron matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, being described in the relaxation time approximation withi n the relativistic mean- field-based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings. W e show how different approximations used in the literature affect the result. Nume rical evaluations of the bulk viscosity with three considered models deviate not much from each other confirming earlier results.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of hadron matter in the relaxation time approximation depends significantly on which additional assumptions (ansatze) are used to satisfy the Landau-Lifshitz matching conditions, but numerical evaluations from three different models deviate little from each other, especially at temperatures below about 150 MeV, confirming earlier results.

## Method

The authors calculate the bulk viscosity for hadron matter produced in heavy-ion collisions using the relativistic mean-field-based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings (SHMC model). They employ the relaxation time approximation to the Boltzmann-like quasiparticle kinetic equations and compare three different ansatze: Model I (from Sasaki-Redlich and SHMCbulk) where quantities depending implicitly on the distribution function are not varied; Model II where mean-field variations are included and Landau-Lifshitz conditions are enforced via a shift procedure; and Model III (from Chakraborty-Kapusta) where the collision term uses the exact energy rather than the local equilibrium energy.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at zero baryon chemical potential shows agreement between the SHMC model and the interacting pion gas model of Weise for temperatures below about 150 MeV.
* The bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio at zero baryon chemical potential is shown as a function of temperature; all three models give results close to each other for temperatures below about 150 MeV.
* At baryon density n_B = n_0 (0.16 fm^-3) and n_B = 4 n_0, the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio is presented as a function of temperature; results from Eqs. (zetaaI) and (zetaKap) are closer to each other than to Eq. (bulkSHMC).
* For the ideal gas limit, curves from Eqs. (zetaaI) and (zetaKap) coincide, and are close to Eq. (bulkSHMC) for temperatures above about 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of bulk viscosity in hadron matter previously performed by the same group in SHMCbulk and by Sasaki-Redlich (2008), and parallels a similar study for gluon matter in GBulk1. The authors compare their results with the linear sigma model calculation by Dobado and collaborators in the crossover region, finding overall agreement despite different models and expressions. The paper enables more reliable use of bulk viscosity values in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions by clarifying which approximations are robust and which introduce uncertainty.
