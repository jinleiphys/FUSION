# Mott-Anderson freeze-out and the strange matter "horn"

> We discuss the $\sqrt{s}$-dependence of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions (the "horn" effect) within a Mott-Anderson localization model for chemical freeze-out. The different response of pion and kaon radii to the hot and dense hadronic medium results in different freeze-out conditions. We demonstrate within a simple model that this circumstance enhances the "horn" effect relative to statistical models with universal chemical freeze-out.

# Mott-Anderson freeze-out and the strange matter "horn"

## Key claim
The Mott-Anderson localization model predicts different chemical freeze-out conditions for kaons and pions due to the different medium dependence of their radii, which enhances the "horn" effect in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio relative to statistical models with universal chemical freeze-out. The enhancement is demonstrated within a simple model, but the agreement with data breaks down for collision energies below the peak of the horn at $\sqrt{s}\sim 8$ GeV.

## Method
The authors model chemical freeze-out using the Mott-Anderson localization scenario, where hadron radii swell in hot and dense matter due to chiral condensate reduction, and freeze-out occurs when collision rates become too low to maintain chemical equilibrium. Pion radii are linked to the light quark condensate via the NJL model, while kaon radii depend on both light and strange quark condensates; the strange condensate is approximated as inert to medium modifications because strangeness-carrying species are suppressed by their larger mass. The kaon freeze-out line is parametrized relative to the pion freeze-out line by a factor $\gamma$ applied to the baryochemical potential, and the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio is computed using thermal statistical model number densities with separate freeze-out conditions for each species.

## Key numbers
- The peak of the "horn" in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio occurs at $\sqrt{s}\sim 8$ GeV, below which the model assumptions break down.
- The parameter $\sqrt{\gamma}$ is varied in the range $1\le \sqrt{\gamma} \le 1.3$ to explore the deviation of kaon freeze-out from pion freeze-out.
- The freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryochemical potential is denoted $T_0 = a$, with the universal freeze-out curve parametrized as $T(\mu_B)=a-b\mu_B^2-c\mu_B^4$.
- For chemical potentials $\mu<0.7\ \mu_{\rm max}$, the Mott-Anderson model prediction agrees well with the extracted kaon freeze-out line.

## Context
This work extends the Mott-Anderson localization model for chemical freeze-out developed by Blaschke et al. (2011) to explain the "horn" effect in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio, which has been a puzzle in heavy-ion collision phenomenology. The approach contrasts with statistical models that assume universal chemical freeze-out for all hadron species, and it relates to alternative explanations such as the modified statistical model of Sagun et al. (2014) and the "quarkyonic" explanation of Andronic et al. (2009). The results enable future studies incorporating resonance decays, excluded volume effects, canonical statistical suppression, and the relationship to strangeness enhancement factors used by Becattini et al. (2012), Petran et al. (2013), and Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001).
