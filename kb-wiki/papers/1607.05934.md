# Landau quantization and neutron emissions by nuclei in the crust of a magnetar

> Magnetars are neutron stars endowed with surface magnetic fields of the order of $10^{14}-10^{15}$~G, and with presumably much stronger fields in their interior. As a result of Landau quantization of electron motion, the neutron-drip transition in the crust of a magnetar is shifted to either higher or lower densities depending on the magnetic field strength. The impact of nuclear uncertainties is explored considering the recent series of Brussels-Montreal microscopic nuclear mass models. All these models are based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with generalized Skyrme functionals. They differ in their predictions for the symmetry energy coefficient at saturation, and for the stiffness of the neutron-matter equation of state. For comparison, we have also considered the very accurate but more phenomenological model of Duflo and Zuker. Although the equilibrium composition of the crust of a magnetar and the onset of neutron emission are found to be model dependent, the quantum oscillations of the threshold density are essentially universal.

## Key claim

The neutron-drip transition density in a magnetar crust oscillates with magnetic field strength in an almost universal way across different nuclear mass models, even though the specific equilibrium nucleus and the exact onset density are model-dependent.

## Method

The equilibrium composition of the outer crust of a magnetar was determined by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per nucleon, using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with generalized Skyrme functionals (BSk22 through BSk27*) and the Duflo-Zuker phenomenological model. The neutron-drip condition was solved numerically for various magnetic field strengths, accounting for Landau quantization of electron motion and electrostatic lattice corrections for a body-centred cubic lattice.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field defining the quantizing regime is \(B_{\rm crit} \simeq 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G.
- The root-mean-square deviation of the Brussels-Montreal mass models from 2353 measured nuclear masses is about 0.5–0.6 MeV.
- The symmetry energy coefficient \(J\) ranges from 29.0 MeV (BSk25) to 32.0 MeV (BSk22) across the models; the slope parameter \(L\) ranges from 28.5 MeV (BSk27*) to 68.5 MeV (BSk22).
- The equilibrium nucleus at the neutron-drip transition varies: e.g., \(^{122}\)Kr or \(^{128}\)Sr for HFB-22 (depending on field), \(^{126}\)Sr for HFB-23, \(^{124}\)Sr for HFB-24, \(^{122}\)Sr for HFB-25, \(^{126}\)Sr for HFB-26, \(^{124}\)Sr for HFB-27*, and \(^{118}\)Kr for the Duflo-Zuker model.
- The threshold electron chemical potential \(\mu_e^{\rm drip}\) ranges from 24.76 MeV (HFB-25 and HFB-27*) to 25.01 MeV (HFB-22).
- In the nonquantizing regime, the neutron-drip density oscillates between a minimum of about \(3/4\) of the zero-field value and a maximum of about \((35 + 13\sqrt{13})/72\) of the zero-field value.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Chamel 2012, Chamel 2015b) on Landau quantization effects in magnetar crusts by systematically comparing six Brussels-Montreal microscopic nuclear mass models (BSk22–BSk27*) and the phenomenological Duflo-Zuker model (1995). The results show that while the equilibrium nucleus and exact drip density depend on the nuclear model, the quantum oscillations of the threshold density with magnetic field are essentially universal, enabling predictions for magnetar crust properties from unmagnetized neutron star values. The authors note that the neglect of magnetic-field-induced changes to nuclear masses (Bas 2015) may undermine this universality.
