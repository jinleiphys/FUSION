# Statistical Properties of Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> Statistical properties of Fermionic Molecular Dynamics are studied. It is shown that, although the centroids of the single--particle wave--packets follow classical trajectories in the case of a harmonic oscillator potential, the equilibrium properties of the system are the quantum mechanical ones. A system of weakly interacting fermions as well as of distinguishable particles is found to be ergodic and the time--averaged occupation probabilities approach the quantum canonical ones of Fermi--Dirac and Boltzmann statistics, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), despite having single-particle wave-packet centroids that follow classical equations of motion, yields equilibrium properties that reproduce quantum canonical ensembles (Fermi-Dirac for fermions, Boltzmann for distinguishable particles) rather than classical statistics, provided the width parameters remain dynamical variables.

## Method

The authors studied a system of four identical fermions in a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential with a weak repulsive two-body Gaussian interaction (strength V0 = 10^4 to 10^5 ω, range r0 = 0.01/√(mω)) necessary to convert integrable motion into chaotic dynamics. They evolved the FMD trial state from an initial nonequilibrium configuration over approximately 2000 oscillator periods (2π/ω = 157 fm/c), then computed time-averaged occupation probabilities of harmonic oscillator eigenstates and compared them to the canonical ensemble at the same mean excitation energy. The same procedure was repeated for distinguishable particles (product states) and for Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) trial states with fixed width parameters.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator frequency ω = 0.04 fm⁻¹, giving a level spacing of 8 MeV between single-particle states
- Interaction strength V0 = (10⁴ to 10⁵) ω, with range r0 = 0.01/√(mω); contribution of ⟨V_I⟩ to total energy is of order 0.1 to 1.0 MeV
- Ground state energy for four fermions: E0 = 8 ω
- Time averaging started at t1 = 10000 fm/c after initial equilibration, over about 2000 oscillator periods (each period = 157 fm/c)
- For the two-oscillator system with distinguishable particles, time averaging performed over more than 30000 periods of the wider oscillator, starting after 30000 periods for equilibration
- For distinguishable particles at excitation energy E*/A = 2.05 MeV, the canonical temperature is T = 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the investigation of statistical properties in molecular dynamics models for nuclear multifragmentation, following earlier thermostatic studies by Ohnishi and Randrup (1993, 1994) that did not fully exploit quantum features of the many-body trial state. The paper shows that FMD and AMD trial states, which form an overcomplete set covering the whole Hilbert space (Klauder, 1985), produce correct thermodynamic behavior despite approximate equations of motion. This enables the extraction of temperature from deterministic molecular dynamics simulations of excited nuclei and provides a foundation for studying the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition of current experimental interest (Pochodzalla, 1995).
