# A self-consistent study of multipole response in neutron-rich nuclei using a modified realistic potential

> The multipole response of neutron rich O and Sn isotopes is computed in Tamm-Dancoff and random-phase approximations using the canonical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasi-particle basis. The calculations are performed using an intrinsic Hamiltonian composed of a $V_{lowk}$ potential, deduced from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, corrected with phenomenological density dependent and spin-orbit terms. The effect of these two pieces on energies and multipole responses is discussed. The problem of removing the spurious admixtures induced by the center of mass motion and by the violation of the number of particles is investigated. The differences between the two theoretical approaches are discussed quantitatively. Attention is then focused on the dipole strength distribution, including the low-lying transitions associated to the pygmy resonance. Monopole and quadrupole responses are also briefly investigated. A detailed comparison with the available experimental spectra contributes to clarify the extent of validity of the two self-consistent approaches.

# A self-consistent study of multipole response in neutron-rich nuclei using a modified realistic potential

## Key claim
The addition of phenomenological density-dependent and spin-orbit terms to a $V_{lowk}$ potential derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction is necessary to bring the computed giant dipole resonance (GDR) peaks into the experimental energy region, but the resulting description remains only qualitatively consistent with data, with discrepancies of about 4 MeV in peak position for $^{16}$O and incorrect relative peak heights in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a $V_{lowk}$ potential derived from the CD-Bonn NN interaction with a cutoff $\Lambda = 1.9$ fm$^{-1}$, supplemented by a phenomenological density-dependent two-body potential and a spin-orbit term. They solved the eigenvalue problem in both the quasi-particle Tamm-Dancoff approximation (QTDA) and the quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) using the canonical HFB basis, and applied a Gramm-Schmidt orthogonalization procedure in QTDA to exactly remove spurious admixtures from center-of-mass motion and particle-number violation.

## Key numbers
- The density-dependent potential parameter was set to $C_\rho = 2600$ MeV fm$^6$ for oxygen isotopes and $C_\rho = 4200$ MeV fm$^6$ for tin isotopes.
- The spin-orbit parameter was $C_{so} = -0.9$ MeV for oxygen and $C_{so} = -0.6$ MeV for tin isotopes.
- In $^{16}$O, the theoretical GDR peak lies approximately 4 MeV below the experimental peak (experimental centroid $\sim 20.7$ MeV with width $\sim 5.4$ MeV).
- In $^{132}$Sn, the experimental GDR centroid is $\sim 16.1$ MeV with width $\sim 4.7$ MeV.
- The QTDA integrated cross section overestimates the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule by a factor of $\sim 1.54$ in $^{16}$O and $\sim 1.48$ in $^{20}$O; QRPA gives factors of $\sim 1.43$ and $\sim 1.42$, respectively.
- Low-lying dipole strength below 15 MeV exhausts approximately 10% of the TRK sum rule in $^{18}$O, 15% in $^{20}$O, and 13% in $^{22}$O.
- In $^{20}$O, two low-lying $1^-$ states were found at $\omega = 7.46$ MeV (near the neutron decay threshold of 7.608 MeV) and $\omega = 12.1$ MeV, with $B(E1) = 0.49$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ and $B(E1) = 0.50$ $e^2$ fm$^2$, respectively.
- The lowest QRPA $1^-$ spurious state had an imaginary energy with $\Im(\omega) = 0.18$ MeV; the lowest $0^+$ spurious state had $\Im(\omega) = 1.64$ MeV.
- In the improved QTDA, the lowest $1^-$ spurious state collected 98-99% of the CM coordinate strength, with residual contamination of $\sim 1.8\%$ in $^{20}$O and $\sim 1.5\%$ in $^{116}$Sn.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Roth and coworkers (Hergert 2009, Hergert 2011), who used a correlated UCOM interaction and later SRG-evolved interactions from the Argonne V18 potential within a self-consistent HFB+QRPA framework. The present study replaces those interactions with a $V_{lowk}$ potential from the CD-Bonn force and adds phenomenological corrections to improve single-particle spectra. The upgraded QTDA with exact spurious-state removal is a necessary first step toward future calculations using the equation-of-motion phonon method (EMPM) for multiphonon states, previously applied to $^{208}$Pb by Bianco and coworkers. The results show that while the modified realistic potential can reproduce global features of giant resonances, the description of low-lying pygmy dipole strength and detailed spectral shapes remains incomplete compared to experiments in oxygen isotopes (Leistenschneider 2001, Tryggestad 2002, Tryggestad 2003) and tin isotopes (Adrich 2005, Ozel 2007, Endres 2010).
