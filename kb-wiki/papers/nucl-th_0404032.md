# Many-Particle and Many-Hole States in Neutron-Rich Ne Isotopes Related to Broken N=20 Shell Closure

> The low-lying level structures of $^{26}{\rm Ne}$, $^{28}{\rm Ne}$ and $^{30}{\rm Ne}$ which are related to the breaking of the N=20 shell closure have been studied by the framework of the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics plus generator coordinate method using Gogny D1S force. The properties of the many-particle and many-hole states are discussed as well as that of the ground band. We predict that the negative-parity states in which neutrons are promoted into $pf$-orbit from $sd$ orbit have the small excitation energy in the cases of $^{28}{\rm Ne}$ and $^{30}{\rm Ne}$ which, we regard, is a typical phenomena accompanying the breaking of N=20 shell closure. It is also found that the neutron $4p4h$ structure of $^{30}{\rm Ne}$ appears in low excitation energy which contains $α+ ^{16}{\rm O}$ correlations.

## Key claim

The authors predict that negative-parity states arising from neutron promotion from the sd-shell to the pf-shell appear at small excitation energies in 28Ne and 30Ne, which they regard as a typical phenomenon accompanying the breaking of the N=20 shell closure; however, this prediction is conditional on the deformed-basis AMD+GCM framework using the Gogny D1S force, and the authors note that the 4p4h structure in 30Ne appears as a shoulder rather than a local minimum on the energy curve, only becoming stabilized through superposition and orthogonalization.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) plus generator coordinate method (GCM) using the Gogny D1S effective interaction, with parity projection before energy variation and subsequent angular momentum projection. They constrained the nuclear quadrupole deformation parameter β and performed variational calculations for 26Ne, 28Ne, and 30Ne, then superposed wave functions with different β values and K quantum numbers via the Hill-Wheeler equation. The single-particle structure of the obtained wave functions was analyzed by constructing the Hartree-Fock single-particle Hamiltonian from the AMD wave function to determine the particle-hole configurations.

## Key numbers

- 30Ne: the 2+1 state has an excitation energy of 0.88 MeV with a B(E2;0+1→2+1) value of 283 e2fm4
- 30Ne: the ground band shows E(2+1)/E(0+1) = 3.08, slightly deviating from rotational behavior
- 30Ne: the 4p4h structure appears within about 5 MeV of the lowest 2p2h structure
- 30Ne: the 1p1h and 3p3h negative-parity states have excitation energies of 3.6 MeV and 4 MeV respectively, measured from the positive-parity 2p2h state
- 30Ne: the overlap between the proton wave function of the 4p4h state and that of 20Ne (which has α+16O cluster structure) amounts to about 80%
- 28Ne: B(E2;0+1→2+1) = 208 e2fm4 (experimental value: 269±136 e2fm4)
- 28Ne: the mixing between oblate 0p2h and prolate 2p4h structures lowers the ground state energy by about 1.9 MeV
- 28Ne: the ground band shows vibrational character with Ex(4+1)/Ex(2+1) = 2.17
- 26Ne: B(E2;0+1→2+1) = 203 e2fm4 (experimental value: 228±41 e2fm4)
- 26Ne: the ground band shows Ex(4+1)/Ex(2+1) = 2.22
- 26Ne: the 2p6h superdeformed structure appears about 6 MeV above the ground state
- 26Ne: the 1p5h negative-parity state appears about 6 MeV above the positive-parity 0p4h state

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of the N=20 shell closure breaking in neutron-rich nuclei, following the experimental discovery of anomalous ground state spin in 31Na by Thibault (1975) and the shell model predictions by Otsuka (1993) that neutron 2p2h configurations dominate the ground state of 32Mg. The authors compare their results with the Monte Carlo shell model by Utsuno (1999) and HFB+AMPGCM by Egido (2000), and their calculated B(E2) values for 26Ne and 28Ne are compared with experimental data from Pritychenko (2000). The finding of α+16O cluster correlations in the 4p4h structure of 30Ne parallels similar cluster survival in neutron-rich Be isotopes studied by Enyo and Itagaki, and the work enables future experimental searches for low-lying negative-parity states and excited 4p4h bands in neutron-rich Ne isotopes.
