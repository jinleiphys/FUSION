# Thermal freeze-out parameters and pseudo-entropy from charged hadron spectra in high energy collisions

> We collected the transverse momentum (mass) spectra of charged hadrons ($π^{-}$, $π^{+}$, $K^{-}$, $K^{+}$, $\overline{p}$, and $p$) produced in collisions over a center-of-mass energy range from 2.70 to 200 GeV (per nucleon pair). The modified Tsallis--Pareto-type function (the TP-like function) with average transverse flow velocity is used to describe the contribution of participant or constituent quarks to transverse momentum of considered hadron. The experimental spectra of $π^{\mp}$ and $K^{\mp}$ (or $\overline{p}$ and $p$) are fitted by the convolution of two (or three) TP-like functions due to the fact that two (or three) constituent quarks are regarded as two (or three) energy resources in the formation of considered hadron. From the reasonable fits to the spectra, the thermal freeze-out parameters are extracted, and the pseudo-entropy is newly defined and extracted. Some parameters quickly change in the energy range of less than 7.7 GeV, and slowly change in the energy range of greater than 7.7 GeV, indicating the variation of collision mechanism at around 7.7 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified Tsallis-Pareto-type (TP-like) function, applied as a convolution of two (for mesons) or three (for baryons) functions at the constituent quark level, can describe the transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons across a wide energy range from 2.70 to 200 GeV, and that the extracted thermal freeze-out parameters and a newly defined pseudo-entropy show a change in collision mechanism around 7.7 GeV.

## Method

The authors collected transverse momentum (mass) spectra of pi-, pi+, K-, K+, antiproton, and proton from Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 2.70 to 200 GeV per nucleon pair. They fitted the spectra using a modified TP-like function incorporating average transverse flow velocity, where meson spectra were fitted by the convolution of two TP-like functions (one per constituent quark) and baryon spectra by the convolution of three TP-like functions. From the fits, they extracted the thermal freeze-out temperature T0, average transverse flow velocity, and defined a new pseudo-entropy based on the probability density function of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T0 for pi- in 0-5% centrality at 7.7 GeV: 0.165 +/- 0.003 GeV
- Average transverse flow velocity for antiproton in 0-5% centrality at 7.7 GeV: 0.347 +/- 0.008 c
- Power index n for K- in 0-5% centrality at 7.7 GeV: 27.9 +/- 1.7
- Correction index a0 for pi- in 0-5% centrality at 7.7 GeV: -0.396 +/- 0.005
- Freeze-out temperature T0 for pi+ in 0-5% centrality at 14.5 GeV: 0.179 +/- 0.003 GeV
- Constituent quark masses used: 0.31 GeV/c^2 for u and d quarks, 0.5 GeV/c^2 for s quark
- Bin width for pseudo-entropy calculation: 0.1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis-type non-extensive thermodynamics to heavy-ion collisions across a broad energy range, building on prior studies using the Tsallis-Pareto-type function (Tsallis 1988; Wilk and Wlodarczyk 2000). The convolution approach at the constituent quark level is similar to the analysis at the particle level in terms of the same mathematical structure. The results enable systematic comparison of freeze-out parameters across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and suggest a transition in collision dynamics near 7.7 GeV, which may relate to the search for the QCD critical point.
