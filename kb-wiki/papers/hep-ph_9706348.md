# alpha_s dependence in the equilibration in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The dependence of the equilibration of the parton plasma on the value of the strong coupling is studied in Au+Au collisions at LHC and at RHIC energies. With increasing coupling, the following are found to happen: 1) both thermal and chemical equilibration speed up, 2) in the final degree of equilibration, only quarks and antiquarks show obvious improvements but not gluons and 3) the plasma cools much more rapidly. The deconfinement phase transition will therefore takes place sooner and it naturally results in the shortening of the parton phase of the plasma. The exact duration of this phase is however sensitive to the value of the coupling. A change from $\a_s=0.3$ to $\a_s=0.5$, for example, reduces the lifetime of the parton phase at LHC by as much as 4.0 fm/c. The total generated entropy is another sensitive quantity to the coupling. Larger values of $\a_s$ will lead to entropy reduction and therefore reduction both in the duration of the mixed phase, as well as in the final pion multiplicity. It is shown that the common choice of $\a_s=0.3$ is not a good value for the entire duration of the evolution given that the system undergoes substantial changes from the beginning to the time that the deconfinement phase transition is about to take place. Instead, by using a more consistent simple recipe, the system is allowed to decide its own strength of the interactions which evolves with the system as it should. With this approach, $\a_s$ increases with time and this leads to acceleration in the equilibration even as equilibrium is near. This is opposite to the behavior of the equilibration of a molecular gas or ordinary many-body system where the interaction strength is fixed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the common choice of a fixed strong coupling constant αs = 0.3 is not appropriate for the entire evolution of the parton plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions, because the system undergoes substantial changes from the initial stage to the time of the deconfinement phase transition; instead, using a running coupling that evolves with the system leads to an accelerated approach to equilibrium that is opposite to the behavior of ordinary many-body systems.

## Method

The author solves the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation time approximation for one-dimensional boost-invariant longitudinal expansion in Au+Au collisions at LHC and RHIC energies. The collision terms include leading-order QCD interactions (gg ↔ ggg, gg ↔ gg, gg ↔ qq̄, gq ↔ gq, gq̄ ↔ gq̄, qq̄ ↔ qq̄, qq ↔ qq, q̄q̄ ↔ q̄q̄) with infrared screening via Debye screening masses and quark medium masses, plus a partial incorporation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Midgal suppression for gluon radiation. The evolution is studied for fixed values αs = 0.3, 0.5, 0.8 and for a running coupling αs(Q) determined by the average parton energy using the one-loop running coupling formula with ΛQCD = 235 MeV and nf = 2.5.

## Key numbers

- A change from αs = 0.3 to αs = 0.5 reduces the lifetime of the parton phase at LHC by as much as 4.0 fm/c
- At LHC, shifting from αs = 0.3 to 0.5 shortens the time at which quark temperatures drop to 200 MeV from 12.0 fm/c to 8.4 fm/c
- At RHIC, shifting from αs = 0.3 to 0.5 shortens the time at which quark temperatures drop to 200 MeV from 7.0 fm/c to 4.3 fm/c
- The reduction in the duration of gluon temperatures is about 2.0 fm/c at LHC and 1.0 fm/c at RHIC at maximum
- For quarks at LHC, final fugacities are improved by a factor of 1.5-2.0 when increasing αs from 0.3 to larger values
- For quarks at RHIC, final fugacities are improved by a factor of 1.8-2.8 when increasing αs from 0.3 to larger values
- The deconfinement phase transition is assumed to occur at Tc ∼ 200 MeV
- The initial conditions use HIJING-generated values with initial temperature T0, initial fugacities l0i, energy densities ε0i, and number densities n0i for i = g, q, q̄

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigations into equilibration of the QCD plasma (Wong 1996, 1997) and builds on earlier studies of chemical equilibration by Geiger and Muller (1992), Geiger (1992), Geiger and Kapusta (1993), and Biro et al. (1993). The paper shows that the common fixed-coupling approach is inconsistent because the average parton energy varies considerably during evolution, and it demonstrates that a running coupling leads to accelerated equilibration as equilibrium is approached, a feature unique to QCD with asymptotic freedom that is not found in ordinary many-body systems. This enables more realistic modeling of the parton plasma evolution and its transition to the hadronic phase.
