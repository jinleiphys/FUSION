# Chiral symmetry and strangeness at SIS energies

> In this talk we review the consequences of the chiral SU(3) symmetry for strangeness propagation in nuclear matter. Objects of crucial importance are the meson-baryon scattering amplitudes obtained within the chiral coupled-channel effective field theory. Results for antikaon and hyperon-resonance spectral functions in cold nuclear matter are presented and discussed. The importance of the Sigma(1385) resonance for the subthreshold antikaon production in heavy-ion reaction at SIS is pointed out. The in-medium properties of the latter together with an antikaon spectral function based on chiral SU(3) dynamics suggest a significant enhancement of the pi Λ-> bar K N reaction in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Chiral SU(3) dynamics, applied via the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach, predicts that the in-medium enhancement of the subthreshold antikaon production in heavy-ion reactions at SIS energies is driven primarily by the p-wave Sigma(1385) resonance coupling to the pi Lambda -> bar K N channel, rather than by the s-wave Lambda(1405) resonance which dominates the pi Sigma -> bar K N channel.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral SU(3) effective field theory (chi-BS(3) approach) applied to meson-baryon scattering amplitudes via the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation. Antikaon and hyperon-resonance spectral functions in cold nuclear matter are computed self-consistently, respecting constraints from covariance, using the in-medium scattering amplitudes together with the antikaon spectral function to define the final-state phase space for the pi Y -> bar K N reactions.

## Key numbers

- The empirical isospin-averaged pion-nucleon scattering length is a_pi N ~ -0.01 fm, confirming the Weinberg-Tomozawa prediction that it vanishes in the chiral SU(2) limit.
- The Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem predicts a_K- N ~ +0.9 fm, while the empirical K- nucleon scattering length is a_K- N ~ (-0.6 + i 1.1) fm.
- The threshold energy difference between antikaon and kaon production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is sqrt(s)_thres^(bar K) - sqrt(s)_thres^(K) = m_K + m_N - m_Lambda ~ 320 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density rho = 0.17 fm^-3, the antikaon spectral function strength starts at omega ~ 200 MeV.
- At 2 rho_0, a kaon with momentum ~400 MeV experiences a repulsive mass shift of about 45 MeV, smaller than the 70 MeV used in some prior simulations.
- The self-consistent many-body evaluation shows a dramatic enhancement for the pi Lambda -> bar K N reaction in nuclear matter, but only a moderate enhancement for the pi Sigma -> bar K N reaction.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel effective field theory approach developed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002-2003) and applies it to antikaon production at SIS energies, building on earlier K-matrix analyses by A.D. Martin (1970s) and transport simulations by Cassing and Mosel (1990s), Li and Brown (1990s), and Schaffner, Effenberger and Koch (1990s). The results contradict the large enhancement factors for the pi Sigma -> bar K N reaction predicted by Pauli-blocking-only schemes (Koch, 1990s; Schaffner, 1990s) and instead identify the Sigma(1385) resonance as the key driver for enhanced antikaon production. The findings enable improved transport model simulations that incorporate the Sigma(1385) dynamics and the momentum-dependent antikaon spectral function, which should help resolve the factor 3-4 discrepancy between current simulations and empirical antikaon yields.
