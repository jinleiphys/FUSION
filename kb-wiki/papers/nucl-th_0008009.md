# On the Solution of the Number-Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations

> The numerical solution of the recently formulated number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations is studied in an exactly soluble cranked-deformed shell model Hamiltonian. It is found that the solution of these number-projected equations involve similar numerical effort as that of bare HFB. We consider that this is a significant progress in the mean-field studies of the quantum many-body systems. The results of the projected calculations are shown to be in almost complete agreement with the exact solutions of the model Hamiltonian. The phase transition obtained in the HFB theory as a function of the rotational frequency is shown to be smeared out with the projection.

# On the Solution of the Number-Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations

## Key claim

The number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) equations can be solved with similar numerical effort as bare HFB, and the results agree almost completely with exact solutions of a cranked-deformed shell model Hamiltonian, but only for the specific model system studied (single j=11/2 shell with N=6 particles and a delta-interaction).

## Method

The authors solve the recently formulated number-projected HFB equations for a cranked-deformed shell model Hamiltonian consisting of a Nilsson mean-field plus a delta-interaction, using a single j=11/2 shell with 6 particles. The gauge-angle integration in the projected fields is performed using Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature with M=4 mesh points. Calculations are carried out for three pairing interactions: monopole (L=0), monopole plus quadrupole (L=0 and 2), and full delta-interaction, and results are compared with exact shell model diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- For N=6 particles in a j=11/2 shell with degeneracy Omega=6, the optimal number of Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature points is M=4.
- The HFB phase transition from superfluid to normal phase occurs at hbar*omega=0.5G for monopole pairing and at hbar*omega=0.55G for full delta-interaction.
- The HFB alignment jumps to <J_x>=10 at the bandcrossing for monopole pairing, while PHFB and exact results show no such sharp transition.
- Average CPU time on a Pentium 166MHz was 6.14 seconds per rotational frequency with projection versus 5.97 seconds for normal HFB.

## Context

This work extends the recently developed number-projected HFB formalism of Sheikh and Ring (2000) by demonstrating its numerical feasibility in an exactly soluble model. It addresses the known problem that HFB theory produces sharp phase transitions (superfluid to normal) as a function of rotational frequency, which are artifacts of the mean-field approach and not observed experimentally. The method improves upon the Lipkin-Nogami prescription, which has been shown by Fanto (1996) to break down at high rotational frequencies and violate the variational principle. The results enable application of number-projected HFB to realistic nuclear structure calculations using existing HFB computer codes with modified field expressions.
