# The Operator form of 3H (3He) and its Spin Structure

> An operator form of the 3N bound state is proposed. It consists of eight operators formed out of scalar products in relative momentum and spin vectors, which are applied on a pure 3N spin 1/2 state. Each of the operators is associated with a scalar function depending only on the magnitudes of the two relative momenta and the angle between them. The connection between the standard partial wave decomposition of the 3N bound state and the operator form is established, and the decomposition of these scalar function in terms of partial wave components and analytically known auxiliary functions is given. That newly established operator form of the 3N bound state exhibits the dominant angular and spin dependence analytically. The scalar functions are tabulated and can be downloaded. As an application the spin dependent nucleon momentum distribution in a polarized 3N bound state is calculated to illustrate the use of the new form of the 3N bound state.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an operator form of the three-nucleon (3N) bound state consisting of eight scalar operators acting on a pure spin-1/2 state, which provides analytic insight into the spin and momentum dependence of the 3H and 3He wave functions. The authors establish that the ninth operator term from the earlier Gerjuoy and Schwinger (1942) formulation is redundant, reducing the representation to eight independent operators.

## Method

Starting from the standard partial wave decomposition of the 3N bound state in momentum space, the authors reformulate each partial wave component (2S1/2, 2P1/2, 4P1/2, and 4D1/2) into operator forms using spin operators and Jacobi momentum vectors. They derive explicit expressions for the eight scalar functions φi(p,q,cosθ) in terms of the partial wave components ΨlλLsS(pq) and analytically known coefficient functions (Al, Bl, Cl, Dl, El, and cl), establishing the connection between the operator form and the standard Faddeev partial wave representation.

## Context

This work extends the operator form approach introduced by Gerjuoy and Schwinger (1942) for the 3N bound state, providing for the first time explicit numerical scalar functions that were previously unknown. The operator form is analogous to the Rarita and Schwinger (1941) operator representation of the deuteron, which has been used to study spin-dependent momentum distributions. The method enables analytic calculation of spin-dependent observables in polarized 3H and 3He, such as the neutron momentum distribution in polarized 3He, without requiring full numerical wave function reconstruction from partial wave components.
