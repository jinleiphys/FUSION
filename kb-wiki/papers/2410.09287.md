# Learning from knockout reactions using a dispersive optical model

> We present the empirical dispersive optical model (DOM) as applied to direct nuclear reactions. The DOM links both scattering and bound-state experimental data through a dispersion relation which allows for fully-consistent, data-informed predictions for nuclei where such data exists. In particular, we review investigations of the electron-induced proton knockout reaction from both $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca in a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) utilizing the DOM for a fully-consistent description. Viewing these reactions through the lens of the DOM allows us to connect the documented quenching of spectroscopic factors with increased high-momentum proton content in neutron-rich nuclei. A similar DOM-DWIA description of the proton-induced knockout from $^{40}$Ca, however, does not currently fit in the consistent story of its electron-induced counterpart. With the main difference in the proton-induced case being the use of an effective proton-proton interaction, we suggest that a more sophisticated in-medium interaction would lead to consistent results.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM) provides a fully-consistent, data-informed framework for describing both electron-induced and proton-induced knockout reactions, but the DOM-DWIA description of proton-induced knockout from $^{40}$Ca does not currently fit the consistent story of its electron-induced counterpart, suggesting that a more sophisticated in-medium proton-proton interaction is needed.

## Method

The authors applied the dispersive optical model (DOM) to generate all ingredients for distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations of knockout reactions: the distorted outgoing proton wave, the overlap function, and its normalization (spectroscopic factor). For $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca, the DOM self-energy parameters were constrained through weighted $\chi^2$ minimization against elastic differential cross sections, analyzing powers, reaction cross sections, total cross sections, charge density, energy levels, particle number, and root-mean-square charge radius. The DOM-DWIA calculations were performed for both electron-induced proton knockout $(e,e'p)$ and proton-induced knockout $(p,2p)$ reactions.

## Key numbers

- The DOM-provided spectroscopic factor of 0.71 perfectly describes the $^{40}$Ca$(e,e'p)^{39}$K data
- The spectroscopic factor had to be further reduced to 0.56 to reproduce the $^{40}$Ca$(p,2p)^{39}$K data
- For closed-shell nuclei, $(e,e'p)$ cross sections typically require a scaling factor of 0.6 to 0.7 to generate an accurate description of the data, as established by the Nikhef group
- Proton-induced knockout $(p,2p)$ has about 15% uncertainty for incident energy above 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical model framework first proposed by Mahaux and Sartor (1991) and later developed with nonlocal dispersive potentials by Mahzoon et al. (2014) and Dickhoff et al. (2017). The DOM-DWIA approach provides a consistent alternative to the traditional Nikhef analysis, which used separate structure ingredients (phenomenological overlap functions from Woods-Saxon potentials) and unrelated distorted waves from local optical potentials. The inconsistency between electron-induced and proton-induced knockout results points to the need for a more sophisticated in-medium proton-proton interaction, which could enable reliable spectroscopic studies of exotic nuclei where only proton-induced knockout reactions are feasible.
