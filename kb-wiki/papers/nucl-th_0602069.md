# Resonant tunneling in a schematic model

> Tunneling of an harmonically bound two-body system through an external Gaussian barrier is studied in a schematic model which allows for a better understanding of intricate quantum phenomena. The role of finite size and internal structure is investigated in a consistent treatment. The excitation of internal degrees of freedom gives rise to a peaked structure in the penetration factor. The model results indicate that for soft systems the adiabatic limit is not necessarily reached although often assumed in fusion of nuclei and in electron screening effects at astrophysical energies.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a soft composite object (where the internal harmonic oscillator frequency is small), the adiabatic limit is not necessarily reached during tunneling, contrary to what is often assumed in nuclear fusion and electron screening at astrophysical energies. The peaked structure in the transmission coefficient arises from a Landau-Zener pseudo-crossing of levels, which invalidates the adiabatic approximation for soft systems.

## Method

The authors model a two-body system with an harmonic internal potential and a Gaussian external barrier, solving the coupled-channel equations for a two-level system (ground state and second excited state, since the coupling to the first excited state vanishes due to mirror symmetry). The potential matrix elements are calculated analytically using Razavy's formula, and the coupled differential equations are solved both by direct integration and by the variable reflection amplitude method to ensure numerical accuracy.

## Key numbers

- For a soft system with $\hbar\Omega = 1$ MeV, $m = 938$ MeV, $V_0 = 10$ MeV, and $\nu = 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$, the peak in the total transmission coefficient occurs at $E_{\rm peak} \approx 14$ MeV.
- The enhancement of the transmission coefficient due to coupling is about 5 orders of magnitude in the peak region for $\hbar\Omega = 1$ MeV compared to the no-coupling case.
- The condition for stiffness versus softness is given by comparing $\hbar\Omega$ with $\hbar\nu \sqrt{V_0/\mu} = 2.88$ MeV (for the chosen parameters).
- For $\hbar\Omega = 1$ MeV, the extra diagonal term from the mixing angle derivative is $\frac{\hbar^2}{2M}(\theta'_{max})^2 \approx 3.25$ MeV (at $R = \pm 6.6$ fm), and the off-diagonal term is $\frac{\hbar^2}{2M}\theta''_{max} \approx 4.34$ MeV.
- For $\hbar\Omega = 3$ MeV, the adiabatic limit is recovered: $\frac{\hbar^2}{2M}(\theta'_{max})^2 \approx 0.018$ MeV and $\frac{\hbar^2}{2M}\theta''_{max} \approx 0.19$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of sub-barrier fusion and tunneling of composite objects by treating the coupling between macroscopic and internal degrees of freedom consistently, without assuming a parameterized form for the potential matrix elements as done in Dasso and Landowne (1987) and related papers (paper2, paper3). The model shows that the peaked structure in the transmission coefficient found in the consistent treatment is not reproduced by the simpler Gaussian parameterization, which yields only a shoulder-like structure. The results suggest that the adiabatic approximation commonly used in fusion of soft nuclei and electron screening at astrophysical energies should be re-examined, and that a full coupled-channel treatment may be necessary for soft systems.
