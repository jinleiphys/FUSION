# Liénard-Wiechert potential of a heavy quark moving in QGP medium

> We investigate the nature of the complex retarded potential of a heavy quarkonium moving in a hot and dense static deconfined nuclear medium. The well-known concept of the retarded potential in electrodynamics is extended to the context of the heavy-quark by modifying the static vacuum Cornell potential through Lorentz transformation to the static frame of the medium. The resulting potential in the vacuum is further corrected to incorporate the screening effect offered by the thermal medium. To do so, the retarded Cornell potential is modified by the dielectric function of the static quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium. We present the numerical results for the real and imaginary parts of the potential along with the analytical expression of the potential approximated by a small velocity limit. Finally, we present the thermal width of quarkonia in the QGP medium derived using the imaginary part of the potential and study its dependence on velocity and temperature.

## Key claim
The paper extends the Liénard-Wiechert potential concept from electrodynamics to QCD to derive a complex retarded potential for a heavy quarkonium moving through a static QGP medium, but the results are limited to chromoelectric interactions only and do not automatically describe suppression signals in a rapidly expanding QGP.

## Method
The authors start with the static vacuum Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear string terms) written in covariant form, then perform a Lorentz transformation to the frame where the heavy quark moves with constant velocity. The resulting vacuum retarded potential is Fourier-transformed and modified by dividing by the complex dielectric permittivity of the static QGP medium, which encodes screening effects. The medium-modified potential in coordinate space is obtained by inverse Fourier transformation, with numerical integration over momentum and angular variables, and analytical results are derived in the small-velocity limit keeping terms up to O(v²).

## Context
This work extends the velocity-dependent heavy quarkonium potential studies of Thakur (2016) and the anisotropic medium studies of Jamal (2018) and Agotiya (2016) by deriving the full angular-dependent retarded potential using the Liénard-Wiechert analogy rather than the real-time formalism or hard thermal loop approximation. The results enable calculation of the thermal dissociation width of charmonium and bottomonium states as a function of both velocity and temperature, with the thermal width showing qualitative agreement with the calculations of Song (2007) at leading order in perturbative QCD. The framework can be extended to include color magnetic field effects and to solve the Schrodinger equation with the full real part of the potential for more accurate wave functions.
