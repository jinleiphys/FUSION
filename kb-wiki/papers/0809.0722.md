# Asymmetrically Doped Polyacetylene

> Doped one-dimensional (1D) conjugated polymers, such as polyacetylene, have a conductivity of some metals, like copper. We show that when this polymer is asymmetrically doped, unexpected properties are revealed, when compared to the behavior of the symmetrically standard doped systems (SDS). Depending on the level of imbalance between the chemical potentials of the two involved fermionic species, the polymer can be converted into a 1D partially or fully spin polarized organic conductor.

# Asymmetrically Doped Polyacetylene

## Key claim
When polyacetylene is asymmetrically doped, meaning an imbalance is introduced between the chemical potentials of spin-up and spin-down electrons, the polymer can undergo a first-order quantum phase transition from a zero-gap state to a narrow-gap state, converting it into a partially or fully spin-polarized organic conductor. The direction of this transition (from zero gap to finite gap) is opposite to that observed in symmetrically doped systems, where increasing chemical potential drives the gap from finite to zero.

## Method
The author uses the Gross-Neveu (GN) model in 1+1 dimensions, which is equivalent to the continuum Takayama–Lin-Liu–Maki (TLM) model of polyacetylene, to study the mean-field zero-temperature phase diagram under asymmetric doping. Asymmetric doping is introduced by assigning distinct chemical potentials μ↑ = μ̄ + δμ and μ↓ = μ̄ − δμ to the two spin species, with μ̄ fixed at the critical chemical potential μc = Δ₀/√2. The effective potential Veff(Δ, μ↑, μ↓) is derived, renormalized, and minimized to obtain the gap equation and determine the ground state as a function of the imbalance δμ.

## Key numbers
- The critical chemical potential for the symmetric system is μc = Δ₀/√2, where Δ₀ ≈ 0.7 eV for trans-(CH)x.
- The critical chemical potential asymmetry is δμc = 0.38 Δ₀, above which the system develops a finite gap and becomes fully spin-polarized.
- The critical magnetic field required for full spin polarization is B0,c ≈ 4.6 kT.
- The lattice spacing a ≈ 1.22 Å, the hopping parameter t₀ ≈ 3 eV, and the Fermi velocity vF ≈ 10⁶ m/s for trans-(CH)x.
- The critical doping concentration yc ≈ 6% for the symmetric case, as found in prior work (Caldas and Mota, 2007).

## Context
This work extends the Gross-Neveu model treatment of doped polyacetylene previously applied to symmetrically doped systems (Caldas and Mota, 2007; Wolff, 1987) by introducing spin-dependent chemical potentials. It predicts that an external magnetic field, via Zeeman splitting, can induce partial or full spin polarization in the polymer, producing itinerant magnetism from conduction electrons. The results open the possibility of studying finite-temperature effects on magnetization, critical field, and susceptibility in this field-theoretic framework, and suggest that polyacetylene could serve as a 1D organic spin-polarized conductor, analogous to graphene in its relativistic electron behavior (Jackiw, 1984; Chamon, 2000; Franz, 2002).
