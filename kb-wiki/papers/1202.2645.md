# Triple layered compact star with strange quark matter

> We explore the possibility of three phases in the core of neutron star in a form of triple layers. From the center, strange quark matter, kaon condensed nuclear matter and nuclear matter form a triple layer. We discuss how the phase of strange quark matter is smoothly connected to kaon condensed nuclear matter phase. We also demonstrate that the compact star with triple layered structure can be a model compatible with the 1.97-solar-mass object PSR J1614-2230 recently observed.

# Triple layered compact star with strange quark matter

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a neutron star can accommodate three distinct phases, nuclear matter, kaon condensed nuclear matter, and strange quark matter, in a layered structure, and that such a configuration can be compatible with the observed 1.97-solar-mass object PSR J1614-2230, provided the kaon-nucleon sigma term is taken as approximately 259 MeV and the bag constant and QCD correction parameter are chosen accordingly.

## Method
The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian for kaon-nucleon interactions at tree level, combined with an empirically parameterized nuclear matter equation of state (the "LCK" parametrization). They solve the kaon condensation condition, chemical equilibrium equations, and charge neutrality to obtain the equation of state for kaon condensed nuclear matter, then match it to a strange quark matter equation of state using the MIT bag model with perturbative QCD corrections. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation is integrated to compute mass-radius relations for the triple-layered compact star.

## Key numbers
- The kaon threshold density is found at \( n_t = 4.20 n_0 \), where \( n_0 \) is normal nuclear density.
- The critical density for the transition to strange quark matter is \( n_c = 6.36 n_0 \), obtained with \( \Sigma_{KN} \simeq 259 \) MeV.
- The critical quark number density at the phase boundary is \( n_c^Q = 19.08 n_0 \).
- With QCD correction parameter \( a_4 = 0.624 \), the bag constant is \( B^{1/4} = 101.15 \) MeV.
- The maximum mass for the triple-layered star is \( M_{max} = 1.99 M_\odot \), with radius \( R = 11.12 \) km and central density \( n_{(c)} = 11.7 n_0 \).
- The density at which nuclear matter transitions to kaon condensed nuclear matter (using Maxwell construction) is \( n_{t'} = 5.96 n_0 \).

## Context
This work extends the earlier suggestion by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) that kaons can form a condensate in dense matter, and builds on the authors' previous work (Kim, Lee, Rho) showing that kaon condensation can serve as a "doorway" to quark matter. The scenario of kaon condensation smoothly connecting to strange quark matter was predicted earlier by Forkel (via a K- bound to a skyrmion in hypersphere). The resulting equation of state is consistent with the analysis by Ozel et al. (2010) of neutron star masses. The triple-layered structure enables compatibility with the high-mass observation of PSR J1614-2230, and the authors note that further details on strangeness distribution and density profile may be relevant for gravitational wave signals from compact star mergers.
