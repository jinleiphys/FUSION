# Phases of a bilayer Fermi gas

> We investigate a two-species Fermi gas in which one species is confined in two parallel layers and interacts with the other species in the three-dimensional space by a tunable short-range interaction. Based on the controlled weak coupling analysis and the exact three-body calculation, we show that the system has a rich phase diagram in the plane of the effective scattering length and the layer separation. Resulting phases include an interlayer s-wave pairing, an intralayer p-wave pairing, a dimer Bose-Einstein condensation, and a Fermi gas of stable Efimov-like trimers. Our system provides a widely applicable scheme to induce long-range interlayer correlations in ultracold atoms.

# Phases of a bilayer Fermi gas

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a two-species Fermi gas with one species confined to two parallel layers and the other in three-dimensional space exhibits at least four distinct quantum phases, interlayer s-wave pairing, intralayer p-wave pairing, dimer Bose-Einstein condensation, and a Fermi gas of stable Efimov-like trimers, but the phase diagram is conjectured rather than proven, with the central region where multiple phases meet left as an open question.

## Method

The author performs a controlled weak-coupling perturbative analysis (to leading order in the effective scattering length) to compute the induced interaction between A atoms mediated by B atoms, then applies BCS mean-field theory to determine pairing gaps and compare energy densities for interlayer s-wave and intralayer p-wave pairing. For the strong-coupling and unitarity regimes, the author solves the exact three-body integral equations for two A atoms in different layers interacting with one B atom, obtaining the trimer binding energies as poles of the T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Critical layer separation dividing interlayer s-wave from intralayer p-wave pairing is plotted as a function of kFA/kFB and is independent of a_eff and m_A/m_B to leading order in a_eff
- Trimer binding energies are computed for mass ratios m_A/m_B = 0.15, 1, and 6.67 (corresponding to 6Li-40K, same-species, and 40K-6Li mixtures) as a function of d/a_eff
- At the two-body resonance |a_eff| → ∞, the geometric spectrum of trimer states follows E_3^{(n)} = -e^{-2π n/s_0} κ²/(2m_AB d²) for n → ∞, with scaling exponents s_0 = 0.741, 0.828, 1.30 and Efimov parameters κ = 0.0311, 0.0807, 0.231 for m_A/m_B = 0.15, 1, 6.67 respectively
- Dimer binding energy in the strong-coupling limit is E_2 = -1/(2m_AB a_eff²)
- BKT temperature for dimer BEC in the limit a_eff → +0 is T_BKT → (2π n_d / M) ln⁻¹(-380/(4π) ln(n_d a_eff²))

## Context

This work extends the author's prior studies of mixed-dimensional Fermi systems (Nishida 2008) and draws an analogy to the interlayer exciton condensation in bilayer semiconductors (Lozovik 1975, Shevchenko 1976), quantum Hall bilayers (Eisenstein 2004), and bilayer graphenes (Kharitonov 2009). The trimer states found at resonance resemble the Efimov effect in three dimensions (Efimov 1970) but are stabilized against three-body recombination because the two A atoms are spatially separated. The system is proposed as a tunable model for the slab phase of nuclear matter (Ravenhall 1983, Hashimoto 1984), and the intralayer p_x+ip_y-wave pairing is noted as potentially applicable to topological quantum computation (Read 2000, Tewari 2007).
