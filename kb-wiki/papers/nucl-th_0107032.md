# Extracting forward strong amplitudes from elastic differential cross sections

> The feasibility of a model-independent extraction of the forward strong amplitude from elastic nuclear cross section data in the Coulomb-nuclear interference region is assessed for $π$ and $K^+$ scattering at intermediate energies. Theoretically-generated "data" are analyzed to provide criteria for optimally designing experiments to measure these amplitudes, whose energy dependence (particularly that of the real parts) is needed for disentangling various sources of medium modifications of the projectile-nucleon interaction. The issues considered include determining the angular region over which to make the measurements, the role of the most forward angles measured, and the effects of statistical and systematic errors. We find that there is a region near the forward direction where Coulomb-nuclear interference allows reliable extraction of the strong forward amplitude for both pions and the $K^+$ from .3 to 1 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a model-independent extraction of the forward strong amplitude from elastic differential cross sections is feasible for pion and kaon scattering in the Coulomb-nuclear interference region, provided that statistical and systematic errors are kept below about 5% and that data are taken over an optimized angular range. The method is assessed using theoretically-generated "data" rather than actual experimental measurements, and the reliability depends on the specific projectile and momentum.

## Method

The authors generated model elastic differential cross sections for π⁻ and K⁺ scattering from ¹²C at momenta of 500 MeV/c and 1 GeV/c using a momentum-space optical model. They added Gaussian-distributed statistical errors and uniform systematic renormalizations to create synthetic data sets, then extracted the forward strong amplitude by fitting a Taylor-series expansion of the nuclear amplitude in powers of sin²(θ/2) to the Coulomb-subtracted cross sections. The extraction was tested for sensitivity to angular range, statistical error magnitude, systematic error magnitude, and the inclusion or removal of the most forward-angle data points.

## Key numbers

- For π⁻ at 500 MeV/c, the minimum angle for data collection is θ_min = 4°; at 1 GeV/c, θ_min = 2°.
- For both π⁻ and K⁺, the maximum angle for a linear expansion yielding better than 1% accuracy is approximately θ_max = 16°.
- For π⁻, the percent error in the extracted imaginary part A_I is approximately equal to the percent error in the data; for K⁺ at 500 MeV/c, the percent error in A_R is about three times the data error and in A_I about seven times the data error.
- For π⁻ at 500 MeV/c, each 1% systematic error produces only 0.3% error in extracted A_R and 0.5% error in A_I.
- For K⁺ at 500 MeV/c, each 1% systematic error produces 5.7% error in extracted A_I and 1.5% error in A_R.
- Removing the two most forward data points roughly doubles the errors in extracted parameters for data with 1% or 2% statistical errors.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Chen, Johnson, and Ernst (1998) that found inconsistencies between total cross sections extracted from elastic scattering data and those measured directly in transmission experiments for pions. The paper provides practical experimental design criteria for measuring the forward strong amplitude, which is needed to resolve disagreements among existing data sets and to constrain microscopic models of medium modifications of the projectile-nucleon interaction. The method enables future experiments to extract both the real and imaginary parts of the forward amplitude, with the real part providing new constraints on theories of in-medium resonance modifications for pions and enhanced kaon-nucleon interactions in the nuclear medium.
