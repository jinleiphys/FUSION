# Shower Power: Isolating the Prompt Atmospheric Neutrino Flux Using Electron Neutrinos

> At high energies, the very steep decrease of the conventional atmospheric component of the neutrino spectrum should allow the emergence of even small and isotropic components of the total spectrum, indicative of new physics, provided that they are less steeply decreasing, as generically expected. One candidate is the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux, a probe of cosmic ray composition in the region of the knee as well as small-$x$ QCD, below the reach of collider experiments. A second is the diffuse extragalactic background due to distant and unresolved AGNs and GRBs, a key test of the nature of the highest-energy sources in the universe. Separating these new physics components from the conventional atmospheric neutrino flux, as well as from each other, will be very challenging. We show that the charged-current {\it electron} neutrino "shower" channel should be particularly effective for isolating the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux, and that it is more generally an important complement to the usually-considered charged-current {\it muon} neutrino "track" channel. These conclusions remain true even for the low prompt atmospheric neutrino flux predicted in a realistic cosmic ray scenario with heavy and varying composition across the knee (Candia and Roulet, 2003 JCAP {\bf 0309}, 005). We also improve the corresponding calculation of the neutrino flux induced by cosmic ray collisions with the interstellar medium.

## Key claim

The charged-current electron neutrino "shower" channel can isolate the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux from the conventional atmospheric neutrino background more effectively than the usual muon neutrino "track" channel, even for the low prompt flux predicted in a realistic cosmic ray scenario with heavy and varying composition across the knee (Candia and Roulet, 2003).

## Method

The authors calculated the detectable shower spectra by convolving the assumed neutrino spectra (conventional atmospheric, prompt atmospheric, extragalactic, and Galactic) with differential cross sections, expressing results as a function of visible energy rather than neutrino energy. They excluded charged-current muon neutrino events (recognizable by long muon tracks) and considered only downgoing neutrinos to avoid Earth absorption effects, using a km^3 detector exposure over 10 years. The prompt atmospheric neutrino flux was evaluated using two different parton distribution functions (CTEQ3 and the Golec-Biernat, Wusthoff model with gluon saturation) to illustrate the range of small-x QCD uncertainties, and a mixed cosmic ray composition with all nuclear species from hydrogen to nickel was assumed following Candia and Roulet (2003).

## Key numbers

- The conventional atmospheric neutrino spectrum falls as E^{-gamma} with spectral index in the range gamma ~ 3-3.7.
- The prompt atmospheric neutrino flux predictions from different parton distribution functions spread over more than an order of magnitude.
- In the shower channel, the spectral break occurs about an order of magnitude lower in energy than in the muon neutrino channel.
- The neutral-current to charged-current cross section ratio is sigma_NC/sigma_CC ~ 0.4, and the average inelasticity <y> ~ 0.4-0.3 (falling with increasing energy).
- The muon neutrino shower fluxes from neutral-current interactions are suppressed by a factor ~ <y>^(gamma-1) x sigma_NC/sigma_CC, roughly an order of magnitude relative to electron neutrino charged-current shower fluxes.
- The AMANDA bound allows prompt atmospheric neutrino fluxes up to about two orders of magnitude larger than the low flux considered.
- For the Galactic flux, the column density in a typical direction is x_not-GC ~ 10^21 cm^-2, while towards the Galactic Center it is x_GC ~ 10^22 cm^-2.
- The Galactic Center region is defined as |b| <= 10 degrees and |l| <= 10 degrees, corresponding to a solid angle DeltaOmega_GC = 0.12 sr.
- The angular resolution for showers is about 20 degrees, compared to about 1 degree for the muon neutrino charged-current channel.
- Neutrinos passing through the whole diameter of Earth are absorbed at about 4 x 10^4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hooper et al. (2003) by expressing spectra as a function of visible energy rather than neutrino energy and by excluding all muon neutrino charged-current events, allowing consideration of much smaller prompt atmospheric and extragalactic fluxes. The paper builds on the realistic cosmic ray composition model of Candia and Roulet (2003) and improves the corresponding calculation of the neutrino flux induced by cosmic ray collisions with the interstellar medium. The technique enables future km^3 detectors like IceCube to isolate the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux as a probe of cosmic ray composition in the knee region and small-x QCD, and to distinguish it from the diffuse extragalactic background from AGNs and GRBs.
