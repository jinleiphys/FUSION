# Spontaneous heavy cluster emission rates using microscopic potentials

> The nuclear cluster radioactivities have been studied theoretically in the framework of a microscopic superasymmetric fission model (MSAFM). The nuclear interaction potentials required for binary cold fission processes are calculated by folding in the density distribution functions of the two fragments with a realistic effective interaction. The microscopic nuclear potential thus obtained has been used to calculate the action integral within the WKB approximation. The calculated half lives of the present MSAFM calculations are found to be in good agreement over a wide range of observed experimental data.

## Key claim

The microscopic superasymmetric fission model (MSAFM), using double-folded nuclear potentials from the M3Y effective interaction, reproduces cluster decay half-lives over a wide range of experimental data, with a normalization factor of 0.9 applied to the microscopic nuclear potential to obtain the optimum fit.

## Method

The nuclear interaction potential for binary cold fission was calculated by double-folding the density distributions of the two fragments (using a spherically symmetric Fermi form with radius parameter 1.13 A^(1/3) fm and diffuseness 0.54 fm) with the finite-range M3Y effective interaction. The total interaction energy (nuclear plus Coulomb) was used to compute the WKB action integral between the two turning points, and the half-life was obtained from the action integral and the zero-point vibration energy. Experimental ground-state masses were used for Q-values where available, otherwise theoretical masses from the mass table of Myers and Swiatecki (1994/1996) were employed.

## Key numbers

- For 221Fr emitting 14C: MSAFM log T = 13.39 s, experimental log T = 14.52 s
- For 222Ra emitting 14C: MSAFM log T = 10.41 s, experimental log T = 11.02 s
- For 226Ra emitting 14C: MSAFM log T = 20.36 s, experimental log T = 21.33 s
- For 228Th emitting 20O: MSAFM log T = 21.05 s, experimental log T = 20.86 s
- For 232U emitting 24Ne: MSAFM log T = 20.83 s, experimental log T = 21.06 s
- For 236Pu emitting 28Mg: MSAFM log T = 21.70 s, experimental log T = 21.68 s
- For 242Cm emitting 34Si: MSAFM log T = 24.16 s, experimental log T = 23.15 s
- The nuclear potential normalization factor used was 0.9
- The density distribution used a radius parameter R = 1.13 A^(1/3) fm and diffuseness a = 0.54 fm

## Context

This work extends the superasymmetric fission model (SAFM) approach of Poenaru, Greiner, and collaborators (1986, 1991) by replacing the phenomenological parabolic nuclear potentials with microscopically calculated double-folded potentials using the M3Y effective interaction. The MSAFM results are comparable to the best available ASAFM calculations of Poenaru et al. (1991) but place the SAFM on a firmer theoretical basis. The approach enables future calculations of cluster decay lifetimes for exotic nuclei and could be refined by incorporating dissipation during tunneling or dynamic shape deformations in the density distributions.
