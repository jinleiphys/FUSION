# Electroweak three-body decays in the presence of two- and three-body bound states

> Recently, formalism has been derived for studying electroweak transition amplitudes for three-body systems both in infinite and finite volumes. The formalism provides exact relations that the infinite-volume amplitudes must satisfy, as well as a relationship between physical amplitudes and finite-volume matrix elements, which can be constrained from lattice QCD calculations. This formalism poses additional challenges when compared with the analogous well-studied two-body equivalent one, including the necessary step of solving integral equations of singular functions. In this work, we provide some non-trivial analytical and numerical tests on the aforementioned formalism. In particular, we consider a case where the three-particle system can have three-body bound states as well as bound states in the two-body subsystem. For kinematics below the three-body threshold, we demonstrate that the scattering amplitudes satisfy unitarity. We also check that for these kinematics the finite-volume matrix elements are accurately described by the formalism for two-body systems up to exponentially suppressed corrections. Finally, we verify that in the case of the three-body bound state, the finite-volume matrix element is equal to the infinite-volume coupling of the bound state, up to exponentially suppressed errors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically and numerically that the recently developed relativistic three-body formalism for electroweak transitions correctly reduces to the well-established two-body Lellouch-Lüscher formalism when a two-body bound state (dimer) is present, and that finite-volume matrix elements for a three-body bound state (trimer) equal the infinite-volume coupling up to exponentially suppressed volume corrections, but only for kinematics below the three-body threshold and in the limit where the three-body K matrix is set to zero.

## Method

The authors perform analytical checks using the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann (LSZ) reduction formula to extract two-body amplitudes from the three-body formalism in the presence of a dimer, and verify that the resulting amplitudes satisfy Watson's theorem from S-matrix unitarity. They also carry out numerical tests of the finite-volume formalism by solving the partial-wave projected ladder integral equation for identical scalar particles with S-wave interactions, using a smooth cutoff function and a leading-order effective range expansion parametrization of the two-body scattering amplitude with a positive scattering length to generate a bound state.

## Key numbers

* The two-body bound state (dimer) mass is given by \( m_b = 2\sqrt{m^2 - 1/a^2} \), where \(a\) is the scattering length and the binding momentum is \(\kappa_b = 1/a\).
* The dimer coupling to two particles is \( g = 8\sqrt{2\pi\sqrt{s_b}\kappa_{b}} \), where \(s_b = m_b^2\).
* The two-body phase space for the \(\varphi b\) system is \(\rho_{\varphi b} = q_{\varphi b}/(8\pi\sqrt{s})\), with relative momentum \( q_{\varphi b} = \frac{1}{2E}\lambda^{1/2}(s, s_b, m^2) \).
* The trimer mass \(m_t\) satisfies \( m_t = \sqrt{m^2 - \kappa_t^2} + \sqrt{m_b^2 - \kappa_t^2} \), where \(\kappa_t\) is the trimer binding momentum.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic field-theoretic formalism for three-hadron electroweak transitions derived by Hansen et al. (2021) by providing the first non-trivial analytical and numerical consistency checks. It builds upon the two-body Lellouch-Lüscher formalism (Lellouch and Lüscher, 2000) and the three-body scattering formalism developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of processes such as \(\gamma^\star \to 3\pi\) and CP-violating kaon decays \(K \to 3\pi\) by validating that the three-body formalism correctly reproduces known two-body results in appropriate kinematic limits.
