# Shell corrections for finite depth potentials with bound states only

> A new method of calculating unique values of ground-state shell corrections for finite depth potentials is shown, which makes use of bound states only. It is based on (i) a general formulation of extracting the smooth part from any fluctuating quantity proposed by Strutinsky and Ivanjuk, (ii) a generalized Strutinsky smoothing condition suggested recently by Vertse et al., and (iii) the technique of the Lanczos $σ$ factors. Numerical results for some spherical heavy nuclei ($^{132,154}$Sn, $^{180,208}$Pb and $^{298}$114) are presented and compared to those obtained with the Green's function oscillator expansion method.

# Shell corrections for finite depth potentials with bound states only

## Key claim
A new method for calculating ground-state shell corrections in finite-depth nuclear potentials using only bound states is presented, which combines N-averaging with a generalized Strutinsky smoothing condition and Lanczos sigma factors to produce unique (converged) values. The method works well for well-bound (stable) nuclei but is not recommended for drip-line nuclei, where the results deviate significantly from the Green's Function Oscillator Expansion (GFOE) method.

## Method
The method uses the general Strutinsky-Ivanjuk formulation for extracting smooth parts from fluctuating quantities in the nucleon number space (N-averaging), combined with a generalized Strutinsky smoothing condition that minimizes the deviation of the smoothed single-particle level density from linearity in an intermediate energy region. The Lanczos sigma factors are applied to damp Gibbs oscillations in the curvature-correction polynomial expansion, improving convergence with respect to the polynomial degree M. Bound state energies are obtained using the Potential Separable Expansion (PSE) method for a spherical Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit coupling.

## Key numbers
- For $^{208}$Pb: neutron shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_n = -11.74$ MeV (present method) vs $\delta E_n = -11.00$ MeV (GFOE method); proton shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_p = -8.85$ MeV vs $\delta E_p = -8.12$ MeV
- For $^{132}$Sn: neutron shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_n = -8.70$ MeV vs $\delta E_n = -9.18$ MeV; proton shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_p = -7.40$ MeV vs $\delta E_p = -7.10$ MeV
- For $^{298}$114: neutron shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_n = -9.37$ MeV vs $\delta E_n = -8.40$ MeV; proton shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_p = -3.78$ MeV vs $\delta E_p = -3.90$ MeV
- For $^{154}$Sn (drip-line): neutron shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_n = -1.20$ MeV vs $\delta E_n = 9.30$ MeV
- For $^{180}$Pb (drip-line): proton shell correction $\bar{\delta U}_p = -0.22$ MeV vs $\delta E_p = -8.0$ MeV
- The maximal deviation for well-bound nuclei is about 1 MeV (neutrons in $^{298}$114)
- The optimal smoothing parameter $\Delta_{op}$ for neutrons in $^{208}$Pb was found to be 0.99 for M = 10
- The symmetry correction between N-averaging and E-averaging was found to be about 2-3 MeV for the nuclei studied

## Context
This work extends the Strutinsky method (Strutinsky, 1967; Brack, 1972) for calculating shell corrections, which works well for infinite-depth potentials but faces difficulties with finite-depth potentials where bound levels above the Fermi energy are insufficient. It builds on the generalized Strutinsky smoothing condition proposed by Vertse et al. (recent work) and the GFOE method developed in that same group, which required both bound and quasibound states. The new method avoids the need for unbound states entirely, which is advantageous for calculating potential energy surfaces in macroscopic-microscopic approaches for fusion and fission using two-center Woods-Saxon potentials. The method strongly reduces the known difference between N-averaging and E-averaging prescriptions for stable nuclei, but further investigation is needed for drip-line nuclei.
