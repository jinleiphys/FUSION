# Strangeness production in $O+O$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=7$ TeV using various model approaches

> We present the predictions of various observables for strange ($\mathrm{K}^{0}_{\mathrm S}$, $Λ$($\overlineΛ$)) and multi-strange hadrons ($Ξ^-$($\overlineΞ^+$), $ϕ$, and $Ω^-$($\overlineΩ^+$)) using the recently updated 3+1D hydrodynamics-based EPOS4 framework and AMPT model. In this study, we report the transverse momentum (${p_{\rm T}}$) spectra, particle yields (${\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}y}$), and ${p_{\rm T}}$ integrated yield ratios relative to pions for $O+O$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=7$~TeV. The results reveal that there are indications of stronger radial flow in EPOS4 compared to AMPT. We observe a final state multiplicity overlap with small ($p+p$ and $p+Pb$) and large ($Pb+Pb$) collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents model predictions for strange and multi-strange hadron production in oxygen-oxygen collisions at LHC energies, claiming that EPOS4 systematically predicts higher yields for multi-strange baryons than AMPT, and that the final-state multiplicity of O+O collisions overlaps with both small (p+p, p+Pb) and large (Pb+Pb) systems, but the abstract's hedge is that these are only "indications" of stronger radial flow in EPOS4 and that "none of the models can fully describe trends of strangeness enhancement observed in existing experimental data."

## Method

The authors simulated minimum-bias O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV using the EPOS4 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic framework with core-corona approach and the AMPT model in both default (AMPT-Def) and string melting (AMPT-SM) versions. Approximately 3 million events were generated with EPOS4 and 6 million events each with AMPT-SM and AMPT-Def, with multiplicity classes defined by charged particle pseudorapidity density within |eta|<0.5. The study computed transverse momentum spectra, rapidity density distributions (dN/dy), and pT-integrated yield ratios relative to pions for K0_S, Lambda+Antilambda, Xi-+Antixi+, Omega-+Antionega+, and phi mesons.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle multiplicity overlap range: from approximately 5 to 185 for AMPT and from approximately 5 to 230 for EPOS4 in terms of <dN_ch/deta>
- The 0-5% most central O+O collisions correspond to a charged-particle multiplicity comparable to 50-60% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions
- EPOS4 predicts higher dN/dy for Lambda, Xi, and Omega compared to both AMPT-Def and AMPT-SM
- The pT spectra of heavier particles exhibit a significantly flatter slope compared to lighter particles, indicating radial flow effects

## Context

This work extends predictions for strangeness production in small collision systems, building on recent LHC findings (ALICE Collaboration, 2010-2023) that revealed unexpected QGP-like behavior in high-multiplicity p+p and p+Pb collisions. The study uses the EPOS4 framework (Werner et al., 2020-2023) and AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) to provide baseline predictions for upcoming O+O data at the LHC, which will help constrain model parameters and test whether QGP formation occurs once a threshold in initial energy density is surpassed regardless of system size, as proposed by Kabana and Minkowski (2001).
