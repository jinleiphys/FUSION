# Charged Lepton Production from Iron Induced by Atmospheric Neutrinos

> The charged current lepton production induced by neutrinos in $^{56}Fe$ nuclei has been studied. The calculations have been done for the quasielastic as well as the inelastic reactions assuming $Δ$ dominance and take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and the renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium. The quasielastic production cross section for lepton production are found to be strongly reduced due to nuclear effects while there is about 10% reduction in the inelastic cross sections in the absence of the final state interactions of the pions. The numerical results for the momentum and angular distributions of the leptons averaged over the various atmospheric neutrino spectra at the Soudan and Gransasso sites have been presented. The effect of nuclear model dependence and the atmospheric flux dependence on the relative yield of $μ$ to e has been studied and discussed.

# Charged Lepton Production from Iron Induced by Atmospheric Neutrinos

## Key claim
The quasielastic charged-current lepton production cross section on iron is strongly reduced by nuclear effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and renormalization of weak transition strengths), while the inelastic cross section through Δ dominance shows only about 10% reduction when final-state interactions of pions are omitted. The relative yield of muons to electrons depends on both the nuclear model and the choice of atmospheric neutrino flux at the Soudan and Gran Sasso sites.

## Method
The authors calculated quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections using a local density approximation with a two-parameter Fermi density for ⁵⁶Fe, implementing Pauli blocking via the Lindhard function and including medium renormalization of weak transition strengths through random-phase approximation (RPA) correlations with π and ρ exchange potentials. For inelastic production, they assumed Δ dominance with a modified Δ propagator incorporating self-energy corrections (real and imaginary parts) from the nuclear medium, including Pauli blocking of the Δ → Nπ decay and additional absorption channels (ΔN → NN, ΔN → πNN, ΔNN → NNN).

## Key numbers
- Quasielastic cross section reduction at energies above 1 GeV: about 20% for neutrinos and 40% for antineutrinos due to nuclear medium effects beyond Pauli suppression.
- Inelastic Δ production cross section reduction: 5–10% due to nuclear medium effects.
- Q-value of 6.8 MeV used for νₗ + ⁵⁶Fe → l⁻ + ⁵⁶Co* reaction (transition to lowest 1⁺ state).
- Q-value of 4.3 MeV used for ν̄ₗ + ⁵⁶Fe → l⁺ + ⁵⁶Mn* reaction (transition to lowest 1⁺ state).
- Nuclear density parameters: c = 3.971 fm, z = 0.5935 fm for the two-parameter Fermi density.
- Axial mass M_A = 1.032 GeV for quasielastic form factors; M_A = 1.28 GeV for Δ transition form factors.
- Vector mass M_V = 0.84 GeV for quasielastic; M_V² = 0.54 GeV² for Δ vector form factors.
- Landau-Migdal parameter g′ = 0.7 used for short-range correlations.
- At E_ν ≈ 1.4 GeV, quasielastic and inelastic lepton production cross sections become comparable; below 1.4 GeV, quasielastic events dominate for fully contained events.
- Reduction in iron cross section is about 5% more than the reduction in carbon at energies ≥ 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of neutrino-nuclear cross sections for ¹⁶O (relevant to IMB and Kamiokande) and ¹²C (relevant to LSND and KARMEN) to ⁵⁶Fe, which is the target material for Soudan, FREJUS, NUSEX, and planned detectors like MINOS, MONOLITH, and INO. The authors compare their quasielastic results with the Fermi gas model of Llewellyn Smith (1972) and with experimental data from LSND, SKAT, and other experiments on lighter nuclear targets (carbon, freon, aluminum). The model improves agreement with antineutrino data compared to the Fermi gas model. The results enable more accurate predictions of lepton momentum and angular distributions for atmospheric neutrino experiments, and the formalism can be extended to calculate zenith angle dependence and L/E distributions for stopping and through-going muons from iron.
