# On the origin of current quark mass within nonperturbative QCD

> We develop the theory of fermion induced phononlike excitations of the instanton liquid. It suggests a mechanism of current quark mass generation which is easily understandable by calculating the corresponding functional integral in the tadpole approximation. We systematically study the quark condensate excitations influenced by the phononlike modes and rederive the relation Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner with realistic pion mass. The picture of $σ$-meson as being mixed with the soft scalar glueball-like excitation is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the current quark mass arises from a small "inverse influence" of quarks on the instanton liquid, specifically through phononlike excitations (adiabatic size deformations of instantons) that are treated as a small perturbation around the equilibrium instanton liquid parameters, rather than from a fundamentally new mechanism beyond the instanton liquid approach.

## Method

The authors extend the instanton liquid model by introducing deformable (anti-)instanton configurations whose size \(\rho\) becomes a slowly varying field \(\rho(x,z)\), and they calculate the fermion determinant in the tadpole approximation, integrating out the scalar deformation field \(\varphi = \rho - \rho_c\) to obtain an effective four-fermion interaction. The quark Green function is then solved self-consistently in momentum space, and the saddle point equation for the auxiliary parameter \(\mu\) is solved iteratively to account for the shift in equilibrium instanton size induced by the quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The mass gap of the phononlike excitations is estimated as \(M \approx 1.21\,\Lambda\) for \(N_c = 3\) in the quenched approximation, using \(c = 4\), \(\bar\rho \Lambda \approx 0.37\), \(\beta \approx 17.5\), and \(n \Lambda^{-4} \approx 0.44\).
- The kinetic coefficient \(\kappa\) is estimated to be \(\kappa \sim c \beta\) with \(c \sim 1.5 \div 6\) (a few instanton actions), but the final current quark mass expression cancels \(\kappa\).
- The current quark mass is given by \(m_f(k) = \frac{N_c}{n\bar\rho^4} \frac{\mu^3}{\nu} c(\mu) \gamma_1(k,k)\), where \(\nu = (b-4)/2\) and \(b = (11 N_c - 2 N_f)/3\).
- The average instanton size is \(\bar\rho \Lambda = \exp\left\{-\frac{2N_c}{2\nu-1}\right\}\), and the packing fraction parameter is \(n\bar\rho^4 \approx 0.44\) (in quenched approximation).

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model of Diakonov and Petrov (1984) by incorporating the back-reaction of quarks on the instanton ensemble, which was previously neglected. It provides a mechanism for generating the current quark mass within the nonperturbative QCD framework, and it rederives the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation with a realistic pion mass. The approach enables further study of meson excitations and the mixing of the \(\sigma\)-meson with soft scalar glueball-like excitations, and it suggests a connection between the phononlike modes and the quark condensate fluctuations.
