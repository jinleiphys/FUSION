# The Spinless Relativistic Kink-Like Problem

> We constrain the possible bound-state solutions of the spinless Salpeter equation (the most obvious semirelativistic generalization of the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation) with an interaction between the bound-state constituents given by the kink-like potential (a central potential of hyperbolic-tangent form) by formulating a bunch of very elementary boundary conditions to be satisfied by all solutions of the eigenvalue problem posed by a bound-state equation of this type, only to learn that all results produced by a procedure very much liked by some quantum-theory practitioners prove to be in severe conflict with our expectations.

# The Spinless Relativistic Kink-Like Problem

## Key claim
The authors derive rigorous constraints on bound-state solutions of the spinless Salpeter equation with a kink-like (hyperbolic-tangent) potential, and demonstrate that a recent approximate solution by Hassanabadi et al. (2013) violates even the most elementary of these constraints, producing negative binding energies for a non-negative potential where all binding energies must be non-negative.

## Method
The authors apply operator inequality techniques and spectral comparison theorems to the spinless Salpeter Hamiltonian H = T(p) + V(r), where T(p) = sqrt(p² + m₁²) + sqrt(p² + m₂²) and V(r) = κ ρ tanh(ρ r). They bound the Hamiltonian from above by the nonrelativistic Schrödinger Hamiltonian and from below by Coulomb and exponential potentials, then use Bargmann's bound and Daubechies' bound to constrain the maximum number of bound states. These rigorous constraints are compared against the approximate solutions published by Hassanabadi et al. (2013).

## Key numbers
- The kink-like potential ranges from V_K(0) = 0 to V_K(∞) = κ ρ, so all binding energies B_k satisfy 0 ≤ B_k ≤ κ ρ
- For the equal-mass case with ρ = 0.0001 and κ = 0.1, the binding energy range is 0 ≤ B_k ≤ 10⁻⁵ (arbitrary units)
- Daubechies bound constant C = 14.107590867 for m > 0, C = 6.074898097 for m = 0
- Bargmann bound gives N_NR < (π² μ κ)/(24 ρ) × ((π² μ κ)/(12 ρ) + 1)
- For parameters from Figure 1 of Hassanabadi et al. (m₁ = m₂ = 0.5, ρ = 0.0001, κ = 0.01): relativistic bound N ≤ 171, nonrelativistic bound N_NR ≤ 221
- For parameters from Figure 2 (m₁ = m₂ = 0.5, ρ = 0.0001, κ = 0.1): relativistic bound N ≤ 5419, nonrelativistic bound N_NR ≤ 21241
- For parameters from Figure 3 (m₁ = m₂ = 2.0, ρ = 0.0001, κ = 0.1): relativistic bound N ≤ 43356, nonrelativistic bound N_NR ≤ 338637

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous rigorous studies of spinless Salpeter equations (Lucha 1996, 1999, 2004) and applies operator inequality methods to the kink-like potential, which had previously been studied in the Dirac equation by Grosse (1992) and Castro (2006), and in the Klein-Gordon equation by Castro (2007). The paper directly contradicts the approximate solutions published by Hassanabadi et al. (2013) in the same journal, showing that those solutions produce negative binding energies in violation of the fundamental constraint that binding energies must be non-negative for a non-negative potential. The rigorous bounds derived here enable future workers to check any proposed solution against elementary necessary conditions before accepting it.
