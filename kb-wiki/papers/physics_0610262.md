# Optical properties and one-particle spectral function in non-ideal plasmas

> A basic concept to calculate physical features of non-ideal plasmas, such as optical properties, is the spectral function which is linked to the self-energy. We calculate the spectral function for a non-relativistic hydrogen plasma in $GW$-approximation. In order to go beyond $GW$ approximation, we include self-energy and vertex correction to the polarization function in lowest order. Partial compensation is observed. The relation of our approach to $GW$ and $GWΓ$ calculations in other fields, such as the band-structure calculations in semiconductor physics, is discussed. From the spectral function we derive the absorption coefficient due to inverse bremsstrahlung via the polarization function. As a result, a significant reduction of the absorption as compared to the Bethe-Heitler formula for bremsstrahlung is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including self-energy and vertex corrections beyond the GW approximation leads to a significant reduction of the absorption coefficient due to inverse bremsstrahlung compared to the Bethe-Heitler formula, but the authors note that the vertex correction gives at most a 20% reduction of the self-energy, indicating partial compensation between the two corrections.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-particle spectral function for a non-relativistic hydrogen plasma using the GW approximation and then extend it to a GWGamma approximation by including self-energy and vertex corrections to the polarization function in lowest order. They solve the coupled equations for the self-energy and spectral function iteratively, first using a full RPA screened interaction and then a simplified static Born approximation for electron-ion collisions, and finally compute the absorption coefficient from the dielectric function via the polarization function.

## Key numbers

- Solar core plasma parameters: temperature T = 100 Ry (approximately 1360 eV) and particle density n = 7 x 10^24 cm^-3
- Degeneracy parameter theta = k_B T / E_F approximately 10; coupling parameter Gamma approximately 0.03
- At low frequencies, reduction of free-free absorption of about 15% compared to Born approximation
- Vertex correction gives at most a 20% reduction of the self-energy
- Momentum used for spectral function plots: p = 0.21 a_B^-1
- Gaussian spectral function widths compared: sigma = 6.5 Ry and sigma = 13 Ry

## Context

This work extends earlier GW calculations for non-ideal plasmas by Fehr et al. and Wierling et al. (1998), who studied spectral properties using perturbative and self-consistent GW approaches respectively. The paper also relates to band-structure calculations in semiconductor physics using GW and GWGamma methods, such as those by Northrup et al. for silicon and by Takada (2001) for aluminum. The results enable improved predictions of optical properties for astrophysical plasmas like the solar core, where the Bethe-Heitler formula overestimates absorption.
