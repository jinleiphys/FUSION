# Shell model calculation of the beta- and beta+ partial halflifes of 54Mn and other unique second forbidden beta decays

> The nucleus 54Mn has been observed in cosmic rays. In astrophysical environments it is fully stripped of its atomic electrons and its decay is dominated by the beta- branch to the 54Fe ground state. Application of 54Mn based chronometer to study the confinement of the iron group cosmic rays requires knowledge of the corresponding halflife, but its measurement is impossible at the present time. However, the branching ratio for the related beta+ decay of 54Mn was determined recently. We use the shell model with only a minimal truncation and calculate both beta+ and beta- decay rates of 54Mn. Good agreement for the beta+ branch suggests that the calculated partial halflife of the beta- decay, (4.94 \pm 0.06) x 10^5 years, should be reliable. However, this halflife is noticeably shorter than the range 1-2 x 10^6 y indicated by the fit based on the 54Mn abundance in cosmic rays. We also evaluate other known unique second forbidden beta decays from the nuclear p and sd shells (10Be, 22Na, and two decay branches of 26Al) and show that the shell model can describe them with reasonable accuracy as well.

## Key claim

The shell model calculation predicts a partial beta- halflife for fully stripped 54Mn of (4.94 ± 0.06) × 10^5 years, which is noticeably shorter than the range of 1-2 × 10^6 years inferred from cosmic ray abundance data, though the calculation reproduces the measured beta+ branch within errors.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations for the pf-shell nuclei using the KB3 residual interaction with an inert 40Ca core, allowing up to 7 particles in the r-orbits (p3/2, p1/2, f5/2) and diagonalizing with the Lanczos method using the code ANTOINE. For the unique second forbidden transitions, they evaluated the nuclear form factor using both harmonic oscillator and Woods-Saxon radial wave functions, with the oscillator length parameter b determined from experimental charge radii. They also performed full sd-shell calculations without truncation for 10Be, 22Na, and 26Al using the Wildenthal USD effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- 54Mn beta+ partial halflife: calculated (HO) 5.55 × 10^8 years, experimental (6.7 ± 1.3) × 10^8 years
- 54Mn beta- partial halflife: calculated (HO) 4.89 × 10^5 years, calculated (Woods-Saxon) 4.98 × 10^5 years
- 54Mn beta+ form factor: calculated (HO) 7.82 fm^2, experimental 7.1 ± 0.7 fm^2
- 54Mn beta- form factor: calculated (HO) 11.7 fm^2, calculated (Woods-Saxon) 11.6 fm^2
- 10Be beta- halflife: calculated (HO) 1.18 × 10^6 years, experimental (1.51 ± 0.06) × 10^6 years
- 22Na beta+ halflife: calculated (HO) 2.04 × 10^3 years, experimental (4.8 ± 1.3) × 10^3 years
- 26Al beta+ to first excited state (1.809 MeV) in 26Mg: calculated (HO) 8.64 × 10^5 years, experimental (9.1 ± 0.4) × 10^5 years
- 54Mn electric quadrupole moment: calculated Q = 34 e fm^2, experimental Q = 33 ± 3 e fm^2
- 54Mn magnetic moment: calculated μ = 2.78 μ_N, experimental μ = 3.2819 ± 0.0013 μ_N
- Weighted mean of measured beta+ branching ratio: (1.28 ± 0.25) × 10^{-9}

## Context

This work extends the earlier truncated shell model calculations of Warburton, Garvey, and Towner (1970) for unique second forbidden decays in the p and sd shells, using modern full-space calculations without truncation. The calculated beta- halflife for 54Mn is shorter than the range inferred from cosmic ray abundance measurements by DuVernois (1996, 1997), creating a tension that motivates experimental determination of the beta- branching ratio. The results also update comparisons for 10Be and 26Al using new experimental data from Ajzenberg-Selove (1988) and Endt (1990).
