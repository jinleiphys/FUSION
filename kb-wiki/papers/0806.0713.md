# Spectroscopic factors for alpha decay in the $N_pN_n$ scheme

> Lifetime values for alpha decay in even-even nuclei with $Z=84-98$ and $N=128-152$ have been calculated in the superasymmetric fission model. The interaction between the alpha particle and the daughter nucleus has been formed in the double folding approach using a density dependent NN interaction. The densities have been obtained using the Relativistic Mean Field formalism. The spectroscopic factors for the decays have been deduced and are shown to vary smoothly as a function of effective numbers of valence nucleons, $N_p$ and $N_n$ chosen with a suitable core. The implication of such a smooth behaviour has been discussed.

## Key claim

Spectroscopic factors for alpha decay in even-even nuclei with Z=84-98 and N=128-152 vary smoothly as a function of the Casten factor P = N_p N_n / (N_p + N_n), but only when the effective numbers of valence protons and neutrons are chosen by invoking subshell closures (e.g., at Z=92, Z=94, N=138, N=152) that are not the standard magic numbers; the smooth trend fails for Pu (Z=94) isotopes, which could not be fitted into the scheme.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha-decay half-lives in the superasymmetric fission model using a double-folding potential between the alpha particle and the daughter nucleus, employing the density-dependent DDM3Y1 nucleon-nucleon interaction. Nuclear densities for both the alpha particle and the daughter nuclei were obtained from Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations using the FSU Gold parameter set, with pairing treated in the BCS approximation. Spectroscopic factors were extracted as the ratio of the calculated half-life to the experimental value, and then plotted against the Casten factor P using effective valence nucleon numbers chosen to produce a smooth trend.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for alpha decay of 212Po: S = 0.019 (compared to theoretical value 0.025 from prior work and 0.031 from Mohr using experimental charge distributions)
- The zero-range pairing force strength used in RMF+BCS: 300 MeV-fm for both protons and neutrons
- Parameters of the DDM3Y1 interaction: C = 0.2845, alpha = 3.6391, beta = 2.9605 fm^2
- In 234Cm, the gap between the nu 1i_11/2 state and other states in the same shell is more than 3.8 MeV, more than double the gap observed in 218Po
- The smooth trend in spectroscopic factors is observed for P values greater than or equal to 1.0

## Context

This work extends the N_p N_n scheme, previously applied by Casten (1985) and collaborators to deformation, B(E2) values, and energy systematics, to alpha-decay spectroscopic factors in heavy actinides. The authors use effective valence nucleon numbers, following the approach of Zhao et al. (2000) for the Z=64 shell gap, to account for subshell closures at Z=92, Z=94, N=138, and N=152 that emerge from the RMF calculations. The smooth behavior of spectroscopic factors and binding energy differences as functions of P suggests that the residual neutron-proton interaction is the primary driver of these observables, and the failure of Pu isotopes to follow the trend mirrors similar difficulties encountered by Casten (1985) for B(E2) values in nuclei near N=90-92. The results enable future investigations into the microscopic origin of effective valence nucleon numbers, particularly around Z=94 and N=152.
