# Caloric curve for nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in relativistic mean-field hadronic model

> The main thermodynamical properties of the first order phase transition of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model were explored in the isobaric, the canonical and the grand canonical ensembles on the basis of the method of the thermodynamical potentials and their first derivatives. It was proved that the first order phase transition of the RMF model is the liquid-gas type one associated with the Gibbs free energy $G$. The thermodynamical potential $G$ is the piecewise smooth function and its first order partial derivatives with respect to variables of state are the piecewise continuous functions. We have found that the energy in the caloric curve is discontinuous in the isobaric and the grand canonical ensembles at fixed values of the pressure and the chemical potential, respectively, and it is continuous, i.e. it has no plateau, in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles at fixed values of baryon density, while the baryon density in the isotherms is discontinuous in the isobaric and the canonical ensembles at fixed values of the temperature. The general criterion for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in the canonical ensemble was identified.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the first-order phase transition in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model is of the liquid-gas type associated with the Gibbs free energy G, and demonstrates that the caloric curve (energy versus temperature) is continuous with no plateau in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles at fixed baryon density, contrary to the discontinuous behavior seen in the isobaric and grand canonical ensembles.

## Method

The author analyzed the RMF hadronic model using the method of thermodynamic potentials and their first derivatives across four statistical ensembles: grand canonical (T,V,μ), isobaric (T,p,B), canonical (T,V,B), and microcanonical. The Maxwell construction was applied to handle multiple-valued thermodynamic functions, and the critical point was determined from the condition min(∂μ/∂ρ)=0. The model parameters were fixed at T=0 using the binding energy per nucleon εb0 = -16 MeV and normal nuclear density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Critical point: Tc = 18.927 MeV, μc = 910.033 MeV, ρc = 0.441 ρ0 (where ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Normal nuclear density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: εb0 = -16 MeV
- Nucleon mass: m = 939 MeV
- Chemical potential at T=0 on coexistence curve: μ0 = 923 MeV (m + εb0)
- Dimensionless coupling constants at T=0: Cv² ≈ 250, Cs² ≈ 330
- Critical pressure: pc = 0.484 MeV fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions in the RMF model (Walecka 1974, Serot 1986) by providing a systematic analysis across multiple statistical ensembles using the Ehrenfest classification. The finding that the caloric curve has no plateau in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles resolves a long-standing discrepancy between different classes of statistical multifragmentation models: some (Bondorf 1985, Gross 1990) predict a plateau in these ensembles while others (Elliott 2000, Samaddar 2004, Aguiar 2006) predict a smooth transition. The results enable a clear criterion to distinguish liquid-gas phase transitions (associated with Gibbs free energy G) from first-order transitions associated with Helmholtz free energy F, which is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments such as the 600 MeV/nucleon Au+Au experiment (Pochodzalla) and the 1 GeV/nucleon Au+C experiment (Hauger).
