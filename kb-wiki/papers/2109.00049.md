# Bayesian Estimation of the D(p,$γ$)$^3$He Thermonuclear Reaction Rate

> Big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) is the standard model theory for the production of the light nuclides during the early stages of the universe, taking place for a period of about 20 minutes after the big bang. Deuterium production, in particular, is highly sensitive to the primordial baryon density and the number of neutrino species, and its abundance serves as a sensitive test for the conditions in the early universe. The comparison of observed deuterium abundances with predicted ones requires reliable knowledge of the relevant thermonuclear reaction rates, and their corresponding uncertainties. Recent observations reported the primordial deuterium abundance with percent accuracy, but some theoretical predictions based on BBN are at tension with the measured values because of uncertainties in the cross section of the deuterium-burning reactions. In this work, we analyze the S-factor of the D(p,$γ$)$^3$He reaction using a hierarchical Bayesian model. We take into account the results of eleven experiments, spanning the period of 1955--2021; more than any other study. We also present results for two different fitting functions, a two-parameter function based on microscopic nuclear theory and a four-parameter polynomial. Our recommended reaction rates have a 2.2\% uncertainty at $0.8$~GK, which is the temperature most important for deuterium BBN. Differences between our rates and previous results are discussed.

## Key claim
The D(p,γ)³He reaction rate uncertainty is reduced to 2.2% at 0.8 GK (the temperature most important for deuterium BBN) using a hierarchical Bayesian model that includes eleven experiments spanning 1955–2021, but the resulting predicted primordial deuterium abundance may still show tension with observations depending on which other reaction rates are adopted in BBN simulations.

## Method
The authors analyzed S-factor data from eleven direct and one reverse (³He(γ,p)D) experiment using a hierarchical Bayesian model that separately treats statistical and systematic uncertainties, and allows for non-Gaussian error distributions. Two fitting functions were employed: a two-parameter function (multiplicative scale factor plus offset) applied to the microscopic nuclear theory S-factor of Marcucci et al. (2005), and a four-parameter cubic polynomial. Data from five experiments lacking separate statistical and systematic uncertainties were treated as relative S-factors to extract only energy-dependence information.

## Key numbers
- Recommended reaction rate uncertainty of 2.2% at 0.8 GK, the temperature most important for deuterium BBN
- Eleven experiments included in the analysis, spanning 1955–2021
- The microscopic theory S-factors of Marcucci et al. (2005) and Marcucci et al. (2016) differ by >7% near 91 keV (center of Gamow peak at 0.8 GK), but their energy dependence agrees within 0.8% over the BBN energy range
- The D(p,γ)³He rate used by Pitrou et al. (2018) had 3.7% uncertainty at 0.8 GK (from Iliadis et al. 2016), compared to the present 2.2%

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian hierarchical model approach previously applied to D(p,γ)³He by Iliadis et al. (2016) and to other BBN reactions by Gomez et al. (2017) and de Souza et al. (2019, 2020). It incorporates three new experimental datasets (Tisma et al. 2019; Mossa et al. 2020; Turkat et al. 2021) that were not available to earlier analyses. The reduced rate uncertainty enables more precise BBN predictions of primordial deuterium abundance, which can be compared to the observed value of Cooke et al. (2018) to test for possible new physics beyond the standard model.
