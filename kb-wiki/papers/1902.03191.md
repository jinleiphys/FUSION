# Symmetries of spatial meson correlators in high temperature QCD

> Based on a complete set of $J = 0$ and $J=1$ spatial isovector correlation functions calculated with $N_F = 2$ domain wall fermions we identify an intermediate temperature regime of $T \sim 220 - 500$ MeV ($1.2T_c$--$2.8T_c$), where chiral symmetry is restored but the correlators are not yet compatible with a simple free quark behavior. More specifically, in the temperature range $T \sim 220 - 500$ MeV we identify a multiplet structure of spatial correlators that suggests emergent $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(4)$ symmetries, which are not symmetries of the free Dirac action. The symmetry breaking effects in this temperature range are less than 5%. Our results indicate that at these temperatures the chromo-magnetic interaction is suppressed and the elementary degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound into color-singlet objects by the chromo-electric component of the gluon field. At temperatures between 500 and 660 MeV the emergent $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(4)$ symmetries disappear and one observes a smooth transition to the regime above $T \sim 1$ GeV where only chiral symmetries survive, which are finally compatible with quasi-free quarks.

## Key claim

The paper identifies an intermediate temperature regime between approximately 220 and 500 MeV (1.2T_c to 2.8T_c) where spatial meson correlators exhibit emergent SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries that are not symmetries of the free Dirac action, with symmetry breaking effects below 5%, contradicting the expectation that quarks above T_c behave as quasi-free deconfined particles.

## Method

The authors computed a complete set of J=0 and J=1 spatial isovector correlation functions using lattice QCD with N_F=2 domain wall fermions, analyzing the multiplet structure of these correlators as a function of temperature. They compared the full QCD correlators to analytic calculations of free continuum correlators for massless quarks, using the pattern of degeneracies among different Dirac structures (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor-vector, axial-tensor-vector) to identify emergent symmetries across a temperature range from approximately 220 MeV to 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range of emergent SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries: T ~ 220 - 500 MeV (1.2T_c to 2.8T_c)
- Symmetry breaking effects in this temperature range: less than 5%
- Temperature range where emergent symmetries disappear: 500 to 660 MeV
- Temperature above which only chiral symmetries survive and behavior becomes compatible with quasi-free quarks: T ~ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice study (Rohrhofer et al., 2017) which identified SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries in J=1 correlators up to T ~ 380 MeV, now extending the analysis to T ~ 1 GeV and including J=0 correlators. The results contradict the simple picture that chiral symmetry restoration above T_c implies deconfined quasi-free quarks, and instead suggest that between 1.2T_c and 2.8T_c the effective degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound into color-singlet objects by the chromo-electric component of the gluon field. The observed approximate symmetries should persist at finite chemical potential since the quark chemical potential term is manifestly SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetric, as noted by Glozman (2015).
