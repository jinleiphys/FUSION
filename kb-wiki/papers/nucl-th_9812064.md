# Pion Exchange Effects in Elastic Backward Proton-Deuteron Scattering

> The elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation with realistic meson-exchange interaction. Contributions of the one-nucleon and one-pion exchange mechanisms to the cross section and polarization observables are investigated in explicit form. Results of numerical calculations for the cross section, tensor analyzing power and spin transfers are presented. The one-pion exchange contribution is essential for describing the spin averaged cross section, while in polarization observables it is found to be less important.

## Key claim

The one-pion exchange mechanism is essential for describing the spin-averaged cross section of elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering, but it is found to be less important for polarization observables such as the tensor analyzing power and spin transfers.

## Method

The authors analyze elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach using a realistic meson-exchange interaction. They compute the total amplitude as a sum of the one-nucleon exchange mechanism and triangle diagrams representing one-pion exchange, with the subprocess amplitude for pp to pi+ D taken from the partial-wave analysis of Arndt et al. The contributions to the cross section, tensor analyzing power T20, and polarization transfers kappa_D to p and kappa_p to p are evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion exchange contribution is significant in the region of outgoing proton momentum P_lab approximately 0.2 to 0.4 GeV/c, corresponding to initial proton momentum p approximately 0.66 to 1.85 GeV/c.
- The cross section formula for the one-nucleon exchange mechanism with only positive-energy Bethe-Salpeter waves is given by dsigma_0/dOmega = (12 m^2 / S) (Psi_S^2(P_Lab) + Psi_D^2(P_Lab))^2 P_Lab^4.
- The tensor analyzing power T20 and polarization transfer kappa_D to p are shown in figures to be essentially unaffected by the triangle diagrams, with no improvement in agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends previous Bethe-Salpeter studies of elastic pD scattering by the same authors (Kaptari et al., 1996) that considered only one-nucleon exchange. It addresses the failure of the impulse approximation to describe the cross section, which had been noted by earlier authors including Wilkin (1980), Nakamura (1984), and Kolybasov (1985). The results enable future experiments at facilities such as COSY to probe effects beyond one-nucleon and one-pion exchange, such as Delta-Delta components in the deuteron wave function.
