# Production of charged pions off nuclei with 3...30 GeV incident protons and pions

> We compare calculations for the production of charged pions by pion or proton beams off nuclei calculated within our coupled channel transport model (GiBUU) with recent data of the HARP collaboration for beam energies from 3 up to 13 GeV. Predictions for the 30 GeV data for pions and kaons from the NA61/SHINE experiment are included.

## Key claim
The GiBUU coupled-channel transport model, using Pythia 6.4 for elementary hadron-nucleon interactions without any fine-tuning to the HARP data, reproduces charged-pion production cross sections from 3 to 13 GeV beam energies on nuclear targets, but shows systematic discrepancies at very forward and very backward angles for proton-induced reactions that are attributed to final-state interactions rather than the primary interaction.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations for 61 baryons and 21 mesons, using a test-particle ansatz. Elementary hadron-nucleon collisions are treated with the Pythia 6.4 event generator for invariant masses above 2.2 GeV (meson-baryon) or 2.6 GeV (baryon-baryon), with a smooth transition from a resonance description at lower energies. Produced pre-hadrons are propagated through the nuclear medium with reduced cross sections during their hadronization time, incorporating Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers
- Beam momentum range: 3 to 13 GeV/c for HARP data comparisons, with predictions for 30 GeV/c for NA61/SHINE
- Invariant mass range for elementary collisions: sqrt(s) = 2.8 to 4.9 GeV for both pion-nucleon and proton-nucleon systems
- Forward spectrometer data: detected pion momenta up to 8 GeV/c for 12 GeV/c proton beam on carbon
- Large-angle spectrometer data: all detected pion momenta below 1 GeV/c for 3 GeV proton beam
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom values: up to ~20 for theta < 950 mrad and theta > 1550 mrad with proton beam in large-angle analysis; drops to ~4 for intermediate angles; ~3 for negative pion and ~1 for positive pion channels in forward-angle pion-induced data
- Switching thresholds: sqrt(s) = 2.2 ± 0.2 GeV for meson-baryon collisions, 2.6 ± 0.2 GeV for baryon-baryon collisions

## Context
This work extends the GiBUU transport model comparisons to the HARP experiment data (Catanesi 2008), which previously showed discrepancies up to factors of three for other generator models used in GEANT4 and MARS simulation packages. The model covers the full energy range from 3 to 13 GeV where other approaches required separate low-energy and high-energy treatments. The predictions for 30 GeV proton beams on carbon are intended for the NA61/SHINE experiment (Antoniou 2006, Abgrall 2007) to reduce uncertainties on neutrino flux calculations for T2K, and the results enable future studies of formation time effects and color transparency from beam energy dependence.
