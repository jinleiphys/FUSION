# Additional $J/Ψ$ Suppression from High Density Effects

> At high energies the saturation effects associated to the high parton density should modify the behavior of the observables in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering. In this paper we investigate the saturation effects in the nuclear $J/Ψ$ production and estimate the modifications in the energy dependence of the cross section as well as in the length of the nuclear medium. In particular, we calculate the ratio of $J/Ψ$ to Drell-Yan cross sections and show that it is strongly modified if the high density effects are included. Moreover, our results are compared with the data from the NA50 Collaboration and predictions for the RHIC and LHC kinematic regions are presented. We predict an additional $J/Ψ$ suppression associated to the high density effects.

# Additional $J/\Psi$ Suppression from High Density Effects

## Key claim
The authors predict an additional suppression of $J/\Psi$ production in nuclear collisions at high energies due to high parton density (saturation) effects in the nuclear wave functions, independent of quark-gluon plasma formation. This suppression is presented as a lower bound for the total $J/\Psi$ suppression, since if a QGP is formed, additional deconfinement mechanisms would further reduce the yield.

## Method
The authors use the color evaporation model (CEM) within collinear factorization to compute $J/\Psi$ production cross sections in $pA$ and $AA$ collisions. They incorporate high density effects into nuclear parton distributions using the AG parameterization, which combines the Glauber-Mueller formula for saturation with the DGLAP-based EKS parameterization. Final state interactions of the $c\bar{c}$ pair with the nuclear medium are included via the QVZ model, where multiple scatterings increase the relative momentum of the pair, pushing some pairs above the open charm threshold.

## Key numbers
- The ratio $\sigma_{J/\Psi}/\sigma_{DY}$ in $pA$ processes is modified by almost 40% at LHC energies when high density effects are included, compared to calculations without these effects.
- For SPS energies, the parameter $\epsilon^2$ (squared relative momentum gained per unit length) must be adjusted from 0.225 GeV$^2$/fm to 0.250 GeV$^2$/fm when initial state nuclear effects (EKS parameterization) are included, to describe the NA50 Pb-Pb data.
- The saturation momentum scale $Q_s$ is estimated as $Q_s \sim 1$ GeV at RHIC and $Q_s \sim 2-3$ GeV at LHC.
- The normalization factor $K N_{J/\Psi} = 0.250$ is used for the initial state analysis, and $K N_{J/\Psi} = 0.458$ for the final state analysis following QVZ model conventions.

## Context
This work extends the study of $J/\Psi$ suppression beyond the standard final-state interaction models (e.g., comover absorption by Capella (1998) and Kharsatz (1998), QGP suppression by Blaizot (1996), percolation deconfinement by Nardi and Satz (1998)) by including initial-state high density effects. The authors use the AG parameterization (Ayala Filho and Goncalves, 2001) which improves upon the EKS parameterization (Eskola, Kolhinen, Salgado, 1999) by incorporating perturbative saturation effects via the Glauber-Mueller formula. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC to disentangle initial-state saturation effects from final-state QGP signatures, particularly through $pA$ collisions where QGP formation is not expected.
