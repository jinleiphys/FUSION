# Dyson-Schwinger Equations - aspects of the pion

> The contemporary use of Dyson-Schwinger equations in hadronic physics is exemplified via applications to the calculation of pseudoscalar meson masses, and inclusive deep inelastic scattering with a determination of the pion's valence-quark distribution function.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework yields a model-independent identity, \(f_H m_H^2 = \mathcal{M}_H^\zeta r_H^\zeta\), that unifies the description of pseudoscalar mesons from the light-quark (Goldstone) regime to the heavy-quark regime, but the heavy-quark linear mass trajectory is a poor approximation for current-quark masses below that of the \(b\)-quark.

## Method

The authors solved the dressed-quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations for the axial-vector and pseudovector vertices, using a chiral-symmetry-preserving truncation scheme that respects the nonperturbative Ward-Takahashi identity. For the pion valence-quark distribution, they calculated the handbag contributions to the virtual photon-pion forward Compton scattering amplitude in the deep inelastic Bjorken limit using an algebraic DSE model, then evolved the distribution via leading-order nonsinglet renormalization group equations.

## Key numbers

* Dressed-quark valence-quark mass: \(\check M = 301\) MeV at a resolving scale \(q_0 = 0.54\) GeV.
* At \(q_0 = 0.54\) GeV, dressed-quarks carry 71% of the pion's momentum; after evolution to \(q = 2\) GeV, they carry 49%.
* Moments of the valence-quark distribution at \(q_0 = 0.54\) GeV: \(\langle x^2 \rangle_{q_0} = 0.18\), \(\langle x^3 \rangle_{q_0} = 0.10\).
* Evolved moments at \(q = 2\) GeV: \(\langle x \rangle_q = 0.24\), \(\langle x^2 \rangle_q = 0.098\), \(\langle x^3 \rangle_q = 0.049\).
* QCD scale used for evolution: \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{N_f=3} = 0.242\) GeV; for comparison with data at \(q = 4.05\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{N_f=4} = 0.204\) GeV.
* Phenomenological fit to Drell-Yan data gives \(\alpha = 0.64 \pm 0.03\), \(\beta = 1.15 \pm 0.02\) for the parametrization \(x u_v(x) \propto x^\alpha (1-x)^\beta\).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach to hadronic physics reviewed by Roberts and collaborators (1990s) and provides a unified treatment of pseudoscalar mesons that reproduces the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation in the chiral limit (Mertens, Roberts, Tandy, 1998) and predicts linear heavy-quark mass trajectories (Hecht, 2000). The calculated pion valence-quark distribution improves upon earlier point-particle models (Dorokhov, 1990s) by incorporating a finite-size Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and its moments agree with phenomenological fits (Sutton, 1992) and lattice QCD results (Lattice, 2000). The work enables future reanalysis of Drell-Yan data using more flexible parametrizations and motivates new experiments to expose the signature of quark-antiquark binding in QCD's Goldstone mode.
