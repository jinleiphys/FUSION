# The Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Large N_c QCD

> The nature of the nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon potential in the large N_c limit is discussed. In particular, we address the consistency of the meson exchange picture of nucleon interactions. It is shown that the nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon potential extracted from the Feynmann graphs up to and including two-meson exchange diagrams satisfies the spin-flavor counting rules of Kaplan and Savage, and Kaplan and Manohar, provided the nucleon momenta is of order N_{c}^{0}. The key to this is a cancelation of the retardation effect of the box graphs against the contributions of the crossed-box diagram. The consistency requires including $Δ$ as an intermediate state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon potential extracted from Feynman graphs up to and including two-meson exchange diagrams satisfies the spin-flavor counting rules of Kaplan and Savage, and Kaplan and Manohar, provided the nucleon momenta are of order \(N_{c}^{0}\); the consistency requires including the \(\Delta\) resonance as an intermediate state and relies on a cancellation of the retardation effect of the box graphs against the contributions of the crossed-box diagram.

## Method

The authors analyze the large \(N_c\) scaling of two-meson exchange Feynman diagrams (box, crossed-box, and triangle graphs) contributing to the nucleon-nucleon potential, using nonrelativistic baryon propagators and relativistic meson propagators with contracted \(SU(4)\) symmetric meson-baryon couplings. They demonstrate that the baryon pole contributions from box diagrams correspond to iterations of the one-meson exchange potential and must be excluded, while the remaining retardation effects from box diagrams cancel exactly against the crossed-box diagram contributions for momenta of order \(N_{c}^{0}\), preserving the Kaplan-Savage-Manohar (KSM) counting rules. The analysis explicitly includes intermediate \(\Delta\) states and shows that the cancellation fails if \(\Delta\)'s are excluded or if nucleon momenta are of order \(N_c\).

## Key numbers

* The dominant nucleon-nucleon interaction is of order \(N_c\) (e.g., one-meson exchange contributions from \(f_0\) and \(\pi\) scale as \((\sqrt{N_c})^2 = N_c\)).
* The isoscalar tensor potential \(V_T^0\) is of order \(N_c^{-1}\) (suppressed by two powers of \(N_c\) relative to the leading \(N_c\) terms).
* The isovector central potential \(V_0^1\) and isoscalar spin-spin potential \(V_\sigma^0\) are of order \(N_c^{-1}\).
* The spin-orbit and quadratic spin-orbit potentials are of order \(N_c^{-2}\) (suppressed by \(1/m_B \sim 1/N_c\)).
* The \(\Delta\)-nucleon mass difference scales as \(m_\Delta - m_N \sim N_c^{-1}\).
* The cancellation between the retardation effect and the crossed-box diagram for two-scalar exchange yields a residual of order \(N_c^{-1}\) (Eq. (cancel): \({\cal M}_{\Box}^{ret}+{\cal M}_{X}={\cal O}({1/N_c})\)).

## Context

This work extends the large \(N_c\) QCD analysis of nucleon-nucleon interactions initiated by Witten (1979) and the spin-flavor counting rules developed by Kaplan and Savage (1993) and Kaplan and Manohar (1993). It addresses a fundamental inconsistency in the meson-exchange picture: generic crossed-box diagrams scale as \(N_c^2\), violating the proposed \(N_c\) scaling of the potential. The paper demonstrates that this inconsistency is resolved by a cancellation with retardation effects in box diagrams, but only when \(\Delta\) intermediate states are included and nucleon momenta are of order \(N_c^0\). This enables a consistent meson-exchange description of the nucleon-nucleon potential in the large \(N_c\) limit and clarifies the essential role of the \(\Delta\) resonance in maintaining the contracted \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor symmetry structure.
