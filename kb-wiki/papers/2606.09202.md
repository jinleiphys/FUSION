# Five-flavor $udsc\bar{b}$ molecular pentaquarks from heavy-quark and local hidden gauge symmetries

> We study a family of genuinely exotic five-flavor molecular pentaquark states containing the five quark flavors $u,d,s,c,b$ that form experimentally accessible hadrons. We construct the meson-baryon interaction from the local hidden gauge symmetry combined with heavy-quark spin symmetry, following the chiral unitary description that reproduces the LHCb hidden-charm strange pentaquarks. Heavy-quark flavor symmetry allows us to obtain the $udsc\bar{b}$ sector by replacing the anti-charm quark in the meson with an anti-bottom quark while keeping the charm quark in the baryon. As a result, we obtain ten threshold-associated isoscalar poles with $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-,5/2^-$ in the range $7.72$ to $7.96$ GeV. They are narrow and organized into heavy-quark spin multiplets with predicted near-degeneracies. There are four states that overlap with the earlier two-sector study in the literature to about $2$ MeV, which shows that the separate-sector treatment is recovered as a limit of this work. Moreover, we also identify two additional, more deeply bound $B_sΛ_c$ and $B_s^{*}Λ_c$ poles generated by strong inter-channel coupling because these poles are farther from their dominated thresholds. This is an interesting signal that can be searched for at LHCb in the $B_cΛ$ and $B_s^{*}Λ_c$ invariant mass spectra.

# Five-flavor $udsc\bar{b}$ molecular pentaquarks from heavy-quark and local hidden gauge symmetries

## Key claim
The authors predict ten isoscalar molecular pentaquark states with quark content $udsc\bar{b}$ in the mass range 7.72 to 7.96 GeV, organized into heavy-quark spin multiplets, but the absolute binding energies depend on a single adjustable subtraction constant $a(\mu = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = -3.1$, and the predictions are a symmetry-constrained extrapolation from the charm sector rather than a parameter-free calculation.

## Method
The authors construct the meson-baryon interaction using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term from local hidden gauge symmetry, combined with heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) to relate pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon channels, and heavy-quark flavor symmetry (HQFS) to map the charm-sector dynamics onto the bottom sector by replacing the anti-charm quark with an anti-bottom quark while keeping the charm quark in the baryon. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in on-shell factorization for fixed $J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-$ at isospin $I=0$, searching for poles on the second Riemann sheet of the loop function regularized in dimensional regularization with a single subtraction constant.

## Key numbers
- Ten threshold-associated isoscalar poles found in the mass range 7.72 to 7.96 GeV, with $J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-$
- $B\Xi_c$ ($1/2^-$) pole at 7717.8 MeV with half width 2.6 MeV, bound by about 31 MeV below its threshold of 7748.8 MeV
- $B^{*}\Xi_c$ ($1/2^-, 3/2^-$) doublet at 7766.5 MeV with half width 2.5 MeV, bound by about 28 MeV below threshold of 7794.1 MeV
- $B\Xi_c'$ ($1/2^-$) pole at 7848.5 MeV with half width less than 1 MeV
- $B^{*}\Xi_c'$ ($1/2^-, 3/2^-$) doublet at 7895.5 MeV with half width less than 1 MeV
- $B\Xi_c^{*}$ ($3/2^-$) pole at 7916.5 MeV with half width less than 1 MeV
- $B^{*}\Xi_c^{*}$ ($1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-$) triplet at 7963.4 MeV with half width less than 1 MeV (zero for $5/2^-$)
- Four states overlap with the earlier two-sector study of Shen (2022) to about 2 MeV
- Compositeness $X_{\rm dom} = 0.98$ for the two deeply bound $B\Xi_c$ and $B^{*}\Xi_c$ states; $X_{\rm dom} \simeq 0.99$ for the six weakly bound states
- Heavy-vector suppression factor $\gamma_b = m_V^2 / m_{B^{*}}^2 = 0.023$, compared to $\gamma_c \simeq 0.16$ in charm, giving $\gamma_c / \gamma_b \simeq 7$

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary description that reproduces the LHCb hidden-charm strange pentaquarks $P_{cs}(4459)$ and $P_{cs}(4338)$ from Xiao (2019) to the five-flavor $udsc\bar{b}$ sector using heavy-quark flavor symmetry. It recovers the separate-sector treatment of Shen (2022) as a limit, while predicting six additional states (including $J=5/2$ and $\Xi_c^{*}$ partners) organized into heavy-quark spin multiplets that are absent in that earlier work. The results are consistent with the broader symmetry survey of Peng (2022) who place five-flavor states in the 7.77 to 7.91 GeV window from contact-range effective field theory. The predicted states can be searched for at LHCb in the $B_c\Lambda$ and $B_s^{*}\Lambda_c$ invariant mass spectra.
