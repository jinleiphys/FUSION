# Monte Carlo Simulations of Hadronic Fragmentation Functions using NJL-Jet Model

> The recently developed Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) - Jet model is used as an effective chiral quark theory to calculate the quark fragmentation functions to pions, kaons, nucleons, and antinucleons. The effects of the vector mesons rho, K* and phi on the production of secondary pions and kaons are included. The fragmentation processes to nucleons and antinucleons are described by using the quark-diquark picture, which has been shown to give a reasonable description of quark distribution functions. We incorporate effects of next-to-leading order (NLO) in the Q^2 evolution, and compare our results with the empirical fragmentation functions.

# Monte Carlo Simulations of Hadronic Fragmentation Functions using NJL-Jet Model

## Key claim
The NJL-Jet model, combined with Monte Carlo simulation, can reproduce empirical fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, nucleons, and antinucleons at next-to-leading order in Q^2 evolution, provided that vector meson decays (rho, K*, phi) and a quark-diquark picture for baryons are included. The Monte Carlo approach yields results equivalent to the integral equation method in the limit of many emitted hadrons, but the equivalence breaks down at very small z due to the finite number of emissions used in the simulation.

## Method
The authors extended the NJL-Jet model by calculating elementary fragmentation functions for vector mesons (rho, K*, phi) using the proper-time regularization scheme for meson-quark couplings, and for nucleons/antinucleons using a scalar diquark picture with the Drell-Levy-Yan crossing relation applied to quark distribution functions. They implemented a Monte Carlo cascade where at each emission vertex the hadron type and light-cone momentum fraction z are sampled from the elementary splitting functions, and vector meson decays to two pseudoscalar mesons are included by sampling decay probabilities with constant amplitude squared and two-body phase space factors. The resulting fragmentation functions were evolved at NLO from the model scale Q0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 using the software of Botje (2010).

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: M_u = 300 MeV, M_s = 537 MeV
- Pseudoscalar meson-quark couplings: g_pi = 3.15, g_K = 3.39
- Vector meson-quark couplings: g_rho = 1.5, g_K* = 1.91, g_phi = 2.29
- Scalar antidiquark mass: M_D = 0.687 GeV
- Model scale for NLO evolution: Q0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2
- LB regularization cutoff: Lambda_3 = 0.67 GeV
- NLO evolution scale for comparison: Q^2 = 4 GeV^2
- Monte Carlo simulations with N_Links >= 8 produce results equivalent to integral equation solutions except at extremely small z

## Context
This work extends the NJL-Jet model previously applied to pions and kaons by Matevosyan, Thomas, and Bentz (2010) to include vector meson decays and nucleon/antinucleon channels. The approach builds on the Field-Feynman jet model (1977) and the NJL model description of quark distribution functions by Cloet (2005). The Monte Carlo method provides a flexible alternative to solving coupled integral equations as done by Ito (2009) and earlier work, enabling future inclusion of three-body decays and more complex phase space factors that cannot be treated analytically. The results enable predictions for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets at facilities like HERMES, JLab, and the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
