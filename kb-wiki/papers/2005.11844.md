# Power-law intensity distribution in $γ$-decay cascades -- Nuclear Structure as a Scale-Free Random Network

> By modeling the transition paths of the nuclear $γ$-decay cascade using a scale-free random network, we uncover a universal power-law distribution of $γ$-ray intensity $ρ_I(I) \propto I^{-2}$, with $I$ the $γ$-ray intensity of each transition. This property is consistently observed for all datasets with a sufficient number of $γ$-ray intensity entries in the National Nuclear Data Center database, regardless of the reaction type or nuclei involved. In addition, we perform numerical simulations which support the model's predictions of level population density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the intensity distribution of gamma-ray transitions in nuclear decay cascades follows a universal power-law ρ_I(I) ∝ I^{-2}, but this result is conditional on the network being scale-free and acyclic, with the edge weight distribution satisfying a specific self-similar form f(n,m) = (1/m) g(n/m).

## Method

The authors model nuclear gamma-decay cascades as a random walk on a scale-free random acyclic network, where nuclear energy levels correspond to nodes and gamma-ray transitions correspond to directed edges. They derive analytically that for networks satisfying the self-similarity condition on branching ratios, the distribution of edge passage probabilities (gamma-ray intensities) follows a power-law with exponent -2. They then test this prediction against experimental data from the ENSDF database for 69 reactions with more than 200 gamma-ray intensity entries, and also perform numerical simulations using the RAINIER code with realistic nuclear models including the back-shifted Fermi gas level density and generalized Lorentzian gamma strength function.

## Key numbers

- The predicted power-law exponent is b = 2.0, with the density scale parameter η ranging from 1/4 to 12/25 depending on the energy regime.
- For the 69 experimentally observed gamma-cascade spectra, the fitted power-law index is concentrated in the range b = 2.0 ± 0.3.
- The fitted density scale a = 0.54 ± 0.28 per cascade, consistent with the predicted range of 1/4 to 12/25.
- The numerical simulation using RAINIER reproduces the power-law over more than four orders of magnitude in intensity.
- The level density temperature T varies by a factor of 5 across the nuclei studied, and the level density scale ρ_0 varies by more than 10^3, yet the intensity distribution remains universal.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier finding (Fujii 2020) that atomic emission lines in plasmas exhibit a power-law intensity distribution, by showing the same phenomenon occurs in nuclear gamma-decay cascades. The approach is philosophically similar to Wigner's random matrix theory for nuclear Hamiltonians, but instead models the transition paths using a scale-free random network. The paper connects to work by Corominas-Murtra et al. (2015, 2016) on random walks in acyclic networks, and shows that nuclear structure can be understood as a scale-free random network. This enables the use of the universal distribution for calibrating gamma-ray intensities, as demonstrated by identifying a factor-of-10 error in the ENSDF database for 193Ir.
