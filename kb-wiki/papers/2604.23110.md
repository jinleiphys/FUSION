# On the Cancellation of Nuclear Effects in the Valence Region

> Deep-inelastic $e/μ$ scattering data off targets ranging from deuterium to lead indicate that the nuclear modifications to the structure functions of bound nucleons are minimal in the kinematic region around the peak of the valence quark distributions. An analysis of world measurements of the isoscalar cross-section ratios $σ^A/σ^{{}^2\text{H}}$ in the region of $0.25 \leq x \leq 0.35$ shows a remarkable cancellation across all nuclei, with an average value of $0.9985 \pm 0.0022$. We discuss these results and possible interpretations in the context of a microscopic model of nuclear modifications of the structure functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear modifications to bound-nucleon structure functions cancel almost exactly in the kinematic region \(0.25 \leq x \leq 0.35\) across all nuclear targets from deuterium to lead, with the average isoscalar cross-section ratio \(\sigma^A/\sigma^{{}^2\text{H}}\) being \(0.9985 \pm 0.0022\), a result that holds only when averaged over this specific valence region and depends on the particular isoscalarity corrections applied.

## Method

The authors compiled world deep-inelastic scattering data on isoscalar cross-section ratios \(R_2^A = F_2^{A(\text{is})}/F_2^{{}^2\text{H}}\) from experiments at CERN, SLAC, DESY, and JLab, selecting only measurements in the range \(0.25 \leq x \leq 0.35\). For each nuclear target and each experiment, they computed weighted averages combining statistical and systematic uncertainties in quadrature, treating normalization uncertainties as fully correlated within each experiment. They then combined averages across experiments and fitted the resulting values as a function of mass number \(A\) with both a constant and a logarithmic function, and also recalculated the averages after applying consistent isoscalarity corrections based on \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) measurements from the NMC and MARATHON experiments.

## Key numbers

* Average isoscalar cross-section ratio for all nuclei: \(0.9985 \pm 0.0022\) (after applying consistent isoscalarity corrections based on NMC and MARATHON \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) data)
* Average isoscalar cross-section ratio for all nuclei (published data, no correction): \(0.9974 \pm 0.0022\)
* Average for light nuclei \({}^2\text{H}\), \({}^3\text{He}\), \({}^4\text{He}\) (published data): \(0.9973 \pm 0.0040\)
* Slope from fit with \(R_0 + R_1 \log A\): \(R_1 = 0.0005 \pm 0.1549\), consistent with zero
* Ratio of model predictions to data (all nuclei): \(1.0004 \pm 0.0022\)
* MARATHON \({}^3\text{H}/{}^2\text{H}\) average: \(0.9980 \pm 0.0073\); MARATHON \({}^3\text{He}/{}^2\text{H}\) average: \(1.0010 \pm 0.0082\)
* Cross-over point \(x_0\) where \(R_2^{A(\text{is})}=1\) is approximately \(x_0 = 0.3\) for all nuclei at \(Q^2 = 5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)

## Context

This work extends earlier observations by Gomez (1993), Kulagin (2010), and Weinstein (2010) that nuclear effects cancel near \(x=0.3\), providing the first quantitative global analysis of all available data in the valence region. The authors compare their results to the microscopic model of Kulagin (2004, 2010, 2014), which includes Fermi motion broadening, off-shell corrections, meson exchange currents, and nuclear shadowing, finding excellent agreement. The cancellation is explained by a major cancellation between nuclear smearing (convolution with the nuclear spectral function) and the off-shell correction to bound nucleon structure functions, with the meson exchange current contribution playing a sub-leading role in determining the exact cross-over point. This result enables improved absolute normalization for current and future measurements at JLab, the LHC, the future Electron-Ion Collider, and long-baseline neutrino experiments like DUNE.
