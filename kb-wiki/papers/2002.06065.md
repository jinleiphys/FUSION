# Dispersive optical model description of nucleon scattering on Pb-Bi isotopes

> A recently derived dispersive optical model potential (DOMP) for $^{208}$Pb is extended to consider the non-locality in the real potential and the shell-gap in the definition of the nuclear imaginary potentials near the Fermi energy. The modified DOMP improves the simultaneous description of nucleon scattering on $^{208}$Pb and of the $^{208}$Pb particle-hole bound states. The new potential is shown to give a very good description of nucleon scattering data on near-magic targets $^{206,207}$Pb and $^{209}$Bi.

# Dispersive optical model description of nucleon scattering on Pb-Bi isotopes

## Key claim
The authors extend a previously derived dispersive optical model potential (DOMP) for 208Pb by incorporating non-locality in the real potential via a Perey-Buck approximation and introducing a shell-gap in the imaginary potentials near the Fermi energy, which together improve the simultaneous description of both nucleon scattering data and bound-state properties for lead and bismuth isotopes, though the quasi-elastic (p,n) scattering description still shows some discrepancies.

## Method
The authors parameterize the real Hartree-Fock potential using the Perey-Buck non-local Gaussian form, solved via the local energy approximation, and define the imaginary volume, surface, and spin-orbit potentials to be zero within a shell gap of width 2(EP - EF) around the Fermi energy for magic nuclei. The dispersive corrections to the real potential are calculated analytically from the imaginary potentials, and the full DOMP is fitted to experimental nucleon scattering data (total cross sections, elastic angular distributions, analyzing powers) and bound-state energies of 208Pb using the OPTMAN code with chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers
- Shell gap for n+208Pb: Δ = 2(EP - EF) = 3.4 MeV
- Neutron separation energies for 208Pb: calculated Sn(A) = 7.47 MeV, Sn(A+1) = 3.85 MeV; experimental values are 7.37 MeV and 3.94 MeV respectively
- Hartree-Fock potential parameters: V0 = 81.5 + 0.0292(A-208) MeV, non-locality range β = 0.912 fm
- Volume imaginary parameters: Av = 12.81 MeV, Bv = 65.56 MeV, Ea = 56 MeV, α = 0.12 MeV^(1/2)
- Surface imaginary parameters: W0 = 19.66 MeV, Bs = 8.99 MeV, Cs = 0.025 MeV^(-1)
- Spectroscopic factors for valence neutron states in 208Pb range from 0.70 to 0.97 for various orbitals (e.g., 3d3/2: 0.90, 4s1/2: 0.92, 1j15/2: 0.73)

## Context
This work extends the DOMP framework previously applied to 208Pb by Sun (2017) and Zhao (2019), incorporating non-locality treatments from Perey and Buck (1962) and the CEA Bruyeres-le-Chatel group (Romain and Delaroche, 1997; Morillon and Romain, 2004, 2006), as well as the shell-gap concept from the Washington University group (Charity, 2006; Mueller, 2011). The improved potential enables accurate simultaneous description of scattering and bound-state data for near-magic targets 206,207Pb and 209Bi, which is important for nuclear data applications and provides a Lane-consistent potential for both neutron and proton induced reactions.
