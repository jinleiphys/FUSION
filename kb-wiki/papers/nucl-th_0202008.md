# Off-shell pions in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport theory

> Due to large pi N --> Delta cross section a pion acquires a large width in nuclear matter. Therefore, a Delta-resonance decaying in the nuclear medium can produce far off-shell pions, which will gradually lose their virtuality while propagating to the vacuum. To describe such off-shell dynamics, we implemented a pion spectral function for finite nuclear matter density -- calculated on the basis of the Delta-hole model -- in the BUU transport approach. The pion off-shell dynamics leads to an enhancement of the low-p_t pion yields in the collisions of Au+Au at 1 A GeV.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a pion spectral function (based on the Δ-hole model) in BUU transport theory leads to an enhancement of low-transverse-momentum pion yields in Au+Au collisions at 1 A GeV, but this effect depends on the off-shell propagation mechanism; without the off-shell transport term that drives pions back to their mass shell in vacuum, the low-p_t enhancement disappears.

## Method
The authors implemented a pion spectral function for finite nuclear matter density, calculated from the Δ-hole model including the Migdal parameter for short-range correlations, into the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport code. Pion masses were sampled from the spectral function at production (resonance decay or NN collisions), and off-shell pions were propagated using a phenomenological scalar potential that drives them back to the mass shell as they leave the nuclear medium. The spectral function was pre-computed on a four-dimensional grid in (M_π², |k|, ρ, T) and interpolated during the BUU calculation.

## Key numbers
- The pion optical potential Re(U) changes sign and -Im(U) reaches its maximum at |k| ≈ 0.380 GeV/c for normal nuclear matter (ρ = ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, T = 5 MeV).
- The Δ-resonance pole mass used is m_Δ = 1.232 GeV, the nucleon mass m_N = 0.938 GeV, and the pion mass m_π = 0.138 GeV.
- The vacuum Δ decay width parameters are Γ₀ = 0.118 GeV and β₀ = 0.2 GeV.
- The Migdal parameter g' = 0.5 was used for short-range correlations.
- The energy violation from the off-shell potential was ~0.2 MeV/nucleon for central Au+Au at 1 A GeV (~0.1% of total c.m. kinetic energy per nucleon).
- The spectral function normalization condition was satisfied with an accuracy of 10%.

## Context
This work extends earlier BUU studies by Helgesson and Randrup (1998) and Xiong et al. (1993) and Fuchs et al. (1997), which used the quasiparticle approximation for pions and found only small in-medium effects on pion observables. The present paper is the first to implement a full pion spectral function in BUU transport, enabling the description of far off-shell pions produced by Δ decays in dense matter. The approach enables future studies of how in-medium pion modifications affect other particles (e.g., kaons) through multistep processes, and can be extended to include higher baryonic resonances.
