# Determination of the proton spectral function of \isotope[12][]{C} from $(e,e^\prime p)$ data

> The determination of the nuclear spectral function from the measured cross section of the electron-nucleus scattering process $e + A \to e^\prime + p + (A-1)$is discussed, and illustrated for the case of a carbon target. The theoretical model based on the local density approximation, previously employed to derive the spectral function from a combination of accurate theoretical calculations and experimental data, has been developed further by including additional information obtained from measurements performed with high missing energy resolution. The implications for the analysis of $γ$-ray emission associated with nuclear deexcitation are considered.

# Determination of the proton spectral function of 12C from (e,e'p) data

## Key claim

The improved spectral function model for carbon, derived by combining high-resolution NIKHEF data with the existing Local Density Approximation (LDA) framework, resolves three previously unresolved p-shell states at specific removal energies, but the modified energy dependence does not significantly affect inclusive electron scattering cross sections because those calculations involve integrations over broad energy ranges.

## Method

The authors combined the LDA-based spectral function of carbon from Benhar et al. (1990s), which used Saclay data from Mougey et al. (1970s) and nuclear matter calculations, with high-resolution missing energy spectra from the NIKHEF experiment by Van der Steenhoven et al. The NIKHEF data, measured with 150 keV resolution using a 500 MeV electron beam, were fitted with Gaussian peaks and renormalized to match the p-state normalization of the LDA spectral function (Sp = 2.8). The improved spectral function uses the NIKHEF fit for missing energies E ≤ 21.5 MeV and the LDA distribution for E > 21.5 MeV, with the s-shell contribution modeled as a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution peaked at 37 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton knockout threshold Ethr = 15.96 MeV, computed from nuclear masses M(12C) = 11177.93 MeV and M(11B) = 10255.11 MeV
- Spectroscopic factors from LDA model: Sp = 2.8 and Ss = 1.2, corresponding to occupation probabilities of 0.7 and 0.6 respectively (shell model predicts Sp = 4 and Ss = 2)
- Three resolved p-state removal energies: 15.96 MeV (p3/2), 18.08 MeV (p1/2), and 20.97 MeV (p3/2), with spectroscopic strengths Sα = 1.747, 0.273, and 0.201 respectively
- Total strength from resolved peaks ΣSα = 2.22, compared to Sp = 2.8 from LDA, a discrepancy of approximately 25%
- s-shell spectroscopic strength Ss = 1.15 from the Maxwell-Boltzmann fit, within 5% of the Saclay result
- NIKHEF missing energy resolution: 150 keV FWHM
- Nuclear matter equilibrium density ρ0 ≈ 0.16 fm−3

## Context

This work extends the LDA-based spectral function formalism of Benhar et al. (1990s), which combined Saclay data from Mougey et al. (1970s) with nuclear matter calculations, by incorporating high-resolution NIKHEF data from Van der Steenhoven et al. The improved spectral function enables extension of the gamma-ray emission analysis from oxygen by Ankowski et al. (2015) to carbon targets, relevant for neutrino interactions in liquid scintillator detectors such as KamLAND and JUNO. The results also support Monte Carlo cascade simulations of final-state interactions in neutrino-nucleus interactions as discussed by Ershova et al. (2023).
