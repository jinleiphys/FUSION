# Comments on the CMS discovery of the "Ridge" in High Multiplicity pp collisions at LHC

> A very recent paper by the CMS collaboration \cite{cms_ridge} has created large discussion in the media, which call it important but did not explain why, in some places even calling it "unundestandable". While it is of course too soon to know what causes the correlations in question, a very similar observation in heavy ion collisions at RHIC has rather simple explanation related to explosion of high energy density matter. Perhaps this observation is the first hint for an explosive behavior in pp, which was anticipated and looked for for decades, yet never been seen.

# Comments on the CMS discovery of the "Ridge" in High Multiplicity pp collisions at LHC

## Key claim
The CMS observation of a "ridge" in high-multiplicity pp collisions may be the first hint of explosive behavior (collective flow) in proton-proton collisions, a phenomenon anticipated for decades but never seen. However, it is too soon to know what causes the correlations, and the similarity to the AuAu ridge at RHIC does not guarantee the same origin.

## Method
The CMS collaboration studied two-particle correlations in high-multiplicity pp events at LHC, selecting events with charged particle multiplicity greater than 110. They measured correlations as a function of rapidity difference Δη and azimuthal angle difference Δφ, finding a correlation that is wide in Δη but concentrated at small Δφ (< 1 radian). This contrasts with normal (minimum-bias) pp events, which show only short-range rapidity correlations and no azimuthal preference.

## Key numbers
- The ridge correlation is concentrated at azimuthal angle difference δφ < 1 radian, unlike the arbitrary Δφ seen in normal pp events.
- The CMS selection required charged particle multiplicity Nch > 110 for the highest multiplicity bin.
- In AuAu collisions at RHIC, the transverse velocity at the surface is about v⊥ ∼ 0.7c, which roughly explains the width δφ of the ridge correlation.
- The best pT window for ridge observation is 1–3 GeV, the same for both the CMS pp ridge and the AuAu ridge.
- Hydrodynamics works for AuAu for about 99% of particles up to p⊥ ∼ 2 GeV, ending around 3 GeV due to jets.
- The mean free path in QGP derived from viscosity is about 0.2 fm.
- The CMS data sample contains about 0.3 million high-multiplicity events.

## Context
This comment extends the interpretation of the "ridge" phenomenon first observed in AuAu collisions at RHIC, where it is understood as a hydrodynamical flow effect from the exploding Quark-Gluon Plasma. The author notes that earlier searches for collective expansion in pp collisions at the ISR (Shuryak and Zhirov, 1979) found no evidence, and the MiniMax experiment at Fermilab (Brooks, 1999) also failed to find explosive behavior. The observation opens the possibility that high-multiplicity pp events may produce enough matter density for hydrodynamical behavior, which would enable tests such as measuring identified particle spectra (pions, kaons, nucleons) for radial flow signatures and the baryon/meson ratio inside the ridge to distinguish flow from jet-based explanations.
