# Flow fluctuations and long-range correlations: elliptic flow and beyond

> These proceedings consist of a brief overview of the current understanding of collective behavior in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In particular, recent progress in understanding the implications of event-by-event fluctuations have solved important puzzles in existing data -- the "ridge" and "shoulder" phenomena of long-range two-particle correlations -- and have created an exciting opportunity to tightly constrain theoretical models with many new observables.

# Flow fluctuations and long-range correlations: elliptic flow and beyond

## Key claim
Event-by-event fluctuations in the initial geometry of heavy-ion collisions, rather than non-flow correlations such as jets, can explain the entire observed long-range two-particle correlation structure (the "ridge" and "shoulder" phenomena) when higher harmonic flow coefficients (especially v3) are properly included, though this conclusion depends on the assumption that flow correlations dominate at large relative pseudorapidity and that no independent measurement of flow coefficients exists to rigorously separate flow from non-flow.

## Method
The paper reviews the standard Fourier decomposition of azimuthal particle distributions into flow coefficients vn and reference angles Ψn, then demonstrates that event-by-event fluctuations in the initial transverse density profile generate non-zero odd harmonics (particularly v3) that were previously neglected. It describes a systematic expansion of the initial density in terms of spatial moments εm,n and shows how hydrodynamic response relates these moments to final flow coefficients, with v2 proportional to the participant eccentricity εPP to first order, while v4 and v5 depend on combinations of multiple moments.

## Key numbers
- The second Fourier component v2 is proportional to the participant eccentricity εPP via v2 = C × εPP, where C encodes viscosity and equation of state information
- Factorization of two-particle correlations into vn{2}(pt) × vn{2}(pt) holds for all harmonics except the first for particles below approximately 4-5 GeV transverse momentum
- Higher harmonics (v3, v4, v5, v6) were newly measured at Quark Matter 2011 by PHENIX, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and STAR collaborations
- The fourth cumulant elliptic flow v2{4} and newly measured v3{4} provide independent information about event-by-event distributions of flow coefficients

## Context
This proceedings paper extends the work of Alver (2010) who first recognized that event-by-event fluctuations imply non-negligible v3, and Luzum (2010) who showed that flow correlations alone can generate the entire observed two-particle correlation at large Δη. It contradicts earlier approaches such as the Zero-Yield-At-Minimum (ZYAM) prescription of Adler (2005) which assumed only v2 contributed to flow and attributed remaining structure to non-flow. The paper enables systematic extraction of multiple flow harmonics to constrain initial condition models (e.g., ruling out particular MC-KLN implementations with insufficient eccentricity fluctuations) and to more precisely characterize viscosity and pre-equilibrium dynamics.
