# Heavy flavor molecular states with strangeness

> We proposed a unified framework to describe the interactions of the observed $T_{cc}$, $P_c$, and $P_{cs}$ within a quark level interaction in our previous work. In this work, we generalize our framework to the loosely bound hadronic molecules composed of heavy flavor di-hadrons with strangeness. We predict the possible $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}_s$ molecular states in the SU(3) limit with the masses of the $P_c$ states as the inputs. We also investigate the baryon-meson and baryon-baryon systems and consider the SU(3) breaking effect in their flavor wave functions. We generalize our isospin criterion of the formation of heavy flavor di-hadron molecules to the $U/V$ spin case. For a specific heavy flavor meson-meson, baryon-meson, or baryon-baryon system, the interactions for the states with the same flavor and spin matrix elements can be related by a generalized flavor-spin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed unified quark-level framework for heavy-flavor di-hadron interactions to systems containing strangeness, predicting that the same flavor-spin symmetry that describes the observed \(T_{cc}\), \(P_c\), and \(P_{cs}\) states also governs the binding of \(D^{(*)}D_s^{(*)}\) meson-meson, strange baryon-meson, and strange baryon-baryon molecules, provided the SU(3) limit is taken for the coupling constants.

## Method

The authors adopt a quark-level Lagrangian with scalar and axial-vector meson exchange, parameterizing the effective potential in terms of SU(3) flavor matrix elements \(\langle \bm{\lambda}_1\cdot\bm{\lambda}_2 \rangle\) and spin matrix elements \(\langle \bm{\sigma}_1\cdot\bm{\sigma}_2 \rangle\) of the light degrees of freedom. They construct flavor wave functions for meson-meson, baryon-meson, and baryon-baryon systems both in the SU(3) limit and with explicit SU(3) breaking (distinguishing the \(s\) quark from \(u,d\)), then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a hard momentum cutoff to search for bound states.

## Key numbers

* Five molecular candidates are predicted in the \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}\) systems with anti-symmetric flavor wave functions: \([\bar{D}\bar{D}_s]_0^{\frac{1}{2}A}\) at 3834.6 MeV (binding energy \(-1.6\) MeV); \([\bar{D}\bar{D}_s^*(\bar{D}_s\bar{D}^*)]_1^{\frac{1}{2}A}\) at 3976.4 MeV (\(-2.1\) MeV); \([\bar{D}^*\bar{D}_s^*]_0^{\frac{1}{2}A}\) at 4106.1 MeV (\(-14.7\) MeV); \([\bar{D}^*\bar{D}_s^*]_1^{\frac{1}{2}A}\) at 4112.7 MeV (\(-8.0\) MeV); \([\bar{D}^*\bar{D}_s^*]_2^{\frac{1}{2}A}\) at 4120.8 MeV (\(-0.0\) MeV).
* The input parameters from the previous work (Chen 2021) are \(\tilde{g}_s = 11.739\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\tilde{g}_a = -2.860\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and \(\Lambda = 0.409\) GeV, obtained by fitting the masses of \(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\).
* The \(\bar{D}_s^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}\) systems yield no molecular candidates because their effective potentials are all repulsive (positive).

## Context

This work extends the unified molecular framework of Chen (2021), which successfully described the observed \(T_{cc}\), \(P_c\), and \(P_{cs}\) states, to all doubly heavy di-hadron systems with strangeness listed in Table 1. It complements prior studies of strange tetraquark molecules (e.g., Molina 2010, Dong 2021) and OBE-model analyses of \(\Xi_c^{(\prime,*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}\) (Wang 2020) and \(\Omega_c^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}\) (Wang 2021) systems. The predicted \(D^{(*)}D_s^{(*)}\) molecules provide concrete targets for experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II, and the generalized flavor-spin symmetry allows the results to be mapped onto bottomed partners (e.g., \(BB_s\) systems with binding energies up to \(-36.4\) MeV).
