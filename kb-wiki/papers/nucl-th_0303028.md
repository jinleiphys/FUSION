# Faddeev calculations for the A=5,6 Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei

> Faddev calculations are reported for Lambda-Lambda-5H, Lambda-Lambda-5He and Lambda-Lambda-6He in terms of two Lambda hyperons plus the respective nuclear clusters, using Lambda-Lambda central potentials considered in past non-Faddeev calculations of Lambda-Lambda-6He. The convergence with respect to the partial-wave expansion is studied, and comparison is made with some of these Lambda-Lambda hypernuclear calculations. The Lambda-Lambda <--> Xi-N mixing effect is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the A=5,6 double-Lambda hypernuclei, the s-wave approximation in Faddeev calculations works fairly well, with contributions from higher partial waves being small (less than 0.2 MeV) when ordered by increasing l_max, but this convergence is not necessarily reproduced by non-Faddeev methods where the partial-wave ordering is defined only via l_Lambda-Lambda irrespective of l_Lambda-C.

## Method

The authors solved differential Faddeev equations in configuration space for three-body systems consisting of two Lambda hyperons plus a nuclear cluster (³H, ³He, or ⁴He), using central Lambda-Lambda potentials of three-range Gaussian form and Isle-type Lambda-cluster potentials. The calculations were performed with a partial-wave expansion up to l_max=6, and the convergence was tested by comparing results at successive l_max values. The systems studied were ⁶_ΛΛHe (ΛΛα), ⁵_ΛΛH (ΛΛ³H), and ⁵_ΛΛHe (ΛΛ³He), with spin-averaged and full spin-dependent treatments for the A=5 cases.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶_ΛΛHe using purely attractive Gaussian potentials, the Faddeev calculation gave B_ΛΛ values increasing monotonically with l_max, with convergence reached at l_max=2, only 0.06 MeV higher than the s-wave approximation (l_max=0).
- For ⁶_ΛΛHe with realistic Isle Lambda-alpha and Lambda-Lambda potentials (NSC97b, NSC97e, ND), the deviation of B_ΛΛ from the s-wave approximation reached about 0.2 MeV at l_max=6.
- For the NSC97e calculation with a weaker p-wave Isle potential for l_Λα=1, B_ΛΛ = 6.74 MeV for l_max=1, compared to 6.79 MeV when the standard s-wave Isle potential was used for all partial waves.
- For the A=5 hypernuclei ⁵_ΛΛH and ⁵_ΛΛHe, the s-wave approximation held to about 0.13 MeV.
- The inclusion of Lambda-Lambda to Xi-N coupling increased the calculated binding energy for ⁶_ΛΛHe by 0.48 ± 0.04 MeV for the NSC97e-based potentials.
- For the older ND model, the Lambda-Lambda to Xi-N coupling added 0.50 MeV, with Pauli suppression calculated as 0.27 MeV by Carr et al. (1997).

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2002) that used an s-wave approximation, confirming their estimates and providing full partial-wave convergence tests. The results are compared to non-Faddeev calculations including the Schrodinger-equation approach of Ikeda et al. (1985), the variational calculation of Portilho and Coon (1991), the hyperspherical harmonics calculation of Khan and Das (2000), and the variational calculation of Myint et al. (2002). The paper also critically examines claims by Myint et al. (2002) regarding Lambda-Lambda to Xi-N mixing effects, arguing that the coupling effect should not exceed 0.2 MeV in ⁶_ΛΛHe for models like NSC97e, and that proper treatment requires explicit introduction of the Xi-N channel with Pauli suppression projection operators as done by Carr et al. (1997) and Yamada and Nakamoto (2000).
