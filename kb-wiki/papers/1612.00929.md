# Gauge symmetry breaking in the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method

> We study gauge symmetry breaking by adiabatic approximation in the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method. In the previous study, we found that the gauge symmetry of the equation of collective submanifold is (partially) broken by its decomposition into the three moving-frame equations depending on the order of $p$. In this study, we discuss the gauge symmetry breaking by the truncation of the adiabatic expansion. A particular emphasis is placed on the symmetry under the gauge transformations which are not point transformations. We also discuss a possible version of the ASCC method including the higher-order operators which can keep the gauge symmetry.

## Key claim

The gauge symmetry of the canonical-variable conditions under non-point gauge transformations (Examples 1 and 2) is broken by truncating the adiabatic expansion of the generator \(\hat G\) to first order in the collective momentum \(p\) and particle number fluctuation \(n\); including second-order operators in \(\hat G\) restores the gauge symmetry at order \(p\) and \(n\).

## Method

The author expands the generator \(\hat G(q,p,n)\) of the state vector in the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method up to second order in \(p\) and \(n\), introducing operators \(\hat Q^{(2)}\), \(\hat \Theta^{(2)}\), and \(\hat X\). The gauge transformations of the canonicity conditions and the derived canonical-variable conditions are examined for four examples of gauge transformations (Examples 1–4), with focus on the non-point transformations (Examples 1 and 2). The moving-frame HFB and QRPA equations are re-derived including the second-order operators, and their gauge invariance is checked under the transformations.

## Key numbers

* The canonical-variable condition \(\langle \phi(q)|\hat X + \frac{i}{2}[\hat Q^{(1)},\hat \Theta^{(1)}]|\phi(q)\rangle =0\) changes by \(\alpha\) under the gauge transformation of Example 1 when second-order operators are included, restoring gauge symmetry.
* The canonical-variable condition \(\langle \phi(q)|\hat Q^{(2)}|\phi(q)\rangle =0\) is gauge invariant under Example 1, as it transforms to \(\langle \phi(q)|\hat Q^{(2)} + i\alpha[\tilde N,\hat Q^{(1)}]|\phi(q)\rangle =0\).
* The canonical-variable condition \(\langle \phi(q)|\hat \Theta^{(2)}|\phi(q)\rangle =0\) is gauge invariant under Example 1, transforming to \(\langle \phi(q)|\hat \Theta^{(2)} + i\alpha[\hat P,\hat \Theta^{(1)}]|\phi(q)\rangle =0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gauge symmetry in the ASCC method initiated by Hinohara (2007) and formalized by Sato (2015) using Dirac-Bergmann constrained systems theory. It addresses the gauge symmetry breaking previously observed in the moving-frame QRPA equations when the equation of collective submanifold is decomposed into three moving-frame equations. The inclusion of higher-order operators in \(\hat G\) provides a path toward a gauge-invariant version of the ASCC method applicable to large-amplitude collective dynamics in atomic nuclei with superfluidity.
