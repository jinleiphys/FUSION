# Relativistic Fock space coupled cluster study of bismuth electronic structure to extract the Bi nuclear quadrupole moment

> We report the value of the electric quadrupole moment of $^{209}$Bi extracted from the atomic data. For this, we performed electronic structure calculations for the ground $^4S^o_{3/2}$ and excited $^2P^o_{3/2}$ states of atomic Bi using the Dirac-Coulomb-Breit Hamiltonian and the Fock space coupled cluster method with single, double, and full triple amplitudes for the three-particle Fock space sector. The value of the quadrupole moment of $^{209}$Bi, $Q(^{209}$Bi$)=-418(6)$~mb, derived from the resulting electric field gradient values and available atomic hyperfine splittings is in excellent agreement with molecular data. Due to the availability of the hyperfine constants for unstable isotopes of Bi, current atomic calculation allows also to correct their quadrupole moments.

# Relativistic Fock space coupled cluster study of bismuth electronic structure to extract the Bi nuclear quadrupole moment

## Key claim

The electric quadrupole moment of 209Bi is determined to be -418(6) mb from atomic data, which coincides within uncertainty with recent molecular results (approximately -420 mb) and resolves a long-standing discrepancy with earlier atomic calculations that gave -516(15) mb. The agreement between values extracted from two different electronic states is achieved only when full triple cluster amplitudes are included in the Fock space coupled cluster treatment.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic electronic structure calculations for the ground 4So3/2 and excited 2Po3/2 states of neutral atomic bismuth using the Dirac-Coulomb-Breit Hamiltonian and the Fock space coupled cluster method with single, double, and full iterative triple amplitudes (FS-CCSDT) for the three-particle Fock space sector. The electric field gradient (EFG) at the bismuth nucleus was calculated for both states using a finite-field approach, with all 83 electrons correlated in the FS-CCSD calculation and 60 inner-core electrons excluded in the FS-CCSDT calculation due to computational demands. The nuclear quadrupole moment was extracted from the calculated EFG values and experimentally known electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constants B.

## Key numbers

- Final value of the 209Bi nuclear quadrupole moment: Q(209Bi) = -418(6) mb, averaged over the ground and excited electronic states
- EFG for the ground 4So3/2 state: total 3.097 a.u. (FS-CCSD: 2.983, basis set correction: 0.055, FS-CCSDT minus FS-CCSD: 0.117, Breit contribution: -0.058)
- EFG for the excited 2Po3/2 state: total -9.978 a.u. (FS-CCSD: -10.292, basis set correction: -0.050, FS-CCSDT minus FS-CCSD: 0.276, Breit contribution: 0.088)
- Experimental hyperfine constants used: B = -305.067(2) MHz for the ground state and B = 978.638(10) MHz for the excited state, from Hull and Brink (1970)
- Triple cluster amplitudes contribute +3.8% to EFG for the ground state and -2.8% for the excited state
- Inner-core electrons (1s through 4f) contribute about 10% and 7% to EFG for the ground and excited states, respectively
- Uncertainty of Q from the ground state: 9 mb; from the excited state: 6 mb
- Previous atomic calculation gave Q = -516(15) mb (Bieron et al., 2001); molecular studies gave Q = -420(8) mb (Quevedo et al., 2013) and Q = -415.1 mb (Avijit, 2016)

## Context

This work resolves a 20% discrepancy between earlier atomic calculations of the bismuth nuclear quadrupole moment (Bieron et al., 2001, giving -516(15) mb) and more recent molecular studies (Quevedo et al., 2013; Avijit, 2016, giving approximately -420 mb). The authors attribute the earlier discrepancy to inadequate treatment of electron correlation in atomic calculations, particularly the need for full triple excitations in the Fock space coupled cluster treatment of the three-valence-electron system. The calculated EFG values also enable the authors to update the quadrupole moments for unstable 202-213Bi isotopes using previously measured hyperfine constants from Pearson (2000) and Schmidt (2018).
