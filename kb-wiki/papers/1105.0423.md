# Ultrasoft Fermionic Mode in Yukawa Theory at High Temperature

> We explore whether an ultrasoft fermionic mode exists at extremely high temperature in Yukawa theory with massless fermion (coupling constant is g). We find that the fermion propagator has a pole at ω= \pm p/3-iζ, for ultrasoft momentum p, where ζ\sim g^4T ln 1/g, and the residue is Z \sim g^2. It is shown that one needs to take into account the asymptotic masses and the damping rate of hard particles to get a sensible result for such an ultrasoft fermionic mode; possible vertex correction turns out unnecessary for the scalar coupling in contrast to the gauge coupling.

## Key claim

The fermion propagator in Yukawa theory at high temperature has a pole at ω = ±p/3 − iζ for ultrasoft momentum p, where ζ ∼ g⁴T ln(1/g) and the residue Z ∼ g², but this result requires using dressed propagators with asymptotic masses and damping rates of hard particles; vertex corrections are unnecessary for scalar coupling, unlike in gauge theories.

## Method

The authors calculate the retarded fermion self-energy in the ultrasoft momentum region (p ≲ g²T) using dressed propagators that incorporate the asymptotic masses (m_f² = g²T²/8, m_b² = g²T²/6) and damping rates (ζ_f, ζ_b ∼ g⁴T ln g⁻¹) of hard fermions and bosons. They expand the self-energy in powers of 2(p·k + i k⁰ ζ)/δm², where δm² = m_b² − m_f² = g²T²/24, and evaluate the leading contribution to obtain the fermion propagator. They then verify that ladder-type vertex corrections are suppressed by an additional factor of g² due to the on-shell condition of internal particles, so only the one-loop diagram with dressed propagators contributes at leading order.

## Key numbers

* Pole position: ω = ±p/3 − iζ, where ζ ∼ g⁴T ln(1/g) for ultrasoft momentum p
* Residue at the pole: Z = g²/(72π²) ∼ g²
* Asymptotic masses: m_f² = g²T²/8 for fermions, m_b² = g²T²/6 for bosons
* Mass difference squared: δm² = m_b² − m_f² = g²T²/24
* Damping rates of hard particles: ζ_f, ζ_b ∼ g⁴T ln g⁻¹
* Energy scale of ultrasoft mode: ≪ g²T (compared to ∼ gT for normal fermion and antiplasmino)
* Damping rate of ultrasoft mode: ∼ g⁴T ln g⁻¹ (compared to ∼ g²T for normal fermion and antiplasmino)
* Residue of ultrasoft mode: ∼ g² (compared to ∼ 1 for normal fermion and antiplasmino)

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions of a three-peak structure in the fermion spectral function found in one-loop calculations with massive bosons (Mitsutani, 2003; Kitazawa et al., 2004) and in massive vector boson theories (Hidaka et al., 2009). It also relates to studies of ultrasoft fermionic modes (phoninos) in supersymmetric models at finite temperature (Lebedev and Smilga, 1991; Lebedev, 1992). The result establishes the existence of an ultrasoft fermionic mode in a nonsupersymmetric Yukawa theory for the first time, and the authors note that a similar analysis for QED and QCD would require summing ladder diagrams due to vertex corrections not present in the scalar coupling case.
