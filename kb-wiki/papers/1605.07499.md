# Comment on "Structure effects in the $^{15}$N(n,$γ$)$^{16}$N radiative capture reaction from the Coulomb dissociation of $^{16}$N"

> In their recent study Neelam, Shubhchintak, and Chatterjee have claimed that "it would certainly be useful to perform a Coulomb dissociation experiment to find the low energy capture cross section for the reaction" $^{15}$N(n,$γ$)$^{16}$N. However, it is obvious that a Coulomb dissociation experiment cannot constrain this capture cross section because the dominating branchings of the capture reaction lead to excited states in $^{16}$N which do not contribute in a Coulomb dissociation experiment. An estimate of the total $^{15}$N(n,$γ$)$^{16}$N cross section from Coulomb dissociation of $^{16}$N requires a precise knowledge of the $γ$-ray branchings in the capture reaction. Surprisingly, the calculation of Neelam, Shubhchintak, and Chatterjee predicts a strongly energy-dependent ground state branching of the order of 0.05\% to 0.6\% at energies between 100 and 500 keV which is almost 2 orders of magnitude below calculations in the direct capture model. Additionally, this calculation of Neelam, Shubhchintak, and Chatterjee deviates significantly from the expected energy dependence for $p$-wave capture.

# Comment on "Structure effects in the 15N(n,γ)16N radiative capture reaction from the Coulomb dissociation of 16N"

## Key claim

A Coulomb dissociation experiment on 16N cannot constrain the total 15N(n,γ)16N capture cross section because the dominating branches of the capture reaction lead to excited states in 16N that do not contribute in a Coulomb dissociation experiment. The calculation by Neelam, Shubhchintak, and Chatterjee (NSC) predicts a strongly energy-dependent ground state branching of 0.05% to 0.6% between 100 and 500 keV, which is almost two orders of magnitude below direct capture model calculations and deviates significantly from the expected energy dependence for p-wave capture.

## Method

The author compares the Coulomb dissociation (CD) calculations of NSC for the four low-lying states in 16N (Jπ = 2-, 0-, 3-, and 1- below excitation energy ≈ 400 keV) against direct capture (DC) model calculations from Mei96. The DC calculations use the parametrization σ(n,γ) = A E^0.5 - B E^1.2 for p-wave neutron capture, and the resulting branching ratios are compared to the energy-dependent branching ratios predicted by NSC. The author also examines prior CD studies of 8Li(n,γ)9Li and 14C(n,γ)15C to assess whether those cases support NSC's methodology.

## Key numbers

- Ground state branching in NSC calculation: 0.05% at 100 keV and 0.6% at 500 keV, showing strong energy dependence
- Ground state branching in DC calculations (Mei96): approximately 10% across the entire energy range up to 500 keV, nearly energy-independent
- The NSC ground state branching is almost 2 orders of magnitude below the DC calculations
- The DC parametrization uses σ(n,γ) = A E^0.5 - B E^1.2 with B < A, making the second term a small correction below 1 MeV
- Excited state contributions in the 7Li(n,γ)8Li reaction require corrections of about 10% to 20% from experimental branching ratios (Izsak13)

## Context

This comment directly contradicts the claim of Neelam, Shubhchintak, and Chatterjee (NSC, 2015) that a Coulomb dissociation experiment could determine the low-energy 15N(n,γ)16N capture cross section. The author shows that unlike the 8Li(n,γ)9Li case (Ban08) and the 14C(n,γ)15C case (Shu14), where ground state contributions dominate, the 15N(n,γ)16N reaction has dominant branches to excited states. The energy-dependent branching ratios predicted by NSC also disagree with DC calculations by Mei96 and with similar DC results for neighboring nuclei 14C(n,γ)15C (Wie90) and 16O(n,γ)17O (Iga95, Men00), where experimental confirmation of weak energy dependence exists for the 16O case. This work enables the conclusion that experimental CD data on 16N cannot be corrected using theoretical branching ratios from NSC to obtain the total capture cross section.
