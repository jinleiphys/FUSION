# The path from lattice QCD to the short-distance contribution to $0νββ$ decay with a light Majorana neutrino

> Neutrinoless double-$β$ ($0νββ$) decay of certain atomic isotopes, if observed, will have significant implications for physics of neutrinos and models of physics beyond the Standard Model. In the simplest scenario, if the mass of the light neutrino of the Standard Model has a Majorana component, it can mediate the decay. Systematic theoretical studies of the decay rate in this scenario, through effective field theories matched to \emph{ab initio} nuclear many-body calculations, are needed to draw conclusions about the hierarchy of neutrino masses, and to plan the design of future experiments. However, a recently identified short-distance contribution at leading order in the effective field theory amplitude of the subprocess $nn \to pp\,(ee)$ remains unknown, and only lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) can directly and reliably determine the associated low-energy constant. While the numerical computations of the correlation function for this process are underway with lattice QCD, the connection to the physical amplitude, and hence this short-distance contribution, is missing. A complete framework that enables this complex matching is developed in this paper. The complications arising from Euclidean and finite-volume nature of the corresponding correlation function are fully resolved, and the value of the formalism is demonstrated through a simple example. The result of this work, therefore, fills the gap between first-principle studies of the $nn \to pp\,(ee)$ amplitude from lattice QCD and those from effective field theory, and can be readily employed in the ongoing lattice-QCD studies of this process.

## Key claim

The paper develops a complete theoretical framework to extract the short-distance low-energy constant (LEC) \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\) of the \(nn \to pp\,(ee)\) subprocess from lattice QCD, resolving the complications of Euclidean and finite-volume correlation functions; the framework is demonstrated to be practical because, for a plausible kinematic choice with \(L = 8\) fm, no on-shell intermediate states appear, making the Minkowski amplitude directly obtainable from the Euclidean lattice correlation function without subtraction.

## Method

The authors construct a matching relation between the finite-volume Minkowski matrix element \(\mathcal{T}_L^{(\rm M)}\) for the \(nn \to pp\,(ee)\) process and the physical infinite-volume amplitude \(\mathcal{M}^{(\rm{Int.})}_{nn\to pp}\) at leading order in pionless effective field theory. They then show how to obtain \(\mathcal{T}_L^{(\rm M)}\) from the Euclidean lattice four-point function \(G_L^{(\rm E)}(\tau)\) by separating contributions from on-shell intermediate states (which must be subtracted) and off-shell contributions (which can be directly integrated). The finite-volume function \(\delta J^V\) for the neutrino-exchange loop is defined and evaluated numerically, and the zero spatial-momentum mode of the neutrino propagator is removed to match the finite-volume treatment.

## Key numbers

* The ground-state energy of the two-neutron isotriplet channel in a finite volume of \(L = 8\) fm is \(E_{n_i} \approx -2.6\) MeV.
* The low-lying finite-volume spectrum of the two-nucleon isotriplet channel at rest in \(L = 8\) fm is \(\widetilde{E}_{*m} \approx \{-5.6, 13.9, \dots\}\) MeV.
* The minimum allowed energy of an on-shell neutrino in a volume of \(L = 8\) fm is \(|\bm{P_*}| = 2\pi/L \approx 155.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for the two-neutrino double-beta decay process \(nn \to pp\,(ee\bar{\nu}_e\bar{\nu}_e)\) by Davoudi (2020) to the neutrinoless case, which involves a propagating Majorana neutrino and requires new components for the matching condition. It builds on prior effective field theory analyses by Cirigliano (2017, 2018, 2019) that identified the short-distance contribution and its associated LEC \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\), and on lattice QCD studies of related lepton-number violating processes by Feng (2018), Tuo (2019), and Detmold (2020). The framework enables direct lattice QCD determination of \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\) in the coming years, and is also applicable to other processes with light lepton or photon propagators, such as semi-leptonic kaon decays and virtual-photon contributions to charge-invariance breaking in the two-nucleon sector.
