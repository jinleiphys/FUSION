# Quantum Liquid Crystal Phases in Fermionic Superfluids with Pairing between Fermion Species of Unequal Densities

> Superfluidity in fermionic systems originates from pairing of fermions, and Bose condensation of these so-called Cooper pairs. The Cooper pairs are usually made of fermions of different species; for example in superconductors they are pairs of electrons with opposite spins. Thus the most favorable situation for pairing and superfluidity is when the two species of fermions that form pairs have the same density. This paper studies the possible superfluid states when the two pairing species have different densities, and show that the resultant states have remarkable similarities to the phases of liquid crystals. This enables us to provide a unified description of the possible pairing phases, and understand the phase transitions among them.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the various proposed pairing states for fermionic superfluids with unequal densities, specifically the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO), deformed Fermi surface pairing (DFSP), and breached pairing (BP) states, are not distinct phenomena but rather different phases of a quantum liquid crystal, unified by the common underlying structure of vortex/antivortex chains that accommodate the excess magnetization.

## Method
The author develops a theoretical analogy between the symmetry-breaking patterns of liquid crystal phases and those of fermionic superfluid states with unbalanced species. The mean-field domain wall picture of the FFLO state is reinterpreted in terms of vortex-antivortex chains when fluctuations are included, and the melting of these ordered chains under increasing thermal or quantum fluctuations is mapped onto the sequence of smectic → nematic → isotropic liquid crystal transitions. A concrete wavefunction for the breached pairing state in the strong-coupling (BEC) regime is presented, where unpaired spin-polarized fermions co-exist with closely-bound bosonic pairs.

## Key numbers
- The FFLO state has a pairing order parameter oscillating with period \(2\pi\hbar/p\), where \(p \approx p_{F\uparrow} - p_{F\downarrow}\) is the net momentum of the Cooper pair.
- For pairing fermions with the same mass, destabilization of the BCS state requires a Zeeman splitting \(\Delta\mu \sim \Delta_0\), where \(\Delta_0\) is the pairing gap at \(T=0\) and \(\Delta\mu=0\).
- Quantum fluctuation strength is parametrized by the dimensionless ratio \(\Delta_0/E_F\), where \(E_F = p_F^2/2M\) is the Fermi energy.
- In the breached pairing wavefunction, the Fermi wave-vector of the excess (unpaired) fermions is \(k_F' = [6\pi^2(n_\uparrow - n_\downarrow)]^{1/3}\).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing theoretical discussion of pairing under population imbalance, which began with the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) proposal (Fulde and Ferrell, 1964; Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964) and later included deformed Fermi surface pairing (Muther and Sedrakian, 2002; Sedrakian, 2001) and breached pairing (Liu and Wilczek, 2003; Gubankova, Liu, and Wilczek, 2003; Forbes, 2005). By providing a unified liquid-crystal classification, the paper enables a global phase diagram for these states and clarifies the nature of phase transitions among them, with direct experimental relevance to trapped cold atom systems where pairing strength can be tuned via Feshbach resonances.
