# Constituent Quark Model and nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> In these notes, while focusing on the meson-nucleon vertices, we give a derivation of the nucleon-nucleon 9NN) potentials from meson-exchange between quarks. To establish such a relation the quark-quark-meson (QQM) interactions are properly defined. Hitherto, the coefficients in the Pauli-spinor expansion of the meson-nucleon-nucleon (NNM) vertices are equated with those of the QQM-vertices. In these notes we employ the description of the nucleon with Dirac-spinors in the SU(6) semi-relativistic "constituent" quark-model (CQM) as formulated by LeYouanc, et al. It appears that the constituent quark model with $m_q= M_N/3$, is able to produce the same ratio's for the central-, spin-spin-, tensor-, spin-orbit-, and quadratic-spin-orbit Pauli-invariants as in the phenomenological NNM-vertices. In order to achieve this, the scalar-, magnetic-vector, and axial-vector interactions require, besides the standard ones, an extra coupling to the quarks without the introduction of new parameters. in the case of the axial-vector mesons an extra coupling to the quarks is necessary, which is related to the quark orbital angular momentum contribution to the nucleon spin. Furthermore, a momentum correlation between the quark that is coupled to the meson and the remaining quark pair, and a (gaussian) QQM form factor, are necessary to avoid "spurious" terms. From these results we have obtained a formulation of the QQ-interactions which is directly related to the NN extended-soft-core (ESC) interactions. This has been applied to mixed quark-nuclear matter in a study of (heavy) neutron stars.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model (CQM) with quark mass \(m_Q = M_N/3\) can reproduce the same ratios for the central, spin-spin, tensor, spin-orbit, and quadratic-spin-orbit Pauli invariants as those found in phenomenological meson-nucleon-nucleon (NNM) vertices, but only if extra couplings to quarks are introduced for scalar, magnetic-vector, and axial-vector interactions, and if a momentum correlation between the active quark and the spectator quark pair (plus a gaussian QQM form factor) is used to eliminate "spurious" terms.

## Method

The author derives nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from meson exchange between quarks by folding quark-quark-meson (QQM) vertices with the three-quark nucleon wave functions of the SU(6) semi-relativistic constituent quark model (CQM) as formulated by LeYouanc et al. (1973). The Dirac spinors for the constituent quarks are approximated as \(u_i(\mathbf{p}_i) \approx [1, \boldsymbol{\sigma}_i\cdot\mathbf{p}_i/(2m_i)]^T\) with \(m_Q = M_N/3\), and the spatial part of the nucleon wave function is taken as a gaussian with size parameter \(R_N\). The overlap integrals for meson exchange are evaluated analytically, and the resulting NNM vertex expansions in Pauli-spinor invariants are compared term-by-term with the phenomenological extended-soft-core (ESC) interactions.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass is set to \(m_Q = M_N/3\), where \(M_N\) is the nucleon mass.
* The instanton density in the QCD vacuum model is \(n_c = 8 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-4}\) and the instanton radius is \(\rho_c = (600\ \mathrm{MeV})^{-1} \approx 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* The non-perturbative vacuum expectation value for quark fields is \(\langle \mathrm{vac} | \bar{\psi}\psi | \mathrm{vac} \rangle \approx -10^{-2}\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\).
* The effective quark mass calculated in the instanton vacuum is \(m_Q(p=0) = 345\ \mathrm{MeV}\), close to \(M_N/3\).
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_{\chi SB} \approx 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and the confinement scale is \(\Lambda_{QCD} \approx 100-300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach to meson-exchange NN potentials by including all meson nonets (pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, axial-vector) rather than only pseudoscalar exchange as in the Glozman-Riska model (1996). It builds on the extended-soft-core (ESC) baryon-baryon interactions (Rijken 2006, Rijken 2015, Nagels, Rijken, Yamamoto 2019) and the quark-pair creation (QPC) model for meson-baryon-baryon coupling constants. The results enable a formulation of quark-quark interactions directly related to the ESC interactions, which can be applied to studies of mixed quark-nuclear matter in neutron stars.
