# Triply-heavy/strange baryons with Cornell potential on a quantum computer

> We present a computation of triply-heavy baryons on a quantum computer, employing the Cornell quark model in line with the earlier quarkonium work of Gallimore and Liao. These baryons are some of the most interesting Standard Model particles which have not yet been detected, as they bear on the short range (colour) behaviour of the nuclear force. The spectrum here obtained is compatible with predictions from earlier works, with our uncertainty dominated by traditional few-body approximations (size of the variational basis, center of mass recoil, parameter estimation...) and not by the statistical error from the quantum computer (deployed here as a small diagonalizer), which turns out to be negligible respect to the other sources of uncertainty, at least in the present unsophisticated few-body approximation. We have also substituted one or more heavy quarks for strange quarks.

## Key claim

The authors compute triply-heavy baryon masses using a quantum computer as a small diagonalizer, finding that the statistical error from the quantum computer (less than 8 MeV) is negligible compared to the dominant uncertainty from traditional few-body approximations (variational basis truncation, center-of-mass recoil correction, and parameter estimation), which reaches about 230 MeV.

## Method

The authors employ the Cornell quark model with a static central potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement) and a harmonic oscillator variational basis truncated to N=3 states. For the three-body baryon system, they use a product basis of single-quark orbitals, approximate the center-of-mass kinetic energy subtraction by a 1/A scaling, and implement the Hamiltonian on IBM's Eagle quantum processor via the Jordan-Wigner transform to express creation/annihilation operators in terms of Pauli gates. The variational parameters are first optimized on a classical simulator, then the expectation value of the Hamiltonian is measured on the quantum computer with 10-25 runs per state.

## Key numbers

* Charmonium spin-averaged ground state mass: 3072 ± 12 MeV (computed), compared to experimental 3069.4 ± 0.1 MeV
* Charmonium first excited state mass: 3779 ± 10 MeV (computed), compared to experimental spin-average 3674.0 ± 0.3 MeV (2.9% error)
* Omega_ccc (triply charmed baryon) mass: 4980 ± 230 MeV, in agreement with earlier pNRQCD computation of 4900 ± 250 MeV (Llanes-Estrada 2011)
* Omega_bbb (triply bottom baryon) mass: 14270 ± 190 MeV
* Omega_bbc baryon mass: 11270 ± 200 MeV
* Omega_bcc baryon mass: 8180 ± 220 MeV
* Bottomonium spin-averaged ground state mass: 9450 ± 11 MeV (computed), compared to experimental 9445.0 ± 0.6 MeV
* b-quark mass extracted: 4594 ± 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier quarkonium quantum-computer calculation of Gallimore and Liao (2023) to triply-heavy baryons (Omega_ccc, Omega_bbb, and mixed b/c combinations) and to systems where one or more heavy quarks are replaced by strange quarks. The computed Omega_ccc mass agrees with the variational pNRQCD result of Llanes-Estrada (2011) and with lattice QCD calculations (Brown 2014), while the uncertainties are dominated by the approximate treatment of center-of-mass recoil rather than quantum computer noise. The results suggest that the all-heavy baryons should be within reach of discovery at Belle-II or the HL-LHC, and demonstrate that current quantum computers can reproduce existing calculations, though without computational advantage over classical methods.
