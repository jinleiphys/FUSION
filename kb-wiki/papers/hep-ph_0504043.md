# Production of $f_0(1710)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1370)$ in $J/ψ$ hadronic decays

> A coherent study of the production of $f_0^i$ ($i=1$, 2, 3 corresponding to $f_0(1710)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1370)$) in $J/ψ\to V f_0 \to V PP$ is reported based on a previously proposed glueball and $Q\bar{Q}$ nonet mixing scheme, and a factorization for the decay of $J/ψ\to V f_0^i$, where $V$ denotes the isoscalar vector mesons $ϕ$ and $ω$, and $P$ denotes pseudoscalar mesons. The results show that the $J/ψ$ decays are very sensitive to the structure of those scalar mesons, and suggest a glueball in the $1.5-1.7$ GeV region, in line with Lattice QCD. The presence of significant glueball mixings in the scalar wavefunctions produces peculiar patterns in the branching ratios for $J/ψ\to V f_0^i\to VPP$, which are in good agreement with the recently published experimental data from the BES collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the production patterns of the scalar mesons $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1710)$ in $J/\psi$ hadronic decays are consistent with a mixing scheme where a glueball state in the 1.5–1.7 GeV region mixes significantly with quarkonium components, but only if the doubly OZI-disconnected diagrams contribute at a non-perturbative strength (parameter $r \sim 1$), contrary to naive perturbative expectations.

## Method

The authors apply a previously proposed three-state mixing scheme among a pure glueball $|G\rangle$, an $|s\bar{s}\rangle$ state, and an $|n\bar{n}\rangle$ state to describe the physical $f_0(1710)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1370)$. They factorize the $J/\psi \to V f_0^i$ decay amplitudes into contributions from the glueball and quarkonium components, introducing a parameter $r$ for the relative strength of doubly OZI-disconnected diagrams. The mixing matrix elements are determined by fitting to WA102 data on $f_0^i \to PP$ decays and to BES data on $J/\psi \to V f_0^i \to V PP$ branching ratios.

## Key numbers

* The ratio $br(f_0(1710)\to \pi\pi)/br(f_0(1710)\to K\bar{K}) = 0.20 \pm 0.03$ from WA102 data is a major constraint in the fit.
* The BES measurement gives $R_{exp} = \Gamma_{J/\psi\to\phi f_0(1710)\to\phi K\bar{K}} / \Gamma_{J/\psi\to\omega f_0(1710)\to\omega K\bar{K}} \simeq 0.15$, compared to the PDG value of 0.75.
* The BES branching ratio for $J/\psi\to \omega f_0(1710)\to \omega K\bar{K}$ is $(13.2 \pm 2.6) \times 10^{-4}$.
* The pure glueball mass $M_G$ is found in the range $1.46$–$1.52$ GeV from the fits.
* The $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing angle is determined to be between $-23^\circ$ and $-18^\circ$ in the fits.
* The predicted branching ratio for $J/\psi\to \omega f_0(1710)\to \omega \pi\pi$ is $1.45 \times 10^{-4}$ using the BES input.

## Context

This work extends the glueball-$Q\bar{Q}$ mixing scheme of Close and Kirk (2001) and Close, Amsler, and Kirk (2002) by applying it to $J/\psi$ hadronic decays measured by the BES collaboration. It contrasts with the perturbative QCD approach of Brodsky et al. (2003) for glueball production at higher energies, and provides a self-consistent explanation for the puzzling OZI-rule violations observed in BES data for $f_0(1710)$ production in $\phi$ versus $\omega$ channels. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted branching ratios for $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1370)$ in $J/\psi$ decays.
