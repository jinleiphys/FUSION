# Constraints on the Symmetry Energy from PREX-II in the Multimessenger Era

> The neutron skin thickness $Δr_{\rm{np}}$ of heavy nuclei is essentially determined by the symmetry energy density slope $L({ρ})$ at $ρ_c = 0.11~{\rm {fm}^{-3}}\approx 2/3ρ_0$ ($ρ_0$ is nuclear saturation density), roughly corresponding to the average density of finite nuclei. The PREX collaboration recently reported a model-independent extraction of $Δr^{208}_{\rm{np}} = 0.283 \pm 0.071$ fm for the $Δr_{\rm{np}}$ of $^{208}$Pb, suggesting a rather stiff symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}({ρ})$ with $L({ρ_c }) \ge 52$ MeV. We show that the $E_{\rm{sym}}({ρ})$ cannot be too stiff and $L({ρ_c }) \le 73$ MeV is necessary to be compatible with (1) the ground-state properties and giant monopole resonances of finite nuclei, (2) the constraints on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter at suprasaturation densities from flow data in heavy-ion collisions, (3) the largest neutron star (NS) mass reported so far for PSR J0740+6620, (4) the NS tidal deformability extracted from gravitational wave signal GW170817 and (5) the mass-radius of PSR J0030+045 measured simultaneously by NICER. This allows us to obtain $52 \le L({ρ_c }) \le 73$ MeV and $0.212 \le Δr^{208}_{\rm{np}} \le 0.271$ fm, and further $E_{\rm{sym}}({ρ_0 }) = 34.3 \pm 1.7$ MeV, $L({ρ_0 }) = 83.1 \pm 24.7$ MeV, and $E_{\rm{sym}}({2ρ_0 }) = 62.8 \pm 15.9$ MeV. A number of critical implications on nuclear physics and astrophysics are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L(\rho_c)\) at subsaturation density \(\rho_c = 0.11\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) must satisfy \(52 \le L(\rho_c) \le 73\ \mathrm{MeV}\) when combining the PREX-II neutron skin measurement of \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) with multimessenger constraints; the abstract's suggestion of a stiff symmetry energy is qualified by an upper limit of 73 MeV imposed by neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision data.

## Method

The authors use the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (eSHF) model, which includes momentum dependence of effective many-body forces, to simultaneously describe nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron stars. They fix 10 of 13 macroscopic model parameters by fitting nuclear data (binding energies, charge radii, giant monopole resonance energies, spin-orbit splittings) and then vary the remaining three parameters (\(L_c\), \(J_0\), \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\)) to explore consistency with constraints from PREX-II, GW170817 tidal deformability, PSR J0740+6620 maximum mass, NICER mass-radius measurements, and heavy-ion collision flow data. The core-crust transition density is determined self-consistently, and all parameter sets satisfy causality.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\): \(\Delta r^{208}_{\mathrm{np}} = 0.283 \pm 0.071\ \mathrm{fm}\) (PREX-II model-independent extraction)
- Constrained range: \(0.212 \le \Delta r^{208}_{\mathrm{np}} \le 0.271\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Symmetry energy slope at \(\rho_c = 0.11\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(52 \le L(\rho_c) \le 73\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 34.3 \pm 1.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy slope at saturation: \(L(\rho_0) = 83.1 \pm 24.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy at \(2\rho_0\): \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 62.8 \pm 15.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Neutron star radius for \(1.4 M_\odot\): \(R_{1.4} = 13.66 \pm 0.71\ \mathrm{km}\)
- Crust-core transition density: \(\rho_t = 0.065 \pm 0.010\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Threshold mass for direct Urca process: \(M_{\mathrm{DU}} = (1.09 \pm 0.41) M_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the symmetry energy by combining the PREX-II result (2021) with multimessenger constraints from GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2018), PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al., 2019), NICER (Riley et al., 2019; Miller et al., 2019), and heavy-ion collision flow data (Danielewicz et al., 2002). It contradicts the softer symmetry energy predictions from *ab initio* coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen et al., 2016) and from earlier compilations (Li et al., 2013; Oertel et al., 2017), while agreeing with earlier transport model analyses (Chen et al., 2005). The results enable precise predictions for the neutron skin of \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\) (to be tested by CREX), for isobaric collisions at RHIC, and for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments.
