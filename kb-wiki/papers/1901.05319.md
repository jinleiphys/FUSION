# Van Hove Singularities and Excited-State Quantum Phase Transitions in Graphene-like Microwave Billiards

> We discuss solutions of an algebraic model of the hexagonal lattice vibrations, which point out interesting localization properties of the eigenstates at van Hove singularities (vHs), whose energies correspond to Excited-State Quantum Phase Transitions (ESQPT). We show that these states form stripes oriented parallel to the zig-zag direction of the lattice, similar to the well-known edge states found at the Dirac point, however the vHs-stripes appear in the bulk. We interpret the states as lines of cell-tilting vibrations, and inspect their stability in the large lattice-size limit. The model can be experimentally realized by superconducting 2D microwave resonators containing triangular lattices of metallic cylinders, which simulate finite-sized graphene flakes. Thus we can assume that the effects discussed here could be experimentally observed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that eigenstates at the van Hove singularities (vHs) in a hexagonal lattice model localize into stripes oriented parallel to the zig-zag direction in the bulk, similar to edge states at the Dirac point but appearing in the interior; this localization is robust against weak disorder but can be destroyed by second-neighbor hopping unless noise is reintroduced.

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalized an algebraic Hamiltonian with nearest-neighbor hopping (and optionally next-to-nearest-neighbor hopping) on a finite hexagonal lattice of \(30 \times 41\) sites with open (Dirichlet) boundary conditions. They computed the local-basis participation ratio \(\mathrm{PR}_\mathrm{loc}^\nu\) for eigenstates at the ground state, Dirac point, and both van Hove singularities as functions of the relative hopping strength \(\lambda_{II}/\lambda_{I}\) and lattice size \(m \times (2m+5)\), with uniform noise \(|\delta\lambda_i/\lambda| \leq 10^{-3}\) added to the hopping coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For \(\lambda_{II}/\lambda_{I} \approx 0\), the participation ratio \(\mathrm{PR}_\mathrm{loc}\) for both vHs states is roughly \(2\times\) larger than for the Dirac-point state, and about \(10\times\) lower than for the ground state.
- As \(\lambda_{II}/\lambda_{I}\) increases, both vHs states delocalize up to \(\lambda_{II}/\lambda_{I} \approx 0.06\), while edge states at the Dirac point remain essentially unaffected.
- The participation ratio scales as \(\mathrm{PR}_\mathrm{loc} \propto m^1\) for states at both vHss and the Dirac point, versus \(\mathrm{PR}_\mathrm{loc} \propto m^2\) for the ground state, indicating linear versus quadratic spatial spread.

## Context

This work extends the experimental identification of excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) in artificial graphene simulated by microwave photonic crystals (Dietz 2013, Dietz 2015) and complements a prior study (Crystals) that first showed stripe localization at vHs energies. The results suggest that the striped vHs states could be observed experimentally in superconducting 2D microwave resonators with triangular lattices of metallic cylinders, similar to the apparatus used for detecting edge states (Dietz 2015).
