# Nonanalyticity, sign problem and Polyakov line in Z3-symmetric heavy quark model at low temperature: Phenomenological model analyses

> The nonanalyticity and the sign problem in the Z3-symmetric heavy quark model at low temperature are studied phenomenologically. For the free heavy quarks, the nonanalyticity is analyzed in the relation to the zeros of the grand canonical partition function. The Z3-symmetric effective Polyakov-line model (EPLM) in strong coupling limit is also considered as an phenomenological model of Z3-symmetric QCD with large quark mass at low temperature. We examine how the Z3-symmetric EPLM approaches to the original one in the zero-temperature limit. The effects of the Z3-symmetry affect the structure of zeros of the microscopic probability density function at the nonanalytic point. The average value of the Polyakov line can detect the structure, while the other thermodynamic quantities are not sensible to the structure in the zero-temperature limit. The effect of the imaginary quark chemical potential is also discussed. The imaginary part of the quark number density is very sensitive to the symmetry structure at the nonanalytical point. For a particular value of the imaginary quark number chemical potential, large quark number may be induced in the vicinity of the nonanalytical point.

## Key claim

The Z3-symmetric effective Polyakov-line model (Z3-EPLM) approaches the original EPLM in the zero-temperature limit, but the Polyakov line expectation value can detect the symmetry structure of zeros of the probability density function at the nonanalytic point, while other thermodynamic quantities are not sensitive to this structure in that limit.

## Method

The authors studied nonanalyticity and the sign problem in the Z3-symmetric heavy quark model at low temperature using two approaches: (1) a free heavy quark model (FHQM) on the lattice with Ns spatial sites, Nf=3 flavors, and Nc=3 colors, where quarks have no spatial momentum and energy equal to their mass M; (2) the Z3-symmetric effective Polyakov-line model (Z3-EPLM) in the strong coupling limit (kappa=0), where the partition function factorizes into independent site integrals over the Polyakov line phase variables. The Z3-symmetrization was implemented by introducing imaginary isospin chemical potentials of i(2pi/3)T, -i(2pi/3)T, and 0 for the u, d, and s quarks respectively.

## Key numbers

- In FHQM, the pressure at T=0 for mu >= M is given by P = (g mu/(6 pi^2))(mu^2 - M^2)^(3/2) - (g/(8 pi^2)){mu sqrt(mu^2 - M^2)(mu^2 - M^2/2) - (1/2)M^4 log((mu + sqrt(mu^2 - M^2))/M)}
- The quark number density at T=0 in FHQM is n_q = (g/(6 pi^2))(mu^2 - M^2)^(3/2)
- The third derivative of pressure diverges at mu -> M+0: d^2 rho/d mu^2 = (g/(4 pi^2))(sqrt(mu^2 - M^2) + mu^2/sqrt(mu^2 - M^2))
- In FHQM, the grand canonical partition function Z = 0 when mu_f = M_f + i(2k+1)pi T for integer k
- In Z3-FHQM, the partition function Z becomes zero at three points: mu = M - i(pi/3)T, M + i(pi/3)T, and M + i pi T
- In Z3-FHQM, the partition function equals Z(T, 3mu; 3M, Nf=1), showing the effective mass is 3M and effective chemical potential is 3mu

## Context

This work extends the study of Z3-symmetric QCD (Z3-QCD) introduced by Kouno (2012) and Kouno (2015), where the sign problem is expected to be weaker than in original QCD. The authors build on previous results from Hirakida (2016) showing that the Z3-symmetric three-dimensional three-state Potts model has no sign problem, and from Hirakida (2017) showing that Z3-EPLM has a much weaker sign problem than EPLM without Z3-symmetry. The analysis uses the Lee-Yang theorem framework from Yang (1952) and Lee (1952) to connect zeros of the partition function to nonanalyticity, and the Roberge-Weiss periodicity from Roberge (1986) to relate imaginary chemical potential to temporal boundary conditions. This work enables better understanding of how to circumvent the sign problem in lattice QCD simulations at finite chemical potential, particularly for mu/T > 1.
