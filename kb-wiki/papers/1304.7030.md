# Photon elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions: hadronic versus partonic sources

> We study the transverse momentum spectrum and the elliptic flow v2 of photons produced in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. As sources for photon production, we incorporate the interactions of off-shell quarks and gluons in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (q+qbar->g+gamma and q/qbar+g->q/qbar+gamma), the decays of hadrons (pi->gamma+gamma, eta->gamma+gamma, omega->pi+gamma, eta'->rho+gamma, phi->eta+gamma, a_1->pi+gamma) as well as their interactions (pi+pi->rho+gamma, rho+pi->pi+gamma, bremsstrahlung m+m->m+m+gamma). The PHSD calculations reproduce the transverse momentum spectrum, the effective temperature T_eff and the elliptic flow v2 of both inclusive and direct photons as measured by the PHENIX Collaboration. The photons produced in the QGP contribute slightly less then 50% to the observed spectrum, but have small v2. We find that the large direct photon v2 - comparable to that of hadrons - can be attributed to the intermediate hadronic scattering channels not subtracted from the data when following the same extraction procedure for v2 as in the PHENIX experiment. On the other hand the v2 of direct photons - as evaluated by the weighted average of direct photon channels - gives a lower signal. The difference between the two extraction procedures for the direct photon v2 can be attributed to different definitions for the yield ratio of direct photons to the background photons. The QGP phase causes the strong elliptic flow of photons indirectly, by enhancing the v2 of final hadrons due to the partonic interaction in terms of explicit parton collisions and the mean-field potentials. We also show that the presence of the QGP radiation is manifest in the slope of the direct photon spectrum, leading to a slope parameter T_eff far above the critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The large elliptic flow v2 of direct photons observed by PHENIX, comparable to that of hadrons, can be attributed to intermediate hadronic scattering channels that are not subtracted from the data when following the same extraction procedure as the experiment, rather than originating from the quark-gluon plasma itself. The QGP phase causes the strong elliptic flow of photons only indirectly, by enhancing the v2 of final hadrons through partonic interactions and mean-field potentials.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, incorporating photon production from off-shell quark and gluon interactions in the strongly interacting QGP (q+qbar->g+gamma and q/qbar+g->q/qbar+gamma), hadronic decays (pi, eta, omega, eta', phi, a1), and hadronic interactions (pi+pi->rho+gamma, rho+pi->pi+gamma, meson-meson bremsstrahlung). The cross sections for partonic photon production were derived from the dilepton production cross sections using the relation between real and virtual photon production, incorporating finite masses and spectral widths for quarks and gluons from the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model.

## Key numbers

- QGP photons contribute slightly less than 50% to the observed direct photon spectrum in minimal bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The effective slope parameter T_eff for QGP photons is 260 +/- 20 MeV, for hadronic photons is 200 +/- 20 MeV, and for the total direct photon spectrum is 220 +/- 20 MeV, compared to the PHENIX experimental value of 233 +/- 14 +/- 19 MeV (fit range 0.3 < p_T < 3.0 GeV/c)
- The elliptic flow v2 of photons from QGP parton collisions saturates at about 0.02 after approximately 5 fm/c, which is lower than the pion v2 of about 0.15 by an order of magnitude
- The purely hadronic HSD model (without QGP phase transition) clearly underestimates the direct photon data

## Context

This work extends the PHSD transport approach, previously used for dilepton production at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies (Linnyk 2011, 2012), to real photon production at RHIC energies. It contradicts earlier theoretical expectations and predictions (Chatterjee 2005, Liu 2009, Dion 2011) that QGP photons should show negligible elliptic flow, and addresses the surprising PHENIX observation of large direct photon v2. The study enables discrimination between hadronic and partonic sources of photon elliptic flow and shows that the QGP radiation is manifest in the slope parameter T_eff far above the critical temperature for deconfinement.
