# Goldstone boson counting in linear sigma models with chemical potential

> We analyze the effects of finite chemical potential on spontaneous breaking of internal symmetries within the class of relativistic field theories described by the linear sigma model. Special attention is paid to the emergence of ``abnormal'' Goldstone bosons with quadratic dispersion relation. We show that their presence is tightly connected to nonzero density of the Noether charges, and formulate a general counting rule. The general results are demonstrated on an SU(3)xU(1) invariant model with an SU(3)-sextet scalar field, which describes one of the color-superconducting phases of QCD.

# Goldstone boson counting in linear sigma models with chemical potential

## Key claim
Nonzero density of a commutator of two broken generators implies one Goldstone boson with quadratic dispersion relation, provided the chemical potential is assigned to an Abelian generator of the exact global symmetry group; this is demonstrated within the class of relativistic linear sigma models at tree level.

## Method
The author analyzes spontaneous symmetry breaking in a general linear sigma model with quartic self-interaction, where finite chemical potential is introduced via covariant derivatives. The field is parameterized using a nonlinear decomposition into Goldstone modes (exponential of broken generators) and Higgs fields, then the bilinear Lagrangian is expanded to extract dispersion relations. The general counting rule is demonstrated on an SU(3)×U(1) invariant model with an SU(3)-sextet scalar field, representing a color-superconducting phase of QCD, for two distinct symmetry-breaking patterns (a>0 and a<0).

## Key numbers
- For the a>0 phase (SO(3) unbroken): the singlet massive mode has energy squared E² = 24μ² − 2M² + O(p²); the singlet linear GB has E² = [(4μ² − M²)/(12μ² − M²)] p² + O(p⁴)
- For the a>0 phase: the 5-plet massive modes have E² = [(24μ² − 2M²)a + 48μ²b]/(a+3b) + O(p²); the 5-plet linear GBs have E² = [(4μ² − M²)a]/[(12μ² − M²)a + 24μ²b] p² + O(p⁴)
- For the a<0 phase (SU(2)×U(1) unbroken): the singlet massive mode has E² = 24μ² − 2M² + O(p²); the singlet linear GB has E² = [(4μ² − M²)/(12μ² − M²)] p² + O(p⁴)
- For the a<0 phase: the doublet of quadratic GBs has dispersion E² = p⁴/(16μ²) + O(p⁶); the doublet of massive modes has E² = 16μ² + O(p²)
- The vacuum expectation value for the a>0 case is Δ² = (1/2)(4μ² − M²)/(a+3b); for the a<0 case it is Δ² = (1/2)(4μ² − M²)/(a+b)
- At the phase transition point (2μ → M+), all GBs become quadratic with dispersion E = p²/(4μ)

## Context
This work extends the Nielsen–Chadha (1976) counting rule for Goldstone bosons in nonrelativistic theories to relativistic field theories at finite density, where Lorentz invariance is explicitly broken. It builds on the theorem of Schafer et al. (2001) that zero commutator expectation values yield the standard GB count, and on Leutwyler's (1994) Ward identity analysis linking nonzero charge density to single-time-derivative terms in the effective Lagrangian. The results enable systematic classification of Goldstone modes in dense QCD phases, particularly color-superconducting phases where abnormal GBs with quadratic dispersion appear, and clarify the fate of the Higgs mechanism for gauge bosons in such systems (Gusynin et al., 2003).
