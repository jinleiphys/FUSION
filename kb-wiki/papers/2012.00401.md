# Atomic calculations of hyperfine structure anomaly in gold

> The magnetic hyperfine structure constants have been calculated for low-lying levels in neutral gold atom and gold-like ion of mercury taking into account Bohr--Weisskopf (BW) effect. BW effect is represented as a product of atomic and nuclear ($d_\mathrm{nuc}$) factors. We have calculated the atomic factors, which enable one to extract BW-correction values for far from stability gold nuclei from the experimental data. The possible uncertainty of our atomic calculations have been estimated by the comparison with the available experimental data. It has been shown that the standard single-particle approach in $d_\mathrm{nuc}$ calculation reasonably well describes experimental data for $11/2^-$ gold isomers and $3/2^+$ ground state of $\rm ^{199}Au$. At the same time, it fails to describe the hyperfine constant in $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$. This indicates the more pronounced configuration mixing in $\rm ^{197}Au$ than in $\rm ^{199}Au$.

# Atomic calculations of hyperfine structure anomaly in gold

## Key claim

The standard single-particle nuclear model for the Bohr-Weisskopf effect can describe the hyperfine structure of $11/2^-$ gold isomers and the $3/2^+$ ground state of $^{199}\mathrm{Au}$, but fails for $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$, indicating more pronounced configuration mixing in $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ than in $^{199}\mathrm{Au}$; the atomic calculations achieve an estimated uncertainty of 2.5% for the ${\cal A}_0(6s_{1/2})$ parameter in both Au I and Hg II.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic atomic structure calculations for neutral gold (Au I) and gold-like mercury ion (Hg II) using Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) with many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) and linearized single-double coupled clusters (LCC) methods, including Breit corrections and random phase approximation (RPA) for core polarization. The Bohr-Weisskopf effect was factorized into atomic parameters ($b_M$, $b_N$, ${\cal A}_0$) and a nuclear factor $d_\mathrm{nuc}$, with the atomic parameters calculated numerically for the $6s_{1/2}$ and $6p_{1/2}$ states using basis sets up to $22spdfghi$ (224 orbitals). Nuclear factors were extracted from experimental relative hyperfine anomaly data using the calculated atomic factor $\eta = b_{M,s_{1/2}}/b_{M,p_{1/2}}$, and compared with single-particle nuclear model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Final atomic factor for gold: $\eta(\mathrm{Au}) = 4.0(3)$, adopted as the mean value across different approximations with uncertainty covering the dispersion
- Final atomic factor for Hg II: $\eta(\mathrm{Hg\,II}) = 3.71(9)$
- Relative hyperfine anomaly $\rm ^{197}\Delta^{198}= 8.53(8)\%$ and $\rm ^{197}\Delta^{180}= 21(14)\%$ reported in prior work for gold isotopes
- Experimental $A(6s_{1/2})$ for $^{193}\mathrm{Au}^m$: 32391(30) MHz; theoretical value from RPA+LCC+Breit: 33101 MHz (relative error 2.2%)
- Experimental $A(6p_{1/2})$ for $^{193}\mathrm{Au}^m$: 3696(4) MHz; theoretical value: 3558 MHz (relative error -3.7%)
- Experimental $A(6s_{1/2})$ for $^{199}\mathrm{Hg\,II}$: 40507.3479968416(4) MHz; theoretical value: 40474 MHz (relative error -0.08%)
- Nuclear factor from single-particle model for $^{199}\mathrm{Au}$ ($I=3/2$): $d_\mathrm{nuc} = -3.7$; from experimental data: $d_\mathrm{nuc}^{199} = -3.2(5)$
- Nuclear factor from experimental data for $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$: $d_\mathrm{nuc}^{197} = -5.1(5)$; single-particle model gives $d_\mathrm{nuc} = -8.0$
- Nuclear factor for $^{193}\mathrm{Au}^m$ ($11/2^-$ isomer): $d_\mathrm{nuc} = 0.73$ from single-particle model
- BW correction for $6s_{1/2}$ state of $^{199}\mathrm{Au}$: $\varepsilon^{199} = 6.0(9)\%$; for $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$: $\varepsilon^{197} = -9.6(9)\%$

## Context

This work extends the method of relative hyperfine anomaly determination from atomic calculations previously applied to thallium (b2012) and bismuth (Schmidt 2018) isotopes, and improves upon the authors' own preliminary results for gold (B2020) by significantly extending basis sets and improving correlation corrections. The large RHFA values reported for gold by Ekstrom (1980) and Harding (2020) motivated the need for reliable atomic calculations to extract nuclear g-factors for short-lived gold isotopes far from stability. The results enable extraction of nuclear magnetization distribution information (the $d_\mathrm{nuc}$ factor) for gold isotopes from experimental hyperfine structure data, and reveal that the $3/2^+$ ground states of $^{191,193,195,197}\mathrm{Au}$ have significant configuration mixing compared to the relatively pure $d_{3/2}$ configuration in $^{199}\mathrm{Au}$.
