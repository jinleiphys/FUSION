# Formation and decay of super heavy systems

> We investigate the formation and the decay of heavy systems which are above the fission barrier. By using a microscopic simulation of constraint molecular dynamics (CoMD) on Au+Au collision, we observe composite states stay for very long time before decaying by fission.

# Formation and decay of super heavy systems

## Key claim
Using constraint molecular dynamics (CoMD) simulations of Au+Au collisions, the authors find that super-heavy composite systems above the fission barrier can survive for surprisingly long times (10^3 to 10^4 fm/c) before fissioning, but the exact lifetime depends on the choice of effective interaction parameters and the authors present results as upper and lower estimates rather than a single prediction.

## Method
The authors apply the constraint molecular dynamics (CoMD) model, which incorporates the Fermionic nature of nucleons through a phase-space occupation constraint (f ≤ 1) and stochastic momentum adjustments, to simulate head-on (b=0 fm) and peripheral (b=6 fm) collisions of two 197Au nuclei at laboratory energies from 5 to 35 MeV/nucleon. They compare results using two parameter sets for the effective interaction, as well as comparisons with quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) and Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) calculations. The fission time is extracted by fitting the survival probability of fragments with A > 350 to a delayed-fission model with constant fission width.

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of 197Au: 7.6 MeV/nucleon (parameter set I) and 8.4 MeV/nucleon (parameter set II)
- Ground state root mean square radius of 197Au: 5.76 fm (parameter set I) and 5.34 fm (parameter set II)
- Nucleon evaporation from ground state over 1000 fm/c: 2.75 nucleons (parameter set I) and 3.1 nucleons (parameter set II)
- Longest composite lifetime observed at Elab = 10 MeV/nucleon for both parameter sets
- Composite lifetime range: 10^3 to 10^4 fm/c for head-on collisions
- Mass asymmetry of fission fragments at Elab = 7 MeV/nucleon: approximately 0.1
- Mass asymmetry of fission fragments at Elab = 10 MeV/nucleon: approximately 0.2
- For U+U system with asymmetry 0.2, largest fragment charge estimated at Z = 112 assuming no proton loss

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of fission dynamics and super-heavy element formation, particularly addressing systems where the classical fission barrier no longer exists, a regime previously considered ineffective for fusion by Gaggeler and Herrmann. The CoMD model was developed by Papa and colleagues to include Fermionic nature while remaining computationally feasible for heavy systems (total mass > 200), unlike antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) or fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD). The results connect to earlier interest in spontaneous positron production from strong electric fields in U+U collisions (Reinhardt and collaborators) and suggest that around 10 MeV/nucleon incident energy may optimize the lifetime of the strong electric field for such QED processes. The authors encourage experimental confirmation using 4π detectors and measurements of energy-averaged angular distributions and excitation functions for binary processes.
