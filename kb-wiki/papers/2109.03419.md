# Study on deformed halo nucleus $^{31}$Ne with Glauber model based on microscopic self-consistent structures

> We study the exotic deformed nucleus $^{31}$Ne using an approach that combines self-consistent structure and reaction theory. We utilize the fully-relativistic, microscopic deformed Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to demonstrate that deformation and pairing correlations give rise to a halo structure with large-amplitude $p$-wave configuration in $^{31}$Ne. We then use the valence nucleon wave functions and angle-averaged density distributions of $^{30}$Ne from this model as input for a Glauber reaction model to study the observables of neutron-rich Neon isotopes and search for halo signatures. Our predictions of the reaction cross sections of these exotic Neon isotopes on a Carbon target can better reproduce the experimental data than those from relativistic mean field model for a spherical shape with resonances and pairing correlations contributions, as well as those from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model. The one-neutron removal cross section at 240 MeV/nucleon, the inclusive longitudinal momentum distribution of the $^{30}$Ne, and the valence neutron residues from the $^{31}$Ne breakup reaction are largely improved over previous theoretical predictions and agree well with data. These reaction data indicate a dilute density distribution in coordinate space and are a canonical signature of a halo structure.

# Study on deformed halo nucleus $^{31}$Ne with Glauber model based on microscopic self-consistent structures

## Key claim

Deformation and pairing correlations in $^{31}$Ne give rise to a halo structure with a large-amplitude $p$-wave configuration, but the predicted spin-parity of the valence neutron orbital is $1/2^-$ rather than the $3/2^-$ value reported in experiments by Nakamura (2014) and others, which the authors attribute to a slightly smaller predicted deformation.

## Method

The authors used the fully-relativistic, microscopic deformed Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the NL1 effective interaction to calculate ground-state properties of $^{26-31}$Ne, including density distributions, single-particle levels, and deformation parameters. They then used the angle-averaged density distributions of $^{30}$Ne and valence nucleon wave functions from DRHBc as input for a Glauber reaction model to calculate reaction cross sections, one-neutron removal cross sections, and longitudinal momentum distributions for $^{31}$Ne bombarding a $^{12}$C target at 240 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

* RMS matter radii: 3.29 fm for $^{29}$Ne, 3.34 fm for $^{30}$Ne, and 3.46 fm for $^{31}$Ne, with quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2$ of 0.16, -0.07, and 0.26 respectively.
* The radius increase from $^{30}$Ne to $^{31}$Ne is 2.4 times larger than the increase from $^{29}$Ne to $^{30}$Ne.
* One-neutron separation energy $S_n$ for $^{31}$Ne is 1.03 MeV, a factor of 2.6 lower than that of $^{29}$Ne.
* The valence neutron orbital ($1/2^-$) has occupation probability 0.5, with $p$-wave components contributing 41.4% ($2p_{3/2}$), 9.8% ($3p_{3/2}$), and 7.8% ($2p_{1/2}$), plus 35.3% from $1f_{7/2}$.
* The predicted full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the longitudinal momentum distribution for $^{30}$Ne residues from $^{31}$Ne breakup is approximately 72 MeV/c.
* One-neutron removal cross section for $^{31}$Ne is a factor of 5-7 times larger than those of neighboring $^{29}$Ne and $^{27}$Ne.
* Measured reaction cross sections increase by $39 \pm 7$ mb/amu from $^{26}$Ne to $^{30}$Ne, then by $86 \pm 39$ mb/amu from $^{30}$Ne to $^{31}$Ne (a factor of $2.2 \pm 1.1$ increase in slope); the DRHBc+Glauber model predicts an increase of 37 mb/amu from $^{26}$Ne to $^{30}$Ne, then a factor of 2.7 increase from $^{30}$Ne to $^{31}$Ne.
* Total cross section predictions using NL3 and PK1 effective interactions are approximately 40 mb (3%) lower than predictions using the NL1 interaction.

## Context

This work extends previous spherical-model studies of $^{31}$Ne, including the RAB model by Zhang (2014) which used relativistic mean field theory with BCS pairing but assumed spherical shape, and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model by Horiuchi (2012) which could not reproduce the measured increase in interaction cross section at $^{31}$Ne. It also improves upon the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) approach by Sumi (2012) which underestimated the reaction cross section increase. The DRHBc theory had previously been applied to deformed halos in $^{17,19}$B by Yang (2021) and Sun (2021), carbon isotopes by Sun (2018, 2020), and $^{36,38}$Ne and $^{42,44}$Mg by Zhou (2010) and Li (2012). The present approach enables self-consistent predictions of multiple halo signatures (enhanced reaction cross section, enhanced one-neutron removal cross section, and narrow momentum distribution) within a single framework that includes deformation effects, and the authors anticipate it can be applied to other halo nuclei.
