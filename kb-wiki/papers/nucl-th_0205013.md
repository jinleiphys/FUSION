# Spectral function and relativistic mean-field description of (un)polarized (e,e'p) reactions: a consistent picture

> We analyze the unpolarized and polarized electron-induced proton-knockout reactions on $O^{16}$ in different kinematical settings using two theoretical approaches. The first one is based on a relativistic mean-field distorted-wave description of the bound and scattering states of the proton, including a fully relativistic electromagnetic current operator. The second approach adopts the same current operator, but describes the proton properties consistently on the basis of microscopic calculations of the self-energy in $O^{16}$ below the Fermi energy and final-state damping in nuclear matter above the Fermi energy, using the same realistic short-range and tensor correlations. Good agreement with all unpolarized and polarized data is obtained at low and high $Q^2$ by using the same spectroscopic factors fixed by the low-$Q^2$ analysis, indicating that a high degree of internal consistency has been reached.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent microscopic description of both unpolarized and polarized (e,e'p) reactions on 16O can be achieved across low and high momentum transfer Q² using the same spectroscopic factors fixed by low-Q² analysis, but the agreement depends on the specific off-shell prescription for the electromagnetic current and is limited by the absence of spin-orbit effects in the eikonal treatment of final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical approaches: (1) a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) using relativistic mean-field bound states and Schrödinger-equivalent optical potentials for scattering states, with an effective relativistic current operator; (2) a microscopic approach using quasihole states from self-energy calculations with realistic short-range and tensor correlations for 16O, combined with an eikonal description of final-state damping in nuclear matter. Both approaches employ the same effective Pauli reduction and are tested against unpolarized cross section data at low (90 MeV proton energy) and high (Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²) momentum transfer, as well as polarization transfer observables from the JLAB E89033 experiment.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors from low-Q² analysis: Z_{p1/2} = 0.644 and Z_{p3/2} = 0.537 for the quasihole approach
- RDWIA spectroscopic factors from χ² fit using cc3 current: Z_{p1/2} = 0.708 and Z_{p3/2} = 0.602
- High-Q² data at Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)² in perpendicular kinematics for 16O(e,e'p) reaction
- Sensitivity to off-shell effects in polarization observables at most ≤ 15%
- The p3/2 state at missing energy E_m = 6.32 MeV and s1/2 state at E_m ≈ 28 MeV are analyzed

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Radici, Meucci, and Dickhoff (2002) from unpolarized to polarized (e,e'p) reactions, comparing with the RDWIA approach of Meucci, Giusti, and Pacati (2001). It addresses the long-standing issue of spectroscopic factor extraction from (e,e'p) reactions, where nonrelativistic DWIA analyses (Kelly, 1996; Lapikas, 1993) required systematic rescaling from mean-field expectations. The consistent treatment enables future extraction of spectroscopic factors independent of Q², though the authors note that direct extraction from the 8 data points of the E89003 experiment produces ambiguous results with high χ² per degree of freedom.
