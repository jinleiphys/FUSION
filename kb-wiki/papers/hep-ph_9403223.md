# Charge symmetry breaking via rho-omega mixing from model quark-gluon dynamics

> The quark-loop contribution to the $ρ^0-ω$ mixing self-energy function is calculated using a phenomenologically successful QCD-based model field theory in which the $ρ^0$ and $ω$ mesons are composite $\bar{q}q$ bound states. In this calculation the dressed quark propagator, obtained from a model Dyson-Schwinger equation, is confining. In contrast to previous studies, the meson-$\bar{q}q$ vertex functions are characterised by a strength and range determined by the dynamics of the model; and the calculated off-mass-shell behaviour of the mixing amplitude includes the contribution from the calculated diagonal meson self-energies. The mixing amplitude is shown to be very sensitive to the small isovector component of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The spacelike quark-loop mixing-amplitude generates an insignificant charge symmetry breaking nuclear force.

## Key claim

The quark-loop contribution to the rho-omega mixing amplitude generates an insignificant charge symmetry breaking nuclear force at spacelike momenta relevant to nucleon-nucleon exchange, even when the calculation includes self-consistent features from a QCD-based model field theory that were absent in previous studies.

## Method

The authors calculated the quark-loop contribution to the rho-omega mixing self-energy using the Global Colour-symmetry Model (GCM), a QCD-based model field theory where rho and omega mesons are composite quark-antiquark bound states. The dressed quark propagator was obtained from a model Dyson-Schwinger equation that yields an entire function in the complex momentum plane, implementing quark confinement, and the meson-quark-antiquark vertex functions were determined by the model dynamics rather than being inserted phenomenologically. The mixing amplitude was computed including the off-mass-shell contribution from the calculated diagonal meson self-energies, and the resulting charge symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential was evaluated using standard Bonn meson exchange parameters.

## Key numbers

- The established on-mass-shell mixing amplitude from e+e- to 2pi data is -4520 +/- 600 MeV^2.
- With the isovector component of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking treated as a free parameter, fitting the mass-shell mixing amplitude requires c1 = 0.009.
- The quark loop mixing amplitude near P^2 approximately 0 is about four times smaller than the empirical mass-shell value.
- The node in the mixing amplitude occurs at approximately P0^2 about 0.5 GeV^2.
- The model parameters lambda = 0.893 GeV and c0 = 0.523 yield quark condensate (183 MeV)^3, f_pi = 90 MeV, and r_pi = 0.56 fm.
- The current masses used are m_u = 5 MeV and m_d = 10 MeV, with (m_u + m_d)/2 = 7.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-loop calculations of rho-omega mixing by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (1992) and by Krein, Thomas and Williams (1993), which used free constituent quark propagators or simple phenomenological dynamical quark propagators. The authors find that even with improved treatment of confinement, vertex functions, and off-shell propagator structure, the quark-loop mechanism alone produces a negligible charge symmetry breaking nuclear force, contradicting the successful phenomenology that treats the mixing amplitude as a constant. The paper suggests that other isospin-symmetry breaking intermediate states, such as the virtual pion-loop process rho to pi pi to omega through a G-parity violating omega-pi-pi vertex, may contribute significantly and should be investigated.
