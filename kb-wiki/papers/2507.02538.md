# Matching from quark to hadronic operators: external source vs spurion methods

> Various weak processes at the hadronic scale have been utilized to search for new physics at high energy scale, which can be described by the QCD chiral Lagrangian matched from the low-energy effective theory (LEFT). Utilizing the chiral symmetry $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R \to SU(2)_V$ at the quark and hadronic levels, we make a comprehensive comparison of various matching methods, including the external source method, conventional spurion method, and our systematic spurion method. Although different methods show agreements for dimension-6 LEFT operator matching, we find that for higher-dimensional operators, the external source method is quite limited or inapplicable, the conventional spurion methods needs to introduce more and more spurions, while our spurion method does not need to introduce any new spurions than the ones in the dimension-6 matching. Using minimal set of spurions, we thus establish an one-to-one correspondence between the LEFT and chiral operators, with several examples, such as derivative operators at dimensions 7 and 8 with a single bilinear, four-quark operators at the dimension-9 level with two quark bilinears, which can be applied to the study of neutrino/electron scatterings and neutrinoless double beta decay.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares three methods for matching low-energy effective theory (LEFT) operators to chiral perturbation theory operators, finding that while all methods agree for dimension-6 operators, the external source method becomes limited or inapplicable for higher-dimensional operators, the conventional spurion method requires increasingly many spurions, and the authors' systematic spurion method requires no new spurions beyond those used at dimension-6.

## Method

The authors construct chiral Lagrangians from quark-level LEFT operators using three approaches: the external source method (treating lepton currents as external sources coupled to QCD), the conventional spurion method (mapping quark fields to meson fields via substitution rules at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order), and their systematic spurion method (using Young tensor techniques with a minimal set of spurions that transform under SU(2)L × SU(2)R → SU(2)V). They match vector, axial-vector, scalar, pseudo-scalar, and tensor currents up to O(p⁴) in the chiral expansion, then extend the analysis to derivative operators at dimensions 7 and 8 and four-quark operators at dimension 9.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant F₀ ≈ 92.4 MeV is used in the definition of the Goldstone boson matrix field U(x) = exp(i φ·τ / F₀)
- The low-energy constant B ≈ 2.8 GeV describes quark condensation in the chiral Lagrangian
- The chiral Lagrangian at O(p²) contains the term (F₀²/4) Tr[DμU(DμU)†] with F₀ ≈ 92.4 MeV
- At O(p⁴), the chiral Lagrangian for tensor interactions contains 4 independent LECs (Λ₁, Λ₂, Λ₃, Λ₄) in the u-basis formulation
- The systematic spurion method establishes matching for derivative operators at dimensions 7 and 8 with a single quark bilinear, and four-quark operators at dimension 9 with two quark bilinears

## Context

This work extends the matching framework established by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for external source matching to χPT, and the conventional spurion method used by Grinstein (1985), Savage (1998), and Cirigliano (2017, 2018) for four-quark operators in neutrinoless double beta decay studies. The systematic spurion method builds on the Young tensor technique developed by Li (2020, 2022) and Song (2025) for constructing complete chiral Lagrangians, enabling matching for higher-dimensional LEFT operators that cannot be handled by the external source method, with applications to neutrino/electron scatterings and neutrinoless double beta decay.
