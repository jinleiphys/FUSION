# Bayesian Inference of Dense-Matter Equations of State from Small-Radius Compact Stars with Twin-Star Scenarios

> We investigate dense-matter equations of state (EOSs) within a Bayesian framework, with particular emphasis on whether recent small-radius compact-star candidates can be accommodated in a twin-star scenario. For the hadronic sector, we adopt a meta-modeling EOS constrained by the NICER mass--radius measurements of PSR J0030$+$0451, PSR J0437$-$4715, PSR J0614$-$3329, and the massive pulsar PSR J0740$+$6620. The hadronic inference indicates that PSR J0614$-$3329 favors a somewhat softer EOS than the other two \(\sim1.4\,M_\odot\) pulsars, while the \(\sim2\,M_\odot\) constraint prevents the EOS from becoming too soft. We then introduce a strong first-order phase transition through a constant-speed-of-sound quark-matter segment. Using HESS J1731$-$347 and XTE J1814$-$338 to constrain the phase-transition parameters, we find a preferred transition density of \(n_\mathrm{t}\sim2.7\text{--}2.8\,n_0\), a sizable energy-density jump of \(600\text{--}700\) MeV, and a relatively large post-transition sound speed of \(c_s^2/c^2\sim0.85\). Such a phase transition generates a disconnected hybrid branch with radii of about \(6\text{--}7\) km at masses around \(1.2\text{--}1.4\,M_\odot\), and strongly suppresses the dimensionless tidal deformability relative to the purely hadronic branch. This pronounced change in tidal deformability is a characteristic signature of the twin-star mechanism and may provide an important observational tool for identifying phase transitions in neutron-star matter in future multimessenger measurements. These results show that small-radius compact stars can provide direct constraints on both the strength of a first-order phase transition and the stiffness of the post-transition phase in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that small-radius compact-star candidates (HESS J1731-347 and XTE J1814-338) can be accommodated within a twin-star scenario generated by a strong first-order phase transition, but only if the transition occurs at a density of approximately 2.7-2.8 times nuclear saturation density, with a large energy-density jump of 600-700 MeV and a very stiff post-transition phase with sound speed near 0.85c^2.

## Method

The authors construct a two-phase equation of state combining a hadronic meta-modeling baseline (parametrized by nuclear empirical parameters around saturation density) with a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) quark-matter segment for the high-density phase. They perform Bayesian inference using NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0437-4715, PSR J0614-3329, and PSR J0740+6620 to constrain the hadronic sector, then use HESS J1731-347 and XTE J1814-338 to constrain the phase-transition parameters (transition density, energy-density jump, and post-transition sound speed) under the Seidov stability criterion for disconnected hybrid-star branches.

## Key numbers

* Transition density from Bayesian inference: n_t = 2.80^{+0.68}_{-0.40} n_0 for HESS J1731-347 alone; n_t = 2.75^{+0.47}_{-0.33} n_0 for joint analysis with XTE J1814-338
* Energy-density jump above the critical value: Delta-epsilon - Delta-epsilon_cr = 588.58^{+298.47}_{-303.54} MeV for HESS J1731-347 alone; 720.73^{+139.19}_{-127.91} MeV for joint analysis
* Post-transition sound speed: c_s^2/c^2 = 0.85^{+0.10}_{-0.10} for both analyses
* Hybrid branch radii: approximately 6-7 km at masses around 1.2-1.4 solar masses
* For a representative hadronic baseline with Q_sat = -120 MeV, L_sym = 10 MeV, K_sym = -130 MeV, Q_sym = 400 MeV: maximum NS mass = 2.03 solar masses with R_max = 10.81 km at center density around 5 n_0
* For transition density n_t = 2.5 n_0 with Delta-epsilon - Delta-epsilon_cr = 450 MeV and c_s^2/c^2 = 0.8: maximum hybrid-branch mass = 1.455 solar masses with radius 6.997 km

## Context

This work extends the constant-speed-of-sound parametrization framework introduced by Alford and Han (2013) and the meta-modeling approach developed by Margueron et al. (2018), building on previous Bayesian inference studies by Dong et al. (2025) and Grundler et al. (2025). The paper specifically addresses whether recently reported small-radius compact objects (HESS J1731-347 from Doroshenko et al. 2022 and XTE J1814-338 from Kini et al. 2024) can be explained by a strong first-order phase transition producing a twin-star branch, which would provide an observational signature through strongly suppressed tidal deformability that could be tested by future multimessenger measurements.
