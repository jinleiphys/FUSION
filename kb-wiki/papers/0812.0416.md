# Survey of nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> A dressed-quark core contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors is calculated. It is defined by the solution of a Poincare' covariant Faddeev equation in which dressed-quarks provide the elementary degree of freedom and correlations between them are expressed via diquarks. The nucleon-photon vertex involves a single parameter; i.e., a diquark charge radius. It is argued to be commensurate with the pion's charge radius. A comprehensive analysis and explanation of the form factors is built upon this foundation. A particular feature of the study is a separation of form factor contributions into those from different diagram types and correlation sectors, and subsequently a flavour separation for each of these. Amongst the extensive body of results that one could highlight are: r_1^{n,u}>r_1^{n,d}, owing to the presence of axial-vector quark-quark correlations; and for both the neutron and proton the ratio of Sachs electric and magnetic form factors possesses a zero.

## Key claim

The dressed-quark core contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors, defined via a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation with diquark correlations, provides a comprehensive description of the form factors, but the results are understood as the core contribution that must be supplemented by pseudoscalar meson loop effects to match experimental data; the nucleon and Delta masses are deliberately inflated (1.18 GeV and 1.33 GeV, respectively) to allow for this additional attractive contribution.

## Method

The nucleon is described by a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation in which dressed-quarks are the elementary degrees of freedom and two-quark correlations are expressed via scalar (0⁺) and axial-vector (1⁺) diquarks. The nucleon-photon vertex is constructed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity and involves a single free parameter: a diquark charge radius, set to 0.8 fm. Form factors are computed by solving the Faddeev equation and evaluating the six diagrams of the nucleon-photon vertex, with a subsequent separation of contributions by diagram type, diquark sector, and quark flavour.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass set to \(M_N = 1.18\) GeV and Delta mass to \(M_\Delta = 1.33\) GeV (Table 1), inflated above experimental values to allow for pseudoscalar meson loop contributions.
- Scalar diquark mass \(m_{[ud]_{0^+}} = 0.796\) GeV; axial-vector diquark mass \(m_{(uu)_{1^+}} = m_{(ud)_{1^+}} = m_{(dd)_{1^+}} = 0.893\) GeV (Table 1).
- Diquark charge radius parameter \(r_{qq} = 0.8\) fm (Eq. rqqvalue).
- Proton Dirac form factor probabilities at \(Q^2=0\): quark contribution 0.474, diquark contribution 0.346, exchange contribution 0.180 (Table 2).
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_p = 1.674\) (in magnetons defined by the calculated nucleon mass), with flavour decomposition \(\kappa_p^u = 1.174\) and \(\kappa_p^d = 0.500\) (Table 3).
- Proton Dirac radius \(r_1^p = 0.615\) fm; the softest contribution to the proton's Dirac form factor comes from diagrams involving an axial-vector diquark, with \(r_1^{p,a} = 0.766\) fm (Table 4).
- The neutron Dirac form factor satisfies \(r_1^{n,u} > r_1^{n,d}\) (Eq. r1nund), a consequence of axial-vector diquark correlations.

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation approach to nucleon structure previously applied by Alkofer (2004) and Holl (2005), improving upon those studies by introducing a diquark form factor to account for diquark substructure. The calculation provides a systematic, symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations for baryons, analogous to the rainbow-ladder truncation that successfully describes mesons. The results enable future quantitative studies of pseudoscalar meson loop corrections to nucleon form factors and provide a foundation for interpreting form factor data in terms of dressed-quark and diquark degrees of freedom.
