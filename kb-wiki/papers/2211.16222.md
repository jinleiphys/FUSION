# $D_1(2420)$ and its interactions with a kaon: open charm states with strangeness

> In this work we present an attempt to describe the $X_1(2900)$ found by the LHCb collaboration, in the experimental data on the invariant mass spectrum of $ D^-K^+$, as a three-meson molecular state of the $Kρ\bar D$ system. We discuss that the interactions in all the subsystems are attractive in nature, with the $ρ\bar D$ interaction generating $\bar D_1(2420)$ and the $K ρ$ resonating as $K_1(1270)$. We find that the system can form a three-body state but with a mass higher than that of $X_1(2900)$. We investigate the $K ρD$ system too, finding that the three-body dynamics generates an isoscalar state, which can be related to $D_{s1}^*(2860)$, and an exotic isovector state. This latter state has a mass similar to that of the $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ states found by LHCb, but a very small width ($\sim 7.4 \pm 0.9$ MeV) and necessarily requires more than two quarks to describe its properties. We hope that our findings will encourage experimental investigations of the isovector $K ρD$ state. Finally, in the pursuit of finding a description for $X_1(2900)$, we study the $π\bar K^* D^*$ system where $ \bar K^*D^*$ forms $0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$ states. We do not find a state which can be associated with $X_1(2900)$.

## Key claim

The paper attempts to describe the $X_1(2900)$ state found by LHCb as a three-meson molecular state of the $K\rho\bar D$ system, but finds that the resulting three-body state has a mass higher than that of $X_1(2900)$, and no state associated with $X_1(2900)$ is found in the $\pi\bar K^* D^*$ system either.

## Method

The authors solve Bethe-Salpeter equations for the $\rho D$, $K\rho$, and $KD$ subsystems, then solve three-body equations within the fixed center approximation (FCA) treating $\rho\bar D$ as a cluster that generates $\bar D_1(2420)$. They also investigate the $K\rho D$ system and the $\pi\bar K^* D^*$ system, with the latter motivated by the fact that the masses of $X_0$ and $X_1$ differ by less than the mass of a pion.

## Key numbers

- The $D_1(2420)$ state generated from $\rho D$ interactions has a mass of approximately 2428 MeV and a total width of 33 MeV, in agreement with LHCb data ($M=2424.8\pm0.1\pm0.7$ MeV, $\Gamma=33.6\pm0.3\pm2.7$ MeV) and BES data ($M=2427.2\pm1.0\pm1.2$ MeV, $\Gamma=23.2\pm2.3\pm2.3$ MeV)
- The isovector state found in the $K\rho D$ system has a width of approximately $7.4 \pm 0.9$ MeV
- The $K\rho \bar D$ system with total isospin 0 shows a bump at approximately 3085 MeV for a cutoff of 1000 MeV, which does not agree with the LHCb mass for $X_1(2900)$ of $2904 \pm 5$ MeV
- The $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ masses from LHCb are $M_{X_0}=2866\pm 7$ MeV, $\Gamma_{X_0}=57\pm 13$ MeV, $M_{X_1}=2904\pm 5$ MeV, $\Gamma_{X_1}=110\pm 12$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ found by LHCb (2020), where most works attribute an isoscalar $\bar D^* K^*$ quasi-bound state to $X_0(2900)$ following Molina et al. (2010, 2020). The authors update the model of Gamermann et al. (2007) for $D_1(2420)$ by adding box diagrams and fine-tuning parameters, and investigate three-body systems that have not been studied in prior works on $\bar D_1 K$ systems by Dong et al. (2020), He et al. (2020), Qi et al. (2021), and Chen et al. (2021). The findings suggest that the isovector $K\rho D$ state requires more than two quarks to describe its properties and should motivate experimental searches.
