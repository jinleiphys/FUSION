# Effective chiral theory of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We present a new chiral expansion scheme for the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude which preserves unitarity exactly. Our effective field theory builds on the power counting rules for 2-nucleon reducible diagrams proposed in \cite{lutz}. We evaluate the leading order terms of the isospin one scattering amplitude and elaborate in detail on the $^1S_0$ phase shift. Our chiral description of the $^1S_0$-phase shift does compete in quality with modern phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials. We describe elastic and inelastic scattering quantitatively up to laboratory energies of $E_{\rm lab} \simeq 600$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new chiral expansion scheme for the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude that preserves unitarity exactly, but the scheme requires a specific subtraction procedure at a "small" subtraction scale (mu ~ m_pi) to make the power counting manifest; without this subtraction, the previously proposed Lutz (1999) scheme fails at momenta p > m_pi due to a second non-perturbative phenomenon in the subthreshold scattering amplitude.

## Method

The author develops a relativistic chiral perturbation theory framework with modified power counting rules for two-nucleon reducible diagrams, assigning each pair of nucleon propagators exhibiting an s-channel unitarity cut a chiral power of -3 instead of the naive -2. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved with a kernel expanded according to these rules, using a finite cutoff Lambda (with Lambda < m) to restrict nucleon virtuality and justify a 1/m expansion, while a subtraction at a scale mu ~ m_pi is introduced to render the dispersion integrals finite and preserve the counting. The leading order terms of the isospin one scattering amplitude are evaluated, focusing on the ^1S_0 partial wave.

## Key numbers

- The chiral description of the ^1S_0 phase shift competes in quality with modern phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials up to laboratory energies of E_lab ~ 600 MeV.
- The ^1S_0 partial wave shows a zero at intermediate energies E_lab ~ 280 MeV.
- The dimensionless expansion parameter for the once-iterated kernel is p/(4 f_pi), indicating convergence for small momenta p < 350 MeV, with the dimensionless parameter m_pi/(4 f_pi) ~ 0.4.
- The coupling constant lambda^{-1} = 2 f_pi ~ 186 MeV identifies the chiral limit value of the pion decay constant.
- The leading-order coupling g_0 is estimated as g_0 ~ -1/(8 f_pi^2) from vector meson exchange, with the dimensionless parameter hat g_0 of order unity.

## Context

This work extends the chiral power counting rules for two-nucleon reducible diagrams proposed by Lutz (1999) and shows similarities with the framework of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), but demonstrates that the KSW implementation violates unitarity and fails at unexpectedly small momenta. The scheme provides a systematic alternative to phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials such as those from Machleidt, the Nijmegen group, the Paris group, and the Bochum group, and enables a consistent description of elastic and inelastic s-wave scattering including pion production processes like pp -> pp pi^0 and pn pi^+.
