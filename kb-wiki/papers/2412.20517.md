# Flavour-Dependent Chemical Freeze-Out of Light Nuclei in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the production of light nuclei in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7 - 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV within a flavour-dependent freeze-out framework, assuming different flavoured hadrons undergo separate chemical freeze-out. Using the Thermal-FIST package, thermal parameters extracted from fits to various sets of hadron yields, including and excluding light nuclei, are used to calculate the ratios of the yields of light nuclei, namely, $d/p$, $\bar{d}/\bar{p}$, $t/p$, and $t/d$. A comparison with data from the STAR and ALICE collaborations shows that a sequential freeze-out scenario provides a better description of light nuclei yield ratios than the traditional single freeze-out approach. These results suggest the flavour-dependent chemical freeze-out for final state light-nuclei production persists in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a flavour-dependent (sequential) chemical freeze-out scenario, where strange hadrons freeze out earlier than light hadrons and light nuclei, provides a better description of light nuclei yield ratios in heavy-ion collisions than the traditional single freeze-out approach, but this improvement is conditional on including light nuclei in the thermal fits and, in many cases, on using a partial chemical equilibrium (PCE) framework.

## Method

The authors used the Thermal-FIST package to perform thermal fits within the Grand Canonical Ensemble to hadron yields (pions, kaons, protons, phi, Lambda, Xi, Omega) and light nuclei (deuterons, tritons, helium-3) from central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7–200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. They extracted chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T_ch, baryon chemical potential mu_B, and fireball radius R) under both a single freeze-out (1CFO) scenario and a two-freeze-out (2CFO) scenario where light hadrons and strange hadrons freeze out at different temperatures, and then calculated light nuclei yield ratios (d/p, dbar/pbar, t/p, t/d, He-4/He-3, and hypernucleus H-3_Lambda/He-3) for comparison with STAR and ALICE data.

## Key numbers

* The limiting freeze-out temperature for light hadrons in the 2CFO scenario is 149.41 ± 1.50 MeV without nuclei and 149.05 ± 0.40 MeV with nuclei included; for strange hadrons it is 166.72 ± 0.88 MeV.
* The limiting freeze-out temperature for the 1CFO scenario is 162.89 ± 0.67 MeV for hadrons alone and 152.72 ± 0.28 MeV when light nuclei are included in the fit.
* The freeze-out temperature of light hadrons plus nuclei is 10–20 MeV lower than that of strange hadrons across all collision energies.
* The kinetic freeze-out temperature used in the PCE framework is T_kin = 100 MeV, motivated by blast-wave model analyses.
* The 2CFO scenario within the HRG-PCE framework provides the best description of the d/p, dbar/pbar, t/p, and t/d ratios, except for t/d at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flavour-dependent freeze-out by Chatterjee et al. (2013, 2014) and Flor et al. (2020, 2021), who found that strange hadrons freeze out at higher temperatures than light hadrons. It also builds on a recent study by Yu et al. (2024) that reported distinct freeze-out temperatures for light nuclei (141.7 ± 1.4 MeV) at RHIC energies. The key advance is the simultaneous inclusion of deuterons and tritons (or helium-3) with other hadrons in thermal fits, combined with the partial chemical equilibrium framework and energy-dependent Breit-Wigner resonance treatment, which was not done in earlier analyses. The results enable more precise thermal model predictions for light nuclei production across a broad range of collision energies and support the need for flavour-dependent freeze-out scenarios in interpreting final-state light nuclei yields.
