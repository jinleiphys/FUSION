# Towards Precision Muonic X-Ray Measurements of Charge Radii of Light Nuclei

> We propose an experiment to measure the nuclear charge radii of light elements with up to 20~times higher accuracy. These are essential both for understanding nuclear physics at low energies, and for experimental and theoretical applications in simple atomic systems. Such comparisons advance the understanding of bound-state quantum electrodynamics and are useful for searching for new physics beyond the Standard Model. The energy levels of muonic atoms are highly susceptible to nuclear structure, especially to the mean square charge radius. The radii of the lightest nuclei (with the atomic number, $Z=1,2$) have been determined with high accuracy using laser spectroscopy in muonic atoms, while those of medium mass and above were determined using X-ray spectroscopy with semiconductor detectors. In this communication, we present a new experiment, aiming to obtain precision measurements of the radii of light nuclei $3 \leq Z \leq 10$ using single-photon energy measurements with cryogenic microcalorimeters; a quantum-sensing technology capable of high efficiency with outstanding resolution for low-energy X-rays.

## Key claim

The authors propose an experiment to measure nuclear charge radii of light elements with atomic numbers 3 through 10 using cryogenic microcalorimeters, aiming for up to 20 times higher accuracy than current X-ray measurements with semiconductor detectors. The abstract's claim of "up to 20 times higher accuracy" is a projected goal for the proposed experiment, not a demonstrated result.

## Method

The experiment will measure muonic X-ray transition energies from muonic atoms of light nuclei (Z=3 to 10) using single-photon energy measurements with cryogenic microcalorimeters, a quantum-sensing technology that provides high efficiency and outstanding energy resolution for low-energy X-rays. The nuclear charge radius is extracted from the measured muonic X-ray energies, which are highly sensitive to the mean square charge radius due to the muon's close proximity to the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Projected accuracy improvement: up to 20 times higher accuracy compared to existing measurements for light nuclei with Z=3 to 10
- Target nuclei range: atomic numbers Z=3 to 10 (lithium through neon)
- Existing high-accuracy measurements exist for Z=1,2 (hydrogen and helium) using laser spectroscopy in muonic atoms
- Medium-mass and above nuclei have been measured using X-ray spectroscopy with semiconductor detectors

## Context

This work extends the successful laser spectroscopy measurements of muonic atoms for Z=1,2 (Pohl et al., 2010; Antognini et al., 2013) to the Z=3-10 region, where current X-ray measurements with semiconductor detectors have limited precision. The proposed use of cryogenic microcalorimeters represents a technological advance over conventional semiconductor detectors for low-energy X-ray spectroscopy. The improved charge radii will enable more stringent tests of bound-state quantum electrodynamics in simple atomic systems and may help constrain searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model.
