# Normal mode analysis within a mutilated relaxation time approximation

> In this paper, we present a detailed analysis of normal modes based on the Boltzmann equation within the mutilated relaxation time approximation (RTA). Using this linearized effective kinetic description, our analysis encompasses a complete order calculation in wavenumber k, extending the conventional hydrodynamic mode analysis to intermediate and short-wavelength regions. Furthermore, our linear mode analysis can provide a natural classification of kinetic modes into collective modes and non-collective single-particle excitations. In the case of an energy-independent relaxation time, the behavior of hydrodynamic onset transitions is recovered (Romatschke in Eur Phys J C 76:352, 2016). However, for the case with an energy-dependent relaxation time, the distinct classification becomes less clear, as the location of hydrodynamic modes is not well separated from non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the mutilated relaxation time approximation (RTA), the classification of kinetic modes into collective hydrodynamic modes and non-collective single-particle excitations is clear for an energy-independent relaxation time, but becomes ambiguous for an energy-dependent relaxation time because the hydrodynamic modes are not well separated from non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Method

The authors linearize the Boltzmann equation around global equilibrium and approximate the full linearized collision operator with a mutilated operator that retains the five zero modes (collision invariants) while collapsing all positive eigenvalues into the smallest positive eigenvalue γ_s. They then perform a Fourier transform and solve the resulting secular equations for the fluctuation amplitudes ρ_n of conserved charge densities using the residue theorem (winding number theorem) to determine the existence and dispersion relations of collective modes at intermediate and large wavenumbers.

## Key numbers

- The critical ratio for the hydrodynamic onset transition is γ/κ = 0.2208, below which the two sound modes cease to exist
- The threshold value of γ is determined to be γ/κ = 0.2207, consistent with Romatschke (2016)
- For the energy-dependent case, the relaxation time is parameterized as τ_R = (βE_p)^α t_R, where α = 0 corresponds to the traditional Anderson-Witting RTA, α = 0.38 approximates QCD kinetic descriptions, and α = 0.5 models jet interactions

## Context

This work extends the normal mode analysis of the Boltzmann equation beyond the long-wavelength hydrodynamic regime to intermediate and short-wavelength regions, recovering the hydrodynamic onset transition first identified by Romatschke (2016) in the study of thermal correlators using effective kinetic theory. The analysis provides an alternative way to unravel the non-analytical structures within retarded correlators, addressing the debate between Romatschke (2015) who found hydrodynamic poles as dominant long-lived modes, and Kurkela (2017) who found branch-cuts to be the dominant non-analytical structures. The mutilated RTA approach enables a natural classification of kinetic modes while preserving collision invariance, which the traditional Anderson-Witting RTA fails to do for energy-dependent relaxation times.
