# J/$ψ$p Scattering Length from GlueX Threshold Measurements

> The quality of the recent GlueX $J/ψ$ photoproduction data from Hall~D at Jefferson Laboratory, and the proximity of the data to the energy threshold, gives access to a variety of interesting physics aspects. As an example, an estimation of the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering length $α_{J/ψp}$ is provided within the vector meson dominance model. It results in $|α_{J/ψp}| = (3.08\pm 0.55 ({\rm stat.}) \pm 0.45 ({\rm syst.}))$~mfm.

# J/ψp Scattering Length from GlueX Threshold Measurements

## Key claim
The J/ψp scattering length extracted from GlueX near-threshold photoproduction data using the vector meson dominance model is |α_{J/ψp}| = (3.08 ± 0.55 (stat.) ± 0.42 (syst.)) mfm, but this should be considered only an estimate because the VMD model may be violated by a factor of 5 or so, and a polarization factor of 2/3 at threshold for S-wave has not been included in the extraction.

## Method
The authors fit the GlueX total cross section σ_t(γp → J/ψp) data as a function of the center-of-mass momentum q of the final-state particles using a series expansion σ_t(q) = a_1q + a_3q^3 + a_5q^5, where the linear term a_1 is determined by S-waves only. They then relate the threshold behavior of the photoproduction cross section to the J/ψp scattering length using the vector meson dominance model, combining the fitted a_1 parameter with the γ → J/ψ coupling g_{J/ψγ} = 5.58 ± 0.07 determined from the J/ψ → e+e- decay width measured by the KEDR Collaboration.

## Key numbers
- |α_{J/ψp}| = (3.08 ± 0.55 (stat.) ± 0.42 (syst.)) mfm, extracted from GlueX data using VMD
- a_1 = 0.46 ± 0.16 nb/(GeV/c) from fit to GlueX data alone; a_1 = 0.53 ± 0.12 nb/(GeV/c) from combined GlueX and SLAC data fit
- g_{J/ψγ} = 5.58 ± 0.07, the γ → J/ψ coupling from the KEDR measurement of Γ(J/ψ → e+e-) = (5.55 ± 0.11) keV
- The first GlueX data bin has a weighted average of q = 230 MeV/c, corresponding to 1/q = 0.86 fm, of the order of the proton size
- The ωp scattering length from A2 Collaboration at MAMI is |α_{ωp}| = (0.82 ± 0.03) fm, for comparison

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the first exclusive near-threshold J/ψ photoproduction measurements from the GlueX Collaboration at Jefferson Lab (Ali 2019), covering photon energies from 8.2 GeV to 11.85 GeV. The result disagrees with the earlier global fit by Gryniuk (2016) that gave |α_{J/ψp}| = 0.046 ± 0.005 fm from previous SLAC data (Camerini 1975) and other data far from threshold. The extracted scattering length is much smaller than a typical hadron size (~1 fm), indicating the proton is transparent to J/ψ, and is also much smaller than the ωp scattering length from the A2 Collaboration at MAMI. The result falls within the broad range of theoretical predictions from lattice QCD (Yokokawa 2006: 0.71 ± 0.48 fm), QCD sum rules (Hayashigaki 1998: -0.1 fm), and other approaches. Future Jefferson Lab experiments on charmonium production off proton and nuclei will enable further studies of the J/ψN interaction.
