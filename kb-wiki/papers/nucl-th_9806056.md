# Semiclassical Approximation to Neutron Star Superfluidity Corrected for Proximity Effects

> The inner crust of a neutron star is a superfluid and inhomogeneous system, consisting of a lattice of nuclei immersed in a sea of neutrons. We perform a quantum calculation of the associated pairing gap and compare it to the results one obtains in the Local Density Approximation (LDA). It is found that the LDA overestimates the spatial dependence of the gap, and leads to a specific heat of the system which is too large at low temperatures, as compared with the quantal result. This is caused by the neglect of proximity effects and the delocalized character of the single-particle wavefunctions close to the Fermi energy. It is possible to introduce an alternative, simple semiclassical approximation of the pairing gap which leads to a specific heat that is in good agreement with the quantum calculation.

# Semiclassical Approximation to Neutron Star Superfluidity Corrected for Proximity Effects

## Key claim

The Local Density Approximation (LDA) overestimates the spatial variation of the pairing gap in the inner crust of a neutron star because it neglects proximity effects and the delocalized character of single-particle wavefunctions near the Fermi energy; however, a simple semiclassical approximation that averages the LDA gap over the spatial extent of the Cooper pair yields a specific heat in good agreement with the full quantum Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent HFB calculations for a single Wigner-Seitz cell (radius 29 fm) containing a nucleus representative of the inner crust, using a Woods-Saxon potential for the mean field and the Gogny force in the \(^1S_0\) channel for the pairing interaction. They compared these quantum results to the LDA, where the local pairing gap is obtained by replacing the Fermi energy in the uniform-matter BCS gap equation with the local Fermi energy \(\epsilon_F(R) = \epsilon_F - U(R)\). They then introduced a corrected semiclassical approximation in which the LDA gap is averaged over the density distribution of single-particle states near the Fermi energy, approximated by plane waves.

## Key numbers

- The Wigner-Seitz cell radius is 29 fm, with a Fermi energy \(\epsilon_F = 13.5\) MeV, corresponding to a density of about \(3 \times 10^{13}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (or \(\rho \approx 0.02\) n fm\(^{-3}\)).
- The Woods-Saxon potential has depth \(V_0 = -31\) MeV, radius \(R = 7.5\) fm, and diffusivity \(a = 0.9\) fm; the interior neutron density is \(\rho_{int} = 0.1\) n fm\(^{-3}\) and the exterior density is \(\rho_{ext} = 0.018\) n fm\(^{-3}\).
- The pairing gap in uniform neutron matter at \(\epsilon_F = 13.5\) MeV is about 3.5 MeV, giving a coherence length \(\xi = 3.2\) fm.
- Inside the nucleus at \(R = 3\) fm, the local Fermi energy is 44.5 MeV, the local gap is 1.6 MeV, and the coherence length is 12 fm; at \(R = 8\) fm, the local Fermi energy is 25 MeV, the local gap is 3.5 MeV, and the coherence length is 4.1 fm.
- The root-mean-square relative distance of the Cooper pair is 10.3 fm at \(R = 3\) fm and 3.8 fm at \(R = 8\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier quantum calculation of Barranco et al. (1997) for pairing in the inner crust and directly contradicts the common LDA-based approach used in prior work by Pethick and collaborators, which assumed the local pairing gap equals the bulk value at the corresponding density. The results enable improved modeling of the thermal evolution of neutron stars by providing a simple semiclassical correction that reproduces the quantum specific heat, which is essential for understanding cooling timescales. The specific heat calculation builds on the formalism of De Blasio et al. and Lazzari et al., and the corrected semiclassical approximation offers a practical alternative to full HFB calculations for astrophysical applications.
