# Relativistic N-Boson Systems Bound by Oscillator Pair Potentials

> We study the lowest energy E of a relativistic system of N identical bosons bound by harmonic-oscillator pair potentials in three spatial dimensions. In natural units the system has the semirelativistic ``spinless-Salpeter'' Hamiltonian H = \sum_{i=1}^N \sqrt{m^2 + p_i^2} + \sum_{j>i=1}^N gamma |r_i - r_j|^2, gamma > 0. We derive the following energy bounds: E(N) = min_{r>0} [N (m^2 + 2 (N-1) P^2 / (N r^2))^1/2 + N (N-1) gamma r^2 / 2], N \ge 2, where P=1.376 yields a lower bound and P=3/2 yields an upper bound for all N \ge 2. A sharper lower bound is given by the function P = P(mu), where mu = m(N/(gamma(N-1)^2))^(1/3), which makes the formula for E(2) exact: with this choice of P, the bounds coincide for all N \ge 2 in the Schroedinger limit m --> infinity.

## Key claim

The paper derives upper and lower bounds on the ground-state energy of a relativistic system of N identical bosons bound by harmonic-oscillator pair potentials, using a formula that becomes exact for N=2 and converges to the exact nonrelativistic solution in the large-mass limit; the bounds are expressed in terms of a function P(mu) that lies strictly between 1.376 and 3/2 for all finite masses.

## Method

The authors work with Jacobi relative coordinates to enforce translation invariance and exploit boson permutation symmetry to reduce the N-body problem to a scaled one-body problem. For the lower bound, they use an operator property (Lemma 1) to remove center-of-mass momentum operators from inside the square-root kinetic-energy operator, then minimize over all translation-invariant wave functions without the boson symmetry constraint. For the upper bound, they employ a Gaussian trial wave function and Jensen's inequality to estimate the expectation value of the concave square-root kinetic-energy operator, then minimize over a scale variable.

## Key numbers

- The function P(m) is bounded by 1.376 < P(m) < 3/2 for all masses m, with P(0) = 1.376 and P(infinity) = 3/2.
- In the ultrarelativistic limit m -> 0, the one-body ground-state energy e(0) = 2.33810741 for the linear potential in three dimensions.
- In the nonrelativistic large-m limit, the exact N-body energy is E_NR = N m + 3 (gamma/(2m))^(1/2) N^(1/2) (N-1), reproducing the result of Houston (1935) and Post (1953).
- For N=2, the exact energy is E = (4 gamma)^(1/3) e(m (2/gamma)^(1/3)), where e(m) is the one-body ground-state energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of the one-body semirelativistic harmonic oscillator (Hall, Lucha, Schoeberl, 2001) to the N-body case, and generalizes the nonrelativistic N-body harmonic-oscillator solution known since Houston (1935) and Post (1953). The results provide a first step toward energy bounds for more general central pair interactions in semirelativistic many-body systems, and the method of using Jacobi coordinates to remove center-of-mass effects from the square-root kinetic-energy operator may be applicable to other relativistic N-body problems.
