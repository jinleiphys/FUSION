# Kinetic Freeze-Out Conditions and Net Baryon Density in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$--$39$ GeV within a Collective Flow Fireball Model

> We investigate the effects of transverse and longitudinal collective flow on kinetic freeze-out conditions and net baryon density in 0--5\% central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$--$39$ GeV within the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. Using a covariant statistical fireball model, we fit the transverse momentum spectra of protons and positive pions from STAR data to simultaneously extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T$, baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, and transverse flow velocity $v_T$, for three fixed longitudinal velocities $v_z = 0.0$, $0.2$, and $0.4$. Longitudinal flow induces a systematic upward shift in $T$, spanning $143$--$171$ MeV, $150$--$180$ MeV, and $175$--$209$ MeV for $v_z = 0$, $0.2$, and $0.4$, respectively, arising from a kinematic degeneracy between $v_z$ and $T$ in the Lorentz-invariant distribution function rather than from any hardening of the $p_T$ spectra. While temperatures extracted at $v_z = 0$ and $0.2$ are broadly consistent with the QCD crossover temperature $T_c \approx 155$--$160$ MeV expected from lattice QCD, the values obtained at $v_z = 0.4$ significantly exceed $T_c$, suggesting that the system would no longer be described by a hadron resonance gas at these conditions and indicating that large longitudinal velocities may be physically disfavored at these collision energies. The baryon chemical potential decreases monotonically from $μ_B \sim 420$ MeV at $7.7$ GeV to $\sim 200$ MeV at $39$ GeV, independently of $v_z$. Reconstructing the freeze-out trajectory in the $(ρ_B, \varepsilon)$ plane, we identify a maximum in net baryon density at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 11.5$ GeV, with dynamical flow enhancing the inferred baryon compression by up to $\sim$20\% relative to the static limit. These results provide refined freeze-out benchmarks for future hydrodynamic modeling and for experiments at FAIR and low-energy RHIC runs.

## Key claim

Longitudinal collective flow induces a systematic upward shift in the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature, spanning 143-171 MeV for zero longitudinal flow up to 175-209 MeV for longitudinal velocity 0.4c, but this shift arises from a kinematic degeneracy between longitudinal velocity and temperature in the Lorentz-invariant distribution function rather than from any physical hardening of the transverse momentum spectra; temperatures at longitudinal velocities of 0.4c significantly exceed the QCD crossover temperature of approximately 155-160 MeV, suggesting such large longitudinal velocities may be physically disfavored at these collision energies.

## Method

The authors fit a covariant statistical fireball model incorporating both transverse and longitudinal collective flow to the transverse momentum spectra of protons and positive pions from STAR data for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV. They simultaneously extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, and transverse flow velocity v_T for three fixed longitudinal velocities v_z = 0.0, 0.2, and 0.4, then compute the net baryon density and energy density using the grand-canonical partition function summed over hadron species with the leading-order term in the fugacity expansion.

## Key numbers

- Temperature ranges extracted for three longitudinal velocities: 143-171 MeV (v_z = 0), 150-180 MeV (v_z = 0.2), and 175-209 MeV (v_z = 0.4) across sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7-39 GeV
- Baryon chemical potential decreases monotonically from mu_B ~ 420 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to ~ 200 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV, independently of v_z
- QCD crossover temperature from lattice QCD: T_c approximately 155-160 MeV
- Maximum net baryon density occurs at sqrt(s_NN) less than or approximately 11.5 GeV
- Dynamical flow enhances inferred baryon compression by up to approximately 20% relative to the static limit
- Parametrization coefficients for mu_B(sqrt(s_NN)): d = 1.308 +/- 0.028 GeV, e = 0.273 +/- 0.008 GeV^{-1}
- Parametrization coefficients for T(mu_B): a = 0.166 +/- 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 +/- 0.016 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.053 +/- 0.021 GeV^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the static fireball analysis of Randrup and Cleymans (2006), who first identified the maximum in net baryon density at intermediate collision energies and mapped freeze-out conditions in the (rho_B, epsilon) plane. By incorporating collective flow into the fireball description, the authors show that dynamical expansion reshapes the location and prominence of the rho_B maximum, bringing freeze-out closer to the conjectured QCD critical region at mu_B approximately 400-500 MeV. The results provide refined freeze-out benchmarks for future hydrodynamic modeling and for experiments at FAIR and low-energy RHIC runs.
