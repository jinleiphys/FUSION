# Triton-3He relative and differential flows and the high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy

> Using a transport model coupled with a phase-space coalescence after-burner we study the triton-3He relative and differential transverse flows in semi-central 132Sn+124Sn reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. We find that the triton-3He pairs carry interesting information about the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The t-3He relative flow can be used as a particularly powerful probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

# Triton-³He Relative and Differential Flows and the High Density Behavior of Nuclear Symmetry Energy

## Key claim
The triton-³He relative transverse flow in semi-central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 400 MeV/nucleon is a powerful probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, but the differential flow is less sensitive because it is dominated by triton clusters which show weaker symmetry energy dependence than ³He clusters.

## Method
The authors coupled the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model with a simple phase-space coalescence after-burner using coalescence parameters P₀ = 263 MeV/c and R₀ = 3 fm. They simulated semi-central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and computed the transverse flow per nucleon ⟨pₓ/A⟩ as a function of reduced center-of-mass rapidity for tritons and ³He clusters individually, then constructed the relative and differential flows between the two species.

## Key numbers
- Coalescence parameters used: P₀ = 263 MeV/c and R₀ = 3 fm
- For the t-³He relative flow slope F(x) ≡ d⟨pₓ/A⟩/d(y/y_beam) at mid-rapidity: F(x=1) ≈ -74 MeV/c and F(x=-1) ≈ -22 MeV/c
- For the t-³He differential flow slope: F(x=1) ≈ 21 MeV/c and F(x=-1) ≈ 42 MeV/c
- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions

## Context
This work extends the IBUU04 transport model analysis of π⁻/π⁺ ratios from the FOPI Collaboration at SIS/GSI, which had provided circumstantial evidence for a soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities (Xiao et al., 2009). The study builds on earlier predictions that neutron-proton differential flow is sensitive to high-density symmetry energy (Li, 2000), but addresses the experimental difficulty of measuring neutron observables by proposing the t-³He pair as a charged-particle alternative. The results enable experimental verification of the soft symmetry energy conclusion using a different observable, independent of pion ratio measurements.
