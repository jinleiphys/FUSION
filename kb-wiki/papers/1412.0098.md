# On formation of equation of state of evolving quantum field

> Stylized model of evolution of matter created in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions is considered. Systematic procedure of computing quantum corrections in the framework of Keldysh formalism is formulated. Analytical expressions for formation of equations of state taking into account leading quantum corrections is worked out, complete description of subleasing corrections and analytical expressions for some of them is presented.

# On formation of equation of state of evolving quantum field

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in a stylized scalar field model of matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, averaging over initial conditions with a Gaussian Wigner function leads to formation of the ultrarelativistic equation of state ε = 3p in the large-time limit, but only within the leading-order (LO) approximation in quantum corrections; the result is conditional on the specific Gaussian ansatz and the saddle-point approximation used for analytical integration.

## Method
The authors formulate a systematic procedure for computing quantum corrections using the Keldysh (closed-time-path) formalism for a scalar field theory with λφ⁴/4! interaction. They expand the Keldysh action in powers of the quantum field φ_q around its saddle-point value, which corresponds to a quasiclassical expansion. At leading order (LO), they neglect the φ_q³ term and show that the expectation value of an observable reduces to averaging over classical trajectories weighted by the Wigner function. They then perform an analytical calculation of pressure relaxation using a Gaussian Wigner function ansatz, a change of variables to the amplitude and phase of the classical Jacobi elliptic function solution, and a saddle-point approximation followed by Fourier expansion of the elliptic function.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of equation-of-state formation in scalar field theory previously studied by the same group (initial1, initial2, initial3) using a different method based on summation of leading quantum corrections via integration over initial conditions. The paper introduces a systematic Keldysh formalism that allows computation of subleading corrections, which were not addressed in the earlier work. The results enable future calculation of correlation functions describing transport properties of the system, and the NLO corrections open the way for studying two-point correlation functions within the same framework.
