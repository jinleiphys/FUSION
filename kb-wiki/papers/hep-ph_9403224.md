# Dyson-Schwinger Equations and the Application to Hadronic Physics

> We review the current status of nonperturbative studies of gauge field theory using the Dyson-Schwinger equation formalism and its application to hadronic physics. We begin with an introduction to the formalism and a discussion of renormalisation in this approach. We then review the current status of studies of Abelian gauge theories [e.g., strong coupling quantum electrodynamics] before turning our attention to the non-Abelian gauge theory of the strong interaction, quantum chromodynamics. We discuss confinement, dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the application and contribution of these techniques to our understanding of the strong interactions.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) formalism provides a nonperturbative, continuum-based framework for studying gauge field theories, but its practical application requires truncation of the infinite tower of coupled integral equations at some finite n (currently n ≤ 3), which necessitates employing Ansätze for the omitted higher n-point functions, constrained by symmetries, renormalisability, and known perturbative limits.

## Method

The authors derive the unrenormalised DSEs for the photon polarisation tensor, fermion self-energy, and fermion-photon vertex in quantum electrodynamics (QED) using functional integral methods, then extend the discussion to quantum chromodynamics (QCD). They review how renormalisation is implemented via multiplicative renormalisation constants (Z₁, Z₂, Z₃) and the Ward identity Z₁ = Z₂, and survey existing DSE-based studies of Abelian gauge theories (QED in three and four dimensions) and non-Abelian QCD, focusing on the infrared behaviour of the gluon propagator, dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and quark confinement.

## Key numbers

- The DSE tower must be truncated at some n, where n is the maximum number of legs on any Green's function included; current efforts are limited to n ≤ 3 (no known attempts for n > 3).
- The photon propagator in QED is given by D^{μν}(q) = (-g^{μν} + q^μ q^ν/(q²+iε))/(q²+iε) × 1/(1+Π(q²)) - ξ₀ q^μ q^ν/(q²+iε)², where Π(q²) is defined via Π^{μν}(q) ≡ (-g^{μν}q² + q^μ q^ν)Π(q²).
- The renormalisation constants satisfy Z₁^f = Z₂^f (Ward identity), ensuring charge universality across fermion flavours.
- The fermion propagator is given by S^f(p) = 1/[p̸ - m₀^f - Σ^f(p)], where Σ^f(p) is the fermion self-energy.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on DSEs by Dyson (1949) and Schwinger (1951) and provides a comprehensive survey of nonperturbative continuum methods complementary to lattice gauge theory (Rothe, 1992). The formalism enables studies of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, and the authors note that future lattice gauge theory simulations may provide additional insight into the form of higher n-point functions needed to improve truncation schemes.
