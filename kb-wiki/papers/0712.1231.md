# Trajectory of virtual, bound and resonant Efimov states

> The pole trajectory of Efimov states for a three-body $ααβ$ system with $αα$ unbound and $αβ$ bound is calculated using a zero-range Dirac-$δ$ potential. It is showed that a three-body bound state turns into a virtual one by increasing the $αβ$ binding energy. This result is consistent with previous results for three equal mass particles. The present approach considers the $n-n-^{18}C$ halo nucleus. However, the results have good perspective to be tested and applied in ultracold atomic systems, where one can realize such three-body configuration with tunable two-body interaction.

## Key claim
The paper shows that for a three-body ααβ system where αα is unbound and αβ is bound, an excited three-body bound state turns into a virtual state (not a resonance) as the αβ binding energy increases, passing through the elastic scattering cut. This trajectory differs from the virtual-bound-resonance route seen for three equal-mass particles when the two-body scattering length varies from positive to negative.

## Method
The authors solve coupled integral equations for bound and virtual three-body states using a zero-range Dirac-δ potential in the odd-man-out notation, with units ℏ = mα = 1. They fix the three-body ground state energy and the αα two-body virtual energy, then vary only the αβ binding energy to track the pole trajectory of the excited Efimov state in the complex energy plane. The analytic continuation to the second Riemann sheet is performed through the elastic scattering cut.

## Key numbers
- Three-body ground state energy fixed at E3b(0) = −3.5 MeV for the n-n-18C system.
- αα two-body energy fixed at Eβ = −143 keV (unbound system).
- Results shown for mass ratios A = 1, 18, and 100, where A is the mass of particle β.
- The transition from bound to virtual state occurs at a specific ratio of two-body energies to the three-body ground state energy, which depends only on A in the Efimov limit.

## Context
This work extends the study of Efimov state trajectories from three equal-mass particles (Amado and collaborators, earlier work) to systems with unequal masses, specifically the n-n-18C halo nucleus. The results contradict a different trajectory found by Mazumdar and collaborators for 20C. The findings have good perspective for application in ultracold atomic systems where tunable two-body interactions can realize such three-body configurations, and are consistent with the universal scaling behavior described by Braaten and collaborators.
