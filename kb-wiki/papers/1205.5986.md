# Interpretation of the large-deformation high spin bands in selected A=158-168 nuclei

> The high-spin rotational bands in 168Hf and the triaxial bands in Lu nuclei are analyzed using the configuration-constrained Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model. Special attention is given to the up-sloping extruder orbitals. The relative alignment between the bands which appear to correspond to triaxial shape is also considered, including the yrast ultra-high spin band in 158Er. This comparison suggests that the latter band is formed from rotation around the intermediate axis. In addition, the standard approximations of the CNS approach are investigated, indicating that the errors which are introduced by the neglect of off-shell matrix elements and the cut-off at 9 oscillator shells (N_{max}=8) are essentially negligible compared to other uncertainties. On the other hand, the full inclusion of the hexadecapole degree of freedom is more significant; for example it leads to a decrease of the total energy of ~ 500 keV in the TSD region of 168Hf.

# Interpretation of the large-deformation high spin bands in selected A=158-168 nuclei

## Key claim
The yrast ultra-high spin band in 158Er is formed from rotation around the intermediate axis (γ < 0), but this interpretation remains tentative because the γ < 0 minimum appears to be a saddle point if the rotation axis is allowed to change direction, as noted in a recent study by Shi et al. (2012). The standard approximations of the Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approach, neglect of off-shell matrix elements and cut-off at 9 oscillator shells, introduce errors that are essentially negligible compared to other uncertainties, while full inclusion of the hexadecapole degree of freedom is more significant.

## Method
The configuration-constrained Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model was used to analyze high-spin rotational bands in 168Hf and triaxial bands in Lu isotopes, with the nucleons moving independently in a deformed and rotating mean-field described by the cranked modified oscillator Hamiltonian. The total energy was obtained using the Strutinsky shell correction method combined with the rotating liquid drop energy (Lublin Strasbourg drop model), and minimized with respect to deformation parameters (ε₂, γ, ε₄) for a given angular momentum. The authors tested the standard CNS approximations by investigating the effects of neglecting off-shell matrix elements and the cut-off at 9 oscillator shells (N_max = 8), and performed a complete minimization in the three hexadecapole degrees of freedom for the first time at large triaxial deformation.

## Key numbers
- Full inclusion of the hexadecapole degree of freedom leads to a decrease of the total energy of ~500 keV in the triaxial superdeformed (TSD) region of 168Hf.
- The TSD bands in Lu isotopes have deformation parameters (ε₂, γ) ~ (0.37, 20°) for the yrast TSD bands in Lu isotopes with N = 92–96, while the (42)6 configuration in 161Lu has a larger deformation of ε₂ ~ 0.43, γ ~ 23°.
- The TSD bands in Lu nuclei are characterized by deformation parameters (ε₂, γ) ~ (0.4, ±20°) for the TSD minima, associated with proton numbers Z = 71 and 72 and neutron numbers N = 94 and 96.
- The proton configuration 8(21) (two h₉/₂ and one i₁₃/₂ protons) is favoured for TSD deformations at frequencies up to ħω ~ 0.6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the CNS model analysis of high-spin rotational bands in the A = 158–168 mass region, building on prior studies of TSD bands in Lu isotopes by Odegard et al. (2001) and Gjorup et al. (1995), and on the ultra-high spin bands in 157–158Er studied by Paul et al. (2007) and Wang et al. (2011). The analysis provides a consistent interpretation of the large-deformation TSD band in 168Hf and clarifies the neutron configurations of TSD bands in 161–167Lu, enabling future comparisons with experimental data and further tests of the CNS formalism, particularly regarding the role of hexadecapole deformations and the interpretation of the 158Er yrast ultra-high spin band.
