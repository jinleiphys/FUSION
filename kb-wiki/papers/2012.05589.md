# In-medium effects in strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions at (sub-)threshold energies

> We study the in-medium effects in strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions at (sub-)\-threshold energies of 1 - 2 A GeV based on the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The in-medium modifications of the antikaon $(\bar K = K^-, \bar K^0)$ properties are described via the self-consistent coupled-channel unitarized scheme based on a SU(3) chiral Lagrangian which incorporates explicitly the $s-$ and $p-$ waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction. This scheme provides the antikaon potential, spectral functions and reaction cross sections as well as their dependence on baryon density, temperature and antikaon momentum in the nuclear medium, which are incorporated in the off-shell dynamics of the PHSD. The in-medium modification of kaons $(K = K^+, K^0)$ are accounted via the kaon-nuclear potential, which is assumed to be proportional to the local baryon density. The manifestation of the medium effects in observables is investigated for the $K$ and ${\bar K}$ rapidity distributions, $p_T$-spectra as well as the polar and azimuthal angular distributions, direct ($v_1$) and elliptic ($v_2$) flow in C+C, Ni+Ni, and Au+Au collisions. We find - by comparison to experimental data from the KaoS, FOPI and HADES Collaborations - that the modifications of (anti)kaon properties in nuclear matter are necessary to explain the data in a consistent manner. Moreover, we demonstrate the sensitivity of kaon observables to the equation-of-state of nuclear matter.

# In-medium effects in strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions at (sub-)threshold energies

## Key claim

The in-medium modifications of antikaon properties (described via a self-consistent coupled-channel unitarized scheme based on a SU(3) chiral Lagrangian incorporating s- and p-waves) and kaon properties (via a repulsive potential proportional to local baryon density) are necessary to consistently explain experimental data from KaoS, FOPI, and HADES Collaborations for heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 A GeV, but the agreement depends on the specific implementation of the off-shell dynamics and the equation-of-state of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach, incorporating in-medium antikaon-nucleon interactions from a chiral unitary coupled-channel model that includes s- and p-waves, Pauli blocking, mean-field binding for baryons, and pion and kaon self-energies at finite density and temperature. The in-medium kaon modification was implemented via a repulsive potential linear in baryon density, while detailed balance was implemented for 2-to-3 reactions for strangeness production and absorption channels. The approach was applied to C+C, Ni+Ni, and Au+Au collisions at 1-2 A GeV, and results were compared to experimental data on rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, polar and azimuthal angular distributions, and directed (v1) and elliptic (v2) flow.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 1-2 A GeV for heavy-ion collisions (sub-threshold and near-threshold energies)
- Antikaon-nucleon potential depth: moderate attractive depths of -40 to -60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ0 (from self-consistent chiral calculations cited in the paper)
- Kaon repulsive potential: +20-30 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ0 (consistent with experimental data from p+A, π+A, and A+A collisions)
- Cutoff parameter for loop regularization: qmax = 630 MeV (fixed to reproduce Λ(1405) properties and threshold branching ratios)
- Bare masses used in p-wave amplitudes: Λ = 1030 MeV, Σ = 1120 MeV, Σ* = 1385 MeV (with Σ* mass given as 1371 MeV in the text)
- Coupling constants: D = 0.85, F = 0.52 (for the chiral Lagrangian)
- Antikaon cross sections shown at ρ = ρ0 and 2ρ0 at T = 0 and 100 MeV for K-p to final states π0Σ0, π-Σ+, π+Σ-, and π0Λ
- Spectral functions shown at ρ = 0.5ρ0, ρ0, 1.5ρ0, and 2ρ0 for antikaon momenta p = 0, 150, 300, and 450 MeV at T = 50 and 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier off-shell transport studies using the G-matrix approach based on the Jülich meson-exchange model by Tolos et al. (2002, 2003) and the HSD transport approach, incorporating an improved chiral unitary scheme by Cabrera et al. (2014) that achieves full self-consistency in s- and p-waves at finite density and temperature. The paper builds on the theoretical framework of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) regarding kaon condensation, the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis, and earlier transport calculations by Aichelin et al. (1986), Ko et al. (1990s), Cassing and Bratkovskaya (1997, 1999), and Hartnack et al. (2011). The results enable further constraints on the nuclear equation-of-state from kaon observables and provide a benchmark for future studies of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies.
