# Neutron-proton mass difference in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> Isospin-breaking effects in the baryonic sector are studied in the framework of a medium-modified Skyrme model. The neutron-proton mass difference in infinite, asymmetric nuclear matter is discussed. In order to describe the influence of the nuclear environment on the skyrmions, we include energy-dependent charged and neutral pion optical potentials in the s- and p-wave channels. The present approach predicts that the neutron-proton mass difference is mainly dictated by its strong part and that it strongly decreases in neutron matter.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton mass difference in asymmetric nuclear matter is mainly dictated by its strong part and strongly decreases in neutron matter, with the total mass difference becoming negative at normal nuclear density in pure neutron matter, in contrast to the small density dependence found in the authors' previous work using energy-independent optical potentials.

## Method

The authors employed a medium-modified Skyrme model incorporating energy-dependent charged and neutral pion optical potentials in the s- and p-wave channels, parameterized using chiral perturbation theory results for the pion-nucleon scattering lengths and volumes. They solved the classical equation of motion for the hedgehog profile function by iteration together with a constraint that removes the mesonic isospin-breaking term at the classical level, then quantized the collective isospin rotations to extract the strong and electromagnetic contributions to the neutron-proton mass difference in infinite, homogeneous nuclear matter with varying isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The strong part of the neutron-proton mass difference in isospin-symmetric matter increases by about 42% relative to its free space value at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.5 m_pi^3), whereas in the authors' previous work it decreased by about 2% at the same density.
- In pure neutron matter at normal nuclear density rho_0, the total neutron-proton mass difference is Delta m_np* = -25 MeV.
- The free-space neutron-proton mass splitting used as input is Delta m_np^(exp) = 1.29 MeV, fitted by varying the charged pion mass to m_pi± = 135.015 MeV (with neutral pion mass fixed at m_pi0 = 134.977 MeV).
- The Skyrme model parameters reproducing the isospin-averaged nucleon and delta masses (938 MeV and 1232 MeV) in free space are F_pi = 108.11 MeV and e = 4.826.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous medium-modified Skyrme model study (Meissner 2006) by incorporating energy-dependent pion optical potentials and explicit treatment of isospin-asymmetric environments, which produced qualitatively different density dependence even in symmetric matter. The prediction that the neutron-proton mass difference becomes negative in neutron-rich matter is consistent with relativistic approaches such as QCD sum rule studies (Drukarev 2004, which found about -70 MeV at rho_0) and density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory (Hofmann 2000), while it contrasts with nonrelativistic approaches (Bombaci 1991, Zuo 2005) that find positive values. The results enable future studies of mirror nuclei properties and may be relevant for understanding the stability of drip-line nuclei and transport in neutron-rich matter from heavy-ion collisions.
