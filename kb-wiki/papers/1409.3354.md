# Isotopic ratio, isotonic ratio, isobaric ratio and Shannon information uncertainty

> The isoscaling and the isobaric yield ratio difference (IBD) probes, which both are constructed by yield ratio of fragment, provide cancelation of parameters. The information entropy theory is introduced to explain the physical meaning of the isoscaling and IBD probes. The similarity between the isoscaling and IBD results is found, i.e., the information uncertainty determined by the IBD method equals to $β-α$ determined by the isoscaling [$α$ ($β$) is the parameter fitted from the isotopic (isotonic) yield ratio].

## Key claim
The paper introduces Shannon information entropy theory to reinterpret the physical meaning of the isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio difference (IBD) probes, showing that the information uncertainty determined by the IBD method equals the difference β−α obtained from isoscaling, where α and β are parameters fitted from isotopic and isotonic yield ratios respectively.

## Method
The authors derive the information uncertainty U(e) = −ln σ(N,Z) for a fragment yield σ in the grand-canonical ensemble framework. They then compute the difference in information uncertainty between two similar reactions for isotopic ratios, isotonic ratios, and isobaric ratios, showing how the cancellation of free energy terms leads to expressions involving the chemical potential differences Δμ_n and Δμ_p. The IBD result Δ₂₁U(e_b) = β−α is obtained by assuming equal temperatures and cancelling free energy differences between isobars.

## Key numbers
- The information uncertainty difference for isobars differing by 2 in neutron-excess I is Δ₂₁U(e_b) = (β−α), where β = Δμ_p₂₁/T and α = Δμ_n₂₁/T
- For isotopic ratios, the information uncertainty difference is Δ₂₁U(e_p) = Δc + C_z + NΔμ_n₁₂/T, where C_z = Zβ is treated as constant
- For isotonic ratios, the information uncertainty difference is Δ₂₁U(e_n) = Δc + C_n + ZΔμ_p₁₂/T, where C_n = Nα is treated as constant
- In neutron-proton symmetric matter, the relation α/β = Z/N holds when C_z = C_n

## Context
This work extends the isoscaling method of Huang et al. (2010) and the isobaric yield ratio difference (IBD) method of Ma et al. (2012-2014) by providing a theoretical foundation in information theory. The Shannon entropy approach was previously applied to heavy-ion collisions by Y.G. Ma (1999) for liquid-gas transitions and to hadron decay branching. The paper enables a unified understanding of fragment ratio probes for nuclear symmetry energy, showing that IBD has advantages over isoscaling when fragment distributions do not obey regular scaling, and can be applied to reactions beyond those using isotopic projectiles or targets.
