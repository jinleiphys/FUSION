# Extension of the LAQGSM03.01 Code to Describe Photo-Nuclear Reactions up to Tens of GeV

> The Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model implemented in the code LAQGSM03 was extended to describe photonuclear reactions at energies up to tens of GeV. We have incorporated 56 channels to consider gamma+p elementary interactions during the cascade stage of reactions and 56 channels for gamma+n interactions, in addition to absorption of photons on two-nucleon pairs, into LAQGSM03 and have tested the extended code referred to as LAQGSM03.01 on a number of measured high-energy photonuclear reactions. Here, we present the photonuclear reaction model of LAQGSM03.01 and show examples of its results compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The LAQGSM03.01 code was extended to describe photonuclear reactions up to tens of GeV by incorporating 56 channels for gamma-proton and 56 channels for gamma-neutron elementary interactions, but the model is not yet ready to properly describe reactions induced by gammas of tens of MeV in the Giant Dipole Resonance region.

## Method

The authors extended the existing LAQGSM03 code by incorporating high-energy event generators for gamma-proton and gamma-neutron elementary interactions from the Moscow photonuclear reaction model, adding 56 reaction channels for each nucleon type. For two-body channels (1-14), they used smooth approximations of experimental data with their own algorithms for angular distributions; for channels with two and three pions (15-21), they used the Moscow generators with their own interpolation for integral cross sections; and for multi-pion channels (22-56), they used the isospin statistical model from the Moscow generators without changes. After the photon interacts, the subsequent cascade, preequilibrium, and evaporation/fission stages are handled by the existing LAQGSM03.01 framework.

## Key numbers

* The previous LAQGSM03 code described photonuclear reactions only up to about 1.5 GeV, while the extended LAQGSM03.01 covers energies up to tens of GeV.
* At photon energies of 10-40 MeV, the de Broglie wavelength is 20-5 fm, greater than the average inter-nucleonic distance, making the intranuclear cascade inapplicable in that region.
* The quasi-deuteron absorption model uses a Levinger factor L approximately equal to 10.
* Proton spectra from bremsstrahlung on carbon were measured at E_max = 1.05 GeV and compared with LAQGSM03.01 results.
* Proton and pion spectra were measured at E_max = 2.0, 3.0, and 4.5 GeV on targets of C-12, Al-27, Cu-63, and Pb-208.
* Product yields from 4.5 GeV bremsstrahlung on Nb-93 were compared with LAQGSM03.01 calculations.
* Recoil properties were measured for 4.5 GeV bremsstrahlung on Cu-65.
* Mass distribution of products from 6 GeV bremsstrahlung on U-238 was compared with LAQGSM03.01 results.

## Context

This work extends the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) and its precursor, the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), which previously did not consider photonuclear reactions at all, and the 2003 version LAQGSM03 which only described such reactions up to about 1.5 GeV. The extension uses event generators from the Moscow photonuclear reaction model by Iljinov et al. (1997), which itself improved upon the Genova gamma-proton and gamma-neutron event generators by Corvisiero et al. that described interactions up to 4 GeV. The code was tested against experimental data from the Yerevan Physics Institute (Alanakyan et al., 1977-1980) that had never before been reproduced by any theoretical model. LAQGSM03.01 has been incorporated into the MARS transport code and is being incorporated into MCNP6 and MCNPX, enabling simulation of high-energy photonuclear reactions for applications such as the Accelerator Transmutation of nuclear Wastes (ATW), Accelerator Production of Tritium (APT), and Proton Radiography (PRAD).
