# Density functional approach to finite temperature nuclear properties and the role of a momentum dependent isovector interaction

> Using a density functional approach based on a Skyrme interaction, thermodynamic properties of finite nuclei are investigated at non-zero temperture. The role of a momentum dependent isovector term is now studied besides volume, symmetry, surface and Coulomb effects. Various features associated with both mechanical and chemical instability and the liquid-gas coexistence curve are sensitive to the Skyrme interaction. The separated effects of the isoscalar term and the isovector term of momentum dependent interaction are studied for a modified SKM($m^*=m$) interaction. The frequently used Skyrme interaction SLy4 is one of the cases considered and is shown to have better features for neutron star studies due to a larger symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a momentum-dependent isovector term in a Skyrme density functional significantly alters thermodynamic properties of finite nuclei at non-zero temperature, with the isovector term having a larger effect than the isoscalar term, and that the SLy4 interaction shows better features for neutron star studies due to its larger symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors used a density functional approach based on Skyrme interactions to compute thermodynamic quantities (pressure, chemical potentials, energy density, entropy) for nuclear matter at finite temperature, incorporating volume, symmetry, surface, Coulomb, and momentum-dependent terms. They isolated the effects of the isoscalar and isovector momentum-dependent terms by varying the Skyrme parameters \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) (or \(t_1\) and \(t_2\)) while keeping other parameters fixed for a modified SKM(\(m^*=m\)) interaction, and also considered the SLy4 parameter set. The mechanical and chemical instability boundaries and the liquid-gas coexistence curves were determined from the conditions of equal pressure and equal chemical potentials between the liquid and gas phases.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.14509\) fm\(^{-3}\) for SKM(\(m^*=m\)) and \(\rho_0 = 0.15954\) fm\(^{-3}\) for SLy4.
- Symmetry energy \(S_V = 18.6080\) MeV for SKM(\(m^*=m\)), 23.7451 MeV for the no-isovector variant, 24.6730 MeV for the momentum-independent variant, and 32.0038 MeV for SLy4.
- Compressibility \(\kappa = 367.556\) MeV for SKM(\(m^*=m\)), 312.281 MeV for the no-isovector variant, and 229.901 MeV for SLy4.
- Effective mass \(m^*/m = 0.999987\) for SKM(\(m^*=m\)), 0.894430 for the no-isovector variant, and 0.694658 for SLy4.
- Binding energy per nucleon \(E_B/A = 15.8173\) MeV for SKM(\(m^*=m\)) and 15.9722 MeV for SLy4.
- At \(T = 10\) MeV and \(y = 0\), the spinodal instability region occurs in the density range approximately \(1/5 < \rho/\rho_0 < 1/3\) and around \(P = 0.08\) MeV/fm\(^3\) (0.0755 to 0.0834 MeV/fm\(^3\)).
- For \(P = 0.08\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and \(y = 0\), a gap appears in the density range \(0.0401 < \rho < 0.0598\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lee and Mekjian (2002, 2004, 2008) that considered only isoscalar momentum-dependent terms alongside volume, surface, symmetry, and Coulomb effects in heated nuclei. The inclusion of the isovector momentum-dependent term and its associated effective mass splitting between neutrons and protons is important for understanding symmetry energy effects at finite temperature. The results enable more detailed comparisons with other Skyrme interactions and with relativistic mean field models such as those by Serot (1992), and are relevant for neutron star studies and isospin fractionation phenomena in heavy ion collisions.
