# Magnetic susceptibility and magnetization properties of asymmetric nuclear matter under a strong magnetic field

> We study the effect of a strong magnetic field on the proton and neutron spin polarization and magnetic susceptibility of asymmetric nuclear matter within a relativistic mean-field approach. It is shown that magnetic fields $B \sim 10^{16} - 10^{17}$ G have already noticeable effects on the range of densities of interest for the study of the crust of a neutron star. Although the proton susceptibility is larger for weaker fields, the neutron susceptibility becomes of the same order or even larger for small proton fractions and subsaturation densities for $B > 10^{16}$ G. We expect that neutron superfluidity in the crust will be affected by the presence of magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for magnetic fields $B > 10^{16}$ G, the neutron magnetic susceptibility can become comparable to or larger than the proton susceptibility at subsaturation densities and small proton fractions, even though the proton susceptibility dominates at weaker fields. This effect is expected to impact neutron superfluidity in the crust, but the authors note that fields as high as $10^{19}$ G are included for completeness despite no evidence they exist in the crust.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach using the FSUGold parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model, where nucleons interact via exchange of $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons. They compute the spin polarization, magnetization, and differential magnetic susceptibility for both protons and neutrons in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, including the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of nucleons with $\kappa_n = -1.91315$ and $\kappa_p = 1.79285$. The study covers baryon densities up to three times saturation density and magnetic field intensities from $10^{15}$ G to $10^{19}$ G, for proton fractions $Y_p = 0.1$, $0.3$, $0.5$, and for $\beta$-equilibrium matter.

## Key numbers

- Neutron critical density for total polarization: at $B = 3 \times 10^{16}$ G, a polarization of 10% is expected at neutron density $\rho_n = 0.001$ fm$^{-3}$, and 50% polarization at $\rho_n = 0.0001$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For $B = 10^{19}$ G, pure neutron matter is bound up to approximately $1.5$ times saturation density.
- For $B \geq 10^{18}$ G, noticeable effects on the energy per particle appear at densities $\rho \leq 0.25 \rho_0$.
- The maximum magnetic field supported by a star before black hole formation is estimated as $B \le 8 \times 10^{18} (1.4 M_\odot / M)$ G, with stable configurations found for $B \le 3 \times 10^{18}$ G using a hyperonic equation of state.
- For $B = 10^{17}$ G, neutrons are totally polarized only at very small densities; above neutron density $0.02 \rho_0$, the partial polarization of neutrons is below 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetization in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter by Kha (2002), Perez-Garcia (2008, 2011), Aguirre (2011, 2013), and Abh (2014), and generalizes the work of Broderick et al. (2000) and Dong et al. (2013) by including a detailed comparison of proton and neutron susceptibilities across a range of proton fractions and magnetic field strengths. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star crust properties, particularly the inner crust where a pasta phase is expected, and inform studies of neutron superfluidity under strong magnetic fields.
