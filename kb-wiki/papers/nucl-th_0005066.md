# Transition Strength Sums and Quantum Chaos in Shell Model States

> For the embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (EGOE) of random matrices, the strength sums generated by a transition operator acting on an eigenstate vary with the excitation energy as the ratio of two Gaussians. This general result is compared to exact shell model calculations, with realistic interactions, of spherical orbit occupancies and Gamow-Teller strength sums in some $(ds)$ and $(fp)$ shell examples. In order to confirm that EGOE operates in the chaotic domain of the shell model spectrum, calculations are carried out using two different interpolating hamiltonians generating order-chaos transitions. Good agreement is obtained in the chaotic domain of the spectrum, and strong deviations are observed as nuclear motion approaches a regular regime (transition strength sums appear to follow the Dyson's $Δ_3$ statistic). More importantly, they shed new light on the newly emerging understanding that in the chaotic domain of isolated finite interacting many particle systems smoothed densities (they include strength functions) define the statistical description of these systems and these densities follow from embedded random matrix ensembles; some EGOE calculations to this end are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for shell-model states in the chaotic domain, the smoothed transition strength sums (expectation values of an operator as a function of excitation energy) follow the ratio-of-Gaussians form predicted by the embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (EGOE), but this agreement breaks down as the nuclear motion approaches a regular regime, where the strength sums instead appear to follow Dyson's Δ₃ statistic.

## Method

The authors performed exact shell-model calculations of spherical orbit occupancies in a 307-dimensional (2s1d)⁶ space with J=2, T=0 using Kuo's interaction, and Gamow-Teller (GT) strength sums in an 814-dimensional (1f2p)⁶ space with J=0, T=0 using the KB3 interaction. To study order-chaos transitions, they constructed two interpolating Hamiltonians: a mean-field (MF) Hamiltonian H(λ) = h(1) + λ V(2) and an SU(4)-ST scalar Hamiltonian H(λ) = H_SU(4)-ST + λ (H_SM - H_SU(4)-ST), varying λ from 0 (regular) to 1 (chaotic). The EGOE prediction for the smoothed strength sum ⟨K⟩ᴱ was computed as the ratio of a Gaussian density for the operator K to a Gaussian density for the Hamiltonian, using the centroids and widths of both densities.

## Key numbers

* In the (ds)-shell example, the d₅/₂ occupancy density has centroid ε_K = -35.08 MeV, width σ_K = 9.63 MeV, skewness γ₁:K = 0.08, and excess γ₂:K = -0.09; the d₃/₂ density has ε_K = -29.50 MeV, σ_K = 10.24 MeV, γ₁:K = -0.08, and γ₂:K = -0.17; the state density has ε = -32.78 MeV, σ = 10.24 MeV, γ₁ = 0.05, and γ₂ = -0.18.
* In the (fp)-shell GT example, the K-density has ε_K = 11.12 MeV, σ_K = 8.65 MeV, γ₁:K = 0.09, and γ₂:K = -0.18; the state density has ε = 9.51 MeV, σ = 8.62 MeV, γ₁ = 0.10, and γ₂ = -0.19.
* The scaled centroid shifts Δ̂_K = (ε_K - ε)/σ are -0.225 for d₅/₂, 0.32 for d₃/₂, and 0.187 for the (fp)-shell GT example.
* The value of Ê = (E-ε)/σ for the ground state is -2.6 for the (ds)-shell example and -3.1 for the (fp)-shell example.
* For the λ=1 case in the SU(4) interpolating calculation, the state density parameters are (ε = -52.59 MeV, σ = 13.15 MeV, γ₁ = 0.10, γ₂ = 0.03); the d₅/₂ density parameters are (-55.30 MeV, 12.31 MeV, 0.01, -0.06); the d₃/₂ density parameters are (-48.70 MeV, 13.42 MeV, 0.07, 0.05); the GT density parameters are (-48.37 MeV, 12.64 MeV, 0.06, -0.1).

## Context

This work extends the EGOE-based statistical spectroscopy developed by French and collaborators (1975, 1988) and Kota and collaborators (1989, 1995) to transition strength sums, providing a test of the chaotic domain hypothesis. It builds on prior studies of level fluctuations and strength functions in shell-model spaces, and it enables a statistical description of finite interacting many-particle systems (nuclei, atoms, molecules, quantum dots) in the chaotic regime, where smoothed densities replace detailed microscopic calculations. The results suggest that thermodynamic concepts (effective temperatures, chemical potentials) may be introduced in the chaotic domain, as argued by Flambaum (1994, 1997) and others.
