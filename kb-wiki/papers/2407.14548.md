# Triply charm and bottom tetraquarks in a constituent quark model

> Singly, doubly and fully charmed tetraquark candidates, \emph{e.g.}, $T_{c\bar{s}}(2900)$, $T^+_{cc}(3875)$ and $X(6900)$ have been recently reported by the LHCb collaboration. Therefore, it is timely to implement a theoretical investigation on triply heavy tetraquark systems; herein, the S-wave triply charm and bottom tetraquarks, $\bar{Q}Q\bar{q}Q$ $(q=u,\,d,\,s;\,Q=c,\,b)$, with spin-parity $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$, isospin $I=0$ and $\frac{1}{2}$, are systematically studied in a constituent quark model. Besides, all tetraquark configurations, \emph{i.e.} meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type arrangements, along with any allowed color structure, are comprehensively considered. The Gaussian expansion method (GEM), in combination with the complex-scaling method (CSM), which is quite ingenious in dealing with either bound or resonances, is the approach adopted in solving the complex scaled Schrödinger equation. This theoretical framework has already been applied in various tetra- and penta-quark systems. In a fully coupled-channel calculation within the GEM$+$CSM, narrow resonances are found in each $I(J^P)$ channel of the charm and bottom sector. In particular, triply charm and bottom tetraquark resonances are obtained in $5.6-5.9$ GeV and $15.3-15.7$ GeV, respectively. We provide also some insights of the compositeness of these exotic states, such as the inner quark distance, magnetic moment and dominant wave function component. All this may help to distinguish them in future high energy nuclear and particle experiments.

## Key claim
The paper claims that narrow triply heavy tetraquark resonances exist in all studied isospin and spin-parity channels of the $\bar{Q}Q\bar{q}Q$ system, but only within a constituent quark model that includes all possible tetraquark configurations (meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type) and uses the complex-scaling method; no bound states are found, only resonances above threshold.

## Method
The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation for the four-body system using a QCD-inspired constituent quark model with confinement and one-gluon exchange potentials. They employed the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) combined with the complex-scaling method (CSM) to treat bound and resonant states simultaneously, considering all allowed color structures and three types of spatial configurations (meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and five K-type arrangements) in a fully coupled-channel calculation.

## Key numbers
- Triply charm tetraquark resonances are obtained in the mass range 5.6–5.9 GeV.
- Triply bottom tetraquark resonances are obtained in the mass range 15.3–15.7 GeV.
- For the $\bar{c}c\bar{d}c$ system with $I(J^P)=\frac{1}{2}(0^+)$, a resonance pole is found at $5592-i2.5$ MeV (mass 5592 MeV, width 5 MeV from $\Gamma=-2\,\text{Im}(E)$).
- The constituent quark masses used are: $m_q=313$ MeV (u,d), $m_s=555$ MeV, $m_c=1752$ MeV, $m_b=5100$ MeV.
- The confinement parameters are $a_c=430$ MeV, $\mu_c=0.70$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Delta=181.10$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on singly, doubly, and fully heavy tetraquarks (e.g., $T_{c\bar{s}}(2900)$, $T^+_{cc}(3875)$, $X(6900)$ reported by LHCb) to the triply heavy sector, where no experimental data yet exist. Prior theoretical studies using the MIT bag model (Zhu 2023), effective field theory (Liu 2019), the extended chromomagnetic model (Weng 2021), the relativized quark model (Lu 2021), and QCD sum rules (Jiang 2017) found no bound states in $QQ\bar{Q}\bar{q}$ systems, while a chiral quark model (Liu 2022) found some bound states in $cc\bar{c}\bar{q}$. The present work uses the same constituent quark model framework previously applied by the authors to hidden-, single-, double-, and fully-heavy tetraquarks (Yang 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023) and pentaquarks (Yang 2015, 2018, 2022, 2023), providing predictions for resonance masses, widths, magnetic moments, and internal quark distances to guide future experimental searches.
