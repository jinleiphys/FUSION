# Scalar field and QCD constraints in Nuclear Physics

> Relativistic theories of nuclear matter are discussed in a new pespective. First the chiral character of the scalar nuclear field is introduced in the framework of the linear sigma model. With the assumption that the nucleon mass originates in part from the coupling to the quark condensate it is possible to relate the optical potential for the propagation of the scalar field to the QCD scalar susceptibility of the nucleon, on which indications exist from the lattice evolution of the nucleon mass with the quark mass. Constraining the parameters of the nuclear scalar potential by the lattice expansion parameters a successful description of the nuclear saturation properties can be reached.

Based on the abstract and the available full text (which is truncated and contains only LaTeX preamble and the beginning of the paper), here is the wiki page body.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the parameters of the nuclear scalar potential in relativistic models can be constrained by lattice QCD data on the evolution of the nucleon mass with the quark mass, specifically through the QCD scalar susceptibility of the nucleon. This link is conditional on identifying the nuclear scalar field with the chiral-invariant radial mode of the linear sigma model, rather than the chiral-non-invariant sigma field itself.

## Method
The authors start from the linear sigma model and transform to polar coordinates (radial mode s and angular pion field) to satisfy chiral constraints. They then relate the in-medium scalar field to the nucleon mass evolution and the quark condensate. The QCD scalar susceptibility of the nuclear medium is expressed in the model via the in-medium sigma propagator, and its linear term in density is identified with the scalar susceptibility of a free nucleon, which can be constrained by lattice QCD.

## Key numbers
* The nucleon sigma commutator, Sigma_N, is used to relate the condensate evolution to the scalar density, giving a mean scalar field value of approximately 30 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
* The in-medium sigma mass, m*_sigma, is lowered by the tadpole term according to m*^2_sigma = m^2_sigma - (3 g_S / f_pi) rho_S^N, where g_S is the sigma-nucleon coupling constant and rho_S^N is the scalar density.
* The scalar susceptibility of a free nucleon, chi^N_S, is derived as chi^N_S = 2 * (langle q q>_vac)^2 / (f_pi^2 * m_sigma^4) * (-3 g_S / f_pi).

## Context
This work extends relativistic nuclear matter models, such as the Walecka-Serot model (1986) and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988), by introducing a direct connection to QCD. It addresses the failure of the simple linear sigma model to produce nuclear saturation, a problem noted by Kerman and Miller (1974), by incorporating a nucleonic scalar polarizability. The approach enables a description of nuclear saturation properties that is constrained by lattice QCD data on the nucleon mass evolution with quark mass.
