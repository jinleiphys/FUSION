# Multicomponent Fermi systems at low densities

> We calculate, to second order in the scattering length between two fermions, the Landau quasiparticle interaction for a low-density mixture of two fermion species with unequal densities at temperature zero. From the Landau parameters we evaluate the energy density and find agreement with the result of Kanno, Prog. Theor. Phys. 44, 813 (1970). The calculations are then extended to the case of two fermion components with different total densities, each with two spin components, a situation of interest in nuclear physics and astrophysics, where the species are neutrons and protons. An interesting finding is that, for low proton concentrations, $x \ll 1$, the leading term in the energy density, beyond the $x^{5/3}$ contribution from the kinetic energy and the $x^2$ one due to the two-body interaction in the mean-field approximation, varies as $x^{7/3} \ln x$. This is to be contrasted with the higher powers of $x$ implicit in many phenomenological energy-density functionals employed in nuclear physics, such as those of the Skyrme type.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the Landau quasiparticle interaction to second order in the scattering length for a low-density mixture of two fermion species with unequal densities, and find that for low proton concentrations (x << 1), the leading term beyond kinetic energy and mean-field two-body interaction in the energy density varies as x^{7/3} ln x, which differs from the higher powers of x implicit in phenomenological Skyrme-type energy-density functionals.

## Method

The authors first calculate the Landau quasiparticle interaction for a binary fermion mixture to second order in the scattering length using the static Lindhard function formalism, then derive the Landau parameters (f_0 and f_1) from angular moments of the interaction. From these parameters, they compute the sound velocity and integrate twice with respect to density to obtain the energy density, extending the approach of Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1961) to the case of unequal Fermi momenta. The calculations are generalized to neutron-proton mixtures with four spin-isospin states using the appropriate effective interactions for nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers

- The second-order contribution to the energy density for equal Fermi momenta (p_a = p_b = p_F) is E^{(2)} = (m U_0^2 p_F^7)/(840 pi^6) (11 - 2 ln 2)
- For low proton concentrations, the expansion of Phi(p_n, p_p) yields a leading term proportional to y^7 ln(1/y) where y = p_p/p_n, corresponding to an n_p^{7/3} ln n_p dependence in the energy density
- The quadratic approximation underestimates the difference between energy densities for x=0 and x=0.5 by approximately 13%
- The effective mass for the unpolarized spin-1/2 system (p_a = p_b) is m*/m = 1 + (8/(15 pi^2)) (7 ln 2 - 1) (k_F a)^2

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Kanno (1970) for two-component Fermi systems with unequal densities by providing a detailed derivation via Landau parameters, which had not previously been evaluated. The results contradict the implicit assumptions in phenomenological Skyrme-type energy-density functionals (Stone and Reinhard, 2007), which predict higher powers of proton concentration x rather than the x^{7/3} ln x behavior found here. The work enables improved descriptions of neutron-rich matter in neutron stars, where proton fractions are typically around 5% at nuclear saturation density, and can be extended to study induced interactions on superfluid gaps in multicomponent fermion mixtures following the approach of Martikainen et al. (2009).
