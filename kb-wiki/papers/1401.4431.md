# Quark structure of the $X(3872)$ and $χ_b(3P)$ resonances

> We discuss the nature of the $χ_b(3P)$ and $X(3872)$ mesons: Are the $χ_b(3P)$'s standard $b \bar b$ mesons or $b \bar b$ states with a significative continuum component? Is the $X(3872)$ a $c \bar c$ state with continuum coupling effects or a meson-meson molecule? To do that, we compare quark model and unquenched quark model results for the mass barycenter and splittings of the $χ_b(3P)$ multiplet. Future and more precise experimental results will discriminate between the two interpretations. In the case of the $X(3872)$, we interpret it a $c \bar c$ core plus higher Fock components due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum, thus we think that it is compatible with the meson $χ_{c1}(2P)$, with $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$. The $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ quantum numbers are in agreement with the experimental results found by the LHCb collaboration. In our view, the $X(3872)$'s mass is lower than the quark model's predictions because of self energy shifts. We also provide an estimation of the open charm/bottom strong decay modes of the $X(3872)$ and $χ_b(3P)$ mesons, such as $X(3872) \rightarrow D \bar D^*$ and $χ_{b2}(3P) \rightarrow B \bar B$, and radiative transitions.

# Quark structure of the $X(3872)$ and $\chi_b(3P)$ resonances

## Key claim

The $X(3872)$ is compatible with the $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium state ($J^{PC}=1^{++}$) but includes higher Fock components from coupling to the meson-meson continuum, which produces a downward self-energy shift that lowers its mass below standard quark model predictions; the $\chi_b(3P)$ multiplet may similarly contain significant continuum components due to proximity to open-bottom thresholds, and only more precise experimental data on masses and splittings will distinguish between a standard $b\bar b$ interpretation and one with strong threshold effects.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical frameworks for charmonium and bottomonium: a standard relativized quark model (Godfrey-Isgur) where the Fock space is limited to $q\bar q$, and an unquenched quark model that adds self-energy corrections from coupling to the meson-meson continuum via a $^3P_0$ pair-creation mechanism. They compute mass spectra, mass splittings, open-flavor strong decay widths using the $^3P_0$ model, and $E1$ radiative transition rates using relativized quark model wave functions, then compare these results to experimental data and to predictions from molecular models.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $\chi_{c1}(2^3P_1)$ state (the $X(3872)$ candidate) calculated in the unquenched model: 3.908 GeV
- Mass barycenter of the $\chi_b(3P)$ multiplet from ATLAS: $10.530 \pm 0.005 (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.009 (\text{syst.})$ GeV
- Mass barycenter of the $\chi_b(3P)$ multiplet from D0: $10.551 \pm 0.014 (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.017 (\text{syst.})$ GeV
- Unquenched quark model prediction for $\chi_b(3P)$ mass barycenter: 10551 MeV (10.551 GeV)
- Relativized quark model prediction for $\chi_b(3P)$ mass barycenter: 10510 MeV (10.510 GeV)
- Unquenched model mass splittings for $\chi_b(3P)$: $\Delta M_{21}(3P) = -2$ MeV, $\Delta M_{10}(3P) = 85$ MeV
- Relativized quark model mass splittings for $\chi_b(3P)$: $\Delta M_{21}(3P) = 17$ MeV, $\Delta M_{10}(3P) = 13$ MeV
- Calculated $X(3872) \rightarrow D^0 \bar D^{0*}$ decay width: 2.5 MeV (for $M_X = 3871.85$ MeV) and 8.3 MeV (for $M_X = 3871.95$ MeV)
- Experimental $X(3872) \rightarrow D^0 \bar D^{0*}$ decay width: $3.9^{+2.8+0.2}_{-1.4-1.1}$ MeV and $3.0^{+1.9}_{-1.4}\pm0.9$ MeV
- Calculated $X(3872) \rightarrow J/\Psi \gamma$ radiative width (charmonium picture): 11 KeV; molecular model prediction: 8 KeV; experimental estimate: approximately 7 KeV
- Calculated $X(3872) \rightarrow \Psi(2S) \gamma$ radiative width (charmonium picture): 70 KeV; molecular model prediction: 0.03 KeV; experimental estimate: approximately 36 KeV
- Unquenched model prediction for $\chi_{b2}(3P)$ open-bottom strong decay width into $B\bar B$: 13 MeV
- Estimated intrinsic error of quark model calculations: 30-40 MeV (for bottomonium) and 30-50 MeV (for charmonium)

## Context

This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously applied to charmonium by the same group (Ferretti, Santopinto, and collaborators, 2013) to the bottomonium sector, specifically the $\chi_b(3P)$ multiplet discovered by ATLAS (2012) and D0 (2012). It directly addresses the ongoing debate about whether the $X(3872)$ is a $c\bar c$ charmonium state (Suzuki 2005, Meng 2007, Pennington 2007) or a $D\bar D^*$ molecule (Hanhart 2007, Baru 2011, Swanson 2003). The authors compare their results to molecular model calculations by Swanson (2003) for radiative transitions and to potential model calculations by Dib (2012) for $\chi_b(3P)$ masses. The work enables future experimental discrimination between standard quark model and unquenched interpretations for both the $X(3872)$ and $\chi_b(3P)$ states, particularly through measurements of mass splittings and the presence or absence of open-bottom strong decays.
