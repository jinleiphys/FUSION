# Spin dynamics in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with rigorous angular momentum conservation

> We have revisited the spin dynamics in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions based on the improved spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, particularly with the constraint of rigorous angular momentum conservation incorporated. We have studied the spin polarization of free nucleons and tritons/$^3$He as well as the spin alignment of deuterons, and predicted the flow splittings for their different spin states. We have also demonstrated that the spin-dependent potential may enhance dissipations and thus have a non-negligible effect on the spin-averaged transverse flow at low collision energies. When rigorous angular momentum conservation in each spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon collision is incorporated, it affects the overall dynamics, the flow, and also the spin polarization, while the effects of the spin-orbit potential on the spin-related observables are still appreciable. The well-developed SIBUU model could be further extended to include hyperons or vector mesons, or used as a hadronic afterburner for spin-related studies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with more inelastic channels incorporated in the future.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that incorporating rigorous angular momentum conservation in each spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon collision within the SIBUU transport model weakens the global and local spin polarizations of free nucleons and light clusters compared to calculations without this constraint, while the spin-orbit potential still has appreciable effects on spin-related observables.

## Method

The authors extended the spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SIBUU) transport model by enforcing total angular momentum conservation in each nucleon-nucleon collision through a procedure that selects the post-collision relative momentum direction perpendicular to the required orbital angular momentum vector, and by applying a global momentum correction at each time step to conserve angular momentum during mean-field evolution. They simulated midcentral Au+Au collisions at 100 AMeV using a lattice Hamiltonian framework with Skyrme-type spin-orbit interactions and spin-dependent elastic scattering cross sections, then applied a spin-dependent coalescence model to study light cluster production.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 100 AMeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 8 fm (midcentral collisions)
- Spin-orbit coupling strength: default value 133 MeV fm^5
- Lattice parameters: N = 400 parallel events, l = 1 fm lattice spacing, n = 2 range parameter
- Momentum correction from angular momentum conservation in mean-field evolution: about a few keV/c per nucleon
- Violations of total energy and total momentum after correction: of order 0.0001%
- Wigner function width parameters: sigma_d = 2.26 fm for deuterons, sigma_t = 1.59 fm for tritons, sigma_^3He = 1.76 fm for ^3He

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SIBUU transport model studies (Xu 2012, Xia 2014, Xia 2019) by incorporating rigorous angular momentum conservation, which was first investigated for nucleon-nucleon collisions in transport simulations by Gale (1990) and recently revisited by Liu (2023). The results show that angular momentum conservation reduces the spin polarization magnitudes compared to calculations without this constraint, while the spin-orbit potential still produces observable effects on spin-averaged transverse flow at low collision energies. The improved model can be extended to include hyperons or vector mesons for spin-related studies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
