# Strangelets formation in high energy heavy-ion collisions

> The properties of phase diagram of strange quark matter in equilibrium with hadronic matter at finite temperature are studied, where the quark phase and hadron phase are treated by baryon density-dependent quark mass model and hadron resonance gas model with hard core repulsion factor, respectively. The thermodynamic conditions for the formation of metastable strange quark droplets ("strangelets") in relativistic nuclear collisions are discussed. We obtained a rich structure of the phase diagram at finite temperature, and study the dynamical trajectories of an expanding strange fireball. Our results indicate that the strangeness fraction fs, perturbation parameter C, and confinement parameter D have strong influence on the properties of phase diagram and the formation of strangelets. Consider the isentropic expansion process, we found that the initial entropy per baryon is less than or equal to 5, which gives a large probability for the formation of strangelets. Furthermore, a sufficiently large strangeness fraction fs and one-gluon-exchange interaction and sufficiently small confinement interaction create possibilities for the formation of strangelets. On the contrary, the fireball will always complete the hadronization process when fs=0 or C>=0 or D^{1/2}>=170 MeV.

## Key claim

The formation of metastable strange quark droplets (strangelets) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is possible only when the expanding fireball has a low initial entropy per baryon (less than or equal to 5) and a sufficiently large strangeness fraction, combined with a strong one-gluon-exchange interaction and a weak confinement interaction; conversely, the fireball always completes hadronization when the strangeness fraction is zero, the perturbation parameter C is zero or positive, or the confinement parameter D^{1/2} is 170 MeV or larger.

## Method

The authors model the quark phase using a baryon density-dependent quark mass model that includes confinement and first-order perturbative interactions, with gluon masses fitted to lattice QCD pressure data. The hadronic phase is described by a hadron resonance gas model with a hard-core repulsion factor (Hagedorn correction). Phase equilibrium is determined by Gibbs conditions (equal temperature, pressure, and chemical potentials) with conserved baryon number and strangeness fraction, and isentropic expansion trajectories are computed for fixed entropy per baryon.

## Key numbers

- Initial entropy per baryon S/n_b less than or equal to 5 gives a large probability for strangelet formation; at S/n_b = 10 the fireball always completes hadronization.
- The fireball always completes hadronization when D^{1/2} is greater than or equal to 170 MeV.
- The confinement parameter D and perturbation parameter C are varied; C less than 0 represents one-gluon-exchange interaction, while C greater than or equal to 0 leads to complete hadronization.
- The temperature scale parameter Lambda_T is 280 MeV.
- Current quark masses used: m_u0 = 5 MeV, m_d0 = 5 MeV, m_s0 = 120 MeV.
- Bag constant B^{1/4} = 180 MeV for the Hagedorn correction.
- Strangeness fraction f_s is studied in the range 0 to 3, with specific results shown for f_s = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangelet formation using the MIT bag model by Lee and Heinz (1993) and He et al. (1996), and the quark mass density-dependent model by Wen et al. (2007). The baryon density-dependent quark mass model predicts a similar isentropic expansion trajectory for strangelet formation but shows a narrower mixed-phase region at high temperature and low density, with a more significant reheating effect than the bag model. The results provide thermodynamic conditions for future experimental searches for strangelets at facilities such as RHIC and the LHC.
