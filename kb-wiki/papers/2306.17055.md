# Cross section for supernova axion observation in neutrino water Cherenkov detectors

> Axions coupled to nucleons might be copiously emitted from core-collapse supernovae (SNe). If the axion-nucleon coupling is strong enough, axions would be emitted from the SN as a burst and, reaching Earth, may excite the oxygen nuclei in water Cherenkov detectors (${}^{16}{\rm O} + a \to {}^{16}{\rm O}^{*}$). This process will be followed by decay(s) of the excited state resulting in an emission of photons and thus providing a possibility for a direct detection of axions from a Galactic SN in large underground neutrino Cherenkov detectors. Motivated by this possibility, we present an updated calculation of axion-oxygen cross section obtained by using self-consistent continuum Random Phase Approximation. We calculate the branching ratio of the oxygen nucleus de-excitation into gamma-rays, neutrons, protons and $α$-particles and also consider photon emission from secondary nuclei to compute a total $γ$ spectrum created when axions excite ${}^{16}{\rm O}$. These results are used to revisit the detectability of axions from SN 1987A in Kamiokande-II.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated calculation of the axion-oxygen cross section for supernova axion detection in water Cherenkov detectors, using self-consistent continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) with finite-range Gogny interactions, and finds that the detectability of axions from SN 1987A in Kamiokande-II depends on the specific nuclear model used, with theoretical uncertainties arising from different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors used self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations to describe the nuclear ground state of 16O and continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) to describe excited states, employing the same finite-range Gogny-type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (D1S, D1M, and D1MT parametrizations) for both ground and excited states. They calculated the axion-nucleus cross section from the axion-nucleon Lagrangian with KSVZ-like couplings (Cp = -0.47, Cn = 0.0), performed a multipole expansion of the transition operator, and computed branching ratios for de-excitation of 16O into gamma-rays, neutrons, protons, and alpha-particles, including photon emission from secondary nuclei to obtain a total gamma spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Axion-nucleon coupling constant fixed at g_aN = 2.0 x 10^-9 for cross section calculations
- Binding energies per nucleon for 16O: 7.976 MeV (D1S), 8.112 MeV (D1M), 7.975 MeV (D1MT), compared to experimental value of 8.025 MeV
- RMS charge radii for 16O: 2.699 fm (D1S), 2.793 fm (D1M), 2.760 fm (D1MT), compared to experimental value of 2.759 fm
- First 0- state energies: IS state at 10.52 MeV and IV state at 14.78 MeV for D1S interaction
- 2- excitation states identified at 3.77 MeV and 12.05 MeV for D1S interaction
- Cross section calculated for axion energies up to 200 MeV, with contributions from all unnatural parity multipoles from 0- up to 5+

## Context

This work extends the seminal proposal by Engel et al. (1990) for detecting axions from supernovae via oxygen excitation in water Cherenkov detectors, using more refined nuclear models than previously available. The self-consistent CRPA approach with finite-range interactions, previously applied to electron scattering quasi-elastic responses and neutrino-nucleus scattering by Donati et al. (2011, 2014, 2016), allows the authors to evaluate theoretical uncertainties by comparing results from three different Gogny force parametrizations (D1S, D1M, D1MT). The results enable a revised estimate of the expected axion event rate in Kamiokande-II under the assumption of a supernova similar to SN 1987A, updating the constraints on the axion-nucleon coupling g_aN.
