# Ground state energy fluctuations in nuclear matter II

> Improvements are performed on a recently proposed statistical theory of the mean field of a many-fermion system. The dependence of the predictions of the theory upon its two basic ingredients, namely the Hartree-Fock energy and the average energy of the two particle-two hole excitations, is explored.

# Ground state energy fluctuations in nuclear matter II

## Key claim
The statistical theory of the nuclear mean field yields consistent results when the average excitation energy of 2p-2h states is known, making the approach essentially parameter-free; however, the theory's predictions depend critically on the choice of the Hartree-Fock energy and the average 2p-2h energy, and the spectroscopic factor remains unrealistically close to one due to the restricted P-space.

## Method
The authors improve upon a previously proposed statistical theory (Caracciolo et al., 1998) by replacing rough approximations with a more realistic treatment of 2p-2h state densities using Ericson's statistical formula. They solve two coupled nonlinear equations, one for the mean field energy and one for the energy fluctuation, to extract the residual effective interaction strength beta^2 and the mean field energy bar_E_0, imposing the constraint that the mean field must be identical whether derived from the upper or lower bound of the energy fluctuation band. The dependence on the two key inputs (Hartree-Fock energy and average 2p-2h excitation energy) is explored by varying these parameters and solving the self-consistency condition numerically.

## Key numbers
- Empirical nuclear matter energy: E_NM = [-16 + 39.5(k_F - 1.36)^2] MeV, with k_F in fm^{-1}
- For a simple two-body force, the HF energy minimum is -7.3 MeV at k_F = 1.78 fm^{-1}; at k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}, E_HF = -4.99 MeV
- Bare residual interaction strength: beta^2_bare = 5.2 x 10^4 MeV^2/nucleon
- For the improved HF force (minimum -13 MeV at k_F = 1.5 fm^{-1}), E_HF = -12.28 MeV at k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}
- For the standard HF case with fluctuation width W = 1 MeV and 2p-2h excitation energy E = 14 MeV: epsilon = 2.56 MeV, alpha = -0.78, bar_E_0 = -16.11 MeV, beta^2_l = 24.12 MeV^2/nucleon, beta^2_u = 35.25 MeV^2/nucleon
- For the improved HF case with W = 0.4 MeV and E = 14 MeV: epsilon = 3.99 MeV, alpha = -0.50, bar_E_0 = -16.01 MeV, beta^2_l = 14.19 MeV^2/nucleon, beta^2_u = 15.68 MeV^2/nucleon
- 2p-2h level density for A=208: rho^{(4)}_{2p-2h}(E, J=0) = 3.76 x 10^{-2} E^{9/4} exp(-0.0013/sqrt(E)) MeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the statistical theory of the nuclear mean field developed by Caracciolo, De Pace, Feshbach, and Molinari (1998) by removing severe approximations made in that earlier treatment, particularly regarding the treatment of the fluctuation equation. The improved formalism now incorporates Ericson's statistical theory of nuclear states (Ericson, 1960) for the density of 2p-2h excitations, replacing the earlier ad-hoc treatment. The results confirm the earlier finding of dramatic quenching of the residual interaction relative to the bare interaction, but now on firmer theoretical ground. The approach enables future work to enlarge the P-space beyond the simple Hartree-Fock ground state to include more complex configurations, which should yield more realistic spectroscopic factors (currently S ~ 0.7 from other work, versus the values near 1 obtained here).
