# The multiple-scattering series in pion-deuteron scattering and the nucleon-nucleon potential: perspectives from effective field theory

> Important contributions to meson-nucleus scattering are produced by terms in the multiple-scattering series, which is defined as the sum of all diagrams where the meson scatters back and forth between a pair of static nucleons before leaving the nucleus. In particular, the sum of this series is needed for an accurate description of kaon-deuteron scattering, and appears as part of the nucleon-nucleon potential. In this article we present some effective-field-theory (EFT)-based insights into this series in the case of two-nucleon systems. In particular, we discuss the fact that, if meson-nucleon scattering is approximated by the scattering-length term, individual terms of the series are divergent, and enhanced with respect to the straightforward expectation from chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT). This apparently indicates the presence of similarly enhanced counterterms. However, we show that when the series is resummed the divergences cancel, such that no additional information on short-range interactions is needed to obtain predictions for observables after resummation. We discuss the conditions under which this resummation is justified. We show that the same issues arise in the $NN$ potential, where the resummed series produces poles whose appearance indicates the breakdown scale of the $χ$PT expansion for that quantity. This demonstrates unequivocally that $χ$PT cannot be applied to compute $V(r)$ for distances smaller than $r \sim 1$ fm at least in the theory without explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. We briefly discuss whether this bound can be lowered if the Delta resonance is included in the EFT as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while individual terms in the multiple-scattering series (MSS) for pion-deuteron scattering are divergent and enhanced relative to naive chiral perturbation theory (χPT) expectations, apparently requiring similarly enhanced counterterms, the divergences cancel upon resummation, so that no additional short-range information is needed for predictions after resummation, provided the meson-nucleon scattering length is natural.

## Method

The authors evaluate the MSS perturbatively for a zero-momentum pion scattering from a two-nucleon system, treating nucleons as static and approximating the πN interaction by the scattering-length term. They then regularize each term in the series with UV and IR cutoffs, resum the full series analytically, and remove the regulator to obtain a finite closed-form expression in both coordinate and momentum space. The same analysis is extended to the contributions of the MSS to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential.

## Key numbers

- The isovector πN scattering length in χPT is given by a = M_π / (8π F_π^2).
- The third term in the MSS (triple scattering) yields A^(3)(r) = -a^3 / (4π r^2).
- The fourth term in the MSS (quadruple scattering) yields A^(4)(q) = -a^4 log(Q/μ) + f_0(μ)/(32π^2), where the logarithmic divergence is regularized at scale 1/a upon resummation.
- The resummed MSS in coordinate space is A(r) = -a r / [4π (r + a)].
- For the NN potential, the resummed MSS produces unphysical poles for distances r < 1 fm, indicating the breakdown scale of the χPT expansion for V(r) in the theory without explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom.
- For πd scattering within χPT, the effect of finite regulator Λ ~ M_ρ ~ 800 MeV on the resummed MSS contributes less than 3% to the pion-deuteron scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the multiple-scattering series for meson-deuteron scattering, which was first derived by Foldy (1949) and applied to πd scattering by Brückner (1953), and later studied in EFT by Weinberg and refined by Beane (1998), Liebig (2011), and others. The paper shows that the enhanced contributions of MSS terms, previously observed for triple scattering, persist for all higher orders but cancel upon resummation, resolving the apparent need for enhanced counterterms. The results enable controlled EFT treatments of kaon-deuteron scattering, where the KN scattering lengths are large (~1 fm), and clarify the limitations of χPT for the NN potential at short distances (r < 1 fm). The authors briefly discuss whether including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom could lower this bound.
