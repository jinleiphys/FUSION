# Comment on stability of atoms and nuclei in multidimensional TeV scale gravity

> We discuss the stability of atoms and nucleons in the presence of multidimensional gravity characterized by the unified energy scale 1TeV. We point out that the multidimensional gravitational attraction can create additional bound states deep inside of atomic and nucleon constituents. These states would be characterized by sizes comparable to the TeV scale distance. We show that shallow gravity induced bound states between an electron and a quark are ruled out, because collapse processes in atoms to such bound states would occur on time scales which are in contradiction to current data on the stability of ordinary matter. The gravitational attraction may also produce diquark states, which should be considered in addition to those discussed in the literature. The estimates indicate that, for TeV scale gravity, the problem of UV divergencies and UV renormalization is crucial. Some universal renormalization mechanism should exist, which stabilizes atoms in the presence of multidimensional gravity.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in multidimensional TeV-scale gravity, the singular short-distance behavior of the gravitational potential (scaling as \(1/r^{1+n}\) for \(n\) extra dimensions) would create additional bound states inside atoms and nucleons; shallow gravity-induced bound states for electrons are ruled out because collapse transitions from atomic orbitals would occur on time scales far shorter than the observed stability of ordinary matter, but deep bound states (with energies below \(-mc^2\)) could be stabilized by the Pauli principle.

## Method

The authors analyze the non-relativistic and relativistic quantum mechanics of a particle in the \((4+n)\)-dimensional gravitational potential \(\varphi_G(r) = -G M (R_n/r)^n / r\) (valid for \(r \ll R_n\)), regularized by a cut-off distance \(r_s\) below which the potential is made non-singular. They estimate the critical distance \(r_c\) where the gravitational potential energy equals the kinetic energy \(\hbar c / r\), and compare this to the TeV-scale distance \(R_T = \hbar/(M_{4+n}c)\). They then evaluate the overlap of hypothetical shallow bound states with atomic wavefunctions and use experimental limits on K-shell electron disappearance in iodine to constrain the existence of such states.

## Key numbers

* For \(M_{4+n}c^2 = 1\) TeV, the size of compactified dimensions is \(R_2 \approx 10^{-1}\) cm (for \(n=2\)) and \(R_3 \approx 10^{-6}\) cm (for \(n=3\)).
* The TeV-scale distance is \(R_T = \hbar/(M_{4+n}c) \approx 2.0 \times 10^{-17}\) cm for \(M_{4+n}c^2 = 1\) TeV.
* For hydrogen (\(Mc^2 = 0.94\) GeV, \(mc^2 = 0.51\) MeV) at the Bohr radius, the ratio of gravitational to Coulomb potential is \(\varphi_G(r_B) m / \varphi_C(r_B) e \approx 10^{-7} \times 4^n 10^{-9n}\); at the proton surface (\(r_p \approx 10^{-13}\) cm), the ratio is \(\approx 10^{-7} \times 10^{-4n}\).
* The estimated size of the largest gravity-induced bound state orbit is \(r_c \approx 10^{-17 - 10/n}\) cm for \(M_{4+n}c^2 \sim 1\) TeV, \(Mc^2 = 0.94\) GeV, \(mc^2 = 0.51\) MeV.
* Experimental lower bounds on K-shell electron disappearance in iodine give a half-life \(> 10^{29} - 10^{31}\) s.
* Typical atomic transition rates are \(10^{15} - 10^{8}\) s\(^{-1}\); after reduction by the overlap factor \(\chi \sim 10^{-8}\) to \(10^{-2}\), the collapse rates would be \(\sim 1 - 10^7\) s\(^{-1}\), far exceeding the inverse age of the Universe (\(\sim 10^{-18}\) s\(^{-1}\)).

## Context

This comment extends the analysis of Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, and Dvali (1998, 1999) on TeV-scale gravity with large extra dimensions, pointing out that the singular short-distance behavior of the multidimensional gravitational potential leads to a collapse problem for atoms and nucleons. The authors show that shallow bound states are excluded by stability data on ordinary matter (citing Aharonov et al. 1995 and Belli et al. 1999 for K-shell limits), and that a universal renormalization mechanism (e.g., from string theory with a lowered string scale as considered by Lykken 1996 and Antoniadis and Benakli 2000) is required to avoid instability. The work also notes that similar stability issues arise in Randall-Sundrum models (1999) but not in models with induced metric on the brane (Dvali and Gabadadze 2000).
