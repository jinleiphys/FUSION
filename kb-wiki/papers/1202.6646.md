# Fluctuating Glasma initial conditions and flow in heavy ion collisions

> We compute initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework by combining the impact parameter dependent saturation model (IP-Sat) with the classical Yang-Mills description of initial Glasma fields. In addition to fluctuations of nucleon positions, this IP-Glasma description includes quantum fluctuations of color charges on the length-scale determined by the inverse nuclear saturation scale Q_s. The model naturally produces initial energy fluctuations that are described by a negative binomial distribution. The ratio of triangularity to eccentricity is close to that in a model tuned to reproduce experimental flow data. We compare transverse momentum spectra and v_(2,3,4)(p_T) of pions from different models of initial conditions using relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma model, which combines the impact-parameter-dependent saturation model (IP-Sat) with classical Yang-Mills solutions, produces initial-state eccentricities and triangularities that are close to those of a tuned MC-Glauber model that reproduces experimental flow data, but does so without parameter tuning of the fluctuations themselves.

## Method

The authors sample nucleon positions from a Fermi distribution and determine the saturation scale \(Q_{s,p}^2(x,\mathbf{b}_\perp)\) for each nucleon using the IP-Sat dipole cross section constrained by HERA data. Color charge densities are then sampled from Gaussian distributions with variance proportional to \(g^2\mu_A^2(x,\mathbf{x}_\perp)\), and the classical Yang-Mills equations are solved on a transverse lattice to obtain the Glasma fields at \(\tau=0\). The resulting energy density distributions are used as initial conditions for relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution with the music code, using shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 0.08\).

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\,\mathrm{GeV}\), the event-by-event energy distribution is best fit by a negative binomial distribution (NBD).
* The MC-Glauber model with participant scaling uses a Gaussian width of \(0.4\,\mathrm{fm}\) for each nucleon's energy density.
* The IP-Glasma model's ratio of triangularity to eccentricity \(\varepsilon_3/\varepsilon_2\) is larger than that of the MC-KLN model and closer to that of the tuned MC-Glauber model.
* The hydrodynamic evolution uses \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and average maximal energy densities are normalized to assure similar final multiplicities across models.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework by incorporating quantum fluctuations of color charges on the scale \(1/Q_s\) into initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions, going beyond the MC-KLN model (Hirano 2005, Drescher 2006) which uses \(k_\perp\)-factorization and lacks such fluctuations. The IP-Glasma model is contrasted with MC-Glauber models (Esumi 2011, Qiu 2011) that can be tuned to reproduce elliptic and triangular flow data but lack a dynamical QCD mechanism for fluctuations. The results enable future discrimination between the Glasma flux tube scenario with granularity on the scale \(1/Q_s\) and other non-perturbative string scenarios sensitive to \(1/\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) (Wang 1991, Magas 2000).
