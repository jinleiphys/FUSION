# Beyond the wake: non-hydrodynamic response of an expanding Quark-gluon plasma

> We study the response function which describes the evolution of energy density induced by an initial disturbance for a Bjorken-expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We compare the results from solving linearized Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation with those from viscous hydrodynamics. While in long time limit the response becomes hydrodynamics, the non-hydrodynamic response is important at intermediate times when excitations of wavelength larger than the inverse of the relaxation time $1/τ_{R}$ are not fully damped. Therefore observables sensitive to the jet-induced medium excitations might be employed to explore the properties of QGP at the "mesoscopic scale".

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a Bjorken-expanding quark-gluon plasma, the response to an initial energy disturbance is well-described by hydrodynamics only in the long-time limit; at intermediate times (around Δτ = 4τ_R to 9τ_R), non-hydrodynamic "mesoscopic response" dominates for wavelengths comparable to or shorter than the relaxation time, meaning that jet-induced medium excitations could probe QGP properties at scales where neither fluid dynamics nor perturbative QCD is adequate.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation (with constant relaxation time τ_R) for a conformal QGP undergoing Bjorken expansion, computing the energy-energy response function G in Fourier space and then transforming to real space. They compared these kinetic theory results to those from first-order viscous hydrodynamics and from the free-streaming limit (collision integral neglected), focusing on the response at three snapshots Δτ = 1, 4, and 9 τ_R.

## Key numbers

- The response function G is evaluated at Δτ = 1, 4, and 9 τ_R, with the initial time τ_I = τ_R.
- The speed of sound used is c_s = 0.57, indicated by a dashed vertical line in the real-space plots.
- The background energy density decays as (τ/τ_I)^{-4/3} during Bjorken expansion, and G is normalized by this factor.
- The transition between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic response occurs at wave number kτ_R ≈ 1; for kτ_R ≤ 1, G agrees with hydrodynamics at Δτ = 4 τ_R and even more so at Δτ = 9 τ_R.
- The effective propagation speed v_eff approaches c_s = 0.57 at late time and approximately 1 (speed of light) at early time, with significant deviation at intermediate Δτ = 4 τ_R.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet-induced medium excitations in heavy-ion collisions, comparing kinetic theory (Boltzmann equation) to viscous hydrodynamics as in Hong et al. (2011). It enables future quantitative modeling of jet-medium interactions that incorporates non-hydrodynamic response, and the authors propose a modified Müller-Israel-Stewart-like model with two additional parameters to capture both hydrodynamic and mesoscopic response in a single framework.
