# Search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions provide an ideal environment to study the emergent phenomena in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is one of the most interesting, arising from the topological charge fluctuations of QCD vacua, immersed in a strong magnetic field. Since the first measurement nearly a decade ago of the possibly CME-induced charge correlation, extensive studies have been devoted to background contributions to those measurements. Many new ideas and techniques have been developed to reduce or eliminate the backgrounds. This article reviews these developments and the overall progress in the search for the CME.

## Key claim

This paper reviews the extensive experimental and theoretical efforts to search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, concluding that while charge-dependent correlations qualitatively consistent with CME expectations have been observed, these signals can be largely or entirely accounted for by background effects such as resonance decays coupled with elliptic flow, and no definitive observation of the CME has been achieved.

## Method

The paper reviews measurements of the gamma correlator (three-particle azimuthal correlation) and its charge-dependent difference Delta-gamma in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC across multiple beam energies. Various background subtraction techniques are described, including event-by-event selection on elliptic flow, event shape engineering using the reduced flow vector q2, isobaric collisions, small-system control experiments, and invariant mass dependent measurements to identify resonance decay backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- The measured mean a1 of both positive and negative charges is less than 5x10^-4 at 95% confidence level in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- In Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the extracted CME fraction f_CME from event shape engineering is 0.10 +/- 0.13 (MC-Glauber), 0.08 +/- 0.10 (MC-KLN CGC), and 0.08 +/- 0.11 (EKRT) for 10-50% centrality, corresponding to upper limits of 33%, 26%, and 29% at 95% confidence level
- The v2-independent fraction of the three-particle correlator in Pb+Pb collisions is less than 7% at 95% CL from CMS event shape engineering analysis
- The v2-independent fraction in p+Pb collisions is less than 13% at 95% CL from the same CMS analysis

## Context

This review extends the first CME-related gamma correlator measurements by STAR in 2009 (Abelev 2009) and subsequent beam energy scan results from STAR (Adamczyk 2014) and ALICE (Abelev 2012). It addresses the background interpretation challenge raised by calculations from Pratt (2010), Schlichting (2010), and Bzdak (2010) showing that local charge conservation and momentum conservation can account for the measured Delta-gamma signal. The review enables future CME searches using isobaric collisions (Ru+Ru versus Zr+Zr) proposed by Voloshin (2010) and new differential measurement techniques relative to reaction plane and participant plane proposed by Xu (2017).
