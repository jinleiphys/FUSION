# Anomaly-induced Chiral Mixing in Cold and Dense Matter

> We construct the spectral functions for light vector mesons at finite density and temperature in the presence of a novel mixing between parity partners, induced by baryon density via the Wess-Zumino-Witten action. As the main origin of in-medium broadening, a set of baryon resonances that strongly couple to the vector mesons and the modifications of kaons and anti-kaons due to the Kaplan-Nelson term are included. It is shown that the vector spectra, even with the broadening effects, exhibit sizable signatures of chiral symmetry restoration thanks to the chiral mixing depending on three-momenta carried by the vector mesons. Those spectral functions are used to calculate the integrated production rates of lepton pairs, and a proper binning in momenta and potential decrease in the vector-meson masses due to chiral symmetry restoration are discussed in quantifying the signatures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that signatures of chiral symmetry restoration via density-induced chiral mixing persist in vector meson spectral functions even after including realistic in-medium broadening from baryon resonances and modified kaons, but these signatures are largely washed out in the momentum-integrated dilepton production rates unless the vector meson masses themselves decrease by 20-30% near the chiral crossover.

## Method

The author constructs in-medium spectral functions for light vector mesons (ρ/ω and φ) at finite baryon density and low temperature (T = 50 MeV) by including a chiral mixing term derived from the Wess-Zumino-Witten action. For the ρ/ω sector, a set of six baryon resonances (Δ(1232), N(1520), Δ(1620), Δ(1700), N(1720), Δ(1905)) are incorporated via their coupling to the ρ meson, while for the φ sector, in-medium modifications of kaons and anti-kaons from the Kaplan-Nelson term are included. The spectral functions are then used to compute dilepton production rates integrated over three-momenta.

## Key numbers

- The chiral mixing parameter c is approximately 0.1 GeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ from the chiral approach, compared to about 1 GeV from the holographic model
- At the chiral crossover (ρ_B^crit/ρ₀ = 2.5) and T = 50 MeV, the mass difference between vector and axial-vector mesons is δm = 47 MeV and the mixing strength is c = 98 MeV
- The ω meson in-medium width at chiral restoration is assumed to be larger than its vacuum value by a factor of 7, reproducing about 60 MeV at ρ_B/ρ₀ = 2
- The three s-wave baryon resonances with negative parity are assumed to have their masses reduced by 25% near chiral symmetry restoration
- At the chiral crossover, the in-medium kaon mass is m_K* = 560 MeV and anti-kaon mass is m_Ḵ* = 230 MeV
- The effective φ meson width at chiral crossover is Γ_φ(s=m_φ²) = 61 MeV
- The dilepton production rates for ρ/ω show a much larger contribution below √s = 0.7 GeV when the vector meson mass is reduced to 550 MeV (CSR-1 scenario)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of vector spectra with density-induced chiral mixing (Sasaki, 2022) by including baryon resonances that strongly couple to vector mesons in nuclear matter, following the resonance set from Rapp, Urban, and Wambach (2000). The chiral mixing mechanism was first identified in holographic QCD (Domokos and Harvey, 2010) and reformulated in 4-dimensional chiral effective theory (Harada and Sasaki, 2016). The results enable quantitative predictions for dilepton measurements at future heavy-ion experiments at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, and suggest that careful binning of dilepton data in three-momenta above 0.5 GeV would be crucial to extract signals of restored chiral symmetry.
