# Extrapolation Method for the No-Core Shell Model

> Nuclear many-body calculations are computationally demanding. An estimate of their accuracy is often hampered by the limited amount of computational resources even on present-day supercomputers. We provide an extrapolation method based on perturbation theory, so that the binding energy of a large basis-space calculation can be estimated without diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in this space. The extrapolation method is tested for 3H and 6Li nuclei. It will extend our computational abilities significantly and allow for reliable error estimates.

# Extrapolation Method for the No-Core Shell Model

## Key claim
The authors propose that the binding energy of a no-core shell model (NCSM) calculation can be estimated by a linear extrapolation of the ground-state energy \(E_0\) versus the energy variance \(\Delta E^2\), motivated by second-order perturbation theory, provided the diagonal elements of the effective Hamiltonian are dominated by the kinetic energy and flatten at high oscillator quantum numbers. The method is tested only for the \(P\)-space truncated at \(N_m=20\) for \(^3\)H and \(N_m=12\) for \(^6\)Li, and does not account for three-nucleon forces.

## Method
The authors decompose the NCSM Hamiltonian matrix \(\mathbf{H}\) in a harmonic-oscillator basis into a block \(\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}\) for a small subspace \(\widetilde{P}\) (truncated at \(\widetilde{N}_m\)) and the remainder, then compute the ground-state energy \(E_0\) and variance \(\Delta E^2 = \psi_0^\mathrm{T}\mathbf{B}\mathbf{B}^\mathrm{T}\psi_0\) from the small-space solution. Using second-order perturbation theory and the observation that the diagonal elements of the effective Hamiltonian are roughly constant for high quantum numbers, they derive a linear relation \(E_0 \simeq \mathcal{E}_0 + \alpha^{-1}\Delta E^2\) and obtain the extrapolated binding energy \(\mathcal{E}_0\) by linear regression of \(E_0\) vs. \(\Delta E^2\) for several \(\widetilde{N}_m\) values, extrapolating to \(\Delta E^2 = 0\). The method is tested on \(^3\)H (Jacobi basis) and \(^6\)Li (Slater-determinant basis) using CD-Bonn and AV18 potentials.

## Key numbers
- For \(^3\)H with CD-Bonn potential and \(\hbar\Omega = 28\) MeV, the extrapolation from \(\widetilde{N}_m = 10\) to 18 yields \(\mathcal{E}_0 = -7.995\) MeV (the full \(N_m=20\) result), with an extrapolation error of 25 keV for \(\widetilde{N}_m=18\) and 15 keV for \(\widetilde{N}_m=16\).
- The constant \(\alpha\) for \(^3\)H with CD-Bonn at \(\hbar\Omega = 28\) MeV is 268 MeV.
- For \(^6\)Li with \(N_m=12\), the extrapolation errors are 290 keV (CD-Bonn) and 220 keV (AV18); the CD-Bonn potential provides 1.1 MeV more binding than AV18.
- The CPU time for the extrapolation at \(\widetilde{N}_m=14\) is a factor of 13 faster than the full \(N_m=20\) calculation; a perturbative calculation of comparable accuracy (Eq. (3PT)) is 8 times slower than the extrapolation.

## Context
This work extends the extrapolation idea of Mizusaki and Imada (2002, 2003) from traditional shell-model calculations to the no-core shell model, where the Hamiltonian structure (kinetic-energy dominance and flattening of diagonal elements at high \(N\)) justifies a linear \(E_0\) vs. \(\Delta E^2\) scaling rather than the quadratic forms used in the traditional shell model. The method enables reliable error estimates for NCSM results and can estimate uncertainties due to \(\hbar\Omega\) dependence and interaction choice, as demonstrated for \(^3\)H and \(^6\)Li. Future work should extend the method to larger model spaces and include three-nucleon forces.
