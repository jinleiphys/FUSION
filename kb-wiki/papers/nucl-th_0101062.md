# Topological susceptibility at zero and finite temperature in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We consider the three flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the 't Hooft interaction incorporating the U(1)_A anomaly. In order to set the coupling strength of the 't Hooft term, we employ the topological susceptibility $χ$ instead of the eta' meson mass. The value for $χ$ is taken from lattice simulations. We also calculate $χ$ at finite temperature within the model. Comparing it with the lattice data, we extract information about the behavior of the U(1)_A anomaly at finite temperature. We conclude that within the present framework, the effective restoration of the U(1)_A symmetry does not necessarily take place even at high temperature where the chiral symmetry is restored.

## Key claim

Within the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the 't Hooft interaction, the effective restoration of the U(1)_A symmetry does not necessarily coincide with chiral symmetry restoration; the lattice data for topological susceptibility up to 1.4 T_c can be reproduced with a constant six-point coupling constant K, implying that the U(1)_A symmetry is not restored at least up to that temperature.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the topological susceptibility χ in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by identifying the topological charge density Q(x) with the imaginary part of the determinant of the quark bilinear field Φ, using the 't Hooft interaction term. They computed χ at leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, summing ring diagrams that correspond to η_8 and η_0 meson propagations at zero momentum. At zero temperature, they fixed the model parameters using χ = (175 ± 5 MeV)^4 from lattice simulations instead of the η' meson mass, then calculated χ at finite temperature and compared with lattice data to extract the temperature dependence of the six-point coupling constant K.

## Key numbers

- The topological susceptibility from lattice simulations used as input: χ = (175 ± 5 MeV)^4
- With parameters determined using m_η' as input (from Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994)), the NJL model yields χ_NJL = (166 MeV)^4, giving χ_NJL / χ_Lat = 0.80
- The Witten-Veneziano mass formula ratio within the NJL model with those parameters: (2 N_f χ) / (f_π^2 (m_η^2 + m_η'^2 - 2 m_K^2)) = 0.81
- Parameters determined using χ = (175 MeV)^4: m_s = 135.7 MeV, Λ = 631.4 MeV, G Λ^2 = 1.765, K Λ^5 = 11.32
- The inferred η' mass from the Witten-Veneziano formula with the new parameters: m_η' = 942 MeV
- Input quantities used for parameter fixing: m_π = 138 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, m_K = 495.7 MeV
- The chiral transition temperature from the lattice data was rescaled to T_c = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the NJL model studies of Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) by replacing the problematic η' meson mass input with the topological susceptibility from lattice simulations by Alles, D'Elia, and Di Giacomo (1997), avoiding the issue that η' is not a bound state in the NJL model due to lack of confinement. The results contradict the assumption made by Schaffner-Bielich (2000) that the drop in χ at T_c implies simultaneous U(1)_A restoration, and instead support the argument of Birse, Cohen, and McGovern (1996) that the drop is driven by chiral symmetry restoration through the Witten-Veneziano formula. The work enables future studies of the temperature dependence of the U(1)_A anomaly using lattice data at higher temperatures.
