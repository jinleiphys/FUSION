# Hydrodynamic Attractors in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> One of the many physical questions that have emerged from studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC concerns the validity of hydrodynamic modelling at the very early stages, when the Quark-Gluon Plasma system produced is still far from isotropy. In this article we review the idea of far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractors as a way to understand how the complexity of initial states of nuclear matter is reduced so that a hydrodynamic description can be effective.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic attractors provide a mechanism to explain how far-from-equilibrium initial states in heavy-ion collisions rapidly lose memory of their detailed initial conditions, enabling a hydrodynamic description to become effective much earlier than traditional thermalization timescales would suggest. The authors emphasize that this attractor behavior is a generic feature of conformal theories undergoing Bjorken flow, ultimately due to the specific kinematics of the earliest stages of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and that the resolution of the early thermalization puzzle relies on these symmetry assumptions remaining approximately valid for a sufficiently long time.

## Method

The authors review the hydrodynamic attractor concept in the context of Bjorken flow within MIS (Mueller-Israel-Stewart) theory, specifically the BRSSS version, by analyzing the coupled ordinary differential equations governing the effective temperature and pressure anisotropy. They examine both early-time and late-time asymptotic behavior using series expansions and transseries analysis, and compare attractor solutions across different hydrodynamic models including HJSW hydrodynamics and general-frame MIS theory, as well as kinetic theory and strongly coupled theories based on the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key numbers

- The leading late-time asymptotic behavior of the pressure anisotropy is given by \(\mathcal{A}(w) \sim 8 C_\eta / w\), where \(C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)\) for the AdS/CFT-matched case
- The early-time regular attractor solution approaches \(\mathcal{A}(0) = 6\sqrt{C_\eta/C_{\tau\Pi}}\), with \(C_{\tau\Pi} = (2-\log 2)/(2\pi)\) for the AdS/CFT-matched case
- The singulant parameter governing exponential damping of nonhydrodynamic modes is \(A = 3/(2C_{\tau\Pi})\)
- The power-law approach to the attractor at early times scales as \(\delta\mathcal{A} \sim w^{-4}\), independent of transport coefficients
- The gradient expansion coefficients diverge factorially as \(a_n \sim \Gamma(n+\beta) A^{-n}\) at large \(n\)
- In the HJSW model, the early-time attractor solution is \(\mathcal{A}(w) = 4 + [54 C_\eta |\Omega|^2 - 48 \Omega_I] / [20 - 9 C_\eta C_\sigma] w + \dots\)

## Context

This review extends the hydrodynamic attractor concept first identified in MIS theory by Heller and Spalinski (2015) and subsequently studied in kinetic theory and strongly coupled theories via AdS/CFT. The work builds on the gradient expansion analysis of Heller, Janik, and Witaszczyk (2013) which found that such expansions have vanishing radius of convergence, and on the transseries framework developed by Aniceto, Basar, and Heller (2015). The attractor framework enables a potential explanation of the early thermalization puzzle in heavy-ion collisions and provides a foundation for comparing different microscopic models of equilibration, though the authors note that full exploration of conformal symmetry breaking and transverse dynamics remains a task for the future.
