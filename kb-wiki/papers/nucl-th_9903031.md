# Electromagnetic Modes in Deformed Nuclei

> A strength function method is adopted to describe a coupling between electric and magnetic modes of different multipolarity. The collective vibrations are analysed for a separable residual interaction in the framework of the random-phase approximation. The coupling between $M2$ and $E1$ giant resonances is considered as an illustrative example.

# Electromagnetic Modes in Deformed Nuclei

## Key claim

The coupling between electric and magnetic modes of different multipolarity (such as M2 and E1) in deformed nuclei can be treated consistently using a strength function method within the random-phase approximation, but for the specific case of the M2 giant resonance in 154Sm the influence of the electric dipole mode on the total strength function is relatively small.

## Method

The authors adopt a phonon+rotor model with a separable residual interaction consisting of multipole-multipole and spin-multipole-spin-multipole forces, including both isoscalar and isovector components. The Hamiltonian is treated in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation, and the equations of motion are solved in the random-phase approximation (RPA). An averaging technique is applied to the strength function, avoiding the need to solve the full characteristic equation for each individual phonon root, and the resulting expression involves the characteristic determinant of the RPA algebraic equations evaluated at a complex energy.

## Key numbers

- The total decay probability from initial to final state is given by: T_{i→f}(E_γ) = 5.49985×10^22 Σ_λ [E_γ/(197.327)]^(2λ+1) (2λ+1)/[λ((2λ+1)!!)^2] [B(Eλ, i→f) + 0.011064×B(Mλ, i→f)], where B(Eλ) is in e^2 fm^(2λ), B(Mλ) is in μ_N^2 fm^(2λ-2), E_γ is in MeV, and T is in 1/s.
- The photo-absorption cross section is: σ(E_γ) = 1.40534×10^5 Σ_λ [E_γ/(197.327)]^(2λ-1) (λ+1)/[λ((2λ+1)!!)^2] [S(Eλ, i→f) + 0.011064×S(Mλ, i→f)], where S(Eλ) is in e^2 fm^(2λ)/MeV and S(Mλ) is in μ_N^2 fm^(2λ-2)/MeV.
- The M2 strength function is computed for 154Sm, comparing the full coupling of M2 and E1 operators against the magnetic quadrupole term alone; the coupling effect is found to be relatively small in the giant resonance region.

## Context

This work extends the strength function method developed by Kvasil, Nesterenko, and collaborators (Kv1, KN) to include coupling between electric and magnetic modes of different multipolarity, which is neglected in standard approaches such as Soloviev (Sol) and the textbook treatment by Ring and Schuck (RS). The formalism enables systematic analysis of how different electromagnetic operators contribute to the same parity and K quantum number in axially deformed nuclei, with applications to both giant resonances and low-lying states. The authors note that analysis of the coupling for low-lying states and for other operators is in progress.
