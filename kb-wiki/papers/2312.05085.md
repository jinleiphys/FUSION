# Feasibility of perturbative generation of bound-states from resonances or virtual states

> I investigate whether it is possible to generate bound-states from resonances or virtual states through first-order perturbation theory. Using contact-type potentials as those appeared in pionless effective field theory, I show that it is possible to obtain negative-energy states by sandwiching a next-to-leading order (NLO) interaction with the leading-order (LO) wavefunctions, under the presence of LO resonances or virtual states. However, at least under the framework of time-independent Schrödinger equation and Hermitian Hamiltonian, there is an inability to create bound-states with structure similar to those formed by the non-perturbative treatments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while first-order perturbation theory can produce negative-energy states from LO resonances or virtual states by sandwiching an NLO interaction with LO wavefunctions, it is impossible to create bound-states with structure similar to those formed by non-perturbative treatments under the framework of the time-independent Schrodinger equation and Hermitian Hamiltonian.

## Method

The author solves the time-independent Lippmann-Schwinger equation non-perturbatively at LO to generate scattering amplitudes and wavefunctions for the NN 3P0 channel (resonances) and 1S0 channel (virtual states), using contact-type potentials with a momentum regulator. NLO corrections are then added via first-order perturbation theory (DWBA) by sandwiching the NLO potential with LO wavefunctions expanded in a Harmonic-Oscillator basis, and the results are compared to non-perturbative treatments of the same combined potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the 3P0 channel case I (c_lo = -2.5 x 10^-9 MeV^-4, Lambda = 150 MeV): extracted resonance energy E_R = 0.53 MeV and width Gamma = 0.23 MeV
- For the 3P0 channel with a less attractive LO (c_lo = -2 x 10^-9 MeV^-4): broader resonance with E_R = 2.75 MeV and Gamma = 2.52 MeV
- For the 3P0 channel case I: a 4 times difference between the c_nlo required to generate a bound-state perturbatively versus non-perturbatively (c_nlo = -120 x 10^-11 MeV^-4 for perturbative bound-states at -0.11 and -0.026 MeV, versus c_nlo = -30 x 10^-11 MeV^-4 for non-perturbative bound-state at -0.194 MeV)
- For the 3P0 channel case I with hbar-omega = 1 MeV and n_max = 150: perturbative bound-states appear only at c_nlo = -120 x 10^-11 MeV^-4, while non-perturbative bound-states appear already at c_nlo = -30 x 10^-11 MeV^-4

## Context

This work extends the study of perturbative corrections in effective field theory frameworks such as pionless EFT (Hammer 2019) and modified chiral EFT (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1998; Long 2007; Yang 2009), where LO interactions are treated non-perturbatively while higher-order corrections are added perturbatively under DWBA. The results suggest that perturbatively generating bound-states from resonances or virtual states is fundamentally different from non-perturbative generation, and the author notes that this poses challenges for systems like 16O which are unstable at LO in pionless EFT (pionless16 2016) and chiral EFT (Yang 2020), where bound-states must be restored through perturbative corrections.
