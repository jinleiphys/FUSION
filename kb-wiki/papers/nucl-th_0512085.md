# Variational Monte Carlo study of pentaquark states in a correlated quark model

> Accurate numerical solution of the five-body Schrodinger equation is effected via variational Monte Carlo in a correlated quark model. The spectrum is assumed to exhibit a narrow resonance with strangeness S=+1. A fully antisymmetrized and pair-correlated five-quark wave function is obtained for the assumed non-relativistic Hamiltonian which has spin, isospin, and color dependent pair interactions and many-body confining terms which are fixed by the non-exotic spectra. Gauge field dynamics are modeled via flux tube exchange factors. The energy determined for the ground states with spin-parity 1/2- (1/2+) is 2.22 GeV (2.50 GeV). A lower energy negative parity state is consistent with recent lattice results.

## Key claim

The variational Monte Carlo calculation finds that the negative parity pentaquark state (J^π = 1/2^-) lies approximately 280 MeV below the positive parity state (J^π = 1/2^+), consistent with lattice results at large pion mass, but only under the assumption that the θ^+ is a narrow resonance treated as a stable bound state.

## Method

The five-body Schrodinger equation was solved using variational Monte Carlo with a fully antisymmetrized, pair-correlated wave function for a non-relativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian includes spin, isospin, and color dependent pair interactions from one-gluon exchange and one-pion exchange (between light quarks), plus many-body confining terms based on the flux tube model with three possible topologies for 4q\bar{q} states. The model parameters were fixed by the non-exotic single hadron spectrum and six-quark (deuteron) properties, and expectation values were evaluated via Monte Carlo integration of the 15-dimensional spatial coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Negative parity ground state mass (J^π = 1/2^-): 2.22 GeV
- Positive parity ground state mass (J^π = 1/2^+): 2.50 GeV
- Energy difference between negative and positive parity states: approximately 280 MeV
- Statistical errors on all mass values: less than 5 MeV
- Quark masses used: m_q = 313 MeV (light quarks), m_s = 550 MeV (strange quark)
- Constant V_0 fit to nucleon mass: 385.5(5) MeV
- Kinetic energy of negative parity state: 1.68 GeV; potential energy: 0.92 GeV
- Kinetic energy of lowest positive parity state: 2.13 GeV; potential energy: 0.74 GeV
- States with n ≠ 1 contribute less than 10% to the true positive parity ground state

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic flux tube quark model, previously applied to single hadrons and the deuteron by Paris (2000), to the flavor-exotic pentaquark sector following experimental claims of a narrow θ^+ resonance near 1540 MeV (Eidelman 2004). The results are consistent with lattice QCD calculations by Mathur (2004) and Lasscock (2005) in finding the negative parity state lower than positive parity, but the predicted masses (2.22-2.50 GeV) are significantly higher than the experimental candidate mass, suggesting the need for either different dynamics or that the experimental state is not a simple five-quark bound state. The calculation enables future work incorporating chiral symmetry effects and more sophisticated treatments of the resonance nature of the state.
