# The least-squares analysis of the moments of the charge distribution in the mean-field models

> Comparing the moments of the charge distribution in the mean-field models with experimental values from electron scattering, each value of the related moments of the point proton and neutron distributions is estimated in $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb by the least-squares analysis(LSA).

## Key claim

The least-squares analysis (LSA) of mean-field model predictions allows estimation of the point proton and neutron distribution moments from charge distribution moments, but the sixth moment of the charge distribution (\(H^6_c\)) currently lacks reliable experimental values, so the Fourier-Bessel and sum-of-Gaussians values are used only for reference.

## Method

The author calculated the sixth moment of the charge distribution (\(H^6_c\)) and its components using 11 relativistic mean-field (RMF) models and 9 Skyrme-type mean-field (SMF) models for \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(^{208}\)Pb. For each model framework, least-squares lines (LSLs) were constructed between \(H^6_c\) and each component (e.g., \(H^6_{cp}\), \(H^6_p\), \(Q^4_p\), \(R^2_p\), \(Q^4_n\), \(R^2_n\)), and the LSL-value of each component was determined by the intersection of the LSL with the experimental (or quasi-experimental) value of the higher-order moment.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{40}\)Ca, the LSL-values of \(H^6_{cp}\) are 5581.031 fm\(^6\) (RMF) and 5572.169 fm\(^6\) (SMF), using the SOG experimental value of \(H^6_c = 5.390 \times 10^3\) fm\(^6\).
- The SOG experimental value of \(H^6_c\) for \(^{48}\)Ca is \(4.299 \times 10^3\) fm\(^6\), and for \(^{208}\)Pb is \(5.294 \times 10^4\) fm\(^6\).
- The FB experimental value of \(H^6_c\) for \(^{40}\)Ca is \(4.234 \times 10^3\) fm\(^6\), which is overestimated by all MF models except SKI(1).
- The neutron contribution \(H^6_{cn}\) to \(H^6_c\) is \(9-12\%\) in \(^{48}\)Ca and \(4-5\%\) in \(^{208}\)Pb, with \(H_{4n}\) accounting for more than 50% of \(H^6_{cn}\).
- The LSLs for \(H^6_{cp}\) vs \(H^6_c\) in \(^{40}\)Ca have standard deviations of 0.7718 (RMF) and 1.5701 (SMF), with correlation coefficients about 1.0000.

## Context

This work extends the LSA method of Suzuki (2023, Ref. [kss]), which previously analyzed the fourth moment (\(Q^4_c\)) for the same nuclei, to the sixth moment (\(H^6_c\)) for the first time in nuclear physics. The analysis shows that neutron contributions are more significant in the sixth moment than in the fourth moment, and that the spin-orbit density plays a larger role in \(H^6_{cn}\) than in \(Q^4_{cn}\). The results enable future extraction of experimental \(H^6_c\) values once a new method for analyzing electron scattering cross sections is developed, replacing the conventional Fourier-Bessel and sum-of-Gaussians methods.
