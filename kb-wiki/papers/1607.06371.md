# Mirage in Temporal Correlation functions for Baryon-Baryon Interactions in Lattice QCD

> Single state saturation of the temporal correlation function is a key condition to extract physical observables such as energies and matrix elements of hadrons from lattice QCD simulations. A method commonly employed to check the saturation is to seek for a plateau of the observables for large Euclidean time. Identifying the plateau in the cases having nearby states, however, is non-trivial and one may even be misled by a fake plateau. Such a situation takes place typically for the system with two or more baryons. In this study, we demonstrate explicitly the danger from a possible fake plateau in the temporal correlation functions mainly for two baryons ($ΞΞ$ and $NN$), and three and four baryons ($^3{\rm He}$ and $^4{\rm He})$ as well, employing (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD at $m_π=0.51$ GeV on four lattice volumes with $L=$ 2.9, 3.6, 4.3 and 5.8 fm. Caution is given for drawing conclusion on the bound $NN$, $3N$ and $4N$ systems only based on the temporal correlation functions.

## Key claim

The plateau-like behavior observed in effective energy shifts for multi-baryon systems at accessible Euclidean times (t ~ 1-2 fm) can be a "fake plateau" caused by contamination from excited elastic scattering states, and this fake plateau can lead to incorrect conclusions about bound states even when statistical errors appear controlled.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at m_pi = 0.51 GeV using the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quarks on four lattice volumes with L = 2.9, 3.6, 4.3, and 5.8 fm. They computed temporal correlation functions for Xi-Xi, NN, 3N, and 4N systems using two different source types (smeared source and wall source) and two different baryon operator types (non-relativistic and relativistic), then compared the effective energy shifts obtained from each combination to test for consistency.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.51 GeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 1.32 GeV; Xi mass: m_Xi = 1.46 GeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.08995(40) fm (a^{-1} = 2.194(10) GeV)
- Four lattice volumes: L = 2.9, 3.6, 4.3, and 5.8 fm
- For Xi-Xi (^1S_0) with smeared source and non-relativistic operator, infinite volume extrapolation gave Delta E = -7.70(0.89)(+0.37/-0.20) MeV, suggesting a bound state
- For Xi-Xi (^3S_1) with smeared source and non-relativistic operator, infinite volume extrapolation gave Delta E = +6.81(1.04)(+0.52/-0.48) MeV, which is unphysical for a two-particle system
- The typical lowest excitation energy of elastic two-baryon scattering states on the 4.3 fm lattice was delta E_el = 50 MeV
- Signal-to-noise ratio for an A-baryon system decays as exp[ -A (m_B - 3m_M/2) t ]

## Context

This work directly addresses the reliability of the plateau fitting method used by Yamazaki et al. (2012) and Yamazaki et al. (2015), who claimed bound dineutron, deuteron, He-3, and He-4 at similar pion masses using the same lattice setup. The authors demonstrate that the plateau method can produce inconsistent results between different source operators, and that the positive energy shift obtained for Xi-Xi (^3S_1) proves the method fails. This motivates the use of alternative approaches such as the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2008, Aoki 2009, Aoki 2011, Aoki 2012), which utilizes space-time correlation functions rather than just temporal correlations to study multi-hadron systems.
