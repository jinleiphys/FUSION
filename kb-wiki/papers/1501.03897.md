# Charge asymmetry in the differential cross section of high-energy bremsstrahlung in the field of a heavy atom

> The distinction between the charged particle and antiparticle differential cross sections of high-energy bremsstrahlung in the electric field of a heavy atom is investigated. The consideration is based on the quasiclassical approximation to the wave functions in the external field. The charge asymmetry (the ratio of the antisymmetric and symmetric parts of the differential cross section) arises due to the account for the first quasiclassical correction to the differential cross section. All evaluations are performed with the exact account of the atomic field. We consider in detail the charge asymmetry for electrons and muons. For electrons, the nuclear size effect is not important while for muons this effect should be taken into account. For the longitudinal polarization of the initial charged particle, the account for the first quasiclassical correction to the differential cross section leads to the asymmetry in the cross section with respect to the replacement $φ\rightarrow-φ$, where $φ$ is the azimuth angle between the photon momentum and the momentum of the final charged particle.

# Charge asymmetry in the differential cross section of high-energy bremsstrahlung in the field of a heavy atom

## Key claim
The charge asymmetry (difference between particle and antiparticle differential cross sections) in high-energy bremsstrahlung from electrons and muons in the field of a heavy atom arises solely from the first quasiclassical correction to the Coulomb corrections, and is absent in both the Born approximation and the leading quasiclassical approximation. For electrons the nuclear size effect is not important, while for muons this effect must be taken into account because the muon Compton wavelength (1.87 fm) is smaller than the nuclear radius (7.3 fm for gold, 7.2 fm for lead).

## Method
The authors used the quasiclassical approximation to the Dirac equation wave functions in an external atomic field, computing the matrix element for bremsstrahlung with exact account of the atomic potential. They derived the Green's function of the squared Dirac equation up to second order in the correction term involving the gradient of the potential, obtaining the leading terms and first quasiclassical corrections for the wave function structures. The differential cross section was then calculated for arbitrary localized potential and specifically for the pure Coulomb field, with the antisymmetric part (odd in the parameter η = Zα) extracted from the full cross section.

## Key numbers
- Muon Compton wavelength: λ_μ = 1.87 fm
- Nuclear radius for gold: R = 7.3 fm
- Nuclear radius for lead: R = 7.2 fm
- At η ≪ 1: Re g(η) ≈ η and Im g(η) ≈ -(4 ln 2)η², where g(η) = η Γ(1-iη)Γ(1/2+iη)/[Γ(1+iη)Γ(1/2-iη)]
- The ratio S₁/S₀ = πΔ Re g(η)/(2ε_p) at ω ≪ ε_p, which can exceed ten percent
- The antisymmetric correction to the spectrum: dσ_a/dω = απ³η² Re g(η)/(4mωε_p²) [2(ε_p²+ε_q²)/(ε_pε_q) - 1] (ε_p+ε_q)

## Context
This work extends the quasiclassical treatment of Coulomb corrections to bremsstrahlung beyond the leading approximation, following earlier calculations of the first quasiclassical correction for pair photoproduction by Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko (2004, 2005) and by DiPiazza, Milstein, and collaborators (2010, 2012, 2014). The charge asymmetry predicted here for bremsstrahlung is analogous to the asymmetry previously found for e⁺e⁻ pair photoproduction by Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko (2012) and for μ⁺μ⁻ pair photoproduction by DiPiazza, Lee, Milstein, and Rudenko (2014). The results enable precise calculations of bremsstrahlung cross sections for electrons and muons in heavy atoms, which are needed for electromagnetic shower simulations in detectors and for radiative corrections.
