# Dbar-N interaction from meson-exchange and quark-gluon dynamics

> We investigate the Dbar-N interaction at low energies using a meson-exchange model supplemented with a short-distance contribution from one-gluon-exchange. The model is developed in close analogy to the meson-exchange KN interaction of the Juelich group utilizing SU(4) symmetry constraints. The main ingredients of the interaction are provided by vector meson (rho, omega) exchange and higher-order box diagrams involving D*N, DΔ, and D*Δintermediate states. The short range part is assumed to receive additional contributions from genuine quark-gluon processes. The predicted cross sections for Dbar-N for excess energies up to 150 MeV are of the same order of magnitude as those for KN but with average values of around 20 mb, roughly a factor two larger than for the latter system. It is found that the omega-exchange plays a very important role. Its interference pattern with the rho-exchange, which is basically fixed by the assumed SU(4) symmetry, clearly determines the qualitative features of the Dbar-N interaction -- very similiar to what happens also for the KN system.

## Key claim

The authors construct a model for the low-energy \(\bar D N\) interaction by extending a known \(KN\) meson-exchange model via SU(4) symmetry, supplemented with a one-gluon-exchange quark contribution, but they emphasize that the scalar-meson sector introduces the main uncertainty because the SU(4) relations for scalar couplings are unclear and they must resort to rough estimates or alternative scenarios.

## Method

The authors build a \(\bar D N\) potential in close analogy to the Juelich group's meson-exchange \(KN\) model, using SU(4) symmetry to relate the \(\bar D \bar D \rho\) and \(\bar D \bar D \omega\) coupling constants to the known \(\pi \pi \rho\) coupling, and they include vector-meson exchange (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)), scalar-meson exchange, baryon exchange (\(\Lambda_c\), \(\Sigma_c\)), and higher-order box diagrams with \(\bar D^* N\), \(\bar D \Delta\), and \(\bar D^* \Delta\) intermediate states. They also add a short-range contribution from one-gluon-exchange (Coulomb and spin-spin parts) using a quark model with constituent quark masses and Gaussian wave functions. The full potential is iterated in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain the scattering amplitude and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The predicted \(\bar D N\) cross sections for excess energies up to 150 MeV have average values around 20 mb, roughly a factor two larger than for the \(KN\) system.
- The \(\bar D \bar D \rho\) coupling constant from SU(4) is \(g_{\bar D \bar D \rho} = g_{KK\rho} = g_{\pi \pi \rho}/2\), with \(g_{\pi \pi \rho} = 6.0\).
- The \(\bar D \bar D \omega\) coupling constant from SU(4) and ideal mixing is \(g_{\bar D \bar D \omega} = g_{KK\rho}\).
- The scalar-meson coupling constants for the \(\bar D N\) system are reduced by a factor of 4 compared to the \(KN\) values in one scenario, with the product of couplings for the \(\sigma\) exchange given as 0.25 (1.00) in the two scenarios.
- The quark-gluon coupling constant is taken as \(\alpha_s = 0.6\), with light quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 330\) MeV, strange quark mass \(m_s = 550\) MeV, and charm quark mass \(m_c = 1600\) MeV.
- The Gaussian width of the nucleon wave function is \(\alpha_N = 400\) MeV, and for the \(D\) meson \(\beta_D = 383.5\) MeV (with an alternative value of 440 MeV tested).
- The \(\bar D N\) cross sections from the OGE part are roughly a factor 1.5 larger than those for \(KN\).

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange \(KN\) model of the Juelich group (Juel1, Juel2) to the \(\bar D N\) system using SU(4) symmetry, and it builds on a prior study of the \(KN\) system by the same authors (HHK) that incorporated one-gluon-exchange. It contrasts with earlier estimates of \(DN\) cross sections by Lin00 (using Born approximation with SU(4) Lagrangians) and by Sibirtsev (1999) (using quark rearrangement arguments), and it improves upon those by iterating the potential in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation rather than using Born approximation. The results are intended to provide urgently needed estimates for the design of detectors at the FAIR facility (PANDA collaboration) and to enable future studies of charmed meson interactions in medium for heavy-ion collision physics.
