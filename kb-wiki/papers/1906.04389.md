# Diffractive dijet production from the Color Glass Condensate and the small-$x$ gluon distributions

> We study exclusive dijet production in electron-proton deep inelastic scattering at a future Electron Ion Collider. We predict the elliptic modulation of the cross section as a function of the angle between the dijet transverse momentum and the recoil momentum, and show that this modulation is due to non-trivial angular correlations between the transverse coordinate and transverse momentum in the Wigner (or Husimi) distribution. The small-$x$ evolution is shown to decrease the elliptic modulation in the EIC kinematics, because of the growth of the proton with decreasing $x$.

## Key claim

The elliptic modulation (v2) of the diffractive dijet cross section in electron-proton deep inelastic scattering is predicted to decrease by almost a factor of 2 from the lowest to highest center-of-mass energies at the EIC, because small-x evolution causes the proton to grow and reduces density gradients; however, the modulation is relatively small and likely difficult to measure.

## Method

The authors model the proton using Wilson lines obtained from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, with color charge density sampled as a local Gaussian variable and evolved to smaller Bjorken-x via the perturbative JIMWLK evolution equations. They compute the dipole-proton scattering amplitude and the gluon Wigner and Husimi distributions, then derive the diffractive dijet production cross section in the correlation limit where the average dijet momentum is much larger than the recoil momentum. The elliptic Fourier harmonic v2 is extracted from the angular dependence of both the dipole amplitude and the dijet cross section as a function of the angle between the transverse coordinate and transverse momentum (or between the dijet momentum and recoil momentum).

## Key numbers

- The elliptic coefficient v2 of the diffractive dijet production cross section decreases by almost a factor of 2 across the EIC energy range, from the lowest to highest center-of-mass energies.
- The diffractive dip location for charmed dijets is estimated to be at inverse dipole size ~ sqrt(m_c^2 + Q^2) ≈ 1.4 GeV.
- The smearing parameter for the Husimi distribution is chosen as l = 1 GeV^{-1}, corresponding to a distance scale much smaller than the proton size.
- The recoil momentum is fixed at |Delta| = 0.1 GeV to maintain the correlation limit |P| >> |Delta|.
- The initial evolution rapidity corresponds to x0 = 0.01.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical connection between diffractive dijet production and the gluon Wigner distribution established by Hatta (2016) by performing explicit CGC calculations with JIMWLK evolution, rather than using parametrizations such as IPsat (Kowalski, 2003) which have no angular dependence between dipole size and impact parameter. The results predict that the elliptic modulation will be measurable at a future Electron Ion Collider (EIC) or LHeC, though the signal is small in the kinematics where the connection to the Wigner distribution is most rigorous; larger signals are expected at larger recoil momenta as shown by Salazar (2019).
