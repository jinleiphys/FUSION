# Solutions of Nuclear Pairing

> We give the exact solution of orbit dependent nuclear pairing problem between two non-degenerate energy levels using the Bethe ansatz technique. Our solution reduces to previously solved cases in the appropriate limits including Richardson's treatment of reduced pairing in terms of rational Gaudin algebra operators.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact solution for the nuclear pairing problem with two non-degenerate energy levels and orbit-dependent pairing strengths, which had previously only been solved in three limiting cases (degenerate levels with equal strengths, non-degenerate levels with equal strengths, and degenerate levels with different strengths).

## Method

The authors use the Bethe ansatz technique with step operators of the form \(J^+(x)=\sum_j\frac{c_j^*}{2\varepsilon_j-|c_j|^2x}S_j^+\) to construct eigenstates of the separable pairing Hamiltonian. They derive Bethe ansatz equations for the parameters \(x_k\) and show that the energy eigenvalues are given by sums over these parameters. The solution is extended to hole pairs for shells more than half full, and explicit analytical results are given for one and two pairs.

## Key numbers

* For one pair with two levels \(j_1\) and \(j_2\), the two energy eigenvalues are given by \(E_{N=1}=\frac{\Omega_{j_1}\sin^2\vartheta+\Omega_{j_2}\cos^2\vartheta}{2}-(\varepsilon_{j_1}+\varepsilon_{j_2})\pm\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{4\Delta^2-4(\Omega_{j_1}\sin^2\vartheta-\Omega_{j_2}\cos^2\vartheta)\Delta+(\Omega_{j_1}\sin^2\vartheta+\Omega_{j_2}\cos^2\vartheta)^2}\), where \(\Delta=\varepsilon_{j_1}-\varepsilon_{j_2}\) and \(|c_{j_1}|^2+|c_{j_2}|^2=1\) with \(c_{j_1}=\sin\vartheta\), \(c_{j_2}=\cos\vartheta\).
* For two pairs, the three energy eigenvalues are given by \(E_{N=2}=\frac{w}{\cos(2\vartheta)}+2(\varepsilon_{j_1}+\varepsilon_{j_2})\) where \(w\) is a root of a cubic polynomial (equation 32) with coefficients depending on \(\Omega_{j_1}\), \(\Omega_{j_2}\), \(\Delta\), and \(\vartheta\).
* In the degenerate limit (\(\varepsilon_j=\varepsilon\)), the energy for states with all \(x_k\neq0\) approaches \(E_N=2N\varepsilon\).
* The energy of the fully occupied shell is \(E_{N_{max}}=\sum_j(2\varepsilon_j-|c_j|^2)\Omega_j\).

## Context

This work extends the exact solutions for nuclear pairing beyond the three previously known limits: the degenerate-level equal-strength case solved by Kerman (1961) using quasi-spin algebra, the non-degenerate equal-strength case solved by Richardson (1963) using rational Gaudin algebra operators, and the degenerate-level different-strength case solved by Pan et al. (1998) and Balantekin et al. (2007). The solution enables study of the interplay between one-body effects and orbit-dependent pairing in a realistic two-level model, and represents a step toward the exact analytical solution of the most general nuclear pairing problem.
