# Inelastic scattering of protons from $^{6,8}$He and $^{7,11}$Li in a folding model approach

> The proton-inelastic scattering from $^{6,8}$He and $^{7,11}$Li nuclei are studied in a folding model approach. A finite-range, momentum, density and isospin dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction (SBM) is folded with realistic density distributions of the above nuclei. The renormalization factors N$_R$ and N$_I$ on the real and volume imaginary part of the folded potentials are obtained by analyzing the respective elastic scattering data and kept unaltered for the inelastic analysis at the same energy. The form factors are generated by taking derivatives of the folded potentials and therefore required renormalizations. The $β$ values are extracted by fitting the p + $^{6,8}$He,$^{7,11}$Li inelastic angular distributions. The present analysis of p + $^8$He inelastic scattering to the 3.57 MeV excited state, including unpublished forward angle data (RIKEN) confirms L = 2 transition. Similar analysis of the p + $^6$He inelastic scattering angular distribution leading to the 1.8 MeV (L = 2) excited state fails to satisfactorily reproduce the data.

# Inelastic scattering of protons from $^{6,8}$He and $^{7,11}$Li in a folding model approach

## Key claim
The folding model analysis using a finite-range, momentum, density and isospin dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction (SBM) successfully describes proton inelastic scattering to the 3.57 MeV excited state of $^{8}$He as an L = 2 transition, but the same formalism fails to satisfactorily reproduce the p + $^{6}$He inelastic scattering angular distribution leading to the 1.8 MeV (L = 2) excited state, despite using multiple ground state density prescriptions.

## Method
The authors performed a semi-microscopic folding model analysis of proton inelastic scattering from $^{6,8}$He and $^{7,11}$Li nuclei. A finite-range, momentum, density and isospin dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (SBM) was folded with realistic density distributions of each nucleus. Renormalization factors N$_R$ and N$_I$ on the real and volume imaginary parts of the folded potentials were first obtained by fitting elastic scattering data and then kept fixed for inelastic analysis. Form factors for inelastic scattering were generated by taking derivatives of the folded potentials, and deformation parameters $\beta$ were extracted by fitting the inelastic angular distributions using DWBA calculations with the code DWUCK4.

## Key numbers
- For p + $^{7}$Li inelastic scattering at 49.75A MeV: deformation parameter $\beta$ = 0.59 for the 0.478 MeV excited state (L = 2, 3/2$^-$ to 1/2$^-$) and $\beta$ = 0.82 for the 4.63 MeV excited state (L = 2, 3/2$^-$ to 7/2$^-$)
- For p + $^{11}$Li inelastic scattering at 68.4A MeV: deformation parameter $\beta$ = 0.58 for the 1.3 MeV excited state (L = 1), using N$_R$ = 0.50 and N$_I$ = 0.15 from elastic scattering
- For p + $^{8}$He inelastic scattering at 72.5A MeV to the 3.57 MeV excited state: deformation parameter $\beta$ = 0.28 for L = 2, which is smaller than the value of 0.44 from a previous Woods-Saxon coupled channel calculation (Golovkova 1995)
- For p + $^{6}$He inelastic scattering at 40.9A MeV to the 1.8 MeV state: extracted $\beta$ value agrees closely with previous works (Aumann 1999, Rusek 2000) giving $\beta$ approximately 0.78
- The $^{6}$He ground state density models used have rms radii ranging from 2.32 fm to 2.76 fm, assuming a bare $^{4}$He core rms radius of 1.49 fm

## Context
This work extends earlier folding model analyses of elastic proton scattering from these nuclei (Gupta and Samanta 2000) to inelastic scattering, and builds upon prior studies of soft dipole resonance in $^{11}$Li (Korsheninnikov 1997, Kanungo 1997, 1998, 1999) and coupled channel calculations for $^{8}$He (Golovkova 1995). The analysis confirms the L = 2 spin-parity assignment for the 3.57 MeV state in $^{8}$He using previously unpublished forward-angle data from RIKEN. The failure to satisfactorily describe p + $^{6}$He inelastic scattering suggests that the simple derivative form factor approach may be inadequate for this nucleus, and that more sophisticated treatments of the reaction mechanism or structure may be needed.
