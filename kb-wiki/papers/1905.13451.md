# Perey-effect in Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel description of $(d,p)$ reactions

> The Perey-effect in two-body channels of $(d,p)$ reactions has been known for a long time. It arises when the nonlocal two-body deuteron-target and/or proton-target problem is approximated by a local one, manifesting itself in a reduction of the scattering channel wave functions in the nuclear interior. However, the $(d,p)$ reaction mechanism requires explicit accounting for three-body dynamics involving the target and the neutron and proton in the deuteron. Treating nonlocality of the nucleon-target interactions within a three-body context requires significant effort and demands going beyond the widely-used adiabatic approximation, which can be done using a continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method. However, the inclusion of nonlocal interactions into the CDCC description of $(d,p)$ reactions has not been developed yet. Here, we point out that, similarly to the two-body nonlocal case, nonlocality in a three-body channel can be accounted for by introducing the Perey factors. We explain this procedure and present the first CDCC calculations to our knowledge including the Perey-effect.

# Perey-effect in Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel description of $(d,p)$ reactions

## Key claim

The Perey-effect, previously known only in two-body channels, can be incorporated into three-body CDCC calculations of $(d,p)$ reactions by factorizing the total wave function as a product of nucleon Perey factors and a solution of a Schrödinger equation without velocity-dependence on the center-of-mass coordinate $\ve{R}$, provided the nonlocal potentials are of the Perey-Buck type and the spin-orbit interaction is neglected.

## Method

The authors constructed local-equivalent nucleon-target potentials ${\tilde U}^{\rm eff}_{NA}$ from the nonlocal Perey-Buck potential of Giannini and Ricco (1976) by solving the transcendental equation for $U_{loc}^0$ and adding next-to-leading-order corrections including gradient terms. They then solved the three-body $A+n+p$ Schrödinger equation using the CDCC expansion in FRESCO, including recoil-induced three-body terms $\Delta U_1$ and $\Delta U_2$ that arise from the velocity-dependent parts, and applied Perey factors $P_n(r_n)P_p(r_p)$ to modify the overlap function in the $(d,p)$ transition amplitude. Calculations were performed for $^{12}$C$(d,p)^{13}$C at $E_d=30$ MeV and $^{40}$Ca$(d,p)^{41}$Ca at $E_d=10$, 20, and 56 MeV, using only $s$-wave continuum discretized into 7-10 bins and the Hulthen $n$-$p$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C$(d,p)^{13}$C($1/2^-$) at $E_d=30$ MeV and $\theta=6^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.15$ (Perey factor reduces cross section by 15% at first peak)
- For $^{12}$C$(d,p)^{13}$C($1/2^+$) at $E_d=30$ MeV and $\theta=0^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 0.77$ (Perey factor increases cross section by 23% at first peak); at $\theta=32^\circ$ (second peak): $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.47$ (47% reduction)
- For $^{12}$C$(d,p)^{13}$C($5/2^+$) at $E_d=30$ MeV and $\theta=5^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.19$
- For $^{40}$Ca$(d,p)^{41}$Ca($7/2^-$) at $E_d=10$ MeV and $\theta=37^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.005$ (negligible Perey effect)
- For $^{40}$Ca$(d,p)^{41}$Ca($7/2^-$) at $E_d=20$ MeV and $\theta=25.5^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.07$
- For $^{40}$Ca$(d,p)^{41}$Ca($7/2^-$) at $E_d=56$ MeV and $\theta=0^\circ$: $\sigma_{\rm all}/\sigma_{\rm all}^{\rm P} = 1.34$
- The modification of $U^0_{loc}$ to ${\tilde U}^{\rm eff}_{NA}$ alone (without Perey factors) affects cross sections by up to 7% for nodeless final states and up to 11% for the $1s_{1/2}$ state in $^{13}$C
- The nonlocality range used was $\beta = 0.9552 \left(\frac{A+1}{A}\right)^{1/2}$ fm from Giannini and Ricco (1976)
- The Perey factor reduces the overlap function in the nuclear interior by 70-80%

## Context

This work extends the treatment of nonlocality in $(d,p)$ reactions from the two-body Perey effect (Perey, 1963) and the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) approach of Timofeyuk (2013) to the full CDCC framework, which explicitly accounts for three-body dynamics including deuteron breakup. The authors compare their results to the leading-order local-equivalent CDCC (LECDCC) method of Gómez-Ramos (2018) and find deviations of 3-7% for $E_d \leq 30$ MeV, rising to 20% at 56 MeV, suggesting that the Perey-effect treatment may represent a satisfactory approximation to the full nonlocal problem. The work enables quantification of uncertainties in spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients extracted from CDCC analyses of $(d,p)$ experiments, particularly at higher deuteron energies where the Perey effect becomes significant.
