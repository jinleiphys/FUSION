# Chiral SU(3) dynamics and the quasibound K^-pp cluster

> The prototype of a Kbar nuclear cluster, K^-pp, has been investigated using effective KbarN potentials based on chiral SU(3) dynamics. Variational calculation shows a bound state solution with shallow binding energy B(K^-pp)=20 \pm 3 MeV and broad mesonic decay width Γ(KbarNN -> πY N)=40 - 70 MeV. The KbarN(I=0) pair in the K^-pp system exhibits a similar structure as the Λ(1405). We have also estimated the dispersive correction, p-wave KbarN interaction, and two-nucleon absorption width.

## Key claim

The K⁻pp system is a weakly bound state with a shallow binding energy of 20 ± 3 MeV, contrary to earlier predictions of deep binding, because the chiral SU(3) dynamics produces a relatively weak KbarN interaction compared to phenomenological potentials.

## Method

A variational calculation was performed for the K⁻pp system with spin-parity J^π = 0⁻ and isospin (T, T_z) = (1/2, 1/2), using the Argonne v18 potential for the NN interaction and an effective KbarN potential derived from chiral SU(3) dynamics. The wave function included two isospin components (T_N = 1 and T_N = 0) with spatial parts built from single-particle wave packets and two-particle correlation functions. Self-consistency was enforced between the energy-dependent KbarN potential and the binding energy of the antikaon in the three-body system, with two ansatze (Type I and Type II) for how the antikaon energy is shared between the two nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of K⁻pp: B(K⁻pp) = 20 ± 3 MeV
- Mesonic decay width: Γ(KbarNN → πYN) = 40–70 MeV
- Mean NN distance in K⁻pp: R_NN = 2.21 fm (for one chiral model with Type I ansatz)
- Mean KbarN(I=0) distance in K⁻pp: R^{KbarN}_{I=0} = 1.82 fm, compared to R_{KbarN}(Λ*) = 1.86 fm for the isolated Λ(1405)
- Binding energy of isolated KbarN(I=0) system: about 12 MeV from KbarN threshold
- Two-nucleon absorption width estimate: 4–12 MeV
- p-wave KbarN contribution to binding energy: repulsive, about −3 MeV
- p-wave contribution to decay width: increase of 10–35 MeV
- Estimated dispersive correction to K⁻pp binding: ΔB ≲ 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Kbar nuclear clusters using phenomenological potentials by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), which predicted deeply bound states with small widths, and contrasts with a Faddeev calculation by Ikeda and Sato using chiral interactions that found 80 MeV binding energy. The results enable comparison with experimental data from the DISTO collaboration, which reported a broad structure about 100 MeV below the KbarNN threshold, and suggest that such a structure may be interpreted in terms of πΣN dynamics rather than a deeply bound KbarNN state.
