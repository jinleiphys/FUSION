# Improved Nearside-Farside Decomposition of Elastic Scattering Amplitudes

> A simple technique is described, that provides improved nearside-farside (NF) decompositions of elastic scattering amplitudes. The technique, involving the resummation of a Legendre partial wave series, reduces the importance of unphysical contributions to NF subamplitudes, which can arise in more conventional NF decompositions. Applications are made to a strong absorption model and to a $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C optical potential at $E_{\text{lab}} = 132$ MeV.

# Improved Nearside-Farside Decomposition of Elastic Scattering Amplitudes

## Key claim
The paper presents a modified resummation technique for nearside-farside (NF) decompositions of elastic scattering amplitudes that reduces unphysical contributions to NF subamplitudes, but the improvement is conditional: the method works by eliminating low partial waves from the resummed partial wave series, and its effectiveness depends on the specific choice of resummation parameters, with the best results obtained when the parameters are chosen to zero out the leading coefficients in the resummed series.

## Method
The authors modify the standard Fuller NF decomposition by first applying a generalized Yennie-Ravenhall-Wilson (YRW) resummation to the Legendre partial wave series for the elastic scattering amplitude, using a parameterized form with coefficients beta_i chosen to eliminate the lowest partial wave coefficients (a_0, a_1, etc.) from the resummed series. After this improved resummation of order r (r = 0, 1, 2), they apply the standard Fuller splitting of Legendre polynomials into traveling wave components Q_l^(+/-)(cos theta), followed by a final YRW resummation of the NF subamplitudes to ensure convergence. The method is tested on a strong absorption model (SAM) with a two-parameter symmetric S-matrix and on a 16O + 12C optical potential at Elab = 132 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For the SAM with parameters Lambda = 10 and Delta = 2: the R = 1_Y method (beta_1 = -0.800) produced F (= N) cross sections and LAMs in agreement with semiclassical results up to theta approximately 120 degrees; the R = 2_Y method (beta_{1,2} = -0.879 +/- 0.076 i) extended agreement over almost the full angular range.
- For the 16O + 12C optical potential at Elab = 132 MeV: the R = 1_Y method (beta_1 = -0.999 - 0.099 i) removed oscillations in the N cross section and N LIP curves for theta greater than approximately 30 degrees, while the R = 0_Y method showed N LIP oscillating around 0 at theta approximately 90 degrees.
- For the SAM with R = 0 (no resummation), the F (= N) LAM was approximately 0 for theta greater than approximately 60 degrees, indicating unphysical contributions dominating over most of the angular range.

## Context
This work extends the original Fuller NF decomposition (Fuller, 1975) and the modified NF decomposition proposed by Hatchell (1989), who first applied YRW resummation to reduce unphysical contributions. The authors build on earlier work showing that using Fuller's Q_l^(+/-) functions with YRW resummation gives better results than Hatchell's original approach (Holstein, 1999). The improved resummation technique enables cleaner identification of semiclassical trajectories in heavy-ion scattering, as demonstrated for the 16O + 12C system, and provides a systematic method to test which features of NF decompositions are physically meaningful rather than artifacts of the decomposition technique.
