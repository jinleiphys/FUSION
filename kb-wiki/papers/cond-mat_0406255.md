# Fermionic Casimir Effect in Case of Andreev Reflection

> We describe the Fermionic Casimir effect in the case of two spherical superfluid scatterers immersed in a normal Fermi system. It is shown that due to the focusing property of Andreev reflection this new Casimir-like energy is significantly enhanced when compared to the case of normal scatterers with specular reflection.

# Fermionic Casimir Effect in Case of Andreev Reflection

## Key claim

The Fermionic Casimir energy between two spherical superfluid scatterers embedded in a normal Fermi system is significantly enhanced compared to the case of normal hard-wall scatterers with specular reflection, but only when the separation between grain centers satisfies R/2a ≤ k_p/(k_p − k_h); otherwise the periodic orbits become unstable and the enhancement is reduced.

## Method

The authors solve the Bogoliubov–de Gennes equations for two spherical superfluid grains (with constant pairing field Δ inside, vanishing outside) immersed in a normal Fermi gas, using Krein's formula to compute the change in density of states from the scattering phase shifts. They construct exact expressions involving T-matrix elements for particle and hole scattering off a single grain and Hankel functions for propagation between grains, then derive asymptotic approximations valid for k_F R ≫ 1 by retaining only the leading term in the Hankel function expansion. The Casimir energy is obtained by integrating the energy-weighted change in density of states over quasiparticle energy.

## Key numbers

- The interaction energy for two hard-wall spherical voids of radius a with center separation R is E_C(R) ≈ −ν_deg (ℏ²k_F²/2m) (a²/2πR(R−2a)) j₁[2k_F(R−2a)], where ν_deg is the spin-isospin degeneracy factor.
- For two superfluid grains, the Casimir energy is E_SC(R) ≈ ν_deg (ℏ²k_F²/2m) (k_F a⁴ cos φ_Δ)/(2π R²(R−2a)) X, where X = O(1) and positive, and φ_Δ is the relative phase of the pairing fields.
- The stability condition for periodic orbits is R/2a ≤ k_p/(k_p − k_h), with |k_p − k_h|/k_F ≪ 1.
- Numerical example uses μ = 200/a² and Δ = 50/a² for two identical disks with R = 6a.
- The quantization condition for bound states is (k_p − k_h)(R−2a) − 2 arccos(E/Δ) = 2π n, for n = 0, ±1, ±2, ...
- At E = 0, the squared integral factor in the density of states becomes 4k_F²a², the squared number of partial waves undergoing Andreev reflection.

## Context

This work extends the Fermionic Casimir effect previously studied for hard-wall voids by Bulgac et al. (2002) and by Volovik (2001) for one-dimensional systems, now incorporating Andreev reflection at normal-superfluid boundaries. The authors show that while normal hard spheres produce only one unstable periodic orbit between their noses, superfluid grains generate many stable periodic orbits due to retro-reflection, leading to a much larger change in density of states. The results are relevant to neutron star physics where superfluid grains are expected to exist, as noted by Bulgac et al. (2002) and in studies of neutron star crust structure, and may also apply to QCD color-superfluid grains in quark stars and cosmological scenarios.
