# Precision calculation of the axion-nucleon coupling in chiral perturbation theory

> We derive the axion-nucleon interaction Lagrangian in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order. The effective axion-nucleon coupling is calculated to a few percent accuracy.

## Key claim

The axion-nucleon coupling constant is calculated to a few percent accuracy using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order, but the numerical precision is limited by the uncertainty in the low-energy constants, particularly the newly introduced isoscalar low-energy constants \(\bar{d}_{16}^i\) which are "hitherto undetermined."

## Method

The authors construct the axion-nucleon interaction Lagrangian in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) by adapting the standard \(\pi N\) Lagrangian from Fettes (1998) and adding isoscalar axial current terms that are not traceless, since the axion current is anomaly-free by construction. They derive tree-level contributions at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) and one-pion-loop contributions (diagrams with \(NN\pi\), \(NN\pi a\), and \(NN\pi\pi a\) vertices), then renormalize the divergences using a set of \(\beta\)-functions, including three new ones (\(\beta_{16}^i\), \(\beta_{25}^i\), \(\beta_{29}^i\)) worked out for the first time. The final axion-nucleon coupling at zero momentum transfer is expressed in terms of the nucleon spin matrix elements \(\Delta q\) (from lattice QCD) and the model-dependent axion-quark couplings \(X_q\) for the KSVZ and DFSZ axion models.

## Key numbers

- Axion-proton leading-order coupling: \(g_a^p = -0.430(36) + 0.847(50)X_u - 0.407(34)X_d - 0.035(13)X_s\)
- Axion-neutron leading-order coupling: \(g_a^n = -0.002(30) - 0.407(34)X_u + 0.847(50)X_d - 0.035(13)X_s\)
- Axion-proton loop contribution: \(g_a^{p,\text{loop}} = -0.002(1) + 0.0001(3)X_u + 0.005(2)X_d - 0.0004(2)X_s\)
- Axion-neutron loop contribution: \(g_a^{n,\text{loop}} = -0.003(1) + 0.005(2)X_u + 0.0001(3)X_d - 0.0004(2)X_s\)
- Nucleon spin matrix elements (FLAG review, \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) at \(\mu=2\) GeV): \(\Delta u = 0.847(50)\), \(\Delta d = -0.407(34)\), \(\Delta s = -0.035(13)\)
- Quark mass ratio \(z = m_u/m_d = 0.485(19)\), \(w = m_u/m_s = 0.025(1)\)
- Leading-order pion mass: \(M_\pi = 136.10(1.82)\) MeV
- Low-energy constants: \(\bar{d}_{16} = 0.4(1.3)\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(d_{18} = -0.44(24)\) GeV\(^{-2}\)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of the axion-nucleon coupling by di Cortona (2015) to next-to-next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, improving the precision from order-of-magnitude estimates to a few percent accuracy. The results enable more precise determinations of astrophysical constraints on the axion mass and decay constant from processes such as axion bremsstrahlung in supernovae, as studied by Iwamoto (1984), Raffelt (1987), and others. The new low-energy constants \(\bar{d}_{16}^i\) introduced here remain undetermined and represent the main source of uncertainty for future improvements.
