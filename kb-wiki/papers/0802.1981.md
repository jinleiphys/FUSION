# Exact stochastic simulation of dissipation and non-Markovian effects in open quantum systems

> The exact dynamics of a system coupled to an environment can be described by an integro-differential stochastic equation of its reduced density. The influence of the environment is incorporated through a mean-field which is both stochastic and non-local in time and where the standard two-times correlation functions of the environment appear naturally. Since no approximation is made, the presented theory incorporates exactly dissipative and non-Markovian effects. Applications to the spin-boson model coupled to a heat-bath with various coupling regimes and temperature show that the presented stochastic theory can be a valuable tool to simulate exactly the dynamics of open quantum systems. Links with stochastic Schroedinger equation method and possible extensions to "imaginary time" propagation are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact stochastic formulation for open quantum systems that replaces the full system-plus-environment dynamics with an integro-differential stochastic equation for the reduced system density alone, incorporating environment effects through a stochastic mean-field that is non-local in time. The method is exact for any coupling strength and temperature, provided the initial state is separable and the coupling operator allows explicit memory functions.

## Method

Starting from a previously developed stochastic mean-field (SMF) formulation where system and environment evolve via coupled stochastic equations, the author projects out the environment dynamics to obtain a closed stochastic integro-differential equation for the reduced system density. The environment influence enters through two memory functions that, for a heat-bath of harmonic oscillators, reduce to the standard two-time correlation functions of the environment involving the spectral density. The stochastic process uses complex noises with specific Ito rules that keep the densities non-Hermitian along individual paths but yield Hermitian results upon averaging.

## Key numbers

* For the spin-boson model with spectral density \(J(\omega) = \eta \omega \frac{\Delta_c^2}{\Delta_c^2 + \omega^2}\), \(\Delta_c = 5 \omega_0\), \(k_B T = 2 \hbar\omega_0\), and \(\hbar\varepsilon=0\): weak coupling \(\pi \eta = 0.2 \hbar\omega_0\) and strong coupling \(\pi \eta = 4 \hbar\omega_0\) were simulated.
* Numerical time steps used: \(\Delta t \omega_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{-3}\) for weak coupling and \(\Delta t \omega_0 = 2.2 \times 10^{-4}\) for strong coupling.
* Simulations used \(2 \times 10^4\) trajectories for the spin-boson results in Fig. 1, with computer time less than one hour (weak coupling) to several hours (strong coupling) on a standard personal computer.
* For the TCL2 comparison, simulations used \(4 \times 10^4\) trajectories with parameters \(\hbar\omega_0 = 0\), \(\Delta_c = 10 \varepsilon\), \(k_B T = 4 \hbar\varepsilon\) and \(k_B T = 20 \hbar\varepsilon\), and couplings \(\pi \eta = 0.2 \hbar\varepsilon\) and \(\pi \eta = 1.0 \hbar\varepsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the exact stochastic mean-field approach of Lacroix (2005) by deriving a reduced system equation that avoids explicit environment propagation, addressing the long-time statistical error growth that limited earlier exact stochastic methods. The results are compared with the hierarchical approach of Yan (2004) and the time-convolutionless (TCL) projection operator method of Breuer (1999, 2002); the stochastic scheme matches the hierarchical method for weak coupling and shows deviations for strong coupling, while the TCL2 method systematically underestimates damping as coupling increases. The approach enables exact simulation of open quantum systems for longer times than previously possible and can be extended to imaginary-time propagation and non-separable initial conditions.
