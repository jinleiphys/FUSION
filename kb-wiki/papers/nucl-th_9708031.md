# Integrability of the pairing hamiltonian

> We show that a many-body Hamiltonian that corresponds to a system of fermions interacting through a pairing force is an integrable problem, i.e. it has as many constants of the motion as degrees of freedom. At the classical level this implies that the Time-dependent Hartree-Fock- Bogoliubov dynamics is integrable and at the quantum level that there are conserved operators of two-body character which reduce to the number operators when the pairing strength vanishes. We display these operators explicitly and study in detail the three-level example.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pairing Hamiltonian for fermions interacting through a monopole pairing force is integrable for any number of single-particle levels, contrary to the expectation that extending the model beyond two levels would yield non-integrable dynamics. The integrability holds both classically (in the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation) and quantum mechanically, with the conserved operators reducing to the number operators when the pairing strength vanishes.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general hermitian two-body operator that conserves the total number of pairs and required it to commute with the pairing Hamiltonian, deriving relations among its coefficients. They then selected a specific set of operators \(R_i\) that commute with each other and with the Hamiltonian, and verified their independence from the Hamiltonian and total number operator. The classical limit was obtained through the time-dependent variational principle using coherent states, with the degeneracy \(\Omega_i\) of each level taken to infinity as the semiclassical parameter.

## Key numbers

- The three-level case with equal degeneracy \(\Omega_i = \Omega = 14\) and total number of pairs \(N = \Omega = 14\) yields a set of 120 levels, whose simultaneous eigenvalues of \(H\) and \(O_2\) lie on a regular grid.
- The classical Hamiltonian for the three-level case with \(\epsilon_2 = 0\) as energy reference is expressed in terms of scaled action variables \(I_1 = n_1/\Omega\) and \(I_2 = n_2/\Omega\), with the mid-shell value \({\cal N} = \Omega\).

## Context

This work extends the known integrability of the two-level pairing model (Hogasen 1984, Cambiaggio and Saraceno 1990, Broglia 1994) to the case of three or more levels, which was previously expected to be non-integrable by analogy with the particle-hole Lipkin model (Lipkin 1965) where three or more levels show chaotic behavior (Wilkinson 1987, Meredith 1988, Leboeuf 1991). The result enables the study of highly excited states and eigenfunctions with Poisson rather than GOE statistics (Bohigas 1984), and provides an integrable model for perturbation expansions that need not start from the non-interacting fermion system.
