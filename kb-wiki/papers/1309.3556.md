# Two-Nucleon Systems in a Finite Volume: (II) 3S1-3D1 Coupled Channels and the Deuteron

> The energy spectra of two nucleons in a cubic volume provide access to the two phase shifts and one mixing angle that define the S-matrix in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels containing the deuteron. With the aid of recently derived energy quantization conditions for such systems, and the known scattering parameters, these spectra are predicted for a range of volumes. It is found that extractions of the infinite-volume deuteron binding energy and leading scattering parameters, including the S-D mixing angle at the deuteron pole, are possible from Lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon systems with boosts of |P| <= 2pi sqrt{3}/L in volumes with 10 fm <~ L <~ 14 fm. The viability of extracting the asymptotic D/S ratio of the deuteron wavefunction from Lattice QCD calculations is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that extracting the S-D mixing angle at the deuteron pole from Lattice QCD calculations is feasible using boosted two-nucleon systems with momenta up to |P| ≤ 2π√3/L, but only if the calculations are performed in volumes with L between approximately 10 fm and 14 fm; the mixing angle cannot be reliably extracted from systems at rest because the bound-state energy in the T1 irrep of the cubic group is insensitive to the small mixing angle, with energy shifts from mixing not exceeding a few keV even in smaller volumes.

## Method

The authors used the recently derived finite-volume quantization conditions for coupled-channel systems (Briceno 2013) to predict the energy spectra of two nucleons in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels in a cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions. They employed the Blatt-Biedenharn parameterization of the S-matrix, fitting the phase shifts δ1α, δ1β, δ(3D2), δ(3D3) and mixing angle ε1 to six different phenomenological NN phase-shift analyses (PWA93, Nijm93, Nijm1, Nijm2, Reid93, ESC96) and continuing the fit functions to negative energies to reach the deuteron pole. The quantization conditions were decomposed into irreducible representations of the cubic, tetragonal, orthorhombic, and trigonal point groups corresponding to boost vectors d = (0,0,0), (0,0,1), (1,1,0), and (1,1,1), and the bound-state energies were computed as functions of the spatial volume L.

## Key numbers

- The infinite-volume deuteron binding energy is B_d^∞ = 2.224644(34) MeV (experimental value).
- The asymptotic D/S ratio of the deuteron wavefunction is η = 0.02713(6) (experimental value).
- The deuteron electric quadrupole moment is Q_d = 0.2859(3) fm^2 (experimental value).
- For the d = (0,0,1) system at L = 8 fm, the energy gap between the A2 and E irreps is approximately 80% of the infinite-volume deuteron binding energy, decreasing to about 5% at L = 14 fm.
- For the d = (1,1,0) system at L = 8 fm, the energy gap between the B1 and B2/B3 irreps is approximately 30% of the infinite-volume deuteron binding energy, decreasing to about 5% at L = 14 fm.
- The difference between the bound-state energy from the full T1 QC and that obtained with ε1 = 0 does not exceed a few keV in volumes L ≲ 9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism developed for coupled-channel systems (Detmold 2004, He 2005, Bernard 2008, Briceno 2012, Hansen 2012) to the specific case of the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels containing the deuteron, building on the general formalism for NN systems with arbitrary center-of-mass momenta and spin (Briceno 2013). Previous Lattice QCD analyses (Beane 2006, Beane 2012, Beane 2013, Yamazaki 2012) using Luscher's method treated the deuteron as purely S-wave, neglecting the D-wave admixture and introducing systematic uncertainty; this work quantifies that uncertainty and demonstrates that boosted systems with d = (0,0,1) and (1,1,0) provide enhanced sensitivity to the mixing angle, enabling its extraction from future LQCD calculations. The results enable precision determinations of the deuteron binding energy and scattering parameters from a single lattice volume using the six bound-state energies associated with center-of-mass momenta up to |P| ≤ 2π√3/L.
