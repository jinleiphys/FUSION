# Nuclear energy density functional and the nuclear alpha decay

> The nuclear $α$ decay of heavy nuclei is investigated based on the nuclear energy density functional, which leads to the $α$ potential inside the parent nucleus in terms of the proton and neutron density profiles of the daughter nucleus. We use the Skyrme force model, Gogny force model, and relativistic mean field model to get the nucleon density profiles inside heavy nuclei. Once the nucleon density profiles are determined, the parameters of the nuclear $α$ potential are fitted to the observed $α$ decay half-lives of heavy nuclei. This approach is then applied to predict unknown $α$ decay half-lives of heavy nuclei. To estimate the $Q$ values of unobserved $α$ decays, we make use of the liquid droplet model.

## Key claim

The authors develop a realistic α-particle potential based on nucleon density profiles from microscopic nuclear models, and they find that using realistic proton distributions improves the root-mean-square deviation in calculated α-decay half-lives compared to a simple Fermi density profile, though the approach still requires fitting six potential parameters to experimental half-life data.

## Method

The authors compute α-decay half-lives within the WKB approximation by constructing the α-cluster potential from nucleon density profiles obtained with three microscopic models: Skyrme SLy4, Gogny D1S, and relativistic mean-field DD-ME2. The nuclear potential for the α particle is expressed as a function of proton and neutron densities with six adjustable parameters, which are fitted to experimental α-decay half-lives of even-even nuclei. For unobserved decays, Q-values are estimated using a liquid droplet model with two parameter sets (global fit to 2336 nuclei, and fit to 100 nuclei with Z ≥ 100) and a local mass formula.

## Key numbers

- RMSD in log₁₀(halflife) for the three density models: 0.209 (SLy4), 0.198 (D1S), 0.218 (DD-ME2) for observed α decays
- LDM case I (global fit to 2336 nuclei) gives RMSD of 1.144 MeV for binding energies; case II (fit to 100 nuclei with Z ≥ 100) gives RMSD of 0.218 MeV for Q-values
- Local Q-value formula for Z ≥ 100 gives RMSD of 0.255 MeV
- For ²¹²Po decay, a 0.1 MeV difference in Q-value (Q_expt ≈ 8.95 MeV) results in about a factor of 1.7 difference in calculated half-life
- Predicted half-life for (122,307): 9.467×10⁻⁵ s (SLy4), 9.982×10⁻⁵ s (D1S), 6.999×10⁻⁵ s (DD-ME2) using LDM Q-value of 12.594 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lim et al. (2015) by replacing a simple Fermi density profile with realistic density distributions from Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean-field models, improving the RMSD in α-decay half-life calculations. The method enables predictions of α-decay half-lives for unobserved superheavy elements (Z up to 122), which is relevant for identifying new nuclides produced at rare isotope facilities. The authors compare with previous approaches such as the square well potential model of Buck et al. (1991, 1992), the cosh potential model of Buck et al. (1992), the unified fission model of Dong et al. (2010), and the double-folding model of Röpke et al. (2005, 2008).
