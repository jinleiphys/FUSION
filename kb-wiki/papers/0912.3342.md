# Parton rescattering effect on particle production in ultra-relativistic p+p collisions

> The parton rescattering effect on the charged particle production in ultra-relativistic p+p collisions is studied by the parton and hadron cascade model, PACIAE, based on PYTHIA. We have calculated charged particle pseudorapidity density ($dN_{ch}/dη$) at mid-rapidity and the pseudorapidity distribution in inelastic and non-single diffractive p+p collisions at $\sqrt s$=200, 900, 5500, and 14000 GeV with the PYTHIA and PACIAE models. The calculated results of $\sqrt s$=900 GeV are well compared with the ALICE data. The calculated $dN_{ch}/dη$ as a function of center-of-mass energy well meets with the experimental data as well. Comparing the PYTHIA results (without parton rescattering) with the PACIAE results (with parton rescattering), it turned out that the parton rescattering effect plays an important role and this effect increases with increasing center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim

The parton rescattering effect, as implemented in the PACIAE model, is important for reproducing experimental charged particle yields in p+p collisions, and this effect increases with increasing center-of-mass energy, suggesting that quark-gluon matter formation may be possible in p+p collisions at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA event generator (without parton rescattering) and the PACIAE model (with parton rescattering) to simulate inelastic (INEL) and non-single diffractive (NSD) p+p collisions. In PACIAE, string fragmentation is temporarily switched off to create a partonic initialization stage, then parton rescattering is performed using leading-order pQCD 2→2 cross sections, followed by hadronization via Lund string fragmentation and subsequent hadronic rescattering. All model parameters were kept at default values except the k factor, set to 3 to account for higher-order and non-perturbative QCD corrections.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity (|η|<0.5) for INEL p+p at √s=900 GeV: PYTHIA gives 2.59, PACIAE gives 2.80, compared to ALICE data of 3.10 ± 0.13 ± 0.22.
- Charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity for NSD p+p at √s=900 GeV: PYTHIA gives 3.15, PACIAE gives 3.33, compared to ALICE data of 3.51 ± 0.15 ± 0.25.
- The difference between PACIAE and PYTHIA NSD results increases from 5.1% at √s=200 GeV to 15.4% at 5500 GeV and then to 24.2% at 14000 GeV.
- The difference in charged particle pseudorapidity density between NSD p+p and p+p collisions at √s=900 GeV is about 4.5% in PYTHIA and 4.9% in PACIAE.

## Context

This work extends the study of parton rescattering effects from heavy-ion collisions to ultra-relativistic p+p collisions, using the PACIAE model developed by Sa (2007) based on PYTHIA. The results are compared directly to the first ALICE measurements at √s=900 GeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2009) and UA5 data (UA5 Collaboration, 1987). The finding that parton rescattering effects increase with energy supports the argument by Bozek (2009), Casalderrey-Solana (2009), and Chaudhuri (2009) that elliptic flow may be measurable in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at LHC energies, and suggests that quark-gluon matter formation probability in p+p collisions increases with energy, enabling future searches for QCD medium effects in small systems.
