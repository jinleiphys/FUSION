# Reaction mechanisms for weakly-bound, stable nuclei and unstable, halo nuclei on medium-mass targets

> An experimental overview of reactions induced by the stable, but weakly-bound nuclei 6Li, 7Li and 9Be, and by the exotic, halo nuclei 6He, 8B, 11Be and 17F on medium-mass targets, such as 58Ni, 59Co or 64Zn, is presented. Existing data on elastic scattering, total reaction cross sections, fusion processes, breakup and transfer channels are discussed in the framework of a CDCC approach taking into account the breakup degree of freedom.

# Reaction mechanisms for weakly-bound, stable nuclei and unstable, halo nuclei on medium-mass targets

## Key claim

The paper argues that while weakly-bound and halo projectiles produce larger total reaction and fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies than predicted by one-dimensional barrier penetration models, the halo nature of projectiles such as 6He does not enhance fusion probability as much as anticipated; instead, one- and two-neutron transfer channels play a prominent role, and for stable weakly-bound projectiles the effect of non-conventional transfer or stripping processes appears less significant.

## Method

The authors compiled and compared existing experimental data on elastic scattering, total reaction cross sections, fusion processes, breakup, and transfer channels for reactions induced by stable weakly-bound nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 9Be) and exotic halo nuclei (6He, 8B, 11Be, 17F) on medium-mass targets (58Ni, 59Co, 64Zn). They analyzed these data using Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations that incorporate the breakup degree of freedom, and for the 6Li+59Co benchmark reaction they performed statistical-model calculations with CASCADE (CACARIZO), PACE2, and EMPIRE-II codes to compare with experimental fusion-evaporation residue excitation functions.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross sections for halo systems 6He+64Zn, 8B+58Ni, and 6He+209Bi lie well above the systematic behavior defined by tightly-bound systems such as 16O+64Zn (blue curve in Fig. 1)
- Preliminary total reaction cross sections for 11Be+64Zn are at least twice those for 9Be+64Zn
- For 6Li+59Co at Elab = 29.6 MeV, a very preliminary estimate of the total experimental incomplete-fusion (ICF) cross section is approximately 150 mb
- The statistical-model simulation with CASCADE for 6Li+59Co fusion-evaporation residue channels agrees within 30% with both PACE2 and EMPIRE-II calculations
- The 6Li excitation energy range for direct breakup (DBU) CDCC calculations spans from E* = 1.48 MeV (breakup threshold) to E* = 2.10 MeV in the continuum

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparisons of reaction mechanisms for weakly-bound and halo projectiles previously reported by Beck (2007) and Aguilera (2009), and addresses conflicting conclusions about whether breakup enhances or suppresses fusion. The paper contrasts the behavior of halo systems (6He, 8B, 11Be) with stable weakly-bound systems (6Li, 7Li, 9Be) and notes that 17F+58Ni total reaction cross sections follow the same trend as tightly-bound 16O+58Ni, indicating nuclear structure effects remain important. The results enable future experiments with high-intensity radioactive ion beams at facilities such as SPIRAL/GANIL, Rex-Isolde, and MSU/NSCL, including a proposed active-target time-projection chamber study of 8B+40Ar and 11Be+40Ar reactions.
