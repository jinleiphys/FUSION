# Supercurrent Flow in NJL_{2+1} at High Baryon Density

> We present results of numerical simulations of the 2+1d Nambu -- Jona-Lasinio model with non-zero baryon chemical potential mu and spatially-varying complex diquark source strength j. By choosing arg(j) to vary smoothly through 2 pi across the spatial extent of the lattice, a baryon number current is induced which in the high density phase remains non-vanishing as |j|->0; we are hence able to extract a quantity characteristic of a superfluid known as the helicity modulus. We also study supercurrent flow at non-zero temperature and estimate the critical temperature at which the normal phase is restored, which is consistent with the conventional picture for thin-film superfluids in which the transition is viewed in terms of vortex -- anti-vortex unbinding.

# Supercurrent Flow in NJL_{2+1} at High Baryon Density

## Key claim

The 2+1d Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at high baryon density exhibits superfluid behavior characterized by a non-vanishing helicity modulus, even though long-range order is absent due to the Coleman-Mermin-Wagner theorem; the supercurrent persists in the limit of vanishing diquark source strength, consistent with a critical phase analogous to the low-temperature phase of the 2d X-Y model rather than orthodox BCS symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of the NJL model in 2+1 dimensions with two staggered fermion flavors, a non-zero baryon chemical potential μ, and a spatially-varying complex diquark source j whose argument varies smoothly through 2π across the lattice. By measuring the induced spacelike baryon number current J₂ for fixed twist integers (n₁,n₂) = (0,1) over a range of source magnitudes j₀, they extrapolated to j₀→0 to extract the helicity modulus Υ, and repeated the procedure at various temperatures by varying the temporal lattice extent L_t.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at μ=0: Σa = 0.71 (sets the scale)
- Critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration: 0.65 < μ_c a < 0.68
- Baryon density at μa = 0.8: n_B a² ≈ 0.25
- Helicity modulus at μa = 0.8 after thermodynamic and j₀→0 extrapolation: Υ = 0.1413(14), giving Υ/Σ = 0.200(2)
- Critical temporal extent estimated from fixed j₀ = 0.025 data: L_tc = 7.25(95), yielding Υ/T_c = 1.02(13) compared to the Kosterlitz-Thouless prediction of 0.637

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the NJL model in 2+1 dimensions by Hands, Morrison, and Strouthos (2001) which found algebraic decay of diquark correlators and no long-range order, consistent with the Coleman-Mermin-Wigner theorem for 2d systems. The results provide direct verification of superfluid behavior via the helicity modulus, supporting the interpretation that the high-density phase is a critical phase analogous to the low-temperature phase of the 2d X-Y model described by Kosterlitz and Thouless (1973). The measured helicity modulus is an order of magnitude smaller than the value predicted by a Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation assuming Goldstone boson dominance, confirming that the scalar diquark excitations are strongly self-interacting rather than weakly-interacting Goldstone modes. The temperature study enables future work to determine whether the model lies in the Kosterlitz-Thouless universality class or a different one due to the presence of massless fermion degrees of freedom.
