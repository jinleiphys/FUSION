# Odd-even shape staggering and kink structure of charge radii of Hg isotopes by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> We examined the shape staggering of relative charge radii in $^{180 - 186}$Hg isotopes, which was first measured in 1977 and recently confirmed using advanced spectroscopy techniques. To understand the nuclear structure underlying this phenomenon, we employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). Our analysis revealed that the shape staggering can be attributed to nuclear shape transition in the Hg isotopes. Specifically, we demonstrated that prolate shapes of $^{181,183,185}$Hg lead to an increase in the charge radii compared to oblate shapes of $^{180,182,184,186}$Hg isotopes. We explained the nuclear shape staggering in terms of the evolution of occupation probability (OP) of $ν1 i_{13/2}$, $ν1 h_{9/2}$, $π1 h_{9/2}$, and $π3 s_{1/2}$ states. Additionally, we clarified the kink structure of the charge radii in the Hg isotopes near $N = 126$ magic shell does not come from the change of the OP of $π1 h_{9/2}$ state, but mainly by the increase of the OPs of $ν1 i_{11/2}$ and $ν2 g_{9/2}$ states.

# Odd-even shape staggering and kink structure of charge radii of Hg isotopes by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

## Key claim

The shape staggering of charge radii in Hg isotopes (mass 180-186) is attributed to nuclear shape transition, where prolate shapes of odd isotopes 181,183,185Hg lead to increased charge radii compared to oblate shapes of even isotopes 180,182,184,186Hg. The kink structure near N=126 arises primarily from increased occupation probabilities of neutron states ν1i11/2 and ν2g9/2, not from changes in the π1h9/2 proton state.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with an automatic blocking method to treat odd-even nuclei. They calculated total binding energy curves as a function of quadrupole deformation β2 for Hg isotopes 179-189, and computed relative changes in mean-square charge radii δ⟨r²⟩ with respect to 198Hg. Occupation probabilities of key single-particle states (ν1i13/2, ν1h9/2, ν2f7/2, π1h9/2, π3s1/2) were analyzed to explain the shape staggering and kink structure.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy and binding energy per nucleon show average deviations less than 1.5 MeV and 0.02 MeV respectively compared to experimental data from AME2020
- Deformation parameters for 181Hg: β2 = 0.3458 (prolate); for 182Hg: β2 = -0.1360 (oblate); for 183Hg: β2 = 0.3367 (prolate); for 184Hg: β2 = -0.1430 (oblate); for 185Hg: β2 = 0.3118 (prolate); for 186Hg: β2 = -0.1460 (oblate)
- Charge radii discrepancies for odd isotopes are maximally 0.05 fm
- Relative charge radii data for oblate-deformed isotopes are reproduced within δ⟨r²⟩ ≤ 0.25 fm²
- Pairing strength V0 = -325.0 MeV fm³ with pairing window of 100 MeV and saturation density ρsat = 0.152 fm⁻³
- Energy cutoff Ecut⁺ = 300 MeV, angular momentum cutoff Jmax = (23/2)ℏ, Legendre expansion truncation λmax = 8

## Context

This work extends previous experimental measurements of Hg charge radii first reported in 1977 and recently confirmed by Good (2021) and Marsh (2018) using advanced spectroscopy at CERN-ISOLDE. The DRHBc results are compared with non-relativistic Skyrme density functional theory calculations using SIII, SKI3, and SLy4 parameter sets, showing superior performance for the shape staggering pattern. The study also relates to Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations by Otsuka (2020) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by More (2022), while noting that explicit Pauli blocking treatment in odd nuclei was absent in those earlier works. The findings enable future investigations of shape staggering and kink structures in K, Ca, Pb, and Au isotopes near magic shells using the DRHBc framework.
