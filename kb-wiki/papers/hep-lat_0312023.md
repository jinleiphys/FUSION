# Pion structure from improved lattice QCD: form factor and charge radius at low masses

> The charge form factor of the pion is calculated in lattice QCD. The non-perturbatively improved Sheikholeslami-Wohlert action is used together with the $\mathcal{O}(a)$ improved vector current. Other choices for the current are examined. The form factor is extracted for pion masses from 970 MeV down to 360 MeV and for momentum transfers $Q^2 \leq 2 \mathrm{GeV}^2$. The mean square charge radius is extracted, compared to previous determinations and its extrapolation to lower masses discussed.

## Key claim
The paper reports lattice QCD calculations of the pion charge form factor using a non-perturbatively improved action and current, which reduces discretization errors to O(a^2). The authors find that the form factor results depend significantly on the choice of vector current operator, especially at lower pion masses and higher momentum transfers, and that the improved current yields results closer to vector meson dominance predictions at lighter pion masses.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 24^3 x 32 lattice at beta=6.0 using the non-perturbatively improved Sheikholeslami-Wohlert action with c_SW=1.769. They computed 2-point and 3-point Green's functions for pion masses from 970 MeV down to 360 MeV, using five values of the hopping parameter. The form factor was extracted by simultaneously fitting the 2-point and 3-point functions, including contributions from the ground state and one excited state, with the improved vector current (including the tensor term) to remove O(a) discretization errors.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses studied: 970(4) MeV, 780(4) MeV, 670(4) MeV, 540(6) MeV, and 360(9) MeV
- Momentum transfers covered: Q^2 <= 2 GeV^2
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.105 fm (from Edwards 1998)
- Minimal momentum used: p_i^2 = p_f^2 = 0.48 GeV^2
- Fitted vector meson masses (m_V) from monopole form: 1086(26) MeV at m_pi=970 MeV, 833(75) MeV at m_pi=360 MeV
- Mean square charge radii from monopole fits: 0.197(9) fm^2 at m_pi=970 MeV, 0.34(6) fm^2 at m_pi=360 MeV
- Bethe-Salpeter mean square radii (with w=2): 0.1414(2) fm^2 at m_pi=970 MeV, 0.1528(4) fm^2 at m_pi=360 MeV
- Physical value for comparison: <r^2> = 0.439(8) fm^2 (Amendolia 1986)

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the pion form factor by Martinelli (1988) and Draper (1989), which were limited to pion masses around 1 GeV, by reaching down to 360 MeV and using O(a) improved techniques. The results enable systematic study of the pion mass dependence of the charge radius and test vector meson dominance predictions. The authors note that the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude method (Gupta 1993) yields significantly smaller radii than the form factor method, suggesting the center-of-mass treatment affects the interpretation. Future work could extrapolate the charge radius to the physical pion mass and compare with experimental data.
