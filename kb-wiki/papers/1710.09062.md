# Mass hierarchy and energy scaling of the Tsallis--Pareto parameters in hadron productions at RHIC and LHC energies

> The latest, high-accuracy identified hadron spectra measurements in high-energy nuclear collisions led us to the investigation of the strongly interacting particles and collective effects in small systems. Since microscopical processes result in a statistical Tsallis-Pareto distribution, the fit parameters $q$ and $T$ are well suited for identifying system size scalings and initial conditions. Moreover, parameter values provide information on the deviation from the extensive, Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics in finite-volumes. We apply here the fit procedure developed in our earlier study for proton-proton collisions. The observed mass and $\sqrt{s}$ energy trends in the hadron production are compared to RHIC dAu and LHC pPb data in different centrality/multiplicity classes. Here we present new results on mass hierarchy in pp and pA from light to heavy hadrons.

# Mass hierarchy and energy scaling of the Tsallis–Pareto parameters in hadron productions at RHIC and LHC energies

## Key claim

The Tsallis–Pareto fit parameters q and T extracted from identified hadron spectra in pp, dAu, and pPb collisions exhibit a mass hierarchy where higher-mass hadrons yield higher T and lower q (closer to 1), but this trend is clearest only for low-multiplicity events; high-multiplicity central collisions show deviations from the pp-like scaling, especially for heavy hadrons.

## Method

The authors fit the Tsallis–Pareto distribution d²N/(N_ev 2π p_T dp_T dy) ∝ [1 + (q-1) m_T/T]^{-1/(q-1)} to published identified hadron spectra from STAR dAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and ALICE pPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, using the same fitting procedure developed earlier for pp collisions (Bíró et al. 2016, 2017). Fits were performed in multiple centrality bins (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-100% for STAR; 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-80%, 80-100% for ALICE) over the widest possible p_T ranges using a minimal χ² method within the ROOT framework.

## Key numbers

- STAR dAu data at √s_NN = 200 GeV: fits performed in three centrality classes (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-100%) with data-to-fit agreement of 20%
- ALICE pPb data at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: fits performed in seven centrality classes (0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-80%, 80-100%) with data-to-fit agreement of 40-50%
- pp results from earlier work (Bíró et al. 2017) covered energies from 62.4 GeV to 7 TeV, shown as empty markers and shaded area in the T vs (q-1) parameter space
- The dAu data points accompany the lowest energy pp fit parameters, attributed to low multiplicity or limited p_T range

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of π, K, and p(anti-p) spectra in pp collisions (Bíró et al. 2016, 2017) to include heavier hadron species and to compare with dAu and pPb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The results are consistent with previous center-of-mass energy scaling investigations for peripheral (pp-like) centrality classes, but deviations appear for central high-multiplicity collisions. The observed mass hierarchy and strangeness enhancement trends support the idea of quark-gluon plasma like, close-to-thermalized matter in small colliding systems (pp, dAu, pPb), and enable further study of how non-extensive statistics parameters connect to system size and initial conditions in small systems.
