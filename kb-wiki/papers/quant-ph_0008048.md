# New Lower Bound on Fermion Binding Energies

> We derive a new lower bound for the ground state energy $E^{\rm F}(N,S)$ of N fermions with total spin S in terms of binding energies $E^{\rm F}(N-1,S \pm 1/2)$ of (N-1) fermions. Numerical examples are provided for some simple short-range or confining potentials.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new lower bound for the ground-state energy of N fermions with total spin S, expressed in terms of the binding energies of (N-1) fermions with neighboring spins S ± 1/2, which improves upon previous bounds for small systems but is not always saturated for the harmonic oscillator and does not reach the correct large-N behavior when iterated.

## Method

The authors use group-theoretical techniques, decomposing the translation-invariant N-body Hamiltonian into a sum of (N-1)-body Hamiltonians with modified constituent mass, then applying the variational principle with the N-body ground state as trial wave function. The symmetry structure of the wave function under the permutation group S_N is incorporated via Young tableaux and Yamanouchi symbols, allowing the N-body state to be expressed as a sum over (N-1)-body states with specific orbital symmetries. Numerical tests are performed using a Gaussian expansion method with stochastic optimization for various short-range and confining potentials.

## Key numbers

* For N=3 electrons in a quantum dot with mass m=1, charge e=1, spin S=3/2, and orbital momentum L^P=1^+, the bound gives epsilon >= 0.1668 for oscillator frequency omega=0.01 (exact value 0.1680) and epsilon >= 54.968 for omega=10 (exact value 54.973)
* For N=4 electrons with S=2, L^P=0^-, the bound gives epsilon >= 0.297 for omega=0.01 (exact value 0.299) and epsilon >= 84.895 for omega=10 (exact value 84.907)
* The bound is saturated at q=2 for N=3 fermions and for some spin configurations of N=4 with power-law potentials r^q

## Context

This work extends the Hall-Post bound (Hall and Post, 1967) from bosons to fermions and improves upon the earlier bound by Levy-Leblond (1966), which neglected the overall kinetic energy of (N-1)-body subsystems and was never saturated. The result also improves the bound by Basdevant and Martin (1996), which was restricted to confining potentials r^q with q >= 1 and gave the best results near q=2 but deteriorated quickly for other potentials. The new bound enables applications to quantum dot systems with Coulomb interactions and external harmonic confinement.
