# Nucleon form factors and spin content in a quark-diquark model with a pion cloud

> We propose a new model of the nucleon in which quark-diquark configurations immersed in a pion cloud are treated in a manner consistent with Poincaré invariance. With suitably chosen parameters, the computations employing this model reproduce the measured electromagnetic form factors and the quark-spin contribution to the total nucleon angular momentum.

## Key claim
A quark-diquark model of the nucleon with a pion cloud, treated consistently with Poincaré invariance, can simultaneously reproduce the measured electromagnetic form factors and yield a quark-spin contribution to the total nucleon angular momentum that is consistent with the experimentally observed small fraction, provided the model parameters are suitably chosen.

## Method
The authors construct a light-front wave function for the nucleon consisting of quark-scalar-diquark and quark-axial-vector-diquark components, with two invariant forms for each component. A pion cloud is added via a single pion loop around the bare nucleon, with the pion-nucleon vertex regulated by a Gaussian form factor. The ten model parameters (quark and diquark masses, wave function shape parameters, and the pion-nucleon cutoff) are determined by minimizing a chi-squared function comparing the computed Dirac and Pauli form factors to the empirical parametrization of Kelly (2004) over the range Q² ∈ [0,10] GeV².

## Key numbers
- The fit yields a chi-squared value of 0.078516 with n_q = 11 points in Q².
- Constituent quark mass m = 0.191 GeV; scalar diquark mass M_s = 0.414 GeV; axial-vector diquark mass M_a = 0.167 GeV.
- The computed proton magnetic moment is μ_p = 2.794 μ_N (experimental: 2.79 μ_N) and the neutron magnetic moment is μ_n = -1.849 μ_N (experimental: -1.91 μ_N).
- The ratio G_Ep/G_Mp crosses zero at Q² ≈ 12.3 GeV².
- The pion-nucleon vertex cutoff parameter Λ = 1.035 GeV; the pion form factor uses Λ_π² = 0.5 GeV².
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is taken as g_πN = 13.5.

## Context
This work extends the earlier three-constituent-quark light-front model of Frank, Jennings, and Miller (1995) and its pion-cloud extension by Miller (2002) and Matevosyan, Miller, and Thomas (2005), which reproduced form factors but gave a quark-spin contribution of about 75% of the nucleon total angular momentum, far above the measured value of approximately 30%. By using a more sophisticated quark-diquark wave function with both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, the present model achieves agreement with form factor data while also accounting for the small quark-spin fraction, consistent with the findings of Thomas (2008) and Myhrer and Thomas (2007). The flavor decomposition of the form factors is compared to the analysis of Cates et al. (2011).
