# Dilepton photoproduction on a deuteron target

> We investigate the sensitivity of the cross section for lepton pair production off a deuteron target, $γd \to l^+ l^- d$, to the deuteron charge radius. We show that for small momentum transfers the Bethe-Heitler process dominates, and that it is sensitive to the charge radius such that a cross section ratio measurement of about $0.1 \%$ relative accuracy could give a deuteron charge radius more accurate that the current electron scattering value and sufficiently accurate to distinguish between the electronic and muonic atomic values.

## Key claim
The cross section for the Bethe-Heitler process in gamma d -> l+ l- d is sensitive to the deuteron charge radius such that a cross section ratio measurement with about 0.1% relative accuracy could yield a deuteron charge radius more precise than the current electron scattering value and sufficiently accurate to distinguish between the electronic and muonic atomic values, provided the measurement is performed at small momentum transfers where the Bethe-Heitler mechanism dominates and the Compton contribution is more than two orders of magnitude smaller.

## Method
The authors calculated the Bethe-Heitler differential cross section for gamma d -> l+ l- d using deuteron electromagnetic form factors (Coulomb monopole G_C, magnetic dipole G_M, and Coulomb quadrupole G_Q) from the parameterization of Abbott et al. (2000) and Sick and Trautmann (1998). They integrated over lepton angles to obtain the cross section differential in momentum transfer t and dilepton invariant mass M_ll^2, and compared the sensitivity to different deuteron charge radii by modifying the G_C form factor via a linear correction term. The Compton contribution was estimated using a once-subtracted dispersion relation for the forward real Compton amplitude, with the imaginary part obtained from photoproduction total cross sections and structure function fits from Bosted and Christy (2007) and Capella et al. (1994).

## Key numbers
- The deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium Lamb shift is 2.12562(78) fm, and from atomic deuterium spectroscopy is 2.1415(45) fm, showing a 3.5 sigma discrepancy between these values.
- The deuteron charge radius from e-d elastic scattering (Sick and Trautmann, 1998) is 2.130(10) fm, with an uncertainty too large to distinguish between the two spectroscopic values.
- The Compton contribution to the gamma d -> l+ l- d cross section is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than the Bethe-Heitler contribution at beam energy 0.65 GeV and the momentum transfer range studied.
- The cross section ratio R(t, t0) is defined relative to a reference value t0 = -0.01 GeV^2, and a measurement with 0.1% relative accuracy would allow distinguishing between different deuteron radius fits.

## Context
This work extends the approach previously applied to protons by Pauk (2015) to the deuteron case, where the cross sections are smaller due to the faster falloff with |t| of the deuteron form factors. The deuteron charge radius puzzle involves a 3.5 sigma discrepancy between values from muonic deuterium Lamb shift (Pohl, 2016) and ordinary deuterium atomic spectroscopy (Pohl, 2016), while the elastic scattering value from Sick and Trautmann (1998) has too large an error bar to distinguish between them. The proposed photoproduction measurement would provide a complementary method to elastic electron scattering, with the advantage that a fixed outgoing deuteron angle allows better experimental calibration than elastic scattering experiments where different momentum transfers require different scattering angles for a given beam energy.
