# On $X(3872)$ production in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We have determined the production cross sections of the $X(3872)$ state in the reactions $\bar D D\toπX$, $\bar D^* D\to πX$ and $\bar D^* D^*\toπX$, information which is useful for studies of the $X(3872)$ meson abundance in heavy ion collisions. We construct a formalism considering $X$ as a molecular bound state of $\bar D^0 D^{*0} - \textrm{c.c}$, $D^- D^{*+} - \textrm{c.c}$ and $D^-_s D^{*+}_s - \textrm{c.c}$. To obtain the amplitudes related to these processes we have made use of effective field Lagrangians. The evaluation of the cross section of the processes involving $D^*$ meson(s) requires the calculation of an anomalous vertex, $X\bar D^* D^*$, which has been obtained by considering triangular loops motivated by the molecular nature of $X(3872)$. Proceeding in this way, we have evaluated the cross section for the reaction $\bar D^* D\to πX$, and find that the diagrams involving the $X\bar D^* D^*$ vertex give a large contribution. Encouraged by this finding we estimate the $X\bar D^* D^*$ coupling, which turns out to be $1.95\pm 0.22$. We then use it to obtain the cross section for the reaction $\bar D^* D^*\toπX$ and find that, in this case too, the $X\bar D^* D^*$ vertex is relevant. We also discuss the role of the charged components of $X$ in the determination of the production cross sections.

## Key claim

The production cross sections for X(3872) in heavy ion collisions are calculated for the reactions D̅D → πX, D̅*D → πX, and D̅*D* → πX, with the finding that the anomalous XD̅*D* vertex (previously neglected) gives a large contribution to the cross sections, and that both neutral and charged components of X must be included despite the much smaller binding energy of the charged components.

## Method

The authors construct effective field Lagrangians with SU(4) symmetry, treating X(3872) as a molecular bound state of D̅0D*0, D−D*+, and D−sD*+s pairs. For reactions involving D* mesons, the anomalous XD̅*D* vertex is calculated via triangular loop diagrams motivated by the molecular nature of X, and the resulting coupling is then used to compute cross sections for D̅*D* → πX. The cross sections are evaluated in the center-of-mass frame using the standard formula involving the Mandelstam variable t and the Källén function.

## Key numbers

* The XD̅*D* coupling constant is estimated to be 1.95 ± 0.22.
* The coupling of X to D−D*+ is 3638/√2 MeV, to D̅0D*0 is 3663/√2 MeV, and to D−sD*+s is 3395/√2 MeV.
* The binding energy of the neutral D̅0D*0 component is approximately 0.2 MeV, while the charged D−D*+ component has a binding energy of about 8 MeV.
* The pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV is used in the coupling calculations.
* The PPV coupling for vertices involving D and D* mesons is gPPV ≈ 9, yielding a decay width for D*+ → D0π+ of 71 keV, consistent with the experimental result of (65 ± 15) keV.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of the ExHIC collaboration (Cho et al., 2011a, 2011b) by including anomalous vertices (πD*D* and XD̅*D*) that were neglected in earlier studies, and by incorporating charged components of X following the approach of Daniel et al. (2010, 2011) which showed that short-range interactions require inclusion of both neutral and charged components. The results enable more accurate predictions of X(3872) abundance in heavy ion collisions, potentially distinguishing between molecular and tetraquark configurations through coalescence formalism as proposed by Cho et al. (2011c).
