# Covariant calculation of strange decays of baryon resonances

> We present results for kaon decay widths of baryon resonances from a relativistic study with constituent quark models. The calculations are done in the point-form of Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics with a spectator-model decay operator. We obtain covariant predictions of the Goldstone-boson-exchange and a variant of the one-gluon-exchange constituent quark models for all kaon decay widths of established baryon resonances. They are generally characterized by underestimating the available experimental data. In particular, the widths of kaon decays with increasing strangeness in the baryon turn out to be extremely small. We also consider the nonrelativistic limit, leading to the familiar elementary emission model, and demonstrate the importance of relativistic effects. It is found that the nonrelativistic approach evidently misses sensible influences from Lorentz boosts and some essential spin-coupling terms.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first covariant (relativistic) predictions for kaon decay widths of baryon resonances using constituent quark models, finding that these theoretical results generally underestimate the available experimental data, with widths for decays that increase strangeness in the baryon being extremely small (essentially vanishing).

## Method

The authors performed calculations in the point-form of Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics using a spectator-model decay operator (the point-form spectator model, PFSM). They employed two dynamical constituent quark models: the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) CQM and a relativistic variant of the Bhaduri-Cohler-Nogami one-gluon-exchange (OGE) CQM, computing kaon decay widths for all established N, Delta, Lambda, Sigma, and Xi resonances below 2 GeV. They also considered the nonrelativistic limit of the PFSM, which reduces to the elementary emission model (EEM), to demonstrate the importance of relativistic effects including Lorentz boosts and full spin-coupling terms.

## Key numbers

- For Lambda(1520) -> NK decay: relativistic GBE CQM prediction with theoretical mass gives 12 MeV, with experimental mass gives 6 MeV; experimental value is (7.02 +/- 0.16) MeV
- For Lambda(1670) -> NK decay: relativistic GBE CQM prediction with theoretical mass gives 0.3 MeV, with experimental mass gives 0.4 MeV; experimental value is (8.75 +/- 1.75) MeV
- For N(1650) -> Lambda K decay (increasing strangeness): relativistic GBE CQM prediction with theoretical mass gives approximately 0 MeV, with experimental mass gives 0.1 MeV; experimental value is (11.6 +/- 6.6) MeV
- For N(1710) -> Sigma K decay: relativistic OGE CQM prediction with theoretical mass gives 389 MeV, but with experimental mass gives 27 MeV; no experimental value quoted
- For Xi(1820) -> Lambda K decay: relativistic GBE CQM with theoretical mass gives 2.7 MeV, with experimental mass gives 3.5 MeV; PDG reports this channel as "large"
- For Xi(1820) -> Sigma K decay: relativistic GBE CQM with theoretical mass gives 4.1 MeV, with experimental mass gives 5.1 MeV; PDG reports this channel as "small"

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic studies of pion and eta decays of light and strange baryon resonances (Melde 2005, Melde 2006) to the kaon channels, providing the first covariant quark-model results for strange decays. It contrasts with earlier nonrelativistic or relativized approaches such as those by Koniuk and Isgur (1980) using the elementary emission model, and Capstick and Roberts (1998) using a quark-pair-creation operator, which introduced additional parametrizations to fit data. The results establish a consistent deficiency across all mesonic decay channels (pi, eta, K) that the authors suggest may be addressed by including additional mesonic degrees of freedom or improving the decay operator beyond the spectator model.
