# Constraints on $Σ$ Nucleus Dynamics from Dirac Phenomenology of $Σ^-$ Atoms

> Strong interaction level shifts and widths in $Σ^-$ atoms are analyzed by using a $Σ$ nucleus optical potential constructed within the relativistic mean field approach. The analysis leads to potentials with a repulsive real part in the nuclear interior. The data are sufficient to establish the size of the isovector meson--hyperon coupling. Implications to $Σ$ hypernuclei are discussed.

# Constraints on Σ Nucleus Dynamics from Dirac Phenomenology of Σ⁻ Atoms

## Key claim

The Σ⁻ atomic data, when analyzed within the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach, require a Σ-nucleus optical potential that is repulsive in the nuclear interior with only a shallow attractive pocket near the nuclear surface, and this repulsion precludes the existence of bound Σ hypernuclear states for most nuclei. The data are sufficient to determine the isovector meson-hyperon coupling ratio α_ρ ≈ 2/3, but the isoscalar coupling ratio α_ω must be at least 2/3 to achieve acceptable fits, ruling out weak ω-Σ coupling.

## Method

The authors constructed Schrödinger-equivalent Σ-nucleus optical potentials from Dirac scalar and vector potentials within the RMF approach, using linear (Horowitz-Serot) and nonlinear (NL1) parametrizations for the nucleon Lagrangian. The hyperon coupling ratios α_σ, α_ω, and α_ρ (relative to nucleon couplings) were fitted to experimental strong-interaction level shifts and widths in Σ⁻ atoms, using Si (N=Z) to fix isoscalar parameters and Pb (N>Z) to determine the isovector coupling. A phenomenological imaginary potential proportional to the proton density was used for the absorptive part, and the fits were compared to earlier density-dependent phenomenological analyses using more realistic single-particle nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- The earlier phenomenological Σ-nucleus potential well depths were −Re V_opt^Σ(0) ≈ 25–30 MeV and −Im V_opt^Σ(0) ≈ 10–15 MeV, but the RMF analysis yields potentials that become repulsive in the nuclear interior.
- The isovector coupling ratio α_ρ ≈ 2/3 was determined unambiguously from fits to the Pb data across all RMF parametrizations.
- For α_ω = 1/3, the fit failed to achieve χ² below 26.5; acceptable fits (χ² ≈ 23) required α_ω ≥ 2/3.
- The Σ-nucleus isovector potential V_1^(Σ) ≈ 80 MeV was derived from the Pb data, compared to estimates of 55–60 MeV from other approaches.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the Coulomb-generated Σ⁻ harmonic oscillator binding energies (without strong interaction) are: 1s: 20.35 MeV, 1p: 16.92 MeV, 1d: 13.63 MeV, 2s: 13.83 MeV, 1f: 10.58 MeV, 2p: 11.11 MeV.
- Including the RMF optical potential (linear model, α_ω = 2/3) shifts these levels by 2–7.5 MeV to lower binding; the 1s level becomes very broad (≈ 36 MeV), while other levels have widths of 1.2–2.6 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator spacing ħω = 3.25 ± 0.03 MeV between ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analysis of Σ⁻ atoms by Batty et al. (1995), which suggested a repulsive interior potential but could not determine the isovector component. By using more realistic single-particle nuclear densities and the RMF framework, the authors independently confirm the repulsive interior and establish the isovector coupling. The results contradict earlier expectations of attractive Σ-nucleus potentials from analyses of Σ hypernuclear spectra in (K⁻,π⁺) reactions, and are consistent with the negative results of recent searches for Σ hypernuclear bound states. The finding that α_ρ ≈ 2/3 differs from the SU(3) pure F-coupling value of α_ρ = 1, but the derived isovector potential V_1^(Σ) ≈ 80 MeV is comparable to estimates from the Nijmegen Model D and from ¹²C(K⁻,π⁺) phenomenology. The work enables future searches for Coulomb-assisted Σ⁻ states in heavy nuclei, though the authors note that cross sections for exciting such states are expected to be small.
