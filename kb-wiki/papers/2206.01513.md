# Electron Mass Singularities in Semileptonic Kaon Decays

> We show that recent improvements in the $O(α)$ long-distance quantum electrodynamics (QED) corrections to the radiative inclusive $K_{e3}$ decay rate using the Sirlin representation are free from infrared divergences and collinear electron mass singularities in the limit $m_e\rightarrow0$, as predicted by the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg theorem. We also verify that in massless QED with the simultaneous dimensional regularization of QED photon infrared divergences and electron mass singularities leads to the same result for the inclusive rate in the limit of four space-time dimensions. The equivalence of the two approaches results in part from an interesting interplay between a small chirality-breaking effect in the massless electron limit and the generalization of space-time algebra and phase space integrals to $d>4$ dimensions. Our finding supports the small theoretical uncertainty claimed for $K_{e3}$ radiative inclusive rates and reaffirms its utility in precision unitarity tests of the quark mixing matrix.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recent \(O(\alpha)\) long-distance quantum electrodynamics corrections to the radiative inclusive \(K_{e3}\) decay rate, computed using the Sirlin representation, are indeed free from both infrared divergences and collinear electron mass singularities in the limit \(m_e \rightarrow 0\), as required by the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg theorem. This verification was not directly tested in the original numerical calculations, which used the physical electron mass as input.

## Method

The authors employ two complementary regularization approaches to verify the cancellation of singularities in the total radiative inclusive \(K_{e3}\) decay rate. First, they use a "mass-expansion" method, extending analytically-calculable terms in both virtual and real corrections and expanding them with respect to the electron mass \(m_e\) to isolate logarithmic divergences. Second, they apply dimensional regularization (DR) with space-time dimension \(d = 4 - \epsilon\), treating both the photon and electron as massless from the start, and compare the finite results from both methods in the limit \(\epsilon \rightarrow 0\) and \(m_e \rightarrow 0\).

## Key numbers

- The current determination of \(|V_{us}|\) from \(K_{\ell 3}\) decays is \(0.22308(55)\), while from \(K_{\mu 2}\) decays it is \(0.22520(50)\), showing a discrepancy of about \(2.9\sigma\).
- The precision of the new \(K_{\ell 3}\) electromagnetic radiative corrections analysis is at the level of \(O(10^{-4})\), an order of magnitude better than existing pure Chiral Perturbation Theory analyses.
- The first-row CKM unitarity test shows an apparent violation of \(|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 = 1\) at about \(3.2\sigma\).
- The tree-level \(K_{e3}\) decay rate is described by three scalar kinematic variables \(\{x, y, z\}\) defined with \(M_K^2 x = (p - p' - p_e)^2\), \(\frac{1}{2} M_K^2 y = p \cdot p_e\), and \(\frac{1}{2} M_K^2 z = p \cdot p'\).

## Context

This work extends the recent re-analysis of \(K_{\ell 3}\) electromagnetic radiative corrections by Seng, Marciano, and Meißner (2021-2022), which hybridized the classic Sirlin representation (1977) with Chiral Perturbation Theory and lattice calculations of mesonic \(\gamma W\)-box diagrams by Feng et al. (2020) and Ma et al. (2021). A similar study of mass singularity cancellation was performed previously by Bytev et al. (2002) but only for the \(K_{e3}^+\) channel; the present analysis constitutes a more general proof covering both charged and neutral channels. The equivalence demonstrated between the mass-expansion and dimensional regularization methods provides a robust check on the theoretical uncertainty estimates and supports the utility of \(K_{e3}\) decays in precision unitarity tests of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark mixing matrix.
