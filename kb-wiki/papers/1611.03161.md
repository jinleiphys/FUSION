# HoTCoffeeh: Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlation functions and radii from event-by-event hydrodynamics

> $\textbf{HoTCoffeeh}$ ($\textbf{H}$anbury Br$\textbf{o}$wn-$\textbf{T}$wiss $\textbf{Co}$rrelation $\textbf{f}$unctions and radii $\textbf{f}$rom $\textbf{e}$vent-by-$\textbf{e}$vent $\textbf{h}$ydrodynamics) is a new computational tool which determines Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) charged pion ($π^+$) correlation functions and radii for event-by-event (EBE) hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions in terms of Cooper-Frye integrals, including resonance decay contributions. In this paper, we review the basic formalism for computing the HBT correlation functions and radii with resonance decay contributions included, and discuss our implementation of this formalism in the form of HoTCoffeeh. This tool may be easily integrated with other numerical packages for the purpose of simulating the evolution of heavy-ion collisions and thereby extracting predictions for heavy-ion observables.

## Key claim

HoTCoffeeh enables the computation of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) charged pion correlation functions and radii from event-by-event hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions, including resonance decay contributions from approximately 340 resonance species and 1700 decay channels, which were omitted in prior studies such as Plumberg (2015) due to computational complexity.

## Method

The code implements a purely hydrodynamic approach, computing HBT radii via Cooper-Frye integrals over the freeze-out hypersurface using either the source-variances (SV) method, which extracts radii from space-time variances of the emission function, or the Gaussian fit (GF) method, which fits the correlation function to a Gaussian in relative momentum. Resonance decay contributions are incorporated by summing over all decay channels of approximately 340 resonance species with masses below 2 GeV, integrating the decay phase space over parent resonance momenta and including the exponential decay law in proper time.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 340 resonance species and roughly 1700 pion-producing decay channels are included in the calculation
- Resonance masses included are those below 2 GeV
- The code computes HBT radii for charged pions (π⁺) specifically
- The formalism assumes boost-invariance, allowing source variances to be computed on a two-dimensional grid at midrapidity (y=0) and then extended to other rapidities via hyperbolic functions

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic paradigm for heavy-ion collisions, which has been successfully applied to observables such as radial flow (⟨p_T⟩), anisotropic flow (v_n), and total multiplicity (N^ch), to include HBT radii with full resonance decay contributions. Prior studies by Plumberg (2015) demonstrated that event-by-event distributions of HBT radii are experimentally accessible and could yield sensitivity to the specific shear viscosity η/s of the quark-gluon plasma, but omitted resonance decays. The code can be integrated with other numerical packages such as Shen (2014) for simulating heavy-ion collision evolution, enabling systematic exploration of HBT distributions within a reasonable timeframe.
