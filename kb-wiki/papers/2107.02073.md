# A Muon-Ion Collider at BNL: the future QCD frontier and path to a new energy frontier of $μ^+μ^-$ colliders

> We propose the development and construction of a novel muon-ion collider (MuIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) in the USA as an upgrade to succeed the electron-ion collider (EIC) that is scheduled to commence in the early 2030s, by a joint effort of the nuclear and particle physics communities. The BNL facility could accommodate a muon storage beam with an energy up to about 1~TeV with existing magnet technology. When collided with a 275~GeV hadron beam, the MuIC center-of-mass energy of about 1~TeV will extend the kinematic coverage of deep inelastic scattering physics at the EIC (with polarized beams) by more than an order of magnitude in $Q^2$ and $x$, opening a new QCD frontier to address many fundamental scientific questions in nuclear and particle physics. This coverage is comparable to that of the proposed Large Hadron-Electron Collider (LHeC) at CERN, but with complementary lepton and hadron kinematics and beam polarization. Additionally, the developmentof a MuIC at BNL will focus the worldwide R\&D efforts on muon collider technology and serve as a demonstrator toward a future muon-antimuon collider at {$\mathcal O$}(10)~TeV energies, which is an attractive option to reach the next high energy frontier in particle physics at an affordable cost and a smaller footprint than a future circular hadron collider. We discuss here the possible design parameters of the MuIC, kinematic coverage, science cases, and detector design considerations including estimates of resolutions on DIS kinematic variables. A possible road map toward the future MuIC and muon-antimuon colliders is also presented.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a Muon-Ion Collider (MuIC) at BNL, reusing existing EIC infrastructure, can achieve a center-of-mass energy of about 1 TeV and extend the kinematic coverage of deep inelastic scattering by more than an order of magnitude in Q² and x compared to the EIC, while simultaneously serving as a demonstrator for future muon-antimuon collider technology. The claim is conditional on using existing or near-term magnet technology (8.4 T to 16 T dipole magnets) and assumes the muon storage ring can achieve sufficient luminosity, with the authors noting their luminosity estimates are "order of magnitude" and "likely optimistic."

## Method

The authors present a design concept for a MuIC at BNL that would convert the planned EIC electron storage ring into a muon storage ring, reusing the RHIC tunnel and hadron beams. They estimate achievable center-of-mass energies for three magnet scenarios (8.4 T LHC-type, 11 T HL-LHC-type, and 16 T FCC-type) and calculate rough instantaneous luminosities using a proton driver scheme with muon cooling, assuming parameters from proposed muon collider designs and existing RHIC proton beam parameters. The kinematic coverage in Q² and x is mapped for muon-proton and muon-nucleus collisions, and detector resolution estimates are provided for three reconstruction methods (lepton-only, Jacquet-Blondel, and Double Angle) using assumed detector performance parameters.

## Key numbers

- Muon beam energy of 960 GeV achievable with 11 T bending magnets (HL-LHC technology), yielding muon-proton center-of-mass energy of about 1 TeV
- Muon-Au center-of-mass energy of 650 GeV with 197Au ion beam energy of 110 GeV per nucleon
- Estimated muon-proton instantaneous luminosity of 5-10 × 10³³ cm⁻²s⁻¹, comparable to the LHeC design luminosity
- Muon bunch repetition frequency of 15 Hz, with each bunch surviving an average of about 300B(Tesla) cycles in the ring
- Transverse RMS beam size for muon beam estimated at 5-6 μm based on proposed muon collider parameters
- Natural muon beam polarization of about 20%, with possibility of 50% polarization at reduced luminosity of about 25% of maximum
- Kinematic coverage extends to Q² values above MW² (approximately 6400 GeV²) for electroweak studies
- Higgs boson inclusive cross section of order 100 fb at the MuIC
- For a benchmark point at Q² = 3 GeV² and x = 5×10⁻⁵, scattered muon energy is 906 GeV at pseudorapidity -7.0

## Context

This proposal extends the concept of lepton-hadron colliders beyond the EIC at BNL (scheduled for early 2030s) and the proposed LHeC at CERN, offering a muon-based alternative that achieves similar kinematic coverage to the LHeC (about 1 TeV center-of-mass energy) but with complementary lepton and hadron kinematics and the unique capability of beam polarization. The MuIC would build on prior discussions of muon-hadron colliders (Shiltsev 1997, Ginzburg 1998, Sultansoy 1999, Acar 2016, Canbay 2017, Ketenoglu 2018, Kaya 2019, Cheung 2021) and would serve as a demonstrator toward a future muon-antimuon collider at O(10) TeV energies, which has been argued (Delahaye 2019) to match the discovery potential of a 100 TeV proton-proton collider. The paper enables future quantitative studies of physics reach, detector design, and accelerator parameters for a MuIC, and identifies synergies with the nuSTORM neutrino facility (Adey 2015).
