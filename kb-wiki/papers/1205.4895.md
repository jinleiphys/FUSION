# Heavy quark damping rate in hot viscous QCD plasma

> We derive an expression for the heavy quark damping rate in hot quark gluon plasma in presence of flow. Here all the bath particles here are out of equilibrium due to the existence of non-zero velocity gradient. The magnetic sector shows similar infrared divergences even after hard thermal loop corrections as one encounters in case of non-viscous plasma. We estimate the first order correction in ($η/s$) for heavy quark damping rate due to the non-zero viscosity of the QCD plasma.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytic expression for the heavy quark damping rate in a hot QCD plasma with longitudinal flow, including the first-order correction in the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio (η/s). The magnetic sector retains the same infrared logarithmic divergence (∫ dq/q) as in the non-viscous case even after hard thermal loop (HTL) corrections, requiring further non-perturbative resummation.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann kinetic equation and compute the heavy quark damping rate from 2→2 scattering processes (quark-quark and quark-gluon) in a QCD plasma with a non-zero velocity gradient along the longitudinal direction. The bath particle distribution functions are modified by a viscous correction term δfᵢ^η proportional to (η/s) and involving the momentum anisotropy (k²/3 − k_z²). The t-channel divergences are handled by HTL-dressed gluon propagators, separating hard (q > q*) and soft (q < q*) momentum transfer regions, and the calculation is carried out to leading order in η/s.

## Key numbers

- The viscous correction to the heavy quark damping rate from quark-quark scattering in the longitudinal (electric) sector is proportional to (η/s) × (g⁴ A_{b,q} / [4(2π)³ τ v_p]) × [π²(−v_p/6 − v_p³/9 + v_p⁵/10) + ζ(3)(3v_p/4 + 3v_p³/2 − 21v_p⁵/20)] × (−1/2 + ln(q_max/m_D)), where m_D is the Debye mass and q_max ∼ √(E_p T).
- The transverse (magnetic) sector contribution to the viscous correction contains a logarithmic infrared divergence ∫₀^{q*} dq/q with coefficient (η/s) × (A_{b,q} g⁴ π T² / [16(2π)³ τ v_p m_D²]) × (7π⁴/15 − 45ζ(5)/2).
- The quark-gluon scattering contribution to the viscous correction is (η/s) × (A_f g⁴ / [32 T³ τ π³ E_p]) × [2π⁴ T⁴/135 − (T³ m_q²/(3E_p))(2ζ(3) ln|4E_p T/m_q²| + 3ζ(3) − 2γζ(3) + 2ζ'(3))].
- The non-viscous heavy quark damping rate from quark-quark scattering is Γ(p)_{qq}^{0,L} = g⁴ A_b T³/(96π) × (1/m_D² − 1/q_max²) for the longitudinal part and Γ(p)_{qq}^{0,T} = g⁴ A_b T³ v_p/(48π m_D²) × ∫₀^{q*} dq/q for the transverse part.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the heavy quark damping rate in equilibrium QCD plasma (Pisarski 1992, 1993; Blaizot 1996, 1997) to the case of a viscous medium with longitudinal flow, relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions where the matter behaves as a near-ideal fluid with η/s ∼ 1/(4π) (Kovtun 2005). The authors follow the viscous correction formalism for distribution functions developed by Teaney (2003), Dusling (2010), and others, and find that the infrared divergence in the magnetic sector persists as in the non-viscous case, requiring the same non-perturbative resummation methods (Bloch-Nordsieck propagator or renormalization group) as used by Blaizot (1996, 1997) and Boyanovsky (1998). This enables future calculations of heavy quark energy loss and thermalization in viscous QGP.
