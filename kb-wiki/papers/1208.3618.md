# Bayesian inference of the resonance content of p(gamma,K+)Lambda

> A Bayesian analysis of the world's p(gamma,K+)Lambda data is presented. We adopt a Regge-plus-resonance framework featuring consistent couplings for nucleon resonances up to spin J=5/2, and evaluate 2048 model variants considering all possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances. The best model, labeled RPR-2011, is discussed with special emphasis on nucleon resonances in the 1900-MeV mass region.

## Key claim

The RPR-2011 model, selected via Bayesian evidence from 2048 variants, finds decisive evidence for the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance and significant-to-decisive evidence for two "missing" resonances ($P_{11}(1900)$ and $D_{13}(1900)$) in the 1900-MeV mass region, but the authors hedge that adding a $P_{11}(1924)$ bound-state candidate to the RPR-2011 set yields a model with the third-highest evidence, and they do not rule out that an alternative resonance set including that state could outperform RPR-2011.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of the world's $p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda$ data using the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework, which combines Reggeized $t$-channel $K^+(494)$ and $K^{\ast+}(892)$ exchange with tree-level $s$-channel nucleon resonance ($N^\ast$) exchanges up to spin $J=5/2$ using consistent couplings. They evaluated the Bayesian evidence $\mathcal{Z}^\prime$ for 2048 model variants constructed from all possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances, and selected the model with the highest evidence as RPR-2011.

## Key numbers

- The RPR-2011 model has 14 $N^\ast$ parameters and includes 8 resonances: $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $P_{13}(1900)$, $F_{15}(2000)$, $D_{13}(1900)$, and $P_{11}(1900)$.
- For the $P_{11}(1924)$ resonance added to the RPR-2011 set, the evidence difference is $\Delta\ln\mathcal{Z}^\prime = 6.17$, which is "decisive evidence" in favor of RPR-2011 according to Jeffreys' scale.
- The $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance has PDG three-star status with Breit-Wigner mass $1900$ MeV and width $500^{+80}_{-360}$ MeV.
- The missing $D_{13}(1900)$ and $P_{11}(1900)$ resonances each have Breit-Wigner mass $1895$ MeV and width $200$ MeV.
- The $P_{11}(1924)$ resonance has Breit-Wigner mass $1924$ MeV and width $60$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance formalism previously developed by the same group (Vancraeyveld et al., 2011) and applies Bayesian model selection to kaon photoproduction, a technique they introduced in earlier papers (Vancraeyveld et al., 2011; De Cruz et al., 2011). The analysis confirms the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance recently promoted to three-star status in the Particle Data Group review, and finds evidence for two resonances not listed in the PDG ($P_{11}(1900)$ and $D_{13}(1900)$). The investigation of the $P_{11}(1924)$ state tests a prediction from Jido and Oset (2009) of a $K\bar{K}N$ bound state with significant decay in the $K\Lambda$ channel, but the current data favor the RPR-2011 resonance set over including this state.
