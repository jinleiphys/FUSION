# Observing true tauonium via two-photon fusion at $e^+e^-$ and hadron colliders

> The feasibility of observing true tauonium, the bound state of two tau leptons, $\mathcal{T}_0\equiv(τ^+τ^-)_0$, via photon-photon collisions at $e^+e^-$ colliders and at the LHC, is studied. The production cross sections of the process $γγ\to\mathcal{T}_0\toγγ$ -- as well as those of all relevant backgrounds: spin-0 and 2 charmonium resonances decaying to diphotons, and light-by-light scattering -- are computed in the equivalent photon approximation for $e^+e^-$ collisions at BES III ($\sqrt{s} = 3.8$ GeV), Belle II ($\sqrt{s} = 10.6$ GeV), and FCC-ee ($\sqrt{s} = 91.2$ GeV), as well as for ultraperipheral p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Despite small $\mathcal{T}_0$ production cross sections and a final state swamped by decays from overlapping pseudoscalar and tensor charmonium states -- the $\mathrm{χ_{c2}}$, $\mathrm{η_{c}(2S)}$, and $\mathrm{χ_{c0}}$ states have masses only 2.5, 84, and 139 MeV away, respectively, from the $\mathcal{T}_0$ peak -- evidence and observation of the ground state of the heaviest leptonium appears feasible at Belle II and FCC-ee, respectively, with in-situ high-precision measurements of the irreducible backgrounds.

## Key claim

The paper claims that observation of true tauonium (para-ditauonium, $\mathcal{T}_0$) via two-photon fusion is feasible at Belle II and FCC-ee, but only if the irreducible charmonium backgrounds, particularly the $\chi_{c2}$ state whose mass lies only 2.5 MeV away from the $\mathcal{T}_0$ peak, can be measured in situ with high precision to control their contamination.

## Method

The authors computed production cross sections for the process $\gamma\gamma \to \mathcal{T}_0 \to \gamma\gamma$ and all relevant backgrounds (spin-0 and spin-2 charmonium resonances decaying to diphotons, and light-by-light scattering) using the equivalent photon approximation for $e^+e^-$ collisions at BES III (3.8 GeV), Belle II (10.6 GeV), and FCC-ee (91.2 GeV), as well as for ultraperipheral p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The HELAC-Onia Monte Carlo code was used for signal and charmonium background generation, while MadGraph5_aMC@NLO was used for the loop-induced light-by-light background. A boosted decision-tree multivariate analysis was applied to simulated events to discriminate signal from backgrounds, followed by a profile likelihood ratio analysis of diphoton invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Para-ditauonium mass: $m_{\mathcal{T}_0} = 3553.696 \pm 0.240$ MeV
- Para-ditauonium diphoton decay width: $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(\mathcal{T}_0) = 0.018533$ eV (for the $n=1$ state)
- Para-ditauonium binding energy: $E_\text{bind} = -23.7$ keV for $n=1$
- Para-ditauonium Bohr radius: $a_0 = 30.4$ fm
- Mass differences from $\mathcal{T}_0$ peak: $\chi_{c2}$ is 2.5 MeV away, $\eta_c(2S)$ is 84 MeV away, $\chi_{c0}$ is 139 MeV away
- Production cross section times branching fraction at Belle II (50 ab$^{-1}$): $\sigma \times \mathcal{B}_{\gamma\gamma}(\mathcal{T}_0) = 0.015$ fb, yielding 750 signal events
- Production cross section times branching fraction at FCC-ee (50 ab$^{-1}$): $\sigma \times \mathcal{B}_{\gamma\gamma}(\mathcal{T}_0) = 0.11$ fb, yielding 5,600 signal events
- $\chi_{c2}$ background yield at Belle II: 38,500 events; at FCC-ee: $3 \times 10^5$ events
- $\eta_c(1S)$ diphoton width: $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} = (5.06 \pm 0.34) \times 10^{-3}$ MeV
- $\chi_{c2}$ diphoton width: $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} = (5.614 \pm 0.197) \times 10^{-4}$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of ditauonium production, such as those by Baur (2001) for Pb-Pb UPCs at the LHC and by Fael (2018) for B-hadron decays, by providing the first full experimental feasibility assessment including detector effects, physical backgrounds, and statistical significance. The paper enables a concrete search strategy at Belle II and FCC-ee, where the $\eta_c(1S)$ resonance can serve as a standard candle for in-situ calibration, and where independent measurements of charmonium diphoton widths (e.g., via charged-particle decay channels) can control the irreducible backgrounds that swamp the tauonium signal.
