# $p_T$-Dependent Particle Number Fluctuations From Principal Component Analyses in Hydrodynamic Simulations of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We carry out a principal component analysis of fluctuations in a hydrodynamic simulation of heavy-ion collisions, and compare with experimental data from the CMS collaboration. The leading and subleading principal components of elliptic and triangular flow reproduce the trends seen in data. By contrast, the principal components of multiplicity fluctuations show an interesting difference in their $p_T$ dependence for simulations compared to experimental data. Specifically, the leading component increases with $p_T$ in hydrodynamics, while it is constant in experiment. In order to understand how the leading and subleading modes arise, we construct a toy model where the principal components have a simple analytic form. We show how the PCA components depend on fluctuations of the average transverse momentum and of the total multiplicity, as well as correlations between the two, and we verify that hydrodynamic simulations agree with the predictions of the toy model. The difference in the momentum trend is likely due to the fact that hydrodynamic models typically have transverse momentum fluctuations that are larger than seen experimentally.

## Key claim

The leading principal component of multiplicity fluctuations increases with transverse momentum in hydrodynamic simulations, while it is constant in CMS experimental data; this discrepancy is likely because hydrodynamic models produce transverse momentum fluctuations that are larger than those observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the NeXSPheRIO code with NeXus initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They carried out a principal component analysis (PCA) of the covariance matrix of Fourier coefficients V_n(p_T) across transverse momentum bins, extracting the leading and subleading scaled principal components v_n^(alpha)(p_T) for n=0 (multiplicity), n=2 (elliptic flow), and n=3 (triangular flow). They also constructed an analytic toy model assuming an exponential p_T spectrum with event-by-event fluctuations of total multiplicity N and mean transverse momentum p_T_bar, expanded to first order in the fluctuations.

- Hydrodynamic sigma_pT/<p_T_bar> values are too large by a factor of approximately 2 compared to CMS data

## Context

This work extends the PCA framework for event-by-event fluctuations proposed by Bhalerao (2014) and Mazeliauskas (2015) to multiplicity fluctuations, comparing hydrodynamic simulations with CMS experimental data from Sirunyan (2017). The authors show that the n=0 PCA components are sensitive to the covariance between multiplicity and mean transverse momentum fluctuations, which previous studies of transverse momentum fluctuations by Bozek (2012) had found to be overestimated in hydrodynamic models. The results suggest that existing initial condition models overestimate fluctuations in the transverse size of the fireball, and the PCA method provides a new tool to rule out such models.
