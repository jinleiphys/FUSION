# Baryon resonances in the strangeness production

> We have studied the $N^*(2120)$, $Δ^*(1940)$, and the possible $Σ^*(1380)$ resonances in the $γp \to K^+ Λ(1520)$, $pp \to n K^+ Σ(1385)$, and $Λp \to Λp π^0$ reactions within the resonance model and the effective Lagrangian approach. It is shown that when the contributions from these baryonic states were considered, the current experimental measurement could be well reproduced. In addition, we also demonstrate that the angular distributions provide direct information of these reactions, which could be useful for the investigation of those states and may be tested by future experiments.

# Baryon resonances in the strangeness production

## Key claim
The N*(2120), Δ*(1940), and a possible Σ*(1380) resonance with J^P = 1/2^- can reproduce existing experimental data for three strangeness-production reactions when included in a resonance model with effective Lagrangians, but the Σ*(1380) is a predicted state not yet independently confirmed, and the angular distributions require future experimental verification.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with an isobar (resonance) model to compute differential and total cross sections for three reactions: γp → K⁺Λ(1520), pp → nK⁺Σ(1385), and Λp → Λpπ⁰. For each reaction, they constructed invariant scattering amplitudes including s-channel, t-channel, and u-channel contributions from known baryons and the candidate resonances, with off-shell form factors and propagators, then fitted the model parameters to reproduce existing experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For the γp → K⁺Λ(1520) reaction, the N*(2120) resonance contribution (green-dotted curves) combined with t-channel K⁻ exchange, s-channel nucleon pole, u-channel Λ(1115), and Reggeon exchange (red-solid curves) reproduces LEPS and CLAS differential cross section data as a function of LAB frame photon energy for forward c.m. K⁺ angles.
- For the pp → nK⁺Σ(1385) reaction, the Δ*(1940) resonance contribution is predominant over the Λ(1115) pole in the whole considered energy region; the cutoff parameters used are Λ_π = Λ*_π = 1.1 GeV and Λ_s = 0.9 GeV.
- For the Λp → Λpπ⁰ reaction, the Σ*(1380) state and Σ*(1385) resonance dominate the total cross section at beam momenta below and above 1.3 GeV, respectively; the Σ*(1380) decays to πΛ in relative S-wave (J^P = 1/2⁻) while Σ*(1385) decays in P-wave (J^P = 3/2⁺).

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of baryon resonances in the strangeness sector, where nucleon and Δ* resonances above 2.0 GeV and hyperon excited states remain poorly explored according to Agashe (2014). The Σ*(1380) state was predicted near 1380 MeV by penta-quark models from Zhang (2004) and near 1405 MeV by Helminen (2000); the present analysis provides a natural explanation for the near-threshold enhancement in Λp → Λpπ⁰ data from Kadyk (1971). The results enable future experimental tests of angular distributions to distinguish these resonances and to validate penta-quark interpretations of baryon structure.
