# Ruling out color transparency in quasi-elastic $^{12}$C(e,e'p) up to $Q^2$ of 14.2 (GeV/c)$^2$

> Quasielastic $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ scattering was measured at space-like 4-momentum transfer squared $Q^2$~=~8, 9.4, 11.4, and 14.2 (GeV/c)$^2$, the highest ever achieved to date. Nuclear transparency for this reaction was extracted by comparing the measured yield to that expected from a plane-wave impulse approximation calculation without any final state interactions. The measured transparency was consistent with no $Q^2$ dependence, up to proton momenta of 8.5~GeV/c, ruling out the quantum chromodynamics effect of color transparency at the measured $Q^2$ scales in exclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions. These results impose strict constraints on models of color transparency for protons.

## Key claim

The measured nuclear transparency for quasi-elastic $^{12}$C(e,e'p) scattering shows no $Q^2$ dependence up to $Q^2 = 14.2$ (GeV/c)$^2$ and outgoing proton momenta of 8.5 GeV/c, ruling out the QCD effect of color transparency for protons at these scales; however, this conclusion applies specifically to the exclusive $(e,e'p)$ reaction channel and does not exclude the possibility that the rise in transparency observed in the earlier BNL $A(p,2p)$ experiment could be due to other mechanisms such as nuclear filtering or charmed quark resonances.

## Method

The experiment was performed in Hall C at Jefferson Lab using continuous wave electron beams of 6.4 and 10.6 GeV incident on a carbon foil target (4.9% radiation lengths). Scattered electrons were detected in the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) in coincidence with knocked-out protons detected in the Super High Momentum Spectrometer (SHMS), with the SHMS central angle set along the electron three-momentum transfer direction. Nuclear transparency was extracted as the ratio of the measured $^{12}$C(e,e'p) yield to the yield expected from a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculation using the De Forest $\sigma_{1}^{cc}$ prescription for the off-shell cross section and independent particle spectral functions, integrated over a phase space defined by missing energy $E_m < 80$ MeV and missing momentum $|\vec{p}_m| < 300$ MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency measured at four $Q^2$ values: 8, 9.4, 11.4, and 14.2 (GeV/c)$^2$, the highest ever achieved in quasi-elastic electron scattering.
- Outgoing proton momenta up to 8.5 GeV/c were accessed.
- The combined data set from all measurements above $Q^2 = 3.0$ (GeV/c)$^2$ was fit to a constant value with a reduced $\chi^2$ of 1.3.
- Total systematic uncertainty on the transparency measurement was 4.0%, with an additional model-dependent uncertainty of 3.9%.
- The experiment probed transverse sizes as small as approximately 0.05 fm in the three-quark nucleon system.

## Context

This work extends previous $A(e,e'p)$ transparency measurements from experiments such as NE18 (1990s) and later JLab 6 GeV experiments (Donnelly et al., Garrow et al.) that had reached up to $Q^2 = 8.1$ (GeV/c)$^2$ and found no significant $Q^2$ dependence. The new results cover the complete kinematic phase space of the enhancement observed by the BNL E850 $A(p,2p)$ experiment, which had reported a rise in transparency at effective beam momenta of 5.9-9 GeV/c followed by a decrease at higher momenta. By ruling out color transparency as the cause of that rise, these results impose strict constraints on models of color transparency for baryons and highlight the difference between the observed onset of color transparency in mesons at $Q^2 \approx 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$ and its absence in protons at more than an order of magnitude higher $Q^2$. Future experiments at JLab and elsewhere will further investigate these differences for pions, rho-mesons, and photons.
