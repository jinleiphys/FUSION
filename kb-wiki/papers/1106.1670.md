# A model for space and time-like proton (neutron) form factors

> A model is suggested to describe the known experimental behavior of nucleon form factors both in space and time-like regions. It implies quarks to be colorless in the region of high intensity chomo-magnetic gluon field inside the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a qualitative model where, inside the nucleon, a region of strong chromo-electromagnetic gluon field causes quarks to become effectively colorless, leading to a suppression of the electric form factor relative to the magnetic form factor in both space-like and time-like regions; the model is not derived by analytical continuation from the space-like region to the time-like region, and the authors note that discrepancies may appear in the fitting parameters between the two regions.

## Method

The authors assume that in the central region of the nucleon, the strong gluon field stochastically averages out quark color, causing identical-flavor quarks (uu in proton, dd in neutron) to be pushed out and a quark-diquark dipole to form. They model the electric form factor suppression using a Debye-like screening equation for the scalar part of the electromagnetic field, yielding an additional monopole factor. For the time-like region, they generalize the form factor definition as a Fourier transform of a space-time charge distribution and parametrize the magnetic and electric form factors with dipole and monopole terms, respectively, using the variable (q² − 4M_p²) to enforce normalization at threshold.

## Key numbers

- The strength of the chromo-electric field inside the nucleon is estimated as E ≈ 0.245 GeV², derived from the gluon condensate value ⟨0|(α_s/π)(G^a_{μν})²|0⟩ = 0.012 GeV⁴.
- The minimal distance for the quark-diquark picture is r₀ = 0.22 fm in the proton and r₀ = 0.31 fm in the neutron, corresponding to p₀² ≈ 1.21 GeV² and p₀² ≈ 2.43 GeV², respectively.
- In the space-like region, the electric form factor suppression parameter is q₁² = 3.6 GeV² for the proton.
- In the time-like region, the magnetic form factor suppression parameter is q₂² = 1.2 GeV², and the electric form factor suppression parameter is q₁² = 1.2 GeV².
- For the neutron electric form factor in the space-like region, the suppression parameter is q₁² = 2.42 GeV².
- The dipole form factor is G_D(Q²) = [1 + Q²/(0.71 GeV²)]⁻².

## Context

This work extends nucleon form factor models that describe space-like data, such as those reviewed by Perdrisat (2006), and attempts to also describe time-like data from experiments like BaBar (2009) and Fenice (1994). The model provides a qualitative explanation for the fast decrease of the proton charge distribution at small distances observed by the GEp collaboration (2010) using the polarization method (1967). It enables simple parametrizations for all four nucleon form factors in both space-like and time-like regions, which can be tested by future precise measurements, and notes that crossing symmetry violations in QED (Kuraev, 1974) may cause differences between the space-like and time-like parameters.
