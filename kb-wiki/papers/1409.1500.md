# Double parton correlations and constituent quark models: a Light Front approach to the valence sector

> An explicit evaluation of the double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), within a relativistic Light-Front approach to constituent quark models, is presented. dPDFs encode information on the correlations between two partons inside a target and represent the non-perturbative QCD ingredient for the description of double parton scattering in proton-proton collisions, a crucial issue in the search of new Physics at the LHC. Valence dPDFs are evaluated at the low scale of the model and the perturbative scale of the experiments is reached by means of QCD evolution. The present results show that the strong correlation effects present at the scale of the model are still sizable, in the valence region, at the experimental scale. At the low values of x presently studied at the LHC the correlations become less relevant, although they are still important for the spin-dependent contributions to unpolarized proton scattering.

## Key claim

The authors show that strong correlation effects between two partons, present in double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) at the low hadronic scale of a constituent quark model, remain sizable in the valence region after perturbative QCD evolution to experimental scales, although at the low x values studied at the LHC these correlations become less relevant while remaining important for spin-dependent contributions to unpolarized proton scattering.

## Method

The authors evaluate valence double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) for two unpolarized and two longitudinally polarized u quarks within a relativistic Light-Front approach to the hypercentral constituent quark model, using a Bakamjian-Thomas construction to relate Light-Front and Instant-Form wave functions via Melosh rotations. The dPDFs are computed at the hadronic scale of the model (Q0 ~ Lambda_QCD) and then evolved to higher momentum scales using perturbative QCD evolution equations for dPDFs, following the procedure established for single parton distributions by Pape and Jaro (1982).

## Key numbers

* The hypercentral constituent quark model parameters are tau = 3.30 and kappa = 1.80 fm^{-2} for the relativistic case (tau = 4.59 and kappa = 1.61 fm^{-2} for the non-relativistic reduction).
* The dPDFs are evaluated at transverse momentum k_perp = 0 for three values of x2 (shown in Figures 1-4, though specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text).
* The dPDFs satisfy the correct support condition x1 + x2 < 1, unlike previous bag model and non-relativistic constituent quark model calculations where the "bad support" problem (x1 + x2 > 1) occurred.

## Context

This work extends the earlier bag model calculation of dPDFs by Manohar and Waalewijn (2012) and the non-relativistic constituent quark model calculation by Rinaldi, Scopetta, and Vento (2013), both of which suffered from the "bad support" problem (dPDFs non-vanishing for x1 + x2 > 1) and lacked perturbative QCD evolution. The Light-Front approach, previously applied to generalized parton distributions by Pasquini, Boffi, and collaborators (2005-2008) and to transverse momentum dependent distributions, resolves the support problem through full Poincare covariance. The results enable future comparisons with LHC double parton scattering data and provide a framework for evaluating spin-dependent dPDFs that contribute to unpolarized proton scattering cross sections.
