# Non-conformal Holography of Heavy Quark Quenching

> We develop a holographic (bottom-up) gravity model for QCD to understand the connection between the peak in the trace anomaly and the magnitude of heavy quark energy loss in a strongly-coupled plasma. The potential of the scalar field on the gravity side is constructed to reproduce some properties of QCD at finite temperature and its parameters are constrained by fitting lattice gauge theory results. The energy loss of heavy quarks (as predicted by the holographic model) is found to be strongly sensitive to the medium properties.

# Non-conformal Holography of Heavy Quark Quenching

## Key claim
The energy loss of heavy quarks in a strongly-coupled plasma is strongly sensitive to the medium properties, particularly near the crossover temperature where the trace anomaly peaks, but this result depends on the assumption that the trailing string model applies to both charm and bottom quarks and that the model parameters are fully constrained by lattice data for the speed of sound and Polyakov loop.

## Method
The authors construct a 5-dimensional gravity theory coupled to a scalar dilaton field with a potential of the form V(phi) = (1/L^2)(-12 cosh(gamma phi) + b2 phi^2 + b4 phi^4 + b6 phi^6). They solve the equations of motion using a nonlinear master differential equation for a generating function G(phi), then compute thermodynamic quantities from Hawking formulas and fit the potential parameters to lattice QCD data from the hotQCD collaboration. The heavy quark energy loss is computed using the trailing string model from the Nambu-Goto action, with the string tension L^2/alpha' determined by fitting the holographic Polyakov loop to lattice results.

## Key numbers
- Potential parameters: gamma = 0.606, b2 = 0.703, b4 = -0.12, b6 = 0.00325, corresponding to operator dimension Delta approximately 3.
- From the Polyakov loop fit, L^2/alpha' is roughly 1.
- The critical temperature T_c is defined as the temperature where the speed of sound reaches its minimum.
- Energy loss is computed for charm and bottom quarks at fixed momentum p = 10 GeV as a function of temperature, and as a function of momentum for several fixed temperatures.

## Context
This work extends bottom-up holographic approaches to QCD that use a scalar field to break conformal invariance, following the framework of Gubser and Nellore for constructing non-conformal holographic models. It builds on earlier work by Kiritsis and collaborators and by Kajantie and collaborators on the role of the beta function in scalar field potential construction. The model enables predictions for heavy quark energy loss in the crossover region near T_c, which is relevant for understanding nuclear modification factor R_AA in heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that alpha-prime corrections may be important since L^2/alpha' is of order 1.
