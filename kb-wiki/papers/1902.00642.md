# Impact of the nuclear mass uncertainties on the r process

> Based on a simple site-independent approach, we attempt to reproduce the solar $r$-process abundance with four nuclear mass models, and investigate the impact of the nuclear mass uncertainties on the $r$ process. In this paper, we first analyze the reliability of an adopted empirical formula for $β$-decay half-lives which is a key ingredient for the $r$ process. Then we apply four different mass tables to study the $r$-process nucleosynthesis together with the calculated $β$-decay half-lives, and the existing $β$-decay data from FRDM+QRPA is also considered for comparison. The numerical results show that the main features of the solar $r$-process pattern and the locations of the abundance peaks can be reproduced well via the $r$-process simulations. Moreover, we also find that the mass uncertainties can significantly affect the derived astrophysical conditions for the $r$-process site, and resulting in a remarkable impact on the $r$ process.

## Key claim

Using a site-independent approach with four nuclear mass models, the authors find that nuclear mass uncertainties significantly affect the derived astrophysical conditions for the r-process site and the resulting abundance pattern, but the best reproduction of the solar r-process abundances comes from the WS4 mass model, while the RMF model gives the worst agreement.

## Method

The authors employed Zhou et al.'s (2017) empirical formula for beta-decay half-lives, which depends on Q-values derived from nuclear masses, and combined it with four global mass models: FRDM, WS4, HFB-31, and RMF. They then performed site-independent r-process simulations using 16 components with neutron densities from 10^20 to 3x10^27 cm^-3 at a temperature of 1.5 GK, fitting the weighting factors and irradiation times to reproduce the solar r-process abundances from Simmerer et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation sigma for beta-decay half-lives with T <= 1 s (381 nuclei) was 0.316 for WS4, 0.381 for FRDM, 0.375 for HFB-31, 0.433 for RMF, and 0.391 for FRDM+QRPA.
- The rms deviation sigma for beta-decay half-lives with T <= 1000 s (824 nuclei) was 0.529 for WS4, 0.598 for FRDM, 0.651 for HFB-31, 0.730 for RMF, and 0.597 for FRDM+QRPA.
- The rms deviation of binding energy B relative to experimental data was 0.302 MeV for WS4, 0.677 MeV for FRDM, 0.584 MeV for HFB-31, and 2.258 MeV for RMF.
- The longest neutron irradiation time required was up to 10 s for the RMF model and up to 4 s for HFB-31, while FRDM and WS4 favored relatively constant irradiation times.

## Context

This work extends the site-independent r-process approach used by Kratz et al. (2007), Sun et al. (2008), Cowan et al. (1999), and Pfeiffer et al. (1997) by systematically comparing four global mass models (FRDM, WS4, HFB-31, RMF) with a common empirical beta-decay formula from Zhou et al. (2017). The results enable future studies to prioritize the WS4 mass model for r-process simulations and highlight the need for improved mass predictions for neutron-rich nuclei, particularly for the RMF model which produced the largest discrepancies.
