# Four-body calculation of elastic deuteron-deuteron scattering

> Fully converged calculations of deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering observables are performed at energies above three- and four-body breakup threshold. Differential cross sections and analyzing powers are obtained using realistic nucleon-nucleon force models together with the Coulomb repulsion between protons. For all observables we find a very reasonable agreement with the available experimental data limited to deuteron beam energies up to 25.3 MeV.

# Four-body calculation of elastic deuteron-deuteron scattering

## Key claim

Fully converged calculations of deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering observables using realistic nucleon-nucleon force models with Coulomb repulsion show very reasonable agreement with experimental data up to 25.3 MeV, but the agreement is qualified by a slight underprediction of the differential cross section minimum above 13.2 MeV that does not increase with beam energy or scale with three-nucleon binding energy, unlike discrepancies seen in nucleon-trinucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors solved the exact Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) four-body integral equations in momentum space using the complex-energy method, restricting the model space to total isospin $\mathcal{T}=0$ states after verifying that $\mathcal{T}=1$ states have negligible effect on deuteron-deuteron elastic observables. The Coulomb repulsion between protons was included via the screening and renormalization method with screening radii of 10 to 12 fm. Calculations employed four realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials: INOY04, N3LO chiral effective field theory, CD Bonn, and CD Bonn with explicit $\Delta$-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energies studied: 4.75 MeV to 25.3 MeV, with the three-cluster threshold at 4.45 MeV
- At 6 MeV beam energy, the largest difference between the four force models for differential cross section is about 6% around $\Theta_{cm} = 40^\circ$ and $140^\circ$
- At 14.2 MeV, calculations overpredict the differential cross section data by up to 9%
- At 25.3 MeV, calculations underpredict the differential cross section magnitude by about 6% at forward angles and around $\Theta_{cm} = 90^\circ$
- At 25.3 MeV, the spread of predictions between force models at $\Theta_{cm} = 90^\circ$ is about 3%
- The vector analyzing power $iT_{11}$ nearly vanishes at 8.0 MeV beam energy and changes sign at higher energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-nucleon scattering studies (Deltuva 2012, 2013, 2014) that were mostly devoted to nucleon-trinucleon collisions, where discrepancies in nucleon analyzing power and differential cross section minima were observed above 25 MeV nucleon energy. By studying deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering, which is dominated by total isospin $\mathcal{T}=0$ states, the authors investigate whether those disagreements originate from $\mathcal{T}=1$ states or are shared by $\mathcal{T}=0$ components. The results enable comparison with the only other rigorous four-body method, the complex scaling method in coordinate space (Lazauskas 2015), and call for new experimental data above 15 MeV where measurements are very scarce.
