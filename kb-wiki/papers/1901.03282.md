# An essential singularity of the cotangent of the Coulomb-nuclear phase shift, and a finite limit of the nuclear part of the effective-range function derived at zero energy

> The Coulomb-nuclear phase shift $δ^{(cs)}_l$, $\cotδ^{(cs)}_l$ and a finite limit of the nuclear part $Δ_l(k)$ of the effective-range function (ERF) are derived for an arbitrary orbital momentum $l$ when energy $E\rightarrow0$. It is proved that $\cotδ^{(cs)}_l$ has an essential singularity at zero energy, but $Δ_l(k)$ does not. The explicit finite limit of $Δ_l(0)$ is found. The property of $Δ_l(k)$ as a meromorphic function makes possible the analytical continuation of a re-normalized scattering amplitude from the physical energy region to a bound state pole. Then the asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) can be deduced from experimental phase-shift data and applied to radiative capture processes which are important in nuclear astrophysics for new elements creation. Our results are in agreement with the results published for $S$ wave scattering.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the nuclear part of the effective-range function, Δ_l(k), has a finite limit at zero energy and therefore does not possess an essential singularity, contrary to claims in prior work by Blokh and LDlast; the essential singularity resides only in cot δ^{(cs)}_l, which cancels with the same singularity in the denominator of the Δ_l definition.

## Method

The author derives explicit expressions for the Coulomb-nuclear phase shift δ^{(cs)}_l, its cotangent, and the nuclear part Δ_l(k) of the effective-range function (ERF) for arbitrary orbital momentum l as energy E → 0. Starting from the standard ERF formula for charged particles, the limit of Δ_l(k) is obtained by expanding the ERF in powers of k² and using the known behavior of the Coulomb function Re h(η) near zero energy. The resulting finite limit for Δ_l(0) is expressed in terms of the scattering length a^{(cs)}_l, the Bohr radius a_B, and l.

## Key numbers

- Δ_l(0) = -(l!)² / [2 a^{(cs)}_l ξ^{2l+1}] for l > 0, where ξ = 1/a_B.
- Δ_0(0) = -a_B / (2 a^{(cs)}_0) for S-wave (l=0).
- cot δ^{(cs)}_l(k) = -[(l!)² / (2π ξ^{2l+1} a^{(cs)}_l)] exp(2π / (k a_B)) when k a_B ≪ 1.
- δ^{(cs)}_l(k) = -[2π / (l!)²] ξ^{2l+1} a^{(cs)}_l exp(-2π / (k a_B)) when k a_B ≪ 1.
- For S-wave: cot δ^{(cs)}_0(k) = -[1 / (2π ξ a^{(cs)}_0)] exp(2π / (k a_B)).
- For S-wave: δ^{(cs)}_0(k) = -2π ξ a^{(cs)}_0 exp(-2π / (k a_B)).

## Context

This work extends the Δ method previously validated by Orlov and Irgaziev (2018) for bound and resonance states, and directly contradicts claims by Blokh and LDlast (2018) that Δ_l(k) has an essential singularity at zero energy, which would invalidate the method. The results agree with S-wave treatments in Landau and Smorodinsky (1977) and with the proton-proton scattering analysis in Brown (1979), and enable the analytical continuation of the re-normalized scattering amplitude to bound-state poles for deducing asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from experimental phase-shift data, relevant for nuclear astrophysics radiative capture reactions.
