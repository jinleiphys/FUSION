# Radiative neutron capture on a proton at BBN energies

> The total cross section for radiative neutron capture on a proton, $np \to d γ$, is evaluated at big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) energies. The electromagnetic transition amplitudes are calculated up to next-to leading order within the framework of pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields. We also calculate the $dγ\to np$ cross section and the photon analyzing power for the $d\vecγ\to np$ process from the amplitudes. The values of low energy constants that appear in the amplitudes are estimated by a Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis using the relevant low energy experimental data. Our result agrees well with those of other theoretical calculations except for the $np\to dγ$ cross section at some energies estimated by an R-matrix analysis. We also study the uncertainties in our estimation of the $np\to dγ$ cross section at relevant BBN energies and find that the estimated cross section is reliable to within $\sim$1% error.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the total cross section for radiative neutron capture on a proton at big bang nucleosynthesis energies using pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields up to next-to-leading order, and find that the estimated cross section is reliable to within approximately 1% error, except for a significant discrepancy of about 4.6% compared to an R-matrix analysis at certain energies.

## Method

The electromagnetic transition amplitudes for the \(np \to d\gamma\) process were calculated up to next-to-leading order within the framework of pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, using a modified counting of the vector-dibaryon-dibaryon vertex to improve convergence. The values of low energy constants appearing in the amplitudes were estimated by a Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis using low energy experimental data including \(np\) scattering cross sections, thermal neutron capture cross sections, \(np \to d\gamma\) capture data, photo-dissociation cross sections, and photon analyzing power measurements.

## Key numbers

- Thermal neutron capture cross sections used in fitting: 334.2 ± 0.5 mb (Cox et al.) and 332.6 ± 0.7 mb (Cokinos and Melkonian)
- Deuteron binding energy fixed at \(B = 2.2245671 \pm 0.0000042\) MeV
- Fitted parameters from MCMC analysis: \(a_0 = -23.745 \pm 0.008\) fm, \(r_0 = 2.730 \pm 0.044\) fm, \(\rho_d = 1.740 \pm 0.007\) fm, \(l_1 = 0.893 \pm 0.038\) fm
- \(l_2\) determined from deuteron magnetic moment: \(l_2 = -0.0154\) fm
- Minimum \(\chi^2\) from MCMC fit: \(\chi^2_{\text{tot,min}} = 2303.00\) with \(N_{\text{tot}} = 2147\) data points
- Significant difference of approximately 4.6% found between the authors' result and the R-matrix analysis at \(E = 0.1\) and 1 MeV (total two-nucleon kinetic energy in center-of-mass frame)

## Context

This work extends previous pionless effective field theory calculations of the \(np \to d\gamma\) cross section by Chen and Savage (1999), who found a 4% error at N3LO, and Rupak (2000), who found a 1% error at N4LO, by using dibaryon fields to improve convergence and by employing a Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis to determine low energy constants from experimental data. The results agree well with the previous EFT calculations and with a potential model calculation using the Argonne v18 potential with meson exchange currents (Nakamura, 2004), but disagree significantly with an R-matrix analysis (Hale, 2004). This work enables more reliable predictions of primordial light element abundances in big bang nucleosynthesis by providing a cross section with quantified uncertainty of about 1%.
