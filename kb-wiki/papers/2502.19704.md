# Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule from lattice QCD

> We compute the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule, i.e. the lowest odd moment of the parity-violating structure function, $F_3$, of the nucleon from a lattice QCD calculation of the Compton amplitude. Our calculations are performed on $48^3 \times 96$ lattices at the $SU(3)$ symmetric point for two lattice spacings. We extract the moments for several values of the current momenta in the range $0.5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 10 \; {\rm GeV}^2$, covering both the nonperturbative and perturbative regimes. We compare our moments to the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule and discuss the implications for higher-twist effects, a determination of $α_s(Q^2)$ from a hadronic quantity complementing the phenomenological and other lattice approaches, and electroweak box contributions crucial for Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix unitarity studies.

## Key claim

The authors compute the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule from lattice QCD for the first time, but the results are obtained at the SU(3) symmetric point with unphysical quark masses (mπ ≈ 415 MeV) and without a continuum extrapolation, meaning the comparison to perturbative QCD is qualitative rather than a precision determination of αs.

## Method

The Compton amplitude for the γZ interference is computed using a Feynman-Hellmann approach on 48³ × 96 lattices at two lattice spacings (a = 0.068 fm and 0.052 fm) with 2+1 flavours of dynamical quarks at the SU(3) symmetric point. The parity-odd structure function F₃ is isolated by choosing specific current directions (μ=1, ν=3) and kinematics (nucleon at rest, q₁=0, q₂≠0), and the lowest odd Mellin moment M₁⁽³⁾(Q²) is extracted directly from the Compton amplitude at ω=0. A lattice perturbation theory correction is applied to reduce discretisation errors from broken rotational symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: a = 0.068 fm (β=5.65) and a = 0.052 fm (β=5.95)
- Pion mass at the SU(3) symmetric point: mπ ≈ 415 MeV
- Momentum range covered: 0.5 ≲ Q² ≲ 10 GeV²
- Number of configurations: 535 (β=5.65) and 507 (β=5.95)
- Perturbative coefficients for N³LO corrections: C₁ = 1, C₂ = 55/12 − (1/3)n_f, C₃ = 41.440 − 8.020 n_f + 0.177 n_f², C₄ = 479.4 − 117.6 n_f + 7.464 n_f² − 0.1037 n_f³ (with n_f=3)
- 3-flavour Λ parameter used for comparison: Λ_MSbar⁽³⁾ = 338(10) MeV (from FLAG averaging group)

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Hellmann approach previously applied to unpolarised (PhysRevD.102.114505, QCDSFUKQCDCSSM:2022ncb) and polarised (Can:2022chd) parity-conserving structure functions to the parity-violating case. The results complement phenomenological determinations of the GLS sum rule and provide a first-principles input for electroweak box diagram calculations relevant to CKM matrix unitarity studies (Feng:2020zdc, Ma:2021azh, Yoo:2022lmt). Future work requires a continuum extrapolation and physical quark masses before a precision determination of αs from this hadronic observable becomes feasible.
