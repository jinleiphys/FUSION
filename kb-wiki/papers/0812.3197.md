# Optimal pair density functional for description of nuclei with large neutron excess

> Toward a universal description of pairing properties in nuclei far from stability, we extend the energy density functional by enriching the isovector density dependence in the particle-particle channel (pair density functional, pair-DF). We emphasize the necessity of both the linear and quadratic isovector density terms. The parameters are optimized by the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation for 156 nuclei of the mass number A=118-196 and the asymmetry parameter (N-Z)/A<0.25. We clarify that the pair-DF should include the isovector density dependence in order to take into account the effect of the isoscalar and isovector effective masses in the particle-hole channel consistently. The different Skyrme forces can give the small difference in the pairing gaps toward the neutron drip line, if the optimal pair-DF consistent with the particle-hole channel is employed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a pair density functional (pair-DF) must include both linear and quadratic isovector density (\(\rho_1\)) terms to reproduce the experimental asymmetry dependence of pairing gaps across 156 nuclei, and that the optimal linear \(\rho_1\) coefficient is linearly correlated with the inverse isovector effective mass of the Skyrme particle-hole functional.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for 156 even-even open-shell nuclei with mass numbers \(A=118-196\) and asymmetry parameter \(\alpha=(N-Z)/A<0.25\), using the Skyrme SLy4 force for the particle-hole channel and a parameterized pair-DF with isoscalar density (\(\rho\)), linear \(\rho_1\), and quadratic \(\rho_1\) terms. The parameters \(\eta_1\) and \(\eta_2\) of the pair-DF were optimized by minimizing the root-mean-square (r.m.s.) deviation between calculated and experimental pairing gaps extracted from odd-even mass differences, with the strength \(V_0\) fixed to reproduce the neutron pairing gap of \(^{156}\)Dy. The analysis was extended to 13 different Skyrme parameterizations to study the correlation between the optimal \(\eta_1\) and the effective masses.

## Key numbers

* The experimental neutron pairing gaps follow \(\Delta_n^{(\exp)}(\alpha)/\Delta_n^{(A)} = 1 - 7.74\,\alpha^2\) with \(\Delta_n^{(A)} = 6.75/A^{1/3}\) MeV, and proton gaps follow \(\Delta_p^{(\exp)}(\alpha)/\Delta_p^{(A)} = 1 - 8.25\,\alpha^2\) with \(\Delta_p^{(A)} = 6.36/A^{1/3}\) MeV, fitted from data in the region \(N,Z \ge 40\).
* The optimal pair-DF parameters for SLy4 are \((\eta_0,\eta_1,\eta_2) = (0.5, 0.2, 2.5)\) with \(V_0 = -346.5\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), yielding total r.m.s. deviation \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} = 0.17\) MeV (\(\sigma_n = 0.16\) MeV, \(\sigma_p = 0.17\) MeV).
* The optimal \(\eta_1\) for each Skyrme force is linearly correlated with the inverse isovector effective mass: \(\eta_1 = -0.340 + 0.464\,(m/m_v^*)\), with correlation coefficient \(r = 0.85\) (excluding SkI3 and SkT6).
* The strength \(V_0\) for \(\eta_1=\eta_2=0\) is linearly correlated with the isoscalar effective mass: \(V_0 = -505.05 + 264.47\,(m_s^*/m)\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) with \(r = 0.99\) (excluding SkT6 and SkI3).

## Context

This work extends the pair-DF approach of Yamagami and Shimizu (2008) and Matsuo and Serizawa (2007) by adding a quadratic isovector density term, motivated by the failure of standard mixed-type pairing forces (with only isoscalar density dependence) to reproduce the strong \(\alpha\)-dependence of experimental pairing gaps. The optimized pair-DF enables consistent global description of pairing properties across nuclei with large neutron excess, and the established correlation between \(\eta_1\) and the isovector effective mass provides a guideline for constructing pair-DFs compatible with different Skyrme particle-hole functionals, reducing the spread in predicted pairing gaps toward the neutron drip line.
