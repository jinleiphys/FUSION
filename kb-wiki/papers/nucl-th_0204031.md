# Hybrid Baryons

> We review the status of hybrid baryons. The only known way to study hybrids rigorously is via excited adiabatic potentials. Hybrids can be modelled by both the bag and flux-tube models. The low-lying hybrid baryon is N 1/2^+ with a mass of 1.5-1.8 GeV. Hybrid baryons can be produced in the glue-rich processes of diffractive gamma N and pi N production, Psi decays and p pbar annihilation.

# Hybrid Baryons

## Key claim

Hybrid baryons, three quarks with a gluonic excitation, are a necessary consequence of QCD, but the only rigorous definition is via excited adiabatic potentials, which is exact only for very heavy quarks or for specific simplified dynamics; for light quarks, mixing between redefined adiabatic potentials is estimated to be of order 1%, making the concept sensible but approximate.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of hybrid baryons by comparing two modeling approaches: the bag model, where gluons become massive due to confinement inside the bag, and the flux-tube model, where three quarks are connected by a Y-shaped linearly confining flux-tube system. Lattice QCD data for the three-quark potential are parameterized with a Coulomb term and a Y-shaped confining term, and the string tension is extracted. Model predictions for masses and quantum numbers are compared across the bag model, flux-tube model, and QCD sum rules, and production mechanisms in diffractive gamma N and pi N production, Psi decays, and p pbar annihilation are surveyed.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying hybrid baryon is N 1/2+ with a mass of 1.5–1.8 GeV (from abstract and text).
- The string tension from lattice QCD for the three-quark Y-shaped potential is sigma = 0.1528(27) GeV^2 (from Eq. 1 and surrounding text).
- In the flux-tube model, N hybrids are at 1.87(10) GeV and Delta hybrids at 2.08(10) GeV (from text).
- The bag model obtains the lightest hybrid, N 1/2+, at approximately 1.55 GeV (from text).
- QCD sum rules estimate the low-lying N 1/2+ hybrid at approximately 1.5 GeV (from text).
- A J^P = 1/2+ 2-sigma peak at mass 1834(+46/-55) MeV was seen in Psi -> p pbar eta (from text).
- The bag model predicts a flavor-singlet Lambda hybrid at approximately 1.65 GeV (from text).
- Higher mass N and Delta hybrids are in the range 1.5–2.5 GeV (from text).
- For large Q^2 electroproduction, conventional baryon transverse amplitudes scale as 1/Q^3 while hybrid transverse amplitudes scale as 1/Q^5; longitudinal amplitudes scale as 1/Q^4 for both (from Table 2).

## Context

This review extends the study of gluonic excitations from hybrid mesons and glueballs to the baryon sector, where hybrid baryons lack J^P exotics that make hybrid mesons easier to identify. The work builds on lattice QCD calculations of the three-quark potential (Takahashi et al., 2001) that favor Y-shaped confinement over pairwise confinement, and on model calculations by Capstick and Isgur (1986) for the conventional baryon spectrum and by Capstick and Page (1999) for flux-tube model hybrids. The review enables experimental searches at Jefferson Lab, ELSA, and BES by identifying promising production channels (diffractive gamma N and pi N production, Psi decays, p pbar annihilation) and decay modes (to N rho, N omega, and P-wave baryons such as S11(1535)).
