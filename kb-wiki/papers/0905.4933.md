# Renormalization vs Strong Form Factors for One Boson Exchange Potentials

> We analyze the One Boson Exchange Potential from the point of view of Renormalization theory. We show that the nucleon-meson Lagrangean while predicting the NN force does not predict the NN scattering matrix nor the deuteron properties unambiguously due to the appearance of short distance singularities. While the problem has traditionally been circumvented by introducing vertex functions via phenomenological strong form factors, we propose to impose physical renormalization conditions on the scattering amplitude at low energies. Working in the large Nc approximation with pi,sigma,rho and omega mesons, we show that, once these conditions are applied, results for low energy phases of proton-neutron scattering as well as deuteron properties become largely insensitive to the form factors and to the vector mesons and yield a reasonable agreement with the data, for realistic values of the coupling constants.

## Key claim

The nucleon-meson Lagrangian, while predicting the NN force, does not predict the NN scattering matrix or deuteron properties unambiguously due to short-distance singularities; imposing physical renormalization conditions on the scattering amplitude at low energies makes results largely insensitive to the form factors and vector mesons, yielding reasonable agreement with data for realistic coupling constants.

## Method

The authors derived the One Boson Exchange (OBE) potential from a chiral Lagrangian in the large Nc approximation, including π, σ, ρ, and ω mesons. They implemented coordinate space renormalization by means of boundary conditions to remove the cutoff and achieve short-distance insensitivity, solving the Schrödinger equation for the local, energy-independent OBE potential. The renormalization approach was compared with the traditional method of introducing phenomenological strong form factors (monopole and exponential parameterizations) to regularize the short-distance singularities.

## Key numbers

- The tensor force component W_T(r) behaves as ~1/r^3 at short distances, with the coefficient proportional to (g_πNN^2 - f_ρNN^2)/(4πΛ_N^2) * 1/4
- The central potential V_C(r) at short distances approaches (g_ωNN^2 - g_σNN^2)/(4π) * 1/r
- The spin component W_S(r) at short distances approaches (1/12) * (g_πNN^2 m_π^2 + 2 f_ρNN^2 m_ρ^2)/(4π Λ_N^2) * 1/r
- For monopole form factors, the regularized Yukawa potential at short distances becomes (1/4π) * (Λ-m)^2/(2Λ), diverging linearly as Λ → ∞
- The pion-nucleon cutoff Λ_πNN must be > 1.3 GeV to fit deuteron properties with traditional form factors
- The nucleon mass is given by M_N = g_σNN f_π, with f_π = 92.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to the One Pion Exchange potential by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005) to the full OBE potential including σ, ρ, and ω mesons. It contradicts the traditional approach of using phenomenological strong form factors as in the work of Machleidt (1987, 2000) and the Nijmegen group's Partial Wave Analysis (Stoks, 1993), which parameterized the interaction below 1.4 fm with an energy-dependent square well potential. The renormalization approach enables a consistent treatment of short-distance singularities without introducing ad hoc form factors, and opens the possibility of analyzing consequences for electroweak processes such as Meson Exchange Currents.
