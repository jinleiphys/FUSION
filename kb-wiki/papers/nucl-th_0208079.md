# Jet Quenching with Detailed Balance in a Thermal QCD Medium

> The effect of detailed balance in induced gluon radiation for an energetic parton propagating inside a quark-gluon plasma is discussed. The stimulated thermal gluon emission reduces while absorption increases the parton's energy. The net contribution results in a reduction of the effective parton energy loss. The relative reduction of the parton energy loss is found to be important for intermediate parton energies and negligible for very high energies. This will increase the energy dependence of the parton energy loss and will affect the shape of suppression of moderately high p_T hadron spectra.

## Key claim

The paper argues that detailed balance (stimulated thermal gluon emission and absorption) in a quark-gluon plasma reduces the net energy loss of an energetic parton, with the reduction being significant for intermediate parton energies but negligible for asymptotically high energies, thereby increasing the energy dependence of parton energy loss.

## Method

The authors calculate the parton energy loss from induced gluon radiation including both stimulated emission and thermal absorption, using the opacity expansion approach of Gyulassy, Lévai, and Vitev (GLV) to first order in opacity. They model the medium as a static color-screened Yukawa potential (Gyulassy-Wang model) and subtract the zero-temperature vacuum radiation spectrum to isolate the thermal contributions. The final-state radiation and rescattering-induced radiation are treated separately, with the LPM interference effect included via a factor \(1-\exp(i\omega_1 y_{10})\).

## Key numbers

- The relative reduction of effective parton energy loss due to thermal absorption is about 30–10% for intermediate parton energies, for \(L/\lambda_g = 3\) and 5 with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).
- The asymptotic energy loss from rescattering-induced radiation (zero-temperature part) scales as \(\Delta E_{rad}^{(1)}/E \approx (\alpha_s C_F \mu^2 L^2)/(4\lambda_g E) [\ln(2E/\mu^2 L) - 0.048]\).
- The asymptotic thermal absorption contribution from rescattering scales as \(\Delta E_{abs}^{(1)}/E \approx -(\pi \alpha_s C_F)/(3) (L T^2)/(\lambda_g E^2) [\ln(\mu^2 L/T) - 1 + \gamma_E - 6\zeta'(2)/\pi^2]\), where \(\gamma_E \approx 0.5772\) and \(\zeta'(2) \approx -0.9376\).
- The final-state thermal absorption (without rescattering) scales as \(\Delta E_{abs}^{(0)}/E \approx -(\pi \alpha_s C_F)/3 (T^2/E^2) [\ln(4ET/\mu^2) + 2 - \gamma_E + 6\zeta'(2)/\pi^2]\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of jet quenching via induced gluon radiation (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff 1997, Zakharov 1996, Gyulassy-Lévai-Vitev 2000, Guo-Wang 2000, Wiedemann 2000) by incorporating detailed balance in a thermal QCD medium. The authors show that thermal absorption partially cancels stimulated emission, reducing the net energy loss, which modifies the shape of the suppression of moderately high \(p_T\) hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions. This enables more accurate modeling of parton energy loss at intermediate energies relevant to RHIC and LHC experiments.
