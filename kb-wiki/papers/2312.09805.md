# A new causal and stable theory of viscous chiral hydrodynamics

> We construct the general theory of first-order relativistic hydrodynamics for a fluid exhibiting a chiral anomaly, including all possible viscous terms allowed by symmetry. Using standard techniques, we compute the necessary and sufficient conditions for this theory to be relativistically causal in the nonlinear regime and for thermal equilibria to be linearly stable. This is the first theory of first-order chiral hydrodynamics suitable for numerical simulations.

## Key claim
This paper constructs the first theory of first-order relativistic viscous chiral hydrodynamics that is both causal in the fully nonlinear regime and linearly stable around thermal equilibria, making it suitable for numerical simulations. The authors note that causality requires the heat diffusion term proportional to vorticity to vanish, which they address through a specific frame transformation.

## Method
The authors apply the BDNK (Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronh-Kovtun) method to write the most general constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and axial current up to first order in derivatives, including all symmetry-allowed terms. They derive necessary and sufficient conditions for causality using characteristic analysis of the partial differential equations, and for linear stability via Fourier analysis around homogeneous thermal equilibrium states in zero external fields.

## Key numbers
- The theory contains 20 transport parameters in the constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and axial current
- Causality demands that the coefficient $\theta_\omega$ multiplying the vorticity term in the heat current must vanish
- The characteristic speed of shear waves $\eta/\theta_2$ must be less than the speed of light for causality
- The causal region of parameter space is defined by combinations of transport parameters denoted $\lambda_i$ for $i=0,\dots,3$, shown in a phase diagram

## Context
This work extends the BDNK method (Bemfica 2017, Kovtun 2019, Bemfica 2019, Hoult 2020, Bemfica 2020) to include chiral anomaly effects, addressing the problem that previous chiral hydrodynamic theories (Erdmenger 2008, Son 2009, Neiman 2010, Sadofyev 2010, Chen 2015) violated causality and stability as shown by Speranza (2021). The theory enables consistent numerical simulations of chiral transport effects in quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, incorporating axial and vector charge currents alongside energy-momentum flow.
