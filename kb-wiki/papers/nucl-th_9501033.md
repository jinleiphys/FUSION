# Instant Two-Body Equation in Breit Frame

> A quasipotential formalism for elastic scattering from relativistic bound states is based on applying an instant constraint to both initial and final states in the Breit frame. This formalism is advantageous for the analysis of electromagnetic interactions because current conservation and four momentum conservation are realized within a three-dimensional formalism. Wave functions are required in a frame where the total momentum is nonzero, which means that the usual partial wave analysis is inapplicable. In this work, the three-dimensional equation is solved numerically, taking into account the relevant symmetries. A dynamical boost of the interaction also is needed for the instant formalism, which in general requires that the boosted interaction be defined as the solution of a four-dimensional equation. For the case of a scalar separable interaction, this equation is solved and the Lorentz invariance of the three-dimensional formulation using the boosted interaction is verified. For more realistic interactions, a simple approximation is used to characterize the boost of the interaction.

## Key claim

The paper develops a quasipotential formalism for elastic scattering from relativistic bound states using an instant constraint applied to both initial and final states in the Breit frame, which preserves current conservation and four-momentum conservation within a three-dimensional formalism; however, the approach requires a dynamical boost of the interaction (rather than a simple kinematical boost) to maintain Lorentz invariance, and for realistic interactions this boost must be approximated rather than solved exactly.

## Method

The authors solve the three-dimensional quasipotential equation numerically in the Breit frame, where the total three-momentum is nonzero, using basis functions with good total angular momentum projection and parity-time-reversal symmetry instead of the usual partial-wave analysis. For a scalar separable interaction, the four-dimensional equation defining the boosted quasipotential is solved exactly, and the Lorentz invariance of the three-dimensional formulation is verified by checking that the deuteron mass remains invariant under boost. For more realistic meson-exchange interactions (based on the Bonn B potential), the boost is approximated by a renormalization factor λ(P) applied to the rest-frame interaction, fitted to maintain constant deuteron mass.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy used: 2m - M ≈ 2.22464 MeV
- For the scalar separable potential, the form factor χ(p) = (1 + p²/μ²)⁻² with μ = 200 MeV was used
- For the Bonn B potential, the scalar coupling was increased by about 6% (from g²_σ/4π = 8.0769 to 8.5503) to obtain correct deuteron binding when negative-energy sectors are included
- When λ(P) = 1 (no boost correction), the deuteron binding energy increases from ≈2.2 MeV at P² = 0 to ≈4.2 MeV at P² = 50 fm⁻²
- With the perturbative boost approximation (Eq. 6.4 in the text), a renormalization factor λ = 0.984 at P² = 50 fm⁻² is required to keep the mass invariant, compared with λ = 0.882 when using the simpler approximation k^QP(P) ≈ k^QP(P₀)/λ

## Context

This work extends the quasipotential formalism of Mandelzweig and Wallace (1987) and Wallace and Mandelzweig (1989) by solving the instant-constraint two-body equation in the Breit frame rather than the center-of-mass frame, which is necessary for consistent treatment of electromagnetic form factors. The approach contrasts with that of Hummel and Tjon (recent work cited in the paper), who used an instant quasipotential with a kinematical boost that leads to inconsistency with current conservation. The formalism enables calculation of deuteron wave functions at nonzero total momentum, which will be used in a subsequent paper for computing electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron.
