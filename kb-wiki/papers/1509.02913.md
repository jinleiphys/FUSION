# Quasiparticle equation of state for anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We present a new method for imposing a realistic equation of state in anisotropic hydrodynamics. The method relies on the introduction of a single finite-temperature quasiparticle mass which is fit to lattice data. By taking moments of the Boltzmann equation, we obtain a set of coupled partial differential equations which can be used to describe the 3+1d spacetime evolution of an anisotropic relativistic system. We then specialize to the case of a 0+1d system undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion and subject to the relaxation-time approximation collisional kernel. Using this setup, we compare results obtained using the new quasiparticle equation of state method with those obtained using the standard method for imposing the equation of state in anisotropic hydrodynamics. We demonstrate that the temperature evolution obtained using the two methods is nearly identical and that there are only small differences in the pressure anisotropy. However, we find that there are significant differences in the evolution of the bulk pressure correction.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for imposing a realistic equation of state in anisotropic hydrodynamics by introducing a single finite-temperature quasiparticle mass fit to lattice data, and demonstrates that while the temperature evolution and pressure anisotropy obtained with this method are nearly identical to the standard method, there are significant differences in the evolution of the bulk pressure correction.

## Method

The authors introduce a temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass into the Boltzmann equation by adding extra terms from spacetime gradients of the thermal mass, and enforce energy-momentum conservation by introducing a non-equilibrium background field \(B g^{\mu\nu}\) to the energy-momentum tensor. They take moments of the Boltzmann equation to obtain 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics equations, then specialize to a 0+1d boost-invariant Bjorken expansion with a relaxation-time approximation collisional kernel. The quasiparticle mass is determined by matching to lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration for the QCD interaction measure, and the resulting equations are solved numerically and compared to the standard method for imposing the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for quark-gluon plasma creation is \(T_c \sim 160 \; \rm MeV\).
- The Stefan-Boltzmann pressure limit is \({\cal P}_{\rm SB}= N_{\rm dof} T^4 / \pi^2\) with \(N_c=N_f=3\).
- The lattice parameterization for the interaction measure uses \(t\equiv T/(0.2 \; \rm GeV)\) with parameters \(h_0=0.1396\), \(h_1=-0.1800\), \(h_2=0.0350\), \(f_0=2.76\), \(f_1=6.79\), \(f_2=-5.29\), \(g_1=-0.47\), \(g_2=1.04\), and \(h_3=0.01\).
- In the massless limit, the relaxation time relation gives \(\tau_{\rm eq}(T)= \frac{5 \eta}{4{\cal P}_{\rm eq}(T)}\).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/{\cal S}_{\rm eq}\equiv \bar{\eta}\) is held fixed during evolution.

## Context

This work extends previous anisotropic hydrodynamics approaches by Martinez (2010), Florkowski (2010), and Ryblewski (2011) that relied on conformal factorization, as well as the massive anisotropic hydrodynamics of Nopoush (2014) which used temperature-independent masses. The method enables self-consistent incorporation of non-conformal effects from the QCD equation of state into anisotropic hydrodynamics, which is important for phenomenological applications involving freezeout hypersurfaces in heavy-ion collisions. The approach builds on the quasiparticle framework of Gorenstein (1995), Jeon (1995), and Romatschke (2011) for ensuring thermodynamic consistency with temperature-dependent masses.
