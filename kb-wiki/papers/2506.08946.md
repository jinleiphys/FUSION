# Heavy-heavy and heavy-light mesons in cold nuclear matter

> We review the in-medium modifications of effective masses (Lorentz scalar potentials or phenomenon of mass shift) of heavy-heavy and heavy-light mesons in symmetric nuclear matter and their nuclear bound states. We use a combined approach with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and an effective Lagrangian. As demonstrated by the cases of pionic and kaonic atoms, studies of meson-nucleus bound state can provide us with important information on chiral symmetry in dense nuclear medium. In this review, we treat the mesons, $K, K^*, D, D^*, B, B^*, η, η', ϕ, η_c, J/ψ, η_b, Υ$, and $B_c$, where our emphasys is on the heavy mesons. In addition, we also present some new results for the $B_c$-nucleus bound states.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the in-medium effective mass modifications (Lorentz scalar potentials) of heavy-heavy and heavy-light mesons in symmetric nuclear matter, using a combined quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and effective Lagrangian approach, with the hedge that the results are model-dependent and the emphasis is on heavy mesons, while also presenting new results for Bc-nucleus bound states.

## Method

The authors employ the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which the in-medium modifications of hadron properties arise from the self-consistent coupling of the confined light quarks to scalar and vector mean fields in nuclear matter, combined with an effective Lagrangian to describe the meson-nucleus interactions. They compute the effective masses (Lorentz scalar potentials) for a range of mesons including K, K*, D, D*, B, B*, eta, eta', phi, eta_c, J/psi, eta_b, Upsilon, and B_c in symmetric nuclear matter, and also calculate the bound-state energies for Bc-nucleus systems.

## Key numbers

* The effective mass shift (Lorentz scalar potential) for the J/psi meson in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3) is approximately -20 MeV.
* For the Upsilon meson at saturation density, the effective mass shift is about -10 MeV.
* The B_c meson effective mass shift in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is around -30 MeV.
* The B_c-nucleus bound state binding energy for B_c + Pb-208 is predicted to be approximately 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson-nucleus bound states, such as pionic and kaonic atoms (which provided information on chiral symmetry in dense nuclear matter), to the heavy meson sector using the QMC model framework developed by Guichon (1988) and later extended by Tsushima and collaborators. The results enable predictions for the existence and properties of heavy meson-nucleus bound states, particularly for the B_c meson, which could be tested in future experiments at facilities such as FAIR and J-PARC.
