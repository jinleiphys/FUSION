# Rho meson form factors in a confining Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> Elastic electromagnetic form factors for the $ρ^+$ meson are calculated in a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model which incorporates quark confinement through the use of the proper-time regularization scheme. A comparison is made with recent lattice QCD results and previous quark model calculations for static quantities and the Sachs form factors. The results are qualitatively in good agreement with the lattice QCD calculations, with the exception of the quadrupole moment and corresponding form factor, which may be related to a lack of spherical symmetry on the lattice.

## Key claim

Elastic electromagnetic form factors for the rho+ meson calculated in a confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model show qualitative agreement with lattice QCD results, except for the quadrupole moment and corresponding form factor, which the authors suggest may be related to a lack of spherical symmetry on the lattice rather than a failure of the model.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with proper-time regularization that incorporates an infrared cutoff to mimic quark confinement. The rho meson electromagnetic current is computed from Feynman diagrams involving dressed quark propagators, Bethe-Salpeter vertices for the rho meson, and a dressed quark-photon vertex that is treated at three levels of sophistication: pointlike, dressed via the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (including vector meson contributions), and further including pion cloud corrections that generate a Pauli form factor for the dressed quarks. The model parameters are fixed by fitting to the physical pion decay constant and the masses of the pi, rho and omega mesons.

## Key numbers

* Dressed quark mass M = 0.4 GeV, infrared cutoff Lambda_IR = 0.24 GeV, ultraviolet cutoff Lambda_UV = 0.645 GeV
* Lagrangian couplings: G_pi = 19.04 GeV^-2, G_rho = 11.04 GeV^-2, G_omega = 10.41 GeV^-2
* At the physical pion mass (140 MeV), the model gives for the rho+: charge radius squared = 0.67 fm^2, magnetic moment = 3.14 mu_N, quadrupole moment = -0.070 fm^2
* The lattice QCD result from Owen (2015) at m_pi = 161 MeV gives: charge radius squared = 0.670(68) fm^2, magnetic moment = 2.613(97) mu_N, quadrupole moment = -0.0452(61) fm^2
* Including the BSE dressing and pion cloud increases the magnetic moment by 24%, the quadrupole moment by 22%, and the charge radius squared by 16% relative to the pointlike vertex

## Context

This work extends the rho meson form factor calculation of Cloet (2014) to investigate quark mass dependence and perform detailed comparison with recent lattice QCD results from Owen (2015) and Shultz (2015). The model results are compared with constituent quark models (Cardarelli 1994, De Melo 1997, Melikhov 2001, Jaus 2002, Choi 2004, Biernat 2014), QCD sum rules (Samsonov 2003, Aliev 2004), and Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations (Hawes 1998, Bhagwat 2006, Roberts 2011, Pitschmann 2012). The agreement with the Owen (2015) lattice data for the charge radius as a function of pion mass is described as remarkable, and the pion cloud contributions become negligible for m_pi^2 greater than about 0.4 GeV^2.
