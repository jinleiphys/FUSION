# Intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling in multipion production processes

> We propose an intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling Hamiltonian within a unitary multiparticle-production model of the Auerbach- Avin-Blankenbecler-Sugar (AABS) type in which the pion field is represented by the thermal-density matrix.Using this Hamiltonian, we explain the appearance of the negative-binomial (NB) distribution for pions and the well-known empirical relation, the so-called Wr\' oblewski relation, in which the dispersion $D$ of the pion- multiplicity distribution is linearly related to the average multiplicity $<n>$ : $D = A <n> + B$, with the coefficient $A < 1$. The Hamiltonian of our model is expressed linearly in terms of the generators of the $SU(1,1)$ group.We also find the generating function for the pion field, which reduces to the generating function of the NB distribution limit $T \to 0$.

# Intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling in multipion production processes

## Key claim
The authors propose that the negative-binomial (NB) distribution observed in pion multiplicity distributions and the empirical Wróblewski relation (linear dependence of dispersion on average multiplicity) can be explained by an intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling Hamiltonian with SU(1,1) dynamical symmetry, provided the vacuum expectation value of the Hamiltonian exceeds the "zero-point energy" corresponding to λ = 1/2.

## Method
The authors construct a unitary eikonal model of the Auerbach-Avin-Blankenbecler-Sugar (AABS) type in which the pion field is represented by a thermal-density operator. The effective Hamiltonian is expressed linearly in terms of the generators of the SU(1,1) group using a Holstein-Primakoff realization, with an intensity-dependent coupling term that depends on the pion number operator. The pion-generating function is derived analytically for both zero and finite temperature, and the resulting multiplicity distribution and its moments are computed in impact-parameter space.

## Key numbers
- The shape parameter of the NB distribution at T = 0 is 2λ, where λ labels the SU(1,1) unitary irreducible representation and is related to the vacuum expectation value of the Hamiltonian.
- For the k-mode at T = 0, the Wróblewski relation gives coefficients A = (2λ)^(-1/2) and B = (λ/2)^(1/2); if λ > 1/2, then A < 1.
- The average number of k-mode pions at T = 0 is given by n̄(θ_k) = 2λ sinh²(θ_k).
- At T = 0, the dispersion-to-mean relation is d²(θ_k)/n̄²(θ_k) = 1/(2λ) + 1/n̄(θ_k).
- In the limit T → ∞, the ratio d_T²(θ_k)/n̄_Tk²(θ_k) approaches 1 + tanh²(2θ_k), tending toward chaotic behavior when ω_k ≫ g_k(Y,B).

## Context
This work extends the AABS unitary eikonal models (Auerbach, Avin, Blankenbecler, Sugar, 1970s) by incorporating a thermal-density operator for the pion field and an intensity-dependent coupling inspired by an observation of Golab-Meyer and Ruijgrok (date not given) that the Wróblewski relation could be satisfied if the pion-nucleon coupling increases linearly with mean multiplicity. The model provides a dynamical explanation for the NB distribution that differs from the clan-structure interpretation, which the authors note may be merely a parametrization (citing reference [9]). The approach enables calculation of all higher-order moments of the pion field via the generating function, and the authors suggest future extension to include two-pion correlations and mode-mode interactions to describe resonance emission.
