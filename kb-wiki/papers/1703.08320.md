# Linking structure and dynamics in $(p,pn)$ reactions with Borromean nuclei: the $^{11}$Li$(p,pn){^{10}}$Li case

> One-neutron removal $(p,pn)$ reactions induced by two-neutron Borromean nuclei are studied within a Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) reaction framework, which incorporates the three-body character of the incident nucleus. The relative energy distribution of the residual unbound two-body subsystem, which is assumed to retain information on the structure of the original three-body projectile, is computed by evaluating the transition amplitude for different neutron-core final states in the continuum. These transition amplitudes depend on the overlaps between the original three-body ground-state wave function and the two-body continuum states populated in the reaction, thus ensuring a consistent description of the incident and final nuclei. By comparing different $^{11}$Li three-body models, it is found that the $^{11}$Li$(p,pn){^{10}}$Li relative energy spectrum is very sensitive to the position of the $p_{1/2}$ and $s_{1/2}$ states in $^{10}$Li and to the partial wave content of these configurations within the $^{11}$Li ground-state wave function. The possible presence of a low-lying $d_{5/2}$ resonance is discussed. The coupling of the single particle configurations with the non-zero spin of the $^{9}$Li core, which produces a spin-spin splitting of the states, is also studied. Among the considered models, the best agreement with the available data is obtained with a $^{11}$Li model that incorporates the actual spin of the core and contains $\sim$31\% of $p_{1/2}$-wave content in the $n$-$^9$Li subsystem, in accord with our previous findings for the $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li transfer reaction, and a near-threshold virtual state.

## Key claim

The relative energy spectrum of the residual unbound $^{10}$Li subsystem in the $^{11}$Li$(p,pn){^{10}}$Li reaction is very sensitive to the position of the $p_{1/2}$ and $s_{1/2}$ states in $^{10}$Li and to the partial wave content of these configurations within the $^{11}$Li ground-state wave function, but the best agreement with available data is obtained only when the actual spin of the $^{9}$Li core is included, producing a spin-spin splitting of the states.

## Method

The authors applied a Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) reaction framework, which incorporates the three-body character of the incident Borromean nucleus $^{11}$Li, to compute the relative energy distribution of the residual $n$-$^{9}$Li system after one-neutron removal. The transition amplitudes were evaluated for different neutron-core final states in the continuum, using overlaps between the three-body ground-state wave function of $^{11}$Li (expanded in hyperspherical harmonics with an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator basis) and the two-body continuum states of $^{10}$Li. The $p$-$n$ continuum was discretized using a binning procedure with a step of $\Delta E=15$ MeV, and the $p$-$^{11}$Li, $p$-$^{10}$Li and $n$-$^{10}$Li interactions were obtained by folding an effective $NN$ interaction (Paris-Hamburg $g$-matrix parametrization) with ground-state densities from Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key numbers

* The $^{11}$Li ground-state energy was taken as $-0.37$ MeV.
* For the spinless-core model P3, the $s_{1/2}$ virtual state had a scattering length of $-29.8$ fm, the $p_{1/2}$ resonance was at 0.50 MeV, and the $d_{5/2}$ resonance at 4.3 MeV; the $^{11}$Li ground state contained 64% $s_{1/2}$, 30% $p_{1/2}$, and 3% $d_{5/2}$ partial wave content.
* For the spinless-core model P4, the $s_{1/2}$ scattering length was $-16.2$ fm, the $p_{1/2}$ resonance at 0.23 MeV, and the $d_{5/2}$ resonance at 4.3 MeV; the $^{11}$Li ground state contained 27% $s_{1/2}$, 67% $p_{1/2}$, and 3% $d_{5/2}$.
* For the spinless-core model P5, the $s_{1/2}$ scattering length was $-29.8$ fm, the $p_{1/2}$ resonance at 0.50 MeV, and the $d_{5/2}$ resonance at 1.5 MeV; the $^{11}$Li ground state contained 39% $s_{1/2}$, 35% $p_{1/2}$, and 23% $d_{5/2}$.
* For the model including the $^{9}$Li spin (P1I), the $2^-$ ground state of $^{10}$Li had a scattering length of $-37.9$ fm, the $p$ resonances were at 0.37 and 0.61 MeV, and the $^{11}$Li ground state contained 67% $s_{1/2}$, 31% $p_{1/2}$, and 1% $d_{5/2}$ partial wave content.
* The best agreement with experimental data was obtained with model P1I, which contains approximately 31% $p_{1/2}$-wave content in the $n$-$^{9}$Li subsystem.

## Context

This work extends the Transfer-to-the-Continuum framework previously applied to $(p,pn)$ reactions with two-body projectiles (Moro 2015) to Borromean three-body projectiles, and builds on a prior study of the $^{11}$Li(p,d)$^{10}$Li transfer reaction (Gomez-Ramos 2017). The formalism enables direct comparison of absolute cross sections with experimental data from GSI (Aksyutina 2008), avoiding the limitations of simple Breit-Wigner fits that ignore reaction dynamics. The results show that the spin-spin splitting of states due to the $^{9}$Li core spin, rather than a low-lying $d_{5/2}$ resonance, can explain the measured spectrum, but the authors note that experiments with better energy resolution are needed to distinguish between these possibilities.
