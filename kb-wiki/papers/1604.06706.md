# Octupole correlations in the nucleus $^{144}$Ba described with symmetry conserving configuration mixing calculations

> We study the interplay of quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom in the structure of the isotope $^{144}$Ba. A symmetry conserving configuration mixing method (SCCM) based on a Gogny energy density functional (EDF) has been used. The method includes particle number, parity and angular momentum restoration as well as axial quadrupole and octupole shape mixing within the generator coordinate method. Predictions both for excitation energies and electromagnetic transition probabilities are in good agreement with the most recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a symmetry conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) method based on the Gogny D1S energy density functional, including simultaneous particle number, parity, and angular momentum restoration plus axial quadrupole and octupole shape mixing, can reproduce the low-lying positive and negative parity states of \(^{144}\)Ba in qualitative agreement with experiment, but the theoretical spectrum is systematically stretched because triaxial and time-reversal symmetry breaking components are not included.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations with constraints on axial quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and octupole (\(\beta_3\)) deformation parameters to generate a set of 130 intrinsic states. Particle number, parity, and angular momentum are restored via projection operators, and these projected states are mixed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) in one dimension (\(\beta_2\) only or \(\beta_3\) only) and two dimensions (\(\beta_2,\beta_3\) simultaneously). The Gogny D1S interaction is used consistently at both the mean-field and beyond-mean-field levels.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy from the 2D-GCM calculation: \(-1187.547\) MeV, compared to \(-1185.931\) MeV (1D-GCM-\(\beta_2\)) and \(-1186.709\) MeV (1D-GCM-\(\beta_3\)).
* Mean-field minimum of the HFB potential energy surface: \(-1180.772\) MeV at \((\beta_2,\beta_3) = (0.2, \pm 0.1)\).
* Correlation energy gained by symmetry restoration for \(J^\pi = 0^+\): approximately 4.5 MeV at the absolute minima.
* In-band E2 transition matrix element for \(0^+ \rightarrow 2^+\): 1.023 \(e b\) (2D-GCM), compared to experimental 1.042\(^{+17}_{-22}\) \(e b\).
* In-band E2 transition matrix element for \(2^+ \rightarrow 4^+\): 1.845 \(e b\) (2D-GCM), compared to experimental 1.860\(^{+86}_{-81}\) \(e b\).
* E3 transition matrix element for \(0^+ \rightarrow 3^-\): 0.460 \(e b^{3/2}\) (2D-GCM), corresponding to \(B(E3,3^- \rightarrow 0^+) = 25\) W.u., compared to experimental \(48^{+25}_{-34}\) W.u.
* E3 transition matrix element for \(2^+ \rightarrow 5^-\): 0.810 \(e b^{3/2}\) (2D-GCM), with experimental upper limit \(<1.2\) \(e b^{3/2}\).

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field calculations with the Gogny EDF to include octupole degrees of freedom, which had previously been explored only within the relativistic mean-field framework for nuclei such as \(^{224}\)Ra (PRC 92, 041304, 2015; PLB 753, 227, 2016). The results for \(^{144}\)Ba are compared to recent multi-step Coulomb excitation experiments at ATLAS-CARIBU (PRL 116, 112503, 2016). The authors note that including triaxial cranking intrinsic states, as done in earlier work for quadrupole degrees of freedom (PLB 746, 341, 2015; PRL 116, 052502, 2016), would compress the stretched theoretical spectrum and improve quantitative agreement.
