# Vacuum Structures in Hamiltonian Light-Front Dynamics

> Hamiltonian light-front dynamics of quantum fields may provide a useful approach to systematic non-perturbative approximations to quantum field theories. We investigate inequivalent Hilbert-space representations of the light-front field algebra in which the stability group of the light-front is implemented by unitary transformations. The Hilbert space representation of states is generated by the operator algebra from the vacuum state. There is a large class of vacuum states besides the Fock vacuum which meet all the invariance requirements. The light-front Hamiltonian must annihilate the vacuum and have a positive spectrum. We exhibit relations of the Hamiltonian to the nontrivial vacuum structure.

# Vacuum Structures in Hamiltonian Light-Front Dynamics

## Key claim

Inequivalent Hilbert-space representations of the light-front field algebra exist beyond the Fock vacuum, and these nontrivial vacuum structures are associated with the zero-mode behavior of fields at vanishing longitudinal momentum $p^+ = 0$, but the paper does not demonstrate that any specific interacting Hamiltonian actually requires such a representation for a Poincaré-invariant theory.

## Method

The authors formulate an abstract $C^*$-algebra for light-front fields using Weyl operators $U(f)$ built from Schlieder-Seiler test functions, then apply the Gelfand-Naimark-Segal (GNS) construction to generate Hilbert-space representations from vacuum functionals $E[U(f)]$. They classify admissible vacuum functionals by requiring positivity, normalization, reality, invariance under the null-plane stability group $\mathcal{N}$, and cluster properties, and show that functionals depending only on the zero-mode limit $f_0(p_\perp) = \lim_{p^+ \to 0} f(p)/ip^+$ produce inequivalent representations.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal momentum operator $P^+$ has spectrum $[0, \infty]$ with a point eigenvalue $0$ for the vacuum state.
- The test function space $\hat{\mathcal{S}}(\mathbb{R}^3)$ consists of functions $f(\mathbf{p})$ that are Schwartz functions in $\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^3)$ multiplied by $p^+$.
- The canonical commutation relation for Hermitean scalar fields is $[\phi(\mathbf{p}), \phi(\mathbf{p}')] = \delta(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{p}') / (2p^+)$.
- The Fock vacuum functional is $E_0(f) = e^{-\frac{1}{4}(f,f)}$, where $(f,g)$ is the invariant inner product $\int d^3\mathbf{p}\,\theta(p^+) \bar{f}(\mathbf{p}) g(\mathbf{p}) / p^+$.
- A nontrivial vacuum example uses $w_T^{(1)}(f) = \varphi_0 f_0(0)$ for a real parameter $\varphi_0$ and $w_T^{(2)}(f) = \frac{1}{2} \int d^2 p_\perp |f_0(p_\perp)|^2 \eta(|p_\perp|^2)$ for a fixed positive function $\eta$.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic field-theory approach of Araki (1960s) and Haag to the light-front formalism pioneered by Dirac (1949), where the null plane $x^+ = 0$ is invariant under a kinematic subgroup $\mathcal{N}$. It directly addresses the claim by Weinberg (1966) that light-front dynamics suppresses vacuum polarization, showing that nontrivial vacua can still arise from zero-mode distributions. The paper provides the formal foundation for investigating whether quark condensates and other nonperturbative vacuum effects can be accommodated in light-front Hamiltonian approaches such as the discretized light-cone quantization of Pauli and Brodsky (1985) and the Tamm-Dancoff truncations of Tang (1991). It does not construct explicit Hamiltonians or regularization schemes that realize these vacua in interacting theories.
