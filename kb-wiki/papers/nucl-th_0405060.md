# Form factors of hadronic systems in various forms of relativistic quantum mechanics

> The form factor of hadronic systems in various forms of relativistic quantum mechanics is considered. Motivated by the agreement of the nucleon ``point-form'' results with experiment, results for a toy model corresponding to the simplest Feynman diagram are first presented. These ones include the results for this diagram, which plays the role of an experiment, for the front-form and instant-form in standard kinematics (q^+=0 and Breit frame), but also in unconventional kinematics and finally a Dirac's point-form inspired approach. Results for an earlier ``point-form'' approach are reminded. Results are also presented for the pion charge form factor. Conclusions as for the efficiency of various approaches are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, for calculating form factors of hadronic systems using a single-particle current, the standard front-form (with \(q^+=0\)) and instant-form (in the Breit frame) approaches are reliable, while the Dirac-inspired point-form and other unconventional kinematics require large contributions from two-body currents to match exact results.

## Method

The authors compare form factors from a toy model of two scalar particles exchanging an infinitely-massive boson (\(\mu=\infty\)), using the exact Bethe-Salpeter solution as an "experiment." They compute the charge form factor \(F_1(Q^2)\) in the instant form, front form, Dirac's point form, and an earlier "point-form" approach, using a mass-operator solution \(\phi(\vec{k})\propto (\sqrt{e_k}\; (4\,e_k^2-M^2))^{-1}\) with constituent mass \(m=0.3\) GeV and total mass \(M=0.14\) GeV. They then extend the same comparison to the physical pion charge form factor, using a confining potential with string tension 1 GeV/fm and gluon exchange with \(\alpha_s=0.35\).

## Key numbers

- Constituent mass \(m = 0.3\) GeV and total mass \(M = 0.14\) GeV used in the toy model.
- For the pion, the confining potential uses a string tension of 1 GeV/fm and the gluon-exchange strength is \(\alpha_s = 0.35\).
- In the toy model, the earlier "point-form" approach yields an approximate asymptotic behavior of \(Q^{-4}\) for the charge form factor, while most other approaches scale as \(Q^{-2}\).
- For the instant-form and front-form with unconventional kinematics, the charge radius scales like the inverse of the system mass, producing a steep slope at low \(Q^2\) and a suppression factor \(\simeq (M/2m)^2\) at high \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior studies (Amghar 2002) on form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics, using the Wick-Cutkosky model and the vertex-function limit (\(\mu=\infty\)) as testing grounds. It directly compares the Dirac-inspired point form (Desplanques 2004) and an earlier point-form implementation (Sokolov 1985, Klink 1998) against the standard front-form and instant-form approaches. The results enable a critical assessment of which relativistic formalisms can reliably compute hadronic form factors without requiring large two-body current corrections, and they suggest that the standard front- and instant-form approaches are the most appropriate for extracting constituent form factors from pion data.
