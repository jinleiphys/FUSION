# Mixing the Strong and E-W Higgs Sectors

> After noting the well known similarity of the minimal electro-weak Higgs sector with the linear sigma model for the pion and the sigma, it is found that a small mixing term between the two models generates a pion mass. Although the custodial SU(2)L x SU(2)R, and the gauged SU(2)L x U(1) symmetry for the whole model remains intact, the mixing breaks the relative chiral symmetry between the two sectors. The mixing should be calculable from light quark masses as a quantum correction. This simple mechanism of "relative symmetry breaking" is believed to have applications for other forms of symmetry breaking.

## Key claim
A small mixing term between the electro-weak Higgs sector and the linear sigma model for strong interactions generates a pion mass, while the overall custodial SU(2)L x SU(2)R and gauged SU(2)L x U(1) symmetries remain intact; the mixing breaks only the relative chiral symmetry between the two sectors.

## Method
The author constructs a Lagrangian that adds the minimal electro-weak Higgs Lagrangian and the linear sigma model Lagrangian, plus a small mixing term proportional to epsilon^2. The pseudoscalar mass matrix is diagonalized to find the mass eigenvalues, and the vacuum expectation values are recalculated including the mixing corrections. The mixing angle between the two sectors is determined from the ratio of the two vacuum expectation values.

## Key numbers
- The electro-weak Higgs vacuum expectation value is v = 246 GeV, while the linear sigma model vacuum expectation value (pion decay constant) is hat v = 92.6 MeV; their ratio v/hat v is about 2656.
- The mixing angle between the two sectors is theta = 3.764 x 10^-4, approximately equal to hat v / v.
- To generate the correct pion mass, the mixing parameter epsilon should be about 2.70 MeV.
- The pseudoscalar mass eigenvalue for the pion is approximately epsilon^2 v / hat v.

## Context
This work extends the well-known analogy between the minimal electro-weak Higgs sector and the linear sigma model for pions and sigma, as previously discussed by Willenbrock. The mixing approach is similar to the Two-Higgs-Doublet Model but applied to strong and electro-weak sectors rather than supersymmetric or technicolor extensions. A similar effective Lagrangian was previously suggested by Close and Tornqvist for two light scalar meson nonets with only strong interactions. The mechanism of "relative symmetry breaking" is proposed as a general idea that could apply to other symmetry breaking scenarios, such as coupled U(1) models.
