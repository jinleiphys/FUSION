# Contribution of excited states to stellar weak-interaction rates in odd-A nuclei

> Weak-interaction rates, including beta-decay and electron capture, are studied in several odd-A nuclei in the pf-shell region at various densities and temperatures of astrophysical interest. Special attention is paid to the relative contribution to these rates of thermally populated excited states in the decaying nucleus. The nuclear structure involved in the weak processes is studied within a quasiparticle random-phase approximation with residual interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels on top of a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations. In the range of densities and temperatures considered, it is found that the total rates do not differ much from the rates of the ground state fully populated. In any case, the changes are not larger than the uncertainties due to the nuclear model dependence of the rates.

## Key claim

The total stellar weak-interaction rates (beta-decay and electron capture) for odd-A nuclei in the pf-shell region do not differ much from the rates calculated assuming only the ground state is fully populated, and any changes from including thermally populated excited states are not larger than the uncertainties already present due to nuclear model dependence of the rates.

## Method

The nuclear structure was described using a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations (SLy4 interaction), on top of which a quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with residual interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels was applied. Weak-interaction rates were calculated for six odd-A nuclei (45Sc, 51V, 53Fe, 55Fe, 55Mn, 57Fe) by summing Boltzmann-weighted contributions from the ground state and low-lying excited states below 1 MeV, using experimental excitation energies and spin-parity assignments for the thermal population factors.

## Key numbers

- Selfconsistent quadrupole deformations: beta2 = -0.06 in 45Sc, 0.16 in 53Fe, 0.13 in 55Mn, and 0.18 in 57Fe
- QEC values: 45Sc = -0.259 MeV, 51V = -2.472 MeV, 53Fe = +3.742 MeV, 55Fe = +0.231 MeV, 55Mn = -2.603 MeV, 57Fe = -2.659 MeV
- Temperature range considered: T9 = 1 to T9 = 10 (GK)
- Density range considered: rho Ye = 10^6 to 10^10 mol/cm^3
- Maximum thermal energy scale: k_B T = 0.862 MeV at T = 10 GK
- Coupling strengths used in QRPA: chi_ph_GT = 0.10 MeV, kappa_pp_GT = 0.05 MeV
- Effective axial-vector coupling ratio: (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.7 times the bare value
- D = 6146 s in the rate formula

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations by Sarriguren (2013) on even-even nuclei to odd-A nuclei, where low-lying excited states (quasiparticle states below 1 MeV) can contribute significantly to weak rates, unlike in even-even nuclei where first excited states are typically above 1 MeV. The study uses the same Skyrme SLy4 interaction and QRPA framework as Sarriguren (2013), and compares GT strength distributions with experimental data from charge-exchange reactions by Alford (1991, 1993), Baumer (2003), and Elkateb (1994). The results enable more reliable estimates of weak-interaction rates for presupernova and core-collapse simulations, particularly for the silicon-burning stage (rho Ye = 10^7 mol/cm^3, T9 = 3) and pre-collapse conditions (rho Ye = 10^9 mol/cm^3, T9 = 10).
