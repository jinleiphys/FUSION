# Properties of the neutron star crust informed by nuclear structure data

> We perform a Bayesian analysis of the neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) based on a wide set of Skyrme functionals, derived from previous nuclear physics inferences. The novelty of this approach lies in starting from the full multidimensional posterior distribution of nuclear matter parameters, consistent with a comprehensive set of static and dynamic nuclear structure observables. We construct unified EoSs for $npeμ$ matter, where the inner crust of the NS is treated using an extended Thomas-Fermi method, providing for the first time a fully consistent Bayesian treatment of the correlation of bulk with surface as well as with spin-orbit and effective mass parameters. We then employ a standard Bayesian framework to identify those EoSs that satisfy astrophysical constraints from NS mass measurements, the tidal deformability from GW170817, and NICER mass-radius observations. We also examine NS observables, such as the crustal moment of inertia, which is crucial in understanding pulsar glitches. Compared to previous works, we observe an increase in both the NS surface thickness and the crustal moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The authors perform a Bayesian analysis of the neutron star equation of state that, for the first time, uses the full multidimensional posterior distribution of Skyrme functionals from nuclear structure data as a prior, enabling a fully consistent treatment of correlations between bulk, surface, spin-orbit, and effective mass parameters when constructing unified EoSs for the crust and core.

## Method

The authors map a posterior distribution of Skyrme functionals (informed by nuclear masses, charge radii, giant resonances, dipole polarizabilities, and parity-violating asymmetries) onto a metamodel representation that decouples high-order nuclear matter parameters from low-order ones. They construct unified EoSs for npeμ matter using an extended Thomas-Fermi method for the inner crust, then apply Bayesian inference with likelihoods from the mass of PSR J0348+0432, the tidal deformability from GW170817, NICER mass-radius observations, and chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The most probable value of the symmetry energy Esym shifts from approximately 29 MeV (prior from nuclear experiments) to almost 31 MeV (after including chiral constraints and astrophysical data)
- The most probable value of the symmetry energy slope Lsym shifts from approximately 15 MeV to approximately 30 MeV after including chiral constraints
- The crust-core transition density from the ETF method shows a tail at ncc greater than approximately 0.11 fm^{-3}
- The dynamical spinodal estimate from a previous Bayesian study gave ncc = (0.071 ± 0.011) fm^{-3} and Pcc = (0.294 ± 0.102) MeV fm^{-3}, while the current work finds higher values with optimized surface parameters
- The surface parameters from Klausner (2025) are G0 = 125 ± 10 and G1 = 9 ± 36, compared to G0 = 30 ± 9 and G1 = 115 ± 143 from a prior study (Antic 2019)
- The ETF calculation for SLy4 yields a crust-core transition density agreeing with the full ETF result of Martin (2015) within approximately 2.5%

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of Klausner (2025) by incorporating astrophysical constraints from NS mass measurements, GW170817, and NICER, using the nuclear-structure-informed posterior as a physically grounded prior. It improves upon previous Bayesian studies of NS crustal properties (Carreau 2019, Lim 2019, Dinh Thi 2021, Newton 2022) that employed the simpler Compressible Liquid Drop Model, by using an extended Thomas-Fermi method that fully incorporates the Skyrme functional's finite-size terms. The results enable more reliable predictions of crustal properties such as the moment of inertia, which is crucial for understanding pulsar glitches, and demonstrate that the spinodal instability criterion (used in Antic 2019) systematically underestimates the crust-core transition density compared to unified EoS treatments.
