# The Mystery of Bloom-Gilman Duality: A Light Front Holographic QCD Perspective

> Light front wave functions motivated by holographic constructions are used to study Bloom-Gilman duality of deep inelastic scattering. Separate expressions for structure functions in terms of quark and hadronic degrees of freedom are presented, with a goal of relating the two expressions. A two-parton model is defined and resonance transition form factors are computed using previously derived light front wave functions. A new form of global duality is derived from the valence quark-number sum rule. Using a complete set of hadronic states is necessary for this new global duality to be achieved. Previous original work does not provide such a set. This is remedied by amending the model to include a longitudinal confining potential, and the resulting complete set is sufficient to carry out the study of Bloom-Gilman duality. Expressions for transition form factors are obtained and all are shown to fall asymptotically as 1/Q2. The Feynman mechanism dominates the asymptotic behavior of the model. These transition form factors are used to assess the validity of the global and local duality sum rules, with the result that both neither are satisfied. Evaluations of the hadronic expression for q(x,Q2) provide more details about this lack. This result shows that the observed validity of both global and local forms of duality for deep inelastic scattering must be related to a feature of QCD that is deeper than completeness. Our simple present model suggests a prediction that Bloom-Gilman duality would not be observed if deep inelastic scattering experiments were to be made on the pion. The underlying origin of the duality phenomenon in deep inelastic scattering is deeply buried within the confinement aspects of QCD, and remains a mystery.

## Key claim

The authors show that in their two-parton light front holographic QCD model, both global and local forms of Bloom-Gilman duality are violated, suggesting that the observed validity of duality in real QCD must arise from a feature deeper than mere completeness of hadronic states. The model further predicts that Bloom-Gilman duality would not be observed if deep inelastic scattering experiments were performed on the pion.

## Method

The authors use light front wave functions derived from holographic QCD with a soft-wall confining potential to compute transition form factors for a two-parton (quark-antiquark) system representing a spinless hadron. They derive separate expressions for deep inelastic scattering structure functions using both quark degrees of freedom (via the operator product expansion) and hadronic degrees of freedom (via a sum over intermediate states with transition form factors). To achieve a complete set of hadronic states, they amend the model by adding a longitudinal confining potential, producing wave functions that include excitations in the longitudinal momentum fraction variable via Legendre polynomials. They then evaluate the transition form factors and test both global and local duality sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The transition form factors for all states fall asymptotically as \(1/Q^2\) at large momentum transfer.
- The sum over states \(S(Q) = \sum_X |F_X|^2\) equals 1 only at \(Q=0\); for large \(Q\), \(\lim_{Q\to\infty} S(Q) \approx \pi^{3/2}/(8Q)\), approaching zero rather than unity.
- The spectrum of the model with longitudinal confinement is given by \(M^2_{nL\mathcal{L}}(\lambda) = \kappa^2(4n + 2L + 2\lambda\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{L}+1) + 2)\), where \(\lambda\) is a dimensionless parameter.
- The ground state mass is \(M^2 = 2\kappa^2\) from the soft-wall model eigenvalues \(M^2_{nL} = \kappa^2(4n + 2L + 2)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Bloom-Gilman duality reviewed by Melnitchouk (2005), which include QCD in 1+1 dimensions (PhysRevD.14.3451), phenomenological approaches by Domokos (1971, 1972), harmonic oscillator models by Isgur (2001), Jeschonnek (2003), Close (2002), and others, and non-relativistic potential models. The authors note that prior approaches lacked both confinement and proper relativistic treatment, which light front holographic QCD (reviewed by Brodsky, 2014) provides. The paper enables future work on understanding the role of confinement in quark-hadron duality and suggests that experimental tests on pions could probe the fundamental origin of the phenomenon.
