# New approach to search for parity-even and parity-odd time-reversal violation beyond the Standard Model in a storage ring

> Time-reversal breaking and parity-conserving millistrong interactions, suggested in 1965, still remain a viable mechanism of CP-violation beyond the Standard Model. One of its possible manifestations is the T-odd asymmetry in the transmission of tensor-polarized deuterons through a vector-polarized hydrogen gas target. Upon the rotation of the deuteron polarization from the vertical direction into the ring plane, the T-odd asymmetries, odd against the reversal of the proton polarization in the target, will continuously oscillate with first or second harmonics of the spin precession frequency. The Fourier analysis of the oscillating T-odd asymmetries allows for an easy separation from background persistent in conventional experiments employing static vector and tensor polarizations.

# New approach to search for parity-even and parity-odd time-reversal violation beyond the Standard Model in a storage ring

## Key claim

The paper proposes that by rotating the vertical polarization of stored deuterons into the ring plane using an RF spin rotator, the T-odd asymmetries (both TVPC and TVPV) will continuously oscillate with harmonics of the spin precession frequency, allowing Fourier analysis to separate the signal from systematic backgrounds that plague conventional experiments with static polarizations. The authors claim this approach can simultaneously search for both parity-even and parity-odd time-reversal violation with about the same sensitivity, but note this depends on achieving the working point where the RF spin rotator is turned off at exactly the rotation angle epsilon n* = pi/2.

## Method

The authors derive the evolution of vector and tensor polarizations of deuterons in a storage ring under the action of an RF solenoid spin rotator, using Bogoliubov-Krylov-Mitropolsky averaging to obtain the stroboscopic spin evolution in factorized form. They then compute the time-dependent spin-dependent total cross section for double-polarized proton-deuteron scattering, identifying which terms oscillate with the first or second harmonic of the idle spin precession frequency and how they depend on the reversal of the proton target polarization. The method extends the technique previously developed by the JEDI collaboration for high-precision measurement of the spin precession frequency at the COSY ring.

## Key numbers

- The TVPC asymmetry oscillates with twice the idle precession frequency (2 theta_s n), providing a unique signature distinguishable from background
- The working point for the experiment is reached when epsilon n* = pi/2, at which point the RF spin rotator is turned off
- The best previous upper bound on TVPC asymmetry from transmission experiments is A_5 < 2.2 x 10^{-5}, achieved with 5.9 MeV polarized neutrons interacting with tensor-polarized 165Ho crystals (PhysRevC.55.2684)
- The dimensional estimate for the neutron electric dipole moment from MSCPV amounts to d_n ~ 10^{-24} e.cm, compared to the SM expectation of d_n ~ 10^{-32} e.cm and the experimental upper bound of d_n = (0.0 +/- 1.1_stat +/- 0.2_sys) x 10^{-26} e.cm (PhysRevLett.124.081803)
- The TVPC asymmetry formula contains the factor (3/4) x (sigma_TVPC/sigma_0) x T_yy(0) x P_y^p x sin^2(epsilon n*) x sin(2 theta_s n)
- The interaction energy considered is for deuterons of about 270 MeV incident on a proton target, close to the optimum energy for deuteron polarimetry on carbon

## Context

This work extends the 1965 proposal by Okun, Prentki and Veltman, and Lee and Wolfenstein of millistrong, parity-conserving but time-reversal violating interactions as a source of CP violation beyond the Standard Model. It builds on the JEDI collaboration's 2014 demonstration of using precessing in-plane deuteron polarization for high-precision spin precession frequency measurement (Bagdasarian 2014, Eversmann 2015), and addresses the systematic background problem identified in earlier proposals for TVPC searches in pd scattering (Valdau 2011, Lenisa 2019) where residual vector polarization in tensor-polarized cell targets creates hard-to-control false signals (Eversheim 2017, Temerbayev 2015). The approach enables simultaneous measurement of TVPC and TVPV asymmetries, and also provides new access to a family of single- and double-polarization observables including the previously unsearched parity-violating tensor asymmetry A_PV^T.
