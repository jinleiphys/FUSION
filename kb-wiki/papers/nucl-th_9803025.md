# The d'-dibaryon in a colored cluster model

> The mass and wave function of a six-quark system with quantum numbers J^P=0^-, T=0, called d', are calculated. We use a colored diquark-tetraquark cluster model for the six-quark wave function. A constituent quark model Hamiltonian with a two-body confinement potential, and residual one-gluon, one-pion, and one-sigma exchange interactions is used. The complications due to the quark exchange interactions between tetraquark and diquark clusters (Pauli principle) are taken into account within the framework of the Resonating Group Method. The calculated d' mass is some 350 MeV above the empirical value if the same two-body confinement strength as in the nucleon and Delta is used. This paper also examines the validity of the usual assumption of a universal two-quark confinement strength. We propose that the effective two-body confinement strength in an exotic six-quark system, such as the d', could be weaker than in a single baryon. The weaker confinement hypothesis leads to a d' mass of M=2092 MeV and a d' radius of r=1.53 fm.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the effective two-body confinement strength in an exotic six-quark system such as the d' could be weaker than in a single baryon, and that this weaker confinement hypothesis leads to a d' mass of 2092 MeV, whereas using the same two-body confinement strength as in the nucleon and Delta yields a mass some 350 MeV above the empirical value of 2065 MeV.

## Method

The authors used a colored diquark-tetraquark cluster model for the six-quark wave function with quantum numbers J^P=0^-, T=0, employing a constituent quark model Hamiltonian with two-body confinement potential and residual one-gluon, one-pion, and one-sigma exchange interactions. Quark exchange interactions between tetraquark and diquark clusters were treated within the Resonating Group Method (RGM) framework, with the relative wave function expanded in a finite sum of Gaussians using the Generator Coordinate Method.

## Key numbers

- The empirical d' resonance energy is M_d' = 2065 MeV with a free decay width of Gamma_piNN approximately 0.5 MeV
- With the weaker confinement hypothesis, the calculated d' mass is M_d' = 2092 MeV and radius r_d' = 1.53 fm
- Using the same two-body confinement strength as in the nucleon and Delta, the calculated d' mass is some 350 MeV above the empirical value
- With quadratic confinement and one-gluon exchange only (model I), the d' mass is 2610 MeV, approximately 550 MeV above the experimental mass
- With quadratic confinement including pion and sigma exchange (model II), the d' mass is 2440 MeV, about 380 MeV above the experimental value
- With color-screened error-function confinement (model VI), the d' mass is 2288 MeV, still some 220 MeV above the experimental value
- The constituent quark mass used is m_q = 313 MeV = m_N/3
- The harmonic oscillator parameter for the nucleon is b_3, determined by the stability condition on the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends earlier stringlike bag model calculations by Mulders et al. (1980) and Konen et al. (1987), which predicted M_d' approximately 2100 MeV but used rigid impenetrable clusters without full antisymmetrization. The present RGM calculation improves upon those by rigorously including quark exchange interactions via the Pauli principle, and also compares with the authors' previous single-bag shell model calculations (Glozman et al. 1994, Wagner et al. 1995, Ito et al. 1996). The results enable testing whether the d' is a stretched q^2-q^4 system as assumed in the stringlike bag model, and suggest that a universal two-body confinement strength may not be adequate for compact six-quark systems.
