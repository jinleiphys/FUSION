# A resonance interpretation for the nonmonotonic behavior of the phi-photoproduction cross section near threshold

> We study whether the nonmonotonic behavior found in the differential cross section of the phi-meson photoproduction near threshold can be described by a resonance. The resonant contribution is evaluated by using an effective Lagrangian approach. We find that, with the assumption of a J^P=3/2^- resonance with mass of 2.10 \pm 0.03 GeV and width of 0.465 \pm 0.141 GeV, LEPS data can indeed be well described. The ratio of the helicity amplitudes A_1/2/A_3/2 calculated from the resulting coupling constants differs in sign from that of the known D13(2080). We further find that the addition of this postulated resonance can substantially improve the agreement between the existing theoretical predictions and the recent omega photoproduction data if a large value of the OZI evading parameter x_OZI = 12 is assumed for the resonance.

## Key claim

The nonmonotonic behavior observed in the differential cross section of phi-meson photoproduction near threshold can be described by a postulated resonance with J^P = 3/2^-, but only under the assumption that this resonance has a mass of 2.10 ± 0.03 GeV and width of 0.465 ± 0.141 GeV, and the ratio of its helicity amplitudes A_1/2/A_3/2 differs in sign from the known D13(2080) resonance.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level to evaluate the resonant contribution to phi photoproduction on protons, adding a resonance to a baseline model consisting of t-channel Pomeron and (pi, eta) exchange. The coupling constants were determined by fitting to LEPS experimental data using the MINUIT minimization routine. The analysis considered resonances with spin 1/2 and 3/2, and the extracted properties were tested for stability against variations in Pomeron parameters.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the postulated J^P = 3/2^- resonance: 2.10 ± 0.03 GeV
- Width of the postulated J^P = 3/2^- resonance: 0.465 ± 0.141 GeV
- Ratio of helicity amplitudes A_1/2/A_3/2 = 1.16 for the postulated resonance, compared to -1.18 for the known D13(2080)
- OZI evading parameter x_OZI = 12 assumed for the resonance when applied to omega photoproduction
- Coupling constant product eg_gamma N N*^(1) g_phi N N*^(1) = -0.186 ± 0.079
- Coupling constant product eg_gamma N N*^(2) g_phi N N*^(1) = -0.212 ± 0.076

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi photoproduction that used t-channel exchange models (Titov et al., 1997-2007) which could not account for the nonmonotonic behavior observed by the LEPS collaboration (2005). A similar analysis was carried out in a coupled-channel model by Ozaki (2009), but that analysis was marred by a confusion in the phase of the Pomeron-exchange amplitude as noted by Hosaka (2010). The results enable future experimental measurements of polarization observables to help resolve the parity of the resonance, and the resonance can also improve agreement with omega photoproduction data when a large OZI evading parameter is assumed.
