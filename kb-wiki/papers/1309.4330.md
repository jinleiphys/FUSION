# Correlations in nuclear energy recurrence relations

> The excitation energies of states belonging to the ground state bands of heavy even-even nuclei are analysed using recurrence relations. Excellent agreement with experimental data at the 10 keV level is obtained by taking into account strong correlations which emerge in the analysis. This implies that the excitation energies can be written as a polynomial of maximum degree four in the angular momentum.

# Correlations in nuclear energy recurrence relations

## Key claim
The excitation energies of ground-state-band states in heavy even-even nuclei can be predicted from a recurrence relation that yields agreement with experiment at the ~10 keV level, but only when strong correlations between the deviations from a simpler linear relation are taken into account. The abstract's claim of "excellent agreement" depends on using five consecutive known band members to start the recurrence, and the relation is equivalent to assuming the excitation energy is a polynomial of at most degree four in angular momentum.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental excitation energies of ground-state bands in 87 even-even nuclei from Ba to Cm, using recurrence relations based on angular momenta J=0,2,4,... They defined deviations d^{J,K}_L from a simple linear relation and discovered a strong empirical correlation: d^{10,0}_2 = 2 × d^{10,0}_4. This correlation was used to derive a recurrence relation (Eq. 5) that predicts E(10) from the known energies of J=2,4,6,8 states, and a more general form (Eq. 6) applicable to higher spins. The relation was shown to follow from a Taylor series expansion of E(J) truncated at fourth order.

## Key numbers
- r.m.s. deviation ΔE = 10.4 keV between theoretical and experimental values of {E(10)-E(0)} when using Eq.(5), omitting the anomalous case of ¹³⁴Ce
- Least-squares fit to the correlation d^{10,0}_2 = 2 × d^{10,0}_4 gave gradient 2.007 ± 0.028 and intercept 0.00006 ± 0.00032 across all 87 nuclei
- Six nuclei showed statistically significant deviations from the linear correlation: ¹³⁰Ce, ¹³⁴Ce, ¹³⁴Nd, ¹³⁶Sm, ¹⁴⁰Gd, and ¹⁷²Os
- The recurrence relation (Eq. 6) has been observed to work for states up to J ~ 30 in some cases

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Buck, Merchant, Perez 2010) which found that a simpler recurrence relation (Eq. 1) was satisfied to within a few percent. The new result improves precision by an order of magnitude by incorporating the observed correlations. The approach is model-independent, contrasting with the Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello) and rotational-vibrational models (Bohr and Mottelson, Eichler) which typically struggle to achieve precision at the few-10-keV level. The derived quartic polynomial form encompasses both the perfect rotor (quadratic in J(J+1)) and the anharmonic vibrator model (Zamick and Casten). The method enables prediction of unobserved band members and can flag unusual spectra due to band crossing or backbending, as seen in ¹³⁴Ce.
