# Model approach to the sign problem on lattice QCD with theta vacuum

> We propose a practical way of circumventing the sign problem in lattice QCD simulations with a theta-vacuum term. This method is the reweighting method for the QCD Lagrangian after the chiral transformation. In the Lagrangian, the P-odd mass term as a cause of the sign problem is minimized. Additionally, we investigate theta-vacuum effects on the QCD phase diagram for the realistic 2+1 flavor system, using the three-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the entanglement PNJL model as an extension of the PNJL model. The theta-vacuum effects make the chiral transition sharper. We finally investigate theta dependence of the transition temperature and compare with the result of the pure gauge lattice simulation with imaginary theta parameter.

## Key claim

The authors propose a reweighting method for lattice QCD with a theta vacuum that works by performing an SU_A(3) ⊗ U_A(1) chiral transformation to move the theta dependence into the light-quark mass term, then neglecting the resulting P-odd mass term because its scale is much smaller than Λ_QCD. The method is justified only if the P-odd condensates are indeed negligible compared to the P-even condensates, which the authors test using effective models.

## Method

The authors perform an SU_A(3) ⊗ U_A(1) chiral transformation on the QCD Lagrangian to move the theta dependence from the topological term into the light-quark mass terms, producing a P-odd mass term proportional to iγ₅ sin(θ/2). They then propose a reweighting method where the reference theory omits this P-odd mass term, and the expectation value of an operator is computed as the ratio of fermion determinants with and without the P-odd term. To test the validity of this approximation, they use the three-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model at mean-field level, computing P-even and P-odd condensates at zero temperature and mapping the chiral transition phase diagram in the T-μ-θ space.

## Key numbers

- The upper limit of the theta angle from neutron dipole moment measurements is |θ| < 10⁻⁹ (zero temperature only).
- The EPNJL model parameters are fitted to (α₁, α₂) = (0.25, 0.1) to reproduce degenerate three-flavor lattice QCD results with imaginary chemical potential.
- The lattice result for the deconfinement transition temperature dependence on theta at μ=0 is T_c(θ)/T_c(0) = 1 - R_θ θ² + O(θ⁴) with R_θ = 0.0175(7), from pure Yang-Mills theory with imaginary theta parameter.
- The EPNJL model shows that the critical endpoint in the T-μ plane moves to smaller μ and higher T when θ is increased from 0 to π, and for the EPNJL model there is no critical endpoint at large θ.

## Context

This work extends the approach of performing chiral transformations on the QCD Lagrangian to handle the sign problem at finite theta, building on the earlier proposal by Sasaki et al. (2012). The authors compare their model results to pure gauge lattice simulations with imaginary theta parameter by Negro (2012) and find that the theta dependence of the transition temperature in their effective models is much smaller than the lattice result, indicating that dynamical quarks are crucial for theta vacuum effects. The EPNJL model, which reproduces lattice results for the Roberge-Weiss endpoint from de Forcrand and D'Elia (2010) better than the PNJL model, predicts that the chiral transition can become first-order even at zero chemical potential when theta is large, which could change scenarios of cosmological evolution such as inhomogeneous Big-Bang nucleosynthesis or baryogenesis.
