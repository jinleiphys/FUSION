# Study of Giant Pairing Vibrations with neutron-rich nuclei

> We investigate the possible signature of the presence of giant pairing states at excitation energy of about 10 MeV via two-particle transfer reactions induced by neutron-rich weakly-bound projectiles. Performing particle-particle RPA calculations on $^{208}$Pb and BCS+RPA calculations on $^{116}$Sn, we obtain the pairing strength distribution for two particles addition and removal modes. Estimates of two-particle transfer cross sections can be obtained in the framework of the 'macroscopic model'. The weak-binding nature of the projectile kinematically favours transitions to high-lying states. In the case of $(~^6He, \~^4He)$ reaction we predict a population of the Giant Pairing Vibration with cross sections of the order of a millibarn, dominating over the mismatched transition to the ground state.

# Study of Giant Pairing Vibrations with neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the Giant Pairing Vibration (GPV) at excitation energies around 10 MeV can be populated with cross sections of the order of a millibarn in the ($^6$He,$^4$He) reaction, dominating over the mismatched ground-state transition, but only under the condition that the projectile is neutron-rich and weakly bound, providing optimal Q-value matching.

## Method
The author performed particle-particle RPA calculations on the closed-shell nucleus $^{208}$Pb and BCS+RPA calculations on the superfluid nucleus $^{116}$Sn to obtain the pairing strength distribution for two-particle addition and removal modes. Two-neutron transfer form factors were constructed using the macroscopic model and used in DWBA calculations with the code Ptolemy to estimate cross sections for the ($^6$He,$^4$He) and ($^{14}$C,$^{12}$C) reactions.

## Key numbers
- For $^{208}$Pb, the GPV is calculated at 11.81 MeV, compared to the Broglia and Bes estimate of 11.47 MeV.
- For $^{116}$Sn, the GPV is calculated at 12.68 MeV, compared to the Broglia and Bes estimate of 14.76 MeV.
- For the ($^6$He,$^4$He) reaction on $^{208}$Pb, the cross section to the GPV is 3.1 mb, while the ground-state transition is 1.8 mb.
- For the ($^6$He,$^4$He) reaction on $^{116}$Sn, the cross section to the GPV is 2.4 mb, while the ground-state transition is 0.4 mb.
- For the ($^{14}$C,$^{12}$C) reaction on $^{208}$Pb, the cross section to the GPV is 0.04 mb, while the ground-state transition is 15.3 mb.
- For the ($^{14}$C,$^{12}$C) reaction on $^{116}$Sn, the cross section to the GPV is 0.14 mb, while the ground-state transition is 19.4 mb.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical predictions of Giant Pairing Resonances first discussed in the 1970s by Broglia and Bes and further developed in the 1980s by Herzog and collaborators, which have lacked conclusive experimental confirmation despite early efforts using (p,t) reactions by Crawley. The paper enables experimental planning at radioactive ion beam facilities by identifying $^{208}$Pb and $^{116}$Sn as promising targets and demonstrating that the ($^6$He,$^4$He) reaction provides optimal Q-value matching to populate the GPV, unlike conventional beams such as ($^{14}$C,$^{12}$C) which suffer from large energy mismatch.
