# Transport properties of the hot and dense sQGP

> The transport properties of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) are studied in a QCD medium at finite temperature and chemical potential. We calculate the shear viscosity $η(T,μ_q)$ and the electric conductivity $σ_e(T,μ_q)$ for a system of interacting massive and broad quasi-particles as described by the dynamical quasi-particle model "DQPM" at finite temperature $T$ and quark chemical potential $μ_q$ within the relaxation time approximation. Our results are in a good agreement with lattice QCD at finite temperature and show clearly the increase of the transport coefficients with increasing $T$ and $μ_q$. Our results provide the basic ingredients for the study of the hot and dense matter in the Beam Energy Scan (BES) at RHIC and CBM at FAIR.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity and electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma increase with both temperature and quark chemical potential, with the results showing good agreement with lattice QCD data at finite temperature, but only within the specific modeling assumptions of the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) and the relaxation time approximation.

## Method

The authors compute transport coefficients for a system of interacting massive and broad quasi-particles described by the DQPM at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ_q. Relaxation times are calculated using transition rates for elastic (qq, qq̄, qg, gg) and inelastic (gg ↔ qq̄) scattering processes, employing both on-shell (Dressed pQCD, DpQCD) and off-shell (Infrared Enhanced Hard Thermal Loop, IEHTL) approaches. The shear viscosity η and electric conductivity σ_e are then obtained within the relaxation time approximation using the computed relaxation times and parton densities.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s shows a minimum around T_c and then increases slowly for higher temperatures; η increases like T^3 for large temperature for all chemical potentials
- The electric conductivity over temperature σ_e/T increases slowly until it reaches at high temperatures the pQCD regime where σ_e/T ~ const
- The coupling constant α_s is much larger than one near T_c(μ_q), demonstrating non-perturbative effects at these temperatures
- The relaxation time τ decreases with temperature since quark and gluon densities are increasing functions of temperature; higher power coefficients in τ for T < 1.2 T_c in DpQCD/IEHTL approaches are traced to infrared enhancement of the effective coupling
- A finite μ_q increases smoothly τ_u especially close to T_c(μ_q)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) approach from Cassing (2009) to compute transport properties at finite chemical potential, going beyond previous calculations that were limited to zero baryon density. The results provide input for hydrodynamic calculations and transport simulations for the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and CBM at FAIR, and are consistent with a crossover transition at small μ_q in line with lattice calculations. The approach contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations by incorporating non-perturbative effects through the DQPM's temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent masses and widths.
