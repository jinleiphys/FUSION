# Impact of spin-orbit currents on the electroweak skin of neutron-rich nuclei

> Background: Measurements of neutron radii provide important constraints on the isovector sector of nuclear density functionals and offer vital guidance in areas as diverse as atomic parity violation, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron-star structure. Purpose: To assess the impact of spin-orbit currents on the electromagnetic- and weak-charge radii of a variety of nuclei. Special emphasis is placed on the experimentally accessible electroweak skin, defined as the difference between weak-charge and electromagnetic-charge radii. Methods: Two accurately calibrated relativistic mean field models are used to compute proton, neutron, charge, and weak-charge radii of a variety of nuclei. Results: We find that spin-orbit contributions to the electroweak skin of light neutron-rich nuclei, such as 22O and 48Ca, are significant and result in a substantial increase in the size of the electroweak skin relative to the neutron skin. Conclusions: Given that spin-orbit contributions to both the charge and weak-charge radii of nuclei are often as large as present or anticipated experimental error bars, future calculations must incorporate spin-orbit currents in the calculation of electroweak form factors.

## Key claim

Spin-orbit currents produce a significant increase in the electroweak skin (defined as the difference between weak-charge and electromagnetic-charge radii) of light neutron-rich nuclei such as 22O and 48Ca, relative to the neutron skin; this effect is often as large as present or anticipated experimental error bars and must be incorporated in future calculations of electroweak form factors.

## Method

Two accurately calibrated relativistic mean field models (FSU and NL3) were used to compute proton, neutron, charge, and weak-charge radii for a variety of nuclei. The charge and weak form factors were calculated from one-body currents that include single-nucleon electromagnetic and weak form factors, with special attention to the spin-orbit current contributions arising from the tensor form factor term in the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

* For 48Ca, the spin-orbit contribution to the weak skin is about 0.03 fm, within the projected accuracy of the proposed C-REX experiment.
* For 22O, the spin-orbit contribution to the weak skin amounts to about 0.04 fm (FSU model: weak skin 0.487 fm with spin-orbit versus 0.442 fm without).
* For 48Ca, the FSU model predicts a weak skin of 0.247 fm with spin-orbit included versus 0.214 fm without; the NL3 model gives 0.275 fm versus 0.243 fm.
* For 208Pb, the PREX collaboration measured a weak skin of 0.32 ± 0.18 (exp) ± 0.03 (mod) fm.
* The weak-charge radius of 48Ca predicted by FSU is 3.683 fm with spin-orbit versus 3.669 fm without.
* The spin-orbit contribution to the mean-square charge radius of 48Ca from the unpaired 1f7/2 neutron orbital is approximately -0.101 fm² in the free-space limit.
* The weak spin-orbit contribution to the mean-square radius of 48Ca is approximately +0.077 fm².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of spin-orbit currents on charge densities, previously studied for light nuclei by Ong (2010), to the weak-charge density for the first time. The results are directly relevant to the PREX measurement on 208Pb (Abrahamyan 2012, Horowitz 2012) and the proposed C-REX experiment on 48Ca (2012), which aim to measure weak-charge radii to accuracies of ±0.05 fm and ±0.03 fm respectively. The findings enable more precise extraction of neutron skin information from parity-violating electron scattering experiments, with implications for atomic parity violation, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron-star structure.
