# On the breaking and restoration of symmetries within the nuclear energy density functional formalism

> We review the notion of symmetry breaking and restoration within the frame of nuclear energy density functional methods. We focus on key differences between wave-function- and energy-functional-based methods. In particular, we point to difficulties encountered within the energy functional framework and discuss new potential constraints on the underlying energy density functional that could make the restoration of broken symmetries better formulated within such a formalism. We refer to Ref.~\cite{duguet10a} for details.

# On the breaking and restoration of symmetries within the nuclear energy density functional formalism

## Key claim
Symmetry restoration within the multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) framework faces fundamental difficulties because the energy kernel is not derived from a genuine scalar operator, unlike in wave-function-based methods; new constraints on the functional form of the EDF kernel, such as requiring it to depend strictly on integer powers of the density matrices, are necessary to make the restoration of broken symmetries physically sound, but for arbitrary symmetry groups the required constraints may not be as transparent as for the U(1) particle-number case.

## Method
The authors compare wave-function-based symmetry breaking and restoration (e.g., symmetry-unrestricted Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov followed by projection) with the single-reference (SR) and multi-reference (MR) energy density functional approaches. They analyze the mathematical structure of the energy kernel E[g',g] and its expansion over irreducible representations of the symmetry group, focusing on the U(1) group for particle-number restoration and the SO(3) group for angular-momentum restoration. For the SO(3) case, they derive an exact mathematical property of the potential energy density V^{LM}(R) from a rotationally-invariant Hamiltonian, showing that its expansion over spherical harmonics is limited to L' ≤ 2L, and argue that this property cannot be guaranteed a priori in the EDF method.

## Key numbers
- For the U(1) group (particle-number restoration), Fourier components E^N were demonstrated to be different from zero for negative particle numbers N ≤ 0, whereas in wave-function-based methods E^N is zero for N ≤ 0 (Bender 2009, Duguet 2009).
- For the SO(3) group (angular-momentum restoration), the potential energy density V^{LM}(R) derived from a wave-function-based method has an expansion over spherical harmonics Y^0_{L'}(R̂) limited to L' ≤ 2L, where L is the angular momentum quantum number of the underlying many-body state.
- The symmetry-restored potential energy V^L extracts the coefficient of the lowest harmonic: V^L = √(4π) ∫ dR υ^{[2]}_{L0}(R).

## Context
This review extends the analysis of symmetry breaking and restoration in nuclear EDF methods, building on prior work by Bender (2003) on EDF methods and by Ring (1980) on wave-function-based projection techniques. The paper specifically addresses difficulties identified in particle-number restoration calculations by Almehed (2001), Anguiano (2001), and Dobaczewski (2007), and discusses the regularization method proposed by Lacroix (2009) that restores the validity of particle-number restoration only when the EDF kernel depends strictly on integer powers of the density matrices. The work enables future development of constraints for angular-momentum restoration and other symmetry groups, with the goal of making MR-EDF calculations physically well-defined.
