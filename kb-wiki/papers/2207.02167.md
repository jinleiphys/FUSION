# Solving the matrix exponential function for the groups SU(3), SU(4) and Sp(2)

> The well known analytical formula for $SU(2)$ matrices $U = \exp(i \vec τ\!\cdot\! \vec φ\,) = \cos|\vec φ\,| + i\vec τ\!\cdot\! \hatφ\, \sin|\vec φ\,|$\\ is extended to the $SU(3)$ group with eight real parameters. The resulting analytical formula involves the sum over three real roots of a cubic equation, corresponding to the so-called irreducible case, where one has to employ the trisection of an angle. When going to the special unitary group $SU(4)$ with 15 real prameters, the analytical formula involves the sum over four real roots of a quartic equation. The associated cubic resolvent equation with three positive roots belongs again to the irreducible case. Furthermore, by imposing the pertinent condition on $SU(4)$ matrices one can also treat the symplectic group $Sp(2)$ with ten real parameters. Since there the roots occur as two pairs of opposite sign, this simplifies the analytical formula for $Sp(2)$ matrices considerably. An outlook to the situation with analytical formulas for $SU(5)$, $SU(6)$ and $Sp(3)$ is also given.

## Key claim

The paper extends the well-known analytical formula for the matrix exponential of SU(2) to SU(3), SU(4), and Sp(2) by using the Cayley-Hamilton theorem and solving the resulting polynomial equations, but the formulas for SU(3) and SU(4) require solving cubic or quartic equations in the "irreducible case" where trigonometric functions (trisection of an angle) are needed, making them quasi-analytical rather than purely algebraic.

## Method

The author starts with the exponential representation of a special unitary matrix U = exp(i λ·v) in terms of the group generators (Gell-Mann matrices for SU(3) and SU(4) or their 15-dimensional generalization) and a real parameter vector v. Using the Cayley-Hamilton relation, which expresses the highest power of the rescaled matrix Σ = λ·v̂ as a linear combination of lower powers, the exponential series is reduced to a finite sum over the eigenvalues z_j of Σ, which are the real roots of the characteristic polynomial P(z) = 0. For SU(3) the cubic equation z³ - z - η = 0 is solved via the substitution z = (2/√3) cos ψ with ψ = (1/3) arccos(3√3 η/2); for SU(4) the quartic equation z⁴ - z² - ζz + η = 0 is solved via a cubic resolvent equation that also belongs to the irreducible case; for Sp(2) the condition ζ = 0 reduces the quartic to a biquadratic equation with roots occurring as opposite-sign pairs.

## Key numbers

- For SU(3): the determinant η = det Σ is confined to the range -2√3/9 ≤ η ≤ 2√3/9 (approximately -0.3849 to 0.3849).
- For SU(4): the determinant η is confined to the range -1/12 ≤ η ≤ 1/4 (approximately -0.0833 to 0.25), and the extremal values of ζ are ζ± = ±2√6/9 (approximately ±0.5443).
- For Sp(2): the determinant η is confined to the range 0 ≤ η ≤ 1/4.
- For Sp(3): the minimal value of η is η_min = -1/27 (approximately -0.03704), and ξ is confined to -1/3 ≤ ξ ≤ 0.

## Context

This work extends the standard analytical formula for SU(2) matrices (which involves simple trigonometric functions of a single magnitude |φ|) to higher-rank compact Lie groups relevant to chiral effective field theories for low-energy quantum chromodynamics, where SU(3) matrices describe three-flavor pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, and the η(548)-meson). The results enable explicit exponential parametrizations for SU(3), SU(4), and Sp(2) that can be used in calculations of chiral perturbation theory, and the paper provides an outlook to the more complicated cases of SU(5), SU(6), and Sp(3) where higher-degree polynomial equations must be solved.
