# Neutron Star Structure with Hyperons and Quarks

> We discuss the high-density nuclear equation of state within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. Particular attention is paid to the effects of nucleonic three-body forces, the presence of hyperons, and the joining with an eventual quark matter phase. The resulting properties of neutron stars, in particular the mass-radius relation, are determined. It turns out that stars heavier than 1.3 solar masses contain necessarily quark matter.

# Neutron Star Structure with Hyperons and Quarks

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron stars heavier than 1.3 solar masses necessarily contain quark matter, but this conclusion depends critically on the inclusion of hyperons in the hadronic equation of state; without hyperons, purely nucleonic models with three-body forces yield maximum masses up to 2.3 solar masses, but once hyperons are introduced the maximum mass drops below 1.3 solar masses regardless of the nucleonic three-body force model used.

## Method

The authors employed the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne v18 two-body nucleon-nucleon potential, supplemented by either microscopic meson-exchange three-body forces or phenomenological Urbana IX three-body forces. They extended the BHF formalism to include hyperons (Lambda and Sigma-minus) using the Nijmegen soft-core nucleon-hyperon potential, and constructed the equation of state for charge-neutral beta-equilibrium matter. For quark matter they used an extended MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag constant, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter with microscopic three-body forces: 0.19 fm^-3, with binding energy -15 MeV and compression modulus 210 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass with only two-body forces: slightly above 1.6 solar masses, radius 9 km, central density 9 times saturation density
- Maximum neutron star mass with microscopic three-body forces (nucleons only): approximately 2.3 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass with phenomenological Urbana three-body forces (nucleons only): approximately 1.8 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons included: less than 1.3 solar masses for all nucleonic three-body force models
- Hyperon onset densities: approximately 2-3 times normal nuclear matter density for Sigma-minus and Lambda hyperons
- Symmetry energy at saturation: approximately 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter by Baldo and collaborators to include hyperonic degrees of freedom and a possible quark matter phase, building on earlier studies of hyperonic neutron stars (hypns, barc) and quark matter in stars (qm). The finding that hyperons reduce the maximum neutron star mass below 1.3 solar masses contradicts earlier purely nucleonic predictions and poses a challenge for explaining observed neutron star masses near 2 solar masses. The paper suggests that a transition to quark matter at high density is the only remaining mechanism to reach larger maximum masses, and provides a framework for future studies combining hadronic and quark matter equations of state with density-dependent bag parameters.
