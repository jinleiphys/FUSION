# Kaon-meson coupling from SU(3) flavour symmetry and application to antikaon condensed dense matter in neutron star

> Observations of massive pulsars suggest that the central density of neutron stars can exceed several times the nuclear saturation density, creating a favourable environment for the appearance of exotic states, such as strange and non-strange baryons, meson condensates, and deconfined quark matter. The antikaon condensate is the most studied and plausible candidate among meson condensates. However, little is known about the exact interaction mechanisms between antikaons and mediator mesons. In this work, we investigate these interactions by, for the first time, employing SU(3) flavor symmetry to study antikaon condensation in dense matter. We determine hadron couplings in the mesonic sector using SU(3) flavour symmetry. Among the three key parameters we calculate $θ_v$, the mixing angle between the octet meson $ω_8$ and the singlet meson $ϕ_1$; the ratio of the octet to singlet couplings $z$; and leave the weight factor that balances the symmetric and antisymmetric couplings $α_v$ as a free parameter to explore its impact on the system. Using this approach, we derive the couplings for antikaon interactions with both singlet and octet mesons in the nonet vector meson family and examine the corresponding implications for dense matter featuring antikaon condensation. Our findings reveal that the equation of state for dense matter becomes progressively stiffer with increasing values of $α_v$, which delays the onset of antikaon condensation and increases the maximum achievable mass of neutron stars.

## Key claim
For the first time, SU(3) flavour symmetry is used to determine antikaon couplings with mediator vector mesons in dense matter, with the symmetric–antisymmetric weight factor \(\alpha_v\) left as a free parameter; the equation of state becomes progressively stiffer with increasing \(\alpha_v\) or \(z\), delaying the onset of antikaon condensation and raising the maximum neutron-star mass, but the exact interaction mechanisms between antikaons and mediator mesons remain poorly constrained by direct experiment.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density-dependent nucleon–meson couplings (DDME2 parametrization) and derive antikaon–vector-meson couplings from SU(3) flavour symmetry using Clebsch–Gordan coefficients, with the mixing angle \(\theta_v\) fixed to the ideal-mixing value (\(\tan\theta_v = 1/\sqrt{2}\)) and the octet-to-singlet coupling ratio \(z_m\) either fixed to its SU(6) value (\(\sqrt{3}/2\sqrt{2}\)) or varied. They consider two scenarios: (1) varying \(\alpha_m\) with fixed \(z_m\) under three baryonic-sector assumptions (\(\alpha_b = \alpha_m\), \(2\alpha_m\), \(\alpha_m/2\)); (2) varying \(z_m\) with fixed \(\alpha_m\) under three baryonic-sector assumptions (\(z_b = z_m\), \(2z_m\), \(z_m/2\)). The scalar coupling \(g_{\sigma\bar{K}}\) is fixed by requiring an antikaon optical potential depth \(U_{\bar{K}} = -130\) MeV at nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.152\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (used for DDME2 parametrization).
- Ideal mixing angle: \(\theta_v \approx 35.3^\circ\) (from \(\tan\theta_v = 1/\sqrt{2}\)).
- SU(6) value of \(z_m\): \(z_m = \sqrt{3}/(2\sqrt{2})\).
- Antikaon optical potential depth: \(U_{\bar{K}} = -130\) MeV (chosen within the range \(-40\) to \(-200\) MeV from literature).
- For \(\alpha_b = \alpha_m\) with \(\alpha_m = 0.0\): \(g_{\omega\bar{K}} = 13.02\), \(g_{\rho\bar{K}} = -7.37\), \(g_{\phi\bar{K}} = -18.41\).
- For \(\alpha_b = \alpha_m\) with \(\alpha_m = 1.0\): \(g_{\omega\bar{K}} = 1.86\), \(g_{\rho\bar{K}} = 7.37\), \(g_{\phi\bar{K}} = -13.15\).
- For \(\alpha_b = 2\alpha_m\) with \(\alpha_m = 0.5\): \(g_{\omega\bar{K}} = 3.72\), \(g_{\rho\bar{K}} = 0.00\), \(g_{\phi\bar{K}} = -10.52\).
- For \(\alpha_b = \alpha_m/2\) with \(\alpha_m = 0.0\): \(g_{\omega\bar{K}} = 13.02\), \(g_{\rho\bar{K}} = -7.37\), \(g_{\phi\bar{K}} = -18.41\).
- For \(\alpha_b = \alpha_m/2\) with \(\alpha_m = 1.0\): \(g_{\omega\bar{K}} = 2.60\), \(g_{\rho\bar{K}} = 7.37\), \(g_{\phi\bar{K}} = -18.41\).
- QMIC rule values for comparison: \(g_{\omega K}/g_{\omega N} = 1/3\), \(g_{\rho K}/g_{\rho N} = 1\), \(g_{\phi K}\) from \(\rho \to 2\pi\) decay width: \(\sqrt{2}\, g_{\phi K} = g_{\rho\pi} = 6.04\).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous study (Athira et al., 2024) that used SU(6) spin-flavour symmetry for antikaon couplings, where condensation occurred only in neutron stars above \(1.8\,M_\odot\). It contrasts with the quark-model and isospin-counting (QMIC) rule used by earlier works (Glendenning, 1999; Banik, 2001; Miyatsu, 2021) and with the SU(6) approach that often leads to premature hyperon appearance and an overly soft equation of state. By employing SU(3) flavour symmetry, the framework allows controlled symmetry breaking and more flexible adjustment of couplings to match empirical hypernuclear potentials, as previously applied to hyperon couplings by Lopes (2014) and Weissenborn (2012). The results enable future studies to constrain antikaon–meson interactions using neutron-star mass and radius observations, and to compare with alternative frameworks such as chiral perturbation theory.
