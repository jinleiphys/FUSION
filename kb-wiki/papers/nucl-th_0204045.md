# Model for pairing phase transition in atomic nuclei

> A model is developed which allows the investigation and classification of the pairing phase transition in atomic nuclei. The regions of the parameter space are discussed for which a pairing phase transition can be observed. The model parameters include: number of particles, attenuation of pairing correlations with increasing seniority, single particle level spacing, and pairing gap parameter. It is argued that for nuclear models, the pairing phase transition has to be separated in temperature from the signal of the exhaustion of the finite model space.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the pairing phase transition in atomic nuclei must be separated in temperature from the signal of the exhaustion of the finite model space, and that the classification of the transition order depends critically on the number of nucleon pairs in the reservoir; for sufficiently large model spaces (seven or more pairs), the pairing phase transition is classified as second-order for even systems and higher-order for odd systems, while the transition to a quasi-classical phase remains first-order.

## Method

The authors develop a phenomenological canonical-ensemble model where nucleon pairs are broken from a reservoir and promoted into an infinite, equidistant, doubly degenerate single-particle level scheme, with the energy required to break each successive pair reduced by a factor r (set to 0.56). The canonical partition function is constructed by summing over all possible numbers of unpaired nucleons, including separate partition functions for protons and neutrons as well as rotational and vibrational contributions. The order of phase transitions is classified using the distribution of zeros (DOZ) of the canonical partition function in the complex temperature plane, extracting the parameters alpha and nu that determine the transition order.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle level spacing is given by epsilon = 3*pi^2/A MeV, with the level density parameter a = A/9 MeV^{-1}
- The pairing-gap parameter is Delta = 12/sqrt(A) MeV
- The quenching parameter r = 0.56 is adopted for all nuclei studied, based on theoretical studies suggesting a quenching of the pairing energy per broken pair in the mass 190 region
- For the pairing phase transition in even systems with seven or more pairs in the reservoir, alpha ~ 0.86, indicating a second-order phase transition
- The inverse critical temperature beta_1 for the pairing phase transition is independent of the size of the model space
- The inverse critical temperature for the transition from unpaired to quasi-classical phase obeys a power law a*N^b with b ~ -1

## Context

This work extends previous schematic models (Schiller et al. 2001a, 2001b) by introducing the quenching of pairing correlations (r < 1) and applying the classification scheme of Borrmann et al. (2000) and Mertens et al. (2001) based on distribution of zeros. The model reproduces the S-shaped heat capacity curves observed experimentally in dysprosium isotopes (Schiller et al. 2001) and compares with shell-model Monte Carlo calculations for iron nuclei (Langanke et al. 2001). The results address the ongoing debate about whether the quenching of pairing correlations represents a second-order phase transition (as suggested by analogy with superconductivity) or a series of first-order transitions (as suggested by convex intruders in the microcanonical entropy curve observed by Melby et al. (1999, 2001)).
