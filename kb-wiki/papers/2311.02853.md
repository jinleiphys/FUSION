# Constraints on the Neutron-Star Structure from the Clocked X-Ray Burster 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058

> Type-I X-ray bursts are rapid-brightening transient phenomena on the surfaces of accreting neutron stars (NSs). Some X-ray bursts, called {\it clocked bursters}, exhibit regular behavior with similar light curve profiles in their burst sequences. The periodic nature of clocked bursters has the advantage of constraining X-ray binary parameters and physics inside the NS. In the present study, we compute numerical models, based on different equations of state and NS masses, which are compared with the observation of a recently identified clocked burster, 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058. We find that the relation between accretion rate and recurrence time is highly sensitive to the NS mass and radius. We determine, in particular, that 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058 appears to possess a mass less than $1.7M_{\odot}$ and favors a stiffer nuclear equation of state (with an NS radius $\gtrsim12.7{\rm km}$). Consequently, the observations of this new clocked burster may provide additional constraints for probing the structure of NSs.

## Key claim
The authors find that the clocked X-ray burster 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058 likely has a neutron-star mass less than 1.7 solar masses and favors a stiffer nuclear equation of state with a neutron-star radius greater than or approximately equal to 12.7 km, based on the sensitivity of the accretion-rate--recurrence-time relation to neutron-star mass and radius.

## Method
The authors computed numerical models of Type-I X-ray bursts using different nuclear equations of state and neutron-star masses, then compared the predicted relation between accretion rate and recurrence time against observational data from the clocked burster 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058. The models were used to derive constraints on neutron-star mass and radius by matching the observed burst recurrence behavior.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-star mass less than 1.7 solar masses (M_sun) for 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058
- Neutron-star radius greater than or approximately equal to 12.7 km, favoring a stiffer nuclear equation of state

## Context
This work extends the methodology previously applied to the well-known clocked burster GS 1826$-$24 (e.g., Galloway et al. 2017, Heger et al. 2007, Meisel et al. 2018, Johnston et al. 2020) to a newly identified clocked burster, 1RXS J180408.9$-$342058. The results demonstrate that this source can provide additional independent constraints on neutron-star structure, complementing existing constraints from other clocked bursters and enabling tighter limits on the nuclear equation of state.
