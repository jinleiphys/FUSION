# Kerman-Klein-Donau-Frauendorf model for odd-odd nuclei: formal theory

> The Kerman-Klein-Donau-Frauendorf (KKDF) model is a linearized version of the Kerman-Klein (equations of motion) formulation of the nuclear many-body problem. In practice, it is a generalization of the standard core-particle coupling model that, like the latter, provides a description of the spectroscopy of odd nuclei in terms of the properties of neighboring even nuclei and of single-particle properties, that are the input parameters of the model. A divers sample of recent applications attest to the usefulness of the model. In this paper, we first present a concise general review of the fundamental equations and properties of the KKDF model. We then derive a corresponding formalism for odd-odd nuclei that relates their properties to those of four neighboring even nuclei, all of which enter if one is to include both multipole and pairing forces. We treat these equations in two ways. In the first we make essential use of the solutions of the neighboring odd nucleus problem, as obtained by the KKDF method. In the second, we relate the properties of the odd-odd nuclei directly to those of the even nuclei. For both choices, we derive equations of motion, normalization conditions, and an expression for transition amplitudes. We also solve the problem of choosing the subspace of physical solutions that arises in an equations of motion approach that includes pairing interactions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a formal extension of the Kerman-Klein-Dönau-Frauendorf (KKDF) model to odd-odd nuclei, relating their properties to those of four neighboring even nuclei, but the formalism is presented as a theoretical framework without numerical application; the authors note that when pairing interactions are included, the manifold of solutions is four times the size of the manifold of physical solutions, requiring a method to select the physical subspace.

## Method

The authors start from a shell-model Hamiltonian with multipole and pairing interactions and derive equations of motion for the single-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP) that relate an odd-odd nucleus to its four neighboring even nuclei. They present two approaches: a sequential method that uses the solutions for neighboring odd nuclei (obtained from the KKDF method for odd nuclei) to derive equations for the odd-odd nucleus, and a symmetrical method that relates the odd-odd nucleus directly to the even nuclei without intermediate odd-nucleus solutions. For both approaches, they derive eigenvalue equations, normalization conditions, and expressions for transition amplitudes, and they solve the problem of selecting the physical subspace of solutions by identifying the half of the solutions with the largest energies as physical.

## Key numbers

- The normalization condition for odd-nucleus CFP is: \(\sum_{aIn}[|v_{J\nu}(aIn)|^2 + |u_{J\nu}(aIn)|^2] = \Omega\), where \(\Omega = \sum_{j_a}(2j_a + 1)\).
- The odd-odd nucleus with proton-neutron number \((Z, N)\) is related to four neighboring even nuclei: \((Z+1,N+1)\), \((Z-1,N+1)\), \((Z+1,N-1)\), and \((Z-1,N-1)\).
- When pairing interactions are included, the manifold of solutions is four times the size of the manifold of physical solutions.

## Context

This work extends the KKDF model, which was previously developed for odd nuclei by Klein, Dönau, Frauendorf and others (Dönau and Frauendorf, 1980s; Protopapas et al., 1990s), to the case of odd-odd nuclei. The formalism generalizes the standard core-particle coupling model and builds upon a restricted version applied by Starosta et al. (2001) to chirality in odd-odd triaxial nuclei, which omitted pairing interactions. The paper enables future calculations of odd-odd nuclear spectroscopy using properties of neighboring even nuclei as input, and provides the formal foundation for subsequent applications such as those by Koike et al. (2002).
