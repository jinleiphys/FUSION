# Hybrid potential/R-matrix models for the 12C + alpha system

> I present ongoing developments on hybrid models which combine the potential model, for the description of the background, with the R-matrix model, for the description of discrete states. Physical parameters of discrete states are converted into formal R-matrix parameters, taking into account the potential background. The nuclear part of this potential is constructed by a new variant of an inversion method based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics. The method is illustrated on 12C + alpha S-wave elastic phase shifts.

# Hybrid potential/R-matrix models for the 12C + alpha system

## Key claim

The hybrid model combining a potential-model background with R-matrix discrete states allows physical resonance parameters (energy and width) to be used as fitting parameters that are practically independent of the R-matrix radius, provided the R-matrix radius is taken above 12 fm; this independence is demonstrated for the 12C + alpha S-wave one-pole case, but the soft-sphere potential used for the background may lack strong physical meaning and appears to be partial-wave dependent.

## Method

The total R-matrix is constructed as the sum of a potential-model R-matrix (describing the non-resonant background) and a sum of R-matrix poles representing discrete resonant states. Physical resonance parameters (energy and reduced width) are converted into formal R-matrix parameters using a one-pole approximation formula that accounts for the potential background, replacing the usual hard-sphere reference with the potential S-matrix. The nuclear part of the background potential is built by a supersymmetric inversion method starting from V0=0, using only two imaginary-axis poles at wave number 0.4463 i fm^-1 to fit the S-wave phase shifts, then adding Coulomb and centrifugal terms.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit physical resonance parameters for the 12C + alpha S-wave: energy = 4.89271 MeV, width = 1.32 keV.
- The soft-sphere background potential becomes negligible above 12 fm, which is larger than the usual R-matrix range estimate of 5.5 to 6.5 fm.
- The nuclear background potential is constructed with only two poles at wave number kappa1 = kappa2 = 0.4463 i fm^-1.
- For a pure Coulomb reference potential V0, 10 poles are needed to reproduce the same S-wave phase shifts.

## Context

This work extends earlier hybrid potential/R-matrix models by Westin (1971) and Langanke (1985) by introducing physical resonance parameters as advocated by Angulo (2000) and Brune (2002), and by constructing the background potential via inversion rather than using phenomenological Woods-Saxon forms. The method is preliminary to analyzing P and D waves for the 12C + alpha system, which dominate the capture reaction at astrophysical energies; the 2+ subthreshold state was previously studied in the inversion potential model by Sparenberg (2004a), but a hybrid model is needed to study the 1- subthreshold state and achieve precise fits of all available data.
