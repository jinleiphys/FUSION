# Cheshire Cat Scenario in a 3+1 dimensional Hybrid Chiral Bag

> The total energy in the two-phase chiral bag model is studied, including the contribution due to the bag (Casimir energy plus energy of the valence quarks), as well as the one coming from the Skyrmion in the external sector. A consistent determination of the parameters of the model and the renormalization constants in the energy is performed. The total energy shows an approximate independence with the bag radius (separation limit between the phases), in agreement with the Cheshire Cat Principle.

# Cheshire Cat Scenario in a 3+1 dimensional Hybrid Chiral Bag

## Key claim
The total energy of the two-phase chiral bag model shows an approximate independence of the bag radius (the separation limit between the confined quark phase and the external Skyrme phase) for radii up to 1 fm, supporting the Cheshire Cat Principle in 3+1 dimensions, but only as an approximate, not exact, result, unlike the exact bosonization-driven Cheshire Cat behavior in 1+1 dimensions.

## Method
The authors compute the total energy of a hybrid chiral bag model consisting of quarks and gluons confined to a static spherical bag of radius R, coupled through chiral boundary conditions to a truncated external Skyrme field in a hedgehog configuration. The bag energy includes the Casimir energy (difference between chiral and MIT bag boundary conditions, plus the MIT bag reference Casimir energy for fermions and gluons) and the valence quark contribution when the fundamental eigenvalue becomes positive for chiral angles θ < π/2. The external Skyrme sector energy is evaluated using the Atiyah-Manton instanton-derived profile for the chiral angle, with the Skyrme parameters Fπ and e(R) determined by imposing continuity of the axial flux at the bag boundary and fitting to the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- The stabilization strength for the pure Skyrmion (R → 0 limit) is e(R=0) = 4.216, compared to the value e = 5.45 obtained by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten from fitting nucleon and Δ masses.
- The pion decay constant is fixed to Fπ = 99.59 MeV, near the value Fπ = 129 MeV obtained by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (experimental value Fπ_exp = 186 MeV).
- The Δ particle mass calculated with these parameters is MΔ = 1206 MeV (experimental value MΔ_exp = 1230 MeV).
- The total energy shows approximate independence with bag radius for the range 0 ≤ R ≤ 1 fm.
- The Atiyah-Manton profile parameter is fixed to λ̂ = 1.452.
- The fitted coefficients for the Casimir energy parametrization are: C2 = -0.13381, C4 = 0.05085, C6 = -0.01257, C8 = 0.01241.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of Casimir energies in chiral bag models by Bolsa-Quiral and MIT bag Casimir energies by Mitdef, using analytic regularization techniques. It builds on the Cheshire Cat Principle proposed by Rho, Nadkarni, and others, which in 1+1 dimensions follows exactly from bosonization of fermionic fields (Rho, 1980s), while in 3+1 dimensions topological quantities like baryon number show similar behavior (Goldstone, 1980s) but non-topological quantities are expected to be only approximately independent of bag radius. The results provide a foundation for studying deconfinement transitions at finite temperature in chiral bag models, as pursued by Deconf and Loewe, by justifying the Cheshire Cat hypothesis at zero temperature so that only temperature-dependent contributions to the free energy need be examined for transition signals.
