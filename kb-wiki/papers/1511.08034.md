# Phonon-particle coupling effects in odd-even double mass differences of magic nuclei

> A method is developed to consider the particle-phonon coupling (PC) effects in the problem of finding odd-even double mass differences (DMD) of magic nuclei within the approach starting from the free $NN$-potential. Three PC effects are taken into account, the phonon induced interaction, the renormalization of the "ends" due to the $Z$-factors and the change of the single-particle energies. We use the perturbation theory in $g^2_L$, where $g_L$ is the vertex of the $L$-phonon creation. PC corrections to single-particle energies are found self-consistently with an approximate account for the so-called tadpole diagram. Results for double-magic $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb nuclei show that the PC corrections make agreement with the experimental data better.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to include particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections in the calculation of odd-even double mass differences (DMD) of magic nuclei, showing that these corrections improve agreement with experimental data. The method is applied within a semi-microscopic model that starts from the free NN-potential, and the authors find that after including PC effects explicitly, the phenomenological parameter gamma of the effective pairing interaction should be reduced from its previous value of 0.06.

## Method

The authors solve a Schrodinger-like equation for two-particle states in magic nuclei using an effective pairing interaction (EPI) derived from the Brueckner theory with the Argonne v18 potential, supplemented by a phenomenological delta-function term with parameter gamma. Three PC effects are included via perturbation theory in g^2_L (where g_L is the L-phonon creation vertex): the phonon-induced interaction, renormalization of the wavefunction ends via Z-factors, and corrections to single-particle energies found self-consistently with an approximate treatment of the tadpole diagram. The calculations use the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a and are performed for the double-magic nuclei 132Sn and 208Pb, limiting the model space to two shells near the Fermi level.

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic model previously developed for the pairing problem (Pankratov et al., Saperstein et al.) and applied to odd-even DMD of magic nuclei (Gnezdilov et al., 2014-2015). It addresses uncertainties identified in comparisons between different BCS calculations for 120Sn, where Duguet et al. (using the low-k force) obtained Delta_BCS ~ 1.6 MeV while other calculations gave different results, with the discrepancies traced to the treatment of effective mass. The method enables systematic inclusion of PC corrections in DMD calculations for magic nuclei, and the authors suggest it should be extended to a wider set of nuclei including other magic nuclei and non-superfluid subsystems of semi-magic nuclei to determine the optimal value of the phenomenological parameter gamma.
