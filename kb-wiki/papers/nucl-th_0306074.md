# Quasifree eta photoproduction from nuclei and medium modifications of resonances

> We investigate the sensitivity of the differential cross section, recoil nucleon polarization and the photon asymmetry to changes in the elementary amplitude, medium modifications of the resonance $(S_{11},D_{13})$ masses, as well as nuclear target effects. All calculations are performed within a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation formalism resulting in analytical expressions for all observables. The spin observables are shown to be unique tools to study subtle effects that are not accessible by only looking at the unpolarized differential cross section.

## Key claim
The paper argues that quasifree eta photoproduction from nuclei is a valuable tool for studying medium modifications of the $S_{11}$(1535) and $D_{13}$(1520) resonances, but the abstract's claim of sensitivity is hedged: the polarization observables are largely insensitive to nuclear target effects, and the sensitivity to medium modifications depends on the specific resonance and observable.

## Method
The authors employ a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) formalism to calculate the differential cross section, recoil nucleon polarization, and photon asymmetry for the $A(\gamma,\eta N)B$ reaction. The elementary amplitude uses the effective Lagrangian model of Benmerrouche, Zhang, and Mukhopadhyay (BZM), which includes nucleon Born terms, vector meson exchanges ($\omega$ and $\rho$), and the $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ resonances. Nuclear structure is described using a relativistic mean-field approximation to the Walecka model, with bound-nucleon spinors obtained from Fourier transforms of Dirac radial wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Incident photon laboratory kinetic energy: 750 MeV, used for all calculations.
- Missing momentum fixed at $|\vec{p}_m| = 100$ MeV, near the peak of the bound nucleon momentum distribution.
- The $\eta NN$ coupling constant $g_{\eta}$ is varied in the range $0.2 \le g_{\eta} \le 6.2$, as specified in the BZM model.
- The $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ resonance masses are varied by $\pm 3\%$ to simulate medium modifications.
- Branching ratios: $S_{11}$ resonance has 50% branching ratio to $\eta N$, while $D_{13}$ has 0.1% branching ratio to $\eta N$.

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) study of quasifree eta photoproduction by Lee, Wright, Bennhold, and Tiator (1996) by providing a fully relativistic treatment of both the reaction dynamics and nuclear structure. It is the third application of the authors' established quasifree formalism, following studies of kaon (Abu-Raddad and Piekarewicz, 2000) and electron (Abu-Raddad and Piekarewicz, 2001) quasifree reactions. The paper enables future experimental measurements of polarization observables at facilities like Jefferson Lab and Mainz to discriminate between different models of chiral symmetry assignment for the $S_{11}$ resonance, such as the "mirror assignment" of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) versus the "naive assignment" of Jido, Nemoto, Oka, and Hosaka (2000).
