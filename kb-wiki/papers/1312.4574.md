# Local Efficiency Corrections to Higher Order Cumulants

> In this brief note we derive and present the formulas necessary to correct measurements of cumulants for detection efficiency. In particular we consider the case where the efficiency may depend on the phase-space, such as transverse momentum, rapidity etc.

## Key claim

The paper derives formulas to correct measured cumulants of particle number distributions for detection efficiency that varies across phase-space (e.g., transverse momentum, rapidity), extending previous constant-efficiency treatments; the correction is significant but unlikely to change results by factors of two or more for realistic detector configurations.

## Method

The authors model detection as a binomial process in each phase-space bin, with efficiency \(\epsilon(x)\) that can depend on the bin location \(x\). They relate factorial moments of the observed particle distribution to those of the produced distribution via local factorial moments summed over bins, then show how to extract cumulants from the corrected factorial moments. The formalism is applied to estimate the difference between correcting with a global average efficiency versus the full local efficiency, using Gaussian models for the particle distribution, efficiency profile, and correlation function.

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian model with correlation range \(r_c = \sigma_c/\sigma_N\) and efficiency range \(r_\epsilon = \sigma_\epsilon/\sigma_N\), the ratio \(R = \overline{\delta}/\delta\) (global/local correction) is \(R = \sqrt{(2+r_c^2)/(r_c^2 + 2r_\epsilon^2/(1+r_\epsilon^2))}\) when integrating over full phase-space; in the limit \(r_\epsilon \to \infty\) (constant efficiency), \(R = 1\).
- For a finite acceptance where efficiency is 20% at the boundary, \(\epsilon(\Delta) = 0.2\), numerical studies give \(R \approx 1.25\), meaning global efficiency correction overestimates the deviation from Poisson by 25%.
- The number of terms in the correction formula scales as \(m^{i+k}\) where \(m\) is the number of phase-space bins and \(i+k\) is the order of the factorial moment.

## Context

This work extends the constant-efficiency correction formulas of Bzdak (2012) and Garg (2012) to the realistic case where detection efficiency depends on particle kinematics. It provides the necessary formalism for experiments such as the RHIC beam energy scan, where cumulants of proton number distributions are used to search for the QCD critical point (Stephanov, 2011; Skokov, 2010). The results enable experimental collaborations (e.g., STAR) to properly correct higher-order cumulants for detector effects that vary with transverse momentum, rapidity, or azimuthal angle.
