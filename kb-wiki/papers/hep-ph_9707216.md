# Surface Induced Phase Transition in Quark-Gluon Plasma Produced in Laboratory

> We discuss an outside-inside scenario for a first order quark-hadron transition in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) expected to be produced in heavy ion collisions, wherein the entire QGP region itself becomes like a subcritical bubble, and starts shrinking. We argue that this shrinking QGP bubble will lead to concentration of baryon number in a narrow beam like region in center, which can be detected by HBT analysis, or as a raised plateau in the rapidity plot of baryon number.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in heavy ion collisions, the entire quark-gluon plasma (QGP) region can become a subcritical bubble and shrink from its boundary wall, rather than undergoing phase transition via homogeneous nucleation of hadronic bubbles in the interior. This scenario requires that the QGP region becomes subcritical at a temperature slightly above the critical temperature \(T_c\), with the exact supercooling depending on the system size and geometry.

## Method

The authors solve the Bjorken longitudinal expansion model for the energy density evolution, coupled with a model for the velocity of the collapsing boundary wall. They treat the QGP region as a cylindrical volume with a domain wall at its boundary, and determine the critical temperature at which the region becomes subcritical by comparing the free energy of the QGP bubble to the critical radius condition. The wall velocity is parameterized as \(v(T) = a[1 - T/(T_c + \Delta T)]^b\) with two sets of parameters, and the temperature evolution is computed for both the central region (near \(z=0\)) and the end regions of the cylinder.

## Key numbers

- For a spherical QGP region with radius \(R = 5\) fm, surface tension \(\sigma = 50\) MeV/fm\(^2\), and \(T_c = 170\) MeV, the supercooling required for the region to become subcritical is \(\Delta T \simeq 2\) MeV; for \(T_c = 120\) MeV, \(\Delta T \simeq 5\) MeV.
- For a cylindrical geometry, the supercooling for transverse collapse is \(\Delta T_{tr} = \Delta T_{sph}/2\), and for longitudinal collapse \(\Delta T_{long} = \Delta T_{sph}\).
- For the parameter set \(a = 3, b = 3/2\) with \(R_T = 7\) fm, the temperature drops to about \(0.9 T_c\) then reheats to about \(0.98 T_c\) by \(\tau \simeq 16\) fm and \(0.99 T_c\) by \(\tau \simeq 21\) fm; critical bubble sizes at these temperatures are \(R_c \simeq 3.4\) fm and \(6.7\) fm respectively.
- The collapsing boundary wall concentrates baryon number into a beam-like region of thickness 1-2 fm and length of few tens of fm.
- The longitudinal collapse can reduce the frame rapidity at the wall end by up to one unit by the time transverse collapse is completed.

## Context

This work extends the study of first-order quark-hadron phase transitions in heavy ion collisions beyond the standard homogeneous nucleation scenario of nucleating hadronic bubbles in a uniform QGP background, as discussed in earlier work by Kurki-Suonio, et al. (1990). The authors apply ideas from baryon number inhomogeneity generation at the end of the quark-hadron transition in the early Universe, where collapsing QGP droplets concentrate baryon number. The proposed observable signatures (HBT analysis of baryons and a raised plateau in the rapidity plot of baryon number) could provide experimental evidence for a first-order transition and for baryon concentration by shrinking QGP bubbles, analogous to early Universe scenarios.
