# Monte Carlo Calculation for the neutron-rich Ca isotopes

> We computed ground-state energies of calcium isotopes from 42Ca to 48Ca by means of the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method. Calculations were performed by replacing the 40Ca core with a mean-field self consistent potential computed using Skyrme interaction. The energy of the external neutrons is calculated by projecting the ground-state from a wave function built with the single particle orbitals computed in the self consistent external potential. The shells considered were the 1F_7/2 and the 1F_5/2. The Hamiltonian employed is semi-realistic and includes tensor, spin--orbit and three--body forces. While absolute binding energies are too deep if compared with experimental data, the differences between the energies for nearly all isotopes are in very good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The AFDMC method, using a semi-realistic Hamiltonian (AV8' plus Urbana IX) and replacing the ⁴⁰Ca core with a Skyrme mean-field potential, reproduces the experimental energy differences between neutron-rich calcium isotopes very well, even though the absolute binding energies are systematically too deep by about 6–7 MeV.

## Method

The authors replaced the ⁴⁰Ca core with a self-consistent mean-field potential obtained from Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme I interaction. They then treated only the external neutrons explicitly, using the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method with a trial wave function built from single-particle orbitals in the 1F₇/₂ and 1F₅/₂ shells, including a Jastrow correlation factor. The Hamiltonian included the Argonne AV8' two-body potential and the Urbana IX three-body force, and the ground-state energies were projected by imaginary-time propagation with importance sampling and a constrained-path approximation to control the fermion sign problem.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ⁴²Ca: –25.25(7) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –19.85 MeV (experimental)
- Ground-state energy of ⁴⁴Ca: –46.3(1) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –38.91 MeV (experimental)
- Ground-state energy of ⁴⁶Ca: –62.9(3) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –56.72 MeV (experimental)
- Ground-state energy of ⁴⁸Ca: –80.3(8) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –73.09 MeV (experimental)
- Energy difference E(⁴²Ca) – E(⁴⁸Ca): –55.1(8) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –54.09 MeV (experimental)
- Energy difference E(⁴⁴Ca) – E(⁴⁸Ca): –34.0(8) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –35.03 MeV (experimental)
- Energy difference E(⁴⁶Ca) – E(⁴⁸Ca): –17.4(8) MeV (AFDMC) vs. –17.22 MeV (experimental)

## Context

This work extends the core-plus-external-neutron approach previously applied to oxygen isotopes by Gandolfi (2006) to the calcium isotopic chain, where the higher neutron density and larger angular momentum (l=3) make core polarization effects potentially more important. The results demonstrate that the AFDMC method, which had already given results comparable to Green's Function Monte Carlo for pure neutron systems (Pederiva 2004), can successfully treat the many-body correlations among external neutrons in medium-mass nuclei. This enables future studies of heavier neutron-rich nuclei and excited states, where the single-particle picture might otherwise break down.
