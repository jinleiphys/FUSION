# Asymmetric Neutrino Emission from Magnetized Proto-Neutron Star Matter including Hyperons in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> We calculate asymmetric neutrino absorption and scattering cross sections on hot and dense magnetized neutron-star matter including hyperons in fully relativistic mean field theory. The absorption/scattering cross sections are suppressed/enhanced incoherently in the direction of the magnetic fielld, B. The asymmetry is 2-4% at the matter density ρ_0 < ρ_B < 3ρ_0 and temperature T < 40 MeV for B=2 X 10^{17} G. This asymmetry is comparable to the effects owing to parity violation or asymmetric magnetic field topology proposed for the origin of pulsar kicks.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that asymmetric neutrino absorption in magnetized proto-neutron star matter, including hyperons, produces a 2-4% asymmetry in the neutrino momentum flux at densities between ρ₀ and 3ρ₀ and temperatures below 40 MeV for a magnetic field of 2 × 10¹⁷ G, which is comparable to the effects from parity violation or asymmetric magnetic field topology previously proposed as origins of pulsar kicks.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative calculations of neutrino scattering and absorption cross-sections in hot, dense neutron-star matter containing nucleons, Lambda hyperons, electrons, and electron-neutrinos within a fully relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework using the PM1-L1 parameter set. They included the effects of a strong uniform magnetic field along the z-direction by treating the baryon spin interaction perturbatively and solving the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport in a 1D model, accounting for Fermi motion of baryons and electrons, recoil effects, distortion of Fermi spheres by the magnetic field, and energy differences of the mean field between initial and final baryons.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry in neutrino absorption cross-section is 2-4% at matter densities ρ₀ < ρ_B < 3ρ₀ and temperatures T < 40 MeV for magnetic field B = 2 × 10¹⁷ G
- The asymmetry in neutrino absorption is estimated to be as much as 2.2% of the entire neutrino momentum for an interior magnetic field of ~2 × 10¹⁷ G
- The parameter set PM1-L1 gives binding energy per baryon BE = 16 MeV, nucleon effective mass M_N*/M_N = 0.7, and incompressibility K = 200 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Lambda particles appear when ρ_B ≳ 2ρ₀ and significantly affect the equation of state for ρ_B ≳ 3ρ₀
- Anti-neutrino density is less than 0.5% of neutrino density at ρ_B = ρ₀ and T = 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic calculations of neutrino-nucleon scattering by Lai et al. (1998, 1999) to a fully relativistic framework including hyperons, and addresses the criticism by Kusenko, Segre and Vilenkin (1998) that asymmetry in scattering cross-sections does not lead to asymmetric neutrino emission in complete thermodynamic equilibrium. The results enable more realistic predictions for pulsar kick velocities from asymmetric neutrino emission in magnetized proto-neutron stars, with the calculated asymmetries being comparable to observed pulsar velocities.
