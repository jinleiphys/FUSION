# Lorentz covariance of three-dimensional equations

> We show how the invariance under the charge conjugation and CPT symmetry, present in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, is lost in the reduction to certain relativistic three-dimensional equations. This in particular leads to the breakdown of the standard Lorentz structure and renormalization procedures for the resulting single-particle propagators. We formulate the equal-time approximation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the form which manifestly satisfies the above symmetries, and apply it to the description of the pion-nucleon interaction in a dynamical model based on hadron exchanges. We also consider the one-body limit of various three-dimensional equations for the case of $t$- and $u$-channel one-particle-exchange potential.

## Key claim

The charge conjugation and CPT symmetries present in the Bethe-Salpeter equation are lost in the reduction to certain relativistic three-dimensional equations, leading to the breakdown of the standard Lorentz structure and renormalization procedures for single-particle propagators; the authors formulate an equal-time approximation that manifestly satisfies these symmetries.

## Method

The authors analyze the Lorentz covariance properties of various three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, focusing on the role of charge conjugation symmetry. They formulate a manifestly covariant three-dimensional equation by imposing that the interaction is insensitive to off-shellness along a direction defined by a unit four-vector, which in the center-of-mass frame becomes equivalent to the equal-time approximation. They then apply this equal-time approximation to a dynamical model of pion-nucleon scattering based on hadron exchanges, including N(938), N*(1450), Delta(1232), D13(1525), S11(1555), rho(770) and sigma(550) exchanges.

## Key numbers

* The one-body limit study uses nucleon mass m_N = 1, pion mass m_pi = 0.01, and center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 1.1 m_N, with t = 0, as a function of the exchange particle mass mu.
* The pion-nucleon phase-shift description is presented up to 600 MeV pion kinetic laboratory energy.
* For the u-exchange potential, the nucleon spectator calculation disagrees with the quantum field theory result by an order of magnitude (beyond the scale of the figure).

## Context

This work extends the quasipotential approach to relativistic two-body equations, building on the instantaneous approximation of Salpeter (1952) and the spectator equation of Gross (1969). The authors examine the one-body limit constraint previously studied by Gross and Surya (1993) for the pion-nucleon system, finding that the difference between nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon situations arises from the type of potential (t-channel versus u-channel exchange) rather than the mass of the exchanged particle. The equal-time formulation enables consistent treatment of charge conjugation symmetry in dynamical models of meson-nucleon scattering.
