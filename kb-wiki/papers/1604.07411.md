# Protophobic Fifth Force Interpretation of the Observed Anomaly in $^8$Be Nuclear Transitions

> Recently a 6.8$σ$ anomaly has been reported in the opening angle and invariant mass distributions of $e^+ e^-$ pairs produced in $^8\text{Be}$ nuclear transitions. The data are explained by a 17 MeV vector gauge boson $X$ that is produced in the decay of an excited state to the ground state, $^8\text{Be}^* \to {}^8\text{Be} \, X$, and then decays through $X \to e^+ e^-$. The $X$ boson mediates a fifth force with a characteristic range of 12 fm and has milli-charged couplings to up and down quarks and electrons, and a proton coupling that is suppressed relative to neutrons. The protophobic $X$ boson may also alleviate the current 3.6$σ$ discrepancy between the predicted and measured values of the muon's anomalous magnetic moment.

## Key claim
The 6.8σ anomaly in e+e- pairs from 8Be nuclear transitions can be explained by a 17 MeV vector gauge boson X that mediates a fifth force, but only if the boson has "protophobic" couplings where the proton coupling is suppressed relative to the neutron coupling, satisfying |εp/εn| < 0.078, which distinguishes it from a simple dark photon interpretation.

## Method
The authors analyzed the 8Be decay anomaly reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2015), where an excited 8Be* state (18.15 MeV, J^P = 1^+) decays to the ground state (0^+) with production of e+e- pairs. They considered a massive vector gauge boson X coupling to standard model fermions with charges εf in units of e, and determined the required quark and lepton couplings by fitting the observed branching ratio B(8Be* → 8Be X)/B(8Be* → 8Be γ) ≈ 5.8 × 10-6. They then tested these couplings against constraints from pion decays (NA48/2), electron beam dump experiments (E141), neutron-nucleus scattering, neutrino-electron scattering (TEXONO), and anomalous magnetic moment measurements.

## Key numbers
- Boson mass: mX = 16.7 ± 0.35 (stat) ± 0.5 (sys) MeV from the fit to the e+e- invariant mass distribution
- Required quark coupling sum: |εu + εd| ≈ 3.7 × 10-3 from the branching ratio fit
- Fifth force range: 12 fm, corresponding to the Compton wavelength of a 17 MeV boson
- Electron coupling lower bound: |εe| > 1.3 × 10-5 from requiring X decay length less than 1 cm
- Electron coupling upper bound: |εe| < 1.4 × 10-3 (3σ) from (g-2)e
- Neutron coupling upper bound: |εn| < 2.5 × 10-2 from n-Pb scattering
- Neutrino coupling bound: |εν εe|1/2 < 7 × 10-5 from TEXONO ν-e scattering
- Signal significance: 6.8σ, corresponding to background fluctuation probability 5.6 × 10-12
- χ2/dof = 1.07 for the best fit with the X boson hypothesis

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the 8Be anomaly reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2015) by providing a specific particle physics interpretation. The protophobic vector boson differs from the dark photon hypothesis (which would require kinetic mixing ε ≈ 0.011, excluded by NA48/2 at ε < 8 × 10-4) and instead requires a specific ratio of up to down quark charges (εd/εu between -2.3 and -1.8). The proposed boson may also alleviate the 3.6σ discrepancy in the muon's anomalous magnetic moment if lepton couplings are approximately generation-independent. The work enables searches in existing and planned experiments including DarkLight, HPS, LHCb, MESA, Mu3e, VEPP-3, SeaQuest, and SHiP.
