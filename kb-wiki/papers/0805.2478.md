# Short-range correlations and shell structure of medium-mass nuclei

> The single-particle spectrum of the two nuclei 133Sb and 101Sn is studied within the framework of the time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory starting from a class of onshell-equivalent realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. These potentials are derived from the CD-Bonn interaction by using the so-called V-low-k approach with various cutoff momenta. The results obtained evidence the crucial role of short-range correlations in producing the correct 2s1d0g0h shell structure.

## Key claim

The single-particle spectra of 133Sb and 101Sn, calculated using onshell-equivalent Vlow-k potentials derived from the CD-Bonn interaction, show that the correct 2s1d0g0h shell structure emerges only when the cutoff momentum is large enough (2.6 fm-1) to include high-momentum components of the nucleon-nucleon force; for small cutoffs the intruder 0h11/2 level lies far from the other levels, implying an incorrect shell closure at Z=70.

## Method

The authors describe each nucleus as a doubly-closed core plus one valence nucleon and compute single-particle energies using the time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory (Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion). They include all one-body irreducible valence-linked Goldstone diagrams up to third order in the residual interaction, summing the folded-diagram series to all orders via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. The input interactions are a class of Vlow-k potentials derived from the CD-Bonn potential with cutoff momenta Λ ranging from 2.1 to 2.6 fm-1, which are all onshell equivalent but differ in their offshell tensor force strength as measured by the deuteron D-state probability.

## Key numbers

- For 133Sb, the harmonic oscillator parameter used is ħω = 7.88 MeV, derived from the formula ħω = 45 A-1/3 - 25 A-2/3 for A=132.
- For 101Sn, the harmonic oscillator parameter is ħω = 8.55 MeV.
- The cutoff momenta Λ for the Vlow-k potentials range from 2.1 to 2.6 fm-1.
- The deuteron D-state probability PD for Vlow-k varies from 3.96% at Λ=2.1 fm-1 to 4.49% at Λ=2.6 fm-1, compared to 4.85% for the original CD-Bonn potential.
- Intermediate states in the diagram calculations include excitation energies up to Nmax=15 harmonic-oscillator quanta, achieving stability within a few tens of keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of single-particle valence nuclei in the sd-shell using the same perturbative framework (Kassis 1972, Maglione 1994, Sharon 2001) to the more challenging 2s1d0g0h shell, which includes the intruder 0h11/2 state. The results demonstrate that short-range (high-momentum) components of the nuclear force are crucial for reproducing the observed shell structure, and the authors note that for small cutoffs the missing high-momentum components could be compensated by an effective three-body force, as discussed by Schwenk (2006) and Hagen (2007). The work enables further investigation of how core softness (the 100Sn core is much softer than 132Sn) affects perturbative convergence and shell evolution toward the drip lines.
