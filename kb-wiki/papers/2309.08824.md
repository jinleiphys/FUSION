# Azimuthal distribution of exponential format for particle collective motions in heavy-ion collisions under asynchronous assumption

> Particle azimuth distributions are widely studied in heavy-ion collisions. They are often expanded in Fourier series to extract anisotropic flow harmonics simultaneously. It was recently proposed that the different orders of flows could happen asynchronously or noninterdependently. This study extends this idea to an exponential format of the azimuth distribution, which makes it straightforward to extract the asynchronous flow coefficients. We compare these new coefficients to the conventional ones, and find consistency in the leading coefficient and discrepancy in higher-order ones.

## Key claim

The exponential format of the azimuthal distribution (Eq. 4) provides a mathematically consistent alternative to the Fourier series for extracting flow coefficients under the assumption that different harmonic orders build up asynchronously; however, the new coefficients \(\tilde{v}_n\) and conventional Fourier coefficients \(v_n\) agree only for the leading harmonic (\(n=2\)) and differ for higher orders (e.g., \(n=4\)), with the discrepancy explained by contributions from lower-order \(\tilde{v}_n\) terms.

## Method

The author derives an exponential expansion of the particle azimuthal distribution \(f(\varphi) = A \exp\{\sum_n 2\tilde{v}_n \cos n\varphi + \sum_n 2\tilde{a}_n \sin n\varphi\}\) from the asynchronous flow assumption of Xu (2023), where each harmonic is built up in infinitesimal steps. The new coefficients \(\tilde{v}_n\) are extracted by taking the logarithm of the azimuthal histogram and integrating over \(\varphi\). The method is tested using the AMPT model (v1.25t4cu2/v2.25t4cu2) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, with 22 million minimum-bias events; the reaction plane is fixed to zero, and \(\tilde{v}_n\) values are extrapolated to zero bin width to correct for finite histogram resolution.

## Key numbers

- For second-order coefficients in AMPT simulations of Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the relative difference between \(v_2\) and \(\tilde{v}_2\) is less than 0.02% across centralities.
- For fourth-order coefficients, \(v_4\) and \(\tilde{v}_4\) show obvious differences; the leading contribution to this difference is \(\tilde{v}_2^2/2\), derived from the modified Bessel function relation \(v_{kn} = I_k(2\tilde{v}_n)/I_0(2\tilde{v}_n) \approx \tilde{v}_n^k/k!\) for small \(\tilde{v}_n\).
- The simplified exponential distribution \(f_n(\varphi) \propto e^{2\tilde{v}_n \cos n\varphi}\) yields \(v_{kn} = I_k(2\tilde{v}_n)/I_0(2\tilde{v}_n)\), with asymptotic form \(v_{kn} \sim \tilde{v}_n^k/k!\) for small \(\tilde{v}_n\) (typically a few percent).
- AMPT simulations use particles with transverse momentum \(0.2 < p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) and pseudorapidity \(-1 < \eta < 1\), for centrality range 0–80%.

## Context

This work extends the asynchronous flow hypothesis of Xu (2023), who proposed that different harmonic orders act as independent multiplicative operators, by further considering that each harmonic builds up gradually over time. The exponential format bridges the conventional Fourier expansion (Poskanzer and Voloshin, 1998) and the asynchronous product format, and it simplifies the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling relation (Molnar and Voloshin, 2003; STAR Collaboration, 2003; PHENIX Collaboration, 2003) by making the scaling factor \(n_q\) appear linearly in the exponent. The results raise the physics question of whether \(v_n\) or \(\tilde{v}_n\) is the more fundamental coefficient, which requires further investigation of the time evolution of flow and effects such as the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev et al., 1998, 2004, 2007).
