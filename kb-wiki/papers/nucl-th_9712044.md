# In-medium kaon and antikaon properties in the quark-meson coupling model

> The properties of the kaon, $K$, and antikaon, $\kbar$, in nuclear medium are studied in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Employing a constituent quark-antiquark (MIT bag model) picture, their excitation energies in a nuclear medium at zero momentum are calculated within mean field approximation. The scalar, and the vector mesons are assumed to couple directly to the nonstrange quarks and antiquarks in the $K$ and $\kbar$ mesons. It is demonstrated that the $ρ$ meson induces different mean field potentials for each member of the isodoublets, $K$ and $\kbar$, when they are embedded in asymmetric nuclear matter. Furthermore, it is also shown that this $ρ$ meson potential is repulsive for the $K^-$ meson in matter with a neutron excess, and renders $K^-$ condensation less likely to occur.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ρ meson induces different mean field potentials for each member of the kaon and antikaon isodoublets in asymmetric nuclear matter, and that this ρ meson potential is repulsive for the K⁻ meson in matter with a neutron excess, making K⁻ condensation less likely to occur. The authors note that the model gives a very slightly attractive K⁺N potential, which they consider a minor shortcoming they compensate for by rescaling the ω coupling constant.

## Method

The authors employ the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using a static spherical MIT bag description for the kaon and antikaon mesons, with σ, ω, and ρ mesons coupling directly only to the nonstrange quarks and antiquarks. Excitation energies at zero momentum are calculated within mean field approximation for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, with the bag radius determined self-consistently. The coupling constants g^q_σ, g^q_ω, and g^q_ρ are adjusted to reproduce the saturation energy and density of symmetric nuclear matter and the bulk symmetry energy, while the ω coupling to the kaon is rescaled to reproduce the empirical K⁺N potential of +20 MeV at ρ_B = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model, initiated by Guichon, to study kaon and antikaon properties in nuclear matter for the first time, building on the pioneering work of Kaplan and Nelson on K⁻ condensation in dense matter. The results contrast with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) predictions by showing a non-linear decrease of effective masses with density due to the quark structure. The findings are relevant for neutron star studies and heavy ion collisions, where the ρ meson effect on K⁻ has been omitted in prior work by Li and others, and the authors note that future work should include hyperons self-consistently as discussed by Schaffner and Mishustin.
