# Beyond the quark model of hadrons from lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD can give direct information on OZI-violating contributions to mesons. Here we explore the contributions that split flavour singlet and non-singlet meson masses. I discuss in detail the spectrum and decays for scalar mesons (ie including glueball effects). I also review the status of hybrid mesons and their decays.

# Beyond the quark model of hadrons from lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can provide direct, first-principles information on OZI-violating gluonic contributions to mesons, but the results are hampered by the need to extrapolate from heavier quark masses to physical light quark masses using chiral perturbation theory, and by the difficulty of taking the continuum limit (lattice spacing \(a \to 0\)) computationally.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of flavour-singlet meson correlators, which separate into connected and disconnected quark-loop contributions; the ratio of these correlators yields the mass splitting between flavour-singlet and non-singlet mesons, quantifying OZI-violating effects. For scalar mesons, quenched (no sea quarks) glueball spectra are computed and extrapolated to the continuum limit, and the mixing between glueball and quark-antiquark states is studied using both quenched and dynamical (\(N_f=2\)) fermion simulations. Hybrid meson masses and decays are investigated using static heavy-quark potentials from lattice data, solved via the Schrodinger equation in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and also from direct light-quark hybrid spectrum calculations.

## Key numbers

- Scalar glueball mass in quenched QCD: approximately 1.6 GeV (using scale \(r_0 = 0.5\) fm)
- Tensor glueball mass in quenched QCD: approximately 2.2 GeV
- Mixing energy between scalar glueball and two degenerate quarks of mass around the strange quark mass: \(E \approx 0.36\) GeV (Weingarten, 1999) and 0.44 GeV (Michael, 2002)
- Estimated width for scalar glueball decay to two pseudoscalar mesons: of order 100 MeV
- Lightest spin-exotic hybrid meson (\(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\)) for \(b\bar{b}\) quarks: \((m_H - m_{2S}) r_0 = 1.9 \pm 0.1\) (quenched); with \(N_f=2\) studies give \((m_H - m_{2S}) r_0 = 2.4 \pm 0.2\)
- Using the \(\Upsilon(2S)\) mass, the lightest spin-exotic hybrid for bottomonium is in the mass range 10.7 to 10.9 GeV
- Lightest spin-exotic hybrid for strange quarks: mass around 2 GeV; for light (\(u,d\)) quarks, approximately 120 MeV lighter
- Lightest spin-exotic hybrid for charm quarks: mass near 4.4 GeV from light-quark extrapolation; naive static-quark estimate gives 4.0 GeV

## Context

This review extends the quark model by quantifying gluonic contributions to mesons that are absent in the naive quark model, particularly for flavour-singlet scalar and pseudoscalar mesons where glueball mixing is significant. The work builds on quenched glueball spectrum calculations from the UKQCD Collaboration (1999) and Morningstar and Peardon (1999), and on dynamical fermion studies by Bali et al. (2000) and by Michael (2001, 2002). The results enable future lattice studies to incorporate full QCD with realistic light quarks and to compute decay widths of hybrid mesons, which can be compared with experimental candidates from \(p\bar{p}\) annihilation and other facilities. Open questions include whether the large scalar glueball mixing seen on coarse lattices persists to the continuum limit, and whether the experimental hybrid candidates near 1.4 and 1.6 GeV can be reconciled with lattice mass estimates that are significantly higher.
