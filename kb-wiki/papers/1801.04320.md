# Two-photon production of dilepton pairs in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> The STAR collaboration has observed an excess production of $e^+e^-$ pairs in relativistic heavy ion collisions, over the expectations from hadronic production models. The excess pairs have transverse momenta $p_T < 150\ {\rm MeV}/c$ and are most prominent in peripheral gold-gold and uranium-uranium collisions. The pairs exhibit a peak at the $J/ψ$ mass, but include a wide continuum, with pair invariant masses from 400 MeV/c$^2$ up to 2.6 GeV/c$^2$. The ALICE Collaboration observes a similar excess in peripheral lead-lead collisions, but only at the $J/ψ$ mass, without a corresponding continuum. This paper presents a calculation of the cross-section and kinematic for two-photon production of $e^+e^-$ pairs, and find general agreement with the STAR data. The calculation is based on the STARlight simulation code, which is based on the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon approach. The STAR continuum observations are compatible with two-photon production of $e^+e^-$ pairs. The ALICE analysis required individual muon $p_T$ be greater than 1 GeV/c; this eliminated almost all of the pairs from two-photon interactions, while leaving most of the $J/ψ$ decays.

# Two-photon production of dilepton pairs in peripheral heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The continuum excess of low-transverse-momentum electron-positron pairs observed by STAR in peripheral heavy-ion collisions is compatible with two-photon production, but the ALICE non-observation of a similar continuum in muon pairs is expected because their muon transverse momentum cut of 1 GeV/c eliminates almost all two-photon pairs while retaining most J/ψ decays.

## Method

The calculation uses the STARlight Monte Carlo code, which employs the Weizsäcker-Williams equivalent photon approximation to compute the photon flux from each nucleus, combined with the lowest-order Breit-Wheeler cross-section for γγ → e⁺e⁻. The code was modified to restrict the impact parameter integration range to match specific centrality bins (e.g., 60-80% centrality), and the cross-section is integrated over the transverse positions of both ions and the interaction point. The pair transverse momentum is obtained from the vector sum of the individual photon transverse momenta, which are derived from the Weizsäcker-Williams approach including the nucleon form factor.

## Key numbers

- The excess pairs observed by STAR have transverse momenta pT < 150 MeV/c and invariant masses from 400 MeV/c² up to 2.6 GeV/c²
- For 60-80% centrality Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon, the calculated γγ → e⁺e⁻ cross-section within STAR acceptance (Mee > 0.4 GeV/c², |yee| < 1) is 3.7 mb, with 3.3% of events passing individual lepton cuts (pT,e > 200 MeV/c, |ηe| < 1), yielding 8.9×10⁻⁵ visible pairs per hadronic event
- For 60-80% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV/nucleon, the corresponding cross-section is 6.4 mb with 3.5% acceptance, yielding 1.4×10⁻⁴ visible pairs per hadronic event
- The exponential slope parameters for Au-Au pair pT² distributions are 430 ± 4 (GeV/c)⁻² for mass range 0.4-0.76 GeV/c², 402 ± 6 (GeV/c)⁻² for 0.76-1.2 GeV/c², and 367 ± 9 (GeV/c)⁻² for 1.2-2.6 GeV/c², fitted over 0.002 < pT² < 0.02 (GeV/c)²
- The integrated cross-sections for Mee > 0.4 GeV/c² in 60-80% centrality collisions are 6.7 mb for Au-Au, 8.7 mb for U-U, and 24.2 mb for Pb-Pb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-photon interactions in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) by STAR (Adams 2004), ATLAS (Dyndal 2017), ALICE (Abbas 2013), and CMS (Khachatryan 2016) into the peripheral hadronic collision regime. The calculation explains the STAR continuum observation (Shuai, Yang) as two-photon production, while showing why the ALICE forward muon spectrometer (Adam 2015) does not see the continuum due to its muon pT cut. The results enable future ALICE mid-rapidity studies to search for this continuum with sufficiently low lepton pT thresholds, and provide a basis for more detailed comparisons between data and two-photon theory.
