# Quarks stars in SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector coupling

> In this work we study the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the SU (2) version with repulsive vector coupling and apply it to quark stellar matter. We discuss the influence of the vector interaction on the equation of state (EoS) and study quark stars that are composed of pure quark matter with two flavors. We show that, increasing the vector coupling, we obtain more massive stars with larger radii for the same central energy density.

# Quarks stars in SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector coupling

## Key claim

Increasing the repulsive vector coupling in the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model produces more massive quark stars with larger radii for the same central energy density, but the authors caution that within this model quark matter will hadronize at the star surface because the energy per particle at zero pressure is greater than the nucleon mass, making pure quark stars unlikely and hybrid stars more plausible.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar-isovector, and repulsive vector-isoscalar interaction terms, computed the mean-field thermodynamic potential density at zero temperature in the Hartree approximation, and regularized the vacuum contribution with a cutoff. They imposed beta equilibrium and charge neutrality including leptons as a relativistic degenerate Fermi gas, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for pure quark stars at different ratios of vector to scalar coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for $g_V/g_S = 0$: 1.29 solar masses, radius 7.36 km, central energy density 13.69 fm$^{-4}$
- Maximum mass for $g_V/g_S = 0.25$: 1.51 solar masses, radius 7.99 km, central energy density 11.61 fm$^{-4}$
- Maximum mass for $g_V/g_S = 0.5$: 1.67 solar masses, radius 8.50 km, central energy density 11.01 fm$^{-4}$
- Maximum mass for $g_V/g_S = 0.75$: 1.76 solar masses, radius 8.79 km, central energy density 9.56 fm$^{-4}$
- Maximum mass for $g_V/g_S = 1.0$: 1.86 solar masses, radius 9.11 km, central energy density 8.69 fm$^{-4}$
- The chiral phase transition changes from first order to second order when $g_V/g_S \geq 0.75$

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to quark and hybrid stars by Buballa (2005), Baldo et al., Blaschke et al., and Debora et al., adding a repulsive vector coupling to study its effect on the equation of state and stellar structure. The results show that vector interactions stiffen the equation of state and increase maximum masses beyond the 1.2 solar masses typical of many neutron stars, but the radii remain below 10 km, smaller than the average neutron star value of 12-15 km. The authors note that a work in the SU(3) version with vector interaction is underway, and a superconducting quark phase proposed by Mane et al. and Linares et al. should also be considered.
