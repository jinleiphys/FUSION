# Finite density phase transition of QCD with $N_f=4$ and $N_f=2$ using canonical ensemble method

> In a progress toward searching for the QCD critical point, we study the finite density phase transition of $N_f = 4$ and 2 lattice QCD at finite temperature with the canonical ensemble approach. We develop a winding number expansion method to accurately project out the particle number from the fermion determinant which greatly extends the applicable range of baryon number sectors to make the study feasible. Our lattice simulation was carried out with the clover fermions and improved gauge action. For a given temperature, we calculate the baryon chemical potential from the canonical approach to look for the mixed phase as a signal for the first order phase transition. In the case of $N_f=4$, we observe an "S-shape" structure in the chemical potential-density plane due to the surface tension of the mixed phase in a finite volume which is a signal for the first order phase transition. We use the Maxwell construction to determine the phase boundaries for three temperatures below $T_c$. The intersecting point of the two extrapolated boundaries turns out to be at the expected first order transition point at $T_c$ with $μ= 0$. This serves as a check for our method of identifying the critical point. We also studied the $N_f =2$ case, but do not see a signal of the mixed phase for temperature as low as 0.83 $T_c$.

## Key claim
The authors use a canonical ensemble approach with a winding number expansion method to study finite-density QCD phase transitions, observing an "S-shape" structure in the chemical potential-density plane for N_f=4 as a signal of first-order phase transition, but they do not see such a signal for N_f=2 at temperatures as low as 0.83 T_c, which suggests the method's applicability depends on the number of flavors.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on 6^3 x 4 lattices using clover fermions and an improved Iwasaki gauge action. They computed the canonical partition function via a winding number expansion method that projects out fixed baryon number sectors from the fermion determinant, then measured the baryon chemical potential as a function of baryon density to detect the mixed phase signal. For N_f=4, they applied Maxwell construction to determine phase boundaries at three temperatures below T_c and extrapolated to find the intersection point at T_c with zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- For N_f=4: simulations performed at T/T_c = 0.89(1), 0.91(2), and 0.95(2) with pion masses around 828-834 MeV
- For N_f=2: simulations performed at T/T_c = 0.83(1), 0.87(2), and 0.92(1) with pion masses around 710-744 MeV
- The lattice spacing for N_f=4 ranges from a = 0.312(4) fm at beta=1.62 to a = 0.351(2) fm at beta=1.56
- The lattice spacing for N_f=2 ranges from a = 0.270(3) fm at beta=1.81 to a = 0.321(2) fm at beta=1.75
- The average sign for N_f=4 ranges mostly from 0.6 to 0.2 across the baryon number scan
- The winding number expansion method with 16 determinant evaluations extends the applicable range of baryon number sectors up to k=40, compared to discrete Fourier transform which is only valid up to k=6

## Context
This work extends the canonical ensemble approach to finite-density lattice QCD previously developed by Liu (2003) and Alexandru (2005), using a winding number expansion technique introduced by Meng (2008) to overcome numerical instabilities in the Fourier projection. The N_f=4 case serves as a testbed because it is known to have a first-order phase transition at zero chemical potential, and the successful extrapolation of phase boundaries to meet at T_c with mu=0 validates the method. The results enable future searches for the QCD critical point in the N_f=3 case, which is the primary target for upcoming experimental programs at RHIC and FAIR.
