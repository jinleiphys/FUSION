# Revisiting the deuteron mass radius via near-threshold $ρ^0$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson photoproduction

> We present a comprehensive analysis of near-threshold photoproduction of $ρ^0$, $ω$, and $ϕ$ mesons on a deuterium target, utilizing published datasets from DESY and SLAC for $ρ^0$ and $ω$ production, as well as data from the LEPS and CLAS Collaborations for $ϕ$ production. In extracting the deuteron mass radius, we adopt a dipole parametrization for the scalar gravitational form factor, which effectively captures the $|t|$-dependence of the differential cross sections associated with vector meson photoproduction. In addition, results from alternative commonly used form factor parametrizations are also considered and compared. Employing the vector meson dominance (VMD) framework and invoking low-energy Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) theorems, we extract the deuteron mass radius from near-threshold photoproduction data of $ρ^0$, $ω$, and $ϕ$ mesons. The mass radii obtained from the various datasets are found to be consistent within statistical uncertainties, yielding an average value of $2.03 \pm 0.13$ fm under the dipole form assumption. We also provide a detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the extracted radius to different choices of gravitational form factor models. Our result represents a significant improvement in precision compared to earlier estimates based solely on $ϕ$ meson photoproduction, offering new constraints for theoretical models of nuclear structure and deepening our understanding of the mass distribution within the deuteron.

# Revisiting the deuteron mass radius via near-threshold ρ⁰, ω and φ meson photoproduction

## Key claim

The deuteron mass radius extracted from near-threshold photoproduction of ρ⁰, ω, and φ mesons yields an average value of 2.03 ± 0.13 fm under the dipole form assumption, but this result is model-dependent: different parameterizations of the scalar gravitational form factor produce radii ranging from approximately 0.68 fm (uniform model) to 4.04 fm (Yukawa model), with the dipole form favored by the authors as the most physically interpretable and consistent across channels.

## Method

The authors analyzed published differential cross-section data for near-threshold photoproduction of ρ⁰ mesons on deuterium from the ABHHM Collaboration at DESY (Eγ = 1.8–2.5 GeV), ω mesons from Eisenberg et al. at SLAC (Eγ = 4.3 GeV), and φ mesons from the CLAS and LEPS Collaborations. Using the vector meson dominance (VMD) framework combined with a low-energy QCD theorem relating the photoproduction amplitude to the scalar gravitational form factor, they fitted the |t|-dependence of the differential cross sections with six different form factor parameterizations (Yukawa, dipole, tripole, fourthpole, Gaussian, and uniform) to extract the deuteron mass radius from each dataset.

## Key numbers

- Average deuteron mass radius under dipole form assumption: 2.03 ± 0.13 fm (combined analysis of ρ⁰, ω, and φ photoproduction)
- Deuteron mass radius from ρ⁰ photoproduction (dipole, global average of two resonance models): 2.33 ± 0.27 fm
- Deuteron mass radius from ω photoproduction (dipole): 2.04 ± 0.47 fm
- Deuteron mass radius from φ photoproduction (dipole, CLAS): 1.71 ± 0.23 fm
- Deuteron mass radius from φ photoproduction (dipole, LEPS): 2.14 ± 0.26 fm
- Deuteron mass radius from φ photoproduction (dipole, global average of CLAS and LEPS): 1.90 ± 0.17 fm
- Deuteron charge radius (CODATA-2020, for comparison): 2.1424 ± 0.0021 fm
- Dipole parameter Λ from ρ⁰ data (Model i): 0.27 ± 0.05 GeV
- Dipole parameter Λ from ω data: 0.33 ± 0.08 GeV
- Dipole parameter Λ from φ data (LEPS): 0.32 ± 0.04 GeV

## Context

This work extends the previous determination of the deuteron mass radius by Wang et al. (2021), which was based solely on φ meson photoproduction data and yielded 1.95 ± 0.19 fm, by incorporating ρ⁰ and ω meson channels for the first time in a combined analysis. The results are consistent with earlier extractions using the VMD framework applied to nucleon and light-nuclei mass radii by Wang et al. (2021), Kharzeev (2021), and Han et al. (2022). The analysis demonstrates that the dipole-type gravitational form factor, motivated by the two-gluon exchange picture from Frankfurt et al. (2002) and holographic QCD duality from Mamo et al. (2019, 2021, 2022), provides the most consistent description across all three vector meson channels. Future high-precision measurements of forward differential cross sections, particularly for heavier vector mesons such as J/ψ and Υ, are needed to constrain the form factor models and reduce the current model dependence.
