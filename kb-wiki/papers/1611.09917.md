# Nucleon spin-averaged forward virtual Compton tensor at large $Q^2$

> The nucleon spin-averaged forward virtual Compton tensor determines important physical quantities such as electromagnetically-induced mass differences of nucleons, and two-photon exchange contributions in hydrogen spectroscopy. It depends on two kinematic variables: $ν$, the virtual photon energy in the proton rest frame, and $Q^2$, the photon's invariant four-momentum squared. Using the operator product expansion, we calculate the tensor's large-$Q^2$ behavior for $ν=0$, including for the first time the full spin-2 contribution and correcting a previous result in the literature for the spin-0 contribution. Implications for the proton radius puzzle are discussed.

## Key claim

Using the operator product expansion (OPE), the authors calculate the large-\(Q^2\) behavior of the nucleon spin-averaged forward virtual Compton tensor at \(\nu=0\), including for the first time the full spin-2 contribution and correcting a previous result in the literature for the spin-0 contribution; the spin-2 contribution is shown to be numerically dominant for applications to muonic hydrogen.

## Method

The authors perform an OPE evaluation of the forward virtual Compton tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}_{\rm sym}\) at leading power in \(1/Q^2\), matching onto dimension-four quark and gluon operators (spin-0 and spin-2) at one-loop order in QCD perturbation theory. They compute the Wilson coefficients for both quark and gluon operators using on-shell quark states and a background-field analysis in Fock-Schwinger gauge, regularizing infrared divergences in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions. The nucleon matrix elements of the spin-0 and spin-2 operators are then evaluated using lattice QCD results, chiral perturbation theory inputs, and parton distribution function analyses (MSTW), and the resulting expression for \(W_1(0,Q^2)\) is assembled.

## Key numbers

- The spin-0 quark matrix element for the proton is given by \(f^{(0)}_{u,p}\) and \(f^{(0)}_{d,p}\) derived from \(\Sigma_{\pi N} = 44(13)\ \text{MeV}\) and the quark mass ratio \(R_{ud} = 0.48(10)\).
- The strange quark spin-0 matrix element is \(m_N f^{(0)}_{s,N} = 40(20)\ \text{MeV}\); the charm quark spin-0 matrix element from lattice QCD is \(m_N f^{(0)}_{c,N} = 80(30)\ \text{MeV}\) and from perturbative QCD is \(69(3)\ \text{MeV}\).
- The spin-2 proton matrix elements at \(\mu = 2\ \text{GeV}\) are: \(f_{u,p}^{(2)} = 0.346(6)\), \(f_{d,p}^{(2)} = 0.192(5)\), \(f_{s,p}^{(2)} = 0.034(2)\), \(f_{c,p}^{(2)} = 0.0088(3)\).
- The renormalized gluon Wilson coefficients at scale \(\mu\) are: \(c_{1g}(\mu) = (\alpha_s/4\pi) \sum_f e_f^2 (1/3)\), \(c_{2g}(\mu) = (\alpha_s/4\pi) \sum_f e_f^2 (-14/3 + (8/3)\log(Q^2/\mu^2))\), \(c_{3g}(\mu) = (\alpha_s/4\pi) \sum_f e_f^2 (2 - (8/3)\log(Q^2/\mu^2))\).

## Context

This work extends the OPE analysis of the forward Compton amplitude originally performed by Collins (1979), which considered only spin-0 operators and contained an error in the spin-0 contribution. The inclusion of spin-2 operators is essential for applications to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, where the subtraction function \(W_1^p(0,Q^2)\) dominates the uncertainty in the proton charge radius measurement (Pohl 2010). The results also impact the determination of the electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass splitting, previously studied by Cottingham (1963), Walker-Loud (2012), and Gasser (2015). The corrected and extended OPE calculation enables more reliable constraints on two-photon exchange contributions in hydrogen spectroscopy and on the isovector nucleon electromagnetic self-energy.
