# Bound State Formation in Time Dependent Potentials

> We study the temporal formation of quantum mechanical bound states within a one-dimensional attractive square-well potential, by first solving the time-independent Schroedinger equation and then study a time dependent system with an external time-dependent potential. For this we introduce Gaussian potentials with different spatial and temporal extensions, and generalize this description also for subsequent pulses and for random, noisy potentials. Our main goal is to study the time scales, in which the bound state is populated and depopulated. Particularly we clarify a likely connection between the uncertainty relation for energy and time and the transition time between different energy eigenstates. We demonstrate, that the formation of states is not delayed due to the uncertainty relation but follows the pulse shape of the perturbation. In addition we investigate the (non-)applicability of first-order perturbation theory on the considered quantum system.

## Key claim
The formation of bound states in a time-dependent potential is not delayed by the energy-time uncertainty relation; instead, the population of the bound state follows the pulse shape of the perturbation essentially instantaneously on the timescale of the pulse duration.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schroedinger equation for a single particle in a one-dimensional attractive square-well potential with infinite walls, using a basis expansion in the eigenstates of the time-independent Hamiltonian. They introduce time-dependent Gaussian potential pulses of varying spatial and temporal widths, and numerically integrate the coupled differential equations using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with a truncated Hilbert space of 110 eigenstates. The study also extends to multiple sequential pulses and to stochastic potentials with Gaussian-distributed amplitudes to simulate random interactions.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the single bound state: -2.3 MeV, obtained with potential depth V0 = -18 MeV, well half-width a = 0.6 fm (2a = 1.2 fm), and box size L = 100 fm.
- For a single pulse with sigma_t = 1 fm, sigma_x = 0.12 fm, and V = 100 MeV, the initially populated bound state is depopulated by about 4%.
- For a single pulse with sigma_t = 1 fm, sigma_x = 1.2 fm, and V = 100 MeV, the initially populated bound state is depopulated by about 30%.
- The energy cutoff of the truncated Hilbert space is about 140 MeV (110 eigenstates).
- For the stochastic potential, 2000 pulses were used with Delta t = 0.02 fm, sigma_V = 50 MeV, and sigma_x = 1.2 fm; results were averaged over 200 Monte-Carlo realizations.
- For a long pulse with sigma_t = 30 fm, sigma_x = 1.2 fm, and V = 100 MeV, the normalization constant from first-order perturbation theory is N approximately 74, indicating strong non-perturbative behavior.

## Context
This work addresses the question of bound state formation times in nuclear physics, motivated by the production of light nuclei such as deuterons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It extends the discussion of whether formation times scale as the inverse binding energy (tau ~ 1/E_B) due to the energy-time uncertainty relation, as might be inferred from Heisenberg's principle. The study contrasts with phenomenological coalescence models (e.g., Toneev (1983), Nagle and Sorge, Monreal and Greiner) and kinetic transport approaches (e.g., Danielewicz (1991), Oliinychenko (2018), Vovchenko (2020), Neidig (2022)) that describe deuteron production via three-body reactions. The results enable further development of quantum-theoretical descriptions for bound state formation in open quantum systems, relevant for light nuclei and heavy quarkonia production in heavy-ion collisions, and point toward the need for incorporating dissipation mechanisms such as those in the Caldeira-Leggett master equation or Kadanoff-Baym equations.
