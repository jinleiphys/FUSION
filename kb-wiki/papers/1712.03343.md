# Isospin nonconservation for fp-shell nuclei by spectral distribution theory

> The one- plus two-body isospin nonconserving nuclear interactions are included in the prediction of ground state energies of fp shell nuclei using spectral distribution theory. This in turn is used to calculate the linear term in the isobaric mass-multiplet equation and the predictions are then compared to experimental values after the addition of the Coulomb contribution. The agreement is found to be reasonable as observed for sd shell nuclei earlier. One also sees that in this method the contribution to the linear term comes almost completely from the one body isovector Hamiltonian and that results in a huge simplification of the problem.

## Key claim

Spectral distribution theory, which avoids explicit diagonalization of the Hamiltonian, can predict the linear term (b coefficient) of the isobaric mass-multiplet equation for fp-shell nuclei with reasonable agreement to experimental data, provided the Coulomb contribution is added separately. The contribution to the linear term comes almost completely from the one-body isovector Hamiltonian, making the two-body isovector and isotensor parts negligible for this specific calculation.

## Method

The authors used spectral distribution theory with the FPV interaction for the fp-shell (orbits 0f5/2, 0f7/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2) with 40Ca as the closed core, calculating ground state energies via the Ratcliff procedure for fixed-isospin configurations with 3, 5, and 6 valence particles. They computed configuration centroids and widths by evaluating traces of the isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor Hamiltonians in proton-neutron formalism, then extracted the IMME b coefficient by comparing lowest state energies from the isoscalar-plus-isovector Hamiltonian with those from the isoscalar Hamiltonian alone. The Coulomb contribution to b was taken from the global fit of Ormand (1997).

## Key numbers

- For A=43 (T=1/2): total predicted |b| = 7.79 MeV, observed |b| = 7.65 MeV
- For A=45 (T=1/2): total predicted |b| = 8.08 MeV, observed |b| = 7.91 MeV
- For A=46 (T=1): total predicted |b| = 8.22 MeV, observed |b| = 8.11 MeV
- For the (3,1/2,7/2) ground state: lowest state energy with total H = -35.21 MeV, with H(0) only = -31.52 MeV
- For the (5,1/2,7/2) ground state: lowest state energy with total H = -61.01 MeV, with H(0) only = -57.30 MeV
- For the (6,1,0) ground state: lowest state energy with total H = -78.57 MeV, with H(0) only = -71.16 MeV
- Coulomb contributions to b: -0.41 MeV for A=43, -0.68 MeV for A=45, -0.81 MeV for A=46

## Context

This work extends the spectral distribution theory approach previously applied to sd-shell nuclei by Kar and Sarkar (2015) to the fp-shell region, using the Ormand and Brown (1989) parametrization of isospin-nonconserving interactions. The results are consistent with earlier shell-model calculations by Ormand and Brown (1989) and Lam, Smirnova, and Caurier (2013), but the spectral distribution method avoids the computational cost of full diagonalization. The finding that only the one-body isovector Hamiltonian contributes to the linear term enables a huge simplification for future calculations of the IMME b coefficient across other nuclear regions.
