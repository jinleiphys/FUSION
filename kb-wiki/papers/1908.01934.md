# $Ξ$ hyperons in the nuclear medium described by chiral NLO interactions

> Properties of the baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness $S=-2$ sector of chiral effective field theory at the next-to-leading order (NLO) level are explored by calculating $Ξ$ single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter. The results are transformed to the $Ξ$ potential in finite nuclei by a local-density approximation with convolution by a Gaussian form factor to simulate finite-range effects. The $Ξ$ potential is repulsive in a central region, and attractive in a surface area when the $Ξ$ energy is low. The attractive pocket can lower the $Ξ^-$ $s$ and $p$ atomic states. The obtained binding energies in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N are found to be conformable with those found in emulsion experiments at Japan's National Laboratory for High Energy Physics (KEK). $K^+$ spectra of $(K^-, K^+)$ $Ξ$ production inclusive processes on $^9$Be and $^{12}$C are also evaluated, using a semi-classical distorted wave method. The absolute values of the cross section are properly reproduced for $^9$Be, but the peak locates at a lower energy position than that of the experimental data. The calculated spectrum of $^{12}$C should be compared with the forthcoming result from the new experiments recently carried out at KEK with better resolution than before. The comparison would be valuable to improve the understanding of the $ΞN$ interaction, the parametrization of which has still large uncertainties.

## Key claim
The chiral NLO baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness S=-2 sector produce a Ξ-nucleus potential that is repulsive in the central region but attractive in the surface area at low Ξ energies, yielding Ξ⁻ bound states in ¹²C and ¹⁴N whose binding energies are consistent with emulsion experiments at KEK, though the calculated (K⁻,K⁺) inclusive production spectrum on ⁹Be shows a peak at a lower energy position than the experimental data.

## Method
The author employed lowest-order Brueckner theory (G-matrix equation) with baryon-channel coupling to ΛΛ, ΛΣ, and ΣΣ states, using chiral NLO interactions with a 550 MeV cutoff. The resulting Ξ single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter were transformed to finite-nucleus potentials via a local-density approximation with Gaussian convolution (β=1.0 fm) to simulate finite-range effects. Inclusive (K⁻,K⁺) spectra were calculated using a semiclassical distorted wave method.

## Key numbers
- Ξ single-particle potential at zero momentum in symmetric nuclear matter: U_Ξ_real(k=0) = -8.8 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F=1.35 fm⁻¹ (normal density ρ₀)
- Ξ⁻ 0s bound state in ¹²C: energy -4.22 MeV (at E=-5 MeV potential) with root-mean-square radius 3.20 fm
- Ξ⁻ 0s bound state in ¹⁴N: energy -5.40 MeV (at E=-5 MeV potential) with root-mean-square radius 3.03 fm
- Ξ⁻ 0p bound state in ¹²C: energy -0.35 MeV (at E=-5 MeV potential) with radius 16.00 fm
- Ξ⁻ 0p bound state in ¹⁴N: energy -0.67 MeV (at E=-5 MeV potential) with radius 9.04 fm
- Experimental Ξ⁻-¹⁴N binding energy from emulsion: 4.38±0.25 MeV or 1.11±0.25 MeV (Nakazawa 2015)
- Experimental Ξ⁻-¹²C candidate binding energies: 3.89±0.24, 2.84±0.15, and 0.82±0.17 MeV (Aoki 2009)

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach from the S=-1 sector (ΛN and ΣN interactions by Haidenbauer 2013) to the S=-2 sector using updated NLO interactions (Haidenbauer 2019). It contrasts with earlier phenomenological potentials such as the canonical 14 MeV Woods-Saxon depth inferred from BNL data (Khaustov 2000) and with other baryon-baryon interaction models like the Kyoto-Niigata quark-model potential fss2 and the Nijmegen ESC08c. The results enable comparison with forthcoming higher-resolution KEK (K⁻,K⁺) data on ¹²C (Nagae 2018) and help constrain the poorly determined ΞN interaction parametrization, which is relevant for understanding the hyperon puzzle in neutron star matter.
