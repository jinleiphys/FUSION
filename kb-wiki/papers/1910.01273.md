# Holographic Approaches to DIS on a Nucleus

> We consider deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a dense nucleus described as an extremal RN-AdS black hole with holographic quantum fermions in the bulk. We find that the R-ratio (the ratio of the structure function of the black hole to proton) exhibit shadowing for $x < 0.1$, anti-shadowing for $0.1 < x < 0.3$, EMC-like effect for $0.3 < x < 0.8$ and Fermi motion for $x > 0.8$ in a qualitative agreement with the experimental observation of the ratio for DIS on nucleus for all range of $x$. We also take the dilute limit of the black hole and show that its R-ratio exhibits EMC-like effect for $0.2 < x < 0.8$ and the Fermi motion for $x > 0.8$, and no shadowing is observed in the dilute limit for both bottom-up (using Thomas-Fermi approximation for the nucleon distribution inside the dilute nucleus), and top-down (considering the dilute nucleus to be a Fermi gas in AdS) approaches.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a dense nucleus modeled as an extremal RN-AdS black hole with holographic quantum fermions produces an R-ratio that qualitatively matches experimental nuclear DIS data across all x, including shadowing, anti-shadowing, EMC-like effect, and Fermi motion, but only when the black hole is dense enough; the dilute limit fails to produce shadowing.

## Method
The author models a dense nucleus as an extremal Reissner-Nordstrom AdS black hole and computes the structure function from the current-current correlation function in the classical black hole background at leading order in 1/N_c, then adds subleading quantum corrections from bulk Dirac fermions. For a dilute nucleus, two approaches are used: a bottom-up approach using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for nucleon distribution with incoherent scattering on individual holographic nucleons, and a top-down approach treating the nucleus as a Fermi gas in AdS spacetime with one-loop Witten diagrams.

## Key numbers
- For the dense classical black hole: structure function components scale as F^A_T ~ (3x^2 Q^2 / 4 m_N^2)^(2/3) and F^A_L ~ (3x^2 Q^2 / 4 m_N^2) with prefactors involving pi^5 (48 alpha)^2 / (2 N_c^2)
- For the dilute top-down approach at large-x: F_2(x,q^2)/A is proportional to (beta m_N^2 / q^2)^(tau-1) with tau=3, using k_F/m_N=1, e_R=0.3, and 2pi^2 c_5 / sqrt(4pi lambda)=0.01
- For the dense quantum black hole: the classical contribution scales as x^(1/3) with prefactor C_T (3q^2 / 4 m_N^2)^(2/3), and the quantum fermion correction scales as (mu^2 / q^2)^(nu_{k_F}+2) with k_F/m_N=3.5
- The R-ratio exhibits shadowing for x < 0.1, anti-shadowing for 0.1 < x < 0.3, EMC-like effect for 0.3 < x < 0.8, and Fermi motion for x > 0.8 in the dense case

## Context
This work extends holographic modeling of nuclear structure functions using AdS/CFT correspondence, building on prior calculations for DIS on single nucleons by Polchinski (2002) and on uncharged thermal AdS black holes by Hatta (2007). The author's own prior work (Mamo 2018, 2019) on holographic approaches to proton-nucleus collisions and nuclear structure functions is directly extended. The results enable a unified holographic description of nuclear DIS across all Bjorken-x, connecting black hole physics to nuclear shadowing and the EMC effect, and suggest that the dense nucleus behaves qualitatively differently from a dilute Fermi gas.
