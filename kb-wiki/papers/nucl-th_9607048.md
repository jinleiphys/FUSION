# How short is too short? Constraining zero-range interactions in nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We discuss a number of constraints on the effects of zero-range potentials in quantum mechanics. We show that for such a potential $p \cot(δ)$, where $p$ is the momentum of the nucleon in the center of mass frame and $δ$ is the S-wave phase shift, must be a monotonically decreasing function of energy. This implies that the effective range of the potential is non-positive. We also examine scattering from the sum of two potentials, one of which is a short-range interaction. We find that if the short-range interaction is of zero-range then it must be attractive, and the logarithmic derivative of the radial wave function at the origin must be a monotonically decreasing function of energy. If the short-range interaction is not of zero range then a constraint which gives the minimum possible range for it to fit the phase shifts exists. The implications of these results for effective field theory treatments of nucleon-nucleon interactions are discussed.

# How short is too short? Constraining zero-range interactions in nucleon-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

Zero-range interactions in nucleon-nucleon scattering cannot reproduce the observed positive effective range of the \(^1S_0\) channel, because for any potential that is the limit of finite-range interactions as the range goes to zero, the function \(p \cot(\delta)\) must be monotonically decreasing with energy, implying a non-positive effective range. Even when a long-range one-pion-exchange potential is explicitly included, the zero-range part must be attractive and its logarithmic derivative at the origin must be a monotonically decreasing function of energy.

## Method

The authors derive constraints on zero-range interactions by taking the limit \(R \rightarrow 0\) of a sequence of finite-range potentials \(V_R(r,r')\) that vanish for all \(r,r' \geq R\). They use Wigner's causality bound (1955) on the derivative of the S-wave phase shift with respect to momentum, and rewrite it as a constraint on \(p \cot(\delta)\). For the case with a long-range potential (one-pion exchange), they match logarithmic derivatives of the radial wave function at the matching radius \(R\) and analyze the small-\(R\) behavior of the solutions to the radial Schrödinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The effective range \(r_e\) for a zero-range interaction must satisfy \(r_e \leq 0\), as derived from the bound \(r_e \leq 2 \left[ R - \frac{R^2}{a} + \frac{R^3}{3 a^2} \right]\) in the limit \(R \rightarrow 0\).
- For the surface delta function potential \(V_R(r,r') = \frac{C}{4 \pi R^2} \delta(r - R) \delta^{(3)}(r - r')\), the coupling constant must behave as \(C \rightarrow - 4 \pi R\) as \(R \rightarrow 0\), showing the zero-range interaction must be attractive.
- The one-pion-exchange potential used is \(V_L(r) = -\alpha_\pi \frac{e^{-m_\pi r}}{r}\), with \(\alpha_\pi\) the pion-nucleon coupling constant and \(m_\pi\) the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends the earlier result of Cohen (1996) that repulsive zero-range interactions are trivial in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics, and generalizes it to the case where a long-range potential (one-pion exchange) is present. It directly contradicts the approach of Kaplan et al. (1996), who used dimensional regularization and \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) renormalization to obtain a \(T\)-matrix with arbitrary scattering length and effective range, including positive values. The paper shows that the two regularization methods used in effective field theory treatments of nucleon-nucleon scattering (momentum-space cutoff and dimensional regularization) are not equivalent. The results enable future work to derive a minimum range \(R\) for which a finite-range potential can fit the \(^1S_0\) scattering data, as announced for a forthcoming publication by Phillips (1996).
