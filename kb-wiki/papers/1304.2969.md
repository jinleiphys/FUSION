# Selected Experimental Results from Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC

> We review a subset of experimental results from the heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) facility at CERN. Excellent consistency is observed across all the experiments at the LHC (at center of mass energy of 2.76 TeV) for the measurements such as charged particle multiplicity density, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients and nuclear modification factor of charged hadrons. Comparison to similar measurements from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at lower energy (center of mass energy of 200 GeV) suggests that system formed at LHC has a higher energy density, larger system size, and lives for a longer time. These measurements are compared to model calculations to obtain physical insights on the properties of matter created at the RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental results from Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, claiming excellent consistency across ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS experiments for charged particle multiplicity density, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients, and nuclear modification factor, while noting that the comparison to RHIC results at √sNN = 200 GeV suggests the system at LHC has higher energy density, larger system size, and longer lifetime, though the review does not present new experimental data but rather summarizes existing measurements.

## Method

The authors compiled and compared published results from the three LHC heavy-ion experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) for charged particle multiplicity density dNch/dη, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v2, v3, v4 as functions of pT, and nuclear modification factor RAA of charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. They then compared these LHC measurements to corresponding results from RHIC Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, and compared both sets of data to model calculations including statistical thermal models, hydrodynamic models, and Glauber model simulations.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity density per participating nucleon pair at midrapidity increases by a factor of 2 from RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) to LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) in central heavy-ion collisions
- Bjorken energy density at LHC is about a factor of 3 larger than at RHIC, assuming formation time τ0 = 1 fm/c
- Decoupling time τf at LHC is about 10 fm/c, approximately 40% larger than at RHIC
- Average transverse mass ⟨mT⟩ for pions shows about 25% increase from RHIC to LHC
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin appears to saturate from RHIC top energy to LHC, while radial flow velocity ⟨β⟩ reaches a value close to 0.6 times the speed of light at LHC
- pT-integrated v2 increases by about 30% from top RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV) to LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) for charged particles in 20-30% centrality collisions
- Volume of homogeneity region from HBT analysis is two times larger at LHC than at RHIC
- Nuclear modification factor RAA for charged hadrons in 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions reaches a minimum at pT = 6-7 GeV/c and then gradually increases to an almost constant value at about 40 GeV/c

## Context

This review paper summarizes and compares the first heavy-ion results from LHC experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) with previous measurements from RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS) and lower-energy facilities (AGS, SPS). The work extends the energy reach of heavy-ion collision studies by a factor of about 14 in center-of-mass energy, enabling investigation of temperature dependence of observables and extended kinematic regions in rapidity and pT. The paper enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram by establishing baseline measurements at LHC energies and identifying open questions such as the discrepancy between chemical freeze-out temperatures extracted from different particle ratios (164 MeV from multi-strange hadrons versus 152 MeV from p/π and Λ/π ratios).
