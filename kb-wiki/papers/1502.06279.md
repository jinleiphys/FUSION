# Cluster virial expansion for quark and nuclear matter

> We employ the $Φ-$ derivable approach to many particle systems with strong correlations that can lead to the formation of bound states (clusters) of different size. We define a generic form of $Φ-$ functionals that is fully equivalent to a selfconsistent cluster virial expansion up to the second virial coefficient for interactions among the clusters. As examples we consider nuclei in nuclear matter and hadrons in quark matter, with particular attention to the case of the deuterons in nuclear matter and mesons in quark matter. We derive a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck equation of state, where the quasiparticle virial expansion is extended to include arbitrary clusters. The approach is applicable to nonrelativistic potential models of nuclear matter as well as to relativistic field theoretic models of quark matter. It is particularly suited for a description of cluster formation and dissociation in hot, dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Phi$-derivable approach to many-particle systems can be made fully equivalent to a selfconsistent cluster virial expansion up to the second virial coefficient for interactions among clusters, provided the $\Phi$ functional is defined via the T-matrix for binary collisions. The key qualifier is that this equivalence holds only when the vertex functions are replaced by the nonperturbative binary collision T-matrix in the channel A+B.

## Method

The author generalizes the $\Phi$-derivable approach to include A-particle correlations in a many-fermion system, defining a generic $\Phi$ functional where higher-order Green functions are replaced by T-matrices describing binary collisions of clusters. For the nonrelativistic case of deuterons in nuclear matter, a separable potential model is employed with a cluster decomposition of the nucleon selfenergy and density. For the relativistic case of mesons in quark matter, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is used with a meson polarization loop defining the meson T-matrix, and the quark selfenergy includes both meanfield and mesonic correlation contributions.

## Key numbers

- The correlation density for scattering states in nuclear matter contains a factor $2\sin^2\delta(E) \frac{d\delta(E)}{dE}$ in the integrand of the continuum contribution, where $\delta(E)$ is the scattering phase shift.
- The mesonic contribution to the thermodynamic potential includes a factor $2\sin^2\delta_M(k,\omega) \frac{\delta_M(k,\omega)}{d\omega}$ in the integrand, where $\delta_M(k,\omega)$ is the meson phase shift.
- The quark meanfield thermodynamic potential contains a term $\sigma_{\rm MF}= 2 N_f N_c G_S \int \frac{d^3 p}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{m}{E_p}[1-f_-(E_p)-f_+(E_p)]$ with $N_f=2$, $N_c=3$ for the NJL model.

## Context

This work extends the $\Phi$-derivable approach of Kadanoff and Baym (1962) to systems with composite particles, building on earlier cluster decomposition methods by Zimmermann (1985) and Ropke (2012). It provides a unified framework connecting the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for deuterons in nuclear matter (Ropke, 2012) with the relativistic treatment of mesons in quark matter (Blaschke, 2013), and corrects previous works by Hufner (1994), Zhuang (1994), and Blaschke (2013) that had disregarded the backreaction from mesonic correlations on the quark meanfield thermodynamics. The approach enables a consistent description of cluster formation and dissociation in hot, dense matter, applicable to both nonrelativistic nuclear matter and relativistic quark matter, and can be extended to include diquarks and baryons in a unified quark-hadron matter description.
