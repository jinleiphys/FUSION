# The Systematics of Quarkonium Production at the LHC and Double Parton Fragmentation

> In this paper we discuss the systematics of quarkonium production at the LHC. In particular, we focus on the necessity to sum logs of the form log(Q/p_perp) and log(p_perp/m_Q). We show that the former contributions are power suppressed, while the latter, whose contribution in fragmentation is well known, also arise in the short distance (i.e., non-fragmentation) production mechanisms. Though these contributions are suppressed by powers of m_Q/p_perp, they can be enhanced by inverse powers of v, the relative velocity between heavy quarks in the quarkonium. In the limit p_perp >> m_Q short distance production can be thought of as the fragmentation of a pair of partons (i.e., the heavy quark and anti-quark) into the final state quarkonium. We derive an all order factorization theorem for this process in terms of double parton fragmentation functions (DPFF) and calculate the one-loop anomalous dimension matrix for the DPFF.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in quarkonium production at the LHC, when the transverse momentum \(p_\perp\) is much larger than the heavy quark mass \(m_Q\) but not yet asymptotically large, the dominant theoretical uncertainty comes from large logarithms \(\log(p_\perp/m_Q)\) that arise in both fragmentation and short-distance production mechanisms; these logs can be resummed by treating short-distance production as double parton fragmentation, though the authors explicitly state they do not perform the resummation in this paper, leaving that for a subsequent publication.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive factorization theorems for quarkonium production in the regime \(p_\perp \gg m_Q\). They show that contributions with \(\log(Q/p_\perp)\) are power suppressed, while \(\log(p_\perp/m_Q)\) contributions arise in both fragmentation and short-distance production. They introduce double parton fragmentation functions (DPFF) to describe short-distance production in the limit \(p_\perp \gg m_Q\), derive an all-order factorization theorem for this process, and calculate the one-loop anomalous dimension matrix for the DPFF.

## Key numbers

- For \(J/\psi\) production at the LHC, CMS and ATLAS have measured \(p_\perp\) as high as 70 GeV at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV (Chatrchyan 2011, Aad 2011).
- For \(\Upsilon\) production at the LHC, CMS and ATLAS have measured \(p_\perp\) up to 24 GeV at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV (Khachatryan 2010, Aad 2011).
- For the \(J/\psi\) system, \(\alpha_s \sim v^2 \sim 0.3\); for the \(\Upsilon\) system, \(\alpha_s \sim v^2 \sim 0.1\).
- The crossover between short-distance production and fragmentation dominance occurs at \(p_\perp \sim 5\) GeV for \(J/\psi\) and \(p_\perp \sim 30\) GeV for \(\Upsilon\), based on the condition \(\frac{p_\perp^2}{(2m_Q)^2} \frac{v^4}{\alpha_s} \sim 1\).
- The short-distance production operator scales as \(\lambda^8\) in the SCET power counting, which is \(\lambda^2 \sim m_Q^2/p_\perp^2\) suppressed relative to the fragmentation contribution (which scales as \(\lambda^6\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium production started by Chung, Leibovich, and collaborators (2011, 2012) by providing a systematic framework for resumming \(\log(p_\perp/m_Q)\) at leading logarithmic order, which was previously only accomplished for single-parton fragmentation by Braaten, Cheung, and Yuan (1993, 1994). The paper addresses the intermediate \(p_\perp\) regime where most LHC data falls, and where the extraction of NRQCD matrix elements from global fits is complicated by large logs. The authors note that the classic NRQCD prediction of purely transverse polarization at high \(p_\perp\) has not been confirmed by data, and they suggest that resummation of \(\log(p_\perp/m_Q)\) may help resolve this polarization puzzle.
