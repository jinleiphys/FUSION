# Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo study of the Hyperon-Nucleon interaction in $Λ$-hypernuclei

> We investigate the role of two- and three-body $Λ$-nucleon forces by computing the ground state of a few $Λ$-hypernuclei with the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm. Calculations have been performed for masses up to A=41, including some open-shell hypernuclei. The results show that the use of a bare hyperon-nucleon force fitted on the available scattering data yields a consistent overestimate of the $Λ$-separation energy $B_Λ$. The inclusion of a hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interaction systematically reduces $B_Λ$, leading to a qualitatively good agreement with experimental data over the range of masses investigated.

## Key claim

The bare two-body Λ-nucleon force fitted to scattering data consistently overestimates the Λ-separation energy BΛ across hypernuclei up to A=41; including a three-body Λ-nucleon-nucleon interaction systematically reduces BΛ, yielding qualitatively good agreement with experiment, but the authors note that fine-tuning of the ΛNN parameters (particularly the repulsive term vDΛij) could further improve the results.

## Method

The authors used the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to compute ground-state energies of Λ-hypernuclei with masses up to A=41, including open-shell systems. They employed the Argonne AV4' and AV6' two-body nucleon-nucleon potentials (and the Minnesota potential as a test) together with the two-body ΛN and three-body ΛNN potentials developed by Usmani et al. (1995, 2008), which include two-pion exchange diagrams and a phenomenological repulsive term. The Λ-separation energy BΛ was obtained as the difference between the binding energy of the nucleus A-1Z and that of the hypernucleus AΛZ.

## Key numbers

- For 5ΛHe, BΛ with only the two-body ΛN interaction is 7.1(1) MeV (AV4'), 6.3(1) MeV (AV6'), and 7.4(1) MeV (Minnesota), all roughly twice the experimental value of 3.12(2) MeV.
- For 5ΛHe, including the three-body ΛNN interaction reduces BΛ to 5.0(1) MeV (AV4'), 5.1(1) MeV (AV6'), and 5.0(1) MeV (Minnesota), bringing it closer to experiment.
- The saturation energy of BΛ estimated from the two-body-only curve is around 30 MeV, which is unrealistic; including the three-body force brings the extrapolated saturation energy reasonably close to experimental data.
- For 17ΛO, the expected experimental BΛ is 13.59 MeV, and calculations with different values of the repulsive parameter WD show that the separation energy can be moved closer to this value.

## Context

This work extends the AFDMC method to Λ-hypernuclei, building on the coordinate-space ΛN and ΛNN potentials developed by Usmani et al. (1995, 2008). It addresses the need for reliable benchmarks on hyperon-nucleon forces from hypernuclei, given that only limited Λ-nucleon scattering data exist and that various hyperon-nucleon interactions (Nijmegen, Julich, χEFT) give no overall agreement in neutron star equation-of-state calculations. The results enable future refitting of the ΛNN force parameters to reproduce experimental BΛ data across a wide mass range, and suggest that the repulsive three-body force should lead to a stiffer equation of state for Λ-neutron matter, potentially reconciling hyperon onset in neutron stars with observed masses of order 2 M⊙.
