# Hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter with boson condensation

> In the present work we study the hadron-quark phase transition with boson condensation in asymmetric matter by investigating the binodal surface and extending it to finite temperature in order to mimic the QCD phase diagram. We consider a system with two conserved charges (isospin and baryon densities) using the Gibbs' criteria for phase equilibrium. In order to obtain these conditions we use two different models for the two possible phases, namely the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) for the hadron matter (also including hyperons) and the MIT bag model for the quark phase. It is shown that the phase transition is very sensitive to the density dependence of the equation of state and the symmetry energy. For isospin asymmetry of 0.2 and a mixed phase with a fraction of 20% of quarks, a transition density in the interval 2 rho_0 to 4 rho_0 was obtained for temperatures 30 < T < 65 MeV.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter is highly sensitive to both the density dependence of the equation of state and the symmetry energy, with the inclusion of boson condensation modifying the isospin of the hadron phase and affecting the transition density, particularly at lower temperatures where the nuclear symmetry energy plays a dominant role.

## Method

The authors used the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) with seven different parametrizations spanning hard and soft equations of state with varying symmetry energy slopes to describe the hadron phase, including nucleons, hyperons, pions, and kaons. The quark phase was described using the MIT bag model with various bag constant values and gluons, and the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium were applied to a system with two conserved charges (baryon and isospin densities) to construct the binodal surface and mixed phase at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- For isospin asymmetry of 0.2 and a mixed phase with 20% quark fraction, the transition density lies in the interval 2ρ₀ < ρ_t < 4ρ₀ for temperatures 30 < T < 65 MeV
- The bag constant values used were B^(1/4) = 145, 160, 190, and 210 MeV, with B^(1/4) = 160 MeV chosen for intermediate energy analysis
- The strong coupling constant was taken as α_s = 0.349 for the quark phase calculations
- The symmetry energy slope values of the five parametrizations considered in prior work ranged from 85 < L < 103 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Muller and Serot (1995), Di Toro et al. (2006), and Di Toro et al. (2010) that investigated the hadron-quark phase transition with different parametrizations and ingredients, but without the systematic variation of both isoscalar and isovector interactions or the inclusion of both pions and kaons. The results are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at intermediate energies planned at facilities such as NICA at JINR/Dubna and FAIR at GSI/Darmstadt, and provide qualitative orientation on features arising when two conserved charges are considered at finite baryon density and isospin.
