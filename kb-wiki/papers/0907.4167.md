# Ab-initio computation of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes

> We compute the binding energy of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes and employ the coupled-cluster method and chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with two different cutoffs. We obtain rather well-converged results in model spaces consisting of up to 21 oscillator shells. For interactions with a momentum cutoff of 500 MeV, we find that 28O is stable with respect to 24O, while calculations with a momentum cutoff of 600 MeV result in a slightly unbound 28O. The theoretical error estimates due to the omission of the three-nucleon forces and the truncation of excitations beyond three-particle-three-hole clusters indicate that the stability of 28O cannot be ruled out from ab-initio calculations, and that three-nucleon forces and continuum effects play the dominant role in deciding this question.

## Key claim
The stability of the neutron-rich oxygen isotope 28O with respect to 24O cannot be ruled out from ab-initio calculations; the outcome depends critically on the cutoff of the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction, with a 500 MeV cutoff yielding a bound 28O and a 600 MeV cutoff yielding a slightly unbound 28O, indicating that three-nucleon forces and continuum effects are the dominant factors.

## Method
The authors employed the coupled-cluster method with single and double excitations plus a perturbative triples correction (ΛCCSD(T)) to compute ground-state energies of 16O, 22O, 24O, and 28O. They used chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) with two different high-momentum cutoffs (500 MeV and 600 MeV) in model spaces of up to 21 harmonic-oscillator shells. The cutoff dependence of the results was used to quantify uncertainties from omitted three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers
- For the chiral interaction with cutoff Λχ=500 MeV, 28O is bound by about 2.7 MeV with respect to 24O.
- For the chiral interaction with cutoff Λχ=600 MeV, 28O is unbound by about -1.3 MeV with respect to 24O.
- The total binding energy of 28O is -157.01 MeV (Λχ=500 MeV) and -136.71 MeV (Λχ=600 MeV), compared to -154.34 MeV and -138.04 MeV for 24O, respectively.
- The triples correction ΔE3 accounts for 10% of the CCSD correlation energy at Λχ=500 MeV and 13% at Λχ=600 MeV.
- The root-mean-square point matter radius for 28O is 2.825 fm (Λχ=500 MeV), compared to an experimental value of 3.19(13) fm for 24O.
- The uncertainty from truncation of cluster amplitudes is estimated at approximately 5 MeV, and the uncertainty from omitted three-nucleon forces is estimated at 10-20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations from light nuclei (Pieper 2001, Navratil 2000, 2007) and medium-mass isotopes (Hagen 2008) to neutron-rich oxygen isotopes near the drip line. It contradicts phenomenological shell-model predictions that 28O is unbound by about 1 MeV (Utsuno 2002) and that 26O is unstable (Volya 2006), while also showing that earlier shell-model results for a stable 28O (Poves 1994) cannot be ruled out. The results enable future studies that include explicit three-nucleon forces and continuum coupling to resolve the stability of 28O.
