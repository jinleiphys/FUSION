# The dynamics of strongly correlated gluons at high energies

> We describe some of the recent progress in our understanding of the dynamics of strongly correlated gluons at high parton densities. Computations in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory provide a good description of inclusive and semi-inclusive final states in DIS, p+p and p+A collisions at small x. In nucleus-nucleus collisions, they provide an ab initio description of entropy generation, decoherence, isotropization and the onset of hydrodynamic flow. The successful description of a wide range of phenomena from RHIC to LHC is outlined and possibilities for more stringent tests noted.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory provides a good description of inclusive and semi-inclusive final states in DIS, p+p, and p+A collisions at small x, and offers an ab initio description of entropy generation, decoherence, isotropization, and the onset of hydrodynamic flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions, though the precise mechanism of thermalization in a longitudinally expanding gauge theory remains unresolved.

## Method

The paper reviews computations within the CGC effective field theory, where dynamical gluon fields at small x couple coherently to static classical color charges at large x, with energy evolution governed by the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the collision is described as a collision of classical gluon field shock waves (the Glasma), with small quantum fluctuations around these fields growing nearly exponentially and causing isotropization on time scales not significantly greater than 1/Q_s. The IP-Glasma model incorporates event-by-event fluctuations from nucleon positions, color charge distributions constrained by IP-Sat fits to HERA data, and gluon multiplicity fluctuations, solved via 2+1-D Yang-Mills evolution.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_S is dynamically generated and satisfies Q_S >> Λ_QCD, with α_S(Q_S) << 1 in Regge-Gribov asymptotics.
- Phase space occupancy in the saturation regime approaches the maximal parametric value of 1/α_S.
- The enhancement of Glasma graphs in high multiplicity p+p events is proportional to α_S^{-8}, corresponding to a factor of 10^4–10^5 for a reasonable range in the coupling.
- The non-perturbative parameter ζ in the negative binomial distribution from the Glasma flux tube framework is determined numerically to be ~0.3–1.5 from Yang-Mills computations, and ~0.2 from event-by-event solutions for smooth distributions at large Q_S^2 S_⊥.
- Gluon multiplicity (dN_g/dy)/(N_part/2) at τ=0.4 fm/c times 2/3 is compared to experimental charged particle data for √s=200 GeV Au+Au and √s=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions as a function of N_part.

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the Balitsky-JIMWLK hierarchy (Balitsky 1995, Jalilian-Marian et al. 1997-1998), building on the BK equation (Balitsky 1995, Kovchegov 1999) and its running coupling extension rcBK (Albacete 2007). It contrasts with the MC-KLN model, which does not include color charge fluctuations on scales of 1/Q_S, and shows that the IP-Glasma model produces larger odd harmonic eccentricities ε_3 and ε_5 and smaller ε_2 than MC-KLN, making v_2/v_3 ratios more compatible with experimental observations. The results enable more stringent tests of saturation dynamics at the LHC and motivate high luminosity studies of exclusive processes at a future Electron Ion Collider to connect saturation physics to chiral symmetry breaking and confinement.
