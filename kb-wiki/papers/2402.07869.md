# Jet Suppression and Azimuthal Anisotropy from RHIC to LHC

> Azimuthal anisotropies of high-$p_T$ particles produced in heavy-ion collisions are understood as an effect of a geometrical selection bias. Particles oriented in the direction in which the QCD medium formed in these collisions is shorter, suffer less energy loss, and thus, are over-represented in the final ensemble compared to those oriented in the direction in which the medium is longer. In this work we present the first semi-analytical predictions, including propagation through a realistic, hydrodynamical background, of the azimuthal anisotropies for jets, obtaining a quantitative agreement with available experimental data as function of the jet $p_T$, its cone size $R$ and the collisions centrality. Jets are multi-partonic, extended objects and their energy loss is sensitive to substructure fluctuations. This is determined by the physics of color coherence that relates to the ability of the medium to resolve those partonic fluctuations. Namely, color dipoles whose angle is smaller than a critical angle, $θ_c$, are not resolved by the medium and they effectively act as a coherent source of energy loss. We find that jet azimuthal anisotropies have a specially strong dependence on coherence physics due to the marked length-dependence of $θ_c$. By combining our predictions for the collision systems and center of mass energies studied at RHIC and the LHC, covering a wide range of typical values of $θ_c$, we show that the relative size of jet azimuthal anisotropies for jets with different cone-sizes $R$ follow a universal trend that indicates a transition from a coherent regime of jet quenching to a decoherent regime. These results suggest a way forward to reveal the role played by the physics of jet color decoherence in probing deconfined QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first semi-analytical predictions of jet azimuthal anisotropy (elliptic flow, v2) that include propagation through a realistic hydrodynamical background, achieving quantitative agreement with experimental data; the key hedge is that this agreement depends on the physics of color coherence, specifically the marked length-dependence of the critical angle θc, which governs whether jet substructure fluctuations are resolved by the medium and thus determines the transition from a coherent to a decoherent regime of jet quenching.

## Method

The authors compute jet cross sections in proton-proton collisions using leading-order DGLAP evolution with a running coupling to resum logarithms of the cone angle R, then embed these into a heavy-ion environment by convolving with nuclear parton distribution functions (EPS09) and applying a quenching factor derived from the Laplace transform of the energy loss probability distribution. The medium-induced energy loss is computed within the improved opacity expansion (IOE) scheme, which expands around the harmonic oscillator and incorporates both BDMPS-Z multiple soft scattering and GLV single hard scattering limits, while the angular evolution of the quenching factor is governed by a collimator resummation equation that includes a resolved phase-space constraint requiring the formation time to be less than the decoherence time and the decoherence time to be less than the medium length L.

## Key numbers

- The critical angle for color decoherence is defined as θc ~ 1/√(q̂ L³), where q̂ is the jet transport coefficient and L is the medium length.
- The decoherence time is tdecoh ~ (q̂ θ²)^(-1/3), and the formation time is tform = 2/(ω θ²).
- The running coupling αs is regularized as αs(kt) = min[1, 2π/(β₀ log(kt/Q₀))] with β₀ = 23/3 and Q₀ = 0.09 GeV.
- The initial jet spectra at R₀ = 1 are parameterized as dσ̂(k)/dpT = σ₀(k) (p(k)T,0/pT)^(n(k)(pT)) with n(k)(pT) = Σᵢ cᵢ(k) logⁱ(p(k)T,0/pT) for i = 0 to 5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical description of jet yield suppression as a function of jet size R (Mehtar-Tani, 2021) to include jet azimuthal anisotropy at both RHIC and LHC energies, and to study the R-dependence of jet v2 across centrality selections. The framework builds on the antenna setup calculations that revealed the decoherence time and critical angle (Mehtar-Tani, 2010; Casalderrey-Solana, 2011; Mehtar-Tani, 2011; Mehtar-Tani, 2012), and on the observation that color decoherence explains why single inclusive hadron RAA is larger than jet RAA (Mehtar-Tani, 2017). The results suggest that the ratio θc/R provides a universal scaling variable for jet v2 across different collision systems and energies, enabling experimental tests of the transition between coherent and decoherent regimes of jet quenching.
