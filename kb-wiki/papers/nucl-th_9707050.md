# Constraining the $(γ, π)$ amplitudes for E2 $N \to Δ$

> We reply to the Comment of A.M. Sandorfi et al. [nucl-th/9707028] on the E2/M1 ratio from the Mainz photoproduction data, nucl-th/9707028 v2. We support our published result for the E2/M1 ratio of REM = -(2.5 +- 0.2 +- 0.2)%.

# Constraining the $(γ, π)$ amplitudes for E2 $N \to Δ$

## Key claim

The authors defend their published value of the E2/M1 ratio for the $N \to Δ$ transition, $R_{EM} = -(2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.2)\%$, against the criticism of Sandorfi et al. (1997) by arguing that contributions from higher partial waves (d‑waves and above) are negligible at the top of the Δ(1232) resonance, and that no ambiguities arise from neglecting them in their analysis of the $\vec{\gamma}p \to p\pi^0$ data.

## Method

The authors re‑examine the effect of including d‑waves ($l_\pi \ge 2$) in the angular expansion of the differential cross section for $\vec{\gamma}p \to p\pi^0$, showing that the additional coefficients D and E and the modifications to A, B, C vanish or are negligible at the resonance energy ($E_\gamma = 340$ MeV) because the real part of $(M_{1+} - M_{1-})$ goes through zero and the imaginary part of $E_{2-}$ is small. They also analyze the linear polarization cross‑section difference $d\sigma_\bot - d\sigma_{||}$, which becomes independent of pion angle when only s‑ and p‑waves contribute, and find no indication of non‑Born d‑wave contributions in their data around the resonance.

## Key numbers

- $R_{EM} = ImE^{3/2}_{1^+} / ImM^{3/2}_{1^+} = -(2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.2)\%$ (statistical and systematic uncertainties) for the $N \to Δ$ transition extracted from $\vec{\gamma}p \to p\pi^0$ at the Mainz Microtron MAMI.
- At the resonance energy $E_\gamma = 340$ MeV, contributions from higher partial waves ($l_\pi \ge 2$) to the differential cross section at $\theta_\pi = 90^\circ$ are far below $1\%$.
- Below and above the resonance (energy region 200–500 MeV), contributions from $l_\pi \ge 2$ are of the order of $10–20\%$ of the differential cross section at $\theta_\pi = 0^\circ$ and $180^\circ$.

## Context

This reply addresses a Comment by Sandorfi et al. (1997) on the E2/M1 ratio from Mainz photoproduction data. The authors argue that the LEGS analysis criticized by Sandorfi et al. suffers from the classical multipole‑analysis problem of handling systematic errors from different experiments, and they cite the earlier multipole analysis of the Kharkov data (Grushin 1981) which demonstrated that no non‑Born higher‑partial‑wave contributions are needed in polarization observables ($\Sigma$, $T$, $P$). The work supports the reliability of the Mainz result and clarifies that the extracted $R_{EM}$ value is not affected by higher‑partial‑wave ambiguities, enabling continued use of this precise ratio in studies of the Δ(1232) resonance structure.
