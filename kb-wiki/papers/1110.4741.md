# Photon-photon production of lepton, quark and meson pairs in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> We review our recent results on exclusive production of $μ^+ μ^-$, heavy quark-antiquark, and meson-antimeson pairs in ultraperipheral, ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the authors' recent calculations showing that exclusive production of lepton, quark, and meson pairs in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is enhanced by a factor proportional to $Z_1^2 Z_2^2$ from naive expectations, but that inclusion of realistic nuclear charge distributions and form factors significantly reduces the cross sections compared to simpler monopole form factor approximations, especially at high transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors use the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) to calculate cross sections for $AA \to AA c_1 c_2$ reactions, where the photon fluxes from each nucleus are convolved with elementary $\gamma\gamma \to c_1 c_2$ subprocess cross sections. For heavy quark pairs ($c\bar{c}$, $b\bar{b}$), they include Born direct contributions, leading-order QCD corrections, four-quark contributions via a color dipole model, and single-resolved gluon contributions. For meson pairs ($\pi^+\pi^-$, $\pi^0\pi^0$, $\rho^0\rho^0$), they apply the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD formalism with Wu-Huang distribution amplitudes and the hand-bag model, using realistic nuclear charge form factors from the two-parameter Fermi model.

## Key numbers

- For $c\bar{c}$ production at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV, the total cross section is 2.47 mb, with contributions: Born 42.5%, QCD corrections 14.6%, four-quark 27.1%, single-resolved 15.8%
- For $b\bar{b}$ production at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV, the total cross section is 10.83 $\mu$b, with contributions: Born 18.9%, QCD corrections 7.7%, four-quark 64.5%, single-resolved 8.9%
- At RHIC with STAR conditions ($y_3, y_4 \in (-1,1)$, $p_{3t}, p_{4t} \geq 1$ GeV, $W_{NN}=200$ GeV), the damping factor from realistic form factors relative to monopole form factor reaches a factor of 100 at $p_t = 10$ GeV for $\mu^+\mu^-$ production
- Heavy quark masses used: $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, $m_b = 4.75$ GeV
- Nuclear cross sections for $\pi^+\pi^-$ production show enhancement of order $10^4$ over elementary cross sections, less than the naive $Z_1^2 Z_2^2$ expectation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on exclusive $\mu^+\mu^-$ production (KS_mumu, 2011), heavy quark pair production (KSMS_qqbar, 2011), and meson pair production (KSS_rhorho, KS_pipi, LS_DDbar) in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. It contrasts with earlier calculations that used monopole form factors (HTB94) by demonstrating the importance of realistic charge distributions from electron scattering experiments (Barrett and Jackson). The results enable experimental tests at RHIC and LHC, particularly for disentangling different production mechanisms through invariant mass distributions of heavy quark pairs.
