# The pole and screening masses of neutral pion in hot and magnetized medium: a comprehensive study in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> In this work, we investigate not only the pole masses but also the screening masses of neutral pions at finite temperature and magnetic field by utilizing the random phase approximation (RPA) approach in the framework of the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. And two equivalent formalisms in the presence of a magnetic field, i.e. the Landau level representation (LLR) and the proper-time representation (PTR), are applied to obtain the corresponding analytical expressions of the polarization functions (except the expressions for the pole masses in the PTR). In order to evaluate the applicable region of the low-momentum expansion (LME), we compare the numerical results within the full RPA (FRPA) with those within the reduced RPA (RRPA), i.e. the RPA in the LME. It is confirmed that the pole masses of π0in the FRPA suffer a sudden mass jump at the Mott transition temperature when in the presence of external magnetic field, and the Mott transition temperature is catalyzed by the magnetic field. And by analyzing the behaviors of the directional sound velocities of π0, which are associated with the breaking of the Lorentz invariance by the heat bath and the magnetic field, we clarify the two problems existing in previous literatures: one is that the transverse sound velocities in the medium are always larger than unity and thus violate the law of causality on account of the non-covariant regularization scheme, the other is that the longitudinal sound velocities are identically equal unity at finite temperature on account of the limitation of the derivative expansion method used.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that neutral pion pole masses in the full random phase approximation (FRPA) exhibit a sudden mass jump at the Mott transition temperature in the presence of an external magnetic field, and that the Mott transition temperature is catalyzed by the magnetic field; it also clarifies that previous works obtained erroneous transverse sound velocities exceeding unity (violating causality) due to non-covariant regularization, and erroneous longitudinal sound velocities identically equal to unity at finite temperature due to limitations of the derivative expansion method.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the random phase approximation (RPA) to compute both pole masses and screening masses of neutral pions at finite temperature and magnetic field. They employed two equivalent formalisms for the magnetic field, the Landau level representation (LLR) and the proper-time representation (PTR), to derive analytical expressions for the polarization functions, and applied the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme to preserve Lorentz invariance. They compared results from the full RPA (FRPA) with those from the reduced RPA (RRPA), which uses the low-momentum expansion (LME), to evaluate the applicable region of the LME.

## Key numbers

- The Pauli-Villars regularization parameters were fixed using the decay constant \(f_{\pi}=93\) MeV, the chiral condensate \(\left<\bar{u}u\right>=(-250\, \text{MeV})^3\), and the pion mass \(m_{\pi}=135\) MeV, with \(G\Lambda^2=2.87\), \(\Lambda=851\) MeV, and \(m_0=5.2\) MeV.
- The gap equation for the constituent quark mass \(m\) was solved using the Pauli-Villars scheme with coefficients \((C_0,C_1,C_2)=(1,1,-2)\) and parameters \((a_0,a_1,a_2)=(0,2,1)\).
- The transverse and longitudinal screening masses are defined as \(m_{\pi_0,scr,\perp}=m_{\pi_0,pole}/u_{\perp}\) and \(m_{\pi_0,scr,\parallel}=m_{\pi_0,pole}/u_{\parallel}\), where \(u_{\perp}\) and \(u_{\parallel}\) are the directional sound velocities.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutral pion masses in hot and magnetized medium, particularly those by Fayazbakhsh et al. (2012, 2013) and Wang et al. (2017), which used the derivative expansion method (equivalent to the reduced RPA) in the NJL model. The present paper provides the first complete analytical derivations and numerical calculations for both pole and screening masses within the full RPA under magnetic field, correcting earlier errors where transverse sound velocities exceeded unity (due to non-covariant regularization) and longitudinal sound velocities were incorrectly set to unity at finite temperature (due to the derivative expansion method's limitations). The results enable more reliable predictions for pion properties in strongly magnetized environments relevant to heavy ion collisions and compact stars.
