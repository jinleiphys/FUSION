# Pion Induced Reactions for the Study of Charmed Baryons

> In this talk, we report the study of $π^- p \to K^{*0} Λ$ and $π^- p \to D^{*-} Λ_c^+$ reactions by using an effective Lagrangian method and a hybrid Regge model. The total and differential cross sections for the $K^{*0} Λ$ production are first calculated and then those for the $D^{*-} Λ_c^+$ one are estimated. The two models yield different results, each exhibiting specific features. Our prediction for the charm production provides valuable information about the upcoming J-PARC experiment.

# Pion Induced Reactions for the Study of Charmed Baryons

## Key claim

The cross sections for the charm production reaction π⁻ p → D*⁻ Λc⁺ are predicted to be approximately 10⁴ times smaller than those for the analogous strangeness production π⁻ p → K*⁰ Λ, with the two theoretical models (effective Lagrangian method and hybrid Regge model) yielding different results that each exhibit specific features. The prediction for charm production provides valuable information for the upcoming J-PARC experiment, but the authors note that the only previous experimental search at BNL found only an upper limit of 7 nb at 13 GeV pion-beam energy with no signals for charmed baryons.

## Method

The authors compute total and differential cross sections for π⁻ p → K*⁰ Λ and π⁻ p → D*⁻ Λc⁺ using two approaches: an effective Lagrangian method with tree-level Feynman diagrams including K, K*, Λ, and Σ exchanges, and a hybrid Regge model where Feynman propagators are replaced by Regge propagators with trajectories from the quark-gluon string model (QGSM). Coupling constants are determined from experimental hadron decay data, Nijmegen-potential, or SU(3) flavor symmetry, and form factors with cutoff masses are introduced to account for hadron internal structure.

## Key numbers

- For the effective Lagrangian method, the cross sections for charm production are approximately 10⁴ times smaller than those for strangeness production.
- For the hybrid Regge model, the cross sections for charm production are about 10⁴ to 10⁶ times smaller than those for strangeness production, depending on the energy range.
- The predicted production cross section for the D*⁻ Λc⁺ reaction at s/s_th ~ 2.1 (the expected maximum energy at J-PARC) is around 2 nb.
- The only previous experimental upper limit for π⁻ p → D*⁻ B_c at BNL was 7 nb at 13 GeV pion-beam energy (95% confidence level).
- Cutoff masses used in the effective Lagrangian method: Λ_K(D), K*(D*) = 0.55 GeV, Λ_N, Σ(Σ_c) = 0.60 GeV.
- Cutoff masses used in the hybrid Regge model: Λ = 1 GeV in common, with parameters a_K(D) = 0.6, a_K*(D*) = 0.8, a_Σ(Σ_c) = 1.5.

## Context

This work extends the earlier simple Regge model study by Kim (2014) by employing both an effective Lagrangian method and a hybrid Regge model, and by including contributions from K (D) and Λ (Λ_c) reggeons in addition to K* (D*) reggeon exchange. The only previous experimental search for this charm production process was conducted at BNL by Christenson (1985), which found no charmed baryon signals and set an upper limit of 7 nb. The predictions are intended to guide the upcoming J-PARC experiment at a high-momentum beam line of up to 20 GeV/c, where the expected maximum energy corresponds to s/s_th ~ 2.1.
