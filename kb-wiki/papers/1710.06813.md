# TDHF investigations of the U+U quasifission process

> The use of actinide collisions have been suggested as a way to produce neutron rich isotopes of high Z nuclei. The collision dynamics of these reactions can be studied using unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations. Here, we report on the recent studies of quasifission for the $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U system.

# TDHF investigations of the U+U quasifission process

## Key claim

The TDHF calculations for the 238U+238U system show that at lower energies in the range of current experimental studies, high-Z and high-A fragments are not produced; instead, neutron-rich lighter elements may be formed. At higher energies, high-Z and high-A primary fragments are observed, but their large excitation energies (hundreds of MeV) make their survival probabilities very low, challenging the original suggestion that actinide-actinide collisions could produce neutron-rich isotopes of high-Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a 3D Cartesian grid without spatial symmetries, using the Skyrme SLy4 energy density functional with BCS pairing for neutrons in 238U. They simulated central collisions of 238U+238U at various center-of-mass energies for two initial orientations of the deformed nuclei (tip-tip and tip-side), and also examined impact parameter dependence at one energy, extracting contact times, fragment masses, charges, and excitation energies from the time evolution of the mass density.

## Key numbers

- Maximum contact time for tip-tip collisions at E_cm = 1237 MeV is approximately 4 zs (zeptoseconds), with a peak in contact time observed in the energy interval E_cm = 1230-1243 MeV
- For tip-side collisions at E_cm = 1350 MeV, the heavy fragment in the exit channel has average charge Z = 124 and mass A = 325
- For tip-tip collisions at E_cm = 875 MeV, ternary quasifission produces a small central fragment with charge Z = 7 and mass A = 18
- For tip-tip collisions at E_cm = 1237 MeV, the heavy fragment has charge Z = 134 and mass A = 353
- For tip-tip collisions at E_cm = 1300 MeV, ternary quasifission produces a middle fragment with charge Z = 103 and mass A = 274
- For tip-side collisions at E_cm = 875 MeV, the heavy fragment stays in the range Z = 98-101 and A = 252-260
- A single TDHF run at fixed E_cm energy and zero impact parameter takes about 2 weeks of CPU time on a 16-processor LINUX workstation; total CPU time was about 6 months
- The lattice used was 70x40x40 grid points with initial separation R = 34 fm; one test run used 90x60x60 grid points with R = 40 fm
- Time step for propagation was Delta t = 0.4 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of quasifission dynamics (Wakhle 2014, Oberacker 2014, Hammerton 2015) and specifically follows up on early TDHF calculations for the 238U+238U system that used a plane of symmetry (Golabek 2009), which found maximum contact times of about 4 zs in agreement with quantum molecular dynamics simulations (Tian 2008). The study also relates to the dinuclear system model (Feng 2009), relativistic mean-field and Skyrme HF studies (Gupta 2007), and improved quantum molecular dynamics calculations (Tian 2008, Zhao 2016). The results indicate that while actinide-actinide collisions may serve as a means to produce neutron-rich isotopes, the large excitation energies of high-Z fragments challenge their survival, motivating future work with beyond-mean-field techniques and coupling TDHF with statistical decay codes to investigate cold fragment distributions.
