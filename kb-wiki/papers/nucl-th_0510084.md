# Correlations in the Parton Recombination Model

> We describe how parton recombination can address the recent measurement of dynamical jet-like two particle correlations. In addition we discuss the possible effect realistic light-cone wave-functions including higher Fock-states may have on the well-known elliptic flow valence-quark number scaling law.

## Key claim

Parton recombination can explain the observed jet-like two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions, provided that parton-parton correlations of order ≈10% exist in the quark-gluon plasma prior to hadronization. The well-known valence-quark number scaling law for elliptic flow is modified by higher Fock states in realistic light-cone wave functions, introducing deviations on the order of ~10% that may be visible in scaling analyses.

## Method

The authors model two-particle correlations by assuming that correlations among partons in a quark-gluon plasma translate into correlations among hadrons formed by recombination, with an amplification factor Q = n_A n_B (where n is the number of valence quarks). They also extend the recombination formalism to include higher Fock states (e.g., |q\bar{q}g⟩ and |q\bar{q}q\bar{q}⟩) in the light-cone wave function of hadrons, deriving a generalized scaling law for elliptic flow v_2^(H)(P) ≈ ∑_ν |c_ν|^2 n_ν v_2(P/n_ν) that accounts for the probability |c_ν|^2 of each Fock state.

## Key numbers

- Two-parton correlations of order ≈10% are sufficient to explain hadron correlations as measured by the PHENIX collaboration.
- The ratio of protons over positively charged pions is equal to or above one for P_T > 1.5 GeV/c and is approximately constant up to 4 GeV/c.
- The nuclear suppression factor R_AA below 4 GeV/c is close to one for baryons, while it is about 0.3 for mesons.
- The relative difference between scaled meson and baryon elliptic flow is shown for three sizes of the higher Fock state component: 0%, 30%, and 50%.
- Deviations from the valence-quark scaling law are on the order of ~10%.

## Context

This work extends the parton recombination model developed by Fries (2003), Greco (2003), and Hwa (2002), which successfully described baryon enhancement and elliptic flow scaling at RHIC. It addresses the challenge posed by jet-like correlations measured by STAR (2003) and PHENIX (2004), which appeared incompatible with uncorrelated thermal recombination. The results enable future studies of parton correlations in the quark-gluon plasma and refine the interpretation of elliptic flow data, suggesting that while quark degrees of freedom are confirmed, the connection to measured flow may be less straightforward than previously thought.
