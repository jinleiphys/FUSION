# Variance of the decay intensity of superdeformed bands

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic formulae for the energy average and variance of the intraband decay intensity of a superdeformed band, but these formulae are strictly valid only in the strongly overlapping resonance region where the ratio of the electromagnetic decay width of normally deformed states to their mean level spacing satisfies Γ_N/d ≫ 1, though they work well at Γ_N/d = 1 and provide qualitative description even at Γ_N/d = 0.1.

## Method

The authors model the decay-out of a superdeformed band as an isolated doorway resonance coupled to normally deformed states, writing the intraband decay amplitude as a sum of a background contribution and a fluctuation contribution. They compute the energy average of the background and fluctuation contributions to the decay intensity using the calculus of residues, and derive the variance of the total intraband decay intensity in terms of three dimensionless variables: Γ^↓/Γ_S (spreading width over SD decay width), Γ_N/d (ND decay width over ND level spacing), and Γ_N/(Γ_S + Γ^↓) (the correlation length).

## Key numbers

- The average background contribution to the intraband decay intensity is I_ina^av = 1/(1 + Γ^↓/Γ_S), where Γ^↓ is the spreading width for mixing of an SD state with ND states and Γ_S is the electromagnetic decay width of the SD state.
- The average fluctuation contribution to the intraband decay intensity is ⟨I_ina^fl⟩ = 2(π Γ_N/d)^(-1) (Γ^↓/Γ_S)^2 / (1 + Γ^↓/Γ_S)^3, where Γ_N is the electromagnetic decay width of ND states and d is their mean level spacing.
- The variance of the decay intensity ⟨(ΔI_ina)^2⟩ depends on the correlation length ξ = (Γ_S + Γ^↓)/Γ_N through the functions f_1(ξ) = 1/(1+ξ) + ξ/(1+ξ)^2 + ξ^2/[2(1+ξ)^3] and f_2(ξ) = 1/[2(1+ξ)].

## Context

This work extends the model of Gu and Weidenmuller (1999) by providing analytic formulae for the variance of the intraband decay intensity, whereas Gu and Weidenmuller had provided a fit formula for the average fluctuation contribution. The results are derived using the doorway resonance approach of Sargeant et al. (2002) and are reminiscent of Ericson's (1963) expression for the variance of the cross section in compound nucleus scattering. The variance provides a second equation beyond that for the average intensity, enabling unambiguous determination of both the spreading width Γ^↓ and the mean level spacing d from experimental data, which can probe the spin dependence of the barrier separating the SD and ND wells.
