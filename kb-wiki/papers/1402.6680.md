# A method for studying the rapidity fluctuation and decorrelation of harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> An "event-shape twist" technique is proposed to study the longitudinal dynamics of harmonic flow, in particular the effects of rapidity fluctuation and event-plane decorrelation. This technique can distinguish between two types of rapidity decorrelation effects: a systematic rotation versus a random fluctuation of flow angles along the rapidity direction. The technique is demonstrated and the magnitude of the two decorrelation effects is predicted using the AMPT model via a single particle analysis and two-particle correlation analysis. An observed decorrelation can be attributed to a systematic rotation of event-plane angle along the pseudorapidity, consistent with a collective response to an initial state twist of the fireball proposed by Bozek {\it et.al.}. This rotation is also observed for several higher-order harmonics with the same sign and similar magnitudes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an "event-shape twist" technique that can distinguish between two types of rapidity decorrelation effects in harmonic flow: a systematic rotation of the event-plane angle along pseudorapidity versus a random fluctuation of flow angles between different rapidity regions. The technique is demonstrated using the AMPT model, and the observed decorrelation is attributed to a systematic rotation consistent with a collective response to an initial state twist of the fireball, as proposed by Bozek et al. (2010).

## Method

The method selects events based on the angle difference between the m-th-order event planes in forward and backward pseudorapidity regions (the "twist" angle). Events are divided into bins of this twist angle and of the flow vector magnitude, then the n-th-order flow angle is calculated as a function of pseudorapidity near mid-rapidity using either single-particle distributions or two-particle correlations. The rotation angle is extracted from the sine and cosine components of the azimuthal distribution relative to a reference event-plane angle, using the relation tan(nΔΦ_n^rot) = v_n^s,raw / v_n^c,raw, which requires no event-plane resolution correction.

## Key numbers

- AMPT model simulation for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm (approximately 30% centrality)
- Each subevent contains 1000-2000 particles
- Events are divided into 10 bins in q_m^S with equal statistics, then further divided into 10 bins based on the twist angle Ψ_m^cut; only the top 10% of events with largest Ψ_m^cut in each q_m^S bin are used
- The rotation angle nΔΦ_n^rot varies linearly with η and crosses zero at η = 0 over the region -3 < η < 3
- For event-shape twist based on elliptic flow (m=2), significant rotations are observed for n=4 and 5 with magnitude similar to that for n=2
- For event-shape twist based on triangular flow (m=3), rotation for n=5 is coupled to n=3
- The slope d(v_2^s,raw)/dη saturates past the fourth q_2^S bin

## Context

This work extends studies of event-plane correlations by Jia (2012) and Aad (2014) and event-shape selection techniques by Schukraft (2012) and Huo (2013). The results are qualitatively consistent with the hydrodynamic response to an initial state fireball twisted along rapidity, as proposed by Bozek et al. (2010). The technique enables experimental separation of systematic rotation from random fluctuation contributions to longitudinal flow decorrelation, and can be applied to study these effects in p+A collisions, peripheral A+A collisions, and lower collision energies at RHIC.
