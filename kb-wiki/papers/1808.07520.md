# Mean field approach to reconstructed neutrino energy distributions in accelerator-based experiments

> The reconstruction of the neutrino energy is crucial in oscillation experiments that use interactions with nuclei to detect the neutrino. The common reconstruction procedure is based on the kinematics of the final-state lepton. The interpretation of the reconstructed energy in terms of the real neutrino energy must rely on a model for the neutrino-nucleus interaction. The Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model is frequently used in these analyses. In the Hartree-Fock (HF) model for quasielastic nucleon knockout, the bound nucleon wave functions are obtained using an effective nucleon-nucleon force. The final-state wave function is constructed from continuum states in the same potential which have the correct asymptotic behavior. The Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) model extends the HF approach taking long range correlations into account in a self-consistent way. Considering only single-nucleon processes, the distributions of reconstructed neutrino energies obtained within the HF-CRPA approach are compared with the results of the RFG, an RPWIA calculation, and the RPA+np-nh model of Martini et al. We find that the distributions of reconstructed energies for a fixed incoming energy in the HF-CRPA display additional strength in the low reconstructed energy tails compared to models without elastic distortion of the outgoing nucleon. This asymmetry redistributes strength from higher to lower values of the reconstructed energy. The mean field description of the nuclear dynamics results in a reshaping of the reconstructed energy distribution that cannot be accounted for in a plane wave impulse approximation model, even by modifying ad hoc parameters such as the binding energy. In particular it is shown that in the RFG calculations there is no value of the binding energy which is able to reproduce the entire T2K $ν_μ$ oscillated spectrum as calculated in HF-CRPA.

## Key claim

The mean-field description of nuclear dynamics (HF-CRPA) produces a reshaping of reconstructed neutrino energy distributions that cannot be reproduced by the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, even when the RFG binding energy parameter is freely adjusted; in particular, no value of the binding energy in RFG calculations can reproduce the entire T2K oscillated muon neutrino spectrum as calculated in HF-CRPA.

## Method

The authors computed the double-differential cross section for quasielastic single-nucleon knockout using the Hartree-Fock Continuum Random Phase Approximation (HF-CRPA) model, where bound nucleon wave functions are obtained from a self-consistent HF calculation with an effective Skyrme nucleon-nucleon force (SkE2) and the outgoing nucleon is described as a continuum state in the same potential. They then constructed the single-differential cross section d(E_nu, E_rec) by transforming the cross section to variables of true neutrino energy and reconstructed energy, integrating over lepton energy for fixed reconstructed energy. The HF-CRPA results were compared to the RFG model, a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) calculation, and the RPA+np-nh model of Martini et al.

## Key numbers

- The RFG model used a Fermi momentum of k_F = 221 MeV and an energy shift E_s = 34 MeV, matching the binding energy E_B = 34 MeV used in the MiniBooNE reconstruction formula.
- The reconstructed energy distribution N(E_rec) for the MiniBooNE flux shows that HF-CRPA models are skewed towards lower reconstructed energies, while RFG and RPWIA models shift the peak to the right and enhance the high-E_rec tail.
- For a fixed neutrino energy of E_nu = 0.7 GeV, the peak of d(E_nu, E_rec) in HF-CRPA is displaced to larger reconstructed energies compared to RFG and RPWIA, where the peak is centered around E_rec.
- At E_nu = 1.0 GeV, the CRPA distribution shows a quenched peak and high-E_rec tail in favor of the low-E_rec tail, similar to the effect of multinucleon knockout in the RPA+np-nh model.
- The T2K far detector is located 295 km from the J-PARC accelerator.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of reconstructed neutrino energy distributions beyond the RFG model commonly used in oscillation experiments, building on prior studies by Martini et al. (2009, 2010, 2012) who demonstrated the importance of multinucleon excitations, and by Ankowski et al. who analyzed the reconstruction problem in a spectral function formalism with effective nucleon distortion. The results show that elastic final-state interactions in the mean-field approach produce low-E_rec tails analogous to those from multinucleon knockout, implying that single-nucleon knockout alone, when treated with realistic nuclear dynamics, can mimic some features previously attributed only to 2p-2h processes. This enables improved modeling of the CC0pi signal in experiments like T2K and MiniBooNE, though the authors note that a full prediction requires also assessing two-nucleon knockout, resonance contributions, and other reaction mechanisms.
