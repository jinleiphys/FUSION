# Nuclear Physics Meets the Sources of the Ultra-High Energy Cosmic Rays

> The determination of the injection composition of cosmic ray nuclei within astrophysical sources requires sufficiently accurate descriptions of the source physics and the propagation - apart from controlling astrophysical uncertainties. We therefore study the implications of nuclear data and models for cosmic ray astrophysics, which involves the photo-disintegration of nuclei up to iron in astrophysical environments. We demonstrate that the impact of nuclear model uncertainties is potentially larger in environments with non-thermal radiation fields than in the cosmic microwave background. We also study the impact of nuclear models on the nuclear cascade in a gamma-ray burst radiation field, simulated at a level of complexity comparable to the most precise cosmic ray propagation code. We conclude with an isotope chart describing which information is in principle necessary to describe nuclear interactions in cosmic ray sources and propagation.

## Key claim
The impact of nuclear model uncertainties on cosmic ray disintegration rates is potentially larger in astrophysical sources with non-thermal radiation fields (such as gamma-ray bursts) than in the cosmic microwave background, but random cross-section fluctuations tend to average out in the nuclear cascade unless systematic offsets (e.g., between neutron-rich and proton-rich isotopes) are present.

## Method
The authors simulated the photo-disintegration of nuclei up to iron in a gamma-ray burst radiation field by solving a coupled system of partial differential equations for 481 isotopes with 41,000 inclusive disintegration channels, using the TALYS, PEANUT, and PSB nuclear models. They compared the resulting isotope populations and emitted cosmic ray composition, and assessed uncertainties by randomly varying unmeasured absorption cross sections by factors of 0–2 and partially measured ones by 0.5–1.5. The GRB model assumed a broken power-law photon spectrum with indices −1 and −2 and a break at 1 keV, with iron injection following an E⁻² spectrum.

## Key numbers
- The uncertainty among different nuclear models for total photo-absorption cross sections of isobars with A=40 is estimated to be of order a factor of two.
- For the PSB model, the σ(⁵⁶Fe(γ,1p)) cross section is overestimated by a factor of 5 to 10 compared to more complete models (TALYS, PEANUT).
- The emitted cosmic ray composition from a GRB with iron injection shows deviations in ⟨ln A⟩ of up to a factor of two between the PSB model and the TALYS model at energies around 10¹⁰ GeV.
- The GRB simulation parameters include: isotropic gamma-ray luminosity L_γ,iso = 10⁵² erg/s, Lorentz factor Γ = 300, variability time t_v = 0.01 s, redshift z = 2, and maximum iron energy E_Fe,max ≈ 10¹¹ GeV in the observer's frame.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of cosmic ray propagation through the CMB and EBL (e.g., Allard 2008, Taylor 2011, Aloisio 2013) by simulating the nuclear cascade within astrophysical sources at a level of complexity comparable to propagation codes like CRPropa2/3. It contrasts with simpler source models that use box-function approximations for the GDR (Murase 2008, Bustamante 2014) or the PSB model (Puget 1976). The results enable combined source-propagation models for determining the injection composition of UHECRs and identifying astrophysical parameters via multi-messenger observations, and call for new measurements of photo-absorption cross sections for unstable isotopes at facilities such as ELI-NP, newSUBARU, or HIγS.
