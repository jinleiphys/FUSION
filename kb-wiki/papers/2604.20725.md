# Interaction between nuclear clusters and superfluid phonons in the neutron-star inner crust

> The interaction between lattice vibrations of nuclear clusters and superfluid phonons associated with neutron superfluidity plays an important role in the dynamics of the neutron-star inner crust. While this coupling has been discussed mainly within macroscopic approaches such as hydrodynamics and effective field theory, its microscopic origin and the value of the effective coupling constant have remained unclear. In this work, we derive the interaction between nuclear clusters and superfluid phonons starting from a microscopic description of inner-crust matter. Using nuclear density functional theory, we analyze the response of a neutron superfluid around a single nuclear cluster within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation. From this microscopic response, we obtain the interaction between the cluster and the surrounding superfluid. Matching this result to the long-wavelength effective description, we determine the coupling constant in an effective Hamiltonian describing the mixing between lattice and superfluid phonons. The resulting coupling strength is found to be significantly smaller than previous hydrodynamical estimates. This reduction originates from the suppression of the superfluid phonon amplitude inside and around the nuclear cluster. Our results provide a microscopic determination of the coupling parameter governing lattice-superfluid phonon mixing in the neutron-star inner crust.

## Key claim

The coupling constant governing the mixing between lattice phonons and superfluid phonons in the neutron-star inner crust is significantly smaller than previous hydrodynamical estimates, because the superfluid phonon amplitude is suppressed inside and around the nuclear cluster.

## Method

The authors used nuclear density functional theory (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework) to analyze the linear response of a neutron superfluid around a single nuclear cluster within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA). They derived the microscopic coupling between cluster vibrations and superfluid phonons from the transition density obtained in this single-cluster setup, then matched this result to a long-wavelength effective Hamiltonian to extract the coupling constant. The matching condition equated the matrix element of the microscopic potential gradient with that of an effective local potential acting on plane-wave superfluid phonon states.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant g0 is determined by the volume integral of the effective interaction potential, v_eff(0) = ∫ v_eff(r) dr, combined with the cluster number density ρ_cl, cluster mass M, and the derivative ∂μ_n/∂ρ_n (the second derivative of neutron energy density with respect to neutron density).
* The superfluid phonon velocity is given by v_s = √(ρ_n / m_n × ∂μ_n/∂ρ_n), where m_n is the neutron mass.
* The pair potential in the self-consistent field is of order 1 MeV, much smaller than the one-body potential of order 50 MeV, justifying the neglect of the pair potential in the coupling calculation.
* The Wigner-Seitz radius R_WS and cluster size R_clust satisfy R_WS ≫ R_clust, with the long-wavelength regime requiring k_n R_clust ≪ 1.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies by Inakura (2017, 2018) that used the Wigner-Seitz approximation to examine dipole coupling of superfluid phonons to nuclear clusters, finding that the superfluid phonon does not penetrate into cluster interiors. It contradicts macroscopic hydrodynamic estimates by Pethick (2010), Kobyakov (2013), and Chamel (2012, 2013), which gave larger coupling constants. The results enable microscopic determination of the lattice-superfluid phonon mixing parameter needed for modeling neutron-star glitches, thermal conductivity, and elastic properties, though effects specific to the periodic lattice structure such as the entrainment effect described by Pethick (2010) and Cirigliano (2011) are left for future work.
