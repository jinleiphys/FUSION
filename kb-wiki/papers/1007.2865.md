# Fermionic-bosonic couplings in a weakly-deformed odd-mass nucleus, $^{93}_{41}$Nb

> A comprehensive decay scheme of $^{93}$Nb below 2 MeV has been constructed from information obtained with the $^{93}$Nb(n,n$^\prime$$γ$) and $^{94}$Zr(p,2n$γ$$γ$)$^{93}$Nb reactions. Branching ratios, lifetimes, transition multipolarities and spin assignments have been determined. From $M1$ and $E2$ strengths, fermionic-bosonic excitations of isoscalar and isovector character have been identified from the weak coupling $\pi1g_{9/2}$$\otimes$$^{92}_{40}$Zr and $\pi2p_{1/2}^{-1}$$\otimes$$^{94}_{42}$Mo configurations. A microscopic interpretation of such excitations is attained from shell-model calculations using low-momentum effective interactions.

## Key claim

Fermionic-bosonic excitations of both isoscalar and isovector character have been identified in the weakly-deformed odd-mass nucleus $^{93}$Nb from measured M1 and E2 transition strengths, with the interpretation that the weak coupling of a single proton (or proton hole) to the bosonic core provides structural simplifications that allow these excitations to be distinguished despite the complexity of the odd-mass system.

## Method

The authors constructed a comprehensive decay scheme of $^{93}$Nb below 2.2 MeV using the $^{93}$Nb(n,n$'$$\gamma$) reaction at the University of Kentucky and the $^{94}$Zr(p,2n$\gamma$$\gamma$)$^{93}$Nb reaction at the University of Cologne. Lifetimes were determined via the Doppler-shift attenuation method following the (n,n$'$$\gamma$) reaction, and transition multipolarities, mixing ratios, and spin assignments were obtained from angular correlation measurements. Shell-model calculations using low-momentum effective interactions ($V_{low\,k}$) with a $^{88}$Sr core provided a microscopic interpretation of the observed excitations.

## Context

This work extends the identification of mixed-symmetry states, previously studied in weakly-deformed even-even nuclei (as reviewed in the mixed-symmetry state report by Pietralla et al.), into the odd-mass nucleus $^{93}$Nb. It builds on earlier work by Orce et al. (2009) that identified isoscalar and isovector excitations in the negative-parity structure of $^{93}$Nb, and on shell-model calculations by Lisetskiy et al. (2004) that explained mixed-symmetry states using a surface delta interaction. The results enable future studies of fermionic-bosonic coupling in odd-mass nuclei and provide a microscopic many-body interpretation of isovector excitations that complements the algebraic Interacting Boson Model-2 description.
