# Transverse momentum broadening from NLL BFKL to all orders in pQCD

> We study, to all orders in perturbative QCD, the universal behavior of the saturation momentum $Q_s(L)$ controlling the transverse momentum distribution of a fast parton propagating through a dense QCD medium with large size $L$. Due to the double logarithmic nature of the quantum evolution of the saturation momentum, its large $L$ asymptotics is obtained by slightly departing from the double logarithmic limit of either next-to-leading log (NLL) BFKL or leading order DGLAP evolution equations. At fixed coupling, or in conformal $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM theory, we derive the large $L$ expansion of $Q_s(L)$ up to order $α_s^{3/2}$. In QCD with massless quarks, where conformal symmetry is broken by the running of the strong coupling constant, the one-loop QCD $β$-function fully accounts for the universal terms in the $Q_s(L)$ expansion. Therefore, the universal coefficients of this series are known exactly to all orders in $α_s$.

## Key claim

The authors derive the universal asymptotic behavior of the saturation momentum \(Q_s(L)\) controlling transverse momentum broadening of a fast parton in a dense QCD medium, showing that at large system size \(L\) the expansion coefficients are known exactly to all orders in \(\alpha_s\) when the one-loop QCD \(\beta\)-function is accounted for, but only up to order \(\alpha_s^{3/2}\) in fixed-coupling or conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory.

## Method

The authors map the non-linear evolution equation for the dipole cross-section (which governs transverse momentum broadening) onto the BFKL equation in the dilute regime, using the traveling wave front formalism to extract the asymptotic behavior of the saturation momentum. They analyze the NLL BFKL kernel in Mellin space, focusing on the double logarithmic collinear regime (poles at \(\gamma=1\)), and solve the evolution equation with a saturation boundary using an ansatz \(\hat{q}(\rho,Y)=e^{\rho_s(Y)-Y}e^{\beta x}f(x)\) where \(x=\rho-\rho_s(Y)\). The running coupling case is treated by incorporating the one-loop QCD \(\beta\)-function into the BFKL kernel.

## Key numbers

* In fixed-coupling QCD or conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, the large \(L\) expansion of \(\ln Q_s^2(L)\) is derived up to order \(\alpha_s^{3/2}\).
* For QCD with massless quarks, the asymptotic expansion reads: \(\ln Q_s^2(L) = Y + 2\sqrt{4 b_0 Y} + 3\xi_1(4b_0Y)^{1/6} - \frac{1}{4}(3+8b_0)\ln Y + \mathcal{O}(1)\), where \(Y=\ln(L)\) and \(b_0=1/\beta_0\) is the inverse of the one-loop QCD \(\beta\)-function coefficient.
* At tree-level, the saturation momentum behaves as \(Q_s^{2,(0)}(L) \simeq \hat{q}_0 L \ln(\hat{q}_0 L / \mu^2)\), with \(\hat{q}_0 = \alpha_s N_c m_D^2 T\).
* At NLO, the saturation momentum receives a double logarithmic enhancement: \(Q_s^2(L) = Q_s^{2,(0)}(L) \times [1 + \bar{\alpha}_s \ln^2(L/\tau_0) + \bar{\alpha}_s (2\ln 2 - \gamma_E - 1/3) \ln(L/\tau_0) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)]\), with \(\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) results for the saturation momentum from Iancu (2014) and Caucal (2022) by incorporating single logarithmic corrections from the NLL BFKL equation. It enables precise predictions for transverse momentum broadening in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for jet quenching observables such as dijet azimuthal asymmetry and the Soft Drop grooming angle. The method builds on the traveling wave front analogy previously applied to small-\(x\) physics by Munier (2003) and Mueller (2002), but adapted to the jet quenching problem where the evolution is dominated by the double logarithmic collinear regime rather than the standard BFKL critical exponent.
