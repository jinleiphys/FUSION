# $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton in chiral effective theory

> We compute the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton in chiral effective theory, including both nucleon and $Δ$ degrees of freedom, within both the relativistic and heavy baryon frameworks. In addition to the distribution at $x>0$, we compute the corrections to the asymmetry arising from zero momentum contributions from pion rainbow and bubble diagrams at $x=0$, which have not been accounted for in previous analyses. We find that the empirical $x$ dependence of $\bar d - \bar u$ as well as the integrated asymmetry can be well reproduced in terms of a transverse momentum cutoff parameter.

## Key claim
The paper computes the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton using chiral effective theory including both nucleon and $\Delta$ degrees of freedom, and finds that the empirical $x$ dependence and integrated asymmetry can be reproduced with a transverse momentum cutoff parameter, but only after including previously neglected zero-momentum contributions at $x=0$ from pion rainbow and bubble diagrams.

## Method
The authors compute the $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry by convoluting pion light-cone momentum distributions (from rainbow diagrams with nucleon and $\Delta$ intermediate states, and from bubble diagrams) with valence pion parton distribution functions. They evaluate these distributions in both relativistic and heavy baryon chiral effective theory frameworks, using a transverse momentum cutoff $\Lambda$ and a separate cutoff $\mu$ on the $k^-$ integration to regularize ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers
- The integrated asymmetry over the measured $x$ range from E866 Drell-Yan data is $\int_{0.015}^{0.35} dx (\bar d-\bar u) = 0.0803(11)$.
- The $\delta$-function pieces at $x=0$ can contribute up to approximately 16% (for $\mu=1$ GeV) of the $\bar d-\bar u$ difference integrated over the measured region.
- For $\mu$ ranging between 0.1 and 1 GeV, the cutoff $\Lambda$ varies from approximately 0.18 to 0.23 GeV.
- The $\pi N \Delta$ coupling constant is $g_{\pi N\Delta} = (3\sqrt{2}/5) g_A/f_\pi \approx 11.8$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The axial charge is $g_A = 1.267$, the pion decay constant is $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, and the nucleon mass is $M$.

## Context
This work extends previous chiral effective theory calculations of PDF moments (Chen 2002, Arndt 2001) to compute the $x$ dependence of $\bar d - \bar u$ directly, building on the formalism of Moiseeva (2013) for nonlocal operator matching. It corrects earlier Sullivan process calculations (Thomas 1983, Kumano 1998, Speth 1998) by including off-shell and bubble diagram contributions at $x=0$ that were not accounted for in previous analyses, and finds that the net $\Delta$ contribution is much smaller than found in many prior studies. The results enable future applications to polarized sea asymmetries and strange-antistrange asymmetries using SU(3) generalizations.
