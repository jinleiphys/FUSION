# Elastic Compton Scattering from the Nucleon and Deuteron

> Goal of this work is the consistent description of elastic Compton scattering from the single nucleon and the deuteron. The theoretical framework chosen is Chiral Perturbation Theory, which is the low-energy formulation of Quantum Chromodynamics, where we extend the spectrum of active degrees of freedom from only pions and nucleons to also the Delta(1232) resonance. In the deuteron sector, we treat the nucleon-nucleon interaction non-perturbatively. Besides the Compton cross sections, our main concern is with the nucleon polarizabilities, which are a useful tool to describe the reaction of the internal nucleonic degrees of freeedom to the external electromagnetic field. Experimentally, only a few nucleon polarizabilities are known. Especially our present knowledge of the neutron polarizabilities is not satisfying. The reason why it is so difficult to determine these quantities experimentally is the finite lifetime of the free neutron. Therefore, we want to contribute to the ongoing discussion of the neutron polarizabilities by fitting the average over proton and neutron polarizabilities to the elastic deuteron Compton-scattering data. Similarly, we determine the proton polarizabilities from fits to the single-proton Compton data. We finally combine both results in order to identify the neutron polarizabilities.

## Key claim

This work aims to provide a consistent description of elastic Compton scattering from both the nucleon and the deuteron within Chiral Perturbation Theory extended to include the Delta(1232) resonance, with the specific goal of extracting neutron polarizabilities by fitting the average of proton and neutron polarizabilities to deuteron Compton-scattering data, rather than measuring them directly from free neutrons.

## Method

The theoretical framework is Chiral Perturbation Theory with pions, nucleons, and the Delta(1232) resonance as active degrees of freedom, while the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the deuteron sector is treated non-perturbatively using the AV18 potential. The author evaluates two-nucleon reducible amplitudes for Compton scattering on the deuteron, including contributions from intermediate states, double commutators involving the kinetic energy and the one-pion-exchange potential, and performs double integrals over radial coordinates with Green's functions. An approximation is made for the u-channel energy denominator by expanding in the photon energy over the intermediate-state mass, allowing the double integral to be evaluated once per energy and then corrected by a factor depending on the scattering angle.

## Key numbers

* The AV18 potential uses a neutron electric form factor slope of β_n = 0.0189 fm², as determined experimentally by Krohn.
* The cutoff parameter in the AV18 potential is c = 2.1 fm⁻².
* The Woods-Saxon function parameters in the AV18 potential are r_0 = 0.5 fm and a = 0.2 fm.
* The pion-nucleon coupling constant squared is f² = 0.075.
* The approximation for the u-channel energy denominator introduces an error well below 2% for all considered cases, checked at θ = 5° and 180°.

## Context

This work extends the application of Chiral Perturbation Theory to Compton scattering on the deuteron, building on prior approaches such as the AV18 potential (Wiringa et al.) and the one-pion-exchange potential treatment by Arenhoevel and Karakowski. The study aims to improve the determination of neutron polarizabilities, which are poorly known experimentally due to the finite lifetime of the free neutron, by combining fits to proton Compton data with fits to deuteron Compton data. The results enable future extractions of neutron polarizabilities from deuteron Compton-scattering experiments and test the consistency of the chiral effective field theory framework with the Delta resonance included.
