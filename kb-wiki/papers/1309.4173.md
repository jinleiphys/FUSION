# Hypernuclear matter in a complete SU(3) symmetry group

> Using the well known quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), we study the effects of meson-hyperon coupling constants on the onset of hyperons in dense nuclear matter. We use the SU(3) symmetry group to fix all these coupling constants, constrained to experimental nuclear matter results and astrophysical observations. While the discovery of massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 points towards a very stiff equation of state at very large densities, results from heavy ion collisions point in opposite direction for densities below five times the nuclear saturation density. We study some well known parametrizations and see that most of them cannot satisfy both types of constraints. Indeed, although in our model we can simulate a 2.25 $M_\odot$ hyperonic neutron star, the heavy ion collision constraints limits the maximum mass around 2.06 $M_\odot$.

## Key claim

Using a complete SU(3) symmetry group to fix all meson-hyperon coupling constants (rather than only the isoscalar-vector ones), the authors show that most standard parametrizations cannot simultaneously satisfy both the stiff equation of state required by massive pulsar observations and the soft equation of state required by heavy ion collision data below five times nuclear saturation density; while their model can simulate a 2.25 solar mass hyperonic neutron star, the heavy ion collision constraints limit the maximum mass to around 2.06 solar masses.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) in the mean field approximation with three standard parametrizations (GM1, GM3, NL3) in both the traditional σωρ model and the extended σωρφ model. They fixed all hyperon-meson coupling constants using a complete SU(3) symmetry group with two free parameters (α_v for vector mesons and α_s for scalar mesons), imposing the known Λ potential depth U_Λ = -28 MeV to determine α_s, while letting the uncertain Σ and Ξ potentials be determined solely by symmetry properties. They then calculated particle fractions, strangeness fraction, equation of state, and speed of sound for hypernuclear matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass of 2.25 M_⊙ achievable in the model, but heavy ion collision constraints limit this to 2.06 M_⊙
- Λ hyperon potential depth fixed to U_Λ = -28 MeV
- For GM1 with α_v = 1 (SU(6) symmetry): U_Σ = +32 MeV, U_Ξ = +40 MeV
- For GM1 with α_v = 0.0: U_Σ = -2 MeV, U_Ξ = +22 MeV
- For GM1LM with α_v = 0.25: U_Σ = -157 MeV (too attractive, discarded)
- Nuclear saturation densities: 0.153 fm⁻³ (GM1, GM3) and 0.148 fm⁻³ (NL3)
- Binding energy per baryon B/A = -16.3 MeV for all three parametrizations
- Nuclear compression modulus K: 300 MeV (GM1), 240 MeV (GM3), 272 MeV (NL3)
- Symmetry energy coefficient S_0: 32.5 MeV (GM1, GM3), 37.4 MeV (NL3)
- Without hyperons, the QCD speed of sound limit (v_s = 1/√3 ≈ 0.58) is exceeded at density 0.352 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the SU(6) symmetry group (Pais, 1966) and the Glendenning Conjecture (Glendenning, 1982-1985) for fixing hyperon-meson couplings, which typically produced maximum neutron star masses below 2.0 M_⊙. It builds on the approach of Weiss (2010) and Weiss (2012) who used SU(3) symmetry but only for isoscalar-vector mesons while forcing isovector-vector mesons to obey SU(6). The authors provide a fully consistent SU(3) treatment where all meson couplings are determined by symmetry, enabling systematic comparison with both astrophysical observations (massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432) and heavy ion collision constraints (Danielewicz, 2002; Lynch, 2009). The results enable future investigation of hadron-quark phase transitions through the calculated speed of sound profiles.
