# Cold dilute neutron matter on the lattice I: Lattice virial coefficients and large scattering lengths

> We study cold dilute neutron matter on the lattice using an effective field theory. We work in the unitary limit in which the scattering length is much larger than the interparticle spacing. In this paper we focus on the equation of state at temperatures above the Fermi temperature and compare lattice simulations to the virial expansion on the lattice and in the continuum. We find that in the unitary limit lattice discretization errors in the second virial coefficient are significantly enhanced. As a consequence the equation of state does not show the universal scaling behavior expected in the unitary limit. We suggest that scaling can be improved by tuning the second virial coefficient rather than the scattering length.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in lattice simulations of cold dilute neutron matter in the unitary limit, discretization errors in the second virial coefficient are significantly enhanced, causing the equation of state to fail to show the expected universal scaling behavior; the authors propose that scaling can be improved by tuning the interaction coefficient to reproduce the correct second virial coefficient rather than the scattering length.

## Method
The authors used an effective field theory on a 3+1 dimensional lattice with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to simulate cold dilute neutron matter. They computed the energy per neutron and density as functions of fugacity for several scattering lengths (ranging from -4.675 fm to +18.70 fm) at temperatures of 2, 3, and 4 MeV, using hybrid Monte Carlo with diagonal preconditioning. The lattice results were compared to the virial expansion in both the continuum and on the lattice, with the second virial coefficient extracted from bubble chain diagrams.

## Key numbers
- Neutron scattering length: \(a_{nn} \simeq -18\) fm; effective range: \(r_{nn} \simeq 2.8\) fm.
- Spatial lattice spacing: \(a = (50\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\); temporal lattice spacing: \(a_t = (24\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\).
- For \(a_{\text{scatt}} = -18.70\) fm at \(T = 4\) MeV and \(\mu = 0\), the simulated density was \(\rho^{\text{sim}} = 8.70(3) \times 10^{-3}\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) on a \(4^3\) lattice and \(8.81(10) \times 10^{-3}\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) on a \(6^3\) lattice.
- The continuum second virial coefficient in the unitary limit is \(b_2(T) = 3 \cdot 2^{-5/2} \approx 0.530\); for a free Fermi gas, \(b_2(T) = -2^{-5/2} \approx -0.177\).
- Lattice second virial coefficient for \(a_{\text{scatt}} = -18.70\) fm at \(T = 4\) MeV: 1.03 (lattice) vs. 0.411 (continuum); at \(T = 2\) MeV: 1.61 (lattice) vs. 0.371 (continuum).
- Lattice first virial coefficient \(b_1(T)\) at \(T = 4\) MeV: 1.273; at \(T = 0.5\) MeV: 1.038.

## Context
This work extends lattice effective field theory simulations of dilute neutron matter by Lee and Schaefer (2004) to systematically study the equation of state at finite temperature, comparing with the virial expansion. It identifies a significant discretization error in the second virial coefficient that prevents the lattice results from matching the universal scaling behavior expected in the unitary limit, a regime relevant to neutron star crusts (Pethick, 1995) and cold atom experiments using Feshbach resonances (O'Hara, 2002; Gupta, 2002). The proposed tuning method enables more reliable lattice simulations of neutron matter in the unitary limit, which will be applied in a sequel paper to study scaling behavior.
