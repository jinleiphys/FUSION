# Competition between $T$=1 and $T$=0 pairing in $pf$-shell nuclei with $N=Z$

> The pairing correlation energy for two-nucleon configurations with the spin-parity and isospin of $J^π=0^+$, $T$=1 and $J^π=1^+$, $T$=0 are calculated with $T$=1 and $T$=0 pairing interactions, respectively. To this end, we consider the $(1f2p)$ shell model space, including single-particle angular momenta of $l=3$ and $l=1$. It is pointed out that a two-body matrix element of the spin-triplet $T$=0 pairing is weakened substantially for the $1f$ orbits, even though the pairing strength is much larger than that for the spin-singlet $T$=1 pairing interaction. In contrast, the spin-triplet pairing correlations overcome the spin-singlet pairing correlations for the $2p$ configuration, for which the spin-orbit splitting is smaller than that for the $1f$ configurations, if the strength for the T=0 pairing is larger than that for the T=1 pairing by 50% or more. Using the Hartree-Fock wave functions, it is also pointed out that the mismatch of proton and neutron radial wave functions is at most a few % level, even if the Fermi energies are largely different in the proton and neutron mean-field potentials. These results imply that the configuration with $J^π=0^+$, $T$=1 is likely in the ground state of odd-odd $pf$ shell nuclei even under the influence of the strong spin-triplet $T$=0 pairing, except at the middle of the $pf$ shell, in which the odd proton and neutron may occupy the $2p$ orbits. These results are consistent with the observed spin-parity $J^π=0^+$ for all odd-odd $pf$ shell nuclei except for $^{58}_{29}$Cu, which has $J^π=1^+$.

## Key claim

The spin-triplet (T=0) pairing correlation energy can overcome the spin-singlet (T=1) pairing correlation energy in the 2p configuration when the T=0 pairing strength is more than 50% larger than the T=1 strength, but the T=0 pairing matrix element is substantially weakened for the 1f orbits due to the large spin-orbit splitting, even if the intrinsic T=0 pairing strength is much larger than the T=1 strength.

## Method

The authors calculated two-body matrix elements for T=1 (spin-singlet, Jπ=0+) and T=0 (spin-triplet, Jπ=1+) pairing interactions using a separable form in the (1f2p) shell model space, with single-particle angular momenta l=3 and l=1. They diagonalized the model space separately for the l=1 and l=3 configurations, using Hartree-Fock single-particle wave functions from the Skyrme interaction SLy4, with the spin-orbit splitting parametrized as Δε_ls = -V_ls (l·s) with V_ls = 24/A^(2/3). The T=1 pairing strength was fixed at G^(T=1) = 24/A MeV, while the T=0 pairing strength was varied by a factor f from 1 to 2 relative to G^(T=1).

## Key numbers

- The T=1 pairing strength is fixed as G^(T=1) = 24/A MeV, with A=56 used in the calculations.
- The spin-orbit splitting between 1f_7/2 and 1f_5/2 states in ⁴¹Ca is 7.0 MeV.
- The overlap integrals of proton and neutron HF wave functions deviate at most 3% from unity, with values ranging from 97.7% to 100% across ⁴⁸Cr, ⁵⁶Ni, and ⁶⁴Ge.
- For the l=3 (1f) configuration, the Jπ=1+ state gains more binding energy than the Jπ=0+ state when the strength factor f ≥ 1.6.
- For the l=1 (2p) configuration, the Jπ=1+ state gains more binding energy than the Jπ=0+ state when f ≥ 1.4.
- The Fermi energies of proton and neutron potentials in ⁵⁶Ni differ by about 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Poves (1998), Bertsch (2012), and Brown (2010) on how the nuclear spin-orbit field suppresses spin-triplet pairing more than spin-singlet pairing. The results are consistent with the observed spin-parity Jπ=0+ for all odd-odd pf-shell N=Z nuclei except ⁵⁸₂₉Cu, which has Jπ=1+, and suggest that the 2p orbit configuration at the middle of the pf shell may favor T=0 pairing. The findings enable further examination of spin-triplet pair condensation using HF-Bogoliubov or large-scale shell model calculations as suggested by Gezelis (2011).
