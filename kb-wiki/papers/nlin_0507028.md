# Angularly localized Skyrmions

> Quantized Skyrmions with baryon numbers $B=1,2$ and 4 are considered and angularly localized wavefunctions for them are found. By combining a few low angular momentum states, one can construct a quantum state whose spatial density is close to that of the classical Skyrmion, and has the same symmetries. For the B=1 case we find the best localized wavefunction among linear combinations of $j=1/2$ and $j=3/2$ angular momentum states. For B=2, we find that the $j=1$ ground state has toroidal symmetry and a somewhat reduced localization compared to the classical solution. For B=4, where the classical Skyrmion has cubic symmetry, we construct cubically symmetric quantum states by combining the $j=0$ ground state with the lowest rotationally excited $j=4$ state. We use the rational map approximation to compare the classical and quantum baryon densities in the B=2 and B=4 cases.

## Key claim

By combining only a few low angular momentum states (up to \(j=3/2\) for \(B=1\), \(j=1\) for \(B=2\), and \(j=4\) for \(B=4\)), one can construct quantum wavefunctions whose spatial baryon density approximates that of the classical Skyrmion, but the localization is necessarily imperfect because only a finite number of states are available and the energy cost of including higher states is prohibitive.

## Method

The authors treat the Skyrmion as a rigid rotor, quantizing its collective rotational coordinates via Wigner functions. For each baryon number, they select a small set of low-lying angular momentum states allowed by Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints, then form linear combinations that maximize angular localization. The classical baryon density is computed using the rational map approximation (maps \(R(z)=z\) for \(B=1\), \(R(z)=z^2\) for \(B=2\), and a cubic rational map for \(B=4\)), and the quantum density is obtained by averaging the classical density over orientations weighted by the squared wavefunction.

## Key numbers

* For \(B=1\), the best localized wavefunction among combinations of \(j=1/2\) and \(j=3/2\) states occurs at \(\kappa = -3/8\) in the form \(\Psi = \frac{\sqrt2}{\pi}\left(\frac{5}{16}+\kappa+\kappa^2\right)^{-1/2}(a_0^3 + \kappa a_0)\); a second localization criterion gives \(\kappa = -1/4\).
* For \(B=2\), the quantum density after averaging the classical toroidal density over the \(j=1\) ground state wavefunction \(\Psi = \sqrt{3/(8\pi^2)}\cos\beta\) becomes \(\rho_\Psi = c_{00}Y_{00} + \frac{2}{5}c_{20}Y_{20}\), where \(c_{00}=1/\sqrt{\pi}\) and \(c_{20}=-0.36\); the classical density had \(c_{20}\) without the factor \(2/5\).
* For \(B=4\), the cubically symmetric wavefunction combining \(j=0\) and \(j=4\) states has a positive maximum of \(24/5\) at the identity orientation and a negative minimum of \(-104/45\) at a \(60^\circ\) rotation around a cube diagonal.

## Context

This work extends the rigid-body quantization of Skyrmions (Adkins, Nappi, Witten 1983; Braaten 1988; Krusch 2003) by explicitly constructing angularly localized superpositions that partially recover the classical shape information lost in states of definite angular momentum. It parallels the approach of Cooper et al. (2004) for Bose condensates, where combining many angular momentum states reveals vortex arrays. The results enable comparison of Skyrmion-model predictions with four-nucleon potential models (Forest et al. 1996) and suggest that deuteron and alpha-particle wavefunctions may contain admixtures of higher angular momentum states (e.g., \(\Delta\)-resonance components in the nucleon).
