# Reaction dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies: impact of incomplete fusion on the angular distribution of direct alpha-production

> The classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup for nuclear collision dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei is further developed. It allows a quantitative study of the importance of incomplete fusion dynamics in the angular distribution of direct alpha-production. Model calculations indicate that the incomplete fusion contribution diminishes with decreasing energy towards the Coulomb barrier, notably separating in angles from the contribution of no-capture breakup events. This should facilitate the experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes.

## Key claim

Model calculations indicate that the incomplete fusion contribution to direct alpha-production diminishes with decreasing energy towards the Coulomb barrier, and its angular centroid separates substantially from that of no-capture breakup events, which should facilitate experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes.

## Method

The classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup (implemented in the PLATYPUS code) was extended to propagate the surviving breakup fragment and the incomplete fusion product in time after capture, enabling calculation of their asymptotic angular distributions. For the test reaction ⁸Be + ²⁰⁸Pb, an ensemble of projectile trajectories was sampled with breakup positions determined from an empirical exponential breakup probability density, and the resulting fragment trajectories were classified as no-capture breakup, incomplete fusion, or complete fusion events.

## Key numbers

- S-wave Coulomb barrier for the projectile-target system is 39.9 MeV.
- Breakup probability parameters: A = 5.98 × 10³ and α = 0.85 fm⁻¹.
- At E₀ = 45 MeV: σ_ICF = 84.7 mb, σ_NCBU = 238.84 mb, σ_CF = 149.2 mb.
- At E₀ = 55 MeV: σ_ICF = 252.3 mb, σ_NCBU = 255.1 mb, σ_CF = 624.6 mb.
- At E₀ = 65 MeV: σ_ICF = 273.6 mb, σ_NCBU = 259.3 mb, σ_CF = 981.5 mb.
- Woods-Saxon potential for alpha + ²¹²Po: V = 33.98 MeV, r = 1.48 fm, a = 0.63 fm.

## Context

This work extends the classical dynamical reaction model of Diaz-Torres (2007) to include time propagation of incomplete fusion products and surviving fragments, enabling calculation of asymptotic angular distributions. The continuum-discretised coupled channels (CDCC) framework had been successful for breakup calculations but could not unambiguously calculate integrated incomplete fusion and complete fusion cross sections. The results enable experimental groups to separate incomplete fusion from no-capture breakup contributions in inclusive and exclusive coincidence measurements, such as those by Souza (2009) on alpha-production yields.
