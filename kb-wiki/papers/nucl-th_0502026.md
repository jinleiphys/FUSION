# Long-wavelength spin- and spin-isospin correlations in nucleon matter

> We analyse the long-wavelength response of a normal Fermi liquid using Landau theory. We consider contributions from intermediate states containing one additional quasiparticle-quasihole pair as well as those from states containing two or more additional quasiparticle-quasihole pairs. For the response of an operator corresponding to a conserved quantity, we show that the behavior of matrix elements to states with more than one additional quasiparticle-quasihole pair at low excitation energies $ω$ varies as $1/ω$. It is shown how rates of processes involving transitions to two quasiparticle-quasihole states may be calculated in terms of the collision integral in the Landau transport equation for quasiparticles.

# Long-wavelength spin- and spin-isospin correlations in nucleon matter

## Key claim
For operators that do not correspond to conserved quantities (such as spin in systems with noncentral interactions), the matrix element for transitions to two-quasiparticle-quasihole states at low excitation energies ω varies as 1/ω, leading to a divergence in the two-pair contribution to the response function at low frequencies that is cut off by the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect. This behavior contrasts with the case of conserved quantities, where the two-pair matrix element vanishes at long wavelengths.

## Method
The authors use Landau theory of normal Fermi liquids to analyze the long-wavelength response of nucleon matter, considering contributions from intermediate states containing one additional quasiparticle-quasihole pair as well as those containing two or more additional pairs. They express the two-quasiparticle-quasihole contribution to the response in terms of the collision integral in the Landau transport equation for quasiparticles, and evaluate the sums over momenta and energy integrals to obtain explicit expressions for the imaginary part of the response function.

## Key numbers
- The two-pair contribution to the imaginary part of the response function at zero temperature is proportional to ω and independent of q: Im χ^tp ∝ ω
- At nonzero temperature, Im χ^tp ∝ T^2/ω, with the explicit expression containing the factor [ω^2 + (2πT)^2]/(6ω)
- The energy integral over distribution functions yields the factor ω[ω^2 + (2πT)^2]/6
- The quasiparticle relaxation rate 1/τ_i^O(ε_1 + ω) contains the factor [(ω+ε_1)^2 + (πT)^2]/2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of long-wavelength matrix elements by Pines and Nozières (1966) from scalar density operators to more general responses including spin and spin-isospin operators. It builds on earlier work by Leggett (1965) and by Baym and Pethick (1991) on conservation laws in Fermi liquid response, and on the extension of Landau theory to noncentral interactions by Olsson, Pethick, and Haensel (2004). The results are directly applicable to neutrino processes in dense matter, particularly bremsstrahlung of neutrino-antineutrino pairs and the modified Urca process, where multipair contributions have been emphasized by Raffelt and collaborators (1996, 1999). The paper provides a framework for calculating two-pair contributions in terms of the quasiparticle scattering amplitudes recently computed by Schwenk and Friman (2004) including all contributions to second order in the low-momentum effective interaction V_low k.
