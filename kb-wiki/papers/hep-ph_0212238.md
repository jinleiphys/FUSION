# Generalized parton distributions and double distributions for q q-bar pions

> We consider two simple covariant models for pions (one with scalar and the other with spin-1/2 constituents). Pion generalized parton distributions are derived by integration over the light-cone energy. The model distributions are consistent with all known properties of generalized parton distributions, including positivity. We also construct the corresponding double distributions by appealing to Lorentz invariance. These ostensibly constructed double distributions lead to different generalized parton distributions that need not respect the positivity constraints. This inconsistency arises from the ambiguity inherent in defining double distributions in a one-component formalism (even in the absence of the Polyakov-Weiss term). We demonstrate that the correct model double distributions can be calculated from non-diagonal matrix elements of twist-two operators.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constructing double distributions (DDs) solely from Lorentz invariance and the form factor in the Drell-Yan frame yields only one component of the DD, which leads to incorrect generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that can violate positivity constraints, even when the Polyakov-Weiss D-term is absent.

## Method

The authors derived GPDs for two covariant pion models (scalar constituents and spin-1/2 constituents) by integrating the Feynman triangle diagram over the light-cone energy to project onto the light cone. They then constructed the corresponding DDs from the form factor in the Drell-Yan frame using Lorentz invariance, and compared the resulting GPDs from these DDs with the directly computed light-cone GPDs. Finally, they calculated the correct two-component DDs from non-diagonal matrix elements of twist-two operators.

## Key numbers

- For the scalar constituent model, the GPD from the one-component DD differs from the light-cone projection by a term proportional to \(\frac{\zeta}{2-\zeta} G(x,y;t)\), where \(G\) is the missing second DD component; the difference \(\delta\) is plotted for \(\zeta = 0.7\) and \(t = -m^2\) with \(M_\pi = m < 2m\)
- For the spin-1/2 constituent model, the GPD is plotted for parameters \(M_\pi = 0.14\) MeV and \(m = 0.33\) MeV, with fixed \(t = -m^2\) for varying \(\zeta\) and fixed \(\zeta = 0.5\) for varying \(t\)
- The positivity constraint ratio \(R(x,\zeta) \equiv (1 - \zeta/2) |H(x,\zeta,0)| / \sqrt{q(x) q(x^\prime)} \leq 1\) is violated by the DD-based GPD for the spin-1/2 model at \(\zeta = 0.4\) and \(t = 0\), while the light-cone projection satisfies it
- The zeroth moment of the GPD gives the form factor \(F(t)\); polynomiality is checked for moments \(n = 0,1,2,3\) at \(t = 0\)

## Context

This work extends earlier covariant model studies of GPDs, including the scalar triangle diagram considered by Pobylitsa (2002) and Theussl (2002), and the spin-1/2 model based on earlier work by Sawicki and by Bakker (2000). It directly addresses the inconsistency found by Mukherjee (2002) where two-body light-front wave function models of the pion produced GPDs that violated positivity constraints, attributing this to missing contributions from non-wave function vertex diagrams. The paper enables future construction of correct DDs from twist-two operator matrix elements and shows that the ambiguity in defining DDs (analogous to gauge freedom, as noted by Teryaev (2001)) allows generating infinitely many GPD models sharing the same form factor and quark distribution while satisfying polynomiality.
