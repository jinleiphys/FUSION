# The Effect of Pion Exchange in a Relativistic Quark Model of Baryons

> We examine the effect of adding pion exchange between quarks and pion self-energy corrections to the Los Alamos Relativistic Quark model with a short distance cutoff of the Bethe form. The contributions to the nucleon and the Delta-baryon are small. We conclude that the model is stable under this change in the sense that significant changes to the model parameters are not required.

## Key claim

Adding pion exchange between quarks and pion self-energy corrections to the Los Alamos Relativistic Quark model produces contributions to the nucleon and Delta-baryon masses that are small (a few MeV), so the model parameters do not require significant adjustment; however, the authors note that predictions for nuclei such as the deuteron may still be affected because significant cancellations affect the net binding energy there.

## Method

The authors calculated the contribution of one-pion exchange between pairs of quarks in the nucleon and Delta-baryon using S-wave quark wave functions from the Los Alamos Relativistic Quark model, with a short-distance cutoff of the Bethe form implemented via a theta function requiring the interquark separation to be at least r_cut. They also computed the pion-loop contribution to the quark self-energy in three approximations: a bound-state approximation where the intermediate quark remains in the ground state, and two approximations using a free massless quark propagator with either a sharp momentum cutoff or a monopole form factor. The spin-isospin factors for the pion-exchange interaction were evaluated for both the nucleon and Delta states.

## Key numbers

- The bound quark in the LARQ model has a total energy of 361.6 MeV, with parameters kappa^2 = 0.9 GeV/fm and r_0 = 0.57 fm
- For r_cut = 1.0 fm, the integral I(r_cut) = -0.487 MeV
- The maximum pion-exchange contribution between quarks is 5.02 MeV for the nucleon and 1.00 MeV for the Delta, occurring near r_cut = 1.05 fm
- The pion-loop self-energy contribution at r_cut = 1.0 fm gives I_tilde = 6.17 MeV, with the total self-energy B_SE dominated by this term
- The coupling constant g_qqpi^2/4pi is approximately 0.17, about two orders of magnitude smaller than the nucleon-pion coupling

## Context

This work extends the Los Alamos Relativistic Quark model (LARQ), previously applied to octet and decuplet baryons and to small nuclei like He-3 and He-4 by Goldman, Maltman, Stephenson, and Schmidt (GMSS), by adding pion exchange with a short-distance cutoff. The non-relativistic Quark Delocalization, Color Screening Model (QDCSM) had previously been extended with pion exchange to describe the deuteron and improve phase shifts, and this paper examines the analogous extension for the relativistic model. The results enable future calculations of pion-exchange effects in nuclear systems within the relativistic framework, where the authors note significant cancellations may affect binding energies.
