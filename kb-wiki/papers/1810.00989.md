# Probing BSM and High-x Physics with SoLID at JLab

> The program of parity violation with the proposed new SoLID spectrometer at JLab is presented. Physics topics include searched for physics beyond the Standard Model, studies of charge symmetry violation at the quark level, searched for quark-quark correlations, and a measurement of the ratio of up and down PDF's in the proton.

# Probing BSM and High-x Physics with SoLID at JLab

## Key claim

The SoLID spectrometer at Jefferson Lab can improve precision on the parity-violating electron-quark couplings \(C_{2u}\) and \(C_{2d}\) by almost an order of magnitude compared to previous work (Wang 2014), but this sensitivity is contingent on the large-acceptance design that enables measurements at high \(y\) where the asymmetry becomes sensitive to the \(g_{VA}^{ei}\) couplings. The program also claims unique sensitivity to leptophobic \(Z'\) bosons and dark \(Z_d\) bosons that are difficult to detect at colliders.

## Method

The paper describes a parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering (PVDIS) program using the proposed SoLID spectrometer at JLab with 11 GeV polarized electrons scattered from liquid hydrogen or deuterium targets. The spectrometer uses a 1.5 T solenoid field, five GEM detectors for tracking, a gas Cerenkov detector, and an electron calorimeter to distinguish electrons from pions, with dead-timeless readout. The asymmetry \(A_{LR} = (\sigma_L - \sigma_R)/(\sigma_L + \sigma_R)\) is measured for left- and right-handed electron scattering, and for an isoscalar deuteron target the PDF and many other corrections cancel, isolating the \(g_{VA}^{ei}\) couplings.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry for PVDIS is typically \((10^{-4} - 10^{-5}) Q^2\), where \(Q^2\) is in (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- SoLID has sensitivity to new physics scales (compositeness) at the 20 TeV level for its particular Lorentz structure.
- For dark \(Z_d\) boson searches, SoLID has best sensitivity in the range \(Q^2 \approx 10\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The LHC Drell-Yan data (ATLAS, 35/fb at 13 TeV) set limits on \(\Lambda\) for the \(C_2\) (VA) term well above 14 TeV, probably on the order of 20 TeV, though this analysis has not been formally completed (Falkowski 2017).

## Context

This work extends the PVDIS program originally established by Prescott (1978, 1979) that provided critical data for the Standard Model, and is complementary to the Qweak experiment (Androic 2018) which measured the \(C_1\) couplings. The SoLID program aims to improve precision on the \(C_2\) couplings beyond previous measurements (Wang 2014) and can test explanations for the NuTeV anomaly (Zeller 2001) involving charge symmetry violation (Londergan 2003, 2009) or the isovector EMC effect (Cloet 2012). The measurement of the \(d/u\) PDF ratio on a proton target provides a method independent of nuclear structure corrections that affect traditional deuteron-based extractions (Accardi 2016).
