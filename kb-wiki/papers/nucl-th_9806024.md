# Higher Nucleon Resonances in Exclusive $(γ, πN)$ Reactions on Nuclei

> We report calculations for exclusive pion photoproduction on nuclei beyond the first resonance region in the distorted wave impulse approximation. The elementary operator contains contributions from the resonances $P_{33}(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{33}(1740)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$, in addition to the usual Born plus vector meson contributions. It gives a good description of single pion photoproduction data up to about 1.1 GeV. Final state interactions are incorporated via optical potentials and are found to be substantial in the coincidence cross sections, but insensitive to the photon asymmetry. Clear signatures of possible medium modifications of the $D_{13}$ and $F_{15}$ resonances are predicted.

# Higher Nucleon Resonances in Exclusive $(\gamma, \pi N)$ Reactions on Nuclei

## Key claim

Exclusive quasifree pion photoproduction on nuclei, calculated in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with an elementary operator containing seven nucleon resonances up to $F_{15}(1680)$, predicts clear signatures of possible medium modifications of the $D_{13}(1520)$ and $F_{15}(1680)$ resonances that are large enough to be detected by experiments, especially if the photon asymmetry is measured. The claim is hedged by the fact that the medium modifications are explored phenomenologically using parameter variations guided by existing models, without a fully dynamical treatment such as a delta-hole model.

## Method

The authors performed DWIA calculations for the exclusive reaction $A(\gamma, \pi N)A-1$ on $^{12}C$ for photon energies up to 1.1 GeV, using an elementary pion photoproduction operator that includes Born terms, vector meson contributions, and seven resonances ($P_{33}(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{33}(1740)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $F_{15}(1680)$) described by unitarized Breit-Wigner parameterizations. Final state interactions were incorporated via optical potentials: the Cooper et al. (1993) global Dirac-based optical model for the outgoing nucleon and the Gmitro et al. (1987) microscopic global potential for the outgoing pion. The reaction was studied under quasifree kinematics at missing momentum $p_m = 100$ MeV/c, and medium modification effects were explored by varying the mass (3% decrease), width (60% increase), and coupling strength (12% reduction for $D_{13}$, 22% reduction for $F_{15}$) of the two dominant higher resonances.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range of the model: up to about 1.1 GeV
- Missing momentum restricted to below 400 MeV/c for quasifree kinematics
- Missing momentum used in calculations: $p_m = 100$ MeV/c
- For $^{12}C(\gamma,\pi^- p)^{11}C_{g.s.}$ at $E_\gamma = 750$ MeV and $\theta_\pi = 60^\circ$: $T_\pi = 382.9$ MeV, $T_p = 210.1$ MeV, $\theta_p = 49.9^\circ$ (with free-nucleon values in parentheses: $T_\pi = 398.4$ MeV, $T_p = 212.0$ MeV, $\theta_p = 42.4^\circ$)
- For $^{12}C(\gamma,\pi^- p)^{11}C_{g.s.}$ at $E_\gamma = 750$ MeV and $\theta_\pi = 120^\circ$: $T_\pi = 200.3$ MeV, $T_p = 392.7$ MeV, $\theta_p = 22.6^\circ$
- Medium modification parameters tested: mass decreased by 3%, width increased by 60%, strength decreased by 12% for $D_{13}$ and 22% for $F_{15}$
- Nucleon FSI alone causes less than 20% reduction in cross sections
- Pion FSI causes up to a factor of 2 reduction in cross sections at backward angles and energies below 800 MeV
- 10 to 15 partial waves needed for pion convergence; around 20 partial waves for nucleon

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DWIA studies of pion photoproduction (Lee et al., 1993) and electroproduction (Lee et al., 1997) in the delta resonance region to higher energies, addressing the puzzle of "damped resonances" observed in inclusive photoabsorption data on nuclei by Bianchi et al. (1993). The elementary operator builds on the tree-level model of Hanstein (1993) improved by Kamalov et al. (1997) with additional resonances and new Mainz data. The calculations enable future experimental investigations with polarized photons at facilities like Jefferson Lab to isolate the mechanism behind the damping of higher nucleon resonances in the nuclear medium, complementing earlier work on eta photoproduction (Lee et al., 1996) and kaon photoproduction (Lee et al., 1997a) from nuclei.
