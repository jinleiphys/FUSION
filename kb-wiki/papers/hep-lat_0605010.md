# The nucleon axial-vector coupling beyond one loop

> We analyze the nucleon axial-vector coupling to two loops in chiral perturbation theory. We show that chiral extrapolations based on this representation require lattice data with pion masses below 300 MeV.

# The nucleon axial-vector coupling beyond one loop

## Key claim

The two-loop chiral perturbation theory representation of the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) can produce a flat pion mass dependence for pion masses below 400 MeV, but chiral extrapolations based on this representation require lattice data with pion masses below 300 MeV to keep the theoretical uncertainty moderate.

## Method

The authors performed a two-loop calculation of the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory using the heavy baryon framework. They applied renormalization group methods to determine the coefficient of the double logarithm \(\sim M_\pi^4 \ln^2 M_\pi\) from one-loop parameters alone, following Weinberg's approach, and estimated the numerically leading contributions to the single logarithm and polynomial terms at order \(M_\pi^4\) and \(M_\pi^5\) from graphs with insertions of the large low-energy constants \(c_3\), \(c_4\), and \(\bar{d}_{16}\).

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of the double logarithm \(\alpha_4\) is \(-\frac{16}{3} - \frac{11}{3} g_0^2 + 16 g_0^4\), which evaluates to 7 for \(g_0 = 1\).
- The single logarithm coefficient \(\gamma_4\) receives a contribution from the \(d_{16}\) operator of \(-12\, d_{16}^r(\lambda) (5/(3g_0) + g_0)\), and the total \(\gamma_4\) is 115.3 GeV\(^{-2}\) for the central parameter set.
- The relative corrections at the physical pion mass are: \(\Delta^{(2)} = -15.3\%\), \(\Delta^{(3)} = 25.6\%\), \(\Delta^{(4)} = -5.6\%\), \(\Delta^{(5)} = -0.1\%\).
- The chiral limit value of \(g_A\) extracted from the two-loop representation is \(g_0 = 1.21\) (or 1.12 for a different choice of \(\bar{d}_{16} = -0.92\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), using the physical value \(g_A = 1.267\).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculation of \(g_A\) by Kambor and Mojžiš (1998) and the earlier one-loop result by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1992). It addresses the problem that the one-loop representation rises steeply with pion mass while lattice data from Edwards et al. (2005) show little pion mass dependence. The authors note that the approach of Hemmert, Procura, and Weise (2003) using explicit delta degrees of freedom at leading one-loop order can produce a flat mass dependence but requires fine-tuning of low-energy constants and neglects other resonance contributions. The two-loop representation enables model-independent chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results, but only if lattice data are available at pion masses below 300 MeV.
