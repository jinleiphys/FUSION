# Possible Evidence for Neutral Color-Singlet $q\bar q$ Quark Matter from High-Energy Pb-Emulsion Collisions

> The invariant mass spectrum of $e^+e^-$ pairs produced in high-energy Pb-emulsion collisions at 160 A GeV at CERN SPS exhibits a complex structure of many resonances resting on top of a broad enhancement at invariant masses below 50 MeV, with the prominent resonance at 19 $\pm$1 MeV providing independent support for the hypothetical X17 particle. We show that this complex structure may be coherently described as signatures for the neutral color-singlet $q\bar q$ quark matter in both its deconfined and confined phases. That is, the broad enhancement may arise from thermal annihilation of QED(U(1))-deconfined quarks and antiquarks into $e^+e^-$ pairs at the phase transition temperature $T_c$(QED), theoretically estimated to be 4.75 $\pm$ 1.2 MeV from the transitional equilibrium condition. The observed 3$\pm$1 and 7$\pm$1 MeV resonances may correspond to the QED(U(1))-deconfined $d\bar d$ and $u\bar u$ Coulomb bound states near their quark rest masses, respectively, whereas the observed 19 $\pm$ 1 MeV resonance may correspond to the QED(U(1))-confined isoscalar QED meson. The approximate agreement between the theoretical and the experimental spectrum suggests that both QED(U(1))-confined and QED(U(1))-deconfined neutral color-singlet $q\bar q$ quark matter may have been produced in these high-energy Pb-emulsion collisions. We propose future experiments to confirm or refute these findings.

## Key claim

The complex structure of the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass spectrum from Pb-emulsion collisions at 160 A GeV, including both a broad enhancement and multiple resonances, may be coherently described as signatures of neutral color-singlet $q\bar q$ quark matter in both its QED(U(1))-deconfined and QED(U(1))-confined phases, though the author acknowledges high experimental uncertainties and the need for future confirmation.

## Method

The author analyzes the experimental invariant mass spectrum of $e^+e^-$ pairs from Jain and Singh (2007), obtained from 1220 events in central Pb-AgBr collisions at 160 A GeV at CERN SPS, with vertex separation distances of 50 $\mu$m to 200 $\mu$m. The theoretical $e^+e^-$ yield from thermal quark-antiquark annihilation in QED(U(1))-deconfined neutral color-singlet quark matter is calculated using Eq. (6) from Wong (1993) with the annihilation cross section for quarks into lepton pairs, evaluated at the critical temperature $T_c$(QED) estimated from the transitional equilibrium condition. The observed resonances are then compared to predicted bound states of deconfined $d\bar d$ and $u\bar u$ Coulomb states and a confined isoscalar QED meson.

## Key numbers

- The prominent resonance at 19 $\pm$ 1 MeV in the Pb-emulsion spectrum provides independent support for the hypothetical X17 particle
- The critical temperature for QED(U(1)) confinement-to-deconfinement phase transition is theoretically estimated as $T_c$(QED) = 4.75 $\pm$ 1.2 MeV, derived from the transitional equilibrium condition using $M$(X17) = 16.94 $\pm$ 0.12(stat) $\pm$ 0.21(sys) MeV from ATOMKI and quark masses $m_u$ = 2.5 $\pm$ 0.90 MeV and $m_d$ = 4.95 $\pm$ 1.85 MeV from PDG
- Observed resonances at 3 $\pm$ 1 MeV and 7 $\pm$ 1 MeV may correspond to QED(U(1))-deconfined $d\bar d$ and $u\bar u$ Coulomb bound states near their quark rest masses, respectively
- The peak of the broad enhancement is at 12 $\pm$ 1 MeV, coinciding with the $e^+e^-$ enhancement observed by Krasznahorkay (1996)
- The transverse momentum distribution $d$(Yield)/$dP_T$ of produced $e^+e^-$ pairs has a peak at about $P_T$(peak) $\sim$ 6 MeV, decreasing to nearly zero at $P_T \sim$ 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous theoretical framework of QED mesons and neutral color-singlet quark matter (Wong 2009-2026) to explain the complex Pb-emulsion anomaly first reported by Jain and Singh (2007). The analysis connects to earlier observations of anomalous neutral bosons from cosmic ray events (Anand 1953), nucleus-emulsion collisions (de Boer and van Dantzig 1988; El-Nadi and Badawy 1988; El-Nagdy, Abdelsalam, and Badwady 2007), and nuclear transition experiments (Krasznahorkay et al. 2016 at ATOMKI; de Boer et al. 1996-2001). The proposed interpretation enables future experiments to confirm or refute the production of neutral color-singlet quark matter in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, with the author suggesting that the X17 particle observed at ATOMKI, DUBNA, and HUS is a QED meson and a stable quantum of this quark matter at zero temperature.
