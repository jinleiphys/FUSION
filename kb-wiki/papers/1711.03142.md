# Structure of the nucleon's low-lying excitations

> A continuum approach to the three valence-quark bound-state problem in quantum field theory is used to perform a comparative study of the four lightest $(I=1/2,J^P = 1/2^\pm)$ baryon isospin-doublets in order to elucidate their structural similarities and differences. Such analyses predict the presence of nonpointlike, electromagnetically-active quark-quark (diquark) correlations within all baryons; and in these doublets, isoscalar-scalar, isovector-pseudovector, isoscalar-pseudoscalar, and vector diquarks can all play a role. In the two lightest $(1/2,1/2^+)$ doublets, however, scalar and pseudovector diquarks are overwhelmingly dominant. The associated rest-frame wave functions are largely $S$-wave in nature; and the first excited state in this $1/2^+$ channel has the appearance of a radial excitation of the ground state. The two lightest $(1/2,1/2^-)$ doublets fit a different picture: accurate estimates of their masses are obtained by retaining only pseudovector diquarks; in their rest frames, the amplitudes describing their dressed-quark cores contain roughly equal fractions of even- and odd-parity diquarks; and the associated wave functions are predominantly $P$-wave in nature, but possess measurable $S$-wave components. Moreover, the first excited state in each negative-parity channel has little of the appearance of a radial excitation. In quantum field theory, all differences between positive- and negative-parity channels must owe to chiral symmetry breaking, which is overwhelmingly dynamical in the light-quark sector. Consequently, experiments that can validate the contrasts drawn herein between the structure of the four lightest $(1/2,1/2^\pm)$ doublets will prove valuable in testing links between emergent mass generation and observable phenomena and, plausibly, thereby revealing dynamical features of confinement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the four lightest (I=1/2, J^P = 1/2^±) baryon doublets have fundamentally different internal structures depending on parity, with positive-parity states dominated by scalar and pseudovector diquarks in S-wave configurations, while negative-parity states require only pseudovector diquarks and are predominantly P-wave. However, the authors qualify that their Faddeev kernel omits meson-baryon final-state interactions, so their computed masses represent the dressed-quark core rather than the fully-dressed observable resonance, and a single free parameter g_DB = 0.43 was introduced to compensate for missing spin-orbit repulsion effects.

## Method

The authors solved the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for three valence-quark bound states using algebraic parametrizations of dressed-quark propagators and diquark correlation amplitudes (scalar, pseudovector, pseudoscalar, and vector), with diquark masses fixed at m_0+ = 0.8 GeV, m_1+ = 0.9 GeV, m_0- = 1.2 GeV, and m_1- = 1.3 GeV. A single parameter g_DB = 0.43 was introduced to multiply opposite-parity diquark amplitudes in the Faddeev kernel, chosen to reproduce the empirical 0.1 GeV mass splitting between the lowest negative-parity state and the first excited positive-parity state. The resulting linear homogeneous integral equations were solved directly on the baryon mass-shells to obtain masses and Faddeev amplitudes for the two lowest solutions in each parity channel.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon ground state mass: 1.19 GeV (dressed-quark core)
- N(1440) 1/2^+ mass: 1.73 GeV (dressed-quark core)
- N(1535) 1/2^- mass: 1.83 GeV (dressed-quark core)
- N(1650) 1/2^- mass: 1.91 GeV (dressed-quark core)
- g_DB = 0.43 (fitted parameter to produce 0.1 GeV splitting between lowest P- and first excited P+ states)
- Diquark masses used: m_0+ = 0.8 GeV, m_1+ = 0.9 GeV, m_0- = 1.2 GeV, m_1- = 1.3 GeV
- Diquark couplings: g_0+ = 14.8, g_1+ = 12.7, g_0- = 12.8, g_1- = 5.4, g_1bar- = 2.5
- rms-relative-difference between predicted quark-core masses and bare masses from Suzuki (2009) is 1.7%
- With g_DB = 0 (eliminating opposite-parity diquarks): N(1535) mass = 2.16 GeV, N(1650) mass = 2.31 GeV
- With g_DB = 1.0 (unmodified opposite-parity diquarks): N(1535) mass = 1.43 GeV, N(1650) mass = 1.61 GeV

## Context

This work extends the continuum quantum field theory approach to baryon structure previously applied to the nucleon, Delta-baryon, and Roper resonance by Segovia (2014, 2015), now systematically comparing all four lightest (1/2,1/2^±) doublets. The results contradict constituent-quark model classifications from Isgur (1978) that describe N(1535) and N(1650) as simple P-wave states with different spin couplings, showing instead that both negative-parity states can be built from pseudovector diquarks alone with roughly equal even- and odd-parity diquark content. The computed dressed-quark core masses agree within 1.7% with bare masses extracted from dynamical coupled-channels analyses by Suzuki (2009), enabling future calculations of N(1535) and N(1650) electroproduction form factors for comparison with forthcoming Jefferson Lab experiments.
