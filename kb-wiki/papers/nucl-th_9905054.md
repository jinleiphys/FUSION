# Large $p_t$ enhancement from freeze out

> Freeze out of particles across three dimensional space-time hypersurface is discussed in a simple kinetic model. The final momentum distribution of emitted particles, for freeze out surfaces with space-like normal, shows a non-exponential transverse momentum spectrum. The slope parameter of the $p_t$ distribution increases with increasing $p_t$, in agreement with recently measured SPS pion and $h^-$ spectra.

## Key claim

The final momentum distribution of particles emitted from a freeze-out surface with a space-like normal vector shows a non-exponential transverse momentum spectrum, where the slope parameter increases with increasing \(p_t\). This effect arises from the gradual freeze-out process in a kinetic model where the interacting component is instantly re-thermalized, and the resulting distribution is a superposition of cut Jüttner distributions from a series of slowing-down thermal sources.

## Method

The authors model freeze-out as a stationary, one-dimensional kinetic process in which particles cross a three-dimensional space-time hypersurface with a space-like normal vector. They assume an infinitely long tube initially filled with nuclear matter on one side and vacuum on the other; by continuously removing particles at the right end and supplying them on the left, a stationary flow is established. The momentum distribution is split into an interacting component (initially a Jüttner distribution) and a free component; particles freeze out with a probability proportional to \(\cos \theta_{\vec{p}}\) (the cosine of the angle relative to the surface normal), and the interacting component is assumed to instantly re-thermalize (relaxation length \(\lambda' \ll \lambda\)). The coupled differential equations for the two components are solved in the relaxation-time approximation, and the parameters of the interacting component (density, temperature, flow velocity) are updated at each step using conservation laws for baryon current and energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

* For a baryon-free, massless gas with initial temperature \(T_0 = 130\) MeV, the transverse momentum (here \(p_x\)) distribution is curved, with the apparent slope parameter increasing with increasing transverse momentum (Fig. 1).
* Approximately 95% of the matter is frozen out by a distance \(x = 3\lambda\) (where \(\lambda\) is the mean free path), so the distribution at this distance can be considered a first estimate of the post-freeze-out distribution.
* The model uses the equation of state \(e = \sigma_{SB} T^4\) for a baryon-free, massless gas.

## Context

This work extends the general theory of discontinuities in relativistic flow, originally developed by Taub (1948) for propagating hypersurfaces with space-like normals and generalized by Csernai (1987) to surfaces with time-like normals. It builds on the Cooper-Frye formula (1974) and recent suggestions for solving the problem of the post-freeze-out distribution (Bugaev 1996, Anderlik et al. 1998, Grassi et al. 1999). The predicted non-exponential transverse momentum spectrum and increasing slope parameter with \(p_t\) are in agreement with measured SPS pion and \(h^-\) spectra (NA44 and NA49 collaborations). The study indicates that a realistic freeze-out description may lead to large \(p_t\) enhancement, motivating more accurate dynamical calculations, especially for rapid hadronization of quark-gluon plasma with simultaneous freeze-out.
