# Holographic Pomeron: Saturation and DIS

> We briefly review the approach to dipole-dipole scattering in holographic QCD developed in ARXIV:1202.0831. The Pomeron is modeled by exchanging closed strings between the dipoles and yields Regge behavior for the elastic amplitude. We calculate curvature corrections to this amplitude in both a conformal and confining background, identifying the holographic direction with the virtuality of the dipoles. The it wee-dipole density is related to the string tachyon diffusion in both virtuality and the transverse directions. We give an explicit derivation of the dipole saturation momentum both in the conformal and confining metric. Our holographic result for the dipole-dipole cross section and the it wee-dipole density in the conformal limit are shown to be identical in form to the BFKL pomeron result when the non-critical string transverse dimension is $D_\perp=3$. The total dipole-dipole cross section is compared to DIS data from HERA.

## Key claim

The holographic dipole-dipole cross section and wee-dipole density in the conformal limit are identical in form to the BFKL pomeron result when the non-critical string transverse dimension is D_perp = 3, but with different pomeron intercept and diffusion constant values that depend on the strong coupling regime.

## Method

The authors model the Pomeron as closed string exchange between dipoles in holographic QCD, extending a previous stringy Schwinger mechanism analysis (Basar 2012) from flat space to curved AdS backgrounds. They calculate curvature corrections to the elastic amplitude in both conformal AdS and a confining hard-wall background, identifying the holographic direction z with the virtuality of the dipoles. The wee-dipole density is derived from string tachyon diffusion in curved space, and the saturation momentum is obtained from the condition that the eikonalized dipole-dipole cross section reaches order unity.

## Key numbers

* Holographic pomeron intercept: alpha_P - 1 = 1/4 - 1/(2*sqrt(lambda)) = 0.146, using lambda = 23
* Holographic diffusion constant: D = alpha_prime/2 = 1/(2*sqrt(lambda)) = 0.10, with alpha_prime = 1/sqrt(lambda)
* BFKL pomeron intercept: alpha_BFKL - 1 = 0.477, with lambda = 23
* BFKL diffusion constant: D_BFKL = 0.72, with lambda = 23
* String coupling: g_s = kappa * lambda / (4*pi*N_c), with kappa = 2.5, N_c = 3, lambda = 23
* Confinement scale: z_0 = 2 GeV^{-1}; target dipole size: z_T = 1.8 GeV^{-1}; s_0 = 10^{-1} GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the holographic Schwinger mechanism for dipole-dipole scattering developed by Basar (2012) from flat space to curved AdS backgrounds, connecting to the BFKL pomeron approach of Mueller (1989, 1994) and Gribov's diffusion picture. The holographic results for the wee-dipole density are compared to the BFKL 1-pomeron approximation and to improved BFKL results from Salam (1995). The saturation momentum derivation relates to earlier holographic saturation studies by Hatta (2007), Cornalba (2008), and Albacete (2008), and the total cross section is compared to DIS data from HERA.
