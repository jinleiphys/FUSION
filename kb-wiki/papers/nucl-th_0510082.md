# Alpha-nucleus potential for alpha-decay and sub-barrier fusion

> The set of parameters for alpha-nucleus potential is derived by using the data for both the alpha-decay half-lives and the fusion cross-sections around the barrier for reactions alpha+40Ca, alpha+59Co, alpha+208Pb. The alpha-decay half-lives are obtained in the framework of a cluster model using the WKB approximation. The evaluated alpha-decay half-lives and the fusion cross-sections agreed well with the data. Fusion reactions between alpha-particle and heavy nuclei can be used for both the formation of very heavy nuclei and spectroscopic studies of the formed compound nuclei.

# Alpha-nucleus potential for alpha-decay and sub-barrier fusion

## Key claim

The authors derive a global set of parameters for the alpha-nucleus nuclear potential that simultaneously describes both alpha-decay half-lives and sub-barrier fusion cross-sections, but the potential is validated only for three specific fusion reactions (alpha+40Ca, alpha+59Co, alpha+208Pb) and 367 ground-state-to-ground-state alpha-decay transitions, with the caveat that the fusion data for alpha+40Ca and alpha+59Co have lower accuracy and are given less weight in the parameter fitting.

## Method

The alpha-decay half-lives are calculated using a cluster model with the WKB approximation for barrier penetration, averaging partial widths over emission angles to account for deformation of daughter nuclei. The sub-barrier fusion cross-sections are computed using the same alpha-nucleus potential with a one-dimensional WKB approximation for energies below the barrier and a parabolic barrier approximation above it. The potential parameters (strength, radius, diffuseness) are determined by minimizing a weighted sum of squared deviations between theoretical and experimental values for 367 alpha-decay half-lives and fusion cross-section data for alpha+208Pb, with additional constraints from alpha+40Ca and alpha+59Co fusion data.

## Key numbers

- 367 alpha-decay transitions between ground states of parent and daughter nuclei were used, comprising 166 even-even, 84 even-odd, 67 odd-even, and 50 odd-odd parent nuclei
- The nuclear potential depth is given by V(A,Z,Q) = -[30.275 - 0.45838 Z/A^(1/3) + 58.270 I - 0.24244 Q] MeV, where I = (A-2Z)/A
- The diffuseness parameter d = 0.49290 fm
- The assault frequency factor xi = (6.1814 + 0.2988 A^(-1/6)) x 10^19 s^(-1) for spherical nuclei, modified by exp(-13.116 beta) for deformed nuclei
- The alpha-particle radius extracted from fitting is 1.5268 fm, close to the experimental value of 1.57 +/- 0.05 fm
- The difference between theoretical and experimental log10(T_1/2) values is smaller than 0.5 for most even-even, even-odd, and odd-even nuclei
- Alpha-decay half-lives span from approximately 10^(-7) s to approximately 10^(+25) s across the dataset
- For the reaction alpha+252Cf forming 256Fm, the fusion cross-section exceeds 0.2 microbarns at collision energies above approximately 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine the alpha-nucleus potential by combining alpha-decay and fusion data for the first time, addressing the lack of global potentials that fit both types of reaction data with good accuracy (as noted in the IAEA Reference Input Parameter Library). The authors compare their potential with parameterizations from Huizenga (various years), Nolte (various years), Avrigeanu (various years), Blendowske (various years), and Royer (various years), finding that their potential is shallower than those derived from high-energy reactions or M3Y nucleon-nucleon forces, which is attributed to Pauli repulsion at low energies. The results enable spectroscopy studies of heavy and superheavy compound nuclei using alpha-particle fusion reactions, as demonstrated for the 256Fm system.
