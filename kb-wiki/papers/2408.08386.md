# Quadrupole-Octupole Residual Interaction in the Proxy-$SU(3)$ Scheme

> The octupole deformation of atomic nuclei is a relevant research area given its implications in the nuclear structure and fundamental physics, however, inclusion of octupole degrees of freedom in the nuclear interaction has been explored little in $SU(3)$ symmetry-based models. In this article we expand the octupole operator $\mathcal{O}^3_μ$ in the $SU(3)$ second quantization formalism and use it to formulate an octupole-octupole residual interaction $\mathcal{O}^3\cdot \mathcal{O}^3$ which is incorporated in Elliott's model Hamiltonian. We compute the matrix elements of this extended Hamiltonian and use them to calculate energy levels and analyse the $B(E3; 3^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ transition strength of the isotope $^{224}$Th using the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM) based on the proxy-$SU(3)$ scheme.

## Key claim
The paper extends Elliott's SU(3) model by adding an octupole-octupole residual interaction to the Hamiltonian, but the improvement in reproducing the low-energy spectrum of 224Th is slight (mean square error of 0.1739 MeV2 versus 0.1180 MeV2 for a two-parameter model without octupole interaction), and the authors note that the octupole interaction has too little influence in the reduced model space used.

## Method
The octupole operator was expanded in SU(3) second quantization formalism for two-shell proton (shells 5,6) and neutron (shells 6,7) spaces in the actinide region. The scalar octupole-octupole interaction was constructed and its reduced matrix elements were computed using recoupling coefficients and single-shell reduced matrix elements, with computer codes integrating several SU(3) libraries. The Hamiltonian was applied to 224Th within the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM) using a truncated model space of 97 intermediate states from the ground band (60,8)0+ and octupole band (65,6)0-.

## Key numbers
- Harmonic oscillator strength: ℏω = 6.7318 MeV for 224Th
- Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength: χ = -0.0032
- Octupole-octupole interaction strength: τ = -0.0016
- Proton-neutron mixing parameter: α = 0.2398
- Mean square error of the three-parameter model: 0.1739 MeV2
- Mean square error of the two-parameter model from PhysRevC.101.054306: 0.1180 MeV2
- Mean square error of Elliott model alone: 0.1771 MeV2
- Collective model estimate for B(E3; 3-1 → 0+1) in 224Th: 77.6001 W.u.
- Experimental quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.174(6) for 224Th
- Calculated octupole deformation β3 = -0.153 for 224Th from Möller (2016)

## Context
This work extends the SU(3) symmetry-based approach to nuclear structure by incorporating octupole degrees of freedom, which had been explored little in such models. It builds on the SAQM framework developed by Cseh (2013) and the proxy-SU(3) scheme, and continues previous work on 224Th by Restrepo (2024) and PhysRevC.101.054306. The paper enables future studies of octupole collectivity in actinide nuclei using algebraic models, and the authors note that experimental measurement of the B(E3; 3-1 → 0+1) transition in 224Th is needed for further analysis of octupole effective charges.
