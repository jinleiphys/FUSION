# Kaon Condensation in Dense Stellar Matter

> This talk is based on work done in collaboration with G.E. Brown and D.-P. Min on kaon condensation in dense baryonic medium treated in chiral perturbation theory using heavy-baryon formalism. It contains, in addition to what was recently published, some new results based on the analysis on kaonic atoms by Friedman, Gal and Batty and a discussion on a renormalization-group analysis to meson condensation made together with H.K. Lee and Sin. Negatively charged kaons are predicted to condense at the critical density $2\lsim ρ/ρ_0\lsim 4$, in the range to allow all the intriguing new phenomena predicted by Brown and Bethe to take place in compact star matter.

## Key claim

Negatively charged kaons are predicted to condense in dense stellar matter at a critical density in the range \(2 \lesssim \rho/\rho_0 \lesssim 4\), but this result carries significant uncertainty from the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is not controlled by chiral symmetry. The calculation confirms the original Kaplan-Nelson prediction at tree order, but the authors note that the largest sensitivity comes from the part of the interaction that is not directly given by chiral Lagrangians.

## Method

The authors used heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) to compute kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes to next-to-next-to-leading (NNL) order, fixing the parameters from experimental scattering lengths and kaonic atom data from Friedman, Gal and Batty. They then constructed the in-medium kaon self-energy including two-loop contributions involving the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance and four-Fermi interactions, and determined the critical density for kaon condensation by extremizing the energy density of dense nuclear star matter under conditions of beta equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Critical density range: \(2 \lesssim \rho/\rho_0 \lesssim 4\) for kaon condensation in dense stellar matter
- Kaon effective mass at nuclear saturation density (\(u=1.0\), symmetric matter): \(M_K^* = 294.5\) MeV, with attraction \(\Delta V = -200.5\) MeV
- Kaon effective mass at \(u=3.0\): \(M_K^* = 153.0\) MeV, with attraction \(\Delta V = -342.0\) MeV
- Kaon effective mass at \(u=4.0\): \(M_K^* = 110.4\) MeV, with attraction \(\Delta V = -384.6\) MeV
- Four-Fermi interaction parameter determined from kaonic atoms: \((C_{\Lambda^\star}^S - C_{\Lambda^\star}^T) f^2 \approx 20\)
- Experimental scattering lengths used: \(a_0^{K^+p} = -0.31\) fm, \(a_0^{K^-p} = -0.67 + i 0.63\) fm, \(a_0^{K^+n} = -0.20\) fm, \(a_0^{K^-n} = +0.37 + i 0.57\) fm

## Context

This work extends the original Kaplan-Nelson (1986) prediction of kaon condensation in neutron matter at \(\rho \lesssim 3\rho_0\) by performing a calculation to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, confirming the earlier result while uncovering new physical elements. The predicted critical density range supports the Bethe-Brown (1994) scenario for the formation of light-mass black holes from compact stars exceeding \(M = 1.5 M_\odot\). The calculation uses the framework of Thorsson, Prakash and Lattimer (1994) for treating kaon condensation in dense matter, and incorporates the analysis of kaonic atoms by Friedman, Gal and Batty to fix four-Fermi interaction parameters. The main uncertainty comes from the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is not directly constrained by chiral symmetry.
