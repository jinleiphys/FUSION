# Empirical signatures of shape phase transitions in nuclei with odd nucleon numbers

> Nuclear level density at low excitation energies is proposed as an indicator of the first order phase transitions in nuclei. The new signature, a maximum value of the level density at the critical point, appears to be sensitive to the detailed way the phase transition takes place in different nuclear regions: it is consistent with phase coexistence in the N=90 region, and with a rapid crossing of the two phases, without their coexistence/mixing at the critical point in the N=60 region, respectively. Candidates for critical point nuclei are proposed for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, using correlations between relative excitation energies, and their ratios, for structures (bands) based on unique-parity orbitals.

# Empirical signatures of shape phase transitions in nuclei with odd nucleon numbers

## Key claim

The nuclear level density at low excitation energies, parameterized by the back-shifted Fermi gas model parameter $a$, shows a maximum at the critical point of first-order shape phase transitions, but this behavior is not universal: it is consistent with phase coexistence in the $N=90$ region (mass ~150) while in the $N=60$ region (mass ~100) the level density shows only a slope change without a maximum, indicating a rapid crossing of phases without coexistence or mixing.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimentally determined nuclear level densities for approximately 300 nuclei using the back-shifted Fermi gas model, extracting the level density parameter $a$ from fits to cumulative low-lying levels and neutron resonance spacings. They compared these empirical level densities with values predicted by a compact empirical formula involving shell corrections and pairing, and examined correlations between relative excitation energies and their ratios for unique-parity orbital bands in odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei to identify critical point candidates.

## Key numbers

- Level density parameter $a$ for nuclei near $N=90$ (Nd to Dy isotopes) is approximately 18 MeV$^{-1}$, compared to about 16.5 MeV$^{-1}$ for the average behavior of nuclei in that mass region
- The critical point in the $N=90$ region corresponds to $E(2^+_1) \approx 150$ keV in even-even cores, and to $E_c(j+2) \approx 200$ keV for the $\nu i_{13/2}$ unique-parity orbital in odd-mass nuclei
- The empirical formula for the level density parameter is $a = (0.199 + 0.0096 S') A^{0.869}$, where $S'$ incorporates shell corrections and pairing
- For the $N=60$ region, the level density parameter $a$ shows a change of slope at $N=60$ followed by nearly constant values, rather than a maximum

## Context

This work extends previous empirical approaches to shape phase transitions in odd-mass nuclei (Bucurescu 2016) and builds on IBM calculations predicting maximally compressed $0^+$ state spectra at the critical point (Cejnar 2003). The level density signature corroborates experimental findings of enhanced numbers of $0^+$ states in $^{154}$Gd and other X(5) critical point nuclei. The contrasting behavior between the $N=90$ and $N=60$ regions aligns with large-scale Monte Carlo shell model calculations for $^{96}$Zr to $^{110}$Zr (type II shell evolution) and Coulomb excitation studies of $^{96,98}$Sr showing weak mixing between coexisting configurations. The correlation method for identifying critical point odd-mass nuclei using unique-parity orbital bands extends the approach of von Brentano (2004) for even-even nuclei.
