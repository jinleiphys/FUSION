# Electric dipole moment of the deuteron in the standard model with $NN - ΛN - ΣN$ coupling

> We calculate the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the deuteron in the standard model with $|ΔS| =1$ interactions by taking into account the $NN - ΛN - ΣN$ channel coupling, which is an important nuclear level systematics. The two-body problem is solved with the Gaussian Expansion Method using the realistic Argonne $v18$ nuclear force and the $YN$ potential which can reproduce the binding energies of $^3_Λ$H, $^3_Λ$He, and $^4_Λ$He. The $|ΔS| =1$ interbaryon potential is modeled by the one-meson exchange process. It is found that the deuteron EDM is modified by less than 10\%, and the main contribution to this deviation is due to the polarization of the hyperon-nucleon channels. The effect of the $YN$ interaction is small, and treating $ ΛN$ and $ ΣN$ channels as free is a good approximation for the EDM of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The deuteron electric dipole moment (EDM) in the standard model, calculated with full $NN - \Lambda N - \Sigma N$ channel coupling, is modified by less than 10% compared to the previous calculation where hyperons were integrated out, confirming that treating the $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ channels as free is a good approximation for the deuteron EDM.

## Method

The two-body problem was solved using the Gaussian Expansion Method with the realistic Argonne $v18$ nuclear force and a $YN$ potential that reproduces the binding energies of $^3_\Lambda$H, $^3_\Lambda$He, and $^4_\Lambda$He. The $|\Delta S| = 1$ interbaryon potential was modeled by one-meson (pion, kaon, and eta meson) exchange, with the weak couplings calculated using the vacuum saturation approximation and next-to-leading logarithmic renormalization group evolution of the Wilson coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Total deuteron EDM: $d_d = 2.8 \times 10^{-31} e \, \text{cm}$ (central value)
- Final result with uncertainty: $d_d = (2.8 \pm 2.3) \times 10^{-31} e \, \text{cm}$
- Pion exchange contribution from $\Sigma N - NN$ channel: $3.50 \times 10^{-31} e \, \text{cm}$ (dominant contribution)
- Pion exchange contribution from $\Lambda N - NN$ channel: $-8.25 \times 10^{-32} e \, \text{cm}$
- Kaon exchange contributions: $\Lambda N - NN$ at $3.76 \times 10^{-32} e \, \text{cm}$, $\Sigma N - NN$ at $-3.31 \times 10^{-32} e \, \text{cm}$
- Eta exchange contributions: $\Lambda N - NN$ at $2.53 \times 10^{-33} e \, \text{cm}$, $\Sigma N - NN$ at $4.89 \times 10^{-33} e \, \text{cm}$
- Deuteron binding energy reproduced to 5 digits: 2.224536 MeV using Argonne $v18$ potential
- $^4_\Lambda$H and $^4_\Lambda$He binding energies reproduced as 2.33 MeV and 2.28 MeV, respectively
- $^3_\Lambda$H binding energy: $B = 0.19$ MeV (consistent with observed $0.13 \pm 0.05$ MeV)
- Estimated theoretical uncertainty: $0.8 d_d$ (approximately 67% from factorization, 25% from chiral perturbation theory, 20% from QCD renormalization)

## Context

This work extends the previous calculation by Yamanaka (2015) of the standard model contribution to the deuteron EDM via $|\Delta S| = 1$ interactions, which had integrated out the intermediate hyperon states using the on-shell Dirac equation. By explicitly including the $NN - \Lambda N - \Sigma N$ coupled channels, the author quantifies the nuclear-level systematics from hyperon-nucleon dynamics and polarization effects. The result enables future calculations for $^3$He and $^3$H EDMs, where similar relativistic corrections are expected, and opens the question of how Pauli exclusion effects may suppress EDMs in heavier $p$-shell nuclei such as $^6$Li and $^9$Be.
