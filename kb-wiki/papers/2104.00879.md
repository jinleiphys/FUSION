# Influence of non-statistical properties in nuclear structure on emission of prompt fission neutrons

> The Hauser-Feshbach Fission Fragment Decay (HF$^3$D) model is extended to calculate the prompt fission neutron spectrum (PFNS) for the thermal neutron induced fission on $^{235}$U, where the evaporated neutrons from all possible fission fragment pairs are aggregated. By studying model parameter sensitivities on the calculated PFNS, as well as non-statistical behavior of low-lying discrete level spin distribution, we conclude that discrepancies between the aggregation calculation and the experimental PFNS seen at higher neutron emission energies can be attributed to both the primary fission fragment yield distribution and the possible high spin states that are not predicted by the statistical theory of nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between calculated and experimental prompt fission neutron spectra (PFNS) at high outgoing neutron energies (above 5-7 MeV) can be attributed to both the primary fission fragment yield distribution and the possible presence of high-spin states in low-lying discrete levels that are not predicted by the statistical theory of nuclear structure, rather than to deficiencies in statistically averaged model parameters.

## Method

The authors extended the Hauser-Feshbach Fission Fragment Decay (HF\(^3\)D) model to perform a deterministic aggregation calculation of neutron evaporation from all fission fragment pairs for thermal neutron induced fission on \(^{235}\)U. They studied model parameter sensitivities by adjusting the primary fission fragment yield distribution, total kinetic energy, anisothermal parameter, spin distribution scaling, and charge distribution odd-even staggering, and separately investigated the effect of non-statistical spin distributions by artificially increasing unknown discrete-level spins by 4\(\hbar\) or 6\(\hbar\) or by scaling the spin cutoff parameter \(\sigma\) in the level density formula.

## Key numbers

- The average energies of the calculated PFNS were 1.95 MeV for the prior parameter set and 1.99 MeV for the posterior set, compared to a Maxwellian temperature of 1.32 MeV used for ratio plots.
- The posterior parameter set reproduced experimental PFNS data up to approximately 7 MeV, but still underestimated data at higher outgoing energies.
- Increasing unknown discrete-level spins by 4\(\hbar\) or 6\(\hbar\) significantly lifted the PFNS tail above 7 MeV, while the calculated prompt neutron multiplicity \(\overline{\nu}\) increased by less than 0.3%.
- Scaling the spin cutoff parameter \(\sigma_0\) by factors of 1.1, 1.2, and 1.5 lifted the PFNS tail but tended to overshoot experimental data near 6 MeV.
- The fission fragment pair with masses \(A_L=102\) and \(A_H=134\) gave the highest contribution to the total PFNS above 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the HF\(^3\)D model previously developed by Okumura (2018) and Lovell (2021) to address the long-standing "too soft PFNS" problem reported by Becker (2013) and Capote (2016), where Hauser-Feshbach aggregation calculations systematically underestimate the high-energy tail. The non-statistical spin distribution argument parallels the explanation proposed by Makii (2019) for the prompt fission gamma-ray spectrum. The results suggest that precise spectroscopic studies of neutron-rich fission fragments are essential to improve PFNS predictions and spectrum-average cross sections for high-threshold reactions such as (n,2n).
