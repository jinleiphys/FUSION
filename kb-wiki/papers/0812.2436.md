# Comment on "Observation of neutronless fusion reactions in picosecond laser plasmas"

> The paper by Belyaev et al. [Phys. Rev. E {\bf 72}, 026406 (2005)] reported the first experimental observation of alpha particles produced in the thermonuclear reaction $^{11}$B($p,α$)$^{8}$Be induced by laser-irradiation on a $^{11}$B polyethylene (CH$_2$) composite target. The laser used in the experiment is characterized by a picosecond pulse duration and a peak of intensity of 2$\times10^{18}$ W/cm$^2$. We suggest that both the background-reduction method adopted in their detection system and the choice of the detection energy region of the reaction products are possibly inadequate. Consequently the total yield reported underestimates the true yield. Based on their observation, we give an estimation of the total yield to be higher than their conclusion, i.e., of the order of 10$^5 α$ per shot.

# Comment on "Observation of neutronless fusion reactions in picosecond laser plasmas"

## Key claim

The authors argue that the background-reduction method and detection energy region used by Belyaev et al. (2005) were inadequate, causing the reported yield of alpha particles from the ¹¹B(p,α)⁸Be reaction to underestimate the true yield by at least a factor of 100; based on the same experimental data, they estimate the total yield to be of the order of 10⁵ alpha particles per shot.

## Method

The authors analyzed the energy spectrum of alpha particles from the ¹¹B(p,α)⁸Be reaction and used SRIM/TRIM simulations to calculate the transmission of alpha particles through 11 μm and 22 μm thick aluminum foils, which were used as filters on CR-39 track detectors in the original experiment. They then used the ratio of astrophysical S-factors for the two reaction channels (α₁ and α₀) to estimate the total fusion yield from the observed α₀ yield, assuming a plasma temperature in the range of 33 to 84 keV.

## Key numbers

- The laser intensity was 2×10¹⁸ W/cm² with a pulse duration of 1.5 ps and pulse energy up to 15 J.
- The reaction Q-values are 8.59 MeV for the α₀ channel (¹¹B + p → α₀ + ⁸Be) and 5.65 MeV for the α₁ channel (¹¹B + p → α₁ + ⁸Be*), with the ⁸Be* decay having Q = 3.028 MeV.
- Only 1% of reaction products are α₀ from the ground-state channel.
- A 11 μm Al foil stops alpha particles with energies lower than 3 MeV; a 22 μm Al foil stops alpha particles with energies lower than 5 MeV.
- The transmitted α₀ through 22 μm Al foil has an energy of about 1.27 ± 0.08 MeV, producing track diameters of about 11 μm on CR-39 detectors.
- The observed α₀ yield from Belyaev et al. (2005) was about 1.5×10³ per 4π Sr.
- The ratio of S-factors for α₁ to α₀ channels ranges from 100 to 170 for plasma temperatures from 33 to 84 keV.
- The estimated total fusion yield is more than 2×10⁵ fusions per shot, or 4×10⁴ using the averaged value over bursts reported by Belyaev et al. (2005).

## Context

This comment critiques the experimental methodology of Belyaev et al. (2005), who reported the first observation of alpha particles from the ¹¹B(p,α)⁸Be reaction in laser-induced plasmas. The work extends the discussion of aneutronic fusion reactions in laser plasmas, which had previously focused on D(d,n)³He reactions studied by Ditmire et al., Norreys et al., Disdier et al., Fritzler et al., Izumi et al., and Habara et al. The authors call for more precise measurements of angular distributions of reaction products, and their analysis suggests that the true fusion yield in such experiments may be substantially higher than previously reported, motivating improved detection schemes for future work.
