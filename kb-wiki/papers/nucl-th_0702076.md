# Pion-nucleon scattering within a gauged linear sigma model with parity-doubled nucleons

> We compute pion-nucleon scattering at tree-level within a gauged linear sigma model which contains the nucleon and its chiral partner. Such an investigation in principle allows to make definite predictions as to whether the main contribution to the nucleon mass comes from the chiral condensate or from the mixing with its chiral partner. We find that there seems to be no set of model parameters that allows for a simultaneous description of all experimentally measured scattering lengths and range parameters. This indicates the need to improve the dynamical ingredients of the model.

# Pion-nucleon scattering within a gauged linear sigma model with parity-doubled nucleons

## Key claim
The paper finds that no single set of model parameters simultaneously describes all experimentally measured pion-nucleon scattering lengths and range parameters at tree level, making it impossible to unambiguously determine whether the nucleon mass originates primarily from the chiral condensate or from mixing with its chiral partner. This conclusion is hedged by the caveat that the analysis is preliminary and depends on three poorly constrained parameters: the ratio Z = m_{a1}/m_rho, the sigma meson mass m_sigma, and the mass of the chiral partner N*.

## Method
The authors computed pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes at tree level within a gauged linear sigma model with U(2)_R × U(2)_L symmetry, incorporating scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector mesons alongside parity-doubled nucleon fields (N and N*) in the mirror assignment. They derived analytic expressions for the s-wave and p-wave scattering lengths and the effective s-wave range parameters at threshold, then compared these quantities as functions of the mixing parameter delta and the sigma meson mass to experimental data from precision X-ray experiments on pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Ratio Z = m_{a1}/m_rho ≈ 1.59 (from the model), with an alternative value Z = sqrt(2) from the KSFR relation
- The parameter m_0 (the mass in the chirally restored phase) was cited as approximately 800 MeV from prior work by Zschiesche
- For small m_sigma ≈ 400-600 MeV, a small value of delta is favored for a_0^{(+)} to match experiment; for large m_sigma, a large delta is required
- All p-wave scattering lengths were found to be too small in magnitude to compare reasonably with experimental data

## Context
This work extends the parity-doubled nucleon model of DeTar (1988) and Jido (2000) by computing pion-nucleon scattering as a test of the model's viability, following earlier applications to cold dense nuclear matter by Zschiesche (2006). The results indicate that the model requires improvement, such as inclusion of the Weinberg-Tomozawa term (needed to correctly reproduce the axial coupling constant g_A) and the Delta resonance, before definitive conclusions can be drawn about the origin of the nucleon mass. The study also highlights the unresolved experimental identification of the nucleon's chiral partner (the N*(1535) is one candidate, but a broad resonance near 1.2 GeV has also been proposed).
