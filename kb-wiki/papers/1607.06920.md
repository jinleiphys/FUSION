# Manifestation of nuclear cluster structure in Coulomb sums

> Experimental Coulomb sum values of 6^Li and 7^Li nuclei have been obtained, extending the earlier reported momentum transfer range of Coulomb sums for these nuclei up to q = 0.750 ... 1.625 fm^-1. The dependence of the Coulomb sums on the momentum transfers of 6^Li and 7^Li is shown to differ substantially from similar dependences for all the other nuclei investigated. Relationship between the nuclear cluster structure and Coulomb sums has been considered. The momentum transfer value, above which the Coulomb sum becomes constant, is found to be related to the cluster isolation parameter x, which characterizes the degree of nuclear clusterization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the momentum transfer value \(q_{\rm p}\) at which the Coulomb sum becomes constant is linearly proportional to the cluster isolation parameter \(x\), which quantifies the degree of nuclear clusterization; this relationship is established for nuclei with atomic mass \(A \geq 4\), but the authors note that the hypothesis was previously based only on the \(^6\)Li nucleus and required confirmation for \(^7\)Li.

## Method

The authors measured electron scattering cross sections on isotopically enriched \(^6\)Li (90.5%) and \(^7\)Li (93.8%) targets using the SP-95 facility at the NSC KIPT electron linear accelerator LUE-300, with beam energies from 104 to 259 MeV and scattering angles from 34.2° to 160°. From these measurements, they extracted longitudinal response functions \(R_{\rm L}(q,\omega)\) and computed Coulomb sums \(S_{\rm L}(q)\) by integrating over energy transfer, applying corrections for the nuclear electrostatic field and nucleon form factors. The momentum transfer \(q_{\rm p}\) was defined as the intersection of a horizontal line fitting the plateau of \(S_{\rm L}(q)\) and a line fitting the rising portion starting from \(S_{\rm L} \approx 2/3 S_{\rm L,p}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^7\)Li, the plateau onset momentum transfer is \(q_{\rm p} = 1.20 \pm 0.10\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For \(^6\)Li, the plateau onset momentum transfer is \(q_{\rm p} = 1.35 \pm 0.10\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For \(^{12}\)C, the plateau onset momentum transfer is \(q_{\rm p} = 1.65\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For \(^4\)He, \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(^{56}\)Fe, the \(q_{\rm p}\) values are grouped at \(q_{\rm p} = (1.9 \div 2.1)\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The cluster isolation parameter \(x\) for \(^6\)Li is between 0.3 and 0.4; for \(^7\)Li, \(x = 0.5\); for \(^{12}\)C, \(x\) ranges from 0.7 to 0.8; for non-clusterized nuclei (e.g., \(^4\)He), \(x = 1.0\).
* New Coulomb sum data were obtained for \(^6\)Li at \(q = 0.750 \div 1.000\) fm\(^{-1}\) and for \(^7\)Li at \(q = 0.750 \div 1.125\) fm\(^{-1}\), extending the previously reported range.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of Coulomb sums for \(^6\)Li by Buki et al. (2005, 2013) and for \(^7\)Li by Buki et al. (2013), which had suggested a lower \(q_{\rm p}\) for lithium isotopes compared to other nuclei but lacked sufficient data at low momentum transfers to confirm the relationship with cluster structure. The results contradict the expectation that \(q_{\rm p} \approx 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) for all nuclei, as observed for \(^4\)He, \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(^{56}\)Fe in Saclay and Bates data (1990s), and instead show a linear dependence of \(q_{\rm p}\) on the cluster isolation parameter \(x\) defined by Fujiwara et al. (1980). The finding enables future determination of clusterization degree from Coulomb sum measurements, and the authors suggest that measuring \(^9\)Be (\(x = 0.6\)) at \(q = 0.8 \div 1.7\) fm\(^{-1}\) would provide a further test of the proposed linear relationship.
