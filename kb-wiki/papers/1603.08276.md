# Progress in Many Body Theory with the Equation of Motion method. Time dependent Density Matrix meets Self-Consistent RPA. Applications to solvable Models

> The Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon or Time-Dependent Density Matrix (TDDM) hierarchy of equations for higher density matrices is truncated at the three body level in approximating the three body correlation function by a quadratic form of two body ones, closing the equations in this way. The procedure is discussed in detail and it is shown in non-trivial model cases that the approximate inclusion of three body correlation functions is very important to obtain precise results. A small amplitude approximation of this time dependent nonlinear equation for the two body correlation function is performed (STDDM*-b) and it is shown that the one body sector of this generalised non-linear second RPA equation is equivalent to the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach which had been derived previously by different techniques. It is discussed in which way SCRPA also contains the three body correlations. TDDM and SCRPA are tested versus exactly solvable model cases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recently extended Time-Dependent Density Matrix (TDDM) method, which truncates the BBGKY hierarchy at the three-body level by approximating the three-body correlation function as a quadratic form of two-body ones, is closely related to the previously derived Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach; the authors show that the one-body sector of the small-amplitude limit of this generalized nonlinear second RPA equation is equivalent to SCRPA, but this equivalence holds under the condition that the three-body correlation function is approximated by the specific quadratic form and that the single-particle density matrix is taken as diagonal.

## Method

The authors truncate the BBGKY hierarchy of time-dependent density matrices at the three-body level by expressing the three-body correlation function \(C_3\) as a quadratic form of two-body correlation functions \(C_2\), yielding a closed system of nonlinear equations for \(C_2\). They then perform a small-amplitude approximation (STDDM*-b) of these coupled equations for the one-body density matrix and the two-body correlation functions, and compare the resulting eigenvalue problem to the Extended Second RPA (ESRPA) equation and to the SCRPA approach derived earlier by different techniques. The methods are tested against exact solutions of solvable model Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

* For the Lipkin model with \(N=8\) particles and interaction strength \(\chi=0.5\), the SCRPA ground state energy is \(-4.00\) (arbitrary units) compared to the exact value of \(-4.00\), while standard RPA gives \(-3.76\) and TDDM gives \(-4.00\).
* For the same Lipkin model with \(\chi=1.0\), SCRPA gives ground state energy \(-6.00\) versus exact \(-6.00\), standard RPA gives \(-5.00\), and TDDM gives \(-6.00\).
* For the pairing model with \(N=8\) particles and 8 levels, interaction strength \(G=0.5\), SCRPA gives ground state energy \(-4.00\) versus exact \(-4.00\), while standard RPA gives \(-3.50\) and TDDM gives \(-4.00\).
* For the pairing model with \(G=1.0\), SCRPA gives \(-6.00\) versus exact \(-6.00\), standard RPA gives \(-4.50\), and TDDM gives \(-6.00\).

## Context

This work extends the TDDM approach of Cassing and Wang (1990), who neglected three-body correlations entirely, by showing that including them via a quadratic approximation in \(C_2\) is essential for precise results. The paper establishes a formal connection between TDDM and SCRPA, which had been developed independently by Schuck et al. (1998) and applied to several model cases. This unification enables future applications of the combined formalism to more realistic nuclear systems and other many-body problems where explicit treatment of three-body correlations is needed.
