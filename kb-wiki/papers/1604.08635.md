# Statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory with width fluctuation correction including direct reaction channels for neutron induced reaction at low energies

> A model to calculate particle-induced reaction cross sections with statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory including direct reactions is given. The energy average of scattering matrix from the coupled-channels optical model is diagonalized by the transformation proposed by Engelbrecht and Weidenmüller. The ensemble average of $S$-matrix elements in the diagonalized channel space is approximated by a model of Moldauer [Phys.Rev.C {\bf 12}, 744 (1975)] using newly parametrized channel degree-of-freedom $ν_a$ to better describe the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) reference calculations. Moldauer approximation is confirmed by a Monte Carlo study using randomly generated $S$-matrix, as well as the GOE three-fold integration formula. The method proposed is applied to the $^{238}$U(n,n') cross section calculation in the fast energy range, showing an enhancement in the inelastic scattering cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical method to include direct reaction channels in statistical Hauser-Feshbach calculations with width fluctuation correction, showing that the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation combined with a newly parametrized channel degree-of-freedom \(\nu_a\) produces cross sections nearly identical to the rigorous Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) triple-integral formula, provided the number of open channels is not extremely small.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the energy-averaged coupled-channels optical model S-matrix using the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation, then apply Moldauer's width fluctuation correction model in the diagonalized channel space with a newly parametrized channel degree-of-freedom \(\nu_a\) from Kawano (2014). They validate the approximation by Monte Carlo simulations using randomly generated S-matrices with 2 to 7 channels, comparing against the GOE triple-integral reference. The method is applied to neutron-induced reactions on \(^{238}\)U in the fast energy range using the coupled-channels Hauser-Feshbach code CoH\(_3\) with a dispersive coupled-channels optical potential.

## Key numbers

- For \(\Lambda=2\) channels, the standard deviation of the Moldauer approximation from the GOE reference is 0.83%; for \(\Lambda=5\) channels, it is 0.29%.
- The \(^{238}\)U(n,n') inelastic scattering cross sections to the \(2^+\), \(4^+\), \(6^+\), and \(8^+\) states show enhancement when the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation is applied compared to the generalized transmission coefficient method.
- Changes in capture and fission cross sections due to the transformation are less than 2%.
- The average resonance spacing for \(^{239}\)U used in level density adjustment is \(D_0 = 20.26 \pm 0.72\) eV.
- Deformation parameters used for \(^{238}\)U are \(\beta_2 = 0.214\), \(\beta_4 = 0.00931\), and \(\beta_6 = -0.0148\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Moldauer (1975) and the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation (1973) to provide a practical alternative to the computationally prohibitive Nishioka-Weidenmüller-Yoshida (NWY) theory (1989) based on GOE. It builds on the Monte Carlo study of Kawano (2014) for spherical nuclei and the preliminary \(^{238}\)U study by Capote (2014). The method enables realistic cross section calculations for deformed actinides and rare-earth nuclei where static deformation is large, and can be implemented in existing codes such as Empire, TALYS, CCONE, and CoH\(_3\).
