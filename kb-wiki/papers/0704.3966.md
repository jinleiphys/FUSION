# Dick Dalitz: Examples of His Contributions to Particle Physics

> Dick Dalitz produced lasting contributions to particle physics for 50 years. Along with Dalitz pairs, Dalitz plots and CDD poles, he did major work in parity violation, hyperon resonance identification, the quark model, and hypernuclei. These topics will be summarized, with some emphasis on the author's collaboration with Dalitz on quark spin and fragmentation correlations, and, particularly, the work on the identification of the top quark and its mass. Short biographical information is interspersed. This paper is based on a presentation at the International School of Subnuclear Physics, Erice 2006.

# Dick Dalitz: Examples of His Contributions to Particle Physics

## Key claim
This biographical review argues that Richard Dalitz's most influential contributions to particle physics, including the Dalitz plot, Dalitz pairs, and CDD poles, emerged from his characteristic approach of beginning with experimental data and puzzling phenomena, then developing thorough phenomenological analyses. The paper emphasizes that Dalitz's later work on top quark mass determination using a geometrical probability method, while yielding an estimate around 160 GeV with a large spread, was on the low side of the currently reported mass nearer to 174 GeV.

## Method
The paper surveys Dalitz's career through his published work and collaborations, with particular focus on the author's own collaboration with Dalitz on quark spin and fragmentation correlations and on top quark identification. For the top quark mass determination, the method constructs probability distributions for each candidate event by imposing 4-momentum conservation constraints in momentum space, where allowed top and antitop momenta form intersecting ellipses for each assumed mass value, and Standard Model probabilities are assigned to each intersection.

## Key numbers
- The top quark mass estimate from the Dalitz-Goldstein method was around 160 GeV, compared to the currently reported mass nearer to 174 GeV
- The top quark lifetime scales as 1/m_t^5 in the Standard Model, becoming comparable to strong decay timescales for masses above approximately 100 GeV
- The LEP lower limit on the top quark mass was 100 GeV
- The Σ*(1385) resonance was discovered in 1960 using Dalitz plot analysis
- The Ξ*(1530) resonance was also discovered via Dalitz plot methods
- The Λ(1405) hyperon was predicted by Dalitz and Tuan to lie below the K⁻ + p threshold
- The τ meson was determined to have quantum numbers 0⁻ (even spin, odd parity) from Dalitz plot analysis of 29 decay events

## Context
This review summarizes Dalitz's contributions spanning 50 years, extending from his early work on nuclear 0→0 transitions in ¹⁶O through the development of the Dalitz plot for the τ-θ puzzle, which directly enabled Lee and Yang's 1956 proposal that parity is not conserved in weak processes. The paper connects Dalitz's symmetric quark model (presented at Les Houches in 1965) to the later SU(6) unification by Sakita and by Gürsey and Radicati, and notes that the top mass determination method was subsequently used by the D0 collaboration to further constrain the top mass and set limits on the Higgs mass. The work on quark spin fragmentation correlations led to Collins's later development of the Collins function for transversely polarized quarks.
