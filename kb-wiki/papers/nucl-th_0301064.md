# Nuclear structure calculations with a separable approximation for Skyrme interactions

> A finite rank separable approximation for the quasiparticle RPA calculations with Skyrme interactions that was proposed in our previous work is extended to take into account the coupling between one- and two-phonon terms in the wave functions of excited states. It is shown that characteristics calculated within the suggested approach are in a good agreement with available experimental data.

# Nuclear structure calculations with a separable approximation for Skyrme interactions

## Key claim
The finite-rank separable approximation for quasiparticle RPA calculations with Skyrme interactions is extended to include coupling between one- and two-phonon terms in the wave functions of excited states, and the calculated characteristics show reasonable agreement with available experimental data, though the QRPA alone overestimates both energies and transition probabilities for the $2^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ states in $^{112}$Sn.

## Method
The authors start from the Skyrme interaction SIII parametrization and compute the single-particle spectrum via Hartree-Fock (HF) with the continuum discretized on a harmonic oscillator basis. The particle-hole residual interaction is approximated by its Landau-Migdal form keeping only $l=0$ terms, then expressed as a sum of $N$ separable terms using a Gauss integration formula with $N=45$ points. The QRPA equations are solved in this finite-rank separable form, and the wave functions of excited states are constructed as a mixture of one-phonon and two-phonon configurations, with the coupling matrix elements computed from the QRPA phonon amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- $N=45$ is sufficient for multipolarities $\lambda \le 3$ in nuclei with $A\le 208$; increasing to $N=60$ in $^{208}$Pb does not change energies or transition probabilities practically.
- For $^{112}$Sn: QRPA gives $2^+_1$ energy 1.49 MeV and B(E2) 2600 $e^2$fm$^4$; including two-phonon terms gives 0.90 MeV and 2200 $e^2$fm$^4$ (experiment: 1.26 MeV, 2400$\pm$140 $e^2$fm$^4$).
- For $^{112}$Sn: QRPA gives $3^-_1$ energy 2.73 MeV and B(E3) 97000 $e^2$fm$^6$; including two-phonon terms gives 1.90 MeV and 72000 $e^2$fm$^6$ (experiment: 2.36 MeV, 87000$\pm$12000 $e^2$fm$^6$).
- The single-particle spectra are cut off at 190 MeV, which exhausts practically all the energy-weighted sum rule.
- The BCS subspace includes all subshells lying below 5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the finite-rank separable approximation for Skyrme interactions previously developed by the same authors (Severyukhin et al., 1998; Severyukhin et al., 2002) from QRPA to include two-phonon coupling, analogous to the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev (1992) but with all parameters derived from Skyrme forces rather than being phenomenological. The approach enables structure calculations in very large configuration spaces by reducing matrix dimensions to at most 40 even for giant resonance calculations in heavy nuclei, whereas non-separable methods would require diagonalizing matrices of order a few thousand. The results for $^{112}$Sn show that two-phonon coupling reduces the overestimate of energies and transition probabilities from QRPA alone, bringing them into reasonable agreement with experimental data from Raman (2001) and Speidel (2002).
