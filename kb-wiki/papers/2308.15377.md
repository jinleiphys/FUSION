# Unraveling anomalies in Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> We calculate the one-loop quark box diagrams relevant to polarized and unpolarized Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering by introducing an off-forward momentum $l^μ$ as an infrared regulator. This regularization approach allows us to reveal the poles associated with the chiral anomaly in the polarized scenario, as well as the trace anomaly in the unpolarized case. We provide an interpretation of our findings in the context of pertinent Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs). Furthermore, we discuss the implications of these poles on the QCD factorization pertaining to Compton amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors show that one-loop quark box diagrams for both polarized and unpolarized Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) contain poles proportional to \(1/l^2\) when computed in off-forward kinematics with an infrared regulator \(l^\mu\), but these poles are not a threat to QCD factorization because they are an intrinsic part of the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) themselves and must be absorbed into the GPDs.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop quark box diagrams for the Compton scattering amplitude \(T^{\mu\nu}\) in off-forward kinematics, using an off-forward momentum \(l^\mu\) as an infrared regulator in the region \(\Lambda^2_{\rm QCD} \ll l^2 \ll Q^2\). They separated the amplitude into antisymmetric (polarized) and symmetric (unpolarized) parts, evaluated the diagrams using the Mathematica package `Package-X`, and interpreted the resulting pole structures in terms of GPDs \(\tilde{H}, \tilde{E}\) for the polarized case and \(H, E\) for the unpolarized case.

## Key numbers

* The polarized box diagram yields a pole term proportional to \(1/l^2\) with coefficient \(\delta C_g^{\rm anom}(\hat{x}) = 4T_R(1-\hat{x})\) where \(T_R = 1/2\).
* The polarized splitting function is \(\Delta P_{qg}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R(2\hat{x}-1)\).
* The unpolarized box diagram yields a pole term in \(\bar{F}^{\rm off}_1\) with kernel functions \(K(\hat{x},\hat{\xi}) = 2T_R \frac{\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})}{1-\hat{\xi}^2}\) and \(L(\hat{x},\hat{\xi}) = 2T_R \frac{\hat{x}(\hat{\xi}-\hat{x})}{1-\hat{\xi}^2}\).
* The unpolarized splitting function is \(P_{qg}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R((1-\hat{x})^2+\hat{x}^2)\).
* The calculation is performed in the kinematic region \(\Lambda^2_{\rm QCD} \ll l^2 \ll Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Jaffe and Manohar (1989) that the axial anomaly appears as a pole in momentum transfer in off-forward kinematics, and revitalizes it following recent work by Tarasov and Venugopalan (2020, 2021) who demonstrated the box diagram pole using the worldline formalism. The authors connect the polarized pole to the GPD \(\tilde{E}\) and the unpolarized pole to the GPDs \(H\) and \(E\), showing that the anomaly poles are intrinsic to the GPDs and must be cancelled nonperturbatively, analogous to the mechanism responsible for the \(\eta'\) meson mass generation via the Veneziano mechanism (1979). This enables future studies of anomaly manifestations in exclusive processes and Lattice QCD.
