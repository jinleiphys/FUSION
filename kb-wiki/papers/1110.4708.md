# Compact stars within an asy-soft quark-meson-coupling model

> We investigate compact star properties within the quark meson coupling model (QMC) with a soft symmetry energy density dependence at large densities. In particular, the hyperon content and the mass/radius curves for the families of stars obtained within the model are discussed. The hyperon-meson couplings are chosen according to experimental values of the hyperon nuclear matter potentials, and possible uncertainties are considered. It is shown that a softer symmetry energy gives rise to stars with less hyperons, smaller radii and larger masses. Hyperon-meson couplings may also have a strong effect on the mass of the star.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a nonlinear omega-rho coupling in the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model softens the symmetry energy at high densities, which reduces the hyperon fraction in compact stars and increases the maximum stellar mass; however, the effect on maximum mass depends on both the symmetry energy slope and the uncertain hyperon-nuclear potentials, particularly for the cascade (Xi) hyperon.

## Method

The authors extend the QMC model by adding a nonlinear omega-rho coupling term (parameterized by Lambda_v) to the energy density, which modifies the isovector channel and softens the symmetry energy at large densities. They solve the mean-field equations for baryonic matter composed of the eight lightest baryons (n, p, Lambda, Sigma+, Sigma0, Sigma-, Xi0, Xi-) under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, using MIT bag parameters fixed to nuclear saturation properties. The hyperon-omega couplings are determined from experimental hyperon nuclear matter potentials (V_Lambda = -28 MeV, V_Sigma = 30 MeV, V_Xi = -18 MeV), with variations for the cascade potential tested at -10 MeV and 0 MeV. The resulting equations of state are input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation: 33.70 MeV for QMC (Lambda_v=0), decreasing to 27.78 MeV for QMComega-rho with Lambda_v=0.1
- Symmetry energy slope L at saturation: 93.59 MeV for QMC (Lambda_v=0), decreasing to 39.04 MeV for QMComega-rho with Lambda_v=0.1
- Maximum star mass for QMC with hyperons (V_Xi=-18 MeV): 1.776 solar masses (Lambda_v=0), increasing to 1.888 solar masses (Lambda_v=0.1)
- Maximum star mass for QMComega-rho with Lambda_v=0.1 and V_Xi=0 MeV: 1.969 solar masses
- Radius of maximum mass star for QMC with hyperons (V_Xi=-18 MeV): 12.657 km (Lambda_v=0), decreasing to 12.345 km (Lambda_v=0.1)
- Crust-core transition density for QMC: 0.076 fm^-3, increasing to 0.098 fm^-3 for QMComega-rho with Lambda_v=0.1
- Hyperon-omega couplings: x_omega_Lambda=0.743, x_omega_Sigma=1.04, x_omega_Xi=0.346 (for V_Xi=-18 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the QMC model of Guichon (1988) by introducing a nonlinear omega-rho coupling similar to that used by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) in nonlinear Walecka models, allowing a softer symmetry energy at high densities. The study builds on prior work by Santos et al. (2009) on the QMC model with delta-meson inclusion, which produced a stiffer symmetry energy. The results are compared with the NL3 model of Lalazissis et al. (1997) and the density-dependent TW model of Typel and Wolter (1999), showing that the modified QMC behaves more like TW in terms of crust-core transition densities and distillation effects. The paper addresses the recent mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses) by Demorest et al. (2010) and the empirical equation of state from Steiner et al. (2010), demonstrating that hyperonic stars can reach such masses if the cascade potential is weakly attractive. This enables further studies of how both symmetry energy and hyperon couplings affect neutron star properties, particularly the maximum mass and radius.
