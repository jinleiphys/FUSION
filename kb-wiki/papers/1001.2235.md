# Formation of $ϕ$ mesic nuclei

> We study the structure and formation of the $ϕ$ mesic nuclei to investigate the in-medium modification of the $ϕ$-meson spectral function at finite density. We consider (${\bar p},ϕ$), ($γ,p$) and ($π^-,n$) reactions to produce a $ϕ$-meson inside the nucleus and evaluate the effects of its medium modifications to the reaction cross sections. We also estimate the consequences of the uncertainties of the ${\bar K}$ selfenergy in medium to the $ϕ$-nucleus interaction. We find that it may be possible to see a peak structure in the reaction spectra for the strong attractive potential cases. On the other hand, for strong absorptive interaction cases with relatively weak attractions, it is very difficult to observe clear peaks and we may need to know the spectrum shape in a wide energy region to deduce the properties of $ϕ$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that observing clear peak structures in missing-mass spectra for phi mesic nuclei will be difficult unless the real part of the phi-nucleus optical potential is at least as attractive as -70 MeV at normal nuclear density, which is significantly deeper than the -7.5 MeV predicted by the theoretical model based on kaon-loop contributions.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation with a complex optical potential derived from the phi meson selfenergy, using the local density approximation and a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution. They calculate bound-state binding energies and widths for four nuclei (11B, 39K, 123In, 207Tl) and then compute formation spectra for three proton pick-up reactions: (p-bar,phi), (gamma,p), and (pi-,n), using the Green's function method within the impulse approximation. The theoretical optical potential from Ref. [ref:8] is scaled to simulate deeper potentials corresponding to a 3% mass reduction suggested by QCD sum rules and KEK data.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical optical potential at the nuclear center: Re V_opt^phi(0, E=m_phi) ~ -7.5 MeV, with absorptive part of similar strength
- Deep potential scaled to Re V_opt^phi(0, E=m_phi) = -30 MeV to simulate 3% mass reduction
- For 11B with deep potential: 1s state binding energy 10.2 MeV, width 17.9 MeV (excluding free decay width 4.26 MeV)
- For 207Tl with shallow potential: 1s state binding energy 3.73 MeV, width 22.5 MeV
- For 207Tl with deep potential: 1s state binding energy 40.1 MeV, width 17.5 MeV
- Clear peak structures appear only when |Re V_opt^phi(0, E=m_phi)| >= 70 MeV
- Momentum transfer for (p-bar,phi) reaction is always larger than 100 MeV/c in the plotted energy range

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of mesic nuclei (pionic, kaonic) to the phi meson, motivated by QCD sum rules and KEK data (Refs. [ref:5, ref:6]) suggesting a 3% mass reduction at normal nuclear density, while earlier theoretical work by Refs. [ref:7, ref:8] predicted a much weaker attractive potential based on kaon-loop effects through the OZI rule. The results indicate that standard missing-mass experiments will likely fail to observe phi mesic nuclei unless the attractive potential is far stronger than current theoretical predictions, redirecting attention to sub-threshold meson production as a more promising avenue for studying phi meson properties in nuclear matter.
