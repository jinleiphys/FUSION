# SU3 isoscalar factors

> A summary of the properties of the Wigner Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and isoscalar factors for the group SU3 in the SU2$\otimes$U1 decomposition is presented. The outer degeneracy problem is discussed in detail with a proof of a conjecture (Braunschweig's) which has been the basis of previous work on the SU3 coupling coefficients. Recursion relations obeyed by the SU3 isoscalar factors are produced, along with an algorithm which allows numerical determination of the factors from the recursion relations. The algorithm produces isoscalar factors which share all the symmetry properties under permutation of states and conjugation which are familiar from the SU2 case. The full set of symmetry properties for the SU3 Wigner-Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and isoscalar factors are displayed.

# SU3 isoscalar factors

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete algorithm for numerically determining SU3 isoscalar factors (ISFs) from recursion relations, which produces ISFs that share all the Racah symmetry properties under permutation of states and conjugation familiar from SU2, provided a specific sign convention and degeneracy resolution scheme (based on Braunschweig's conjecture) is adopted. The algorithm works for couplings of arbitrary outer degeneracy, not just degeneracy one, and is independent of the particular scheme chosen for outer degeneracy resolution.

## Method

The author derives four-term recursion relations for SU3 isoscalar factors by applying the SU3 ladder operators V_+, U_-, V_-, and U_+ to composite states of highest weight and using the linearity of the generators. For couplings to a state of highest weight (k=p+q, l=0), two recursion relations are obtained from the vanishing of V_+ and U_- on that state; two additional relations allow stepping down to arbitrary (k,l) values. The algorithm initializes ISFs for the highest s-value (where s = k1-l1+k2-l2) using Kronecker delta assignments for each degeneracy index n, then uses the recursion relations to determine all remaining ISFs, with Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization to resolve degeneracies greater than one. A C language implementation was developed to evaluate ISFs as floating point values and as exact square roots of rational numbers.

## Key numbers

- Dimension of an SU3 irrep (p,q): d = (p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)/2
- Degeneracy η of the coupling (p1,q1) ⊗ (p2,q2) → (p,q) is given by η = max(η′+1−max(γ,σ),0) where η′ = min(p1+σ, p2+σ, q+σ, q1+γ, q2+γ, p+γ, 2(σ+γ), p1+q1−γ−σ, p2+q2−γ−σ) and γ ≡ (p1+p2−p)/3, σ ≡ (q1+q2−q)/3
- The number of non-vanishing WCGs of the form coupling two states where one is highest weight is shown to be ≥ the degeneracy η, proving Braunschweig's conjecture
- Normalization condition for ISFs: sum over k1,l1,k2,l2 of [F(SHW:k1,l1;k2,l2)]² = 1
- Phase factor for (1↔2) transformation: (−1)^(γ+σ+max(γ,σ)+n)
- Phase factor for conjugation transformation: (−1)^(γ+σ+min(γ,σ)+n)
- Phase factor for (1↔3̃) transformation includes √[(p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)]/[(p1+1)(q1+1)(p1+q1+2)]

## Context

This work extends the program of Biedenharn and coworkers (Biedenharn 1960s) who developed canonical SU3 unit tensor operators, and addresses the outer degeneracy problem that distinguishes SU3 from SU2 recoupling. The paper proves Braunschweig's conjecture, which had been used by Braunschweig (1970s), LeBlanc (1980s), and Biedenharn (1980s) in prior work on SU3 coupling coefficients. The algorithm produces ISFs with full Racah symmetries, unlike the canonical coefficients of Biedenharn which lack permutation symmetry. This enables simplified calculations of physical states, reduced complexity in defining SU3 6-j and 9-j type recoupling coefficients, and shorter databases and printed tables of WCGs and ISFs. The work uses the phase convention of de Swart (1963) for the generators and the Condon and Shortley phase convention for SU2 Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.
