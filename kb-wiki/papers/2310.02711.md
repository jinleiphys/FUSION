# Anisotropy of magnetized quark matter

> Strong transient magnetic fields are generated in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions. These fields induce anisotropy within the strongly interacting medium that, in principle, can affect the thermodynamic properties of the medium. We use the Polyakov loop extended Nambu Jona-Lasinio model to study the quark matter subjected to an external magnetic field at vanishing baryon chemical potential ($μ_{B}$). We have estimated the degree of anisotropy in the speed of sound and isothermal compressibility within the magnetized quark matter as a function of temperature ($T$) and magnetic field ($eB$). This study helps us to understand the extent of directionality generated in the initial stages of non-central collisions while giving us useful information about the system.

## Key claim

The paper uses the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant to study anisotropy in the speed of sound and isothermal compressibility of quark matter at vanishing baryon chemical potential, finding that the degree of anisotropy can reach 20% to 60% depending on magnetic field strength, but only within the model's specific parameterization that reproduces inverse magnetic catalysis.

## Method

The authors employ a 2+1 flavor PNJL model with a three-momentum cutoff regularization scheme and a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant G_B(eB) fitted to reproduce lattice QCD chiral transition temperatures. They compute the thermodynamic potential, then derive longitudinal and transverse components of pressure, speed of sound, and isothermal compressibility by minimizing the potential with respect to the quark condensates, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate. The anisotropic speed of sound is obtained from derivatives of pressure with respect to energy density along constant entropy-per-number-density paths, while isothermal compressibility splits into longitudinal and transverse components via the magnetization.

## Key numbers

- The three-momentum cutoff parameter is set to Lambda = 602.3 MeV, with coupling constants G*Lambda^2 = 1.835 and K*Lambda^5 = 12.36, and current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 140.7 MeV, fixed to reproduce m_pi = 135 MeV, m_K = 497.7 MeV, m_eta' = 957.8 MeV, and f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- The Polyakov loop potential parameters are a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75, with T_0 = 190 MeV.
- For eB = 0.2 GeV^2, anisotropy in squared speed of sound is around 20% at T = 0.1 GeV; for eB = 0.4 GeV^2, anisotropy is around 60% at the same temperature.
- The dip in speed of sound shifts to lower temperatures with increasing magnetic field, consistent with decreasing deconfinement temperature.
- At high temperature, squared speed of sound approaches the ideal gas limit of 1/3.

## Context

This work extends studies of anisotropic thermodynamic properties in magnetized quark matter, following prior PNJL model calculations by Ferreira et al. (2014) and Yang et al. (2021) that explored the equation of state and critical endpoint in the presence of magnetic fields. It also connects to lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (2014) showing inverse magnetic catalysis, and to experimental estimates of speed of sound from CMS data at zero magnetic field. The results enable future comparisons with peripheral heavy-ion collision data where strong transient magnetic fields are expected, potentially providing a probe of magnetic field effects on the quark-gluon plasma evolution.
