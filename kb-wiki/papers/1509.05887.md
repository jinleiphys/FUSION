# Dileptons in a coarse-grained transport approach

> We calculate dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions using a coarse-graining approach to the simulation of the created medium with the UrQMD transport model. This enables the use of dilepton-production rates evaluated in equilibrium quantum-field theory at finite temperatures and chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their coarse-grained transport approach, which maps UrQMD Monte-Carlo simulations onto a local thermal equilibrium state, successfully describes dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions across a range of beam energies. The key hedge is that the method relies on the assumption that the fireball evolution is close to local thermal equilibrium, and the temperature and baryo-chemical potential are extracted via an equation of state using an anisotropic-fluid ansatz to account for pressure anisotropy in the early stages.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model to simulate an ensemble of heavy-ion collision events, then coarse-grained the energy-momentum tensor and net-baryon-number current on a space-time grid. From these, they extracted a temperature and baryo-chemical potential using the Eckart definition of the local rest frame and an equation of state that matches lattice-QCD results to a hadron-resonance gas. They then combined this bulk-medium evolution with state-of-the-art in-medium dilepton-production rates from equilibrium quantum-field theory at finite temperatures and chemical potentials to calculate dilepton invariant-mass and transverse-momentum spectra.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the parton-hadron transition: \(T_c \simeq 170 \; \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Dimuon spectra calculated for \(158 A \, \mathrm{GeV}\) In+In collisions at the CERN SPS, compared to NA60 data.
- Dielectron spectra calculated for \(1.76 A \, \mathrm{GeV}\) Ar+KCl collisions at the GSI SIS, compared to HADES data.
- Prediction provided for \(1.23 A \, \mathrm{GeV}\) Au+Au collisions at the GSI SIS, also measured by HADES.

## Context

This work extends the line of research using macroscopic fireball parametrizations (e.g., blast-wave fits by Rapp (2013)) and detailed hydrodynamics simulations (e.g., Teaney (2001), Hirano (2002), Kolb (2003)) by instead using a microscopic transport model (UrQMD) to describe the fireball evolution. It enables the use of equilibrium many-body quantum-field theoretical dilepton rates (Rapp and Wambach (1999), Gale and Rapp (1999)) in a transport framework, and the authors plan to apply the model to RHIC energies and photon production in heavy-ion collisions.
