# Inverse Potentials for all l-channels of Neutron-Proton Scattering using Reference Potential Approach

> Reference potential approach (RPA) is successful in obtaining inverse potentials for weakly bound diatomic molecules using Morse function. In this work, our goal is to construct inverse potentials for all available l-channels of np-scattering using RPA. The Riccati-type phase equations for various l-channels are solved using 5th order Runge-Kutta method to obtain scattering phase shifts (SPS) in tandem with an optimization procedure to minimize mean squared error (MSE). Interaction potentials for a total of 18 states have been constructed using only three parameter Morse interaction model. The obtained MSE is < 1% for 1S0 , 3P1 and 3D1 channels and < 2% for 1P1 channel and < 0.1% for rest of the 14 channels. The obtained total scattering cross-sections at various lab energies are found to be matching well with experimental ones. This phase wave analysis study of all channels of np-scattering using RPA has been undertaken using Morse function as zeroth reference, by us, is for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors construct inverse potentials for all 18 l-channels of neutron-proton scattering using the reference potential approach (RPA) with a three-parameter Morse function as the zeroth reference, achieving mean squared errors below 1% for three channels and below 0.1% for 14 channels, but the method produces purely exponential (repulsive) rather than Morse-shaped potentials for states with purely negative scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The Riccati-type phase equations for each l-channel (l = 0 to 5) were solved using a 5th order Runge-Kutta method to obtain scattering phase shifts, coupled with an iterative optimization procedure that minimized the mean squared error between calculated and experimental phase shifts from the Granada group (Perez et al., 2016). The Morse potential parameters V0, rm, and am were adjusted for each of the 18 spin-orbit coupled states, with an additional data point at 0.1 MeV lab energy from Arndt (private communication) included for the 3S1 and 1S0 states to improve low-energy scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- Mean squared error below 1% for 1S0, 3P1, and 3D1 channels; below 2% for the 1P1 channel; below 0.1% for the remaining 14 channels
- For the 1S0 state: V0 = 70.438 MeV, rm = 0.901 fm, am = 0.372 fm; scattering length a = -23.390 fm (experimental: -23.749(8) fm), effective range re = 2.42 fm (experimental: 2.81(5) fm)
- For the 3S1 state: V0 = 114.153 MeV, rm = 0.841 fm, am = 0.350 fm; scattering length a = 5.356 fm (experimental: 5.424(3) fm), effective range re = 1.75 fm (experimental: 1.760(5) fm)
- Total scattering cross-sections calculated from 0.1 MeV to 350 MeV lab energy, with contributions from S, P, D, F, G, and H partial waves

## Context

This work extends the reference potential approach previously applied by the same group to S-waves of np scattering (Sastri, year not specified), nD scattering (Shikha, year not specified), and n-alpha and alpha-alpha scattering (Kumar L and Khachi A, years not specified). It uses the Granada group phase shift data (Perez et al., 2016) as the experimental reference, and contrasts with more complex phenomenological potentials such as Nijmegen (Nagels, year not specified), Argonne v18 (Wiringa, year not specified), CD-Bonn (Machleidt, year not specified), and Granada (Perez, year not specified) potentials. The work enables a simple three-parameter description of all np scattering channels and opens the question of extending RPA to proton-proton scattering.
