# Attractive Holographic Baryons

> We propose a holographic model of baryon interactions based on non-supersymmetric D7-anti-D7 flavor branes embedded in the Klebanov-Strassler background. The baryons are D3-branes wrapping the 3-sphere of the conifold with M strings connecting the D3 and the flavor branes. Depending on the location of the latter there are two possibilities: the D3 either remains separate from the flavor branes or dissolves in them and becomes a flavor instanton. The leading order interaction between the baryons is a competition between the attraction and the repulsion due to the sigma and omega mesons. The lightest 0++ particle sigma is a pseudo-Goldstone boson associated with the spontaneous breaking of scale invariance. In a certain range of parameters it is parametrically lighter than any other massive state. As a result at large distances baryons attract each other. At short distances the potential admits a repulsive core due to an exchange of the omega vector meson. We discuss baryon coupling to glueballs, massive mesons and pions and point out the condition for the model to have a small binding energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a holographic model of baryon interactions where, in a certain range of parameters, the lightest scalar meson (sigma) is parametrically lighter than the vector meson (omega), leading to an attractive force at large distances; the model can produce a small binding energy through near-cancellation of attractive and repulsive forces, but this requires the scalar coupling to be tuned small near the antipodal configuration where the D7-branes reach the tip of the conifold.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model using non-supersymmetric D7-anti-D7 flavor branes embedded in the Klebanov-Strassler background, with baryons represented as D3-branes wrapping the S^3 of the conifold connected by M strings to the flavor branes. They analyze two regimes: for large separation between the D7-branes and the conifold tip (r0 >> r_epsilon), the D3-brane sits near the tip with stretched strings; for small separation (r0 near r_epsilon), the D3 dissolves into the D7-branes and becomes a flavor instanton. The interaction potential between baryons is computed from exchanges of the lightest scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) mesons, with masses estimated from the geometry of the D7 embedding and the breaking of scale invariance.

## Key numbers

* Glueball mass scale: m_gb ~ r_epsilon / (alpha' lambda), where lambda = g_s M
* Meson mass scale (for r0 >> r_epsilon): m_meson ~ (r0 / r_epsilon) * m_gb
* Pseudo-Goldstone sigma mass: m_sigma ~ (r_epsilon^2 / r0^2) * m_gb
* Baryon mass: m_baryon = (r_epsilon M) / alpha'
* Binding energy for large r0 regime: E_binding ~ (1/g_s) * m_vector ~ r0 / (g_s^2 M alpha')
* Binding energy for r0 near r_epsilon: E_binding ~ kappa * r_epsilon / (g_s^2 M alpha'), where kappa is a small dimensionless number

## Context

This work extends holographic approaches to baryon interactions beyond the Sakai-Sugimoto model, where the scalar meson is always heavier than the vector meson resulting in net repulsion (Kaplunovsky, 2009). The model builds on the D7-anti-D7 embedding in the Klebanov-Strassler background (Dymarsky, Klebanov, Sonnenschein, 2009) and the Klebanov-Witten conformal case (Klebanov and Witten, 1998). The key advance is that the sigma meson can be parametrically lighter than the omega meson, enabling an attractive potential at large distances, unlike in the Sakai-Sugimoto construction. The paper enables future detailed computation of the meson spectrum near the antipodal point (r0 = r_epsilon) to determine whether the natural near-cancellation of forces can explain the small nuclear binding energy.
