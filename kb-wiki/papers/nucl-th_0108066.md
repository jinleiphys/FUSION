# Neutron electric form factor at large momentum transfer

> Based on the recent, high precision data for elastic electron scattering from protons and deuterons, at relatively large momentum transfer $Q^2$, we determine the neutron electric form factor up to $Q^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$. The values obtained from the data (in the framework of the nonrelativistic impulse approximation) are larger than commonly assumed and are in good agreement with the Gari-Krümpelmann parametrization of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

# Neutron electric form factor at large momentum transfer

## Key claim

The neutron electric form factor $G_{En}$ at momentum transfers up to $Q^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$ is larger than commonly assumed and does not vanish at large $Q^2$, but this extraction relies on the nonrelativistic impulse approximation for deuteron elastic scattering and on the assumption that the proton electric form factor follows either a dipole form or a faster-decreasing parametrization fitted to recent data.

## Method

The authors extract $G_{En}$ by inverting the expression for the deuteron elastic scattering structure function $A(Q^2)$ in the nonrelativistic impulse approximation, using experimental $A(Q^2)$ data from Jefferson Lab and earlier measurements together with the Paris deuteron wave function. They assume a dipole form for the magnetic nucleon form factors $G_{Mp}$ and $G_{Mn}$ and test two parametrizations for the proton electric form factor $G_{Ep}$: a standard dipole and a modified dipole with an additional mass term $m_x^2=5.88$ GeV$^2$ fitted to the recoil polarization data of Jones et al. (2000). The isoscalar electric form factor $G_{Es}=G_{Ep}+G_{En}$ is solved from the quadratic equation for $A(Q^2)$, and $G_{En}$ is then obtained by subtracting the chosen $G_{Ep}$.

## Key numbers

- $G_{En}$ is extracted up to $Q^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$, the maximum momentum transfer of the Jefferson Lab proton form factor data used.
- The proton electric form factor is parametrized as $G_{Ep}=G_D/(1+Q^2/m_x^2)$ with $m_x^2=5.88$ GeV$^2$, where $G_D=1/(1+Q^2/0.71\text{ GeV}^2)^2$.
- The magnetic form factors are taken as $G_{Mp}/\mu_p=G_{Mn}/\mu_n=G_D$ with $\mu_p=2.79$ and $\mu_n=-1.91$, valid at the 3% level up to $Q^2\simeq 10$ GeV$^2$.
- At $Q^2\simeq 2$ GeV$^2$, $G_{En}$ becomes larger than $G_{Ep}$.
- A direct measurement at $Q^2=0.67$ GeV$^2$ by Rohe et al. (1999) gives $G_{En}=0.052\pm 0.011\pm 0.005$, consistent with the extracted values.
- Replacing the dipole $G_{Ep}$ with the faster-decreasing parametrization changes $A(Q^2)$ by up to 40% at $Q^2=4$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Platchkov et al. (1990), which extracted $G_{En}$ only up to $Q^2=0.7$ GeV$^2$, by using new high-precision Jefferson Lab data for elastic electron-deuteron scattering (Alexa et al., 1999; Abbott et al., 1999) and the recoil polarization measurement of the proton form factor (Jones et al., 2000). The results agree with the Gari-Krumpelmann (1992) parametrization of nucleon form factors and contradict the common assumption that $G_{En}$ is negligible at large momentum transfer. The analysis enables future direct measurements of $G_{En}$ planned at Jefferson Lab up to $Q^2=2$ GeV$^2$ (Madey, JLab proposal PR-93-038) to be compared with these indirect extractions, and it motivates a reexamination of meson exchange current corrections in deuteron physics at large momentum transfer.
