# Role of quark-interchange processes in evolution of mesonic matter

> We divide the cross section for a meson-meson reaction into three parts. The first part is for the quark-interchange process, the second for quark-antiquark annihilation processes and the third for resonant processes. Master rate equations are established to yield time dependence of fugacities of pions, rhos, kaons and vetor kaons. The equations include cross sections for inelastic scattering of pions, rhos, kaons and vector kaons. Cross sections for quark-interchange-induced reactions, that were obtained in a potential model, are parametrized for convenient use. The number densities of pion and rho (kaon and vector kaon) are altered by quark-interchange processes in equal magnitudes but opposite signs. The master rate equations combined with the hydrodynamic equations for longitudinal and transverse expansion are solved with many sets of initial meson fugacities. Quark-interchange processes are shown to be important in the contribution of the inelastic meson-meson scattering to evolution of mesonic matter.

## Key claim

Quark-interchange processes are shown to be important in the contribution of inelastic meson-meson scattering to the evolution of mesonic matter, and the authors argue that if resonances and quark-antiquark annihilation processes are included, quark-interchange processes should be included on an equal footing. The claim is conditional on the specific mesonic matter composition considered (pions, rhos, kaons, and vector kaons only) and on the neglect of 2-to-1 reactions and meson decays in the core analysis.

## Method

The authors decompose the cross section for each meson-meson reaction into three parts: quark-interchange, quark-antiquark annihilation, and resonant processes, with no interference assumed between them. Master rate equations for the fugacities of pions, rhos, kaons, and vector kaons are established, incorporating thermal-averaged cross sections and coupled to Bjorken's scaling solution for longitudinal hydrodynamic expansion. Cross sections for seven quark-interchange-induced reactions, previously calculated in a potential model using the Buchmuller-Tye potential and constituent-constituent interaction, are parametrized for convenient use in the rate equations.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX Collaboration ratios at mid-rapidity in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: pi-/pi+ = 0.984, K-/K+ = 0.933, K+/pi+ = 0.171, K-/pi- = 0.162
- STAR Collaboration found rho0/pi- = 0.169 in peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Spin-isospin degeneracy factors: g=3 for pi, 9 for rho, 2 for K or anti-K, 6 for K* or anti-K*
- The relation P=0.15 epsilon is used for the equation of state in the hydrodynamic equation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of cross sections for seven quark-interchange-induced reactions (Li, 2007) by incorporating them into a dynamical evolution model of mesonic matter. It contrasts with earlier approaches that used effective numbers of pions and kaons (Gavai, 1991; Prakash, 1999; Song, 1997) to approximate evolution, and with transport models (RQMD, ART, HSD, UrQMD, ARC, PACIAE) that often rely on constant cross sections or approximate isospin relations when experimental data are unavailable. The study enables more precise modeling of hadronic matter evolution in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, which is necessary to subtract hadronic backgrounds when identifying deconfined matter signals.
