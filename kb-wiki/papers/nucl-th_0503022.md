# Two-scale scalar mesons in nuclei

> We generalize the linear sigma model in order to develop a chiral-invariant model of nuclear structure. The model is natural, and contains not only the usual sigma meson which is the chiral partner of the pion but also a new chiral-singlet that is responsible for the medium-range nucleon-nucleon attraction. This approach provides significant advantages in terms of its description of nuclear matter and finite nuclei in comparison with conventional models based on the linear sigma model.

## Key claim

The authors construct a chiral-invariant nuclear model that requires two distinct scalar mesons, a heavy sigma meson (the chiral partner of the pion) and a light chiral-singlet scalar meson, to simultaneously satisfy chiral symmetry and reproduce nuclear saturation properties, but only if an additional coupling term is introduced to fix the incompressibility.

## Method

The linear sigma model is generalized by adding a chiral-singlet scalar field (s) that couples to the nucleon and a repulsive omega meson. The sigma field is shifted to its vacuum expectation value, and the Mexican-hat potential is retained. A new chiral-invariant term coupling the omega field to the s field is added to control the effective omega mass and achieve a realistic incompressibility. The model is solved in the mean-field approximation for symmetric nuclear matter and for finite nuclei (40Ca) using an iterative procedure.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon: E/A - M = -15.7 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility target: K = 210 ± 30 MeV
- For m_σ = 1.0–1.05 GeV, the s meson mass falls in the desired range of 400–500 MeV
- For 40Ca with ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³: m_σ = 0.92 GeV, m_s = 404.0 MeV, g_s² = 18.07, ζ = 0.7, K = 218 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca: E_B/A = -8.28 MeV (observed: -8.45 MeV)
- Root-mean-square charge radius for 40Ca: r_ch = 3.44 fm (observed: 3.48 fm)
- Neutron-proton radius difference for 40Ca: r_n - r_p = -0.157 fm (observed: 0.05 ± 0.05 fm)
- Free masses used: M = 939 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model approach to nuclear structure, which historically suffered from bifurcations in the equation of state (Boguta, 1983; Matsui, 1981) and unrealistic oscillating charge densities (Tang, 1993). It builds on the chiral nuclear model of Chanfray et al. (2003) and the broken-scale-invariance approach of Furnstahl et al. (1997), which also required two scalar scales. The model enables future studies of unstable nuclei and neutron stars by including the rho meson, and it connects to the QMC model (Guichon, 1988; Saito, 1996) through the scalar polarizability and the possibility of omega-nucleus bound states.
