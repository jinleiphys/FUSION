# Systematic study of hadronic excitation energy using Schottky anomaly

> We extract the excitation energy scales of the hadron spectra in a less model-dependent method using Schottky anomaly. Schottky anomaly is a thermodynamical phenomenon that the specific heat of a system consisting of a finite number of energy levels has a peak at finite temperature due to the energy gaps. Using the masses of all hadrons that are experimentally established, we obtain the excitation energy scales of the hadron spectra and investigate their flavor dependence.

## Key claim

The authors extract excitation energy scales of hadron spectra using Schottky anomaly, a thermodynamic method applied to observed hadron masses, and find that the orbital excitation energy scale is approximately 400-450 MeV across all meson systems, showing flavor independence. The method carries an inherent uncertainty of about 20% in the extracted energy scales due to the finite number of observed states and the approximation of treating the hadron spectrum as a two-state system.

## Method

The authors construct canonical partition functions from experimentally established hadron masses listed by the Particle Data Group, including degeneracy factors from total angular momentum and isospin. They compute the specific heat as a function of temperature from the partition function, identify the temperature at which the Schottky peak occurs, and extract the excitation energy scale using the relation ΔE = 2.4 T_peak, which holds exactly for a two-state system. The method is applied separately to charmonia, bottomonia, open charm mesons, open bottom mesons, and light mesons, with further decomposition by spin configuration, strangeness, and isospin to identify the origins of different peaks.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium: excitation energies of 106 MeV (hyperfine splitting) and 441 MeV (orbital excitation) from the full spectrum; for s=0 states, orbital excitation at 453 MeV; for s=1 states, orbital excitation at 424 MeV
- Bottomonium: excitation energies of 51 MeV (hyperfine splitting) and 445 MeV (orbital excitation) from the full spectrum; for s=0 states, orbital excitation at 446 MeV; for s=1 states, orbital excitation at 437 MeV
- Open charm (all states): excitation energies of 108 MeV and 344 MeV; for D_s mesons: 119 MeV and 353 MeV; for D mesons: 143 MeV and 338 MeV
- Open bottom (all states): excitation energies of 41 MeV and 367 MeV; for B_s mesons: 40 MeV and 386 MeV; for B mesons: 39 MeV and 378 MeV
- Light mesons (excluding Nambu-Goldstone bosons and exotic candidates): excitation energies of 13 MeV and 432 MeV; after isospin decomposition: 401 MeV (I=0), 450 MeV (I=1), 438 MeV (I=1/2)
- For the charmonium model calculation with string tension k=0.9 GeV/fm, the 1P state lies at 430 MeV above the 1S ground state; the extracted energy using states up to 1P gives 362 MeV, about 20% lower

## Context

This work extends the method of Ref. (Schotano) which first applied Schottky anomaly to heavy meson spectra for identifying exotic hadrons, and also builds on earlier work by Ref. (7) that used specific heat to extract effective degrees of freedom in hadrons. The authors systematically apply the method to all observed hadrons, finding that the orbital excitation energy scale of approximately 400-450 MeV is common across heavy quarkonia, open heavy mesons, and light mesons, suggesting a universal confinement scale. This enables future studies to identify hadrons with anomalous excitation patterns that may indicate exotic structures or different effective degrees of freedom.
