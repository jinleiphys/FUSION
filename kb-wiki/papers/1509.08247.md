# Heavy Vector and Axial-Vector Mesons in Hot and Dense Asymmetric Strange Hadronic Matter

> We calculate the effects of finite density and temperature of isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter, for different strangeness fractions, on the in-medium properties of vector $\left( D^{\ast}, D_{s}^{\ast}, B^{\ast}, B_{s}^{\ast}\right)$ and axial-vector $\left( D_{1}, D_{1s}, B_{1}, B_{1s}\right)$ mesons, using chiral hadronic SU(3) model and QCD sum rules. We focus on the evaluation of in-medium mass-shift and shift in decay constant of above vector and axial-vector mesons. In QCD sum rule approach, the properties, e.g., the masses and decay constants of vector and axial-vector mesons are written in terms of quark and gluon condensates. These quark and gluon condensates are evaluated in the present work within chiral SU(3) model, through the medium modification of, scalar-isoscalar fields $σ$ and $ζ$, the scalar-isovector field $δ$ and scalar dilaton field $χ$, in the strange hadronic medium which includes both nucleons as well as hyperons. As we shall see in detail, the masses and decay constants of heavy vector and axial-vector mesons are affected significantly due to isospin asymmetry and strangeness fraction of the medium and these modifications may influence the experimental observables produced in heavy ion collision experiments. The results of present investigations of in-medium properties of vector and axial-vector mesons at finite density and temperature of strange hadronic medium may be helpful for understanding the experimental data from heavy-ion collision experiments in-particular for the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment of FAIR facility at GSI, Germany.

# Heavy Vector and Axial-Vector Mesons in Hot and Dense Asymmetric Strange Hadronic Matter

## Key claim
The masses and decay constants of heavy vector (D*, Ds*, B*, Bs*) and axial-vector (D1, D1s, B1, B1s) mesons are significantly modified in isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite density and temperature, with the magnitude of these modifications depending on the isospin asymmetry parameter and strangeness fraction of the medium, which may influence observables in heavy-ion collision experiments at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment.

## Method
The authors combine a chiral SU(3) hadronic model with QCD sum rules to evaluate in-medium properties of heavy mesons. The chiral SU(3) model provides the medium-modified scalar quark condensates (⟨ūu⟩, ⟨dd̄⟩, ⟨s̄s⟩) and scalar gluon condensate (⟨αs/π G²⟩) through the scalar fields σ, ζ, δ and the dilaton field χ, solved in mean-field approximation for a hadronic medium containing nucleons and hyperons at finite density and temperature. These condensates are then used as input to the Borel-transformed QCD sum rule equations for vector and axial-vector mesons, from which the unknown parameters a and b are extracted to compute the mass shift and shift in decay constant via the forward scattering amplitude formalism.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density adopted: 0.15 fm⁻³
- Coupling constants g(D*NΛc), g(D*NΣc), and equivalent for all vector and axial-vector mesons: 3.86 (average value from light cone QCD sum rules)
- Quark masses used: u = 0.005 GeV, d = 0.007 GeV, s = 0.095 GeV, c = 1.35 GeV, b = 4.7 GeV
- Vacuum masses of vector mesons: D*+ = 2.01 GeV, D*0 = 2.006 GeV, B*0 = 5.325 GeV, B*+ = 5.323 GeV, Ds* = 2.112 GeV, Bs* = 5.415 GeV
- Vacuum masses of axial-vector mesons: D1+ = 2.423 GeV, D10 = 2.421 GeV, B10 = 5.723 GeV, B1+ = 5.721 GeV, D1s = 2.459 GeV, B1s = 5.828 GeV
- Vacuum decay constants: f(D*) = 0.270 GeV, f(B*) = 0.195 GeV, f(D1) = 0.305 GeV, f(B1) = 0.255 GeV; strange meson decay constants taken as 1.16 times the non-strange values
- Continuum threshold parameters s0: D* = 6.5 GeV², B* = 35 GeV², Ds* = 7.5 GeV², Bs* = 38 GeV², D1 = 8.5 GeV², B1 = 39 GeV², D1s = 9.5 GeV², B1s = 41 GeV²
- Light quark condensate ⟨q† i D0 q⟩ = 0.18 GeV² ρB; strange quark condensate ⟨q† i D0 q⟩ = 0.018 GeV² ρB

## Context
This work extends previous studies of heavy meson properties in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules and chiral models. It builds on the authors' earlier work (Kumar and Chhabra, 2015) on scalar, vector and axial-vector mesons in symmetric nuclear matter, and on studies of pseudoscalar D and B mesons in strange matter using the chiral SU(3) model generalized to SU(4) and SU(5) sectors. The approach differs from coupled-channel methods (Tolos et al., various years) and from QCD sum rule studies in symmetric matter (Wang et al., various years; Hilger et al., various years) by including both isospin asymmetry and strangeness fraction simultaneously, as well as finite temperature effects through the temperature dependence of the scalar fields. The results are intended to provide input for interpreting data from the CBM experiment at FAIR and for understanding charmed mesic nuclei formation.
