# Resonances and the thermonuclear reaction rate

> We present an approximate analytic expression for thermonuclear reaction rate of charged particles when the cross section contains a single narrow or wide resonance described by a Breit-Wigner shape. The resulting expression is uniformly valid as the effective energy and resonance energy coalesce. We use our expressions to calculate the reaction rate for $^{12}$C(p,$γ$)$^{13}$N.

## Key claim

The paper presents an approximate analytic expression for the thermonuclear reaction rate of charged particles that remains uniformly valid even when the effective energy of the Gamow window and the resonance energy coalesce, unlike conventional approximations that fail in this regime.

## Method

The authors start from the standard Breit-Wigner cross section for a single resonance and introduce a uniform asymptotic expansion using the method of Dingle and Bleistein, transforming the integration variable to obtain an expression involving the complementary error function. They compare this uniform approximation to the conventional resonance-plus-tail approximation, which separately estimates the resonance and Gamow window contributions, for the reaction 12C(p,gamma)13N.

## Key numbers

* The resonance parameters used for 12C(p,gamma)13N are: resonance energy Er = 457 keV, total width Gamma_r approximately equal to Gamma_p(Er) = 39 keV (both laboratory frame values), and cross section sigma(Er) = 130 microbarns.
* The binding energy of 8B used as an illustrative example of a sub-threshold pole is EB = 137.5 keV.
* The effective width of the Gamow window in units of kBT is given by Delta_x = 4 sqrt(x0/3), where x0 = E0/kBT.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous asymptotic expansion for the thermonuclear reaction rate (Ueda 2000) by developing a uniform approximation that remains valid when the resonance energy approaches the Gamow peak energy, a regime where the conventional resonance-plus-tail approximation fails. The method addresses limitations in the compilations of Angulo (1999) and Iliadis (2001), which used simpler approximations for narrow resonances, and the results differ from those of Nelson (2000) due to resonance inclusion. The uniform approximation enables more reliable rate calculations for reactions where the resonance energy lies near the Gamow window, as demonstrated for 12C(p,gamma)13N.
