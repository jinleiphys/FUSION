# Chiral Corrections to Baryon Electromagnetic Form Factors

> Corrections motivated by chiral symmetry arguments have long been known to give important contributions to hadronic observables, particularly at low momentum transfer. It is possible to separate these approaches into two broad groups; either the corrections are implemented at the parton level, or at the hadron level. We explore the results of incorporating pion loop corrections at the hadron level to a calculation of electromagnetic form factors in the NJL model. These calculations are compared with the result of an earlier implementation of pion loops at the parton level using the same NJL model formalism. A particular parameter set yields a good description of low energy nucleon properties within both approaches. However, for the $Σ^-$ there is a remarkable improvement when the chiral corrections are implemented at the hadronic level.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while implementing pion loop corrections at the hadron level versus the parton level yields comparable results for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, the hadron-level implementation produces a dramatic improvement for the Sigma- magnetic moment, reducing it by roughly 30% compared to the parton-level calculation and bringing it into excellent agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization to calculate the bare electromagnetic form factors of nucleons and Sigma hyperons as quark-diquark bound states. They then incorporated pion loop corrections at the hadron level using the light-front cloudy bag model (LFCBM) formalism developed by Miller (2002), which ensures the correct leading non-analytic behavior required by chiral symmetry. The results were compared with an earlier implementation where pion loops were evaluated at the parton level on individual valence quarks, using the same NJL model formalism.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: 2.78 mu_N (LFCBM), compared to experimental value 2.793 mu_N
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.71 mu_N (LFCBM), compared to experimental value -1.913 mu_N
- Sigma- magnetic moment: -1.17 mu_N (LFCBM), compared to -1.58 mu_N from the previous parton-level NJL calculation and experimental value -1.160(25) mu_N
- Sigma+ magnetic moment: 2.33 mu_N (LFCBM), compared to experimental value 2.458(10) mu_N
- Proton charge radius: 0.89 fm (LFCBM), compared to experimental value 0.84 fm from Pohl (2010)
- Neutron charge radius: 0.41 fm (LFCBM), compared to experimental value 0.335 fm
- Sigma- charge radius: 0.78 fm (LFCBM), compared to experimental value 0.780 fm
- Sigma+ charge radius: 0.88 fm (LFCBM), compared to lattice QCD result 0.61(8) fm from Shanahan (2014)
- Self-energy contribution from N to N pi process fixed at 130 MeV, corresponding to regulator mass Lambda = 0.72 GeV
- NJL model parameters: Lambda_IR = 0.24 GeV, Lambda_UV = 0.67 GeV, m_l = 0.35 GeV, m_s = 0.52 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model calculations of baryon electromagnetic form factors by Cloet (2014) and Carrillo-Serrano (2016), which included pion loops at the parton level, and compares them with hadron-level chiral corrections using the light-front cloudy bag model of Miller (2002). The study addresses the long-standing question of how to correctly implement chiral symmetry in quark models, following earlier work by Thomas and Krein (1999, 2000) showing that parton-level implementations can yield incorrect leading non-analytic behavior. The results enable more reliable predictions for hyperon electromagnetic form factors, which are difficult to measure experimentally due to short lifetimes, and provide guidance for future lattice QCD calculations and experimental programs at facilities studying baryon structure.
