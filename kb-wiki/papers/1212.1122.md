# The low lying modes of triplet-condensed neutron matter and their effective theory

> The condensation of neutrons into a 3P2 superfluid phase occurs at densities relevant for the interior of neutron stars. The triplet pairing breaks rotational symmetry spontaneously and leads to the existence of gapless modes (angulons) that are relevant for many transport coefficients and to the star's cooling properties. We derive the leading terms of the low energy effective field theory, including the leading coupling to electroweak currents, valid for a variety of possible 3P2 phases.

## Key claim

The paper derives the low-energy effective field theory for the gapless Goldstone modes (angulons) that arise from spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry in triplet-condensed (3P2) neutron matter, and provides explicit numerical coefficients for the kinetic terms and interactions in Phase A, assuming the quasiparticle interactions are weakly coupled.

## Method

The authors start from a microscopic model of non-relativistic neutrons with an attractive short-range potential in the 3P2 channel, introduce an auxiliary field for the condensate, and integrate out the fermions. They perform a derivative expansion of the resulting one-loop effective action to second order in derivatives, keeping only the leading terms in the small ratio of the pairing gap to the Fermi energy. The Goldstone boson fields (angulons) are introduced as spacetime-dependent rotations of the order parameter, and the effective Lagrangian is evaluated explicitly for Phase A, where the condensate has eigenvalues (-1/2, -1/2, 1).

## Key numbers

* The angulon velocities along the x,y axes are approximately 0.477 v_F and 0.709 v_F for the two modes, and along the z axis approximately 0.519 v_F for both modes.
* The angulon contribution to the specific heat is c_v ≈ 16.16 T^3 / v_F^3, which evaluates to 1.44 × 10^(-13) (T/K)^3 / (v_F/c)^3 erg/(K cm^3).
* The effective theory is valid for densities around 1.5 times nuclear matter density, where the neutron pairing gap is below approximately 2 MeV and much smaller than the Fermi energy.

## Context

This work extends the earlier angulon effective theory of Bedaque (2003) by deriving the effective Lagrangian directly from a microscopic model rather than assuming a unitary order parameter (Phase B), and by providing explicit numerical coefficients for Phase A. The results enable calculations of transport coefficients and neutrino emission rates in neutron star interiors, relevant to interpreting the rapid cooling of the Cassiopeia A neutron star observed by Heinke (2010) and interpreted as evidence for triplet pairing by Page (2010) and Shternin (2010). The paper also addresses the question raised by Leinson (2012) about the existence of angulons, and provides a framework that can be extended to other 3P2 phases once the ground state is determined.
