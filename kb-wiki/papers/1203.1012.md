# Monitoring parton equilibration in heavy ion collisions via dilepton polarization

> In this note we discuss how angular distribution of the dileptons produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC/LHC energies can provide an information about a degree of local equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma produced at different invariant mass regions.

# Monitoring parton equilibration in heavy ion collisions via dilepton polarization

## Key claim

The angular distribution (polarization) of dileptons produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC/LHC energies can serve as an experimental probe of the degree of local equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma, with the invariant mass of the dilepton acting as a "clock" that selects different stages of the collision, but the direct translation of stress tensor anisotropy into parton distribution anisotropy assumes a kinetic (weak coupling) description of QGP, which may not hold for the strongly coupled sQGP.

## Method

The author calculates the dilepton angular distribution parameter a(alpha) from a one-parameter model of parton angular distributions W ~ exp[-alpha cos^2 theta_p], where alpha characterizes the anisotropy of the colliding partons. The resulting dilepton distribution is expressed as 1 + a(alpha) cos^2 theta_k, and the mapping between the parton anisotropy parameter alpha and the observable dilepton polarization parameter a is computed analytically and plotted. The invariant mass M of the dilepton pair is related to the production time via a "temperature profile" function, where the production rate W ~ exp(-M/T) and the space-time volume Phi(T) ~ 1/T^p combine to give a sharp maximum at temperature T* = M/p.

## Key numbers

- For Drell-Yan process (stage i, large negative alpha): a ≈ 1, corresponding to partons collimated near the beam direction
- For pre-equilibrium stage (stage ii, large positive alpha): asymptotic value a(alpha → infinity) = -1/3
- For equilibrated isotropic stage (stage iii, alpha → 0): a(0) = 0
- Drell-Yan process dominates for dilepton masses above the charmonium region: M > 4 GeV
- Intermediate mass dileptons (IMD) from pre-equilibrium phase: m_phi < M < m_J/psi, with expected a ≈ -0.2
- Equilibrated stage produces dileptons with M < 1 GeV, tending to unpolarized (a → 0)
- The temperature profile power p ∼ 5-7, giving a sharp Gaussian width in the production temperature

## Context

This work extends the original proposal by Shuryak (1978) suggesting dileptons as a diagnostic tool for QGP, by adding the polarization degree of freedom as a measure of anisotropy. It addresses the puzzle that hydrodynamical descriptions of elliptic flows (Teaney 2000, Teaney 2001, Huovinen 2001, Hirano 2002) require thermalization within ∼0.5 fm/c, which is faster than perturbative "bottom-up" equilibration (Baier 2000) can explain, while AdS/CFT approaches (Lin 2008, Chesler 2008, Chesler 2010, Janik) ascribe thermalization to infall times ∼0.2 fm/c. The paper calls for experimental measurement of a(M) and for theoretical calculation of the vector channel spectral function in out-of-equilibrium settings, noting that recent work (Caron-Huot 2011) suggests non-local observers may give different information than the one-point stress tensor.
