# Quadrupole-octupole coupling and the onset of octupole deformation in actinides

> The evolution of quadrupole and octupole collectivity and their coupling is investigated in a series of even-even isotopes of the actinide Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, and Cf with neutron number in the interval $130\leqslant N\leqslant 150$. The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation, based on the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional, is employed to generate potential energy surfaces depending upon the axially-symmetric quadrupole and octupole shape degrees of freedom. The mean-field energy surface is then mapped onto the expectation value of the $sdf$ interacting-boson-model Hamiltonian in the boson condensate state as to determine the strength parameters of the boson Hamiltonian. Spectroscopic properties related to the octupole degree of freedom are produced by diagonalizing the mapped Hamiltonian. Calculated low-energy negative-parity spectra, $B(E3;3^{-}_{1}\to 0^{+}_{1})$ reduced transition rates, and effective octupole deformation suggest that the transition from nearly spherical to stable octupole-deformed, and to octupole vibrational states occurs systematically in the actinide region.

## Key claim

The transition from nearly spherical to stable octupole-deformed, and to octupole vibrational states occurs systematically across actinide isotopes from Ra to Cf, with the most pronounced octupole minimum found at neutron number N=134, though the IBM-PESs are systematically softer in the octupole direction than the underlying SCMF-PESs, leading to octupole deformation energies that are a factor between two to five lower in the IBM framework.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1M energy density functional to generate potential energy surfaces (PESs) as functions of axially-symmetric quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation parameters for even-even isotopes of Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, and Cf with neutron numbers 130 ≤ N ≤ 150. The microscopic PESs were then mapped onto the expectation value of an sdf interacting-boson-model (IBM) Hamiltonian in a boson condensate state to determine the Hamiltonian's strength parameters, and the resulting boson Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The most pronounced octupole minimum is obtained at N=134 for ²²⁸Pu around β₃,min ≈ 0.17
- The maximum predicted B(E3;3⁻₁→0⁺₁) value is 72 W.u. for ²²⁸U at around N=136
- The E1 charge is fixed at e₁ = 0.0277 e·b¹/² for all considered isotopic chains
- For Cf nuclei, only three isotopes are octupole deformed with β₃,min < 0.15
- The f-boson expectation value ⟨n̂f⟩ ≈ 2 for the 0⁺₂ and 2⁺₂ states, indicating a double-octupole phonon structure
- For N > 140, the 0⁺₁ and 2⁺₁ states have ⟨n̂f⟩ ≈ 0, meaning they are made of s and d bosons alone

## Context

This work extends the EDF-to-IBM mapping procedure previously applied to Ra and Th isotopes (Nomura, 2020) to heavier actinide nuclei (U, Pu, Cm, Cf), verifying that the octupole-related shape phase transition generally occurs in the actinide region. The results provide quantitative predictions for spectroscopy in proton-rich actinides that have not been explored experimentally, complementing generator coordinate method calculations (Rayner, 2020) which are computationally expensive for heavy systems, and extending phenomenological spdf-IBM studies (Zamfir, 2003; Spieker, 2018) that were limited to stable nuclei.
