# Dynamics of heavy flavor quarks in high energy nuclear collisions

> A general overview on the role of heavy quarks as probes of the medium formed in high energy nuclear collisions is presented. Experimental data compared to model calculations at low and moderate pT are exploited to extract information on the transport coefficients of the medium, on possible modifications of heavy flavor hadronization in a hot environment and to provide quantitative answers to the issue of kinetic (and chemical, at conceivable future experimental facilities) thermalization of charm. Finally, the role of heavy flavor at high pT as a tool to study the mass and color-charge dependence the jet quenching is also analyzed.

## Key claim
Heavy flavor quarks (charm and beauty) serve as probes of the quark-gluon plasma formed in high energy nuclear collisions, but the interpretation of experimental data on D meson nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow remains ambiguous: the observed features could arise either from charm approaching kinetic equilibrium with the fireball or from modifications of hadronization in the medium, such as coalescence with light thermal partons.

## Method
The paper reviews transport calculations based on the Boltzmann equation and its Fokker-Planck/Langevin limit to describe heavy quark propagation through a hydrodynamically evolving fireball. Transport coefficients (friction and momentum diffusion) are evaluated from weak-coupling calculations, lattice-QCD simulations for static heavy quarks, and phenomenological models. Heavy quarks are hadronized either through standard in-vacuum fragmentation or via a Lund string fragmentation model where charm quarks couple to light antiquarks from a local thermal distribution at freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- STAR data for D0 meson R_AA in Au-Au collisions at RHIC show a sharp peak around p_T ~ 1.5 GeV in the 0-10% most central events
- Lattice-QCD results for the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient kappa in a gluon plasma are shown as a function of temperature, with work in progress to extrapolate to the continuum limit
- Charm chemical equilibration rate estimates: Gamma_chem^{-1} ~ 60 fm/c for T ~ 400 MeV and Gamma_chem^{-1} ~ 10 fm/c for T ~ 600 MeV
- For conceivable FCC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV with initial energy density e_0 ~ 250 GeV/fm^3, charm remains far from chemical equilibrium
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV with initial energy density e_0 ~ 100 GeV/fm^3, charm also remains far from chemical equilibrium
- The dynamic medium modeling replaces the gluon mean free path with lambda_dyn = [3 alpha_s T]^{-1}

## Context
This review extends the line of work using heavy quarks as probes of the quark-gluon plasma, building on prior transport calculations by Rapp (2009), Gossiaux (2012), and the Torino group (2013). The paper discusses how lattice-QCD results for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient from Banerjee (2012) and Ding (2012) can be linked to experimental observables, particularly for beauty quarks where the static heavy quark approximation is more reliable. The results enable future experimental programs at the LHC and FCC to test whether charm reaches kinetic or chemical equilibrium, and to extract transport coefficients from direct B meson measurements.
