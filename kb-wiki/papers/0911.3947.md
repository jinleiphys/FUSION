# Probing the weakly-bound neutron orbit of $^{31}$Ne with total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections

> A candidate of a neutron-halo nucleus, $^{31}$Ne, contains a single neutron in the $pf$ shell. Within the Glauber and eikonal models, we analyze reactions used to study $^{31}$Ne. We show in a $^{30}$Ne+n model that the magnitudes of the total reaction and above all of the one-neutron removal cross sections of $^{31}$Ne on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets strongly depend on the orbital angular momentum of the neutron, thereby providing us with efficient ways to determine both the spin-parity and structure of the ground state of $^{31}$Ne. Besides these inclusive observables, we also calculate energy and parallel-momentum distributions for the breakup of $^{31}$Ne, and show their strong dependence upon the orbital of the valence neutron in the bound state of $^{31}$Ne.

## Key claim

The total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections of the neutron-halo candidate 31Ne on 12C and 208Pb targets depend strongly on whether the valence neutron occupies a 1p3/2 or 0f7/2 orbital, providing a way to determine the ground-state spin-parity and structure of 31Ne, provided the neutron separation energy is known.

## Method

The authors model 31Ne as a 30Ne core plus a valence neutron, using Woods-Saxon potentials to generate single-particle wave functions for the 1p3/2 and 0f7/2 orbitals. They compute total reaction cross sections within the Glauber model using symmetrized phase-shift functions, and one-neutron removal cross sections as the difference between the reaction cross sections of 31Ne and 30Ne. For breakup observables, they employ the Coulomb-corrected eikonal model (CCE) with optical potentials derived from the same density-folding approach.

## Key numbers

- The neutron separation energy Sn of 31Ne is 0.33 MeV, though with large uncertainty.
- At 240 MeV/nucleon on a 12C target, the total reaction cross section for the 1p3/2 configuration is 1.45 b, and for the 0f7/2 configuration is 1.37 b; the difference is 87 mb.
- The increase in reaction cross section from 30Ne to 31Ne at 240 MeV/nucleon on 12C is 96 mb for the 1p3/2 orbit and 26 mb for the 0f7/2 orbit.
- At 240 MeV/nucleon on a 208Pb target, the nuclear reaction cross section increases from 4.36 b to 4.69 b for the 1p3/2 case, and from 4.33 b to 4.37 b for the 0f7/2 case.
- The rms radius of the valence neutron orbit is about 7 fm for the 1p3/2 orbital and about 4 fm for the 0f7/2 orbital.
- The difference in matter radius between 31Ne and 30Ne is 0.19–0.20 fm for the 1p3/2 configuration and 0.04 fm for the 0f7/2 configuration.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of halo nuclei and shell inversion near the neutron dripline, particularly the predictions of shell inversion in 31Ne from shell-model calculations (Poves and Retamosa, 1994) and microscopic cluster models (Descouvemont, 1999). The calculations are compared with recent measurements of total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections at RIKEN (Ohtsubo et al., 2009; Nakamura et al., 2009). The results enable experimental determination of the ground-state spin-parity of 31Ne through comparison of measured cross sections with the distinct predictions for the 1p3/2 and 0f7/2 configurations.
