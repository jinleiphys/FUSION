# The nuclear effects in $g_{1^3\rm{He}}$ and the Bjorken sum rule for A=3

> The Bjorken sum rules for the $A=3$ and $A=1$ are used as a guide to estimate nuclear effects in extracting $g_{1n}(x,Q^{2})$ from the $\vec{e}\,\vec{^3\rm{He}}$ data. We estimate that the combination of the spin depolarization, the nonnucleonic degrees of freedom in the $A=3$ system and nuclear shadowing is likely to reduce $g_{1^3\rm{He}}(x\le 0.05)$ by $\sim 15 \%$ while a significant enhancement of the structure functions $g_{1^3\rm{He}}, g_{1^3\rm{H}}$ at $x \sim 0.1$ is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard convolution model for extracting the neutron spin structure function \(g_{1n}(x,Q^2)\) from polarized \(\vec{e}\,\vec{^3\mathrm{He}}\) data is incomplete, because it neglects nonnucleonic degrees of freedom (primarily \(\Delta\)-isobar admixtures) and nuclear shadowing; these two effects together are likely to reduce \(g_{1^3\mathrm{He}}(x\le 0.05)\) by about 15% and produce a compensating enhancement at \(x\sim 0.1\), significantly altering the naive depolarization factor.

## Method

The authors use the Bjorken sum rule for \(A=3\) and \(A=1\) as a consistency check. They compare the impulse-approximation prediction for the ratio of axial coupling constants \(G_A(^3\mathrm{H})/G_A(n)\) (derived from the \(^3\)He wave function) with the experimental value, finding a \(\sim 4\%\) violation that they attribute to \(\Delta\)-isobar admixtures. They then model nuclear shadowing for the polarized structure function using a modified Glauber approach with a simple \(S\)-state wave function for \(^3\)He, and combine the two effects multiplicatively to estimate the net modification of \(g_{1^3\mathrm{He}}/g_{1n}\).

## Key numbers

* The impulse-approximation prediction for the ratio of axial coupling constants is \(G_A(^3\mathrm{H})/G_A(n) = 1 - (0.0785 \pm 0.0060)\), based on realistic three-nucleon wave functions.
* The experimental value from tritium \(\beta\)-decay is \(G_A(^3\mathrm{H})/G_A(n) = 1 - (0.0366 \pm 0.0030)\), showing a \(\sim 4\%\) discrepancy.
* The \(\Delta\)-isobar model of Ref. [delta] gives \(G_A(^3\mathrm{H})/G_A(n) = 1 - 0.0378 \pm 0.002\), consistent with experiment.
* Nuclear shadowing alone reduces the ratio \(g_{1^3\mathrm{He}}(x,Q_0^2)/g_{1n}(x,Q_0^2)\) to approximately 0.9 for \(x \le 0.03\).
* The combined effect of depolarization, nonnucleonic degrees of freedom, and shadowing reduces \(g_{1^3\mathrm{He}}(x\le 0.05)\) by about 15%, with a compensating enhancement of order 10–15% at \(x\sim 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier convolution-model analyses of Ciofi degli Atti and Sauer (1990s) and the three-nucleon wave-function calculations of Friar (1990s) by pointing out that those models violate the Bjorken sum rule for \(A=3\) by \(\sim 4\%\). The authors build on the observation of Kaptari and Umnikov (1990s) that the convolution model is inconsistent with the sum rule, and they incorporate the \(\Delta\)-isobar mechanism previously studied by Ericson and Weise (1980s) and more recent calculations by [delta] and [delta1]. The paper also applies the color-screening model of nuclear shadowing developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and others, and it predicts a pattern of screening at low \(x\) and enhancement at moderate \(x\) that could be tested in future experiments with polarized \(^3\)He and heavier nuclei.
