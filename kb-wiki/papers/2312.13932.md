# Towards consistent nuclear interactions from chiral Lagrangians II: Symmetry preserving regularization

> Low-energy nuclear structure and reactions can be described in a systematically improvable way using the framework of chiral effective field theory. This requires solving the quantum mechanical many-body problem with regularized nuclear forces and current operators, derived from the most general effective chiral Lagrangian. To maintain the chiral and gauge symmetries, a symmetry preserving cutoff regularization has to be employed when deriving nuclear potentials. Here, we discuss various regularization techniques and show how this task can be accomplished by regularizing the pion field in the effective chiral Lagrangian using the gradient flow method. The actual derivation of the nuclear forces and currents from the regularized effective Lagrangian can be carried out utilizing the novel path-integral approach introduced in our earlier paper.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that symmetry-preserving cutoff regularization of chiral nuclear forces and currents can be achieved by regularizing the pion field in the effective chiral Lagrangian using the gradient flow method, but only after showing that simpler higher-derivative approaches fail due to deregularization of multi-pion vertices and electromagnetic current contributions.

## Method

The authors first test a naive higher-derivative regularization by modifying the pion Lagrangian with exponential factors on the kinetic term, then test a more sophisticated higher-derivative approach where cutoff-dependent terms are proportional to the equation of motion. After showing both approaches fail for certain nuclear forces or electromagnetic currents, they implement the gradient flow method, extending the SU(2) matrix U(x) to W(x,τ) via a chirally covariant gradient flow equation ∂_τ W = -i w EOM(τ) w, with boundary condition W(x,0)=U(x). The resulting regularized Lagrangian is derived using a path-integral approach introduced in their earlier paper (Krebs 2023).

## Key numbers

- The gradient flow introduces a Gaussian smearing in Euclidean space-time extending over a distance ~√(2τ), as shown by the Green's function G(x,τ) = θ(τ)/(16π²τ²) exp[-(x²+4M²τ²)/(4τ)]
- The regularized pion propagator in the chiral limit takes the form Δ^E(x_i-x_j) = ∫ d⁴q/(2π)⁴ e^{iq·(x_i-x_j)} e^{-(q₀²+𝐪²)/Λ²}/(q₀²+𝐪²)
- The four-nucleon force at N³LO using the EOM-based higher-derivative regularization yields regulator functions f_Λ = exp[-(𝐪₁²+M²)/Λ²] exp[-(𝐪₂²+M²)/Λ²] exp[-(𝐪₃²+M²)/Λ²] exp[-(𝐪₄²+M²)/Λ²] and f_Λ^{ijk} = exp[-(𝐪ᵢ²+M²)/Λ²] exp[-(𝐪ⱼ²+M²)/Λ²] exp[-(𝐪ₖ²+M²)/Λ²]

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier paper (Krebs 2023) on deriving nuclear forces from regularized chiral Lagrangians, and addresses the inconsistency pointed out in their previous work (Epelbaum 2019, Krebs 2019) that multiplying chiral EFT potentials by cutoff functions violates chiral symmetry for three- and more-nucleon systems. The gradient flow method, suggested in talks by Kaplan, provides a symmetry-preserving regularization that avoids the deregularization problems of the Slavnov (1971) higher-derivative approach and the naive Gaussian regulator of Reinert (2017). This enables consistent derivation of regularized nuclear forces and currents for use in many-body calculations.
