# Pion-pair formation and the pion dispersion relation in a hot pion gas

> The possibility of pion--pair formation in a hot pion gas, based on the bosonic gap equation, is pointed out and discussed in detail. The critical temperature for condensation of pion pairs (Evans--Rashid transition) is determined as a function of the pion density. As for fermions, this phase transition is signaled by the appearance of a pole in the two--particle propagator. In bose systems there exists a second, lower critical temperature, associated with the appearance of the single--particle condensate. Between the two critical temperatures the pion dispersion relation changes from the usual quasiparticle dispersion to a Bogoliubov--like dispersion relation at low momenta. This generalizes the non-relativistic result for an attractive bose gas by Evans et al. Possible consequences for the inclusive pion spectra measured in heavy--ion collisions at ultra--relativistic energies are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a hot pion gas can undergo an Evans–Rashid transition to a paired state (pion-pair condensation) at a critical temperature \(T_c^u\), and that this transition is signaled by a pole in the two-pion propagator; however, the existence of such a solution depends on the chemical potential exceeding about 40 MeV to provide sufficient attraction in the \(\ell=I=0\) channel, and the effect is weakened by the repulsive off-shell behavior required by chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors solve the bosonic gap equation (derived via Gorkov decoupling) for a thermal pion gas, using a rank-2 separable \(\pi\pi\) interaction in the \(\ell=I=0\) channel inspired by the linear sigma model, with form factors fitted to experimental phase shifts and a sigma mass \(M_\sigma = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\). They compute the upper critical temperature \(T_c^u\) (where the gap vanishes) by linearizing the gap equation, and the lower critical temperature \(T_c^\ell\) (where the quasiparticle energy vanishes at zero momentum) from the condition \(m_\pi - \mu = \delta_1 + \delta_2\). They also evaluate the in-medium \(T\)-matrix for pion pairs to identify poles below the \(2\pi\) threshold.

## Key numbers

* For a fixed chemical potential \(\mu = 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the upper critical temperature is \(T_c^u \sim 123\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the lower critical temperature is \(T_c^\ell \sim 77\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* At \(T = T_c^\ell\) with \(\mu = 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the maximum gap value is \(\delta_1 + \delta_2 = m_\pi - \mu \sim 3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The condition for attraction in the gap equation requires \(|\mu| > M_\sigma m_\pi / 2\sqrt{3M_\sigma^2 - 2m_\pi^2} \sim 40\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* A pole in the \(T\)-matrix exists for temperatures above \(T_0^\ell \approx 47\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(\mu = 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\); this \(T_0^\ell\) is close to \(T_c^\ell\).
* The critical momentum for the disappearance of the pole in the \(T\)-matrix at \(T = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\mu = 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\) lies between \(100\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\) and \(250\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic boson pairing theory of Evans and Imry (1975) and Evans and Rashid (1976) to a relativistic pion gas, generalizing their results to a system with a realistic \(\pi\pi\) interaction that respects chiral symmetry. It builds on earlier studies by Stoof (1992) and Chanfray et al. (1993) who showed that the critical temperature for the Evans–Rashid transition is given by a Thouless criterion for the bosonic \(T\)-matrix. The findings enable future development of a Brückner–Hartree–Fock–Bogoliubov equation of state for a hot pion gas and motivate investigation of higher clusters such as four-pion bound states.
