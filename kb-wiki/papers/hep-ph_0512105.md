# Beam normal spin asymmetry in the quasi-RCS approximation

> The two-photon exchange contribution to the single spin asymmetries with the spin orientation normal to the reaction plane is discussed for elastic electron-proton scattering in the equivalent photon approximation. In this case, hadronic part of the two-photon exchange amplitude describes real Compton scattering (RCS). We show that in the case of the beam normal spin asymmetry, this approximation selects only the photon helicity flip amplitudes of RCS. At low energies, we make use of unitarity and estimate the contribution of the $πN$ multipoles to the photon helicity flip amplitudes. In the Regge regime, QRCS approximation allows for a contribution from two pion exchange, and we provide an estimate of such contributions. We furthermore discuss the possibility of the quark and gluon GPD's contributions in the QRCS kinematics.

## Key claim

The beam normal spin asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering, when calculated using the quasi-real Compton scattering (QRCS) approximation, receives contributions only from photon helicity-flip amplitudes of real Compton scattering, while the contribution from photon helicity-conserving amplitudes vanishes in this approximation.

## Method

The author uses the equivalent photon approximation to study the two-photon exchange contribution to the beam normal spin asymmetry, where the hadronic part of the two-photon exchange amplitude describes real Compton scattering (RCS). The hadronic tensor is taken at the kinematical point where both exchanged photons are nearly real and is factored out of the integration over the electron phase space. The remaining integral yields a logarithmic enhancement factor, and the resulting expression for the asymmetry is related to specific combinations of RCS amplitudes that correspond to photon helicity-flip amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The logarithmic enhancement factor from the QRCS kinematics is approximately ln²(-t/m²) ~ 100, with the leading term being quadratic in the logarithm
- At forward kinematics, the leading term is governed by a single log term, ln(-t/m²) ~ 10, giving predictions for B_n of approximately -(4-6) ppm
- The two-pion exchange contribution in the Regge regime yields asymmetries of order 10⁻² ppm for energies between 6 and 45 GeV
- The threshold for the sign change of the first term in the low-energy expression is at E_lab ≤ 0.45 GeV for the proton target

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-photon exchange effects in elastic electron-proton scattering, which became important after polarization transfer data for the form factor ratio G_E/G_M (Jones et al., 2000) appeared incompatible with Rosenbluth data. The calculation addresses the beam normal spin asymmetry, which is directly related to the imaginary part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, and builds on prior work by Afanasev et al. (2005) who first identified the QRCS kinematics. The results can be compared to calculations using the full set of single pion electroproduction multipoles (Vanderhaeghen and Guidal, 2004) and to an effective field theory calculation without dynamical pions (Diaconescu and Ramsey-Musolf, 2004). The work enables improved understanding of the imaginary part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, which is necessary for model-independent extraction of the electric form factor through dispersion relations.
