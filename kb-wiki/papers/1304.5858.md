# A Time Dependent Local Isospin Density Approximation Study of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The dynamic response of asymmetric nuclear matter is studied by using a Time-Dependent Local Isospin Density (TDLIDA) approximation approach. Calculations are based on a local density energy functional derived by an Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculation of bulk nuclear matter. Three types of excited states emerge: collective states, a continuum of quasi-particle-quasi-hole excitations and unstable solutions. These states are analyzed and discussed for different values of the nuclear density $ρ$ and isospin asymmetry $ξ=(N-Z)/A$. An analytical expression of the compressibility as a function of $ρ$ and $ξ$ is derived which show explicitly an instability of the neutron matter around $ρ\simeq 0.09 fm^{-3}$ when a small fraction of protons is added to the system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Time-Dependent Local Isospin Density Approximation (TDLIDA) approach, based on an energy functional derived from Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations, can describe the dynamic response of asymmetric nuclear matter, but the approach is limited to low momentum and low frequency regimes and relies on the accuracy of the underlying density functional.

## Method

The authors derived an isospin-density energy functional from AFDMC calculations of bulk nuclear matter by Gandolfi et al. (2010), parameterizing the interaction-correlation energy per particle for symmetric and neutron matter. They then applied a Time-Dependent Local Isospin Density Approximation (TDLIDA) to compute the density-density and isovector-density response functions for infinite asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, using free response functions valid in the low-q, low-omega limits.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density assumed: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Interaction-correlation energy for symmetric nuclear matter (ξ=0): ε₀ = -38.1 MeV
- Interaction-correlation energy for pure neutron matter (ξ=1): ε₁ = -19.8 MeV
- Neutron matter instability found around ρ ≃ 0.09 fm⁻³ when a small fraction of protons is added
- For symmetric nuclear matter at ρ = 0.30 fm⁻³, the isoscalar collective state at s̄_s = 1.1188 exhausts 83.6% of the f-sum rule
- For pure neutron matter at ρ = 0.30 fm⁻³, the collective state at s̄ = 1.3907 exhausts 81.4% of the f-sum rule
- For symmetric nuclear matter at ρ = 0.10 fm⁻³, the isovector collective state at s̄_v = 1.1036 exhausts 81.4% of the f-sum rule

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear matter response using methods such as BHF-RPA theories (Shen, 2003) and Landau theory with CBF effective interactions (Benhar, 2013), and semiclassical simulations of neutron matter structure factors (Horowitz et al., 2004, 2005). The approach enables calculation of the dynamic structure factor for arbitrary asymmetry, relevant for neutrino mean free paths in neutron star interiors as discussed by Sawyer (1975, 1989) and Burrows et al. (2006), and for identifying mechanical instabilities related to the transition to inhomogeneous phases (Chamel, 2008).
