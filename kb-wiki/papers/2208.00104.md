# Neutron $3s_{1/2}$ occupation change across the stable tin isotopes investigated using isotopic analysis of proton scattering at 295 MeV

> The isotopic systematics of Sn isotopes in the range A=116--124 were investigated by combining the nuclear structure and reaction calculations for the analysis of Sn(p,p) reactions at 295 MeV. The Sn(p,p) reactions were calculated employing relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with theoretical densities obtained for the Sn isotopes from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with the DD-ME2 interaction and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) calculations with the SKM* interaction. Calculation using the DD-ME2 density reproduced the experimental data for $^{122}$Sn(p,p) but overestimated the $^{116}$Sn(p,p) and $^{118}$Sn(p,p) cross sections at backward angles. In the isotopic analysis of the cross section ratio $R(σ)$ of $^{122}$Sn to $^{116}$Sn, a calculation using the SKM* density reproduced the peak amplitudes of $R(σ)$ obtained from the experimental cross sections, whereas a calculation using the DD-ME2 density did not. The ratio $R(σ)$ was found to be sensitive to the isotopic change of the neutron $3s_{1/2}$ occupation through the isotopic difference of the surface neutron density around r=4--5 fm. Isotopic analysis indicated that a rapid increase of the $3s_{1/2}$ occupation from $^{116}$Sn to $^{122}$Sn obtained by the DD-ME2 calculation is unlikely. This result derived from the 295 MeV Sn(p,p) cross section data is consistent with the direct measurement of the neutron occupations in Sn isotopes by neutron transfer and pick-up reactions.

## Key claim

The isotopic cross section ratio \(R(\sigma)\) of \(^{122}\text{Sn}(p,p)\) to \(^{116}\text{Sn}(p,p)\) at 295 MeV is sensitive to the change in neutron \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation between the two isotopes, and the analysis indicates that a rapid increase of this occupation from \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) to \(^{122}\text{Sn}\) as predicted by the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculation with the DD-ME2 interaction is unlikely, consistent with direct transfer and pick-up reaction measurements.

## Method

The author combined nuclear structure calculations (spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov with the DD-ME2 interaction and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with the SKM* interaction) with reaction calculations using the relativistic impulse approximation plus density-dependent meson-exchange (RIA+ddMH) model to compute \(^{A}\text{Sn}(p,p)\) cross sections at 295 MeV. The isotopic cross section ratio \(R(\sigma)\) of \(^{122}\text{Sn}\) to \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) was defined and compared with experimental data from Terashima (2008). A model analysis (me2+3s) was performed by artificially increasing the \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation in \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) by 0.5 to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The isotopic difference in \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation between \(^{122}\text{Sn}\) and \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) is 0.46 in the DD-ME2 calculation, 0.20 in the SKM* calculation, and 0.14(7) from experimental transfer/pick-up data.
- The \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation probability in \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) is 0.35 (DD-ME2), 0.61 (SKM*), and 0.42(5) (experiment); in \(^{122}\text{Sn}\) it is 0.80 (DD-ME2), 0.80 (SKM*), and 0.56(5) (experiment).
- The isotopic difference in \(2d_{3/2}\) occupation is 0.43 (DD-ME2), 0.30 (SKM*), and 0.24(7) (experiment).
- The isotopic difference in \(1h_{11/2}\) occupation is 0.23 (DD-ME2), 0.22 (SKM*), and 0.25(7) (experiment).
- The surface neutron density region sensitive to the \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation change is around \(r = 4\)–\(5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the isotopic analysis method previously applied to Pb isotopes by Kanada-En'yo (2021) to the Sn isotope chain, using the same RIA+ddMH reaction framework. The results contradict the rapid \(3s_{1/2}\) occupation increase predicted by the DD-ME2 interaction and instead support the more modest change seen in the SKM* calculation and in direct neutron transfer/pick-up measurements by Szwec (2021). The analysis enables future use of 295 MeV \((p,p)\) cross section ratios as a sensitive probe of low-angular-momentum neutron orbital occupations across isotopic chains.
