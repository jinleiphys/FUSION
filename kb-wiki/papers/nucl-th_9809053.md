# The $np \to Λp$ reaction within a one-meson-exchange model

> With the advent of high precision proton accelerators, such as COSY in Jülich, or with medium energy accelerators like RCNP at Osaka, it may become possible to perform a direct study of the strangeness changing hadronic $np \to Λp$ process. In this work we study this reaction using a one-meson-exchange weak potential and realistic $NN$ and $YN$ strong interactions to distort both the initial $np$ and the final $Λp$ states. The calculated cross sections are very sensitive to the different mesons included in the weak transition, as well as to the strong $YN$ interaction model that generates the distorted final state.

# The \(np \to \Lambda p\) reaction within a one-meson-exchange model

## Key claim

The calculated cross sections for the strangeness-changing hadronic \(np \to \Lambda p\) reaction are very sensitive to both the choice of mesons included in the weak transition potential and the strong \(YN\) interaction model used to distort the final \(\Lambda p\) state; the authors note that the reaction may become measurable at facilities such as COSY or RCNP, but the cross sections depend strongly on the still-uncertain \(YN\) interaction.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section in the distorted-wave Born approximation using a one-meson-exchange weak potential that includes pseudoscalar (\(\pi, \eta, K\)) and vector (\(\rho, \omega, K^*\)) mesons. The initial \(np\) distorted waves are generated from the Nijmegen 93 or Bonn B strong \(NN\) potentials, while the final \(\Lambda p\) distorted waves use the Nijmegen soft-core or Jülich A strong \(YN\) interactions. The weak transition matrix elements are evaluated with regularized potentials and include form factors and strong correlations.

## Key numbers

- The opening of the coupled \(\Sigma N\) channel appears as a step in the total cross section at a proton lab momentum around **1140 MeV/c**.
- When only one-pion-exchange is considered and form factors and strong distortions are omitted, the cross section is an increasing function of proton lab momentum; including the strong repulsive part of the \(NN\) interaction reduces the cross section by a factor of about **2**.
- The Jülich A \(YN\) model produces a clear enhancement of the cross section near the \(p\Sigma\) threshold already at the level of only pion-exchange, in contrast to the Nijmegen soft-core model.

## Context

This work extends the one-meson-exchange weak potential developed by Parreño, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997) for hypernuclear decay to the direct \(np \to \Lambda p\) reaction. The results show that the cross section is highly sensitive to the \(YN\) strong interaction model, particularly the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel coupling in the Jülich A model versus the Nijmegen soft-core model. The study enables future experimental searches at COSY or RCNP by providing theoretical predictions that can discriminate between different \(YN\) interaction models once data become available.
