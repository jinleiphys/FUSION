# Mean-Field Description of Fusion Barriers with Skyrme's Interaction

> Fusion barriers are determined in the framework of the Skyrme energy-density functional together with the semi-classical approach known as the Extended Thomas-Fermi method. The barriers obtained in this way with the Skyrme interaction SkM* turn out to be close to those generated by phenomenological models like those using the proximity potentials. It is also shown that the location and the structure of the fusion barrier in the vicinity of its maximum and beyond can be quite accurately described by a simple analytical form depending only on the masses and the relative isospin of target and projectile nucleus.

## Key claim

Fusion barriers calculated using the Skyrme energy-density functional with the semi-classical Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method, with no adjustable parameters, produce barrier heights close to those from phenomenological proximity models, but the agreement is best for very asymmetric reactions and can differ by up to 7 MeV (about 2%) for nearly symmetric systems.

## Method

The authors used the ETF method carried through fourth order in the semi-classical expansion to determine the ground-state densities of target and projectile nuclei, using modified Fermi functions as variational trial densities. The nuclear part of the interaction potential between two spherical colliding ions was then computed in the sudden approximation by integrating the Skyrme energy-density functional (with the SkM* interaction) over the sum of the two fixed densities minus the individual energies. The total fusion barrier was obtained by adding the Coulomb potential computed from the ETF proton densities, and the resulting barriers for 269 reactions leading to super-heavy elements with Z = 108–114 were compared to the Myers-Świątecki proximity model.

## Key numbers

- The r.m.s. deviation between the exact ETF fusion potential and the analytical approximation is 0.27 MeV at the top of the barrier and 0.37 MeV at the touching configuration (s = 0).
- Barrier heights obtained with the Skyrme SLy4 interaction are identical to those with SkM* to within 1 MeV.
- For the reaction 110Cd + 154Sm, the ETF barrier height differs from the proximity model by about 7 MeV (or 2%).
- For lighter systems (160,162,166Dy + 46-50Ti), the analytical expression fitted to super-heavy systems reproduces ETF results with a deviation of less than 0.7 MeV; for 48Ca + 48Ca the deviation is about 1.3 MeV.
- The fitted parameters for the analytical fusion potential are: r0 = 1.183 fm, b = -2.400 fm, v0 = -46.07 MeV, κ = -0.4734, α = 0.173 fm^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical ETF approach previously applied to fission and fusion barriers (Brack and collaborators, 1985; Bartel and Bencheikh, 2002) to a systematic study of fusion barriers for super-heavy element synthesis. The results are compared to the phenomenological proximity model of Myers and Świątecki (2000) and to the prescription of Möller and collaborators (2001), showing good overall agreement but with systematic differences for symmetric systems. The analytical expression developed here, fitted to the ETF barriers, provides a simple tool for estimating fusion barriers across the periodic table and could guide experiments on super-heavy element formation, though the authors note that deformation effects (which can lower barriers substantially, as shown by Płoszajczak and collaborators, 1994) are not included.
