# Multiquark states in the covariant quark confinement model

> This talk reviews the last applications of the covariant quark model for studying the properties of the multiquark states: B_s-meson (quark-antiquark state), light baryons (three-quark states) and tetraquark (four-quark state). The form factors of the B(B_s)-> P(V)-transitions are evaluated in the full kinematical region of momentum transfer squared. The widths of some B_s-nonleptonic decays are calculated. The static properties of the proton and neutron, and the ╬ø-hyperon (magnetic moments and charge radii) and the behavior of the nucleon form factors at low momentum transfers are described. The consequences of treating the X(3872) meson as a tetraquark bound state are explored. The decay widths of the observed channels X->J/Žł+2ŽĆ(3ŽĆ) and X-> \bar D^0 + D^0+ŽĆ^0 via the intermediate off--shell states X-> J/Žł+Žü(Žē) and X-> \bar D + D^* are calculated. Its one-photon decay X-> ╬│+ J/Žłis also analyzed.

## Key claim

The covariant quark model with infrared confinement can describe mesons, baryons, and tetraquarks on equal footing, but the results for the X(3872) as a tetraquark are only consistent with available experimental data for "reasonable values of the size parameter $\Lambda_X$", implying that the agreement depends on tuning this hadron-size parameter.

## Method

The model couples hadrons to their constituent quarks via nonlocal vertex functions of Gaussian form, with coupling constants fixed by the compositeness condition $Z_H=0$ (Weinberg 1962, Salam 1962). Infrared confinement is implemented by cutting off the Schwinger-parameter integration at an upper limit $1/\lambda^2$, removing all quark-production thresholds. Matrix elements for $B(B_s)\to P(V)$ transitions, baryon electromagnetic properties, and tetraquark decays are computed from quark-loop diagrams using a FORM program and FORTRAN numerical integration.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark model with infrared confinement developed by Branz (2009) and Gutsche (2012) to multiquark states including tetraquarks. It provides form factors in the full kinematical region, complementing light-cone sum rule calculations by Ball (2007) which are limited to large recoil. The results enable predictions for $B_s$ mixing observables and for the $J/\psi$ dissociation cross section via s-channel X(3872) dominance, and allow tests of low-recoil symmetry relations among form factors.
