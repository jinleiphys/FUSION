# Entanglement in joint $Λ\barΛ$ decay

> We investigate the joint $Λ\barΛ$ decay in the reaction $e^+ e^- \rightarrow γΛ(\rightarrow pπ^-) \barΛ(\rightarrow \bar{p}π^+)$. This reaction may provide information on the electromagnetic form factors of the Lambda baryon, in the time-like region. We present a conventional diagram-based calculation where production and decay steps are coherent and summations over final-state proton and anti-proton spins are performed. The resulting cross-section distribution is explictly covariant as it is expressed in scalar products of the four-momentum vectors of the participating particles. We compare this calculation with that of the folding method which we extend and make explicitly covariant. In the folding method production and decay distributions, not amplitudes, are folded together. Of particular importance is then a correct counting of the number of possible intermediate-hyperon-spin states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the conventional diagram-based calculation and the folding method for the joint $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ decay in $e^+ e^- \rightarrow \gamma \Lambda(\rightarrow p\pi^-) \bar{\Lambda}(\rightarrow \bar{p}\pi^+)$ yield identical results, provided the number of possible intermediate-hyperon-spin states is correctly counted; the folding method, when properly extended to a covariant form, reproduces the entanglement between the decay products of the two hyperons.

## Method

The author performed a conventional perturbation-theory calculation of the Feynman diagrams for the reaction, where production and decay steps are coherent and summations over final-state proton and anti-proton spins are performed, yielding a cross-section distribution expressed in scalar products of four-momentum vectors. The hadron tensor was decomposed into powers of the decay parameters $R$ and $S$, and contracted with the lepton tensor to obtain the reduced cross-section distribution. Separately, the folding method was extended into a covariant form by averaging product distributions over spin-quantization directions using the spin-tensor average $\langle s^\mu s^\nu \rangle = p^\mu p^\nu / M^2 - g^{\mu\nu}$, and the results were compared term-by-term with the conventional calculation.

## Key numbers

- The reduced cross-section distribution is decomposed as $\overline{|{\cal{M}}_{red}|^2} = \bar{R}_\Lambda R_\Lambda M^{RR} + \bar{R}_\Lambda S_\Lambda M^{RS} + \bar{S}_\Lambda R_\Lambda M^{SR} + \bar{S}_\Lambda S_\Lambda M^{SS}$, with each $M$ function having the form $M = (1/y_1 y_2)[-4P^2 A - (2sP^2 + y_1^2 + y_2^2)B]$, where $s = (k_1+k_2)^2$, $y_1 = 2k_1 \cdot q$, $y_2 = 2k_2 \cdot q$, and $P^2 = s - y_1 - y_2$.
- The spin-averaged hadron tensor for the $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ production is $K_{\nu\mu}^{00}(0,0) = \frac{1}{4} H_{\nu\mu}^{RR}$, where $H_{\nu\mu}^{RR} = 2[(P_\nu P_\mu - P^2 g_{\nu\mu}) - Q_\nu Q_\mu]|G_1|^2 + 2Q_\nu Q_\mu[2\Re(G_1 G_2^\star) - (Q^2/4M^2)|G_2|^2]$, with $P = p_1+p_2$, $Q = p_1-p_2$, and $M$ the Lambda mass.
- The Lambda decay distribution is $\sum|{\cal{M}}|^2 = R_\Lambda + M S_\Lambda l \cdot s$, with $R_\Lambda = |A|^2((M+m)^2-\mu^2) + |B|^2((M-m)^2-\mu^2)$ and $S_\Lambda = 4\Re(A^\star B)$, where $m$ is the proton mass and $\mu$ the pion mass; the decay asymmetry parameter is $\alpha_\Lambda = -l_\Lambda M S_\Lambda / R_\Lambda$, with $l_\Lambda$ the decay momentum in the Lambda rest system.
- The integrated cross-section for $e^+ e^- \rightarrow \gamma \Lambda \bar{\Lambda}$ is $d\sigma = [(4\pi\alpha)^3 / (2s(P^2)^2)] (\Gamma_\Lambda^2 / \Gamma^2) M^{RR} \, \text{dLips}(k_1+k_2; q, p_1, p_2)$, where $\Gamma = \Gamma(\Lambda \rightarrow all)$ and $\Gamma_\Lambda = \Gamma(\Lambda \rightarrow p\pi^-)$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the reaction $e^+ e^- \rightarrow \gamma \Lambda \bar{\Lambda}$ previously analyzed by Novo and Czyz (both cited in the paper) using the folding method, which the author argues had issues with coordinate system transformations and counting of intermediate hyperon-spin states. The paper provides a covariant formulation that resolves these issues and enables determination of the electromagnetic form factors $G_1$ and $G_2$ (related to the electric $G_E$ and magnetic $G_M$ form factors) of the Lambda baryon in the time-like region from BaBar experimental data. The explicit covariant expressions allow direct use of measured four-momenta without transformation to other coordinate systems.
