# Higher Anomalies, Higher Symmetries, and Cobordisms III: QCD Matter Phases Anew

> We explore QCD$_4$ quark matter, the $μ$-T (chemical potential-temperature) phase diagram, possible 't Hooft anomalies, and topological terms, via non-perturbative tools of cobordism theory and higher anomaly matching. We focus on quarks in 3-color and 3-flavor on bi-fundamentals of SU(3), then analyze the continuous and discrete global symmetries and pay careful attention to finite group sectors. We input constraints from $T=CP$ or $CT$ time-reversal symmetries, implementing QCD on unorientable spacetimes and distinct topology. Examined phases include the high T QGP (quark-gluon plasma/liquid), the low T ChSB (chiral symmetry breaking), 2SC (2-color superconductivity) and CFL (3-color-flavor locking superconductivity) at high density. We introduce a possibly useful but only approximate higher anomaly, involving discrete 0-form axial and 1-form mixed chiral-flavor-locked center symmetries, matched by the above four QCD phases. We also enlist as much as possible, but without identifying all of, 't Hooft anomalies and topological terms relevant to Symmetry Protected/Enriched Topological states (SPTs/SETs) of gauged SU(2) or SU(3) QCD$_d$-like matter theories in general in any spacetime dimensions $d=2,3,4,5$ via cobordism.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a possibly useful but only approximate higher 't Hooft anomaly involving discrete 0-form axial and 1-form mixed chiral-flavor-locked center symmetries that can be matched by the four main QCD phases (QGP, ChSB, 2SC, CFL), but the authors acknowledge this anomaly is not exact and they do not identify all relevant 't Hooft anomalies and topological terms.

## Method

The authors apply cobordism theory and higher anomaly matching to analyze QCD$_4$ quark matter with 3 colors and 3 flavors of quarks in the bi-fundamental representation of SU(3), examining both continuous and discrete global symmetries with careful attention to finite group sectors. They implement constraints from $T=CP$ or $CT$ time-reversal symmetries by considering QCD on unorientable spacetimes with distinct topology, and compute cobordism group classifications for QCD$_d$-like matter theories in spacetime dimensions $d=2,3,4,5$ both with and without time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers

* 3 colors and 3 flavors of quarks in the fundamental representation of SU(3) color gauge group and SU(3) flavor global symmetry group
* 4 dominant phases examined: QGP at high T, ChSB at low T and low density, 2SC at intermediate density, CFL at high density
* 8 pseudo-Goldstone bosons from ChSB (mesons: $\pi^0, \pi^{\pm}, K^0, \bar{K}^0, \eta, K^{\pm}$) plus one massive $\eta'$ meson
* 5 massive gluons from SU(3) breaking to SU(2) in the 2SC phase
* 4 gapped and 5 un-paired gapless quasiparticle fermions in the 2SC Bogoliubov basis
* 9 gapped quasiparticle fermions in the CFL Bogoliubov basis
* 25 continuous Lie group generators in QGP broken down to 8 generators of SU(3)$_{C+L+R}$ in CFL
* 7 massive gluons and 1 mixture of photon+gluon gauge boson in CFL, plus 8+1 Goldstone bosons

## Context

This work extends the program of applying cobordism theory and higher anomaly matching to QCD matter phases, building on the authors' prior work (Wan and Wang, 2018, 2019) and the general framework of higher symmetries from Gaiotto et al. (2014). The paper contrasts with and complements recent studies by Unsal (2017), Yonekura (2017), Tanizaki (2017, 2018), and Anber (2019) who employed global anomalies or global inconsistency to constrain the QCD phase diagram, and addresses the quark-hadron continuity debate between Cherman (2018) and Hirono (2018). The results enable future classification of symmetry-protected topological states for QCD matter phases via cobordism group computations.
