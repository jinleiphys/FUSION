# Coexistence of Ordered and Chaotic States in Nuclear Structure

> In atomic nuclei, as in other many-body systems, the classical phase space is mixed, so ordered and chaotic states generally coexist. In this contribution we discuss some models, showing the transition from order to chaos. In several cases a clear correspondence between classical and quantum chaos has been established. In particular the transition from ordered to chaotic states will be discussed in the framework of the shell and roto-vibrational models.

# Coexistence of Ordered and Chaotic States in Nuclear Structure

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ordered and chaotic states coexist in nuclear structure, with the degree of chaoticity depending on the nuclear system and excitation energy; the abstract's claim of a "clear correspondence between classical and quantum chaos" is qualified by the finding that Ca isotopes are systematically less chaotic than neighboring nuclei, and that in the roto-vibrational model the transition from chaos to order occurs as rotational frequency increases.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the (f7/2, p3/2, f5/2, p1/2) model space with 40Ca as an inert core, using a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic force with monopole improvements and the Lanczos algorithm implemented in the code ANTOINE. The Brody parameter ω was extracted from the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution of unfolded energy levels for states up to 4, 5, and 6 MeV above the yrast line, for spins 0 ≤ J ≤ 9 and isospin T = Tz. Separately, the Gaussian curvature criterion of the effective potential was applied to the axially symmetric roto-vibrational model to study the order-chaos transition as a function of rotational frequency ω.

## Key numbers

- Brody parameter ω for 46V at ≤4 MeV: 1.14 (exceeds the Wigner value of 1.0 for chaotic systems)
- Brody parameter ω for 46Ca at ≤4 MeV: 0.41 (closer to Poisson value of 0 for regular systems)
- Brody parameter ω for 48Ca at ≤4 MeV: 0.58
- Brody parameter ω for 50Ca at ≤4 MeV: 0.67
- Combined Brody parameter for 46V+46Ti+46Sc at ≤6 MeV: 0.95
- Combined Brody parameter for 46Ca+48Ca+50Ca at ≤6 MeV: 0.61
- Critical frequency for γ-unstable nuclei: ωc = sqrt( (C2/(14B)) (χ - 14/3) ), where χ = C3^2/(C2 C4)
- Threshold for chaotic motion in spherical and deformed nuclei: χ ≥ 14/3, with chaotic region for 0 ≤ ω < sqrt( (C2/(14B)) (χ - 14/3) )

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear physics by applying the Brody distribution analysis to realistic shell-model calculations in the A=46-50 region, building on earlier spectral statistics studies of nuclear levels. The shell-model results show that Ca isotopes, having only neutrons in valence orbits, exhibit more regular behavior than nuclei with both protons and neutrons, consistent with the weaker two-body matrix elements for identical nucleons. The roto-vibrational model analysis provides a classical counterpart using the Gaussian curvature criterion, which complements the Poincaré section method of earlier work (referred to as "third" in the text) and demonstrates a chaos-to-order transition with increasing rotational frequency, relevant for understanding rapidly rotating nuclei.
