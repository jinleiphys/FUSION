# Nuclear Incompressibility in Asymmetric Systems at Finite Temperature and Entropy

> The nuclear incompressibility $κ$ is investigated in asymmetric systems in a mean field model. The calculations are done at zero and finite temperatures and include surface, Coulomb and symmetry energy terms for several equations of state. Also considered is the behavior of the incompressibility at constant entropy $kappa_Q$ which is shown to have a very different behavior than the isothermal $kappa$. Namely, $kappa_Q$ decreases with increasing entropy while the isothermal $kappa$ increases with increasing $T$. A duality is found between the adiabatic $kappa_Q$ and the T=0 isothermal $kappa$. Analytic and also simple approximate expressions for $kappa$ are given.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how nuclear incompressibility behaves in asymmetric systems at finite temperature and entropy, showing that the isothermal incompressibility increases with temperature while the adiabatic incompressibility decreases with entropy, with a duality found between the adiabatic incompressibility at finite entropy and the isothermal incompressibility at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors use a mean field model based on a Skyrme interaction to calculate the nuclear incompressibility for asymmetric systems, including surface, Coulomb, and symmetry energy contributions. They derive analytic expressions for the incompressibility at zero and finite temperatures, and at constant entropy, using Taylor expansion methods around the saturation density to incorporate small corrections from finite size effects, temperature, and entropy.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature for symmetric nuclear matter, the incompressibility ranges from 165 MeV (for the softest equation of state with α→0) to 372 MeV (for a stiff equation of state with α=1), with α=1/3 giving κ=234 MeV
- For α=1/3 at finite temperature, the isothermal incompressibility values are 242 MeV at T=2.5 MeV, 265 MeV at T=5 MeV, and 302 MeV at T=7.5 MeV
- For α=1/3 at constant entropy, the adiabatic incompressibility follows κ_Q = 234 - 38 (S/A)^2 MeV
- The surface energy at saturation is 10.36 A^(2/3) MeV for a surface thickness parameter a=0.53 fm, with the gradient term parameter B=85.47 MeV·fm^5
- For the excluded volume model, the incompressibility reaches a minimum value of 754 MeV at b=3.60

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear incompressibility at zero temperature (Blaizot 1980, Pearson 1991) to include temperature and entropy dependence in asymmetric systems, building on earlier finite-temperature calculations (Muller 1995, Mekjian 2004). The results enable better understanding of nuclear matter properties relevant to heavy ion collisions, supernovae explosions, and neutron stars, and provide simple analytic expressions that can be used to estimate incompressibility for various equations of state and nuclear systems.
