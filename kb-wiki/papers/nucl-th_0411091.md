# Coulomb breakup effects on the optical potentials of weakly bound nuclei

> The optical potential of halo and weakly bound nuclei has a long range part due to the coupling to breakup that damps the elastic scattering angular distributions. In order to describe correctly the breakup channel in the case of scattering on a heavy target, core recoil effects have to be taken into account. We show here that core recoil and nuclear breakup of the valence nucleon can be consistently taken into account. A microscopic absorptive potential is obtained within a semiclassical approach and its characteristics can be understood in terms of the properties of the halo wave function and of the reaction mechanism. Results for the case of medium to high energy reactions are presented.

# Coulomb breakup effects on the optical potentials of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim

The authors show that core recoil and nuclear breakup of the valence nucleon can be consistently taken into account within a semiclassical approach to obtain a microscopic absorptive optical potential, but the results are exploratory due to the lack of experimental data and reliable bare potentials for the core-target interaction.

## Method

The imaginary part of the optical potential due to breakup was extracted from the relation between the surface optical potential and the breakup probability, using a semiclassical method based on the time-dependent scattering Schrodinger equation. Nuclear breakup was calculated in the eikonal approximation, while Coulomb breakup was treated in first-order perturbation theory, with the total breakup probability given by the sum of the two independent contributions. The breakup probabilities were fitted to sums of exponentials, and the potentials were then obtained from the relation between the integrated potential and the probability along a straight-line trajectory.

## Key numbers

- For a typical halo separation energy of 0.5 MeV, the decay length parameter a = (2γ)^{-1} = 3.2 fm; for a normal binding energy of 10 MeV, a = 0.7 fm.
- The diffuseness of the nuclear breakup potential is between 2 and 3 fm, depending weakly on incident energy.
- The Coulomb breakup potential diffuseness β₃ ≈ (ε_k^{max} - ε_i)/ħv, where ε_k^{max} = 2-3 MeV.
- For ¹¹Be on ²⁰⁸Pb at 20 A.MeV, the strong absorption radius increases by about 1 fm; at 40 A.MeV by 0.8 fm; at 80 A.MeV by 0.6 fm.
- The reaction cross section increase from nuclear breakup is 500-600 mb; from Coulomb breakup it is 1.6 b to 4.4 b depending on incident energy.
- Total reaction cross sections including both breakup channels: 7074 mb at 20 A.MeV, 5900 mb at 40 A.MeV, 4584 mb at 80 A.MeV.
- Coulomb halo breakup cross sections: 4280 mb at 20 A.MeV, 2638 mb at 40 A.MeV, 1570 mb at 80 A.MeV.
- Nuclear halo breakup cross sections: 615 mb at 20 A.MeV, 551 mb at 40 A.MeV, 490 mb at 80 A.MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical approach (Ibraheem and Bonaccorso, 2004) for nuclear breakup optical potentials to include Coulomb breakup effects, which are known to be dominant for neutron halo projectiles on heavy targets. The method is consistent with the lower-energy formalism of Andres et al. (1999) and with the eikonal method of Canto et al. (1995), but the authors note that the approach of Carlos et al. (1996) for proton halo breakup is not applicable to neutron breakup. The results enable calculation of elastic scattering angular distributions and reaction cross sections for halo nuclei such as ¹¹Be on heavy targets, though the authors emphasize the need for experimental data and reliable core-target potentials to validate the predictions.
