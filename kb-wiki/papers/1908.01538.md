# Electroweak Current Operators in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> In this proceeding I briefly review current status of the construction of nuclear electro-weak currents within chiral effective field theory. I show that gauge and chiral symmetry requirements lead to the well-known continuity equations for the current and charge operators which, however, get modified at higher orders. Regularization of the current will be also discussed. I demonstrate that implementation of a cutoff regulator in a naive way leads to violation of chiral symmetry. To respect the underlying symmetries I propose to use higher derivative regularization in the nuclear forces and currents.

## Key claim

Gauge and chiral symmetry requirements lead to continuity equations for current and charge operators that are modified at higher orders by explicit energy-transfer dependence, and a naive cutoff regularization of these currents violates chiral symmetry, requiring instead a symmetry-preserving higher derivative regularization.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of nuclear electroweak current operators within chiral effective field theory using the unitary transformation (UT) technique, which decouples pion-nucleon and purely nucleonic states in Fock space. Current operators are extracted as functional derivatives of the rotated Hamiltonian with respect to external sources, and their chiral expansion is carried out up to N3LO (next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order). The author then demonstrates the inconsistency of combining dimensionally regularized currents with cutoff-regularized nuclear forces by showing that the first iteration of a relativistic axial-vector two-nucleon current with a cutoff-regularized one-pion-exchange potential produces a linear cutoff singularity that cannot be absorbed by any chiral-symmetry-allowed counterterm.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is given as Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV.
* The breakdown scale of the chiral expansion is Lambda_b ~ 600 MeV.
* Nuclear forces have been worked out up to N4LO (chiral dimension nu=5), while electroweak currents have been calculated up to N3LO (chiral dimension nu=1).
* The leading order for nuclear forces starts at nu=0, and for vector and axial-vector currents starts at nu=-3.
* The linear cutoff singularity from the inconsistent regularization scales as Lambda times a factor g_A^3/(32 sqrt(2) pi^(3/2) F_pi^4).

## Context

This proceeding reviews the status of nuclear electroweak currents in chiral EFT, extending the work of Weinberg (1991) on nuclear forces and the pioneering calculations by Park et al. (1993, 1995). It compares the UT technique used by the Bochum-Bonn group (Krebs, 2016, 2019) with the time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) approach used by the Pisa-JLab group (Pastore, 2008, 2009; Piarulli, 2012; Baroni, 2015), noting that while vector currents can be related by a unitary transformation, axial-vector currents disagree even at zero momentum transfer. The key advance enabled is the development of a consistent regularization scheme using higher derivative regularization (Slavnov, 1971) that preserves chiral symmetry when applied to both nuclear forces and currents simultaneously, which is described as work in progress.
