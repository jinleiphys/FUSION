# Covariant Chiral Kinetic Equation in Non-Abelian Gauge field from "covariant gradient expansion"

> We derive the chiral kinetic equation in 8 dimensional phase space in non-Abelian $SU(N)$ gauge field within the Wigner function formalism. By using the "covariant gradient expansion", we disentangle the Wigner equations in four-vector space up to the first order and find that only the time-like component of the chiral Wigner function is independent while other components can be explicit derivative. After further decomposing the Wigner function or equations in color space, we present the non-Abelian covariant chiral kinetic equation for the color singlet and multiplet phase-space distribution functions. These phase-space distribution functions have non-trivial Lorentz transformation rules when we define them in different reference frames. The chiral anomaly from non-Abelian gauge field arises naturally from the Berry monopole in Euclidian momentum space in the vacuum or Dirac sea contribution. The anomalous currents as non-Abelian counterparts of chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect have also been derived from the non-Abelian chiral kinetic equation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a covariant chiral kinetic equation for non-Abelian SU(N) gauge fields using the Wigner function formalism with a "covariant gradient expansion," showing that only the time-like component of the chiral Wigner function is independent up to first order, but unlike the Abelian case, extra constraint equations arise for the zeroth-order Wigner function due to the non-commutativity of covariant derivatives.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum transport theory for massless fermions in an external non-Abelian gauge field, using the Wigner function formalism with gauge-invariant density matrices and Wilson lines. They apply a "covariant gradient expansion" to expand the Wigner equations in powers of ℏ up to first order, then decompose the Wigner functions along an auxiliary time-like vector n^μ to disentangle the equations. Finally, they further decompose the results in color space into singlet and multiplet components using SU(N) generators.

## Key numbers

- The Wigner function is expanded as W(x,p) = Σ_k ℏ^k W^(k)(x,p), with the zeroth-order result given by J^(0)μ = p^μ f^(0) δ(p^2)
- The first-order Wigner function contains terms proportional to δ'(p^2) with coefficients involving the field strength F_{αβ} and the coupling g
- The constraint equation for the zeroth-order Wigner function is [F̃^{αβ}, J^{(0)}_α] = 0, where F̃^{αβ} = (1/2) ε^{αβμν} F_{μν}
- The first-order constraint equation is [F̃_{αβ}, J^{(1)α}] = -(3/32)[[F̃_{να}∂^p_β - F̃_{νβ}∂^p_α, F^{νκ}∂^p_κ], J^{(0)α}]

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory from Abelian gauge fields (previously developed by Gao (2018) and others) to non-Abelian SU(N) gauge fields relevant for quantum chromodynamics in heavy ion collisions. It builds on the quantum transport theory formalism of Heinz (1983) and Elze (1986, 1989) and the covariant gradient expansion method of Ochs (1998). The results enable the derivation of non-Abelian counterparts of the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect, and provide a framework for studying the decoherence from classical color fields to quark-gluon plasma in the color glass condensate picture.
