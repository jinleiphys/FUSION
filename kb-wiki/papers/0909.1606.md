# Master formula approach to broken chiral U(3)xU(3) symmetry

> The master formula approach to chiral symmetry breaking proposed by Yamagishi and Zahed is extended to the U_R(3)xU_L(3) group, in which effects of the U_A(1) anomaly and the flavor symmetry breaking m_u \not= m_d \not= m_s are properly contained. New identities for the gluon topological susceptibility and pi^0, eta, eta' -> gamma^(*) gamma^(*) decays are derived, which exactly embody the consequence from broken chiral symmetry in QCD without relying on any unphysical limit.

## Key claim

The paper extends the master formula approach to chiral symmetry breaking, originally formulated for SU(2)×SU(2) and SU(3)×SU(3) in the isospin symmetric limit, to the full U_R(3)×U_L(3) group while properly incorporating both the U_A(1) anomaly and the flavor symmetry breaking m_u ≠ m_d ≠ m_s, without relying on any unphysical limits such as chiral, soft pion, or large N_c expansions.

## Method

The author generalizes the Veltman-Bell equations to the U_R(3)×U_L(3) group by introducing new pseudoscalar and scalar external fields J^P and Y^P that separate the one-pion component from the axial current and the gluon topological charge density. A new extended S-matrix operator is constructed with additional contact terms that account for the quark-antiquark condensation and the non-Abelian anomaly, yielding master equations that incorporate the renormalization-group-invariant decay constants of Shore and Veneziano and the mass relations for the full nonet of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_π and pseudoscalar coupling constant G satisfy the mass relation f_π m_π² = m̂ G for the SU(2) case, with the constant C = f_π G appearing in the scalar density shift
- The U(3) mass relation is given by (f_π m_π² f_π^T)^{ab} = (m̄_q G f_π^T)^{ab} + A^{ab}, where A^{ab} = Tr[λ^a](A f_π^T)^b and A^{ab}=0 for a≠0
- The RG-invariant decay constant f_π^{0P} is introduced to replace the scale-dependent t̃_π^{0P}, with the combination (t̃_π m_π²)^{0P} - Tr[λ⁰]Ã^P being RG invariant

## Context

This work extends the master formula approach of Yamagishi and Zahed (1996) and Lee (1999) from the two-flavor and three-flavor isospin-symmetric cases to the full U(3) flavor group with explicit symmetry breaking. The approach provides a systematic procedure (chiral reduction formula) for deriving chiral Ward identities for scattering amplitudes involving any number of pions with physical masses, enabling future investigations of η and η' meson reactions and gluonic aspects of low-energy QCD without relying on unphysical limits.
