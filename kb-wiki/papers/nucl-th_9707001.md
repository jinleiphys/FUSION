# Fine-Tuning Two-Particle Interferometry I: Effects from Temperature Gradients in the Source

> A comprehensive model study of Bose-Einstein correlation radii in heavy ion collisions is presented. The starting point is a longitudinally and transversally expanding fireball, represented at freeze-out by an azimuthally symmetric emission function. The freeze-out temperature is allowed to feature transverse and temporal gradients. Their effects on the correlation radii are studied. In particular, we evaluate numerically their dependence on the transverse mass of the particle pairs and check a recent suggestion, based on analytical approximations, that for certain reasonable source parameters all three correlation radii satisfy simultaneously a 1/\sqrt{M_\perp} scaling.

## Key claim

The paper numerically tests whether all three Cartesian HBT radii can simultaneously satisfy a \(1/\sqrt{M_\perp}\) scaling law, as suggested by earlier analytical approximations (Chapman and Nix, 1995; Chapman et al., 1996). The authors find that, within the phenomenologically allowed parameter range, this common scaling does not hold even when temperature gradients are included; instead, the longitudinal radius shows a stronger \(M_\perp\)-dependence than the transverse radius, consistent with earlier numerical studies of sources with constant temperature (Wiedemann et al., 1996; Heiselberg and Vischer, 1996).

## Method

The authors model the source as a longitudinally and transversely expanding fireball with an azimuthally symmetric emission function at freeze-out, using a hydrodynamic parametrisation with boost-invariant longitudinal flow and a linear radial profile for transverse flow. The freeze-out temperature is allowed to have both transverse and temporal gradients, parameterized by coefficients \(a\) and \(d\) respectively. The HBT radii are computed numerically from space-time variances of the emission function in both the Cartesian (Pratt-Bertsch-Chapman) and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski\u\i (YKP) parametrisations, for pions and kaons at various rapidities and transverse masses.

## Key numbers

- Source parameters: freeze-out temperature \(T_0 = 100\) MeV, average freeze-out proper time \(\tau_0 = 7.8\) fm/\(c\), mean proper emission duration \(\Delta \tau = 2\) fm/\(c\), geometric transverse radius \(R = 7\) fm, Gaussian width of space-time rapidity profile \(\Delta \eta = 1.3\).
- For a source without transverse flow or temperature gradients, the longitudinal radius parameters \(R_l\) and \(R_\parallel\) scale approximately as \(M_\perp^{-0.5}\) (exponents \(\alpha_l\) and \(\alpha_\parallel\) range from 0.478 to 0.626 depending on rapidity and particle species), while the temporal YKP parameter \(R_0\) decreases more slowly (exponents \(\alpha_0\) range from 0.185 to 0.403).
- Transverse temperature gradient parameter \(a = 0.8\) reduces the transverse radius \(R_s = R_\perp\) compared to the case \(a = 0\), but does not break the \(M_\perp\)-scaling of the YKP radii.
- Temporal temperature gradient parameter \(d = 1.5\) reduces the temporal YKP parameter \(R_0\) and the Cartesian difference \(R_o^2 - R_s^2\) compared to \(d = 0\).

## Context

This work extends numerical studies of expanding sources with constant freeze-out temperature (Wiedemann et al., 1996; Heiselberg and Vischer, 1996) by adding transverse and temporal temperature gradients, following the analytical framework of Chapman and Nix (1995, 1996). It directly tests the claim by Chapman and Nix (1995) that all three Cartesian HBT radii could obey a common \(1/\sqrt{M_\perp}\) scaling, and finds this scaling does not hold for phenomenologically realistic parameters. The results enable more quantitative comparisons with experimental data from CERN SPS collisions (NA44, NA35, NA49) and clarify the separate roles of flow and temperature gradients in shaping the correlation radii.
