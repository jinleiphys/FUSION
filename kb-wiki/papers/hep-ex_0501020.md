# The antinucleon-nucleon interaction at low energy : annihilation dynamics

> The general properties of antiproton-proton annihilation at rest are presented, with special focus on the two-meson final states. The data exhibit remarkable dynamical selection rules : some allowed annihilation modes are suppressed by one order of magnitude with respect to modes of comparable phase-space. Various phenomenological analyses are reviewed, based on microscopic quark dynamics or symmetry considerations. The role of initial- and final-state interaction is also examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that antiproton-proton annihilation at rest exhibits dynamical selection rules that suppress certain allowed two-meson annihilation modes by about one order of magnitude relative to modes with comparable phase space, with the suppression linked to the flavor quantum numbers of intermediate four-quark configurations rather than to any fundamental conservation law.

## Method

The authors review experimental data from LEAR experiments (Crystal Barrel, Obelix, Asterix) on antiproton-proton annihilation at rest into two-meson final states, including Dalitz plot analyses and partial-wave decompositions. They apply a dynamical correction model proposed by Vandermeulen, extended to account for centrifugal barrier effects, to extract branching ratios from specified initial atomic states and compare these to predictions from quark-line diagrams and symmetry considerations.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for $\rho\pi$ production from the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ initial state: $(63.6 \pm 3.3) \times 10^{-3}$; from the $^1\mathrm{S}_0$ state: $(6.0 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-3}$, giving a ratio of $0.094 \pm 0.032$
- Ratio of $\mathrm{a}_2\pi$ production from the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ isovector state versus the $^1\mathrm{S}_0$ isoscalar state: $(6.4 \pm 1.2) / (82.0 \pm 12.0)$
- Branching ratio for $\pi\pi$ from the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ state: $(2.79 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-3}$; from the $^3\mathrm{P}_0$ state: $(54.3 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-3}$
- Branching ratio for $\mathrm{K}^+\mathrm{K}^-$ from the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ state: $(1.47 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-3}$; from the $^3\mathrm{P}_0$ state: $(4.2 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-3}$
- Fireball temperature of 120 MeV and size $1/m_\pi$ for thermodynamic description of pion multiplicity distributions

## Context

This review synthesizes results from the LEAR program, extending earlier bubble-chamber studies and providing a comprehensive framework for understanding annihilation dynamics that had previously been treated as a side product of meson spectroscopy. The dynamical selection rules identified here connect to the "$\rho\pi$ puzzle" known in the literature and to quark-line diagram analyses by Dover (1992) and others. The work enables future comparisons with lattice QCD simulations, effective Lagrangian approaches, and studies of hadronization in heavy quark decays, while noting that polarized antiproton experiments were not carried out at LEAR and remain needed.
