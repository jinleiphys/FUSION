# Hard pion bremsstrahlung in the Coulomb region

> Hard high-energy pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung, $π^- +A\toπ^- +γ+A$, is studied in the Coulomb region, i.e. the small-angle region where the nuclear scattering is dominated by the Coulomb interaction. Special attention is focussed on the possibility of measuring the pion polarizability in such reactions. We study the sensitivity to the structure of the underlying the pion-Compton amplitude through a model with $σ$, $ρ$, and a_1 exchanges. It is found that the effective energy in the virtual pion-Compton scattering is often so large that the threshold approximation does not apply.

# Hard pion bremsstrahlung in the Coulomb region

## Key claim

The authors model the energy dependence of the pion-Compton amplitude using sigma, rho, and a1 meson exchanges to assess whether hard pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung in the Coulomb region can measure pion polarizabilities. They find that when the polarizability contributions become appreciable (near x = omega_2/E_1 close to unity), the effective energy in the virtual pion-Compton scattering is often so large that the threshold approximation to the polarizability functions breaks down.

## Method

The authors develop a Glauber-model-based formalism for the reaction pi- + A -> pi- + gamma + A in the Coulomb region (small momentum transfers to the nucleus where Coulomb interaction dominates). They model the pion-Compton amplitude as a sum of Born terms plus contributions from sigma(0+), rho(1-), and a1(1+) exchange diagrams, with coupling constants fixed from experimental data on gamma gamma -> pi pi and radiative decay widths. The cross-section distributions are derived for two kinematic regimes: the double Coulomb region (both transverse momenta of order q_min) and the single Coulomb region (transverse momenta much larger than m_pi but with their vector sum remaining small).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 139.6 MeV
- Rho meson mass: m_rho = 775 MeV, total width Gamma_rho = 150 MeV
- a1 meson mass: m_a1 = 1230 MeV, total width Gamma_a1 = 450 MeV
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 547 MeV, with Gamma(sigma -> gamma gamma) = 0.62 keV and Gamma(sigma -> pi+ pi-) = 803 MeV (from Fil'kov and Kashevarov), but the authors adopt a reduced product of coupling constants g_{sigma -> gamma gamma} * g_{sigma -> pi pi} = 0.400
- Rho radiative width: Gamma(rho+ -> pi+ gamma) = 68 keV, giving m_rho * g_{rho -> pi gamma} = 0.5644
- a1 radiative width: Gamma(a1+ -> pi+ gamma) = 640 keV, giving m_a1 * g_{a1 -> pi gamma} = 1.334
- Chiral-Lagrangian threshold values: alpha_pi + beta_pi = 0, alpha_pi = 2.7 x 10^{-4} fm^3; threshold polarizability functions lambda_1(m_pi^2,0) = 0 and lambda_2(m_pi^2,0) = -0.013
- In the exchange model at threshold: lambda_1 = 0 + 0.0003 + 0.0003 (from rho and a1), lambda_2 = -0.0261 + 0.0004 + 0 (sigma and rho contributions)
- For the single Coulomb region illustration with q_{2_perp} = 3.5 m_pi, the center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) ranges from 990 to 1230 MeV over x in (0.2, 0.8)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Glauber model for pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung (Faldt and Tengblad, 2005, cited as PrimakI), which retained only Born terms and polarizabilities. The present paper adds meson-exchange contributions to model the energy dependence of the pion-Compton amplitude, following the approach of Fil'kov and Kashevarov for gamma gamma -> pi pi. The study is motivated by earlier suggestions (from the 1970s and 1980s) that pionic bremsstrahlung could extract pion polarizabilities, and by a COMPASS experiment that obtained reasonable polarizability values. The results show that extracting reliable values for alpha_pi and beta_pi requires accurate experiments, because the kinematic region where polarizability effects become visible also forces the pion-Compton energy into a regime where the threshold approximation is unreliable.
