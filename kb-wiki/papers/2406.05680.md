# Thermodynamically consistent accreted crust of neutron stars: The role of proton shell effects

> Observations of accreting neutron stars are widely used to constrain the microphysical properties of superdense matter. A key ingredient in this analysis is the heating associated with nuclear reactions in the outer layers of the neutron star (crust), as well as the equation of state and composition of these layers. As recently shown, the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion (nHD) condition is valid in the inner part of the crust, where some of the neutrons are not bound to the nuclei, and this condition should be properly incorporated into crustal models. Here we construct models of the accreted crust of a neutron star, taking into account the nHD condition and proton shell effects in nuclei. For numerical illustration, we employ the recently proposed compressible liquid drop model, which incorporates shell effects. However, our approach is general and can also be used in future studies relying on more sophisticated nuclear physics models.

## Key claim

The paper constructs models of the accreted crust of a neutron star that simultaneously incorporate the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion (nHD) condition, which requires the redshifted neutron chemical potential to be constant in the inner crust, and proton shell effects in nuclei, but the authors treat their shell-effect models as qualitative proof-of-principle demonstrations rather than definitive quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors developed a step-by-step algorithm that advances through layers of the inner crust, at each step increasing the pressure and updating the neutron chemical potential via the nHD condition, then determining the optimal nuclear charge number Z by minimizing the thermodynamic potential Ψ at fixed pressure and neutron chemical potential. They applied this algorithm using the compressible liquid drop model with proton shell corrections added on top (CLD+sh model) from Carreau et al. (2020), treating the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface as a free parameter to generate a family of nHD equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, P_oi, is constrained from below by a critical value P_oi^(min) at which an instability causes complete disintegration of nuclei; the instability occurs when the second derivative of the potential ψ with respect to Z vanishes at fixed pressure and neutron chemical potential.
- The neutron chemical potential at the top of the inner crust is set to μ_n,oi = m_n c^2 by definition, where m_n is the bare neutron mass and c is the speed of light.
- For the numerical illustration with pure ⁵⁶Fe ash composition, the typical nuclear charge realized in the inner crust is Z ≈ 20, which is too large for pycnonuclear reactions to occur at significant rates.

## Context

This work extends the nHD crust framework introduced by Gusakov and Chugunov (2020) by incorporating proton shell effects, which were previously neglected in that smooth compressible liquid drop model. The approach enables future studies using more sophisticated nuclear physics models to determine the heat release profile and equation of state of accreting neutron star crusts, which are essential for interpreting observations of crust cooling following accretion episodes (e.g., from nine sources analyzed by Brown et al. 2018 and others).
