# Two Ridges, One Explanation

> We attribute the phenomenon known as "the ridge" to long range initial state correlations from Color Glass Condensate flux tubes and later stage radial flow. We show that this description can explain the amplitude and azimuthal width of the soft ridge and nearly explain that of the hard ridge, suggesting that the two are essentially the same phenomenon.

# Two Ridges, One Explanation

## Key claim
The soft ridge and hard ridge are essentially the same phenomenon, both arising from long-range initial-state correlations from Color Glass Condensate (CGC) flux tubes combined with later-stage radial flow. The authors show that bulk-bulk correlations alone account for a significant fraction of the hard ridge data, and adding jet-bulk correlations gives agreement with both amplitude and azimuthal width.

## Method
The authors extend their earlier soft ridge model (Moschelli and Gavin, 2009) by increasing the lower transverse momentum ($p_t$) limit of correlated particles toward the hard ridge range. They calculate the quantity $\left[\Delta\rho/\sqrt{\rho_{ref}}\right]_{p_{t~min}}$ integrated over $p_{t~min}<p_{t,1},p_{t,2}<\infty$ for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, and convert $\Delta\rho/\sqrt{\rho_{ref}}$ to yield for direct comparison to hard ridge data. They also calculate jet-bulk correlations by correlating the radial position of a hard collision with a flux tube at the same position, following the approach of Shuryak (2007).

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au 200 GeV, bulk-bulk correlations alone are a "significant fraction" of the hard ridge data and "slightly narrower" than the data (no explicit numerical fraction given in text).
- The jet-bulk contribution contributes "less to the amplitude" and is "wider" than the data (no explicit numerical values given in text).
- The addition of jet-bulk and bulk-bulk yields gives "nice agreement with both the amplitude and azimuthal width of the data" (no explicit numerical values given in text).
- The data shown is for correlations with pseudorapidity separation $\eta>1$ unit of rapidity apart.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier model (Moschelli and Gavin, 2009) that explained the soft ridge using CGC flux tubes and radial flow, and applies it to the hard ridge previously thought to be a separate phenomenon involving jets. The hard ridge was measured with a jet trigger by Putschke (2007) and Daugherity (2006). The jet-bulk correlation calculation follows Shuryak (2007) with the modification of correlating hard collision position with flux tube position. This explanation suggests that long-range correlations in both soft and hard ridges share a common origin, enabling a unified description of ridge phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.
