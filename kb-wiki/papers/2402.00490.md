# Testing the Number of Neutrino Species with a Global Fit of Neutrino Data

> We present the first experimental constraints on models with many additional neutrino species in an analysis of current neutrino data. These types of models are motivated as a solution to the hierarchy problem by lowering the species scale of gravity to TeV. Additionally, they offer a natural mechanism to generate small neutrino masses and provide interesting dark matter candidates. This study analyzes data from DayaBay, KamLAND, MINOS, NOvA and KATRIN. We do not find evidence for the presence of any additional neutrino species, therefore we report lower bounds on the allowed number of neutrino species realized in nature. For the normal/inverted neutrino mass ordering, we can give a lower bound on the number of neutrino species of O(30) and O(100), respectively, over a large range of the parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first experimental constraints on the Dvali-Redi (DR) model, which introduces many copies of the Standard Model, using a global fit of neutrino oscillation and mass data; the authors do not find evidence for additional neutrino species and instead report lower bounds on the number of species, with the qualification that these bounds apply "over a large range of the parameter space" and depend on the neutrino mass ordering.

## Method

The authors performed a combined maximum likelihood fit of nine free parameters (the six Standard Model oscillation parameters plus the lightest neutrino mass, the number of neutrino copies N, and the mass ratio mu) to publicly available data from five experiments: KamLAND (reactor antineutrinos, solar mass splitting regime), DayaBay (reactor antineutrinos, atmospheric mass splitting regime), MINOS/MINOS+ (accelerator muon neutrinos, off oscillation maximum), NOvA (accelerator muon neutrinos, at oscillation maximum), and KATRIN (tritium beta decay, electron neutrino mass endpoint). The DR model predicts a specific pattern where the three heavy mass eigenstates are related to the three Standard Model mass eigenstates by a single parameter mu, and the mixing between Standard Model and heavy states is controlled by N. A likelihood ratio test statistic was used, profiling over all nuisance parameters, to set exclusion limits on N as a function of mu.

## Key numbers

- For normal neutrino mass ordering, the lower bound on the number of neutrino species N is O(30) over a large range of the parameter space.
- For inverted neutrino mass ordering, the lower bound on the number of neutrino species N is O(100) over a large range of the parameter space.
- The Yukawa matrix element b is bounded by unitarity as b <= 1/sqrt(N), with a expected to be of the same order.
- The DR model introduces only two beyond-Standard-Model parameters (N and mu), compared to 22 independent parameters in a general 3+3 sterile neutrino framework.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the DR model by Ettengruber (2022) and Dvali and Redi (2009), which proposed that small Dirac neutrino masses arise naturally from many Standard Model copies, and provides the first experimental test of this framework using terrestrial neutrino data. The analysis is complementary to cosmological constraints on the DR model from Ettengruber (2023) and Zander (2023), but deliberately avoids model-dependent cosmological assumptions by relying only on laboratory experiments. The results enable future searches for deviations from Standard Model oscillations in the DR parameter space, particularly at experiments sensitive to the atmospheric mass splitting regime where DayaBay provides the strongest constraints on N.
