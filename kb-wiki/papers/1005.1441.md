# Critical Examination of the "Field-Theoretical Approach" to the Neutron-Antineutron Oscillations in Nuclei

> We demonstrate that so called "infrared divergences" which have been discussed in some publications during several years, do not appear within the correct treatment of analytical properties of the transition amplitudes, in particular, of the second order pole structure of the amplitudes describing the $n - \bar n$ transition in nuclei. Explicit calculation with the help of the Feynman diagram technique shows that the neutron-antineutron oscillations are strongly suppressed in the deuteron, as well as in heavier nuclei, in comparison with the oscillations in vacuum. General advantages and some difficulties of the field theoretical methods applied in nuclear theory are reminded for the particular example of the parity violating $np\to dγ$ capture amplitude.

# Critical Examination of the "Field-Theoretical Approach" to the Neutron-Antineutron Oscillations in Nuclei

## Key claim

The "infrared divergences" previously claimed to appear in the neutron-antineutron transition amplitude in nuclei do not arise when the second-order pole structure of the amplitude is treated correctly using standard Feynman diagram techniques; the neutron-antineutron oscillations are strongly suppressed in the deuteron and heavier nuclei compared to vacuum, contrary to claims by V.I. Nazaruk (VIN1, VIN2) that the suppression does not occur.

## Method

The authors perform explicit Feynman diagram calculations of the neutron-antineutron transition amplitude in nuclei, treating the second-order pole in the intermediate energy variable via the Cauchy theorem. For the deuteron case, they integrate over the internal 4-momentum in the nonrelativistic approximation, taking the annihilation amplitude out of the integration at small momentum transfer, and relate the result to the deuteron charge formfactor normalization at zero momentum transfer as a consistency check.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-antineutron oscillation time in vacuum from reactor experiments is greater than 0.86 × 10^8 sec (approximately 2.7 years), corresponding to μ_{n\bar n} < 1.5 × 10^{-23} eV.
- The deuteron binding energy is ε_d ≈ 2.22 MeV, with κ = √(m_N ε_d).
- The normalization constant g_{dnp} satisfies g²_{dnp}/(16π) = √(ε_d/m_N) ≈ 0.049.
- The annihilation cross section at low velocity satisfies [v_0 σ^{ann}_{ȳ p}]_{v_0→0} ≈ (50–55) mb ≈ (130–140) GeV^{-2}.
- From deuteron stability data (τ_d > 6.5 × 10^{31} yr, using the Fe nucleus limit), the authors obtain μ_{n\bar n} ≤ 2.5 × 10^{-24} eV, or τ_{n\bar n} > 5 × 10^8 sec.
- The additional suppression factor for the deuteron compared to a free neutron is of order μ_{n\bar n}/κ ∼ 10^{-31}.
- The resulting nuclear decay width is Γ(A → (A-2) + mesons) < 10^{-30} μ_{n\bar n}, at least 30 orders of magnitude smaller than the inverse vacuum oscillation time μ_{n\bar n}.

## Context

This paper extends the critique of the "field-theoretical approach" of Nazaruk (VIN1, VIN2) that claimed no suppression of neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei. The authors follow the diagram technique of Kondratyuk (LAK) and agree with results from potential approaches by Friedman and Gal (FAGA) and diagrammatic calculations by Kopeliovich et al. (BZK). The work also draws an analogy between the second-order pole in the neutron-antineutron transition amplitude and the deuteron charge formfactor at zero momentum transfer, a well-understood case with no infrared divergence. The result enables consistent extraction of the neutron-antineutron transition parameter from nuclear stability experiments, with the vacuum oscillation time bound from reactor experiments (BAL) remaining the stronger constraint.
