# Global study of quadrupole correlation effects

> We discuss the systematics of ground-state quadrupole correlations of binding energies and mean-square charge radii for all even-even nuclei, from O16 up to the superheavies, for which data are available. To that aim we calculate their correlated J=0 ground state by means of the angular-momentum and particle-number projected generator coordinate method, using the axial mass quadrupole moment as the generator coordinate and self-consistent mean-field states only restricted by axial, parity, and time-reversal symmetries. The calculation is performed within the framework of a non-relativistic self-consistent mean-field model using the same non-relativistic Skyrme interaction SLy4 and a density-dependent pairing force to generate the mean-field configurations and mix them. (See the paper for the rest of the abstract).

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates quadrupole correlation energies for all even-even nuclei with known masses, finding that the total dynamical correlation energy varies between a few hundred keV and about 5.5 MeV, but that the magnitude of suppression of shell effects on binding energies around magic numbers is not large enough to explain the relative overbinding at N=82 and N=126 neutron shell closures in mean-field models.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum and particle-number projected generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations using the axial mass quadrupole moment as the generator coordinate, starting from self-consistent mean-field states generated with the Skyrme interaction SLy4 and a density-dependent pairing force. The mean-field states were restricted by axial, parity, and time-reversal symmetries, and the GCM mixing was performed together with projection on J=0 and on good particle numbers. To reduce computational cost, the authors employed a topological Gaussian overlap approximation (topGOA) for the angular-momentum projection kernels, using either a two-point or three-point approximation depending on the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole correlation energy varies between a few 100 keV and about 5.5 MeV across all even-even nuclei studied
- Angular-momentum projection on J=0 provides the major part of the energy gain, up to about 4 MeV
- Mixing of projected states with different intrinsic axial deformation adds a few 100 keV up to 1.5 MeV to the correlation energy
- The study covered 605 even-even nuclei from O-16 up to the superheavies
- The mesh spacing used was 0.8 fm, with box side lengths ranging from 25.6 to 28.8 fm
- The pairing strength was taken as -1000 MeV fm^3 for both protons and neutrons
- The SCMF energy code has an accuracy for a mesh size of 0.8 fm given roughly by 0.007 A MeV, where A is the mass number

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of quadrupole correlations in nuclear binding energies and charge radii, addressing the need for explicit treatment of correlation effects beyond the mean-field approximation. The authors note that previous self-consistent mean-field theories based on Skyrme parameterizations achieved rms residuals in the range 1.5-1.7 MeV (Bertsch et al., 2005), while fits including phenomenological corrections (Tondeur et al., 2000; Moller et al., 1995) reach about 0.6-0.7 MeV rms. The results enable improved description of mass systematics, particularly around shell closures and for differential quantities like two-nucleon separation energies and two-nucleon gaps, and provide a foundation for a universal nuclear model that can simultaneously describe many observables including excited states.
