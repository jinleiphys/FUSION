# Charmonium Production at the SPS

> We reexamine the production of J/Psi and other charmonium states for a variety of target-projectile choices at the SPS, in particular for the interesting comparison between S+U at 200 GeV/c and Pb+Pb at 158 GeV/c as observed in the experiments NA38 and NA50 respectively. The result is a description of the NA38 and NA50 data in terms of a conventional, quasi-hadronic picture. The apparently anomalous suppression found in the most massive Pb+Pb system arises in the present simulation from three sources: destruction in the initial nucleon-nucleon cascade phase, use of coupled channels to exploit the larger breakup in the less bound Chi and Psi' states, and comover interaction in the final low energy phase.

## Key claim

The apparently anomalous J/ψ suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS can be explained within a conventional, quasi-hadronic picture using a two-phase cascade model, without invoking quark-gluon plasma creation, provided that coupled-channel effects from higher charmonium states (χ and ψ′) and comover interactions are included.

## Method

The authors employed a two-stage cascade (LUCIFER II) that separates hard and soft processes by time scale: a high-energy fast cascade records nucleon-nucleon collision histories and produces charmonium states, followed by a reinitialization and a low-energy soft cascade that includes secondary meson (comover) interactions and baryonic breakup. The model incorporates a coupled-channel treatment of the charmonium family {J/ψ, χ^i, ψ′}, with state-dependent absorption cross-sections and feeding decays from higher states into J/ψ, and uses a reaction matrix for charmonium states constrained by unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross-sections used: 6.6 mb for J/ψ, 20.0 mb for χ^i, and 25.2 mb for ψ′ in charmonium-baryon collisions; charmonium-meson cross-sections are reduced by a constituent quark factor of 2/3.
- Survival probabilities for J/ψ in S+U: 0.50 in the hard cascade and 0.67 in the soft cascade; for Pb+Pb: 0.42 in the hard cascade and 0.775 in the soft cascade.
- The χ to J/ψ production ratio in pp collisions is taken as χ/ψ ∼ 4.5, based on ISR data.
- The absorption cross-section that reproduces p+A observations at 800 GeV/c is σ_abs ∼ 7.0 mb; the coupled-channel model reproduces the Glauber result using a smaller direct breakup cross-section σ_abs(J/ψ) = 5–6.0 mb.
- Comover interactions contribute approximately 40% of the difference in suppression between S+U and Pb+Pb.
- The formation time for secondary mesons is taken as τ_f ∼ 0.5–1 fm/c.
- The theoretical E_t scale for Pb+Pb required a scaling factor of ∼1.25 to match the NA50 experimental scale, with 10–15% attributable to differences with NA49 measurements.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-based calculations of Kharzeev and Satz (1997) and the comover annihilation suggestions of Gavin (1996) by implementing a full dynamical cascade that includes coupled-channel charmonium effects. The authors argue that previous failures of hadronic models to describe the Pb+Pb data arose from neglecting the role of higher charmonium states (χ and ψ′) and their larger breakup cross-sections, combined with comover interactions in the soft cascade phase. The approach enables a conventional hadronic description of the NA38 and NA50 data without requiring quark-gluon plasma formation, though the authors note that high baryonic and mesonic energy densities (ρ_B ∼ 4–5 GeV/fm^3 and ρ_E ∼ 3 GeV/fm^3) are achieved in central Pb+Pb collisions, leaving open the possibility of partonic matter creation.
