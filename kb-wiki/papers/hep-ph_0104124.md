# Obtaining the nuclear gluon distribution from heavy quark decays to lepton pairs in p$A$ collisions

> We have studied how lepton pairs from decays of heavy-flavoured mesons produced in p$A$ collisions can be used to determine the modifications of the gluon distribution in the nucleus. Since heavy quark production is dominated by the $gg$ channel, the ratio of correlated lepton pair cross sections from $D\bar D$ and $B\bar B$ decays in p$A$ and pp collisions directly reflects the ratio $R_g^A \equiv f_g^A/f_g^p$. We have numerically calculated the lepton pair cross sections from these decays in pp and p$A$ collisions at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies. We find that ratio of the p$A$ to pp cross sections agrees quite well with the input $R_g^A.$ Thus, sufficiently accurate measurements could be used to determine the nuclear modification of the gluon distribution over a greater range of $x$ and $Q^2$ than presently available, putting strong constraints on models.

## Key claim

The ratio of correlated lepton pair cross sections from heavy quark decays in pA versus pp collisions closely follows the input nuclear gluon modification ratio \(R_g^A\), but always lies slightly below it due to residual contributions from the \(q\bar{q}\) channel and phase-space smearing effects.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo calculations of lepton pair production from semi-leptonic decays of \(D\bar{D}\) and \(B\bar{B}\) mesons in pp and pA collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. They used the MRST LO parton distribution functions with the EKS98 nuclear modifications, employed delta-function and Peterson fragmentation functions, and generated \(2 \times 10^6\) heavy quark pairs with \(10^3\) fragmentations each using JETSET/PYTHIA subroutines for meson decays.

- Total \(c\bar{c}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at SPS: 1.188 \(\mu\)b; in pPb at SPS: 1.282 \(\mu\)b
- Total \(c\bar{c}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at RHIC: 143.7 \(\mu\)b; in pAu at RHIC: 140.1 \(\mu\)b
- Total \(c\bar{c}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at LHC: 6137 \(\mu\)b; in pPb at LHC: 4826 \(\mu\)b
- Total \(b\bar{b}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at SPS: \(1.674 \times 10^{-5}\) \(\mu\)b; in pPb at SPS: \(1.464 \times 10^{-5}\) \(\mu\)b
- Total \(b\bar{b}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at RHIC: 1.412 \(\mu\)b; in pAu at RHIC: 1.513 \(\mu\)b
- Total \(b\bar{b}\) cross section per nucleon in pp at LHC: 251.9 \(\mu\)b; in pPb at LHC: 226.7 \(\mu\)b

- Quark masses used: \(m_c = 1.2\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.75\) GeV; meson masses: \(m_D = 1.8693\) GeV, \(m_B = 5.278\) GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gavai et al. (1996) and Lin and Gyulassy (1996) on using heavy quark production to probe nuclear gluon distributions, but now using the more constrained EKS98 parameterization of nuclear effects from Eskola et al. (1998, 1999). The results show that lepton pairs from heavy flavor decays in pA collisions can provide direct constraints on the nuclear gluon modification \(R_g^A\) over a wider range of \(x\) and \(Q^2\) than currently available from DIS measurements alone, particularly in the shadowing and antishadowing regions.
