# Solving the nuclear pairing model with neural network quantum states

> We present a variational Monte Carlo method that solves the nuclear many-body problem in the occupation number formalism exploiting an artificial neural network representation of the ground-state wave function. A memory-efficient version of the stochastic reconfiguration algorithm is developed to train the network by minimizing the expectation value of the Hamiltonian. We benchmark this approach against widely used nuclear many-body methods by solving a model used to describe pairing in nuclei for different types of interaction and different values of the interaction strength. Despite its polynomial computational cost, our method outperforms coupled-cluster and provides energies that are in excellent agreement with the numerically-exact full configuration interaction values.

# Solving the nuclear pairing model with neural network quantum states

## Key claim
The authors present a variational Monte Carlo method using neural network quantum states (NQS) in the occupation-number formalism that solves the nuclear pairing model with polynomial computational cost, achieving energies in excellent agreement with exact diagonalization across a wide range of interaction strengths, while respecting the variational principle, unlike coupled-cluster and many-body perturbation theory, which violate it in the strong-coupling regime.

## Method
The ground-state wave function is parametrized as a real-valued neural network ansatz consisting of two fully-connected neural networks (one for magnitude, one for sign) acting on occupation-number strings. The network is trained by minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation value using a memory-efficient stochastic reconfiguration algorithm regularized with an RMSprop-inspired diagonal shift. The method is benchmarked against exact diagonalization, iterative solutions of the Richardson-Gaudin equations, many-body perturbation theory (MBPT), and pair coupled cluster doubles (pCCD) for both constant and separable pairing interactions.

## Key numbers
- For the constant-coupling model with P=10 energy levels and M=5 pairs, VMC-NQS discrepancies from exact energies remain smaller than 10⁻⁴ over the entire range of g values considered.
- For the constant-coupling model with g=-0.05 and d_p=p/10, VMC-NQS yields correlation energy -1.785 for P=40, M=20, while the iterative Richardson method gives -2.111 (the authors note the iterative method did not converge for this case).
- For P=10, M=5 with constant coupling, pCCD yields approximately 20% overbinding (correlation energy -0.193 vs exact -0.160).
- For the separable-coupling model with P=10, M=5 and cutoff α=10, VMC-NQS matches exact diagonalization up to 8 significant digits for some values of the pairing strength.
- The overall algorithm complexity scales as O(P⁴) in the number of energy levels P.

## Context
This work extends the application of neural network quantum states from spin models (Carleo 2017) and quantum chemistry (Choo 2019, Hermann 2019, Pfau 2019) to nuclear pairing problems in the occupation-number formalism, complementing prior NQS work on light nuclei in first quantization (Adams 2020, Gnech 2021, Lovato 2022). The pairing model itself builds on the Richardson-Gaudin integrable models (Richardson 1963a, 1963b; Gaudin 1976) and the separable-pairing Hamiltonian that approximates Gogny interactions (Dukelsky 2011). The method enables future applications to realistic shell-model Hamiltonians and chiral effective field theory interactions, potentially overcoming limitations of matrix product state approaches (Tichai 2022).
