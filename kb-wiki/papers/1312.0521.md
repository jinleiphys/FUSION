# Quantitative studies of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski space

> The Bethe-Salpeter Equation for a bound system, composed by two massive scalars exchanging a massive scalar, is quantitatively investigated in ladder approximation, within the Nakanishi integral representation approach. For the S-wave case, numerical solutions with a form inspired by the Nakanishi integral representation, have been calculated. The needed Nakanishi weight functions have been evaluated by solving two different eigenequations, obtained directly from the Bethe-Salpeter equation applying the Light-Front projection technique. A remarkable agreement, in particular for the eigenvalues, has been achieved, numerically confirming that the Nakanishi uniqueness theorem for the weight functions, demonstrated in the context of the perturbative analysis of the multi-leg transition amplitudes and playing a basic role in suggesting one of the two adopted eigenequations, can be extended to a non perturbative realm. The detailed, quantitative studies are completed by presenting both probabilities and Light-Front momentum distributions for the valence component of the bound state.

## Key claim

The paper numerically confirms that the Nakanishi uniqueness theorem for the weight functions, originally demonstrated in the perturbative analysis of multi-leg transition amplitudes, can be extended to a non-perturbative realm, specifically for the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation in ladder approximation for a scalar-scalar bound system.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation for two massive scalars exchanging a massive scalar in ladder approximation, using the Nakanishi integral representation approach for S-wave bound states. They evaluated the Nakanishi weight functions by solving two different eigenequations obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter Equation through the Light-Front projection technique: one generalized eigenequation based on the valence component extraction, and one true eigenequation obtained by applying the Nakanishi uniqueness theorem. The numerical solutions were compared between the two approaches, and the resulting weight functions were used to compute valence probabilities and Light-Front momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies and masses of the exchanged scalar were calculated for the same parameter sets as in Kusaka and Williams (1995) and Carbonell and Karmanov (2003), with the coupling defined as α = (1/16π)(g/m)² where g is the coupling constant with dimension of mass
- The valence probability P_val is given by P_val = (1/(16π)²) ∫_{-1}^{1} dz (1-z²) ∫_{0}^{∞} dγ [∫_{0}^{∞} dγ' g_b(γ',z;κ²)/(γ'+γ+z²m²+(1-z²)κ²)²]²
- The Nakanishi weight function g_b(γ,z;κ²) must vanish for γ → ∞ to ensure finite transverse density at the origin, and g_b(γ,z=±1;κ²)=0 due to the structure of the kernel

## Context

This work extends the formal developments of Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2012) by providing the first quantitative numerical comparison between two approaches for solving the Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski space: the method of Kusaka and Williams (1995) using the Nakanishi uniqueness theorem directly, and the Light-Front projection method of Carbonell and Karmanov (2003). The results enable future phenomenological studies of interacting relativistic systems by providing reliable valence probabilities and Light-Front momentum distributions, and confirm that the Nakanishi integral representation approach can be applied non-perturbatively to bound state problems beyond the Wick-Cutkosky model.
