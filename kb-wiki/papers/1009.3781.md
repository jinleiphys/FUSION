# The reaction pi+ p --> K+ Sigma+ in a unitary coupled-channels model

> Elastic pi N scattering and the reaction pi+ p --> K+ Sigma+ are described simultaneously in a unitary coupled-channels approach which respects analyticity. SU(3) flavor symmetry is used to relate the t- and u-channel exchanges that drive the meson-baryon interaction in the different channels. Angular distributions, polarizations, and spin-rotation parameters are compared with available experimental data. The pole structure of the amplitudes is extracted from the analytic continuation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unitary coupled-channels model with explicit meson and baryon exchanges, related by SU(3) flavor symmetry, can simultaneously describe elastic πN scattering and the π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺ reaction up to about 2.25 GeV, but the authors do not claim validity above 2 GeV because the analysis was originally limited to that energy.

## Method

The authors extended the dynamical coupled-channels Jülich model, which solves a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for meson-baryon scattering, to include the KΛ and KΣ channels. The non-resonant interaction was constructed from t- and u-channel exchange diagrams with couplings related to existing πN channels via SU(3) flavor symmetry, using physical masses and different cut-offs to break the symmetry. Bare s-channel resonance states were introduced in each I=3/2 partial wave (S31, P31, D33, D35, F35, F37, and two in P33) and fitted to the π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺ differential cross section and polarization data from Candlin et al. (1982, 1988) and earlier experiments, while using the GWU/SAID partial wave analysis of elastic πN scattering as input.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers the energy range from the K⁺Σ⁺ threshold at √s = 1.68 GeV up to √s = 2.35 GeV, with pion beam momenta from p_π = 1.282 GeV/c to p_π = 2.473 GeV/c.
* The fit included 40 parameters for the pole part T^po: bare masses and couplings to πN, KΣ, πΔ, and ρN channels for the s-channel states.
* The spin-rotation parameter β was measured at two beam momenta: p_π = 1.69 GeV/c and p_π = 1.88 GeV/c (Candlin et al., 1988).
* The model reproduces data up to z ∼ 2.25 GeV, with significant deviations above 2.2 GeV near cos θ = ±0.5.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels Jülich model (Schutz 1998, Krehl 1999, Gasparyan 2003, Doring 2009) from the channels Nπ, Nη, Nσ, Δπ, and Nρ into the kaon-hyperon sector. It contrasts with K-matrix approaches such as those of the Gießen group (Penner 2002) and the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich 2004, Sarantsev 2005), which neglect the real dispersive parts of intermediate states, and with the GWU/SAID analysis (Arndt 2006) which provides precise πN amplitudes but does not include a microscopic background. The results enable improved treatment of strangeness production in NN collisions at COSY/Jülich and clarify the role of Δ resonances in heavy ion collisions, particularly the Δ(1920)P33.
