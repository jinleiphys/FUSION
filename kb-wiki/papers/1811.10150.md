# Local meson-baryon coupled-channels potential for the Lambda(1405)

> A local coupled-channels Kbar N-pi Sigma-pi Lambda potential is constructed, equivalently reproducing the scattering amplitude in chiral SU(3) dynamics. By analyzing the wave function of the Lambda(1405), we show the Kbar N dominance of the upper pole within the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405). The resulting potential will be useful for investigating Kbar few-nucleon systems including their coupled channels.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a local coupled-channels potential that exactly reproduces the chiral SU(3) scattering amplitude, but the potential is energy-dependent and requires a second-order polynomial parametrization for the energy dependence, unlike the single-channel version which needed a tenth-order polynomial.

## Method

The authors start from a coupled-channels Schrödinger equation for the $\bar{K}N$, $\pi\Sigma$, and $\pi\Lambda$ channels and determine the potential matrix $V_{ij}^{eq}$ by imposing matching conditions with the chiral SU(3) interaction kernel both on the real energy axis and in the complex energy plane. The potential is parametrized as a Gaussian spatial distribution with channel-dependent range parameters multiplied by an energy-dependent polynomial of order $\alpha_{max}=2$ with real coefficients. The wave function of the $\Lambda(1405)$ is then evaluated at the pole energies to extract the compositeness $X_i$ and elementarity $Z$ for each channel.

## Key numbers

- The two poles of the $\Lambda(1405)$ are found at $\sqrt{s} = 1424-27i$ MeV and $\sqrt{s} = 1380-81i$ MeV, consistent with the original scattering amplitude.
- For the high-mass pole at $1424-27i$ MeV, the $\bar{K}N$ compositeness is $X_{\bar{K}N} = 1.01-0.13i$, the $\pi\Sigma$ compositeness is $X_{\pi\Sigma} = -0.02-0.25i$, and the elementarity is $Z = 0.01+0.37i$.
- The energy dependence of the coupled-channels potential is accurately parametrized by a second-order polynomial ($\alpha_{max}=2$), in contrast to the single-channel version which required $\alpha_{max}$ as large as 10.

## Context

This work extends the single-channel $\bar{K}N$ potential of Miyahara (2015) by explicitly including the $\pi\Sigma$ and $\pi\Lambda$ coupled channels, which is necessary for systems where the quasibound state appears near the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold. The potential is based on the chiral SU(3) scattering amplitudes of Ikeda (2011, 2012) and uses the compositeness analysis framework of Hyodo (2013) and Sekihara (2014). The resulting potential enables rigorous few-body calculations of $\bar{K}$ few-nucleon systems with proper treatment of coupled-channel dynamics.
