# Hot QCD at finite isospin density: confronting SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with recent lattice data

> Extending our recently published $SU(2)$ results for zero temperature we now compute the QCD equation of state for finite isospin density within the three flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation, motivated by the recently obtained Lattice QCD results for both zero and finite temperatures. Like our previous study, here also we have considered both the commonly used Traditional cutoff Regularization Scheme and the Medium Separation Scheme. Our results are compared with recent high-precision lattice simulations as well as previously obtained results in two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The agreement between the lattice results and the predictions from three-flavor NJL model is very good for low values of $μ_I$ (for both zero and finite temperatures). For larger values of $μ_I$, the agreement between lattice data and the two-flavor NJL predictions is surprisingly good and better than with the three-flavor predictions.

## Key claim

The three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation shows very good agreement with lattice QCD results for low values of the isospin chemical potential (for both zero and finite temperatures), but for larger values of μ_I the two-flavor NJL predictions agree surprisingly better with lattice data than the three-flavor predictions.

## Method

The authors compute the QCD equation of state at finite isospin density using the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation, including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) term to account for the U_A(1) anomaly. They employ two regularization schemes: the Traditional Regularization Scheme (TRS), which applies a sharp three-dimensional cutoff to all momentum integrals, and the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS), which separates finite medium contributions from divergent vacuum integrals so that only vacuum integrals are regularized. The model parameters are fitted separately for zero temperature (Set-I) and finite temperature (Set-II) to match the pion masses and decay constants used in the corresponding lattice QCD simulations.

## Key numbers

- For zero temperature, the SU(3)-NJL model in the MSS scheme shows better agreement with lattice data for normalized pressure than TRS for μ_I ≤ 2 m_π (where m_π = 131.7 MeV for Set-I)
- The critical temperature for pion condensate melting at μ_I = 0.206 GeV is approximately 158 MeV for TRS and approximately 173 MeV for MSS in the SU(3)-NJL model
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for energy density at finite μ_I is given by ε_SB(μ_I) = (N_c N_f / 4π²) μ_I⁴, and only the MSS scheme approaches this limit at higher μ_I
- The SU(2) NJL model parameters use Λ = 659.325 MeV for both parameter sets, with G = 2.07835/Λ² (Set-I) and G = 2.07691/Λ² (Set-II)
- The SU(3) NJL model parameters use Λ = 574.68 MeV (Set-I) and Λ = 608.431 MeV (Set-II)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(2) NJL study at zero temperature (Avancini, 2019) to three flavors and finite temperatures, motivated by recent high-precision lattice QCD results from Brandt and collaborators (2017-2020). The study enables consistency checks of the NJL model against first-principles lattice calculations for systems with finite isospin density, which are relevant for understanding pion stars and early universe scenarios with high lepton asymmetry. The comparison between two-flavor and three-flavor NJL predictions, along with the regularization scheme dependence, provides guidance for which model variants are most reliable in different regimes of isospin chemical potential.
