# Prerequisites for heavy quark coalescence in heavy-ion collisions

> The coalescence model assumes instant formation of a bound state from unbound particles based on the overlapping of two states in spatial and momentum spaces and quantum numbers. Therefore, applied to the hadronization of partons, it provides a snapshot of a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) just before hadronization. We use the coalescence model for the formation of the ground state of open heavy flavor and the statistical model for heavier states. Assuming that all heavy flavors in thermal equilibrium hadronize through the coalescence, we find that the QGP just before hadronization is not composed of completely randomized partons but must have strong correlations in color charges as well as in momentum and/or coordinate spaces between heavy quark and light (anti-)quark.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if all heavy flavors in thermal equilibrium hadronize exclusively through coalescence at the critical temperature, the QGP just before hadronization cannot consist of completely randomized partons but must exhibit strong correlations in color charges as well as in momentum and/or coordinate spaces between heavy quarks and light (anti-)quarks.

## Method

The authors combine a coalescence model for the ground state of open heavy flavor mesons (using a Gaussian Wigner function) with a statistical model for excited states and heavy baryons. They compute the total coalescence probability of a charm or bottom quark at the critical temperature T_c = 0.16 GeV, varying parameters such as light quark mass, meson radius, and correlation lengths, while keeping the energy density fixed at the lattice QCD value of 0.4 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

- Thermal averaged coalescence probability of charm quark to form D^0/D^+ at T_c = 0.16 GeV is roughly 0.71% for r_D = 0.5 fm and 1.1% for r_D = 1 fm
- Ratio of number density of all charm hadrons to number density of D^0 at T_c is 7.47 from the statistical model
- Energy density at critical temperature for mu_B = 0 (T_c ≈ 0.16 GeV) is around 0.4 GeV/fm^3 from lattice QCD
- Light quark mass reproducing this energy density is 0.45 GeV; strange quark mass is 0.55 GeV
- Total coalescence probability of charm quark with randomized thermal light quarks is at most around 16-17% for massless quark and r_D = 1 fm
- Ratio of number density of all bottom hadrons to number density of B^- at T = 0.16 GeV is 11.4 from the statistical model

## Context

This work extends the parton coalescence model approach used by Greco (2003), Fries (2008), and Song (2016, 2017) for heavy flavor hadronization in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with previous approaches by Oh (2009), Cho (2019), and Cao (2019) who used a common coalescence radius for all charm mesons and obtained total coalescence probability reaching one at zero momentum, whereas the authors find that randomized thermal partons yield probabilities far below one. The study enables future coupling of this hadronization model to dynamical evolution of QGP matter near T_c to understand the origin of the required momentum correlations.
