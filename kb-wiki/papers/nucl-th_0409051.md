# A General Effective Theory for Dense Quark Matter

> A general effective action for quark matter at nonzero temperature and/or nonzero density is derived. Irrelevant quark modes are distinguished from relevant quark modes, and hard from soft gluon modes, by introducing two separate cut-offs in momentum space, one for quarks, $Λ_q$, and one for gluons, $Λ_g$. Irrelevant quark modes and hard gluon modes are then exactly integrated out in the functional integral representation of the QCD partition function. Depending on the specific choice for $Λ_q$ and $Λ_g$, the resulting effective action contains well-known effective actions for hot and/or dense quark matter, for instance the ``Hard Thermal Loop'' (HTL) or the ``Hard Dense Loop'' (HDL) action, as well as the high-density effective theory proposed by Hong and others.

# A General Effective Theory for Dense Quark Matter

## Key claim
The paper derives a general effective action for quark matter at nonzero temperature and/or density that contains the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL), Hard Dense Loop (HDL), and high-density effective theories as special cases, depending on the specific choice of separate momentum cutoffs for quarks (Λ_q) and gluons (Λ_g). The derivation is performed under the approximations that hard gluons do not couple to soft gluons and that interactions among hard gluons are negligible.

## Method
The authors start from the QCD partition function in Euclidean space-time and introduce two separate momentum-space cutoffs: Λ_q for quarks and Λ_g for gluons. Irrelevant quark modes and hard gluon modes are integrated out exactly in the functional integral, yielding an effective action for relevant quark modes and soft gluons. The integration is performed by first integrating out irrelevant quarks, then expanding the resulting action in powers of hard gluon fields, performing a Gaussian integration over hard gluons, and applying two approximations: (1) hard gluons do not couple to soft gluons due to momentum conservation, and (2) the interaction action for hard gluons is set to zero.

## Key numbers
- The color-superconducting gap parameter to subleading order in g is φ = 512 π^4 (2 / (N_f g^2))^(5/2) μ exp(-3π^2/(√2 g) - (π^2 + 4)/8) [1 + O(g)], where N_f is the number of massless quark flavors, g is the strong coupling constant, and μ is the quark chemical potential.
- For the HTL/HDL case, the quark and gluon cutoffs are set equal: Λ_q = Λ_g ≡ Λ, separating hard modes with momenta ∼ T or ∼ μ from soft modes with momenta ∼ gT or ∼ gμ.
- For the high-density effective theory, the condition to obtain the subleading-order gap result is Λ_q ≲ gμ ≪ Λ_g ≲ μ.
- The free inverse gluon propagator for hard gluons scales as Δ_{0,22}^(-1) ∼ T^2 or ∼ μ^2, while the loop contribution to the hard gluon self-energy is at most ∼ g^2 T^2 or ∼ g^2 μ^2.

## Context
This work extends the high-density effective theory originally proposed by Hong (2000) and refined by others including Schäfer (2003) and the authors of the Hard Thermal Loop and Hard Dense Loop approaches. The general effective action unifies these previously separate effective theories under a single framework by allowing independent choices of Λ_q and Λ_g. The formalism enables systematic computation of sub-subleading corrections to the color-superconducting gap parameter, which are necessary for reliable extrapolation from asymptotically large chemical potentials (μ ≫ Λ_QCD) to physically relevant values around μ ∼ 500 MeV.
