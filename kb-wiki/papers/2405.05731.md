# Robustness of the Hedgehog Skyrmion

> We investigate the radial profile function of the hedgehog Skyrmion with unit baryon number in generic EFTs (effective field theories) of pions. The analysis assumes chiral symmetry and ignores the pion mass term. The Skyrmion is always smooth, because it has no point source at the origin, and terms in the EFT with higher numbers of pion derivatives do not result in uncontrolled large corrections or singularities there. The profile varies in quite a limited way as the terms in the EFT change, and a universal profile function is proposed.

# Robustness of the Hedgehog Skyrmion

## Key claim

The hedgehog Skyrmion profile function f(r) for unit baryon number remains smooth and varies only in a limited way across generic chiral effective field theories of pions, provided the energy density is constructed from symmetric polynomials in the strain eigenvalues; a universal profile function can be proposed that matches the small-r and large-r asymptotic forms with continuous first and second derivatives, though the matching radius R must still be determined by energy minimisation for each specific EFT.

## Method

The author considers static hedgehog Skyrmion configurations with profile f(r) satisfying f(0)=π and f(∞)=0, in chiral EFTs with massless pions and no pion mass term. The energy functional is constructed from symmetric polynomials of the strain eigenvalues (I₂, I₄, I₆ or equivalently J₂, J₄, J₆), which are chirally invariant. The Euler-Lagrange equation is solved asymptotically: for small r, f(r)=π-βr+γβ³r³+O(r⁵); for large r, f(r)=C/r² - (1/21)C³/r⁶+O(1/r⁸). These asymptotic forms are matched at a radius R with continuity of f, rf', and r²f'' to determine a universal profile, with R fixed by global energy minimisation.

## Key numbers

- For the universal profile in the original Skyrme theory (Dirichlet + Skyrme terms), the matching gives x = C/R² = 0.696, y = βR = 3.045, and γ = 0.0207.
- The universal profile yields total energy E = 4π(3.538R + 9.615/R), minimised at R = 1.649, giving E = 146.6 (equivalently 1.238 × 12π²).
- The exact profile from shooting method has energy 1.232 × 12π², so the universal profile is accurate to 0.5%.
- For the universal profile, β = 1.847 and C = 1.890; the exact numerical value of C is 2.16 (11% larger).
- The simpler Ruback-Manton profile (1986) gave R = 1.26 and energy E = 151 (about 3% above the true profile).
- For the original Skyrme theory, the large-r tail has leading term C/r² with C arbitrary at leading order, and the O(1/r⁶) correction coefficient is -C³/21.
- The small-r cubic coefficient γ for the original Skyrme theory is γ = (2/15)[(1+½β²)/(1+2β²)].
- With an additional quartic term coefficient μ, γ becomes γ = (2/15)[(1+(½+3μ)β²)/(1+(2+6μ)β²)].
- For the Skyrme-type theory including the BPS-Skyrme sextic term with coefficient ν, γ = (2/15)[(1+½β²-(3/2)νβ⁴)/(1+2β²+3νβ⁴)].

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hedgehog Skyrmion profiles beyond Skyrme's original theory (Skyrme 1961) to generic chiral EFTs, addressing criticism that higher-derivative terms could substantially alter the Skyrmion core structure. The approach builds on the strain tensor formalism of Manton (1987) and the matching method of Ruback and Manton (1986), which used only linear small-r and 1/r² large-r terms. The classification of chirally-invariant energy densities follows Gudnason and Nitta (2016), but they did not study the B=1 hedgehog profile. Marleau (1991) solved for the profile with recursion relations to all orders but required three undetermined parameters from numerical minimisation, whereas the present matching fixes two parameters at the price of not having smoothness to all orders. The results enable using Skyrmions to model nucleons in a wider range of chiral EFTs, including perturbative EFTs used for nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear spectra.
