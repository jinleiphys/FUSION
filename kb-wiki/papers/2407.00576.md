# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group at Finite Temperature

> The study of nuclei at finite temperature is of immense interest for many areas of nuclear astrophysics and nuclear-reaction science. A variety of ab initio methods are now available for computing the properties of nuclei from interactions rooted in Quantum Chromodynamics, but applications have largely been limited to zero temperature. In the present work, we extend one such method, the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG), to finite temperature. Using an exactly-solvable schematic model that captures essential features of nuclear interactions, we show that the FT-IMSRG can accurately determine the energetics of nuclei at finite temperature, and we explore the accuracy of the FT-IMSRG in different parameter regimes, e.g., strong and weak pairing. In anticipation of FT-IMSRG applications for finite nuclei and infinite matter, we discuss differences arising from the choice of working with the canonical and the grand canonical ensembles. In future work, we will apply the FT-IMSRG with realistic nuclear interactions to compute nuclear structure and reaction properties at finite temperature, which are important ingredients for understanding nucleosynthesis in stellar environments, or modeling reactions of hot compound nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper extends the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) method to finite temperature, demonstrating that the FT-IMSRG can accurately determine the energetics of nuclei at finite temperature in an exactly-solvable schematic model, but only in certain parameter regimes (e.g., weak coupling) and with the caveat that the canonical ensemble treatment omits correction terms to Wick's theorem that scale exponentially with basis size.

## Method

The authors implement finite-temperature Hartree-Fock (FT-HF) to construct a reference ensemble with fractional occupation numbers, then apply the IMSRG flow equations (truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level, IMSRG(2)) using an arctan generator adapted for finite temperature. The method is tested on the exactly-solvable pairing-plus-particle-hole (P3H) schematic model with four fermions in eight single-particle states, comparing FT-IMSRG results to exact solutions across varying coupling strengths and temperatures.

## Key numbers

- For the P3H model with parameters $g=0.3$, $b=-0.1$ and $A=4$, $N=8$, the FT-IMSRG internal energy results are "extremely close" to exact internal energies at low temperatures (high $\beta$), with relative errors below 1% shown in red on a logarithmic color scale for $|g|, |b| \leq 0.5$.
- The FT-IMSRG performs weakest at mid-range temperatures around $\beta = 1$ (inverse temperature in units of $\delta^{-1}$, where $\delta=1$ is the single-particle level spacing).
- The convergence criterion for FT-HF is defined as $|E^{(i+1)} - E^{(i)}|/A \leq 10^{-5}$.
- The particle number variance in the grand canonical ensemble is given by $\Delta A = \sum_p \bar{n}_p n_p$, where $\bar{n}_p = 1 - n_p$.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature IMSRG method (Hergert 2016, 2017, 2020) to finite temperature, joining prior finite-temperature extensions of mean-field methods like FT-HF and FT-HFB (Goodman 1981, Duguet 2020) and beyond-mean-field methods like Shell-Model Monte Carlo (Lang 1993, Alhassid 1994). The FT-IMSRG shows comparable relative errors to Finite-Temperature Coupled Cluster for similar schematic models (White 2020, Harsha 2022). The authors aim to apply the FT-IMSRG with realistic nuclear interactions to compute nuclear structure and reaction properties at finite temperature for nucleosynthesis in stellar environments and reactions of hot compound nuclei, and to link finite-temperature density functional theory with ab initio methodology.
