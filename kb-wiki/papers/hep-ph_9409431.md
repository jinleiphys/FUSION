# Longitudinal Transitions Of Baryon Resonances in Constituent Quark Model

> A longitudinal transition operator that satisfies the gauge invariance requirement is introduced in constituent quark model. The corresponding longitudinal transitions between the nucleon and baryon resonances are calculated. We show that the study of the longitudinal coupling plays an important role in understanding the structure of baryons.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a gauge-invariant longitudinal transition operator for constituent quark models, showing that the standard nonrelativistic operator violates gauge invariance and that the relativistic corrections significantly alter the predicted longitudinal helicity amplitudes, especially for P-wave resonances.

## Method

The authors derive a gauge-invariant longitudinal transition operator by starting from the relativistic electromagnetic current, imposing current conservation exactly, and then taking the nonrelativistic limit. They calculate the longitudinal helicity amplitudes \(S_{1/2}(Q^2)\) for transitions between the nucleon and several baryon resonances (\(S_{11}(1530)\), \(D_{13}(1520)\), \(D_{15}(1670)\), \(F_{16}(1688)\), and \(P_{33}(1232)\)) in the \(SU(6)\otimes O(3)\) symmetry limit, including spin-orbit and nonadditive relativistic corrections. The \(Q^2\) dependence is computed using a Lorentz boost factor and an ad hoc form factor.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of longitudinal couplings for \(S_{11}(1530)\) and \(D_{13}(1520)\) in the nonrelativistic limit is \(S_{1/2}(S_{11}(1530))/S_{1/2}(D_{13}(1520)) = -1/\sqrt{2}\), and relativistic corrections multiply this ratio by a factor of 1.45 using standard quark model parameters \(m_q = 0.33\) GeV and \(\alpha^2 = 0.17\) GeV\(^2\).
* The longitudinal amplitude \(S_{1/2}(Q^2)\) for \(S_{11}(1530)\) is increased by about 25 percent due to relativistic effects.
* The ratio \(R = S_{1/2}^2(Q^2)/(A_{1/2}^2(Q^2)+A_{3/2}^2(Q^2))\) for \(F_{16}(1688)\) is less than 0.1 with relativistic corrections.
* For the \(P_{33}(1232)\) resonance, the relativistic corrections approximately double the ratio \(R = -S_{1/2}(Q^2)/(\sqrt{2} M1)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electromagnetic transitions in the constituent quark model by Copley, Karl, and Obryk (1970) and Feynman, Kisslinger, and Ravndal (1971), which focused on transverse amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}\). It addresses the gauge invariance problem noted by Close (1979) and Bourdeau and Mukhopadhyay (1992) in the longitudinal operator used by Warns (1990). The results enable systematic comparison with electroproduction data from CEBAF and provide a foundation for studying spin structure functions \(g_{1,2}(x,Q^2)\) in the resonance region at low \(Q^2\).
