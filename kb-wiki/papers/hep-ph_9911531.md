# Chiral dynamics of many pion systems: the rho to 4 pi and omega to 5 pi decays

> Based on the Weinberg lagrangian or, in a new language, the lagrangian of hidden local symmetry added with the term induced by the anomalous lagrangian of Wess and Zumino, the dynamics of the decays rho to 4 pi and omega to 5 pi is considered. The excitation curves of rho resonance in its decay to 4 pi are obtained for the first time. The comparison with recent CMD-2 data is given. The partial widths of the decays omega to 2 pi^+ 2 pi^- pi^0 and omega to pi^+ pi^-3 pi^0 which can be measured on colliders with luminosity 10^{33}{cm }^{-2}{s}^{-1} are evaluated for the first time.

# Chiral dynamics of many pion systems: the rho to 4 pi and omega to 5 pi decays

## Key claim
The partial widths of ρ→4π decays are not adequately characterized by a single value at the ρ mass because the width rises rapidly with energy inside the broad ρ resonance; the resonance excitation curves in e+e−→ρ0→4π are the proper test of chiral models, and the left shoulder of the ρ peak (below ~700 MeV) is the cleanest region for studying chiral dynamics because contributions from higher derivatives and the a1 meson vanish rapidly there.

## Method
The authors use the Weinberg Lagrangian (equivalently, the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian) plus the Wess–Zumino anomalous term to construct amplitudes for ρ→4π and ω→5π decays. They compute partial widths at the ρ mass and then evaluate the energy-dependent excitation curves for e+e−→ρ0→2π+2π− and e+e−→ρ0→π+π−2π0, comparing with CMD-2 data. For ω→5π, they derive amplitudes from the ω→ρπ→5π chain using nonrelativistic approximations valid to ~10% accuracy and integrate over the 4π invariant mass range.

## Key numbers
- Γ(ρ0→2π+2π−, mρ2) = 0.89 keV at √s = mρ = 770 MeV
- Γ(ρ0→π+π−2π0, mρ2) = 0.24 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.44 keV (with anomaly term)
- Γ(ρ+→π+3π0, mρ2) = 0.41 keV
- Γ(ρ+→2π+π−π0, mρ2) = 0.71 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.90 keV (with anomaly term)
- The ratio Γ(ρ→2π+2π−, s)/ΓLIPS(ρ→2π+2π−, s) changes from 0.4 at √s = 650 MeV to 1 at √s = mρ
- The difference between widths with and without the anomaly term for ρ0→π+π−2π0 is 100% at √s = mρ, 8% at √s = 700 MeV, and 0.3% at √s = 650 MeV
- B(ω→2π+2π−π0) = 1.6×10−9
- B(ω→π+π−3π0) = 1.2×10−9
- At luminosity L = 1033 cm−2 s−1, about 3 events per week are expected for ω→5π decays
- At √s = 650 MeV with L = 1032 cm−2 s−1, one event per day of e+e−→2π+2π− is expected; at √s = 700 MeV, up to 10 events per day

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of ρ→4π by Rittenberg (1969), Bramon (1993), Kuraev (1995), and Birse (1996), which evaluated partial widths only at the ρ mass. Birse (1996) had corrected earlier violations of chiral invariance and obtained B(ρ→4π)~10−5, an order of magnitude smaller than earlier estimates. The present paper is the first to compute energy-dependent excitation curves for e+e−→ρ0→4π and to evaluate ω→5π partial widths. The results enable experimental tests of chiral dynamics in e+e− colliders at the left shoulder of the ρ resonance, where higher-derivative and loop corrections are minimal, and provide predictions for ω→5π decays measurable at future colliders with luminosity 1033 cm−2 s−1.
