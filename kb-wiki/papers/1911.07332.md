# Properties of binary components and remnant in GW170817 using equations of state in finite temperature field theory models

> We investigate gross properties of binary components and remnant in GW170817 using equations of state within the finite temperature field theoretical models. We also adopt finite temperature equations of state in the density dependent hadron field theory for this study. Properties of binary components are studied using zero temperature equations of state. Particularly, we investigate tidal deformabilities and radii of binary components in light of GW170817. An analytical expression relating the radii and the combined tidal deformability is obtained for binary neutron star masses in the range $1.1M_{\odot}\lesssim M\lesssim 1.6 M_{\odot}$. The upper bound on the tidal deformability gives the upper bound on the neutron star radius as 13 km. Next, the role of finite temperature on the remnant in GW170817 is explored. In this case, we investigate the gravitational and baryon mass, radius, Kepler frequency and moment of inertia of the rigidly rotating remnant for different equations of state at fixed entropy per baryon. The remnant radius is enlarged due to thermal effects compared with the zero temperature case. Consequently, it is found that the Kepler frequency is much lower at higher entropy per baryon than that of the case at zero temperature. These findings are consistent with the results found in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical expression relating neutron star radii to the combined tidal deformability for binary masses in the range \(1.1M_{\odot}\lesssim M\lesssim 1.6 M_{\odot}\), and uses the upper bound on tidal deformability from GW170817 to place an upper bound on the neutron star radius of 13 km, but this bound depends on the assumption that radii of the two components are nearly equal.

## Method

The authors use zero-temperature equations of state (EoSs) from relativistic mean field models (DD2, SFHo, SFHx, TM1, TMA, BHB\(\Lambda\phi\), and hybrid hadron-quark EoSs) to compute tidal deformabilities and radii of binary neutron star components in GW170817. For the remnant, they employ finite-temperature EoSs at fixed entropy per baryon (\(s=0\) and \(s=2\)) within the density dependent hadron field theory and non-linear Walecka models, and use the numerical library LORENE to solve for stationary, axisymmetric, rigidly rotating neutron star structures in general relativity.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on neutron star radius from the combined tidal deformability upper limit \(\widetilde{\Lambda}=720\): 13.04 km; lower bound from \(\widetilde{\Lambda}=70\): 8.85 km (for chirp mass \(\mathcal{M}=1.188 M_{\odot}\)).
- Upper bound on radius of 1.2 \(M_{\odot}\) neutron star: 13.08 km; of 1.55 \(M_{\odot}\) neutron star: 12.99 km.
- Maximum gravitational masses of non-rotating neutron stars for various EoSs: DD2 2.42 \(M_{\odot}\), BHB\(\Lambda\phi\) 2.1 \(M_{\odot}\), SFHo 2.06 \(M_{\odot}\), SFHx 2.13 \(M_{\odot}\), TM1 2.21 \(M_{\odot}\), TMA 2.02 \(M_{\odot}\), Hybrid 2.05 \(M_{\odot}\).
- Kepler frequencies for rigidly rotating remnant at \(s=0\): DD2 1.236 kHz, BHB\(\Lambda\phi\) 1.424 kHz, SFHo 1.763 kHz, SFHx 1.581 kHz, TM1 1.228 kHz, TMA 1.315 kHz; at \(s=2\): DD2 1.109 kHz, BHB\(\Lambda\phi\) 1.269 kHz, SFHo 1.606 kHz, SFHx 1.425 kHz, TM1 1.011 kHz, TMA 1.099 kHz.
- Gravitational masses of rigidly rotating remnant at Kepler frequency for DD2 at \(s=0\): 2.606 \(M_{\odot}\); at \(s=2\): 2.657 \(M_{\odot}\).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of tidal deformabilities from GW170817 (LIGO-Virgo Collaboration, 2017) and radius constraints (Zhao and Lattimer, 2018) by providing an analytical radius-tidal deformability relation for a range of binary masses. It also extends studies of remnant properties (Kaplan et al., 2014; LIGO-Virgo Collaboration, 2019) by using finite-temperature EoSs at fixed entropy per baryon rather than fixed temperature, enabling more realistic modeling of the hot, rigidly rotating remnant formed after differential rotation is removed by magnetorotational instability and viscosity.
