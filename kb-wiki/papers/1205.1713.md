# Finite size scaling on the phase diagram of QCD

> In the last years there has been remarkable progress in the comparison of experimental data on the shape of event-by-event distributions of conserved quantities and lattice thermodynamic predictions based on the grand canonical ensemble. In this talk we discuss how the QCD crossover temperature and the freezeout curve are extracted from the analysis of fluctuations. We report that one can also go further and locate the QCD critical point already from published data. We also list the systematics which must be brought under control in future.

# Finite size scaling on the phase diagram of QCD

## Key claim

The QCD critical point can be located indirectly from published fluctuation data at high beam energies, without direct lattice input, by exploiting the coincidence that the freezeout temperature at top RHIC energy is close to the critical temperature. The extracted critical point position is consistent with lattice results but currently has very large uncertainties, with only a lower bound of \(z_E \ge 0.59\) at 68% confidence.

## Method

The pressure is expanded in a Maclaurin series in \(z = \mu/T\) around \(\mu = 0\), with coefficients given by nonlinear susceptibilities \(\chi^{(n)}\) computed on the lattice. Dimensionless ratios of these susceptibilities (\(m_0, m_1, m_2, m_3\)) are compared to experimental measurements of event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities from the STAR collaboration. By using the NLOPadé resummation of the series and assuming \(r_1 = r_2 = z_E\) (the critical point location), a simultaneous fit of \(m_0\) and \(m_3\) data at \(\sqrt{S} = 200\) GeV extracts both the freezeout chemical potential and the critical point position.

## Key numbers

- Lattice determination of the QCD crossover scale: \(T_c(P) = 176 \pm 3 \pm 4\) MeV, where the first error is from finite-temperature lattice computations and the second from scale-setting to zero-temperature hadron properties.
- Lattice estimate of the critical end point: \(z_E = 1.8 \pm 0.1\) and \(t_E = 0.94 \pm 0.01\), using the Polyakov loop susceptibility peak as the temperature scale.
- Freezeout chemical potential at \(\sqrt{S} = 200\) GeV from leading-order analysis: \(z = 0.1884 \pm 0.0007\; (\pm 0.033)\) from \(m_0\) and \(z = 0.183 \pm 0.036\; (\pm 0.076)\) from \(m_3\), where the first errors are statistical and the bracketed errors include systematic uncertainties added in quadrature.
- Critical point extracted from data: \(z_E = 2.03^{+ \infty}_{-1.44}\) corresponding to \(\mu_E = 355^{+ \infty}_{-250}\) MeV, with a 68% confidence lower bound of \(z_E \ge 0.59\).
- Alternative estimate using only \(m_0\) at \(\sqrt{S} = 62.4\) GeV and HRGM freezeout: \(z_E = 2.04^{+0.15}_{-0.12}\; (^{+ \infty}_{-1.29})\), where the first errors are statistical and the second include systematic uncertainties.
- Scale determination from bulk matter fluctuations: \(T_c(P) = 175^{+1}_{-7}\) MeV (statistical errors only).

## Context

This review extends the program initiated by Gupta et al. (2011) and Karsch et al. (2011) which demonstrated that lattice QCD predictions for fluctuation ratios agree with STAR data at RHIC. It builds on the lattice methodology of the ILGTI collaboration and the hadron resonance gas model freezeout parametrization of Cleymans et al. (2006). The key new result is that the same data can be used to extract the QCD critical point location, complementing direct lattice searches. The paper also identifies systematic effects that must be controlled: lattice cutoff effects (shown by comparing \(N_t = 4\) and \(N_t = 6\) computations), finite volume corrections from the ratio of observed to full fireball volume (Koch, 2010), the Peclet number criterion for fluctuation freezeout (Bhalerao et al., 2011), and the effect of slow isospin-changing reactions (Asakawa et al., 2011). Future work requires improved experimental error analysis with covariance between \(m_0\) and \(m_3\), and hydrodynamic computations of the Peclet scale.
