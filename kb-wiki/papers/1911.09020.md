# A dispersive optical model analysis of $^{208}$Pb generating a neutron-skin prediction beyond the mean field

> A nonlocal dispersive-optical-model analysis has been carried out for neutrons and protons in $^{208}$Pb. Elastic-scattering angular distributions, total and reaction cross sections, single-particle energies, the neutron and proton numbers, the charge distribution, and the binding energy have been fitted to extract the neutron and proton self-energies both above and below the Fermi energy. From the single-particle propagator derived from these self-energies, we have determined the charge and matter distributions in $^{208}$Pb. The predicted spectroscopic factors are consistent with results from the $(e,e'p)$ reaction and inelastic-electron-scattering data to very high spin states. Sensible results for the high-momentum content of neutrons and protons are obtained with protons appearing more correlated, in agreement with experiment and \textit{ab initio} calculations of asymmetric matter. A neutron skin of $0.25\pm0.05$ fm is deduced. An analysis of several nuclei leads to the conclusion that finite-size effects play a non-negligible role in the formation of the neutron skin in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM) analysis of \(^{208}\)Pb yields a neutron skin of \(0.25 \pm 0.05\) fm, but the authors emphasize that finite-size effects play a non-negligible role in the formation of the neutron skin in finite nuclei, meaning the result goes beyond a simple mean-field prediction.

## Method

A nonlocal dispersive-optical-model analysis was performed for neutrons and protons in \(^{208}\)Pb, fitting elastic-scattering angular distributions, total and reaction cross sections, single-particle energies, neutron and proton numbers, the charge distribution, and the binding energy to extract the neutron and proton self-energies both above and below the Fermi energy. The single-particle propagator derived from these self-energies was used to determine the charge and matter distributions. The self-energy was parametrized with nonlocal volume, surface, and spin-orbit terms, and the fit was carried out using a Powell minimization method, with a parallel Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis used for cross-validation.

## Key numbers

* Neutron skin of \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.25 \pm 0.05\) fm, defined as the difference between neutron and proton root-mean-square radii.
* Neutron skin of \(^{48}\)Ca from prior DOM analysis: \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.249 \pm 0.023\) fm (Mahzoon 2017).
* PREX experimental value for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm.
* Proton spectroscopic factors for orbitals near the Fermi energy in \(^{208}\)Pb: \(1f_{7/2}\) = 0.67, \(0h_{9/2}\) = 0.60, \(2s_{1/2}\) = 0.69, \(1d_{3/2}\) = 0.66, \(0h_{11/2}\) = 0.61, \(1d_{5/2}\) = 0.68.
* Neutron spectroscopic factors for orbitals near the Fermi energy in \(^{208}\)Pb: \(0i_{11/2}\) = 0.77, \(1g_{9/2}\) = 0.77, \(2p_{1/2}\) = 0.81, \(1f_{5/2}\) = 0.81, \(2p_{3/2}\) = 0.82, \(0i_{13/2}\) = 0.80.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal DOM framework previously applied to \(^{40}\)Ca (Mahzoon 2014, Atkinson 2018) and \(^{48}\)Ca (Mahzoon 2017) to the heavier doubly magic nucleus \(^{208}\)Pb. The analysis provides a prediction for the neutron skin that is independent of the mean-field approaches reviewed by Tsang (2012) and Piekarewicz (2012), and is timely given the PREX2 measurement conducted in summer 2019. The results enable future comparisons with parity-violating electron scattering data and constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which is relevant for understanding neutron star properties and supernovae.
