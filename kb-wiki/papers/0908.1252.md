# The cranking formula and the spurious behaviour of the mass parameters

> We discuss some aspects of the approach of the mass parameters by means of the simple cranking model. In particular, it is well known that the numerical application of this formula is often subject to ambiguities or contradictions. It is found that these problems are induced by the presence of two derivatives in the formula. To overcome these problems, we state a useful ansatz and we develop a number of simple arguments which tend to justify the removal of these terms. As soon as this is done, the formula becomes simpler and easier to interpret. In this respect, it is shown how the shell effects affect the mass parameters. A number of numerical tests help us in our conclusions.

## Key claim

The numerical ambiguities and singularities in the cranking formula for nuclear mass parameters arise from the presence of derivatives of the gap parameter Δ and the Fermi level λ with respect to deformation; the author proposes that these derivative terms can be removed via an ansatz based on a "virtual change in deformation" principle, yielding a corrected formula that avoids unphysical divergences.

## Method

The author derives the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula for mass parameters in the BCS approximation, identifies the diagonal term containing derivatives ∂λ/∂β and ∂Δ/∂β as the source of singular behavior near phase transitions, and proposes an ansatz where the deformation dependence of the BCS occupation probabilities u_ν and v_ν is attributed solely to the single-particle energies ε_ν rather than to λ and Δ. Numerical tests are performed using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with quadrupole deformation parameters (β,γ) for the nucleus ¹³⁶Xe, solving the Schrödinger equation via diagonalization in an anisotropic harmonic oscillator basis (16-20 major shells) and solving the BCS equations for Δ and λ.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁶Xe (N=82, magic) at β≈0.1 and γ=0°, the version with derivatives gives D_ββ ≈ 6,000,000 MeV/ħ² while the version without derivatives gives ≈25 MeV/ħ², with Δ≈0.004 MeV at that deformation.
- The denominator a²+b² in the derivative formulas becomes very small: at β=0.1, a=0.0096 MeV⁻², b=0.0034 MeV⁻², leading to ∂Δ/∂β = 2366.3 MeV and ∂λ/∂β = -832.6 MeV.
- For Δ > 0.2 MeV, both formula versions give close (though non-identical) results, with mass parameters D_ββ ~ 50-250 ħ²MeV⁻¹ for mid-shell nuclei.
- For the magic nucleus at β=0.2, the version without derivatives shows a maximum, and from β=0.3 gives mean values about 100-250 ħ²MeV⁻¹.

## Context

This work addresses known problems with the cranking formula for mass parameters reported by several authors (e.g., the references cited as [1], [10]-[14]), where singular behavior occurs near critical deformations where the pairing gap Δ becomes small. The author extends the approach of the "lowest-order expansion" method for computing derivatives of λ and Δ (as in Ref. [11] and [1]) and proposes a corrected formula that removes these derivatives entirely. This enables more reliable calculations of mass parameters for nuclear fission lifetime estimates and collective nuclear levels, particularly near magic numbers where BCS solutions break down.
