# New information on photon fragmentation functions

> Thermal photons radiated in heavy-ion collisions represent an important signal for a recently discovered new state of matter, the deconfined quark-gluon plasma. However, a clean identification of this signal requires precise knowledge of the prompt photons produced simultaneously in hard collisions of quarks and gluons, mostly through their fragmentation. In this paper, we demonstrate that PHENIX data on photons produced in proton-proton collisions with low transverse momenta allow to extract new information on this fragmentation process. While existing data do not yet convincingly favor one parameterization (BFG II) over the two other frequently used photon fragmentation functions (BFG I and GRV NLO), the data sets recorded by PHENIX and STAR at BNL RHIC in 2013 with tenfold higher statistics should allow for such an analysis.

## Key claim

Existing PHENIX data on prompt photons in proton-proton collisions at low transverse momenta do not yet convincingly favor one photon fragmentation function parameterization (BFG II) over the two other frequently used ones (BFG I and GRV NLO), but the data sets recorded by PHENIX and STAR at BNL RHIC in 2013 with tenfold higher statistics should allow for such an analysis.

## Method

The authors separated PHENIX data into a control region of high transverse momenta (above 10 GeV) dominated by direct photon production and a signal region (below 5 GeV) dominated by fragmentation photons. They performed chi-square tests of three available modern photon fragmentation functions (BFG I, BFG II, and GRV NLO) against the data, allowing the renormalization, factorization, and fragmentation scales to vary independently among the choices (0.5, 1, 2) times the photon transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The control region fit (p_T > 10 GeV) yielded a minimal chi-square per degree of freedom of 1.2 for the scale combination mu_R = mu_D = 0.5 p_T and mu_F = 2 p_T for BFG I and BFG II fragmentation functions
- In the signal region (p_T < 5 GeV), the minimal chi-square per degree of freedom was 2.8 for BFG II, while BFG I gave 5.2 and GRV NLO gave 4.5, allowing rejection of BFG I and GRV NLO at 99% confidence level
- When omitting the two real photon data points, chi-square per degree of freedom values became 0.68 for BFG II, 0.61 for BFG I, and 0.63 for GRV NLO
- A reduction in statistical error by at least a factor of five would be needed to apply the method successfully
- The PHENIX and STAR collaborations recorded 574 and 526 inverse picobarns respectively in 2013 pp collisions at BNL RHIC, representing tenfold higher statistics than the 4 inverse picobarns of 2006 data analyzed

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to constrain photon fragmentation functions, which traditionally relied on e+e- collider data and vector meson production data (Abachi 1989, Buskulic 1995) using Vector Meson Dominance models (Bourhis 1997, Gluck 1992). The authors demonstrate that hadron collider data from BNL RHIC can in principle discriminate between the three modern parameterizations (BFG I, BFG II, and GRV NLO), and that the much higher statistics data recorded in 2013 by PHENIX and STAR should enable such discrimination. Future photon-jet correlations at RHIC and measurements at the CERN LHC by ALICE could further constrain the fragmentation functions, though LHC bandwidth limitations impede triggering on low-p_T data.
