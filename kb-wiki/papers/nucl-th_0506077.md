# Microscopic description of elastic and inelastic proton scattering from 208Pb

> Information on the equation of state (EOS) of neutron matter may be gained from studies of 208Pb. Descriptions of 208Pb require credible models of structure, taking particular note also of the spectrum. Such may be tested by analyses of scattering data. Herein, we report on such analyses using an RPA model for 208Pb in a folding model of the scattering. No a posteriori adjustment of parameters are needed to obtain excellent agreement with data. From those analyses, the skin thickness of 208Pb is constrained to lie in the range 0.13-0.17 fm.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of 208Pb is constrained to lie in the range 0.13–0.17 fm, based on a fully microscopic parameter-free model of elastic and inelastic proton scattering that requires no a posteriori adjustment of parameters to achieve agreement with data.

## Method

The authors used a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculation with the D1S effective nucleon-nucleon force of Gogny to obtain ground state and transition densities for 208Pb. These densities were folded with the Melbourne g matrices (derived from the Bonn-B potential) to produce microscopic optical potentials for elastic scattering and transition operators for inelastic scattering within a distorted wave approximation (DWA), using the code DWBA98. For comparison in elastic scattering, densities from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation using the SkM* interaction were also used.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb constrained to range 0.13–0.17 fm
- RPA model predicts skin thickness of 0.13 fm; SkM* model predicts 0.17 fm
- Proton rms radius from RPA: 5.47 fm; from SkM*: 5.45 fm
- Neutron rms radius from RPA: 5.59 fm; from SkM*: 5.62 fm
- B(E2) for transition to 2+1 state (4.08 MeV): model gives 0.296 e2fm4; experimental value is 0.318(13) e2fm4 (We84)
- B(E3) for transition to 3-1 state (2.61 MeV): model gives 0.692 e2fm6; experimental value is 0.611(12) e2fm6 (Go80)
- Elastic scattering data used at 121 MeV (Nadasen et al., 1981); inelastic scattering calculations performed at 135 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Melbourne g-folding model approach, previously successful for light nuclei (Am00), to heavy nuclei using RPA structure calculations. It addresses the need for credible models of 208Pb structure to constrain the neutron equation of state, following prior estimates of the skin thickness ranging from 0.1 to 0.3 fm (Ka02, Cl03). The results enable further analyses of inelastic scattering up to the 12+ state and motivate generalization to QRPA or Generator Coordinate Method for studying neutron densities in heavy nuclei and the neutron equation of state.
