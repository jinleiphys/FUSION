# Derivation of spontaneously broken gauge symmetry from the consistency of effective field theory II: Scalar field self-interactions and the electromagnetic interaction

> We extend our study of deriving the local gauge invariance with spontaneous symmetry breaking in the context of an effective field theory by considering self-interactions of the scalar field and inclusion of the electromagnetic interaction. By analyzing renormalizability and the scale separation conditions of three-, four- and five-point vertex functions of the scalar field, we fix the two couplings of the scalar field self-interactions of the leading order Lagrangian. Next we add the electromagnetic interaction and derive conditions relating the magnetic moment of the charged vector boson to its charge and the masses of the charged and neutral massive vector bosons to each other and the two independent couplings of the theory. We obtain the bosonic part of the Lagrangian of the electroweak Standard Model as a unique solution to the conditions imposed by the self-consistency conditions of the considered effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors show that demanding perturbative renormalizability and scale separation in an effective field theory (EFT) of a scalar field interacting with massive vector bosons and the electromagnetic field uniquely fixes all coupling constants to those of the spontaneously broken SU(2)×U(1) gauge theory in unitary gauge, without assuming gauge invariance as a starting principle. The derivation hinges on the condition that divergences in physical amplitudes must be removable by renormalizing only the leading-order parameters, which imposes constraints that the abstract states as "self-consistency conditions."

## Method

The authors constructed the most general Lorentz-invariant leading-order EFT Lagrangian for a scalar field, three massive vector bosons (two charged, one neutral), and later an Abelian gauge field for electromagnetism, with coupling constants of non-negative mass dimension. They calculated one-loop divergent parts of three-, four-, and five-point vertex functions of the scalar field using dimensional regularization, and imposed that all divergences in on-mass-shell physical amplitudes must be cancelled by renormalizing only the leading-order parameters (scale separation). For the electromagnetic extension, they analyzed one-loop divergences in the ΦZZ, ΦW⁺W⁻, ΦΦΦ, and ZW⁺W⁻ vertex functions to fix the magnetic moment of the charged vector boson and the neutral vector boson mixing parameter.

## Key numbers

- The condition from the five-point scalar vertex function yields two possible solutions: \( b = -2 g m^2 / M \) or \( b = -3 g m^2 / (2M) \), with the latter leading to self-consistent renormalization.
- The self-consistent solution for the scalar self-couplings is \( b = -3 g m^2 / (2M) \) and \( \lambda = 3 g^2 m^2 / (4 M^2) \), matching the spontaneously broken gauge theory values.
- The electromagnetic coupling parameters are fixed to \( \kappa_R = e_R \) and \( c_R = -e_R / g_R \), where \( \kappa_R \) is the magnetic moment coupling of the charged vector boson and \( c_R \) is the neutral vector boson mixing parameter.
- The one-loop counterterm for the vector boson mass is given by an expression involving \( g_R \), \( e_R \), \( c_R \), \( \kappa_R \), \( m_R \), and \( M_R \) (see Eq. for δM₁ in the text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Djukanovic, Gegelia, Meißner, 2018) which derived the gauge-invariant Lagrangian for vector bosons and a scalar but left the scalar self-couplings unfixed. It also builds on earlier work (Djukanovic, Gegelia, Meißner, 2005) on including electromagnetic interactions in such EFT derivations. The result shows that the bosonic sector of the electroweak Standard Model emerges uniquely from EFT consistency conditions, without assuming gauge symmetry as an input, and the authors state that inclusion of fermionic degrees of freedom is the next step to complete the program.
