# Harmonic moments of the gluon density distribution in AA collisions

> By using Monte-Carlo implementations of $k_T$-factorization formula with running-coupling BK unintegrated gluon distributions for nucleus-nucleus collisions, we compute higher order harmonic moments of the initial density distribution for both RHIC(Au+Au@200GeV) and LHC(Pb+Pb@2.76TeV) collisions. We study their sensitivity to the size of the valence parton distribution in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper computes higher-order harmonic moments of the initial gluon density distribution in heavy-ion collisions using a Monte-Carlo implementation of the \(k_T\)-factorization formula with running-coupling BK unintegrated gluon distributions, and finds that the magnitude of these moments is sensitive to the Gaussian width of the valence parton distribution in the nucleon, with smaller widths producing larger higher-order moments.

## Method

The author uses a Monte-Carlo implementation of the \(k_t\)-factorization formula (MCrcBK) with unintegrated gluon distributions obtained from numerical solutions of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. Nucleons in the two incident nuclei are sampled according to the Woods-Saxon distribution, and the thickness function is computed at each grid point to obtain the local saturation scale and gluon density. The Gaussian width \(B\) of the valence parton distribution is varied within the range \(B = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) fm\(^2\) to study its effect on the harmonic moments \(\varepsilon_n\) for \(n = 2, 3, 4, 5\).

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the centrality dependence of \(\varepsilon_2\), \(\varepsilon_3\), \(\varepsilon_4\), and \(\varepsilon_5\) is computed and compared with Monte-Carlo Glauber models.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section is set to \(\sigma_{NN} = 61.36\) mb.
- The Gaussian width \(B\) is varied over \(B = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) fm\(^2\) for both Au+Au at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV.
- For central collisions (\(b < 3\) fm), harmonic moments \(\varepsilon_2\) through \(\varepsilon_5\) are shown as a function of the Gaussian width for both collision systems.

## Context

This work extends the Monte-Carlo KLN (MC-KLN) model by incorporating running-coupling BK evolution for the unintegrated gluon distributions, and it studies the sensitivity of higher-order harmonic moments to the valence parton size, a topic previously explored by Sorensen (2011) regarding the role of a length scale. The results are compared with Monte-Carlo Glauber models (point-like and hard-disc nucleons) and with predictions from the DIPSY event generator (Flensburg, 2011), which includes additional fluctuations from BFKL cascades not present in this model. The findings enable future studies of how these initial-state length-scale effects propagate into higher-order hydrodynamic flow harmonics, providing constraints on both the initial conditions and the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
