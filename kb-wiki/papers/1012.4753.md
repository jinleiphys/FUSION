# Thermalization of Strongly Coupled Field Theories

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we probe the scale-dependence of thermalization in strongly coupled field theories following a quench via saddlepoint calculations of 2-point functions, Wilson loops and entanglement entropy in $d=2,3,4$. For homogeneous initial conditions, the entanglement entropy thermalizes slowest, and sets a timescale for equilibration that saturates a causality bound. The growth rate of entanglement entropy density is nearly volume-independent for small volumes, but slows for larger volumes. In this strongly coupled setting, the UV thermalizes first.

## Key claim
Using holographic duality, the authors find that entanglement entropy thermalizes slowest among the probes studied and sets a timescale that saturates a causality bound, but this conclusion is conditioned on homogeneous initial conditions and the specific thin-shell quench model; the UV thermalizes first, and for large volumes the entanglement entropy growth rate deviates from volume-proportional behavior.

## Method
The authors model thermalization after a sudden energy injection by an infalling thin mass shell in asymptotically AdS spacetimes (AdS₃, AdS₄, AdS₅), using the Vaidya metric with a tanh mass function. They compute holographic equal-time 2-point functions of large-dimension operators via geodesic lengths, Wilson loop expectation values via minimal surface areas, and entanglement entropy via minimal surface areas (or volumes in AdS₅), all as functions of boundary time and spatial scale. For d=2 they obtain analytic results; for d=3,4 they perform numerical integration of the geodesic and minimal-surface equations.

## Key numbers
- For d=2, the complete thermalization time for 2-point correlators is analytically τ_dur ≡ ℓ/2, where ℓ is the boundary separation.
- For d=2, the renormalized geodesic length in the thin-shell limit (v₀→0) is δℒ(ℓ,t₀) = 2 ln[ sinh(√M t₀) / (√M s(ℓ,t₀)) ], with s(ℓ,t₀) ∈ [0,1] defined parametrically.
- For d=2, the thermal equilibrium geodesic length is δℒ_thermal(ℓ) = 2 ln[ (1/√M) sinh(√M ℓ/2) ].
- For strongly coupled matter at heavy ion collider energies (T ≈ 300–400 MeV), the thermalization time is estimated as τ_dur ∼ 0.3 fm/c.
- In d=2, the growth rate of entanglement entropy density falls asymptotically as 1/ℓ for large ℓ.

## Context
This work extends holographic studies of thermalization via gravitational collapse in AdS, following earlier work on black hole formation by Chesler (2008) and on local observable thermalization by Bhattacharyya (2009). It generalizes the d=2 geodesic-length analysis of AbajoArrastia (2010) to d=3 and d=4, and extends the d=3 entanglement entropy study of Albash (2010) to d=4. The results contrast with the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) for weakly coupled gauge theories, and raise questions about the relationship between entanglement entropy growth and Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rates defined by coarse graining.
