# Sigma Meson Cloud and Proton's Light Flavor Sea Quarks

> We take into account the sigma meson cloud effect in the meson cloud model to calculate the distributions of light flavor sea quarks in the proton. Our calculation gives a better description of the data for $\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)$. We also provide a picture that the probability of finding a physical proton in a Fock state $|Nω> $ is reasonable small with a smaller cutoff $Λ_ω$.

## Key claim

Including the sigma meson cloud effect in the meson cloud model improves the description of the experimental data for the ratio \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) in the proton, and also allows a smaller cutoff for the omega meson cloud, yielding a more physically reasonable probability for the \(|N\omega\rangle\) Fock state.

## Method

The authors calculate the light flavor sea quark distributions in the proton using the meson cloud model, where the physical proton is a superposition of a bare nucleon plus baryon-meson Fock states. They include contributions from pion, sigma, and omega meson clouds with the nucleon, using time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame with an exponential form factor cutoff. The splitting functions for the sigma meson are derived from the linear sigma model with a coupling constant \(g_\sigma^2/4\pi = 13.6\) and a sigma mass of 600 MeV, and the quark distributions of the sigma meson are assumed equal to those of the pion.

## Key numbers

- The integrated Gottfried sum rule violation is \(D \equiv \int_0^1 [\bar{d}(x) - \bar{u}(x)] \, dx = 0.148 \pm 0.039\) from NMC data.
- The sigma meson mass is set to \(m_\sigma = 600\ \mathrm{MeV}\), based on a reanalysis of \(\pi\pi\) scattering giving \(m_\sigma = 585 \pm 20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_\sigma = 580 \pm 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from pp central collisions.
- The cutoff parameter for the sigma meson is varied in the range \(1.0 < \Lambda_\sigma < 1.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The pion cutoff is \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.88 \pm 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV}\), chosen to reproduce the integrated asymmetry.
- The omega meson coupling constant is \(g_\omega^2/4\pi = 8.1\), and its cutoff is varied in the range \(1.0 < \Lambda_\omega < 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The number of pions in the proton is \(n_\pi = 0.175\); the number of sigma mesons ranges from \(n_\sigma = 0.023\) to \(0.078\); the number of omega mesons ranges from \(n_\omega = 0.063\) to \(0.671\).
- In Alberg and Henley's work (no sigma), \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.83\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\Lambda_\omega = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(n_\pi = 0.150\), and \(n_\omega = 0.671\).

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model calculations of Alberg, Henley, and Miller (2002), who found that including the isoscalar omega meson gave a good description of the \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) data but required a large cutoff \(\Lambda_\omega = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\). The authors follow the suggestion in that work to add the sigma meson effect, which allows a smaller and more physically reasonable omega cutoff. This enables a more consistent picture of the proton's nonperturbative sea quark structure and can be used to refine future calculations of flavor asymmetries in the nucleon.
