# Emission of gamma rays shifted from resonant absorption by electron-nuclear double transitions in ^{151}Eu^{2+}:CaF_2

> We show that the emission of a gamma-ray photon by a nucleus can be influenced by a microwave magnetic field acting on the atomic electrons. We study theoretically these electron-nuclear double transitions (ENDTs) for ^{151}Eu nuclei in a CaF_2 lattice at low temperature, in the presence of a static magnetic field and of a microwave magnetic field. The ENDTs acquire a significant intensity for certain resonance frequencies. The ENDTs are of interest for the identification of the position of the lines in complex Mössbauer spectra.

## Key claim

The emission of a gamma-ray photon by a nucleus can be influenced by a microwave magnetic field acting on atomic electrons via a two-photon process (electron-nuclear double transition, ENDT), but the effect only acquires significant intensity when the microwave photon energy is resonant with a specific electron Zeeman transition.

## Method

The authors performed a theoretical second-order perturbation theory calculation of the electron-nuclear double transition (ENDT) rate for \(^{151}\)Eu\(^{2+}\) ions in a CaF\(_2\) lattice at low temperature (1 K), under a static magnetic field and a microwave magnetic field. They considered the 21.54 keV M1 nuclear transition from the 7/2+ excited state (half-life 9.4 ns) to the 5/2+ ground state, coupled to the \(^8S_{7/2}\) electron ground state of Eu\(^{2+}\), and computed the relative intensity of the ENDT gamma-ray line compared to the unperturbed single-photon Mössbauer line.

## Key numbers

- Relative intensity of the resonant ENDT line: \(R = 0.098\) for a microwave magnetic field amplitude \(B_{mw} = 10^{-4}\) T, independent of nuclear magnetic quantum numbers \(m_u, m_l\).
- For static magnetic field \(B = 2\) T: resonance microwave frequency \(\omega_{mw}/(2\pi) = 59.098\) GHz; ENDT gamma-ray line shift \((E_\gamma - E_0)/(2\pi\hbar) = -148\) MHz; fractional electron population 0.941 in \(M=-7/2\) state and 0.054 in \(M=-5/2\) state at 1 K.
- For static magnetic field \(B = 0.2342\) T: resonance microwave frequency \(\omega_{mw}/(2\pi) = 9.797\) GHz; ENDT gamma-ray line shift \((E_\gamma - E_0)/(2\pi\hbar) = -142\) MHz; fractional electron population 0.31 in \(M=-7/2\) state and 0.19 in \(M=-5/2\) state at 1 K.
- Ground state hyperfine constant \(A_l/(2\pi\hbar) = -102.9069\) MHz; excited state hyperfine constant \(A_u/(2\pi\hbar) = -54.8185\) MHz.
- Half-life of the 21.54 keV state: \(t_{1/2} = 9.4\) ns, giving width \(\Gamma = \ln 2 / t_{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hack and Hamermesh on influencing gamma-ray emission with oscillatory electromagnetic fields, applying it specifically to the \(^{151}\)Eu\(^{2+}\):CaF\(_2\) system. The electron energy levels used are based on prior electron paramagnetic resonance measurements by Ryter and Baker et al., and ENDOR observations by Baker and Williams. The ENDT approach enables decomposition of complex, overlapping Mössbauer spectra into simpler partial spectra by tuning the microwave frequency to specific electron Zeeman resonances, and may have applications for research on amplification of gamma rays without inversion of nuclear population.
