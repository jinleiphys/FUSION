# Melting Hadrons, Boiling Quarks

> In the context of the Hagedorn temperature half-centenary I describe our understanding of the hot phases of hadronic matter both below and above the Hagedorn temperature. The first part of the review addresses many frequently posed questions about properties of hadronic matter in different phases, phase transition and the exploration of quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The historical context of the discovery of QGP is shown and the role of strangeness and strange antibaryon signature of QGP illustrated. In the second part I discuss the corresponding theoretical ideas and show how experimental results can be used to describe the properties of QGP at hadronization. The material of this review is complemented by two early and unpublished reports containing the prediction of the different forms of hadron matter, and of the formation of QGP in relativistic heavy ion collisions, including the discussion of strangeness, and in particular strange antibaryon signature of QGP.

## Key claim

This review argues that the Hagedorn temperature, proposed half a century ago, marks the boundary between a hadron gas and a quark-gluon plasma (QGP), and that the transition between these phases can be studied experimentally using strangeness and strange antibaryon production as a signature of QGP formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The paper is a review that first describes the historical development of the Hagedorn temperature concept and the theoretical prediction of QGP, then presents the role of strangeness as a signature for QGP formation. The second part discusses how experimental results from heavy-ion collisions can be used to characterize QGP properties at hadronization, including the use of statistical hadronization models to extract thermodynamic parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature was proposed in 1965 by R. Hagedorn (Hagedorn, 1965) as an "ultimate temperature" of hadronic matter.
- The review includes two early unpublished reports from 1980 and 1983 (appended as appendix A and appendix B) that predicted different forms of hadron matter and QGP formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
- The paper references a CERN Courier article (Ericson and Rafelski, 2003) that discusses the Hagedorn temperature, with an evaluation of the hadron mass spectrum made by Letessier and Rafelski (unpublished) based on the method of Tounsi, Letessier, and Rafelski (1995).

## Context

This review commemorates the half-centenary of the Hagedorn temperature, originally proposed by Hagedorn (1965) and developed further by Frautschi (1971) and others. It extends the work of Koch, Muller, and Rafelski (1986) on strangeness in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the work of Letessier and Rafelski (2002) on hadrons and quark-gluon plasma. The review enables future studies connecting QGP heavy-ion physics to the early universe, as discussed by Rafelski (2013), and provides a comprehensive reference for understanding the transition from hadronic matter to QGP.
