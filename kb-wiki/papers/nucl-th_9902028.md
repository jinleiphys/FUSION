# Analysis of resonance multipoles from polarization observables in eta photoproduction

> A combined analysis of new eta photoproduction data for total and differential cross sections, target asymmetry and photon asymmetry is presented. Using a few reasonable assumptions we perform the first model-independent analysis of the E0+, E2- and M2- eta photoproduction multipoles. Making use of the well-known A3/2 helicity amplitude of the D13(1520) state we extract its branching ratio to the eta-N channel, Gamma(eta,N)/Gamma = (0.08 +- 0.01)%. At higher energies, we show that the photon asymmetry is extremely sensitive to small multipoles that are excited by photons in the helicity 3/2 state. The new GRAAL photon asymmetry data at higher energy show a clear signal of the F15(1680) excitation which permits extracting an F15(1680)->eta,N branching ratio of (0.15 +0.35 -0.10)%.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first model-independent analysis of the E0+, E2-, and M2- eta photoproduction multipoles, but this analysis relies on the assumption of s-wave dominance and the neglect of phase differences between electric and magnetic multipoles for higher partial waves. The extracted branching ratios for the D13(1520) and F15(1680) resonances to the eta-N channel are conditional on the specific assumptions made about the S11(1535) phase and the use of Breit-Wigner parameterizations.

## Method

The authors performed a combined multipole analysis of total and differential cross sections, target asymmetry (T), and photon asymmetry (Sigma) for eta photoproduction from threshold up to 1.05 GeV. Using the assumption of s-wave (E0+) dominance from the S11(1535) resonance, they expressed the three observables as power series in cos(theta) with coefficients a through f, which were fitted to experimental data from MAMI, ELSA, and GRAAL. From the fitted coefficients, the d-wave multipoles E2- and M2- and their helicity combinations B2- (helicity 3/2) and A2- (helicity 1/2) were extracted, and resonance parameters for the D13(1520) and F15(1680) were determined using the helicity matrix element formalism.

## Key numbers

* D13(1520) branching ratio to eta-N: Gamma(eta,N)/Gamma = (0.08 +/- 0.01)%, extracted using the known A3/2 helicity amplitude of the D13(1520) state.
* F15(1680) branching ratio to eta-N: (0.15 +0.35 -0.10)%, extracted from the GRAAL photon asymmetry data at higher energy.
* S11(1535) resonance parameters from Breit-Wigner fit: M_R = (1549 +/- 8) MeV, Gamma_R = (202 +/- 35) MeV.
* Absolute value of the s-wave multipole at threshold: |E0+| = 16 x 10^-3 / m_pi^+.
* Phase of S11(1535) at the D13(1520) mass: delta_0 = 136 degrees from model-independent analysis; delta_0 = 72 degrees from Breit-Wigner assumption.
* Phase of S11(1535) at the F15(1680) mass: delta_0 = 137 degrees from Breit-Wigner formula.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of eta photoproduction that were dominated by the S11(1535) resonance (Krusche 1995, Wilhelm 1993) and isobar model calculations (Knoechlein 1995). It provides the first model-independent extraction of d-wave multipoles, enabling the determination of small branching ratios for resonances like D13(1520) and F15(1680) that are invisible in total cross sections due to S11 dominance. The results confirm predictions from the constituent quark model of Koniuk and Isgur (1980) for eta-N branching ratios and contradict the larger A3/2/A1/2 ratio for D13 given in the Particle Data Tables, while also finding an unexpected phase shift between S11 and D13 that requires experimental verification.
