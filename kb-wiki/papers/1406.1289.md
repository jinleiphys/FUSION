# Disagreement between capture probabilities extracted from capture and quasi-elastic backscattering excitation functions

> Experimental quasi-elastic backscattering and capture (fusion) excitation functions are usually used to extract the s-wave capture probabilities for the heavy-ion reactions. We investigated the $^{16}$O+$^{120}$Sn,$^{144}$Sm,$^{208}$Pb systems at energies near and below the corresponding Coulomb barriers and concluded that the probabilities extracted from quasi-elastic data are much larger than the ones extracted from fusion excitation functions at sub and deep-sub barrier energies. This seems to be a reasonable explanation for the known disagreement observed in literature for the nuclear potential diffuseness derived from both methods.

## Key claim

The s-wave capture probabilities extracted from quasi-elastic backscattering data are systematically larger than those extracted from capture (fusion) excitation functions at sub-barrier and deep sub-barrier energies for the three systems studied, providing a plausible explanation for the known discrepancy in nuclear potential diffuseness parameters derived from the two methods.

## Method

The authors extracted s-wave capture probabilities from experimental capture cross sections using the derivative relation \(P_{cap}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}},J=0) = \frac{1}{\pi R_b^2} \frac{d[E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}\sigma_{cap}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}})]}{dE_{\mathrm{c.m.}}}\), and from experimental quasi-elastic backscattering data using the relation \(P_{cap}(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}},J=0) = 1 - d\sigma_{qe}/d\sigma_{Ru}\), where \(d\sigma_{qe}/d\sigma_{Ru}\) is the ratio of quasi-elastic to Rutherford differential cross sections at backward angles. These two extraction methods were applied to the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{120}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{144}\mathrm{Sm}\), and \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) systems at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{120}\mathrm{Sn}\) system, there is good agreement between the two extracted probabilities at energies near the Coulomb barrier, but disagreement appears at deep sub-barrier energies.
* For the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) system, the deviations are dramatic even at near-barrier energies and increase with decreasing energy below the barrier.
* For the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{144}\mathrm{Sm}\) system, disagreement is observed even at energies not too much below the barrier; shifting the capture probabilities by 1 MeV to lower energies improves agreement at near-barrier energies but disagreement remains important at deep sub-barrier energies.
* The critical angular momenta \(J_{cr}\) for the studied systems range from 54 to 62, for systems with \(Z_1 \times Z_2 < 2000\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sargsyan et al. (2013) on the relationship between capture and quasi-elastic probabilities, and directly addresses the discrepancies in nuclear potential diffuseness parameters reported by Mukherjee et al., Gasques et al., and Evers et al. The findings suggest that the overestimation of capture probability from quasi-elastic data at deep sub-barrier energies may arise from underestimation of the total reaction differential cross section when using the Rutherford cross section as reference. The study calls for more precise experimental measurements of quasi-elastic backscattering excitation functions and further theoretical investigation.
