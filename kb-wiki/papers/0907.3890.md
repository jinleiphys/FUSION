# Model-independent constraints on spin observables

> We discuss model-independent constraints on spin observables in exclusive and inclusive reactions, with special attention to the case of photoproduction.

# Model-independent constraints on spin observables

## Key claim

Given a first set of spin-observable measurements, the remaining observables are often constrained to an interval much smaller than the naive [-1,+1] range, meaning that one can identify which new observable will best discriminate among competing models without necessarily measuring all observables.

## Method

The authors derive model-independent identities and inequalities among spin observables from the positivity of the density matrix, applied to exclusive reactions (pion-nucleon elastic scattering, antiproton-proton to Lambda-antilambda, and photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons) and inclusive reactions. They use algebraic manipulation of bilinear combinations of helicity amplitudes and, for exploratory work, generate random fictitious amplitudes to reveal the allowed domain boundaries before proving the inequalities rigorously.

## Key numbers

- For pion-nucleon elastic scattering, the three observables (polarisation P_n, spin-rotation parameters A and R) satisfy the identity P_n^2 + A^2 + R^2 = 1.
- For the reaction pbar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda, the inequalities D_nn^2 + C_mm^2 ≤ 1 and D_nn^2 + C_ll^2 ≤ 1 hold, where C_ij are spin correlations in the final state.
- For photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons, the triple {Sigma, O_x, O_z} satisfies Sigma^2 + O_x^2 + O_z^2 ≤ 1 (the unit sphere), while the triple {Sigma, O_x, O_z} for pbar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda gives the intersection of three orthogonal cylinders, which is slightly wider than the unit sphere.
- For inclusive reactions a+b -> anything, the helicity asymmetry Delta_sigma_L and transversity asymmetry Delta_sigma_T satisfy |Delta_sigma_T| ≤ sigma_tot + Delta_sigma_L/2.
- The Soffer inequality q(x) + Delta q(x) ≥ 2 |delta q(x)| relates helicity asymmetry Delta q and transversity asymmetry delta q, giving a triangular domain identical to that for Delta_sigma_L and Delta_sigma_T.

## Context

This work extends the earlier program of Doncel, Minnaert, Michel and others on the polarisation domain, applying positivity constraints to modern photoproduction data from CLAS and GRAAL (Lleres 2008). The authors show that for the reaction pbar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda, the near-zero value of D_nn could have been anticipated from earlier data on C_mm and C_ll via model-independent inequalities, and that both Yukawa-type meson-exchange models and quark-based models had been unable to distinguish themselves before this constraint was recognised. The results enable experimentalists to prioritise which spin observables to measure next, and they connect to quantum-information concepts such as Bell-inequality violation in hadronic reactions.
