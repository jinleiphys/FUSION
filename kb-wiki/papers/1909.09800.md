# Antiproton production in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies

> Within the framework of the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics model, the deep subthreshold antiproton production in heavy-ion collisions has been investigated thoroughly. The elastic scattering, annihilation and charge exchange reactions associated with antiproton channels are implemented in the model. The attractive antiproton potential extracted from the G-parity transformation of the nucleon selfenergies enhance the antiproton yields to some extent. The calculated invariant spectra are consistent with the available experimental data. The primordial antiproton yields increase with the mass number of colliding system. However, the annihilation reactions reduce the antiproton production and enable independent to colliding partners. Antiflow phenomena of antiprotons correlated to the mean-field potential and annihilation mechanism is found in comparison with proton flows.

# Antiproton production in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies

## Key claim

The LQMD transport model, which includes an attractive antiproton potential derived from G-parity transformation of nucleon self-energies (with a scaling factor ξ=0.25) and explicit annihilation channels, reproduces available experimental invariant spectra of antiprotons produced in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies, but only when the annihilation channel is included; the primordial antiproton yields are reduced by annihilation to approximately 4% of their initial value in central ²⁸Si+²⁸Si collisions at 3 GeV/nucleon.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was extended to include antiproton production channels via pion-baryon and nucleon-baryon collisions (πB → Np̄p̄, BB → NNp̄p̄) with parametrized cross sections, together with antiproton-nucleon elastic scattering, charge-exchange reactions, and annihilation reactions described by a statistical model with SU(3) symmetry. The antiproton-nucleon optical potential was constructed from the relativistic mean-field model (NL3 parameter) using G-parity transformation with a scaling factor ξ=0.25 to reproduce a potential depth of -164 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³, and the effective mass m*ₚ̄ was used to evaluate the threshold energy for antiproton production.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton production threshold energy in nucleon-nucleon collisions: E_th = 5.62 GeV
- Attractive antiproton potential strength: V_Ṇ = -164 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- In central ²⁸Si+²⁸Si collisions at 3 GeV/nucleon: primordial antiproton multiplicity ~10⁻⁵, reduced by annihilation to ~4×10⁻⁷ (approximately 4% of primordial antiprotons escape annihilation)
- Escaping probability from annihilation varies from 1.3% at 2 GeV/nucleon to 7.5% at 4 GeV/nucleon in central ²⁸Si+²⁸Si collisions
- Cross section parametrization for pion-induced reactions: a=1 mb, b=2.31, c=2.3; for nucleon-induced reactions: a=0.12 mb, b=3.5, c=2.7
- Calculated invariant spectra compared with experimental data for ²⁸Si+²⁸Si at 2 GeV/nucleon and ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni at 1.85 GeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic transport approaches to antiproton production, including the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) model (Li, 1994) and earlier models such as the fireball model (Ko, 1989), first-chance nucleon-nucleon collision model (Sh, 1990), and quasicoherent multiparticle collision model (Da, 1990). The LQMD model provides a self-consistent treatment of all reaction channels including annihilation products, which earlier approaches treated less completely. The results enable future investigations of antiproton physics at the PANDA facility at Darmstadt and at the high-intensity heavy-ion accelerator facility (HIAF) in Huizhou, China, particularly for studying in-medium hadron properties, chiral symmetry restoration, and the nuclear equation of state at high density.
