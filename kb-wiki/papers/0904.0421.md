# Serber symmetry, Large Nc and Yukawa-like One Boson Exchange Potentials

> The Serber force has relative orbital parity symmetry and requires vanishing NN interactions in partial waves with odd angular momentum. We illustrate how this property is well fulfilled for spin triplet states with odd angular momentum and violated for odd singlet states for realistic potentials but fails for chiral potentials. We analyze how Serber symmetry can be accommodated within a large Nc perspective when interpreted as a long distance symmetry. A prerequisite for this is the numerical similarity of the scalar and vector meson resonance masses. The conditions under which the resonance exchange potential can be approximated by a Yukawa form are also discussed. While these masses arise as poles on the Second Riemann in pi-pi scattering, we find that within the large Nc expansion the corresponding Yukawa masses correspond instead to a well defined large Nc approximation to the pole which cannot be distinguished from their location as Breit-Wigner resonances.

## Key claim

Serber symmetry (vanishing NN interactions in odd-angular-momentum partial waves) holds well for spin-triplet odd-L states but is violated for odd singlet states in realistic potentials, and fails for chiral potentials; the symmetry can be accommodated within a large Nc perspective when interpreted as a long-distance symmetry, provided scalar and vector meson resonance masses are numerically similar.

## Method

The authors analyze the Serber symmetry by computing mean potentials and phase shifts for the center of orbital multiplets from high-quality NN potentials (Argonne V-18, Nijmegen PWA) and from Vlowk renormalization-group evolved potentials. They examine the OPE contribution, boundary-condition parameterizations of short-range physics, chiral two-pion exchange at N3LO, and the large Nc expansion of meson-exchange potentials, focusing on the conditions under which resonance-exchange potentials can be approximated by Yukawa forms.

## Key numbers

* The OPE potential gives a factor of 9 for the singlet-to-triplet ratio in odd-L waves (V^π_1P = 9 W_S^π vs V^π_3P = W_S^π), providing a close approximation to the Serber limit where the potential is small.
* In the Nijmegen PWA boundary-condition analysis, the short-range potential for the center of the P-wave multiplet is V_S,^1P(k^2) = 139.448 - 23.417 k^2 + 2.479 k^4 (MeV) while V_S,^3P(k^2) = 14.666 + 0.92 k^2 + 0.029 k^4 (MeV), showing V_S,^3P ≪ V_S,^1P.
* For F-waves in the Nijmegen PWA, V_S,^1F = 248.73 MeV while V_S,^3F = -33.08 + 5.90 k^2 MeV, again with triplet much smaller than singlet.
* The scalar meson (σ) pole from ππ scattering is √s_σ = 441^(+16)_(-8) - i 272^(+9)_(-12) MeV; the vector meson (ρ) pole is √s_ρ = 775.49 ± 0.34 - i 149.4 ± 1.0 MeV.
* The chiral TPE potential at very long distances behaves as V_2π^ChPT(r) = (1 + 2 τ₁·τ₂) e^(-2m_π r)/r × (3 g_A^4 m_π^5)/(1024 f_π^4 M_N π^2) + …, breaking Serber symmetry already at long range.
* For the N3LO chiral potential with cut-off Λ_χ = 600 MeV (vs 500 MeV), a 5% violation of Wigner symmetry appears in S-wave Vlowk potentials.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of Wigner SU(4) symmetry (Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola, 2008) to the Serber symmetry case, showing that while Wigner symmetry holds for even-L waves, Serber symmetry holds for odd-L triplet waves at distances above 1.5 fm. The paper connects these empirical symmetries to the large Nc expansion of QCD (following 't Hooft 1974, Witten 1979, and reviews by Manohar 1998, Jenkins 1998, Lebed 1998) and to the resonance saturation hypothesis used in chiral effective field theory (Ecker 1988). The finding that Yukawa masses from large Nc approximations to ππ resonance poles cannot be distinguished from Breit-Wigner resonance locations provides a rationale for the numerical similarity of scalar and vector meson masses needed for Serber symmetry, and suggests that the symmetry may be understood from QCD in the large Nc limit.
