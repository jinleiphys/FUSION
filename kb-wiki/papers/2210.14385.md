# Bump of sound velocity in dense 2-color QCD

> We obtain the equation of state (EoS) and the sound velocity for 2-color QCD at low temperature and high density and find that in the superfluid phase, $c_s^2/c^2 >1/3$, where $1/3$ is the value at the relativistic limit. Several independent Monte Carlo studies on 2-color QCD have been conducted intensively in recent years. These works have shown a clear evidence of phase transition between hadronic and superfluid phases. In our paper[1], we have investigated the EoS and sound velocity in both phases. Our result is consistent with chiral perturbation theory in a low $μ$ regime of the superfluid phase including the Bose-Einstein condensed phase, and shows a peak of sound velocity in the high-density BCS phase. We also give detailed simulation results and a comment on the holography bound in this proceedings.

## Key claim

In the superfluid phase of dense 2-color QCD at low temperature (T ≈ 79 MeV), the squared sound velocity exceeds the relativistic limit of 1/3 and exhibits a peak around μ ≈ m_PS, a feature previously unknown from lattice calculations for QCD-like theories.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of 2-color QCD using the Iwasaki gauge action and naive Wilson fermions with a diquark source term, employing the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm. They used a fixed scale method with parameters (β, κ, N_s, N_τ) = (0.80, 0.159, 16, 16) corresponding to T ≈ 79 MeV and a ≈ 0.17 fm, generating configurations at intervals of aΔμ = 0.05 with 100-300 configurations per parameter. The trace anomaly was computed from the beta-functions of β and κ evaluated at μ = T = 0 along the line of constant physics, and the sound velocity was obtained via symmetric finite differences of pressure and energy density.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-scalar meson mass at μ = 0 is am_PS = 0.6229(34), corresponding to m_PS ≈ 750 MeV
- The critical chemical potential for the hadronic-superfluid phase transition is μ_c/m_PS ≈ 0.5
- The pressure reaches p/p_SB ≈ 0.84 at the highest density studied (μ/m_PS = 1.28, μ ≲ 960 MeV)
- The nonperturbative beta-functions are a dβ/da = -0.352 and a dκ/da = 0.0282 at (β, κ) = (0.80, 0.159)
- The simulation temperature is T = 0.39 T_c ≈ 79 MeV, with T_c = 200 MeV as the pseudo-critical temperature at μ = 0

## Context

This work extends recent intensive Monte Carlo studies of 2-color QCD (e.g., Cotter (2012), Braguta (2016), Iida (2019), Boz (2019), Bornyakov (2022)) that established the phase diagram including hadronic, BEC, and BCS phases. The results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory (Kogut (2000), Son (2001)) in the low-μ regime of the superfluid phase, and support predictions from quarkyonic matter models (McLerran and Reddy (2019), Kojo (2021), Kojo and Suenaga (2021)) that a peak in sound velocity emerges from quark Fermi sea saturation. The finding that c_s^2/c^2 exceeds 1/3 at finite density provides a counterexample to the holography bound conjecture (Cherman (2009)) and enables future investigations into whether such a peak is a universal property of superfluids in the BEC-BCS crossover regime, with implications for neutron star structure in 3-color QCD.
