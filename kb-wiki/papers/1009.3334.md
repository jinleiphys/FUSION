# System-size dependence of the pion freeze-out volume as a potential signature for the phase transition to a Quark Gluon Plasma

> Hanburry-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation functions and radii of negatively charged pions from C+C, Si+Si, Cu+Cu, and In+In at lower RHIC/SPS energies are calculated with the UrQMD transport model and the CRAB analyzing program. We find a minimum in the excitation function of the pion freeze-out volume at low transverse momenta and around $E_{lab}\sim 20-30A$GeV which can be related to the transition from hadronic to string matter (which might be interpreted as a pre-cursor of the QGP). The existence of the minimum is explained by the competition of two mechanisms of the particle production, resonance decays and string formation/fragmentation.

## Key claim

The paper finds a minimum in the excitation function of the pion freeze-out volume at low transverse momenta around \(E_{lab}\sim 20-30A\) GeV, which is interpreted as a potential signature for the transition from hadronic to string matter (a pre-cursor to the Quark Gluon Plasma), but the authors note that this minimum is system-size dependent in cascade calculations and only becomes universally present when mean-field potentials are included.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (in both cascade mode and with a modified equation of state including mean-field potentials for formed and pre-formed hadrons) to simulate central collisions of C+C, Si+Si, Cu+Cu, and In+In at beam energies from 2A GeV to 80A GeV. They extracted HBT correlation functions and Pratt radii for negatively charged pion pairs using the CRAB analyzing program (version 3.0\(\beta\)), fitting the three-dimensional correlation function with a standard Gaussian form. The pion freeze-out volume was then calculated as \(V_f = (2\pi)^{3/2} R_L R_S^2\) for two transverse momentum bins (0-100 MeV/\(c\) and 100-200 MeV/\(c\)).

## Key numbers

* The extracted temperature parameter \(T\) from pion transverse mass spectra increases from about 0.11 GeV to 0.14 GeV with increasing beam energy at AGS energies, then becomes flat at higher energies.
* In C+C collisions at the \(k_T\) bin 0-100 MeV/\(c\), a minimum in the longitudinal HBT radius \(R_L\) is seen at approximately 25A GeV for calculations both with and without mean-field potentials.
* In In+In collisions, a minimum in \(R_L\) appears at approximately 35A GeV only in calculations with mean-field potentials and at the higher \(k_T\) bin 100-200 MeV/\(c\).
* For C+C with mean-field potentials, the freeze-out volume \(V_f\) decreases by about 7% from 10A GeV to 20A GeV at the lower \(k_T\) bin.
* For heavier systems (Si+Si, Cu+Cu, In+In) with mean-field potentials, the amplitude of the decrease of \(V_f\) from 10A GeV to 35A GeV is about 9% at the higher \(k_T\) bin.
* The minimum in the freeze-out volume, when present, lies consistently around the beam energy of 30A GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous HBT analyses by Adamova (2002) and others that found a minimum in the pion freeze-out volume between AGS and SPS energies, but which could not be explained by cascade-mode transport model calculations (Lisa, 2005; Li, 2007). The present study provides a baseline calculation for the system-size and transverse-momentum dependence of the HBT pion freeze-out volume, showing that the minimum is robust when mean-field potentials are included. The results are directly relevant to the NA61/SHINE experiment at CERN-SPS, which is currently taking data for systems from p+p to Au+Au at beam energies from 10A GeV to 158A GeV, and could help locate the critical endpoint and the boundary of the first-order phase transition in the QCD phase diagram.
