# Resummation of fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams to all orders

> A system of fermions with a short-range interaction proportional to the scattering length $a$ is studied at finite density. At any order $a^n$, we evaluate the complete contributions to the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f)$ arising from combined (multiple) particle-particle and hole-hole rescatterings in the medium. This novel result is achieved by simply decomposing the particle-hole propagator into the vacuum propagator plus a medium-insertion and correcting for certain symmetry factors in the $(n-1)$-th power of the in-medium loop. Known results for the low-density expansion up to and including order $a^4$ are accurately reproduced. The emerging series in $a k_f$ can be summed to all orders in the form of a double-integral over an arctangent function. In that representation the unitary limit $a\to \infty$ can be taken and one obtains the value $ξ= 0.5067$ for the universal Bertsch parameter. We discuss also applications to the equation of state of neutron matter at low densities and mention further extensions of the resummation method.

# Resummation of fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams to all orders

## Key claim

The paper claims that the complete contributions to the energy per particle from combined particle-particle and hole-hole rescatterings at any order a^n can be summed to all orders in the form of a double-integral over an arctangent function, yielding a Bertsch parameter of ξ = 0.5067 in the unitary limit a → ∞. This result is conditional on the specific decomposition of the particle-hole propagator into vacuum propagator plus medium-insertion and the correction for symmetry factors in the (n-1)-th power of the in-medium loop.

## Method

The author decomposes the in-medium fermion propagator into the vacuum propagator plus a "medium-insertion" involving a delta-function for on-shell kinematics and a step-function restricting momenta inside the Fermi sphere. The complex-valued in-medium loop is constructed from contributions with zero, one, and two medium-insertions, and the energy per particle at order a^n is obtained by reweighting terms in the binomial expansion of the (n-1)-th power of the in-medium loop with symmetry factors 1/(j+1). The resulting series in a k_f is summed to all orders via a complex logarithm, producing a double-integral representation over the quarter unit-disc involving an arctangent function.

## Context

This work extends the partial resummation results of Schaefer et al. (2005), who resummed particle-particle ladders (giving ξ^(pp) ≈ 0.24) and hole-hole ladders separately, and corrects a factor-of-2 error in Steele's (unpublished) calculation of the mixed particle-particle and hole-hole ladder at order a^4, as confirmed by Hammer et al. The complete resummation of all mixed ladders yields a Bertsch parameter ξ = 0.5067, which is larger than the ξ ≈ 0.38 from quantum Monte-Carlo simulations and the ξ ≈ 0.36 from the self-consistent treatment by Haussmann et al. (2007). The result enables improved equation-of-state calculations for low-density neutron matter, where the large neutron-neutron scattering length makes the system close to the unitary limit.
