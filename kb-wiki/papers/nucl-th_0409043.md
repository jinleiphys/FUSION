# Ratio of the proton electromagnetic form factors from meson dressing

> The Dressed K-matrix Model, developed previously for low- and intermediate-energy Compton scattering, is generalized to calculate the photon-nucleon vertex with a virtual photon and an off-shell nucleon. Using this model, the ratio of the proton electromagnetic form factors is computed. The calculated ratio is in excellent agreement with the ratio measured in polarized electron-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The Dressed K-matrix Model (DKM), with parameters previously fixed by fitting Compton scattering and pion-nucleon phase shifts, can reproduce the momentum-transfer dependence of the ratio of the proton electromagnetic form factors measured in polarized electron-proton scattering, provided the coupling of a vector isoscalar particle is adjusted to a large value (κ_vect = 60) that differs significantly from the ωNN coupling used earlier.

## Method

The authors generalized the DKM dressing procedure from real to virtual space-like photons, calculating the six invariant functions of the half-off-shell γNN vertex for all nucleon off-shell momenta and photon virtualities. The vertex was dressed nonperturbatively with an infinite number of meson loops (pions, ρ and σ mesons) using iterative dispersion relations, with the πNN, πNΔ vertices and nucleon/Δ propagators also dressed consistently. The on-shell limit then yielded the Dirac and Pauli form factors, from which the Sachs form factor ratio was obtained.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ratio G_E/G_M agrees with JLab polarization-transfer measurements over the range of Q² shown (approximately 0–5.5 GeV²).
- The vector isoscalar coupling constants used were g_vect = 8 (compared to g_ω = 12 in earlier DKM work) and κ_vect = 60 (compared to κ_ω = -0.8).
- The slope of G_E/G_M at Q² = 0 is negative, implying ⟨r_E²⟩ > ⟨r_M²⟩/μ_p, consistent with the proton electric polarizability α_E ≈ 12 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ being larger than the magnetic polarizability β_M ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The model fails to describe G_E(Q²) and G_M(Q²) separately; the calculated electric form factor deviates from the data analysis of Arrington (2004).

## Context

This work extends the DKM, previously applied to real Compton scattering and πN scattering (Kondratyuk et al., 2000–2004), to the virtual photon vertex, enabling a unified description of reactions involving off-shell nucleons (Compton scattering) and off-shell photons (form factors). The result contradicts the traditional Rosenbluth extraction (which would give μ_p G_E/G_M ≈ constant) and supports the JLab polarization-transfer measurements (Jones et al., 2000). The calculated off-shell dependence of the vertex (shown for p² values) may be relevant for two-photon exchange contributions to electron-proton scattering, which have not yet included such off-shell effects (Blunden et al., 2003).
