# Hints on the quadrupole deformation of the $Δ$(1232)

> The E2/M1 ratio (EMR) of the $Δ$(1232) is extracted from the world data in pion photoproduction by means of an Effective Lagrangian Approach (ELA).This quantity has been derived within a crossing symmetric, gauge invariant, and chiral symmetric Lagrangian model which also contains a consistent modern treatment of the $Δ$(1232) resonance. The \textit{bare} s-channel $Δ$(1232) contribution is well isolated and Final State Interactions (FSI) are effectively taken into account fulfilling Watson's theorem. The obtained EMR value, EMR$=(-1.30\pm0.52)$%, is in good agreement with the latest lattice QCD calculations [Phys. Rev. Lett. 94, 021601 (2005)] and disagrees with results of current quark model calculations.

# Hints on the quadrupole deformation of the Δ(1232)

## Key claim

The bare E2/M1 ratio (EMR) of the Δ(1232) extracted from world pion photoproduction data using an Effective Lagrangian Approach is (−1.30 ± 0.52)%, which agrees with lattice QCD calculations but disagrees with current quark model predictions; this value suggests a small oblate deformation of the Δ(1232), though the extraction is model-dependent and the bare EMR is not directly measurable.

## Method

The authors employed an Effective Lagrangian Approach (ELA) for pion photoproduction on free nucleons from threshold up to 1 GeV, including Born terms, vector meson exchange (ρ and ω), and all four-star resonances up to 1.7 GeV mass and spin-3/2. The model incorporates chiral symmetry, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and a modern non-pathological treatment of spin-3/2 resonances following Pascalutsa (2001). Final State Interactions (FSI) were included phenomenologically by adding a phase to the electromagnetic multipoles such that Watson's theorem is fulfilled below the two-pion threshold, with the phase determined from the SAID energy-dependent solution.

## Key numbers

- Bare EMR = (−1.30 ± 0.52)% (average of two fits, Table 1)
- Dressed (physical) EMR = (−3.9 ± 1.1)% for photon energy 328 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 343 MeV
- Δ(1232) pole mass: 1215 ± 2 MeV (Set #1) and 1209 ± 2 MeV (Set #2)
- Helicity amplitudes: A1/2Δ = −0.123 ± 0.003 GeV−1/2 (both sets); A3/2Δ = −0.225 ± 0.005 GeV−1/2 (Set #1) and −0.224 ± 0.004 GeV−1/2 (Set #2)
- Electric form factor G_EΔ = −0.076 ± 0.042 (Set #1) and −0.071 ± 0.042 (Set #2)
- Magnetic form factor G_MΔ = 5.650 ± 0.070 (Set #1) and 5.701 ± 0.071 (Set #2)
- Weisskopf ratio estimate: R_W ≈ 0.22 (using nucleon radius R0 = 0.875 fm and mass difference MΔ − MN ≈ 270 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the line of experimental and theoretical efforts to determine the quadrupole deformation of the nucleon through its lowest-lying excitation, the Δ(1232), following earlier measurements by the LEGS Collaboration (Blanpied, 1997) and Mainz group (1990s). The extracted bare EMR agrees with lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou et al. (2005) at Q2 = 0.1 GeV2, which gave (−1.40 ± 0.60)% at mπ = 370 MeV, but disagrees with the positive bare EMR of (3.8 ± 1.6)% obtained by Pascalutsa and Tjon (2004) using a different dynamical model. The result enables comparison between reaction-model extractions and nucleonic structure models, including quark models (Becchi and Morpurgo, 1965; Buchmann, 1990s), the Skyrme model (Wirzba, 1990s), and lattice QCD, and suggests that further improvements in quark models are needed.
