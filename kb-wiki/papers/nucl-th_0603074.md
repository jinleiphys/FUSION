# Proton-neutron multiplets in exotic 134Sb: testing the shell-model effective interaction

> The experimental level structure of 134Sb is compared with the results of a shell-model calculation in which the two-body matrix elements of the effective interaction have been derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The experimental data, including the very low-lying first-excited 1- state, are remarkably well reproduced by the theory. The results of this paper complement those of our previous studies on 135Sb and 134Sn, showing that our effective interaction is well suited to describe 132Sn neighbors beyond N=82. The various terms which contribute to the matrix elements of the proton-neutron effective interaction are examined and their relative importance is evidenced.

## Key claim

The shell-model effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via a low-momentum potential Vlow-k and a Q-box folded-diagram method reproduces the experimental level structure of 134Sb, including the nearly degenerate 0- ground state and 1- first-excited state, without requiring modifications to the shell structure.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for 134Sb assuming 132Sn as a closed core, with valence protons occupying the five orbitals of the 50-82 shell and valence neutrons occupying the six orbitals of the 82-126 shell. The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using a low-momentum potential Vlow-k renormalization and a Q-box folded-diagram method including first- and second-order diagrams, with single-particle energies taken from the experimental spectra of 133Sb and 133Sn. The calculations were carried out using the OXBASH shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy: calculated 12.85 ± 0.05 MeV versus experimental 12.96 ± 0.05 MeV
- First-excited 1- state: calculated at 53 keV excitation energy versus experimental 13 keV
- 7- state discrepancy: calculated lies about 130 keV above its experimental counterpart
- Wave function purity for πg7/2 νf7/2 multiplet: leading component ranges from 94% to 100% for all states except the 2- state (minimum 88%)
- For the πd5/2 νf7/2 multiplet, the 2- state receives up to 50% contribution from configurations other than the dominant one
- Second 3- and 5- states predicted at 1.42 and 1.46 MeV excitation energy, belonging to the πg7/2 νp3/2 configuration

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of 135Sb (Coraggio 2005) and 134Sn (Covello 2005) using the same Hamiltonian, demonstrating that a single consistent realistic effective interaction can describe all three nuclei beyond the 132Sn doubly magic core. The results contradict the conclusion of Shergur (2005) that a consistent Hamiltonian for these nuclei has yet to be found, and improve upon earlier calculations by Shergur (2005) that overestimated the 1- state excitation energy by about 200 keV and gave an average deviation of about 150 keV for the πg7/2 νf7/2 multiplet. The analysis shows that core-polarization effects from 1p-1h excitations (bubble diagrams) play a crucial role in reproducing the small 0- - 1- splitting, and the overall success suggests no need to invoke shell-structure modifications for neutron-rich nuclei beyond 132Sn.
