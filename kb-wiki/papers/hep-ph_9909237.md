# Interference in Exclusive Vector Meson Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Photons emitted from the electromagnetic fields of relativistic heavy ions can fluctuate into quark anti-quark pairs and scatter from a target nucleus, emerging as vector mesons. These coherent interactions are identifiable by final states consisting of the two nuclei and a vector meson with a small transverse momentum. The emitters and targets can switch roles, and the two possibilities are indistinguishable, so interference may occur. Vector mesons are negative parity so the amplitudes have opposite signs. When the meson transverse wavelength is larger than the impact parameter, the interference is large and destructive. The short-lived vector mesons decay before amplitudes from the two sources can overlap, and so cannot interfere directly. However, the decay products are emitted in an entangled state, and the interference depends on observing the complete final state. The non-local wave function is an example of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox.

# Interference in Exclusive Vector Meson Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

In exclusive vector meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, destructive interference occurs between the two indistinguishable production amplitudes (where the two nuclei swap roles as photon emitter and Pomeron target) when the meson transverse wavelength exceeds the impact parameter; however, the short-lived vector mesons decay before their wave functions can overlap, so the interference instead depends on observing the complete entangled final state of the decay products, constituting an example of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox.

## Method

The authors calculate vector meson production cross sections by convolving the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon spectrum with the photonuclear interaction cross section, using a Glauber formalism to determine the cross section for γA → VA. The two-source amplitude is constructed with opposite signs due to the negative parity of vector mesons, and the interference term depends on the product of the meson momentum and the impact parameter. The decay is incorporated by multiplying the production amplitude by the decay amplitude, which is identical for both production directions and therefore does not affect the observed cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive ρ⁰ production was about 10% of the hadronic cross section for gold on gold collisions at √S_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC), rising to 30% for √S_NN = 5.5 TeV lead on lead collisions (LHC)
- J/ψ cross sections of 0.3 mb (gold at RHIC) up to 32 mb (Pb at LHC)
- Hundreds of ρ⁰ per second at RHIC, and hundreds of thousands of ρ⁰ per second with calcium beams at the LHC
- J/ψ rates range from 0.06 Hz with gold at RHIC up to 780 Hz with calcium at LHC
- Median impact parameters for gold beams at RHIC: 46 fm for ρ⁰ and ω, 38 fm for φ, and 23 fm for J/ψ, corresponding to p⊥ < 4 MeV/c for light mesons and p⊥ < 10 MeV/c for J/ψ
- At the LHC, impact parameters are 170-290 fm for light vector mesons and 44-68 fm for J/ψ
- For the light mesons, the soft Pomeron parameter ε = 0.22 gives a phase φ ∼ 10°
- For J/ψ, σ(γN → VN) ∼ W^0.8, inconsistent with the soft Pomeron model
- The cross section oscillates with period Δp⊥ = ħ/b for a given impact parameter b

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier calculations of vector meson production cross sections (Klein and Nystrand, 1999) which summed cross sections from the two nuclei and found very large cross sections. The interference mechanism is analogous to bremsstrahlung by identical particles (Stabler, 1965), where almost complete destructive interference can occur. The paper enables experimental searches for this interference effect at RHIC and LHC using reconstructible decays such as ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻, ω → π⁺π⁻, φ → K⁺K⁻, and J/ψ → e⁺e⁻, and raises open questions about the role of additional interference terms from backgrounds such as γγ → e⁺e⁻ interfering with J/ψ decays.
