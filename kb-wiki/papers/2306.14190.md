# Universal relations for compact stars with heavy baryons

> A set of hadronic equations of state derived from covariant density functional theory and constrained by terrestrial experiments, and astrophysical observations, in particular by the NICER experiment inferences is used to explore the universal relations among the global properties of compact stars containing heavy baryons at high densities. We confirm the validity of universal $I$-Love-$Q$ relations connecting the moment of inertia $(I)$, the tidal deformability ($Λ$), and the spin-induced quadrupole moment ($Q$) for isolated non-rotating stars. We further confirm the validity of the $I$-$C$-$Q$ relations connecting the moment of inertia, compactness $(C)$, and quadrupole moment for uniformly and slowly rotating stars, and extend the validity of these relations to maximally rotating sequences. We then investigate the relations between integral parameters of maximally rotating and static compact stars. The universalities are shown to persist for equations of state and compositions containing hyperons and $Δ$ degrees of freedom. When heavy baryons are included, however, the radial profiles of integrands in expressions of global properties exhibit ``bumps", which are not present in the case of nucleonic stars in which case the profiles are smooth. We determine the coefficients entering the universal relations in the case of hyperonic and $Δ$-resonance containing stars.

# Universal relations for compact stars with heavy baryons

## Key claim

The paper confirms that universal I-Love-Q relations (connecting moment of inertia, tidal deformability, and spin-induced quadrupole moment) and I-C-Q relations (connecting moment of inertia, compactness, and quadrupole moment) remain valid for compact stars containing hyperons and Delta resonances, but only for the specific set of equations of state derived from covariant density functional theory that satisfy constraints from NICER, GW170817, and PREX-II. The radial profiles of integrands in global properties exhibit "bumps" when heavy baryons are included, unlike the smooth profiles seen in purely nucleonic stars.

## Method

The authors used relativistic Hartree and Hartree-Fock approaches within covariant density functional theory to generate equations of state for three composition types: purely nucleonic (N), hyperonic (NY), and hyperon-Delta admixed (NYDelta) matter. They computed global properties of compact stars (mass, radius, moment of inertia, tidal deformability, quadrupole moment) for static, slowly rotating, and rapidly rotating configurations up to the Keplerian limit, then fitted universal relations using polynomial expansions in logarithmic or inverse compactness variables.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for static nucleonic models: 2.49 solar masses (PKO3) and 2.48 solar masses (DDME2), with canonical-mass radii of 13.96 km and 13.22 km respectively
- Static configurations cover mass range 2.0 to 2.5 solar masses and radius range 12 to 14 km for 1.4 solar mass stars
- Maximally rotating stars have masses approximately 20% larger and equatorial radii about 40% larger than static counterparts, covering 2.5 to 3.0 solar masses and 17 to 20 km for 1.4 solar mass stars
- Absolute fractional differences for I-Love-Q relations are less than 1% for all three combinations (I-Love, Q-Love, I-Q)
- Maximum fractional differences for I-C and Q-C relations are close to 7-8% for static and slowly rotating stars
- For rapidly rotating sequences, the Q-I relation holds to 1-2% maximum absolute fractional difference
- Hyperonic models with broken SU(6) symmetry have maximum masses in the range 2.1 to 2.4 solar masses
- Inclusion of Delta resonances reduces star radius by up to 1-2 km at central densities slightly above nuclear saturation density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of universal relations by Yagi (2013, 2017) and others, which were primarily tested on nucleonic equations of state, to include heavy baryonic degrees of freedom (hyperons and Delta resonances). The authors use their own previously developed equations of state from covariant density functional theory (Li 2018, Sedrakian 2020, 2022) that satisfy constraints from NICER, GW170817, and PREX-II. The results enable the use of universal relations for inferring properties of compact stars even when exotic baryonic matter is present in their cores, which is important given robust physical arguments for the onset of heavy baryons at high densities.
