# Mesonic interactions and their contribution to strong phases in flavor physics

> We analyze the contributions of hadronic final-state interactions to the strong phases generated in the B -> Kpipi weak decays. To this end, we develop an alternative approach to the commonly employed isobar model based upon scalar and vector form factors for pion-pion and pion-kaon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper develops an alternative to the isobar model for describing strong phases in B → Kππ weak decays, using scalar and vector form factors for ππ and πK interactions derived from unitary coupled-channel equations, but the QCD factorization amplitudes alone do not reproduce experimental branching fractions unless long-distance "charming penguin" amplitudes are included.

## Method

The authors derive weak decay amplitudes for quasi two-body B → Kππ states following the QCD factorization approach of Beneke and Neubert, where the creation of ππ or πK pairs in S- or P-waves from vacuum is described by scalar or vector form factors. These form factors are obtained from unitary coupled-channel equations constrained by chiral perturbation theory and experimental data on ππ or πK phase shifts and inelasticities via dispersion relations. The decay amplitudes include both QCD factorization contributions and phenomenological long-distance amplitudes with c- or u-quark loops in penguin topologies, parametrized by four complex parameters S_u, S_c, P_u, and P_c.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical branching fractions from QCD factorization without charming penguin amplitudes are too small by a factor spanning from 2.3 to 3.6 compared to experimental values.
- The analysis covers the Kπ mass range below 2 GeV, where the resonances K*(892) and K0*(1430) dominate the vector and scalar form factors, respectively.
- Twelve branching fractions and CP-violating asymmetries for B → K*(892)π and B → K0*(1430)π are available, along with 285 data points for Kπ effective mass and helicity angle distributions.

## Context

This work extends the approach of El-Bennich et al. (2007) on ππ final-state interactions in B decays to include πK interactions, motivated by experimental data from Belle and BaBar collaborations (Abe, Aubert, Garmash, 2005-2006). It contrasts with the commonly employed isobar model by describing S- and P-waves through single unitary waves containing all resonant and non-resonant contributions, and the results agree with calculations by Cheng, Chua, and Soni (2005). The approach enables extraction of branching fractions for B → f0(980)K, B → ρ(770)0K, B → K*(892)π, and B → K0*(1430)π by integrating over appropriate mass ranges, though direct comparison with experimental branching fractions requires extracting the resonant part of the Kπ waves.
