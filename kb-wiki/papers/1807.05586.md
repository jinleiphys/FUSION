# Initial conditions for nuclear collisions: theory overview

> We overview the current status and recent developments on initial conditions in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. Specifically, we look at the progress in understanding the role of sub-nucleonic fluctuations in large and small collision systems. Next, we review the current ideas of going beyond boost invariant initial conditions and introducing physically motivated rapidity fluctuations at RHIC and LHC energies. Finally, we discuss the time evolution of initial stages and the matching between different descriptions of the QGP.

## Key claim

This overview argues that while the transverse-plane geometry of initial conditions in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is well understood and can successfully reproduce flow harmonics, the understanding of rapidity fluctuations and pre-equilibrium dynamics is much less advanced, and the applicability of hydrodynamic behavior to small collision systems (p-Pb, peripheral Pb-Pb) remains an open question.

## Method

The paper reviews three main areas of theoretical progress: (1) sub-nucleonic fluctuations in the transverse plane, studied via models such as IP-Glasma (using impact parameter saturation dipole model fitted to HERA data) and EKRT (using NLO pQCD mini-jet production with saturation condition); (2) rapidity fluctuations, explored through JIMWLK evolution of color-glass-condensate initial conditions and phenomenological string models; and (3) pre-equilibrium dynamics, connecting classical Yang-Mills simulations, QCD effective kinetic theory (with 2-to-2 scatterings and 1-to-2 radiation), and viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* Hydrodynamization time for homogeneous boost-invariant systems: tau_hydro approximately equals 4 pi times (eta/s) divided by T (temperature), derived from QCD kinetic theory evolution.
* In the IP-Glasma model, hydrodynamic evolution is initialized at tau = 0.4 fm/c; in the EKRT model, at tau = 0.2 fm/c.
* For large collision systems, flow observables (integrated flow harmonics v_n) show no significant dependence on small-scale structure with smoothing length lambda less than or equal to 1 fm.
* The ratio of hydrodynamization time to transverse system size: tau_hydro / R approximately equals (4 pi (eta/s) / 2)^(3/2) times (dN_ch/deta / 63)^(-1/2) times (S/N_ch / 7) times (nu_eff / 40)^(1/2).
* In central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta is large enough that hydrodynamization can occur for a range of eta/s values; small systems like p-Pb are unlikely to hydrodynamize by tau approximately equal to R.
* Sub-nucleonic constituents with width w approximately 0.5 fm and distributed in radius r approximately 1 fm are preferred in Bayesian analysis fits to p-Pb and Pb-Pb data.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on initial conditions for heavy ion collisions, covering models such as the Monte-Carlo Glauber model (Miller, 2007), IP-Glasma (Schenke, 2012), and EKRT (Niemi, 2015). It discusses recent progress in constraining sub-nucleonic fluctuations through comparisons with HERA data on incoherent diffractive vector meson production (Mantysaari, 2017) and Bayesian analysis (Moreland, 2018). The paper enables future work on connecting classical field descriptions to hydrodynamic evolution via QCD kinetic theory (Kurkela, 2018), and highlights open questions about the origin of collective signals in small collision systems, where jet quenching has not been observed in p-Pb collisions (Acharya, 2018).
