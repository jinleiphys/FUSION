# Spin Polarization in $γd \to \vec{n}p$ at Low Energies with a Pionless Effective Field Theory

> With the use of pionless effective field theory including dibaryon fields, we study the $γd \to \vec{n} p$ reaction for the laboratory photon energy $E_γ^{lab}$ ranging from threshold to 30 MeV. Our main goal is to calculate the neutron polarization $P_{y'}$ defined as $P_{y'} = (σ_+ - σ_-)/(σ_+ + σ_-)$, where $σ_+$ and $σ_-$ are the differential cross sections for the spin-up and spin-down neutrons, respectively, along the axis perpendicular to the reaction plane. We also calculate the total cross section as well as the differential cross section $σ(θ)$, where $θ$ is the colatitude angle. Although the results for the total and differential cross sections are found to agree reasonably well with the data, the results for $P_{y'}$ show significant discrepancy with the experiment. We comment on this discrepancy.

## Key claim

The authors use pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT) to calculate the neutron spin polarization \(P_{y'}\) in the \(\gamma d \to \vec{n}p\) reaction, and find that while their total and differential cross sections agree reasonably well with data, the calculated \(P_{y'}\) shows significant discrepancy with experiment, particularly when compared to the higher-precision data set of Jewell et al. (1965).

## Method

The authors employ pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT), including two dibaryons in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels, and calculate amplitudes up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The two low-energy constants \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) are fixed from the low-energy \(np \to d\gamma\) cross section and the deuteron magnetic moment, leaving no free parameters. They compute the total cross section, differential cross section \(\sigma(\theta)\), and neutron polarization \(P_{y'}\) for laboratory photon energies from threshold to 30 MeV, and compare with experimental data and with the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) calculations of Schiavilla (2005).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(\gamma d \to np\) calculated from threshold to \(E_\gamma^{lab} = 30\) MeV; data at \(E_\gamma^{lab} \geq 15\) MeV show scatter but overall consistency with the dEFT calculation.
- Differential cross section \(\sigma(\theta)\) at \(E_\gamma^{lab} = 19.8\) MeV shown to be consistent with the data of De Pascale (1985).
- \(P_{y'}\) at \(E_\gamma^{lab} = 2.75\) MeV: the dEFT curve lies below the experimental values of Jewell et al. (1965) for the entire angular range; the authors note that multiplying the calculated \(P_{y'}\) by a factor of about 0.7 would bring agreement with Jewell65.
- \(P_{y'}\) at \(\theta_{lab} = 45^\circ\) as a function of \(E_\gamma^{lab}\): dEFT results completely disagree with the data of Nath et al. (1972); the dEFT curve tapers off around \(E_\gamma^{lab} \approx 10\) MeV, while SNPA values increase almost linearly.
- \(P_{y'}\) at \(\theta_{lab} = 90^\circ\): the average behavior of experimental values agrees with dEFT within large uncertainties; data from Nath et al. (1972), Drooks, and Holt et al. (1983) show pronounced scatter.
- \(P_{y'}\) at \(\theta_{lab} = 135^\circ\): for \(E_\gamma^{lab} \leq 5\) MeV, dEFT and SNPA results are close; for \(E_\gamma^{lab} \geq 8\) MeV, qualitative differences appear, with dEFT showing slow monotonic increase while SNPA results decrease around \(E_\gamma^{lab} \geq 10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory with dibaryons (dEFT) to the \(\gamma d \to \vec{n}p\) reaction, following the formalism of Ando et al. (2005). It directly addresses the long-standing "\(P_{y'}\) puzzle" previously studied by Schiavilla (2005) using the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) with high-precision phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials and meson-exchange currents. The authors find that dEFT and SNPA give significantly different results for \(P_{y'}\), indicating sensitivity of polarization observables to the theoretical framework, and that the discrepancy with experiment persists in both approaches. The work enables future higher-order (N\(^2\)LO) calculations in dEFT and motivates new experimental measurements of \(P_{y'}\) to resolve the puzzle.
