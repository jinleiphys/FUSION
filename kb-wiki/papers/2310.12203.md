# Vanishing Polyakov Loop for QCD with Twelve Massless Quarks

> We consider continuum-formulation QCD in four dimensions with twelve massless fundamental quark flavors. Splitting the SU(\(N\)) gauge field into background and fluctuation parts, we use well-developed techniques to calculate the one-loop effective action for the theory. We find that for constant self-dual background field-strength tensor the notorious infrared divergences of the effective action cancel between gauge and matter sectors if the number of massless quark flavors is exactly . The ultraviolet divergencies of the effective action are non-perturbatively renormalized with a $β$-function that matches the known perturbative result in the high energy limit. The resulting UV- and IR-finite effective action possesses a non-trivial saddle which has lower free energy than the perturbative vacuum, and for which the expectation value of the Polyakov loop vanishes. Inclusion of finite temperature effects points to the presence of a first-order phase transition to the perturbative vacuum with a calculable critical temperature.

# Vanishing Polyakov Loop for QCD with Twelve Massless Quarks

## Key claim

For SU(3) gauge theory with exactly twelve massless fundamental fermions, the one-loop effective action in a constant self-dual background field becomes both ultraviolet- and infrared-finite, and possesses a non-trivial saddle point with lower free energy than the perturbative vacuum where the Polyakov loop expectation value vanishes. The infrared divergence cancellation between gluons and fermions occurs only for the specific choice N_f = 4N, and the calculation relies on truncating the expansion at one-loop order and restricting to constant self-dual background fields, making the semi-classical expansion uncontrolled.

## Method

The authors split the SU(N) gauge field into a background field and fluctuations, then gauge-fix using Weinberg's condition and expand the action to second order in fluctuations (one-loop approximation). They evaluate the resulting functional determinants for ghosts, gluons, and massless fundamental Dirac fermions using the heat-kernel method, restricting to constant self-dual background field-strength configurations to avoid Nielsen-Olesen instabilities. The infrared-divergent terms in the effective action cancel between gauge and matter sectors when N_f = 4N, and the ultraviolet divergences are renormalized non-perturbatively, yielding a beta-function that matches the known perturbative one-loop result. Finite-temperature effects are treated by compactifying the Euclidean time direction and restricting to the even-n thermal subsector where the infrared cancellation persists.

## Key numbers

- The infrared divergence cancels exactly when N_f = 4N; for SU(3) this gives N_f = 12 massless flavors.
- The non-trivial saddle in the abelian subsector along color direction a=8 occurs at sigma_8 = 4.27 Lambda^2_MSbar (with explicit expression sigma_8 = e^{-12 zeta'(-1) - 5/6} 2^{-8/9} 3^{5/6} Lambda^2_MSbar).
- The free energy density of the dominant non-trivial saddle is ln Z / vol = 3 sigma_8^2 / (32 pi^2), giving a bag constant C_bag = -0.173 Lambda^4_MSbar.
- The critical temperature for the first-order phase transition to the perturbative vacuum is T_c = 0.81 Lambda_MSbar.
- The Polyakov loop integrated over spatial volume vanishes identically for the dominant non-perturbative saddle.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical approach to non-perturbative QCD pioneered by Savvidy (1977), Nielsen and Olesen (1978), and Leutwyler (1980), who found infrared instabilities and massless "chromon" excitations in pure Yang-Mills with constant background fields. By adding exactly twelve massless fundamental fermions, the authors cancel the infrared divergences that plagued earlier calculations, obtaining a finite effective action without regulators. The result contrasts with lattice QCD studies by DeGrand (2015) and Di Pietro (2020) and recent combined lattice-perturbative analyses by Chung (2023) which suggest N_f=12 QCD lies near the conformal window boundary. The calculation enables analytic study of confinement and deconfinement in a controlled setting, including a first-order phase transition at calculable T_c and the possibility of partially deconfined phases as discussed by Hanada (2018, 2022, 2023).
