# Symmetry energy and density

> The nuclear equation-of-state is a topic of highest current interest in nuclear structure and reactions as well as in astrophysics. In particular, the equation-of-state of asymmetric matter and the symmetry energy representing the difference between the energy densities of neutron matter and of symmetric nuclear matter are not sufficiently well constrained at present. The density dependence of the symmetry energy is conventionally expressed in the form of the slope parameter L describing the derivative with respect to density of the symmetry energy at saturation. Results deduced from nuclear structure and heavy-ion reaction data are distributed around a mean value L=60 MeV. Recent studies have more thoroughly investigated the density range that a particular observable is predominantly sensitive to. Two thirds of the saturation density is a value typical for the information contained in nuclear-structure data. Higher values exceeding saturation have been shown to be probed with meson production and collective flows at incident energies in the range of up to about 1 GeV/nucleon. From the measurement of the elliptic-flow ratio of neutrons with respect to light charged particles in recent experiments at the GSI laboratory, a new more stringent constraint for the symmetry energy at suprasaturation density has been deduced. It confirms, with a considerably smaller uncertainty, the moderately soft to linear density dependence of the symmetry energy previously deduced from the FOPI-LAND data. Future opportunities offered by FAIR will be discussed.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities is constrained by the elliptic-flow ratio of neutrons to charged particles in heavy-ion collisions, yielding a moderately soft to linear behavior with a power-law coefficient \(\gamma = 0.72 \pm 0.19\) and a slope parameter \(L = 72 \pm 13\) MeV, but these values depend on the chosen parametrization of the symmetry energy's potential part at saturation.

## Method

The authors measured elliptic flows of neutrons and light charged particles in \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon using the Large Area Neutron Detector (LAND) and other detector systems at GSI. They compared the experimental ratio of neutron to charged-particle elliptic flow (\(v_2^n/v_2^{ch}\)) with predictions from the UrQMD transport model using soft (\(\gamma=0.5\)) and stiff (\(\gamma=1.5\)) power-law parametrizations of the symmetry energy's potential part. A linear interpolation between the model predictions yielded the best-fit power-law coefficient. A sensitivity study using the Tübingen QMD model quantified the density range probed by defining the Difference of Elliptic-Flow Ratio (DEFR) for different stiffness choices.

## Key numbers

- Power-law coefficient for the potential part of the symmetry energy from acceptance-integrated elliptic-flow ratio: \(\gamma = 0.72 \pm 0.19\) for \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- Slope parameter at saturation density: \(L = 72 \pm 13\) MeV, derived from the same analysis.
- Power-law coefficient from transverse-momentum-dependent analysis: \(\gamma = 0.75 \pm 0.10\), representing a factor-of-two improvement in statistical accuracy over the earlier FOPI-LAND result.
- Symmetry pressure at saturation: \(p_0 = \rho_0 L/3 = 3.8 \pm 0.7\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a pressure of 3.4 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) for neutron-star matter with asymmetry \(\delta = 0.9\).
- Alternative analysis with \(E_{\rm sym}^{\rm pot}(\rho_0) = 19\) MeV yields \(\gamma = 0.68 \pm 0.19\) and \(L = 63 \pm 11\) MeV.
- Sensitivity maximum for neutron-vs-charged-particle flow ratio occurs near saturation density and extends beyond twice saturation; for neutron-vs-proton flow ratio, the maximum lies in the \(1.4\) to \(1.5 \rho_0\) region.

## Context

This work extends the earlier FOPI-LAND analysis (Russotto 2011) which gave \(\gamma = 0.9 \pm 0.4\), confirming the moderately soft to linear density dependence with considerably smaller uncertainty. The results are compatible with low-density constraints from nuclear-structure studies (Brown 2013; Zhang and Chen 2013) and from heavy-ion collisions (Tsang 2009) and isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2014). The sensitivity study shows that the elliptic-flow ratio probes suprasaturation densities up to about \(2.5 \rho_0\), making it a unique terrestrial probe of the high-density symmetry energy. Future experiments at FAIR with the NeuLAND detector could access the curvature parameter \(K_{\rm sym}\) and extend constraints to twice saturation density.
