# Thermodynamic instabilities in dynamical quark models with complex conjugate mass poles

> We show that the CJT thermodynamic potential of dynamical quark models with a quark propagator represented by complex conjugate mass poles inevitably exhibits thermodynamic instabilities. We find that the minimal coupling of the quark sector to a Polyakov loop potential can strongly suppress but not completely remove such instabilities. This general effect is explicitly demonstrated in the framework of a covariant, chirally symmetric, effective quark model.

# Thermodynamic instabilities in dynamical quark models with complex conjugate mass poles

## Key claim

The Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) thermodynamic potential of dynamical quark models whose quark propagator is represented by complex conjugate mass poles (CCMPs) inevitably exhibits thermodynamic instabilities; coupling the quark sector to a Polyakov loop potential can strongly suppress but not completely remove these instabilities, as demonstrated in a covariant, chirally symmetric, effective quark model.

## Method

The authors derived the kinetic contribution to the thermodynamic potential for a quark propagator with complex conjugate mass poles, showing that the pressure contains oscillatory cosine terms proportional to the imaginary parts of the poles. They then introduced the Polyakov loop via a modification of the quark occupation number function and worked out the color trace to obtain the modified thermodynamic potential. As a concrete example, they studied a separable Dyson-Schwinger model (equivalent to a mean-field non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) with a Gaussian regulator, comparing full numerical solutions to approximations using only the first one or two quartets of poles, both with and without the Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

- The critical gap parameter for the Gaussian regulator model is $b_c = 0.295$ GeV, while the vacuum solution of the gap equation is $b_\mathrm{vac} = 0.678$ GeV (overcritical case)
- Model parameters: $\Lambda_0 = 0.687$ GeV, $m = 0.0096\Lambda_0$, and $D_0 = 128/\Lambda_0^2$
- Pressure oscillations appear at temperatures around 150 MeV, where the mass gap drops below $b_c$ and the lowest quartet splits into two real doublets
- At $\mu/T = 1$ without the Polyakov loop, the pressure becomes significantly negative; with the Polyakov loop, residual negative pressure persists at $\mu/T = 1$ in the full numerical calculation

## Context

This work extends earlier observations of pressure instabilities in separable Dyson-Schwinger models (Blaschke 1999, General 2000, Gomez Dumm 2001, Gomez Dumm 2004, Loewe 2011) by providing an analytic explanation: the instabilities are driven by the cosine terms arising from the imaginary parts of complex conjugate mass poles. The study builds on the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model framework (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005, Roessner 2006) and shows that the same mechanism that suppresses quark degrees of freedom at low temperature in the PNJL model also suppresses the oscillatory instabilities, though not completely. The results enable further work on whether alternative analytic structures (such as entire functions or branch cuts) might avoid these instabilities entirely.
