# Hyperon-Induced Inhomogeneous Pion Condensation and Moat Regimes in Neutron Star Cores

> We perform a stability analysis of the homogeneous ground state of nuclear matter against inhomogeneous perturbations of the pion condensate. In $β$-equilibrium, restricting the baryon species to nucleons only, we observe no instability; however, at high densities, the pseudoscalar density-density correlations assume a moat regime, i.e. a damped oscillatory patterned spatial correlation, which in momentum space appears as a non-zero global minimum for some finite three-momentum. When hyperons are permitted to appear, this minimum can cross down to negative values, which configures an instability towards an inhomogeneous pion condensate which ultimately will affect the equation of state.

## Key claim

In beta-equilibrium nuclear matter containing only nucleons, the homogeneous ground state remains stable against inhomogeneous pion condensate perturbations; however, when hyperons are included, the inverse static pion propagator develops a negative minimum at finite three-momentum for densities above approximately 0.65 fm⁻³, signaling an instability toward an inhomogeneous (crystalline) phase.

## Method

The authors perform a stability analysis of the homogeneous ground state of dense nuclear matter by computing the static (zero-frequency) inverse meson propagators and the static dielectric function within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using the Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) for the baryonic self-energies. They solve for beta-equilibrium and charge-neutral compositions including the full baryon octet (nucleons, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and leptons, then evaluate the momentum dependence of the pion two-point function and the isoscalar static dielectric function as functions of total baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The moat regime (non-monotonic inverse propagator) in the isoscalar sector sets in as early as 0.15 fm⁻³ total baryon density.
- For matter with nucleons only, the inverse pion propagator becomes non-monotonic near n_B ≈ 0.5 fm⁻³ but never crosses zero.
- When hyperons are included, the inverse pion propagator minimum crosses zero at n_B ≈ 0.65 fm⁻³, indicating an instability toward inhomogeneous pion condensation.
- Hyperons first appear at approximately 0.5 fm⁻³, with the Lambda appearing first, followed by the cascade baryons.
- The sigma meson mass is fixed at 600 MeV (with an alternative 700 MeV case also considered); the omega mass is 783 MeV, the rho mass is 770 MeV, and the pion mass is 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends a previous study of symmetric nuclear matter by Motta (2025) which found that inhomogeneous phases are unlikely in that context, and confirms the finding of Papadopoulos (2024, 2025) that inhomogeneous phases are disfavored in dense nuclear matter with charge neutrality and weak equilibrium when only nucleons are present. However, the key new result is that including hyperons reverses this conclusion, producing an instability toward inhomogeneous pion condensation. This opens the possibility that neutron star cores of moderate mass (up to about 1.7 solar masses) may reside in a moat regime, and that the equation of state and observable properties of neutron stars could be affected by the resulting crystalline phase.
