# On electromagnetic off-shell effects in proton-proton bremsstrahlung

> We study the influence of the off-shell structure of the nucleon electromagnetic vertex on proton-proton bremsstrahlung observables. Realistic choices for these off-shell effects are found to have considerable influences on observables such as cross sections and analyzing powers. The rescattering contribution diminishes the effects of off-shell modifications in negative-energy states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that realistic off-shell effects in the nucleon electromagnetic vertex can produce considerable changes in proton-proton bremsstrahlung observables (cross sections and analyzing powers), but the rescattering contribution strongly diminishes these effects for modifications in negative-energy states, particularly at low photon energies.

## Method

The authors used a fully relativistic framework for proton-proton bremsstrahlung, employing a nucleon-nucleon T-matrix calculated in a Blankenbecler-Sugar approximation by Fleischer and Tjon. They introduced an ad hoc parametrization of the off-shell electromagnetic form factors based on a low-energy expansion, with two free parameters (k and kappa^-) that characterize the slope of the positive-energy form factor and the strength of the negative-energy form factors at the on-shell point. Calculations were performed for two parameter sets (F^- and partial F^+) and compared with and without rescattering contributions, using both the full T-matrix and a one-boson-exchange model.

## Key numbers

- Off-shell effects change the cross section up to approximately 15% for the partial F^+ case and approximately 30% for the F^- case.
- Corrections to the analyzing power are of the order of approximately 30% for the partial F^+ case and approximately 25% for the F^- case.
- Varying the parameter d from 0.5 to 1000 changes observables by less than 2%.
- At T_lab = 500 MeV, off-shell effects are larger than at T_lab = 280 MeV by at most a factor of 1.5.
- In the one-boson-exchange model, the ratio Delta_sigma(rho)/Delta_sigma(omega) is approximately 2 for the partial F^+ case, while for the F^- case the omega-meson dominates with Delta_sigma(omega)/Delta_sigma(rho) >= 10.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of competing mechanisms in proton-proton bremsstrahlung, such as meson-exchange currents, isobar components, and relativity (Eden, Martinus, Fearing). It addresses the specific issue of off-shell effects in the electromagnetic nucleon vertex, which had been treated by different approaches including dispersion relations (Nyman 1970, Hare), one-loop models (Naus and Koch 1987, Tiemeijer and Tjon), and Chiral Perturbation Theory (Bos and Koch). The results enable future definitive comparisons with experimental data from TRIUMF by showing that off-shell electromagnetic effects must be controlled before extracting the off-shell nucleon-nucleon T-matrix from bremsstrahlung experiments.
