# Microscopic analysis of the octupole phase transition in Th isotopes

> A shape phase transition between stable octupole deformation and octupole vibrations in Th nuclei is analyzed in a microscopic framework based on nuclear density functional theory. The relativistic functional DD-PC1 is used to calculate axially-symmetric quadrupole-octupole constrained energy surfaces. Observables related to order parameters are computed using an interacting-boson Hamiltonian, with parameters determined by mapping the microscopic energy surfaces to the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the boson condensate. The systematics of constrained energy surfaces and low-energy excitation spectra point to the occurrence of a phase transition between octupole-deformed shapes and shapes characterized by octupole-soft potentials.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully microscopic prediction of a quantum phase transition (QPT) from stable octupole deformation to octupole vibrations in the Th isotopic chain, using a global relativistic energy density functional (DD-PC1) that was not specifically adjusted for octupole degrees of freedom. The isotope \({}^{226}\)Th is identified as closest to the critical point, though the authors note that because the control parameter (neutron number) is discrete, it is not always possible to associate a specific isotope to the critical point.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the DD-PC1 functional to generate axially-symmetric energy surfaces in the \((\beta_2, \beta_3)\) plane for \({}^{222-232}\)Th. They then mapped these microscopic energy surfaces onto the expectation value of an \(sdf\)-interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian in a boson condensate state, determining all Hamiltonian parameters without any adjustment to experimental data. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was numerically diagonalized to compute excitation spectra and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

* The energy minimum for \({}^{224}\)Th is located at \((\beta_2, \beta_3) \approx (0.15, 0.1)\) on the microscopic energy surface.
* The quadrupole parameter \(\chi_d\) approaches the SU(3) limit value \(\chi_d = -\sqrt{7}/2 \approx 1.3\) for \({}^{228}\)Th and heavier isotopes.
* The strength parameters are nearly constant: \(\kappa_2 \approx -0.06\) MeV, \(\kappa_3 \approx -0.015\) MeV, and \(\alpha \approx -0.02\) MeV.
* Experimental \(1^-_1\) bandhead energies are 251 keV in \({}^{224}\)Th, 230 keV in \({}^{226}\)Th, and 328 keV in \({}^{228}\)Th.
* The effective charges used for transition probabilities are \(e_2 = 0.19\) \(e\)b and \(e_3 = 0.19\) \(e\)b\(^{3/2}\).
* The boson number \(N_B\) varies between 6 and 12 for the \({}^{220-232}\)Th nuclei, with \({}^{208}\)Pb as the boson vacuum.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of octupole phase transitions (e.g., Bizzeti (2004), Bonatsos (2005), Lenis (2006), Bizzeti (2008)) that required model parameters adjusted to empirical data, by providing a fully microscopic prediction using a global density functional. The results are in excellent agreement with available experimental data and previous phenomenological studies, and the authors conclude that Th presents the best case for octupole deformations in atomic nuclei. The framework enables future studies of symmetry-dictated descriptions of octupole shape phase transitions.
