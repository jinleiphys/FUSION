# $Nπ$-excited state contamination in nucleon 3-point functions using ChPT

> The $Nπ$-state contribution to nucleon 3-pt functions involving the pseudoscalar density $P(x)$ and the time component $A_4(x)$ of the axial vector current are computed to LO in ChPT. In case of the latter the $Nπ$ contribution is O($M_N$) enhanced compared to the single-nucleon ground state contribution. In addition, a relative sign in two terms of the $Nπ$ contribution leads an almost linear dependence on the operator insertion time, as it is observed in lattice data. In case of the pseudoscalar density the $Nπ$ contribution is strongly dependent on the momentum transfer, leading to a distortion of the pseudoscalar nucleon form factor. Finally, the $Nπ$ state contamination in the form factors result in a violation of the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation as observed in various lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The Nπ-state contribution to the nucleon 3-point function involving the temporal component A₄ of the axial vector current is O(M_N) enhanced compared to the single-nucleon ground state contribution, and a relative sign between two terms in this contribution produces the nearly linear dependence on operator insertion time observed in lattice data.

## Method

The author computed the Nπ-state contribution to nucleon 3-point functions for the pseudoscalar density P(x) and the time component A₄(x) of the axial vector current using leading-order (LO) SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). The calculation used the kinematic setup with final-state nucleon at rest (p'=0) and employed the non-relativistic expansion for the nucleon energy. The ChPT results were compared directly to lattice data from the RQCD and PACS collaborations without fitting, using only phenomenological values for the low-energy coefficients (axial charge, pion decay constant, pion and nucleon masses).

## Key numbers

- The Nπ contribution to the A₄ 3-point function is O(M_N) enhanced compared to the single-nucleon contribution, which is O(M_N⁻¹)
- For the pseudoscalar form factor at source-sink separation t=2 fm and t'=t/2, the mid-point estimate underestimates the true form factor by up to approximately -20% for Q² less than 0.06 GeV², and overestimates by up to approximately +50% for Q² greater than 0.06 GeV²
- The RQCD data for R₄ shown in Figure 1 uses a source-sink separation t=1.06 fm and momentum transfer Q²=0.073 GeV²
- The PACS lattice data for the normalized pseudoscalar form factor uses Q²_ref = 0.072(2) GeV² and source-sink separation t=1.3 fm
- The ChPT calculation for the discrete Q² values assumes spatial volumes satisfying M_πL=6

## Context

This work extends the author's previous ChPT calculations of Nπ-state contamination in nucleon mass, charges, and PDF moments (Bar 2015, Bar 2016, Bar 2016) to the axial vector current's temporal component and the pseudoscalar form factor. It directly addresses the violation of the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation observed in lattice calculations by Bali (2018), Rajan (2017), and Jang (2018). The results enable lattice practitioners to analytically remove the predicted Nπ-state contamination from their data, as demonstrated by correcting PACS collaboration data (Ishikawa 2018) to achieve agreement with the pion-pole-dominance model.
