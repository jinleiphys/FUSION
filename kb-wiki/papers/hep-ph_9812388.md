# Strange form factors of the proton: a new analysis of the neutrino (antineutrino) data of the BNL-734 experiment

> We consider ratios of elastic neutrino(antineutrino)-proton cross sections measured by the Brookhaven BNL-734 experiment and use them to obtain the neutral current (NC) over charged current (CC) neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry. We discuss the sensitivity of these ratios and of the asymmetry to the electric, magnetic and axial strange form factors of the nucleon and to the axial cutoff mass M_A. We show that the effects of the nuclear structure and interactions on the asymmetry and, in general, on ratios of cross sections are negligible. We find some restrictions on the possible values of the parameters characterizing the strange form factors. We show that a precise measurement of the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry would allow the extraction of the axial and vector magnetic strange form factors in a model independent way. The neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry turns out to be almost independent on the electric strange form factor and on the axial cutoff mass.

## Key claim

The BNL-734 neutrino(antineutrino)-proton scattering data are compatible with a wide range of strange form factor parameters, including zero strangeness, and the experimental uncertainties are too large to draw definitive conclusions; the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry is almost independent of the electric strange form factor and the axial cutoff mass, making it a promising observable for future model-independent extractions.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed the BNL-734 experiment's measured ratios of elastic neutrino(antineutrino)-proton cross sections (R_nu, R_nubar, R) and constructed the neutral-current over charged-current neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry. They compared these observables to theoretical calculations that included strange form factors parameterized with dipole forms (axial: F_A^s = g_A^s G_D^A, magnetic: G_M^s = mu_s G_D^V, electric: G_E^s = rho_s tau G_D^V) and varied the axial cutoff mass M_A, while also checking that nuclear structure and final-state interaction effects on the ratios and asymmetry are negligible (less than 2%).

## Key numbers

* The BNL-734 measured ratios: R_nu = 0.153 ± 0.007 (stat) ± 0.017 (syst), R_nubar = 0.218 ± 0.012 (stat) ± 0.023 (syst), R = 0.302 ± 0.019 (stat) ± 0.037 (syst).
* The extracted folded integral asymmetry: ⟨A_p⟩ = 0.136 ± 0.008 (stat) ± 0.019 (syst).
* The maximum relative change in ⟨A_p⟩ from varying strange parameters is ~12% for g_A^s and ~14% for mu_s.
* Varying M_A from 0.996 to 1.068 GeV changes R_nubar by ~18%, R by ~10%, and R_nu by ~5%, but leaves ⟨A_p⟩ practically independent.
* The axial strange constant from deep inelastic scattering is g_A^s = -0.10 ± 0.03 (Ellis 1995) or g_A^s = -0.13 ± 0.03 (Magnon).
* The BATES measurement of the strange magnetic form factor: G_M^s(0.1 GeV^2) = 0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Garvey et al. (1993), who found g_A^s = -0.21 ± 0.10 and M_A = 1.032 ± 0.036 GeV from the same BNL-734 data but with strong correlations between the two parameters. The authors show that the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry, previously proposed by Alberico et al. (1996), is largely insensitive to the electric strange form factor and to M_A, unlike the individual cross-section ratios. This enables a path forward: future precise measurements of the asymmetry could determine the axial and magnetic strange form factors in a model-independent way, complementing parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as the BATES measurement.
