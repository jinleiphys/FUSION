# Effect of Hadronic Rescattering on Elliptic Flow Following Hydrodynamics Model

> The effect of hadronic rescattering on the elliptic flow has been investigated by the Cooper-Frye hadronization model from hydrodynamic evolution following by the afterburner hadronic rescattering model for 200 GeV/c Au + Au at 20-40% centrality. It is found that the hadronic rescattering can suppress elliptic flow $v_2$ in momentum space especially in lower transverse momentum region. In addition, the hadronic rescattering effects on transverse momentum spectra and anisotropy coordinate space of hadrons are studied.

## Key claim
Hadronic rescattering after hydrodynamic hadronization suppresses the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of final-state hadrons in momentum space, especially at low transverse momentum, by at least 20–40% for 200 GeV/c Au+Au collisions at 20–40% centrality. This suppression is a conditional effect that depends on the initial anisotropy of the hadronic phase space and the correlation between coordinate and momentum space at hadronization.

## Method
The authors used a cylindrically symmetric hydrodynamic model with longitudinal scaling flow to evolve the quark-gluon plasma, then applied the Cooper-Frye hadronization prescription at a critical energy density of 0.42 GeV/fm³. An initial anisotropic phase space of hadrons was constructed by hand with a mean momentum-space anisotropy factor \(F = \langle P_x/P_y \rangle = 1.18\) and a corresponding anti-elliptic coordinate-space distribution. The subsequent hadronic rescattering was simulated using the LUCIAE afterburner model, which includes elastic and inelastic reactions (e.g., \(\pi N \rightleftharpoons \Delta \pi\), \(NN \rightleftharpoons \Delta N\), \(\pi\pi \rightleftharpoons k\overline{k}\)) and treats collisions via a minimum-distance criterion.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density = 10.3 GeV/fm³, initial time = 0.6 fm/c for Au+Au at 200 GeV/c, 20–40% centrality.
- Critical energy density for hadronization = 0.42 GeV/fm³.
- Mean momentum-space anisotropy factor \(F = 1.18\) for all produced hadrons.
- At least 20–40% of \(v_2\) is lost due to hadronic rescattering in the hydrodynamics + rescattering model.
- The suppression factor \(s_{v_2}\) (ratio of \(v_2\) after rescattering to before) increases with transverse momentum, indicating weaker suppression at higher \(p_T\).
- The width of the \(\psi\)-\(\phi\) correlation in the hadronization was set to 0.2\(\pi\) (default) and varied to 0.5\(\pi\) in one test.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow (e.g., Kolb et al., Huovinen, Hirano) by explicitly adding a hadronic rescattering stage after Cooper-Frye hadronization, an effect that had not been investigated in detail previously. It contradicts the implicit assumption that the hydrodynamic \(v_2\) directly matches final-state data, showing that rescattering can significantly suppress flow. The results enable more realistic comparisons to RHIC data (e.g., Adler et al., Adams) and motivate inclusion of hadronic afterburners in hybrid models, while also suggesting that partonic effects (e.g., parton cascade as in the Texas multi-phase transport model) may be needed to describe high-\(p_T\) flow.
