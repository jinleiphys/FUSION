# Light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral collisions of heavy ions with future FoCal and ALICE 3 detectors

> I present possible future studies of light-by-light scattering using FoCal@ALICE and ALICE 3 detectors. Different mechanisms are discussed. The PbPb$\to$PbPb$γγ$ cross section is calculated within equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. Several differential distributions are presented and discussed. We predict cross section in the (mb-b) range for typical ALICE 3 cuts, a few orders of magnitude larger than for the current ATLAS or CMS experiments. We also consider the two-$π^0$ background which can, in principle, be eliminated at the new kinematical range for the ALICE 3 measurements by imposing dedicated cuts on diphoton transverse momentum and$\slash$or so-called vector asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that future ALICE 3 measurements of light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions will access cross sections in the millibarn range, several orders of magnitude larger than current ATLAS or CMS experiments, but only if the two-pion background can be eliminated by imposing dedicated cuts on diphoton transverse momentum and vector asymmetry.

## Method

The cross section for PbPb → PbPb γγ is calculated using the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space, with nuclear photon fluxes derived from realistic charge distributions and absorption modeled either by a sharp cut at b > 14 fm or by an exponential survival factor. The elementary γγ → γγ amplitude includes fermionic box diagrams, double hadronic fluctuations within the VDM-Regge approach (including light vector mesons ρ⁰, ω, φ and J/ψ), and π⁰-exchange contributions. The two-π⁰ background from γγ → π⁰π⁰ is simulated by computing the nuclear cross section in EPA and then performing Monte Carlo decays of both pions.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for PbPb → PbPb γγ with ALICE 3 cuts (|y_γ| < 4, E_γ > 50 MeV) is in the millibarn range, "a few orders of magnitude larger than for the current ATLAS or CMS experiments"
* For the FoCal detector (3.4 < η < 5.8) with E_γ > 200 MeV, the diphoton invariant mass distribution shows the π⁰π⁰ background exceeds the signal without additional cuts
* For ALICE 3 with |y_γ| < 4 and E_γ > 50 MeV, at diphoton invariant masses 0.5 GeV < M_γγ < 1 GeV, the π⁰π⁰ background is "almost as big as the signal contribution"
* Imposing cuts on |p_{1t} + p_{2t}| < 0.1 GeV and vector asymmetry A_V < 0.02 reduces the π⁰π⁰ background to "much smaller than the signal" across the full diphoton mass range
* For a planned soft photon detector (3 < y_γ < 5, p_t = 5-50 MeV), the light-by-light signal "by far exceeds" the π⁰π⁰ background at low M_γγ and low p_t without any background suppression
* The VDM-Regge contribution can appear as a ~10% modification of the cross section relative to box-only calculations in combined ALICE and FoCal rapidity coverage (|y_1| < 0.9, y_2 ∈ (3.4, 5.8)) with p_t > 200 MeV and M_γγ > 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Szczurek 2015, 2016) of light-by-light scattering at lower photon-photon energies using ALICE and LHCb, where techniques to reduce the γγ → π⁰π⁰ background were first developed. It also builds on the ATLAS (2021) and CMS (2019) measurements that established the dominance of box diagrams at higher energies. The paper enables future experimental searches for subleading mechanisms (VDM-Regge, two-gluon exchange, meson resonances) that were negligible in ATLAS/CMS kinematics but may become observable with ALICE 3's extended acceptance and lower transverse momentum thresholds.
