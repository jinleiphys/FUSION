# Magnetic contribution to dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate a novel "magnetic contribution" to the dilepton spectrum in heavy-ion collisions arising from interaction of relativistic quarks with intense magnetic field. Synchrotron radiation by quarks, which can be approximated by the equivalent photon flux, is followed by dilepton decay of photons in intense magnetic field. We argue that "magnetic contribution" dominates the dilepton spectrum at low lepton energies, whereas a conventional photon dilepton decay dominates at higher lepton energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a novel "magnetic contribution" to dilepton production, arising from synchrotron radiation by quarks followed by photon decay in the intense magnetic field of heavy-ion collisions, dominates the dilepton spectrum at low lepton energies (below about 0.5 GeV at midrapidity at RHIC), while conventional photon dilepton decay dominates at higher energies. The claim is hedged by the condition that the calculation only includes the square of the amplitude shown in Figure 1 (the process most sensitive to magnetic field) and does not include other magnetic contributions shown in Figure 2, nor the contribution of soft quarks at energies below the plasma temperature.

## Method

The author calculates the dilepton production rate by hard quarks (with energies between the plasma temperature T and the saturation momentum Q_s) using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon approximation, which relates the process q → q ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ to a convolution of the real photon flux from a fast quark with the rate of photon dissociation into a dilepton pair in a magnetic field. The photon dissociation rate in a magnetic field is computed using the formalism of Berestetsky, Lifshitz, and Pitaevskii (1982), involving Airy functions, and is then boosted from a frame where the photon momentum is perpendicular to the magnetic field to the laboratory frame. The initial hard quark distribution is calculated using the quasi-classical approximation for heavy-ion collisions, with the saturation momenta of the two colliding nuclei as key parameters.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at t=0.2 fm after a collision of two Gold nuclei at √s_NN = 200 GeV is eB = 1.3 m_π² (where m_π is the pion mass).
- The total number of leptons produced per event at RHIC due to the magnetic contribution (from the square of the amplitude in Figure 1 only) is N_e = 0.049 when the magnetic field time-dependence includes the QGP medium effect, and N_e = 0.0033 when the magnetic field is modeled as a boosted Coulomb field in vacuum.
- The magnetic contribution to the dilepton yield dominates over the conventional virtual photon splitting at photon energies ω < 200 MeV at midrapidity.
- At k_⊥ = ω ≈ 0.6 GeV, the magnetic contribution still contributes about 20% to the dilepton yield.

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic processes in heavy-ion collisions by addressing the puzzle of enhanced dilepton and photon production at low momentum observed by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare 2008, Adare 2009), which was underestimated by prevailing theories that neglect strong electromagnetic interactions. The calculation builds on prior work on magnetic field dynamics in heavy-ion collisions (Tuchin 2010, Tuchin 2013) and on the quark production mechanism in the saturation regime (Kharzeev 2008, Tuchin 2004). The results enable a more complete theoretical description of dilepton spectra that includes the magnetic field contribution, though the author notes that accurate knowledge of magnetic field dynamics is required to pin down the exact value.
