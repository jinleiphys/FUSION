# Exclusive scalar $f_0(1500)$ meson production for energy ranges available at the GSI Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (GSI-FAIR) and at the Japan Proton Accelerator Research Complex (J-PARC)

> We evaluate differential distributions for exclusive scalar $f_0(1500)$ meson (glueball candidate) production for $p \bar p \to N_1 N_2 f_0$ (FAIR@GSI) and $p p \to p p f_0$ (J-PARC@Tokai). Both QCD diffractive, pion-pion meson exchange current (MEC) components as well as double-diffractive mechanism with intermediate pionic loop are calculated for the first time in the literature. The pion-pion component, which can be reliably calculated, dominates close to the threshold while the diffractive component may take over only for larger energies. At the moment only upper limit for the QCD-diffractive component can be obtained. The diffractive component is calculated based on two-gluon impact factors as well as in the framework of Khoze-Martin-Ryskin approach proposed for diffractive Higgs boson production. Different unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs) from the literature are used. Rather large cross sections due to pion-pion fusion are predicted for PANDA energies, where the gluonic mechanism is shown to be negligible. The production of $f_0(1500)$ close to threshold could limit the so-called $πNN$ form factor in the region of larger pion virtualities. We discuss in detail the two-pion background to the production of the $f_0(1500)$ meson. We compare our result with the existing data of %the WA102 Collaboration.

## Key claim

The pion-pion fusion mechanism, which can be reliably calculated, dominates exclusive scalar \(f_0(1500)\) meson production close to threshold, while the QCD-diffractive component may take over only at larger energies; at present only an upper limit for the QCD-diffractive component can be obtained because the partial decay width \(\Gamma(f_0(1500) \to gg)\) is not well known and is taken as the total width to give an upper estimate.

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross sections for exclusive \(f_0(1500)\) production in \(p \bar p \to N_1 N_2 f_0\) (for FAIR) and \(p p \to p p f_0\) (for J-PARC) using three mechanisms: a QCD-diffractive amplitude based on two-gluon impact factors and the Khoze-Martin-Ryskin (KMR) approach with various unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs); a pion-pion meson exchange current (MEC) component; and a double-diffractive mechanism with an intermediate pionic loop. The two-pion background to \(f_0(1500)\) production was also discussed in detail.

## Key numbers

- The branching fraction for \(f_0(1500) \to \pi\pi\) is \(34.9 \pm 2.3\%\) (from the Particle Data Group).
- The KMR skewed UGDFs use a slope parameter \(b_0 = 2\) GeV\(^{-2}\) for the \(t\)-dependent form factor.
- The reggeon trajectories used are \(\alpha_{I\!\!P}(0) = 1.0808\), \(\alpha_{I\!\!P}' = 0.25\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\alpha_{I\!\!R}(0) = 0.5475\), and \(\alpha_{I\!\!R}' = 0.93\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The strength parameters for \(\pi N\) scattering are \(C_{\pi p}^{I\!\!P} = 13.63\) mb and \(C_{\pi p}^{I\!\!R} = (27.56 + 36.02)/2\) mb.
- The slope parameter for \(\pi N\) scattering is \(B_{\pi N} \approx 6\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The cut-off parameters for the double-diffractive mechanism are \(W_{cut} = 2\) GeV and \(a_{cut} = 0.1\) GeV.
- The off-shell form factor parameter for the triangle is \(\Lambda_{off} = 1\) GeV, and the triangle-vertex form factor parameter is \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Close and Kirk (1999) for central exclusive \(f_0(1500)\) production, which lacked absolute normalization and energy dependence, by providing a QCD-inspired approach with absolute normalization and predictions for FAIR and J-PARC energies. The paper also builds on the Khoze-Martin-Ryskin approach for diffractive Higgs boson production and on prior work by Szczurek, Pasechnik, and Teryaev (2007) on exclusive \(\eta'\) production. The results enable future experimental studies at PANDA (FAIR) and J-PARC to distinguish between gluonic and pion-exchange production mechanisms, potentially constraining the \(\pi NN\) form factor at larger pion virtualities.
