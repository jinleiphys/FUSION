# Mesoscopic Fluctuations of the Pairing Gap

> A description of mesoscopic fluctuations of the pairing gap in finite-sized quantum systems based on periodic orbit theory is presented. The size of the fluctuations are found to depend on quite general properties. We distinguish between systems where corresponding classical motion is regular or chaotic, and describe in detail fluctuations of the BCS gap as a function of the size of the system. The theory is applied to different mesoscopic systems: atomic nuclei, metallic grains, and ultracold fermionic gases. We also present a detailed description of pairing gap variation with particle number for nuclei based on a deformed cavity potential.

# Mesoscopic Fluctuations of the Pairing Gap

## Key claim
The size of mesoscopic fluctuations of the BCS pairing gap depends on whether the corresponding classical single-particle motion is regular or chaotic, and the fluctuations are suppressed when the pairing time exceeds the period of the shortest periodic orbit (i.e., when the dimensionless parameter D = τ_min/τ_Δ is large). The theory applies only in the pairing regime where the average gap exceeds the mean level spacing (Δ̄/δ > 1).

## Method
The authors develop a semiclassical periodic-orbit theory that separates the BCS gap equation into a smooth average part and a fluctuating part. The fluctuating part of the pairing gap is expressed as a sum over classical periodic orbits of the underlying mean-field Hamiltonian, weighted by a modified Bessel function that cuts off contributions from orbits longer than the pairing time τ_Δ = h/(2πΔ̄). The second moment of the fluctuations is then computed using the spectral form factor, which takes different universal forms for integrable (Poisson) and chaotic (GOE) dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Average pairing gap from odd-M three-point mass differences: Δ̄ = 2.7/A^(1/4) MeV
- Average pairing gap from combined even/odd M: Δ̄ = 12/A^(1/2) MeV
- For nuclei, the parameter D = 2R/ξ₀ = 0.22 A^(1/12), ranging from 0.27 to 0.33 for A = 25–250
- For chaotic nano-sized metallic grains, the universal variance is σ²_ch = 1/(2π²)
- For ultracold fermionic gases with k_F|a| = 0.2 and N = 10⁴, regular-system fluctuations reach σ_reg ≈ 0.5 Δ̄/δ

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical theory of shell effects in finite Fermi systems to pairing correlations, building on Berry’s (1985) spectral form factor approach and Monastra’s (2003) treatment of non-universal time scales. It provides analytic predictions that agree with experimental odd-even mass differences in nuclei and with numerical random-matrix calculations of condensation energy fluctuations by Dukelsky (2002) and shell-structure calculations by Garcia-Garcia (2003). The theory enables quantitative predictions for pairing fluctuations in metallic grains and ultracold fermionic gases, where experimental control of interaction strength and confinement allows access to both regular and chaotic regimes.
