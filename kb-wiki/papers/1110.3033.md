# Hydrodynamic elliptic and triangular flow in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76ATeV

> It is shown that a simultaneous comparison of both elliptic and triangular flow from (2+1)-dimensional viscous fluid dynamics with recent measurements in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) favors a small specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP approximately equal to 1/(4pi) for the quark-gluon plasma. Using this viscosity value, the relative magnitude of the elliptic and triangular flow is well described with Monte-Carlo Glauber (MC-Glauber) initial conditions while Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) initial conditions require twice as large viscosity to reproduce the elliptic flow and then underpredict triangular flow by about 30%.

## Key claim

A combined analysis of elliptic and triangular flow from (2+1)-dimensional viscous fluid dynamics with ALICE Pb-Pb data at 2.76 A TeV favors Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions with a specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP approximately equal to 0.08, while Monte-Carlo KLN initial conditions require twice as large viscosity to reproduce elliptic flow and then underpredict triangular flow by about 30%.

## Method

The authors performed single-shot (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with longitudinal boost-invariance, using both Monte-Carlo Glauber and Monte-Carlo KLN fluctuating initial conditions for the entropy and energy density profiles. The hydrodynamic evolution used equation of state s95p-PCE with chemical freeze-out at 165 MeV and an isothermal decoupling surface at 120 MeV, converting to final hadron distributions via the Cooper-Frye prescription with full resonance decays. The MC-Glauber runs used eta/s = 0.08 with a wounded nucleon to binary collision ratio fit to ALICE multiplicity data, while MC-KLN runs used eta/s = 0.2 as previously calibrated at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- MC-KLN initial conditions require eta/s = 0.2 to reproduce elliptic flow, but then underpredict triangular flow by about 30% compared to ALICE data
- MC-Glauber initial conditions with eta/s = 0.08 describe both elliptic and triangular flow well
- The initial ellipticity from MC-KLN is about 20% larger than from MC-Glauber
- For the MC-Glauber model, the wounded nucleon to binary collision mixing parameter x = 0.118 was determined for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem = 165 MeV and decoupling temperature T_dec = 120 MeV were used

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to extract the QGP shear viscosity from elliptic flow data (Teaney 2003, Luzum 2008, Song 2010) which suffered from O(100%) uncertainty due to insufficient knowledge of initial fireball shape. The authors build on the discovery of triangular flow at RHIC (Alver 2010) and LHC (ALICE 2011) and the realization that shear viscosity suppresses higher harmonic flow more strongly than elliptic flow (Alver 2010, Schenke 2011). The combined analysis of v2 and v3 reduces model uncertainty in initial conditions and enables more precise determination of eta/s. The results contradict the possibility of using MC-KLN initial conditions with large viscosity and instead favor MC-Glauber with eta/s near the conjectured lower bound of 1/(4 pi).
