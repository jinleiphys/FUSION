# Theory and Phenomenology of the Polyakov loop in QCD Thermodynamics

> A synthesis of Polyakov loop models of the deconfinement transition and quasiparticle models of gluon plasma thermodynamics leads to a class of models in which gluons move in a non-trivial Polyakov loop background. These models are successful candidates for explaining both critical behavior and the equation of state for the SU(3) gauge theory at finite temperature. Two sensitive lattice probes which can discriminate between models are the Polyakov loop P and e-3p.

## Key claim

A class of models that synthesizes Polyakov loop models of the deconfinement transition with quasiparticle models of gluon plasma thermodynamics can successfully describe both the critical behavior and the equation of state for SU(3) gauge theory at finite temperature, but the ability to fit lattice thermodynamic results is not unique to this model.

## Method

The authors construct a free energy density that combines a phenomenological potential favoring confinement at low temperature, a quasiparticle pressure term for gluons moving in a nontrivial Polyakov loop background, and a temperature-dependent bag term. They extend their earlier "model B" to "model B+M" by including a temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass M(T) = g(T)T/√2, with the coupling g(T) running according to a crossover function that prevents the coupling from diverging at low temperatures. The model parameters are fixed by setting the critical temperature Tc and matching the pressure at Tc to lattice simulation data from Boyd (1996).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: Tc = 0.272 GeV
- Model B+M inverse length scale: R⁻¹ = 0.61 Tc = 0.17 GeV (compared to model B value R⁻¹ = 0.73 Tc = 0.2 GeV)
- Running coupling scale parameter: Λ = 0.3 Tc = 0.081 GeV
- Crossover temperature for coupling: Tcr = 2.4 Tc
- Crossover exponent: n = 10

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model B from Meisinger (2001) by adding quasiparticle mass effects, and synthesizes approaches from quasiparticle models of the quark-gluon plasma (Peshier 1995, Levai 1997, Schneider 2001) with Polyakov loop models of deconfinement (Pisarski 2000, Meisinger 2001). The model B+M reproduces the peak in the interaction measure (ε-3p)/T⁴ just above Tc and the latent heat of the first-order SU(3) deconfinement transition, matching lattice data from Boyd (1996) and Kaczmarek (2002). The approach enables future extensions to include quarks through phenomenological models of chiral symmetry breaking, such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models.
