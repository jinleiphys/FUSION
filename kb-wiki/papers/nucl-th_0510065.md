# Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations for nuclei with tetrahedral deformation

> Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov solutions corresponding to the tetrahedral deformation are found in six tetrahedrally doubly-magic nuclei. Values of the beta32 deformation, depths of the tetrahedral minima, and their energies relative to the co-existing quadrupole minima are determined for several versions of the Skyrme force. Reduction of the tetrahedral deformation energies by pairing correlations is quantitatively analysed. In light nuclei, shallow tetrahedral minima are found to be the lowest in energy, while in heavy nuclei, the minima are deeper but appear at a few MeV of excitation.

## Key claim

Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations find tetrahedral minima in six tetrahedrally doubly-magic nuclei, but the energy and stability of these minima depend strongly on the choice of Skyrme force and on whether pairing correlations are included; in light nuclei the minima can be the lowest in energy, while in heavy nuclei they appear at a few MeV of excitation.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations using four Skyrme force parameterizations (SLy4, SkM*, SkP, SIII) in the particle-hole channel, with a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing. The quasi-particle wave functions were expanded in a harmonic-oscillator basis of 14 spherical shells for Zr isotopes and 16 shells for heavier nuclei. Reflection symmetries in two or three perpendicular planes were imposed to search for tetrahedral and quadrupole solutions, respectively, and HF calculations without pairing were also performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Tetrahedral deformation \(\beta_{32}\) values range from 0.08 to 0.26 across all studied nuclei and forces.
- In \(^{80}\)Zr, the energy difference between tetrahedral and lowest quadrupole minima (\(\Delta E_{hq}\)) ranges from -3 MeV to +3 MeV depending on the Skyrme force (HFB results).
- In \(^{126}\)Ba, \(\Delta E_{hq}\) is not smaller than 7 MeV (HFB results).
- In \(^{160}\)Yb and \(^{226}\)Th, the minimum \(\Delta E_{hq}\) is about 3 MeV (HFB results).
- The depth of the tetrahedral minimum relative to the spherical point (\(\Delta E_{sh}\)) in \(^{110}\)Zr varies from about 0.4 MeV (SLy4) to 2 MeV (SIII) in HFB calculations.
- Pairing reduces \(\Delta E_{sh}\) by as much as from 3 to 1 MeV in \(^{110}\)Zr with SkM*.
- In \(^{80}\)Zr with SIII, pairing vanishes at the tetrahedral minimum and remains non-zero at the spherical point.
- Admixtures of \(\beta_{40}\) deformation range from about 0.01 to 0.07.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tetrahedral deformation that used deformed Woods-Saxon potentials (Li and Dudek, 1994) and Strutinsky shell-correction calculations (Dudek, 2002; Schunck, 2004) by providing the first systematic self-consistent HFB survey across multiple mass regions. The results show that predictions depend significantly on the Skyrme force parameterization, and that pairing correlations reduce the depth of tetrahedral minima, sometimes suppressing them entirely. The findings enable future experimental searches for tetrahedral states in specific nuclei such as Zr isotopes, where some forces predict tetrahedral ground states, and in heavier nuclei like \(^{160}\)Yb and \(^{226}\)Th where the minima are deeper but excited.
