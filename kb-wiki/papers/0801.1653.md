# Nuclear constraints on the momenta of inertia of neutron stars

> Properties and structure of neutron stars are determined by the equation of state (EOS) of neutron-rich stellar matter. While the collective flow and particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions have constrained tightly the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter up to about five times the normal nuclear matter density, the more recent experimental data on isospin-diffusion and isoscaling in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies have constrained considerably the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. Although there are still many uncertainties and challenges to pin down completely the EOS of neutron-rich nuclear matter, the heavy-ion reaction experiments in terrestrial laboratories have limited the EOS of neutron-rich nuclear matter in a range much narrower than that spanned by various EOSs currently used in astrophysical studies in the literature. These nuclear physics constraints could thus provide more reliable information about properties of neutron stars. Within well established formalisms using the nuclear constrained EOSs we study the momenta of inertia of neutron stars. We put the special emphasis on the component A of the extremely relativistic double neutron star system PSR J0737-3039. Its moment of inertia is found to be between 1.30 and 1.63 $(\times10^{45}g$ $cm^2)$. Moreover, the transition density at the crust-core boundary is shown to be in the narrow range of $ρ_t=[0.091-0.093](fm^{-3})$.

## Key claim

The moment of inertia of the A component of the double neutron star system PSR J0737-3039 is constrained to a range between 1.30 and 1.63 (×10^45 g cm^2) using equations of state that are consistent with terrestrial heavy-ion reaction data, specifically those with the symmetry energy parameter x between -1 and 0.

## Method

The authors used the RNS code to solve the hydrostatic and Einstein field equations for rigidly rotating neutron stars, employing four nucleonic equations of state: two versions of the momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) with x=0 and x=-1, the APR equation of state from variational calculations, and the DBHF+Bonn B equation of state from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The MDI equations of state were constrained by analyzing isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions within the IBUU04 transport model, which limited the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy to the range between x=0 and x=-1. For the slowly rotating pulsar A (ν=44.05 Hz), the moment of inertia was computed both exactly and using the empirical slow-rotation approximation of Lattimer (2005).

## Key numbers

- Moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A (M=1.338 M_sun, ν=44.05 Hz): I = 1.30 × 10^45 g cm^2 for MDI(x=0) and I = 1.63 × 10^45 g cm^2 for MDI(x=-1)
- Transition density at the crust-core boundary: ρ_t = [0.091-0.093] fm^{-3}
- For a canonical 1.4 M_sun neutron star, the difference in moment of inertia between MDI(x=0) and MDI(x=-1) is more than 30%
- The empirical relation of Lattimer (2005) approximates the moment of inertia with average uncertainty of about 2% for most EOSs, and about 8% for DBHF+Bonn B

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star properties using nuclear-constrained equations of state, following the approach of Li (2005) and Krastev, Li, and Worley (2007) who applied the MDI EOS to mass-radius correlations and gravitational constant variations. The results are consistent with the slow-rotation calculations of Morrison (2004) and Bhattacharyya and Bhattacharya (2005) for the APR EOS, and with the isoscaling analyses of Shetty (2007) that favor the x=0 symmetry energy. The constrained moment of inertia range for PSR J0737-3039A provides a target for future measurements via periastron advance, which Lattimer (2005) estimated could achieve 10% accuracy and would discriminate among competing equations of state.
