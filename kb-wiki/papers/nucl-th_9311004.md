# E2 properties of nuclei far from stability and the proton-halo problem of 8B

> E2 properties of A=6--10 nuclei, including those of nuclei far from stability, are studied by a $(0+2)\hbarω$ shell-model calculation which includes E2 core-polarization effects explicitly. The quadrupole moments and the E2 transition strengths in A=6--10 nuclei are described quite well by the present calculation. This result indicates that the relatively large value of the quadrupole moment of $^8$B can be understood without introducing the proton-halo in $^8$B. An interesting effect of the $2\hbarω$ core-polarization is found for effective charges used in the $0\hbarω$ shell model; although isoscalar effective-charges are almost constant as a function of nucleus, appreciable variations are needed for isovector effective-charges which play important roles in nuclei with high isospin-values.

## Key claim

The relatively large quadrupole moment of ⁸B can be understood within a (0+2)ħω shell-model calculation without introducing a proton-halo, contrary to earlier claims by Minamisono et al. (1992) and Kitagawa and Sagawa (1992); the authors hedge that a small 8% difference in Q(⁸B) might originate from some halo-like structure, but with a much reduced amplitude compared to ¹¹Li or ¹¹Be.

## Method

The authors performed a (0+2)ħω shell-model calculation for A=6–10 nuclei using the interaction of Wolters et al. (1990), which includes E2 core-polarization effects explicitly via 2ħω excitations, with harmonic-oscillator basis and bare charges. They computed quadrupole moments and B(E2) transition strengths, introducing a small isoscalar effective-charge correction δe_IS = 0.05e to improve agreement, and compared results to experimental data and to 0ħω calculations with the Cohen-Kurath (8–16)TBME interaction.

## Key numbers

- Q(⁸B) is reproduced within approximately 8% accuracy using δe_IS = 0.05e (i.e., e_π^eff = 1.05e, e_ν^eff = 0.05e) in the (0+2)ħω calculation
- The proton separation energy S_p of ⁸B is approximately 0.14 MeV
- For the 0ħω calculation with b = 1.77 fm (derived from matter radii of ⁸B and ⁸Li from Tanihata (1992)), the effective charges extracted from mirror-nuclei data are e_π^eff = 2.1e and e_ν^eff = 0.3e, giving e_IS^eff = 1.2e
- The R_T=0 values (ratio of (0+2)ħω to 0ħω matrix elements) are mostly in the 0.9–1.2 range for A=6–10, corresponding to e_IS^eff = 0.45e–0.6e for the 0ħω space
- The R_T=1 values fluctuate from 0.9–1.5 for A=7 and 8 to 0.0–0.5 for A=9 and 10
- The sum of correlation energies E(⁷Be; g.s.) + ε_p(0p_3/2) – E(⁸B; g.s.) is approximately 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of E2 properties to unstable nuclei far from stability, directly addressing the proton-halo hypothesis for ⁸B proposed by Minamisono et al. (1992) and Kitagawa and Sagawa (1992) based on quadrupole moment measurements. The results contradict the proton-halo interpretation and are consistent with interaction-radius measurements by Tanihata (1992) showing no enhancement for ⁸B. The study enables a more reliable treatment of E2 properties in light nuclei by reducing ambiguities from effective charges, and reveals that isovector effective charges show significant nucleus-to-nucleus variation in the A=6–10 region, unlike the nearly constant isoscalar effective charges.
