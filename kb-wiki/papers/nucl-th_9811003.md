# Protoninduced Pion-, Eta- and Kaon-Production at Threshold

> We present some details of our recent theoretical investigations on the total cross section of the reactions $pp\to pp π^0$, $pp\to pp η$ and $pp\to pΛK^+$ at threshold. Applying standard nonrelativistic perturbation theory within the framework of meson exchange models (MEC) to all of the investigated processes we observe that theory is still far--off from a quantitative understanding of the existing data, although the qualitative behaviour of the threshold cross sections can be reproduced. The often quoted $η^2$--dependence of the $pp\to pp π^0$ cross section at threshold is due to a calculational mistake of Koltun and Reitan (1966) and not based on theoretical grounds, where threebody phasespace behaviour would yield $η^4$, which can be affected by final state and short range effects. To respect the high momentum transfers for $pp\to pp η$ and $pp\to pΛK^+$ relativistic models were developed. Hereby the $η$--production still is based on MEC, while the $K^+$ production was investigated in the quark-gluon-picture based on the Bethe--Salpeter formalism.

## Key claim

The authors find that standard nonrelativistic meson exchange models can reproduce the qualitative behavior of threshold cross sections for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\), \(pp\to pp\eta\), and \(pp\to p\Lambda K^+\), but theory remains far from a quantitative understanding of existing data; the often-cited \(\eta^2\) dependence of the \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) threshold cross section is attributed to a calculational mistake by Koltun and Reitan (1966), with the correct three-body phase-space behavior yielding \(\eta^4\).

## Method

The authors applied standard nonrelativistic perturbation theory within meson exchange models (MEC) to calculate total cross sections for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\), \(pp\to pp\eta\), and \(pp\to p\Lambda K^+\) at threshold. For \(pp\to pp\eta\) and \(pp\to p\Lambda K^+\), which involve high momentum transfers, relativistic models were developed: \(\eta\) production remained within MEC, while \(K^+\) production was treated using a quark-gluon picture based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, with final-state interactions multiplicatively separated following Watson and Migdal.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic meson exchange model for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) describes the total cross section qualitatively up to \(\eta_\pi \approx 0.3\).
- The relativistic model cross section for \(pp\to pp\eta\) is expressed analytically in terms of meson-exchange couplings and the \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonance parameters, with explicit dependence on nucleon mass \(m_N\), pion mass \(m_\pi\), and eta mass \(m_\eta\).
- The cross section for \(pp\to p\Lambda K^+\) is given by a phase-space integral over the squared T-matrix, evaluated using quark-gluon Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes with running coupling \(\alpha_s(Q^2) \simeq 4\pi (\beta_0)^{-1} / \ln(1+Q^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier meson exchange calculations for pion production near threshold, notably correcting the energy-dependence claim of Koltun and Reitan (1966) for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\). It applies the same framework to \(\eta\) production via the \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonance and introduces a novel quark-gluon Bethe-Salpeter approach for kaon production. The authors note that a similar quark-gluon treatment for \(pp\to pp\phi\) and a nucleon-meson treatment for \(pp\to d\pi^+\) at threshold are in progress, indicating that this work provides a foundation for unified descriptions of meson production in proton-proton collisions.
