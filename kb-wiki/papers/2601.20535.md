# De-excitation effects on entanglement in multi-nucleon transfer reactions

> This study quantifies the impact of nuclear de-excitation on correlations in multi-nucleon transfer (MNT) reactions. To bridge the gap between initial collision dynamics and final experimental observables, we introduce a hybrid TDCDFT+GEMINI approach, integrating time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) with the statistical de-excitation model GEMINI++. Applied to the $^{40}$Ca + $^{208}$Pb reaction, our method demonstrates that the de-excitation is essential for reconciling theoretical cross sections with experimental data. Analysis of the cross-section Shannon entropy reveals that new reaction channels open abruptly at a specific energy threshold. By employing mutual information, we show that the de-excitation process significantly degrades the initial quantum entanglement between the projectile-like and the target-like fragments, revealing a key mechanism through which fundamental quantum correlations are lost.

## Key claim

The de-excitation process in multi-nucleon transfer reactions significantly degrades the initial quantum entanglement between projectile-like and target-like fragments, with the loss of mutual information being most pronounced at small impact parameters and substantially larger for neutrons than for protons, indicating neutron evaporation as the dominant de-excitation mechanism.

## Method

The authors developed a hybrid TDCDFT+GEMINI approach that combines time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) for the collision dynamics with the statistical de-excitation model GEMINI++ for the subsequent decay of primary fragments. The method applies particle number projection to extract cross sections for specific reaction channels, computes fragment excitation energies and angular momenta from the projected wave functions, and then simulates the de-excitation cascade using Monte Carlo trials in GEMINI++. The correlation between projectile-like and target-like fragments is quantified using mutual information computed from the joint probability distributions of neutron and proton numbers.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied is 40Ca + 208Pb at laboratory energies from 235 to 270 MeV, with a benchmark calculation at Elab = 249 MeV
- The maximum fusion impact parameter increases from b = 3.9 fm at 235 MeV to b = 5.1 fm at 270 MeV
- The particle number projection uses a spherical division region with radius RV = 12 fm and a uniform mesh of M = 200 points
- Only reaction products with probability P(N,Z) greater than 10^-5 are included in the analysis
- Ntrial = 1000 Monte Carlo trials were performed for each primary fragment in the GEMINI++ simulation
- The maximum impact parameter for fusion at Elab = 249 MeV is b = 4.63 fm, with nucleon transfer becoming negligible for b greater than or equal to 10 fm
- The post-de-excitation mutual information for neutrons peaks at an intermediate impact parameter of b = 6 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Li et al. (2024) and Zhang et al. (2025) that analyzed entanglement and spin correlations between primary fragments in multi-nucleon transfer reactions using TDCDFT, but did not account for de-excitation effects. The hybrid TDCDFT+GEMINI approach bridges the gap between initial collision dynamics and final experimental observables, reconciling theoretical cross sections with experimental data from Szilner et al. (2005) for the 40Ca + 208Pb reaction. The results are compared with prior TDHF+GEMINI calculations by Sekizawa (2017), and the persistent discrepancies in multi-nucleon stripping channels indicate fundamental limitations of mean-field descriptions. The method enables quantitative assessment of how much information about target-like fragments can be inferred from measurements of projectile-like fragments in experiments.
