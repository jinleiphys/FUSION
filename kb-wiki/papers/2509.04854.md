# Dissipative contributions to spin polarization

> Utilizing the Zubarev non-equilibrium density operator method and a first-order gradient expansion, we provide a comprehensive classification of all possible spin polarization effects. A key finding is that, with the unique exception of those induced by the gradient of the spin potential, all first-order dissipative corrections are chiral, implying their dependence on parity-breaking interactions or chiral imbalance. The work also introduces new non-dissipative phenomena akin to the Spin Hall Effect, including the Chiral Spin Hall Effect, which offers a novel observable to probe QCD topological configurations.

## Key claim

All first-order dissipative corrections to spin polarization are chiral (requiring parity-breaking interactions or chiral imbalance) with the sole exception of those induced by gradients of the spin potential; the paper also introduces new non-dissipative phenomena including the Chiral Spin Hall Effect as a probe of QCD topological configurations.

## Method

The author employs the Zubarev non-equilibrium density operator method with a first-order gradient expansion to derive all possible contributions to the axial part of the Wigner function for fermions in a relativistic fluid. Linear response theory is used to obtain momentum-dependent Kubo formulas that define the thermal and transport coefficients for each induced effect. The contributions are classified by their transformation properties under parity (P), time-reversal (T), and charge conjugation (C) to distinguish non-dissipative from dissipative and non-chiral from chiral components.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization $S^\mu(k)$ of fermions with momentum $k$ is defined as $S^\mu(k) = \frac{1}{2} \frac{\int_\Sigma d\Sigma\cdot k\; \mathcal{A}_+^\mu(x,k)}{\int_\Sigma d\Sigma\cdot k\; \mathcal{F}_+(x,k)}$, where $\mathcal{A}_+^\mu$ is the axial part and $\mathcal{F}_+$ the scalar part of the particle Wigner function
- The non-dissipative thermal vorticity contribution to the axial Wigner function is given by $\Delta_{LTE,\varpi} \mathcal{A}_+^\mu = -a_{\varpi} \frac{2\tilde\varpi^{\mu\nu}k_\nu}{(2\pi)^3} - (a_{wu} - a_{w\Delta}) \frac{(k\cdot w)u^\mu}{(k\cdot u)} + (a_{\alpha\epsilon} - a_{w\Delta}) \frac{\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} k_\perp^\nu u_\sigma \alpha_\rho}{(k\cdot u)} + a_{wk} Q^{\mu\rho} w_\rho$, where in free fields the degeneracies $a_{wu} = a_{w\Delta}$, $a_{\alpha\epsilon} = a_{w\Delta}$, and $a_{wk}=0$ hold
- The Spin Hall Effect contribution is $\Delta_{SHE} \mathcal{A}_+^\mu(x,k) = \epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \frac{k_\perp^\nu u_\sigma}{(k\cdot u)} [ a_{r\epsilon}^c(k) \partial_\rho \zeta + \mathfrak{a}_{r_A\epsilon}(k) \partial_\rho \zeta_A ]$, where $a_{r\epsilon}^c$ is the non-chiral Spin Hall Effect and $\mathfrak{a}_{r_A\epsilon}$ is the new Chiral Spin Hall Effect
- The vector part of the Wigner function receives contributions $\Delta_{SHE} \mathcal{V}_+^\mu(x,k) = \epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \frac{k_\perp^\nu u_\sigma}{(k\cdot u)} [ \mathfrak{v}_{r\epsilon}(k) \partial_\rho \zeta + v_{r_A\epsilon}^c(k) \partial_\rho \zeta_A ]$, where $\mathfrak{v}_{r\epsilon}$ is the Chiral Electrical Effect and $v_{r_A\epsilon}^c$ is the Axial Hall Effect

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin hydrodynamics developed by Florkowski (2017) and Weickgenannt (2019), and addresses discrepancies between experimental measurements of local spin polarization by STAR (2019) and ALICE (2021) and predictions based on local thermal equilibrium from Becattini (2015). The comprehensive classification of dissipative contributions provides a foundation for computing transport coefficients and testing whether spin degrees of freedom in the quark-gluon plasma are fully equilibrated, as questioned by Hongo (2022) and Wagner (2024). The Chiral Spin Hall Effect offers a new observable to probe QCD topological configurations through sphaleron transitions, particularly relevant for p-Pb collisions where dissipative effects are expected to be more prominent.
