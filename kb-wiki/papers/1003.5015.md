# An introduction to the theory of rotating relativistic stars

> These lecture notes are intended to introduce the theory of rotating stars in general relativity. The focus is put on the theoretical foundations, with a detailed discussion of the spacetime symmetries, the choice of coordinates and the derivation of the equations of structure from the Einstein equation. The global properties of rotating stars (mass, angular momentum, redshifts, orbits, etc.) are also introduced.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide a self-contained theoretical introduction to the theory of rotating stars in general relativity, focusing on perfect fluid stars in equilibrium with stationary and axisymmetric spacetimes, but explicitly exclude discussion of neutron star applications except for illustrative purposes, and do not cover stability, superfluidity, dissipation, or magnetized stars.

## Method

The author derives the system of elliptic partial differential equations governing the structure of rotating relativistic stars in quasi-isotropic (QI) coordinates, starting from the 3+1 formalism of general relativity and assuming stationarity, axisymmetry, and circularity. The four metric functions (N, A, B, ω) are solved from four coupled elliptic equations derived from the Einstein equation, with boundary conditions imposed by asymptotic flatness. The fluid motion is treated via the relativistic Euler equation at zero temperature, using the baryon chemical potential and enthalpy.

## Key numbers

- The metric outside a spherically symmetric star in isotropic coordinates takes the Schwarzschild form: \( g_{\alpha\beta} dx^\alpha dx^\beta = -[(1 - M/(2r))/(1 + M/(2r))]^2 dt^2 + (1 + M/(2r))^4 [dr^2 + r^2(d\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta d\phi^2)] \)
- The asymptotic behavior of the lapse function is \( N = 1 + K_0/r + O(1/r^2) \) with \( K_0 = -M \), where M is the gravitational mass of the star
- The asymptotic behavior of the frame-dragging potential is \( \omega = K_1/r^3 + O(1/r^4) \) with \( K_1 = 2J \), where J is the angular momentum of the star
- The relation between isotropic coordinate r and areal radius outside the star is \( \mathcal{r} = r(1 + M/(2r))^2 \)

## Context

These lecture notes extend the foundational work on rotating relativistic stars by Weyl (1917), Lense and Thirring (1918), and Lanczos (1924), and present the full system of four elliptic equations first written by Bonazzola, Gourgoulhon, Salgado and Marck (1993). The notes can be complemented by the review of Stergioulas (2003) and the textbook by Friedman and Stergioulas (2010), and enable the computation of rotating neutron star models using the method described, with the textbook by Haensel, Potekhin and Yakovlev (2007) providing a good introduction to such models.
