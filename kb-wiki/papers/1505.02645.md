# Local Gauge Transformation for the Quark Propagator in an SU(N) Gauge Theory

> In an SU(N) gauge field theory, the n-point Green functions, namely, propagators and vertices, transform under the simultaneous local gauge variations of the gluon vector potential and the quark matter field in such a manner that the physical observables remain invariant. In this article, we derive this intrinsically non perturbative transformation law for the quark propagator within the system of covariant gauges. We carry out its explicit perturbative expansion till O(g_s^6) and, for some terms, till O(g_s^8). We study the implications of this transformation for the quark-anti-quark condensate, multiplicative renormalizability of the massless quark propagator, as well as its relation with the quark-gluon vertex at the one-loop order. Setting the color factors C_F=1 and C_A=0, Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation for the abelian case of quantum electrodynamics is trivially recovered.

## Key claim

The authors derive a non-perturbative local gauge transformation law for the quark propagator in an SU(N) gauge theory within covariant gauges, but the full closed-form expression is only obtained for the abelian-like series in the color factor C_F; the series involving the adjoint color factor C_A remains an open problem and is only given as a perturbative expansion up to O(g_s^6) and partially to O(g_s^8).

## Method

The authors start from the definition of the quark propagator as a vacuum expectation value of time-ordered quark fields and apply a local SU(N) gauge transformation to the quark fields, introducing a free scalar field operator carrying color. They expand the resulting exponentials in powers of the strong coupling g_s, use Wick contractions to evaluate the vacuum expectation values of the scalar fields, and employ color algebra identities to simplify the products of SU(N) generators. The calculation is carried out explicitly to O(g_s^4) and O(g_s^6), with some terms verified to O(g_s^8), and the resulting expression is then used to study the gauge invariance of the chiral quark condensate and the multiplicative renormalizability of the massless quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* The perturbative expansion of the quark propagator transformation is carried out to O(g_s^6) and, for some terms, to O(g_s^8).
* At O(g_s^4), the non-abelian correction term is proportional to g_s^4 * C_A * C_F / 24, multiplied by a combination of scalar field propagators.
* The one-loop calculation of the transverse quark-gluon vertex in the limit k^2 >> p^2 yields an abelian contribution proportional to (C_F - 1/2 C_A) * alpha_s * xi / (8 pi k^2) and a non-abelian contribution proportional to C_A * alpha_s * (3 xi - 1) / (64 pi k^2).
* For QED, setting C_F=1 and C_A=0 recovers the standard Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformations (LKFT), originally derived for QED by Landau and Khalatnikov (1956) and later by Johnson and Zumino (1959), to the non-abelian case of SU(N) gauge theories. The derived transformation complements the Slavnov-Taylor identities (Slavnov and Taylor, 1971) which relate different Green functions in QCD, whereas LKFT govern the behavior of a single Green function under variation of the covariant gauge parameter. The results enable future studies of gauge invariance in non-perturbative QCD, particularly for the quark propagator, the chiral quark condensate, and the construction of quark-gluon vertex ansatze that ensure multiplicative renormalizability, following the approach of Curtis and Pennington (1993) and Bloch (2002) in QED and QCD respectively.
