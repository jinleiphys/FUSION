# Polarized Neutron Matter: A Lowest Order Constrained Variational Approach

> In this paper, we calculate some of the polarized neutron matter properties, using the lowest order constrained variational method with the $AV_{18}$ potential and employing a microscopic point of view. A comparison is also made between our results and those of other many-body techniques.

## Key claim
Using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 potential, the authors find no phase transition to a ferromagnetic state in neutron matter, contradicting some Skyrme-interaction-based predictions but agreeing with recent Monte Carlo and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations that exclude such a transition up to at least five times nuclear saturation density.

## Method
The authors applied the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to polarized neutron matter, using the AV18 two-body potential and a Jastrow-type trial wave function with spin-dependent correlation functions. They minimized the two-body cluster energy subject to a normalization constraint, computed the energy per particle as a function of density and spin polarization parameter δ, and derived the magnetic susceptibility, equation of state, sound velocity, and Landau parameter G0 from the energy functional.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ (used as reference density throughout)
- The ratio χ/χF (magnetic susceptibility relative to free Fermi gas) was computed and shown to vary continuously with density, with no indication of ferromagnetic transition
- The Landau parameter G0 was found to be always positive and monotonically increasing with density, never reaching the instability condition G0 < -1
- The velocity of sound vs was always less than the speed of light c for all densities and polarizations considered, satisfying causality

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter (Bordbar, 2005-2007) and spin-polarized liquid ³He (Bordbar, 2005) to polarized neutron matter. The results contradict Skyrme-interaction-based predictions of ferromagnetic transition at densities (1-4)ρ0 (as cited from prior work), and instead agree with recent Monte Carlo calculations (Fantoni, 2004) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (ZLS, 2006) using modern realistic interactions that exclude ferromagnetism up to at least 5ρ0. The calculations enable further studies of neutron star magnetic field generation and neutrino transport in protoneutron stars.
