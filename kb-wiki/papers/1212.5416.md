# Colour-octet bound states, induced by Higgs mechanism

> The current limits for fourth generation quarks allows to expect their mass of the order of 500 GeV. In this mass region for quark-anti-quark pair the additional Yukawa-type attraction due to Higgs mechanism is expected to emerge. This Higgs induced attraction greatly exceeds strong interaction between quarks and leads to the formation of bound states in both colour octet $S^{(8)}$ and singlet $S^{(1)}$ states. In the key of recent works on significance of colour octet channel for production of colour singlet state of fourth generation $Q\bar{Q}$ we calculated the binding energies for both octet and singlet states. Such attraction localizes quarks in extremely small area. Hence colour octet pair of fourth generation quarks can form the "nucleus" and together with colour neutralizing light particle that is captured by strong interaction in orbit around the nucleus, create particle, similar by its structure to Deuterium.

## Key claim

For fourth-generation quarks with masses above approximately 620 GeV, the Yukawa attraction mediated by Higgs exchange can overcome the repulsive strong interaction in the colour-octet channel, leading to the formation of bound states; the octet state then decays to the singlet state via gluon emission with a width much smaller than the binding energy.

## Method

The authors used a variational approach with a hydrogen-like trial wavefunction to solve the Schrödinger equation for a fourth-generation quark–antiquark pair, including relativistic kinetic energy, Yukawa potential from Higgs exchange, Coulomb-like strong interaction (repulsive for octet, attractive for singlet), and relativistic and radiative corrections. They minimized the ground-state energy with respect to the variational parameter \(q\) to obtain binding energies for both colour-octet and colour-singlet states. The decay width of the octet-to-singlet transition was calculated using an M1 transition formalism adapted for gluon emission, with wavefunction overlap integrals evaluated from the variational solutions.

## Key numbers

* The critical quark mass for bound-state formation in the colour-octet channel is \(m_{cr} \approx 620\) GeV; for masses above this value the ground-state energy becomes negative.
* The Higgs vacuum expectation value is taken as \(v = 246\) GeV, defining the Higgs coupling constant \(\alpha_h = m^2/(4\pi v^2)\).
* For a Higgs mass \(m_h = 125\) GeV, the mass difference between the fourth-generation quarks \(t'\) and \(b'\) is \(\Delta m = m_{t'} - m_{b'} \approx \left(1 + \frac{1}{5} \ln(125/115)\right) \times 55\) GeV, which is less than the \(W\) boson mass, suppressing the semi-leptonic decay channel.
* The decay width of the octet-singlet transition is almost two orders of magnitude smaller than the corresponding binding energies (see Fig. 2 in the paper).
* The size of the bound state is estimated as \(r_H \sim 1/(m_{t'}\alpha_h)\), which is several orders smaller than known mesons.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Flambaum (2011) on Higgs-induced binding of colour-singlet fourth-generation quarkonium to the colour-octet channel, where gluon exchange is repulsive. It builds on earlier studies by Cho (1996), Wong (1999), Bodwin (1995), Braaten (1995, 1999), and Leibovich (2001) on colour-octet contributions to quarkonium production, and on Enkhbat (2011) who considered pseudo-scalar Nambu-Goldstone coupling. The results suggest that long-lived colour-octet bound states of fourth-generation quarks could form a "nucleus" that captures a light colour-charged particle via strong interaction, analogous to the structure of Deuterium, enabling a new class of exotic hadronic states.
