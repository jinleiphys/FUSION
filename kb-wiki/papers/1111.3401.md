# Surprises in relativistic matter in a magnetic field

> A short review of recent advances in understanding the dynamics of relativistic matter in a magnetic field is presented. The emphasis is on the dynamics related to the generation of the chiral shift parameter in the normal ground state. We argue that the chiral shift parameter contributes to the axial current density, but does not modify the conventional axial anomaly relation. The analysis based on gauge invariant regularization schemes in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model suggests that these findings should be valid also in gauge theories. It is pointed out that the chiral shift parameter can affect observable properties of compact stars and modify the key features of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral shift parameter \(\Delta\) is dynamically generated in the normal ground state of dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field, contributing to the axial current density but not modifying the conventional axial anomaly relation. The authors hedge that these findings, derived in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using gauge-invariant regularization, should also be valid in gauge theories like QED and QCD, but note that the cutoff dependence in the NJL model means the chiral shift parameter will become a running quantity in renormalizable theories.

## Method

The authors analyze the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in a constant external magnetic field, using a mean-field approximation to derive gap equations for the dynamical parameters: the chemical potential \(\mu\), the fermion mass \(m\), and the chiral shift parameter \(\Delta\). They solve these equations in perturbation theory and using gauge-invariant regularization schemes (proper-time and point-splitting) to compute the axial current density and verify the axial anomaly relation. The Fermi surface for left-handed and right-handed fermions is then derived from the modified dispersion relations, and the physical consequences for compact stars and heavy ion collisions are discussed.

## Key numbers

- The topological axial current density at zero order is \(\langle j_5^3\rangle_0 = -eB\mu_0/(2\pi^2)\), where \(e>0\) is the elementary charge, \(B\) is the magnetic field strength, and \(\mu_0\) is the bare chemical potential.
- The correction to the axial current density due to the chiral shift parameter is \(\langle j_5^3\rangle - \langle j_5^3\rangle_0 \propto a\Lambda^2 \Delta\), where \(\Lambda\) is a cutoff parameter and \(a\) is a dimensionless constant of order 1.
- The chiral shift parameter scales as \(\Delta \sim g\mu \, eB/\Lambda^2\) in the NJL model, where \(g\) is the coupling constant.
- The chiral shift parameter is robust at temperatures \(10~\text{MeV} \lesssim T\lesssim 50~\text{MeV}\) present during the earliest stages of protoneutron stars, with weak temperature dependence when \(\mu\gg T\).
- The proposed pulsar kick mechanism can explain kicks with velocities \(v\gtrsim 1000~\text{km/s}\).

## Context

This review extends the work of Son et al. (2005) and Metlitski (2005) on topological axial currents at the lowest Landau level, and the authors' own prior work (Gorbar et al., 2009) proposing the chiral shift parameter. It contradicts the expectation that interactions do not affect the induced axial current, showing instead that the chiral shift parameter modifies it while preserving the axial anomaly relation of Adler, Bell, and Jackiw (1969). The results enable new mechanisms for pulsar kicks (Gorbar et al., 2009) and a modified chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions (Gorbar et al., 2011), and suggest future studies of collective modes and the QCD phase diagram in magnetic fields (Gatto et al., 2010; D'Elia, 2011).
