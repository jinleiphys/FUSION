# Jet momentum broadening in the pre-equilibrium Glasma

> Jets are important probes of heavy ion collisions as they can provide information on the interaction of a highly energetic parton with the medium it traverses. In the hydrodynamic stage of dense, strongly interacting matter, these interactions can be explained in terms of scattering processes, soft gluon emission and collinear parton splittings. However, jets originate even before the hydrodynamic stage. Here we report on the first numerical simulation of transverse momentum broadening of jets stemming from the interaction of partons with boost-invariantly expanding Glasma flux tubes. The Glasma stage is a pre-hydrodynamic stage based on the Color Glass Condensate framework. Our calculation shows strong time-dependence and an intrinsic anisotropy of momentum broadening in the directions transverse to the jet propagation direction.

## Key claim

The first numerical simulation of transverse momentum broadening of jets from the pre-equilibrium Glasma shows that the broadening is strongly time-dependent and intrinsically anisotropic, with larger broadening along the rapidity direction than the azimuthal direction, even though the Glasma itself is boost-invariant.

## Method

The authors performed non-perturbative SU(3) real-time lattice simulations of the Glasma, which evolves according to the source-free Yang-Mills equations from initial conditions given by the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. A high-energy quark test particle is propagated through the classical background Glasma fields using Wong's equations, and the accumulated squared transverse momentum is computed from the color-rotated Lorentz force along the particle trajectory. The results are also cross-checked using a dipole approximation relating momentum broadening to rectangular Wilson loops.

## Key numbers

* Total squared transverse momentum accumulated by a quark up to proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c is roughly ⟨p²⊥⟩ ≈ Q²s, with Qs the saturation momentum.
* For Qs = 2.0 GeV, the jet broadening parameter q̂⊥ peaks at very high values in the first τ ≈ 0.1 fm/c, then drops quickly; the quark obtains 3–4 GeV² of squared transverse momentum by τ = 0.6 fm/c.
* For Qs = 1.5 GeV, the same qualitative behavior is observed, with q̂⊥ scaling as (Q′s/Qs)³ and τ scaling as (Q′s/Qs) τ for other values of Qs.
* The momentum broadening anisotropy ⟨p²z⟩/⟨p²y⟩ is largely independent of Qs but shows strong dependence on the infrared regulator m/(g²μ), with higher anisotropy for more dilute Glasmas.
* The simulation used a transverse lattice of size 1024² and Ns = 50 color sheets for longitudinal discretization, with g²μ L = 100 for the m = 0 case.

## Context

This work extends the study of jet momentum broadening from the hydrodynamic quark-gluon plasma stage into the pre-equilibrium Glasma stage, which had previously only been treated with small proper time approximations (Carrington 2020) or perturbative methods. The results provide input for simulations of later jet evolution stages (Andres 2019) and offer a possible explanation for ridge-like structures in dihadron correlations (Mizukawa 2008). The anisotropic broadening mechanism differs from that in the QGP phase, where anisotropy arises from momentum-space anisotropy (Romatschke 2006, Majumder 2006, Dumitru 2007), whereas here it originates from anti-correlated regions around color-magnetic Glasma flux tubes.
