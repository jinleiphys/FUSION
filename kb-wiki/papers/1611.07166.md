# Lepton production cross sections in quasielastic $ν/\barν-$A scattering

> We present the results of (anti)neutrino induced CCQE cross sections from some nuclear targets in the energy region of $E_ν\le 1 GeV$. The aim of the study is to confront electron and muon production cross sections relevant for $ν_μ\leftrightarrow ν_e$ or $\barν_μ\leftrightarrow \barν_e$ oscillation experiments. The effects due to lepton mass and its kinematic implications, second class currents and uncertainties in the axial and pseudoscalar form factors are discussed for (anti)neutrino induced reaction cross sections on free nucleon as well as the nucleons bound in a nucleus where nuclear medium effects influence the cross section. The calculations have been performed using local Fermi gas model with nucleon correlation effects. The details are given in Ref.[1].

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the differences between electron-type and muon-type (anti)neutrino charged-current quasielastic cross sections on nuclear targets, showing that these differences are significant at energies below about 0.4 GeV and are enhanced by nuclear medium effects, which is relevant for oscillation experiments that assume lepton universality.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current quasielastic cross sections for (anti)neutrinos on free nucleons and on nuclear targets (12C, 16O, 40Ar, 56Fe, 208Pb) using a local Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and nucleon-nucleon correlations treated via random phase approximation (RPA). They compared cross sections for electron and muon production by varying the lepton mass, axial dipole mass (MA), pseudoscalar form factor, second class currents, and radiative corrections, defining fractional differences to isolate each effect.

## Key numbers

- At Eν = 0.3 GeV on 40Ar, the reduction in total cross section due to nuclear medium effects using LFG alone is ~26% for νe and ~26% for νμ; with LFG+RPA the reduction is ~55% for νe and ~57% for νμ.
- At Eν = 0.3 GeV on 40Ar, the reduction for antineutrinos using LFG alone is ~44% for νe and ~45% for νμ; with LFG+RPA the reduction is ~58% for νe and ~61% for νμ.
- At Eν = 0.2 GeV, the fractional change ΔI between νe and νμ cross sections is ~27% for free nucleon, ~40% for 12C using LFG, and ~44% for 12C using LFG+RPA; for antineutrinos the corresponding values are ~25%, ~33%, and ~42%.
- The axial dipole mass MA was varied from a world average of 1.026 GeV to 0.9 GeV and 1.2 GeV to study cross section dependence.

## Context

This work extends the study of Day and McFarland (2012) on lepton mass effects in quasielastic scattering from free nucleons to multiple nuclear targets (12C, 16O, 40Ar, 56Fe, 208Pb) using a local Fermi gas model with RPA correlations, following the formalism of Singh (1992) and Nieves (2004). The results are directly applicable to oscillation experiments such as T2K, NOνA, DUNE, SBND, and T2HK, where nuclear targets are used and the beam energy is in the few-GeV range, enabling improved background estimates for νμ → νe and νμ → νe appearance channels.
