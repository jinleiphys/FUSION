# Interaction of eta-meson with light nuclei

> A microscopic treatment of $η$--nucleus scattering is presented. When applying the underlying exact integral equations, the excitation of the target are neglected, and an input $ηN$ amplitude is chosen which reproduces the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance. It is shown that the $η$--nucleus scattering lengths are quite sensitive to the $ηN$ parameters and the nuclear wave functions. For a special choice of the parameters an $η{^4\!H}e$ quasi--bound state occurs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an eta-4He quasi-bound state can exist for realistic values of the eta-nucleon scattering length, but only for the 4He system; for lighter nuclei (deuteron, triton, 3He) the physical eta-nucleon input must be modified to support a corresponding quasi-bound state.

## Method

The authors solve a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation for eta-nucleus scattering, derived from a Finite-Rank-Approximation of the Hamiltonian, neglecting target excitations. The input eta-nucleon amplitude is a separable S-wave form that reproduces the S11(1535) resonance, with parameters chosen to match various eta-nucleon scattering lengths from the literature. Nuclear ground-state wave functions for A=2,3,4 are constructed using the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet-Tjon I+III nucleon-nucleon potential, and also using phenomenological Gaussian wave functions for comparison.

## Key numbers

- eta-nucleus scattering lengths for 2H, 3H, 3He, and 4He are tabulated for 9 combinations of the eta-nucleon range parameter alpha (2.357, 3.316, 7.617 fm^-1) and three eta-nucleon scattering lengths (0.27+i0.22 fm, 0.28+i0.19 fm, 0.55+i0.30 fm); for example, with alpha=3.316 fm^-1 and a_etaN=0.55+i0.30 fm, the 4He scattering length is -2.96+i1.39 fm using IDEA wave functions
- Using Gaussian wave functions with a_etaN=0.55+i0.30 fm and alpha=3.316 fm^-1, the 4He scattering length is -3.07+i1.48 fm
- The critical enhancement factor g1 (multiplying the real part of a_etaN=0.55+i0.30 fm) needed to produce a quasi-bound state in 4He ranges from 0.899 to 1.075 depending on alpha and the imaginary part factor g2; for deuteron the required g1 ranges from 1.535 to 1.654
- The experimental cross-section for d+d -> 4He+pi0 is 0.97 pb/sr at E_d=1100 MeV, compared to 0.003 pb/sr from electromagnetic considerations and 0.12 pb/sr from a mixing model by Coon et al.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Haider and Liu (1986) that eta-nucleus bound states might exist for A>=12, by performing microscopic few-body calculations for lighter nuclei down to A=2. The results provide input for understanding Charge Symmetry Breaking in reactions like d+d -> 4He+pi0, where eta-pi0 mixing is invoked as an explanation for the unexpectedly large cross-section observed at Saturne by Frascaria et al. The method enables future fully microscopic calculations of such reactions including eta-pi0 mixing, and establishes that the fixed-scatterer approximation (using T^0 instead of the full T-matrix) is inadequate for eta-nucleus systems.
