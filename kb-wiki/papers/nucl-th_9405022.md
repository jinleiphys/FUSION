# Cold Kaons from Hot Fireballs

> The E814-collaboration has found a component of very low $m_t$ $K^+$ mesons with a slope parameter of $T \sim 15 \, \rm MeV$. We will present a scenario which explains the observed slope parameter and which allows us to predict the expected slope parameter for kaons produced in heavier systems such as Au+Au. Implications for the restoration of chiral symmetry in relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.(figures are being sent on request)

# Cold Kaons from Hot Fireballs

## Key claim

The observed very low transverse-mass component of K⁺ mesons with a slope parameter of T ~ 15 MeV in Si+Au collisions at AGS energies can be explained by collective adiabatic cooling, provided that the fireball expands slowly due to a low-pressure phase associated with chiral symmetry restoration and that kaons experience an attractive potential in hot nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors model the fireball expansion using a relativistic transport model (BUU/VUU type) that includes nucleons, deltas, pions, and kaons with known cross sections, plus a mean field based on the Walecka (σ-ω) model to simulate the softening of the equation of state expected from the chiral restoration transition. Kaon propagation in the time-dependent mean-field potential is treated via the test-particle method solving the Vlasov equation, with initial kaon distributions following a thermal Bose distribution at T = 170 MeV and zero chemical potential. The model parameters (expansion time and potential depth) are fixed by comparing with E-814 data for Si+Au, then used to predict the K⁺ spectrum for Au+Au.

## Key numbers

- Observed slope parameter of low-mₜ K⁺ mesons: T ~ 15 MeV (E-814 collaboration, Si+Au at AGS, mₜ − m_K ≤ 25 MeV, rapidity 2.2 ≤ Y ≤ 2.6)
- Comparison slope from E-802 collaboration: ~150 MeV at central rapidities for mₜ − m > 100 MeV
- Initial fireball radius for Si+Au: R = 3.5 fm, surface thickness Δ = 1 fm
- Initial temperature for kaon distribution: T = 170 MeV
- Attractive kaon potential depth extracted from data: U₀ ≃ 50 MeV
- Walecka model parameters used: g_v = 5.5, g_s = 9.27 (compared to original Walecka values g_v = 11.56, g_s = 9.27)
- Bag pressure from chiral restoration: p_bag ≃ −200 MeV/fm³
- Predicted fireball radius for Au+Au: 6.5 fm with 350 baryons
- K⁺N scattering length: a_{K⁺N} ≃ −0.2 fm (isospin averaged s-wave)
- K⁺π cross section parameters: σ₀ = 60 mb, m_{K*} = 895 MeV, Γ = 50 MeV

## Context

This work explains the E-814 collaboration's observation of a very cold K⁺ component (Stachel 1994) which contrasts with the ~150 MeV slope seen by E-802 (Abbott 1990) and in p-Be data (Abbott 1991). The mechanism extends prior discussions of attractive mean-field effects on particle spectra (Koch et al. 1991, Shuryak 1991, Xu et al. 1993, Furnstahl et al. 1993, Koch and Brown 1993) and the requirement for adiabatic expansion (Koch and Brown 1993). The scenario enables predictions for Au+Au collisions already being analyzed by E-814, and suggests that the soft kaon component may be the first observed signature of chiral restoration in the laboratory, with implications for understanding soft pions at CERN energies.
