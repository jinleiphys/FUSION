# The Pauli principle in a three-body cluster model and the momentum distributions after fragmentation of 6He and 11Li

> We investigate two simple prescriptions to account for the Pauli principle in a three-body cluster model employing a new method based on an adiabatic hyperspherical expansion to solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space. The resulting wave functions are computed and compared. They are furthermore tested on halo nuclei by calculations of momentum distributions and invariant mass spectra arising after fragmentation of fast $^6$He and $^{11}$Li in collisions with light targets. The prescriptions are very accurate and the available measured quantities are remarkably well reproduced when final state interactions are included.

# The Pauli principle in a three-body cluster model and the momentum distributions after fragmentation of 6He and 11Li

## Key claim

The two simple prescriptions for incorporating the Pauli principle in a three-body cluster model (projecting out forbidden states versus using a phase-equivalent potential without forbidden states) produce very similar wave functions and momentum distributions for halo nuclei when final state interactions are included, despite the fact that the underlying two-body potentials differ substantially; the agreement with experimental data is "remarkably well reproduced" only when final state interactions are included, and the two prescriptions give clearly distinguishable results when final state interactions are neglected.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using an adiabatic hyperspherical expansion, computing the angular eigenvalue spectra and radial wave functions for the three-body systems 6He (4He+n+n) and 11Li (9Li+n+n). They compare two treatments of the Pauli principle: (1) using a repulsive neutron-core s-wave potential that has no Pauli-forbidden bound state, and (2) using an attractive neutron-core s-wave potential that supports a Pauli-forbidden bound state, which is then removed by omitting the corresponding diverging lambda-value from the hyperspherical expansion. The resulting wave functions are used to compute momentum distributions and invariant mass spectra after fast fragmentation on light targets, with and without inclusion of final state interactions between the fragments.

## Key numbers

- 6He binding energy: B(6He) = 1.0 MeV for both prescriptions (experimental value 0.97 ± 0.04 MeV)
- 6He root mean square radius: R(6He) = 2.45 fm (attractive potential) and 2.50 fm (repulsive potential) (experimental value 2.57 ± 0.10 fm)
- 11Li binding energy: B(11Li) = 0.30 MeV for both prescriptions (experimental value 0.295 ± 0.035 MeV)
- 11Li root mean square radius: R(11Li) = 3.34 fm (deep potential) and 3.35 fm (shallow potential) (experimental value 3.1 ± 0.3 fm)
- Neutron-4He s-wave scattering length: a_s = -3.07 ± 0.02 fm (experimental)
- 5He p3/2 resonance: E(p3/2) = 0.77 MeV, Gamma(p3/2) = 0.64 MeV (experimental); computed values E(p3/2) = 0.77 MeV, Gamma(p3/2) = 0.73 MeV
- 5He p1/2 resonance: E(p1/2) ≈ 1.97 MeV, Gamma(p1/2) ≈ 5.22 MeV (experimental); computed values E(p1/2) = 1.94 MeV, Gamma(p1/2) = 4.0 MeV
- 6He p-wave content in neutron-core relative motion: 87% for repulsive potential, 93% for attractive potential
- 10Li virtual s-state: E(s) ≈ 0.15 ± 0.15 MeV (experimental); computed virtual level at 0.20 MeV
- 10Li p1/2 resonance: E(p1/2) = 0.77 MeV, Gamma(p1/2) = 0.89 MeV (deep potential); E(p1/2) = 1.7 MeV, Gamma(p1/2) = 4.2 MeV (shallow potential)
- Final state interaction reduces the full width at half maximum of neutron momentum distributions for 6He neutron removal by a factor of 2-3

## Context

This work extends the three-body cluster model approach to halo nuclei previously developed by the authors and others (Zhu et al. 1993, Fedorov et al. 1994a, 1994c, 1995) by incorporating a practical method to handle the Pauli principle within the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion of the Faddeev equations. The paper addresses the open question of which prescription for the Pauli principle is preferable, showing that both give nearly identical results for observables when final state interactions are included. The computed momentum distributions are compared to experimental data from Kobayashi et al. (1992, 1993) for 6He fragmentation on carbon targets at 400 and 800 MeV/u. The results enable more reliable interpretation of experimental fragmentation data for halo nuclei like 6He and 11Li, and demonstrate that final state interactions must be included to correctly reproduce measured momentum distributions.
