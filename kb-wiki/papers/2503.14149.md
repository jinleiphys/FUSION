# Lattice Monte Carlo meets lattice functional Renormalization Group: A quantitative comparison

> Lattice Monte Carlo (MC) simulations and the functional Renormalization Group (RG) are powerful approaches that allow for quantitative studies of non-perturbative phenomena such as bound-state formation, spontaneous symmetry breaking and phase transitions. While results from both methods have recently shown remarkable agreement for many observables, e.g., in Quantum Chromodynamics, an analysis of deviations in certain quantities turns out to be challenging. This is because calculations with the two methods are based on different approximations, regularizations and scale fixing procedures. In the present work, we present a framework for a more direct comparison by formulating the functional RG approach on a finite spacetime lattice. This removes all ambiguities of regularization, finite size and scale fixing procedures in concrete studies. By investigating the emergence of spontaneous symmetry breaking and phase transitions in a $Z(2)$ scalar theory in $d=1,2,3$ spacetime dimensions, we demonstrate at the example of the local potential approximation how this framework can be used to evaluate and compare the systematic errors of both approaches.

## Key claim

By formulating the functional Renormalization Group (fRG) directly on a finite spacetime lattice with the same discretized action as used in lattice Monte Carlo (MC) simulations, the authors remove all ambiguities of regularization, finite size, and scale fixing procedures, enabling a direct comparison of systematic errors between the two methods. The comparison is demonstrated for a Z(2) scalar theory in d=1,2,3 dimensions using the local potential approximation (LPA), which is the simplest truncation that already includes fluctuation effects.

## Method

The authors set up a Z(2) scalar field theory on a d-dimensional isotropic lattice with lattice spacing a and periodic boundary conditions, using the same bare action for both lattice Monte Carlo simulations (employing a Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with sign flips) and lattice fRG calculations (solving the Wetterich equation on the finite lattice). For the fRG side, they employ the local potential approximation (LPA) as a truncation of the derivative expansion and use the Litim regulator, which allows them to separate the RG flow into ultraviolet, intermediate, and infrared regimes. The effective potential and susceptibility are reconstructed from magnetization calculations at multiple values of an external symmetry-breaking field c.

## Key numbers

- The lattice has N_mu = N_sigma sites in each direction, with the same extent in all dimensions, and lattice spacing a.
- The highest kinetic energy level is epsilon_max = 2*sqrt(d)/a, and the lowest non-zero level is Delta_epsilon = 2*sin(pi/N_sigma)/a.
- The Litim regulator has the form R_k(epsilon_q) = (k^2 - epsilon_q^2) * Theta(k^2 - epsilon_q^2), with lattice site decoupling scale k* = epsilon_max and M_k = k.
- The Litim regulator satisfies the property that partial_t R_k(epsilon_q) = 0 for k <= epsilon_q, so in the infrared regime (k < Delta_epsilon) only the zero mode contributes to the Wetterich equation.

## Context

This work extends earlier fRG studies of quantum field theories in finite spacetime volumes (Braun 2004, Braun 2005, Braun 2009, Fister 2015) and on spacetime lattices (Dupuis 2008, Machado 2010, Rancon 2011, Krieg 2017, Pawlowski 2017) by providing a framework for direct comparison with lattice MC simulations that avoids any non-trivial matching of parameters. The approach enables systematic evaluation of cutoff artefacts, finite-volume effects, and truncation artefacts that are also present in QCD studies, and may trigger cross-fertilization between the two methods. The Z(2) scalar theory in d=3 is of particular interest for QCD at finite temperature because of dimensional reduction, as O(4)-type models describe the chiral QCD phase transition and Z(2) symmetry may play a role near a potential critical endpoint.
