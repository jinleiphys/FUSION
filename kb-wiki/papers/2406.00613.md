# Compact dwarfs made of light-quark nuggets

> Utilizing an equivparticle model with both linear confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions, we obtain systematically the properties of strangelets and nonstrange quark matter ($ud$QM) nuggets at various baryon ($A$) and charge ($Z$) numbers, where the detailed single-quark-energy levels are fixed by solving Dirac equations in mean-field approximation (MFA). We then examine the structures of compact dwarfs made of light strangelets or $ud$QM nuggets forming body-centered cubic lattices in a uniform electron background. Despite the strangelets and $ud$QM nuggets generally become more stable at larger $A$, the compact dwarfs are still stable since the fusion reactions between those objects do not take place in the presence of a Coulomb barrier, which is similar to the cases of light nuclei in normal white dwarfs. If $ud$QM dwarfs or strangelet dwarfs are covered with normal matter, their masses and radii become larger but do not exceed those of ordinary white dwarfs. Finally, we investigate the radial oscillation frequencies of $ud$QM dwarfs and strangelet dwarfs, and find that their frequencies are typically higher than traditional white dwarfs. The stability of compact dwarfs are then analysised by examining radial oscillation frequencies of the fundamental mode, where compact dwarfs covered by normal matter are still stable.

## Key claim

Compact dwarfs made of light-quark nuggets (strangelets or nonstrange quark matter nuggets) are stable not because the nuggets themselves are the most stable configuration at a given size, but because Coulomb barriers prevent fusion reactions between nuggets, analogous to the stability of light nuclei in normal white dwarfs.

## Method

The authors used an equivparticle model with linear confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions, solving Dirac equations in the mean-field approximation to obtain single-quark-energy levels and properties of strangelets and nonstrange quark matter nuggets at various baryon (A) and charge (Z) numbers. They then applied the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model to construct equations of state for compact dwarf matter, where nuggets form body-centered cubic lattices in a uniform electron background, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to determine mass-radius relations and radial oscillation frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Strangelets become absolutely stable (energy per baryon below 930 MeV) at baryon number A ≥ 120.
- Nonstrange quark matter nuggets become absolutely stable at A ≥ 447, and more stable than nuclei at A ≥ 274.
- The heaviest β-stable nucleus considered is 266Hs, which is more stable than the corresponding nonstrange quark matter nugget.
- Compact dwarfs covered by normal matter (16O) can account for observed ultra-low-mass white dwarfs with masses from approximately 0.02 M⊙ to 0.08 M⊙ and radii spanning 4,270 km to 10,670 km.
- Radial oscillation frequencies of compact dwarfs are typically higher than those of traditional white dwarfs.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations on compact dwarfs (Alford 2012, Wang 2021, Xia 2022, Liu 2023) that assumed a small surface tension requiring nuggets at specific sizes (A ≈ 1000) to be most stable. The authors show that this condition is not necessary for stability, as Coulomb barriers prevent fusion. The results enable interpretation of observed ultra-low-mass white dwarfs (Kurban 2022) and underluminous Type Ia supernovae (Flörs 2019) as potential compact dwarfs made of quark nuggets, and connect to studies of strange star crusts (Alford 2008) and quark star crust formation.
