# Resummed hydrodynamic expansion for a plasma of particles interacting with fields

> A novel description of kinetic theory dynamics is proposed in terms of resummed moments that embed information of both hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes. The resulting expansion can be used to extend hydrodynamics to higher orders in a consistent and numerically efficient way; at lowest order it reduces to an Israel-Stewart-like theory. This formalism is especially suited to investigate the general problem of particles interacting with fields. We tested the accuracy of this approach against the exact solution of the coupled Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell equations for a plasma in an electromagnetic field undergoing Bjorken-like expansion, including extreme cases characterized by large deviations from local equilibrium and large electric fields. We show that this new resummed method maintains the fast convergence of the traditional method of moments. We also find a new condition, unrelated to Knudsen numbers and pressure corrections, that justifies the truncation of the series even in situations far from local thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a resummed moment expansion for the Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell equations that avoids couplings to ill-defined moments with negative energy index in the ultra-relativistic limit, and shows that this expansion converges rapidly to the exact solution even under extreme conditions far from local equilibrium and with large electric fields.

## Method

The authors introduce a new set of moments of the distribution function that include a Gaussian weight factor \(e^{-\xi^2(p\cdot u)^2}\), which resums an infinite number of standard \(\reducible\)-moments with different energy indices. They derive exact evolution equations for these resummed moments from the Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell equations and test the expansion against the exact solution of the coupled system in 0+1 dimensions for a massless plasma undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion with a relaxation time approximation collision term.

## Key numbers

* The resummed moment expansion was tested against the exact solution of the Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell equations for a plasma in an electromagnetic field undergoing Bjorken-like expansion, including "extreme cases characterized by large deviations from local equilibrium and large electric fields."
* The paper demonstrates that the resummed method "maintains the fast convergence of the traditional method of moments" when compared to the exact solution.
* A new condition is found, "unrelated to Knudsen numbers and pressure corrections," that justifies truncation of the series even in situations far from local thermal equilibrium.

## Context

This work extends the method of moments for the relativistic Boltzmann equation, previously developed by Denicol et al. (2012) and tested for convergence by Denicol et al. (2016), to include long-range mean fields via the Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell equations. The resummed approach addresses a fundamental difficulty: in the presence of electromagnetic fields, standard moments with negative energy index \(r+s < -2\) become ill-defined in the massless limit, which would otherwise prevent systematic improvement of the hydrodynamic description. The formalism enables the study of dissipative relativistic magnetohydrodynamics derived from kinetic theory, as recently pursued by Denicol et al. (2018), and can be extended to non-Abelian gauge fields and medium-dependent particle masses.
