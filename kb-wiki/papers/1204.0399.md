# Constraining the nuclear equation of state at subsaturation densities

> Only one third of the nucleons in $^{208}$Pb occupy the saturation density area. Consequently nuclear observables related to average properties of nuclei, such as masses or radii, constrain the equation of state (EOS) not at saturation density but rather around the so-called crossing density, localised close to the mean value of the density of nuclei: $ρ\simeq$0.11 fm$^{-3}$. This provides an explanation for the empirical fact that several EOS quantities calculated with various functionals cross at a density significantly lower than the saturation one. The third derivative M of the energy at the crossing density is constrained by the giant monopole resonance (GMR) measurements in an isotopic chain rather than the incompressibility at saturation density. The GMR measurements provide M=1110 $\pm$ 70 MeV (6% uncertainty), whose extrapolation gives K$_\infty$=230 $\pm$ 40 MeV (17% uncertainty).

## Key claim

Nuclear observables such as masses and radii constrain the equation of state not at saturation density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³) but around a crossing density ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³, which is close to the mean density of nuclei; consequently, the giant monopole resonance constrains the third derivative M of the energy at this crossing density rather than the incompressibility K_∞ at saturation density.

## Method

The authors used Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations to compute the density distribution of ²⁰⁸Pb and other nuclei, demonstrating that only one third of nucleons lie at saturation density. They then applied the constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) sum-rule approach to calculate the isoscalar giant monopole resonance centroid energy as a function of the derivative M of the incompressibility at the crossing density, using multiple Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic energy density functionals. The correlation between GMR energy and M was established across an isotopic chain (¹¹²⁻¹²⁴Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹⁴⁴Sm) to extract a value of M.

## Key numbers

- Mean density of ²⁰⁸Pb: ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.12 fm⁻³ with variance √(⟨ρ²⟩ − ⟨ρ⟩²) = 0.04 fm⁻³
- Mean density of ¹²⁰Sn: 0.11 fm⁻³; mean density of ⁴⁰Ca: 0.07 fm⁻³
- Crossing density: ρ_c ≈ 0.7 ρ₀ ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility at crossing density: K(ρ_c) ≈ 40 MeV
- M = 1110 ± 70 MeV (6% uncertainty) from GMR measurements across multiple nuclei
- Extrapolated K_∞ = 230 ± 40 MeV (17% uncertainty)

## Context

This work extends the empirical observation of a crossing density previously noted for the symmetry energy (Piekarewicz 2011), pairing gap (Khan 2009b), and neutron EOS (Brown 2000; Typel 2001), providing a unified explanation based on the mean density of nuclei. The paper contradicts the traditional approach of extracting K_∞ directly from GMR measurements, which had yielded values ranging from 210 MeV (Blaizot 1980) to 260 MeV (Vretenar 2003), and shows that the linear E_GMR vs. K_∞ correlation is an artifact of the specific density dependence of Skyrme functionals. The results enable a more reliable determination of the EOS at subsaturation densities and open the possibility to study the isospin dependence of the incompressibility through GMR measurements in more neutron-rich nuclei.
