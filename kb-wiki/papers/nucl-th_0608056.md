# Neutrino Nucleus Reactions based on New Shell Model Hamiltonians

> A new shell model Hamiltonian for p-shell nuclei which properly takes into account important roles of spin-isospin interactions is used to obtain cross sections of neutrino-^12C reactions induced by decay-at-rest (DAR) neutrinos as well as supernona neutrinos. Branching ratios to various decay channels are calculated by the Hauser-Feshbach theory. Neutrino-^4He reactions are also investigated by using recent shell model Hamiltonians. The reaction cross sections are found to be enhanced for both ^12C and ^4He compared with previous calculations. As an interesting consequence of this, a possible enhancement of the production yields of light elements, ^7Li and ^11B, during supernova explosions is pointed out.

## Key claim

The new shell model Hamiltonian (SFO) for p-shell nuclei, which enhances spin-isospin interactions and tensor components compared to the Cohen-Kurath Hamiltonian, yields enhanced neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections for both 12C and 4He, but the enhancement is conditional on the choice of effective axial-vector coupling constants (gA^eff) needed to reproduce experimental data, with quenching factors of 0.70-0.95 required for different reaction channels.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations using the SFO Hamiltonian for 12C and the WBP and SPSDMK Hamiltonians for 4He, with configuration spaces including up to 2-3 hbar-omega excitations. Cross sections for charge-exchange and neutral current reactions induced by decay-at-rest (DAR) neutrinos and supernova neutrinos were computed using multipole expansions of the weak interaction current operators, and branching ratios for decay channels were calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model with optical model transmission coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive reaction 12C(nu_e, e-)12N(1_g.s.+): SFO Hamiltonian gives 9.96 x 10^-42 cm^2 (bare gA) and 9.06 x 10^-42 cm^2 (gA^eff = 0.95 gA), compared to experimental values of 8.9 +/- 0.3 +/- 0.9 x 10^-42 cm^2 (LSND) and 9.1 +/- 0.5 +/- 0.8 x 10^-42 cm^2 (KARMEN)
- Neutral current reaction on 12C to 1+ (T=1, 15.1 MeV) state: SFO with gA^eff = 0.95 gA gives sum of (nu_e, nu_e') and (anti-nu_mu, anti-nu_mu') cross sections of 9.76 x 10^-42 cm^2, compared to experimental value of 10.4 +/- 1.0 +/- 0.9 x 10^-42 cm^2 (KARMEN)
- Inclusive reaction 12C(nu_e, e-)12N*: SFO gives 8.35 x 10^-42 cm^2 (bare gA) and 5.22 x 10^-42 cm^2 (gA^eff = 0.70 gA), compared to experimental values of 4.3 +/- 0.4 +/- 0.6 x 10^-42 cm^2 (LSND) and 5.1 +/- 0.6 +/- 0.5 x 10^-42 cm^2 (KARMEN)
- Spin-dipole strengths in 12C summed up to Ex = 50 MeV: SFO gives 1.79 fm^2 (0-), 4.33 fm^2 (1-), and 7.19 fm^2 (2-) states; PSDMK2 gives 1.78 fm^2, 4.12 fm^2, and 7.03 fm^2 respectively
- Spin-dipole strengths in 4He summed up to Ex = 50 MeV: WBP gives 3.34 fm^2, SPSDMK gives 4.71 fm^2
- Production yields in supernova model: WBP+SFO gives M(7Li) = 3.06 x 10^-7 solar masses and M(11B) = 7.51 x 10^-7 solar masses; SPSDMK+PSDMK2 gives M(7Li) = 4.24 x 10^-7 solar masses and M(11B) = 9.38 x 10^-7 solar masses; previous HW92 calculation gives M(7Li) = 2.36 x 10^-7 solar masses and M(11B) = 6.29 x 10^-7 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous shell model calculations of neutrino-nucleus reactions on 12C by Woosley et al. (1990) and Hayes and Towner (2000), and on 4He by Woosley et al. (1990), using the improved SFO Hamiltonian developed by Suzuki et al. (2003) that better reproduces magnetic moments and Gamow-Teller transitions in p-shell nuclei. The enhanced cross sections enable a possible explanation for increased production yields of 7Li and 11B during supernova explosions, addressing discrepancies between earlier nucleosynthesis calculations and observed abundances. The results also connect to ab initio calculations by Gazit et al. using the AV8' interaction for 4He neutral current reactions, which show similar cross sections at T=10 MeV but different temperature dependence.
