# Quantum Monte Carlo studies of a trimer scaling function with microscopic two- and three-body interactions

> We present an energy scaling function to predict, in a specific range, the energy of bosonic trimers with large scattering lengths and finite range interactions, which is validated by quantum Monte Carlo calculations using microscopic Hamiltonians with two- and three-body potentials. The proposed scaling function depends on the scattering length, effective range, and a reference energy, which we chose as the trimer energy at unitarity. We obtained the scaling function as a limit cycle from the solution of the renormalized zero-range model with effective range corrections. We proposed a simple parametrization of the energy scaling function. Besides the intrinsic interest in theoretical and experimental investigations, this scaling function allows one to probe Efimov physics with only the trimer ground states, which may open opportunities to identify Efimov trimers whenever access to excited states is limited.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an energy scaling function that predicts trimer binding energies for bosonic systems with large scattering lengths and finite effective ranges, validated by quantum Monte Carlo calculations. The scaling function is claimed to be universal within a specific parameter window where it does not depend on the details of the microscopic interactions, but the authors explicitly note that deviations occur for larger values of the dimensionless range parameter y, particularly when the three-body force range is large.

## Method

The authors solved the subtracted Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation with effective range corrections to obtain a limit cycle for the energy scaling function F(x,y) for the first three excited trimer states. They then performed diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations using microscopic Hamiltonians with Gaussian and modified Poschl-Teller two-body potentials plus a repulsive Gaussian three-body force, computing trimer ground-state energies at unitarity and at finite scattering lengths for various values of the dimensionless parameters x and y.

## Key numbers

- The zero-range limit of the scaling function gives F(x,0) = 1 + 2.113x for |x| << 1, where the slope 2.113 comes from the two-body contact C2 = 53.097 sqrt(-m E3(0,r0,nu))/hbar
- The fitted scaling function parameters are: c1 = 2.106(1), c2 = 1.26(1), c3 = 0.804(4), c4 = 1.0(2), c5 = 1.2(1), sigma = 0.680(3)
- The ansatz-based parameters are: c1 = 2.107, c2 = 1.35, c3 = 0.804, c4 = 1.03, c5 = 1.05, sigma = 0.641, where sigma = 2s0/pi with s0 the Efimov parameter
- The trimer energy at unitarity without a three-body force (lambda3=0) is about 1000 times larger than with the most repulsive three-body force considered
- The range of y studied is 0 <= y <= 0.30, and x values span -0.15 <= x <= 0.15 in steps of 0.05

## Context

This work extends the zero-range Efimov universal function (Braaten 2006, Naidon 2017) by incorporating finite effective range corrections, building on earlier studies of range corrections to the Phillips line (Efimov and Tkachenko 1985) and subsequent systematic studies (Hammer 2001, Castin 2011, Werner 2012, Gattobigio 2014, Kievsky 2014). The scaling function enables probing Efimov physics using only trimer ground states rather than requiring access to excited states, which may facilitate experimental identification of Efimov trimers in cold-atom systems where excited states are difficult to observe.
