# Relativistic model of 2p-2h meson exchange currents in (anti)neutrino scattering

> We develop a model of relativistic, charged meson-exchange currents (MEC) for neutrino-nucleus interactions. The two-body current is the sum of seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole and $Δ$-pole operators. These operators are obtained from the weak pion-production amplitudes for the nucleon derived in the non-linear $σ$-model together with weak excitation of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance and its subsequent decay into $Nπ$. With these currents we compute the five 2p-2h response functions contributing to $(ν_l,l^-)$ and $(\overlineν_l,l^+)$ reactions in the relativistic Fermi gas model. The total current is the sum of vector and axial two-body currents. The vector current is related to the electromagnetic MEC operator that contributes to electron scattering. This allows one to check our model by comparison with the results of De Pace {\em et al.,} Nuclear Physics A 726 (2003) 303. Thus our model is a natural extension of that model to the weak sector with the addition of the axial MEC operator. The dependences of the response functions on several ingredients of the approach are analyzed. Specifically we discuss relativistic effects, quantify the size of the direct-exchange interferences, and the relative importance of the axial versus vector current.

## Key claim

The paper develops a fully relativistic model of weak meson-exchange currents (MEC) for neutrino-nucleus scattering, extending the electromagnetic MEC model of De Pace et al. (2003) to the weak sector by adding the axial MEC operator. The authors note that the resulting number of Dirac trace terms would be "almost intractable" using analytical methods, so they instead compute the spin traces numerically.

## Method

The two-body current is constructed as the sum of seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta-pole operators, derived from the weak pion-production amplitudes of Hernandez et al. (2007) based on the non-linear sigma-model, together with weak excitation of the Delta(1232) resonance. The five 2p-2h response functions (R^CC, R^CL, R^LL, R^T, R^T') for both neutrino and antineutrino charged-current reactions are computed in the relativistic Fermi gas model by evaluating the resulting seven-dimensional integrals numerically, including both direct and exchange contributions that were neglected in prior neutrino-scattering models.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum k_F used for symmetric nuclear matter (Z=N=A/2), with system volume V = 3π² Z/k_F³
- Nucleon axial coupling g_A = 1.26 and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV used in the Goldberger-Treiman relation
- pi N Delta coupling constant f* = 2.13 used in the Delta-pole current
- Delta(1232) resonance mass M_Delta and width Gamma_Delta appear in the Rarita-Schwinger propagator

## Context

This work extends the fully relativistic electromagnetic MEC model of De Pace et al. (2003) to the weak sector by adding the axial MEC operator, enabling calculation of neutrino-nucleus 2p-2h response functions. It contrasts with the non-relativistic MEC treatment of Martini et al. (2009, 2010) and the relativistic but approximate calculation of Nieves et al. (2011) that neglected direct/exchange interference. The model enables future application to neutrino oscillation experiments using the superscaling approach (SuSA), including studies of asymmetric nuclei such as ⁴⁰Ar, ⁵⁶Fe, or ²⁰⁸Pb relevant for experiments like DUNE.
