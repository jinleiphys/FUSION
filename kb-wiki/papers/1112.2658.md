# Evidence for non-analytic light quark mass dependence in the baryon spectrum

> Using precise lattice QCD computations of the baryon spectrum, we present the first direct evidence for the presence of contributions to the baryon masses which are non-analytic in the light quark masses; contributions which are often denoted "chiral logarithms". We isolate the poor convergence of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory to the flavor-singlet mass combination. The flavor-octet baryon mass splittings, which are corrected by chiral logarithms at next to leading order in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, yield baryon-pion axial coupling constants D, F, C and H consistent with QCD values; the first evidence of chiral logarithms in the baryon spectrum. The Gell-Mann--Okubo relation, a flavor-27 baryon mass splitting, which is dominated by chiral corrections from light quark masses, provides further evidence for the presence of non-analytic light quark mass dependence in the baryon spectrum; we simultaneously find the GMO relation to be inconsistent with the first few terms in a taylor expansion in m_s - m_l, which must be valid for small values of this SU(3) breaking parameter. Additional, more definitive tests of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory will become possible with future, more precise, lattice calculations.

# Evidence for non-analytic light quark mass dependence in the baryon spectrum

## Key claim
Using precise lattice QCD computations of the baryon spectrum, the paper presents the first direct evidence for non-analytic light quark mass dependence (chiral logarithms) in baryon masses, but this is explicitly not the definitive work on the subject as many systematics remain to be resolved.

## Method
The study uses lattice QCD results from a mixed-action calculation with domain-wall fermion propagators on asqtad-improved staggered sea quark configurations from the MILC Collaboration, at a single lattice spacing. The baryon spectrum data are analyzed using linear combinations of baryon masses (R1, R3, R4) designed to have definite scaling in both the 1/Nc expansion and SU(3) symmetry breaking, combined with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers
- Physical pion mass: 138 MeV, physical kaon mass: 496 MeV (used for extrapolation to physical point)
- Extrapolated light quark mass at physical point: m_l = 3.0(2) MeV
- Extrapolated strange quark mass at physical point: m_s = 99(5) MeV
- Experimental Gell-Mann-Okubo relation value: Delta_GMO = 6.45 MeV
- Pion masses in lattice data range from 320(2) MeV to 905(2) MeV across six ensembles
- R1 values range from 1285(6) MeV to 1769(9) MeV across the ensembles
- R4 values range from -39(2) MeV to 0 MeV across the ensembles
- Delta_GMO values range from 5.6(2.3) MeV to 0 MeV across the ensembles

## Context
This work extends the combined large Nc and SU(3) chiral expansion approach first explored by Jenkins (2009), which demonstrated that predicted scaling with both 1/Nc and (m_s - m_l) was visible in lattice data. It addresses the long-standing difficulty that previous SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory fits to the baryon spectrum by Walker-Loud (2008) and Ishikawa (2009) yielded axial coupling constants consistent with zero, in contradiction with direct lattice calculations of hyperon axial charges by Lin (2007). The results enable future, more definitive tests of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory with more precise lattice calculations.
