# Quark and gluon condensates in the quark-meson coupling model

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, we study the density dependence of the quark and gluon condensates in nuclear matter. We show that the change of the quark condensate is mainly driven by the scalar field in the medium and that the reduction of the quark condensate is suppressed at high density, even in the mean-field approximation. The gluon condensate decreases by 4 - 6 % at nuclear saturation density. We also give a simple relationship between the change of the quark condensate and that of a hadron mass in the medium.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the reduction of the quark condensate in nuclear matter is suppressed at high density even in the mean-field approximation, contrary to the leading-order model-independent prediction; the gluon condensate decreases by only 4–6% at nuclear saturation density, but this result carries a large uncertainty from the strange quark content of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors apply the QMC model in its version QMC-II, where both nucleons and vector mesons are described by the MIT bag model and the sigma meson mass is parametrized as a quadratic function of the scalar field. They use the Hellmann–Feynman theorem applied to the chiral-symmetry-breaking quark mass term of the total Hamiltonian to derive the density-dependent quark condensate, and the trace anomaly together with the Hellmann–Feynman theorem to obtain the gluon condensate. The coupling constants are fixed to reproduce the saturation energy and density of symmetric nuclear matter, and spurious contributions from the dependence of these couplings on the quark mass are removed to extract the physically meaningful result.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum quark condensate: \( Q_0 \equiv \langle \bar{q} q \rangle_0 \simeq -(225 \pm 25\ \text{MeV})^3 \)
- Vacuum gluon condensate: \( G_0 \equiv \langle G_{\mu\nu}^a G^{a\mu\nu} \rangle_0 \simeq (360 \pm 20\ \text{MeV})^4 \)
- For small density (parameter set B), the quark condensate ratio follows \( Q(\rho_B)/Q_0 \simeq 1 - 0.357\, \rho_r \), where \( \rho_r = \rho_B/\rho_0 \) and \( \rho_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3} \)
- The gluon condensate at low density (set B) is given by \( G(\rho_B)/G_0 = 1 - (0.03892\, \rho_r + 0.001292\, \rho_r^{5/3}) \), yielding a reduction of about 4% at saturation density
- If the strange quark content of the nucleon is taken as zero, the gluon condensate reduction at \( \rho_0 \) is about 6%
- The sigma field value at saturation density is \( g_\sigma \bar{\sigma} \simeq 200\ \text{MeV} \) and at \( 3\rho_0 \) it is about 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier QMC model calculations of the quark condensate (Saito, Tsushima, Thomas 1995; 1996) by including the in-medium structure of mesons (QMC-II), and it contrasts with the model-independent leading-order result of Drukarev and Levin (1988), Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992), and Lutz, Friman, and Soyeur (1992). The finding that higher-order contributions hinder chiral symmetry restoration at high density agrees with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Li and Ko (1995) and Brockmann and Weise (1996). The gluon condensate result is consistent with the model-independent prediction of Cohen et al. (1992). The simple relation between the change of the quark condensate and hadron masses in medium connects to the scaling ideas of Brown and Rho (1991), but the authors note that the leading non-analytic contribution to the pion-nucleon sigma term complicates this relation, as discussed by Birse (1996).
