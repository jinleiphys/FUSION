# Higgs response and pair condensation energy in superfluid nuclei

> The pairing correlation in nuclei causes a characteristic excitation, known as the pair vibration, which is populated by the pair transfer reactions. Here we introduce a new method of characterizing the pair vibration by employing an analogy to the Higgs mode, which emerges in infinite superconducting/superfluid systems as a collective vibrational mode associated with the amplitude oscillation of the Cooper pair condensate. The idea is formulated by defining a pair-transfer probe, the Higgs operator, and then describing the linear response and the strength function to this probe. We will show that the pair condensation energy in nuclei can be extracted with use of the strength sum and the static polarizability of the Higgs response. In order to demonstrate and validate the method, we perform for Sn isotopes numerical analysis based the quasi-particle random phase approximation to the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model. We discuss a possibility to apply this new scheme to pair transfer experiment.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the pair condensation energy in nuclei can be extracted from the strength sum and static polarizability of a newly defined "Higgs response," which probes amplitude oscillations of the pair condensate, though the method's validity is demonstrated only for neutron pairing in Sn isotopes using a specific Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model.

## Method

The authors define a Higgs operator as a linear combination of pair-addition and pair-removal operators, then compute the linear response and strength function for this operator using the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) applied to the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model. They perform numerical calculations for neutron pairing in spherical Sn isotopes, using the SLy4 Skyrme parameter set and a density-dependent delta interaction for the pairing channel, with the HFB equation solved in a spherical coordinate system with box boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

* The pair rotation mode appears at excitation energy 0.60 MeV in 120Sn, which should be zero-energy but deviates due to numerical errors and truncations.
* The low-lying pair vibration in 120Sn appears at excitation energy 2.44 MeV, consistent with the predicted value E ≈ 2Δ where the average pair gap Δ = 1.15 MeV.
* High-lying pair vibrations appear in the interval from 3 MeV up to around 20 MeV, with specific peaks at 12.36 and 17.45 MeV in pair-addition strength and at 3.85 and 17.31 MeV in pair-removal strength.
* The radial coordinate is truncated at Rmax = 20 fm, partial waves are included up to lmax = 12 and jmax = 25/2, and quasiparticle states are truncated at maximum energy Emax = 60 MeV.
* The DDDI pairing parameters are v0 = -458.4 MeV fm^-3, ρ0 = 0.08 fm^-3, α = 0.845, and η = 0.71, chosen to reproduce the scattering length in the 1S0 channel and the experimental gap in 120Sn.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Higgs mode from infinite superconducting/superfluid systems to finite nuclei, building on the established analogy between nuclear pairing and BCS superconductivity developed by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) and Belyaev (1959). The approach connects to prior studies of pair vibrations by Bes and Broglia (1966) and Broglia (1973), as well as the giant pair vibration discussed by Broglia (1977) and more recent experimental observations by Cappuzzello (2015) and Cavallaro (2019). The method enables a new way to extract the pair condensation energy from pair transfer experiments, potentially providing a direct link between measured pair transfer strengths and the energy gain from Cooper pair condensation in nuclei.
