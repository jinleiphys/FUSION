# Electron-deuteron DIS with spectator tagging at EIC: Development of theoretical framework

> An Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) would enable next-generation measurements of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) on the deuteron with detection of a forward-moving nucleon (p, n) and measurement of its recoil momentum ("spectator tagging"). Such experiments offer full control of the nuclear configuration during the high-energy process and can be used for precision studies of the neutron's partonic structure and its spin dependence, nuclear modifications of partonic structure, and nuclear shadowing at small x. We review the theoretical description of spectator tagging at EIC energies (light-front nuclear structure, on-shell extrapolation in the recoil nucleon momentum, final-state interactions, diffractive effects at small x) and report about on-going developments.

# Electron-deuteron DIS with spectator tagging at EIC: Development of theoretical framework

## Key claim

Spectator tagging in electron-deuteron DIS at a future Electron-Ion Collider can provide full control of nuclear configurations during high-energy scattering, enabling precision extraction of free neutron structure functions and spin structure via on-shell extrapolation to the nucleon pole, provided that final-state interactions and off-shell effects are accounted for theoretically and do not prevent the extrapolation.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework based on light-front nuclear structure and the impulse approximation, expressing the tagged deuteron structure function in terms of the deuteron light-front wave function and the nucleon structure function evaluated at a shifted Bjorken variable. They describe the on-shell extrapolation procedure that exploits the nucleon pole in the invariant momentum transfer t, and estimate final-state interactions using a schematic model with empirical DIS hadron distributions and rescattering cross sections. Diffractive effects at small x are treated using methods analogous to those for nuclear shadowing in inclusive DIS.

## Key numbers

- Recoil momenta accessible in tagging: p_R ~ few 10–100 MeV/c in the deuteron rest frame
- JLab EIC detector resolution corresponds to p_R ~ 20 MeV/c in the rest frame
- Kinematic limit for on-shell extrapolation: t - M_N^2 = -0.0041 GeV^2 (deuteron binding energy)
- EIC center-of-mass energies: sqrt(s_eN) ~ 15–100 GeV
- EIC luminosity: L ~ 10^33–10^34 cm^-2 s^-1
- Expected correlated F_2n uncertainty from on-shell extrapolation: less than 3% with JLab EIC design
- Integrated luminosity for neutron spin structure measurements: ~100 fb^-1
- Region where nuclear modifications are negligible in inclusive data: x_tilde ~ 0.2
- Region where EMC effect is seen in inclusive data: x_tilde > 0.3

## Context

This work extends the theoretical description of spectator tagging from fixed-target experiments like the JLab CLAS BONuS experiment (Tkachenko, 2014) to collider kinematics at a future EIC. It builds on light-front nuclear structure methods (Frankfurt, 1981) and the on-shell extrapolation technique (Sargsian, 2005) to extract free neutron structure. The framework enables next-generation measurements of neutron spin structure, nuclear modifications of partonic structure (EMC effect, antishadowing), and nuclear shadowing at small x, with the potential to separate final-state interactions from genuine nuclear modifications through comparative measurements across kinematic regions.
