# A new calculation for D(e,e'p)n at GeV energies

> We perform a fully relativistic calculation of the D(e,e'p)n reaction in the impulse approximation. We employ the Gross equation to describe the deuteron ground state, and we use the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude to describe the final state interactions (FSIs). We include both on-shell and positive-energy off-shell contributions in our FSI calculation. We show results for momentum distributions and angular distributions of the differential cross section, as well as for various asymmetries. We identify kinematic regions where various parts of the final state interactions are relevant, and discuss the theoretical uncertainties connected with calculations at high missing momenta.

## Key claim

The authors present a fully relativistic calculation of the D(e,e'p)n reaction that includes both on-shell and positive-energy off-shell contributions to final state interactions, but they explicitly neglect negative-energy state contributions, which means the current matrix elements are no longer covariant and are calculated in the laboratory frame.

## Method

The calculation uses the Gross equation to describe the deuteron ground state and the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude for final state interactions, including all spin-dependent parts (central, double-spin-flip, and single-spin-flip terms). The authors decompose the FSI contribution into three parts: an on-shell contribution where all four legs of the pn scattering amplitude are on mass shell, a principal value integral over positive-energy off-shell momenta, and a negative-energy contribution that is neglected. The off-shell behavior of the scattering amplitude is estimated using a simple prescription with a cutoff form factor, and the invariant functions are obtained from interpolation of the on-shell SAID helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* At beam energy 3.1095 GeV, Q^2 = 0.665 GeV^2, x_Bj = 0.964, and phi_p = 180 degrees, the largest discrepancy between calculation and data appears at p_m = 15 MeV, where the calculation overpredicts the data by 15%
* For beam energy 5.5 GeV, Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, x_Bj = 1, and phi_p = 180 degrees, the PWIA and on-shell FSI curves are almost identical up to p_m = 0.05 GeV, then FSI reduces the cross section from roughly p_m = 0.05 GeV to p_m = 0.33 GeV
* For the same kinematics at x_Bj = 1.3, the off-shell FSI contribution becomes significant for p_m > 0.4 GeV, with much greater sensitivity to the cutoff value than at x_Bj = 1
* The angular distribution at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 and p_m = 0.4 GeV shows a pronounced peak at approximately theta_m = 70 degrees, corresponding to x_Bj = 1

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic calculations of D(e,e'p)n reactions by providing a fully relativistic framework, and it addresses the long-standing "low missing momentum puzzle" where calculations have consistently overpredicted data at very low missing momenta, as compiled by Boeglin and Trento (2005). The calculation enables systematic studies of spin-dependent FSI effects and off-shell contributions at GeV energies relevant to Jefferson Lab experiments, and it provides a baseline for identifying non-nucleonic effects such as meson exchange currents and six-quark admixtures in the deuteron wave function. The authors note that their results improve upon previous calculations by Ulmer et al. (2002) at low missing momenta, though the discrepancy is not fully removed.
