# Ab initio mapping of the boundary of the $N=20$ island of inversion

> Starting from a chiral two- plus three-nucleon interaction, we perform a systematic study of the low-lying states of neutron-rich nuclei around $N=20$ using the in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM), which combines the multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group (MR-IMSRG) with the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) defined in a full single-particle space. The main features of the energy spectra and electromagnetic properties of low-lying states in both even-even and odd-mass nuclei of this mass region are reasonably reproduced. The boundary of the $N=20$ island of inversion (IOI) is investigated, and the results indicate that $^{30}$Ne, $^{29,31,33}$Na, $^{31-34}$Mg, and $^{35}$Al lie within the IOI, whereas $^{29}$F, $^{29}$Ne, $^{30}$Mg, $^{31, 33}$Al, $^{34,35}$Si, and $^{35}$P fall outside it.

## Key claim
Using the in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM) starting from a chiral two- plus three-nucleon interaction (EM1.8/2.0), the authors map the boundary of the N=20 island of inversion (IOI) and find that 30Ne, 29,31,33Na, 31-34Mg, and 35Al lie inside the IOI, while 29F, 29Ne, 30Mg, 31,33Al, 34,35Si, and 35P fall outside it, with the caveat that the spectra remain somewhat stretched and the energy ordering of competing states with different shapes remains a challenge.

## Method
The IM-GCM combines the multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group (MR-IMSRG) with the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) in a full single-particle space. Starting from the EM1.8/2.0 chiral interaction, the Hamiltonian is evolved via the IMSRG flow equation using the Magnus expansion, then PGCM calculations with angular-momentum and particle-number projection are performed on ensembles of VAPNP+HFB states (for even-even nuclei) or one-quasiparticle states (for odd-mass nuclei) to obtain low-lying state energies and wave functions. Electromagnetic observables are computed using consistently evolved one- and two-body operators.

## Key numbers
- 30Ne: energy surface stabilizes around flow parameter s = 0.16 MeV^-1, with two minima: spherical and prolate at β2 = 0.5.
- 32Mg: extrapolation to eMax → ∞ increases the 2+1 excitation energy by about 1% and B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) by about 6%; the 0+2 excitation energy increases by up to 25%.
- Varying ℏω between 12 and 16 MeV changes 2+1 and 0+2 excitation energies by approximately 7% and spectroscopic quadrupole moment by about 5%.
- Calculations use a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis with eMax = 8 (9 major oscillator shells) and ℏω = 16 MeV; for the 3N interaction, matrix elements with e1+e2+e3 > 14 are discarded.

## Context
This work extends previous IM-GCM studies of 48Ti (Yao 2020) and 76Ge/76Se (Belley 2024) to neutron-rich nuclei around N=20, addressing shape coexistence and the weakening of the N=20 magic number. It improves upon VS-IMSRG calculations (Miyagi 2020) that systematically overestimated 2+1 excitation energies and underestimated B(E2) values, and upon coupled-cluster studies (Hagen 2022, Sun 2024) that struggled with energy ordering of competing shapes. The results enable future ab initio studies of shape coexistence and collectivity in exotic nuclei, though accurately predicting the energy ordering of weakly and strongly deformed bands remains a challenge, as also noted in PGCM-PT(2) studies (Frosini 2022).
