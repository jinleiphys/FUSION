# Study of the neutrino-oxygen cross sections of the charged-current reaction 16O($\bar־½_e,e^+$)16N(0 MeV,$2^-$) and the neutral-current reaction 16O($־½,־½$')16O(12.97/12.53 MeV,$2^-$), producing high-energy gamma rays

> In the previous work, we discussed the cross section and the detection of 4.4-MeV $־³$ rays produced in the neutrino neutral-current (NC) reaction $^{16}$O($־½, ־½^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O(12.97 MeV and 12.53 MeV, $2^-$) in a water Cherenkov detector at the low energy below 100 MeV. In this report, we further investigated both the charged-current (CC) reaction $^{16}$O($\bar־½_e, e^+$)$^{16}$N(0 MeV, $2^-$) and the NC reaction$^{16}$O($־½, ־½^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O(12.97 MeV and 12.53 MeV, $2^-$), producing high-energy $־³$ rays, in which the more solid identification of the reactions can be applied via the coincidence method.

## Key claim

The paper investigates both the charged-current (CC) reaction 16O(anti-nu_e, e+)16N(0 MeV, 2-) and the neutral-current (NC) reaction 16O(nu, nu')16O(12.97/12.53 MeV, 2-), proposing that a coincidence method can provide more solid identification of these reactions than previously considered, but the cross sections depend on quenching factors (f_s=0.65ֲ±0.05, f_A=0.68ֲ±0.05) and an isospin-mixing parameter (beta=0.25ֲ±0.05) that carry significant uncertainties.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for the CC reaction to the 16N ground-state group (bound states at 0, 0.120, 0.298, and 0.397 MeV) using quenching factors evaluated from electron scattering form factors, muon capture rates, and beta decay data. For the NC reaction, they computed cross sections for both the 4.4-MeV gamma-ray production via alpha decay and the high-energy gamma-ray production via electromagnetic cascade decay of the two 2- states at 12.53 MeV and 12.97 MeV in 16O, incorporating the isospin-mixing parameter beta=0.25. The cross sections were evaluated as functions of neutrino energy from 12 to 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- CC cross section to 16N(g.s. group) at E_nu=30 MeV: 6.99ֳ—10^-42 cm^2; at E_nu=100 MeV: 4.02ֳ—10^-41 cm^2
- CC cross section to 16N(g.s., 2-) alone at E_nu=30 MeV: 4.25ֳ—10^-42 cm^2; at E_nu=100 MeV: 2.78ֳ—10^-41 cm^2
- Quenching factors used: f_s=0.65ֲ±0.05 (spin g-factor) and f_A=0.68ֲ±0.05 (axial-vector coupling constant)
- Isospin-mixing parameter: beta=0.25ֲ±0.05
- Branching ratio for 6.13-MeV gamma ray from 16N(g.s.) beta decay: 0.662ֲ±0.006
- Total probability of delayed coincidence signal with visible energy >5 MeV from 16N(g.s.) beta decay: about 86%
- Half-life of 16N(g.s.) beta decay: T_1/2=7.13ֲ±0.02 sec
- Threshold energy for CC reaction to 16N(g.s. group): E_th=11.44 MeV
- Branching ratio for gamma-ray production from D state (12.53 MeV): Gamma_gamma/Gamma=3.1ֲ±0.04%
- Branching ratio for gamma-ray production from U state (12.97 MeV): Gamma_gamma/Gamma=0.27ֲ±0.02%
- Alpha-decay branching ratio Br^U(alpha_1)=0.35 and Br^D(alpha_1)=0.83 used for 4.4-MeV gamma production

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Sakuda et al. (2023) that evaluated quenching factors and isospin mixing for the 2- states of 16O, and builds on the foundational calculations by Donnelly and Walecka (1970s) who first computed neutrino-oxygen cross sections with 15-20% accuracy. The authors compare their CC cross section to that of Haxton (1980s), finding their result is larger by about 40%. The study is motivated by the well-studied neutrino-carbon reactions measured by KARMEN and LSND experiments, and aims to enable unambiguous identification of neutrino-oxygen interactions in water Cherenkov detectors like Super-Kamiokande and Hyper-Kamiokande for supernova neutrino detection, using coincidence methods similar to those employed for inverse beta decay with neutron tagging in SK-Gd.
