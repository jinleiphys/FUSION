# Testing the Influence of Surface Tension and Finite Width of QGP Bags on the QCD Matter EOS Properties at NICA Energies

> Here I give some strong arguments that the central issues for theoretical studies of the (tri)critical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram are the surface tension of large/heavy QGP bags and their medium dependent width. Then I discuss three major directions to further develop the realistic exactly solvable statistical models which simultaneously are able to describe the 1-st order deconfinement phase transition, the 2-nd order one and the cross-over. Also I analyze the most necessary projects that have to be studied in order to formulate the reliable and convincing signals of the mixed phase formation at NICA energies.

# Testing the Influence of Surface Tension and Finite Width of QGP Bags on the QCD Matter EOS Properties at NICA Energies

## Key claim

The surface tension of large/heavy QGP bags and their medium-dependent finite width are the central physical mechanisms that determine the existence and properties of the (tri)critical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram, and these effects have been largely ignored by other low-energy programs. The author argues that the negative value of the QGP bag surface tension at transition temperatures and small baryonic chemical potential is the only physical reason that transforms the first-order deconfinement phase transition into a cross-over at low baryonic densities.

## Method

The author reviews and synthesizes results from the quark gluon bags with surface tension model (QGBSTM) and its extension QGBSTM2, which are exactly solvable statistical models that unify first-order and second-order deconfinement phase transitions with cross-over behavior. The finite width model (FWM) is introduced to incorporate the medium-dependent decay width of heavy QGP bags, with its parameters estimated from lattice QCD data across a range of temperatures. The analysis also uses a novel relation between string tension, surface tension, thermal pressure, and bag radius derived from equating free energies of the confining color string and an elongated cylindrical bag, applied to lattice QCD entropy data.

## Key numbers

- The mean width of a resonance of mass M in vacuum behaves as Γ(M) ≈ 600 [M/M₀]^(1/2) MeV, with M₀ ≈ 2.5 GeV.
- The minimal resonance width at zero temperature is about 600 MeV for QGP bags heavier than 2.5 GeV.
- The minimal resonance width at the Hagedorn temperature is about 2000 MeV.
- Lattice QCD pressure contains T-linear and T⁴ ln T terms in the temperature range between 240 MeV and 420 MeV, in addition to the σT⁴ term.
- At high temperatures up to 4 T_c (where T_c is the cross-over temperature), the QGP pressure and energy density remain well below the corresponding quantities of non-interacting quarks and gluons.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous developments of the QGBSTM (Bugaev, 2007) and QGBSTM2 (Bugaev, 2008) models for the QCD tricritical and critical endpoint, and the finite width model (Bugaev, 2008). It directly addresses the unresolved disagreements between different lattice QCD groups regarding the endpoint location and the failure of mean-field models to explain the degeneration of first-order deconfinement into cross-over. The analysis enables the formulation of firm signals for mixed phase formation at NICA energies, including potential connections to soft photon interferometry data from the WA98 Collaboration and to the nuclear multifragmentation community's studies of liquid-gas phase transitions.
