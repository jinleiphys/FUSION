# Insights into the $\mathbf{γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}}$ transition

> We report novel theoretical results for the $γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ transition, utilizing a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact interaction (SCI) within the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) formalism. In this approach, both nucleon, $N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+$, and $Δ(1232)$'s parity partner, $Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$, are treated as quark-diquark composites, with their internal structures governed accordingly by a tractable truncation of the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation. Nonpointlike quark+quark (diquark) correlations within baryons, which are deeply tied to the processes driving hadron mass generation, are inherently dynamic in the sense that they continually break apart and recombine guided by the Faddeev kernel. For the nucleon, isoscalar-scalar and isovector-axial-vector diquarks dominate, while the $Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ state only includes contributions from isovector-axial-vector diquarks because the SCI-interaction excludes isovector-vector diquarks. Once the Faddeev wave function of the baryons involved in the electromagnetic transition is normalized taking into account that its elastic electric form factor must be one at the on-shell photon point, we compute the transition form factors that describe the $γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ reaction and, using algebraic relations, derive the corresponding helicity amplitudes. When comparing with experiment, our findings highlight a strong sensitivity of these observables to the internal structure of baryons, offering valuable insights. Although the SCI-framework has obvious limitations, its algebraic simplicity provides analytical predictions that serve as useful benchmarks for guiding more refined studies within QCD-based DSEs frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical calculation of the electromagnetic transition form factors for the $\gamma^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to \Delta(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ process, using a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) within the Dyson-Schwinger equations formalism. The authors acknowledge that the SCI framework has obvious limitations, but its algebraic simplicity provides analytical predictions that serve as useful benchmarks for guiding more refined studies within QCD-based DSEs frameworks.

## Method

The nucleon and $\Delta(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ baryons are treated as quark-diquark composites, with their internal structures governed by a tractable truncation of the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation using a vector⊗vector contact interaction. The nucleon includes isoscalar-scalar and isovector-axial-vector diquark correlations, while the $\Delta(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ state only includes contributions from isovector-axial-vector diquarks because the SCI-interaction excludes isovector-vector diquarks. The Faddeev wave functions are normalized using the condition that the elastic electric form factor equals one at the on-shell photon point, then the transition form factors and corresponding helicity amplitudes are computed.

## Key numbers

- Dressed quark mass M = 0.37 GeV for both up and down quarks, with current-quark mass m0 = 7 MeV
- Scalar diquark mass m_{[ud]_{0+}} = 0.78 GeV; axial-vector diquark masses m_{{uu}_{1+}} = m_{{ud}_{1+}} = m_{{dd}_{1+}} = 1.06 GeV
- Computed nucleon mass m_{N(940)} = 1.14 GeV; computed Delta mass m_{Delta(1700)} = 1.72 GeV
- Effective couplings: g_N = 1.18 for the nucleon; g_Delta = 1.56 × sqrt(0.1) for the Delta(1700)
- Faddeev amplitude values: for N(940), s = 0.88, a1^{{uu}} = -0.38, a2^{{uu}} = -0.06; for Delta(1700), d^{{uu}} = 0.58
- Regularization parameters: 1/tau_ir = 0.240 GeV, 1/tau_uv = 0.905 GeV
- Effective gluon mass m_g = 0.5 GeV; effective coupling alpha_IR = 0.36π

## Context

This work extends prior DSE-based studies of nucleon resonance transitions, which have successfully described low-lying states such as N(940)1/2+ (Wilson 2011, Segovia 2014), Delta(1232)3/2+ (Segovia 2013), N(1440)1/2+ (Wilson 2011, Segovia 2015), Delta(1600)3/2+ (Lu 2019), and N(1535)1/2- (Raya 2021). The calculation of the Delta(1700)3/2- transition was previously missing from the literature. The results can be compared with experimental measurements from CLAS at JLab (Burkert 2002, Mokeev 2013, 2020, 2024) and enable insights into the internal structure of the Delta(1700)3/2- resonance, while also providing benchmarks for more sophisticated QCD-based DSEs frameworks.
