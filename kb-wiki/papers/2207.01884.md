# Nuclear incompressibility and sound speed in uniform matter and finite nuclei

> We have extended the compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with a density-dependent surface term (eCLDM), which allows for a unified description of both the nuclear ground state energies and the incompressibility modulus in finite nuclei $K_A$. We analyse the role of the nuclear empirical parameters, e.g., $K_{sat}$, $Q_{sat}$, $L_{sym}$ and $K_{sym}$, which contribute to the bulk properties, as well as the role of the finite size contributions. For the bulk properties, the density and isospin dependencies of the nuclear incompressibility in infinite matter are characterized by introducing new empirical parameters, and two new constraints for the value of $K_{sym}$ are suggested. For finite nuclei, we employ a Bayesian approach coupled to a Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC) exploration of the parameter space to confront the model predictions of $K_A$ in Zr, Sn and Pb isotopes to the experimental data. We show that $Q_{sat}\approx -950\pm 200$~MeV describes the experimental measurements of $K_A$ in these isotopes. This value is different from the ones deduced from phenomenological nuclear energy density functionals, suggesting a possible explanation of their difficulty to accurately describe Zr, Sn and Pb data all together. In addition we explore the impact of a fictitious measurement of the Giant Monopole Resonance energy in $^{132}$Sn. We show that this measurement, provided it is accurate enough, will allow to better determine $K_{sym}$ and $K_τ$. Finally we explore the properties of the sound speed around saturation density and show the important role of finite size terms in finite nuclei since they reduce the sound speed to approximately half compared to nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper extends the compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with a density-dependent surface term (eCLDM) to unify the description of nuclear ground state energies and the incompressibility modulus in finite nuclei, showing that the empirical parameter \(Q_{sat} \approx -950 \pm 200\) MeV describes experimental measurements of \(K_A\) in Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes, a value different from those deduced from phenomenological nuclear energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended compressible liquid-drop model (eCLDM) with a density-dependent surface tension, using the meta-model for the bulk term where parameters are the nuclear empirical parameters of uniform matter. They employed a Bayesian approach coupled to Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC) exploration of the parameter space to confront model predictions of the incompressibility modulus \(K_A\) in Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes against experimental data from isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) measurements.

## Key numbers

- \(Q_{sat} \approx -950 \pm 200\) MeV describes the experimental measurements of \(K_A\) in Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes.
- The saturation density of nuclear matter is taken as \(n_{sat} = 0.155 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The incompressibility modulus in symmetric matter at saturation is \(K_{sat} = 210 \pm 30\) MeV from Blaizot (1980), later corrected to \(240 \pm 20\) MeV as a compromise between data in \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{90}\)Zr.
- The isospin dependence parameter \(K_\tau \approx -550 \pm 100\) MeV was extracted from systematic exploration of the Sn isotopic chain.
- The parameter \(K_{sym} \approx -107 \pm 88\) MeV is a compilation from 16 independent analyses of neutron star observational data since GW170817.
- A lower bound on \(K_{sym}\) is derived: \(K_{sym} \geq -K_{sat} + \sqrt{2 L_{sym} Q(\delta=1)} \sim -150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop model framework originally developed by Myers (1969) and Weiss (1969) and addresses the difficulty noted by Blaizot (1980) that the CLDM is not appropriate for accurately extracting \(K_{sat}\) from finite nuclei due to the absence of a density-dependent surface term. The analysis builds on prior work by Khan (2012, 2013) suggesting that systematic differences in extracted \(K_{sat}\) values could relate to the density dependence encoded in \(Q_{sat}\), and by Margueron (2019) showing that large uncertainty in higher-order empirical parameters impacts precise determination of lower-order ones. The results enable better determination of \(K_{sym}\) and \(K_\tau\) through future measurements of the ISGMR energy in \(^{132}\)Sn, and show that finite size terms reduce the sound speed in finite nuclei to approximately half compared to nuclear matter.
