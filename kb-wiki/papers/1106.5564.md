# On the Determination of \bar{d}/\bar{u} Ratios from Proton-Proton and Proton-Deuteron Drell-Yan Processes

> By using the convolution formula derived within the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics, we have examined the Fermi Motion effects on the ratios R_{pd/pp}= sigma^{pd}/(2 sigma^{pp}) between the proton-proton (p-p) and proton-deuteron (p-d) Drell-Yan cross sections. We have found that in the small x_2 < 0.3 region, the Fermi Motion effect is less than 1% and our results for the ratios R_{pd/pp} = sigma^{pd}/(2 sigma^{pp}) agree well with the data at 800 GeV. In the large Bjorken x_2 > about 0.4 region, the p-d Drell-Yan cross sections can be influenced strongly by the Fermi motion effect. At 120 GeV the predicted Fermi Motion effect can enhance the ratios R_{pd/pp} by about 20% at x_2 \sim 0.6 and about a factor of 2.5 at x_2 -> 1.0. Our results suggest that the Fermi motion effect, along with other possible nuclear effects, must be included, in extracting the \bar{d}/\bar{u} ratios in the proton from the experiments on p-p and p-d Drell-Yan processes at large x_2.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Fermi motion effects in the deuteron must be included when extracting the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio from proton-proton and proton-deuteron Drell-Yan data at large Bjorken \(x_2 > 0.4\), contrary to the common assumption that such nuclear effects are negligible; at 120 GeV the predicted enhancement of the ratio \(R_{pd/pp} = \sigma^{pd}/(2\sigma^{pp})\) can reach about 20% at \(x_2 \sim 0.6\) and about a factor of 2.5 as \(x_2 \rightarrow 1.0\).

## Method

The author derives a convolution formula for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section within the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics, using the impulse approximation to express the cross section in terms of the elementary \(q\bar{q} \rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-\) cross section, the parton distribution functions of the free nucleon, and the nucleon momentum distribution \(\rho_{p_d}(\vec{p}_N)\) of a moving deuteron. Numerical calculations are performed using the CTEQ5m parton distributions and realistic deuteron wavefunctions from high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (such as the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential) to compute the ratio \(R_{pd/pp}\) at beam energies of 800 GeV and 120 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At 800 GeV and small \(x_2 < 0.3\), the Fermi motion effect on \(R_{pd/pp}\) is less than 1%.
- At 120 GeV and \(x_2 \sim 0.6\), the predicted Fermi motion effect enhances \(R_{pd/pp}\) by about 20%.
- At 120 GeV and \(x_2 \rightarrow 1.0\), the predicted Fermi motion effect enhances \(R_{pd/pp}\) by about a factor of 2.5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Drell-Yan data from Fermilab experiments (Peng 2001) by including nuclear Fermi motion effects that were previously neglected in the standard analysis using Eq. (1) and Eq. (2) of the paper. The approach is analogous to the treatment of nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron (Acharya 2009) and uses the relativistic quantum mechanics framework of Dirac, as reviewed by Keister and Polyzou. The results enable more precise extraction of the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio at large \(x\) from the forthcoming Fermilab experiment (Peng 2011), which is needed to distinguish between models of the nonperturbative sea of the proton.
