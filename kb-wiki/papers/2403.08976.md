# Hybrid analysis of radiative corrections to neutron decay with current algebra and effective field theory

> We introduce a useful framework for high-precision studies of the neutron beta decay by merging the current algebra description and the fixed-order effective field theory calculation of the electroweak radiative corrections to the neutron axial form factor. We discuss the advantages of this hybrid method and show that it only requires a minimal amount of lattice QCD inputs to achieve a $10^{-4}$ theory accuracy for the Standard Model prediction of the neutron lifetime and the axial-to-vector coupling ratio $λ$, both important to the search for physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a hybrid framework that merges the current algebra (Sirlin's representation) with fixed-order effective field theory (HBChPT) to compute electroweak radiative corrections to the neutron axial form factor, claiming that this method requires only minimal lattice QCD inputs to achieve a $10^{-4}$ theory accuracy for the Standard Model prediction of the neutron lifetime and the axial-to-vector coupling ratio $\lambda$, with the caveat that the percent-level inner radiative correction to $g_A$ discovered in Cirigliano (2022) is entirely contained in the "three-point function" of Sirlin's representation, which decouples from the lepton piece and can be computed on lattice without requiring a five-point correlation function.

## Method

The author combines Sirlin's representation (SR) of radiative corrections with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) to compute the form-factor radiative corrections to neutron beta decay. The SR formalism is used to identify the three-point function as the only non-trivial contribution to the inner radiative correction of the axial coupling, while the EFT framework (HBChPT up to chiral order $n=3$) provides the explicit calculation of this three-point function and the residual integral $\Box^A_\text{int}$. The method splits the full radiative correction into outer corrections (analytically calculable from point-charge interactions) and inner corrections (requiring non-perturbative QCD input), with the hybrid approach allowing the three-point function to be isolated for future lattice QCD computation.

## Key numbers

- The universal inner radiative correction $\Delta_R^U$ contains a perturbative QCD correction $\tilde{a}_g \approx -0.083$ and a higher-order QED effect $\delta_\text{HO}^\text{QED} = 0.00109(10)$.
- The residual integral contribution to the axial inner correction is $\Box^A_\text{int} \approx 2.47 \times 10^{-3}$ using point-like form factors, shifting slightly to $2.44 \times 10^{-3}$ with full nucleon form factor dependence and an uncertainty of order $10^{-6}$.
- The expansion of $\Box^A_\text{int}$ in powers of $m_\pi/m_N$ gives $\Box^A_\text{int} = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi}\left\{\frac{1}{2}\left(\ln\frac{m_N^2}{m_\pi^2}-1\right)+\frac{\pi m_\pi}{6m_N}(1+2\mu_p-2\mu_n)+\mathcal{O}\left(\frac{m_\pi^2}{m_N^2}\right)\right\}$.
- The Fermi constant is $G_F = 1.1663787(6) \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$ from muon decay (MuLan, 2012).
- The neutron-proton mass splitting is approximately $m_n - m_p \approx 1.3$ MeV, and the recoil effects are at most $(m_n-m_p)/m_\pi \sim 10^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the current algebra formalism of Sirlin (1974) for radiative corrections in neutron beta decay, which previously identified the $\gamma W$-box diagram as the main source of inner corrections for the vector coupling (used for $V_{ud}$ extraction in Seng (2018, 2020) and Czarnecki (2019)). It directly addresses the percent-level inner correction to $g_A$ discovered by Cirigliano (2022) using chiral perturbation theory, and demonstrates that this correction is contained in the three-point function of Sirlin's representation rather than being a new effect. The hybrid method enables future lattice QCD calculations of the three-point function (requiring only the matrix element $\langle n|(J_W^\mu)_A|n\rangle_{\text{QCD+QED}}$ with a small photon mass) to achieve sub-percent precision comparisons between the lattice-calculated pure-hadronic $\mathring{g}_A$ and the experimentally measured $g_A$, which is important for constraining right-handed currents and other beyond-Standard-Model physics.
