# Nuclear Dependence in Drell-Yan Transverse Momentum Distribution

> We calculate the nuclear enhancement in Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution in hadron-nucleus collisions. In terms of multiple scattering picture, we compute the contributions from double scattering. Applying the generalized factorization theorem, we express the nuclear enhancement in terms of twist-4 nuclear parton correlation functions. We demonstrate that these twist-4 nuclear parton correlation functions are universal, and are the same as those used to explain the nuclear dependence in di-jet momentum imbalance and in direct photon production. Using the known information on the twist-4 parton correlation functions, we estimate the nuclear enhancement for Drell-Yan pair in large $q_T$ region. We also discuss the source of nuclear suppression in small $q_T$ region.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the nuclear enhancement in the Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution for hadron-nucleus collisions, specifically for the large \(q_T\) region where fixed-order perturbative QCD is valid, and demonstrates that the enhancement arises from double scattering described by universal twist-4 nuclear parton correlation functions, which are the same as those used for di-jet momentum imbalance and direct photon production.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized factorization theorem at higher twist, as developed by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS), to compute the double scattering contribution to the Drell-Yan differential cross section \(d\sigma/dQ^2 dq_T^2 dy\) in hadron-nucleus collisions. They separate the double scattering into two types, soft-hard and double-hard processes, based on the poles of propagators in the Feynman diagrams, and derive the partonic hard parts for each subprocess by performing collinear expansion and integrating over parton momentum fractions. The final expressions for the nuclear enhancement are given in terms of twist-4 quark-gluon and four-gluon correlation functions, \(T_q(x,A)\) and \(T_g(x,A)\), which are universal and previously introduced by LQS.

## Key numbers

* The observed nuclear enhancement in average squared transverse momentum, \(\langle q_T^2 \rangle\), grows approximately as \(A^{1/3}\) with atomic number \(A\), based on experimental data from NA10 and E772.
* The size of the relevant twist-4 parton distributions was estimated by LQS using Fermilab E683 data to be of the order of \(0.05-0.1\) GeV\(^2\) times typical twist-2 parton distributions.
* The parton-level Born Drell-Yan cross section is given by \(\sigma^{DY}_0 = 4\pi \alpha_{em}^2 / (9Q^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist factorization formalism developed by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS) for di-jet momentum imbalance in photon-nucleus collisions to the Drell-Yan process in hadron-nucleus collisions. The paper demonstrates that the same universal twist-4 nuclear parton correlation functions, \(T_q\) and \(T_g\), which explain nuclear dependence in di-jet production and direct photon production, also govern the nuclear enhancement in Drell-Yan transverse momentum distributions. This enables future calculations of nuclear effects in the small \(q_T\) region, where resummation of large logarithms is required, as noted by the author in reference to a forthcoming work (GQS).
