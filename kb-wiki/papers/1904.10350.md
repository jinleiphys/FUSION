# Primordial non-Gaussianity in heavy-ion collisions

> We show that experimental data on cumulants of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions probe the non-Gaussian statistics of the energy density field created right after the collision. We carry out a perturbative expansion of the initial anisotropies of the system in terms of its density fluctuations. We argue that the correlation between the magnitudes of elliptic flow and triangular flow, dubbed $sc(3,2)$, is generically of the same sign and order of magnitude as the kurtosis of triangular flow in a hydrodynamic picture. The experimental observation that these quantities are negative implies that the distribution of energy around a given point has positive skew.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed negative values of the symmetric cumulant \(sc(3,2)\) and the kurtosis of triangular flow \(c_3\{4\}\) in heavy-ion collisions imply that the distribution of energy density fluctuations created immediately after the collision has positive skew, not that the fluctuations are Gaussian.

## Method

The authors carry out a perturbative expansion of the initial anisotropies \(\varepsilon_2\) and \(\varepsilon_3\) in powers of the density fluctuations \(\delta\rho\) around the average density profile, keeping terms up to second order in \(\delta\rho\). They then evaluate the fourth-order cumulants of \(\varepsilon_n\) (including the mixed cumulant \(SC(3,2)\)) to leading non-trivial order, decomposing the contributions into terms arising from the kurtosis (\(C_4\)), skewness (\(C_3\)), and variance (\(C_2\)) of the initial density field. The analytic results are tested against Monte Carlo simulations using a model of identical pointlike sources with a Gaussian average density profile.

## Key numbers

*   For a symmetric Gaussian density profile of identical pointlike sources, the standardized kurtosis of \(\varepsilon_3\) is \(c_3\{4\}/c_3\{2\}^2 \simeq -7.34/N\), where \(N\) is the number of sources.
*   For the same model, the standardized mixed kurtosis is \(sc(3,2) = -3/(4N) = -0.75/N\).
*   For the same model, the standardized kurtosis of \(\varepsilon_2\) is \(c_2\{4\}/c_2\{2\}^2 = -4/N\).
*   Switching off the recentering correction in the Monte Carlo simulation changes the sign of \(sc(3,2)\) from negative to positive, yielding \(sc(3,2) = 9/(4N)\) for a Gaussian profile.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative framework for initial-state fluctuations developed by Blaizot (2014) and Gronqvist (2016) by including the effect of the recentering correction, which is shown to be crucial for the symmetric cumulant \(SC(3,2)\). The paper directly connects experimental measurements of anisotropic flow cumulants (such as those from ALICE and STAR) to the non-Gaussian statistics of the initial energy density, analogous to studies of primordial non-Gaussianity in cosmology (Ade 2013). It enables the use of standardized kurtoses like \(sc(3,2)\) and \(c_n\{4\}/c_n\{2\}^2\) as direct probes of the skewness and kurtosis of the initial density field, allowing quantitative comparisons between models of initial conditions and experimental data.
