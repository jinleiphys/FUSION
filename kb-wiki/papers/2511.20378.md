# Toward a Unified Understanding of the Dense Matter Equation of State

> Efforts to understand the equation of state (EOS) of dense nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities have grown more sophisticated over the past decade, driven by a surge in high-precision data from both terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations. While for the former, heavy-ion collisions (HIC) represent a unique opportunity to constraint the EOS in a controlled laboratory setting, the latter can be precisely probed thanks to the advent of multi-messenger astronomy (MMA). However, as we move away from our understanding drawn from individual sources and limited statistics to the era of precision physics with improved datasets, the need for a systematic way to combine them becomes clear. In this article, we trace the individual methods for extracting the EOS both for HIC and MMA. We then review the current state-of-the-art efforts to combine these individual information sources from Bayesian multi-source analysis, e.g., the Nuclear Physics and Multi-Messenger Astrophysics (NMMA) and Bayesian Analysis of Nuclear Dynamics (BAND) frameworks, and fully integrated EOS frameworks, i.e., the Modular Unified Solver for the Equation of State (MUSES) calculation engine. We highlight the scientific advances made possible by each step and outline the remaining challenges that must be addressed to build a coherent, predictive picture of dense nuclear matter across all relevant regimes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that combining heavy-ion collision (HIC) data and multi-messenger astrophysics (MMA) observations through Bayesian multi-source frameworks (such as NMMA, BAND, and MUSES) is necessary to achieve a unified, predictive equation of state (EOS) for dense nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities, but acknowledges that significant challenges remain in modeling the detailed evolution of the medium and in reconciling different experimental regimes.

## Method

The authors review the individual methods for extracting the EOS from relativistic heavy-ion collisions (using collective flow observables, sub-threshold kaon yields, and pion ratios analyzed via transport models like BUU and QMD) and from multi-messenger astrophysics (using gravitational wave inspiral from LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA, neutron star mass-radius measurements from NICER, and electromagnetic counterparts). They then survey integrative frameworks that combine these information sources: the Nuclear Physics and Multi-Messenger Astrophysics (NMMA) framework, the Bayesian Analysis of Nuclear Dynamics (BAND) framework, and the Modular Unified Solver for the Equation of State (MUSES) calculation engine, which aim to merge EOS constraints in statistically robust ways.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\) (corresponding to mass density \(2.17 \times 10^{14}~\text{g/cm}^{-3}\))
* Heavy-ion collisions probe densities of approximately \(1-5n_0\) (supra-saturation regime)
* Multi-messenger astrophysics probes densities of approximately \(2-10n_0\)
* Heavy-ion collisions at SIS-18 probe densities \(1-2.5n_0\) with sub-threshold \(K^+\) production measured at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.16-2.70~\text{GeV}\) (KaoS experiment)
* Heavy-ion collisions at AGS and RHIC-FXT probe densities above \(2.5n_0\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.70-4.72~\text{GeV}\) (E895, E877) and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0-4.5~\text{GeV}\) (STAR)
* The S\(\pi\)RIT experiment studied charged pion ratios at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.01~\text{GeV}\) with tin isotopes of varying asymmetry (\(^{108,112,124,132}\text{Sn}\) on \(^{112,124}\text{Sn}\))
* Hyperons become energetically favorable at \(2-3n_0\) in neutron star cores, reducing maximum mass below observed maxima of approximately \(2M_\odot\)
* ALICE at LHC studied hypernuclei in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76, 5.02~\text{TeV}\); STAR at RHIC studied them in Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3, 200~\text{GeV}\)
* Femtoscopic studies at ALICE use source sizes of \(1-2~\text{fm}\) (pp at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 13~\text{TeV}\), p+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02~\text{TeV}\))
* Femtoscopic studies at STAR use source sizes of \(5-6~\text{fm}\) (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200~\text{GeV}\) and \(3~\text{GeV}\))

## Context

This review extends prior work on dense matter EOS extraction from individual sources (HIC experiments at SIS-18, AGS, RHIC, and LHC; astrophysical observations from LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA and NICER) by surveying recent integrative frameworks. It builds on Bayesian multi-source analysis approaches such as NMMA (Pang 2022), BAND (Phillips 2020), and MUSES (Reinke Pelicer 2025), which aim to combine constraints from heavy-ion collisions and neutron star observations. The paper identifies remaining challenges in dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions across the QCD phase diagram, particularly the breakdown of hybrid hydrodynamics-transport approaches at lower collision energies and the need for improved particilization models in the baryon-dominated regime, pointing toward future work with multi-fluid hydrodynamics and upcoming facilities like FAIR, NICA, and FRIB.
