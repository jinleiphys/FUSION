# Holographic Jet Shapes and their Evolution in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> Recently our group analyzed how the probability distribution for the jet opening angle is modified in an ensemble of jets that has propagated through an expanding cooling droplet of plasma [1]. Each jet in the ensemble is represented holographically by a string in the dual 4+1- dimensional gravitational theory with the distribution of initial energies and opening angles in the ensemble given by perturbative QCD. In [1], the full string dynamics were approximated by assuming that the string moves at the speed of light. We are now able to analyze the full string dynamics for a range of possible initial conditions, giving us access to the dynamics of holographic jets just after their creation. The nullification timescale and the features of the string when it has nullified are all results of the string evolution. This emboldens us to analyze the full jet shape modification, rather than just the opening angle modification of each jet in the ensemble as in [1]. We find the result that the jet shape scales with the opening angle at any particular energy. We construct an ensemble of dijets with energies and energy asymmetry distributions taken from events in proton-proton collisions, opening angle distribution as in [1], and jet shape taken from proton-proton collisions and scaled according to our result. We study how these observables are modified after we send the ensemble of dijets through the strongly-coupled plasma.

## Key claim

The authors find that the jet shape scales with the opening angle at any particular energy, enabling them to construct an ensemble of holographic dijets whose modification by strongly coupled plasma shows qualitative agreement with CMS data, though the model does not include soft particles from the plasma wake or three-jet events.

## Method

The authors represent each jet as a string in a dual 4+1-dimensional gravitational theory (AdS/CFT correspondence), evolving the full Nambu-Goto string dynamics in vacuum to determine the nullified energy distribution, then propagating the strings through an expanding, cooling droplet of strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma modeled with a blast-wave temperature profile and boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They construct an ensemble of dijets with initial energies and energy asymmetry distributions taken from proton-proton collision data, opening angle distributions from perturbative QCD, and jet shapes from proton-proton collisions scaled according to their scaling result, then compute the modification of jet shapes and dijet asymmetries after propagation through the plasma.

## Key numbers

- The free parameter a relating the jet shape variable C_1^(1) to the string endpoint angle sigma_0 is fixed to a=2 (best fit), with a range a=1.8-2.5 shown as a pink band
- The temperature rescaling parameter b = T_N=4 / T_QCD is fixed to b=0.203, giving R_AA^jet approximately 0.4
- The jet radius parameter is R=0.3 for consistency with CMS data
- The hydrodynamic profile starts at tau = 1 fm/c after the collision
- Freezeout temperature is T_FO = 175 MeV
- The cut p_T^jet > 100 GeV/c is imposed for jet shape modification comparisons
- For dijet asymmetry, p_T,1 > 120 GeV/c and p_T,2 > 30 GeV/c are used for consistency with CMS data from the 0-10% centrality bin

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Rajagopal (2016) which approximated string dynamics by assuming the string moves at the speed of light and only studied opening angle modification, by now analyzing full string dynamics and computing the complete jet shape modification. The authors compare their results to CMS data on jet shape modification from Chatrchyan (2013) and dijet asymmetry from Chatrchyan (2012), finding qualitative agreement at small angles but noting the model's limitations at larger angles where wake contributions from Casalderrey-Solana (2016) become important. The work enables future incorporation of holographic three-jet events into the ensemble.
