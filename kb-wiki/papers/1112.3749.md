# Triaxial projected shell model study of chiral rotation in odd-odd nuclei

> Chiral rotation observed in $^{128}$Cs is studied using the newly developed microscopic triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. The observed energy levels and the electromagnetic transition probabilities of the nearly degenerate chiral dipole bands in this isotope are well reproduced by the present model. This demonstrates the broad applicability of the TPSM approach, based on a schematic interaction and angular-momentum projection technique, to explain a variety of low- and high-spin phenomena in triaxial rotating nuclei.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, using a schematic interaction and angular-momentum projection technique, reproduces the observed energy levels and electromagnetic transition probabilities of the nearly degenerate chiral dipole bands in \(^{128}\)Cs, though the calculated B(E2) values show only a marginal drop compared to the experimental data due to the mean-field being held fixed for all projected states.

## Method

The TPSM basis space for odd-odd nuclei was constructed from one-neutron and one-proton quasiparticle configurations built on a triaxial Nilsson+BCS vacuum. Three-dimensional angular-momentum projection was applied to each intrinsic state to restore rotational symmetry, and the shell model Hamiltonian (containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions) was diagonalized in the projected basis. Electromagnetic transition probabilities B(E2) and B(M1) were then computed directly from the laboratory-frame projected wave functions using effective charges of 1.6e for protons and 0.6e for neutrons, and free gyromagnetic factors damped by 0.85 for the spin contributions.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters used for \(^{128}\)Cs: \(\epsilon = 0.220\) and \(\epsilon' = 0.14\), corresponding to \(\gamma \sim 30^\circ\)
- Quasiparticle energy of the lowest intrinsic configuration: 1.34 MeV; a crossing configuration at 1.77 MeV appears at higher spins
- For \(\epsilon'\) larger than 0.125, the energy difference \(\Delta E\) between the two dipole bands becomes quite small for all angular-momentum states
- Experimental B(E2) for the yrast band at I=13: \(48^{+21}_{-11}\) W.u. (theory: 22.94 W.u.); at I=20: \(20^{+5}_{-3}\) W.u. (theory: 22.40 W.u.)
- Experimental B(E2) for the side band at I=13: \(93^{+63}_{-24}\) W.u. (theory: 32.46 W.u.); at I=16: \(13.5^{+6.0}_{-5.9}\) W.u. (theory: 14.18 W.u.)
- Monopole-pairing force constants: \(G_M^\nu = [20.12 - 13.13 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1}\), \(G_M^\pi = 20.12 A^{-1}\); quadrupole pairing strength \(G_Q = 0.16 G_M\)

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach, previously applied to even-even and odd-mass triaxial nuclei (Y. Sun 2000; J. A. Sheikh 2008, 2009, 2010), to odd-odd nuclei for the first time, using \(^{128}\)Cs as a test case. It addresses discrepancies found in earlier two-quasiparticle triaxial rotor (2QPTR) calculations (E. Grodner 2006), which showed an opposite trend of increasing B(E2) with spin compared to the measured drop, by providing a microscopic treatment of the core rather than a fixed triaxial rotor. The results enable future studies of excited configurations requiring broken proton and neutron pairs in odd-odd nuclei, and demonstrate that the TPSM approach can describe both low- and high-spin phenomena in triaxial rotating nuclei.
