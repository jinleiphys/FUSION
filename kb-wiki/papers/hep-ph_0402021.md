# Pion Velocity near the Chiral Phase Transition

> We study the pion velocity near the critical temperature $T_c$ of chiral symmetry restoration in QCD. Using the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model as the effective field theory, where the chiral symmetry restoration is realized as the vector manifestation (VM), we show that the pion velocity for $T \to T_c$ receives neither quantum nor (thermal) hadronic corrections at the critical temperature even when we start from the bare theory with Lorentz symmetry breaking. We show that this is related to a new fixed point structure originated in the VM. Further we match at a matching scale the axial-vector current correlator in the HLS with the one in the operator product expansion for QCD, and present the matching condition to determine the bare pion velocity. We find that the pion velocity is close to the speed of light, $v_π(T) = 0.83 - 0.99$.

## Key claim

Using the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model with vector manifestation (VM), the pion velocity near the critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration receives neither quantum nor thermal hadronic corrections, even when starting from a bare theory with Lorentz symmetry breaking; the physical pion velocity at \(T_c\) equals the bare pion velocity determined by matching to QCD, yielding a value close to the speed of light.

## Method

The author starts from a Lorentz-non-invariant HLS Lagrangian at leading order, with separate temporal and spatial decay constants for the pion and the \(\sigma\) field (the longitudinal vector meson). The VM conditions \((g_L, a^t, a^s) \to (0,1,1)\) for \(T \to T_c\) are imposed, which enforce equality of the pion and \(\sigma\) velocities. The bare pion velocity is then determined by matching the axial-vector current correlator from the HLS model to the operator product expansion (OPE) for QCD at a matching scale \(\Lambda\), using a dilute-gas approximation for thermal matrix elements that includes both pions and longitudinal vector mesons near \(T_c\).

## Key numbers

* Bare pion velocity deviation: \(\delta_{\rm bare}(T_c) = 0.0061 - 0.29\), for matching scale \(\Lambda = 0.8 - 1.1\) GeV, QCD scale \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.30 - 0.45\) GeV, and critical temperature \(T_c = 0.15 - 0.20\) GeV.
* Physical pion velocity at chiral restoration: \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\) (in units of the speed of light).
* Hadronic thermal correction to the pion velocity away from \(T_c\): \(v_\pi^2(T) \simeq V_\pi^2 - N_f \frac{2\pi^2}{15}\frac{T^4}{(F_\pi^t)^2 M_\rho^2}\) for \(T < T_c\).
* Intrinsic temperature dependence of the bare pion velocity starts at \(\mathcal{O}(T^6)\) from the matching condition.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) framework developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and Harada, Kim, and Rho (2003) for hot/dense matter, where the vector meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion at the critical point. It contrasts with the chiral perturbation theory result of Son and Stephanov (2002), who found that the pion velocity vanishes as \(T \to T_c\) when only pions are considered as relevant degrees of freedom. The non-renormalization property shown here enables a direct determination of the physical pion velocity from the bare HLS Lagrangian via Wilsonian matching, and suggests that experimental measurements could distinguish between \(v_\pi \sim 1\) and \(v_\pi \to 0\) near the chiral phase transition.
