# The observation of light nuclei at ALICE and the X(3872) conundrum

> The new data reported by ALICE on the production of light nuclei with pT < 10 GeV in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV are used to compute an order-of-magnitude estimate of the expected production cross sections of light nuclei in proton-proton collisions at high transverse momenta. We compare the hypertriton, helium-3 and deuteron production cross sections to that of X(3872), measured in prompt pp collisions by CMS. The results we find suggest a different production mechanism for the X(3872), making questionable any loosely bound molecule interpretation.

## Key claim
The production cross sections of light nuclei (deuteron, helium-3, hypertriton) in proton-proton collisions, extrapolated from ALICE lead-lead data using a Glauber model with no medium effects, fall short of the X(3872) production cross section measured by CMS by two to three orders of magnitude, making a loosely bound molecule interpretation of the X(3872) questionable.

## Method
The authors use ALICE data on light nuclei production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and rescale them to pp collisions using a Glauber model, assuming each nucleus-nucleus collision is an independent product of N_coll proton-proton collisions. They then compare the estimated pp cross sections for deuteron, helium-3, and hypertriton to the prompt X(3872) production cross section measured by CMS in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV. Exponential and blast-wave fits are applied to the light nuclei data to extrapolate to the high pT region where the X(3872) is observed.

## Key numbers
- The X(3872) production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is compared to light nuclei cross sections; the extrapolated hypertriton production cross section in pp collisions falls short by about 2 to 3 orders of magnitude relative to the X(3872) production.
- The deuteron nuclear modification factor R_CP in Pb-Pb collisions becomes larger than unity for pT greater than about 2.5 GeV, with R_CP = 1.7 at pT = 3.1 GeV.
- An independent estimate of R_AA for deuteron finds it exceeds 1 at pT = 2.1 GeV and reaches 5 at pT = 4.3 GeV.
- The ALICE Pb-Pb data used are from the 0-10% centrality class with N_coll = 1518, and the 0-20% centrality class with N_coll = 1226.
- The inelastic pp cross section at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is taken as 73 mb, and a rescaling factor of 1.1 is applied to compare with sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV data.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of hadron molecule production suppression in high-energy pp collisions first raised by Bignamini et al., and directly addresses the conundrum of the strong production of the X(3872) at the LHC despite its small binding energy. The authors use the coalescence model and the nuclear modification factor to argue that medium effects in Pb-Pb collisions enhance light nuclei production, making the Glauber rescaling an overestimate of the true pp cross section. The paper motivates future experimental searches for deuteron and hypertriton in pp collisions at high pT by ALICE and LHCb during Run II to enable a definitive comparison with the X(3872).
