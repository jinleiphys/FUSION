# Testing the strength of the $\text{U}_A(1)$ anomaly at the chiral phase transition in two-flavour QCD

> We study the thermal transition of QCD with two degenerate light flavours by lattice simulations using $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson quarks. Particular emphasis lies on the pattern of chiral symmetry restoration, which we probe via the static screening correlators. On $32^3$ volumes we observe that the screening masses in transverse iso-vector vector and axial-vector channels become degenerate at the transition temperature. The splitting between the screening masses in iso-vector scalar and pseudoscalar channels is strongly reduced compared to the splitting at zero temperature and is actually consistent with zero within uncertainties. In this proceedings article we extend our studies to matrix elements and iso-singlet correlation functions. Furthermore, we present results on larger volumes, including first results at the physical pion mass.

## Key claim
The paper finds that at the chiral phase transition temperature in two-flavour QCD, the effective restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry is not yet complete for the quark masses studied, but a chiral extrapolation of the screening-mass difference between pseudoscalar and scalar channels suggests that the breaking may be weak or even restored in the chiral limit. However, results on larger volumes show a tendency toward stronger breaking, and the chiral extrapolation on the larger volumes is not yet possible due to the lack of a third quark mass.

## Method
The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate flavours of O(a)-improved Wilson quarks on 32^3 and 48^3 volumes, varying the temperature by changing the lattice coupling at fixed temporal extent N_t=16. They compute static screening correlators in spatial directions for iso-vector (pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, axial-vector) and iso-singlet (pseudoscalar, scalar) channels, extracting screening masses and renormalised matrix elements to probe the pattern of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers
- For scan C1 (m_pi ≈ 300 MeV, 32^3 volume): critical temperature T_c = 211(6) MeV at β_c = 5.405.
- For scan D1 (m_pi ≈ 220 MeV, 32^3 volume): critical temperature T_c = 190(12) MeV at β_c = 5.340.
- For scan B1_κ (m_pi ≈ 485 MeV, 32^3 volume): critical temperature T_c = 232(19) MeV at β_c = 5.465.
- For scan E2 (m_pi ≈ 135 MeV, 48^3 volume): critical temperature T_c ≈ 183 MeV at β_c = 5.317.
- The screening-mass difference ΔM_PS (pseudoscalar minus scalar) at T_c on 32^3 volumes is non-zero for all quark masses studied, but decreases toward the chiral limit; a linear chiral extrapolation gives a value consistent with zero within uncertainties, indicating weak U_A(1) breaking.
- On 48^3 volumes, ΔM_PS at T_c tends to be larger than on 32^3 volumes for the same quark masses, suggesting finite-size effects that strengthen the breaking.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Brandt et al., 2016) to larger volumes, physical pion masses, matrix elements, and iso-singlet correlation functions. It complements other lattice studies using staggered (Cheng et al., 2010), domain-wall (Bazavov et al., 2012; Bhattacharya et al., 2014; Cossu et al., 2014), and overlap (Cossu et al., 2013; Tomiya et al., 2014) fermion formulations, which also find non-zero U_A(1) breaking at T_c for non-zero quark masses. The results are relevant for determining the order and universality class of the chiral phase transition in the chiral limit, where the strength of U_A(1) breaking determines whether the transition is in the O(4) or U(2)×U(2) universality class (Pisarski and Wilczek, 1984; Butti et al., 2003; Pelissetto and Vicari, 2013). The next steps require higher statistics for the iso-singlet correlators and additional quark masses on the larger volumes to enable a reliable chiral extrapolation.
