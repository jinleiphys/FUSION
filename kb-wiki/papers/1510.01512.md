# Diffractive $ρ$ production at small $x$ in future Electron - Ion Colliders

> The future Electron - Ion ($eA$) Collider is expected to probe the high energy regime of the QCD dynamics, with the exclusive vector meson production cross section being one of the most promising observables. In this paper we complement previous studies of exclusive processes presenting a comprehensive analysis of diffractive $ρ$ production at small $x$. We compute the coherent and incoherent cross sections taking into account non-linear QCD dynamical effects and considering different models for the dipole - proton scattering amplitude and for the vector meson wave function. The dependence of these cross sections with the energy, photon virtuality, nuclear mass number and squared momentum transfer is analysed in detail. Moreover, we compare the non-linear predictions with those obtained in the linear regime. Finally, we also estimate the exclusive photon, $J/Ψ$ and $ϕ$ production and compare with the results obtained for $ρ$ production. Our results demonstrate that the analysis of diffractive $ρ$ production in future electron - ion colliders will be important to understand the non-linear QCD dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that diffractive ρ production in future electron-ion colliders will be an important observable for understanding non-linear QCD dynamics, but the sensitivity to saturation effects is strongly dependent on photon virtuality and nuclear mass number, with the clearest signatures appearing at small Q² and large nuclei where the non-linear effects are amplified.

## Method

The authors computed coherent and incoherent cross sections for diffractive ρ production in eA collisions using the color dipole formalism, incorporating non-linear QCD effects through the dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude derived from the Glauber-Gribov approach. They considered two models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude (rcBK and bCGC) and two models for the ρ meson wave function (Boosted Gaussian and Gaus-LC), analyzing the dependence on energy W, photon virtuality Q², nuclear mass number A, and squared momentum transfer t.

## Key numbers

- For light nuclei (Ca), bCGC predictions are approximately 15% larger than rcBK predictions for the cross sections
- The energy dependence slope δ for ep cross sections ranges from δ ≈ 0.22 at small Q² (soft regime) to δ ≈ 0.8 at large Q² (hard regime)
- The dips in coherent differential cross sections appear in the range |t| ≤ 0.3 GeV², with positions increasing with atomic mass number
- Analysis restricted to Q² ≤ 10 GeV² and center-of-mass energies W of order 100 GeV
- For incoherent predictions, results are only presented for |t| ≥ 0.03 GeV² due to model limitations at very small momentum transfer

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive J/Ψ and φ production in eA collisions (Goncalves et al., 2013; Caldwell et al., 2010; Lappi et al., 2010) by providing the first comprehensive predictions for ρ production including t-distributions, which probe the spatial distribution of gluons and fluctuations of nuclear color fields. The results enable future experimental separation of coherent and incoherent processes at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and LHeC, with the comparison between ρ and J/Ψ production allowing systematic study of the transition between linear and non-linear QCD regimes.
