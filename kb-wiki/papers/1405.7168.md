# Constraints on millicharged neutrinos via analysis of data from atomic ionizations with germanium detectors at sub-keV sensitivities

> With the advent of detectors with sub-keV sensitivities, atomic ionization has been identified as a promising avenue to probe possible neutrino electromagnetic properties. The interaction cross-sections induced by millicharged neutrinos are evaluated with the ab-initio multi-configuration relativistic random-phase approximation. There is significant enhancement at atomic binding energies compared to that when the electrons are taken as free particles. Positive signals would distinctly manifest as peaks at specific energies with known intensity ratios. Selected reactor neutrino data with germanium detectors at analysis threshold as low as 300 eV are studied. No such signatures are observed, and a combined limit on the neutrino charge fraction of | \numq | < 1.0 X 10^{-12} at 90% confidence level is derived.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new direct laboratory limit on the neutrino millicharge by analyzing reactor neutrino data with germanium detectors at sub-keV thresholds, finding no evidence for the characteristic atomic ionization signatures that would appear as peaks at specific binding energies with known intensity ratios.

## Method

The interaction cross-sections for millicharged neutrinos with germanium atoms were calculated using the Multi-Configuration Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation (MCRRPA), an ab-initio approach that accounts for atomic many-body effects and relativistic corrections. These cross-sections were then compared with reactor neutrino data from the TEXONO experiment using point-contact germanium detectors with an analysis threshold of 300 eV, as well as re-analyzed data from previous TEXONO and GEMMA experiments. The reactor ON minus OFF residual spectra were fitted to the predicted millicharge signal shapes to extract limits.

## Key numbers

- Combined limit on neutrino charge fraction: |delta_Q| < 1.0 x 10^{-12} at 90% confidence level from all reactor neutrino data analyzed
- TEXONO point-contact Ge detectors (124.2 kg-days ON, 70.3 kg-days OFF, threshold 300 eV): |delta_Q| < 2.1 x 10^{-12} at 90% CL
- GEMMA 1.5 kg Ge (1133.4 kg-days ON, 280.4 kg-days OFF, threshold 2.8 keV): |delta_Q| < 1.1 x 10^{-12} at 90% CL using MCRRPA analysis
- TEXONO 1 kg Ge (570.7 kg-days ON, 127.8 kg-days OFF, threshold 12 keV): |delta_Q| < 8.8 x 10^{-12} at 90% CL using MCRRPA analysis
- Projected sensitivity for next-generation experiments (flux 2.7 x 10^{13} cm^{-2} s^{-1}, 800/200 kg-days, threshold 100 eV): |delta_Q| ~ 6 x 10^{-14}
- MCRRPA calculations reproduce measured photo-absorption cross-section of solid Ge to within 5% accuracy at energy transfer above 100 eV

## Context

This work extends previous direct laboratory searches for neutrino millicharge by Studenikin (2013) and Gninenko, Krasnikov, and Rubbia (2005), which used simplistic scaling relationships from neutrino magnetic moment results and neglected atomic effects. The MCRRPA approach improves upon the Free Electron Approximation and Equivalent Photon Approximation methods, showing an order-of-magnitude enhancement in cross-section at atomic binding energies compared to free-electron treatments. The results enable future searches with lower detector thresholds (targeting 100 eV) and higher statistics to probe millicharge values down to ~6 x 10^{-14}, and the methodology can be extended to study neutrino electromagnetic interactions at different kinematics regimes and possible electromagnetic interactions with WIMPs.
