# Pairing and continuum effects on low-frequency quadrupole vibrations in deformed Mg isotopes close to the neutron drip line

> Low-frequency quadrupole vibrational modes in deformed $^{36,38,40}$Mg close to the neutron drip line are studied by means of the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation based on the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism. Strongly collective $K^π=0^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ excitation modes carrying 10-20 Weisskopf units in the intrinsic isoscalar quadrupole transition strengths are obtained at about 3 MeV. There are two reasons for the enhancement of the transition strengths. First, the quasiparticle wave functions generating these modes possess spatially very extended structure. The asymptotic selection rules characterizing the $β$ and $γ$ vibrations in stable deformed nuclei are thus strongly violated. Second, the dynamic pairing effects act strongly to enhance the collectivity of these modes. It is suggested that the lowest $K^π=0^{+}$ collective mode is a particularly sensitive indicator of the nature of pairing correlations in deformed nuclei close to the neutron drip line.

## Key claim

Low-frequency quadrupole vibrational modes in deformed $^{36,38,40}$Mg near the neutron drip line acquire strongly collective character (10-20 Weisskopf units) at about 3 MeV, but this enhancement depends critically on two conditions: the spatially extended structure of quasiparticle wave functions that violates asymptotic selection rules, and the strong action of dynamic pairing effects.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation directly in cylindrical coordinate space using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with $\beta_2=0.28$, treating pairing self-consistently with a density-dependent contact interaction of surface type ($\eta=1.0$). They then performed quasiparticle-random-phase approximation calculations in the standard matrix formulation, using a Skyrme-type residual interaction for the particle-hole channel and the same contact interaction for the particle-particle channel, with renormalization factors to place spurious modes at zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron average pairing gaps: $\overline{\Delta}_\nu = 1.81$ MeV ($^{36}$Mg), 1.98 MeV ($^{38}$Mg), 2.14 MeV ($^{40}$Mg); proton pairing gaps vanish for all three isotopes
- Neutron root-mean-square radii: 3.74 fm ($^{36}$Mg), 3.86 fm ($^{38}$Mg), 3.99 fm ($^{40}$Mg); proton radii remain nearly constant at about 3.06-3.10 fm, giving a neutron skin of about 0.9 fm in $^{40}$Mg
- Lowest $K^\pi=0^+$ mode in $^{40}$Mg at 3.2 MeV carries $B(Q^{\mathrm{IS}}2)=182$ fm$^4$ (about 22 Weisskopf units, where 1 W.u. $\simeq$ 8.1 fm$^4$ for $^{40}$Mg)
- Lowest $K^\pi=0^+$ mode in $^{36}$Mg at 3.4 MeV carries $B(Q^{\mathrm{IS}}2)=170$ fm$^4$ (about 24 W.u., where 1 W.u. $\simeq$ 7.1 fm$^4$ for $^{36}$Mg)
- Lowest $K^\pi=2^+$ mode in $^{40}$Mg at 2.9 MeV carries $B(Q^{\mathrm{IS}}2)=147$ fm$^4$
- Neutron chemical potentials: $\lambda_\nu = -2.09$ MeV ($^{36}$Mg), $-1.15$ MeV ($^{38}$Mg), $-0.41$ MeV ($^{40}$Mg)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of octupole excitations in neutron-rich sulfur isotopes (Yoshida 2005) by including pairing correlations, which were absent in that earlier study. It also goes beyond calculations using the BCS approximation for gamma vibrations in $^{38}$Mg (Hagino 2004) by employing the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism, which properly treats continuum coupling effects that the BCS approximation cannot handle due to the unphysical nucleon gas problem (Dobaczewski 1984). The results suggest that the lowest $K^\pi=0^+$ collective mode can serve as a sensitive indicator of pairing correlations in deformed nuclei near the neutron drip line, and the approach enables future studies of how continuum and pairing effects modify collective excitations in medium-mass drip-line nuclei.
