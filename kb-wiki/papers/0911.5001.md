# Two-photon Exchange Corrections to Single Spin Asymmetry of Neutron and $^3$He

> In a simple hadronic model, the two-photon exchange contributions to the single spin asymmetries for the nucleon and the $^3$He are estimated. The results show that the elastic contributions of two-photon exchange to the the single spin asymmetries for the nucleon are rather small while those for the $^3$He are relatively large. Besides the strong angular dependence, the two-photon contributions to the single spin asymmetry for the $^3$He are very sensitive to the momentum transfer.

## Key claim

In a simple hadronic model that includes only elastic (nucleon intermediate state) contributions, the two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to the single spin asymmetry (SSA) for the nucleon are rather small, while those for the \(^3\)He are relatively large and strongly dependent on both scattering angle and momentum transfer, particularly in the low \(Q^2 < 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) region relevant to the Jefferson Lab experiment E05-015.

## Method

The authors compute the TPE amplitude for elastic electron-nucleon and electron-\(^3\)He scattering using a hadronic model with only the nucleon as the intermediate state. The loop integrals are evaluated analytically in terms of Passarino-Veltman functions using FeynCalc, then numerically with LoopTools. The electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and \(^3\)He are parameterized as sums of monopoles, and the infrared divergences in the unpolarized cross section are canceled by standard Mo and Tsai corrections, while the SSA itself is infrared finite.

## Key numbers

- For \(Q^2 = 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\), the TPE contribution to the SSA for the proton reaches about \(1\%\) and for the neutron about \(0.25\%\) in the backward limit; for \(^3\)He the SSA is negative and reaches a minimum of approximately \(1.6\%\) at \(\theta \approx 135^\circ\).
- For \(Q^2 = 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\), the absolute values of the TPE corrections to the SSA for the proton (neutron) are less than \(0.2\%\) (\(0.15\%\)) at forward angles; for \(^3\)He the minimum value is \(15\%\).
- For \(Q^2 = 1.0\ \text{GeV}^2\), the absolute values for the proton (neutron) are more than \(0.4\%\) (about \(0.4\%\)) at forward angles; for \(^3\)He the minimum value is \(3\%\).
- The SSA for \(^3\)He obtained directly from the TPE amplitude shows a deep dip at \(Q^2 \simeq 0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\) with a minimum of about \(3\%\) for \(\theta = \pi/10\), about \(-8\%\) at \(Q^2 \simeq 0.43\ \text{GeV}^2\) for \(\theta = \pi/3\), and about \(-11\%\) at \(Q^2 \simeq 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\) for \(\theta = 2\pi/3\).
- The effective polarizations inside \(^3\)He are taken as \(P_n = 0.86 \pm 0.02\) and \(P_p = -0.028 \pm 0.004\) from prior work (PRC-2310).

## Context

This work extends earlier TPE calculations for the proton (PRC-034612) to the neutron and to the \(^3\)He nucleus, motivated by the Jefferson Lab experiment E05-005 designed to measure the neutron SSA using a polarized \(^3\)He target. The results show that the simple convolution approximation relating the \(^3\)He SSA to nucleon SSAs (PRC-064317) fails for \(Q^2 > 0.3\ \text{GeV}^2\), and the authors recommend direct TPE calculations or plane wave impulse approximation methods for future studies. The strong sensitivity of the \(^3\)He SSA to the zeros of its charge and magnetic form factors at \(Q^2 \simeq 0.45\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 \simeq 0.7\ \text{GeV}^2\) (NPA-596) is identified as a key feature.
