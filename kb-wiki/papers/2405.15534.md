# $K^-d\rightarrowπΛN$ reaction with in-flight kaons for studying the $ΛN$ interaction

> The $ΛN$ invariant mass spectra for the reactions $K^-d\rightarrowπ^-Λp$ and $K^-d\rightarrowπ^0Λn$ are calculated for experimental study of isospin symmetry breaking in the $ΛN$ scattering at low energies, the difference in the scattering lengths and effective ranges of $Λp$ and $Λn$ systems. The calculations are performed for in-flight kaons with a momentum of 1000 MeV/c with employing partial wave analysis up to the p-wave for meson-baryon amplitudes and the spin-flip term for baryon-baryon amplitudes. Kinematic selection is utilized to suppress the background processes by selecting forward-emitting pions and higher momentum nucleons. It is worth noting that isospin symmetry breaking in the $ΛN$ system can be extracted from the difference of the $ΛN$ invariant mass spectra between the $K^-d\rightarrowπ^-Λp$ and $K^-d\rightarrowπ^0Λn$ reactions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the $\Lambda N$ invariant mass spectra for the $K^-d\rightarrow\pi^-\Lambda p$ and $K^-d\rightarrow\pi^0\Lambda n$ reactions with in-flight kaons at 1000 MeV/c, claiming that isospin symmetry breaking in the $\Lambda N$ system can be extracted from the difference of these spectra. The key hedge is that the spin-singlet component contributes very little due to kinematic selection for forward-going pions, so the analysis focuses only on the spin-triplet state ratios $a^t_{n/p}$ and $r^t_{n/p}$.

## Method

The authors compute the $K^-d\rightarrow\pi\Lambda N$ reaction amplitude up to two-step scattering processes using partial wave analysis up to p-wave for meson-baryon amplitudes and including the spin-flip term for baryon-baryon amplitudes. The amplitude includes impulse, hyperon exchange, $\Sigma$ exchange, $\bar{K}$ exchange, and $\pi$ exchange processes, with the deuteron wave function incorporated via integrals over intermediate momenta. Kinematic selection is applied by choosing forward-emitting pions ($0.9\le\cos\theta\le1.0$ in the c.m. frame) and higher momentum nucleons to suppress background processes.

## Key numbers

- Incident kaon momentum: 1000 MeV/c for the main calculations; comparison with experiment uses 760 MeV/c incident momentum.
- $\Lambda p$ scattering lengths from experiment: $a^s_{\Lambda p} = -2.43^{+0.16}_{-0.25}$ fm, $a^t_{\Lambda p} = -1.56^{+0.19}_{-0.22}$ fm.
- $\Lambda p$ effective ranges: $r^s_{\Lambda p} = 2.21^{+0.16}_{-0.36}$ fm, $r^t_{\Lambda p} = 3.7^{+0.6}_{-0.6}$ fm.
- For $p_\text{cut}=75$ MeV/c, a gradual peak is seen around 2065 MeV in the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass; for $p_\text{cut}=150$ MeV/c, a peak is seen around 2060 MeV.
- The experimental incident flux used for scaling is 7.1 $\mu$b.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for stopped kaons by Iizawa (2022) and Jido (2009) to in-flight kaons at 1000 MeV/c, enabling experimental study of isospin symmetry breaking in $\Lambda N$ scattering. The calculations are compared with experimental data from Braun (1977) for incident momenta 680-840 MeV/c, showing quantitative agreement. The results enable future experiments to extract the difference in scattering lengths and effective ranges between $\Lambda p$ and $\Lambda n$ systems by comparing the two reaction channels.
