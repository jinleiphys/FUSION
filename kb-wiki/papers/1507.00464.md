# Grand canonical potential of a magnetized neutron gas

> We compute the effective action for stationary and spatially constant magnetic fields, when coupled anomalously to charge neutral fermions, by integrating out the fermions. From this the grand canonical partition function and potential of the fermions and fields are computed. This also takes care of magnetic field dependent vacuum corrections to the grand canonical potential. Possible applications to neutron stars are indicated.

## Key claim

The paper computes the full magnetic field dependence of the grand canonical potential for a gas of charge-neutral fermions (neutrons) with an anomalous magnetic moment coupling, including vacuum (zero-point energy) contributions that previous studies had omitted; the vacuum corrections are found to be small for the free neutron magnetic moment but could become significant if medium effects modify the coupling strength.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian describing neutrons coupled anomalously to a stationary, spatially constant magnetic field via a Pauli-type term \(-\frac{g_b}{2}F^{\mu\nu}\sigma_{\mu\nu}\). They integrate out the fermion fields in the path integral formulation of the grand canonical partition function at finite temperature and chemical potential, reducing the problem to a functional determinant. The resulting grand potential is expanded in powers of the magnetic field \(B\), and ultraviolet divergences in the vacuum contribution are removed via minimal subtraction, with the renormalization condition that setting the anomalous coupling \(g_b=0\) recovers the free Maxwell term \(\frac{1}{2}B^2\).

## Key numbers

* The free neutron anomalous coupling strength is \(g_b^{(0)} \approx 0.0305\) fm, derived from \(g_b = \frac{1}{2} \times 1.913 \mu_N\).
* The expansion of the vacuum contribution to the grand potential yields a \(B^2\) coefficient \(\left(\frac{g_b^{2}\Lambda^2}{4\pi^2}+\frac{g_b^2m^2\left(1-\log[4]\right)}{8\pi^2}+\frac{g_b^2m^2}{4\pi^2}\log\left[\frac{m}{\Lambda}\right]\right)\) and a \(B^4\) coefficient \(\left(\frac{g_b^4\log[2]}{24\pi^2}+\frac{g_b^4}{24\pi^2}\log\left[\frac{m}{\Lambda}\right]\right)\), where \(\Lambda\) is a momentum cut-off.
* After renormalization, the zero-temperature grand potential (Eq. OB) includes a term \(\frac{B^2}{2}\left(1-\frac{g_b^2m^2\left(1-\log[4]\right)}{4\pi^2}-\frac{g_b^4B^2\log[2]}{12\pi^2}\right)\).
* The vacuum contribution to the thermodynamic potential is shown in Figure 1 to be small relative to the bare \(B^2/2\) term for the free value of \(g_b\).
* The expansion is valid for \(B < 1\) fm\(^{-2}\) (Heaviside-Lorentz units).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of magnetized fermion systems by including both the anomalous magnetic moment coupling and the vacuum (zero-point energy) contributions to the grand potential, which were neglected in earlier calculations by Cangemi (1997) and Ferrer (2010). It builds on the formalism of Kapusta and Gale (2006) for finite-temperature field theory and on the single-particle spectrum derived by Diener (PhD thesis). The results enable a more complete computation of equilibrium properties of neutron-rich matter in strong magnetic fields, as found in magnetars, and may help resolve controversies about anisotropic pressure raised by Ferrer (2010) and Potel (2015). The authors also note that the neutron dipole moment may increase with density by about 50% based on experimental evidence from Stone (2005), suggesting that vacuum corrections could become more important in dense matter.
