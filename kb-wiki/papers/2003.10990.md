# Properties of spherical and deformed nuclei using regularized pseudopotentials in nuclear DFT

> We developed new parameterizations of local regularized finite-range pseudopotentials up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), used as generators of nuclear density functionals. When supplemented with zero-range spin-orbit and density-dependent terms, they provide a correct single-reference description of binding energies and radii of spherical and deformed nuclei. We compared the obtained results to experimental data and discussed benchmarks against the standard well-established Gogny D1S functional.

## Key claim

The authors developed new parameterizations of local regularized finite-range pseudopotentials up to N3LO that, when combined with zero-range spin-orbit and density-dependent terms, provide a correct single-reference description of binding energies and radii of spherical and deformed nuclei; however, the slope of the symmetry energy coefficient had to be fixed to the low value of L=15 MeV because it proved very difficult to adjust parameters leading to the commonly accepted range of 40-70 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed nuclear density functionals using local regularized finite-range pseudopotentials with a Gaussian regulator, expanded up to N3LO, and supplemented them with zero-range spin-orbit and density-dependent terms. They performed a three-step adjustment procedure: first, preliminary fits to detect pitfalls; second, exploratory adjustments with fixed infinite-nuclear-matter properties; and third, final optimizations allowing small tolerance intervals around target values. The penalty function included constraints from infinite nuclear matter properties, potential energies in spin-isospin channels, neutron matter equation of state, polarized matter, pairing gaps, binding energies of 17 spherical nuclei, proton rms radii, central densities, surface energy coefficient, and vector pairing coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- RMS binding-energy residual for REG6d (N3LO, m*/m=0.85): 1.458 MeV for 214 spherical nuclei, compared to 2.582 MeV for Gogny D1S and 1.900 MeV for UNEDF0
- RMS binding-energy residual for REG6a (N3LO, m*/m=0.70): 1.717 MeV
- Saturation density fixed at rho_sat = 0.158 fm^-3 with tolerance 0.003 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon in infinite symmetric matter fixed at E/A = -16.0 MeV with tolerance 0.3 MeV
- Compression modulus fixed at K_infinity = 230 MeV with tolerance 5 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient fixed at J = 29.0 MeV with tolerance 0.5 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy fixed at L = 15.00 MeV with tolerance 0.05 MeV
- Regulator range a = 1.50 fm for the optimal REG6d pseudopotential
- Charge radii residuals of order 0.02 fm, typical for Skyrme-like EDFs

## Context

This work extends the program of building nuclear density functionals from regularized finite-range pseudopotentials, previously developed by Dobaczewski (2012) and Bennaceur (2017), by adjusting parameters up to N3LO and benchmarking against the well-established Gogny D1S functional from Berger (1991). The approach aims to overcome limitations of Skyrme-type EDFs, whose limits were reached as shown by Kortelainen (2014), and to provide a foundation for future beyond-mean-field multi-reference calculations, though the current density-dependent term makes these particular parameterizations unsuitable for that purpose. The results enable systematic improvement of nuclear DFT through order-by-order expansion, with the N3LO pseudopotentials showing consistently better description of binding energies and radii than lower orders.
