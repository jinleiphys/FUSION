# Chiral extrapolations for nucleon electric charge radii

> Lattice simulations for the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon yield insights into the internal structure of hadrons. The logarithmic divergence of the charge radius in the chiral limit poses an interesting challenge in achieving reliable predictions from finite-volume lattice simulations. Recent results near the physical pion mass are examined in order to confront the issue of how the chiral regime is approached. The electric charge radius of the nucleon presents a forum for achieving consistent finite-volume corrections. Newly-developed techniques within the framework of chiral effective field theory are used to achieve a robust extrapolation of the electric charge radius to the physical pion mass, and to infinite volume. The chiral extrapolations exhibit considerable finite-volume dependence; lattice box sizes of L > 7 fm are required in order to achieve a direct lattice simulation result within 2% of the infinite-volume value at the physical point. Predictions of the volume-dependence are provided to guide the interpretation of future lattice results.

# Chiral extrapolations for nucleon electric charge radii

## Key claim

The chiral extrapolation of the nucleon electric charge radius to the physical pion mass and infinite volume, using finite-range regularization within chiral effective field theory, shows that lattice box sizes of L > 7 fm are required to achieve a direct simulation result within 2% of the infinite-volume value at the physical point; however, a discrepancy remains between the extrapolated result and the experimental value, possibly due to excited state contamination, the use of only two quark flavors, or neglected O(a) effects.

## Method

The authors applied finite-range regularization (FRR) chiral effective field theory to lattice QCD results from the QCDSF Collaboration for the isovector nucleon electric charge radius, using a dipole regulator. They corrected the electric form factors from finite volume to infinite volume by subtracting finite-volume corrections computed from chiral loop integrals, then extracted charge radii using two different Q^2 extrapolation ansatze (dipole and inverse quadratic). A renormalization flow analysis was performed to determine the optimal regularization scale, and chiral extrapolations were carried out to the physical pion mass for various lattice volumes.

## Key numbers

- The optimal regularization scale for the dipole regulator was found to be Lambda_scale_dip = 1.08 (+0.58, -0.32) GeV, determined from a chi^2/dof analysis of renormalization flow curves.
- The extrapolated isovector nucleon electric charge radius at the physical point, using the lightest seven lattice points (m_pi,max^2 ~ 0.48 GeV^2), was 0.753 fm^2 with a total uncertainty of 0.043 fm^2.
- Lattice box sizes of L > 7 fm are required to achieve a direct lattice simulation result within 2% of the infinite-volume value at the physical point.
- For a box size of L ~ 4 fm, the finite-volume radius was 0.745 fm, significantly below the physical value of 0.886 fm used to constrain the fit.
- The sharp cutoff regulator gave an optimal scale of Lambda_scale_sc = 0.51 (+0.17, -0.10) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous FRR chiral extrapolation techniques applied to the nucleon mass (Hall 2010, Hall 2011) and magnetic moment (Hall 2012) to the electric charge radius, using the same QCDSF lattice data (Collins 2011). The analysis addresses the challenge of the logarithmic divergence of the charge radius in the chiral limit and the associated finite-volume effects, which were previously noted by Tiburzi (2007), Hu (2007), Jiang (2008), and Hall (2012). The results provide predictions for finite-volume dependence that can guide interpretation of future lattice simulations, such as those from the PACS-CS Collaboration (Aoki 2008), and emphasize the need for chiral effective field theory corrections until very large lattice volumes become available.
