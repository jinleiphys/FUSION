# Large directed flow of open charm mesons probes the three dimensional distribution of matter in heavy ion collisions

> Thermalized matter created in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions is expected to be tilted in the reaction plane with respect to the beam axis. The most notable consequence of this forward-backward symmetry breaking is the observation of rapidity-odd directed flow for charged particles. On the other hand, the production points for heavy quarks are forward-backward symmetric and shifted in the transverse plane with respect to the fireball. The drag of heavy quarks from the asymmetrically distributed thermalized matter generates a large directed flow for heavy flavor mesons. We predict a very large rapidity odd directed flow of $D$ mesons in non-central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, $several$ $times$ $larger$ than for charged particles. A possible experimental observation of a large directed flow for heavy flavor mesons would represent an almost direct probe of the 3-dimensional distribution of matter in heavy-ion collisions.

# Large directed flow of open charm mesons probes the three dimensional distribution of matter in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors predict that the rapidity-odd directed flow $v_1$ of $D$ mesons in non-central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV is several times larger than that of charged particles, but this prediction depends on the specific choice of the drag coefficient $\gamma$ for charm quarks in the medium and on the tilt parameter $\eta_T$ of the initial fireball. The large signal arises because heavy quark production points are shifted in the transverse plane relative to the fireball center, creating a biased drag from the tilted thermalized matter.

## Method

The initial entropy density is modeled with a two-component Glauber ansatz that includes a tilted fireball profile parameterized by $\eta_T$, with the tilt magnitude extracted from d+Au collisions. The 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution (shear viscosity $\eta/s=0.08$, bulk viscosity $\zeta/s=0.04$ in the hadronic phase) is performed using the HLLE algorithm with a cross-over equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas. Charm quarks are sampled from the binary collision profile with $p_T$ spectra from p+p collisions, then evolved via Langevin dynamics with a drag coefficient $\gamma = \gamma_0 T (T/m)^x$ (with $\gamma_0=0.2$ and $x=0$ or $0.5$) through the hydrodynamic background until hadronization via Peterson fragmentation at $T=150$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The predicted $v_1$ slope at mid-rapidity for $D$ mesons is $5$ to $20$ times larger than that of charged particles, depending on the choice of parameters.
- The tilt parameter $\eta_T$ is set to $\eta_T \simeq y_{beam} - 2 = 3.36$ (with beam rapidity $y_{beam}=5.36$), and is varied between $2$ and $5.36$ to demonstrate sensitivity.
- When changing $\eta_T$ from $y_{beam}$ (small tilt) to $0.4 y_{beam}$ (large tilt), the charged particle $v_1$ changes by $\sim 0.5\%$ at $\eta=0.75$, while the heavy flavor $v_1$ at the same $\eta$ bin changes by $2-3\%$ and the $v_1$ slope increases by a factor of $3$.
- The calculation uses impact parameter $b = 8.3$ fm for 0-80% centrality Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- The drag coefficient parameters are $\gamma_0 = 0.2$ with $x = 0$ or $x = 0.5$, chosen to qualitatively describe $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ data at mid-rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the tilted fireball initial condition model of Bożek (2010), which successfully described charged particle directed flow measured by STAR (Abelev 2008) and PHOBOS (Back 2005), to heavy flavor observables. The prediction of a large $D$ meson $v_1$ is approximately three times larger than earlier transport model calculations by Bratkovskaya (2004). The measurement of heavy flavor directed flow would provide a sensitive probe of the three-dimensional distribution of matter in heavy-ion collisions and could distinguish between different saturation mechanisms for hard processes, as suggested by Adil (2005). The authors note that electromagnetic field effects (Das 2016) would affect $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with opposite sign and thus not influence the average directed flow.
