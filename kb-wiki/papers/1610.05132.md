# Radiative corrections to the magnetic moments of the proton and the neutron

> We estimate the radiative corrections of order $α/π$ to the magnetic moments of the proton and the neutron. The photon-loop diagram of the vertex-correction type is evaluated with phenomenological nucleon vector form factors. Infrared-finiteness and gauge-invariance require the inclusion of the wave-function renormalization factor from the self-energy diagram. Using recent empirical form factor parametrizations the corrections amount to $δκ_p= -3.42 \cdot 10^{-3}$ and $δκ_n= 1.34 \cdot 10^{-3}$. We study also the effects from photon-loops with internal $Δ(1232)$-isobars. For two customary versions of the $ΔNγ$-vertex and spin-3/2 propagator, these radiative corrections have values of $δκ_p^{(Δ)}= (-0.9,\, 0.0)\!\cdot\! 10^{-3}$ and $δκ_n^{(Δ)} = (1.2,\,-0.8)\!\cdot\! 10^{-3}$, respectively.

## Key claim

The radiative corrections of order α/π to the nucleon magnetic moments are estimated to be δκ_p = −3.42·10⁻³ and δκ_n = 1.34·10⁻³ using recent empirical form factor parametrizations, but these values depend on the treatment of the Δ(1232)-isobar contributions, which range from δκ_p^(Δ) = (−0.9, 0.0)·10⁻³ and δκ_n^(Δ) = (1.2, −0.8)·10⁻³ depending on the choice of vertex and propagator.

## Method

The one-photon loop diagram of the vertex-correction type was evaluated with phenomenological nucleon vector form factors (dipole, dispersion relation analysis, and recent fits including two-photon exchange corrections). Infrared-finiteness and gauge-invariance were enforced by including the wave-function renormalization factor from the self-energy diagram. Additional contributions from photon-loops with internal Δ(1232)-isobars were calculated using two customary versions of the ΔNγ-vertex and spin-3/2 propagator, with a dipole-times-exponential transition form factor.

## Key numbers

* δκ_p = −3.42·10⁻³ (radiative correction to proton magnetic moment, using the Ina form factor parametrization)
* δκ_n = 1.34·10⁻³ (radiative correction to neutron magnetic moment, using the Ina form factor parametrization)
* δκ_p^(Δ) = (−0.9, 0.0)·10⁻³ (Δ-isobar contribution to proton, for two vertex/propagator versions)
* δκ_n^(Δ) = (1.2, −0.8)·10⁻³ (Δ-isobar contribution to neutron, for two vertex/propagator versions)
* Nucleon mass M = 939 MeV; Δ(1232) mass M_Δ = 1.312 M; dipole mass Λ = 843 MeV
* Fine-structure constant α = 1/137.036; Schwinger term α/2π = 1.1614·10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger (1948) calculation of the one-loop radiative correction to the lepton magnetic moment (α/2π) to the case of composite nucleons with internal electromagnetic structure. It uses phenomenological form factor parametrizations from dispersion relation analyses and from recent fits by Ina (2015) that include two-photon exchange corrections. The results enable more precise comparisons between experimental nucleon magnetic moments and predictions from strong-interaction theories, by quantifying the electromagnetic corrections that must be subtracted.
