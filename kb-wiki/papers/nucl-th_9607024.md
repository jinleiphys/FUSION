# Translationally invariant treatment of pair correlations in nuclei: I. Spin and isospin dependent correlations

> We study the extension of our translationally invariant treatment of few-body nuclear systems to heavier nuclei. At the same time we also introduce state-dependent correlation operators. Our techniques are tailored to those nuclei that can be dealt with in $LS$ coupling, which includes all nuclei up to the shell closure at $A=40$. We study mainly $p$-shell nuclei in this paper. A detailed comparison with other microscopic many-body approaches is made, using a variety of schematic nuclear interactions. It is shown that our methodology produces very good energies, and presumably also wave functions, for medium mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper extends a translationally invariant configuration interaction (TICI) method, previously applied to few-body nuclear systems, to heavier p-shell nuclei up to A=40, while introducing state-dependent (spin- and isospin-dependent) pair correlation operators. The authors claim that their methodology produces "very good energies, and presumably also wave functions" for medium mass nuclei, but this claim is qualified by the use of schematic nuclear interactions rather than fully realistic forces, and by the restriction to nuclei that can be treated in LS coupling.

## Method

The authors use a trial wave function of the form |Ψ⟩ = Σ_{i<j} f(ij) |Ψ₀⟩, where |Ψ₀⟩ is a harmonic-oscillator shell-model Slater determinant and f(ij) is a pair correlation operator expanded in a V4 basis (central, spin-exchange, isospin-exchange, and spin-isospin exchange terms). The expectation value of the Hamiltonian is reduced to a sum of 16 topologically distinct diagrams involving up to six particles, with spin and isospin traces computed using binary representations of exchange operators, and spatial integrals evaluated by expanding correlation functions in Gaussians matched to harmonic oscillator states. The energy is obtained by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem for the Gaussian expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For the B1 interaction, the state-dependent (SD) correlation gives ground-state energies of -37.86 MeV (⁴He), -61.30 MeV (⁸Be), -103.93 MeV (¹²C), and -167.30 MeV (¹⁶O), compared to state-independent (SI) values of -37.86, -49.18, -84.91, and -145.94 MeV respectively.
- For the S3 interaction, SD energies are -28.19 MeV (⁴He), -44.26 MeV (⁸Be), -87.78 MeV (¹²C), and -164.88 MeV (¹⁶O), versus SI values of -25.41, -34.15, -71.14, and -141.64 MeV.
- For the MT V potential in ¹⁶O, the TICI2(SD) result is 973.67 MeV binding, compared to a diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) result of 1189 ± 1 MeV, representing about 82% of the DMC binding energy.
- For the B1 potential in ¹²C, TICI2(SD) gives 103.93 MeV binding, versus a variational Monte Carlo (VMC) result of 82.9 ± 0.2 MeV and an integro-differential equation approach (IDEA) range of 79.8–80.1 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator inverse length parameter α is optimized for each nucleus and interaction, ranging from 0.582 fm⁻¹ (⁸Be, MS3) to 1.078 fm⁻¹ (¹⁶O, MT V).

## Context

This work extends the translationally invariant configuration interaction (TICI) method previously developed by the same group for few-body systems (Guardiola et al., prior work) to p-shell nuclei up to A=40, introducing state-dependent correlations of V4 type. The results are compared with several other many-body approaches: Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations by Zabolitsky (1980s) and others, Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) calculations by Arias de Saavedra et al. (1996) and Krotscheck, variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations by Bishop and collaborators, and the stochastic variational method (SVM) by Varga and Suzuki. The paper enables future inclusion of three-body correlations as a systematic improvement, which the authors suggest could yield highly accurate results for medium-mass nuclei.
