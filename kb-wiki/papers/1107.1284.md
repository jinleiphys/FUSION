# Hadron interactions in lattice QCD

> Progress on the potential method, recently proposed to investigate hadron interactions in lattice QCD, is reviewed. The strategy to extract the potential in lattice QCD is explained in detail. The method is applied to extract $NN$ potentials, hyperon potentials and the meson-baryon potentials. A theoretical investigation is made to understand the origin of the repulsive core using the operator product expansion. Some recent extensions of the method are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the potential method for extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD, where a non-local, energy-independent potential is defined from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function; the method is justified below the inelastic threshold (W < 2m_N + m_pi) and the potential is scheme-dependent, meaning it depends on the choice of nucleon operator used to define the NBS wave function, though physical observables like phase shifts are scheme-independent.

## Method

The equal-time NBS wave function for two nucleons is computed from lattice QCD eigenstates, and its asymptotic behavior at large separation is shown to encode the QCD scattering phase shifts via free Schrodinger equation behavior. A non-local, energy-independent potential U(x,y) is then defined from the NBS wave function by projecting onto the space of states below the inelastic threshold, and this potential is expanded in a velocity (derivative) expansion to extract local potentials at leading order (central, spin-spin, and tensor terms) and next-to-leading order (spin-orbit term). The method is applied to NN, hyperon-nucleon, and meson-baryon systems using both quenched and full QCD simulations.

## Key numbers

- The long-range part of the nuclear force dominates at relative distance r > 2 fm, described by one-pion exchange (Yukawa, 1935).
- The medium-range part (1 fm < r < 2 fm) receives contributions from two-pion and heavy meson exchange, with spin-isospin independent attraction of about 50-100 MeV.
- The short-range part (r < 1 fm) is described by a strong repulsive core, originally introduced by Jastrow (1951).
- High-precision NN potentials fit more than 2000 data points with chi^2/dof approximately 1 at T_lab < 300 MeV (Machleidt, 1989; Machleidt and Slaus, 2001).
- The inelastic threshold for NN scattering is W_th = 2m_N + m_pi, below which the NBS wave function's asymptotic behavior is given by equations involving phase shifts delta_{lS}(k).

## Context

This work extends the Luscher method (Luscher, 1991) for extracting scattering phase shifts from finite-volume lattice QCD by instead defining a potential from the NBS wave function, enabling direct calculation of nuclear forces from first principles. It builds on the initial proposal by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2007) and subsequent developments by the HAL QCD Collaboration, and contrasts with the approach reviewed by Beane, Detmold, Orginos, and Savage (2011) which focuses on Luscher's method. The potential method enables future studies of three-nucleon forces, hyperon interactions relevant for neutron stars, and applications to charmonium-nucleon potentials and two-color QCD.
