# Elements of QED-NRQED Effective Field Theory: I. NLO scattering at leading power

> The proton radius puzzle, i.e. the large discrepancy in the extraction of the proton charge radius between regular and muonic hydrogen, challenges our understanding of the structure of the proton. It can also be an indication of a new force that couples to muons, but not to electrons. An effective field theory analysis using Non Relativistic Quantum Electrodynamics (NRQED) indicates that the muonic hydrogen result can be interpreted as a large, compared to some model estimates, muon-proton spin-independent contact interaction. The muonic hydrogen result can be tested by a muon-proton scattering experiment, MUSE, that is planned at the Paul Scherrer Institute in Switzerland. The typical momenta of the muons in this experiment are of the order of the muon mass. In this energy regime the muons are relativistic but the protons are still non-relativistic. The interaction between the muons and protons can be described by a hybrid QED-NRQED effective field theory. We present some elements of this effective field theory. In particular we consider ${\cal O}(Zα)$ scattering up to power $m^2/M^2$, where $m$ ($M$) is the muon (proton) mass and $Z=1$ for a proton, and ${\cal O}(Z^2α^2)$ scattering at leading power. We show how the former reproduces Rosenbluth scattering up to power $m^2/M^2$ and the latter the relativistic scattering off a static potential. Proton structure corrections at ${\cal O}(Z^2α^2)$ will be considered in a subsequent paper.

## Key claim

The muonic hydrogen result can be interpreted as a large muon-proton spin-independent contact interaction within an effective field theory framework, but this interpretation depends on the assumption that the proton charge radius is known from another source; the paper develops a hybrid QED-NRQED effective field theory for the kinematic regime where muons are relativistic and protons are non-relativistic, which is relevant for the planned MUSE experiment at Paul Scherrer Institute.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid effective field theory combining relativistic QED for the muon with non-relativistic NRQED for the proton, using Coulomb gauge for photon propagators. They calculated elastic lepton-proton scattering at O(Zα) up to power m²/M² (where m is muon mass and M is proton mass) and at O(Z²α²) at leading power in m/M, showing that the former reproduces Rosenbluth scattering and the latter reproduces scattering off a static Coulomb potential. The calculations used Feynman diagrams with NRQED vertices for the proton (Coulomb, Fermi, and Darwin terms) and standard QED vertices for the lepton.

## Key numbers

- The NRQED Lagrangian includes operators up to 1/M² with Wilson coefficients c_F = F₁(0) + F₂(0), c_D = F₁(0) + 2F₂(0) + 8M²F₁'(0), and c_S = 2c_F - F₁(0)
- The O(Zα) spin-averaged squared amplitude expanded to 1/M² is given by: e⁴Z²Q_ℓ²[1/q̅²][(1/q̅²)(4E² - q̅²) - 2E/M + (q̅² + c_F²(q̅² + 4E² - 4m²) + c_D(q̅² - 4E²))/(4M²)]
- The O(Z²α²) cross section at leading power is: dσ/dΩ = [4Z²α²Q_ℓ²E²(1 - v²sin²(θ/2))/q̅⁴] × [1 - αZQ_ℓ(πv sin(θ/2)(1 - sin(θ/2)))/(1 - v²sin²(θ/2))]
- The typical momentum of muons in the MUSE experiment is of order m ~ 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the NRQED framework used for muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pineda 2002, Hill 2011) to the relativistic muon regime needed for the MUSE scattering experiment, following the QED-NRQED approach suggested by Hill (2012). The paper reproduces known results including Rosenbluth scattering (Rosenbluth 1950) and relativistic scattering off a static potential (Dalitz 1951, Itzykson 1980), establishing the effective field theory as a foundation for future calculations of proton structure corrections at O(Z²α²) and determination of contact interaction coefficients that connect muonic hydrogen spectroscopy to muon-proton scattering.
