# Study of Neutron Magic Drip-Line Nuclei within Relativistic Mean Field plus BCS Approach

> Encouraged by the success of RMF+BCS approach for the description of the ground state properties of the chains of isotopes of proton magic nuclei with proton number Z=8, 20, 28, 50 and 82 as well as those of proton sub-magic nuclei with Z=40, we have further employed it, in an analogous manner, for a detailed calculations of the ground state properties of the neutron magic isotones with neutron number N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82 and 126 as well as those of neutron sub-magic isotones with N = 40 using the TMA force parameterizations in order to explore low lying resonance and other exotic phenomenon near drip-lines. The results of these calculations for wave-function, single particle pairing gaps etc. are presented here to demonstrate the general validity of our RMF+BCS approach. It is found that, in some of the proton-rich nuclei in the vicinity of the proton drip-line, the main contribution to the pairing correlations is provided by the low-lying resonant states, in addition to the contributions coming from the states close to the Fermi surface, which results extended proton drip-line for isotonic chain.

# Study of Neutron Magic Drip-Line Nuclei within Relativistic Mean Field plus BCS Approach

## Key claim
The RMF+BCS approach with TMA force parametrization successfully describes ground state properties of neutron magic isotones (N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) and sub-magic isotones (N = 40), but the extension of the proton drip-line via resonant state contributions is less pronounced than the analogous neutron drip-line extension in proton magic nuclei, due to the disruptive effect of Coulomb forces.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with nonlinear sigma and omega meson terms using the TMA force parametrization, coupled with a state-dependent BCS pairing treatment using a delta-force interaction (strength V0 = 350 MeV fm^3). For spherical nuclei, the continuum was replaced by positive energy states generated by enclosing the nucleus in a spherical box, while for axially deformed cases the basis expansion method with anisotropic harmonic oscillator was employed. Calculations were carried out for entire isotonic chains of neutron magic numbers N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126 and sub-magic N = 40, spanning from proton-rich to neutron-rich regions up to the drip-lines.

## Key numbers
- The pairing interaction strength used was V0 = 350 MeV fm^3, determined by fitting to binding energies of Ni isotopes.
- For the proton-rich nucleus 58Zn28 (N = 28 isotone), the resonant states 1f5/2, 2p3/2, and 2p1/2 lie at energies 0.57, 0.84, and 1.82 MeV, respectively, with pairing gap values close to 1 MeV.
- The proton Fermi energy for 58Zn28 is lambda_p = -0.097 MeV.
- For 60Ge28 (beyond the proton drip-line), the two-proton separation energy S2p = -0.52 MeV and one-proton separation energy Sp = 0.27 MeV.
- In 60Ge28, the resonant states 1f5/2, 2p3/2, and 2p1/2 lie at energies 0.74, 1.13, and 2.12 MeV, respectively.
- For the N = 28 isotonic chain, the difference Delta S2p between calculated and experimental two-proton separation energies ranges from near zero to a maximum of about 4 MeV.
- The proton single-particle gap between 1f7/2 and the next states (1f5/2, 2p3/2) in 56Ni28 is about 8 MeV.
- Representative resonant states in other isotonic chains: 1g7/2 at 2.67 MeV in 96Pd50 (N = 50), 1h9/2 at 5.73 MeV in 154Hf82 (N = 82), and 1i13/2 at 1.00 MeV in 220Pu126 (N = 126).

## Context
This work extends the RMF+BCS approach previously applied to proton magic isotopes (Yadav et al., 2011) to neutron magic isotones, using the same methodology as earlier studies of Ca isotopes and other nuclei (Saxena et al., 2011; Geng et al., 2003). The results are compared with continuum relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations (Meng et al., 1998) and with experimental data where available. The study enables systematic investigation of shell closure evolution near drip-lines, including the quenching of N = 28 magicity in proton-deficient nuclei such as 40Mg, consistent with recent work by Li et al. (2011), and identifies candidates for two-proton radioactivity such as 60Ge28, following experimental observations of 45Fe (Pfutzner et al., 2002; Giovinazzo et al., 2002) and 54Zn (Blank et al., 2005; Ascher et al., 2011).
