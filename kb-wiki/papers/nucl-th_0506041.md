# Further study on 5q configuration states in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The structure of the $5q$ configuration states with strangeness ${\cal{S}}=+1$ is further studied in the chiral SU(3) quark model based on our previous work. We calculate the energies of fifteen low configurations of the $5q$ system, four lowest configurations of $J^π={1/2}^-$ with $4q$ partition $[4]_{orb}(0s^4)[31]^{σf}$, four of $J^π={1/2}^+$ with $4q$ partition $[31]_{orb}(0s^30p)[4]^{σf}$ and seven of $J^π={1/2}^+$ with $4q$ partition $[4]_{orb}(0s^30p)[31]^{σf}$. Some modifications are made in this further study, i.e., the orbital wave function is extended as an expansion of 4 different size harmonic oscillator forms; three various forms (quadratic, linear and error function form) of the color confinement potential are considered; the states with $4q$ partition $[4]_{orb}(0s^30p)[31]^{σf}$ are added, which are unnegligible in the $J^π={1/2}^+$ case and were not considered in our previous paper, further the mixing between configurations $[31]_{orb}(0s^30p)[4]^{σf}$ and $[4]_{orb}(0s^30p)[31]^{σf}$ is also investigated. The results show that the T=0 state is still always the lowest one for both $J^π={1/2}^-$ and $J^π={1/2}^+$ states, and $J^π={1/2}^-, T=0$ state is always lower than that of $J^π={1/2}^+$. All of these modifications can only offer several tens to hundred MeV effect, and the theoretical value of the lowest state is still about 245 MeV higher than the experimental mass of $Θ^+$. It seems to be difficult to get the calculated mass close to the observed one with the reasonable parameters in the framework of the chiral SU(3) quark model when the model space is chosen as a $5q$ cluster.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, even after extending the orbital wave function, testing three confinement potentials, and adding previously omitted configurations, still predicts the lowest pentaquark state about 245 MeV above the experimental mass of the Θ⁺ (1540 MeV); the authors conclude it is difficult to obtain the observed mass with reasonable parameters when the model space is a 5q cluster.

## Method

The authors calculated energies of fifteen low-lying 5q configurations with strangeness S=+1 using the chiral SU(3) quark model. They extended the orbital wave function as an expansion of four different harmonic oscillator sizes, tested three forms of the color confinement potential (quadratic, linear, and error function), added seven J^π=1/2⁺ states with 4q partition [4]_{orb}(0s³0p)[31]^{σf}, and studied mixing between [31]_{orb}(0s³0p)[4]^{σf} and [4]_{orb}(0s³0p)[31]^{σf} configurations. Three parameter sets were used: one fitted to NN and YN scattering data, and two fitted to KN scattering phase shifts with different scalar meson mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- The lowest J^π=1/2⁻, T=0 state energy ranges from 1785 MeV (error function confinement, case I) to 1901 MeV (quadratic confinement, case II).
- The lowest J^π=1/2⁺ state with partition [4]_{orb}[31]_{ts=01}^{σf} has energy 1917 MeV (error function, case I) to 2123 MeV (quadratic, case III).
- Configuration mixing between J^π=1/2⁺ states with LST=1½1 lowers energies by 40–90 MeV (e.g., from 2203 MeV to 2165 MeV for quadratic confinement, case I).
- Increasing the size parameter from b_u=0.5 fm to b_u=0.6 fm reduces the lowest J^π=1/2⁻ energy from 1785 MeV to 1665 MeV (error function, case I).
- The theoretical lowest state remains about 245 MeV above the experimental Θ⁺ mass of 1540 MeV; with b_u=0.6 fm the gap is about 125 MeV.
- The S-wave KN component in the lowest J^π=1/2⁻ state has a probability of 25%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zhang et al., 2004) by improving the orbital wave function, testing three confinement potentials, and adding previously omitted J^π=1/2⁺ configurations. It contradicts claims by Takeuchi and Shimizu (2004) that positive-parity states could become lower than negative-parity states, finding instead that J^π=1/2⁻, T=0 is always lowest. The results are consistent with Takeuchi et al. (2004), who also could not obtain a low mass close to the observed Θ⁺ using diquark-like correlations. The work suggests that if the Θ⁺ exists, it cannot be explained as a five-constituent-quark cluster, and its structure requires other mechanisms.
