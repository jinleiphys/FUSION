# Astrophysical S factor of {$^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O} Calculated with the Reduced R-matrix Theory

> Determination of the accurate astrophysical S factor of {$^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O} reaction has been regarded as a holy grail of nuclear astrophysics for decades. In current stellar models, a knowledge of that value to better than 10\% is desirable. Due to the practical issues, tremendous experimental and theoretical efforts over nearly 50 years are not able to reach this goal, and the published values contradicted with each other strongly and their uncertainties are 2 times larger than the required precision. To this end we have developed a Reduced R-matrix Theory, based on the classical R-matrix theory of Lane and Thomas, which treats primary transitions to ground state and four bound states as the independent reaction channels in the channel spin representation. With the coordination of covariance statistics and error propagation theory, a global fitting for almost all available experimental data of $^{16}$O system has been multi-iteratively analyzed by our powerful code. A reliable, accurate and self-consistent astrophysical S factor of {$^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O} was obtained with a recommended value $S_{tot}$ (300) = 162.7 $\pm$ 7.3 keV b (4.5\%) which could meet the required precision.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Reduced R-matrix Theory, combined with a global multi-iterative fit of nearly all available experimental data on the 16O system, yields a recommended total astrophysical S factor for the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction of Stot(300) = 162.7 ± 7.3 keV b (4.5% uncertainty), which meets the long-standing goal of better than 10% precision that previous experimental and theoretical efforts over 50 years had failed to achieve.

## Method

The authors developed a Reduced R-matrix Theory based on the classical R-matrix theory of Lane and Thomas (1958), treating primary gamma transitions to the ground state and four bound states of 16O as independent reaction channels in the channel spin representation. They performed a global, multi-iterative fit of almost all available experimental data for the 16O system, including total cross sections, angular distributions, beta-delayed alpha spectra from 16N, transfer reactions, elastic scattering, and other reaction channels, using their RAC code with covariance statistics and error propagation theory to achieve internal consistency.

## Key numbers

- Recommended total astrophysical S factor at 0.3 MeV: Stot(300) = 162.7 ± 7.3 keV b (4.5% uncertainty)
- Ground state (E1) component: SE10(300) = 98.0 ± 7.0 keV b
- Ground state (E2) component: SE20(300) = 56.0 ± 4.1 keV b
- Cascade transitions component: Scas(300) = 8.7 ± 1.8 keV b
- The external contribution to the ground state transition accounts for lower than 3% at 0.3 MeV, based on results from Barker and Kajino (1991) and Kettner et al. (1982)

## Context

This work extends the classical R-matrix framework of Lane and Thomas (1958) and Lane (1960) to perform a global fit of the 16O system, addressing the long-standing discrepancy among previous R-matrix analyses that produced strongly conflicting S-factor values with uncertainties roughly twice the desired 10% precision (e.g., Hammer 2005: 162±39 keV b; Kunz 2001: 165±50 keV b; Buchmann 1996: 165±75 keV b). The approach enables a self-consistent determination of the astrophysical S factor that meets the precision required for stellar models, and the authors note that their RAC code was previously validated by producing accurate cross sections for 6Li(n,α) and 10B(n,α) in the International Evaluation of Neutron Cross Section Standards.
