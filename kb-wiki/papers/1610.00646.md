# The first moment of azimuthal anisotropy in nuclear collisions from AGS to LHC energies

> We review topics related to the first moment of azimuthal anisotropy ($v_1$), commonly known as directed flow, focusing on both charged particles and identified particles from heavy-ion collisions. Beam energies from the highest available, at the CERN LHC, down to projectile kinetic energies per nucleon of a few GeV per nucleon, as studied in experiments at the Brookhaven AGS, fall within our scope. We focus on experimental measurements and on theoretical work where direct comparisons with experiment have been emphasized. The physics addressed or potentially addressed by this review topic includes the study of Quark Gluon Plasma, and more generally, investigation of the Quantum Chromodynamics phase diagram and the equation of state describing the accessible phases.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys experimental measurements and theoretical comparisons of directed flow ($v_1$) in heavy-ion collisions across beam energies from the CERN LHC down to a few GeV per nucleon at the Brookhaven AGS, with the caveat that while directed flow has been proposed as a probe of the QCD phase diagram and Quark Gluon Plasma, recent hydrodynamic and transport calculations incorporating significant theoretical improvements do not reproduce the notable qualitative features of the data, casting doubt on any overall conclusion about inferred properties of the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors compile and compare experimental measurements of the first harmonic coefficient $v_1 = \langle\cos(\phi-\Psi)\rangle$ in the Fourier expansion of azimuthal particle distributions relative to the reaction plane, covering charged particles and identified species (protons, antiprotons, pions, kaons, $\Lambda$ hyperons) across multiple experiments (E877, E895, NA49, PHOBOS, STAR, PHENIX, ALICE). They also review theoretical calculations from hydrodynamic models, transport models (UrQMD, AMPT, PHSD), and three-fluid hydrodynamic approaches that allow direct comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle $v_1$ slope at LHC (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb) is a factor of approximately 3 smaller than at top RHIC energy (200 GeV Au+Au)
- Charged particle $v_1(p_T)$ shows a sign change around $p_T \sim 1.5$ GeV/$c$ in central collisions at both RHIC and LHC
- Proton $dv_1/dy$ shows a peak near 2$A$ GeV beam kinetic energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 2.7$ GeV) in the AGS energy range, then decreases smoothly
- Proton and $\Lambda$ directed flow slope changes from positive to negative close to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV in 10-40% centrality Au+Au collisions
- The minimum in proton $dv_1/dy$ lies between $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ and 19.6 GeV
- In Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the electric charge difference between the two ions is $79 - 29 = 50$ units
- The PHSD model predicts a $v_1$ difference signal between positive and negative charges that is an order of magnitude larger than observed in Cu+Au collisions

## Context

This review extends the body of work on collective flow in nuclear collisions that began with hydrodynamic descriptions by Belenkij and Landau (1956) and the first unambiguous measurement of directed flow at the Bevalac in the mid-1980s. It covers measurements from the AGS (E877, E895), SPS (NA49), RHIC (STAR, PHOBOS, PHENIX), and LHC (ALICE), and addresses the proposed "softest point collapse" in three-fluid hydrodynamic calculations by Stoecker et al. as well as the "anti-flow" or "wiggle" phenomenon. The review enables future work on constraining the QCD phase diagram through beam energy scans, with the caveat that current models do not reproduce the qualitative features of the data, and it provides quantitative information relevant to the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Magnetic Wave.
