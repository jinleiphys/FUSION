# Recent progress on charmed hadron interactions from lattice QCD

> Recent years have witnessed rapid progress in charmed hadron physics, driven by numerous experimental discoveries of exotic states such as $T^+_{cc}$ and $P_c$. These findings have highlighted the importance of understanding charmed hadron interactions. With significant advances in theory and computation, lattice QCD has become a reliable tool for studying nonperturbative dynamics of low-energy QCD. In this talk, I review recent lattice QCD studies of charmed hadron interactions, focusing on three representative systems: $D^*$-$D$, $N$-$c\bar c$, and $Ω_{ccc}$-$Ω_{ccc}$.

# Recent progress on charmed hadron interactions from lattice QCD

## Key claim
Lattice QCD using the HAL QCD method with nearly physical quark masses (mπ ≈ 146 MeV) can reliably extract charmed hadron interactions, and for the T+cc system the D*-D interaction produces a near-threshold virtual state at this pion mass that evolves into a loosely bound state when extrapolated to the physical pion mass of 135 MeV.

## Method
The HAL QCD method defines an effective potential through spatiotemporal correlation functions, allowing extraction of hadron-hadron interactions without requiring correlation functions to be dominated by a single state. Calculations used (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations with almost physical light quark masses (mπ ≈ 146 MeV, mK ≈ 525 MeV) and physical charm quark mass, with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.0846 fm and volume L4 = 964 (La ≈ 8.1 fm). The local potential at leading order in the derivative expansion was used, with truncation errors estimated through the t-dependence of V(r).

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.0846 fm; lattice volume: L4 = 964, giving La ≈ 8.1 fm
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 146 MeV; kaon mass: mK ≈ 525 MeV; spin-averaged 1S charmonium mass: mcc̄ ≈ 3068 MeV
- For D*-D in the T+cc channel at mπ = 146.4 MeV: 1/a0 = 0.05(5)(+2-2) fm-1, reff = 1.12(3)(+3-8) fm, κpole = -8(8)(+3-5) MeV, Epole = -59(+53-99)(+2-67) keV
- For D*-D at mπ = 135.0 MeV (extrapolated): 1/a0 = -0.03(4) fm-1, κpole = +5(8) MeV, Epole = -45(+41-78) keV
- N-J/ψ scattering lengths: a0 = 0.30(2)(+0-2) fm (spin 3/2), a0 = 0.38(4)(+0-3) fm (spin 1/2); N-ηc: a0 = 0.21(2)(+0-1) fm
- J/ψ mass reduction in normal nuclear matter (ρnm = 0.17 fm-3): δmJ/ψ ≈ 19(3) MeV
- Ωccc-Ωccc (1S0) without Coulomb: a0 = -1.57(0.08)(+0.04-0.12) fm, reff = 0.57(0.02)(+0.01-0.00) fm, binding energy B = 5.68(0.77)(+0.46-1.02) MeV, √⟨r2⟩ = 1.13(0.06)(+0.08-0.03) fm
- Ωccc-Ωccc with Coulomb: a0C = 19(7)(+6-7) fm, reffC = 0.45(0.01)(+0.01-0.00) fm, ratio reffC/a0C ≈ 0.024

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of charmed hadron interactions using the HAL QCD method, addressing limitations of earlier calculations that used quenched approximations, unphysically heavy pion masses, or had large statistical uncertainties (Yokokawa 2006, Kawanai 2010, Skerbis 2018). The D*-D results connect to experimental discoveries by LHCb (2021) of the T+cc doubly charmed tetraquark. The N-cc̄ results provide first-principles constraints to resolve tension between experimental determinations of the N-J/ψ scattering length that differ by orders of magnitude (Pentchev 2020, JPAC 2023). The Ωccc-Ωccc study extends previous dibaryon predictions for pΩ (Iritani 2019) and ΩΩ (Gongyo 2018) into the charm sector. These results enable future studies of charmed exotic hadrons, charmonium-nucleus bound states, and the role of heavy quark spin symmetry in hadron interactions.
