# Understanding the mirror asymmetry in Gamow-Teller transition rates between $^{28}$Al($β^-$)$^{28m}$Si and $^{28}$P($β^+$)$^{28m}$Si

> The long-standing discrepancy between shell-model and experimental values of the mirror asymmetry in Gamow-Teller transition rates for $^{28}$Al($β^-$)$^{28m}$Si and $^{28}$P($β^+$)$^{28m}$Si is partially resolved by integrating the $p$-orbital contribution into the radial mismatch correction term. This approach offers a potential estimate of the radial mismatch-induced core-orbital contribution based on the French-Macfarlane sum rules, avoiding the need for heavy calculations required by the first-principle shell model. Additionally, the isospin-mixing contribution undergoes considerable improvement through scaling the calculated isospin-mixing correction with the experimental energy separation between the lowest two admixed states, in accordance with the result from the two-level model.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between shell-model and experimental values of the mirror asymmetry in Gamow-Teller transition rates for the $^{28}$Al($\beta^-$)$^{28m}$Si and $^{28}$P($\beta^+$)$^{28m}$Si pair is partially resolved by integrating the $p$-orbital contribution into the radial mismatch correction term, but the total theoretical asymmetry of 3.664$\pm$3.245% still exceeds the experimental value of -3.706$\pm$0.444% by about one standard deviation.

## Method

The authors decompose the isospin-symmetry breaking correction $\delta_C$ into two components: $\delta_{C1}$ (configuration admixtures within the $sd$ valence space) and $\delta_{C2}$ (radial mismatch between proton and neutron wave functions). They improve $\delta_{C1}$ by scaling calculated values with the experimental energy separation between the two lowest admixed states using a two-level model. For $\delta_{C2}$, they extend the standard Woods-Saxon treatment to include $p$-orbital (core-orbital) contributions, estimating spectroscopic factors via the French-Macfarlane sum rules under nine different scenarios for the distribution of spectroscopic factors across intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- Experimental mirror asymmetry: -3.706$\pm$0.444% (from NNDC ENSDF)
- Shell-model calculation using $sd$-shell interactions alone yields asymmetry: 7.716$\pm$2.961%
- Averaged isospin-symmetry GT matrix element $\mathcal{M}_0$ for three universal $sd$-shell interactions: 0.751$\pm$0.058
- Scaled $\delta_{C1}$ values: 0.453$\pm$0.100% for $^{28}$Al($\beta^-$)$^{28}$Si and 0.747$\pm$0.296% for $^{28}$P($\beta^+$)$^{28}$Si, contributing 0.294$\pm$0.312% to the asymmetry
- $\delta_{C2}$ from $sd$-shell only: 0.631$\pm$0.892% ($\beta^-$) and 8.299$\pm$2.809% ($\beta^+$), contributing 7.668$\pm$2.947% to asymmetry
- Averaged $p$-shell contribution to asymmetry across nine scenarios: -4.346$\pm$1.291%
- Total asymmetry including $p$-shell contribution: 3.664$\pm$3.245%
- Calculated $\Delta E^{cal}$ for isospin-mixing scaling: 0.961$\pm$0.111 MeV

## Context

This work extends the shell-model reevaluation of isospin-symmetry breaking in mirror GT transitions by Xayavong (2023), which found several cases with deviations from experiment. The specific $A=28$ mirror pair showed a negative asymmetry, opposite to the expected trend from Coulomb repulsion in proton-rich nuclei. The authors improve upon the standard treatment using Woods-Saxon potentials (Xayavong 2018, 2022) and the effective interactions USD, USDA, and USDB (from the 1980s and 1990s). The approach enables estimation of core-orbital contributions without full first-principle shell-model calculations, and suggests that experimental data on spectroscopic factors for nucleon removal reactions could further constrain the intermediate-state contributions.
