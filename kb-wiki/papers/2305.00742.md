# Impact of isovector pairing fluctuation on neutrinoless double-beta decay in multi-reference covariant density functional theory

> We extend the multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) by including fluctuations in quadrupole deformations and average isovector pairing gaps simultaneously for the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of neutrinoless double-beta $(0Î½Î²Î²)$ decay in the candidate nuclei $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe assuming the exchange of either light or heavy neutrinos. The results indicate a linear correlation between the predicted NMEs and the isovector pairing strengths, as well as the excitation energies of $2^{+}_1$ and $4^{+}_1$ states. By adjusting the pairing strengths based on the excitation energies of the $2^{+}_1$ states, we calculate the NMEs for $0Î½Î²Î²$ decay, which are reduced by approximately $12\%$ to $62\%$ compared to the results obtained in the previous studies by Song et al. [Phys. Rev. C95, 024305 (2017)]. Additionally, upon introducing the average isovector pairing gap as an additional generator coordinate in the calculation, the NMEs increase by a factor ranging from $56\%$ to $218\%$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including fluctuations in both quadrupole deformations and average isovector pairing gaps simultaneously in multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) changes the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta decay by factors ranging from a 62% reduction to a 218% increase, depending on whether pairing fluctuations are included, and that the NMEs can be calibrated using experimental excitation energies of the 2+1 states.

## Method

The authors extended MR-CDFT by using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with two collective coordinates: axial quadrupole deformation Î²2 and the average isovector pairing gap Î”uv. Mean-field wave functions were generated from relativistic mean-field plus BCS theory with constraints on both quadrupole moment and pairing amplitude, using the relativistic point-coupling density functional PC-PK1. The NMEs for 0Î½Î²Î² decay were computed for five candidate nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 130Te, 136Xe) assuming exchange of either light or heavy Majorana neutrinos, with the pairing strengths adjusted to reproduce experimental excitation energies of 2+1 states.

## Key numbers

- NMEs reduced by approximately 12% to 62% compared to results from Song et al. (2017) when pairing strengths were adjusted to 2+1 state excitation energies without including isovector pairing fluctuation
- NMEs increased by a factor ranging from 56% to 218% when the average isovector pairing gap was introduced as an additional generator coordinate
- For 130Te, the NME for light neutrino exchange converged to 4.916 (with nf=12 harmonic oscillator shells) and for heavy neutrino exchange to 259.389 (with nf=12)

## Context

This work extends previous MR-CDFT studies by Song et al. (2017) that only included quadrupole shape fluctuations, and addresses the known sensitivity of NMEs to pairing treatments identified in earlier studies using interacting shell model (Yoshinaga 2018, Higashiyama 2020) and QRPA methods (Simkovic 2013). The linear correlation found between NMEs and isovector pairing strengths, as well as with excitation energies of 2+1 and 4+1 states, provides a new method to calibrate NMEs using experimental nuclear structure data, which could help reduce the factor-of-three discrepancy among different nuclear model predictions for 0Î½Î²Î² decay NMEs.
