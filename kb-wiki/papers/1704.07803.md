# Big-bang nucleosynthesis constraints on dark-matter sectors revisited

> We extend previous studies of big bang nucleosynthesis, with the assumption that ordinary matter and dark matter sectors are entangled through the number of degrees of freedom entering the Friedmann equations. This conjecture allows us to find a relation between the temperatures of the weakly interacting matter and dark-matter sectors. The constraints imposed by observations are studied by comparison with calculations of big bang nucleosynthesis for the abundance of light elements. It is found that these constraints are compatible with cold dark matter and a wide range number of dark sectors.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous BBN studies by assuming ordinary and dark matter sectors are entangled through the number of degrees of freedom in the Friedmann equations, finding that while the temperature of dark sectors is constrained to be colder than ordinary matter (T' ~ 0.3 T_BBN), the number of dark sectors N_DM can vary widely (1-50) and still be compatible with observed light-element abundances, contrary to the specific N_DM=5 assumption in earlier work by Oliveira (2016).

## Method

The authors used a standard BBN code with baryon-to-photon ratio eta = 6.2 x 10^-10, three neutrino families, and neutron lifetime tau_n = 880.3 s to calculate primordial abundances of D, ^3He, ^4He, and ^7Li as functions of the dark-sector temperature ratio x = T'/T and the number of dark sectors N_DM. They introduced an effective number of degrees of freedom g_bar* = g* (1 + N_DM x^4) in the Friedmann equation and compared predictions to observational abundance data, also incorporating variations in effective neutrino number Delta N_nu.

## Key numbers

- For N_DM = 5 and T'/T = 0.3, predicted ^4He mass fraction Y_p = 0.249, compared to observed 0.2449 ± 0.004
- For N_DM = 5 and T'/T = 0.3, predicted D/H = 2.62 x 10^-5, compared to observed (2.53 ± 0.04) x 10^-5
- For N_DM = 5 and T'/T = 0.3, predicted ^3He/H = 0.97 x 10^-5, compared to observed (1.1 ± 0.2) x 10^-5
- For N_DM = 5 and T'/T = 0.3, predicted ^7Li/H = 4.38 x 10^-10, compared to observed (1.58 ± 0.31) x 10^-10
- Dark-sector temperature constraint: T' less than or approximately 0.3 T from ^4He abundance
- For T' = 0.3 T, N_DM can range from 1 to 50 while D, ^3He, and ^4He abundances remain within observational constraints
- Only N_DM approximately 100 would explain ^7Li abundance, but this would conflict with D, ^3He, and ^4He observations
- For N_DM = 5, dark-baryon-to-ordinary-baryon asymmetry ratio eta' approximately 7 eta

## Context

This work extends the gauge model of Oliveira (2016), which proposed five dark-matter sectors as exact copies of the standard model, by systematically testing BBN constraints on both the number of sectors and their temperature. The results are consistent with earlier asymmetric dark matter models by Buckley (2011) and others, and show that the specific N_DM = 5 assumption is viable but not uniquely required by BBN data. The findings enable future work to explore dark-sector physics with a wider range of sector numbers, while the lithium problem remains unresolved under this scenario.
