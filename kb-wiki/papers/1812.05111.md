# Sorting out quenched jets

> We introduce a new 'quantile' analysis strategy to study the modification of jets as they traverse through a droplet of quark-gluon plasma. To date, most jet modification studies have been based on comparing the jet properties measured in heavy-ion collisions to a proton-proton baseline at the same reconstructed jet transverse momentum ($p_T$). It is well known, however, that the quenching of jets from their interaction with the medium leads to a migration of jets from higher to lower $p_T$, making it challenging to directly infer the degree and mechanism of jet energy loss. Our proposed quantile matching procedure is inspired by (but not reliant on) the approximate monotonicity of energy loss in the jet $p_T$. In this strategy, jets in heavy-ion collisions ordered by $p_T$ are viewed as modified versions of the same number of highest-energy jets in proton-proton collisions, and the fractional energy loss as a function of jet $p_T$ is a natural observable ($Q_{\rm AA}$). Furthermore, despite non-monotonic fluctuations in the energy loss, we use an event generator to validate the strong correlation between the $p_T$ of the parton that initiates a heavy-ion jet and the $p_T$ of the vacuum jet which corresponds to it via the quantile procedure ($p_T^{\rm quant}$). We demonstrate that this strategy both provides a complementary way to study jet modification and mitigates the effect of $p_T$ migration in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "quantile" matching procedure for comparing jets in heavy-ion collisions to a proton-proton baseline, which mitigates the effect of jet transverse momentum ($p_T$) migration due to medium-induced energy loss, but the authors acknowledge that the procedure is inspired by (not reliant on) the approximate monotonicity of energy loss in jet $p_T$ and that non-monotonic fluctuations in energy loss are present.

## Method

The authors introduce a quantile matching procedure where jets in heavy-ion collisions ordered by $p_T$ are viewed as modified versions of the same number of highest-energy jets in proton-proton collisions. They define the observable $Q_{\rm AA}$ as the ratio of heavy-ion jet $p_T$ ($p_T^{\rm AA}$) to proton-proton jet $p_T$ ($p_T^{\rm quant}$) at the same cumulative cross-section, and validate the procedure using the event generator Jewel 2.1.0 with 2 million each of proton-proton and head-on (0-10% centrality) heavy-ion events at 2.76 TeV, reconstructing anti-$k_t$ jets with radius parameter $R=0.4$ and pseudorapidity $|\eta|<2$.

## Key numbers

- Jet production spectrum scales approximately as $\sigma \sim p_T^{-6}$ (steeply-falling).
- For Z+jet events in Jewel at 2.76 TeV, the mean value of $\langle p_T^{\rm vac}/p_T^{\rm Z} \rangle \equiv \tilde{\mu}_1 \approx 0.87$ at $p_T^{\rm Z} = 300$ GeV.
- The standard deviation of $\sigma(p_T^{\rm vac}/p_T^{\rm in}) \equiv \tilde{\sigma}_1 \approx 0.17$ at $p_T^{\rm Z} = 300$ GeV.
- The mean value of $\langle p_T^{\rm AA}/p_T^{\rm Z} \rangle \approx 0.74$ at $p_T^{\rm Z} = 300$ GeV.
- The standard deviation of $\sigma(p_T^{\rm AA}/p_T^{\rm Z}) \approx 0.18$ at $p_T^{\rm Z} = 300$ GeV.
- From the gaussian smearing model, extracted values yield $\tilde{\mu}_2 \approx 0.85$ and $\tilde{\sigma}_2 \approx 0.12$.
- The highest-$p_T$ jets lose a small fraction of their energy on average, with $(1 - Q_{\rm AA}) \sim 5\%$.

## Context

This work extends previous jet quenching studies that compared heavy-ion and proton-proton jets at the same reconstructed $p_T$ using the standard $R_{\rm AA}$ observable, which suffers from biases due to jet migration from higher to lower $p_T$ (as noted by Casalderrey-Solana (2007) and d'Enterria (2009)). The quantile procedure is inspired by the $S_{\rm loss}$ observable introduced by PHENIX for single hadrons (Adler 2006, Adare 2012, Adare 2015). The authors validate their method using Jewel (Zapp 2013, Kunnawalkam Elayavalli 2016) and show that $p_T^{\rm quant}$ approximates the initial jet $p_T$ before quenching with comparable fidelity to the unmodified boson energy $p_T^{\rm Z}$ available only in rarer Z+jet events. This enables future experimental tests in Z+jet or gamma+jet events to validate the procedure, after which it can be applied to re-analyze jet modification observables in general jet events to minimize $p_T$ migration effects.
