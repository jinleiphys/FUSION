# Flavorful Lepton Number Violation at the EIC

> We explore the prospects of detecting flavorful lepton number violation at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) through resonant production of heavy neutral leptons (HNLs), resulting in $e^- p \to \ell^+_α+ k\, j+X$, where $α\in \{e, μ, τ\}$ and $k$ denotes the number of jets. We work in the $ν$SMEFT framework of the Standard Model Effective Field Theory augmented with $n$ singlet HNLs, one of which is in the mass range $10-100$~GeV, within kinematic reach of the EIC. To explore the EIC sensitivity, we focus on the HNL production mechanism induced by mixing with light neutrinos. We study kinematic distributions for signal and backgrounds, including hadronization and detector effects, and suggest a set of cuts to minimize backgrounds. In the mass range considered, we find that the EIC with muon detection capabilities and an integrated luminosity of $100~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}$ can reach sensitivities comparable to the strongest direct (LHC) and indirect constraints, and is especially relevant in the $ν$SMEFT framework beyond dimension four. Our study motivates further assessment of muon detection capabilities at the EIC and $τ$ hadronic reconstruction, as well as a more general theoretical analysis involving production mechanisms mediated by higher-dimensional operators in the effective theory.

## Key claim
The EIC with muon detection capabilities and 100 fb⁻¹ integrated luminosity can reach sensitivities to heavy neutral lepton (HNL) mixing parameters comparable to the strongest direct LHC and indirect constraints, but only for the specific case where HNL production is induced by mixing with light neutrinos within the νSMEFT framework, and the analysis focuses on the dimension-4 Yukawa interactions rather than higher-dimensional operators that could also contribute.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of the LNV signal process e⁻ p → ℓ⁺_α + k j + X (α ∈ {e, μ, τ}) mediated by resonant HNL production, using MadGraph5_aMC for matrix-element generation, Pythia8 for parton showering and hadronization, and Delphes3 with a customized EIC detector card including a hypothetical muon detector. They simulated three background categories (NC-DIS, CC-DIS, and heavy quark production) and applied a cut-based analysis using kinematic variables including lepton transverse momentum p_Tℓ, scalar sum of jet transverse momenta H_T, and missing transverse energy E_T^miss to isolate signal from backgrounds.

## Key numbers
- HNL mass range considered: 10–100 GeV, within kinematic reach of the EIC at center-of-mass energy √s = 141 GeV (E_e × E_p = 18 GeV × 275 GeV)
- Integrated luminosity assumed: 100 fb⁻¹ with electron polarization P_e = 70%
- Generator-level cuts applied: p_Tj > 10 GeV, p_Tℓ > 5 GeV, |η_{j,ℓ}| < 5, ΔR_{jj, jℓ} > 0.4
- Electron charge-misidentification probability benchmark: P_misID = 10⁻³
- Signal selection requires exactly 2 or 3 jets (N_j = 2,3) and exactly one positively charged lepton
- Kinematic cuts for all channels: p_Tℓ > 5 GeV and H_T > 50 GeV
- For the τ channel, additional E_T^miss > 5 GeV requirement motivated by neutrinos from leptonic τ decays (τ → μ branching ratio ≈ 17%)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Batell (2022) by including parton showering and detector effects, studying flavor-changing LNV channels (e⁻ → μ⁺ and e⁻ → τ⁺) in addition to the same-flavor channel, and comparing EIC sensitivity to constraints from LHC, LEP, and low-energy processes such as 0νββ decay and lepton-flavor-violating processes. The study motivates further assessment of muon detection capabilities at the EIC and τ hadronic reconstruction, as well as more general theoretical analysis involving production mechanisms mediated by higher-dimensional operators in the effective theory beyond the mixing mechanism considered here.
