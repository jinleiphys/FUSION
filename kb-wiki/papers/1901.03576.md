# An extended Agassi model: algebraic structure, phase diagram, and large size limit

> The Agassi model is a schematic two-level model that involves pairing and monopole-monopole interactions. It is, therefore, an extension of the well known Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model. In this paper we review the algebraic formulation of an extension of the Agassi model as well as its bosonic realization through the Schwinger representation. Moreover, a mean-field approximation for the model is presented and its phase diagram discussed. Finally, a $1/j$ analysis, with $j$ proportional to the degeneracy of each level, is worked out to obtain the thermodynamic limit of the ground state energy and some order parameters from the exact Hamiltonian diagonalization for finite$-j$.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extended Agassi model that includes an additional monopole-pairing term (parameter \(h\) or \(\Lambda\)) beyond the original Agassi model, and claims that this extension produces a richer phase diagram with up to five distinct phases and a point where all five solutions are degenerate, but only within the mean-field approximation and for the half-filling condition \(N = 2j\).

## Method

The authors formulate the extended Agassi model using an o(5) algebra and construct its bosonic image via the Schwinger representation with five boson operators. They then derive the mean-field energy surfaces using two equivalent approaches: a boson condensate variational method in the Schwinger representation and a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with two alternative phase selections for the Bogoliubov transformation, minimizing the energy with respect to the variational parameters \(\varphi\) and \(\beta\). Finally, they perform exact diagonalizations of the Hamiltonian for finite system sizes \(j\) ranging from 10 to 300 and extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit using a \(1/j\) analysis.

## Key numbers

- The system is studied at half-filling, where the number of fermion pairs is fixed to \(j\) (i.e., \(N = 2j\)), with \(\Omega = 2j\) the degeneracy of each level.
- The symmetric (spherical) phase exists for \(\chi < 1\), \(\Sigma < 1\), and \(\Lambda < 1\), with ground state energy \(E/(j\varepsilon) = -1\).
- The HF deformed phase exists for \(\chi > 1\), \(\chi > \Sigma\), and \(\Lambda < (1+\chi^2)/(2\chi)\), with energy \(E/(j\varepsilon) = -(1+\chi^2)/(2\chi)\).
- The BCS deformed phase exists for \(\Sigma > 1\), \(\Sigma > \chi\), and \(\Lambda < (1+\Sigma^2)/(2\Sigma)\), with energy \(E/(j\varepsilon) = -(1+\Sigma^2)/(2\Sigma)\).
- The combined HF-BCS deformed phase exists for \(\Lambda > 1\), \(\Lambda > (1+\Sigma^2)/(2\Sigma)\), and \(\Lambda > (1+\chi^2)/(2\chi)\), with energy \(E/(j\varepsilon) = -\Lambda\).
- Exact diagonalizations are performed for \(j\) ranging from 10 to 300, with example parameters \(\chi = 0.5\), \(\Sigma = 0.5\), \(\Lambda = 0\).
- At the single point \(\chi = \Sigma = \Lambda = 1\), all five solutions for the system are degenerate.

## Context

This work extends the original Agassi model (Agassi, 1968) by adding a monopole-pairing term that couples pairs across the two levels, building on a recent study by Garcia-Ramos et al. (2018) that first proposed this extended version. The model is a schematic two-level system that generalizes the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, 1965) by including pairing interactions, and its phase diagram is richer than those of related models such as the two-fluid LMG model (Garcia-Ramos, 2016), the proton-neutron interacting boson model (Arias, 2004), or Hamiltonians with three-body interactions (Fortune, 2011). The results enable future tests of many-body approximations such as coupled cluster theory (Hermes, 2017) and provide a benchmark for studying quantum phase transitions in algebraic models.
