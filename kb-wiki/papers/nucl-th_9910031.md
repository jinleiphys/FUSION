# Annihilation of antiprotons on nuclear targets at low energies: a simple analysis

> We set up a plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) formalism for the analysis of the annihilation cross sections of antinucleons on nuclear targets at very low momenta (below 100 MeV/c), where semiclassical approximations can't be applied. Since, as we test here, PWIA fails in reproducing the unexpected ``inversion'' behavior of the $\bar{p}p$ and $\bar{p}-$nucleus annihilation cross sections found in a recent experiment\cite{obe1,obe2} we discuss some further possibilities, with a special attention to the optical potential model.

## Key claim

The authors test a Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) formalism for antiproton annihilation on nuclear targets at momenta below 100 MeV/c and find that it fails to reproduce the experimentally observed "inversion" behavior (where annihilation cross sections on deuteron and helium-4 become smaller than on free protons), suggesting that multiple scattering effects or optical potential models are needed instead.

## Method

The authors set up a PWIA formalism that expresses the nuclear annihilation cross section as an incoherent integral over the nuclear momentum distribution of the elementary antiproton-nucleon cross sections, using closure approximation on the residual nuclear states. They then compare the PWIA predictions with experimental data on antiproton annihilation on proton, deuteron, and helium-4 targets at momenta below 60 MeV/c, and also analyze a black sphere optical potential model to explore the conditions under which inversion can occur.

## Key numbers

- The inversion pattern appears at projectile momenta below 60 MeV/c, where total annihilation cross sections on deuteron and helium-4 are smaller than on free proton targets.
- The naive expectation for the ratio of low-energy antiproton-proton to antiproton-helium-4 annihilation cross sections could be as small as 1/AZ = 1/8, but the measured cross sections on helium-4 are even smaller than on protons.
- The Fermi momentum of bound nucleons is approximately 200 MeV/c, which smears any resonance structure in the elementary cross section over a range Δp ∼ p_Fermi.
- The PWIA results show sensitivity to high momentum components of the nuclear momentum distribution at the level of approximately 10% depression of the annihilation rate.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Obe (1999) and Obe (1999) on antiproton annihilation cross sections on light nuclei, which showed an unexpected inversion pattern below 60 MeV/c. The authors note that a black sphere potential model (Pro1) shows that S-wave reaction cross sections tend to zero as the imaginary potential strength goes to infinity, but this model alone does not reproduce the inversion. The analysis is motivated by upcoming experiments at the AD accelerator at CERN and builds on earlier work by Tru (1975) relating antiprotonic atom level widths to low-energy annihilation rates, and by Wic (1999) on antiprotonic deuterium.
