# Fluctuation and dissipation dynamics in fusion reactions from stochastic mean-field approach

> By projecting the stochastic mean-field dynamics on a suitable collective path during the entrance channel of heavy-ion collisions, expressions for transport coefficients associated with relative distance are extracted. These transport coefficients, which have similar forms to those familiar from nucleon exchange model, are evaluated by carrying out TDHF simulations. The calculations provide an accurate description of the magnitude and form factor of transport coefficients associated with one-body dissipation and fluctuation mechanism.

## Key claim

By projecting stochastic mean-field dynamics onto a collective path for the entrance channel of heavy-ion collisions, the authors extract transport coefficients for relative motion that have similar forms to those from the nucleon exchange model, but without assuming instantaneous equilibration of transferred nucleons or relying on adiabatic/diabatic approximations.

## Method

The authors perform three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) simulations using the SLy4d Skyrme effective force for head-on collisions of \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{40}\)Ca. They define collective variables (relative distance, relative momentum, mass numbers) via the reduced Wigner distribution integrated over phase space on a separation plane between projectile-like and target-like fragments. Using the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, they compute the momentum diffusion coefficient from the correlation function of the reduced Wigner distribution on the window, and the friction coefficient from the net momentum flow across the window, both as functions of relative distance.

## Key numbers

- Reduced friction coefficient \(\beta(R) = \gamma(R)/\mu(R)\) is extracted for \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{40}\)Ca at \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 100\) MeV and \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 150\) MeV, with good agreement between the SMF-based method and the Dissipative-Dynamics TDHF (DD-TDHF) method above and close to the Coulomb barrier at approximately 9.8 fm.
- Momentum diffusion coefficient \(D_{PP}(R)\) is computed from Eq. (24) for the same system and energies; the Einstein relation \(D^{\rm eq}_{PP}(R) = \gamma(R) T(t)\) with level density parameter \(a = A/12\) severely underestimates the dynamical diffusion coefficient.
- Neck radius \(r_{\rm neck}\) is determined for the \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{40}\)Ca reaction at \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 100\) MeV as a function of relative distance, using both a geometric definition and an alternative approach based on the reduced Wigner function.

## Context

This work extends the DD-TDHF method of Washiyama et al. (2008) for extracting nucleus-nucleus potential and friction coefficients from TDHF simulations by additionally incorporating fluctuation dynamics via the SMF approach of Ayik et al. (2005, 2006). The derived transport coefficients have the same form as those from the phenomenological nucleon exchange model of Randrup (1979) and Feldmeier (1987), but are derived microscopically without the instantaneous-equilibration assumption. This enables microscopic determination of one-body dissipation and fluctuation coefficients for fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier, with planned extensions to off-central collisions and nucleon diffusion in grazing collisions.
