# Electroweak boson-tagged jet event asymmetries at the Large Hadron Collider

> Tagged jet measurements provide a promising experimental channel to quantify the similarities and differences in the mechanisms of jet production in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions. We present the first calculation of the transverse momentum asymmetry of $Z^0/γ^*$-tagged jet events and the momentum imbalance of $γ$-tagged jet events in $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV reactions at the LHC. Our results combine the ${\cal O}(G_Fα_s^2)$, ${\cal O}(G_Fα_s^2)$ perturbative cross sections with the radiative and collisional processes that modify parton showers in the presence of dense QCD matter. We find that strong asymmetry momentum and imbalance, respectively, are generated in central Pb+Pb reactions that have little sensitivity to the fluctuations of the underlying soft hadronic background. We present theoretical model predictions for their shape and magnitude.

# Electroweak Boson-Tagged Jet Event Asymmetries at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

The first calculation of the transverse momentum asymmetry of Z0/γ*-tagged jet events and the momentum imbalance of γ-tagged jet events at √s = 2.76 TeV LHC reactions shows that strong asymmetry and imbalance are generated in central Pb+Pb reactions, but these results are theoretical model predictions whose magnitude depends on the coupling strength gmed between the jet and the medium, with values in the range 1.8–2.2 that have previously described di-jet asymmetry and inclusive jet suppression.

## Method

The calculation combines O(αemαs²) and O(GFαs²) perturbative cross sections with initial-state cold nuclear matter effects and final-state parton shower modification in the quark-gluon plasma. Medium-modified tagged cross sections are computed as a probabilistic superposition of cross sections where the jet has higher initial transverse momentum, using probability distributions for the fraction of parton energy converted to medium-induced parton showers, including both radiative (medium-induced parton splitting in the soft gluon approximation) and collisional energy loss processes. The asymmetry AJ = (pTZ0 − pTjet)/(pTZ0 + pTjet) and imbalance zJγ = pTjet/pTγ distributions are obtained by changing variables and integrating over the specified kinematic domain.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √s = 2.76 TeV for LHC Pb+Pb and p+p reactions
- Z0 transverse momentum range used in asymmetry calculation: pTZ ∈ (80, 100) GeV
- Jet transverse momentum threshold for Z0-tagged events: pTjet > 20 GeV
- Photon transverse momentum threshold for γ-tagged events: pTγ > 60 GeV
- Jet transverse momentum threshold for γ-tagged events: pTjet > 30 GeV
- Jet radius parameters studied: R = 0.2, 0.4 for Z0-tagged events; R = 0.3 for γ-tagged events
- Photon isolation cone radius: Riso = 0.4 with total energy within cone less than 5 GeV
- Rapidity cuts for γ-tagged events: |yγ| < 1.44, |yjet| < 1.6
- Azimuthal separation cut for γ-tagged events: |φjet − φγ| > 7π/8
- Coupling strength values investigated: gmed = 1.8, 2.0, 2.2

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD approach of He (2011) that described inclusive jet suppression and di-jet asymmetry at the LHC to the new channel of electroweak boson-tagged jet events. The calculations provide theoretical predictions that can be compared to recent measurements by the ATLAS and CMS collaborations in √s = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, and enable direct comparison between RHIC and LHC results in this exclusive channel for the first time. The approach shows that collisional energy loss has a more pronounced effect for larger jet radii because it comes primarily from radiated gluons, as demonstrated by Neufeld (2011).
