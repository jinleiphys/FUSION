# Double Beta Decay

> We review the recent developments in the field of nuclear double beta decay, which is presently an important topic in both nuclear and particle physics. The mechanism of lepton number violation within the neutrinoless double beta decay is discussed in context of the problem of neutrino mixing and the R-parity violating supersymmetric extensions of the Standard model. The problem of reliable determination of the nuclear matrix elements governing both two-neutrino and neutrinoless modes of the double beta decay is addressed. The validity of different approximation schemes in the considered nuclear structure studies is analyzed and the role of the Pauli exclusion principle for a correct treatment of nuclear matrix elements is emphasized. The constraints on different lepton number violating parameters like effective electron neutrino mass, effective right-handed weak interaction parameters, effective Majoron coupling constant and R-parity violating SUSY parameters are derived from the best presently available experimental limits on the half life of neutrinoless mode of this process.

## Key claim

This review argues that reliable extraction of lepton-number-violating parameters (e.g., effective neutrino mass) from neutrinoless double beta decay experiments depends critically on accurate nuclear matrix elements, but the proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA), the most widely used method for medium and heavy nuclei, suffers from a collapse of the ground state and extreme sensitivity to the particle-particle interaction strength, making its predictive power questionable.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical formalism for both two-neutrino (2νββ) and neutrinoless (0νββ) double beta decay, including the Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism and R-parity violating supersymmetric (SUSY) mechanisms. They analyze the validity of different nuclear structure approximations, particularly the pn-QRPA and its renormalized extension (pn-RQRPA), and compare calculated nuclear matrix elements with experimental half-life limits to derive constraints on lepton number violating parameters such as the effective electron neutrino mass, right-handed weak interaction parameters, the Majoron coupling constant, and R-parity violating SUSY parameters.

## Key numbers

- The best experimental lower bound for the 0νββ-decay half-life of ⁷⁶Ge is T_{1/2}^{0νββ-exp}(0⁺ → 0⁺) ≥ 1.1 × 10²⁵ years (90% C.L.), measured by the Heidelberg-Moscow collaboration (1997).
- From the ⁷⁶Ge experiment, the extracted upper limit on the effective neutrino mass is |⟨m_ν⟩| < 1.1 eV (Simkovic et al., 1997).
- The most stringent laboratory upper limit on the electron neutrino mass from the Troitsk tritium beta decay experiment is 4.35 eV (Belesev et al., 1995).
- The upper limit on the muon neutrino mass from PSI pion decay is 170 keV (Particle Data Group, 1996).
- The upper limit on the tau neutrino mass from τ⁻ → 2π⁺3π⁻ν_τ decay is 24 MeV (Particle Data Group, 1996).
- The CHOOZ and Bugey reactor experiments imply |⟨m_ν⟩| < 3 × 10⁻² eV in a scheme with three light Majorana neutrinos and a hierarchy of neutrino masses (Bilenky et al., 1997).
- The 2νββ-decay half-lives range from 10¹⁹ to 10²⁴ years across different isotopes.

## Context

This review extends earlier comprehensive reviews of double beta decay by Primakoff and Rosen (1981), Boehm and Vogel (1984), Haxton and Stephenson (1984), Doi et al. (1985), Vergados (1986), and Moe and Vogel (1994). It addresses the persistent puzzle of the suppression mechanism for 2νββ-decay matrix elements and the collapse of the QRPA ground state, which Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) and Schwieger, Simkovic and Faessler (1996) attempted to remedy with the renormalized QRPA (pn-RQRPA). The review enables future work to improve nuclear matrix element calculations, for example, by incorporating the Pauli exclusion principle more carefully, and to interpret upcoming experimental sensitivities of 0.1–0.3 eV for |⟨m_ν⟩| planned by the Heidelberg-Moscow and NEMO collaborations.
