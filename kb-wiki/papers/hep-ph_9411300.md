# Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory A.D. 1994

> In these lectures, the status of baryon chiral perturbation theory is reviewed. Particular emphasis is put on the two--flavor sector and the physics related to electromagnetic probes. I discuss in some detail the structure of the effective Lagrangian at next--to--leading order, the meaning of low--energy theorems in Compton scattering and pion photoproduction and confront the chiral predictions with the existing data. Some remaining problems and challenges are outlined.

## Key claim

Baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) provides a systematic low-energy expansion around the chiral limit for processes involving nucleons, but the straightforward generalization from the meson sector fails because the baryon mass does not vanish in the chiral limit, requiring a velocity-dependent field transformation to achieve a consistent power counting.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of the effective field theory for baryons, using velocity-dependent fields to eliminate the nucleon mass term from the lowest-order Lagrangian and thereby establish a consistent chiral power counting where tree diagrams start at order \(p\) and one-loop graphs at order \(p^3\). The structure of the next-to-leading order Lagrangian \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\) is derived, showing that some terms have fixed coefficients from the expansion of the relativistic pion-nucleon Lagrangian while others contain low-energy constants (LECs) determined from pion-nucleon scattering and nucleon magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F_\pi = 93\) MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.26\) (from neutron \(\beta\)-decay)
- Goldberger-Treiman relation fulfilled within 5% in nature: \(\mathring{g}_{\pi N} = \mathring{g}_A \mathring{m} / F\)
- Nucleon mass shift from pion loop: \(\delta m = -\frac{3g_A^2}{32\pi} \frac{M_\pi^3}{F_\pi^2}\) (non-analytic in quark masses, \(\delta m \sim (\hat m)^{3/2}\))
- LEC values from \(\pi N\) scattering (in GeV\(^{-1}\)): \(c_1' = -1.63 \pm 0.21\), \(c_2' = 6.20 \pm 0.38\), \(c_3' = -9.86 \pm 0.41\), \(c_4' = 7.73 \pm 0.18\)
- LEC values from nucleon anomalous magnetic moments: \(c_6' = 7.41 \pm 0.00\), \(c_7' = -7.17 \pm 0.00\)
- Nucleon mass used for normalization: \(m = (m_p + m_n)/2 = 0.93892\) GeV
- Kaon loop correction factor relative to pion: \((M_K / M_\pi)^3 \sim 46\)

## Context

This review extends the meson-sector chiral perturbation theory developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) and Weinberg (1979) to the baryon sector, addressing the problem first identified by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) that baryon masses do not vanish in the chiral limit. The heavy-baryon formalism follows Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and resolves the power-counting issue by using velocity-dependent fields. The work enables systematic calculations of low-energy processes such as Compton scattering, pion photoproduction, and nucleon polarizabilities, and it confronts chiral predictions with precise data from experiments at Illinois, Saskatoon, Oak Ridge, and Mainz. Remaining challenges include the treatment of the scalar three-flavor sector and the role of the nearby spin-3/2 decuplet.
