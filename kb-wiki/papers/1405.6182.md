# Finite pulse effects on $e^{+}e^{-}$ pair creation from strong electric fields

> We investigate electron-positron pair creation from the vacuum in a pulsed electric background field. Employing the Sauter-type pulsed field $E(t)=E_0 {\rm sech}^2 (t/τ)$ with height $E_0$ and width $τ$, we demonstrate explicitly the interplay between the nonperturbative and perturbative aspects of pair creation in the background field. We analytically compute the number of produced pairs from the vacuum in the Sauter-type field, and the result reproduces Schwinger's nonperturbative formula in the long pulse limit (the constant field limit), while in the short pulse limit it coincides with the leading-order perturbative result. We show that two dimensionless parameters $ν= |eE_0| τ^2$ and $γ= |eE_0| τ/m_e$ characterize the importance of multiple interactions with the fields and the transition from the perturbative to the nonperturbative regime. We also find that pair creation is enhanced compared to Schwinger's formula when the field strength is relativity weak $|eE_0|/m_e^2 \lesssim 1$ and the pulse duration is relatively short $m_e τ\lesssim 1$, and reveal that the enhancement is predominantly described by the lowest order perturbation with a single photon.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for a Sauter-type pulsed electric field, the number of produced electron-positron pairs can be computed analytically and reproduces Schwinger's nonperturbative formula in the long-pulse limit while matching leading-order perturbative results in the short-pulse limit; crucially, pair creation is enhanced relative to Schwinger's formula when the field strength is relatively weak (|eE0|/m_e^2 less than or approximately 1) and the pulse duration is relatively short (m_e tau less than or approximately 1), with this enhancement predominantly described by lowest-order perturbation theory with a single photon.

## Method
The authors analytically computed the number of electron-positron pairs created from the vacuum in a Sauter-type pulsed electric field E(t) = E0 sech^2(t/tau) using both lowest-order perturbation theory and the nonperturbative Bogoliubov coefficient method (solving the Dirac equation exactly for this field profile). They compared the momentum-dependent number density and total number of produced electrons from both approaches as functions of the pulse duration tau and field strength E0, identifying two dimensionless parameters nu = |eE0| tau^2 and gamma = |eE0| tau/m_e that characterize the transition between perturbative and nonperturbative regimes.

## Key numbers
- The total number of produced electrons per volume in the perturbative result is N/V = m^3 |eE0/m^2|^2 f(pi m tau), where f(x) asymptotically behaves as x/(18 pi^2) for x less than or approximately 1 and as (x^(5/2)/(2 pi^(7/2))) (1 + 7/(16x)) e^(-2x) for x greater than or approximately 1.
- The peak position of the number density as a function of tau occurs at p0 tau_peak approximately 0.61, independent of field strength, with peak value (1/V) d^3 N/dp^3 approximately (5.0 x 10^(-4)) x (1 - pz^2/p0^2) |eE0/p0^2|^2.
- For subcritical field strength |eE0|/m^2 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8, the perturbative and nonperturbative results agree for short pulses m tau much less than 1, with the nonperturbative result approaching Schwinger's value (proportional to exp(-pi m^2/|eE0|)) in the long-pulse limit.
- For supercritical field strength |eE0|/m^2 = 5, 10, 15, and 20, the perturbative description applies only for very short pulses m tau much less than 1, with deviation increasing for longer pulses.

## Context
This work extends the study of the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger 1951) for electron-positron pair creation from a constant electric field to time-dependent pulsed fields, specifically the Sauter-type profile. It provides analytic results that clarify the interplay between perturbative and nonperturbative regimes, complementing previous numerical studies using quantum kinetic equations (Hebenstreit 2008, Kohlfurst 2013, Skokov and Levai 2009). The findings enable quantitative predictions for pair creation in intense laser experiments and heavy-ion collisions where fields have finite duration, and they show that the enhancement over Schwinger's formula for weak, short pulses is a perturbative effect rather than a nonperturbative one as previously suggested by Skokov and Levai (2009).
