# Three-body recombination in Bose gases with large scattering length

> An effective field theory for the three-body system with large scattering length is applied to three-body recombination to a weakly-bound s-wave state in a Bose gas. Our model independent analysis demonstrates that the three-body recombination constant alpha is not universal, but can take any value between zero and 67.9 \hbar a^4/m, where a is the scattering length. Other low-energy three-body observables can be predicted in terms of a and alpha. Near a Feshbach resonance, alpha should oscillate between those limits as the magnetic field B approaches the point where a -> infinity. In any interval of B over which a increases by a factor of 22.7, alpha should have a zero.

## Key claim

The three-body recombination constant α for Bose gases with large scattering length is not universal, but can take any value between zero and 67.9 ħ a⁴/m, where a is the scattering length; the abstract's claim of "model independent analysis" hedges that this result applies only to recombination into a weakly-bound s-wave state, and the coefficient oscillates as a function of ln(a) rather than being a fixed constant.

## Method

The authors applied an effective field theory (EFT) for non-relativistic bosons with large scattering length, introducing a dummy dimer field to rewrite the Lagrangian and summing loop diagrams to all orders via an integral equation for the three-body recombination amplitude. Renormalization of the three-body force required a single three-body parameter Λ* that cancels ultraviolet cutoff dependence, with the coupling constant h varying periodically in ln(Λ) as described by H(Λ) ≈ −tan[s₀ ln(Λ/Λ*) − π/4] where s₀ ≈ 1.0064.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of the dimensionless coefficient C in α = C ħ a⁴/m is C_max = 67.9 ± 0.7, with phase δ = 1.74 ± 0.02 in the fit formula α ≈ (ħ a⁴/m) C_max cos²[s₀ ln(a Λ*) + δ].
- The asymptotic constant s₀ ≈ 1.0064 determines the periodicity: successive zeros of α occur when a increases by a factor of exp(π/s₀) ≈ 22.7.
- The binding energy of the weakly-bound dimer is B_d = ħ²/(m a²).
- For the 907 G Feshbach resonance in ²³Na, the authors give example values of Λ* = 1.94 nm⁻¹, 1.16 nm⁻¹, and 0.65 nm⁻¹ for the predicted α(B) curves.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for three-body systems with large scattering length developed by Bedaque, Braaten, and Hammer (1999) for neutron-deuteron scattering and the triton binding energy, applying it to three-body recombination in Bose gases. It confirms the results of Nielsen and Macek (1999), who found C_max ≈ 68.4 using hidden crossing theory, and Esry et al. (1999), who found C_max = 60 ± 13 from coupled channel calculations, while contradicting the universal value C = 3.9 claimed by Fedichev et al. (1996). The work enables prediction of other low-energy three-body observables (such as the atom-dimer scattering length) in terms of a and the three-body parameter, and predicts oscillatory behavior of the recombination rate near Feshbach resonances that could be tested experimentally.
