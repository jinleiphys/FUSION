## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules, specifically both finite energy sum rules (FESR) and Borel sum rules (BSR), applied to the off-diagonal correlation function of interpolating currents for őõ and ő£^0 baryons in the presence of an isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium. The operator product expansion (OPE) was carried out up to dimension 4, keeping Lorentz-tensor and isospin-asymmetric operators that have non-vanishing expectation values in the asymmetric medium. The in-medium condensates were evaluated using a low-density expansion, and the sum rules were constructed by matching the OPE side to a phenomenological ansatz for the spectral function that includes the mixing matrix element.

## Context

This work extends prior QCD sum rule studies of ő£^0-őõ mixing in vacuum (ZHY, NYM) to the isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium, analogous to earlier studies of ŌĀ^0-ŌČ mixing in asymmetric matter (DHP). The key new feature is that baryon mixing, unlike meson mixing, has multiple Lorentz structures (scalar and vector) in the medium, leading to different mixing angles for particles and anti-particles, similar to the scalar and vector self-energies of nucleons and anti-nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter (Serot and Walecka). The results enable future studies of hyperon properties in neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron stars.
