# In-medium Σ^0-ΛMixing in QCD Sum Rules

> The Σ^0-Λmixing angle in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium is investigated by using QCD sum rules. From the general consideration of the in-medium baryonic correlations, in-medium baryon mixings are shown to have several Lorentz structures such as the scalar mixing angle θ^S and the vector mixing angle θ^V. This causes a difference between the particle mixing θ(= θ^S + θ^V) and the anti-particle mixing \barθ (= θ^S - θ^V). From the finite energy sum rules for the Σ^0-Λmixing, we find that the in-medium part of the mixing angle has a relation θ^S_{Med} \simeq - θ^V_{Med} in the isospin-asymmetric medium. This implies that the medium affects mainly the anti-particle mixing. From the Borel sum rules, we obtain |\barθ - θ_0| \simeq 0.39 |(ρ_n - ρ_p)| / ρ_0 with θ_0, ρ_n, ρ_p and ρ_0 being the vacuum mixing angle, the neutron density, the proton density and the normal nuclear matter density respectively.

## Key claim

The in-medium Σ^0-Λ mixing angle in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter splits into scalar (θ^S) and vector (θ^V) Lorentz structures, leading to a difference between particle mixing (θ = θ^S + θ^V) and anti-particle mixing (θ̄ = θ^S − θ^V); the medium primarily affects anti-particle mixing because the in-medium parts satisfy θ^S_Med ≃ −θ^V_Med.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules, specifically both finite energy sum rules (FESR) and Borel sum rules (BSR), applied to the off-diagonal correlation function of interpolating currents for Λ and Σ^0 baryons in the presence of an isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium. The operator product expansion (OPE) was carried out up to dimension 4, keeping Lorentz-tensor and isospin-asymmetric operators that have non-vanishing expectation values in the asymmetric medium. The in-medium condensates were evaluated using a low-density expansion, and the sum rules were constructed by matching the OPE side to a phenomenological ansatz for the spectral function that includes the mixing matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mixing angle |θ_0| is reported as 1.4 × 10^−3 from prior QCD sum rule work (NYM) and 7 × 10^−3 from another QCD sum rule study (ZHY), comparable to estimates of ~1 × 10^−2 from the naive quark model (Isgur) and chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, Gasser et al.).
- The Borel sum rule yields the relation |θ̄ − θ_0| ≃ 0.39 |(ρ_n − ρ_p)| / ρ_0, where ρ_n is neutron density, ρ_p is proton density, and ρ_0 is normal nuclear matter density.
- The QCD parameters used include the average light quark mass m̂ = (m_u + m_d)/2 = 7.7 MeV, the quark mass difference δm/m̂ = 0.51, and the vacuum quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = (−275 MeV)^3.

## Context

This work extends prior QCD sum rule studies of Σ^0-Λ mixing in vacuum (ZHY, NYM) to the isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium, analogous to earlier studies of ρ^0-ω mixing in asymmetric matter (DHP). The key new feature is that baryon mixing, unlike meson mixing, has multiple Lorentz structures (scalar and vector) in the medium, leading to different mixing angles for particles and anti-particles, similar to the scalar and vector self-energies of nucleons and anti-nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter (Serot and Walecka). The results enable future studies of hyperon properties in neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron stars.
