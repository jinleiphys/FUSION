# The isospin breaking effect on baryons with Nf=2 domain wall fermions

> We study the isospin breaking effect on octet baryons. Using the two-flavor dynamical domain-wall QCD configurations combined with the quenched non-compact QED configurations, the electromagnetic mass splittings between isomultiplets (p, n), (Sigma^+, Sigma^0, Sigma^-), (Xi^0, Xi^-) are investigated. We evaluate the main source of statistical fluctuations in the two-point correlation function, and find that the elimination of O(e) fluctuation (e: the QED charge) is essential to extract the signal. Preliminary results for $m_p - m_n$ as well as other mass splittings are presented. Possible origin of systematic uncertainty is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first non-perturbative lattice QCD+QED calculation of electromagnetic mass splittings for octet baryons using dynamical (unquenched) QCD, but the results are preliminary and afflicted by large statistical fluctuations that prevent a reliable chiral extrapolation to the physical quark mass; the reported numbers are therefore only qualitative or upper bounds.

## Method

The authors combine \(N_f=2\) dynamical domain-wall QCD configurations (generated by the RBC collaboration on a \(16^3\times 32\) lattice with \(L_s=12\), \(\beta=0.8\), and sea quark masses \(m_{\rm sea}=0.02,0.03,0.04\)) with quenched non-compact QED configurations. Baryon two-point correlation functions are computed for all octet baryons, including off-diagonal correlators in the \(Q=0, S=-1\) channel, where a variational method is used to extract the \(\Sigma^0\) as a first excited state. To eliminate \(\mathcal{O}(e)\) statistical noise, the average of correlation functions with \(e=+\sqrt{4\pi\alpha_{\rm em}}\) and \(e=-\sqrt{4\pi\alpha_{\rm em}}\) is taken for each QED charge.

## Key numbers

* Proton-neutron mass difference from the EM effect at physical QED charge \(\alpha_{\rm em}^{\rm phy}=1/137\): at each quark mass \(m=0.02,0.03,0.04\) the results are shown in Fig. 5; the values are roughly consistent with the model estimate of 0.76(30) MeV from the Cottingham formula (Gasser and Leutwyler).
* The splitting \(m_{\Sigma^+}+m_{\Sigma^-}-2m_{\Sigma^0}\) from the EM effect at physical QED charge: at each quark mass the results are shown in Fig. 6; after linear chiral extrapolation only a zero-consistent result is obtained, with an upper bound smaller than the experimental value of 1.5 MeV.
* The lattice spacing is determined to be \(1.691(53)\) GeV from the \(\rho\) meson mass \(m_\rho=770\) MeV.
* The physical volume is approximately \(L^3 \simeq (1.9\ {\rm fm})^3\).
* The strange quark mass is fixed to \(m_s = 0.0446\) from kaon input.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering quenched QCD+QED study of Duncan et al. by using \(N_f=2\) dynamical domain-wall fermions, which eliminates both the QCD quenching artifact and the artificial chiral symmetry breaking contamination present in earlier Wilson-fermion calculations. The meson-sector results for \(\pi^+-\pi^0\) and \(K^+-K^0\) mass splittings were previously reported by the same collaboration (N. Yamada et al., Lattice 2005). The present baryon results are preliminary and suffer from large statistical noise, especially at the lightest quark mass, and from unknown finite-volume artifacts; the authors plan to address these with larger-volume \(N_f=2+1\) configurations from the RBC-UKQCD collaboration.
