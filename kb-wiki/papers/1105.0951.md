# Review of two-photon exchange in electron scattering

> We review the role of two-photon exchange (TPE) in electron-hadron scattering, focusing in particular on hadronic frameworks suitable for describing the low and moderate Q^2 region relevant to most experimental studies. We discuss the effects of TPE on the extraction of nucleon form factors and their role in the resolution of the proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio puzzle. The implications of TPE on various other observables, including neutron form factors, electroproduction of resonances and pions, and nuclear form factors, are summarized. Measurements seeking to directly identify TPE effects, such as through the angular dependence of polarization measurements, nonlinear epsilon contributions to the cross sections, and via e+ p to e- p cross section ratios, are also outlined. In the weak sector, we describe the role of TPE and gamma-Z interference in parity-violating electron scattering, and assess their impact on the extraction of the strange form factors of the nucleon and the weak charge of the proton.

## Key claim

This review argues that two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections, while historically assumed to be negligible, are essential to resolve the long-standing discrepancy between proton form factor ratios extracted from unpolarized Rosenbluth separations and those from polarization transfer measurements, and that current TPE calculations are consistent with all existing experimental constraints.

## Method

The authors review hadronic frameworks for computing TPE corrections in elastic electron-nucleon scattering at low and moderate momentum transfers, including hadronic box-diagram calculations and dispersion relation approaches. They also survey experimental constraints from comparisons of electron-proton and positron-proton cross sections, nonlinear epsilon contributions to unpolarized cross sections, and angular dependence of polarization observables, and extend the analysis to gamma-Z interference in parity-violating electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- The proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer methods reaches a factor of approximately 3 at Q^2 around 5-6 (GeV/c)^2
- TPE corrections to the reduced cross section sigma_R are typically at the level of a few percent for Q^2 below several (GeV/c)^2, but can be significantly larger at backward angles
- The fine structure constant alpha = e^2/4pi is approximately 1/137
- The proton magnetic moment mu_p = 2.793 and neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -1.913 in nuclear magnetons
- The variable epsilon is defined as epsilon = (1 + 2(1+tau)tan^2(theta/2))^(-1), where tau = Q^2/(4M^2)

## Context

This review extends earlier work on nucleon form factors by Perdrisat, Punjabi and Vanderhaeghen (2007) and Arrington, Roberts and Zanotti (2007), and builds on the TPE calculations of Blunden, Melnitchouk and Tjon (2003, 2005). It synthesizes the theoretical and experimental progress since the form factor discrepancy was confirmed around 2000, and identifies the set of ongoing experiments (including VEPP-3, OLYMPUS, and Jefferson Lab experiments E07-005 and E05-017) that will provide direct verification of TPE calculations, enabling reliable corrections for future precision measurements of nucleon structure and weak interactions.
