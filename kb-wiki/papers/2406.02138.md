# Eikonal calculation of (p,3p) cross sections for neutron-rich nuclei

> In this work, we present the first, to our knowledge, theoretical description of two-proton removal reactions with proton target $(p,3p)$ for medium-mass nuclei at intermediate energies and present cross sections for the different bound states of the residual nucleus with two fewer protons. The description of the reaction assumes two sequential ``quasifree'' collisions between the target and removed protons and considers eikonal propagation in between. The formalism is applied to the reactions $^{12}\mathrm{C}(p,3p)^{10}\mathrm{Be}$, $^{28}\mathrm{Mg}(p,3p)^{26}\mathrm{Ne}$ and $^{54}\mathrm{Ca}(p,3p)^{52}\mathrm{Ar}$, finding reasonable agreement to experimental data for the $^{12}$C target and an overestimation of a factor $\sim3$ for the more neutron-rich and $^{54}$Ca, which is similar to the results found in two-proton knockout experiments with $^9$Be and $^{12}$C targets.

## Key claim

This work presents the first theoretical description of (p,3p) two-proton removal reactions for medium-mass nuclei at intermediate energies, but the formalism overestimates experimental cross sections for neutron-rich nuclei (²⁸Mg and ⁵⁴Ca) by a factor of approximately 3, a discrepancy similar to that seen in two-proton knockout experiments with ⁹Be and ¹²C targets.

## Method

The reaction is modeled as two sequential quasifree collisions between the incoming proton and two protons in the target nucleus, with eikonal propagation of the projectile between collisions. The formalism uses eikonal wavefunctions for the protons, a zero-range approximation for the proton-proton interaction, and the free nucleon-nucleon cross section to describe each collision. The overlap function for the two removed protons is computed from shell-model two-nucleon amplitudes and single-particle wavefunctions from Woods-Saxon potentials.

## Key numbers

* For ¹²C(p,3p)¹⁰Be at 1.05 GeV/A, the calculation shows reasonable agreement with experimental data from Ols83, with an estimated scaling factor of (A/(A+1))⁴ ≈ 0.7 for finite-mass effects.
* For ⁵⁴Ca(p,3p)⁵²Ar, the calculation overestimates experimental cross sections by a factor of approximately 3.
* The integration step for the spatial coordinates r₁ and r₂ is 0.2 fm, giving convergence to approximately 5%.
* Single-particle wavefunctions use Woods-Saxon potentials with diffusivity a = 0.7 fm, with radii adjusted to reproduce rms radii from Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkX interaction (Bro98).

## Context

This work extends the quasifree reaction formalism previously applied to (p,2p) reactions (Jacob 1966, Aumann 2013, Moro 2015, Oganessian 2015) to the more complex (p,3p) case, addressing a need identified by Tanaka (2019) where the lack of such theory hindered experimental analysis. The approach is analogous to the eikonal sudden description of two-proton knockout with heavier targets (Tostevin 2004, Simpson 2009) but adapted for proton targets where target recoil is significant. The results enable future use of hydrogen active targets (Becher 2015, Obertelli 2014) for spectroscopic studies of exotic nuclei via (p,3p) reactions, though the systematic overestimation for neutron-rich systems requires further investigation of reaction mechanism or structure inputs.
