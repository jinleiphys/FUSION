# Shear viscosity in microscopic calculations of A+A collisions at energies of Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA)

> Time evolution of shear viscosity $η$, entropy density $s$, and their ratio $η/ s$ in the central area of central gold-gold collisions at NICA energy range is studied within the UrQMD transport model. The extracted values of energy density, net baryon density and net strangeness density are used as input to (i) statistical model of ideal hadron gas to define temperature, baryo-chemical potential and strangeness chemical potential, and to (ii) UrQMD box with periodic boundary conditions to study the relaxation process of highly excited matter. During the relaxation stage, the shear viscosity is determined in the framework of Green-Kubo approach. The procedure is performed for each of 20 time slices, corresponding to conditions in the central area of the fireball at times from 1~fm/$c$ to 20~fm/$c$. For all tested energies the ratio $η/ s$ reaches minimum, $\left( η/s \right)_{min} \approx 0.3$ at $t \approx 5$~fm/$c$. Then it increases up to the late stages of the system evolution. This rise is accompanied by the drop of both, temperature and strangeness chemical potential, and increase of baryo-chemical potential.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) in central Au+Au collisions at NICA energies reaches a minimum of approximately 0.3 at about 5 fm/c after the collision, but this minimum is about four times larger than the theoretical lower bound of 1/4π, and the result is conditional on the matter in the central cell being out of equilibrium at early times (t ≤ 5 fm/c) where the thermodynamic parameters are extracted assuming full equilibrium.

## Method

Central Au+Au collisions at laboratory energies 10A, 20A, 30A, and 40A GeV (center-of-mass energies 4.5 to 8.8 GeV) were simulated using the UrQMD transport model. Energy density, net baryon density, and net strangeness density were extracted from a central cell of volume 125 fm³ at 20 time slices from 1 to 20 fm/c. These values were used as input to (i) a statistical model of an ideal hadron gas to determine temperature, baryochemical potential, and strangeness chemical potential, and (ii) a UrQMD box with periodic boundary conditions to study relaxation processes, where shear viscosity was extracted via the Green-Kubo formalism.

## Key numbers

- Minimum η/s ≈ 0.3 at t ≈ 5 fm/c for all tested collision energies (10A to 40A GeV)
- Central cell volume: 125 fm³ (5 × 5 × 5 fm³)
- Box volume: 1000 fm³ (10 × 10 × 10 fm³)
- Ensemble of 51200 central Au+Au collisions per energy for cell calculations; 12800 box simulations per each of 80 points
- Relaxation times extracted over plateau region t₀ ∈ [200, 800] fm/c in box calculations
- At t = 6 fm/c, τ_int exceeds τ_fit by 25%; at t ≥ 15 fm/c, they agree within 10% accuracy
- Minimum η/s is about four times larger than the theoretical minimum 1/4π

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shear viscosity in hadronic matter using transport models, such as UrQMD box calculations for light mesons (Muronga 2004) and SMASH model calculations (Rose et al. 2018). It differs from prior work by studying the time evolution of η/s in heavy-ion collisions where all characteristics change rapidly, rather than temperature dependence at fixed chemical potentials. The results enable comparison with experimental data from NICA and FAIR, and show qualitatively different temperature dependence of η/s compared to SMASH calculations, which the authors attribute to the nonzero baryochemical and strangeness chemical potentials in their dynamical approach.
