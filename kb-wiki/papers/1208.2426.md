# Center Domains and their Phenomenological Consequences

> We argue that the domain structure of deconfined QCD matter, which can be inferred from the properties of the Polyakov loop, can simultaneously explain the two most prominent experimentally verified features of the quark-gluon plasma, namely its large opacity as well as its near ideal fluid properties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the domain structure of deconfined QCD matter, inferred from the Polyakov loop, can simultaneously explain both the large opacity and near-ideal fluid properties of the quark-gluon plasma, but this explanation depends on the specific form of the Polyakov loop potential and the assumption that the Polyakov loop takes values near zero inside domain walls.

## Method

The authors propose that the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy ion collisions contains center domains, regions where the Polyakov loop expectation value clusters around one of the three Z_3 minima of SU(3). They assume that domain walls between these regions are characterized by vanishing Polyakov loop values, similar to the confined phase, and then derive phenomenological consequences for shear viscosity and jet energy loss using kinetic theory and the Weizsäcker-Williams approximation for gluon radiation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale Q_s ≈ 1.5 GeV at RHIC and Q_s ≈ 2 GeV at LHC
- For T = 400 MeV and domain size R_d = 0.5 fm, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ≈ 0.125
- Energy loss per unit length dE/dx in the range (0.2–1) × C_2 × α_s GeV/fm, using ω_c ≈ 1–2 GeV, ω_0 ≈ 0.4 GeV, and R_d ≈ 0.5 fm
- Characteristic frequency of undulator radiation ω_L = 4πγ²/R_d for a massive parton with Lorentz factor γ

## Context

This work extends the concept of center domains previously discussed by Pisarski (2000) and Dumitru (2000), and builds on lattice evidence for such domains even with dynamical quarks from Borsanyi (2010) and Danzer (2010). The mechanism for lowering shear viscosity resembles the anomalous viscosity approach of Asakawa (2006), but with a different origin for the small mean free path. The paper enables a unified nonperturbative explanation for both the hydrodynamic behavior and jet quenching observed at RHIC and LHC, and suggests that the domain structure persists throughout the QGP evolution until hadronization.
