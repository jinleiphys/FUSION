# Lattice Gauge Description of Colliding Nuclei

> We propose a novel formalism for simultaneously describing both, the hard and soft parton dynamics in ultrarelativistic collisions of nuclei. The emission of gluons from the initially coherent parton configurations of the colliding nuclei and low-$p_t$ color coherence effects are treated in the framework of a Yang-Mills transport equation on a coupled lattice-particle system. A collision term is added to the transport equation to account for the remaining intermediate and high-$p_t$ interactions in an infrared finite manner.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel formalism that, for the first time, allows the simultaneous treatment of both hard and soft parton dynamics in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions within a consistent transport approach, by coupling a Yang-Mills transport equation on a lattice-particle system to a collision term for intermediate and high-\(p_t\) interactions. The hedge is that the precise choice of the momentum cutoff \(k_c\) separating the two regimes is not expected to be important, but this must be verified by future numerical calculations.

## Method

The authors develop a Hamiltonian lattice gauge formulation in 3+1 dimensions, using the temporal gauge (\(A_0 = 0\)), where the soft gluon fields are discretized on a spatial lattice and the valence quarks of the colliding nuclei are represented as classical point particles with color charge. The dynamics of the system are governed by the coupled Wong equations (for particle motion and color charge precession) and the Yang-Mills equations (for the mean color field), which are solved numerically. A stochastic collision term, based on perturbative QCD matrix elements for processes like \(q+q' \to q+q'\) and \(g+g \to g+g\), is added to the transport equation to handle hard, short-range interactions, with an infrared cutoff \(k_c = \pi/a\) imposed by the lattice spacing to regulate the gluon propagators.

## Key numbers

- The lattice cutoff is set to \(k_c = \pi/a\), where \(a\) is the elementary lattice spacing, used to separate the perturbative regime (large \(k_{\rm T}\)) from the non-perturbative regime (small \(k_{\rm T}\)).
- Each nucleon is represented by two quarks (instead of three) for the SU(2) gauge group, initially carrying opposite color charge.
- The collision term includes processes A through D: \(q+q' \to q+q'\), \(q+q \to q+q\), \(q+\bar q \to g+g\), and \(g+g \to g+g\), with cross sections given by \(\frac{{\rm d}\hat \sigma}{{\rm d}\hat t} = \frac{1}{16 \pi \hat s^2} \langle |{\cal M}^{(c)}(\hat s, \hat t, \hat u)|^2 \rangle\).
- The constraint for hard interactions is \(Q^2(\hat s, \hat t, \hat u) > k_c^2\), ensuring only momentum transfers larger than \(k_c\) contribute to the total cross section.

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model of Geiger (1992) by addressing its failure to describe the coherent initial state of colliding nuclei and the divergence of perturbative QCD at small transverse momenta. It builds on the random light-cone source model (RLSM) of McLerran (1994) and Kovchegov (1996), which describes the mean color field of a heavy nucleus, and on the lattice formulation of Krasnitz (1997) for solving the classical RLSM equations in 2+1 dimensions. The formalism enables future numerical studies of gluon radiation and thermalization in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and it offers a unified treatment of coherence effects that avoids the infrared cutoff problems of earlier parton cascade models.
