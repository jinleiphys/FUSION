# Coriolis mixing of the K=1 and K=0 mixed symmetry states in the well deformed even-even nuclei

> The Coriolis matrix elements responsible for mixing of the $1^+ K=1$ and $1^+ K=0$ states are calculated in the framework of the Quasiparticle Phonon Model for several Gd and Dy isotopes. In many considered cases these matrix elements are equal to several tens of keV and are comparable with energy distances between the mixed levels. The results obtained indicates that Gd isotopes could be more suitable for finding deviations from Alaga rules in M1 transitions from $1^+$ state to the states of the ground band.

# Coriolis mixing of the K=1 and K=0 mixed symmetry states in the well deformed even-even nuclei

## Key claim

The Coriolis matrix elements responsible for mixing of the 1+ K=1 and 1+ K=0 states in Gd and Dy isotopes are calculated to be several tens of keV, and in many cases are comparable with the energy distances between the mixed levels; however, the values fluctuate significantly from one pair of states to another, and the authors conclude that Gd isotopes could be more suitable for finding deviations from Alaga rules in M1 transitions, rather than claiming a definitive observation of such deviations.

## Method

The authors employed the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPNM) to construct the wave functions of the 1+ K=1 and 1+ K=0 states in terms of RPA phonon operators built from magnetic-type two-quasiparticle creation operators. The Coriolis interaction matrix elements between these states were then calculated using the expression derived from the intrinsic angular momentum operator j_x and the Bogoliubov transformation coefficients, with the single-particle spectrum obtained from a deformed axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential including quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters.

## Key numbers

- For 156Gd: Coriolis matrix element of 53 keV between the 1+ K=1 state at 2790 keV and the 1+ K=0 state at 2750 keV; 68 keV between the 2790 keV K=1 state and the 2800 keV K=0 state; 209 keV between the 3110 keV K=1 state and the 2750 keV K=0 state
- For 158Gd: Coriolis matrix element of 112 keV between the 3220 keV K=1 state and the 3190 keV K=0 state; 39 keV between the 3220 keV K=1 state and the 3000 keV K=0 state
- For 160Gd: Coriolis matrix element of 52 keV between the 2710 keV K=1 state and the 2760 keV K=0 state; 78 keV between the 3020 keV K=1 state and the 3060 keV K=0 state
- For 160Dy: Coriolis matrix element of 60 keV between the 2930 keV K=1 state and the 3020 keV K=0 state; values of 1 keV for several other pairs
- For 162Dy: Coriolis matrix element of 35 keV between the 3090 keV K=1 state and the 2930 keV K=0 state; values of 1-5 keV for other pairs
- For 164Dy: Coriolis matrix element of 9 keV between the 3070 keV K=1 state and the 3200 keV K=0 state
- The calculated Coriolis matrix elements range from 1 keV to 200 keV across all considered nuclei

## Context

This work extends the microscopic treatment of mixed-symmetry states in deformed nuclei, following the experimental observation by Beck1 of a 1+ K=0 state in 164Dy at around 3 MeV with branching ratios deviating significantly from Alaga rule. The calculations are performed within the QPNM framework developed by Soloviev, and the average magnitude of the mixing matrix elements is comparable to the approximate 100 keV value obtained by Scholten in the IBM-2 approach. The results enable future experimental searches for K-mixing effects in M1 transitions, particularly in Gd isotopes where the calculated matrix elements are larger on average than in Dy isotopes.
