# Evolution of spectral properties along the O(6)-U(5) transition in the interacting boson model. I. Level dynamics

> We investigate the evolution of quantal spectra and the corresponding wave functions along the [O(6)-U(5)]$\supset$O(5) transition of the interacting boson model. The model is integrable in this regime and its ground state passes through a second-order structural phase transition. We show that the whole spectrum as a function of the Hamiltonian control parameter, as well as structures of all excited states, exhibit rather organized and correlated behaviors, that provide deeper insight into the nature of this transitional path.

# Evolution of spectral properties along the O(6)-U(5) transition in the interacting boson model. I. Level dynamics

## Key claim

The paper shows that the entire spectrum of the interacting boson model along the integrable O(6)-U(5) transitional path exhibits highly organized and correlated behaviors, including a "shock-wave" pattern propagating from upper to lower parts of the spectrum that terminates at the ground state at the critical point eta_c = 4/5 = 0.8, but this mechanism is demonstrated only for finite boson numbers (N=40, 80, 20) and the authors note that the phase-transitional scenario is generically allowed only in the limit of infinite Hilbert-space dimensions (N approaching infinity).

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalize the interacting boson model Hamiltonian H(eta) = a[-(1-eta)/N^2 (Q.Q) + (eta/N) n_d] for boson numbers N=40, 80, and 20, considering only states with zero angular momentum (l=0). They analyze the evolution of eigenvalues and eigenstates as functions of the control parameter eta in [0,1] using the Pechukas-Yukawa theory of level dynamics, which describes level energies as particles interacting via a two-dimensional Coulomb force, and separate states by seniority quantum number v to exploit the unbroken O(5) dynamical symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The critical point for the second-order phase transition is located at eta_c = 4/5 = 0.8, where the deformed ground-state configuration changes into the pure s-boson condensate.
- For N=40 bosons, the level bunching pattern around E approximately 0 shows a regular sequence of total numbers of levels involved in individual bunches: 1, 2, 3, 4, ... when descending from eta=0.8 to approximately 0.4.
- For N=80 bosons with v=0, the spectral dispersion (squared spread) is maximally compressed at eta_0 approximately 0.56, while the kinetic energy from the Pechukas-Yukawa integral of motion shows a minimum at eta approximately 0.67.
- For N=20 bosons, branch points in the complex parameter plane for v=0 form a chain at eta < 0.8 that approach close to the real axis.
- The approximate focal point of the transition is located at (eta, E) = (1, 1/2), corresponding to an average d-boson number of approximately N/2 in O(6) eigenstates with v=0.
- The ground-state deformation parameter beta_gs drops to zero as beta_gs proportional to sqrt(eta_c - eta), and the ground-state average of the perturbation operator V behaves as proportional to (eta_c - eta) in the N approaching infinity asymptotic limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of IBM phase transitions (Dieperink, 1980; Jolie, 2002; Cejnar, 2003) by providing a detailed analysis of level dynamics along the integrable O(6)-U(5) transitional path, which preserves O(5) dynamical symmetry and allows analytical solution (Pan, 1998; Dukelsky, 2001). The results connect to the E(5) critical-point dynamical symmetry (Iachello, 2000) and quasidynamical symmetry (Rowe, 2004), and the authors show that the organized pattern of branch points in the complex parameter plane is a privilege of only this transitional class. The findings enable deeper understanding of the triggering mechanism for quantum phase transitions and will be complemented by a semiclassical analysis using Gutzwiller and Berry-Tabor trace formulas in Part II of this work.
