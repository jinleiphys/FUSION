# A Standard Model Explanation for the "ATOMKI Anomaly"

> Using the $e^+e^-$ pair spectrometer at the 5 MV Van de Graaff accelerator at the Institute for Nuclear Research, Hungarian Academy of Sciences (ATOMKI), Krasznahorkay et al. have claimed a 6.8$σ$ excess at high $e^+e^-$ opening angles in the internal pair creation isoscalar transition $^8\mathrm{Be}(18.15)\to\,^8\mathrm{Be}\,e^+e^-$. A hypothetical gauge boson with the mass circa $17$ MeV, "X17", has been proposed as an explanation for the excess. We show that the observed experimental structure can be reproduced within the Standard Model by adding the full set of second-order corrections and the interference terms to the Born-level decay amplitudes considered by Krasznahorkay et al. We implement a detailed model of the ATOMKI detector, and also show how experimental selection and acceptance bias exacerbate the apparent difference between the experimental data and the Born-level prediction.

# A Standard Model Explanation for the "ATOMKI Anomaly"

## Key claim

The observed 6.8σ excess at high e+e- opening angles in the 8Be(18.15) internal pair conversion transition, previously attributed to a hypothetical 17 MeV "X17" boson, can be reproduced within the Standard Model by including second-order QED corrections and interference terms to the Born-level decay amplitudes, combined with detector acceptance and selection biases that exacerbate the apparent discrepancy.

## Method

The authors computed the full next-to-leading-order (NLO) QED corrections to the 8Be* → 8Be e+e- decay, including vacuum polarization, vertex corrections with soft-photon bremsstrahlung treatment, and box diagrams with 8Be* and 8Be in the loops, using FeynArts, FormCalc, and LoopTools for numerical evaluation. They constructed a Monte Carlo model of the ATOMKI detector based on published descriptions, including angular acceptance blocks and an energy disparity cut |y| < 0.5, and fitted the simulated opening-angle distributions to the experimental data by varying the normalization of the Born contribution and the coefficient of the interference term.

## Key numbers

- The ATOMKI experiment reported a 6.8σ excess at high e+e- opening angles in the 8Be(18.15) internal pair conversion transition, with a proposed X17 boson mass of approximately 17 MeV (circa 17 MeV).
- The best fit to the ATOMKI angular distribution using the Standard Model NLO calculation yields χ² = 38 for 7 degrees of freedom.
- The best-fit interference coefficient is ε = 57.72 ± 0.06.
- The detector block sizes in the Monte Carlo model are set to Δθ = 0.59 rad and δφ = 0.58 rad, with uncertainty of roughly 0.02 rad in each dimension.
- The energy disparity cut removes pairs with |y| > 0.5, where y = (Ee- - Ee+)/(Ee- + Ee+).
- An additional 1 MeV of visible energy is added to the positron signal to approximate the difference in plastic scintillator response between electrons and positrons.

## Context

This work directly challenges the interpretation by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016) of the ATOMKI anomaly as evidence for a new protophobic gauge boson, as proposed by Feng et al. (2016). The authors extend earlier suggestions by Kalman (2020) and Zhang (2017) that subleading nonresonant contributions could produce the observed effects, and provide the first full second-order QED calculation of the 8Be* decay. The results call for independent measurements of internal pair conversion spectra in 8Be and other isoscalar nuclear systems with larger acceptance and better invariant mass resolution to discriminate between resonant and nonresonant contributions.
