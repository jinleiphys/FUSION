# Charged Majoron Emission in Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We examine in detail the predictions of the charged majoron model, introduced recently by Burgess and Cline, for 0+ --> 0+ double beta decay transitions. The relevant nuclear matrix elements are evaluated, within the quasiparticle random phase approximation, for 76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 128Te and 150Nd nuclei. The calculated transition rates turn out to be much smaller than the experimental upper limits on possible majoron emission, except in a small region of the model's parameter space.

# Charged Majoron Emission in Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

## Key claim

The charged majoron model (CMM) of Burgess and Cline predicts transition rates for neutrinoless double beta decay that are four to five orders of magnitude smaller than current experimental upper limits, except in a small region of parameter space requiring large mixing between exotic sterile neutrinos of mass above about 100 MeV and strong couplings among those sterile neutrinos.

## Method

The authors evaluated nuclear matrix elements for the charged majoron emission process using the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) for five nuclei: 76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 128Te, and 150Nd. They simplified the analytic expressions from Burgess and Cline (1994) for the transition amplitude, expressed the inverse half-life in terms of an effective majoron coupling g_CM, and performed numerical calculations with a delta-force interaction in an eleven-dimensional model space including oscillator shells 3hw, 4hw, and selected orbitals from 5hw.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge: the experimental upper limit on the majoron-emitting decay rate is < 6.0 x 10^-23 yr^-1; the kinematical factor G_CM = 2.10 x 10^-20 yr^-1; the nuclear matrix element |M|_CM = 0.28; the effective coupling g_CM needed to match the limit is < 0.19.
- For 82Se: the experimental upper limit on the majoron-emitting decay rate is < 6.2 x 10^-22 yr^-1; G_CM = 3.55 x 10^-19 yr^-1; |M|_CM = 0.28; g_CM needed is < 0.15.
- For 100Mo: the experimental upper limit on the majoron-emitting decay rate is < 3.0 x 10^-21 yr^-1; G_CM = 7.33 x 10^-19 yr^-1; |M|_CM = 0.31; g_CM needed is < 0.21.
- For 128Te: the experimental upper limit on the majoron-emitting decay rate is < 1.3 x 10^-25 yr^-1; G_CM = 5.24 x 10^-22 yr^-1; |M|_CM = 0.27; g_CM needed is < 0.059.
- For 150Nd: the experimental upper limit on the majoron-emitting decay rate is < 1.9 x 10^-21 yr^-1; G_CM = 6.06 x 10^-18 yr^-1; |M|_CM = 0.13; g_CM needed is < 0.077.
- The nuclear matrix element for 76Ge becomes insensitive to the heavy neutrino mass below about 100 MeV, with a 1/M^2 suppression above that threshold.

## Context

This work extends the charged majoron model introduced by Burgess and Cline (1993, 1994), who had estimated nuclear matrix elements for the same process. The authors provide a more detailed QRPA calculation, following the approach of Hirsch et al. (1990) that successfully described two-neutrino double beta decay rates. The results contradict the possibility that the charged majoron model could explain anomalous beta-beta decay events reported by some experimental groups, and they agree with a concurrent analysis by Hirsch et al. (1995). The work enables future experimental searches to set tighter constraints on the model's parameter space, particularly the mixing angle theta and the effective coupling g_CM.
