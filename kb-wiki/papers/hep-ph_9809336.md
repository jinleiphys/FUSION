# Feasibility of search for nuclear electric dipole moments at ion storage rings

> The sensitivity much better than $10^{-24} e$ cm may be expected in the searches for electric dipole moments (EDM) of $β$-active nuclei at ion storage rings. It would be a serious progress in studies of the CP-violation problem.

## Key claim

A sensitivity much better than $10^{-24}$ e cm may be expected in searches for electric dipole moments (EDM) of beta-active nuclei at ion storage rings, provided that the effective g-factor of the ion can be tuned close to 2 (as for the muon) and that the ion has an appreciable P-odd correlation in beta decay between the electron momentum and the nuclear spin.

## Method

The author adapts the proposed muon EDM storage-ring technique (Farley et al., proposal) to beta-active nuclei, where the spin precession due to the EDM interaction with a vertical magnetic field is isolated by compensating the magnetic-moment precession with a radial electric field. The accumulated vertical spin component is monitored via the parity-violating asymmetry in the angular distribution of beta-decay electrons relative to the nuclear spin. Candidate isotopes are selected based on magnetic moment values allowing fine-tuning of the effective anomalous magnetic moment a to near zero, using ions with closed electron shells to minimize atomic electron contributions.

## Key numbers

- The muon EDM experiment aims for a sensitivity of $10^{-24}$ e cm, improving by six orders of magnitude over current limits.
- The best upper limit on the neutron EDM is $d_n < 10^{-25}$ e cm.
- The dimensionless CP-odd nuclear force parameter $\xi$ has an upper limit $\xi < 2\cdot 10^{-3}$ from atomic experiments.
- Typical nuclear EDM induced by CP-odd forces is estimated as $d_N \sim 10^{-21} \xi$ e cm.
- The effective anomalous magnetic moment a for candidate ions ranges from approximately -0.0281 (for $^{93}$Rb$^{+27}$) to +0.0272 (for $^{82}$Br$^{+13}$), with uncertainties typically at the $10^{-3}$ level.
- The diamagnetic correction for $^{24}_{11}$Na$^{+5}$ changes its a-value from 0.015 to about -0.1.
- For ions with closed electron shells, the correction factor to a from neutron-proton mass difference and electron mass is typically $(8-9)\cdot 10^{-4}$.
- The condition for fine-tuning is $\mu \approx 0.8 J$ (nuclear magneton units), or more generally $\mu \leq 0.8 J$.

## Context

This work extends the muon EDM storage-ring proposal (Farley et al., proposal) to beta-active nuclei, noting that nuclear lifetimes can exceed the muon lifetime by many orders of magnitude and that nuclear magnetic moments are typically an order of magnitude smaller than the muon's, reducing spurious magnetic effects. The paper provides a table of candidate isotopes (e.g., $^{24}$Na, $^{60}$Co, $^{82}$Br, $^{93}$Rb, $^{94}$Rb, $^{110}$Ag, $^{118}$In, $^{120}$In, $^{121}$Sn, $^{125}$Sb, $^{131}$I, $^{133}$I, $^{133}$Xe, $^{134}$Cs, $^{136}$Cs, $^{137}$Cs, $^{139}$Cs, $^{141}$Cs, $^{143}$Cs, $^{140}$La, $^{160}$Tb, $^{170}$Tm, $^{177}$Lu, $^{183}$Ta, $^{192}$Ir, $^{196}$Au, $^{198}$Au, $^{203}$Hg, $^{222}$Fr, $^{223}$Fr, $^{224}$Fr, $^{242}$Am) with their magnetic moments, ion charges, anomalous magnetic moments, half-lives, quadrupole moments, and beta-decay branchings. The approach could compete with the best existing EDM studies from neutron experiments (Illinois and St. Petersburg groups) and atomic experiments (Lamoreaux et al.), even at the same $10^{-24}$ e cm sensitivity as the muon proposal.
