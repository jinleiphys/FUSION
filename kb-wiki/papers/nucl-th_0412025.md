# DVCS-Dissociation of the Deuteron and the EMC Effect

> The break-up of the deuteron during deeply-virtual Compton scattering, gamma* d --> gamma(*) n p, is explored. In the effective field theory describing nucleon dynamics at momenta below the pion mass, the EMC effect results from four-nucleon interactions with the twist-2 operators, appropriate for describing forward, and near-forward, matrix elements in the two-nucleon system. We point out that the break-up of the deuteron to low-energy final states during deeply-virtual Compton scattering is a process with which to explore strong-interaction physics closely related to that responsible for the EMC effect. The single-nucleon contribution to the break-up depends on the moments of the spin-dependent structure functions and contributions from local four-nucleon operators. Experimental deviations from the single-nucleon prediction would provide a probe of strong interactions complimentary to the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The EMC effect in the deuteron arises from local four-nucleon operators that contribute to matrix elements of twist-2 operators, and the break-up of the deuteron during deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) provides a complementary probe of the same short-distance strong-interaction physics; experimental deviations from the single-nucleon prediction for this break-up would reveal these nuclear effects, with the caveat that the analysis is valid only for momentum and energy transfers much less than the pion mass.

## Method

The authors construct the most general set of operators in pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) and its di-baryon formulation (dEFT(π̸)) that reproduce matrix elements of twist-2 operators in the two-nucleon system. They compute the leading-order contributions to deuteron matrix elements and to the DVCS-dissociation amplitude γ*d → γ(*)np, keeping only S-wave to S-wave interactions and working at momentum transfers |q| ≪ mπ. The amplitude for break-up into a 1S0 final state is expressed in terms of single-nucleon spin-dependent structure function moments and unknown four-nucleon operator coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter for relativistic corrections in the deuteron scales as γ²/M², where γ is the deuteron binding momentum and M the nucleon mass; this is suppressed relative to the nuclear expansion parameter γ/mπ.
- The single-nucleon contribution to the deuteron matrix element of isoscalar-vector twist-2 operators is given by 2⟨xⁿ⁻¹⟩_{q_V^{(0)}} F_C(|q|), where F_C(|q|) is the electric charge form factor; the nuclear contribution adds a term −η̃_{0;q}^{(n)} γ r₃/(1−γ r₃).
- For baryon-number current (n=1), η̃_{0;q}^{(1)}=0 and the matrix element reduces to the leading-order electric charge form factor of the deuteron.
- The DVCS-dissociation amplitude for a ¹S₀ final state contains a term proportional to ⟨xⁿ⁻¹⟩_{q_A^{(1)}} from single-nucleon contributions and a term proportional to σ̃^{(n)}_q from short-distance strong interactions.
- The authors estimate that deviations from the single-particle estimate for the break-up cross section will be at the level of 5% to 10%.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of nuclear matrix elements of twist-2 operators, building on prior calculations of nucleon structure function moments in chiral perturbation theory by Arndt (2001), Chen (2001), and others, and on the di-baryon formalism developed by Kaplan (1996, 1998) and Beane (2000). The analysis is complementary to model-based explanations of the EMC effect (e.g., Smith 2003, Steffens 2004) and to earlier work on DVCS on nuclei by Kirchner (2003) and Freund (2003). The paper enables future detailed calculations of deuteron break-up with realistic kinematics and suggests experimental measurements at Jefferson Laboratory or elsewhere to probe short-distance strong interactions in nuclei.
