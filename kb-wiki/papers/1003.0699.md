# Spectral densities for hot QCD plasmas in a leading log approximation

> We compute the spectral densities of $T^{μν}$ and $J^μ$ in high temperature QCD plasmas at small frequency and momentum,\, $ω,k \sim g^4 T$. The leading log Boltzmann equation is reformulated as a Fokker Planck equation with non-trivial boundary conditions, and the resulting partial differential equation is solved numerically in momentum space. The spectral densities of the current, shear, sound, and bulk channels exhibit a smooth transition from free streaming quasi-particles to ideal hydrodynamics. This transition is analyzed with conformal and non-conformal second order hydrodynamics, and a second order diffusion equation. We determine all of the second order transport coefficients which characterize the linear response in the hydrodynamic regime.

## Key claim
The paper computes spectral densities of the energy-momentum tensor and current in hot QCD plasmas at small frequency and momentum (omega, k ~ g^4 T) using a leading log approximation, showing a smooth transition from free-streaming quasi-particles to ideal hydrodynamics, but the analysis is limited to a leading log approximation where hard collisions and collinear bremsstrahlung are neglected for typical particles, except that bremsstrahlung determines boundary conditions at low momentum.

## Method
The leading log Boltzmann equation was reformulated as a Fokker-Planck equation with non-trivial boundary conditions, including an absorptive boundary condition at p=0 to account for particle flux to the Debye scale and rapid number-changing processes. The resulting partial differential equation was solved numerically in momentum space by discretizing momentum space and using an implicit scheme with conjugate gradient matrix inversion, with boundary conditions at high momentum selected by neglecting noise and keeping only the drag term to choose the non-exponentially growing solution.

## Key numbers
- The drag coefficient for a high momentum gluon in leading log approximation: mu_A = (g^2 C_A m_D^2)/(8 pi) log(T/m_D)
- Debye mass for pure glue theory: m_D^2 = (g^2 T^2/3) N_c
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio bound from RHIC: eta/s < 0.4 hbar, with preferred value close to AdS/CFT prediction eta/s = hbar/(4 pi)
- xi_B = integral of n_p(1+n_p) over momentum = T^3/6 for gluons; xi_F = T^3/12 for quarks
- The total rate for hard particles to absorb/emit a gluon from a ball of radius Delta p ~ gT: Gamma_{1<->2}^{total} ~ g^3 T

## Context
This work extends previous leading order calculations of spectral weights at zero momentum by Moore and Robert (2006) and Moore and Saremi (2008) to finite momentum and frequency, and provides perturbative spectral densities that can be compared to AdS/CFT results and lattice QCD simulations. The results enable lattice groups (such as Meyer 2009 and Aarts 2006) to better interpret Euclidean correlators measured at finite spatial momentum, and provide a framework for simulating jet-medium interactions in heavy ion collisions.
