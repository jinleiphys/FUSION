# Nuclear Half-Lives for Alpha Radioactivity of Elements with 100 $\leq$ Z $\leq$ 130

> Theoretical estimates for the half lives of about 1700 isotopes of heavy elements with Z from 100 to 130 are tabulated using theoretical Q-values. The quantum mechanical tunneling probabilities are calculated within a WKB framework using microscopic nuclear potentials. The microscopic nucleus-nucleus potentials are obtained by folding the densities of interacting nuclei with a density dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The alpha-decay half lives calculated in this formalism using the experimental Q-values were found to be in good agreement over a wide range of experimental data spanning about twenty orders of magnitude. The theoretical Q-values used for the present calculations are extracted from three different mass estimates viz. Myers-Swiatecki [MS], Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski [M] and Koura-Tachibana-Uno-Yamada [KUTY].

## Key claim

The paper provides theoretical estimates for alpha-decay half-lives of about 1700 isotopes with Z = 100–130, but the authors explicitly note that none of their calculations indicate extra stability at Z = 120 and N = 172 or 184, and that predictions using the KUTY mass estimates at high Z and N are sometimes far longer than the age of the universe, while calculations with the Muntian mass formula do not indicate any superheavy element above Z = 102 with a half-life comparable to the age of the earth.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha-decay half-lives using the WKB barrier penetration formalism with microscopic nuclear potentials obtained by double folding the density distributions of the alpha particle and the daughter nucleus with a density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Theoretical Q-values were extracted from three different mass estimates: Myers-Swiatecki (MS), Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski (M), and Koura-Tachibana-Uno-Yamada (KUTY). Half-lives were also estimated using the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski (VSS) semi-empirical relationship for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Half-lives were calculated for about 1700 isotopes with atomic numbers from 100 to 130
- The formalism using experimental Q-values agreed with experimental data spanning about twenty orders of magnitude
- For the nucleus $^{289}114$, the measured lifetime is $2.7^{+1.4}_{-0.7}$ s
- The assault frequency for alpha decay from $^{217}100$ is $5.481\times10^{20}$ s$^{-1}$ using theoretical Q-values from KUTY masses
- The assault frequency for alpha decay from $^{330}130$ is $7.108\times10^{20}$ s$^{-1}$ using theoretical Q-values from KUTY masses
- The zero point vibration energies used are: 0.1045Q for even-even, 0.0962Q for odd Z-even N, 0.0907Q for even Z-odd N, and 0.0767Q for odd-odd parent nuclei
- For $^{248}102$, the DDM3Y half-life using MS Q-values is 4.26E-02 s and using M Q-values is 1.87E-02 s
- For $^{264}102$, the DDM3Y half-life using MS Q-values is 7.63E+09 s and using M Q-values is 3.06E+09 s

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WKB-DDM3Y calculations (Roy Chowdhury 2006, Roy Chowdhury 2007, Samanta 2007) that showed excellent agreement with measured half-lives of 27 new nuclei with atomic numbers between 106 and 118. The paper provides updated half-life estimates into regions of nuclei far from stability and of superheavy elements using three different mass tables, which will be useful for experimental studies toward the predicted island of stability. The results contradict earlier predictions (Fiset 1972, Randrup 1974, Moller 1976) that some superheavy nuclei might have lifetimes comparable to the age of the earth, as the present calculations do not indicate such stability.
