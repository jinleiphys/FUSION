# Addition and removal energies of circular quantum dots

> We present and compare several many-body methods as applied to two-dimensional quantum dots with circular symmetry. We calculate the approximate ground state energy using a harmonic oscillator basis optimized by Hartree-Fock (HF) theory and further improve the ground state energy using two post-HF methods: in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) and coupled cluster with singles and doubles (CCSD). With the application of quasidegenerate perturbation theory (QDPT) or the equations-of-motion (EOM) method to the results of the previous two methods, we obtain addition and removal energies as well. Our results are benchmarked against full configuration interaction (FCI) and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) where available. We examine the rate of convergence and perform extrapolations to the infinite basis limit using a power-law model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that post-Hartree-Fock methods (IM-SRG and CCSD) combined with quasidegenerate perturbation theory or equations-of-motion methods can accurately compute addition and removal energies for circular quantum dots, with results benchmarked against full configuration interaction and diffusion Monte Carlo where available, but the accuracy depends on the specific system parameters and basis size.

## Method

The authors model circular quantum dots as N electrons in a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential with Coulomb interactions, using atomic units. They first obtain a Hartree-Fock optimized harmonic oscillator basis, then apply the in-medium similarity renormalization group truncated at the two-body level (IM-SRG(2)) and coupled cluster with singles and doubles (CCSD) to improve ground state energies. Addition and removal energies are computed by applying quasidegenerate perturbation theory or equations-of-motion methods to the IM-SRG and CCSD results, with convergence to the infinite basis limit studied using a power-law extrapolation model.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies are computed for closed-shell quantum dots with magic numbers N = K_F(K_F + 1), where K_F is the number of filled shells
- The IM-SRG(2) CPU cost scales roughly as O(N_b^6), where N_b is the number of single-particle basis states
- IM-SRG(2) ground state energy is third-order exact, with dominant error terms corresponding to fourth-order energy diagrams
- For nuclei, differences between IM-SRG(2) and perturbative IM-SRG(3) approximations were typically on the order of hundreds of keV, while binding energies are on the order of 8 MeV per nucleon
- For electronic systems, differences between IM-SRG(2) and perturbative IM-SRG(3) were typically less than a few millihartrees

## Context

This work extends the application of IM-SRG, previously successful in nuclear physics (Hergert, 2016) and quantum chemistry, to two-dimensional quantum dot systems which exhibit shell structures and magic numbers similar to atoms and nuclei (Tarucha, 1996). The results enable systematic benchmarking of many-body methods for confined electronic systems and provide a testbed for methods applicable to other systems with similar geometry, such as nuclei or neutron drops (PhysRevC.84.044306, 2011).
