# Bethe-Salpeter Study of Vector Meson Masses and Decay Constants

> The masses and decay constants of the light vector mesons $ρ/ω$, $ϕ$ and $K^\star$ are studied within a ladder-rainbow truncation of the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations of QCD with a model 2-point gluon function. The approach is consistent with quark and gluon confinement, reproduces the correct one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD, generates dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and preserves the relevant Ward identities. The one phenomenological parameter and two current quark masses are fixed by requiring that the calculated $f_π, m_π$ and $m_K$ are correct. The resulting $f_K$ is within 3% of the experimental value. For the vector mesons, all eight transverse covariants are included and the dominant ones are identified; the complete angle dependence of the amplitudes is also retained. The calculated values for the masses $m_ρ, m_ϕ$ and $m_{K^\star}$ are within 5%, while the decay constants $f_ρ, f_ϕ$ and $f_{K^\star}$ for electromagnetic and leptonic decays are within 10% of the experimental values.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using a model gluon 2-point function without the delta-function infrared term, can reproduce vector meson masses within 5% and decay constants within 10% of experimental values, provided the phenomenological parameter and current quark masses are first fixed to pion and kaon observables.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for vector mesons in Euclidean space using a ladder truncation consistent with a rainbow truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation, preserving the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The effective quark-quark interaction was modeled by a finite-width Gaussian infrared term plus a perturbative QCD tail, with the delta-function term from earlier pion studies removed to avoid analytic complications in the complex momentum plane. All eight transverse Dirac covariants for the vector Bethe-Salpeter amplitude were included, and the angle dependence was resolved either by direct two-variable iteration or by Chebyshev expansion of the amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The calculated vector meson masses \(m_\rho\), \(m_\phi\), and \(m_{K^\star}\) are within 5% of experimental values.
- The decay constants \(f_\rho\), \(f_\phi\), and \(f_{K^\star}\) are within 10% of experimental values.
- The resulting kaon decay constant \(f_K\) is within 3% of the experimental value.
- The experimental value \(f_\rho = 216\) MeV is derived from the decay width \(\Gamma_{\rho^0\rightarrow e^+e^-} = 6.77\) keV and the coupling \(g_\rho = 5.03\).
- The model parameters used include \(\omega = 0.3\) GeV, \(m_t = 0.5\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{N_f=4} = 0.234\) GeV, and renormalization point \(\mu = 19\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to pseudoscalar mesons by Maris and Tandy (1997) to the vector meson sector, where no symmetry-based simplification analogous to the Goldstone theorem exists. It improves upon earlier vector meson studies by Jain and Munczek (1993) that used a derivative expansion, and upon separable-ansatz treatments by including the full momentum dependence and all eight transverse covariants. The resulting vector Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes enable future calculations of meson decays and form factors without compromising analytic structure.
