# Two-nucleon excitation from $p$ to $sd$ shell in $^{12,14}$C

> The effect of the two-nucleon excitation from $p$ to $sd$ shell is discussed in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$C in the frame work of shell model. The recently suggested shell-model Hamiltonian YSOX provides an suitable tool to investigate the $2~\hbarω$ excitation in $psd$ region. Because the strength of the $<pp|V|sdsd>$ interaction, which represents the interaction between the $0~\hbarω$ and $2~\hbarω$ configurations, is considered in the construction of the YSOX. The level of $^{12}$C is almost independent on the $<pp|V|sdsd>$ interaction, but excitation energies of certain states in $^{14}$C are strongly affected by it. Further investigation shows that the percentage of $2~\hbarω$ configuration in these states is quite different from that of the ground state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strength of the `<pp|V|sdsd>` interaction, which couples the 0 and 2 ℏω configurations, strongly affects the excitation energies of certain states in ¹⁴C but leaves the levels of ¹²C almost unchanged; this difference arises because the percentage of 2 ℏω configuration in the affected ¹⁴C states is very different from that of the ground state.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the `psd` model space using the phenomenological Hamiltonian YSOX, which includes the `<pp|V|sdsd>` interaction term. They varied the strength of the central part of this interaction by a factor x relative to the original V_MU potential and computed the energy levels of ¹²C and ¹⁴C as a function of x. The percentage of 0 ℏω and 2 ℏω configurations in the ground and excited states was extracted from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁶O, the ground state has 25% 2 ℏω configuration; this decreases to 10% in ²⁴O.
- In ¹⁰B, the 1⁺₁ state has 16% 2 ℏω configuration, which is about 10% larger than that of the ground state.
- In ¹⁴C, the 0⁺₂, 2⁺₂, and 4⁺₁ states are dominated by the 2 ℏω configuration, with the 4⁺₁ state being 100% 2 ℏω configuration (all contributed by that configuration).
- In ¹⁴C, the ground state, 2⁺₁, and 1⁺₁ states have about 20% 2 ℏω configuration.
- In ¹²C, the 2 ℏω configuration is approximately 10% in each state and does not change much across states.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of two-nucleon excitation from the p to the sd shell previously studied in ¹⁰B and ¹⁷C by Yuan (2012), where the `<pp|V|sdsd>` interaction was shown to affect some excited states strongly. The present study applies the same YSOX Hamiltonian to ¹²C and ¹⁴C, demonstrating that the effect depends on whether the excited state has a 2 ℏω configuration similar to or different from the ground state. The results enable future searches for other nuclei whose levels are sensitive to this interaction, which could help constrain the nuclear force driving cross-shell excitations.
