# Nuclear pairing and Coriolis effects in proton emitters

> We introduce a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field approach to treat the problem of proton emission from a deformed nucleus. By substituting a rigid rotor in a particle-rotor-model with a mean-field we obtain a better description of experimental data in $^{141}$Ho. The approach also elucidates the softening of kinematic coupling between particle and collective rotation, the Coriolis attenuation problem.

## Key claim

By replacing the rigid rotor of the particle-rotor model with a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field, the authors obtain a better description of experimental proton-emission data in 141Ho, specifically by elucidating the softening of kinematic coupling between particle and collective rotation (the Coriolis attenuation problem).

## Method

The authors implement a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach for an axially-symmetric deformed proton emitter, where the total Hamiltonian combines collective rotation with an intrinsic Hamiltonian that includes single-particle energies and a pairing interaction. The HFB equations are solved self-consistently to determine the quasiparticle mean-field, which absorbs kinematic couplings and residual nucleon-nucleon correlations, with the off-diagonal Coriolis interaction suppressed by a self-consistently determined average mean-field value ⟨j⟩. Decay widths are computed using the resulting wave functions and compared with the standard particle-rotor model (PRM) and experimental data for 141Ho.

## Key numbers

- For 141Ho, the experimental partial decay width Γ0 to the 0+ ground state of 140Dy is 10.9 × 10−20 MeV; the PRM with Coriolis+pairing gives 1.7 × 10−20 MeV, while the RHFB calculation gives 7.0 × 10−20 MeV.
- The experimental branching ratio Γ2/Γ0 (decay to the 2+ first excited state relative to the ground state) is 0.71%; the PRM with Coriolis+pairing gives 1.7%, while the RHFB calculation gives 0.3%.
- In the adiabatic limit (infinite moment of inertia), both PRM and RHFB give Γ0 = 15.0 × 10−20 MeV and Γ2/Γ0 = 0.73%.
- The Coriolis suppression factor ξ is defined as ξ = 1 − ⟨j⟩/√(I(I+1)−⟨Ω²⟩) and is shown as a function of pairing gap for 141Ho.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of proton emission from deformed nuclei by Esbensen (2001) and Fiorin (2003), which highlighted the same lack of kinematic coupling between particle and deformed rotor that was inferred decades ago from energy spectra of odd-A nuclei (Ring and Schuck; Muller, 1984). The approach enables a more microscopic treatment of Coriolis attenuation than the phenomenological substitution of rotor spin suggested by Kreiner (1979) or the j² operator contributions discussed by Ring (1977), and can be extended to treat non-rigidity of the core as considered by Protopapas (1997).
