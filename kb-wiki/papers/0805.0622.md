# Indications of Conical Emission of Charged Hadrons at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Three-particle azimuthal correlation measurements with a high transverse momentum trigger particle are reported for pp, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV by the STAR experiment. Dijet structures are observed in pp, d+Au and peripheral Au+Au collisions. An additional structure is observed in central Au+Au data, signaling conical emission of correlated charged hadrons. The conical emission angle is found to be 1.37 +- 0.02(stat) +0.06-0.07(syst), independent of pt.

## Key claim

Three-particle azimuthal correlation measurements in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV reveal an additional structure beyond the dijet pattern seen in pp, d+Au, and peripheral Au+Au collisions, which the authors interpret as "signaling conical emission of correlated charged hadrons." The claim is hedged by the word "indications" in the title and by the fact that the conical emission interpretation is one of several possible mechanisms (including broadened jets, deflected jets, and Mach-cone shock waves) that could account for the data.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured three-particle azimuthal correlations between a high transverse momentum trigger particle and two lower transverse momentum associated particles in pp, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 200 GeV. The analysis integrated over pseudorapidity because the near- and away-side jets are not correlated in that direction. The correlation patterns were compared across collision systems and centralities to isolate the conical emission signal from the dijet background.

## Key numbers

- Conical emission angle: theta = 1.37 +/- 0.02 (statistical) +0.06/-0.07 (systematic) radians, reported as independent of transverse momentum.
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for pp, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions.
- The correlated yield on the away side (Delta-phi approximately pi) is strongly suppressed at transverse momentum greater than 2 GeV/c, while at lower transverse momentum the yield is enhanced and broadly distributed in azimuth.

## Context

This work extends prior observations of jet quenching at RHIC, where the away-side correlated yield of high transverse momentum particles is suppressed in central Au+Au collisions relative to pp collisions. The conical emission signal is consistent with predictions for Mach-cone shock waves generated by large energy deposition in the hydrodynamic medium (conical flow, Renk) or Cerenkov gluon radiation (Cerenkov). The result enables further study of the medium properties such as its speed of sound and equation of state, and discriminates between mechanisms including broadened jets due to large angle gluon radiation (Vitev), deflected jets due to collective radial flow (Armesto) or pathlength dependent energy loss (Hwa), and conical emission.
