# The Fate of Hadron Masses in Dense Matter: Hidden Local Symmetry and Color Flavor Locking

> The notion that hadron masses scale according to the scaling of the quark condensate in hadronic matter, partly supported by a number of observations in finite nuclei, can be interpreted in terms of Harada-Yamawaki's ``vector manifestation" (VM) of chiral symmetry. In this scenario, near chiral restoration, the vector meson masses drop to zero {\it in the chiral limit} with vanishing widths. This scenario appears to differ from the standard linear sigma model scenario. We exploit a link between the VM and color-flavor locking inferred by us from lattice data on quark number susceptibility (QNS) measured as a function of temperature to suggest that local flavor symmetry gets mapped to color gauge symmetry at the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that as density increases toward the critical density for chiral restoration, vector meson masses drop to zero in the chiral limit with vanishing widths, a scenario called "vector manifestation" (VM) that differs from the standard linear sigma model scenario. The authors propose that local flavor symmetry gets mapped to color gauge symmetry at the chiral phase transition, linking Harada-Yamawaki's VM with color-flavor locking (CFL).

## Method

The authors combine Harada-Yamawaki's hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory, which describes pions, rho mesons, and omega mesons under a [U(2)_L × U(2)_R]_global × [U(2)_V]_local symmetry, with Berges and Wetterich's color-flavor locking framework where diquark and quark-antiquark condensates break color symmetry. They interpret lattice data on quark number susceptibility (QNS) as a function of temperature to infer a connection between the hidden gauge coupling g_V and the color gauge coupling g_c, and extend this reasoning to dense matter. The mass formula m_V = sqrt(a) g_V f_pi is used to relate vector meson masses to the pion decay constant and gauge coupling in medium.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor Phi(n) ≡ m_rho^star(n)/m_rho(0) at nuclear matter density n_0 is approximately 0.78, extracted from nuclear matter saturation, Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner formula, or QCD sum rules.
- The Landau parameter F_1_tilde(pi) at nuclear matter density n_0 takes the value -0.153.
- The predicted anomalous gyromagnetic ratio delta g_l = 0.23 tau_3 was confirmed by measurement for protons in the Pb region, giving delta g_l^p = 0.23 ± 0.03.
- The vector meson mass formula in free space is m_V = sqrt(a(m_V)) g_V(m_V) f_pi(m_V), with the KSRF-like relation m_rho = sqrt(2) g_{rho pi pi} f_pi(0) noted as similar but not identical.

## Context

This work extends the BR scaling proposal by Brown and Rho (1991), which suggested hadron masses scale with the quark condensate in dense matter, and interprets it through Harada and Yamawaki's vector manifestation framework. The paper connects to lattice QCD results on quark number susceptibility and to the color-flavor locking proposal of Berges and Wetterich, suggesting that the hidden flavor gauge symmetry cedes to fundamental QCD color gauge symmetry at the chiral phase transition. The results enable a unified description of hadron mass modification from nuclear matter density up to the chiral restoration density, with implications for heavy-ion collision observables such as lepton pair spectra.
