# QCD sum rules for D mesons in dense and hot nuclear matter

> Open charm mesons (pseudo-scalar and scalar as well as axial-vector and vector) propagating or resting in nuclear matter display an enhanced sensitivity to the chiral condensate. This offers new prospects to seek for signals of chiral restoration, in particular in p-A and p-bar-A reactions as envisaged in first-round experiments by the CBM and PANDA collaborations at FAIR. Weinberg type sum rules for charming chiral partners are presented, and the distinct in-medium modifications of open-charm mesons are discussed. We also address the gluon condensates near Tc and their impact on QCD sum rules.

# QCD sum rules for D mesons in dense and hot nuclear matter

## Key claim
Open charm mesons in nuclear matter exhibit enhanced sensitivity to the chiral condensate, with the spectral strengths of D and anti-D mesons possibly displaced by about 60 MeV at saturation density, but the authors note that a safe average mass shift cannot be extracted from their approach due to uncertainties of input parameters.

## Method
The authors employ QCD sum rules, relating current-current correlation functions to integrated spectral densities via the operator product expansion. They evaluate moments of the spectral function using a pole-plus-continuum ansatz to extract mass splitting and mass shift parameters for D and anti-D mesons in cold nuclear matter. For finite width effects, a Breit-Wigner ansatz is used to explore mass-width correlations. Weinberg-type sum rules for chiral partners (vector-axial-vector and scalar-pseudo-scalar) are derived analytically for heavy-light meson systems.

## Key numbers
- The D-anti-D mass splitting Δm at saturation density is approximately -30 MeV (negative, meaning D+ lighter than D-), with a confidence range shown in Figure 2.
- The center of gravity m for D and anti-D mesons is taken as 1.915 GeV for the mass-width correlation analysis.
- At quark chemical potential μ_q = 270 MeV, the gluon condensate ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩_T drops significantly compared to μ_q = 0, while the symmetric traceless gluon condensate is much less influenced by density effects.
- The spectral strengths of D and anti-D may be displaced by about 60 MeV at saturation density.

## Context
This work extends QCD sum rule studies of in-medium hadron modifications from the vector meson and strange meson sectors (previously studied extensively) to the charm sector, motivated by upcoming experiments at FAIR by the CBM and PANDA collaborations. The Weinberg-type sum rules for chiral partners build on the foundational work of Weinberg and the finite-temperature extension by Shuryak and Kapusta. The gluon condensate analysis near T_c follows the approach of SuHong Lee and uses lattice QCD equation-of-state data from Bazavov, extrapolated to finite baryon density via the Rossendorf quasiparticle model. The results enable predictions for pA and anti-pA reactions and open the avenue for evaluating QCD sum rules near or above the deconfinement transition at non-zero baryon densities.
