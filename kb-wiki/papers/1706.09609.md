# Experimental Determination of One- and Two-Neutron Separation Energies for Neutron-Rich Copper Isotopes

> A method is proposed to determine the one-neutron $S_n$ or two-neutron $S_{2n}$ separation energy of neutron-rich isotopes. Relationships between $S_n$ ($S_{2n}$) and isotopic cross sections have been deduced from an empirical formula, i.e., the cross section of an isotope exponentially depends on the average binding energy per nucleon $B/A$. The proposed relationships have been verified using the neutron-rich copper isotopes measured in the 64$A$ MeV $^{86}$Kr + $^{9}$Be reaction. $S_n$, $S_{2n}$, and $B/A$ for the very neutron-rich $^{77, 78, 79}$Cu isotopes are determined from the proposed correlations. It is also proposed that the correlations between $S_n$, $S_{2n}$ and isotopic cross sections can be used to find the location of neutron drip line isotopes.

## Key claim

A linear correlation between isotopic cross sections and one- or two-neutron separation energies can be used to determine \(S_n\) and \(S_{2n}\) for very neutron-rich copper isotopes, provided the parameters \(C\) and \(\tau\) are calibrated from known data. The method yields \(S_n\) values within \(\pm 0.5\) MeV of the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME16) for isotopes where experimental data exist, but for the unmeasured \(^{77,78,79}\)Cu the predicted values are systematically larger than the AME16 evaluated results.

## Method

The authors derived relationships between the one-neutron separation energy \(S_n\) (or two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\)) and the isotopic cross section \(\sigma\) from an empirical exponential dependence of \(\sigma\) on the average binding energy per nucleon \(B/A\). Using cross sections for neutron-rich copper isotopes measured in the 64\(A\) MeV \(^{86}\)Kr + \(^{9}\)Be reaction at RIKEN (Tsang et al., 2007), they performed linear fits of \(\sigma_{(Z,A)}^{(-n)} \equiv A\ln\sigma_{(Z,A)} - (A-1)\ln\sigma_{(Z,A-1)}\) versus \(S_n'\) and of \(\sigma_{(Z,A)}^{(-2n)}\) versus \(S_{2n}'\) to determine the parameters \(C\) and \(\tau\). They then applied the fitted correlations to predict \(S_n\), \(S_{2n}\), and \(B/A\) for \(^{77,78,79}\)Cu, for which no experimental binding energies exist.

## Key numbers

* The fitted parameters for the \(S_n\) correlation are \(C = 3.81 \times 10^{-18}\) mb and \(\tau = 0.0225\) MeV, using cross sections from the 64\(A\) MeV \(^{86}\)Kr + \(^{9}\)Be reaction.
* For \(^{65-76}\)Cu, the largest difference between \(S_n\) determined from the correlation and the AME16 data is no more than \(\pm 0.5\) MeV, with most differences within \(\pm 0.3\) MeV.
* For \(^{77}\)Cu, the predicted \(S_n\) is 5.961 MeV (AME16 evaluated: 5.72 MeV); for \(^{78}\)Cu, 4.523 MeV (AME16: 3.95 MeV); for \(^{79}\)Cu, 6.497 MeV (AME16: 5.31 MeV).
* For \(^{79}\)Cu, the predicted \(S_{2n}\) is 10.80 MeV, which is 1.54 MeV larger than the AME16 evaluated value of 9.26 MeV.
* The predicted \(B/A\) for \(^{77}\)Cu is 8.411 MeV (from \(S_n\)) and 8.406 MeV (from \(S_{2n}\)), close to the AME16 value of 8.408 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the empirical correlation between isotopic yields and binding energy per nucleon first proposed by Tsang et al. (2007) for neutron-rich copper isotopes, and applies it to determine separation energies rather than total binding energies. The method provides an alternative to direct mass measurements for very neutron-rich isotopes near the drip line, where production cross sections are low. The predicted \(S_n\) and \(S_{2n}\) for \(^{77,78,79}\)Cu can guide experimental searches for more neutron-rich copper isotopes and help refine theoretical calculations of neutron-skin thickness, as discussed in relation to work by Typel and Brown (2001) and subsequent studies.
