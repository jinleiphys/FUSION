# Temperature and fluid velocity on the freeze-out surface from $π$, $K$, $p$ spectra in pp, p--Pb and Pb--Pb collisions

> We present a new approach to take into account resonance decays in the blast-wave model fits of identified hadron spectra. Thanks to pre-calculated decayed particle spectra, we are able to extract, in a matter of seconds, the multiplicity dependence of the single freeze-out temperature $T_{\rm fo}$, average fluid velocity $\left<β_{\rm T}\right>$, velocity exponent $n$, and the volume $dV/dy$ of an expanding fireball. In contrast to blast-wave fits without resonance feed-down, our approach results in a freeze-out temperature of $T_{\rm fo}\approx 150\,\text{MeV}$, which has only weak dependence on multiplicity and collision system. Finally, we discuss separate chemical and kinetic freeze-outs separated by partial chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper presents a blast-wave model fit that includes resonance decay feed-down, yielding a freeze-out temperature of approximately 150 MeV that shows only weak dependence on multiplicity and collision system, in contrast to traditional blast-wave fits without resonance decays which show strong centrality dependence.

## Method

The authors pre-calculate decayed particle spectra using irreducible scalar distribution functions from the FastReso code, then perform simultaneous fits of pion, kaon, and proton transverse momentum spectra from ALICE data across pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions. The blast-wave model parameters (freeze-out temperature T_fo, mean transverse velocity <beta_T>, velocity exponent n, and volume dV/dy) are extracted using a single normalization factor for all particle species, with fits performed in the momentum range 0.5 < p_T < 3.0 GeV/c. For separate chemical and kinetic freeze-outs, the partial chemical equilibrium model is employed with fixed chemical freeze-out temperature and varied kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_fo ≈ 150 MeV from the single freeze-out fits including resonance decays
- For Pb-Pb collisions, freeze-out temperature in the 148-152 MeV range
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV from statistical model fits (used as reference)
- Fit range: 0.5 < p_T < 3.0 GeV/c for pion, kaon, and proton spectra
- χ²/dof range 0.5-2.9 for Pb-Pb spectra fits
- χ²/dof grows from 1.5 in highest multiplicity p-Pb to 8.2 in lowest multiplicity pp bin
- For partial chemical equilibrium in 0-10% central Pb-Pb: T_kin = 126 ± 2 MeV with T_chem = 150 MeV
- 3291 2-body and 513 3-body decays for 739 resonances with masses up to 3.0 GeV/c²

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model originally developed by Schnedermann (1993) by incorporating resonance decay feed-down using an efficient computational method from Mazeliauskas (2018). The results contradict traditional blast-wave fits without resonance decays (as performed by ALICE in Abelev 2013) which show strong freeze-out temperature dependence on centrality. The approach enables rapid extraction of freeze-out parameters and prediction of multi-strange hadron spectra (Λ, Ξ, Ω) without additional parameters, and opens the way for simple but realistic studies of freeze-out surfaces, viscous corrections, and freeze-out criteria in small collision systems.
