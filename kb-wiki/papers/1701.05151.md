# Electromagnetic scaling functions within the Green's Function Monte Carlo approach

> We have studied the scaling properties of the electromagnetic response functions of $^4$He and $^{12}$C nuclei computed by the Green's Function Monte Carlo approach, retaining only the one-body current contribution. Longitudinal and transverse scaling functions have been obtained in the relativistic and non relativistic cases and compared to experiment for various kinematics. The characteristic asymmetric shape of the scaling function exhibited by data emerges in the calculations in spite of the non relativistic nature of the model. The results are consistent with scaling of zeroth, first and second kinds. Our analysis reveals a direct correspondence between the scaling and the nucleon-density response functions.

## Key claim

The Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of electromagnetic response functions for \(^4\)He and \(^{12}\)C, retaining only one-body currents, produce the characteristic asymmetric shape of the scaling function seen in experimental data, despite the non-relativistic nature of the model. The results are consistent with scaling of zeroth, first, and second kinds, and the analysis reveals a direct correspondence between the scaling and the nucleon-density response functions.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions of \(^4\)He and \(^{12}\)C using the Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach, retaining only the one-body current contribution. They obtained the scaling functions by dividing the GFMC response functions by appropriate single-nucleon prefactors, using both relativistic and non-relativistic definitions of the scaling variable and prefactors, and compared the results to experimental data for various momentum transfers. The Laplace transforms (Euclidean responses) of the response functions were inverted using maximum-entropy techniques based on Bayesian inference.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C, the longitudinal and transverse scaling functions were computed at momentum transfers \(|{\bf q}| = 300\), 380, and 570 MeV.
- For \(^4\)He, the scaling functions were computed at \(|{\bf q}| = 300\), 400, 500, 600, and 700 MeV.
- The Fermi momentum for \(^4\)He was set to 180 MeV for comparison with experimental data.
- The non-relativistic scaling variable \(\psi^{nr}\) is defined as \(\psi^{nr} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\xi^{nr}_F}}\left(\frac{\lambda}{\kappa} - \kappa\right)\), where \(\lambda = \omega/2m\) and \(\kappa = |{\bf q}|/2m\).
- The GFMC approach is applicable in the non-relativistic regime typically corresponding to \(|{\bf q}| \lesssim 500\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of scaling in nuclear response functions, which began with the work of West (1974), Day (1987), and Donnelly (1998). The authors compare their GFMC results to the Global Relativistic Fermi Gas (GRFG) model, which predicts a symmetric scaling function, and to experimental data from Barreau (1983) for \(^{12}\)C and Carlson (2001) for \(^4\)He. The study builds on earlier work by Caballero (2005, 2006, 2007) and Meucci (2009) who showed that final state interactions are crucial for obtaining the asymmetric tail of the scaling function, and by Lovato (2013, 2014, 2015, 2016) who developed the GFMC calculations of nuclear response functions. The results enable future studies of scaling in heavier nuclei and provide a benchmark for testing relativistic and two-body current effects.
