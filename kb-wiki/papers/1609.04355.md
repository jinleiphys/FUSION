# Photoproduction of vector mesons: from ultraperipheral to semi-central heavy ion collisions

> We discuss nuclear cross sections for $AA \to AAV$ and $AA \to AAVV$ reactions with one or two vector mesons in the final state. Our analysis is done in the impact parameter space equivalent photon approximation. This approach allows to consider the above processes taking into account distance between colliding nuclei. We consider both ultraperipheral and semi-central collisions. We are a first group which undertook a study of single $J/ψ$ photoproduction for different centrality bins. We show that one can describe new ALICE experimental data by including geometrical effects of collisions in the flux factor. Next, total and differential cross section for double-scattering mechanism in the exclusive $AA \to AAVV$ reaction in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is presented. In this context we consider double photoproduction and photon-photon processes. Simultaneously, we get very good agreement of our results with STAR (RHIC), CMS and ALICE (LHC) experimental data for single $ρ^0$ and $J/ψ$ vector meson production. A comparison of our predictions for exclusive four charged pions production is also presented.

## Key claim

The authors show that the ALICE experimental data for single \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in semi-central heavy ion collisions can be described by including geometrical effects of collisions in the photon flux factor, specifically by integrating the photon flux only over the non-overlapping part of the target nucleus.

## Method

The analysis uses the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space, incorporating realistic nuclear form factors (Fourier transforms of the charge distribution) to compute cross sections for \(AA \to AAV\) and \(AA \to AAVV\) reactions. For semi-central collisions, the photon flux is modified by imposing geometrical conditions that account for nuclear overlap and absorption. The double-scattering mechanism for two-vector-meson production is calculated from the probability densities of single meson production, and contributions from \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion are also included.

## Key numbers

- For coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in lead-lead UPC at LHC energy, the result with the monopole form factor overestimates the ALICE and CMS data, while the realistic form factor gives better agreement.
- For \(\rho^0\) photoproduction in UPC, the ALICE data point is much better described by the classical rescattering calculations (lower line) than by quantal rescattering.
- At both RHIC and LHC energies, the contribution of \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion to \(AA \to AA \rho^0 \rho^0\) is one order of magnitude smaller than that of the double-scattering mechanism.
- The double-scattering contribution accounts only for \(20\%\) of the cross section measured by the STAR Collaboration for four-pion production.
- For semi-central collisions, the standard photon flux exceeds the ALICE data for \(J/\psi\) production, while the \(N^{(2)}\) photon flux (obtained with realistic form factor) achieves rather good agreement.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) by groups such as Baltz (2007) and Klein et al. (2005) to semi-central collisions, where nuclear overlap occurs. The authors are the first to study single \(J/\psi\) photoproduction as a function of centrality bins. The results enable future studies of the interplay between coherent photoproduction and quark-gluon plasma formation, and suggest that the missing contribution in four-pion production likely comes from decays of excited \(\rho^0\) resonances (e.g., \(\rho^0(1450)\) or \(\rho^0(1700)\)) which require further modeling.
