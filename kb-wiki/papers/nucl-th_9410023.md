# Nuclear Charge Density Distributions from Elastic Electron Scattering Data

> The model independent procedure of extracting charge density distributions from elastic electron scattering data is investigated. The charge density distributions are expanded on an orthonormal basis and the parameters of the expansions are fixed by the comparison with the experimental data. Two bases with different analytical properties (Fourier-Bessel and Hermite) are used. This allows us to disentangle the uncertainties coming from the choice of the expansion basis from those intrinsic to the extraction procedure. We design a set of tests to select the number of the expansion coefficients adequate for a proper description of the data. The procedure is applied to elastic data measured on $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb nuclei.

## Key claim

The theoretical uncertainties from truncating the expansion basis in model-independent charge density extraction can be as large as, or larger than, the experimental errors from modern high-accuracy electron scattering data, and these uncertainties depend on the choice of expansion basis (Fourier-Bessel versus Hermite).

## Method

The authors expanded nuclear charge density distributions on two orthonormal bases with different analytical properties: Fourier-Bessel (FB) and Hermite functions. They fitted the expansion coefficients to elastic electron scattering cross sections by solving the Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit, using both pseudodata generated from known Woods-Saxon densities and experimental data on 12C, 40Ca, and 208Pb. They designed a stability test based on the quantity G(N), defined as the ratio of the average confidence level to a stability measure S(N), to select the optimal number of expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For 12C with data up to q_max ~ 3.0 fm^-1, the FB cutoff radius R_c was chosen as 8 fm; for 208Pb, R_c = 11 fm.
- The empirical relation for the maximum number of FB coefficients is n_max = q_max * R_c / pi.
- For the Hermite basis, the approximate relation for the number of coefficients is n ~ 0.5 * (q_max^2 * beta^2), with beta ~ 1.5-1.7 fm for the nuclei studied.
- For 40Ca pseudodata, fits with 9, 10, and 11 FB coefficients showed stable density bands with average chi^2 ~ 1 per degree of freedom; adding a 12th coefficient produced instability with 4 out of 12 coefficients compatible with zero.
- For the Hermite basis on 40Ca pseudodata, fits with 10 and 11 terms had one term compatible with zero but stable density bands; with 14 terms, 10 out of 14 coefficients were undetermined.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent extraction methods previously applied with Fourier-Bessel expansions (Sick 1974, Dreher et al. 1974) by introducing Hermite functions as an alternative basis to disentangle basis-choice uncertainties from intrinsic extraction uncertainties. The authors apply their procedure to elastic scattering data on 12C, 40Ca, and 208Pb from experiments in the 1970s-1980s. The study enables a more rigorous assessment of theoretical uncertainties in charge density extraction, providing a criterion (the G(N) function) to select the optimal number of expansion coefficients that balances fit quality and stability against statistical fluctuations.
