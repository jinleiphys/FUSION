# Anisotropic flows from colour strings: Monte-Carlo simulations

> By direct Monte-Carlo simulations it is shown that the anisotropic flows can be successfully described in the colour string picture with fusion and percolation provided anisotropy of particle emission from the fused string is taken into account. Quenching of produced particles in the strong colour field of the string is the basic mechanism for this anisotropy. The concrete realization of this mechanism is borrowed from the QED. Due to dependence of this mechanism on the external field strength the found flows grow with energy, with values for $v_2$ at LHC energies greater by ~15% than at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The colour string model with fusion and percolation can describe both elliptic ($v_2$) and triangular ($v_3$) flows in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, provided that anisotropy of particle emission from fused strings is included via a quenching mechanism borrowed from QED. The model has a single adjustable parameter (the quenching coefficient $\kappa$), and the results for $v_3$ come out somewhat smaller than average observed values, but the large event-by-event fluctuations in $v_3$ mean the observed values lie within the calculated range.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV, 200 GeV, and 2.76 TeV, modeling colour strings as discs of radius 0.3 fm distributed in the transverse plane according to the participant nucleon distribution. Strings fuse and percolate into clusters; particles emitted from these clusters lose energy via radiative quenching as they traverse the strong colour field, with the energy loss formula taken from QED for a charged particle in a constant electric field. The quenching coefficient $\kappa$ was adjusted to reproduce the experimental $v_2$ for mid-central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV integrated over transverse momentum, and then all other results (centrality dependence, energy dependence, $p_T$ dependence, and higher harmonics) were predicted without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- The quenching coefficient was adjusted to $\kappa = 0.058$ to fit the experimental $v_2$ for mid-central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV and centrality 40-50%, the integrated $v_2$ with Poisson-distributed multiplicities was $0.588 \times 10^{-1} \pm 0.186 \times 10^{-1}$ and $v_3$ was $0.668 \times 10^{-2} \pm 0.399 \times 10^{-2}$.
- With average (non-fluctuating) multiplicities at the same energy and centrality, $v_2$ was $0.594 \times 10^{-1} \pm 0.145 \times 10^{-1}$ and $v_3$ was $0.574 \times 10^{-2} \pm 0.403 \times 10^{-2}$.
- With quenching turned off ($\kappa = 0$) at the same conditions, $v_2$ dropped to $0.180 \times 10^{-2} \pm 0.839 \times 10^{-3}$ and $v_3$ to $0.112 \times 10^{-2} \pm 0.622 \times 10^{-3}$, i.e., practically zero.
- The percolation parameter $\rho$ for Au+Au at 2.76 TeV was taken to be roughly 4 times larger than at RHIC energies.
- The number of strings per participant nucleon pair was approximately 7 at 200 GeV, 4 at 62.4 GeV, and 28 at 2.76 TeV.
- The string tension parameter was $T_0 = 0.08$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- The string colour decay coefficient was $\alpha = 0.03$ 1/fm.

## Context

This work extends the colour string approach with fusion and percolation, previously applied to particle production and correlations (Armesto, Braun, Pajares, and others), to the description of anisotropic flows. It follows crude estimates by Braun, Pajares, and Vechernin (2011) that suggested a sizable elliptic flow from the quenching mechanism, and it provides the first detailed Monte-Carlo study of both even and odd harmonics in this framework. The results contrast with the Colour Glass Condensate approach, which was found to underestimate $v_3$ unless treated non-perturbatively with non-vanishing viscosity (as discussed by the authors referencing work by Venugopalan and collaborators). The model enables future studies of rapidity dependence by introducing strings of finite rapidity length.
