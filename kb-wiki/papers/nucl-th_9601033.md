# Semiclassical treatment of the Dirac sea contribution for finite nuclei

> Dirac sea corrections for bulk properties of finite nuclei are computed within a self-consistent scheme in the $σ$-$ω$ model. The valence part is treated in the Hartree approximation whereas the sea contribution is evaluated semiclassically up to fourth order in $\hbar$. Numerically, we find a quick convergence of the semiclassical expansion; the fourth order contributing much less than one percent to the binding energy per nucleon.

## Key claim

The Dirac sea contribution to finite nuclei can be reliably computed using a semiclassical expansion up to fourth order in ℏ, with the fourth order contributing much less than one percent to the binding energy per nucleon, but the fast numerical convergence does not guarantee that the expansion converges to the exact Hartree result.

## Method

The valence part of the nuclear wavefunction was treated in the Hartree approximation using the σ-ω model, while the Dirac sea contribution was evaluated using a derivative (semiclassical) expansion of the fermionic determinant up to fourth order in ℏ. The fourth order sea corrections were treated perturbatively, using the self-consistent second-order meson fields as input, rather than solving the resulting fourth-order differential equations. The parameters of the model (g_s, g_v, m_s, m_v) were adjusted to reproduce the ω-meson mass, nuclear matter saturation properties, and the mean squared charge radius of ⁴⁰Ca.

## Key numbers

- The fourth order contribution to the binding energy per nucleon is much less than one percent of the total binding energy per nucleon.
- A simple dimensional estimate for the fourth order sea correction to the binding energy per nucleon is of order 1/(RA), which for calcium corresponds to about 1 MeV, but the actual calculated value is much smaller.
- The sixth order correction is estimated to be suppressed by a further factor of 1/(UR)², where U is the depth of the scalar or vector potentials, yielding about one percent of the fourth order correction for calcium.

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical treatments of the Dirac sea up to second order in ℏ by Perry (1986) and Fiolhais and Perry (1990), which found that sea effects cannot be considered negligible. The present calculation demonstrates that the semiclassical expansion converges rapidly, with fourth order terms being negligible, and provides a foundation for an exact mean field description of Dirac sea polarization. The approach avoids the high-momentum instability problems that arise in other treatments of the sea, such as those using the Kallen-Lehmann representation by Tanaka (1991, 1992), and circumvents the numerical difficulties of diagonalizing continuum states.
