# Noether Theorem of Relativistic-Electromagnetic Ideal Hydrodynamics

> We present a variational approach for relativistic ideal hydrodynamics interacting with electromagnetic fields. The momentum of fluid is introduced as the canonical conjugate variable of the position of a fluid element, which coincides with the conserved quantity derived from the Noether theorem. We further show that our formulation can reproduce the usual electromagnetic hydrodynamics which is obtained so as to satisfy the conservation of the inertia of fluid motion.

# Noether Theorem of Relativistic-Electromagnetic Ideal Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors derive relativistic-electromagnetic ideal hydrodynamics from a variational principle in Lagrangian coordinates, showing that the canonical momentum defined as the conjugate variable of a fluid element's position coincides with the Noether charge from spatial translation invariance. The formulation reproduces the usual phenomenological electromagnetic hydrodynamics, but only under the strong assumption that each fluid element carries identical, constant entropy and charge, and that these are homogeneous with respect to the Lagrangian coordinates.

## Method

The authors express a gauge-invariant Lagrangian for a relativistic ideal fluid interacting with electromagnetic fields in Lagrangian coordinates, where each fluid element is treated as a particle with fixed entropy and charge. They define the canonical momentum as the conjugate variable of the fluid element velocity, derive the Euler-Lagrange equations for both the fluid trajectory and gauge fields, and apply the Noether theorem for spatial and temporal translations to obtain the conservation laws for momentum and energy.

## Key numbers

- The canonical momentum in Euler coordinates is given by \( p^{i} ({\bf x},t) = \frac{\gamma}{c} \left\{ (\varepsilon^* + P^*) u^{i} + n^*_e A^{i} \right\} \), where \(\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1 - ({\bf v}/c)^2} = u^0\).
- The total conserved momentum density is \( (T_{fluid})^{0i} + (T_{em})^{0i} = \gamma (\varepsilon^* + P^*)u^{i} + ({\bf E} \times {\bf B})^{i} \).
- The total conserved energy density is \( T^{00}_{fluid} + T^{00}_{em} = (\varepsilon^*+P^*)\gamma^2-P^* + \frac{1}{2}({\bf E}^2+{\bf B}^2) \).
- The fluid element volume density evolves as \( \frac{d}{dt} \rho({\bf r}({\bf R},t),t) = - \rho({\bf r}({\bf R},t),t) (\nabla \cdot {\bf v}({\bf r}({\bf R},t),t) ) \).

## Context

This work extends prior variational formulations of relativistic ideal hydrodynamics in Euler coordinates (discussed in Refs. cited as "else") by reformulating the problem in Lagrangian coordinates, which is more suitable for discussing canonical momentum and the Noether theorem. The authors note that a previous Noether theorem analysis of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (Ref. cited as "ach") produced a hydrodynamic equation that does not coincide with the usual theory and employed special magnetohydrodynamic conditions, whereas the present result is consistent with standard relativistic ideal hydrodynamics and treats electric and magnetic fields on equal footing. The formulation enables future investigation of how symmetries such as chiral symmetry can be expressed in terms of hydrodynamic variables, and suggests that the stochastic variational method could be used to extend the approach to dissipative hydrodynamics.
