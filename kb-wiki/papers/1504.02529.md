# Collective flow without hydrodynamics: simulation results for relativistic ion collisions

> Flow signatures in experimental data from relativistic ion collisions are usually interpreted as a fingerprint of the presence of a hydrodynamic phase during the evolution of these systems. In this work, flow signatures arising from event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics are compared to those arising from event-by-event non-interacting particle dynamics (free-streaming), both followed by a late-stage hadronic cascade, in d+Au, 3He+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5 TeV, respectively. For comparison, also Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV are simulated. It is found that non-hydrodynamic evolution can give rise to equal or larger radial flow than hydrodynamics with eta/s=0.08 in all simulated collision systems. In light-on-heavy-ion collisions, free-streaming gives rise to triangular and quadrupolar flow comparable to or larger than that from hydrodynamics, but it generally leads to considerably smaller elliptic flow. As expected, free-streaming leads to considerably less elliptic, triangular and quadrupolar flow than hydrodynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions, such as event-by-event Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV.

## Key claim

Non-hydrodynamic (free-streaming) evolution followed by a hadronic cascade can generate radial flow equal to or larger than viscous hydrodynamics with η/s=0.08 in all simulated collision systems, and can produce triangular (v₃) and quadrupolar (v₄) flow comparable to or larger than hydrodynamics in light-on-heavy-ion collisions, though elliptic flow (v₂) remains considerably smaller.

## Method

The author compared two extreme descriptions of the hot QCD matter phase, event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (η/s=0.08) and non-interacting free-streaming particle dynamics, both followed by the same hadronic cascade (B3D) for the low-temperature phase. Initial conditions were generated using a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with Woods-Saxon distributions for heavy ions, a Hulthen wavefunction for the deuteron, and realistic calculations for the ³He wavefunction, with the same one-component massive gas equation of state (m=0.779 GeV, Z=116) matched to the hadron gas at T_SW=0.17 GeV used in both scenarios. Simulations were performed for d+Au and ³He+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, p+Pb collisions at √s=5 TeV, and Pb+Pb collisions at √s=2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature T_SW = 0.17 GeV used to match the one-component gas equation of state to the hadron gas
- One-component gas parameters: m = 0.779 GeV, Z = 116, chosen to match the hadron gas pressure and its first derivative at T_SW
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 used for the hydrodynamic simulations
- Hadronic cascade B3D includes resonances up to masses of 2.2 GeV with constant s-wave scattering cross-section of 10 mb
- Lattice spacing Δx ≈ 0.1 fm and time increment of 0.25 fm/c used for both hydrodynamic and free-streaming evolution grids
- 100 initial conditions generated for each collision system; at least 100,000 B3D events run per hydrodynamic evolution event
- In p+Pb collisions at √s=5 TeV, free-streaming plus cascade produced v₄ amplitude larger than hydrodynamics with η/s=0.08
- In smooth Cu+Cu collisions at b=4 fm, free-streaming gave elliptic flow v₂ approximately 50% of hydrodynamics with η/s=0.08

## Context

This work directly addresses the debate over whether flow signals observed in light-on-heavy-ion collisions (d+Au, ³He+Au, p+Pb) at RHIC and the LHC require a hydrodynamic phase or could arise from non-hydrodynamic mechanisms. It extends comparisons made by He (2015) and Bozek (2015) using the AMPT model, and by Zhou (2015) using the URQMD hadronic cascade, by providing a fully transparent comparison where only the hot-phase dynamics differs while keeping the equation of state and hadronic cascade identical. The results enable future work to better quantify the transport properties of the hadron gas phase, which the author identifies as essential for regenerating anisotropic flow from free-streaming initial conditions.
