# Nonstrange hybrid compact stars with color superconducting matter

> Realistic nonstrange hybrid compact stars with color superconducting quark matter in their interior are constructed. It is shown that a positively charged two-flavor color superconducting phase could naturally appear in the core of a hybrid star as one of the components of a globally neutral mixed phase. The negatively charged normal quark phase is the other component of the mixed phase. The quark core of the star is surrounded by another mixed phase made of hadronic and normal quark matter. The two mixed phases are separated by a sharp interface. Finally, the lowest density regions of the star are made of pure hadronic matter and nuclear crust.

## Key claim

The authors construct a model of nonstrange hybrid compact stars where a positively charged two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase can appear in the core as one component of a globally neutral mixed phase, but only under the assumption that the surface tension between quark phases is not too large (below about 20 MeV/fm²); otherwise the homogeneous gapless 2SC phase would be more favorable.

## Method

The authors use an SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a four-fermion interaction in the chiral limit to derive the equation of state for two-flavor quark matter, including normal quark matter, ordinary 2SC matter, and gapless 2SC matter. They apply the Gibbs construction to build mixed phases of normal and 2SC quark matter under global charge neutrality, and separately construct a hadron-quark mixed phase using a QCD-motivated chiral SU(3) model for hadronic matter at low densities. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain the mass-radius relation for the resulting hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- The hadron-quark mixed phase begins at baryon density ρ_B ≈ 1.49 ρ_0, where ρ_0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.
- The hadron-quark mixed phase extends up to ρ_B ≈ 2.56 ρ_0, at which point the volume fraction of hadronic matter is about 0.59 and normal quark matter about 0.41.
- At the transition from the hadron-quark mixed phase to the quark mixed phase, the baryon density jumps from about 2.56 ρ_0 to 2.75 ρ_0, and the energy density jumps from 378 MeV/fm³ to 415 MeV/fm³.
- Right after this transition, the volume fractions of 2SC and normal quark phases are 0.53 and 0.47, respectively.
- The maximum surface tension allowing the quark mixed phase is σ_max ≲ 20 MeV/fm²; for values between 20 and 50 MeV/fm² the mixed phase first appears at higher densities (3ρ_0 ≲ ρ_B ≲ 5ρ_0).
- The pressure difference between gapless 2SC matter and normal quark matter lies between 1 and 2.5 MeV/fm³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superconducting matter in compact stars by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and by Buballa (2002) on mixed phases, and by Huang and Shovkovy (2003) on the gapless 2SC phase. It contradicts the claim by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) that charge neutrality would prevent the 2SC phase in stars, by showing that a globally neutral mixed phase of normal and 2SC quark matter can exist. The construction enables future studies of hybrid stars with color superconducting cores, though the authors note that inclusion of strange quarks or different surface tension values could alter the results.
