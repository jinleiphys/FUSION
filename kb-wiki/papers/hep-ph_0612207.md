# Instanton-Induced Polarization in Exclusive Hyperon Photoproduction

## Key claim
Instantons can explain the strong polarization transfer from the initial photon to the final hyperon observed in exclusive hyperon photoproduction at JLab, but this mechanism is only applicable at low energy excess above threshold (Q ≤ 2 GeV), and the one-instanton approximation is better justified in exclusive electroproduction where multiinstanton contributions are suppressed.

## Method
The author applies the 't Hooft instanton-induced four-quark interaction to exclusive hyperon photoproduction reactions γ + p → K⁺ + Λ and γ + p → K⁺ + Σ⁰ in the center-of-mass system. The calculation assumes massless quarks where chirality coincides with helicity, and uses the valence quark approximation for hadron and photon wave functions, considering both four-quark and six-quark instanton-induced interaction contributions.

## Key numbers
- The sphaleron energy (height of the potential barrier between different QCD vacua) is M_S ≈ 2 GeV, which sets the upper limit for applicability of the one-instanton mechanism
- The energy excess Q above threshold must satisfy Q = W - M_Λ - M_K ≤ M_S for the one-instanton mechanism to be applicable

## Context
This work extends the qualitative phenomenological explanation by Schumacher (2006), which assumed complete spin transfer from photon to strange quark without a QCD mechanism. The instanton-based mechanism provides a theoretical foundation for the strong polarization transfer observed in JLab experiments by Bradford (2006) and Ambrozewicz (2006). The mechanism can be tested through studies of exclusive hyperon photoproduction in association with a strange vector meson (K*), where fully longitudinal polarization of the K* is predicted.
