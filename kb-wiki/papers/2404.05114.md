# Analysis of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) [ֿˆ(2S)] ֿ€^+ֿ€^- (K^+ K^-)$ decays

> We have phenomenologically investigated the decays $B_s^0 \to X(3872) ֿ€^+ֿ€^- (K^+ K^-)$ and $B_s^0 \to ֿˆ(2S) ֿ€^+ ֿ€^- (K^+K^-)$. In our analysis, the scalar meson $f_0(980)$ is formed through the final state interactions of coupled channels $ֿ€ֿ€$ and $K\bar{K}$. Our findings indicate that the $ֿ€^+ֿ€^-$ invariant mass distribution of the $B_s^0 \to ֿˆ(2S)ֿ€^+ֿ€^-$ decay can be accurately reproduced. Furthermore, we have explored the $ֿ€^+ֿ€^- (K^+ K^-)$ invariant mass distribution of the $B_s^0 \to X(3872) ֿ€^+ֿ€^- (K^+ K^-)$ decay, accounting for the different production mechanisms between $X(3872)$ and $ֿˆ(2S)$, up to a global factor. It is found that the production rates for $X(3872)$ and $ֿˆ(2S)$ are much different, which indicates that the structure of $X(3872)$ is more complicated than the $ֿˆ(2S)$, which is a conventional $c\bar{c}$ state. Additionally, we have considered the contributions from $f_0(1500)$ to $ֿ€^+ֿ€^-$ and the $ֿ•$ meson to $K^+ K^-$ in our analysis. Utilizing the model parameters, we have calculated the branching fraction of $B_s^0 \to X(3872) K^+ K^-$, and anticipate that the findings of our study can be experimentally tested in the future.

## Key claim

The production rates for \(X(3872)\) and \(\psi(2S)\) in \(B_s^0\) decays are much different, indicating that the structure of \(X(3872)\) is more complicated than the conventional charmonium state \(\psi(2S)\); however, the analysis relies on a global factor to account for the different production mechanisms, and the conclusion is hedged by the need to adjust the production coupling parameter \(g_1'\) to \(0.69 g_1\) to match experimental data.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary approach to describe the final-state interactions of coupled channels \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar{K}\), dynamically generating the scalar meson \(f_0(980)\). They fit the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) invariant mass distribution of \(B_s^0 \to \psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-\) to LHCb data with five free parameters: \(Cg_2^2\), \(g_1/g_2\), the relative phase \(\varphi\), and the mass and width of \(f_0(1500)\) treated as a Breit-Wigner resonance. The same formalism is then applied to \(B_s^0 \to X(3872)\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(B_s^0 \to X(3872)K^+K^-\), with the production coupling \(g_1'\) adjusted separately, and the \(\phi\) meson contribution to the \(K^+K^-\) channel is included via a \(p\)-wave amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The fitted mass of \(f_0(1500)\) is \(1450.0 \pm 6.8\) MeV and its width is \(164.4 \pm 22.4\) MeV, from the \(\chi^2\) fit to \(B_s^0 \to \psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-\) data with \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.4\).
* The branching fraction of \(B_s^0 \to X(3872) (\phi \to K^+K^-)\) is \((8.3 \pm 3.0) \times 10^{-5}\), consistent with the narrow-width approximation value \((5.4 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-5}\).
* The ratio \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872) (f_0(980) \to K^+K^-)] / \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872)(K^+K^-)_{\text{non-}\phi}] = 0.06 \pm 0.02\).
* The ratio \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872) (f_0(980) \to K^+K^-)] / \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to X(3872) (f_0(980) \to \pi^+\pi^-)] = 0.5 \pm 0.3\).
* The ratio \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) (f_0(980) \to K^+K^-)] / \mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \psi(2S) (f_0(980) \to \pi^+\pi^-)] = 0.6 \pm 0.3\).
* The ratio \(R_1\) for \(f_0(980)\) (comparing \(X(3872)\) to \(J/\psi\) production) is \(0.16 \pm 0.09\), while \(R_2\) for \(\psi(2S)\) is \(0.51 \pm 0.28\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to \(B_s^0 \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-\) by Liang et al. (2014) and Xie et al. (2018) to the analogous decays involving \(\psi(2S)\) and the exotic candidate \(X(3872)\). It directly addresses the recent LHCb observation of \(B_s^0 \to X(3872)\pi^+\pi^-\) (LHCb, 2023) and provides predictions for the \(K^+K^-\) channels that can be tested in future experiments. The finding that the production coupling for \(X(3872)\) differs from that for \(\psi(2S)\) supports the interpretation that \(X(3872)\) is not a pure charmonium state, consistent with molecular or hybrid explanations discussed in the literature.
