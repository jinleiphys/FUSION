# Hybrid meson decay from the lattice

> We discuss the allowed decays of a hybrid meson in the heavy quark limit. We deduce that an important decay will be into a heavy quark non-hybrid state and a light quark meson, in other words, the de-excitation of an excited gluonic string by emission of a light quark-antiquark pair. We discuss the study of hadronic decays from the lattice in the heavy quark limit and apply this approach to explore the transitions from a spin-exotic hybrid to $χ_b η$ and $χ_b S$ where $S$ is a scalar meson. We obtain a signal for the transition emitting a scalar meson and we discuss the phenomenological implications.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a spin-exotic hybrid meson (with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\)) in the heavy quark limit can decay via de-excitation of the gluonic string by emitting a light quark-antiquark pair, and that a lattice signal for the transition emitting a scalar meson is obtained, but the transition to a pseudoscalar meson (\(\eta\)) is too weak to be measured, yielding only an upper limit.

## Method

The authors use lattice QCD with \(N_f=2\) dynamical fermion configurations (sea quarks of approximately strange quark mass) at \(\beta=5.2\) on a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a \approx 0.1\) fm. They compute static potentials for the ground state (\(A_{1g}\)) and the first gluonic excitation (\(E_u\)) as a function of heavy quark separation \(R\), and evaluate three-point correlation functions to extract the transition amplitude \(x\) for the decay of a hybrid meson (in the \(E_u\) representation) to a ground-state quarkonium (\(A_{1g}\)) plus a flavour-singlet light meson (scalar or pseudoscalar), using momentum projections to achieve approximate on-shell conditions.

## Key numbers

- Hybrid meson mass estimate for \(b\) quarks: \(m(H) = 10.76(7)\) GeV, approximately 1.3 GeV heavier than the \(\Upsilon\).
- Spatial wavefunction overlap factor between hybrid and \(\chi_b\) states: \(\int u_H u_{\chi} dR = 0.63\).
- Transition amplitude for scalar meson emission at \(R = 2a = 0.2\) fm: \(ax = 0.009(1)\).
- Transition rate for scalar meson emission at \(R = 0.2\) fm: \(\Gamma = 0.061(14)\) GeV.
- Upper limit on transition rate for pseudoscalar (\(\eta\)) emission at \(R = 2a\): \(\Gamma < 1\) MeV (from \(xt = 0 \pm 0.0009\) at \(t=1\)).
- Lattice spacing: \(a \approx 0.1\) fm, from \(r_0/a = 5.04(4)\).
- Pion mass on lattice: \(am = 0.294(4)\); scalar meson mass: \(am = 0.628(30)\).
- Energy of scalar meson with momentum \(\mathbf{n}=(1,0,0)\): \(aE(100) = 0.74(3)\).
- Energy of pseudoscalar meson with momentum \(\mathbf{n}=(1,1,0)\): \(aE(110) = 0.66\).
- Transition amplitude values at various \(R\) (in fm): \(ax = 0.005(1)\) at \(R=0.1\), \(0.009(1)\) at \(R=0.2\), \(0.012(1)\) at \(R=0.3\), \(0.013(2)\) at \(R=0.4\), \(0.015(2)\) at \(R=0.5\), \(0.017(2)\) at \(R=0.6\).

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of hybrid mesons in the static quark limit, building on prior determinations of hybrid spectra (reviewed in ref. hf8) and the adiabatic approximation for gluonic excitations. It directly tests selection rules for hybrid decays proposed by Page (1995) and applies the lattice method for hadronic transitions developed by McNeile and Michael (2001) and the UKQCD Collaboration (2001). The results enable future experimental searches for spin-exotic hybrid mesons in \(b\bar{b}\) systems, particularly by providing a predicted decay rate to \(\chi_b\) plus a scalar meson (which subsequently decays to \(\pi\pi\)), while suggesting that decays to \(\chi_b\eta\) are strongly suppressed.
