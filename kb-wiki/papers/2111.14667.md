# Off-equilibrium non-Gaussian fluctuations near the QCD critical point: an effective field theory perspective

> The non-Gaussian fluctuations of baryon density are sensitive to the presence of the conjectured QCD critical point. Their observational consequences are crucial for the ongoing experimental search for this critical point through the beam energy scan program at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). In the expanding fireball created in a heavy-ion collision, critical fluctuations would inescapably fall out of equilibrium, and require a systematic description within a dynamical framework. In this paper, we employ newly developed effective field theory (EFT) for fluctuating hydrodynamics to study the real-time critical non-Gaussian fluctuations of a conserved charge density. In particular, we derive the evolution equations for multi-point correlators of density fluctuations and obtain the closed-form solutions with arbitrary initial conditions that can readily be implemented in realistic simulations for heavy-ion collisions. We find that non-linear interactions among noise fields, which are missing in traditional stochastic hydrodynamics, could potentially contribute to the quartic (fourth order) fluctuations in scaling regime even at tree level in off-equilibrium situations.

## Key claim

Using an effective field theory (EFT) for fluctuating hydrodynamics, the authors derive evolution equations for multi-point correlators of conserved charge density fluctuations near the QCD critical point and obtain closed-form solutions with arbitrary initial conditions; they find that non-linear interactions among noise fields, which are absent in traditional stochastic hydrodynamics, could contribute to quartic (fourth-order) fluctuations in the scaling regime even at tree level in off-equilibrium situations.

## Method

The authors construct an EFT action on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour for a generic conserved charge density, incorporating symmetry constraints including gauge invariance, local shift symmetry, and dynamical KMS symmetry. They derive Schwinger-Dyson equations for equal-time multi-point correlators of density fluctuations, expand the Lagrangian density in fluctuating fields up to fourth order, and introduce rescaled fields with a power counting scheme based on an effective coupling constant \(g_{\text{eff}} = g_0 (\tilde{\xi})^{3/2}\) where \(\tilde{\xi} = \xi/\xi_0\) is the scaled correlation length. The evolution equations are solved at tree level, including contributions from stochastic coefficients \(\vartheta_1\) and \(\vartheta_2\) that parametrize non-linear noise-noise interactions.

## Key numbers

- The effective coupling constant scales as \(g_{\text{eff}} = g_0 (\tilde{\xi})^{3/2}\), where \(\tilde{\xi} = \xi/\xi_0\) and the critical exponent \(\delta_c \approx 5\) (3d Ising universality class) is used to derive the exponent \(3/2\).
- The equilibrium two-point correlator takes the Ornstein-Zernike form \(\bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}) = 1/[a f(\mathbf{q})]\) with \(f(\mathbf{q}) = 1 + c \mathbf{q}^2\) and \(c = K/a = \xi^2\).
- The three-point equilibrium correlator is \(\bar{W}_3(\mathbf{q}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{q}_3) = -a' \prod_{i=1}^3 \bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}_i)\).
- The four-point equilibrium correlator is \(\bar{W}_4(\mathbf{q}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{q}_4) = [-a'' + (a')^2(\bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}_{12})+\bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}_{13})+\bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}_{14}))] \prod_{i=1}^4 \bar{W}_2(\mathbf{q}_i)\), where \(\mathbf{q}_{ij} = \mathbf{q}_i + \mathbf{q}_j\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical modeling of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions beyond traditional stochastic hydrodynamics (Landau-Lifshitz approach) by employing the EFT framework developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) and the non-linear noise couplings identified by Jain and Kovtun (2020). It complements prior studies on evolution equations for two-point correlators using Hydro-Kinetics (Akamatsu, 2018) and Hydro+ formalism (Stephanov, 2017; Rajagopal, 2019; Du, 2020), as well as works on non-Gaussian fluctuations by Mukherjee (2015, 2016) and An (2020). The results are ready for implementation in realistic simulations of heavy-ion collisions for the beam energy scan program at RHIC, and they open the question of the critical behavior of the stochastic coefficients \(\vartheta_1\) and \(\vartheta_2\), which remains unknown.
