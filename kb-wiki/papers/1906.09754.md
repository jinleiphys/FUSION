# Finite size of hardons and HBT interferometry for hydrodynamic sources

> Hadrons formed in heavy-ion collisions are not point-like objects, they cannot occupy too close space-time points. When the two bosons are too close to each other, their constituents start to mix and they cannot be considered as bosons subjected to Bose-Einstein statistics, this effect is called excluded volume effect. We study the volume effect on HBT for the sources with various sizes. The effect on HBT was shown in out, side and long directions, and it is more obvious for the source with a narrow space-time distribution. The correlation functions for high transverse momenta are more suppressed by the volume effect. Hence the incoherence parameter may be more suppressed by the volume effect for high transverse momenta in small collision systems.

# Finite size of hadrons and HBT interferometry for hydrodynamic sources

## Key claim
The excluded volume effect (finite hadron size) suppresses two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions more strongly for sources with narrow space-time distributions and for high transverse momenta, which may lead to a smaller slope of the incoherence parameter with respect to transverse momentum in small collision systems, but the effect depends on the interplay between initial energy density and initial source radius.

## Method
The authors used ideal relativistic hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions with Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and the s95p-PCE equation of state to model pion-emitting sources. They computed the two-pion HBT correlation function with and without an excluded volume correction, using a Gaussian cut-off function with spatial cut-off distance Δr = 1 fm and a newly introduced temporal cut-off Δt = 0.2 fm/c, applied to the Cooper-Frye freeze-out emission function at T = 130 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density parameters: ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³ and ε₀ = 100 GeV/fm³
- Initial Gaussian source radii: R_x = R_y = 1 fm and R_x = R_y = 2 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T = 130 MeV
- Cut-off distance: Δr ≈ 1 fm (corresponding to 2 r_V, where r_V is the pion excluded volume radius)
- Cut-off time difference: Δt = 0.2 fm/c
- Initial proper time: τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- For initial condition (a) (ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³, R = 1 fm): average freeze-out time τ̄ = 2.07 fm/c, average transverse radius |r⊥| = 1.37 fm, time width σ_τ = 0.67 fm/c, spatial width σ_r = 0.53 fm
- For initial condition (b) (ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³, R = 2 fm): τ̄ = 1.14 fm/c, |r⊥| = 1.94 fm, σ_τ = 0.30 fm/c, σ_r = 0.76 fm
- For initial condition (c) (ε₀ = 100 GeV/fm³, R = 1 fm): τ̄ = 3.60 fm/c, |r⊥| = 1.85 fm, σ_τ = 0.94 fm/c, σ_r = 0.65 fm
- For initial condition (d) (ε₀ = 100 GeV/fm³, R = 2 fm): τ̄ = 2.82 fm/c, |r⊥| = 2.66 fm, σ_τ = 1.09 fm/c, σ_r = 1.10 fm
- Transverse momentum intervals studied: 0.2 GeV/c ≤ K_T ≤ 0.4 GeV/c and 0.4 GeV/c ≤ K_T ≤ 0.6 GeV/c
- Relative momentum q for other two directions: less than 40 MeV/c
- Spatial rapidity η_s range: (-1, 1)

## Context
This work extends the study of the excluded volume effect on HBT interferometry, first introduced by Bialas et al. (2013) using the blast-wave model for pp collisions at 7 TeV, to hydrodynamic sources with varying sizes and initial conditions. The authors use a more realistic hydrodynamic description with the s95p-PCE equation of state (Shen et al., 2010) and introduce a new temporal cut-off parameter Δt. The results suggest that the excluded volume effect could explain the observed small slope of the incoherence parameter λ with respect to transverse momentum in small collision systems, complementing previous explanations involving partial coherence, resonance decays, and non-Gaussian correlation functions (STAR, 2004; STAR, 2005; Ma et al.).
