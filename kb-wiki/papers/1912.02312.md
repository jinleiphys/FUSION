# Exploring hybrid equation of state with constraints from tidal deformability of GW170817

> With a interpolation method on the P-$μ$ plane, a hybrid equation of state is explored. The quark phase is described by our newly developed self-consistent two-flavor Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model. It retains the contribution from the vector channel in the Fierz-transformed Lagrangian by introducing a weighting parameter $α$ [Chin. Phys. C \textbf{43}, 084102 (2019)]. In the hadron phase we use the relativistic mean-field theory. We study the dependence of hybrid EOS and mass-radius relation on $α$. It is found that increasing $α$ makes the hybrid EOS softer in the medium pressure. We can get stellar mass larger than $2M_\odot$. Further, we calculate the tidal deformability $\tildeΛ$ for binary stars and compare with recent analysis GW170817 [Phys. Rev. X \textbf{9}, 011001 (2019)].

# Exploring hybrid equation of state with constraints from tidal deformability of GW170817

## Key claim

The hybrid equation of state constructed via a sigmoid interpolation on the P-μ plane can simultaneously satisfy the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (̃Λ within 300^{+420}_{-230}) and produce stellar masses above 2 M⊙, but only when the interpolation width Γ is sufficiently large (≥300 MeV); narrower interpolations yield tidal deformabilities that exceed the observational bounds.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by interpolating between a hadronic phase described by the nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with NL3 parameter set and a quark phase described by a self-consistent two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a weighting parameter α that controls the vector channel contribution from the Fierz-transformed Lagrangian. The interpolation uses a sigmoid function f±(μ) = ½[1 ± tanh((μ−μc)/Γ)] on the P-μ plane, with central chemical potential μc and half-width Γ as free parameters, plus a thermodynamic consistency term Δε. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and tidal deformabilities are computed via the l=2 tidal Love number formalism including the nonzero surface energy density from the NJL model.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of pure neutron star with NL3 parameters: 2.76 M⊙ with radius about 12.66 km
- Hadron matter baryon density at μ=1300 MeV: 2.67 ρ0 (with ρ0=0.148 fm⁻³); pressure: 108 MeV/fm³
- Hadron matter baryon density at μ=1400 MeV: 2.98 ρ0; pressure: 151 MeV/fm³
- Quark matter baryon density at μ=1300 MeV: about 4.5 ρ0 for different α; pressure range 100–400 MeV
- Quark matter baryon density at μ=1400 MeV: about 5.6 ρ0
- Transition chemical potential for Maxwell construction at α=0.5: 1215 MeV
- For Γ=50 MeV and μc=1300 MeV, calculated ̃Λ values range from 839 to 889, exceeding the GW170817 low-spin constraint ̃Λ = 300^{+420}_{-230}
- For Γ=300 MeV and μc=1300 MeV, ̃Λ values range from 459 to 520 across α=0.5–0.9, within the GW170817 low-spin range
- For Γ=500 MeV, μc=1300 MeV, and α=0.5, ̃Λ values range from 331 to 353, closer to the central value of 300
- Radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star is less than 13.76 km and larger than 10.7 km

## Context

This work extends the hybrid equation of state approach using interpolation methods previously applied by Alford et al. (2013) and others, where an additional speed-of-sound term is added in the quark phase, and by Macher and Baym (2018) who treated pressure combination as a function of endpoint positions. The authors compare with the Maxwell construction (which yields mass-twin stars as discussed by Gerlach, Kaltenborn, and Blaschke) and the Gibbs construction. The NJL model used is the authors' own self-consistent mean-field approach from Wang et al. (2019) that introduces the weighting parameter α. The results are compared directly with the GW170817 analysis by Abbott et al. (2019) which gave the ̃Λ constraint 300^{+420}_{-230} for the low-spin case, and with high-mass pulsar measurements from PSR J1614-2230 (1.928±0.017 M⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01±0.04 M⊙). The work enables future studies of hybrid star properties with controlled interpolation parameters and suggests that the existence of hybrid stars is compatible with current gravitational-wave data when the mixed phase region is sufficiently broad.
