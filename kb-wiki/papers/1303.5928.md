# Determination of $sin^{2}θ_W$ using $ν(\barν)$-Nucleus scattering

> We have studied nonisoscalarity and medium effects in the extraction of weak mixing angle using Paschos and Wolfenstein relation in the iron nucleus. Paschos and Wolfenstein(PW) relation is valid for an isoscalar target. We have modified the PW relation for nonisoscalar target as well as incorporated the medium effects like Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, nuclear binding energy and pion rho cloud contributions. In our calculations we have used the relativistic nuclear spectral function which includes nucleon correlations. Finally local density approximation is applied to translate the numerical results to the finite nuclei. We have studied the dependence of $sin^{2}θ_W$ on Bjorken variables $x$ and $y$, four momentum transfer square ($Q^2$), energy of the neutrino and antineutrino, and effect of excess neutrons over protons in the nuclear target.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the extraction of the weak mixing angle \(\sin^2\theta_W\) from neutrino-iron scattering is significantly affected by both nonisoscalarity corrections (the excess of neutrons over protons in iron) and nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, binding energy, and pion-rho cloud contributions), and that the extracted value depends on the kinematic variables \(x\), \(y\), \(Q^2\), and neutrino energy, not just on the target composition.

## Method

The authors modify the Paschos-Wolfenstein (PW) relation, originally valid for isoscalar targets, to include a nonisoscalarity correction term \(\delta R\) for a \(^{56}\text{Fe}\) nucleus. They compute the nuclear hadronic tensor using a relativistic nuclear spectral function that incorporates nucleon correlations, with separate Fermi seas for protons and neutrons, and apply the local density approximation to translate results to finite nuclei. The differential cross sections for charged-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering are then used to extract \(\sin^2\theta_W\) from the modified PW relation, including the effects of pion and rho meson cloud contributions from a microscopic meson-nucleus self-energy model.

## Key numbers

- The NuTeV Collaboration obtained \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2277 \pm 0.0004\) from an iron target, which is 3 standard deviations above the global fit value of \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2227 \pm 0.0004\).
- The nonisoscalarity correction \(\delta R\) is shown for an anti(neutrino) energy of \(E = 80\ \text{GeV}\) as a function of \(y\) at different \(x\) values.
- The extracted \(\sin^2\theta_W\) is presented for \(^{56}\text{Fe}\) at \(E = 80\ \text{GeV}\) and \(Q^2 = 25\ \text{GeV}^2\), where at low \(x\) the value is "almost close to the standard value" (no explicit numerical value given for this specific point).
- The dependence of \(\sin^2\theta_W\) on \(E\), \(Q^2\), and \(x\) is shown, with significant changes observed across different kinematic regions.

## Context

This work addresses the NuTeV anomaly, where the NuTeV Collaboration (Zeller, 2002) found a 3-sigma deviation of \(\sin^2\theta_W\) from the global electroweak fit using an iron target. The paper extends prior studies of nuclear effects in neutrino scattering by using a relativistic spectral function approach that includes nucleon correlations, building on the authors' own earlier work (Haider et al., 2011; Ruiz Simo et al., 2012). The results enable more accurate extraction of the weak mixing angle from nonisoscalar nuclear targets, which is important for future neutrino oscillation experiments and tests of the Standard Model.
