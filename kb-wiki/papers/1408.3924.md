# Hidden-Charm Tetraquarks and Charged Zc States

> Experimentally several charged axial-vector hidden-charm states were reported. Within the framework of the color-magnetic interaction, we have systematically considered the mass spectrum of the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom tetraquark states. It is impossible to accommodate all the three charged states $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4025)$ and $Z_c(4200)$ within the axial vector tetraquark spectrum simultaneously. Not all these three states are tetraquark candidates. Moreover, the eigenvector of the chromomagnetic interaction contains valuable information of the decay pattern of the tetraquark states. The dominant decay mode of the lowest axial vector tetraquark state is $J/ψπ$ while its $D^*\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}^*D^*$ modes are strongly suppressed, which is in contrast with the fact that the dominant decay mode of $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4025)$ is $\bar{D}D^*$ and $\bar{D}^*D^*$ respectively. We emphasize that all the available experimental information indicates that $Z_c(4200)$ is a very promising candidate of the lowest axial vector hidden-charm tetraquark state.

## Key claim

It is impossible to accommodate all three charged states Zc(3900), Zc(4025) and Zc(4200) within the axial vector tetraquark spectrum simultaneously; at least one or two of these states is not a tetraquark candidate, and the authors emphasize that all available experimental information indicates Zc(4200) is a very promising candidate of the lowest axial vector hidden-charm tetraquark state.

## Method

The authors used the chromomagnetic (CM) interaction Hamiltonian derived from one-gluon exchange to compute the mass spectrum of hidden-charm and hidden-bottom tetraquark states with J^P = 0^+, 1^+, and 2^+. They constructed SU(6)_cs color-spin wavefunctions for the qq\bar{q}\bar{q} tetraquark system in both the diquark (Q \otimes \bar{Q}) and meson-meson (\tilde{Q} \otimes \tilde{Q}) representations, diagonalized the CM interaction matrix to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and extracted tetraquark masses using two schemes: fixing the CM strength parameter v from the mass of one Zc state, or from the mass splitting between two Zc states.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses extracted from meson masses: m_c = 1534 MeV, m_u = 437 MeV, m_s = 542 MeV, m_b = 4730 MeV
- For the J^P=1^+ qc\bar{q}\bar{c} tetraquark, the six CM eigenvalues are: -15.9v, -4.1v, -1.5v, 1.7v, 5.6v, 5.8v
- When Zc(4025) is assigned as the state with eigenvalue 1.7v (v = 48.8), the state with eigenvalue 5.6v has mass 4215.4 MeV (close to Zc(4200)) and the state with eigenvalue -1.5v has mass 3868.8 MeV (30 MeV below Zc(3900))
- When Zc(4200) is assigned as the state with eigenvalue 5.6v (v = 46.1), the state with eigenvalue 1.7v has mass 4020.3 MeV (close to Zc(4020)) and the state with eigenvalue -1.5v has mass 3872.9 MeV (28 MeV below Zc(3900))
- For the lowest J^P=1^+ qc\bar{q}\bar{c} tetraquark state (eigenvalue -15.9v), when v is fixed by Zc(3900) as this state, v = 2.6 and the mass is 3900 MeV
- For the J^P=0^+ qc\bar{q}\bar{c} tetraquark, the lowest eigenvalue is -18.6v giving mass 3033.9 MeV when v is fixed by Zc(4025) as the 1.7v state

## Context

This work extends the chromomagnetic interaction approach previously applied to light tetraquarks in the MIT bag model by Jaffe (1977) and to hidden-charm tetraquarks by Chao (1980, 1981) and by Cui (2006, 2007) in the constituent quark model. The study systematically compares the computed tetraquark spectrum with the experimentally observed charged Zc states reported by BESIII, Belle, and CLEO collaborations, finding that Zc(3900) and Zc(4025) have decay patterns inconsistent with the lowest axial vector tetraquark state, while Zc(4200) is a promising tetraquark candidate. The eigenvectors of the CM interaction provide predictions for decay branching fractions that can be tested by future experiments.
