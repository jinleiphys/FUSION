# Radiative corrections to two-neutrino double-beta decay

> We use heavy-nucleus effective field theory to compute radiative corrections to two-neutrino double-$β$ decay ($2νββ$). Our main result is the first derivation of a universal radiative-correction factor for double-weak decays -- the analogue of the Sirlin function in single-$β$ decay -- independent of nuclear matrix elements and excitation energies. This "double-weak Sirlin function" depends on the individual electron energies as well as their relative angle and differs significantly from the approximation obtained by summing two single-$β$ decay Sirlin functions. In addition, we calculate the nuclear-structure-dependent component of the radiative corrections and find that they can still be neglected at current experimental sensitivities. On the other hand, the double-weak Sirlin function induces distortions of the electron energies and angular spectra that are comparable in size to the leading nuclear-structure corrections parametrized by the ratio of nuclear matrix elements, $ξ_{31}$. Our results indicate that extractions of nuclear structure information and tests of the Standard Model from high-precision $2νββ$ measurements must include double-weak radiative corrections, implying that recent extractions of $ξ_{31}$ should be revisited.

## Key claim
The paper derives the first universal radiative-correction factor for double-weak decays (the "double-weak Sirlin function") that depends on both electron energies and their relative angle, and shows that this correction differs significantly from the approximation obtained by summing two single-beta decay Sirlin functions. The authors find that these radiative corrections induce distortions in electron energy and angular spectra comparable in size to the leading nuclear-structure correction parametrized by the ratio of nuclear matrix elements, ξ₃₁, implying that recent extractions of ξ₃₁ from experimental data must be revisited.

## Method
The authors constructed a heavy-nucleus effective field theory (EFT) containing fields for the initial and final 0⁺ nuclear states and vector fields for each 1⁺ intermediate nuclear excitation, with minimal couplings to photons. They evaluated all virtual and real photon diagrams at O(α) using dimensional regularization, including topologies with photons attached to the intermediate nucleus and between the two electrons that have no analogue in single-beta decay, and expanded the results in the ratio of lepton energies to nuclear excitation energies (large-ωₙ limit). The calculation was checked by matching onto an even lower-energy EFT with only initial and final state nuclei, and the IR divergences from virtual diagrams were canceled against those from real emission diagrams.

## Key numbers
- The vector coupling g_V at scale μ = 2E₀ for ⁷⁶Ge is 1.02054(12), which is important for the total rate but does not affect spectral shape.
- The large-ωₙ expansion agrees with the full expression within 10% for the lowest intermediate states in ⁷⁶Ge and ¹³⁶Xe, sufficient for spectrum accuracy at the O(10⁻³) level.
- Nuclear Shell Model values of ξ₃₁ for the four isotopes: {⁷⁶Ge, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹³⁶Xe, ¹³⁰Te} ≃ {0.12, 0.33, 0.12, 0.16}.
- Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation values of ξ₃₁: {⁷⁶Ge, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹³⁶Xe, ¹³⁰Te} ≃ {0.11, 0.49, 0.20, 0.32}.
- The branching ratio for 2νββ+γ is O(10⁻²–10⁻³) for photon energy cuts x_γ^cut = 0.01–0.1, and falls rapidly for harder cuts.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for 2νββ decay beyond the leading-order factorization of nuclear matrix elements and leptonic phase space developed by Simkovic (2018) and the subleading nuclear effects (weak magnetism, pion-exchange currents) computed by Morabit (2024). Prior estimates of radiative corrections by Nitescu (2024) approximated them by summing two single-beta decay Sirlin functions, which the present calculation shows to be inadequate. The results enable precision extractions of the nuclear-structure parameter ξ₃₁ from upcoming tonne-scale experiments (LEGEND, nEXO, CUPID, NEXT) and motivate dedicated searches for radiative 2νββ+γ events, whose branching ratios are quantified here for the first time.
