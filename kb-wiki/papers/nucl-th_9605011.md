# Can dileptons be observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC?

> Both dilepton and charm production at RHIC are considered to be important signatures for Quark-Gluon Plasma production. Recently it was argued by S.Gavin, P.L.McGaughey,P.V. Ruuskanen and R.Vogt that the background from semileptonic correlated charm decays is so large that it makes dilepton measurements virtually impossible. We show that this conclusion in fact is in fact reversed if the energy loss due to secondary interaction of charmed quarks is included.

# Can dileptons be observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC?

## Key claim
The background from correlated semileptonic charm decays does not make dilepton measurements impossible at RHIC, *provided* that energy loss of charmed quarks due to secondary interactions in dense matter is included. The earlier pessimistic conclusion by Gavin, McGaughey, Ruuskanen, and Vogt (1995) is reversed because charmed quarks are not penetrating probes and are mostly stopped in matter.

## Method
The author performs a Monte Carlo simulation of charm production in central AuAu collisions at RHIC, following the same input assumptions as Gavin et al. (1995) for the charm production cross-section and transverse momentum distribution. The key addition is the inclusion of quark energy loss dE/dx in the quark-gluon plasma, estimated using the BDPS (Baier-Dokshitzer-Peigné-Schiff) approach generalized to QCD, with a value of approximately 2 GeV/fm. The simulation then tracks whether charmed and bottom quarks escape the nuclear medium before decaying semileptonically, and computes the resulting dilepton invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Energy loss of charmed quarks in QGP at RHIC: dE/dx ≈ 2 GeV/fm, estimated using the BDPS formula with parameters from the "hot glue scenario"
- The distribution of single quark path lengths through the nucleus is flat between d ≈ 0.2 R (≈ 1 fm for heavy nuclei) and 2R (the nuclear diameter)
- The boundary between stopped and escaping charm decay contributions in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum is at M ≈ 1.7 GeV for charm quarks and M ≈ 4.5 GeV for bottom quarks
- Above these mass boundaries, the background suppression from energy loss is roughly 2 orders of magnitude
- The signal-to-background ratio from Gavin et al. (1995) is about 1/10 for dilepton masses M = 2-8 GeV from charm, and about 1/3 for M > 5 GeV from bottom decays

## Context
This work directly contradicts the conclusions of Gavin, McGaughey, Ruuskanen, and Vogt (1995), who argued that correlated charm decay background makes dilepton measurements impossible at RHIC. The author extends the penetrating probe concept from Shuryak (1980) by noting that while dileptons and photons are penetrating, charmed quarks are not. The energy loss treatment follows the BDPS approach (Baier et al., 1995) for QCD. This work enables optimism for dilepton measurements at RHIC (particularly for PHENIX) and ALICE at LHC, and suggests that both dilepton and charm signals may be measurable using angular distribution differences and nuclear scaling (A^(4/3)) to separate signal from background.
