# Hyperons in nuclear matter from SU(3) chiral effective field theory

> Brueckner theory is used to investigate the properties of hyperons in nuclear matter. The hyperon-nucleon interaction is taken from chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order with SU(3) symmetric low-energy constants. Furthermore, the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction is also derived within chiral effective field theory. We present the single-particle potentials of Lambda and Sigma hyperons in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter computed with the continuous choice for intermediate spectra. The results are in good agreement with the empirical information. In particular, our calculation gives a repulsive Sigma-nuclear potential and a weak Lambda-nuclear spin-orbit force.

## Key claim

Using Brueckner theory with hyperon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions both derived from SU(3) chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order, the authors find that the resulting single-particle potentials for Lambda and Sigma hyperons in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are consistent with empirical hypernuclear information, provided the continuous choice for intermediate spectra is employed.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in nuclear matter using the continuous choice for intermediate-state spectra, with the hyperon-nucleon interaction taken from SU(3) chiral EFT at NLO and the nucleon-nucleon interaction also derived from chiral EFT at NLO. The single-particle potentials for Lambda and Sigma hyperons were computed in symmetric nuclear matter, asymmetric nuclear matter, and pure neutron matter by summing contributions from all baryon species in the nuclear Fermi sea, with the Pauli blocking operator and energy denominator angle-averaged in the standard approximation.

## Key numbers

- Lambda single-particle potential at saturation density (k_F = 1.35 fm^-1) in symmetric nuclear matter: approximately -28 to -24 MeV (NLO, continuous choice, cutoff 500-650 MeV)
- Sigma single-particle potential at saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter: approximately +7 to +15 MeV (NLO, continuous choice, cutoff 500-650 MeV), indicating a repulsive mean field
- Lambda spin-orbit splitting parameter S_Lambda(k=0) at saturation density: -3.0 to -3.6 MeV (NLO, continuous choice, cutoff 500-650 MeV), significantly weaker than the NSC97f model value of -14.2 MeV
- Sigma spin-orbit splitting parameter S_Sigma(k=0) at saturation density: -17.7 to -15.3 MeV (NLO, continuous choice, cutoff 500-650 MeV)
- Deuteron binding energy from the chiral NLO NN interaction: -2.257 to -2.145 MeV (cutoff 500-650 MeV), compared to the empirical value of -2.224575 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier hypernuclear matter calculations by Haidenbauer and Meissner (2015) by using a chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon interaction instead of a phenomenological one, implementing the continuous choice for intermediate spectra, and investigating isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The results are consistent with empirical information from hypernuclear phenomenology, particularly the repulsive Sigma-nuclear potential and weak Lambda-nuclear spin-orbit force, and provide a starting point for future studies of hypernuclei and neutron star matter within SU(3) chiral EFT.
