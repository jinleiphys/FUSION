# Proton and neutron skins of light nuclei within the Relativistic Mean Field theory

> The Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory is applied to the analysis of ground-state properties of Ne, Na, Cl and Ar isotopes. In particular, we study the recently established proton skin in Ar isotopes and neutron skin in Na isotopes as a function of the difference between the proton and the neutron separation energy. We use the TMA effective interaction in the RMF Lagrangian, and describe pairing correlation by the density-independent delta-function interaction. We calculate single neutron and proton separation energies, quadrupole deformations, nuclear matter radii and differences between proton radii and neutron radii, and compare these results with the recent experimental data.

# Proton and neutron skins of light nuclei within the Relativistic Mean Field theory

## Key claim
The deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the TMA effective interaction reproduces the experimentally observed monotonic increase of neutron skin in Na isotopes and proton skin in Ar isotopes, but fails to reproduce the proton skin in 37Ar and 38Ar specifically. The correlation between skin thickness ΔR = Rp − Rn and the difference between proton and neutron separation energy ΔS = Sp − Sn is confirmed as the driving force for skin formation.

## Method
The authors applied a deformed RMF+BCS method using a density-independent delta-function pairing interaction with strength V0 = 343.7 MeV fm3. They expanded fermion and boson fields in 14 oscillator shells and performed constrained quadrupole calculations to find the ground-state deformation, then non-constrained calculations at that deformation. Calculations were carried out with three parameter sets (TMA, TM2, and NL3) for Ne, Na, Cl, and Ar isotopes, and results were compared with experimental data and with the HF+BCS model using Skyrme parameters (Goriely, 2001).

## Key numbers
- Pairing strength V0 = 343.7 MeV fm3 used for both protons and neutrons in the density-independent delta-function interaction
- 14 oscillator shells used for expansion of both fermion and boson fields
- The last bound proton-rich Na isotope predicted is 20Na with Sp = 3.099 MeV; 19Na has Sp = −0.013 MeV
- The last bound proton-rich Ar isotope predicted is 31Ar with Sp = 0.741 MeV
- For 21Na, the calculated binding energy for 20Ne is about 1.3 MeV smaller than experiment and for 21Na is about 1.2 MeV larger than experiment, giving a total difference of 2.5 MeV in Sp
- For 35Ar, the calculated binding energy for 34Cl is about 2.5 MeV larger than experiment, while for 35Ar it is about 1 MeV larger than experiment
- The most neutron-rich Ne isotope observed experimentally is 34Ne (Notani, 2002) and for Na isotopes it is 37Na (Notani, 2002), consistent with the calculations

## Context
This work extends the deformed RMF+BCS method previously developed by Geng et al. (2003) to light nuclei in the mass region A < 40, building on the TMA parameter set introduced by Sugahara (1995) which interpolates between the TM1 set (for heavy nuclei, A > 40) and TM2 set (for light nuclei). The study compares with the HF+BCS model of Goriely (2001) and with earlier RHB calculations by Lalazissis et al. (2001) for Be, B, C, N, F, Ne, and Na isotopes. The results enable systematic comparison of relativistic and non-relativistic approaches for proton and neutron skin phenomena, while the discrepancies in proton radii and the failure to reproduce skin in 37Ar and 38Ar point to the need to include alpha-clustering and deformation softness effects beyond the mean field level.
