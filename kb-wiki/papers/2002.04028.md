# Jet quenching and medium response in high-energy heavy-ion collisions: a review

> Jet quenching has been used successfully as a hard probe to study properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in high-energy heavy-collisions at both the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We will review recent progresses in theoretical and phenomenological studies of jet quenching with jet transport models. Special emphasis is given to effects of jet-induced medium response on a wide variety of experimental measurements and their implication on extracting transport properties of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Jet quenching, the modification of high-energy jets in heavy-ion collisions due to interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), can be used to extract the jet transport coefficient of the QGP, but a complete understanding requires including the transport of recoil partons and the hydrodynamic response to jet-medium interactions, not just the energy loss of leading partons.

## Method

The authors review theoretical and phenomenological studies of jet quenching using jet transport models, with a focus on the high-twist (HT) framework for calculating medium-induced gluon radiation and parton energy loss. They compare the HT approach with the opacity expansion method (GLV), the BDMPS-Z formalism, the AMY approach at finite temperature, and the SCET_G formalism, and discuss how jet-induced medium excitation through recoil parton transport and hydrodynamic response modifies jet observables.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient extracted by the JET Collaboration from hadron suppression data is about 2 orders of magnitude larger than that inside a cold nucleus, indicating extremely high temperature and density of the QGP formed in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
- The quadratic Casimir factor for quarks is C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c) = 4/3 and for gluons is C_A = N_c = 3.
- The color factors for parton-parton scattering are C_gg = 9/4, C_qg = 1, and C_qq = 4/9 for gluon-gluon, gluon-quark, and quark-quark scattering, respectively.
- The Debye screening mass in a medium with 3 flavors of massless quarks is mu_D^2 = 6 pi alpha_s T^2.
- The minimum allowed virtuality scale for a parton in vacuum parton showers is typically taken as 1 GeV^2 (nucleon mass scale).

## Context

This review extends the work of the JET Collaboration (Burke et al., 2013) on systematic extraction of the jet transport coefficient from single hadron suppression data at RHIC and LHC. It builds on theoretical frameworks including the Gyulassy-Wang static potential model (Gyulassy and Wang, 1993), the BDMPS formalism (Baier et al., 1996), the GLV opacity expansion (Gyulassy et al., 1999), the AMY finite-temperature approach (Arnold et al., 2002), and the high-twist approach (Guo and Wang, 2000; Wang, 2001). The review enables future precision studies of QGP properties using fully reconstructed jets and jet substructure measurements at RHIC and LHC, particularly through the inclusion of jet-induced medium response.
