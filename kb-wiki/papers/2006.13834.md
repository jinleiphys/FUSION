# An improved anisotropic hydrodynamics ansatz

> We introduce an improved form for the anisotropic hydrodynamics distribution function which explicitly takes into account the free-streaming and equilibrating contributions separately. We demonstrate that with this improvement one can better reproduce exact results available in the literature for the evolution of moments of the distribution function, in particular, for moments which contain no powers of the longitudinal momentum in their definition (m=0 moments). Using the resulting dynamical equations, we extract the non-equilibrium attractor associated with our improved aHydro ansatz and demonstrate that the improvement also allows one to better reproduce the exact dynamical attractor obtained using kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, particularly at early rescaled times and for m=0 moments.

# An improved anisotropic hydrodynamics ansatz

## Key claim

The improved anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) ansatz, which explicitly separates the distribution function into free-streaming and equilibrating contributions, can better reproduce exact solutions of the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation for moments of the distribution function, particularly for moments with no powers of longitudinal momentum (m=0 moments), but the improvement is most significant at early rescaled times and becomes less accurate for higher-order moments with m>1.

## Method

The authors introduce a two-component ansatz for the one-particle distribution function consisting of a free-streaming contribution (damped exponentially in time) and an equilibrating contribution of Romatschke-Strickland form, with a damping function D(τ,τ0) controlling the transition between them. They derive equations of motion from the first and second moments of the RTA Boltzmann equation for a conformal system undergoing 0+1d boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, then solve these coupled differential equations iteratively to obtain the evolution of the anisotropy parameter ξ and momentum scale Λ. The resulting scaled moments are compared to exact RTA solutions and to the canonical (single-component) aHydro ansatz.

## Key numbers

- The equilibration time is given by τ_eq(T) = 5η̄/T, where η̄ = η/s is the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.
- The scaled time variable used throughout is w̄ = τT/(5η̄).
- The iterative solution converges to a part in 10^8 within five iterations.
- The QGP volume is estimated at ∼100–1000 fm^3, with a lifetime on the order of 10 fm/c in central collisions.
- The effective temperature is defined as T = R_eff^(1/4) Λ0, where R_eff ≡ D(τ,τ0) R(ξ_FS) + [1-D(τ,τ0)] R(ξ) Λ̂^4.

## Context

This work extends prior comparisons between hydrodynamic models and exact RTA Boltzmann solutions by Strickland (2018), which found that the canonical aHydro formulation (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010) performed poorly for m=0 moments. The improved two-component ansatz addresses this failure by incorporating the explicit two-component nature of exact RTA solutions (Florkowski 2013, 2014). The method is presented for 0+1d conformal systems but is stated to be extendable to full 3+1d, potentially enabling better phenomenological descriptions of quark-gluon plasma dynamics at RHIC and LHC, including future applications with temperature-dependent masses similar to quasiparticle aHydro (Alqahtani 2015, 2017).
