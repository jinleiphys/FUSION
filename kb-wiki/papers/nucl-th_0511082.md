# Spectra and binding energy predictions of chiral interactions for 7Li

> Using the no-core shell model approach, we report on the first results for 7Li based on the next-to-next-to-leading order chiral nuclear interaction. Both, two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions are taken into account. We show that the p-shell nuclei are sensitive to the subleading parts of the chiral interactions including three-nucleon forces. Though chiral interactions are soft, we do not observe overbinding for this p-shell nucleus and find a realistic description for the binding energy, excitation spectrum and radius.

## Key claim

Using the no-core shell model (NCSM) with the next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral nuclear interaction including both two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, the authors report the first predictions for 7Li and find that, contrary to expectations from phenomenological models with hard cores, the soft chiral interactions do not produce overbinding for this p-shell nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed ab-initio NCSM calculations for 7Li using the Idaho-N3LO chiral NN interaction combined with a chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) at order Q³. The two unknown 3NF strength parameters c_D and c_E were determined by fitting to the experimental binding energies of ³H (8.482 MeV) and ⁴He (28.3 MeV) via Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations, yielding two parameter sets labeled 3NF-A and 3NF-B. The NCSM calculations used a three-body cluster approximation (a=3) with harmonic oscillator basis states up to N=6 (N_max=9), requiring model spaces of up to 663,527 Slater determinants and approximately 3×10⁹ matrix elements for the A-body Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- ³H binding energy: 8.482 MeV (used as fit constraint, matching experiment)
- ⁴He binding energy: 28.3 MeV (used as fit constraint, matching experiment)
- 3NF-A parameters: c_D = -1.11, c_E = -0.66 (dimensionless)
- 3NF-B parameters: c_D = 8.14, c_E = -2.02 (dimensionless)
- NCSM model space: up to N=6 (N_max=9) with P-space dimension 663,527
- Three-body cluster calculations: Q-spaces up to N_3=48 for J_3 up to 7/2 and N_3=40 for J_3 from 9/2 to 21/2
- Largest three-body P-space dimension: 157 for J_3^π = 5/2⁻, T_3 = 1/2
- Total number of bound states required for cluster calculations: 2,352 across all channels

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory interactions, previously tested only in few-nucleon systems and Nd scattering (Epelbaum 2002), to p-shell nuclei for the first time. It complements phenomenological approaches such as the AV18/Urbana-IX interaction (Pieper 2001) and the Illinois series of 3NFs, which required hard cores to prevent overbinding. The results enable systematic tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions for light nuclei and provide a foundation for future calculations of other p-shell nuclei and reaction rates for astrophysical processes.
