# Theoretical study on favored alpha-decay half-lives of deformed nuclei

> A systematic study on $α$-decay half-lives for the nuclei in the range $93\leq Z \leq 118$ is done by employing various versions of proximity potentials. To obtain the more reliable results, the deformation terms are included up to hexadecapole ($β_{4}$) in the spherical-deformed nuclear and Coulomb interaction potentials. The favored $α$-decays process in this region are categorized as the even-even, odd A, and odd-odd nuclei, first, and second they are grouped into two parts as a ground state to ground state and ground state to isomeric states transition. Due to their root-mean-square deviations (RMSD's) comparison, $Bass 77$ and $Ngo 80$ have the least values and better compromise in reproducing experimental data. Besides, by considering preformation probability within the cluster formation model, the results validate that the significant reduction in root-mean-square deviations has been obtained for different versions of proximity; Hence detract the deviation between calculated and experimental data.

# Theoretical study on favored alpha-decay half-lives of deformed nuclei

## Key claim

The Bass 1977 and Ngo 1980 proximity potentials, when including deformation up to hexadecapole (β₄) and incorporating the alpha-preformation factor from the cluster formation model, provide the best reproduction of experimental alpha-decay half-lives for favored decays in the range 93 ≤ Z ≤ 118, though the quality of agreement depends strongly on whether the decay is even-even, odd-A, or odd-odd, and whether the transition is to a ground state or isomeric state.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha-decay half-lives for 108 transuranium nuclei using 23 versions of proximity potentials within the WKB approximation, treating the alpha particle as spherical and the daughter nucleus as deformed up to hexadecapole (β₄) deformation. The nuclear and Coulomb interaction potentials included deformation and orientation dependence, with the average penetrability obtained by integrating over emission angles. The alpha-preformation factor was computed using the cluster formation model (CFM) based on binding energy differences, and half-lives were compared to experimental data from NUBASE2020 using root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) analysis.

## Key numbers

- 108 transuranium nuclei studied in the atomic range 93 ≤ Z ≤ 118
- 141 favored alpha-decays analyzed: 54 even-even, 81 odd-A, and 6 odd-odd nuclei
- 59 ground-state to ground-state transitions and 82 ground-state to isomeric-state transitions
- Bass 1977 RMSD for even-even nuclei: 0.8281 (without Pα), 0.6547 (with CFM Pα)
- Ngo 1980 RMSD for even-even nuclei: 1.0474 (without Pα), 0.5963 (with CFM Pα)
- Bass 1977 RMSD for odd-A nuclei: 2.2505 (without Pα), 1.8153 (with CFM Pα)
- Ngo 1980 RMSD for odd-A nuclei: 2.4430 (without Pα), 1.9144 (with CFM Pα)
- Bass 1977 RMSD for odd-odd nuclei: 2.2620 (without Pα), 1.6867 (with CFM Pα)
- Ngo 1980 RMSD for odd-odd nuclei: 2.4907 (without Pα), 1.8443 (with CFM Pα)
- Universal Decay Law (UDL) RMSD values: 0.6769 (even-even), 1.8092 (odd-A), 1.7120 (odd-odd), 0.9650 (gs-gs), 1.7550 (gs-is)
- Prox.66 RMSD for even-even: 1.3660 (without Pα), 0.7961 (with CFM Pα)
- Denisov DP RMSD for even-even: 5.4896 (without Pα), 4.7003 (with CFM Pα)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha-decay half-lives using proximity potentials by including deformation up to hexadecapole (β₄) and applying the cluster formation model for preformation factors, following the approach of Deng et al. who studied nuclei around Z=82, N=126 closed shells. The deformation parameters are taken from Moller et al., and the results are compared with the Universal Decay Law (UDL) of Qi et al. (2014). The study enables systematic comparison of 23 proximity potential versions for deformed nuclei and identifies Bass 1977 and Ngo 1980 as the most reliable for predicting favored alpha-decay half-lives in the transuranium region, with the CFM preformation factor significantly reducing RMSD for all versions.
