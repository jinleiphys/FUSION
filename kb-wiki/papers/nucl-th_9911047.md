# Single Photon Production in Relativisitic Heavy Ion Collisions and Quark Hadron Phase Transition

> We discuss the recent developments in the study of single photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular their production at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies is re-examined in view of the results of Aurenche et al which show that the rate of photon production from quark gluon plasma, evaluated at the order of two loops far exceeds the rates evaluated at one-loop level which have formed the basis of all the estimates of photons so far. We find that the production of photons from quark matter could easily out-shine those from the hadronic matter in certain ideal conditions. We further show that the earlier results lending support to the possibility of quark-hadron phase transition from the measured yield of single photons in $S+Au$ collisions at CERN SPS remain valid when an account is made for these developments, though they leave a scope for the formation of (chemically) non-equilibrated plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when photon production rates from quark-gluon plasma are evaluated at two-loop order (following Aurenche et al.), the quark matter contribution can outshine hadronic matter over a wide transverse-momentum window, but only under the condition that a chemically and thermally equilibrated plasma is formed at the initial time. The earlier support for a quark-hadron phase transition from the WA80 S+Au data remains valid, though it leaves scope for a chemically non-equilibrated plasma.

## Method

The author performs hydrodynamic evolution calculations for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, assuming a chemically and thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formed at proper times τ0 = 1 fm/c (SPS) or 0.5 fm/c (RHIC, LHC). Initial temperatures are obtained from the Bjorken condition using specified particle rapidity densities. Photon emission rates from quark matter are taken from the two-loop results of Aurenche et al., while hadronic matter rates use a two-loop photon self-energy with π-ρ interactions and A1 resonance contributions. The same framework is applied to reanalyze the WA80 S+Au data at SPS energies, comparing one-loop and two-loop quark matter rates.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, the quark matter out-shines hadronic matter up to pT = 2 GeV when two-loop rates are used.
- For RHIC energies, the window over which quark matter out-shines hadronic contributions stretches to almost 3 GeV.
- At LHC energies, this window extends to beyond 4 GeV.
- The initial particle rapidity densities used are 825 (SPS), 1734 (RHIC), and 5625 (LHC) for central Pb+Pb collisions.
- The phase transition temperature is taken as T = 160 MeV, with freeze-out at 100 MeV.
- For the S+Au reanalysis, the rapidity density is taken as 225, with τ0 = 1 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the earlier one-loop photon rate calculations of Kapusta et al. and the S+Au analysis of Srivastava and Sinha (1992) by incorporating the two-loop results of Aurenche et al., which show bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering contributions far exceeding one-loop estimates. The findings support the interpretation that a quark-hadron phase transition occurred in S+Au collisions at CERN SPS, consistent with other signatures such as dilepton excess, strange particle enhancement, and J/ψ suppression. The results enable future studies of chemically non-equilibrated plasmas and pre-equilibrium photon contributions from parton cascade models.
