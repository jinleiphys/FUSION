# Charge-changing cross sections for $^{42\textrm{--}51}$Ca and effect of charged-particle evaporation induced by neutron removal reaction

> Charge-changing cross sections $σ_\mathrm{CC}$ for $^{42\textrm{--}51}$Ca on a carbon target at around 280~MeV/nucleon have been measured. The measured $σ_\mathrm{CC}$ values differ significantly from the previously developed calculations based on the Glauber model. However, through introduction of the charged-particle evaporation effect induced by the neutron-removal reaction in addition to the Glauber-model calculation, experimental $σ_\mathrm{CC}$ values on $^{12}$C at around 300~MeV/nucleon for nuclides from C to Fe isotopes are all reproduced with approximately 1\% accuracy. This proposed model systematically reproduces $σ_\mathrm{CC}$ data without phenomenological corrections, and can also explain experimental $σ_\mathrm{CC}$ values obtained in other energy regions.

## Key claim

The measured charge-changing cross sections for calcium isotopes deviate significantly from standard Glauber-model calculations, but are reproduced with approximately 1% accuracy when the charged-particle evaporation effect induced by neutron-removal reactions is included, without requiring phenomenological correction factors.

## Method

Charge-changing cross sections were measured for 42-51Ca on a carbon target at approximately 280 MeV/nucleon using the transmission method at the RIKEN RIBF facility. The authors implemented a zero-range optical-limit approximation (ZROLA) Glauber-model calculation with a nucleon-nucleon total cross section that includes Fermi motion effects, and then added a charged-particle evaporation component from neutron-removal reactions to account for the discrepancy between the bare Glauber calculation and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Measured sigma_CC for 42Ca on 12C at 297 MeV/nucleon: 1378(11)(6) mb
- Measured sigma_CC for 48Ca on 12C at 300 MeV/nucleon: 1259(14)(16) mb
- Measured sigma_CC for 51Ca on 12C at 271 MeV/nucleon: 1319(33)(28) mb
- The proposed Glauber(CE) model reproduces experimental sigma_CC values on 12C at around 300 MeV/nucleon for nuclides from C to Fe isotopes with approximately 1% accuracy
- The wedge-shaped carbon target had a central-point thickness of 1.803(3) g/cm^2
- The average mean energy of the 42-51Ca data was approximately 280 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous charge-changing cross section measurements for calcium isotopes by Yamaguchi et al. (2013, 2014) into the neutron-rich region, revealing a decreasing trend with neutron number that differs from the flat A-dependence seen in light-mass nuclei. The proposed model resolves a long-standing discrepancy between experimental sigma_CC data and Glauber-model calculations that had been observed for medium-mass nuclei, and enables systematic extraction of point-proton radii from charge-changing cross sections without empirical scaling factors.
