# Magnetic moments of the octet, decuplet, low-lying charm, and low-lying bottom baryons in a nuclear medium

> We study the magnetic moments of the octet, decuplet, low-lying charm, and low-lying bottom baryons with nonzero light quarks in symmetric nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which satisfies the constraint for the allowed maximum change (swelling) of the in-medium nucleon size derived from the $y$-scaling data for $^3$He$(e,e')$ and $^{56}$Fe$(e,e')$. The present QMC model also satisfies the expected allowed maximum enhancement of the nucleon magnetic moments in nuclear matter. Moreover, it has been proven that the calculated in-medium to free proton electromagnetic form factor (EMFF) ratios calculated within the QMC model, reproduce well the proton EMFF super ratio extracted from $^4{\rm He}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})^3{\rm H}$ at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab). The medium modifications of the magnetic moments are estimated by evaluating the in-medium to free space baryon magnetic moment ratios to compensate the MIT bag deficiency to describe the free space octet baryon magnetic moments, where ratios are often measured directly in experiments even without knowing the absolute values, such as the free and bound proton electromagnetic form factors, as well as the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect to extract the structure function $F_2$ ratio of the bound to free nucleons by the corresponding cross section ratio. We also present the results calculated with the different current quark mass values for the strange and bottom quarks to see the possible impact. Furthermore, for a practical use, we give the explicit density dependent parametrizations for the vector potentials of the baryons and light-$(u, d)$ quarks, as well as for the effective masses of the baryons treated in this study, and of the mesons, $ω,ρ,K,K^*,η,η',D,D^*,B$, and $B^*$.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the in-medium magnetic moments of octet, decuplet, low-lying charm, and low-lying bottom baryons with nonzero light quarks in symmetric nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, but the results are presented as in-medium to free space ratios rather than absolute values to compensate for the MIT bag model's known deficiency in reproducing free space octet baryon magnetic moments.

## Method

The QMC model was applied with self-consistent exchange of Lorentz-scalar-isoscalar sigma, Lorentz-vector-isoscalar omega, and Lorentz-vector-isovector rho meson fields coupled directly to confined, relativistically moving light u and d quarks in the MIT bag model framework. The in-medium modifications of magnetic moments were estimated by evaluating the in-medium to free space baryon magnetic moment ratios, using the same light-quark-meson coupling constants constrained by nuclear matter saturation properties. The model satisfies the constraint for the allowed maximum change (swelling) of the in-medium nucleon size derived from y-scaling data for 3He(e,e') and 56Fe(e,e'), and reproduces the proton electromagnetic form factor super ratio extracted from 4He(e,e'p)3H at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key numbers

- The allowed maximum increase of the bound nucleon size from y-scaling data is 3-6% in 3He and 2-3% in 56Fe, with an upper limit of 3.6% in 3He from McKeown's analysis
- Using average baryon density estimates from the QMC model, <rho_B(3He)> ~ 0.35 rho_0 and <rho_B(56Fe)> ~ 0.71 rho_0, the allowed maximum swelling at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 is 8.5-17.1% from 3He data and 4.2-8.5% from 56Fe data
- The QMC model gives nuclear incompressibility K ~ 280 MeV with free space inputs m_q = 5 MeV and nucleon bag radius 0.8 fm
- The meson cloud contributions for total medium modifications of electromagnetic form factors are at the order of 0.2-0.3% for total modifications of about 10%

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Tsushima, 2018) on strong interaction properties of octet, low-lying charm, and low-lying bottom baryons in symmetric nuclear matter, now including decuplet baryons and magnetic moment modifications. It is the first study to estimate in-medium magnetic moments of low-lying charm and bottom baryons with nonzero light quarks, building on prior studies of in-medium octet baryon magnetic moments (Ryu, 2008) and decuplet baryon magnetic moments (Singh, 2020). The results have potential impacts on studies of neutron star and magnetar structure, particularly given renewed interest in Delta baryon properties in nuclear medium (Chen, 2007; Kolomeitsev, 2016; Reichert, 2019; Motta, 2019; Li, 2020; Thapa, 2021; Dexheimer, 2021).
