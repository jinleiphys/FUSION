# Density Fluctuations as Signature of a Non--Equilibrium First Order Phase Transition

> We show that in the presence of spinodal instabilities which develop at a first order phase transition, the fluctuations of conserved charges can be as strong as those at the critical end point (CEP). In particular, the net baryon number susceptibility diverges as the system crosses the isothermal spinodal lines. This indicates that charge density fluctuations can be used not only to probe the CEP but also the non--equilibrium first order chiral phase transition in heavy ion collisions.

# Density Fluctuations as Signature of a Non-Equilibrium First Order Phase Transition

## Key claim
The authors show that net baryon number susceptibility diverges at the isothermal spinodal lines of a first-order phase transition, producing fluctuations as strong as those at the critical end point (CEP). This holds specifically for non-equilibrium systems where spinodal instabilities develop, not for equilibrium first-order transitions where density fluctuations are suppressed.

## Method
The authors calculate the net quark number susceptibility as a function of quark number density at fixed temperature T=50 MeV using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean field approximation. They analyze the thermodynamic relation connecting the pressure derivative with respect to volume to the susceptibility, demonstrating that vanishing of the pressure derivative at the isothermal spinodal lines forces the susceptibility to diverge and change sign.

## Key numbers
- At T=50 MeV, the net quark number susceptibility diverges at the isothermal spinodal lines and becomes negative in the region between them
- The critical exponent at the isothermal spinodal line is γ=1/2, with χ_q ∼ (μ−μ_c)^(−γ)
- The critical exponent at the CEP is γ=2/3, so the two spinodal singularities join at the CEP to yield a stronger divergence

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study of spinodal instabilities (Sasaki, Friman, Redlich, 2007) and contrasts with equilibrium treatments where density fluctuations are suppressed along a first-order transition. The results indicate that large baryon and electric charge fluctuations in heavy ion collisions can signal not only the critical end point but also a non-equilibrium first-order chiral phase transition. The negative specific heat predicted here has been reported as experimental evidence for anomalous behavior in low-energy nuclear collisions (experiment, 2008), following theoretical arguments by Chomaz (2004) for the liquid-gas phase transition.
