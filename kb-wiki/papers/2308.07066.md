# Dibaryons and where to find them

> In recent years there has been tremendous progress in the investigation of bound systems of quarks with multiplicities beyond the more usual two- and three-quark systems. Experimental and theoretical progress has been made in the four-, five- and even six-quark sectors. In this paper, we review the possible lightest six-quark states using a simple ansatz based on SU(3) symmetry and evaluate the most promising decay branches. The work will be useful to help focus future experimental searches in this six-quark sector.

## Key claim

The paper provides estimates of masses, widths, and decay branches for the strange-quark-containing partners of the \(d^*(2380)\) hexaquark within an SU(3) antidecuplet, under both a genuine hexaquark and a molecular interpretation, to guide future experimental searches; the authors note that the actual mass of each member is expected to lie between the two limiting cases.

## Method

The authors use a simple SU(3) symmetry ansatz to construct the antidecuplet of six-quark dibaryon states built on the \(d^*(2380)\). They calculate masses for the genuine hexaquark case using constituent quark masses and hyperfine splitting, and for the molecular case using a pion-exchange binding model with coupling strengths scaled by strangeness content. Decay widths into \(8\oplus8\) and \(10\oplus10\) baryon channels are computed with energy-dependent Breit-Wigner forms, Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factors, and a monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda = 0.16\) GeV/c fixed to reproduce the measured \(d^*(2380)\to\Delta\Delta\) width.

## Key numbers

* \(d^*(2380)\) mass: \(M_{d^*}=2380\) MeV, vacuum width \(\Gamma = 70\) MeV, quantum numbers \(I(J^P)=0(3^+)\).
* \(d^*(2380)\to pn\) partial width: \(\Gamma(d^*\to pn) = 8\) MeV (used to fix the \(g_8\) coupling constant).
* \(d^*(2380)\to\Delta\Delta\) partial width: \(\Gamma(d^*\to\Delta\Delta)=62\) MeV.
* Genuine hexaquark mass predictions: \(d_s\) at 2474 MeV, \(d_{ss}\) at 2573 MeV, \(d_{sss}\) at 2677 MeV; corresponding bindings relative to decuplet-decuplet pole: 141 MeV, 193 MeV, 234 MeV.
* Molecular mass predictions: \(d_s\) at 2578 MeV (binding 39 MeV), \(d_{ss}\) at 2753 MeV (binding 13 MeV), \(d_{sss}\) at 2909 MeV (binding 2 MeV).
* Constituent quark masses used: \(M_n=363\) MeV (light), \(M_s=538\) MeV (strange); hyperfine splitting parameter \(K/M_n^2=50\) MeV.
* Form factor cutoff parameter: \(\Lambda = 0.16\) GeV/c; Blatt-Weisskopf barrier radius \(R=6.3\) GeV/c; decuplet decay parameters \(\gamma = 0.74, 0.28, 0.13\) for \(\Delta,\Sigma^*,\Xi^*\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) multiplet approach originally applied by Dyson and Xuong (1964) to non-strange dibaryons, now focusing on the strange-quark sector of the \(d^*(2380)\) antidecuplet. It contrasts with the deuteron antidecuplet and the NN-FSI 27-plet, where states are predominantly molecular and mostly unbound, whereas the \(d^*(2380)\) exhibits large binding suggestive of a significant genuine hexaquark component. The predictions enable experimental searches at facilities such as KLF and JPARC, and the results can help constrain ChPT coupling constants and improve understanding of the nuclear equation of state with strangeness for neutron star physics.
