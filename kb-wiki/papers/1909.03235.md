# Impact of Uncertainties in Astrophysical Reaction Rates on Nucleosynthesis in the $νp$ Process

> The $νp$ process appears in proton-rich, hot matter which is expanding in a neutrino wind and may be realised in explosive environments such as core-collapse supernovae or in outflows from accretion disks. The impact of uncertainties in nuclear reaction cross sections on the finally produced abundances has been studied by applying Monte Carlo variation of all astrophysical reaction rates in a large reaction network. As the detailed astrophysical conditions of the $νp$ process still are unknown, a parameter study was performed, with 23 trajectories covering a large range of entropies and $Y_\mathrm{e}$. The resulting abundance uncertainties are given for each trajectory. The $νp$ process has been speculated to contribute to the light $p$ nuclides but it was not possible so far to reproduce the solar isotope ratios. It is found that it is possible to reproduce the solar $^{92}$Mo/$^{94}$Mo abundance ratio within nuclear uncertainties, even within a single trajectory. The solar values of the abundances in the Kr-Sr region relative to the Mo region, however, cannot be achieved within a single trajectory. They may still be obtained from a weighted superposition of different trajectories, though, depending on the actual conditions in the production site. For a stronger constraint of the required conditions, it would be necessary to reduce the uncertainties in the 3$α$ and $^{56}$Ni(n,p)$^{56}$Co rates at temperatures $T>3$ GK.

## Key claim
The solar \(^{92}\)Mo/\(^{94}\)Mo abundance ratio can be reproduced within nuclear uncertainties even in a single trajectory of the \(\nu p\) process, but the solar ratios of \(^{78}\)Kr/\(^{94}\)Mo and \(^{84}\)Sr/\(^{94}\)Mo cannot be simultaneously achieved in a single trajectory; a weighted superposition of different trajectories may still yield all solar ratios, depending on the actual astrophysical conditions.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo variation of all astrophysical reaction rates for isotopes of Fe and heavier elements in a large reaction network using the PizBuin framework, applied to 23 post-processed trajectories covering electron abundances \(0.55 \leq Y_\mathrm{e} \leq 0.725\) and entropies \(11.4 \leq S \leq 184\) \(k_B\) baryon\(^{-1}\) at freeze-out from nuclear statistical equilibrium at 7 GK. Each reaction rate was assigned a temperature-dependent uncertainty factor, and 10,000 Monte Carlo runs were used to generate distributions of final abundances. Key rates dominating the uncertainty in a given nuclide's abundance were identified using the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient, with a threshold of \(|r| \geq 0.65\).

## Key numbers
- 23 trajectories were studied, covering \(Y_\mathrm{e}\) from 0.55 to 0.725 and entropy \(S\) from 11.4 to 184 \(k_B\) baryon\(^{-1}\) at 7 GK.
- The solar \(^{92}\)Mo/\(^{94}\)Mo abundance ratio (1.6) is reproduced within uncertainties in all trajectories when using the 3\(\alpha\) rate of Fynbo et al.; for trajectory #16, the median ratio \(Y_{50}\) is 0.8 with an upper bound of 2.8 and lower bound of 0.7.
- For trajectory #18 using the Fynbo 3\(\alpha\) rate, the \(^{78}\)Kr/\(^{94}\)Mo ratio has \(Y_{50}=0.2\) (solar: 0.82) with upper bound 5.1 and lower bound 0.6; the \(^{84}\)Sr/\(^{94}\)Mo ratio has \(Y_{50}=0.3\) (solar: 0.54) with upper bound 3.6 and lower bound 0.5.
- Most final abundance uncertainties are within a factor of 2–3, except for the strong effects of the 3\(\alpha\) and \(^{56}\)Ni(n,p)\(^{56}\)Co rates.
- The reaction \(^{92}\)Mo(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{93}\)Tc was identified as a key rate inversely correlated with the \(^{92}\)Mo/\(^{94}\)Mo ratio.

## Context
This work extends the Monte Carlo uncertainty framework PizBuin, previously applied to the \(\gamma\) process in core-collapse supernovae (Rauscher 2016), the \(p\) process in type Ia supernovae (Travaglio 2015), the weak \(s\) process in massive stars (Nishimura 2017), and the main \(s\) process in AGB stars (Cescutti 2018). It addresses the long-standing difficulty in reproducing solar \(p\)-nuclide ratios from the \(\nu p\) process, as noted by Wanajo (2006) and Xing (2018). The results enable future studies to constrain the required astrophysical conditions by reducing uncertainties in the 3\(\alpha\) rate and the \(^{56}\)Ni(n,p)\(^{56}\)Co rate at temperatures above 3 GK, and by considering superpositions of trajectories from realistic hydrodynamic simulations.
