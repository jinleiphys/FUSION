# Coherence features of the spin-aligned neutron-proton pair coupling scheme

> The seniority scheme has been shown to be extremely useful for the classification of nuclear states in semi-magic nuclei. The neutron-proton ($np$) correlation breaks the seniority symmetry in a major way. As a result, the corresponding wave function is a mixture of many components with different seniority quantum numbers. In this contribution we show that the $np$ interaction may favor a new kind of coupling in $N=Z$ nuclei, i.e., the so-called isoscalar spin-aligned $np$ pair mode. Shell model calculations reveal that the ground and low-lying yrast states of the $N = Z$ nuclei $^{92}$Pd and $^{96}$Cd may mainly be built upon such spin-aligned $np$ pairs each carrying the maximum angular momentum $J = 9$ allowed by the shell $0g_{9/2}$ which is dominant in this nuclear region.

# Coherence features of the spin-aligned neutron-proton pair coupling scheme

## Key claim
The neutron-proton interaction may favor a new kind of coupling in N=Z nuclei, the isoscalar spin-aligned np pair mode, where the ground and low-lying yrast states of 92Pd and 96Cd may mainly be built upon such spin-aligned np pairs each carrying the maximum angular momentum J=9 allowed by the shell 0g9/2. The claim is hedged by the use of "may" and the qualification that this coupling scheme competes with and can be overcome by isovector aligned pair configurations in certain states, such as the 8+1 state in 96Cd.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed in the 0g9/2 single-j shell for the N=Z nuclei 96Cd (two np hole pairs) and 92Pd (four np hole pairs), using the standard representation of tensor products of neutron and proton degrees of freedom. The wave functions were re-expressed in terms of np pair components using two-particle coefficients of fractional parentage and overlap matrices that account for the Pauli principle. The controlling parameter δ was introduced to vary the strength of the (0g9/2)2_9, T=0, J=9 matrix element V9 to explore the transition from seniority coupling to aligned np pair coupling.

## Key numbers
- The ground state wave function of 96Cd contains a 0.76 amplitude for the |[π2(0)ν2(0)]I⟩ component, 0.57 for |[π2(2)ν2(2)]I⟩, 0.24 for |[π2(4)ν2(4)]I⟩, 0.13 for |[π2(6)ν2(6)]I⟩, and 0.14 for |[π2(8)ν2(8)]I⟩.
- The spin-aligned np pair state |[νπ(9)νπ(9)]0⟩ for j=9/2 has amplitudes: 0.62 for |[π2(0)ν2(0)]I⟩, 0.75 for |[π2(2)ν2(2)]I⟩, 0.23 for |[π2(4)ν2(4)]I⟩, 0.02 for |[π2(6)ν2(6)]I⟩, and 0.00 for |[π2(8)ν2(8)]I⟩.
- The aligned np pairing coupling overcomes the normal monopole pair as the dominant component when V9/V0 ~ 0.5 in the schematic Hamiltonian for 96Cd.
- The energy of the predicted 16+ spin trap in 96Cd is E16 = (8/17)V7 + 3V8 + (43/17)V9.
- The maximum spin from spin-aligned np coupling for two pairs is 2(2j-1), which for j=9/2 gives a maximum of 16.

## Context
This work extends the seniority coupling scheme, which has been extremely useful for semi-magic nuclei but is broken by neutron-proton correlations, as discussed by Talmi (1993). It builds on the spin-aligned np pair coupling proposed by Cederwall et al. (2010) and Qi et al. (2011), and compares with the stretch scheme proposed by Danos and Greiner (1967). The results enable future generalization of the np pair coupling to multi-shell systems and studies of its effects on nuclear binding energy and radioactive decay properties.
