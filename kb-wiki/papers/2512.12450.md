# Relativistic Dispersion Spectra across Lorentz boosted frames: Spurious modes and the enigma of causality

> The analysis of excitation spectra in gradient-expanded relativistic fluid theories frequently leads to pathologies under Lorentz boosts. However, extracting the dispersion modes in a Lorentz boosted inertial frame can be nontrivial. Motivated by this problem, we develop a general framework for deriving the linearized dispersion spectra in Lorentz boosted frames using only information from the local rest-frame dispersion structure, particularly its mode-expansion coefficients. We observe that, under a Lorentz transformation from the local rest frame, additional boosted solutions may appear that conflict with the causality of the theory; we refer to these as "spurious modes." The key developments presented here are: (i) a convenient method for obtaining dispersion spectra across inertial frames, bypassing the traditional procedure of solving the boosted polynomial, and (ii) the establishment of a direct connection between mode conservation and the causality of a theory, supported by a detailed proof.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general framework for deriving linearized dispersion spectra in Lorentz boosted frames using only the local rest-frame dispersion coefficients, and shows that additional "spurious modes" appearing under Lorentz transformation are directly linked to causality violation in the theory.

## Method

The authors derive a parametric solution relating the dispersion modes in a Lorentz boosted frame to those in the local rest frame (LRF) via the transformation equations ω̃ = γ(ω + vk) and k̃ = γ(k + vω). They show that the boosted frame modes can be obtained by eliminating a parameter p from the relations k = γ{p − vW(p)} and ω = γ{W(p) − vp}, where W(p) is the LRF mode. For non-spurious modes, they develop perturbative expansions around k=0 to obtain the boosted mode coefficients from the LRF expansion coefficients, while spurious modes are identified as those arising from multiple solutions of the parametric equations that diverge in the v→0 limit.

## Key numbers

* The first three expansion coefficients for the parametric solution p(k,v) are: P₁ = 1/(1−vW⁽¹⁾), P₂ = vW⁽²⁾/{2(1−vW⁽¹⁾)³}, P₃ = (vW⁽²⁾)²/{2(1−vW⁽¹⁾)⁵} + vW⁽³⁾/{6(1−vW⁽¹⁾)⁴}, where W⁽ⁿ⁾ are derivatives of the LRF mode evaluated at p=kₛ(v)
* The first three coefficients for the expansion of kₛ(v) in powers of v are: B₁ = [W]_{p=0}, B₂ = B₁[dW/dp]_{p=0}, B₃ = B₂[dW/dp]_{p=0} + (1/2)B₁²[d²W/dp²]_{p=0}
* A spurious mode is identified when lim_{v→0} kₛ(v) ≠ 0, leading to divergent ω in the v→0 limit

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dispersion spectra in relativistic fluid theories beyond the local rest frame, addressing the problem that pathologies in higher-derivative theories may not be manifest in the LRF but become apparent under Lorentz boosts (Hiscock 1985, Denicol 2008, Pu 2009, Mitra 2021). The framework complements recent work by Hoult (2023) which showed that the number of modes in a causal relativistic theory must match in the small-k and large-k limits, with extra roots at large momentum indicating acausality. The results enable a systematic method to diagnose causality violations in effective theories including gauge theory plasmas, strongly coupled systems, and heavy quark effective theory, bypassing the traditional procedure of directly solving the boosted polynomial.
