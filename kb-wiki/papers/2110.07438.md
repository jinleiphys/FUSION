# The $Z_{cs}(3985)$ structure, if a triangle/kinematic singularity, would disappear when heated in Heavy Ion Collisons

> Triangle and other kinematic singularities are very sensitive to the precise masses and widths of the intervening particles. Therefore, the effect that the heavy-ion collision medium can have on those masses and widths, as captured by finite-temperature field theory and reported in the literature, may erase the singularity from the spectrum if the effect is large enough and the loop completes before the hot gas freezes out. A very timely example is provided by the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ structure recently reported by BES-III in a $(D_s^-D^{*0}+D_s^{*-}D^0)$ spectrum recoiling against a $K^+$. If a new hadron, this would be a clear exotic $c\bar{c}s\bar{u}$ tetraquark candidate, the first of a charmonium-like family with strangeness: to accept this, alternative explanations first need to be tested and discarded. As shown in figure 1, the mass spectrum recoiling against the kaon is near the $m_{D_s^{*-}}+m_{D^0}$ threshold, whereas the production cross-section seems to peak around that for $\sqrt{s(e^-e^+)}=m_{D_{s2}^{*+}} +m_{D_s^{*-}} $. Therefore, this structure may well be caused by a triangle with the three charmed mesons $D_{s2}^{*+} / D_s^{*-} / D^0$ running in the loop, with the amplitude enhanced at the two thresholds. If so, the structure would be erased from the spectrum in Heavy Ion Collisions, whereas a hadron would continue to exist although with a mass decreased by a few percent as computed by other authors.

# Wiki Page: The Z_cs(3985) Structure and Triangle Singularities in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The Z_cs(3985) structure observed by BES-III may be explained by a triangle singularity involving D_s2*+, D_s*-, and D^0 mesons, rather than being a genuine exotic tetraquark hadron. If this kinematic explanation is correct, the structure would be erased from the spectrum in heavy ion collisions at sufficiently high temperature, whereas a true hadron would persist (though with a mass decreased by a few percent).

## Method

The authors evaluate a triangle loop diagram using finite-temperature field theory for an infinite, stationary hadron gas, incorporating Bose-Einstein factors and in-medium masses and widths of the three charmed mesons (D_s2*+, D_s*-, D^0) running in the loop. They estimate the temperature-dependent masses and widths based on available literature, with values tabulated at T=0, T=100 MeV, and T=150 MeV. The triangle completion time is computed from the decay width of the D_s2*+ meson.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, the D_s2*+ mass is 2568 ± 1 MeV with width 6.9 ± 0.7 MeV; at T=150 MeV, its mass decreases to 2500 MeV and width increases to 15 MeV
- At T=0, the D_s*- mass is 2112.2 ± 0.4 MeV; at T=150 MeV, it decreases to 2087 MeV with width 10 MeV
- At T=0, the D^0 mass is 1864.84 ± 0.05 MeV; at T=150 MeV, it decreases to 1776 MeV with width 2 MeV
- The triangle completion time is computed as tau = 10 fm, based on a decay width Gamma_1 ~ 7-15 MeV (corresponding to 14-28 fm^-1)
- The production cross-section peak for the Z_cs(3985) occurs at sqrt(s) = 4.68 GeV in e^-e^+ collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method (Abreu 2020, Abreu 2021) for distinguishing triangle singularities from genuine hadrons using medium effects, previously applied to the Z_c(3900) structure in Y(4260) decays. The Z_cs(3985) was reported by BES-III (2020) and has been debated as a possible tetraquark (Garcilazo 2021), a molecular state (Sun 2020), or a threshold/triangle production effect (Ikeno 2020). The paper enables future heavy-ion collision experiments to test whether the structure is a kinematic artifact or a genuine exotic hadron, with the caveat that the triangle must complete before freeze-out (estimated at 10 fm).
