# Impact of a thermal medium on $D$ mesons and their chiral partners

> We study $D$ and $D_s$ mesons at finite temperature using an effective field theory based on chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries within the imaginary-time formalism. Interactions with the light degrees of freedom are unitarized via a Bethe-Salpeter approach, and the $D$ and $D_s$ self-energies are calculated self-consistently. We generate dynamically the $D^*_0(2300)$ and $D_s(2317)$ states, and study their possible identification as the chiral partners of the $D$ and $D_s$ ground states, respectively. We show the evolution of their masses and decay widths as functions of temperature, and provide an analysis of the chiral-symmetry restoration in the heavy-flavor sector below the transition temperature. In particular, we analyse the very special case of the $D$-meson, for which the chiral partner is associated to the double-pole structure of the $D^*_0(2300)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the chiral partners of the $D$ and $D_s$ mesons (the dynamically generated $D^*_0(2300)$ and $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ states) do **not** exhibit a clear tendency toward mass degeneracy with their ground states up to 150 MeV, but the authors caution that this temperature is still below the chiral transition temperature $T_\chi$ and that degeneracy might occur only at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory based on $SU(3)$ chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries at next-to-leading order (NLO), using low-energy constants fitted to lattice-QCD data from Guo (2018). They compute unitarized scattering amplitudes for $D$ and $D_s$ mesons with light pseudoscalar mesons via a Bethe-Salpeter equation in the imaginary-time formalism, and calculate the $D$ and $D_s$ self-energies self-consistently by iterating the dressed propagator and the $T$-matrix. The spectral functions of the ground states and the dynamically generated resonances are extracted at finite temperature up to 150 MeV, and masses and widths are obtained from fits to the spectral shapes (Breit-Wigner-Fano for the lower pole of the $D^*_0(2300)$ and the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, Flatte-type for the higher pole of the $D^*_0(2300)$).

## Key numbers

* The $D$ meson ground state mass decreases by $\Delta m_D \sim 40$ MeV at $T = 150$ MeV.
* The $D_s$ meson ground state mass decreases by $\sim 20$ MeV at $T = 150$ MeV.
* The $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ mass decreases by $\sim 19$ MeV at $T = 150$ MeV.
* The $D$ meson acquires a width of $\sim 70$ MeV at $T = 150$ MeV.
* At $T=0$, the lower pole of the $D^*_0(2300)$ is found at $M_R = 2081.9$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma_R/2 = 86.0$ MeV, coupling most strongly to $D\pi$ ($|g_{D\pi}| = 8.9$ GeV).
* At $T=0$, the higher pole of the $D^*_0(2300)$ is found at $M_R = 2521.2$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma_R/2 = 121.7$ MeV, coupling most strongly to $D_s\bar{K}$ ($|g_{D_s\bar{K}}| = 14.0$ GeV).
* At $T=0$, the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ is a bound state at $M_R = 2252.5$ MeV with zero width, coupling to $DK$ ($|g_{DK}| = 13.3$ GeV) and $D_s\eta$ ($|g_{D_s\eta}| = 9.2$ GeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration in the heavy-flavor sector, notably the $SU(4)$ chiral effective approach of Cleven (2017) and the non-unitarized chiral perturbation theory calculation of Sasaki (2014), by using a more complete NLO Lagrangian based on both chiral and heavy-quark symmetries with self-consistent unitarization. The results enable future studies of heavy-flavor transport coefficients and charm production in heavy-ion collisions, as the medium-modified $D$ and $D_s$ properties are needed to characterize the hadronic phase below the deconfinement temperature.
