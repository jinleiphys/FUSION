# The Smile of Cheshire Cat At High Density

> Baryons in finite nuclei, nuclear matter and dense compact-star matter are described in terms of Cheshire Cat for QCD. A potential conceptual link, admittedly short in mathematical rigor, between their manifestations is made by what's called Cheshire Cat Principle. Put in terms taken ``dual" to QCD variables, going to very high density exposes quantum Hall droplets -- or pancakes -- at which the dilaton-limit fixed point with $g_A\to 1$, $f_π\to f_χ$ -- where $f_π$ and $f_χ$ are respectively the pion and dilaton decay constants -- and the baryon parity-doubling are reached. This scenario suggests a thus-far totally unexplored structure of dual baryonic matter at high density which does neither require nor rule out (rapid) first-order phase transitions from hadrons to quarks in the core of compact stars on the verge of gravitational collapse.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at very high density, baryonic matter can be described by a "Cheshire Cat Principle" (CCP) where the confinement radius becomes physically irrelevant, and that this framework leads to a novel structure of dual baryonic matter involving quantum Hall droplets at the dilaton-limit fixed point, without requiring or ruling out first-order phase transitions from hadrons to quarks in compact stars. The author explicitly acknowledges that the conceptual link is "admittedly short in mathematical rigor."

## Method

The author reviews and extends the Cheshire Cat Model (CCM), originally proposed by Nadkarni, Nielsen and Zahed (1985), which treats baryons as topological solitons (skyrmions) with a bag boundary that is rendered physically irrelevant by quantum anomalies. The analysis combines the chiral bag model with hidden local symmetry bosons (rho, omega mesons) and the flavor-singlet eta-prime meson, applying the framework to dense baryonic matter via skyrmion-crystal simulations on a lattice. The author then extends the CCP to describe a 1+2 dimensional chiral bag surrounding a quantum Hall droplet, where the eta-prime meson plays a central role through the U_A(1) anomaly.

## Key numbers

* The half-skyrmion phase sets in at a lattice size L_{1/2} approximately 2.9 fm, corresponding to a density n_{1/2} greater than approximately 3 n_0 (where n_0 is nuclear matter saturation density).
* In the chiral bag calculation of the flavor-singlet axial coupling constant g_A^(0) of the proton, results are plotted for bag radii in the range 0 < R < 1.5 fm, with the sum of all contributions falling within the shaded experimental range from the EMC experiment at CERN.
* The magic chiral angle for baryon charge leakage is theta = pi/2, at which exactly half of the baryon charge B = 1/2 leaks out of the bag and is picked up by a half-skyrmion.

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat Principle originally developed by Nadkarni, Nielsen and Zahed (1985) and the chiral bag model of Rho, Brown and collaborators, applying it to the regime of superdense neutron matter relevant to compact stars observed in gravitational-wave measurements. The paper connects to the earlier "Little Bag" versus "MIT Big Bag" debate from the 1970s, and builds on the skyrmion crystal simulations of dense matter by Park, Kim, Lee, Ma and Rho (PKLMR) and the precocious pseudo-conformality observed in those simulations. The quantum Hall droplet description follows from the work of Komargodski (ZOHAR) on N_f=1 chiral effective theory and domain walls, and the Callan-Harvey anomaly out-flow mechanism. This scenario enables a new approach to describing baryonic matter at densities near the gravitational collapse threshold of compact stars, without requiring a first-order phase transition to quark matter.
