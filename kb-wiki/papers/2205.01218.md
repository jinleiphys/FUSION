# Detailed Study of Quark-Hadron Duality in Spin Structure Functions of the Proton and Neutron

> In this paper, we present for the first time comprehensive and detailed results on the correspondence between the extrapolated deep inelastic structure function $g_1$ of both the proton and the neutron with the same quantity measured in the nucleon resonance region. We use a QCD parameterization of the world data on DIS spin structure functions, extrapolated into the nucleon resonance region and averaged over various intervals in the scaling variable $x$. We compare the results with the large data set collected in the quark-hadron transition region by the CLAS collaboration, averaged over the same intervals. We present this comparison as a function of the momentum transfer $Q^2$. We find that, depending on the averaging interval and the minimum momentum transfer chosen, a clear transition to quark-hadron duality can be observed in both nucleon species. Furthermore, we show, for the first time, the scaling behavior of $g_1$ measured in the resonance region at sufficiently high momentum transfer. Our results can be used to quantify the deviations from the applicability of pQCD for data taken at moderate energies, and help with extraction of quark distribution functions from such data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quark-hadron duality for the spin structure function \(g_1\) holds for both the proton and neutron only under specific conditions: global duality (integrating over the entire resonance region) requires inclusion of the elastic peak, while local duality fails for the Delta resonance region but works well for the second and third resonance regions.

## Method

The authors compared truncated first moments of the spin structure function \(g_1\) measured by the CLAS EG1b experiment in the nucleon resonance region (W from 1.072 to 2 GeV) with extrapolated predictions from the JAM15 QCD global analysis of deep inelastic scattering data. They sorted experimental data into 11 bins in \(Q^2\) from 0.92 to 6.45 GeV\(^2\) and six different ranges in final-state mass \(W\), then integrated over the corresponding \(x\) ranges. Additionally, they studied the approach to scaling by averaging \(g_1\) over fixed \(x\)-bins of width \(\Delta x = 0.05\) as a function of \(Q^2\).

## Key numbers

- The \(Q^2\) bins span from 0.92 to 6.45 GeV\(^2\), with central values ranging from 1.00 to 5.90 GeV\(^2\)
- The six \(W\) ranges are: 1.072-1.38 GeV (Delta resonance), 1.38-1.58 GeV, 1.58-1.82 GeV, 1.82-2 GeV, 1.072-2 GeV (full resonance region), and 0.939-2 GeV (including elastic peak)
- For the proton, global duality including the elastic peak shows agreement between data and PDF prediction starting around \(Q^2 = 1.4\) GeV\(^2\)
- For the Delta resonance region (1.072-1.38 GeV), convergence of resonance data toward the PDF extrapolation begins around \(Q^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\) for the neutron and later for the proton
- The \(x\)-bins for the scaling study have width \(\Delta x = 0.05\)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Bloom-Gilman duality from unpolarized structure functions (Bloom and Gilman, 1970; Niculescu et al., 2000; Christy et al., 2007) to polarized spin structure functions, using the most comprehensive data set available from the CLAS EG1b experiment (Fersch et al., 2017). It includes for the first time unfolded neutron spin structure functions, and uses the JAM15 global analysis (Sato et al., 2016) which has the largest kinematic coverage in \(x\) among existing fits. The results enable quantification of deviations from perturbative QCD applicability at moderate energies and can help constrain polarized parton distribution functions at high \(x\) using resonance region data.
