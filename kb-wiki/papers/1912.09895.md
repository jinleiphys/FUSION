# Constraining resonance properties through kaon production in pion-nucleus collisions at low energies

> Hadronic interactions are crucial for the dynamical description of heavy-ion reactions at low collision energies and in the late dilute stages at high collision energies. In particular, the properties and decay channels of resonances are an essential ingredient of hadronic transport approaches. The HADES collaboration measured particle production in collisions of pions with carbon and tungsten nuclei at $E_\text{kin} = 1.7\,\text{GeV}$. Such reactions are of high interest, because they allow to probe the properties of baryonic resonances produced in a much cleaner environment than the usual nucleus-nucleus collisions. We study these reactions with two transport approaches: SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) and UrQMD (Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) which follow the same underlying concept but with different implementations. The differential spectra in rapidity and transverse momentum are used to show how model parameters, as the decay channels of high mass resonances and angular distributions of kaon elastic scattering, can be constrained. It is found that the data favor the production of more particles with lower momentum over the production of few particles with higher momentum in these decays. In addition, the shape of the rapidity distribution of the kaons strongly depends on the angular distribution of the elastic kaon-nucleon cross section.

## Key claim
The HADES pion-nucleus data at 1.7 GeV favor resonance decay chains (e.g., N*, Delta* -> Y K*) that produce kaons with lower momentum over direct two-body decays (N*, Delta* -> Y K) that yield higher-momentum kaons, but the total kaon yield remains poorly constrained across models.

## Method
The authors simulated pi- + C and pi- + W collisions at E_kin = 1.7 GeV using two hadronic transport models, SMASH (versions 1.301 and 1.6) and UrQMD (version 3.4 and a modified version). They compared the resulting K+ transverse momentum and rapidity spectra to HADES data, varying the branching ratios of heavy baryonic resonances (N*, Delta*) to YK versus YK* channels and the angular distribution of elastic kaon-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers
- Total pion-nucleus cross section for pi- + C: 180 mb (SMASH 1.6), 184 mb (SMASH 1.301), 186 mb (UrQMD 3.4)
- Total pion-nucleus cross section for pi- + W: 1432 mb (SMASH 1.6), 1530 mb (SMASH 1.301), 1310 mb (UrQMD 3.4)
- K+ multiplicity in pi- + C: 0.018 (SMASH 1.6), 0.0028 (SMASH 1.301), 0.0065 (UrQMD mod.), 0.0087 (UrQMD 3.4)
- K+ multiplicity in pi- + W: 0.022 (SMASH 1.6), 0.0051 (SMASH 1.301), 0.0066 (UrQMD mod.), 0.0089 (UrQMD 3.4)

## Context
This work extends studies of strangeness production in hadronic transport models (SMASH and UrQMD) previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at HADES energies, such as Ni+Ni, Au+Au, and Ar+KCl collisions. The pion-nucleus data from HADES (Adamczewski-Musch 2018) provide a cleaner probe of baryonic resonance properties than nucleus-nucleus collisions, because the pi N -> N*, Delta* channel dominates. The results enable future constraints on kaon-nucleon potentials and in-medium effects, and the authors plan to extend the study to K- production measured by HADES.
