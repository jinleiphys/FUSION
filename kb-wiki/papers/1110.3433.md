# Analytical formula for the Uehling potential

> The closed analytical expression for the Uehling potential is derived. The Uehling potential describes the lowest-order correction on vacuum polarisation in atomic and muon-atomic systems. We also derive the analytical formula for the interaction potential between two electrically charged point particles which includes correction to the vacuum polarisation, but has correct asymptotic behaviour at larger $r$. Our three-term analytical formula for the Uehling potential opens a new avenue in the study of the vacuum polarisation in light atomic systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed analytical expression for the Uehling potential, contradicting the widespread statement that the integral defining it "cannot be evaluated in closed form." The derivation shows that the Uehling potential can be expressed as a three-term formula involving the modified Bessel function \(K_0\) and its recursive integrals \(Ki_1\) and \(Ki_2\), and the authors also provide a combined potential that includes the Wichmann-Kroll correction to achieve correct asymptotic behavior at both short and long distances.

## Method

The authors start from the standard integral representation of the Uehling potential, Eq. (Int0), and perform a substitution \(\xi = \cosh x\) to rewrite the integral in terms of the modified Bessel function \(K_0\) and its recursive integrals \(Ki_2\) and \(Ki_4\). Using known relations for these recursive integrals (Eq. 11.2.14 from Abramowitz and Stegun), they reduce the expression to a closed three-term formula involving \(K_0\), \(Ki_1\), and \(Ki_2\). They then combine this analytical Uehling potential with the Wichmann-Kroll potential, regularized by substituting \(r \rightarrow r + \alpha\) to remove the singularity at the origin, to produce a total interaction potential \(\Phi(r)\) with correct asymptotic behavior at all distances.

## Key numbers

- The fine structure constant is given as \(\alpha^{-1} \approx 137.03599911\).
- The Compton wavelength of the electron is \(\Lambda_e = \alpha a_0 \approx 3.861592 \times 10^{-11}\) cm.
- The short-range asymptotic form of the potential \(\phi(r)\) as \(r \rightarrow 0\) includes the Euler constant \(\gamma \approx 0.5772156649\) and \(\ln \alpha \approx -4.92024365857\).
- The long-range asymptotic form of \(\phi(r)\) as \(r \rightarrow \infty\) decays as \(\frac{\alpha^{5/2}}{4 \sqrt{\pi} r^{3/2}} \exp(-\frac{2}{\alpha} r)\).
- The Wichmann-Kroll potential is given as \(W_K(r) = -\frac{2 Q^3 \alpha^{7}}{225 \pi (r + \alpha)^5}\) in atomic units.

## Context

This work extends the original derivation of the Uehling potential by Uehling (1935), which was previously thought to have no closed analytical form, as stated in textbooks by Akhiezer and Berestetskii and by Greiner. The derived analytical formula enables direct use in highly accurate computations of vacuum polarization corrections for bound state energies in few-electron atoms and ions, and the authors plan to describe the procedure of Coulomb quantization for the Uehling potential in a subsequent study.
