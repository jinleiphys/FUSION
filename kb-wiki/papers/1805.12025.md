# Baryon number probability distribution at finite temperature

> The probability distribution of the net baryon number is investigated within the functional renormalization group approach. We find that the Roberge-Weiss periodicity related to the Z(3) symmetry of the gluon fields results directly in that, states of the net baryon number $N_B=N\pm 1/3$ with $N\in\mathbb{Z}$ are prohibited, and only those of $N_B=N$ are possible. By employing the probability distribution of the net baryon number, we also compute the cumulants of the baryon number distribution, which are found to be well consistent with those obtained from the generalized susceptibilities. A question about the relation between the color confinement and the probability distribution of net baryon number is put forward.

## Key claim

The Roberge-Weiss periodicity of the thermodynamic potential, which arises from the Z(3) symmetry of the gluon fields, directly prohibits states with net baryon number N_B = N ± 1/3 (N integer) and allows only integer net baryon number states, providing an indication of color confinement from the probability distribution perspective.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) low energy effective model within the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach, using the local potential approximation (LPA). They computed the thermodynamic potential with an imaginary chemical potential μ_q = iθT to obtain the canonical partition function via a contour integral, from which the net baryon number probability distribution was derived. The glue dynamics were incorporated through a parameterized Polyakov loop potential, and the flow equations for the effective potential were solved using a Taylor expansion around the physical point up to fifth order.

## Key numbers

- The Roberge-Weiss critical temperature is found to be T_c^RW = 208 MeV in these calculations.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition is T_c^Poly = 178 MeV at vanishing chemical potential.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral phase transition is T_c^χ = 194 MeV at vanishing chemical potential.
- The initial UV scale for the FRG flow is Λ = 700 MeV.
- Hadronic observables used for parameter fixing: m_π = 135.9 MeV, m_σ = 502.2 MeV, f_π = 92.0 MeV, and constituent quark mass m_q = 299.1 MeV.
- The scaling constant for the glue potential is α = 0.57 for two-flavor QCD, with T_c^glue = 250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon number fluctuations within the FRG approach (Fu 2015, Fu 2016, Fu 2018) and the probability distribution formalism developed by Morita (2013, 2014). The results demonstrate that the probability distribution approach, which uses only imaginary chemical potential, yields cumulants consistent with the generalized susceptibility method that requires real chemical potential and approximate treatment of the Polyakov loop. The finding that the Roberge-Weiss periodicity enforces integer baryon number states raises a question about the relation between color confinement and the baryon number probability distribution, and the calculated probability distributions can serve as input for transport simulations in heavy-ion collisions.
