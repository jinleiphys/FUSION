# On Charm Production near the Phase Boundary

> We discuss aspects of the statistical hadronization model for the production of mesons with open and hidden charm in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. Emphasis is placed on what can be inferred from the dependence of the yield of charmonia on the number of participants in the collisions.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model can quantitatively explain J/ψ yields in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy, but the agreement depends on the poorly known direct open charm cross section; the centrality dependence of the J/ψ yield is directly related to the magnitude of the open charm cross section relative to the thermal open charm yield.

## Method

The authors apply a statistical hadronization model where all c\(\bar{\rm c}\) pairs are produced in hard collisions (thermal production neglected), and the hadronization of charm quarks into charmonia and open charm mesons and baryons is described using thermal model parameters (T=168 MeV, μB=266 MeV) from fits to other hadron yields. Because the number of charm pairs per collision is less than one, the system is treated within canonical thermodynamics using modified Bessel functions, with a charm enhancement factor gc determined by requiring charm conservation. The model is applied to published NA50 data on J/ψ production as a function of the number of participating nucleons (Npart) in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy.

## Key numbers

- Thermal model parameters from fits to hadron yields: T = 168 MeV, μB = 266 MeV.
- For Npart = 100: N(J/ψ)/N(h⁻) = 1.1 × 10⁻⁶, leading to N(J/ψ)/Npart = 1.83 × 10⁻⁶.
- For Npart = 350: direct open charm yield N(c\(\bar{\rm c}\))^direct = 0.144, thermal open charm yield N(oc)^therm = 0.55, gc = 1.43, N(J/ψ)/Npart = 1.08 × 10⁻⁶, and α = -0.61.
- Increasing the open charm cross section by 50% gives gc = 1.78 and α = -0.60.
- For RHIC (central Au+Au, Npart = 350): N(c\(\bar{\rm c}\))^direct ≈ 1.5 per unit rapidity, dN(ch)/dη = 1000, gc = 9.0, α = -0.25, J/ψ yield = 0.011 per unit rapidity.
- For LHC (central Pb+Pb, Npart = 350): N(c\(\bar{\rm c}\))^direct ≈ 50 per unit rapidity, dN(ch)/dη = 6300, gc = 41, α = 0.62, J/ψ yield ≈ 1.4 per unit rapidity (about a factor of 20 above the direct yield).

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously introduced by the same authors (Braun-Munzinger and Stachel, 2000) for charm production, building on the success of thermal model descriptions for light and strange hadrons (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999). The canonical treatment follows the approach proposed by Redlich (2000) and used by Gorenstein et al. (2000) to investigate limits on open charm production. The model predicts that at LHC energies, where the number of charm pairs per unit rapidity is large, J/ψ yields should be strongly enhanced compared to direct hard scattering production, a result also obtained by Thews et al. (2000) using kinetic equations. A direct measurement of open charm production in Pb+Pb collisions is needed to remove the main uncertainty in the present analysis.
