# From nuclear reactions to compact stars: a unified approach

> An equation of state (EoS) for symmetric nuclear matter is constructed using the density dependent M3Y effective interaction and extended for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Theoretically obtained values of symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility, isobaric incompressibility, symmetry energy and its slope agree well with experimentally extracted values. Folded microscopic potentials using this effective interaction, whose density dependence is determined from nuclear matter calculations, provide excellent descriptions for proton, alpha and cluster radioactivities, elastic and inelastic scattering. The nuclear deformation parameters extracted from inelastic scattering of protons agree well with other available results. The high density behavior of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter satisfies the constraints from the observed flow data of heavy-ion collisions. The neutron star properties studied using $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter obtained from this effective interaction for pure hadronic model agree with the recent observations of the massive compact stars such as PSR J1614-2230, but if a phase transition to quark matter is considered such agreement is no longer possible.

# From nuclear reactions to compact stars: a unified approach

## Key claim

The density-dependent M3Y effective interaction, with its density dependence determined from nuclear matter saturation properties, yields an equation of state that simultaneously describes nuclear reactions (radioactive decays, elastic and inelastic scattering) and neutron star masses up to ~1.95 M☉ in a pure hadronic model; however, if a phase transition to quark matter is considered, the maximum mass of stars rotating below the r-mode instability drops to ~1.7 M☉, which is lower than the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97±0.04 M☉) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01±0.04 M☉), thus excluding quark cores within this model for such massive pulsars.

## Method

The authors construct the equation of state for symmetric and isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the density dependence parameters C and β determined by solving the saturation condition at ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³ and ε₀ = -15.26 MeV. They compute the nuclear symmetry energy, its slope L and curvature K_sym, and the isobaric incompressibility K_τ, then use the folded microscopic potentials to describe proton, alpha, and cluster radioactivity half-lives via the improved WKB approximation, as well as elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions. For neutron star properties, they solve Einstein's field equations for rotating stars using β-equilibrated neutron star matter in pure hadronic and hybrid (with quark core) models, comparing maximum masses with observations of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³
- Saturation energy per nucleon: ε₀ = -15.26 ± 0.52 MeV
- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility: K_∞ = 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.71 ± 0.26 MeV (or 30.03 ± 0.26 MeV using the alternative definition)
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: L = 45.11 ± 0.02 MeV
- Curvature parameter of the symmetry energy: K_sym = -183.7 ± 3.6 MeV
- Isospin-dependent part of isobaric incompressibility: K_τ = -408.97 ± 3.01 MeV
- Third-order derivative parameter of symmetric nuclear matter: Q₀ = -276.5 ± 10.5 MeV
- Maximum mass of neutron stars rotating below r-mode instability in pure hadronic model: ~1.95 M☉ with radius ~10 km
- Maximum mass of compact stars with quark cores rotating below r-mode instability: ~1.7 M☉
- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 M☉
- Observed mass of PSR J0348+0432: 2.01 ± 0.04 M☉

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent M3Y interaction framework previously applied to proton radioactivity (Basu et al., 2008) and alpha radioactivity (Basu, 2003; Chowdhury et al., 2006) to a unified description spanning nuclear reactions and neutron star structure. The calculated symmetry energy and its slope are compared with constraints from isospin diffusion data (Chen et al., 2005; Li et al., 2005), isoscaling analyses (Shetty et al., 2007), giant monopole resonances in Sn isotopes (Li et al., 2007), and neutron skin thickness measurements (Centelles et al., 2009). The high-density behavior satisfies flow data constraints from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz et al., 2002). The neutron star results are compared with the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (1998) variational calculation and relativistic mean-field models (FSUGold, NL3, Hybrid from Piekarewicz, 2009). The finding that quark cores are excluded for the most massive observed pulsars sets a constraint on the deconfinement transition density within this model and motivates further studies of alternative quark matter equations of state.
