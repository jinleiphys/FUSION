# Universal range corrections to the Efimov trimer for a class of paths to the unitary limit

> Using potential models we analyze range corrections to the universal law dictated by the Efimov theory of three bosons. In the case of finite-range interactions we have observed that, at first order, it is necessary to supplement the theory with one finite-range parameter, $Γ_n^3$, for each specific $n$-level [Kievsky and Gattobigio, Phys. Rev. A {\bf 87}, 052719 (2013)]. The value of $Γ_n^3$ depends on the way the potentials is changed to tune the scattering length toward the unitary limit. In this work we analyze a particular path in which the length $r_B=a-a_B$, measuring the difference between the two-body scattering length $a$ and the energy scattering length $a_B$, results almost constant. Analyzing systems with very different scales, as atomic or nuclear systems, we observe that the finite-range parameter remains almost constant along the path with a numerical value of $Γ_0^3\approx 0.87$ for the ground state level. This observation suggests the possibility of constructing a single universal function that incorporate finite-range effects for this class of paths. The result is used to estimate the three-body parameter $κ_*$ in the case of real atomic systems brought to the unitary limit thought a broad Feshbach resonances. Furthermore, we show that the finite-range parameter can be put in relation with the two-body contact $C_2$ at the unitary limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for a specific class of paths to the unitary limit, where the difference between the two-body scattering length \(a\) and the energy scattering length \(a_B\) remains nearly constant, the finite-range correction parameter \(\Gamma_0^3\) for the ground-state Efimov trimer is itself almost universal, with a value of approximately 0.87, independent of the potential model or physical scale (atomic or nuclear).

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for three identical bosons using four different potential models: the LM2M2 helium-helium interaction, the MTIII nucleon-nucleon interaction, and two Gaussian representations of these potentials. They varied the potential strength \(\lambda\) to tune the two-body scattering length \(a\) toward the unitary limit (\(\lambda \to \lambda_c\)), following a path where the length \(r_B = a - a_B\) remains approximately constant. The three-body ground-state energies were then analyzed using a modified Efimov radial law that includes a finite-range shift \(\Gamma_n^3\), and the shift was extracted by fitting numerical results to this law.

## Key numbers

- The finite-range shift for the ground-state trimer, using Gaussian potentials, is \(\Gamma_{0,0}^3 \approx 0.87\), with a small first-order correction \(\Gamma_{0,1}^3 \approx -0.14\) in the expansion \(\Gamma_0^3 = \Gamma_{0,0}^3 + \Gamma_{0,1}^3/(\kappa_* a_B)\).
- For the LM2M2 potential, the constant term of the shift is \(\Gamma_{0,0}^3 \approx 0.82\); for the MTIII potential, it is \(\Gamma_{0,0}^3 \approx 0.85\).
- For Gaussian potentials, the three-body parameter at the unitary limit satisfies \(\kappa_*^{\rm He} r_0^{\rm He} = \kappa_*^{NN} r_0^{NN} \approx 0.488\).
- The two-body contact for the Gaussian trimer at the unitary limit is \(\widetilde C_2 \approx 96.924\,\kappa_*\), compared to the zero-range value \(C_2 \approx 53.01\,\kappa_*\).
- The shift is related to the contact difference by \(\Gamma_{0,0}^3 = (\widetilde C_2 - C_2)/(16\pi\kappa_*) \approx 0.8653\).
- At the three-body dissociation point, the Gaussian universal function gives \(\kappa_* a^-_B = -2.483\).
- Using the van der Waals length \(\ell\), the three-body parameter is estimated as \(\kappa_* \approx 0.22/\ell\), based on \(a_-/\ell \approx -9.5\) and \(r_B/\ell \approx 1.4\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kievsky and Gattobigio, 2013) which introduced a finite-range parameter \(\Gamma_n^3\) to correct the Efimov radial law for each energy level. It shows that for a specific class of paths to the unitary limit (constant \(r_B\)), the shift is nearly universal across very different systems, from atomic helium to nuclear systems. This enables the construction of a single Gaussian universal function \(\widetilde\Delta(\xi)\) that incorporates finite-range effects, which can be used to estimate the three-body parameter \(\kappa_*\) for real atomic systems brought to the unitary limit via broad Feshbach resonances, and connects the shift to the two-body contact \(C_2\) at the unitary limit.
