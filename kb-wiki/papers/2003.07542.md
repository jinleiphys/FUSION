# Two-neutron halo structure of $^{31}$F

> We apply the Gamow shell model to study $^{25-31}$F isotopes. As both inter-nucleon correlations and continuum coupling are properly treated therein, the structure shape of $^{31}$F at large distance can be analyzed precisely. For this, one-nucleon densities, root-mean square radii and correlation densities are calculated in neutron-rich fluorine isotopes. It is then suggested that $^{31}$F exhibits a two-neutron halo structure, built from both continuum coupling and nucleon-nucleon correlations.

# Two-neutron halo structure of $^{31}$F

## Key claim
The paper suggests that $^{31}$F exhibits a two-neutron halo structure, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of the Gamow shell model (GSM) with effective interactions fitted to reproduce oxygen and fluorine isotopes at the neutron drip-line; the halo character is inferred from calculated one-nucleon densities, root-mean-square radii, and correlation densities, not from direct experimental measurement.

## Method
The authors applied the Gamow shell model (GSM) using a Berggren basis generated by a Woods-Saxon potential to study $^{25-31}$F isotopes. Valence protons were described with $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ harmonic oscillator states, while the most important neutron partial waves ($d_{3/2}$, $f_{7/2}$, $p_{3/2}$) were treated in the complex-energy Berggren basis to include continuum coupling. Two effective interactions were fitted: a Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) interaction and an effective field theory (EFT) interaction, both above an $^{24}$O core.

## Key numbers
- Two-neutron separation energy of $^{31}$F is about 170 keV (from Wang 2017 AME2016).
- Neutron rms radius of $^{31}$F sharply increases by about 0.4 fm compared to $^{29}$F, while the increase from $^{27}$F to $^{29}$F is about 0.1 fm.
- The $\chi^2$ deviation for binding energies with the FHT interaction is about 300 keV; with the EFT interaction it is about 170 keV.
- Woods-Saxon parameters: diffuseness $d$ = 0.65 fm, radius $R_0$ = 3.663 fm, spin-orbit coupling $V_{\ell s}$ = 7.5 MeV fm$^2$, central depth $V_0$ = 65.659 MeV for protons (67.659 MeV for $\ell = 0$ with EFT) and for neutrons: 39.978 MeV for $\ell = 0,2$, 43.3 MeV for $\ell = 1$, 39.9 MeV for $\ell = 3$.
- The one-nucleon density of $^{31}$F is about one to two orders of magnitude larger than those of $^{27,29}$F in the asymptotic region.

## Context
This work extends previous GSM studies of halo nuclei, particularly the two-neutron halo of $^6$He (Michel 2002, Papadimitriou 2011) and one-neutron halos of $^{31}$Ne (Nakamura 2014) and $^{37}$Mg (Kobayashi 2014). It contrasts with bound-basis methods such as HF-MBPT (Hjorth-Jensen 1995, Wu 2019) and VS-IMSRG (Stroberg 2017, Holt 2019), which fail to reproduce binding energies of $^{30,31}$F due to neglect of continuum coupling. The results enable future studies of how proton-neutron and neutron-neutron correlations in the continuum produce extended spatial structures in $sdpf$ shell nuclei near the neutron drip-line.
