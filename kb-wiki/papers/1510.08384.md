# Direct calculation of hadronic light-by-light scattering

> We report calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering amplitudes via lattice QCD evaluation of Euclidean four-point functions of vector currents. These initial results include only the fully quark-connected contribution. Particular attention is given to the case of forward scattering, which can be related via dispersion relations to the $γ^* γ^* \to$ hadrons cross section, and thus allows lattice data to be compared with phenomenology. We also present a strategy for computing the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of hadronic light-by-light scattering amplitudes via Euclidean four-point functions, but only includes the fully quark-connected contribution and uses unphysically heavy pion masses (277–451 MeV), so the results are not yet directly applicable to the physical muon g-2.

## Method

The authors compute Euclidean four-point functions of vector currents using \(N_f=2\) \(O(a)\)-improved Wilson fermions on three CLS ensembles with lattice spacing \(a=0.063\) fm. They evaluate only the fully-connected quark contractions using a combination of point-source, single-sequential, and double-sequential propagators, with fixed kernels summed over two of the three spatial positions to efficiently obtain momentum-space amplitudes. For forward scattering kinematics (\(p_1 = -p_2\)), they compare the lattice results to a phenomenological model for the \(\gamma^*\gamma^* \to\) hadrons cross section via a subtracted dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing \(a = 0.063\) fm, with pion masses between 277 and 451 MeV.
- A maximum of 300 samples used on each ensemble.
- Fixed photon virtuality \(Q_1^2 = 0.377\) GeV\(^2\) used for the forward scattering amplitude comparison.
- The phenomenological model parameter \(\Lambda\) set to 1.6 GeV for the two-photon transition form factors; varying \(\Lambda\) by \(\pm 0.4\) GeV changes the curves by up to \(\pm 50\%\).
- Experimental value of \(a_\mu = 116592089(63) \times 10^{-11}\) and theory value \(a_\mu = 116591790(65) \times 10^{-11}\), disagreeing by three standard deviations.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering beyond the initial calculations by Blum et al. (2014), which used non-perturbative lattice QCD+QED. The method enables direct comparison with phenomenology via dispersion relations and provides a framework for an independent lattice calculation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. The authors note that reaching physical pion masses and including disconnected diagrams will be necessary for a complete calculation, as argued by Bijnens et al. (2015).
