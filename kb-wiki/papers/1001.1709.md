# Deconfinement transition in protoneutron stars: analysis within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the effect of color superconductivity and neutrino trapping on the deconfinement transition of hadronic matter into quark matter in a protoneutron star. To describe the strongly interacting matter a two-phase picture is adopted. For the hadronic phase we use different parameterizations of a non-linear Walecka model which includes the whole baryon octet. For the quark matter phase we use an $SU(3)_f$ Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective model which includes color superconductivity. We impose color and flavor conservation during the transition in such a way that just deconfined quark matter is transitorily out of equilibrium with respect to weak interactions. We find that deconfinement is more difficult for small neutrino content and it is easier for lower temperatures although these effects are not too large. In addition they will tend to cancel each other as the protoneutron star cools and deleptonizes, resulting a transition density that is roughly constant along the evolution of the protoneutron star. According to these results the deconfinement transition is favored after substantial cooling and contraction of the protoneutron star.

# Deconfinement transition in protoneutron stars: analysis within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including color superconductivity, deconfinement in protoneutron stars is more difficult for small neutrino content and easier for lower temperatures, but these effects are not too large (less than 10% variation in transition density) and tend to cancel each other as the star cools and deleptonizes, resulting in a transition density that is roughly constant along the evolution. This behavior is qualitatively opposite to that found in earlier MIT Bag model calculations without color superconductivity (Lugones 1998, Lugones 1999), where neutrino trapping pushed the transition density much higher.

## Method

The authors adopt a two-phase description of strongly interacting matter in protoneutron star conditions. For the hadronic phase, they use two parameterizations (GM1 and GM4) of a non-linear Walecka model that includes the whole baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ+, Σ0, Σ-, Ξ-, Ξ0) plus electrons and electron neutrinos in weak equilibrium. For the quark matter phase, they employ an SU(3)_f Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective model with color superconducting quark-quark interactions, using two parameter sets for the NJL model. They impose color and flavor conservation during the transition, meaning the just deconfined quark matter has the same flavor composition as the β-stable hadronic phase from which it originated, and is transitorily out of equilibrium with respect to weak interactions; the most likely configuration of the just deconfined phase is 2SC provided the pairing gap is large enough. The deconfinement transition is determined by applying the Gibbs criteria (equal pressure, Gibbs free energy per baryon, and temperature) between the two phases.

## Key numbers

- The transition density of the hadronic phase for the GM4 parametrization is smaller than that for GM1 by approximately 30%.
- At fixed temperature, there is a slight increase in transition density by less than 10% when the neutrino chemical potential μ_ν_e^H falls from 200 MeV to 0 MeV.
- At fixed μ_ν_e^H, there is a slight decrease of the transition density ρ_H by less than 10% when the temperature falls from 30 MeV to 2 MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density used is ρ_0 = 2.7 × 10^14 g cm^-3.
- The maximum mass of a hadronic star for the GM1 parametrization is 1.78 M_⊙; for GM4 it is 2.2 M_⊙.
- The NJL parameter sets have cutoffs Λ = 602.3 MeV (set 1) and Λ = 631.4 MeV (set 2), with current quark masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 112.0 MeV (set 1) or 110.05 MeV (set 2).
- The calculations cover temperatures in the range 0-60 MeV and μ_ν_e^H in the range 0-200 MeV, corresponding to typical protoneutron star conditions during the first tens of seconds of evolution.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deconfinement in protoneutron stars (Lugones 1998, Lugones 1999) that used the MIT Bag model without color superconductivity and found that neutrino trapping strongly disfavors the transition. It also builds on analyses of color superconductivity in deleptonized neutron stars (Bombaci 2007, Lugones 2009) and on the flavor conservation condition during deconfinement (Madsen 1994, Iida and Sato 1998). The results show that the NJL model with color superconductivity gives qualitatively different behavior from the MIT Bag model: the deconfinement density is not as strongly affected by neutrino trapping, and color superconductivity makes the transition easier at lower temperatures. The work enables future full numerical simulations of protoneutron star evolution to determine whether and when deconfinement conditions are attained, as the effects of temperature and neutrino trapping are smoothed by color superconductivity.
