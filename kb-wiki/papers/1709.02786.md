# Structure of the lightest tin isotopes

> We link the structure of nuclei around $^{100}$Sn, the heaviest doubly magic nucleus with equal neutron and proton numbers ($N=Z=50$), to nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and three-nucleon ($NNN$) forces constrained by data of few-nucleon systems. Our results indicate that $^{100}$Sn is doubly magic, and we predict its quadrupole collectivity. We present precise computations of $^{101}$Sn based on three-particle--two-hole excitations of $^{100}$Sn, and reproduce the small splitting between the lowest $J^π=7/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states. Our results are consistent with the sparse available data.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the structure of nuclei around $^{100}$Sn can be linked to nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces constrained by few-nucleon data, but the predictions for the ground-state spin of $^{101}$Sn remain ambiguous because theory is not sufficiently precise to determine whether the $J^\pi=5/2^+$ or $7/2^+$ state is lower.

## Method
The authors employed coupled-cluster calculations at the CCSD and CCSD(T) levels, and valence-space in-medium similarity-renormalization-group (VS-IMSRG) calculations combined with importance-truncated large-scale shell model diagonalization. They used chiral interactions with SRG evolution cutoffs of 1.8, 2.0, and 2.2 fm$^{-1}$ (labeled 1.8/2.0(EM), 2.0/2.0(EM), 2.2/2.0(EM), and 2.0/2.0(PWA)) from Hebeler (2011). For $^{101}$Sn, a new completely renormalized particle-attached equation-of-motion (CR-PA-EOM) method was developed to include perturbative $3p$-$2h$ corrections.

## Key numbers
- The excitation gap in $^{100}$Sn is about 4 MeV, consistent with it being doubly magic.
- The energy splitting between the lowest $J^\pi=7/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states in $^{101}$Sn is 172 keV from experiment.
- For the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction, the different many-body methods agree on the level ordering in $^{101}$Sn, with the energy splitting varying by at most 140 keV.
- The VS-IMSRG result for the $2^+_1$ state in $^{100}$Sn is about 1.5 MeV higher than the EOM-CCSD(T) result.
- When $E_{\rm 3 max}$ is increased from 16 to 18, the binding energy of $^{100}$Sn changes by 2% for the 2.0/2.0(PWA) interaction and less than 1% for the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction.

## Context
This work extends ab initio computations from light and medium-mass nuclei to the heavy $^{100}$Sn region, which previously relied on large-scale shell-model calculations using $^{80}$Zr or $^{88}$Sr cores with phenomenologically adjusted interactions (Hjorth-Jensen 1995). The 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction had already been shown to describe binding energies and spectra up to neutron-rich nickel isotopes (Hagen 2015, 2016; Simonis 2016, 2017). The results open the avenue for reliable calculations of even heavier nuclei based on nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, though definitive spin assignments for $^{101}$Sn await upcoming measurements.
