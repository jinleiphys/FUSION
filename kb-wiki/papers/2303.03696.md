# The study on the structure of exotic states $χ_{c 1}(3872)$ via beauty-hadron decays in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=8\,\mathrm{TeV}$

> A dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model was introduced to study the exotic state $χ_{c 1}(3872)$ yield for three possible structures: tetraquark state, nuclear-like state, and molecular state respectively, where the hadronic final states generated by the parton and hadron cascade model (PACIAE). The $χ_{c 1}(3872)$/$ψ(2S)$ cross-section ratio from beauty-hadron decays (non-prompt) based on the $χ_{c 1}(3872)$ or $ψ(2S)\to J/ψ{π^+}{π^-}$ bound state in the decay chains as a function of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse momentum in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=8\,\mathrm{TeV}$ are calculated. A tetraquark state scenario from PACIAE+DCPC model shows better agreement with the LHCb and ATLAS measurements for the non-prompt $χ_{c 1}(3872)$/$ψ(2S)$ cross-section ratio distributions, indicating that the $χ_{c 1}(3872)$ is more likely to be a compact tetraquark state.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the exotic state χc1(3872) is more likely to be a compact tetraquark state rather than a nuclear-like or molecular state, based on the observation that the tetraquark scenario from the PACIAE+DCPC model shows better agreement with LHCb and ATLAS measurements for the non-prompt χc1(3872)/ψ(2S) cross-section ratio distributions. This conclusion is hedged by the qualifier that the model only shows "better agreement" and "more likely" rather than definitive proof.

## Method
The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to simulate pp collisions at √s = 8 TeV, generating final-state hadrons including J/ψ, π+, and π−. They then applied the Dynamically Constrained Phase-Space Coalescence (DCPC) model to construct χc1(3872) states from these three particles, using different spatial radius intervals to distinguish three possible structures: tetraquark state (R0 < 1.2 fm), nuclear-like state (1.2 < R0 < 1.96 fm), and molecular state (1.96 < R0 < 10 fm). The non-prompt χc1(3872)/ψ(2S) cross-section ratios were calculated as functions of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse momentum, and compared with LHCb and ATLAS measurements.

## Key numbers
- χc1(3872) rest mass: m0 = 3871.69 MeV/c2
- Mass uncertainty parameter: Δm = Γ/2 = 1.95 MeV (half the decay width)
- Tetraquark state radius interval: R0 < 1.2 fm
- Nuclear-like state radius interval: 1.2 < R0 < 1.96 fm
- Molecular state radius interval: 1.96 < R0 < 10 fm
- PACIAE model parameters: parj(1) = 0.10, parj(2) = 0.20, parj(3) = 0.90
- Non-prompt J/ψ yield from PACIAE: (1.60 ± 0.03) × 10−5, compared to LHCb data (1.60 ± 0.01 ± 0.23) × 10−5
- π+ yield from PACIAE: 2.26 ± 0.01, compared to ALICE data 2.26 ± 0.10
- π− yield from PACIAE: 2.25 ± 0.03, compared to ALICE data 2.23 ± 0.10
- The pT interval for comparison with ATLAS data: 10 < pT < 22 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the study of exotic charmonium states, specifically χc1(3872) (also known as X(3872)), which was first observed by the Belle Collaboration in 2003 and later confirmed by CDF II, D0, BESIII, and BABAR. The paper builds on previous theoretical models that describe χc1(3872) as a D*0D0 molecular state (as proposed by various authors), a compact tetraquark state, a hybrid meson, or a charmonium-molecule. The PACIAE+DCPC approach provides a new method to distinguish between these structural interpretations by comparing model predictions with LHCb (2020) and ATLAS measurements of the non-prompt χc1(3872)/ψ(2S) cross-section ratio. The results enable future tests with high-luminosity Run 3 data at the LHC and suggest that coalescence mechanisms play an important role in beauty-quark hadronization in small systems.
