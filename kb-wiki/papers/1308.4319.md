# Conserved Charge Fluctuations in a Chiral Hadronic Model including Hadrons and Quarks

> In this work the susceptibility coefficients of the strange and non-strange quark number of second and fourth order are presented. The results at zero baryonchemical potential are obtained using a well tested chiral effective model including all known hadron degrees of freedom and additionally incorporating quarks and gluons in a PNJL model approach. Quark number susceptibilities are sensitive to the fundamental degrees of freedom in the model and signal the shift from massive hadrons to light quarks at the deconfinement transition by a sharp rise at the critical temperature. Furthermore, all susceptibilities are found to be largely suppressed by repulsive vector field interactions of the particles. In the hadronic sector vector repulsion of baryon resonances restrains fluctuations to a region determined in lattice QCD. However, in the quark sector above $T_c$ even small vector field interactions of quarks quench all fluctuations almost completely. For this reason, vector field interactions for quarks have to vanish in the deconfinement limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while repulsive vector field interactions of baryon resonances are necessary to suppress conserved charge fluctuations to match lattice QCD results, any non-zero vector field coupling for quarks (even small values) quenches fluctuations unreasonably strongly above the critical temperature, forcing the conclusion that quark vector couplings must vanish in the deconfinement limit.

## Method

The authors compute second- and fourth-order baryon number susceptibilities and second-order strange quark susceptibilities at zero baryochemical potential using a chiral SU(3)-flavor sigma-omega model combined with a PNJL-type approach for deconfinement. The model includes all known hadron degrees of freedom with masses up to 2.6 GeV, dynamically generated masses via scalar meson fields, and quarks coupled to the Polyakov loop, with hadrons assigned an excluded volume to suppress them at high temperature. The susceptibilities are obtained by numerically differentiating the grand canonical potential with respect to chemical potentials, and the authors systematically vary the resonance vector coupling strength (r_v = 0 or 0.8) and the quark vector coupling strength (g_qv = 0 or 4.0) to study their effects.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the chiral/deconfinement transition is T_c ≈ 165 MeV (from prior work Rau (2012)) and T_c ≈ 175 MeV in the model including quarks
- The nucleon vector coupling is g_ω^N = 11.56; the quark vector coupling studied is g_qω = 4.0 (set equal to g_Nω/3)
- The resonance vector coupling values studied are r_v = 0 and r_v = 0.8
- The strange quark susceptibility χ_2^S/T^2 is computed for quark vector couplings g_qφ = 0 and g_qφ = -7.4
- The non-strange quark susceptibility χ_2^{u,d}/T^2 saturates at approximately 1 slightly above T_c for the interacting HRG with r_v = 0.8
- The light quark explicit mass is δm_{u,d} = 6 MeV; the strange quark explicit mass is δm_s = 105 MeV; the nucleon explicit mass is δm_i = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective model approach of Rau (2012, 2013) and Dexheimer (2008, 2009) by computing conserved charge fluctuations as a probe of the deconfinement transition, directly comparing to lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2012), Borsanyi (2011, 2013), and Cheng (2008). The finding that quark vector couplings must vanish to reproduce lattice susceptibilities above T_c contradicts the requirement from neutron star studies (Lastowiecki (2012), Blaschke (2013)) that large quark vector couplings are needed to describe two-solar-mass hybrid stars (Demorest (2010)). The results enable future work to refine the treatment of the transition from hadrons to quarks, potentially incorporating bound states in the quark-gluon plasma as suggested by Ratti (2012) to resolve the discrepancy between PNJL models and lattice QCD.
