# Coulomb corrections to bremsstrahlung in electric field of heavy atom at high energies

> The differential and partially integrated cross sections are considered for bremsstrahlung from high-energy electrons in atomic field with the exact account of this field. The consideration exploits the quasiclassical electron Green's function and wave functions in an external electric field. It is shown that the Coulomb corrections to the differential cross section are very susceptible to screening. Nevertheless, the Coulomb corrections to the cross section summed up over the final-electron states are independent of screening in the leading approximation over a small parameter $1/mr_{scr}$ ($r_{scr}$ is a screening radius, $m$ is the electron mass, $\hbar=c=1$). Bremsstrahlung from an electron beam of the finite size on heavy nucleus is considered as well. Again, the Coulomb corrections to the differential probability are very susceptible to the beam shape, while those to the probability integrated over momentum transfer are independent of it, apart from the trivial factor, which is the electron-beam density at zero impact parameter. For the Coulomb corrections to the bremsstrahlung spectrum, the next-to-leading terms with respect to the parameters $m/ε$ ($ε$ is the electron energy) and $1/mr_{scr}$ are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Coulomb corrections to the differential bremsstrahlung cross section are strongly affected by screening, contradicting a prior claim by Olsen (2003); however, the Coulomb corrections to the cross section integrated over final-electron states remain independent of screening in the leading approximation over the small parameter \(1/mr_{scr}\).

## Method

The authors use quasiclassical electron Green's functions and wave functions in an external electric field to compute the bremsstrahlung matrix element for high-energy electrons (\(\varepsilon, \varepsilon' \gg m\)) in the field of a heavy atom. They split the integration region for the matrix element into two overlapping momentum-transfer regimes (\(\Delta \ll m\omega/\varepsilon\) and \(\Delta \gg \Delta_{min}\)) and derive a universal representation valid in both. The Coulomb corrections are obtained by subtracting the Born-approximation result from the exact cross section, and the analysis is extended to the case of an electron beam of finite transverse size.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic scale for the transverse momentum distribution in the Yukawa potential is \(\sqrt{\Delta_z^2 + \beta^2}\), where \(\beta = 1/r_{scr}\) is the screening parameter; for \(\beta \gg |\Delta_z|\) this scale is entirely determined by the screening radius \(r_{scr}\).
- The integrated Coulomb correction to the cross section over \(\Delta_\perp\) is \(R = -32 \pi^3 (Z\alpha)^2 f(Z\alpha)\), where \(f(Z\alpha) = \text{Re}\,\psi(1+iZ\alpha) + C\) with \(C\) the Euler constant, independent of screening in the leading approximation.
- For a finite-size electron beam, the integrated Coulomb correction to the probability is \(R = -32 \pi^3 (Z\alpha)^2 f(Z\alpha) |\phi(0)|^2\), depending only on the electron density at zero impact parameter.
- The next-to-leading corrections to the bremsstrahlung spectrum enter as a sum of two terms: one proportional to \(m/\varepsilon\) (independent of screening) and one small in \(1/mr_{scr}\) (independent of energy).

## Context

This work extends the high-energy asymptotics of bremsstrahlung in a Coulomb field studied by Bethe and Maximon (1954) and the treatment of screening effects by Olsen, Maximon, and Wergeland (1957) and Olsen (1955, 1959). It corrects the claim by Olsen (2003) that Coulomb corrections to the differential cross section are independent of screening, showing instead that screening strongly modifies the differential distribution while the integrated spectrum remains screening-independent. The method using quasiclassical Green's functions was previously applied to photoproduction by Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko (2003, 2004). The results enable precise calculations of bremsstrahlung from high-energy electrons in heavy atoms, accounting for both screening and finite beam-size effects.
