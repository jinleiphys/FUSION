# Two-body Mechanisms in Pion Photoproduction on the Trinucleon

> A breakdown of the Impulse Approximation is studied in pion photoproduction on $^3$He at high momentum transfers. The usual DWIA formalism with Faddeev wave functions which works well for small momentum transfers deviates from experimental measurements by up to two orders of magnitude for $Q^2>6 fm^{-2}$. It is found that the explicit inclusion of two-body mechanisms, where the photon is absorbed on one nucleon and the pion is emitted from another nucleon can restore agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The standard Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) with Faddeev wave functions, which works well for momentum transfers below 6 fm⁻², fails dramatically for \(Q^2 > 6\) fm⁻², underpredicting experimental cross sections by up to two orders of magnitude; including explicit two-body mechanisms where the photon is absorbed on one nucleon and the pion is emitted from another restores agreement with the data.

## Method

The authors derived two-body photoproduction operators by starting from an effective Lagrangian for the pion-nuclear production process and introducing the electromagnetic field via minimal substitution, which guarantees gauge invariance. They then evaluated the contributions from both the convection current and the magnetic interaction (using nucleon magnetic moments \(\mu_p = 2.79\) and \(\mu_n = -1.91\)) within a multiple-scattering framework, and compared the resulting cross sections to existing \(^3\text{He}(\gamma,\pi^+)^3\text{H}\) data at high momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

- For \(Q^2 > 6\) fm⁻², the DWIA formalism deviates from experimental measurements by up to two orders of magnitude.
- The two-body mechanisms become dominant for \(Q^2 > 7\) fm⁻², raising the cross section by up to two orders of magnitude.
- At \(E_\gamma = 500\) MeV and \(\theta_\pi > 150^\circ\), corresponding to \(Q^2 > 16\) fm⁻², the two-body mechanisms increase the differential cross section by up to two orders of magnitude.
- In a harmonic oscillator model at \(E_\gamma = 400\) MeV, the ratio of two-body to one-body form factors is \(F^{(2)}/F^{(1)} \approx 340\) at \(\theta_\pi = 137^\circ\) and \(\approx 3000\) at \(\theta_\pi = 180^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends earlier multiple-scattering calculations by Kamalov, Tiator, and Bennhold (1991, 1993) and by Cheon (1994) that described pion scattering and photoproduction on trinucleon systems well at low momentum transfers but failed at high \(Q^2\). The authors show that the previously neglected two-body mechanisms (where the photon is absorbed on one nucleon and the pion emitted from another) resolve the long-standing discrepancy, and they note that similar two-body effects should be important in coherent \(\pi^0\) photoproduction on the deuteron or \(^4\)He, \((\gamma,\eta)\) on light systems, nuclear Compton scattering, and meson elastic scattering and single charge exchange processes.
