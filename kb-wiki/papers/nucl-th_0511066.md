# Charge Transfer Fluctuations as a QGP Signal

> In this study, we analyze the recently proposed charge transfer fluctuations within a finite pseudo-rapidity space. As the charge transfer fluctuation is a measure of the local charge correlation length, it is capable of detecting inhomogeneity in the hot and dense matter created by heavy ion collisions. We predict that going from peripheral to central collisions, the charge transfer fluctuations at midrapidity should decrease substantially while the charge transfer fluctuations at the edges of the observation window should decrease by a small amount. These are consequences of having a strongly inhomogeneous matter where the QGP component is concentrated around midrapidity. We also show how to constrain the values of the charge correlations lengths in both the hadronic phase and the QGP phase using the charge transfer fluctuations. Current manuscript is based on the preprints hep-ph/0503085 (to appear in Physical Review C) and nucl-th/0506025.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that charge transfer fluctuations, measured as a function of (pseudo-)rapidity, can detect the presence of a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) even when the QGP component constitutes only about 20% of the final state particles, provided the measurement is performed locally rather than averaged over a large rapidity window.

## Method

The authors use a neutral cluster model where charged hadrons originate from neutral clusters with a characteristic rapidity separation between decay products. They simulate events with two components: a hadronic gas component with correlation length γ_HG = 1.75 and a QGP component with a smaller correlation length γ_QGP, concentrated near midrapidity. They compare their model predictions to results from hadronic models without QGP (HIJING, UrQMD, RQMD) and to experimental net charge fluctuation data from RHIC within the pseudo-rapidity window -1 < η < 1.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic correlation length is fixed at γ_HG = 1.75 based on fits to HIJING, UrQMD, and RQMD model results.
- The value of κ(y) at midrapidity varies by as much as 30% as collisions become more central.
- Current net charge fluctuation data is consistent with QGP having about 20% of the entropy fraction and about 1/3 of the charge correlation length of the hadronic phase.
- Within -1 < η < 1, about 50% of particles remember their QGP origin in central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of charge transfer fluctuations first introduced by Quigg and Thomas (1973) and Chao and Yang (1973), which were shown to be constant in elementary pp and K-p collisions. The authors address why the net charge fluctuation signal, previously proposed by Jeon (2000) and Asakawa and Bass (2000), did not show the expected large reduction in RHIC data: the QGP entropy fraction is too small to be visible when averaged over a large rapidity window. The paper enables experimental determination of both the size of the QGP component and the charge correlation length within the QGP by measuring κ(y) as a function of centrality.
