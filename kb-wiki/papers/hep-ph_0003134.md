# Nonlinear evolution of the momentum dependent condensates in strong interaction: the ``pseudoscalar laser''

> We discuss the relaxation of the scalar and pseudoscalar condensates after a rapid quench from an initial state with fluctuations. If we include not only the zero-mode but also higher modes of the condensates in the classical evolution, we observe parametric amplification of those ``hard'' modes. Thus, they couple nonlinearly to the ``soft'' modes. As a consequence, domains of coherent pi-field emerge long after the initial spinodal decomposition. The momentum-space distribution of pions emerging from the decay of that momentum-dependent condensate is discussed.

## Key claim

If the lattice cutoff for classical Fourier modes of the chiral order parameter is pushed to smaller values (lattice spacing about 0.1-0.2 fm) than the 1 fm used in prior simulations, parametric resonance can amplify hard modes which then nonlinearly drive the soft modes, producing large coherent pion-field domains long after the initial spinodal decomposition has ended.

## Method

The authors performed classical lattice simulations of the linear sigma model in 3+1 dimensions, using a second-order leap-frog algorithm with energy and momentum conserved to within 0.1%. They initialized the chiral fields as Gaussian random variables with zero mean and fluctuations of variance v^2/16 for the fields and v^2/4 fm^2 for the time derivatives, then evolved the classical equations of motion on lattices of physical size 10 fm x 10 fm in transverse directions and 40 fm in longitudinal direction, comparing results for lattice spacings a=1 fm and a=0.2 fm. They also analyzed a simplified linearized model of parametric resonance where the sigma field oscillates harmonically with amplitude sigma_0, solving the mode equations numerically with a routine requiring accuracy 10^-9.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, sigma mass m_sigma = 600 MeV, yielding lambda^2 approximately 20.
- For lattice spacing a=0.2 fm at time t=120 fm, the spatially averaged sigma field relaxed to <sigma> = 0.80 f_pi; for a=1 fm at the same time, <sigma> = 0.56 f_pi.
- The momentum-space distribution of pions shows a sharp rise below k approximately 70 MeV at t=100 fm for a=0.2 fm, indicating spatial homogeneity of the pseudoscalar condensate.
- In the linearized model, occupation numbers of pion modes after 10 sigma-field oscillations with amplitude sigma_0 = v show strong amplification of soft modes (k near 0) and additional resonance bands at higher k.
- The energy density of the pion field increases exponentially in time in the linearized model, while the quark energy density (for coupling g=3.3) remains well below that of the pions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinodal decomposition after a rapid quench in the linear sigma model, particularly the lattice simulations of Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994) and the analytical work of Boyanovsky, de Vega, and Holman (1995), which found that no large domains of coherent pion field form in the strong-coupling case. The authors show that including higher Fourier modes classically (by using a finer lattice spacing) allows parametric resonance, a mechanism known from inflationary cosmology (Kofman, Linde, and Starobinsky, 1994), to produce large domains over time scales much longer than 1 fm. This enables the possibility of observing coherent pseudoscalar condensates ("pseudoscalar laser") in high-energy proton or heavy-ion collisions, as speculated by Bjorken (1992) and Anselm (1989).
