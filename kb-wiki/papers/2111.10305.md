# Pseudo-spontaneous $U(1)$ Symmetry Breaking in Hydrodynamics and Holography

> We investigate the low-energy dynamics of systems with pseudo-spontaneously broken $U(1)$ symmetry and Goldstone phase relaxation. We construct a hydrodynamic framework which is able to capture these, in principle independent, effects. We consider two generalisations of the standard holographic superfluid model by adding an explicit breaking of the $U(1)$ symmetry by either sourcing the charged bulk scalar or by introducing an explicit mass term for the bulk gauge field. We find agreement between the hydrodynamic dispersion relations and the quasi-normal modes of both holographic models. We verify that phase relaxation arises only due to the breaking of the inherent Goldstone shift symmetry. The interplay of a weak explicit breaking of the $U(1)$ and phase relaxation renders the DC electric conductivity finite but does not result in a Drude-like peak. In this scenario we show the validity of a universal relation, found in the context of translational symmetry breaking, between the phase relaxation rate, the mass of the pseudo-Goldstone and the Goldstone diffusivity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in systems with pseudo-spontaneous U(1) symmetry breaking, the interplay of weak explicit breaking and phase relaxation renders the DC electric conductivity finite but does not produce a Drude-like peak, and that a universal relation between the phase relaxation rate, the pseudo-Goldstone mass, and the Goldstone diffusivity holds in this context.

## Method

The authors construct a hydrodynamic framework for pseudo-spontaneous U(1) symmetry breaking in the probe limit, incorporating explicit symmetry breaking via charge relaxation and a pseudo-Goldstone mass, as well as independent phase relaxation. They then consider two holographic generalizations of the standard holographic superfluid model: one where the charged bulk scalar is sourced (Section 3) and another where an explicit mass term for the bulk gauge field is introduced (Section 4). The hydrodynamic dispersion relations are compared to the quasi-normal modes computed from both holographic models.

## Key numbers

* The fourth sound speed in the purely spontaneous case is given by \(v_s^2 = \rho_s / [\mu (\partial \rho_t / \partial \mu)]\) and the attenuation by \(\Gamma_s = \sigma_0 / (\partial \rho_t / \partial \mu) + \zeta_3 \rho_s / \mu\).
* The gap (pinning frequency) for the pseudo-spontaneous modes is \(\omega_0^2 = m / (\partial \rho_t / \partial \mu)\), where \(m\) is related to the pseudo-Goldstone mass.
* The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation derived is \(\mathfrak{m}^2 = \chi_{\xi\xi} m\) with \(\chi_{\xi\xi} = \mu / \rho_s\), where \(\mathfrak{m}^2\) is the pseudo-Goldstone mass squared.
* The low-frequency AC conductivity is \(\sigma(\omega) = \sigma_0\), independent of the phase relaxation rate \(\Omega\) and the explicit breaking parameter \(m\).
* The Kubo formula for the phase relaxation rate is \(\Omega = \lim_{\omega \to 0} (m / (\omega \chi_{\rho\rho}^2)) \, \mathrm{Im}[G^\mathrm{R}_{J^t J^t}(\omega,0)]\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic and holographic study of pseudo-spontaneous symmetry breaking, previously examined in the context of translational symmetry breaking by Amoretti et al. (2018) and others, to the case of U(1) symmetry. The results are relevant for chiral symmetry breaking in QCD at finite quark masses (Son, 2002; Grossi et al., 2021) and for the phenomenology of vortices in superfluids and ordered phases such as charge-density waves. The paper enables future studies of the universal nature of effective phase relaxation, as also discussed in the concurrent work of Delacretaz et al. (2021).
