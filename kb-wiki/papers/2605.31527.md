# Accessing Exotic Hadronic States via Charmed-Meson Femtoscopy in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The two-particle correlation function measured in femtoscopic analyses provides access to the interaction potentials between emitted particles. This offers a unique opportunity to investigate interactions among charmed mesons and to explore the nature of possible exotic hadronic states. In this Letter, we study femtoscopic correlations of various charmed-meson pairs in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The dynamical evolution of the system and charm hadron production are described within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, while the correlation functions are computed using the Correlation Analysis Tool using the Schrödinger equation (CATS). We demonstrate that heavy-ion collisions provide a significantly more favorable environment than $pp$ collisions for accessing charmed meson femtoscopic correlations. This arises from enhanced charm-quark production, reduced relative momenta due to in-medium energy loss, and a strong suppression of initial-state correlations. Our results indicate that femtoscopic measurements in heavy-ion collisions offer a sensitive probe of charmed meson interactions and possible hadronic molecular states.

## Key claim

Heavy-ion collisions provide a significantly more favorable environment than proton-proton collisions for accessing charmed-meson femtoscopic correlations, due to enhanced charm-quark production, reduced relative momenta from in-medium energy loss, and strong suppression of initial-state correlations; this makes femtoscopic measurements a sensitive probe of charmed meson interactions and possible hadronic molecular states.

## Method

The dynamical evolution of charm quarks and their hadronization into charmed mesons (ground states D0, D+, Ds and excited states D*0, D*+, Ds*, D*0(2300)0, D1(2420)0, D*2(2460)0,+) was simulated using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. The final-state interactions among charmed mesons were incorporated using interaction potentials derived from effective field theory with light-hadron exchange (pi, K, eta, rho, omega, phi mesons) and a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Lambda. The femtoscopic correlation functions were then computed using the Correlation Analysis Tool using the Schrodinger equation (CATS) by solving the radial Schrodinger equation and convolving the two-body scattering wave function with the emission source function from PHSD.

## Key numbers

- Emission source RMS radii for D-Dbar pairs in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: 2.46 fm (0-10% centrality), 1.78 fm (30-50% centrality), 1.37 fm (60-80% centrality)
- At low relative momentum k, the scaled distribution of D-Dbar pairs in heavy-ion collisions is nearly twice as large as in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The cutoff parameter Lambda for the monopole form factor was varied from 0.5 GeV to 0.6 GeV for the D*0-Dbar0 system; when Lambda exceeds 0.57 GeV, the bound state disappears
- Time difference selection criterion for charmed meson pairs: |t1-t2| < 0.5 fm/c in the nucleus-nucleus center-of-mass frame

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies from light-hadron correlations (previously measured by ALICE, STAR, and other collaborations at RHIC and LHC) into the heavy-flavor sector, building on theoretical proposals to probe exotic hadrons such as X(3872) (Kamiya 2022), Zc(3900) (Liu 2024), and Tcc (Albaladejo 2023, Kamiya 2022, Ge 2026) in elementary collisions. The authors demonstrate that heavy-ion collisions overcome the low charm-quark production rates that have made experimental investigations challenging in pp and e+e- collisions, enabling future measurements with high-statistics data from upgraded LHC experiments.
