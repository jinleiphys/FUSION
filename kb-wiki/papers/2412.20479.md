# Dipole response of deformed halo nuclei $^{31}$Ne and $^{37}$Mg

> We study the soft electric dipole ($E1$) response of deformed halo nuclei $^{31}$Ne and $^{37}$Mg using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with the potential depth adjusted to reproduce empirical separation energy of last neutron orbit, i.e., 150 keV for $^{31}$Ne and 220 keV for $^{37}$Mg. The configuration dependence of the $E1$ strength near the neutron threshold is pointed out. The halo configurations $[321]3/2$ at $β_2=0.5$ and $[330]1/2$ at $β_2=0.24$ in $^{31}$Ne contain large amplitudes of halo $p$-shell orbits, which significantly enhance the threshold strength by several times compared to the non-halo configuration $[202]5/2$ at $β_2=0.32$. In $^{37}$Mg, the last neutron configuration is assigned as $[321]1/2$ at a large deformation of $β_2=0.46$, which involves a halo $p$-shell configuration that significantly enhances the soft dipole strength. This enhancement is about 60\% larger than that of the $[321]3/2$ configuration in $^{31}$Ne because of large $p$-shell probability in $^{37}$Mg. Experimental confirmation of the soft dipole strength is highly desired to determine the deformation and the configuration of the last neutron orbits both in $^{31}$Ne and $^{37}$Mg.

## Key claim

The soft dipole (E1) strength near the neutron threshold in deformed halo nuclei \(^{31}\)Ne and \(^{37}\)Mg depends strongly on the configuration of the last neutron orbit, with halo configurations containing large \(p\)-shell amplitudes enhancing the threshold strength by several times compared to non-halo configurations; the enhancement in \(^{37}\)Mg is about 60% larger than in \(^{31}\)Ne due to a larger \(p\)-shell probability, but experimental confirmation is required to determine the deformation and configuration definitively.

## Method

The authors used a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with axial symmetry, expanding the potential to linear order in the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\). The potential depth was adjusted to reproduce the empirical single-neutron separation energy (150 keV for \(^{31}\)Ne, 220 keV for \(^{37}\)Mg). The bound-state wave function was obtained as an eigenfunction of the deformed Hamiltonian, and the continuum final states were described using a plane wave approximation. The electric dipole transition strength distribution was calculated using the effective charge for neutrons from the center-of-mass frame, summing over all final angular momenta with orbital angular momentum truncated at \(l=4\).

## Key numbers

- Separation energy of last neutron in \(^{31}\)Ne: 150 keV; in \(^{37}\)Mg: 220 keV
- For \(^{31}\)Ne, the \([321]3/2\) configuration at \(\beta_2=0.5\) has a \(p_{3/2}\) probability of 32.7%, nearly identical to the 32% obtained from Coulomb breakup cross section experiments by Nakamura (2014)
- The \([330]1/2\) configuration at \(\beta_2=0.24\) in \(^{31}\)Ne has a total integrated \(B(E1)\) strength of 0.831 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\), nearly 2 times that of the \([321]3/2\) configuration (0.414 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\))
- The non-halo \([202]3/2\) configuration at \(\beta_2=0.32\) in \(^{31}\)Ne has a total integrated \(B(E1)\) strength of 0.334 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\)
- For \(^{37}\)Mg, the \([321]1/2\) configuration at \(\beta_2=0.46\) has a \(p_{1/2}\) probability of 42.8% and a \(p_{3/2}\) probability of 14.9%, giving a total \(p\)-shell probability of 57.7%
- The enhancement of soft dipole strength in \(^{37}\)Mg is about 60% larger than that of the \([321]3/2\) configuration in \(^{31}\)Ne

## Context

This work extends the deformed Woods-Saxon model calculations of Hamamoto (2010, 2012, 2019) on the Coulomb dissociation of \(^{31}\)Ne, and complements studies using the particle-rotor model (Urata 2011, 2012, 2013), the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (Zhou 2010, Sun 2018, 2020, 2021), and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Takatsu 2023). The results show that measuring the soft dipole strength distribution could distinguish between possible configurations of the last neutron in \(^{31}\)Ne and \(^{37}\)Mg, thereby determining the deformation and configuration of these halo nuclei, which lie in the island of inversion region.
