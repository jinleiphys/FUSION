# Degeneracies when T=0 Two Body Interacting Matrix Elements are Set Equal to Zero : Talmi's method of calculating coefficients of fractional parentage to states forbidden by the Pauli principle

> In a previous work we studied the effects of setting all two body T=0 matrix elements to zero in shell model calculations for $^{43}$Ti ($^{43}$Sc) and $^{44}$Ti. The results for $^{44}$Ti were surprisingly good despite the severity of this approximation. In this approximation degeneracies arose in the T=1/2 I=$({1/2})^-_1$ and $({13/2})^-_1$ states in $^{43}$Sc and the T=1/2 $I=({13/2})_2^-$, $({17/2})^-_1$, and $({19/2})_1^-$ in $^{43}$Sc. The T=0 $3_2^+$, $7_2^+$, $9_1^+$, and $10_1^+$ states in $^{44}$Ti were degenerate as well. The degeneracies can be explained by certain 6j symbols and 9j symbols either vanishing or being equal as indeed they are. Previously we used Regge symmetries of 6j symbols to explain these degeneracies. In this work a simpler more physical method is used. This is Talmi's method of calculating coefficients of fractional parentage for identical particles to states which are forbidden by the Pauli principle. This is done for both one particle cfp to handle 6j symbols and two particle cfp to handle 9j symbols. The states can be classified by the dual quantum numbers ($J_π,J_ν$).

# Degeneracies when T=0 Two Body Interacting Matrix Elements are Set Equal to Zero: Talmi's method of calculating coefficients of fractional parentage to states forbidden by the Pauli principle

## Key claim
The degeneracies observed in shell model calculations for 43Sc and 44Ti when all T=0 two-body matrix elements are set to zero can be explained by Talmi's method of calculating coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp) for states forbidden by the Pauli principle, rather than by Regge symmetries of 6j symbols as used previously. The degeneracies occur specifically for angular momenta I that cannot exist for T=3/2 states in 43Sc or T=2 states in 44Ti (i.e., for systems of identical particles such as 43Ca and 44Ca).

## Method
The authors set all T=0 two-body interaction matrix elements to zero in shell model calculations for 43Ti (43Sc) and 44Ti, then identified degeneracies among states of different total angular momentum. They applied Talmi's method of constructing coefficients of fractional parentage for three identical particles (to handle 6j symbols) and four identical particles (to handle 9j symbols) for states forbidden by the Pauli principle, setting those cfp to zero to derive conditions on 6j and 9j symbols. The states were classified by dual quantum numbers (Jπ, Jν).

## Key numbers
- In 43Sc, the T=1/2 I=(1/2)⁻₁ and (13/2)⁻₁ states are degenerate, as are the T=1/2 I=(13/2)₂⁻, (17/2)⁻₁, and (19/2)₁⁻ states.
- In 44Ti, the T=0 3₂⁺, 7₂⁺, 9₁⁺, and 10₁⁺ states are degenerate.
- For the 6j symbol condition from three identical particles: {j j (2j-3); (3j-4) j (2j-1)} = 0 for both half-integer and integer j.
- For the diagonal 6j condition: {j j (2j-1); j I (2j-1)} = (-1)^(2j)/(8j-2) for I = (3j-1), (3j-2), and (3j-4).
- For the 9j symbol condition from four identical particles: {j j (2j-1); j j (2j-1); (2j-1) (2j-3) (4j-4)} = 0.
- For the diagonal 9j condition: {j j (2j-3); j j (2j-1); (2j-3) (2j-1) I} = 1/[16(2j-2)(2j-1)-12] = 1/[4(4j-5)(4j-1)] for I = (4j-4), (4j-5), and (4j-7).
- B(E2) for 10₁⁺ → 9₁⁺ in 44Ti: 7.167 e²fm⁴ in the single j shell and 6.589 e²fm⁴ in a full f-p calculation.
- B(M1) for 10₁⁺ → 9₁⁺ in 44Ti: zero in the single j shell and 1.07×10⁻⁴ μₙ² in a full f-p calculation.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Robinson and Zamick, 2001) where Regge symmetries of 6j symbols were used to explain degeneracies arising from setting T=0 matrix elements to zero. The present paper provides a more physical explanation using Talmi's method of cfp for Pauli-forbidden states, as described in Talmi's textbook. The method can be applied to other single j shells, such as g₉/₂, predicting degeneracies in 97Cd (I=19/2⁺, 23/2⁺, and 25/2⁺) and 96Cd (I=13⁺ and 14⁺). The results enable understanding of why certain 6j and 9j symbols vanish or become equal without relying on numerical lookup tables.
