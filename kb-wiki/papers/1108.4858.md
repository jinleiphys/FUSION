# Isospin dependent global neutron-nucleus optical model potential

> In this paper, we construct a new phenomenological isospin dependent global neutron-nucleus optical model potential. Based on the existing experimental data of elastic scattering angular distributions for neutron as projectile, we obtain a set of the isospin dependent global neutron-nucleus optical model potential parameters, which can basically reproduce the experimental data for target nuclei from $^{24}$Mg to $^{242}$Pu with the energy region up to 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new isospin-dependent global neutron-nucleus optical model potential that "basically" reproduces experimental elastic scattering angular distributions for target nuclei from ²⁴Mg to ²⁴²Pu with energies up to 200 MeV, but the hedge is that the fit quality is only "as equally good as" the Koning and Delaroche (2003) potential for most nuclei, with significant improvement only for actinide targets.

## Method

The authors used the APMN code, modified to include isospin-dependent terms in the real volume, imaginary surface, and imaginary volume potentials following the Lane model, with radius and diffuseness parameters made functions of target mass. They performed a chi-squared minimization against existing experimental neutron elastic scattering angular distribution data for 45 target nuclei from ²⁴Mg to ²⁴²Pu, with incident energies from 0.134 to 225 MeV, using the Becchetti and Greenlees (1969) parameters as starting values. Compound nucleus elastic scattering was calculated with the Hauser-Feshbach statistical theory including Lane-Lynn width fluctuation corrections.

## Key numbers

- The real central potential depth is given by V_v = 54.983 - 0.3278E + 0.00031E² - (18.495 - 0.219E)(N-Z)/A (MeV), where E is incident neutron energy in MeV.
- The imaginary surface absorption depth is W_s = 11.846 - 0.182E - (16.66 - 0.0141E)(N-Z)/A (MeV).
- The imaginary volume absorption depth is W_v = -2.5028 + 0.2144E - 0.00126E² - (0.000248 - 0.2139E)(N-Z)/A (MeV).
- The spin-orbit potential depths are V_so = 8.797 MeV and W_so = 0.019 MeV.
- The average chi-squared value over the 45 fitted nuclei is 32.27 for the new potential, compared to 30.11 for the Koning and Delaroche (2003) potential.
- The potential covers target nuclei from ²⁴Mg to ²⁴²Pu with incident neutron energies up to 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the global optical model approach of Koning and Delaroche (2003), which covered targets from ²⁴Mg to ²⁰⁹Bi, by adding explicit isospin dependence and extending the target range to include actinides up to ²⁴²Pu. The new potential provides significantly improved description of neutron elastic scattering for actinide nuclei (Th, U, Pu) compared to the Koning and Delaroche (2003) potential, while maintaining comparable quality for lighter nuclei. The results enable more accurate neutron-nucleus reaction calculations for applications such as accelerator-driven subcritical systems and studies of nuclear symmetry energy.
