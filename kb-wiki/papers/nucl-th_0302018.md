# On the stability of three-body bound states on the light front

> We investigate the stability of the relativistic three-boson system with a zero range force in the light front form. In particular we study the dependence of the system on an invariant cut-off. We discuss the conditions for the relativistic Thomas collapse. Finally, we fix the parameters of the model introducing a scale.

## Key claim

The relativistic three-boson system with zero-range force on the light front exhibits the relativistic analog of the Thomas collapse (the three-body bound state mass going to zero) for all values of the invariant cut-off, but the cut-off dependence is mild for weakly bound states.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Faddeev equation on the light front for bosons interacting via a zero-range force, introducing an invariant cut-off \(\Lambda\) on the virtual three-particle mass \(M_{30}^2\) to regularize the divergent integrals. They compare results for different cut-off values (\(\Lambda = 4m, 6m, 8m, 10^{15}m\)) and also against a previous regularization scheme (scheme A) that restricts the two-body mass to be positive. The two-body t-matrix is renormalized either by fixing a two-body bound state mass \(M_{2B}\) or by using the cut-off directly.

## Key numbers

- The three-body bound state mass \(M_{3B}\) approaches zero (Thomas collapse) for finite \(M_{2B}\) as the interaction strength \(\lambda_\Lambda\) is varied, for all cut-off values \(\Lambda = 4m, 6m, 8m, 10^{15}m\).
- For \(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty\) (numerically \(\Lambda/m = 10^{15}\)), the results for \(M_{3B}(M_{2B})\) coincide with those of Carbonell and Karmanov (2002).
- When fitting the proton mass \(m_p = 938\) MeV, for constituent mass \(m = 400\) MeV, no two-body bound state exists for \(\Lambda\) above approximately \(9.6m\).
- For constituent mass \(m = 315\) MeV, no two-body bound state exists for any value of \(\Lambda\) considered, suggesting the nucleon could be a Borromean system.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic light-front three-body calculations of Frederico (1992) and de Araujo (1995) by introducing an invariant cut-off that allows treatment of systems without a two-body bound state, and reproduces the covariant light-front results of Carbonell and Karmanov (2002) in the \(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty\) limit. It demonstrates that the Thomas collapse (Thomas 1935) persists in the relativistic case, contrary to the earlier regularization scheme A which prevented it. The results enable future studies of Borromean nuclear systems and provide a framework for including spin degrees of freedom along the lines of Beyer (1998).
