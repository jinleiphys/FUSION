# Vector-Isovector Excitations at Finite Density and Temperature and the Validity of Brown-Rho Scaling

> Recent work reported at the Quark Matter Conference 2005 has led to the suggestion that Brown-Rho scaling is ruled out by the NA60 data. (Brown-Rho scaling describes the reduction of hadronic masses in matter and at finite temperature.) In the present work we argue that the interpretation of the experimental data presented at the Quark Matter Conference is not correct and that Brown-Rho scaling is valid. To make this argument we discuss the evolution in time of the excited hadronic system and suggest that the system is deconfined at the earliest times and becomes confined when the density and temperature decrease as the system evolves. Thus, we suggest that we see both the properties of the deconfined and confined systems in the experimental data. In our interpretation, Brown-Rho scaling refers to the later times of the collision, when the system is in the confined phase.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the NA60 data do not rule out Brown-Rho scaling, contrary to claims made at the Quark Matter Conference 2005, because the experimental signal contains contributions from both the deconfined phase at early times (t less than 1 fm/c) and the confined phase at later times, with Brown-Rho scaling applying only to the later confined phase.

## Method

The authors use a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a covariant confinement interaction to calculate meson masses as functions of baryon density and temperature. They compute the hadronic correlation functions for vector-isovector excitations in both the confined phase (below the deconfinement temperature T_c) and the deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase (above T_c), using density-dependent and temperature-dependent coupling constants and confining potentials. The results are compared to the NA60 dilepton data from semi-central In+In collisions at 158A GeV.

## Key numbers

- The linear density dependence of the rho meson mass is given by m_rho^* = m_rho (1 - 0.15 n_B/n_0), where n_0 is nuclear matter density.
- The temperature-dependent form used is m_rho^* = m_rho (1 - 0.15 n_B/n_0) (1 - [T/T_c]^2)^{0.3}.
- For the NA60 experiment, at times less than 1 fm/c, the baryon density satisfies 1.2 less than or equal to n_B/n_0 less than or equal to 1.7.
- The temperature in the NA60 experiment for 0 less than t_kin less than 1 fm/c is in the range 162 MeV to 170 MeV.
- The baryon density n_B/n_0 is approximately 1.0 at t approximately 1.3 fm/c.
- For T/T_c = 1.2, the peak in the vector-isovector spectral function is at about 600 MeV.
- The width of the rho meson in matter is approximately 300 MeV at rho/rho_NM = 1.5, based on the work of Ruppert, Renk and Muller.
- The omega meson mass in matter follows m_omega^* = m_omega (1 - 0.14 rho/rho_0) from a photoproduction experiment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of mesonic excitations using a generalized NJL model with confinement (Shakin and Li, 2005) and directly addresses the interpretation of NA60 data presented at the Quark Matter Conference 2005. The paper argues against the conclusion that Brown-Rho scaling is ruled out, instead proposing that the data show both deconfined and confined phases. The analysis builds on the work of Hatsuda and Lee (using QCD sum rules) and references recent discussions by Brown and Rho (2005) on the validity of their scaling law. The results enable a time-resolved interpretation of heavy-ion collision dilepton data that separates early deconfined contributions from later confined contributions.
