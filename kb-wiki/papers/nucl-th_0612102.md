# Kaon-Nucleon scattering lengths from the experiments on kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium

> Within the framework of a low-energy effective field theory we consider the procedure of extraction of the S-wave kaon-nucleon scattering lengths a0 and a1 from a combined fit to the kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium data. It is demonstrated that, if the present DEAR central values for the kaonic hydrogen ground-state energy and width are used in the analysis of the data, a solution for a0 and a1 exists only in a restricted domain of input values for the kaon-deuteron scattering length. We therefore conclude that forthcoming measurement of this scattering length imposes stringent constraints on the theoretical description of the kaon-deuteron interactions at low energies.

## Key claim
Using the DEAR central values for kaonic hydrogen ground-state energy and width, a solution for the S-wave kaon-nucleon scattering lengths a0 and a1 exists only in a restricted domain of input values for the kaon-deuteron scattering length; the combined analysis is more restrictive than one would a priori expect.

## Method
The author uses a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) framework to derive a multiple-scattering series for the kaon-deuteron scattering amplitude, relating it to the S-wave kaon-nucleon scattering lengths a0 and a1. The kaonic hydrogen data is connected to the K-p elastic scattering amplitude via the Deser-type formula, and isospin-breaking corrections from unitary cusps are included. The inverse problem is solved by scanning the complex kaon-deuteron scattering length plane and using the DEAR central values for kaonic hydrogen to find allowed regions for a0 and a1.

## Key numbers
- DEAR kaonic hydrogen ground-state energy shift: epsilon_1s = 193 +/- 37 (stat) +/- 6 (syst) eV
- DEAR kaonic hydrogen ground-state width: Gamma_1s = 249 +/- 111 (stat) +/- 30 (syst) eV
- Solutions for a0 and a1 exist only when Im A_Kd is less than or approximately 1 fm, and when Im A_Kd is about 1 fm, solutions exist only in a small interval around Re A_Kd approximately -1 fm
- The scanned region in the complex kaon-deuteron scattering length plane was -2 fm < Re A_Kd < 0 and 0.5 fm < Im A_Kd < 2.5 fm

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to kaon-deuteron interactions previously applied by Kamalov (2000) and others, and addresses the inverse problem of extracting a0 and a1 from combined hydrogen and deuterium data, which had not been done before. The analysis uses the DEAR experiment results (Beer, 2005) and compares with earlier KpX data from KEK, as well as with potential model calculations from Torres (1986), Deloff (1999), and Bahaoui. The results enable future analysis of SIDDHARTA experiment data and impose stringent constraints on theoretical descriptions of low-energy kaon-deuteron interactions.
