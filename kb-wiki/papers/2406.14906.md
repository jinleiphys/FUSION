# Inference of neutron-star properties with unified crust-core equations of state for parameter estimation

> Relating different global neutron-star (NS) properties, such as tidal deformability and radius, or mass and radius, requires an equation of state (EoS). Determining the NS EoS is therefore not only the science goal of a variety of observational projects, but it also enters in the analysis process; for example, to predict a NS radius from a measured tidal deformability via gravitational waves (GW) during the inspiral of a binary NS merger. To this aim, it is important to estimate the theoretical uncertainties on the EoS, one of which is the possible bias coming from an inconsistent treatment of the low-density region; that is, the use of a so called non-unified NS crust. We propose a numerical tool allowing the user to consistently match a nuclear-physics informed crust to an arbitrary high-density EoS describing the core of the star. We introduce an inversion procedure of the EoS close to saturation density that allows users to extract nuclear-matter parameters and extend the EoS to lower densities in a consistent way. For the treatment of inhomogeneous matter in the crust, a standard approach based on the compressible liquid-drop (CLD) model approach was used in our work. A Bayesian analysis using a parametric agnostic EoS representation in the high-density region is also presented in order to quantify the uncertainties induced by an inconsistent treatment of the crust. We show that the use of a fixed, realistic-but-inconsistent model for the crust causes small but avoidable errors in the estimation of global NS properties and leads to an underestimation of the uncertainties in the inference of NS properties. Our results highlight the importance of employing a consistent EoS in inference schemes. The numerical tool that we developed to reconstruct such a thermodynamically consistent EoS, CUTER, has been tested and validated for use by the astrophysical community.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using a fixed, realistic-but-inconsistent model for the neutron-star crust (rather than a thermodynamically unified crust-core equation of state) causes small but avoidable errors in estimating global neutron-star properties and leads to an underestimation of the uncertainties in parameter inference, though these errors are currently small compared to observational precision.

## Method

The authors developed a numerical tool (CUTER) that extracts nuclear-matter parameters from a given high-density equation of state around saturation density via an inversion procedure, then uses those parameters to compute a consistent crust equation of state within a compressible liquid-drop model. They performed a Bayesian analysis using a parametric agnostic equation-of-state representation in the high-density region, comparing scenarios where a consistent crust is matched to each agnostic realization against scenarios using a single fixed crust model. The crust equation of state was calculated variationally by minimizing the energy density of Wigner-Seitz cells with cluster properties (A, I, n_i, n_e, n_g) as variational variables under baryon number conservation and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density n_sat = 0.16 ± 0.01 fm^{-3} used as a prior range for the isoscalar parameters in the Bayesian analysis.
* Saturation energy E_sat = -16.00 ± 1.00 MeV used as a prior range.
* Incompressibility K_sat = 230 ± 40 MeV used as a prior range.
* Skewness Q_sat = 0.0 ± 1000.0 MeV used as a prior range.
* Effective mass ratio m*_sat/m = 0.7 ± 0.1 used as a prior range.
* Effective mass splitting Δm*_sat/m = 0.1 ± 0.1 used as a prior range.
* For code validation, the BSk24 functional parameters were used: n_sat = 0.1578 fm^{-3}, E_sat = -16.048 MeV, K_sat = 245.5 MeV.
* The surface tension parameters were optimized to reproduce experimental nuclear masses in the 2020 Atomic Mass Evaluation table.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Essick (2021) for extracting nuclear parameters from agnostic equations of state, and builds on the compressible liquid-drop model crust calculations of Carreau (2019) and Dinh Thi (2021a, 2021b, 2021c). It addresses the concern raised by Fortin (2016), Ferreira (2020), Suleiman (2021), and Suleiman (2022) that inconsistent crust-core matching can introduce spurious errors, which will become relevant as third-generation gravitational-wave detectors aim for sub-1% radius precision (Chatziioannou 2021, Huxford 2023, Iacovelli 2023). The CUTER code enables the astrophysical community to construct thermodynamically consistent unified crust-core equations of state for use in inference schemes.
