# Vacuum pseudoscalar susceptibility

> We derive a novel model-independent result for the pion susceptibility in QCD via the isovector-pseudoscalar vacuum polarisation. In the neighbourhood of the chiral-limit, the pion susceptibility can be expressed as a sum of two independent terms. The first expresses the pion-pole contribution. The second is identical to the vacuum chiral susceptibility, which describes the response of QCD's ground-state to a fluctuation in the current-quark mass. In this result one finds a straightforward explanation of a mismatch between extant estimates of the pion susceptibility.

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent result showing that the pion (vacuum pseudoscalar) susceptibility in QCD, in the neighborhood of the chiral limit, splits into a sum of two independent terms: the pion-pole contribution and the vacuum chiral susceptibility. This decomposition provides a straightforward explanation for the apparent mismatch between previous estimates of the pion susceptibility obtained via two-point versus three-point sum-rule methods.

## Method

The authors analyze the isovector-pseudoscalar vacuum polarization using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pseudoscalar vertex. They evaluate the pseudoscalar susceptibility at zero momentum by inserting the decomposition of the pseudoscalar vertex into a regular part and a pion-pole part, then use the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity to relate the regular piece to the quark self-energy. Numerical illustrations are computed using two model kernels for the gap equation: the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and the Ball-Chiu (BC) vertex ansatz.

## Key numbers

* For the RL truncation: the regular part of the pseudoscalar susceptibility gives $\sqrt{{\cal X}_5^R} = 0.39$ (dimensionless, with the chiral-limit condensate $\kappa = 0.25$ GeV and pion decay constant $f_\pi^0 = 0.091$ GeV).
* For the BC vertex: the regular part gives $\sqrt{{\cal X}_5^R} = 0.28$ (with $\kappa = 0.26$ GeV and $f_\pi^0 = 0.11$ GeV).
* For the RL truncation: the pion-pole contribution gives $\sqrt{{\cal X}_5^\pi} = 1.77$; for the BC vertex: $\sqrt{{\cal X}_5^\pi} = 1.66$.
* The current-quark mass used in the RL calculation is $m = 0.0050$ GeV; in the BC calculation, $m = 0.0064$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of vacuum susceptibilities in QCD, building on the authors' previous study of the scalar (chiral) susceptibility (Chang 2008). It resolves a long-standing discrepancy between two-point sum-rule estimates of the pion susceptibility (Belyaev 1983) and three-point sum-rule estimates (Johnson 1997), showing that the former isolates the pion-pole term while the latter isolates the regular piece. The result enables proper separation of contributions in sum-rule estimates of meson-hadron couplings such as the pion-nucleon coupling constants, where only the regular piece should be retained following Reinders (1982).
