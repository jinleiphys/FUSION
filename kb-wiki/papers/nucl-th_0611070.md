# The neutron matter equation of state from low-momentum interactions

> We calculate the neutron matter equation of state at finite temperature based on low-momentum nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. Our results are compared to the model-independent virial equation of state and to variational calculations. We provide a simple estimate for the theoretical error, important for extrapolations to astrophysical conditions.

## Key claim

The neutron matter equation of state at finite temperature can be calculated perturbatively using low-momentum nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, with the cutoff dependence providing a simple estimate for the theoretical error; the perturbative character is due to a combination of Pauli blocking and an appreciable effective range.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion of the grand-canonical potential around the Hartree-Fock free energy, following the work of Kohn, Luttinger and Ward. They include first-order nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon contributions, as well as anomalous and normal second-order diagrams with nucleon-nucleon interactions, using low-momentum interactions derived from the Argonne v18 potential. The free energy is obtained by a Legendre transformation, and the pressure, entropy and energy are calculated using standard thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- Free energy per particle calculated at temperatures T = 3 MeV, 6 MeV and 10 MeV using low-momentum interaction Vlowk with cutoff Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹
- Cutoff variation over the range Λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹ (lower curve) to Λ = 2.5 fm⁻¹ (upper curve) provides error estimates for the T = 6 MeV results
- The virial equation of state curve ends where the fugacity z = e^(μ/T) is 0.5

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum interaction approach of Vlowk (2004) and the perturbative treatment of nuclear matter to finite temperature, building on the formal framework of Kohn, Luttinger and Ward (1960, 1966). The results are compared to the model-independent virial equation of state and to the variational calculations of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), showing good agreement at low densities and highlighting the importance of correct finite-temperature treatment of second-order contributions. This enables improved nuclear equation of state input for astrophysical applications, including neutron star mass and radius predictions with theoretical error estimates.
