# How Nuclear Diffuseness Affects RHIC Data

> The fact that nuclei have diffuse surfaces (rather than being simple spheres) has dramatic consequences on the interpretation of RHIC heavy-ion data. The effect is quite small (but not negligible) for central collisions, but gets increasingly important with decreasing centrality. One may actually divide the collision zone into a central part ("core"), with expected high energy densities, and a peripheral part ("corona"), with smaller energy densities, more like in pp or pA collisions. We will discuss that many complicated "features" observed at RHIC become almost trivial after subtracting the corona background. We are focussing on AuAu collisions at 200 GeV.

## Key claim

Nuclear diffuseness creates a "corona" of low-density, pp-like interactions in the periphery of AuAu collisions at RHIC; after subtracting this corona background, the remaining "core" contribution shows no centrality dependence in particle production, contrary to the general belief that such dependence exists.

## Method

The author uses the EPOS parton model to simulate AuAu collisions at 200 GeV, dividing string segments at proper time τ₀=1 fm into high-density core regions (density > ρ₀=1 fm⁻³) and low-density corona regions. The corona is treated with standard EPOS string fragmentation (validated against pp and dAu data), while the core is parameterized with collective radial flow and statistical hadronization using six global parameters (τ₀, ρ₀, hadronization energy density ε_hadr=0.22 GeV/fm³, maximal radial flow rapidity y_rad=0.83, eccentricity coefficient f_ecc=0.5, and strangeness factor γ_s=1.3) applied to all centralities.

## Key numbers

- Corona contributes roughly 20% to final particle production in semi-peripheral (40-50%) AuAu collisions at 200 GeV
- Core formation time τ₀=1 fm and core formation density ρ₀=1 fm⁻³ used to distinguish core from corona
- Hadronization energy density ε_hadr=0.22 GeV/fm³ used for core cluster hadronization
- Maximal radial flow rapidity y_rad=0.83 and eccentricity coefficient f_ecc=0.5 used in core parameterization
- Strangeness hadronization factor γ_s=1.3 used in core statistical hadronization
- For central (0-5%) AuAu collisions, core contribution dominates at around 90% of the total spectrum
- Only string segments with transverse momenta less than 4 GeV contribute to the core (others escape freely, no jet quenching)

## Context

This work extends the EPOS model approach (Werner, 2006) from pp and dAu collisions to AuAu collisions at RHIC, directly addressing the nuclear diffuseness effect that is well-known in pA scattering but often neglected in heavy-ion analyses. The core-corona separation provides a simple explanation for several "mysteries" in RHIC data, including the centrality dependence of particle ratios and nuclear modification factors, and enables future work to focus on the core physics without needing a fully microscopic description of the high-density region.
