# Exploring axial $U(1)$ restoration in a modified 2+1 flavor Polyakov quark meson model

> We report on the $U_{A}(1)$ symmetry restoration resulting due to temperature dependence of the coefficient c(T) for the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant (KMT) term in a modified 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop quark meson model having fermionic vacuum correction term (PQMVT).Temperature dependence of KMT coupling c(T) drives the non-strange condensate melting to significantly smaller temperatures in comparison to the constant c case.Further due to c(T), $m_{η^{\prime}}$ decreases from its vacuum value by 220 MeV near T=176 MeV after the chiral transition ($T_{c}^χ=154.9$ MeV).This is similar to the $η^{\prime}$ in-medium mass drop of at least 200 MeV as reported by Csorgo and Vertesi in Ref (Phys Rev Lett 105:182301,2010; Phys Rev C 83:054903,2011), as an experimental signature of the effective restoration of $U_A(1)$ symmetry. The pseudoscalar mixing angle $θ_p$ achieves anti-ideal mixing in the influence of c(T). The $η$ meson becomes light quark system ($ η_{NS} $) at T=176 MeV and changes its identity with $ η' $ meson which becomes strange quark system ($ η_{S} $). The degenerated temperature variations of $σ$, $π$ meson masses merges with the temperature variations of the masses of degenerated $a_{0}$, $η$ mesons near 275 MeV. It means that for c(T) when $m_σ=400$ MeV, the $U_{A}(1)$ restoration takes place at 1.75 $T_{c}^χ$=275 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper reports that introducing a temperature-dependent Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) coupling c(T) in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov quark meson model with fermionic vacuum correction (PQMVT) drives the non-strange condensate melting to significantly smaller temperatures compared to the constant c case, and produces an eta-prime mass drop of 220 MeV near T=176 MeV, which is similar to the experimental signature of effective U_A(1) restoration reported by Csorgo and Vertesi (2010, 2011). The U_A(1) restoration is claimed to occur at 1.75 T_c^chi = 275 MeV when the sigma meson mass equals 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors use a modified 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop quark meson model (PQMVT) that includes a fermionic vacuum correction term with explicit renormalization scale independence. They introduce a temperature-dependent KMT coupling c(T) that is constant below T_1 and follows an exponential suppression exp[-(T-T_1)^2/b_1^2] above T_1, with parameters T_1 and b_1 determined by fitting the subtracted chiral order parameter to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal group. The grand potential is minimized in the mean-field approximation to obtain the temperature evolution of the chiral condensates, Polyakov loop expectation values, and meson masses computed from the curvature of the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature is T_c^chi = 154.9 MeV.
- The eta-prime meson mass decreases from its vacuum value by 220 MeV near T = 176 MeV.
- The pseudoscalar mixing angle achieves anti-ideal mixing at T = 176 MeV, where the eta meson becomes a light quark system (eta_NS) and the eta-prime meson becomes a strange quark system (eta_S).
- The degenerated temperature variations of sigma and pi meson masses merge with those of degenerated a_0 and eta mesons near 275 MeV.
- For c(T) when m_sigma = 400 MeV, U_A(1) restoration takes place at 1.75 T_c^chi = 275 MeV.
- The suppression parameter b for the instanton density is about 0.70 T_c for N_f = N_c = 3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of U_A(1) symmetry restoration in effective models, particularly the 2+1 flavor PQM model studied by Gupta and collaborators, and the 2+1 flavor QM model studied by Schaefer and collaborators (2009, 2008). The temperature-dependent KMT coupling approach follows the work of Kunihiro, Costa and collaborators (2004, 2005) in the NJL model, and more recently Ishii and collaborators in the entanglement-PNJL model where they found U_A(1) restoration at T=180 MeV = 1.1 T_c. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations using improved staggered fermions by Cheng and collaborators, and to the experimental observation of an eta-prime in-medium mass drop of at least 200 MeV reported by Csorgo and Vertesi (2010, 2011). The work enables further investigation of how instanton suppression affects the interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and U_A(1) axial symmetry restoration in the temperature range from about 0.7 T_c to 2.0 T_c.
