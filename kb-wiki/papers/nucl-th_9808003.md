# Long Range Alpha the Ternary Cold Fission of $^{252}$Cf

> We compute the final kinetic energies of the fragments emitted in the light charged particle accompanied cold fission of $^{252}$Cf taking into account the ground state quadrupole deformation and the finite-size effects of the fragments and integrating the equations of motion for a three-body system subjected only to Coulomb forces. The initial conditions for the trajectory calculations were derived in the frame of a deformed cluster model which includes also the effect due to the absorbative nuclear part. Although the distributions of initial kinetic energies is rather broad we show that in cold fission the initial conditions can be better determined than in the usual spontaneous fission.

## Key claim

The authors compute final kinetic energies and emission angles of alpha particles in ternary cold fission of $^{252}$Cf, showing that the initial conditions for trajectory calculations can be better determined than in hot spontaneous fission, but the resulting alpha kinetic energies (12-20 MeV) do not show a distinctive distribution for cold fission, consistent with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors derived initial conditions from a deformed cluster model including ground-state quadrupole deformations and nuclear M3Y potentials, then integrated the equations of motion for a three-body system under Coulomb forces using double-folded Coulomb potentials with Fermi charge distributions. The alpha particle was placed at the electro-nuclear saddle point between the two heavy fragments, with its initial kinetic energy estimated from the zero-point energy in a harmonic potential well perpendicular to the fission axis. Trajectories were computed numerically for three mass splittings at tip distances of 6, 7, and 8 fm.

## Key numbers

- For the splitting $^{92}$Kr + $^{156}$Nd at tip distance d = 6 fm: final alpha kinetic energy 20.10 MeV (point-like forces) and 21.36 MeV (finite-size forces); asymptotic angle 80.83 degrees and 77.86 degrees respectively
- For the splitting $^{104}$Mo + $^{144}$Xe at d = 7 fm: final alpha kinetic energy 15.57 MeV (point-like) and 16.44 MeV (finite-size); angle 83.77 degrees and 82.08 degrees
- For the splitting $^{116}$Pd + $^{132}$Sn at d = 8 fm: final alpha kinetic energy 11.53 MeV (point-like) and 11.19 MeV (finite-size); angle 85.68 degrees and 86.93 degrees
- Total kinetic energy of the two main fragments ranges from 25 to 50 MeV depending on tip distance and mass splitting
- The most probable alpha-particle energy in hot fission is 16.0 +/- 0.2 MeV; extrapolation to cold fission (TXE = 0 MeV) gives 17.5 MeV
- The alpha particle cannot be emitted at tip distances larger than 8 fm or smaller than 6 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier trajectory calculations for ternary spontaneous fission by Halpern (1971), Boneh et al. (1967), and others who used point-charge approximations without nuclear forces, and by Carjan and Leroux (1980) and Rau et al. (1982) who considered finite-size and deformation effects. The authors apply the deformed cluster model previously used for ternary cold fission (Sandulescu et al., 1998) to determine initial conditions more precisely than in hot fission. The results enable future Monte-Carlo calculations and suggest that including collective molecular excitations and dynamical deformation changes would be needed for a complete description of the scission configuration.
