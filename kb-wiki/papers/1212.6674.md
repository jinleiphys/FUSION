# Compton scattering: from deeply virtual to quasi-real

> We address the question of interpolation of the virtual Compton scattering process off a polarized nucleon target between the deeply virtual regime for the initial-state photon and its near on-shell kinematics making use of the photon helicity-dependent Compton Form Factors (CFFs) as a main ingredient of the formalism. The five-fold differential cross section for the reaction with all possible polarization options for the lepton and nucleon spins is evaluated in terms of CFFs in the rest reference frame of the initial-state nucleon. We suggest a rather simple parametrization of the Compton hadronic tensor in terms of CFFs which are free from kinematical singularities and are directly related, at large photon virtualities, to Generalized Parton Distributions. We also provide a relation of our basis spanned by a minimal number of Dirac bilinears to the one introduced by Tarrach for the parametrization of the virtual Compton tensor and utilize the former to establish a set of equalities among our CFFs and Generalized Polarizabilities. As a complementary result, we express Compton scattering in the Born approximation in terms of CFFs as well.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact, unified formalism for virtual Compton scattering that interpolates between the deeply virtual regime (large photon virtuality) and the quasi-real regime (near on-shell kinematics) using photon helicity-dependent Compton Form Factors (CFFs), with the caveat that the connection between these CFFs and Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) is only valid at leading order in the 1/Q expansion and is subject to kinematical and dynamical higher-twist uncertainties.

## Method

The authors evaluate the five-fold differential cross section for the leptoproduction of a real photon, including all polarization options for the lepton beam and nucleon target, in the target rest frame using helicity amplitudes. They parameterize the hadronic Compton tensor in terms of a minimal set of Dirac bilinears (vector and axial-vector form factors) that are free from kinematical singularities, and relate this basis to the Tarrach decomposition of the virtual Compton tensor. The squared VCS amplitude is decomposed into Fourier harmonics in the azimuthal angle, with coefficients expressed as bilinear combinations of helicity-dependent CFFs for unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, and transversely polarized targets.

## Key numbers

- The five-fold differential cross section is given by d sigma = (alpha_em^3 x_B y^2) / (16 pi^2 Q^4 sqrt(1 + epsilon^2)) |T/e^3|^2 d x_B d Q^2 d|t| d phi d varphi, where epsilon = 2 x_B M / Q incorporates target mass effects.
- The squared leptonic helicity amplitudes are provided exactly, e.g., L_{++}(lambda) = [2 - 2y + y^2 + (epsilon^2/2) y^2] / [y^2 (1 + epsilon^2)] - [(2 - y) / (y sqrt(1 + epsilon^2))] lambda.
- The kinematical factor tilde K = sqrt((1 - x_B) x_B + epsilon^2/4) sqrt((t_min - t)(t - t_max) / Q^2) vanishes at the phase space boundaries t_min and t_max.
- The leading twist-two contribution to the helicity CFFs is given by F_{++} = F + O(1/Q^2), F_{0+} = (sqrt(2) tilde K) / (sqrt(1 + epsilon^2) Q (2 - x_B + x_B t/Q^2)) F^{eff} + O(1/Q^2) + O(alpha_s), and F_{+-} = (tilde K^2) / (2 M^2 (2 - x_B + x_B t/Q^2)^2) F_T + O(1/Q^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Belitsky, Mueller, Ji, 2010) by providing complete exact results for helicity amplitudes including transversely polarized targets and double helicity-flip effects that were not fully worked out before. It establishes a dictionary between CFFs and the generalized polarizabilities introduced by Guichon, Liu, and Thomas (1995) and Drechsel et al. (1997), using the Tarrach decomposition of the Compton tensor (Tarrach, 1975). The formalism enables a unified description of nucleon structure from low-energy polarizabilities to partonic degrees of freedom at high energies, and the results are made available as a Mathematica code for experimental data analysis.
