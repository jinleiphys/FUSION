# $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ nature of the superdeformed band of $^{32}{\rm S}$ and the evolution of the molecular structure

> The relation between the superdeformed band of $^{32}{\rm S}$ and $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular bands is studied by the deformed-base antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with the Gogny D1S force. It is found that the obtained superdeformed band members of $^{32}{\rm S}$ have considerable amount of the $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ component. Above the superdeformed band, we have obtained two excited rotational bands which have more prominent character of the $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular band. These three rotational bands are regarded as a series of $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular bands which were predicted by using the unique $^{16}{\rm O}$ -$^{16}{\rm O}$ optical potentil. As the excitation energy and principal quantum number of the relative motion increase, the $^{16}{\rm O} + ^{16}{\rm O}$ cluster structure becomes more prominent but at the same time, the band members are fragmented into several states.

## Key claim

The superdeformed band of $^{32}{\rm S}$ obtained by mean-field calculations and the lowest Pauli-allowed $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular band (principal quantum number N=24) are essentially identical, but the molecular structure is significantly distorted by the deformed mean-field and spin-orbit force effects; the higher N=26 and N=28 bands have a more prominent molecular character and are fragmented into several states.

## Method

The authors used deformed-base antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (deformed-base AMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) and the Gogny D1S effective nuclear force. They performed variational calculations under constraints on the nuclear deformation parameter $\beta$ after parity projection, then superposed the optimized wave functions for various deformations via GCM with angular momentum projection. The amount of $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ cluster component in each state was evaluated by projecting the wave function onto the $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ cluster model space using 26 orthonormalized spin-projected Brink wave functions with inter-cluster distances from 0.5 fm to 13.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+$ state of the superdeformed band has a $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ component $w^{J=0}=0.57$ in the deformed-base AMD wave function, which decreases to $w^{J=0}=0.42$ after the GCM calculation.
- The $0^+$ states of the N=26 and N=28 bands have summed $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ components of 0.90 and 0.98, respectively, when including the $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ Brink wave functions in the GCM basis.
- The $0^+$ state of the N=26 band is fragmented into three states at excitation energies 23.8, 24.0, and 25.3 MeV, with $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ components 0.54, 0.13, and 0.23, respectively; the averaged energy is 24.2 MeV.
- The $0^+$ state of the N=28 band is fragmented into three states at excitation energies 31.2, 34.0, and 38.7 MeV, with $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ components 0.32, 0.45, and 0.20, respectively; the averaged energy is 33.7 MeV.
- Compared to the pure $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ bands, the $0^+$ states of N=24, 26, and 28 bands are lowered by about 12, 6, and 2 MeV, respectively.
- At the superdeformed minimum, the single-particle wave packets have width parameters $\nu_x=\nu_y=0.160$ fm$^{-2}$ and $\nu_z=0.115$ fm$^{-2}$, with an inter-cluster distance of 3.1 fm.
- The expectation value of the two-body spin-orbit force in the deformed-base AMD wave function is about -4.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic optical-potential studies of $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular bands by Ohkubo and Yamashita (2002) and Kondo, Robson, and Smith (1988), which used a unique $^{16}{\rm O}-^{16}{\rm O}$ optical potential determined after the discovery of nuclear rainbow scattering by Stiliaris et al. (1989). It also connects to mean-field calculations of the superdeformed structure of $^{32}{\rm S}$ by Yamagami and Matsuyanagi (2000), Dobaczewski et al. (2000), and Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2000). The results enable future comparison with observed $^{16}{\rm O}+^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular resonances and suggest that the N=28 band members plausibly correspond to these observed resonances, while the fragmentation of the higher bands requires further study of couplings to medium-deformation states and distorted $^{16}{\rm O}^*$ cluster configurations.
