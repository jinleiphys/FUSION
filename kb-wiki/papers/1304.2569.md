# Cooper pair transfer in nuclei

> The second order DWBA implementation of two-particle transfer direct reactions which includes simultaneous and successive transfer, properly corrected by non-orthogonality effects is tested with the help of controlled nuclear structure and reaction inputs against data spanning the whole mass table, and showed to constitute a quantitative probe of nuclear pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a second-order Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) treatment of two-nucleon transfer reactions, which includes both simultaneous and successive transfer mechanisms corrected for non-orthogonality effects, can reproduce absolute differential cross sections across the nuclear mass table with uncertainties below the 10% level, provided that accurate nuclear structure spectroscopic amplitudes and empirical optical potentials are used.

## Method

The authors implemented a second-order DWBA formalism that includes simultaneous transfer (where the nucleon-nucleon interaction acts once) and successive transfer (where the mean field acts twice), with non-orthogonality corrections to avoid double counting. They used spectroscopic amplitudes from BCS theory for superfluid nuclei and from Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) for normal and halo nuclei, combined with global empirical optical potentials from the literature, to calculate absolute differential cross sections for (p,t), (t,p), and heavy-ion two-nucleon transfer reactions spanning the mass table.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap: Δ ≈ 12 MeV/√A, with pairing coupling constant G ≈ 25 MeV/A
- Correlation energy of Cooper pairs: E_corr ≈ 0.5–2 MeV
- For ¹²⁰Sn: level spacing D ≈ 0.4 MeV, parameter x = 2GΩ/D ≈ 3 (pairing dominates over shell effects)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb: level spacing D ≈ 3.4 MeV, parameter x = 0.35 (shell effects dominate)
- Exotic decay constant of ²²³Ra → ²⁰⁹Pb + ¹⁴C: λ = 4.3 × 10⁻¹⁶ s⁻¹
- Two-phonon pairing vibrational state in ²⁰⁸Pb observed at 4.9 MeV, with correlation energies of 0.640 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb ground state) and 1.237 MeV (²¹⁰Pb ground state)
- Theory reproduces absolute differential cross sections within uncertainties below the 10% level

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework initiated by Yoshida (1962), who first showed that two-nucleon transfer cross sections between superfluid ground states are proportional to (Δ/G)², and by Glendenning (1965) and Bayman (1968), who provided microscopic DWBA descriptions. The paper resolves the long-standing issue of properly including both simultaneous and successive transfer mechanisms with non-orthogonality corrections, enabling quantitative comparison of absolute differential cross sections (not just relative ones) with experimental data across the entire mass table, from light halo nuclei like ¹¹Li to heavy superfluid nuclei like the Sn isotopes.
