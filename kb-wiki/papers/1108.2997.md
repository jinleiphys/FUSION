# Resonance-Parton Duality and the Transverse Response of Nucleons

> QCD-based scaling arguments predict the predominance of longitudinal over transverse electroproduction of pions by terms $\propto Q^2$. However, data from JLAB, Cornell and DESY, covering a wide kinematical range $1 < Q^2 < 11\, {\rm GeV}^2$ and $2\, {\rm GeV} < W < 4\, {\rm GeV}$, do not show this expected behavior. At the same time standard descriptions of pion-electroproduction on nucleons have given a very good description of the longitudinal components of the cross sections. However, these very same models have failed grossly in describing the transverse component. We discuss here a common solution to these two problems by considering the contributions of high-lying ($W > 2$ GeV) nucleon resonances to pion production. The coupling strengths and form factors are obtained through hadron-parton duality. We show that an excellent description of data in a wide range of electron energies and four-momentum transfers can be obtained in such a model.

## Key claim

The large transverse strength observed in exclusive pion electroproduction, which contradicts QCD-based scaling predictions of longitudinal dominance, can be explained by adding contributions from high-lying nucleon resonances (W > 2 GeV) as an effective description of deep-inelastic scattering excitations, using hadron-parton duality to link resonance properties to partonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors extended the standard t-channel + Born model of Vanderhaeghen et al. (1997) by replacing the nucleon-pole s-channel diagram with a sum over high-lying nucleon resonances, converted into an integral over resonance masses. They used Bloom-Gilman duality in its local form to relate the deep-inelastic structure function F2^p to the resonance contributions, thereby determining the electromagnetic couplings and form factors of the resonances from partonic quantities.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 3.91 GeV^2, the transverse cross section σ_T is about a factor of two larger than the longitudinal cross section σ_L, contrary to QCD-based expectations
- At Q^2 = 2.15 GeV^2, σ_T has the same size as σ_L
- The model of Vanderhaeghen et al. (1997) underestimates σ_T at Q^2 = 2.15 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 3.91 GeV^2 by about one order of magnitude
- The data cover a kinematic range of 1 < Q^2 < 11 GeV^2 and 2 GeV < W < 4 GeV
- The resonance contributions provide about 30% of the longitudinal cross section at forward angles (small -t)
- The model successfully describes data at HERMES in the range 4 < Q^2 < 11 GeV^2

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing puzzle in exclusive pion electroproduction where standard models (Vanderhaeghen et al., 1997) successfully described longitudinal cross sections but failed grossly for transverse components, while QCD-based scaling arguments (Brodsky, 1974; Collins, 1996) predicted longitudinal dominance that was not observed in data from JLAB, Cornell (Bebek, 1977), and DESY/HERMES (2007). The approach extends Bloom-Gilman duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970, 1971) from inclusive to exclusive reactions and has been extended to photo- and electroproduction of π^0 (Kaskulov, 2011) without new parameters, enabling predictions for JLAB at 12 GeV kinematics.
