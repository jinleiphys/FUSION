# Structure of strange baryons

> The charge radii and quadrupole moments of baryons with nonzero strangeness are calculated using a parametrization method based on the symmetries of the strong interaction.

## Key claim

The charge radii and quadrupole moments of strange baryons can be calculated using a parametrization method based on SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and its breaking, but the numerical predictions depend on the assumed value of the Sigma- charge radius input and the treatment of flavor symmetry breaking via the ratio zeta = m_u/m_s, which is only an approximate treatment.

## Method

The author uses a model-independent parametrization method developed by Morpurgo (1989) that formally maps QCD matrix elements onto matrix elements evaluated in a basis of auxiliary three-quark states with L=0. The charge radius and quadrupole moment operators are expressed as sums of one-, two-, and three-quark operators in spin-flavor space multiplied by unknown constants A, B, C (for charge radii) and B', C' (for quadrupole moments), which are determined from experimental input. Flavor symmetry breaking is introduced by replacing the unit operator with a linear quark mass dependence and the spin-spin operator with a cubic mass dependence, characterized by the ratio zeta = m_u/m_s.

## Key numbers

* Neutron charge radius: r^2_n = -0.1161(22) fm^2 (used as input)
* Proton charge radius: r^2_p = 0.779(25) fm^2 (used as input)
* Sigma- charge radius: r^2_Sigma- = 0.61(21) fm^2 from Esc01 (used as input for set (a))
* Quark mass ratio: zeta = m_u/m_s = 0.613 (determined from Lambda magnetic moment)
* Extracted parameters for set (a): A = 0.723 fm^2, B = 0.039 fm^2, C = -0.009 fm^2
* Predicted Lambda charge radius: r^2_Lambda = 0.035 fm^2 (set a)
* Predicted Omega- charge radius: r^2_Omega- = 0.457 fm^2 (set a)
* Predicted N to Delta transition quadrupole moment: Q_p->Delta+ = Q_n->Delta0 = -0.082 fm^2 (all sets)
* Predicted Omega- quadrupole moment: Q_Omega- = 0.014 fm^2 (set a)

## Context

This work extends the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry approach previously applied to non-strange baryons by Morpurgo (1989, 1999, 2001) and Hen02 to the full baryon octet and decuplet including strange baryons. It provides charge radius and quadrupole moment relations that can be tested by future hyperon experiments, such as the WA89 experiment at CERN which measured the Sigma- charge radius (Esc01). The results enable predictions for the Omega- quadrupole moment expressed in terms of measured charge radii, and establish connections between the neutron charge radius and baryon deformation that could guide experimental programs at facilities studying hypernuclear and strange particle physics.
