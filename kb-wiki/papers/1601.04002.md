# Coulomb breakup of neutron-rich $^{29,30}$Na isotopes near the island of inversion

> First results are reported on the ground state configurations of the neutron-rich $^{29,30}$Na isotopes, obtained via Coulomb dissociation (CD) measurements as a method of the direct probe. The invariant mass spectra of those nuclei have been obtained through measurement of the four-momentum of all decay products after Coulomb excitation on a $^{208}Pb$ target at energies of 400-430 MeV/nucleon using FRS-ALADIN-LAND setup at GSI, Darmstadt. Integrated Coulomb-dissociation cross-sections (CD) of 89 $(7)$ mb and 167 $(13)$ mb up to excitation energy of 10 MeV for one neutron removal from $^{29}$Na and $^{30}$Na respectively, have been extracted. The major part of one neutron removal, CD cross-sections of those nuclei populate core, in its' ground state. A comparison with the direct breakup model, suggests the predominant occupation of the valence neutron in the ground state of $^{29}$Na${(3/2^+)}$ and $^{30}$Na${(2^+)}$ is the $d$ orbital with small contribution in the $s$-orbital which are coupled with ground state of the core. The ground state configurations of these nuclei are as $^{28}$Na$_{gs (1^+)\otimesν_{s,d}$ and $^{29}$Na$_{gs}(3/2^+)\otimesν_{ s,d}$, respectively. The ground state spin and parity of these nuclei, obtained from this experiment are in agreement with earlier reported values. The spectroscopic factors for the valence neutron occupying the $s$ and $d$ orbitals for these nuclei in the ground state have been extracted and reported for the first time. A comparison of the experimental findings with the shell model calculation using MCSM suggests a lower limit of around 4.3 MeV of the sd-pf shell gap in $^{30}$Na.

## Key claim

The ground state configurations of $^{29}$Na and $^{30}$Na are dominated by the valence neutron occupying the $d$ orbital with a small $s$-orbital contribution, but the abstract's claim that these results are "first results" is qualified by the fact that the ground state spin and parity values agree with earlier reported measurements, and the spectroscopic factors for the $s$ and $d$ orbitals are reported for the first time.

## Method

The experiment used Coulomb dissociation (CD) of $^{29,30}$Na projectiles on a $^{208}$Pb target at 400-430 MeV/nucleon using the FRS-ALADIN-LAND setup at GSI, Darmstadt. The invariant mass spectra were reconstructed from four-momentum measurements of all decay products (reaction fragment, neutron, and gamma rays from the excited core) after Coulomb excitation. Nuclear background contributions were subtracted using data from a $^{12}$C target with a scaling factor of 1.8, and the differential CD cross-sections were compared with direct breakup model calculations to extract spectroscopic factors for the valence neutron orbitals.

## Key numbers

- Integrated Coulomb dissociation cross-section for one neutron removal from $^{29}$Na: 89 (7) mb up to 10 MeV excitation energy
- Integrated Coulomb dissociation cross-section for one neutron removal from $^{30}$Na: 167 (13) mb up to 10 MeV excitation energy
- For $^{29}$Na, approximately 67 (11)% of the breakup cross-section leaves the core $^{28}$Na in its ground state (59 (8) mb), and approximately 33 (5)% populates excited states (30 (4) mb up to 3.7 MeV)
- For $^{30}$Na, approximately 72 (10)% of the breakup cross-section leaves the core $^{29}$Na in its ground state (121 (14) mb), and approximately 28 (4)% populates excited states (46 (6) mb up to 4.7 MeV)
- Spectroscopic factors for $^{29}$Na: $s$-orbital 0.07 (7), $d$-orbital 2.1 (3)
- Spectroscopic factors for $^{30}$Na: $s$-orbital 0.05 (5), $d$-orbital 2.03 (30)
- Lower limit of the sd-pf shell gap in $^{30}$Na: approximately 4.3 MeV from comparison with MCSM shell model calculations
- Neutron separation energies: $^{29}$Na S$_n$ = 4.4 MeV, $^{30}$Na S$_n$ = 2.27 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the "island of inversion" region around N=20, where intruder configurations from the $pf$ shell dominate ground state wave functions, as first observed in mass measurements of $^{31,32}$Na by Thibault et al. (1975) and later in $^{32}$Mg. The results contradict earlier interpretations from gamma-ray spectroscopy by Tripathi et al. (2007) and Hurst et al. (2007) that suggested intruder-dominated ground states for $^{29,30}$Na, instead showing that $^{29}$Na can be explained by USD-B shell model calculations while $^{30}$Na shows a reduced $d$-orbital spectroscopic factor compared to USD-B predictions. The findings enable more detailed theoretical calculations of nucleon-nucleon interactions for neutron-rich nuclei near the island of inversion and suggest that the boundary of the island of inversion may start from $^{30}$Na rather than $^{29}$Na.
