# The imaginary part of the static gluon propagator in an anisotropic (viscous) QCD plasma

> We determine viscosity corrections to the retarded, advanced and symmetric gluon self energies and to the static propagator in the weak-coupling "hard loop" approximation to high-temperature QCD. We apply these results to calculate the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential which is found to be smaller (in magnitude) than at vanishing viscosity. This implies a smaller decay width of quarkonium bound states in an anisotropic plasma.

## Key claim

Viscosity corrections to the static gluon propagator reduce the magnitude of the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential compared to the vanishing-viscosity case, implying a smaller decay width of quarkonium bound states in an anisotropic plasma; this result holds at leading order in the anisotropy parameter ξ, which is related to the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio η/s via ξ = (10/Tτ)(η/s) in the Navier-Stokes limit.

## Method

The authors compute viscosity corrections to the retarded, advanced, and symmetric gluon self energies using the Keldysh real-time formalism within the hard-loop approximation to high-temperature QCD, expanding the non-equilibrium phase-space distribution to first order in the anisotropy parameter ξ. They then obtain the resummed temporal component of the static gluon propagator in Coulomb gauge and Fourier-transform the symmetric propagator to extract the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential, evaluating the resulting integrals analytically in the small-distance limit.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: m_D² = (g²T²/6)(N_f + 2N_c) for N_f massless quark flavors and N_c colors (Eq. 3a14)
- Isotropic imaginary part of the potential: Im V_(0)(r) = -(g²C_F T)/(4π) φ(r̂) with φ(r̂) = 2∫₀^∞ dz [z/(z²+1)²][1 - sin(z r̂)/(z r̂)] (Eqs. 46-47)
- Leading logarithmic small-distance expansion: Im V(r,ξ) = -(g²C_F T)/(4π) r̂² ln(1/r̂) [1/3 - ξ(3 - cos(2θ))/20] (Eq. 52)
- Quarkonium decay width at leading order: Γ = (16πT)/(g²C_F) (m_D²/M_Q²) (1 - ξ/2) ln(g²C_F M_Q / 8π m_D) (Eq. 53)
- For ξ ≃ 1, the decay width decreases by about 50% compared to the ideal equilibrated plasma

## Context

This work extends the hard-thermal-loop calculations of the heavy-quark potential imaginary part by Laine (2006, 2007) and Beraudo (2007) to include viscous corrections via an anisotropic momentum-space distribution following the approach of Romatschke (2003). The results enable quantitative estimates of how shear viscosity affects quarkonium dissociation temperatures in heavy-ion collisions, with the authors noting that a concurrent paper by Burnier (2009) obtained numerically different results due to a different non-equilibrium setup (equilibrated soft gluons versus out-of-equilibrium hard gluons).
