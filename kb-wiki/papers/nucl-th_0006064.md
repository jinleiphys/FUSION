# Six-body calculation of the alpha-deuteron radiative capture cross section

> We have computed the cross section for the process d + alpha -> 6Li + gamma at the low energies relevant for primordial nucleosynthesis and comparison with laboratory data. The final state is a six-body wave function generated by the variational Monte Carlo method from the Argonne v_18 and Urbana IX potentials, including improved treatment of large-particle-separation behavior. The initial state is built up from the alpha-particle and deuteron ground-state solutions for these potentials, with phenomenological descriptions of scattering and cluster distortions. The dominant E2 cross section is in reasonable agreement with the laboratory data. Including center-of-energy and other small corrections, we obtain an E1 contribution which is larger than the measured contribution at 2 MeV by a factor of 7. We calculate explicitly the impulse-approximation M1 contribution, which is expected to be very small, and obtain a result consistent with zero. We find little reason to suspect that the cross section is large enough to produce significant 6Li in the big bang.

## Key claim

The paper computes the \(d(\alpha,\gamma)^6\text{Li}\) radiative capture cross section at low energies using a six-body variational Monte Carlo approach, finding that the dominant E2 cross section agrees reasonably with laboratory data, but the E1 contribution at 2 MeV is larger than the measured value by a factor of seven, and the M1 contribution is consistent with zero; the authors conclude there is little reason to suspect the cross section is large enough to produce significant \(^6\text{Li}\) in standard big bang nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The final \(^6\text{Li}\) ground-state wave function was generated by the variational Monte Carlo method from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials, with an improved treatment of large-particle-separation behavior using Whittaker functions to enforce asymptotic cluster structure. The initial alpha-deuteron scattering state was built from the alpha-particle and deuteron ground-state solutions for these potentials, with phenomenological descriptions of scattering and cluster distortions using potentials from Kukulin and Pomerantsev and from Langanke. The electromagnetic transition operators were evaluated including one- and two-body charge and current densities, with retardation corrections beyond the long-wavelength approximation for the suppressed E1 and M1 transitions.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic normalization constant \(C_0\) for the \(^6\text{Li}\) ground state is \(2.28 \pm 0.02\), at the lower end of the range 2.3–2.9 used in other studies.
- The asymptotic \(D/S\) ratio \(\eta\) is \(-0.026 \pm 0.001\), consistent with the analysis of \(^6\text{Li} + ^4\text{He}\) elastic scattering which gives \(-0.025 \pm 0.006 \pm 0.010\).
- The spectroscopic factor for the alpha-deuteron cluster in \(^6\text{Li}\) is 0.86, in good agreement with the Robertson value of \(0.85 \pm 0.04\).
- The quadrupole moment of \(^6\text{Li}\) from the variational wave function is \(-0.71 \pm 0.08\) fm\(^2\), an order of magnitude larger than the experimental value of \(-0.08\) fm\(^2\).
- The E1 contribution at 2 MeV is larger than the measured contribution by a factor of seven.
- The \(^6\text{Li}\) binding energy from the variational trial function is \(-27.19(5)\) MeV, compared to the experimental value of \(-31.99\) MeV; the Green's function Monte Carlo result is \(-31.15(11)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior variational Monte Carlo studies of light nuclei by Wiringa, Pieper, and collaborators (1997, 2000) to include radiative capture, improving the asymptotic cluster behavior of the \(^6\text{Li}\) wave function compared to earlier shell-model-like wave functions that gave a \(D/S\) ratio of \(-0.07 \pm 0.02\). The calculation addresses the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical and experimental E1 contributions noted in earlier two-body treatments, and provides cross sections needed for extrapolation to the 20–200 keV range relevant for standard big bang nucleosynthesis, where the primordial \(^6\text{Li}\) yield is most sensitive near 60 keV. The results enable more reliable predictions of \(^6\text{Li}\) production in standard cosmology and constrain the need for exotic physics to explain observed \(^6\text{Li}\) abundances in metal-poor halo stars.
