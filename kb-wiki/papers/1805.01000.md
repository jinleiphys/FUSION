# Universal correlations in the nuclear symmetry energy, slope parameter, and curvature

> From general Fermi liquid theory arguments, we derive correlations among the symmetry energy (J), its slope parameter (L), and curvature (K_sym) at nuclear matter saturation density. We argue that certain properties of these correlations do not depend on details of the nuclear forces used in the calculation. We derive as well a global parametrization of the density dependence of the symmetry energy that we show is more reliable, especially at low densities, than the usual Taylor series expansion around saturation density. We then benchmark these predictions against explicit results from chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

From general Fermi liquid theory arguments, the authors derive universal slope parameters for the correlations between the symmetry energy (J), its slope parameter (L), and curvature (K_sym) at nuclear matter saturation density that are nearly independent of the details of the nuclear interaction, provided the quasiparticle interaction at a low reference density (k_F = 0.9 fm^{-1}) is well described by any realistic nucleon-nucleon potential fitted to scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The authors start from the Fermi liquid theory expression for the nuclear isospin-asymmetry energy in terms of the isotropic component f_0' of the isovector-scalar quasiparticle interaction and the effective mass parameter f_1. They perform a Taylor series expansion of the combination 3f_0' - f_1 around a reference Fermi momentum k_r = 0.9 fm^{-1} and rearrange the resulting expressions for L and K_sym to minimize the explicit dependence on higher-order expansion coefficients a_1 and a_2. The derived correlations are then benchmarked against explicit results from seven chiral nuclear potentials at N1LO, N2LO, and N3LO in the chiral expansion with different momentum-space cutoffs.

## Key numbers

* The universal slope parameter for the J vs. L correlation is m_L = 6.7, derived from the Fermi liquid theory analysis with reference Fermi momentum k_r = 0.9 fm^{-1}.
* The universal slope parameter for the J vs. K_sym correlation is m_K = 18.4, derived from the same analysis.
* The expansion coefficients averaged over seven chiral potentials are: a_0 = 5.88 ± 0.35 fm^2, a_1 = -6.04 ± 0.91 fm^2, and a_2 = 6.08 ± 2.48 fm^2.
* The reference Fermi momentum is chosen as k_r = 0.9 fm^{-1} = 175 MeV, corresponding to a density ρ = 0.05 fm^{-3}.
* The nuclear matter saturation density is taken as ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, with a range ρ = 0.155-0.165 fm^{-3} used for error estimation.
* The symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility is taken as K_0 = 220-260 MeV for error estimation in the J vs. K_sym correlation.

## Context

This work extends the observation from Holt (2017) that even chiral potentials at next-to-leading order exhibit a correlation slope consistent with higher-order calculations, suggesting the correlation arises from low-energy physics. The authors derive these correlations from first principles using Fermi liquid theory, rather than relying on phenomenological models. The derived universal parametrization of the symmetry energy density dependence in terms of powers of (ρ/ρ_0)^(i+2)/3 is shown to be more reliable at low densities than the usual Taylor series expansion around saturation density, and the results can be used to constrain the symmetry energy from future low-density calculations of Fermi liquid parameters.
