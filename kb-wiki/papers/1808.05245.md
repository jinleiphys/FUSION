# Multiplicity dependence of light nuclei production at LHC energies in the canonical statistical model

> The statistical model with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness - the Canonical Statistical Model (CSM) - is used to analyze the dependence of yields of light nuclei at midrapidity on charged pion multiplicity at the LHC. The CSM calculations are performed assuming baryon-symmetric matter, using the recently developed Thermal-FIST package. The light nuclei-to-proton yield ratios show a monotonic increase with charged pion multiplicity, with a saturation at the corresponding grand-canonical values in the high-multiplicity limit, in good qualitative agreement with the experimental data measured by the ALICE collaboration in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at different centralities and energies. Comparison with experimental data at low multiplicities shows that exact conservation of charges across more than one unit of rapidity and/or a chemical freeze-out temperature which decreases with the charged pion multiplicity improves agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The canonical statistical model (CSM) with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness can qualitatively reproduce the observed monotonic increase of light nuclei-to-proton yield ratios with charged pion multiplicity at LHC energies, but the agreement at low multiplicities requires either a correlation volume spanning more than one unit of rapidity or a chemical freeze-out temperature that decreases with increasing multiplicity.

## Method

The authors performed canonical ensemble calculations of the ideal hadron resonance gas model using the Thermal-FIST package, fixing baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness exactly to zero (baryon-symmetric matter) across a correlation volume V_c. They varied V_c between one and three units of rapidity volume (dV/dy) and computed the rapidity densities of light nuclei (deuteron, helium-3, hypertriton, helium-4) and protons as functions of charged pion multiplicity, comparing to ALICE data from pp and Pb-Pb collisions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature of T = 155 MeV used for central Pb-Pb collisions, based on prior fits to hadron yields
- CSM calculations performed at T = 155 MeV and T = 170 MeV, with V_c ranging from dV/dy to 3*dV/dy
- For T = 155 MeV and V_c = dV/dy, the CSM significantly underpredicts the pp data for d/p and 3He/p ratios at low multiplicities
- For T = 155 MeV and V_c = 3*dV/dy, much better agreement with pp data is observed
- For T = 170 MeV and V_c = dV/dy, the CSM significantly overpredicts the high-multiplicity Pb-Pb values for all four ratios
- If finite resonance widths are neglected, CSM results are pushed down by about 15% due to enhanced proton yield

## Context

This work extends the canonical statistical model approach previously applied to strangeness production at LHC energies (Kraus 2008, Adam 2015, Vislavicius 2016) and to hadron abundances in small systems (Becattini 1995, Andronic 2008), but is the first to include full canonical treatment of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness for light nuclei production. The results enable systematic studies of whether the thermal production mechanism or coalescence models (Butler 1961, Csernai 1986) better describe light nucleus formation, and motivate future work incorporating excluded-volume corrections in the canonical ensemble.
