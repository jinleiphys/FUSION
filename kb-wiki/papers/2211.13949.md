# Hidden-flavor pentaquarks

> We have recently studied hidden-charm pentaquarks, $c\bar c qqq$, using dynamical correlations between the heavy quarks arising from the Coulomb-like nature of the short-range interaction. A pattern was obtained that compares well with the experimental data. We extend our description to other flavor sectors which can be framed within the same type of structures discussed in the original paper. A detailed comparison is made with other results in the literature and with experimental data. Predictions will be a useful tool to discriminate between different models of multiquark system dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously successful model of hidden-charm pentaquarks (based on dynamical correlations from the Coulomb-like short-range interaction) to other flavor sectors, specifically hidden-bottom pentaquarks and strange hidden-flavor pentaquarks, with the hedge that the pattern of states that compares well with experimental data in the charm sector provides parameter-free predictions for the bottom sector that can discriminate between different models of multiquark dynamics.

## Method

The five-body problem for hidden-flavor pentaquarks \(Q\bar Q qqq'\) (with \(Q=b\) or \(c\), \(q=u\) or \(d\), and \(q'=u,d\) or \(s\)) is reduced to a three-body problem by exploiting the color Coulomb-like nature of the short-range one-gluon exchange interaction, which forces the heavy quark-antiquark pair into a color singlet. The three-body problem is solved exactly using Faddeev equations, with the quark-quark interaction taken from the AL1 constituent model (Semay and Silvestre-Brac, 1994), which includes a Coulomb-plus-linear central potential and a smeared chromomagnetic interaction, tuned to reproduce the masses of 36 mesons and 53 baryons.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Garcilazo and Valcarce (2022) on hidden-charm pentaquarks to hidden-bottom and strange hidden-flavor sectors, using the same correlation-driven reduction of the five-body problem to a three-body Faddeev problem. The results are compared with other approaches: the color-magnetic interaction model of Wulzca et al. (2017), the chiral quark model of Yan et al. (2019), the hadro-quarkonium model of Ferretti et al. (2019, 2020), the chiral effective field theory of Wang et al. (2020), and the chiral quark model of Hupp et al. (2022). The predictions for hidden-bottom pentaquarks in the 11.0-11.2 GeV region (quark-based models) versus the 10.4-10.9 GeV region (hadro-quarkonium models) provide a test to discriminate between different dynamics of multiquark systems.
