# Studies of mesic nuclei via decay reactions

> Collisions in a system of two particles at energies close to a bound state in different channels are discussed. Next, the bound state decays into a third coupled channel. A phenomenological approach to dd->pi-p3He reaction is presented.

# Studies of mesic nuclei via decay reactions

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological calculation suggesting that the cross section for the $dd \rightarrow (\eta ^4\text{He})_{bound} \rightarrow \pi^- p ^3\text{He}$ reaction is approximately 4.5 nb at the peak, which is below the experimental upper limit of 25 nb; however, the authors note that the relation to the experimental limit is not straightforward due to interference with the quasi-free reaction.

## Method
The authors develop a three-step phenomenological model for bound state formation and decay: initial collision of two deuterons generates an unstable $\eta ^4\text{He}$ bound state, which then propagates and decays into the $\pi^- p ^3\text{He}$ final channel. The transition matrix element is constructed using Gaussian wave functions for the bound state and the $^4\text{He}$ nucleus, with the constant $C_{DB}$ extracted from experimental cross sections of the $dd \rightarrow ^4\text{He}\eta$ reaction near threshold. The cross section is then estimated by combining the formation amplitude, the bound state propagator with width $\Gamma$, and the decay amplitude determined from the partial width $\Gamma^\pi$.

## Key numbers
- The experimental cross section for $dd \rightarrow \pi^- p ^3\text{He}$ is about 200 nb, attributed to quasi-free reaction, with an upper limit of 25 nb for the fraction proceeding through a quasi-bound state (citing Adler et al. 2013).
- The factor $\sigma_{DB}/p_B \simeq 0.3$ nb/(MeV/c) is obtained from experimental cross sections collected in Budzanowski et al. (2009).
- The estimated peak cross section for the bound state process is $\sigma_{DF} \simeq 4.5$ nb, using $\Gamma^\pi \simeq 10$ MeV, $\Gamma \simeq 20$ MeV, and $R \simeq 2.5$ fm.
- The absorptive potential from two-nucleon decay at central nuclear densities is Im $W_{NN}(r=0) \simeq 13$ MeV for the helium nucleus, compared to 3.2 MeV at normal nuclear densities.
- The average subthreshold energy involved in the $\eta N$ center of mass is -36 MeV (21 MeV binding plus about 14 MeV residual nucleus recoil).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing search for $\eta$-nuclear quasi-bound states first discussed by Haider and Liu, where experimental evidence has remained indirect. The authors build on prior measurements of $pd \rightarrow \eta ^3\text{He}$ (Berger, Mayer), $dp \rightarrow \eta ^3\text{He}$ (Johansson, Jurek), and $\gamma ^3\text{He} \rightarrow \eta ^3\text{He}$ (MAMI collaboration), as well as the observation by Wilkin (1993) that rapid threshold slope may indicate a quasi-bound state. The analysis contradicts earlier calculations by Garcilazo and Workman (1995) that indicated a bound $\eta ^4\text{He}$ state, and instead suggests that strong absorption from two-nucleon decay modes may prevent binding or lead to much larger level widths. The ongoing WASA-at-COSY analysis of $dd \rightarrow ^3\text{He} p \pi^-$ and $dd \rightarrow ^3\text{He} n \pi^0$ reactions, reaching sensitivity of several nb, should help resolve the question of the bound state's existence.
