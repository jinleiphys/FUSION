# Pairing and symmetry energy in N \simeq Z nuclei

> It is shown that in N \simeq Z nuclei the effects of isovector pairing and the symmetry energy are intimately related. In particular, in the odd-odd N = Z nuclei these two effect are essentially equal in magnitude, causing near degeneracy of the lowest T = 1 and T = 0 states and appearance of the T = 1 ground state isospin in many such nuclei. Following the earlier work of Jaenecke, it is shown that the global symmetry energy fit can be reproduced using just the excitation energies of the lowest T = 3/2 states in N = Z+1 nuclei. Similarly, the global pairing gap fit can be related to the excitation energy of the T = |N - Z|/2 +1 states in N \simeq Z even A nuclei.

## Key claim

In N ≃ Z nuclei, the effects of isovector pairing and the symmetry energy are intimately related and, in odd-odd N = Z nuclei, these two effects are essentially equal in magnitude, causing near degeneracy of the lowest T = 1 and T = 0 states; this explains why many such nuclei have a T = 1 ground state despite the expectation from pairing alone.

## Method

The author performed a phenomenological analysis using experimental nuclear masses and excitation energies of the lowest T = |N-Z|/2 + 1 states relative to the lowest T = |N-Z|/2 states in N = Z, N = Z+1, and N = Z+2 nuclei. The symmetry energy was extracted from the odd-A N = Z+1 nuclei and compared to the global fit of Duflo and Zuker (1995). Pairing gaps were extracted from even-A nuclei using an averaging procedure that separates the symmetry energy contribution from the pairing contribution.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy formula fitted to 1751 nuclei: E_sym = [T(T+1)/A] × [134.4 − 203.6/A^(1/3)] MeV (Duflo and Zuker 1995)
- Pairing gap for N = Z nuclei: 6.24/A^(1/3) MeV
- Pairing gap for N = Z + 2 nuclei: 5.39/A^(1/3) MeV
- General pairing gap from Duflo and Zuker (1995): 5.18/A^(1/3) MeV
- Wigner energy term: E_W ≃ 47|N-Z|/A MeV
- Coulomb energy formula used: E_Coul = −0.699 × Z(Z-1)/A^(1/3) × [1 − 0.76/(Z(Z-1))^(1/3)] MeV
- About 20% increased pairing gaps in N = Z nuclei compared to N > Z nuclei

## Context

This work extends the earlier phenomenological analysis of Jaenecke (1965), which was itself inspired by Baz and Smorodinsky (1955), by showing that the global symmetry energy fit can be reproduced using only excitation energies of the lowest T = 3/2 states in N = Z+1 nuclei. The paper reconciles the apparent paradox that odd-odd N = Z nuclei are less bound than even-even N = Z nuclei despite expectations from isovector pairing, demonstrating that the symmetry energy and pairing act against each other in these systems. This enables a proper treatment of pairing in proton-rich nuclei where neutron-proton pairing is expected to play an important role, and provides a framework for understanding the Wigner energy term in mass formulae.
