# Equation of state of a quark-meson mixture in the improved-PNJL model at finite chemical potential

> We study the equation of state of QCD using an improved version of the three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model beyond the mean-field approximation. It incorporates the effects of unquenched quarks into the Polyakov-loop effective potential, as well as mesonic contributions to the grand-canonical potential. We study in full detail the calculation of the thermodynamical potential in this approach and compare the resulting pressure and entropy density with the most-recent lattice-QCD calculations at zero baryochemical potential. Finally, we present some exploratory results at finite chemical potential which include the phase diagram of the model, the quark and meson masses, and finally, the thermodynamical pressure.

## Key claim

The improved-PNJL model, which incorporates both quark back-reaction into the Polyakov-loop effective potential and mesonic fluctuations beyond mean field, can simultaneously resolve two long-standing discrepancies with lattice-QCD results: the pressure deficit at low temperatures and the excessive pressure rise above the critical temperature. The authors hedge that this agreement is achieved only after implementing both modifications jointly, and that the choice of the glue potential transition temperature \(T_{glue}^{cr}=190\) MeV is fixed to reproduce lattice data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors extend the three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model beyond mean-field approximation by (1) replacing the standard Yang-Mills Polyakov-loop effective potential with a "glue" potential that includes quark back-reaction, using a temperature rescaling relation \(T_{YM} - T_{YM}^{cr} / T_{YM}^{cr} = 0.57 \cdot (T - T_{glue}^{cr}) / T_{glue}^{cr}\) derived from functional renormalization group studies (Haas 2013); and (2) adding mesonic fluctuations as next-to-leading-order corrections in the \(1/N_c\) expansion to the grand-canonical potential. The grand-canonical potential is minimized with respect to the quark condensates, the Polyakov loop, and its conjugate to obtain the equation of state at zero and finite baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature (inflection point of the quark condensate) is \(T_{c,l}=209\) MeV for the light sector and \(T_{c,s}=208\) MeV for the strange sector in the PNJL model with the glue potential.
- The renormalized chiral condensate \(\Delta_{l,s}\) shows close correspondence with lattice-QCD results from Borsanyi (2010) when plotted as a function of \(T/T_c\).
- The glue potential rescaling coefficient is 0.57, relating the Yang-Mills temperature \(T_{YM}\) to the unquenched temperature \(T\) via \(T_{YM}^{cr}=270\) MeV and \(T_{glue}^{cr}=190\) MeV.
- The critical temperature from lattice QCD is quoted as \(T_c = 155\) MeV (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011) and \(T_c = 157 \pm 6\) MeV (Borsanyi 2010).

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model framework of Ratti (2005) and the authors' previous work (Torres-Rincon 2015) by addressing two known deficiencies: the low-temperature pressure deficit, which is cured by including mesonic fluctuations following the large-\(N_c\) expansion approach of Quack (1993) and Hufner (1994); and the excessive high-temperature pressure, which is corrected by incorporating the quark back-reaction into the Polyakov-loop effective potential as developed by Haas (2013) using functional renormalization group methods. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryochemical potential, including the behavior of quark and meson masses and the thermodynamic pressure beyond the mean-field level.
