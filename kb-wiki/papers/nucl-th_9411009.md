# A quark-cluster description of the EMC effect (Seminar given in the 18th Course of the "International School on Nuclear Physics", 16-23/9/'94, Erice, IT. To be published in Progress in Particle and Nuclear Physics, Vol. 34, Pergamon Press)

> The response of a nucleus composed of nucleons and confined quarks is calculated in a non-relativistic cluster model and is shown to be a convolution of responses of, respectively, a nucleus with interacting point-nucleons and of a nucleon composed of quarks. We further show that the apparent off-shell energy in the expression for the nucleon structure function in Impulse Approximation does not constitue a real problem. We conjecture a generalization to the relativistic regime.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within a non-relativistic quark-cluster model, the total nuclear response for inclusive lepton scattering is an exact convolution of a nuclear response (for point-like nucleons) and a nucleon response (for confined quarks), and they conjecture that this convolution form generalizes to the relativistic regime, thereby resolving the apparent off-shell energy problem in the Impulse Approximation.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic cluster Hamiltonian with spin-0 quarks confined in bags (nucleons) that interact via effective, real, energy-independent nucleon-nucleon potentials between bag centers. They compute the total nuclear response per nucleon as a function of energy transfer and momentum transfer, factorizing the charge-density operator into separate quark and nuclear parts. They then express the result as a convolution of the nuclear response for point-nucleons and the nucleon response for confined quarks, and extend this form to relativistic kinematics using light-cone variables and the Bjorken scaling variable.

## Key numbers

- The convolution for the total nuclear response in the non-relativistic model is given by Eq. (a6): \(W_{\alpha A}(q,\nu) = \int^{\nu}_{q^2/2M_A} d\nu'\, W_{N/A}(q,\nu') W_{\alpha N}(q,\nu-\nu'-{\bm q}^2/2M)\), valid for any finite \(q\).
- The scaling variable for the nucleon response is \(y = -q/2 + m\nu/q\) (Eq. a7), and for the nuclear response it is \(Y = -q/2 + M\nu/q = (M/m)(y + (M-m)q/(2M))\) (Eq. a8).
- In the relativistic extension, the conjectured structure function convolution is \(F_{\alpha A}(x,Q^2) = \int^{M_A/M}_x dz\, F_{N/A}(z,Q^2) F_{\alpha N}(x/z, Q^2)\) (Eq. a26), with \(x \to x^{Bj}\) for large, finite \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) treatment of the EMC effect, as reviewed by Bickerstaff and Thomas (1989), by incorporating final-state interactions through a cluster model that separates nucleonic and sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom. It contrasts with approaches that invoke nucleon swelling or rescaling (e.g., Close, Roberts and Ross, 1984; Nachtmann and Pirner, 1984) and with off-shell treatments (e.g., Akulinichev, Kulagin and Vagradov, 1985). The result enables a framework where the soft nuclear part \(F_{N/A}\) can in principle be calculated from nuclear dynamics, while the hard nucleon part \(F_{\alpha N}\) can be taken from data, and it invites more sophisticated theories (e.g., the quark-meson coupling model of Saito and Thomas, 1994) to address the same questions.
