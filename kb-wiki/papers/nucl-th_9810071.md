# Covariant representations of the relativistic Brueckner T-matrix and the nuclear matter problem

> We investigate nuclear matter properties in the relativistic Brueckner approach. The in-medium on-shell T-matrix is represented covariantly by five Lorentz invariant amplitudes from which we deduce directly the nucleon self-energy. We discuss the ambiguities of this approach and the failure of previously used covariant representations in reproducing the nucleon self-energies on the Hartree-Fock level. To enforce correct Hartree-Fock results we develop a subtraction scheme which treats the bare nucleon-nucleon potential exactly in accordance to the different types of meson exchanges. For the remaining ladder kernel, which contains the higher order correlations, we employ then two different covariant representations in order to study the uncertainty inherent in the approach. The nuclear matter bulk properties are only slightly sensitive on the explicit representation used for the kernel. However, we obtain new Coester lines for the various Bonn potentials which are shifted towards the empirical region of saturation. In addition the nuclear equation-of-state turns out to be significantly softer in the new approach.

## Key claim

The paper develops a subtraction scheme that treats the bare nucleon-nucleon potential exactly at the Hartree-Fock level using a mixed pseudo-scalar plus pseudo-vector representation, while the remaining ladder kernel (higher-order correlations) can be represented in either pure pseudo-scalar or complete pseudo-vector form; this reduces the ambiguity in the covariant representation of the relativistic Brueckner T-matrix and yields new Coester lines shifted toward the empirical saturation region with a significantly softer equation of state.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Thompson equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) in the two-particle center-of-mass frame to obtain the in-medium on-shell T-matrix for nuclear matter. They represent the T-matrix covariantly using five Lorentz invariant amplitudes (scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, pseudo-scalar) and extract the nucleon self-energy via trace formulas. A subtraction scheme is introduced: the bare nucleon-nucleon potential (the leading-order Born term) is treated separately in a mixed pseudo-scalar plus pseudo-vector representation to enforce correct Hartree-Fock results, while the remaining ladder kernel (the subtracted T-matrix containing higher-order correlations) is represented either as pure pseudo-scalar or complete pseudo-vector to study the residual uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum of empirical saturation density: \(k_F = 1.34 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\)
- Fixed reduced effective mass for Hartree-Fock comparison: \(\tilde{m}^*_F = 500 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Density relation: \(\rho = 2 k_F^3 / (3\pi^2)\)
- The five on-shell helicity matrix elements are reduced from 16 possible positive-energy matrix elements by symmetry arguments

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach of Horowitz and Serot (1987) and addresses ambiguities in the covariant representation of the T-matrix previously identified by Fuchs et al. (1998). The authors show that the "conventional" pseudo-vector representation used by Sehn et al. (1997) and Ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) fails to reproduce correct Hartree-Fock results, while their subtraction method resolves this issue. The new Coester lines for the Bonn A, B, and C potentials are shifted toward the empirical saturation region compared to earlier work by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), and the equation of state is significantly softer. This enables more reliable predictions for nuclear matter bulk properties within the relativistic Brueckner framework.
