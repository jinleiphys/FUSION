# Non-perturbative methods for NN singular interactions

> Chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) has been extensively used to study the $NN$ interaction during the last three decades. In Effective Field Theories (EFTs) the renormalization is performed order by order including the necessary counter terms. Due to the strong character of the $NN$ interaction a non-perturbative resummation is needed. In this work we will review some of the methods proposed to completely remove cutoff dependencies. The methods covered are renormalization with boundary conditions, renormalization with one counter term in momentum space (or equivalently substractive renormalization) and the exact $N/D$ method. The equivalence between the methods up to one renormalization condition will be checked showing results in the $NN$ system. The exact $N/D$ method allows to go beyond the others, and using a toy model it is shown how it can renormalize singular repulsive interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews and demonstrates the equivalence of three non-perturbative renormalization methods, renormalization with boundary conditions, renormalization with one counter term in momentum space (substractive renormalization), and the exact N/D method, for removing cutoff dependencies in NN singular interactions, but notes that the exact N/D method can go beyond the others by also renormalizing singular repulsive interactions, as shown with a toy model.

## Method

The authors compare three approaches for non-perturbative resummation of NN interactions in chiral effective field theory (χEFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). Renormalization with boundary conditions is performed in coordinate space by fixing the logarithmic derivative of the wave function at a short-range point r_c using zero-energy scattering length. Renormalization with one counter term is implemented in momentum space by regularizing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a cutoff Λ, fixing the T-matrix at zero energy to the scattering length, and taking Λ→∞. The exact N/D method uses dispersion relations along the left-hand cut and right-hand cut, with subtractions to fix low-energy parameters of the effective range expansion, solving coupled integral equations for the N(A) and D(A) functions.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹S₀ partial wave at LO with one-pion exchange, the scattering length from regular boundary conditions is a = -0.91299 fm; fixing to the Granada value a = -23.735 fm yields the renormalized phase shift.
- The NLO potential in ¹S₀ behaves at the origin as -R_NLO⁴/r⁵ with R_NLO = 0.767 fm; the NNLO potential behaves as -R_NNLO⁵/r⁶ with R_NNLO = 1.056 fm.
- For the toy model with singular repulsive interaction, the full theory scattering length is a = -0.615182 fm, while the order-1 regular potential gives a = -1.50659 fm.
- Phase shifts at onshell momentum k = 400 MeV for the ¹S₀ partial wave at NLO and NNLO converge as r_c → 0 or Λ → ∞, with agreement between boundary condition, one-counter-term, and subtractive renormalization methods.

## Context

This work extends the program of non-perturbative renormalization of NN interactions in χEFT, building on earlier coordinate-space boundary condition methods (PhysRevC.74.054001, PhysRevC.74.064004) and momentum-space subtractive renormalization (Frederico 1999, PhysRevC.77.014002). The exact N/D method (Entem and Oller 2017, Oller 2019) provides a new tool that can handle singular repulsive interactions, which the other methods cannot renormalize in coordinate space. The results enable further study of renormalization in χEFT without cutoff dependence and may clarify the debate between cutoff regularization at finite Λ versus the standard renormalization limit Λ→∞.
