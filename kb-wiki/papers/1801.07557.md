# Baryon-antibaryon dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction ($B{\bar B} \leftrightarrow 3 M$) is studied within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach for Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions as a function of centrality from lower Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) up to Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies on the basis of the quark rearrangement model (QRM). At Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) energies we find a small net reduction of baryon-antibaryon ($B {\bar B}$) pairs while for the LHC energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 GeV a small net enhancement is found relative to calculations without annihilation (and reproduction) channels. Accordingly, the sizeable difference between data and statistical calculations in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.76 TeV for proton and antiproton yields \cite{53}, where a deviation of 2.7 $σ$ was claimed by the ALICE Collaboration, should not be attributed to a net antiproton annihilation. This is in line with the observation that no substantial deviation between the data and statistical hadronization model (SHM) calculations is seen for antihyperons, since according to the PHSD analysis the antihyperons should be modified by the same amount as antiprotons. As the PHSD results for particle ratios are in line with the ALICE data (within error bars) this might point towards a deviation from statistical equilibrium in the hadronization (at least for protons/antiprotons). Furthermore, we find that the $B {\bar B} \leftrightarrow 3 M$ reactions are more effective at lower SPS energies where a net suppression for antiprotons and antihyperons up to a factor of 2 -- 2.5 can be extracted from the PHSD calculations for central Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The observed 2.7 sigma deviation between ALICE data and statistical hadronization model (SHM) calculations for proton and antiproton yields in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV should not be attributed to net antiproton annihilation, because PHSD calculations including full baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction channels show a small net enhancement of baryon-antibaryon pairs at LHC energies rather than a net reduction.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates both a partonic phase described by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) and a hadronic phase, with dynamical hadronization at energy densities around 0.5 GeV/fm^3. The baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction reactions (B anti-B <-> 3M) were implemented using the quark rearrangement model (QRM) extended to the SU(3)_flavor sector, with matrix elements extracted from experimental p anti-p annihilation data and detailed balance enforced on a channel-by-channel basis for over 2500 individual mass channels. Simulations were performed for Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions from sqrt(s_NN) = 3.5 GeV to 2.76 TeV, comparing calculations with full 2<->3 reactions, with only annihilation channels, and without these channels.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (LHC), PHSD calculations with full B anti-B <-> 3M channels show a small net enhancement of baryon-antibaryon pairs relative to calculations without these channels, in contrast to the 2.7 sigma deviation claimed by ALICE between data and SHM calculations
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC), a small net reduction of baryon-antibaryon pairs is found, with antiproton and antihyperon suppression of about 15-20% when comparing full calculations to those without 2<->3 channels
- At lower SPS energies (sqrt(s_NN) approximately 3.5 GeV), a net suppression of antiprotons and antihyperons up to a factor of 2-2.5 is extracted from PHSD calculations for central Au+Au collisions
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the proton and antiproton yields from PHSD with full 2<->3 channels differ by only about 6% between HSD and PHSD calculations
- The net annihilation of antiprotons and antihyperons disappears at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 10 GeV (top SPS and lower RHIC energies)

## Context

This work extends the quark rearrangement model for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction (B anti-B <-> 3M) previously applied at SPS energies in Paper1 (2017) to RHIC and LHC energies using the PHSD transport approach. The results contradict earlier model calculations by Stock (using UrQMD as a hadronic afterburner without backward channels) and Pratt (simplified model with detailed balance predicting approximately 40% net reduction at LHC), while being consistent with the findings of Huovinen (chemical rate equations on hydrodynamic evolution) at RHIC energies. The study enables a microscopic assessment of whether final-state baryon-antibaryon annihilation can explain the discrepancy between ALICE data and statistical hadronization model calculations, and suggests instead a deviation from statistical equilibrium in hadronization for protons and antiprotons.
