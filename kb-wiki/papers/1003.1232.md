# Conical Emission from Shock Waves in Ne(1-20 AGeV)+U Collisions

> The formation and propagation of high-density compression waves, e.g. Mach shock waves, in cold nuclear matter is studied by simulating high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions of Ne with U in the energy range from E_lab = 0.5 AGeV to 20 AGeV. In an ideal hydrodynamic approach, the high-density shock wave created by the small Ne nucleus passing through the heavy U nucleus is followed by a slower and more dilute Mach shock wave which causes conical emission of particles at the Mach cone angle. The conical emission originates from low-density regions with a small flow velocity comparable to the speed of sound. Moreover, it is shown that the angular distributions of emitted baryons clearly distinguish between a hydrodynamic approach and binary cascade processes used in the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that conical emission of low-energy baryons at Mach cone angles arises from a dilute Mach shock wave propagating behind the high-density head shock in asymmetric Ne+U collisions, but this signal is only clearly visible in a free-streaming freeze-out scenario; when a Cooper-Frye freeze-out is applied, the boosted thermal distribution shifts the apparent emission peak to smaller angles, and the Mach cone signal can be recovered only by plotting \(dN/d\cos\theta\) instead of \(dN/d\theta\).

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations of Ne+U collisions at beam energies from 0.5 AGeV to 20 AGeV using a chiral hadronic equation of state with a phase transition, and compared these to default UrQMD transport model calculations without a hydrodynamic phase. The hydrodynamic evolution was stopped after the head shock passed through the target, and particle spectra were obtained via three freeze-out methods: free-streaming freeze-out, Cooper-Frye freeze-out, and Cooper-Frye freeze-out followed by an UrQMD afterburner for resonance decays and rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Maximum local rest frame energy densities reached in the head shock: \(e_{\rm max}/e_0 = 3.5\) at 0.5 AGeV, 13 at 1 AGeV, 32 at 10 AGeV, and 50 at 20 AGeV (with \(e_0 = 147\) MeV/fm\(^3\)).
- Maximum local rest frame baryon densities: \(n_{\rm max}/n_0 = 3\) at 0.5 AGeV, 10 at 1 AGeV, 11 at 10 AGeV, and 11 at 20 AGeV (with \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- Laboratory frame energy density at final time \(t_f\) reaches up to \(e_{\rm lab} = 11\) GeV/fm\(^3\) at \(E_{\rm lab} = 20\) AGeV.
- For the free-streaming freeze-out, the Mach emission angle for low-energy particles (\(E_{\rm kin} \le 100\) MeV) lies in the range \(70^{\circ} < \theta_{\rm MC} < 80^{\circ}\) for \(E_{\rm lab} > 1\) AGeV.
- Applying the UrQMD afterburner shifts the emission angle by \(\Delta \theta_{\rm lab} = +10^{\circ}\) relative to the Cooper-Frye freeze-out without afterburner.
- For non-central Ne+U collisions at \(E_{\rm lab}=5\) AGeV, the hydrodynamic emission angle stays at \(\theta_{\rm MC} \simeq 77^{\circ}\) up to \(b=4\) fm, then decreases to \(\theta_{\rm lab} \simeq 60^{\circ}\) at \(b=9\) fm; UrQMD shows the angle growing with impact parameter up to \(\theta_{\rm max} = 90^{\circ}\).

## Context

This work returns to the original idea of Mach shock waves in asymmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions, extending earlier studies by Hofmann (1975), Baumgardt (1975), and Stoecker (1977, 1980) to higher beam energies and modern freeze-out treatments. It contrasts the hydrodynamic shock-wave picture with the binary cascade approach of the UrQMD transport model (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999), and shows that the impact-parameter dependence of the emission angle can distinguish collective shock-wave formation from kinetic scattering processes. The results enable experimental searches for Mach cone signals at FAIR energies and suggest that the emission angle probes only the hadronic phase, not the chirally restored phase reached in the head shock.
