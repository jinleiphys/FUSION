# Pion transition form factor through Dyson-Schwinger equations

> In the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE), we compute the $γ^*γ\toπ^0$ transition form factor, $G(Q^2)$. For the first time, in a continuum approach to quantun chromodynamics (QCD), it was possible to compute $G(Q^2)$ on the whole domain of space-like momenta. Our result agrees with CELLO, CLEO and Belle collaborations and, with the well-known asymptotic QCD limit, $2f_π$. Our analysis unifies this prediction with that of the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude (PDA) and elastic electromagnetic form factor.

# Pion transition form factor through Dyson-Schwinger equations

## Key claim
For the first time in a continuum approach to QCD, the pion transition form factor G(Q^2) is computed on the whole domain of space-like momenta, agreeing with CELLO, CLEO, and Belle data and the asymptotic QCD limit 2f_pi; however, the result fails to reconcile with the Babar data at large Q^2, and the asymptotic limit is reached from below only after a logarithmic overshoot that originates from the rainbow-ladder truncation's inability to fully express interferences between anomalous dimensions.

## Method
The computation uses Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) in the rainbow-ladder truncation, with the quark propagator expressed as a complex conjugate pole representation and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude parameterized via perturbation theory integral representations (PTIR). The quark-photon vertex is constructed using the gauge technique to satisfy the Ward-Green-Takahashi identity and the Abelian anomaly. All elements are expressed in terms of quadratic forms, allowing analytic integration of four-momentum integrals after Feynman parameterization, yielding G(Q^2) for arbitrarily large space-like momenta.

## Key numbers
- Interaction radius of the neutral pion: r_pi0 = 0.68 fm, practically identical to the value computed in Chang (2013) within the same scheme.
- The Abelian anomaly condition is satisfied: 2 f_pi G(Q^2=0) = 1.
- The asymptotic QCD limit is 2 f_pi, reached from below except for a logarithmic miss-match.
- Agreement with all available data below Q^2 < 10 GeV^2, and with Belle data at large Q^2 scales.
- The pion parton distribution amplitude at resolution scale mu = 2 GeV is a broad concave function, slowly evolving toward the asymptotic form phi_asy(x) = 6x(1-x) via ERBL evolution.

## Context
This work extends earlier DSE calculations by Maris (2002) that could not reach momentum scales larger than Q^2 > 4 GeV^2, and unifies the prediction of the pion transition form factor with the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude from Chang (2013) and the elastic electromagnetic form factor from Chang (2013). The analysis enables a fully self-contained continuum treatment connecting a broad concave pion PDA at hadronic scales with perturbative QCD predictions in the hard photon limit, and the same techniques are being applied to gamma* gamma -> eta_c and gamma* gamma -> eta_b transitions.
