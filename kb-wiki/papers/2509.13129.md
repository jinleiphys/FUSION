# Rotating neutron stars within the macroscopic effective-surface approximation

> The macroscopic model for a neutron star (NS) as a perfect liquid drop at the equilibrium is extended to rotating systems by incorporating the linear perturbation expansion over a small frequency $ω$ near the Schwarzschild gravitational metric within the effective-surface (ES) approach. The NS angular momentum $I$ and moment of inertia (MI) for a slow stationary azimuthal rotation around the symmetry axis is calculated by using the Kerr metric approach in the Boyer-Lindquist and Hogan coordinates for the perfect liquid-drop model of NSs. The off-diagonal metric element is derived analytically from equations of the General Relativity Theory (GRT) and is compared with Boyer-Lindquist and Hogan expressions. The gradient surface terms of the macroscopic NS energy density $\mathcal{E}(ρ)$ [Equation of State] are taken into account along with the volume ones at the leading order of the leptodermic parameter $a/R \ll 1$, where $a$ is the ES crust thickness and $R$ is the NS effective radius. The macroscopic NS angular momentum $I$ at small frequencies $ω$, up to quadratic terms, are specified for calculations of the adiabatic moments of inertia (MI), $Θ= d I/ d ω$. The analytical NS MI expressions, $Θ=\tildeΘ/(1-\mathcal{T}_{tφ})$, has been obtained in terms of the statistically averaged MI, $\tildeΘ$, and its time and azimuthal-angle $t,φ$ correlation, $\mathcal{T}_{tφ}$, as sums of the volume and surface components. The MI $Θ$ is changed significantly as function of the effective radius $R$ because of a strong gravity. We found the additional constraint for the NS radius to smaller accessible ranges due mainly to the $t,φ$ correlations and surface contributions. The adiabaticity condition is carried out for many neutron stars with a strong gravity.

## Key claim

The paper extends the macroscopic effective-surface (ES) approximation for neutron stars as perfect liquid drops to rotating systems, showing that the moment of inertia (MI) acquires an additional constraint on the NS radius to smaller accessible ranges due mainly to time-azimuthal angle correlations and surface contributions, with the adiabaticity condition holding for many neutron stars with strong gravity.

## Method

The authors incorporate a linear perturbation expansion over a small rotational frequency ω near the Schwarzschild gravitational metric within the effective-surface (ES) approach, using the Kerr metric in Boyer-Lindquist and Hogan coordinates for a perfect liquid-drop model of neutron stars. They derive the off-diagonal metric element analytically from General Relativity Theory equations, taking into account gradient surface terms of the macroscopic NS energy density along with volume terms at leading order of the leptodermic parameter a/R ≪ 1, where a is the ES crust thickness and R is the NS effective radius. The angular momentum I at small frequencies ω, up to quadratic terms, is specified for calculations of the adiabatic moments of inertia Θ = dI/dω.

## Key numbers

* The leptodermic parameter a/R ≪ 1, where a is the ES crust thickness and R is the NS effective radius, is used as the small expansion parameter
* The inner density is approximately 1 ≤ ρ̄/ρ₀ ≤ 4, where ρ₀ = 10¹⁴ g/cm³ is the nuclear matter density
* The upper limit for NS masses is M ≲ 3 M⊙
* The gravitational radius is rg = 2MG/c² and the Schwarzschild radius is RS = √(3c⁴/(8πG ε̄))
* The analytical NS MI expression is Θ = Θ̃/(1 − T_{tφ}), where Θ̃ is the statistically averaged MI and T_{tφ} is the time and azimuthal-angle correlation
* The Bessel function solution for the inner off-diagonal metric element τ(x) = c₁ √x J_p(√5 x) with x = r/RS and p = √(9 − 12ξ²)/2, where ξ = R/RS
* The expansion coefficient α₀ ≈ 0.826765 for the leading x² term of the inner τ solution

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous macroscopic effective-surface approach for non-rotating neutron stars (Magner et al., 2024; Magner et al., 2025) to rotating systems, building on Tolman's (1939) liquid-drop model and the Oppenheimer-Volkoff (1939) equations. The rotation treatment follows the Kerr metric approach (Kerr, 1963) as formulated by Boyer and Lindquist (1967) for the outer region and Hogan (1976, 1978) for the inner region, along with the Friedman-Ipser-Stergioulas formalism (Friedman et al., 1986; Stergioulas, 2003). The results enable analytical calculations of neutron star moments of inertia that incorporate surface and correlation corrections, providing constraints that can be tested against observational data on NS masses and radii from double rotating pulsars.
