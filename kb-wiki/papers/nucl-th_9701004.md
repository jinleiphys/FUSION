# Accurate Four-Body Response Function with Full Final State Interaction: Application to Electron Scattering off 4He

## Key claim
The longitudinal (e,e') response function of 4He can be calculated precisely with full final state interaction using the Lorentz transform method, avoiding explicit solution of the four-body continuum problem. The agreement with experimental data is very good over a large energy range for momentum transfers q=300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, but only at higher q does the theoretical response overestimate the experimental one beyond the quasi-elastic peak, indicating the need for subnuclear and/or relativistic effects.

## Method
The authors avoid explicit calculation of four-body continuum states by first computing the Lorentz transform of the response function, solving (H - E0 - sigma_R + i sigma_I) |Psi> = Theta |0> for the ground state |0> and excitation operator Theta, then obtaining the response R(omega,q) via numerical inversion of the transform. The solution is expanded over a correlated hyperspherical basis with Jastrow correlation factor, using central even NN potentials that simulate the tensor force via a dispersive correction, with matrix elements calculated by Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of 4He: 31.3 MeV; rms radius: 1.40 fm
- Momentum transfers considered: q = 300, 400, and 500 MeV/c
- Lorentz transform width parameter sigma_I = 20 MeV
- Maximal hyperradial quantum numbers: N_max = 20 (q=300 MeV/c), 25 (q=400 MeV/c), 30 (q=500 MeV/c)
- Maximal multipole orders: L_max = 4 (q=300 MeV/c), 5 (q=400 MeV/c), 6 (q=500 MeV/c), exhausting the Coulomb sum rule by more than 99%
- Hyperangular quantum number K_max = 7 (K_max = 8 for L_max = 6)
- Average sum rule errors from separate multipole inversion: 1.1% (q=300 MeV/c), 1.0% (q=400 MeV/c), 2.0% (q=500 MeV/c)
- Total Coulomb sum rule errors from inversion of total LT: 0.2% (q=300 MeV/c), 0.4% (q=400 MeV/c), 1.6% (q=500 MeV/c)
- Higher multipoles (L >= 4) at q=500 MeV/c underestimated by about 3% to 4%

## Context
This work extends the Lorentz transform method previously applied to nuclear two- and three-body systems (Efros, Leidemann, Orlandini, 1994) to the four-body system 4He, which is the lightest tightly bound nucleus. Prior calculations using Laplace transforms with Green Function Monte Carlo (Carlson and Schiavilla, 1992, 1994) obtained transforms for q=300 and 400 MeV/c but faced fundamental inversion problems, whereas the Lorentz transform approach yields much more reliable inversion. The results enable precise tests of non-relativistic nuclear dynamics with single-particle electromagnetic operators, establishing the limits of this conventional framework and paving the way for calculations with fully realistic nuclear forces and for transverse response calculations.
