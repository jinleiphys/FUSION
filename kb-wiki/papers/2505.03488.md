# Gamow-Teller strength and lepton captures rates on 66-71Ni in stellar matter

> Charge-changing transitions play a significant role in stellar weak-decay processes. The fate of the massive stars is decided by these weak-decay rates including lepton (positron and electron) captures rates, which play a consequential role in the dynamics of core collapse. As per previous simulation results, weak interaction rates on nickel isotopes have significant influence on the stellar core vis-$\grave{a}$-vis controlling the lepton content of stellar matter throughout the silicon shell burning phases of high mass stars up to the presupernova stages. In this paper we perform a microscopic calculation of Gamow-Teller charge-changing transitions, in the $β$-decay and electron capture directions, for neutron-rich nickel isotopes ($^{66-71}$Ni). We further compute the associated weak-decay rates for these selected nickel isotopes in stellar environment. The computations are accomplished by employing the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model. A recent study showed that the deformed pn-QRPA theory is well suited for the estimation of Gamow-Teller transitions. The astral weak-decay rates are determined over densities in the range of 10 -- 10$^{11}$g/cm$^{3}$ and temperatures in the range of 0.01$\times$10$^{9}$ -- 30$\times$10$^{9}$K. The calculated lepton capture rates are compared with the previous calculation of Pruet and Fuller. The overall comparison demonstrates that, at low stellar densities and high temperatures, our electron captures rates are bigger by as much as two orders of magnitude. Our results show that, at higher temperatures, the lepton capture rates are the dominant mode for the stellar weak rates and the corresponding lepton emission rates may be neglected.

# Gamow-Teller strength and lepton captures rates on 66-71Ni in stellar matter

## Key claim
The deformed pn-QRPA model produces electron capture rates on neutron-rich nickel isotopes (66-71Ni) that are up to two orders of magnitude larger than the previous independent-particle-model calculations of Pruet and Fuller, specifically at low stellar densities and high temperatures. At higher temperatures, lepton capture rates dominate over lepton emission rates and may be the only significant weak-rate contribution.

## Method
The authors employed the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson single-particle basis, BCS pairing, and separable particle-hole and particle-particle Gamow-Teller forces. They calculated Gamow-Teller charge-changing transition strength distributions for both the beta-decay (GT-) and electron-capture (GT+) directions for 66-71Ni, using a quenching factor of 0.6, and then computed stellar electron capture and positron capture rates over densities from 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3 and temperatures from 0.01x10^9 K to 30x10^9 K, including contributions from parent excited states via Boltzmann occupation factors.

## Key numbers
- Electron capture rates on 66-71Ni at low stellar densities (10 g/cm^3) and high temperatures are larger than the Pruet and Fuller (2003) calculations by up to **two orders of magnitude**.
- The quenching factor used for Gamow-Teller strength was **f_q = 0.6**, consistent with previous RPA calculations for 54Fe.
- The particle-hole interaction strength was set to **chi = 4.2/A MeV** (with A the mass number), and the particle-particle strength to **kappa = 0.10 MeV** for all isotopes.
- Pairing gaps were taken as **Delta_n = Delta_p = 12/sqrt(A) MeV**.
- The constant D in the ft-value relation was taken as **6295 s** and the axial-vector to vector coupling ratio as **g_A/g_V = -1.254**.
- Calculations included up to **7 major oscillator shells** and considered approximately **100 states in daughter nuclei** up to excitation energy of about **45 MeV**.
- The Ikeda sum rule was re-normalized as **ISR_re-norm = 3 f_q^2 (N-Z)** and was satisfied in the calculation.
- At T_9 = 30 (30x10^9 K), the pn-QRPA electron capture rates are greater by an order of magnitude compared to Pruet and Fuller rates.
- For 68Ni at high density and low temperature, the Pruet and Fuller electron capture values are an order of magnitude larger than the pn-QRPA values.

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of Gamow-Teller strength and weak rates on nickel isotopes from 56Ni (Nabi, 2005) through 57-65Ni (Nabi, 2012) to the neutron-rich isotopes 66-71Ni, filling a gap in the mass range. It also complements earlier work on 72-78Ni (Nabi, 2014) and first-forbidden transitions in even-even 72,74,76,78Ni (Nabi, 2016). The results are compared directly with the independent-particle-model calculations of Pruet and Fuller (2003), which followed the same approach as the foundational Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN, 1980) calculations. The enhanced capture rates reported here may impact simulations of presupernova evolution and core collapse, particularly the lepton-to-baryon ratio (Y_e) and shock wave energetics, and the authors note that the large model space and avoidance of the Brink-Axel hypothesis (used by Pruet and Fuller) are key reasons for the differences.
