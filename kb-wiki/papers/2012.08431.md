# Theoretical calculation of nuclear reactions of interest for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) predicts the abundances of the light elements in the early universe. Even if the overall agreement with the experimental data is good, still some discrepancies exist on the relic abundances of ${}^7$Li and ${}^6$Li. In order to exclude or confirm these scenarios, the BBN model needs precise input parameters, in particular the cross-sections of the BBN nuclear reaction network. However, the suppression of the cross-sections due to the Coulomb barrier makes the measurement very difficult and so affected by large systematic errors. Therefore, reliable theoretical calculations result fundamental in order to reduce the uncertainties. In this work we present a theoretical study of two nuclear reactions connected to ${}^6$Li abundance and recently the $α$+d$\rightarrow$ ${}^6$Li + $γ$ and the p+${}^6$Li$\rightarrow$${}^7$Be+$γ$ radiative captures. For the first reaction we use a so-called ab-initio approach in which we solve the full six-body problem by using realistic nuclear potentials to describe the nucleon interactions. In particular we concentrate on the calculation and characterization of the final state of the reaction, the ${}^6$Li ground state, focusing on the electromagnetic static structure and the quantities relevant from the astrophysical point of view such as the asymptotic normalization coefficient. For doing this we use the Hyperspherical Harmonic approach developed by the Pisa group providing for the first time the possibility of using this approach beyond A = 4 nuclear systems. The second reaction is instead studied by using a two-body cluster approach where the proton and ${}^6$Li are considered as structureless particles. The angular distribution of the emitted photon obtained in this work were used by the LUNA Collaboration to determine the efficiency of the detector used in the measurement of the reaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical study of two nuclear reactions relevant to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) and the lithium abundance problem: the α+d→⁶Li+γ radiative capture and the p+⁶Li→⁷Be+γ radiative capture. For the first reaction, the author uses an ab-initio approach solving the full six-body problem with realistic nuclear potentials, but the work concentrates specifically on calculating and characterizing the ⁶Li ground state rather than the full reaction cross-section, while the second reaction is studied using a simpler two-body cluster approach where the proton and ⁶Li are treated as structureless particles.

## Method

For the α+d→⁶Li+γ reaction, the author employs the Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) method developed by the Pisa group to solve the Schrödinger equation for the six-nucleon bound state of ⁶Li, expanding the wave function in terms of HH basis states with definite total angular momentum, spin, isospin, and parity, and using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle to determine the coefficients. For the p+⁶Li→⁷Be+γ reaction, a two-body cluster approach is used where the proton and ⁶Li are considered structureless particles, and the angular distribution of the emitted photon is calculated.

## Key numbers

- The number of independent antisymmetric states for the ⁶Li ground state (J=1, T=0, π=+) at grand angular quantum number K=10 is 509 for L=0, S=1; 1535 for L=2, S=1; 804 for L=2, S=2; 145 for L=2, S=3; 615 for L=1, S=0; 1077 for L=1, S=1; and 597 for L=1, S=2.
- The hyperradial functions use Laguerre polynomials with a non-variational parameter γ in the range 3.5–5.5 fm⁻¹.
- The total number of even permutations for the six-particle system is 360.
- For K=0 and LSTJπ=0101+, there is no independent state due to the Pauli principle.

## Context

This work extends the Hyperspherical Harmonic approach beyond A=4 nuclear systems, which was previously limited to smaller nuclei as developed by the Pisa group (Kievsky, 2008). The calculation of the photon angular distribution for the p+⁶Li→⁷Be+γ reaction was used by the LUNA Collaboration to determine the efficiency of their detector. The study addresses the longstanding discrepancy in BBN predictions for ⁶Li and ⁷Li abundances, where precise theoretical cross-sections are needed because experimental measurements are suppressed by the Coulomb barrier and affected by large systematic errors.
