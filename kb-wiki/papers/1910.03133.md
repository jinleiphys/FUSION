# Time-Reversal-Invariance Violation in the $N\!d$ System and Large-$N_C$

> A minimal set of five low energy constants (LECs) for time-reversal and parity violating ($\not{T}\!\!\!\not{P}$) nucleon-nucleon ($N\!N$) interactions at low energies ($E\!<\!m_π^2/M_N$) is given. Using a large-$N_C$ (number of colors in QCD) analysis we show that one linear combination of LECs is $\mathcal{O}(N_C)$, three LECs are $\mathcal{O}(N_C^{0})$, and one linear combination of LECs is $\mathcal{O}(N_C^{-1})$. We also calculate the $\not{T}\!\!\!\not{P}$ observables of neutron spin rotation through a polarized deuteron target and a spin correlation coefficient in nucleon-deuteron scattering using pionless effective field theory. Using the large-$N_C$ analysis we show that the spin correlation coefficient and the neutron spin rotation are predominantly determined by same two LECs in the large-$N_C$ basis.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, using a large-\(N_C\) analysis, the five low-energy constants (LECs) describing time-reversal and parity violating nucleon-nucleon interactions at low energies have a hierarchy: one linear combination is \(\mathcal{O}(N_C)\), three LECs are \(\mathcal{O}(N_C^0)\), and one linear combination is \(\mathcal{O}(N_C^{-1})\); this hierarchy implies that the spin correlation coefficient and neutron spin rotation in nucleon-deuteron scattering are predominantly determined by the same two LECs in the large-\(N_C\) basis, but this conclusion is contingent on the validity of the large-\(N_C\) expansion and holds only to leading order in pionless effective field theory.

## Method

The authors constructed a minimal set of five low-energy constants (LECs) for time-reversal and parity violating nucleon-nucleon interactions using pionless effective field theory (EFT(\(/\!\!\!\pi\))). They performed a large-\(N_C\) analysis of QCD to determine the scaling of these LECs with the number of colors, relating the LECs in the dibaryon formalism to those in the partial wave and Girlanda bases via Fierz transformations. They then calculated the time-reversal violating observables of neutron spin rotation through a polarized deuteron target and a spin correlation coefficient in nucleon-deuteron scattering by solving the leading-order integral equations for the scattering amplitude in a total angular momentum basis.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding momentum used to fit two-body parameters is \(\gamma_t = 45.7025\) MeV.
* The \(^1S_0\) virtual bound state momentum used to fit two-body parameters is \(\gamma_s = -7.890\) MeV.
* The LO three-body force is fit to the \(^2S_{1/2}\) \(nd\) scattering length \(a_{nd} = 0.65\) fm.
* For the spin correlation coefficient \(A_{xy}\) at a nucleon lab energy of 1 MeV, the coefficient for the \(\mathcal{O}(N_C^0)\) LEC \(\bar{g}_2^{(N_C^0)}\) is 118 MeV, while the coefficient for the \(\mathcal{O}(N_C^0)\) LEC \(\bar{g}_3^{(N_C^0)}\) is -22.4 MeV.
* For the spin correlation coefficient \(A_{xy}\) at a nucleon lab energy of 5 MeV, the coefficient for the \(\mathcal{O}(N_C)\) LEC \(\bar{g}_1^{(N_C)}\) is 79.6 MeV, and the coefficient for the \(\mathcal{O}(N_C^{-1})\) LEC \(\bar{g}_5^{(N_C^{-1})}\) is 181 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of time-reversal violating observables in the neutron-deuteron system by Song et al. (2011), which used a hybrid method combining phenomenological strong potentials (AV18+UIX) with pionless EFT for the time-reversal violating interactions, by instead performing a fully consistent pionless EFT calculation. The large-\(N_C\) analysis is analogous to that done in the parity-violating sector by Schindler et al. (2015) and builds on the general large-\(N_C\) analysis of time-reversal violating nucleon-nucleon operators by Samart et al. (2016). The results enable future next-to-leading order calculations in pionless EFT and provide a framework for disentangling contributions from different beyond-the-Standard-Model CP-violating operators through measurements of light nuclear electric dipole moments and few-nucleon scattering observables.
