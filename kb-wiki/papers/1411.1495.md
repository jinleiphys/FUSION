# Pion, Kaon and Antiproton Production in $Pb+Pb$ Collisions at LHC Energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV : A Model-based Analysis

> Large Hadron Collider (LHC) had produced a vast amount of high precision data for high energy heavy ion collision. We attempt here to study (i) transverse momenta spectra, (ii) $K/π$, $p/π$ ratio behaviours, (iii)rapidity distribution, and (iv) the nuclear modification factors of the pion, kaon and antiproton produced in $p+p$ and $Pb+Pb$ collisions at energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV, on the basis of Sequential Chain Model (SCM). Comparisons of the model-based results with the measured data on these observables are generally found to be modestly satisfactory.

## Key claim

The Sequential Chain Model (SCM), which treats protons as bound states of pions and a spectator particle and has no QGP-tag, can reproduce the transverse momentum spectra, particle ratios, rapidity distributions, and nuclear modification factors for pions, kaons, and antiprotons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy with "modestly satisfactory" agreement, though the model shows systematic disagreements at very low pT and high pT depending on the observable.

## Method

The authors apply the Sequential Chain Model (SCM), in which high-energy hadronic interactions are reduced to pion-pion interactions, with protons represented as p = (pi+ pi0 theta). They compute inclusive cross-sections for pi-, K-, and antiproton production in pp collisions using field-theoretical expressions with Feynman diagram techniques, then transform these to Pb+Pb collisions using a wounded-nucleon scaling factor and an energy-degradation factor. The model parameters (alpha, beta, and the constituent rearrangement exponent N_R) are fitted to experimental data from CMS and ALICE for pp and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- The average pion multiplicity in pp collisions is given by <n_pi+>_pp ~ <n_pi->_pp ~ <n_pi0>_pp ~ 1.1 s^(1/5), where s is the square of the center-of-mass energy.
- The average kaon multiplicity in pp collisions is given by <n_K+>_pp ~ <n_K->_pp ~ <n_K0>_pp ~ <n_anti-K0>_pp ~ 5 x 10^(-2) s^(1/4).
- The average antiproton multiplicity in pp collisions is given by <n_antip>_pp ~ <n_p>_pp ~ 2 x 10^(-2) s^(1/4).
- The inelastic AB cross-section is parameterized as sigma_AB = sigma_0 (A^(1/3)_projectile + A^(1/3)_target - delta)^2 with sigma_0 = 68.8 mb and delta = 1.32.
- The antiproton mass used in the calculation is m_antip ~ 938 MeV/c^2.
- The SCM-based rapidity distribution for pions in central Pb+Pb collisions is given by dN/dy = 1895 exp(-0.025 sinh y_cm).
- The nuclear modification factors for central Pb+Pb collisions are parameterized as: (R_AA)_pi = 0.35 pT^(0.33), (R_AA)_K = 0.40 pT^(0.73), and (R_AA)_antip = 0.25 pT^(1.33).

## Context

This work extends the Sequential Chain Model, previously applied by Guptaroy and collaborators to lower-energy collisions, to LHC energies for the first time. It provides an alternative to standard QGP-based interpretations of heavy-ion data, such as those from ALICE (2013) and CMS (2012), and contrasts with PYTHIA-based results shown in the same figures. The model enables a non-QGP description of particle production in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, though the authors note that the exact nature of the QGP-hadron phase transition remains uncertain, citing Zhang (year not given) and others.
