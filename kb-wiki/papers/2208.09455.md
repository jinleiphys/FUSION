# Continuum random-phase approximation for (n,γ) reactions on neutron-rich nuclei: collective effects and resonances

> We formulate a microscopic theory to calculate cross section of the radiative neutron capture reaction on neutron-rich nuclei using the continuum random-phase approximation (cRPA) to the time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT). With an intension of applying to the r-process, for which the statistical reaction model may not be appropriate, we describe the transition between a initial state of incident neutron and a final state of the gamma decay by means of a single many-body framework of the cRPA-TDDFT. With the cRPA approach, it is possible to describe various excitation modes present in the $(n,γ)$ reaction, including soft dipole excitation, the giant resonances as well as non-collective excitations and the single-particle resonances. Furthermore, it enables us to describe the $(n,γ)$ reaction where the final states of the gamma transition are low-lying surface vibrational states. We demonstrate the theory by performing numerical calculation for the reaction $^{139}{\rm Sn} (n,γ) ^{140}{\rm Sn}$. We discuss various new features which are beyond the single-particle model; presence of narrow and wide resonances originating from non-collective and collective excitations and roles of low-lying quadrupole and octupole vibrational states.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a microscopic theory for radiative neutron capture on neutron-rich nuclei using the continuum random-phase approximation (cRPA) to time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT), which can describe collective effects beyond the single-particle model. The authors claim this approach is particularly relevant for r-process nuclei where the statistical compound-nucleus model may not be appropriate due to small neutron separation energies (approximately 2 MeV).

## Method

The authors extended a previous cRPA-TDDFT formulation to describe (n,γ) reactions where the final state of the gamma transition is a low-lying excited state (such as surface vibrational states), not just the ground state. They used the Zangwill-Soven method to define the T-matrix for photo-absorption followed by particle emission, then obtained the (n,γ) cross section for the inverse process using the reciprocity theorem (detailed balance). Numerical calculations were performed for the reaction ¹³⁹Sn(n,γ)¹⁴⁰Sn using a Woods-Saxon potential with a Skyrme-type contact interaction as the residual two-body force.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of ¹⁴⁰Sn: approximately 3 MeV (estimated)
- Highest occupied neutron orbit in ¹⁴⁰Sn: 2f₇/₂ with energy -2.59 MeV (Fermi energy)
- Excitation energies of low-lying states in ¹⁴⁰Sn: 2⁺₁ = 0.888 MeV, 2⁺₂ = 1.093 MeV, 3⁻₁ = 1.768 MeV
- Single-particle energies of neutron orbits near Fermi energy: 2f₅/₂ at -0.31 MeV, 3p₁/₂ at -0.81 MeV, 3p₃/₂ at -1.46 MeV, 1h₉/₂ at -1.53 MeV
- RPA forward amplitudes for 2⁺₁ state: (1h₉/₂)(2f₇/₂)⁻¹ = -0.601, (3p₃/₂)(2f₇/₂)⁻¹ = 0.789
- RPA forward amplitudes for 2⁺₂ state: (1h₉/₂)(2f₇/₂)⁻¹ = 0.791, (3p₃/₂)(2f₇/₂)⁻¹ = 0.600
- RPA forward amplitude for 3⁻₁ state: (1i₁₃/₂)(2f₇/₂)⁻¹ = 0.831 (neutron configuration)
- Maximal radius for numerical calculation: 20 fm (with interval 0.2 fm)
- Small real constant η in response equation: 1.0 × 10⁻⁵ or 0.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous prototype formulation (Matsuo 2015) which only described gamma transitions to the ground state, and builds on their extended linear response theory (Saito 2021) for photo-absorption transitions from low-lying excited states. The approach addresses limitations of both the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model (Hauser 1952), which is inappropriate for r-process nuclei with small neutron separation energy, and direct capture models (Lane 1960, Mathews 1983) which cannot include collective correlations such as pairing, low-lying collective states, and giant resonances. The method enables future inclusion of pairing correlation effects and application to r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
