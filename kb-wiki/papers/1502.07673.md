# Conservation laws in the $1f_{7/2}$ shell model of $^{48}$Cr

> Conservation laws in the $1f_{7/2}$ shell model of $^{48}$Cr found in numeric studies by Escuderos, Zamick and Bayman [A. Escuderos, L. Zamick, and B. F. Bayman, arXiv:0506050 (2005)] and me [K. Neergård, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{90}, 014318 (2014)] are explained by symmetry under particle-hole conjugation and the structure of the irreps of the symplectic group Sp(4). A generalization is discussed.

## Key claim

The conservation of the Sp(8) irrep \(vt=61\) in \(^{48}\)Cr for angular momentum and isospin \(I=T=0\), previously observed numerically without explanation, is shown to follow from the conservation of the parity of \((v_n+v_p)/2\) under particle-hole conjugation in a midshell nucleus, combined with the structure of Sp(4) irreps and Helmers duality.

## Method

The author derives the decomposition of Sp(4) irreps into direct sums of irreps of Sp(2)\(_n\)\(\times\)Sp(2)\(_p\) (the quasispin groups for neutrons and protons), giving a rule for the range of quasispin pairs \((\mathcal{S}_n,\mathcal{S}_p)\) that appear. Particle-hole conjugation is reviewed and shown to conserve the parity of \((v_n+v_p)/2\) in the midshell (\(n_n=n_p=\Omega\)) space for any rotationally and time-reversal invariant one-plus-two-body Hamiltonian. For \(^{48}\)Cr in the \(1f_{7/2}\) shell (\(j=7/2\), \(\Omega=4\)), the weight diagrams of the Sp(4) irreps corresponding to the Sp(8) irreps \(vt=00,40,80\) contain only even \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p\), while \(vt=61\) contains only odd \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p\); thus the parity conservation law forces \(vt=61\) to be conserved. The analysis is extended to list all \((I,T)\) subspaces for \(j=3/2,5/2,7/2\) where this mechanism applies.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{48}\)Cr in the \(1f_{7/2}\) shell (\(j=7/2\), \(\Omega=4\)), the Sp(8) irrep \(vt=61\) is conserved for \(I=T=0\).
* The Sp(4) irrep \([\lambda\mu]\) has row lengths \(\lambda = \Omega - \tfrac12 v + t\) and \(\mu = \Omega - \tfrac12 v - t\).
* For \(j=7/2\), the Sp(8) irreps present in the \(n_n=n_p=\Omega=4\), \(I=T=0\) subspace are \(vt=00\), \(40\), \(61\), and \(80\).
* The condition for constant parity of \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p\) within a Sp(4) irrep is either \(t=0\) (giving even \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p\)) or \(v/2+t=\Omega\) (giving \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p = t\), with parity determined by \(t\)).
* Tables list all \((I,T)\) subspaces for \(j=3/2\), \(5/2\), and \(7/2\) where states with even and odd \(\mathcal{S}_n+\mathcal{S}_p\) (equivalently even and odd \((v_n+v_p)/2\)) do not mix; for \(j=7/2\) these include e.g. \(I=0,T=0\) (even: 00, 40\(^2\), 80; odd: 61\(^2\)) and \(I=0,T=1\) (odd: 61\(^2\)).

## Context

This work explains numerical observations by Escuderos, Zamick, and Bayman (2005) that states with opposite parities of \((v_n+v_p)/2\) did not mix in \(^{48}\)Cr, and by Neergård (2014) that the Sp(8) irrep \(vt=61\) was conserved for \(I=T=0\). It extends the known particle-hole conjugation symmetry in midshell nuclei (Condon and Shortley 1935, Racah 1942) by connecting it to the symplectic group classification of Flowers (1952) and the Helmers duality (1960). The results enable systematic identification of all \((I,T)\) subspaces in \(j=3/2\)–\(7/2\) shells where analogous conservation laws hold, and provide a group-theoretic explanation for the numerical dimensions observed by Escuderos, Zamick, and Bayman.
