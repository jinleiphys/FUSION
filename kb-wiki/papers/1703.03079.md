# Sound waves and vortices in a polarized relativistic fluid

> We extend the effective theory approach to the ideal fluid limit where the polarization of the fluid is non-zero. After describing and motivating the equations of motion, we expand them around the hydrostatic limit, obtaining the sound wave and vortex degrees of freedom. We discuss how the presence of polarization affects the stability and causality of the ideal fluid limit.

## Key claim

The paper extends the effective field theory approach to ideal relativistic hydrodynamics to include non-zero polarization, but finds that the resulting linearized equations of motion for sound waves and vortices are generally acausal and unstable (via Ostrogradski's theorem) unless polarization effects are very soft, suggesting that a stable ideal hydrodynamic limit may not exist for polarized fluids and that relaxation dynamics must be introduced even in the "ideal" regime.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for a polarized relativistic fluid by introducing a polarization tensor \(y^{\mu\nu}\) as an auxiliary field, coupling it to the standard comoving coordinate fields \(\phi^I\) via a modified free energy \(F(b, y) = F(b \times f(y))\) where \(f(y) = 1 - c y_{\mu\nu} y^{\mu\nu}\). They derive the Euler-Lagrange equations of motion, linearize them around a hydrostatic background, and decompose the perturbations into longitudinal (sound) and transverse (vortex) modes to obtain their dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

* The free part of the linearized Lagrangian contains constants \(A = T_0 F'(b_0)\), \(B = A c \chi^2(b_0,0)\), and \(C = \frac{1}{2} b_0^2 F''(b_0)\), where \(T_0 \sim b_0^{1/3}\) is the background "temperature" scale.
* The longitudinal (sound) dispersion relation is \(4B w_L^4 - A w_L^2 + 2C k^2 = 0\), with solutions \(w_L^2 = \frac{A}{8B} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{A}{8B}\right)^2 - \frac{C k^2}{2B}}\).
* The transverse (vortex) dispersion relation is \(4B w_T^4 - 4B k^2 w_T^2 - A w_T^2 = 0\), with solutions \(w_T = 0\) and \(w_T^2 = \frac{A}{4B} + k^2\).
* The condition for real longitudinal frequencies is \(A^2 - 32 B C k^2 \ge 0\), which is always satisfied for small momenta but may fail at high momenta depending on the sign of \(BC\).
* For parameter values \(A/B\) and \(C/B\) significantly above \(10^{-2}\), the maximum phase velocity \(w/k\) and group velocity significantly exceed the causal limit (speed of light).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to relativistic hydrodynamics developed by Nicolis et al. (2011-2015) and Torrieri et al. (2012-2015) to include polarization, motivated by experimental observations of \(\Lambda\) polarization in heavy ion collisions (Upsal, 2016) and theoretical work on spin-vorticity coupling (Becattini et al., 2013-2015; Csernai et al., 2013). It contradicts the assumption that ideal hydrodynamics with polarization can be a stable, causal effective theory, and suggests that a relaxation-time equation (analogous to Israel-Stewart theory for viscosity) is necessary even in the non-dissipative regime, potentially providing a quantitative lower bound on dissipation for fluids with spin.
