# Progress in the determination of Mellin moments of the pion LCDA using the HOPE method

> The pion light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) is a central non-perturbative object of interest for the calculation of high-energy exclusive processes in quantum chromodynamics. In this article, we discuss the calculation of the second and fourth Mellin moment of the pion LCDA using a heavy-quark operator product expansion. The resulting value for the second Mellin moment is $ \langle{ ξ^2 }\rangle(μ= 2~\text{GeV})= 0.210 \pm 0.013\text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.034\text{ (sys.)}$. This result is compatible with those from previous determinations of this quantity.

## Key claim

The second Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude is determined as ⟨ξ²⟩(μ = 2 GeV) = 0.210 ± 0.036, where the uncertainty is dominated by systematic effects from higher-twist terms and the continuum extrapolation, and the result is compatible with previous determinations of this quantity.

## Method

The authors use a heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method on quenched lattice QCD ensembles, constructing ratios of three-point correlators and analyzing them in both time-momentum and momentum-space representations. Two separate analyses are performed to control systematics: a time-momentum analysis that performs continuous inverse Fourier transforms of the OPE and fits the pion decay constant and heavy quark mass from the imaginary part before extracting ⟨ξ²⟩ from the real part, and a momentum-space analysis that performs discrete Fourier transforms and continuum extrapolations before fitting to the HOPE formula. A global fit removes discretization and higher-twist artifacts using the functional form ⟨ξ²⟩(a, m_Ψ) = ⟨ξ²⟩ + A/m_Ψ + B a² + C a² m_Ψ + D a² m_Ψ².

## Key numbers

- ⟨ξ²⟩(μ = 2 GeV) = 0.210 ± 0.013 (stat.) ± 0.034 (sys.) = 0.210 ± 0.036 (total, excluding quenching)
- Systematic error budget: discretization 0.016, higher-twist 0.025, unphysical pion mass (m_π ≈ 550 MeV) 0.014, higher loops 0.008, excited states 0.002, fit range 0.002
- Fourth Mellin moment exploratory result: ⟨ξ⁴⟩ ∼ 0.15 with approximately 50% statistical error, from data at one lattice spacing (a = 0.0813 fm) and two heavy quark masses (κ_heavy = 0.1200, 0.097)
- Lattice ensemble: 24³ × 48, β = 6.10050, a = 0.0813 fm, 3150 configurations
- Hadronic boost for fourth moment analysis: |p| ∼ 1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method introduced by Detmold (2020) and Detmold (2021) for extracting Mellin moments of the pion LCDA, and the results are compatible with previous determinations from Del Debbio (2003), Arthur (2011), and Bali (2019). The approach enables further investigations of the pion LCDA including dynamical studies of the second moment, determination of higher Mellin moments, and application to other light-cone quantities such as the kaon LCDA and pion PDF and helicity PDF. The success of the method also suggests it can be applied to study other light-cone quantities beyond the pion LCDA.
