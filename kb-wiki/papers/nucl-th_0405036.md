# Energy Systematics of Jet Tomography at RHIC: sqrt{s} = 62.4 vs 200 AGeV

> The collision energy dependence of jet tomography is investigated within the GLV formalism. The emphasis is on estimating systematic uncertainties resulting from the interplay of energy loss fluctuations and the rapid increase of the parton transverse momentum slopes as sqrt{s} decreases from 200 to 62 AGeV.

## Key claim

The collision energy dependence of jet tomography is investigated within the GLV formalism, with the emphasis on estimating systematic uncertainties resulting from the interplay of energy loss fluctuations and the rapid increase of the parton transverse momentum slopes as sqrt(s) decreases from 200 to 62 AGeV; the authors note that their calculations neglect k_T smearing, Cronin enhancement, gluon and quark shadowing, and nonperturbative baryon dynamical contributions, limiting applicability to p_T above 4-5 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear modification factor R_AA(p_T, y=0, sqrt(s)) for neutral pions in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=62.4 and 200 AGeV using the GLV formalism for radiative energy loss. They compare two simplified forms of the energy loss fluctuation distribution, a "uniform" distribution and a "squeezed" distribution, to assess systematic uncertainties in the predicted quenching factors. The mean energy loss fraction for gluons at 200 AGeV is fitted to PHENIX data in the p_T ~ 8 GeV region, and the energy dependence of the mean energy loss is scaled according to the produced gluon rapidity density dN_g/dy from PHOBOS multiplicity systematics.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, the fitted mean gluon energy loss fraction using the squeezed distribution is 0.65 < bar_epsilon_g(200) < 0.76.
- At sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, the fitted mean gluon energy loss fraction using the uniform distribution is 0.70 < bar_epsilon_g(200) < 0.80, corresponding to mean quark energy loss fraction 0.31 < bar_epsilon_q(200) < 0.36.
- The gluon rapidity density at 200 AGeV is approximately dN_g/dy(200) ~ 1000.
- The total charged particle rapidity density at 62.4 AGeV from PHOBOS is dN_tot/dy ~ 650-770.
- The predicted mean gluon energy loss fraction at 62.4 AGeV using the squeezed distribution is 0.42 < bar_epsilon(62.4) < 0.49 for dN_g/dy=650 and 0.50 < bar_epsilon(62.4) < 0.59 for dN_g/dy=770.
- The predicted mean gluon energy loss fraction at 62.4 AGeV using the uniform distribution is 0.46 < bar_epsilon_g(62) < 0.52 for dN_g/dy=650 and 0.54 < bar_epsilon_g(62) < 0.62 for dN_g/dy=770.
- The K-factor and Q^2 values used for pQCD fits are: K=2.0, Q^2=p_T^2 for 200 GeV; K=2.5, Q^2=0.5 p_T^2 for 62.4 GeV; K=3.5, Q^2=0.5 p_T^2 for 53.1 GeV; K=4.5, Q^2=0.5 p_T^2 for 30.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the GLV formalism (Gyulassy 2003, Gyulassy 2001) for jet tomography to study the energy dependence of the nuclear modification factor at RHIC, comparing predictions at sqrt(s)=62.4 and 200 AGeV. Previous predictions for 62 AGeV have been published by Wang (2003) and Vitev (2004), and the authors note that their results are consistent with Vitev (2004) in the p_T region 4-6 GeV, with differences attributed to the inclusion of Cronin interactions and k_T smearing in that work. The study enables future tomographic inversion of single and di-jet quenching by highlighting the need to account for the rapid increase of parton transverse momentum slopes at lower energies, and suggests that identified particle ratios at p_T > 5 GeV could help constrain the form of energy loss fluctuations.
