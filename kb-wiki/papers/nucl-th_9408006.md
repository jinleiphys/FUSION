# Inclusive Aand Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering at Cebaf at Higher Energies

> We summarize the discussion on the possibilities of doing inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering experiments at CEBAF with beam energy of the order of 10 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an upgrade of the CEBAF beam energy to about 10 GeV would provide only a relatively limited access to the region of inclusive deep inelastic scattering and even less to the region where semi-inclusive deep inelastic processes factorize, but that such an upgrade would nonetheless allow addressing fundamental questions about the valence quark structure of hadrons.

## Method

The authors summarize workshop discussions on the kinematic domain accessible at CEBAF with beam energies of 8-12 GeV, using the standard deep inelastic scattering variables (momentum transfer Q², energy transfer ν, Bjorken x, and invariant mass W). They evaluate the feasibility of inclusive and semi-inclusive measurements, including polarized structure functions, parity violation experiments, higher twist structure functions, and tagged structure functions in nuclei, based on the constraints Q > 1-2 GeV and W ≥ 2 GeV for deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The accessible kinematic range for deep inelastic scattering at CEBAF is 1 ≤ Q² ≤ 10 GeV² and 0.1 ≤ x ≤ 1 for scattering on a nucleon.
- The lower boundary for deep inelastic scattering requires momentum transfer Q > 1 to 2 GeV, corresponding to a spatial resolution λ ≈ 1/Q = 0.1-0.2 fm.
- The invariant mass W must satisfy W ≥ 2 GeV to be above the region of discrete baryon resonances.
- For the proposed neutron spin structure measurement, a 40 cm long high-pressure polarized ³He target with a 15 μA electron beam yields a luminosity of 10³⁶ /cm²/s.
- The proposed parity violation experiment targets x = 0.5, y = 0.5.
- For semi-inclusive measurements, the paper states that an energy of 10 GeV is too low for clear factorization of target and current fragmentation regions.

## Context

This work extends discussions from the Pegasys proposal and the ELFE proposal, and references the review of Sloan, Smadja and Voss (1990) for deep inelastic studies. The paper summarizes workshop contributions from A. Mueller, A. Schaefer, N. Bianchi, X. Ji, J.-M. Laget, P. Markowitz, W. Melnitchouk, Z.-E. Meziani, J. Milana, P. Mulders, S. Simula, P. Souder and M. Strikman. It identifies the transition region between inclusive deep inelastic scattering and exclusive resonance excitation as the most important domain for CEBAF, enabling connections between effective hadronic theories, quark models, and quantum chromodynamics.
