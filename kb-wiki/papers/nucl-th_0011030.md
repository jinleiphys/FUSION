# A complete set of total cross sections for imaginary parts of nd forward scattering amplitudes and three-nucleon force effects

> In the neutron-deuteron scattering, four total cross sections are shown to form a complete set for the determination of the imaginary parts of the forward amplitudes because of the optical theorem. The amplitudes are decomposed into scalar and tensor ones in the spin space. Contributions of three-nucleon forces (3NF) to these amplitudes are studied, by the Faddeev calculation, which predicts significant tensor effects of the 3NF.

## Key claim

Four total cross sections in neutron-deuteron scattering form a complete set for determining the imaginary parts of all forward scattering amplitudes via the optical theorem, and Faddeev calculations predict that one tensor amplitude (W_T) is particularly sensitive to three-nucleon force (3NF) effects, though this sensitivity is most appreciable at higher incident energies rather than at very low energies.

## Method

The authors decompose the forward nd scattering matrix into four independent amplitudes (M1–M4) and relate them via the optical theorem to four measurable total cross sections: the unpolarized cross section σ₀^tot, the longitudinal and transverse vector-polarized cross section asymmetries Δσ_L and Δσ_T, and the tensor-polarized cross section σ₂₀^tot. They further decompose these amplitudes into scalar (U1, U3) and tensor (T1, T3) invariant amplitudes, and into operator-form amplitudes (S0, S_σ, W_D, W_T) that connect directly to nuclear force components. Faddeev calculations in coordinate space are performed with the Argonne V18 two-nucleon force (2NF) plus either the 2π-exchange Brazil 3NF (BR-3NF) or a fictitious Gaussian spin-independent 3NF (GS-3NF), with partial waves truncated at total NN angular momenta j ≤ 2 and total 3N angular momentum J ≤ 19/2.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized total cross section σ₀^tot is given by σ₀^tot = (α/3) Im(M1 + M2 + M4), where α = 4π/k with k the nd relative momentum.
- The longitudinal asymmetry Δσ_L = -α Im(M1 – M2) and the transverse asymmetry Δσ_T = -α√2 Im(M3).
- The tensor-polarized cross section σ₂₀^tot = (α/(3√2)) Im(M1 + M2 – 2M4).
- The operator-form amplitudes satisfy: α Im(S0) = σ₀^tot, α Im(S_σ) = -(1/3)(Δσ_L + 2Δσ_T), α Im(W_D) = (3/√2)σ₂₀^tot, and α Im(W_T) = -(Δσ_L – Δσ_T).
- Faddeev calculations are performed for neutron incident energies up to 15 MeV.
- The GS-3NF uses r_G = 1.0 fm and V₀^G = -45 MeV, adjusted to reproduce the empirical triton binding energy.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon systems that found 2NF alone deficient in reproducing observables (Glöckle 1996), with improvements from 2π-exchange 3NF for binding energy (Sasakawa 1986) and pd elastic cross section minima (Witała 1998), but failures for polarization observables like proton vector analyzing power (Stephenson 1999) and deuteron tensor analyzing powers (Sakamoto 2000). The paper provides a systematic framework to extract spin-dependent information from total cross sections, enabling experimental tests of 3NF tensor components that are inaccessible through differential cross sections alone. The predicted sensitivity of W_T to 3NF, distinct from the doublet scalar amplitude U1 which correlates with triton binding energy (Friar 1984, Ishikawa 1999), motivates measurements of the four total cross sections to isolate tensor 3NF effects.
