# Roper State from Overlap Fermions

> The Roper state is extracted with valence overlap fermions on a $2+1$-flavor domain-wall fermion lattice (spacing $a = 0.114$ fm and $m_π = 330$ MeV) using both the Sequential Empirical Bayes (SEB) method and the variational method. The results are consistent, provided that a large smearing-size interpolation operator is included in the variational calculation to have better overlap with the lowest radial excitation. Similar calculations carried out for an anisotropic clover lattice with similar parameters find the Roper $\approx 280$ MeV higher than that of the overlap fermion. The fact that the prediction of the Roper state by overlap fermions is consistently lower than those of clover fermions, chirally improved fermions, and twisted-mass fermions over a wide range of pion masses has been dubbed a "Roper puzzle." To understand the origin of this difference, we study the hairpin $Z$-diagram in the isovector scalar meson ($a_0$) correlator in the quenched approximation. Comparing the $a_0$ correlators for clover and overlap fermions, at a pion mass of 290 MeV, we find that the spectral weight of the ghost state with clover fermions is smaller than that of the overlap at $a = 0.12$ fm and $0.09$ fm, whereas the whole $a_0$ correlators of clover and overlap at $a = 0.06$ fm coincide within errors. This suggests that chiral symmetry is restored for clover at $a \le 0.06$ fm and that the Roper should come down at and below this $a$. We conclude that this work supports a resolution of the "Roper puzzle" due to $Z$-graph type chiral dynamics. This entails coupling to higher components in the Fock space (e.g. $Nπ$, $Nππ$ states) to induce the effective flavor-spin interaction between quarks as prescribed in the chiral quark model, resulting in the parity-reversal pattern as observed in the experimental excited states of $N, Δ$ and $Λ$.

## Key claim

The Roper state extracted using overlap fermions is consistently lower than results from clover, chirally improved, and twisted-mass fermions across a wide range of pion masses; this work supports a resolution of the "Roper puzzle" through Z-graph type chiral dynamics, where the difference arises from the degree of chiral symmetry restoration at finite lattice spacing rather than from algorithmic differences between the Sequential Empirical Bayes (SEB) and variational methods.

## Method

The authors extracted the Roper state using valence overlap fermions on a 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion lattice (a = 0.114 fm, m_pi = 330 MeV) with both the Sequential Empirical Bayes (SEB) method and the variational method, including a large smearing-size interpolation operator. They also performed SEB and variational calculations on an anisotropic clover lattice (a_s = 0.12 fm, m_pi = 396 MeV) to compare results between fermion actions. To investigate the origin of the mass difference, they studied the hairpin Z-diagram in the isovector scalar meson (a_0) correlator in the quenched approximation, comparing clover and overlap fermions at three lattice spacings (a = 0.12 fm, 0.09 fm, and 0.06 fm) with a valence pion mass of 290 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Roper mass from overlap fermions: m_R = 1.64(9) GeV at the valence pion mass where clover fermions give m_R = 1.92(6) GeV, a difference of approximately 280 MeV
- Roper mass from overlap fermions at physical pion limit from chiral extrapolation: M_R = 1.40(11) GeV, in agreement with the experimental Roper N(1440) at about 1370 MeV
- Nucleon mass at physical pion limit from overlap fermions: M_N = 0.999(46) GeV
- On the anisotropic clover lattice, the SEB method gave the first positive-parity state at 1.87(13) GeV, while the variational method with large smearing (w = 0,4,7,11) gave 1.92(6) GeV, and with small smearing (w = 0,2,4) gave 2.19(11) GeV
- Overlap fermion variational calculation at m_pi = 343 MeV gave M_R = 1.55(10) GeV, consistent with the SEB value of 1.60(12) GeV
- The node in the Roper wavefunction occurs at R = 0.76 fm for m_pi = 633 MeV and at R = 0.9 fm for m_pi = 438 MeV
- The ratios of Wilson ghost-state magnitudes (correlator minima) at a = 0.12 fm and 0.09 fm are about half of those of overlap; at a = 0.06 fm the whole a_0 correlators coincide within errors

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD study of the Roper resonance, which has been controversial due to discrepancies between results from overlap fermions (Liu 2014, Liu 2016) and those from clover fermions (CSSM Collaboration 2012, JLab Collaboration 2011), twisted-mass fermions (Alexandrou 2013), and chirally improved fermions (BGR Collaboration 2013). The authors demonstrate that the SEB and variational methods yield consistent results when large smearing operators are included, resolving the algorithmic concern. The study of the ghost state in the quenched a_0 correlator suggests that chiral symmetry restoration for clover fermions occurs at a <= 0.06 fm, implying the Roper puzzle originates from finite-lattice-spacing effects related to chiral symmetry breaking. This work supports the chiral quark model picture where Z-graph type dynamics generate flavor-spin interactions, producing the parity-reversal pattern observed in experimental N, Delta, and Lambda excited states.
