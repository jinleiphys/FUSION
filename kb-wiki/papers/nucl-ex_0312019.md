# Search for a Theta++ Pentaquark State

> 9 pages, 11 figures. Original version is the contribution to proceedings of the "VIII International Conference on Hypernuclear & Strange Particle Physics", Jefferson Lab, Newport News, VA, USA, October 14-18, 2003. The revised version contains more details for clarification, additional comparisons with theoretical predictions and other experimental results with references to recent papers. All qualitative results are unchanged

## Key claim
The paper reports a candidate signal for a $\Theta^{++}$ pentaquark state at 1.59 GeV/c$^2$ in the reaction $\gamma$p$\rightarrow$K$^-\Theta^{++}$, but the analysis demonstrates that this peak can be dismissed as a reflection from $\phi$ production and hyperon-resonance production, leaving no evidence for the $\Theta^{++}$.

## Method
The CLAS Collaboration analyzed exclusive $\gamma$p$\rightarrow$K$^+$K$^-$p events from the g1a data set with photon energies E$_\gamma$ = 0.5–2.4 GeV. They applied a four-momentum conservation cut $|m_X-m_{(K^+,p)}| \le 35$ MeV/c$^2$ to select $\Theta^{++}$ candidates, then systematically removed events from $\phi$, $\Lambda(1520)$, and $\Lambda(1670)$ production to test whether the observed peak at 1.59 GeV/c$^2$ survived.

## Key numbers
- The candidate $\Theta^{++}$ signal appeared at mass $m_{\Theta^{++}} = 1.59$ GeV/c$^2$ with fitted width $\sigma_m = 0.01$ GeV/c$^2$ and $\Gamma_m \approx 0.025$ GeV/c$^2$.
- The photon beam energy threshold for the reaction $\gamma$p$\rightarrow$K$^-\Theta^{++}$ is E$_\gamma = 1.85$ GeV, assuming $m_{\Theta^{++}} = 1.59$ GeV/c$^2$.
- The four-momentum conservation cut used was $|m_X-m_{(K^+,p)}| \le 35$ MeV/c$^2$.
- After removing $\phi$, $\Lambda(1520)$, and $\Lambda(1670)$ events, no peak remained in the mass range 1.5–1.6 GeV/c$^2$, including at 1.54 GeV/c$^2$ or 1.59 GeV/c$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the search for pentaquark states following reports of the $\Theta^+$ (strangeness S=+1) by several experiments, with theoretical predictions from the chiral soliton approach (Diakonov 1997) and predictions of a pentaquark family including $\Theta^{++}$ (Capstick 2003, Bijnens 2003, Ellis 2004). The analysis contradicts the existence of an isotensor $\Theta^{++}$ state and supports the conclusion that the $\Theta^+$ is an isospin-0 isosinglet, consistent with results from Arndt (2003) and Aira (2003). The finding enables future searches to focus on isosinglet pentaquark interpretations rather than isotensor states.
