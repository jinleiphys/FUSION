# Nuclear shell-model simulation in digital quantum computers

> The nuclear shell model is one of the prime many-body methods to study the structure of atomic nuclei, but it is hampered by an exponential scaling on the basis size as the number of particles increases. We present a shell-model quantum circuit design strategy to find nuclear ground states by exploiting an adaptive variational quantum eigensolver algorithm. Our circuit implementation is in excellent agreement with classical shell-model simulations for a dozen of light and medium-mass nuclei, including neon and calcium isotopes. We quantify the circuit depth, width and number of gates to encode realistic shell-model wavefunctions. Our strategy also addresses explicitly energy measurements and the required number of circuits to perform them. Our simulated circuits approach the benchmark results exponentially with a polynomial scaling in quantum resources for each nucleus. This work paves the way for quantum computing shell-model studies across the nuclear chart and our quantum resource quantification may be used in configuration-interaction calculations of other fermionic systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum circuit design strategy for nuclear shell-model simulations using an adaptive variational quantum eigensolver (ADAPT-VQE) algorithm, claiming that the simulated circuits approach benchmark results exponentially with polynomial scaling in quantum resources, but the authors explicitly note that the number of layers and CNOT gates shown do not demonstrate an exponential quantum advantage with respect to classical computation cost.

## Method

The authors implement the nuclear shell model in a quantum computer using a Jordan-Wigner mapping that associates each qubit with a single-particle state in the configuration space. They employ an ADAPT-VQE algorithm that iteratively builds a wavefunction by adding parametrized two-body fermionic excitation operators selected according to the largest energy gradient, using the BFGS optimizer with a gradient tolerance of 10^{-6} at each iteration. The circuit implementation is benchmarked against circuit-free ADAPT-VQE simulations using regular matrix calculus and against classical shell-model diagonalization results for nuclei across the p, sd, and pf shells.

## Key numbers

- Number of qubits required: 6 for p-shell (neutrons only), 12 for p-shell (both nucleon types), 12 for sd-shell (neutrons only), 24 for sd-shell (both nucleon types), 20 for pf-shell (neutrons only), 40 for pf-shell (both nucleon types)
- Number of ansatz layers needed for convergence: 2 layers for 6Be (relative error bound 10^{-8}), 9 layers for 6Li (10^{-7}), 48 layers for 8Be (10^{-7}), 5 layers for 18O (10^{-6}), 70 layers for 20O (10^{-6}), 167 layers for 20Ne (2 x 10^{-2}), 345 layers for 24Ne (2 x 10^{-2}), 9 layers for 42Ca (10^{-8}), 221 layers for 50Ca (10^{-2})
- Average number of CNOT gates per layer: 42-92 for p-shell nuclei, 85-99 for sd-shell with 12 qubits, 137-138 for sd-shell with 24 qubits, 116-153 for pf-shell with 20 qubits
- Upper bound on CNOT gates per ansatz layer: 16(N_{qb} - 2), where N_{qb} is the number of qubits
- Number of circuits needed for energy measurement in pf-shell: 10613 total circuits (3500 minimum) for both nucleon types with 40 qubits
- For 22O, approximately 20 ansatz layers and 2000 CNOT gates achieve few percent error in ground-state energy

## Context

This work extends previous nuclear shell-model quantum computing studies by Dumitrescu (2018), Lu and Klco (2019), Stetcu, and Papenbrock, which had only partially addressed the stages of variational quantum eigensolver implementation. The authors compare their ADAPT-VQE results to UCC-VQE implementations by Stetcu and colleagues, finding that for 8Be their approach requires 48 parameters to reach relative error 10^{-7} while Stetcu required 112 variational parameters to reach approximately 1% error even with triple and quadruple excitation operators. For 6Li, the authors find 9 layers suffice for convergence to 10^{-7}, in contrast to an alternative ADAPT-VQE implementation by Kiss and colleagues that reached only approximately 10^{-3}. This work enables future quantum computing shell-model studies across the nuclear chart and provides quantum resource quantification applicable to configuration-interaction calculations of other fermionic systems.
