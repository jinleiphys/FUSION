# Astrophysical weak-interaction processes and nuclear effective field theory

> Low-energy nuclear weak-interaction processes play important roles in many astrophysical contexts, and effective field theory is believed to be a highly useful framework for describing these processes in a model-independent manner. I present a brief account of the basic features of the nuclear effective theory approach, and some examples of actual calculations carried out in this method.

## Key claim

Nuclear effective field theory (EFT) can provide a model-independent framework for low-energy weak-interaction processes in astrophysics, but in practice the "hybrid EFT" approach (using EFT-derived transition operators with standard nuclear physics wave functions) is the most practical method; the paper argues that this hybrid approach is more than a mere expedient and that the "MEEFT" variant can correlate observables across different nuclei with controlled error estimates.

## Method

The author compares three frameworks: the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), which uses phenomenological potentials fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering data and solves the Schrodinger equation; nuclear effective field theory (nuclear chi-PT) in the Weinberg scheme, which classifies Feynman diagrams into irreducible and reducible types and uses chiral counting to organize contributions; and hybrid EFT (MEEFT), which uses EFT-derived one- and two-body transition operators but evaluates their matrix elements using SNPA wave functions. For the specific reactions studied, the unknown low-energy constant (LEC) hat-d_R appearing in the two-body axial current operator at N3LO is fixed by fitting the tritium beta-decay rate, enabling parameter-free predictions for pp fusion and neutrino-deuteron reactions.

## Key numbers

- The pp fusion S-factor at zero energy: S_pp(0) = 3.94 x (1 +/- 0.005) x 10^{-25} MeV b, with only ~0.1% variation against changes in the cutoff Lambda
- The cutoff range used: 500 MeV less than or similar to Lambda less than or similar to 800 MeV
- The best-fit value of the LEC L_1A from Butler-Chen-Kong: L_1A = 5.6 fm^3, consistent with naturalness bound |L_1A| <= 6 fm^3
- The neutrino-deuteron cross sections from MEEFT agree within 1% with the SNPA results of Nakamura et al. for all four reactions in equation (1) over the solar neutrino energy range E_nu less than or similar to 20 MeV

## Context

This invited talk (KIAS-APCTP Astro-Hadron Symposium, 2003) extends the program of applying chiral perturbation theory to nuclear weak interactions, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1990) and the development of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The MEEFT results for pp fusion improve by a factor of ~10 over previous naive hybrid EFT results from Park et al. (1998), and the neutrino-deuteron cross sections confirm and refine the SNPA calculations of Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov and Kubodera (2001, updated as NETAL). The work enables reliable interpretation of SNO (Sudbury Neutrino Observatory) data at the 1% precision level, and the author notes that future comparisons with the formally consistent framework of Epelbaum, Gloeckle and Meissner would be informative.
