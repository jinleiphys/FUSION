# In-medium $Δ(1232)$ potential, pion production in heavy-ion collisions and the symmetry energy

> Using the dcQMD transport model, the isoscalar and isovector in-medium potentials of the $Δ$(1232) baryon are studied and information regarding their effective strength is obtained from a comparison to experimental pion production data in heavy-ion collisions below 800 MeV/nucleon impact energy. The best description is achieved for an isoscalar potential moderately more attractive than the nucleon optical potential and a rather small isoscalar relative effective mass m$^*_Δ\approx$ 0.45. For the isovector component only a constraint between the potential's strength at saturation and the isovector effective mass difference can be extracted, which depends on quantities such as the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass difference. These results are incompatible with the usual assumption, in transport models, that the $Δ$(1232) and nucleon potentials are equal. The density dependence of symmetry energy can be studied using the high transverse momentum tail of pion multiplicity ratio spectra. Results are however correlated with the value of neutron-proton effective mass difference. This region of spectra is shown to be affected by uncertain model ingredients such as the pion potential or in-medium correction to inelastic scattering cross-sections at levels smaller than 10$\%$. Extraction of precise constraints for the density dependence of symmetry energy above saturation will require experimental data for pion production in heavy-ion collisions below 800 MeV/nucleon impact energy and experimental values for the high transverse momentum tail of pion multiplicity ratio spectra accurate to better than 5$\%$.

## Key claim

Using the dcQMD transport model, the authors find that the best description of experimental pion production data in heavy-ion collisions below 800 MeV/nucleon requires a Δ(1232) isoscalar potential moderately more attractive than the nucleon optical potential, contradicting the usual assumption that Δ and nucleon potentials are equal; however, for the isovector component only a constraint between the potential strength at saturation and the isovector effective mass difference can be extracted, which depends on the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass difference.

## Method

The dcQMD (dynamical constrained Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions, treating the Δ(1232) in-medium potential as an independent quantity with separate parametrizations for its isoscalar and isovector components. The model includes threshold effects from global energy conservation, in-medium modifications of inelastic cross-sections using effective masses, and pion optical potentials via the Ericson-Ericson parametrization; the vacuum Li-Machleidt and Cugnon parametrizations for elastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections were used with an empirical density- and momentum-dependent medium modification factor.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar relative effective mass of the Δ(1232): m*_Δ ≈ 0.45 (dimensionless, relative to vacuum mass)
- The high transverse momentum tail of pion multiplicity ratio spectra is affected by uncertain model ingredients (pion potential, in-medium corrections to inelastic cross-sections) at levels smaller than 10%
- Extraction of precise constraints for the density dependence of symmetry energy above saturation requires experimental data for the high transverse momentum tail of pion multiplicity ratio spectra accurate to better than 5%
- Charged pion production cross-sections from the Huber parametrizations underpredict experimental values for nn/pp and np reactions by 20% and 40% respectively at 400 MeV/nucleon impact energy
- In-medium modification factors for inelastic cross-sections amount to values in the range of 0.5-0.7 at saturation density

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Cozma (2014, 2016) that demonstrated sensitivity of the charged pion multiplicity ratio to Δ(1232) potentials and used the ratio of average transverse momenta of charged pions to constrain the isovector Δ potential strength. The results are incompatible with the standard assumption in transport models (Li, 2002) that Δ(1232) and nucleon potentials are equal, and build on the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP) benchmark calculations by Xu (2016), Zhang (2017), and Ono (2019). The findings enable improved extraction of the density dependence of the symmetry energy above saturation density from pion production data, but require experimental pion multiplicity ratio spectra with better than 5% accuracy.
