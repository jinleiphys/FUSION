# Dynamical moment of inertia and quadrupole vibrations in rotating nuclei

> The contribution of quantum shape fluctuations to inertial properties of rotating nuclei has been analysed within the self-consistent one-dimensional cranking oscillator model. It is shown that in even-even nuclei the dynamical moment of inertia calculated in the mean field approximation is equivalent to the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia calculated in the random phase approximation if and only if the self-consistent conditions for the mean field are fulfilled.

## Key claim

The dynamical moment of inertia calculated in the mean field approximation is equivalent to the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia calculated in the random phase approximation, but only when the self-consistent conditions for the mean field are fulfilled.

## Method

The authors used a self-consistent cranked triaxial harmonic oscillator model as a rotating mean field, adding a self-consistent residual interaction to describe collective excitations. They solved the RPA equations of motion analytically for the positive signature Hamiltonian, which contains the zero-frequency rotational mode, to extract the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia. Numerical calculations were performed for two systems with particle numbers A=20 and A=64 (N=Z) to compare the Inglis, Thouless-Valatin, mean-field dynamical, and RPA dynamical moments of inertia as functions of rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- For A=20 (N=Z=10) and A=64 (N=Z=32) systems, the Inglis moment of inertia is smaller than both the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia and the mean-field dynamical moment of inertia, and has a different rotational dependence.
- For A=64, the onset of oblate deformation (where collective rotation vanishes) occurs at a smaller rotational frequency compared to the A=20 system.
- The RPA dynamical moment of inertia is larger than the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia, but the contribution of RPA ground state correlations decreases with increasing number of particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier cranking+RPA approaches by Egido (1980s) that suffered from inconsistency between the mean field and residual interaction and were restricted to only three shells. The authors use an integral representation method developed by Døssing (1990s) to calculate the full RPA energy with high accuracy. The paper numerically demonstrates for the first time the equivalence between the mean-field dynamical moment of inertia and the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia, clarifying a long-standing theoretical point. This enables future work to include both shape and pairing vibrations to improve agreement with experimental dynamical moments of inertia at high spins.
