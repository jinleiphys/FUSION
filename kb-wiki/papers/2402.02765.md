# Charmonium states in a coupled-channel model

> We systematically investigate the mass spectrum and two-body open-charm strong decays of charmonium states in a coupled-channel model where the $^3P_0$ quark-antiquark pair creation mechanism is employed. The results of masses, mass shifts, proportions of the $c\bar{c}$ component, and open-charm decay widths are provided. The $S$-$D$ wave mixing angles and di-electric decay widths for vector mesons are also presented. Based on our results, we find that the $ψ(3770)$, $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, $ψ(4360)$, and $ψ(4415)$ can be assigned as the $1^3D_1$-, $3^3S_1$-, $2^3D_1$-, $4^3S_1$-, and $3^3D_1$-dominated charmonium states, respectively. The $ψ_3(3842)$ is a good candidate of the $ψ_3(1D)$ charmonium state. The calculated mass and strong decay width of $χ_{c1}(2P)$ with significant continuum contribution ($\sim$57\%) favor the charmonium interpretation for the mysterious $χ_{c1}(3872)$. When considering the large uncertainty in the observed decay width, the possibility to assign the $χ_{c0}(3860)$ as the $χ_{c0}(2P)$ charmonium state cannot be ruled out. One may describe well the properties of $χ_{c2}(3930)$ with the $χ_{c2}(2P)$ charmonium. The predictions on properties of other $c\bar{c}$ states can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim
The paper systematically assigns observed charmonium states to specific \(c\bar{c}\) configurations using a coupled-channel model with \(^3P_0\) pair creation, but the assignments for \(\chi_{c1}(3872)\) and \(\chi_{c0}(3860)\) are conditional: the former requires a large continuum component (\(\sim\)57%) to match its mass and width, while the latter is only "not ruled out" given the large experimental uncertainty in its decay width.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation where a bare charmonium state (from a quenched potential model with Coulomb, linear, and spin-dependent terms) couples to open-charm meson-antimeson continua (\(D\bar{D}\), \(D\bar{D}^*\), \(D^*\bar{D}^*\), \(D_s\bar{D}_s\), \(D_s\bar{D}^*_s\), \(D^*_s\bar{D}^*_s\)) via the \(^3P_0\) quark-pair-creation mechanism. For \(J^{PC}=1^{--}\) states, the coupled-channel equations also yield \(S\)-\(D\) mixing angles, which are then used to compute di-electric decay widths. The model parameters are refitted to measured charmonium masses.

## Key numbers
* \(\chi_{c1}(3872)\): calculated mass 3870.1 MeV, with a \(c\bar{c}\) component probability of 43.4% (i.e., \(\sim\)57% continuum contribution), favoring its charmonium interpretation.
* \(\psi(3770)\): assigned as \(1^3D_1\)-dominated; calculated mass 3763.2 MeV (experiment 3773.7\(\pm\)0.4 MeV); total open-charm width 23.3 MeV (experiment 27.2\(\pm\)1.0 MeV).
* \(\psi(4040)\): assigned as \(3^3S_1\)-dominated; calculated mass 4044.4 MeV (experiment 4039\(\pm\)1 MeV); total width 36.3 MeV (experiment 80\(\pm\)10 MeV).
* \(\psi(4160)\): assigned as \(2^3D_1\)-dominated; calculated mass 4111.6 MeV (experiment 4191\(\pm\)5 MeV); total width 48.6 MeV (experiment 70\(\pm\)10 MeV).
* \(\psi(4360)\): assigned as \(4^3S_1\)-dominated; calculated mass 4361.2 MeV (experiment 4372\(\pm\)9 MeV); total width 37.5 MeV (experiment 115\(\pm\)13 MeV).
* \(\psi(4415)\): assigned as \(3^3D_1\)-dominated; calculated mass 4408.4 MeV (experiment 4421\(\pm\)4 MeV); total width 42.4 MeV (experiment 62\(\pm\)20 MeV).
* \(\psi_3(3842)\): assigned as \(1^3D_3\); calculated mass 3805.6 MeV (experiment 3842.71\(\pm\)0.20 MeV); total width 1.9 MeV (experiment 2.8\(\pm\)0.6 MeV).
* \(\chi_{c2}(3930)\): assigned as \(\chi_{c2}(2P)\); calculated mass 3953.3 MeV (experiment 3922.5\(\pm\)1.0 MeV); \(c\bar{c}\) probability 75.1%.
* \(\chi_{c0}(3860)\): assigned as \(\chi_{c0}(2P)\); calculated mass 3858.7 MeV (experiment 3862\({}^{+26+40}_{-32-13}\) MeV); \(c\bar{c}\) probability 89.0%.

## Context
This work extends earlier coupled-channel studies of vector charmonia by Fu et al. (2018) to include all charmonium states up to the \(D_s^{*+}D_s^{*-}\) threshold, incorporating \(S\)-\(D\) mixing for \(1^{--}\) states. It addresses discrepancies between quenched potential model predictions (e.g., Godfrey-Isgur model, Barnes et al. 2005) and observed masses for states like \(\chi_{c1}(3872)\), \(\psi(4360)\), and \(\psi(4230)\). The results enable future experimental tests of predicted properties for unobserved \(c\bar{c}\) states and provide a unified framework for interpreting charmonium-like states as conventional mesons with significant continuum admixture.
