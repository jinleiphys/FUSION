# Quantum microscopic dynamical approaches

> Nuclear physics is ideal to test and develop techniques to describe the microscopic dynamics of quantum many-body systems. At low energy, nuclear dynamics is described with non-relativistic approaches based on the mean-field approximation and its extensions. Variational principles based on the stationarity of the action are introduced to build theoretical models with different levels of approximation. In particular, the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation for mean-field dynamics and its linear approximation, also known as the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), are derived. Predictions of vibrational spectra at the RPA level are presented as an application. The inclusion of beyond TDHF correlations and fluctuations are then discussed. In particular, pairing correlations are treated at the BCS and Bogoliubov levels. The Balian-Veneroni variational principle is finally introduced. In addition to provide some insight into mean-field limitations, it offers a possibility to incorporate quantum fluctuations of one-body observables with the time-dependent RPA formalism.

## Key claim

The paper presents a pedagogical derivation of time-dependent mean-field approaches (TDHF and RPA) for low-energy nuclear dynamics, emphasizing that these methods are approximations obtained by restricting the variational space to Slater determinants, which necessarily neglects correlations such as pairing and quantum fluctuations, but remain tractable for systems with up to ~500 nucleons.

## Method

The author derives the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation from the Dirac action variational principle by restricting the many-body state to a single Slater determinant, yielding a set of non-linear single-particle Schrödinger equations with a self-consistent mean-field Hamiltonian. The Random Phase Approximation (RPA) is then obtained as the linear response of TDHF to small-amplitude perturbations, allowing extraction of vibrational energies and transition amplitudes from the time evolution of multipole moments after a small boost excitation. Pairing correlations are incorporated via extensions to time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) and BCS approaches, and the Balian-Vénéroni variational principle is introduced to incorporate quantum fluctuations of one-body observables.

## Key numbers

- Systems of up to ~500 nucleons are tractable, as in the case of actinide collisions (the heaviest nuclear systems that can be studied on Earth)
- Typical TDHF solvers use Cartesian grids with mesh spacing Δx = 0.6–1.0 fm
- The low-energy regime considered is typically few MeV per nucleon

## Context

This chapter provides a pedagogical introduction to the theoretical framework of time-dependent mean-field approaches in nuclear physics, extending the foundational work of Dirac (1930) on TDHF. It builds upon and summarizes recent reviews of the field (Simenel 2012; Lacroix and Ayik 2014; Nakatsukasa et al 2016; Simenel and Umar 2018; Sekizawa 2019; Stevenson and Barton 2019) and references the Sky3D solver (Maruhn et al 2014; Schuetrumpf et al 2018). The work enables further development of beyond-mean-field techniques, including the treatment of pairing correlations and quantum fluctuations, and provides the theoretical basis for studying nuclear vibrations, fusion, transfer reactions, and deep-inelastic collisions.
