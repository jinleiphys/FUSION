# Photon- and meson-induced reactions on the nucleon

> In an unitary effective Lagrangian model we develop a unified description of both meson scattering and photon-induced reactions on the nucleon. Adding the photon to an already existing model for meson-nucleon scattering yields both Compton and meson photoproduction amplitudes. In a simultaneous fit to all available data involving the final states $γN$, $πN$, $ππN$, $ηN$ and $K Λ$ the parameters of the nucleon resonances are extracted.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a unified, unitary effective Lagrangian model using the K-matrix approximation can simultaneously describe both meson scattering and photon-induced reactions on the nucleon, but they find that a readjustment of resonance masses and widths from hadronic-only fits is necessary for eta photoproduction, and that the model-dependence from restoring gauge invariance is significant mainly for resonances with small helicity couplings.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed K-matrix model for meson-nucleon scattering (Feuster and Mosel, 1998) by adding the photon perturbatively to the potential V, enlarging the channel matrices to include the gamma N final state. They calculated the potential from s-, u-, and t-channel Feynman diagrams using effective Lagrangians, then unitarized the amplitudes via the K-matrix approximation T = V/(1 - iV) for hadronic channels and used T = V + iTV for photoproduction and Compton scattering. A simultaneous fit was performed to all available data for the final states gamma N, pi N, pi pi N, eta N, and K Lambda, extracting resonance parameters including electromagnetic couplings.

## Key numbers

- The P33(1232) resonance mass was found to be 1.229 GeV and its width 110-113 MeV in the hadronic fits
- The eta photoproduction required readjustment of resonance masses and widths from the hadronic-only fits to describe the data
- For the D13(1520) resonance, a possible disagreement was found for the helicity couplings extracted using the combined dataset versus pion photoproduction multipoles alone
- The energy range was restricted to sqrt(s) less than or equal to 1.9 GeV
- The magnetic transition moments used were kappa_Lambda = -0.613 and kappa_Sigma = 1.610
- The omega NN coupling constants used were g_omegaNN = 7.98 and kappa_omegaNN = -0.12
- The gamma-gamma-pi0 coupling was -0.044, gamma-gamma-eta was 0.167, gamma-pi0-rho0 was 0.131, gamma-pi+-rho+- was 0.103, gamma-eta-rho0 was 1.020, gamma-pi0-omega was 0.313, gamma-eta-omega was 0.329, gamma-pi0-K*0 was 0.631, and gamma-pi+-K*+- was 0.415

## Context

This work extends the K-matrix effective Lagrangian approach for hadronic reactions developed by Feuster and Mosel (1998) to include photon-induced reactions, building on prior unitary models for meson-nucleon scattering from the Carnegie-Mellon Berkeley group and others (Dytman et al., 1997). It enables a unified extraction of both hadronic and electromagnetic resonance parameters from combined datasets, and the comparison with dispersion theoretical analyses provides information about the quality of the K-matrix approximation. The model is designed to benefit from high-precision data from facilities like MAMI, ELSA, GRAAL, and TJNAF, and the authors note that a description of the pi pi N final state in terms of two-particle intermediate states like pi Delta, pi P11(1440), and rho N is currently under development.
