# Factorization and resummation of QED radiative corrections for neutron beta decay

> Details of the two-loop analysis of long-distance QED radiative corrections to neutron beta decay are presented. Explicit expressions are given for hard, jet, and soft functions appearing in the factorization formula that describes the small mass/large energy limit. Power corrections, cancellation of singularities in the small mass expansion, renormalization scheme dependence, and bound state effects are discussed. The results impact the determination of $|V_{ud}|$ from the measured neutron lifetime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the large one-loop QED radiative correction to neutron beta decay, proportional to $\pi\alpha/\beta$, originates from an infrared divergence in the hard amplitude that can be systematically resummed to all orders using renormalization group methods, with the two-loop analysis showing that the naive Fermi function approximation differs from the exact result starting at $O(\alpha^2)$.

## Method

The authors employ a soft-collinear-hard factorization formalism that separates the neutron beta decay amplitude into hard, jet, soft, and remainder functions in the small mass/large energy limit ($E\gg m$). They compute the two-loop corrections to each component using dimensional regularization and the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, then use renormalization group evolution to resum large logarithms arising from the analytic continuation from spacelike to timelike kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The hard function ratio $H(\mu^2) = \exp[\pi\alpha/\beta] H(\hat{\mu}^2)$ is derived to all orders, with the exponentiated enhancement factor $\pi\alpha/\beta$ appearing at leading order
- The resummed hard function modulus squared at $\mu=2E$ yields $|F_H(\mu)|^2 = 1 + 6.8(\bar{\alpha}/\pi) + (23.9 - 5.1 n_e)(\bar{\alpha}/\pi)^2 + \dots$, compared to $|F_H(\hat{\mu})|^2 = 1 - 3.1(\bar{\alpha}/\pi) + (5.4 + 3.6 n_e)(\bar{\alpha}/\pi)^2 + \dots$ when large logarithms are minimized
- The $O(\alpha^3)$ contribution from the small-velocity region yields a non-analytic $|\alpha|^3$ term that cancels with bound-state contributions to leave an analytic $\alpha^3 \times (\pi\zeta_3)$ term in the integrated rate
- The two-loop remainder function $F_R$ contains a term $n_e(L-1)(-\frac43 \log^2(m^2/\mu^2) - \frac{40}{9}\log(m^2/\mu^2) - \frac{112}{27})$ at $O(\alpha^2)$

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach of Hill (2023) and VanderGriend (2025) for QED corrections to neutron beta decay, providing explicit two-loop results for the hard, jet, and soft functions. The analysis resolves the long-standing question about the convergence of the QED perturbative series by identifying the $\pi\alpha/\beta$ enhancement as a large logarithm that can be resummed via renormalization group methods, in contrast to the traditional Fermi function approach of Fermi (1934) and Wilkinson (1982) which is justified only for large nuclear charge or small electron velocity. The results enable improved determinations of $|V_{ud}|$ from neutron lifetime measurements at the $10^{-4}$ precision level, and the formalism can be extended to other semileptonic beta decay processes.
