# Decay modes of ideally mixed narrow pentaquark states

> We investigate the decay modes of pentaquark baryons for both the antidecuplet and the octet states, which were recently claimed by Close and Dudek to be potentially narrow due to a hidden selection rule. We discuss how ideal mixing between the pentaquark octet and antidecuplet states can be related to the OZI rule, which also naturally leads to certain selection rules in the pentaquark decays. We then introduce a tensor representation for the pentaquark states, and present the possible decay modes for both the unmixed and ideally mixed pentaquark octet and antidecuplet states. The exclusive decay modes can be used to experimentally search for the other pentaquark states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "generalized OZI rule" (the suppression of disconnected quark-line diagrams) leads to ideal mixing between pentaquark octet and antidecuplet states, and that this mixing produces specific selection rules in their decays, but only if the matrix element ⟨[ud][ud]d̄|H|[ud][us]₊s̄⟩ is small, a condition that must be confirmed experimentally rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct SU(3) tensor representations for pentaquark antidecuplet (T^{ijk}) and octet (P^i_j) states, deriving SU(3)-invariant effective Lagrangians for their decays into baryon octet plus meson octet. They impose the "fall-apart" mechanism (no quark-pair creation or annihilation) by requiring that the antiquark index in the pentaquark field contracts with the antiquark index of the meson field, which fixes the f/d ratio for octet couplings to f/d = 1/3. They then compute all coupling constants for unmixed and ideally mixed states, and examine couplings to baryon decuplet plus meson octet.

## Key numbers

- The Θ⁺ baryon has a mass of 1540 MeV and a decay width < 25 MeV (from experimental references).
- For the antidecuplet nucleon decay into KΣ, the ratio ⟨N₈|KΣ⟩ / ⟨N₁₀̄|KΣ⟩ = −√2.
- For the antidecuplet nucleon decay into πN, the ratio ⟨N₈|πN⟩ / ⟨N₁₀̄|πN⟩ = 1/√2.
- The OZI rule fixes the octet f/d ratio to f = 1/2 and d = 3/2, giving f/d = 1/3.
- The N₈ N η₈, Λ₈ Ξ K, and Ξ₈ Λ K couplings vanish (coupling constant = 0) under the OZI rule.

## Context

This work extends the pentaquark decay analysis of Close and Dudek (2004), who proposed a hidden selection rule making pentaquark states narrow, and builds on the diquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). The paper provides a systematic tensor formalism to compute all allowed decay modes for both unmixed and ideally mixed pentaquark octet and antidecuplet states. It enables experimental searches for other narrow pentaquark states (beyond Θ⁺ and Ξ*(1862)) by predicting their exclusive decay channels, and rules out N(1440) and N(1710) as ideally mixed pentaquark states because their large Δπ branching ratios violate the OZI rule.
