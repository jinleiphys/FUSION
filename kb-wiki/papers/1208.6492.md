# Probing the symmetry energy with isospin ratio from nucleons to fragments

> Within the framework of ImQMD05, we study several isospin sensitive observables, such as DR(n/p) ratios, isospin transport ratio (isospin diffusion), yield ratios for LCPs between the projectile region and mid-rapidity region for the reaction systems Ni+Ni, Zn+Zn, Sn+Sn at low-intermediate energies. Our results show that those observables are sensitive to the density dependence of symmetry energy, and also depend on the cluster formation mechanism. By comparing these calculations to the data, the information of the symmetry energy and reaction mechanism is obtained.

## Key claim
The density dependence of the symmetry energy can be constrained by comparing ImQMD05 transport-model calculations to experimental data on isospin ratio observables (DR(n/p), isospin transport ratios, and mid-rapidity yield ratios), but the extracted constraints depend on the cluster formation mechanism and on which observable and tracer is used, with the analysis ruling out very soft (\(\gamma_i=0.35\)) and very stiff (\(\gamma_i=2.0\)) forms while bracketing \(\gamma_i\) between 0.4 and 1.05 from DR(n/p) and between 0.45 and 0.95 from \(R_i\) data.

## Method
The authors used the ImQMD05 code (a Quantum Molecular Dynamics model) to simulate collisions of \({}^{124}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn, \({}^{112}\)Sn+\({}^{112}\)Sn, \({}^{70}\)Zn+\({}^{70}\)Zn, \({}^{64}\)Zn+\({}^{64}\)Zn, and \({}^{64}\)Ni+\({}^{64}\)Ni at beam energies of 35 and 50 MeV per nucleon, with a Skyrme-like symmetry energy parameterized by \(S(\rho) = \frac{1}{3}\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\rho_0^{2/3}(\frac{3\pi^2}{2}\frac{\rho}{\rho_0})^{2/3} + \frac{C_s}{2}(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0})^{\gamma_i}\) with \(C_s=35.19\) MeV and varying the exponent \(\gamma_i\) (0.35, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 2.0). They computed the double ratio DR(n/p) for emitted nucleons, the isospin transport ratio \(R_i\) from the isospin asymmetry of emitted nucleons and fragments, and the mid-rapidity yield ratio \(R^{mid}_{yield}\) for light charged particles (LCPs), comparing these to experimental data from Famiano (2006), Tsang (2004, 2009), and Kohley (2011).

## Key numbers
- DR(n/p) data for \({}^{124}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn and \({}^{112}\)Sn+\({}^{112}\)Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter \(b=2\) fm rule out \(\gamma_i=0.35\) and \(\gamma_i=2.0\); a \(\chi^2\) analysis with \(2\sigma\) uncertainty gives \(0.4 \le \gamma_i \le 1.05\) for \(C_s=35.2\) MeV.
- Isospin transport ratio \(R_i\) data for the same Sn systems, using a \(2\sigma\) criterion, bracket \(\gamma_i\) in the range \(0.45 \le \gamma_i \le 0.95\).
- For \({}^{70}\)Zn+\({}^{70}\)Zn at 35 MeV/nucleon and \(b=4\) fm with \(\gamma_i=2.0\), half of events are multi-fragmentation events (multiplicity of fragments with \(Z\ge3\) greater than 3); the yield of \({}^{3}\)He at \(Y_r=0\) from binary fragmentation events is 35% larger than from multi-fragmentation events, and the yield of \({}^{6}\)He at \(Y_r=0\) from binary events is 70% larger than from multi-fragmentation events.
- For \({}^{64}\)Ni+\({}^{64}\)Ni at 35 MeV/nucleon and \(b=4\) fm, the ImQMD05 calculations with stiffer symmetry energy (\(\gamma_i=2.0\)) reproduce the reduced rapidity distributions for \(p\), \(d\), \(t\), \({}^{3}\)He, \({}^{4}\)He, and \({}^{6}\)He at forward rapidity (\(Y_r>0\)).

## Context
This work extends prior constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from isospin observables in heavy-ion collisions, such as the DR(n/p) measurements by Famiano (2006) and the isospin transport ratio studies by Tsang (2004, 2009) and Liu (2007). It also compares ImQMD05 results to Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) calculations by Rizzo (2008) and Kohley (2011), highlighting differences in fragmentation mechanisms and Pauli blocking between QMD and BUU-type models. The findings enable further refinement of the symmetry energy by combining multiple observables and by examining the role of cluster formation and tracer selection in transport-model comparisons.
