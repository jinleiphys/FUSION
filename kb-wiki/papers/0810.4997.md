# Feasibility of extracting a $Σ^-$ admixture probability in the neutron-rich $^{10}_Λ$Li hypernucleus

> We examine theoretically production of the neutron-rich $^{10}_Λ$Li hypernucleus by a double-charge exchange ($π^-$, $K^+$) reaction on a $^{10}$B target with distorted-wave impulse approximation calculations. The result shows that the magnitude and shape of the calculated spectrum at 1.20 GeV/c by a one-step mechanism $π^-p \to K^+ Σ^-$ via $Σ^-$ doorways caused by a $Σ^-p \leftrightarrow Λn$ coupling can explain the recent experimental data, and the $Σ^-$ admixture probability in $^{10}_Λ$Li is found to be the order of 10$^{-1}$ %. The ($π^-$, $K^+$) reaction provides a capability of extracting properties of wave functions with $Λ$-$Σ$ coupling effects in neutron-rich nuclei, together with the reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The one-step mechanism ($\pi^- p \to K^+ \Sigma^-$ via $\Sigma^-$ doorways caused by $\Sigma^- p \leftrightarrow \Lambda n$ coupling) can explain the experimental data for the $^{10}$B($\pi^-$, $K^+$) reaction at 1.20 GeV/c, yielding a $\Sigma^-$ admixture probability in $^{10}_\Lambda$Li of order $10^{-1}$%, which is one to two orders of magnitude larger than earlier theoretical predictions by Tretyakova and Lanskoy (2003).

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for the double-charge exchange ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) reaction on a $^{10}$B target at 1.20 GeV/c, using a $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ coupled-channel Green's function method. They modeled the $^{10}_\Lambda$Li wave function as a two-channel coupled state of $^{9}$Li$\otimes\Lambda$ and $^{9}$Be$^*\otimes\Sigma^-$, with Woods-Saxon optical potentials for the diagonal channels and a real coupling potential $U_{\Sigma\Lambda}$ of Woods-Saxon form. The inclusive $\Lambda$ spectrum was computed and compared to KEK experimental data, with the parameters $W_\Sigma$ (imaginary strength for the $\Sigma^-$ channel) and $V_{\Sigma\Lambda}$ (coupling strength) adjusted phenomenologically.

## Key numbers

- The $\Sigma^-$ admixture probability in the $^{10}_\Lambda$Li $2^-$ bound state is $P_{\Sigma^-} = 0.57\%$ for $V_{\Sigma\Lambda} = 11$ MeV, with values ranging from $0.075\%$ to $0.68\%$ for $V_{\Sigma\Lambda} = 4$ to $12$ MeV.
- The calculated spectrum with $-W_\Sigma = 20$–$30$ MeV reproduces the shape of the experimental data in the continuum region.
- The bound state peak appears at $E_\Lambda \simeq -10.0$ MeV for the $[0p_{3/2}^{-1}s_{1/2}^\Lambda]_{2^-}$ configuration, with excited states at $E_\Lambda = 1$–$3$ MeV.
- The energy shift from $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ coupling is $\Delta E_\Lambda \simeq -456$ keV for $V_{\Sigma\Lambda} = 11$ MeV.
- The two-step mechanism contributes only 1–2 nb/sr to the cross section at 1.20 GeV/c ($6^\circ$), compared to the dominant one-step mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of neutron-rich $\Lambda$ hypernuclei production via double-charge exchange reactions, following the first measurement by Saha et al. (2005) at KEK. It contradicts the earlier prediction by Tretyakova and Lanskoy (2003), who found $\Sigma^-$ admixture probabilities of order $10^{-3}$–$10^{-2}\%$ using Hartree-Fock single-particle calculations and argued that the two-step mechanism dominates. The present result suggests that the one-step mechanism via $\Sigma^-$ doorways is dominant at 1.20 GeV/c, and that the ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) reaction can be used to extract $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ coupling effects in neutron-rich nuclei. This analysis provides a framework for interpreting future experiments at J-PARC.
