# Electromagnetic nucleon form factors in instant and point form

> We present a study of the electromagnetic structure of the nucleons with constituent quark models in the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics. In particular, we address the construction of spectator-model currents in the instant and point forms. Corresponding results for the elastic nucleon electromagnetic form factors as well as charge radii and magnetic moments are presented. We also compare results obtained by different realistic nucleon wave functions stemming from alternative constituent quark models. Finally, we discuss the theoretical uncertainties that reside in the construction of spectator-model transition operators.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the point-form spectator model (PFSM) yields nucleon electromagnetic form factors in surprisingly good agreement with experimental data without parameter adjustments, while the instant-form spectator model (IFSM) produces results far from experiment, even though both models share the same nonrelativistic limit; however, the PFSM construction retains residual ambiguities that must be resolved by imposing charge normalization and time-reversal invariance.

## Method

The authors constructed spectator-model electromagnetic current operators in both instant form (IFSM) and point form (PFSM) within relativistic quantum mechanics, using constituent quark model (CQM) wave functions from the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) CQM and the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) Bhaduri-Cohler-Nogami (BCN) CQM. They calculated the elastic nucleon Sachs form factors G_E and G_M, charge radii, and magnetic moments for momentum transfers up to Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, comparing predictions from the two relativistic forms and from the nonrelativistic impulse approximation (NRIA). They also examined the dependence of PFSM results on the choice of the normalization factor N in the current operator.

## Key numbers

* Proton magnetic moment: GBE CQM with PFSM gives 2.70 n.m. (experiment: 2.79 n.m.); IFSM gives 1.24 n.m.; NRIA gives 2.74 n.m.
* Neutron magnetic moment: GBE CQM with PFSM gives -1.70 n.m. (experiment: -1.91 n.m.); IFSM gives -0.79 n.m.; NRIA gives -1.82 n.m.
* Proton charge radius squared: GBE CQM with PFSM gives 0.824 fm^2 (experiment: 0.766 fm^2); IFSM gives 0.156 fm^2; NRIA gives 0.102 fm^2.
* Neutron charge radius squared: GBE CQM with PFSM gives -0.135 fm^2 (experiment: -0.116 fm^2); IFSM gives -0.020 fm^2; NRIA gives -0.009 fm^2.
* The factor N in the PFSM current must have exponent x = 3 to yield the correct proton charge (G_E^p = 1 at Q^2 = 0).
* The parameter y in the PFSM factor N must be y = 1/2 to satisfy time-reversal invariance (vanishing third component of the transition amplitude in the Breit frame).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of baryon electromagnetic properties using the point-form spectator model (Wagenbrunn 2000, Boffi 2001, Berger 2004) and addresses the puzzle of why PFSM predictions fall so close to experiment despite using simplified current operators. It contrasts the PFSM and IFSM approaches, showing that the IFSM results are similar to the NRIA and far from data, while PFSM results agree well with experiment. The paper also compares predictions from the GBE CQM, the OGE BCN CQM, and the instanton-induced (II) CQM of the Bonn group (Merten 2002), finding that all three realistic CQMs produce similar form factors when using PFSM, but that a confinement-only potential fails to describe the neutron electric form factor. The analysis of ambiguities in the PFSM construction, following earlier work by Melde (2004) and Polyzou (1986), shows that charge normalization and time-reversal invariance uniquely fix the form of the normalization factor N, enabling consistent predictions for future studies of baryon structure.
