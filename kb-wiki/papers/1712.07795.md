# Symmetry Realization via a Dynamical Inverse Higgs Mechanism

> The Ward identities associated with spontaneously broken symmetries can be saturated by Goldstone bosons. However, when space-time symmetries are broken, the number of Goldstone bosons necessary to non-linearly realize the symmetry can be less than the number of broken generators. The loss of Goldstones may be due to a redundancy or the generation of a gap. This phenomena is called an Inverse Higgs Mechanism (IHM). However, there are cases when a Goldstone boson associated with a broken generator does not appear in the low energy theory despite the lack of the existence of an associated IHM. In this paper we will show that in such cases the relevant broken symmetry can be realized, without the aid of an associated Goldstone, if there exists a proper set of operator constraints, which we call a Dynamical Inverse Higgs Mechanism (DIHM). We consider the spontaneous breaking of boosts, rotations and conformal transformations in the context of Fermi liquids, finding three possible paths to symmetry realization: pure Goldstones, no Goldstones and DIHM, or some mixture thereof. We show that in the two dimensional degenerate electron system the DIHM route is the only consistent way to realize spontaneously broken boosts and dilatations, while in three dimensions these symmetries could just as well be realized via the inclusion of non-derivatively coupled Goldstone bosons. We have present the action, including the leading order non-linearities, for the rotational Goldstone (angulon), and discuss the constraint associated with the possible DIHM that would need to be imposed to remove it from the spectrum. Finally we discuss the conditions under which Goldstone bosons are non-derivatively coupled, a necessary condition for the existence of a Dynamical Inverse Higgs Constraint (DIHC), generalizaing the results for Vishwanath and Wantanabe.

## Key claim

The paper shows that spontaneously broken space-time symmetries can be realized without a Goldstone boson even when no standard Inverse Higgs Mechanism (IHM) exists, provided a set of operator constraints, called a Dynamical Inverse Higgs Mechanism (DIHM), can be imposed; this resolves the puzzle of "missing" Goldstones such as the framon (boost Goldstone) and non-relativistic dilaton in Fermi liquids.

## Method

The authors use the coset construction method of Volkov and Ogievetsky to parameterize the Goldstone manifold for broken space-time symmetries, extracting the vierbein, covariant derivatives, and gauge fields from the Maurer-Cartan form. They then construct invariant actions for Fermi liquids with spontaneously broken boosts, rotations, and conformal transformations, treating the would-be Goldstone bosons as Lagrange multipliers to generate dynamical constraints. The analysis generalizes the criteria for non-derivatively coupled Goldstones from Vishwanath and Watanabe (2014) by using the condition \([\bar P^\mu, X] \neq 0\) where \(\bar P^\mu\) are unbroken translations.

## Key numbers

- In two-dimensional degenerate electron systems, the DIHM route is the only consistent way to realize spontaneously broken boosts and dilatations; in three dimensions, these symmetries could alternatively be realized via inclusion of non-derivatively coupled Goldstone bosons.
- The paper presents the action including leading order non-linearities for the rotational Goldstone (angulon), and discusses the constraint that would need to be imposed to remove it from the spectrum.
- The width of quasi-particles in nematic Fermi liquids (where rotational invariance is broken) scales as \(\Gamma \sim E^{\alpha}\) with \(\alpha < 2\), due to decay into Goldstones.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of "framids" introduced by Nicolis, Penco, and Piazza (2013) and the counting rules for Goldstone bosons from Nielsen and Chadha (1976) and Watanabe and Murayama (2012). It generalizes the criteria for non-derivatively coupled Goldstones set forth by Vishwanath and Watanabe (2014) by using the coset construction to include the Hamiltonian in the non-commutation condition. The results enable construction of effective field theories for degenerate fermions near zero temperature, including systems at the unitary limit where Schrodinger invariance is broken, and were recently used by the authors to prove that degenerate electrons in the unitary limit cannot behave as a Fermi liquid.
