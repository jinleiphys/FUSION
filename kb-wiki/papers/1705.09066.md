# A QFT-induced phase in neutrino flavour oscillations

> In the extended Standard Model of particle physics, each neutrino mass eigenstate is predicted to have a tiny but non vanishing magnetic moment induced by quantum field theoretic corrections. These QFT-induced magnetic momenta depend linearly on masses of the underlying mass eigenstates with a proportionality constant ${3eG_{F}}/{(8\sqrt{2}π^{2})} $. As a consequence when neutrinos are embedded in an environment containing magnetic fields the flavour oscillations get a contribution from the induced relative phases.

## Key claim

In the extended Standard Model, each neutrino mass eigenstate acquires a QFT-induced magnetic moment that depends linearly on its mass; when neutrinos propagate through a magnetic field, this adds a new, previously unconsidered phase to the flavour oscillation amplitude, but the effect is too small to be observed in the present universe and may only become relevant in the early universe under strong magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors compute the interaction energy of a Dirac neutrino mass eigenstate with a constant, uniform magnetic field using the effective electromagnetic current matrix element with a magnetic-moment coupling. They evaluate the energy shift for relativistic helicity eigenstates (the relevant case for neutrinos produced in weak interactions) and for the non-relativistic limit, then incorporate this shift as an additive correction to the standard kinematical oscillation phase.

## Key numbers

- The QFT-induced magnetic moment for each mass eigenstate is \(\mu_i = 1.85 \times 10^{-27} \left( \frac{m_i}{\text{eV}} \right) \left( \frac{\text{eV}}{\text{Gauss}} \right)\).
- The standard kinematical phase is \(\varphi_{ji} = 1.27 \left( \frac{\Delta m_{ji}^2}{\text{eV}^2} \right) \left( \frac{L}{\text{km}} \right) \left( \frac{\text{GeV}}{E} \right)\).
- The new QFT-induced correction to the phase is \(\varphi_{ji}^{\text{new}} = \varphi_{ji} \times 3.7 \times 10^{-27} \cos(\eta) \left( \frac{B}{\text{Gauss}} \right)\), where \(\eta\) is the angle between the magnetic field and the direction of propagation.
- Magnetar magnetic fields of order \(10^{16}\) Gauss are about eleven orders of magnitude too small to affect neutrino oscillations in the present cosmic epoch.
- CMB observations give an upper limit for the present-day cosmological magnetic field of \(\lesssim 0.05\) nano-Gauss, as argued by Trivedi and Subramanian (2014).

## Context

This work extends the standard Pontecorvo formalism for neutrino oscillations by adding a phase contribution from the neutrino magnetic moment interacting with an external magnetic field, an effect not previously considered in the literature. The magnetic moment itself is a well-studied prediction of Standard Model extensions (Marciano 1977, Lee 1977, Pal 1981, Schechter 1981, Shrock 1982, and others), and the physics of neutrino magnetic moments has been extensively explored in spin precession and helicity-flipping scattering (Cisneros 1970, Fujikawa 1980, Dvornikov 2002, Studenikin 2004, Giunti 2008, and others). The result suggests that while the effect is negligible for present-day observations, it could become significant in the early universe if magnetic fields were much stronger, motivating further study of neutrino oscillations in cosmological contexts.
