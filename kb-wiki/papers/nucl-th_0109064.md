# An Analysis of the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg Reaction Using A New Coupling Potential

> We introduce a new coupling potential to explain the experimental data for the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg system at numerous energies in the laboratory system from 16.0 MeV to 24.0 MeV. This new coupled-channels based approach involves replacing the usual first derivative coupling potential by a new, second-derivative coupling potential. This paper first shows and discusses the limitation of the standard coupled-channels theory in the case where one of the nuclei in the reaction is strongly deformed. Then, this new approach is shown to improve consistently the agreement with the experimental data and has made major improvement on all the previous coupled-channels calculations for this system.

# An Analysis of the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{24}\)Mg Reaction Using A New Coupling Potential

## Key claim

The paper claims that replacing the standard first-derivative coupling potential with a new second-derivative coupling potential consistently improves agreement with experimental data for the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{24}\)Mg system across the energy range 16.0–24.0 MeV, using the empirical deformation parameter \(\beta_2 = 0.52\) without arbitrary adjustments. However, the abstract glosses over the fact that the standard coupled-channels method could only fit the data by artificially increasing the Coulomb deformation parameter to \(\beta_2^C = 0.93\), whereas the new approach succeeds with the exact empirical value.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{24}\)Mg system using a deformed optical potential with a real potential of Woods-Saxon squared shape and an imaginary potential of standard Woods-Saxon volume shape whose depth increases linearly with laboratory energy. They first applied the standard coupled-channels method with a first-derivative coupling potential, then replaced it with a new second-derivative coupling potential of the form \(V_C(r) = -V_{C_0} e^{(r-R)/a}(e^{(r-R)/a}-1) / [a^2 (1+e^{(r-R)/a})^3]\), with parameters \(V_{C_0}=185.0\) MeV, \(R=3.67\) fm, and \(a=0.62\) fm. The calculations included the first two excited states of \(^{24}\)Mg (\(2^+\) at 1.47 MeV and \(4^+\) at 4.12 MeV) with a \(0^+\)-\(2^+\)-\(4^+\) coupling scheme and reorientation effects, using a modified version of the code CHUCK.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energy range studied: 16.0 MeV to 24.0 MeV for the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{24}\)Mg system.
- Empirical deformation parameter used: \(\beta_2 = 0.52\) (derived from deformation length \(\delta = 1.50\) fm).
- Reduced electromagnetic transition probability for \(^{24}\)Mg: B(E2) = 430 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- Deformation length for \(^{24}\)Mg: \(\delta\) between 1.48 fm and 1.50 fm; the authors used \(\delta = 1.50\) fm.
- In standard calculations, the Coulomb deformation parameter required to fit the data was \(\beta_2^C = 0.93\), larger than the empirical value.
- New coupling potential parameters: \(V_{C_0} = 185.0\) MeV, \(R = 3.67\) fm, \(a = 0.62\) fm.
- Imaginary potential depth given by \(W = 4.375 E_{\text{Lab}} - 67.0\) (in MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful application of the second-derivative coupling potential to the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C system (Boztosun and Rae, 2001) and the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{28}\)Si system (Boztosun and Rae, 2001). It directly addresses the long-standing problems identified in earlier studies of light heavy-ion reactions, particularly the out-of-phase problem between theory and experiment and the need for unphysically large deformation parameters, as documented by Sciani et al. (1997) and others (Carvalho et al., 1976; Carvalho et al., 1978; Filho et al., 1989). The new approach enables simultaneous fits to elastic and inelastic scattering data over a wide energy range without ad-hoc parameter adjustments, and the authors suggest the coupling potential may indicate the presence of super-deformed configurations in the compound nucleus \(^{36}\)Ar, as speculated by Rae (1988) and Feshbach (1992).
