# Freeze-out Conditions from Lattice QCD

> We describe a procedure for determination of freeze-out parameters of heavy-ion collisions through direct comparisons between experimentally measured higher order cumulants of charge fluctuations and first principle (lattice) QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a procedure to determine freeze-out parameters (temperature and chemical potentials) of heavy-ion collisions by directly comparing lattice QCD calculations with experimentally measured higher-order cumulants of charge fluctuations, but notes that these freeze-out parameters do not necessarily correspond to chemical freeze-out parameters because charge fluctuations freeze out through diffusion processes rather than the cessation of inelastic scatterings.

## Method

The author uses 2+1 flavor Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) lattice QCD calculations with a physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of 160 MeV, performed at three lattice spacings (temporal extents N_tau = 6, 8, 12). The electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials are expressed as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential by imposing strangeness neutrality and initial iso-spin asymmetry constraints, then Taylor expanding the densities up to cubic order. Two volume-independent ratios of charge cumulants, R_{31}^Q (thermometer) and R_{12}^Q (baryometer), are constructed from lattice QCD results to determine the freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential respectively.

## Key numbers

- Light quark masses correspond to Goldstone pion mass of 160 MeV
- NLO contributions to mu_Q and mu_S are less than 10% and well under control for mu_B less than or equal to 200 MeV, corresponding to RHIC energies down to sqrt(s_NN) greater than or equal to 19.6 GeV
- Lattice QCD results for mu_Q and mu_S differ from HRG model predictions by 10-15%
- NLO correction to R_{31}^Q is at most 10% for mu_B/T approximately equal to 1 over the whole temperature range
- For an experimentally measured R_{31}^Q greater than or equal to 2, freeze-out temperature T^f is less than or equal to 150 MeV; R_{31}^Q approximately equal to 1.5 gives T^f approximately equal to 160 MeV; R_{31}^Q less than or equal to 1 gives T^f greater than or equal to 170 MeV
- For T^f = 160 MeV, an experimental R_{12}^Q of 0.01-0.02 gives mu_B^f/T^f = 0.1-0.2; R_{12}^Q of 0.03-0.04 gives mu_B^f/T^f = 0.3-0.4; R_{12}^Q of 0.05-0.08 gives mu_B^f/T^f = 0.5-0.8
- NLO corrections to R_{12}^Q are less than 10% for mu_B less than or equal to 200 MeV (sqrt(s_NN) greater than or equal to 19.6 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the approach of using statistical hadronization models based on the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to describe freeze-out conditions, but replaces those models with first-principle lattice QCD calculations. While the QCD transition line in the T-mu_B plane was previously known from lattice QCD calculations by Bazavov (2011) and Kaczmarek (2011), this paper provides the first procedure for determining freeze-out parameters specifically for conserved charge fluctuations from lattice QCD. The method enables parameter-free predictions of higher-order cumulants from lattice QCD along the freeze-out line, allowing subsequent comparisons with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan to clarify whether higher-order cumulants contain critical signatures of the conjectured QCD critical point.
