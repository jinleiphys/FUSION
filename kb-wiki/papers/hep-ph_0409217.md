# Effect of bound nucleon internal structure change on nuclear structure functions

> Effect of bound nucleon internal structure change on nuclear structure functions is investigated based on local quark-hadron duality. The bound nucleon structure functions calculated for charged-lepton and (anti)neutrino scattering are all enhanced in symmetric nuclear matter at large Bjorken-$x$ ($x \agt 0.85$) relative to those in a free nucleon. This implies that a part of the enhancement observed in the nuclear structure function $F_2$ (in the resonance region) at large Bjorken-$x$ (the EMC effect) is due to the effect of the bound nucleon internal structure change. However, the $x$ dependence for the charged-lepton and (anti)neutrino scattering is different. The former [latter] is enhanced [quenched] in the region $0.8 \alt x \alt 0.9$ [$0.7 \alt x \alt 0.85$] due to the difference of the contribution from axial vector form factor. Because of these differences charge symmetry breaking in parton distributions will be enhanced in nuclei.

## Key claim

The bound nucleon internal structure change, introduced via in-medium form factors and a shifted pion threshold within a local quark-hadron duality framework, can explain a part of the enhancement observed in nuclear structure functions at large Bjorken-\(x\) (\(x \agt 0.85\)), but cannot explain the depletion seen in the EMC effect for \(0.7 \alt x \alt 0.85\). The enhancement and quenching patterns differ between charged-lepton and (anti)neutrino scattering due to the axial vector form factor contribution.

## Method

The authors used local quark-hadron duality to extract bound nucleon structure functions (\(F_1^*, F_2^*, g_1^*, g_2^*\) for charged-lepton scattering; \(F_1^{WN*}, F_2^{WN*}, F_3^{WN*}\) for (anti)neutrino scattering) at the in-medium pion threshold \(x^*_{th}\). The bound nucleon electromagnetic and axial vector form factors were taken from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Ratios of bound to free nucleon structure functions were calculated for symmetric nuclear matter at baryon density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), both with and without convolution over a Fermi gas momentum distribution to account for Fermi motion.

## Key numbers

- The bound nucleon structure functions for charged-lepton scattering are enhanced in the region \(0.8 \alt x \alt 0.9\) relative to free nucleon values.
- For (anti)neutrino scattering, the structure functions \(F_{2,3}^{WN*}\) are quenched in the region \(0.7 \alt x \alt 0.85\) and enhanced only in \(0.85 \alt x \alt 0.9\).
- The free-space pion threshold values \(x_{th} = 0.7\) and \(x_{th} = 0.9\) correspond to \(Q^2 \simeq 0.65\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 \simeq 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), respectively.
- The bound proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio in \(^4\)He differs by \(\sim 10\%\) from that in \(^1\)H, as measured in polarized \((\vec e, e' \vec p)\) scattering.

## Context

This work extends the local duality approach of Wally (2003) and the QMC model calculations of bound nucleon form factors (Tsushima, Saito, Steffens, 2003) to compute multiple bound nucleon structure functions for both charged-lepton and (anti)neutrino scattering. It addresses the EMC effect observed by the European Muon Collaboration (1983) and the resonance-region data from Jefferson Lab (Arrington, 2002). The results suggest that charge symmetry breaking in parton distributions, previously studied in free space by Feynman and Kisslinger (2002), would be enhanced in nuclei, potentially affecting the interpretation of the NuTeV anomaly. The authors note that conventional nuclear binding effects (Saito and Thomas, 1994) must be included in future work to fully describe the EMC depletion region.
