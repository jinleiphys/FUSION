# Folding procedure for $Ω$-$α$ potential

> Using the folding procedure, we investigate the bound state of the $Ω$+$α$ system based on $Ω$-$N$ ($^{5}S_{2}$) HAL QCD potential. Previous theoretical analyses have indicated the existence of a deeply bound ground state, which is attributed to the strong $Ω$-nucleon interaction. By employing well-established parameterizations of nucleon density within the alpha particle, and the central HAL QCD $Ω$-$N$ potential, we performed numerical calculations for the folding $Ω$-$α$ potential. Our results show that the $V_{Ωα}(r)$ potential can be accurately fitted using a Woods-Saxon function, with a phenomenological parameter $R = 1.1A^{1/3} \approx 1.74$ fm ($A=4$) in the asymptotic region where $2 < r < 3$ fm. We provide a thorough description of the corresponding numerical procedure. Our evaluation of the binding energy of the $Ω$+$α$ system within the cluster model is consistent with both previous and recent reported findings. To further validate the folding procedure, we also calculated the $Ξ$-$α$ folding potential based on a simulation of the ESC08c $Y$-$N$ Nijmegen model. A comprehensive comparison between the $Ξ$-$α$ folding and $Ξ$-$ α$ phenomenological potentials is presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The folding procedure for the $\Omega$-$\alpha$ potential yields a binding energy of approximately 20 MeV for the $^{5}_{\Omega}$He hypernucleus, but this value is accompanied by significant uncertainties (1-2 MeV variations) arising from the choice of mesh points in the Woods-Saxon fit and the assumption that the $\Omega$+$\alpha$ channel dominates in the asymptotic region $1.9 < r < 3.2$ fm.

## Method

The authors used a single folding model to construct the $\Omega$-$\alpha$ potential by convolving the HAL QCD $\Omega$-$N$ ($^{5}S_{2}$) central potential with nucleon density distributions of $^{4}$He, using both a Gaussian model (Eq. R1) and a modified Gaussian with central depression (Eq. R2) that reproduce root-mean-square matter radii of 1.70 fm and 1.56 fm respectively. The resulting folding potentials were fitted with a Woods-Saxon function $V(r)=V_{0}[1+\exp((r-R)/c)]^{-1}$ using three coordinate points $r_i$ in the asymptotic region $1.9 < r < 3.2$ fm, solving a set of nonlinear equations numerically. For validation, the same procedure was applied to the $\Xi$-$\alpha$ system using a simulated ESC08c $\Xi$-$N$ Nijmegen potential with two Yukawa-type terms.

## Key numbers

- The $\Omega$-$N$ ($^{5}S_{2}$) HAL QCD potential parameters: $b_1 = -306.5$ MeV, $b_2 = 273.9$ fm$^{-2}$, $b_3 = -266$ MeV·fm, $b_4 = 0.78$ fm$^{-2}$ (Eq. HALInteraction)
- The $\Omega$-$N$ potential produces a quasi-bound state with binding energy 1.54 MeV for $n\Omega^{-}(uddsss)$ and 2.46 MeV for $p\Omega^{-}(uudsss)$
- The phenomenological Woods-Saxon radius parameter $R = 1.1A^{1/3} \approx 1.74$ fm ($A=4$) in the asymptotic region $2 < r < 3$ fm
- The $\Omega$+$\alpha$ binding energy from the preferred Folding 1 potential (Gaussian density, $rms=1.70$ fm) is $B_2 = 20.137$ MeV with WS parameters $V_0 = -59.53$ MeV, $R = 1.715$ fm, $c = 0.539$ fm
- The $\Omega$+$\alpha$ binding energy from Folding 2 potential (modified Gaussian density, $rms=1.56$ fm) is $B_2 = 23.751$ MeV with WS parameters $V_0 = -62.714$ MeV, $R = 1.744$ fm, $c = 0.486$ fm
- The $\Xi$-$\alpha$ folding potential (Folding 4, $rms=1.70$ fm) gives binding energy $B_2 = 1.6755$ MeV with WS parameters $V_0 = -25.17$ MeV, $R = 1.697$ fm, $c = 0.383$ fm
- For comparison, the experimental binding energy of $^{5}_{\Lambda}$He is $3.12 \pm 0.02$ MeV, and the $\Xi$-$\alpha$ DG phenomenological potential gives $B_2 = 2.09$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the folding procedure previously applied to the $\Omega$+$\alpha$ system by Etminan (2020) and to the $\phi$+$\alpha$ system by Filikhin and Kezerashvili (2024), using the HAL QCD $\Omega$-$N$ potential from Iritani (2019). The authors find that the $\Omega$+$\alpha$ binding energy (approximately 20 MeV) is about ten times larger than the binding energies of similar hypernuclear systems $^{5}_{\Lambda}$He (3.12 MeV) and $^{5}_{\Xi}$He (2.09 MeV), which they attribute to the purely attractive nature of the folding potential lacking a repulsive core. The study reveals that the folding procedure for $\Xi$-$\alpha$ using the ESC08c Nijmegen model produces unstable results with a short asymptotic region, unlike the $\Omega$-$\alpha$ case, and concludes that the large binding energy is not yet a reliable value, requiring further investigation of $\Omega$-$N$ interactions at short distances.
