# Probing Fractional Quantum Hall Sheets in Dense Baryonic Matter

> Unlike the octet baryons for $N_f=3$, there is no skyrmion coming from the $η^\prime$ meson. It is instead described as a fractional quantum Hall droplet, a pancake or a pita involving a singular $η^\prime$ ring in which Chern-Simons fields live. By incorporating hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry in nuclear dynamics, I describe how to access baryon-charged quantum Hall droplets in dense nuclear matter in terms of the nuclear scale-chiral effective field theory approach ``G$n$EFT" with the $U_A(1)$ anomaly taken into account. I discuss how the single-flavor baryon that I will call ${\cal B}^s$ could be exposed in superdense baryonic matter, figuring, perhaps, in ``quark stars" associated with the baryon-quark continuity involving CFL or phase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the single-flavor baryon $\mathcal{B}^s$, described as a fractional quantum Hall droplet involving a singular $\eta^\prime$ ring with Chern-Simons fields, could become accessible in dense baryonic matter at densities beyond those probed in compact stars, by driving the system through the vector manifestation fixed point and dilaton-limit fixed point of hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry.

## Method

The author combines hidden local symmetry (HLS) and hidden scale symmetry (via the "genuine dilaton" scheme) into a scale-chiral effective field theory called G$n$EFT, which has previously been applied to compact-star matter up to densities $\sim 7 n_0$. The strategy is to extract robust topological properties from G$n$EFT (without the $\eta^\prime$ field) and inject them into a bosonic Lagrangian that includes $\eta^\prime$ and the homogeneous Wess-Zumino term, then analyze how solitons evolve at densities above the compact-star regime. The key step is taking the dilaton-limit fixed point where the $\rho$ meson decouples from nucleons and the decay constants $f_\pi^\ast = f_\chi^\ast \to 0$, which reduces the Lagrangian to a form supporting quantum Hall sheet solitons.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: $n_0 \sim 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- The skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition density $n_{1/2}$ is at $\sim (2.5 - 3.5) n_0$
- G$n$EFT applies from $\sim n_0$ to $\sim (4-7) n_0$, relevant to neutron stars
- The $\eta^\prime$ mass in matter-free space is $\sim 960$ MeV, and could drop to $\sim 100$ MeV at nuclear matter density
- The vector manifestation fixed point density $n_{VM} \gsim 25 n_0$ where the $\rho$ meson mass goes to zero
- The pseudo-conformal sound speed found in G$n$EFT is $v_s^2/c^2 \approx 1/3$, becoming conformal $v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3$ at higher densities

## Context

This work extends the G$n$EFT approach developed by Rho and collaborators (reviewed in MR-reviews) for compact-star physics, which itself builds on the skyrmion matter simulations of Park and Vento and the hidden local symmetry framework of Harada and Yamawaki. The paper specifically addresses the topological object associated with the $\eta^\prime$ meson, which Komargodski (2018) described as a fractional quantum Hall droplet, and Karasik (2020, 2021) showed can be connected to HLS vector mesons. The proposal enables future investigation of whether $\mathcal{B}^s$ baryons could appear in quark stars or in experiments studying $\eta^\prime$ mesonic nuclei in $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O etc. (Jido et al.).
