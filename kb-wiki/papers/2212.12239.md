# Medium modifications of Heavy Quarkonia masses in a generalized Linear Sigma Model

> We study the mass shifts of the charmonium ($\bar{c}c$) states ($J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $ψ(1D)$, $χ_{c0}$, $χ_{c1}$ and $χ_{c2}$) as well as the bottomonium ($\bar{b}b$) states ($Υ(1S)$, $Υ(2S)$, $Υ_2(1D)$, $χ_{b0}$, $χ_{b1}$ and $χ_{b2}$) in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. These are investigated using a generalized linear sigma model. The broken scale invariance of QCD is incorporated in the chiral $SU(2)\times SU(2)$ Lagrangian through an effective potential involving logarithmic terms of a scalar (glueball) dilaton field $χ$. The mass shifts of the quarkonium states are obtained through the medium modifications of the dilaton field which simulates the scalar gluon condensate of QCD. We observe an appreciable mass drop in the states of heavy quarkonia under this study. The in-medium masses at finite densities thus obtained should modify the in-medium partial decay widths of heavy quarkonia to open heavy flavor mesons. These density effects can be probed in in the high energy nuclear collisions at the future facility at GSI (at Germany) and JINR (at Russia) in the experiments producing highly dense baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The mass shifts of heavy quarkonia in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are obtained through medium modifications of the scalar dilaton field, which simulates the scalar gluon condensate of QCD; the observed mass drop is appreciable but depends on the non-monotonic behavior of the dilaton field with density, meaning the mass shift first increases in magnitude up to around 3 times nuclear saturation density and then decreases at higher densities.

## Method

The authors use a generalized linear sigma model based on chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry, incorporating QCD scale invariance breaking through an effective potential with logarithmic terms of a scalar dilaton (glueball) field χ. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields σ and χ, along with the vector fields ω and ρ, are solved under the mean field approximation to obtain the in-medium dilaton field values. The mass shifts of charmonium and bottomonium states are then computed at leading order using the formula Δm ∝ (χ⁴ − χ₀⁴), with the wave functions of each quarkonium state obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with a harmonic oscillator potential.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum masses (in MeV) of the charmonium states studied are: J/ψ = 3096.9, ψ(2S) = 3686.1, ψ(1D) = 3773.7, χc0 = 3414.71, χc1 = 3510.67, χc2 = 3556.17
- The vacuum masses (in MeV) of the bottomonium states studied are: Υ(1S) = 9460.3, Υ(2S) = 10023.26, Υ₂(1D) = 10163.7, χb0 = 9859.44, χb1 = 9892.78, χb2 = 9912.21
- The charm quark mass is taken as 1.95 GeV and the bottom quark mass as 5.36 GeV
- The nuclear matter saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- At ρ_B = ρ₀, the dilaton field value is 146.84 MeV for η = 0 (symmetric matter) and 146.88 MeV for η = 0.5 (asymmetric matter)
- At ρ_B = 3ρ₀, the dilaton field decreases to 145.69 MeV for η = 0 and 145.94 MeV for η = 0.5
- At ρ_B = 6ρ₀, the dilaton field becomes 146.88 MeV for η = 0 and 147.72 MeV for η = 0.5
- The vacuum value of the dilaton field is χ₀ = 148.8 MeV
- The vacuum value of the sigma field is σ₀ = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium heavy quarkonium properties using chiral effective models, following the approach of Lee and Ko (2004) who used a QCD second-order Stark effect approach for charmonium states, and the chiral SU(3) model studies of charmonium and bottomonium by Mishra and collaborators. The results are relevant for interpreting heavy ion collision experiments at future facilities such as FAIR at GSI (Germany) and NICA at JINR (Russia), where highly dense baryonic matter is produced. The in-medium masses obtained here can be used to compute modified partial decay widths of heavy quarkonia to open heavy flavor mesons, which may affect dilepton spectra and production yields in these experiments.
