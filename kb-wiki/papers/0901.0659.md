# Decay of Polarized Delta

> The resonance $Δ(1232)$ with spin-parity ${3 \over 2}^+$, which contributes dominantly to the reactions like $γN \to πN$ and $NN \to NNπ$ at intermediate energies, may be expected to be produced in characteristically different polarized spin states. As such an analysis of the decay of polarized delta is presented, which may be utilized to probe empirically the production mechanism. It is shown that measurements of the angular distributions of the pion and the polarization of the nucleon arising out of ${\vecΔ}$ decay can determine empirically the Fano statistical tensors characterizing the ${\vecΔ}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that measuring the angular distribution of the pion and the polarization of the nucleon from the decay of a polarized Δ(1232) resonance can empirically determine the Fano statistical tensors characterizing the Δ's polarization state, but only for events where the π-N invariant mass equals the Δ resonance mass.

## Method

The authors express the decay reaction matrix M for Δ → πN using transition spin operators and spherical harmonics. They then derive expressions for the pion angular distribution I(q̂) and the nucleon polarization vector P in terms of the Fano statistical tensors t^k_q (k=1,2,3) of the Δ. By expanding these observables in spherical harmonics, they show that t^2_q can be extracted from the pion angular distribution, t^1_q from the spherically symmetric term of the nucleon polarization, and t^3_q from the l=2 term of the nucleon polarization.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) resonance has spin-parity 3/2⁺.
- The NN interaction is probed at distances of 0.53 fm for π production, 0.21 fm for ω production, and 0.18 fm for φ production (citing Nakayama).
- The total cross section for pp → ppπ⁰ was found to exceed theoretical predictions by more than a factor of 5 (citing Meyer).
- The NN → NΔ reaction matrix contains 16 amplitudes, of which 10 are second rank tensors (citing Silbar).
- The Fano statistical tensors t^k_q exist for ranks k = 1, 2, 3 with q ranging from -k to k.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent irreducible tensor approach previously applied to pp → ppπ⁰ by Ramachandran and collaborators (Deepak, 2008), which showed that the Julich meson exchange model (Hanhart, 2000) deviates significantly for certain partial wave transitions. The formalism enables empirical determination of Δ polarization without relying on specific production models, and can be applied to both hadronic reactions like NN → NNπ and electromagnetic reactions like γN → πN. The authors note that while the simplest oriented Δ scenario is unlikely in NN → NNπ, the method can determine the six independent axes characterizing the full polarization state of the Δ.
