# Fragment formation in proton induced reactions within a BUU transport model

> The formation of fragments in proton-induced reactions at low relativistic energies within a combination of a covariant dynamical transport model and a statistical approach is investigated. In particular, we discuss in detail the applicability and limitations of such a hybrid model by comparing data on fragmentation at low relativistic $SIS/GSI$-energies.

## Key claim

The hybrid model combining a relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) provides a "quite satisfactory description of fragmentation data" for proton-induced reactions at low relativistic energies, but the pre-equilibrium dynamics appears to lead to a "more excited configuration with respect to the experiment," as seen in moderately smoother theoretical distributions compared to experimental data, particularly in detailed isotopic distributions.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic BUU (RBUU) transport equation with a non-linear Walecka model mean-field (compression modulus K=200 MeV) to simulate the non-equilibrium dynamics of proton-nucleus collisions, then switched to the SMM for statistical decay of the equilibrated residual source. The transition from dynamical to statistical description was controlled by monitoring the local anisotropy ratio Q(x) = 2T^{zz}(x)/(T^{xx}(x)+T^{yy}(x)) at the center of the nucleus, with equilibration defined when this ratio approached unity within 10%. The fragmenting source was defined by a density cut at rho_cut = rho_sat/100 (rho_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}), and 200 test particles per nucleon were used for each impact parameter from b=0 fm to b_max.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of the relativistic mean-field: K = 200 MeV
- Saturation density used for density cut: rho_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Equilibration time for p+Au at E_beam = 0.8 AGeV: t in [100, 120] fm/c, depending on centrality
- Binding energy fluctuations of ground state nucleus: maximal +/- 1% using 200 test particles per nucleon
- Average pre-equilibrium mass loss: <A_loss> ≈ 5, average charge loss: <Z_loss> ≈ 3
- Excitation energy per nucleon drops from <E_exc/A> ≈ 4.2 MeV to <E_exc/A> ≈ (1.5-1.7) MeV during pre-equilibrium emission
- Temperature thresholds in SMM: T < 2 MeV for evaporation/fission, T ≈ 5 MeV for onset of multifragmentation, T = 5-17 MeV for exponentially decreasing yields
- Beam energy for the main comparison: E_lab = 0.8 GeV for p+Au reactions

## Context

This work extends prior studies of fragment formation in proton-induced reactions that used intranuclear cascade (INC) models and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) prescriptions combined with SMM, as done by the INC and QMD groups (various authors, 1990s-2000s). The new feature is the use of a fully covariant relativistic BUU transport model (GiBUU) as the dynamical stage, which the authors argue is advantageous for consistent dynamics and kinematics and for future extension to hypernucleus production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results enable further investigations of hypernucleus formation using the GiBUU transport approach combined with an SMM code extended to statistical decay of fragments with finite strangeness content, as developed by Botvina (2000s).
