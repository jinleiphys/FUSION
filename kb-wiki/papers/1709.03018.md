# Exploring hadrons' partonic structure using ab initio lattice QCD calculations

> Following our previous proposal [1], we construct a class of good "lattice cross sections" (LCSs), from which we could study partonic structure of hadrons from ab initio lattice QCD calculations. These good LCSs, on the one hand, can be calculated directly in lattice QCD, and on the other hand, can be factorized into parton distribution functions (PDFs) with calculable coefficients, in the same way as QCD factorization for factorizable hadronic cross sections. PDFs could be extracted from QCD global analysis of the lattice QCD generated data of LCSs. We also show that proposed functions for lattice QCD calculation of PDFs in the literature are special cases of these good LCSs.

## Key claim

The authors construct a class of "good lattice cross sections" (LCSs) that can be calculated directly in lattice QCD and factorized into parton distribution functions (PDFs) with perturbatively calculable coefficients, provided the largest separation between fields in the nonlocal operator satisfies \(|\vec{\xi}| \ll 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\) and, for momentum-space LCSs, the condition \(|\frac{2P\cdot q}{-q^2}| < 1\) holds.

## Method

The authors define coordinate-space hadronic matrix elements of renormalized nonlocal operators, such as two-current correlations and gauge-linked quark bilinears, and show they are analytic functions of \(\omega = P\cdot\xi\) and \(P^2\xi^2\) for short-distance \(\xi\). Using the operator product expansion (OPE) and the relation between reduced matrix elements and moments of PDFs, they prove factorization into PDFs with perturbative hard coefficients. They calculate the leading-order (LO) matching coefficients for several LCS operators (scalar, vector, flavor-changing vector, and quark bilinear) by evaluating Feynman diagrams with \(P^2=0\) and expanding in powers of \(x\omega\).

## Key numbers

- The LO matching coefficient for the quark bilinear operator is \(K^{q(0)}_q(x\omega,\xi^2,0,\mu) = 2 x\omega e^{i x\omega}\).
- The LO matching coefficient for the scalar operator is \(K^{q(0)}_S(x\omega,\xi^2,0,\mu) = \frac{i}{\pi^2} x\omega (e^{i x\omega} - e^{-i x\omega})\).
- The LO matching coefficient for the flavor-changing vector operator is \(K^{q(0)}_{V'}(x\omega,\xi^2,0,\mu) = \frac{i}{\pi^2} x\omega e^{i x\omega}\) and \(K^{q'(0)}_{V'}(x\omega,\xi^2,0,\mu) = \frac{-i}{\pi^2} x\omega e^{-i x\omega}\).
- The Fourier-transformed LO matching coefficient for scalar/vector operators is \(\widetilde{K}^{q(0)}_{S/V/\widetilde{V}}(x\widetilde{\omega},q^2,0,\mu) = -2i \frac{x^2\widetilde{\omega}^2}{1 - x^2\widetilde{\omega}^2}\), which has cuts at \(\widetilde{\omega} = \pm 1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous factorization-based proposal (Ma, 2014) to construct a general class of LCSs for extracting PDFs from lattice QCD. It unifies and generalizes several existing approaches: quasi-PDFs (Ji, 2013), pseudo-PDFs (Orginos, 2017), and the "OPE without OPE" method (Chambers, 2017), showing they are special cases of the LCS framework. The approach enables QCD global analyses of lattice-generated data for PDFs, particularly at large \(x\), and can be extended to polarized PDFs, gluon distributions, and partonic structure of hadrons like neutrons, pions, and kaons that are difficult to study in scattering experiments.
