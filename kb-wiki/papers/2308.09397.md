# $K^+ N$ elastic scatterings for estimation of in-medium quark condensate with strange quarks

> We revisit the low-energy $K^+N$ elastic scatterings in the context of the in-medium quark condensate with strange quarks. The chiral ward identity connects the in-medium quark condensate to the soft limit value of the pseudoscalar correlation function evaluated in nuclear matter. The in-medium correlation function of the psuedoscalar fields with strangeness describes in-medium kaon propagation and is obtained by kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in the low density approximation. We construct the kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading order and add some terms of the next-to-next-to-leading order with the strange quark mass to improve expansion of the strange quark sector. We also consider the effect of a possible broad resonance state around $P_\mathrm{lab} = 600$ MeV/c for $I=0$ reported in the previous study. The low energy constants are determined by existent $K^+N$ scattering data. We obtain good reproduction of the $K^+p$ scattering amplitude by chiral perturbation theory, while the description of the $KN$ amplitude with $I=0$ is not so satisfactory due to the lack of low energy data. Performing analytic continuation of the scattering amplitudes obtained by chiral perturbation theory to the soft limit, we estimate the in-medium strange quark condensate.

## Key claim

The in-medium strange quark condensate \(\langle \bar uu + \bar ss \rangle^*\) can be estimated from the soft-limit value of the kaon-nucleon scattering amplitude via a chiral Ward identity and low-density theorem, but the extrapolation to the soft limit requires chiral perturbation theory amplitudes that reproduce \(K^+N\) scattering data; the description of the \(I=0\) channel remains unsatisfactory due to the lack of low-energy data.

## Method

The authors constructed kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order (NLO), supplemented by selected next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) terms proportional to the strange quark mass to improve convergence in the strange sector. Low-energy constants (LECs) were determined by fitting to existing \(K^+N\) elastic scattering data, including the possible broad resonance state around \(P_\mathrm{lab} = 600\) MeV/c for \(I=0\) reported in prior work. The resulting amplitudes were analytically continued to the soft limit \(q_\mu = 0\) and inserted into the low-density relation \(\langle \bar uu + \bar ss \rangle^* / \langle \bar uu + \bar ss \rangle_0 = 1 + (\rho / M_K^2) (T_{KN}(q=0) / 2M_N)\) to estimate the in-medium strange quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The broad resonance state considered for \(I=0\) is located around \(P_\mathrm{lab} = 600\) MeV/c, as reported in a previous study (Aoki 2018).
- The in-medium strange quark condensate is expressed as \(\langle \bar uu + \bar ss \rangle^* / \langle \bar uu + \bar ss \rangle_0 = 1 + (\rho / M_K^2) (T_{KN}(q=0) / 2M_N)\), where \(M_K\) is the kaon mass and \(M_N\) the nucleon mass.
- The kaon mass is related to the quark masses via \(M_K^2 = B_0 (m + m_s)\) at tree level.

## Context

This work extends the correlation-function approach previously applied to the non-strange quark condensate \(\langle \bar uu + \bar dd \rangle\) in nuclear matter (Jido 2008, Goda 2013, Hubsch 2021) to the strange-quark sector, using kaon-nucleon scattering instead of pion-nucleus systems. It complements evaluations via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem (Oller 2001, Meissner 2001, Kaiser 2007) and builds on prior \(K^+N\) scattering studies in chiral perturbation theory (Aoki 2017) and chiral unitary approaches (Aoki 2018). The results enable systematic comparison of partial chiral restoration across different quark flavors and motivate improved low-energy \(K^+N\) data, especially for the \(I=0\) channel.
