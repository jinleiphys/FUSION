# Form Factors and Generalized Parton Distributions in Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We calculate the elastic form factors and the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) for four low-lying bound states of a demonstration fermion-antifermion system, strong coupling positronium ($e \bar{e}$), using Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ). Using this approach, we also calculate the impact-parameter dependent GPDs $q(x, {\vec b_\perp})$ to visualize the fermion density in the transverse plane (${\vec b_\perp}$). We compare selected results with corresponding quantities in the non-relativistic limit to reveal relativistic effects. Our results establish the foundation within BLFQ for investigating the form factors and the GPDs for hadronic systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) can successfully compute elastic form factors and Generalized Parton Distributions for a model fermion-antifermion system (strong-coupling positronium), establishing a foundation for future applications to hadronic systems; however, the results are limited to the leading Fock sector \(|e\bar{e}\rangle\) and to helicity non-flip GPDs, with the total angular momentum \(J\) being only an approximate quantum number due to truncation.

## Method

The authors solve the positronium bound-state problem using BLFQ, a Hamiltonian-based non-perturbative approach that employs a basis of 2D harmonic oscillator functions in the transverse direction and discretized longitudinal momentum with anti-periodic boundary conditions. They compute elastic form factors and helicity non-flip GPDs via overlap integrals of light-front wavefunctions in the Drell-Yan frame (\(\Delta^+=0\)), and obtain impact-parameter dependent GPDs through Fourier transformation of the GPDs with respect to the transverse momentum transfer. The calculations are performed for four low-lying bound states (\(1^1S_0\), \(1^3S_1\), \(2^1S_0\), \(2^3P_0\)) at fixed truncation parameters \(N_{\text{max}}=31\), \(K=61\), coupling constant \(\alpha=0.3\), and photon mass \(\mu = 0.02 m_e\), with results compared to non-relativistic quantum mechanics (NRQM) form factors based on the multipole expansion of the one-body charge density.

## Key numbers

- Form factors are computed for four states at coupling constant \(\alpha = 0.3\), with photon mass \(\mu = 0.02 m_e\), basis truncation \(N_{\text{max}} = 31\), and longitudinal resolution \(K = 61\).
- Differences between BLFQ and NRQM form factors become evident around \(|t| \sim 2 a_\text{B}^{-2}\) for nodeless states and at \(|t| \sim 0.2 a_\text{B}^{-2}\) for states with radial nodes, where \(a_\text{B} = 1/(\alpha m_e)\) is the Bohr radius.
- Basis scale \(b\) is chosen as \(b = 0.5 m_e\) for the \(1^1S_0\) and \(1^3S_1\) states, and \(b = 0.1 m_e\) for the \(2^1S_0\) and \(2^3P_0\) states.
- Convergence of form factors with respect to \(N_{\text{max}}\) is demonstrated for the \(2^1S_0\) state, showing better convergence at lower \(|t|\).
- The parton distribution in the GPDs for all four states is peaked at \(x = 0.5\), reflecting electron-positron symmetry.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of form factors and GPDs for quark-antiquark bound states, such as those by Frederico (2009) using Mandelstam-inspired light-front models and by de Melo (2002) for pion-like systems in the Breit frame, by applying the BLFQ approach to strong-coupling positronium as a prototype for quarkonium systems. The results enable future applications to charmonium and bottomonium systems solved in the same BLFQ framework (Li, 2015), and ultimately aim toward computing form factors and GPDs for the proton in QCD.
