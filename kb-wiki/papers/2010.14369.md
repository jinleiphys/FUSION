# Constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy: the isospin transport ratio revisited

> The isospin diffusion of the quasi-projectile formed in the $^{64,58}Ni$ on $^{64,58}Ni$ reactions in the Fermi energy domain is investigated in the framework of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model. The well known isospin transport ratio observable is revisited, with the aim of insuring an optimal comparison between experimental data and theoretical calculations and reducing the present uncertainties in the extraction of empirical equation of state parameters. We show that isospin transport ratios are sensitive to all the low order isovector parameters ($E_{sym}$, $L_{sym}$ and $K_{sym}$). We demonstrate that realistic models of the equation of state, covering the uncertainty that presently affects the theoretical description of neutron stars static observables, can be effectively discriminated by isospin diffusion experiments, provided the neutron to proton ratio of the projectile remnant is precisely measured as a function of centrality.

## Key claim

The isospin transport ratio is sensitive to all low-order isovector parameters of the nuclear equation of state (EoS), \(E_{sym}\), \(L_{sym}\), and \(K_{sym}\), but the neutron-to-proton ratio of the quasi-projectile (QP) is a more robust observable than that of free nucleons for discriminating between realistic EoS models, provided the QP's neutron-to-proton ratio is precisely measured as a function of centrality.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model (BUU@VECC-McGill) to simulate \(^{64,58}\)Ni on \(^{64,58}\)Ni reactions at 52A MeV in the Fermi energy domain. The mean-field potential was derived from a meta-functional for the nuclear EoS, allowing independent variation of \(E_{sym}\), \(L_{sym}\), and \(K_{sym}\), and the isospin transport ratio \(R\) was computed from the neutron-to-proton ratio of the quasi-projectile (\(R_{QP}\)) and from free nucleons emitted from the QP (\(R_{free}\)) at a freeze-out time of 300 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{58}\)Ni on \(^{64}\)Ni reaction at 52A MeV and impact parameter \(b = 7\) fm, the isospin transport ratio \(R_{QP}\) (from QP N/Z) shows clear sensitivity to the EoS parameters, with values differing between the Bsk25, Sly5, and TM1 functionals.
- The three functionals have symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{sym}\) values of 29.00 MeV (Bsk25), 32.03 MeV (Sly5), and 36.94 MeV (TM1); slope \(L_{sym}\) values of 36.9 MeV (Bsk25), 48.3 MeV (Sly5), and 111.0 MeV (TM1); and isovector incompressibility \(K_{sym}\) values of -28 MeV (Bsk25), -112 MeV (Sly5), and 34 MeV (TM1).
- The isospin transport ratio \(R_{QP}\) saturates at approximately \(t = 300\) fm/c, independent of impact parameter and projectile-target combination.
- For the \(^{58}\)Ni on \(^{64}\)Ni reaction at 52A MeV and \(b = 7\) fm, the observable \(R_{free}\) (from free nucleons) shows differences between the three functionals that are within the error bars, while \(R_{QP}\) allows discrimination.

## Context

This work revisits the isospin transport ratio observable first proposed by Tsang et al. (2000) and later used to constrain the symmetry energy (Tsang et al., 2004, 2009; Zhang et al., 2020). It extends prior studies by using a meta-functional for the EoS (Margueron et al., 2018) that allows independent variation of \(E_{sym}\), \(L_{sym}\), and \(K_{sym}\), and by directly comparing the QP neutron-to-proton ratio (a robust transport-model prediction) with free-nucleon observables. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the INDRA/FAZIA collaboration (Manip, 2020) on \(^{64,58}\)Ni reactions at 32A MeV and 52A MeV, potentially reducing uncertainties in the isovector EoS parameters that affect neutron-star static observables.
