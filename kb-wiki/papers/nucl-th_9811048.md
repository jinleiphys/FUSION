# Imaging of Sources in Heavy-Ion Reactions

> We discuss imaging sources from low relative-velocity correlations in heavy-ion reactions. When the correlation is dominated by interference, we can obtain the images by Fourier transforming the correlation function. In the general case, we may use the method of optimized discretization. This method stabilizes the inversion by adapting the resolution of the source to the experimental error and to the measured velocities. The imaged sources contain information on freeze-out density, phase-space density, and resonance decays, among other things.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that model-independent imaging of particle-emitting sources in heavy-ion reactions is possible using correlation functions, but the inversion stability requires a method of optimized discretization that adapts the source resolution to experimental error and measured velocities, rather than using fixed bin sizes or Fourier transforms in the general case.

## Method

The authors invert the linear relation between the two-particle correlation function C(q) and the relative source function S(r) by discretizing the source and minimizing chi-squared. For cases where the kernel is dominated by interference (e.g., neutral pions or gammas), they use a Fourier transform of the correlation function. For the general case including interactions (e.g., proton pairs), they develop a method of optimized discretization that first determines optimal binning for the source based only on the q-values and errors, then extracts source values from the correlation data using that binning. The kernel K(q,r) is constructed from two-particle wavefunctions calculated by solving radial Schrodinger or Klein-Gordon equations with appropriate potentials.

## Key numbers

* For the Au + Au reaction at 10.8 GeV/nucleon, the Fourier transform of the Gamow-corrected pi- pi- correlation function was cut off at q_max = 200 MeV/c, giving a source resolution of Delta r approximately 2.0 fm and a maximum distance of r_max approximately 20 fm
* For the 75 MeV/nucleon 14N + 27Al reaction, the freeze-out density extracted from the intermediate momentum range (450-780 MeV/c) is rho_freeze approximately 0.16 rho_0, using participant multiplicity N_part = 17 and source value at r=0 of 0.0015 fm^-3
* The space-averaged phase-space density from the same intermediate momentum range is <f> approximately 0.23
* The Boltzmann equation model gives a freeze-out density of rho_freeze approximately 0.14 rho_0
* For the 75 MeV/nucleon reaction, the imaged source integrals lambda(r_max) are 0.69 +/- 0.15 (270-390 MeV/c, r_max=20.0 fm), 0.574 +/- 0.053 (450-780 MeV/c, r_max=18.8 fm), and 0.87 +/- 0.14 (840-1230 MeV/c, r_max=20.8 fm)
* For IMF sources from 84Kr + 197Au reactions, lambda(90 fm) ranges from 0.96 to 0.99 across beam energies 35-70 MeV/nucleon, while lambda(20 fm) ranges from 0.72 to 0.79
* For Au + Au at 11.4 GeV/nucleon, the Gaussian fit radius R_0 is 2.76 fm for K+ and 6.42 fm for pi-; the fitted normalization bar-lambda is 0.702 for K+ and 0.384 for pi-
* For Au + Au at 10.8 GeV/nucleon, the pi- Gaussian fit gives R_0 = 6.43 fm and bar-lambda = 0.486

## Context

This work extends the application of intensity interferometry techniques, used in astronomy for imaging stars like Betelgeuse, to nuclear reactions. The method of optimized discretization builds on the stability theory of Tikhonov (1963) and addresses the instability problems first recognized by Hadamard (1923). The authors apply their technique to data from Gong (1991) for proton-proton correlations in 14N + 27Al reactions, from Hamilton (1996) for intermediate mass fragment correlations in 84Kr + 197Au reactions, and from the E877 collaboration (Barrette, 1997) for pion and kaon correlations in Au + Au reactions. The results enable extraction of freeze-out density, phase-space density, and information on resonance decays, with qualitative differences between kaon and pion sources predicted earlier by Sullivan using the RQMD model.
