# Tunnelling amplitudes through localised external potentials from Feynman diagram summation

> Currently there is no general theory of quantum tunnelling of a particle through a potential barrier which is compatible with QFT. We present a complete calculation of tunnelling amplitudes for a scalar field for some simple potentials using quantum field-theoretic methods. Using the perturbative S-matrix formalism, starting with the Klein-Gordon Lagrangian, we show that an infinite summation of Feynman diagrams can recover tunnelling amplitudes consistent with relativistic quantum mechanics. While this work does not include many-particle effects arising from a fully quantised QFT, it is necessary to investigate QFT corrections to tunnelling amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an infinite summation of Feynman diagrams, analytically continued beyond its perturbative radius of convergence, can recover tunnelling amplitudes for a neutral scalar field through localised external potentials that are consistent with relativistic quantum mechanics, but only for systems where the Klein paradox does not arise (i.e., neutral scalar fields with no antiparticles).

## Method

The authors start with the Klein-Gordon Lagrangian for a neutral scalar field coupled to a classical external potential as a mass perturbation term. They compute the S-matrix elements perturbatively using Feynman diagram expansion, where each vertex contributes a factor proportional to the Fourier transform of the potential and internal lines carry scalar propagators. For one-dimensional Dirac delta potentials (single and double), the infinite series of diagrams forms a geometric series that can be summed analytically, and the resulting expression is analytically continued from the above-barrier scattering regime (where the series converges) into the tunnelling regime (where it does not).

## Key numbers

* For a single Dirac delta potential, the transmission coefficient is \( T = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{iaeV_0}{2p_3}} \), where \( p_3 \) is the incoming momentum, \( aV_0 \) is the barrier strength, and \( e \) is the coupling constant.
* For a single Dirac delta potential, the reflection coefficient is \( R = \frac{1}{\frac{2ip_3}{aeV_0} - 1} \), satisfying \( |R|^2 + |T|^2 = 1 \).
* For a double Dirac delta potential with separation \( b \), the transmission coefficient is \( T_{2\delta} = \frac{16 p_3^2}{(a e V_0 e^{i b p_3})^2 + (4 p_3 + i a e V_0)^2} \).
* For a double Dirac delta potential, the reflection coefficient is \( R_{2\delta} = -\frac{2 i a e V_0 [a e V_0 \sin(b p_3) + 4 p_3 \cos(b p_3)]}{(a e V_0 e^{i b p_3})^2 + (4 p_3 + i a e V_0)^2} \).

## Context

This work extends the above-barrier scattering calculations from external potentials in QFT, previously treated by Xu (2016) and de Leo (2009) using the Dirac Lagrangian, into the tunnelling regime for scalar fields. It provides a proof-of-concept that standard QFT computational tools (Feynman diagrams and S-matrix formalism) can reproduce tunnelling behaviour, serving as a necessary precursor to investigating how genuine QFT corrections (such as pair-production and virtual-particle effects) might modify tunnelling probabilities. The authors note that this approach fails for fermion systems coupled to vector potentials, where the Klein paradox arises, and highlight the pathological problems with delta function potentials in Dirac systems as discussed by Subramanian and Bhagwat (1971) and Calkin et al. (1987).
