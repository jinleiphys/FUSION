# A Study of the Di-Hadron Angular Correlation Function in Event by Event Ideal Hydrodynamics

> The di-hadron angular correlation function is computed within boost invariant, ideal hydrodynamics for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV using Monte Carlo Glauber fluctuating initial conditions. When $0<p_T< 3$ GeV, the intensity of the flow components and their phases, $\left\{v_n, Ψ_n \right \}$ ($n=2,3$), are found to be correlated on an event by event basis to the initial condition geometrical parameters $\left\{\varepsilon_{2,n}, Φ_{2,n} \right \}$, respectively. Moreover, the fluctuation of the relative phase between trigger and associated particles, $Δ_n =Ψ_n^t - Ψ_n^a$, is found to affect the di-hadron angular correlation function when different intervals of transverse momentum are used to define the trigger and the associated hadrons.

## Key claim

The di-hadron angular correlation function in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV depends not only on the flow harmonic coefficients v_n but also on the relative phase between trigger and associated particle flow angles, Δ_n = Ψ_n^t - Ψ_n^a, when the trigger and associated particles are selected from different transverse momentum intervals; this relative phase fluctuates event-by-event and can significantly modify the correlation function shape, particularly when the associated particles have low transverse momentum relative to the triggers.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the Smoothed Particles Hydrodynamics (SPH) method with Monte Carlo Glauber fluctuating initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They used the EOS S95n-v1 equation of state combining lattice QCD results with a hadron resonance gas, set the initial time τ_0 = 1 fm, and employed the Cooper-Frye prescription with freeze-out temperature T_fo = 0.14 GeV to compute final spectra of direct positively charged pions. The analysis used 1000 events per centrality window, computing the Fourier coefficients {v_n, Ψ_n} for n = 2,3,4 and correlating them with the initial geometry parameters {ε_{2,n}, Φ_{2,n}}.

## Key numbers

- For central (0-5)% collisions with associated particles in 0 < p_T^a < 1 GeV and triggers in 3 < p_T^trigg < 5 GeV: ⟨cos(2Δ_2)⟩ = 0.456, indicating significant deviation from unity due to relative phase fluctuations.
- For peripheral (20-30)% collisions with the same p_T bins: ⟨cos(2Δ_2)⟩ = 0.931, showing much stronger alignment of flow angles.
- Linear correlation coefficient λ_2 between ε_{2,2} and v_2 in (0-5)% centrality for 2 < p_T < 3 GeV: λ_2 = 0.612.
- Linear correlation coefficient λ_2 in (20-30)% centrality: closer to unity than in central collisions (exact value not given in text).
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo = 0.14 GeV.
- Initial time: τ_0 = 1 fm.
- Maximum average temperature in (0-5)% centrality: 0.31 GeV.
- Total expansion time in (0-5)% centrality: approximately 15 fm; in (20-30)% centrality: approximately 10 fm.
- Number of events computed: 1000.

## Context

This work extends studies of harmonic flow and initial condition fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, building on the finding by Takahashi et al. (2009) that fluctuating tube-like initial conditions can produce observed di-hadron correlation structures. It connects to the work of Gardim et al. (2012) and Heinz et al. (2013) who studied relative phase fluctuations in 3+1 and 2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, respectively. The results show that the factorization approximation for the correlation function coefficients becomes reasonable after 1000 events, and that the relative phase distribution is even (sine factors average to zero), restoring parity of the di-hadron correlation function in the large-event limit. The study enables more precise modeling of di-hadron correlations when trigger and associated particles are from different p_T bins, which is relevant for understanding jet-medium interactions and the ridge phenomenon.
