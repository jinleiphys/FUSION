# Proton radii of 4,6,8He isotopes from high-precision nucleon-nucleon interactions

> Recently, precision laser spectroscopy on 6He atoms determined accurately the isotope shift between 4He and 6He and, consequently, the charge radius of 6He. A similar experiment for 8He is under way. We have performed large-scale ab initio calculations for 4,6,8He isotopes using high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions within the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach. With the CD-Bonn 2000 NN potential we found point-proton root-mean-square (rms) radii of 4He and 6He 1.45(1) fm and 1.89(4), respectively, in agreement with experiment and predict the 8He point proton rms radius to be 1.88(6) fm. At the same time, our calculations show that the recently developed nonlocal INOY NN potential gives binding energies closer to experiment, but underestimates the charge radii.

## Key claim

Using the CD-Bonn 2000 nucleon-nucleon potential within the no-core shell model, the authors obtain point-proton root-mean-square radii for 4He and 6He that agree with experiment and predict the 8He point-proton radius to be 1.88(6) fm; however, the INOY potential, while giving binding energies closer to experiment, underestimates the charge radii of all three isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 4He, 6He, and 8He using two high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials: the CD-Bonn 2000 and the INOY (Inside Nonlocal Outside Yukawa) potentials. They solved the many-body problem in a finite harmonic oscillator basis using a two-body effective Hamiltonian derived from a unitary transformation, with model spaces up to Nmax=22 for 4He, Nmax=16 for 6He, and Nmax=12 for 8He, and diagonalized the effective Hamiltonian using the Antoine shell-model code adapted for NCSM applications.

## Key numbers

- 4He point-proton rms radius with CD-Bonn 2000: 1.45(1) fm, in agreement with the experimental value of 1.455(1) fm
- 6He point-proton rms radius with CD-Bonn 2000: 1.89(4) fm, in agreement with the experimental value of 1.912(18) fm
- 8He point-proton rms radius predicted with CD-Bonn 2000: 1.88(6) fm
- 4He point-proton rms radius with INOY: 1.37(1) fm, underestimating experiment
- 6He point-proton rms radius with INOY: 1.76(3) fm, underestimating experiment
- 8He point-proton rms radius with INOY: 1.74(6) fm
- 4He binding energy with CD-Bonn 2000: 26.16(6) MeV, underbinding the experimental value of 28.296 MeV by about 2 MeV
- 4He binding energy with INOY: 29.10(5) MeV, overbinding the experimental value by 800 keV
- 6He binding energy with CD-Bonn 2000: 26.9(3) MeV, underbinding the experimental value of 29.269 MeV
- 8He binding energy with CD-Bonn 2000: 26.0(4) MeV, underbinding the experimental value of 31.408(7) MeV
- 6He point-neutron rms radius with CD-Bonn 2000: 2.67(5) fm, within the experimental range of 2.59-2.85 fm
- 8He point-neutron rms radius with CD-Bonn 2000: 2.80(10) fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.69(4) fm

## Context

This work extends the ab initio NCSM approach to describe the helium isotope chain, complementing Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations by Pieper et al. (2004) that used Argonne NN potentials. The results show that the CD-Bonn 2000 potential, like other standard high-precision NN interactions, underbinds the helium isotopes and must be augmented by a three-nucleon interaction, while the INOY potential, which was fine-tuned to reproduce A=3 binding energies by Doleschall et al. (2003, 2004), gives better binding energies but fails on radii. The predicted 8He point-proton radius provides a benchmark for the ongoing precision laser spectroscopy experiment to determine the 8He charge radius.
