# JIMWLK Evolution, Lindblad Equation and Quantum-Classical Correspondence

> In the Color Glass Condensate(CGC) effective theory, the physics of valence gluons with large longitudinal momentum is reflected in the distribution of color charges in the transverse plane. Averaging over the valence degrees of freedom is effected by integrating over classical color charges with some quasi probability weight functional $W[{\mathbf{j}}]$ whose evolution with rapidity is governed by the JIMWLK equation. In this paper, we reformulate this setup in terms of effective quantum field theory on valence Hilbert space governed by the reduced density matrix $\hatρ$ for hard gluons, which is obtained after properly integrating out the soft gluon "environment". We show that the evolution of this density matrix with rapidity in the dense and dilute limits has the form of Lindblad equation. The quasi probability distribution (weight) functional $W$ is directly related to the reduced density matrix $\hatρ$ through the generalization of the Wigner-Weyl quantum-classical correspondence, which reformulates quantum dynamics on Hilbert space in terms of classical dynamics on the phase space. In the present case the phase space is non Abelian and is spanned by the components of transverse color charge density ${\mathbf{j}}$. The same correspondence maps the Lindblad equation for $\hatρ$ into the JIMWLK evolution equation for $W$ .

## Key claim
The paper shows that JIMWLK evolution can be understood as a Lindblad-type equation for a reduced density matrix of hard gluons, but only in the dense (JIMWLK) and dilute (KLWMIJ) limits; away from these limits, the evolution is unlikely to be of Lindblad form due to the role of rapidity as both an evolution parameter and a label on quantum states of integrated-out gluons.

## Method
The authors reformulate the Color Glass Condensate effective theory by treating the system as bipartite (hard gluons as observed system, soft gluons as environment) and deriving the reduced density matrix for hard gluons after tracing over soft gluon degrees of freedom. They compute the evolution of this density matrix with rapidity using the soft gluon ground state wavefunction, expressed through coherent and Bogoliubov operators, and show that in the dilute and dense limits the differential evolution takes the Lindblad form. The mapping between the quantum density matrix and the classical weight functional W[j] is established via a generalization of the Wigner-Weyl correspondence to the non-Abelian phase space spanned by color charge density operators.

## Key numbers
- The color charge density operators satisfy SU(N) commutation relations: [j^a(x), j^b(y)] = i g f^{abc} j^c(x) δ(x - y)
- The dilute limit corresponds to color charge j^a ~ g (parametrically small)
- The dense limit corresponds to color charge j^a ~ 1/g (parametrically large)
- The soft gluon longitudinal momentum integration in the coherent operator runs between Λ^+ e^{-Δy} and Λ^+

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of JIMWLK evolution (Balitsky 1995, Jalilian-Marian et al. 1997, Iancu et al. 2000, Ferreiro et al. 2001) by providing a quantum density matrix formulation, motivated by a suggestion from Armesto et al. (2019) that the generalized CGC density matrix evolution might be of Lindblad type. The paper builds on the Hamiltonian formalism of Kovner (2005, 2007) and the wavefunction calculations of Altinoluk et al. (2009). The results enable a clearer quantum-classical correspondence between the density matrix and the weight functional W[j] used in standard CGC calculations, and clarify the conditions under which Lindblad-type evolution can be expected in high-energy QCD.
