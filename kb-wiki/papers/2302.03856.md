# $g$-mode of neutron stars in pseudo-Newtonian gravity

> The equation of state (EOS) of nuclear dense matter plays a crucial role in many astrophysical phenomena associated with neutron stars (NSs). Fluid oscillations are one of the most fundamental properties therein. NSs support a family of gravity $g$-modes, which are related to buoyancy. We study the gravity $g$-modes caused by composition gradient and density discontinuity in the framework of pseudo-Newtonian gravity. The mode frequencies are calculated in detail and compared with Newtonian and general-relativistic (GR) solutions. We find that the $g$-mode frequencies in one of the pseudo-Newtonian treatments can approximate remarkably well the GR solutions, with relative errors in the order of $1\%$. Our findings suggest that, with much less computational cost, pseudo-Newtonian gravity can be utilized to accurately analyze oscillation of NSs constructed from an EOS with a first-order phase transition between nuclear and quark matter, as well as to provide an excellent approximation of GR effects in core-collapse supernova (CCSN) simulations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that one pseudo-Newtonian treatment (Case A potential with lapse function) can approximate general-relativistic g-mode frequencies for neutron stars with relative errors of about 1%, but this accuracy depends on the specific pseudo-Newtonian scheme used and the inclusion of a lapse function.

## Method

The authors solved linearized non-radial perturbation equations for quadrupole (ℓ=2) g-modes in non-rotating neutron stars using six different gravitational schemes: Newtonian, Newtonian with lapse function, Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) general relativity, TOV with lapse function, Case A pseudo-Newtonian potential, and Case A potential with lapse function. They constructed equilibrium configurations using two nuclear equations of state (AU and UU) based on microscopic calculations from Wiringa (1988), including composition gradients from beta-equilibrated neutron-proton-electron matter and density discontinuities from first-order phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- The Case A+lapse scheme approximates GR g-mode frequencies with relative errors in the order of 1%
- For model UU, the Case A formulation has absolute percentage differences of 5-17% for the stellar radius compared to GR solutions
- The central density used for comparison was 2.0 × 10^15 g cm^-3 for model UU
- The g-mode frequencies for a polytropic model with Γ₁=2.15 gave g₁=532 Hz, g₂=369 Hz, and g₃=284 Hz for the first three quadrupolar modes
- The f-mode frequency for the same polytropic model was 2032 Hz with Γ₁=2.15

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron star oscillations by Tang (2021) on radial and non-radial modes in pseudo-Newtonian gravity, and builds on the g-mode calculations of Lai (1993) for composition-gradient modes in Newtonian gravity. The results enable computationally efficient approximations of general-relativistic effects in core-collapse supernova simulations, where full GR hydrodynamic codes remain scarce and expensive, and can be applied to analyze oscillations of hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions between nuclear and quark matter.
