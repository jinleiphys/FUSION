# Anisotropic heavy quark potential in strongly-coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM in a magnetic field

> In this work we use the gauge/gravity duality to study the anisotropy in the heavy quark potential in strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super-Yang Mills (SYM) theory (both at zero and nonzero temperature) induced by a constant and uniform magnetic field $\mathcal{B}$. At zero temperature, the inclusion of the magnetic field decreases the attractive force between heavy quarks with respect to its $\mathcal{B}=0$ value and the force associated with the parallel potential is the least attractive force. We find that the same occurs at nonzero temperature and, thus, at least in the case of strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM, the presence of a magnetic field generally weakens the interaction between heavy quarks in the plasma.

## Key claim

The magnetic field generally weakens the interaction between heavy quarks in strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM, both at zero and nonzero temperature, with the parallel potential (pair axis aligned with the magnetic field) being the least attractive; however, this conclusion is explicitly hedged as applying "at least in the case of strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM."

## Method

The authors use the gauge/gravity duality (holography) to compute the heavy quark potential from rectangular Wilson loops in the D'Hoker-Kraus holographic dual of $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM with a constant magnetic field. At zero temperature, they numerically solve the Einstein-Maxwell equations in an asymptotically $AdS_5$ background that interpolates between $AdS_3\times\mathbb{R}^2$ in the infrared and $AdS_5$ in the ultraviolet; at nonzero temperature, they use the corresponding anisotropic black brane solution. The parallel and perpendicular potentials are obtained by evaluating the Nambu-Goto action for U-shaped string configurations with the quark-antiquark axis either along or perpendicular to the magnetic field direction, followed by regularization of the quark self-energies.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the isotropic $\mathcal{B}=0$ potential is $\sim -0.228/L$ (in units where $\alpha'=1$).
- The physical magnetic field in the gauge theory at zero temperature is $\mathcal{B}=\sqrt{3}B/1.12365\approx 5.34$ (in the units of the numerical solution).
- The infrared expansion of the metric functions yields $a=a_+\approx 0.758$ for the exponent in $W(r)$ and $\omega\approx -0.634$ for the next coefficient.
- The bulk magnetic field parameter $b$ must satisfy $b<2\sqrt{3}$ to maintain asymptotic $AdS_5$ behavior; the physical magnetic field $\mathcal{B}=\sqrt{3}\,b/\omega(b)$ can cover $[0,\infty)$ because $\omega(b\rightarrow 2\sqrt{3})\rightarrow 0$.
- At finite temperature, the Hawking temperature is fixed to $T=1/(4\pi)$ by the coordinate rescaling $h'(1)=1$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of magnetic field effects on strongly coupled plasmas, following the D'Hoker-Kraus construction (D'Hoker and Kraus, 2009) of the dual geometry and the earlier finding by Critelli et al. (2014) that the shear viscosity tensor becomes anisotropic in this setup. It is motivated by lattice QCD results on magnetic field effects on the heavy quark potential (Bonati et al., 2014) and provides a holographic counterpart at strong coupling. The results enable future studies of anisotropic bound state properties and the solution of the Schrödinger equation for this anisotropic potential, as well as comparisons with lattice data at finite temperature.
