# Ab Initio Coupling of Jets to Collective Flow in the Opacity Expansion Approach

> We calculate the leading corrections to jet momentum broadening and medium-induced branching that arise from the velocity of the moving medium at first order in opacity. These results advance our knowledge of jet quenching and demonstrate how it couples to collective flow of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and to the orbital motion of partons in cold nuclear matter in deep inelastic scattering at the electron-ion collider. We also compute the leading corrections to jet momentum broadening due to transverse gradients of temperature and density. We find that these effects lead to both anisotropic transverse momentum diffusion proportional to the medium velocity and anisotropic medium-induced radiation emitted preferentially in the direction of the flow. We isolate the relevant sub-eikonal corrections by working with jets composed of scalar particles with arbitrary color factors interacting with the medium by scalar QCD. Appropriate substitution of the color factors and light-front wave functions allow us to immediately apply the results to a range of processes including $q \rightarrow q g$ branching in real QCD. The resulting general expressions can be directly coupled to hydrodynamic simulations on an event-by-event basis to study the correlations between jet quenching and the dynamics of various forms of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading sub-eikonal corrections to jet momentum broadening and medium-induced branching that arise from the velocity of a moving medium at first order in opacity, showing that these effects produce anisotropic transverse momentum diffusion and anisotropic medium-induced radiation preferentially emitted in the direction of flow, but only within the scalar QCD approximation and at first order in the opacity expansion.

## Method

The authors work within the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) opacity expansion formalism, treating jets composed of scalar particles with arbitrary color factors interacting with the medium via scalar QCD. They compute the external gluonic potential generated by moving medium particles, then derive the single Born and double Born scattering diagrams including velocity and transverse gradient corrections, and finally calculate the in-medium splitting amplitudes for the $\phi \rightarrow \phi \phi$ branching process.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at first order in opacity, corresponding to a single scattering of the jet with the medium
- The jet energy $E$ is taken to be much larger than the medium momentum scales characterized by temperature $T$ or Debye mass $\mu = g T$, with $E \gg \mu$
- The sub-eikonal corrections scale as $\sim \mathcal{O}(\frac{\bot}{E})$ where $\bot$ represents characteristic transverse scales such as $|\vec{p}_\bot|$ or $\mu$
- The elastic scattering cross section in Regge kinematics scales as $\frac{d\sigma}{d|t|} \sim \frac{1}{s^2} \langle |\mathcal{M}|^2 \rangle \sim \text{const}$ in the eikonal limit
- For $t$-channel scalar exchange, the cross section scales as $\sigma \sim E^{-2}$, while for $t$-channel quark exchange it scales as $\sigma \sim E^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the GLV opacity expansion formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 1999-2000) by including sub-eikonal corrections from medium motion, which were previously neglected. It parallels holographic studies of gradient corrections to drag force (Lekaveckas, 2013; Rajagopal, 2015; Sadofyev, 2015) but within a perturbative QCD framework. The results can be directly coupled to hydrodynamic simulations on an event-by-event basis, advancing the program of jet tomography in heavy-ion collisions and enabling tomographic studies at the electron-ion collider.
