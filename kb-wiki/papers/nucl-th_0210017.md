# Estimate of crossed-boson-exchange contributions to the binding energy of two-body systems

> Binding energies calculated from using the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the simplest ladder approximation significantly differ from those obtained in the non-relativistic standard instantaneous approximation. While they should a priori be better, they turn out to be further away from an exact calculation in the case of scalar neutral particles or from experiment in the case of the Coulomb interaction. Part of the discrepancy is due to the omission in the interaction kernel of contributions corresponding to crossed-boson-exchange diagrams. The role of these contributions is examined numerically, using a simple approximation. The sensitivity to both the coupling constant and the mass of the exchanged boson is considered.

## Key claim

The contribution of crossed-boson-exchange diagrams to the binding energy of two-body systems is numerically estimated, showing that the convergence to the instantaneous-approximation result is slow except for very low binding energies; the authors assume the cancellation between retardation and crossed-boson effects holds exactly, which is a condition that limits the generality of the result.

## Method

The authors start from an energy-dependent single-boson-exchange interaction derived from second-order perturbation theory and remove the energy dependence via a self-consistency equation to obtain an effective local potential. They then expand this effective potential in a series whose terms correspond to crossed-boson-exchange diagrams of increasing order, truncate the expansion at a given order, solve the resulting Schrödinger equation, and compute the binding energy as a function of the number of exchanged bosons. The study is performed at lowest order in the inverse of the constituent mass, neglecting non-locality and relativistic corrections.

## Key numbers

- For coupling constant α = 0.5 and boson mass μ = 0.15 m, the first-order crossed diagram provides more than 2/3 of the missing binding energy in the least bound case (E = -0.013 m).
- For the same coupling α = 0.5 but with μ = 0, the convergence to the asymptotic binding energy is slower than for μ = 0.15 m.
- For α = 1.5 and μ = 0.15 m or μ = 0.5 m, the first-order crossed diagram provides only about one-third of the missing binding energy, and including crossed diagrams up to order 7 still leaves a discrepancy of 7-8% from the asymptotic value.
- The convergence slows as the coupling strength increases (comparing α = 0.5 and α = 1.5 at fixed μ = 0.15 m) and as the boson mass decreases.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of retardation effects in the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Nieuwenhuis and Tjon, 1996; Philipsen et al., 1996; Amghar and Desplanques, 1998) and of crossed-boson-exchange contributions (Theussl et al., 1999; Amghar and Desplanques, 2000; Tjon, 1999). The authors find that their results agree with the more modest contributions found by Amghar and Desplanques (2000) and Tjon (1999), but disagree with the much larger effects reported by Theussl et al. (1999). The work enables future studies that would need to include relativistic corrections and solve a three-body problem to sum the crossed-diagram series more accurately.
