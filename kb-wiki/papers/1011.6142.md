# Rapid Cooling of the Neutron Star in Cassiopeia A Triggered by Neutron Superfluidity in Dense Matter

> We propose that the observed cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A is due to enhanced neutrino emission from the recent onset of the breaking and formation of neutron Cooper pairs in the 3P2 channel. We find that the critical temperature for this superfluid transition is ~0.5x10^9 K. The observed rapidity of the cooling implies that protons were already in a superconducting state with a larger critical temperature. Our prediction that this cooling will continue for several decades at the present rate can be tested by continuous monitoring of this neutron star.

## Key claim

The observed rapid cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A is triggered by enhanced neutrino emission from the recent onset of neutron Cooper pair breaking and formation in the 3P2 channel, with a critical temperature of approximately 0.5 × 10^9 K; the rapidity of the cooling implies that protons were already in a superconducting state with a larger critical temperature.

## Method

The authors performed numerical solutions of the general relativistic energy balance and heat transport equations for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star built from the APR equation of state, with microphysical inputs including modified Urca and nucleon bremsstrahlung neutrino emission processes. They also developed an analytical model based on energy balance between the star's total specific heat and neutrino luminosity, deriving scaling relations for the internal temperature evolution during the superfluid phase transition. The models included a carbon atmosphere envelope with light element mass compatible with observations of Cas A's surface composition.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for neutron 3P2 superfluidity: T_C ≈ 0.5 × 10^9 K, deduced from the age of Cas A (330 years) and the timing of the cooling onset
- Observed surface temperature decrease: from 2.12 × 10^6 K to 2.04 × 10^6 K over the period 2000-2009 (10-year interval)
- Observed cooling slope over 10 years: s_obs ≈ -1.4 (in log-log space)
- Distance to Cas A: 3.4^{+0.3}_{-0.1} kpc
- Emitting radius from spectral fits: 8-17 km
- Modified Urca cooling timescale: τ_MU ∼ 1 year
- PBF neutrino emission enhancement factor relative to modified Urca: f ∼ 10
- Transit timescale for rapid cooling: τ_TR = t_C/f, giving several decades for Cas A
- Model slopes for different masses at current age: -1.4 (1.3 M⊙), -0.9 (1.6 M⊙), -0.5 (1.9 M⊙)

## Context

This work extends the "Minimal Cooling" paradigm of Page (2004) and Yakovlev (2004) by providing the first observational evidence for neutron 3P2 superfluidity in a neutron star core, using the young Cas A neutron star discovered by Tananbaum (1999). The results contradict alternative interpretations by Heinke (2010) involving crustal energy release or fossil disk accretion, which predict the cooling should flatten soon, whereas the authors predict continued cooling at a nearly constant rate for several decades. The inferred critical temperature is compatible with the lower bound T_C ≥ 0.5 × 10^9 K required by the minimal cooling paradigm of Page (2009).
