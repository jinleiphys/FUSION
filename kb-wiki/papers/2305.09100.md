# On cancellation of non-adiabatic and off-shell effects in the antiproton annihilation in deuteron

> As known, some approximate approaches to the hadron scattering from nuclei work rather well far beyond the limits of their applicability. This was explained by cancellation of the contributions (non-adiabatic and off-shell effects) omitted in these approaches. Moreover, in some cases (in particular, for the reaction $\bar{p}d \to e^+e^-n$) this cancellation allowed to derive rather simple analytical formula for the reaction amplitude. Solving the Faddeev equations, we confirm numerically this formula and, hence, the cancellations.

## Key claim

By directly solving the Faddeev equations for the $\bar{p}pn$ system, the authors numerically confirm that the cancellation of non-adiabatic and off-shell effects in the reaction $\bar{p}d \to e^+e^-n$ restores the amplitude to an impulse-approximation-like form but with a shifted, always-positive $\bar{p}p$ energy, thereby preventing access to the proton form factor below the $\bar{p}p$ threshold except near bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in coordinate space for the three-body $\bar{p}pn$ system, keeping only S-waves and omitting the $\bar{p}n$ interaction. They used the Hulthen and exponential potentials for the $\bar{p}p$ and $pn$ interactions. From the resulting three-body continuous spectrum wave function, they computed the transition amplitude to the $e^+e^-n$ final state via a delta-function annihilation operator, and compared the numerical results to the analytical impulse approximation formula $M_u$ and the corrected formula $M_v$ derived in earlier work.

## Key numbers

* The impulse approximation amplitude $M_u$ uses the $\bar{p}p$ energy $u = \frac{3(p_{\bar{p}}^2 - p_n^2)}{4m} - |\epsilon_d|$, which can be negative (below threshold).
* The corrected amplitude $M_v$ uses the shifted $\bar{p}p$ energy $v = \frac{(p_n + 2p_{\bar{p}})^2}{4m} > 0$, which is always positive.
* The accuracy of the analytical formula $M_v$ was estimated in prior work (Dalkarov 1981, 1983) as 30%; the present numerical Faddeev confirmation achieves better than that 30% precision.
* The incident antiproton momenta considered are low: below 52.8 MeV/c in the $\bar{p}d$ c.m. frame (or below 79.2 MeV/c in the lab frame) to avoid breakup to the $\bar{p}pn$ asymptotic state.

## Context

This work extends the analytical studies of Dalkarov (1981, 1983), who derived the cancellation of non-adiabatic and off-shell effects in the $\bar{p}d \to e^+e^-n$ reaction using diagrammatic analysis. It provides an independent numerical confirmation via direct solution of the Faddeev equations, a method not available at the time of the earlier work. The result confirms that the simple impulse approximation formula is distorted by multiple scattering, and that the corrected formula $M_v$ prevents extraction of the proton form factor in the time-like region below the $\bar{p}p$ threshold, except near $\bar{p}p$ bound states.
