# Baryon clustering at the critical line and near the hypothetical critical point in heavy-ion collisions

> We study clustering of baryons at the freeze-out point of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using a Walecka-Serot model for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction we analyze how the modified/critical $σ$ mode---responsible for the NN attraction---allows for clustering of nucleons when the system is close to a possible critical point of QCD. We investigate clusters of few nucleons, and also the internal cluster configuration when the system is long lived. For realistic heavy-ion collisions we study to how extend such clusters can be formed in a finite time, and perform the statistical analysis of cumulants and higher-order moments (skewness and kurtosis) for collisions at the Beam Energy Scan of RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relatively small modifications to the inter-nucleon potential, specifically those expected near a hypothetical QCD critical point, can dramatically enhance baryon clustering at freeze-out, and that this clustering, rather than critical fluctuations alone, may explain the observed growth in higher-order cumulants of net-baryon distributions in the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC.

## Method

The authors use a Walecka-Serot model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, implementing four versions of the potential with progressively reduced sigma meson mass (down to m_sigma^2/6) to mimic critical modifications. They perform classical molecular dynamics simulations supplemented by Langevin forces to represent the mesonic heat bath, studying systems from few nucleons up to N~100 particles at freeze-out temperatures T~80-100 MeV. They also calculate self-consistent mean-field clusters analogous to globular clusters in galaxies, and compute the resulting skewness and kurtosis of baryon number distributions.

## Key numbers

- The unmodified Walecka potential (model A) gives total energy per nucleon of +2.0 MeV for N=20 in a Gaussian cluster with r.m.s. radius R=2 fm, while the modified potential B2 gives -35.4 MeV/nucleon and model C gives -53.9 MeV/nucleon for the same cluster.
- For self-consistent mean-field clusters with r.m.s. radius R=2.2 fm, model A binds 25.4 nucleons with mean potential -180 MeV/nucleon, model B2 binds 10.5 nucleons with -113 MeV/nucleon, and model C binds 7.8 nucleons with -119 MeV/nucleon.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature for high-energy collisions is T_ch ~ 150 MeV, while the kinetic freeze-out temperature for baryons is T_kin ~ 80-100 MeV.
- For the BES conditions, the baryonic chemical potential is in the range bar-mu = 500-700 MeV.
- The critical temperature T_c is approximately 120-155 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon clustering from low-energy multifragmentation (Bondorf 1995) to the intermediate-energy regime of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, where the system is near a possible QCD critical point. It builds on the proposal by Stephanov (1998, 1999) to search for the critical point using enhanced fluctuations, and on experimental observations of large kurtosis at low energies (Luo 2015). The paper suggests that out-of-equilibrium production of light nuclei (deuterium, tritium, He-3, He-4) should be studied alongside higher-order moments, and introduces an analogy to globular cluster dynamics to describe partially-clustered out-of-equilibrium stages.
