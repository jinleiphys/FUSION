# Decuplet contribution to the meson-baryon scattering lengths

> We calculate decuplet contributions to the s-wave pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering lengths to the third order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT). Using experimental pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering lengths as inputs, we determine low-energy constants and predict other meson-baryon scattering lengths. Numerically we consider three cases: (1) the case with only baryon octet contributions; (2) with decuplet contributions and (3) in the large N_c limit. Hopefully, the analytical expressions and the predictions are helpful to future investigations of the meson-baryon scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The paper calculates decuplet baryon contributions to s-wave pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering lengths to third order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the decuplet loop contributions generate divergences proportional to \(\delta m^2\) that cannot be absorbed by the available counter-terms, so these divergent analytical parts are simply ignored in the numerical evaluation.

## Method

The authors computed all one-loop diagrams with intermediate decuplet baryons contributing at \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\) in the small scale expansion (SSE) framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, using the complete third-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian to include counter-terms explicitly. They determined the low-energy constants (LECs) by fitting six experimental pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering lengths as inputs, then predicted all other meson-baryon scattering lengths for three cases: with only octet baryon contributions, with decuplet contributions included, and in the large \(N_c\) limit where the octet-decuplet mass difference \(\delta\) vanishes.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon scattering lengths used as inputs: \(a_{\pi N}^{+} = -0.0001^{+0.0009}_{-0.0021} \, m_\pi^{-1}\), \(a_{\pi N}^{-} = 0.0885^{+0.0021}_{-0.0010} \, m_\pi^{-1}\)
* Kaon-nucleon scattering lengths used as inputs: \(a_{KN}^{(1)} = -0.33 \,{\rm fm}\), \(a_{KN}^{(0)} = 0.02 \,{\rm fm}\), \(a_{\overline{K}N}^{(1)} = 0.37 + 0.60 i \,{\rm fm}\), \(a_{\overline{K}N}^{(0)} = -1.70 + 0.68 i \,{\rm fm}\)
* Decuplet-octet mass difference: \(\delta = 294 \,{\rm MeV}\)
* Coupling constant for decuplet-octet-meson vertex: \({\cal C} = -1.5\)
* With decuplet contributions, the predicted \(\pi\Lambda\) scattering length: \(a_{\pi\Lambda} = -0.041 \pm 0.004 \,{\rm fm}\)
* With decuplet contributions, the predicted \(\eta N\) scattering length: \(a_{\eta N} = (-0.29 \pm 0.07) + 0.42 i \,{\rm fm}\)
* With decuplet contributions, the predicted \(\eta\Lambda\) scattering length: \(a_{\eta\Lambda} = (-0.60 \pm 0.11) + 0.89 i \,{\rm fm}\)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of meson-baryon scattering lengths in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory by Liu and Zhu (2006) and the kaon-nucleon calculations by Kaiser (1997), which included only octet baryon intermediate states. The inclusion of decuplet baryons is motivated by the moderate mass difference \(\delta = 294\) MeV and the large coupling constant \({\cal C}\), and follows the small scale expansion formalism of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998). The results provide predictions for many unmeasured meson-baryon scattering lengths that can be tested by future experiments and lattice QCD calculations, though the incomplete renormalization of the decuplet contributions remains an open issue.
