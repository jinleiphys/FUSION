# Strange tribaryons

> We use two-body potentials derived from a constituent quark cluster model to analyze the bound-state problem of the $ΣNN$ system. The observables of the two-body subsystems, $NN$ and $ΣN$, are well reproduced. We do not find $ΣNN$ bound states, but there are two attractive channels with a resonance close above the three-body threshold. These channels are the $(I,J)=(1,1/2)$ and $(0,1/2)$, their quantum numbers, widths and energy ordering consistent with the recently measured strange tribaryons from the $^{4}{\rm He}(K_{\rm stopped}^{-},N)$ reactions in the KEK PS E471 experiment.

## Key claim

Using two-body potentials derived from a constituent quark cluster model, the authors find no bound states in the ΣNN system, but identify two attractive channels with resonances close above the three-body threshold, the (I,J) = (1,1/2) and (0,1/2) channels, whose quantum numbers, widths, and energy ordering are consistent with the strange tribaryons S⁰(3115) and S⁺(3140) measured in the KEK PS E471 experiment, though the model's baseline calculation does not produce bound states without artificially increasing the attraction.

## Method

The authors used two-body potentials derived from a constituent quark cluster model, including confinement, one-gluon exchange, and meson-exchange terms (π, σ, K, η), to construct NN and ΣN interactions that reproduce low-energy scattering data. They solved the three-body ΣNN system using Faddeev equations in a truncated T-matrix approximation, where the two-body t-matrices were expanded in Legendre polynomials and only S-wave components were retained, with the orbital angular momentum in initial and final states set to zero. The calculations were performed both with and without coupling to the ΛNN channels, and the Fredholm determinant method was used to search for bound states and resonances.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy obtained from the model in the truncated T-matrix approximation is 6.90 MeV (compared to 6.58 MeV for the Reid soft-core potential in the same approximation).
- The experimentally measured S⁰(3115) tribaryon has mass 3117.0⁺¹.⁵₋₄.₄ MeV and width < 21 MeV, with isospin I=1 and strangeness S=-1.
- The experimentally measured S⁺(3140) tribaryon has mass 3140.5⁺³.⁰₋₀.₈(syst) ± 2.3(stat) MeV and width < 21.6 MeV, with isospin I=0.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter for strange baryons was set to b_s = 0.7 fm to reproduce ΣN scattering cross sections.
- When the model's attraction was artificially increased (b_s = 0.6 fm), the I=1, J=1/2 channel developed a bound state near threshold, while the I=0, J=1/2 channel showed a resonance close above the three-body threshold.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-baryon systems (ΔNN, ΔΔN, ΔΔΔ) by the same authors (Gar97, Mot99, Gar99) using the same constituent quark cluster model formalism. The results contradict earlier theoretical predictions of deeply bound K⁻³He states by Akaishi (2002), which predicted an I=0 state at about 3195 MeV with an I=1 state 87 MeV higher, the opposite isospin ordering to the experimentally observed tribaryons. The present calculation supports the interpretation of the KEK PS E471 experimental results by Suzuki (2004, 2005) and enables further studies of the role of Λ-Σ conversion and relativistic effects in understanding strange tribaryon structure.
