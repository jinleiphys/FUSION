# QCD Sum Rules for Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> The QCD sum rules for spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions are formulated and their physical implications are studied. The basic object of the study is the correlation function of the nucleon interpolating field, where the matrix element is taken with respect to the one-nucleon state. The dispersion integral of the correlation function around the nucleon threshold is investigated in detail. It turns out that the integral can be identified as a measure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction strength, which is proportional to the scattering length in the small scattering length limit and to one half of the effective range in the large scattering length limit. New operators must be taken into account in the OPE of the correlation function. There behavior operators do not vanish when the matrix element is taken with respect to the spin-nonaveraged one-nucleon state. The Wilson coefficients of such operators are calculated. The sum rules obtained in this manner relate the spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction strengths with the spin-dependent nucleon matrix elements of the quark-gluon composite operators. The sum rules imply that the interaction is stronger in the spin-triplet channel than in the spin-singlet channel, but that the spin-dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interactions is rather small. In the spin-singlet channel the calculated strength is in qualitative agreement with the empirical strength, which is estimated by the empirical low energy scattering observables.

## Key claim

The dispersion integral of the nucleon correlation function around the nucleon threshold can be identified as a measure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction strength, which is proportional to the scattering length in the small scattering length limit and to one half of the effective range in the large scattering length limit; this identification holds even though the naive dispersion relation is ill-defined due to divergent terms.

## Method

The authors formulate QCD sum rules for the spin-dependent correlation function of the nucleon interpolating field, where the matrix element is taken with respect to the spin-nonaveraged one-nucleon state. They derive a well-defined Borel-transformed dispersion relation by symmetrizing the correlation function multiplied by a factor that removes the divergent behavior at threshold, and they parametrize the imaginary part near threshold using the off-shell optical theorem and the effective-range expansion of the scattering amplitude. The operator product expansion (OPE) is performed including operators up to dimension six, and new operators such as \(\bar q\gamma_\mu\gamma_5 q\) and \(\bar q\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu} q\) are taken into account because they do not vanish when the matrix element is taken with respect to the spin-nonaveraged one-nucleon state.

## Key numbers

* The spin-singlet channel interaction strength calculated from the sum rules is in qualitative agreement with the empirical strength estimated from low-energy scattering observables.
* In the small scattering length limit, the dispersion integral \(I\) is dominated by the threshold term: \(I = -\frac{1}{\MBS}\exp\left(-\frac{\M^2}{\MBS}\right)\frac{4\pi}{\M}a + O(a^2)\), where \(a\) is the scattering length and \(\M\) is the nucleon mass.
* In the large scattering length limit (bound state just formed), the integral becomes \(I = -\frac{1}{\MBS}\exp\left(-\frac{\M^2}{\MBS}\right)\frac{2\pi r}{\M} + O(1/a)\), where \(r\) is the effective range.
* The continuum contribution in the small scattering length limit is \(I_c \rightarrow \frac{1}{\MBS}\exp\left(-\frac{\M^2}{\MBS}\right)\frac{4\pi}{\sqrt{1+\M^2 b}} a^2\) as \(a \rightarrow 0\).
* The continuum contribution in the large scattering length limit is \(I_c \rightarrow \frac{1}{\MBS}\exp\left(-\frac{\M^2}{\MBS}\right)\frac{4\pi}{\M}|a|\left(1-\frac{r}{2a}\right) + O(1/a)\) as \(a \rightarrow \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Kondo and Morimatsu, 1995), which considered only spin-averaged correlation functions and obtained scattering lengths smaller than experimental values. The present paper addresses the unresolved issues of continuum dominance near threshold and the need for spin-dependent sum rules by constructing a formalism that handles the divergent dispersion integral and includes spin-dependent operators. This enables future calculations of spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction strengths directly from QCD, relating them to nucleon matrix elements of quark-gluon composite operators.
