# The Analysis of Space-Time Structure in QCD Vacuum II: Dynamics of Polarization and Absolute X-Distribution

> We propose a framework for quantitative evaluation of dynamical tendency for polarization in arbitrary random variable that can be decomposed into a pair of orthogonal subspaces. The method uses measures based on comparisons of given dynamics to its counterpart with statistically independent components. The formalism of previously considered X-distributions is used to express the aforementioned comparisons, in effect putting the former approach on solid footing. Our analysis leads to definition of a suitable correlation coefficient with clear statistical meaning. We apply the method to the dynamics induced by pure-glue lattice QCD in local left-right components of overlap Dirac eigenmodes. It is found that, in finite physical volume, there exists a non-zero physical scale in the spectrum of eigenvalues such that eigenmodes at smaller (fixed) eigenvalues exhibit convex X-distribution (positive correlation), while at larger eigenvalues the distribution is concave (negative correlation). This chiral polarization scale thus separates a regime where dynamics enhances chirality relative to statistical independence from a regime where it suppresses it, and gives an objective definition to the notion of "low" and "high" Dirac eigenmode. We propose to investigate whether the polarization scale remains non-zero in the infinite volume limit, in which case it would represent a new kind of low energy scale in QCD.

## Key claim

In finite physical volume, there exists a non-zero physical scale in the eigenvalue spectrum such that eigenmodes at smaller eigenvalues exhibit convex X-distribution (positive correlation) while at larger eigenvalues the distribution is concave (negative correlation); the authors propose to investigate whether this chiral polarization scale remains non-zero in the infinite volume limit, in which case it would represent a new kind of low energy scale in QCD.

## Method

The authors construct an "absolute X-distribution" by comparing the actual joint probability distribution of left and right components of overlap Dirac eigenmodes to a reference distribution where left and right components are statistically independent. This comparison is performed by computing cumulative probability functions along rays in the (|psi_L|, |psi_R|) plane, eliminating the ray angle to obtain a curve in the cumulative distribution plane, and then converting the slope of this curve into a distribution in a rescaled polarization variable. The method is applied to low-lying eigenmodes of the overlap Dirac operator computed on pure-glue lattice QCD configurations with Iwasaki gauge action at five ensembles with varying lattice spacings and volumes.

## Key numbers

- Ensembles E1-E4 have fixed physical volume (1.32 fm)^4 with lattice spacings ranging from 0.165 fm (E1, 8^4 lattice) to 0.055 fm (E4, 24^4 lattice)
- Ensemble E5 has larger physical volume (1.76 fm)^4 at lattice spacing 0.110 fm (16^4 lattice)
- The average magnitude of the lowest near-zero eigenvalue (Lambda_LOW_AVE) ranges from 226 MeV (E1) down to 136 MeV (E4) for the fixed-volume ensembles
- The average magnitude of the highest computed eigenvalue (Lambda_HIGH_AVE) ranges from 1980 MeV (E1) down to 1366 MeV (E4)
- About 50 pairs of lowest near-zero modes with complex-conjugated eigenvalues were computed per configuration, with zero modes excluded

## Context

This work extends the X-distribution framework introduced by Horvath (2001) for characterizing local chirality of QCD Dirac eigenmodes, putting that approach on a more rigorous footing by removing dependence on the arbitrary choice of polarization function. The authors aim to build model-independent information on low-lying Dirac eigenmode properties as part of a "bottom-up" approach to QCD vacuum structure formulated by Horvath (2006). The results connect to the Banks-Casher relation (1980) and the expected connection between low-lying Dirac eigenmodes and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, with the chiral polarization scale potentially providing a new objective definition of "low" versus "high" Dirac eigenmodes.
