# The Dirac-Brueckner Approach

> In this review, we give a thorough introduction into the Dirac-Brueckner approach including the mathematical details of the formalism involved. Furthermore, we present results for nuclear matter, NN scattering in the nuclear medium, and finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner approach introduces a relativistic many-body effect that is strongly density-dependent and repulsive, which allows the empirical nuclear matter saturation point to be explained, in contrast to non-relativistic Brueckner theory that fails to reproduce saturation even when three- and four-hole line contributions are included.

## Method

The authors present a thorough mathematical derivation of the Dirac-Brueckner formalism, starting from the Bethe-Salpeter equation and its three-dimensional reductions (Blankenbecler-Sugar and Thompson choices). They describe how the Dirac equation is used for single-particle motion in nuclear matter, with full self-consistency of relativistic single-particle energies and wave functions, and how the relativistic G-matrix is evaluated using one-boson-exchange potentials or more realistic models involving 2pi-exchange with Delta-isobars.

## Key numbers

- Empirical nuclear matter saturation occurs at energy per particle E/A = -16 +/- 1 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.35 +/- 0.05 fm^{-1}
- Three- and four-hole line diagrams contribute about 5-7 MeV to the binding energy per nucleon at saturation
- For the Bonn A potential: saturation energy E/A = -17.1 MeV at k_F = 1.74 fm^{-1} with deuteron D-state probability P_D = 4.4% and wound integral kappa = 5.4%
- For the Bonn B potential: saturation energy E/A = -14.0 MeV at k_F = 1.61 fm^{-1} with P_D = 5.0% and kappa = 6.6%
- For the Bonn C potential: saturation energy E/A = -12.1 MeV at k_F = 1.54 fm^{-1} with P_D = 5.6% and kappa = 8.1%
- The change in numerical results from replacing E_k by E_{1/2 P + k} in the Thompson equation denominator is less than 0.1 MeV for the energy per nucleon at nuclear matter density

## Context

This review extends the non-relativistic Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone approach, which produces the "Coester band" of saturation points that miss the empirical region even when three- and four-hole line contributions are included (Day, 1983; Day, 1985). The Dirac-Brueckner approach builds on the Dirac-Hartree model developed by Miller and Green (1970s) and further by Brockmann, Horowitz and Serot, and on the relativistic phenomenology of Clark and coworkers for proton-nucleus scattering. The work enables consistent relativistic calculations of nuclear matter, NN scattering in the nuclear medium, and finite nuclei using free NN interactions without parameters adjusted in the many-body problem.
