# Model for the Quasifree Polarization-Transfer Measurements in the (p,n) reaction at 495 MeV

> The recent (p,n) polarization transfer experiments at LAMPF are explained in terms of a dropping rho-meson mass in the medium.

## Key claim

The ratio of spin-longitudinal to spin-transverse response functions \(R_L/R_T\) measured in \((\vec p,\vec n)\) reactions at 495 MeV can be explained as essentially unity at small energy loss if the nucleon-nucleon tensor interaction is zero at the relevant momentum transfer, which is partially achieved by introducing a density-dependent \(\rho\)-meson mass \(m_\rho^*\) and a nucleon effective mass \(m_N^*\), along with a softened \(\pi NN\) form factor.

## Method

The authors used a spin-isospin interaction based on the OBEPH potential of Hippchen et al., which includes \(\rho\)-meson exchange and the \(\pi\pi\) continuum, with short-range correlations included via a correlation function \(f(r)=1-j_0(r/r_c)\) with \(r_c=0.19\) fm. They treated \(m_\rho^*/m_\rho\) as a parameter to force the tensor interaction \(V_{ten}\) to zero at the momentum transfer \(q=1.72\) fm\(^{-1}\), and performed nuclear matter and semi-classical finite-nucleus calculations using a local density approximation (LDA) with a linear density dependence for \(m_\rho^*/m_\rho\). The finite-nucleus response functions were obtained by integrating local nuclear matter response functions weighted by a projectile distortion function in the Eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

* The three-momentum transfer of the experiment is \(q = 1.72\) fm\(^{-1}\) (equivalent to \(2.4 m_\pi\)).
* The schematic estimate requires \((m_\rho/m_\rho^*)^4 \cong 2\), yielding \(m_\rho^*/m_\rho = 0.84\).
* QCD sum rule calculations give \(m_\rho^*(\rho_0)/m_\rho \cong 0.8\).
* The more quantitative OBEPH calculation yields \(m_\rho^*(\rho_0)/m_\rho = 0.8\) to force the tensor interaction zero at \(q=1.72\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* Using a Glauber estimate, the nucleus is probed at \(\sim 0.6 \rho_0\), giving an average \(\langle m_\rho^*/m_\rho \rangle = 0.88\).
* From analysis of \((e,e')\) and \((p,p')\) scattering on stretched states in \(^{208}\)Pb, effective masses \(\langle m_\rho^*/m_\rho \rangle = 0.79-0.86\) are found.
* From \((\vec p,\vec p')\) polarization transfer at \(E_p=200\) MeV in \(^{16}\)O, Stephenson and Tostvin find \(\langle m_\rho^*/m_\rho \rangle = 0.94 \pm 0.2\).
* The \(\pi NN\) form factor cutoff \(\Lambda_\pi\) is lowered from 2 GeV to about 1 GeV in the medium.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Brown and Rho (1991) that meson masses and the nucleon effective mass decrease with increasing density in the medium, and connects the analysis of quasifree polarization-transfer measurements with Drell-Yan experiments by Alde et al. (1990) and with charge and current response measurements in quasielastic electron scattering by Sosnowski et al. (1991). The model contradicts earlier theoretical predictions of a "softness" in the longitudinal channel due to the pion-exchange tail of the \(NN\) interaction, and enables predictions for measurements at different momentum transfers (e.g., \(q=1.1\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(q>2\) fm\(^{-1}\)) to test the model.
