# Pion-Nucleon Scattering at Low Energies

> We study pion-nucleon scattering at tree level with a chiral lagrangian of pions, nucleons, and $Δ$-isobars using a K-matrix unitarization procedure. Evaluating the scattering amplitude to order $Q^2$, where $Q$ is a generic small momentum scale, we obtain a good fit to the experimental phase shifts for pion center-of-mass kinetic energies up to $50 $MeV. The fit can be extended to 150 MeV when we include the order-$Q^3$ contributions. Our results are independent of the off-shell $Δ$ parameter.

## Key claim

Using a chiral Lagrangian with only pions, nucleons, and Δ-isobars at tree level with K-matrix unitarization, the authors obtain a good fit to experimental πN phase shifts up to 50 MeV at order Q² and up to 150 MeV when order-Q³ contributions are included, with the results independent of the off-shell Δ parameter Z.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral Lagrangian up to order Q⁴ (where Q is a generic small momentum scale) containing pions, nucleons, and Δ-isobars, with the most general set of contact interactions consistent with chiral symmetry. They computed the πN scattering amplitude at tree level from the Feynman diagrams (contact terms, nucleon exchange, and Δ exchange), then used a K-matrix unitarization procedure to obtain unitary phase shifts from the real tree-level amplitudes. The model parameters were determined by χ² fits to the S- and P-wave phase shift data of Arndt for pion center-of-mass kinetic energies between 10 and 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Good fit to experimental phase shifts obtained for pion c.m. kinetic energies up to 50 MeV at order Q², extended to 150 MeV at order Q³
- Standard values used: M = 939 MeV, M_Δ = 1232 MeV, m_π = 139 MeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV, g_A = 1.26, h_A = 1.46, Γ_Δ = 120 MeV
- At O(Q²): χ² = 1 for relative weight of 15%; at O(Q³): χ² = 1 for relative weight of 8%
- S-wave scattering lengths (in units of m_π⁻¹) at O(Q³): a₁ = 0.144, a₃ = −0.087; P-wave scattering volumes (in units of m_π⁻³) at O(Q³): a₁₁ = −0.071, a₁₃ = −0.031, a₃₁ = −0.040, a₃₃ = 0.209
- Isoscalar and isovector S-wave scattering lengths at O(Q³): b₀ = −0.010 m_π⁻¹, b₁ = −0.077 m_π⁻¹
- Effective πNN coupling constant: g_πNN = 12.8 from Goldberger-Treiman relation, decreasing to 12.6 with λ₁ term included

## Context

This work extends the chiral Lagrangian approach of Peccei (1969) to study phase shift data rather than just scattering lengths, using Weinberg's power counting arguments (1990) to systematically enumerate the Lagrangian. The authors show that a minimal model with only N, π, and Δ fields can fit the data as well as models with additional fields such as the ρ meson (Boffinger and Woolcock, 1991; Olsson, 1975) or the isoscalar-scalar φ (Goudsmit et al., 1994), while using a comparable number of parameters. The calculation confirms that the Z parameter of the πNΔ vertex is irrelevant when a sufficiently general Lagrangian is employed, and the work enables future calculations including loop contributions in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to obtain a naturally unitary scattering amplitude.
