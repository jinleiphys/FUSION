# Extraction of multiple parton interactions and color reconnection from forward-backward multiplicity correlations

> Forward-backward multiplicity correlations have been studied in electron-positron, proton-proton and more recently in lead-lead collisions. For the proton-proton case, comparison of experimental results to different models reveals an incomplete understanding of the physical phenomenon associated with these correlations. In this work, we present a study of forward-backward multiplicity correlations in proton-proton collisions using the PYTHIA event generator, at LHC energies. A detailed analysis is presented with and without weak decays, splitting data samples into soft and hard QCD processes, and comparing the computed correlations for short and long range pseudorapidity regions. Each of these regions is analyzed accounting for the effects of color reconnection and independent multiple parton interactions. We show that a combination of these effects is required to explain the latest measurements on proton-proton data. Furthermore, is shown that from measurements of multiplicity correlations is possible to extract the average number of multiple parton interactions in the event producing these correlations, and albeit model depending, to predict the strength of these correlations, not yet measured, for higher energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that forward-backward multiplicity correlations in proton-proton collisions can be explained only by a combination of color reconnection and multiple parton interactions, and that the average number of multiple parton interactions can be extracted from these correlations, albeit in a model-dependent way.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA 8.2 event generator with the Monash 2013 tune to simulate proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, generating 25 billion events for each set of studies. They computed the forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength b_Corr as a function of pseudorapidity bin width, splitting events into soft and hard QCD processes, with and without weak decays, and for central and forward pseudorapidity regions. They then varied the strength of color reconnection and the range of the number of multiple parton interactions to compare with ALICE experimental data at 0.9 and 7 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The average number of multiple parton interactions ranges from 2 to 4 to describe ALICE data at 0.9 TeV, and from 6 to 10 to describe data at 7 TeV.
- Weak decays produce an enhancement of approximately 40% on b_Corr for pseudorapidity bin width delta_eta = 0.2 and approximately 5% for delta_eta = 0.9 in the central pseudorapidity region.
- Color reconnection produces a maximum reduction of approximately 14% on the strength of b_Corr for all delta_eta values.
- For hard QCD processes, the transverse momentum transfer between interacting partons is larger than 30 GeV/c.
- The average multiplicities for event classes with multiple parton interaction ranges 0 < MPI < 5, 0 < MPI < 10, and 5 < MPI < 10 at 7 TeV are 5.05, 6.85, and 15.24, respectively.

## Context

This work extends studies of forward-backward multiplicity correlations from earlier experiments at the ISR (52.6 GeV), SPS (540 GeV), and Tevatron (1 TeV), as well as recent results from ATLAS and ALICE at the LHC at 0.9, 2.7, and 7 TeV. The analysis uses the PYTHIA event generator to disentangle the contributions of multiple parton interactions and color reconnection, which are not separately accessible in experimental data, and enables predictions for correlations at higher energies not yet measured.
