# Statistical hadronization of charm quarks in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We discuss the production of charmonium and open charm in nuclear collisions at SPS/FAIR energies within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. The increasing importance at lower energies of Lambda_c production is discussed and provides a challenge for future experiments. We also show that possible modifications of charmed hadrons in the hot hadronic medium do not lead to measurable changes in the cross sections for D-meson production. A possible influence of medium effects can be seen, however, in yields of charmonium. These effects are visible at all energies and results will be presented for the energy range between charm threshold and RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model (SHM) can describe charmonium and open charm production across a wide energy range (from near threshold at √sNN ≈ 6 GeV up to RHIC at 200 GeV), but the assumption of full charm thermalization may be questionable at lower energies where collision, plasma formation, and hadron formation times are all of the same order.

## Method

The authors use the statistical hadronization model (SHM) which assumes charm quarks are produced in primary hard collisions and their total number stays constant until hadronization, with thermal equilibration in the quark-gluon plasma near the critical temperature. A charm balance equation incorporating canonical suppression factors is solved to determine a fugacity factor gc, from which yields of charmonia (proportional to gc²) and open charm hadrons (proportional to gc) are computed. The input parameters include charm production cross sections in pp collisions extrapolated from NLO pQCD calculations, and chemical freeze-out characteristics (temperature T, baryochemical potential μb, and volume VΔy=1) taken from thermal model fits to non-charmed hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Charm production cross section parameterization: σc̄c = k (1 − √sthr/√s)^a (√sthr/√s)^b with k = 1.85 μb, √sthr = 4.5 GeV (for mc = 1.3 GeV), a = 4.3, b = −1.44
- Rapidity density of initially produced charm quark pairs rises from 1.1 × 10⁻³ to 1.7 over the energy range √sNN = 7–200 GeV (for Npart = 350)
- Temperature T rises from 151 to 161 MeV from √sNN = 7 to 12 GeV and stays constant for higher energies; μb decreases from 434 to 22 MeV
- Volume at midrapidity VΔy=1 rises from 760 to 2400 fm³ over the same energy range
- Canonical suppression factor I₁/I₀ varies from 1/30 to 1/1.2; charm fugacity gc increases from 0.96 to 8.9
- At LHC energy, the J/ψ yield per c̄c pair approaches 1%
- For in-medium width effects: D-meson yields increase by 11% for a width of 100 MeV and by 2.7% for a width of 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously applied at top SPS energy (√sNN ≈ 17 GeV) and RHIC (200 GeV) by Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, Redlich, and Stachel (2006-2007) down to near-threshold energies accessible at the future FAIR facility's CBM experiment. The results contradict approaches that assume significant medium modifications to open charm cross sections (Tolos et al., 2005; Zschiesche et al., 2004), showing instead that charm conservation forces a redistribution of charm quarks with negligible net effect on total open charm yields. The predictions provide guidance for future experiments at FAIR and enable comparison with transport models (Zhang et al., 2004; Bratkovskaya et al., 2004) and coalescence models (Thews et al., 2001; Greco et al., 2004) for charmonium production.
