# Microcanonical mean-field thermodynamics of self-gravitating and rotating systems

> We derive the global phase diagram of a self-gravitating $N$-body system enclosed in a finite three-dimensional spherical volume $V$ as a function of total energy and angular momentum, employing a microcanonical mean-field approach. At low angular momenta (i.e. for slowly rotating systems) the known collapse from a gas cloud to a single dense cluster is recovered. At high angular momenta, instead, rotational symmetry can be spontaneously broken and rotationally asymmetric structures (double clusters) appear.

## Key claim

The paper derives the global phase diagram of a self-gravitating N-body system as a function of both total energy and angular momentum, showing that at high angular momenta the rotational symmetry of the Hamiltonian can be spontaneously broken, producing double-cluster structures previously seen only in dynamical simulations.

## Method

The authors employ a microcanonical mean-field approach, starting from the N-body Hamiltonian with Newtonian gravity and a spherical container to preserve rotational symmetry. They use a Laplace transform to integrate over momenta, then apply a mean-field approximation following Lynden-Bell (1966) and Laliena (1998), subdividing the volume into cells with a maximum occupancy to introduce a short-range cutoff. The resulting integral-algebraic equations for the density profile are solved numerically by expanding in real spherical harmonics up to l=16, iterating until convergence for fixed energy E and angular momentum L.

## Key numbers

- Energy and angular momentum are measured in units of GN²/R and (RGN³)^(1/2) respectively, with particle masses set to one.
- The cell occupancy parameter Θ was fixed at 0.02 for all calculations.
- Calculations were performed with even harmonics up to and including l=16.
- The phase diagram was computed for energy values ranging from approximately E=-0.9 to E=-0.06 and angular momentum values up to L=0.5.
- At L=0.5 and specific energies (E=-0.9, -0.72, -0.42, -0.06), the entropy was plotted as a function of the order parameter I₁₁-I₂₂, showing double peaks at non-zero values for double-cluster configurations.

## Context

This work extends static theories of self-gravitating systems (Thirring 1970, Padmanabhan 1990) that previously considered only total energy as a control parameter, often removing rotational symmetry artificially by using non-spherical boxes. It bridges the gap with dynamical approaches (Lynden-Bell 1966, Chandrasekhar 1969) that suggested double clusters, rings, and disks could appear upon increasing rotational energy. The results enable future studies incorporating correlations beyond the mean-field approximation and including additional forces (nuclear and sub-nuclear) relevant to real star formation.
