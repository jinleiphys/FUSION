# Transverse single spin asymmetry $A_{UT}^{\sin(ϕ_h-ϕ_S)}$ for single hadron production in SIDIS

> In this paper we study the single spin asymmetry $A_{UT}^{\sin(ϕ_h-ϕ_S)}$ of a single hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) within the framework of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization up to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order of QCD. The asymmetry is contributed by the convolution of the Sivers function and the unpolarized fragmentation function. Specifically, the Sivers function in the coordinate space and perturbative region can be represented as the convolution of the $C$-coefficients and the corresponding collinear correlation functions, among which the Qiu-Sterman function is the most relevant one. We perform a detailed phenomenological analysis of the Sivers asymmetry at the kinematics of the HERMES and the COMPASS measurements. It is found that the obtained $x_B$-, $z_h$- and $P_{h\perp}$-dependent asymmetries are basically consistent with the HERMES and the COMPASS measurements.

## Key claim

The authors find that their TMD-evolved calculations of the Sivers asymmetry \(A_{UT}^{\sin(\phi_h-\phi_S)}\) for single hadron production in SIDIS are "basically consistent" with HERMES and COMPASS measurements, but they note that the contribution of the \(Y\) terms (which dominate at \(P_{h\perp} \ge Q\)) "might not be negligible" in the kinematical regions of HERMES and part of COMPASS, where \(Q^2\) may not be large.

## Method

The asymmetry is computed within transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, using the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) evolution formalism in coordinate (\(b\)) space. The Sivers function in the perturbative region is expressed as a convolution of \(C\)-coefficients with the Qiu-Sterman function \(T_{q,F}(x,x)\), which is evolved from an initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 2.4\ \text{GeV}^2\) using the QCDNUM evolution package with a modified kernel. The unpolarized cross section uses CT10 NLO parton distribution functions and NLO fragmentation functions for pions and kaons, while the nonperturbative Sudakov form factors are taken from parametrizations in the literature.

## Key numbers

- The initial scale for Qiu-Sterman function evolution is \(Q_0^2 = 2.4\ \text{GeV}^2\), with \(\alpha_s(Q_0) = 0.327\).
- Nonperturbative Sudakov parameters for the Sivers function: \(g_1^{\text{Siv}} = 0.0705\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(g_2 = 0.16\ \text{GeV}^2\), and for the fragmentation function: \(g_1^{\text{ff}} = 0.0475 / z_h^2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For \(K^+\) production at HERMES, the largest asymmetry reaches 0.1; for \(K^-\) production, the largest asymmetry reaches 0.05.
- HERMES kinematic cuts include \(0.023 < x_B < 0.4\), \(0.1 < y < 0.95\), \(0.2 < z_h < 0.7\), \(P_{h\perp} > 0.1\ \text{GeV}\), \(Q^2 > 1\ \text{GeV}^2\), and \(W^2 > 10\ \text{GeV}^2\), with \(P_{h\perp} \le 0.5\ \text{GeV}\) applied for the TMD region.

## Context

This work extends the TMD-evolution analysis of the Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS by Echevarria et al. (2014), who used leading-order \(C\)-coefficients and a fixed-scale Qiu-Sterman function, by including NLL perturbative Sudakov factors and evolving the Qiu-Sterman function with its proper evolution kernel. The improved treatment yields better agreement with HERMES data for kaon production, where the earlier work underestimated the asymmetry. The results enable more reliable comparisons with ongoing and future SIDIS experiments and can be extended to Drell-Yan processes, where the Sivers function is expected to have the opposite sign.
