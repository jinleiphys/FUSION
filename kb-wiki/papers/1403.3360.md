# The Nucleon as a Holographic Cheshire Cat

> The Cheshire cat principle emerges naturally from the holographic approach of the nucleon in terms of a bulk instanton. The cat hides in the holographic direction. I briefly review the one-nucleon problem in the holographic limit.

## Key claim

The Cheshire cat principle, which states that the bag boundary in quark bag models is physically immaterial at low energy, emerges naturally from the holographic approach to the nucleon when the nucleon is described as a bulk instanton; the "cat" (the bag boundary) hides in the holographic (fifth) direction, with the separation between core and cloud becoming arbitrary only in the limit of an infinite tower of vector mesons.

## Method

The paper reviews the holographic dual of QCD based on the Sakai-Sugimoto D4/D8-anti-D8 brane setup, where baryons are described as SU(2) instantons in a five-dimensional Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory on a curved background. The classical baryon is obtained as the holonomy of the instanton gauge field along the holographic Z-direction, yielding a Skyrmion profile. Semiclassical quantization is performed using collective coordinates (position, size, and orientation), with a non-rigid gauge constraint that separates zero modes from vector meson fluctuations, defining a cutoff scale Z_C that acts as the Cheshire cat smile.

## Key numbers

* The instanton size is approximately rho ~ 1/sqrt(lambda) in units of M_KK, where lambda = g^2 N_c is the 't Hooft coupling, making it much smaller than 1 for large lambda.
* The Cheshire cat cutoff scale is Z_C ~ rho ~ 1/sqrt(lambda), which approaches the origin for large 't Hooft coupling.
* The exact sum rule sum_{n=1}^{infinity} a_{v^n} psi_{2n-1} = 1 ensures that the core contribution to the baryonic current vanishes exactly, making vector meson dominance exact in holography.

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire cat principle (CAT) and the Stony Brook bag model (BAGS) into the holographic QCD framework of Sakai and Sugimoto (SAKAI), showing that the bag boundary ambiguity is realized as a cutoff in the holographic direction. The results support the bottom-up effective approach described by Rho (RHO) using heavy nucleon expansion, and were independently derived by Hashimoto (HASHI) using strong coupling source quantization. The paper enables a holographic understanding of the 50/50 scenario for baryon form factors long advocated by Gerry Brown, where a minimal number of vector mesons in a truncated cloud fixes the Cheshire cat smile position.
