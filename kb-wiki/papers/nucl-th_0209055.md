# Particle Correlations at RHIC - Scrutiny of a Puzzle

> We present calculations of two-pion and two-kaon correlation functions in relativistic heavy ion collisions from a relativistic transport model that includes explicitly a first-order phase transition from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma to a hadron gas. We compare the obtained correlation radii with recent data from RHIC. The predicted R_side radii agree with data while the R_out and R_long radii are overestimated. We also address the impact of in-medium modifications, for example, a broadening of the rho-meson, on the correlation radii. In particular, the longitudinal correlation radius R_long is reduced, improving the comparison to data.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas, modeled via a hybrid transport approach, produces pion correlation radii that agree with RHIC data for R_side but overestimate R_out and R_long; the disagreement with data (the "HBT-puzzle") is partially mitigated by including in-medium modifications such as a broadened rho-meson spectral function, which specifically reduces R_long.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic hybrid transport model that couples hydrodynamics (with a bag-model equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition) for the dense initial stage to a microscopic hadronic transport model for the later dilute stage, matched at the hadronization hypersurface. Two-pion and two-kaon correlation functions were computed explicitly from the transport source function using a correlation afterburner, then fitted to a Gaussian form to extract the HBT radii R_out, R_side, R_long, and the intercept parameter lambda. The calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV and compared to STAR and PHENIX data.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV used in the baseline calculation (also T_c = 200 MeV explored for kaons).
- In-medium rho-meson width increased from vacuum value Gamma_rho = 150 MeV to Gamma_rho^{i.m.} = 350 MeV to study opacity effects.
- Kaon direct emission fraction from the phase boundary reaches up to ~30% at high transverse momentum (K_T ~ 1 GeV/c).
- Freeze-out temperature range T_f.o. ~ 100-120 MeV for the hadronic phase.

## Context
This work extends earlier hybrid model studies (Soff et al., 2001; Bass, 1998) that predicted large emission durations from a first-order phase transition, and directly addresses the "HBT-puzzle" identified by STAR (2001) and PHENIX (2001) data, where the measured R_out/R_side ratio decreases with transverse momentum (below unity) while calculations yield ratios above unity. The paper enables future kaon interferometry measurements as a cleaner probe of the phase boundary dynamics and suggests that in-medium effects (rho-meson broadening) may help resolve the discrepancy, pointing toward more realistic treatments of the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collision models.
