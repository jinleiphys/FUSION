# Multipole Analysis of Recent Pion Electroproduction Data

> We have performed a series of global and single-Q2 fits to the growing database for pion electroproduction. We compare to some other recent fits which have used different formalisms, more selective datasets, and truncated multipole expansions. We present results for the E2/M1 and S2/M1 ratios, as a function of Q2.

## Key claim
The authors present global and single-Q² fits to pion electroproduction data, finding that the E2/M1 ratio always crosses zero as a function of Q², in disagreement with the 4 (GeV/c)² Hall C value from Frolov (2003), though the crossing point shifts significantly when preliminary data are included.

## Method
The authors performed energy- and Q²-dependent fits over the kinematic range W up to 1.7 GeV and Q² up to 5 (GeV/c)², using a parametrization that includes Born terms, phenomenological structure functions, and a Q²-dependent form factor with a universal cutoff factor Λ and searched parameters α and β for each multipole. They also performed single-Q² fits to data clustered at specific Q² values, using the symmetry σ(φ) = σ(360° − φ) to reduce the number of data points in the fitting routine.

## Key numbers
- Q² values for single-Q² fits: 0.40, 0.50, 0.65, 0.75, 0.90, 1.15, 1.45, and 1.80 (GeV/c)², associated with JLab CLAS π⁰p binning.
- For the set of π⁰p data from the CLAS Collaboration, the first term in the χ² expression contributed a χ² of 15040 for 12528 φ-pairs.
- The global fit covers Q² up to 5 (GeV/c)² and W up to 1.7 GeV.
- The form factor includes a factor 1/(1 + Q²/0.71)², with Q² in (GeV/c)².

## Context
This work extends the SAID group's previous photoproduction analysis (Arndt et al., 2002) to the electroproduction regime, comparing results to the MAID model (Drechsel et al., 1999) and the Sato-Lee model (Sato and Lee, 2001). The analysis uses world data plus JLab CLAS data, and finds systematic disagreement with the single-Q² value at 4 (GeV/c)² from Frolov (2003), motivating further study of that kinematic point.
