# Measurements of the gamma*p->Delta Reaction At Low Q^2: Probing the Mesonic Contribution

> The determination of non-spherical angular momentum amplitudes in nucleons at long ranges (low Q^{2}), was accomplished through the $p(\vec{e},e'p)π^0$ reaction in the Delta region at $Q^2=0.060$, 0.127, and 0.200 (GeV/c)^2 at the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) with an accuracy of 3%. The results for the dominant transition magnetic dipole amplitude and the quadrupole to dipole ratios have been obtained with an estimated model uncertainty which is approximately the same as the experimental uncertainty. Lattice and effective field theory predictions agree with our data within the relatively large estimated theoretical uncertainties. Phenomenological models are in good agreement with experiment when the resonant amplitudes are adjusted to the data. To check reaction model calculations additional data were taken for center of mass energies below resonance and for the $σ_{TL'}$ structure function. These results confirm the dominance, and general Q^2 variation, of the pionic contribution at large distances.

## Key claim

The paper reports measurements of the $\gamma^*p \rightarrow \Delta$ reaction at low $Q^2$ that confirm the dominance and general $Q^2$ variation of the pionic contribution at large distances, but the agreement with lattice and effective field theory predictions holds only within the relatively large estimated theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The $p(\vec{e},e'p)\pi^0$ reaction was measured in the Delta resonance region using the A1 spectrometers at the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) with a longitudinally polarized electron beam. Cross sections were extracted at three $Q^2$ values using sequential kinematic settings that kept $W$ and $Q^2$ constant while varying the proton azimuthal angle $\phi_{pq}^*$, allowing algebraic extraction of the two-fold differential cross sections $\sigma_0$, $\sigma_{TT}$, and $\sigma_{LT}$. The helicity-dependent structure function $\sigma_{LT'}$ was measured by reversing the electron beam helicity at non-zero out-of-plane angles.

## Key numbers

* Cross sections measured at $Q^2 = 0.060$, $0.127$, and $0.200$ (GeV/c)$^2$ with an overall systematic uncertainty of 3 to 4%
* The $Q^2 = 0.060$ (GeV/c)$^2$ point is the lowest $Q^2$ value probed to date in modern electroproduction experiments
* Beam polarization was approximately 75%, measured periodically with a Moller polarimeter
* Beam energy had an absolute uncertainty of $\pm 160$ keV and a spread of 30 keV (FWHM)
* A luminosity correction factor of $(3 \pm 1)\%$ was applied for beam currents above 12.5 $\mu$A
* Phase space correction factors were on the order of 2 to 3% with a 1% systematic uncertainty

## Context

This work fills an important gap in the $Q^2$ evolution of the $\gamma^*N \rightarrow \Delta$ transition between the photon point ($Q^2=0$) from Beck and Blanpied and previously published electroproduction experiments at Jefferson Lab by Joo, Frolov, and Ungaro for $Q^2$ from 0.4 to 6.0 (GeV/c)$^2$. The $Q^2=0.200$ (GeV/c)$^2$ point provides overlap with newly obtained Jefferson Lab data from CLAS (2007), while the $Q^2=0.127$ (GeV/c)$^2$ point allows comparison with previous results from Bates by Warren, Mertz, Kunz, and Sparveris and from Mainz by Pospischil, Bartsch, and Elsner. The results enable tests of pion cloud model calculations by Sato and Lee and by the DMT group, as well as effective field theory (chiral) calculations by Gail and Hemmert and by Pascalutsa.
