# Neutrino-nucleus cross section within the extended factorization scheme

> The factorization scheme, based on the impulse approximation and the spectral function formalism, has been recently generalized to allow the description of electromagnetic nuclear interactions driven by two-nucleon currents. We have extended this framework to the case of weak charged and neutral currents and carried out calculations of the double-differential neutrino-carbon and neutrino-oxygen cross sections using two different models of the target spectral functions. The results, showing a moderate dependence on the input spectral function, confirm that our approach provides a consistent treatment of all reaction mechanisms contributing to the signals detected by accelerator-based neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper extends the factorization scheme (based on the impulse approximation and spectral function formalism) to include weak charged-current and neutral-current two-body meson-exchange currents, showing that the approach provides a consistent treatment of reaction mechanisms relevant for accelerator-based neutrino experiments, with only a moderate dependence on the input spectral function model.

## Method

The authors generalized the factorization scheme to include relativistic meson-exchange two-body currents for weak interactions, using two different models of the target spectral functions: one from correlated-basis function (CBF) theory and one from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory. They developed a dedicated code using the Metropolis Monte Carlo algorithm to compute the two-body current spin-isospin matrix elements, and validated their implementation by benchmarking against relativistic Fermi gas model results. Calculations were performed for double-differential cross sections of neutrino-carbon and neutrino-oxygen scattering at 1 GeV incoming energy for two scattering angles, and for total charged-current cross sections as a function of energy.

## Key numbers

- Double-differential cross sections were computed for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets at incoming (anti)neutrino energy of 1 GeV and scattering angles $\theta_\mu=30^\circ$ and $\theta_\mu=70^\circ$
- The Fermi momentum used for the normalization volume was $k_F=225$ MeV
- The SCGF calculations employed a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with frequency $\hbar\Omega=20$ MeV and dimension $N_{\rm max}=13$
- The weak coupling constant used was $G_F = 1.1803 \times 10^{-5}\,\rm GeV^{-2}$, with $\cos\theta_c=0.97425$ for charged-current processes
- The impulse approximation is considered applicable for momentum transfer $|{\bf q}| \gtrsim 500$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the factorization scheme previously developed for electromagnetic interactions with two-body currents (Benhar 2015, Rocco 2015) to weak charged-current and neutral-current interactions. It builds on the impulse approximation and spectral function formalism (Benhar 2006) and uses two-body currents derived from the weak pion-production model (Hernandez 2007). The approach enables consistent treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions across the energy regime relevant for current-generation experiments (MiniBooNE, T2K, NOvA, MINERvA) and next-generation experiments (DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande), while the comparison between CBF and SCGF spectral functions provides an estimate of theoretical uncertainties.
