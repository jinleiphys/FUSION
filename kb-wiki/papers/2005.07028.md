# Exploring the partonic phase at finite chemical potential in and out-of equilibrium

> We study the influence of the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ on the properties of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) in and out-of equilibrium. The description of the QGP in equilibrium is based on the effective propagators and couplings from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) that is matched to reproduce the equation-of-state of the partonic system above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ from lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We calculate the transport coefficients such as the ratio of shear viscosity $η$ and bulk viscosity $ζ$ over entropy density $s$, i.e., $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ in the $(T,μ_B)$ plane and compare to other model results available at $μ_B =0$. The out-of equilibrium study of the QGP is performed within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach extended in the partonic sector by explicitly calculating the total and differential partonic scattering cross sections (based on the DQPM propagators and couplings) evaluated at the actual temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ in each individual space-time cell of the partonic scattering. The traces of their $μ_B$ dependences are investigated in different observables for relativistic heavy-ion collisions with a focus on the directed and elliptic flow coefficients $v_1, v_2$ in the energy range 7.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le 200$ GeV.

# Exploring the partonic phase at finite chemical potential in and out-of equilibrium

## Key claim

The influence of baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$ on QGP properties is studied both in equilibrium and out-of-equilibrium, but the $\mu_B$ dependence of partonic masses, widths, and cross sections produces only very small changes in hadronic observables and collective flow coefficients, with the mildest sensitivity appearing in strange hadrons and anti-strange hyperons.

## Method

The equilibrium QGP is described using the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), which employs effective propagators with complex self-energies matched to lattice QCD equation-of-state data. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity $\eta$ and bulk viscosity $\zeta$) are calculated via the Kubo formalism and relaxation-time approximation (RTA) across the $(T,\mu_B)$ plane. The out-of-equilibrium study uses the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach (version 5.0), where partonic masses, widths, and differential cross sections from leading-order Feynman diagrams are evaluated at the local temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$ in each space-time cell, and results are compared to the earlier PHSD4.0 version where these quantities depend only on $T$.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ at $\mu_B=0$: DQPM results agree with gluodynamic lattice QCD data from Astrakhantsev (2017) and with Bayesian analysis from Bernhard (2016); the ratio increases with scaled temperature $T/T_c$.
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio $\zeta/s$ at $\mu_B=0$: DQPM results agree with lattice QCD data from Astrakhantsev (2018) and Meyer, showing a rise near $T_c$; the ratio is positive near $T_c$ unlike the Bayesian result which drops to zero.
- Both $\eta/s$ and $\zeta/s$ show a very weak dependence on $\mu_B$ for $\mu_B \le 400$ MeV.
- Directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ of identified hadrons are studied for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27$ GeV, comparing PHSD4.0, PHSD5.0 with $\mu_B=0$, and PHSD5.0 with full $\mu_B$ dependence; differences between the three versions are very small.
- The energy range studied for heavy-ion collisions is $7.7\ \mathrm{GeV} \le \sqrt{s_{NN}} \le 200\ \mathrm{GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the DQPM and PHSD approaches (Cassing 2007, 2008, 2009; Linnyk 2015) to include explicit $\mu_B$ dependence of partonic properties, building on the earlier extension by Moreau (2019). The transport coefficient results are compared to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2012, 2013) and Astrakhantsev (2017, 2018), to the non-conformal holographic model of Attems, and to Bayesian analyses of experimental data from Bernhard (2016). The flow observables are compared to STAR collaboration data from the Beam Energy Scan program. The finding that $\mu_B$ dependence is weak in bulk observables and flow coefficients motivates further experimental searches at FAIR, NICA, and the BESII program at RHIC, particularly focusing on strange and anti-strange hyperons as the most sensitive probes.
