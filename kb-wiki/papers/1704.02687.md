# How tightly is nuclear symmetry energy constrained by unitary Fermi gas?

> We examine critically how tightly the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy \esym is constrained by the universal equation of state (EOS) of the unitary Fermi gas $E_{\rm{UG}}(ρ)$ considering currently known uncertainties of higher order parameters describing the density dependence of the Equation of State of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. We found that $E_{\rm{UG}}(ρ)$ does provide a useful lower boundary for the \esym. However, it does not tightly constrain the correlation between the magnitude $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_0)$ and slope $L$ unless the curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy at saturation density $ρ_0$ is more precisely known. The large uncertainty in the skewness parameters affects the $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_0)$ versus $L$ correlation by the same almost as significantly as the uncertainty in $K_{\rm{sym}}$.

## Key claim

The universal equation of state of the unitary Fermi gas provides a useful lower boundary for the nuclear symmetry energy, but it does not tightly constrain the correlation between the symmetry energy at saturation density and its slope L unless the curvature K_sym of the symmetry energy at saturation density is more precisely known; the large uncertainty in the skewness parameters affects the E_sym(rho_0) versus L correlation almost as significantly as the uncertainty in K_sym.

## Method

The authors expanded the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy to third order in density around saturation density rho_0, using the incompressibility K_0, skewness J_0, and the symmetry energy parameters E_sym(rho_0), slope L, curvature K_sym, and skewness J_sym. They applied the conjecture that the energy of pure neutron matter is greater than or equal to the unitary Fermi gas energy E_UG(rho) = xi * E_F(rho) with Bertsch parameter xi = 0.37, and derived parametric equations for the lower boundary of E_sym(rho_0) versus L. They then varied K_sym, J_0, J_sym, K_0, and xi within their known uncertain ranges from 274 Skyrme and relativistic mean field models that pass pure neutron matter constraints.

## Key numbers

* The Bertsch parameter xi is taken as 0.37 +/- 0.005, consistent with the most accurate experimental value of 0.376(4) and lattice Monte Carlo value of 0.372(5).
* For symmetric nuclear matter, the incompressibility K_0 is 240 +/- 20 MeV or 230 +/- 40 MeV, and the skewness J_0 covers the range approximately -800 to +400 MeV across 274 Skyrme and RMF models.
* The total curvature parameter K_n = K_0 + K_sym varies approximately from -150 to +370 MeV, and the total skewness J_n = J_0 + J_sym varies approximately from -500 to +1000 MeV for the 275 models examined.
* The latest experimental constraint on the curvature from heavy-ion collisions gives K_sym = 0 +/- 370 (exp) +/- 220 (th) +/- 150 (sys) MeV and L = 59 +/- 24 (exp) +/- 16 (th) +/- 10 (sys) MeV at 1-sigma confidence level.
* Using the most conservative lower boundary parameters (rho_0 = 0.157 fm^-3, E_0(rho_0) = -15.5 MeV, xi = 0.37, K_n = 370 MeV, J_n = -500 MeV), only the TMA, NLrho_delta, NL3, and LS220 models are excluded, while models previously excluded by assuming K_sym = 0 (such as STOS, TM1, NLrho, LS220, and KVR) may be allowed.

## Context

This work critically examines the conjecture of Kolomeitsev et al. (2017) that the unitary Fermi gas equation of state provides a lower boundary for pure neutron matter and thus constrains the nuclear symmetry energy. The authors extend that analysis by including third-order density expansion terms (skewness parameters J_0 and J_sym) and considering the full range of K_sym values from both Skyrme and relativistic mean field models, using the model surveys of Dutra et al. (2012, 2014). The results show that the constraint on the E_sym(rho_0)-L correlation is much weaker than previously claimed, and that better experimental determination of K_sym (e.g., from giant resonances of neutron-rich nuclei or from the ASY-EOS and FOPI-LAND flow data) is needed before the unitary gas constraint can exclude specific functionals.
