# Charmonium properties at high temperatures from lattice QCD

> We study charmonium properties at non-zero temperature in the temperature range 153 MeV $<T<$ 305 MeV using lattice QCD. We use HISQ action for dynamical quarks and Wilson clover action for valence charm quarks and calculate the correlation function of extended meson operators. Our lattice QCD results are consistent with the existence of all charmonium states below the open charm threshold in this temperature region. However, charmonium states acquire sizable thermal width, which increases with increasing temperature. The size of the thermal width follows the hierarchy of charmonium sizes, i.e. the smaller ground state charmonium has a smaller thermal width than the larger excited charmonia.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD results are consistent with the existence of all charmonium states below the open charm threshold in the temperature range 153 MeV to 305 MeV, but the authors qualify that this consistency depends on using extended meson operators with improved overlap, and that the states acquire sizable thermal widths that increase with temperature following the hierarchy of charmonium sizes.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using 2+1-flavor HISQ action for dynamical quarks and Wilson clover action for valence charm quarks on 64^3 x N_tau lattices at two lattice spacings (a = 0.0493 fm and a = 0.0404 fm). They constructed correlation functions using extended meson operators with Gaussian smearing and wave-function-optimized operators obtained from a potential model, then extracted in-medium charmonium properties by fitting spectral function Ansatze to the subtracted correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 153 MeV < T < 305 MeV
- Lattice spacings: a = 0.0493 fm (beta = 7.596) and a = 0.0404 fm (beta = 7.825)
- Pion mass: approximately 161 MeV in the continuum limit
- Zero-temperature masses obtained: eta_c(1S) = 2993.2 +/- 0.9 MeV (beta=7.596) and 2995.1 +/- 0.5 MeV (beta=7.825); J/psi(1S) = 3092.1 +/- 0.4 MeV (beta=7.596) and 3096.1 +/- 0.8 MeV (beta=7.825); chi_c0(1P) = 3420.1 +/- 2.7 MeV (beta=7.596) and 3422.4 +/- 2.8 MeV (beta=7.825); chi_c1(1P) = 3505.8 +/- 5.0 MeV (beta=7.596) and 3506.0 +/- 4.8 MeV (beta=7.825); eta_c(2S) = 3650.0 +/- 8.8 MeV (beta=7.596) and 3654.1 +/- 7.1 MeV (beta=7.825); psi(2S) = 3695.0 +/- 5.7 MeV (beta=7.596) and 3703.6 +/- 8.1 MeV (beta=7.825)
- Gaussian smearing parameters: lambda = 7, 10, or 12 with N = 150, 300, or 450 iterations respectively

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to bottomonium using lattice NRQCD with extended operators (Larsen 2019, Larsen 2020, Ding 2025) to the charmonium system, which requires a fully relativistic treatment since NRQCD is not well justified for charm quarks. The use of extended meson operators addresses limitations of earlier charmonium studies that relied on point meson operators (Umeda 2002, Datta 2002, Karsch 2002, Asakawa 2003), which had limited overlap with bound states and reduced sensitivity to in-medium modifications (Mocsy 2007, Petreczky 2010, Burnier 2015). The results enable comparison with the bottomonium case to clarify whether color screening or dynamical imaginary-potential effects dominate quarkonium dissociation in the quark-gluon plasma.
