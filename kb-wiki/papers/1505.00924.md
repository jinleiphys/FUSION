# Collectivity in high-multiplicity events of proton-proton collisions in the framework of String Percolation

> We analyze high multiplicity proton-proton ($pp$) collision data at the energies $\sqrt{s}=900$ GeV, $2.76$ TeV and $7$ TeV in the framework of the String Percolation Model (SPM) in terms of the ratio of shear viscosity and entropy density ($η/s$) showing that the model allows the formation of strongly interacting collective medium in the high energy and high multiplicity events in p-p collisions.

## Key claim

The String Percolation Model (SPM) can describe the formation of a strongly interacting collective medium in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions, but only for events where the transverse impact parameter density of strings (ζ^t) exceeds the critical percolation threshold (ζ^t_c ≈ 1.2–1.5), below which no collective effects occur.

## Method

The authors fit transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from CMS data at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV for different multiplicity classes using the SPM formalism, extracting the string density parameter ζ^t for each class. From ζ^t they compute the color suppression factor F(ζ^t), the effective temperature T via the Schwinger mechanism, and finally the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s using relativistic kinetic theory with a mean free path derived from the percolation parameters.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 7 TeV, ζ^t values range from 0.77 ± 0.13 (⟨N_track⟩ = 40) to 4.98 ± 0.40 (⟨N_track⟩ = 131)
- The critical string density threshold for percolation is ζ^t_c ≈ 1.2–1.5 (depending on profile function)
- Single string average transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩_1 is approximately 200 MeV, with values from 184.76 ± 7.80 MeV (T_c = 154 MeV, ζ_c = 1.5) to 207.18 ± 3.21 MeV (T_c = 167.7 MeV, ζ_c = 1.2)
- Effective temperature T at 7 TeV increases from 145.86 ± 8.52 MeV (dN/dη = 5.88) to 201.46 ± 15.69 MeV (dN/dη = 43.67)
- η/s at 7 TeV decreases from 0.285 ± 0.052 (T/T_c = 0.964) to a minimum of 0.192 ± 0.017 (T/T_c ≈ 1.13–1.18), then rises slightly to 0.206 ± 0.166 (T/T_c = 1.319)

## Context

This work extends the String Percolation Model, previously successful in describing collectivity and phase transitions in nucleus-nucleus collisions (refs. [ref19, ref20, ref21] in the paper), to high-multiplicity proton-proton events at LHC energies. It builds on the observation of the "ridge" in long-range angular correlations in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV by CMS (ref. [ref10]), and supports the possibility of QGP-like collective behavior in small systems. The results enable further investigation of the onset of collectivity as a function of multiplicity and energy in pp collisions, and provide a framework for comparing with heavy-ion results.
