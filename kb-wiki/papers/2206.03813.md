# Quark and gluon GPDs at finite skewness from strings in holographic QCD: evolved and compared to experiment

> We present a framework for constructing Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) using holographic QCD in the large $N_c$ limit, with a focus on low-x and finite skewness. Our approach utilizes holographic amplitudes for exclusive electroproduction processes to extract the spin-j conformal (Gegenbauer) moments of GPDs, which are then evolved to higher resolution scales using QCD evolution equations. Our evolved GPDs (reconstructed from their evolved conformal moments) are applied to analyze the electroproduction of $ρ^{0,+}$ and $ϕ$ mesons, and we account for non-perturbative contributions in the $s+u$ - channel using holographic QCD. The results compare well with the existing experimental data. Our GPDs provide detailed information about partonic distributions and are useful for future experimental studies and global data analyses.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the conformal (Gegenbauer) moments of quark and gluon GPDs at finite skewness can be extracted from tree-level Witten diagrams in holographic QCD at a low resolution scale \(\mu_0 \sim 1.8\) GeV, then evolved to higher scales via perturbative QCD evolution equations, but this extraction relies on matching the large-\(N_c\) dependence of holographic amplitudes to leading-order QCD factorization amplitudes, and the resulting GPDs are only validated for low-\(x\) and finite skewness in the Regge limit.

## Method

The authors match the large-\(N_c\) dependence of exclusive electroproduction amplitudes (for vector mesons, pion pairs, DVCS, and neutral pions) computed from tree-level Witten diagrams in soft-wall holographic QCD to the corresponding leading-order QCD factorization amplitudes written in terms of Gegenbauer moments of GPDs. The extracted conformal moments at the initial scale \(\mu_0\) are then evolved to higher resolution scales using leading-order renormalization group equations (ERBL/DGLAP-type evolution). The evolved GPDs, reconstructed from their evolved conformal moments, are used to compute cross-sections for \(\rho^{0,+}\), and \(\phi\) meson electroproduction, which are compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The initial resolution scale is set to \(\mu_0 \sim 1.8\) GeV, where the gauge coupling is \(\alpha_s(\mu_0) \sim 1/3\) and the 't Hooft coupling is \(\lambda \equiv 4\pi\alpha_s(\mu_0)N_c = 11.243\).
* The anomalous dimension for the spin-\(j\) conformal gluon operator at \(\mu_0\) is \(\Delta_g(j) = 2 + \sqrt{2\sqrt{\lambda}(j - j_{0g})}\) with \(j_{0g} = 2 - 2/\sqrt{\lambda}\).
* The anomalous dimension for the spin-\(j\) conformal valence quark operator at \(\mu_0\) is \(\Delta_q(j) = 2 + \sqrt{\sqrt{\lambda}(j - j_{0q})}\) with \(j_{0q} = 1 - 1/\sqrt{\lambda}\).
* The Dirac electromagnetic form factor for spin-1 exchange is given by \(F_1(t) = \mathcal{F}_1(j=1, \tau, \Delta_q(j=1)=3, -t=K^2)\).
* The axial electromagnetic form factor for spin-1 exchange is given by \(F_A(t) = \mathcal{F}_A(j=1, \tau=3, \Delta_q(j=1)=3, -t=K^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to GPDs, building on prior analyses of holographic GPDs (Nishio 2014) and holographic electroproduction of heavy mesons near threshold (Mamo 2019, Mamo 2021, Mamo 2022). The framework provides a non-perturbative method to bypass the deconvolution problem in GPD extraction by directly identifying conformal moments from string theory amplitudes. The results are intended to be useful for future experimental studies at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and EIcC (Abdul Khalek 2021, Anderle 2021) and for global GPD data analyses.
