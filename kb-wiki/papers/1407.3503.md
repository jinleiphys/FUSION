# Quark Antiscreening at Strong Magnetic Field and Inverse Magnetic Catalysis

> The dependence of the QCD coupling constant with a strong magnetic field and the implications for the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition are investigated. It is found that the coupling constant becomes anisotropic in a strong magnetic field and that the quarks, confined by the field to the lowest Landau level where they pair with antiquarks, produce an antiscreening effect. These results lead to inverse magnetic catalysis, providing a natural explanation for the behavior of the critical temperature in the strong-field region.

# Quark Antiscreening at Strong Magnetic Field and Inverse Magnetic Catalysis

## Key claim
The QCD coupling constant becomes anisotropic in a strong magnetic field, and quarks confined to the lowest Landau level (LLL) produce an antiscreening effect rather than screening, which leads to inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC), a decrease of the chiral critical temperature with increasing magnetic field, provided the coupling is allowed to run with the field rather than being treated as a constant.

## Method
The authors extract the running coupling from the color Coulomb potential calculated using the background perturbation method (BPM), which incorporates nonperturbative gluon vacuum configurations to handle the infrared region where standard perturbation theory fails. They then incorporate the field-dependent parallel coupling into a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that respects the symmetries of QCD in a magnetic field, including both scalar and magnetic-moment condensates, and compute the chiral critical temperature from the mean-field thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers
- The infrared mass scale from the BPM is \(M_B \approx 1\) GeV, interpreted as the ground-state mass of two gluons connected by the fundamental string with string tension \(\tau = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\).
- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in the strong-field limit is \(T_{C_\chi} = 1.16 \sqrt{qB} \exp\left[-\pi / (2 N_c \alpha_s^\|)\right]\), where \(\alpha_s^\|\) is the parallel coupling.
- The magnetic field strengths considered are in the range \(qB \gg \Lambda_{QCD}^2 \sim (200\ \text{MeV})^2\), with the strong-field approximation requiring \(T \ll \sqrt{qB}\).
- The parallel coupling \(\alpha_s^\|\) decreases with increasing magnetic field for energy scales \(k_3 = (0.01, 0.1, 1) \Lambda_{QCD}\), with curves that essentially overlap across these scales.

## Context
This work addresses the disagreement between earlier NJL-model studies (which found that the chiral critical temperature increases with magnetic field, known as magnetic catalysis) and lattice QCD results (Lattice, 2012) that showed a decrease of \(T_c\) with field, the inverse magnetic catalysis. The authors extend prior work by Ferrer, de la Incera, and collaborators (NJL-B, 2014) that introduced a magnetic-moment condensate and anisotropic couplings in NJL models, and by Simonov and collaborators (Simonov, 2000s) who developed the BPM for nonperturbative QCD. The approach differs from that of Farias (2014), who proposed an ansatz for the NJL coupling with a logarithmic dependence on \(B\) that is not reliable in the strong-field region. The results enable a natural explanation of IMC as arising from the color paramagnetism of LLL quark-antiquark pairs, and suggest that if lattice QCD finds the string tension also decreases with field (lattice-Btension, 2014), the IMC effect would be even stronger.
