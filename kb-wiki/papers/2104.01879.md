# First Saturation Correction in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions: II. Single Inclusive Semi-Hard Gluon Production

> Exploiting recently obtained analytic solutions of classical Yang-Mills equations for higher order perturbations in the field of the dilute object (proton), we derive the complete first saturation correction to the single inclusive semi-hard gluon production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions by applying the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann reduction formula. We thus finalize the program started by Balitsky (see Ref.~\cite{Balitsky:2004rr}) and independently by Chirilli, Kovchegov and Wertepny (see Ref.~\cite{Chirilli:2015tea}) albeit using a very different approach to carry out our calculations. We extracted the functional dependence of gluon spectrum on the color charge densities of the colliding objects; thus our results can be used to evaluate complete first saturation correction to the double/multiple inclusive gluon productions.

## Key claim

This paper derives the complete first saturation correction to single inclusive semi-hard gluon production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions, including both the previously known order-\(g^3\) amplitude and the previously uncomputed order-\(g^5\) amplitude, thereby finalizing a program initiated by Balitsky (2004) and independently by Chirilli, Kovchegov and Wertepny (2015), but using a different approach based on analytic solutions of classical Yang-Mills equations.

## Method

The authors exploit recently obtained analytic solutions of classical Yang-Mills equations for higher-order perturbations in the field of the dilute object (proton), expressed as power series in the coupling constant \(g\) while preserving non-perturbative dependence on the target color charge density. They apply the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann reduction formula adapted to the Milne coordinate system to extract the single inclusive gluon production amplitude from the classical field solutions, computing both the order-\(g^3\) amplitude squared and the crossing terms between order-\(g\) and order-\(g^5\) amplitudes that constitute the first saturation correction.

## Key numbers

* The single inclusive gluon production spectrum is expressed as \(\frac{dN}{d^2\mathbf{k} dy} = \frac{1}{\alpha_s} f\left(\frac{Q_{s,P}^2}{k_{\perp}^2}, \frac{Q_{s,T}^2}{k_{\perp}^2}\right)\), where the leading term is \(\frac{1}{\alpha_s} \frac{Q_{s,P}^2}{k_{\perp}^2} f_1\left(\frac{Q_{s,T}^2}{k_{\perp}^2}\right)\) and the first saturation correction is proportional to \(\frac{1}{\alpha_s} Q_{s,P}^4\) in a Gaussian model.
* The leading order amplitude \(M_{(1)}\) scales as \(g\rho_P\), the order-\(g^3\) amplitude \(M_{(3)}\) scales as \(g^3\rho_P^2\), and the order-\(g^5\) amplitude \(M_{(5)}\) scales as \(g^5\rho_P^3\).
* The first saturation correction to the gluon spectrum consists of \(|M_{(3)}(\mathbf{k})|^2 + M_{(1)}(\mathbf{k})M_{(5)}^{\ast}(\mathbf{k}) + M_{(1)}^{\ast}(\mathbf{k})M_{(5)}(\mathbf{k})\), all proportional to \(g^6 \rho_P^4\).

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Balitsky (2004) and independently by Chirilli, Kovchegov and Wertepny (2015), who computed only the order-\(g^3\) single gluon production amplitude but not the order-\(g^5\) amplitude needed for the complete first saturation correction. The result provides the functional dependence of the gluon spectrum on the color charge densities of both colliding objects, enabling future evaluation of complete first saturation corrections to double and multiple inclusive gluon productions, as well as studies of event-by-event fluctuations and non-Gaussian color charge correlations in the proton.
