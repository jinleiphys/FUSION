# Chiral Dynamics and Nuclear Matter

> We calculate the equation of state of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter in the three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. The contributions to the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f)$ from one- and two-pion exchange diagrams are ordered in powers of the Fermi momentum $k_f$ (modulo functions of $k_f /m_π$). It is demonstrated that, already at order ${\cal O}(k_f^4)$, two-pion exchange produces realistic nuclear binding. The underlying saturation mechanism is surprisingly simple (in the chiral limit), namely the combination of an attractive $k_f^3$-term and a repulsive $k_f^4$-term. The empirical saturation point and the nuclear compressibility $K\simeq 250 $MeV are well reproduced at order ${\cal O}(k_f^5)$ with a momentum cut-off of $Λ\simeq 0.65$ GeV which parametrizes short-range dynamics. No further short-distance terms are required in our calculation of nuclear matter. In the same framework we calculate the density-dependent asymmetry energy and find $A_0\simeq 34 $MeV at the saturation point, in good agreement with the empirical value. The pure neutron matter equation of state is also in fair qualitative agreement with sophisticated many-body calculations and a resummation result of effective field theory, but only for low neutron densities $ρ_n <0.25 $fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral two-pion exchange, computed in a three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory, can reproduce realistic nuclear matter saturation and compressibility using only a single adjustable momentum cut-off \(\Lambda \simeq 0.65\) GeV to parametrize short-range dynamics, without introducing additional zero-range NN-contact interactions with freely adjustable strengths as done in prior work by Lutz et al. (1999).

## Method

The authors calculate the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter by evaluating one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, and irreducible two-pion exchange diagrams up to order \(\mathcal{O}(k_f^5)\) in the Fermi momentum \(k_f\), using a non-relativistic expansion and cut-off regularization. The in-medium nucleon propagator is used to incorporate the filled Fermi sea, and all contributions are ordered in powers of \(k_f\) (modulo functions of \(k_f/m_\pi\)), with the cut-off \(\Lambda\) absorbing the power divergences from the chiral two-pion exchange diagrams. The same framework is then applied to compute the density-dependent asymmetry energy and the pure neutron matter equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.178\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (corresponding to Fermi momentum \(k_{f0} = 272.7\ \text{MeV} = 1.382\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)) with binding energy \(\bar E_0 = -15.26\ \text{MeV}\), achieved with cut-off \(\Lambda = 646.3\ \text{MeV}\).
- Nuclear compressibility: \(K = 255\ \text{MeV}\), in agreement with the empirical value \(K = (250 \pm 25)\ \text{MeV}\).
- Asymmetry energy at saturation: \(A_0 \simeq 34\ \text{MeV}\), in good agreement with the empirical value.
- Pure neutron matter equation of state agrees with sophisticated many-body calculations only for low neutron densities \(\rho_n < 0.25\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- In the chiral limit (\(m_\pi = 0\)) at order \(\mathcal{O}(k_f^4)\), the dimensionless coefficient \(\beta = 13.55\) from the \(k_f^4\) term is close to the value \(\beta = 12.22\) extracted from the realistic equation of state of the Urbana group (1987).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear matter of Lutz et al. (1999) by computing terms up to order \(\mathcal{O}(k_f^5)\) and by using a single momentum cut-off \(\Lambda\) to represent short-range dynamics instead of introducing adjustable zero-range NN-contact interactions. The results show that chiral two-pion exchange alone can produce realistic nuclear binding and saturation, with the saturation mechanism in the chiral limit arising from the combination of an attractive \(k_f^3\) term and a repulsive \(k_f^4\) term. This enables a systematic exploration of the role of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter and provides a framework for further studies of isospin-asymmetric matter and neutron stars.
