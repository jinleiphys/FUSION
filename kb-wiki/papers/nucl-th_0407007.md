# $Θ^+$ Hypernuclei

> We present results for the selfenergy of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark in nuclei associated with two sources: the $KN$ decay of the $Θ^+$ and the two meson baryon decay channels of the $Θ^+$ partners in an antidecuplet of baryons. The first source is shown to produce a small potential, unable to bind the $Θ^+$ in nuclei, while the second source gives rise to a large attractive potential. At the same time we show that the width of the $Θ^+$ in nuclei is small, such that, in light and medium nuclei, many bound $Θ^+$ states would appear with a separation between levels appreciably larger than the width of the states, thus creating an ideal scenario for pentaquark spectroscopy in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the $KN$ decay channel alone produces a potential too weak to bind the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark in nuclei, the two-meson cloud mechanism (via coupling to an antidecuplet of baryons) generates a large attractive potential sufficient to produce many narrow bound $\Theta^+$ states in light and medium nuclei, provided the $\Theta^+$ is $J^P = 1/2^+$ and belongs to an $SU(3)$ antidecuplet with the $N^*(1710)$ as a partner.

## Method

The authors calculated the $\Theta^+$ selfenergy in infinite nuclear matter from two sources: the $KN$ decay channel (using in-medium kaon and nucleon propagators with Pauli blocking) and the two-meson ($K\pi N$, $K\eta N$) decay channels derived from $SU(3)$-symmetric Lagrangians whose couplings were fixed by the $N^*(1710)$ decay widths. Medium effects were incorporated by modifying the kaon propagator with $s$-wave and $p$-wave selfenergies and the pion propagator with its spectral function from $ph$ and $\Delta h$ excitation, then applying the local density approximation to obtain potentials for finite nuclei and solving the Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

* Free $\Theta^+$ width assumed as 15 MeV (upper experimental limit); studies cited suggest width below 5 MeV or even ~1 MeV.
* For $L=1$ ($J^P=1/2^+$) at 20 MeV binding, the $KN$ decay width in medium drops from 15 MeV to less than 6 MeV.
* Two-meson contribution to the $\Theta^+$ selfenergy at normal nuclear density $\rho_0$: real part between -70 MeV (cutoff 700 MeV) and -120 MeV (cutoff 800 MeV).
* Free $\Theta^+$ selfenergy contribution at $p^0=1540$ MeV and $\Lambda=700$ MeV: 48 MeV from scalar Lagrangian, 40 MeV from vector Lagrangian.
* For bound states with ~20 MeV binding, the two-meson decay width is below 5 MeV; for ~40 MeV binding, it is negligible.
* In $^{12}C$ with $V=-60\rho/\rho_0$ MeV: bound states at -34.0 MeV (1s), -14.6 MeV (1p), -0.3 MeV (2s).
* In $^{12}C$ with $V=-120\rho/\rho_0$ MeV: bound states at -87.3 MeV (1s), -59.5 MeV (1p), -32.0 MeV (2s), -31.9 MeV (1d), -8.6 MeV (2p), -5.6 MeV (1f).
* In $^{40}Ca$ with $V=-120\rho/\rho_0$ MeV: deepest bound state at -98.2 MeV (1s), with 14 bound levels listed down to -0.5 MeV (4s).

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei beyond the earlier calculation by Kim (2004), which considered only the $KN$ decay channel and found no binding. The authors introduce a new mechanism based on the two-meson cloud of the antidecuplet, following the Lagrangian approach of the Madrid group (2004) that couples the $\Theta^+$ to $K\pi N$ and $K\eta N$ channels via $SU(3)$ symmetry with the $N^*(1710)$ resonance. The results support the schematic quark-pair model of Miller (2004) that predicted stable $\Theta^+$ hypernuclei, and the predicted narrow widths and well-separated levels would enable experimental pentaquark spectroscopy in nuclei, complementing studies of $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ hypernuclei.
