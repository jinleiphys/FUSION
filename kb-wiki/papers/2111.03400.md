# Transverse momentum broadening in real-time lattice simulations of the glasma

> The study of jets in heavy-ion collisions provides important information about the interaction of partons with the medium that they traverse. The seeds of jets are highly energetic partons, which are produced from hard scatterings during the collision event. As such, they are affected by all different stages of the medium's time evolution, including the glasma, which is the pre-equilibrium precursor state of the quark-gluon plasma. Here we report on our numerical lattice simulations of partons traversing the boost-invariant, non-perturbative glasma as created at the early stages of collisions at RHIC and LHC. We find that partons quickly accumulate transverse momentum up to the saturation momentum during the glasma stage. Furthermore, we observe an interesting anisotropy in transverse momentum broadening of partons with larger broadening in the rapidity than in the azimuthal direction. Its origin can be related to correlations among the longitudinal color-electric and color-magnetic flux tubes in the initial state of the glasma. We compare these observations to the semi-analytic results obtained by a weak-field approximation, where we also find such an anisotropy in a parton's transverse momentum broadening.

## Key claim

The authors report that partons traversing the glasma quickly accumulate transverse momentum up to the saturation momentum during the glasma stage, and they observe an anisotropy in transverse momentum broadening with larger broadening in the rapidity direction than in the azimuthal direction; this anisotropy is present in both the full lattice simulations and the semi-analytic weak-field approximation, and its origin can be related to correlations among the longitudinal color-electric and color-magnetic flux tubes in the initial state of the glasma.

## Method

The authors perform real-time lattice simulations of the boost-invariant glasma by discretizing the transverse plane as a regular N_T x N_T lattice with periodic boundary conditions and solving the discretized Yang-Mills equations using a leapfrog scheme. They simulate an ultra-relativistic test parton moving at the speed of light in the x-direction through the glasma, computing the accumulated transverse momentum squared via discretized versions of the Wong equations, with the color-electric and color-magnetic fields evaluated to second order in both lattice spacing and time step. The results are averaged over random color charge densities drawn from a Gaussian distribution according to the McLerran-Venugopalan model, and the lattice simulations are compared to a semi-analytic weak-field approximation where the glasma initial conditions are treated perturbatively in the charge density.

## Key numbers

* For a dense glasma with fixed saturation momentum Q_s, the y-component of momentum broadening rises sharply until tau approximately equal to Q_s^{-1} and flattens out afterwards, while the z-component exhibits a peak just below Q_s tau = 10 and decreases afterwards.
* The total transverse momentum broadening at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c is approximately Q_s^2, with the exact value depending on the density of the glasma parameterized by m / g^2 mu.
* The lattice simulations use N_T = 1024 points in each transverse direction, a time step Delta tau = a_T / 16, N_s = 50 color sheets per nucleus, and lattice resolution g^2 mu a_T approximately 0.1 for g^2 mu L = 100.
* The dense glasma simulations use m / g^2 mu values in the set {0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2}, with results averaged over 50 random initial conditions.
* The momentum broadening anisotropy is largely independent of the saturation momentum but depends on the glasma density.

## Context

This work extends the semi-analytic results of Ipp (2020a) and Ipp (2020b), which derived an analytic relation between the anisotropy of momentum broadening and correlations among color flux tubes in the initial state of the glasma using a weak-field approximation. The present paper goes beyond the dilute limit by performing full real-time lattice simulations for the physically relevant dense glasma, where strong color fields are present. The results enable future studies that could incorporate more realistic initial conditions including finite nuclear spatial extent and rapidity dependence, as recently explored by Ipp (2021), as well as relaxation of the ultra-relativistic test-particle approximation to include parton deflection and back-reaction on the glasma.
