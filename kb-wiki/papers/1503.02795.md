# Hybrid neutron stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model and various quark-gluon vertices

> We study cold dense quark matter and hybrid neutron stars with a Dyson-Schwinger quark model and various choices of the quark-gluon vertex. We obtain the equation of state of quark matter in beta equilibrium and investigate the hadron-quark phase transition in combination with a hadronic equation of state derived within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory. Comparing with the results for quark matter with the rainbow approximation, the Ball-Chiu ansatz and the 1BC ansatz for the quark-gluon vertex lead to a reduction of the effective interaction at finite chemical potential, qualitatively similar to the effect of our gluon propagator. We find that the phase transition and the equation of state of the quark or mixed phase and consequently the resulting hybrid star mass and radius depend mainly on a global reduction of the effective interaction due to effects of both the quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator, but are not sensitive to the vertex ansatz.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the equation of state of hybrid neutron stars and the resulting stellar mass-radius relation depend mainly on a global reduction of the effective interaction due to both the quark-gluon vertex and the gluon propagator, but are not sensitive to the particular vertex ansatz (rainbow, 1BC, or Ball-Chiu) once the phenomenological parameter α is rescaled to yield the same Maxwell phase transition point.

## Method

The authors combine a hadronic equation of state from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory (using the Bonn B potential with a compatible three-body force) with a Dyson-Schwinger quark model for cold, beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral quark matter. They solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using three different ansätze for the dressed quark-gluon vertex: the rainbow approximation (bare vertex), the 1BC vertex (first term of the Ball-Chiu vertex), and the full Ball-Chiu vertex, each with a Gaussian gluon propagator modified by a chemical-potential-dependent parameter α. The hadron-quark phase transition is studied under both Maxwell and Gibbs constructions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- The gluon propagator parameters are ω = 0.5 GeV, d = 1 GeV² (rainbow), d = 0.5 GeV² (BC vertex), d = 0.75 GeV² (1BC vertex), with m_{u,d} = 0 and m_s = 0.115 GeV.
- A phenomenological bag constant B_DS = 90 MeV fm⁻³ is used for the quark phase.
- For the rainbow approximation with α = 1 and α = 2, the model previously supported hybrid stars with maximum masses larger than two solar masses.
- To match the same Maxwell phase transition point, the rescaled α values are α = 0.8 (1BC) and α = 0.25 (BC) corresponding to α = 1 for rainbow; and α = 1.7 (1BC) and α = 0.93 (BC) corresponding to α = 2 for rainbow.
- The maximum hybrid star masses are higher than two solar masses for all three vertex ansätze with the chosen α values, but lower than the pure nucleonic maximum mass.
- The nuclear saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger model study (Chen, 2011) that used only the rainbow approximation, by incorporating the Ball-Chiu and 1BC vertex ansätze. It contrasts with the MIT bag model (which gives a softer equation of state) and with other quark models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Buballa, 2004; Schertler, 1999) and the color dielectric model, which yield smaller effective bag constants. The results enable future work to focus on obtaining model-independent information about the quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator at finite baryon density from their own Dyson-Schwinger equations.
