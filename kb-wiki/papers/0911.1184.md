# The Brieva-Rook Localization of the Microscopic Nucleon-Nucleus Potential

> The nonlocality of the microscopic nucleon-nucleus optical potential is commonly localized by the Brieva-Rook approximation. The validity of the localization is tested for the proton+$^{90}$Zr scattering at the incident energies from 65 MeV to 800 MeV. The localization is valid in the wide incident-energy range.

## Key claim

The Brieva-Rook (BR) localization of the microscopic nucleon-nucleus optical potential is valid for proton+⁹⁰Zr scattering across the incident energy range 65–800 MeV, but the approximation is most accurate at forward scattering angles (q ≲ 1.7 fm⁻¹) at the lower end of this range, with noticeable deviations at larger momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors solved the full nonlocal Schrödinger equation for the microscopic folding potential (constructed from the Melbourne g-matrix interaction and Hartree-Fock wave functions for ⁹⁰Zr using the Gogny D1S force) and compared the resulting elastic-scattering cross sections and partial wave functions with those obtained from the BR-localized equivalent local potential. They separately tested the three components of the BR approximation, the local semi-classical approximation (LSCA), the local Fermi-gas approximation (LFGA), and the spherical Bessel approximation (j₀ approximation), and demonstrated that the j₀ approximation is redundant when LFGA is evaluated at the coordinate R (LFGA-R).

## Key numbers

- The BR localization was tested for p+⁹⁰Zr scattering at incident energies of 65, 185, 400, and 800 MeV.
- At 65 MeV, the BR localization error is small for transferred wave numbers q ≲ 1.7 fm⁻¹ (where experimental data exist) but becomes sizable near q ≈ 3.5 fm⁻¹.
- The Hartree-Fock binding energy of ⁹⁰Zr was 785.995 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 783.894 MeV.
- The range of the g-matrix interaction is approximately s ≲ 1.5 fm; the short-ranged interaction g_Tz^EX has a range of about 0.5 fm.
- LSCA and LSCA-A (using asymptotic momentum) are accurate for E ≳ 65 MeV; at 65 MeV the accuracy is slightly worse than at 185 and 400 MeV.
- The r_g-ambiguity in the BR local potential is appreciable only at dips for 65 MeV and at tops for 185 MeV; for E ≳ 400 MeV the differences become negligible.

## Context

This work extends the validation of the Brieva-Rook localization (Brieva and Rook, 1977) beyond the previously tested energy range of 40–250 MeV (Amos and collaborators) up to 800 MeV, using the Melbourne g-matrix interaction that includes pion-production effects (von Geramb). The study enables practical use of local equivalent potentials in few-body reaction calculations such as continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) for unstable projectiles like ⁸B, where nonlocal potentials would otherwise be computationally prohibitive. The authors show that the j₀ approximation in the original BR derivation is redundant, simplifying the theoretical justification for the method.
