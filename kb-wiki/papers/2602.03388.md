# Exploring Hyperon Skyrme Forces in Multi-$Λ$ Hypernuclei and Neutron Star Matter

> A major source of uncertainty in modeling the strangeness-rich interiors of neutron stars arises from the poorly constrained two-body and three-body interactions among hyperons and nucleons. We perform a comprehensive Bayesian analysis of the $ΛΛ$ and $ΛΛN$ interaction parameters within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework, constrained by both hypernuclei experimental data and astrophysical observations. Our results show that the parameter space of the $ΛΛ$ interaction is tightly constrained by combining nuclear and astrophysical data, while the parameters of the $ΛΛN$ three-body interaction remain sensitive to astrophysical inputs alone. Specifically, the local, momentum-independent two-body interaction parameter $λ_0$ is tightly constrained and predominantly attractive, while the momentum-dependent parameters $λ_1$ and $λ_2$ contribute repulsive effects at high densities. A key role is played by the $ΛΛ$ potential depth in pure $Λ$ matter, which effectively constrains the two-body $ΛΛ$ interaction and governs the balance between attraction at low densities and repulsion at high densities. The repulsive components of $ΛΛ$ interactions then decrease hyperon fractions and reconcile hyperon-rich equations of state with the observed $\sim2\,M_{\odot}$ neutron stars, increasing the maximum mass by up to 22\%. The inclusion of $ΛΛN$ three-body forces further stiffens the EOS, raising the maximum mass by up to $\sim 0.1\,M_{\odot}$. Our study represents a promising step toward a complete, experimentally grounded description of dense matter across a wide range of densities and strangeness compositions.

# Exploring Hyperon Skyrme Forces in Multi-Λ Hypernuclei and Neutron Star Matter

## Key claim
The parameter space of the ΛΛ two-body interaction is tightly constrained by combining nuclear hypernuclei data with astrophysical observations, while the ΛΛN three-body interaction parameters remain sensitive to astrophysical inputs alone; the repulsive components of ΛΛ interactions can reconcile hyperon-rich equations of state with observed ~2 M⊙ neutron stars, increasing the maximum mass by up to 22%.

## Method
The authors perform a Bayesian analysis of ΛΛ and ΛΛN interaction parameters within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework, using three NN interactions (SLy4, SKI3, SGI), three ΛN interactions (YMR, HPΛ2, SLL4), and five ΛΛ interaction sets (SLL1, SLL2, SLL3, SLL1′, SLL3′). They construct equations of state for neutron star matter including leptonic contributions, enforce β-equilibrium and charge neutrality, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar configurations, and compute tidal deformabilities for comparison with GW170817 constraints.

## Context
This work extends previous efforts by the same group to constrain nuclear matter parameters near saturation density (Zhao et al. 2023), quantify ΛN interaction effects (Sun et al. 2023), and explore double-strangeness hypernuclei (Deng et al. 2025). It addresses the hyperon puzzle, the difficulty of reconciling hyperon-rich equations of state with 2 M⊙ neutron stars, by showing that repulsive ΛΛ and ΛΛN interactions can stiffen the equation of state sufficiently. The Bayesian framework enables systematic combination of hypernuclear experimental data (from double-Λ hypernuclei such as ¹⁰ΛΛBe, ¹³ΛΛB, and ⁶ΛΛHe) with astrophysical constraints from NICER and LIGO/Virgo (GW170817), providing a path toward experimentally grounded descriptions of dense matter with strangeness.
