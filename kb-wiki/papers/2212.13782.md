# Comprehensive theory of the Lamb shift in light muonic atoms

> A comprehensive theory of the Lamb shift in light muonic atoms such as $μ$H, $μ$D, $μ^3$He$^+$, and $μ^4$He$^+$ is presented, with all quantum electrodynamic corrections included at the precision level constrained by the uncertainty of nuclear structure effects. This analysis can be used in the global adjustment of fundamental constants and in the determination of nuclear charge radii. Further improvements in the understanding of electromagnetic interactions of light nuclei will allow for a promising test of fundamental interactions by comparison with ``normal" atomic spectroscopy, in particular, with H-D and $^3$He-$^4$He isotope shifts.

## Key claim

The paper presents a comprehensive theory of the Lamb shift in light muonic atoms (μH, μD, μ³He⁺, μ⁴He⁺) with all quantum electrodynamic corrections included, but the precision is ultimately constrained by the uncertainty of nuclear structure effects rather than by QED itself.

## Method

The authors calculate the 2P₁/₂−2S₁/₂ energy difference (Lamb shift) by summing contributions classified by powers of the fine-structure constant α and Zα, with exact muon-nucleus mass dependence for corrections up to α⁵ and mass-ratio expansions for α⁶ terms. Point-nucleus QED corrections are separated from finite nuclear size and nuclear structure effects, using the Breit-Pauli Hamiltonian, vacuum polarization potentials (Uehling, Källén-Sabry), and Bethe logarithms. Numerical values are obtained using CODATA 2018 constants and compared to experimental Lamb shift measurements to extract nuclear charge radii.

## Key numbers

- Leading one-loop electron vacuum polarization contribution to the Lamb shift: 205.00738 meV (μH), 227.63470 meV (μD), 1641.8862 meV (μ³He⁺), 1665.7731 meV (μ⁴He⁺)
- Total point-nucleus QED contribution E_QED: 206.0344(3) meV (μH), 228.7740(3) meV (μD), 1644.348(8) meV (μ³He⁺), 1668.491(7) meV (μ⁴He⁺)
- Two-photon exchange nuclear structure contribution: 0.0292(25) meV (μH), 1.979(20) meV (μD), 16.38(31) meV (μ³He⁺), 9.76(40) meV (μ⁴He⁺)
- Extracted nuclear charge radii from this review: r_p = 0.84060(39) fm (proton), r_d = 2.12758(78) fm (deuteron), r_h = 1.97007(94) fm (helion), r_α = 1.6786(12) fm (alpha particle)
- Experimental Lamb shift values used: 202.3706(23) meV (μH), 202.8785(34) meV (μD), 1258.598(48) meV (μ³He⁺), 1378.521(48) meV (μ⁴He⁺)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Borie (1982) and the approach exact in mass ratio by Pachucki (1996) for light muonic atoms, incorporating recalculations and updates from recent literature on μH, μD, μ³He⁺, and μ⁴He⁺. The consistent treatment across all four muonic atoms enables improved determination of nuclear charge radii for use in the CODATA global adjustment of fundamental constants, and allows comparison with electronic atom spectroscopy (H-D and ³He-⁴He isotope shifts) as a test of lepton universality and search for unknown lepton-nucleus interactions.
