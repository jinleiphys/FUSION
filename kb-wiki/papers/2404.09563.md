# Upper Limit of Sound Speed in Nuclear Matter: A Harmonious Interplay of Transport Calculation and Perturbative Quantum Chromodynamic Constraint

> Very recently, it has been shown that there is an upper bound on the squared sound speed of nuclear matter from the transport, which reads $c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781$. In this work, we demonstrate that this upper bound is corroborated by the reconstructed equation of state (EOS; modeled with a nonparametric method) for ultradense matter. The reconstruction integrates multimessenger observation for neutron stars, in particular, the latest radius measurements for PSR J0437-4715 ($11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}$ km), PSR J0030+0451 ($11.71^{+0.88}_{-0.83}$ km, in the ST+PDT model), and PSR J0740+6620 ($12.49^{+1.28}_{-0.88}$ km) by NICER have been adopted. The result shows in all cases, the $c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781$ upper limit for EOS will naturally yield the properties of matter near the center of the massive neutron star consistent with the causality-driven constraint from pQCD, where, in practice, the density in implementing the pQCD likelihood ($n_{\rm L}$) is applied at $10n_s$ (where $n_s$ is the nuclear saturation density). We also note that there is a strong correlation for the maximum $c_s^2$ with $n_{\rm L}$, and $c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781$ is somehow violated when $n_{\rm L} = n_{\rm c,TOV}$. The result indicates that a higher $n_{\rm L}$, even considering the uncertainties from statistics, is more natural. Moreover, the remarkable agreement between the outcomes derived from these two distinct and independent constraints (i.e., the transport calculation and pQCD boundary) lends strong support to their validity. In addition, the latest joint constraint for $R_{1.4}$, $R_{2.0}$, $R_{1.4}-R_{2.0}$, and $M_{\rm TOV}$ are $11.94_{-0.68}^{+0.77}$ km, $11.99_{-0.67}^{+0.88}$ km, $-0.1_{-0.27}^{+0.42}$ km, and $2.24_{-0.10}^{+0.13}M_\odot$ (at $90\%$ credible level), respectively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recently proposed transport-based upper bound on the squared sound speed of nuclear matter, \(c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781\), is consistent with the equation of state reconstructed from multimessenger neutron star observations combined with perturbative QCD constraints, but only when the pQCD likelihood is applied at a high density of \(10n_s\) rather than at the central density of the maximum-mass neutron star.

## Method

The authors used a nonparametric Gaussian-process method to generate an ensemble of neutron star equations of state, then applied Bayesian inference incorporating likelihoods from gravitational-wave data (GW170817), NICER radius measurements for three pulsars, a maximum-mass cutoff distribution, and a pQCD likelihood term. They compared two scenarios for applying the pQCD likelihood: at the central density of the maximum-mass neutron star (\(n_{\rm c,TOV}\)) and at a fixed density of \(10n_s\) (where \(n_s\) is nuclear saturation density), and also examined the effect of imposing the transport bound \(c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781\) across the entire equation of state without pQCD information.

## Key numbers

- The transport bound on squared sound speed is \(c_{\rm s}^2 \leq 0.781\).
- NICER radius measurements used: PSR J0437-4715 at \(11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}\) km, PSR J0030+0451 at \(11.71^{+0.88}_{-0.83}\) km (ST+PDT model), and PSR J0740+6620 at \(12.49^{+1.28}_{-0.88}\) km.
- The pQCD likelihood was applied at \(n_{\rm L} = 10n_s\) where \(n_s\) is nuclear saturation density.
- Joint constraints at 90% credible level: \(R_{1.4} = 11.94_{-0.68}^{+0.77}\) km, \(R_{2.0} = 11.99_{-0.67}^{+0.88}\) km, \(R_{1.4} - R_{2.0} = -0.1_{-0.27}^{+0.42}\) km, and \(M_{\rm TOV} = 2.24_{-0.10}^{+0.13} M_\odot\).
- The tidal deformability for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star is constrained to \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 351_{-113}^{+195}\).

## Context

This work extends the recent transport-based bound on sound speed from Hiramatsu et al. (2024) by testing it against neutron star observations and pQCD constraints. It builds on the Gaussian-process equation-of-state reconstruction framework developed by Landry et al. (2020), Essick et al. (2020), and Legred et al. (2021), and addresses the ongoing debate about the appropriate density for applying pQCD likelihoods, as discussed by Komoltsev and Kurkela (2022), Gorda et al. (2023), and others. The results enable future multimessenger observations to potentially distinguish between different pQCD implementation densities and support the existence of quark matter cores in massive neutron stars.
