# Branching ratios of $α$-decay to excited states of even-even nuclei

> We make a systematic calculation on $α$-decay branching ratios to members of ground-state rotational band and to excited $0^{+}$ states of even-even nuclei by a simple barrier penetration approach. The branching ratios to the excited states of daughter nucleus are determined by the $α$-decay energy, the angular momentum of $α$-particle, and the excitation probability of the daughter nucleus. Our calculation covers isotopic chains from Hg to Fm in the mass regions 180$<$A$<$202 and A$\geq$224. The calculated branching ratios of $α$-transitions are consistent with the experimental data. Some useful predictions are made for future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a simple barrier penetration approach, using a square-well potential and a single free parameter (c = 1.5) to represent the excitation probability of the daughter nucleus via a Boltzmann distribution, can systematically reproduce experimental α-decay branching ratios to excited states of even-even nuclei across a wide mass range, with the caveat that the model systematically overestimates the branching ratio to the 4⁺ state of the ground-state rotational band.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation with a square-well nuclear potential and a Coulomb-plus-centrifugal barrier, using the WKB approximation to obtain the penetration probability. They multiply this penetration factor by a Boltzmann excitation probability factor w(E*_ℓ) = exp(-c E*_ℓ) with c = 1.5, and define the total transition probability I_ℓ⁺ as their product. Branching ratios to each rotational-band member (0⁺, 2⁺, 4⁺, 6⁺, 8⁺) and to excited 0⁺ states are then computed as the ratio of I_ℓ⁺ for that state to the sum over all considered states.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸Pu, the experimental branching ratio to the 4⁺ state is 0.105%, while the ratio b(4⁺)/b(2⁺) is approximately 276, compared to ratios of ~35 for b(4⁺)/b(6⁺) and ~44 for b(6⁺)/b(8⁺).
- For ²²⁶Th, the calculated branching ratio to the excited 0⁺ state at 0.914 MeV is 0.00040%, compared to the experimental value of 0.00034%.
- For ²³⁰U, the calculated branching ratio to the excited 0⁺ state at 0.805 MeV is 0.00042%, compared to the experimental value of 0.00030%.
- For ²³⁴U, the calculated branching ratio to the excited 0⁺ state at 0.635 MeV is 0.00025%, which is ten times larger than the experimental value of 2.6×10⁻⁵%.
- For ¹⁸⁰Hg, the calculated branching ratio to the excited 0⁺ state at 0.443 MeV is 0.18%, compared to the experimental value of 2.6×10⁻²%.
- The experimental branching ratio to the excited 0⁺ state varies by a factor of approximately 10³ across the nuclei studied (from 0.0006% down to 5.1×10⁻⁷%).

## Context

This work extends the Gamow theory of α-decay (Gamow 1928) to hindered (unfavored) transitions, which are strongly suppressed compared to ground-state-to-ground-state decays. It builds on earlier systematic calculations of favored α-decay half-lives by Royer using the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) and by the authors themselves using the density-dependent cluster model (DDCM). The approach enables predictions of unobserved branching ratios to excited 0⁺ states for nuclei such as ²³²Th, ²³⁶U, ²⁴²Pu, and ²⁴⁴Cm, and identifies a systematic "abnormity" of the 4⁺ state that warrants further microscopic investigation.
