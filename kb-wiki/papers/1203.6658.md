# Coupling dependence of jet quenching in hot strongly-coupled gauge theories

> Previous top-down studies of jet stopping in strongly-coupled QCD-like plasmas with gravity duals have been in the infinite 't Hooft coupling limit lambda -> infinity. They have found that, though a wide range of jet stopping distances are possible depending on initial conditions, the maximum jet stopping distance l_max scales with energy as E^(1/3) at large energy. But it has always been unclear whether the large-coupling and high-energy limits commute. In this paper, we use the string alpha' expansion in AdS-CFT to study the corrections to the lambda=infinity result in powers of 1/lambda. For the particular type of "jets" that we study, we find that (i) the naive expansion in 1/lambda breaks down for certain initial conditions but (ii) the relative corrections to the maximum stopping distance are small when 1/lambda is small. More specifically, we find that the expansion in 1/lambda is well behaved for jets whose stopping distance l_stop is in the range lambda^(-1/6) l_max << l_stop <~ l_max, but the expansion breaks down (and the fate of lambda=infinity results is uncertain) for jets created in such a way that l_stop << lambda^(-1/6) l_max. The analysis requires assessing the effects of all higher-derivative corrections to the supergravity action for the gravity dual.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for finite but large 't Hooft coupling lambda, the naive expansion in powers of 1/lambda for jet stopping distances breaks down for jets with stopping distances much smaller than lambda^{-1/6} times the maximum stopping distance, but the relative corrections to the maximum stopping distance itself remain small when 1/lambda is small.

## Method

The authors use the string alpha' expansion in AdS-CFT to study higher-derivative corrections to the supergravity action, including terms of the form R^4, D^{2n}R^4, and D^{2n}R^m. They analyze how these corrections modify the geodesic trajectories of 5-dimensional wave packets in AdS_5-Schwarzschild that represent high-momentum excitations created by external sources, using a geometric optics approximation valid for stopping distances much smaller than the maximum.

## Context

This work extends previous top-down studies of jet stopping in strongly-coupled plasmas with gravity duals by Hatta, Iancu, and Mueller (2011) and by Arnold, Szepietowski, and Vaman (2011), which were limited to the infinite coupling limit lambda -> infinity. The paper addresses the unresolved question of whether the large-coupling and high-energy limits commute, which is important for applying these results to real QCD where lambda is not fantastically large. The analysis enables future work on how the E^{1/3} scaling at strong coupling interpolates to the E^{1/2} scaling at weak coupling, and it identifies the regime where a full string-theory analysis would be needed to determine the fate of jets with very small stopping distances.
