# 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present the first comparisons of experimental data with phenomenological results from 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP). We compare charged-hadron multiplicity, identified-particle spectra, identified-particle average transverse momentum, charged-particle elliptic flow, and identified-particle elliptic flow produced in LHC 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The dynamical equations used for the hydrodynamic stage utilize non-conformal aHydroQP. The resulting aHydroQP framework naturally includes both shear and bulk viscous effects in addition to higher-order non-linear transport coefficients. The 3+1d aHydroQP evolution obtained is self-consistently converted to hadrons using anisotropic Cooper-Frye freezeout performed on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface. The final production and decays of the primordial hadrons are modeled using a customized version of THERMINATOR 2. In this first study, we utilized smooth Glauber-type initial conditions and a single effective freeze-out temperature T_FO = 130 MeV with all hadronic species in full chemical equilibrium. With this rather simple setup, we find a very good description of many heavy-ion observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first phenomenological comparisons of experimental data with 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP), finding good agreement with many heavy-ion observables using a simple setup of smooth Glauber-type initial conditions and a single freeze-out temperature, but the authors note this is only a first step and that improvements such as fluctuating initial conditions and temperature-dependent shear viscosity are needed.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1d aHydroQP dynamical equations on a 64^3 lattice with fourth-order centered-differences for spatial derivatives and fourth-order Runge-Kutta time stepping, starting at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c. They used smooth Glauber-type initial conditions with a binary mixing factor α = 0.15 and a tilted longitudinal profile, then converted the hydrodynamic evolution to hadrons using anisotropic Cooper-Frye freezeout on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface at T_FO = 130 MeV, with final hadronic decays modeled by a customized version of THERMINATOR 2.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature T0 = 600 MeV, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.159, and freeze-out temperature T_FO = 130 MeV obtained from fitting to identified-particle spectra
- Lattice size of 64^3 with spacings Δx = Δy = 0.5 fm and Δς = 0.375, time step Δτ = 0.02 fm/c
- Longitudinal profile parameters Δς = 2.3 and σς = 1.6 fitted to charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions
- Between 7,400 and 36,200 hadronic events used per centrality class depending on the observable
- Binary mixing factor α = 0.15 used in initial energy density calculation

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) program, which was developed to address issues with traditional second-order viscous hydrodynamics at early times when momentum-space anisotropy is large (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010). It builds on prior quasiparticle aHydro developments (Alqahtani 2015, 2016) and provides the first phenomenological test of the full 3+1d framework against LHC 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collision data from the ALICE collaboration. The results enable future studies with fluctuating initial conditions, temperature-dependent η/s, and more realistic collisional kernels, and suggest that the peak bulk viscosity required is substantially smaller than assumed in previous work such as Ryu (2015).
