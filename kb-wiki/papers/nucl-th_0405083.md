# Multiplicity Distributions of Intermediate Mass Fragments in the Thermodynamic Model

> Multiplicity distributions of intermediate mass fragments (IMF) seen in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions have been of great interest in the last ten years. The distributions of single intermediate sized element are close to Poissonian and have been subjected to widely different physical interpretations. We calculate the multiplicity distributions in a thermodynamic model whose physical basis is equal population in phase space which implies equilibrium statistics. We found distributions of single element are to a good approximation Poissonian, and such result is attributed to the small emission probability of a given IMF among all the possible channels. We also found significant deviation from Poisson distribution for a range of IMFs, and suggest strong correlations as an explanation. Systematics of the multiplicity distributions are explored in both one component and two component models. In one component model, sudden changes for the multiplicity distributions are found at the phase transition region. In two component model, the multiplicity distributions have more subtleties. Results from the current model are compared with available data, and good agreements are found. New signals for the multi-particle correlations are also suggested.

## Key claim

The authors calculate multiplicity distributions of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) in a thermodynamic canonical model based on equal population in phase space, finding that single-element distributions are approximately Poissonian due to the small emission probability of any given IMF, but that significant deviations from Poissonian behavior appear for a range of IMFs, which they attribute to strong correlations.

## Method

The authors use a canonical thermodynamic model where the partition function for a system of A identical particles (or for two-component systems with neutrons and protons) is computed via recursion relations, allowing exact calculation of multiplicity distributions without Monte Carlo simulations. They compute the probability P_n(k) that n clusters of a given size k are emitted using a tagging method that sets the partition function for that cluster to zero, and extend this to multiplicity distributions for a range of IMFs (k=6 to 40 in the one-component model, or Z=3 to 20 in the two-component model). They also incorporate the effects of cluster decay and feeding by redefining formation probabilities and recomputing the partition function.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition temperature T_b = 6.5 MeV for a one-component system with A=200 at density ρ=0.05 fm^{-3}
- For the one-component model, the specific heat peak at T=6.5 MeV defines the phase transition temperature for the finite system
- In the two-component model with A_sys=200 and Z_sys=86, the phase transition temperature is T_b=4.6 MeV
- The surface tension parameterization uses σ_0=18 MeV and T_c=18 MeV, with σ(T)=σ_0[(T_c^2-T^2)/(T_c^2+T^2)]^{5/4}
- The volume energy term W_0=16 MeV and the Fermi-gas parameter ε_0=16 MeV
- The IMF range is taken as k=6 to 40 in the one-component model and Z=3 to 20 in the two-component model
- The system isospin asymmetry is δ=(A-2Z)/A=0.14 for the two-component system with A=200, Z=86

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic canonical model previously developed by Chase (1993), Chase (1995), and Das (2003) to calculate multiplicity distributions, which were previously difficult to obtain from BUU simulations (Bertsch, 1988) or from Monte Carlo-based models such as the Copenhagen model (Bondorf, 1995) or the Berlin model (Gross, 1997). The results enable comparison with experimental data on IMF multiplicity distributions from intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, and the authors suggest new signals for multi-particle correlations, particularly the Poisson test parameter λ(M) which reveals deviations from Poissonian behavior near the phase transition temperature.
