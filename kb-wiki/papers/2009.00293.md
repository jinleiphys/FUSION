# Enhancement of Lithium in Red Clump Stars by the Additional Energy Loss Induced by New Physics

> Since 7Li is easily destroyed in low temperatures, the surface lithium abundance decreases as stars evolve. This is supported by the lithium depletion observed in the atmosphere of most red giants. However, recent studies show that almost all of red clump stars have high lithium abundances A(Li)>-0.9, which are not predicted by the standard theory of the low-mass stellar evolution. In order to reconcile the discrepancy between the observations and the model, we consider additional energy loss channels which may come from physics beyond the Standard Model. A(Li) slightly increases near the tip of the red giant branch even in the standard model with thermohaline mixing because of the 7Be production by the Cameron-Fowler mechanism, but the resultant 7Li abundance is much lower than the observed values. We find that the production of 7Be becomes more active if there are additional energy loss channels, because themohaline mixing becomes more efficient and a heavier helium core is formed.

## Key claim

The observed ubiquitous lithium enhancement in red clump stars (A(Li) > -0.9) can be partially explained by additional energy loss channels from physics beyond the Standard Model, such as a neutrino magnetic moment, which make thermohaline mixing more efficient and produce a heavier helium core; however, the neutrino magnetic moment values needed (μ₁₂ = 1-5) exceed current astrophysical upper limits, so other new physics channels like axions or extra dimensions may be required.

## Method

The authors used the Modules for Experiments in Stellar Astrophysics (MESA, version 10398) to construct one-dimensional low-mass stellar models with initial mass 1 M⊙ and solar metallicity (Z = 0.0148). They included thermohaline mixing with diffusion coefficients α_thm = 50, 100, and 1000, and incorporated additional energy loss from neutrino magnetic moments via plasmon decay and pair production, scanning values μ₁₂ = μ_ν/(10⁻¹² μ_B) from 0 to 5. The evolution of surface lithium abundance A(Li) was tracked from the main sequence through the red giant branch to the red clump phase.

## Key numbers

- Observed red clump stars have lithium abundances distributed around A(Li) = 0.71 ± 0.39, based on GALAH DR2 samples selected by asteroseismology (Kumar 2020).
- In the standard model (μ₁₂ = 0) with α_thm = 100, the predicted red clump lithium abundance is A(Li) = -1.08; with α_thm = 50 it is A(Li) = -0.73.
- With μ₁₂ = 5 and α_thm = 100, the red clump lithium abundance reaches A(Li) = 0.07; with α_thm = 50 it reaches A(Li) = 0.10.
- The helium core mass at the tip of the red giant branch increases from 0.467 M⊙ (μ₁₂ = 0) to 0.509 M⊙ (μ₁₂ = 5) for α_thm = 1000.
- The additional energy loss rate from neutrino magnetic moment at the stellar center just before helium flash is approximately 6 erg g⁻¹ s⁻¹ for μ₁₂ = 5.
- The current best experimental limit on the neutrino magnetic moment is μ_ν < 2.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B from the GEMMA experiment, while astrophysical limits give μ_ν < 1.5 × 10⁻¹² μ_B (Capozzi 2020) and μ_ν < 2.2 × 10⁻¹² μ_B (Arceo-Diaz 2015).

## Context

This work extends the finding by Kumar (2020) that nearly all red clump stars have lithium abundances A(Li) > -0.9, which standard stellar evolution models cannot reproduce. The authors build on previous studies of thermohaline mixing (Charbonnel 2007, Lagarde 2015) and the Cameron-Fowler mechanism (Cameron 1971) for ⁷Be production, and on earlier constraints of neutrino magnetic moment from globular cluster stars (Raffelt 1996, Arceo-Diaz 2015). The results open the possibility that other new physics channels such as axion-like particles (Raffelt 1987, Armengaud 2014) or extra dimensions (Cassisi 2000) could similarly enhance lithium production, and the slow timescale of enhancement (∼1 Myr before the helium flash) distinguishes this mechanism from the rapid helium-flash-induced mixing proposed by Schwab (2020).
