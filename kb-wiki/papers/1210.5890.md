# Effect of three nucleon forces in p-3He scattering

> The effect of the inclusion of different models of three nucleon (3N) forces in p-3He elastic scattering at low energies is studied. Two models have been considered: one derived from effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order and one derived from a more phenomenological point of view -- the so-called Illinois model. The four nucleon scattering observables are calculated using the Kohn variational principle and the hyperspherical harmonic technique and the results are compared with available experimental data. We have found that with the inclusion of both 3N force models the agreement with the experimental data is improved, in particular for the proton vector analyzing power A_y.

# Effect of three nucleon forces in p-3He scattering

## Key claim

Including either the chiral N2LO three-nucleon force (N-N2LO) or the phenomenological Illinois-7 (IL7) three-nucleon force in calculations of p-3He elastic scattering at low energies (E_p ≤ 6 MeV) significantly improves agreement with experimental data for the proton vector analyzing power A_y, reducing the discrepancy to about 10% at the peak, though the improvement for the N-N2LO model is not imposed by fitting to A_y data but emerges from fitting only A=3 and A=4 binding energies.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using the Kohn variational principle with the wave function expanded in hyperspherical harmonic (HH) functions. Two interaction combinations were compared: the Argonne v18 (AV18) NN potential with the Illinois-7 (IL7) 3N force, and the chiral N3LO NN potential by Entem and Machleidt (I-N3LO, cutoff Λ=500 MeV) with the local N2LO 3N force (N-N2LO) whose two free parameters were fixed to reproduce A=3 and A=4 binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts for p-3He elastic scattering at E_p = 5.54 MeV: ¹S_0 = -68.5°, ³S_1 = -60.1°, ³P_0 = 25.1°, ³P_1 = 44.3°, ¹P_1 = 23.0°, ³D_1 = -1.51°, mixing parameter ε(1⁺) = -1.07°, ε(1⁻) = 9.36° (HH method with I-N3LO potential)
- The A_y discrepancy after including 3N forces is reduced to about 10% at the peak, compared to the larger underprediction with NN-only models
- Differential cross sections and analyzing powers were calculated at incident proton energies E_p = 2.25, 4, and 5.54 MeV

## Context

This work extends benchmark calculations of four-nucleon scattering (Viviani et al. 2011) that showed AGS, FY, and HH methods agree to within 1% for p-3He phase shifts, and addresses the long-standing "A_y puzzle" previously observed in N-d scattering (Kievsky 1996) and confirmed in p-3He scattering (Fonseca 1999, Viviani et al. 2001, Fisher et al. 2006). The results enable systematic testing of chiral 3N forces at next order and provide a foundation for understanding spin-orbit interactions in A>2 systems, though the authors note that the same I-N3LO/N-N2LO model does not significantly improve the N-d A_y puzzle (Messer et al. 2009).
