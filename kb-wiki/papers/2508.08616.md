# Impact of shape coexistence on the symmetric to asymmetric fission mode transition in Th isotopes

> We study the evolution of fission modes along the Th isotopic chain using a microscopic framework combining the time-dependent generator coordinate method and finite-temperature covariant density functional theory. Theoretical fission fragment charge distributions agree well with experiments, and reveal a rapid symmetric-to-asymmetric transition from $A=222$ to 234. By analyzing the collective potential energy surfaces and time evolution of collective probability density distributions, we demonstrate that this fission mode transition is strongly correlated with the rapidly deepening asymmetric fission valley $-$ a phenomenon driven by the reduction of deformation energies of both the heavy and light fragments formed in the asymmetric fission valley. Further analysis attributes the decrease of light-fragment deformation energies to the onset of a coexisting large-deformed minimum in neutron-rich Kr and Sr isotopes (dominated isotopes for light asymmetric peak), which arises from a deformed proton $Z=38$ shell closure near $β_2\approx0.46$. Notably, we identify, for the first time, the pivotal role of the light fragment and its shape coexistence structure on the fission mode transition in Th isotopes in a fully microscopic framework.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the rapid symmetric-to-asymmetric fission mode transition in Th isotopes (A=222 to 234) is driven primarily by the reduction of deformation energies of both heavy and light fragments, and identifies for the first time the pivotal role of the light fragment's shape coexistence structure, specifically the onset of a large-deformed minimum in neutron-rich Kr and Sr isotopes, in a fully microscopic framework.

## Method

The authors combine the time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) in the Gaussian overlap approximation with finite-temperature covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the PC-PK1 functional. They construct collective potential energy surfaces in the quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation plane for even-even ²²²⁻²³⁴Th isotopes, compute the mass tensor via the finite-temperature perturbative cranking approximation, and simulate fission dynamics to obtain fission fragment charge distributions (FFCDs). Post-scission fragment properties are analyzed using density-constrained calculations with frozen fragments.

## Key numbers

- The rapid symmetric-to-asymmetric transition occurs from A=222 to 234 in Th isotopes.
- The asymmetric fission valley deepens by approximately 8 MeV for the scission configuration from ²²²Th to ²³⁴Th.
- Deformation energy of the heavy fragment decreases from ~7.4 MeV to ~1 MeV along the isotopic chain.
- Deformation energy of the light fragment decreases from ~6.8 MeV to ~2 MeV along the isotopic chain.
- The deformed proton Z=38 shell closure emerges near β₂ ≈ 0.46 in neutron-rich Sr isotopes.
- Temperatures for ²²²⁻²³⁴Th range from 0.84 to 0.77 MeV, corresponding to an excitation energy of 11 MeV in photon-induced fission experiments.
- The Gaussian convolution width used for charge yields is σ_Z = 2 for all Th isotopes.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of fission dynamics using TDGCM based on covariant DFT, which had reproduced the triple-peak structure of ²²⁶Th (Tao 2017, Zhao 2019) but lacked a systematic study across the Th isotopic chain. It contrasts with macroscopic-microscopic models (Randrup 2011, Pomorski 2021) and scission point models (Pasca 2016) that could not provide a clear microscopic explanation of the symmetric/asymmetric transition. The finding that light-fragment shape coexistence, driven by the deformed Z=38 shell closure, is crucial complements prior work attributing asymmetric fission in actinides primarily to heavy-fragment shell effects (Scamps 2018). This enables future systematic dynamical calculations for Th and other isotopes using Fourier shape parametrization in 3D potential energy surfaces.
