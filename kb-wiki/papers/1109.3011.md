# Influence of the nuclear symmetry energy on the structure and composition of the outer crust

> We review and extend with nonrelativistic nuclear mean field calculations a previous study of the impact of the nuclear symmetry energy on the structure and composition of the outer crust of nonaccreting neutron stars. First, we develop a simple "toy model" to understand the most relevant quantities determining the structure and composition of the outer crust: the nuclear symmetry energy and the pressure of the electron gas. While the latter is a well determined quantity, the former ---specially its density dependence--- still lacks an accurate characterization. We thus focus on the influence of the nuclear symmetry energy on the crustal composition. For that, we employ different nuclear models that are accurate in the description of terrestrial nuclei. We show that those models with stiffer symmetry energies ---namely, those that generate thicker neutron skins in heavy nuclei and have smaller symmetry energies at subnormal nuclear densities--- generate more exotic isotopes in the stellar crust than their softer counterparts.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nuclear models with a stiffer symmetry energy (larger slope parameter \(L\)) produce more neutron-rich (exotic) isotopes in the outer crust of nonaccreting neutron stars, but this occurs because such models have a *smaller* symmetry energy at subnormal nuclear densities, not simply because of the stiffness itself.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic nuclear mean-field framework to compute the composition and equation of state of the outer crust, minimizing the chemical potential at constant pressure. They supplement these calculations with a simple liquid-drop "toy model" to isolate the roles of the electron gas pressure and the nuclear symmetry energy, and they compare results from four mean-field interactions that span a wide range of the symmetry-energy slope parameter \(L\).

## Key numbers

- The neutron-drip density is consistently found to be about \(4\times10^{11}\ \mathrm{g/cm^{3}}\) across all calculations.
- At the outer-inner crust interface, the electronic contribution to the energy per baryon is approximately \(\varepsilon_{e}^{\mathrm{drip}}\approx 30\,y^{4/3}_{\mathrm{drip}}\approx 6.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the drip nucleus \({}^{118}\)Kr.
- The lattice contribution at the drip density is about \(\varepsilon_{\ell}^{\mathrm{drip}}\approx -0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), an order of magnitude smaller than the electronic term.
- The toy model first-order solution gives the proton fraction as \(y(p_{\mathrm{F}}) = 0.45405 - 0.00419\,p_{\mathrm{F}}\) (with \(p_{\mathrm{F}}\) in MeV), showing a linear decrease with density.
- The neutron skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb predicted by the set of mean-field models ranges from about 0 fm to about 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Roca-Maza (2008) on the impact of the symmetry energy on the outer crust, and it complements the comprehensive survey by Ruester, Hempel, and Schaffner-Bielich (2005). The results connect to the Parity Radius Experiment (PREx) at Jefferson Laboratory, which provides a model-independent measurement of the neutron skin of \({}^{208}\)Pb, and to observations of high-frequency oscillations in giant flares from magnetars, which are sensitive to crustal composition and the symmetry energy.
