# Proton Spin Based On Chiral Dynamics

> Chiral spin fraction models agree with the proton spin data only when the chiral quark-Goldstone boson couplings are pure spinflip. For axial-vector coupling from soft-pion physics this is true for massless quarks but not for constituent quarks. Axial-vector quark-Goldstone boson couplings with {\bf constituent} quarks are found to be inconsistent with the proton spin data.

# Proton Spin Based On Chiral Dynamics

## Key claim

Chiral spin fraction models reproduce the proton spin data only when the axial-vector quark-Goldstone boson couplings are pure spinflip, which holds for massless quarks and at high momentum for light quarks, but fails when constituent quark masses are used because the non-spinflip contributions from quark mass terms dominate at low momentum.

## Method

The author analyzes chiral field theory applied to deep inelastic scattering, using the effective interaction between quarks and the octet of Goldstone boson fields with axial-vector coupling. The polarized and unpolarized chiral splitting functions are derived from the axial-vector interaction, and the quark spin fractions are obtained by integrating the polarized quark distributions over Bjorken x, with chiral fluctuations treated perturbatively as convolutions of splitting functions with valence quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration scale is approximately 1.17 GeV, derived from the pion decay constant f_pi = 0.093 GeV.
- For the chiral strength parameter a = 0.12 and SU(3) breaking parameter epsilon = 0.2, the model yields Delta u = 0.83 (without eta') and 0.81 (with eta' at zeta = -0.3), compared to the E143 data value of 0.84 +/- 0.05 at 3 GeV^2 and SMC value of 0.82 +/- 0.02 at 5 GeV^2.
- The model gives Delta d = -0.39 (both with and without eta'), compared to the data value of -0.43 +/- 0.05 (E143) and -0.43 +/- 0.02 (SMC).
- Delta s is predicted as -0.07 (both cases), compared to data of -0.08 +/- 0.05 (E143) and -0.10 +/- 0.02 (SMC).
- The total spin Delta Sigma is 0.36 (without eta') and 0.35 (with eta'), compared to data of 0.30 +/- 0.06 (E143) and 0.29 +/- 0.06 (SMC).
- The axial-vector coupling g_A^(3) is predicted as 1.22 (without eta') and 1.21 (with eta'), compared to the PDG value of 1.2573 +/- 0.0028.
- The ratio F/D is 0.58 in both model cases, compared to the data value of 0.575 +/- 0.016.
- The Gottfried sum rule value I_G is 0.27 (without eta') and 0.25 (with eta'), compared to the data value of 0.235 +/- 0.026.

## Context

This work extends the chiral spin fraction models developed by Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1990s) and by Schreiber, Miller, and collaborators, which showed that chiral dynamics reduces the proton spin fractions carried by valence quarks and the axial-vector coupling constant. The paper contradicts the naive application of constituent quark masses in axial-vector couplings, as done in the chiral quark model of Georgi and Manohar, and shows that such models are ruled out by the proton spin data. The results enable a clearer distinction between current quark and constituent quark descriptions in chiral perturbation theory, and suggest that the successful description of proton spin data by instanton-based models (Diakonov and collaborators) is consistent because the instanton-quark interaction is also pure spinflip.
