# A Light Front Treatment of the Nucleus-Implications for Deep Inelastic Scattering

> A light front treatment of the nuclear wave function is developed and applied, using the mean field approximation, to infinite nuclear matter. The nuclear mesons are shown to carry about a third of the nuclear plus momentum, p+; but their momentum distribution has support only at p+ =0, and the mesons do not contribute to nuclear deep inelastic scattering. This zero mode effect occurs because the meson fields are independent of space-time position.

# A Light Front Treatment of the Nucleus-Implications for Deep Inelastic Scattering

## Key claim

Nuclear mesons in the mean field approximation carry about one third of the nuclear plus momentum, but because the meson fields are constant in space-time (zero modes), their momentum distribution has support only at p+ = 0, so they do not contribute to deep inelastic scattering. This means a model can have a large binding effect with nucleons carrying significantly less than unity of the plus momentum without requiring meson contributions that could be ruled out in a Drell-Yan experiment.

## Method

The author develops a light front quantization treatment of infinite nuclear matter using a renormalizable Lagrangian with nucleons, scalar mesons, and vector mesons, evaluated in the mean field approximation. The field equations are solved for a normal Fermi gas ground state with equal numbers of neutrons and protons, and the plus momentum distribution of nucleons is computed from the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor. The nuclear deep inelastic structure function is then obtained via the convolution model relating the nucleon momentum distribution f(y) to the nucleon structure function.

## Key numbers

- Nucleons carry only 65% of the nuclear plus momentum; mesons carry the remaining 35%
- The effective nucleon mass in the mean field is M + g_s φ = 0.56 M (where M is the bare nucleon mass)
- The binding energy per particle of nuclear matter is 15.75 MeV with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}
- The coupling parameters used are g_v^2 M^2 / m_v^2 = 195.9 and g_s^2 M^2 / m_s^2 = 267.1
- The average value of the dimensionless variable y = k^+ / M_bar is 0.65, compared to 0.91 computed for nuclear matter using equal time quantization including correlations (Dieperink and Miller, 1991)

## Context

This work extends the light front quantization approach to nuclear matter initiated by Glazek and Shakin (1991) by including both scalar and vector mesons, and addresses the EMC effect discovered by Aubert et al. (1982) and collaborators. The result challenges the conventional interpretation of the momentum sum rule used by Ericson and Thomas (1983) and others, where pions were expected to carry missing momentum and produce observable antiquark enhancement in Drell-Yan experiments; the null result of the nuclear Drell-Yan experiment by Alde et al. (1990) led Bertsch, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1993) to question the role of pions in nuclear binding. The present work shows that mesons in a zero mode can carry momentum without contributing to deep inelastic scattering, vitiating the predictive power of the momentum sum rule for such mean-field models, and calls for future work including chiral symmetry, correlations, and finite nuclei before comparison with data.
