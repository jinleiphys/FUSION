# Investigating the role of partonic and hadronic dynamics in mass splitting of elliptic anisotropy in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV

> The mass ordering of $v_{2}^{hadron}$ is regarded as one of the key signatures of collective behaviour in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions. This observation has been found to be in compliance with the hydrodynamical response of a strongly interacting system to the initial spatial anisotropy. Flow co-efficients measured with identified particles in p-Pb/d-Au collisions have shown similar mass-splitting of $v_{2}^{hadron}$ indicating towards the presence of collective dynamics in small collision systems. Arguably, small size in the overlap geometry of such colliding systems may not be suitable for hydrodynamical treatment that demands an early thermalization. Studies based on a multi phase transport model suggests that elliptic or triangular anisotropy is primarily due to escape mechanism of partons rather than hydro like collectivity and mass ordering of $v_{2}^{hadron}$ can be generated from coalescence dynamics as implemented in string melting version of AMPT even when parton azimuthal directions are randomized. In this work, studies have been performed on p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV using AMPT model which has been found to explain the elliptic and traingular flow in such a system where escape mechanism is the dominant source of flow generation. We report that the mass splitting of $v_{2}^{hadron}$ can originate independently both at the partonic and hadronic level in the string melting version of the AMPT model.

## Key claim

The mass splitting of hadron elliptic flow ($v_{2}^{hadron}$) in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV can originate independently from both partonic dynamics (Zhang's Parton Cascade combined with quark coalescence) and hadronic dynamics (hadronic rescattering) in the string melting version of the AMPT model, and is therefore not uniquely associated with hydrodynamical evolution of a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model in its string melting version to simulate p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. Events were generated with parton scattering cross-sections of 0 mb and 3 mb, and the elliptic flow $v_2$ was extracted using the Q-cumulant method with a pseudorapidity gap of $|\Delta\eta| > 0.4$ for freezeout partons and final-state hadrons (pions and protons). The analysis was performed across four multiplicity classes (60-100%, 40-60%, 20-40%, 0-20%) based on charged particle multiplicity in the ALICE VZERO-A acceptance range ($2.8 < \eta < 5.1$), with and without hadronic rescattering (ART) to isolate partonic and hadronic contributions.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross-sections used: 0 mb (ZPC off) and 3 mb (ZPC on), derived from leading-order pQCD with $\alpha_s$ and Debye screening mass $\mu$.
- Fragmentation parameters in default AMPT: $a = 0.5$ and $b = 0.9$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- Pseudorapidity range for analysis: $|\eta| < 1.0$ for freezeout partons and final-state hadrons, with sub-event separation $|\Delta\eta| > 0.4$.
- Multiplicity classes defined by charged particles with $p_T > 0.05$ GeV/c within $2.8 < \eta < 5.1$.
- In the string melting version with 3 mb cross-section and no hadronic scattering, mass splitting of $v_2^{hadron}$ is observed even in the lowest multiplicity class (60-100%).
- In the string melting version with 0 mb cross-section and hadronic scattering on, mass splitting of $v_2^{hadron}$ is observed in the highest multiplicity class (0-20%) but not in the lowest (60-100%).

## Context

This work extends studies using the AMPT model that showed elliptic anisotropy in small systems arises primarily from the escape mechanism of partons rather than hydro-like collectivity (AMPT_escape_mech, 2019) and that mass splitting can be generated by coalescence dynamics even with randomized parton azimuthal directions (AMPT_mass_splitting, 2021). It contradicts the interpretation that mass ordering of $v_2$ is a unique signature of hydrodynamic collective flow, as previously assumed from measurements in p-Pb collisions by ALICE (alice_pPb_double_ridge, 2013; pPb_mass_ordering, 2014). The findings enable further investigation into whether mass splitting in small systems can be disentangled from genuine collective behavior, and suggest that both partonic and hadronic phases contribute independently to this observable.
