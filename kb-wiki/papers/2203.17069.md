# Analytic Map of Three-Channel S Matrix -Generalized Uniformization and Mittag-Leffler Expansion-

> We explore the analytic structure of the three-channel $S$ matrix by generalizing uniformization and making a single-valued map for the three-channel $S$ matrix. First, by means of the inverse Jacobi's elliptic function we construct a transformation from eight Riemann sheets of the center-of-mass energy squared complex plane onto a torus, on which the three-channel $S$ matrix is represented single-valued. Secondly, we show that the Mittag-Leffler expansion, a pole expansion, of the three-channel scattering amplitude includes not only topologically trivial but also nontrivial contributions and is given by the Weierstrass zeta function. Finally, we examine the obtained formula in the context of a simple three-channel model. Taking a simple non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interaction for the $S=-2$, $I=0$, $J^P = 0^+$, $ΛΛ-NΞ-ΣΣ$ coupled-channel scattering, we demonstrate that the scattering amplitude as a function of the uniformization variable is, in fact, given by the Mittag-Leffler expansion with the Weierstrass zeta function and that it is dominated by contributions from neighboring poles.

## Key claim

The paper constructs, for the first time, an explicit uniformization variable for the three-channel S matrix using the inverse Jacobi elliptic function, and derives the corresponding Mittag-Leffler expansion in terms of the Weierstrass zeta function; the authors demonstrate the validity of this formal result only in a simple non-relativistic effective field theory model with contact interaction, not in a realistic physical system.

## Method

The authors first map the eight Riemann sheets of the center-of-mass energy squared complex plane onto a torus by a two-step uniformization: they uniformize channels 1 and 2 using the standard two-channel variable \(z_{12} = (q_1 + q_2)/\Delta_{12}\), then uniformize channel 3 via the inverse Jacobi elliptic function \(\mathrm{sn}^{-1}\) with modulus \(k = 1/\gamma^2\), where \(\gamma = (\Delta_{13} + \Delta_{23})/\Delta_{12}\). They then express the three-channel scattering amplitude as a meromorphic function on the torus and apply the Mittag-Leffler theorem, obtaining an expansion in terms of the Weierstrass zeta function \(\zeta(z)\). The formalism is tested on a non-relativistic effective field theory for the \(S=-2\), \(I=0\), \(J^P = 0^+\), \(\Lambda\Lambda\)-\(N\Xi\)-\(\Sigma\Sigma\) coupled-channel system with a contact interaction.

## Key numbers

* Threshold energies used in the model: \(\varepsilon_1 = 2.231\) GeV (\(\Lambda\Lambda\)), \(\varepsilon_2 = 2.257\) GeV (\(N\Xi\)), \(\varepsilon_3 = 2.381\) GeV (\(\Sigma\Sigma\)).
* For coupling \(C = 40.00\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (case a), pole 1 is at \(z = -0.267i\) with residue \(0.172i\), located on the \([ttt]\) sheet at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.221\) GeV.
* For coupling \(C = 45.60\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (case b), pole 1 is at \(z = -0.371i\) with residue \(1.750i\), located on the \([btt]\) sheet at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.231\) GeV.
* For coupling \(C = 60.00\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (case c), pole 1 is at \(z = 0.177 - 0.392i\) with residue \(-0.215 + 0.018i\), located on the \([btt]\) sheet at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.253 - 0.005i\) GeV.
* For coupling \(C = 80.00\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (case d), pole 1 is at \(z = 0.271 - 0.402i\) with residue \(-0.249 + 0.028i\), located on the \([tbt]\) sheet at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.259 + 0.002i\) GeV.
* The periods of the torus are \(2\omega_1 = 4K(1/\gamma^2)\) and \(2\omega_2 = 2iK'(1/\gamma^2)\), where \(K\) and \(K'\) are complete elliptic integrals.

## Context

This work extends the uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion method, previously developed for single-channel scattering (Humblet, 1961; Ramirez Jimenez, 2018) and two-channel scattering (Kato, 1965; PhysRevC.102.055201; Yamada, 2021; PhysRevD.105.014034), to the three-channel case. Prior attempts at three-channel analysis used only approximated local uniformization (Krupa, 1995; Surovtsev, 2011; PhysRevA.8.754). The explicit uniformization variable and Mittag-Leffler expansion on the torus enable model-independent extraction of resonance information from three-channel scattering data, with planned applications to experimental data from LHCb (2015, 2019) and Belle (2018).
