# A dispersive approach to two-photon exchange in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We examine the two-photon exchange corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering within a dispersive approach, including contributions from both Nucleon and Delta intermediate states. The dispersive analysis avoids off-shell uncertainties inherent in traditional approaches based on direct evaluation of loop diagrams, and guarantees the correct unitary behavior in the high energy limit. Using empirical information on the electromagnetic nucleon elastic and N-Delta transition form factors, we compute the two-photon exchange corrections both algebraically and numerically. Results are compared with recent measurements of e+p to e-p cross section ratios from the CLAS, VEPP-3 and OLYMPUS experiments, as well as with polarization transfer observables.

## Key claim

The dispersive approach to two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections avoids off-shell uncertainties inherent in traditional loop-diagram calculations, particularly for the Δ resonance intermediate state where off-shell dependence leads to divergences in the forward-angle (high energy) limit and signals a violation of unitarity; the method guarantees correct unitary behavior by using exclusively on-shell transition form factors.

## Method

The authors compute the imaginary parts of the TPE amplitude analytically using Passarino-Veltman scalar functions (B₀, C₀, D₀) with monopole form factor parametrizations, then construct the real parts via fixed-t dispersion relations (principal value integrals over ν). For the nucleon and Δ intermediate states, they evaluate the dispersion integrals numerically using empirical electromagnetic form factor data, comparing results to both the traditional loop-calculation method and experimental data from CLAS, VEPP-3, and OLYMPUS.

## Key numbers

- The TPE correction δ_γγ is defined as the relative correction to the Born cross section: σ_R = σ_R^Born (1 + δ_γγ)
- The infrared subtraction follows the Maximon-Tjon prescription: Δ_IR(MTj) = -(α/π) log((M²-s)/(M²-u)) log(Q²/λ²)
- The threshold for the elastic cut is ν_th = -τ, corresponding to electron energy E = 0
- For nucleon intermediate states, the polynomial degree N = 2 in the primitive integrals I_ij, indicating monopole form factors suffice to eliminate UV divergences

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to TPE developed at forward angles by Gorchtein (2006) and at non-forward angles by Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2008-2015) and Tomalak and Vanderhaeghen (2014), generalizing beyond monopole form factor parametrizations to allow more general classes of transition form factors. The method addresses limitations of earlier loop calculations by Blunden (2003) and Kondratyuk (2005) that required half off-shell form factors and produced unreliable results at forward angles; it enables meaningful comparison with high-precision e⁺p to e⁻p cross section ratio measurements from CLAS, VEPP-3, and OLYMPUS experiments over the full range of currently accessible kinematics.
