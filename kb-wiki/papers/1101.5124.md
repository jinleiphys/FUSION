# Origin of the anomalous long lifetime of 14C

> We report the microscopic origins of the anomalously suppressed beta decay of 14C to 14N using the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with the Hamiltonian from chiral effective field theory (EFT) including three-nucleon force (3NF) terms. The 3NF induces unexpectedly large cancellations within the p-shell between contributions to beta decay, which reduce the traditionally large contributions from the NN interactions by an order of magnitude, leading to the long lifetime of 14C.

## Key claim

The anomalously long 5730-year half-life of 14C arises because the three-nucleon force (3NF) from chiral effective field theory induces unexpectedly large cancellations within the p-shell that reduce the traditionally large contributions from nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions by an order of magnitude; however, the precise lifetime can only be reproduced by fine-tuning the 3NF coupling constant c_D to a value (c_D ≈ -2.0) that differs from the value preferred by the triton half-life (c_D ≈ -0.2).

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 14C and 14N using a Hamiltonian from chiral effective field theory (chiral EFT) at N3LO for the NN interaction plus a chiral 3NF, with the 3NF coupling constants c_D and c_E fit to A=3 binding energies. They solved the many-body Schrödinger equation in a harmonic oscillator basis with Nmax=8 (up to 872 million M-scheme basis states for 14C and 1.09 billion for 14N) at ℏΩ=14 MeV, computing the Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix element M_GT for the beta decay of 14C to 14N and decomposing it into contributions from individual harmonic oscillator shells and LS-scheme components.

## Key numbers

- Measured half-life of 14C: 5730 ± 30 years
- The GT matrix element must be |M_GT^M| ≈ 2 × 10^(-3) to reproduce the measured half-life, compared to conventional strong GT transitions with |M_GT| ≈ 1
- Without the 3NF, binding energies at Nmax=8 are (97.20, 96.36) MeV for (14C, 14N); with the 3NF at c_D=-0.2 they increase to (108.43, 108.47) MeV, and at c_D=-2.0 to (108.30, 108.41) MeV (experimental values: 105.28, 104.66 MeV)
- The p-shell contribution to M_GT is reduced from 0.292 (NN only) to -0.019 (c_D=-0.2) and 0.024 (c_D=-2.0) when the 3NF is included
- Total M_GT at Nmax=8: 0.275 (NN only), -0.063 (c_D=-0.2), -0.013 (c_D=-2.0)
- Beta endpoint energy E_0 = 156 keV
- Basis space dimensions: 872,999,912 for 14C and 1,090,393,922 for 14N at Nmax=8; non-vanishing 3NF matrix elements ≈ 2.9 × 10^13 (14C) and ≈ 3.9 × 10^13 (14N)
- B(M1) transition in 14N: 1.002 (NN only), 0.037 (c_D=-0.2), 0.098 (c_D=-2.0), compared to experimental 0.047(2)

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM calculations for A=10-13 nuclei by Navratil et al. (2007) using the same chiral NN+3NF Hamiltonian, and provides a microscopic explanation for the anomalously suppressed beta decay that traditional realistic NN interactions alone could not produce (Aroua et al., 2004). The results contradict the earlier explanation by Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2009) who used a density-dependent effective NN interaction and found the c_E term played a leading role, whereas the present work finds comparable sensitivity to c_D and c_E; the authors also note that contributions from shells beyond the p-shell, absent in that earlier work, overwhelm the net p-shell contribution. The findings enable future studies of GT transitions to excited A=14 states (Negret et al., 2007) and suggest that higher-order effects (meson-exchange currents, additional chiral EFT terms, larger basis spaces) may be absorbed into an allowed choice of the 3NF coupling constants.
