# Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass term for the Kratzer potential

> The Deformation Dependent Mass (DDM) Kratzer model is constructed by considering the Kratzer potential in a Bohr Hamiltonian, in which the mass is allowed to depend on the nuclear deformation, and solving it by using techniques of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM), involving a deformed shape invariance condition. Analytical expressions for spectra and wave functions are derived for separable potentials in the cases of gamma-unstable nuclei, axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, and triaxial nuclei, implementing the usual approximations in each case. Spectra and B(E2) transition rates are compared to experimental data. The dependence of the mass on the deformation, dictated by SUSYQM for the potential used, moderates the increase of the moment of inertia with deformation, removing a main drawback of the model.

## Key claim

The deformation-dependent mass (DDM) Bohr Hamiltonian with the Kratzer potential yields analytical solutions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates, and the dependence of the mass on deformation (dictated by supersymmetric quantum mechanics for this potential) moderates the increase of the moment of inertia with deformation, removing a main drawback of the constant-mass Bohr model. The Kratzer potential specifically avoids the overestimation of level spacings within the beta_1 band that plagued the DDM Davidson model, and can describe N=90 isotones at the X(5) critical point where the Davidson potential fails.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bohr Hamiltonian in which the nuclear mass depends on the deformation coordinate beta via B(beta)=B0/(f(beta))^2, with f(beta)=1+a beta for the Kratzer potential u(beta)=-1/beta + tilde-B/beta^2. They solved the radial equation using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) techniques involving a deformed shape invariance condition, obtaining analytical expressions for spectra and wave functions for three separable cases: gamma-unstable nuclei, axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, and triaxial nuclei with gamma approximately pi/6. Spectra and B(E2) transition rates were fitted to experimental data for over 100 nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For gamma-unstable Xe isotopes, the Kratzer parameter tilde-B increases smoothly from near the N=82 shell closure to midshell, while the deformation-dependence parameter a is zero or near zero for the first 4 isotopes close to the border and acquires substantially non-zero values for the last 5 isotopes near midshell
- In the limit a->0 (no deformation dependence of mass), the spectrum for gamma-unstable nuclei becomes epsilon_n = -1/[2(n+1/2+sqrt((tau+3/2)^2+2 tilde-B))^2], in agreement with Eq. (11) of Fortunato and Vitturi (2003)
- For axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei in the a->0 limit, the energies read epsilon_n = -1/[2(n+1/2+sqrt(9/4+L(L+1)/3+2 tilde-B))^2], in agreement with Eq. (10) of Fortunato and Vitturi (2004)
- The quality of fits was measured by sigma = sqrt(sum_i (E_i(exp)-E_i(th))^2 / [(n-1)E(2_1^+)^2])

## Context

This work extends the deformation-dependent mass (DDM) approach previously applied to the Davidson potential by Bonatsos et al. (2011) to the Kratzer potential, which was first used for nuclear descriptions by Fortunato and Vitturi (2003, 2004). The Kratzer potential differs from the Davidson potential in having a tail that goes to zero at large beta (rather than rising to infinity), which avoids the overestimation of beta_1 band level spacings. The model also succeeds in describing N=90 isotones (150Nd, 152Sm, 154Gd, 156Dy) that are considered hallmark examples of the X(5) critical point symmetry of Iachello (2001), where the DDM Davidson model failed. The results enable further investigation of the physical content of the deformation-dependence parameter a through equivalence to curved space or connections to the interacting boson model.
