# Establishing mass spectrum of $S=-1$ hyperon resonances via a dynamical coupled-channels analysis of $K^-p$ reactions

> We report our recent effort for the extraction of resonance parameters (complex pole mass and residues etc.) associated with Lambda* and Sigma* hyperons. This was accomplished via a comprehensive partial-wave analysis of the data for K^- p --> barK N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda, eta Lambda, K Xi reactions from the thresholds up to W=2.1 GeV within a dynamical coupled-channels approach. The results suggest a possible existence of new narrow J^P=3/2^+ Λresonance with pole mass 1671^{+2}_{-8} -i(5^{+11}_{-2}) MeV, located close to the eta Lambda threshold. This resonance is found to be responsible for reproducing the data for K^-p --> eta Lambda differential cross sections near the threshold, and thus the data seem favor its existence. The extracted poles for J^P=1/2^- Lambda resonances below the barK N threshold, including Lambda(1405), are also presented.

# Establishing mass spectrum of $S=-1$ hyperon resonances via a dynamical coupled-channels analysis of $K^-p$ reactions

## Key claim
A dynamical coupled-channels analysis of $K^-p$ reaction data suggests the possible existence of a new narrow $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda$ resonance with pole mass $1671^{+2}_{-8} -i(5^{+11}_{-2})$ MeV, located just above the $\eta \Lambda$ threshold. However, this resonance appears in only one of two equally good solutions (Model B), and the analysis dependence cannot be resolved without new data.

## Method
A dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model was developed based on multichannel scattering equations for partial-wave amplitudes, with transition potentials consisting of hadron-exchange terms and s-channel bare hyperon propagators. The model parameters were determined by fitting over 17,000 data points for $K^- p \to \bar K N, \pi \Sigma, \pi \Lambda, \eta \Lambda, K\Xi$ reactions from threshold up to center-of-mass energy $W=2.1$ GeV, including both unpolarized and polarized observables. The channel space included two-body $\bar K N$, $\pi \Sigma$, $\pi \Lambda$, $\eta \Lambda$, and $K\Xi$ states, plus three-body $\pi\pi\Lambda$ and $\pi\bar K N$ states with resonant $\pi\Sigma^*$ and $\bar K^* N$ components.

## Key numbers
- Pole mass of the new candidate $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda$ resonance: $1671^{+2}_{-8} -i(5^{+11}_{-2})$ MeV, located just above the $\eta \Lambda$ threshold
- Energy range of the analysis: from reaction thresholds up to $W=2.1$ GeV
- More than 17,000 data points were fitted from $K^- p \to \bar K N, \pi \Sigma, \pi \Lambda, \eta \Lambda, K\Xi$ reactions
- Two distinct solutions (Model A and Model B) were found, both giving almost the same $\chi^2$ values
- For $J^P=1/2^-$ $\Lambda$ resonances below the $\bar K N$ threshold, both models yield two resonance poles, with the higher-mass poles (A1 and B1) corresponding to $\Lambda(1405)$

## Context
This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach previously applied to $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ resonances into the strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon sector, providing the first comprehensive extraction of $Y^*$ resonance parameters defined by poles of scattering amplitudes within a dynamical-model framework. The results are compared with the KSU analysis by Zhang et al. (2013) and with Breit-Wigner parameters from the Particle Data Group (2014). The analysis reveals a possible new $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda$ resonance near the $\eta \Lambda$ threshold and predicts two $J^P=1/2^-$ $\Lambda$ poles below the $\bar K N$ threshold, consistent with chiral unitary model predictions (e.g., Ikeda et al., 2011). Future work aims to extend the analysis to the $\bar K N$ subthreshold region via $K^- d \to \pi Y N$ reactions being measured at J-PARC E31, and to provide elementary amplitudes for hypernuclei and kaonic-nuclei production studies.
