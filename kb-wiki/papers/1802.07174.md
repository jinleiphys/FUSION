# Soft-photon corrections to the Bethe-Heitler process in the $γp\rightarrow l^+l^-p$ reaction

> We report on the calculation of first-order QED corrections for the $γp\rightarrow l^+l^-p^{\prime}$ process. An upcoming experiment at MAMI (Mainz) aims to compare the cross sections of muon- and electron-pair production in this reaction to test lepton universality. Precise knowledge of the electromagnetic radiative corrections is needed for these measurements. As a first step, we present the leading QED radiative corrections in the soft-photon approximation when accounting for the finite lepton mass. For the kinematics at MAMI, we find corrections of the percent level for muons, and of order $10\%$ for electrons.

# Soft-photon corrections to the Bethe-Heitler process in the γp → l⁺l⁻p reaction

## Key claim

The first-order QED radiative corrections in the soft-photon approximation shift the ratio of muon- and electron-pair photoproduction cross sections by more than the 3σ band needed to test lepton universality, making their inclusion indispensable; however, the calculation is limited to the soft-photon approximation and does not include full one-loop corrections or two-photon exchange contributions from the hadronic side.

## Method

The authors computed the first-order QED corrections to the Bethe-Heitler process γp → l⁺l⁻p in the soft-photon limit, where loop momenta and real photon momenta are taken small compared to external scales. They evaluated the virtual corrections from box diagrams and counterterm diagrams in the on-shell subtraction scheme, and the real corrections from soft-photon bremsstrahlung integrated up to a maximum soft-photon energy ΔE_s determined by experimental angular resolution. The infrared divergences cancel between real and virtual contributions, and the resulting correction factorizes in terms of the tree-level cross section, with exponentiation applied for higher-order soft-photon effects following Yennie (1961).

## Key numbers

- For kinematics at MAMI, radiative corrections are of order 10% for electrons and of order 1% for muons (percent level for muons).
- At fixed s_ll = 0.077 GeV² with ΔE_s = 0.01 GeV, the first-order correction δ differs from the exponentiated form by about 0.006 for electron-pair production and by about -1.0×10⁻⁴ for muon-pair production.
- The normalization factor F deviates from unity by approximately -2.4×10⁻³ for electrons and -8.5×10⁻⁶ for muons at s_ll = 0.077 GeV².
- The muon-pair production threshold is at s_ll = 4m_μ² ≈ 0.045 GeV².
- The experimental resolution needed for a 3σ test of lepton universality is σ = 7×10⁻⁴, as proposed by Pauk (2015).

## Context

This work extends the tree-level calculation of lepton-pair photoproduction by Pauk (2015), who proposed the ratio R of muon- and electron-pair cross sections as a test of lepton universality and found that a measurement with absolute precision of 7×10⁻⁴ could test lepton universality at 3σ significance. The present calculation provides the first step toward the full QED radiative corrections needed for the upcoming MAMI experiment (Mainz). The authors plan to extend this soft-photon approximation to a full one-loop QED calculation on the lepton side and to include two-photon exchange box diagrams between lepton and proton using techniques developed by Tomalak (2014) for elastic l⁻p scattering, which are expected to give per-mille-level corrections to the ratio.
