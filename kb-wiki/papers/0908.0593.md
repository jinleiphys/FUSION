# Algebraic Density Functionals

> A systematic strategy for the calculation of density functionals (DFs) consists in coding informations about the density and the energy into polynomials of the degrees of freedom of wave functions. DFs and Kohn-Sham potentials (KSPs) are then obtained by standard elimination procedures of such degrees of freedom between the polynomials. Numerical examples illustrate the formalism.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that density functionals (DFs) and Kohn-Sham potentials (KSPs) can be constructed systematically by encoding both density and energy information into polynomials of wave-function parameters, then eliminating those parameters via standard polynomial manipulations. The authors hedge that this algebraic approach is demonstrated only for toy models with small configuration spaces, and that practical application to realistic systems remains a future goal.

## Method

The authors represent the energy and density constraints as polynomial functions of the coefficients of configuration-mixing wave functions (or orbital coefficients for Slater determinants). They then eliminate the wave-function parameters using polynomial elimination techniques (resultants, Legendre transforms via determinants of constrained Hamiltonians) to obtain a polynomial relation between the energy and a finite set of density parameters. The method is illustrated with two toy models: a 4×4 configuration-mixing problem for two fermions in a harmonic oscillator basis with a separable interaction, and a Slater-determinant model using two orbitals built from the same basis.

## Key numbers

- For the configuration-mixing toy model with interaction strength V₀=3, the ground state energy is η=-1 and the second moment constraint D=2.
- The highest and lowest eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian matrix are 45/8 (≈5.625) and -1; those of the constraint matrix D are approximately 6.81 and 1.19.
- For the Slater-determinant toy model with V₀=3, the lowest root of the final polynomial for η is -2.98623, but this is identified as spurious; the true ground state energy is η=-1.
- The optimal density for the Slater model is ρ=(1+2r²)e^{-r²}/√π, with coefficients a₀=1, a₂=2, a₄=0, a₆=0.
- The allowed domain for the density parameter a₆ is 0 ≤ a₆ ≤ 4/3, and for a₀ is 0 ≤ 2a₀ ≤ 3.

## Context

This work extends the radial density functional (RDF) theory of Giraud (2005) and the constrained-search formulation of Levy and Lieb (1970s) by providing a constructive algebraic alternative to the Kohn-Sham method. It contrasts with local and quasi-local approximations that treat the continuous infinity of density values as parameters, instead using a finite number of density parameters (moments or expansion coefficients). The approach enables systematic calculation of correlation energies and Kohn-Sham potentials within finite subspaces, and the authors state that a forthcoming paper will address more realistic problems beyond these toy models.
