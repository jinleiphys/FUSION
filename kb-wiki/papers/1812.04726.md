# Polarisabilities from Compton Scattering on 3He

> This executive summary of recent theory progress in Compton scattering off 3He focuses on determining neutron polarisabilities; see ref. [2] and references therein for details and a better bibliography. Prepared for the Proceedings of the 22nd International Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics, Caen 9-13 July 2018.

## Key claim

Compton scattering on 3He at photon lab energies between 50 and 120 MeV can extract neutron polarisabilities, but only if the full chiral effective field theory treatment including two-body currents is used; an impulse approximation that treats 3He as a simple sum of two protons and one neutron severely distorts the extraction because charged pion-exchange currents interfere strongly with the neutron-structure effects.

## Method

The authors extended chiral effective field theory (ChiEFT) predictions for elastic Compton scattering on 3He to N3LO [order e^2 delta^3] by adding a dynamical Delta(1232) degree of freedom. They evaluated the complete photonuclear operator including Thomson terms, magnetic-moment couplings, virtual pion loops, Delta excitation, and couplings to charged-pion exchange, using 3He wave functions from the same ChiEFT expansion. They computed the differential cross section, beam asymmetry Sigma_3, and two double asymmetries with circularly polarised photons and transversely or longitudinally polarised targets (Sigma_2x and Sigma_2z) for photon lab energies between 50 and 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Photon lab energy range studied: 50 to 120 MeV
- The differential cross section between 50 and 120 MeV is sensitive to the neutron electric and magnetic dipole polarisabilities (alpha_E1^(n) and beta_M1^(n))
- Dynamical Delta effects become obvious in all observables for omega_lab greater than approximately 100 MeV
- The double asymmetries are 10 to 20 times more sensitive to the spin polarisabilities of the neutron than of the proton
- The beam asymmetry Sigma_3 is dominated by the single-nucleon Thomson term and not very useful for directly determining polarisabilities

## Context

This work extends earlier ChiEFT predictions by Shukla et al. (2007) which were carried out at lower order and contained mistakes in the computer code, triggering an erratum to that paper. The findings echo similar results for deuteron Compton scattering where Delta effects invert the fore-aft asymmetry. The calculations are part of an ongoing dialogue with experimental colleagues at MAMI and HIgammaS facilities, where approved proposals aim to extract neutron polarisabilities from elastic gamma-3He scattering. The authors note that varying the single-nucleon amplitudes of complementary approaches like dispersion relations leads to sensitivities hardly discernible from theirs, and that a full N4LO calculation is under way to enable even more accurate extractions from upcoming data.
