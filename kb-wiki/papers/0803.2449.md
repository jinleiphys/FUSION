# Transverse Velocity Dependence of the Proton-Antiproton Ratio as a Signature of the QCD Critical Endpoint

> The presence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram can deform the trajectories describing the evolution of the expanding fireball in the $μ_B-T$ phase diagram. If the average emission time of hadrons is a function of transverse velocity, as microscopic simulations of the hadronic freeze-out dynamics suggest, the deformation of the hydrodynamic trajectories will change the transverse velocity ($β_{\rm T}$) dependence of the proton-antiproton ratio when the fireball passes in the vicinity of the critical point. An unusual $β_{\rm T}$-dependence of the $\bar{p}/p$ ratio in a narrow beam energy window would thus signal the presence of the critical point.

# Transverse Velocity Dependence of the Proton-Antiproton Ratio as a Signature of the QCD Critical Endpoint

## Key claim
If the QCD critical point exists and the fireball passes near it during heavy-ion collisions, the transverse velocity dependence of the antiproton-to-proton ratio will show an anomalous decrease with increasing transverse velocity, rather than the normal increase or flat behavior, but only in a narrow beam energy window where the fireball trajectory is deformed by the critical point's attractive basin.

## Method
The authors constructed a model of the QCD phase diagram with a critical point at (μ_c, T_c) = (550, 159) MeV by interpolating between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma entropy densities using a function proportional to the critical part of the entropy density from the 3-dimensional Ising model. They computed hydrodynamic trajectories in the μ_B-T plane with and without the critical point, then used the UrQMD microscopic transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 40 GeV/A and extract emission time distributions for protons and antiprotons in two transverse velocity windows: β_T < 0.25 (slow) and 0.5 < β_T < 0.75 (fast).

## Key numbers
- Critical point location: (μ_c, T_c) = (550, 159) MeV
- Critical region parameters: ΔT_crit = 20 MeV, Δμ_crit = 100 MeV, D = 0.5
- Chemical freeze-out point from experimental data: (μ_ch, T_ch) ≈ (400, 145) MeV
- Average emission time for fast protons: 19.5 ± 0.02 fm/c
- Average emission time for fast antiprotons: 16.5 ± 0.06 fm/c
- Average emission time for slow protons: 22.9 ± 0.01 fm/c
- Average emission time for slow antiprotons: 21.2 ± 0.2 fm/c
- Fast particles are emitted approximately 4-5 fm/c earlier than slow particles
- Width of cross-over at μ_B = 0: approximately 45 MeV

## Context
This work proposes an alternative to fluctuation-based signatures of the QCD critical point, which the authors argue suffer from critical slowing down (Berdnikov 1999) and washout before freeze-out. The idea extends the observation by Nonaka and Asakawa (2004) that the critical point acts as an attractor for hydrodynamic trajectories. The authors note that NA49 data at 40 GeV/A shows an antiproton spectrum with a steeper slope than the proton spectrum, which is compatible with their predicted signature, though statistical errors prevent firm conclusions. The approach enables experimental searches using the beam energy scan at RHIC, NA61 at CERN-SPS, and the future FAIR facility.
