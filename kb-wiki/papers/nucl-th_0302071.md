# Nucleon participants or quark participants?

> We show that centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is well described as proportional to We show that centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is well described as proportional to the number of participating constituent quarks. In this approach there is no need for an additional contribution from hard processes usually considered in the models based on the number of the nucleon participants.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is well described as proportional to the number of participating constituent quarks, with no need for an additional contribution from hard processes; the observed increase per nucleon participant pair is explained by the relative increase in the number of interacting constituent quarks in more central collisions.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo implementation of the nuclear overlap model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile to calculate the mean number of nucleon participants and quark participants for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. To compute quark participants, they modified the code by tripling the nuclear density (n0q = 3 n0 = 51 fm−3) and replacing the inelastic NN cross section (σNN = 41 mb at √sNN = 130 GeV) with quark-quark cross sections of σqq = 4.56 mb and σqq = 6 mb. They then renormalized the PHOBOS charged particle pseudorapidity density data from per nucleon participant pair to per quark participant pair.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic NN cross section used: σNN = 41 mb at √sNN = 130 GeV
- Quark-quark cross sections used: σqq = σNN/9 = 4.56 mb and σqq = 6 mb
- Nuclear density parameters: n0 = 0.17 fm−3, R = (1.12 A1/3 − 0.861/3) fm, d = 0.54 fm; quark density n0q = 3 n0 = 51 fm−3
- In the 5% most central Au+Au collisions, charged particle density per nucleon participant is about 20% larger than in semi-peripheral collisions (50–70% centrality region) and about 50% larger compared to pp collisions at the same energy

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark (valon) picture, previously applied to hadron-hadron and hadron-nucleus collisions (references to Hwa (1982) and others), to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC. It directly contradicts the standard approach (Kharzeev and others) that attributes the rise in particle production per nucleon participant to hard processes scaling with binary collisions. The result enables a simpler description of centrality dependence without invoking hard processes and suggests that the number of nucleon participants is no longer the relevant measure of particle production sources.
