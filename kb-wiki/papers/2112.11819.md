# Imaging nuclear modifications on parton distributions with triple-differential dijet cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions

> Dijet production in proton-nucleus ($p$A) collisions at the LHC provides invaluable information on the underlying parton distributions in nuclei, especially the gluon distributions. Triple-differential dijet cross sections enable a well-controlled kinematic scan (over momentum fraction $x$ and probing scale $Q^2$) of the nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), i.e., $f^\textrm{A}_i(x,Q^2)$. In this work, we study several types of triple-differential cross sections for dijet production in proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-lead ($p$Pb) collisions at the LHC, to next-to-leading order within the framework of perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD). Four sets of nPDF parametrizations, EPPS16, nCTEQ15, TUJU19, and nIMParton16 are employed in the calculations for $p$Pb collisions. We show that the observable nuclear modification factor $R_{p\textrm{Pb}}$ of such cross sections can serve as a nice image of the nuclear modifications on parton distributions, quantified by the ratio $r^{\textrm{A}}_i(x,Q^2)\!=\!f^\textrm{A,proton}_i(x,Q^2)/f^\textrm{proton}_i(x,Q^2)$. Considerable differences among the $R_{p\textrm{Pb}}$ predicted by the four nPDF sets can be observed and intuitively understood. Future measurements of such observables are expected to not only constrain the nPDF parametrizations, but also help confirm various nuclear effects, e.g., shadowing, anti-shadowing, EMC, and Fermi motion in different regions of $x$ and their variations with probing scale $Q^2$.

## Key claim

Triple-differential dijet cross sections in proton-lead collisions can serve as a direct image of the nuclear modification factor \(r^A_i(x,Q^2)\) for both quark and gluon distributions, but the correspondence between the observable \(R_{p\textrm{Pb}}\) and the underlying nPDFs is clearest at leading order and becomes partially smeared by higher-order corrections and finite kinematic bin sizes.

## Method

The authors calculated triple-differential dijet cross sections in proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at LHC energies to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD using the NLOJet++ code, with CT14 proton PDFs as the baseline. Four nuclear PDF parametrizations (EPPS16, nCTEQ15, TUJU19, and nIMParton16) were employed for the nuclear modification factors \(R_{p\textrm{Pb}}\), defined as the per-nucleon ratio of the \(p\)Pb to \(pp\) cross sections. Three types of triple-differential cross sections were studied: \(d^3\sigma/dp_{T,\textrm{avg}}dy_bdy^*\), \(d^3\sigma/dX_BdX_Ady^*\), and the dijet pseudo-rapidity distribution with a \(p_{T,\textrm{avg}}\) cut.

## Key numbers

* The normalized dijet pseudo-rapidity distribution in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV was calculated with \(75 < p_{T,\textrm{avg}} < 95\) GeV, cone size \(R=0.3\), and rapidity cut \(|y_{\textrm{lab}}| < 3\), and the NLO results with CT14 PDFs described CMS data well.
* For the triple-differential cross section \(d^3\sigma/dp_{T,\textrm{avg}}dy_bdy^*\) in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=8\) TeV, the CMS data covered \(p_{T,\textrm{avg}}\) from approximately 50 to 500 GeV with \(y_b \in [0,1]\) and \(y^* \in [0,1]\), and the relative statistical uncertainties in the CMS data were much smaller than the differences among nPDF predictions in a wide \(p_{T,\textrm{avg}}\) range.
* For the cross section \(d^3\sigma/dX_BdX_Ady^*\) in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=8.16\) TeV with \(p_{T1}>30\) GeV and \(p_{T2}>20\) GeV, the dijet yields for \(X_A \sim 0.01\) in the large-\(X_B\) region increased by approximately two orders of magnitude compared to the \(X_A \sim 0.1\) case.
* The nuclear correction factors \(r^{\textrm{Pb}}_i(x,Q^2)\) from the four nPDF sets were compared at \(Q=100\) GeV, showing considerable differences especially for gluon distributions, with TUJU19 predicting strong anti-shadowing near \(x \sim 0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using triple-differential dijet cross sections, previously applied to proton PDFs in \(pp\) collisions by Ellis and Soper (1994) and the CMS collaboration (2017), to the study of nuclear PDFs in proton-nucleus collisions. It builds on global nPDF analyses such as EPPS16 by Eskola et al. (2016), nCTEQ15 by Kovarik et al. (2015), TUJU19 by Walt et al. (2019), and nIMParton16 by Wang et al. (2016), and on CMS dijet data in \(p\)Pb collisions (2018) that already constrained gluon shadowing. The results enable future LHC measurements to discriminate among competing nPDF parametrizations and to map the \(x\) and \(Q^2\) dependence of nuclear effects including shadowing, anti-shadowing, EMC, and Fermi motion.
