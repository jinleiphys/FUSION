# Radiative decays of phi-meson about nature of light scalar resonances

> We show on gauge invariance grounds that the fine threshold phenomenon is discovered in the radiative decays phi->gamma a0->gamma pi0 eta and phi->gamma f0->gamma pi0pi0. This enables to conclude that production of the lightest scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) in these decays is caused by the four-quark transitions, resulting in strong restrictions on the large NC expansions of the decay amplitudes. The analysis shows that these constraints give new evidences in favor of the four-quark nature of a0(980) and f0(980) mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fine threshold phenomenon in the radiative decays phi -> gamma a0 -> gamma pi0 eta and phi -> gamma f0 -> gamma pi0 pi0 is discovered on gauge invariance grounds, establishing that the K+ K- loop mechanism is the principal production mechanism and that this constitutes a four-quark transition, which provides new evidence for the four-quark nature of the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons.

## Method

The author analyzes the invariant mass spectra of the pi0 eta and pi0 pi0 systems from the radiative decays of the phi meson, using the K+ K- loop model where phi -> K+ K- -> gamma R (with R = a0 or f0). The gauge-invariant transition amplitude is expressed in terms of the function g_R(m), and the author demonstrates that the observed spectral shapes require a suppression of g_R(m) that is naturally provided by the K+ K- threshold effect. The large N_C expansion of the decay amplitudes is then examined to compare the two-quark and four-quark interpretations of the scalar mesons.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Achasov and Ivanchenko, 1989) that radiative phi decays can probe the nature of the light scalar mesons, and it builds on experimental measurements from the SND, CMD-2, and KLOE collaborations. The analysis contradicts the two-quark interpretation of a0(980) and f0(980) by showing that the observed decay intensities and spectral shapes are naturally explained by four-quark transitions, consistent with Jaffe's (1977) MIT bag model prediction of light four-quark scalar states. The results enable future studies of scalar meson spectroscopy and provide constraints on large N_C expansions of decay amplitudes.
