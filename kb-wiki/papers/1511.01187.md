# Neutrino-induced meson productions

> We develop a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model for neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region, by extending the DCC model that we have previously developed through an analysis of $πN, γN\to πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$ reaction data for $W\le 2.1$ GeV. We analyze electron-induced reaction data for both proton and neutron targets to determine the vector current form factors up to $Q^2\le$ 3.0 (GeV/$c$)$^2$. Axial-current matrix elements are derived in accordance with the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) relation to the $πN$ interactions of the DCC model. As a result, we can uniquely determine the interference pattern between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes. Our calculated cross sections for neutrino-induced single-pion productions are compared with available data, and are found to be in reasonable agreement with the data. We also calculate the double-pion production cross sections in the resonance region, for the first time, with relevant resonance contributions and channel couplings. The result is compared with the double-pion production data. For a future development of a neutrino-nucleus reaction model and/or a neutrino event generator for analyses of neutrino experiments, the DCC model presented here can give a useful input.

## Key claim

The paper develops a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model for neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region, claiming that the interference pattern between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes is uniquely determined by deriving axial-current matrix elements from the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) relation to the πN interactions of the DCC model, but notes that the Q²-dependence of axial-current matrix elements still requires a simple ansatz due to lack of experimental information.

## Method

The authors extended their previously developed DCC model, which had been fit to πN, γN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ reaction data for W ≤ 2.1 GeV, to neutrino reactions by analyzing electron-induced reaction data on both proton and neutron targets to determine vector current form factors up to Q² ≤ 3.0 (GeV/c)². Axial-current matrix elements were derived using the PCAC relation to the πN interactions of the DCC model, and cross sections for neutrino-induced single-pion and double-pion productions were calculated and compared with available data from ANL and BNL experiments.

## Key numbers

- Vector current form factors determined up to Q² ≤ 3.0 (GeV/c)² from electron-induced reaction data
- Axial mass taken as M_A = 1.02 GeV from prior work
- For ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ p, the ratio σ(ν_μ n → μ⁻ π⁰ p)/σ(ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ p) = 0.28, 0.27, 0.29 at E_ν = 0.5, 1, 1.5 GeV respectively
- For ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ p, the ratio σ(ν_μ n → μ⁻ π⁺ n)/σ(ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ p) = 0.13, 0.17, 0.21 at E_ν = 0.5, 1, 1.5 GeV respectively
- Double-pion production cross section ratio σ(ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ π⁺ n)/σ(ν_μ p → μ⁻ π⁺ π⁰ p) ≈ 13% at E_ν = 2 GeV
- Towards E_ν ∼ 2 GeV, double-pion production cross section becomes 1/8 (proton target) and 1/4 (neutron target) of single-pion production cross section
- ηN, KΛ, and KΣ production cross sections are 10⁻² to 10⁻³ times smaller than single-pion production cross sections

## Context

This work extends the DCC model previously developed by the author and collaborators (Nakamura et al., 2013) for analyzing πN, γN reactions to the neutrino sector, addressing the lack of multi-channel couplings in existing neutrino-nucleon models and the absence of serious studies of neutrino-induced double-pion production over the entire resonance region. The model provides a basic ingredient for constructing neutrino-nucleus reaction models and neutrino event generators for analyses of neutrino oscillation experiments, and enables future work such as analyzing ANL and BNL deuterium target data with proper treatment of nuclear effects as initiated by the model of Wu et al. (2015).
