# Neutron star matter equation of state and gravitational wave emission

> The EOS of strongly interacting matter at densities ten to fifteen orders of magnitude larger than the typical density of terrestrial macroscopic objects determines a number of neutron star properties, including the pattern of gravitational waves emitted following the excitation of nonradial oscillation modes. This paper reviews some of the approaches employed to model neutron star matter, as well as the prospects for obtaining new insights from the experimental study of gravitational waves emitted by neutron stars.

## Key claim

This review argues that the equation of state (EOS) of strongly interacting matter at densities ten to fifteen orders of magnitude larger than terrestrial densities determines neutron star properties, including gravitational wave emission patterns from nonradial oscillation modes; however, the paper emphasizes that all available EOS are model-dependent because quantum chromodynamics (QCD) cannot be solved from first principles at the relevant densities and temperatures.

## Method

The paper reviews two main theoretical approaches for modeling neutron star matter EOS at nuclear and supranuclear densities (ρ ≥ 2.7 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³): nonrelativistic nuclear many-body theory (NMBT), which uses a Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon interactions fitted to scattering data and nuclear binding energies, and relativistic mean field theory (RMFT), which treats nucleons as Dirac particles interacting through meson exchange in the mean field approximation. The resulting EOS are then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for nonrotating neutron star structure and to compute the frequencies of quasi-normal oscillation modes (f-mode, p-modes, w-modes) that determine gravitational wave emission.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of uniform nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 2.7 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Neutron drip density: ρ_d = 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³
- Density range of neutron star core: 2 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ to 1–4 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³
- Observed neutron star masses range between approximately 1.1 and 1.9 M⊙
- Gravitational redshift measurement from EXO0748-676: z = 0.35, giving mass-radius ratio M/R = 0.153 M⊙/km
- Maximum mass from Fermi gas model (polytropic EOS): approximately 0.7 M⊙
- For a 1.4 M⊙ star using the Akmal et al. EOS: fundamental mode frequency ν_f = 1983 Hz and damping time τ_f = 0.184 s
- Signal-to-noise ratio of 5 in VIRGO corresponds to mode energy E_f ∼ 6 × 10⁻⁷ M⊙ for a source at 10 kpc distance

## Context

This review extends earlier work on neutron star EOS by Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939) and the Fermi gas model, which failed to predict observed neutron star masses. It discusses calculations by Akmal et al. (APR) using NMBT, Baldo et al. on hyperonic matter, and Baym and Chin on quark matter using the MIT bag model. The gravitational wave analysis builds on the scaling relations of Andersson and Kokkotas (1998) and the more recent fits by Benhar et al., showing that improved EOS models over a decade shift predicted f-mode frequencies by approximately 100 Hz. The work enables future gravitational wave detections to discriminate between EOS models, particularly identifying signatures of hyperon presence in neutron star cores.
