# A Detailed Examination of Astrophysical Constraints on the Symmetry Energy and the Neutron Skin of $^{208}$Pb with Minimal Modeling Assumptions

> The symmetry energy and its density dependence are pivotal for many nuclear physics and astrophysics applications, as they determine properties ranging from the neutron-skin thickness of nuclei to the crust thickness and the radius of neutron stars. Recently, PREX-II reported a value of $0.283\pm0.071$ fm for the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb, $R_{\rm skin}^{^{208}\text{Pb}}$, implying a symmetry-energy slope parameter $L$ of $106\pm37$ MeV, larger than most ranges obtained from microscopic calculations and other nuclear experiments. We use a nonparametric equation of state representation based on Gaussian processes to constrain the symmetry energy $S_0$, $L$, and $R_{\rm skin}^{^{208}\text{Pb}}$ directly from observations of neutron stars with minimal modeling assumptions. The resulting astrophysical constraints from heavy pulsar masses, LIGO/Virgo, and NICER favor smaller values of the neutron skin and $L$, as well as negative symmetry incompressibilities. Combining astrophysical data with chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) and PREX-II constraints yields $S_0 = 33.0^{+2.0}_{-1.8}$ MeV, $L=53^{+14}_{-15}$ MeV, and $R_{\rm skin}^{^{208}\text{Pb}} = 0.17^{+0.04}_{-0.04}$ fm. We also examine the consistency of several individual $χ$EFT calculations with astrophysical observations and terrestrial experiments. We find that there is only mild tension between $χ$EFT, astrophysical data, and PREX-II's $R_\mathrm{skin}^{^{208}\mathrm{Pb}}$ measurement ($p$-value $= 12.3\%$) and that there is excellent agreement between $χ$EFT, astrophysical data, and other nuclear experiments.

## Key claim

The paper finds that astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations favor smaller values of the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and the symmetry-energy slope parameter L than the PREX-II measurement, but that there is only mild tension (p-value = 12.3%) between chiral effective field theory, astrophysical data, and the PREX-II result when all are combined, contrary to claims of significant disagreement.

## Method

The authors use a nonparametric Gaussian process representation of the neutron star equation of state that avoids restrictive parametric modeling assumptions, generating EOS realizations that are causal, thermodynamically stable, and matched to a crust model at low densities. They then perform a hierarchical Bayesian inference by assigning marginal likelihoods to each EOS realization from astrophysical observations including heavy pulsar masses (PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0348+0432), the gravitational-wave event GW170817 from LIGO/Virgo, and the NICER X-ray pulse-profile measurement of PSR J0030+0451. The symmetry energy parameters S0, L, and Ksym are extracted from each EOS realization by solving the beta-equilibrium condition and using empirical relations between L and the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb based on nuclear density functional calculations.

## Key numbers

- PREX-II reported neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, implying L = 106 ± 37 MeV
- Combined astrophysical data with chiral EFT and PREX-II constraints yields: S0 = 33.0^{+2.0}_{-1.8} MeV, L = 53^{+14}_{-15} MeV, and R_skin^{208Pb} = 0.17^{+0.04}_{-0.04} fm
- The empirical fit between R_skin^{208Pb} and L from Mondal (2016) gives: R_skin^{208Pb} [fm] = 0.0724 + 0.0019 × (L [MeV]), with standard deviation 0.0143 fm
- The empirical fit between alpha_D^{208Pb} · S0 and L from Roca-Maza (2013) gives: alpha_D^{208Pb} · S0 [fm^3 MeV] = 493.5 + 3.08 × (L [MeV]), with standard deviation 27.6 fm^3 MeV
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses; PSR J0348+0432 mass: 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- SNM parameters used: n0 = 0.164 ± 0.007 fm^{-3}, E0 = -15.86 ± 0.57 MeV, K0 = 215 ± 40 MeV
- p-value for tension between chiral EFT, astrophysical data, and PREX-II: 12.3%

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric inference framework developed by Essick, Landry, and Holz (2019, 2020) to directly constrain symmetry energy parameters from astrophysical observations with minimal modeling assumptions, in contrast to parametric EOS representations such as speed-of-sound, polytropic, or spectral extension schemes used in earlier work by Alford (2013), Hebeler (2013), and Lindblom (2010). The results contradict the large L value inferred from PREX-II by Reed (2021) and show that the correlation between L and neutron star radii is significantly weaker when allowing for nonparametric high-density EOS extensions than previously claimed by Lattimer (2000, 2012). The findings enable future joint analyses of nuclear experiments, chiral EFT calculations, and astrophysical observations to refine constraints on the symmetry energy.
