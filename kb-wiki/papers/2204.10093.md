# Medium modification of the nucleon mechanical properties: Abel tomography case

> We investigate how the gravitational form factors of the nucleon undergo changes in nuclear matter, emphasizing the Abel transformation from the three-dimensional (3D) Breit frame to the two-dimensional (2D) light-front frame. Since the gravitational form factors reveal the mechanical structure of the nucleon, we examine also the medium modifications of the energy-momentum, pressure, and shear-force distributions. We scrutinize the stabilities of the nucleon in nuclear matter. For this purpose, we employ the in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model to study these mechanical quantities of the nucleon, since it provides a simple but clear framework. In this in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model, the modification of pionic properties is performed by using low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data and the saturation properties of nuclear matter near the normal nuclear matter density, $ρ_0=0.5m_π^3$. The results reveal how the nucleon swells in nuclear matter as the mass distribution of the nucleon is broadened in medium. We also show that the mean square radii corresponding to the mass and angular momentum distributions increase in nuclear medium. This feature is kept both in 3D and 2D cases. We visualize how the strong force fields inside the nucleon in the 2D plane are distributed and illustrate how these forces undergo change in nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the nucleon, and consequently its mechanical properties such as pressure and shear-force distributions, undergo modification in nuclear matter, with the nucleon swelling as the mass distribution broadens. The hedge is that these conclusions are drawn specifically within the in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model, which relies on low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data and saturation properties of nuclear matter near normal density.

## Method
The authors employ the in-medium modified SU(2) Skyrme model, incorporating density-dependent medium functions that modify the pion decay constant, Skyrme parameter, and pion mass based on low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data and nuclear matter saturation properties. They compute the gravitational form factors A(t), J(t), and D(t) from the energy-momentum tensor of the rotating soliton, then extract three-dimensional (3D) Breit frame distributions for energy, angular momentum, pressure, and shear force. Using the Abel transform, they project these 3D distributions onto two-dimensional (2D) light-front transverse distributions, which provide a probabilistic interpretation free from relativistic ambiguities.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.5 m_π³
- D(0) in free space: approximately -2.8; D*(0) at normal nuclear matter density: approximately -3.9
- Central 3D energy density ε*(0) decreases from 1.25 GeV/fm³ at ρ=0 to 0.62 GeV/fm³ at ρ=ρ₀ (approximately 50% decrease)
- Central 2D energy density ε*(0) decreases from 1.25 GeV/fm² at ρ=0 to 0.80 GeV/fm² at ρ=ρ₀ (approximately 36% decrease)
- 3D mass mean square radius ⟨r²⟩*_ε increases from 0.68 fm² at ρ=0 to 0.95 fm² at ρ=ρ₀
- 2D mass mean square radius ⟨x_⊥²⟩*_ε increases from 0.30 fm² at ρ=0 to 0.44 fm² at ρ=ρ₀
- 3D mechanical mean square radius ⟨r²⟩*_mech increases from 0.75 fm² at ρ=0 to 1.01 fm² at ρ=ρ₀
- 2D mechanical mean square radius ⟨x_⊥²⟩*_mech increases from 0.50 fm² at ρ=0 to 0.68 fm² at ρ=ρ₀
- 3D pressure nodal point r*₀ increases from 0.72 fm at ρ=0 to 0.91 fm at ρ=ρ₀
- 2D pressure nodal point (x_⊥)*₀ increases from 0.59 fm at ρ=0 to 0.73 fm at ρ=ρ₀

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon gravitational form factors in nuclear matter previously initiated by Kim (2012) using the same in-medium Skyrme model framework developed by Rakhimov (1996, 1998) and refined by Yakhshiev (2010). The key advance is the application of the Abel transform, as formulated by Panteleeva (2021) and Kim (2021), to convert the 3D Breit frame distributions into 2D light-front transverse distributions that carry proper probabilistic meaning, addressing criticisms raised by Freese (2021) about the validity of 3D distributions for relativistic particles. This enables visualization of how the strong force fields inside the nucleon in the 2D plane change in nuclear matter, and the results show that the increasing absolute value of D(0) in medium is consistent with trends observed in heavier nuclei by Guzey (2005) and Polyakov (2002).
