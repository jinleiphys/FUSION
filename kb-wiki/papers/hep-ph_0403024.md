# Whitening of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Parton-parton collisions do not neutralize local color charges in the quark-gluon plasma as they only redistribute the charges among momentum modes. We discuss color diffusion and color conductivity as the processes responsible for the neutralization of the plasma. For this purpose, we first compute the conductivity and diffusion coefficients in the plasma that is significantly colorful. Then, the time evolution of the color density due to the conductivity and diffusion is studied. The conductivity is shown to be much more efficient than the diffusion in neutralizing the plasma at the scale longer than the screening length. Estimates of the characteristic time scales, which are based on close to global equilibrium computations, suggest that first the plasma becomes white and then the momentum degrees of freedom thermalize.

## Key claim

Parton-parton collisions do not neutralize local color charges in the quark-gluon plasma, only redistributing them among momentum modes; the authors argue that color conductivity, rather than color diffusion, is the dominant mechanism for "whitening" the plasma at scales longer than the screening length, with estimates based on close-to-global equilibrium suggesting that the plasma becomes white before momentum degrees of freedom thermalize.

## Method

The authors used QCD transport theory with linearized kinetic equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, assuming small deviations from a colorful local equilibrium state. They derived the color diffusion constant and conductivity tensor using a relaxation-time approximation improved to satisfy covariant current conservation (a BGK-type collision term), then solved the macroscopic equation for the temporal evolution of the color charge density under combined diffusive and ohmic currents.

## Key numbers

* Color conductivity near global equilibrium is of order \(\sigma \sim T / \ln(1/g)\), where \(T\) is temperature and \(g\) is the QCD coupling.
* The relaxation time \(1/\gamma\) is estimated as \(1/\gamma \sim 1/(g^2 \ln(1/g) T)\), which is the characteristic soft scattering time \(t_{\rm soft}\).
* The ratio \(\sigma / \mathcal{D} \sim g^2 T^2 \sim m_D^2\), where \(m_D\) is the screening mass, meaning conductivity dominates over diffusion for modes longer than the screening length.
* The hard thermalization time scale is \(t_{\rm hard} \sim 1/(g^4 \ln(1/g) T)\), which is much longer than \(t_{\rm soft}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Manuel and Mrowczynski, 2003) which proved that local equilibrium in the quark-gluon plasma is generically colorful, meaning collisions alone cannot neutralize local color charges. It builds on earlier studies of color conductivity by Heinz (1983), Czyz and Dyrek (1986), Mrowczynski (1987-1989), Selikhov (1993), Heiselberg (1994), and the more recent systematic treatments by Bodeker (1998-1999), Litim and Manuel (1999), Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (1998-1999), and Blaizot and Iancu (1999). The results enable future studies of the full equilibration process, including the role of color plasma instabilities in anisotropic systems (Mrowczynski, 1996; Randrup and Mrowczynski, 2003; Romatschke and Strickland, 2003; Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore, 2003).
