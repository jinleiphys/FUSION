# Positioning the neutron drip line and the r-process paths in the nuclear landscape

> Exploring nucleon drip lines and astrophysical rapid neutron capture process (r-process) paths in the nuclear landscape is extremely challenging in nuclear physics and astrophysics. While various models predict similar proton drip line, their predictions for neutron drip line and the r-process paths involving heavy neutron-rich nuclei exhibit a significant variation which hampers our accurate understanding of the r-process nucleosynthesis mechanism. Using microscopic density functional theory with a representative set of non-relativistic and relativistic interactions, we demonstrate for the first time that this variation is mainly due to the uncertainty of nuclear matter symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_{\rm{sc}})$ at the subsaturation cross density $ρ_{\rm{sc}}=0.11/0.16\timesρ_0$ ($ρ_0$ is saturation density), which reflects the symmetry energy of heavy nuclei. Using the recent accurate constraint on $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_{\rm{sc}})$ from the binding energy difference of heavy isotope pairs, we obtain quite precise predictions for the location of the neutron drip line, the r-process paths and the number of bound nuclei in the nuclear landscape. Our results have important implications on extrapolating the properties of unknown neutron-rich rare isotopes from the data on known nuclei.

# Positioning the neutron drip line and the r-process paths in the nuclear landscape

## Key claim
The variation in model predictions for the neutron drip line and r-process paths is primarily due to uncertainty in the nuclear matter symmetry energy at subsaturation cross density, not at saturation density. Using a recent constraint on this symmetry energy from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs yields precise predictions for the neutron drip line location, r-process paths, and number of bound nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations using the HFBTHO code and relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the DIRBHZ code, with 20 major harmonic oscillator shells and axial deformation. They varied the symmetry energy at subsaturation cross density while keeping other macroscopic quantities fixed at their MSL1 default values, and also included results from 42 other non-relativistic and relativistic interactions to examine correlations between drip line locations and symmetry energy parameters.

## Key numbers
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $N_{\text{drip}}$ for $Z=68$ and $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{sc}})$ from 43 interactions is $-0.90$, indicating a strong anticorrelation
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $N_{\text{drip}}$ for $Z=68$ and $L(\rho_{\text{sc}})$ is $0.20$, showing very weak correlation
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $N_{\text{drip}}$ for $Z=68$ and $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0)$ is $-0.32$, and with $L(\rho_0)$ is $0.28$
- The constraint on $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{sc}})$ is $26.65 \pm 0.2$ MeV, extracted from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs
- The number of bound even-even nuclei with $2 \leq Z \leq 120$ is predicted to be $1941 \pm 31$ (only 800 have been discovered experimentally)
- The total number of bound nuclei (including odd-A and odd-odd) is estimated at $6866 \pm 166$ (only 3191 have been discovered experimentally)
- The subsaturation cross density is defined as $\rho_{\text{sc}} = 0.11/0.16 \times \rho_0$, where $\rho_0$ is saturation density

## Context
This work resolves a puzzle in the community where Oyamatsu et al. (2010) found a correlation between neutron drip line location and the density slope $L(\rho_0)$, but Afanasjev et al. (2013) and Agbemi et al. (2014) found no such evidence. The authors demonstrate that the relevant quantity is the symmetry energy at subsaturation density rather than at saturation density. The results enable more precise predictions for the r-process path (shown for $S_{\text{2n}} = 2$ MeV) and suggest that future FRIB measurements may cover the neutron drip line for $Z \lesssim 30$ and $Z \approx 40$, as well as the r-process path for $Z \lesssim 50$ and $Z \approx 70$.
