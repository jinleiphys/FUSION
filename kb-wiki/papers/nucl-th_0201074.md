# Charm meson production from meson-nucleon scattering

> Using an effective hadronic Lagrangian with physical hadron masses and coupling constants determined either empirically or from SU(4) flavor symmetry, we study the production cross sections of charm mesons from pion and rho meson interactions with nucleons. With a cutoff parameter of 1 GeV at interaction vertices as usually used in studying the cross sections for $J/ψ$ absorption and charm meson scattering by hadrons, we find that the cross sections for charm meson production have values of a few tenth of mb and are dominated by the s channel nucleon pole diagram. Relevance of these reactions to charm meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed.

# Charm meson production from meson-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

Using an effective hadronic Lagrangian with a cutoff parameter of 1 GeV at interaction vertices, the cross sections for charm meson production from pion-nucleon and rho-nucleon scattering are found to be a few tenths of a millibarn, which is much larger than the few microbarn predicted by the Quark-Gluon String Model; however, the magnitude depends sensitively on the cutoff parameter, and using a smaller cutoff of 0.4 GeV (as needed to reproduce kaon production data) would reduce the cross sections by more than an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(4) flavor symmetry with empirical hadron masses, using coupling constants determined from empirical data (e.g., the D* meson width) or from SU(4) relations. They evaluated the cross sections for the reactions πN → D̄Λc and ρN → D̄Λc by computing the s-channel nucleon pole, t-channel charm meson exchange, and u-channel charm baryon pole diagrams, introducing form factors with a cutoff parameter Λ at the interaction vertices.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section for πN → D̄Λc with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV: about 0.2 mb.
- Cross section for ρN → D̄Λc with Λ = 1 GeV: about a factor of two larger than the pion-nucleon cross section (i.e., about 0.4 mb).
- Coupling constant fπNN/mπ = 7.18 GeV⁻¹ (empirical value).
- Coupling constant gπDD* = 5.56, obtained from the measured D* width of ΓD* ≈ 96 keV.
- Coupling constant gρDD = 2.52, determined from vector meson dominance.
- Coupling constant gD*NΛc = √3 gρNN = 5.58 from SU(4) relations.
- Using Λ = 2 GeV increases the cross sections by an order of magnitude; using Λ = 0.5 GeV reduces them by more than an order of magnitude.
- Reproducing the empirical cross section for πN → KΛ requires Λ ≈ 0.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of J/ψ absorption and charm meson scattering by hadrons (Matinyan, Haglin, Lin, Oh, Liu) using the same effective Lagrangian approach. It directly contradicts the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) predictions of a few microbarn for charm meson production, which only included t-channel vector charm meson exchange and neglected s-channel contributions. The results suggest that if these larger cross sections were used during the initial string stage of heavy ion collisions as in the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) study by Cassing, they would lead to too large an enhancement of charm meson production; however, they may be more appropriate for collisions between mesons and baryons in the hadronic matter phase.
