# Scalar field in nuclear matter: the roles of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and nucleon structure

> Chiral theories with spontaneous symmetry breaking such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model lead to the existence of a scalar mode. We present in a detailed manner how the corresponding low momentum effective lagrangian involving the scalar field can be constructed starting from the NJL model. We discuss the relevance of the scalar mode for the problem of the nuclear binding and saturation. We show that it depends on the nucleon mass origin with two extreme cases. If this origin is entirely due to confinement the coupling of this mode to the nucleons vanishes, making it irrelevant for the nuclear binding problem. If instead it is entirely due to spontaneous symmetry breaking it couples to the nucleons but nuclear matter collapses. It is only in the case of a mixed origin with spontaneous breaking that nuclear matter can be stable and reach saturation. We describe models of nucleon structure where this balance is achieved. We also show how chiral constraints and confinement modify the QCD sum rules for the mass evolution in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the relevance of the scalar field from chiral symmetry breaking for nuclear binding and saturation depends critically on the mixed origin of the nucleon mass: if the nucleon mass comes entirely from confinement the scalar field coupling vanishes, while if it comes entirely from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking nuclear matter collapses; only a mixed origin allows stable, saturating nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors construct a low-momentum effective Lagrangian for the scalar field starting from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using a derivative expansion of the fermion determinant, first with a sharp momentum cutoff and then with a delocalized (non-local) version employing a Gaussian form factor. They then incorporate the scalar field into nuclear matter calculations at the Hartree level and discuss how the nucleon response to the scalar field, governed by the confinement mechanism inside the nucleon, determines whether nuclear matter saturates or collapses. The analysis is extended using a simple quark-diquark string model to explore the interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement in nucleon structure.

## Key numbers

- The scalar mass parameter from the NJL model is around twice the constituent quark mass, which is a low-momentum parameter related to the inverse of the vacuum scalar susceptibility.
- The cutoff parameter Lambda in the delocalized NJL model is of the order of 1 GeV, possibly related to the inverse of the instanton size.
- The pion decay constant F_pi is identified with the parameter F, where F^2 = tilde_I(M_0) * M_0^2, and the vacuum pion mass parameter is M_pi^2 = m * M_0 / (G_1 * F_pi^2).
- The vector and axial-vector low-momentum mass parameters satisfy M_A^2 / M_V^2 = 1 + 4 * G_2 * I(bar_S) * bar_S^2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework (Chanfray and Ericson, 2005; 2007; Massot and Chanfray, 2008) that associates nuclear mean-field attraction with in-medium modification of a chiral-invariant background scalar field, incorporating the nucleon response originally introduced by Guichon (1988). It contrasts with pure NJL model treatments where nuclear matter collapses (Bentz and Thomas, 2001) and with approaches where the nucleon mass is entirely due to confinement (MIT bag model), where the scalar field coupling vanishes. The paper enables future work on realistic models of nucleon structure that balance chiral symmetry breaking and confinement contributions to the nucleon mass, and it modifies QCD sum rules for the mass evolution in nuclear matter by incorporating chiral constraints and confinement.
