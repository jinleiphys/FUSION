# Symbolic integration of a product of two spherical bessel functions with an additional exponential and polynomial factor

> We present a mathematica package that performs the symbolic calculation of integrals of the form \int^{\infty}_0 e^{-x/u} x^n j_ν (x) j_μ (x) dx where $j_ν (x)$ and $j_μ (x)$ denote spherical Bessel functions of integer orders, with $ν\ge 0$ and $μ\ge 0$. With the real parameter $u>0$ and the integer $n$, convergence of the integral requires that $n+ν+μ\ge 0$. The package provides analytical result for the integral in its most simplified form. The novel symbolic method employed enables the calculation of a large number of integrals of the above form in a fraction of the time required for conventional numerical and Mathematica based brute-force methods. We test the accuracy of such analytical expressions by comparing the results with their numerical counterparts.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Mathematica package that performs symbolic (analytical) integration of integrals of the form \(\int^{\infty}_0 e^{-x/u} x^n j_{\nu} (x) j_{\mu} (x) dx\) for integer orders \(\nu \ge 0\) and \(\mu \ge 0\), with the convergence condition \(n+\nu+\mu \ge 0\). The method is based on empirical observations from Mathematica symbolic experiments rather than a fully analytic derivation, though all conjectures are verifiable at each stage of the computation.

## Method

The algorithm first recasts the integral as a limit \(I(u, n, \nu, \mu) = \lim_{y\to\infty} F(y, u, n, \mu, \nu)\) where \(F\) is the integral from 0 to \(y\). Mathematica evaluates \(F\) and separates it into a y-dependent part \(G\) and a y-independent part \(H\). The core of the algorithm exploits the observation that \(G\) always consists of exponential functions and exactly three distinct exponential integral functions \(Ei(g_j(y,u))\) with specific arguments. The limits of combinations of these exponential integral functions are evaluated analytically to \(h_1(u)=0\) and \(h_2(u)=-i2\pi\), and the package then parses, simplifies, and checks the resulting expression using Mathematica's symbolic manipulation functions.

## Key numbers

* The convergence condition for the integral is \(n+\nu+\mu \ge 0\).
* For the sample integral \(I(u,-1,1,1)\), the simplified analytical result is \(\frac{1}{96 u^4} \bigl(4 u^2 (-1+6 u^2) - 32 u^3 \text{ArcTan}[2 u] + (1+12 u^2) \text{Log}[1+4 u^2]\bigr)\).
* For \(I(u,-3,3,3)\), the simplified result is \(\frac{1}{967680 u^{10}}\bigl[4 u^2 (-15-240 u^2-1556 u^4+4272 u^6+672 u^8) -1536 u^7 (15+4 u^2) \text{ArcTan}[2 u] + 3 (5+90 u^2+672 u^4+3360 u^6) \text{Log}[1+4 u^2]\bigr]\).
* Numerical validation for \(I(u,-3,3,3)\) at \(u=0.5\) gives analytical and numerical values both equal to 0.0000216; at \(u=1.0\) both equal 0.0001523; at \(u=2.0\) both equal 0.0005597; at \(u=4.0\) both equal 0.0011940; at \(u=8.0\) both equal 0.0017990.

## Context

This work was motivated by the need to evaluate hundreds of such integrals analytically in the application of the density matrix expansion (Negele, 1970s; Gebremariam et al., 2009) to the Hartree-Fock energy obtained from chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon interactions at N\(^2\)LO (Bogner et al., 2000s; Gebremariam et al., 2009). The package complements existing numerical packages for Bessel function integrals (Singh, 2000s; Deun, 2000s) and enables the efficient symbolic calculation of large batches of integrals that would be impractical with direct numerical or brute-force Mathematica methods. Future extensions could include general Bessel functions, scaling parameters, and products of more than two Bessel functions.
