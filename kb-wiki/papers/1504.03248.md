# Dispersive calculation of complex Regge trajectories for the lightest $f_2$ resonances

> We apply a recently developed dispersive formalism to calculate the Regge trajectories of the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2'(1525)$ mesons. Trajectories are calculated, not fitted to a family of resonances. Assuming that these spin-2 resonances can be treated in the elastic approximation the only input are the pole position and residue of the resonances. In both cases, the predicted Regge trajectories are almost real and linear, with slopes in agreement with the universal value of order 1 GeV$^{-2}$.

# Dispersive calculation of complex Regge trajectories for the lightest $f_2$ resonances

## Key claim
The dispersive formalism predicts that the Regge trajectories of the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2'(1525)$ mesons are almost real and linear with slopes near 1 GeV$^{-2}$, but only under the assumption that these spin-2 resonances can be treated in the elastic approximation using only their pole position and residue as input.

## Method
The authors apply a twice-subtracted dispersion relation for the Regge trajectory function $\alpha(s)$, coupled with unitarity and the analytic structure of the partial-wave amplitude, to solve iteratively for $\alpha(s)$ and the residue $\beta(s)$. The free parameters $\alpha_0$, $\alpha'$, and $b_0$ are fixed by requiring that the resulting Regge amplitude reproduces the known complex pole position and coupling of each resonance in the second Riemann sheet. For the $f_2(1270)$, the input pole comes from a conformal parameterization of D0-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering data; for the $f_2'(1525)$, the pole is inferred from PDG mass and width assuming an elastic Breit-Wigner shape.

## Key numbers
- $f_2(1270)$ pole: $\sqrt{s_{f_2}} = 1267.3^{+0.8}_{-0.9} - i(87 \pm 9)$ MeV; coupling $|g_{f_2\pi\pi}|^2 = 25 \pm 3$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- $f_2(1270)$ trajectory parameters: $\alpha_0 = 0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.3}$, $\alpha' = 0.7^{+0.3}_{-0.2}$ GeV$^{-2}$, $b_0 = 1.3^{+1.4}_{-0.8}$.
- $f_2'(1525)$ pole: $\sqrt{s_{f_2'}} = 1525 \pm 5 - i(34^{+4}_{-5})$ MeV; coupling $|g_{f_2'KK}|^2 = 19 \pm 3$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- $f_2'(1525)$ trajectory parameters: $\alpha_0 = 0.53^{+0.10}_{-0.44}$, $\alpha' = 0.63^{+0.20}_{-0.05}$ GeV$^{-2}$, $b_0 = 1.33^{+0.63}_{-0.09}$.
- With three subtractions, the quadratic term $\alpha''$ is $0.044 \pm 0.011$ GeV$^{-4}$ for $f_2(1270)$ and $0.025^{+0.008}_{-0.004}$ GeV$^{-4}$ for $f_2'(1525)$, confirming near-linearity in the elastic region.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive formalism developed by Londergan et al. (2013) for calculating Regge trajectories from a single resonance pole, previously applied to the $\rho(770)$ (which gave a linear trajectory) and the $\sigma$/$f_0(500)$ (which gave a non-linear trajectory with a slope more than an order of magnitude smaller than the universal value). The present results for $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2'(1525)$ confirm that the method correctly identifies ordinary $\bar q q$ mesons and that the linearity is not an artifact of the twice-subtracted dispersion relation, as demonstrated by the three-subtraction analysis. The approach enables future classification of other resonances (e.g., $K^*(892)$, $K^*(800)$, heavy mesons, and baryonic resonances like $\Delta(1232)$) and can be extended to coupled channels.
