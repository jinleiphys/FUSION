# Resummed azimuthal decorrelation and transverse momentum imbalance of dijets at the LHC

> We present a theoretical study of the azimuthal decorrelation $δϕ$ and transverse momentum imbalance $q_T$ in dijet production at the LHC, offering intriguing insights into the dynamics of quantum chromodynamics. We define the jet axes using the recoil-free winner-take-all (WTA) recombination scheme. For the azimuthal decorrelation $δϕ$, this axis choice eliminates non-global logarithms (NGLs) entirely. For the transverse momentum imbalance $q_T$, NGLs emerge specifically in the small jet radius limit ($R \ll 1$). In this regime, the WTA scheme simplifies the theoretical framework by restricting jet radius logarithms to the soft sector. We derive factorization formulae for both observables within soft-collinear effective theory. To address the small-$R$ NGLs in the $q_T$ distribution, we refactorize the soft function into global soft, collinear-soft, and ultra-collinear-soft modes. We perform the resummation of global large logarithms $\ln(δϕ)$ and $\ln(q_T/Q)$ up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. For the $q_T$ distribution, this is combined with a leading-logarithmic resummation of the non-global $\ln R$ terms. We match our predictions to leading fixed-order $O(α_s^3)$ calculations. We also numerically investigate the structure of the first subleading power corrections. Comparisons with PYTHIA8 simulations demonstrate that the observables we consider are robust against non-perturbative multi-parton interactions and hadronization effects.

## Key claim

The winner-take-all (WTA) recombination scheme eliminates non-global logarithms entirely for the azimuthal decorrelation $\delta\phi$ in dijet production, while for the transverse momentum imbalance $q_T$, non-global logarithms persist specifically in the small jet radius limit ($R \ll 1$), where they can be resummed at leading-logarithmic accuracy through a refactorization of the soft function into global soft, collinear-soft, and ultra-collinear-soft modes.

## Method

The authors derived factorization formulae for both $\delta\phi$ and $q_T$ distributions in inclusive dijet production at hadron colliders using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), defining jet axes with the recoil-free winner-take-all (WTA) recombination scheme. For the $q_T$ distribution, they performed a refactorization of the soft function in the small jet radius limit to address non-global logarithms, separating the soft function into global soft, collinear-soft, and ultra-collinear-soft modes. They resummed global large logarithms $\ln(\delta\phi)$ and $\ln(q_T/Q)$ up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy, combined with leading-logarithmic resummation of the non-global $\ln R$ terms for $q_T$, and matched their predictions to leading fixed-order $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)$ calculations.

## Key numbers

- Resummation of global large logarithms $\ln(\delta\phi)$ and $\ln(q_T/Q)$ performed up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy
- Fixed-order matching performed at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)$ (leading order)
- Non-global logarithms in $q_T$ distribution resummed at leading-logarithmic (LL) accuracy for the $\ln R$ terms
- The soft function rapidity anomalous dimension includes terms up to $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$ with coefficients $\gamma_0^{\text{cusp}}$ and $\gamma^\nu_1$
- The hard function anomalous dimension includes terms up to $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)$ with color factors $C_H = n_q C_F + n_g C_A$

## Context

This work extends the WTA-based resummation framework from vector-boson-plus-jet ($V$+jet) production (Chien 2020, Chien 2022) to inclusive dijet production, which involves more complex partonic channels and non-trivial color structures. The authors build on recent developments in transverse momentum slicing for WTA jets (Fu 2024) and the SCET factorization approach for non-global observables (Becher 2015, Becher 2016). This enables high-precision theoretical predictions for dijet azimuthal decorrelation and transverse momentum imbalance at the LHC, which are relevant for jet calibration, tests of QCD, and studies of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions.
