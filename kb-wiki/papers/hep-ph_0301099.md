# QCD Equations of State and the QGP Liquid Model

> Recent advances in the study of equations of state of thermal lattice Quantum Chromodynamics obtained at non-zero baryon density allow validation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) liquid model equations of state (EoS). We study here the properties of the QGP-EoS near to the phase transformation boundary at finite baryon density and show a close agreement with the lattice results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a "QGP liquid model" – combining lowest-order perturbative QCD with a non-perturbative, temperature-dependent strong coupling constant and a bag constant – agrees extremely well with lattice QCD equations of state at finite baryon density, *except* in the temperature region directly near the phase transition boundary.

## Method

The authors construct a QGP partition function that includes a vacuum bag constant B, temperature-dependent running coupling α_s(T,μ_q) evolved via the two-loop renormalization group equation, and first-order thermal corrections for quarks and gluons. They compare the model's predictions for pressure difference ΔP, baryon density n_B, absolute pressure P(T,μ_b=0), and the interaction measure (ε-3P)/T^4 against lattice data from Fodor et al. (2002) for baryochemical potentials μ_b = 100, 210, 330, 410, and 530 MeV. To match the absolute pressure, they also introduce an effective gluon mass m_G ≈ 0.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant B = (0.211 GeV)^4, adjusted from an earlier value of (0.195 GeV)^4 to match a higher critical temperature T_c.
- Critical temperature T_c = 172 MeV (from the empirical α_s fit) or 173 MeV (used in the (ε-3P)/T^4 comparison).
- Strange quark mass m_s = 170 MeV used in the model; for comparison with lattice data, m_q = 65 MeV and m_s = 135 MeV are used.
- Effective gluon mass m_G = 200 MeV (0.2 GeV) required to match the absolute pressure magnitude.
- For μ_b = 100 MeV, agreement with lattice ΔP data holds for T ≥ 1.1 T_c; for μ_b = 530 MeV, agreement holds for T ≥ 1.5 T_c.
- Baryon density n_B agrees with lattice data for T ≥ 1.2 T_c for all baryochemical potentials considered.
- The interaction measure (ε-3P)/T^4 agrees with lattice data for T > 1.15 T_c.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hamaguchi (2000), who described zero-baryon-density lattice results using ideal quantum gases with a temperature-dependent coupling and vacuum latent heat. It directly compares with the finite-density lattice QCD results of Fodor et al. (2002) and notes a discrepancy with earlier lattice work by Karsch (2000) regarding the absolute pressure. The model enables study of non-equilibrium chemical composition evolution in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for RHIC initial conditions where T > 1.5 T_c ≈ 260 MeV and μ_b ≈ 45 MeV, confirming that a deconfined QGP state is established in those reactions.
