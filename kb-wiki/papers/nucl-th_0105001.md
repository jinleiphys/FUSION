# From known to undiscovered resonances

> Electromagnetic meson production formalisms are reviewed, with emphasise placed on their ability in search for new baryon resonances via gamma p -> K+ Lambda and gamma p -> eta p processes. The relevant studies, aiming to deepen our insights to hadron spectroscopy, constitute strong tests of the QCD inspired theoretical developements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective Lagrangian approaches are not suitable for searching for new baryon resonances because they cannot incorporate all known resonances, while constituent quark models are the appropriate framework. The claim that SAPHIR data for gamma p -> K+ Lambda provides evidence for a missing D13 resonance is contested, as the data can be explained by known resonances when off-shell effects for spin-3/2 resonances and hyperonic resonances are included.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares two families of formalisms for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction: tree-level effective Lagrangian approaches (Feynman diagrams with s-, u-, and t-channel exchanges) and constituent quark approaches based on broken SU(6) x O(3) symmetry. For the gamma p -> K+ Lambda channel, the author fits SAPHIR data using the Saclay-Lyon model with off-shell treatments and hyperonic resonances, and also uses a constituent quark model including all known nucleon and hyperon resonances. For the gamma p -> eta p channel, the author uses a constituent quark model with configuration mixings and fits data from MAMI/Mainz, Graal, and ELSA.

## Key numbers

- For the new S11 resonance in gamma p -> eta p: mass M = 1.729 GeV and total width Gamma = 183 MeV, close to a predicted third S11 resonance with M = 1.712 GeV and Gamma_T = 184 MeV
- The gamma p -> K+ Lambda total cross section data from SAPHIR shows a structure around W = 1.9 GeV that can be reproduced without a missing resonance
- In the gamma p -> eta p channel, the model I reproduces data up to W approximately 1.61 GeV, overestimates data between approximately 1.61 and 1.68 GeV, and underestimates data above 1.68 GeV
- The constituent quark model includes all known nucleon resonances with mass M_N* less than or equal to 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon resonances beyond traditional pionic processes (pi N -> pi N, eta N, gamma N -> pi N) analyzed by the Particle Data Group (2000) and partial wave analyses. It directly challenges the claim by Mart and Bennhold (2000) that SAPHIR data for gamma p -> K+ Lambda provides evidence for a missing D13 resonance. The paper enables future searches for new baryon resonances using constituent quark models, with extensions to eta electroproduction and vector meson electromagnetic production being in progress.
