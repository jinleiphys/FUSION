# A Guided Tour of Ab Initio Nuclear Many-Body Theory

> Over the last decade, new developments in Similarity Renormalization Group techniques and nuclear many-body methods have dramatically increased the capabilities of ab initio nuclear structure and reaction theory. Ground and excited-state properties can be computed up to the tin region, and from the proton to the presumptive neutron drip lines, providing unprecedented opportunities to confront two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral Effective Field Theory with experimental data. In this contribution, I will give a broad survey of the current status of nuclear many-body approaches, and I will use selected results to discuss both achievements and open issues that need to be addressed in the coming decade.

## Key claim

Over the past decade, ab initio nuclear many-body theory has expanded its reach to compute ground and excited-state properties up to the tin region and from the proton to the presumptive neutron drip lines, though the author notes this survey is "highly subjective" and cannot encompass the field in its entirety, serving instead as a jumping-off point for the cited literature.

## Method

The paper surveys the current status of ab initio nuclear many-body theory by reviewing three essential components: chiral Effective Field Theory interactions organized by a power counting expansion in (Q/Lambda), Similarity Renormalization Group techniques that continuously decouple low- and high-momentum physics via unitary transformations, and a variety of many-body methods including Many-Body Perturbation Theory, In-Medium SRG, Coupled Cluster, Self-Consistent Green's Function, and no-core configuration space approaches. Selected results from the past decade are presented to discuss both achievements and open issues.

## Key numbers

- Chiral EFT breakdown scale Lambda_chi is traditionally considered in the range 700-1000 MeV, though newer Bayesian analyses favor slightly lower values
- SRG flow is re-parameterized by lambda = s^{-1/4}, which characterizes the width of the band in momentum space and controls momentum transfers |k_i - k_f| less than or approximately lambda in a two-nucleon system
- Second-order Møller-Plesset MBPT ground-state energy calculation scales as O(N^4) in single-particle basis size N, or more favorably as O(N_p^2 N_h^2) with N_h approximately A much less than N_p

## Context

This review extends the line of work on ab initio nuclear structure that began with widespread adoption of Renormalization Group techniques and chiral EFT in the 2000s, following foundational developments by Bogner (2010), Machleidt (2016), and Epelbaum (2020). It documents progress from initial calculations at closed-shell nuclei by Hagen (2012), Hergert (2013), and Binder (2014) through to recent multi-shell valence space developments by Miyagi (2020) that will fill the open region between nickel and tin isotopic chains. The paper enables future work by identifying open issues in EFT regularization and renormalization, and by highlighting the need for improved interactions revealed by confronting chiral NN+3N forces with experimental data.
