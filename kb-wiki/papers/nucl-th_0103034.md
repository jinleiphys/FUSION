# Anatomy of nuclear shape transition in the relativistic mean field theory

> A detailed microscopic study of the temperature dependence of the shapes of some rare-earth nuclei is made in the relativistic mean field theory. Analyses of the thermal evolution of the single-particle orbitals and their occupancies leading to the collapse of the deformation are presented. The role of the non-linear $σ-$field on the shape transition in different nuclei is also investigated; in its absence the shape transition is found to be sharper.

# Anatomy of nuclear shape transition in the relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The sudden collapse of nuclear deformation with increasing temperature in rare-earth nuclei is not a universal feature; in lighter nuclei such as ⁶⁴Zn, the shape transition is significantly smoother and occurs at a higher temperature, even when the ground state deformation is nearly the same as in rare-earth systems.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 parameter set for even-even isotopes of Sm, Gd, and Dy with neutron numbers 86 and 88, and for ⁶⁴Zn and ⁶⁶Zn. Single-particle states were computed in a spherical oscillator basis with twelve shells, and pairing was included via the BCS approximation. The thermal evolution of quadrupole deformation β₂, single-particle level structures, occupancies, and heat capacity were analyzed, with additional calculations using the HS parameter set (which omits the non-linear σ-coupling term) to isolate its effect.

## Key numbers

- Shape transition temperature T_c = 1.15 MeV for ¹⁴⁸Sm and 1.6 MeV for ¹⁵⁰Sm (NL3 parameter set).
- Ground state deformation β₂⁰ increases significantly with addition of two neutrons (e.g., from ¹⁴⁸Sm to ¹⁵⁰Sm) but not with addition of two protons (¹⁴⁸Sm vs. ¹⁵⁰Gd).
- Deformation collapses within a temperature window of approximately 0.2 MeV near T_c for rare-earth nuclei.
- Shape transition temperature for ⁶⁴Zn is approximately 2.0 MeV, higher than the approximately 1.6 MeV for ¹⁵⁰Sm despite similar ground state deformations.
- With the HS parameter set (no non-linear σ-term), the spin-orbit splitting is larger by typically about 40% compared to the NL3 set.
- Shape transition temperatures in the range of approximately 1.0 to 1.8 MeV were found in prior non-relativistic calculations with pairing-plus-quadrupole interactions, while the RMF results yield noticeably higher values.

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies by the same group (Agrawal 2000a, 2000b) and provides a microscopic explanation for the thermal collapse of deformation that had been observed in macroscopic Landau theory approaches (Levit 1977, Alhassid 1984) and in non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations (Brack 1985, Quentin 1985) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with pairing-plus-quadrupole interactions (Goodman 1981, 1986; Egido 1993). The results agree with later HFB calculations using the realistic Gogny force (Egido 2000), which also found higher transition temperatures than the schematic models. The study enables future work on whether the smooth shape transition seen in lighter nuclei like ⁶⁴Zn is a general feature of nuclei outside the rare-earth region, and clarifies the role of the non-linear σ-field in determining the sharpness of the transition.
