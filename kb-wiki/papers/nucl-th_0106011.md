# The Character of Goldstone Bosons

> A succinct review of the QCD gap equation and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; their connection with Bethe-Salpeter equations and resolving the dichotomous nature of the pion; the calculation of the pion's valence-quark distribution; and first results for the pi-exchange contribution to the gamma N -> omega N cross-section, which is important in the search for missing nucleon resonances.

# The Character of Goldstone Bosons

## Key claim

The pion's dichotomous nature as both a Goldstone boson and a quark-antiquark bound state is resolved through the QCD gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, but only if the effective interaction possesses sufficient infrared strength: the interaction-tension must exceed approximately 4 GeV², a ten-fold enhancement over the critical value of 0.5 GeV² required for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking to occur at all.

## Method

The authors employ the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow approximation to the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the dressed-quark propagator, combined with the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states. The effective interaction is parametrised by an effective coupling whose infrared strength is characterised by the interaction-tension, and the model is used to compute the pion's valence-quark distribution function from handbag diagrams and the pi-exchange contribution to gamma N -> omega N photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass M_q is approximately 350 MeV, while m_pi/2 is only about 0.2 M_q (approximately 70 MeV), illustrating the pion's anomalously low mass.
- Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking does not occur for interaction-tension sigma^Delta less than about 0.5 GeV² (approximately 9 Lambda_QCD²), and reproducing observables requires sigma^Delta greater than about 4 GeV² (approximately 70 Lambda_QCD²).
- The vacuum quark condensate at renormalisation scale zeta = 1 GeV is required to be -(0.241 GeV)³, corresponding to a density of 1.8 fm⁻³ and a Cooper-pair-like radius of 0.76 fm.
- At a resolving scale of q_0 = 0.54 GeV, valence quarks with an active mass of 300 MeV carry 71% of the pion's momentum.
- The calculated moments of the pion valence-quark distribution at 2 GeV are: <x> = 0.24, <x²> = 0.098, <x³> = 0.049, compared with experimental values 0.24 ± 0.01, 0.10 ± 0.01, and 0.058 ± 0.004 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to hadron physics as developed by Roberts and collaborators (1997, 1999) and Maris and Tandy (1999), providing a unified framework that connects dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the Goldstone theorem, and observable hadron properties. The calculation of the pion's valence-quark distribution confronts Drell-Yan data from the E615 experiment, which suggests a (1-x) behaviour at large x that would challenge perturbative QCD's prediction of (1-x)². The omega-photoproduction results provide a parameter-free prediction for the pi-exchange contribution, enabling searches for missing nucleon resonances at JLab by comparison with the meson-exchange model of Lee (1999).
