# The Quark Spin Distributions of the Nucleon

> The quark helicity measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering is different from the quark spin in the rest frame of the nucleon. We point out that the quark spin distributions $Δq_{RF}(x)$ are connected with the quark helicity distributions $Δq(x)$ and the quark transversity distributions $δq(x)$ by an approximate relation: $Δq_{RF}(x) + Δq(x)=2 δq(x)$. This relation will be useful in order to measure the rest frame (or quark model) spin distributions of the nucleon once the quark helicity distributions and quark transversity distributions are themselves measured. We also calculate the $x$-dependent quark transversity distributions $δq(x)$ and quark spin distributions $Δq_{RF}(x)$ in a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model, and present discussions on possible effect from the sea quark-antiquark pairs.

# The Quark Spin Distributions of the Nucleon

## Key claim

The quark helicity distributions Δq(x) measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering are not identical to the quark spin distributions Δq_{RF}(x) in the nucleon rest frame; the two are connected to the quark transversity distributions δq(x) by the approximate relation Δq_{RF}(x) + Δq(x) = 2 δq(x), which holds at a model energy scale Q²₀ ≈ 1 to 5 GeV² and is derived from the Melosh-Wigner rotation effect in the light-cone formalism.

## Method

The authors derive the relation between quark helicity, transversity, and rest-frame spin distributions by applying the Melosh-Wigner rotation to the axial and tensor quark currents in the light-cone formalism, showing that the rotation factors satisfy 1 + M_q = 2\widetilde{M}_q. They then calculate the x-dependent quark transversity distributions δq(x) and quark spin distributions Δq_{RF}(x) for valence u and d quarks in a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model, using the Glück-Reya-Vogt parameterization of unpolarized quark distributions as input and incorporating mass differences between scalar and vector diquark spectators.

## Key numbers

- The relation Δq_{RF}(x) + Δq(x) = 2 δq(x) is considered valid at a model energy scale Q²₀ ≈ 1 to 5 GeV².
- Lattice QCD calculations give an axial charge ΔΣ = 0.18 ± 0.10 and a tensor charge δΣ = 0.562 ± 0.088; from Eq. (1) the spin carried by quarks from lattice QCD is Σ Δq_{RF} = 0.94 ± 0.28.
- The predicted strange tensor charge from meson-baryon fluctuations is δs ≈ -0.02 to -0.03, corresponding to a K⁺Λ probability P_{K⁺Λ} = 5 to 10%.
- The calculated quark spin distributions satisfy the inequality |Δq_{RF}(x)| ≥ |δq(x)| ≥ |Δq(x)| for valence quarks in this model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ma (1991) and Brodsky (1994), who first pointed out that quark helicity in polarized DIS differs from the quark spin in the nucleon rest frame due to the Melosh-Wigner rotation. The relation provides a way to measure the rest-frame quark spin distributions (or quark model spin distributions) once both helicity and transversity distributions are experimentally determined, and it connects to lattice QCD calculations of axial and tensor charges. The paper also discusses the role of sea quark-antiquark pairs from meson-baryon fluctuations, as studied by Brodsky (1996), which can produce spin asymmetries between strange quarks and antiquarks.
