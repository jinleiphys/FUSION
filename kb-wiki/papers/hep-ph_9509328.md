# Subthreshold Antiproton Spectra in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the structure of antiproton spectra at extreme subthreshold bombarding energies using a thermodynamic picture. Antiproton production processes and final state interactions are discussed in detail in order to find out what can be learned about these processes from the observed spectra.

## Key claim

Using a thermodynamic model, the authors show that subthreshold antiproton spectra in heavy ion collisions are dominated by production from a thermalized medium rather than pre-equilibrium processes, but the final observed spectra are largely insensitive to the production mechanism due to rapid thermalization from elastic scattering and strong absorption, making it difficult to extract quantitative information about medium properties.

## Method

The authors developed a hadrochemical model based on Montvay and Zimanyi (1993) that splits the reaction into an ignition phase and a spherically symmetric explosion phase, assuming local Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions for particles that have scattered at least once. They calculated antiproton production rates from baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and rho-rho channels using parametrized cross sections, modeled elastic scattering via a Fokker-Planck equation with coefficients derived from differential p-bar-p cross sections, and included annihilation using a thermal average of the annihilation cross section. The time-dependent chemical composition and expansion dynamics were then used to compute final antiproton spectra at decoupling.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for antiproton production in NN collisions is s >= (4m)^2 = 14.1 GeV^2, compared to the initial delta-like distribution at s = 5.5 GeV^2 for a bombarding energy of 1 GeV/A.
- The absorption rate A is of order 0.5 (fm/c)^(-1) at normal nuclear matter density, meaning 99% of antiprotons are absorbed within 9 fm/c.
- The elastic scattering thermalization time is about 10 fm/c for the spectrum to become practically thermalized in a medium at T=100 MeV and normal nuclear density.
- The pion-baryon channel cross section normalization is sigma^0_ij = 0.35 mb for pi- p -> n p p-bar collisions.
- The model reproduces the absolute order of magnitude of GSI data for Na-Na and Ni-Ni collisions at 2 GeV/nucleon kinetic beam energy, assuming a formation time of tau = 1 fm/c and that 70% of the incoming energy is thermalized.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach of Schurmann et al. (1990) and the hadrochemical model of Montvay and Zimanyi (1993) to subthreshold antiproton production, addressing the ambiguity noted by previous microscopic models (Teis et al., Batko et al., Spieles et al.) that could equally well describe GSI and BEVALAC data using different assumptions. The paper shows that meson-baryon channels, particularly pion-induced production as emphasized by Ko and Ge (1994), are crucial for reproducing observed yields without invoking medium effects on hadron masses, but concludes that turning antiproton production into a quantitative probe remains a serious challenge due to strong sensitivity to poorly known parameters like the antiproton formation time.
