# Anomalous A-dependence of diffractive leptoproduction of radial excitation $ρ'(2S)$

> We predict a strikingly different $A$ and $Q^{2}$ dependence of quasielastic leptoproduction of the $ρ^{0}(1S)$-meson and its radial excitation $ρ'(2S)$ on nuclei. Whereas for the $ρ^{0}$ production nuclear transparency $T_{A}$ decreases monotonically with $A$, for the $ρ'$ nuclear transparency $T_{A}$ can have the counterintuitive nonmonotonic $A$-dependence, having the minimum for light nuclei, and increasing with $A$ for medium and heavy nuclei. Strong enhancement of the $ρ' /ρ^{0}$ cross section ratio makes nuclear targets the $ρ'$-factory. The origin of the anomalous $A$-dependence is in the interplay of color transparency effects with the nodal structure of the $ρ'$ wave function. The predicted effects take place at moderate $Q^{2}\lsim (2-3)$GeV$^{2}$, which can be explored in high statistics experiments at CEBAF.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that nuclear transparency for diffractive leptoproduction of the radially excited rho-prime meson (rho'(2S)) on nuclei can exhibit a counterintuitive nonmonotonic A-dependence, with a minimum for light nuclei and an increase for medium and heavy nuclei, in contrast to the monotonic decrease seen for the ground-state rho(1S) meson. This anomalous behavior arises from the interplay of color transparency effects with the nodal structure of the rho'(2S) wave function and is predicted to occur at moderate Q^2 less than or approximately 2-3 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear transparency for quasielastic (incoherent) leptoproduction of rho(1S) and rho'(2S) mesons on nuclei using a lightcone dipole formalism. The production amplitude is expressed as an integral over the transverse separation r of the q-qbar pair, where the photon wave function provides an exponential cutoff at large r and the color-dipole cross section sigma(r) is proportional to r^2 at small r (color transparency), together defining a scanning radius r_S. The nuclear matrix element includes an attenuation factor exp[-1/2 sigma(r) T(b)] that depends on the optical thickness T(b) of the nucleus at impact parameter b, and the authors evaluate the resulting A-dependence using harmonic oscillator wave functions for the vector mesons and the dipole cross section from Ref. [10].

## Key numbers

* For the rho(1S) meson on a lead target, nuclear transparency T_Pb(Q^2=0) is approximately 0.1 and T_Pb(Q^2=2 GeV^2) is approximately 0.23.
* The predicted ratio sigma(gamma N -> Psi' N)/sigma(gamma N -> J/Psi N) is 0.17, in agreement with the NMC result of 0.20 +/- 0.05(stat) +/- 0.07(syst).
* The scanning radius is given by r_S = 6 / sqrt(m_V^2 + Q^2), and at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 the scanning radius is still large, r_S approximately 0.8 fm.
* The condition for the onset of color transparency for the rho(1S), rho'(2S) system requires neutrino energy nu greater than or approximately (3-4) * A^(1/3) GeV.
* The rho(1S) meson has sigma_tot(rho N) approximately 25 mb, essentially identical to sigma_tot(pi N).

## Context

This work extends the color transparency framework previously applied to J/Psi and Psi' production by the same authors (Nemchik, Nikolaev, Zakharov, 1994) and others, and builds on the FNAL E665 experiment that confirmed color transparency in exclusive rho(1S) photoproduction on nuclei. The paper predicts that the node effect in the rho'(2S) wave function, combined with the Q^2-dependent scanning radius driven by color transparency, produces a spectacular pattern of anomalous A and Q^2 dependence that can be tested at CEBAF upgraded to 8-12 GeV, where high-statistics experiments with the CLAS detector could make the first accurate measurements of rho' production on nuclei and turn nuclear targets into a "rho'-factory" for studying radially excited vector meson spectroscopy.
