# Generation of a scalar vortex in a rotational frame

> We consider generation from the vacuum of a scalar charged field in a rigidly rotating frame. Adding an external magnetic field opens the way to Bose condensation of the field. This phenomenon has been studied for external uniform magnetic field occupying the whole volume of the uniformly rotating cylindrical system of finite radius $R$ with a Dirichlet boundary condition imposed on it. Besides continuing this study, we consider the field formed by a flux tube of small radius. We find numerical solutions of the Ginzburg-Pitaevskii equation for the charged scalar field, the critical rotation frequencies, the mean radii and the condensate energies, and compare them with those found in a linearization scheme and with approximate analytical solutions. We show that for the same input parameters the energy of the condensate in the case of the flux tube is lower than in the case of uniform magnetic field in the whole cylinder.

# Generation of a scalar vortex in a rotational frame

## Key claim

The vacuum of a charged scalar field in a rigidly rotating frame becomes unstable to Bose condensation only when an external magnetic field is added to counteract the centrifugal force; the condensate energy is lower for a magnetic field confined to a thin flux tube than for a uniform magnetic field filling the entire cylinder, for the same input parameters.

## Method

The authors solve the Gross-Pitaevskii-like equation (a relativistic generalization of the Ginzburg-Pitaevskii equation) for a charged scalar field inside a uniformly rotating cylinder of radius R with Dirichlet boundary conditions, considering two magnetic field configurations: a uniform field throughout the cylinder and a thin flux tube of radius R_s much smaller than R. They compare numerical solutions of the full nonlinear equation with results from a linearization scheme (where the nonlinear term is replaced by a constant epsilon^2) and with approximate analytical solutions obtained by neglecting the curvature term in the radial equation.

## Key numbers

- Critical rotation frequency for the thin flux tube case with parameters m=1, R=10, delta_phi=50, l=50: Omega_cr approximately 0.021 (in units where m_pi=1)
- For the same parameters, the causality condition Omega R < 1 is satisfied up to epsilon approximately 4.8
- The linearization scheme and exact numerical solution coincide well for epsilon less than or approximately 1, corresponding to Omega R less than or approximately 0.29
- The first excited state solution (from root j_{nu,2}) appears only at Omega R approximately 0.56, epsilon approximately 2.61

## Context

This work extends studies of pion condensation in rotating systems motivated by heavy-ion collisions, where the STAR collaboration observed global polarization of Lambda hyperons with rotation frequency Omega approximately 10^22 Hz approximately 0.05 m_pi (Adamczyk et al.). It builds on prior work by Liu et al. (2018) and Guo et al. (2022) who studied Bose condensation due to rotation with homogeneous magnetic fields, and by Bordag (2025) who developed the linearization scheme used here. The results enable comparison of condensate properties for different magnetic field profiles and provide a benchmark for the linearization approximation against exact numerical solutions.
