# gamma* N -> Delta transition form factors: a new analysis of the JLab data on p(e,e'p)pi0 at Q^2=2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)^2

> Recent JLab data of the differential cross section for the reaction p(e,e'p)pi0 in the invariant mass region of 1.1 < W < 1.4 GeV at four-momentum transfer squared Q^2= 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)$^2$ are analyzed with two models, both of which give an excellent description of most of the existing pion electroproduction data below W < 1.5 GeV. We find that at up to Q^2 = 4.0(GeV/c)^2, the extracted helicity amplitudes A_{3/2} and A_{/2} remain comparable with each other, implying that hadronic helicity is not conserved at this range of Q^2. The ratios E_{1+}/M_{1+} obtained show, starting from a small and negative value at the real photon point, a clear tendency to cross zero, and to become positive with increasing Q^2. This is a possible indication of a very slow approach toward the pQCD region.Furthermore, we find that the helicity amplitude A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}, but not A_{3/2}, starts exhibiting the scaling behavior at about Q^2 \ge 2.5(GeV/c)^2.

## Key claim

The extracted ratio \(R_{EM} = E_{1+}/M_{1+}\) for the \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition, starting from a small negative value at the real photon point, shows a clear tendency to cross zero and become positive with increasing \(Q^2\), in contrast to the original analysis of the same data which found it remaining negative and tending toward more negative values. This is presented as a possible indication of a very slow approach toward the perturbative QCD region, not as a definitive observation.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed the JLab differential cross section data for \(p(e,e'p)\pi^0\) at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and \(4.0\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) using two models: the unitary isobar model MAID (a new version of the Mainz model) and a dynamical model developed by Kamalov and Yang (1999). Both models describe the nonresonant background and the resonant \(\Delta(1232)\) contribution, with the resonant multipoles expressed via Breit-Wigner forms and the background treated with K-matrix unitarization. All 751 data points at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and 867 points at \(Q^2 = 4.0\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) in the invariant mass range \(1.1 < W < 1.4\) GeV were included in a global fit to extract the electromagnetic multipoles and helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 2.8\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), \(R_{EM}^{(p\pi^0)} = -0.56 \pm 0.33\%\) (MAID) and \(-1.28 \pm 0.32\%\) (dynamical model).
- At \(Q^2 = 4.0\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), \(R_{EM}^{(p\pi^0)} = 0.09 \pm 0.50\%\) (MAID) and \(-0.84 \pm 0.46\%\) (dynamical model), showing a sign change in the MAID result.
- At \(Q^2 = 2.8\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), \(R_{SM}^{(p\pi^0)} = -9.14 \pm 0.54\%\) (MAID) and \(-11.65 \pm 0.52\%\) (dynamical model).
- At \(Q^2 = 4.0\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), \(R_{SM}^{(p\pi^0)} = -13.37 \pm 0.95\%\) (MAID) and \(-17.70 \pm 1.0\%\) (dynamical model).
- The magnetic form factor \(G_M^* \times 100\) is \(6.78 \pm 0.05\) at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and \(2.86 \pm 0.02\) at \(Q^2 = 4.0\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) (MAID).
- The maximal value for \(Q^3 A_{1/2}^{\Delta}\) obtained is about \(-0.11\ \text{GeV}^{5/2}\), lying between the asymptotic values of \(-0.08\) and \(-0.17\ \text{GeV}^{5/2}\) discussed by Carlson (1986).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the JLab data originally reported by Frolov et al. (1999), which used two different extraction methods and found \(R_{EM}\) remaining small and negative. The present analysis uses the improved MAID model and the dynamical model of Kamalov and Yang (1999), both of which give smaller \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom than the original fits. The results contradict the earlier conclusion that \(R_{EM}\) becomes more negative with \(Q^2\), instead showing a zero crossing that suggests a very slow approach toward the perturbative QCD regime. The finding that \(A_{1/2}^{\Delta}\) and \(S_{1/2}^{\Delta}\) begin to exhibit pQCD scaling behavior at \(Q^2 \ge 2.5\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) while \(A_{3/2}^{\Delta}\) does not indicates that scaling may set in earlier than hadronic helicity conservation, motivating further measurements at higher momentum transfer.
