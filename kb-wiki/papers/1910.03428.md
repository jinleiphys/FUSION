# Lifetime estimations and a non-monotonic initial energy density in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We highlight some connections between the final state hadronic observables and the initial conditions using a recently found new exact family of solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics. These relations provide explicit examples of the scaling behaviour in relativistic hydrodynamics and may provide an advanced estimate of the lifetime and the initial energy density in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4$, $130$, and $200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.0$ TeV Pb+Pb and $5.44$ TeV Xe+Xe as well as $\sqrt{s} = 7$, $8$ and $13$ TeV p+p collisions at LHC energies. A surprising result is that these advanced estimates yield a non-monotonic increase of the initial energy density with increasing collision energy at the RHIC energy range.

# Lifetime estimations and a non-monotonic initial energy density in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

Using a new exact family of solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics (the CKCJ solution), the authors provide advanced estimates of the lifetime and initial energy density that correct Bjorken's oversimplified estimate; these estimates yield a surprising non-monotonic increase of the initial energy density with increasing collision energy in the RHIC energy range, which is presented as preliminary and treated carefully due to limitations of the CKCJ solution.

## Method

The authors fit the CKCJ solution to experimental pseudorapidity density distributions, transverse mass spectra, and longitudinal HBT radii from Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, and p+p collisions at LHC. From these fits they extract the acceleration parameter lambda, the effective temperature, and the lifetime parameter tau_f, then use an analytic formula derived from the CKCJ solution to compute the initial energy density corrected for non-boost-invariant expansion and pressure work.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density estimates were performed at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC in the 0-30% centrality class
- The speed of sound used is c_s = 0.35 +/- 0.05, corresponding to kappa = 10 (+1/-3), as measured by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare 2006)
- Fits to CMS Xe+Xe data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV in the 0-80% centrality class are shown
- A lower limit of the initial energy density was estimated for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in the 10-20% centrality class
- The non-monotonic behavior of HBT-radii in the RHIC beam energy scan points to a QCD critical point near mu_B approximately 95 MeV, corresponding to sqrt(s_NN) approximately 47.5 GeV (Lacey 2014)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers on the CKCJ exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics (Csorgo 2018, Kasza 2018) and applies them to experimental data from RHIC and LHC. The method corrects the Bjorken estimate (Bjorken 1982) by accounting for finite pseudorapidity width and pressure work during expansion. The non-monotonic behavior of the initial energy density with collision energy may serve as a new tool to search for the QCD critical point, and the results are cross-checked against 1+3 dimensional numerical hydrodynamics with lattice QCD equation of state (Bozek 2009), showing surprisingly good agreement. Further studies are needed to investigate shock-wave effects at lower energies, proper-time dependence of the acceleration parameter, and temperature dependence of the speed of sound.
