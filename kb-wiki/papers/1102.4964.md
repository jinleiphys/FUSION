# Hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter with dynamical quark masses

> The two-Equation of State (EoS) model is used to describe the hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter formed at high density in heavy-ion collisions. For the quark phase, the three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective theory is used to investigate the influence of dynamical quark mass effects on the phase transition. At variance to the MIT-Bag results, with fixed current quark masses, the main important effect of the chiral dynamics is the appearance of an End-Point for the coexistence zone. We show that a first order hadron-quark phase transition may take place in the region T=(50-80)MeV and ρ_B=(2-4)ρ_0, which is possible to be probed in the new planned facilities, such as FAIR at GSI-Darmstadt and NICA at JINR-Dubna. From isospin properties of the mixed phase somepossible signals are suggested. The importance of chiral symmetry and dynamical quark mass on the hadron-quark phase transition is stressed. The difficulty of an exact location of Critical-End-Point comes from its appearance in a region of competition between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, where our knowledge of effective QCD theories is still rather uncertain.

## Key claim

Using the NJL model with dynamical quark masses instead of the MIT-Bag model with fixed current masses, the hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter shows an End-Point for the coexistence zone, and a first-order transition may occur in the region T = (50-80) MeV and ρ_B = (2-4) ρ_0, but the exact location of the Critical-End-Point remains uncertain due to the competition between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement.

## Method

The authors used a two-Equation of State (EoS) model with Gibbs criteria for the mixed phase, combining a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach for the hadron phase (with NLρ and NLρδ parametrizations including σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons) and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective theory for the quark phase. The NJL model parameters (Λ = 603.2 MeV, GΛ² = 1.835, KΛ⁵ = 12.36, m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV) were taken from Rehberg, Klevansky, and Hüfner (1995) by fitting f_π, M_π, m_K, and m_η to experimental values, with the strange quark chemical potential set to zero to enforce net strangeness conservation.

## Key numbers

- First-order hadron-quark phase transition region: T = (50-80) MeV and ρ_B = (2-4) ρ_0, accessible at FAIR (GSI-Darmstadt) and NICA (JINR-Dubna)
- NJL model parameters: Λ = 603.2 MeV, GΛ² = 1.835, KΛ⁵ = 12.36, m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV
- Dynamical quark masses of u,d quarks reach approximately 200 MeV at ρ ∼ ρ_0 for some parameter sets
- MIT-Bag constants used for comparison: (B_MIT)^(1/4) = 160 MeV and 190 MeV
- Isospin asymmetry parameter α = 0.2 used in calculations, corresponding to values just above 0.2 for neutron-rich stable beams (e.g., α = 0.227 in ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions)
- Symmetry energy of quark matter is much smaller than that of hadron matter at T = 0 and T = 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in asymmetric matter by Müller et al. (1997), Di Toro et al. (2006, 2009), Pagliara et al. (2010), and Cavagnoli et al. (2010), which used MIT-Bag models with fixed current quark masses. The key advance is incorporating chiral dynamics via the NJL model, which introduces an End-Point for the coexistence zone absent in MIT-Bag calculations. The results enable predictions for experimental signals at FAIR and NICA, particularly through isospin distillation effects in the mixed phase, and suggest that the symmetry energy difference between hadron and quark phases (larger with the NLρδ parametrization) reduces the onset density of the phase transition.
