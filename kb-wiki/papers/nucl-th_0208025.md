# Scalar ground-state observables in the random phase approximation

> We calculate the ground-state expectation value of scalar observables in the matrix formulation of the random phase approximation (RPA). Our expression, derived using the quasiboson approximation, is a straightforward generalization of the RPA correlation energy. We test the reliability of our expression by comparing against full diagonalization in 0 h-bar omega shell-model spaces. In general the RPA values are an improvement over mean-field (Hartree-Fock) results, but are not always consistent with shell-model results. We also consider exact symmetries broken in the mean-field state and whether or not they are restored in RPA.

## Key claim

The RPA expression for ground-state expectation values of scalar observables, derived using the quasiboson approximation, generally improves upon mean-field (Hartree-Fock) results but is not always consistent with exact shell-model results; the improvement is unreliable and can even yield nonphysical values, especially for deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the RPA ground-state expectation value of a general scalar operator by expanding the operator in the harmonic-oscillator boson representation around the Hartree-Fock minimum, analogous to the derivation of the RPA correlation energy. They tested this expression by comparing RPA values against full diagonalization in complete 0 hbar omega shell-model spaces using realistic interactions (Wildenthal USD in the sd shell and monopole-modified Kuo-Brown KB3 in the pf shell), computing expectation values of one-body operators (occupation numbers) and two-body operators (S^2, L^2, Q^2, and the pairing Hamiltonian P^dagger P).

## Key numbers

* For the one-body operator n(d_{3/2})+n(s_{1/2}) in spherical nuclei: RPA gave 0.67 vs shell-model 0.54 for ^22O, and RPA gave 2.50 vs shell-model 2.25 for ^24O; for deformed ^24Mg, RPA gave 1.85 vs shell-model 2.31 (HF was 2.13).
* For S^2 in ^20Ne: HF 0.35, RPA 0.33, shell-model 0.26; for ^28Si: HF 1.62, RPA 1.28, shell-model 1.45.
* For L^2 in ^20Ne: HF 15.90, RPA -0.25, shell-model 0.26; for ^24Mg: HF 20.65, RPA -1.17, shell-model 1.03.
* For the pairing operator P^dagger P in ^20Ne: HF 2.99, RPA 5.47, shell-model 6.81; in ^24O: HF 4.00, RPA 6.52, shell-model 6.58.
* For Q^2 in ^20Ne: HF 715, RPA 825, shell-model 793; in ^24O: HF 122, RPA 194, shell-model 191.
* For J^2 in ^20Ne: HF 16.06, RPA -0.45, shell-model 0; in ^22O (spherical HF): HF 0.00, RPA 7.99, shell-model 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic test of RPA correlation energies against shell-model results (Stetcu and Johnson, 2002) to the expectation values of general scalar observables. The RPA expression generalizes the one-particle density derived by Rowe (1970) for spherical nuclei. The results show that RPA provides only an approximate and unreliable restoration of broken symmetries, such as rotational invariance, and that the RPA ground state of even-even deformed nuclei retains a significant quadrupole moment, indicating incomplete symmetry restoration. This work is part of a larger program to rigorously test the reliability of RPA as an approximation for global nuclear structure calculations, where mean-field theory remains the standard approach.
