# A Tale of Two and Three Bodies

> 6 pages LaTeX2e with 3 figures, using ws-p8-50x6-00.cls (included), epsfig (4 .eps figures) and feynmp (necessary Metapost files included). Talk held at the Conference Bologna 2000 - Structure of the Nucleus at the Dawn of the Century, Bologna (Italy) 29th May -- 3rd June 2000; to be published in the Proceedings

## Key claim
Effective Field Theory (EFT) methods can systematically describe two- and three-nucleon systems at low energies using a power counting that treats contact interactions as leading order and pion exchange as a perturbative correction, but this scheme relies on accommodating unnaturally large scattering lengths through fine-tuning of the contact interaction coefficients.

## Method
The author constructs an EFT with non-relativistic nucleons and pions as the relevant degrees of freedom, using a chirally invariant Lagrangian with contact interactions and pion-nucleon couplings. A power counting scheme is established where the small parameter is the typical momentum Q divided by the breakdown scale Lambda, with contact interactions at leading order summed non-perturbatively and pion exchange treated as a perturbation at higher orders. The Faddeev equation for the three-body system is solved numerically, comparing results between the theory with explicit pions and the theory with pions integrated out.

## Key numbers
- Scattering lengths: 1/a(1S0) = -8.3 MeV, 1/a(3S1) = 36 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.225 MeV, corresponding to binding momentum gamma = sqrt(M B) ≈ 46 MeV
- Elastic deuteron Compton scattering cross section calculated to NLO with accuracy of ±10% at incident photon energies of 49 MeV and 69 MeV
- Comparative study of nd scattering in spin quartet S wave performed for momenta up to 300 MeV in the center-of-mass frame (E_cm ≈ 70 MeV)
- Pion-less theory calculations of higher partial waves l=1,...,4 in spin quartet and doublet channels show convergence within the theoretical uncertainty

## Context
This work extends the EFT approach to nuclear systems originally suggested by Weinberg, using the power counting scheme developed by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) to handle the fine-tuning of large scattering lengths. The results for nd scattering are compared with realistic potential model calculations and pd phase shift analysis, showing agreement within theoretical uncertainty. The approach enables systematic higher-order calculations of problematic spin observables like the Ay problem, where EFT will differ from potential model calculations due to the inclusion of three-body forces.
