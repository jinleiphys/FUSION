# Entanglement and collective flavor oscillations in a dense neutrino gas

> We investigate the importance of going beyond the mean-field approximation in the dynamics of collective neutrino oscillations. To expand our understanding of the coherent neutrino oscillation problem, we apply concepts from many-body physics and quantum information theory. Specifically, we use measures of nontrivial correlations (otherwise known as "entanglement") between the constituent neutrinos of the many-body system, such as the entanglement entropy and the Bloch vector of the reduced density matrix. The relevance of going beyond the mean field is demonstrated by comparisons between the evolution of the neutrino state in the many-body picture vs the mean-field limit, for different initial conditions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that deviations between the many-body and mean-field descriptions of collective neutrino oscillations are correlated with the entanglement entropy of the system, but the authors hedge that while large entanglement often accompanies large deviations, the converse is not always true: some cases with large entanglement still yield small deviations from mean-field theory.

## Method

The authors study a toy model of N neutrinos in two flavors (e and x) with discrete energy bins, using the single-angle approximation where the collective interaction strength mu depends only on radius r. They obtain adiabatic eigenvalues and eigenstates of the many-body Hamiltonian using the Richardson-Gaudin algebraic approach, then evolve the system from various initial configurations and compare the asymptotic (large r) values of the z-component of the polarization vector P_z between the many-body and mean-field calculations, while also computing the entanglement entropy of individual neutrinos with the rest of the ensemble.

## Key numbers

* The neutrino sphere radius R_nu is set to 60 km.
* The base oscillation frequency omega_0 is 1.055 x 10^{-16} MeV.
* The collective interaction strength at the neutrino sphere, mu(R_nu), is 3.62 x 10^4 omega_0.
* The mixing angle sin^2(2theta) is 0.10.
* Systems with up to N=9 neutrinos are studied, with N=M (one neutrino per energy bin) and frequencies omega_p = p omega_0.
* For the initial state of N electron neutrinos at mu_0 = 5 omega_0 (r_0 approximately 6.6 R_nu), the discrepancy between many-body and mean-field asymptotic P_z for the highest-frequency neutrino grows with increasing N for the small N values examined.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the neutrino many-body Hamiltonian using the Richardson-Gaudin approach (Pehlivan 2011, Birol 2018, Patwardhan 2019) and earlier investigations of many-body effects in collective oscillations (Bell 2003, Friedland 2003). It goes beyond prior work by quantifying entanglement entropy as a diagnostic for deviations from mean-field theory, and by examining multiple initial configurations for systems up to N=9. The results suggest that mean-field approximations may be inadequate for certain initial conditions in dense neutrino gases, with implications for interpreting supernova neutrino signals in experiments such as DUNE.
