# Gluon Radiation and Coherent States in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We explore the correspondence between classical gluon radiation and quantum radiation in a coherent state for gluons produced in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. The expectation value of the invariant momentum distribution of gluons in the coherent state is found to agree with the gluon number distribution obtained classically from the solution of the Yang-Mills equations. A criterion for the applicability of the coherent state formalism to the problem of radiation in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is discussed. This criterion is found to be fulfilled for midrapidity gluons with perturbative transverse momenta larger than about 1-2 GeV and produced in collisions between valence partons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that classical gluon radiation computed from Yang-Mills equations agrees with the quantum radiation described by a coherent state, but only under the condition that the number of emitted gluons is small compared to the number of colliding color charges, specifically when the squared expectation value of the total gluon number is much less than twice the mass number of the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a coherent state for gluons produced in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions using the classical source current derived from the Yang-Mills equations to next-to-lowest order in the coupling constant g. They compute the expectation value of the invariant momentum distribution of gluons in this coherent state and compare it with the classical gluon number distribution obtained from solving the Yang-Mills equations. The validity of the coherent state formalism is assessed by analyzing the combinatorics of multiple collisions, where terms corresponding to multiple collisions of the same charge are identified as sources of correlations that the coherent state approach neglects.

## Key numbers

- The criterion for applicability of the coherent state formalism is that the squared expectation value of the total gluon number satisfies \(\bar{N}^2 \ll 2A\), where A is the mass number of the colliding nuclei (assuming \(A_1 \simeq A_2 \gg 1\)).
- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, the coherent state approach is applicable for midrapidity gluons with perturbative transverse momenta larger than about 1-2 GeV produced in collisions between valence partons.
- The number of possible pairs of colliding charges is \(4 A_1 A_2\), corresponding to the number of terms in the source current \(\tilde{J}^{(3)a}_\mu\).
- The probability to find n gluons in the final state follows a Poisson distribution \(P_n = e^{-\bar{N}} \bar{N}^n / n!\), where \(\bar{N}\) is the expectation value of the total gluon number.

## Context

This work extends the classical approach to gluon radiation in nuclear collisions developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the detailed derivation by Kovchegov and Rischke (1997) to next-to-lowest order. It establishes a formal connection between the classical Yang-Mills description and quantum coherent states, showing that the classical result for the gluon number distribution agrees with the quantum calculation of soft gluon emission by colliding color charges from Gunion and Bertsch (1982). The paper enables future work to determine whether the coherent state approximation remains valid for larger gluon multiplicities expected at higher collision energies, and provides a framework for computing corrections from multiple collisions of the same charge.
