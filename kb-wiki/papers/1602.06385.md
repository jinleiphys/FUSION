# Gamow-Teller strength distributions and neutrino energy loss rates due to chromium isotopes in stellar matter

> Gamow-Teller transitions in isotopes of chromium play a consequential role in the presupernova evolution of massive stars. $β$-decay and electron capture rates on chromium isotopes significantly affect the time rate of change of lepton fraction ($\dot{Y_{e}}$). Fine-tuning of this parameter is one of the key for simulating a successful supernova explosion. The (anti)neutrinos produced as a result of electron capture and $β$-decay are transparent to stellar matter during presupernova phases. They carry away energy and this result in cooling the stellar core. In this paper we present the calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions and (anti)neutrino energy loss rates due to weak interactions on chromium isotopes of astrophysical importance. We compare our results with measured data and previous calculations wherever available.

# Gamow-Teller strength distributions and neutrino energy loss rates due to chromium isotopes in stellar matter

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model produces Gamow-Teller strength distributions and (anti)neutrino energy loss rates for chromium isotopes that are systematically larger than previous large-scale shell model and FFN calculations, especially at low stellar temperatures and densities characteristic of hydrostatic phases of stellar evolution, which may affect the lepton-to-baryon ratio going into stellar collapse.

## Method
The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a separable interaction to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions for 11 chromium isotopes (50-60Cr) in both the beta-decay and electron capture directions. Single-particle energies and wave functions were computed using the Nilsson model with nuclear deformation, pairing was treated in the BCS approximation, and the calculated GT strengths were multiplied by a quenching factor of (0.6)^2. Neutrino and antineutrino energy loss rates were then computed from these strength distributions using phase space integrals that depend on stellar temperature, density, and electron Fermi energy, with contributions from electron capture, positron capture, electron emission, and positron emission channels.

## Key numbers
- Total B(GT)+ strength for 56Cr calculated by pn-QRPA: 1.31 (compared to SMMC(KB3) shell model result of 1.5 ± 0.21)
- pn-QRPA places GT strength centroid at 5.41 MeV excitation energy in 52Mn for 52Cr beta-decay direction
- Neutrino energy loss rates due to 50Cr from pn-QRPA are more than two orders of magnitude (factor 43) bigger than LSSM rates at T9 = 1 K at low and medium-low densities (ρYe = 10^2 and 10^5 g cm^-3)
- At T9 = 30 K, pn-QRPA rates for 50Cr are still factor 5 bigger than LSSM and factor 2 bigger than FFN rates
- For 58Cr, FFN rates are up to 7 orders of magnitude smaller than pn-QRPA rates at some densities and temperatures
- Calculated rates are tabulated for densities log(ρYe) from 10^2 to 10^11 g cm^-3 and temperatures T9 from 1 to 30 (10^9 K)

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak interaction rates previously performed by Nabi and Klapdor (1999) for 709 nuclei, applying the model specifically to chromium isotopes identified as astrophysically important by Aufderheide et al. (1994) and Heger et al. (2001). The calculations are compared with the earlier FFN work of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1982) and the large-scale shell model calculations of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000). The authors find that their GT strength distributions place centroids at lower excitation energies than shell model results, leading to enhanced neutrino energy loss rates that may be important for presupernova evolution simulations. The results are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulation codes that require reliable weak interaction rates for chromium isotopes.
