# $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ $ΣNN$ quasibound state

> JLab has recently found indications of the possible existence of a $ΣNN$ resonance at $(3.14 \pm 0.84) - i (2.28 \pm 1.2)$ MeV. In the past, using models that exploit symmetries between the two-baryon sector with and without strangeness, hyperon-nucleon interactions have been derived that reproduce the experimental data of the strangeness $-1$ sector. We make use of these interactions to review existing Faddeev studies of the $ΛNN$-$ΣNN$ system that show theoretical evidences about a $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ $ΣNN$ quasibound state near threshold. The calculated position of the pole is at 2.92$\,-i\,$2.17 MeV, in reasonable agreement with the experimental findings.

# $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ $\Sigma NN$ quasibound state

## Key claim

The authors report that a Faddeev calculation using hyperon-nucleon interactions derived from a chiral constituent quark model predicts a $\Sigma NN$ quasibound state with pole position at $2.92 - i\,2.17$ MeV, which lies within the error bars of the experimental resonance reported by the Hall A Collaboration at Jefferson Lab at $(3.14 \pm 0.84) - i (2.28 \pm 1.2)$ MeV, but the theoretical result is not identical to the central experimental value.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the coupled $\Lambda NN - \Sigma NN$ three-body system using hyperon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from the chiral quark cluster model, which exploits symmetries between the two-baryon sector with and without strangeness. The calculation included full $\Lambda \leftrightarrow \Sigma$ conversion, tensor forces coupling $S$ and $D$ waves, and all three-body configurations with $S$ and $D$ wave components. The $\Lambda N$ spin-triplet and spin-singlet scattering lengths were constrained by requiring correct description of $YN$ cross sections and the hypertriton binding energy within its experimental range.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of the $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ $\Sigma NN$ quasibound state: $2.92 - i\,2.17$ MeV (reference model)
- Experimental resonance reported by Hall A Collaboration: $(3.14 \pm 0.84) - i (2.28 \pm 1.2)$ MeV
- Hypertriton binding energy: $B_{0,1/2}=0.130 \pm 0.050$ MeV (experimental value); reference model gives 129 keV
- $\Lambda N$ spin-triplet scattering length range: $1.41 \le a^{\Lambda N}_{1/2,1} \le 1.58$ fm
- $\Lambda N$ spin-singlet scattering length range: $2.33 \le a^{\Lambda N}_{1/2,0} \le 2.48$ fm
- Reference model values: $a^{\Lambda N}_{1/2,1}=1.41$ fm, $a^{\Lambda N}_{1/2,0}=2.48$ fm
- Hypertriton binding energy with only $S$ wave three-body channels: 89 keV (out of experimental range)

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev studies of the $\Lambda NN - \Sigma NN$ system by Garcilazo and Valcarce (2007) and uses interactions from the chiral quark cluster model of Valcarce et al. (2005). The prediction of a $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ $\Sigma NN$ quasibound state near threshold was previously suggested in a variational calculation by Koi and collaborators (1996) and by Harada and Hirabayashi (2014) using a distorted-wave impulse approximation. The results are directly comparable to the recent experimental indications from the Hall A Collaboration at Jefferson Lab (Pandey et al., 2022), and the theoretical pole lies within the experimental error bars, supporting the interpretation of the observed enhancement as a $\Sigma^0 nn$ state. Future experiments are needed to confirm the isospin assignment.
