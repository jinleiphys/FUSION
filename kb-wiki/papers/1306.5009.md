# Project X: Physics Opportunities

> Part 2 of "Project X: Accelerator Reference Design, Physics Opportunities, Broader Impacts". In this Part, we outline the particle-physics program that can be achieved with Project X, a staged superconducting linac for intensity-frontier particle physics. Topics include neutrino physics, kaon physics, muon physics, electric dipole moments, neutron-antineutron oscillations, new light particles, hadron structure, hadron spectroscopy, and lattice-QCD calculations. Part 1 is available as arXiv:1306.5022 [physics.acc-ph] and Part 3 is available as arXiv:1306.5024 [physics.acc-ph].

## Key claim

Project X, a staged superconducting linac for intensity-frontier particle physics, would enable a broad program of electric dipole moment (EDM) searches across multiple systems (neutrons, atoms, molecules, protons, muons, and light nuclei) that could probe new sources of CP violation at energy scales well beyond the TeV scale, with the next generation of EDM experiments anticipating a factor of 100 in increased sensitivity and improving the energy reach by a factor of 10.

## Method

The paper surveys the theoretical frameworks connecting TeV-scale CP-violating sources to observable EDMs in various systems, including the effective Lagrangian approach with operators of dimension five and six, chiral perturbation theory for hadronic matrix elements, and nuclear structure calculations for Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei. It then reviews the experimental techniques for EDM searches across different systems: ultracold neutrons in bottles, atomic vapor cells (e.g., 199Hg), molecular beam experiments (e.g., YbF), muon storage rings, and proposed proton storage ring experiments at Project X, along with plans for rare isotope EDM measurements in 225Ra and 221/223Rn.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM limit: |d_n| < 2.9 × 10^(-26) e-cm at 90% C.L. (ultracold neutrons in a bottle, Baker 2006)
- Electron EDM limit: |d_e| < 1.05 × 10^(-27) e-cm at 90% C.L. (YbF molecules in a beam, Hudson 2011)
- Muon EDM limit: |d_mu| < 1.9 × 10^(-19) e-cm at 95% C.L. (muon storage ring, Bennett 2008)
- 199Hg atomic EDM limit: |d_nucleus| < 3.1 × 10^(-29) e-cm at 95% C.L. (199Hg atoms in a vapor cell, Griffith 2009)
- Inferred proton EDM limit: [7.9 × 10^(-25)] e-cm from 199Hg (Griffith 2009)
- Standard Model predictions: neutron EDM ~10^(-31) to 10^(-33) e-cm, electron EDM ~10^(-38) e-cm, muon EDM ~10^(-35) e-cm
- 225Ra Schiff moment enhancement estimated at factor of 500 relative to 199Hg for isoscalar contributions (a_0 = 0.01 for 199Hg, a_0 = 5 for 225Ra)
- 205Tl EDM limit: |d_Tl| ≤ 9.4 × 10^(-25) e-cm at 90% C.L., yielding |d_e| ≤ 1.6 × 10^(-27) e-cm at 90% C.L. (Regan 2002)
- Strong CP parameter bound: theta_bar < 10^(-10) from 199Hg (Griffith 2009)
- 225Ra half-life: 14.9 days, with 55 keV spacing between ground 1/2+ state and lowest 1/2- state

## Context

This paper extends the physics case for EDM searches within the broader Project X accelerator concept, building on the theoretical framework reviewed in Engel (2013) and Pospelov (2005) for connecting EDMs to TeV-scale CP violation. It complements the experimental programs at the LHC and B-factories by offering access to CP-violating phases beyond the CKM mechanism, which is known from experiments at B-factories and the Tevatron to be the dominant source of flavor and CP violation in flavor-changing processes (Charles 2004, Isidori 2010). The work enables a systematic program of EDM measurements across multiple systems that could potentially disentangle the sources of CP violation, as proposed by de Vries (2011), and would probe new physics at energy scales of some 3 TeV with current experiments, improving to approximately 30 TeV with next-generation sensitivity.
