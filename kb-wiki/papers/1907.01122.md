# Lepton scattering from $^{40}$Ar and $^{48}$Ti in the quasielastic peak region

> Neutron and proton spectral functions of $^{40}$Ar, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{48}$Ti isotopes are computed using the ab initio self-consistent Green's function approach. The resulting radii and charge distributions are in good agreement with available experimental data. The spectral functions of Ar and Ti are then utilized to calculate inclusive ($e$,$e$') cross sections within a factorization scheme and are found to correctly reproduce the recent Jefferson Lab measurements. Based on these successful agreements, the weak charged and neutral current double-differential cross sections for neutrino-$^{40}$Ar scattering are predicted in the quasielastic region. Results obtained by replacing the (experimentally inaccessible) neutron spectral distribution of $^{40}$Ar with the (experimentally accessible) proton distribution of $^{48}$Ti are compared and the accuracy of this approximation is assessed.

## Key claim

The authors compute *ab initio* spectral functions for \(^{40}\)Ar and \(^{48}\)Ti and show that, after accounting for energy shifts from the Coulomb potential, the proton spectral function of \(^{48}\)Ti is a very accurate proxy for the neutron spectral function of \(^{40}\)Ar in inclusive neutrino scattering, with discrepancies below 1–2% at 1 GeV.

## Method

The one-hole spectral functions for \(^{40}\)Ar, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{48}\)Ti were computed using the *ab initio* Gorkov self-consistent Green’s function (GGF) method at the ADC(2) truncation level, with the chiral NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) Hamiltonian. Inclusive (\(e\),\(e'\)) cross sections were calculated within the impulse approximation using a factorization scheme that folds the spectral functions with one-body current operators, and final-state interactions were included via a Glauber-type folding function. The same formalism was then applied to predict neutral- and charged-current neutrino-\(^{40}\)Ar cross sections at 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Charge radii: 3.43(3) fm for \(^{40}\)Ar, 3.52(4) fm for \(^{40}\)Ca, and 3.60(4) fm for \(^{48}\)Ti, compared to experimental values of 3.427, 3.477, and 3.607 fm, respectively.
* Point proton and neutron radii for \(^{40}\)Ar: \(r_p = 3.33(3)\) fm and \(r_n = 3.41(4)\) fm, giving a neutron skin thickness of 0.08(1) fm.
* The approximation of replacing the neutron spectral function of \(^{40}\)Ar with the proton spectral function of \(^{48}\)Ti yields discrepancies below 1% for neutral-current and below 2% for charged-current neutrino scattering at 1 GeV.
* The quasielastic peak for neutrino-\(^{40}\)Ar scattering at 1 GeV is centered at an energy transfer of about 150 MeV and extends up to approximately 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* GGF approach to open-shell nuclei relevant for neutrino detectors, following earlier applications to \(^{16}\)O (Rocco 2018) and to charge densities of Ca isotopes (Hagen 2016, Lapoux 2016). The computed electron-scattering cross sections are validated against recent Jefferson Lab data (Dai 2018, Dai 2019), which had motivated the use of titanium as a proxy for argon neutrons. The results quantitatively confirm the isospin-symmetry approximation proposed in those experimental studies and provide first-principles predictions for the DUNE neutrino oscillation experiment.
