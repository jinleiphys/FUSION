# Universality of the Diffusion Wake from Stopped and Punch-Through Jets in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We solve (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamical equations with source terms that describe punch-through and fully stopped jets in order to compare their final away-side angular correlations in a static medium. For fully stopped jets, the backreaction of the medium is described by a simple Bethe-Bloch-like model which leads to an explosive burst of energy and momentum (Bragg peak) close to the end of the jet's evolution through the medium. Surprisingly enough, we find that the medium's response and the corresponding away-side angular correlations are largely insensitive to whether the jet punches through or stops inside the medium. This result is also independent of whether momentum deposition is longitudinal (as generally occurs in pQCD energy loss models) or transverse (as the Bethe-Bloch formula implies). The existence of the diffusion wake is therefore shown to be universal to all scenarios where momentum as well as energy is deposited into the medium, which can readily be understood in ideal hydrodynamics through vorticity conservation. The particle yield coming from the strong forward moving diffusion wake that is formed in the wake of both punch-through and stopped jets largely overwhelms their weak Mach cone signal after freeze-out.

## Key claim

The medium's response and away-side angular correlations are largely insensitive to whether the jet punches through or stops inside the medium, and also independent of whether momentum deposition is longitudinal or transverse; the diffusion wake is therefore universal to all scenarios where both momentum and energy are deposited into the medium.

## Method

The authors solved (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamical equations with source terms describing both punch-through jets (constant velocity, constant energy/momentum loss) and fully stopped jets (decelerating according to a Bethe-Bloch-like model with a Bragg peak). The medium was a static gas of massless SU(3) gluons with equation of state p = e/3 and background temperature T0 = 0.2 GeV. Angular correlations were extracted using both isochronous Cooper-Frye freeze-out at p⊥ = 5 GeV and a bulk flow freeze-out procedure.

## Key numbers

* Energy loss rate of dE/dt = 1.5 GeV/fm used for punch-through jets moving at v_jet = 0.999 through a static medium with T0 = 200 MeV.
* Jet path length of Δx = 4.5 fm used for both punch-through and stopped jets.
* Initial jet velocity v0 = 0.999 and constituent quark mass m = 0.3 GeV used in the Bethe-Bloch model for stopped jets.
* Initial energy loss rate a ≃ -1.3607 GeV/fm determined by imposing that the jet stops after Δx = 4.5 fm.
* Additional jet velocities considered: v_jet = 0.75 and v_jet = 0.58 for punch-through scenarios.
* Gaussian smearing parameter σ = 0.3 used in the source term spatial distribution.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of jet-medium interactions by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2004, 2006) and Betz et al. (2008) to include fully stopped jets with a Bethe-Bloch energy loss profile, which had not been studied hydrodynamically before. The results contradict the expectation that the Bragg peak from a stopped jet would significantly alter the away-side angular correlations. The universality of the diffusion wake is explained via vorticity conservation in ideal hydrodynamics, and the findings are consistent with angular correlations obtained for heavy quark jets in AdS/CFT by Noronha et al. (2008) and for punch-through jets in the pQCD chromo-viscous model of Neufeld et al. (2008). The work enables future studies of expanding media with realistic equations of state and viscous effects.
