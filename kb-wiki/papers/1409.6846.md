# Separation of equilibrium part from an off-equilibrium state produced by relativistic heavy ion collisions using a scalar dissipative strength

> We have proposed a novel way to specify the initial conditions of a dissipative fluid dynamical model for a given energy density $\varepsilon=u_μT^{μν}u_ν$ and baryon number density $n=N^μu_μ$, which does not impose the so-called Landau matching condition for an off-equilibrium state. In addition to usual two parameters for equilibrium part, i.e., $α\equiv μ/T$, $β\equiv1/T$ (where $T$ is separation temperature and $μ$ is separation chemical potential introduced to separate equilibrium part from the off-equilibrium state), a dissipative strength $γ$ is newly introduced to specify the off-equilibrium state. These $α$, $β$ and $γ$ can be uniquely determined by $\varepsilon$, $n$ and $P_{\rm eq}(α,β)+Π=-1/3Δ_{μν}T^{μν}$ consisting of both kinetic theoretical definitions and the thermodynamical stability condition. For $γ<10^{-3}$, $T$ and $μ$ are almost independent of $γ$, which means that the Landau matching condition is approximately satisfied. However, this is not the case for $γ\gtrsim 10^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to specify initial conditions for dissipative fluid dynamics without imposing the Landau matching condition, introducing a scalar dissipative strength parameter γ that quantifies the degree of off-equilibrium. For γ < 10⁻³, the separation temperature T and chemical potential μ are nearly independent of γ (approximately satisfying the Landau matching condition), but this approximation fails for γ ≳ 10⁻³.

## Method

The author uses irreducible tensor expansion of the off-equilibrium distribution function, following the approach of Denicol et al. (2012), to separate an equilibrium part from an off-equilibrium state characterized by energy density ε, baryon number density n, and bulk viscous pressure Π. A scalar dissipative strength γ ≡ Π/P_eq is introduced, and the separation temperature T and chemical potential μ are uniquely determined from ε, n, and the thermodynamic stability condition without imposing the Landau matching conditions δT^{μν}u_μu_ν = 0 and δN^μu_μ = 0.

## Key numbers

- γ < 10⁻³: the separation temperature T and chemical potential μ are almost independent of γ, meaning the Landau matching condition is approximately satisfied
- γ ≳ 10⁻³: the Landau matching condition is no longer approximately valid, and T and μ depend significantly on γ
- The dissipative strength γ is defined as the ratio Π/P_eq, where Π is the bulk viscous pressure and P_eq is the equilibrium pressure

## Context

This work extends the relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics framework by addressing the frame-dependence issue noted by Van (2013) and the matching condition problem discussed by Tsumura et al. (2007, 2011). The method enables more realistic initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of matter produced at RHIC and LHC, particularly for strongly off-equilibrium states where the Landau matching condition (Landau, 1959) is inappropriate. The approach builds on the irreducible tensor expansion technique developed by Denicol et al. (2012) and allows future investigations of how the matching condition affects the separation of equilibrium from off-equilibrium states in heavy-ion collisions.
