# Charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock method for the strong tensor correlation and its application to the alpha particle

> We propose a new mean-field-type framework which can treat the strong correlation induced by the tensor force. To treat the tensor correlation we break the charge and parity symmetries of a single-particle state and restore these symmetries of the total system by the projection method. We perform the charge and parity projections before variation and obtain a Hartree-Fock-like equation, which is solved self-consistently. We apply the Hartree-Fock-like equation to the alpha particle and find that by breaking the parity and charge symmetries, the correlation induced by the tensor force is obtained in the projected mean-field framework. We emphasize that the projection before the variation is important to pick up the tensor correlation in the present framework.

## Key claim
The tensor force correlation in the alpha particle can be captured in a mean-field-type framework only by breaking both parity and charge symmetries in single-particle states and restoring them via projection *before* variation (VAP), not after. The abstract's claim of success is conditional on using this specific "variation after projection" (VAP) scheme, which is emphasized as crucial for picking up the tensor correlation.

## Method
The authors formulate a charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) method. Single-particle wave functions are allowed to mix opposite parities (s- and p-waves) and charge states (proton and neutron components). A Slater determinant of these mixed states is constructed, and then parity and charge-number projection operators are applied to restore good quantum numbers for the total system. The energy expectation value of the projected state is minimized with respect to the single-particle wave functions, yielding a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-like equation (the CPPHF equation), which is solved using a gradient method with a Gaussian basis expansion.

## Key numbers
- The tensor force energy contribution $\langle \hat{v}_\rmT \rangle^{(+;2)}$ in the CPPHF scheme is -30.59 MeV, compared to -10.91 MeV in the parity-projected (PPHF) scheme and 0.00 MeV in simple Hartree-Fock (HF).
- The total binding energy $E^{(+;2)}$ of the alpha particle is -28.19 MeV in the CPPHF scheme, using factors $x_{\rmT}=1.5$ and $x_{\rmTE}=0.81$.
- The probability of the p-state component $P(-)$ in the intrinsic state is 16.1% for the CPPHF scheme, versus 7.6% for PPHF and 0.0% for HF.
- The root-mean-square matter radius $R_\rmm$ is 1.39 fm for the CPPHF scheme.

## Context
This work extends the idea of parity-mixed single-particle states previously applied in relativistic mean-field theory by Toki et al. (2002) to a non-relativistic framework, adding the crucial improvement of charge mixing to account for the isovector nature of the pion-mediated tensor force. It contrasts with conventional mean-field models using effective interactions like the Skyrme or Gogny forces, which renormalize the tensor force into the central and LS parts. The CPPHF method enables a more explicit treatment of tensor correlations in finite nuclei, going beyond the mean field by effectively including 2p-2h and 4p-4h excitations, and is applied here to the alpha particle as a test case.
