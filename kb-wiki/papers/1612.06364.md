# On electrodynamics of chiral matter

> Many-body systems with chiral fermions can exhibit novel transport phenomena that violate parity and time reversal symmetries, such as the chiral magnetic effect, the anomalous Hall effect, and the anomalous generation of charge. Based on the Maxwell-Chern-Simons electrodynamics, we examine some electromagnetic and optical properties of such systems including the electrostatics, the magnetostatics, the propagation of electromagnetic waves, the novel optical effects, etc.

# On electrodynamics of chiral matter

## Key claim

Based on the Maxwell-Chern-Simons electrodynamics with a constant four-vector $b^\mu$ (encoding chiral chemical potential and Weyl node separation), the paper systematically derives electromagnetic and optical properties of chiral matter, showing that the presence of $b_0$ (chiral magnetic effect) leads to unstable left-handed electromagnetic waves and topological current-loop interactions, while a nonzero $\vec b$ (anomalous Hall effect) yields birefringence, massive photon modes, and cross-coupling between charges and magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors start from the Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) equations, which modify the usual Maxwell equations by adding terms proportional to $b_0$ and $\vec b$ that violate parity and time-reversal symmetries. They solve the sourceless MCS equations with plane-wave ansätze to obtain dispersion relations for electromagnetic waves in three cases: $b_0 \neq 0$ only, $\vec b \neq 0$ only, and both nonzero. For static configurations, they solve the time-independent MCS equations analytically and via Green's functions in momentum space, then transform to coordinate space to compute fields from point charges and line currents.

## Key numbers

- Dispersion relation for $b_0 \neq 0$: $\omega^2 = k^2 \pm b_0 k$; left-handed mode becomes non-propagating when $k < b_0$, with imaginary frequency $\omega = i \sqrt{k b_0 - k^2}$.
- Dispersion relation for $\vec b \neq 0$: $\omega^2 = k^2 + \frac{1}{2}b^2 \pm \sqrt{(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{b})^2 + \frac{1}{4}b^4}$; at small momentum $k \ll b$, right-handed mode has $\omega_+^2 = k_x^2 + k_y^2 + 2k_z^2 + b^2$ (massive with mass $b$) and left-handed mode has $\omega_-^2 = k_x^2 + k_y^2 + k_z^4/b^2$ (nonrelativistic along $\mathbf{b}$).
- Principal refractive indices for $\vec b \neq 0$ (for $\omega > b$): $n_1 = \sqrt{1 + b/\omega}$, $n_2 = \sqrt{1 - b/\omega}$, $n_3 = 1$.
- Faraday rotation phase difference for propagation along $z$: $\Delta\phi(L) = \omega L (\sqrt{1 + b/\omega} - \sqrt{1 - b/\omega})$ for $\omega > b$.
- For a charged plane at $x=0$ with surface charge density $\sigma$ in chiral matter with $\vec b \neq 0$: fields decay as $e^{-b|x|}$ with $B_z = (\sigma/2) e^{-b|x|}$ and $E_x = (\sigma/2) \operatorname{sgn}(x) e^{-b|x|}$ for $x \neq 0$.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum-anomaly induced transport phenomena in chiral fermion systems, including the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008), the anomalous Hall effect (Haldane 2004, Zyuzin 2012), and anomalous charge generation (Wilczek 1987). It complements earlier studies by Kharzeev (2010), Huang (2013), and Jiang (2014) that treated electromagnetic fields as fixed while matter fields fluctuated; here the authors instead fix the matter parameters $b_0$ and $\vec b$ and study the resulting electromagnetic field configurations. The results enable experimental detection of chiral matter properties in Weyl/Dirac semimetals via polarized laser measurements of birefringence and Faraday rotation, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding magnetic field generation in early universe and neutron star contexts.
