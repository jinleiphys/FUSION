# Global QCD Analysis of Pion Parton Distributions with Threshold Resummation

> We perform the first global QCD analysis of pion valence, sea quark, and gluon distributions within a Bayesian Monte Carlo framework with threshold resummation on Drell-Yan cross sections at next-to-leading log accuracy. Exploring various treatments of resummation, we find that the large-$x$ asymptotics of the valence quark distribution $\sim (1-x)^{β_v}$ can differ significantly, with $β_v$ ranging from $\approx 1$ to $> 2.5$ at the input scale. Regardless of the specific implementation, however, the resummation induced redistribution of the momentum between valence quarks and gluons boosts the total momentum carried by gluons to $\approx 40\%$, increasing the gluon contribution to the pion mass to $\approx 40$ MeV.

## Key claim
The large-$x$ asymptotic exponent $\beta_v$ of the pion valence quark distribution is highly sensitive to the choice of threshold resummation prescription, ranging from $\approx 1$ to $> 2.5$ at the input scale; however, regardless of the specific implementation, resummation consistently boosts the gluon momentum fraction to $\approx 40\%$.

## Method
The authors performed a global QCD analysis of pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data from E615 and NA10, combined with leading neutron electroproduction data from HERA, within a Bayesian Monte Carlo framework. They applied threshold resummation at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy using three different prescriptions: the Mellin-Fourier (MF) method with either a cosine or expansion treatment, and the double Mellin (DM) method. The pion valence, sea quark, and gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) were parametrized at the input scale $\mu_0 = 1.27$ GeV and evolved using DGLAP equations in Mellin space.

## Key numbers
- The effective large-$x$ exponent $\beta_v^{\rm eff}$ for the valence quark PDF ranges from $\approx 1$ (NLO fixed order) to $\approx 2.6$ (cosine method) as $x \to 1$ at the input scale $\mu_0$.
- The total momentum fraction carried by gluons increases to $\approx 40\%$ (0.40(5) for the double Mellin method) with resummation, compared to 0.34(6) at NLO.
- The gluon contribution to the pion mass is $\approx 40$ MeV, representing $\approx 30\%$ of the pion mass and an increase of $\approx 14\%$ relative to the NLO analysis without resummation.
- The total $\chi^2$ per datum for the combined DY and LN datasets is 0.80 for the double Mellin method, 0.95 for the expansion method, 1.29 for the cosine method, and 0.81 for the fixed order NLO analysis.

## Context
This work extends the earlier fixed-order JAM analysis of Barry et al. (2018) by incorporating threshold resummation, and critically examines the universality of the resummation impact on $\beta_v$ previously reported by Aicher, Schafer and Vogelsang (2010) using the cosine method. The results suggest that the double Mellin method, which avoids certain leading-power issues identified by Lustermans et al. (2019), yields $\beta_v^{\rm eff} \approx 1.2$ rather than the $\beta_v \approx 2$ found in the ASV analysis, motivating future experimental checks with tagged deep-inelastic scattering at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, as well as the proposed COMPASS++/AMBER experiment at CERN.
