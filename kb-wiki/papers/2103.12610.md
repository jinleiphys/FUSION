# Unnuclear physics

> We investigate a nonrelativistic version of Georgi's "unparticle physics." We define the unnucleus as a field in a nonrelativistic conformal field theory. Such a field is characterized by a mass and a conformal dimension. We then consider the formal problem of scatterings to a final state consisting of a particle and an unnucleus and show that the differential cross section, as a function of the recoil energy received by the particle, has a power-law singularity near the maximal recoil energy, where the power is determined by the conformal dimension of the unnucleus. We argue that unlike the relativistic unparticle, which remains a hypothetical object, the unnucleus is realized, to a good approximation, in nuclear reactions involving emission of a few neutrons, when the energy of the final-state neutrons in their center-of-mass frame lies in the range between about 0.1 MeV and 5 MeV. Combining this observation with the known universal properties of fermions at unitarity in a harmonic trap, we predict a power-law behavior of an inclusive cross section in this kinematic regime. We compare our predictions with previous effective field theory and model calculations of the $^6$He$(p,pα)2n$, $^3$H$(π^-,γ)3n$, and $^3$H$(μ^-,ν_μ)3n$ reactions and find excellent agreement.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that in nuclear reactions with a few final-state neutrons, when the neutron center-of-mass kinetic energy lies between about 0.1 MeV and 5 MeV, the multi-neutron system behaves as an "unnucleus", a nonrelativistic analog of Georgi's unparticle, and the inclusive differential cross section exhibits a power-law singularity near the maximal recoil energy, with the exponent determined by the conformal dimension of the unnucleus. This claim is conditional on the absence of narrow resonances in the multi-neutron system and on the factorization of the primary scattering process from the final-state neutron interactions.

## Method
The authors define the unnucleus as a primary field in a nonrelativistic conformal field theory, characterized by a mass M and a conformal dimension Δ. They compute the differential cross section for a reaction A₁ + A₂ → B + 𝒰, where 𝒰 is an unnucleus, using the imaginary part of the unnucleus propagator, which exhibits a cut rather than a pole for Δ > 3/2. They then identify multi-neutron final states as approximate unnuclei because neutrons have a large s-wave scattering length (a ≈ -19 fm) relative to their effective range (r₀ ≈ 2.8 fm), placing them in the unitary regime where nonrelativistic conformal field theory applies. The exponent ν in dσ/dE ∼ (E₀ − E)ᵛ is obtained from the known ground-state energies of N unitary fermions in a harmonic trap, via the relation ν = Δ − 5/2, where Δ is the trap energy in units of the oscillator frequency.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a ≈ −19 fm; effective range: r₀ ≈ 2.8 fm.
- The kinematic regime for unnucleus behavior: neutron center-of-mass kinetic energy between ℏ²/ma² ≈ 0.1 MeV and ℏ²/mr₀² ≈ 5 MeV.
- Predicted exponent ν for N=2 final-state neutrons: ν = −1/2 (dσ/dE ∼ 1/√(E₀−E)).
- Predicted exponent ν for N=3 final-state neutrons: ν = 1.77272 (from ground-state energy Δ ≈ 4.27272 of three unitary fermions in a harmonic trap).
- Predicted exponent ν for N=4 final-state neutrons: ν ≈ 2.5–2.6 (from ground-state energy of four trapped unitary fermions between 5.0 and 5.1 oscillator frequencies).
- For the three-neutron case, the first excited state has Δ ≈ 4.66622, giving a subleading contribution ∼ (E₀−E)²·¹⁶⁶².

## Context
This work extends Georgi's (2007) concept of relativistic unparticles to the nonrelativistic domain, arguing that unlike the hypothetical unparticle, the unnucleus is realized in real nuclear reactions involving few-neutron final states. It connects the universal few-body physics of fermions at unitarity, studied in cold-atom systems (as reviewed by Bloch, Dalibard, and Zwerger, 2008), to nuclear reaction theory, specifically the Watson-Migdal final-state interaction formalism (Watson, 1952; Migdal, 1955). The predictions are compared with existing effective field theory and model calculations: the two-neutron spectrum from the ⁶He(p,pα)2n reaction calculated by Göbel et al. (2021) using Halo EFT and the FaCE code; the three-neutron spectra from ³H(π⁻,γ)3n and ³H(μ⁻,ν_μ)3n calculated by Golak et al. (2018, 2016) using the AV18 potential and Urbana IX three-body force. The work enables experimental searches for this power-law behavior in upcoming measurements of two- and four-neutron spectra, and suggests that similar unnucleus phenomena may occur in systems of α particles where the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) takes place.
