# Microscopic model for yields and total kinetic energy in nuclear fission

> An extension of time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT), the generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM), is applied to a study of induced nuclear fission dynamics. In the generalized TDGCM, the correlated nuclear wave function is represented as a coherent superposition of time-dependent DFT trajectories. In the first realistic application, a large basis of 25 TDDFT trajectories is employed to calculate the charge yields and total kinetic energy distribution for the fission of $^{240}$Pu. The results are compared with available data, and with those obtained using a standard TDDFT, that does not consider quantum fluctuations, and the adiabatic TDGCM+GOA (Gaussian overlap approximation). It is shown that fragment yields and kinetic energies can simultaneously be described in a consistent microscopic framework that includes fluctuations in the collective degrees of freedom and the one-body dissipation mechanism.

## Key claim
The generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM), which represents the correlated nuclear wave function as a coherent superposition of time-dependent DFT trajectories, can simultaneously describe fragment yields and kinetic energies in a consistent microscopic framework that includes both quantum fluctuations in collective degrees of freedom and the one-body dissipation mechanism. The authors qualify that this is the "first realistic application" using a basis of 25 TDDFT trajectories for the fission of $^{240}$Pu, and that the calculated total kinetic energies present a lower bound because they do not include the contribution of pre-scission energy from the initial excitation energy of the fissioning nucleus.

## Method
The nuclear wave function is expressed as a superposition of 25 time-dependent DFT product states (generator states) parametrized by axial quadrupole ($\beta_{20}$) and octupole ($\beta_{30}$) deformation coordinates, with the weight functions evolved by the time-dependent Hill-Wheeler equation. The generator states are propagated by time-dependent covariant density functional theory with the PC-PK1 functional and monopole pairing, starting from initial points along an iso-energy curve 1 MeV below the equilibrium minimum on the deformation energy surface of $^{240}$Pu. Fragment charge yields are obtained via particle number projection at 1300 fm/c, and total kinetic energies are computed from the expectation values of fragment velocities and Coulomb energy using the correlated wave function.

## Key numbers
- 25 TDDFT trajectories are used as the time-dependent generator basis for the generalized TDGCM calculation of $^{240}$Pu fission.
- The initial points for the TDDFT trajectories are located along an iso-energy curve 1 MeV below the energy of the equilibrium minimum of $^{240}$Pu.
- The collective wave function components are shown at times 0, 400, 800, and 1200 fm/c, with the charge yields and TKE evaluated at 1300 fm/c.
- The experimental charge yield data used for comparison correspond to an average excitation energy of 10.7 MeV (Ramos 2018).
- The experimental TKE data used for comparison correspond to an average excitation energy of the fissioning nucleus of 9 MeV (Caamano 2015).
- The TDGCM+GOA calculation (Ren 2022) systematically overestimates TKE compared to data, while generalized TDGCM and TDDFT reproduce experimental TKEs for fragments near the peaks of the charge yield distribution but underestimate TKEs for the tails.

## Context
This work extends the standard TDDFT framework, which models fission as a single mean-field trajectory without quantum fluctuations, and the adiabatic TDGCM with Gaussian overlap approximation (TDGCM+GOA) used by Ren (2022), which cannot describe dissipative dynamics beyond the saddle point. The generalized TDGCM was previously implemented for small-amplitude collective motion by Li (2023) and Marevic (2023), and used in an exploratory study of $^{240}$Pu fission by Li (2024). The method enables fully microscopic calculations of fission observables without adjustable parameters beyond those defining the energy density functional and pairing interaction, and can be applied to other physical processes where quantum fluctuations are essential.
