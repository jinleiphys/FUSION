# Finite-temperature linear response theory based on relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov model with point-coupling interaction

> The finite-temperature linear response theory based on the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (FT-RHB) model is developed in the charge-exchange channel to study the temperature evolution of spin-isospin excitations. Calculations are performed self-consistently with relativistic point-coupling interactions DD-PC1 and DD-PCX. In the charge-exchange channel, the pairing interaction can be split into isovector ($T = 1$) and isoscalar ($T = 0$) parts. For the isovector component, the same separable form of the Gogny D1S pairing interaction is used both for the ground-state calculation as well as for the residual interaction, while the strength of the isoscalar pairing in the residual interaction is determined by comparison with experimental data on Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and Isobaric analog resonance (IAR) centroid energy differences in even-even tin isotopes. The temperature effects are introduced by treating Bogoliubov quasiparticles within a grand-canonical ensemble. Thus, unlike the conventional formulation of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) basis, our model is formulated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) quasiparticle basis. Implementing a relativistic point-coupling interaction and a separable pairing force allows for the reduction of complicated two-body residual interaction matrix elements, which considerably decreases the dimension of the problem in the coordinate space. The main advantage of this method is to avoid the diagonalization of a large QRPA matrix, especially at finite temperature where the size of configuration space is significantly increased. The implementation of the linear response code is used to study the temperature evolution of IAR, GTR, and spin-dipole resonance (SDR) in even-even tin isotopes in the temperature range $T = 0 - 1.5$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper develops a finite-temperature proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-PNQRPA) based on the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (FT-RHB) model using point-coupling interactions, formulated in the linear response formalism rather than by diagonalizing a large QRPA matrix. The key advantage claimed is that this approach avoids the diagonalization of a large QRPA matrix at finite temperature, where the configuration space size is significantly increased, and it is formulated within the HFB quasiparticle basis rather than the conventional BCS basis.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (FT-RHB) equations for the nuclear ground state, then derive the linear response Bethe-Salpeter equation in the quasiparticle basis using a separable form of the residual interaction. The residual particle-hole interaction comes from relativistic point-coupling density functionals (DD-PC1 and DD-PCX), while the particle-particle residual interaction uses a separable form of the Gogny D1S pairing force, with isovector pairing for natural parity transitions and isoscalar pairing for unnatural parity transitions. The reduced response function is solved by matrix inversion in coordinate space on a Gauss-Hermite radial mesh, and discrete FT-QRPA modes are obtained via contour integration in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

* Temperature range studied: T = 0 - 1.5 MeV for even-even tin isotopes
* Harmonic oscillator basis: 20 oscillator shells (N_osc = 20)
* Radial mesh: 24 Gauss-Hermite meshpoints (N_gh = 24) within a box of R = 20 fm
* Energy mesh interval: Delta E = 0.1 MeV
* Contour integral radius: 0.1 MeV for discrete FT-QRPA modes
* Separable pairing interaction parameters: G_p = G_n = 728 MeV fm^3 for DD-PC1; G_p = 773.78 MeV fm^3 and G_n = 800.66 MeV fm^3 for DD-PCX; a^2 = 0.644 fm^2 for both interactions

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature proton-neutron RPA approaches developed by Niu et al. (2011) and Paar et al. (2009) by including pairing correlations within a full HFB framework rather than the BCS approximation used in previous FT-QRPA calculations by Yuksel et al. (2017, 2019, 2020) and Ravlic et al. (2020). The method enables the study of temperature evolution of isobaric analog resonance (IAR), Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR), and spin-dipole resonance (SDR) in open-shell nuclei, which is relevant for nuclear astrophysics applications such as r-process nucleosynthesis and core-collapse supernova evolution where temperatures reach 10^9 - 10^10 K.
