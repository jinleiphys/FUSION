# Multiquark states and QCD sum rules

> There have been arguments about hadronic molecules, which are weakly-bound states of two or more hadrons. We investigate the possibility of some candidates (f0 (980), a0 (980), f0 (1500), f0 (1710), etc.) using QCD sum rule approach and compare our results with multiquark states in the MIT bag model. We find that f0 (1500), f0 (1710) can be good candidates for vector-vector molecule-type multiquark states.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules with a specific large-\(N_c\) subtraction strategy, the author finds that \(f_0(1500)\) and \(f_0(1710)\) can be good candidates for vector-vector molecule-type multiquark states, but the work is insufficient to distinguish whether they are molecular states, molecule-type multiquark states, or ordinary quark-antiquark states with large virtual pairs.

## Method

The author constructs QCD sum rules for four-quark interpolating fields corresponding to \(K\bar{K}\) molecules (\(f_0(980)\), \(a_0(980)\)) and vector-vector molecules (\(\rho\rho\) for \(f_0(1500)\), \(K^*\bar{K}^*\) for \(f_0(1710)\)). The operator product expansion (OPE) is computed including quark condensates up to dimension 8, neglecting gluon condensates, and the sum rule is analyzed by first fixing the continuum threshold and Borel window from the leading \(N_c^2\) (two-loop) diagrams to reproduce the free two-meson threshold, then including the subleading \(1/N_c\) (one-loop) diagrams to extract the bound-state mass. The results are compared with the MIT bag model predictions of Jaffe (1977).

## Key numbers

*   For the \(K\bar{K}\) molecule (\(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\)), the mass obtained from the full sum rule is 1.031 GeV for the \(K^0\bar{K}^0 + K^+K^-\) state and 1.000 GeV for the \(K^0\bar{K}^0 - K^+K^-\) state, both above the free two-kaon threshold of 0.990 GeV.
*   For the \(\rho\rho\) molecule (\(f_0(1500)\)), the mass obtained is 1.499 GeV, below the free \(\rho\rho\) threshold of 1.540 GeV.
*   For the \(K^*\bar{K}^*\) molecule (\(f_0(1710)\)), the mass obtained is 1.748 GeV for the isospin I=0 state (\(K^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0} + K^{*+}K^{*-}\)), below the free \(K^*\bar{K}^*\) threshold of 1.784 GeV.
*   The MIT bag model predicts masses of 1450 MeV for the \(\rho\rho\) state and 1800 MeV for the \(K^*\bar{K}^*\) state (Jaffe, 1977).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to multiquark states, following earlier sum rule calculations for the H-dibaryon by Kodama et al. (1994). It directly compares its results with the MIT bag model predictions of Jaffe (1977) and the nonrelativistic quark model calculations of Weinstein and Isgur (1982). The findings suggest that \(f_0(1500)\) and \(f_0(1710)\) are promising vector-vector molecular candidates, but the author notes that further calculations of \(\gamma\gamma\) decay widths or radiative decays \(\phi \to \gamma(f_0, a_0)\) as proposed by Close et al. (1993) are needed to distinguish between molecular and other interpretations.
