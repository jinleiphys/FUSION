# Isoscalar resonances with J^{PC}=1^{--} in e^+e^-annihilation

> The analysis of the vector isoscalar excitations in the energy range between 1 and 2 GeV of the $e^+e^-$ annihilation is presented for the final states $π^+π^-π^0$, $ωπ^+π^-$, $K^+K^-$, $K^0_SK^\pmπ^\mp$ and $K^{\ast0}K^-π^++ c.c$. The effects of both the resonance mixing and the successive opening of multiparticle channels, with the energy dependent partial widths, are taken into account. The work extends our previous analysis hep-ph/9609216 of the vector isovector excitations and is aimed to compare the existing data with the predictions of the $q\bar q$ model. It is shown that this hypothesis does not contradict the data.

## Key claim

The analysis shows that the existing data on vector isoscalar excitations in the 1-2 GeV range do not contradict the simple $q\bar q$ model of $\omega$- and $\varphi$-like resonances, but the errors are so large that one cannot verify the traditional $q\bar q$ assignment or draw definite conclusions about the interquark potential.

## Method

The authors fit data on $e^+e^-$ annihilation into five final states ($\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$, $\omega\pi^+\pi^-$, $K^+K^-$, $K^0_SK^\pm\pi^\mp$, and $K^{\ast0}K^-\pi^+$) in the energy range 1-2 GeV, allowing for mixing among $\omega(782)-\omega^\prime_1-\omega^\prime_2$ and $\varphi(1020)-\varphi^\prime_1-\varphi^\prime_2$ resonances. They use energy-dependent partial widths for successively opened multiparticle channels and treat mixing inside each sector to all orders while taking OZI-rule-breaking terms to first order. The parameters of bare states (masses and coupling constants) are extracted by minimizing $\chi^2$ separately for each reaction, then requiring consistency across channels within one to two standard deviations.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\omega(782)$: $783.4^{+2.7}_{-3.0}$ MeV; mass of $\varphi(1020)$: $1019.8^{+1.6}_{-1.4}$ MeV
- Coupling constant $g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 14.3 \pm 1.6$ GeV$^{-1}$; $g_{\varphi\rho\pi} = 0.63 \pm 0.17$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Leptonic coupling constant $f_\omega = 16.6^{+1.7}_{-1.3}$
- Coupling constant $g_{\varphi K^+K^-} = 4.7 \pm 0.5$
- For $\rho$-like excitations: $|R_S(0,m_{\rho^\prime_1})|^2 = 110^{+30}_{-20} \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^3$; $|R_S(0,m_{\rho^\prime_2})|^2 = (140 \pm 20) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^3$
- For $\omega$-like excitations: $|R_S(0,m_{\omega^\prime_1})|^2 = 40^{+620}_{-40} \times 10^{-4}$ GeV$^3$; $|R_S(0,m_{\omega^\prime_2})|^2 = 430^{+1420}_{-250} \times 10^{-4}$ GeV$^3$
- Range parameter $R_{\rho\pi} = 0.4 \pm 1.0$ GeV$^{-1}$ for the $\omega(782)$ $\rho\pi$ decay mode
- Upper bounds on $\rho^\prime_{1,2}K^\ast\bar K\pi$ couplings: $|c^{(0)}_{\rho^\prime_1}| < 1500$, $|c^{(0)}_{\rho^\prime_2}| < 1100$, $|c^{(1)}_{\rho^\prime_2}| < 390$, $|c^{(1)}_{\rho^\prime_2}| < 210$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of vector isovector excitations (Achasov and Kozhevnikov, 1997) to the isoscalar sector, using the same unitary method with resonance mixing and energy-dependent widths. It differs fundamentally from the approach of Clegg and Donnachie (1994), who used simple Breit-Wigner amplitudes neglecting mixing, and from the conclusions of Donnachie and Kalashnikova (1993) and Close and Page (1995) that heavier resonances contain exotic non-$q\bar q$ components. The extracted parameters enable future comparisons with interquark potential models such as Godfrey and Isgur (1985), but the large errors prevent verification of the usual assignments $\rho^\prime_1 \sim 2^3S_1$ and $\rho^\prime_2 \sim 1^3D_1$ (and similarly for $\omega^\prime_{1,2}$).
