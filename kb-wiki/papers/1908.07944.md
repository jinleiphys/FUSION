# Critical endpoint and universality class of neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids in neutron stars

> We study the thermodynamics and critical behavior of neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids in the inner cores of neutron stars. $^3P_2$ superfluids offer a rich phase diagram including uniaxial/biaxial nematic phases, the ferromagnetic phase, and the cyclic phase. Using the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation as superfluid Fermi liquid theory, we show that a strong (weak) magnetic field drives the first (second) order transition from the dihedral-two biaxial nematic phase to dihedral-four biaxial nematic phase in low (high) temperatures, and their phase boundaries are divided by the critical endpoint (CEP). We demonstrate that the set of critical exponents at the CEP satisfies the Rushbrooke, Griffiths, and Widom equalities, indicating a new universality class. At the CEP, the $^3P_2$ superfluid exhibits critical behavior with nontrivial critical exponents, indicating a new universality class. Furthermore, we find that the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) equation up to the 8th-order expansion satisfies three equalities and properly captures the physics of the CEP. This implies that the GL theory can provide a tractable way for understanding critical phenomena which may be realized in the dense core of realistic magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the critical endpoint (CEP) in neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids exhibits a set of critical exponents that satisfy the Rushbrooke, Griffiths, and Widom equalities, indicating a new universality class distinct from ordinary ones, with the caveat that the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory up to 8th-order expansion captures this behavior consistently with the full Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) approach, though the positions of the CEPs differ between the two formalisms.

## Method

The authors employed the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation as superfluid Fermi liquid theory, derived from the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic functional and quasiclassical approximation, to compute the phase diagram of neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids under a magnetic field. They extracted the critical exponents at the critical endpoint by directly evaluating the critical behavior of thermodynamic quantities, and compared these results with those obtained from a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory expanded up to 8th-order in the order parameter. The critical exponents were checked against the Rushbrooke, Griffiths, and Widom equalities to verify consistency with scaling laws.

## Key numbers

* The critical endpoint in the GL theory is located at \(T_{\mathrm{cep}}/T_{\rm c}=0.774597\) and \(\gamma_{\rm n}B_{\mathrm{cep}}/(\pi T_{\rm c})=0.004465\).
* The critical exponent associated with specific heat is \(\alpha \sim 0.6\).
* The critical exponent associated with magnetization (susceptibility) is \(\gamma \sim 0.5\).
* The neutron gyromagnetic ratio is \(\gamma_{\rm n}=1.2 \times 10^{-13}\) MeV/T.
* The neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids are expected at densities \(\rho \gtrsim 10^{14}{\rm g}/{\rm cm}^{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the phase diagram of neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids previously obtained by Mizushima (2016) using the BdG equation, which identified first and second-order phase transitions between the dihedral-two biaxial nematic (D2-BN) and dihedral-four biaxial nematic (D4-BN) phases meeting at a critical endpoint. The study also builds on the GL theory up to 8th-order developed by Yasui (2019), which showed that the 8th-order term ensures global stability and produces a CEP absent in the 6th-order expansion. The finding of a new universality class with \(\gamma < 1\) (unlike \(\gamma \ge 1\) in ordinary universality classes such as O(n) models) enables future work on transport coefficients and equations of state in neutron star cores, particularly for magnetars with magnetic fields of \(10^{15}-10^{18}\) G.
