# Particle Number Fluctuations in Canonical Ensemble

> Fluctuations of charged particle number are studied in the canonical ensemble. In the infinite volume limit the fluctuations in the canonical ensemble are different from the fluctuations in the grand canonical one. Thus, the well-known equivalence of both ensembles for the average quantities does not extend for the fluctuations. In view of a possible relevance of the results for the analysis of fluctuations in nuclear collisions at high energies, a role of the limited kinematical acceptance is studied.

## Key claim

The well-known equivalence of the canonical ensemble (c.e.) and grand canonical ensemble (g.c.e.) for average quantities does not extend to fluctuations: in the infinite volume limit, the scaled variance of same-charge particle number in the c.e. is 0.5, exactly half the value of 1 in the g.c.e., even though the mean multiplicities become equal.

## Method

The authors consider a relativistic Boltzmann ideal gas of positively and negatively charged particles (e.g., π⁺ and π⁻ mesons) with zero total charge in volume V at temperature T. They calculate the partition functions, mean particle numbers, second moments, and scaled variances for both the grand canonical ensemble (where only the mean charge is zero) and the canonical ensemble (where charge conservation N₊ − N₋ = 0 is imposed on each microscopic state). The analysis uses the single-particle partition function z = V/(2π²) T m² K₂(m/T) and modified Bessel functions Iₙ(2z) to obtain exact expressions and asymptotic limits for small (z ≪ 1) and large (z ≫ 1) systems. The effect of limited kinematical acceptance is modeled by introducing a binomial detection probability q for each particle.

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance of same-charge particles in the c.e. for large systems (z ≫ 1): ω_{c.e.}^{±} ≅ 1/2 + 1/(8z) ≅ 0.5, exactly half the g.c.e. value of ω_{g.c.e.}^{±} = 1.
- Scaled variance of same-charge particles in the c.e. for small systems (z ≪ 1): ω_{c.e.}^{±} ≅ 1 − z²/2 ≅ 1, equal to the g.c.e. value.
- Scaled variance of total charged particle number N_{ch} in the c.e. satisfies ω_{c.e.}^{ch} = 2 ω_{c.e.}^{±} for all z, derived from the recurrence relation I₀(2z) = I₂(2z) + I₁(2z)/z.
- For limited acceptance, the scaled variance of accepted particles is ω_{acc} = q·ω + (1−q), where q is the single-particle detection probability; for q ≪ 1, ω_{acc}⁺ ≅ 1, and for q ≅ 1, ω_{acc}⁺ ≅ ω_{c.e.}⁺.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to hadron production in high-energy nuclear collisions, where previous studies (e.g., the review by Braun-Munzinger et al., and the canonical ensemble treatments by Rafelski and Danos, Redlich and Turko, and others) had only addressed average quantities. The paper shows that the standard textbook equivalence of ensembles fails for fluctuations, a result relevant for interpreting fluctuation data from experiments at the CERN SPS (e.g., results presented at Quark Matter 2004 by Gazdzicki and collaborators). The analysis enables future work on non-zero conserved charges, multiple charged particle species, and energy fluctuations in the canonical ensemble.
