# Relativistic predictions of exclusive $^{208}Pb(\vec{p},2p) ^{207}T\ell$ analyzing powers at an incident energy of 202 MeV

> Within the framework of the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA), we investigate the sensitivity of the analyzing power - for exclusive proton knockout from the 3s$_{1/2}$, 2d$_{3/2}$ and 2d$_{5/2}$ states in $^{208}$Pb, at an incident laboratory kinetic energy of 202 MeV, and for coincident coplanar scattering angles ($28.0^{\circ}$, $-54.6^{\circ}$) - to different distorting optical potentials, finite-range (FR) versus zero-range (ZR) approximations to the DWIA, as well as medium-modified coupling constants and meson masses. Results are also compared to the nonrelativistic DWIA predictions based on the Schrödinger equation. Whereas the nonrelativistic model fails severely, both ZR and FR relativistic DWIA models provide an excellent description of the data. For the FR predictions, it is necessary to invoke a 20% reduction of sigma-nucleon and omega-nucleon coupling constants as well as for $σ$-, $ρ$- and $ω$-meson masses, by the nuclear medium. On the other hand, the ZR predictions suggest that the strong interaction in the nuclear medium is adequately represented by the free nucleon-nucleon interaction associated with the impulse approximation. We also demonstrate that, although the analyzing power is relatively insensitive to the use different relativistic global optical potential parameter sets, the prominent oscillatory behavior of this observable is largely attributed to distortion of the scattering wave functions relative to their plane wave values.

# Relativistic predictions of exclusive $^{208}Pb(\vec{p},2p) ^{207}T\ell$ analyzing powers at an incident energy of 202 MeV

## Key claim

Both zero-range (ZR) and finite-range (FR) relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) models provide an excellent description of the analyzing power data for exclusive proton knockout from $^{208}$Pb, whereas the nonrelativistic model fails severely; however, the FR predictions require a 20% reduction of sigma-nucleon and omega-nucleon coupling constants as well as sigma-, rho- and omega-meson masses by the nuclear medium, while the ZR predictions suggest the free nucleon-nucleon interaction is adequate without medium modifications.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic DWIA calculations using the Dirac equation for exclusive ($\vec{p},2p$) reactions on $^{208}$Pb at 202 MeV incident energy, comparing both finite-range and zero-range approximations to the transition matrix elements. They employed global Dirac optical potentials for generating scattering wave functions and the relativistic Horowitz-Love-Franley (HLF) meson-exchange model for the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix, with a new HLF parameter set generated at 180 MeV. Medium modifications were implemented using the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture, reducing meson masses and coupling constants by 10% and 20%, and results were compared to nonrelativistic Schrödinger-based DWIA predictions from the code THREEDEE.

## Key numbers

- Incident laboratory kinetic energy: 202 MeV for proton knockout from $^{208}$Pb
- Coincident coplanar scattering angles: ($28.0^{\circ}$, $-54.6^{\circ}$)
- NN amplitudes evaluated at $T_{\rm eff}^{\rm \ell ab} \approx 180$ MeV and $\theta_{\rm eff}^{\rm cm} \approx 60^{\circ}$
- For FR predictions, a 20% reduction of sigma-nucleon and omega-nucleon coupling constants and sigma-, rho- and omega-meson masses was necessary
- Medium modification scaling parameters $\xi = \chi$ tested at values of 0.9 (10% reduction) and 0.8 (20% reduction)
- Values of $\xi = \chi < 0.8$ failed to reproduce analyzing powers for both ZR and FR approximations
- Global Dirac optical potential parameter set constrained by $^{208}$Pb($p,p$) elastic scattering data for incident proton energies between 21 MeV and 1040 MeV

## Context

This work extends and resolves the failure of previous relativistic and nonrelativistic DWIA models reported by Neveling et al. (2002) for exclusive ($\vec{p},2p$) analyzing powers on $^{208}$Pb, where both dynamical models severely failed to reproduce data for 3s$_{1/2}$ and 2d$_{3/2}$ knockout. The study builds on the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) for medium modifications and the relativistic meson-exchange model of Horowitz and Love-Franley (1985). The results demonstrate the superiority of the Dirac equation over the Schrödinger equation for describing spin observables, and refute earlier qualitative claims that spin observables are insensitive to nuclear distortion effects. The work enables future studies of complete sets of polarization transfer observables for deeper-lying states in various nuclei to extract more conclusive information on nuclear medium modifications of the strong interaction.
