# Absence of charged pion condensation in a magnetic field with parallel rotation

> We investigate the critical temperature of a relativistic Bose-Einstein condensate of charged bosons driven by rotation in a parallel magnetic field [Y. Liu and I. Zahed, Phys. Rev. Lett. 120, 032001 (2018)]. For non-interacting bosons, the critical temperature can only be determined for a system with fixed angular momentum. We find that the critical temperature of the non-interacting system vanishes due to the fact that the system is quasi-one-dimensional, indicating that non-interacting bosons cannot undergo Bose-Einstein condensation. For interacting bosons, we investigate a system with quartic self-interaction. We show that the order parameter vanishes and the off-diagonal long-range order is absent at any nonzero temperature because of the quasi-one-dimensional feature, in accordance with the Coleman-Mermin-Wagner-Hohenberg theorem.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charged pion Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) driven by a parallel magnetic field and rotation cannot occur at any nonzero temperature, for both non-interacting and interacting bosons. The critical temperature is exactly zero because the system becomes quasi-one-dimensional, and for interacting bosons the order parameter vanishes at any nonzero temperature in accordance with the Coleman-Mermin-Wagner-Hohenberg theorem.

## Method

The authors study a complex scalar field theory for charged bosons in a constant magnetic field along the z-direction with global rigid rotation parallel to the field. They evaluate the partition function using the imaginary-time formalism, first solving the Klein-Gordon equation for non-interacting bosons to obtain the Landau level spectrum and then computing the grand potential and conserved quantities (U(1) charge and angular momentum). For interacting bosons, they introduce a quartic self-interaction term, determine the tree-level ground state via variational calculation, and then analyze the effect of phase fluctuations on the order parameter and off-diagonal long-range order.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity in heavy ion collisions: \(\Omega \approx (9\pm1)\times10^{21}\) Hz \(\sim 0.05 m_\pi\)
- Magnetic field strength in heavy ion collisions: \(eB\sim m_\pi^2\)
- Angular momenta in noncentral collisions: \(10^3\sim10^5\hbar\)
- For the non-interacting system with \(\mu=0\), the critical angular speed is \(\Omega_{\rm c}=\sqrt{qB+m^2}/N\) where \(N=qBR^2/2\) is the Landau level degeneracy
- The critical temperature \(T_{\rm c}=0\) for non-interacting bosons in PRM

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Liu and Zahed (2018) that parallel rotation and magnetic field (PRM) provides a mechanism for charged pion BEC, and the numerical variational study by the same authors (2022) showing the ground state is a giant quantum vortex. The paper contradicts the possibility of a finite-temperature BEC in PRM by demonstrating that the quasi-one-dimensional nature of the system prevents condensation at any nonzero temperature, consistent with the Coleman-Mermin-Wagner-Hohenberg theorem. This result implies that charged pion BEC driven by PRM cannot be realized in noncentral nucleus-nucleus collisions if the freeze-out temperature exceeds zero.
