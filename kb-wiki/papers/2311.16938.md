# New insights into the doubly charmed exotic mesons

> Using effective Lagrangians constrained by the heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, for the light quarks, we analyze the $D^0 D^0ŌÄ^+$, $\bar{D}^0D^0ŌÄ^0$ and $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ invariant mass spectra. Performing a simultaneous analysis of the doubly charmed and charm-anti-charm states gives further insights into the nature of the $T^+_{cc}$ and $Ōá^0_{c1}(3872)$, exotic hadrons. It is confirmed that both states should lie below their respective $DD^*$/$D\bar{D}^*$ thresholds. Also, the contributions of the triangle and box diagrams are negligible.

# New insights into the doubly charmed exotic mesons

## Key claim
Using effective Lagrangians constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, the authors confirm that both the Tcc+ and Ōác1(3872) states lie below their respective DD*/DŐĄD* thresholds, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the tree-level diagrams dominate and that the triangle and box diagram contributions are negligible (three to four orders of magnitude smaller).

## Method
The authors constructed effective interaction Lagrangians for doubly charmed mesons and D/D* mesons based on heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(2) chiral symmetry, then performed simultaneous fits to the D0D0ŌÄ+ invariant mass spectrum from LHCb, the DŐĄ0D0ŌÄ0 spectrum from BESIII, and the D0DŐĄ*0 spectrum from Belle. The three-body decay widths were computed by integrating over Dalitz-plot variables, and the line shapes were described using a Breit-Wigner formula with energy-dependent widths, with fit uncertainties obtained via bootstrap resampling.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to exotic hadrons previously applied to the Tcc+ by Dai (2021) and to the Ōác1(3872) by Fleming (2007) and Dai (2023), providing a simultaneous analysis of both states. The results resolve a long-standing question about whether the Ōác1(3872) lies above or below the D0DŐĄ*0 threshold, finding it is below by 70 ¬Ī 21 keV. The analysis enables predictions for the decay modes of the charge partners Tcc0, Tcc++, and Ōác1¬Ī if these states are isovectors, which can be tested by LHCb, BESIII, and Belle II experiments.
