# New insights into the doubly charmed exotic mesons

> Using effective Lagrangians constrained by the heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, for the light quarks, we analyze the $D^0 D^0π^+$, $\bar{D}^0D^0π^0$ and $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ invariant mass spectra. Performing a simultaneous analysis of the doubly charmed and charm-anti-charm states gives further insights into the nature of the $T^+_{cc}$ and $χ^0_{c1}(3872)$, exotic hadrons. It is confirmed that both states should lie below their respective $DD^*$/$D\bar{D}^*$ thresholds. Also, the contributions of the triangle and box diagrams are negligible.

# New insights into the doubly charmed exotic mesons

## Key claim
Using effective Lagrangians constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, the authors confirm that both the Tcc+ and χc1(3872) states lie below their respective DD*/D̄D* thresholds, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the tree-level diagrams dominate and that the triangle and box diagram contributions are negligible (three to four orders of magnitude smaller).

## Method
The authors constructed effective interaction Lagrangians for doubly charmed mesons and D/D* mesons based on heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(2) chiral symmetry, then performed simultaneous fits to the D0D0π+ invariant mass spectrum from LHCb, the D̄0D0π0 spectrum from BESIII, and the D0D̄*0 spectrum from Belle. The three-body decay widths were computed by integrating over Dalitz-plot variables, and the line shapes were described using a Breit-Wigner formula with energy-dependent widths, with fit uncertainties obtained via bootstrap resampling.

## Key numbers
- Tcc+ mass: 3874.758 ± 0.055 MeV, with total width 0.541 ± 0.047 MeV
- Tcc+ mass relative to D0D*+ threshold (3875.10 MeV): ΔM = -342 ± 55 keV (below threshold)
- χc1(3872) mass: 3871.620 ± 0.021 MeV, with total width 1.496 ± 0.084 MeV
- χc1(3872) mass relative to D0D̄*0 threshold (3871.690 MeV): ΔM = -70 ± 21 keV (below threshold)
- Coupling gx for Tcc+ (isoscalar): 2.12 ± 0.12
- Coupling g̃x for χc10 (isoscalar): 2.47 ± 0.75
- Three-body decay width Γ(χc10 → D0D̄0π0) = 0.75 ± 0.14 MeV, compatible with PDG value of 0.58 ± 0.23 MeV
- For a hypothetical χc10 mass 10 keV above threshold (3871.700 MeV) with g̃x = 2.535, the two-body decay width Γ(χc10 → D0D̄*0) would be 380 MeV, far exceeding the total width

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to exotic hadrons previously applied to the Tcc+ by Dai (2021) and to the χc1(3872) by Fleming (2007) and Dai (2023), providing a simultaneous analysis of both states. The results resolve a long-standing question about whether the χc1(3872) lies above or below the D0D̄*0 threshold, finding it is below by 70 ± 21 keV. The analysis enables predictions for the decay modes of the charge partners Tcc0, Tcc++, and χc1± if these states are isovectors, which can be tested by LHCb, BESIII, and Belle II experiments.
