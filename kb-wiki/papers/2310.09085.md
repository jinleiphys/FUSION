# Low-energy matrix elements of heavy-quark currents

> In QCD at energies well below a heavy-quark threshold, the heavy-quark vector current can be represented via local operators made of the lighter quarks and of the gluon fields. We extract the leading perturbative matching coefficients for the two most important sets of operators from known results. As an application, we analytically determine the ${\rm O}(α_s^3)m_c^2/m_b^2$ effect of the bottom quark current on the $R(s)$ ratio below the bottom but above the charm threshold. For the low-energy representation of the charm quark current, the two most important operators are given by the total divergence of dimension-six gluonic operators. We argue that the charm magnetic moment of the nucleon is effectively measuring the forward matrix elements of these gluonic operators and predict the corresponding bottom magnetic moment. Similarly, the contribution of the charm current to $R(s\approx 1\,{\rm GeV}^2)$, which is associated with quark-disconnected diagrams, is dominantly determined by the decay constants of the $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons with respect to the two gluonic operators.

## Key claim

The heavy-quark vector current at energies well below the heavy-quark threshold can be represented by local operators made of lighter quarks and gluon fields, with the leading contributions coming from the total divergence of dimension-six gluonic operators rather than from chirally suppressed tensor currents, except in cases where light-quark masses provide an enhancement.

## Method

The author extracts leading perturbative matching coefficients for the low-energy representation of heavy-quark vector currents from known results, using the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian approach adapted from QED to QCD with appropriate color and combinatorial factors. The matching is performed by comparing two-point correlation functions and scattering amplitudes between the full theory (with the heavy quark) and the low-energy effective theory (without it), determining coefficients for both the light-quark tensor current operators and the three-gluon operators. The analysis covers both the tensor current contribution (dimension-five operators with chiral suppression) and the gluonic operator contribution (dimension-seven operators without chiral suppression).

## Key numbers

* The matching coefficient for the tensor current operator is \(d_2(M,m,\alpha_s) = \frac{5}{18} d_2^{\rm QED}(M,m,\alpha_s)\) for \(N=3\), where \(d_2^{\rm QED}(M,m,\alpha) = -\frac{c_2}{4} \frac{m}{M^2} \left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\right)^3\) with \(c_2 = \frac{3}{2}\zeta(3) - \frac{19}{16}\)

* The matching coefficients for the three-gluon operators are \(h_1(M,g_s) = -\frac{2}{9} \frac{g_s^3}{16\pi^2 M^4}\) and \(h_2(M,g_s) = +\frac{28}{45} \frac{g_s^3}{16\pi^2 M^4}\)

* The charm magnetic moment of the nucleon from the tensor current contribution is estimated as \((3\pm 3)\times 10^{-8}\) in nuclear magneton units, using \(m_c = 1.27\,{\rm GeV}\) and \(\alpha_s = 0.3\)

* The charm magnetic moment from the gluonic operator contribution is estimated as \(\mu^c_{p,n}|_{\rm glue} = 1.1\times 10^{-3}\xi\) where \(\xi\) is expected to be of order unity, consistent with the lattice QCD result \(\mu^c_{p,n} = (-1.27\pm 0.38_{\rm stat} \pm 0.05_{\rm syst})\times 10^{-3}\) from Sufian (2020)

* The predicted bottom magnetic moment of the nucleon is \(\mu^b_{p,n} \approx -1 \times 10^{-5}\), and the top-quark contribution is at the level of \(-4\times 10^{-12}\)

* The leading perturbative contribution of the bottom current to the spectral function \(R^{b,c}(s)\) above charm threshold but below bottom threshold is \(R^{b,c}(s) = \frac{3}{64} \left(3\zeta(3) - \frac{19}{8}\right) \frac{(N^2-1)(N^2-4)}{N} \left(\frac{\alpha_s(2m_b)}{\pi}\right)^3 \frac{m_c^2}{m_b^2} \sqrt{1-\frac{4m_c^2}{s}}\)

## Context

This work extends the classic Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian approach (Heisenberg, 1936) from QED to QCD for heavy-quark vector currents, complementing the earlier treatment of heavy-quark axial-vector currents by Collins (1978) and Chetyrkin (1993). The results enable quantitative predictions for heavy-quark contributions to nucleon magnetic moments and the \(R(s)\) ratio that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Sufian (2020) for the charm magnetic moment and by the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration (2017) for hadronic vacuum polarisation. The analysis also provides a framework for estimating bottom and top quark contributions to precision observables like the proton magnetic moment, which is known experimentally to 0.3 ppb (Tiesinga, 2021).
