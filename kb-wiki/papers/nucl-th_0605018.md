# Quantum calculation of Coulomb reorientation and near-barrier fusion

> We investigate the role of deformation on the fusion probability around the barrier using the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock theory with a full Skyrme force. We obtain a distribution of fusion probabilities around the nominal barrier due to the different contributions of the various orientations of the deformed nucleus at the touching point. It is also shown that the long range Coulomb reorientation reduces the fusion probability around the barrier.

## Key claim

The paper shows that long-range Coulomb reorientation of a deformed nucleus reduces the near-barrier fusion probability, but this reorientation effect is independent of both the charges and the energy of the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using a full Skyrme force (SLy4d parametrization) for head-on collisions of a prolate deformed 24Mg nucleus with a spherical 208Pb target. They assumed an isotropic distribution of initial orientations at 20 fm separation, then separately computed the Coulomb reorientation during the approach phase from 220 fm to 20 fm using TDHF with an external Coulomb field, and finally combined these to obtain the fusion probability distribution.

## Key numbers

- The prolate deformed 24Mg has a quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = 0.4.
- The fusion probability distribution spans from below 93 MeV (no orientation fuses) to above 99 MeV (all orientations fuse) in center-of-mass energy.
- The Coulomb reorientation causes a fusion hindrance of up to 20% around the barrier.
- The barrier position used in CCFULL calculations is RB = 11.49 fm.
- The TDHF calculation uses a grid step size of 0.8 fm and time step of 0.45 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Coulomb reorientation in TDHF by Simenel (2004) and provides a quantum mean-field interpretation of coupled-channels calculations. The results are compared with CCFULL calculations by Hagino (1999) that include coupling to the first five excited states of the 24Mg rotational band. The paper identifies that TDHF underestimates the fusion barrier by about 15% and misses quantum mechanical effects, pointing to the need for improved treatment of time-odd terms in the Skyrme energy functional as noted by Umar (2006) and Maruhn (2006).
