# Matter in extremis: ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC

> We review the physics of nuclear matter at high energy density and the experimental search for the Quark-Gluon Plasma at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The data obtained in the first three years of the RHIC physics program provide several lines of evidence that a novel state of matter has been created in the most violent, head-on collisions of $Au$ nuclei at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. Jet quenching and global measurements show that the initial energy density of the strongly interacting medium generated in the collision is about two orders of magnitude larger than that of cold nuclear matter, well above the critical density for the deconfinement phase transition predicted by lattice QCD. The observed collective flow patterns imply that the system thermalizes early in its evolution, with the dynamics of its expansion consistent with ideal hydrodynamic flow based on a Quark-Gluon Plasma equation of state.

## Key claim

The data from the first three years of RHIC operation provide several lines of evidence that a novel state of matter has been created in head-on collisions of gold nuclei at 200 GeV per nucleon pair, but the paper hedges that this matter is "evidently a near-ideal, strongly coupled fluid" rather than the ideal non-interacting gas expected from QCD at asymptotically high temperature, and that no clear consensus has emerged that a long-lived Quark-Gluon Plasma was observed at previous fixed-target facilities.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the four RHIC experiments (PHENIX, STAR, BRAHMS, PHOBOS) analyzing data from gold-gold, deuteron-gold, and proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of 20, 130, and 200 GeV per nucleon pair. The analysis combines global measurements of particle production and transverse energy with hard probe measurements including jet quenching, high-transverse-momentum hadron suppression, and collective flow patterns, interpreted within the framework of perturbative QCD, lattice QCD calculations, and hydrodynamic models.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density of the strongly interacting medium is about two orders of magnitude larger than that of cold nuclear matter, well above the critical density for deconfinement predicted by lattice QCD
- Lattice QCD calculation finds a critical temperature of about 170 MeV for the phase transition
- The top collision energy for heavy nuclear beams at RHIC is 200 GeV per nucleon pair; the top energy for proton-proton collisions is 500 GeV
- The RHIC design luminosity for gold-gold collisions at top energy is 2 × 10^26 cm^-2 s^-1
- The integrated luminosity for gold-gold collisions at 200 GeV was 0.24 nb^-1, equivalent to 7.9 pb^-1 in proton-proton collisions
- The quark condensate in vacuum is approximately -(240 MeV)^3
- The gluon condensate vacuum expectation value is approximately 0.02 GeV^4
- The string tension from lattice QCD is approximately 1 GeV/fm

## Context

This review extends the search for the Quark-Gluon Plasma that began at the Berkeley Bevalac in the early 1980s (Nagamiya, 1984) and continued at the Brookhaven AGS and CERN SPS in the late 1980s (Harris, 1996), where results showed anomalous suppression of charmonium (Alexopoulos, 2002) and broadening or mass-shift of vector mesons (Agakishiev, 1995) but no clear consensus on QGP formation. The RHIC results enable future experiments at even higher energies at the CERN LHC (Giubellino, 2003) and provide a foundation for studying universal properties of high-density QCD at low Bjorken x (Iancu, 2003).
