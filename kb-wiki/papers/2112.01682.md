# Most charming dibaryon near unitarity

> We present a first study on a pair of triply charmed baryons, $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ in the $^1S_0$ channel, on the basis of the HAL QCD method. The measurements are perfomed on the $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD configurations with nearly physical light-quark masses and physical charm-quark mass. We show that the system with the Coulomb repulsion taking into account the charge form factor of $Ω_{ccc}$ leads to the scattering length $a^\mathrm{C}_0\simeq-19$ fm and the effective range $r^\mathrm{C}_\mathrm{eff}\simeq0.45$ fm, which indicates $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ is located in the unitary regime.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ system in the $^1S_0$ channel, when Coulomb repulsion is included using the charge form factor of $\Omega_{ccc}$, is located in the unitary regime, meaning it is close to having an infinite scattering length. This claim is conditional on the specific treatment of Coulomb repulsion and the use of the HAL QCD method with nearly physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors used the time-dependent HAL QCD method on $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD configurations with a $96^4$ lattice, Iwasaki gauge action, and nonperturbatively $O(a)$-improved Wilson quark action. They computed the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) amplitude for two $\Omega_{ccc}$ baryons in the $^1S_0$ channel, extracted an effective central potential via a leading-order derivative expansion, and then solved the Schrödinger equation to obtain scattering phase shifts and low-energy scattering parameters. Coulomb repulsion was incorporated using the charge form factor of $\Omega_{ccc}$ from prior lattice calculations.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length without Coulomb repulsion: $a_0 = 1.57(0.08)\left(^{+0.12}_{-0.04}\right)$ fm
- Effective range without Coulomb repulsion: $r_\mathrm{eff} = 0.57(0.02)\left(^{+0.01}_{-0.00}\right)$ fm
- Binding energy of the strong-interaction bound state: $B \simeq 5.7$ MeV
- Root-mean-square distance of the bound state: $\sqrt{\braket{r^2}} \simeq 1.1$ fm
- Scattering length with Coulomb repulsion: $a^\mathrm{C}_0 = -19(7)\left(^{+7}_{-6}\right)$ fm
- Effective range with Coulomb repulsion: $r^\mathrm{C}_\mathrm{eff} = 0.45(0.01)\left(^{+0.01}_{-0.00}\right)$ fm
- Lattice spacing: $a \simeq 0.0846$ fm ($a^{-1} \simeq 2.333$ GeV)
- Pion mass: $m_\pi \simeq 146$ MeV
- Kaon mass: $m_K \simeq 525$ MeV
- Interpolated $\Omega_{ccc}$ mass: $m_{\Omega_{ccc}} \simeq 4796$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of dibaryons in the light-quark sector, such as $p\Omega$ (Iritani 2019) and $\Omega\Omega$ (Gongyo 2018), to the charm sector for the first time. It also builds on phenomenological and lattice studies of charmed dibaryons (Huang 2020, Richard 2020, Junnarkar 2019). The finding that $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ is near unitarity enables future studies of the quark mass dependence of dibaryon scattering parameters, including the bottom counterpart $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$.
