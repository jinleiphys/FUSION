# Chiral restoration phase transition within the quarkyonic matter

> We overview a possible mechanism for confining but chirally symmetric matter at low temperatures and large densities. As a new development we employ a diffused quark Fermi surface and show that such diffusion does not destroy possible existence of a confining but chirally symmetric matter at low temperatures and large density.

## Key claim

The authors show that a confining but chirally symmetric phase of dense matter (quarkyonic matter) can exist even when the quark Fermi surface is diffused rather than rigid, provided the diffusion width is not too large; for any reasonable diffusion width there always exists a critical Fermi momentum at which chiral restoration occurs.

## Method

The authors employ a model with an instantaneous linear Coulomb-like confining potential (inspired by Coulomb gauge QCD) and solve the gap equation for the quark self-energy in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature, assuming a liquid phase with unbroken translational and rotational symmetries. They parameterize the valence quark distribution near the Fermi surface with a smooth function that includes a diffusion width Δ, then solve the gap equation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states to determine the critical Fermi momentum for chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

- The critical Fermi momentum for chiral restoration depends on the diffusion width Δ; for each fixed Δ there exists a critical "Fermi momentum" at which the chiral restoration phase transition takes place (see Fig. 4, which shows the critical line separating broken and restored chiral symmetry phases).
- For the rigid Fermi surface (Δ = 0), the chiral restoration occurs at a specific critical Fermi momentum (shown in Fig. 2 as the point where the quark condensate vanishes).
- Above the critical Fermi momentum, the pion and σ-meson masses become exactly degenerate (Fig. 5 shows masses in units of √σ as functions of p_f and Δ).
- Lattice simulations for N_c=2 QCD suggest deconfinement at low temperatures occurs at densities of order 50 times nuclear matter density (citing Hands).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Glozman et al., 2010) that assumed a rigid quark Fermi surface and found chiral restoration within confining matter. It addresses the realistic concern that quarks near the Fermi surface cluster into color-singlet baryons, which would smooth the distribution. The results reconfirm the possible existence of a confining but chirally symmetric phase (quarkyonic matter) at low temperatures and high densities, which is distinct from the deconfined quark-gluon plasma. The work is motivated by large N_c arguments (Pisarski, 2000) and lattice results for N_c=2 (Hands, 2008), and uses a model previously applied to study chiral symmetry breaking in vacuum and hadrons (the 't Hooft model generalization).
