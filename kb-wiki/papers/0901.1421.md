# Shear Viscosity from Gauss-Bonnet Gravity with a Dilaton Coupling

> We calculate the shear viscosity of field theories with gravity duals of Gauss-Bonnet gravity with a non-trivial dilaton using AdS/CFT. We find that the dilaton filed has a non-trivial contribution to the ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density and after imposing causal constraint for the boundary field theory, the new lower bound $4/25π$, obtained from pure Gauss-Bonnet gravity, may have a small violation.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density in Gauss-Bonnet gravity with a nontrivial dilaton coupling receives a dilaton-dependent correction, and after imposing causal constraints from the boundary field theory, the previously proposed lower bound of \(4/25\pi\) from pure Gauss-Bonnet gravity may be slightly violated, though the violation is small.

## Method

The authors calculate the effective action of transverse graviton perturbations in a five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity action with a non-minimally coupled dilaton field, using a general formalism based on a three-dimensional effective metric. They derive the shear viscosity via the Kubo formula from the on-shell action of the gravitons, and impose causality constraints on the boundary field theory by analyzing the effective speed of graviton propagation along bouncing geodesics in the large-momentum limit.

## Key numbers

* For pure Gauss-Bonnet gravity without dilaton, the ratio \(\eta/s = (1-4\lambda)/4\pi\), which reduces to the KSS bound \(1/4\pi\) when \(\lambda=0\).
* The new lower bound from pure Gauss-Bonnet gravity under causality constraints is \(4/25\pi\), which is smaller than \(1/4\pi\).
* The dilaton-corrected ratio is \(\eta/s = \frac{1}{4\pi}\big(1-4\lambda e^{(\tau-\gamma)\phi_d(1)}(1-24\gamma^2\lambda e^{(\tau-\gamma)\phi_d(1)}+3\gamma\tau)\big)\), where \(\phi_d(1)\) is the dilaton value at the horizon and \(\gamma\), \(\tau\) are coupling constants.
* The entropy density for the Ricci-flat black hole background is \(s = \frac{1}{4G}\frac{r_+^3}{l^3}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of shear viscosity in higher-derivative gravity theories, following the discovery by Brigante et al. (2007, 2008) that \(R^2\) corrections can violate the KSS bound of \(1/4\pi\) and lead to a new lower bound of \(4/25\pi\). The authors build on the membrane paradigm approach of Iqbal and Liu (2008) and the effective action formalism of Cai et al. (2008), but go beyond those by considering a non-minimally coupled dilaton as found in string theory low-energy effective actions, specifically the model of Guo et al. (2008). The result shows that the dilaton introduces additional corrections to \(\eta/s\) and may slightly violate the \(4/25\pi\) bound, motivating further study of causality constraints in more general string-inspired gravity theories.
