# Particle Number Fluctuations in High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions from Microscopic Transport Approaches

> Event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the HSD and UrQMD transport models. The scaled variances of negative, positive, and all charged hadrons in Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV are analyzed in comparison to the data from the NA49 Collaboration. We find a dominant role of the fluctuations in the nucleon participant number for the final hadron multiplicity fluctuations. This fact can be used to check different scenarios of nucleus-nucleus collisions by measuring the final multiplicity fluctuations as a function of collision centrality. The analysis reveals surprising effects in the recent NA49 data which indicate a rather strong mixing of the projectile and target hadron production sources even in peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The dominant source of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV is the fluctuation in the number of target participant nucleons, even when the number of projectile participants is fixed; the NA49 data require a much stronger mixing of projectile and target hadron production sources than the HSD and UrQMD transport models generate, especially in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The HSD and UrQMD microscopic transport models were used to simulate minimum-bias Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV. Events were sorted into samples with fixed numbers of projectile participant nucleons (N_P^proj), matching the NA49 trigger condition, and the scaled variances ω_i of negative, positive, and all charged hadron multiplicities were computed for the NA49 acceptance (1.1 < y < 2.6 in the c.m.s. rapidity) and compared to NA49 data. The fluctuations of target participant number N_P^targ were also extracted from the simulations, and the contributions to the total scaled variance were decomposed into a term from a single source (ω_i^*) and a term from fluctuations in the number of sources (ω_P n_i).

## Key numbers

- Scaled variances from HSD and UrQMD in the NA49 acceptance: ω_- ≈ ω_+ ≈ 1.2, ω_ch ≈ 1.5, with almost no dependence on N_P^proj.
- NA49 data show maximum values at N_P^proj ≈ 50: ω_- ≈ ω_+ ≈ 2, ω_ch ≈ 3, with strong N_P^proj dependence.
- Scaled variance of target participant number fluctuations, ω_P^targ, reaches 3–3.5 at N_P^proj = 20–30.
- For elementary N+N collisions at 158 GeV from HSD: ω_ch^pp = 2.1, ω_ch^pn = 2.4, ω_ch^nn = 2.9; average multiplicities N_ch^pp = 6.2, N_ch^pn = 5.8, N_ch^nn = 5.4.
- The acceptance factor q_i^p ≈ 0.18 for the NA49 acceptance in the projectile hemisphere.
- The "correlation length" in rapidity for the influence of target participant fluctuations is Δy ≈ 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously addressed in statistical models and by the NA49 Collaboration, by using the HSD and UrQMD transport models to isolate the role of participant number fluctuations. The analysis builds on the "participant model" of fluctuations (fluc7) and the method of marking flows via fluctuations proposed by MGMG (2005). The finding that NA49 data require stronger mixing than the transport models provide challenges the hadron/string dynamics picture and motivates further fluctuation studies at higher energies, such as at RHIC.
