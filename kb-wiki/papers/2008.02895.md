# Factorized approach to radiative corrections for inelastic lepton-hadron collisions

> We propose a factorized approach to QED radiative corrections for inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering to systematically account for QED and QCD radiation contributions to both processes on equal footing. This is achieved by utilizing factorization to resum logarithmically enhanced QED radiation into universal lepton distribution and fragmentation (or jet) functions. Numerical simulations suggest that the QED effects induced by the rotational distortion of the hadron transverse momentum, arising from the mismatch between the experimental Breit frame and the true photon-hadron frame, can be as large as 50\% for moderate $Q$, and become increasingly important for large transverse momenta. Our framework provides a uniform treatment of radiative effects for extracting three-dimensional hadron structure from high-energy lepton-hadron scattering at current and future facilities, such as the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a factorized approach to QED radiative corrections for inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering that treats QED and QCD radiation on equal footing, but the abstract's claim of "systematically accounting" for both processes glosses over the condition that the approach relies on collinear factorization for QED contributions and requires the minimum invariant mass of the exchanged virtual photon to be much larger than the QCD scale, which could reduce kinematic reach at small Bjorken x and large y.

## Method

The authors factorize the cross sections for inclusive DIS and semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) by resumming logarithmically enhanced QED radiation into universal lepton distribution functions (LDFs) and lepton fragmentation functions (LFFs), defined analogously to QCD parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions. For SIDIS, they treat QED radiation in collinear factorization and evaluate the partonic cross section in the virtual photon-nucleon frame using TMD factorization, then apply a Lorentz transformation to convert to the experimental frame. The LDFs and LFFs are calculated perturbatively in QED at next-to-leading order in the MSbar scheme, with the logarithmic-enhanced contributions resummed via QED evolution equations.

## Context

This work extends the factorized treatment of QED radiative corrections previously applied to inclusive DIS (Mo and Tsai, 1969; Bardin, 1989; Badelek, 1994) and e+e- collisions (Blumlein, 2007; Frixione, 2019; Bertone, 2019) to also cover semi-inclusive DIS, providing a unified framework. It enables systematic extraction of transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) from current and future facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider, by treating QED and QCD contributions consistently rather than relying on Monte Carlo simulations or matching to Born processes, which becomes impractical for more exclusive reactions.
