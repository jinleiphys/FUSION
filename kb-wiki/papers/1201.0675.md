# The baryon number two system in the Chiral Soliton Model

> We study the interaction between two B = 1 states in a Chiral Soliton Model where baryons are described as non-topological solitons. By using the hedgehog solution for the B = 1 states we construct three possible B = 2 configurations to analyze the role of the relative orientation of the hedgehog quills in the dynamics. The strong dependence of the intersoliton interaction on these relative orientations reveals that studies of dense hadronic matter using this model should take into account their implications.

## Key claim

The interaction between two baryons in the Chiral Soliton Model depends strongly on the relative orientation of the hedgehog quills, but the authors caution that their first-order calculations without modifications to the quark wavefunctions are too primitive to draw conclusions about the short-distance behavior of the potential.

## Method

The authors constructed three possible B=2 configurations by taking the product of two B=1 hedgehog soliton solutions with different relative isospin rotations (unrotated, rotated by pi about the axis joining the centers, and rotated by pi about the perpendicular axis). They calculated the soliton-soliton interaction energy by substituting these approximated meson field configurations into the Hamiltonian and evaluating expectation values with respect to B=2 states where three quarks occupy each energy level described by linear combinations of the single-soliton quark wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The B=1 hedgehog solution has a binding energy of about 400 MeV, with the meson field energy costing more than 800 MeV compensated by quark binding energies
- The rms radius of the baryon number distribution for the B=1 solution is about 0.7 fm
- Model parameters used: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, g = 5, f_pi = 93 MeV
- Configurations A and B are repulsive for all separation distances d, with B more repulsive at large distance
- At large separation, configuration C is the most stable state

## Context

This work extends the non-topological soliton model of Friedberg and Lee (1976) by implementing chiral symmetry and studying two-body dynamics, in contrast to the Skyrme model approach where baryons are topological solitons. The authors aim to establish a foundation for describing baryonic matter where both the color phase transition and chiral phase transition are governed by the same mechanism, and where long-range effects of intersoliton dynamics can be studied. The results resemble closely the Skyrme description of Lee (2003) at large separations, but the explicit quark degrees of freedom represent a new feature compared to that approach.
