# Neutral meson properties in hot and magnetized quark matter: a new magnetic field independent regularization scheme applied to NJL-type model

> A magnetic field independent regularization scheme (zMFIR) based on the Hurwitz-Riemann zeta function is introduced. The new technique is applied to the regularization of the mean-field thermodynamic potential and mass gap equation within the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in a hot and magnetized medium. The equivalence of the new and the standard MFIR scheme is demonstrated. The neutral meson pole mass is calculated in a hot and magnetized medium and the advantages of using the new regularization scheme are shown.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a new magnetic field independent regularization scheme (zMFIR) based on the Hurwitz-Riemann zeta function that separates magnetic from non-magnetic contributions exactly, and demonstrate its equivalence to the standard MFIR scheme while offering advantages for calculations where the standard scheme is difficult to apply, such as neutral meson pole masses in hot and magnetized medium.

## Method

The authors reformulate the density of states for charged fermions in a magnetic field using the Hurwitz-Riemann zeta function, obtaining an exact separation into magnetic and non-magnetic contributions. They apply this zMFIR scheme to regularize the mean-field thermodynamic potential and mass gap equation within the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature and magnetic field, then calculate the neutral pion pole mass using the RPA approximation for the polarization loop.

## Key numbers

- At eB = 0.1 GeV², the vacuum energy calculated via the new zMFIR scheme (E_vac2) and the standard MFIR scheme (E_vac1) agree with relative errors between 1.15×10⁻⁷ and 2.87×10⁻⁷ for effective masses ranging from 10.95 MeV to 408.93 MeV
- The magnetic vacuum contribution is evaluated at eB = 0.1 GeV² for five effective mass values: 408.93 MeV, 368.82 MeV, 118.43 MeV, 43.19 MeV, and 10.95 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MFIR regularization approach previously developed by Norberto et al. and Farias et al., which demonstrated that inappropriate regularization schemes can produce unphysical oscillations in quantities like effective quark masses and imaginary meson masses. The zMFIR scheme adapts the Hurwitz-Riemann zeta function formalism from the study of magnetized relativistic electron gases by Elizalde et al. (1994) to effective QCD models, enabling cleaner calculations of meson properties and thermodynamic quantities where the standard MFIR scheme (using dimensional regularization as in Klimenko (1992) or Nosso et al.) becomes difficult, particularly for polarization loops with poles above the Mott temperature.
