# Isospin Fluctuations from Multiple Domains of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> We describe some detailed numerical simulations of Disoriented Chiral Condensates (DCCs), using the chiral lagrangian as a controlled long-wavelength description. We focus on the possibility of multiple, independently coherent domains, and investigate the degree to which the DCC signal is attenuated. As an intermediate step in our analysis we compute the expected number of detector events in each isospin and momentum channel for a given asymptotic classical field configuration. We find that for sufficiently large initial field strengths, the non-linear interactions between domains become important and can lead to a randomization of isospin orientations. Nevertheless, we argue that viable signals exist for DCC detection, even in the case of multiple domains and strong domain-domain interactions. We briefly discuss some long-lived `pseudo-bound state' configurations which arise at large field strengths and might be observable in HBT correlations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that viable experimental signals for Disoriented Chiral Condensate (DCC) detection exist even with multiple, independently coherent domains and strong domain-domain interactions, but this conclusion is hedged by the finding that for sufficiently large initial field strengths (greater than approximately 1.4 Fπ), non-linear interactions between domains become important and can lead to a randomization of isospin orientations, attenuating the signal.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of domain-domain interactions using the classical field equations derived from the chiral lagrangian (non-linear sigma model) in a cylindrical geometry with axial symmetry. They evolved the fields from initial configurations representing multiple domains with different isospin orientations and computed the expected number of detector events in each isospin and momentum channel for the resulting asymptotic classical field configuration.

## Key numbers
- For a single coherent domain, the probability distribution for the fraction of neutral pions f is given by dP(f) = df / 2√f.
- The standard deviation of f for k coherent domains is σ_coherent = 2 / (3√(5k)).
- The standard deviation of f for N incoherently produced pions is σ_incoherent = (1/3)√(2/N).
- Non-linear effects become significant at field strengths as small as |π| ≃ 1.4 Fπ.
- For the k=2 case, the width of the distribution of f does not change more than 10% up to initial field strengths of 1.6 Fπ.
- For N=100 incoherently produced pions, the probability of measuring f ≥ 0.6 is 4.3×10⁻⁸.
- For a single domain, 23% of events lie above f=0.6.
- Pseudo-bound state dispersion times as long as 10⁻²² s were observed, versus 10⁻²⁴ s for the noninteracting case.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of DCCs, which were often performed in the context of the linear sigma model using approximations like the 1/N expansion (Rajagopal 1993, Cooper 1992, Boyanovsky 1995, Kluger 1992). The authors adopt an agnostic approach to DCC formation and instead use the chiral lagrangian to provide a controlled, long-wavelength description of the subsequent evolution of multiple domains. The results enable future analysis of RHIC data to place upper bounds on the probabilities of multiple domain formation or to discover coherent phenomena in heavy ion collisions, and suggest that long-lived pseudo-bound state configurations might be observable in HBT correlations.
