# The operator product expansion does not imply parity doubling of hadrons

> We examine whether the fact that QCD is chirally invariant at short distances necessarily leads to the prediction that hadrons form approximate parity doublets, as has been recently put forward by Glozman and collaborators. We show that this is not the case, and we exhibit some of the pitfalls of trying to link the operator product expansion to the hadron spectrum. We illustrate our arguments within a model for scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons used recently by Shifman to argue for parity doubling. We find that, whatever the experimental situation may be, there is no theoretical basis for parity doubling based on the use of the operator product expansion.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the operator product expansion (OPE) does not force hadrons to form approximate parity doublets at high excitation, contrary to claims by Glozman and collaborators and by Shifman. They show that the superconvergence relation used to derive parity doubling cannot be derived from the OPE, and that models consistent with the OPE can exhibit no chiral symmetry restoration at all.

## Method

The authors work in the large-\(N_c\) limit where resonances are stable and have well-defined masses, and assume Regge-like behavior \(M_{S_n,P_n}^2 \sim n\Lambda^2\) for scalar and pseudo-scalar meson towers. They compare the once-subtracted dispersion relation for the difference of the scalar and pseudo-scalar two-point functions with the leading OPE behavior \(\Pi_{S-P}(Q^2) \sim \langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle^2 / Q^4\) at large Euclidean momentum \(Q^2\). They then construct an explicit model with equally-spaced towers and constant mass splitting \(\delta M_n^2 = m_S^2 - m_P^2\) and verify that it satisfies all OPE constraints without requiring parity doubling.

## Key numbers

- The spectral overlap measure \(\rho(n) = (M_{P_n} - M_{S_n})/(M_{S_{n+1}} - M_{S_n})\) is defined; in the 't Hooft model (2D QCD at \(N_c=\infty\)), \(\lim_{n\to\infty} |\rho(n)| = 1/2\), showing maximal chiral symmetry breaking.
- In the authors' 4D counterexample model with \(M_{S_n,P_n}^2 = m_{S,P}^2 + n\Lambda^2\) and constant \(\delta M_n^2 = m_S^2 - m_P^2\), the asymptotic limit is \(\lim_{n\to\infty} \rho(n) = \min\{(m_P^2 - m_S^2)/\Lambda^2, (m_S^2 + \Lambda^2 - m_P^2)/\Lambda^2\}\), which is a non-zero constant.
- The model's OPE coefficients are matched to low-energy constants: \(16B^2 L_8 = \kappa(m_P^2 - m_S^2)\), \(B^2 f_0^2 = \frac{1}{2}\kappa(m_P^4 - m_S^4 + \Lambda^2(m_P^2 - m_S^2))\), and \(6\pi\alpha_S \langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle^2 = \frac{1}{6}\kappa(2(m_P^6 - m_S^6) + 3\Lambda^2(m_P^4 - m_S^4) + \Lambda^4(m_P^2 - m_S^2))\), where \(\kappa = N_c/32\pi^2\).
- In the 't Hooft model, the alternative measure \(\chi(n) = (M_{P_n} - M_{S_n})/(M_{P_n} + M_{S_n})\) falls as \(\chi(n) \sim 1/(4n)\) for large \(n\), but this is shown to be a trivial consequence of the denominator growing with \(n\), not a sign of chiral restoration.

## Context

This paper directly challenges the claims of Glozman and collaborators and Shifman (2005) that the OPE implies parity doubling for highly excited hadrons. It extends the earlier critique by Golterman and Peris (2005) that superconvergence relations cannot be derived from the OPE. The authors use the 't Hooft model (1974) as a solvable counterexample in two dimensions, and construct a four-dimensional Regge-like model to show that the OPE is consistent with no chiral symmetry restoration at all. The work enables a clearer understanding of what the OPE can and cannot say about the hadron spectrum, and warns against misinterpreting spectral function falloffs at finite \(N_c\) as evidence for parity doubling.
