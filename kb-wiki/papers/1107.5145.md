# Relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions in charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering at MiniBooNE kinematics

> The results of two relativistic models with different descriptions of the final-state interactions are compared with the MiniBooNE data of charged-current quasielastic cross sections. The relativistic mean field model uses the same potential for the bound and ejected nucleon wave functions. In the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model the final-state interactions are described in the inclusive scattering consistently with the exclusive scattering using the same complex optical potential. The RGF results describe the experimental data for total cross-sections without the need to modify the nucleon axial mass.

## Key claim
The Relativistic Green's Function (RGF) model can describe the MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross section data without modifying the nucleon axial mass from its standard value of 1.03 GeV/c^2, but only when using the EDAI optical potential; the RGF prediction is not univocally determined from elastic phenomenology because different optical potentials (EDAD1 versus EDAI) give different results.

## Method
The authors compared two relativistic models for CCQE neutrino-nucleus scattering on 12C: the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model, which uses the same real potential for bound and ejected nucleon states, and the Relativistic Green's Function (RGF) model, which uses complex optical potentials (EDAD1 and EDAI) fitted to elastic proton-nucleus scattering data and recovers inelastic contributions via dispersion relations. Both models employed self-consistent Dirac-Hartree bound states from a RMF approach with sigma, omega, and rho mesons, and used the standard axial mass MA = 1.03 GeV/c^2. The flux-averaged double differential cross sections and total cross sections were computed and compared to MiniBooNE data.

## Key numbers
- Standard nucleon axial mass used: MA = 1.03 GeV/c^2 (not modified)
- RGF with EDAI potential gave total CCQE cross sections in good agreement with the shape and magnitude of the unfolded MiniBooNE data
- RGF with EDAD1 potential gave total cross sections above RMF but clearly below the data
- RMF results generally underestimate the data, especially for lower muon kinetic energy (T_mu) and smaller scattering angles
- The RGF-EDAI and RGF-EDAD1 differences are sizeable for the bin 0.7 < cos(theta) < 0.8, with RGF-EDAD1 closer to RMF than RGF-EDAI
- For the bin 0.2 < T_mu < 0.3 GeV, RMF underestimates data more than for the bin 0.6 < T_mu < 0.7 GeV
- The overall normalization error on MiniBooNE data is delta N = 10.7%

## Context
This work extends previous comparisons of RMF and RGF models for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering (Meucci 2011, Caballero 2007) to the neutrino sector at MiniBooNE kinematics. It addresses the longstanding discrepancy where relativistic Fermi Gas and relativistic impulse approximation models required an enlarged axial mass (1.35 GeV/c^2) to fit MiniBooNE data (MiniBooNE collaboration 2010), while models incorporating two-particle-two-hole excitations via meson-exchange currents (Nieves 2011, Martini) could fit without increasing MA. The results show that proper treatment of final-state interactions via the RGF approach can also describe the data with the standard axial mass, though the sensitivity to the optical potential parameterization indicates that a better-determined relativistic optical potential fulfilling dispersion relations is needed. This is important for reconciling RMF results with previous CCQE data (Crispin 2006) and for neutrino oscillation analyses.
