# Ultraviolet extrapolations in finite oscillator bases

> The use of finite harmonic oscillator spaces in many-body calculations introduces both infrared (IR) and ultraviolet (UV) errors. The IR effects are well approximated by imposing a hard-wall boundary condition at a properly identified radius L_eff. We show that duality of the oscillator implies that the UV effects are equally well described by imposing a sharp momentum cutoff at a momentum Lambda_eff complementary to L_eff. By considering two-body systems with separable potentials, we show that the UV energy corrections depend on details of the potential, in contrast to the IR energy corrections, which depend only on the S-matrix. An adaptation of the separable treatment to more general interactions is developed and applied to model potentials as well as to the deuteron with realistic potentials. The previous success with a simple phenomenological form for the UV error is also explained. Possibilities for controlled extrapolations for A > 2 based on scaling arguments are discussed.

## Key claim

The ultraviolet (UV) error from truncating a harmonic oscillator basis can be accurately modeled by imposing a sharp momentum cutoff at an effective momentum \(\Lambda_{\text{eff}}\) complementary to the infrared effective radius \(L_{\text{eff}}\), but unlike the universal infrared corrections, the UV energy corrections depend on the high-momentum details of the potential rather than only on low-energy observables.

## Method

The authors established the effective UV cutoff \(\Lambda_2 = \sqrt{2(N_{\text{max}}+3/2+2)}/b\) by duality with the infrared case, matching the smallest eigenvalue of the squared position operator in the truncated oscillator basis. They then derived an exact extrapolation formula for rank-one separable potentials with regulator functions \(f_\lambda(k)\), solving the quantized Schrödinger equation with a sharp momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\). This separable treatment was adapted to more general interactions using a fitting procedure, tested on model potentials, and applied to the deuteron with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials evolved by the similarity renormalization group (SRG).

## Key numbers

- The effective infrared box size for two-body systems is \(L_2 = \sqrt{2(N+3/2+2)}b\), differing by \(\mathcal{O}(1/N)\) from the naive estimate \(L_0 = \sqrt{2(N+3/2)}b\).
- The effective UV cutoff is \(\Lambda_2 = \sqrt{2(N_{\text{max}}+3/2+2)}/b\), with the naive estimate \(\Lambda_0 = \sqrt{2(N_{\text{max}}+3/2)}/b\).
- For the deuteron with the N\(^3\)LO NN potential (500 MeV regulator cutoff, SRG-evolved to \(\lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\)), the relative error \(\Delta E_d/E_d\) runs over six decades when plotted against \(L_2\) for UV-converged points with \(4 < N_{\text{max}} < 100\) and \(1\ \text{MeV} < \Omega < 100\ \text{MeV}\).
- For a separable contact interaction with quartic regulator \(f_\lambda(k) = e^{-(k/\lambda)^4}\), \(\lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), and coupling \(a = -0.1\) fm, the binding momentum is \(\kappa_\infty \approx 0.634\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The asymptotic form for super-Gaussian regulators gives \(\Delta E_\Lambda(\Lambda) \propto (\Lambda/\lambda)^{1-2n} e^{-2(\Lambda/\lambda)^{2n}}\) for \(\Lambda \gg \lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the infrared extrapolation framework developed by Furnstahl et al. (2012, 2013) and More et al. (2013), which showed that IR errors from oscillator truncation are universal and depend only on the S-matrix. The authors demonstrate that UV errors are not universal, depending on the high-momentum behavior of the potential, and explain why the phenomenological Gaussian ansatz proposed by Furnstahl et al. (2012) and used by Jurgenson et al. (2013) for \(A > 2\) works in limited fitting windows. The separable-approximation approach builds on the classic work of Ernst et al. (1973) and enables controlled extrapolations for two-body systems, with scaling arguments suggested for extension to \(A > 2\).
