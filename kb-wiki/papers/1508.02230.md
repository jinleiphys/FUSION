# Simple way to the high-temperature expansion of relativistic Fermi-Dirac integrals

> The pressure of an ideal relativistic Fermi gas is computed as an infinite series for high temperatures at nonzero chemical potentials. The expansion of the particle number density, scalar density, and entropy density as first derivatives of the pressure is also found.

## Key claim

The paper derives a high-temperature expansion of the relativistic Fermi-Dirac integral for the pressure of an ideal Fermi gas as an infinite series in powers of the mass-to-temperature ratio \(\lambda = m/T\) and the chemical-potential-to-temperature ratio \(\nu = \mu/T\), valid for \(\lambda + |\nu| < \pi\), expressed entirely through elementary functions and polynomials without complex special functions in the final result.

## Method

The author starts from the integral representation of the pressure for an ideal relativistic Fermi gas and expands the modified Bessel function \(K_2(k\lambda)\) in a series, then resums the resulting double series using polylogarithms \(\Li_s(z)\). By separating the pressure into even and odd parts in \(\nu\) and substituting series expansions of the polylogarithms and their derivatives with respect to the index, the final expressions are reduced to sums over hypergeometric polynomials that are elementary functions. The same technique is applied to obtain expansions for the particle number density, scalar density, and entropy density as first derivatives of the pressure.

## Key numbers

- The leading term of the even part of the dimensionless pressure for fermions at \(\lambda=0\) is \(I_P(0,\nu) = -\frac{1}{\pi^2}\Li_4(-e^\nu)\), which for \(\nu=0\) gives \(I_P^e(0,0) = \frac{7\pi^2}{720}\).
- The even part of the pressure expansion for fermions includes the term \(-\frac{\lambda^2}{16}\left(\frac{1}{3}+\frac{\nu^2}{\pi^2}\right)\) and the term \(-\frac{\lambda^4}{32\pi^2}\left(\ln\frac{\lambda}{\pi}+\gamma_E-\frac34\right)\), where \(\gamma_E\) is Euler's constant.
- The series representation of \(I_P^e(\lambda,\nu)\) converges for \(\lambda+|\nu|<\pi\).
- For bosons, the leading even term at \(\lambda=0,\nu=0\) is \(\frac{\pi^2}{90}\), and the expansion includes the term \(\frac{(\lambda^2-\nu^2)^{3/2}}{12\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the high-temperature expansion method of Haber and Weldon (1989) for Bose gases to Fermi gases, and provides an alternative to the complex-function approach of Klajn (2015) by expressing results entirely through elementary functions and polynomials. The expansions enable fast analytical calculations of thermodynamic quantities for relativistic Fermi gases at high temperature and nonzero chemical potential, which are useful for studying equations of state in applications such as the chiral transition in QCD (Ohnishi, 2000).
