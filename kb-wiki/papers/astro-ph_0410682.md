# Structure of the electrospheres of bare strange stars

> We consider a thin ($\sim 10^2-10^3$ fm) layer of electrons (the electrosphere) at the quark surface of a bare strange star, taking into account the surface effects at the boundary with the vacuum. The quark surface holds the electron layer by an extremely strong electric field, generated in the electrosphere to prevent the electrons from escaping to infinity by counterbalancing the degeneracy and thermal pressure. Because of the surface tension and depletion of $s$ quarks a very thin (a few fm) charged layer of quarks forms at the surface of the star. The formation of this layer modifies the structure of the electrosphere, by significantly changing the electric field and the density of the electrons, in comparison with the case when the surface effects are ignored. Some consequences of the modification of the electrosphere structure on the properties of strange stars are briefly discussed.

# Structure of the electrospheres of bare strange stars

## Key claim

The paper shows that a thin charged layer at the quark surface of a bare strange star, formed by surface depletion of s quarks, significantly modifies the electrosphere structure compared to earlier models that ignored surface effects, with the magnitude of the modification depending on the surface temperature and the strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors use a Thomas-Fermi model to solve the Poisson equation for the electrostatic potential in the electrosphere, incorporating both finite temperature effects and the surface tension of strange quark matter. They treat the charged surface layer as infinitesimal, producing a discontinuity in the electric field at the quark-vacuum boundary, and solve for the electrostatic potential and electron density distributions both inside and outside the strange quark matter surface for different QCD phases (unpaired, CFL, 2SC).

## Key numbers

- The electric field jump at the SQM surface due to surface depletion of s quarks is ΔE ≃ 5.5 × 10¹⁸ V cm⁻¹ ≃ 4 × 10² E_cr, about ten times larger than the surface electric field calculated when surface effects are neglected.
- For the most conservative SQM parameters (m_s ≃ 150 MeV, μ ≃ 300 MeV), the surface potential V₀ ≃ 2.2 V_q, which is 2.93 times larger than the value V₀ = (3/4)V_q found when surface effects are ignored.
- At the SQM surface with surface effects included, the external electric field is E_ext(z=0) ≃ 4 × 10¹⁸ V cm⁻¹ ≃ 3 × 10² E_cr and the electron density is n_e(z=0) ≃ 3.7 × 10³⁵ cm⁻³.
- The surface effects increase the external electric field by a factor of 8.6 and the electron density by a factor of 25 compared to models ignoring surface effects.
- The parameter η characterizing surface effect importance ranges from ∼ 3 × 10² at m_s ≃ 50 MeV and low temperatures to ∼ 0.1 at m_s ≃ 300 MeV and T_S ≃ 40 MeV.
- For η₀ ≃ 9 (conservative parameters), η ≃ 9 [1 + (T_S/4.5 MeV)²]⁻¹/², remaining > 1 for T_S < 40 MeV.
- The typical electrosphere thickness at zero temperature is z₀ ≃ 125 fm for eV₀ = 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the electrosphere models of Alcock, Farhi, and Olinto (1986) and Kettner et al. (1995) by incorporating surface depletion of s quarks as proposed by Usov (2004), and considers different QCD phases including unpaired, CFL, and 2SC phases. The results enable more accurate calculations of thermal emission from bare strange stars, particularly the luminosity in e⁺e⁻ pairs (which may increase by up to a factor of ten at high temperatures) and the suppression of quark-quark bremsstrahlung radiation, and will modify the cooling curves previously calculated by Page and Usov (2002).
