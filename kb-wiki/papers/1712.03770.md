# Clusters and higher moments of proton number fluctuations

> Production of deuterons by coalescence has an important influence on the moments of the observed proton number distribution. Therefore, this effect must be taken into account when physics conclusions about baryon number fluctuation are drawn from the measurement of proton number fluctuations. We also show that a measurement of the third and fourth moments of the deuteron number distribution would allow to distinguish whether deuterons are produced statistically or by coalescence.

## Key claim

Deuteron production via coalescence significantly modifies the higher moments (skewness and kurtosis) of the observed proton number distribution, so this effect must be accounted for before drawing conclusions about baryon number fluctuations from proton measurements. The authors also claim that measuring the third and fourth moments of the deuteron number distribution can distinguish between statistical (Poissonian) and coalescence production mechanisms.

## Method

The authors construct a model where initial proton numbers fluctuate according to a Poisson distribution, and deuteron numbers in each event are drawn from a Poisson distribution whose mean is proportional to the square of the initial proton number (coalescence model). They fold these distributions to obtain the observed proton number distribution (subtracting deuterons from initial protons) and the deuteron number distribution, then compute the variance, skewness, and kurtosis. For higher collision energies where antiprotons appear, they perform Monte Carlo simulations. They also compare two variants: Model A (deuteron mean proportional to initial proton number squared) and Model B (deuteron mean proportional to product of initial proton and neutron numbers).

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.6 GeV with mean observed proton number 83.4 and coalescence parameter B = 1.6e-3, the scaled skewness S*sigma and scaled kurtosis kappa*sigma^2 for protons can drop as low as 0.2 (compared to 1 for Poisson).
- For the same parameters, Model A deuteron distribution yields: sigma^2/<n_d> = 1.609, S*sigma = 2.218, kappa*sigma^2 = 6.915.
- For Model B at the same energy: sigma^2/<n_d> = 1.308, S*sigma = 1.616, kappa*sigma^2 = 3.422.
- The deuteron-to-proton ratio is parametrized as <n_d>/<n_p> = 0.8 * [sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV)]^{-1.55} + 0.0036.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon number fluctuations via proton measurements (Kitazawa and Asakawa, 2012) by incorporating the previously neglected effect of deuteron coalescence. It builds on lattice QCD calculations of higher-order susceptibilities (Bazavov, 2017; Gunther, 2017) and on earlier work showing that final-state hadronic interactions affect fluctuation measurements (Steinheimer, 2015). The results enable experimentalists to correct proton fluctuation measurements for deuteron effects and motivate measurements of deuteron number higher moments at facilities like the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC.
