# Consistent approach to study gluon quasi-particles

> We discuss a novel approach to estimate the partition function in effective model frameworks when the effective potentials have multiple extrema, so that ascertaining a mean field becomes difficult. Using this approach we present a consistent model to study the thermodynamic properties of gluon quasi-particles as a function of temperature, both in the color confined and the color deconfined phases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing problem of negative gluon quasi-particle pressure in the confined phase (below the deconfinement temperature T_d) arises from using the saddle-point approximation to obtain the mean Polyakov loop, and that a novel "pseudo path integral" approach that integrates over all field configurations instead of just the saddle point resolves this inconsistency.

## Method

The authors construct a partition function for the Polyakov loop as a matrix model, integrating numerically over the two class parameters θ₁ and θ₂ of SU(3) with the Haar measure, using the gluon quasi-particle thermodynamic potential Ω_gqp as the effective action. They assume the configuration space splits into N independent and equivalent points, introducing a single free parameter v = (β₁/T_d)³ with β₁ ≈ 2, and then extract a temperature-dependent effective gluon mass m_g(T) by fitting the model pressure to lattice QCD data from Giusti (2016). Thermodynamic observables (entropy density, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound) are derived from the resulting pressure, and thermal averages of Polyakov loop operators are computed by integrating over all configurations with their thermal weights.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement temperature for pure glue: T_d ~ 270 MeV (first-order transition)
- Crossover temperature for full QCD: T_c ~ 150 MeV
- Fitted mass parameters: α = 0.548 ± 0.006, β = 0.174 ± 0.005, γ = 1.083 ± 0.004, ζ = 2.70 ± 0.07
- Volume parameter: v = (0.5 T_d)⁻³ GeV⁻³, with β₁ = 2
- For T/T_d > 1: m_g(T)/T = α + β/ln(γ T/T_d)
- For T/T_d < 1: m_g(T)/T = ζ (T_d/T)²
- The model reproduces lattice data from Boyd (1996) and Giusti (2016) for pressure, interaction measure, conformal measure, specific heat, and speed of sound
- For heavy quarks with m_q = 3 GeV, results are identical to pure gauge; the transition becomes a crossover between m_q = 1.65 GeV and 1.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the gluon quasi-particle approach of Sasaki (2012), Ruggieri (2012), Islam (2012), and Alba (2014), which all suffered from negative gluonic pressure below T_d and required ad-hoc additions (dilaton fields, bag terms, or pure matrix interaction terms) to restore positivity. The authors' new integration method eliminates the need for such extra terms while achieving excellent agreement with lattice data from Giusti (2016) and Boyd (1996). The approach enables consistent study of gluon thermodynamics from the confined to deconfined phase and can be extended to include heavy quarks, with preliminary results matching the findings of Kashiwa (2012) on the quark-mass dependence of the deconfinement transition order.
