# $\bar K^*$ meson in nuclear matter

> The properties of the $\bar K^*$ meson in dense matter are studied using a unitary approach in coupled channels within the framework of the local hidden gauge formalism. We obtain the $\bar K^*$ spectral function in the nuclear medium and we found that the $\bar K^*$ develops an in-medium width up to five times bigger than in free space. We also estimate the transparency ratio of the $γA \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction, which we propose as a feasible experimental scenario to detect in-medium modifications of the $\bar K^*$ meson.

## Key claim

The $\bar K^*$ meson in symmetric nuclear matter acquires an in-medium width up to five times larger than its free-space width, reaching about 260 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, but the quasiparticle peak is pushed to lower energies by interference with resonant-hole modes and the real part of the optical potential is only moderately attractive (-50 MeV at $\rho_0$).

## Method

The $\bar K^*$ self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter was computed using a unitary coupled-channel approach based on the local hidden gauge formalism. The Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved self-consistently in the nuclear medium for the strangeness $S=-1$ vector meson-baryon sector (isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$), incorporating Pauli blocking, the in-medium modification of the $\bar K \pi$ decay channel (including pion and antikaon self-energies and vertex corrections), and the self-consistent treatment of the $\bar K^* N$ interaction in the Fermi sea. The transparency ratio for the reaction $\gamma A \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ was then estimated to assess experimental feasibility.

## Key numbers

- Free $\bar K^*$ width from $\bar K \pi$ decay: 42 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental value $\Gamma^{\rm exp}_{K^{*-}} = 50.8 \pm 0.9$ MeV.
- $\bar K^*$ width from $\bar K \pi$ decay alone in medium at $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$: 105 MeV (about twice the vacuum value).
- Total $\bar K^*$ width at normal nuclear matter density ($\rho_0$): 260 MeV (about five times the free width).
- Real part of the $\bar K^*$ optical potential at $\rho_0$: -50 MeV (moderately attractive).
- Transparency ratio evaluated for $\sqrt{s} = 3$ GeV and 3.5 GeV (equivalent to photon lab energies of 4.3 GeV and 6 GeV, respectively), showing strong attenuation with increasing nuclear mass number $A$.

## Context

This work extends the study of vector meson properties in nuclear matter (previously focused on $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ mesons, as reviewed by Rapp, Hayano, and Mosel) to the strange vector meson $\bar K^*$, which had not been addressed in the medium before. It builds on the hidden gauge formalism for vector meson-baryon interactions developed by Oset et al. (2009) and follows a self-consistent approach similar to that employed by Ramos et al. (1999) and Tolos et al. (2006) for other mesons. The predicted strong in-medium broadening and the proposed transparency-ratio measurement in $\gamma A \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reactions provide a concrete experimental test for facilities such as those at JLab or ELSA.
