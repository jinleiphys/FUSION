# Energy dependence of the ridge in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions

> We demonstrate that the recent measurement of azimuthally collimated, long range rapidity ("ridge") correlations in $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV proton-proton (p+p) collisions by the ATLAS collaboration at the LHC are in agreement with expectations from the color glass condensate effective theory of high energy QCD. The observation that the integrated near side yield as a function of multiplicity is independent of collision energy is a natural consequence of the fact that multiparticle production is driven by a single semi-hard saturation scale in the color glass condensate framework. We argue further that the azimuthal structure of these recent ATLAS ridge measurements strongly constrain hydrodynamic interpretations of such correlations in high multiplicity p+p collisions.

# Energy dependence of the ridge in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions

## Key claim

The observed energy independence of the near-side ridge yield as a function of multiplicity in p+p collisions at 7 TeV and 13 TeV is a natural consequence of multiparticle production being driven by a single semi-hard saturation scale in the color glass condensate (CGC) framework, and the azimuthal structure of the ATLAS 13 TeV data strongly constrains hydrodynamic interpretations of such correlations.

## Method

The authors compute two-particle correlations in the glasma graph approximation of the CGC effective theory, combining contributions from glasma graphs (two independent ladders enhanced at high parton densities) and di-jet (BFKL) contributions. The unintegrated gluon distributions are obtained by solving the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) renormalization equation with initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, and high multiplicity events are modeled by using larger values of the initial nonperturbative scale Q0^2. The per-trigger yield Y_int is computed using k_T-factorized expressions with fragmentation functions (NLO KKP parametrization) and compared to ATLAS 13 TeV and CMS 7 TeV data.

## Key numbers

- The initial nonperturbative scale Q0^2 = 0.168 GeV^2 (fundamental representation) describes minimum bias HERA data; high multiplicity events correspond to Q0^2 values 5-6 times this minimum bias value.
- Saturation scales Qs ~ 1.5 GeV for minimum bias collisions and Qs ~ 3.0 GeV for central collisions (adjoint representation).
- The nonperturbative constant zeta = 1/6, extracted from independent k_T factorization analysis of n-particle multiplicity distributions.
- The K-factor in the dihadron calculation is K = 1.5, identical to the previous analysis at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV.
- The minimum transverse momentum for charged particle reconstruction is p_T^min = 0.4 GeV for both ATLAS and CMS.
- The minimum bias multiplicity at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is N_ch = 14.
- For the multiplicity bin N_ch > 110, the Q0^2 range is 0.672-0.840 GeV^2 at 13 TeV and 0.840-1.008 GeV^2 at 7 TeV.
- The Y_int values at 7 TeV are scaled by a constant factor of 3.6 to account for acceptance differences in Delta-eta and Delta-phi.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CGC/glasma framework calculations for p+p ridge correlations at 7 TeV (Dusling et al., 2012-2013) to the new ATLAS 13 TeV data. It directly challenges hydrodynamic interpretations of ridge correlations in small systems (Bozek, 2013; Bzdak, 2013; Schenke, 2014; Werner, 2013) by showing that the observed negative odd Fourier coefficients (particularly a_3) are difficult to reconcile with positive-definite flow coefficients from hydrodynamics. The results enable future tests in p+Pb collisions at different energies and at RHIC, and suggest that coherent multiple scattering effects (Schenke, 2015) may become important at very high multiplicities.
