# Quark matter contribution to the heat capacity of magnetized neutron stars

> In this paper, we find the heat capacity of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase of dense quark matter and use it to explore the feasibility of this phase for a neutron star interior. MDCDW is a spatially inhomogeneous phase of quark matter known to be favored at intermediate densities over the chirally symmetric phase and the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase. By comparing our result to the lower limit of the core heat capacity established from observations of transiently accreting neutron stars, we show that the heat capacity of MDCDW quark matter is well above that lower limit and hence cannot be ruled out. This result adds to a wealth of complementary investigations, all of which has served to strengthen the viability of a neutron star interior made of MDCDW quark matter. For completeness, we review the contributions to the heat capacity of the main neutron star ingredients at low, high and intermediate densities, with and without the presence of a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The heat capacity of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase of dense quark matter is well above the lower limit of core heat capacity established from observations of transiently accreting neutron stars, meaning this phase cannot be ruled out as a constituent of neutron star interiors, in contrast to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase which is strongly depleted.

## Method

The authors calculated the heat capacity of the MDCDW phase from the thermodynamic potential using the standard relation \(C_V = -T \partial^2 \Omega / \partial T^2\) in the low-temperature limit. They compared this result to the lower limit of core heat capacity derived from observations of accreting neutron stars in quiescence, and also reviewed contributions to heat capacity from electrons, superfluid neutrons, superfluid phonons, and quark matter in the CFL and magnetic CFL (MCFL) phases.

## Key numbers

- The MDCDW phase heat capacity is \(\tilde{C}_V \simeq 10^{38} (T/10^8)\) erg/K, which is 1 order larger than that of electrons under the same conditions.
- The lower limit of core heat capacity from observations is \(\tilde{C}_V \gtrsim 10^{36} (T/10^8)\) erg/K.
- The electron heat capacity in a magnetic field is \(C_V^e(B) \simeq eBT/6\).
- The electron heat capacity at zero magnetic field is \(C_V^e \simeq \mu_e T \sqrt{\mu_e^2 - m_e^2} / 3\).
- The superfluid neutron heat capacity is \(C_V^n \simeq e^{-(\Delta/T)} \Delta^2 T^{-3/2} \sqrt{m/(2\pi^3)} p_0^2\).
- The CFL phase heat capacity is \(C_V^{CFL} \simeq (\mu^2 + \Delta^2)T/(3\pi^2) e^{-\sqrt{\mu^2 + \Delta^2}/T} + (\mu^2 + 4\Delta^2)T/(3\pi^2) e^{-\sqrt{\mu^2 + \Delta^2}/T}\).
- The MCFL phase heat capacity is \(C_V^{MCFL} \simeq (\tilde{e}\tilde{B})/(2\pi^2 T) (\mu^2 + \Delta_H^2) e^{-\sqrt{\mu^2 + \Delta_H^2}/T}\).
- The superfluid phonon heat capacity is \(C_V^{(sPh)} = 2\pi^2 T^3 / (15 v_s^3)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star cooling by connecting the heat capacity of the MDCDW phase to observational constraints from accreting neutron stars in quiescence, as established by Cv-NS (no year given). It contrasts with the CFL phase, which was argued by Cv-NS to be unsuitable for neutron star interiors due to its exponentially damped heat capacity. The MDCDW phase, previously studied in NJL models by MDCDW and MDCDW-2 (no years given), is favored at intermediate densities over the chirally symmetric phase and the CFL phase, and this result strengthens the viability of a neutron star interior made of MDCDW quark matter.
