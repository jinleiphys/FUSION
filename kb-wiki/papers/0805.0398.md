# Hypernucleus Production by $A(p,pK^+)_ΛB$ Reactions

> The $Λ$-hypernucleus production by $A(p, pK^+)_ΛB$ reactions is investigated within the framework of the distorted wave impulse approximation(DWIA). The amplitude for the elementary process is evaluated in a fully covariant two-nucleon model based on the effective Lagrangian. The reaction cross sections for $Λ$-hypernucleus productions on $^6Li$, $^{12}C$ and $^{16}O$ targets are calculated. It is found that the distortion effects tend to reduce the cross sections by a factor of 3$\sim$10. Various differential cross sections (DCS) and double differential cross sections (DDCS) are presented. It is shown that for the $s_Λ-$wave hypernucleus production, the DCS is decreased with increasing nuclear mass, and the DCS for the $p_Λ-$wave hypernucleus production is normally higher than that for the $s_Λ-$wave hypernucleus production. As a reference, the DDCS with respect to the momenta of the outgoing proton and kaon is also demonstrated. Finally, the missing mass spectra of the inclusive reaction $p+A\to p+ K^+ + X$ for $^6Li$, $^{12}C$ and $^{16}O$ targets are presented, from which the masses of hypernuclei can accurately be extracted. Thus, we conclude that the missing mass spectrum method is an alternative to study hypernuclear physics. And the study of hypernuclear physics can be carried out in COSY and CSR by the $A(p,pK^+)_ΛB$ reaction due to the $μ$b-order reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the missing mass spectrum method in the $A(p,pK^+)_{\Lambda}B$ reaction is an alternative way to study hypernuclear physics, but this conclusion is conditional on the existence of the relevant hypernuclear states and the ability to accurately measure the spectrum. The reaction cross sections are predicted to be of microbarn order, making measurements feasible at COSY and CSR facilities, though distortion effects reduce cross sections by a factor of 3 to 10 compared to plane-wave calculations.

## Method

The authors employed the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) to calculate hypernucleus production cross sections for $^6Li$, $^{12}C$, and $^{16}O$ targets. The elementary $pp \rightarrow pK^+\Lambda$ amplitude was evaluated in a fully covariant two-nucleon model using effective Lagrangians that include contributions from $N(1535)$, $N(1650)$, $N(1710)$, and $N(1720)$ intermediate resonances, with nuclear distortion effects treated via the eikonal approximation. The $\Lambda$-nucleus bound state wave functions were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with a Woods-Saxon phenomenological potential.

## Key numbers

- Distortion effects reduce cross sections by a factor of 3 to 10 compared to plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results
- Binding energy of $^6_{\Lambda}He$ ($s_{\Lambda}$-state): 6.06 MeV (calculated) versus 4.18 MeV (experimental)
- Binding energy of $^{12}_{\Lambda}B$ ($s_{\Lambda}$-state): 11.03 MeV (calculated) versus 11.37 MeV (experimental); $p_{\Lambda}$-state: 0.48 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{16}_{\Lambda}N$ ($s_{\Lambda}$-state): 12.97 MeV (calculated) versus approximately 13 MeV (experimental); $p_{\Lambda}$-state: 2.51 MeV
- Minimum momentum transfer becomes smaller than 300 to 400 MeV/c when incident proton kinetic energy exceeds 2 GeV
- Differential cross sections calculated at incident proton kinetic energy $T_p = 2.7$ GeV for all three targets
- Missing mass spectra calculated with outgoing proton and kaon angles limited to the range $[0^{\circ}, 12^{\circ}]$

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of hypernucleus production in proton-nucleus collisions, particularly the early work by Fetisov (1971) who estimated cross sections in the plane-wave impulse approximation, and the covariant two-nucleon model calculations for exclusive $K^+$ production. The study builds on the resonance model for $pp \rightarrow pK^+\Lambda$ developed by Tsushima et al. (1998) and Liu et al. (2006), who included the $N(1535)$ resonance contribution. The results enable experimental searches for hypernuclei at COSY (Germany) and the Cooling Storage Ring (CSR) at Lanzhou, China, using the missing mass spectrum method as an alternative to conventional $(K^-,\pi^-)$ strangeness exchange and $(\pi^+,K^+)$ reactions.
