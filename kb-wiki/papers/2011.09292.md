# Chemical freeze-out of light nuclei in high energy nuclear collisions and resolution of the hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle

> We present a summary of the recent results obtained with the novel hadron resonance gas model with the multicomponent hard-core repulsion which is extended to describe the mixtures of hadrons and light (anti-, hyper-)nuclei. A very accurate description is obtained for the hadronic and the light nuclei data measured by STAR at the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =200$ GeV and by ALICE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =2.76$ TeV. The most striking result discussed here is that for the most probable chemical freeze-out scenario for the STAR energy the found parameters allow us to reproduce the values of the experimental ratios $S_3$ and $\overline{S}_3$ without fitting.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle (the inability of previous models to reproduce the measured ratios \(S_3\) and \(\overline{S}_3\)) is resolved by using a hadron resonance gas model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion and a small hard-core radius for \(\Lambda\)-(anti-)hyperons of 0.085 fm, but only for the single chemical freeze-out scenario at STAR energy; the separate freeze-out scenario is ruled out by consistency with lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors extended the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state, which already described hadron yields, to include light nuclear clusters (deuterons, helium-3, helium-4, hyper-triton and their antiparticles) by computing classical second virial coefficients for the excluded volumes between hadrons and these roomy clusters. They performed combined fits of particle yields and ratios to STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, testing both a single chemical freeze-out scenario (hadrons and nuclei freeze out together) and a separate freeze-out scenario (nuclei freeze out at a different temperature than hadrons).

## Key numbers

* STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV: single freeze-out scenario gives CFO temperature \(T_A = T_h \simeq 168.30 \pm 3.85\) with \(\chi^2_{tot}/dof \simeq 1.069\).
* ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV: separate freeze-out scenario gives hadron CFO temperature \(T_h \simeq 148.12 \pm 2.03\) and nuclear CFO temperature \(T_A \simeq 169.25 \pm 5.57\) with \(\chi^2_{tot}/dof \simeq 0.753\); single freeze-out gives \(T_h = T_A \simeq 150.29 \pm 1.92\) with \(\chi^2_{tot}/dof \simeq 1.433\).
* Combined best fit for both data sets yields \(\chi^2/dof \simeq 0.91\) (26.261 over 29 degrees of freedom).
* Hard-core radii used: pions \(R_{\pi}=0.15\) fm, kaons \(R_{K}=0.395\) fm, \(\Lambda\)-(anti-)hyperons \(R_{\Lambda}=0.085\) fm, other baryons \(R_{b}=0.365\) fm, other mesons \(R_{m}=0.42\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the multicomponent hard-core hadron resonance gas model previously developed by the same group (Bugaev et al., 2015-2019) which resolved the (anti-)\\(\Lambda\\) puzzle and the Strangeness Horn description puzzle. It directly addresses the hyper-triton ratios \(S_3\) and \(\overline{S}_3\) that neither the standard HRGM nor the coalescence model (Rapp, 2018) could describe. The results enable future precision measurements of hard-core radii of other hyperons through hyper-nuclei formation, and the separate freeze-out scenario at ALICE energy suggests different production mechanisms for hadrons and light nuclei at LHC energies.
