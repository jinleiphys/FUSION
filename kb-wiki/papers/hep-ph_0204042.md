# Infrared Behaviour of The Gluon Propagator in Non-Equilibrium Situations

> The infrared behaviour of the medium modified gluon propagator in non-equilibrium situations is studied in the covariant gauge using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path formalism. It is shown that the magnetic screening mass is non-zero at the one loop level whenever the initial gluon distribution function is non isotropic with the assumption that the distribution function of the gluon is not divergent at zero transverse momentum. For isotropic gluon distribution functions, such as those describing local equilibrium, the magnetic mass at one loop level is zero which is consistent with finite temperature field theory results. Assuming that a reasonable initial gluon distribution function can be obtained from a perturbative QCD calculation of minijets, we determine these out of equilibrium values for the initial magnetic and Debye screening masses at energy densities appropriate to RHIC and LHC. We also compare the magnetic masses obtained here with those obtained using finite temperature lattice QCD methods at similar temperatures at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The magnetic screening mass of the gluon is non-zero at one loop level in non-equilibrium situations whenever the initial gluon distribution function is anisotropic, provided the distribution function is not divergent at zero transverse momentum; for isotropic distributions (including local equilibrium) the one-loop magnetic mass is zero, consistent with finite-temperature field theory.

## Method

The authors used the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path (CTP) formalism in covariant gauge to compute the static limit of the longitudinal and transverse gluon self-energies at one loop, including both gluon-loop and tadpole contributions. They derived general expressions for the Debye and magnetic screening masses in terms of an arbitrary initial gluon distribution function, then evaluated these masses using a minijet distribution function obtained from perturbative QCD (pQCD) with GRV98 and CTEQ6M parton distribution functions and EKS98 nuclear modifications, applying a lower transverse momentum cutoff of 1 GeV at RHIC and 2 GeV at LHC.

## Key numbers

* Debye screening mass at RHIC (Au-Au, sqrt(s)=200 GeV): m_D = 116 MeV, using GRV98+EKS98 structure functions with alpha_s=0.287
* Magnetic screening mass at RHIC: m_g = 82 MeV
* Debye screening mass at LHC (Pb-Pb, sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV): m_D = 150 MeV, using GRV98+EKS98 with alpha_s=0.214
* Magnetic screening mass at LHC: m_g = 105 MeV
* For comparison, equilibrium magnetic mass from non-perturbative methods at T=500 MeV (RHIC): m_g = 563 MeV; at T=1000 MeV (LHC): m_g = 840 MeV
* Equilibrium Debye mass from one-loop finite-temperature QCD: m_D^2 = g^2 T^2, giving m_D = 950 MeV at RHIC (T=500 MeV, alpha_s=0.287) and m_D = 1.639 GeV at LHC (T=1000 MeV, alpha_s=0.214)

## Context

This work extends the study of infrared screening in QCD to non-equilibrium situations relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the parton gas produced immediately after the collision is not in thermal equilibrium. The authors show that anisotropic initial gluon distributions, such as those from minijet production, generate a non-zero magnetic screening mass at one loop, in contrast to equilibrium finite-temperature QCD where the one-loop magnetic mass is zero (as shown by Weldon (1982) and others). The results provide a perturbative cure for the infrared problems in transport equations used to model quark-gluon plasma equilibration, complementing non-perturbative lattice QCD calculations of the magnetic mass in equilibrium (such as those by Wang (1991) and other authors). The computed screening masses are of the same order as those obtained from equilibrium lattice QCD at similar temperatures, suggesting that the non-equilibrium effect is significant for transport theory at RHIC and LHC.
