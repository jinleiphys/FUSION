# Hidden variables in angular correlations of the particles emitted in fission

> A comparative analysis of experiments on studying the (n, f), on one hand, and (n, n), on the other hand, angular correlations in fission is carried out, based on the model proposed by muonic conversion in fragments of prompt fission of 238U with negative muons. Their fundamental difference is shown in the sense of the information that can be inferred from them. Historically, the situation resembles the EPR paradox. An experimental check of the empirical relation between the alignment and polarization parameters, the former is twice as small as the latter one.

## Key claim

The paper argues that treating the unmeasured spin projection of a fission fragment as a "hidden variable" (i.e., assuming it has a definite but unknown direction in the plane perpendicular to the fission axis) leads to angular distributions that contradict quantum mechanics; the contradiction can be tested experimentally via the ratio of alignment and polarization parameters in (n,n) versus (n,f) angular correlations, where the hidden-variable approach predicts the polarization parameter is twice the alignment parameter for E1 transitions.

## Method

The author compares two theoretical treatments of angular correlations for particles (conversion muons or neutrons) emitted from fission fragments: a "hidden variable" (HV) approach, which assumes the fragment spin has a definite direction in the plane perpendicular to the fission axis and averages over azimuthal angles, and a quantum-mechanical (QM) approach, which uses the density matrix with the quantization axis along the fission direction. Formulas for the angular distributions are derived for E1 and E2 multipole transitions in the center-of-mass and laboratory systems, and the results are compared with experimental data from prompt fission of 233U induced by muonic conversion, as reported in Belov et al.

## Key numbers

- For E1 transitions in the HV model, the angular distribution in the lab system is Chi_h.v.(alpha) = 1/2 (1 + cos^2 alpha); in the QM approach it is Chi_QM(alpha) ~ cos^2 alpha.
- The empirical relation between the (n,n) correlation parameter A_nJ and the (n,f) correlation parameter A_nf is A_nJ ~ 2 A_nf for E1 transitions.
- The alignment parameter A_nf is reported to be within 10% (A_nf << 1).
- From comparison with experiment, the E2 component in the radiation spectrum of fission fragments is at the level of 15%, and the share of shake muons is within 5%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of angular correlations in fission, drawing an analogy to the EPR paradox and hidden-variable theories in quantum mechanics. It builds on prior experimental studies of prompt fission in muonic atoms of 233U at JINR (Polkanov et al.) and angular correlation measurements by the CORA collaboration at IPHC Strasbourg. The paper proposes that the CORA experiment, which studies (n,n) and (n,n,f) correlations, can test the predicted factor-of-two relation between alignment and polarization parameters, thereby distinguishing between the hidden-variable and quantum-mechanical descriptions of fragment spin states.
