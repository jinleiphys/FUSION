# The light baryon spectrum and the exchange of pseudoscalar and vector mesons among constituent quarks

> The effects of the exchanges of both pseudoscalar and vector mesons among constituent quarks on the mass spectra of light baryons are investigated, paying particular attention to the contribution of tensor and spin-orbit terms. It is shown that the latter ones heavily affect the calculated spectra at variance with the empirical observation of the weakness of the baryon spin-orbit splittings. The relativistic suppression of the strength of the interaction among light quarks is argued to be a possible way to reproduce the light-baryon mass spectra.

# The light baryon spectrum and the exchange of pseudoscalar and vector mesons among constituent quarks

## Key claim

The authors show that including the full set of spin-orbit and tensor terms at first order in the 1/m^2 expansion of the pseudoscalar Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction destroys the agreement with experimental light-baryon masses, contrary to the success of the GBE model when those terms are omitted. The relativistic suppression of the interaction strength among light quarks is argued to be a possible way to reproduce the light-baryon mass spectra.

## Method

The authors calculated light-baryon masses using both the Isgur (one-gluon-exchange plus confinement) and GBE potentials by expanding the baryon wave function in a truncated set of harmonic oscillators and applying the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle. They first reproduced the results of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Glozman et al. (1997), then added the pseudoscalar tensor term and the scalar spin-orbit term from the confining interaction to the GBE potential without introducing any free parameters, and solved the corresponding Schroedinger equation.

- N-Delta splitting: 295 +/- 6 MeV (Graz model) vs. 217 +/- 21 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 252 +/- 19 MeV (full GBE) vs. 294 +/- 2 MeV (experimental)
- N-Roper splitting: 528 +/- 11 MeV (Graz) vs. 568 +/- 20 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 557 +/- 18 MeV (full GBE) vs. 502 (+30/-10) MeV (experimental)
- N-S*_11 splitting: 698 +/- 6 MeV (Graz) vs. 850 +/- 16 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 1044 +/- 19 MeV (full GBE) vs. 712 (+30/-10) MeV (experimental)
- N-F_15 splitting: 781 +/- 9 MeV (Graz) vs. 854 +/- 13 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 606 +/- 16 MeV (full GBE) vs. 792 (+10/-5) MeV (experimental)
- N-D_13 splitting: 596 +/- 6 MeV (Graz) vs. 651 +/- 11 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 555 +/- 11 MeV (full GBE) vs. 582 (+10/-5) MeV (experimental)
- N-S_11 splitting: 596 +/- 6 MeV (Graz) vs. 604 +/- 12 MeV (Graz + tensor) vs. 665 +/- 12 MeV (full GBE) vs. 597 (+20/-15) MeV (experimental)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model calculations of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Glozman et al. (1997) by examining the full first-order 1/m^2 expansion of the GBE interaction, which had previously been omitted. The authors show that the spin-orbit puzzle, which was solved in the one-gluon-exchange model by Isgur and coworkers using relativistic suppression factors, reappears in the full GBE model. The paper suggests that relativistic suppression factors of the form sqrt(m_i m_j / E_i E_j) may resolve the problem, and that vector meson exchange cannot adequately compensate for the pseudoscalar tensor and spin-orbit terms because the confining spin-orbit term is long-ranged while vector mesons are massive.
