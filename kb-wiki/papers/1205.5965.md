# Exact solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics for a QCD equation of state

> We generalize a previously known class of exact analytic solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics for the first time to arbitrary temperature-dependent Equation of State. We investigate special cases of this class of solutions, in particular, we present hydrodynamical solutions with the Equation of State determined from lattice QCD calculations. We discuss the phenomenological relevance of these solutions as well.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a known class of exact analytic solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics (Csorgo 2003) to arbitrary temperature-dependent equations of state, but the new solutions are restricted to spherically symmetric (non-ellipsoidal) flow and require that the Euler equation forces the arbitrary scaling function to be constant unless the speed of sound is constant.

## Method

The authors start from the known non-accelerating Hubble-like velocity field \(u^\mu = x^\mu / \tau\) and define a scaling variable \(s\) and effective volume \(V = \tau^3\) that satisfy \(u^\mu \partial_\mu s = 0\) and \(u^\mu \partial_\mu V = V \partial_\mu u^\mu\). They derive temperature equations for two cases: with a conserved number density \(n\) (using \(p = nT\)) and without conserved \(n\). For a general temperature-dependent \(\kappa(T)\) in the equation of state \(\varepsilon = \kappa(T) p\), they obtain implicit solutions for \(T(\tau)\) by integrating the temperature equation, then verify that the Euler equation forces the arbitrary function \(\xi(s)\) to be unity unless \(\kappa\) is constant.

## Key numbers

* For a freeze-out temperature of 170 MeV and a freeze-out time \(\tau_0 = 8\) fm/\(c\) with initial time \(\tau_{\rm init} = 1.5\) fm/\(c\), the initial temperature is approximately 550 MeV.
* The temperature falls off almost as fast as in the case of constant \(\kappa = 3\) (ideal relativistic gas).
* The range where \(\frac{d}{dT}(\kappa(T) T)\) becomes negative (making the conserved-\(n\) solution invalid) is 173 MeV to 230 MeV, based on the lattice QCD equation of state from Borsanyi (2010).

## Context

This work extends the exact solutions of Csorgo (2003) and the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model (Csanad 2003) to accommodate the temperature-dependent speed of sound from lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi 2010). The solutions enable determination of the initial temperature of the fireball from a given freeze-out temperature and time evolution duration, and can be used to calculate direct photon spectra with a realistic equation of state, as in Csanad (2011).
