# Quantum Monte Carlo calculation of $δ_C$ in the superallowed beta decay of $^{10}$C

> We perform an ab initio quantum Monte Carlo calculation of the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction $δ_C$ to the superallowed $β$ decay of $^{10}{\rm C}$. Using both phenomenological and chiral nuclear interactions, we evaluate the Fermi matrix element and quantify its deviation from the canonical $\sqrt{2}$ value. The resulting $δ_C$ values lie in the range $\approx 0.15$--$0.25\%$ and are consistent, within sizable uncertainties (approximately $34\%$--$65\%$ relative), across Hamiltonians, indicating no statistically significant dependence on the choice of nuclear interaction. The extracted values of $V_{ud}$ are also found to be compatible with current determinations within these uncertainties.

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C for the superallowed β decay of ¹⁰C is calculated to lie in the range ≈0.15–0.25% using ab initio quantum Monte Carlo methods, but the results carry sizable relative uncertainties of approximately 34%–65%, meaning no statistically significant dependence on the choice of nuclear interaction (phenomenological or chiral) can be established.

## Method

Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations were performed in the charge basis (rather than the good-isospin basis) for the 0⁺; T=1 states of ¹⁰C and ¹⁰B*, using four Hamiltonians: AV18+UX, AV18+IL7, NV2+3-Ia, and NV2+3-Ia*. The imaginary-time propagation included charge-dependent and charge-symmetry-breaking terms from both the electromagnetic interaction and the nuclear force, and the Fermi matrix element was extracted from mixed estimates of the off-diagonal transition matrix element between the initial and final states.

## Key numbers

- δ_C values lie in the range ≈0.15–0.25% across all four Hamiltonians, with relative uncertainties of approximately 34%–65%.
- GFMC propagation was performed out to imaginary time τ = 0.64 MeV⁻¹ for AV18+UX and AV18+IL7 Hamiltonians, and τ = 0.30 to 0.42 MeV⁻¹ for the NV2+3 interactions.
- The GFMC calculation improves the VMC energy estimate by 11 to 13 MeV for all Hamiltonians.
- For AV18+IL7, the charge radius R_ch is 2.71 fm for ¹⁰C and 2.57 fm for ¹⁰B*, agreeing with recommended values (2.64 fm and 2.54 fm) at the ≈2.6% and ≈4.0% level respectively.
- The experimental energy for ¹⁰C ground state is -60.32 MeV and for the first 0⁺ excited state of ¹⁰B is -63.01 MeV; AV18+IL7 underbinds by ≈1 MeV while NV2+3-Ia overbinds by ≈2.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio QMC program for superallowed beta decay corrections, complementing recent GFMC calculations of δ_NS for ¹⁰C by King (2025) and for ¹⁴O by Cirigliano (2024). It provides the first ab initio calculation of δ_C for ¹⁰C, which has traditionally relied on shell-model or mean-field approaches such as those by Towner (2007) and Hardy (2020). Together with the GFMC determination of δ_NS, this work enables a fully ab initio description of all nuclear-structure-dependent corrections entering the determination of V_ud and tests of CKM unitarity.
