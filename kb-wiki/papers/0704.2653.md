# Investigation of Neutron--Deuteron Charge-Exchange Reaction at Small Transfer Momentum

> Analysis of the $nd\to p(nn)$ reaction in a GeV-energy region is performed in the framework based on the multiple-scattering theory for the few-nucleon system. The special kinematic condition, when momentum transfer from neutron beam to final proton closes to zero, is considered. The possibility to extract the spin-dependent term of the elementary $np\to pn $ amplitude from $nd$-breakup process is investigated. The energy dependence of the ratio $R=\frac{dσ_{nd}} {dΩ} / \frac{dσ_{np}}{dΩ}$ is obtained taking account of the final-state interaction of the two outgoing neutrons in $^1 S_0$-state.

# Investigation of Neutron–Deuteron Charge-Exchange Reaction at Small Transfer Momentum

## Key claim

Under the special kinematic condition where momentum transfer from the neutron beam to the final proton is close to zero, the squared amplitude of the $nd\to p(nn)$ reaction factorizes into a spin-dependent part of the elementary $np\to pn$ cross section and a part depending on the deuteron and two-neutron wave functions, but this factorization is model-dependent due to the treatment of final-state interactions, which prevents precise extraction of the spin-dependent $np$ amplitude.

## Method

The analysis uses the Alt–Grassberger–Sandhas (AGS) formulation of multiple-scattering theory for the three-nucleon system, iterating the AGS equations up to second-order terms. The final-state interaction (FSI) of the two outgoing neutrons in the $^1S_0$-state is treated via the matrix inversion method (MIM), with the two-neutron wave function expressed as a series of delta-functions on a Gaussian grid. The $np\to pn$ t-matrix is taken off-energy-shell using relativistic potential theory, and the deuteron wave function uses a pole parameterization with only the S-component retained.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-beam kinetic energy range: 0.8 to 1.3 GeV.
- For $\Delta p = 10$ MeV/c (integration window near zero transfer momentum), the difference between PWIA and full (PWIA+FSI) calculations of the integrated ratio $R_{int}$ is about 30%.
- For $\Delta p = 20$ MeV/c, the difference is about 15%; for $\Delta p = 60$ MeV/c, the PWIA and full calculation lines are practically indistinguishable.
- At neutron kinetic energy 800 MeV, the FSI contribution to the integrated $R$ ratio (with $\Delta p = 30$ MeV/c) is about 10%; at 1300 MeV it is a few percent.
- The ratio $R$ varies by a factor of 10 when transfer momentum changes by a few MeV/c near the peak at $T_n = 1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) treatment of the $nd\to p(nn)$ charge-exchange reaction, first suggested by Pomeranchuk (1951), by including final-state interactions of the two slow neutrons. It is directly motivated by experiments at VBLHE JINR (STRELA and DELTA SIGMA setups) and COSY (ANKE spectrometer). The matrix inversion method was previously applied to deuteron electro-disintegration (Schiavilla et al.) and deuteron–proton breakup (Ladygina and Shebeko). The results enable estimation of the spin-dependent part of the $np$ charge-exchange amplitude from $nd$ breakup data, though with model dependence that limits precision.
