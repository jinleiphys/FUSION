# Relativistic effects in model calculations of double parton distribution function

> In this paper we consider double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) which are the main non perturbative ingredients appearing in the double parton scattering cross section formula in hadronic collisions. By using recent calculation of dPDFs by means of constituent quark models within the so called Light-Front approach, we investigate the role of relativistic effects on dPDFs. We find, in particular, that the so called Melosh operators, which allow to properly convert the LF spin into the canonical one and incorporate a proper treatment of boosts, produce sizeable effects on dPDFs. We discuss specific partonic correlations induced by these operators in transverse plane which are relevant to the proton structure and study under which conditions these results are stable against variations in the choice of the proton wave function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Melosh rotation operators, which are necessary for a proper relativistic treatment of boosts in the Light-Front approach, produce sizeable and partially model-independent effects on double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), including a significant suppression of the distributions and a violation of the commonly assumed factorization between longitudinal and transverse degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors calculate dPDFs for two unpolarized u-quarks using the Light-Front approach, starting from the Light-Cone correlator definition. They employ several constituent quark models (relativistic hypercentral, non-relativistic hypercentral, and harmonic oscillator models with varying parameters) to model the proton wave function, and include Melosh rotation operators to properly convert Light-Front spin into canonical spin. The dPDFs are computed in momentum space and then Fourier transformed to transverse coordinate space, allowing the authors to study the b-perp dependence and to quantify the effects of the Melosh operators by comparing calculations with and without them.

## Key numbers

* The ratio R_sigma(x1=0.2, x2=0.3), which quantifies the suppression of the squared dPDF due to Melosh rotations, ranges from 1.50 (NR model) to 1.85 (RL model), indicating a suppression factor around 2 in the two-fast-parton (FF) configuration.
* In the slow-slow (SS) configuration (x1=0.03, x2=0.04), the suppression ratio R_sigma ranges from 2.36 (HO_nrel model) to 4.83 (RL model), showing a factor of 2 to 5 suppression.
* In the slow-fast (SF) configuration (x1=0.04, x2=0.3), the suppression ratio R_sigma ranges from 2.05 (HO_nrel model) to 4.33 (RL model).
* The experimentally determined effective cross section sigma_eff is approximately 15 mb, which is compatible with a constant value irrespective of centre-of-mass energy and final state.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dPDFs using constituent quark models (Rinaldi et al., 2014; Rinaldi et al., 2013) by systematically investigating the role of relativistic effects through Melosh rotations. The results contradict the commonly used factorized ansatz for dPDFs (Gaunt, 2012) which assumes independence of longitudinal and transverse degrees of freedom. The findings enable future calculations of DPS cross sections that properly account for relativistic effects, and suggest that the suppression factor due to Melosh rotations should be incorporated in model predictions for sigma_eff.
