# Cold Nuclear Matter In Holographic QCD

> We study the Sakai-Sugimoto model of holographic QCD at zero temperature and finite chemical potential. We find that as the baryon chemical potential is increased above a critical value, there is a phase transition to a nuclear matter phase characterized by a condensate of instantons on the probe D-branes in the string theory dual. As a result of electrostatic interactions between the instantons, this condensate expands towards the UV when the chemical potential is increased, giving a holographic version of the expansion of the Fermi surface. We argue based on properties of instantons that the nuclear matter phase is necessarily inhomogeneous to arbitrarily high density. This suggests an explanation of the "chiral density wave" instability of the quark Fermi surface in large N_c QCD at asymptotically large chemical potential. We study properties of the nuclear matter phase as a function of chemical potential beyond the transition and argue in particular that the model can be used to make a semi-quantitative prediction of the binding energy per nucleon for nuclear matter in ordinary QCD.

## Key claim

The Sakai-Sugimoto model at zero temperature exhibits a phase transition to nuclear matter above a critical baryon chemical potential, characterized by a condensate of instantons on the probe D8-branes; however, the nuclear matter phase is necessarily inhomogeneous to arbitrarily high density, suggesting that at large N_c the quarks bind into nucleons even at asymptotically large densities, and the model can provide a semi-quantitative prediction of the binding energy per nucleon for ordinary QCD only under the assumption that certain numerical coefficients are of order one.

## Method

The authors study the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a holographic dual of a non-supersymmetric SU(N_c) gauge theory with N_f fundamental fermions, at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential. They treat the D8-branes as probes in the near-horizon geometry of N_c D4-branes and analyze configurations with non-zero electrostatic potential on the D8-branes, where sources for the electric flux come from either string endpoints from wrapped D4-branes or instanton configurations of the nonabelian gauge field. For the two-flavor case, they approximate the inhomogeneous instanton condensate by singular homogeneous configurations, arguing the approximation becomes exact at large densities, and solve a variational problem numerically to determine the minimal energy configuration as a function of chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The volume term in the Weizsacker-Bethe semiempirical mass formula for nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon is b_vol = 16 MeV.
- The critical chemical potential for the transition to nuclear matter in QCD is approximately mu_c = M_B(1 - b_vol/M_B) ≈ M_B(1 - 0.017).
- The large-lambda result for the baryon mass is M_B^0 = (1/(27 pi)) M_KK lambda N_c.
- The binding energy per nucleon at the threshold for nuclear matter formation, extrapolated to N_c=3, is E_bind = (1/(9 pi)) Lambda_QCD (c'-c) ≈ 7 MeV (c'-c), where c and c' are numerical coefficients of order one.
- For large lambda, the critical chemical potential behaves as mu_c = M_B^0 (1 + c/lambda + O(1/lambda^(3/2))).
- For large mu, the baryon density in the two-flavor case behaves as n_B ∝ mu^3.
- For the single flavor case, the baryon density approaches n_B ∝ mu^(5/2) for large mu.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Sakai-Sugimoto model phase diagram, which had analyzed finite temperature behavior (Aharony, 2006) and finite chemical potential with different focuses (Kim, 2006; Horigome, 2006; Yamada, 2007). The paper argues that the chiral density wave instability of the quark Fermi surface in large N_c QCD (Deryagin, Grigoriev, and Rubakov) simply indicates quarks want to bind into nucleons even at asymptotically large densities, and the model enables a first-principles estimate of the nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon that is relatively insensitive to lambda, potentially providing the most reliable analytic prediction of this quantity.
