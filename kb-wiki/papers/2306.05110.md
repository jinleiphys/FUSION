# Relativistic Mean Field Model parameterizations in the light of GW170817, GW190814, and PSR J0740 + 6620

> Three parameterizations DOPS1, DOPS2, and DOPS3 (named after the Department of Physics Shimla) of the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model have been proposed with the inclusion of all possible self and mixed interactions between the scalar-isoscalar (σ), vector-isoscalar (ω) and vector-isovector (ρ) mesons up to quartic order. The generated parameter sets are in harmony with the finite and bulk nuclear matter properties. A set of Equations of State (EOSs) composed of pure hadronic (nucleonic) matter and nucleonic with quark matter (hybrid EOSs) for superdense hadron-quark matter in β-equilibrium is obtained. The quark matter phase is calculated by using the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The maximum mass of a non-rotating neutron star with DOPS1 parameterization is found to be around 2.6 M$\odot$ for the pure nucleonic matter which satisfies the recent gravitational wave analysis of GW190814 Abbott et al.,(2020) with possible maximum mass constraint indicating that the secondary component of GW190814 could be a non-rotating heaviest neutron star composed of pure nucleonic matter. EOSs computed with the DOPS2 and DOPS3 parameterizations satisfy the X-Ray observational data and the recent observations of GW170817 maximum mass constraint of a stable non-rotating neutron star in the range 2.01 \pm 0.04 - 2.16 \pm 0.03 M\odot and also in good agreement with constraints on mass and radius measurement for PSR J0740+6620 (NICER) Riley et al., L27 (2021)}, Miller et al., (2021). The hybrid EOSs obtained with the NJL model also satisfy astrophysical constraints on the maximum mass of a neutron star from PSR J1614-2230 and Demorest et al., (2010) .We also present the results for dimensionless tidal deformability, $Λ$ which are consistent with the waveform models analysis of GW170817.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the secondary component of GW190814 could be a non-rotating neutron star composed of pure nucleonic matter with mass around 2.6 M⊙, but only for the stiffest parameterization DOPS1; the softer parameterizations DOPS2 and DOPS3 satisfy the lower maximum mass constraints from GW170817 and PSR J0740+6620 instead.

## Method

Three new Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) parameter sets (DOPS1, DOPS2, DOPS3) were generated by fitting to experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei plus the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, using simulated annealing to minimize χ². The hadronic equation of state was combined with a quark matter phase from the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using Glendenning construction for the coexisting phase, and the resulting hybrid equations of state were used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron star structure and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for DOPS1 pure nucleonic matter: approximately 2.6 M⊙, satisfying the GW190814 secondary component mass constraint range 2.50–2.67 M⊙ from Abbott et al. (2020)
- DOPS2 and DOPS3 equations of state satisfy the GW170817 maximum mass constraint range 2.01 ± 0.04 to 2.16 ± 0.03 M⊙ from Rezzolla et al. (2018)
- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb fixed to 0.18 fm in the fitting procedure
- ω-meson self-coupling parameter ζ set to 0.00, 0.01, and 0.02 for DOPS1, DOPS2, and DOPS3 respectively
- NJL model parameters: mu = md = 3.6 MeV, ms = 87 MeV, GSΛ² = 3.6, KΛ⁵ = 8.9, cutoff Λ = 750 MeV, vector coupling GV = 0

## Context

This work extends the RMF model approach of Dhiman (2007) by including all possible self and mixed interactions between σ, ω, and ρ mesons up to quartic order, unlike earlier parameter sets such as NL3 from Lalazissis (1997), FSUGarnet from Chen (2015), IOPB-1 from Kumar (2018), and Big Apple from Fattoyev (2020) which omitted some mixed interaction terms. The new parameterizations enable simultaneous satisfaction of constraints from multiple gravitational wave and X-ray observations (GW170817, GW190814, NICER data for PSR J0740+6620), and the hybrid equations of state with NJL quark matter allow investigation of whether neutron star cores contain deconfined quark matter while still reaching the approximately 2 M⊙ maximum mass required by PSR J1614-2230 from Demorest et al. (2010).
