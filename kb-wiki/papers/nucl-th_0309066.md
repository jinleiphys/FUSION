# N-d scattering including electromagnetic forces

> The electromagnetic potential consisting in the Coulomb plus the magnetic moment interactions between two nucleons is studied in nucleon-deuteron scattering. For states in which the relative N-d angular momentum L has low values the three--nucleon problem has been solved using the correlated hyperspherical harmonic expansion basis. For states in which the angular momentum L has large values, explicit formulae for the nucleon-deuteron magnetic moment interaction are derived and used to calculate the corresponding T-matrices in Born approximation. Then, the transition matrices describing N-d elastic scattering have been derived including an infinite number of partial waves as required by the 1/r^3 behavior of the magnetic moment interaction. Appreciable effects are observed in the vector analyzing powers at low energies. The evolution of these effects by increasing the collision energy is examined.

# N-d scattering including electromagnetic forces

## Key claim

The magnetic moment (MM) interaction produces appreciable effects in the vector analyzing powers of nucleon-deuteron scattering at low energies, but these effects are insufficient to resolve the longstanding underestimation of the peak values of \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) by modern nuclear forces; the improvement at the peak for \(p-d\) scattering around \(E_{lab}=16\) MeV is already less than 5%.

## Method

The three-nucleon scattering problem was solved using the correlated hyperspherical harmonic (PHH) expansion basis with the Kohn variational principle for states with low relative orbital angular momentum \(L\) between the nucleon and deuteron. For states with high \(L\) values, where the centrifugal barrier prevents close approach, the collision was treated as a two-body process and the corresponding \(T\)-matrices were calculated in Born approximation using explicit formulae for the nucleon-deuteron magnetic moment interaction. The transition matrices for elastic \(N-d\) scattering were then constructed by combining the low-\(L\) three-body results with an infinite sum over high-\(L\) partial waves, required by the \(1/r^3\) behavior of the MM interaction.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit coupling constant for \(n-d\) MM interaction: \(C_{so} \approx 2.932 \times 10^{-3}\) fm
- The tensor coupling constant for \(n-d\) MM interaction: \(C_t \approx 1.675 \times 10^{-3}\) fm
- The spin-orbit coupling constant for \(p-d\) MM interaction (proton term): \(C_{so}^+ \approx -3.775 \times 10^{-3}\) fm
- The spin-orbit coupling constant for \(p-d\) MM interaction (deuteron term): \(C_{so}^- \approx -5.936 \times 10^{-4}\) fm
- At \(E_{lab}=16\) MeV, the improvement from MM interaction at the peak of \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) for \(p-d\) scattering is less than 5%
- Coulomb effects in \(p-d\) scattering are important below \(E_{lab}=30\) MeV and considerably reduced at \(E_{lab}=65\) MeV
- For \(n-d\) scattering at \(E_{lab}=1.2\) and 1.9 MeV, the nuclear part of the transition matrix converges already for \(L_{max}=3\)
- For \(p-d\) scattering at \(E_p=1\) MeV, \(L_{max}=4\) was used; at \(E_p=3\) MeV, \(L_{max}=6\) was used; at \(E_p=5\) and 10 MeV, \(L_{max}=8\) was used
- Modifications to differential cross section and tensor analyzing powers from MM interaction are of the order of 1% or less

## Context

This work extends previous three-nucleon scattering calculations by the same authors (Kievsky et al., 2001; Viviani et al., 2000) that included Coulomb but not magnetic moment interactions, using the correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis. It also builds on the treatment of MM interactions in two-nucleon scattering by Knutson (1978) and Stoks (1990), who showed how to sum the infinite partial-wave series analytically for \(p-p\) and \(n-p\) scattering. A first analysis in three-nucleon scattering by Stoks included MM interaction only in Born approximation at high \(L\) values while neglecting it at low \(L\) values, finding no modifications to the peak of \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\). The present work includes MM interaction in both regimes for a complete description, using the AV18 potential as the nuclear interaction. The results confirm that MM interaction does not resolve the \(A_y\) puzzle (the underestimation of vector analyzing powers by modern \(NN\) forces) and justify the application of standard \(n-d\) calculations to \(p-d\) scattering at high energies above 65 MeV, as done by Witala et al.
