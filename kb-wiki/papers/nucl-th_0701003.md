# Contribution of spin 1/2 and 3/2 resonances to two-photon exchange effects in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We calculate contributions of hadron resonances to two-photon exchange effects in electron-proton scattering. In addition to the nucleon and P33 resonance, the following heavier resonances are included as intermediate states in the two-photon exchange diagrams: D13, D33, P11, S11 and S31. We show that the corrections due to the heavier resonances are smaller that the dominant nucleon and P33 contributions. We also find that there is a partial cancellation between the contributions from the spin 1/2 and spin 3/2 resonances, which results in a further suppression of their aggregate two-photon exchange effect.

## Key claim

The contributions of heavier hadron resonances (D13, D33, P11, S11, S31) to two-photon exchange effects in elastic electron-proton scattering are smaller than the dominant nucleon and P33 (Delta) contributions, and a partial cancellation between spin 1/2 and spin 3/2 resonance contributions leads to further suppression of their aggregate effect.

## Method

The authors calculated two-photon exchange corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering by including nucleon and resonance intermediate states in box and crossed-box loop diagrams, using the Dressed K-Matrix Model (DKM) to determine resonance masses and nucleon-photon coupling constants. They employed dipole form factors with a cutoff of 0.84 GeV for all hadrons and used Rarita-Schwinger propagators for spin 3/2 resonances and Dirac propagators for spin 1/2 resonances, neglecting resonance widths.

## Key numbers

- Resonance masses used (in GeV): M_P33 = 1.232, M_D13 = 1.52, M_D33 = 1.7, M_P11 = 1.55, M_S11 = 1.535, M_S31 = 1.62, and nucleon mass M = 0.938
- Dipole form factor cutoff Lambda_R = 0.84 GeV for all hadrons
- The total two-photon exchange correction is given by the sum: delta = delta_N + delta_P33 + delta_D13 + delta_D33 + delta_P11 + delta_S11 + delta_S31

## Context

This work extends the approach of Blunden (2003) and Kondratyuk (2005), which showed that two-photon exchange diagrams can reconcile Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of proton electromagnetic form factors. The earlier work by Kondratyuk (2005) demonstrated the importance of including both nucleon and Delta (P33) intermediate states. The present paper generalizes this to include the full spectrum of the most important hadron resonances, using coupling constants based on dynamical multichannel calculations of nucleon Compton scattering by Kondratyuk (2001) and Korchin (2000). The inclusion of these resonance contributions leads to better agreement between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer data analyses, especially at higher momentum-transfer squared Q^2.
