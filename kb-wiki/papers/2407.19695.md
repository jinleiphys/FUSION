# Neutrinoless double-beta decay in a finite volume from relativistic effective field theory

> The neutrinoless double-beta decay process $nn\rightarrow ppee$ within the light Majorana-exchange scenario is studied using the relativistic pionless effective field theory (EFT) in finite-volume cubic boxes with the periodic boundary conditions. Using the low-energy two-nucleon scattering observables from lattice QCD available at $m_π=300$, 450, 510, and 806 MeV, the leading-order $nn\rightarrow ppee$ transition matrix elements are predicted and their volume dependence is investigated. The predictions for the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ transition matrix elements can be directly compared to the lattice QCD calculations of the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ process at the same pion masses. In particular for the matrix element at $m_π=806$ MeV, the predictions with relativistic pionless EFT are confronted to the recent first lattice QCD evaluation. Therefore, the present results are expected to play a crucial role in the benchmark between the nuclear EFTs and the upcoming lattice QCD calculations of the $nn\rightarrow pp ee$ process, which would provide a nontrivial test on the predictive power of nuclear EFTs on neutrinoless double-beta decay.

# Neutrinoless double-beta decay in a finite volume from relativistic effective field theory

## Key claim

The relativistic pionless effective field theory predicts the leading-order $nn\rightarrow ppee$ transition matrix elements in finite-volume cubic boxes at unphysical pion masses $m_\pi = 300$, 450, 510, and 806 MeV, with the contact-term low-energy constant estimated by integrating out pion contributions from relativistic chiral EFT rather than fitting to lepton-number-violating data. The predictions can be directly compared to lattice QCD calculations of the same process, providing a benchmark for nuclear EFTs without requiring the uncertain $nn\rightarrow ppee$ contact term that is needed at leading order in the nonrelativistic formulation.

## Method

The authors implement the relativistic formulation of pionless effective field theory in finite-volume cubic boxes with periodic boundary conditions, using low-energy two-nucleon scattering observables from lattice QCD at four unphysical pion masses as inputs. The leading-order $nn\rightarrow ppee$ transition matrix elements are computed by combining the long-range neutrino-exchange contribution with a contact term whose low-energy constant is determined by matching to the pion-exchange contributions from relativistic chiral EFT at threshold. The finite-volume matrix elements are evaluated for both scattering states and bound states, with the volume dependence investigated for box sizes $L = 4$–6 fm at unphysical pion masses and $L = 8$–16 fm at the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- At $m_\pi = 806$ MeV, the relativistic pionless EFT predictions for the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ matrix element are compared to the first lattice QCD evaluation from Davoudi (2024) at the same finite volume.
- The LO contact-term low-energy constant $\tilde{g}_\nu^{NN}$ is estimated from $\delta J^\infty(0,0)$ using Eq. (52), with the uncertainty estimated as subleading since $Q^2/m_N^2 \lesssim Q/m_\pi$ for $m_\pi \leq 806$ MeV.
- The effective neutrino mass $m_{\beta\beta}$ is set to 1 MeV throughout the calculations.
- Box sizes considered: $L = 8$–16 fm at physical pion mass; $L = 4$–6 fm at unphysical pion masses $m_\pi = 300$, 450, 510, and 806 MeV.
- The two-loop integral $J^\infty$ is ultraviolet finite in the relativistic formulation, in contrast to the nonrelativistic case where it is logarithmically divergent.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral EFT approach of Yang (2024) for $nn\rightarrow ppee$ amplitudes to the pionless EFT regime in finite volume, enabling direct comparison with lattice QCD calculations at unphysical pion masses. It builds upon the finite-volume matching formalism developed by Davoudi (2021, 2022) for the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ process, which previously relied on the generalized Cottingham formula of Cirigliano (2021) to estimate the contact-term LEC at the physical pion mass only. The present results enable benchmarks between nuclear EFTs and upcoming lattice QCD calculations of $nn\rightarrow ppee$, providing a test of the predictive power of nuclear EFTs for neutrinoless double-beta decay without the model-dependent inputs required by the generalized Cottingham formula approach.
