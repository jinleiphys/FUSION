# Competing decay modes and stability analysis of superheavy nuclei with Z = 120 using relativistic mean-field approach

> We systematically study the competition between α-decay and spontaneous fission in even-even superheavy nuclei with (Z=120) and 256 \leq A \leq 304 within the preformed cluster-decay model using microscopic inputs from relativistic mean-field calculations with the NL3 parameter set. The α-decay half-lives are obtained from WKB barrier penetration with empirically determined preformation factors, self-consistent Q_α values from RMF, and nuclear interaction potentials constructed using both M3Y and relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon forces, and are benchmarked against standard semi-empirical formulas. Our results predict reduced spontaneous fission probabilities and extended α-decay chains toward the fermium region for isotopes with 296 \leq A \leq 304, with enhanced stability reflected in maxima of log_{10} T_{1/2} around neutron numbers N \approx 166-182. In particular, the nuclei 296,298,300,302,304_{120} are identified as the most favorable candidates for survival against fission, demonstrating the crucial role of shell effects, deformation, and pairing correlations and providing quantitative guidance for future experimental searches of Z=120 nuclei.

# Competing decay modes and stability analysis of superheavy nuclei with Z = 120 using relativistic mean-field approach

## Key claim
The paper predicts that five even-even isotopes of Z=120 (296,298,300,302,304120) are the most favorable candidates for survival against spontaneous fission, with enhanced stability reflected in maxima of log₁₀ T₁/₂ around neutron numbers N ≈ 166–182, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of the NL3 parameter set within the relativistic mean-field framework and the use of empirically determined preformation factors rather than fully microscopic ones.

## Method
The authors employed the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) with microscopic inputs from relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 parameter set. Alpha-decay half-lives were computed via WKB barrier penetration using self-consistent Qα values from RMF, empirically determined preformation factors, and nuclear interaction potentials constructed through double-folding with both M3Y and relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon forces. Spontaneous fission half-lives were evaluated using the semi-empirical formulation of Xu et al. (2008), and the competition between decay modes was quantified through the branching ratio b = T_SF / T_α.

## Key numbers
- The study covers even-even superheavy nuclei with Z=120 in the mass range 256 ≤ A ≤ 304.
- Five isotopes are identified as most favorable candidates: 296,298,300,302,304120.
- Maxima of log₁₀ T₁/₂ occur around neutron numbers N ≈ 166–182.
- The standard deviation in calculated Qα values for RMF (NL3) relative to experimental data is 0.94.
- The preformation probability formula uses constant parameters a = 11.98, b = 0.037, and c = 1.52.
- The binding energy of the α-particle used in Qα calculations is 28.296 MeV.
- The barrier radius is defined as r_B = 1.2 fm × (A_c^{1/3} + A_d^{1/3}).

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of superheavy nuclei stability, particularly those predicting shell closures at Z=114, N=184 (Rutz, 1997); Z=120, N=172 or 184 (Gupta, 1997; Patra, 1999); and Z=124 or 126, N=184 (Cwiok, 2005). The study builds on the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) developed by Gupta and collaborators (1988, 1999) and uses the R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential derived from RMF Lagrangian (Singh, 2012), comparable to the phenomenological M3Y potential (Satchler, 1979). The results provide quantitative guidance for future experimental searches of Z=120 nuclei and enable predictions of α-decay chains extending toward the fermium region for the most stable isotopes.
