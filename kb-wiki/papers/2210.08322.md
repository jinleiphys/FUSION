# A journey into the proton structure: Progresses and challenges

> Unraveling the inner dynamics of gluons and quarks inside nucleons is a primary target of studies at new-generation colliding machines. Finding an answer to fundamental problems of Quantum ChromoDynamics, such as the origin of nucleon mass and spin, strongly depends on our ability of reconstructing the 3D motion of partons inside the parent hadrons. We present progresses and challenges in the extraction of TMD parton densities, with particular attention to the ones describing polarization states of gluons, which still represent an largely unexplored field. Then, we highlight connections with corresponding parton densities in the high-energy limit, the so-called unintegrated gluon distributions or UGDs and, more in general, to recent developments in high-energy physics.

## Key claim

Progress in extracting transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) gluon parton distribution functions, particularly those describing gluon polarization states, is presented along with connections to unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) in the high-energy limit; however, proper factorization theorems for gluon-TMD sensitive processes are almost completely lacking, so that factorization is usually assumed in phenomenological studies.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of TMD gluon distributions at twist-2 for spin-1/2 targets, discussing their gauge-link dependence and the distinction between f-type and d-type color structures. A spectator-model approach is employed to calculate all leading-twist T-even gluon TMDs at tree level, where model parameters are fixed by simultaneously fitting the unpolarized and helicity TMDs, \(f_1^g\) and \(g_1^g\), to their collinear counterparts from NNPDF at an initial scale of \(\mu_0 = 1.64\) GeV using the replica method for statistical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy scale for the TMD fit: \(\mu_0 = 1.64\) GeV
- Longitudinal momentum fraction \(x = 10^{-3}\) used for the 3D tomographic reconstruction plots of unpolarized and Boer-Mulders gluon TMD distributions
- The spectator-model spectral function contains seven parameters
- The \(f_1^g/h_1^{\perp g}\) ratio is asymptotically constant in the \(x \to 0^+\) limit

## Context

This work extends the spectator-model approach previously applied to quark TMDs (Bacchetta 2008, Bacchetta 2010) to the gluon sector, building on the identification of gluon TMDs by Mulders (2000) and Meissner (2007). The results connect TMD phenomenology with the high-energy BFKL evolution (Fadin 1975, Kuraev 1976, Balitsky 1978) and enable future studies of proton structure at new-generation colliders such as the HL-LHC and the Electron-Ion Collider, where the recently discovered property of natural stability of high-energy resummation (Celiberto 2022) will be relevant.
