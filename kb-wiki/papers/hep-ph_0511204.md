# Inclusive Nucleon Emission Induced by Quasi--Elastic Neutrino--Nucleus Interactions

> We study the quasi--elastic contribution to the inclusive $(ν_l,ν_l N)$, $(ν_l,l^- N)$, $({\bar ν}_l,{\bar ν}_l N)$ and $({\bar ν}_l,l^+ N)$ reactions in nuclei using a Monte Carlo simulation method to account for the rescattering of the outgoing nucleon. As input, we take the reaction probability from the microscopical many body framework developed in Phys. Rev. {\bf C70} (2004) 055503 for charged-current induced reactions, while for neutral currents we use results from a natural extension of the model described in that reference. The nucleon emission process studied here is a clear signal for neutral--current neutrino driven reactions, that can be used in the analysis of future neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The authors compute inclusive nucleon emission cross sections for quasi-elastic neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus reactions (both charged-current and neutral-current) using a Monte Carlo cascade to model the rescattering of the outgoing nucleon, but the results are limited to intermediate energies (above the giant resonance and below the Δ(1232) region) and rely on a Local Fermi Gas description of the nucleus, which the authors argue is adequate for inclusive observables at excitation energies of order 100 MeV or higher.

## Method

The authors start from a many-body framework (Nieves, Valverde, Vacas 2004) for charged-current quasi-elastic reactions, extend it to neutral-current processes, and compute the double-differential cross section for the primary neutrino-nucleus interaction using a hadronic tensor derived from the Z-boson selfenergy in nuclear matter. They then feed the reaction probability (including its spatial dependence) into a Monte Carlo simulation that propagates the ejected nucleon through the nucleus, accounting for collisions with other nucleons via a cascade model previously tested on pion-, photon-, and electron-induced reactions. Nuclear effects such as Pauli blocking, RPA correlations (including Δ-hole degrees of freedom), and nucleon selfenergy dressing are incorporated in the primary cross section.

## Context

This work extends the many-body framework developed by Nieves, Valverde, and Vacas (2004) for charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus reactions to neutral-current processes, and adds a Monte Carlo cascade to model final-state interactions of the ejected nucleon. It contrasts with plane-wave and distorted-wave impulse approximation calculations (e.g., Garvey 1992, superscaling approaches) which either neglect rescattering or incorrectly treat absorption as removal of events rather than redistribution of nucleon energy, angle, and charge. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutral-current nucleon emission signals in future neutrino oscillation experiments (e.g., FINeSSE) and for studies of strange quark contributions to nucleon spin.
