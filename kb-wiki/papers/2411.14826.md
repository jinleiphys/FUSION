# Relative Hadron Yields in HRG With Medium Modification

> In the framework of a constituent quark mass model, the modified baryon masses are incorporated into the hadron resonance gas (HRG) based analysis of the like mass particle ratios in ultra relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (URNNC) over a wide range of collision energy. In addition we have incorporated an essential feature of the hadronic interaction at short distance, i.e. the hard-core repulsion by using the standard excluded volume type approach. We have extracted the chemical freeze-out conditions. The resulting freeze-out line in our case is compared with those obtained earlier using different model approaches. The correlation between $k^{-}/k^{+}$ and $\bar p/p$ ratios is also studied.

## Key claim

By incorporating temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent baryon masses (from the SU(3) Nambu Jona-Lasinio model) into an excluded-volume hadron resonance gas model, the authors obtain a chemical freeze-out temperature that is lower than in models using vacuum hadron masses; this lower temperature may be more appropriate for the hadronic phase rather than the quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Method

The authors compute temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent constituent quark masses using the SU(3) NJL model in the mean-field approximation, then use a constituent quark mass model to obtain temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent baryon masses. These masses are inserted into a thermodynamically consistent excluded-volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG) model with a hard-core repulsive radius for baryons, and the freeze-out parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B) are extracted by fitting the antiproton-to-proton ratio data from AGS to LHC energies using a chi-squared minimization. The same parameters are then used to predict other antibaryon-to-baryon ratios and meson ratios.

## Key numbers

* Hard-core baryonic radius extracted from the fit: 0.20 ± 0.03 fm.
* Freeze-out temperature parameterization constant c = 145 ± 1.3 MeV (the saturation value at high collision energies).
* Baryon chemical potential parameterization constant f = 1180 ± 16.0 MeV.
* χ²/dof for the fitted antiproton-to-proton ratio: 0.48.
* χ²/dof for the fitted Λ̄/Λ ratio: 2.02; for Ξ̄/Ξ: 1.5; for Ω̄/Ω: 0.9.
* χ²/dof for the fitted K⁻/K⁺ ratio: 1.45; for π⁻/π⁺: 0.96.
* Power-law exponent α for the correlation K⁻/K⁺ = (p̄/p)^α: theoretical model gives α = 0.23, compared to experimental α = 0.21.
* At the highest freeze-out temperature (~145 MeV), the proton mass is lowered by 23% from its vacuum mass; the Λ⁰ mass by 18%; the Ω⁻ mass by only 4%.

## Context

This work extends the excluded-volume HRG approach of Rischke et al. (1991) and the freeze-out parameterization of Cleymans et al. (2006) by incorporating dynamical baryon masses from the SU(3) NJL model, rather than using fixed vacuum masses as in earlier thermal model analyses (e.g., Andronic et al., Sahoo et al., Bhatta et al.). The resulting freeze-out line is compared with those of Cleymans et al., Andronic et al., and Poberezhnyuk et al., showing lower temperatures that are consistent with earlier in-medium mass scaling calculations by Hirsch et al. (2002) and D. Zschiesche et al. The work enables more refined extraction of freeze-out conditions and tests of hadronic versus partonic phases in heavy-ion collisions.
