# Contact parameters in two dimensions for general three-body systems

> We study the two dimensional three-body problem in the general case of three distinguishable particles interacting through zero-range potentials. The Faddeev decomposition is used to write the momentum-space wave function. We show that the large-momentum asymptotic spectator function has the same functional form as derived previously for three identical particles. We derive analytic relations between the three different Faddeev components for three distinguishable particles. We investigate the one-body momentum distributions both analytically and numerically and analyze the tail of the distributions to obtain two- and three-body contact parameters. We specialize from the general cases to examples of two identical, interacting or non-interacting, particles. We find that the two-body contact parameter is not a universal constant in the general case and show that the universality is recovered when a subsystem is composed of two identical non-interacting particles. We also show that the three-body contact parameter is negligible in the case of one non-interacting subsystem compared to the situation where all subsystem are bound. As example, we present results for mixtures of Lithium with two Cesium or two Potassium atoms, which are systems of current experimental interest.

## Key claim

The two-body contact parameter is not a universal constant for general three-body systems in two dimensions, but universality is recovered when a subsystem consists of two identical non-interacting particles; the three-body contact parameter is negligible when one subsystem is non-interacting compared to when all subsystems are bound.

## Method

The authors used the Faddeev decomposition to write the momentum-space wave function for three distinguishable particles interacting through zero-range potentials in two dimensions. They derived analytic relations between the three Faddeev components and analyzed the large-momentum asymptotic behavior of the spectator functions and one-body momentum distributions to extract the two- and three-body contact parameters. Numerical calculations were performed for specific mass-imbalanced systems to verify the analytic results.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^{6}$Li system, the asymptotic spectator function limit is reached within 10% at momentum $\frac{q}{\sqrt{m_A E_{AC}}} \approx 50$ and within 1% at $\frac{q}{\sqrt{m_A E_{AC}}} \approx 10^{4}$.
- The leading-order large-momentum behavior of the spectator functions is $f_\alpha(q) \to \frac{\Gamma}{m_{\beta\gamma}} \frac{\ln q}{q^2}$.
- The $n_1(q_\alpha)$ term contributes $4\pi \frac{m_{\beta\gamma,\alpha}}{m_{\beta\gamma}} \Gamma^2 \frac{\ln^2(q_\alpha)}{q_\alpha^6}$ to the one-body momentum density at large momenta.
- The $n_2(q_\alpha)$ term gives the leading-order contribution $\frac{C_{\beta\gamma}}{q_\alpha^4}$, where $C_{\beta\gamma} = 8\pi \left(m_{\alpha\gamma}^2 \int_0^\infty dk\;k\;|f_\beta(k)|^2 + m_{\alpha\beta}^2 \int_0^\infty dk\;k\;|f_\gamma(k)|^2\right)$.
- The $n_3(q_\alpha)$ term contributes $8\pi \left(\frac{m_{\alpha\gamma}+m_{\alpha\beta}}{m_{\beta\gamma}}\right) \Gamma^2 \frac{\ln^3(q_\alpha)}{q_\alpha^6}$.
- The $n_4(q_\alpha)$ term contributes $4\pi \left(\frac{m_{\alpha\gamma}}{m_{\alpha\beta}} + \frac{m_{\alpha\beta}}{m_{\alpha\gamma}}\right) \Gamma^2 \frac{\ln^3(q_\alpha)}{q_\alpha^6}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of contact parameters from three identical bosons in 2D (Bellotti et al., 2013) to general three-body systems with distinguishable particles and mass imbalance. It contrasts with the 3D case (Castin and Werner, 2011; Braaten et al., 2011; Yamashita et al., 2013) where the sub-leading term in the momentum distribution changes functional form for non-equal masses, while in 2D the functional form remains the same for both equal masses and mixed-species systems. The results enable experimental verification using cold atomic mixtures such as Lithium with two Cesium or two Potassium atoms, which are currently accessible in experiments.
