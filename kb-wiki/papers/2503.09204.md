# Microscopic investigation of wobbling motion in even-even nuclei

> The possibility of observing wobbling mode in the even-even systems of 76Ge, 112Ru, 188,192Os, 192Pt and 232Th is explored using the triaxial projected shell model approach. These nuclei are known to have γ-bands whose odd-spin members are lower than the average of the neighbouring even-spin states. It is shown through a detailed analysis of the excitation energies and the electromagnetic transition probabilities that the observed band structures in these nuclei except for 232Th can be characterised as originating from the wobbling motion. It is further demonstrated that quasiparticle alignment is responsible for driving the systems to the wobbling mode.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the observed γ-bands in five even-even nuclei (⁷⁶Ge, ¹¹²Ru, ¹⁸⁸Os, ¹⁹²Os, and ¹⁹²Pt) can be characterized as originating from wobbling motion, but explicitly excludes ²³²Th from this characterization. The claim is conditional on quasiparticle alignment driving the systems into the wobbling mode at high spin, rather than the wobbling being present across all spin values.

## Method
The authors employed the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, using angular-momentum projected deformed Nilsson states as basis configurations. They diagonalized a shell model Hamiltonian containing monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole interaction terms within a configuration space of three major oscillator shells, using basis states composed of 0-quasiparticle vacuum, two-proton, two-neutron, and four-quasiparticle configurations. The analysis focused on excitation energies, electromagnetic transition probabilities (B(E2) and B(M1)), and K-distributions of angular momenta on the three principal axes for the six nuclei studied.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters: ⁷⁶Ge (ε=0.200, ε′=0.160, γ=39°), ¹¹²Ru (ε=0.289, ε′=0.130, γ=24°), ¹⁸⁸Os (ε=0.183, ε′=0.088, γ=26°), ¹⁹²Os (ε=0.164, ε′=0.085, γ=27°), ¹⁹²Pt (ε=0.150, ε′=0.087, γ=30°), ²³²Th (ε=0.210, ε′=0.085, γ=22°)
- Neutron pairing gaps: ⁷⁶Ge (Δν=0.63 MeV), ¹¹²Ru (Δν=0.54 MeV), ¹⁸⁸Os (Δν=0.46 MeV), ¹⁹²Os (Δν=0.38 MeV), ¹⁹²Pt (Δν=0.57 MeV), ²³²Th (Δν=0.64 MeV)
- Proton pairing gaps: ⁷⁶Ge (Δπ=0.83 MeV), ¹¹²Ru (Δπ=0.63 MeV), ¹⁸⁸Os (Δπ=0.52 MeV), ¹⁹²Os (Δπ=0.41 MeV), ¹⁹²Pt (Δπ=0.74 MeV), ²³²Th (Δπ=0.86 MeV)

- Effective charges used: 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons
- Quadrupole pairing strength GQ is 0.18 times the monopole pairing strength GM

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic TPSM studies (Bhat et al., 2021; Sheikh et al., 2024) where six nuclei were identified as having γ-bands with odd-spin members below even-spin members, a signature of γ-rigid motion. The paper builds on the original wobbling mode prediction by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) for even-even systems, which has only been experimentally observed in odd-A nuclei such as ¹⁶³Lu. The results are compared with recent relativistic density functional theory calculations (Wang et al., 2024) for ¹¹²Ru, ¹¹⁴Pd, ¹⁵⁰Nd, and ¹⁸⁸Os. The findings enable future experimental searches for wobbling bands in these even-even candidates and provide a microscopic framework for understanding the transition from γ-soft to γ-rigid behavior.
