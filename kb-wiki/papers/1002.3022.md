# Toward an Effective Field Theory for Cold Compressed Baryonic Matter

> This is an extended version of the note taken by the first author (W.-G.P.) on a lecture given by the second author (M.R.) as a first part of the series on "Hadronic Matter Under Extreme Conditions," the principal theme of the WCU-Hanyang Program. It covers the attempts to go in a framework anchored on effective field theory of QCD from zero density to the nuclear matter density and slightly beyond, with the ultimate goal of arriving at the density relevant to compact stars, including chiral phase transition and quark matter. The focus is on the conceptual aspects rather than detailed "fitting" of the data on the kinds of physics that are being addressed to in radioactive-ion-beam machines in operation as well as in project (such as `KoRIA' in Korea) and will be explored at such forthcoming accelerators as FAIR/GSI. The approach presented here is basically different from the standard ones found in the literature in that the notion of hidden local symmetry -- which underlies the chiral symmetry of the strong interactions -- and its generalization to dual gravity description involving infinite tower of hidden gauge fields are closely relied on.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an effective field theory approach anchored on hidden local symmetry and its generalization to dual gravity descriptions can describe cold baryonic matter from zero density up to the chiral restoration density, but this framework is conceptually different from standard approaches and remains incomplete for densities significantly above nuclear matter saturation density.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework based on the Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry Lagrangian, incorporating baryons via skyrmion topology on a crystal lattice to generate high-density matter. They employ a "double decimation" renormalization group procedure: first integrating out high-momentum modes from nucleon-nucleon scattering data to obtain a low-momentum potential \(V_{lowk}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda_{NN} \approx 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), then using this potential within Landau Fermi liquid theory to compute nuclear matter properties. The approach also considers the possibility of a phase transition from skyrmion matter to half-skyrmion matter at high density.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The half-skyrmion phase threshold density is \(n_{1/2} \approx 1.3 n_0\), which is approximately independent of the dilaton mass
- The cutoff for the low-momentum potential is \(\Lambda_{NN} \approx 2.1\) fm\(^{-1} \approx 3 m_\pi\)
- The chiral scale cutoff is \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1\) GeV
- The anti-kaon nuclear potential at nuclear matter density is predicted to be \(V_{KN}(n_0) \lesssim -200\) MeV for kaon condensation to occur
- The dilaton mass used in the kaon spectrum calculation is 720 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki and the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling concept (Brown and Rho, 1991) to dense matter, contrasting with standard chiral perturbation theory approaches that the authors argue cannot access high-density regimes. The paper addresses the divergence in predictions for nuclear symmetry energy above nuclear matter density, particularly the "supersoft" symmetry energy extracted from FOPI/GSI data analyzed by Bao-An Li (Das et al.), and discusses implications for kaon condensation in compact stars. The framework enables future connections to experiments at FAIR/GSI and the proposed KoRIA facility, and opens questions about the nature of the half-skyrmion phase and the role of three-body forces in dense matter.
