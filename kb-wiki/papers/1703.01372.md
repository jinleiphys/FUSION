# Effective kinetic description of the expanding overoccupied Glasma

> We report on a numerical study of the Boltzmann equation including $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scatterings of gluons and quarks in an overoccupied Glasma undergoing longitudinal expansion. We find that when a cascade of gluon number to the infrared occurs, corresponding to an infrared enhancement analogous to a transient Bose-Einstein condensate, gluon distributions qualitatively reproduce the results of classical-statistical simulations for the expanding Glasma. These include key features of the distributions that are not anticipated in the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario. We also find that quark distributions, like those of gluons, satisfy self-similar scaling distributions in the overoccupied Glasma. We discuss the implications of these results for a deeper understanding of the self-similarity and universality of parton distributions in the Glasma.

## Key claim

The authors find that a kinetic description using only elastic $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scatterings in the small-angle (Fokker-Planck) approximation can qualitatively reproduce the self-similar scaling behavior of gluon distributions seen in classical-statistical Yang-Mills simulations of the expanding Glasma, but only when a transient infrared enhancement (analogous to a Bose-Einstein condensate) develops; this agreement is conditional on sufficiently weak coupling and does not capture all features of the classical-statistical results, particularly the pressure anisotropy.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Boltzmann equation for gluons and quarks in a longitudinally expanding boost-invariant geometry, using the Landau small-angle approximation to replace the $2\leftrightarrow 2$ collision integral with a Fokker-Planck diffusion term plus source terms. They employed a rescaled time variable and discretized momentum space in $p$ and $\cos\theta$, evolving the coupled equations for gluons (with Bose enhancement factors) and quarks (with Pauli blocking) from overoccupied initial conditions of the form $f(\tau_0,\mathbf{p}) = (n_0/g^2) \exp[-(p_\perp^2 + (\xi_0 p_z)^2)/Q_s^2]$.

## Key numbers

* The initial occupancy parameter $n_0 = g^2 f_0 = 0.1$ was used for most simulations, corresponding to $f_0 = 10^5$ for $g=10^{-3}$.
* The Debye mass squared $m_D^2$ decreases as $1/\tau$ in the scaling regime, confirmed for couplings $g = 10^{-3}$, $10^{-2}$, and $10^{-1}$.
* The longitudinal hard scale $\Lambda_L^2(\tau)$ scales as $(Q_s \tau)^{-2/3}$ for $g \lesssim 10^{-2}$, while for larger couplings it deviates from this classical scaling.
* The initial rescaled time was $Q_s \tilde{\tau}_0 = 1$ for most runs, with specific values $Q_s \tilde{\tau}_0 \simeq 0.33$, $0.74$, and $1.7$ for anisotropy parameters $\xi_0 = 1$, $2$, and $4$ respectively.
* The gluon distribution at $p_z=0$ shows a $1/p_\perp$ infrared behavior, and the scaling relation $f(\tau, p_\perp, p_z) = (Q_s \tau)^{-2/3} f_S(p_\perp, (Q_s \tau)^{1/3} p_z)$ is satisfied for hard momenta $p_\perp \gtrsim Q_s$.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Kurkela and Zhu (2015), who studied the full effective kinetic theory of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) including inelastic processes but only for gluons and focusing on thermalization, by instead restricting to elastic $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scatterings and investigating the earlier classical stage of the Glasma. The results reproduce the self-similar scaling found in classical-statistical Yang-Mills simulations by Berges et al. (2013, 2014), and the authors argue that inelastic processes may be suppressed at early times for weak couplings, enabling a transient Bose-Einstein condensate to form. The work enables further study of quark distributions in the Glasma and provides a framework for investigating photon production, as applied in a companion paper by Berges et al. (2017).
