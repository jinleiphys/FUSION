# Impact of ground-state properties and collective excitations on the Skyrme ansatz: a Bayesian study

> State-of-the-art models based on nuclear Density Functional Theory are successful in the description of nuclei throughout the whole nuclear chart. Among them, some differences arise regarding their accuracy. For a given nuclear model, this depends on the procedure adopted to determine the parameters, and, at the same time, new experimental findings constantly challenge theory. In the present work, we present a Bayesian inference study aimed at assessing the performance of the Skyrme Energy Density Functional. For the sake of simplicity and clarity, we restrict to spherical, double-magic nuclei, giving equal emphasis to ground-state and dynamical properties. Our basic constraints are: i) masses and charge radii, which are known to be very sensitive to the saturation energy and density; ii) spin-orbit splittings, which are associated with the spin-orbit parameter(s); iii) the electric dipole polarizability and parity-violating asymmetry, which are associated with the density dependence of the symmetry energy; iv) the excitation energy of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance, to constrain the nuclear matter incompressibility; v) the energy-weighted sum rule of the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance, to account for the isovector effective mass; and vi) the excitation energy of the Isoscalar Quadrupole Resonance, that is related to the isoscalar effective mass. In this way, we test the Skyrme ansatz in a statistically meaningful way, by determining the posterior distributions of the parameters as well as their correlation, and discussing a possible strategy for future developments.

## Key claim

The Skyrme Energy Density Functional ansatz is tested by performing Bayesian inference on a set of spherical, double-magic nuclei, using both ground-state and collective excitation observables; the authors find that the posterior distributions of nuclear matter parameters become significantly narrower when giant resonance data and parity-violating asymmetry measurements are included, but some parameters like the isovector surface coefficient remain poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors performed seven sequential Bayesian inferences using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, progressively adding experimental constraints: binding energies and charge radii, spin-orbit splittings, electric dipole polarizability, isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonance energies, the energy-weighted sum rule of the isovector giant dipole resonance, and parity-violating asymmetry in 48Ca and 208Pb. To handle the computational cost of Random Phase Approximation calculations for collective excitations, they employed a Gaussian Process emulator (MADAI) trained on approximately 8500 points in the parameter space, where the Skyrme parameters were reparametrized in terms of nuclear matter properties (saturation density, energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy and its slope, effective masses, surface coefficients, and spin-orbit strength) with uniform priors.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density rho_0: posterior mean 0.161 fm^-3 with standard deviation 0.004 fm^-3 (all seven inferences)
* Incompressibility K_0: posterior mean 219 MeV with standard deviation 10 MeV (inference including giant resonances)
* Symmetry energy J: posterior mean 29.4 MeV with standard deviation 1.6 MeV (full inference with all observables)
* Symmetry energy slope L: posterior mean 16.1 MeV with standard deviation 14.7 MeV (full inference)
* Isoscalar effective mass m*_0/m: posterior mean 0.91 with standard deviation 0.08 (full inference)
* Isovector effective mass m*_1/m: posterior mean 0.71 with standard deviation 0.02 (full inference)
* 208Pb binding energy: posterior mean 1636 +/- 1.8 MeV (full inference)
* 208Pb charge radius: posterior mean 5.49 +/- 0.03 fm (full inference)
* 208Pb dipole polarizability alpha_D: posterior mean 19.5 +/- 0.5 fm^3 (full inference)
* 48Ca parity-violating asymmetry A_PV: posterior mean 2591 +/- 54 ppb (full inference)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian approach to Skyrme functionals previously applied by Kortelainen (2014) in building the UNEDF set, and addresses the difficulty noted by Reinhard (2021, 2022) and Essick (2021) in reproducing parity-violating asymmetry measurements in 48Ca and 208Pb. The study enables a statistically meaningful assessment of whether the Skyrme ansatz has reached its limits, as suggested by Becker (2017) who found that adding fourth-order derivative terms did not yield significant improvements. The results provide posterior distributions that can guide future development of improved energy density functionals, particularly by identifying which observables most effectively constrain specific nuclear matter parameters.
