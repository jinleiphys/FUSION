# Microscopic description of axisymmetric vortices in $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids

> We study quantized vortices in ${}^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids using a microscopic theory for the first time. The theory is based on the Eilenberger equation to determine the order parameters and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation to obtain the eigenenergies and the core magnetization. Within axisymmetric vortex configurations, we find several stable and metastable vortex configurations which depend on the strength of a magnetic field, similar to a $v$ vortex and $o$ vortex in $^3$He superfluids. We demonstrate that the $o$ vortex is the most stable axisymmetric vortex in the presence of a strong magnetic field, and we find two zero-energy Majorana fermion bound states in the $o$-vortex core. We show that the profiles of the core magnetization calculated using the BdG equation are drastically different from those calculated using only the order parameter profiles known before.

## Key claim

Using a microscopic theory (Eilenberger and Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations) for the first time, the authors find that in axisymmetric vortex configurations in \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids, the \(o\) vortex is the most stable axisymmetric vortex in the presence of a strong magnetic field and hosts two zero-energy Majorana fermion bound states in its core, but this stability and the existence of zero modes depend on the discrete symmetry (\(P_3\) symmetry) preserved by the vortex.

## Method

The authors self-consistently solved the Eilenberger quasiclassical transport equation coupled with the gap equation to determine the order parameter profiles of axisymmetric vortices in \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids. They then used the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation with the self-consistent order parameters to compute the eigenenergies of fermion excitations and the local spin density (core magnetization) around the vortex core. The calculations were performed at temperature \(T = 0.4 T_{\mathrm{c}}\) and cutoff \(\omega_{\mathrm{c}} = 10 T_{\mathrm{c}}\), with the order parameter tensor expanded in angular momentum components \(\gamma_M(\rho)\) under the axisymmetric condition \((J_3 - \kappa)A(\bm{R}) = 0\).

## Key numbers

* The calculations were performed at temperature \(T = 0.4 T_{\mathrm{c}}\) and cutoff \(\omega_{\mathrm{c}} = 10 T_{\mathrm{c}}\).
* The units of energy and length are \(T_{\mathrm{c}}\) and \(\xi_0 = v_{\mathrm{F}}/(2\pi T_{\mathrm{c}})\), respectively.
* The free-energy densities were integrated out to a boundary radius \(R_{\mathrm{c}} \simeq 100\) (in units of \(\xi_0\)).
* A transition magnetic field \(V_{\mathrm{Zc}}\) separates the \(D_2\)-BN and \(D_4\)-BN states; for \(V_{\mathrm{Z}} < V_{\mathrm{Zc}}\) the UN or \(D_2\)-BN state is realized, while for \(V_{\mathrm{Z}} \ge V_{\mathrm{Zc}}\) the \(D_4\)-BN state is realized.

## Context

This work extends prior Ginzburg-Landau (GL) studies of vortices in \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids (Richardson 1972, Fujita 1972, Sauls 1978, Muzikar 1980, Sauls 1982, Masuda 2015, Masuda 2016, Chatterjee 2016) by incorporating fermion degrees of freedom beyond the GL approximation. It also parallels the classification of \(o\) and \(v\) vortices in superfluid \(^{3}\)He-B (Salomaa 1987, Thuneberg 1986, Tsutsumi 2015) and shows that the core magnetization profiles from the BdG equation are drastically different from those obtained using only order parameter profiles from GL theory (Sauls 1982, Masuda 2015, Masuda 2016, Chatterjee 2016). The finding of zero-energy Majorana fermions in the \(o\)-vortex core may be important for understanding neutron star cooling rates and glitch phenomena.
