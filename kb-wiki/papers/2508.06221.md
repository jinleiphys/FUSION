# Realistic shell model for ordinary muon capture of sd-shell nuclei

> We report about a study of the ordinary muon capture in nuclei belonging to the sd shell, an electroweak process that occurs with exchange momenta far larger than ordinary beta decays (approximately 100 MeV). Such a characteristic places this transition in an energy range that is consistent with the neutrinoless double-beta decay, and represents an interesting test for nuclear models to support their predictions of the nuclear matrix elements for such an unobserved process. For the first time, the calculations are carried out within the realistic shell model (RSM), namely employing effective shell-model Hamiltonians and decay operators derived from realistic nuclear forces, without resorting to any empirical adjustment of the coupling constants. This is a chapter of a research program that is aimed to assess the realistic shell model in reproducing the observables related to electroweak processes in nuclei, and then to evaluate the reliability of nuclear matrix elements for the neutrinoless double-beta decay that are calculated within this approach. We calculate the partial capture rates for many nuclear systems in the sd-shell region, as well as their spectroscopic properties, and compare the results with the available experimental counterparts. Such a comparison tests the relevance of a microscopic approach to the renormalization of transition operators to reproduce data and provide solid predictions of unknown observables.

## Key claim

For the first time, ordinary muon capture (OMC) calculations in sd-shell nuclei are performed using the realistic shell model (RSM), employing effective Hamiltonians and decay operators derived from realistic nuclear forces without any empirical adjustment of coupling constants; the results depend noticeably on the input realistic Hamiltonian, and the quality of reproduction of observed spectroscopy correlates with the comparison between calculated and experimental OMC partial capture rates.

## Method

The authors derive two effective shell-model Hamiltonians from realistic nuclear potentials: one from the CD-Bonn two-nucleon potential renormalized via the Vlow-k procedure with cutoff Λ = 2.6 fm⁻¹, and another from chiral perturbation theory at N³LO for two-nucleon and N²LO for three-nucleon forces with cutoff Λ = 500 MeV. Effective decay operators for OMC are constructed consistently with the Hamiltonians using many-body perturbation theory up to third order, including two-body components of the transition operators. The OMC partial capture rates are calculated for multiple sd-shell nuclei using the KSHELL code, with the muon wave function obtained within the Bethe-Salpeter approximation assuming a point nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Transfer momentum in OMC is approximately 100 MeV/c, consistent with neutrinoless double-beta decay
- The Vlow-k cutoff is set at Λ = 2.6 fm⁻¹
- The chiral Hamiltonian cutoff is Λ = 500 MeV
- The harmonic oscillator parameter is ħω = 14 MeV
- The excitation energy cutoff for Q-space configurations is Emax = Nmax ħω with Nmax = 20
- The axial coupling constant used is gA = 1.2723
- The vector cutoff parameter is ΛV = 0.84 GeV and the axial cutoff is ΛA = 1 GeV
- The difference μp - μn = 3.706 is used for magnetic moments
- Two-neutron separation energies are calculated for oxygen isotopes from A = 18 to 26, with the ChPT Hamiltonian correctly predicting an unbound ²⁶O while the Vlow-k Hamiltonian predicts a bound ²⁶O

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of electroweak decays within the realistic shell model framework (Coraggio 2017, 2019, 2022, 2024) to ordinary muon capture, which involves momentum transfers consistent with neutrinoless double-beta decay. The connection between OMC and neutrinoless double-beta decay has motivated renewed interest from both nuclear structure theory (Kortelainen 2002, 2004; Jokiniemi 2019, 2021, 2024; Civitarese 2025; Simkovic 2020) and experimental neutrino physics (Gorringe 1994; Zinatulina 2018; Hashim 2023; Araujo 2024). The results enable future assessment of the reliability of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay calculated within the realistic shell model approach.
