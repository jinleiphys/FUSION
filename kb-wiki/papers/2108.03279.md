# Radiative corrections to semileptonic beta decays: Progress and challenges

> We review some recent progress in the theory of electroweak radiative corrections in semileptonic decay processes. The resurrection of the so-called Sirlin's representation based on current algebra relations permits a clear separation between the perturbatively-calculable and incalculable pieces in the $\mathcal{O}(G_Fα)$ radiative corrections. The latter are expressed as compact hadronic matrix elements that allow systematic non-perturbative analysis such as dispersion relation and lattice QCD. This brings substantial improvements to the precision of the electroweak radiative corrections in semileptonic decays of pion, kaon, free neutron and $J^P=0^+$ nuclei that are important theory inputs in precision tests of the Standard Model. Unresolved issues and future prospects are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Sirlin current algebra representation, which separates perturbatively-calculable and non-perturbative pieces in the O(G_F α) electroweak radiative corrections, can unify the treatment of both near-degenerate (neutron, nuclear) and non-degenerate (kaon) semileptonic beta decays, potentially bringing theory precision to the 10^{-4} level; however, the main challenge remains the non-perturbative QCD contributions at the hadronic scale (q ∼ 1 GeV) which require lattice QCD and dispersion relation techniques.

## Method

The authors review the derivation of the O(G_F α) electroweak radiative corrections using the Sirlin current algebra formalism, which expresses the non-perturbative inner corrections as hadronic matrix elements of electroweak currents. They apply the free-field operator product expansion (OPE) at large momentum transfer (q' ∼ M_W) combined with Ward identities to separate weak corrections (reabsorbed into G_F via muon decay) from electromagnetic corrections, and then use the on-mass-shell perturbation formula to handle the hadronic vertex corrections. The non-perturbative contributions are analyzed through dispersion relations relating hadronic matrix elements to deep inelastic scattering structure functions, and through lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The top-row CKM unitarity test yields ΔCKM^u = |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 − 1 = −0.0015(3)_{V_ud}(4)_{V_us}, showing a 3σ tension with the Standard Model prediction of zero
- The perturbative QCD correction to the weak corrections is a_pQCD ≈ 0.068
- The physical Fermi constant is G_F = 1.1663787(6) GeV^{−2}
- The WZ-box diagram contributes a residual correction of −(α/2π) ln(M_W^2/M_Z^2) to the effective Fermi interaction
- The charged lepton wavefunction renormalization gives Z_ℓ = 1 − (α/4π)[ln(M_W^2/m_ℓ^2) + 9/2 − 2 ln(m_ℓ^2/M_γ^2)]

## Context

This review extends the foundational work by Sirlin (1967, 1974, 1977) on the current algebra representation of radiative corrections, and builds upon the earlier separation of outer and inner corrections by Wilkinson and Macefield (1970). It contrasts with the Chiral Perturbation Theory approach to kaon decays (Cirigliano 2001, 2004, 2008) which had a natural precision limit of 10^{-3} due to unknown low-energy constants, and with earlier model-dependent calculations by Ginsberg (1966-1970) that used an effective UV cutoff. The work enables precision tests of CKM unitarity and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model through improved theory inputs for V_ud and V_us determinations.
