# Parton coalescence at RHIC

> Using a covariant coalescence model, we study hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions from both soft partons in the quark-gluon plasma and hard partons in minijets. Including transverse flow of soft partons and independent fragmentation of minijet partons, the model is able to describe available experimental data on pion, kaon, and antiproton spectra. The resulting antiproton to pion ratio is seen to increase at low transverse momenta and reaches a value of about one at intermediate transverse momenta, as observed in experimental data at RHIC. A similar dependence of the antikaon to pion ratio on transverse momentum is obtained, but it reaches a smaller value at intermediate transverse momenta. At high transverse momenta, the model predicts that both the antiproton to pion and the antikaon to pion ratio decrease and approach those given by the perturbative QCD. Both collective flow effect and coalescence of minijet partons with partons in the quark-gluon plasma affect significantly the spectra of hadrons with intermediate transverse momenta. Elliptic flows of protons, Lambdas, and Omegas have also been evaluated from partons with elliptic flows extracted from fitting measured pion and kaon elliptic flows, and they are found to be consistent with available experimental data.

## Key claim

Using a covariant coalescence model that includes both soft partons from the quark-gluon plasma and hard partons from minijets, the authors show that the observed antiproton to pion ratio of about one at intermediate transverse momenta at RHIC can be reproduced, but only when coalescence of minijet partons with thermal partons is included alongside independent fragmentation.

## Method

The authors implement a covariant coalescence model using a Monte-Carlo method to evaluate multi-dimensional integrals for meson and baryon formation from parton distributions. Minijet parton spectra are obtained from perturbative QCD with energy loss modeled via the GLV formalism, while thermal partons from the quark-gluon plasma are described by boosted exponential distributions with collective transverse flow velocity of 0.5c. Hadron production includes both direct coalescence and decays of resonances such as rho, Delta, and K*, plus independent fragmentation of minijet partons using KKP fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton to pion ratio reaches a value of about one at intermediate transverse momenta, consistent with PHENIX experimental data at RHIC
- Collective flow velocity of the quark-gluon plasma taken as beta_0 = 0.5c
- Quark-gluon plasma temperature T = 170 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD phase transition temperature of 165-185 MeV
- Light quark mass in QGP taken as 300 MeV; strange quark mass as 475 MeV
- Quark chemical potential mu_q = 10 MeV, giving light antiquark to quark ratio of 0.89
- Strange quark to light quark ratio about 0.27
- Transverse radius R_perp = 8.3 fm at proper time tau = 4 fm, corresponding to volume V = 900 fm^3
- Total transverse energy per unit rapidity about 590 GeV, with minijet contribution only about 10%
- Parton density about 1 fm^{-3}
- Spatial coalescence radius Delta_x = 0.85 fm; momentum coalescence radius Delta_p = 0.24 GeV
- Coalescence of minijet partons with QGP partons enhances pion yield by more than a factor of 2 at transverse momenta around 4 GeV/c
- Coalescence of minijet partons with QGP partons enhances antiproton yield by close to a factor of 5 at transverse momenta around 5.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence studies such as the ALCOR model and the MICOR model, and specifically improves upon the schematic study by Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) by using a fully covariant formalism and Monte-Carlo evaluation rather than semi-analytical approximations with only comoving partons at zero rapidity. The model also builds on the parton recombination approach of Hwa and Yang (2003) and the coalescence studies of Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003). The results enable predictions for antikaon to pion ratios and elliptic flows of protons, Lambdas, and Omegas at RHIC, and demonstrate the importance of minijet-thermal parton coalescence for intermediate transverse momentum hadron production.
