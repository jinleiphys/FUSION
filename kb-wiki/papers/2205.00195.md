# Holographic Schwinger-Keldysh field theory of SU(2) diffusion

> We construct effective field theory for SU(2) isospin charge diffusion, based on holographic Schwinger-Keldysh contour arXiv:2008.01269. The holographic model consists of a probe SU(2) gauge field in a doubled Schwarzschild-AdS$_5$ geometry. Accurate to first order in derivative expansion, we analytically compute the effective action up to quartic order in hydrodynamical fields. The effective theory contains both non-Gaussianity for noises and nonlinear interactions between noises and dynamical variables. Moreover, the effective theory captures both thermal and quantum fluctuations, which perfectly satisfy dynamical Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) symmetry at quantum level. Interestingly, the dynamical KMS symmetry, which is crucial in formulating non-equilibrium effective field theory for a quantum many-body system, is found to have a nice holographic interpretation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a holographic effective field theory for SU(2) isospin charge diffusion that goes beyond previous work by including both non-Gaussian noise and quantum fluctuations, not just the classical statistical limit, and shows that the resulting effective action satisfies dynamical KMS symmetry at the quantum level.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model consisting of a probe SU(2) gauge field in a doubled Schwarzschild-AdS₅ geometry, following the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh contour prescription of Glorioso (2018). They solve the bulk Yang-Mills equations perturbatively in a derivative expansion, linearizing the system order by order in the field amplitude, and compute the partially on-shell bulk action up to quartic order in hydrodynamic fields. The boundary effective action is extracted from the bulk action by identifying the gapless mode as the gauge transformation parameter at the AdS boundary.

## Key numbers

- The Hawking temperature of the Schwarzschild-AdS₅ geometry is \( T = r_h/\pi \), identified as the temperature of the boundary theory.
- The effective action is computed accurate to first order in derivative expansion and up to quartic order in hydrodynamic fields.
- The mean free path for the holographic theory is \(\sim \hbar / T\), implying quantum fluctuations are unavoidably present in the derivative expansion.

## Context

This work extends the holographic derivation of hydrodynamic effective field theory from the U(1) charge diffusion case of Glorioso (2018) and de Boer (2018) to the non-Abelian SU(2) case, which involves significant technical complications due to nonlinearities. It relaxes two approximations made in the recent effective field theory for non-Abelian hydrodynamics by Glorioso (2020): truncating noise to quadratic order and imposing dynamical KMS symmetry only in the classical statistical limit. The results are potentially relevant for studying QCD plasma in the chiral limit and fluctuations in pion fluid at finite temperature.
