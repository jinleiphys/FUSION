# A Continuum Schwinger Method to Study the Pion's Generalized Parton Distribution

> Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs) provide multidimensional insight into hadron structure and are particularly relevant for the pion, whose dynamics are intimately linked to chiral symmetry breaking. We introduce a novel modelling strategy for pion GPDs that satisfies all QCD constraints by construction: support, polynomiality, positivity, and the soft-pion theorem. The approach is illustrated with a simple algebraic model, which is evolved and used to compute deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) Compton Form Factors at next-to-leading order. Our results indicate that gluons dominate the pion response at the Electron Ion Collider kinematics.

# A Continuum Schwinger Method to Study the Pion's Generalized Parton Distribution

## Key claim
The authors introduce a novel modelling strategy for pion GPDs that satisfies all QCD constraints by construction, but the approach is illustrated only with a simple algebraic model (a PDF ansatz of 30x²(1-x)²) rather than a full continuum Schwinger method calculation. The claim that gluons dominate the pion response at Electron Ion Collider kinematics is based on this algebraic model evolved and computed at next-to-leading order, not on a direct Schwinger method computation.

## Method
The authors construct pion GPDs using a three-step procedure: first, a factorised light-front wave function at a reference scale separating longitudinal and transverse dynamics; second, the DGLAP region GPD is built from an overlap representation that saturates the positivity bound; third, the ERBL region is obtained via covariant extension guaranteeing polynomiality, with the soft-pion theorem fixing the chiral limit. The resulting GPDs are evolved using QCD evolution equations implemented with Apfel++ and used to compute DVCS Compton Form Factors at next-to-leading order within the PARTONS framework.

## Key numbers
- The reference scale for the GPD model is μ_ref = 0.331 GeV.
- The simple algebraic PDF ansatz used for illustration is q(x) = 30x²(1-x)².
- The Sullivan process requires the invariant mass of the hadronic system to satisfy W² ≳ 4 GeV² to suppress resonance contributions.
- The momentum transfer must satisfy |t| ≲ 0.6 GeV² to ensure dominance of the one-pion-exchange mechanism.

## Context
This work extends the line of research on pion structure using continuum Schwinger methods, building on prior modelling by Chavez (2021) and Chavez (2021) who employed more sophisticated PDFs derived from these methods. The covariant extension technique follows Chouika (2017). The approach enables future experimental measurements at the Electron Ion Collider to probe pion GPDs via the Sullivan process, with the key finding that gluonic contributions dominate the DVCS response at collider kinematics, contrary to expectations that quark contributions might be dominant.
