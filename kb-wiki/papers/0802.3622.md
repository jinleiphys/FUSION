# A mean field study of single-particle spectra evolution in Z=14 and N=28 chains

> We study the mechanisms which reduce the proton 1d(3/2)-1d(5/2) spin-orbit splitting and the neutron 1f(7/2) subshell closure in 42Si. We use various self-consistent mean field models: non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock. Special attention is devoted to the influence of a tensor component in the effective interaction. It is found that the tensor force indeed governs the reduction of the 1d proton spin-orbit splitting. On the other hand, the reduction of the neutron 1f(7/2) subshell closure is not clearly related to the tensor force.

# A mean field study of single-particle spectra evolution in Z=14 and N=28 chains

## Key claim

The tensor component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction governs the reduction of the proton 1d spin-orbit splitting along the Z=14 isotopic chain from 34Si to 42Si, but the reduction of the neutron 1f7/2 subshell closure along the N=28 isotonic chain from 48Ca to 42Si is not clearly related to the tensor force and instead arises primarily from changes in the central potential and effective mass terms. These conclusions are drawn strictly within a static mean field framework, and the authors caution that effects beyond mean field such as particle-vibration coupling have not been evaluated.

## Method

The authors employed two self-consistent mean field approaches: non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing (using the SLy5 interaction, with and without an added tensor component with parameters alpha_T = -170 MeV fm^5 and beta_T = 100 MeV fm^5) and relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing (using the PKO1 parametrization which includes pion exchange and the PKO2 parametrization which does not). All calculations assumed spherical symmetry and used a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction with parameters fitted to reproduce empirical two-nucleon separation energies. The single-particle energies were decomposed into kinetic, central, spin-orbit, and non-locality contributions to identify the origin of spectral changes.

## Key numbers

- The proton 1d3/2-1d5/2 spin-orbit splitting in 42Si is reduced by 1.52 MeV from its value in 34Si when using SLy5 plus tensor (from 8.62 MeV to 7.10 MeV), compared to essentially zero reduction (6.38 MeV to 6.36 MeV) for SLy5 without tensor.
- The PKO1 relativistic model (with pion exchange) gives a reduction of the proton spin-orbit splitting of 1.43 MeV from N=20 to N=28 (from 7.26 MeV to 5.83 MeV), while PKO2 (without pion exchange) gives a reduction of 0.81 MeV (from 7.46 MeV to 6.65 MeV).
- The neutron 2p3/2-1f7/2 gap in 42Si is 2.55 MeV for SLy5 and 3.79 MeV for SLy5 plus tensor, compared to 3.75 MeV and 5.15 MeV respectively in 48Ca, giving reductions of 1.20 MeV and 1.36 MeV.
- The PKO1 model gives a neutron gap reduction of 2.65 MeV from 48Ca (4.78 MeV) to 42Si (2.13 MeV), and PKO2 gives a reduction of 2.29 MeV (from 5.22 MeV to 2.93 MeV).
- In the SLy5 plus tensor case, the kinetic plus central contribution to the neutron gap reduction from Z=20 to Z=14 is 840 keV, and the non-locality contribution is 564 keV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion initiated by Otsuka (2005) on the possible effects of the neutron-proton tensor force on single-particle spectra along isotopic and isotonic chains, and directly addresses experimental findings from Gaudefroy (2006) and Bastin (2007) who used shell model analyses to identify the shrinkage of the proton spin-orbit splitting in Si isotopes and the quenching of the N=28 gap. The study contrasts with earlier relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches which cannot describe tensor effects because they lack exchange terms, and instead uses the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework developed by Long (2006, 2008) where pion-induced tensor interactions can play a role. The results enable future work incorporating particle-vibration coupling effects beyond mean field, as discussed by Mahaux (1985), which are needed for direct comparison with empirical spectra.
