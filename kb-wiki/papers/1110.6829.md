# Renormalization in the Three-body Problem with Resonant P-wave Interactions

> Resonant P-wave interactions can be described by a minimal zero-range model defined by a truncated effective range expansion, so that the only 2-body interaction parameters are the inverse scattering volume 1/a_P and the P-wave effective range r_P. This minimal model can be formulated as a local quantum field theory with a P-wave interaction between atom fields and a molecular field. In the two-atom sector, the model is renormalizable, but it has unphysical behavior at high energies, because there are negative-probability states with momentum scale r_P. In the sector with three atoms, two of which are identical, renormalization in some parity and angular-momentum channels involves an ultraviolet limit cycle, indicating asymptotic discrete scale invariance. The Efimov effect occurs in the unitary limit a_P^(-1/3), r_P \to 0, but this limit is unphysical because there are low-energy states with negative probability. The minimal model can be of physical relevance only at energies small compared to the energy scale set by r_P, where the effects of negative-probability states are suppressed.

# Renormalization in the Three-body Problem with Resonant P-wave Interactions

## Key claim

The minimal zero-range model for resonant P-wave interactions, defined by truncating the effective range expansion to only the inverse scattering volume 1/a_P and the P-wave effective range r_P, exhibits an ultraviolet limit cycle and Efimov-like discrete scale invariance in certain three-body channels, but this unitary limit (a_P^{-1/3} = r_P = 0) is unphysical because it necessarily contains low-energy states with negative probability. The model can only be physically relevant at energies small compared to the energy scale set by r_P, where the effects of these negative-probability states are suppressed.

## Method

The authors formulate a local quantum field theory with a P-wave interaction between atom fields and a molecular (dimer) field, where the dimer field must be a ghost field (η = -1) to achieve renormalizability. They derive a single-variable integral equation analogous to the Skornyakov–ter-Martirosian (STM) equation for the three-body bound-state problem, decoupled by orbital angular momentum and parity quantum numbers J^P. The renormalization group behavior is analyzed by solving a determinant equation for the discrete scaling factor λ_0 = exp(π/s_0) in each channel, where i s_0 is a pure imaginary solution obtained from the Mellin transform of the integral kernel.

## Key numbers

* For equal masses (m_1 = m_2) with identical bosons in the 1^+ channel, the discrete scaling factor λ_0 = 116.7 (s_0 = 0.660).
* For equal masses with identical fermions, the discrete scaling factors are: 0^+ channel λ_0 = 34.2 (s_0 = 0.889); 1^+ channel λ_0 = 239.3 (s_0 = 0.574); 1^- channel λ_0 = 111.2 (s_0 = 0.667); 2^+ channel λ_0 = 501.9 (s_0 = 0.505).
* For identical fermions with equal masses and J^P = 0^+, an Efimov trimer with binding energy ħ²κ_*²/m at unitarity disappears through the three-atom threshold at a_P^- = -0.522(2)/κ_*³ and through the atom-dimer threshold at a_P^+ = +0.244(2)/κ_*³.
* For identical bosons with equal masses and J^P = 1^+, the corresponding threshold parameters are a_P^- = -0.134(2)/κ_*³ and a_P^+ = +0.452(2)/κ_*³.
* The Wigner bound requires r_P ≤ -2/R for a short-range potential of range R, and for a van der Waals tail the effective range saturates at r_P → -3.4/β_6, where β_6 = (2μC_6/ħ²)^{1/4}.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal few-body physics from the well-understood S-wave case, reviewed by Braaten and Hammer (2004), to resonant P-wave interactions. The authors identify channels exhibiting the Efimov effect, originally discovered by Efimov (1970) for S-wave interactions, and confirm the 1^- channel result previously found by Macek and Sternberg (2006). The paper builds on Nishida's (2011) proof that the minimal zero-range model necessarily contains negative-probability states, and on the work of Jona-Lasinio, Pricoupenko, and Castin (2007) who studied three identical fermions with a finite-range two-channel model. The results enable future applications to halo nuclei such as ⁶He, where Ji, Elster, and Phillips (2011) successfully applied a perturbative treatment of the unitarity term, and to ultracold atom experiments near P-wave Feshbach resonances.
