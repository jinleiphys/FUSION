# Chiral Magnetic Effect in Isobar Collisions from Stochastic Hydrodynamics

> We study chiral magnetic effect in collisions of AuAu, RuRu and ZrZr at s = 200GeV. The axial charge evolution is modeled with stochastic hydrodynamics and geometrical quantities are calculated with Monte Carlo Glauber model. By adjusting the relaxation time of magnetic field, we find our results in good agreement with background subtracted data for AuAu collisions at the same energy. We also make prediction for RuRu and ZrZr collisions. We find a weak centrality dependence of initial chiral imbalance, which implies the centrality dependence of chiral magnetic effect signal comes mainly from those of magnetic field and volume factor. Our results also show an unexpected dependence on system size: while the system of AuAu has larger chiral imbalance and magnetic field, it turns out to have smaller signal for chiral magnetic effect due to the larger volume suppression factor.

## Key claim
The authors model the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions using stochastic hydrodynamics that incorporates both initial axial charge from glasma flux tubes and its subsequent evolution with fluctuation and dissipation, finding that the CME signal depends unexpectedly on system size: AuAu has larger chiral imbalance and magnetic field but a smaller CME signal than RuRu or ZrZr due to a larger volume suppression factor.

## Method
The axial charge evolution is modeled with stochastic hydrodynamic equations that include a dissipative term from topological gluon configurations and a Gaussian white noise term, coupled to vector charge through anomalous mixing terms. The initial axial charge density is estimated from glasma flux tube parameters with Monte Carlo Glauber model geometrical inputs, and the magnetic field is calculated from Liénard-Wiechert potentials using Woods-Saxon nuclear distributions. The effective electric chemical potential at freeze-out is obtained by integrating the CME current from initial thermalization time to freeze-out time, with the magnetic field decay time treated as a fitting parameter fixed by STAR data for AuAu collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial axial chemical potential μ_A0 for AuAu at 0-5% centrality: 36.11 MeV
- Initial axial chemical potential μ_A0 for RuRu at 0-5% centrality: 31.13 MeV
- Initial axial chemical potential μ_A0 for ZrZr at 0-5% centrality: 31.85 MeV
- Magnetic field decay time τ_B = 1.65 fm, fixed by fitting to STAR AuAu data at √s = 200 GeV
- Initial temperature T_0 = 350 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_f = 154 MeV
- Initial proper time τ_0 = 0.6 fm for AuAu
- Chern-Simons diffusion constant Γ_CS = 30 α_s^4 T^4 with α_s = 0.3
- Axial charge susceptibility χ_A = χ_V = 3T^2 for three flavors
- Back-reaction from chiral separation effect estimated as ≤ 3% of axial chemical potential at peak magnetic field

## Context
This work extends the stochastic hydrodynamics framework for axial charge evolution developed by Lin et al. (2018) by incorporating initial axial charge from glasma flux tubes and studying the coupled dynamics of axial and vector charges in the presence of magnetic fields. It provides predictions for CME signals in isobar collisions (RuRu and ZrZr) at √s = 200 GeV, which the STAR collaboration has proposed as a way to distinguish CME from flow-related backgrounds (Skokov et al., 2016). The results enable comparison with upcoming experimental data from isobar collisions and suggest that the centrality dependence of CME signals arises primarily from magnetic field and volume factor rather than from initial chiral imbalance.
