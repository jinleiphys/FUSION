# Multiplicity fluctuations in the Glauber Monte Carlo approach

> We discuss multiplicity fluctuations of charged particles produced in nuclear collisions measured event-by-event by the NA49 experiment at CERN SPS within the Glauber Monte Carlo approach. We use the concepts of wounded nucleons and wounded quarks in the mechanism of multiparticle production to characterize multiplicity fluctuations expressed by the scaled variance of multiplicity distribution. Although Wounded Nucleon Model correctly reproduce the centrality dependence of the average multiplicity in Pb+Pb collisions, it completely fails in description of corresponding centrality dependence of scaled variance of multiplicity distribution. Using subnucleonic degrees of freedom, i.e. wounded quarks within Wounded Quark Model, it is possible to describe quite well the multiplicity distribution of charged particles produced in proton+proton interactions. However, the Wounded Quark Model with parameters describing multiplicity distribution of particles produced in proton+proton interactions substantially exceeds the average multiplicity of charged particles produced in Pb+Pb collisions. To obtain values of average multiplicities close to those experimentally measured in Pb+Pb collisions, the concept of shadowed quark sources is implemented. Wounded Quark Model with implemented shadowing source scenario reproduces the centrality dependence of scaled variance of multiplicity distribution of charged particles produced in Pb+Pb collisions in the range from the most central to mid-peripheral interactions.

## Key claim

The Wounded Quark Model with an implemented shadowing source scenario can partially reproduce the centrality dependence of the scaled variance of multiplicity distribution for charged particles in Pb+Pb collisions, but only for central and mid-peripheral collisions (projectile participant range \(80 < N_p^{proj} < 200\)); for more peripheral collisions the discrepancy between data and model predictions grows.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber Monte Carlo generator GLISSANDO to simulate Pb+Pb and proton+proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, implementing both the Wounded Nucleon Model (WNM) and the Wounded Quark Model (WQM). Multiplicity distributions from a single source were parameterized using the Negative Binomial distribution, fitted to NA49 proton+proton data. For Pb+Pb collisions, the distribution of emitting sources (wounded nucleons or wounded quarks) was convolved with the single-source distribution. To reduce the average multiplicity in Pb+Pb collisions, a shadowing procedure was applied where quark sources behind other sources in the same nucleus have their particle production suppressed by a factor \(S(n,\lambda) = \exp(-n \cdot \lambda)\), with \(\lambda = 0.95\).

## Key numbers

*   The Negative Binomial fit to proton+proton multiplicity data in the WNM gave parameters \(\langle N_{NB}\rangle = 1.4\) and \(k = 9.8\), with a variance \(Var(N_{NB}) = 1.6\) and \(\chi^2/N_{\rm{dof}} = 3.3\).
*   The WQM fit to proton+proton data gave NB parameters \(\langle N_{NB}\rangle = 0.53\) and \(k = 14\), with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm{dof}} \simeq 6\).
*   The average number of wounded quarks per nucleon in proton+proton interactions at this energy is \(Q_W = 1.27\).
*   The shadowing parameter used in the WQM to reproduce Pb+Pb average multiplicities was \(\lambda = 0.95\).
*   The WQM with shadowing reproduces the scaled variance data for Pb+Pb collisions in the projectile participant range \(80 < N_p^{proj} < 200\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bozek (2016) to describe multiplicity fluctuations, using NA49 experimental data from Alt (2006) which showed a non-trivial centrality dependence of the scaled variance that common models (HIJING, HSD, UrQMD, VENUS) fail to reproduce. The paper contradicts the Wounded Nucleon Model of Bialas (1976), which correctly describes average multiplicities but completely fails for scaled variance. The results enable further investigation into the role of subnucleonic degrees of freedom and suggest that a triggering process increasing the number of sources (sea quarks/gluons) may be needed for peripheral collisions with \(N_p^{proj} < 80\).
