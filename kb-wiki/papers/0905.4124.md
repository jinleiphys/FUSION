# Excited baryons as experimental probes of the quark mass

> We observe that excited hadrons provide an opportunity to probe from experiment the power-law running of the quark mass in the mid infrared, while the condition m(k)<k remains valid. A relatively clean analysis is possible for the maximum spin excitations of the Delta baryons, analogous to the yrast states in nuclei. Such states are accessible at current experimental facilities such as ELSA and Jlab.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the power-law running of the light quark mass in the mid-infrared region can be extracted from experimental data on highly excited baryons, specifically by measuring the parity splitting between maximum-spin Delta baryon states as a function of angular momentum, provided the condition m(k) < k holds for the probed momentum scale.

## Method

The authors perform a j-scaling analysis of the parity mass difference |M+ - M-| for highly excited Delta baryon yrast states (maximum spin for a given energy). They expand the QCD Hamiltonian in the weak sense for highly excited resonances where the average quark momentum ⟨k⟩ is large, keeping the leading chiral-invariant term and the next-order chiral-symmetry-breaking term proportional to m(k)/k. By relating the angular momentum j to the average quark momentum via the Regge trajectory and virial theorem, they derive that the parity splitting scales as |M+ - M-| ∝ j^{-i}, which translates into a running quark mass m(k) ∝ k^{-2i+2}.

## Key numbers

- The string tension used for illustrative variational wavefunctions is σ = 0.135 GeV²
- For a linear potential model with 3 quarks in a baryon, the constant c₂ relating average momentum to mass is c₂ = 1/6
- The angular matrix element of the chiral-symmetry-breaking Hamiltonian scales as j (equation showing ⟨H^{QCD'}_χ⟩_ang → j)
- The parity splitting scales as |M+ - M-| ∝ j^{-1} times the running quark mass m(⟨k⟩)
- The running quark mass extracted from the splitting exponent i follows m(k) ∝ k^{-2i+2}

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger studies (Alkofer, 2003) and lattice gauge theory calculations (Bowman, 2006) of the running quark mass by proposing an experimental observable sensitive to its mid-infrared behavior. The parity doubling phenomenon for highly excited hadrons, previously discussed by Detar (1988) and Nefediev (2008), is exploited to access the chiral-symmetry-breaking term. The analysis enables experimental extraction of the quark mass running exponent from existing and future data at facilities such as ELSA and JLab, complementing theoretical determinations from sum rules and lattice QCD.
