# Bound H-dibaryon from Full QCD Simulations on the Lattice

> Using a new method recently proposed by the HAL QCD collaboration to study hadron interactions in lattice QCD, we investigate a possibility for an existence of the H-dibaryon in the flavor SU(3) symmetric world. A potential for the flavor-singlet baryon-baryon channel is derived from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function, and the elusive H-dibaryon is shown to be a bound state, with the binding energy of 20--50 MeV for the pseudo-scalar meson masses in the range that 469--1171 MeV.

## Key claim
Using the HAL QCD method's time-dependent NBS wave function approach, the H-dibaryon is shown to be a bound state in the flavor SU(3) symmetric world, with binding energies of 20–50 MeV for pseudo-scalar meson masses between 469 and 1171 MeV; the authors caution that this binding energy is measured from the flavor-averaged baryon-baryon threshold and may become much smaller or even vanish when realistic SU(3) breaking is introduced.

## Method
The HAL QCD collaboration extracted a baryon-baryon potential in the flavor-singlet channel from the time-dependent Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function on the lattice, using a velocity expansion truncated at leading order. This potential was then used to solve the Schrödinger equation in infinite volume to determine the bound-state energy and size of the H-dibaryon. The simulations used 32³ × 32 lattices with the Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quarks at five quark masses, with lattice spacing 0.121(2) fm and volume 3.87 fm.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy at pseudo-scalar meson mass 1171 MeV: 49.1(3.4)(5.5) MeV (statistical and systematic errors)
- Binding energy at pseudo-scalar meson mass 469 MeV: 26.0(4.4)(4.8) MeV
- Binding energy at pseudo-scalar meson mass 837 MeV: 37.8(3.1)(4.2) MeV
- Binding energy at pseudo-scalar meson mass 672 MeV: 33.6(4.8)(3.5) MeV
- Binding energy at pseudo-scalar meson mass 1015 MeV: 37.2(3.7)(2.4) MeV
- Lattice spacing: 0.121(2) fm; lattice size: 3.87 fm
- Pseudo-scalar meson masses explored: 469–1171 MeV

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2009) for extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD without requiring ground-state saturation, overcoming the finite-volume contamination that plagued earlier lattice studies of the H-dibaryon (Wetzorke 2002). The result is consistent with the full QCD simulation by Beane (2010) who found a binding energy of 16.6(2.1)(4.6) MeV from the ΛΛ threshold at lighter quark masses. The authors note that a deeply bound H-dibaryon was ruled out by the discovery of the double Λ hypernucleus ⁶_ΛΛHe (Takahashi 2001), and their trial calculation with phenomenological SU(3) breaking suggests the H-dibaryon may become a resonance above the ΛΛ threshold but below the NΞ threshold at physical quark masses; coupled-channel lattice simulations at the physical point are needed for a definitive conclusion.
