# $s$-wave scattering lengths for the $^7$Be+p system from an $\textit{R}$-matrix analysis

> The astrophysical $S$-factor for the radiative proton capture reaction on $^7$Be ($S_{17}$) at low energies is affected by the $s$-wave scattering lengths. We report the measurement of elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections for the $^7$Be+p system in the center-of-mass energy range 0.474 - 2.740 MeV and center-of-mass angular range of 70$^\circ$- 150$^\circ$. A radioactive $^7$Be beam produced at Oak Ridge National Laboratory's (ORNL) Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility was accelerated and bombarded a thin polypropylene (CH$_{2}$)$_\text n$ target. Scattered ions were detected in the segmented Silicon Detector Array. Using an $\textit{R}$-matrix analysis of ORNL and Louvain-la-Neuve cross section data, the $s$-wave scattering lengths for channel spins 1 and 2 were determined to be 17.34$^{+1.11}_{-1.33}$ and -3.18$^{+0.55}_{-0.50}$ fm, respectively. The uncertainty in the $s$-wave scattering lengths reported in this work is smaller by a factor of 5-8 compared to the previous measurement, which may reduce the overall uncertainty in $S_{17}$ at zero energy. The level structure of $^8$B is discussed based upon the results from this work. Evidence for the existence of 0$^+$ and 2$^+$ levels in $^8$B at 1.9 and 2.21 MeV, respectively, is observed.

## Key claim

The s-wave scattering lengths for the 7Be+p system are determined with uncertainties smaller by a factor of 5-8 compared to the only previous measurement, which may reduce the overall uncertainty in the astrophysical S-factor S17 at zero energy, but the reduction depends on the range of scattering lengths considered in the calculation and the relationship between the scattering lengths and S17(0) is not highlighted in all theoretical models.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections for the 7Be+p system were measured in inverse kinematics using a radioactive 7Be beam from the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility bombarding a thin polypropylene target, with scattered protons detected in the segmented Silicon Detector Array over a center-of-mass energy range of 0.474-2.740 MeV and angular range of 70-150 degrees. A multichannel, multilevel R-matrix analysis using the code AZURE2 was performed simultaneously on the ORNL data and the low-energy elastic scattering data from Angulo et al. (2003) to extract the s-wave scattering lengths for channel spins 1 and 2.

## Key numbers

* s-wave scattering length for channel spin 1: a01 = 17.34 (+1.11/-1.33) fm
* s-wave scattering length for channel spin 2: a02 = -3.18 (+0.55/-0.50) fm
* Uncertainty reduction factor: 5-8 times smaller than the previous measurement by Angulo et al. (2003), who found a01 = 25 +/- 9 fm and a02 = -7 +/- 3 fm
* Average scattering length: 0.60 (+0.15/-0.18) fm, using asymptotic normalization constants from Zhang (2017)
* Excitation energy of 0+ level in 8B: 1.9 +/- 0.1 MeV, with elastic width 0.120 +/- 0.028 MeV and inelastic width 0.428 +/- 0.130 MeV
* Excitation energy of 2+ level in 8B: 2.21 +/- 0.04 MeV, with elastic width 0.024 +/- 0.009 MeV and inelastic width 0.230 +/- 0.001 MeV

## Context

This work extends the only previous measurement of s-wave scattering lengths for the 7Be+p system by Angulo et al. (2003) with significantly reduced uncertainties, and addresses discrepancies with ab-initio no-core shell model/resonating group method calculations by Navratil et al. (2011) which predicted a01 = -5.2 fm and a02 = -15.3 fm. The improved scattering lengths can better constrain extrapolations of the astrophysical S-factor S17 for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction to solar energies (Gamow peak ~18 keV), which is the most uncertain nuclear input for predicting the 8B solar neutrino flux in standard solar models. The analysis also provides evidence for 0+ and 2+ levels in 8B at 1.9 and 2.21 MeV, respectively, supporting some suggestions by Mitchell et al. (2013) while finding no evidence for the 1+ level at 3.3 MeV reported in that same work.
