# Non-zero transversity distribution of the pion in a quark-spectator-antiquark model

> We calculate the non-zero (naïve) T-odd transverse momentum dependent transversity distribution $h_1^{\perp}(x,\kp^2)$ of the pion in a quark-spectator-antiquark model. The final-state interaction is modelled by the approximation of one gluon exchange between the quark and the antiquark spectator. Using our model result we estimate the unsuppressed cos2$ϕ$ azimuthal asymmetry in unpolarized $π^-p$ Drell-Yan process. We find that the transverse momentum dependence of $h_1^{\perp}(x,\kp^2)$ of the pion is the same as that of $h_1^{\perp}(x,\kp^2)$ of the proton calculated from the quark-scalar-diquark model, although the $x$ dependencies of them are different from each other. This suggests a connection between cos2$ϕ$ asymmetries in Drell-Yan processes with different initial hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the naive T-odd transverse momentum dependent transversity distribution \(h_1^{\perp}(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2)\) of the pion in a quark-spectator-antiquark model, finding that its transverse momentum dependence is identical to that of the proton from a quark-scalar-diquark model, though the \(x\) dependence differs. The authors estimate an unsuppressed \(\cos 2\phi\) azimuthal asymmetry in unpolarized \(\pi^- p\) Drell-Yan process of order \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\), suggesting a connection between such asymmetries for different initial hadrons.

## Method

The authors compute the quark correlation function of the pion in Feynman gauge, expanding the path-ordered exponential (Wilson line) to first order to model the final-state interaction via one-gluon exchange between the struck quark and the antiquark spectator. The pion-quark-antiquark interaction is described by a pseudoscalar coupling, and the trace calculations are performed in momentum space using contour integration and the eikonal approximation. The resulting \(h_{1\pi}^{\perp}(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2)\) is then used to evaluate the \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry coefficient \(\nu\) in the Collins-Soper frame, assuming \(u\)-quark dominance and adopting the proton's \(h_1^{\perp}\) from the quark-scalar-diquark model of Boer, Brodsky, and Hwang (2003).

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(|\mathbf{k}_\perp h_{1\pi}^{\perp}(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2)|/[M_\pi f_{1\pi}(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2)]\) is plotted at \(|\mathbf{k}_\perp| = 0.3\) GeV and at \(x = 0.15\), using constituent quark mass \(m = 0.1\) GeV, pion mass \(M_\pi = 0.137\) GeV, and \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).
* The \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry \(\nu\) in unpolarized \(\pi^- p\) Drell-Yan is evaluated numerically as a function of \(Q_T\) at \(x = \bar{x} = 0.2\), yielding an unsuppressed result of order \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\).
* The parameter \(A_\pi/C_\pi = 0.01\) GeV\(^2\) and \(D_\pi = 2B_\pi \approx 0.014\) are used, corresponding to \(\bar{x} = 0.2\); for the proton, \(A_p/C_p = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) and \(D_p = 4B_p \approx 1/4\) are taken from Boer, Brodsky, and Hwang (2003).

## Context

This work extends the quark-scalar-diquark model calculation of \(h_1^{\perp}\) for the proton by Boer, Brodsky, and Hwang (2003) to the pion using a quark-spectator-antiquark model, providing the first computation of the pion's T-odd transversity distribution. The result enables estimates of the \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry in \(\pi^- p\) Drell-Yan processes, which were previously measured experimentally by the NA10 Collaboration (1986) and Conway et al. (1989) but could not be explained by perturbative QCD. The similarity of the transverse momentum dependence across hadrons suggests a universal mechanism for generating such asymmetries, motivating further studies of Drell-Yan processes with different initial hadrons.
