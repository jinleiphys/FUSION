# XSgen: A Cross Section Generation Methodology for Fuel Cycle Simulation

> This work investigates a method for generating medium fidelity reactor model cross sections. The methodology used here couples a neutron transport code with a depletion calculator. Together the two can be used to generate time dependent group cross sections for medium fidelity reactor models in fuel cycle simulation. This work analyzes XSgen, a software that performs the functions mentioned above. Addtionally, it is shown that XSgen is capable of creating datasets for a medium fidelity reactor model known as Bright-lite. The results of this work show that XSgen produces datasets that match NEA benchmarks for both uranium based LWR, and MOX reactors.

## Key claim

XSgen generates medium-fidelity reactor cross-section datasets that match NEA benchmarks for both uranium-based LWR and MOX reactors, but the agreement depends on having a full and complete reactor specification; when such specification is unavailable (as for the VISION MOX case), discrepancies in output compositions are reasonable and expected.

## Method

The XSgen methodology couples OpenMC (Monte Carlo neutron transport) with ORIGEN v2.2 (depletion solver) via PyNE (cross-section collapse), iterating over user-defined time steps. OpenMC extracts a 1000-group, logarithmically spaced flux (1E-9 MeV to 10 MeV) from the fuel region of a supplied reactor geometry; PyNE collapses this to one-group cross sections using a partial energy matrix; ORIGEN then computes burnup, neutron production/destruction rates, and transmutation matrices. The fuel composition at the end of each time step is fed back into OpenMC, and the process repeats until the maximum time step is reached.

## Key numbers

- Flux energy range: 1000 groups, logarithmically spaced from 1E-9 MeV to 10 MeV.
- LWR test case: 3.5% enriched UOX fuel, constant flux of 3E14 n/s/cm², pin-cell model with fuel cell radius 0.410 cm, clad radius 0.475 cm, unit cell pitch 1.32 cm.
- For 3.0% enriched UOX LWR: U235 mass fraction 6.70E-3 (Bright-lite) vs 6.75E-3 (VISION), -0.78% difference; U238 mass fraction 9.41E-1 vs 9.45E-1, -0.42% difference.
- For 3.5% enriched UOX LWR: U235 mass fraction 6.71E-3 (Bright-lite) vs 6.80E-3 (VISION), -1.30% difference; Am241 mass fraction 5.17E-5 vs 4.59E-5, 12.60% difference.
- For 4.0% enriched UOX LWR: U235 mass fraction 6.91E-3 (Bright-lite) vs 6.95E-3 (VISION), -0.56% difference; Pu241 mass fraction 1.62E-3 vs 1.62E-3, -0.02% difference.
- NEA benchmark (3.1% enriched LWR startup): Batch 1 burnup 13.570 MWd/kgIHM (Bright-lite) vs 12.04 MWd/kg (NEA), 12.754% difference; equilibrium (batch 5+) burnup 33.020 MWd/kgIHM vs 33.00 MWd/kg, 0.060% difference.
- NEA benchmark (3.6% enriched LWR startup): Batch 1 burnup 13.37 MWd/kgIHM (Bright-lite) vs 13.9 MWd/kg (NEA), -3.84% difference; equilibrium (batch 7+) burnup 42.32 MWd/kgIHM vs 42.50 MWd/kg, -0.43% difference.
- MOX verification: Pu239 mass fraction 3.15E-2 (Bright-lite) vs 3.31E-2 (VISION), -4.6% difference; Cm244 mass fraction 9.84E-4 vs 1.03E-3, -4.4% difference.

## Context

This work extends the approach of coupling neutron transport with depletion (as in LOOP) by automating dataset generation for medium-fidelity reactor models like Bright-lite, CLASS, and CyBORG. The paper demonstrates that XSgen can produce datasets that match VISION recipes (used in fuel cycle simulation) to within 5% for most nuclides in LWR cases, with larger deviations for higher-order transuranics such as Am241 (up to 12.6% difference). This enables more accurate comparisons between different medium-fidelity models by providing a common input basis, and allows construction of recipe-based low-fidelity models via the linear reactivity model.
