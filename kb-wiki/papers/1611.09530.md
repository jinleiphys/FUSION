# Hadron tomography studies by generalized parton distributions and distribution amplitudes

> We discuss hadron-tomography studies for the nucleon and exotic hadrons by high-energy hadron reactions. First, the constituent-counting rule is explained for determining internal quark configurations of exotic-hadron candidates by scaling properties of high-energy exclusive cross sections. Next, possibilities are discussed for investigating the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon and exotic hadrons at J-PARC. In particular, we study hadronic $2 \to 3$ process $p+p \to N+π+B$, exclusive Drell-Yan process, and exotic-hadron GPDs. For determining three-dimensional structure of unstable exotic hadrons, we consider $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs called generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which can be investigated at KEKB. We explain possible studies of the GDAs by two-photon processes.

## Key claim

The constituent-counting rule and three-dimensional structure functions (GPDs and GDAs) can be used to determine the internal quark-gluon configurations of exotic-hadron candidates, but the scaling analysis of Λ(1405) production currently has errors too large to draw a solid conclusion on whether it is a five-quark or three-quark state.

## Method

The author reviews the constituent-counting rule for exclusive hadron reactions, where the cross section scales as 1/s^(n-2) with n being the total number of constituents. For GPD studies, the author examines the hadronic 2→3 process p+p→N+π+B and the exclusive Drell-Yan process π⁻+p→μ⁺μ⁻+n at J-PARC kinematics, using meson-pole contributions and the GK2013 GPD parametrization. For unstable exotic hadrons, the author proposes using generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which are s-t crossed quantities of GPDs, accessible via two-photon processes at KEKB.

## Key numbers

- For the constituent-counting rule, the scaling factor n=9 (=1+3+2+3) for γ+p→K⁺+Λ indicates the ground Λ is consistent with a three-quark (qqq) picture.
- Cross-section estimates for the 2→3 process p+p→N+π+B at J-PARC used t=(p_a-p_e)²=-0.3 GeV² and t'=-5.0 GeV² as an example, with proton-beam energies of 30 GeV and 50 GeV.
- For the exclusive Drell-Yan process, three possible pion momenta were considered: p_π=10, 15, and 20 GeV.
- The GDA form factor for two-photon processes is expressed as F_{h(q)}(W²)=1/[1+(W²-4m_h²)/Λ²]^(n-1), where n=2 for ordinary qq̅ mesons and n=4 for tetraquark hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the application of hadron tomography techniques, previously focused on the nucleon spin puzzle via GPDs and TMDs, to exotic-hadron studies. It builds on the constituent-counting rule developed by Kumano et al. (2013, 2016) and proposes specific experimental projects at J-PARC and KEKB. The GDA approach offers a way to study unstable exotic hadrons that cannot be used as fixed targets, complementing DVCS experiments and enabling future investigations of candidates like Λ(1405), f₀(980), and a₀(980).
