# Statistical hadronization of heavy quarks in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We present new results on the statistical hadronization of heavy quarks at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies. Several new aspects are considered, among them a separation of the collision geometry into a ``core'' and a ``corona'' part and an estimate of the annihilation rate of charm quark in a hot plasma, together with a critical assessment of its influence on the results. For RHIC energies we investigate the centrality dependence of J/psi production focusing on the model results for different values of the charm production cross section, including its theoretical and experimental uncertainty. We also study, within this model, the rapidity dependence of the J/psi yield. Recent RHIC data from the PHENIX experiment are well reproduced. At LHC energy, we update our model predictions for the centrality dependence of the J/psi yield and investigate as well the rapidity dependence. We also discuss the transverse momentum distributions of J/psi mesons expected from the model and provide predictions for a range of values of the expansion velocity at chemical freeze-out. Finally, we extend the model to predict Upsilon yields in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model (SHM) can reproduce J/ψ production data at RHIC and SPS energies, but only if charm quark annihilation in the quark-gluon plasma is negligible (less than 1% even for unrealistically high charm densities) and if the collision geometry is separated into a "core" (where QGP forms and SHM applies) and a "corona" (where production follows elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions).

## Method

The authors use the statistical hadronization model (SHM) which assumes all heavy quarks are produced in initial hard collisions and their total number stays constant until hadronization at the critical temperature Tc. A charm balance equation with canonical suppression factors determines a fugacity parameter gc, from which charmonium yields are computed as N_j = gc^2 * N_j^th. The collision geometry is separated into a core region (where QGP is assumed to form and SHM applies) and a corona region (treated as superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions), with the core defined using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile and a corona thickness parameter Xc = 1.2 fm. Charm quark annihilation in the QGP is estimated using perturbative QCD cross sections for c + cbar → g + g, with a Bjorken-type 1-dimensional expansion model for the QGP evolution.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (Au-Au, √sNN = 200 GeV): thermal parameters (T, μb) = (161, 22.4) MeV; charged particle rapidity density dNch/dy = 701 leads to VΔy=1 = 2400 fm³ for central collisions
- At LHC (Pb-Pb, √sNN = 5.5 TeV): thermal parameters (T, μb) = (161, 0.8) MeV; expected dNch/dy = 1816 leads to VΔy=1 = 6200 fm³ at midrapidity
- Charm production cross section in pp at RHIC: dσcbar/dy = 63.7 +95.6/-42.3 μb (pQCD); measured by PHENIX: 123 ± 47 μb; measured by STAR: 300 ± 98 μb
- Charm production cross section in pp at LHC: dσcbar/dy = 639 +639/-319 μb (pQCD, scaled by factor 1.5)
- J/ψ cross section in pp at RHIC: 0.724 ± 0.124 μb (measured by PHENIX); at LHC: 6.4 ± 3.2 μb (assumed as 1% of charm cross section)
- J/ψ cross section in pp at SPS: 0.050 ± 0.030 μb (interpolated)
- Minimal core volume for SHM validity: VQGP_min = 400 fm³
- Charm quark annihilation fraction in QGP: less than 1% even for dNc/dy(τ0) ≈ 60 at LHC; negligible for realistic charm densities (dNc/dy = 1.4 at RHIC, 13.3 at LHC from pQCD)
- QGP lifetime: τc = 2.7 fm at RHIC (initial temperature T(τ0) = 225 MeV); τc = 8.3 fm at LHC (T(τ0) = 325 MeV)
- Bottom cross section at LHC: Υ(1S) in pp assumed as 36.8 nb (1.7×10⁻³ of bbbar cross section at √sNN = 5.5 TeV)

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously developed by the same authors (Andronic et al., 2004; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2001) and independently by a kinetic model (Thews et al., 2001-2003). It introduces the core-corona separation following the approach of Werner (2005) and provides the first quantitative estimate of charm quark annihilation in the QGP, demonstrating it can be safely neglected. The model reproduces PHENIX J/ψ data at RHIC and provides predictions for LHC energies where data were expected within about two years of publication. The approach contrasts with color screening models (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Satz, 2005) which assume quarkonia are suppressed in the QGP rather than formed at hadronization.
