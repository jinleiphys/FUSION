# Spontaneous fission lifetimes from the minimization of self-consistent collective action

> The spontaneous fission lifetime of 264Fm has been studied within nuclear density functional theory by minimizing the collective action integral for fission in a two-dimensional quadrupole collective space representing elongation and triaxiality. The collective potential and inertia tensor are obtained self-consistently using the Skyrme energy density functional and density-dependent pairing interaction. The resulting spontaneous fission lifetimes are compared with the static result obtained with the minimum-energy pathway. We show that fission pathways strongly depend on assumptions underlying collective inertia. With the non-perturbative mass parameters, the dynamic fission pathway becomes strongly triaxial and it approaches the static fission valley. On the other hand, when the standard perturbative cranking inertia tensor is used, axial symmetry is restored along the path to fission; an effect that is an artifact of the approximation used.

## Key claim

The spontaneous fission lifetime of 264Fm depends strongly on whether the collective inertia tensor is computed with a non-perturbative cranking approximation or the standard perturbative cranking approximation; the perturbative approach artificially restores axial symmetry along the fission path, an effect the authors identify as an artifact of that approximation.

## Method

The authors computed the spontaneous fission half-life of 264Fm by minimizing the collective action integral in a two-dimensional quadrupole collective space of elongation (Q20) and triaxiality (Q22). The collective potential and inertia tensor were obtained self-consistently using the Skyrme energy density functional SkM* and a density-dependent mixed pairing force within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, using the symmetry-unrestricted solver HFODD. Minimum-action fission paths were determined using both the dynamic-programming method and the Ritz method, comparing results from non-perturbative cranking inertia and perturbative cranking inertia.

## Key numbers

- The zero-point energy for 264Fm varies between 0 and -0.9 MeV across the deformation range considered.
- Triaxial deformations reduce the fission barrier height by over 2 MeV.
- For the static path with non-perturbative inertia (M^C), the action integral S(L) = 23.4, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -7.7.
- For the static path with perturbative inertia (M^{C^p}), S(L) = 20.8, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -10.0.
- For the DPM path with non-perturbative inertia (M^C), S(L) = 19.1, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -11.4.
- For the RM path with non-perturbative inertia (M^C), S(L) = 18.9, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -11.6.
- For the DPM path with perturbative inertia (M^{C^p}), S(L) = 16.8, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -13.4.
- For the RM path with perturbative inertia (M^{C^p}), S(L) = 16.8, giving log(T1/2/yr) = -13.4.
- The collective ground state energy E0 was assumed to be 1 MeV, and the number of assaults on the fission barrier per unit time n = 10^{20.38} s^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the density-functional theory approach to spontaneous fission by incorporating dynamic action minimization, building on the static calculations of Staszczak (2009) and the cranking inertia formalism of Baran (2011). The finding that perturbative cranking inertia artificially restores axial symmetry confirms and explains similar observations in earlier studies by Smolanczuk (1993, 1995), Gherghescu (1999), Delaroche (2006), and Warda (2012). The results enable future calculations using the full adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov inertia and motivate studies of competition between triaxial and reflection-asymmetric degrees of freedom in fission dynamics.
