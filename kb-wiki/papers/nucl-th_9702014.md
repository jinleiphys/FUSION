# Normalization of the covariant three-body bound state vertex function

> The normalization condition for the relativistic three nucleon Bethe-Salpeter and Gross bound state vertex functions is derived, for the first time, directly from the three body wave equations. It is also shown that the relativistic normalization condition for the two body Gross bound state vertex function is identical to the requirement that the bound state charge be conserved, proving that charge is automatically conserved by this equation.

## Key claim

The normalization condition for the relativistic three-body Bethe-Salpeter and Gross (spectator) bound state vertex functions is derived directly from the three-body wave equations for the first time; additionally, the normalization condition for the two-body Gross vertex function is shown to be identical to the requirement that the bound state charge equals the sum of its constituents' charges, proving that charge is automatically conserved by the spectator equation.

## Method

The authors derive the normalization condition for the two-body Bethe-Salpeter and Gross vertex functions by expanding the scattering matrix around the bound state pole at \(P^2 = M^2\) and equating residues, then generalize this approach to the three-body case using the Faddeev decomposition of the three-body scattering equations. For the two-body Gross equation, they prove charge conservation by evaluating the electromagnetic current matrix element in the \(q \to 0\) limit, using the Ward-Takahashi identity and the spectator equation to show that the normalization condition ensures the bound state charge equals the sum of constituent charges.

## Key numbers

- The bound state pole occurs at \(P^2 = M^2\), where \(M\) is the bound state mass (no specific numerical value given).
- The normalization condition for the two-body Gross vertex function is given by \(1 = \langle \Gamma_1 | G'_2 | \Gamma_1 \rangle - \langle \Gamma_1 | G_2 \overline{V}'_{11} G_2 | \Gamma_1 \rangle\), where \(G'_2 = \partial G_2 / \partial P^2\) and \(\overline{V}'_{11} = \partial \overline{V}_{11} / \partial P^2\).
- The bound state charge \(e_B\) is shown to equal \(e_1 + e_2\) for distinguishable particles, and \(e_B = 2e_1\) for identical particles, where \(e_i\) are the constituent charges.

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of normalization conditions for two-body Bethe-Salpeter and Gross equations (e.g., by Lurié, Gross and Van Orden, and others) to the three-body case for the first time, and provides the first proof that charge is automatically conserved in the spectator formalism. The results enable consistent calculation of electromagnetic matrix elements for three-nucleon bound states using the covariant spectator equation, and the authors note that a forthcoming paper will present general Ward-Takahashi identities for two- and three-body systems in the spectator formalism.
