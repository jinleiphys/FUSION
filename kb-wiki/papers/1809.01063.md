# Effects of neutrino mixing upon electron fraction in core collapse supernovae

> The inclusion of massive neutrinos affects the cross sections involved in the formation of heavy nuclei, modifying their abundances. Rapid neutron capture processes (r-process) are often associated with explosive events such as core-collapse supernovae. In this work we study the effects of active and sterile neutrino oscillations and interactions, upon the calculation of neutrino fluxes, the baryonic density and the electron fraction of the material. We have considered two different initial distribution functions of the neutrinos and different combinations of mixing parameters (including θ 34 = 0). We use the formalism of density matrices for the calculations and included the effects of neutrino oscillations, interactions with matter and self-neutrino interactions. We found that the interactions of the neutrinos with matter and with themselves change the electron fraction, affecting the onset of the r-process.

## Key claim

Neutrino oscillations, including active-sterile mixing, can significantly reduce the electron fraction in the late neutrino-driven wind of core-collapse supernovae, but the magnitude of this reduction depends on the specific combination of neutrino interactions considered (matter interactions, self-neutrino interactions), the mixing parameters, and the initial neutrino distribution function.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled evolution equations for neutrino and antineutrino density matrices in the flavor basis, including vacuum oscillations, neutrino-matter interactions, and neutrino-neutrino interactions under the single-angle approximation, for both a two-flavor active-active mixing scheme and a three-flavor (2+1) active-sterile mixing scheme. They computed neutrino number densities, fluxes, reaction rates, and the resulting electron fraction as a function of radius for the late cooling phase at post-bounce time t_pb ~ 10 sec, using two different initial neutrino distribution functions (Fermi-Dirac and power-law) and fixed entropy per baryon S_100 = 1.5.

## Key numbers

- Electron fraction Y_e was computed as a function of radius for the late cooling phase at t_pb ~ 10 sec, with the neutrino sphere radius taken as R_nu = 10 km.
- The baryon mass-density was modeled as rho_b ~ 38 * (2/11) * g_s * (M_NS^3) / (S_100^4 * r_7^3) in units of 10^3 g cm^{-3}, with S_100 = 1.5.
- Neutrino cross sections used were sigma_nu(E_nu) = 9.6 x 10^{-44} * ((E_nu +/- Delta m_np) / MeV) cm^2, with Delta m_np = 1.293 MeV.
- Mixing parameters used: Delta m_13^2 = 2 x 10^{-3} eV^2, Delta m_14^2 = 2 eV^2, sin^2 2 theta_13 = 0.09, and sin^2 2 theta_14 = 0.16.
- Calculations were performed for two values of alpha particle mass fraction: X_alpha = 0 and X_alpha = 0.3.

## Context

This work extends studies of neutrino oscillations in supernova environments by Balantekin and Yuksel (2004), Tamborra et al. (2012), and Wu et al. (2015), specifically examining the impact of sterile neutrinos on the electron fraction in the late neutrino-driven wind. The results indicate that active-active oscillations alone are the most unfavorable scenario for achieving Y_e <= 0.5, while inclusion of sterile neutrinos can drastically reduce Y_e, affecting the viability of the r-process. The work enables further investigation of how active-sterile mixing parameters and initial neutrino flux modeling influence nucleosynthesis conditions in core-collapse supernovae.
