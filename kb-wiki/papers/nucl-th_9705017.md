# How to count nucleon pairs?

## Key claim

Within the isovector-pairing SO(5) model, three different methods for quantifying numbers of neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp), and neutron-proton (np) pairs give different numerical results, but all three confirm the odd-even staggering in pair numbers for even-even versus odd-odd N=Z nuclei and the reduction of np-pair number with increasing isospin projection T_z. The paper emphasizes that pair numbers are not directly observable and no fermion operator exists that counts specific pair types.

## Method

The study examines three methods for defining average pair numbers in seniority-zero eigenstates of the SO(5) monopole-isovector pairing Hamiltonian. Method I uses the expectation values of operators proportional to S†_ν S_ν, which quantify contributions of each pairing term to the total energy. Method II defines pair numbers via squared overlaps of the wave function with a nonorthogonal pair basis constructed from nn-, pp-, and np-pair creation operators, then scales to satisfy sum and charge constraints. Method III employs a Dyson boson realization of the O(5) algebra, mapping fermion-pair operators onto scalar-isovector bosons and identifying average boson numbers as the pair numbers.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Engel, Leviatan, and Vogel (1996) for quantifying pair numbers in nuclear wave functions, using the exactly solvable SO(5) isovector-pairing model that reflects features of realistic shell-model calculations. The paper proposes two alternative definitions (method II using pair-basis overlaps and method III using Dyson boson mapping) to address deficiencies in the original method, particularly its failure to satisfy the charge constraint ⟨N_nn⟩ - ⟨N_pp⟩ = T_z. The boson counting method (III) is argued to have advantages as it obeys natural constraints and gives zero pair number for a pairing mode switched off in the Hamiltonian. The results enable discussion of at what (N-Z) value np-pairing can be neglected, with the approximate wave function (no np correlations) achieving over 90% overlap with the exact state at (N-Z)=10.
