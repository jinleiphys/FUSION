# Global polarization of QGP in non-central heavy ion collisions at high energies

> Due to the presence of a large orbital angular momentum of the parton system produced at the early stage of non-central heavy-ion collisions, quarks and anti-quarks are shown to be polarized in the direction opposite to the reaction plane which is determined by the impact-parameter and the beam momentum. The global quark polarization via elastic scattering was first calculated in an effective static potential model, then using QCD at finite temperature with the hard-thermal-loop re-summed gluon propagator. The measurable consequences are discussed. Global hyperon polarization from the hadronization of polarized quarks are predicted independent of the hadronization scenarios. It has also been shown that the global polarization of quarks and anti-quarks leads also to spin alignment of vector mesons. Dedicated measurements at RHIC are underway and some of the preliminary results are obtained. In this presentation, the basic idea and main results of global quark polarization are presented. The direct consequences such as global hyperon polarization and spin alignment are summarized.

# Global polarization of QGP in non-central heavy ion collisions at high energies

## Key claim

Quarks and anti-quarks in the parton system produced at the early stage of non-central heavy-ion collisions become polarized opposite to the reaction plane due to the conversion of large local orbital angular momentum into spin via parton scattering, with the polarization magnitude depending sensitively on the ratio of parton center-of-mass energy to temperature and reaching up to 20% at moderate values of that ratio.

## Method

The global orbital angular momentum of the overlapping system in non-central Au+Au collisions was estimated using a hard spherical nuclear distribution. Quark polarization via elastic scattering was first calculated in an effective static potential model under the small-angle approximation, then using QCD at finite temperature with a hard-thermal-loop (HTL) re-summed gluon propagator regularized by introducing a non-perturbative magnetic mass. Numerical integrations of the spin-dependent and spin-independent cross sections in impact parameter space were performed without the small-angle approximation to obtain the quark polarization as a function of the ratio of parton center-of-mass energy to temperature.

## Key numbers

- The global orbital angular momentum $L_y$ of the overlapping system in non-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is of the order of $10^5$ (dimensionless units) for most impact parameters $b$.
- The average relative orbital angular momentum $l_y$ for two neighboring partons separated by $\Delta x = 1$ fm is of order 1, larger than the spin of a quark.
- The quark polarization $P_q$ can be as high as 20% at moderate $\sqrt{\hat{s}}/T$ values.
- At RHIC, the ratio $\sqrt{\hat{s}}/T \sim \Delta p_z/T$ is estimated to lie between 0.1 and 2.
- For the static potential model under small-angle approximation, the polarization is given by $P_q = -\pi \mu_D p / 2E(E+m_q)$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies by Liang and Wang (2004) and others that first showed hadrons can be polarized with respect to the reaction plane in high-energy AA collisions with unpolarized beams. The calculations using HTL gluon propagators improve upon the static potential model and show that the small-angle approximation is not reliable at RHIC energies. The predictions of global hyperon polarization and vector meson spin alignment provide measurable signatures that were being pursued at RHIC, potentially opening a new window to study the properties of quark-gluon plasma. The approach builds on measurements from LEP on $e^+e^- \to Z \to h+X$ that demonstrated quark polarization transfer to final hadrons via hadronization.
