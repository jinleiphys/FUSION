# Transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependences of $'\bm{Mach-like}'$ correlations for central Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV

> The transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependences of partonic {`\it{Mach-like}'} shock wave have been studied by using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) with both partonic and hadronic interactions. The splitting parameter $D$, i.e. half distance between two splitting peaks on away side in di-hadron azimuthal angular ($Δϕ$) correlations, slightly increases with the transverse momentum of associated hadrons ($p^{assoc}_T$), which is consistent with preliminary experimental trend, owing to different interaction-lengths/numbers between wave partons and medium in strong parton cascade. On the other hand, the splitting parameter $D$ as a function of pseudorapidity of associated hadrons ($η^{assoc}$), keeps flat in mid-pseudorapidity region and rapidly drops in high-pseudorapidity region, which is as a result of different violent degrees of jet-medium interactions in the medium that has different energy densities in the longitudinal direction. It is proposed that the research on the properties of {`\it{Mach-like}'} correlation can shed light on the knowledge of both partonic and hadronic interactions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The splitting parameter D (half the distance between the two away-side peaks in di-hadron azimuthal correlations) shows a slight increase with associated hadron transverse momentum and a flat-then-drop behavior with associated hadron pseudorapidity, but these trends are model-dependent and only reproduced when a strong parton cascade is included; purely hadronic rescattering or hydrodynamic approximations give different or no pT dependence.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with string melting to simulate central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, comparing the default version (Lund string fragmentation) with the melting version (parton coalescence). They analyzed di-hadron azimuthal correlations between a high-pT trigger hadron (3-4 GeV/c for pT studies, 2.5-6 GeV/c for eta studies) and lower-pT associated hadrons, applying mixed-event background subtraction with a Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) assumption. The parton interaction cross section in the melting AMPT model was set to 10 mb.

## Key numbers

- Splitting parameter D increases slightly with associated hadron pT from approximately 0.2 rad at pT_assoc ~ 0.5 GeV/c to approximately 0.4 rad at pT_assoc ~ 2.5 GeV/c in the melting AMPT model with hadronic rescattering, consistent with preliminary experimental trend from STAR.
- For the default AMPT model with hadronic rescattering, D values are smaller (approximately 0.1-0.2 rad across the same pT range) and do not match the experimental trend.
- The pseudorapidity dependence of D shows a flat region (D ~ 0.35-0.4 rad) for |eta_assoc| < 2, then drops rapidly to D ~ 0.1 rad at |eta_assoc| ~ 4 in the melting AMPT model.
- Associated away-side yields in the melting AMPT model extend to |eta| > 4, while in the default AMPT model they are confined to |eta| < 4.
- The average transverse momentum of associated particles shows a dip at Delta-phi = pi (the away-jet direction) for all model conditions except the melting AMPT model without parton cascade and hadronic rescattering.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Mach-like correlations in heavy-ion collisions by using the AMPT transport model (Lin et al., 2005) to investigate the pT and pseudorapidity dependence of the splitting parameter, which had not been systematically studied in a dynamical parton cascade framework. The results contrast with predictions from linearized hydrodynamics (Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2005) which found no pT dependence of the peak positions, and with Cherenkov-like gluon radiation models (Koch et al., 2006) which predicted the cone angle narrows with increasing momentum. The findings suggest that the pT and eta dependences of the splitting parameter can serve as a probe to distinguish partonic from hadronic interaction mechanisms and to extract information about the longitudinal energy density distribution of the medium created at RHIC.
