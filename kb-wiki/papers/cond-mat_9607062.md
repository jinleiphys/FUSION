# A Constrained Path Monte Carlo Method for Fermion Ground States

> We describe and discuss a recently proposed quantum Monte Carlo algorithm to compute the ground-state properties of various systems of interacting fermions. In this method, the ground state is projected from an initial wave function by a branching random walk in an over-complete basis of Slater determinants. By constraining the determinants according to a trial wave function $|ψ_T\rangle$, we remove the exponential decay of signal-to-noise ratio characteristic of the sign problem. The method is variational and is exact if $|ψ_T\rangle$ is exact. We illustrate the method by describing in detail its implementation for the two-dimensional one-band Hubbard model. We show results for lattice sizes up to $16\times 16$ and for various electron fillings and interaction strengths. Besides highly accurate estimates of the ground-state energy, we find that the method also yields reliable estimates of other ground-state observables, such as superconducting pairing correlation functions. We conclude by discussing possible extensions of the algorithm.

## Key claim

The Constrained Path Monte Carlo (CPMC) method removes the exponential decay of signal-to-noise ratio characteristic of the fermion sign problem by constraining random walkers to maintain a positive overlap with a trial wave function, yielding an approximate ground-state energy that is an upper bound and becomes exact only if the trial wave function is exact.

## Method

The method projects the ground state from an initial wave function by a branching random walk in an over-complete basis of Slater determinants, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the interacting system into non-interacting systems in fluctuating external auxiliary fields. Importance sampling is introduced via an importance function \(O_T(\phi) = \langle \psi_T | \phi \rangle\), and the constrained path approximation is imposed by requiring \(\langle \psi_T | \phi_k^{(n)} \rangle > 0\) for each random walker at each step, preventing walkers from crossing the trial nodal surface. Expectation values are computed using mixed estimators or a back-propagation scheme for operators that do not commute with the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy upper bound: the method is variational, with \(E_0^c \ge E_0\), and becomes exact if \(|\psi_T\rangle\) is exact.
* System sizes: results are shown for the two-dimensional one-band Hubbard model on lattices up to \(16 \times 16\).
* Imaginary-time step: the Trotter approximation uses a small \(\Delta\tau\), and the effect of finite \(\Delta\tau\) on the constraint is found to be very small for reasonably small steps.
* Signal-to-noise ratio: the constrained path approximation ensures no asymptotic signal-to-noise ratio decay in imaginary time, in contrast to the exponential decay characteristic of the sign problem.

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method of Blankenbecler, Scalapino, and Sugar (1981) and the Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method, combining the Hubbard-Stratonovich formalism of the former with the importance-sampled random walk and fixed-node-like approximation of the latter. The constrained path approximation builds upon the positive projection technique of Fahy and Hamann (1990) but implements the constraint in a random walk in Slater determinant space, making the procedure practical and straightforward. The method enables accurate computation of ground-state properties, including superconducting pairing correlation functions, for lattice models where existing QMC algorithms are often difficult or impossible, and extensions to atoms, molecules, and nuclei are under study.
