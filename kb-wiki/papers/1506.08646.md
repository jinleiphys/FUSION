# Evolution of heavy quark distribution function in quark-gluon plasma: using the Iterative Laplace Transform Method

> The "Iterative Laplace Transform Method" is used to solve the Fokker-Planck equation for finding the time evolution of the heavy quarks distribution functions such as charm and bottom in quark gluon plasma. These solutions will lead us to calculation of nuclear suppression factor RAA. The results have good agreement with available experiment data from the PHENIX collaboration.

## Key claim

The Iterative Laplace Transform Method (ILTM) provides a convergent series solution to the Fokker-Planck equation for heavy quark momentum distributions in quark-gluon plasma, and the resulting nuclear suppression factor R_AA shows good agreement with PHENIX data for p_T below 5 GeV, but only after adding an empirical offset of 0.05 and using a drag coefficient that includes a factor of 1.7 times the radiative contribution.

## Method

The authors solve the Fokker-Planck equation for charm and bottom quark momentum distributions using the Iterative Laplace Transform Method, which combines Laplace transforms with an iterative series expansion. The drag coefficient is derived from the total energy loss (collisional plus radiative) of heavy quarks in a thermalized QGP, using hard thermal loop approximations, and the diffusion coefficient is obtained from the Einstein relation D(p) = M T A(p). The time evolution of the QGP temperature is taken as T(t) = tau_0^(1/3) T_0 / t^(1/3) with initial time tau_0 = 0.33 fm/c and initial temperature T_0 = 0.373 GeV, and the momentum distributions are evolved until the transition temperature T_c = 175 MeV. The nuclear suppression factor R_AA is then computed by convoluting the evolved distributions with heavy quark fragmentation functions and comparing Au-Au to p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time tau_0 = 0.33 fm/c and initial temperature T_0 = 0.373 GeV used in the QGP evolution
- Transition temperature T_c = 175 MeV (0.175 GeV) at which the evolution stops
- Strong coupling constant fixed at alpha_s = 0.3 throughout the calculation
- Charm quark production time scale approximately 0.07 fm/c; bottom quark production time scale approximately 0.02 fm/c
- Heavy quark thermalization time in perturbative QCD estimated as 10-15 fm/c for charm and 25-30 fm/c for bottom
- Best fit to PHENIX data achieved with drag coefficient A_total = A_collision + 1.7 A_radiation, final temperature T_c = 0.16 GeV, and an offset of 0.05 added to R_AA
- Good agreement with experimental data reported for p_T < 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to model heavy quark energy loss in QGP using the Fokker-Planck equation, which has been applied by various authors (e.g., Bjorken for initial perturbative estimates, and others using hard thermal loop approaches). The paper introduces the Iterative Laplace Transform Method as an alternative to finite-difference methods, claiming it handles non-zero boundary conditions where finite-difference methods diverge. The results enable future studies incorporating running coupling constants, heavy quark mass effects, dead cone and LPM effects, QGP viscosity, and different equations of state for the QGP medium.
