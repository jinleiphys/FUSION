# The impact of glasma on heavy quark spectra and correlations

> We investigate the effect of the glasma classical color fields, produced in the very early stage of heavy-ion collisions, on the transport of heavy quarks. The glasma fields evolve according to the classical Yang-Mills equations, while the dynamics of heavy quarks is described by Wong's equations. We numerically solve these equations and compute the transport coefficient $κ$, which is anisotropic and initially very large. Further, we extract observables sensitive to the initial glasma stage. The heavy quark nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is affected by the glasma but the effect is moderate compared to the nPDF contribution. Our main finding is that the glasma has a large impact on the azimuthal correlation between $Q\overline{Q}$ pairs, initially produced back-to-back.

## Key claim

The glasma classical color fields have a moderate effect on the heavy quark nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) compared to the dominant contribution from nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), but the glasma causes a large decorrelation in the azimuthal angle between initially back-to-back heavy quark-antiquark pairs.

## Method

The authors numerically solve classical Yang-Mills equations for the glasma fields and Wong's equations for heavy quark trajectories, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with saturation momentum \(Q_s = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) appropriate for central LHC collisions. They compute the transport coefficient \(\kappa\) from momentum broadening, then evolve realistic heavy quark spectra from the Fixed-Order+Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculation through the glasma stage to extract the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and azimuthal correlations of \(Q\overline{Q}\) pairs.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient \(\kappa\) in glasma is anisotropic with \(\kappa_L > \kappa_T\) at early times, with a large initial peak for both charm and beauty quarks.
- For charm quarks at \(\tau = 0.3\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) with \(Q_s = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\ \mathrm{TeV}\), the glasma alone produces a moderate suppression in \(R_{AA}\) while nPDF effects (EPPS16) dominate the suppression.
- Azimuthal correlation width \(\sigma_{\Delta\phi}\) for charm quarks with initial \(p_T = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in glasma with \(Q_s = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) broadens significantly over proper time, with lower \(p_T\) quarks experiencing more decorrelation than higher \(p_T\) quarks.
- The decorrelation magnitude in glasma is of the same order as that reported during the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase by Nahrgang (2013).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework for heavy quark transport in glasma (Avramescu 2023) to compute observables sensitive to the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and the glasma picture (Lappi 2006) and contributes to a growing line of research studying pre-equilibrium effects on heavy quarks and jets (Andres 2019, Sun 2019, Ipp 2020, Carrington 2021, Boguslavski 2023, Barata 2024, Oliva 2024). The results enable future comparisons with QGP-phase predictions and motivate coupling the glasma stage to kinetic theory for a complete description of heavy quark evolution.
