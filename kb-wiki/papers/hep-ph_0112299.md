# Pion gas viscosity at low temperature and density

> By using Chiral Perturbation Theory and the Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation we compute the viscosity of a pion gas, in the low temperature and low density regime, in terms of the temperature, and the pion fugacity. The viscosity turns out to be proportional to the squared root of the temperature over the pion mass. Next to leading corrections are proportional to the temperature over the pion mass to the 3/2.

## Key claim

Using Chiral Perturbation Theory and the Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation, the authors compute the shear viscosity of a pion gas at low temperature and low density, finding it proportional to the square root of temperature over pion mass; the result applies only when the density is low enough that no Bose-Einstein condensate forms (i.e., the temperature remains above the critical temperature for a given density).

## Method

The authors start from the Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport equation for a dilute, weakly interacting pion gas, using the non-relativistic approximation valid at low temperatures. They linearize the transport equation around local equilibrium, expand the perturbation in a new family of orthogonal polynomials (adapted to Bose-Einstein statistics), and solve for the viscosity coefficient using the lowest-order chiral perturbation theory cross-section for elastic pion-pion scattering. The computation relies only on the pion mass \(M_\pi\) and the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\).

## Key numbers

* The low-energy elastic pion scattering cross-section is \(\sigma = \frac{23}{384} \frac{M_\pi^2}{\pi F_\pi^4}\).
* A classical hard-sphere estimate gives \(\eta = \frac{1920}{368} \frac{F_\pi^4 \pi^{3/2}}{M_\pi} \sqrt{\frac{T}{M_\pi}}\).
* The leading-order viscosity is proportional to \(\sqrt{T/M_\pi}\); next-to-leading corrections are proportional to \((T/M_\pi)^{3/2}\).
* The critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation is \(T_c = \frac{2\pi}{M_\pi} \left( \frac{n}{g \zeta_{3/2}} \right)^{2/3}\), where \(g=3\) is the number of pion species and \(\zeta_{3/2}\) is the Riemann zeta function at \(3/2\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of the pion gas beyond the perfect-fluid approximation used in the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983) for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where viscosity was neglected. It provides a first-principles, model-independent computation of the shear viscosity using chiral symmetry, complementing earlier work on the equation of state and chemical equilibration of the pion gas (Leutwyler, 1994). The results enable the inclusion of dissipative effects in relativistic Navier-Stokes equations for the expanding hadronic fluid at RHIC and LHC energies, and can be used to assess when the ideal fluid approximation breaks down.
