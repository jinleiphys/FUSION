# Deeply inelastic pions in the exclusive reaction $p(e,e'π^+)n$ above the resonance region

> A model for the $p(e,e'π^+)n$ reaction which combines an improved treatment of gauge invariant meson--exchange currents and hard deep--inelastic scattering (DIS) of virtual photons off nucleons is proposed. It is shown that DIS dominates and explains the transverse response at moderate and high photon virtualities $Q^2$ whereas the longitudinal response is dominated by hadronic degrees of freedom and the pion electromagnetic form factor. This leads to a combined description of the longitudinal and transverse components of the cross section in a wide range of photon virtuality $Q^2$ and momentum transfer to the target $t$ and solves the longstanding problem of the observed large transverse cross sections. The latter are shown to be sensitive to the intrinsic transverse momentum distribution of partons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the large transverse cross section in the exclusive reaction \(p(e,e'\pi^+)n\) above the resonance region, which had been a longstanding puzzle, can be explained by deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) of virtual photons off partons followed by hadronization, rather than by purely hadronic mechanisms; the longitudinal cross section remains dominated by hadronic degrees of freedom and the pion electromagnetic form factor.

## Method

The authors combine a gauge-invariant hadron-exchange model (using Reggeized pion-pole and rho-exchange amplitudes with separate pion and nucleon electromagnetic form factors) with a partonic DIS contribution modeled via the Lund string fragmentation model as implemented in Pythia/Jetset. The DIS contribution is computed from the proton structure function \(F_2^p\) and the ratio \(R = \sigma_L^{\rm DIS}/\sigma_T^{\rm DIS}\), and the average intrinsic transverse momentum of partons \(\sqrt{\langle k_t^2\rangle}\) is treated as a free parameter tuned to reproduce the transverse cross section data.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 3.91\) GeV\(^2\), the measured transverse cross section \(\sigma_T\) is about a factor of two larger than the longitudinal cross section \(\sigma_L\).
- At \(Q^2 = 2.15\) GeV\(^2\), \(\sigma_T\) has the same size as \(\sigma_L\).
- The hadron-exchange model alone underestimates \(d\sigma_T/dt\) by a factor of 10 at \(Q^2 = 1.6\) GeV\(^2\) and by a factor of 30 at \(Q^2 = 3.91\) GeV\(^2\).
- The cut-off in the monopole parameterization of the pion form factor is fixed to \(\Lambda_{\gamma\pi\pi}^2 = 0.52\) GeV\(^2\).
- The average intrinsic transverse momentum of partons used for all \(Q^2\) bins is \(\sqrt{\langle k_t^2\rangle} = 1.2\) GeV, close to the default Pythia value of 1 GeV.
- The model describes unseparated cross sections from \(Q^2 \simeq 1\) GeV\(^2\) up to 5 GeV\(^2\) at \(W \simeq 2.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Vanderhaeghen (1997) by allowing separate electromagnetic form factors for the pion and nucleon while maintaining gauge invariance, and it solves the longstanding problem of the large transverse cross section observed in JLab experiments (Horn 2006, 2007; Tadevosyan 2007) that had been underestimated by hadron-exchange models by up to an order of magnitude. The approach enables a reliable extraction of the pion form factor from longitudinal data and suggests that a similar \(\sigma_L/\sigma_T\) puzzle in the reaction \(p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda(\Sigma)\) (Niculescu 1998) may have an analogous solution.
