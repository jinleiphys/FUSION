# Squeeze-out of nuclear matter in peripheral heavy-ion collisions and momentum-dependent effective interactions

> We perform a systematic study of in-plane and out-of-plane proton and neutron flow from nucleus-nucleus collisions within the BUU transport approach employing different parameter sets for the mean-field potentials that can be characterized by the nuclear incompressibility and the stiffness of the momentum-dependent potential. We find that a simultaneous description of the experimental data from the BEVALAC and the SIS on both the nucleon squeeze-out $v_2$ and the in-plane flow $F$ at beam energies $E_{lab} = 0.15 ÷2$ AGeV requires a mean field with strong momentum dependence, i.e. an effective Landau mass $m^* \simeq 0.68 m_0$ at normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, where $m_0=0.938$ GeV is the bare nucleon mass. Some experimental data on the squeeze-out require an even stiffer momentum dependence ($m^* \approx 0.62 m_0$). All systems investigated are found to be compatible with $m^*/m_0 = 0.65 \pm 0.03$.

# Squeeze-out of nuclear matter in peripheral heavy-ion collisions and momentum-dependent effective interactions

## Key claim

A simultaneous description of both nucleon squeeze-out ($v_2$) and in-plane flow ($F$) from heavy-ion collisions at beam energies 0.15–2 AGeV requires a mean field with strong momentum dependence, corresponding to an effective Landau mass $m^*/m_0 = 0.65 \pm 0.03$ at normal nuclear matter density; however, some experimental data on neutron squeeze-out require an even stiffer momentum dependence ($m^* \approx 0.62 m_0$), while other data favor the standard stiffness, indicating that no single parameter set simultaneously describes all available data.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport calculations for nucleus-nucleus collisions, employing different parameter sets for the nuclear mean-field potential characterized by the nuclear incompressibility $K$ and the stiffness of the momentum-dependent interaction (MDI). The mean-field potential followed the form of Welke et al. (1988) with parameters adjusted to satisfy nuclear matter saturation properties and a range of effective masses $0.6 m_0 \leq m^* \leq m_0$. The calculations used four parameter sets (H, HM, SM, and HM1) with varying incompressibility and MDI stiffness, and compared computed elliptic flow $v_2$ and in-plane flow $F$ to experimental data from the BEVALAC and SIS facilities for systems including Au+Au, Nb+Nb, and La+La.

## Key numbers

- Effective Landau mass at normal nuclear density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$: $m^*/m_0 = 0.65 \pm 0.03$ (bare nucleon mass $m_0 = 0.938$ GeV) is the range compatible with all systems investigated.
- The HM1 parameter set gives $m^* = 0.62 m_0$ (about 30% larger MDI stiffness than HM and SM sets).
- Beam energy range studied: $E_{lab} = 0.05$ to 2 AGeV (0.15 to 2 AGeV for the simultaneous description claim).
- Balance energy (where in-plane flow $F$ changes sign) from the SM calculation: $E_{BAL} \simeq 50$ AMeV for free protons, $\simeq 60$ AMeV for all protons.
- Lower transition energy for elliptic flow (where $v_2$ changes sign): $E_{TRA} \simeq 100$ AMeV, in agreement with FOPI data.
- Incompressibility range considered: $K = 200$ to 380 MeV.
- Impact parameter range for Au+Au comparisons: $b = 5$ to 7 fm (centrality selection), with maximum squeeze-out ratio at $b \simeq 7$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Danielewicz (1999), who showed within BUU that elliptic flow of protons at midrapidity is sensitive to the MDI part of the nucleon mean field, finding $m^* \simeq 0.7 m_0$ for Bi+Bi reactions. The present study systematically compares both elliptic and directed flow data from multiple experiments (EOS Collaboration, FOPI Collaboration, MSU-4π Collaboration, FOPI-LAND Collaboration) to constrain the MDI stiffness. The results enable future extraction of the nuclear equation of state by separating the effects of incompressibility $K$ from momentum dependence, though the authors note that in semiperipheral reactions up to 1 AGeV the average density probed is too low to determine $K$ precisely. The work also identifies that the squeeze-out mechanism is primarily a shadowing phenomenon, with the MDI enhancing the effect by producing faster-moving nucleons that are more effectively shadowed by spectators.
