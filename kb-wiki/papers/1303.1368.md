# Total reaction cross section $σ_{\rm{reac}}$ of $α$-induced reactions from elastic scattering: the example $^{140}$Ce($α$,$α$)$^{140}$Ce

> Angular distributions of elastic $^{140}$Ce($α$,$α$)$^{140}$Ce scattering are analyzed in the framework of the optical model from low energies around the Coulomb barrier up to about 40\,MeV. From the local fits the total reaction cross section $σ_{\rm{reac}}$ is extracted. This procedure requires experimental scattering data with small uncertainties. The results for $σ_{\rm{reac}}$ are compared to predictions of different systematic global potentials. It is found that the total reaction cross section $σ_{\rm{reac}}$ is well predicted from all global potentials under study although the reproduction of the angular distributions is not perfect in all cases. In addition, the lower energy limit for the extraction of $σ_{\rm{reac}}$ from elastic scattering angular distributions is analyzed. Finally, the potentials under study are used to calculate the $^{143}$Nd(n,$α$)$^{140}$Ce cross section, and the predictions are compared to experimental data.

## Key claim

The total reaction cross section σ_reac for α-induced reactions can be reliably extracted from elastic scattering angular distributions using local optical model fits, and this quasi-experimental quantity is well predicted by several global α-nucleus potentials even when those potentials do not perfectly reproduce the angular distributions themselves; however, at very low energies far below the Coulomb barrier the extraction becomes unreliable because the elastic scattering angular distribution approaches the Rutherford cross section and the uncertainty in σ_reac increases dramatically.

## Method

Angular distributions of elastic 140Ce(α,α)140Ce scattering at laboratory energies from 14.7 MeV to 37.7 MeV were analyzed using the optical model with a double-folding real potential (modified by strength and width parameters) and a Woods-Saxon imaginary potential (surface type at low energies, volume plus surface at higher energies). The total reaction cross section σ_reac was extracted from the local potential fits using the standard partial-wave sum formula, and uncertainties were estimated via a model-independent phase shift analysis. The results were compared to predictions from three global α-nucleus potentials: McFadden and Satchler (MCF), Avrigeanu (AVR), and ATOMKI-V1.

## Key numbers

- At E_lab = 14.69 MeV (E_c.m. = 14.28 MeV): σ_reac = 28 ± 4 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 26 mb (MCF), 23 mb (AVR), and 24 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_lab = 19.0 MeV (E_c.m. = 18.47 MeV): σ_reac = 399 ± 30 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 464 mb (MCF), 477 mb (AVR), and 460 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_lab = 23.97 MeV (E_c.m. = 23.30 MeV): σ_reac = 999 ± 30 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 987 mb (MCF), 1025 mb (AVR), and 989 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_lab = 32.0 MeV (E_c.m. = 31.11 MeV): σ_reac = 1453 ± 44 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 1472 mb (MCF), 1508 mb (AVR), and 1494 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_lab = 37.7 MeV (E_c.m. = 36.65 MeV): σ_reac = 1824 ± 55 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 1676 mb (MCF), 1712 mb (AVR), and 1712 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_lab = 45.0 MeV (E_c.m. = 43.75 MeV, natural cerium target): σ_reac = 1940 ± 97 mb from the local fit, compared to predictions of 1852 mb (MCF), 1883 mb (AVR), and 1903 mb (ATOMKI-V1)
- At E_c.m. = 10.0 MeV (from 143Nd(n,α)140Ce data): σ_reac = 1.9 ± 0.4 μb, compared to predictions of 9.3 μb (MCF), 0.7 μb (AVR), and 4.5 μb (ATOMKI-V1)
- The real volume integral J_R varied between 315.8 MeV·fm^3 (at 14.28 MeV) and 340.7 MeV·fm^3 (at 23.30 MeV)
- At 14.28 MeV, the ratio σ/σ_R at backward angles was about 0.8 (20% deviation from Rutherford)
- A test at E_lab = 14.0 MeV (E_c.m. = 13.61 MeV) with 3% assumed uncertainties gave σ_reac varying between about 5 and 20 mb (factor of four) from virtual data sets, while the starting value from ATOMKI-V1 was 8.8 mb

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of total reaction cross sections for α-induced reactions, following similar analyses for other systems such as 141Pr(α,n)144Pm by Sauerwein (2011) and Mohr (2011). The results confirm that global α-nucleus potentials by McFadden and Satchler (1966), Avrigeanu (2010), and the ATOMKI-V1 potential by Mohr (2012) all provide similar σ_reac values above the Coulomb barrier despite differences in their reproduction of angular distributions. The study enables improved predictions for α-induced reaction cross sections relevant to nuclear astrophysics (particularly the p-process) and identifies the critical need for precise knowledge of the nuclear potential at large radii for reliable low-energy predictions, as demonstrated by the large discrepancies between global potentials at 10 MeV (ranging from 0.7 μb to 9.3 μb).
