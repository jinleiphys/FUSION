# Flatte-like distributions and the a_0(980)/f_0(980) mesons

> We explore the features of Flatte-like parametrizations. In particular, we demonstrate that the large variation in the absolute values of the coupling constants to the pi-eta (or pi-pi) and KKbar channels for the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons that one can find in the literature can be explained by a specific scaling behaviour of the Flatte amplitude for energies near the KKbar threshold. We argue that the ratio of the coupling constants can be much better determined from a fit to experimental data.

# Flatté-like distributions and the a_0(980)/f_0(980) mesons

## Key claim

The large variation in the absolute values of the coupling constants to the πη (or ππ) and KK̅ channels for the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons found in the literature can be explained by a specific scaling behavior of the Flatté amplitude near the KK̅ threshold; however, the ratio of the coupling constants can be much better determined from fits to experimental data, because the near-threshold mass spectrum depends only on the two dimensionless ratios R = ḡ_K/ḡ_P and α = 2E_BW/Γ_P, not on all three Flatté parameters individually.

## Method

The authors analytically examined the nonrelativistic reduction of the Flatté amplitude for elastic scattering (πη or ππ) near the KK̅ threshold, deriving approximate expressions for the cross section that depend only on the ratios R and α. They then demonstrated the scale invariance of these expressions under the transformation E_BW → λ E_BW, Γ_P → λ Γ_P, ḡ_K → λ ḡ_K, and illustrated the effect by comparing published Flatté parameter sets for both the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons, showing that the resulting phase shifts and cross sections are nearly identical despite drastically different absolute parameter values.

## Key numbers

- For the a_0(980) meson, the ratio R = ḡ_K/ḡ_η ranges from 1.03 (Teige) to 3.63 (SNDa), while the nominal mass m_R varies from 984.8 MeV (KLOEa) to 1003 MeV (AchKi).
- For the f_0(980) meson, the ratio R = ḡ_K/ḡ_π ranges from 0.22 (E791) to 10.78 (OPAL), while the nominal mass m_R varies from 957 MeV (OPAL) to 977 MeV (E791).
- The scaling transformation preserves the cross section near threshold within approximately ±25 MeV of the KK̅ threshold for typical f_0 parameters, and within a narrower range when R is small (e.g., for the a_0 with R ≈ 1.16 from Bugg).
- The effective range r_e = −4/(m_K ḡ_K) is always negative in the standard Flatté parametrization; under scaling it transforms as r_e → r_e/λ.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Baru et al. (2004) on "pole counting" for near-threshold resonance classification, following the suggestion by Morgan (1992) that the pole structure distinguishes elementary (quark-antiquark) states from molecular (KK̅ molecule) states. The paper explains why fits to experimental data for the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons yield widely varying absolute coupling constants (as seen in analyses by Teige, Bugg, Amsler, SND, CMD2, KLOE, E791, WA102, OPAL, and Achasov) while the ratio R remains relatively stable. This enables future experimental determinations of the KK̅ scattering length from near-threshold invariant mass spectra, and allows conclusions about the nature of these resonances (molecular vs. elementary) based on the reliably extracted ratio R rather than the poorly determined absolute couplings.
