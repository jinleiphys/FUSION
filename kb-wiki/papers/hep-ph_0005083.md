# Nonperturbative Renormalization and the QCD Vacuum

> We present a self consistent approach to Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian QCD which allows one to relate single gluon spectral properties to the long range behavior of the confining interaction. Nonperturbative renormalization is discussed. The numerical results are in good agreement with phenomenological and lattice forms of the static potential.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent BCS (Bogoliubov-Valatin) approach to Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian QCD that simultaneously determines the gluon spectral function and the confining non-Abelian Coulomb potential, with the caveat that the long-range linear confinement emerges only after fitting the effective potential to lattice data rather than being derived purely from first principles.

## Method

The authors derive a nonperturbative gap equation for the gluon spectral function by applying a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to the pure gauge QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge, regularized with a Gaussian field smearing cutoff. The gap equation contains a kernel that is the expectation value of the non-Abelian Coulomb interaction in the BCS vacuum, making the equation self-consistent: the effective potential depends on the gluon dispersion relation, and the dispersion relation depends on the potential. The authors solve the one-dimensional nonlinear integral equation numerically using a modified Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm, with two subtractions to implement renormalization conditions at two momentum scales.

## Key numbers

* The QCD scale derived from the string tension is \(\mu^2 = 0.2579\) GeV\(^2\), obtained by taking the string tension \(b = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\).
* The ratio \((\mu_0/\mu)^2 \sim 10\) characterizes the interpolation between infrared and ultraviolet limits of the effective potential.
* The ultraviolet running coupling is given by \(\alpha^{UV}_{eff}(q) = 4\pi / [\beta_c \log(q^2 / 0.2579 \text{ GeV}^2)]\).
* The effective gluon mass squared \(m^2(q) = \omega^2(q) - q^2\) has slope \(m' \sim 0.01\) at low momentum, indicating near-constant behavior.
* The approximation for the integral \(I[\omega]\) is accurate to roughly 10% for \(q/\Lambda < 0.1\).
* The perturbative beta function coefficient for the pure gauge sector is \(\beta_c = +4N_c\), with additional contributions \(\beta_g = -1\) and \(\beta_q = -2n_f/3\).

## Context

This work extends previous unrenormalized gluonic gap equation calculations by Szczepaniak, Swanson, and collaborators (1996) that computed the gluon condensate and glueball spectrum, and earlier renormalization studies using similarity or flow equation methods by the same authors (1998). The self-consistent approach contrasts with the perturbative flow equation methodology and aims to derive the confining potential directly from the BCS vacuum Ansatz, rather than inserting a phenomenological linear potential as was done in prior work. The resulting effective potential reproduces the Cornell potential form (Coulomb plus linear) used in lattice QCD phenomenology, and the authors note that the BCS calculation omits transverse gluon contributions that would cancel certain counterterms in full perturbative QCD, as shown in their earlier work (1998).
