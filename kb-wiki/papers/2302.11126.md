# Global QCD Analysis and Dark Photons

> We perform a global QCD analysis of high energy scattering data within the JAM Monte Carlo framework, including a coupling to a dark photon that augments the standard model electroweak coupling via kinetic mixing with the hypercharge $B$ boson. Including the most recent measurement of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon as a constraint, we find a significant reduction in the combined $χ^2$, favoring the inclusion of a dark photon, with a statistical significance in excess of 8$σ$. With respect to the experimental data, the improvements in the theoretical predictions are spread across a wide range of $x$ and $Q^2$, with the largest improvement corresponding to neutral current data from HERA, while the best fit yields a value of $g-2$ which significantly reduces the disagreement with the latest experimental determination. The best fit yields a dark photon mass in the range 4.2--6.2 GeV and a mixing parameter of order 0.1.

## Key claim

A global QCD analysis of high-energy scattering data within the JAM Monte Carlo framework finds that including a dark photon coupling improves the combined fit with a statistical significance of 6.5σ over the Standard Model, even after accounting for missing higher-order uncertainties; the best fit yields a dark photon mass of 4.8 GeV and a mixing parameter of 0.096, which reduces the muon g-2 discrepancy from 4.2σ to 1.5σ.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) using the JAM Monte Carlo framework, fitting parton distribution functions (PDFs) and two dark photon parameters (mass m_A_D and kinetic mixing parameter ε) simultaneously to inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data from fixed-target experiments (SLAC, BCDMS, NMC) and HERA, plus Drell-Yan, Z-boson rapidity, W-boson asymmetry, and jet production data. The analysis used 200 Monte Carlo replicas with data resampling, included higher-twist power corrections and nuclear off-shell corrections for deuterium, and incorporated the muon anomalous magnetic moment (g-2) as an additional constraint. A hypothesis test using the log-likelihood ratio statistic was performed to compare the dark photon model against the Standard Model-only hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- The best fit yields a dark photon mass m_A_D = 4.8^{+1.5}_{-1.2} GeV and mixing parameter ε = 0.096^{+0.028}_{-0.028} at 95% confidence level.
- The dark photon model is preferred over the Standard Model at 6.5σ significance (p-value ~ 3.8 × 10^{-11}).
- The muon g-2 discrepancy is reduced from (251 ± 59) × 10^{-11} (4.2σ) to 91 × 10^{-11} (1.5σ) with the dark photon.
- For m_A_D > 20 GeV, the mixing parameter ε must be less than approximately 0.08 from exclusion limits.
- The total χ² per degree of freedom improves from 1.05 (baseline) to 1.03 (dark photon) across 3283 degrees of freedom.
- Fixed target DIS χ² per degree of freedom improves from 1.05 to 1.01; HERA NC from 1.03 to 1.02; HERA CC from 1.18 to 1.13.
- With a lower Q² cutoff of 10 GeV² instead of 1.69 GeV², the dark photon is still favored at 5σ.
- At ε = 0.05 and m_A_D = 3 GeV, the Standard Model hypothesis is excluded above 5σ.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory studies by Thomas (2021) that used only a subset of HERA and BCDMS data at leading order, and by Kribs (2020) that fixed PDFs from a HERA-only analysis and assumed smaller uncertainties. The present analysis uses the full JAM framework with NLO accuracy, Monte Carlo uncertainty quantification, and simultaneous PDF extraction, finding a much stronger preference for the dark photon than those earlier works. The results suggest that DIS data provide a promising probe of beyond-Standard-Model physics, and the analysis can be improved by moving to NNLO accuracy, implementing different heavy-quark schemes, and performing direct searches in the mass region around 4.2–6.2 GeV.
