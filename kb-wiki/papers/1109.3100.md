# Magnetoelectric Effect in Strongly Magnetized Color Superconductivity

> The effect of a strong magnetic field on the electric polarization of a three-flavor color superconducting medium is investigated. We found that the electric susceptibility of this strongly magnetized medium is highly anisotropic. In the direction transverse to the applied magnetic field the susceptibility reduces to that of the vacuum, while in the longitudinal direction it depends on the magnetic field and decreases with it. The nature of this behavior is associated with the field's dependence of the Cooper pairs' coherence length, which plays the role of the electric dipole length. The field's dependence of the electric polarization is interpreted as the realization of the magnetoelectric effect in cold-dense quark matter.

## Key claim

The electric susceptibility of a three-flavor color superconducting medium in the magnetic-color-flavor-locked (MCFL) phase is highly anisotropic under a strong magnetic field: it vanishes in the transverse direction but becomes finite and magnetic-field-dependent in the longitudinal direction, realizing a magnetoelectric effect in cold-dense quark matter. This behavior arises because the Cooper pairs' coherence length, which acts as the electric dipole length, decreases with increasing magnetic field.

## Method

The authors calculated the zero-zero component of the one-loop polarization operator for the rotated photon in the MCFL phase, working in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation under the strong-field limit (eB >> Δ₀²). They used Ritus's method to transform the quark propagator to momentum space in a uniform magnetic field, then took the static infrared limit (p₀=0, p₃→0) at zero temperature to extract the electric susceptibility from the quadratic term in the free energy expansion in powers of a weak electric field.

## Key numbers

- Parallel electric susceptibility of the MCFL phase: χ∥\_MCFL = (2α̃|ẽB|)/(3πΔ₀²), where α̃ is the fine-structure constant of the rotated electromagnetism and Δ₀ = Δ_M − Δ_B is the effective LLL gap
- The magnetic field corresponding to ẽB/μ² = 1 is 4×10¹⁹ G for μ = 500 MeV
- The parallel dielectric constant is ε∥ = 1 + χ∥\_MCFL, while the transverse dielectric constant is ε⊥ = 1 (vacuum value)
- The MCFL parallel susceptibility is two orders of magnitude larger than that of magnetized nuclear matter, where the nuclear matter susceptibility is χ∥\_NM = α|eB|/(3πm̂_P²) with proton effective mass m̂_P ≈ 732 MeV at saturation density

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity in strong magnetic fields, specifically the MCFL phase previously characterized by Ferrer, de la Incera, and collaborators (2010), which exhibits two antisymmetric gaps Δ and Δ_B plus a spin-one condensate Δ_M. The magnetoelectric effect found here parallels phenomena known in condensed matter multiferroic materials and contrasts with the zero-field CFL phase, where the electric susceptibility is χ_CFL = 2ẽ²μ²/(9π²Δ_CFL²) as calculated by Rischke (2000) and others. The result enables potential astrophysical applications: the anisotropic susceptibility could suppress electric fields aligned with the magnetic field in neutron star cores, leading to anisotropic γ-ray burst emission from stars with color superconducting cores.
