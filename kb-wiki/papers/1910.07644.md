# Reply to the Comment of S. Ayik and D. Lacroix, posted as arXiv:1909.1361v1, on the recent article "Fission Dynamics of 240Pu from Saddle-to-Scission and Beyond" by Bulgac et al, published as Phys. Rev. C 100, 034615 (2019)

> A point-by-point answer to the comment authored by S. Ayik and D. Lacroix is presented. At this point in time this text is not aimed at being submitted to Phys. Rev. C or any other journal, unless the authors of the comment choose to follow such an avenue. I also suggest a possible formulation of a stochastic mean field approach free of the difficulties in the stochastic mean field model due to S. Ayik.

# Reply to Comment on "Fission Dynamics of 240Pu from Saddle-to-Scission and Beyond"

## Key claim

The stochastic mean field (SMF) model of Ayik and Lacroix, as applied to nuclear fission, contains fundamental inconsistencies because it introduces fluctuations only through initial density variations while ignoring that in a closed isolated nucleus there are no ignored bath or environmental degrees of freedom; the model produces unphysical particle-number fluctuations and divergent energy contributions that cannot be remedied by the coarse-graining procedure suggested in the comment.

## Method

The author provides a point-by-point rebuttal of four specific criticisms raised by Ayik and Lacroix (2019) regarding the original Bulgac et al. (2019) paper on fission dynamics of 240Pu. The analysis examines the mathematical structure of the SMF model, including the stochastic single-particle density matrix and its variance, the treatment of particle-number fluctuations, and the behavior of density-dependent energy functionals under stochastic averaging. The author also proposes an alternative stochastic mean field formulation based on random single-particle energies drawn from a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) distribution, with a thermal-like occupation prescription and optional particle-number projection.

## Key numbers

- The lattice constant used in Bulgac et al. (2016, 2019) simulations corresponds to a single-particle momentum cutoff p_c = h-bar*pi/l ≈ 500 MeV/c and a cell volume l^3 ≈ 2 fm^3
- The average volume occupied by a single nucleon in a nucleus is approximately 6 fm^3
- For a simulation box with N_xyz ≈ 27,000 lattice points and spin-isospin degeneracy g=4, the SMF model gives a value of the integral of rho^2(r,r,t) that is orders of magnitude larger than the expected physical value N*(N/V), namely (g*N_xyz - N)*N/(2V)
- Even increasing the lattice constant to 2 fm (reducing N_xyz to approximately 6,700 and achieving a coarse-graining volume of 8 fm^3), the SMF fluctuations of the density-squared term remain enormous
- The quadrupole deformation Q_20 ≈ 160 barn is cited as the configuration where Tanimura et al. (2017) introduced fluctuations, while Q_20 ≈ 100 barn is the deformation where the nucleus emerges from under the barrier

## Context

This reply addresses the comment by Ayik and Lacroix (2019) on the original paper by Bulgac et al. (2019) which presented fully microscopic time-dependent density functional theory simulations of 240Pu fission from saddle to scission and beyond. The author argues that the SMF model of Ayik (2008), as applied by Tanimura et al. (2017), suffers from fundamental problems including unphysical particle-number variance and divergent energy contributions from density-dependent interactions. The proposed alternative stochastic mean field model, using random single-particle energies from GOE statistics and a thermal occupation prescription, aims to avoid these difficulties while maintaining a stochastic approach to nuclear dynamics. The work extends the ongoing debate about how to properly incorporate fluctuations beyond mean-field in nuclear fission calculations.
