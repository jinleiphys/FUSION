# Baryonic Sources of Thermal Photons

> Thermal radiation of photons and dileptons from hadronic matter plays an essential role in understanding electromagnetic emission spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. In particular, baryons and anti-baryons have been found to be strong catalysts for electromagnetic radiation, even at collider energies where the baryon chemical potential is small. Here, we conduct a systematic analysis of $π$- and $ω$-meson-induced reactions off a large set of baryon states. The interactions are based on effective hadronic Lagrangians where the parameters are quantitatively constrained by empirical information from vacuum decay branchings and scattering data, and gauge invariance is maintained by suitable regularization procedures. The thermal emission rates are computed using kinetic theory but can be directly compared to previous calculations using hadronic many-body theory. The comparison to existing calculations in the literature reveals our newly identified contributions to be rather significant.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates thermal photon emission rates from baryon-induced reactions in hot hadronic matter, finding that newly identified contributions from a large set of baryon resonance states are "rather significant" compared to existing calculations, even at collider energies where the baryon chemical potential is small.

## Method

The authors compute thermal photon emission rates using kinetic theory with effective hadronic Lagrangians for π- and ω-meson-induced reactions off a comprehensive set of baryon states (spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances). The interactions are constrained by empirical vacuum decay branchings and scattering data, with gauge invariance maintained through a heavy-pion formalism for vertex formfactors and the vector meson dominance model coupling the ρ meson to the photon. The rates are classified by partial wave (S-wave, P-wave, D-wave) and by processes involving internal or external ω mesons via the πρω vertex.

## Key numbers

* The pseudocritical temperature for the QCD transition is Tpc ≈ 160 MeV, referenced as the temperature near which the medium has cooled after about 5 fm/c in mid-central heavy-ion collisions.
* The effective inverse-slope parameter for direct photons in 0.2 TeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC is Teff ≈ (240 ± 30) MeV, and in 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC is Teff ≈ (300 ± 40) MeV.
* The freezeout temperature for hadronic kinetic decoupling is Tfo ≈ 100 MeV.
* The average radial flow velocity used to relate effective temperature to local emission temperature is βavg ≈ 1/3, yielding Teff ≈ 230 MeV.
* The ω and φ couplings to the photon are smaller than the ργ coupling by factors of approximately 11 and approximately 7, respectively.
* The ρ di-electron decay coupling constant Cρ differs from the strict VDM value e/gρ by approximately 15%.
* The non-relativistic treatment of spin-3/2 particles maintains accuracy for three-momenta up to at least q ≈ 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of thermal photon emissivity from hadronic matter, particularly the in-medium ρ spectral function approach of Rapp (1999) and Urban (1998, 1999), which treated baryon effects through an effective nucleon density approximation. The authors also build on their own prior work on photon rates from a meson gas (Holt, 2015) by incorporating baryonic contributions. The results enable more precise interpretation of direct photon spectra and elliptic flow measurements at RHIC and LHC, where state-of-the-art calculations by van Hees (2014) and Paquet (2015) still fall somewhat short of experimental data at the 1-2 σ level.
