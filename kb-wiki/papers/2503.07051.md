# Quartet correlations near the surface of $ N = Z $ nuclei

> We theoretically investigate Cooper quartet correlations in $ N = Z $ doubly-magic nuclei ($ {}^{40} \mathrm{Ca} $, $ {}^{100} \mathrm{Sn} $, and $ {}^{164} \mathrm{Pb} $). We first examine the quartet condensation fraction in infinite symmetric nuclear matter by using the quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory. Together with the total nucleon density profiles of doubly-magic nuclei obtained from the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation, we discuss the spatial distribution of quartet correlations in finite nuclei within the local density approximation. Large quartet condensate fractions are found at the surface region of an atomic nucleus due to the strong neutron-proton attractive interaction responsible for the deuteron formation in vacuum. Moreover, we discuss a possible microscopic origin of the Wigner term in the context of nucleon-quartet scattering in dilute symmetric nuclear matter. The nucleon-quartet scattering effect on the Wigner term is numerically estimated to be about one order of magnitude of the total empirical strength, indicating the importance of multinucleon clusters in the symmetry energy and mass formula in addition to the neutron-proton pairing.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Cooper quartet correlations are localized near the surface of N=Z doubly-magic nuclei, and that the nucleon-quartet scattering effect can contribute about one order of magnitude of the total empirical strength of the Wigner term, but this estimate relies on the local density approximation and the assumption that the nucleon-alpha interaction captures the relevant physics in the dilute surface region.

## Method

The authors first computed the quartet condensation fraction in infinite symmetric nuclear matter using the quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (QBCS) theory with a finite-range Gaussian interaction fitted to reproduce the alpha particle binding energy. They then performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 energy density functional to obtain nucleon density profiles for 40Ca, 100Sn, and 164Pb, and applied the local density approximation to map the infinite-matter quartet condensation fraction onto spatial distributions in these finite nuclei. Finally, they estimated the Wigner-type energy contribution from nucleon-quartet scattering using a separable neutron-alpha interaction that reproduces the low-energy scattering length a = 2.64 fm and effective range r = 1.43 fm, computing the neutron self-energy via the Beliaev-type Green's function formalism.

## Key numbers

* The alpha particle binding energy used to fit the interaction parameters is E_alpha = 28.29 MeV.
* The interaction parameters are lambda = 807.5 MeV * fm^3 and b = 1.50 fm^{-1}.
* The maximum quartet condensate density in infinite symmetric nuclear matter is estimated as rho_alpha approximately 2.2 x 10^{-4} fm^{-3}, occurring near rho approximately 0.07 fm^{-3}.
* The neutron-alpha scattering length is a = 2.64 fm and the effective range is r = 1.43 fm.
* The nucleon-quartet scattering effect on the Wigner term is numerically estimated to be about one order of magnitude of the total empirical strength.

## Context

This work extends the quartet BCS theory (Guo 2022) from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei by combining it with Skyrme Hartree-Fock densities and the local density approximation. It builds on prior studies of alpha clustering in nuclei (Tanaka 2021) and the quartet condensation model (Sandulescu 2012, Baran 2020), and addresses the long-standing question of the microscopic origin of the Wigner term in nuclear mass formulas (Wigner 1937, Satula 1997, Bentley 2013). The results suggest that multinucleon clusters beyond pairs contribute to the symmetry energy, and the approach enables future studies of nucleon-cluster correlations in medium-mass and heavy nuclei using polaron concepts from condensed matter physics (Nakano 2020, Tajima 2024).
