# The role of massive states in chiral perturbation theory

> I review some basic facts about the chiral limit of QCD. This allows to formulate an effective field theory below the chiral symmetry breaking scale, chiral perturbation theory (CHPT). I show that for threshold reactions, the spectrum of QCD is most economically encoded in a set of coupling constants of operators of higher chiral dimension. A consistent scheme to incorporate the $Δ(1232)$ is also discussed and some examples are given. It is stressed that more precise low--energy data are needed to further test and sharpen the resonance saturation hypothesis in the presence of baryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for threshold reactions, the spectrum of QCD is most economically encoded in the low-energy constants (LECs) of chiral perturbation theory (CHPT), and that massive states like the Δ(1232) need not be treated as dynamical degrees of freedom near threshold but rather serve to estimate the coefficients of higher-dimensional operators. The author stresses that more precise low-energy data are needed to further test and sharpen the resonance saturation hypothesis in the presence of baryons.

## Method

The paper reviews the effective field theory formulation of QCD below the chiral symmetry breaking scale (≈1 GeV), known as chiral perturbation theory (CHPT). It discusses the decoupling theorem of Gasser and Zepeda (1980s), which states that leading non-analytic corrections to S-matrix elements are given solely by the effective Lagrangian of Goldstone bosons and baryons, independent of massive resonance states. The author then examines resonance saturation, where LECs are estimated from meson (ρ, ω) and baryon (Δ, N*) exchange, and presents a systematic "small scale expansion" for including the Δ(1232) as a dynamical degree of freedom, with three small parameters: E_π/Λ, M_π/Λ, and Δ/Λ (where Δ = m_Δ - m_N = 293 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Δ ≡ m_Δ - m_N = 293 MeV ≈ 3 F_π (the NΔ mass splitting)
- g_{NΔπ} ≈ 2 g_{NNπ} (the NΔπ coupling is roughly twice the NNπ coupling)
- L_9 = (7.1 ± 0.3) × 10^{-3} (empirical value); ρ-meson exchange gives L_9 = F_π^2/(2 M_ρ^2) = 7.2 × 10^{-3}
- a_1 + a_2 = 6.60 GeV^{-4} and b_P = 13.0 GeV^{-3} (fitted LECs for γ p → π^0 p); resonance exchange gives a_1 + a_2 = 3.92 + 2.67 = 6.59 GeV^{-4} and b_P^{Δ+V} = 13.0 GeV^{-3}
- Δσ_{πN} = (7.4 + 7.5) MeV ≈ 15 MeV (Cheng-Dashen point scalar form factor from one-loop CHPT with Δ(1232))
- σ_{πN}(0) = 58.3 (1 - 0.56 + 0.33) MeV = 45 MeV (three-flavor chiral expansion to second order in quark masses)
- M_S / √g_S = 180 MeV (scalar meson mass to coupling ratio from resonance saturation, matching the Bonn potential value M_σ = 550 MeV, g_σ^2/(4π) = 7.1)
- κ_s = -0.12 (isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment from resonance saturation) compared to κ_ω = -0.16

## Context

This review extends the resonance saturation program initiated by Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) in the meson sector and by Gasser and Zepeda (1980s) for the decoupling theorem. It builds on the heavy baryon CHPT formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and the small-scale expansion of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1996) for including the Δ(1232). The work enables systematic calculations of threshold observables (e.g., π^0 photoproduction, scalar nucleon form factors) and sets the stage for future calculations of the E2/M1 ratio in the Δ region, while noting unresolved tensions in the determination of dimension-two πN LECs (c_i) between different analyses (e.g., Mojžiš 1996).
