# Baryon-Meson Loop Effects on the Spectrum of Non Strange Baryons

> Corrections to the masses of baryons from baryon-meson loops can induce splittings between baryons which are comparable to those arising from the residual interactions between the quarks. These corrections are calculated using a pair-creation model to give the momentum-dependent vertices, and a model which includes configuration mixing to describe the wave functions of the baryons. A large set of baryon-meson intermediate states are employed, with all allowed SU(3)_f combinations, and excitations of the intermediate baryon states up to and including the second band of negative-parity excited states. Roughly half of the splitting between the nucleon and Delta ground states arises from loop effects. The effects of such loops on the spectrum of negative-parity excited states are examined, and the resulting splittings are sensitive to configuration mixing caused by the residual interactions between the quarks. With reduced-strength one-gluon-exchange interactions between the quarks fit to the Delta-nucleon splitting, a comparison is made between model masses and the bare masses required to fit the masses of the states extracted from data analyses. This shows that it is necessary to also adjust the string tension or the quark mass to fit the splitting between the average bare masses of the ground states and negative-parity excited states, and that spin-orbit effects are likely to be important.

## Key claim

Roughly half of the splitting between the nucleon and Delta ground states arises from baryon-meson loop effects, but this result depends on including a sufficiently complete set of intermediate states (all allowed SU(3)_f combinations and excited baryons up to the N=3 band) and using a modified ^3P_0 pair-creation vertex with a finite spatial extent to avoid overly hard momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors calculate baryon self-energies from baryon-meson loops using time-ordered perturbation theory, with momentum-dependent vertices from a ^3P_0 pair-creation model that includes a Gaussian form factor (f^2 = 2.8 GeV^-2) to give the quark-pair-creation vertex a size of about 0.33 fm. Baryon wave functions are obtained from a relativized quark model with a linear confinement potential and variable-strength one-gluon-exchange interactions, diagonalized in a harmonic oscillator basis up to the N=7 band. The sum over intermediate states includes all allowed SU(3)_f combinations of mesons (π, K, η, η', ρ, ω, K*) and baryons (N, Δ, N*, Δ*, Λ, Σ, Λ*, Σ*) with excitations up to the N=3 band, and the bare masses required to reproduce physical masses are found by solving self-consistent equations.

## Key numbers

- The ^3P_0 pair-creation vertex form factor parameter f^2 = 2.8 GeV^-2 gives the vertex a size of about 0.33 fm.
- The baryon string tension b = 0.15 GeV^2 and factor F = 0.55 are used in the linear confinement potential.
- The contact interaction smearing parameter σ_0 = 0.83 GeV gives up and down quark interactions a smearing σ = 0.9 GeV, corresponding to a constituent quark size of roughly 0.15 fm.
- The critical strong coupling α_s^critical = 0.55 is used when the Coulomb and contact interactions are included in the model Hamiltonian.
- Roughly half of the splitting between the nucleon and Delta ground states arises from loop effects.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of baryon self-energies that used only ground-state baryons as intermediate states (PZ; Silvestre-Brac; BHM; Fujiwara) by including excited baryons up to the N=3 band, following the demonstration by BB that convergence of the Δ-N splitting requires such states. It also improves on the elementary-meson emission models of BB and BHM by using a ^3P_0 pair-creation model with a finite vertex size, resolving the poor convergence found in BB for negative-parity excited states. The results enable a self-consistent adjustment of the residual quark-quark interactions (one-gluon exchange) to account for loop-induced splittings, and suggest that spin-orbit effects from loop contributions may partially cancel those from one-gluon exchange, potentially resolving the spin-orbit problem in baryons.
