# QCD Factorization and Re-scattering in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> The extension of the factorization theorems of perturbative QCD to power corrections associated with re-scattering in nuclear collisions is reviewed. The importance of hadron-nucleus collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The leading-power QCD factorization theorem remains valid for hard probes in nuclear collisions if free-nucleon parton distributions are replaced by universal effective nuclear parton distributions; however, power-suppressed corrections from multiple parton re-scattering can be substantial in large nuclei due to an $A^{1/3}$ enhancement from nuclear size, and these corrections can be factorized to all powers in hadron-nucleus collisions but not in nucleus-nucleus collisions beyond next-to-leading power.

## Method

The paper reviews the extension of collinear QCD factorization theorems to power corrections in proton-nucleus collisions, classifying multiple scattering into three categories: initial-state interactions internal to the nucleus (which modify nuclear parton distributions), initial-state parton-nucleus interactions (ISI), and final-state parton-nucleus interactions (FSI). It derives a factorized formula for the first power-suppressed (twist-4) contribution to hadronic cross sections, expressing it in terms of universal twist-4 correlation functions of two-pairs of parton fields and an infrared-safe partonic hard part, and argues that this factorization extends to all powers for the $A^{1/3}$-enhanced corrections in hadron-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- The coherence length for small-$x$ partons in a nucleus is approximately $0.1/x$ fm, which by far exceeds nuclear size at sufficiently small $x$.
- For inclusive jet production at the LHC ($\sqrt{s}=14$ TeV), the momentum fraction $x$ can be as small as $10^{-6}$ for the most forward or backward jets or low-$p_T$ Drell-Yan dileptons.
- The re-scattering suppression factor relative to single scattering is $\alpha_s(Q)/Q^2$ times an $A^{1/3}$ enhancement from nuclear size.
- The overall suppression factor for double scattering is $\frac{\alpha_s(Q)\, A^{1/3}\, \lambda^2}{Q^2}$, where $\lambda$ is a nonperturbative scale of twist-4 correlation functions.
- From Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening data, $\lambda^2_{\rm ISI} \approx \lambda^2_{\rm DY} \sim 0.01$ GeV$^2$.
- The off-shellness correction from the fragmenting parton is of order $(m_J/p_T)^2$, where $m_J = \sum_h \langle N_h\rangle m_h$ is the invariant mass of the jet.

## Context

This review extends the QCD factorization framework of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985) to power-suppressed multiple scattering effects in nuclear collisions, building on earlier work by Qiu and Sterman (1991) on higher-twist factorization. It contrasts with the situation in nucleus-nucleus collisions, where factorization of power corrections beyond next-to-leading power fails, as shown by Doria, Rothe, and Rothe (1979) and Brandt, Frenkel, and Taylor (1982). The results enable systematic pQCD calculations of semi-hard observables in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC, particularly for studying nuclear parton distributions and gluon saturation phenomena.
