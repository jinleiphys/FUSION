# Thermalization of gluons at RHIC: Dependence on initial conditions

> We investigate how thermalization of gluons depends on the initial conditions assumed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The study is based on simulations employing the pQCD inspired parton cascade solving the Boltzmann equation for gluons. We consider independently produced minijets with $p_T > p_0=1.3 \sim 2.0$ GeV and a color glass condensate as possible initial conditions for the freed gluons. It turns out that full kinetic equilibrium is achieved slightly sooner in denser system and its timescale tends to saturate. Compared with the kinetic equilibration we find a stronger dependence of chemical equilibration on the initial conditions.

## Key claim
Kinetic equilibrium is achieved slightly sooner in denser systems and its timescale tends to saturate, while chemical equilibration shows a much stronger dependence on the initial conditions than kinetic equilibration.

## Method
The authors performed pQCD-inspired parton cascade simulations solving the Boltzmann equation for gluons, including multiple $gg \leftrightarrow ggg$ scatterings with matrix elements regularized by a Debye screening mass and a Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal cutoff. They simulated central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV) using two separate initial conditions: independently produced minijets with transverse momentum cutoffs $p_0=1.3-2.0$ GeV, and a color glass condensate distribution with saturation scale $Q_s=1.0$ GeV. The space-time evolution was tracked until a final time of 4 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- Initial gluon number per rapidity at midrapidity for minijets: $dN_g/dy = 181$ at $p_0=2.0$ GeV, increasing to $889$ at $p_0=1.3$ GeV
- Initial transverse energy per rapidity at midrapidity for minijets: $dE_T/dy = 478$ GeV at $p_0=2.0$ GeV, increasing to $1569$ GeV at $p_0=1.3$ GeV
- For color glass condensate with $Q_s=1.0$ GeV: initial $dN_g/dy = 830$ and initial $dE_T/dy = 550$ GeV
- Final transverse energy per rapidity at 4 fm/c for minijets at $p_0=1.4$ GeV: $dE_T/dy = 626$ GeV, matching the experimental value of $620\pm 33$ GeV from STAR (2004)
- For minijets at $p_0=2.0$ GeV: kinetic equilibrium achieved at 2 fm/c, full chemical equilibrium at about 3 fm/c
- For color glass condensate: initial gluon fugacity $\lambda_g(\tau_f)=8$ (oversaturated); full chemical equilibrium follows at approximately 1.5 fm/c
- Cross sections at $p_0=1.4$ GeV: $\sigma_{gg\to gg}$ increases from 0.5 mb at 0.5 fm/c to 2.5 mb at 4 fm/c; $\sigma_{gg\to ggg}$ increases from 0.3 mb to 1.4 mb
- Temperature at 4 fm/c: $T=262$ MeV at $p_0=1.4$ GeV versus $T=215$ MeV at $p_0=2.0$ GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Xu and Greiner, 2005) which showed kinetic equilibrium at 2 fm/c and chemical equilibrium at 3 fm/c for an initially dilute minijet system with $p_0=2.0$ GeV. It addresses the discrepancy where that earlier simulation gave final transverse energy ($270$ GeV) lower by a factor of more than two compared to STAR experimental data ($620\pm 33$ GeV). The study contrasts with the saturation picture of Eskola et al. (2000), who assumed immediate thermalization and obtained $dE_T/dy \approx 660$ GeV, and with Serreau and Schiff (2001), who found that elastic interactions alone cannot drive rapid kinetic equilibration. The results enable future investigations of energy loss of high-energy partons, parametric dependence of thermalization timescales on $Q_s$ and $\alpha_s$, elliptic flow generation, and viscosity in the quark-gluon plasma.
