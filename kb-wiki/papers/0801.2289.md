# Universal Short-Range Repulsion in the Baryon System Originating from the Confinement --Approach in String-Junction Model--

> We show a way to unifiedly understand the origins of the repulsive core of baryon-baryon interaction and the universal repulsion of three-baryon interaction needed to avoid dramatic softening of the equation of state of neutron stars due to hyperon mixing. For this aim we adopt the string-junction model which embodies the essential aspects of the confinement in the baryon system confirmed by recent lattice QCD calculations. Key concept of this study lies in the recognition that baryonic short-range repulsion appears as the latent effect implying the energy necessary for full overlap of baryons, for the confinement to persist at such situation. Numerical results are shown and related problems are discussed.

# Universal Short-Range Repulsion in the Baryon System Originating from the Confinement – Approach in String-Junction Model

## Key claim

The repulsive core of baryon-baryon interaction and a universal short-range three-baryon repulsion both originate from the same confinement mechanism in the string-junction model, appearing as a latent effect requiring excitation energy of approximately 2 GeV for full overlap of baryons. This three-body repulsion is claimed to be universal (independent of flavor and spin) because it arises from color degrees of freedom, and its inclusion prevents the dramatic softening of neutron star equations of state that would otherwise contradict observed neutron star masses.

## Method

The author adopts the string-junction model (SJM), where baryons are described by Y-shaped strings with a central junction and three oriented strings linking to quarks, as confirmed by lattice QCD calculations of Suganuma et al. (2002). For two-baryon systems at full overlap, the SJM predicts formation of an exotic dibaryon state \(D_6^{4}\) containing three junctions and one anti-junction connected by interjunction strings, requiring excitation energy \({\cal E}\sim 2\) GeV above the ordinary two-baryon mass. This approach is extended to three-baryon systems by considering virtual creation of one baryon-antibaryon pair when a third baryon approaches a close pair, yielding a short-range three-body repulsion that is then converted into a density-dependent effective two-body potential for use in neutron star matter calculations.

## Key numbers

- String tension for three-quark system: \(\sigma_{3q} \simeq 0.89\) GeV/fm, approximately equal to the quark-antiquark string tension \(\sigma_{q\bar{q}}\)
- String length from junction to quarks in ground state baryon: \(\ell_s \simeq 0.5\) fm, corresponding to minimum total flux-tube length \(L_{\min} \simeq 1.5\) fm for equilateral quark configuration
- Excitation energy for full overlap of two baryons: \({\cal E} \sim 2\) GeV, representing the mass of the exotic dibaryon \(D_6^{4}\) above \(2m_B\)
- Range of the repulsive core: approximately 0.5 fm, corresponding to the relative distance where two Y-shaped structures begin to touch
- For comparison, the OPEG-A nucleon-nucleon potential uses a Gaussian repulsive core with strength \({\cal V}_C^{0} = 2\) GeV and range \(\eta_C = (0.45-0.5)\) fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous SJM study from 1982 on two-baryon repulsion and addresses the problem raised by Takatsuka et al. (2002) that universal three-baryon repulsion is needed to prevent equation of state softening from hyperon mixing in neutron stars. The approach contrasts with meson-exchange three-body forces such as the two-pion exchange via \(\Delta\)-excitation (Fujita and Miyazawa, 1957), which the author notes does not provide universal repulsion because it does not act on \(\Lambda\) particles due to spin-isospin dependence. The SJM framework builds on the Y-shaped string-junction picture first proposed by Nambu (1973) and developed by Imachi, Otsuki and Toyoda (1975-1978), now supported by lattice QCD calculations of Suganuma et al. (2002). The resulting equation of state is claimed to be similar to the sufficiently stiff equation of state given by Takatsuka et al. (2006), enabling consistency with observed neutron star masses.
