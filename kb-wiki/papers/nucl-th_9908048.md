# Dynamical Test of Constituent Quark Models with $πN$ Reactions

> A dynamical approach is developed to predict the $πN$ scattering amplitudes starting with the constituent quark models. The first step is to apply a variational method to solve the three-quark bound state problem. The resulting wave functions are used to calculate the $N^* \to πN, ηN, πΔ$ vertex functions by assuming that the $π$ and $η$ mesons couple directly to quarks. These vertex functions and the predicted baryon bare masses then define a Hamiltonian for $πN$ reactions. We apply a unitary transformation method to derive from the constructed Hamiltonian a multi-channel and multi-resonance reaction model for predicting the $πN$ scattering amplitudes up to $W = 2$ GeV. With the parameters constrained by the $Δ(1232$) excitation, we have examined the extent to which the $πN$ scattering in $S_{11}$ channel can be described by the constituent quark models based on the one-gluon-exchange or one-meson-exchange mechanisms. It is found that the data seem to favor the spin-spin interaction due to one-meson-exchange and the tensor interaction due to one-gluon-exchange. A phenomenological quark-quark potential has been constructed to reproduce the $S_{11}$ amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether constituent quark models based on one-gluon-exchange or one-meson-exchange mechanisms can describe πN scattering in the S11 channel, finding that the data favor the spin-spin interaction due to one-meson-exchange and the tensor interaction due to one-gluon-exchange, but only after constructing a phenomenological quark-quark potential to reproduce the S11 amplitude.

## Method

The authors first solved the three-quark bound state problem using a variational method with harmonic oscillator basis states, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian up to 11ħω. They then used the resulting baryon wave functions to calculate N* → πN, ηN, πΔ vertex functions by assuming π and η mesons couple directly to quarks via a nonrelativistic reduction of the effective Lagrangian. Finally, they applied a unitary transformation method to derive a multi-channel, multi-resonance reaction model from the constructed Hamiltonian, solving the scattering equations in partial-wave representation for the S11 channel including πN, ηN, and πΔ channels with two N* states.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m_q = 340 MeV, close to the value used for describing nucleon magnetic moments within the simple (0s)^3 configuration
- The model predicts πN scattering amplitudes up to W = 2 GeV
- The non-resonant interaction v_{α,β} can shift the mass of Δ by about 60 MeV and account for as much as about 40% of the M1 strength of the Δ → γN decay
- The perturbative widths of N(1535) → πN, ηN, πΔ predicted by Ref. [caps2] are (14.7 ± 0.5, 14.6 ± 0.4, 1.4 ± 0.3) MeV^(1/2), compared to empirical values (8.0 ± 2.8, 8.1 ± 0.8, 0.) MeV^(1/2) determined in Ref. [manley]
- The basis states for diagonalization include states up to 11ħω

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of πN and γN reactions developed by Sato and Lee (SL model) for the Δ(1232) energy region to multi-channel, multi-resonance cases, focusing on the S11 channel. It addresses the long-standing problem that perturbative calculations of decay widths from constituent quark models (such as those by Capstick and Roberts) do not quantitatively agree with empirical values extracted from amplitude analyses (Manley, Höhler, Cutkosky, Arndt, Batinic, Vrana), because non-resonant contributions are neglected. The approach enables a more direct test of constituent quark models by relating πN scattering amplitudes directly to predicted internal quark wave functions, accounting for unitarity and non-resonant interactions that can shift resonance masses and modify decay strengths.
