# Modification of heavy quark energy loss due to shear flow in hot QCD plasma

> We present the derivation of heavy quark energy loss in a viscous QCD plasma using kinetic theory. Shear flow changes both boson and fermion distribution functions which eventually modify heavy quark energy loss. Due to presence of non-zero flow gradient in the medium all the bath particles here are out of equilibrium. In these types of plasmas we show that without plasma screening effects heavy quark energy loss suffers similar type of infrared divergence as one encounters in non-viscous plasma. The screening effects are incorporated consistently through Hard Thermal Loop resummation perturbation theory in the small-momentum-transfer region to obtain finite leading order result in $η/s$. We also quantify the importance of the result and demonstrate that shear flow has significant effect on the heavy quark energy loss.

# Modification of heavy quark energy loss due to shear flow in hot QCD plasma

## Key claim
Shear flow modifies the distribution functions of bath particles in a viscous QCD plasma, leading to a first-order correction in η/s to the heavy quark energy loss that can be either positive or negative depending on the momentum and temperature regime; the correction increases energy loss at high momentum (p > 5 GeV) and high temperature, but decreases it at low momentum and low temperature (T < 0.15 GeV).

## Method
The heavy quark energy loss is derived using kinetic theory with the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, considering only elastic 2→2 scatterings (Q-q and Q-g). The viscous correction to the bath particle distribution functions is incorporated via a quadratic ansatz for the shear flow contribution, with the distribution function written as f = f⁰ + δf^η where δf^η ∝ (η/s)(k²/3 − k_z²). The calculation separates hard and soft momentum transfer regions using the Braaten-Yuan prescription, with Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed propagators used in the soft sector to handle infrared divergences, yielding leading logarithmic results at first order in η/s.

## Key numbers
- The viscous correction to heavy quark energy loss from Q-q scattering in the longitudinal channel is: −dE/dx|^η_{Qq,l} ≈ (η/s) C₁ [f₁(v_p) − 2f₁(v_p) log|q_max/m_D|], where C₁ = A_q g⁴ T / [(2π)³ τ v_p²] × (−7π⁴/60 + 27 ζ(3)/2) and f₁(v_p) = v_p⁵/5 − v_p³/3
- The viscous correction from Q-q scattering in the transverse channel is: −dE/dx|^η_{Qq,t} ≈ (η/s) C₂ [4/15 f₂(v_p) + f₂(v_p) log|2q_max/(√(π v_p) m_D)|], where C₂ = A_q g⁴ T / [(2π)³ 2τ v_p²] × 4/15 × (−7π⁴/60 + 27 ζ(3)/2) and f₂(v_p) = v_p⁵
- The viscous correction from Q-g scattering (s and u channels) is: −dE/dx|^η_{Qg} ≈ (η/s) C₃ [−11T⁴/18 (18 ζ(3) − 2π⁴/15) − T³ m_Q²/(3E_p) ((π² − 4 ζ(3)) ln|4E_p T/m_Q²| − 0.162225)], where C₃ = A_f g⁴/(32 T³ τ π³ v_p)
- The crossover in the fractional energy loss ξ occurs at p ∼ 5 GeV (for T = 0.225 GeV) and at T ∼ 0.15 GeV (for p = 5 GeV)
- The Debye mass is m_D ∼ gT, and the maximum momentum transfer q_max ∼ √(E_p T)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of heavy quark energy loss in ideal (non-viscous) QCD plasma by Bjorken, Thoma, Svetitsky, Moore, and Peigne (various years) to include shear viscosity effects. It is motivated by the experimental finding that the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and LHC behaves as a nearly ideal fluid with η/s near the Kovtun-Starinets-Son bound of 1/4π. The calculation is consistent with earlier work on viscous corrections to fermionic damping rates by the author and collaborators (2013) and complements numerical studies of drag and diffusion coefficients in viscous plasma by Das (2012) that did not include plasma screening. The results enable more realistic predictions for observables such as the nuclear modification factor and particle spectra in heavy ion collision experiments.
