# Nucleon QCD sum rules in nuclear matter including four-quark condensates

> We calculate the nucleon parameters in nuclear matter using the QCD sum rules approach in Fermi gas approximation. Terms up to 1/q^2 in the operator product expansion (OPE) are taken into account. The higher moments of the nucleon structure functions are included. The complete set of the nucleon expectation values of the four-quark operators is employed. Earlier the lack of information on these values has been the main obstacle for the further development of the approach. We show that the four-quark condensates provide the corrections of the order 20% to the results obtained in the leading orders of the OPE. This is consistent with the assumption about the convergence of the OPE. The nucleon vector self-energy Σ_v and the nucleon effective mass m^* are expressed in terms of the in-medium values of QCD condensates. The numerical results for these parameters at the saturation value of the density agree with those obtained by the methods of nuclear physics.

# Nucleon QCD Sum Rules in Nuclear Matter Including Four-Quark Condensates

## Key claim

The four-quark condensates provide corrections of order 20% to the nucleon parameters obtained in the leading orders of the operator product expansion (OPE), which is consistent with the convergence of the OPE, contrary to earlier expectations that in-medium factorization would cause much larger corrections that would contradict nuclear phenomenology.

## Method

The authors employ QCD sum rules in the Fermi gas approximation for symmetric nuclear matter, including terms up to 1/q² in the OPE (dimension d=6 operators). They compute the complete set of nucleon expectation values of four-quark operators using the perturbative chiral quark model (PCQM), and also include the higher moments of nucleon structure functions from the nonlocality of the vector condensate. The sum rules are solved both in a linearized version (extracting Σ_v and m*-m directly) and a nonlinearized version (solving for Σ_v, λ*²_m, and W²_m simultaneously) at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Four-quark condensate contributions to the in-medium modification of the polarization operator are 4–5 times smaller than the leading OPE terms, corresponding to corrections of order 20% to the leading-order results.
- The nucleon vector self-energy Σ_v is reduced by about 25% from inclusion of four-quark condensates and by about 25% from inclusion of higher moments of structure functions.
- The scalar self-energy (m* − m) receives a contribution from four-quark condensates of the same order as the leading OPE term, but this is almost totally compensated by the higher moments of structure functions, leaving the value very close to the leading-order OPE result.
- At densities of order the saturation value ρ₀, the linearized and nonlinearized versions give Σ_v and (m* − m) values that agree within 25% and 10% accuracy, respectively.
- The vacuum parameters used are λ²₀ = 1.9 GeV⁶, W²₀ = 2.2 GeV², and m = 0.94 GeV, obtained in the Borel window 0.8 GeV² < M² < 1.4 GeV².
- The first moment of the ξ distribution is ξ ≈ −0.3, calculated by QCD sum rules in an earlier work (Belyaev and Kogan, 1984).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to nucleons in nuclear matter pioneered by Drukarev and Levin (1988, 1989) and Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991, 1992), which previously could only include condensates of dimensions d=3 and d=4 due to lack of information on four-quark condensates. The authors resolve the long-standing obstacle noted in earlier work (Cohen et al., 1995; Jin and Nielsen, 1995) where the in-medium factorization approximation for four-quark condensates would have produced results contradicting nuclear phenomenology. The four-quark condensate values are obtained from the perturbative chiral quark model developed by Gutsche et al. (2000), with pion contributions computed using current algebra techniques from Novikov et al. (1984). The results enable consistent QCD sum rule calculations of nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter that agree with conventional nuclear physics methods at saturation density.
