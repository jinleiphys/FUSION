# Radiative jet energy loss in a three-dimensional hydrodynamical medium and high $p_T$ azimuthal asymmetry of $π_0$ suppression at mid and forward rapidity at RHIC

> The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for $π_0$ production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV is calculated, and studied at high transverse momenta $p_T$. The soft thermalized nuclear medium is described within the framework of relativistic ideal three-dimensional hydrodynamics. The energy loss of partonic jets is evaluated in the context of gluon bremsstrahlung in the thermalized partonic matter. We provide a systematic analysis of the azimuthal asymmetry of $π_0$ suppression at high $p_T$ in central and non-central collisions, at mid and forward rapidity. The determination of $R_{AA}$ as a function of $p_T$, at different azimuthal angles, and different rapidities makes for a stringent test of our theoretical understanding of jet energy loss over a variety of in-medium path lengths, temperatures and initial partonic jet energies. This lays the groundwork for a precise tomography of the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for \(\pi_0\) production in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV, calculated using the AMY formalism for radiative energy loss coupled with a 3D hydrodynamical medium, can describe experimental data at midrapidity, but the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.33\) must be adjusted to match central collision data, and the shape of \(R_{AA}\) as a function of \(p_T\) is not uniquely determined by the energy loss mechanism alone, being also sensitive to nuclear shadowing effects in the parton distribution functions.

## Method

The soft thermalized medium was modeled using relativistic ideal 3D hydrodynamics with initial conditions at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c, tuned to reproduce bulk observables. The energy loss of hard partonic jets was calculated using the AMY formalism for gluon bremsstrahlung, solving coupled rate equations for jet momentum distributions that include both emission and absorption of thermal partons. The transition rates were evaluated in the local fluid frame and boosted to the laboratory frame using the 3D hydrodynamical temperature and flow profiles. Final \(\pi_0\) spectra were obtained by fragmenting the evolved jets using the KKP fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant was fixed to \(\alpha_s = 0.33\) to describe central collision data; this value was used for all centralities.
- For midcentral collisions at impact parameter \(b = 7.5\) fm, the ratio of \(R_{AA}\) for out-of-plane to in-plane emission at midrapidity is about \(13\%\).
- The initial hydrodynamical evolution started at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters used were \(R = 6.38\) fm and \(d = 0.535\) fm.
- The \(K\)-factor for the leading-order pQCD jet production cross section was taken as \(2.8\).
- The maximum forward rapidity studied was \(y = 2\).
- The center-of-mass energy was \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) AGeV for Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior jet quenching calculations that used simplified medium descriptions (e.g., Bjorken expansion or parameterized fireballs) by employing a fully 3D hydrodynamical medium from Nonaka (2006) that reproduces bulk observables. It applies the AMY formalism (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2001) for radiative energy loss, complementing studies using the BDMPS formalism (Renk, 2005; Renk, 2006; Bass, 2007) and the higher twist formalism (Majumder) with the same 3D hydrodynamical medium. The systematic analysis of \(R_{AA}\) as a function of azimuthal angle and rapidity provides tomographic information that can distinguish between different energy loss models and constrains nuclear parton distribution functions, with the caveat that uncertainties in nuclear shadowing (EKS98 vs. NPDF04) affect the interpretation of \(R_{AA}\) at the level of several percent at forward rapidity.
