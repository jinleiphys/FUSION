# Jet quenching phenomenology from soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons

> We present the first application of a recently-developed effective theory of jet propagation in matter SCETG to inclusive hadron suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC. SCETG-based splitting kernels allow us to go beyond the traditional energy loss approximation and unify the treatment of vacuum and medium-induced parton showers. In the soft gluon emission limit, we establish a simple analytic relation between the QCD evolution and energy loss approaches to jet quenching. We quantify the uncertainties associated with the implementation of the in-medim modification of hadron production cross sections and show that the coupling between the jet and the medium can be constrained with better than 10% accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first application of SCETG (soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons) to inclusive hadron suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC, claiming that the coupling between the jet and the medium can be constrained with better than 10% accuracy when uncertainties from the choice of method and fit to data are combined.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA for inclusive charged hadron production in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV using DGLAP evolution equations with medium-induced splitting kernels derived from SCETG. They compare results from numerical solutions of the full DGLAP evolution equations against analytic solutions in the soft gluon bremsstrahlung limit and against the traditional energy loss approach, using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism for the latter. The medium properties are related to measured charged particle rapidity density, with position and time dependence obtained from an optical Glauber model and Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

* Coupling between jet and medium g=2.0-2.1 gives good description of ALICE and CMS data for 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
* The coupling g can be constrained with 5% accuracy within either approach individually; transport properties of the medium (scaling as g^4) can be extracted with 20% uncertainty
* At any fixed transverse momentum, the difference in predicted magnitude of jet quenching between the energy loss and evolution approaches can be absorbed in less than 2% change of the coupling g
* For the full DGLAP evolution with complete splitting kernels, the uncertainty bands correspond to g=1.9-2.0
* The difference between small-x and full evolution is only noticeable below p_T=20 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SCETG framework developed by Idilbi and Ovanesyan (2008) and Ovanesyan and Vitev (2011) to phenomenological applications at the LHC, going beyond traditional energy loss calculations by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000) that neglect large-x corrections. The paper establishes for the first time an analytic connection between the QCD evolution approach and the energy loss approach to jet quenching, showing they are equivalent in the soft gluon limit. This enables future work on parton flavor separation techniques to better differentiate between the two approaches, as suggested by Ellis (2010) and Gallicchio (2011).
