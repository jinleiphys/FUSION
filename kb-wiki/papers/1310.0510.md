# Neutral current interactions of low-energy neutrinos in dense neutron matter

> We report the results of a calculation of the response of cold neutron matter to neutral-current interactions with low energy neutrinos, carried out using an effective interaction and effective operators consistently derived within the formalism of Correlated Basis Functions. The neutrino mean free path obtained from the calculated responses turns out to be strongly affected by both short and long range correlations, leading to a sizable increase with respect to the prediction of the Fermi gas model. The consistency between the proposed approach and Landau theory of normal Fermi liquids also has been investigated, using a set of Landau parameters obtained from the matrix elements of the effective interaction.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in cold neutron matter is strongly increased relative to the Fermi gas model due to both short-range and long-range correlations, but the inclusion of collective excitations (via the Correlated Tamm-Dancoff approximation) reduces this mean free path by about 25% compared to calculations that omit such collective modes.

## Method

The authors computed the density and spin-density response functions of cold neutron matter using a Correlated Basis Functions (CBF) formalism, with an effective interaction and effective operators derived from the Argonne \(v_{6}^{\prime}\) two-nucleon potential and the UIX three-nucleon potential, including three-body cluster contributions. The responses were evaluated within the Correlated Hartree-Fock (CHF) approximation, which includes mean-field and short-range correlation effects, and the Correlated Tamm-Dancoff (CTD) approximation, which additionally accounts for collective 1p-1h excitations. The neutrino mean free path was then obtained by integrating the scattering rate over momentum transfer, and the results were compared to predictions from Landau theory of normal Fermi liquids using Landau parameters extracted from the same effective interaction.

## Key numbers

* The density response of pure neutron matter at \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for momentum transfer \(q = 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) shows that the maximum of the CTD response is about 20% lower than the result from Landau theory.
* The inclusion of collective excitations in the CTD calculation reduces the neutrino mean free path by about 25% relative to the CHF approximation at density \(\rho = \rho_0\).
* The FHNC calculation of the density sum rule yields \(S^\rho(0) \simeq 0.005\), a small violation of the expected zero-momentum-transfer limit.

## Context

This work extends the CBF-based approach previously applied to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter by Lovato et al. (2013) and Benhar et al. (2013) to the case of pure neutron matter, which is relevant for neutron star cooling. The results are compared to predictions from Landau theory of normal Fermi liquids as formulated by Iwamoto and Pethick (1982) and by Bacca et al. (2009). The study enables more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in neutron star interiors by quantifying the roles of short-range correlations, tensor forces, and collective excitations in determining the neutrino mean free path.
