# The di-Omega in the extended quark delocalization, color screening model

> The Omega-Omega (SIJ=-6,0,0) dibaryon state is studied with the extended quark-delocalization color-screening model including a pi meson exchange tail, which reproduces the properties of the deuteron quantitatively. We find the mass of the di-Omega to be about 45 MeV lower than the Omega-Omega threshold. The effect of channel coupling due to the tensor force has been calculated and found to be small in this case. We have also studied the effect of other pseudoscalar meson exchanges and sensitivity to the short-range cutoff radius, r_0, for the meson exchanges.

## Key claim

The di-Omega (Omega-Omega, SIJ=-6,0,0) dibaryon is predicted to have a mass about 45 MeV below the Omega-Omega threshold in the extended quark delocalization, color screening model, but the authors caution that the model's systematic uncertainty of order 30 MeV and the fact that the model predicts the Omega mass itself to be 21 MeV below the measured value mean the di-Omega could be either stable or unstable with respect to the strong interaction.

## Method

The authors used the extended quark delocalization, color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including confinement, one-gluon exchange, and pseudoscalar meson exchange (pi, K, eta) with a short-range cutoff. They solved the six-quark system using the resonating group method (RGM) with a generating-coordinate basis, including both single-channel (Omega-Omega S=0, L=0) and coupled-channel (S=0, L=0 plus S=2, L=2) calculations to account for tensor force effects.

## Key numbers

- Di-Omega mass: 3290.3 MeV (r0=0.6 fm, single channel), 3298.2 MeV (r0=0.8 fm), 3303.5 MeV (r0=1.0 fm) with pi meson exchange only
- Di-Omega mass without meson exchange: 3312 MeV (dynamic calculation), 3350 MeV (adiabatic calculation)
- Omega-Omega threshold: 3344.9 MeV (twice the model Omega mass of 1651 MeV)
- Binding energy relative to threshold: 40 to 55 MeV depending on cutoff parameter
- Effect of channel coupling: less than 0.3 MeV difference between single-channel and coupled-channel masses
- Effect of adding K and eta exchange: di-Omega mass changes from 3298.2 MeV to 3300.0 MeV (at r0=0.8 fm)
- Model Omega mass: 1651 MeV, which is 21 MeV less than the measured value
- Model systematic uncertainty estimated at approximately 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier QDCSM calculations (Ping et al., 1998) by including pi meson exchange tails, which allowed the model to reproduce deuteron properties quantitatively rather than qualitatively. The results are consistent with predictions of a strong-interaction-stable di-Omega from the Skyrmion model (Kopeliovich, 1990) and the chiral SU(3) quark model (Zhang et al., 1997). The calculation enables experimental searches for the di-Omega to be designed with the understanding that the state may be either bound or unbound relative to the strong interaction, depending on model uncertainties.
