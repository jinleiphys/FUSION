# Study of nucleon resonances at EBAC@JLab

> We present the dynamical origin of the P11 nucleon resonances resulting from a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) analysis of meson production reactions off a nucleon target, which is conducted at Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) of Jefferson Lab. Two resonance poles are found in the energy region where the Roper resonance P11(1440) was identified. Furthermore, the two resonance poles and the next higher resonance pole corresponding to P11(1710) are found to originate from a single bare state.

# Study of nucleon resonances at EBAC@JLab

## Key claim

The Roper resonance P11(1440) is actually associated with **two** distinct resonance poles (at 1357−i76 MeV and 1364−i105 MeV), and these two poles plus the next higher pole corresponding to P11(1710) all originate from a **single bare state** at 1763 MeV, contradicting the naive one-to-one correspondence between bare states and observed resonance poles.

## Method

A dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) analysis was performed for meson production reactions off a nucleon target, solving coupled-channels equations for partial wave amplitudes of MB → M'B' transitions (with channels πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN). The transition potential includes both energy-independent meson-exchange potentials and contributions from bare N* states. Resonance pole positions were extracted by finding zeros of the determinant of the inverse dressed N* propagator after analytic continuation to the complex energy plane, and the evolution of poles from the bare state was traced by varying coupling strengths y_MB from 0 to 1.

## Key numbers

- Two Roper resonance poles found: pole A at 1357−i76 MeV and pole B at 1364−i105 MeV, with |E_A − E_B| ∼ 30 MeV separation
- Third P11 pole (C) found at 1820−i248 MeV, corresponding to N*(1710)
- The single bare state from which all three poles originate is located at E = 1763 MeV
- Analysis covers the energy region up to W = 2 GeV
- Pole A lies on the πΔ unphysical sheet; pole B lies on the πΔ physical sheet; pole C lies on the ρN unphysical sheet

## Context

This work extends the EBAC-DCC analysis program at Jefferson Lab, which previously fixed hadronic parameters from πN scattering up to W = 2 GeV (jlms07) and electromagnetic parameters from γp → πN and ep → e'πN up to W = 1.6 GeV (jlmss08, jklmss09). The two-pole structure of the Roper resonance has also been reported by several other groups (cmb, said, juelich-npa). The theoretical possibility that multi-channel reactions can generate many resonance poles from a single bare state was pointed out four decades ago by Eden and Taylor (eden), but few such evidences were reported in the past (morgan). This result provides new insights into the spectrum of N* states and demonstrates that the coupling to the πΔ channel is essential for the two-pole structure of the Roper resonance.
