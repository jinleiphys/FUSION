# Status of Strangeness-Flavor Signature of QGP

> Is the new state of matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions the deconfined quark--gluon plasma? We survey the status of several strange hadron observables and discuss how these measurement help understand the dense hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The paper surveys whether the new state of matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions is the deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP), concluding that several strange hadron observables are consistent with QGP formation but that competing effects must be carefully considered before a definitive conclusion can be reached.

## Method

The authors use the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) implemented in the public-domain code SHARE (Statistical HAdronization with REsonances) to analyze yields of strange hadrons measured at SPS and RHIC. They allow for full chemical non-equilibrium (γ_q ≠ 1, γ_s ≠ 1) in fitting particle yields and ratios, including resonance decay contributions, and compare these fits to experimental data on strange baryon enhancement, the K⁺/π⁺ ratio, and the strangeness-to-entropy ratio s/S as functions of centrality and reaction energy.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(\overline\Lambda/\bar p\) exceeds unity at all available reaction energies, rising with decreasing energy; at the top AGS energy (11.6 A GeV for central Au-Au), one \(\overline\Lambda\) or \(\bar p\) is produced in one out of 100 Au-Au collisions.
- The enhancement of \(\Omega+\overline\Omega\) in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c relative to pBe reaches a factor of about 20.
- The K⁺/π⁺ ratio shows a pronounced peak as a function of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\); the solid line from chemical nonequilibrium analysis reproduces the NA49 data.
- The strangeness-to-entropy ratio s/S is predicted to be approximately 0.03 for thermally equilibrated non-interacting QGP at large \(\lambda_q\) (RHIC and LHC), with a 10% reduction for \(\lambda_q \simeq 4\) (modest SPS energies).
- The hadronization temperature from full non-equilibrium fits is T = 140 MeV, rising to T ≈ 155 MeV when light quark chemical equilibrium is forced.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier predictions from the 1980s (Rafelski 1980) that \(\overline\Lambda/\bar p > 1\) would signal QGP hadronization, and updates the analysis presented in their 1996 Acta Physica Polonica review. The results contradict the canonical suppression model of Redlich (2001), which reinterprets strange baryon enhancement as reference data suppression. The findings enable predictions for strangeness production at LHC energies and for the low-energy RHIC run, and show that the gradual growth of strange baryon enhancement with centrality is quantitatively consistent with strangeness yield growth rather than canonical suppression.
