# Coherent Coulomb excitation of relativistic nuclei in aligned crystal targets

> We study coherent Coulomb excitation of ultrarelativistic nuclei passing through the aligned crystal target. We develop multiple scattering theory description of this process which consistently incorporates both the specific resonant properties of particle-crystal interactions and the shadowing effect typical of the diffractive scattering. We emphasise that the effect of quantum mechanical diffraction makes the physics of ultrarelativistic nuclear excitations entirely different from the physics of non-relativistic atomic excitations experimentally studied so far. It is found that at small transverse momenta $q_{\perp}$ the shadowing effect drastically changes the dependence of coherent amplitudes on the crystal thickness $L$, from the widely discussed growth $\propto L$ typical of the Born approximation to the inverse thickness attenuation law. At relatively large $q_{\perp}$ no attenuation effect is found but the coherency condition is shown to put stringent constrain on the growth of the transition rate with growing $L$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that multiple diffractive scattering (shadowing) in aligned crystal targets fundamentally alters the coherent Coulomb excitation of ultrarelativistic nuclei, changing the previously claimed growth of transition probability proportional to crystal thickness squared into an inverse thickness attenuation law at small transverse momenta, contrary to earlier Born-approximation predictions by PROP1, PROP2, FUSINA, and PROP3.

## Method

The author develops a multiple scattering theory in the eikonal approximation, using the impact parameter representation to sum diagrams of multiple Coulomb scattering to all orders in the coupling. The transition amplitude for the electric dipole excitation of \(^{19}\mathrm{F}\) (ground state \(1/2^+\) to excited state \(1/2^-\) at 110 keV) is evaluated by splitting the impact parameter integration into two domains: \(b \sim \mu^{-1}\) to \(a\) (using steepest descent) and \(0 < b < \mu^{-1}\) (using stationary phase), with the lattice structure factor \(S(q_\perp)\) accounting for coherence along the crystal axis.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the \(^{19}\mathrm{F}\) \(1/2^-\) state: \(\Delta E \simeq 110\) keV.
- Measured lifetime of the 110 keV level: \(\tau = (0.853 \pm 0.010) \times 10^{-9}\) sec, yielding dipole moment \(d \simeq 4.3 \times 10^{-8}\) keV\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(W\) crystal at the \(n=3\) resonance condition, the critical number of atoms for which the cross section deviates from \(N^2\) scaling is \(N_3 \simeq 30\); for diamond, \(N_3 \simeq 300\).
- The Lorentz factor for the \(n=3\) resonance in \(W\) is \(\gamma \simeq 10\), with \(\Delta = \lambda/\mu \simeq 1.2\).
- For small transverse momenta \(q_\perp \lesssim \mu \xi^{-1}\), the amplitude scales as \(\propto \log^2 N \, (1/N)^\Delta\); for higher \(q_\perp \gg \mu \xi^{-1}\), the amplitude scales as \(\propto \sqrt{\log N} \, (1/N)^\Delta\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of coherent Coulomb excitation in crystals beyond the Born approximation used by PROP1, PROP2, FUSINA, and PROP3, who predicted a \(P(N) \propto N^2\) growth for crystal thicknesses up to \(10^4-10^5\) lattice spacings. The author shows that the Glauber-Gribov shadowing effect, previously discussed by KALASH in elastic particle-crystal scattering, applies here and leads to attenuation rather than enhancement. The results enable quantitative predictions for future experiments on ultrarelativistic nuclear excitations in aligned crystals, but indicate that the coherent enhancement is much weaker than earlier proposals suggested, with the structure factor constraining the growth of the transition rate at large transverse momenta.
