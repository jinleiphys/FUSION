# Hadronic effects on the X(3872) meson abundance in heavy ion collisions

> We study the hadronic effects on the X(3872) meson abundance in heavy ion collisions. We evaluate the absorption cross sections of the X(3872) meson by pions and rho mesons in the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions, and further investigate the variation in the X(3872) meson abundance during the expansion of the hadronic matter for its two possible quantum number states; $J^P=1^+$ and $2^-$. We show that the absorption cross sections and the time evolution of the X(3872) meson abundance are strongly dependent on the structure and quantum number of the X(3872) meson. We thus suggest that studying the abundance of the X(3872) meson in relativistic heavy ion collisions provides a chance to infer its quantum number as well as its structure.

## Key claim

The absorption cross sections and time evolution of the X(3872) meson abundance in heavy ion collisions depend strongly on whether its quantum number is \(J^P=1^+\) or \(2^-\), but the analysis is restricted to a spin-1 tetra-quark state and a spin-2 pure charmonium state, and the strong-coupling constant for the spin-2 case is highly sensitive to the X(3872) mass within experimental uncertainty.

## Method

The authors evaluate absorption cross sections of the X(3872) meson by pions and rho mesons using a one-meson exchange model with effective Lagrangians, considering processes such as \(X\pi\to \bar{D}^*D^*\), \(X\pi\to \bar{D}D\), and \(X\rho\to \bar{D}^*D\). They then solve a kinetic rate equation incorporating thermal averaging and a temperature profile to compute the time evolution of the X(3872) abundance during the hadronic expansion stage of central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The statistical model yields for the X(3872) at chemical freeze-out (\(T_H=175\) MeV, \(V_H=1908\) fm\(^3\)) are \(2.9\times 10^{-4}\) for spin-1 and \(4.8\times 10^{-4}\) for spin-2.
- The coalescence model yields are \(4.0\times 10^{-5}\) for the spin-1 tetra-quark state and \(1.7\times 10^{-4}\) for the spin-2 two-quark state.
- The strong-coupling constants used are \(g_{X_1D^*D}=3.5\pm 0.7\) GeV for spin-1 and \(g_{X_2D^*D}=189\pm 36\) for spin-2; varying the X(3872) mass within its PDG uncertainty (3871.68 \(\pm\) 0.17 MeV) could reduce \(g_{X_2D^*D}\) by a factor of \(\sqrt{3}\) and the spin-2 cross sections by a factor of 3.
- The absorption cross sections for processes involving the spin-2 X(3872) are 10–40 times larger than those for spin-1 in \(X\rho\to D\bar{D}\) and \(X\rho\to D^*\bar{D}^*\) due to the additional derivative in the interaction Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using heavy ion collisions to infer exotic hadron structure, previously applied to the \(D_{sJ}(2317)\) meson by Chen (2007) and to general exotic hadron yields by Cho (2010, 2011). It also builds on one-meson exchange models used for \(J/\psi\) suppression in hadronic matter by Matinyan (1998), Haglin (1999), Lin (1999), and Oh (2000). The results suggest that measuring the X(3872) abundance in heavy ion collisions could help distinguish between the \(1^+\) and \(2^-\) quantum number assignments, complementing decay-mode analyses from Belle, CDF, D0, and BABAR.
