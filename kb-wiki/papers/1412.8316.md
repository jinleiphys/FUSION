# Fluctuation of strongly interacting matter in Polyakov Nambu Jona-Lasinio model in finite volume

> We have estimated the susceptibilities of conserved charges for 2-flavor strongly interacting matter with varying system sizes, using the Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu Jona-Lasinio model. The susceptibilities for vanishing baryon densities are found to show a scaling with the system volume in the hadronic as well as partonic phase. This scaling breaks down for a temperature range of about 30-50 MeV around the cross-over region. Simultaneous measurements of the various susceptibilities may thus indicate how close to the cross-over region the freeze-out occurs for the fireball created in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The susceptibilities of conserved charges for 2-flavor strongly interacting matter show a scaling with system volume in the hadronic and partonic phases, but this scaling breaks down for a temperature range of about 30-50 MeV around the cross-over region, which may indicate how close to the cross-over region freeze-out occurs in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for 2 flavors, incorporating finite volume effects by introducing a non-zero low momentum cut-off \(p_{min} = \pi/R = \lambda\) where \(R\) is the lateral size of a cubic volume \(V = R^3\). They calculated the thermodynamic potential at zero baryon density for system sizes corresponding to \(R = 2\) fm, \(2.5\) fm, \(4\) fm, and infinite volume, then extracted the second, fourth, and sixth order quark number and isospin number susceptibilities by fitting the potential to an eighth order polynomial in chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures \(T_c\) for the cross-over transition at zero baryon density: 167 MeV for \(R = 2\) fm, 171 MeV for \(R = 2.5\) fm, 183 MeV for \(R = 4\) fm, and 186 MeV for infinite volume.
- The scaling of susceptibilities with system volume breaks down over a temperature range of about 30-50 MeV around the cross-over region.
- Freeze-out volumes from prior experimental estimates range from 50 \(fm^3\) to 250 \(fm^3\) (Graf et al.) and 2000 \(fm^3\) to 3000 \(fm^3\) (CERES collaboration).

## Context

This work extends previous finite-volume studies of thermodynamic properties in the PNJL model by Bhattacharyya et al. (2013), which showed that the critical temperature decreases with decreasing volume and that the critical end point vanishes at \(R = 2\) fm. It provides the first computation of finite-size effects on fluctuations of conserved charges, complementing lattice QCD calculations of susceptibilities (Gottlieb et al., Gavai et al., HOTQCD collaboration) and model studies for infinite systems (Ray et al., Sasaki and Redlich, Roessner et al.). The results enable experimental analysis of fireball volume and freeze-out proximity to the cross-over region in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at LHC energies where baryon density is low.
