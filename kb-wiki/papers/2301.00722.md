# Hyperon-nucleon interaction in chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order

> A hyperon-nucleon potential for the strangeness $S=-1$ sector ($ΛN$, $ΣN$) up to third order in the chiral expansion is presented. SU(3) flavor symmetry is imposed for constructing the interaction, however, the explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking by the physical masses of the pseudoscalar mesons and in the leading-order contact terms is taken into account. A novel regularization scheme is employed which has already been successfully used in studies of the nucleon-nucleon interaction within chiral effective field theory up to high orders. An excellent description of the low-energy $Λp$, $Σ^- p$ and $Σ^+ p$ scattering data is achieved. New data from J-PARC on angular distributions for the $ΣN$ channels are analyzed. Results for the hypertriton and $A=4$ hyper-nuclear separation energies are presented. An uncertainty estimate for the chiral expansion is performed for selected hyperon-nucleon observables.

## Key claim
The authors present a hyperon-nucleon (YN) potential at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in chiral effective field theory that achieves an excellent description of low-energy Λp, Σ−p, and Σ+p scattering data, but only by relaxing SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints on the leading-order S-wave contact terms and by employing a novel semilocal momentum-space regularization scheme.

## Method
The YN potential is constructed up to third order (N2LO) in the chiral expansion using SU(3) chiral effective field theory, with one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges (π, K, η) and contact terms up to two momentum powers. A semilocal momentum-space regularization scheme is applied, where a local regulator is used for pion-exchange contributions and a non-local function for contact terms, with cutoff values of Λ = 500, 550, and 600 MeV. The low-energy constants are fitted to 36 low-energy YN scattering data points (Λp, Σ−p, Σ+p) from experiments by Sechi-Zorn (1969), Alexander (1969), Engelmann (1966), Eisele (1971), and others, with the additional constraint that the hypertriton must be bound.

## Key numbers
- Total χ² of 15.56 for 36 data points at N2LO with cutoff Λ = 550 MeV (compared to 16.29 for the earlier NLO19 potential)
- ΛN singlet scattering length a_s = −2.79 fm and triplet scattering length a_t = −1.58 fm at N2LO with Λ = 550 MeV
- ΣN (I=3/2) singlet scattering length a_s = −4.19 fm and triplet scattering length a_t = 0.44 fm at N2LO with Λ = 550 MeV
- Σ+p singlet scattering length a_s = −3.39 fm and triplet scattering length a_t = 0.48 fm at N2LO with Λ = 550 MeV (including Coulomb interaction)
- Cutoff values considered: Λ = 500, 550, and 600 MeV, with the best results at Λ = 550 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous YN potentials at NLO by Haidenbauer (2013) and Haidenbauer (2019) to N2LO, motivated by new differential cross section data from the J-PARC E40 experiment for Σ+p and Σ−p channels at laboratory momenta from 440 to 850 MeV/c. The novel regularization scheme was previously applied successfully to nucleon-nucleon interactions by Reinert (2018). The results enable improved calculations of light hypernuclei up to A=9 using no-core shell model methods and provide uncertainty estimates for the chiral expansion, while the need for three-baryon forces (ΛNN and ΣNN) is indicated by the underestimation of A=4 hypernuclear separation energies.
