# The sigma and f_0(980) from Ke4 + pi-pi scatterings data

> We systematically reconsider, within an improved "analytic K-matrix model", the extraction of the sigma = f_0(600) and f_0(980) masses, widths and hadronic couplings using new Ke4 = K-->pi-pi e nu_e data on pi-pi phase shift below 390 MeV and different sets of pi-pi--> pi-pi / K-K scatterings data from 400 MeV to 1.4 GeV. Our results are summarized in Tables 1, 2 and 5. In units of MeV, the complex poles are: M_sigma=452(12) - i 260(15) and M_f=981(34) -i 18(11), which are comparable with some recent high-precision determinations and with PDG values. Besides some other results, we find: |g_{sigma K+K-}|/|g_{sigma pi+pi-}|=0.37(6) which confirms a sizeable g_{sigma K+K-} coupling found earlier, and which disfavours a large pi-pi molecule or four-quark component of the sigma, while its broad pi-pi width (relative to the one of the rho-meson) cannot be explained within a \bar qq scenario. The narrow pi-pi width of the f_0(980) and the large value: |g_{f K+K-}|/|g_{f pi+pi-}|=2.59(1.34), excludes its pure (\bar uu+\bar dd) content. A significant gluonium component eventually mixed with \bar qq appears to be necessary for evading the previous difficulties.

## Key claim
Using an improved analytic K-matrix model, the authors extract the complex poles of the sigma and f_0(980) from combined Ke4 and pi-pi scattering data, finding that the sigma's broad width and the f_0(980)'s narrow width and coupling ratio disfavor pure qqbar, four-quark, or molecular interpretations, and instead require a significant gluonium component mixed with qqbar.

## Method
The authors fit an analytic K-matrix model to new NA48/2 Ke4 data on the pi-pi phase shift below 390 MeV combined with pi-pi to pi-pi and pi-pi to K-Kbar scattering data from 400 MeV to 1.4 GeV. The model includes one or two bare resonance poles, shape functions with Adler zeros and left-hand cut poles, and unitarized bubble diagrams; the physical poles and couplings are extracted from the residues of the amplitude in the complex s-plane.

## Key numbers
- Complex pole of sigma: M_sigma = 452(12) - i 260(15) MeV (average from elastic and two-channel fits)
- Complex pole of f_0(980): M_f = 981(34) - i 18(11) MeV
- Coupling ratio for sigma: |g_{sigma K+K-}| / |g_{sigma pi+pi-}| = 0.37(6)
- Coupling ratio for f_0(980): |g_{f K+K-}| / |g_{f pi+pi-}| = 2.59(1.34)
- From elastic pi-pi fit with 1 bare resonance: M_sigma = 456(19) MeV, Gamma_sigma/2 = 265(18) MeV, |g_{sigma pi+pi-}| = 2.72(16)

## Context
This work extends the earlier K-matrix analyses of Mennessier, Narison, and Ochs (2008) and Kaminski, Mennessier, and Narison (2009) by incorporating new NA48/2 Ke4 data and a refined shape function. The results are comparable to high-precision determinations from Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006) and Kaminski, Pelaez, and Yndurain (2008). The coupling ratios disfavor the pure qqbar interpretation of Bramon and Narison (1989) and the four-quark or molecular models of Jaffe (1977) and Isgur and Weinstein (1990), and instead support a significant gluonium component mixed with qqbar, enabling further tests of the scalar glueball interpretation via two-photon and hadronic decays.
