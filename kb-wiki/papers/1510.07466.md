# Detailed Rapidity Dependence of $J/ψ$ Production at LHC

> $J/ψ$ suppression with rapidity is studied with both (3+1) dimensional transport equation and hydrodynamics in details at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions. In central rapidity region of the hot medium, $J/ψ$ nuclear modification factor shows weak rapidity dependence due to the boost invariance of the medium. But in forward rapidity region for entire transverse momentum bin, $J/ψ$ regeneration is strongly suppressed due to the decreasing charm pair cross section. This makes $J/ψ$ $R_{AA}(y)$ decrease with rapidity. The ratio $r_{AA}$ of mean transverse momentum square in AA collisions to that in pp collisions increases strongly in forward rapidity region and is more sensitive to the $J/ψ$ production mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strong decrease of the inclusive $J/\psi$ nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(y)$ with rapidity in forward rapidity region at LHC is caused primarily by the rapid decrease of the regenerated charmonium yield, not by cold nuclear matter effects, but this conclusion depends on the specific parameterization of the charm pair cross section rapidity distribution and the assumption that charm quarks are thermalized at the time of regeneration.

## Method

The author solves a (3+1)-dimensional Boltzmann-type transport equation for charmonium phase space evolution coupled with (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the QGP medium evolution at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The transport equation includes gluon dissociation (with temperature-dependent cross section from geometric scaling of the mean radius square) and regeneration via detailed balance, using thermal charm quark distributions. Cold nuclear matter effects (gluon shadowing via EKS98 and Cronin effect via Gaussian smearing) are included in the initial charmonium distribution, and the feed-down from $\chi_c$ (30%) and $\psi^\prime$ (10%) to prompt $J/\psi$ is accounted for.

## Key numbers

* Charm pair cross section at mid-rapidity: ${d\sigma^{pp}_{c{\bar c}}\over dy}(y=0)=0.85$ mb, fitted to reproduce $R_{AA}^{J/\psi}(N_p)$ in central rapidity region.
* Total charm pair cross section: $\sigma^{\mathrm{total}}_{c{\bar c}}= 4.8^{+4.66}_{-2.76}$ mb from ALICE.
* Cronin effect coefficient: $a_{gN}=0.15\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$ for the $p_T$-broadening parameter.
* Mean transverse momentum square of $J/\psi$ in pp collisions at $y=0$: $\langle p_T^2\rangle_\Psi^{pp}|_{y=0}=10\ \mathrm{(GeV/c)^2}$ at LHC.
* Power-law exponent for $p_T$ distribution: $n=4.0$.
* Feed-down fractions: $\chi_c$ contributes 30% and $\psi^\prime$ contributes 10% to final prompt $J/\psi$.
* Non-prompt $J/\psi$ from B decay contributes about 10% to inclusive yield in entire $p_T$ bin, and about 15% in $p_T>3$ GeV/c bin.
* Regenerated charmonium mean transverse momentum: $\langle p_T^\mathrm{regeneration}(J/\psi)\rangle \sim 2$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous transport model calculations (trans1, trans2, trans3, trans4) that used (2+1)D hydrodynamics with boost-invariant approximation in the longitudinal direction, by employing (3+1)D hydrodynamics to study the detailed rapidity dependence. It contradicts the expectation that shadowing effects (which show strong rapidity dependence in p+Pb collisions as shown by Manceau (2013)) are responsible for the $R_{AA}$ decrease, demonstrating instead that in Pb+Pb collisions shadowing is almost rapidity independent (EPS09 and nDSg models cited from midpt1). The work enables future studies of $J/\psi$ elliptic flow, where the model underestimates CMS data, suggesting new interaction mechanisms between $J/\psi$ and QGP as proposed by Kopeliovich (2014) may be needed.
