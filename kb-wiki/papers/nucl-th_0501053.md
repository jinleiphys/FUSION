# Structure and Reactions with Exotic Nuclei within the INFN-PI32 network

> The INFN (Italian National Institute for Nuclear Physics) has approved a national theoretical network on "Structure and Reactions with Exotic Nuclei". The project involves the INFN branches of Laboratorio Nazionale del Sud, Padova and Pisa. The aim of the project is to start coordinating and to homogenize the research already performed in Italy in this field and to strengthen and improve the Italian contribution on the international scenario. Furthermore it aims at creating a solid theoretical structure to support future experimental facilities at the INFN national laboratories such as SPES at LNL and EXCYT at LNS. A review of present and future activities is presented.

# Structure and Reactions with Exotic Nuclei within the INFN-PI32 network

## Key claim
The INFN-PI32 network aims to coordinate and homogenize existing Italian theoretical research on exotic nuclei, but the paper presents a review of planned activities and ongoing research lines rather than reporting completed, unified results from the collaboration.

## Method
The paper reviews the research programs of three INFN groups (Pisa, Padova, and Laboratorio Nazionale del Sud) that will be coordinated under the new PI32 network. The Pisa group develops semiclassical and quantum mechanical models for peripheral reactions including breakup and transfer, with extensions to proton breakup and unbound nuclei such as 10Li and 13Be. The Padova group studies fusion, pairing correlations in low-density nuclear systems, continuum RPA calculations, and algebraic models for phase transitions. The LNS groups develop microscopic transport models for isospin dynamics in dissipative collisions and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with three-body forces to construct energy density functionals for finite nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The network involves INFN branches of Laboratorio Nazionale del Sud, Padova, and Pisa.
- The SPES project at Laboratorio Nazionale di Legnaro has been approved in the form of a proton driver.
- The EXCYT facility and the MAGNEX large acceptance spectrometer are being completed at Laboratorio Nazionale del Sud.
- The Padova group studies medium-mass N=Z nuclei up to 100Sn.
- The LNS group works in the energy range from the Coulomb barrier (Tandem) to Fermi energies (Superconducting Cyclotron).

## Context
This paper describes the formation of the INFN-PI32 theoretical network to coordinate Italian research on exotic nuclei, which had previously been carried out within pre-existing national projects. The work extends international efforts at facilities such as REX-ISOLDE at CERN, GANIL in France, GSI in Germany, and RIKEN in Japan, and aims to support future Italian facilities including SPES at LNL and EXCYT at LNS. The network members are also participating in NuPECC working groups preparing the physics case for the EURISOL European Radioactive Beam Facility. The research addresses open questions about the isospin dependence of the effective nuclear interaction, modification of shell structure for unstable nuclei, and the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter.
