# Weak quasielastic hyperon production leading to pions in the antineutrino-nucleus reactions

> In this review, we have studied the quasielastic production cross sections and polarization components of $Λ$, $Σ^0$ and $Σ^-$ hyperons induced by the weak charged currents in the antineutrino reactions on the nucleon and the nuclear targets like $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ar and $^{208}$Pb. It is shown that the energy and the $Q^2$ dependence of the cross sections and the various polarization components can be effectively used to determine the axial vector transition form factors in the strangeness sector and test the validity of various symmetry properties of the weak hadronic currents like G-invariance, T-invariance and SU(3) symmetry. In particular, the energy and the $Q^2$ dependence of the polarization components of the hyperons is found to be sensitive enough to determine the presence of the second class current with or without T-invariance. These hyperons decay dominantly into pions giving an additional contribution to the weak pion production induced by the antineutrinos. This contribution is shown to be quantitatively significant as compared to the pion production by the $Δ$ excitation in the nuclear targets in the sub-GeV energy region relevant for the $\barν_μ$ cross section measurements in the oscillation experiments. We have also included a few new results, based on our earlier works, which are in the kinematic region of the present and future (anti)neutrino experiments being done with the accelerator (anti)neutrinos at T2K, MicroBooNE, MiniBooNE, NO$ν$A, MINER$ν$A and DUNE, as well as for the atmospheric (anti)neutrino experiments in this energy region.

## Key claim
This review argues that quasielastic hyperon production (Λ, Σ⁰, Σ⁻) induced by antineutrinos on nuclear targets provides a quantitatively significant additional contribution to pion production in the sub-GeV energy region, comparable to the dominant Δ-resonance channel, despite being Cabibbo-suppressed. The authors show that this contribution becomes important because hyperon production has a lower threshold energy than Δ production and the pions from hyperon decay are less affected by final state interactions in the nuclear medium.

## Method
The authors calculate cross sections and polarization components for weak charged-current quasielastic hyperon production on free nucleons and on nuclear targets (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ar, ²⁰⁸Pb) using SU(3) symmetry to relate the N-Y transition form factors to nucleon electromagnetic form factors and axial form factors. Nuclear medium effects are incorporated using a local Fermi gas model for Fermi motion and Pauli blocking, while hyperon final state interactions (elastic and charge-exchange scattering) are treated with a simple model. The hyperon decay into pions is then combined with Δ-resonance pion production to obtain total antineutrino-induced pion production cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The hyperon production cross section is Cabibbo-suppressed by a factor of sin²θ_c ≈ 0.05 relative to ΔS=0 processes
- The axial dipole mass M_A is taken as 1.026 GeV (world average value)
- The axial charge g_A(0) is taken as 1.2723
- The SU(3) parameter x₁ is taken as 0.364, determined from neutron β-decay and semileptonic hyperon decay data
- The πNΔ coupling constant f_{πNΔ} is taken as 2.12 for numerical calculations
- The induced tensor form factor g₂^{pn}(0) is varied in the range 0-3 (real or imaginary values) to study second class current effects
- The antineutrino energy region considered is E_{ν̄_μ} ≈ 0.5-1.2 GeV, where Δ production is kinematically inhibited at lower energies
- Λ decay branching ratios: Λ → n + π⁰ (35.8%), Λ → p + π⁻ (63.9%)
- Σ⁻ decay branching ratio: Σ⁻ → n + π⁻ (99.85%)

## Context
This review extends earlier theoretical work on hyperon production by Singh (2006), Alam et al. (2013, 2014), Akbar et al. (2016), and Fatima et al. (2018) to provide a comprehensive overview for multiple nuclear targets relevant to current and future experiments. The work is motivated by the need to understand antineutrino cross section systematics for oscillation experiments at T2K, MicroBooNE, MiniBooNE, NOνA, MINERνA, and DUNE, where hyperon decay pions constitute a background to ν̄_μ disappearance and ν̄_e appearance measurements. The authors note that while experimental data on hyperon production exist from CERN (Erriquez 1977, Eichten 1972), BNL (Fanourakis 1980), FNAL (Ammosov 1986), and SKAT (Brunner 1989), the MINERνA collaboration has included hyperon production in its future plans and DUNE is also considering such measurements.
