# Low-momentum interaction in few-nucleon systems

> The effective low-momentum interaction Vlowk is applied to three- and four-nucleon systems. We investigate the 3H, 3He and 4He binding energies for a wide range of the momentum cutoffs. By construction, all low-energy two-body observables are cutoff-independent, and therefore, any cutoff dependence is due to missing three-body or higher-body forces. We argue that for reasonable cutoffs Vlowk is similar to high-order interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. This motivates augmenting Vlowk by corresponding three-nucleon forces. The set of low-momentum twoand three-nucleon forces can be used in calculations of nuclear structure and reactions.

# Low-momentum interaction in few-nucleon systems

## Key claim
The cutoff dependence of the low-momentum interaction Vlowk in three- and four-nucleon binding energies is due to missing three-body or higher-body forces, and for cutoffs Λ ≲ 2 fm⁻¹ the induced three-nucleon forces are of natural size and can be treated perturbatively, but the effects of truncation to low momenta and missing degrees of freedom (such as the Δ) are inseparable at low energies.

## Method
The authors solved Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for ³H, ³He, and ⁴He using the Vlowk interaction derived from CD-Bonn 2000 and Argonne v₁₈ potentials, varying the momentum cutoff Λ over a wide range. They then augmented Vlowk with the leading chiral three-nucleon force (including 2π-exchange, D-term, and E-term) and fitted the dimensionless strength constants c_D and c_E to reproduce the ³H and ⁴He binding energies simultaneously. Expectation values of the kinetic energy, two-nucleon interaction, and each three-nucleon force component were computed to assess the size of three-body contributions.

## Key numbers
- The ³H and ³He binding energies were calculated with an estimated accuracy of 2 keV; the ⁴He binding energy with an accuracy of 50 keV.
- For cutoffs Λ ≲ 2 fm⁻¹, the Vlowk results from CD-Bonn 2000 and Argonne v₁₈ become model-independent and the binding energy curves collapse.
- The difference in ³He and ³H binding energies varies by 60 keV across the cutoff range.
- The bare three-nucleon forces provide about 0.7–1 MeV of binding in conventional models, while the Vlowk binding energies change by 1 MeV over the large cutoff range.
- For Λ = 1.0 fm⁻¹, the fitted c_D = 3.621 and c_E = 5.724; for Λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹, c_D = -1.225 and c_E = -0.405.
- For Λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹, the ⁴He binding energy could only be approximately reproduced at -28.8 MeV (experimental value -28.3 MeV).
- For cutoffs Λ ≲ 2 fm⁻¹, the three-nucleon force contributions are 4–10% of the two-nucleon interaction for ⁴He.

## Context
This work extends the Vlowk renormalization group approach of Bogner et al. (2003) from two-nucleon systems to three- and four-nucleon systems, and connects it to chiral effective field theory as developed by Weinberg (1990), Epelbaum et al. (2002), and Entem and Machleidt (2003). The results contradict the interpretation of Fujii et al. (2004) regarding the size of three-body forces in Vlowk. The study enables the use of a soft, cutoff-dependent two- plus three-nucleon interaction model for many-body calculations of nuclear structure and reactions, with applications to symmetric nuclear matter in preparation.
