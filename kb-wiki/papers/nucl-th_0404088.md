# Kaon and antikaon properties in cold nuclear medium

> We present results of a self-consistent calculation for the kaon and antikaon spectral functions in cold nuclear matter, using as input the kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes of the vacuum. We investigate the effect of in-medium pion dressing on the antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes and antikaon spectral function. We find the influence of pion dressing to be minor on the antikaon spectral function and limited on the hyperon resonances causing only a small additional broadening. An exception is the Σ(1690). At nuclear saturation density an attractive mass shift of about 20 MeV and width of about 130 MeV is obtained. The kaon shows a repulsive mass increase of 36 MeV and a small width of the quasiparticle peak at saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in-medium pion dressing has only a minor effect on the antikaon spectral function and hyperon resonances, contrary to earlier claims by Tolos et al. (2002) of a significant influence; the exception is the Σ(1690) resonance, which shows a 20 MeV downward mass shift and substantial broadening at saturation density.

## Method

The authors solve the in-medium Bethe-Salpeter equation self-consistently for kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, using vacuum scattering amplitudes as input. For antikaons, they include coupled channels (πΛ, πΣ) and incorporate pion dressing by first computing the dressed pion propagator from a relativistic delta-hole model or a self-consistent pion-nucleon calculation, then using that as input for the antikaon self-consistent calculation. The kaon calculation follows the same procedure but with kaon-nucleon vacuum amplitudes and no pion dressing.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³), the antikaon shows an attractive mass shift of about 20 MeV and a width of about 130 MeV.
- At saturation density, the kaon shows a repulsive mass increase of 36 MeV and a small width of the quasiparticle peak.
- For the Σ(1690) resonance at saturation density, pion dressing shifts the mass down by 20 MeV and increases its decay width to about 130 MeV.
- The kaon quasiparticle peak width is less than 5 MeV for momenta below 200 MeV at saturation density, increasing to 15 MeV at 400 MeV momentum.
- The self-consistent kaon mass shift exceeds the vacuum-amplitude-based shift by 11 MeV at saturation density and by 22 MeV at twice saturation density (ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent approach of Lutz (2002) by including the effect of in-medium pion dressing on antikaon properties, which was previously claimed by Tolos et al. (2002) to be significant. The authors find only minor effects from pion dressing, disagreeing with Tolos et al. (2002) and attributing the discrepancy to differences in the pion spectral function and transition amplitudes used. The results are relevant for understanding kaonic atoms (Friedmann, 1994) and strangeness production in heavy-ion reactions (Laue, 1999), as well as for evaluating the possibility of antikaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan, 1986).
