# Effects of orbital occupancies on the neutrinoless beta-beta matrix element of 76Ge

> In this work we use the recently measured neutron occupancies in the 76Ge and 76Se nuclei as a guideline to define the neutron quasiparticle states in the 1p0f0g shell. We define the proton quasiparticles by inspecting the odd-mass nuclei adjacent to 76Ge and 76Se. We insert the resulting quasiparticles in a proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) calculation of the nuclear matrix element of the neutrinoless double beta (0-nu-beta-beta) decay of 76Ge. A realistic model space and effective microscopic two-nucleon interactions are used. We include the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and other relevant corrections at the nucleon level. It is found that the resulting 0-nu-beta-beta matrix element is smaller than in the previous pnQRPA calculations, and closer to the recently reported shell-model results.

# Effects of orbital occupancies on the neutrinoless beta-beta matrix element of 76Ge

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of 76Ge computed with the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) is smaller than in previous pnQRPA calculations when experimentally measured neutron occupancies are used to define the neutron quasiparticle states, bringing the result closer to recently reported shell-model values. The reduction depends on the choice of short-range correlation prescription (Jastrow or UCOM) and the value of the axial-vector coupling constant.

## Method

The authors used experimentally measured neutron vacancies in the 1p-0f5/2-0g9/2 (pfg) subspace from (p,t) reactions on 76Ge and 76Se to define BCS occupation amplitudes for neutrons, replacing the standard Woods-Saxon based single-particle energies. Proton quasiparticle states were determined by inspecting odd-mass nuclei 77As and 77Br using the proton-neutron microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (pnMQPM). The resulting quasiparticles were inserted into a pnQRPA calculation of the 0νββ nuclear matrix element for 76Ge, using a realistic model space (N=3 and N=4 oscillator shells plus 0h11/2) and effective microscopic two-nucleon interactions, with the particle-particle strength parameter gpp fixed by reproducing the measured 2νββ half-life.

## Key numbers

- Experimental neutron vacancies in 76Ge: ν1p1/2+ν1p3/2 = 1.13, ν0f5/2 = 1.44, ν0g9/2 = 3.52 (compared to Woods-Saxon values of 0.357, 0.500, and 5.43 respectively)
- Experimental neutron vacancies in 76Se: ν1p1/2+ν1p3/2 = 1.59, ν0f5/2 = 2.17, ν0g9/2 = 4.20 (compared to Woods-Saxon values of 0.495, 0.618, and 7.06 respectively)
- Total 0νββ matrix element (M(0ν))' with Jastrow correlations: 2.779 for gA=1.25 (gpp=1.12) and 2.279 for gA=1.00 (gpp=1.10)
- Total 0νββ matrix element (M(0ν))' with UCOM correlations: 4.112 for gA=1.25 (gpp=1.12) and 3.231 for gA=1.00 (gpp=1.10)
- Gamow-Teller 1+ contribution reduced by 37% (from 0.712 to 0.448) and 2- contribution reduced by 56% (from 0.899 to 0.388) compared to Kortelainen et al. (2008)
- Single β- decay matrix element from 2-1 state reduced by 58% from the value of Kortelainen et al. (2008)
- Half-life limits: t1/2(0ν) = (5.36-8.04)×10^24 yr / (⟨mν⟩[eV])^2 (Jastrow) and t1/2(0ν) = (2.45-4.00)×10^24 yr / (⟨mν⟩[eV])^2 (UCOM)

## Context

This work extends the pnQRPA calculations of Kortelainen et al. (2008) by incorporating experimental neutron occupancies from Frekers et al. (2007) measured via (p,t) reactions, which differ substantially from Woods-Saxon based BCS occupations. The results are compared with shell-model calculations of Menendez et al. (2008) and with the Tubingen group results of Rodin et al. (2007) and Simkovic et al. (2008). The reduction in the matrix element brings pnQRPA results closer to shell-model values, though the authors note that contributions from outside the pfg subspace and differences in shell-model occupations remain open questions. The work enables more reliable extraction of the effective Majorana neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits on 76Ge decay.
