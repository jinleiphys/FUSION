# Two- versus three-body approach to femtoscopic hadron-deuteron correlations

> The three-body approach to hadron-deuteron correlations is shown to turn into a two-body approach if the three-particle hadron-deuteron wave function factorizes into the deuteron wave-function and the wave function of a hadron motion relative to the deuteron. Then, the hadron-deuteron correlation function is as in the two-body approach only the source radius somewhat changes. For this reason, as we argue, the two-body approach works well for kaon-deuteron correlations but it fails for proton-deuteron ones in case of small sources. Applying the three-body approach generalized to the case where the radius of the hadron source is different from the nucleon source radius, we derive the source radius formula which used in the two-body approach gives the correlation function as in the `factorized' three-body one. The formula is discussed in the context of existing and future experimental data.

## Key claim

The three-body approach to hadron-deuteron correlations reduces to the two-body Koonin-Pratt formula when the three-particle wave function factorizes into the deuteron wave function and the hadron-deuteron relative wave function, but this factorization holds for kaon-deuteron systems even with small sources while it fails for proton-deuteron systems unless the source is sufficiently large. The two-body approach therefore works well for kaon-deuteron correlations but fails for proton-deuteron ones in proton-proton collisions with small sources.

## Method

The author derives the hadron-deuteron correlation function from the three-body formalism, where the deuteron is treated as a neutron-proton bound state formed through final-state interactions, and compares it to the standard two-body Koonin-Pratt formula. Using Gaussian source functions for hadrons and nucleons, the author shows that when the three-particle wave function factorizes, the three-body correlation function takes the same form as the two-body one but with a modified source radius. A formula connecting the effective two-body source radius to the hadron and nucleon source radii is derived and tested against experimental data from ALICE at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Kaon-deuteron correlation function in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: experimental radius R_Kd^exp = 1.35 (+0.04, -0.05) fm
- Nucleon source radius estimated as R_N = 1.43 +/- 0.16 fm (from ALICE 2023)
- Kaon source radius read from ALICE 2021 data: R_K = 1.0 +/- 0.15 fm
- Predicted radius from the factorized three-body formula: R_Kd = 1.4 +/- 0.2 fm, consistent with the experimental value
- For light nuclei correlations, the factorized three-body approach predicts: R_pp = sqrt(2) R_p, R_pd = sqrt(3/2) R_p, R_pHe3 = sqrt(4/3) R_p, R_dd = R_p
- Ratio R_pd / R_dd = sqrt(3/2) ≈ 1.2 from the factorized approach, compared to an observed ratio of about 1.7 in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV (STAR 2024)

## Context

This work extends the three-body approach to hadron-deuteron correlations formulated by Mrowczynski (2019) and generalizes it to the case where the hadron source radius differs from the nucleon source radius, which is particularly important for pion-deuteron correlations. The paper explains why the two-body approach succeeds for kaon-deuteron correlations measured by ALICE (2023) in proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV but fails for proton-deuteron correlations, which require full three-body calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials as performed by Viviani (2023). The derived source radius formula enables experimental tests of the factorization assumption and can be applied to future measurements of kaon-deuteron, pion-deuteron, proton-deuteron, and deuteron-deuteron correlations in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions.
