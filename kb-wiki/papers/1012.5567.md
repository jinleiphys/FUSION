# Unique nature of the lowest Landau level in finite graphene samples with zigzag edges: Dirac electrons with mixed bulk-edge character

> Dirac electrons in finite graphene samples with zigzag edges under high magnetic fields (in the regime of Landau-level formation) are investigated with regard to their bulk-type and edge-type character. We employ tight-binding calculations on finite graphene flakes (with various shapes) to determine the sublattice components of the electron density in conjunction with analytic expressions (via the parabolic cylinder functions) of the relativistic-electron spinors that solve the continuous Dirac-Weyl equation for a semi-infinite graphene plane. Away from the sample edge, the higher Landau levels are found to comprise exclusively electrons of bulk-type character (for both sublattices); near the sample edge, these electrons are described by edge-type states similar to those familiar from the theory of the integer quantum Hall effect for nonrelativistic electrons. In contrast, the lowest (zero) Landau level contains relativistic Dirac electrons of a mixed bulk-edge character without an analog in the nonrelativistic case. It is shown that such mixed bulk-edge states maintain also in the case of a square flake with combined zigzag and armchair edges. Implications for the many-body correlated-electron behavior (relating to the fractional quantum Hall effect) in finite graphene samples are discussed.

## Key claim

The lowest (zero) Landau level in finite graphene samples with zigzag edges contains relativistic Dirac electrons of a mixed bulk-edge character that has no analog in the nonrelativistic case, while higher Landau levels exclusively comprise bulk-type electrons away from the edge and edge-type states near the edge. This mixed character is shown to persist even in square flakes with combined zigzag and armchair edges, contrary to what might be expected from simpler geometries.

## Method

The authors performed tight-binding calculations on finite graphene flakes (triangular with 6397 carbon atoms, hexagonal with 6144 atoms, and square with 2074 atoms) under a high magnetic field corresponding to a dimensionless flux of φ=0.01. They compared these numerical results with analytic solutions of the continuous Dirac-Weyl equation for a semi-infinite graphene plane with zigzag edge termination, using parabolic cylinder functions to describe the relativistic-electron spinors for both the K and K' valleys.

## Key numbers

- Triangular flake: N=6397 carbon atoms; magnetic flux φ=0.01 (dimensionless flux through hexagonal unit cell)
- Hexagonal flake: N=6144 carbon atoms; magnetic flux φ=0.01
- Square flake: N=2074 carbon atoms; magnetic flux φ=0.01
- Hopping parameter t=2.7 eV used in tight-binding Hamiltonian
- LLL dispersive band energies start at E/t ~ 10^(-6) and increase gradually, with specific examples at E/t=0.1699×10^(-7) and E/t=0.1475×10^(-3) for triangular flake
- Double-edge state energy on rising branch between n=0 and n=1 Landau levels: E/t=0.3033 (triangular flake)
- Double-bulk state in n=1 Landau level: E/t ≈ 0.32685 (triangular flake)
- Double-edge state between n=1 and n=2: E/t=0.384558 (triangular flake)
- Double-bulk state in n=2 Landau level: E/t ≈ 0.458268 (triangular flake)
- Double-edge state between n=2 and n=3: E/t=0.501967 (triangular flake)
- Hexagonal flake LLL mixed bulk-edge state: E/t=0.1459×10^(-3)
- Hexagonal flake double-edge state between n=0 and n=1: E/t=0.2576

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Romanovsky, Yannouleas, and Landman, 2010) which first identified mixed bulk-edge character in the lowest Landau level for circular graphene samples with zigzag edges, but used simplified assumptions. The present paper systematically demonstrates that this unique mixed character persists in more realistic flake geometries (triangular, hexagonal, and square), confirming that the effect is robust against shape variations. The findings have implications for understanding the fractional quantum Hall effect in finite graphene samples, particularly the ν=1/3 FQHE state and the insulating behavior observed at the Dirac neutrality point by experimental groups (Andrei, Kim, and collaborators, 2009).
