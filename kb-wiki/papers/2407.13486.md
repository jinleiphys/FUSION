# Identifying the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ using an SU(3) flavor filter

> We propose a novel method to identify the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$. The two poles owe their origin to different quark flavor irreducible representations in the meson-baryon coupled-channel interactions, thus they should be individually manifested in reactions that provide good flavor eigenstate sources. Hadronic decays of charmonia into $\barΛΣπ$ and $\barΛ(1520)Σπ$ are such reactions, and the flavor octet and singlet poles can be approximately singled out in these two decay modes. This SU(3) flavor filter works even considering the flavor symmetry breaking. With the huge charmonium data sets collected, it is therefore promising to solve the long-standing $Λ(1405)$ puzzle employing the proposed flavor filter.

## Key claim

The two poles of the Λ(1405) resonance, which originate from different SU(3) flavor irreducible representations (a singlet and an octet), can be individually identified by selecting specific charmonium decay channels: the octet pole dominates in Y → Λ̅Σπ decays, while the singlet pole dominates in Y → Λ̅(1520)Σπ decays, and this SU(3) flavor filter remains effective even when flavor symmetry breaking is considered.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians for the decays of J/ψ, ψ(2S), and χc0 into Λ̅Σπ and Λ̅(1520)Σπ, with coupling constants fitted to experimental branching ratios. They then incorporated meson-baryon final-state interactions using the chiral unitary approach at leading order (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and at next-to-leading order, using parameters from Jido et al. (2003), Ikeda et al. (2012), and Guo et al. (2023). The πΣ invariant mass distributions were computed for each decay channel to show which pole dominates.

## Key numbers

- Pole positions from the leading-order chiral unitary approach: pole 1 (singlet) at (1390 − i66) MeV and pole 2 (octet) at (1426 − i16) MeV, using subtraction constants aπΣ = −2.00, aK̅N = −1.84, aηΛ = −2.25, and aKΞ = −2.67.
- Pole positions from the NLO scheme of Ikeda et al. (2012): Λ(1380) at (1381 − i81) MeV and Λ(1405) at (1424 − i26) MeV.
- Pole positions from the NLO scheme of Guo et al. (2023) without Born terms: (1415 − i165.7) MeV and (1417.9 − i15.6) MeV.
- Ratio of coupling constants for J/ψ decays: RF̃/D̃ = 0.18 ± 0.03; for ψ(2S) decays: RF̃/D̃ = 0.50 ± 0.06; for χc0 decays: RF̃/D̃ = 0.29 ± 0.17.
- BESIII has collected around 10 billion J/ψ events and 3 billion ψ(2S) events; the future Super Tau-Charm Facility expects 3.4 × 10¹² J/ψ or 6.4 × 10¹¹ ψ(2S) events per year.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel chiral dynamics framework of Jido et al. (2003), which first explained the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) using SU(3) flavor symmetry, and the NLO calculations of Ikeda et al. (2012) and Guo et al. (2023). It proposes a specific experimental strategy to resolve the long-standing Λ(1405) puzzle using existing charmonium data from BESIII, complementing recent lattice QCD studies by the BaSc collaboration (2023) and chiral effective field theory analyses by Xie et al. (2023) and Zhuang et al. (2024). The same flavor filter idea can be applied to other multiple-pole systems, such as the D0*(2300) in bottomonium decays.
