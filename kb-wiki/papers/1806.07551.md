# Parton distributions from nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective theory. I. Splitting functions

> We present a new formulation of pseudoscalar meson loop corrections to nucleon parton distributions within a nonlocal covariant chiral effective field theory, including contributions from SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons. The nonlocal Lagrangian, constrained by requirements of local gauge invariance and Lorentz-invariant ultraviolet regularization, generates additional interactions associated with gauge links. We use these to compute the full set of proton $\to$ meson + baryon splitting functions, which in general contain on-shell and off-shell contributions, in addition to $δ$-function terms at zero momentum, along with nonlocal contributions associated with the finite size of the proton. We illustrate the shapes of the various local and nonlocal functions numerically using a simple example of a dipole regulator.

# Parton distributions from nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective theory. I. Splitting functions

## Key claim

The paper presents a new formulation of pseudoscalar meson loop corrections to nucleon parton distributions using a nonlocal covariant chiral effective field theory that preserves local gauge invariance and Lorentz-invariant ultraviolet regularization; the splitting functions derived contain on-shell and off-shell contributions, delta-function terms at zero momentum, and nonlocal contributions from the finite proton size, with the shapes illustrated numerically using a dipole regulator as a specific example.

## Method

The authors construct a nonlocal chiral SU(3) Lagrangian by introducing gauge links via path-ordered exponentials to preserve local gauge invariance when covariant vertex form factors are used, then expand the gauge link to lowest order in the electromagnetic charge. They compute the full set of proton-to-meson-plus-baryon splitting functions from the nonlocal Lagrangian for all allowed SU(3) octet and decuplet intermediate states, including contributions from rainbow, bubble, tadpole, Kroll-Ruderman diagrams, and additional gauge-link diagrams that arise from the nonlocal interactions.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar decay constant: f = 93 MeV
- Coupling constants D and F appear in the meson–octet baryon interactions, with specific combinations such as (D+F)/2 for the p π⁰ vertex and (D+3F)/√12 for the p Λ K⁺ vertex
- Coupling constant C appears in meson–octet–decuplet interactions, with values such as -C/√6 for the p Δ⁰ π⁺ vertex and C/√2 for the p Δ⁺⁺ π⁻ vertex
- The decuplet off-shell parameter Z appears in the transition tensor operator Θ^{μν} = g^{μν} - (Z + 1/2) γ^μ γ^ν

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of pseudoscalar meson loop corrections to nucleon parton distributions that used local Lagrangians with various regularization schemes (transverse momentum cutoffs, Pauli-Villars, dimensional regularization, or finite-range regulators) by introducing a nonlocal formulation that automatically satisfies Ward identities and charge conservation. The approach builds on the method of Terning (1991) for constructing nonlocal Lagrangians with gauge fields and on earlier applications to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Wang and collaborators. The splitting functions derived here will be used in a follow-up paper to compute nucleon parton distribution functions through convolutions with PDFs in the virtual mesons and baryons.
