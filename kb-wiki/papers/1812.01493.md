# Muon-electron lepton-flavor violating transitions: shell-model calculations of transitions in 27Al

> In this paper we present the results of large-scale shell-model calculations of muon-to-electron lepton-flavor violating transitions for the case of the target nucleus 27Al. We extend the previous shell-model calculations, done in the sd model space, by including also the p orbitals in order to see whether the negative-parity states produce any significant effect in the conversion rate. The analysis of the results shows the dominance of coherent transitions mediated by isovector operators and going by the ground state of the target, with practically null influence of excited positive- or negative-parity states.

## Key claim
Large-scale shell-model calculations of muon-to-electron conversion in 27Al, extending the valence space to include p orbitals, confirm that coherent ground-state transitions mediated by isovector operators dominate the conversion rate, with practically no contribution from excited positive-parity or negative-parity states.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for 27Al using a 4He core and a valence space including the 0p, 0d, and 1s orbitals (p-sd model space), with the psdmod Hamiltonian (a modified version of the Utsuno-Chiba interaction). Wave functions and one-body transition densities were computed using the NuShellX@MSU code, calculating ten states for each spin and parity. The nuclear matrix elements for vector and axial-vector operators were evaluated for three processes: W-exchange, SUSY Z-exchange, and photonic exchange.

## Key numbers
- Coherent matrix element squared M²_coherent = 75.31 (present shell model), compared to 77.40 from Siiskonen (2000) and 68.80 from experiment, at momentum transfer q/(ħc) = 0.534.
- For W-exchange: total M² = 666.20, with ground-state contribution S_V(g.s.) = 664.9 and total S_V(total) = 665.1; axial contributions S_A(g.s.) = 0.0 and S_A(total) = 0.371.
- For SUSY Z-exchange: total M² = 764.20, with S_V(g.s.) = 743.5, S_V(total) = 743.9, S_A(g.s.) = 0.0, S_A(total) = 6.765.
- For photonic exchange: total M² = 69.68, with S_V(g.s.) = 69.66, S_V(total) = 69.68, and S_A = 0.0 for both ground-state and total.
- Neutron form factor F_N = 0.662, proton form factor F_Z = 0.667 (present), compared to F_Z = 0.677 from Siiskonen (2000) and experimental F_N = 0.640, F_Z = 0.638.
- Calculated energies of lowest negative-parity states: 1/2⁻ at 3764 keV (experimental 4055 keV), 3/2⁻ at 5137 keV (experimental 5156 keV), 5/2⁻ at 5433 keV (experimental 5438 keV).
- The m-scheme dimension for 1/2⁺ states is 61,578,146 in the p-sd space, compared to 80,115 in the sd shell alone.

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model calculations by Siiskonen (2000), which used only the sd model space, by including p orbitals to assess the effect of negative-parity states on muon-to-electron conversion in 27Al. The results confirm the dominance of coherent ground-state transitions found in that earlier study, with negligible contributions from excited states. The calculations provide nuclear matrix elements needed for interpreting upcoming experiments such as Comet and Mu2e, which aim for single-event sensitivities of about 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁷.
