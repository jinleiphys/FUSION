# Pygmy Quadrupole Resonance in Skin Nuclei

> The electric quadrupole response is investigated theoretically by HFB and QPM calculations along the Sn isotopic chain with special emphasis on excitations above the first collective state and below the particle threshold. Depending on the asymmetry, additional strength clustering as a group of states similar to the known PDR mode is found. The spectral distributions and electric response functions are discussed. The transition densities of these Pygmy Quadrupole Resonances (PQR) states are closely related to the neutron excess and showing special features being compatible with an oscillation of the neutron skin against the nuclear core. These features may indicate a new nuclear quadrupole mode connected to the skin configuration.

# Pygmy Quadrupole Resonance in Skin Nuclei

## Key claim
The paper claims that a new nuclear quadrupole mode, the Pygmy Quadrupole Resonance (PQR), exists in neutron-rich tin isotopes as a clustering of low-energy 2⁺ states below the particle threshold, but this claim is hedged by the observation that the reliable distinction of pure skin neutron (proton) quadrupole oscillations becomes difficult with increasing collectivity, especially for nuclei with small or moderate neutron excess.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a phenomenological energy-density functional to describe nuclear ground states, then applied quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) theory within the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) to compute excited states. The residual interactions included separable multipole-multipole and spin-multipole interactions in the particle-hole channel and multipole pairing in the particle-particle channel. Calculations were carried out along the Sn isotopic chain from ¹⁰⁴Sn to ¹³⁴Sn, covering excitation energies up to 35 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The first 2⁺ state in ¹⁰⁴Sn is predicted at excitation energy E_x = 1.221 MeV with B(E2; g.s. → 2⁺) = 0.10 e²b².
- In ¹³²Sn, the 1g_{9/2}2d_{5/2} two-quasiparticle proton component contributes about 17% of the first QRPA 2⁺ state vector, compared to 1.5% in ¹³⁰Sn and 0.9% in ¹³⁴Sn.
- Low-energy PQR states are located typically in the energy region E_x ≈ 2–4 MeV for most tin isotopes, with neutron oscillations extending up to R ≈ 10 fm in ¹²⁰Sn and up to R ≈ 20 fm in ¹³⁴Sn.
- The isoscalar quadrupole coupling constant κ^{(2)}_0 varies by less than 5% outside the valley of stability and typically by about 1% between neighboring stable nuclei.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in tin isotopes by Tsoneva and Lenske (2008) and others, investigating whether similar skin-related modes exist for quadrupole excitations. The authors build on earlier theoretical studies of low-energy quadrupole states by VIsac (1992), Warner (1997), and Yoko (2006), as well as their own QRPA calculations in ¹²⁰Sn (NT09). The results suggest a connection between the PQR and the neutron skin thickness, analogous to the PDR case, and identify ¹³⁰Sn and ¹³⁴Sn as promising candidates for experimental verification. The work also relates to the theoretical prediction by Pethick and Ravenhall (1996) connecting nuclear skin thickness to surface tension.
