# Photoproduction of $π^+p$ Pairs on the $^{16}$O Nucleus and Isobar Configurations

> The yield of the $^{16}$O$(γ,π^{+} p)$ reaction has been measured in the region of the excitation of the $Δ(1232)$ isobar at high momentum transfers to the residual nuclear system. The experimental data are interpreted within the model taking into account of isobar configurations in the ground state of the $^{16}$O nucleus. Direct and exchange mechanisms of the production of pions with emission of one and two nucleons, which follow from the structures of the density matrices for these reactions, have been considered. The probability of the production of the $Δ$ isobar in the ground state of the $^{16}$O nucleus has been empirically estimated as $P_ Δ = 0.019 \pm 0.003 \pm 0.003$.

## Key claim

The measured yield of the $^{16}$O$(\gamma,\pi^{+}p)$ reaction in the $\Delta(1232)$ region at high momentum transfer is almost completely due to isobar configurations in the ground state of $^{16}$O, but contrary to the simple picture of direct isobar knockout, the dominant mechanism involves pion production with emission of two nucleons, not one.

## Method

The experiment used a bremsstrahlung beam from the Tomsk synchrotron at 450 MeV electron energy, detecting $\pi^{+}$ mesons (average momentum 181.3 MeV/$c$, at 54$^\circ$) and protons (energy 50–130 MeV, at 75$\pm$19$^\circ$) in coincidence in coplanar geometry. The differential yield of $\pi^{+}p$ pairs was measured as a function of proton kinetic energy in a kinematic region where the average momentum transfer to the residual nucleus is about 300 MeV/$c$. The data were interpreted using a model that includes isobar configurations in the $^{16}$O ground state, with the reaction amplitude decomposed into direct and exchange mechanisms for one- and two-nucleon emission, using density matrices derived from a shell-model description of the residual nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Probability of $\Delta$ isobar in the ground state of $^{16}$O: $P_{\Delta} = 0.019 \pm 0.003 \pm 0.003$ (statistical and systematic errors).
- Theoretical estimate of $P_{\Delta}$ from the same nuclear model: 0.015, consistent with the data at a confidence level of 0.05.
- Background contribution from the Valencia model (single-, double-, triple-nucleon absorption, pion rescattering) is less than 3% for proton energies above 50 MeV.
- The direct and exchange mechanisms for the $^{16}$O$(\gamma,\pi^{+}p)^{15}$C reaction (one-nucleon emission) contribute no more than 10% of the experimental cross section summed over residual nuclear states.
- The dominant reaction channel is $^{16}$O$(\gamma,\pi^{+}pn)^{14}$C (two-nucleon emission), with the mechanism shown in Fig. 3b dominating.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of isobar configurations in $p$-shell nuclei, where only four prior experiments had provided estimates (e.g., hadron-induced reactions on $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O). The authors note that hadron-induced reactions suffer from peripheral interaction and multiple scattering, making photon-induced reactions more promising. The empirical $P_{\Delta}$ value for $^{16}$O (0.019) agrees best with the theoretical estimate of 0.0175 obtained using the Argonne V28 local potential, compared to 0.0143 from the Bonn$_{2000}$ potential and 0.0088 from a quark model. The result enables future studies of non-nucleon degrees of freedom in nuclei using electromagnetic probes, particularly in kinematic regions where background mechanisms are suppressed.
