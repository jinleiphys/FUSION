# Light neutrino contribution: is it all there is to neutrinoless double beta decay?

> We consider perturbative one loop QCD corrections to the light neutrino contribution to neutrinoless double beta decay and find large enhancement to the rate. QCD corrections also generate structures which mimic new physics contributions usually considered. Within some approximations, the net effect seem to almost saturate the experimental limits, and therefore seems to implt that this is all there is to neutrinoless double beta decay.

## Key claim

One-loop perturbative QCD corrections to the standard light-neutrino-exchange contribution to neutrinoless double beta decay produce a large enhancement of the decay rate, and within the approximations used the net effect may almost saturate the experimental limits, suggesting that the light neutrino contribution alone could account for the entire observed signal.

## Method

The author computed one-loop QCD corrections (vertex and box diagrams) to the standard tree-level amplitude for neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by light Majorana neutrino exchange with only left-handed SM gauge interactions. The calculation employed the closure and impulse approximations for the nuclear part, expanded the loop integrals in ratios of small masses (quark, electron) to the nucleon mass, and used a gluon mass to regulate infrared divergences, setting the renormalization scale to the nucleon mass. The resulting amplitudes were decomposed into a correction to the tree-level term, a new right-handed current contribution, and a local box-diagram contribution that requires short-range nuclear matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The claimed half-life from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment for $^{76}\mathrm{Ge}$ is $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}) = 2.23^{+0.44}_{-0.31} \times 10^{25}\,\mathrm{yr}$ at 68% confidence level.
- The combined lower limit from Kamland-Zen and EXO-200 for $^{136}\mathrm{Xe}$ is $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}(^{136}\mathrm{Xe}) > 3.4 \times 10^{25}\,\mathrm{yr}$.
- The GERDA experiment lower limit for $^{76}\mathrm{Ge}$ is $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}) > 2.1 \times 10^{25}\,\mathrm{yr}$, and a combination of all previous limits gives $T^{0\nu}_{1/2}(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}) > 3.0 \times 10^{25}\,\mathrm{yr}$ at 90% confidence level.
- The vertex correction enhances the tree-level amplitude by a factor of approximately 2.
- The net QCD correction enhances the total amplitude by a factor of 2.5 to 3, corresponding to a rate enhancement by a factor of about 5 to 10; a mid-value factor $K=8$ is used for phenomenological estimates.
- For the normal hierarchy, the QCD-corrected rate constrains the lightest neutrino mass to $m_{\mathrm{lightest}} \lesssim 0.03$ eV, compared to $m_{\mathrm{lightest}} \lesssim 0.08$ eV without QCD corrections.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study of QCD renormalization-group effects on the short-range contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay (Mahajan 2013) to the long-range light-neutrino-exchange contribution. It shows that QCD corrections generate a right-handed current admixture that mimics contributions from left-right symmetric theories, and a local box-diagram piece that requires short-range nuclear matrix elements, similar to heavy-neutrino exchange. The results imply that the light neutrino contribution, after QCD corrections, could nearly saturate experimental limits, potentially reducing tension with LHC searches for new physics and motivating new nuclear matrix element calculations beyond the closure and impulse approximations.
