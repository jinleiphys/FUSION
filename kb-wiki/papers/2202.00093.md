# Back and Forth with Akito Arima

## Key claim
This tribute paper reviews several topics in nuclear structure physics that were directly influenced by or closely related to the work of Akito Arima, including nuclear symmetries, magnetic and quadrupole moments, analytic expressions in the single j shell model, and schematic interactions. The paper does not present a single new result but rather surveys the authors' own work that paralleled or built upon Arima's contributions, with the implicit claim that Arima's influence shaped multiple subfields of nuclear theory.

## Method
The authors present a retrospective discussion of selected topics in nuclear structure, drawing on published experimental data and shell model calculations from their own work and that of collaborators. They compare experimental measurements of magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, and isotope shifts with predictions from the single j shell model, first-order perturbation theory (Arima-Horie theory), and second-order perturbation theory. For quadrupole moments of Calcium isotopes, they compare experimental data with large-space shell model calculations using effective charges of 1.5 for protons and 0.5 for neutrons, as well as with the simple analytic formula Q(n) = [(2j+1-2n)/(2j-1)] Q(sp).

## Key numbers
- Free neutron magnetic moment: mu_n = -1.913 nuclear magnetons; free proton magnetic moment: mu_p = +2.793 nuclear magnetons
- For a single j shell of protons with j = l + 1/2, magnetic moments are predicted to be (2.793 + l) nuclear magnetons
- For Calcium isotopes A=41 to 47, measured quadrupole moments lie on a straight line as predicted by the single j shell formula Q = (2j-1-2n)/(2j-1)*Q(s.p.)
- For A=49, measured quadrupole moment Q = -0.04 b; for A=51, Q = +0.04 b, nearly equal and opposite as predicted by the simple shell model
- For the g factor of the 2+ state in 44Ca, the f7/2 model gives about -0.5, while a highly deformed intruder state gives about +0.5; the measured result is close to zero, explained by a 50% admixture
- For 21Mg, the measured magnetic moment yields an isoscalar spin expectation value <sigma> = 1.15(2), which the authors note exceeds the Schmidt limit of 1 for j = l + 1/2

## Context
This paper is a tribute to Akito Arima's influence on nuclear physics, particularly his work on the Arima-Horie theory of magnetic moment quenching (first-order perturbation theory) and his extensive work with Yu-Min Zhao on single j shell properties. The authors discuss their own work extending Arima's ideas, including second-order perturbation theory calculations with Gerry Brown and Harry Mavromatis for cases where first-order vanishes (e.g., 17O, 17F, 41Ca, 41Sc), using the Kallio-Koltveit and Hamada-Johnson interactions. The paper also references the work of the "Israeli group" (de-Shalit, Racah, Talmi, Thieberger, Unna) on binding energy formulas, and the McCullen et al. f7/2 paper on signature selection rules. The discussion of double Gamow-Teller transitions connects to double beta decay calculations, and the isotope shift analysis uses a two-body effective radius operator approach by Zamick and Talmi.
