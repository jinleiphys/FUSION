# Quantification of Chiral Magnetic Effect from Event-by-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Mechanics

> Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is the macroscopic manifestation of the fundamental chiral anomaly in a many-body system of chiral fermions, and emerges as anomalous transport current in hydrodynamic framework. Experimental observation of CME is of great interest and significant efforts have been made to look for its signals in heavy ion collisions. Encouraging evidence of CME-induced charge separation has been reported from both RHIC and LHC, albeit with ambiguity due to potential background contributions. Crucial for addressing such issue, is the need of quantitative predictions for both CME signal and the non-CME background consistently, with sophisticated modeling tool. In this contribution we report a recently developed Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework, which simulates the evolution of fermion currents in QGP on top of the data-validated VISHNU bulk hydro evolution. In particular, this framework has been extended to event-by-event simulations with proper implementation of known flow-driven background contributions. We report quantitative results from such simulations and evaluate the implications for interpretations of current experimental measurements. Finally we give our prediction for the CME signal in upcoming isobaric collisions.

## Key claim

The Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework, when extended to event-by-event simulations with proper implementation of known flow-driven background contributions, produces quantitative predictions for the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) signal that are consistent with STAR measurements from 200AGeV AuAu collisions at RHIC, subject to current theoretical uncertainty in the initial axial charge density controlled by the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) in the range \(1 \sim 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Method

The authors solve fluid dynamical equations for chiral fermion currents (right-handed and left-handed currents for u and d quark flavors) as perturbations on top of a data-validated 2+1D second-order viscous hydrodynamic bulk evolution (VISHNU). The framework incorporates external electromagnetic fields with a parametrized time dependence \(B(\tau) = B_0 / (1 + (\tau / \tau_B)^2)\) with \(\tau_B = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}/c\), and initial axial charge densities estimated from gluonic topological charge fluctuations in the glasma. For event-by-event simulations, fluctuating initial conditions and a hadronic afterburner stage using UrQMD are included.

## Key numbers

- Peak magnetic field values \(B_0\) for each centrality are taken from event-by-event simulations by Bloczynski (2012); the magnetic field lifetime is set to \(\tau_B = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}/c\).
- The initial axial charge density depends on the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) in the range \(1 \sim 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) for RHIC 200AGeV collisions.
- For event-by-event simulations of \(50-60\%\) centrality 200GeV Au-Au collisions, three setups are used: (a) null case; (b) \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\) and \(n_A/s = 0.1\) (corresponding to \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)); (c) \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\) and \(n_A/s = 0.2\) (corresponding to \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)).
- For isobaric collisions, the projected magnetic field differs by approximately \(10\%\) between \(^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}\) and \(^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}\) systems, leading to a predicted CME signal difference of approximately \(20\%\), and an expected relative difference in \(\gamma\)-correlations around \(10\%\).

## Context

This work extends the AVFD framework developed by Jiang (2016) and Shi (2017) to event-by-event simulations, enabling direct comparison with experimental two-particle correlation measurements. The results are compared with STAR measurements of the \(H_{SS/OS}\) correlator from the Beam Energy Scan program (STAR Collaboration, 2014), and the framework is used to make predictions for the upcoming RHIC IsoBar Program proposed by Skokov (2016), which aims to disentangle CME signal from non-CME background by comparing collisions of isobaric nuclei with identical mass number but different electric charge.
