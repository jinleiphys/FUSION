# Sextic potential for $Îł$-rigid prolate nuclei

> The equation of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a sextic oscillator potential is solved for $Îł$-rigid prolate nuclei. The associated shape phase space is reduced to three variables which are exactly separated. The angular equation has the spherical harmonic functions as solutions, while the $Î˛$ equation is brought to the quasi-exactly solvable case of the sextic oscillator potential with a centrifugal barrier. The energies and the corresponding wave functions are given in closed form and depend, up to a scaling factor, on a single parameter. The $0^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ states are exactly determined, having an important role in the assignment of some ambiguous states for the experimental $Î˛$ bands. Due to the special properties of the sextic potential, the model can simulate, by varying the free parameter, a shape phase transition from a harmonic to an anharmonic prolate $Î˛$-soft rotor crossing through a critical point. Numerical applications are performed for 39 nuclei: $^{98-108}$Ru, $^{100,102}$Mo, $^{116-130}$Xe, $^{132,134}$Ce, $^{146-150}$Nd, $^{150,152}$Sm, $^{152,154}$Gd, $^{154,156}$Dy, $^{172}$Os, $^{180-196}$Pt, $^{190}$Hg and $^{222}$Ra. The best candidates for the critical point are found to be $^{104}$Ru and $^{120,126}$Xe, followed closely by $^{128}$Xe, $^{172}$Os, $^{196}$Pt and $^{148}$Nd.

## Key claim

The Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a sextic oscillator potential is solved for gamma-rigid prolate nuclei, yielding a model that can simulate a shape phase transition from a harmonic to an anharmonic prolate beta-soft rotor crossing through a critical point, but only for the 0+ and 2+ states are the energies and wave functions exactly determined; for states with L greater than or equal to 4, an approximation is used.

## Method

The Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for gamma-rigid prolate shape (gamma = 0 degrees) was separated into angular and beta equations, with the angular part solved by spherical harmonics. The beta equation was transformed into a Schrodinger form and mapped onto the quasi-exactly solvable case of the sextic oscillator potential with a centrifugal barrier, using a change of variable and a specific ansatz for the wave function. The free parameter alpha was fitted to experimental energy ratios normalized to the first excited state, and the model was applied to 39 nuclei across several isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- For K=2, the model covers ground band states up to 10+, first beta band up to 6+, and second beta band up to 2+.
- The critical point alpha_c lies in the interval [2*sqrt(c_0), 2*sqrt(c_2)], with specific values such as alpha_c = 3.65 for the K=2 case.
- For 104Ru, the fitted alpha = 3.62 is very close to the critical point alpha_c = 3.65, and the experimental energy ratio for the first beta band head is 2.76 while X(3)-Sextic predicts 2.92.
- The root mean square deviation for 104Ru is 0.40, while for 152Sm it is 1.59 and for 154Gd it is 1.51.
- For the parameter-free case at alpha = 0, the energy ratio R_{0,4} = 2.45 and at alpha = alpha_c, R_{0,4} = 2.74 for K=2.

## Context

This work extends the X(3) model of Bonatsos (2004), which used an infinite square well potential for the beta variable, by replacing it with a sextic oscillator potential that allows a flexible description of shape phase transitions. It follows earlier applications of the sextic potential to gamma-unstable nuclei by Levai (1993) and to gamma-stable triaxial nuclei by Raduta (2011) and Buganu (2013), and it is the gamma-rigid prolate counterpart of the Z(4)-Sextic solution. The model enables systematic identification of beta band heads in experimental data and provides a framework for studying first-order shape phase transitions in gamma-rigid prolate nuclei.
