# Excited-state one-neutron halo nuclei within a parallel momentum distribution analysis

> Using a fully quantum mechanical post-form finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation theory of Coulomb breakup, I study the parallel momentum distribution of the core in the Coulomb breakup of suggested excited-state one-neutron halo nuclei considered in their different bound excited states. Narrow momentum distributions obtained in the present calculations for some cases indicate the possibilities of the excited-state halo structure in the nuclei under consideration and therefore favor the previous predictions.

# Excited-state one-neutron halo nuclei within a parallel momentum distribution analysis

## Key claim
Using a fully quantum mechanical post-form finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory of Coulomb breakup, the author calculates parallel momentum distributions (PMD) of the core fragment for several nuclei in their bound excited states, finding that narrow momentum distributions in some cases indicate the possibility of excited-state halo structure. The claim is conditional: the FWHM values for excited states are compared to the well-established ground-state halo nuclei (FWHM around 44 MeV/c) and stable nuclei (FWHM around 140 MeV/c), and only those states with comparably narrow distributions are suggested as halo candidates.

## Method
The author applies the post-form FRDWBA theory of Coulomb breakup to compute the parallel momentum distribution of the charged core fragment in the reaction projectile + Pb target at 100 MeV/nucleon beam energy. The projectile bound-state wavefunction is obtained by solving the radial Schrodinger equation with a Woods-Saxon potential, where the potential depth is adjusted to reproduce the neutron separation energy for each specific bound state. The PMD is calculated for the breakup of 10Be, 11Be, 12B, and 13C in their different bound excited states, and the full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the distribution is extracted as the key observable.

## Key numbers
- 11Be ground state (1/2+, Ex = 0.0 MeV): FWHM = 43.23 MeV/c, agrees with experimental value of 43.6 ± 1.1 MeV/c
- 11Be first excited state (1/2-, Ex = 0.320 MeV): FWHM = 41.15 MeV/c
- 12B 2- state (Ex = 1.673 MeV): FWHM = 68.36 MeV/c
- 12B 1- state (Ex = 2.620 MeV): FWHM = 49.96 MeV/c
- 12B 0+ state (Ex = 2.723 MeV): FWHM = 68.64 MeV/c
- 13C 1/2+ state (Ex = 3.089 MeV): FWHM = 73.76 MeV/c
- 10Be 2- state (Ex = 6.263 MeV): FWHM = 44.65 MeV/c
- 10Be ground state (0+, Ex = 0.0 MeV): FWHM = 191.15 MeV/c
- 13C ground state (1/2-, Ex = 0.0 MeV): FWHM = 169.37 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous predictions of excited-state halo structures based on indirect radius measurements and electromagnetic transitions, such as those by Belyaeva (for 11Be), Liu and Lin (for 12B), Otsuka (for 13C), and Khalili (for 10Be). The author uses a reaction observable (parallel momentum distribution) that is independent of beam energy over a wide range (50 MeV/nucleon to 2 GeV/nucleon) and independent of target mass, enabling predictions that could be tested by future experiments such as the recoil distance transmission method reported by Kobayashi. This approach can be used to predict additional halo candidates in excited states where direct measurements remain difficult.
