# Hybrid Baryon Signatures

> We discuss whether a low-lying hybrid baryon should be defined as a three quark - gluon bound state or as three quarks moving on an excited adiabatic potential. We show that the latter definition becomes exact, not only for very heavy quarks, but also for specific dynamics. We review the literature on the signatures of hybrid baryons, with specific reference to strong hadronic decays, electromagnetic couplings, diffractive production and production in psi decay.

# Hybrid Baryon Signatures

## Key claim
A low-lying hybrid baryon can be rigorously defined as three quarks moving on an excited adiabatic potential, and this definition becomes exact not only for very heavy quarks but also for specific dynamics when the adiabatic potential is redefined relative to the overall center of mass of quarks and gluons. For a simple harmonic oscillator toy model, the redefined adiabatic approximation yields energies and wave functions identical to the exact solution even for light quarks.

## Method
The paper develops a "redefined adiabatic approximation" by fixing quark positions relative to each other but defining all coordinates with respect to the overall center of mass of the three quarks and the gluon junction, then solving the resulting Schrodinger equation. The author compares exact, adiabatic, and redefined adiabatic solutions for a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with three quarks and a junction connected by harmonic oscillator potentials. The paper then reviews existing literature on hybrid baryon signatures, including flux-tube model calculations, bag model results, QCD sum rules, and predictions for decays, diffractive production, electroproduction, and production in psi decays.

## Key numbers
- Four low-lying N hybrid baryons have a mass of 1870 ± 100 MeV in the flux-tube model (Capstick)
- Wave function sizes: sqrt(<rho^2>) = sqrt(<lambda^2>) = 2.1 GeV^-1 for conventional baryons and 2.5 GeV^-1 for hybrid baryons
- A J^P = 1/2^+ 2-sigma peak at mass 1834^{+46}_{-55} MeV was seen in psi -> p pbar eta (Zou)
- Branching ratios Br(psi -> p pbar omega, p pbar eta') ~ 10^-3
- For electroproduction: conventional baryon transverse amplitude scales as 1/Q^3, hybrid as 1/Q^5; longitudinal amplitudes both scale as 1/Q^4

## Context
This work extends the flux-tube model approach of Paton (1985) and Capstick, and the lattice QCD calculations of Bali, by providing a formal justification for the adiabatic definition of hybrid baryons. The paper contrasts with the three-quark-plus-gluon Fock state definition used by Carlson for deep inelastic scattering and by Pirjol for large N_c hybrids, and with bag model treatments where gluons become massive due to confinement. The results enable experimental searches at Jefferson Lab Hall B and C, the Crystal Ball E913 D-line, and through partial wave analysis of electroproduction data at different Q^2 values.
