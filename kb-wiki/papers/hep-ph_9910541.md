# Spatial structure of quark Cooper pairs in a color superconductor

> Spatial structure of Cooper pairs with quantum numbers color 3^*, I=J=L=S=0 in ud 2 flavor quark matter is studied by solving the gap equation and calculating the coherence length in full momentum range without the weak coupling approximation. Although the gap at the Fermi surface and the coherence length depend on density weakly, the shape of the r-space pair wave function varies strongly with density. This result indicates that quark Cooper pairs become more bosonic at higher densities.

# Spatial structure of quark Cooper pairs in a color superconductor

## Key claim
Quark Cooper pairs in the color 3*, isosinglet ¹S₀ channel become more bosonic at higher densities, even though the gap at the Fermi surface and the coherence length depend only weakly on density. This conclusion is drawn from a calculation that retains full momentum dependence without the weak coupling approximation, but uses a static (instantaneous) approximation that neglects frequency dependence of the gap.

## Method
The author solves the gap equation for Δ(k) in a Hamiltonian formalism for ud 2-flavor quark matter at zero temperature, using the one-gluon exchange interaction with leading-order screening and a running coupling constant (Λ_QCD = 0.4 GeV). The gap equation is solved in full momentum space without the weak coupling approximation, and the pair wave function φ(k) = Δ(k)/(2E'(k)) is Fourier-transformed to obtain the r-space pair wave function φ(r). The coherence length ξ is computed from the momentum-space pair wave function.

## Key numbers
- Δ(k_F) ≈ 50 MeV at k_F = 60 fm⁻¹, showing the superconducting phase survives to practically infinite density
- For k_F = 1.5–3.25 fm⁻¹ (ρ/ρ₀ ≈ 1.5–15), Δ(k_F) shows very weak k_F-dependence
- Δ(q⁰ = 10 MeV)/Δ(q⁰ = 0) = 0.70 and Δ(q⁰ = 50 MeV)/Δ(q⁰ = 0) = 0.53 at k_F = 2 fm⁻¹, showing frequency dependence reduces the gap
- London penetration depth λ_L ≈ 20–8 fm for k_F = 1.5–3.25 fm⁻¹
- When M_q is changed to 100 MeV and 300 MeV, Δ(k_F) decreases to 90–98% and 54–81% of the original values respectively for k_F = 1.5–3.25 fm⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the study of color superconductivity in the ud 2-flavor system, following the extensive studies by Rapp et al. (1998) and Alford et al. (1998) that established the ¹S₀ channel as favored. It provides the first visualization of the spatial structure and density dependence of quark Cooper pairs, complementing earlier work on nucleon Cooper pairs in non-relativistic studies by Baldo (1990) and relativistic studies by Matsuzaki and Toki (1999). The results enable comparison with the London penetration depth and classification of quark matter as a type-II superconductor at moderate densities, contrary to the earlier conclusion of Bailin and Love (1984) that it would be type-I.
