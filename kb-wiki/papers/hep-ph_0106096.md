# Model-Independent Predictions for Low Energy Isoscalar Heavy Baryon Observables in the Combined Heavy Quark and Large $N_c$ Expansion

> Model-independent predictions for excitation energies, semileptonic form factors and electromagnetic decay rates of isoscalar heavy baryons and their low energy excited states are discussed in terms of the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ expansion. At leading order, the observables are completely determined in terms of the known excitation energy of the first excited state of $Λ_c$. At next-to-leading order in the combined expansion all heavy baryon observables can be expressed in a model-independent way in terms of two experimentally measurable quantities. We list predictions at leading and next-to-leading order.

# Model-Independent Predictions for Low Energy Isoscalar Heavy Baryon Observables in the Combined Heavy Quark and Large $N_c$ Expansion

## Key claim
At leading order in the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ expansion, all isoscalar heavy baryon observables are completely determined by the known excitation energy of the first excited state of $\Lambda_c$ (approximately 330 MeV), but the theoretical uncertainty is nominally of relative order $\lambda^{1/2} \sim 60\%$; at next-to-leading order, all observables can be expressed in terms of two experimentally measurable quantities, with theoretical uncertainty of relative order $\lambda \sim 30\%$.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian for the collective motion of the "brown muck" (light quarks and gluons) relative to the heavy quark, using counting rules consistent with a contracted $O(8)$ symmetry that emerges in the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ limit. The effective Hamiltonian contains three phenomenological parameters ($c_0$, $\kappa$, $\alpha$) of order $\lambda^0$, but only $\kappa$ and $\alpha$ determine the low-energy dynamics, as $c_0$ only sets the overall ground state energy. The effective operators for semileptonic form factors and electromagnetic decay rates are derived in a model-independent way up to next-to-leading order without additional parameters.

## Key numbers
- $\Delta m_c \equiv m_{\bar{\Lambda}_{c}^{*}} - m_{\Lambda_c} \approx 330$ MeV (excitation energy of first excited state of $\Lambda_c$, spin-averaged)
- Leading order prediction for $\Delta m_b$: 300 MeV
- Leading order prediction for $\Theta_0$ (zero-recoil form factor for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c \ell \bar{\nu}$): approximately 1 (specifically 0.998)
- Leading order prediction for $\rho$ (curvature of $\Theta(z)$ at zero recoil): $-1.2 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-3/2}$
- Leading order prediction for $\sigma$ (slope of $\Xi(z)$ at zero recoil): 0.011 MeV$^{-3/4}$
- Leading order prediction for $\Gamma(\Lambda_{c1} \to \Lambda_c \gamma)$: 0.025 MeV
- Leading order prediction for $\Gamma(\Lambda_{b1} \to \Lambda_b \gamma)$: 0.13 MeV
- Typical momentum scale $\Lambda \equiv (\mu_c \Delta m_c^2)^{1/3} \approx 410$ MeV
- Spin-averaged heavy meson masses: $m_{\bar{D}} \approx 1980$ MeV, $m_{\bar{B}} \approx 5310$ MeV
- Ratio of radiative decay rates at LO: $\Gamma(\Lambda_{c1}\to\Lambda_c\,\gamma)/\Gamma(\Lambda_{b1} \to \Lambda_b \, \gamma) \approx 0.2$

## Context
This work extends the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ expansion framework developed by Chow and Cohen (2000, 2001) and Chow, Cohen, and Gelman (2001) to derive model-independent relations among observables for isoscalar heavy baryons ($\Lambda_c$ and $\Lambda_b$). The approach builds on heavy quark effective theory (HQET) developed by Isgur and Wise (1989-1991) and Georgi (1991), and the large $N_c$ limit of QCD from 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979). The leading order predictions coincide with those from bound state pictures of heavy baryons as composites of a heavy meson and an ordinary baryon (Guralnik, Luke, and Manohar, 1993; Jenkins, Manohar, and Wise, 1993), but placed in a model-independent framework. The results enable future experimental tests once additional observables such as the $\Lambda_b$ excitation energy, semileptonic form factor derivatives, or radiative decay rates are measured; the authors identify $\rho$ (curvature of $\Theta(z)$ at zero recoil), $\sigma$ (slope of $\Xi(z)$ at zero recoil), or $\Gamma_c$ as the most sensitive observables for extracting the next-to-leading order parameters.
