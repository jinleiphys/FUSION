# $J/ψ$ polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Understanding the polarization property of $J/ψ$ is critical to constrain its production mechanism. In addition, the polarization of $J/ψ$ can reveal the impact of strong electromagnetic and vorticity fields in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this study, we analyzed the yield and polarization of $J/ψ$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions at different centrality and transverse momentum regions, using three different reference frames: the Collins-Soper frame, the helicity frame, and the event plane frame. The polarization of initially produced $J/ψ$ is determined by the NRQCD calculation and is similar to that of $pp$ collisions. However, both unpolarization and transverse polarization are considered for the regenerated $J/ψ$. Our results indicate that the polarization at high $p_T$ is similar to that observed in $pp$ collisions. However, at low $p_T$, where regenerated $J/ψ$ dominates, it is likely that the polarized charm quarks in the rotational QGP medium are responsible for this phenomenon. Our study supplies a baseline for future research on the effects of strong electromagnetic and vorticity fields on $J/ψ$ polarization.

## Key claim

The polarization of high-$p_T$ $J/\psi$ in PbPb collisions comes from initial production and matches $pp$ results, while at low $p_T$ the regenerated $J/\psi$ component likely acquires transverse polarization from polarized charm quarks in the rotational QGP medium, though the authors consider both unpolarized and transversely polarized scenarios for the regenerated component.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium phase-space distributions in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, using a (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model (MUSIC) for the medium evolution. The loss term includes gluon dissociation of $J/\psi$ and the gain term includes regeneration via $c\bar c \to g\psi$ with detailed balance; charm quarks are taken as thermalized. The polarization parameters $\lambda_\theta$, $\lambda_\phi$, and $\lambda_{\theta\phi}$ are computed in three reference frames (Collins-Soper, helicity, event plane) by combining the polarization of initially produced $J/\psi$ (from NRQCD) with that of regenerated $J/\psi$ (either unpolarized or transversely polarized).

## Key numbers

- $J/\psi$ dissociation temperature $T_d \approx 2.3 T_c$ from lattice QCD potential
- Cronin parameter $a_{gN} = 0.15\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}$ for PbPb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- Charm production cross section range $0.43\ \text{mb} < d\sigma^{c\bar{c}}_{pp}/d\eta < 0.61\ \text{mb}$ in forward rapidity at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV with shadowing
- Branching ratios: $\mathcal{B}_{\chi_c \to J/\psi} = 22\%$, $\mathcal{B}_{\psi(2S)\to J/\psi} = 61\%$
- At $p_T > 5$ GeV, $\lambda_\theta$ in the event plane frame is close to the NRQCD calculation and consistent with ALICE data
- At $p_T \approx 3$ GeV, the large positive $\lambda_\theta$ in the event plane frame indicates polarized charm quarks in the QGP

## Context

This work extends NRQCD-based polarization studies from $pp$ collisions (Ma 2018) to heavy-ion collisions, where $J/\psi$ has two sources: initial production and regeneration in QGP. It provides a baseline for future studies of strong electromagnetic and vorticity fields on $J/\psi$ polarization, following the first observation of $J/\psi$ polarization relative to the event plane by ALICE (2022). The transport framework builds on prior work by Du (2015), Zhao (2010, 2020), Zhou (2014), Chen (2018), and Liu (2009).
