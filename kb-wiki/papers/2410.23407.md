# Direct nonparametric multimessenger constraints on the equation of state of cold dense nuclear matter

> We utilize the now substantial amount of astrophysical observations of neutron stars (NSs), along with perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) calculations at high density, to directly constrain the NS equation of state (EOS). To this end, we construct nonparametric EOS priors by using Gaussian processes trained on 75 EOSs, which include models with either hadrons, hyperons, or quarks at high densities. We create a prior using the full EOS sample (model agnostic), and one prior for each EOS family to test model discrimination. We introduce a novel inference approach, which allows the simultaneous sampling of intrinsic and extrinsic parameters of binary NS mergers, as well as a nonparametric equation of state. We showcase this method in a Bayesian updating scheme by first performing a complete analysis of the binary NS merger event GW170817 with minimal assumptions, and sequentially adding information from x-ray and radio NS observations, along with pQCD calculations. Besides providing standard constraints, such as the pressure at twice nuclear saturation density $p(2ρ_\text{sat})=4.3^{+0.6}_{-0.6}\,\times 10^{34}\text{dyne/cm}^{2}$, at $95\%$ confidence level, for the model agnostic prior, our methodology shows how the choice of EOS families used in conditioning changes the inferred astrophysical properties of the EOS, namely tidal deformability and maximum supported NS mass. We find hyperonic priors predicting higher tidal deformabilities for a $1.4M_\odot$ NS, and hadronic priors being preferred by the considered astrophysical data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonparametric Gaussian process priors for the neutron star equation of state, when combined with a novel categorical distribution sampling method, can simultaneously infer both intrinsic and extrinsic parameters of binary neutron star mergers without requiring parametric EOS models. The authors show that the choice of EOS family used in prior construction (hadronic, hyperonic, or quark) significantly changes the inferred astrophysical properties, with hyperonic priors predicting higher tidal deformabilities for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star and hadronic priors being preferred by the astrophysical data.

## Method

The authors constructed Gaussian process priors trained on 75 tabulated equations of state from the CompOSE database (32 hadronic, 17 hyperonic, and 26 quark models), creating both a model-agnostic prior from all EOSs and separate model-informed priors for each family. They introduced a categorical distribution over 10000 draws from each Gaussian mixture model, allowing standard Markov Chain Monte Carlo and nested sampling algorithms to sample the EOS index as a single parameter alongside other source parameters. The method was applied in a Bayesian updating scheme: first performing a full parameter estimation of the GW170817 gravitational wave event using the IMRPhenomPv2 waveform approximant with NRTidalv2 tidal extension, then sequentially incorporating NICER mass-radius posteriors, 105 neutron star mass measurements, and perturbative QCD calculations at high density.

## Key numbers

- Pressure at twice nuclear saturation density: p(2ρ_sat) = 4.3^{+0.6}_{-0.6} × 10^34 dyne/cm^2 at 95% confidence level for the model-agnostic prior
- Nuclear saturation mass density: ρ_sat ≈ 2.7 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- Training dataset: 75 equations of state total (32 hadronic, 17 hyperonic, 26 quark models)
- Prior draws: 10000 samples from each Gaussian mixture model (10000 for model-agnostic, 30000 total for model-informed with three families)
- GW170817 data analysis used 256 seconds of data from H1, L1, and V1 detectors in the frequency range 20-16384 Hz
- PSR J0348+0432 mass: M = 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: M = 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric Gaussian process approach to EOS inference introduced by Landry (2019) and Essick (2019), overcoming limitations of parametric methods such as piecewise polytropes (Lindblom, 2018) and spectral decomposition (Lindblom, 2010) that struggle with phase transitions. The novel categorical distribution treatment enables simultaneous sampling of EOS and source parameters in gravitational wave analyses, which was previously only possible with parametric models. The method enables future integration of additional multimessenger observations, including potential BNS merger events like GW190425, GW190814, and GW200105, while providing a framework for model discrimination between hadronic, hyperonic, and quark compositions of neutron star cores.
