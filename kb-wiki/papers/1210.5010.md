# Crucial test for covariant density functional theory with new and accurate mass measurements from Sn to Pa

> The covariant density functional theory with the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1 is compared with new and accurate experimental masses in the element range from 50 to 91. The experimental data are from a mass measurement performed with the storage ring mass spectrometry at GSI [Chen et al., Nucl. Phys. A 882, 71 (2012)]. Although the microscopic theory contains only 11 parameters, it agrees well with the experimental data. The comparison is characterized by a rms deviation of 0.859 MeV. For even-even nuclei, the theory agrees within about 600 keV. Larger deviations are observed in this comparison for the odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. Improvements and possible reasons for the deviations are discussed in this contribution as well.

## Key claim

The covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1, despite having only 11 parameters fitted to 60 spherical nuclei, reproduces new experimental masses of 53 heavy neutron-rich isotopes (Sn to Pa) with a root-mean-square (rms) deviation of 0.859 MeV, but this overall agreement masks a significant caveat: the theory agrees within about 600 keV only for even-even nuclei, while larger deviations appear for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation on an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis (16 major shells) within the relativistic mean-field framework using the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1. Pairing correlations were treated with the BCS method using a zero-range delta force, and corrections for center-of-mass motion and rotational symmetry breaking (via the Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia) were applied. The calculated binding energies were compared to 53 new experimental masses measured by storage ring mass spectrometry at GSI (Chen et al., 2012), none of which were used in fitting the PC-PK1 parameters.

## Key numbers

- Overall rms deviation between CDFT with PC-PK1 and experimental masses for all 53 nuclei: 0.859 MeV.
- For the 31 masses measured for the first time, the rms deviation is 0.805 MeV.
- For the 12 even-even nuclei in the set, the rms deviation is about 0.6 MeV.
- The experimental data have a systematic error of about 10 keV and an overall mean experimental accuracy of about 19 keV.
- The PC-PK1 interaction contains only 11 parameters, fitted to binding energies of 60 spherical nuclei and charge radii of 17 nuclei.

## Context

This work provides a crucial test of CDFT using new, high-precision mass data from storage ring spectrometry at GSI (Chen et al., 2012), which includes 31 previously unmeasured masses and improved precision for 22 others. The results extend earlier systematic mass calculations with CDFT using the TMA interaction (Geng et al., 2005) and are compared to the nonrelativistic HFB-21 model (Goriely et al., 2010) and the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM (Moller et al., 1995). The study identifies that the simple BCS treatment of pairing and neglect of time-odd components in the rotational correction likely cause the larger deviations for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, pointing toward the need for more sophisticated Bogoliubov calculations with continuum for neutron-rich heavy nuclei.
