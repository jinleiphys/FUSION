# Theory of Weak Hypernuclear Decay

> The weak nonmesonic decay of Lambda-hypernuclei is studied in the context of a one-meson-exchange model. Predictions are made for the decay rate, p/n stimulation ratio and the asymmetry in polarized hypernuclear decay.

## Key claim

The weak nonmesonic decay of Lambda-hypernuclei is studied using a one-meson-exchange model, with the claim that the dominant decay mode for hypernuclei with mass number A greater than about 10 is the nonmesonic process Lambda N to NN rather than the pionic decay favored by a free Lambda. The authors hedge that their theoretical predictions are strongly sensitive to the short-ranged correlation function assumed for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and that the only weak couplings which are completely model-independent are those involving pion emission.

## Method

The authors construct weak baryon-baryon-meson vertices using a quark model combined with SU(6)_w symmetry, determining parity-conserving couplings via a pole model and current algebra/PCAC relations, and parity-violating couplings via SU(6)_w symmetry with parameters fit to hyperon decay data. These weak vertices are then inserted into one-meson-exchange diagrams (pi, eta, K, rho, omega, K*) to generate two-body Lambda N to NN transition potentials, which are applied to calculate nonmesonic decay rates in nuclear matter and finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Free Lambda decay rate: Gamma_Lambda = 1/(263 ps), with branching ratios Lambda to p pi- at 64.1% and Lambda to n pi0 at 35.7%
- Weak coupling constants: g_w = 2.35 x 10^{-7}, kappa = -6.7 for the effective Lagrangian describing Lambda to N pi decay
- Kinetic energy of final state nucleon in free Lambda decay: T_N approximately 5 MeV, momentum p_N approximately 100 MeV/c
- Kinetic energy of outgoing nucleons in nonmesonic decay: T_N approximately 90 MeV, momentum p_N approximately 420 MeV/c
- Experimental nonmesonic decay rate relative to free Lambda: for ^5_Lambda He, Gamma_NM/Gamma_Lambda = 0.41 +/- 0.14; for ^11_Lambda B, 1.25 +/- 0.16; for ^12_Lambda C, 1.14 +/- 0.20
- Experimental p/n stimulation ratio: for ^5_Lambda He, 1.07 +/- 0.58; for ^11_Lambda B, 0.96 (+0.8/-0.4); for ^12_Lambda C, 0.75 (+1.5/-0.35)
- Lifetime of ^238_Lambda U: tau = (1 +1/-0.5) x 10^{-10} sec; for ^209_Lambda Bi: tau = (2.5 +2.5/-1.0) x 10^{-10} sec
- Theoretical Lambda n pi0 decay amplitude: T_w(theo.) = 1.35 x 10^{-6}; experimental value: T_w(expt.) = 1.61 x 10^{-6}
- Parity-conserving baryon matrix elements: A_{N Lambda} = -i 4.46 x 10^{-5} MeV, A_{N Sigma} = i 4.36 x 10^{-5} MeV
- Parity-violating coupling parameters: b_V = -b_T = -1.28 c_V = -4.0 x 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the one-meson-exchange approach previously developed for nuclear parity violation (as reviewed in reference 10) to the hypernuclear weak decay problem, building on earlier calculations that included only the longest-range pion exchange component. The authors note that Primakoff and Cheston (early work) first realized that the Lambda decay properties are dramatically altered when bound in a hypernucleus, and that Adams (reference 13) first pointed out the need to include neutral spinless mesons and strange mesons (K, K*) in addition to the lightest mesons. The paper enables future calculations of nonmesonic decay rates, p/n stimulation ratios, and asymmetries in polarized hypernuclear decay that can be compared with experimental data from the CMU-BNL-UNM-Houston-Texas-Vassar collaboration and other groups.
