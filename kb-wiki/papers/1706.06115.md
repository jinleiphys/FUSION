# Correlation Energy of Proton-Neutron Subsystem in Valence Orbit

> Deuteron correlation energy (DCE) of the valence proton-neutron subsystem is evaluated by utilizing a simple three-body model. We focus on the $^6$Li and $^{18}$F nuclei assuming the doubly-closed core and the valence proton and neutron. Two interaction models, schematic density-dependent contact (SDDC) and Minnesota potentials, are utilized to describe the proton-neutron interaction. Evaluating DCE, we conclude that the proton-neutron binding in $^6$Li can be stronger than its counterpart of a deuteron in vacuum. On the other hand, in $^{18}$F, the energetic correlation is remarkably weak, and does not favor the bound, deuteron-like configuration. This significant difference between two systems can be understood from a competition between the proton-neutron kinetic and pairing energies, which are sensitive to the spatial extension of the wave function. This result indicates a remarkable dependence of the deuteron correlation to its environment and the valence orbits.

## Key claim

The deuteron correlation energy (DCE) of the valence proton-neutron subsystem in $^6$Li can be stronger than the binding energy of a free deuteron in vacuum, whereas in $^{18}$F the energetic correlation is remarkably weak and does not favor a bound, deuteron-like configuration; this difference arises from a competition between kinetic and pairing energies that depends on the spatial extension of the wave function and the specific valence orbits.

## Method

The authors employed a core-orbital three-body model assuming a doubly-closed core ($\alpha$ for $^6$Li, $^{16}$O for $^{18}$F) plus valence proton and neutron, solving the three-body Hamiltonian by diagonalization on an uncorrelated basis of single-particle states up to $l_{\rm max}=5$ with a radial box of $R_{\rm box}=20$ fm and energy cutoff $E_{\rm cut}=15$ MeV. Two proton-neutron interaction models were used: a schematic density-dependent contact (SDDC) potential and the Minnesota potential (spin-triplet channel), with core-nucleon interactions described by Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit potentials. The deuteron correlation energy was defined as the expectation value of the partial proton-neutron Hamiltonian $h_{p-n}$ in T-Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$Li with the SDDC interaction (Li-S set): DCE = $-4.34$ MeV, compared to the vacuum deuteron binding energy of $-2.22$ MeV; the three-body binding energy $E_{3b} = -3.70$ MeV reproduces the empirical value.
- For $^6$Li with the original Minnesota potential (Li-MO set): DCE = $-3.21$ MeV, with $E_{3b} = -2.69$ MeV (underbinding by about 1 MeV relative to the empirical $-3.70$ MeV).
- For $^6$Li with enhanced Minnesota potential (Li-MF set, $f=1.13$): DCE = $-4.35$ MeV, with $E_{3b} = -3.70$ MeV.
- For $^{18}$F with the SDDC interaction (F-S set): DCE = $+0.33$ MeV (positive, indicating no bound deuteron-like configuration), with $E_{3b} = -9.78$ MeV.
- For $^{18}$F with the original Minnesota potential (F-MO set): DCE = $-2.05$ MeV, but $E_{3b} = -13.33$ MeV (overbinding by about 3.6 MeV relative to the empirical $-9.75$ MeV).
- For $^{18}$F with reduced Minnesota potential (F-MF set, $f=0.67$): DCE = $+1.78$ MeV, with $E_{3b} = -9.75$ MeV.
- Mean opening angle $\langle \theta_{\rm pn} \rangle$ in $^6$Li: approximately $84$ to $86$ degrees across all interaction models.
- Mean relative distance $\sqrt{\langle \xi^2_{p-n} \rangle}$ in $^6$Li: approximately $5.5$ to $5.7$ fm; in $^{18}$F: approximately $4.6$ to $5.1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton-neutron pairing correlations in $N=Z$ nuclei, particularly the investigations by Tanimura et al. (2012, 2013, 2014) and Masui et al. (2016) who examined deuteron-like correlations and the role of spin-orbit splitting. The three-body model approach is similar to that used by Hagino and Sagawa (2005) for dineutron correlations in $^6$He. The results show that the deuteron correlation is highly sensitive to the nuclear environment: in $^6$Li the valence proton-neutron pair gains extra binding relative to vacuum, while in $^{18}$F the correlation is too weak to form a bound deuteron-like subsystem. This finding enables future studies of how the valence orbit structure and core stability affect proton-neutron pairing in heavier $N=Z$ nuclei, and may inform phenomenological models of Gamow-Teller transitions and nuclear magnetic modes.
