# Role of the low-lying nucleon resonances in the $p\bar{p} \to ψη$ reaction

> Within the effective Lagrangian approach, we study the $p\bar{p} \rightarrow ψη$ [$ψ\equivψ(3686)$, $J/ψ$] reaction at the low energy where the contributions from nucleon pole and low-lying nucleon resonances, $N(1520)$, $N(1535)$ and $N(1650)$ are considered. All the model parameters are determined with the help of current experimental data on the decay of $ψ\to p \bar{p} η$. Within the model parameters, the total and differential cross sections of the $p\bar{p} \rightarrow ψη$ reaction are predicted. We show that the relative phases between different amplitudes of different nucleon resonance will change significantly the angular distributions of the $\bar{p} p \to ψη$ reaction. Therefore, we conclude that these reactions are suitable to study experimentally the properties of the low-lying nucleon resonance and the reaction mechanisms. We hope that these theoretical calculations can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the relative phases between the amplitudes of different nucleon resonances will significantly change the angular distributions of the $\bar{p} p \to \psi \eta$ reaction, making these reactions suitable for studying the properties of low-lying nucleon resonances, but the predictions are qualitative because the contributions from initial state interactions of $\bar{p}p$ are neglected.

## Method

Within an effective Lagrangian approach, the authors calculated the total and differential cross sections for the $p\bar{p} \rightarrow \psi\eta$ reaction ($\psi\equiv\psi(3686)$ and $J/\psi$) at low energy, including contributions from the nucleon pole and three low-lying nucleon resonances: $N(1520)$, $N(1535)$, and $N(1650)$. The model parameters were determined by fitting the experimental data on the $\psi \to p \bar{p} \eta$ decay from the BESIII Collaboration, using a five-parameter $\chi^2$ fit to the $p\eta$ invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

* The fitted relative phases for the $\psi(3686) \to p\bar{p}\eta$ decay are: $\phi_{N(1520)} = 0.14 \pm 0.08$, $\phi_{N(1535)} = 1.76 \pm 0.06$, $\phi_{N(1650)} = 4.63 \pm 0.05$, with a $\chi^{2}/dof$ of $0.39$.
* The fitted cutoff parameter is $\beta = 3.70 \pm 0.82$, with $\Lambda_{N^{*}}=m_{N^{*}}+\beta \Lambda_{QCD}$ and $\Lambda_{QCD} = 220$ MeV.
* The coupling constant $g_{\eta NN}$ is extracted as $8.99 \pm 1.17$, using $g_{\psi(3686) NN} =9.4\times 10^{-4}$ and the fitted $g^{\psi(3686) \eta}_N = (8.45 \pm 1.10) \times 10^{-3}$.
* For $p\bar{p}\rightarrow \psi(3686) \eta$ at $E_{cm}=5.38$ GeV, the predicted total cross section is 23 pb, compared to $33\pm8$ pb from Lundborg (2005) and 9 pb from Lin (2012).
* For $p\bar{p}\rightarrow J/\psi \eta$ at $E_{cm}=4.57$ GeV, the predicted total cross section is 56 pb, compared to $1520 \pm 140$ pb from Lundborg (2005) and 36 pb from Lin (2012).
* For $p\bar{p}\rightarrow J/\psi \eta$, the total cross section has a maximum of about 80.5 pb at $E_{cm}=5.5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of charmonium production in $p\bar{p}$ annihilation, following earlier calculations for $p\bar{p}\rightarrow \psi \pi^{0}$ by Gaillard and Maiani (1982) and for $p\bar{p}\rightarrow \psi X$ by Barnes (2006, 2007, 2010). It specifically builds on the approach of Lin, Xu, and Liu (2012) by including contributions from nucleon resonances $N(1520)$, $N(1535)$, and $N(1650)$ for the first time in the $p\bar{p}\rightarrow \psi \eta$ reaction. The results are intended to guide future experiments at the $\bar{\mathrm{P}}$ANDA facility and to be tested by more precise experimental data, which would also allow inclusion of initial state interaction effects.
