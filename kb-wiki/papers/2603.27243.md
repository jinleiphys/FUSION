# Microscopic theory of the $γ$ decay of giant resonances in superfluid nuclei

> Recent advances in experiments have enabled the measurement of $γ$-decay from giant and pygmy resonances to low-lying states, establishing this technique as a unique probe for nuclear structure. However, a microscopic description of $γ$-decay to low-lying states in superfluid nuclei is still lacking. We develop the Skyrme quasiparticle vibration (QPVC) model to calculate $γ$-decay widths between vibrational states. This model treats initial and final states as quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) phonons and includes all the second-order diagrams for the interaction between the quasiparticles and the phonons, while consistently accounting for the polarization processes. The same Skyrme functional is employed for the ground state and the interaction vertices. As a timely application, the $γ$-decay width from the giant dipole resonance to the $2_{1}^{+}$ state in $^{140}$Ce is calculated, which has recently been measured at the high intensity $γ$-ray source (HI$γ$S). For the 4 Skyrme functionals we used, the total width of the collective dipole states in GDR region is 200-420 eV and the corresponding branching ratio is 0.75-1.20\%. The polarization effect, extracted microscopically, agrees in trend with the macroscopic Bohr-Mottelson formula.

## Key claim

The authors develop the Skyrme quasiparticle vibration coupling (QPVC) model to provide the first fully self-consistent microscopic description of gamma decay from giant resonances to low-lying states in superfluid nuclei, but the results depend on the choice of Skyrme functional and the calculated total width of collective dipole states in the GDR region ranges from 200 to 420 eV across the four functionals used.

## Method

The authors extend the Skyrme particle vibration coupling (PVC) model to superfluid systems by incorporating pairing correlations based on Skyrme energy density functionals, treating initial and final states as quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) phonons. They include all second-order diagrams for the interaction between quasiparticles and phonons within the nuclear field theory (NFT) framework, consistently accounting for polarization processes using the same Skyrme functional for the ground state and interaction vertices. The model is applied to calculate the gamma-decay width from the giant dipole resonance (GDR) to the 2_1^+ state in ^140Ce, using four different Skyrme functionals (SIII, SGII, SkM*, and LNS) with volume pairing.

## Key numbers

* Total width of collective dipole states in the GDR region: 200-420 eV (range across 4 Skyrme functionals for ^140Ce)
* Branching ratio for gamma decay from GDR to 2_1^+ state: 0.75-1.20% (range across 4 Skyrme functionals for ^140Ce)
* Volume pairing strengths used: -206.1 MeV·fm^3 (SIII), -198.1 MeV·fm^3 (SGII), -186.7 MeV·fm^3 (SkM*), and -182.9 MeV·fm^3 (LNS)
* Model space exhausts 100.1% of the isovector energy-weighted sum rule when using the SIII interaction
* Experimental GDR energy in ^140Ce: 15.03 MeV (from Lorentzian fitting to photoneutron cross section, Lepretre 1976)
* Theoretical GDR energies: 16.30 MeV (SIII), 15.20 MeV (SGII), 15.47 MeV (SkM*), 15.66 MeV (LNS)
* Experimental 2_1^+ state energy in ^140Ce: 1.60 MeV; B(E2) value: 0.304 × 10^4 e^2 fm^2 (Peker 1994)

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent Skyrme PVC model previously developed for gamma decay of giant resonances (Brenna 2012, Lv 2021) to include pairing correlations, which were absent in earlier treatments. The model enables microscopic calculations of gamma decay from giant and pygmy resonances to low-lying states in superfluid nuclei, motivated by recent measurements at the High Intensity gamma-ray Source (HIgammaS) for ^140Ce (Kleemann 2024) and similar experiments for ^154Sm (Kleemann 2025) and ^150Nd (Papst 2025). The polarization effect extracted microscopically agrees in trend with the macroscopic Bohr-Mottelson formula, and the approach can be applied to future measurements planned at Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro and Shanghai Synchrotron Radiation Facility.
