# Beam energy dependence of the relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> In this article we investigate the beam energy dependence of relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the single and double $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratios in three heavy-ion reactions with an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU11. With the beam energy increasing from 200 to 400 MeV/nucleon, effects of the relativistically retarded electrical fields on the $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio are found to increase gradually from negligibly to considerably significant as expectedly; it is however, the interesting observation is the relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio are becoming gradually insignificant as the beam energy further increasing from 400 to 800 MeV/nucleon. Moreover, we also investigate the isospin dependence of relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio in two isobar reaction systems of $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr at the beam energies from 200 to 800 MeV/nucleon. It is shown that the relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio are independent of the isospin of reaction. Furthermore, we also examine the double $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio in reactions of $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr over $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru at the beam energies from 200 to 800 MeV/nucleon with the static field and retarded field, respectively. It is shown the double $ŽĆ^{-}/ŽĆ^{+}$ ratio from two reactions is still an effective observable of symmetry energy without the interference of electrical field due to using the relativistic calculation compared to the nonrelativistic calculation.

## Key claim

The relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the ŽĆŌłÆ/ŽĆ+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions are not monotonic with beam energy: they increase from negligible to significant as beam energy rises from 200 to 400 MeV/nucleon, but then become gradually insignificant as beam energy further increases from 400 to 800 MeV/nucleon, due to the competition between enhanced anisotropic features of retarded fields and reduced reaction duration time.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Wei, 2018) which first showed that relativistically retarded electrical fields significantly affect the ŽĆŌłÆ/ŽĆ+ ratio in Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. The present paper systematically maps the beam energy dependence from 200 to 800 MeV/nucleon, finding a non-monotonic behavior with maximum effect around 400 MeV/nucleon. It also demonstrates that the double ŽĆŌłÆ/ŽĆ+ ratio from two isobar reactions (Ōü╣ŌüČZr+Ōü╣ŌüČZr over Ōü╣ŌüČRu+Ōü╣ŌüČRu) can effectively eliminate electrical field interference while retaining sensitivity to the symmetry energy, building on the double-ratio concept proposed by Famiano (2006) and Yong (2006). The results caution that relativistic retardation effects must be carefully considered when using the ŽĆŌłÆ/ŽĆ+ ratio as a probe of nuclear symmetry energy at intermediate energies, especially around 400 MeV/nucleon.
