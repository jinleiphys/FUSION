# Comprehensive evidences of octupole vibration in $^{158}$Gd

> Comprehensive evidences of the SU(3) limit in the spdf interacting boson model, a dynamical symmetry describing octupole vibration in rotational nucleus, are found in the spectrum, E2 and E1 transition rates, and relative intensities in $^{158}$Gd. This gives a good example of rotational nucleus with octupole vibration in rare-earth region.

# Comprehensive Evidences of Octupole Vibration in $^{158}$Gd

## Key claim
The authors claim that $^{158}$Gd provides the first comprehensive experimental evidence of the SU(3) dynamical symmetry in the spdf interacting boson model (IBM) for octupole vibration in a rotational nucleus, despite the general expectation that such a good example should be found in the actinide region rather than in the rare-earth region.

## Method
The authors performed calculations within the SU(3) limit of the spdf IBM, fitting the energy eigenvalues and electromagnetic transition operators to experimental data for $^{158}$Gd. The energy parameters were determined from the spectrum, and the E2 transition operator was taken as the SU(3) generator, while the E1 transition operator was parametrized with two coefficients fitted to experimental data. The calculated spectrum, B(E2) and B(E1) transition rates, and relative intensities were compared with existing experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The valence nucleon pair number N for $^{158}$Gd is 13.
- Energy parameters: $a_1 = -7.793$ keV, $\epsilon_- = 3.160$ MeV, $a_3 = -4.686$ keV; $a_4 = 11.917$ keV for positive parity states and $a_4 = 8.592$ keV for negative parity states.
- E1 transition operator parameters: $\chi_{dp} = -3.825$, $\chi_{df} = 3.676$ (Cal1); the O(10) generator values are $\chi_{dp} = -1.2649$ and $\chi_{df} = 1.1832$ (Cal2).
- At high spin ($L=10$), the theoretical B(E2) is less than the experimental data, attributed to the reduction of collectivity problem in boson models.

## Context
This work extends the search for SU(3) dynamical symmetry in the spdf IBM, which had previously been proposed for $^{232}$U and other actinide nuclei by the authors of Refs. [r11, r12, r13, r17], but those candidates lacked sufficient electromagnetic transition data, particularly E1 transitions. The finding in $^{158}$Gd is somewhat unexpected because the SU(3) octupole deformation dynamical symmetry was thought to reside in the actinide region. This result enables further studies of octupole collectivity in the rare-earth region, leveraging the similarities of Gd isotopes with other rare-earth nuclei.
