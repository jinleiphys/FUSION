# Estimation of CP violating EDMs from known mechanisms in the SM

> New sources of CP violation, beyond the known sources in the standard model (SM), are required to explain the baryon asymmetry of the universe. Measurement of a non-zero permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) for fundamental particles, such as in an electron or a neutron, or in nuclei or atoms, can help us gain a handle on the sources of CP violation, both in SM and beyond. Multiple mechanisms within the SM can generate CP violating EDMs, viz.\ through the CKM matrix in the weak sector or through the QCD $\barθ$ parameter in the strong sector. We will estimate the maximum possible EDMs of leptons, certain baryons, select atoms and molecules in the (CKM$\bigoplus\barθ$) framework, assuming that the EDM wholly originates from either of the two SM mechanisms, independently. These estimates have been presented in light of the current experimental upper limits on the EDMs, in the following systems - leptons: $e^-$, $μ^-$, $τ^-$, $ν_e^0$, $ν^0_μ$, $ν^0_τ$, baryons: $n^0$, $p^+$, $Λ^0$, $Σ^0$, $Ξ^0$, $Λ^+_c$, $Ξ^+_c$, atoms: $^{85}$Rb, $^{133}$Cs, $^{210}$Fr, $^{205}$Tl, $^{199}$Hg, $^{129}$Xe, $^{225}$Ra, $^{223}$Rn, and molecules: HfF$^+$, PbO, YbF, ThO, RaF, TlF. Particularly, to drive home the point that EDMs in different systems constrain CP-violating interactions differently, we show that the same measured constraint on the EDM in two different systems may not actually be equally constraining on CP violating parameters, and to emphasize the need to measure a non-zero EDM in multiple systems before understanding the origins of these CP-violating EDMs.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the maximum possible electric dipole moments (EDMs) for a wide range of leptons, baryons, atoms, and molecules assuming the EDM originates wholly from either of two Standard Model (SM) mechanisms, the CKM matrix or the QCD \(\bar{\theta}\) parameter, independently, and then compares these estimates to current experimental upper limits to show that a non-zero EDM measured in any single system cannot uniquely identify the CP-violating source.

## Method

The authors calculate SM-CKM and SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) contributions to EDMs using known theoretical scaling relations and loop-order estimates from the literature. For charged leptons, they scale the electron EDM by the mass ratio; for baryons, they use one-loop \(\pi NN\) diagram estimates and known ratios between baryon EDMs; for atoms and molecules, they combine contributions from intrinsic particle EDMs, nuclear Schiff moments, and semi-leptonic electron-nucleon interactions (\(C_S\) and \(C_T\)) using published sensitivity coefficients, then compare each to the corresponding 90% confidence level experimental upper limit.

## Key numbers

- Electron: SM-CKM EDM \(d_e \sim 10^{-44}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM \(d_e \sim 8.6\times10^{-38}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; experimental limit \(d_e < 1.1\times10^{-29}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (ThO, ACME Collaboration 2018).
- Neutron: SM-CKM EDM \(d_n \sim 2\times10^{-32}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM \(d_n < 1.8\times10^{-26}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; experimental limit \(d_n < 1.8\times10^{-26}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (Abel 2020).
- Muon: SM-CKM EDM \(d_\mu \sim 2.1\times10^{-42}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM \(d_\mu \sim 1.8\times10^{-35}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; experimental limit \(d_\mu < 1.6\times10^{-19}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (Muon g-2 Collaboration 2009).
- \({}^{199}\)Hg: SM-CKM EDM \(d_{\text{Hg}} \sim 6.2\times10^{-30}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM \(d_{\text{Hg}} < 6.2\times10^{-30}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; experimental limit \(d_{\text{Hg}} < 6.2\times10^{-30}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (Graner 2016).
- ThO molecule: SM-CKM EDM \(d_{\text{ThO}} \sim 1.1\times10^{-20}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM \(d_{\text{ThO}} \sim 1.1\times10^{-22}\) e\(\cdot\)cm; experimental limit \(d_{\text{ThO}} < 1.1\times10^{-20}\) e\(\cdot\)cm (ACME Collaboration 2018).
- QCD \(\bar{\theta}\) parameter constraint from neutron EDM: \(\bar{\theta} < 3\times10^{-10}\) at 90% C.L.

## Context

This work extends prior reviews of EDM mechanisms (Engel 2013, Chupp 2015, Chupp 2019) by providing a unified tabulation of SM-CKM and SM-\(\bar{\theta}\) EDM estimates across many species, from leptons to molecules, in the context of current experimental limits. It enables the community to identify which systems have the largest "white space" between SM predictions and experimental bounds (e.g., charged leptons with over 8 orders of magnitude) as promising targets for beyond-Standard-Model searches, and it underscores the need for multi-system EDM measurements to disentangle CP-violating sources.
