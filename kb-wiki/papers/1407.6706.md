# Analytic Calculation of 1-Jettiness in DIS at $\mathcal O(α_s)$

> We present an analytic $\mathcal O(α_s)$ calculation of cross sections in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) dependent on an event shape, 1-jettiness, that probes final states with one jet plus initial state radiation. This is the first entirely analytic calculation for a DIS event shape cross section at this order. We present results for the differential and cumulative 1-jettiness cross sections, and express both in terms of structure functions dependent not only on the usual DIS variables $x$, $Q^2$ but also on the 1-jettiness $τ$. Combined with previous results for log resummation, predictions are obtained over the entire range of the 1-jettiness distribution.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first entirely analytic calculation of a DIS event shape cross section at $\mathcal O(\alpha_s)$, specifically for the 1-jettiness variable $\tau_1^b$ (equivalent to the DIS thrust $\tau_Q$). The calculation provides fixed-order singular and nonsingular parts of the cross section that, when combined with previous NNLL resummation results, yield predictions over the entire range of the 1-jettiness distribution.

## Method
The authors performed an analytic $\mathcal O(\alpha_s)$ fixed-order calculation of the 1-jettiness differential cross section in deep inelastic scattering using the Breit frame, where the 1-jettiness variable $\tau$ groups final-state particles into back-to-back hemispheres. They computed the hadronic tensor by perturbative matching onto parton distribution functions in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme, using dimensional regularization to handle infrared divergences, and expressed the results in terms of structure functions $\mathcal F_1$ and $\mathcal F_L$ that depend on $x$, $Q^2$, and $\tau$. The cross section was separated into singular parts (containing logarithmic terms) and nonsingular parts (non-logarithmic terms), with the singular parts matching the factorization theorem from soft collinear effective theory (SCET).

## Key numbers
- The 1-jettiness variable $\tau$ is defined as $\tau = \frac{2}{Q^2}\sum_{i\in X}\min\{ q_B^b \cdot p_i, q_J^b \cdot p_i\}$, where $Q^2 \equiv -q^2$ is the momentum transfer squared.
- The physical upper limit on $\tau$ is $\tau^\text{max} = 1$ for $x \le 1/2$ and $\tau^\text{max} = (1-x)/x$ for $x \ge 1/2$.
- The cross section is expressed as $\frac{d\sigma}{dxdQ^2d\tau} = \frac{4\pi \alpha^2}{Q^4}\left[ (1+(1-y)^2)\mathcal F_1 + \frac{1-y}{x}\mathcal F_L\right]$, where $\mathcal F_L \equiv \mathcal F_2 - 2x\mathcal F_1$.
- The leading-order quark contribution to the contracted tensor is $-g^{\mu\nu}W_{\mu\nu}^q/(1-\epsilon) = 4\pi Q_f^2 \delta(1-x)\delta(\tau) + \mathcal O(\alpha_s)$.
- The $\mathcal O(\alpha_s)$ gluon contribution to the contracted tensor is $-g^{\mu\nu}W_{\mu\nu}^g/(1-\epsilon) = 4\alpha_s T_F\sum_f Q_f^2 [ -\frac{1}{\epsilon}P_{qg}(x)\delta(\tau) + w_G^g(x,Q^2,\tau)]$.

## Context
This work extends the SCET-based factorization theorems for DIS 1-jettiness developed by Kang, Lee, and Stewart (2013) by providing the first analytic fixed-order nonsingular corrections at $\mathcal O(\alpha_s)$. It enables matching of NNLL resummation results with fixed-order calculations to obtain predictions over the entire $\tau$ range, improving upon earlier NLL resummation and numerical $\mathcal O(\alpha_s^2)$ calculations by Antonelli, Dasgupta, and Salam (1999, 2002). The results can be compared to existing HERA data from H1 and ZEUS experiments or to future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) data.
