# A String Theory Explanation for Quantum Chaos in the Hadronic Spectrum

> In the 1950's Wigner and collaborators provided an explanation for the spectrum of hadronic excitations in terms of Random Matrix Theory. In the 1980's it was understood that some hadronic spectral properties were generic to systems whose classical limit is chaotic. We use string theory to demonstrate explicitly how, under very general conditions, recent holographic models of strong interactions have a spectrum compatible with Wigner's conjecture.

## Key claim

Using the minisuperspace approximation in string theory, the authors demonstrate that the spectrum of holographic glueballs in two confining supergravity backgrounds (Maldacena-Nunez and Witten QCD) is compatible with the Wigner surmise for the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), but only for energy levels above a certain threshold where the interaction term becomes important; the lowest-lying eigenvalues instead follow a Poisson distribution.

## Method

The authors implement a minisupersonic approximation for winding strings in two ten-dimensional supergravity backgrounds dual to confining field theories (Maldacena-Nunez and Witten QCD). They reduce the string theory problem to a two-dimensional Schrodinger-like eigenvalue equation in the coordinates (R, r) and solve it numerically using spectral decomposition with Hermite functions as basis states, exploiting parity symmetry in the R direction. The resulting eigenvalue spectrum is analyzed by computing the nearest-neighbor level spacing distribution P(s) and comparing it to the Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- The root mean square (RMS) between the Wigner distribution and the numerical data is below 10^{-3} when the distribution is normalized so the sum is one, for both backgrounds.
- The spectrum spans approximately 400 energy levels in the chosen energy range above the small-interaction regime.
- The numerical calculation yields approximately 1500 reliable eigenvalues for the Maldacena-Nunez background and 1000 for the Witten QCD background.
- The parameter w = alpha/(pi alpha') is set to 1, with grid sizes N_R = N_rho ranging from 20 to 160.

## Context

This work extends the connection between Random Matrix Theory and hadronic spectra, originally proposed by Wigner in the 1950s and later linked to quantum chaos by Bohigas (1983). The authors build on experimental analyses by Pascalutsa (2002) showing that the nearest-neighbor mass-spacing distribution of meson and baryon spectra follows the Wigner surmise, and on lattice studies by Markum (2005) and Bittner (2004) finding similar results for the Dirac operator eigenvalues. The paper provides a holographic realization of these observations using the gauge/gravity correspondence, and the results motivate further studies of spectral properties not dictated by integrability in string theory models of confinement.
