# Few-Body Lattice Calculations

> I discuss the recent progress toward computing few-body observables using numerical lattice techniques. The focus is overwhelmingly on the latest results from lattice QCD calculations. I present preliminary results from a lattice calculation of the central and tensor potentials between B-mesons in the heavy-quark limit.

# Few-Body Lattice Calculations

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of few-body observables are becoming feasible when combined with effective field theory, but rigorous results for nucleon-nucleon scattering are currently limited to pion masses above the physical value, and the scattering lengths at those masses are of natural size rather than the unnaturally large values seen in nature.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of single-nucleon properties (axial coupling and neutron-proton mass difference), two-hadron scattering via the Lüscher finite-volume method (for ππ, Kπ, and NN systems), and a quenched lattice calculation of the central and tensor potentials between B-mesons in the heavy-quark limit. The NN scattering analysis uses the complete finite-volume relation rather than the asymptotic Lüscher formula, because the scattering lengths at accessible pion masses are comparable to the lattice spatial dimensions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton mass difference from strong isospin breaking only: M_n - M_p |^{m_d-m_u} = +2.26 ± 0.57 ± 0.42 ± 0.10 MeV, compared with the Cottingham sum-rule estimate of +2.05 ± 0.3 MeV
- The ππ scattering length in the I=2 channel was computed at a single lattice spacing of b ~ 0.125 fm, with a chiral extrapolation uncertainty at the physical point smaller than the experimental value
- The Kπ scattering length a_{K+π+} was computed at the same lattice spacing b ~ 0.125 fm at three different pion masses, enabling extraction of both isospin channels
- The tensor potential between two infinitely massive B-mesons is consistent with zero within uncertainty, with an upper bound V_T ≤ 40 MeV
- The B-meson potential calculation used a quark mass giving m_π ~ 420 MeV and m_ρ ~ 700 MeV, with a lattice spacing approximately half that of previous calculations by Pennanen (1999) and Cook (2002)

## Context

This paper reviews the program of connecting lattice QCD to nuclear observables through a hierarchy of effective field theories, extending the work of Beane (2003) on NN scattering and the NPLQCD collaboration's mixed-action calculations. The B-meson potential results provide qualitative insight into the NN system because the chiral potential form is identical, differing only in counterterm values. The next steps require developing three-body analogues of the Lüscher relation and performing contractions for the triton (2880 contractions versus 48 for pp), which will become feasible with asymmetric Clover lattices from Jefferson Laboratory.
