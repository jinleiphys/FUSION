# Kinetic and Chemical Equilibration of Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We solve a leading-order QCD kinetic theory with light quarks and gluon degrees of freedom to study the non-equilibrium dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). By including both elastic and inelastic scatterings for quarks and gluon, the model is proficient to describe kinetic and chemical equilibration of the QGP, and thus connects the initial (semi-) hard production of partons at early times with the hydrodynamic description of a near-thermalized quark-gluon plasma after the first fm/c of the collision. Within this approach, we investigate the time scales and mechanisms for kinetic and chemical equilibration of the QGP at zero and non-zero net-baryon density and elaborate on the connections to jet quenching physics and hydrodynamics.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a leading-order QCD kinetic theory with both elastic and inelastic scatterings for gluons and light quarks can describe kinetic and chemical equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma at zero and non-zero net-baryon density, with chemical equilibration occurring on roughly the same time scale as kinetic equilibration but slightly later.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for gluons and light quarks (u, d, s and their antiparticles) using a leading-order QCD kinetic theory that includes elastic 2→2 scatterings and effective 1→2 inelastic processes (gluon/quark radiation and absorption). The collision integrals incorporate HTL-constrained thermal masses for screening and account for the Bethe-Heitler and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal regimes. Systems are studied both in static boxes and under boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, with initial conditions spanning over-occupied (low-momentum gluon dominated), under-occupied (high-momentum mini-jet dominated), and near-thermal configurations.

## Key numbers
- Chemical equilibration time scale is approximately 2τ_R, where τ_R = 4πη/s / T_eq, with T_eq the final equilibrium temperature and η/s the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.
- For coupling λ=10 (η/s ≈ 1) and λ=1 (η/s ≈ 35), energy densities of each species approach equilibrium values at ≥ 2τ_R.
- In over-occupied systems, screening masses scale as m_D² ∼ t^{-2/7}, effective temperature T* ∼ t^{-3/7}, and average momentum ⟨p⟩ ∼ t^{-1/7} at coupling λ=0.1.
- For expanding systems with longitudinal expansion, chemical equilibration occurs at dimensionless time ω̃ ≈ 2, where ω̃ = τ T_eff / (4πη/s) and T_eff ∝ e^{1/4}.
- Over-occupied systems studied with initial average momentum ⟨p⟩_0/T_eq = 0.2 at coupling λ=0.1.
- Under-occupied systems studied with initial separation of scale ⟨p⟩_0/T_eq = 3 at coupling λ=1.
- Finite density systems studied with chemical potentials μ_eq/T_eq ranging from 0 to 2.5.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of kinetic equilibration in pure Yang-Mills theory (Kurkela 2018) by including quark and gluon degrees of freedom to capture full non-equilibrium QCD dynamics. It builds on the effective kinetic theory of QCD (Arnold 2002) and connects to the "bottom-up thermalization" pattern (Baier 2000) for under-occupied systems. The framework enables extending pre-equilibrium descriptions like KøMPøST (Kurkela 2018) to full QCD and provides a bridge between far-from-equilibrium initial states and hydrodynamic initial conditions for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
