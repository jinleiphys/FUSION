# Odd-even mass differences in self-consistent Mean-Field calculations

> The odd-even mass staggering (OES) of nuclei is analyzed in the context of self-consistent mean-field calculations. The procedure developed allows to understand the OES for spherical as well as for deformed nuclei. Comparison with results at the Hartree-Fock level shows the non-perturbative effect on this observable of the inclusion of pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering of the three-point mass formula \(\Delta^{(3)}\) arises primarily from the smooth part of the binding energy (the "HFBE" core), not from pairing or deformation effects; the five-point formula \(\Delta^{(5)}\) is the correct quantity to isolate the pairing contribution, contrary to earlier claims that deformation alone could explain the staggering at the Hartree-Fock level.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations with approximate particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami prescription, including time-odd components of the force for odd nuclei. For each odd nucleus, they computed two energies: the full HFB energy (with blocking and time-reversal symmetry breaking) and a "HFBE" energy (the HFB vacuum constrained to an odd average particle number, as if the nucleus were even). They then compared the three-point (\(\Delta^{(3)}\)) and five-point (\(\Delta^{(5)}\)) mass formulas for spherical tin isotopes (\({}^{100}\)Sn to \({}^{169}\)Sn) and deformed cerium isotopes (\({}^{118}\)Ce to \({}^{166}\)Ce), and contrasted their results with pure Hartree-Fock calculations following Satula et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

- For the tin isotopic chain, \(\Delta^{(3)}_{HFBE}(N) \gg \Delta^{(5)}_{HFBE}(N) \approx 0\) except at the magic number \(N=82\), confirming the smoothness of the HFBE energy.
- The difference \(\Delta^{(3)}_{HFB}(N) - \Delta^{(5)}_{HFB}(N)\) agrees with \(\Delta^{(3)}_{HFBE}(N)\) "along the whole isotopic line" (except at \(N=82\)), with the agreement described as "impressive".
- For cerium isotopes, the ground-state quadrupole deformation varies from \(\beta_2 \approx 0.37\) near \({}^{118}\)Ce, through spherical \({}^{140}\)Ce, to \(\beta_2 \approx 0.31\) near \({}^{160}\)Ce.
- The difference between \(\Delta^{(3)}_{HFB}\) and the Hartree-Fock staggering for cerium isotopes exceeds 200 keV for most nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of odd-even mass staggering beyond the Hartree-Fock study of Satula et al. (1998), who attributed the staggering to deformation (Jahn-Teller effect) and proposed that \(\Delta^{(3)}_{odd}\) measures pairing while \(\Delta^{(3)}_{even}\) contains a deformation contribution. The present paper shows that the staggering is instead due to the smooth part of the energy and that \(\Delta^{(5)}\) isolates the pairing+polarization contribution, consistent with earlier suggestions by Bendjaballah et al. The results enable a cleaner extraction of pairing gaps from experimental mass data and set the stage for future work incorporating beyond-mean-field correlations from symmetry restoration, as in methods by Valor et al. and Rodriguez et al.
