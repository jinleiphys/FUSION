# The $^{19}$N($n$,$γ)^{20}$N capture rate in light of the probable bubble nature of $^{20}$N

> We aim to explore the bubble nature of the exotic nucleus $^{20}$N within the microscopic antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) approach. Constraining its structural parameters, we analyse its static properties. Subsequently, we use the AMD infused finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation theory to calculate the Coulomb breakup of $^{20}$N as an indirect approach to estimate the $^{19}$N$(n,γ)^{20}$N radiative capture rate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the bubble nature of \(^{20}\)N, characterized by a central depletion in its matter density due to the dominant occupation of the \(d_{5/2}\) orbital by valence neutrons, is crucial for accurately determining the \(^{19}\)N(\(n,\gamma\))\(^{20}\)N radiative capture rate; the abstract glosses over the condition that this bubble structure leads to a negligibly small spectroscopic factor for the \(d_{3/2}\) orbit, effectively making the \(d_{5/2}\) state the sole contributor to the reaction rate.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method to describe the ground states of \(^{19}\)N and \(^{20}\)N, employing the Gogny D1S density functional. The valence neutron wave functions were extracted from the overlap of the AMD wave functions for the two nuclei and were corrected for proper asymptotic behavior at large distances. These wave functions were then used as input to a fully quantum mechanical finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory to calculate the Coulomb breakup of \(^{20}\)N on a \(^{208}\)Pb target at 256 MeV/u beam energy, from which the photodisintegration cross section and, via detailed balance, the radiative capture cross section and reaction rate were derived.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energy (\(S_n\)) of \(^{20}\)N is 2.16 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor (\(C^2S\)) for the \(1/2^-\otimes d_{5/2}\) channel is 0.27, while for the \(1/2^-\otimes d_{3/2}\) channel it is negligibly small at 0.015.
- The diffuseness parameter for \(^{20}\)N is 0.51 fm for the harmonic oscillator (HO) model and 0.53 fm for the AMD model (in the \(d_{5/2}\)-state).
- The channel radii for the tail correction are 3.8 fm for the \(d_{5/2}\) orbit and 4.1 fm for the \(d_{3/2}\) orbit.
- The matter distribution radius (\(R_m\)) for \(^{20}\)N calculated by AMD is 2.87 fm, compared to experimental values of 2.84(5) fm (bagchi2019) and 2.81(4) fm (OBC01NPA).

## Context

This work extends the study of bubble nuclei, previously investigated in systems like \(^{34}\)Si (Mutaz et al. 2017) and \(^{22}\)O (Choudhary et al. 2020), to the neutron-rich isotope \(^{20}\)N. It provides a microscopic structure input from AMD to the FRDWBA reaction theory, enabling a more realistic estimate of the \(^{19}\)N(\(n,\gamma\))\(^{20}\)N capture rate than statistical models (Rauscher et al. 1994). The results are compared with experimental Coulomb dissociation data (CD20N) and are relevant for determining the final abundance of fluorine isotopes in the neutrino-wind driven model of \(r\)-process nucleosynthesis.
