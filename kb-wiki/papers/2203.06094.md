# Collectivity in Ultra-Peripheral Pb+Pb Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We present the first full (3+1)D dynamical simulations of ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Extrapolating from p+Pb collisions, we explore whether a quasi-real photon $γ^*$ interacting with the lead nucleus in an ultra-peripheral collision can create a many-body system exhibiting fluid behavior. Assuming strong final-state interactions, we provide model results for charged hadron multiplicity, identified particle mean transverse momenta, and charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients, and compare them with experimental data from the ALICE and ATLAS collaborations. The elliptic flow hierarchy between p+Pb and $γ^*$+Pb collisions is dominated by the difference in longitudinal flow decorrelations and reproduces the experimental data well. We have demonstrated that our theoretical framework provides a quantitative tool to study particle production and collectivity for all system sizes, ranging from central heavy-ion collisions to small asymmetric collision systems at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider and even at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full (3+1)D dynamical simulations of ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, showing that the elliptic flow hierarchy between p+Pb and photon+Pb collisions is dominated by differences in longitudinal flow decorrelations rather than initial transverse geometry, assuming strong final-state interactions are the dominant source of collectivity.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid framework combining a Monte-Carlo 3D Glauber initial-state model with (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) and hadronic transport (UrQMD). The virtual photon in ultra-peripheral collisions is treated as a vector meson with fluctuating center-of-mass energy sampled from a probability distribution, and the model is calibrated using p+Pb collision data at 5.02 TeV before making predictions for photon+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The center-of-mass energy of photon+Pb collisions fluctuates from 0 up to approximately 894 GeV for Pb+Pb ultra-peripheral collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- The specific shear viscosity is set to eta T/(e+P) = 0.08 and the width of strings in the transverse plane to sigma_x = 0.4 fm to describe p+Pb elliptic flow data
- The rapidity shift for photon+Pb collisions is Delta y = 1.725 for sqrt(s_gamma*N) = 894 GeV, compared to Delta y = 0.465 for p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV
- The charged hadron pseudorapidity distribution in photon+Pb collisions is reproduced within 10% of ATLAS data
- The identified particle mean p_T of pions and protons in p+Pb collisions is overestimated by 10%
- The v_2(p_T) in photon+Pb collisions in the 20 < N_ch < 60 multiplicity bin is 10-15% smaller than the p+Pb v_2(p_T) across all p_T values
- Increasing the photon virtuality Q^2 from 0.04 GeV^2 to 0.25 GeV^2 causes the v_2(p_T) in photon+Pb collisions to decrease monotonically by about 30%

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework previously applied to proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions (Shen 2022) to the new regime of ultra-peripheral collisions, following recent ATLAS measurements (ATLAS 2021) that showed collective phenomena in photo-nuclear interactions. The results demonstrate that full (3+1)D simulations are necessary for asymmetric systems where boost-invariance is strongly broken, and provide a theoretical bridge to future Electron-Ion Collider measurements where photon virtuality can be used as a dial to control system size.
