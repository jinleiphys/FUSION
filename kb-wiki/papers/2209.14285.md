# Polarized gluon distribution in the proton from holographic light-front QCD

> We obtain the gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the proton within the extended light-front holographic QCD framework, where the proton couples with the spin-two Pomeron in Anti-de Sitter space, together with constraints imposed by the Veneziano model. The gluon helicity asymmetry, after satisfying the perturbative QCD constraints at small and large longitudinal momentum regions, agrees with existing experimental measurements. The polarized gluon distribution is consistent with global analyses. We predict the gluon helicity contribution to the proton spin, $ΔG=0.221^{+0.056}_{-0.044}$, close to the recent analysis with updated data sets and PHENIX measurement and the lattice QCD simulations. We subsequently present the unpolarized and polarized gluon generalized parton distributions in the proton.

# Polarized gluon distribution in the proton from holographic light-front QCD

## Key claim
The gluon helicity contribution to the proton spin is predicted to be ΔG = 0.221^{+0.056}_{-0.044} at the hadronic scale μ² ∼ 1 GeV², but this result depends on fitting the Regge intercept for the polarized Pomeron trajectory to experimental data on the gluon helicity asymmetry ratio, with the central value yielding χ² per d.o.f. of 1.5.

## Method
The authors extend the light-front holographic QCD (HLFQCD) framework by coupling the proton to a spin-two Pomeron in Anti-de Sitter space, using the Veneziano model to express gluon gravitational form factors in terms of Euler Beta functions. The unpolarized gluon PDF is derived from the gluon gravitational form factor using a universal reparametrization function w(x) with parameter a = 0.48 ± 0.04, while the polarized gluon PDF is obtained by replacing the unpolarized Pomeron intercept α_P(0) ≃ 1.08 with a fitted polarized intercept \widetilde{α}_P(0) = 0–0.16, keeping the universal slope α'_P ≃ 0.25 GeV⁻². The gluon helicity asymmetry ratio Δg(x)/g(x) is then constrained by perturbative QCD boundary conditions at x → 0 and x → 1 and fitted to experimental data from COMPASS, HERMES, and SMC.

## Key numbers
- Gluon helicity contribution to proton spin: ΔG = 0.221^{+0.056}_{-0.044} at scale μ² ∼ 1 GeV²
- Unpolarized Pomeron intercept: α_P(0) ≃ 1.08; polarized Pomeron intercept fitted range: \widetilde{α}_P(0) = 0–0.16, with central value 0.08
- Pomeron Regge slope: α'_P ≃ 0.25 GeV⁻²
- Reparametrization function parameter: a = 0.48 ± 0.04
- Coefficient for leading twist-4 Fock state: c_{τ=4} = 0.225 ± 0.014
- χ² per d.o.f. for helicity asymmetry fit at central intercept: 1.5
- Comparison values: ΔG = 0.23(6) for x_g ∈ [0.05, 0.2] from NNPDFpol1.0 (Nocera 2014); ΔG = 0.19(6) for x_g ∈ [0.05, 1] from de Florian (2014); lattice QCD gives ΔG = 0.251(47)(16) at μ² = 10 GeV² (Yang 2016)

## Context
This work extends the HLFQCD framework previously applied to quark distributions in the nucleon and pion (de Teramond 2018, Liu 2019) and to unpolarized gluon distributions (de Teramond 2021) by now computing polarized gluon distributions and the gluon helicity contribution to the proton spin. The predicted ΔG value is close to the recent analysis with updated data sets and PHENIX measurement (PHENIX 2008) which yielded ΔG = 0.2 for x_g ∈ [0.02, 0.3], and is consistent with lattice QCD simulations (Yang 2016). The results enable predictions for gluon generalized parton distributions that can be tested at future Electron-Ion Colliders in the USA (Abdul Khalek 2021) and China (Anderle 2021), which aim to measure gluon GPDs through deeply virtual Compton scattering.
