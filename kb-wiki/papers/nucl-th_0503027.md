# Nuclear pasta structures and the charge screening effect

> Non uniform structures of the nucleon matter at subnuclear densities are numerically studied by means of the density functional theory with relativistic mean-fields coupled with the electric field. A particular role of the charge screening effects is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charge screening effects, specifically the spatial rearrangement of protons and electrons due to the Coulomb interaction, significantly modify nuclear pasta structures and their stability, contrary to prior studies that found electron screening to be of minor importance. The authors show that the proton Debye screening length is typically shorter than the droplet size, making proton density rearrangement essential for pasta structures, while electron screening becomes significant only for bubble configurations at high proton fractions.

## Method

The authors used density functional theory with a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, solving coupled equations of motion for meson mean-fields (σ, ω, ρ) and the Coulomb potential self-consistently in the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation. They performed calculations in 3D (spherical), 2D (cylindrical), and 1D (slab) geometries to explore droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble configurations, using the relaxation method to converge field and density profiles under constraints of global charge neutrality and fixed baryon number density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, with minimum energy per baryon −16.3 MeV, incompressibility K(ρ₀) = 240 MeV, effective nucleon mass m_N*(ρ₀) = 0.78 m_N, and symmetry energy coefficient a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- For symmetric matter (Y_p = 0.5) in droplet configuration, the atomic number of the droplet is ≈ 25 in the low-density limit and ≈ 65 at the maximum droplet density ρ_B,d^(max) ≈ 0.025 fm⁻³
- The energy per baryon in non-uniform structures is lower by up to about 15 MeV/A compared to uniform matter
- The surface tension parameter τ_surf ≈ 1.03 MeV/fm² fits liquid-drop binding energies of finite nuclei; pasta structures disappear for τ_surf > 0.3 MeV/fm² in the simplified bulk calculation

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear pasta structures by Rav83, Has84, Wil85, Oya93, Lor93, Cheng97, Mar98, kido00, Gen00, Gen02, and Gen03, which typically discarded the rearrangement effect of charged particle density profiles due to Coulomb interaction. The authors' previous investigations of charge screening in quark-hadron transitions (voskre, voskre1, emaru1) and kaon condensation (maruKaon, dubna) showed that mixed phases may be largely limited by charge screening and surface effects. This study enables more accurate modeling of supernova matter and neutron star crust properties, including neutrino opacity, hydrodynamic behavior, and mechanical properties relevant to neutron star quakes and pulsar glitches.
