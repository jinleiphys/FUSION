# Probe the color screening in proton-nucleus collisions with complex potentials

> Color screening and parton inelastic scattering modify the heavy-quark antiquark potential in the medium that consists of particles from quantum chromodynamics (QCD), leading to suppression of quarkonium production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Due to small charm/anti-charm ($c\bar{c}$) pair production number in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions, the correlation between different $c\bar{c}$ pairs is negligible, which makes the Schrödinger equation viable for tracking the evolution of only one $c\bar{c}$ pair. We employ the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with in-medium $c\bar{c}$ potential to study the evolution of charmonium wave functions in the hydrodynamic like QCD medium produced in pA collisions. We explore different parametrizations of real and imaginary parts of $c\bar{c}$ potential and calculate the nuclear modification factors ($R_{\rm pA}$) of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV energy p-Pb collisions at Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Comparing a strong and a weak screening scenario with experimental data in this approach, we arrive at the conclusion that the color screening is weak at temperature close to deconfined phase transition. Moreover, the imaginary part of the potential is crucial to describe the experimental data which is consistent with widely studied semi-classical approaches where the dissociation rates are essential.

## Key claim

By comparing a strong and a weak color screening scenario against experimental data from p-Pb collisions at LHC, the authors conclude that color screening is weak at temperatures close to the deconfined phase transition, and that the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential (representing inelastic parton scattering) is essential to describe the observed suppression patterns of \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\).

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation in position space using the Crank-Nicolson implicit method to evolve the wave function of a single \(c\bar{c}\) dipole through a hydrodynamically evolving QCD medium produced in p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV. They parametrize the in-medium complex heavy-quark potential using lattice QCD data, with the real part taken either as the vacuum Cornell potential (weak screening) or as the free energy \(F(r,T)\) (strong screening), and the imaginary part fitted to lattice results via Bayesian inference. The final nuclear modification factors \(R_{\rm pA}\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) are obtained by projecting the evolved \(c\bar{c}\) wave functions onto charmonium eigenstates and including feed-down contributions from \(\chi_c\) and \(\psi(2S)\).

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV for p-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Maximum initial temperature of the hot medium: \(T_0({\bf x}_T=0|b=0)=248\) MeV in forward rapidity and \(289\) MeV in backward rapidity.
- Critical temperature of the deconfined phase transition: \(T_c=165\) MeV.
- Hydrodynamic evolution start time: \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/c.
- Numerical time step: \(\Delta t=0.001\) fm/c; radial step: \(\Delta r=0.03\) fm.
- Parameters for the imaginary potential (maximum posterior set): \(a_1=-0.040\), \(a_2=0.50\).
- Cornell potential parameters: \(\alpha=\pi/12\), \(\sigma=0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- Charmonium direct production ratio in pp collisions: \(f_{pp}^{J/\psi}:f_{pp}^{\chi_c}:f_{pp}^{\psi(2S)} = 0.68:1:0.19\).
- Cronin effect parameter: \(a_{gN}=0.15\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.
- Proton charge radius: \(\langle r\rangle_p=0.9\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Schrödinger equation with complex potentials, previously used for bottomonium evolution in heavy-ion collisions (Strickland 2011, Krouppa 2015, Brambilla 2016), to charmonium in the smaller QCD medium produced in proton-nucleus collisions. It contrasts with semi-classical transport models (Chen 2016) and comover models (Ferreiro 2014) that also describe charmonium suppression in pA collisions, but provides a fully quantum treatment of the \(c\bar{c}\) pair evolution. The results enable discrimination between strong and weak color screening scenarios using existing LHC data, and establish that the imaginary part of the potential (representing parton inelastic scattering) is necessary to reproduce the different suppression of \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\).
