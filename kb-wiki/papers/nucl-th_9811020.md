# Coherent QED, Giant Resonances and $(e^{+}e^{-})$ Pairs in High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We show that the coherent oscillations of the e.m. field induced by the collective quantum fluctuations of the nuclear matter field associated with the giant resonances, with frequencies $ω_{A}\simeq 78A^{-{1/3}}$ MeV, give rise to a significant $(e^+e^-)$ pair production in high energy Heavy Ion collisions. The approximate parameterless calculation of such yield is in good agreement with recent experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the excess of low-mass electron-positron pairs observed in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS can be explained by coherent QED processes involving giant resonances of the colliding nuclei, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors present this as an alternative to the widely accepted QGP-based explanation, noting that their parameterless calculation shows "remarkable" agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the electromagnetic field generated by collective quantum fluctuations of nuclear matter associated with giant resonances, which oscillate at frequency ω_A ≈ 78 A^(-1/3) MeV. They compute the coherent photon field surrounding each nucleus as a "photon cloud" within a coherence domain of radius R_CD = π/ω_A, then calculate the electron-positron pair production rate via the photon-photon fusion mechanism (γγ → e⁺e⁻) when these clouds overlap during ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The calculation uses a Gaussian approximation to replace delta-function approximations for the sharply peaked Fourier transforms of the coherent fields.

## Key numbers

- Giant resonance frequency: ω_A ≈ 78 A^(-1/3) MeV, where A is the nuclear mass number
- For sulfur (A=32): ω = 23 MeV, coherence domain radius R_CD = 27 fm
- For gold (A=197): ω = 13.4 MeV, R_CD = 46 fm
- For lead (A=207): ω = 13.1 MeV, R_CD = 47 fm
- The calculation covers electron-positron pair masses in the range 0.2 < M < 1.3 GeV for S-Au collisions and 0.2 < M < 1.8 GeV for Pb-Au collisions
- Beam energies: 200 GeV per nucleon for S-Au collisions, 158 GeV per nucleon for Pb-Au collisions
- The theoretical excess parameter ε_th is compared with experimental excess ε_exp from CERES data (Agakichiev 1995, Agakichiev 1998)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Fire-String model (Angelini 1983, Preparata 1995) and their theory of QED coherence in matter (Preparata 1995) to explain the electron-positron pair excess observed by the CERES collaboration (Agakichiev 1995, Agakichiev 1998) and others (Ullrich 1996). It directly contradicts the interpretation of Li, Ko, and Brown (1996), who attribute the excess to modified vector meson properties in a quark-gluon plasma. The authors argue that their simpler electromagnetic mechanism leaves "essentially no room" for the ππ-annihilation signal expected from QGP, and they suggest this supports the Fire-String picture where hadron formation occurs far from the interaction region.
