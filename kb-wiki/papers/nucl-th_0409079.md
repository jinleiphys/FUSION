# Many-Body Physics on the Border of Nuclear Stability

> A brief overview is given of the Continuum Shell Model, a novel approach that extends the traditional nuclear shell model into the domain of unstable nuclei and nuclear reactions. While some of the theoretical aspects, such as role and treatment of one- and two-nucleon continuum states, are discussed more in detail, a special emphasis is made on relation to observed nuclear properties, including definitions of the decay widths and their relation to the cross sections, especially in the cases of non-exponential decay. For the chain of He isotopes we demonstrate the agreement of theoretical results with recent experimental data. We show how the interplay of internal collectivity and coherent coupling to continuum gives rise to the universal mechanism of creating pigmy giant resonances.

## Key claim

The Continuum Shell Model (CSM) extends the traditional nuclear shell model into the domain of unstable nuclei and nuclear reactions by consistently combining bound states and continuum, but the formalism's definitions of resonance energies and widths (Breit-Wigner, Gamow, or time-delay) agree only when resonance regions do not overlap and the energy dependence of the effective Hamiltonian is weak.

## Method

The authors project the many-body Hilbert space into an internal space P (shell-model bound states) and an external space Q (states with one or two nucleons in the continuum), then eliminate Q to obtain an energy-dependent, non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian acting within P. The coupling amplitudes between P and Q are computed from a Woods-Saxon mean field and a schematic two-body interaction, and the scattering matrix is constructed via matrix inversion rather than diagonalization. For the helium isotope chain, the model is applied assuming the Hermitian part of the effective Hamiltonian is given by a phenomenological shell-model Hamiltonian, with Q-space limited to one- and two-particle continuum states.

## Key numbers

* For single-particle decay widths far from thresholds, the width scales as Gamma(epsilon) ~ epsilon^(l+1/2), where epsilon is the energy above threshold and l is the partial wave of the decay channel.
* For sequential two-body decay, the overall width behaves as Gamma ~ E_k^(2+l+l'), where E_k is the total available kinetic energy.
* For direct two-body decay, the amplitude a^(L)(epsilon_1, epsilon_2) is parameterized near thresholds with an energy dependence reflecting the phase space kinematics of the free-body final state.

## Context

This work extends the traditional nuclear shell model into the domain of unstable nuclei by incorporating continuum coupling, building on the projection method of Feshbach (1958) and the Gamow quasistationary state formalism of Gamow (1928), Siegert (1939), and Berggren (1968, 1996). The CSM approach is contrasted with the alternative Gamow Shell Model of Michel (2003), which solves a non-Hermitian eigenvalue problem in the complex energy plane. The formalism enables direct calculation of cross sections for comparison with experiment, particularly for weakly bound nuclei such as the helium isotope chain, and provides a framework for understanding the universal mechanism of pygmy giant resonances through the interplay of internal collectivity and coherent coupling to the continuum.
