# Sensitivity of the evaporation residue observables to the symmetry energy

> The static properties of the heaviest residue and unbound particles produced in central $^{64,58}Ni$ on $^{64,58}Ni$ collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon are predicted within the BUU transport model, in order to explore the sensitivity of those observables to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. We include fluctuations in the collision integral and use a meta-modelling for the mean-field which allows an independent variation of the different empirical parameters of the equation of state. We find that the isospin ratio of pre-equilibrium particles is a good estimator of the stiffness of the symmetry energy, in agreement with previous works. In addition to that, whatever be the functional form of the equation of state, we show that a higher symmetry energy at subsaturation densities leads to an increased size and isotopic ratio for the heaviest residue. This is understood in terms of energy sharing between the pre-equilibrium particles and the (quasi)fused system. The combination of the two observables might be an interesting tool to constrain the different density dependence below and above saturation, which is linked to the relatively poorly known parameter $K_{sym}$.

## Key claim

The isospin ratio of pre-equilibrium particles is a good estimator of the stiffness of the symmetry energy, and a higher symmetry energy at subsaturation densities leads to an increased size and isotopic ratio for the heaviest residue, but these conclusions are drawn from a transport model that does not couple to a statistical decay afterburner and instead continues the dynamical evolution to an asymptotic time where evaporation dynamics is essentially over.

## Method

Central collisions of $^{64,58}Ni$ on $^{64,58}Ni$ at 50 MeV/nucleon were simulated using the BUU@VECC-McGill transport code with fluctuations included in the collision integral. A meta-modelling for the mean-field was employed, allowing independent variation of the empirical parameters of the equation of state, specifically the symmetry energy parameters $E_{sym}$, $L_{sym}$, and $K_{sym}$, while keeping isoscalar parameters fixed to the Sly5 functional values. The dynamical evolution was run up to 900 fm/c, with freeze-out identified at 150 fm/c and asymptotic time at 500 fm/c based on isotropy ratio, particle emission rates, and N/Z saturation of emitted light particles.

## Key numbers

* For the Sly5 functional at freeze-out time (150 fm/c), the average N/Z of free particles was 0.878 for $^{58}Ni$+$^{58}Ni$, 1.099 for $^{58}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$, and 1.351 for $^{64}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$.
* For the Sly5 functional at asymptotic time (500 fm/c), the average charge of the heaviest residue ($Z_{max}$) was 21.246 for $^{58}Ni$+$^{58}Ni$ and $^{58}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$, and 22.889 for $^{64}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$.
* For the Sly5 functional at asymptotic time, the average mass of the heaviest residue ($A_{max}$) was 46.332 for $^{58}Ni$+$^{58}Ni$, 49.256 for $^{58}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$, and 51.634 for $^{64}Ni$+$^{64}Ni$.
* The varying parameters for the sensitivity study covered $E_{sym}$ from 26.83 to 38.71 MeV, $L_{sym}$ from 29.2 to 122.7 MeV, and $K_{sym}$ from -394 to 213 MeV, with average values of 33.30 MeV, 76.6 MeV, and -3 MeV respectively.
* The freeze-out time was determined to be 150 fm/c and the asymptotic time 500 fm/c, independent of the entrance channel.

## Context

This work extends previous transport studies that used a simplistic monotropic functional form for the symmetry energy (Tsang 2004, Li 2008) by implementing the meta-functional proposed by Margueron (2018) which allows independent variation of empirical parameters and reproduces both relativistic and non-relativistic energy functionals. The results are intended to guide upcoming experiments by the INDRA/FAZIA collaboration at GANIL, and the combination of pre-equilibrium particle isospin ratio with evaporation residue properties may help constrain the poorly known parameter $K_{sym}$, complementing constraints from nuclear structure experiments (Kortelainen 2012, Danielewicz 2014, Centelles 2010).
