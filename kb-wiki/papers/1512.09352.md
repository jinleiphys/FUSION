# Measurements of $D_{S}^{\pm}$-meson production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV in STAR

> We present the first measurement of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_{2}$ of $D_{s}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV with the STAR detector. These results have been compared with those of other open charm mesons and strange mesons to determine how the (possibly) strangeness equilibrated partonic matter affects the $D_{s}$ meson production. We find that the nuclear modification factor of $D_{S}$ are systematically higher than unity and $D^{0}$ $R_{\rm{AA}}$. The ratio $D_{s}/D^{0}$ is shown as a function of transverse momentum for the 10-40$\%$ most central Au+Au collisions and compared with that in $p+p$ collisions obtained from PYTHIA. It is also compared with that in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV by the ALICE experiment. Our measurement indicates a hint of enhancement of $D_{S}$ production in Au+Au collisions with respect to $p+p$ collisions as compared to non-strange $D$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of D_s mesons in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, finding that the D_s R_AA is systematically higher than unity and higher than that of D^0 mesons, but the authors state this enhancement is "not statistically significant" and the measurement "indicates a hint of enhancement" rather than a definitive observation.

## Method

The STAR experiment reconstructed D_s mesons via the decay channel D_s^± → φ (φ → K^+ + K^-) + π^± using approximately 750 million minimum bias events from the 2014 Au+Au run at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors provided particle identification at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1.0), while the Heavy Flavor Tracker with its Monolithic Active Pixel Sensors enabled displaced vertex reconstruction. The nuclear modification factor was calculated using the measured charm cross-section in p+p collisions at √s = 200 GeV with a fragmentation factor of 0.09 ± 0.01 for c → D_s, and elliptic flow was measured using the full event plane method with charged particles in the TPC.

## Key numbers

- D_s R_AA = 2.1 ± 0.5 (stat.) ± 0.7/0.7 (sys.) at p_T = 2.8 GeV/c for 10-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- D_s R_AA = 1.7 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 0.5/0.7 (sys.) at p_T = 3.9 GeV/c for 10-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The fragmentation factor used for c → D_s conversion was 0.09 ± 0.01
- The track pointing resolution of the HFT detector was about 46 μm for 750 MeV/c kaons
- The analysis used about 750 million minimum bias events from the 2014 Au+Au run

## Context

This work extends the study of open charm meson production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies, comparing D_s results with previously published STAR measurements of D^0 mesons and with ALICE measurements in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The results are compared with PYTHIA 6.4 predictions for p+p collisions and with theoretical model calculations predicting enhanced D_s production through charm-quark recombination with thermal strange quarks in deconfined matter. The limited statistics prevent firm conclusions, but the authors note that higher statistics data to be collected in 2016 with improved HFT performance will enable more precise measurements of D_s production and elliptic flow.
