# Quasi-particle interaction in nuclear matter from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> Based on a recent chiral approach to nuclear matter we calculate the in-medium interaction of nucleons at the Fermi surface $|\vec p_{1,2}|=k_f$. The isotropic part of this quasi-particle interaction is characterized by four density dependent (dimensionful) Fermi-liquid parameters: $f_0(k_f), f_0'(k_f), g_0(k_f)$ and $g_0'(k_f)$. In the approximation to $1π$-exchange and iterated $1π$-exchange (which as such leads already to a good nuclear matter equation of state) we find a spin-isospin interaction strength of $g_0'(2m_π) = 1.14 $fm$^2$, compatible with existing empirical values. In the next step we include systematically the contributions from $2π$-exchange with virtual $Δ(1232) $-isobar excitation which have been found important for good single-particle properties and spin-stability of nuclear matter. Without any additional short distance terms the spin-dependent Fermi-liquid parameters $g_0(k_{f0})$ and $g'_0(k_{f0})$ come out far too large. Estimates of these short-distance parameters from realistic NN-potentials go in the right direction, but sizeable enhancement factors are still needed to reproduce the empirical values of $g_0(k_{f0})$ and $g_0'(k_{f0})$. This points towards the importance of higher order iterations subsumed in the induced interaction. We consider also the tensor part of the quasi-nucleon interaction at the Fermi surface. In comparison to the leading $1π$-exchange tensor interaction we find from the $2π$-exchange corrections almost a doubling of the isoscalar tensor strength $h_0(k_f)$, whereas the isovector tensor strength $h_0'(k_f)$ is much less affected. These features are not changed by the inclusion of the chiral $πNΔ$-dynamics.

# Quasi-particle interaction in nuclear matter from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

## Key claim
The chiral approach to nuclear matter based on one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange yields a spin-isospin interaction strength of g'_0(2m_π) = 1.14 fm² compatible with empirical values, but the inclusion of two-pion exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation produces spin-dependent Fermi-liquid parameters g_0(k_f0) and g'_0(k_f0) that are far too large without additional short-distance terms, requiring sizeable enhancement factors from higher-order iterations to match empirical values.

## Method
The isotropic part of the quasi-particle interaction at the Fermi surface |p₁,₂| = k_f was calculated from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics using tree-level and one-loop pion-exchange diagrams, including contributions from one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, medium modifications, and two-pion exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation. The four density-dependent Fermi-liquid parameters f_0(k_f), f'_0(k_f), g_0(k_f), and g'_0(k_f) were extracted from the angle-averaged quasi-particle interaction, with short-distance contributions parameterized by contact terms and subtraction constants adjusted to empirical nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers
- Spin-isospin interaction strength g'_0(2m_π) = 1.14 fm² from the approximation to 1π-exchange and iterated 1π-exchange, decomposing into 0.13 fm² from static 1π-exchange, 0.99 fm² from iterated 1π-exchange, and 0.02 fm² from medium modified terms
- At saturation density k_f0 = 261.6 MeV with Δ(1232)-isobar contributions included: f_0(k_f0) = 0.20 fm², f'_0(k_f0) = 1.30 fm², g_0(k_f0) = 2.7 fm² and g'_0(k_f0) = 3.0 fm² when short-distance parameters B_5^σ and B_5^{στ} are set to zero
- Without Δ-isobar contributions at saturation density k_f = 2m_π: f_0(2m_π) = 0.55 fm², g_0(2m_π) = -1.44 fm², f'_0(2m_π) = 2.01 fm², g'_0(2m_π) = 1.14 fm²
- Nuclear matter saturation point from 1π-exchange and iterated 1π-exchange with cut-off Λ = 611 MeV: ρ_0 = 0.173 fm⁻³, Ē(k_f0) = -15.3 MeV, compressibility K = 252 MeV, asymmetry energy A(2m_π) = 38.9 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass M* = 0.88 M at saturation density when Δ-isobar contributions are included, compared to M* ≈ 2.9 M without them
- p-wave Landau parameter f_1(k_f0) ≈ -0.4 fm² at saturation density with Δ-isobar contributions

## Context
This work extends the chiral approach to nuclear matter developed by Lutz (2000) and Kaiser (2002) by calculating the in-medium quasi-particle interaction at the Fermi surface, building on the Fermi-liquid theory of Landau (1957) as applied to nuclear matter by Migdal (1967). The results are compared to empirical Fermi-liquid parameters compiled by Backman (1979), Speth (1998), and Schwenk (2003), who employed the renormalization-group motivated low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V_low-k. The finding that higher-order iterations subsumed in the induced interaction are needed to reproduce empirical spin-dependent parameters points toward the importance of the particle-hole parquet diagram approach used by Schwenk (2003), while the role of ρ-meson exchange emphasized by Brown (1988) may also be relevant for the short-distance dynamics.
