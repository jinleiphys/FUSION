# A Dynamical Study of Fusion Hindrance with Nakajima-Zwanzig Projection Method

> A new framework is proposed for the study of collisions between very heavy ions which lead to the synthesis of Super-Heavy Elements (SHE), to address the fusion hindrance phenomenon. The dynamics of the reaction is studied in terms of collective degrees of freedom undergoing relaxation processes with different time scales. The Nakajima-Zwanzig projection operator method is employed to eliminate fast variable and derive a dynamical equation for the reduced system with only slow variables. There, the time evolution operator is renormalised and an inhomogeneous term appears, which represents a propagation of the given initial distribution. The term results in a slip to the initial values of the slow variables. We expect that gives a dynamical origin of parameter "injection point $s$" introduced by Swiatecki et al in order to reproduce absolute values of measured cross sections for SHE. Formula for the slip is given in terms of physical parameters of the system, which confirms the results recently obtained with a Langevin equation.

## Key claim

The Nakajima-Zwanzig projection operator method, applied to the Smoluchowski equation for heavy-ion fusion dynamics, shows that eliminating the fast neck degree of freedom produces a slip in the initial values of the slow variables (radial and mass-asymmetry coordinates), providing a dynamical origin for the phenomenological "injection point parameter s" introduced by Swiatecki et al. to reproduce measured cross sections for superheavy element synthesis.

## Method

The authors start from an N-dimensional Smoluchowski equation describing the time evolution of collective coordinates (neck, radial, and mass-asymmetry variables) for a di-nucleus system. They apply the Nakajima-Zwanzig projection operator method to eliminate the fast neck variable, assuming a parabolic approximation of the liquid-drop model potential and weak coupling between fast and slow variables. The resulting reduced dynamical equation contains a renormalized Smoluchowski operator for the slow variables and an inhomogeneous term that, after integration over the fast relaxation time, yields an effective shift of the initial conditions for the slow variables.

## Key numbers

* For the system 209Bi + 70Zn, the estimated slip for the radial variable is about 2.3 fm, which explains the phenomenological injection point parameter s of about 2 fm used in the Fusion-by-Diffusion model.
* The relaxation time of the fast neck degree is governed by the factor exp(-mu_f * c_f * t), where mu_f is the inverse friction for the neck and c_f is the curvature of the potential in the neck coordinate.
* The formation probability is given by P_form approx (1/(2*sqrt(pi))) * sqrt(T/V_R) * exp(-V_R/T), where V_R is the effective barrier height evaluated with the slipped initial point.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based analysis of Boilley et al. (2019) by using the Fokker-Planck formalism to study fusion hindrance in superheavy element synthesis. It provides a dynamical justification for the injection point parameter s introduced phenomenologically by Swiatecki et al. (2003, 2005) in the Fusion-by-Diffusion model, which was needed to reproduce absolute cross sections that were otherwise overestimated by several orders of magnitude. The approach enables systematic predictions for different incident channels (both cold and hot fusion paths) and can be extended to study quasi-fission dynamics and other aspects of heavy-ion collisions.
