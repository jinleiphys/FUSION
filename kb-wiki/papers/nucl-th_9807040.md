# Chiral dynamics of the nuclear equation of state

> We present a new chiral power expansion scheme for the nuclear equation of state. The scheme is effective in the sense that it is constructed to work around nuclear saturation density. The leading and subleading terms are evaluated and are shown to provide an excellent equation of state. As a further application we considered the chiral quark condensate in nuclear matter. Already at nuclear saturation density we predict a substantially smaller reduction of the condensate as compared to conventional approaches.

# Chiral dynamics of the nuclear equation of state

## Key claim

The paper presents a new chiral power expansion scheme for the nuclear equation of state that is constructed to work around nuclear saturation density, rather than at low density. The scheme predicts a substantially smaller reduction of the chiral quark condensate at nuclear saturation density compared to conventional approaches, but only when pionic retardation effects are included.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian with density-dependent two-nucleon couplings \(g_0(k_F)\) and \(g_1(k_F)\), then evaluates the leading (chiral order \(Q^6\)) and subleading (chiral order \(Q^7\)) contributions to the energy per particle of isospin symmetric nuclear matter. The scheme expands the equation of state around the saturation Fermi momentum \(\bar k_F\) rather than in the ratio \(k_F/m_\pi\), and the coupling functions are adjusted to reproduce saturation properties. The chiral quark condensate is then extracted via the Feynman-Hellman theorem from the pion-mass dependence of the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Saturation Fermi momentum: \(\bar k_F \simeq 265\) MeV, reproducing the empirical saturation density.
- Binding energy: 16 MeV per nucleon at saturation, matching the empirical value.
- Incompressibility: \(\kappa \simeq 234\) MeV, compatible with the empirical value \((210 \pm 30)\) MeV of Blaizot.
- Coupling combination: \(g_0 + g_1 \simeq 2.7\) in the relativistic scheme.
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\Sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 45\) MeV used in the condensate calculation.
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi \simeq 93\) MeV; pion mass: \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV; axial coupling: \(g_A \simeq 1.2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Lutz) to apply chiral power counting directly to nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, overcoming problems with regularization and retardation in the potential approach of Weinberg and of Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck. The scheme is contrasted with the heavy-mass formulation of chiral perturbation theory (Manohar) and with the relativistic approach of Gasser. The result for the quark condensate differs from predictions based on the leading sigma-term term alone (Drukarev, Lutz, Cohen) and from Brueckner and Dirac-Brueckner approaches (Li, Brockmann) that do not include pionic retardation effects systematically. The work enables improved QCD sum rule calculations for hadron properties in nuclear matter and motivates a joint publication on nuclear Landau parameters.
