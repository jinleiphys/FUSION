# Space-Time Picture of Baryon Stopping in the Color-Glass Condensate

> We discuss baryon stopping in the Color Glass Condensate description of high energy scattering. We consider the scattering of a distribution of valence quarks on an ultra-relativistic sheet of colored charge. We compute the distribution of scattered quarks from a composite projectile, and calculate the baryon currents before and after the collisions and on an event by event basis. We obtain simple analytic estimates of the baryon number compression and rapidity shifts, which in the idealized case of plane wave scattering, produce results that agree with considerations of Anishetty-Koehler-McLerran.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that baryon stopping in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, yielding analytic estimates for baryon number compression and rapidity shifts that agree with the earlier Anishetty-Koehler-McLerran (AKM) results, but with the crucial difference that the typical transverse momentum scale is the saturation momentum of the projectile nucleus rather than the QCD scale.

## Method

The authors formulate the scattering of a distribution of valence quarks (the projectile) on an ultra-relativistic sheet of colored charge (the target nucleus) using classical Yang-Mills equations and the Dirac equation in the CGC formalism. They compute the fermion field solution before and after crossing the light-cone using the eikonal approximation, then evaluate the baryon current by expressing the hadronic matrix elements in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the Wigner transform of the dipole scattering amplitude. For a composite projectile, they integrate over the nucleon momentum distribution and the parton distribution functions to obtain the space-time evolution of the baryon density.

## Key numbers

* For a single quark scattering, the rapidity shift is given by \( \langle y \rangle_{\yp>0} - \langle y \rangle_{\yp<0} = \frac{1}{2} \log \frac{Q_s^2}{m_T^2} \), where \( Q_s \) is the saturation momentum and \( m_T^2 = \Pt^2 + m_q^2 \) is the transverse mass of the projectile.
* The baryon density gets compressed by a factor \( \frac{Q_s}{m_T} \), i.e. \( \langle n \rangle_{\yp>0} = \frac{Q_s}{m_T} \langle n \rangle_{\yp<0} \).
* The \( j^- \) component of the current after the collision is enhanced relative to before the collision by \( \frac{Q_s^2}{m_T^2} \), while the \( j^+ \) component remains unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the early baryon stopping analysis of Anishetty-Koehler-McLerran (1980) by placing it within the modern CGC framework, which allows treatment of spatial fluctuations and provides a dynamical origin for the compression factor. The paper generalizes previous momentum-space studies of quark production in dilute-dense collisions by authors such as Martinez (2018), Dumitru (2002, 2005), and Mehtar-Tani (2008, 2009) to include space-time dynamics of the baryon density. The results enable future calculations of gluon production and space-time evolution in the fragmentation region, and may ultimately be generalized to describe the transition between baryon stopping and baryon separation at non-asymptotic energies as discussed by Shen (2017).
