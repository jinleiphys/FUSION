# The d'-Dibaryon in the Nonrelativistic Quark Model

> The narrow peak recently found in various pionic double charge exchange (DCX) cross sections can be explained by the assumption of a universal resonance at 2065 MeV, called d'. We calculate the mass of a six-quark system with J^P=0^-, T=0 quantum numbers employing a cluster model and a shell model basis to diagonalize the nonrelativistic quark model Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic quark model (NRQM) calculation of the d' dibaryon mass yields values typically 300-400 MeV above the experimentally required resonance energy of 2065 MeV, but a weaker confinement strength in the six-quark system (as suggested by an inverse proportionality to quark number) can bring the calculated mass down to approximately 2063 MeV, close to the experimental value.

## Method

The authors calculated the mass of a six-quark system with J^P = 0^- and T = 0 quantum numbers using two complementary approaches: a quark cluster model employing the Resonating Group Method (RGM) with a diquark-tetraquark basis, and a translationally invariant shell model (TISM) with N=1 and N=3 oscillator configurations. Both calculations diagonalized a nonrelativistic quark model Hamiltonian that included a harmonic oscillator confinement potential, one-gluon exchange, and regularized one-pion and one-sigma exchange potentials between constituent quarks, with parameters fixed by reproducing the N(939) and Delta(1232) masses.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally observed resonance (d') has a mass of 2065 MeV and a width in medium of approximately 5 MeV, seen in pionic double charge exchange at incident pion energy T_pi = 50 MeV and forward angle Theta = 5 degrees.
- With the full chiral interaction (Set I), the cluster model gives M_d' = 2440 MeV with size b_6 = 0.76 fm; the shell model with N=1 only gives 2484 MeV (b_6 = 0.78 fm); with N=1 and N=3 configuration mixing it gives 2413 MeV (b_6 = 0.78 fm).
- Without pion and sigma exchange (Set II), the cluster model gives M_d' = 2634 MeV (b_6 = 0.70 fm); the shell model with configuration mixing gives 2553 MeV (b_6 = 0.73 fm).
- With reduced confinement strength a_c = 5.0 MeV/fm^2 in the six-quark system (Set III), the cluster model gives M_d' = 2111 MeV (b_6 = 0.95 fm); the shell model with configuration mixing gives 2063 MeV (b_6 = 0.96 fm).
- The constituent quark mass used is m_q = 313 MeV; the harmonic oscillator parameter for single baryons is b = 0.595 fm (Set I and III) or 0.603 fm (Set II).

## Context

This work extends earlier bag-string model predictions by Mulders et al. (1978) that suggested a d' mass around 2100 MeV, but improves upon them by including quark exchange interactions between clusters (Pauli principle effects) and nonperturbative pion and sigma meson exchange interactions required by spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. The calculation enables future studies of whether the d' resonance can be consistently described within the NRQM framework, and suggests that the confinement strength may be weaker in six-quark systems than in three-quark systems, a hypothesis that does not affect previous NRQM results for NN scattering phase shifts or deuteron electromagnetic form factors as shown by Shimizu (1989).
