# Hadron-Quark Crossover and Massive Hybrid Stars

> On the basis of the percolation picture from the hadronic phase with hyperons to the quark phase with strangeness, we construct a new equation of state (EOS) with the pressure interpolated as a function of the baryon density. The maximum mass of neutron stars can exceed $2M_{\odot}$ if the following two conditions are satisfied; (i) the crossover from the hadronic matter to the quark matter takes place at around three times the normal nuclear matter density, and (ii) the quark matter is strongly interacting in the crossover region and has stiff equation of state. This is in contrast to the conventional approach assuming the first order phase transition in which the EOS becomes always soft due to the presence of the quark matter at high density. Although the choice of the hadronic EOS does not affect the above conclusion on the maximum mass, the three-body force among nucleons and hyperons plays an essential role for the onset of the hyperon mixing and the cooling of neutron stars.

# Hadron-Quark Crossover and Massive Hybrid Stars

## Key claim

The authors propose that hybrid stars with quark cores can exceed 2 solar masses if the transition from hadronic to quark matter occurs as a smooth crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, provided that (i) the crossover takes place at around three times normal nuclear matter density, and (ii) the quark matter is strongly interacting with a stiff equation of state in the crossover region. The abstract's claim that the choice of hadronic equation of state does not affect the maximum mass conclusion is qualified by the finding that the three-body force among nucleons and hyperons plays an essential role for the onset of hyperon mixing and neutron star cooling.

## Method

The authors construct a new equation of state by interpolating the pressure as a function of baryon density between hadronic equations of state (with hyperon mixing) and quark matter equations of state derived from the (2+1)-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector interactions. They use a hyperbolic tangent interpolation function with a crossover window centered at 3 times nuclear matter density and width of 1 times nuclear matter density, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars. Several hadronic equations of state are considered, including TNI2, TNI3, TNI2u, TNI3u from G-matrix calculations, AV18+TBF and Paris+TBF, and the relativistic mean field model SCL3ΛΣ.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars exceeds 2.04 solar masses for all combinations of hadronic and quark equations of state tested, with the highest reaching 2.18 solar masses for TNI3u with g_V = 1.5 G_S
- Central density at maximum mass ranges from 5.4 to 6.1 times normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- The crossover window is centered at ρ̄ = 3ρ₀ with width Γ = ρ₀
- Strangeness threshold density in pure quark matter is 4.0ρ₀, 3.9ρ₀, and 3.0ρ₀ for the HK, RKH, and LKW NJL parameter sets respectively
- Hyperon threshold densities in hadronic equations of state range from 1.8ρ₀ (AV18+TBF for Σ⁻) to 4.06ρ₀ (TNI2u for Σ⁻)
- Without hyperon mixing, maximum masses of pure hadronic stars range from 1.62 to 2.06 solar masses; with hyperon mixing, these drop to 1.08-1.36 solar masses for equations of state without universal three-body repulsion
- TNI3u with universal three-body force achieves maximum mass of 1.83 solar masses with hyperons, compared to 1.88 without hyperons
- Radius of hybrid stars with interpolated equation of state is in the range 11 ± 1 km for masses between 0.5 and 2.0 solar masses (except for SCL3ΛΣ)

## Context

This work extends the percolation picture of hadrons discussed in seminal works by Baym (1979) and Satz (1982), and the concept of hadron-quark continuity proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and Fukushima (2004). It contrasts with the conventional approach assuming first-order phase transition with Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions, which always produces soft equations of state due to quark matter at high density. The paper enables future studies of neutron star cooling, as the internal strangeness content differs significantly between hybrid stars with different hadronic equations of state even when masses and radii are identical, affecting the efficiency of hyperon direct URCA cooling processes.
