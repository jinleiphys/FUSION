# Dilepton production at intermediate energies with in-medium spectral functions of vector mesons

> We report on a self-consistent calculation of the in-medium spectral functions of the rho and omega mesons at finite baryon density. The corresponding in-medium dilepton spectrum is generated and compared with HADES data. We find that an iterative calculation of the vector meson spectral functions provides a reasonable description of the experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper reports that a self-consistent iterative calculation of the in-medium spectral functions of rho and omega mesons, which includes the feedback of in-medium vector meson widths onto nucleon resonance widths, provides a reasonable description of HADES dilepton data for C+C collisions at 2A GeV, whereas neglecting the in-medium properties of the nucleon resonances yields no comparable agreement.

## Method
The in-medium vector meson self-energies were first computed to leading order in density using a nucleon resonance dominance (NRD) model with couplings described by the extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model. The resulting in-medium spectral functions were then used to determine the in-medium widths of the nucleon resonances, and the vector meson spectral functions were recalculated iteratively until convergence. The final self-consistent spectral functions were combined with a relativistic quantum molecular dynamics transport model to generate dilepton spectra for comparison with HADES data.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear saturation density, the rho and omega spectral functions show a slight upward mass shift and substantial broadening, with a two-peak structure at low momenta; the first peak appears around 0.5–0.55 GeV, generated mainly by the N*(1520) resonance for the rho and the N*(1535) resonance for the omega.
- The self-consistent dilepton spectrum was calculated for C+C collisions at 2A GeV and compared with HADES data.

## Context
This work extends the NRD+eVMD approach of Santini (2008) and Krivoruchenko (2001) by including higher-order effects: the in-medium modifications of nucleon resonances are fed back into the vector meson self-energies through an iterative scheme. The self-consistent calculation improves agreement with HADES data (Agakichiev, 2006) compared to the leading-order calculation that neglects these resonance modifications, demonstrating the importance of self-consistency in describing vector meson properties in nuclear matter.
