# Supernova Neutrino-Nucleus Astrophysics

> In this brief review we explore the role of neutrino-nucleus interactions in core-collapse supernovae and discuss open questions. In addition implications of neutrino mass and mixings in such environments are summarized.

## Key claim

Neutrino-nucleus interactions are expected to play an important role in core-collapse supernova dynamics and nucleosynthesis, but the precise mechanisms by which neutrino energy is transferred to the stalled shock and the conditions for r-process nucleosynthesis remain open questions that depend critically on neutrino-nucleus cross sections and neutrino flavor transformation.

## Method

The authors review the role of neutrino-nucleus interactions in core-collapse supernovae, examining charged-current and neutral-current processes on nuclei and free nucleons during infall, bounce, shock propagation, and the neutrino-driven wind phase. They analyze how matter-enhanced active-sterile neutrino flavor transformation can alter the electron fraction and neutron-to-seed ratio in outflow models, and present self-consistent calculations coupling neutrino flavor evolution equations with weak capture rates and thermodynamic evolution of outflowing mass elements.

## Key numbers

- Gravitational binding energy release in collapse to nuclear density is approximately 1% of core rest mass promptly and ultimately 10% of rest mass, radiated as neutrinos of all flavors
- The shock evolves to become a standing accretion shock in approximately 100 milliseconds
- Coulomb energy sets the scale for daughter nucleus excitation energy in neutrino capture on neutron-rich targets, which can be approximately 30 MeV or more for a target like 208Pb
- Electron neutrino capture-induced fission cross sections for heavy nuclei can approach 10^-38 cm^2 for the actinides
- The gravitational binding energy of a nucleon near the neutron star surface is approximately 100 MeV
- The neutrino-driven wind has a small total mass of approximately 10^-4 solar masses
- The temperature-radius relation in the wind is given by r_6 approximately 22.5/(T_9 s_100), where r_6 is radius in units of 10^6 cm, T_9 is temperature in billions of Kelvins, and s_100 is entropy in units of 100 Boltzmann's constant per baryon
- The atmospheric neutrino mass-squared difference is approximately 3 x 10^-3 eV^2 and the solar neutrino value is approximately 7 x 10^-5 eV^2
- The LSND experiment reported an excess of anti-electron-neutrino-induced events with statistical significance of 3 to 4 sigma
- The mass-squared difference range 0.1 eV^2 < delta m^2 < 100 eV^2 in the channel nu_mu,tau to nu_e is worthy of experimental exploration for r-process
- Expansion timescales of tau = 0.3 seconds and tau = 0.9 seconds were used in the active-to-sterile conversion calculations
- Electron fractions as low as Y_e < 0.1 can be produced in active-sterile flavor transformation scenarios

## Context

This review extends the discussion of neutrino-nucleus interactions in supernovae from earlier work by Langanke (2003) on electron capture during collapse and by Blondin (2002) on shock dynamics. The authors connect supernova physics to neutrino oscillation experiments including LSND (Athanassopoulos 1996, Aguilar 2001), atmospheric neutrino measurements (Fukuda 1998, 2000), solar neutrino experiments at SNO (Ahmad 2002), SuperKamiokande (Fukuda 2001), Chlorine (Cleveland), and Gallium experiments (Abdurashitov 2002, Hampel 1998, Altmann 2000), as well as KamLAND (Eguchi 2002). The work enables further investigation of how active-sterile neutrino mixing in the mass-squared difference range probed by mini-BooNE could resolve the alpha effect problem in r-process nucleosynthesis, and highlights the need for better understanding of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for iron peak nuclei at energies from tens of MeV to 100 MeV.
