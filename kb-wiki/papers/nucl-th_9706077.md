# The Three-Nucleon System Near the N-d Threshold

> The three-nucleon system is studied at energies a few hundred keV above the N-d threshold. Measurements of the tensor analyzing powers $T_{20}$ and $T_{21}$ for p-d elastic scattering at $E_{c.m.}=432$ keV are presented together with the corresponding theoretical predictions. The calculations are extended to very low energies since they are useful for extracting the p-d scattering lengths from the experimental data. The interaction considered here is the Argonne V18 potential plus the Urbana three-nucleon potential. The calculation of the asymptotic D- to S-state ratio for $^3$H and $^3$He, for which recent experimental results are available, is also presented.

## Key claim
The paper reports that the AV18 plus Urbana three-nucleon potential model reproduces the measured tensor analyzing powers \(T_{20}\) and \(T_{21}\) for p-d elastic scattering at \(E_{c.m.}=432\) keV with remarkably good agreement, but the authors note that the calculations neglect certain electromagnetic terms of the AV18 potential (vacuum polarization and magnetic moment terms) to simplify the asymptotic solutions, and that including those terms changes the doublet scattering length from \({}^2a_{pd}=0.024\) fm to \({}^2a_{pd}=-0.022\) fm.

## Method
The authors measured the tensor analyzing powers \(T_{20}\) and \(T_{21}\) for p-d elastic scattering at \(E_{c.m.}=432\) keV using tensor-polarized deuteron beams from the atomic beam polarized ion source at TUNL, accelerated to \(E_d=1.3\) MeV. The scattering wave functions were constructed using the Pair-Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis, and the reactance matrix was obtained via the Kohn variational principle, using the Argonne V18 potential plus the Urbana three-nucleon interaction. Calculations were extended to lower energies to evaluate the effective range functions and the p-d scattering lengths \({}^2a_{pd}\) and \({}^4a_{pd}\), and the asymptotic D- to S-state ratio \(\eta\) for \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He was also computed.

## Key numbers
- \(E_{c.m.}=432\pm 3\) keV: the center-of-mass energy for the p-d elastic scattering measurements, with the error including uncertainty in the incident energy.
- Doublet p-d scattering length \({}^2a_{pd}=0.024\) fm (fitted from effective range expansion) and \({}^2a_{pd}=0.027\) fm (direct calculation at zero energy); when all electromagnetic terms of AV18 are included, \({}^2a_{pd}=-0.022\) fm.
- Quartet scattering lengths: \({}^4a_{nd}=6.33\) fm and \({}^4a_{pd}=13.8\) fm, calculated using the AV18+UR model.
- Asymptotic constants for \(^3\)H: \(C_S=1.854\), \(C_D=0.0798\), \(\eta=0.0430\).
- Asymptotic constants for \(^3\)He: \(C_S=1.878\), \(C_D=0.0752\), \(\eta=0.0400\).
- Experimental \(\eta\) values for comparison: \(\eta(^3\mathrm{H})=0.0411\pm 0.0013\pm 0.0012\) (Beata et al.) and \(0.0431\pm 0.0025\) (Knutson et al.); \(\eta(^3\mathrm{He})=0.0386\pm 0.0045 \pm 0.0012\) (Zeid et al.).
- Calculated n-d doublet scattering length \({}^2a_{nd}=0.63\) fm, compared to experimental value \(0.65\pm 0.04\) fm (ndexp).
- Calculated n-d quartet scattering length \({}^4a_{nd}=6.33\) fm, compared to experimental value \(6.35\pm 0.02\) fm (ndexp).

## Context
This work extends the detailed comparisons of N-d scattering observables performed by Kievsky et al. (1996) for \(0.6\le E_{c.m.}\le 2.0\) MeV down to 432 keV, where Coulomb effects are significant and higher partial waves are reduced. The results confirm the singular behavior of the p-d effective range function near zero energy anticipated by Friar et al. (1989) using S-wave potentials, now demonstrated with realistic nuclear interactions. The calculated asymptotic constants and scattering lengths provide a benchmark for future experiments aiming to extract p-d scattering lengths and for calculations of the astrophysical \(S\)-factor for the \(^2\mathrm{H}(p,\gamma)^3\mathrm{He}\) reaction at very low energies.
