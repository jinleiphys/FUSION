# Translationally invariant calculations of form factors, nucleon densities and momentum distributions for finite nuclei with short-range correlations included

> Relying upon our previous treatment of the density matrices for nuclei (in general, nonrelativistic self-bound finite systems) we are studying a combined effect of center-of-mass motion and short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations on the nucleon density and momentum distributions in light nuclei ($^{4}He $ and $^{16}O$). Their intrinsic ground-state wave functions are constructed in the so-called fixed center-of-mass approximation, starting with mean-field Slater determinants modified by some correlator (e.g., after Jastrow or Villars). We develop the formalism based upon the Cartesian or boson representation, in which the coordinate and momentum operators are linear combinations of the creation and annihilation operators for oscillatory quanta in the three different space directions, and get the own "Tassie-Barker" factors for each distribution and point out other model-independent results. After this separation of the center-of-mass motion effects we propose additional analytic means in order to simplify the subsequent calculations (e.g., within the Jastrow approach or the unitary correlation operator method). The charge form factors, densities and momentum distributions of $^{4}He $ and $^{16}O$ evaluated by using the well known cluster expansions are compared with data, our exact (numerical) results and microscopic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, contrary to common practice, the center-of-mass (CM) correction for both form factors and momentum distributions in light nuclei produces a simultaneous shrinking of the intrinsic density and momentum distributions relative to the uncorrected ones, and that this effect persists when short-range correlations are included via Jastrow or unitary correlation operator methods, provided the translationally invariant intrinsic wave function is constructed properly.

## Method

The authors construct intrinsic ground-state wave functions for ⁴He and ¹⁶O using the fixed-center-of-mass approximation of Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, starting from mean-field Slater determinants modified by Jastrow-type or unitary correlation operators. They develop a formalism based on the Cartesian (boson) representation of coordinate and momentum operators, which yields analytic Tassie-Barker factors for both the charge form factor and the momentum distribution. The correlated expectation values are evaluated using cluster expansions (factor-Iwamoto-Yamada and unitary correlation operator method) truncated at the two-body level, and the resulting charge form factors, densities, and momentum distributions are compared with experimental data and exact numerical results.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He, the charge form factor calculated with the Jastrow correlator and CM correction shows agreement with experimental data up to momentum transfer \(q \approx 3.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- For ¹⁶O, the charge form factor with CM correction and Jastrow correlations reproduces the experimental diffraction minimum near \(q \approx 1.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The oscillator parameter used for ⁴He is \(r_0 = 1.38\ \text{fm}\) and for ¹⁶O is \(r_0 = 1.76\ \text{fm}\).
- The Tassie-Barker factor for the form factor is \(F_{TB}(q) = \exp(q^2 r_0^2 / 4A)\), and the corresponding factor for the momentum distribution is \(N_{TB}(z) = \exp(z^2 p_0^2 / 4A)\).
- The renormalized oscillator parameter for intrinsic quantities is \(\bar{r}_0 = \sqrt{(A-1)/A}\, r_0\).

## Context

This work extends the translationally invariant formalism developed by Shebeko, P. A. Grigorov, and V. S. Iurasov (2006) for the 1s-shell to heavier nuclei like ¹⁶O, and incorporates short-range correlations beyond the harmonic oscillator model. It contrasts with earlier approaches by Alvioli et al. (2005) and Massen et al. (1999, 2000), which applied CM corrections only via the standard Tassie-Barker factor to the charge form factor while neglecting the analogous correction to momentum distributions. The results enable more reliable extraction of short-range correlation effects from electron scattering data by properly separating CM motion from intrinsic nuclear structure.
