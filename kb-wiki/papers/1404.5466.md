# Multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field study of triple-humped barriers in actinides

> Potential energy surfaces (PES's) of actinide nuclei are characterized by a two-humped barrier structure. At large deformations beyond the second barrier the occurrence of a third one was predicted by Mic-Mac model calculations in the 1970s, but contradictory results were later reported. In this paper, triple-humped barriers in actinide nuclei are investigated with covariant density functional theory (CDFT). Calculations are performed using the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model, with functionals PC-PK1 and DD-ME2. Pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation with a separable pairing force of finite range. Two-dimensional PES's of $^{226,228,230,232}$Th and $^{232,234,236,238}$U are mapped and the third minima on these surfaces are located. Then one-dimensional potential energy curves along the fission path are analyzed in detail and the energies of the second barrier, the third minimum, and the third barrier are determined. DD-ME2 predicts the occurrence of a third barrier in all Th nuclei and $^{238}$U. The third minima in $^{230,232}$Th are very shallow, whereas those in $^{226,228}$Th and $^{238}$U are quite prominent. With PC-PK1 a third barrier is found only in $^{226,228,230}$Th. Single-nucleon levels around the Fermi surface are analyzed in $^{226}$Th, and it is found that the formation of the third minimum is mainly due to the $Z=90$ proton energy gap at $β_{20} \approx 1.5$ and $β_{30} \approx 0.7$. The possible occurrence of a third barrier in actinide nuclei depends on the effective interaction used in multidimensional CDFT calculations. More pronounced minima are predicted by the DD-ME2 functional, as compared to the functional PC-PK1. The depth of the third well in Th isotopes decreases with increasing neutron number. The origin of the third minimum is due to the proton $Z=90$ shell gap at relevant deformations.

# Multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field study of triple-humped barriers in actinides

## Key claim

The occurrence of a third barrier in actinide nuclei depends on the effective interaction used in multidimensional covariant density functional theory calculations, with the DD-ME2 functional predicting more pronounced third minima than the PC-PK1 functional; the third minimum originates from the proton Z=90 shell gap at deformations β₂₀ ≈ 1.5 and β₃₀ ≈ 0.7.

## Method

The authors performed multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field (MDC-RMF) calculations using two covariant density functionals: the point-coupling functional PC-PK1 and the density-dependent meson exchange functional DD-ME2. Pairing correlations were treated in the BCS approximation with a separable pairing force of finite range, and the deformed RMF equations were solved by expansion in an axially-deformed harmonic oscillator basis. Two-dimensional potential energy surfaces in the (β₂₀, β₃₀) plane were mapped for eight actinide nuclei, and one-dimensional potential energy curves along the lowest static fission path were analyzed to determine barrier heights and minima.

## Key numbers

- For ²²⁶Th with DD-ME2: third minimum at 7.37 MeV above ground state, third barrier at 9.31 MeV, third well depth 1.94 MeV
- For ²²⁶Th with PC-PK1: third minimum at 5.44 MeV, third barrier at 6.73 MeV, third well depth 1.29 MeV
- For ²²⁸Th with DD-ME2: third minimum at 6.69 MeV, third barrier at 7.82 MeV, third well depth 1.13 MeV
- For ²²⁸Th with PC-PK1: third minimum at 4.72 MeV, third barrier at 5.50 MeV, third well depth 0.78 MeV
- For ²³⁰Th with DD-ME2: third minimum at 5.97 MeV, third barrier at 6.60 MeV, third well depth 0.63 MeV
- For ²³⁰Th with PC-PK1: third minimum at 4.01 MeV, third barrier at 4.45 MeV, third well depth 0.44 MeV
- For ²³²Th with DD-ME2: third minimum at 5.42 MeV, third barrier at 5.92 MeV, third well depth 0.50 MeV
- For ²³⁸U with DD-ME2: third minimum at 3.70 MeV, third barrier at 4.81 MeV, third well depth 1.11 MeV
- The hyperdeformed minimum of ²²⁶Th is located at β₂₀ ≈ 1.5 and β₃₀ ≈ 0.7
- The pairing strength parameter used was g/g₀ = 1.1 for PC-PK1 and g/g₀ = 1.2 for DD-ME2, where g₀ = 728 MeV·fm³

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic-microscopic model predictions from the 1970s by Moeller and collaborators that suggested shallow third minima beyond the second barrier in actinides, as well as later contradictory results from Woods-Saxon potential calculations by Kowal (2012) and Jachimowicz (2013) that found third minima disappeared in many nuclei. The study also builds on previous self-consistent approaches using Skyrme forces by Bonneau (2004) and Samyn (2005) and the Gogny force by Berger (1989) and Delaroche (2006), which did not exhibit deep third minima. The results enable systematic comparison between two relativistic density functionals and provide a framework for understanding the single-particle origin of hyperdeformed minima, particularly the role of the Z=90 proton shell gap.
