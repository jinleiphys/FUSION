# Partial wave analysis of $K^+N$ scattering and the possibility of pentaquark $Θ^+(1540)$

> Elastic $K^+ n\to K^+ n$ and charge exchange $K^+n\to K^0p$ reactions at low momenta are investigated using the partial wave analysis. Isospin relation gives constrains for the partial $s$- and $p$-waves among elastic $K^+p$, $K^+n$ and charge exchange $K^+n\to K^0p$ amplitudes. Two sets of the phase shift for these partial waves in the isoscalar channel are obtained from the fit of experimental data on total and differential cross sections. Polarization observable leads to a good criterion to decide which set is valid. Differential cross section data near $P_{\rm Lab}=434$ MeV/$c$ suggests a possibility of a resonance, e.g., the exotic $Θ^+(1540)$ baryon around $\sqrt{s}\simeq$ 1.54GeV.

## Key claim

Differential cross section data near $P_{\rm Lab}=434$ MeV/$c$ suggest a possibility of a resonance, e.g., the exotic $\Theta^+(1540)$ baryon around $\sqrt{s}\simeq$ 1.54 GeV, but the analysis yields two contradictory sets of phase-shift parameters (set I and set II) for the isoscalar channel, and only the polarization observable provides a criterion to decide which set is valid.

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis of elastic $K^+n\to K^+n$ and charge exchange $K^+n\to K^0p$ reactions at low momenta ($P_{\rm Lab}\leq 800$ MeV/$c$). They first extracted the isovector ($I=1$) amplitude from elastic $K^+p$ scattering using only an $s$-wave with a linear phase shift, then used isospin relations to determine the isoscalar ($I=0$) amplitude including $s$- and $p$-waves. Two parameter sets (set I and set II) were obtained by fitting to differential cross section data from Damerell et al. and Giacomelli et al., respectively, and the polarization observable was used to discriminate between them.

## Key numbers

- The isovector $s$-wave phase shift is parameterized as $\delta^1_{0+}(k)=a_0+b_0 k$ with $a_0=3$ and $b_0=-107$ GeV$^{-1}$, fixed to differential cross section data from Goldhaber and Cameron.
- The $\Theta^+(1540)$ is predicted at mass $M \approx 1.53$ GeV/$c^2$ with width $\Gamma < 15$ MeV (Diakonov, Praszalowicz).
- At $P_{\rm Lab}=434$ MeV/$c$, the total cross section for elastic $K^+n$ scattering is $\sigma=6\pm2$ mb, obtained by integrating differential cross section data from Damerell et al.
- At $P_{\rm Lab}=526$ MeV/$c$, the total cross section range is $4.73\leq\sigma\leq6.4$ mb from the same data.
- The resonance peak is illustrated at $P_{\rm Lab}\approx480$ MeV/$c$ using a Breit-Wigner fit with $M_\Theta=1555$ MeV, $\Gamma_\Theta=50$ MeV, $J^P=1/2^+$, $I_R=\sqrt{1/2}$, $X_R=0.25$, and damping parameter $d=1.5$.

## Context

This work extends previous partial wave analyses of $K^+N$ scattering (Arndt, Azimov, Hyslop) and addresses discrepancies found by Sibirtsev et al. and Aoki et al. near $P_{\rm Lab}=434$ MeV/$c$, where the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark is predicted. The analysis enables future experimental searches for the $\Theta^+(1540)$ by providing two candidate phase-shift sets, with polarization data favoring set II (fitted to Giacomelli's data) over set I (fitted to Damerell's data).
