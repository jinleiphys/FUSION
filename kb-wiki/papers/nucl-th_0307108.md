# Semiclassical Distorted Wave Model Analysis of Backward Proton Emission from $(p,p^{\prime}x)$ Reactions at Intermediate Energies

> A semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model with Wigner transform of one-body density matrix is presented for multistep direct $(p,p^{\prime}x)$ reactions to the continuum. The model uses Wigner distribution functions obtained in methods which include nucleon-nucleon correlations to a different extent, as well as Woods-Saxon (WS) single-particle wave function. The higher momentum components of target nucleons that play a crucial role in reproducing the high-energy part of the backward proton spectra are properly taken into account. This SCDW model is applied to analyses of multistep direct processes in $^{12}$C$(p,p^{\prime}x)$, $^{40}$Ca$(p,p^{\prime}x)$ and $^{90}$Zr$(p,p^{\prime}x)$ in the incident energy range of 150--392 MeV. The double differential cross sections are calculated up to three-step processes. The calculated angular distributions are in good agreement with the experimental data, in particular at backward angles where the previous SCDW calculations with the WS single-particle wave function showed large underestimation. It is found that the result with the Wigner distribution function based on the coherent density fluctuation model provides overall better agreement with the experimental data over the whole emission energies.

## Key claim

The semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model, when supplied with Wigner distribution functions that include high-momentum components from nucleon-nucleon correlations, can reproduce backward-angle proton emission spectra that previous SCDW calculations using only Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions had systematically underestimated. The best overall agreement across all targets and emission energies is obtained using the Wigner transform from the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which emphasizes long-range correlations over short-range ones.

## Method

The authors extended the SCDW model for multistep direct (MSD) \((p,p^{\prime}x)\) reactions by replacing the local-density Fermi gas or Woods-Saxon single-particle Wigner transforms with four alternative Wigner transforms: (1) Woods-Saxon (WS) single-particle wave functions; (2) natural orbitals from the Jastrow correlation method (JCM) for short-range correlations; (3) a local-density approximation with short-range correlations (LDA+SRC); and (4) the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). Double differential cross sections were calculated for one-, two-, and three-step processes for \(^{12}\)C at 150 MeV, \(^{40}\)Ca at 186 and 392 MeV, and \(^{90}\)Zr at 160 MeV, using the Melbourne \(G\)-matrix effective interaction and global Dirac optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 150–392 MeV for \((p,p^{\prime}x)\) reactions on \(^{12}\)C, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{90}\)Zr.
- For \(^{12}\)C\((p,p^{\prime}x)\) at 150 MeV incident and 80 MeV outgoing energy, the CDFM calculation gave good overall agreement with data, while the WS calculation dropped at backward angles and the LDA+SRC calculation overestimated the data.
- For \(^{40}\)Ca\((p,p^{\prime}x)\) at 186 MeV and 110 MeV emission energy, CDFM and JCM results showed fair agreement with data; at 150 MeV emission energy the WS result reproduced the backward angular distribution.
- For \(^{40}\)Ca\((p,p^{\prime}x)\) at 392 MeV, CDFM and JCM calculations agreed well with experimental data at 100, 200, and 300 MeV emission energies.
- For \(^{90}\)Zr\((p,p^{\prime}x)\) at 160 MeV incident and 120 MeV emission energy, the CDFM calculation achieved very good agreement with data over forward, intermediate, and backward angles.
- The correlation parameter \(\beta = 1.1\) fm\(^{-1}\) was used in the LDA+SRC method, giving a Jastrow wound parameter \(k_{dir} = 0.23\).
- The effective mass at \(r=0\) was set to \(m^{*} = 0.8m\).
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: radius \(r_0 = 1.27\) fm, surface diffuseness \(a = 0.67\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the SCDW model previously developed by Kawai (1992), Watanabe (1993), and Ogata (1999, 2002), which had successfully described inclusive \((p,p^{\prime}x)\) and \((p,nx)\) cross sections at intermediate energies but systematically underestimated backward-angle yields. The improvement addresses a long-standing problem in backward proton production, previously tackled by Haneda (1986) using a correlated two-nucleon-cluster picture with a phenomenological high-momentum distribution from Fujita (1986). The present results demonstrate that long-range correlations, as captured by the CDFM, are more important than short-range correlations for reproducing backward proton spectra, and the approach enables systematic SCDW studies of preequilibrium emission across a wide range of target masses and incident energies.
