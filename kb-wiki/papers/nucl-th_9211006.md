# $q^2$-Dependence of Meson Mixing in Few-Body Charge Symmetry Breaking: $π^o-η$ Mixing to One Loop in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> It is pointed out that the meson mixing matrix elements usually considered responsible for the bulk of the observed few-body charge symmetry breaking are naturally $q^2$-dependent in QCD. For $π^o-η$ mixing, using the usual representation of the pseudoscalar fields, the leading $q^2$ dependence can be explicitly calculated using chiral perturbation theory to one loop, the result being a significant decrease in the magnitude of the matrix element in going from timelike to spacelike values of $q^2$. Since it is the latter range of $q^2$ which is relevant to NN scattering and the few-body bound state, this result calls into serious question the standard treatment of few-body charge symmetry breaking contributions associated with $π^o-η$ and $ρ-ω$ mixing.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the meson mixing matrix elements responsible for few-body charge symmetry breaking are naturally \(q^2\)-dependent in QCD, and that for \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing the leading \(q^2\) dependence can be calculated to one loop in chiral perturbation theory, producing a significant decrease in the matrix element magnitude when moving from timelike to spacelike \(q^2\). This result calls into serious question the standard treatment of few-body charge symmetry breaking contributions, which assumes \(q^2\)-independent mixing.

## Method

The author computes the \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing angle \(\theta(q^2)\) to one loop using the effective chiral Lagrangian of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) to order \(q^4\), including the low-energy constants \(L_1\) through \(L_8\). The inverse \(\pi_3-\pi_8\) propagator matrix is diagonalized as a function of the external four-momentum \(q^2\), yielding the \(q^2\)-dependent mixing angle, which is then expressed in terms of physical meson masses and the QCD contribution to the kaon mass splitting.

## Key numbers

* The \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing matrix element \(m_{38}^2(q^2)\) is parametrized as \(m_{38}^2 = -[a_0 + (q^2/m_\eta^2) a_1]\), with \(a_0\) ranging from 0.00335 GeV\(^2\) to 0.00426 GeV\(^2\) and \(a_1\) from 0.00028 GeV\(^2\) to 0.00036 GeV\(^2\) depending on the assumed electromagnetic kaon mass splitting between 1.3 MeV and 2.8 MeV.
* In going from \(q^2 = m_\eta^2\) to \(q^2 = -m_\eta^2\), the magnitude of the mixing matrix element decreases by 17\%.
* The overall scale of the result has an uncertainty of order 20\% due to possible violations of Dashen's theorem for the electromagnetic kaon mass splitting.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) to compute the \(q^2\) dependence of \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing, contradicting the assumption of \(q^2\)-independent mixing used in earlier few-body charge symmetry breaking analyses by Coon, McKellar, and Scadron (1986) and others. The result provides a lower bound on the theoretical uncertainties in phenomenological meson-exchange models of few-body systems, and suggests that model calculations of \(\rho-\omega\) mixing, such as the quark-loop model of Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1991), should be constrained by the chiral results to improve reliability.
