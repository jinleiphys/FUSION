# Continuum time-dependent Hartree-Fock for giant resonances in spherical nuclei

> This paper deals with the solution of the spherically symmetric time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation applied in the case of nuclear giant monopole resonances. The problem is spatially unbounded as the resonance state is in the continuum. The practical requirement to perform the calculation in a finite-sized spatial region results in a difficulty with the spatial boundary conditions. Here we propose a absorbing boundary condition scheme to handle the conflict. The derivation, via a Laplace transform method, and implementation is described. The accuracy and efficiency of the scheme is tested and the results presented to support the case that they are a effective way of handling the artificial boundary.

## Key claim

The paper presents an absorbing boundary condition scheme for the spherically symmetric time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approximation applied to nuclear giant monopole resonances, claiming it is an effective way of handling the artificial boundary problem that arises when calculations must be performed in a finite spatial region despite the resonance state being in the continuum.

## Method

The authors split the spatial domain into an interior region containing nuclear dynamics and an exterior region where only the centrifugal barrier remains, then derive absorbing boundary conditions via a Laplace transform method applied to the exterior problem. The TDHF equations are discretized on equally spaced grids using the Crank-Nicholson method for time evolution and three-point finite differences for spatial derivatives, with a simplified Skyrme interaction (t0-t3 version) omitting Coulomb. The absorbing boundary condition is implemented by replacing the last row of the tridiagonal matrix with a discretized version of the convolution integral involving spherical Hankel functions and the Faddeeva function.

## Key numbers

- Skyrme force parameters used: a = -817.5 MeV fm^3 and b = 3241.5 MeV fm^6, taken from PhysRevC.56.857
- For the simplified free-particle test case (l=0,1,2), with grid spacings dx=dt=0.1, the error in the radial wavefunction at final time t=15 was bounded by 10^{-3} throughout the interior; with dx=dt=0.01 the error was bounded by 10^{-5}
- In the temporal probability test over time interval [0,50] with reflecting boundary at r=200, more than 90% of the wavefunction left the interval [0,10]; final probabilities inside the interval were 8.57E-002 (l=0), 6.36E-003 (l=1), and 2.03E-004 (l=2)

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF treatments of giant monopole resonances in spherical doubly-magic nuclei (Vautherin 1979, Stringari 1979, Lacroix 1998, Wu 1999, Almehed 2005, Stevenson 2010) by addressing the boundary condition problem that arises because the resonance lies above the particle decay threshold. The absorbing boundary method, derived via Laplace transform rather than the Green's function approach used by Heinen and Kull, enables calculations without requiring increasingly large spatial grids as time evolves, and the authors demonstrate its application to 4He, 16O, and 40Ca using a simplified Skyrme force as a proof-of-concept.
