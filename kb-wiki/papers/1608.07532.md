# A lower limit on the heat capacity of the neutron star core

> We show that observations of the core temperature of transiently-accreting neutron stars combined with observations of an accretion outburst give a lower limit to the neutron star core heat capacity. For the neutron stars in the low mass X-ray binaries KS 1731-260, MXB 1659-29, and XTE J1701-462, we show that the lower limit is a factor of a few below the core heat capacity expected if neutrons and protons in the core are paired, so that electrons provide the dominant contribution to the heat capacity. This limit rules out a core dominated by a quark color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase, which would have a much lower heat capacity. Future observations of or limits on cooling during quiescence will further constrain the core heat capacity.

## Key claim

Observations of the core temperature of transiently-accreting neutron stars after an accretion outburst, combined with the energy deposited during the outburst, provide a lower limit on the neutron star core heat capacity that is a factor of a few below the value expected if neutrons and protons are paired (so electrons dominate), but this limit rules out a core dominated by a color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark phase, which would have a much lower heat capacity.

## Method

The authors modeled the thermal evolution of the neutron star crust during and after accretion outbursts for three low-mass X-ray binaries (KS 1731-260, MXB 1659-29, and XTE J1701-462) using crust cooling simulations following Brown (2009). They fit the observed quiescent cooling curves by adjusting parameters including core temperature, impurity parameter, temperature at the top of the crust, accretion rate, neutron star mass and radius, and envelope composition (iron or helium). The total energy deposited into the core during outburst was integrated from the modeled inwards luminosity, and combined with the observed core temperature to derive a lower limit on the core heat capacity using the relation C > 2E/Tc_f.

## Key numbers

* For KS 1731-260 with a heavy element envelope, the core temperature is Tc = 7.0 x 10^7 K for an observed effective temperature of 63.1 eV (assuming M=1.4 Msun, R=12 km).
* The total energy deposited into the core during the outburst is E = 7.5 x 10^43 erg for the best-fit heavy element envelope model.
* The resulting lower limit on the core heat capacity is C > 2.1 x 10^36 erg K^-1 (Tc_7/7)^-1 (E/7.5 x 10^43 erg), or C/Tc_8 > 3.1 x 10^36 erg K^-1.
* From MCMC analysis, the peak of the distribution gives C ~ 2.5 x 10^36 erg K^-1 Tc_8, with log10(C/Tc_8 erg K^-1) = 36.4 +/- 0.3.
* For a core of non-superfluid neutrons and protons, the expected heat capacity is C ~ 10^38 Tc_8 erg K^-1; with nucleon pairing, the lepton contribution gives C ~ 10^37 Tc_8 erg K^-1.
* The CFL quark phase heat capacity from Goldstone bosons is about eight orders of magnitude smaller than the electron contribution in nuclear matter at T=10^8 K.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star thermal evolution that used quiescent temperatures to limit neutrino emissivity (Fujimoto 1984, Colpi 2001, Yakovlev 2004) and crust physics (Shternin 2007, Brown 2009, Page 2013, Turlione 2015). It provides the first observational lower limit on the neutron star core heat capacity, which enables discrimination between different dense matter phases: the limit rules out a CFL quark-dominated core, while being consistent with both paired and unpaired nucleon cores. Future observations of cooling during quiescence can further constrain the core heat capacity and potentially distinguish between these scenarios.
