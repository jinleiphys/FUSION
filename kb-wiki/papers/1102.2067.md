# Multipolar model of bremsstrahlung accompanying proton-decay of nuclei

> Emission of bremsstrahlung photons accompanying proton decay of nuclei is studied. The new improved multipolar model describing such a process is presented. The angular formalism of calculations of the matrix elements is stated in details. The bremsstrahlung probabilities for the $^{157}{\rm Ta}$, $^{161}{\rm Re}$, $^{167}{\rm Ir}$ and $^{185}{\rm Bi}$ nuclei decaying from the $2s_{1/2}$ state, the $^{109}_{53}{\rm I}_{56}$ and $^{112}_{55}{\rm Cs}_{57}$ nuclei decaying from the $1d_{5/2}$ state, the $^{146}_{69}{\rm Tm}_{77}$ and $^{151}_{71}{\rm Lu}_{80}$ nuclei decaying from the $0h_{11/2}$ state are predicted. Such spectra have orders of values similar to the experimental data for the bremsstrahlung photons emitted during the $α$-decay. This indicates on real possibility to study bremsstrahlung photons during proton decay experimentally and perform further measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved multipolar model for bremsstrahlung accompanying proton decay, predicting that the bremsstrahlung probabilities for several proton emitters have orders of magnitude similar to experimental data for bremsstrahlung in alpha-decay, which suggests that experimental study of bremsstrahlung during proton decay is feasible; however, the predictions are based on theoretical calculations without normalization to experimental data, and the author notes that the dipole approximation used in prior work gives overvalued spectra in absolute scale.

## Method

The author develops a multipole expansion formalism for the matrix element of photon emission during proton decay, including spin-orbital components for states with nonzero orbital quantum number, which had not been considered before in this context. The angular formalism is constructed using vector spherical harmonics, Clebsch-Gordon coefficients, and spherical Bessel functions, with the wave functions separated into radial and angular components in a spherically symmetric approximation. The differential absolute probability is derived from the matrix element using quantum field theory, dividing by the flux of outgoing particles to obtain volume-independent results.

## Key numbers

- The effective charge squared (Z_eff^2) equals 0.286, 0.285, 0.288, and 0.303 for the proton emitters 157Ta, 161Re, 167Ir, and 185Bi, respectively; for comparison, it equals 0.185 and 0.16 for the alpha-decaying nuclei 214Po and 210Po.
- The Q-value of alpha-decay is 7.83 MeV for 214Po and 4.87 MeV for 226Ra, which the author previously showed gives different contributions of emission from the tunnelling region into the total spectra.
- The bremsstrahlung probabilities are predicted for 157Ta, 161Re, 167Ir, and 185Bi decaying from the 2s_1/2 state; for 109I and 112Cs decaying from the 1d_5/2 state; and for 146Tm and 151Lu decaying from the 0h_11/2 state.

## Context

This work extends the multipole approach previously applied to bremsstrahlung in alpha-decay (Tkalya 1999, Maydanyuk 2003) to the case of proton decay, where the effective charge is larger than in alpha-decay. The author notes that prior work by Kurgalin (2001) estimated the averaged spectrum for 113Cs up to 250 keV, but no detailed study of bremsstrahlung in proton decay had been performed. The model enables absolute predictions without normalization to experimental data, which could guide future experimental measurements of bremsstrahlung photons during proton decay and potentially provide new information about the dynamics of the tunneling process.
