# Symmetries of mesons after unbreaking of chiral symmetry and their string interpretation

> Using the chirally invariant overlap Dirac operator we remove its lowest-lying quasizero modes from the valence quark propagators and study evolution of isovector mesons with J=1. At the truncation level about 50 MeV SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R and U(1)_A symmetries get restored. However, we observe a degeneracy not only within the chiral and U(1)_A multiplets, but also a degeneracy of all possible chiral multiplets, i.e., the observed quantum levels have a symmetry larger than U(2)_L \times U(2)_R and their energy does not depend on the spin orientation of quarks and their parities. We offer a possible interpretation of these energy levels as the quantum levels of the dynamical QCD string. The structure of the radial J=1 spectrum is compatible with E =(n_r +1)\hbarωwith \hbarω= 900 \pm 70 MeV.

## Key claim

After removing the lowest-lying quasizero modes of the overlap Dirac operator from valence quark propagators, the observed degeneracy of all isovector J=1 mesons (ρ, ρ′, a₁, b₁) indicates a symmetry larger than U(2)ₗ × U(2)ᵣ, which the authors interpret as the quantum levels of a dynamical QCD string, with the caveat that this interpretation is offered as a possibility rather than a proven conclusion.

## Method

Using 100 gauge field configurations with n_f=2 dynamical overlap fermions on a 16³×32 lattice (a ~ 0.12 fm, M_π = 289(2) MeV), the authors removed the lowest k eigenmodes (k=10, 20) from valence quark propagators via S_RD(k) = S_Full − Σᵢ₌₁ᵏ (1/λᵢ)|λᵢ⟩⟨λᵢ|. They constructed hadron correlators for ρ(1⁻⁻), a₁(1⁺⁺), and b₁(1⁺⁻) mesons using up to eight interpolators per channel with different Dirac structures and smearing widths, then extracted masses via the variational method and generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the ensemble: M_π = 289(2) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a ~ 0.12 fm
- At truncation level k=10 (energy gap ~40-65 MeV), masses of ground states (n_r=0): ρ = 0.573(10) in lattice units, ρ′ = 0.589(13), a₁ = 0.576(10), b₁ = 0.568(14)
- At truncation level k=10, masses of first excited states (n_r=1): ρ = 1.096(57), ρ′ = 1.108(48), a₁ = 1.051(62), b₁ = 1.077(51)
- At truncation level k=20, ground state masses: ρ = 0.625(7), ρ′ = 0.614(8), a₁ = 0.615(8), b₁ = 0.607(8)
- At truncation level k=20, first excited state masses: ρ = 1.107(58), ρ′ = 1.139(48), a₁ = 1.062(61), b₁ = 1.106(51)
- Fundamental string excitation quantum: ℏω = 900 ± 70 MeV (extracted at truncation energy 65 MeV, k=10)
- Radial spectrum compatible with E = (n_r + 1)ℏω

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Glozman et al., 2013) that used a Wilson-type Dirac operator, where the identification of zero modes was ambiguous; the present study uses the chirally invariant overlap Dirac operator to resolve that issue. The observed degeneracy of all chiral multiplets goes beyond the SU(2)ₗ × SU(2)ᵣ × U(1)ₐ restoration seen in earlier truncation studies (Lang et al., 2013; Denissenya et al., 2014), and the authors interpret the equidistant radial spectrum as evidence for a dynamical QCD string with chiral quarks at the ends, contrasting with the static string picture from heavy-quark lattice simulations (Bali, 2001). Future work requires studying mesons with J≠1 to include rotational levels and larger lattice volumes to distinguish linear from quadratic radial dependence.
