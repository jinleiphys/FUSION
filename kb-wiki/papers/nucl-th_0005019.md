# Band Crossing and Signature Splitting in Odd Mass fp Shell Nuclei

> Structure of two sets of mirror nuclei: 47V - 47Cr and 49Cr - 49Mn, as well as 49V and 51Mn, is studied using the projected shell model. Their yrast spectra are described as an interplay between the angular momentum projected states around the Fermi level which carry different intrinsic K-quantum numbers. The deviations from a regular rotational sequence are attributed to band crossing and signature splitting, which are usually discussed in heavy nuclear systems. Our results agree reasonably with experimental data, and are comparable with those from the full $pf$ shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM), using the same set of parameters previously employed for the even-even nucleus ⁴⁸Cr, can describe the yrast spectra of odd-mass fp-shell nuclei (⁴⁷V, ⁴⁷Cr, ⁴⁹Cr, ⁴⁹Mn, ⁴⁹V, ⁵¹Mn) and attribute their deviations from regular rotational sequences to band crossing and signature splitting, with results comparable to full pf-shell model calculations but with a general tendency to underestimate the moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors applied the projected shell model (PSM) using a deformed Nilsson + BCS basis with a fixed quadrupole deformation ε₂ = 0.25 for all nuclei. The many-body basis included 1-quasiparticle and selected 3-quasiparticle states (1-qp plus a pair of qp's from nucleons of the other kind), and the Hamiltonian contained a spherical single-particle term, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and monopole and quadrupole pairing interactions. Angular momentum projection was performed to restore rotational symmetry, followed by diagonalization in the laboratory frame to obtain yrast energies and band diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation ε₂ = 0.25 was used for all six nuclei studied (⁴⁷V, ⁴⁷Cr, ⁴⁹Cr, ⁴⁹Mn, ⁴⁹V, ⁵¹Mn), the same value as in the previous ⁴⁸Cr calculation.
- Monopole pairing strength was taken as G_M = [g₁ ∓ g₂(N-Z)/A]/A with g₁ = 22.5 and g₂ = 18.0, and quadrupole pairing strength G_Q was taken as 0.16–0.20 times G_M.
- In ⁴⁷V and ⁴⁷Cr, band crossing between the 1-qp K=3/2 band and 3-qp bands occurs near spin I = 19/2.
- In ⁴⁹Cr and ⁴⁹Mn, a 3-qp band crosses the K=5/2 1-qp band sharply around spin I = 17/2.
- For ⁴⁹Cr, the doublet [25/2⁻, 27/2⁻] is predicted at energies above 10 MeV, reproducing experimental energies from the ENSDF compilation.

## Context

This work extends the PSM analysis of backbending in the even-even nucleus ⁴⁸Cr (Hara et al., 1999) to odd-mass fp-shell nuclei, demonstrating that the same band crossing mechanism explains irregularities in rotational bands of these lighter systems. The results are compared with full pf-shell model calculations by Caurier et al. (1995) and Martinez-Pinedo et al. (1997), which provided exact diagonalizations for these nuclei. The study enables a unified microscopic description of both even-even and odd-mass nuclei in the A≈50 region using the PSM, and suggests that adjusting the monopole pairing strength could improve the systematic underestimation of moments of inertia.
