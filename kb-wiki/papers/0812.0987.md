# Medium Effects in rho-Meson Photoproduction

> We compute dilepton invariant mass spectra from the decays of rho mesons produced by photon reactions off nuclei. Our calculations employ a realistic model for the rho photoproduction amplitude on the nucleon which provides fair agreement with measured cross sections. Medium effects are implemented via an earlier constructed rho propagator based on hadronic many-body theory. At incoming photon energies of 1.5 -3 GeV as used by the CLAS experiment at JLAB, the average density probed for iron targets is estimated at about half saturation density. At the pertinent rho-meson 3-momenta the predicted medium effects on the rho propagator are rather moderate. The resulting dilepton spectra approximately agree with recent CLAS data.

## Key claim

The predicted medium effects on the rho-meson propagator at the high 3-momenta (approximately 1-2 GeV) relevant to the CLAS experiment are rather moderate, and the resulting dilepton spectra approximately agree with recent CLAS data, but only after estimating the average nuclear density probed at about half saturation density.

## Method

The authors combined a microscopic model for rho photoproduction on the nucleon (based on meson/Pomeron exchange supplemented by s-channel baryon resonance excitations) with an in-medium rho spectral function computed in hadronic many-body theory. They estimated the average nuclear density at the rho decay point by calculating the travel distance from production to decay using the rho three-velocity and average lifetime, then integrating over a Woods-Saxon density profile for iron targets. The dilepton invariant-mass spectra were computed using Eq. (sigmaF) which incorporates the in-medium rho propagator and accounts for Fermi motion of the incoming nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Average density probed for iron targets: 0.5 rho_0 (half saturation density) at an average incoming photon energy of approximately 2.1 GeV
- Density range used for iron spectra: 0.4-0.6 rho_0, with variation of photon energy between 1.5 GeV and 2.5 GeV affecting the average density by about +/- 0.1 rho_0
- Chi-squared per data point: 1.29 when including resonance contributions and normalizing by least-square fit; 1.4 when excluding resonance contributions
- Average rho lifetime in medium: roughly 1 fm/c from the underlying in-medium spectral function
- Formfactor cutoff parameters for rho-N-B couplings: 600-1200 MeV (specific values listed in Table 1 for each resonance)
- Attractive mass shift of approximately -15 MeV at rho_N = rho_0 in the transverse rho spectral function from reducing Lambda_rho_NN to 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic many-body rho spectral function of Rapp (1999) and Rapp (1997), previously used to interpret dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, to the regime of rho photoproduction off nuclei. It combines the photoproduction amplitude of Oh (2003) with resonance contributions implicit in the in-medium spectral function. The results are compared to CLAS data from Wood (2008) and Clas (2007), and the paper notes that the moderate medium effects differ from the stronger effects observed in heavy-ion collisions because the CLAS data probe rho mesons at high 3-momentum where the spectral function predicts significantly reduced broadening. The work enables future tests by suggesting that a low-momentum cut on dilepton spectra would provide a critical test of predicted increases in medium effects, and that analysis of absolute e+e- production cross sections (nuclear transparency ratios) could further constrain the in-medium spectral width.
