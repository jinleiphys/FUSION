# Astrophysical analysis of the measurement of (alpha,gamma) and (alpha,n) cross sections of 169Tm

> Reaction cross sections of 169Tm(alpha,gamma)173Lu and 169Tm(alpha,n)172Lu have been measured in the energy range 12.6<=E_alpha<=17.5 MeV and 11.5<=E_alpha<=17.5 MeV, respectively, using the recently introduced method of combining activation with X-ray counting. Improved shielding allowed to measure the (alpha,gamma) to lower energy than previously possible. The combination of (alpha,gamma) and (alpha,n) data made it possible to study the energy dependence of the alpha width. While absolute value and energy dependence are perfectly reproduced by theory at energies above 14 MeV, the observed change in energy dependence at energies below 14 MeV requires a modification of the predicted alpha width. Using an effective, energy-dependent, local optical alpha+nucleus potential it is possible to reproduce the data but the astrophysical rate is still not well constrained at gamma-process temperatures. The additional uncertainty stemming from a possible modification of the compound formation cross section is discussed. Including the remaining uncertainties, the recommended range of astrophysical reaction rate values at 2 GK is higher than the previously used values by factors of 2-37.

## Key claim

The measured (α,γ) and (α,n) cross sections on 169Tm show that standard theory reproduces the data above 14 MeV but fails below that energy, requiring an effective modification of the predicted α width; however, even with an energy-dependent local optical α+nucleus potential that fits the data, the astrophysical reaction rate at γ-process temperatures remains poorly constrained, and the recommended rate at 2 GK is higher than previously used values by factors of 2–37.

## Method

The 169Tm(α,γ)173Lu and 169Tm(α,n)172Lu cross sections were measured using activation combined with X-ray counting, with improved shielding around a LEPS detector to reach lower α energies (12.6–17.5 MeV for (α,γ) and 11.5–17.5 MeV for (α,n)). Thulium targets were produced by vacuum evaporation onto Al backings, irradiated with He++ beams from the ATOMKI cyclotron, and the induced activity was measured via characteristic K X-rays after appropriate cooling periods. The experimental S factors were compared to Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using the SMARAGD code with various global α+nucleus optical potentials, and an effective energy-dependent local potential was introduced to reproduce the low-energy data.

## Key numbers

- (α,γ) cross section at Eα = 12.6 MeV: 0.60 ± 0.15 μbarn
- (α,γ) cross section at Eα = 13.0 MeV: 0.87 ± 0.13 μbarn
- (α,n) cross section at Eα = 12.6 MeV: 9.82 ± 0.92 μbarn
- (α,n) cross section at Eα = 13.0 MeV: 27.5 ± 2.61 μbarn
- Astrophysical reaction rate ratios at T = 2 GK for the new effective potentials (S4, S5, S6) relative to the standard potential: 0.06 to 0.136
- Recommended range of astrophysical reaction rate values at 2 GK is higher than previously used values by factors of 2–37
- Average laboratory background count rate after improved shielding: about 1.7 × 10⁻³ 1/(keV s) in the 20–80 keV region

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of α-induced reactions on heavy nuclei near the problematic mass range A = 150–165, where p-nuclei are underproduced in γ-process models of core-collapse supernovae (Arnould 1976, Woosley and Howard 1978, Rayet et al. 1995). The new data reach lower energies than earlier measurements by Kiss et al. (2011) and provide combined (α,γ) and (α,n) information to disentangle the energy dependence of the α width. The results enable improved constraints on the α+nucleus optical potential at low energies and provide updated astrophysical reaction rates for the γ-process network, though the rates remain uncertain due to the unknown energy dependence of the α width near the Coulomb barrier.
