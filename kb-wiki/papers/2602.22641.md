# Universality in spacetime $ω$ modes of quarkyonic stars

> The gravitational wave $ω$ mode spectrum presents a unique window into the dense interior of neutron stars, probing physics inaccessible to electromagnetic observations. This work investigates the $ω$ modes of compact stars composed of quarkyonic matter. The quarkyonic model, which describes a cross-over transition between nucleonic and quark matter treated as quasi-particles, is formulated within the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) theory using the G3 and IOPB-I parameterizations. This core is surrounded by a mantle of hadronic matter, creating a multicomponent stellar interior. The overall Equation of State (EOS) is governed by two key parameters: the transition density ($n_t$), the QCD confinement scale ($Λ_{\rm cs}$), which are varied to construct models consistent with current astrophysical constraints on mass and radius. We compute the complex eigenfrequencies (damped oscillations) of the fundamental and first excited $ω$ modes using the phase-amplitude method within a full general relativistic framework. Our simulations reveal that the admixed quarkyonic structure produces a unique $ω$ mode signature, distinctly different from pure hadronic or hybrid stars. The spectrum exhibits a strong, degenerate dependence on the EOS, where the stiffening effect of the quarkyonic matter influences oscillation frequencies and damping times in a characteristic manner. We also demonstrate that $ω$ mode frequencies for quarkyonic stars follow approximate universal relations, largely independent of the EOS.

# Universality in Spacetime ω Modes of Quarkyonic Stars

## Key claim

The ω mode spectrum of quarkyonic stars exhibits approximate universal relations that are largely independent of the equation of state, but the quarkyonic crossover structure produces a unique ω mode signature distinctly different from pure hadronic or hybrid stars, with a strong, degenerate dependence on the equation of state where the stiffening effect of quarkyonic matter influences oscillation frequencies and damping times in a characteristic manner.

## Method

The authors construct quarkyonic-inspired crossover equations of state within the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) theory using the G3 and IOPB-I parameterizations, with a hadronic mantle surrounding a quarkyonic core. They vary the transition density (n_t) and the QCD confinement scale (Λ_cs) to generate families of equations of state consistent with current astrophysical constraints on mass and radius, then compute the complex eigenfrequencies of the fundamental and first excited ω modes using the phase-amplitude method within a full general relativistic framework.

## Key numbers

- ω mode frequencies lie in the range of approximately 5–20 kHz for neutron-star models (cited from prior work, not original to this paper)
- Typical damping times for ω modes are of order ~10⁻⁴ s (cited from prior work, not original to this paper)
- The quarkyonic model predicts higher maximum masses due to the stiffened equation of state relative to purely nucleonic matter (qualitative claim, no specific numerical value given in the text)
- The secondary compact object in GW190814 has mass ~2.6 M⊙ (cited as observational context)
- The compact companion in GW230529 has mass in the approximate range ~2.5–4.5 M⊙ (cited as observational context)

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter model originally proposed by McLerran and Reddy (2019) and later extended by Zhao and Lattimer (2020), embedding it within a relativistic mean-field framework using the interpolation scheme of Masuda et al. (2013) and Han et al. (2019). The study connects to the broader neutron-star asteroseismology literature on universal relations for quasinormal modes (Anderson 1996; Kokkotas 1992; Leins 1993) and aims to provide a bridge between high-density microphysics and potentially observable high-frequency gravitational-wave signatures from compact objects in the mass gap, such as those inferred from GW190814 (Abbott 2020) and GW230529 (Abac 2024).
