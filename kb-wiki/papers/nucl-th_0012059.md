# Renormalization of the weak hadronic current in the nuclear medium

> The renormalization of the weak charge-changing hadronic current as a function of the reaction energy release is studied at the nucleonic level. We have calculated the average quenching factors for each type of current (vector, axial vector and induced pseudoscalar). The obtained quenching in the axial vector part is, at zero momentum transfer, 19% for the sd shell and 23% in the fp shell. We have extended the calculations also to heavier systems such as $^{56}$Ni and $^{100}$Sn, where we obtain stronger quenchings, 44% and 59%, respectively. Gamow--Teller type transitions are discussed, along with the higher order matrix elements. The quenching factors are constant up to roughly 60 MeV momentum transfer. Therefore the use of energy-independent quenching factors in beta decay is justified. We also found that going beyond the zeroth and first order operators (in inverse nucleon mass) does not give any substantial contribution. The extracted renormalization to the ratio $C_P/C_A$ at q=100 MeV is -3.5%, -7.1$%, -28.6%, and +8.7% for mass 16, 40, 56, and 100, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quenching of the weak hadronic current in the nuclear medium is nearly constant for momentum transfers up to roughly 60 MeV, justifying energy-independent quenching factors for beta decay, but that the quenching is significantly stronger in nuclei where major shell closures separate spin-orbit partners (such as ⁵⁶Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn) compared to lighter nuclei with closed LS shells.

## Method

The authors constructed effective one-body transition operators for the vector, axial vector, and induced pseudoscalar components of the weak hadronic current using degenerate Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory to second order in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. They calculated the renormalized single-particle matrix elements in a harmonic oscillator basis using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, with model spaces appropriate for closed-shell cores of ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Ni, and ¹⁰⁰Sn, and defined average quenching factors ρ_α as the ratio of summed absolute renormalized to bare single-particle matrix elements for each current type.

## Key numbers

- Axial vector quenching factor squared ρ²_A(0) = 0.81 (19% quenching) for the sd shell (¹⁶O core) at zero momentum transfer
- Axial vector quenching factor squared ρ²_A(0) = 0.77 (23% quenching) for the fp shell (⁴⁰Ca core) at zero momentum transfer
- Axial vector quenching factor squared ρ²_A(0) = 0.56 (44% quenching) for ⁵⁶Ni at zero momentum transfer
- Axial vector quenching factor squared ρ²_A(0) = 0.41 (59% quenching) for ¹⁰⁰Sn at zero momentum transfer
- Renormalization of the ratio C_P/C_A at q = 100 MeV: -3.5% for mass 16, -7.1% for mass 40, -28.6% for mass 56, and +8.7% for mass 100
- Oscillator energies used: ℏΩ = 13.9 MeV (A=16), 11.0 MeV (A=40), 10.05 MeV (A=56), and 8.5 MeV (A=100)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent effective operator calculations (Siiskonen 1999) to include all current types (vector, axial vector, induced pseudoscalar) and higher momentum transfers up to the muon capture range. The results support the conclusion of Martinez-Pinedo (1996) that quenching saturates in the fp shell for LS-closed nuclei, but contradict the assumption that this saturation holds for all heavy nuclei, as ⁵⁶Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn show much stronger quenching due to first-order spin-orbit partner transitions. The extracted C_P/C_A ratios provide input for muon capture calculations and can be compared to the continuum RPA results of Kolbe, Langanke, and Vogel (1994), who found reasonable agreement with data using bare couplings in larger model spaces.
