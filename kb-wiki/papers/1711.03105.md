# Probing the time structure of the quark-gluon plasma with top quarks

> The tiny droplets of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) created in high-energy nuclear collisions experience fast expansion and cooling with a lifetime of a few $\text{fm}/c$. Despite the information provided by probes such as jet quenching and quarkonium suppression, and the excellent description by hydrodynamical models, direct access to the time evolution of the system remains elusive. We point out that the study of hadronically-decaying $W$ bosons, notably in events with a top-antitop quark pair, can provide key novel insight, into the time structure of the QGP. This is because of a unique feature, namely a time delay between the moment of the collision and that when the $W$-boson decay products start interacting with the medium. Furthermore, the length of the time delay can be constrained by selecting specific reconstructed top-quark momenta.

# Probing the time structure of the quark-gluon plasma with top quarks

## Key claim
The study of hadronically-decaying W bosons in top-antitop events can provide novel insight into the time structure of the quark-gluon plasma because of a time delay between the collision and when the W-boson decay products start interacting with the medium; the length of this time delay can be constrained by selecting specific reconstructed top-quark momenta. The authors carry out a Monte Carlo feasibility study and find that the LHC has the potential to bring first, limited information on the time structure of the QGP, with substantially increased luminosities or future higher-energy colliders needed for more extensive studies.

## Method
The authors simulate semi-muonic top-antitop events using POWHEGbox with NLO accuracy and Pythia 8.223 showering, then apply a reconstruction procedure inspired by pseudo-top definitions adapted for resilience to additional jets and quenching effects. To mimic heavy-ion medium effects without full embedding, they apply a constant energy loss rescaling factor of 0.85 to all particle momenta for baseline quenching, and introduce a time-dependent quenching model where the quenching factor for W decay products depends on the total delay time tau_tot relative to a medium end-time tau_m. The reconstructed W mass from hadronic decays is used as the observable to measure quenching, with binned likelihood fits to extract the fitted W mass.

## Key numbers
- Top quark lifetime at rest: tau_top = 0.15 fm/c
- W boson lifetime at rest: tau_W = 0.09 fm/c
- Average total delay times for inclusive top production at LHC sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV: <gamma_t,top tau_top> = 0.18 fm/c, <gamma_t,W tau_W> = 0.14 fm/c, <tau_d> = 0.34 fm/c
- Transport coefficient used: qhat = 4 GeV^2/fm
- Quenching rescaling factor for baseline full quenching: Q_0 = 0.85
- Effective relative jet energy resolution: 1.5 GeV^(1/2)/sqrt(p_t), about 15% for p_t = 100 GeV
- b-tagging efficiency assumed: epsilon_b = 70%
- Relation between reconstructed top p_t and average total delay time: <tau_tot>(p_t,top^reco) = (0.37 + 0.0022 * p_t,top^reco/GeV) fm/c for 200 GeV < p_t,top^reco < 1 TeV
- A very short-lived medium with tau_m = 1 fm/c could be distinguished from full quenching at the LHC with currently approved luminosity of 10 nb^(-1)
- At HE-LHC (sqrt_s_NN = 11 TeV), the t-tbar cross section is 6 times larger than at LHC

## Context
This work extends the study of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, which has previously used dijet, gamma+jet, and Z+jet production where jets are produced simultaneously with the collision. The authors point out that top-antitop production offers a unique opportunity because the decay chain introduces time delays, unlike all other jet measurements considered in the literature. The approach builds on the concept of decoherence time from Casalderrey-Solana (2012) and uses hydrodynamic simulation codes such as those reviewed by Gale (2013) for context on QGP time evolution. This work enables future experimental studies of top-quark production in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, HE-LHC, and FCC to probe the time evolution of the QGP over the first few fm/c.
