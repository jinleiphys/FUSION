# Nuclear Anapole Moments and the Parity-nonconserving Nuclear Interaction

> The anapole moment is a parity-odd and time-reversal-even electromagnetic moment. Although it was conjectured shortly after the discovery of parity nonconservation, its existence has not been confirmed until recently in heavy nuclear systems, which are known to be the suitable laboratories because of the many-body enhancement. By carefully identifying the nuclear-spin-dependent atomic parity nonconserving effect, the first clear evidence was found in cesium. In this talk, I will discuss how nuclear anapole moments are used to constrain the parity-nonconserving nuclear force, a still less well-known channel among weak interactions.

# Nuclear Anapole Moments and the Parity-nonconserving Nuclear Interaction

## Key claim
Nuclear anapole moments, extracted from atomic parity-nonconserving experiments, can constrain the parity-nonconserving nucleon-nucleon interaction via the six DDH meson coupling constants, but the constraints from cesium and thallium disagree with each other and with existing nuclear PNC results, likely due to nuclear structure uncertainties.

## Method
The nuclear anapole moment is defined through the operator \(\vec{a} = -\pi \int d^3 r \, r^2 \vec{j}(\vec{r}) \equiv \frac{G_F}{\sqrt{2}} \, \kappa_{am} \, \vec{I}\), where \(\kappa_{am}\) characterizes the strength. The extraction involves atomic many-body calculations to relate experimental atomic PNC results to the PNC electron-nucleus interaction, identification of the nuclear-spin-dependent PNC effect by comparing hyperfine levels, and subtraction of contributions from \(Z^0\) exchange and hyperfine interaction. The resulting \(\kappa_{am}\) values are then expressed in terms of the six PNC meson coupling constants (\(h_{\pi}\), \(h_{\rho}^0\), \(h_{\rho}^1\), \(h_{\rho}^2\), \(h_{\omega}^0\), \(h_{\omega}^1\)) using a shell-model calculation with exchange currents and closure approximation.

## Key numbers
- \(\kappa_{am}(\text{Cs}) = 0.090 \pm 0.016\) (cesium, from Colorado group experiment, 7\(\sigma\) determination)
- \(\kappa_{am}(\text{Tl}) = 0.376 \pm 0.400\) (thallium, Seattle group only; consistent with zero)
- The cesium result favors \(h_{\pi} \sim 9\) (in units of \(10^7\)) when combined with \(pp\) bands, but the \(^{18}\text{F}\) result sets a limit \(|h_{\pi}| \le 1\) (in units of \(10^7\))

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Vaks and Zel'dovich (1958), who first conjectured the anapole moment, and Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984), who predicted its nuclear enhancement scaling as \(A^{2/3}\). The experimental evidence from the Colorado group (1997) on cesium provides the first clear constraint on the PNC NN interaction via anapole moments, but the results conflict with existing nuclear PNC measurements such as those from \(^{18}\text{F}\) and \(p\alpha\) experiments. Resolving this discrepancy requires improved nuclear structure calculations for heavy nuclei and new measurements on cesium, thallium, odd-neutron nuclei (Dy, Yb, Ba), and a chain of francium isotopes.
