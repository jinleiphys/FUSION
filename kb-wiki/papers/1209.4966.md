# Density functional approaches to atomic nuclei

> Nuclear mean-field models are briefly reviewed to illustrate its foundation and necessity of state dependence in effective interactions. This state dependence is successfully taken into account by the density dependence, leading to the energy density functional. Recent results for photoabsorption cross sections in spherical and deformed Nd isotopes are shown.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the failure of naive mean-field models to simultaneously reproduce nuclear binding energy and single-particle properties is resolved by introducing state-dependent effective interactions, which are successfully captured through density dependence in the energy density functional. The abstract's claim of "successfully taken into account" is hedged by the paper's demonstration that this density dependence is essential to resolve a fundamental inconsistency in the independent-particle model.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical development from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory through the density-dependent Hartree-Fock (DDHF) method, showing how the state dependence of the G-matrix is replaced by density dependence via the density matrix expansion (DME). For applications, the time-dependent density functional theory is linearized to obtain the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA), which is used to calculate photoabsorption cross sections for Nd isotopes using the Skyrme SkM* functional.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³, with binding energy per particle B/A ≈ 16 MeV (extrapolated for infinite nuclear matter)
- Fermi momentum from saturation density: k_F ≈ 1.36 fm⁻¹, giving Fermi energy T_F ≈ 40 MeV
- Effective mass from naive mean-field analysis: m*/m ≈ 0.4, which disagrees with empirical values of 0.7 ≲ m*/m ≲ 1
- Nuclear matter incompressibility from giant monopole resonances: K_∞ = 210 ± 30 MeV
- Effective mass from giant quadrupole resonance analysis: m*/m ≈ 0.8–1
- Repulsive core radius in nucleon-nucleon interaction: c ≈ 0.5 fm
- Wound integral in BHF theory: κ ≈ 15%
- Calculated energy-weighted sum-rule values for Nd isotopes are about 40% larger than the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum-rule value

## Context

This work extends the foundational development of nuclear density functional theory from the Brueckner theory (Day 1967, Ring and Schuck 1980) and the density matrix expansion by Negele (1970) and Negele and Vautherin (1972). The paper shows that the DDHF approach, which is essentially identical to the Skyrme energy functional (Vautherin and Brink 1972), resolves the Coester band problem where BHF calculations could not simultaneously reproduce binding energy and saturation density. The results for Nd isotopes demonstrate that the time-dependent density functional approach can describe giant resonances in shape transitional nuclei without adjustable parameters, enabling systematic studies of collective excitations across the nuclear chart.
