# Universal aspects of Efimov states and light halo nuclei

> The parametric region in the plane defined by the ratios of the energies of the subsystems and the three body ground state, in which Efimov states can exist, is determined. We use a renormalizable model that guarantees the general validity of our results in the context of short range interactions. The experimental data for one and two-neutron separation energies, implies that among the halo-nuclei candidates, only C-20 has a possible Efimov state, with energy less than 14 KeV below the scattering threshold.

# Universal aspects of Efimov states and light halo nuclei

## Key claim

Using a renormalizable zero-range three-body model, the authors determine the parametric region in the plane of two-body subsystem energies and three-body ground-state energy where Efimov states can exist. Based on experimental one- and two-neutron separation energies, only the halo nucleus 20C is found to possibly host an Efimov state, with an energy less than 14 keV below the scattering threshold, and only if the neutron separation energy of 19C lies between approximately 50 keV and 200 keV.

## Method

The authors solve coupled s-wave zero-range integral equations for a three-body system of two identical neutrons and a core, using a renormalization procedure that replaces potential form factors and strengths with the two-body binding or virtual-state energies (Enn and Enc) and the three-body ground-state energy as input. The boundary curves for the existence of an excited Efimov state are computed by finding where the (N+1)-th three-body binding energy equals the lowest scattering threshold, for various core mass numbers A. Experimental separation energies for halo nuclei are then plotted on these universal curves to identify candidates.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron virtual-state energy is 143 keV.
- The neutron-core virtual-state energy of 10Li is approximately 50 keV, ruling out an Efimov state in 11Li.
- The three-body ground-state energy of 20C is fixed at 3506 keV.
- The neutron separation energy of 19C is 162 +/- 112 keV; an Efimov state in 20C exists only if this value lies between 50 keV and 200 keV.
- The binding energy of the Efimov state in 20C relative to the n-(nc) scattering threshold is below 14 keV.
- The estimated size of this excited state is at least 35 fm.
- For A=18 (relevant to 20C), the non-dimensional ground-state energy epsilon_0 is 5.41 x 10^-2, the first excited state epsilon_1 is 3.46 x 10^-4, and the second excited state epsilon_2 is 2.38 x 10^-6.

## Context

This work extends the universal three-body approach of Efimov (1970) and the Thomas effect (1935) to light halo nuclei, building on earlier calculations by Fedorov, Jensen and Riisager (1994) who suggested 18C and 20C as promising candidates. The authors use a renormalizable zero-range model following Adhikari, Frederico and Goldman (1995) to map the parametric conditions for Efimov states, and find that among halo nuclei only 20C satisfies those conditions, contradicting earlier expectations for 18C, 12Be, and 11Li. The results enable experimental searches for an Efimov state in 20C with a binding energy below 14 keV and a spatial extent of at least 35 fm.
