# A solvable model for octupole phonons

> A solvable model is proposed for the description of octupole phonons in closed-shell nuclei, formulated in terms of shell-model par\-ticle--hole excitations. With some simple assumptions concerning single-particle energies and two-body interactions, closed expressions are derived for the energy and wave function of the octupole phonon. In particular, it is shown that the components of the octupole phonon are proportional to Wigner $3j$ coefficients. This analytic wave function is proven to be exactly valid in light nuclei, which have $LS$ shell closures that coincide with those of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator, and to be valid to a good approximation in heavier nuclei, which have $jj$ shell closures due to the spin--orbit interaction. The properties of the solvable model are compared with the results of a realistic shell-model calculation for $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a solvable model for octupole phonons in doubly-closed-shell nuclei that yields analytic wave functions proportional to Wigner 3j coefficients, but this analytic form is proven to be exactly valid only in light nuclei with LS shell closures coinciding with the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator, while in heavier nuclei with jj shell closures it is valid only to a good approximation.

## Method

The model assumes degenerate single-particle energies above and below the shell closures and a surface delta interaction (SDI) among nucleons. Under these assumptions, the Hamiltonian matrix for particle-hole excitations reduces to a separable form, allowing the neutron and proton octupole phonon spaces to decouple approximately from the full particle-hole space. The resulting 2x2 matrix for the coupled neutron-proton system is diagonalized analytically, and the wave function components are expressed in terms of Wigner 3j coefficients. The analytic results are compared with numerical shell-model calculations for 208Pb using realistic single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The off-diagonal matrix element coupling neutron and proton octupole phonons is approximately Vνπ ≈ -(a1 + 3a0)/4 × √(SνSπ), where a0 and a1 are the isoscalar and isovector SDI strength parameters
- The neutron and proton octupole phonon energies are approximately E(3-cρ) ≈ Δερ - (a1ρ/2)Sρ, where Sρ is the sum over particle-hole configurations of (2j+1)(2j'+1) times the squared 3j coefficient
- The radial integral I_{nℓn'ℓ'}^{(λ)} is given by an analytic expression involving factorials and binomial coefficients, with p = (ℓ+ℓ'+λ+1)/2
- For 208Pb, the single-particle space includes 6 neutron hole orbitals below N=126, 7 neutron particle orbitals above, 5 proton hole orbitals below Z=82, and 6 proton particle orbitals above, with approximately 35000 two-body matrix elements in the realistic calculation

## Context

This work extends the shell-model description of octupole vibrations in closed-shell nuclei, building on the approach of Brown (2000) for the 3- state in 208Pb. The solvable model provides analytic wave functions that can guide studies of more complex systems such as odd-mass nuclei with particle-phonon coupling (Ralet, 2019) or double octupole phonon states via two-particle-two-hole excitations. The paper shows that while realistic single-particle energies destroy the simple decoupling property, the neutron-proton interaction restores approximate collectivity and the Wigner 3j phases remain correct even in realistic calculations.
