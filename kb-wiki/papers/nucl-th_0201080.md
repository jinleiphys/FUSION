# Regular spectra in the vibron model with random interactions

> The phenomenom of emerging regular spectral features from random interactions is addressed in the context of the vibron model. A mean-field analysis links different regions of the parameter space with definite geometric shapes. The results that are, to a large extent, obtained in closed analytic form, provide a clear and transparent interpretation of the high degree of order that has been observed in numerical studies.

## Key claim

The regular spectral features (dominance of \(L=0\) ground states and vibrational/rotational band structures) that emerge from random interactions in the vibron model can be explained by a mean-field analysis that links different regions of the parameter space to definite geometric shapes (spherical, deformed, and \(p\)-boson condensate), with the results largely obtained in closed analytic form.

## Method

The authors studied the vibron model (an algebraic \(U(4)\) model for two-body relative motion) with a general one- and two-body Hamiltonian whose seven parameters were taken as independent Gaussian random numbers with zero mean. They performed exact numerical diagonalizations (100,000 runs) to obtain the distribution of ground-state angular momenta and the probability distribution of the excitation energy ratio \(R = (E_{2_1}-E_{0_1})/(E_{1_1}-E_{0_1})\). Separately, they applied a Hartree-Bose mean-field analysis using coherent (intrinsic) states to classify equilibrium shapes (spherical, deformed, \(p\)-boson condensate) and computed the moment of inertia via the Thouless-Valatin formula to predict ground-state angular momenta.

## Key numbers

* For even \(N\), the ground state has \(L=0\) in \(\sim 71\%\) of cases; for odd \(N\), in \(\sim 54\%\) of cases (exact numerical results).
* The ground state has the maximum angular momentum \(L=N\) in \(\sim 24\%\) of cases (exact numerical results).
* The probability distribution \(P(R)\) of the energy ratio \(R = (E_{2_1}-E_{0_1})/(E_{1_1}-E_{0_1})\) shows two pronounced peaks: one at the vibrational value \(R=2\) and one at the rotational value \(R=3\).
* Mean-field analysis yields probabilities for equilibrium shapes: spherical shape \(P_1 = 0.396\) (about \(40\%\)), deformed shape \(P_2 = 0.216\) (about \(20\%\)), and \(p\)-boson condensate \(P_3 = 0.388\) (about \(40\%\)).
* For the schematic Hamiltonian, the ground state has \(L=0\) in \(75\%\) of cases for \(N\) even and \(50\%\) for \(N\) odd, with \(L=N\) in \(25\%\) of cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of random interactions in the nuclear shell model (Jin et al., 2001) and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) (Bijker and Frank, 2000), which had shown a surprising statistical preference for \(0^+\) ground states and band structures despite random two-body matrix elements. The present analysis provides an analytic mean-field interpretation for the vibron model, showing that the regular features arise from robust geometric phases (spherical and deformed shapes) rather than from details of angular momentum coupling. The authors note that similar methods applied to the IBM (Bijker and Frank, 2001) yield qualitatively the same results, suggesting a general phenomenon for systems of interacting bosons.
