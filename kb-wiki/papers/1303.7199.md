# Quasielastic and multinucleon excitations in antineutrino-nucleus interactions

> We investigate the MiniBooNE recent data on the antineutrino nucleus interaction, using the same theoretical description with the same parameters as in previous works on neutrino interactions. The double differential quasielastic cross section, which is free from the energy reconstruction problem, is well reproduced by our model once the multinucleon excitations are incorporated. A similar agreement is achieved for the Q^2 distribution.

## Key claim

The authors show that their nuclear model, which includes multinucleon (np-nh) excitations exclusively in the spin-isospin channels and uses the free value of the axial mass, successfully reproduces the MiniBooNE antineutrino data without any parameter changes from their previous neutrino calculations, though a systematic but slight underestimation of the data is noted that could be resolved by a reduction of the data within its 17.2% normalization uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute flux-averaged charged-current quasielastic antineutrino-12C cross sections using a random phase approximation (RPA) treatment for the genuine quasielastic (1p-1h) part, and incorporate two particle-two hole (np-nh) excitations based on the work of Alberico et al. (1983) for the transverse magnetic response, with the non-pionic in-medium Delta decay parameterized from Oset et al. (1987). The model uses the same nuclear response functions and parameters as in their prior neutrino studies (Martini et al., 2009, 2010, 2011), with relativistic corrections applied to the nuclear responses, and the antineutrino flux from the MiniBooNE publication (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2013).

## Key numbers

- The double differential cross section per proton for antineutrino-12C is shown for muon kinetic energies from 0.2 GeV to 2.0 GeV, with the lowest bin (0.2 MeV < T_mu < 0.3 MeV) showing disagreement where theory is lower than data.
- The differential cross section dσ/dcosθ is integrated over muon kinetic energies 0.2 GeV < T_mu < 2.0 GeV.
- The Q^2 distribution shows that RPA effects disappear beyond Q^2 ≳ 0.3 GeV^2, where the multinucleon component is required.
- The experimental data have an additional normalization uncertainty of 17.2% (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2013).
- A reduction of the MiniBooNE data by 17% makes the agreement theory-experiment excellent for the Q^2 distribution.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior successful description of neutrino-nucleus interactions (Martini et al., 2009, 2010, 2011) to the antineutrino channel, testing the same nuclear model against MiniBooNE antineutrino data (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2013). The model differs from other approaches (Amaro et al., 2010, 2011; Nieves et al., 2011, 2013; Meucci et al., 2011, 2012) by restricting multinucleon excitations to spin-isospin channels, leading to a predicted asymmetry between neutrino and antineutrino cross sections that is important for CP violation studies. The work confirms that no modification of the axial mass is needed when multinucleon processes are included, and enables further investigation of nuclear effects in neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry.
