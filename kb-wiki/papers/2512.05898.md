# Differentially rotating neutron stars with dark matter cores

> Dark matter is expected to accumulate inside neutron stars, modifying the structure of isolated stars and influencing both the dynamics of binary mergers and the evolution of the resulting hypermassive remnants. Since differential rotation is the primary mechanism delaying the collapse of these remnants, understanding its behavior is crucial when assessing the impact of an embedded dark component. In this work, we extend the numerical code RNS to describe two gravitationally coupled fluids in differential rotation, with baryonic matter modeled by a realistic nuclear equation of state and dark matter represented as a self-interacting bosonic condensate. Within this framework, we construct equilibrium sequences for a representative differential rotation law, providing a basis to explore how dark matter may influence the global properties and rotational dynamics of binary neutron star remnants.

## Key claim

The paper extends the numerical code RNS to model differentially rotating neutron stars with dark matter cores, constructing equilibrium sequences for a representative differential rotation law. The authors find that the presence of a dark matter component generally reduces the maximum gravitational mass supported by the configuration, but this reduction is softened by rotation, with the detailed behavior depending on how angular momentum is partitioned between baryonic and dark matter fluids.

## Method

The authors extended the RNS code, which solves Einstein's field equations under stationary and axial symmetry using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu scheme, to treat two gravitationally coupled fluids in differential rotation. Baryonic matter was modeled using the relativistic density functional DD2 equation of state, while dark matter was represented as a self-interacting bosonic condensate described by a zero-temperature equation of state derived from a complex scalar field Lagrangian. Equilibrium sequences were constructed for two families of differential rotation profiles: toroidal (Type C) configurations with rotation parameters (λ₁, λ₂) = (2, 0.5) and quasi-spherical (Type A) configurations with (λ₁, λ₂) = (1.8, 1), using the rotation law Ω(j) = Ω_c (1 + j/(B²Ω_c)) / (1 + (j/(A²Ω_c))⁴).

## Key numbers

- Dark matter mass fraction f^M_DM = 5% was adopted, consistent with observations of 2 M⊙ neutron stars together with the Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 constraint from LIGO-Virgo
- For toroidal configurations with f^M_DM = 5% and f^J_DM = 1%, the maximum mass at zero rotation was M_max,J=0 = 2.059 M⊙, compared to 2.398 M⊙ for the pure baryonic case
- For toroidal configurations, sequences were computed up to total angular momentum J_tot = 8 in units of G M⊙² c⁻¹
- For quasi-spherical configurations, sequences were computed up to J_tot = 5 in the same units
- The fitting parameters for the normalized maximum mass relation M̂(Ĵ²_tot) = 1 + (α Ĵ²_tot + β Ĵ⁴_tot)/(1 + γ Ĵ²_tot) for the (f^M_DM=5%, f^J_DM=1%) case were α = 0.269, β = 0.022, γ = 0.454
- For the (f^M_DM=5%, f^J_DM=5%) case, fitting parameters were α = 0.365, β = 0.168, γ = 1.316
- For the constant angular velocity case (Ω_DM/Ω^DM_K = 3/4), fitting parameters were α = 0.396, β = 0.164, γ = 1.739
- For models with Ω_DM = 0.75 Ω^DM_K, most configurations had f^J_DM < 2%, with outliers reaching up to f^J_DM = 12.3% only in slowly rotating sequences with J_tot < 2
- For J_tot = 1, configurations with f^J_DM > 4% occurred for M_tot > 1.64 M⊙ (maximum stable mass 2.12 M⊙); for J_tot = 2, for M_tot > 2.14 M⊙ (maximum stable mass 2.19 M⊙)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of static or uniformly rotating dark matter-admixed neutron stars (Bezares 2019, Bauswein 2020, Emma 2022, Routaray 2024, Cronin 2023, Konstantinou 2024, Mourelle 2024, Cipriani 2025, Giangrandi 2025) into the regime of differential rotation, which is the primary mechanism delaying collapse of hypermassive neutron star remnants formed in binary mergers. The quasi-equilibrium approach complements computationally expensive dynamical simulations (Shibata 1999, Baiotti 2016, Paschalidis 2016) and enables systematic exploration of parameter space, building on earlier one-fluid studies of differentially rotating stars (Uryu 2017, 10.1093/mnras/stab392, 10.1093/mnras/stab3565). The results provide a foundation for interpreting post-merger gravitational wave spectra and identifying possible multimessenger signatures of dark matter in binary neutron star remnants, with the caveat that alternative differential rotation laws may be needed to model the highest angular momentum scenarios expected after mergers.
