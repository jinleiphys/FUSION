# Universal physics of three bosons with isospin

> We show that there exist two types of universal phenomena for three-boson systems with isospin degrees of freedom. In the isospin symmetric limit, there is only one universal three-boson bound state with the total isospin one, whose binding energy is proportional to that of the two-boson bound state. With large isospin symmetry breaking, the standard Efimov states of three identical bosons appear at low energies. Both phenomena can be realized by three pions with the pion mass appropriately tuned in lattice QCD simulations, or by spin-one bosons in cold atom experiments. Implication to the in-medium softening of multi-pion states is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that three-boson systems with isospin degrees of freedom exhibit two distinct types of universal phenomena depending on the symmetry breaking scale: a single universal bound state with total isospin one in the isospin symmetric limit, and the standard infinite Efimov spectrum when isospin symmetry is broken. The abstract glosses over the condition that the two-body scattering length must be much larger than the interaction range, and that the specific numerical result for the single bound state (B3 = 1.04391/(ma^2)) only holds for positive inverse scattering length.

## Method

The authors construct a zero-range effective field theory for three bosons with three internal degrees of freedom (isospin), using a contact interaction that preserves isospin symmetry. They solve the coupled-channel three-body integral equation for the scattering amplitude, projecting onto s-wave and searching for bound state poles in the complex energy plane. For the isospin-breaking case, they introduce a mass splitting between charged and neutral pions and solve the single-channel Efimov equation with a cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- Three-pion bound state energy in the isospin symmetric limit: B3 = 1.04391/(ma^2) for 1/a > 0, where a is the I=0 s-wave ππ scattering length and m is the pion mass
- Three-pion virtual state energy for 1/a < 0: V3 = 1.04391/(ma^2)
- Efimov scaling parameter: s0 = 1.00624, giving the geometric spectrum En → -e^{-2πn/s0} κ*^2/m for large n
- Critical λ values for classification: λ < 2.41840 produces Efimov states, 2.41840 < λ < 3.66811 produces a single universal state, λ > 3.66811 produces no bound state
- Ratio of three-body to two-body binding energies in the isospin symmetric limit: B3/B2 = 1.04391
- Energy separation factor between successive Efimov states: 515

## Context

This work extends the universal Efimov physics of three identical bosons (Braaten 2004) to systems with internal degrees of freedom, specifically the isospin of pions. It builds on earlier studies of universal bound states in the D0D0D̄*0 system (Braaten 2003) which found no bound state due to coupled-channel effects, and on lattice QCD results showing that the σ meson becomes a bound state for heavy quark masses (Kunihiro 2003). The classification by the parameter λ provides a unified framework for understanding when Efimov states, a single universal state, or no bound states appear. The results enable experimental tests using lattice QCD simulations of three pions with tuned quark masses, and suggest the possibility of observing universal three-pion bound states in nuclear matter near chiral symmetry restoration.
