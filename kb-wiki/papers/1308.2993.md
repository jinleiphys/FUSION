# Sudakov Double Logarithms Resummation in Hard Processes in Small-x Saturation Formalism

> In this manuscript, we present a complete study of the Sudakov double logarithms resummation for various hard processes in eA and pA collisions in the small-x saturation formalism. We first employ a couple of slightly different formalisms to perform the one-loop analysis of the Higgs boson production process in pA collisions, and demonstrate that Sudakov-type logarithms arise as the leading correction and that they can be systematically resummed in addition to the usual small-x resummation. We further study the Sudakov double logarithms for other processes such as heavy quark pair production and back-to-back dijet production in eA and pA collisions through detailed calculation of the corresponding one-loop diagrams. As the most important contribution from the one-loop correction, the Sudakov factor should play an important role in the phenomenological study of saturation physics in the pA programs at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Sudakov double logarithms (of the form \(\bar \alpha_s \ln^2 (P_\perp^2/k_\perp^2)\)) can be systematically resummed alongside the usual small-\(x\) resummation in the saturation formalism for hard processes in \(eA\) and \(pA\) collisions, with the caveat that the color factor \(\mathcal{C}\) in the Sudakov term is process-dependent and must be determined case by case for each partonic channel.

## Method

The authors perform one-loop analyses of Higgs boson production, heavy quark pair production, and back-to-back dijet production in \(pA\) and \(eA\) collisions using the dilute-dense (hybrid) factorization formalism, where the proton is treated as dilute with collinear parton distributions and the nucleus as dense with unintegrated gluon distributions. They employ three complementary approaches: momentum-space evaluation, coordinate-space dipole model calculations with splitting functions, and a heuristic derivation, to isolate the Sudakov double logarithmic contributions from soft gluon radiation in the kinematic region where the radiated gluon momentum fractions satisfy \(\alpha_q \sim \beta_q \ll 1\). The collinear divergences parallel to the nucleon are absorbed into DGLAP evolution, the rapidity divergences parallel to the nucleus into BK-JIMWLK small-\(x\) evolution, and the soft gluon divergences into the Sudakov factor via Collins-Soper-Sterman resummation.

## Key numbers

- The Sudakov double logarithmic term is of the form \(\bar \alpha_s \ln^2 (P_\perp^2/k_\perp^2)\) where \(\bar \alpha_s \equiv \frac{\alpha_s \mathcal{C}}{2\pi}\), and this term can become of order 1 when \(P_\perp^2 \gg k_\perp^2\).
- For Higgs boson production in \(pA\) collisions, the Sudakov double logarithmic term is \(\bar \alpha_s \ln^2 (M^2/k_\perp^2)\) where \(M\) is the Higgs mass and \(k_\perp\) its transverse momentum.
- For heavy quark pair production in DIS, the Sudakov double logarithmic term contains an additional factor of \(1/2\) compared to the Higgs production case.
- The Sudakov factor in the resummed cross section is expressed as \(\mathcal{S}_{sud} = \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \mathcal{C} \int_{c_0^2/R_\perp^2}^{P_\perp^2} \frac{d\mu^2}{\mu^2} \ln \frac{P_\perp^2}{\mu^2}\), where \(R_\perp = x_\perp - y_\perp\) is the transverse coordinate separation and \(c_0\) is a constant defined in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculations of dijet production in \(pA\) collisions by Dominguez et al. (2010, 2011) by providing the one-loop corrections and demonstrating that Sudakov resummation can be consistently combined with small-\(x\) resummation via BK-JIMWLK evolution. The results enable phenomenological studies of saturation physics at RHIC and the LHC by providing the necessary resummation framework for comparing differential cross sections (particularly the transverse momentum imbalance \(k_\perp\) distribution) to experimental data, and the authors note that computation of single Sudakov logarithms and subleading contributions requires a matrix formalism similar to that used by Kidonakis et al. (1997) for threshold resummation.
