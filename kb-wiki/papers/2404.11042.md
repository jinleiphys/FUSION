# Polarization phenomenon in heavy-ion collisions

> The strongly interacting system created in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions behaves almost as an ideal fluid with rich patterns of the velocity field exhibiting strong vortical structure. Vorticity of the fluid, via spin-orbit coupling, leads to particle spin polarization. Due to the finite orbital momentum of the system, the polarization on average is not zero; it depends on the particle momenta reflecting the spatial variation of the local vorticity. In the last few years, this field experienced a rapid growth due to experimental discoveries of the global and local polarizations. Recent measurements triggered further development of the theoretical description of the spin dynamics and suggestions of several new mechanisms for particle polarization. In this review, we focus mostly on the experimental results. We compare the measurements with the existing theoretical calculations but try to keep the discussion of possible underlying physics at the qualitative level. Future measurements and how they can help to answer open theoretical questions are also discussed. We pay a special attention to the employed experimental methods, as well as to the detector effects and associated corrections to the measurements.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental results on hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions, showing that the observed global polarization is dominated by thermal vorticity rather than magnetic field contributions, but notes that the theoretical understanding remains qualitative and that differential measurements (e.g., as a function of transverse momentum, rapidity, and centrality) still show disagreements between data and model calculations.

## Method

The authors derive acceptance correction formulas for polarization measurements along the initial angular momentum and along the beam direction, accounting for detector effects and elliptic flow. They present a Blast-Wave model to estimate the longitudinal component of vorticity and resulting polarization from anisotropic flow, and derive equations for correcting spin alignment measurements of vector mesons that account for the interplay between elliptic flow and tracking efficiency effects.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7–39 GeV was first observed by STAR, with later confirmation at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Vorticity estimated from BES-I data: ω ≈ (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s⁻¹, leading to the finding of "the most vortical fluid ever observed"
- Upper limit on magnetic field from particle-antiparticle polarization difference: B ≲ 10^13 T, assuming T = 150 MeV and ignoring feed-down contributions
- Blast-Wave model estimate for z-polarization: P_z ≈ 0.1 sin(nφ_s)[b_n - a_n], with ρ_{t,nmax} ∼ 1, R ∼ 10 fm, T ∼ 100 MeV, and coefficients a_n, b_n < 0.1
- Global polarization at LHC energies expected to be 0.15–0.2 of that at top RHIC energy, based on directed flow comparisons between Pb–Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and Au–Au at 200 GeV
- Magnetic moment of Ω⁻ hyperon: μ_Ω⁻ = -2.02 μ_N, three times larger than μ_Λ = -0.613 μ_N

## Context

This review extends the work of Becattini (2007) on statistical methods for vortical fluids and Becattini (2013) on hydrodynamic calculations with local angular momentum equilibrium. It compares experimental results from STAR (2017, 2018, 2021) and ALICE (2019) with theoretical models including hydrodynamic calculations by Karpenko (2016), Xie (2017), and Ivanov (2020), chiral kinetic approaches by Sun (2017), and transport models by Li (2017). The paper enables future measurements of polarization differences between particles and antiparticles to constrain magnetic field lifetimes and QGP electric conductivity, as well as differential measurements to distinguish between competing theoretical mechanisms for spin polarization.
