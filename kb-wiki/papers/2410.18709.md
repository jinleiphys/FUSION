# Efficient simulation of quarkonium master equation beyond the dipole approximation

> QTRAJ is a computer code that simulates the propagation of quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) based on the quantum-trajectory algorithm. This algorithm solves a master equation in which the quarkonium is treated as an open quantum system (OQS). A major advantage of this approach is that it turns a 3D spatial evolution for a density matrix into a 1D Schrödinger equation for a wavefunction with a non-hermitian Hamiltonian, drastically reducing the computational cost. So far, the interaction implemented in the master equation was obtained within the framework of potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD), and restricted to the regime $rT \ll 1$, where $r$ is the size of the color dipole and $T$ is the temperature. In the environment produced in heavy-ion collisions (HIC's) this limit is accurate for $Υ(1S)$, but the applicability to other quarkonium states is dubious. In the present study we generalize the above approach, extending it to the regime $rT\!\sim\! 1$ in the one-gluon exchange approximation, with proper Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation of medium effects. This is done by implementing new jump operators connecting different color states of the $Q\bar Q$ pair and expanding them in plane waves, giving rise to a variation of the algorithm present in QTRAJ 1.0. Here we provide an overview of this approach comparing the $rT \ll 1$ and $rT\sim 1$ cases, and we discuss prospects for phenomenological application to excited states of bottomonium.

# Efficient simulation of quarkonium master equation beyond the dipole approximation

## Key claim

The authors extend the QTRAJ quantum-trajectory code for quarkonium propagation in the quark-gluon plasma from the previously restricted regime \(rT \ll 1\) (accurate only for \(\Upsilon(1S)\)) to the regime \(rT \sim 1\) using one-gluon exchange with Hard Thermal Loop resummation, but they note that at experimentally accessible temperatures the coupling is not weak (\(g \sim 2\)) and the scale separation required by HTL is only marginally satisfied, so the Debye mass is treated as a free parameter.

## Method

The Lindblad master equation for the reduced density matrix of a heavy quark-antiquark pair in the quark-gluon plasma is solved via the quantum-trajectory algorithm, which converts the 3D density-matrix evolution into a 1D Schrodinger equation with a non-hermitian Hamiltonian. New jump operators connecting color-singlet and color-octet states are derived from the HTL approximation beyond the dipole limit, including a novel parity-conserving octet-to-octet transition operator \(C_{\bm q}^{2}\). The algorithm selects transitions sequentially by color channel, transferred linear momentum, angular momentum of the exchanged gluon, and angular momentum of the final state, using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the latter.

## Key numbers

* The in-medium hermitian Hamiltonian includes a Debye-screened potential \(\frac{\alpha_s}{r}e^{-m_D r}\) with prefactors \(-C_F\) for the singlet and \(1/2N_c\) for the octet channel.
* The asymptotic maximum-entropy state corresponds to \(\rho_s = 1/9\) and \(\rho_o = 8/9\), used as a consistency check for the code.
* Example simulations are performed at a medium temperature of \(T = 700\) MeV with pairs initialized to an average radius \(\langle r \rangle = 0.2\) fm.
* The medium undergoes Bjorken expansion with power-law cooling \(T \sim t^{-1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the QTRAJ 1.0 code (Omar 2021, Brambilla 2022) which was restricted to the dipole approximation \(rT \ll 1\) within pNRQCD. The new implementation enables phenomenological application to excited bottomonium states such as \(\Upsilon(2S)\) and \(\Upsilon(3S)\), where the dipole approximation is unreliable. The theoretical framework builds on the HTL-based open quantum system approach of Blaizot (2018) and Akamatsu (2020), reformulating their results into Lindblad jump operators suitable for numerical implementation.
