# The quark loop calculation of the gamma -> 3 pi form factor

> The presently experimentally interesting form factor for the anomalous process gamma -> pi^+ pi^0 pi^- is calculated as the quark "box"-amplitude where the intermediate fermion loop is the one of constituent quarks with the pseudoscalar coupling to pions. This also corresponds to the form factor, in the lowest order in pion interactions, of the sigma-model and of the chiral quark model. We give the analytic expression for the form factor in terms of an expansion in the pion momenta up to the order O(p^8) relative to the soft point result, and also perform its exact numerical evaluation. We compare our predictions with those of the vector meson dominance and chiral perturbation theory, as well as with the scarce data available so far.

## Key claim

The quark loop model (equivalent to the lowest-order sigma-model and chiral quark model) provides an analytic expansion of the gamma -> 3pi form factor up to order O(p^8) relative to the soft-point anomaly result, but the model's predictions agree with vector meson dominance only for constituent quark masses between 300 and 330 MeV at higher s, and with chiral perturbation theory only for the largest considered mass (M = m_rho/2 = 385 MeV), while the existing single data point is likely an overestimate.

## Method

The authors computed the gamma -> pi+ pi0 pi- form factor as the sum of six "box" diagrams with a loop of constituent quarks (u and d) coupled pseudoscalarly to pions via the Goldberger-Treiman relation g/M = 1/f_pi. They evaluated the resulting Feynman integrals both exactly (numerically via Gauss-Kronrod integration) and analytically by expanding the integrand in powers of pion momenta up to O(p^8), then compared their results to vector meson dominance (Rudaz 1984), chiral perturbation theory (Bijnens 1990, Holstein 1996), and the single experimental data point from Antipov et al. (1987).

## Key numbers

- Soft-point anomaly amplitude: F_gamma^{3pi}(0,0,0) = e N_c / (12 pi^2 f_pi^3) with N_c = 3 and f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- For on-shell pions (t = m_pi^2), the normalized form factor expansion begins: 1 + m_pi^2/(2 M^2) + m_pi^4/(4 M^4) + 169 m_pi^6/(1260 M^6) + 193 m_pi^8/(2520 M^8)
- For the CEBAF-relevant case (t = -m_pi^2), the constant term is: 1 + m_pi^2/(6 M^2) + m_pi^4/(20 M^4) + 13 m_pi^6/(1260 M^6) + m_pi^8/(360 M^8)
- The massless pion amplitude (m_pi = 0) is smaller than the one with m_pi = 138.5 MeV by typically 4% when M = m_rho/2 = 385 MeV, by about 6% when M = 330 MeV, and by more than 10% when M = 250 MeV
- The quark-loop threshold for on-shell q-qbar production is s_tr = (2M)^2; for M = 250 MeV this is s_tr ≈ 13.03 m_pi^2
- The CEBAF experiment accesses s < 16 m_pi^2; the threshold cannot be reached unless M < 2 m_pi = 277 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-loop calculation of the anomalous gamma -> 3pi process beyond the soft-point chiral limit result of Adler et al. (1971), Terentev (1972), and Aviv and Zee (1972), providing the first analytic expansion to O(p^8) in this approach. It complements the numerical-only treatment of the box graph in the Schwinger-Dyson approach by Alkofer and Roberts (1996). The results enable comparison with upcoming Primakoff measurements at CERN (Moinester et al. 1999) and the TJNAF/CEBAF experiment (Miskimen et al. 1994), which should distinguish between the quark-loop model, vector meson dominance, and chiral perturbation theory at higher momentum transfers.
