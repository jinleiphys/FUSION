# Microscopic study of $M$1 resonances in Sn isotopes

> The magnetic dipole ($M$1) resonances of even-even $^{112-120, 124}$Sn isotopes are investigated in the framework of the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) + BCS and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). The Skyrme energy density functionals SLy5 and T11 with and without tensor terms are adopted in our calculations. The mixed type pairing interaction is used to take care of the pairing effect for open-shell nuclei both in the ground and excited states calculations. The calculated magnetic dipole strengths are compared with available experimental data. The QRPA results calculated by SLy5 and T11 with tensor force show a better agreement with the experimental data than those without the tensor force. By analyzing the HF and QRPA strength distributions of $^{112}$Sn and $^{124}$Sn, we discuss the effect of tensor force on the $M$1 resonances in detail. It is found that the $M$1 resonance is sensitive to the tensor interaction, and favors especially a negative triplet-odd tensor one. Depending on the nucleus, a quenching factor of the $M$1 operator of about 0.71-0.95 is needed to reproduce the total observed transition strength. In our calculations, we also find some low-lying, pygmy-type magnetic dipole states distributed below 6.0 MeV, and they are formed mainly from the neutron configuration $ν$2$d_{5/2}$$\rightarrow$$ν$2$d_{3/2}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including tensor terms in Skyrme energy density functionals (specifically a negative triplet-odd tensor term) significantly improves agreement with experimental M1 resonance data in Sn isotopes, but a quenching factor of 0.71-0.95 must still be applied to the M1 operator to reproduce the total observed transition strength, indicating that the tensor force alone does not resolve the long-standing quenching problem.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) + BCS and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations for even-even 112-120,124Sn isotopes using the SLy5 and T11 Skyrme energy density functionals, both with and without tensor terms. They employed a mixed-type pairing interaction and solved the HF+BCS equations in coordinate space with a 24 fm box, building a QRPA model space up to ΔN=8 major shells. The calculated M1 strength distributions were convoluted with a Lorentzian width of 2.0 MeV and compared to experimental data from inelastic proton scattering at RCNP.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor of the M1 operator needed to reproduce total observed transition strength: 0.71-0.95, depending on the nucleus
- Low-lying pygmy-type magnetic dipole states found below 6.0 MeV, formed mainly from the neutron configuration ν2d5/2 → ν2d3/2
- For 120Sn, the main QRPA peak appears at 9.2 MeV (shifted downward from 9.6 MeV in the filling approximation)
- Correlation coefficients between unperturbed M1 peak energies and spin-orbit strength W0: rfit=0.86 for low-lying states and 0.91 for high-lying states in 124Sn (for EDFs without tensor terms)
- Pairing strength V0 adjusted to reproduce the empirical neutron gap in 120Sn: Δn=1.392 MeV

## Context

This work extends non-relativistic Skyrme-based studies of M1 resonances to the new experimental data on Sn isotopes from Bassauer et al. (2020) at RCNP, which previous relativistic calculations by Paar et al. (2021) had addressed. The systematic comparison of Tij family interactions (Lesinski, 2007) with tensor terms allows the authors to isolate the specific role of the triplet-odd tensor component, complementing earlier work by Colò et al. (2007) on tensor effects in single-particle energies. The results enable future studies of how tensor forces affect spin-isospin excitations across the nuclear chart and may impact calculations of neutral-current neutrino interactions in supernovae and reaction cross sections in nucleosynthesis networks.
