# Constraints on Skyrme Equations of State from Properties of Doubly Magic Nuclei and Ab-Initio Calculations of Low-Density Neutron Matter

> We use properties of doubly-magic nuclei and ab-initio calculations of low-density neutron matter to constrain Skyrme equations of state for neutron-rich conditions. All of these properties are consistent with a Skyrme functional form and a neutron-matter equation of state that depends on three parameters. With a reasonable range for the neutron-matter effective mass, the values of the two other Skyrme parameters are well constrained. This leads to predictions for other quantities. The neutron skins for $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca are predicted to be 0.182(10) fm and 0.173(5) fm, respectively. Other results including the dipole polarizability are discussed.

## Key claim

The properties of doubly-magic nuclei and ab-initio calculations of low-density neutron matter are all consistent with a Skyrme functional form, and the neutron-matter equation of state depends on three parameters; with a reasonable range for the neutron-matter effective mass (0.9 to 1.1), the values of the two other Skyrme parameters are well constrained, leading to predictions for neutron skins and other quantities.

## Method

The authors simultaneously fit the experimental properties of doubly-magic nuclei (binding energies, single-particle energies, charge radii, and neutron radii for 11 nuclei) and theoretical ab-initio data points for the neutron-matter energy at three low densities (0.01, 0.02, and 0.04 fm⁻³) from N³LO chiral effective field theory calculations, Quantum Monte Carlo calculations, and other many-body methods. Starting from the CSkP set of 12 Skyrme energy-density functionals, they refit the Skyrme parameters t₀, t₁, t₂, t₃, x₀, x₃, and the spin-orbit strength W to both the nuclear data and the low-density neutron EOS points, with no constraint on the neutron skin in the fit.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb predicted to be 0.182(10) fm (overall result including variation of neutron-matter effective mass from 0.9 to 1.1)
- Neutron skin of ⁴⁸Ca predicted to be 0.173(5) fm (overall result)
- Symmetry energy J = 33.1(20) MeV (overall result)
- Symmetry energy density derivative L = 52(9) MeV (overall result)
- Symmetry-energy incompressibility K_s = -180(40) MeV (overall result)
- Neutron-matter energy per particle at ρ_n = 0.010 fm⁻³: [E/N] = 3.00(13) MeV
- Neutron-matter energy per particle at ρ_n = 0.020 fm⁻³: [E/N] = 4.37(23) MeV
- Neutron-matter energy per particle at ρ_n = 0.030 fm⁻³: [E/N] = 6.1(7) MeV
- Dipole polarizability of ²⁰⁸Pb predicted to be α_D = 21.3(1.3) fm³ (using droplet model with J and L from the fit)
- Neutron EOS at ρ_n = 0.32 fm⁻³: [E/N] = 37-51 MeV (for the set of 12 EDFs with neutron-matter effective mass range 0.9-1.1)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dutra et al. (2012), who identified the CSkP set of Skyrme functionals that best reproduce empirical nuclear matter properties, and of Brown (2013), who refined these functionals by fitting to doubly-magic nuclei. The key advance is the inclusion of ab-initio calculations of low-density neutron matter from N³LO chiral EFT (Hebeler et al., 2013) and Quantum Monte Carlo methods as additional constraints, which fixes the Skyrme parameters for the neutron equation of state without requiring experimental neutron skin data. The predictions for neutron skins and the dipole polarizability of ²⁰⁸Pb can be tested by the PREX-II parity-violating electron-scattering experiment and planned measurements on ⁴⁸Ca, and the resulting Skyrme EDFs provide a starting point for new supernova equations of state.
