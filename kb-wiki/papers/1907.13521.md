# Heavy isotope production in ${}^{136}\text{Xe}+{}^{208}\text{Pb}$ collisions at $E_\text{c.m.}=514$ MeV

> Employing the quantal diffusion mechanism for multi-nucleon transfer, the double differential cross-sections are calculated for production of primary projectile-like and target-like fragments in collisions of ${}^{136}\text{Xe}+{}^{208}\text{Pb}$ system at $E_\text{c.m.} =514$ MeV. Including de-excitation due to neutron emission, the cross-section for production of ${}^{210}\text{Po}$, ${}^{222}\text{Rn}$ and ${}^{224}\text{Ra}$ isotopes are estimated and compared with data.

## Key claim

The quantal diffusion description based on the stochastic mean-field approach, using curvature parameters determined at a higher bombarding energy (526 MeV), can reproduce measured production cross-sections for heavy neutron-rich isotopes in 136Xe+208Pb collisions at 514 MeV after accounting for de-excitation by neutron emission, with estimated cross-sections falling within the range of the data.

## Method

The authors employed the quantal diffusion mechanism derived from the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach to calculate double differential cross-sections for primary projectile-like and target-like fragments in 136Xe+208Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy 514 MeV. Neutron, proton, and mixed dispersions were determined by solving coupled differential equations using diffusion coefficients from TDHF single-particle wave functions, with drift coefficient derivatives obtained from curvature parameters of the potential energy surface at the equilibrium position (determined from neighboring systems at 526 MeV). The primary fragment distributions were then used to estimate secondary cross-sections for 210Po, 222Rn, and 224Ra after de-excitation by neutron emission, assuming ranges of emitted neutrons corresponding to excitation energies of approximately 100 MeV and 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: 514 MeV for the 136Xe+208Pb system
- Angular momentum range considered: l_min = 100 to l_max = 280 (in units of ℏ), with Δl = 20 intervals
- Excitation energy cut-off: E* below 50 MeV used to determine angular momentum range
- Neutron variances toward asymmetry direction (σ²_NN<) range from 26.1 at l=100 to 9.08 at l=280
- Proton variances toward asymmetry direction (σ²_ZZ<) range from 19.9 at l=100 to 5.21 at l=280
- Mass dispersions toward asymmetry (σ_AA<) range from 6.89 at l=100 to 4.10 at l=280
- Mass dispersions toward symmetry (σ_AA>) range from 11.1 at l=100 to 4.62 at l=280
- Estimated number of emitted neutrons: ΔN = 11-13 for polonium isotopes, ΔN = 5-8 for radon and radium isotopes
- Final total kinetic energies (TKE) range from 353 MeV at l=100 to 455 MeV at l=280
- Excitation energies (E*) range from 161 MeV at l=100 to 58.8 MeV at l=280

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantal diffusion study of the 136Xe+208Pb system at 526 MeV (Ayik 2019) to a slightly lower bombarding energy of 514 MeV, where experimental data exist from Kozulin (2012). The SMF approach provides a microscopic alternative to phenomenological Langevin-type semi-classical transport models (Zagrebaev 2008, 2011, 2012; Karpov 2017; Saiko 2019) and overcomes the limitation of standard TDHF theory, which underestimates fluctuations around the most probable path. The results enable improved predictions for producing heavy neutron-rich nuclei via multi-nucleon transfer, with planned future work to include more accurate excitation energy determination and fluctuations.
