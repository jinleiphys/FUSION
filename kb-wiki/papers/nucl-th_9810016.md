# Charmed Mesic Nuclei: Bound D and $\bar{D}$ states with ^{208}Pb

> We show that the D^- meson will form narrow bound states with ^{208}Pb. Mean field potentials for the D^0, D^0-bar and D^- in ^{208}Pb are calculated self-consistently using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in local density approximation. The meson-^{208}Pb bound state energies are then calculated by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with these potentials. The experimental confirmation and comparison with the D^0-bar and D^0 will provide distinctive information on the nature of the interaction between the charmed meson and matter.

## Key claim
The D⁻ meson will form narrow bound states with ²⁰⁸Pb, with binding energies between ten and thirty MeV for the 1s level, provided the vector coupling to light quarks is not enhanced above the value used for nucleons; if the stronger vector coupling suggested by kaon-nucleus scattering is used, the D⁻ is bound only by the Coulomb force as an atomic state.

## Method
The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in local density approximation to calculate self-consistent mean-field potentials for D⁰, D̅⁰, and D⁻ in ²⁰⁸Pb. They solved the Dirac equations for confined light quarks and antiquarks in the meson bags, determined in-medium meson masses via the bag stability condition, and then solved the Klein-Gordon equation (converted to a Schrödinger-like equation in momentum space) to obtain bound-state energies.

## Key numbers
- D⁻ 1s binding energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: between 10 and 30 MeV (with the usual ω coupling constant); the Coulomb force alone provides roughly 24 MeV of binding for the 1s state.
- D̅⁰ is bound in ²⁰⁸Pb with the usual ω coupling constant; with the stronger ω coupling (1.4² V_ω^q) the D̅⁰ is not bound.
- D⁰ binding energy is approximately 100 MeV (deeply bound), but expected large strong-interaction widths (10–100 MeV) may make it experimentally inaccessible.
- The current quark masses used are m_q = 5 MeV and m_c = 1300 MeV; the free-space nucleon bag radius is R_N = 0.8 fm.

## Context
This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to nucleons and light mesons (kaons, η, ω) by the same group (Tsushima, Saito, Thomas, and collaborators, 1995–1998), to charmed mesons. It predicts that D⁻ and D̅⁰ can form bound states with ²⁰⁸Pb, in contrast to the deeply bound D⁰ which suffers large absorption. The results enable experimental searches for charmed mesic nuclei, which would test whether light-quark interactions in heavy mesons are identical to those in nucleons and provide information on the Lorentz scalar σ field and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The authors note that experimental detection is challenging due to small production cross sections and background, as discussed with R.S. Hayano.
