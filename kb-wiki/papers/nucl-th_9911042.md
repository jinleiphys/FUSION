# Phenomenological construction of a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction for the superfluid gap equation in finite density systems

> We construct phenomenologically a relativistic particle-particle channel interaction which suits the gap equation for nuclear matter. This is done by introducing a density-independent momentum-cutoff parameter to the relativistic mean field (Hartree and Hartree-Fock) models so as to reproduce the pairing properties obtained by the Bonn-B potential and not to change the saturation property. The interaction so obtained can be used for the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation, but some reservation is necessary for the Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation.

## Key claim
The authors construct a phenomenological relativistic particle-particle channel interaction for the gap equation by introducing a density-independent momentum-cutoff parameter to the relativistic mean field (RMF) interaction, such that it reproduces the pairing properties of the Bonn-B potential without altering the saturation property; however, the method is fully applicable only to the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation, while its use in the Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation is limited to low densities because the cutoff also affects the particle-hole channel and destroys saturation at higher densities.

## Method
The authors start from the standard σ-ω model Lagrangian and derive the antisymmetrized S-wave component of the RMF interaction. They introduce a density-independent momentum-cutoff parameter Λ via four types of form factor (monopole, dipole, strong(a), strong(b)) applied to each nucleon-meson vertex, and determine the optimal Λ by minimizing a χ² function that compares the gap at the Fermi surface Δ(k_F) and the coherence length ξ to those obtained from the Bonn-B potential. The gap equation and the nucleon effective mass equation are solved self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter at 11 Fermi momenta from 0.2 to 1.2 fm⁻¹, using the standard σ-ω parameter set (g_σ² = 91.64, g_ω² = 136.2, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, M = 939 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Optimal cutoff for the monopole form factor: Λ = 7.26 fm⁻¹; for the dipole form factor: Λ = 10.66 fm⁻¹; for the strong(a) form factor: Λ = 10.98 fm⁻¹ (the strong(b) type was discarded due to pathological Λ-dependence).
- In the Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculation with the Serot-Walecka parameter set, the optimal cutoffs are Λ = 3.40 fm⁻¹ (monopole) and Λ = 5.02 fm⁻¹ (dipole); the ratio Λ(monopole)/Λ(dipole) almost coincides with that of the Hartree case.
- The gap at the Fermi surface from the full RHFB calculation (no cutoff) is about 4 MeV larger at the maximum than the corresponding RHB value; with the Bouyssy et al. parameter set, the plateau in the gap appears at a very small value.
- The saturation energy density in the RHFB case with the optimal monopole cutoff approaches about -15.69 MeV in the large-Λ limit, which is 0.38% less bound than the original value of -15.75 MeV from Serot and Walecka.

## Context
This work extends the earlier attempt by Kucharek and Ring (1991) who used a one-boson exchange interaction with RMF coupling constants but obtained pairing gaps about three times larger than accepted non-relativistic values. The authors previously introduced a sudden cutoff method (Matsuzaki and Tanigawa, 1998) and here generalize to smooth form factors. The constructed interaction is intended to be analogous to the non-relativistic Gogny force (Gogny, 1975) in that it reproduces pairing properties of bare interactions while being built for finite-density systems. The results enable Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations with a consistent p-p channel interaction, but the method cannot be straightforwardly extended to Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations at higher densities because the cutoff also modifies the particle-hole channel and disrupts saturation.
