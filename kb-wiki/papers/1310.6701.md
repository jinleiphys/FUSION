# Two-Gluon Correlations in Heavy-Light Ion Collisions: Energy and Geometry Dependence, IR Divergences, and $k_T$-Factorization

> We study the properties of the cross section for two-gluon production in heavy-light ion collisions derived in our previous paper on the subject in the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework. Concentrating on the energy and geometry dependence of the corresponding correlation functions we find that the two-gluon correlator is a much slower function of the center-of-mass energy than the one- and two-gluon production cross sections. The geometry dependence of the correlation function leads to stronger azimuthal near- and away-side correlations in the tip-on-tip U+U collisions than in the side-on-side U+U collisions, an exactly opposite behavior from the correlations generated by the elliptic flow of the quark-gluon plasma: a study of azimuthal correlations in the U+U collisions may thus help to disentangle the two sources of correlations. We demonstrate that the cross section for two-gluon production in heavy-light ion collisions contains a power-law infrared (IR) divergence even for fixed produced gluon momenta: while saturation effects in the target regulate some of the power-law IR-divergent terms in the lowest-order expression for the two-gluon correlator, other power-law IR-divergent terms remain, possibly due to absence of saturation effects in the dilute projectile. Finally we rewrite our result for the two-gluon production cross-section in a $k_T$-factorized form, obtaining a new factorized expression involving a convolution of one- and two-gluon Wigner distributions over both the transverse momenta and impact parameters. We show that the two-gluon production cross-section depends on two different types of unintegrated two-gluon Wigner distribution functions.

## Key claim

The two-gluon correlation function in heavy-light ion collisions is a much slower function of center-of-mass energy than the one- and two-gluon production cross sections, and in the leading power-of-energy approximation it is independent of the center-of-mass energy; however, this result is derived without evolution corrections between the projectile and the produced gluons, meaning the net rapidity interval equals only the rapidity interval between the produced gluons and the target.

## Method

The authors studied the two-gluon production cross section derived in their previous work (Kovchegov and Wertepny, 2013) within the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework, considering collisions where one nucleus is much smaller than the other (heavy-light ion collisions) with atomic numbers satisfying A2 >> A1 >> 1. They evaluated the energy and geometry dependence of the correlation functions using the McLerran-Venugopalan model and the Gaussian approximation, and analyzed the infrared behavior of the cross section by examining which terms remain divergent after including saturation effects in the target nucleus. The cross section was also rewritten in a kT-factorized form involving convolutions of one- and two-gluon Wigner distributions over both transverse momenta and impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- The two-gluon correlation function in the leading power-of-energy approximation is independent of the center-of-mass energy of the collision (no numerical energy dependence given).
- The ratio of tip-on-tip to side-on-side correlation functions in U+U collisions is given by the expression: C_tip-on-tip / C_side-on-side = [∫ d²b [T_tip-on-tip(b)]⁴] / [∫ d²b [T_tip-on-tip(b)]²]² × [∫ d²b [T_side-on-side(b)]²]² / [∫ d²b [T_side-on-side(b)]⁴].
- The lowest-order correlator contains a power-law IR divergence at l=0 with the integrand scaling as ~1/l⁴, which is stronger than the ~1/l² divergences in single-gluon production cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of two-gluon production in heavy-light ion collisions (Kovchegov and Wertepny, 2013) by studying the energy and geometry dependence of the correlation functions, and by examining the infrared divergences and kT-factorization properties. The results contradict the assumption made by Dumitru et al. (2008) that saturation effects in both colliding nuclei would regulate the power-law IR divergence, showing instead that saturation effects in only one nucleus are insufficient. The finding that CGC correlations are stronger in tip-on-tip U+U collisions than in side-on-side ones is the exact opposite behavior from ellipticity-driven hydrodynamic correlations (Heinz, 2004; Kuhlman, 2005; Kuhlman, 2006), suggesting that U+U collision studies could help disentangle the two sources of azimuthal correlations.
