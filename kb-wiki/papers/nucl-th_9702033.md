# Baryon Resonance Extraction from Pi Nucleon Data using a Multichannel Unitary Model

> We present organization and solutions for a new analysis of all $πN$ elastic and the major inelastic channels to extract detailed characteristics of the contributing baryon resonances. This work is based on the work of R. Cutkosky and collaborators at CMU about 20 years ago. The model features analyticity at the amplitude level and unitarity. Results are similar to previous analyses for strongly excited states, but can vary considerably from previous analyses when the states are weak, the data is poor, or there is a strong model dependence. We emphasize the S_{11}(1535) resonance which has particularly strong model dependence.

## Key claim

The authors apply the Carnegie-Mellon Berkeley (CMB) model to modern pion-nucleon data and find that results for strongly excited baryon resonances agree with previous analyses, but for weakly excited states, states with poor data, or states with strong model dependence the extracted parameters can vary considerably; the S11(1535) resonance is highlighted as having particularly strong model dependence, with extracted full widths ranging from 66 MeV to 270 MeV in prior work.

## Method

The authors implement the CMB model, which uses a separable T-matrix formalism with analyticity enforced via dispersion relations for the channel propagators and unitarity maintained through a Dyson equation for the resonance propagator. They fit the model to partial-wave-decomposed data from the VPI group for piN elastic scattering, from Manley et al. for inelastic channels, and from their own partial wave analysis of piN to etaN data, coupling up to eight asymptotic channels per partial wave. To assess model dependence, they compare results from the full Dyson equation treatment against K-matrix approximations, with and without dispersion relations, and using relativistic versus nonrelativistic Breit-Wigner shapes for bare resonances.

## Key numbers

- For the S11(1535) resonance, the full Dyson equation model with all channels yields a mass of 1542 MeV, a total width of 112 MeV, and a piN branching ratio of 35 percent, compared to the KSU result of 151 MeV width and 51 percent elasticity.
- For the S11(1650) resonance, the full model gives a mass of 1689 MeV, width of 202 MeV, and piN branching ratio of 74 percent, while the K-matrix model without dispersion gives 1694 MeV, 259 MeV, and 72 percent.
- For the D15(1675) resonance, the extracted mass is 1685 MeV, width 131 MeV, and elasticity 35 percent, compared to the KSU values of 1676 MeV, 159 MeV, and 47 percent.
- The chi-squared per data point for elastic fits was 1.7 for S11 and 1.6 for D15, while for inelastic amplitudes it was 9.2 and 22.2 respectively.
- The S11 partial wave fit used 38 real parameters (3 resonances with couplings to 7 channels each, plus 3 background poles).

## Context

This work extends the CMB model of Cutkosky et al. (1979) by applying it to modern partial-wave-decomposed data sets from VPI and Manley et al., and by including piN to etaN data that was absent from the KSU analysis of Manley and Saleski. The results enable comparison of model-dependent effects in baryon resonance extraction, particularly for the S11(1535) where the nearby etaN threshold creates strong sensitivity to the treatment of analyticity and channel coupling. The authors note that improved inelastic data is needed for more objective results, and that a reanalysis of piN to pipiN data is in progress at the University of Pittsburgh.
