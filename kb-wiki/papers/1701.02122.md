# Strong isospin breaking at production of light scalars

> 6 pages, 6 figures, N. N. Achasov's plenary talk at The 14th International Workshop on Tau Lepton Physics, 19-23 September, 2016, IHEP, Beijing, China

## Key claim

Isospin breaking in the production of light scalar mesons, driven by the kaon mass difference, provides a general tool to study the production mechanism and nature of the \(a_0^0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) resonances, but the observed large breaking in the decay \(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to 3\pi\) cannot be explained by the \(a_0^0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing alone and requires additional mechanisms such as pointlike \(K\bar K\pi\) decays or intermediate \(K^*\bar K\) states.

## Method

The authors compute the \(a_0^0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing amplitude via \(K\bar K\) loop diagrams, using the kaon mass difference to generate a narrow resonant structure between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds. They then evaluate the branching ratios for the isospin-violating decays \(f_0(980)\to\eta\pi^0\) and \(a_0^0(980)\to\pi^+\pi^-\) caused by this mixing. For the decays \(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to 3\pi\) and \(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405)\to\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\to\gamma 3\pi\), they estimate contributions from four distinct mechanisms: the \(a_0^0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing, pointlike \(f_1(1285)\to K\bar K\pi^0\) decay, intermediate \(K^*\bar K\) states, and intermediate \(K^*_0\bar K\) states, comparing each to experimental data from BESIII and VES.

## Key numbers

- The \(a_0^0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing amplitude modulus between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds is approximately \(0.03\ \text{GeV}^2\), compared to \(|\Pi_{\rho^0\omega}|\approx|\Pi_{\pi^0\eta}|\approx 0.003\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The branching ratio for \(f_0(980)\to K\bar K\to a_0^0(980)\to\eta\pi^0\) is about \(0.3\%\); for \(a_0^0(980)\to K\bar K\to f_0(980)\to\pi\pi\) it is about \(0.2\%\).
- The ratio \(BR(f_1(1285)\to a_0^0(980)\pi^0\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/BR(f_1(1285)\to a_0^0(980)\pi^0\to\eta\pi^0\pi^0) = (2.5\pm0.9)\%\).
- The pointlike mechanism gives \(BR(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/BR(f_1(1285)\to K\bar K\pi)=0.0022\), while the experimental value is \(0.033\pm0.010\).
- The \(K^*\bar K\) mechanism alone yields \(BR(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)\approx 0.0255\%\), compared to the experimental \((0.30\pm0.09)\%\).
- For \(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405)\to\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\to\gamma\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\), the branching ratio is \((1.50\pm0.11\pm0.11)\times10^{-5}\); the ratio \(BR(\eta(1405)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/BR(\eta(1405)\to a_0^0(980)\pi^0\to\eta\pi^0\pi^0) = (17.9\pm4.2)\%\).
- The \(K^*\bar K\) dominance mechanism for \(\eta(1405)\) gives \(BR(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405)\to\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\to\gamma 3\pi)\approx 1.12\times10^{-5}\), consistent with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own earlier theoretical discovery of \(a_0^0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing (Achasov and Shestakov, 1979; 1981) and its experimental confirmation by VES (Doronin et al., 2008; 2011) and BESIII (Ablikim et al., 2011; 2012; 2013). It generalizes the isospin-breaking effect beyond simple mixing to any mechanism producing \(K\bar K\) pairs in \(S\) wave, as proposed in Achasov et al. (2015; 2016). The analysis shows that the \(f_1(1285)\to 3\pi\) decay requires a "terrible" mixing with inconveniently large coupling constants, while the \(\eta(1405)\to 3\pi\) decay cannot be explained by mixing alone and points to anomalous Landau thresholds from triangle diagrams with \(K^*\) mesons. The work enables future experimental studies of spin asymmetry in polarized target reactions and consistency checks between \(K\bar K\pi\) and \(3\pi\) decay data.
