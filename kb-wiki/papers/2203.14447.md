# Chiral sum rule on the light front and the 3D image of the pion

> The lightest meson, the pion, features two faces -- one is the elementary Goldstone boson of QCD and the other is the structured bound state of quarks and gluons. To accommodate both in a single light-front wave function in the valence space, we obtain a sum rule by analyzing the conserved axial-vector current and the general structures of the wave functions. Using an analytic model motivated by holography, we show this sum rule is consistent with requirements of chiral symmetry breaking in AdS/QCD. Within this model, we find a remarkable feature of the pion, namely that the density is mostly uniform inside its radius; furthermore, we obtain good agreement with the experimental pion form factor at spacelike momenta.

# Chiral sum rule on the light front and the 3D image of the pion

## Key claim

The authors derive a chiral sum rule for the pion valence light-front wave function that must hold in the chiral limit, and show that within a holographic model this sum rule forces the pion's transverse density to be mostly uniform inside its radius, producing a "flat disk" structure. The claim is conditional on working in the chiral limit (vanishing quark mass) and on the assumption that the constant $\hat f_\chi$ in the covariant decomposition does not vanish in that limit, which distinguishes spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking from a scenario where the pion is not a Goldstone boson.

## Method

The authors analyze the partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) and the general covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) decomposition of the pion valence light-front wave function into two Lorentz structures, one proportional to $\gamma_5$ and one proportional to $\gamma_5 \slashed{\omega}/(\omega\cdot p)$. By evaluating the vacuum-to-pion matrix elements of the axial-vector and pseudoscalar currents in the light-front wave function representation and applying the PCAC relation, they derive a sum rule that in the chiral limit reduces to an integral constraint on the leading-twist wave function. They then implement this sum rule in a light-front holography (LFH) model with a separable ansatz, constructing a specific wave function with a quartic-Gaussian form that satisfies the sum rule and comparing its predictions for the pion form factor to experimental data from CERN, Jefferson Lab, and DESY.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate scale is $\Sigma \sim (0.3\ \text{GeV})^3$, associated with the UV boundary behavior of the background scalar field in AdS/QCD.
- The confining strength is $\kappa \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV}$, related to the Regge slope.
- The dimensionless free parameter in the model wave function is chosen as $c = 0.3$ to obtain a good fit to the pion form factor.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is $\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ \text{GeV}$, larger than the confinement scale $\Lambda_\text{QCD} \sim 0.3\ \text{GeV}$.
- The pion electromagnetic form factor from the model is compared with experimental measurements by CERN (NA7, 1986), Jefferson Lab (2006, 2007), and a reanalysis of DESY data (2008).

## Context

This work extends the covariant analysis of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity in Dyson-Schwinger equations by Maris (1997) to the light-front wave function formalism, providing a direct connection between chiral symmetry breaking and partonic structure. It builds on light-front holography as developed by Brodsky and de Teramond (2006, 2014) and the AdS/QCD framework of Erlich (2005), showing that the chiral sum rule is equivalent to the chiral condensate in AdS/QCD. The result enables a 3D coordinate-space picture of the pion via the Ioffe-time transform introduced by Miller and Brodsky (2019), and provides a pathway to understanding how the chiral condensate manifests in light-front wave functions, addressing a long-standing question raised by Brodsky (2010, 2022).
