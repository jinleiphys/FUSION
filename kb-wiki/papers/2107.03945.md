# Non-Abelian Electric Field Correlator at NLO for Dark Matter Relic Abundance and Quarkonium Transport

> We perform a complete next-to-leading order calculation of the non-Abelian electric field correlator in a SU($N_c$) plasma, which encodes properties of the plasma relevant for heavy particle bound state formation and dissociation, and is different from the correlator for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. The calculation is carried out in the real-time formalism of thermal field theory and includes both vacuum and finite temperature contributions. By working in the $R_ξ$ gauge, we explicitly show the results are gauge independent, infrared and collinear safe. The renormalization group equation of this electric field correlator is determined by that of the strong coupling constant. Our next-to-leading order calculation can be directly applied to any dipole singlet-adjoint transition of heavy particle pairs. For example, it can be used to describe dissociation and (re)generation of heavy quarkonia inside the quark-gluon plasma well below the melting temperature, as well as heavy dark matter pairs (or charged co-annihilating partners) in the early universe.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of the non-Abelian electric field correlator relevant for heavy particle bound state formation and dissociation, which is distinct from the correlator used for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient; the calculation is valid under the hierarchy where the binding energy is larger than or comparable to the Debye screening mass, so that fixed-order perturbation theory is infrared and collinear safe without requiring hard-thermal-loop resummation.

## Method

The authors compute the non-Abelian electric field correlator in a SU(N_c) plasma with n_f massless fermions using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, working in a general R_ξ gauge to verify gauge invariance. They perform a fixed-order NLO calculation that includes both vacuum and finite-temperature contributions, and demonstrate that the result is infrared and collinear finite due to the finite energy transfer (the binding energy) that acts as an infrared regulator. The renormalization group evolution of the correlator is shown to be determined solely by the running of the strong coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed for a SU(N_c) gauge theory with n_f effectively massless fermion fields in the plasma.
- The hierarchy assumed is M ≫ 1/r ≫ E, T ≳ m_D, where M is the heavy particle mass, r the typical relative distance, E the binding energy, T the plasma temperature, and m_D the Debye screening mass.
- The leading-order Wilson coefficients are V_A = V_B = 1, and the leading-order potentials are V_s^(0)(r) = -C_2(R) α_s/r and V_adj^(0)(r) = -[C_2(R) - C_2(adj)/2] α_s/r.
- For quarkonium in QCD, the decomposition is 3 ⊗ 3̄ = 1 ⊕ 8, with the octet potential being repulsive.
- For adjoint-representation particles (e.g., gluinos), the decomposition is 8 ⊗ 8 = 1 ⊕ 8_A ⊕ 8_S ⊕ ..., where both octet potentials are attractive.

## Context

This work extends the Abelian (U(1)) NLO calculation of the electric field correlator for dark matter bound state effects by Binder (2020) to non-Abelian gauge theories, which is required for applications to quarkonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma and to dark matter in electroweak and colored coannihilation scenarios. The correlator differs from the well-studied electric field correlator for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient computed by Caron-Huot (2007) and Burnier (2010), as the Wilson line configurations and operator orderings are different. The result enables precise predictions for bound state formation and dissociation rates in the Boltzmann equation derived from the open quantum system framework, applicable to heavy quarkonia below the melting temperature and to heavy dark matter pairs in the early universe.
