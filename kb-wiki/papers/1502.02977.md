# Efficient calculation of chiral three-nucleon forces up to N3LO for ab initio studies

> We present a novel framework to decompose three-nucleon forces in a momentum space partial-wave basis. The new approach is computationally much more efficient than previous methods and opens the way to ab initio studies of few-nucleon scattering processes, nuclei and nuclear matter based on higher-order chiral 3N forces. We use the new framework to calculate matrix elements of chiral three-nucleon forces at N2LO and N3LO in large basis spaces and carry out benchmark calculations for neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. We also study the size of the individual three-nucleon force contributions for $^3$H. For nonlocal regulators, we find that the sub-leading terms, which have been neglected in most calculations so far, provide important contributions. All matrix elements are calculated and stored in a user-friendly way, such that values of low-energy constants as well as the form of regulator functions can be chosen freely.

# Efficient calculation of chiral three-nucleon forces up to N3LO for ab initio studies

## Key claim

The authors present a novel framework for decomposing three-nucleon forces in a momentum-space partial-wave basis that is computationally much more efficient than previous methods, but the efficiency gain depends on the specific form of the interaction and the method makes explicit use of the fact that all unregularized chiral 3NF contributions are either local or contain only polynomial non-local terms.

## Method

The authors reduce the original eight-dimensional angular integral for partial-wave decomposition of local 3NFs to a few discrete sums plus three internal numerical integrals by exploiting rotation invariance and factorizing spin-momentum operators using spherical harmonic identities. They calculate matrix elements of chiral 3NFs up to N3LO in a Jj-coupled three-body plane-wave basis for total three-body angular momentum up to J=9/2, storing 12 separate files per partial wave corresponding to different low-energy constant combinations. The framework is applied to compute Hartree-Fock energies for neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter as benchmarks, and triton binding energies using three different NN interactions with non-local regulators.

## Key numbers

- Speedup factors greater than 1000 for matrix elements of chiral long-range N2LO interactions proportional to couplings c1 and c3; for all 3NF topologies up to N3LO except relativistic corrections, speedup factors are typically greater than 100
- For neutron matter at Fermi momentum kF = 1.7 fm^-1 (number density n ~ 0.166 fm^-3), partial wave channels up to J=5/2 provide significant energy contributions, with results up to J=9/2 showing excellent convergence and agreement with exact results
- For symmetric nuclear matter at neutron and proton Fermi momenta kF^n = kF^p = 1.35 fm^-1 (total number density n ~ 0.166 fm^-3), all 3NF topologies and both three-body isospin channels T=1/2 and T=3/2 contribute
- For triton calculations, binding energies based on Faddeev components and antisymmetrized interactions are identical within sub-keV level
- Matrix elements are calculated with Np = Nq = 15 momentum mesh points for all partial waves
- Total file sizes per partial wave range from 0.2 GB (J=1/2, T=3/2) to 6.8 GB (J=7/2, T=1/2)

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave decomposition methods of Golak et al. (2010) and Skibinski et al. (2011) by providing a computationally much more efficient algorithm that enables calculations of chiral 3NF matrix elements at N3LO in large basis spaces for the first time. The framework enables ab initio studies of few-nucleon scattering, nuclei up to mass A=132, and nuclear matter using complete N3LO chiral 3NFs, addressing the limitation that previous calculations were restricted to N2LO 3NFs while NN forces were included up to N3LO. The results are directly relevant to the goals of the Low Energy Nuclear Physics International Collaboration (LENPIC) and enable systematic investigations of subleading 3NF contributions that have been neglected in most calculations so far.
