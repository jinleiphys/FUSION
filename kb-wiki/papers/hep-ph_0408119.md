# Photon production in relativistic nuclear collisions at SPS and RHIC energies

> Chiral Lagrangians are used to compute the production rate of photons from the hadronic phase of relativistic nuclear collisions. Special attention is paid to the role of the pseudovector a_1 meson. Calculations that include reactions with strange mesons, hadronic form factors and vector spectral densities consistent with dilepton production, as well as the emission from a quark-gluon plasma and primordial nucleon-nucleon collisions, reproduce the photon spectra measured at the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS). Predictions for the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are made.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a hadronic model including the pseudovector a_1 meson, strange mesons, form factors, and vector spectral densities consistent with dilepton data can reproduce the WA98 photon spectra at SPS, but the authors note that the a_1 contribution carries an inherent uncertainty of about a factor of two, as no single parameter set simultaneously reproduces both the experimental D/S ratio and the radiative decay width of the a_1.

## Method
The authors used a Massive Yang-Mills chiral Lagrangian to compute photon production rates from hadronic matter, including reactions such as π+ρ→π+γ with coherent a_1 contributions, strange meson channels (K*, K, ρ, π), and hadronic dipole form factors with a cutoff Λ=1 GeV. They combined these hadronic rates with a leading-order QCD calculation for the quark-gluon plasma (including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) and primordial nucleon-nucleon collisions (with Cronin broadening), then convolved the rates with a thermal fireball evolution model adjusted to reproduce observed flow velocities and hadron ratios.

## Key numbers
- For parameter set II: D/S = -0.099, Γ_{a_1→π+γ} = 0.033 MeV; for set III: D/S = -0.49, Γ_{a_1→π+γ} = 0.44 MeV (experimental value is 0.64 ± 0.246 MeV).
- The total strange meson contribution accounts for ~25% of the net hadronic photon rate at q_0=1 GeV, reducing to ~15% at q_0=3 GeV (at T=200 MeV).
- At SPS, the initial temperature used was T_i = 205 MeV; the QGP contribution is ~20-30% of the thermal yield.
- At RHIC, the QGP contribution becomes dominant for intermediate photon energies around 2 GeV.
- The Cronin broadening for SPS was estimated as ⟨Δk_T²⟩ ~ 0.2-0.3 GeV².

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991) which did not include the a_1 meson, and by Song (1993) which used a different parameter set (set I) giving larger a_1 contributions. The authors apply constraints from dilepton production (Rapp and Wambach, 1999) to ensure consistency between photon and dilepton spectral densities. The results enable predictions for RHIC where the QGP contribution is expected to become measurable in the intermediate photon energy window of 1-3 GeV.
