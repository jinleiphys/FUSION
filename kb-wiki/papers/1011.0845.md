# The nucleon's transversity and the photon's distribution amplitude probed in lepton pair photoproduction

> We describe a new way to access the chiral odd transversity parton distribution in the proton through the photoproduction of lepton pairs on transversely polarized target. The basic ingredient is the interference of the Bethe Heitler or Drell-Yan amplitudes with the amplitude of a process, where the photon couples to quarks through its twist-2 chiral-odd distribution amplitude. This approach permits to scan experimentally the shape of the transversity distribution in a nucleon as well as the photon distribution amplitude.

# The nucleon's transversity and the photon's distribution amplitude probed in lepton pair photoproduction

## Key claim
The chiral-odd transversity parton distribution in the proton can be accessed through the photoproduction of lepton pairs on a transversely polarized target, using the interference of Bethe-Heitler or Drell-Yan amplitudes with an amplitude where the photon couples to quarks via its twist-2 chiral-odd distribution amplitude. This approach permits scanning the shape of both the transversity distribution and the photon distribution amplitude, but only under the condition that the photon distribution amplitude is non-zero and the interference terms are experimentally separable.

## Method
The authors consider the process γ(k,ε) N(r,s_T) → l⁻(p) l⁺(p') X in the kinematical region where Q² = q² is large and the transverse component |Q_⊥| of q is of the same order as Q. They compute the amplitude A_φ for the photon coupling to quarks through its twist-2 chiral-odd distribution amplitude, which develops an absorptive part proportional to φ_γ(αQ²/(Q²+Q_⊥²)). The transversity-dependent differential cross section Δ_Tσ = σ(s_T) - σ(-s_T) contains only interferences between A_φ and the other amplitudes, and a charge asymmetry between l⁻ and l⁺ selects the interference between A_φ and the Bethe-Heitler amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The product of the quark condensate and the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum, χ⟨q̄q⟩, is estimated to be of the order of 50 MeV.
- The normalization of the photon distribution amplitude is chosen as ∫ dz φ_γ(z) = 1.
- The absorptive part of amplitude A_φ is proportional to φ_γ(αQ²/(Q²+Q_⊥²)), where α is the component along the photon momentum k of the lepton pair momentum q.
- The polarization-averaged interference cross section dσ_{φBH} is proportional to the product φ_γ[αQ²/(Q²+Q_⊥²)] · h₁^q(Q²/(αs) + Q_⊥²/(αᾱs)).

## Context
This work extends the pioneering studies on transversity distributions (Tra, 1990s) and the photon distribution amplitude (Braun, 2000s), addressing the difficulty that transversity's chiral-odd character causes its decoupling in most hard amplitudes. Prior work by Barone (2000s) explored many channels with experimental difficulties, while recent extractions of chiral-odd quantities from transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) have demonstrated non-zero values only in a weak sense. This proposal enables scanning the transversity distribution h₁^q and the photon distribution amplitude φ_γ through single spin asymmetries in lepton pair photoproduction, using only leading twist factorizable quantities.
