# The Imaginary Part of Nucleon Self-energy in hot nuclear matter

> A semiphenomenological approach to the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter at finite temperatures is followed. It combines elements of Thermo Field Dynamics for the treatment of finite temperature with a model for the self-energy, which evaluates the second order diagrams taking the needed dynamics of the NN interaction from experiment. The approach proved to be accurate at zero temperature to reproduce Im(Sigma) and other properties of nucleons in matter. In the present case we apply it to determine Im(Sigma) at finite temperatures. An effective NN cross section is deduced which can be easily used in analyses of heavy ion reactions.

# The Imaginary Part of Nucleon Self-energy in hot nuclear matter

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the nucleon self-energy in hot nuclear matter grows steadily with temperature, and at finite temperature it takes nonzero values at the chemical potential where it is zero at T=0; this is a genuine temperature-dependent property that would be lost if one simply replaced the zero-temperature Fermi distribution by a thermal distribution in a T=0 formalism.

## Method

The authors combine Thermo Field Dynamics (a real-time finite-temperature formalism) with a semiphenomenological model that evaluates second-order nucleon self-energy diagrams using experimental NN cross sections instead of an explicit NN potential. Long-range correlations are included via an RPA sum with a phenomenological particle-hole interaction in the spin-isospin transverse channel. The imaginary part of the self-energy is evaluated numerically for nucleons in nuclear matter at several densities and temperatures, and an effective NN cross section is parameterized for use in heavy-ion reaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Calculations performed at densities: ρ₀/2, ρ₀, and 2ρ₀
- Temperatures considered: T = 0, 2, 4, 10, and 20 MeV
- The imaginary part of the self-energy Im Σ̄ is proportional to (ω − μ)² at T = 0
- At finite temperature, Im Σ̄(μ, k) = 2 k_B T × (d/dk⁰) Im Σ^(11)(k⁰,k) evaluated at k⁰ = μ
- The effective NN cross section is parameterized as v σ_eff_NN = Σ_{n=0}^{4} a_n(ρ,T) ω^n, where ω is the nucleon kinetic energy in MeV, giving v σ_eff_NN in mb

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature semiphenomenological approach of Alvarez-Ruso et al. (1995) to finite temperatures, where that earlier approach had successfully reproduced nucleon properties obtained from more microscopic many-body calculations such as those of Fantoni et al. and Benhar et al. The results are consistent with Fermi-liquid theory predictions at all temperatures, as shown analytically. The parameterized effective NN cross section is intended for use in semiclassical analyses of heavy ion reactions, where σ_eff_NN × ρ gives the probability of collision per unit length for a nucleon in the nuclear medium.
