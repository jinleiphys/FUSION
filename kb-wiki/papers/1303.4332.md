# Tau neutrino as a probe of nonstandard interaction

> We study the $Δ$-resonance and deep inelastic scattering contributions in the tau-neutrino nucleon scattering $ν_τ+ N \to τ^- + X$ and $\barν_τ+ N \to τ^+ + X$ in the presence of a charged Higgs and a $W'$ gauge boson. The new physics effects to the quasielastic process have been discussed in a previous work. The extractions of the atmospheric mixing angle $θ_{23}$ rely on the standard model cross sections for $ν_τ+ N \to τ^- + X$ in $ν_τ$ appearance experiments. Corrections to the cross sections from the charged Higgs and $W'$ contributions modify the measured mixing angle. We include form factor effects in the new physics calculations and find the deviations of the mixing angle. If high-energy Long Base Line experiments are designed to measure $θ_{13}$ through tau neutrino appearance, the new physics effects to $ν_τ+ N \to τ^- + X$ and $\barν_τ+ N \to τ^+ + X$ can impact the extraction of this mixing angle. Finally, we investigate the new physics effects on the polarization of the $τ^\mp$ leptons produced in $ν_τ(\barν_τ)$ nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The paper calculates how charged Higgs and \(W'\) boson contributions to tau-neutrino nucleon scattering in the \(\Delta\)-resonance and deep inelastic scattering regimes modify the measured atmospheric mixing angle \(\theta_{23}\) and potentially \(\theta_{13}\), assuming new physics effects are confined to the third-generation lepton sector and that neutral current interactions are neglected.

## Method

The authors compute the standard model cross sections for \(\nu_\tau + N \to \tau^- + X\) and \(\bar{\nu}_\tau + N \to \tau^+ + X\) for \(\Delta\)-resonance production and deep inelastic scattering, then add contributions from a charged Higgs boson and a \(W'\) gauge boson. They include form factor effects in the \(\Delta\)-resonance calculations and relate the ratio of new-physics to standard-model cross sections to the deviation of the extracted mixing angles using model-independent formulas from their earlier work on quasielastic scattering (Rashed 2012). They also study the effect of the new physics on the spin polarization of the produced \(\tau\) leptons.

## Key numbers

- The threshold neutrino energy for \(\Delta\)-resonance production of tau leptons is \(E_{\nu_l}^{\rm th} = 4.35\) GeV.
- A deviation \(\delta_{23} \sim -5^\circ\) in the atmospheric mixing angle requires a cross-section ratio \(r_{23} = \sigma_{NP}(\nu_\tau) / \sigma_{SM}(\nu_\tau) \sim 5\%\).
- A deviation \(\delta_{13} \sim -1^\circ\) in \(\theta_{13}\) requires a cross-section ratio \(r_{13} = \sigma_{NP}(\bar{\nu}_\tau) / \sigma_{SM}(\bar{\nu}_\tau) \sim 25\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of new physics effects in quasielastic tau-neutrino nucleon scattering (Rashed 2012) to the \(\Delta\)-resonance and deep inelastic scattering regimes, which are relevant at higher neutrino energies. The results are applicable to experiments such as Super-Kamiokande (Abe 2006, 2012) and OPERA (2011) that measure \(\nu_\mu \to \nu_\tau\) oscillations via tau appearance, and to future long-baseline experiments that might measure \(\theta_{13}\) through tau neutrino appearance. The DONuT experiment (Kodama 2007) measured the charged-current tau neutrino cross section and found central values about 40% above the standard model prediction, though with large uncertainties.
