# Mean Field Effects In The Quark-Gluon Plasma

> A transport model based on the mean free path approach for an interacting meson system at finite temperatures is discussed. A transition to a quark gluon plasma is included within the framework of the MIT bag model. The results obtained compare very well with Lattice QCD calculations when we include a mean field in the QGP phase due to the Debye color screening. In particular the cross over to the QGP at about 175 MeV temperature is nicely reproduced. We also discuss a possible scenario for hadronization which is especially important for temperatures below the QGP phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a transport model based on the mean free path approach, combined with the MIT bag model for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) transition and an attractive mean field due to Debye color screening, reproduces lattice QCD results for the equation of state, including a crossover at about 175 MeV. The hedge is that the agreement is qualitative without the mean field, and only becomes quantitative after fitting the mean field to lattice data.

## Method

The authors simulate a pion gas in a box with periodic boundary conditions, using a relativistic Boltzmann equation (ReB) approach based on the mean free path method. Pions collide elastically and inelastically according to parametrized experimental cross sections, including resonances and their decays. The transition to QGP is implemented using the MIT bag model: when the local energy density in a hadron-hadron collision exceeds the critical value (3B), quarks and gluons are created assuming local thermal equilibrium. A mean field is added in the QGP phase to account for Debye color screening, with its strength fitted to lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature to QGP: about 175 MeV, reproduced by the model.
- Bag constant used: B^(1/4) = 0.206 GeV, giving critical energy density epsilon_c = 3B = 0.71 GeV/fm^3.
- For N_f = 0 flavors, epsilon/T^4 approaches about 4 at high temperature; for N_f = 2, about 12; for N_f = 3, about 16.
- The fitted Debye screening mass from the mean field is m_d = 1.9 m_d(Ab), where m_d(Ab) is the Abelian limit value.
- Quark masses used: 10 MeV for u,d quarks; 160 MeV for s quarks.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous transport model studies (Tan, 2006) from N_f = 0 and 2 flavors to 3 flavors, and compares results to lattice QCD calculations (Karsch, 2002). The model reproduces the Hagedorn limiting temperature for a pure hadronic system and the crossover to QGP seen in lattice QCD. The approach enables future application to relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, providing a kinetic model with a controlled equation of state that can be extended to out-of-equilibrium systems.
