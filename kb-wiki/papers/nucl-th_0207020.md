# The $ŽĆN \to e^+e^- N$ reaction close to the vector meson production threshold

> The $ŽĆ^-p \to e^+e^- n$ and $ŽĆ^+n \to e^+e^- p$ reaction cross sections are calculated below and in the vicinity of the vector meson ($Žü^0$, $Žē$) production threshold. These processes are largely responsible for the emission of $e^+e^-$ pairs in pion-nucleus reactions and contribute to the dilepton spectra observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. They are dominated by the decay of low-lying baryon resonances into vector meson-nucleon channels. The vector mesons materialize subsequently into $e^+e^-$ pairs. Using $ŽĆN\to Žü^0 N$ and $ŽĆN \to ŽēN$ amplitudes calculated in the center of mass energy interval $1.4<\sqrt s <1.8$ GeV, we compute the $ŽĆ^-p \to e^+e^- n$ and $ŽĆ^+n \to e^+e^- p$ reaction cross sections in these kinematics. Below the vector meson production threshold, the $Žü^0 - Žē$ interference in the $e^+e^-$ channel appears largely destructive for the $ŽĆ^-p \to e^+e^- n$ cross section and constructive for the $ŽĆ^+n \to e^+e^- p$ cross section. The pion beam and the HADES detector at GSI offer a unique possibility to measure these effects. Such data would provide strong constraints on the coupling of vector meson-nucleon channels to low-lying baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the $\pi^-p \to e^+e^- n$ and $\pi^+n \to e^+e^- p$ cross sections using a coupled-channel model that fits pion-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering data, but the model's validity is limited to $e^+e^-$ pair invariant masses close to $(\sqrt s - M_N)$ and to center-of-mass energies $1.5 < \sqrt s \leq 1.75$ GeV, because only s-wave scattering in the $\rho N$ and $\omega N$ channels is included.

## Method

The authors compute $\pi N \to \rho^0 N$ and $\pi N \to \omega N$ amplitudes using a relativistic, unitary coupled-channel approach that fits data on pion-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering and meson photoproduction in the energy window $1.4 < \sqrt s < 1.8$ GeV. The model includes $\pi N$, $\pi \Delta$, $\rho N$, $\omega N$, $K \Lambda$, $K \Sigma$, and $\eta N$ hadronic channels, with only s-wave scattering in the $\rho N$ and $\omega N$ channels and s- and d-waves in $\pi N$ and $\pi \Delta$ scattering. The $\pi N \to e^+e^- N$ cross sections are then obtained by assuming Vector Meson Dominance for the electromagnetic current, converting the vector meson amplitudes into $e^+e^-$ pair production.

## Key numbers

- The $\rho$- and $\omega$-meson masses are $M_\rho$ and $M_\omega$, with the vector meson-photon coupling constants determined from $e^+e^-$ partial decay widths: $|f_\rho| = 0.036$ GeV$^2$ and $|f_\omega| = 0.011$ GeV$^2$.
- The calculation covers $e^+e^-$ pair invariant masses from approximately 0.4 to 0.8 GeV, for center-of-mass energies $\sqrt s$ ranging from 1.5 GeV to 1.75 GeV.
- At $\sqrt s = 1.5$ GeV, the $\pi^-p \to e^+e^- n$ differential cross section is extremely small due to destructive $\rho^0-\omega$ interference, while the $\pi^+n \to e^+e^- p$ cross section is sizeable due to constructive interference.
- Crossing the $\omega$-production threshold ($\sqrt s = 1.72$ GeV) leads to a sharp increase in cross section: by two orders of magnitude in the $\pi^-p \to e^+e^- n$ channel and by one order of magnitude in the $\pi^+n \to e^+e^- p$ channel, at $\sqrt s = 1.75$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Lutz et al. (2001) for meson-nucleon scattering to the calculation of dilepton production, providing a theoretical basis for interpreting $e^+e^-$ pair emission in pion-nucleus reactions and relativistic heavy ion collisions. The predicted isospin-dependent $\rho^0-\omega$ interference pattern (destructive for $\pi^-p \to e^+e^- n$, constructive for $\pi^+n \to e^+e^- p$) can be tested with the pion beam and HADES detector at GSI, and such measurements would constrain the coupling of vector meson-nucleon channels to low-lying baryon resonances like the N(1520) and N(1535).
