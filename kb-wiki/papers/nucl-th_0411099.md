# Magnetic moments of the 2${^+_1}$ states around $^{132}$Sn

> Development of neutron-rich radioactive beams at the HRIBF facility has stimulated experimental and theoretical activity in heavy Sn and Te isotopes. Recently, the g-factor of the first 2$^+$ state in $^{132}$Te has been measured. We report here new shell-model calculation of magnetic moments for selected Sn and Te isotopes. The residual interaction is based on the CD-Bonn renormalized $ G $-matrix. Single-particle spin and orbital effective g-factors are evaluated microscopically including core polarization and meson exchange currents effects.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully microscopic shell-model calculation of magnetic moments for 2⁺ states in Sn and Te isotopes near ¹³²Sn, using effective g-factors derived from core-polarization and meson-exchange current corrections rather than fitted to experimental data. The authors claim that their calculation does not predict the dramatic decrease and sign change of the magnetic moment of the 2⁺₁ state in ¹³⁶Te that appeared in the QRPA model of Terasaki et al. (2002), suggesting that model may have overestimated neutron contributions.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the proton-neutron formalism with ¹³²Sn as a closed core, using the OXBASH code. The residual two-body interaction was derived from a G-matrix based on the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, renormalized via the Q-box method including all non-folded diagrams through third order and summing folded diagrams to infinite order. Single-particle spin and orbital effective g-factors were evaluated microscopically including first-order and RPA core-polarization corrections, meson-exchange current corrections, Δ-isobar resonance contributions, and relativistic corrections, with the closed shell taken as ¹³²Sn.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³²Te 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 0.975 (experimental = 0.70(10) from ENAM)
- For ¹³⁴Te 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 1.724 (no experimental value given)
- For ¹³⁶Te 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 0.695 (no experimental value given)
- For ¹³⁰Te 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 0.693 (experimental = 0.59(7) from Jakob et al. 2002)
- For ¹³⁴Xe 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 0.825 (experimental = 0.708(14) from Jakob et al. 2002)
- For ¹³⁶Xe 2⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 1.823 (experimental = 1.53(9) from Jakob et al. 2002)
- For ¹³²Te 6⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 5.14 (experimental = 5.08(15) from Jakob et al. 2002)
- For ¹³⁴Te 6⁺₁ state: calculated magnetic moment with effective operator = 5.20 (experimental = 4.7(6) from Jakob et al. 2002)
- For ¹³³Sb 7/2⁺ ground state: experimental magnetic moment = 3.00(1) from Stone et al. (1997), compared to free-nucleon value of 1.717 and effective operator value of 2.925
- Proton effective g-factor corrections (average): δg_l = 0.094, δg_s = -2.14, δg_p = 2.03
- Neutron effective g-factor corrections (average): δg_l = -0.039, δg_s = 1.92, δg_p = -0.93
- Oscillator energy ħω = 7.87 MeV used for single-particle radial wave functions

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations in the ¹³²Sn region that used fitted interactions and empirical effective g-factors, such as those by Sarkar and Sarkar (2001) using KH5082 and CW5082 interactions scaled from the ²⁰⁸Pb region, and by Jakob et al. (2002) using the surface delta interaction. It also contrasts with the separable quadrupole-plus-pairing model of Terasaki et al. (2002), which predicted a dramatic decrease and sign change in the ¹³⁶Te 2⁺ magnetic moment. The calculations enable future experimental g-factor measurements in neutron-rich Sn and Te isotopes, particularly for ¹³⁶Te where the experimental determination is noted as being of great interest.
