# Quenching of single-particle strength from direct reactions with stable and rare-isotope beams

> In this review article we discuss the present status of direct nuclear reactions and the nuclear structure aspects one can study with them. We discuss the spectroscopic information we can assess in experiments involving transfer reactions, heavy-ion-induced knockout reactions and quasifree scattering with (p,2p), (p,pn), and (e,e'p) reactions. In particular, we focus on the proton-to-neutron asymmetry of the quenching of the spectroscopic strength.

# Quenching of single-particle strength from direct reactions with stable and rare-isotope beams

## Key claim

The observed correlation between the cross-section ratio R_s = sigma_exp/sigma_th and the asymmetry of the proton-neutron Fermi surfaces (Delta S) suggests that shell-model calculations overestimate spectroscopic strength for well-bound minority-species nucleons by approximately 40% near stability and more strongly in isospin-asymmetric nuclei, but this trend may reflect missing correlations and truncated model spaces rather than a fundamental failure of the reaction description.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from one-nucleon knockout reactions using heavy-ion targets (Be or C) at beam energies above 50 MeV/nucleon, analyzed within the eikonal sudden approximation framework. They compare measured inclusive cross sections to theoretical calculations that combine eikonal reaction dynamics with shell-model spectroscopic factors, and examine the ratio R_s as a function of the cross-section-weighted separation energy difference Delta S = S_n - S_p (or S_p - S_n). The paper also presents a new derivation for the diffraction dissociation cross section that separates nuclear and Coulomb contributions, correcting earlier formulations that mixed these effects.

## Key numbers

- The measured cross section to the 5/2+ ground state of 31Ar from the 9Be(32Ar,31Ar)X reaction at NSCL was found to be only 24(3)% of the shell-model prediction, with a neutron separation energy of 22 MeV and the residue proton-bound by less than 400 keV
- For the reaction 9Be(11Be,10Be) at 60 MeV/nucleon, the removal of a 1s1/2 neutron with separation energy S_n = 0.503 MeV is shown, demonstrating the Serber model works well for loosely-bound halo nuclei
- Diffraction dissociation cross sections for 11Be(2s1/2) + 9Be range from 84.94 mb at 40 MeV/nucleon to 24.78 mb at 1000 MeV/nucleon using the standard formula, versus 87.06 mb to 24.42 mb using the new nuclear-only formula
- For 11Be(2s1/2) + 208Pb at 40 MeV/nucleon, the standard formula gives 1311 mb while the new nuclear-only formula gives 308.7 mb, demonstrating the unreliability of the old formula for heavy targets
- For deeply-bound nucleon removal, such as 32Ar(1d5/2) + 9Be at 70 MeV/nucleon with S_n = 21.56 MeV, the diffraction cross section is 2.942 mb (standard) versus 1.681 mb (new formula)

## Context

This review extends the systematics first reported by Gade (2008) showing the correlation of R_s with Delta S, which had only four cases beyond Delta S >= 14 MeV at that time. The work builds on the knockout reaction framework developed by Hansen and the NSCL team (2003) as a fast-beam alternative to light-ion transfer reactions, and connects to electron-induced proton knockout results from (e,e'p) reactions that established the depletion of spectroscopic strength near stability due to short-range and long-range correlations. The new diffraction dissociation formalism corrects earlier approaches by Yabana (1992), Hencken (1996), and Bertulani (2004, 2006) that mixed nuclear and Coulomb contributions in a way that produces unreliable cross sections for heavy targets.
