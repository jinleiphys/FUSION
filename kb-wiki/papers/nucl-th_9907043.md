# Quark distrbutions in the nucleon based on a relativistic 3-body approach to the NJL model

> Quark light cone momentum distributions in the nucleon are calculated in a relativistic 3-body approach to the NJL model by using a simple 'static approximation' for the Faddeev kernel. A method is presented which automatically satisfies the number and momentum sum rules, even in the regularized theory. In order to assess the sensitivity to the regularization scheme, two schemes which can be formulated in terms of light cone variables are discussed. The effects of the (composite) pion cloud are taken into account in a convolution approach, and the violation of the Gottfried sum rule is discussed. After performing the Q^2 evolution, the resulting distributions are compared to the empirical ones.

## Key claim

The paper calculates quark light cone momentum distributions in the nucleon using a relativistic three-body approach to the NJL model, but employs a "static approximation" for the Faddeev kernel that neglects the momentum dependence of the exchanged quark propagator, which the authors acknowledge leads to momentum distributions that are too stiff due to overbinding and point-like quark-diquark interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Faddeev equation in the NJL model using the static approximation, where the Feynman propagator in the quark exchange kernel is replaced by -1/M_Q, reducing the effective Bethe-Salpeter equation to a geometric series of quark-diquark bubble graphs. Quark distribution functions are evaluated from Feynman diagrams where the quark appears either as a spectator or as a constituent of the scalar diquark, with the diquark contribution expressed as a convolution integral. Pion cloud effects are included via a convolution approach using quark and antiquark distributions within an on-shell parent quark, and the resulting distributions are evolved using DGLAP equations in next-to-leading order to compare with empirical parametrizations at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass M_Q = 400 MeV used in both regularization schemes
- Equivalent 3-momentum cut-off Lambda_3 = 593 MeV in the Lepage-Brodsky (LB) scheme, and Lambda_TR = 407 MeV in the transverse cut-off (TR) scheme, both determined to reproduce f_pi = 93 MeV
- Pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV and nucleon mass M_N = 940 MeV used as input; scalar diquark mass M_D = 600 MeV obtained in the LB scheme
- Coupling constants: g_pi = 6.92 GeV^{-2} and r_s = g_s/g_pi = 0.727 in the LB scheme
- Current quark mass m = 5.96 MeV from the gap equation in the LB scheme
- At the low energy scale Q_0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2, valence quarks carry 92% (LB scheme) or 87% (TR scheme) of the nucleon's light cone momentum
- Probability of the quark state without pion cloud: Z_Q = 0.84 (LB scheme) and Z_Q = 0.83 (TR scheme)
- Gottfried sum rule deviation given by S_G = 1/3 - (4/9)(1 - Z_Q)

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-diquark calculations (DIQ, 1990s) by including the internal structure of the diquark and pion, and by investigating sensitivity to regularization schemes while preserving number and momentum sum rules. The static approximation follows the approach of STAT1 and STAT2 (1990s), which showed the approximation is not unreasonable for the nucleon mass but leads to radii that are too small (RAD, 1990s). The calculation enables future full Faddeev calculations without the static approximation (NOW, in progress) and provides a framework for comparing different regularization schemes in effective quark theories, with the TR cut-off scheme showing better phenomenological agreement than the LB scheme for both valence and antiquark distributions.
