# M. Poincare visits Jefferson Lab: Relativistic Models of Few-Nucleon Systems

> I discuss relativistic models of few-nucleon systems, with particular emphasis on calculations of electron-deuteron scattering and the comparison of these calculations with recent data from Jefferson Lab.

# M. Poincare visits Jefferson Lab: Relativistic Models of Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

There is no unique relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) prediction for electron-deuteron scattering; the RIA is only defined once a particular implementation of the Poincare algebra is chosen, and different implementations produce markedly different predictions even when using the same rest-frame dynamics and single-nucleon current.

## Method

The paper reviews relativistic models of few-nucleon systems by comparing calculations of elastic electron-deuteron scattering observables (structure functions A and B, and tensor polarization T20) across multiple approaches. These include instant-form, front-form, and point-form relativistic quantum mechanics using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction, as well as dynamical calculations that solve the two-body equation in moving frames. All calculations employ the relativistic impulse approximation with single-nucleon currents, using various NN potentials (AV18, NijmII, Bonn-B) and the same single-nucleon form factors from Mergell et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the charge form factor G_C occurs at Q ~ 800 MeV, where relativistic boost effects (length contraction) are non-negligible because the charge form factor varies rapidly there.
- The position of the minimum in structure function B differs by about 30% between the AKP (point-form) and FSR (instant-form) calculations, despite both using the AV18 wave function.
- Jefferson Lab Hall C experiment extended T20 measurements out to Q^2 = 1.7 GeV^2.
- Data from Ref. [Ab00B] enable extraction of G_C, G_Q, and G_M out to Q^2 ~ 2 GeV^2.
- At Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, there is no definite RIA prediction for structure function A across the different models shown.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic "standard model of nuclear physics" (Schrodinger equation with energy-independent potentials, solved exactly for light nuclei as in Carlson and Schiavilla (1998) and Pieper (2003)) into the relativistic regime accessed at Jefferson Lab with GeV-scale momentum transfers. The paper contrasts the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) construction with manifestly covariant quantum field theory approaches, and compares calculations by Forest, Schiavilla, and Riska (instant form); Lev, Pace, and Salme (front form); Carbonell and Karmanov (light front); Allen, Klink, and Polyzou (point form); van Orden, Devine, and Gross (spectator formalism); and Phillips, Wallace, and Devine (approximate boost covariance). The results enable future extension to deuteron electrodisintegration and three-nucleon systems, where different implementations of the Poincare algebra lead to different boosted NN t-matrices that must be accompanied by associated three-body forces.
