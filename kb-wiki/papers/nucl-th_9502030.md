# Time-odd components in the mean field of rotating superdeformed nuclei

> Rotation-induced time-odd components in the nuclear mean field are analyzed using the Hartree-Fock cranking approach with effective interactions SIII, SkM*, and SkP. Identical dynamical moments ${{\cal J}^{(2)}}$ are obtained for pairs of superdeformed bands $^{151}$Tb(2)--$^{152}$Dy(1) and $^{150}$Gd(2)--$^{151}$Tb(1). The corresponding relative alignments strongly depend on which time-odd mean-field terms are taken into account in the Hartree-Fock equations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that identical dynamical moments \({\cal J}^{(2)}\) for pairs of superdeformed bands \(^{151}\text{Tb}(2)\)–\(^{152}\text{Dy}(1)\) and \(^{150}\text{Gd}(2)\)–\(^{151}\text{Tb}(1)\) can be obtained in Hartree-Fock cranking calculations, but the corresponding relative alignments strongly depend on which time-odd mean-field terms are included, implying that the identity of bands is not uniquely determined by the mean-field model without specifying the treatment of time-odd components.

## Method

The authors performed fully selfconsistent Hartree-Fock cranking calculations using the Skyrme effective interactions SIII, SkM*, and SkP, implemented in the HFODD code with a three-dimensional cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis (306 basis states, tested up to 604). They analyzed the role of time-odd mean-field components by considering three cases: the complete Skyrme functional, the functional with certain time-odd terms omitted while preserving local gauge invariance, and the functional with gauge invariance violated. The calculations were applied to the yrast superdeformed bands in \(^{152}\text{Dy}(1)\) and \(^{151}\text{Tb}(1)\) and the first excited bands in \(^{151}\text{Tb}(2)\) and \(^{150}\text{Gd}(2)\), with pairing correlations not taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical moment \({\cal J}^{(2)}\) numerical inaccuracies estimated to be smaller than \(0.2\,\hbar^2\text{MeV}^{-1}\) and total angular momentum \(I\) inaccuracies smaller than \(0.1\,\hbar\).
- Basis harmonic oscillator frequencies: \(\hbar\omega_{\perp}=11.200\) MeV and \(\hbar\omega_{\parallel}=6.246\) MeV, corresponding to Woods-Saxon potential deformations \(\beta_2=0.61\) and \(\beta_4=0.10\).
- Isoscalar-effective-mass coupling constant \(C_0^\tau\): 44.4 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) for SIII (effective mass \(m^*/m=0.76\)), 34.7 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) for SkM* (\(m^*/m=0.79\)), and 0 for SkP (\(m^*/m=1\)).
- For the identical band pairs, \(\gamma\)-ray transition energies \(E_\gamma\) are identical up to 2 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier selfconsistent cranking calculations with Skyrme interactions (e.g., Bonche et al. 1987, 1991; Chabanat et al. 1992; Gall et al. 1994) and relativistic mean-field calculations (König and Ring 1993) that invoked time-odd components to explain identical bands. The study provides the first explicit analysis of how different time-odd mean-field terms affect the relative alignments of identical superdeformed bands, and it enables future systematic investigations of time-odd coupling constants in the energy-density functional approach, particularly for high-spin phenomena such as band crossings and intruder orbital alignments.
