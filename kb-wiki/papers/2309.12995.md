# Study of neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation in Au+Au collisions using PYTHIA8/Angantyr

> Utilizing the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model, which incorporates the multiple-parton interactions (MPI) based color reconnection (CR) mechanism, we study the relative neutron density fluctuation and neutron-proton correlation in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. In this study, we have not only delved into the dependence of these two remarkable observations on rapidity, centrality, and energy, but also presented an analysis of their interplay with the MPI and CR. Our results have shown that the light nuclei yield ratio of proton, deuteron, and triton, expressed by the elegant expression $N_tN_p/N_d^2$, remains unchanged even as the rapidity coverage and collision centrality increase. Interestingly, we have also revealed that the effect of CR is entirely dependent on the presence of MPI; CR has no impact on the yield ratio if MPI is off. Our findings further demonstrate that the light nuclei yield ratio experiences a slight increase with increasing collision energy as predicted by the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model, but it cannot describe the non-monotonic trend observed by the STAR experiment. Based on the Angantyr model simulation results, it is essential not to overlook the correlation between neutron and proton fluctuations. The Angantyr model is a good baseline for studying collisions in the absence of a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) system, given its lack of flow and jet quenching.

## Key claim

The PYTHIA8/Angantyr model, which lacks quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium effects and critical physics, produces a slight monotonic increase in the light nuclei yield ratio \(N_tN_p/N_d^2\) with collision energy, but it cannot reproduce the non-monotonic trend (peak near 20–30 GeV) observed by the STAR experiment; the model serves as a baseline for collisions without QGP.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA8 event generator (version 8.308) with the Angantyr framework to simulate Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. Approximately one million events per energy were generated, with centrality defined by the summed transverse energy in pseudorapidity \(|\eta| < 0.5\). The relative neutron density fluctuation \(\Delta n = \langle (\delta n)^2\rangle/\langle n\rangle^2\) and the neutron-proton correlation coefficient \(\alpha = \langle\delta n\,\delta p\rangle/(\langle n\rangle\langle p\rangle)\) were computed from the simulated nucleon distributions, and the light nuclei yield ratio was evaluated via the coalescence formula \(N_tN_p/N_d^2 = (1+\Delta n+2\alpha)/[2\sqrt{3}(1+\alpha)^2]\).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4,\) and \(200\) GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The light nuclei yield ratio \(N_tN_p/N_d^2\) is compared with the baseline value \(1/(2\sqrt{3}) \approx 0.2887\) (the value when both \(\Delta n\) and \(\alpha\) vanish).
- The experimental non-monotonic peak in the yield ratio occurs at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 20\)–\(30\) GeV in central Au+Au collisions (STAR data).
- The PYTHIA8/Angantyr model predicts a slight increase of the yield ratio with collision energy, but all simulated values lie below the experimental data and below the AMPT model results.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron density fluctuations and light-nuclei production from the AMPT model (which includes QGP medium effects) to the PYTHIA8/Angantyr framework, which explicitly lacks QGP and critical-point physics. The authors compare their results with STAR measurements (Zhang 2020, 2209.08058) and NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions. The finding that color reconnection has no effect when multiple-parton interactions are turned off clarifies the interplay of these two mechanisms in the Angantyr model. The paper establishes PYTHIA8/Angantyr as a baseline for future studies that aim to isolate signatures of the QCD critical point and first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.
