# Analytic solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation

> We present new analytic solutions to the relativistic Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation. We first obtain spherically expanding solutions which are the kinetic counterparts of the exact solutions of the Israel-Stewart equation in the literature. This allows us to compare the solutions of the kinetic and hydrodynamic equations at an analytical level. We then derive a novel boost-invariant solution of the Boltzmann equation which has an unconventional dependence on the proper time. The existence of such a solution is also suggested in second order hydrodynamics and fluid-gravity correspondence.

## Key claim

The paper presents new analytic solutions to the relativistic Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA), but the tensor perturbation solutions satisfy the kinetic equation only up to terms that do not affect the macroscopic hydrodynamic equations, rather than being exact pointwise solutions.

## Method

The authors solve the RTA Boltzmann equation for conformally invariant systems of massless particles by exploiting symmetry reductions. For the Hubble flow, they use a Weyl-rescaled coordinate system where the ideal flow becomes static, then solve the linearized equation for scalar and tensor perturbations. For the Bjorken flow, they analyze the integral form of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation and determine the asymptotic behavior of the energy density by requiring that contributions from the upper integration bound vanish.

## Key numbers

- For the conformal Bjorken flow, the asymptotic energy density scales as \(\varepsilon = C/\tau^4\) with \(C^{1/4} \approx 1.28 \hat{\tau}_\pi\), where \(\hat{\tau}_\pi = \tau_\pi \varepsilon^{1/4}\) is a constant intrinsic parameter of the theory.
- The exponent \(\beta\) in the relation \(\tau_\pi = \tau/\beta\) for the conformal Bjorken solution is \(\beta \approx 1.27672\).
- For the fluid-gravity duality analysis of the \(s=4\) scaling solution, the energy density in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory is \(\varepsilon = (N_c^2 / 2\pi^2) (1/\tau^4)\).

## Context

This work extends the program of finding exact solutions to the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the RTA, previously developed for Gubser flow by Denicol et al. (2014) and for near-equilibrium systems by Baym (1984) and others. The Hubble flow solutions are the kinetic counterparts of exact Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic solutions found by Hatta et al. (2014), and the novel boost-invariant solution with \(\varepsilon \sim 1/\tau^4\) is also suggested by second-order hydrodynamics and the fluid-gravity correspondence of Janik (2005). The results enable direct analytic comparison between kinetic theory and hydrodynamic descriptions of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
