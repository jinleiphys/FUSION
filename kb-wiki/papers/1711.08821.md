# Semilocal momentum-space regularized chiral two-nucleon potentials up to fifth order

> We introduce new semilocal two-nucleon potentials up to fifth order in the chiral expansion. We employ a simple regularization approach for the pion-exchange contributions which (i) maintains the long-range part of the interaction, (ii) is implemented in momentum space and (iii) can be straightforwardly applied to regularize many-body forces and current operators. We discuss in detail the two-nucleon contact interactions at fourth order and demonstrate that three terms out of fifteen used in previous calculations can be eliminated via suitably chosen unitary transformations. The removal of the redundant contact terms results in a drastic simplification of the fits to scattering data and leads to interactions which are much softer (i.e. more perturbative) than our recent semilocal coordinate-space regularized potentials. Using the pion-nucleon low-energy constants from matching pion-nucleon Roy-Steiner equations to chiral perturbation theory, we perform a comprehensive analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering and the deuteron properties up to fifth chiral order and study the impact of the leading F-wave two-nucleon contact interactions which appear at sixth order. The resulting chiral potentials lead to an outstanding description of the proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data from the self-consistent Granada-2013 database below the pion production threshold, which is significantly better than for any other chiral potential. For the first time, the chiral potentials match in precision and even outperform the available high-precision phenomenological potentials, while the number of adjustable parameters is, at the same time, reduced by about ~40%. Last but not least, we perform a detailed error analysis and, in particular, quantify for the first time the statistical uncertainties of the fourth- and the considered sixth-order contact interactions.

## Key claim
The new semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) chiral two-nucleon potentials up to fifth order (N4LO) achieve a description of proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data from the Granada-2013 database that is significantly better than any other chiral potential and matches or outperforms high-precision phenomenological potentials, while using approximately 40% fewer adjustable parameters. This performance depends critically on removing three redundant contact interaction terms at fourth order that were included in all previous calculations, which also makes the potentials much softer and more perturbative.

## Method
The authors constructed chiral two-nucleon potentials up to fifth order (N4LO) using a new semilocal regularization scheme in momentum space that modifies pion propagators to maintain the long-range part of the interaction. They identified and eliminated three redundant contact operators out of fifteen at fourth order via suitably chosen unitary transformations, and performed comprehensive fits of the remaining low-energy constants to the self-consistent Granada-2013 database of nucleon-nucleon scattering data below pion production threshold, using pion-nucleon low-energy constants from Roy-Steiner equation analyses.

## Key numbers
- The number of adjustable parameters is reduced by approximately 40% compared to high-precision phenomenological potentials
- Cutoff values for the coordinate-space regularized potentials were chosen in the range R = 0.8 to 1.2 fm
- The exponent n in the coordinate-space regulator was set to n = 6, with n = 5 or n = 7 shown to give comparable phase shift descriptions
- The momentum-space cutoff Lambda is related to the coordinate-space cutoff by Lambda = 2/R
- The leading F-wave two-nucleon contact interactions appear at sixth order (N5LO) and involve four new low-energy constants for the channels {1F3, 3F2, 3F3, 3F4}
- Off-diagonal matrix element peaks in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels appear for momenta of approximately 600 MeV

## Context
This work extends the semilocal coordinate-space regularized (SCS) chiral potentials developed by Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner (2014-2015) by introducing a momentum-space regularization that can be straightforwardly applied to many-body forces and current operators, addressing a key technical obstacle for the LENPIC Collaboration's program. The authors demonstrate that three of the fifteen fourth-order contact terms used in all previous chiral potentials (Epelbaum 2004, Entem 2003, Epelbaum 2014-2015, Entem 2017) are redundant and can be eliminated, resolving a previously unrecognized over-fitting issue in the NN S-waves first suggested by Furnstahl. The new potentials enable consistent regularization of three-nucleon forces and exchange current operators, which is the next step for applications to few-nucleon systems and nuclear matter.
