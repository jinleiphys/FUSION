# Thermodynamically consistent treatment of repulsive corrections in HRG

> We reformulate the treatment of density-dependent chemical potential shifts appearing in excluded-volume implementations of the hadron resonance gas model. An auxiliary classical representation is constructed in which a common energy shift is determined by preserving the scalar number density, ensuring thermodynamic consistency. Hadron radii are parametrized through a liquid-drop inspired mass-radius relation with two parameters: the pion radius and a scaling exponent. The resulting framework reproduces lattice QCD results for lower-order conserved-charge susceptibilities at zero chemical potentials with only two adjustable parameters.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates the density-dependent chemical potential shifts in excluded-volume implementations of the hadron resonance gas model by constructing an auxiliary classical representation with a common energy shift determined by preserving the scalar number density, thereby ensuring thermodynamic consistency. The claim is hedged by the fact that the model reproduces lattice QCD results only for lower-order conserved-charge susceptibilities at zero chemical potentials, and the authors note that higher-order susceptibilities are not well described due to the oversimplified spherical liquid-drop treatment of hadron radii.

## Method

The authors map the quantum-statistical hadron resonance gas with excluded-volume corrections to an auxiliary Maxwell-Boltzmann representation by introducing a single common energy shift \(\mathcal{E}\) for all hadron species, determined by equating the scalar number density in both pictures. Hadron radii are parametrized through a liquid-drop inspired mass-radius relation \(R_h = R_{\pi} (m_{\pi}/m_h)^{\mathcal{A}}\) with two parameters: the pion radius \(R_{\pi}\) and a scaling exponent \(\mathcal{A}\). The pressure is computed in the original quantum formulation, and all susceptibilities are evaluated using total derivatives that include the implicit chemical-potential dependence of \(\mathcal{E}\).

## Key numbers

* Pion radius set to \(r_{\pi} = 0.2\ \text{fm}\) as the base parameter for the liquid-drop mass-radius relation.
* Scaling exponent \(\mathcal{A} = 3\) used in the relation \(R_h = R_{\pi} (m_{\pi}/m_h)^{\mathcal{A}}\) to determine radii of all hadron species.
* The model reproduces lattice QCD results for second-order baryon number (\(\chi^B_2\)), electric charge (\(\chi^Q_2\)), and strangeness (\(\chi^S_2\)) susceptibilities at zero chemical potentials, as well as fourth-order baryon number (\(\chi^B_4\)) and strangeness (\(\chi^S_4\)) susceptibilities, and crossed susceptibilities containing baryon number.
* Fourth-order electric charge susceptibility (\(\chi^Q_4\)) is not well reproduced, which the authors note exhibits comparatively larger lattice uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model with excluded-volume corrections, addressing the model-dependent contributions to higher-order susceptibilities that arise from density-dependent chemical potential shifts in prior implementations such as those by Rischke (1991) and Steinheimer (2010). The liquid-drop inspired mass-radius relation builds on the work of Polyakov (2002) for hadron structure. The reformulation enables a thermodynamically consistent treatment of repulsive interactions with only two adjustable parameters, and the authors suggest that future discovery of higher-mass strange hadrons may improve agreement for higher-order strange-sector susceptibilities.
