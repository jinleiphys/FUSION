# Effects of drag induced radiation and multi-stage evolution on heavy quark energy loss

> Heavy quarks serve as ideal probes of the QGP properties produced in energetic nuclear collisions, and provide a unique opportunity to study the mass effects on parton energy loss. We develop a multi-stage approach for heavy quark evolution inside the QGP, in which heavy quarks first undergo a rare-scattering multiple-emission evolution at momenta large compared to their mass (sensitive only to the transverse diffusion coefficient $\hat{q}$ ), and then evolve through a single-scattering induced emission (Gunion-Bertsch) stage at momenta comparable to their mass [sensitive to not only $\hat{q}$, but also the longitudinal drag $\hat{e}$ and diffusion $\hat{e}_2$ coefficients]. This multi-stage approach is coupled to a (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model for a quantitative investigation of charm vs. beauty quark energy loss inside the QGP. Based on this approach, we find that drag induced radiation has a considerable impact on the energy loss of intermediate $p_T$ massive beauty quarks. This effect increases the suppression of B mesons and narrows the difference between the $R_{AA}$ of B and D mesons. Our results are consistent with the experimental data at the LHC and contribute to a more quantitative understanding of the transverse momentum dependence of the mass hierarchy of parton energy loss inside the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for intermediate-momentum heavy quarks (with momentum comparable to their mass), the longitudinal drag coefficient \(\hat{e}\) and diffusion coefficient \(\hat{e}_2\) cause a non-negligible contribution to radiative energy loss beyond the additive elastic drag loss, an effect that grows with the quark mass and narrows the difference between the nuclear modification factors of B and D mesons. The hedge is that this effect is only significant when the heavy quark momentum is comparable to its mass; at very high momenta heavy quarks behave essentially like light quarks.

## Method

The authors develop a multi-stage approach for heavy quark evolution in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). At momenta large compared to the quark mass, heavy quarks undergo a rare-scattering multiple-emission (DGLAP-like) phase sensitive only to the transverse diffusion coefficient \(\hat{q}\). At momenta comparable to the quark mass, they transition to a single-scattering induced emission (Gunion-Bertsch) phase sensitive to \(\hat{q}\), \(\hat{e}\), and \(\hat{e}_2\). This evolution is coupled to a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model calibrated to Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, and the medium-modified fragmentation functions are used to compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for D and B mesons.

## Key numbers

- The transition scale between the DGLAP-like phase and the Gunion-Bertsch phase is set at \(Q_0 = 5\) GeV, chosen as the heavy meson mass.
- The hard scale for evolution is \(Q = 20\) GeV.
- The vacuum fragmentation function is measured from PYTHIA at the input scale of \(Q \sim 5\) GeV and evolved to \(Q = 20\) GeV.
- The (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic medium is calibrated to Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
- The value of \(\hat{q}\) used is as measured by the JET collaboration (Burke 2013), with \(\hat{e}\) and \(\hat{e}_2\) scaled as in Qin (2009).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of heavy quark energy loss beyond the standard assumption that light and heavy flavor radiative energy loss depend on the same transport coefficients. It builds on the dead cone effect (Dokshitzer 2001) and the BDMPS multiple-scattering formalism (Baier 1996), and introduces a multi-stage evolution framework (Majumder 2015) that distinguishes between DGLAP-like and Gunion-Bertsch phases for heavy quarks. The results enable more quantitative predictions for the mass hierarchy of parton energy loss in the QGP and are consistent with LHC experimental data from the CMS collaboration.
