# Deconfinement transition in rotating compact stars

> Using the formalism of general relativity for axially symmetric gravitational fields and their sources - rotating compact stars - a perturbation theory with respect to angular velocity is developed and physical quantities such as mass, shape, momentum of inertia and total energy of the star are defined. The change of the internal structure of the star due to rotation has been investigated and the different contributions to the moment of inertia have been evaluated separately. Numerical solutions have been performed using a two-flavor model equation of state describing the deconfinement phase transition as constrained by the conservation of total baryon number and electric charge. During the spin down evolution of the rotating neutron star, below critical values of angular velocity a quark matter core can appear which might be detected as a characteristic signal in the pulsar timing. Within the spin-down scenario due to magnetic dipole radiation it is shown that the deviation of the braking index from $n=3$ could signal not only the occurrence but also the size of a quark core in the pulsar. A new scenario is proposed where, due to mass accretion onto the rapidly rotating compact star, a spin-down to spin-up transition might signal a deconfinement transition in its interior.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that deviations of the pulsar braking index from the canonical value of n=3 could signal not only the occurrence but also the size of a quark matter core in a rotating neutron star, but this signal appears to be weak for most candidate pulsars because significant enhancement of the braking index only occurs for pulsars with periods below 1.5 ms, which have not yet been observed in nature.

## Method

The authors developed a perturbation theory with respect to angular velocity within the general relativistic formalism for axially symmetric gravitational fields, solving the Einstein field equations and Euler equation up to second order in the expansion parameter (Ω/Ω₀)². They used a two-flavor model equation of state describing the deconfinement phase transition, combining a relativistic σ-ω mean-field model for nuclear matter with a bag model for quark matter (bag constant B=75 MeV fm⁻³), imposing Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium with conservation of total baryon number and electric charge.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant B = 75 MeV fm⁻³ used for the quark matter bag model equation of state
- Nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Critical angular velocity Ω_crit = 2.77 kHz for the configuration with total baryon number N_B = 1.55 N_⊙
- Maximum angular velocity Ω_K = 6.6 kHz for the configuration with N_B = 1.8 N_⊙
- Maximal eccentricities at Kepler frequency: ε(Ω_K) = 0.7603 for N_B = 1.3 N_⊙, ε(Ω_K) = 0.7655 for N_B = 1.55 N_⊙, and ε(Ω_K) = 0.7659 for N_B = 1.8 N_⊙
- Quark matter core can have a radius as large as approximately 75% of the star's radius for configurations at the stability limit
- Configurations with total baryon numbers in the interval 1.4 ≤ N_B/N_⊙ ≤ 1.9 show a quark matter core during spin-down evolution
- Significant braking index enhancement occurs only for pulsars with periods P < 1.5 ms (Ω > 4 kHz)

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Glendenning et al. (1997) that the braking index is enhanced when a quark matter core appears during spin-down evolution, using a perturbative general relativistic approach following Hartle (1967) and Sedrakian and Chubarian (1968). The paper enables future observational searches for deconfinement transitions through pulsar timing measurements, though it notes that the predicted signal is weak for currently observed pulsar periods and recommends more complete investigations with a set of realistic equations of state, including those with strangeness degrees of freedom as used by Glendenning et al. (1997) which showed more dramatic signals at lower spin frequencies.
