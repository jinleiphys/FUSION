# Hard photoproduction of a diphoton with a large invariant mass

> The electromagnetic probe has proven to be a very efficient way to access the $3-$dimensional structure of the nucleon, particularly thanks to the exclusive Compton processes. We explore the hard photoproduction of a large invariant mass diphoton in the kinematical regime where the diphoton is nearly forward and its invariant mass is the hard scale enabling to factorize the scattering amplitude in terms of generalized parton distributions. This amplitude has a very peculiar and interesting analytic structure. We calculate unpolarized cross sections and the angular asymmetry triggered by a linearly polarized photon beam.

## Key claim

The hard photoproduction of a large invariant mass diphoton on a nucleon target can be factorized in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at leading twist and leading order, but the cross sections are not large enough to be observable at very high energies such as those of the LHeC proposal, and the process is insensitive to gluon GPDs due to charge symmetry of the two-photon final state.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-order, leading-twist scattering amplitude for the exclusive reaction γ + N → γ + γ + N' in the kinematical regime where the diphoton invariant mass Mγγ is large and the momentum transfer t is small, using collinear QCD factorization in terms of quark GPDs. The hard coefficient functions are computed from the six Feynman diagrams contributing at Born order, and the amplitude is expressed in terms of generalized Compton form factors that depend on valence quark GPDs at the border values x = ±ξ. Unpolarized differential cross sections and the azimuthal asymmetry from a linearly polarized photon beam are evaluated using two different GPD models (GK and VGG).

## Key numbers

- At SγN = 20 GeV² and Mγγ² = 4 GeV², using the VGG GPD model gives a 20% decrease in the cross section relative to the GK model for all values of u'.
- The differential cross section dσ/(dMγγ² dt) at t = tmin and SγN = 20 GeV² is shown for Mγγ² = 4 GeV² and 6 GeV² on both proton and neutron targets.
- The cross section dσ/(dt dMγγ²) decreases roughly as 1/SγN at higher energies, with results shown at SγN = 20 GeV², 100 GeV², and 10⁶ GeV².
- The higher-twist contribution from the photon distribution amplitude is estimated to be smaller than 10% already at Mγγ² = 3 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the framework of collinear QCD factorization for 2 → 3 reactions, previously applied to processes such as γ N → γ ρ N' (Boussarie 2016) and other exclusive channels, to the photoproduction of a diphoton pair. The process is complementary to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) for accessing nucleon GPDs, but unlike those processes it receives no contribution from gluon GPDs or chiral-odd quark distributions. The results enable future GPD extraction programs such as PARTONS, and the authors note that next-to-leading order QCD corrections and the Bethe-Heitler background from lepton beams remain to be studied.
