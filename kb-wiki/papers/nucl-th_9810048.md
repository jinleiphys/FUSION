# Questions on the Quark Model: Panel Discussion

> We discuss the effect of model space truncation on current conservation in quark model calculations.

## Key claim
The panel discussion argues that truncation of the model space in quark model calculations leads to severe violation of current conservation, making predictions of small quantities such as the N-Δ(1232) E2 transition unreliable; even with larger model spaces, the additional problem of consistently relativizing electromagnetic transition operators remains unresolved.

## Method
The authors review the problem of current non-conservation arising from finite model-space diagonalization in quark models, citing prior studies by Bourdeau and Mukhopadhyay and by Drechsel and Giannini that quantified the violation. They discuss attempts to define effective conserved currents (e.g., via a momentum-space subtraction) and note that the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation, used in atomic physics to relativize operators, converges very slowly in quark model calculations.

## Key numbers
- The N-Δ(1232) E2 transition amplitude is cited as a small quantity whose predictions are made unreliable by the severe violation of current conservation.
- Buchmann presented evidence at the workshop that two-body currents, neglected in most quark model calculations of E2, actually dominate this transition amplitude.
- In the Foldy-Wouthuysen scheme, the magnetic interaction energy turns out to be nearly as large as the Coulomb interaction energy (citing Close’s textbook).

## Context
This panel discussion extends the work of Bourdeau and Mukhopadhyay (1990s) and Drechsel and Giannini (1990s), who first investigated the degree of current conservation violation in quark models. It contrasts with the approach of Close and Li (1990s), who proposed general effective operators to address truncation, and Capstick and collaborators (1990s), who partially implemented those operators but still found E2 calculations inaccurate. The discussion enables future work on consistently relativizing transition operators, a problem that remains open despite the use of larger model spaces.
