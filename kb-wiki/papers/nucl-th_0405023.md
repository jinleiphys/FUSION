# N and Delta Resonances in the Rigid Quark-Diquark Vibrator

> A nearest-neighbor analysis of light flavor baryon mass spectra reveals striking degeneracy patterns- narrow mass bands populated by series of parity twins of steadily increasing spins. Each series terminates by an unpaired resonance of the highest spin in the group. We trace back such degenerate series of resonances (to be termed "mega states") to internal baryon structure dominated by a quark -(rigid di-quark) configuration. Nucleon and Delta spectra are, surprisingly enough, exact replicas to each other in the sense that each of them features three mega states of equal quantum numbers. We fit positions and splittings of the observed mega states by an algebraic Hamiltonian which translates into a potential that is a combination of Coulomb- and Morse like potentials and predict few more such degenerate series to be observed by the TJNAF missing resonance program. Finally, we explore consequences of the model for some electrodynamic properties of the spectra.

## Key claim

## Method

The author performed a nearest-neighbor analysis of published N and őĒ resonance masses below 2000 MeV, identifying three series (K=1, 3, 5) of nearly degenerate parity twins in each spectrum. These series were mapped onto the O(4) dynamical symmetry limit of a U(4) vibron model describing a quark‚Äďrigid di-quark system, where the quantum number K corresponds to the O(4) Casimir eigenvalue. An algebraic Hamiltonian was constructed from Casimir operators of the chain U(4) ‚äÉ O(4) ‚äÉ O(3) ‚äÉ O(2), and its parameters were fitted to the observed mega-state masses; the Hamiltonian was then translated into a coordinate-space potential combining Coulomb-like and Morse-like terms.

## Context

This work extends the author's own prior nearest-neighbor analyses (Kirchbach, 1997‚Äď1998) of baryon mass spectra and applies the vibron model, previously used for diatomic molecules (Iachello, 1981), to the quark‚Äďdi-quark configuration. The model contradicts the uniformity expected from SU(6)_sf √ó O(3)_L classification schemes and instead reveals regularities in the form of K-mega states. It enables predictions of "missing" resonance series (with even K) to be searched for by the TJNAF missing resonance program, and suggests that electromagnetic helicity amplitudes could be used to extract those missing states via mixing effects.
