# Landau Damping in a strong magnetic field: Dissociation of Quarkonia

> We have investigated the effects of strong magnetic field on the properties of quarkonia immersed in a thermal medium of quarks and gluons and studied its quasi-free dissociation due to the Landau-damping. Thermalizing the Schwinger propagator in the lowest Landau levels for quarks and the Feynman propagator for gluons in real-time formalism, we have calculated the resummed retarded and symmetric propagators, which in turn give the real and imaginary components of dielectric permittivity, respectively. The magnetic field affects the large-distance interaction more than the short-distance interaction, as a result, the real part of potential becomes more attractive and the magnitude of imaginary part too becomes larger, compared to the thermal medium in absence of strong magnetic field. As a consequence the average size of $J/ψ$'s and $ψ^\prime$'s are increased but $χ_c$'s get shrunk. Similarly the magnetic field affects the binding of $J/ψ$'s and $χ_c$'s discriminately, i.e. it decreases the binding of $J/ψ$ and increases for $χ_c$. However, the further increase in magnetic field results in the decrease of binding energies. On contrary the magnetic field increases the width of the resonances, unless the temperature is sufficiently high. We have finally studied how the presence of magnetic field affects the dissolution of quarkonia in a thermal medium due to the Landau damping, where the dissociation temperatures are found to increase compared to the thermal medium in absence of magnetic field. However, further increase of magnetic field decreases the dissociation temperatures. For example, $J/ψ$'s and $χ_c$'s are dissociated at higher temperatures at 2 $T_c$ and 1.1 $T_c$ at a magnetic field $eB \approx 6~{\rm{and}}~4~m_π^2$, respectively, compared to the values 1.60 $T_c$ and 0.8 $T_c$ in the absence of magnetic field, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strong magnetic field increases the dissociation temperatures of $J/\psi$ and $\chi_c$ quarkonia in a thermal QCD medium compared to the case without a magnetic field, but only up to a certain field strength; further increase of the magnetic field then decreases the dissociation temperatures. The effect is state-dependent, with $J/\psi$ and $\psi^\prime$ increasing in size while $\chi_c$ shrinks under the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors calculated the resummed retarded and symmetric gluon propagators in real-time thermal field theory, using the Schwinger propagator in the lowest Landau level for quarks and the Feynman propagator for gluons. From these propagators they obtained the real and imaginary parts of the dielectric permittivity in the static limit, which yielded the complex heavy quark potential in coordinate space via inverse Fourier transform. The real part of the potential was solved numerically in the Schrodinger equation to obtain binding energies and wavefunctions, while the imaginary part was treated in time-independent perturbation theory to estimate thermal widths from Landau damping.

## Key numbers

- $J/\psi$ dissociates at $2\,T_c$ at a magnetic field $eB \approx 6\,m_\pi^2$, compared to $1.60\,T_c$ in the absence of magnetic field.
- $\chi_c$ dissociates at $1.1\,T_c$ at a magnetic field $eB \approx 4\,m_\pi^2$, compared to $0.8\,T_c$ in the absence of magnetic field.
- The Debye mass in the massless quark limit is $m_D^2 = {g^\prime}^2 T^2 + \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2} \sum_f |q_f B|$, combining a thermal gluon-loop term and a magnetic-field-dependent quark-loop term.
- The imaginary part of the retarded self-energy due to the quark loop in the static limit scales as $\lim_{k_0 \to 0} [\text{Im}\,\Pi_R^L(\mathbf{k})/k_0]_{\text{quark loop}} = -g^2 \frac{\sum_f m_f^2 |q_f B|}{8\pi T} \frac{1}{k_z^2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by some of the same authors (Mujeeb et al., 2017) that computed only the real part of the heavy quark potential in a strong magnetic field, and by Balbeer (2017) who derived the complex potential heuristically by replacing the Debye mass. The present calculation provides a first-principles perturbative derivation of both the real and imaginary components of the potential in a strongly magnetized hot QCD medium, including separate treatment of quark-loop and gluon-loop contributions to the self-energy. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium suppression in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where strong magnetic fields are present.
