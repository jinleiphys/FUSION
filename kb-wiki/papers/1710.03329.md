# Causal Electric Charge Diffusion and Balance Functions in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the propogation and diffusion of electric charge fluctuations in high energy heavy ion collisions using the Cattaneo form for the dissipative part of the electric current. As opposed to the ordinary diffusion equation this form limits the speed at which charge can propagate. Including the noise term in the current, which arises uniquely from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, we calculate the balance functions for charged hadrons in a simple 1+1 dimensional Bjorken hydrodynamical model. Limiting the speed of propagation of charge fluctuations increases the height and reduces the width of these balance functions when plotted versus rapidity. We also estimate the numerical value of the associated diffusion time constant from AdS/CFT theory.

## Key claim
Using the Cattaneo form for the dissipative electric current (which limits signal propagation speed) instead of the ordinary diffusion equation increases the height and reduces the width of charge balance functions in rapidity, provided the diffusion time constant is finite; the authors estimate this time constant from AdS/CFT theory.

## Method
The authors implement a 1+1 dimensional Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic model with zero net charge, neglecting shear and bulk viscosity to isolate charge diffusion effects. They solve the Cattaneo equation for electric charge fluctuations including a noise term derived from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, using Green function methods and Fourier transforms in rapidity space. The density-density correlation functions are computed analytically where possible and numerically for the expanding system, with self-correlations subtracted to obtain physically meaningful balance functions.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time: τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c; initial temperature: T₀ = 350 MeV; final temperature: T_f = 150 MeV; final proper time: τ_f = 6.352 fm/c (assuming entropy density s ∝ T³)
- Diffusion constant: D = 0.162 fm (average over temperature interval 150 to 350 MeV from lattice data)
- Total system lifetime: τ_f - τ₀ = 5.852 fm/c
- For ordinary diffusion (τ_Q → 0): width parameter w² = 8 D_Q (τ₀⁻¹ - τ_f⁻¹)
- For small τ_Q: self-correlation takes exponential form with width inversely proportional to v_Q and height proportional to v_Q

## Context
This work extends the study of electric charge diffusion and balance functions by Todd (2014) from ordinary diffusion to the causal Cattaneo equation with finite relaxation time τ_Q. It builds on earlier applications of the Cattaneo equation to heavy ion collisions by Abdel Aziz and Gavin, and on the relativistic fluctuation-dissipation theorem framework developed by Kapusta (2012). The results enable more realistic comparisons with experimental balance function measurements from RHIC and LHC, and the authors compare their approach with higher-order Gurtin-Pipkin equations and AdS/CFT calculations to estimate diffusion and relaxation time scales.
