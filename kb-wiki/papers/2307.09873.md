# Inverse problem in femtoscopic correlation functions: The $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state

> We study here the inverse problem of starting from the femtoscopic correlation functions of related channels and analyze them with an efficient tool to extract the maximum information possible on the interaction of the components of these channels, and the existence of possible bound states tied to this interaction. The method is flexible enough to accommodate non-molecular components and the effect of missing channels relevant for the interaction. We apply the method to realistic correlation functions for the $D^{*+}D^0$ and $D^{*0}D^+$ channels derived consistently from the properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and find that we can extract the existence of a bound state, its nature as a molecular state of the $D^{*+}D^0$ and $D^{*0}D^+$ channels, the probabilities of each channel, as well as scattering lengths and effective ranges for the channels, together with the size of the source function, all of them with a relatively good precision.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by starting from synthetic femtoscopic correlation functions for the $D^{*+}D^0$ and $D^{*0}D^+$ channels, one can extract the existence of a bound state (the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$), its molecular nature, channel probabilities, scattering lengths, effective ranges, and source size with relatively good precision, provided the correlation functions are measured with about 10% uncertainty.

## Method

The authors generate synthetic correlation function data from a previously calculated model (Vidana 2023) for the $D^{*+}D^0$ and $D^{*0}D^+$ channels, taking 50 momentum points per channel with 10% errors. They then apply three increasingly sophisticated analysis methods to these synthetic data: a simplified method to obtain approximate scattering lengths, a K-matrix approach, and a full coupled-channel unitary scheme using a Bethe-Salpeter equation with a potential that can accommodate non-molecular components. The full method uses a Gaussian source function and a cutoff-regularized loop function, with parameters fitted via MINUIT and uncertainties estimated using bootstrap techniques.

* Isoscalar potential matrix element $V_{I=0} = -435.88(88)$

## Context

This work extends the direct problem studied by Vidana (2023), who calculated correlation functions from known $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ properties, by solving the inverse problem of extracting interaction parameters from correlation function data. It builds on the Koonin-Prat formalism (Koonin 1977, Pratt 1990) and uses a coupled-channel unitary approach similar to that employed by Dai (2023) for analyzing $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ mass distributions from LHCb data (LHCb 2021). The method enables future experimental analyses of femtoscopic correlation functions for the $D^{*+}D^0$ and $D^{*0}D^+$ channels, which the ALICE collaboration plans to measure, to determine the nature and properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state.
