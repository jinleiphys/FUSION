# The Functional Renormalization Group and O(4) scaling

> The critical behavior of the chiral quark-meson model is studied within the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG). We derive the flow equation for the scale dependent thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and density in the presence of a symmetry-breaking external field. Within this scheme, the critical scaling behavior of the order parameter, its transverse and longitudinal susceptibilities as well as the correlation lengths near the chiral phase transition are computed. We focus on the scaling properties of these observables at non-vanishing external field when approaching the critical point from the symmetric as well as from the broken phase. We confront our numerical results with the Widom-Griffiths form of the magnetic equation of state, obtained by a systematic epsilon-expansion of the scaling function. Our results for the critical exponents are consistent with those recently computed within Lattice Monte-Carlo studies of the O(4) spin system.

## Key claim

The Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) applied to the chiral quark-meson model yields critical exponents for the order parameter, susceptibilities, and correlation lengths that are consistent with O(4) universality class values from lattice Monte Carlo studies, even when wavefunction renormalization (anomalous dimension) is neglected in the leading-order derivative expansion.

## Method

The authors derived and solved the FRG flow equation for the scale-dependent thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and zero chemical potential, using optimized regulator functions for bosons and fermions in the leading-order derivative expansion. The potential was expanded in a Taylor series around its minimum truncated at third order in the field variable ρ, and the flow equations were solved numerically starting from a cutoff scale Λ = 1.2 GeV with initial parameters reproducing vacuum pion mass (138 MeV), pion decay constant (93 MeV), sigma mass (600 MeV), and constituent quark mass (300 MeV). The critical scaling of the order parameter, transverse and longitudinal susceptibilities, and correlation lengths was extracted near the chiral phase transition for both vanishing and finite external symmetry-breaking fields.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponent β ≈ 0.402 for the order parameter scaling ⟨σ̃⟩ ∼ (−t)^β at h = 0, t < 0
- Critical exponent δ ≈ 4.818 for the order parameter scaling ⟨σ̃⟩ ∼ h^(1/δ) at t = 0
- Effective critical exponent in the symmetric phase (t > 0) for finite h: R_⟨σ⟩^h ≈ −γ with γ ≈ 1.6
- Lattice comparison: Kanaya and Kaya (1994) found β = 0.3836(46) and δ = 4.851(22) for the three-dimensional O(4) spin model
- Mean-field values differ: β = 0.5 and δ = 3
- Cutoff scale Λ = 1.2 GeV; vacuum parameters: m_π = 138 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, m_σ = 600 MeV, m_q = 300 MeV, Yukawa coupling g = 3.2

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the quark-meson model (Berges 2002, Schaefer 2004) by computing scaling functions and effective critical exponents in both the broken and symmetric phases, and by comparing the magnetic equation of state to the Widom-Griffiths form from epsilon-expansion. The results confirm that the chiral phase transition in two-flavor QCD belongs to the O(4) universality class, consistent with lattice calculations by Kanaya and Kaya (1994), and demonstrate that the leading-order derivative expansion without wavefunction renormalization captures the correct universal behavior, enabling reliable extraction of critical properties from effective models.
