# Compton scattering from the proton at NLO in the chiral expansion

> We present calculations of differential cross sections for Compton scattering from the proton, using amplitudes calculated to fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We compare with available data up to 200 MeV. We find that the agreement for angles below 90 degree is acceptable over the whole energy range, but that at more backward angles the agreement decreases above about 100 MeV, and fails completely above the photoproduction threshold.

## Key claim

The paper finds that fourth-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) gives acceptable agreement with proton Compton scattering data for angles below 90 degrees across the full energy range up to 200 MeV, but at more backward angles the agreement degrades above about 100 MeV and fails completely above the photoproduction threshold, with the fourth-order corrections being small and not resolving the discrepancy.

## Method

The author calculated the full spin-dependent and spin-independent Compton scattering amplitudes for the proton to fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT), using the Breit frame for simplicity. The amplitudes were then used to compute differential cross sections, which were compared with experimental data from multiple experiments (Baranov, Ziegler, Federspiel, Hallin, MacGibbon, and Olmos de Leon) for lab energies up to 200 MeV and various scattering angles. The calculation included a variable change from the Breit frame energy to a shifted variable to place the threshold at the physical value, and the fourth-order low-energy constants contributing to the electric and magnetic polarisabilities were fixed to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- The third-order HBCPT predictions for the proton polarisabilities are α = 12.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β = α/10, compared to the world averages α = 12.1 ± 0.8 ± 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and α+β = 14.1 ± 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The spin polarisability γ₀ from direct polarised photon-proton scattering at MAMI is −0.8 ± 0.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴.
- To reproduce the Mainz data at 180 degrees, the author needed to shift the polarisabilities to α = 8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β = 6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, but even these extreme values did not cure the problem above threshold.
- The pion mass used in the loops is mπ = 139.6 MeV, with gA = 1.267, fπ = 92.32, κs = −0.120, κv = 3.706.
- The low-energy constants from pion-nucleon scattering used are c₁ = −0.81 GeV, c₂ = 2.5 GeV, c₃ = −3.8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the third-order HBCPT calculations of Compton scattering by Bernard et al. (1993) and the study by Babusci et al. (1997), who showed that third-order agreement started to break down for backward angles above about 120 MeV. The paper finds that fourth-order corrections are small and do not resolve the backward-angle discrepancy, and that very large fifth-order contributions would be needed to approach experimental data above threshold at backward angles. The results enable future work incorporating explicit Delta resonance contributions, which are expected to appear at fifth order in the spin-dependent amplitudes.
