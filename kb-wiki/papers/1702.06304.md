# A toy model of elastic scattering of high energy protons

> The ratio of elastic to total proton cross sections is related to the darkness of the spatial profile of inelastic interactions by a single parameter in the framework of a simple analytical model. Their critical values at LHC energies are discussed. Two possible variants of their asymptotical behavior are described.

# Elastic scattering and spatial inelastic profiles of high energy protons

## Key claim
The ratio of elastic to total proton cross sections and the darkness factor of the inelastic spatial profile are both close to 1 at LHC energies, making this a critical regime where small changes in the single parameter \(B|t_0|\) determine whether the inelastic profile saturates at complete darkness or evolves toward a toroidal hollow shape; the model neglects the real part of the elastic amplitude, which contributes less than 1.5% to the elastic cross section.

## Method
The author constructs a simplified analytical model for the imaginary part of the elastic scattering amplitude, parameterized by the total cross section \(\sigma_{tot}\), the slope \(B\) of the diffraction cone, and the zero position \(t_0\) of the imaginary part. The differential cross section is approximated as \(d\sigma/dt \approx f_I^2\) using a quadratic factor \((1-(t/t_0)^2)^2\) times an exponential \(e^{Bt}\), and the elastic cross section and darkness factor \(\zeta\) are derived analytically. The model is benchmarked against the kfk-model (Kuroda, Fujii, Kato; 2015) at 7 TeV, using its values \(B = 19.90\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and \(|t_0| = 0.4757\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers
- At 7 TeV, the ratio of the differential cross section at the dip to that at \(t=0\) is \(d\sigma/dt|_{dip} / d\sigma/dt|_{t=0} \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}\).
- At 7 TeV, the real part at the dip \(f_R(t=t_{dip})\) is less than \(5 \times 10^{-3}\) of the imaginary part at the diffraction peak \(f_I(t=0)\).
- At LHC energies, the product \(B|t_0| \approx 10\) (with \(B \approx 20\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and \(|t_0| \approx 0.5\) GeV\(^2\)), making the ratio \(r/\zeta \approx 1.04\) (exceeding 1 by less than 5%).
- The contribution of the tail beyond the dip to \(\zeta\) is about \(-2 \times 10^{-3}\) at LHC.
- The experimental values of \(r\) at LHC range from \(1.01 \pm 0.06\) (TOTEM, 2013) to \(1.06 \pm 0.06\) (TOTEM, 2014).
- The factor \(\sigma_{tot}/(4\pi B)\) increased from about 0.67 at ISR energies to \(1.02 \pm 0.04\) at LHC.
- In the kfk-model, the asymptotic values are \(r = 1.416\) and \(\zeta = 1\).

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological kfk-model (Kuroda, Fujii, Kato; 2015), which uses 8 energy-dependent parameters to describe elastic scattering from 20 GeV to 8 TeV, by reducing the description to a single dimensionless parameter \(B|t_0|\). The model addresses the unexplained increase of the elastic-to-total cross section ratio from about 0.67 at ISR energies to near 1 at LHC, a trend first noted in reviews by Dremin (2015, 2017). It enables experimentalists to estimate the required precision for measurements of \(\sigma_{tot}\), \(B\), and the dip position \(t_{dip}\) to determine whether the darkness factor \(\zeta\) saturates at 1 or exceeds 1 at higher energies, which would distinguish between complete attenuation at the center of the inelastic profile and the toroidal elastic hollow regime.
