# Proposal to look for the anomalous isotopic symmetry breaking in central diffractive production of the $f_1(1285)$ and $a^0_0(980)$ resonances at the LHC

> At very high energies, and in the central region ($x_F\simeq0$), the double-Pomeron exchange mechanism gives the dominant contribution to the production of hadrons with the positive $C$ parity and isospin $I=0$. Therefore, the observation of resonances in the states with $I=1$ will be indicative of their production or decay with the isotopic symmetry breaking. Here, we bear in mind the cases of the anomalous breaking of the isotopic symmetry, i.e., when the cross section of the process breaking the isospin is not of the order of $10^{-4}$ of the cross section of the allowed process but of the order of $1\%$. The paper draws attention to the reactions $pp\to p(f_1(1285)/f_1(1420))p\to p(π^+π^-π^0)p$ and $pp\to p(K\bar K)p\to p(a^0_0(980))p\to p(ηπ^0)p$ in which a similar situation can be realized, owing to the $K\bar K$ loop mechanisms of the breaking of isotopic symmetry. We note that there is no visible background in the $π^+π^-π^0$ and $ηπ^0$ channels. Observation of the process $pp\to p(f_1(1285))p\to p(π^+π^-π^0)p$ would be a confirmation of the first results from the VES and BESIII detectors, indicating the very large isospin breaking in the decay $f_1(1285))\toπ^+π^-π^0$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the double-Pomeron exchange mechanism at LHC energies can produce anomalously large isospin-breaking signals (on the order of 1% rather than the typical 10^{-4}) in the central exclusive production of the f_1(1285) and a^0_0(980) resonances, mediated by K-bar-K loop mechanisms that exploit the kaon mass difference.

## Method

The authors analyze two specific reactions: pp → p(f_1(1285))p → p(π⁺π⁻π⁰)p and pp → p(a^0_0(980))p → p(ηπ⁰)p, both proceeding via double-Pomeron exchange in the central region (xF ≈ 0). For the f_1(1285) case, they examine the decay chain f_1(1285) → f_0(980)π⁰ → π⁺π⁻π⁰, where isospin breaking arises from incomplete compensation between K⁺K⁻ and K⁰K̄⁰ loop contributions due to the K⁺/K⁰ mass difference. For the a^0_0(980) case, they describe production via a^0_0(980)-f_0(980) mixing through K-bar-K loop transitions, with the cross section given by a formula involving the a^0_0(980) and f_0(980) propagators and their mixing amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The ratio BR(f_1(1285) → f_0(980)π⁰ → π⁺π⁻π⁰) / BR(f_1(1285) → ηπ⁺π⁻) = (0.86 ± 0.16 ± 0.20)% from the VES Collaboration (2011)
- The same ratio = (1.23 ± 0.55)% from the BESIII Collaboration
- BR(f_1(1285) → ηπ⁺π⁻) ≈ 35% (PDG 2016)
- At √s = 7 TeV (LHC), the subprocess energies √s₁ ≈ √s₂ ≈ 84 GeV for M ≈ 1 GeV, ensuring Pomeron dominance
- At lower-energy experiments (CERN, Tevatron fixed target), √s₁ ≈ √s₂ were approximately 3.6, 4.9, 5.4, and 6.3 GeV
- The estimated cross section σ(PP → (K⁺K⁻ + K⁰K̄⁰) → a^0_0(980) → ηπ⁰; m) ≈ |Ã(2m_{K⁺})|² × 0.05 between the K⁺K⁻ and K⁰K̄⁰ thresholds
- The a^0_0(980) peak in the ηπ⁰ channel is predicted to narrow to a width of 10-20 MeV at LHC energies
- WA102 Collaboration measured Γ(a_0(980)) = 72 ± 16 MeV and Γ(a_2(1320)) = 115 ± 20 MeV at √s = 29 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of central exclusive production of hadrons via double-Pomeron exchange, which has been investigated at the ISR, Sp̄pS, Tevatron, and LHC (reviewed by Khoze et al. 2007, Albrow et al. 2010, and others). It directly builds on results from the VES Collaboration (Doronin 2011) and BESIII Collaboration (Ablikim 2015) that showed anomalously large isospin breaking in f_1(1285) → π⁺π⁻π⁰ decays. The proposed measurements at the LHC would test the K-bar-K loop mechanism for isospin breaking (Achasov et al. 1979, 2016, 2017) and could clarify whether the a^0_0(980) production mechanism changes from secondary Regge exchanges at lower energies to double-Pomeron exchange at LHC energies, as indicated by the predicted narrowing of the a^0_0(980) peak.
