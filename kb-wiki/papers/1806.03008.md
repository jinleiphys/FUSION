# Thermomagnetic properties and Bjorken expansion of hot QCD matter in a strong magnetic field

> In this work we have studied the effects of an external strong magnetic field on the thermodynamic and magnetic properties of a hot QCD matter and then explored these effects on the subsequent hydrodynamic expansion of the said matter once produced in the ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. For that purpose, we have computed the quark and gluon self-energies up to one loop in the strong magnetic field, using the HTL approximation with two hard scales - temperature and magnetic field, which in turn compute the effective propagators for quarks and gluons, respectively. Hence the quark and gluon contributions to the free energy are obtained from the respective propagators and finally derive the equation of state (EOS) by calculating the pressure and energy density. We have found that the speed of sound is enhanced due to the presence of strong magnetic field and this effect will be later exploited in the hydrodynamics. Thereafter the magnetic properties are studied from the free energy of the matter, where the magnetization is found to increase linearly with the magnetic field, thus hints the paramagnetic behavior. The temperature dependence of the magnetization is also studied, where the magnetization is found to increase slowly with the temperature. Finally, to see how a strong magnetic field could affect the hydrodynamic evolution, we have revisited the Bjorken boost-invariant picture with our paramagnetic EOS as an input in the equation of motion for the energy-momentum conservation. We have noticed that the energy density evolves faster than in the absence of strong magnetic field, i.e. cooling becomes faster, which could have implications on the heavy-ion phenomenology. As mentioned earlier, this observation can be understood by the enhancement of the speed of sound.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in a strongly magnetized hot QCD medium (where \(|q_fB| \gg T^2\) and \(|q_fB| \gg m_f^2\)), the speed of sound is enhanced relative to the zero-field case, and that this enhancement causes the energy density in a Bjorken-expanding fireball to cool faster than in the absence of a strong magnetic field. The hedge is that these results are obtained within a perturbative hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation using two hard scales (temperature for gluons, magnetic field for quarks) and are restricted to the lowest Landau level (LLL) for quarks.

## Method
The authors computed the one-loop quark and gluon self-energies in a strong magnetic field using the HTL approximation with two hard scales: temperature for gluon loops and the magnetic field scale \(\sqrt{eB}\) for quark loops. From these self-energies, they obtained effective propagators and derived the free energy, from which the equation of state (pressure, energy density, speed of sound) and magnetic properties (magnetization, susceptibility) were extracted. They then used the paramagnetic equation of state as input into the Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic equation of motion to study the time evolution of the energy density.

## Key numbers
- The square of the Debye mass due to the quark-loop contribution is defined as \(m^2_{q,D} = \frac{g^{2}}{4\pi^2}\sum_{f}|q_{f}B|\).
- The square of the Debye mass due to the gluon and ghost-loop is \(m^{\prime2}_{g,D} = \frac{g^{\prime2}T^2N_c}{3}\).
- The gluon-loop contribution to the free energy (up to \(\mathcal{O}(m_{g,D}^{\prime4})\)) includes a term \(-\frac{\pi^2T^4}{45}\) (the Stefan-Boltzmann limit) plus corrections such as \(+\frac{T^2 m^{\prime2}_{g,D}}{24}\) and \(-\frac{T m^{\prime3}_{g,D}}{12\pi}\).
- The quark-loop contribution to the gluon free energy in the soft momentum region is \(-\frac{(N_c^2-1)T m^3_{q,D}}{12\pi}\).
- The magnetization is found to increase linearly with the magnetic field, indicating paramagnetic behavior; the temperature dependence of the magnetization increases slowly with temperature.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous perturbative study of thermodynamic observables in a strong magnetic field (Rath 2017) and complements lattice QCD results for the equation of state in a magnetic field (Bali 2014, Endrodi 2015). It also builds on prior studies of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (Umut 2014, Roy 2015, Pu 2016, Roy 2017) by using a paramagnetic equation of state derived from first principles, rather than parameterizing the magnetic response. The results enable more realistic modeling of the hydrodynamic expansion of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions where strong transient magnetic fields are present.
