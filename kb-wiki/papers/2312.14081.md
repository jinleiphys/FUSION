# Event-by-event Kinetic Description of Pre-Equilibrium Charge Evolution in QCD Plasma

> We use QCD effective kinetic theory to calculate far-from-equilibrium dynamics on an event-by-event basis within the KøMPøST framework. We present non-equilibrium charge response functions and the dynamical evolution of the conserved charge current pertinent to the early-time dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at the highest energies. The KøMPøST framework with conserved baryon, strangeness, and electric charges can then be readily implemented into a multistage model allowing for the initialization of a non-equilibrium charge current in hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key claim

The KøMPøST framework, which evolves far-from-equilibrium QCD matter toward hydrodynamics, has been extended to include the evolution of conserved baryon, strangeness, and electric charges; the charge response functions show a smooth transition from early free-streaming to diffusive hydrodynamic behavior on time scales of order the equilibration time, but the detailed analysis of the hydrodynamic limit for the event-by-event charge current evolution is deferred to a future full manuscript.

## Method

The authors solve QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) with inelastic and elastic collision kernels from the AMY formalism, treating the longitudinally boost-invariant expansion as a background that is isotropic in the transverse plane and adding spatial fluctuations as perturbations. They compute non-equilibrium Green's functions for the conserved charge current in position space, separating scalar (charge-to-charge) and vector (current-to-charge) response functions, and evolve event-by-event initial charge distributions from the McDIPPER framework through the KøMPøST framework to a hydrodynamic starting time.

## Key numbers

- The system is evolved to a hydrodynamic starting time τ_hydro of 1.3 fm/c for the event-by-event charge evolution shown.
- The transition between early free-streaming and hydrodynamic behavior occurs on time scales τ/τ_R^eq ~ 1, where τ_R^eq is the equilibration time.
- The initial fluctuation scale in the charge distribution is of the order of the nucleon size, as determined by the initial state model.

## Context

This work extends the KøMPøST framework of Kurkela (2018) to include conserved charges, which had been absent from state-of-the-art models at the highest LHC energies. It builds on recent theoretical work by Garcia-Montero (2023) that constructed a framework for producing finite net-baryon number at mid-rapidity, and uses the McDIPPER framework of Garcia-Montero (2023) for initial charge distributions. The results enable the initialization of non-equilibrium charge currents in multistage hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, with future plans to include a background for conserved charges and extend to 3+1D.
