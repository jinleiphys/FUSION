# Microscopic Calculation of the $^4{\rm He}$ System

> We report on a consistent, microscopic calculation of the bound and scattering states in the 4He system employing a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential in the framework of the resonating group model (RGM). We present for comparison with these microscopic RGM calculations the results from a charge-independent, Coulomb-corrected R-matrix analysis of all types of data for reactions in the A=4 system. Comparisons are made between the phase shifts, and with a selection of measurements from each reaction, as well as between the resonance spectra obtained from both calculations. In general, the comparisons are favorable, but distinct differences are observed between the RGM calculations and some of the polarisation data. The partial-wave decomposition of the experimental data produced by the R-matrix analysis shows that these differences can be attributed to just a few S-matrix elements, for which inadequate tensor-force strength in the N-N interaction used appears to be responsible.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic resonating group model (RGM) calculation using a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential (the Bonn potential) without any adjustable parameters can reproduce essentially all known experimental features of the 4He system, but distinct differences remain between the RGM calculations and some polarisation data, which the authors attribute to inadequate tensor-force strength in the N-N interaction used.

## Method

The authors performed a consistent microscopic calculation of bound and scattering states in the 4He system using the resonating group model (RGM) with the r-space version of the Bonn potential expressed in Gaussian functions, treating the system as a four-cluster problem. They compared these results with a charge-independent, Coulomb-corrected R-matrix analysis of all types of data for reactions in the A=4 system, including data from p-3H, n-3He, and d-d reactions totaling 4652 data points across 28 observable types. The RGM calculation included three two-fragment channels (triton-proton, 3He-neutron, and deuteron-deuteron) plus distortion channels to allow for variational freedom in the interaction region, with the S-matrix determined from the Kohn-Hulthén variational principle.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 4He: -25.910 MeV (RGM calculation with 227 channels), compared to -25.86(15) MeV from Carlson (Green's function Monte Carlo)
- Triton binding energy: -7.951 MeV (reduced wave function), compared to -8.29 MeV (Carlson Faddeev) and experimental -8.481 MeV
- 3He binding energy: -7.258 MeV (reduced wave function), compared to experimental -7.718 MeV
- Charge radius of 4He ground state: 1.47 fm (full calculation)
- D-state probability (P_{S=2}) for 4He ground state: 10.2% (full calculation), compared to 11.3(1)% from Carlson
- First excited 0+ state energy: -6.417 MeV (full calculation), with charge radius 3.09 fm
- R-matrix analysis included data up to 11 MeV proton energy for p-3H reactions and up to 10 MeV for d-d reactions, with channel radii a_{pt} = a_{n-3He} = 4.9 fm and a_{dd} = 7.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive R-matrix analysis of Hale (1995) and the A=4 compilation of Tilley et al. (1992) by providing a fully microscopic calculation with no adjustable parameters, in contrast to earlier calculations that used effective potentials with modified parameters. The RGM results agree closely with the R-matrix analysis for most phase shifts and resonance energies, enabling future studies of reactions such as d-d radiative capture (Eddington, 1995) and providing a foundation for understanding the structure of the first excited 0+ state, which the authors find has a radius much larger than the ground state, casting doubt on its interpretation as a simple breathing mode.
