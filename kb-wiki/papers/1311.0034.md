# Solution to sign problems in half-filled spin-polarized electronic systems

> We solve the sign problem in a particle-hole symmetric spin-polarized fermion model on bipartite lattices using the idea of fermion bags. The solution can be extended to a class of models at half filling but without particle-hole symmetry. Attractive Hubbard models with an odd number of fermion species can also be solved. The new solutions should allow us to study quantum phase transitions that have remained unexplored so far due to sign problems.

## Key claim

The sign problem in a particle-hole symmetric spin-polarized fermion model on bipartite lattices is solved for all values of \(V > 0\) using the fermion bag approach, but the solution is restricted to half filling and does not yet extend to doped systems away from half filling.

## Method

The authors rewrite the partition function as a series expansion in powers of interaction vertices, then evaluate the trace using Wick's theorem to obtain a determinant of a \(2k \times 2k\) matrix \(G([b,s,t])\). They apply the fermion bag approach to sum over the auxiliary variables \([s]\) that appear only in the diagonal elements of \(G\), which eliminates the diagonal contributions and leaves a determinant of the off-diagonal matrix \(A([b,t])\). Using the property \(A^T = -\tilde{D} A \tilde{D}\) derived from the free matrix \(M\), they show that \((A\tilde{D})\) is a real anti-symmetric matrix with a positive determinant, yielding a positive Boltzmann weight for all configurations when \(V > 0\).

## Key numbers

- On an \(8 \times 8\) square lattice at \(\beta = 10\) with \(k = 125\) interaction bonds (250 interaction vertices), out of 10,000 random configurations, 4972 had positive determinants and 5028 had negative determinants, demonstrating a severe sign problem before the solution.
- The sum over \([s]\) configurations yields a factor of \(4^k\) in the partition function, where \(k\) is the number of interaction bonds.
- The sign of the final weight is determined by \((-1)^k \mathrm{Det}(A([b,t])) = \mathrm{Det}(A([b,t])\tilde{D}) \geq 0\), with \(\mathrm{Det}(\tilde{D}) = (-1)^k\) because \(k\) sites belong to the even sublattice and \(k\) to the odd sublattice.

## Context

This work solves the sign problem for the \(tV\)-model on bipartite lattices, which had previously only been solvable for \(V \geq 2t\) using the meron-cluster approach (PhysRevLett.83.3116). The solution extends to attractive Hubbard models with an odd number of fermion species, such as the \(SU(3)\) symmetric attractive Hubbard model with three species, which could not be studied before due to sign problems. The method enables quantum Monte Carlo studies of quantum phase transitions from a semi-metal phase to a Mott insulating phase in systems with a single flavor of four-component Dirac fermions, previously only accessible for even numbers of flavors (Christofi 2007, Chandrasekharan 2011, PhysRevX.3.031010).
