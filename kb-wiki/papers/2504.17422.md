# Exact solutions for the moments of the binary collision integral and its relation to the relaxation-time approximation in leading-order anisotropic fluid dynamics

> We compute the moments of the nonlinear binary collision integral in the ultrarelativistic hard-sphere approximation for an arbitrary anisotropic distribution function in the local rest frame. This anisotropic distribution function has an angular asymmetry controlled by the parameter of anisotropy $ξ$, such that in the limit of a vanishing anisotropy $\lim_{ξ\rightarrow 0} \hat{f}_{0 \mathbf{k}} = f_{0 \mathbf{k}}$, approaches the spherically symmetric local equilibrium distribution function. The corresponding moments of the binary collision integral are obtained in terms of quadratic products of different moments of the anisotropic distribution function and couple to a well defined set of lower-order moments. To illustrate these results we compare the moments of the binary collision integral to the moments of the widely used relaxation-time approximation of Anderson and Witting in case of a spheroidal distribution function. We found that in an expanding system the nonlinear Boltzmann collision term leads to twice slower equilibration than the relaxation-time approximation. Furthermore we also show that including two dynamical moments helps to resolve the ambiguity which additional moment of the Boltzmann equation to choose to close the conservation laws.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact analytic expressions for the moments of the nonlinear binary collision integral for arbitrary anisotropic distribution functions in the ultrarelativistic hard-sphere approximation, and demonstrates that the widely used relaxation-time approximation of Anderson and Witting drives the system toward equilibrium approximately twice as fast as the full nonlinear Boltzmann collision term in an expanding system.

## Method

The author computes the moments of the gain and loss terms of the binary collision integral separately, using a projection method that decomposes the 12-dimensional integrals into products of anisotropic thermodynamic integrals. The transition rate is taken in the ultrarelativistic hard-sphere approximation with an isotropic, energy-independent total cross section. The resulting moments are expressed as quadratic products of lower-order moments of the anisotropic distribution function, and are then compared analytically and numerically to the corresponding moments of the Anderson-Witting relaxation-time approximation for the spheroidal (Romatschke-Strickland) distribution function in a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant expanding system.

## Key numbers

- The nonlinear Boltzmann collision term leads to equilibration that is "twice slower" than the relaxation-time approximation in an expanding system, irrespective of the choice of dynamical moment(s) used for closure.
- The relaxation time of the RTA must be rescaled by factors \(\tau_{ij}(\tau_R)\) (defined asymptotically from the ratio of the binary collision integral to the RTA collision integral) to match the characteristics of the binary collision integral for higher-order moments.
- The anisotropic thermodynamic integrals \(\hat{I}_{nrq}\) are defined with indices \(i \geq -1\) and \(j \geq 0\) for the powers of \(E_{\mathbf{k}u}\) and \(E_{\mathbf{k}l}\), respectively.
- The total cross section in the hard-sphere approximation is given by \(\sigma_T = 1/(\hat{n} \lambda_{\mathrm{mfp}})\), where \(\hat{n}\) is the particle density and \(\lambda_{\mathrm{mfp}}\) is the mean free path.

## Context

This work extends the exact solution methods for the nonlinear Boltzmann collision integral from isotropic distribution functions (the Bobylev-Krook-Wu model and its relativistic generalization, the Bazow-Denicol-Heinz-Martinez-Noronha model) to anisotropic distribution functions relevant for leading-order anisotropic fluid dynamics. The results enable a direct comparison between the full binary collision integral and the Anderson-Witting relaxation-time approximation (Anderson, 1974), showing that the RTA overestimates the equilibration rate by a factor of two. The paper also resolves the closure ambiguity in the moment hierarchy by demonstrating that including two dynamical moments (rather than one, as in earlier work by Molnar (2016) and Niemi (2017)) improves the fluid dynamical description. This has implications for modeling the momentum-space anisotropies of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, where equilibration timescales are on the order of a few fm/c.
