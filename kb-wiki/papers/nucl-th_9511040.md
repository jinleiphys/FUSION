# Amplitude ambiguities in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction

> We consider the problem of determining amplitudes from observables for the case of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction. We find a number of surprisingly simple constraints which give necessary conditions for a complete set of measurements. These results contradict one of the selection rules derived previously.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the previously accepted selection rule for a complete set of measurements in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (requiring no more than two double-polarization observables from any one set) is insufficient to resolve all amplitude ambiguities; an additional ambiguity involving complex conjugation (Ambiguity IV) imposes further constraints that were missed in the earlier work of Barker, Donnachie and Storrow.

## Method

The authors reexamined the problem of determining helicity amplitudes from observables by generating discrete ambiguity transformations (I through IV) that leave the cross section and single-polarization observables unchanged. They then tested whether proposed sets of double-polarization measurements were invariant under these transformations, using the transversity representation and the naming scheme of Barker, Donnachie and Storrow (1975) to identify necessary conditions for a complete experiment.

## Key numbers

- A set of 6 observables ($H$, $E$, $O_x$, $C_z$, $T_x$, and $L_z$) fails to resolve Ambiguity IV, demonstrating that the earlier "no four from any set" criterion (allowing 768 possible combinations) is insufficient.
- Only 3 double-polarization measurements (not all from the same set) were previously found necessary to determine amplitudes up to "quadrant ambiguities".
- The earlier claim required 5 double-polarization measurements to resolve all ambiguities apart from an overall phase.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of complete experiments in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction originally developed by Barker, Donnachie and Storrow (1975), and contradicts their selection rule for resolving all discrete ambiguities. The results provide necessary conditions for planning experiments at CEBAF, where new photo- and electroproduction data are expected, and the authors note that measurements of $G$, $H$, and $O_x$ exist that resolve all listed ambiguities. The paper leaves open the problem of proving sufficient conditions for a complete set of measurements.
