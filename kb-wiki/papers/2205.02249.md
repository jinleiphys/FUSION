# The Four Loop QCD Rapidity Anomalous Dimension

> The rapidity anomalous dimension controls the scaling of transverse momentum dependent observables in the Sudakov region. In a conformal theory it is equivalent to the soft anomalous dimension, but in QCD this relation is broken by anomalous terms proportional to the $β$-function. In this paper we first give a simple proof of this relation using two different representations of the energy-energy correlator observable. We then calculate the anomalous terms to three loops by computing the three-loop fully differential soft function to $\mathcal{O}(ε)$. Combined with recent perturbative data from the study of on-shell form factors and splitting functions, this allows us to derive the four loop rapidity anomalous dimension in QCD.

## Key claim

The four-loop rapidity anomalous dimension in QCD is derived by exploiting the relation that in a conformal theory it equals the soft anomalous dimension, with the difference in QCD being proportional to the beta-function; this allows the four-loop result to be obtained from a three-loop calculation of the anomalous terms combined with known four-loop soft anomalous dimension data.

## Method

The authors compute the three-loop fully differential soft function to O(epsilon) in dimensional regularization, which provides the higher-order-in-epsilon terms needed to determine the beta-function-proportional corrections that break the conformal equality between the rapidity and soft anomalous dimensions. These corrections are combined with known four-loop results for the cusp anomalous dimension, the virtual anomalous dimension B_delta, and the collinear anomalous dimension from recent form factor and splitting function calculations to assemble the four-loop rapidity anomalous dimension. The relation between soft and rapidity anomalous dimensions in a conformal theory is independently proven by comparing two representations of the energy-energy correlator: a timelike factorization formula from SCET and a Euclidean four-point function representation due to Korchemsky (2019).

## Key numbers

* The four-loop soft anomalous dimension gamma^S_3 for fundamental Wilson lines (C_r = C_F) contains terms such as C_A^3 C_F multiplied by a combination including -9311591/13122, (2189/9) zeta_3^2, and (1057/3) zeta_4 + 414602/243 times zeta_3, among many other rational and zeta-valued coefficients.
* The numerical values for the color coefficients b(N_f C_F^2 C_r) = 80.780 +/- 0.005, b(N_f C_F C_A C_r) = -455.247 +/- 0.005, b(d^{(4)}_{FF}) = -143.6 +/- 0.2, and b(d^{(4)}_{FA}) = -998.0 +/- 0.2 are extracted from the four-loop B_delta anomalous dimension and used in the soft anomalous dimension expression.
* The quartic Casimir invariants are given as d^{(4)}_{FF} = (N_c^4 - 6N_c^2 + 18)(N_c^2 - 1)/(96 N_c^2), d^{(4)}_{FA} = N_c(N_c^2 + 6)(N_c^2 - 1)/48, and d^{(4)}_{AA} = N_c^2(N_c^2 + 36)(N_c^2 - 1)/24 for SU(N_c).

## Context

This work extends the three-loop rapidity anomalous dimension calculation of Li, Zhu, and collaborators (2016) to four loops, and relies on the all-orders proof of the soft-rapidity correspondence by Vladimirov (2016, 2017). It uses recent four-loop results for the cusp anomalous dimension (Henn et al. 2019, von Manteuffel et al. 2020), the collinear anomalous dimension from full four-loop form factors (Lee et al. 2021, 2022; Chakraborty et al. 2022), and the virtual anomalous dimension B_delta (Das et al. 2019, 2020). The result enables N^4LL resummation for the energy-energy correlator in the back-to-back region and for transverse momentum spectra of Higgs and electroweak bosons, pending completion of the four-loop DGLAP splitting functions.
