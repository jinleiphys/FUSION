# Magnetohydrodynamics, charged currents and directed flow in heavy ion collisions

> The hot QCD matter produced in any heavy ion collision with a nonzero impact parameter is produced within a strong magnetic field. We study the imprint that these fields leave on the azimuthal distributions and correlations of the produced charged hadrons. The magnetic field is time-dependent and the medium is expanding, which leads to the induction of charged currents due to the combination of Faraday and Hall effects. We find that these currents result in a charge- dependent directed flow v1 that is odd in rapidity and odd under charge exchange. It can be detected by measuring correlations between the directed flow of charged hadrons at different rapidities, $\langle v_1^\pm(y_1)v_1^\pm(y_2)\rangle$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong, time-dependent magnetic field present in non-central heavy ion collisions induces electric currents in the quark-gluon plasma via Faraday and Hall effects, which produce a charge-dependent directed flow \(v_1\) that is odd in rapidity and odd under charge exchange; however, the authors emphasize that their calculation is an order-of-magnitude estimate based on many simplifying assumptions (constant conductivity, azimuthally symmetric background flow, perturbative treatment of the magnetic field effects) and that a full numerical magnetohydrodynamic analysis is needed for precise predictions.

## Method

The authors first solve Maxwell's equations with a constant electrical conductivity \(\sigma = 0.023\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to compute the magnetic and electric fields produced by spectator and participant protons in a non-central heavy ion collision with impact parameter \(b = 7\ \text{fm}\). They then use Gubser's analytic solution (2010) for conformal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics as the background flow field, describing a boost-invariant, azimuthally symmetric expanding plasma. In the local fluid rest frame, they solve the Lorentz force equation with a drag term to find the small charge-dependent velocity \(\vec{v}\) for positively and negatively charged species, transform back to the center-of-mass frame, and apply the Cooper-Frye freezeout procedure (1974) to compute the directed flow \(v_1\) for pions and (anti)protons as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength at early times: \(e|\vec{B}|/m_\pi^2 \approx 1\text{–}3\) at 0.1–0.2 fm/\(c\) after a RHIC collision at \(\sqrt{s}=200\ \text{AGeV}\), and \(e|\vec{B}|/m_\pi^2 \approx 10\text{–}15\) at an even earlier time after an LHC collision at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\ \text{ATeV}\).
- Electrical conductivity used: \(\sigma = 0.023\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to \(C_{\text{em}}^{-1} \sigma / T = 0.3\) at \(T = 255\ \text{MeV}\) (with \(C_{\text{em}} = 0.061\) for 3-flavor QCD).
- Impact parameter for the calculation: \(b = 7\ \text{fm}\), with nuclear radius \(R = 7\ \text{fm}\).
- Beam rapidities: \(Y_0 \simeq 5.4\) for RHIC and \(Y_0 \simeq 8\) for LHC.
- Drag coefficient used: \(\mu m = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^2 / 2\) with \(\lambda = 6\pi\), evaluated at \(T = 1.5 T_c\) where \(T_c \sim 170\ \text{MeV}\).
- The contribution from participant protons changes the final directed flow results by at most 10%, typically much less.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field effects in heavy ion collisions beyond the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) and the chiral magnetic wave (Kharzeev 2010, Burnier 2011) by focusing on the simplest and most direct electromagnetic consequences: Faraday and Hall currents. It provides an analytic estimate of the charge-dependent directed flow \(v_1\) that can be used to calibrate the magnetic field strength from experimental data, complementing earlier numerical studies of the magnetic field decay in a conducting medium (Skokov 2009, Tuchin 2010, Voronyuk 2011, Deng 2012). The results enable experimental searches for correlations \(\langle v_1^\pm(y_1) v_1^\pm(y_2) \rangle\) that isolate the magnetic field signal from other sources of anisotropic flow.
