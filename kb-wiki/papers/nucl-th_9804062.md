# Pseudovector components of the pion, pi^0 -> gamma gamma, and F_pi(q^2)

> As a consequence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude necessarily contains terms proportional to gamma_5 gamma.P and gamma_5 gamma.k, where k is the relative and P the total momentum of the constituents. These terms are essential for the preservation of low energy theorems, such as the Gell-Mann--Oakes-Renner relation and those describing anomalous decays of the pion, and to obtaining an electromagnetic pion form factor that falls as 1/q^2 for large q^2, up to calculable ln(q^2)-corrections. In a simple model, which correlates low- and high-energy pion observables, we find q^2 F_pi(q^2) ~ 0.12 - 0.19 GeV^2 for q^2 >~10 GeV^2.

## Key claim

As a consequence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude necessarily contains pseudovector components (terms proportional to gamma_5 gamma.P and gamma_5 gamma.k) that are essential for preserving low-energy theorems and for obtaining an electromagnetic pion form factor that falls as 1/q^2 at large q^2, up to calculable ln(q^2)-corrections; in a simple model correlating low- and high-energy observables, the authors find q^2 F_pi(q^2) ~ 0.12 - 0.19 GeV^2 for q^2 >~ 10 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors employ the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) framework with a rainbow-ladder truncation to study the pion as a quark-antiquark bound state. They use an algebraic parametrization of the renormalized dressed-quark propagator that incorporates confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and construct a model for the full pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude including pseudoscalar, pseudovector, and pseudotensor components. The electromagnetic pion form factor is calculated in the renormalized impulse approximation using the Ball-Chiu Ansatz for the dressed-quark-photon vertex, which satisfies the vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- q^2 F_pi(q^2) ~ 0.12 - 0.19 GeV^2 for q^2 >~ 10 GeV^2 (model-dependent range from two parameter sets)
- f_pi = 0.092 GeV (calculated, matching experimental value)
- r_pi = 0.55 fm (calculated; experimental value is 0.663 ± 0.006 fm)
- f_pi * r_pi = 0.25 (dimensionless; experimental value is 0.310 ± 0.003)
- m_pi = 0.1387 GeV (calculated; experimental value is 0.1385 GeV)
- Chiral limit leptonic decay constant f_pi^0 = 0.090 GeV
- Euclidean constituent quark mass M^E_{u/d} = 0.32 GeV
- (-<q qbar>^0_{1 GeV})^{1/3} = 0.236 GeV (calculated; estimated value 0.236 ± 0.008 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to pion structure developed by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Roberts (1996), correcting a misapprehension from Delbourgo and Scadron (1979) that only the pseudoscalar component of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is nonzero. The authors show that pseudovector components are crucial for properly realizing anomalous current divergences (pi^0 -> gamma gamma decay) and for obtaining the correct 1/q^2 behavior of the pion form factor at large momentum transfer, in contrast to earlier calculations by Roberts (1996) that neglected these components and obtained q^4 F_pi(q^2) ~ constant. The results enable future studies that include pi-pi rescattering and rho-meson contributions to improve the impulse approximation at intermediate q^2.
