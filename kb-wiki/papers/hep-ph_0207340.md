# Linking generalized parton distributions to constituent quark models

> The link between the nucleon generalized parton distributions and the non-diagonal one-body density matrix in momentum space is studied. Attention is focussed on the region where quark generalized parton distributions (GPD's) describe emission and reabsorption of a single active quark by the target nucleon. The correct covariant connection with wave functions used in any constituent quark model is established. Results obtained with different constituent quark models are presented for the unpolarized quark GPD's.

# Linking generalized parton distributions to constituent quark models

## Key claim

The paper establishes a correct covariant connection between generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and wave functions from constituent quark models, but only in the kinematic region where a single active quark is emitted and reabsorbed by the target nucleon (ξ ≤ x̄ ≤ 1), and only for the valence-quark contribution to GPDs.

## Method

The authors derive a transformation between instant-form (canonical) and front-form (light-cone) wave functions using Melosh rotations, then express the valence-quark contribution to unpolarized GPDs as overlaps of these transformed wave functions. They apply this formalism to two constituent quark models: the relativistic hypercentral quark model and the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) model, calculating the unpolarized GPDs H^q and E^q for u and d quarks.

## Key numbers

- The GBE model predicts anomalous magnetic moments: k^p = 1.20, k^n = -1.06
- The hypercentral model predicts anomalous magnetic moments: k^p = 0.91, k^n = -0.82
- GPDs vanish at x̄ = ξ due to the valence-quark-only approximation
- GPDs vanish beyond x̄ = 1, satisfying the support condition
- The first moments of H^q and E^q satisfy the sum rule relating to nucleon form factors only for ξ = 0 in this approach

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to connect GPDs to quark models, notably the nonrelativistic approach of Scopetta (2001) which suffered from support violations and incorrect sum rules, and the MIT bag model calculations which also had support problems because initial and final nucleons were not good momentum eigenstates. The paper builds on the overlap representation of GPDs developed by Diehl (2000) and Brodsky (2000) and the light-front description of electromagnetic form factors by Pace (2000). The results enable future calculations that include higher Fock states (gluons and antiquarks) to restore the ξ-dependence of the form factor sum rules and extend the kinematic range below x̄ = ξ.
