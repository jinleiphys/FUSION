# Effect of form factors in fits to photoproduction data

> We compare the effects of several form factor recipes in fits to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction data. The specific examples of pion and kaon photoproduction are used to illustrate how different choices can alter the results of such analyses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that using form factors to damp the divergent Born-term contributions in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction does not necessarily bias multipole analyses that fit low partial waves to data, because the high partial waves (which produce the forward peak) remain dominated by the point-like Born contribution unless the form factor introduces a singularity nearer than the pion pole. However, the authors caution that in effective Lagrangian or isobar models covering a larger energy region, the need to cancel the point-like Born contribution could bias extracted resonance parameters.

## Method

The authors compare several form-factor recipes (Ohta's minimal substitution, Haberzettl's form, and a more restrictive form from Davidson and Workman) in the context of charged-pion and kaon photoproduction off protons. They write the amplitude in terms of four gauge-invariant amplitudes and modify the Born terms with a form factor \(F(s,u,t)\) and a constructed factor \(\hat{F}\) that preserves gauge invariance. They then analyze the effect of these form factors on the multipole expansion, particularly the large-\(l\) multipoles, using a series expansion of Legendre functions \(Q_l(z)\) to show that for sufficiently large \(l\) the form-factor-modified multipoles approach the point-like result.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant for pion production is taken as \(G = \sqrt{2} G_{\pi^0 pp}\) with \(G_{\pi^0 pp}^2/(4\pi) = 13.75\).
- The form factor used is \(F_i = \Lambda^4 / [\Lambda^4 + (x - m^2)^2]\) (with appropriate substitutions for \(s\), \(u\), \(t\) channels), where \(\Lambda\) is a cutoff parameter.
- For \(\pi^+ n\) production, the total cross section from point-like pseudoscalar Born terms exceeds the experimental value starting at a photon lab energy \(E_\gamma \approx 450\) MeV.
- For \(l = 2\) multipoles, form factor effects become significant at about \(E_\gamma = 500\) MeV; for higher \(l\), the onset of form factor effects occurs at higher \(E_\gamma\).
- The analysis is restricted to \(E_\gamma < 2\) GeV, the typical range of multipole analyses.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of form factors in photoproduction from earlier studies by Ohta (1976), Haberzettl (1997), and Davidson and Workman (2000), specifically comparing their prescriptions for damping the divergent Born contributions in kaon and pion photoproduction. The paper shows that the high-\(l\) multipoles (e.g., \(l \ge 6\)) needed to reproduce the forward peak in \(\pi^+ n\) differential cross sections at \(E_\gamma = 1002\) MeV are essentially unchanged by form factors, supporting the validity of multipole analyses like the SAID solution. However, it warns that effective Lagrangian approaches (e.g., the Mainz analysis) or isobar models covering larger energy ranges may suffer bias in extracted resonance parameters if point-like Born terms are used without proper treatment of form factors.
