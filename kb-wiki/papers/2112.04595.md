# Vortices in spin-0 superfluids carry magnetic flux

> Vortices in spin-$0$ superfluids generically carry magnetic fields inside their cores, so that even neutral superfluid vortices may be thought of as magnetic flux tubes. We give a systematic analysis of this `vortex magnetic effect' using effective field theory, clarifying earlier literature on the subject. Our analysis shows that in superfluid Helium-$4$ the vortex magnetic effect may be large enough to be experimentally detectable.

## Key claim

Superfluid vortices in spin-0 superfluids generically carry non-zero magnetic flux inside their cores, even though the order parameter is electrically neutral; the effect may be large enough to be experimentally detectable in superfluid Helium-4, though the estimate carries roughly an order-of-magnitude theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors construct a systematic effective field theory (EFT) for a dilute scalar superfluid coupled to electromagnetism, assuming parity and time-reversal invariance, and imposing linearized Lorentz boost invariance. They identify the leading symmetry-allowed interaction term proportional to $\bm{\omega} \cdot \vec{B}$ (coupling fluid vorticity to magnetic field) and compute the resulting magnetic field and flux for a minimal-circulation vortex by solving the classical equations of motion for the condensate profile and applying Ampère's law.

## Key numbers

- For a minimal vortex in superfluid Helium-4, the estimated magnetic flux is $\Phi_{\rm s}/\Phi_0 \approx 1 \times 10^{-10}$, where $\Phi_0 = \pi\hbar/e$ is the superconducting flux quantum; this estimate has roughly $\mathcal{O}(10)$ theoretical uncertainty.
- The general expression for the flux ratio is $\Phi_{\rm s}/\Phi_0 = 7.2 \times 10^{-7} \, (b/A) \, (\aB/a)$, where $b$ is an $O(1)$ EFT coefficient, $A$ is atomic mass number, $\aB$ is the Bohr radius, and $a$ is the atomic charge radius.
- For Helium-4, the particle density is $\bar n \approx 0.022 \, \text{A}^{-3}$, the charge radius $a \approx \aB$, and $\bar{n} a^3 \approx 0.0034$; the diluteness parameter $\gamma = 2\pi n a^3$ is $O(1)$.
- The healing length is $\xi \equiv (4M\mu)^{-1/2} = a (8f_4\bar n\, a^3)^{-1/2}$, and the core size $\zeta$ is proportional to $\xi$ with $g \equiv \xi/\zeta = 0.412(4)$.
- The magnetic field at the vortex center is $2 \mu_0 \hbar \, b e\, a^2 \bar\phi^2/(M \zeta^2)$, and falls as $O(r^{-4})$ at large distance.
- The flux for a single minimal vortex is $\Phi_{\rm s}^{\nu=1} = 2\pi \mu_0 \hbar \, \bar\phi^2\, b\,e\, a^2 / M$, independent of the vortex internal structure at leading EFT order.
- The flexo-electric result gives $\Phi_B = Z\alpha \, \lambda_C \, a^2 \Delta n \, \tfrac{2}{3} \Phi_0$, where $\lambda_C$ is the Compton wavelength and $\Delta n$ is the density depletion at the vortex core.
- The EFT coefficient $b$ equals $Z/6$ when matching the flexo-electric result in the dilute limit.

## Context

This work provides a systematic EFT derivation of the vortex magnetic effect (VME) in scalar superfluids, clarifying and extending earlier phenomenological analyses by Kosevich, Natsik, and others (e.g., Kosevich (year), Natsik (year)). The result for superfluid Helium-4 is two orders of magnitude larger than the earlier prediction of Natsik (year), which assumed the flexoelectric effect arises primarily from van der Waals interactions rather than the non-uniform spatial distribution of individual atoms. The analysis enables experimental searches using SQUIDs, which with a noise of $\sim 45\times 10^{-9} \Phi_0/\sqrt{Hz}$ and measurement times of several days could detect the flux from a single Helium-4 vortex.
