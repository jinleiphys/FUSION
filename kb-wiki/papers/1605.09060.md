# Right-Handed Neutrinos and T-Violating, P-Conserving Interactions

> We show that experimental probes of the P-conserving, T-violating triple correlation in polarized neutron or nuclear $β$-decay provide a unique probe of possible T-violation at the TeV scale in the presence of right-handed neutrinos. In contrast to other possible sources of semileptonic T-violation involving only left-handed neutrinos, those involving right-handed neutrinos are relatively unconstrained by present limits on the permanent electric dipole moments of the electron, neutral atoms, and the neutron. On the other hand, LHC results for $pp\to e+$ missing transverse energy imply that an order of magnitude of improvement in $D$-coefficient sensitivity would be needed for discovery. Finally, we discuss the interplay with the scale of neutrino mass and naturalness considerations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that P-conserving, T-violating (PCTV) interactions involving right-handed neutrinos, mediated by dimension-six four-fermion operators, can generate a measurable D-coefficient in neutron beta decay at the TeV scale without being ruled out by existing electric dipole moment (EDM) limits, unlike operators involving only left-handed neutrinos. However, LHC results for pp → e + missing transverse energy imply that an order-of-magnitude improvement in D-coefficient sensitivity would be needed for discovery.

## Method

The authors construct a set of gauge-invariant dimension-six effective operators containing right-handed neutrinos (scalar operators O1, O2 and tensor operator O3) and compute their tree-level contribution to the D-coefficient in polarized neutron beta decay, which enters quadratically rather than linearly in the Wilson coefficients. They compare the resulting bound on the new physics scale Λ from the D-coefficient with constraints from hadronic EDMs induced at one-loop order, from LHC monojet-like searches for pp → e + MET, and from neutrino mass naturalness considerations. For comparison, they perform a similar analysis for dimension-six operators without right-handed neutrinos, showing that EDM constraints force fine-tuning at the 10⁻¹¹ level.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental limit on the neutron D-coefficient: |D| < 4 × 10⁻⁴ at 90% CL (from Mumm et al. 2011)
- Bound on the combination of Wilson coefficients from the D-coefficient: |Im[(c₁−c₂)c₃*]/Λ⁴| < 3 × 10⁻¹ TeV⁻⁴ at the hadronic scale μ_h ≈ 1 GeV
- Assuming c_i ∼ 1 at the scale Λ, the current D-coefficient bound implies Λ ≳ 1 TeV
- Neutron EDM limit (d_n < 3.0 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm at 90% CL) probes (v/Λ)²c² ∼ 3 × 10⁻⁴, implying Λ ≳ 10 TeV if c(Λ) ∼ 1
- Neutrino mass naturalness bound: for m_ν < 1 eV and m_q ≈ 5 MeV, c_i < 3 × 10⁻⁵ for i = 1,2
- For operators without right-handed neutrinos, the electron EDM bound implies (v/Λ)²|Im C_eB − Im C_eW| < 7.7 × 10⁻¹³, requiring fine-tuning at the 2 × 10⁻¹¹% level to maintain an observable D-coefficient

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory analysis of PCTV observables in beta decay, particularly following Ng et al. (2011) who studied the left-right four-fermion operator and found that neutron EDM limits are three orders of magnitude more stringent than direct D-coefficient bounds. The authors show that including right-handed neutrinos (as in Dirac neutrino scenarios) opens a class of operators that evade this EDM suppression, building on the operator classification of Grzadkowski et al. (2010) and the LHC constraints from Cirigliano et al. (2012). The paper enables future experimental programs: a next-generation D-coefficient measurement with roughly 15 times improved sensitivity could match LHC reach and potentially discover TeV-scale PCTV interactions involving right-handed neutrinos.
