# Patterns of spin and pseudo-spin symmetries in nuclear relativistic mean-field approaches

> The behavior of spin doublets is known to play a major role in nuclear structure and shell effects. Pseudo-spin doublets are also known to impact the single-particle spectrum. The covariant framework, having these two effects encoded in its approach, is an excellent tool to understand the main mechanism driving theses spin and pseudo-spin symmetries and their breaking. A perturbative expansion of the degeneracy raising related to spin and pseudo-spin effects is proposed, up to second order. It allows to understand the main behavior of spin and pseudo-spin energy doublets, such as their A dependence, as well as their common footing and differences. In the case of the spin symmetry, only the lower component of the Dirac bi-spinor is involved, whereas in the case of the pseudo-spin one, both the upper and lower components are involved. Their interplay with the covariant potentials is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a perturbative expansion (up to second order) of spin and pseudo-spin energy splittings in relativistic mean-field theory, using a spin-symmetric relativistic harmonic oscillator as a reference state, to show that the mass-number (A) dependence of spin-orbit splitting scales as A^{-2/3} while pseudo-spin splitting arises from two competing contributions that yield an approximately constant lower-component term and a roughly linear upper-component term.

## Method

The authors construct a perturbative framework starting from a spin-symmetric relativistic harmonic oscillator (SS-RHO) reference state with harmonic Σ potential and constant Δ potential. They then treat the difference between self-consistent Σ and Δ potentials (obtained from Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov calculations with the DD-MEV parametrization) and the reference potentials as a perturbation W, computing energy splittings between symmetry partners at first and second order in perturbation theory. For spin symmetry, the splitting reduces to an integral involving only the lower component f and the Δ potential; for pseudo-spin symmetry, both upper (g) and lower (f) components and both Σ and Δ potentials contribute.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit splitting scaling law from the perturbative analysis yields ΔE ∼ 24.5 (ℓ + 1/2) A^{-2/3}, consistent with the experimental trend from Mairle (1993)
- The optimized reference state parameters converge to c₀ = -73 MeV and d₀ = -588 MeV, within a few MeV regardless of the state considered
- The perturbation parameter W_m is found to be ∼10^{-2} for spin symmetry partners, justifying the perturbative approach
- The norm of the lower component f scales as ω ∼ A^{-1/3}, contributing one factor of A^{-1/3} to the overall A^{-2/3} spin-orbit scaling
- The overlap between Δ' and (F↓ − F↑) contributes an additional A^{-1/3} factor to the spin-orbit scaling

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of pseudo-spin symmetry breaking proposed by Liang (2011), who suggested the SS-RHO as a reference state but did not derive closed-form expressions. The authors provide explicit formulas for both spin and pseudo-spin gaps, recovering the well-known A^{-2/3} scaling of spin-orbit splitting from Bohr and Mottelson (1998) and Mairle (1993) within a fully covariant framework. The analysis of pseudo-spin splitting builds on the work of Ginocchio (1997, 2004) and Alberto (2002), who used a non-perturbative method requiring regularization of divergent integrals; the present perturbative approach yields closed-form expressions that unify the treatment of both symmetries. The results enable systematic understanding of shell evolution far from stability, particularly in neutron-rich nuclei where neutron skins develop large diffusivity (Dobaczewski, 1994), and in bubble nuclei with depleted central density.
