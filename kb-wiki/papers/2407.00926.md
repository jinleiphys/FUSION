# Applying Deep Learning Technique to Chiral Magnetic Wave Search

> The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) is a collective mode in quark-gluon plasma originated from the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect. Its detection in heavy-ion collisions is challenging due to significant background contamination. In Ref.[1], we have constructed a neural network which can accurately identify the CME-related signal from the final-state pion spectra. In this paper, we generalize such a neural network to the case of CMW search. We show that, after a updated training, the neural network can effectively recognize the CMW-related signal. Additionally, we assess the performance of the neural network compared to other known methods for CMW search.

# Applying Deep Learning Technique to Chiral Magnetic Wave Search

## Key claim
A convolutional neural network trained on AMPT-generated data for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV can effectively recognize CMW-related signals from final-state pion spectra, but this performance is achieved by deliberately biasing the training to a single collision energy, trading some generalization capability for enhanced signal detection brightness.

## Method
The authors generate training data using the string-melting AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, encoding CMW-like signals via an initial charge quadrupole scheme where a fraction f of quarks are interchanged based on the charged particle asymmetry A_ch. A VGG-like convolutional neural network with 4 hidden layers, batch normalization, and 10% dropout is trained in a supervised binary classification scheme on averaged pion spectra (20x24 bins in p_T and azimuthal angle) from 100-event samples, using 1.6 million total samples over 250 epochs.

## Key numbers
- Training uses events with switching fraction f = 2% to generate CMW signal comparable to experimental observables
- Pion spectra are binned into 20 by 24 histograms for p_T in range 0-2 GeV and azimuthal angle phi
- Each training sample averages spectra from 100 events; training uses 64 batches per epoch with 100 samples per batch, totaling 1.6 million samples
- For the hypothesis test, the variance sigma of the latent variable f is estimated as approximately 0.025, derived from the condition that average |f| is approximately 2%
- The hypothesis test uses M = 25 events for the mixed-event analysis shown in Figure 5

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior neural-network-based CME detector (Zhao and Huang, 2021) to the case of CMW search, building on the theoretical prediction that CMW induces a charge quadrupole leading to v2(pi-) > v2(pi+) (Burnier et al., 2011). The approach addresses the challenge of strong background contamination that has obscured CMW detection in experimental analyses by STAR (2015, 2022), ALICE (2015, 2023), and CMS (2017). The method enables a hypothesis-test-based experimental analysis where the distribution of neural network predictions serves as an indicator of CMW occurrence, and could be extended to other collision systems like Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru collisions.
