# Polarization Observables in (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) and (${\vec γ},{\vec N}N$) Reactions

> Nucleon recoil polarization in electromagnetic reactions with two-nucleon emission is discussed for both (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) and (${\vec γ},{\vec N}N$). Numerical results are given for exclusive two-nucleon knockout reactions from $^{16}$O in a theoretical model where final-state interactions, one-body and two-body currents, and the effect of correlations in the initial pair wave function are included.

## Key claim

Nucleon recoil polarization observables in (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) and (${\vec \gamma},{\vec N}N$) reactions can provide complementary information to unpolarized cross sections, with the potential to amplify small transition amplitudes through interference with dominant ones, but the experimental separation of individual structure functions is extremely difficult and measurements of polarization components via asymmetries are simpler and less affected by experimental errors.

## Method

The authors calculated polarization observables for exclusive two-nucleon knockout from $^{16}$O using a theoretical model that includes final-state interactions via phenomenological optical potentials, one-body and two-body currents (with seagull, pion-in-flight, and $\Delta$-isobar terms), and short-range correlations (including tensor correlations for $np$ pairs) in the initial pair wave function. The two-nucleon overlaps were obtained from Brueckner $G$-matrix calculations for $pp$ pairs and coupled cluster methods for $np$ pairs, with defect functions from Bonn-A, Reid, and Argonne V14 $NN$ potentials. The formalism yields 36 structure functions for (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) and 16 for (${\vec \gamma},{\vec N}N$) in the most general kinematics, reduced in coplanar or super-parallel geometries.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C$_{\mathrm{g.s.}}$ in super-parallel kinematics: $E_{0} = 855$ MeV, $\omega = 215$ MeV, $q = 316$ MeV/$c$, with $\gamma_1=0^{\circ}$, $\gamma_2=180^{\circ}$
- For $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C$_{\mathrm{g.s.}}$ in coplanar kinematics: $E_{0} = 584$ MeV, $\omega = 212$ MeV, $q = 300$ MeV/$c$, $T'_1 = 137$ MeV, $\gamma_1 = 30^{\circ}$
- For $^{16}$O(${\vec \gamma},{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C$_{\mathrm{g.s.}}$: $E_\gamma = 120$ MeV, $T'_1 = 45$ MeV, $\gamma_1 = 45^{\circ}$
- The $\Delta$ current contribution to cross sections becomes relevant only at recoil momenta beyond 200 MeV/$c$ for the $pp$ case
- Cross sections with Reid potential defect functions are reduced by a factor of about 2 compared to Bonn-A for the same kinematics

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for two-nucleon knockout reactions developed by the same authors (Giusti and Pacati, 1998; 1999) and others (Oxford group) to include polarization observables, building on earlier studies of photon asymmetry in ($\gamma,pp$) and ($\gamma,pn$) by other groups (BGPR; Gent2). The calculations are motivated by planned polarization measurements at MAMI (MAMIpol) and existing high-resolution experiments at NIKHEF and MAMI that have given evidence for short-range correlations in the $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C ground state transition. The results enable a combined experimental study of cross sections and polarization observables to disentangle the contributions of one-body currents (sensitive to correlations) and two-body currents (sensitive to meson-exchange and $\Delta$ degrees of freedom) in both $pp$ and $np$ knockout channels.
