# QRPA plus Phonon Coupling Model and the Photoabsorbtion Cross Section for $^{18,20,22}$O

> We have calculated the electric dipole strength distributions in the unstable neutron rich oxygen isotopes $^{18,20,22}$O, in a model which include up to four quasi-particle-type configurations. The model is the extension, to include the effect of the pairing correlations, of a previous model very successful around closed shell nuclei, and it is based on the quasi-particle-phonon coupling. Low-lying dipole strength is found, which exhausts between 5 and 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) energy-weighted-sum-rule (EWSR) below 15 MeV excitation energy, in rather good agreement with recent experimental data. The role of the phonon coupling is shown to be crucial in order to obtain this result.

## Key claim

The authors calculate electric dipole strength distributions in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes using a QRPA model extended to include phonon coupling, finding that the coupling to complex configurations (4qp-type) is crucial to reproduce the experimentally observed low-lying dipole strength, which exhausts between 5 and 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule below 15 MeV.

## Method

The model starts from Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations with the Skyrme SIII effective force and a constant pairing gap, then solves the QRPA equations on a basis of 2qp configurations. A second subspace Q2 is built from 2qp states coupled to collective vibrations (phonons) of multipolarity 1-, 2+, 3- and 4+ lying below 30 MeV, and the coupling between Q1 and Q2 is treated via the projection operator formalism to obtain complex eigenvalues and the strength function.

## Key numbers

- Low-lying dipole strength exhausts between 5 and 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) below 15 MeV excitation energy in 18,20,22O.
- Pairing gaps used: 0.74 MeV in 18O, 0.79 MeV in 20O, and 0.21 MeV in 22O (with constant pairing strength G = 0.4 MeV).
- For 20O, the QRPA calculation with the small gap gives B(E2) = 15.6 e2fm4 for the low-lying 2+ state, compared to the experimental value of 28 +/- 2 e2fm4.
- The GDR main peak in 18O is calculated at 24.2 MeV, comparing well with the known experimental value.
- The TRK sum rule is enhanced by a factor of 1.33 for the SIII interaction used.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model of Colo et al. (2001) previously successful for closed-shell stable nuclei to neutron-rich systems by including pairing correlations. It directly compares with the experimental data of Aumann et al. (2001) from GSI on the dipole response of oxygen isotopes up to 22O, and with shell-model calculations of Sagawa et al. (2001). The results demonstrate that particle-vibration coupling is even more important in neutron-rich light nuclei than in stable heavy nuclei due to reduced cancellation between diagrams, and the authors plan to apply the model to heavier nuclei relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis where full shell-model calculations become prohibitive.
