# Anomalous spontaneous induction of magnetic and electric fields in dense quark matter

> In this paper, we will demonstrate that a dense quark-matter system in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase behaves as a ferromagnet in the sense that its magnetic-field dependent magnetization remains different from zero even at $B\rightarrow 0$. The corresponding permanent magnetization is a function of the baryonic chemical potential $μ$, decreasing up to zero as $μ$ increases in the range of intermediate densities ($312$ MeV $\leqslant μ\leqslant 342$ MeV) and then increasing from zero in the higher density interval $490$ MeV$\leqslant μ\leqslant 550$ MeV. We will show that this system's ability to generate permanent magnetization, together with the existence of the axial anomaly, open up the possibility of spontaneously generating a magnetic field coupled to a collinear electric field. The generated magnetic field can reach values up to $10^{16}$ G, depending on $μ$, and the electric field will be 3 orders smaller. The fact that the DCDW phase is able to induce a magnetic field can be seen as its spontaneous tendency to remove the so called Landau-Peierls instability that is present in this single-modulated phase in the absence of a magnetic field. The spontaneous induction of a strong magnetic field at intermediate to high densities can be of interest for the astrophysics of compact stellar objects exhibiting strong magnetic fields as magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dense quark matter in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase can spontaneously generate a permanent magnetization that persists even when the external magnetic field is taken to zero, and that this effect, combined with the axial anomaly, can induce a magnetic field coupled to a collinear electric field. The hedge is that this behavior depends on the baryonic chemical potential and only occurs in specific density intervals where the inhomogeneous condensate exists.

## Method

The authors performed a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the thermodynamic potential for the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase up to order N=10, using the weak-field approximation where the magnetic field is smaller than the chemical potential squared. They solved the minimum equations for the condensate amplitude m and modulation b as functions of chemical potential and magnetic field, then computed the magnetization from the derivative of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the magnetic field. The induced magnetic field was found by solving the minimum equation for B using a graphical method.

## Key numbers

- The DCDW phase exists in two density intervals: 312 MeV ≤ μ ≤ 342 MeV (Region 1) and 490 MeV ≤ μ ≤ 550 MeV (Region 3)
- The permanent magnetization M0 shows a discontinuous jump at the critical chemical potential μ0 = 312 MeV, signaling a first-order phase transition
- Induced magnetic field values: B = 1.19 × 10^16 G at μ = 312 MeV; B = 3.19 × 10^14 G at μ = 340 MeV; B = 7.5 × 10^15 G at μ = 530 MeV; B = 1.45 × 10^16 G at μ = 560 MeV
- The induced electric field is 3 orders of magnitude smaller than the magnetic field
- The GL expansion coefficients use cutoff Λ ≈ 636.790 MeV and coupling constant GΛ^2 = 6

## Context

This work extends the study of the MDCDW phase previously developed by Klimenko (2010) and Incera (2015), which showed that a magnetic field removes the Landau-Peierls instability present in single-modulated inhomogeneous phases. The paper contrasts with the real-kink crystal (RKC) and quarkyonic matter phases, which also occur at finite density but do not break time-reversal symmetry and therefore cannot remove the Landau-Peierls instability. The results enable predictions for magnetar astrophysics, where internal magnetic fields of order 10^17 G are expected, and suggest a microscopic mechanism for generating strong magnetic fields that could complement or exceed the dynamo mechanism.
