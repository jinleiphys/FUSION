# Gamow-Teller transitions from high-spin isomers in $N=Z$ nuclei

> Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions from high-spin isomers are studied using the sum-rule approach and the shell model. The GT transition strengths from the high-spin isomeric states show a stronger collectivity than those from the ground states in two $N=Z$ nuclei, $^{52}$Fe and $^{94}$Ag. It is argued that the spin-up and spin-down Fermi spheres involved in the GT transitions from the high-spin isomeric states play important roles. These Fermi spheres are analogous to the isospin-up and isospin-down Fermi spheres for the GT transitions from the ground states in $N>Z$ nuclei and create a strong collectivity.

## Key claim

Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions from high-spin isomeric states in N=Z nuclei exhibit stronger collectivity than those from ground states because the spin-up and spin-down Fermi spheres in the isomeric states are asymmetric, analogous to the isospin-up and isospin-down Fermi spheres that create collectivity in N>Z nuclei; this collectivity is demonstrated for the specific cases of the 12+ isomer in 52Fe and the 21+ isomer in 94Ag, but the authors note that in reality the quantization axis of spin must be defined in the intrinsic frame of deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors derived a new GT sum rule for high-spin isomeric states in N=Z nuclei using the intrinsic frame of deformed nuclei with Nilsson quantum numbers, then applied it to the 4-quasiparticle configuration of the 12+ isomer in 52Fe and the 6-quasiparticle configuration of the 21+ isomer in 94Ag. Shell-model calculations were performed for 52Fe using the GXPF1J effective interaction in the pf-shell model space, and for 94Ag using a modified P1GD5G3 interaction in the (2p1/2, 1g9/2, 1g7/2, 2d5/2) model space with a 76Sr core; the calculated GT strength distributions were compared between the high-spin isomeric states and the 0+ ground states.

## Key numbers

- For 52Fe: the 12+ isomeric state has excitation energy E=6.96 MeV with half-life T1/2=45.9(6) s; the shell-model 12+ state is found at E=6.75 MeV, dominated by 61% of the (f7/2^2)ν(f7/2^2)π configuration.
- For 52Fe: the summed GT transition strengths from the shell-model calculation are 4.47, 3.11, and 1.44 for final states I'=11, 12, and 13 respectively, giving total BGT=9.02, which is 25% smaller than the 4-qp single-j limit value of 12.
- For 52Fe: the GT strength from the ground state (0+) has a summed transition strength of 8.09; the peaks from the HSI state are at Ex=12.0 MeV (I'=11) and 12.2 MeV (I'=12), while the I'=13 peak is at Ex=10.0 MeV.
- For 94Ag: the 21+ isomeric state has excitation energy E=6.67 MeV with half-life T1/2=0.40(4) s; the shell-model 21+ state is predicted at E=6.79 MeV, dominated by 74% of the (g9/2^3)ν(g9/2^3)π configuration.
- For 94Ag: the total BGT from the shell-model calculation is approximately 10.9, corresponding to about 61% of the sum-rule strength of 18 from the 6-qp configuration; transitions to I'=20, 21, and 22 are 49.8%, 35.6%, and 14.7% of the total.
- For 94Ag: 90% of the total GT strength from the HSI state lies within about a 5 MeV energy window, while the energy window is about 7 MeV for transitions from the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the Ikeda sum rule (Ikeda 1963) to high-spin isomeric states in N=Z nuclei, where the standard sum rule gives zero and collectivity is difficult to evaluate. The study is stimulated by an experimental project aiming to measure GT strengths from the 12+ isomer in 52Fe (Yako, experimental proposal). The results enable future experimental measurements of GT transitions from high-spin isomers in unstable N=Z nuclei at radioactive-ion-beam facilities, and provide a framework for understanding collectivity through the new concept of spin Fermi spheres.
