# Accurate shell-model nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We investigate a novel method of accurate calculation of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay shell-model nuclear matrix elements for the experimentally relevant case of $^{76}$Ge. We demonstrate that with the new method the nuclear matrix elements have perfect convergence properties and, using only the first 100 intermediate states of each spin, the matrix elements can be calculated with better than 1% accuracy. Based on the analysis of neutrinoless double-beta decays of $^{48}$Ca, $^{82}$Se, and $^{76}$Ge isotopes, we propose a new method to estimate the optimal values of the average closure energies at which the closure approximation gives the most accurate nuclear matrix elements. We also analyze the nuclear matrix elements for the heavy-neutrino-exchange mechanism, and we show that our method can be used to quench contributions from different intermediate spin states.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their "mixed method" for calculating neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for ⁷⁶Ge achieves better than 1% accuracy using only the first 100 intermediate states of each spin, but they note that remaining uncertainties from short-range correlations, model space restrictions, and effective interactions are not resolved by this method.

## Method

The authors use the shell-model code NuShellX@MSU with the jj44 model space (⁵⁶Ni core plus f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂, and g₉/₂ orbitals) and the JUN45 effective interaction. They apply a "mixed method" where intermediate states below a cutoff parameter N are treated exactly (nonclosure) while states above N are included via the closure approximation, allowing calculation of NMEs for ⁷⁶Ge beyond the closure approximation for the first time in a realistic shell-model approach.

## Key numbers

- Using only the first 100 intermediate states for each spin and parity of ⁷⁶As, the mixed NMEs can be calculated with better than 1% accuracy
- For the light-neutrino-exchange mechanism, the predicted total NME for ⁷⁶Ge is M⁰ν = 3.5 ± 0.1, estimated from CD-Bonn and AV18 short-range correlation parametrizations
- The mixed total NME with CD-Bonn SRC is 3.57, compared to the closure NME of 3.35 (using standard closure energy 9.41 MeV), representing about a 7% increase
- For the heavy-neutrino-exchange mechanism, the NME for ⁷⁶Ge is 202 with CD-Bonn SRC and 126 with AV18 SRC
- The optimal closure energy for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se with the JUN45 interaction is approximately 3.5 MeV, significantly lower than the standard value of 9.41 MeV for Ge
- The J=1⁺ intermediate states contribute about 30% of the total NME, and J=2⁻ states contribute about 15%

## Context

This work extends the mixed method previously tested on ⁴⁸Ca (Sen'kov and Horoi 2013) and ⁸²Se (Sen'kov and Horoi 2014) to the experimentally important case of ⁷⁶Ge, which is the isotope used in the Heidelberg-Moscow, IGEX, and GERDA experiments. The results enable more accurate interpretation of experimental limits and future measurements from GERDA-II and MAJORANA DEMONSTRATOR, and the proposed method for estimating optimal closure energies offers a way to approximate beyond-closure NMEs without calculating intermediate states for other isotopes.
