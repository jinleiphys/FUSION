# Theoretical insights on nuclear double parton distributions

> In this paper, we address double parton scattering (DPS) in pA collisions. Within the Light-Front approach, we formally derive the two contributions to the nuclear double parton distribution (DPD), namely: DPS1, involving two partons from the same nucleon, and DPS2, where the two partons belong to different parent nucleons. We then generalize the sum rule for hadron DPDs to the nuclear case and analytically show how all contributions combine to give the expected results. In addition partial sum rules for the DPDs related to DPS1 and DPS2 mechanisms are discussed for the first time. The deuteron system is considered for the first calculation of the nuclear DPD by using a realistic wave function obtained from the very refined nucleon-nucleon AV18 potential, embedded in a rigorous Poincaré covariant formalism. Results are used to test sum rules and properly verify that DPS1 contribution compares with the DPS2 one, although smaller. We also introduce EMC-like ratios involving nuclear and free DPDs to address the potential role of DPS in understanding in depth the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The paper formally derives the two contributions to nuclear double parton distributions (DPDs) within a Light-Front approach, showing that the DPS1 contribution (two partons from the same nucleon) is not negligible compared to the DPS2 contribution (two partons from different nucleons) in the deuteron, although smaller, and introduces for the first time partial sum rules for these two mechanisms.

## Method

The authors generalize the definition of hadron DPDs to the nuclear case using a Poincare covariant Light-Front formalism, where the nuclear state is factorized into a nuclear wave function describing nucleonic degrees of freedom and an intrinsic partonic part. The bilinear operators in the DPD correlator are decomposed in impulse approximation into an incoherent sum over nucleon operators, yielding the DPS1 term (operators acting on the same nucleon, giving a factor A) and the DPS2 term (operators acting on different nucleons, giving a factor A(A-1)). For the deuteron, a realistic wave function from the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential is used, embedded in the Light-Front framework.

## Key numbers

- The DPS1 cross-section scales as approximately A times the proton-proton DPS cross-section, while the DPS2 contribution scales approximately as A^(4/3) times the proton-proton DPS cross-section.
- The deuteron is used as the first test case for nuclear DPD calculations, employing the AV18 potential wave function.
- The normalization condition for the nuclear Light-Front wave function is given by an integral over all nucleon variables yielding 1.
- The one-body light-cone momentum distribution for a nucleus with A nucleons is normalized such that the sum over nucleon types of the integral over longitudinal momentum fraction equals 1.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for double parton scattering in nuclear collisions first established by Strikman (2001) and Blok (2012), who identified the DPS1 and DPS2 mechanisms. The Light-Front covariant formalism used here builds on previous work by Keister and Polyzou, Lev, and Del Dotto for nuclear systems. The results enable future studies of double parton scattering in electromagnetic processes at Jefferson Lab and upcoming Electron-Ion Colliders, and the many-body nuclear form factors relevant to DPS2 also play a role in processes such as J/psi diffractive photoproduction as studied by Guzey (2022).
