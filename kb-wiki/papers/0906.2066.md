# Pion electro-production in the Roper region in chiral quark models

> We present a method to calculate pion electro-production amplitudes in a coupled-channel framework incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states. The method offers a clear prescription how to extract the resonant part of the amplitudes, even in the presence of different decay channels and a strong mixing of neighbouring resonances. The method is applied to the calculation of the M1- and the S1- amplitudes in the P11 partial wave in a simple chiral quark model. A good agreement with the observed M1- amplitude is found with a significant contribution from the pion cloud. The same effect is also prominent in the S1- amplitude but a rather uncertain data prevent us to draw a definitive conclusion.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to calculate pion electro-production amplitudes in a coupled-channel framework incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states, claiming good agreement with the observed M1- amplitude when including a significant contribution from the pion cloud, but note that uncertain data prevent a definitive conclusion for the S1- amplitude.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed K-matrix approach for pion scattering to electro-production by incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states (from the Cloudy Bag Model) into a coupled-channel framework that includes πN, πΔ, and σN channels. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the K matrix to extract resonant and background parts of the amplitudes, then compute the M1- and S1- multipoles in the P11 partial wave at Q²=0 and for the helicity amplitudes as a function of Q².

## Key numbers

- Bag radius R = 0.83 fm used as the primary parameter, with R = 1.00 fm tested for comparison
- Pion decay constant f = 76 MeV adopted to reproduce the πNN coupling constant
- ω-meson coupling constant g_{ω1} = 8 used for the phenomenological ω-meson contribution
- g_{γπω} = 0.374 √(4π/137) used for the ω-meson electromagnetic coupling
- Helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^p evaluated at the K-matrix pole W = 1530 MeV
- Zero crossing of A_{1/2}^p occurs at a Q² value higher than experiment; using R ~ 1 fm brings the zero-crossing Q² into acceptable range
- The bare quark core contribution to A_{1/2}^p at the photon point is almost negligible, with the value dominated by pion cloud effects

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel calculation of pion scattering in the Roper region (Golli et al., 2008) to electro-production amplitudes, representing the first attempt to apply a quark model to calculate full electro-production amplitudes with strong background and open inelastic channels. The results are compared to the phenomenological MAID model and SAID partial-wave analysis, and the helicity amplitudes are compared to calculations by Tiator (1988) and Dong using different quark-pion coupling models, as well as recent SL model results by Julia-Diaz (2009). The method enables future extraction of resonant electro-production amplitudes from quark models in the presence of multiple decay channels and resonance mixing.
