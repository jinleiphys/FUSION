# Fourier Harmonics of High-pT Particles Probing the Fluctuating Intitial Condition Geometries in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Second Fourier harmonics of jet quenching have been thoroughly explored in the literature and shown to be sensitive to the underlying jet path-length dependence of energy loss and the differences between the mean eccentricity predicted by Glauber and CGC/KLN models of initial conditions. We compute the jet path-length dependence of energy-loss for higher azimuthal harmonics of jet-fragments in a generalized model of energy-loss for RHIC energies and find, however, that even the high-$p_T$ second moment is most sensitive to the poorly known early-time evolution during the first fm/c. Moreover, we demonstrate that higher-jet harmonics are remarkably insensitive to the initial conditions, while the different $v_n(N_{part})$ vs. $v_n^{I_{AA}}(N_{part})$ correlations between the moments of monojet and dijet nuclear modifications factors remain a most sensitive probe to differentiate between Glauber and CGC/KLN initial state sQGP geometries.

# Fourier Harmonics of High-pT Particles Probing the Fluctuating Initial Condition Geometries in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Even the high-pT second Fourier harmonic (v2) of jet quenching is most sensitive to the poorly known early-time evolution during the first fm/c, not primarily to the differences between Glauber and CGC/KLN initial conditions; higher jet harmonics (v3, v4) are remarkably insensitive to initial conditions, while correlations between monojet and dijet nuclear modification factors remain a sensitive probe to differentiate between Glauber and CGC/KLN geometries.

## Method

The authors compute the jet path-length dependence of energy loss for higher azimuthal harmonics of jet fragments using a generalized energy-loss model dE/dx = -κ P^a τ^z T^(z-a+2) at RHIC energies, where parameters a and z control the jet energy and path-length dependence respectively. They consider both pQCD-like (z=1, a=1/3) and AdS/CFT-like (z=2, a=1/3) energy-loss mechanisms, apply them to Glauber and CGC/KLN initial conditions with 1D Bjorken expansion, and compute R_AA, v_n, and I_AA for monojets and dijets as functions of N_part. The analysis includes both event-by-event fluctuations and averaged initial conditions, with κ fitted to the most central R_AA data point from RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The spectral index n is taken to be approximately 6 for the jet production spectrum.
- For the Bethe-Heitler limit, a=1 and z=0; for deep LPM pQCD, a~0 and z~1; for the model referred to as "AdS/CFT" in prior work, a=0 and z=2; on-shell AdS/CFT calculations give a=1/3 and z=2.
- The initial time τ0 is varied between 0.01 fm and 1 fm, with τ0=1 fm in line with recent hydrodynamic calculations.
- The analysis considers an impact parameter of b=8 fm that maximizes v_n.
- RHIC data for v2 at N_part=125.7 are taken from Adare (2010) for comparison.
- The coupling κ is fitted to the most central R_AA data point at RHIC for final energies E_f ~ 6-9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jia (2008) and Adare (2010) who first attempted to disentangle initial conditions (Glauber vs. CGC/KLN) and energy-loss mechanisms (pQCD vs. AdS/CFT) using R_AA(N_part) and v_2(N_part) for high-pT particles. The authors show that the conclusion of Adare (2010), that only CGC/KLN initial conditions with AdS/CFT energy loss can describe both observables, cannot be sustained when τ0=1 fm, as both pQCD-like and AdS/CFT-like energy loss reproduce RHIC data with CGC/KLN initial conditions. The work also extends the dijet analysis of Horojia (2010) and mentions that higher harmonics were discussed but not shown explicitly by Fries (2010). The results enable future measurements at LHC energies and suggest that simultaneous measurement of v2, v3, and v4 could elucidate the microscopic mechanism of jet-energy loss.
