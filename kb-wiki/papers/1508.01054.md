# Correlation functions and correlation widths in Quantum-Chaotic Scattering for mesoscopic systems and nuclei

> We derive analytical expressions for the correlation functions of the electronic conductance fluctuations of an open quantum dot under several conditions. Both the variation of energy and that of an external parameter such as an applied perpendicular or parallel magnetic fields are considered in the general case of partial openness.. These expressions are then used to obtain the ensemble averaged density of maxima, a measure recently suggested to contain invaluable information concerning the chaoticity of the system. The correlation width is then calculated for the case of energy variation and a significant deviation from the Weisskopf estimate is found in the case of two terminals. The results are extended to more than two terminals. All our results are analytical.The use of these results in other fields, such as nuclei, where the system can only be studied through a variation of the energy, is then discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for correlation functions of conductance fluctuations in open quantum dots, finding that the correlation width deviates significantly from the Weisskopf estimate (by up to about 19%) for partially open systems, with the deviation disappearing only in the limits of completely open or completely closed extra terminals.

## Method

The authors employ the random matrix theory (RMT)-based stub model to analytically calculate ensemble averages of products of four scattering matrix elements, obtaining correlation functions for energy variation and for three types of external magnetic field (perpendicular, Rashba-Dresselhaus, and parallel). They extract the correlation width from these functions and compare it to the Weisskopf estimate, extending the calculation from two terminals to systems with three or more terminals where extra terminals are lumped into an effective terminal characterized by a transmission coefficient T₃.

## Key numbers

- For a two-terminal system with equal transmission coefficients T₁ = T₂ = T, the ratio D = Γ_corr/Γ_W = √[(5T² - 10T + 6)/(9T² - 18T + 10)]; for almost closed systems (T << 1), D = √(3/5) = 0.81
- For the parallel magnetic field case, the average density of maxima ⟨ρ_H∥⟩ = √3/(π Γ_H∥) ≈ 0.55/Γ_H∥, independent of the openness parameter
- For the energy variation case, the average density of maxima ⟨ρ_E⟩ = (√3)/(π Γ_E) √[(9T² - 18T + 10)/(5T² - 10T + 6)]
- Numerical simulation used 50 open channels and 5 × 10³ resonances over an energy range Δε/γ ∈ [-100, 100]; the number of maxima increased from 99 to 101 when an extra terminal with T₃ = 0.3 was added

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of correlation functions in quantum chaotic scattering beyond the classic results of Ericson (1960) for energy variation and the square-Lorentzian shapes found by Efetov and Vallejos for parametric variation. It confirms and generalizes the numerical findings of Richter, who observed deviations of the correlation width from the Weisskopf estimate in microwave resonators. The results enable quantitative predictions for dwell time modifications in mesoscopic systems, graphene flakes, and compound nuclei, and have potential applications in artificial atomic clocks and cryptography based on transport observables.
