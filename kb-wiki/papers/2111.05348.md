# Universality (beyond leading log) of soft radiative corrections to $\hat q$ in $p_\perp$ broadening and energy loss

> It has been known for many years that soft radiation can give potentially large double-logarithm corrections to $p_\perp$ broadening of a high-energy particle traveling through QCD matter, but that this soft radiation correction can be absorbed into an effective value $\hat q_{\rm eff}$ for the medium $p_\perp$-broadening parameter $\hat q$. Here "soft" means high energy compared to medium scales but soft compared to the original high-energy particle traveling through the medium. A similar situation arises in the case of soft corrections to hard splitting of a high-energy particle, such as hard $g{\to}gg$, where double logarithms can also be absorbed using the same effective $\hat q_{\rm eff}$. In this paper, I study whether the same holds true for potentially large, subleading, *single*-logarthim corrections. The correspondence is more indirect for single logarithms, but I show (in the large-$N_{\rm c}$ limit) that single logarithms from soft radiation in the case of $p_\perp$ broadening also determine the single logarithms from soft radiative corrections to hard $g{\to}gg$ splitting. Along the way, there is an interesting variation of the original BDMPS-Z calculation of splitting rates in the $\hat q$ approximation. I also discuss how, for soft-radiative corrections to hard splitting processes, there are two different types of "$\hat q_{\rm eff}$" that come into play, which differ by "$iπ$" terms that multiply single logarithms.

## Key claim

The paper shows that subleading single-logarithm corrections from soft radiation are universal between $p_\perp$ broadening and hard $g{\to}gg$ splitting in QCD matter, but only in the large-$N_{\rm c}$ limit and for the portion of phase space where the $\hat q$ approximation remains valid at all boundaries.

## Method

The author re-derives the BDMPS-Z calculation of the hard $g{\to}gg$ splitting rate in a more general way that incorporates the LMW result for soft radiative corrections to $p_\perp$ broadening, then verifies that the resulting single-log corrections match those extracted from more laborious generic-$y$ calculations of $g \to ggg$ in ref. [logs]. The analysis is restricted to the red region of the double-log parameter space where the $\hat q$ approximation can be used at all boundaries, and the author also finds an algebraic rewriting of the single-log correction formula that directly connects to the LMW result.

## Key numbers

- The double-log correction to $\hat q$ from soft radiation is $\delta \hat q = \frac{C_A \alpha_s}{2\pi} \ln^2(L/\tau_0) \, \hat q$, where $\tau_0$ is the mean-free path for small-angle elastic scattering
- The single-log coefficient for soft corrections to hard $g{\to}gg$ splitting is $\bar s(x) = -\ln(16\,x(1-x)(1-x+x^2)) + 2\frac{[x^2(\ln x - \pi/8) + (1-x)^2(\ln(1-x) - \pi/8)]}{(1-x+x^2)}$
- The integrated single-log correction to the splitting rate takes the form $\delta[d\Gamma/dx] = \frac{C_A \alpha_s}{4\pi} [d\Gamma/dx]_{\rm LO} [\frac12 \ln^2 y_{\rm cut} + \bar s(x) \ln y_{\rm cut}]$

## Context

This work extends the leading-log universality found by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (LMW) for soft radiative corrections to $\hat q$ in $p_\perp$ broadening, and the leading-log results of Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu for in-medium splitting rates. The paper provides a non-trivial cross-check of the more general double-splitting $g \to gg \to ggg$ calculations in refs. [2brem, seq, dimreg, QEDnf, qcd], and enables future applications where the single-log structure of soft radiative corrections to $\hat q$ can be used directly in energy loss calculations.
