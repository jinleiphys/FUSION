# The Form of the Effective Interaction in Harmonic-Oscillator-Based Effective Theory

> I explore the form of the effective interaction in harmonic-oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET) in next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). As the included space in a HOBET (as in the shell model) is defined by the oscillator energy, both long-distance (low-momentum) and short-distance (high-momentum) degrees of freedom reside in the high-energy excluded space. A HOBET effective interaction is developed in which a short-range contact-gradient expansion is combined with an exact summation of the relative kinetic energy. By this means the very strong coupling of the included (P) and excluded (Q) spaces by the kinetic energy is removed. One finds that the interplay of QT and QV is governed by a single parameter kappa, the ratio of an observable, the binding energy |E|, to a parameter in the effective theory, the oscillator energy. Once the functional dependence on kappa is identified, the remaining order-by-order subtraction of the short-range physics residing in Q becomes systematic and rapidly converging. Numerical calculations are used to demonstrate how well the resulting expansion reproduces the running of Heff from high scales to a typical shell-model scale of 8 hbar omega. At N3LO various global properties of Heff are reproduced to a typical accuracy of 0.01%, or about 1 keV. The state-dependence of the effective interaction has been a troubling problem in nuclear physics, and is embodied in the energy dependence of Heff(|E|) in the Bloch-Horowitz formalism. It is shown that almost all of this state dependence is also extracted in the procedures followed here, isolated in the analytic dependence of Heff on kappa. Thus there exists a simple, Hermitian Heff that can be use in spectral calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the effective interaction in harmonic-oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET) can be systematically expanded using a short-range contact-gradient expansion combined with an exact summation of the relative kinetic energy, with the interplay of long- and short-range physics governed by a single parameter κ (the ratio of binding energy to oscillator energy), and that at N3LO this expansion reproduces global properties of the effective interaction to about 0.01% accuracy (approximately 1 keV) at a typical shell-model scale of 8 ħω, with the caveat that this success depends on the prior removal of the strong coupling between included (P) and excluded (Q) spaces by the kinetic energy operator.

## Method

The author solves the Bloch-Horowitz equation for the deuteron using the Argonne v18 potential, generating effective interaction matrix elements in a harmonic-oscillator basis as the included space (P) is varied from high scales down to 8 ħω. The Bloch-Horowitz equation is reorganized to sum the relative kinetic energy operator QT to all orders analytically, isolating the short-range contributions from V(E-QH)^{-1}QV which are then expanded in a contact-gradient expansion appropriate to the harmonic-oscillator basis (using operators redefined as Ō = e^{r²/2} O e^{r²/2} to eliminate operator mixing). The coefficients of the contact-gradient expansion are determined from the lowest-energy matrix elements and used to predict the remaining matrix elements, with the running of Heff(Λ) tracked as excitations in Q are progressively integrated out from high scales down to ΛP.

## Key numbers

- At N3LO, global properties of Heff are reproduced to a typical accuracy of 0.01%, or about 1 keV, at a shell-model scale of 8 ħω.
- For typical matrix elements, Δ(ΛP) = Heff - H is approximately -12 MeV for the ³S₁ channel.
- The parameter κ is defined as κ = √(2|E|/ħω), where |E| is the binding energy and ħω is the oscillator energy.
- In the contact-gradient expansion, the gradient operators acting on harmonic-oscillator states yield ∇² ∼ -4(n-1) and ∇⁴ ∼ 16(n-1)(n-2) for nodal quantum number n.
- For nonedge-state matrix elements, the scale at which residuals exceed 10 keV is brought down successively: from ∼100 ħω (LO), to ∼60 ħω (LO fit), to ∼30 ħω (NLO), to ∼20 ħω (NNLO).

## Context

This work extends the HOBET approach developed in earlier studies by Song, Luu, and collaborators (2004-2006) that examined the evolution of effective interaction matrix elements for the deuteron and ³He/³H systems. It addresses the long-standing problem of state dependence in effective interactions, which in the Bloch-Horowitz formalism appears as energy dependence of Heff(|E|); the paper shows this state dependence is largely isolated in the analytic dependence on κ. The approach enables future efforts to determine the HOBET interaction directly from experiment rather than from an underlying NN potential, and provides a systematic operator expansion analogous to the contact-gradient expansions used in effective field theories (Weinberg, 1990; Savage, 1999).
