# Low-energy Dipole Excitations in Nuclei at the N=50,82 and Z=50 Shell Closures as Signatures for a Neutron Skin

> Low-energy dipole excitations have been investigated theoretically in N=50, several N=82 isotones and the Z=50 Sn isotopes. For this purpose a method incorporating both HFB and multi-phonon QPM theory is applied. A concentration of one-phonon dipole strength located below the neutron emission threshold has been calculated in these nuclei. The analysis of the corresponding neutron and proton dipole transition densities allows to assign a genuine pattern to the low-energy excitations and making them distinct from the conventional GDR modes. Analyzing also the QRPA wave functions of the states we can identify these excitations as Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) modes, recently studied also in Sn and N=82 nuclei. The results for N=50 are exploratory for an experimental project designed for the bremsstrahlung facility at the ELBE accelerator.

## Key claim

Low-energy dipole excitations in nuclei near the N=50, N=82, and Z=50 shell closures can be identified as Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) modes, and their total strength is closely correlated with the neutron skin thickness defined by the relative difference of neutron and proton root-mean-square radii; however, in the most exotic neutron-deficient Sn isotopes (100-104Sn), the lowest dipole states are of almost pure proton structure, indicating a proton-driven PDR.

## Method

The authors combined Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov (HFB) calculations of nuclear ground states with the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) to compute excited states. The QPM used a separable residual interaction with empirical isoscalar and isovector coupling constants, and excited states were constructed from Quasiparticle-Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) phonons. The one-body transition densities of the resulting one-phonon dipole states were analyzed to distinguish PDR modes from conventional Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) modes based on the spatial pattern of neutron and proton contributions.

## Key numbers

- In N=50 nuclei (88Sr, 90Zr), low-energy dipole states were identified at excitation energies E* ≤ 9 MeV.
- In N=82 isotones and Z=50 Sn isotopes, low-energy dipole states were identified at E* ≤ 8 MeV.
- States in the region E* = 8-8.5 MeV in Z=50 and N=82 nuclei, and E* = 9-9.5 MeV in N=50 nuclei, were found to be compatible with the low-energy part of the GDR.
- In 100Sn, a state at E* = 8.3 MeV with a proton structure was found, indicating a possible proton PDR.
- The total PDR strength was compared to the nuclear skin thickness δr = sqrt(<r_n^2>) - sqrt(<r_p^2>) as a function of mass number for N=50, N=82, and Z=50 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in Sn and N=82 nuclei (Adrich 2005, Volz 2006) by including N=50 isotones and providing a systematic comparison across three shell closures. The results for N=50 are exploratory for planned experiments at the ELBE bremsstrahlung facility, and the findings for Sn isotopes and N=82 nuclei agree with available data from Volz (2006), Gov, and Ozel. The predicted proton-driven PDR in 100-104Sn suggests a new phenomenon in heavy nuclei with N slightly larger than or equal to Z, attributed to Coulomb repulsion pushing weakly bound proton orbitals into the nuclear surface.
