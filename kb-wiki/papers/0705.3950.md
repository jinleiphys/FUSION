# The role of $N^*(1535)$ in $pp \to pp ϕ$ and $π^- p \to n ϕ$ reactions

> The near threshold $ϕ$ meson production in proton-proton and $π^- p$ collisions is studied with the assumption that the production mechanism is due to the sub-$Nϕ$-threshold $N^*(1535)$ resonance. The $π^0$, $η$ and $ρ^0$-meson exchanges for proton-proton collisions are considered. It is shown that the contribution to the $pp \to pp ϕ$ reaction from the t-channel $π^0$ meson exchange is dominant. With a significant $N^*(1535)Nϕ$ coupling ($g^2_{N^*(1535)N ϕ}/4 π$ = 0.13), both $pp \to pp ϕ$ and $π^- p \to n ϕ$ data are very well reproduced. The significant coupling of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance to $N ϕ$ is compatible with previous indications of a large $s \bar{s}$ component in the quark wave function of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance and may be the real origin of the significant enhancement of the $ϕ$ production over the naive OZI-rule predictions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the near-threshold production of the φ meson in both pp → ppφ and π⁻p → nφ reactions is dominated by the intermediate excitation of the sub-Nφ-threshold N*(1535) resonance, rather than by t-channel ρ exchange or nucleon-pole contributions assumed in prior work. The authors claim that a coupling constant of g²_{N*(1535)Nφ}/4π = 0.13 reproduces both sets of data, but this value is extracted from a fit to the π⁻p → nφ data and then applied without further adjustment to the pp → ppφ reaction.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with meson-exchange mechanisms (π⁰, η, ρ⁰) for the pp → ppφ reaction and an s-channel N*(1535) resonance diagram for the π⁻p → nφ reaction. Coupling constants for N*(1535)Nπ, N*(1535)Nη, and N*(1535)Nρ are determined from the experimental partial decay widths of the N*(1535) resonance, while the N*(1535)Nφ coupling is extracted by fitting the total cross section of π⁻p → nφ. For pp → ppφ, the authors include a Jost-function-based final-state interaction (FSI) enhancement factor for the ¹S₀ di-proton state at low excess energies.

## Key numbers

- g²_{N*(1535)Nφ}/4π = 0.13, extracted from fitting the π⁻p → nφ total cross section data.
- g²_{N*(1535)Nπ}/4π = 0.033, from the adopted branching ratio of 0.45 for N*(1535) → Nπ.
- g²_{N*(1535)Nη}/4π = 0.28, from the adopted branching ratio of 0.53 for N*(1535) → Nη.
- g²_{N*(1535)Nρ}/4π = 0.10, from the adopted branching ratio of 0.02 for N*(1535) → Nρ → Nππ.
- g²_{πNN}/4π = 14.4, g²_{ρNN}/4π = 0.9, and κ = 6.1, taken from the Bonn potential model.
- Cutoff parameters: Λ_π = Λ_η = 1.3 GeV, Λ_ρ = 1.6 GeV for meson-nucleon form factors; Λ*_π = Λ*_η = Λ*_ρ = 1.3 GeV for N*(1535)-nucleon-meson form factors.
- For the pp FSI, scattering parameters α = -20.5 MeV/c and β = 166.7 MeV/c (corresponding to a = -7.82 fm and r = 2.79 fm).
- The π⁰ meson exchange contribution to pp → ppφ is dominant; the η exchange contribution is negligible.

## Context

This work extends the study of φ meson production in nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon collisions, which had previously been modeled via t-channel ρ exchange and sub-threshold nucleon pole contributions (Sibirtsev et al. 2006, Titov et al., Nakayama, Kaptari). The authors argue that the N*(1535) resonance, which has been shown to couple strongly to ηN, KΛ, and η'N channels (Liu et al., Mosel, Saghai, Oset, Dugger), provides a more natural explanation for the observed enhancement of φ production over naive OZI-rule predictions. The significant N*(1535)Nφ coupling found here supports the hypothesis of a large s‾s component in the N*(1535) quark wave function and suggests that extracting nucleon strangeness properties from these reactions (as proposed by Ellis et al.) may be more difficult than previously thought. The work also notes that an Nφ bound state (suggested by Gao and Zhang) would produce a sharper threshold structure not seen in the data.
