# N-Jettiness Subtractions for $gg\to H$ at Subleading Power

> $N$-jettiness subtractions provide a general approach for performing fully-differential next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculations. Since they are based on the physical resolution variable $N$-jettiness, $\mathcal{T}_N$, subleading power corrections in $τ=\mathcal{T}_N/Q$, with $Q$ a hard interaction scale, can also be systematically computed. We study the structure of power corrections for $0$-jettiness, $\mathcal{T}_0$, for the $gg\to H$ process. Using the soft-collinear effective theory we analytically compute the leading power corrections $α_s τ\lnτ$ and $α_s^2 τ\ln^3τ$ (finding partial agreement with a previous result in the literature), and perform a detailed numerical study of the power corrections in the $gg$, $gq$, and $q\bar q$ channels. This includes a numerical extraction of the $α_sτ$ and $α_s^2 τ\ln^2τ$ corrections, and a study of the dependence on the $\mathcal{T}_0$ definition. Including such power suppressed logarithms significantly reduces the size of missing power corrections, and hence improves the numerical efficiency of the subtraction method. Having a more detailed understanding of the power corrections for both $q\bar q$ and $gg$ initiated processes also provides insight into their universality, and hence their behavior in more complicated processes where they have not yet been analytically calculated.

## Key claim

The paper analytically computes the leading logarithmic (LL) power corrections at NLO and NNLO for 0-jettiness subtractions in gluon-fusion Higgs production, finding partial disagreement with a prior result for the gg channel, and demonstrates numerically that including these subleading-power logarithms significantly reduces the size of missing power corrections, thereby improving the numerical efficiency of the subtraction method.

## Method

Using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), the authors systematically organize all sources of power corrections to the 0-jettiness distribution for gg → H, including subleading Lagrangian insertions, subleading hard-scattering operators, and power corrections to the measurement function. They analytically compute the leading-logarithmic (LL) coefficients at NLO and NNLO by exploiting consistency relations that relate the LL terms to collinear or hard-collinear coefficients, and they extract the next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) corrections numerically from full NLO H+1-jet results obtained with MCFM8.

## Key numbers

*   The NLO LL power correction coefficient for the gg channel is (8CA - 8 nf TF) ln τ, where CA = 3, TF = 1/2, and nf is the number of light flavors.
*   The NNLO LL power correction coefficient for the gg channel is [-32 CA^2 + 16 nf TF (CF + CA)] ln^3 τ, with CF = 4/3.
*   The NLO LL power correction for the gq channel is 8 CF ln τ.
*   The NNLO LL power correction for the gq channel is -16 CF (CF + CA) ln^3 τ.
*   The NLO LL power correction for the qqbar channel is 0; the first nonzero contribution is NLL at NLO, with coefficient -8 CF ln τ.

## Context

This work extends the study of subleading-power corrections for N-jettiness subtractions from Drell-Yan processes (Moult et al., 2016; Boughezal et al., 2016) to gluon-fusion Higgs production, providing the first detailed numerical study of the partonic channel dependence and the importance of NLL power-suppressed terms. The analytic LL results for the gg and gq channels partially disagree with those of Boughezal et al. (2016), and the paper finds that the LL approximation works well for the gg channel but is unusually small for the gq channel compared to its NLL correction. The improved understanding of power corrections enables more numerically efficient NNLO calculations for Higgs production and provides insight into the universality of subleading-power structures for more complicated processes.
