# Conserved and non-conserved Noether currents from the quantum effective action

> The quantum effective action yields equations of motion and correlation functions including all quantum corrections. We discuss here how it encodes also Noether currents at the full quantum level. Interestingly, the construction can be generalized beyond the standard symmetry transformations that leave the action invariant. We also discuss an extended set of transformations, which change the action by a term that is locally known on the level of the quantum effective action. Associated to such extended gauge transformations are currents for which we obtain a divergence-type equation of motion, but they are not conserved. We call them non-conserved Noether currents. We discuss in particular symmetries and extended transformations associated to space-time geometry for relativistic quantum field theories. These encompass local dilatations or Weyl gauge transformation, local Lorentz transformations and local shear transformations. Together they constitute the symmetry group of the frame bundle GL$(d)$. The corresponding non-conserved Noether currents are the dilatation or Weyl current, the spin current and the shear current. In particular for the latter we obtain a new divergence-type equations of motion.

## Key claim

The quantum effective action encodes Noether currents at the full quantum level, and the construction can be generalized to "extended symmetry transformations" that change the action by a locally known term, yielding currents with divergence-type equations of motion that are not conserved; the paper argues that these non-conserved Noether currents, specifically the dilatation or Weyl current, the spin current, and the shear current, are useful for describing fluid dynamics and arise from quantum fluctuations even when absent classically.

## Method

The authors introduce external gauge fields that render symmetry transformations local, then take functional derivatives of the quantum effective action with respect to these gauge fields to obtain expectation values and correlation functions of Noether currents. They extend this procedure to transformations where the action changes by a term linear in composite operators coupled to external sources, deriving divergence-type equations for the associated non-conserved currents. For space-time symmetries, they work with a general affine connection including torsion and non-metricity, and study local dilatations (Weyl gauge transformations), local Lorentz transformations, and local shear transformations as examples of extended symmetries.

## Context

This work extends the concept of extended symmetries introduced by Canet (2014), Tarpin (2017), and Tarpin (2018) from transformations linear in fields to transformations linear in composite operators coupled to external sources. It generalizes the standard Noether construction for the quantum effective action to obtain non-conserved currents, the dilatation current, spin current, and shear current, which together form the hypermomentum tensor studied by Hehl (1976) and others in the context of modified gravity. The authors argue these currents are relevant for relativistic fluid dynamics, particularly for Israel-Stewart-type theories and for describing spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions as measured by the STAR Collaboration (2017), and they enable a first-principles derivation of divergence-type equations for shear stress and bulk viscous pressure from the quantum effective action.
