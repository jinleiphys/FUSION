# Theoretical description of deeply virtual Compton scattering off $^3$He

> Recently, coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off $^3$He nuclei has been proposed to access the neutron generalized parton distributions (GPDs). In Impulse Approximation (IA) studies, it has been shown, in particular, that the sum of the two leading twist, quark helicity conserving GPDs of $^3$He, $H$ and $E$, at low momentum transfer, is dominated by the neutron contribution, so that $^3$He is very promising for the extraction of the neutron information. Nevertheless, such an extraction could be not trivial. A technique, able to take into account the nuclear effects included in the IA analysis in the extraction procedure, has been therefore developed. In this work, the IA calculation of the spin dependent GPD $\tilde H$ of $^3$He is presented for the first time. This quantity is found to be largely dominated, at low momentum transfer, by the neutron contribution, which could be extracted using arguments similar to the ones previously proposed for the other GPDs. The known forward limit of the IA calculation of $\tilde H$, yielding the polarized parton distributions of $^3$He, is correctly recovered. The knowledge of the GPDs $H, E$ and $\tilde H$ of $^3$He will allow now the evaluation of the cross section asymmetries which are relevant for coherent DVCS off $^3$He at Jefferson Lab kinematics, an important step towards the planning of possible experiments.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent generalized parton distribution (GPD) \(\tilde H\) of \(^3\)He, calculated for the first time in Impulse Approximation (IA), is largely dominated by the neutron contribution at low momentum transfer, but only for values of the momentum transfer squared \(-\Delta^2 \le 0.15\) GeV\(^2\) and for the Bjorken-like variable \(x \le 0.7\); the extraction of the neutron GPD from \(^3\)He data requires a specific procedure using axial point-like form factors to account for nuclear effects.

## Method

The authors performed an Impulse Approximation (IA) calculation of the spin-dependent GPD \(\tilde H\) of \(^3\)He, using a convolution-like equation that relates the nuclear GPD to the corresponding nucleon GPDs via spin-dependent, one-body off-diagonal spectral functions. The numerical evaluation used overlap integrals from the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential wave functions, and a simple model for the nucleonic GPDs \(\tilde H^{N}_q\) and \(\tilde G_M^{N,q}\) extended to spin-dependent quantities. The forward limit of the calculation was verified to reproduce the known polarized parton distributions of \(^3\)He.

## Key numbers

* The neutron effective polarization in \(^3\)He is \(p_n = 0.878\) and the proton effective polarization is \(p_p = -0.023\), calculated with the AV18 potential.
* The calculation is valid for momentum transfer values \(-\Delta^2 \le 0.15\) GeV\(^2\), which are relevant for coherent DVCS off \(^3\)He.
* The extraction procedure for the neutron GPD works for \(x \leq 0.7\), where possible data are expected from Jefferson Lab.
* The GPD \(\tilde H\) results are shown at \(\Delta^2 = -0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\xi_3 = 0.1\) (where \(\xi_3 = (M_3/M) \xi\)).

## Context

This work extends previous IA studies of the GPDs \(H\) and \(E\) of \(^3\)He (Scopetta et al., 2004; Rinaldi and Scopetta, 2012) to the spin-dependent GPD \(\tilde H\) for the first time. The calculation enables the evaluation of cross section asymmetries for coherent DVCS off \(^3\)He at Jefferson Lab kinematics, which is a necessary step toward planning experiments to extract neutron GPDs. The approach builds on the established use of \(^3\)He as an effective neutron target in polarized deep inelastic scattering (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1993) and extends it to the GPD framework.
