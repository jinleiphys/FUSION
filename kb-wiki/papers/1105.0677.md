# The $^{150}$Nd($^3$He,$t$) and $^{150}$Sm($t$,$^3$He) reactions with applications to $ОІОІ$ decay of $^{150}$Nd

> The $^{150}$Nd($^3$He,$t$) reaction at 140 MeV/u and $^{150}$Sm($t$,$^3$He) reaction at 115 MeV/u were measured, populating excited states in $^{150}$Pm. The transitions studied populate intermediate states of importance for the (neutrinoless) $ОІОІ$ decay of $^{150}$Nd to $^{150}$Sm. Monopole and dipole contributions to the measured excitation-energy spectra were extracted by using multipole decomposition analyses. The experimental results were compared with theoretical calculations obtained within the framework of Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA), which is one of the main methods employed for estimating the half-life of the neutrinoless $ОІОІ$ decay ($0ОЅОІОІ$) of $^{150}$Nd. The present results thus provide useful information on the neutrino responses for evaluating the $0ОЅОІОІ$ and $2ОЅОІОІ$ matrix elements. The $2ОЅОІОІ$ matrix element calculated from the Gamow-Teller transitions through the lowest $1^{+}$ state in the intermediate nucleus is maximally about half of that deduced from the half-life measured in $2ОЅОІОІ$ direct counting experiments and at least several transitions through $1^{+}$ intermediate states in $^{150}$Pm are required to explain the $2ОЅОІОІ$ half-life. Because Gamow-Teller transitions in the $^{150}$Sm($t$,$^3$He) experiment are strongly Pauli-blocked, the extraction of Gamow-Teller strengths was complicated by the excitation of the $2\hbarП‰$, $О”L=0$, $О”S=1$ isovector spin-flip giant monopole resonance (IVSGMR). However, the near absence of Gamow-Teller transition strength made it possible to cleanly identify this resonance, and the strength observed is consistent with the full exhaustion of the non-energy-weighted sum rule for the IVSGMR.

## Key claim

The measured Gamow-Teller strength through the lowest 1+ state in the intermediate nucleus 150Pm accounts for at most about half of the 2ОЅОІОІ matrix element deduced from direct counting experiments, indicating that the Single-State Dominance hypothesis does not hold for 150Nd and that several transitions through higher-lying 1+ intermediate states are required to explain the 2ОЅОІОІ half-life.

## Method

The 150Nd(3He,t) reaction at 140 MeV/u and the 150Sm(t,3He) reaction at 115 MeV/u were measured to populate excited states in 150Pm, the intermediate nucleus for ОІОІ decay of 150Nd. Multipole decomposition analyses were performed on the excitation-energy spectra to extract monopole (О”L=0) and dipole (О”L=1) contributions, separating Gamow-Teller strength from isovector spin-flip giant monopole resonance and spin-dipole contributions. The experimental results were compared with Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations used for estimating neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The 2ОЅОІОІ matrix element calculated from Gamow-Teller transitions through the lowest 1+ state in 150Pm is maximally about half of that deduced from the half-life measured in 2ОЅОІОІ direct counting experiments
- The 150Nd(3He,t) experiment was performed at 140 MeV/u with an excitation-energy resolution of 33 keV FWHM and a reconstructed laboratory scattering angle resolution of 0.42 degrees FWHM
- The 150Sm(t,3He) experiment was performed at 115 MeV/u using a secondary triton beam with intensity of 10^7 s^-1
- The isobaric analog state in 150Pm was observed at 14.35 MeV excitation energy
- The Q value for 2ОЅОІОІ decay of 150Nd is 3.37 MeV
- The 150Sm target had a thickness of 18.0 mg/cm^2 with 96% isotopic purity; the 150Nd target was 1.0 mg/cm^2 thick with 96% isotopic purity

## Context

This work extends charge-exchange reaction studies of ОІОІ-decay intermediate nuclei, following earlier efforts such as those by Akimune (1997) and Grewe (2008) on other candidate nuclei. The results test the Single-State Dominance hypothesis proposed by Abad (1984) for 2ОЅОІОІ decay and provide experimental constraints for QRPA calculations by Fang (2010, 2011) that include deformation effects for 150Nd. The near absence of Gamow-Teller strength in the 150Sm(t,3He) reaction due to Pauli blocking enabled clean identification of the isovector spin-flip giant monopole resonance, with observed strength consistent with full exhaustion of the non-energy-weighted sum rule for this resonance.
