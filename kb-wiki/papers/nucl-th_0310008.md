# Short- and long-distance QCD effects in B-meson decays

> Various exclusive and inclusive decays of B mesons are studied, at present, with dedicated experiments at ``B factories''. In order to compete with the experimental accuracy, we need a reliable theoretical framework to compute strong interaction effects in a hadronic environment. I discuss how the separation of (perturbatively calculable) short-distance QCD effects from (non-perturbative) long-distance phenomena helps to obtain precise theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that separating perturbatively calculable short-distance QCD effects from non-perturbative long-distance phenomena, using effective field theory techniques such as HQET and SCET, is essential for obtaining precise theoretical predictions for B-meson decays that can compete with experimental accuracy from B factories.

## Method

The author reviews the effective Hamiltonian approach where heavy particles (W, Z bosons, top quark) are integrated out, with Wilson coefficients calculated by matching scattering amplitudes in the full Standard Model and the effective theory at the electroweak scale. The renormalization-group evolution resums large logarithms when evolving these coefficients down to the b-quark mass scale, while heavy-quark expansion (HQET) and soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) systematically separate short-distance from long-distance dynamics in inclusive and exclusive decays.

## Context

This review extends the effective field theory approach to B decays developed in works such as Buchalla (1996) for the effective Hamiltonian, Neubert (1994) for HQET, and Beneke (1999) for factorization in non-leptonic B → ππ decays. The paper enables improved determinations of CKM matrix elements and tests of the Standard Model flavour sector by comparing theoretical predictions with data from B factories, while also constraining new physics contributions through measurements of Wilson coefficients.
