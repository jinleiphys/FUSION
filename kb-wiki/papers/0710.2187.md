# Theoretical analysis for the apparent discrepancy between pbarp and pp data in charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlations

> The strength of charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlation in pbar+p and p+p collisions at s^1/2 = 200 GeV is studied by PYTHIA 6.4 and compared to the UA5 and STAR data correspondingly. It is turned out that a factor of 3-4 apparent discrepancy between UA5 and STAR data can be attributed to the differences in detector acceptances and observing bin interval in both experiments. A mixed event method is introduced and used to calculate the statistical correlation strength and the dynamical correlation strengths stemming from the charge conservation, four- momentum conservation, and decay, respectively. It seems that the statistical correlation is much larger than dynamical one and the charge conservation, four-momentum conservation and decay may account for most part of the dynamical correlation. In addition, we have also calculated the correlation strength by fitting the charged particle multiplicity distribution from PYTHIA to the Negative Binomial Distribution and found that the result agrees well with the correlation strength calculated by mixed events.

# Theoretical analysis for the apparent discrepancy between pbarp and pp data in charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlations

## Key claim
The factor of 3-4 apparent discrepancy between UA5 antiproton-proton data and STAR proton-proton data for the charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength at sqrt(s)=200 GeV can be attributed entirely to differences in detector acceptances (transverse momentum cuts and pseudorapidity coverage) and the interval of the pseudorapidity bin used in each experiment, rather than to any fundamental difference between antiproton-proton and proton-proton collisions.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA 6.4 event generator with default parameters to simulate charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlations in both antiproton-proton and proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They applied the different detector acceptance cuts of UA5 (pT>0 GeV/c, 0.0<|eta|<4) and STAR (pT>0.15 GeV/c, 0.0<|eta|<1.0) and varied the pseudorapidity bin interval (Delta eta=1.0 for UA5, 0.2 for STAR) to isolate the source of the discrepancy. A mixed event method was introduced to separate statistical from dynamical correlations, with individual constraints for charge conservation, four-momentum conservation, and decay of unstable particles. The charged particle multiplicity distribution from PYTHIA was also fitted to the Negative Binomial Distribution (NBD) to extract the correlation strength.

## Key numbers
- The apparent discrepancy between UA5 and STAR data is a factor of 3-4 in the correlation strength b at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.
- UA5 detector acceptance: pT>0 GeV/c and 0.0<|eta|<4; STAR detector acceptance: pT>0.15 GeV/c and 0.0<|eta|<1.0.
- UA5 pseudorapidity bin interval: Delta eta=1.0; STAR pseudorapidity bin interval: Delta eta=0.2.
- The NBD parameter k=6.6 was obtained from fitting the charged particle multiplicity distribution from PYTHIA.
- 150 thousand events were used for the mixed event analysis.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of forward-backward multiplicity correlations by the UA5 Collaboration (Alpgard et al., 1983; Ansorge et al., 1988) and the STAR Collaboration (Srivastavs, 2007; Tarnowsky, 2007) by providing a theoretical explanation for the apparent discrepancy between their results. The Dual Parton Model (Capella and Tran Thanh Van, 1983) and a statistical model (Liu and Meng, 1983, 1986) had previously studied the energy dependence of UA5 correlation strengths, while Hwa and Yang (2007) studied the STAR forward-backward multiplicity covariance using a partonic scattering model. This paper resolves the discrepancy by showing it is an experimental artifact, and introduces a mixed event method that enables separation of statistical and dynamical correlation components. The results enable future studies of the partonic origin of forward-backward multiplicity correlations using transport models.
