# Pairing: from atomic nuclei to neutron-star crusts

> Nuclear pairing is studied both in atomic nuclei and in neutron-star crusts in the unified framework of the energy-density functional theory using generalized Skyrme functionals complemented with a local pairing functional obtained from many-body calculations in homogeneous nuclear matter using realistic forces.

## Key claim

Nuclear pairing can be described consistently in both atomic nuclei and neutron-star crusts using energy-density functional (EDF) theory with generalized Skyrme functionals, but the density dependence of the pairing strength remains very poorly known and the local density approximation (LDA) strongly overestimates spatial variations of the pairing field in the deep crust.

## Method

The authors constructed three nuclear EDFs (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) based on generalized Skyrme forces with density-dependent generalizations of the t1 and t2 terms. The neutron-pairing functional was obtained by inverting the BCS gap equations to express the pairing strength in terms of 1S0 pairing gaps from Brueckner calculations including medium polarization effects, while the proton-pairing functional included renormalizing factors fitted to nuclear masses. For neutron-star crusts, the HFB equations were solved using band theory of solids with Floquet-Bloch boundary conditions, treating both nuclear clusters and superfluid neutrons consistently.

## Key numbers

- The three EDFs (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) fit 2149 measured masses of nuclei with N and Z >= 8 from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV for all three.
- The incompressibility coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) was restricted to K_v = 240 +/- 10 MeV.
- For neutron-star crust at average baryon density 0.06 fm^-3 with BSk21, the critical temperature for neutron superfluidity is T_c = 1.1 MeV.
- The critical temperature varies only weakly with cutoff: for cutoff energies from 2 to 32 MeV, T_c ranges from 1.06 to 1.16 MeV.
- The optimal pairing cutoff for nuclear mass fits was found to be epsilon_Lambda ~ 16 MeV, with clear minima in rms deviation around 7, 16, and 24 MeV.
- The low-density limit of the pairing strength gives an optimal cutoff of epsilon_Lambda ~ 7-8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme EDF approach of Bohr, Mottelson and Pines (1958) and Belyaev (1959) to provide a unified description of pairing from finite nuclei to neutron-star crusts. It builds on the earlier Brussels-Montreal functionals by incorporating pairing functionals constrained by Brueckner calculations from Cao et al. (2006) and medium polarization effects. The band theory treatment of the inner crust goes beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation used by Baldo (2007), Grill (2010), and Pastore (2010), and shows that the LDA approach incorrectly predicts quenching of pairing correlations inside clusters, especially near the critical temperature. The results suggest that a revision of interpretations of pulsar glitches may be necessary, as previously indicated by Chamel and Carter (2006).
