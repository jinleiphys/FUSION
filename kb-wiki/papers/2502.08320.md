# $ϕ$ meson in nuclear matter and atomic nuclei

> The properties (masses and decay widths) of the $ϕ$ meson are investigated in nuclear matter from the $ϕ$ meson self-energy, using the tree-level $ϕK\bar{K}$ Lagrangian, and, incorporating in-medium masses of (anti)kaons calculated within the quark meson coupling (QMC) model. These mass shifts and decay widths are incorporated in the Breit-Wigner spectral function of the $ϕ$ meson to calculate the production cross-section of $ϕ$ in asymmetric nuclear matter. Considerable modifications to the production cross-section are observed at normal nuclear matter density, driven by the in-medium mass reduction and the increase in the decay width of $ϕ$ meson. The potential experienced by $ϕ$ meson in nuclear matter is used to study the possibility of formation of the $ϕ$ mesic bound state with atomic nuclei. We explore the potential formation of $ϕ$-mesic bound states in ${\rm{^{4}He}}$, ${\rm{^{12}C}}$, ${\rm{^{16}O}}$, ${\rm{^{40}Ca}}$, ${\rm{^{90}Zr}}$, ${\rm{^{197}Au}}$ and ${\rm{^{208}Pb}}$ nuclei by investigating their binding energies and absorption widths based on the corresponding $ϕ$-nucleus potentials. Our study shows shallow bound states with the light nuclei and deeply bound states in heavy nuclei. Among the investigated nuclei, a particularly distinct signal for a $ϕ$-mesic bound state is identified in ${\rm{^{16}O}}$, suggesting its potential experimental observability. The work provides valuable insights into $ϕ$ meson interactions in infinite nuclear matter and the potential formation of exotic $ϕ$-mesic nuclear states, offering promising probes for strongly interacting matter in the upcoming experiments at J-PARC, JLab, and ${\rm{\bar{P}}ANDA}$@FAIR physics program.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the in-medium mass reduction and decay width broadening of the phi meson in nuclear matter, calculated using a tree-level phi-K-antiK Lagrangian with in-medium (anti)kaon masses from the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, lead to considerable modifications of the production cross-section at normal nuclear matter density, and that shallow bound states may form in light nuclei while deeply bound states appear in heavy nuclei, with a particularly distinct signal identified in oxygen-16.

## Method

The authors computed the phi meson self-energy from kaon-antikaon loop diagrams using a tree-level phi-K-antiK Lagrangian, incorporating in-medium masses of kaons and antikaons calculated within the quark meson coupling (QMC) model that includes a scalar-isovector delta meson to break isospin symmetry in asymmetric nuclear matter. They solved the dispersion relation self-consistently to obtain the in-medium phi mass and decay widths, then used these in a Breit-Wigner spectral function to calculate the production cross-section from K-antiK scattering. For finite nuclei, they constructed phi-nucleus potentials from the density-dependent mass shift and decay width, and solved the Klein-Gordon equation to find binding energies and absorption widths for seven nuclei ranging from helium-4 to lead-208.

## Key numbers

* At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0) with cutoff Lambda_K = 2 GeV and symmetric nuclear matter, the phi meson mass drop is approximately 2.1% from its vacuum value of 1019.461 MeV for the neutral kaon loop, and the partial decay width increases by a factor of about 13.7 from its vacuum value of 1.44 MeV.
* At normal nuclear matter density, the total decay width of the phi meson increases to around 35-40 MeV across the cutoff range Lambda_K = 2-4 GeV, compared to its vacuum total width of 3.5 MeV.
* The lifetime of the phi meson is reduced to approximately 4.4 fm at normal nuclear matter density, compared to its vacuum lifetime of about 55 fm.
* At normal nuclear matter density, the ratio of partial decay widths for charged to neutral kaon channels decreases from the vacuum value of about 1.45 to nearly 1.05, approaching unity at higher densities.
* For the neutral kaon loop at Lambda_K = 2 GeV and symmetric nuclear matter, the mass drop is about 3.5% at 4 times normal nuclear matter density, and the partial decay width increases by a factor of about 37.3 from its vacuum value.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson in-medium properties using the QMC model by Cobos-Martinez et al. (2017) and Mishra (2021), but incorporates isospin symmetry breaking via the scalar-isovector delta meson, leading to smaller in-medium masses and larger decay widths compared to those obtained with chiral SU(3) models by Mishra (2021) and Rajesh et al. (2020). The results are relevant for upcoming experiments at J-PARC (E16, E88, E29 collaborations), JLab, and the PANDA@FAIR physics program, which aim to observe phi-mesic bound states and in-medium modifications of the phi meson.
