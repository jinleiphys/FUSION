# Semi-Inclusive Jet Functions and Jet Substructure in $J_{E_T}^{(I)}$ and $J_{E_T}^{(II)}$ Algorithms

> Within the framework of Soft Collinear Effective Theory, we present calculations of semi-inclusive jet functions and fragmenting jet functions at next-to-leading order (NLO) for both quark- and gluon-initiated jets, for jet algorithms of $J_{E_T}^{(I)}$ and $J_{E_T}^{(II)}$ where one maximizes a suitable jet function. We demonstrate the consistency of the obtained results with the standard perturbative QCD calculations for $J_{E_T}^{(I)}$ algorithm, while the results for fragmenting jet functions with the $J_{E_T}^{(II)}$ algorithm are new. The renormalization group (RG) equation for both semi-inclusive jet functions and fragmenting jet functions are derived and shown to follow the time-like DGLAP evolution equations, independent of specific jet algorithms. The RG equation can be used to resum single logarithms of the jet size parameter $β$ for highly collimated jets in these algorithms where $β\gg 1$.

## Key claim

The authors present next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations of semi-inclusive jet functions and fragmenting jet functions for both quark- and gluon-initiated jets in the \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) and \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) maximized jet algorithms, demonstrating that the renormalization group equations for these functions follow the time-like DGLAP evolution equations independent of the specific jet algorithm, while the finite parts exhibit algorithm dependence. The results for fragmenting jet functions with the \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) algorithm are new, and the consistency with standard perturbative QCD calculations for the \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) algorithm is shown.

## Method

Within the Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) framework, the authors performed explicit NLO calculations of semi-inclusive jet functions and fragmenting jet functions for both quark and gluon jets, using both light-cone and covariant gauges for cross-checking. The calculations considered the phase space constraints from the maximized jet algorithms \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) (with \(n=1\)) and \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) (with \(n=2\)), where the jet is formed by maximizing a suitable function of the total four-momentum of final-state particles, and the narrow jet approximation (\(\beta \gg 1\)) was applied to obtain fully analytical expressions. The renormalization group equations were derived from the pole terms of the bare jet functions.

## Key numbers

- For the quark jet function in the \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) algorithm, the NLO result contains a term \(\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} C_F \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon^2} + \frac{3}{2\epsilon} + \frac{1}{\epsilon} \hat{L} + \frac{1}{2}\hat{L}^2 + \frac{3}{2} \hat{L} + 5 + \frac{3}{2} \ln 2 - \frac{7}{12}\pi^2\right)\) for the \(q\to qg\) channel, where \(\hat{L} = \ln\left(\frac{\beta \mu^2}{E_{\mathrm{J}}^2}\right)\).
- For the gluon jet function in the \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) algorithm, the NLO result for the \(g\to gg+q\bar{q}\) channel includes \(\delta(1-z) \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} \left[\frac{C_A}{\epsilon^2} + \frac{\beta_0}{2\epsilon} + \frac{C_A}{\epsilon} \hat{L} + \frac{C_A}{2} \hat{L}^2 + \frac{\beta_0}{2} \hat{L} + C_A \left(\frac{45}{8} + \frac{11}{6}\ln2 - \frac{7\pi^2}{12}\right) - n_f T_F \left(\frac{23}{12} + \frac{2}{3}\ln 2\right)\right]\), with \(\beta_0 = \frac{11}{3}C_A - \frac{4}{3}T_F n_f\).
- For the quark jet function in the \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) algorithm, the algorithm-dependent finite part for the \(\delta(1-z)\) term is \(C_F \left(-\frac{\pi^2}{2} + \frac{13}{2} - \frac{15\ln 2}{2} - \ln^2 2 + \frac{9\ln 3}{2} - \mathrm{Li}_2(1/4)\right)\), where \(\mathrm{Li}_2(1/4) = 0.267653\dots\).
- For the gluon jet function in the \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) algorithm, the algorithm-dependent finite part for the \(\delta(1-z)\) term is \(C_A\left(\frac{289}{36} - \frac{\pi^2}{2} - \frac{79\ln 2}{6} - \ln^2 2 + \frac{15}{2}\ln 3 - \mathrm{Li}_2(1/4)\right) - n_f T_f \left(\frac{67}{18} - \frac{34\ln 2}{3} + 6 \ln 3\right)\).

## Context

This work extends previous NLO calculations of semi-inclusive jet functions in cone and anti-\(k_T\) algorithms by Kang et al. (2016) and Dai et al. (2016) to the maximized jet algorithms \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) and \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) proposed by Georgi (2014) and improved by Bai et al. (2014). The results are consistent with standard perturbative QCD calculations for the \(J_{E_T}^{(I)}\) algorithm by Kaufmann et al. (2014, 2015), while the fragmenting jet function results for \(J_{E_T}^{(II)}\) are new. The derived renormalization group equations enable resummation of single logarithms of the jet size parameter \(\beta\) for highly collimated jets (\(\beta \gg 1\)), analogous to the resummation of logarithms of the jet radius \(R\) in anti-\(k_T\) algorithms, and the authors note that phenomenological implementations in pp and AA collisions are left for future work.
