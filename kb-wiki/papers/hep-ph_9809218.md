# Magnetic Catalysis and Oscillating Effects in Nambu -- Jona-Lasinio Model at Nonzero Chemical Potential

> Phase structure of the four dimensional Nambu -- Jona-Lasinio model has been investigated in two cases: 1) in nonsimply connected space-time of the form $R^3\times S^1$ (space coordinate is compactified and the length of the circle $S^1$ is $L$) with nonzero chemical potential $μ$ and 2) in Minkowski space-time at nonzero values of $μ,H$, where $H$ is the external magnetic field. In both cases on phase portraits of the model there are infinitly many massless chirally symmetric phases as well as massive ones with spontaneously broken chiral invariance. Such phase structure leads unavoidably to oscillations of some physical parameters at $L\to\infty$ or $H\to 0$, including magnetization, pressure and particle density of the system as well as quark condensate and critical curve of chiral phase transitions. Phase transitions of 1st and 2nd orders and several tricritical points have been shown to exist on phase diagrams of the model.

## Key claim

The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model exhibits infinitely many massless chirally symmetric phases and massive phases with broken chiral symmetry when either a spatial dimension is compactified (with circumference \(L\)) or an external magnetic field \(H\) is applied, leading to oscillatory behavior of physical quantities as \(L\to\infty\) or \(H\to 0\). The abstract's claim of "oscillations" is qualified by a specific definition: a continuous function \(f(x)\) oscillates at \(x\to a\) if there exists a monotonic sequence \(\{x_n\}\) converging to \(a\) such that \(f\) is continuous at each \(x_n\) but \(f'\) is discontinuous at those points.

## Method

The author analyzes the four-dimensional Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with \(N\) fermion flavors using the large-\(N\) expansion and the auxiliary-field method to obtain the effective potential. For the compactified case (space-time \(R^3\times S^1\)), the spatial momentum component along the compactified direction is discretized (\(p_{1n}=2\pi n/L\)), and the effective potential \(V_{\mu L}(\Sigma)\) is derived by summing over Matsubara-like modes at zero temperature but nonzero chemical potential \(\mu\). For the magnetic case, the effective potential \(V_{H\mu}(\Sigma)\) is obtained using the proper-time method, with Landau-level quantization (\(s_k=\sqrt{\Sigma^2+2eHk}\)) and summation over levels. Phase diagrams are constructed by finding the global minimum of the effective potential as a function of \(\Sigma\) (the chiral condensate/fermion mass) and identifying first- and second-order phase transitions via discontinuities in derivatives of the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

* For the compactified case with coupling \(G_c<G<G_2\equiv(1.225...)G_c\), the critical curve \(\mu_c(\lambda)\) (where \(\lambda=1/L\)) oscillates at \(\lambda\to 0\) with frequency \(\bar\lambda_0/(2\sqrt{6})\), where \(\bar\lambda_0\) is defined by \(\pi^2/(2G)-\Lambda^2/8\equiv -\pi^2\bar\lambda_0^2/6\).
* The fermionic condensate \(\Sigma(\mu,\lambda)\) at \(M<\mu<\mu_c(0)\) and \(\lambda\to 0\) oscillates with frequency \(\sqrt{\mu^2-m^2(\mu)}/(2\pi)\), where \(m(\mu)=\Sigma(\mu,0)\).
* The particle density \(n(\mu,\lambda)\) at \(M<\mu<\mu_c(0)\) and \(\lambda\to 0\) oscillates with frequency \(\sqrt{\mu^2-m^2(\mu)}/(2\pi)\).
* The pressure (and thus Casimir force) at \(\mu>\mu_c(0)\) and \(L\to\infty\) oscillates with frequency \(\mu/(2\pi)\).
* For the magnetic case with \(G<G_c\), the critical curve \(\mu_c(H)\) separating the symmetric phase from the symmetry-broken phase is given by \(\mu_c(H)=\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{eH}}[V_H(0)-V_H(\Sigma_0(H))]^{1/2}\).
* At \(H\to 0\) and \(G<G_c\), the dynamically generated fermion mass scales as \(\Sigma_0^2(H)\approx\frac{eH}{\pi}\exp\left\{-\frac{1}{eH}\left(\frac{4\pi^2}{G}-\Lambda^2\right)\right\}\).
* At \(H\to\infty\) and \(G<G_c\), \(\Sigma_0(H)\approx\frac{eH}{\pi}\sqrt{\frac{G}{12}}\).
* The second derivative \(\partial^2\Omega/(\partial\mu)^2\) has an infinite jump at each line \(l_k=\{(\mu,H):\mu=\sqrt{2eHk}\}\) for \(k=1,2,...\), indicating second-order phase transitions.

## Context

This work extends the study of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in the NJL model under external conditions, building on prior analyses of the model at nonzero temperature and chemical potential by Kawati (1983) and others, and on the magnetic catalysis effect discovered in the (2+1)-dimensional Gross-Neveu model by Klimenko (1992) and Gusynin (1995). The compactified-space analysis relates to Casimir-type effects and superstring-inspired topologies, as studied by Inagaki (1997) and Kim (1997). The magnetic oscillations predicted here are analogous to de Haas-van Alphen oscillations in quantum electrodynamics, as discussed by Peres (1996) and Aslamazov (1996). The results enable predictions of oscillatory Casimir forces in fermionic systems at nonzero chemical potential and provide a framework for studying chiral phase transitions in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
