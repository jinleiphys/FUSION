# On the validity of the O(18) coupling scheme in N~Z nuclei

## Key claim

The newly proposed O(18) coupling scheme in IBM-3 provides at least as good a first approximation for low-lying states as the more familiar U(6) scheme, using the largest O(18) label α=N and smallest O(15) label δ, when tested against numerical wave-functions from a shell-model mapping applied to Zn, Ge, and Se isotopes. However, the authors hedge that this conclusion assumes the result is not a specific property of the rather simple shell-model interaction used in their calculation.

## Method

The authors compared wave-functions from the O(18) ⊃ O(15) coupling scheme with numerical IBM-3 wave-functions obtained from an NT-dependent IBM-3 Hamiltonian deduced from a shell-model mapping, applied to Ni, Zn, Ge, and Se isotopes in the first half of the p3/2, f5/2, p1/2 shell. They analyzed the percentage of wave-function content in the favoured representations of both the U(6) chain and the new O(18) ⊃ O(15) chain for low-lying states of each spin, using the classification scheme where α is the O(18) label and δ is the O(15) label.

## Key numbers

- For 62Zn (N=3, T=1): the 0+1 state has 94% in the fully symmetric U(6) representation [N] and 99% in the favoured O(18) ⊃ O(15) representation (α=3, δ=0)
- For 64Ge (N=4, T=0): the 0+1 state has 94% in U(6) [N] and 95% in the O(18) ⊃ O(15) favoured representation
- For 68Se (N=6, T=0): the 0+1 state has 91% in U(6) [N] and 84% in the O(18) ⊃ O(15) favoured representation
- For 62Zn: the 2+1 state has 96% in U(6) [3] and 93% in O(18) ⊃ O(15) (α=3, δ=1)
- For 64Zn (N=4, T=2): the 2+1 state has 94% in U(6) [4] and 97% in O(18) ⊃ O(15) (α=4, δ=1)
- For 66Ge (N=5, T=1): the 4+1 state has 94% in U(6) [5] and 97% in O(18) ⊃ O(15) (α=5, δ=2)
- For 64Ge (T=0): both 3+ states are extremely well described by α=N, δ=3, with 89% and 9% in U(6) for 3+1 and 3+2 respectively, versus 96% and 99% in the O(18) chain
- The 0+2 states show zero (very small) percentages in the favoured O(18) representation because only one state exists with α=N, δ=0, which is mostly taken up by the 0+1 state

## Context

This work extends the earlier development of O(18) coupling schemes in IBM-3 by Ginocchio (1996), who discovered two new group chains starting from O(18) as a subgroup of U(18). The authors had previously performed numerical IBM-3 calculations (Evans, Elliott, and Lac, 1996) using an NT-dependent Hamiltonian from a shell-model mapping applied to Ni, Zn, Ge, and Se isotopes. The present analysis tests whether the new O(18) ⊃ O(15) chain, which breaks the U(6) symmetry known to be reasonably good, can serve as a useful truncation scheme. The authors find that one of the new chains (O(6) × SU(2)) is of little interest since it is identical to the old U(6) × SUc(3) classification for most low states, while the O(18) ⊃ O(15) chain shows promise for simplifying IBM-3 calculations through state truncation or reduction of Hamiltonian parameters. The authors note that a corresponding new group chain O(12) ⊃ O(10) exists in IBM-2, which would imply mixing of F-spin analogous to the mixing of U(6) labels studied here.
