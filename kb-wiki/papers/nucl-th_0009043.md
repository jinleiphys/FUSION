# Low-Spin Spectroscopy of 50Mn

> The data on low spin states in the odd-odd nucleus 50Mn investigated with the 50Cr(p,ngamma)50Mn fusion evaporation reaction at the FN-TANDEM accelerator in Cologne are reported. Shell model and collective rotational model interpretations of the data are given.

# Low-Spin Spectroscopy of 50Mn

## Key claim

The low-spin structure of the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 50Mn up to 3.6 MeV excitation energy shows excellent agreement with full pf-shell model calculations, and the data support the interpretation that strong isovector M1 transitions arise from quasideuteron configurations while collective rotational bands can be described by a Nilsson model with deformation parameter β = 0.25. The paper hedges that the proposed strongly deformed Kπ=3+ band (β ≈ 0.3) is a theoretical hypothesis that still lacks direct experimental confirmation, as the predicted strong E2 transition between the 3+2 and 4+2 states was not observed due to their small energy spacing.

## Method

Low-spin states in 50Mn were populated via the 50Cr(p,nγ)50Mn fusion evaporation reaction at a proton beam energy of 15 MeV using the FN-TANDEM accelerator at the University of Cologne. Gamma-gamma coincidence data were analyzed to place 25 new transitions in the level scheme, establishing 16 new levels, with new spin assignments for six levels, nine new intensity ratios, and eight new multipole mixing ratios δ. The experimental results were compared to full pf-shell model calculations using both KB3 and FPD6 residual interactions, as well as to a collective rotational model based on the Nilsson deformed orbital [312]5/2−.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the 2+1 state in 50Mn: 800 keV (interpreted as the T=1 isobaric analogue of the 2+1 state at 783 keV in 50Cr)
- Excitation energy of the 4+ state in 50Mn: 1931 keV (corrected from the previously reported 1917 keV by Svensson et al. (1998)), interpreted as the T=1 isobaric analogue of the 4+1 state at 1882 keV in 50Cr
- Deformation parameter β = 0.25 used for Nilsson model calculations of Kπ=0+ and Kπ=5+ intraband B(E2) values
- Estimated B(M1;2+1→1+1) value in 50Mn derived from measured decay intensity ratios and the B(E2;2+1→0+1) value in the T=1 isospin partner 50Cr
- Estimated summed M1 0+→1+ transition strength from quasideuteron picture: up to approximately 4.5 μN2 for the 0+1→1+1 transition, out of a predicted total of 18 μN2 for odd-odd N=Z nuclei in the f7/2 orbital
- Proposed deformation parameter β ≈ 0.3 for the hypothetical Kπ=3+ band

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of low-spin structures in odd-odd N=Z nuclei previously performed by the Cologne group for 54Co (Schneider et al., 1999) and 46V (Friesner et al., 1998), and complements high-spin data for 50Mn from Svensson et al. (1998). The results establish the equivalence of full pf-shell model and deformed mean-field treatments for low-energy structures in 50Mn, and the identification of strong isovector M1 transitions supports the quasideuteron picture proposed by Lisetskiy et al. (1999). The paper calls for future experimental searches for the predicted strongly deformed Kπ=3+ band, whose intraband E2 transition could not be observed due to the small level spacing between the 1798 keV and 1917 keV states.
