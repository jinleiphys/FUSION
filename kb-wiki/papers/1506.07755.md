# Bayesian analysis for two-parameter hybrid EoS with high-mass compact star twins

> We perform a Bayesian analysis in the basis of a recently developed two-parameter class of hybrid equations of state that allow for high-mass compact star twins. While recently a wide range of radii, from 9 - 15 km, has been inferred for different neutron stars using different techniques, we perform our analysis under the supposition that the radii are towards the large end ($13-15$ km). We use this radius constraint together with the undebated statistically independent constraint for high masses ($\sim 2~M_\odot$) as priors in selecting the most probable hybrid equations of state from a family with two free parameters: the baryon excluded volume in the hadronic phase and the 8-quark vector channel interaction in the quark matter phase.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that neutron star radii are large (13–15 km), a Bayesian analysis of a two-parameter hybrid equation of state family shows that the most probable models exhibit high-mass compact star twins, two stars with nearly the same mass but different radii, connected by a strong first-order deconfinement phase transition, provided the excluded volume parameter is sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of hybrid equations of state by combining a density-dependent relativistic mean-field hadronic model (DD2) with an excluded volume correction (parameter ν, the closest packing density) and an NJL quark matter model with an 8-quark vector channel interaction (parameter η₄). They performed a Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition between the two phases, solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius sequences, and applied Bayesian inference using a Gaussian mass prior (2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ from PSR J0348+0432) and a Gaussian radius prior (15.5 ± 1.5 km from PSR J0437-4715) to compute posterior probabilities for each parameter combination.

## Key numbers

- Mass constraint: μ_A = 2.01 M⊙, σ_A = 0.04 M⊙ (from PSR J0348+0432, Antoniadis 2013)
- Radius constraint: μ_B = 15.5 km, σ_B = 1.5 km (from PSR J0437-4715, Bogdanov 2012)
- Optimal parameter values: ν = 33 (closest packing density n_ν = 0.33 fm⁻³) and η₄ = 5
- Most probable parameter range: 33 < ν < 38 and 3 < η₄ < 7
- Radius difference between high-mass twin stars: about 2 km
- Parameter scan range: ν from 33 to 100 (n_ν = 0.33 to 1.00 fm⁻³), η₄ from 0 to 30

## Context

This work extends the hybrid equation of state model introduced by Benic et al. (2014) that allows for high-mass twin stars, and applies a Bayesian analysis similar in spirit to earlier work by Steiner et al. (2010) but using updated mass and radius constraints. The results enable a path toward identifying a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram and address several open issues in compact star physics: the hyperon puzzle, the masquerade problem, and the reconfinement case. The next steps include expanding the parameter space to include purely hadronic cases without a phase transition and incorporating pasta-phase structures in the transition region.
