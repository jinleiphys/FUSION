# On the upper bound of entropy production rate from particle multiplicity in heavy ion collisions

> We provide a simple derivation for particle production in heavy-ion collisions that is proportional to the rate of entropy production. We find that the particle production depends only on the power of the centre-of-mass collision energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ and the effective phase-space/volume (e.g. geometry of the collision approximated by the number of nucleons participating in the collision $N_{\rm part}$). We show that at low-energies the pseudo-rapidity density of particles per participating nucleon pair scales linearly with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ while at high-energies with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$. The $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$ region is directly related to sub-nucleon degrees of freedom and creation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP). This picture explains experimental observation that the shape of the distributions of pseudorapidity-density per nucleon pair of charged particles does not depend on $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ over a large span of collision energies. We provide an explanation of the scaling and connect it with the maximum rate per unit time of entropy production. We conclude with remarks on the hadron-parton phase transition. In particular, our considerations suggest that the pseudo-rapitidy density of the produced particles per $N_{\rm part}/2$ larger than approximately 1 (excluding particles from jet fragmentation) is a signature of a QGP formation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed scaling of charged particle multiplicity with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$ at high energies represents a maximum achievable rate of entropy production per unit time, and that a pseudorapidity density per participating nucleon pair greater than approximately 1 (excluding jet fragmentation) is a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors derive a simple model where particle production is proportional to entropy production rate, relating it to the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ and the number of participating nucleons $N_{\rm part}$. They compare two regimes: a three-dimensional phase-space scenario for hadronic gas where multiplicity scales linearly with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$, and a one-dimensional phase-space scenario for quark-gluon plasma where multiplicity scales with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$. The model is compared to published experimental data from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC for charged particle pseudorapidity density in central and semi-central heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The transition between $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ scaling and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$ scaling occurs at about 10 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- The $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$ scaling region spans from approximately 10 GeV to 5.02 TeV.
- The pseudorapidity density per participating nucleon pair threshold for QGP formation is approximately 1 (excluding particles from jet fragmentation).
- The interesting transition region where $1 \leq {\rm d}N_{\rm ch}/{\rm d}\eta / N_{\rm part} / 2 \leq 1.5$ lies between $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 0.007$ TeV and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 0.015$ TeV.
- For semi-central collisions, a fit to RHIC data for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} > 20$ GeV gives $f=0.15$, while $f=0.1$ matches LHC top energy.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological understanding of particle production scaling observed by PHOBOS (Alver 2010) and PHENIX (Adare 2015), who noted the logarithmic and power-law behavior of multiplicity with energy. The authors connect the $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}^{1/3}$ scaling to the Lund string fragmentation model (Andersson 1979) and to the concept of maximum entropy production rate. The results suggest that QGP formation occurs already at energies as low as 10 GeV, and the framework enables predictions for future accelerators such as the Future Circular Collider at 40 TeV.
