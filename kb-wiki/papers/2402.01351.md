# Production of hypernuclei from antiproton capture within a relativistic transport model

> Production yields of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei from simulated peripheral annihilations of antiprotons after capture on various target nuclei are reported. The initial annihilation process and the production of excited hypernuclei are estimated within the GiBUU transport framework, while their deexcitation process is treated with the ABLA++ code. The yield of excited hypernuclei range from $\sim$ 0.3% for $^{16}$O to $\sim$ 1.2% for $^{132}$Xe per annihilation, consistent with previous measurements at LEAR, CERN. The yield of specific ground state hypernuclei after deexcitation reaches up to a few 10$^{-4}$ per annihilation. The hypernuclei are produced in strangeness exchange reactions occuring between a nucleon of the target and the kaons originating from the annihilation in $\sim$ 80% of the cases, while the strangeness pair production in secondary pion-nucleon collision contributes to the remaining $\sim$ 20%. The simulations indicate that antiproton annihilations at rest on different nuclei could populate a wide range of so-far unexplored hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reports that simulated peripheral antiproton annihilations at rest on target nuclei produce excited hypernuclei with yields ranging from approximately 0.3% for oxygen-16 to approximately 1.2% for xenon-132 per annihilation, and that after deexcitation the yield of specific ground-state hypernuclei reaches up to a few 10^-4 per annihilation, consistent with previous measurements at LEAR, CERN. The authors hedge that an experimental method to tag and identify produced hypernuclei still needs to be implemented.

## Method

The initial annihilation of antiprotons at rest (simulated with 10 eV kinetic energy) on target nuclei and the subsequent production of excited hypernuclei were simulated using the GiBUU relativistic transport framework, with impact parameters set to reproduce the radial annihilation profile observed in antiprotonic atoms. The deexcitation of the excited hypernuclei into ground states via nucleon, lambda, and photon emission was then treated with the ABLA++ evaporation and fission module, using a modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula for hypernuclei to extract excitation energies.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental studies at the CERN-PS-177 experiment at LEAR (Bocquet 1987, Armstrong 1993), which observed hypernuclei via delayed fission following antiproton stopping on bismuth and uranium targets with estimated production rates of about 0.3% to 0.7% per annihilation. The simulations indicate that antiproton annihilations at rest on different stable nuclei could populate a wide range of so-far unexplored hypernuclei, with over 200 new hypernuclei in the medium-mass region becoming accessible, and the authors note that such conditions could be reached at the extra-low energy antiproton (ELENA) ring at CERN.
