# Front-form calculation of the deuteron EM form factors

> The deuteron form factors are calculated in the framework of the relativistic nucleon-meson dynamics. The relativistic effects change considerably the S- and D-waves of the deuteron, result in the dominating extra component in the deuteron wave function and generate the contact interaction between the nucleon, mesons and photon. The prediction for the polarization observable $t_{20}$ is in good agreement with the recent data of TJNAF/CEBAF.

# Front-form calculation of the deuteron EM form factors

## Key claim
The relativistic nucleon-meson dynamics calculation of deuteron electromagnetic form factors, which includes a dominating extra component in the deuteron wave function and a contact interaction, yields predictions for the polarization observable \(t_{20}\) that are in close agreement with existing experimental data across all momentum ranges, including the new TJNAF/CEBAF results at \(\theta=70^\circ\).

## Method
The authors used the explicitly covariant version of light-front dynamics, incorporating a relativistic one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) kernel with six mesons (\(\pi,\eta,\rho,\omega,\delta,\sigma\)) and parameters from the Bonn model. The deuteron wave function was calculated perturbatively starting from the nonrelativistic wave function, keeping three dominating spin components (\(f_1, f_2, f_5\)). The electromagnetic vertex included both the impulse approximation and a contact (instantaneous) interaction \(NNB\gamma\) summed over all six mesons, and the physical form factors \(\mathcal{F}_1, \mathcal{F}_2, \mathcal{G}_1\) were extracted by separating nonphysical \(\omega\)-dependence using explicit covariant formulas.

## Key numbers
- The component \(f_5\) dominates over all other components (including \(f_1\) and \(f_2\)) starting from \(k \approx 0.5\) GeV/c.
- The calculation is compared to experimental data at \(\theta=70^\circ\), including the new TJNAF/CEBAF results from Kox (1998).
- The polarization observable \(t_{20}\) depends on momentum transfer and explicitly on \(\tan^2\frac{1}{2}\theta\).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic nucleon-meson dynamics approach to deuteron electromagnetic form factors, building on the Bonn model (Machleidt 1987) and the explicitly covariant light-front formalism developed by Carbonell, Desplanques, Karmanov, and Mathiot (1994). The calculation incorporates the extra wave function component \(f_5\) whose physical meaning was clarified by Desplanques, Karmanov, and Mathiot (1995) as automatically incorporating 50% of the pair term contribution in the isovector electrodisintegration near threshold. The results enable further improvements including exact solution of the deuteron equation, redefinition of NN kernel parameters, inclusion of higher Fock states (\(NN\pi\), etc.), and direct meson exchange currents from photon-meson interactions.
