# Determination of Chemical Freeze-out Parameters from Net-kaon Fluctuations at RHIC

> We calculate the mean-over-variance ratio of the net-kaon fluctuations in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) Model for the five highest energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) for different particle data lists. We compare these results with the latest experimental data from the STAR collaboration in order to extract sets of chemical freeze-out parameters for each list. We focused on the PDG2012 and PDG2016+ particle lists, which differ largely in the number of resonant states. Our analysis determines the effect of the amount of resonances included in the HRG on the freeze-out conditions.

## Key claim

The separation between freeze-out temperatures for kaons and light hadrons persists even when additional strange resonances are included in the Hadron Resonance Gas model, though the kaon freeze-out temperature is slightly lower with the more complete PDG2016+ particle list than with the PDG2012 list.

## Method

The authors calculated the mean-over-variance ratio of net-kaon fluctuations in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model using two different particle data lists (PDG2012 and PDG2016+) for the five highest collision energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. They first determined freeze-out parameters for light particles via a combined fit of net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations, then constructed isentropic trajectories by fixing entropy per baryon number along the path in the phase diagram. Finally, they compared the HRG model results for net-kaon fluctuations along these isentropes to experimental data from the STAR collaboration to extract chemical freeze-out parameters for kaons.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV, there is a separation of about 10 MeV between the freeze-out conditions for net-kaons and light hadrons.
- The analysis covers the five highest energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES), with specific collision energies not listed in the provided text.
- The freeze-out parameters were extracted by comparing HRG model calculations to experimental data from the STAR collaboration for the mean-over-variance ratio of net-kaon fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used the HRG model to determine chemical freeze-out conditions from net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations (Alba 2014) and addresses the tension between light and strange particle freeze-out temperatures observed at both RHIC and the LHC (Acharya 2018, Adamczyk 2017). The results enable further investigation into whether missing resonances (Noronha-Hostler 2014, Bazavov 2014) or a higher freeze-out temperature for strange hadrons (Bellwied 2013, Bluhm 2019) can resolve the discrepancy between light and strange particle thermal fits.
