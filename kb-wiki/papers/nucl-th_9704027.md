# Muon capture by oriented nuclei: new possibilities for studying induced pseudoscalar interaction

> Angular distribution of neutrinos (recoil nucleus) in muon capture for an allowed Gamov-Teller transition is considered by taking account of hyperfine effects. This angular distribution is shown to include a correlation of the form P_2(cos A), where A is the angle between the neutrino momentum and the axis specifying the orientation of the initial mesic atom. This correlation, which arises only if the initial mesic atom is aligned, proves highly sensitive to the form factor g_P of induced pseudoscalar interaction. The proposed method for determinating g_P may be realized for the transition 1+ to 2+ from the ground state of the 6-Li nucleus to the narrow resonance of the 6-He nucleus in continuum, as well as in the processes like 10-B(3+) to 10-Be(2+), 11-B(3/2-) to 11-Be(1/2-), 23-Na(3/2+) to 23-Ne(1/2+) with transitions to bound states.

# Muon capture by oriented nuclei: new possibilities for studying induced pseudoscalar interaction

## Key claim

The angular distribution of neutrinos (or recoil nuclei) in muon capture for an allowed Gamow-Teller transition includes a correlation proportional to P₂(cos θ) that is highly sensitive to the induced pseudoscalar form factor g_P, but this correlation arises **only if** the initial mesic atom is aligned (i.e., has a nonzero rank-2 spin-tensor), which in turn requires preoriented nuclei. The method is unsuitable for the 1⁺ → 0⁺ transition in ⁶Li because the F=1/2 hyperfine state cannot be aligned and capture from F=3/2 is forbidden in the single s-wave approximation.

## Method

The authors derived a general expression for the differential probability of muon capture from a given hyperfine structure state |F⟩ of a mesic atom, using the nonrelativistic Foldy-Wouthuysen transformed Hamiltonian to first order in 1/M. They considered an allowed Gamow-Teller transition Jᵢ → J_f = Jᵢ ± 1 with no parity change, retaining only the single leading s-wave reduced matrix element [101] in the multipole expansion. The angular distribution was expressed as a series in Legendre polynomials P₀, P₁, and P₂, with coefficients depending on the form factors g_A, g_V, g_M, g_P, the nuclear spins, and the hyperfine angular momentum F.

## Key numbers

- The form factor of induced pseudoscalar interaction from muon capture by a free proton was estimated at g_P = 8.7 ± 1.9, compared to a PCAC prediction of 8.4 ± 0.9.
- The ratio E_ν / Mc² is about 10⁻³ in beta decay and about 10⁻¹ in muon capture.
- The constant λ_μ = 1.005 × 10³ s⁻¹ appears in the expression for the muon capture probability.
- For the ⁶Li 1⁺ → 2⁺ transition to the continuum resonance of ⁶He, the resonance energy is E = 1.8 MeV and width Γ = 0.1 MeV.
- For the specific case Jᵢ=1, J_f=2, F=3/2, the angular distribution coefficients are C₁=3/2, C₂=-3/2, C₃=-3/5, C₄=2/5.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Hwang (1978) for the particular 1⁺ → 0⁺ Gamow-Teller transition to arbitrary Jᵢ → Jᵢ±1 transitions, incorporating hyperfine structure effects. It addresses the longstanding discrepancy between the PCAC-predicted g_P ≈ 8.4 and the substantially lower values extracted from muon capture experiments on ⁶Li (Deutsch, 1968) and ²⁸Si (Brudanin, 1995), where the total capture probability showed insufficient sensitivity to g_P. The proposed alignment-based method enables future experiments on oriented nuclei such as ¹⁰B, ¹¹B, and ²³Na, and could resolve the g_P puzzle by providing an observable (the P₂ correlation coefficient) that is directly proportional to (g_P - g_A - g_V - g_M).
