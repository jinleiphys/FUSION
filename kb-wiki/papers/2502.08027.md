# Benchmarking nuclear matrix elements of $0νββ$ decay with high-energy nuclear collisions

> Reducing uncertainties in the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) remains a critical challenge in designing and interpreting experiments aimed at discovering neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay. Here, we identify a class of observables, distinct from those employed in low-energy nuclear structure applications, that are strongly correlated with the NMEs: momentum correlations among hadrons produced in high-energy nuclear collisions. Focusing on the $^{150}$Nd$\rightarrow$$^{150}$Sm transition, we combine a Bayesian analysis of the structure of $^{150}$Nd with simulations of high-energy $^{150}$Nd+$^{150}$Nd collisions. We reveal prominent correlations between the NMEs and features of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in these processes, such as spatial gradients and anisotropies, which are accessible via collective flow measurements. Our findings demonstrate collider experiments involving $0νββ$ decay candidates as a platform for benchmarking theoretical predictions of the NMEs.

## Key claim

The authors identify a new class of observables, momentum correlations among hadrons produced in high-energy nuclear collisions, that are strongly correlated with the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of neutrinoless double beta decay, specifically for the $^{150}$Nd$\rightarrow$$^{150}$Sm transition, but only within the framework of multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) and under the assumption that the correlations survive a combined Bayesian analysis of both low- and high-energy data.

## Method

The authors coupled a Bayesian analysis of the nuclear structure of $^{150}$Nd within MR-CDFT to simulations of ultra-central $^{150}$Nd+$^{150}$Nd collisions at ultra-relativistic energies using the TRENTo model of initial conditions. They varied nine parameters of the relativistic energy density functional around their optimal PC-PK1 values using quasi Monte-Carlo sampling, generating 10$^3$ parameter sets, and for each set computed the NME and the intrinsic nuclear shape. The intrinsic shape was then used to simulate 10$^6$ collision events per parameter set, from which they extracted spatial ellipticity ($\varepsilon_2$) and energy-to-entropy ratio ($E/S$) of the quark-gluon plasma, which are accessible experimentally via elliptic flow ($v_2$) and mean transverse momentum ($[p_T]$) measurements.

## Key numbers

* The Pearson correlation coefficient between the NME and the quadrupole deformation of the parent nucleus $^{150}$Nd is $r = -0.93$, while the correlation with the difference in quadrupole deformations between initial and final nuclei is $r = -0.78$.
* The relative systematic uncertainty on the proton radius is less than 0.5%, while the distribution of $\beta_2$ shows variations up to 10% around the mean value, leading to a relative systematic uncertainty on the NME exceeding 10%.
* The covariance of $v_2^2$ and $[p_T]$ varies by about 10% across the parameter sets, showing the strongest variation among the high-energy observables studied.
* Both $v_2$ and $[p_T]$ fluctuations vary by several percent across the parameter sets, as does the fourth-order cumulant $v_2\{4\}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that explored correlations between NMEs and low-energy nuclear structure observables such as excitation energies of the first $2^+$ states and $B(E2)$ values, using Bayesian analyses within interacting shell models (Horoi, 2022; Horoi, 2023), valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (Belley, 2022), and MR-CDFT (Zhang, 2024). It also builds on established techniques in high-energy nuclear physics where collective flow measurements in isobaric collisions have been used to probe nuclear shapes (STAR, 2015; ALICE, 2018; CMS, 2019; ATLAS, 2019). The results enable a combined Bayesian analysis of low- and high-energy data to better constrain nuclear deformation and thus reduce NME uncertainties, potentially connecting the LHC ion program to next-generation $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay searches.
