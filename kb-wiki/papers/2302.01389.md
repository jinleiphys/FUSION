# Masses of compact (neutron) stars with distinguished cores

> The impact of the core mass on the compact/neutron-star mass-radius relation is studied. Besides the mass, the core is parameterized by its radius and surface pressure, which supports the outside one-component Standard Model (SM) matter. The core may accommodate SM matter with unspecified (or poorly known) equation-of-state or several components, e.g. consisting of admixtures of Dark Matter and/or Mirror World matter etc. beyond the SM. Thus, the admissible range of masses and radii of compact stars can be considerably extended.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the admissible range of masses and radii of compact stars can be considerably extended when the core is parameterized by only its radius, mass, and surface pressure, without specifying the core's equation of state, which may include Standard Model matter with poorly known properties or admixtures of Dark Matter or Mirror World matter beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors employ a core-corona decomposition of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, where the core is described by a minimum set of parameters (radius r_x, mass m_x, and surface pressure p_x) and the corona (envelope) is assumed to consist of one-component Standard Model matter with a known equation of state. For small cores, a truncated Taylor expansion of the core equation of state is used with a density jump parameter λ and sound velocity v_s^2, while for multi-component cores (e.g., Standard Model plus Mirror World matter), the TOV equations are solved in parallel for each component coupled only by gravity. The NYΔ DD-EM2 density functional equation of state is deployed as an explicit example for the corona.

## Key numbers

- The scaling of TOV equations yields characteristic scales: (G_N s)^{-1/2} = 7 km / sqrt(10^{-2} n / n_0) and (G_N^3 s)^{-1/2} = 4.8 M_sun / sqrt(10^{-2} n / n_0), where n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} is nuclear saturation density.
- Using the nucleon mass scale s = m_p^4 gives (G_N s)^{-1/2} = 2.74 km and (G_N^3 s)^{-1/2} = 1.86 M_sun.
- For a one-component core with λ = 1, the core compactness C_x = 2 m_x^{max} / r_x at m_x^{max} ranges from 0.37 (v_s^2 = 1/6, Δ = 0.1) to 0.70 (v_s^2 = 1, Δ = 0.3).
- The approximate core radius relation is r_x ≈ 60.1 km / sqrt(W) * sqrt(δ * 100 MeV/fm^3 / p_x), where δ = p_c/p_x - 1 and W = 3 + 4λ e_x/p_x + λ^2 (e_x/p_x)^2.
- The approximate core mass relation is m_x ≈ 81.2 M_sun / W^{3/2} * λ * (e_x / 100 MeV/fm^3) * (δ * 100 MeV/fm^3 / p_x)^{3/2}.
- For the NYΔ DD-EM2 equation of state, the maximum masses agree between the parameterized and tabulated forms, but radii differ by about 1 km at M ≈ 1.4 M_sun.

## Context

This work extends the core-corona approach previously launched by Zollner (2022) and builds upon the known phenomenon of "third family" compact stars and twin stars, where a strong local softening or first-order phase transition in the equation of state can give rise to additional stable configurations beyond white dwarfs and neutron stars, as studied by Gerlach (1968), Kampfer (1981, 1983, 1985), and Haensel (1983, 1987). The parameterization of the trace anomaly measure Δ follows the approach of Fujimoto (2022), and the explicit equation of state example uses the NYΔ DD-EM2 density functional from Li (2019). The work enables systematic exploration of how exotic core compositions, including Dark Matter or Mirror World matter, can populate regions of the mass-radius plane that are inaccessible to pure Standard Model neutron stars, with implications for the mass gap between compact stars and black holes highlighted by the GW190814 event.
