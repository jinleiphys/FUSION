# Effective convergence of the 2PI-1/N expansion for nonequilibrium quantum fields

> The 1/N expansion of the two-particle irreducible effective action offers a powerful approach to study quantum field dynamics far from equilibrium. We investigate the effective convergence of the 1/N expansion in the O(N) model by comparing results obtained numerically in 1+1 dimensions at leading, next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order in 1/N as well as in the weak coupling limit. A comparison in classical statistical field theory, where exact numerical results are available, is made as well. We focus on early-time dynamics and quasi-particle properties far from equilibrium and observe rapid effective convergence already for moderate values of 1/N or the coupling.

## Key claim

The 2PI-1/N expansion for nonequilibrium quantum fields shows rapid effective convergence already for moderate values of 1/N or the coupling, but the authors restrict their NNLO analysis to only the 3-loop diagram (denoted NNLO') and note that the 4-loop diagram is suppressed by two powers of the coupling constant, so no subtle cancellation between them is expected.

## Method

The authors solved the 2PI effective action equations for the O(N) model in 1+1 dimensions at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO') in the 1/N expansion, using a lattice discretization with spatial spacing a and temporal spacing a_t/a=0.2. They extracted quasiparticle masses and widths from unequal-time two-point correlation functions at zero momentum, and compared these results to exact classical statistical field theory solutions obtained by numerically integrating the classical field equations with 2.5x10^5 initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant used: λ/m² = 30, where m is the renormalized mass in vacuum
- Lattice parameters: am = 0.5, volume Lm = 32
- Quasiparticle mass in the large N limit: M/m = 1.48 when N → ∞
- For N ≥ 10, NLO and NNLO' results are "practically indistinguishable"
- For N=4, classical theory comparisons used λ/m² = 30/n with n = 1, 2, 4, 8

## Context

This work extends the 2PI-1/N expansion approach developed by Berges (2004) and Aarts (2002) by presenting the first results beyond NLO in field theory, where previous studies had only used NLO truncations. The authors compare their results to classical statistical field theory solutions (Aarts 2000, Aarts 2001) where exact numerical results are available, and find that NLO truncations already give quantitatively accurate results for moderate N or coupling strength. This enables confidence in using NLO truncations for applications in inflationary preheating, prethermalization, and transport coefficients, though the authors note that the full NNLO calculation (including the 4-loop diagram) remains numerically prohibitive for larger times and higher dimensions.
