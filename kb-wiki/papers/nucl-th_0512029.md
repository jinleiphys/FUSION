# Role of isospin in the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition

> We study the thermodynamics of asymmetric nuclear matter using a mean field approximation with a Skyrme effective interaction, in order to establish its phase diagram and more particularly the influence of isospin on the order of the transition. A new statistical method is introduced to study the thermodynamics of a multifluid system, keeping only one density fixed the others being replaced by their intensive conjugated variables. In this ensemble phase coexistence reduces to a simple one dimensional Maxwell construction. For a fixed temperature under a critical value, a coexistence line is obtained in the plane of neutron and proton chemical potentials. Along this line the grand potential presents a discontinuous slope showing that the transition is first order except at the two ending points where it becomes second order. This result is not in contradiction with the already reported occurrence of a continuous transformation when a constant proton fraction is imposed. Indeed, the proton fraction being an order parameter in asymmetric matter, the constraint can only be fulfilled by gradual phase mixing along the first-order phase transition line leading to a continuous pressure.

# Role of isospin in the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that asymmetric nuclear matter exhibits a first-order liquid-gas phase transition when analyzed in the full space of thermodynamic variables, contrary to previous claims of a continuous transition based on constant proton-fraction transformations. The apparent continuous behavior arises because the proton fraction is an order parameter, and constraining it forces the system to follow the coexistence line, producing a continuous transformation that masks the underlying first-order discontinuity.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field approximation with the SLy230a Skyrme effective interaction to compute the thermodynamic properties of infinite nuclear matter at finite temperature. They introduce a new statistical ensemble (proton-canonical, neutron-grand-canonical) where only one density is kept fixed while the other is replaced by its intensive conjugate variable, reducing the multidimensional Gibbs construction to a one-dimensional Maxwell construction. Phase coexistence is then determined by finding the convex envelope of the thermodynamic potential as a function of proton density at fixed neutron chemical potential and temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter phase transitions using Skyrme forces (Barranco, Lattimer-PRC, Shlomo, Douchin) and addresses the confusion in the literature between "continuous transition" and "continuous transformation." The paper resolves an apparent contradiction with earlier work by Muller-Serot, Das-PRC, Qian, Sil, Srivastava, and Carmona, who reported continuous behavior when a constant proton fraction is imposed. The results enable a proper understanding of isospin distillation in asymmetric nuclear matter and have implications for neutron star physics and type-II supernova evolution, as previously discussed by Lattimer-PhysRep and Glendenning.
