# Novel Perspectives for Hadron Physics

> I discuss several novel and unexpected aspects of quantum chromodynamics. These include: (a) the nonperturbative origin of intrinsic strange, charm and bottom quarks in the nucleon at large light-cone momenta x; the breakdown of pQCD factorization theorems due to the lensing effects of initial- and final-state interactions; (b) important corrections to pQCD scaling for inclusive reactions due to processes in which hadrons are created at high transverse momentum directly in the hard processes and their relation to the baryon anomaly in high-centrality heavy-ion collisions; and (c) the nonuniversality of quark distributions in nuclei. I also discuss some novel theoretical perspectives in QCD: (a) light-front holography -- a relativistic, color-confining, first approximation to QCD based on the AdS/CFT correspondence principle; (b) the principle of maximum conformality -- a method which determines the renormalization scale at finite order in perturbation theory yielding scheme independent results; (c) the replacement of quark and gluon vacuum condensates by "in-hadron condensates" and how this helps to resolves the conflict between QCD vacuum and the cosmological constant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several standard assumptions of perturbative QCD (pQCD) factorization and parton-model universality fail in detail for many hard inclusive reactions, with the caveat that these breakdowns arise from nonperturbative initial- and final-state interactions (lensing effects) that are not power-law suppressed and thus persist at leading twist.

## Method

The author reviews a range of experimental anomalies and theoretical developments in QCD, including: the nonperturbative origin of intrinsic heavy-quark Fock states in the nucleon light-front wavefunction; the role of direct higher-twist subprocesses in high-transverse-momentum hadron production; the calculation of single-spin asymmetries (Sivers and Collins effects) via final-state gluon exchange; and the application of Gribov-Glauber theory to nuclear structure functions. The paper also presents the principle of maximum conformality (PMC) as a method to eliminate the renormalization-scale ambiguity in pQCD by absorbing all non-conformal \(\beta\)-function terms into the running coupling.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of intrinsic charm to intrinsic bottom scales as \(m_c^2/m_b^2 \simeq 1/10\), from the operator product expansion in non-Abelian QCD.
* The D0 collaboration measured \(\bar p p \to c + \gamma + X\) and \(\bar p p \to b + \gamma + X\) at photon transverse momentum \(p_T^\gamma \sim 120~{\rm GeV/c}\); the charm jet cross section deviates from NLO pQCD for \(p_T^\gamma > 60~{\rm GeV/c}\).
* The effective power-law fall-off \(n_{\rm eff}\) for inclusive hadron production at fixed \(x_T = 2 p_T/\sqrt s\) is measured to be \(\simeq 4.5\) for isolated photons and jets, but deviates significantly from the leading-twist prediction for hadrons.
* The nuclear \(A\) dependence of \(J/\psi\) hadroproduction shows \(\alpha(x_F)\) decreasing to \(\alpha = 2/3\) at \(x_F \sim 1\), as measured by E866/NuSea.
* Approximately 10% of deep inelastic lepton-proton scattering events at HERA are diffractive (DDIS).
* Nuclear antishadowing is observed in the domain \(0.1 < x < 0.2\) where \(R_A(x,Q^2) > 1\).

## Context

This paper extends the author's long-standing work on intrinsic heavy quarks (Brodsky 1980, 1984) and light-front holography, and it directly addresses the breakdown of standard pQCD factorization theorems as noted by Collins and Qiu (2007). The discussion of the Sivers effect and its nonuniversality between SIDIS and Drell-Yan reactions builds on Collins (2002) and Brodsky (2002). The PMC method is presented as a resolution to the renormalization-scale ambiguity problem in pQCD, and the distinction between static and dynamic structure functions (Brodsky 2009) reframes how nuclear shadowing and antishadowing should be interpreted. The work enables future tests of factorization breakdown at the LHC (e.g., \(\cos 2\phi\) correlations in dijet production) and motivates experimental confirmation of the SELEX doubly-charmed baryon results.
