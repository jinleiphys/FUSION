# Chiral condensate in hadronic matter

> The finite temperature chiral condensate for 2+1 quark flavors is considered in the framework of the hadron resonance gas model. This requires some dynamical information, for which two models are employed: one based on the quark structure of hadrons combined with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approach to chiral symmetry breaking, and one originating from gauge/gravity duality. Using these insights, hadronic sigma terms are discussed in the context of recent first principles results following from lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory. For the condensate, in generic agreement with lattice data it is found that chiral symmetry restoration in the strange quark sector takes place at higher temperatures than in the light quark sector. The importance of this result for a recently proposed dynamical model of hadronic freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, within the hadron resonance gas model, chiral symmetry restoration in the strange quark sector occurs at higher temperatures than in the light quark sector, in generic agreement with lattice QCD data; this result depends on the specific dynamical assumptions for hadron mass dependence on current quark masses, which are provided by two distinct models (a constituent quark counting model and a holographic model).

## Method

The authors compute the finite-temperature chiral condensate for 2+1 quark flavors using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, where the partition function is a sum over free hadronic degrees of freedom up to masses of approximately 2 GeV. The required sigma terms (derivatives of hadron masses with respect to current quark masses) are obtained from two approaches: a constituent quark picture based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the dynamical quark mass dependence, and baryon mass formulae from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model of QCD. These results are compared with leading-order chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as a baseline.

## Key numbers

* Light quark condensate in vacuum: \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle_0 = (-240)^3\) MeV\(^3\)
* Light quark mass: \(m_q = m_u = m_d \approx 5.5\) MeV
* Strange quark mass: \(m_s = 138\) MeV
* Ratio of strange to light quark condensates in vacuum: \(0.8 \pm 0.3\) (from QCD sum rules) or \(0.75 \pm 0.12\) (other estimates)
* Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV; kaon decay constant: \(f_K = 113\) MeV (giving \(f_K/f_\pi \approx 1.22\))
* Next-to-leading order ChPT parameter: \(\kappa = 0.021 \pm 0.008\)
* Nucleon sigma term from constituent quark model: \(\sigma_N = 37.5\) MeV
* Strange sigma term for \(\Lambda\) baryon from constituent quark model: \(\sigma_s^\Lambda = 252.9\) MeV
* Nucleon strange sigma term from next-to-leading order ChPT: \(\sigma_s^N \approx -4\) MeV; from next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order ChPT: \(\sigma_s^N \approx 130\) MeV; from lattice QCD with \(N_f=2+1\): \(\sigma_s^N = 49 \pm 25\) MeV
* Strangeness content of the nucleon: \(y \approx 0.21\)
* HRG model agrees with lattice QCD thermodynamics for temperatures up to approximately 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas approach to chiral condensates by incorporating dynamical information from two specific models: a constituent quark counting model generalizing the work of Leupold (2006) and Blaschke et al. (2011), and the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model (2004) with baryon mass formulae from Hashimoto et al. (2009). The results are compared with first-principles lattice QCD simulations (Borsanyi et al., 2010) and chiral perturbation theory (Martin Camalich et al., 2010; Ren et al., 2012). The findings enable improved dynamical models of hadronic freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions, where previously only the light quark condensate was considered (Blaschke et al., 2011).
