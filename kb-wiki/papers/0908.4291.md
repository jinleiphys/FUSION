# Reply to "Comment on `The polarizability of the pion: no conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory'"

> We reply to the Comment by Fil'kov and Kashevarov, arXiv:0805.4486 [hep-ph]. We show that the discrepancies between ChPT and dispersion theory, reported for the polarizability of the pion, result from applying dispersion theory to non-analytic functions.

# Reply to "Comment on `The polarizability of the pion: no conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory'"

## Key claim

The discrepancies between chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and dispersion theory predictions for pion polarizabilities arise not from a genuine physical conflict, but because the dispersion-theory treatment by Fil'kov and Kashevarov applies dispersion relations to non-analytic functions, specifically by introducing unphysical singularities via energy-dependent couplings and then improperly discarding the resulting unphysical cut.

## Method

The authors analyze the analytic structure of the Compton scattering amplitudes used by Fil'kov and Kashevarov, focusing on the vector-meson exchange contributions (ρ and ω) to the amplitudes M⁺⁻(s) and M⁺⁺(s). They evaluate the consequences of the energy-dependent coupling g(s)² ∼ 1/√s and the energy-dependent width Γ(s), showing that these introduce a square-root singularity at s=0, creating an unphysical cut. They demonstrate that Fil'kov and Kashevarov's procedure of setting the imaginary part to zero below the two-pion threshold (s < 4m²) while retaining the real part from the unphysical cut violates analyticity, as it introduces a non-analytic "wall" in the complex plane rather than the allowed isolated singularities.

## Key numbers

- ChPT at O(p⁴) predicts α_{π⁺} = 2.64 ± 0.09 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), based on the ratio F_A/F_V from radiative pion beta decay
- ChPT predictions for charged pion polarizabilities: (α + β)_{π⁺} = 0.16 ± 0.1, (α - β)_{π⁺} = 5.7 ± 1.0 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Dispersion-relation predictions from Fil'kov and Kashevarov: (α + β)_{π⁺} = 0.17 ± 0.02, (α - β)_{π⁺} = 13.60 ± 2.15 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Fil'kov and Kashevarov's ω contribution to (α - β)_{π⁰} is -12.56 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), compared to the ChPT estimate of approximately -0.7 from the ω
- The ratio R = (α - β)/(α + β) from a proper magnetic dipole transition should be -1, but Fil'kov and Kashevarov obtain R ≈ -20

## Context

This reply extends the debate between ChPT and dispersion-theory approaches to pion polarizabilities, specifically addressing the work of Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005, 2009). The ChPT predictions at O(p⁴) were established by Bijnens (1987) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1983), with O(p⁶) corrections calculated by Burgi (1996) and Gasser (2006). The authors argue that the large vector-meson contributions reported by Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005) are artifacts of applying dispersion relations to non-analytic functions, and that when treated correctly, the ChPT and dispersion-theory results should be consistent. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of the proper analytic structure required for dispersion-relation calculations of polarizabilities.
