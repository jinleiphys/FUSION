# Isospin asymmetric nuclear matter and properties of axisymmetric neutron stars

> Pure hadronic compact stars, above a limiting value ($\approx$1.6 M$_\odot$) of their gravitational masses, to which predictions of most of other equations of state (EoSs) are restricted, can be reached from the equation of state (EoS) obtained using DDM3Y effective interaction. This effective interaction is found to be quite successful in providing unified description of elastic and inelastic scattering, various radioactivities and nuclear matter properties. We present a systematic study of the properties of pure hadronic compact stars. The $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter using this EoS with a thin crust is able to describe highly-massive compact stars, such as PSR B1516+02B with a mass M=1.94$^{+0.17}_{-0.19}$ M$_\odot$ and PSR J0751+1807 with a mass M=2.1$\pm$0.2 M$_\odot$ to a 1$σ$ confidence level.

## Key claim

The DDM3Y effective interaction yields an equation of state that can support pure hadronic neutron stars with gravitational masses above approximately 1.6 M☉, a regime where most other equations of state fail, but only if the nuclear symmetry energy is "super-soft" (peaking near twice saturation density and becoming negative at higher densities) while the symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility remains stiff at 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to compute the energy per nucleon for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, from which they derived the equation of state for β-equilibrated charge-neutral neutron star matter. They solved the Einstein field equations for both static and rotating stellar structures using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu method implemented in the "rns" code, assuming a perfect fluid interior with a thin crust.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³, with corresponding saturation energy per nucleon ε₀ = -15.26 MeV and total mass-energy density ε₀ = 2.524 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ = 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: 30.71 ± 0.26 MeV
- Maximum static neutron star mass: 1.92 M☉ with radius ≈ 9.7 km
- Maximum rotating neutron star mass (Keplerian limit): 2.27 M☉ with radius ≈ 13.1 km
- Maximum β-equilibrium proton fraction: 4.4%, which does not allow direct URCA cooling
- Symmetry energy peaks at ρ ≈ 1.95 ρ₀ and becomes negative at ρ ≈ 4.7 ρ₀
- The EoS reproduces the mass of PSR B1516+02B (M = 1.94⁺⁰·¹⁷₋₀·¹⁹ M☉ at 1σ) and PSR J0751+1807 (M = 2.1 ± 0.2 M☉ at 1σ)

## Context

This work extends the DDM3Y interaction framework, previously successful for elastic/inelastic scattering and nuclear radioactivity (Basu 2002, Chowdhury 2005, 2006, 2007), to the study of massive neutron stars. It contrasts with relativistic mean-field models such as DD-F, KVOR, and DBHF (Klahn 2006), which predict symmetry energies that increase monotonically with density; the present "super-soft" symmetry energy is instead consistent with FOPI/GSI data on π⁺/π⁻ ratios in heavy-ion collisions (Wei 2009, Zhang 2009). The results enable future tests of r-mode instability limits and cooling mechanisms in rapidly rotating pulsars.
