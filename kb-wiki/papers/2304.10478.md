# Real and Imaginary Phase Shifts for Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering using Phase Function Method

> The neutron-deuteron (nd) and proton-deuteron (pd) scattering are the simplest nucleon-nucleus scenario which throws light on understanding few body systems. In this work, real and imaginary parts of scattering phase shifts (SPS) for nd and pd scattering are obtained using optical potential, with Malfliet-Tjon (MT) model of interaction, by phase function method (PFM). The SPS for doublet 2S1/2 and quartet 4S3/2 states of nd and pd systems have been obtained for real and imaginary parts separately by solving the phase equation for l= 0, using Runge-Kutta 5 th order technique for laboratory energies up to 19 M eV . The obtained (real,imaginary) SPS for 2S1/2 and 4S3/2 states are matching with standard data with mean absolute error (MAE) of (1.32, 0.06) for 2S1/2 state and (0.19, 0.06) for 4S3/2 state of nd scattering, (1.47,0.62) for 2S1/2 state and (0.55, 0.14) for 4S3/2 state of pd scattering.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the phase function method (PFM) with a Malfliet-Tjon (MT) optical potential can reproduce both real and imaginary scattering phase shifts for nucleon-deuteron systems up to 19 MeV, with mean absolute errors (MAE) below 2 degrees for all channels, though discrepancies of about 5 degrees appear at the highest energy (19 MeV) for the nd system.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon-deuteron interaction using an optical potential whose real and imaginary parts both take the Malfliet-Tjon form (attractive and repulsive Yukawa terms). For the pd system, a Coulomb potential with an error-function cutoff is added. The phase equation (a first-order Riccati-type equation derived from the radial Schrodinger equation for l=0) is solved separately for the real and imaginary parts using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta method, from the origin to the asymptotic region, for laboratory energies from 1 to 19 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   For nd scattering, the MAE for the real and imaginary parts of the phase shift is (1.32, 0.06) degrees for the 2S1/2 state and (0.19, 0.06) degrees for the 4S3/2 state.
*   For pd scattering, the MAE for the real and imaginary parts of the phase shift is (1.47, 0.62) degrees for the 2S1/2 state and (0.55, 0.14) degrees for the 4S3/2 state.
*   The scattering length and effective range for the nd system are (1.86 fm, 2.83 fm) for the 2S1/2 state and (6.31 fm, 2.35 fm) for the 4S3/2 state.
*   The scattering length and effective range for the pd system are (1.54 fm, 3.92 fm) for the 2S1/2 state and (11.25 fm, 1.56 fm) for the 4S3/2 state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that obtained only real phase shifts for nd and pd systems below the deuteron breakup threshold of 3 MeV, such as those by Chen et al. and the Pisa group (Viviani). It also builds on the authors' prior work using a Morse potential for the doublet state. By using the MT potential in an optical model framework, the method reproduces the standard phase shift data of Huber for nd and Arvieux for pd up to 19 MeV, including the imaginary parts that become significant above the breakup threshold. This enables a simpler, wavefunction-free calculation of phase shifts for few-body systems at energies where absorption is important.
