# Diffractive neutrino-production of pions on nuclei: Adler relation within the color-dipole description

> Effects of coherence in neutrino-production of pions off nuclei are studied employing the color dipole representation and path integral technique. If the nucleus remains intact, the process is controlled by the interplay of two length scales. One is related to the pion mass and is quite long (at low Q^2), while the other, associated with heavy axial-vector states, is much shorter. The Adler relation is found to be broken at all energies, but especially strongly at ν> 10 GeV, where the cross section is suppressed by a factor ~A^{-1/3}. On the contrary, in a process where the recoil nucleus breaks up into fragments, the Adler relation turns out to be strongly broken at low energies, where the cross section is enhanced by a factor ~A^{1/3}, but has a reasonable accuracy at higher energies, where all the coherence length scales become long.

## Key claim

The Adler relation for diffractive neutrino-production of pions on nuclei is broken at all energies, not just at high energies as previously thought; even in the intermediate energy range (few hundred MeV to about 10 GeV) where a simple two-channel model predicted the Adler relation would hold, the more detailed color-dipole approach finds a considerable suppression of the nuclear cross section relative to the Adler relation prediction, due to the contribution of light dipoles with longer coherence lengths.

## Method

The authors employed the color-dipole representation combined with the light-cone Green function technique and the Glauber-Gribov approach to calculate nuclear effects in coherent (nucleus remains intact) and incoherent (nucleus breaks up) diffractive neutrino-production of pions. The propagation of a quark-antiquark dipole through the nuclear medium was described as motion in an absorptive potential, with the Green function solved numerically to account for dipole size fluctuations during propagation. The dipole scattering amplitude was modeled using phenomenological functions fitted to DIS, real photoproduction, and pion-proton scattering data, and the calculations were performed in the kinematics of the MINERvA experiment.

## Key numbers

- At neutrino energies ν > 10 GeV, the coherent cross section on nuclei is suppressed by a factor ~A^{-1/3} compared to the Adler relation prediction.
- For incoherent production at low energies, the cross section is enhanced by a factor ~A^{1/3} relative to the Adler relation.
- Gluon shadowing gives a correction of only a few percent for energies ν < 100 GeV on lead (A=208), and can be safely neglected.
- The coherence length for pion production is l_c^π = 2ν/(Q^2 + m_π^2), and for axial-vector states (effective a-pole with mass ~1 GeV) is l_c^a = 2ν/(Q^2 + m_a^2).
- The Fermi coupling constant used is G_F = 1.166 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-channel model results (Kopeliovich, Schmidt, Siddikov, 2011) to a full color-dipole description, and extends their earlier neutrino-proton calculations (Kopeliovich, Schmidt, Siddikov, 2011) to nuclear targets. It contradicts earlier calculations by Rein (1982) which were based on an incorrect model for nuclear effects, and improves upon the Glauber eikonal calculations by Belkov and collaborators that relied on the Adler relation. The results enable more accurate predictions for ongoing experiments like MINERvA at Fermilab, and demonstrate that the Adler relation cannot be reliably used for nuclear targets even at moderate energies where it was previously assumed valid.
