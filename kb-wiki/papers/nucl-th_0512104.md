# Hadronization in nuclear DIS and ultra-relativistic HIC

> We present a transport theoretical analysis of hadron attenuation in deep inelastic lepton scattering (DIS) off complex nuclei in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment. The HERMES data indicate the presence of strong prehadronic final state interactions shortly after the elementary lepton-nucleon interaction. The contribution of such (pre-)hadronic final state interactions to the observed jet quenching in ultra-relaticistic heavy ion collisions (HIC) at RHIC is estimated under the assumption that a similar space-time picture for hadronization also holds in a hot hadronic medium. Our results show that an additional mechanism for jet quenching must be at work for most central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that prehadronic final state interactions (color-neutral prehadrons rescattering) are the predominant source of hadron attenuation in cold nuclear matter in deep inelastic scattering, but that an additional mechanism for jet quenching must be at work for most central heavy ion collisions at RHIC, beyond what prehadronic rescattering alone can explain.

## Method

The authors split lepton-nucleus interactions into two steps: first, the virtual photon produces a final state X determined using the Lund Monte Carlo generators PYTHIA and FRITIOF, accounting for nuclear binding energies, Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and coherence length effects; second, the state X is propagated through the nuclear target using a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that allows coupled-channel description of particle creation, annihilation, and elastic scattering. For heavy ion collisions, the same hadronization picture is applied with the additional assumption that prehadrons do not form at energy densities larger than 1 GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity ratio \(R_M^h\) for charged hadron production on a \(^{84}\)Kr target is compared with HERMES data at beam energy \(E_\text{beam}=27.5\) GeV; the model shows perfect agreement with experimental findings.
- A universal formation proper time \(\tau_f=0.5\) fm/c is assumed in the rest frame of each prehadron, with formation time in the nuclear rest frame given by \(t_f = (E_h/m_h)\tau_f\).
- Prehadronic cross sections are scaled from hadronic cross sections: \(\sigma^*_\text{prebaryon} = (n_\text{org}/3)\sigma_\text{baryon}\) and \(\sigma^*_\text{premeson} = (n_\text{org}/2)\sigma_\text{meson}\), where \(n_\text{org}\) is the number of leading quarks.
- The model underestimates jet suppression for most central heavy ion collisions at RHIC, as shown by the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\rm AA}(p_T)\) compared to PHENIX and STAR data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous transport model approach (Falter 2004) that successfully described HERMES data at 12 GeV beam energy, and applies the same space-time hadronization picture to heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The results contradict the possibility that prehadronic final state interactions alone explain jet quenching in central collisions, supporting the need for an additional mechanism such as a quark gluon plasma liquid (Andre). The analysis enables future studies of hadron attenuation in the kinematic regime of Jefferson Lab experiments.
