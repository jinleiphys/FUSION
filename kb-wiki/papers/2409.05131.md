# Relaxation time approximation revisited and non-analytical structure in retarded correlators

> In this paper, we give a rigorous mathematical justification for the relaxation time approximation (RTA) model. We find that only the RTA with an energy-independent relaxation time can be justified in the case of hard interactions. Accordingly, we propose an alternative approach to restore the collision invariance lacking in traditional RTA. Besides, we provide a general statement on the non-analytical structures in the retarded correlators within the kinetic description. For hard interactions, hydrodynamic poles are the long-lived modes. Whereas for soft interactions, commonly encountered in relativistic kinetic theory, the gapless eigenvalue spectrum of linearized collision operator leads to gapless branch-cuts. We note that particle mass and inhomogeneous perturbations would complicate the above-mentioned non-analytical structures.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous mathematical justification for the relaxation time approximation (RTA) and finds that only the RTA with an energy-independent relaxation time can be justified, and only in the case of hard interactions; for soft interactions or energy-dependent relaxation times, the approximation lacks a well-defined foundation.

## Method

The authors analyze the linearized Boltzmann equation and its collision operator, examining the eigenvalue spectrum structure of the redefined linearized collision operator \(\mathcal{L}_\alpha = E_p^\alpha \mathcal{L}_0\) for different values of the energy-dependence parameter \(\alpha\). They compare the full eigenfunction expansion of the linearized Boltzmann equation with the truncated form corresponding to the RTA, evaluating whether a gap exists between zero and nonzero eigenvalues that would justify replacing the full operator with a single relaxation time. The analysis covers hard interactions (gapped spectrum), soft interactions (gapless spectrum), and the cases of positive, zero, and negative energy-dependence parameter \(\alpha\).

## Key numbers

- For the AW RTA (\(\alpha = 0\)), the relaxation time \(\tau_R\) is identified as the inverse of the smallest nonzero eigenvalue \(\gamma_6\) of \(\mathcal{L}_0\), with the mode \(\psi_6\) persisting until the late stage of evolution.
- For \(\alpha = 1\) in Eq. (tauR), the model becomes \(p\cdot\partial f = -\frac{1}{\beta t_R}(f - f_0)\), corresponding to the energy-dependent RTA used in Kurkela (2017) and Denicol (2022).
- For \(\alpha < 0\) with massive particles, the relaxation timescale is bounded from above by \(\frac{m^\alpha}{\gamma'_6}\), but this reduces to the AW RTA case and does not introduce a new class of RTAs.
- For massless particles with \(\alpha < 0\), the quantity \(\frac{E_p^\alpha}{\gamma'_6}\) is unbounded, so a well-defined RTA is lacking.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Anderson-Witting (AW) model (1974) and the BGK model (Bhatnagar, Gross, and Krook) to clarify the mathematical conditions under which the RTA is justified within kinetic theory. It directly addresses the "poles or cuts?" dilemma raised by Romatschke (2015) and Hartnoll (2005) regarding non-analytical structures in retarded correlators, and it provides a rigorous basis for the phenomenological energy-dependent RTA parameterizations used by Dusling (2009, 2011) and Kurkela (2017). The results enable a clearer understanding of when hydrodynamic descriptions emerge from kinetic theory and when non-hydrodynamic branch-cuts dominate the linear response.
