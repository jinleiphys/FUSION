# Debye screening in the hot non-Abelian plasma

## Key claim

The Debye mass in the deconfined phase of QCD is determined nonperturbatively by chromomagnetic confinement, yielding the relation m_D = c_D sqrt(sigma_s(T)) with c_D approximately 2, where sigma_s(T) is the spatial string tension; this result is the leading term in a background perturbation theory expansion and agrees with lattice data without free parameters, though the authors note the accuracy of the first approximation is around 10 percent and the temperature region near T_c requires additional care.

## Method

The authors use the Field Correlator Method and Background Perturbation Theory at nonzero temperature to compute the thermal Wilson loop for a static quark-antiquark pair in the singlet color state. They separate the gauge field into a nonperturbative background field and a valence gluon field, expand the Wilson loop in powers of the valence field, and express the resulting gluon Green's function as a gluelump Green's function where the adjoint source trajectory is connected by a string to the Wilson loop plane. The lowest eigenvalue of the gluelump Hamiltonian yields the screened Coulomb potential and the Debye mass.

## Key numbers

- m_D = c_D sqrt(sigma_s(T)) with c_D approximately 2.06 from the gluelump calculation, and c_D = 2.09 used for the numerical comparison with lattice data.
- The adjoint spatial string tension for SU(3) is sigma_adj^H = (9/4) sigma_s, where sigma_s is the fundamental spatial string tension.
- For pure glue QCD, the spatial string tension parameters are c_sigma = 0.566 plus or minus 0.013 and L_sigma = 0.104 plus or minus 0.009, where sqrt(sigma_s(T)) = c_sigma g^2(T) T.
- The gluon correlation length lambda_g is approximately 0.2 fm.
- The temperature of dimensional reduction T_dr is approximately (1.2 to 1.5) T_c.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative approach to Debye screening beyond the perturbative leading-order result m_D^(LO) = (N_c/3 + N_f/6)^(1/2) gT from the 1980s, which suffers from gauge-invariance issues and fails to describe lattice data. The authors compare their results to lattice calculations by Kaczmarek and Zantow (2005) for both quenched (N_f=0) and 2-flavor QCD, showing that their prediction m_D = c_D sqrt(sigma_s(T)) agrees with lattice data in the temperature interval T_c less than T less than or equal to 5 T_c, with slightly better agreement than a perturbative-like ansatz that requires a fitting constant A_Nf approximately 1.5. The approach enables gauge-invariant nonperturbative calculations of screening masses in hot QCD and can be extended to include contributions from chromoelectric fields above T_c.
