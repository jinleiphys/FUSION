# Joint lattice QCD - dispersion theory analysis confirms the quark-mixing top-row unitarity deficit

> Recently, the first ever lattice computation of the $γW$-box radiative correction to the rate of the semileptonic pion decay allowed for a reduction of the theory uncertainty of that rate by a factor of $\sim3$. A recent dispersion evaluation of the $γW$-box correction on the neutron also led to a significant reduction of the theory uncertainty, but shifted the value of $V_{ud}$ extracted from the neutron and superallowed nuclear $β$ decay, resulting in a deficit of the CKM unitarity in the top row. A direct lattice computation of the $γW$-box correction for the neutron decay would provide an independent cross-check for this result but is very challenging. Before those challenges are overcome, we propose a hybrid analysis, converting the lattice calculation on the pion to that on the neutron by a combination of dispersion theory and phenomenological input. The new prediction for the universal radiative correction to free and bound neutron $β$-decay reads $Δ_R^V=0.02477(24)$, in excellent agreement with the dispersion theory result $Δ_R^V=0.02467(22)$. Combining with other relevant information, the top-row CKM unitarity deficit persists.

# Joint lattice QCD - dispersion theory analysis confirms the quark-mixing top-row unitarity deficit

## Key claim

The authors propose a hybrid analysis that converts a lattice QCD calculation of the γW-box radiative correction on the pion to the corresponding correction on the neutron using dispersion theory and phenomenological input, finding that the top-row CKM unitarity deficit persists at the level of 1.7-5σ depending on whether newly identified nuclear corrections are included.

## Method

The authors parametrize the parity-odd structure function F₃ for both the pion and neutron, separating contributions into process-specific parts (elastic, resonance, low-energy continuum) and universal asymptotic parts (Regge and perturbative QCD). They use the lattice QCD result for the pion's Nachtmann moment M₃π⁽⁰⁾(1,Q²) from Feng et al. (2020) to fix the Regge contribution for the pion, then apply Regge factorization to relate this to the neutron's Regge contribution, using ππ and πN scattering data to determine the rescaling factor Rπ/N. The resonance contribution to the pion is subtracted using effective Lagrangians with couplings from measured decay widths, and the resulting neutron Regge contribution is matched to CCFR neutrino scattering data at Q₀² = 2 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The new prediction for the universal radiative correction to free and bound neutron β-decay is Δ_R^V = 0.02477(24), shifted upward from the previous dispersion theory result of 0.02467(22)
- The Regge contribution to δ_γW,N^VA is determined as 1.12(16)_a(9)_b(3)_c × 10⁻³, in agreement with the previous determination of 1.02(16) × 10⁻³ from Seng et al. (2018)
- From superallowed nuclear β decay with nuclear corrections included: |V_ud| = 0.97366(33)
- From neutron β decay: |V_ud| = 0.97297(58) using τ_n = 879.7(8) s and λ = -1.27641(56)
- The top-row CKM unitarity deficit Δ_CKM^u ranges from -0.0012(4) to -0.0021(7) depending on the choice of V_us measurement and inclusion of nuclear corrections

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of the γW-box radiative correction by Seng et al. (2018) and Gorchtein et al. (2018), which first identified the CKM unitarity deficit, by incorporating the first-ever lattice QCD calculation of this correction on the pion by Feng et al. (2020). The analysis contradicts the lower estimate Δ_R^V = 0.02426(32) from Czarnecki et al. (2019), which assumed perturbative behavior down to Q² = 1 GeV²; the lattice data show non-negligible higher-twist effects already below Q² = 2 GeV². This work enables a future direct lattice calculation of the γW-box on the neutron to provide an independent cross-check, after which the emphasis should shift to reassessing nuclear structure corrections in superallowed nuclear β decay.
