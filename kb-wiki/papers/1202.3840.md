# Vector- and Pseudoscalar-baryon coupled channel systems

> In this manuscript, I will report the details of our recent work on the vector meson-baryon (VB) interaction, which we studied with the motivation of finding dynamical generation of resonances in the corresponding systems. We started our study by building a formalism based on the hidden local symmetry and calculating the leading order contributions to the scattering equations by summing the diagrams with: (a) a vector meson exchange in the t-channel (b) an octet baryon exchange in the s-, u-channels and (c) a contact interaction arising from the part of the vector meson-baryon Lagrangian which is related to the anomalous magnetic moment of the baryons. We find the contribution from all these sources, except the s-channel, to be important. The amplitudes obtained by solving the coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equations for the systems with total strangeness zero, show generation of one isospin 3/2, spin 1/2 resonance and three isospin 1/2 resonances: two with spin 3/2 and one with spin 1/2. We identify these resonances with $Δ$ (1900) $S_{31}$, $N^*$(2080) $D_{13}$, $N^*$(1700) $D_{13}$, and $N^*$(2090) $S_{11}$, respectively. We have further extended our study by including pseudoscalar meson-baryon (PB) as the coupled channels of VB systems. For this, we obtain the PB $\rightarrow$ VB amplitudes by using the Kroll-Ruddermann term where, considering the vector meson dominance phenomena, the photon is replaced by a vector meson. The calculations done within this formalism reveal a very strong coupling of the VB channels to the low-lying resonances like $Λ$(1405) and $Λ$(1670), which can have important implications on certain reactions producing them. In addition to this, we find that the effect of coupling the higher mass states to the lighter channels is not restricted to increasing the width of those states, it can be far more strong.

# Vector- and Pseudoscalar-baryon coupled channel systems

## Key claim
Dynamical generation of baryon resonances from vector meson-baryon (VB) interactions requires including all leading-order diagrams (t-channel vector exchange, u-channel baryon exchange, and a contact term from the anomalous magnetic moment) because the s-channel contribution is negligible; the spin degeneracy of the t-channel alone is lifted by the spin-dependent contact and u-channel terms. Coupling pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) channels to VB systems reveals that low-lying resonances like Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) develop very strong couplings to heavy, closed VB channels, though their pole positions and PB couplings change only modestly.

## Method
The authors built a gauge-invariant Lagrangian based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) for vector meson-baryon interactions, generalized to SU(3) with D = 2.4 and F = 0.82. They calculated tree-level amplitudes from four diagrams: t-channel vector exchange, s- and u-channel octet baryon exchange, and a contact interaction from the [ρμ, ρν] term of the vector tensor. These amplitudes were used as kernels in coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations for total strangeness zero systems (ρN, ωN, φN, K*Σ, K*Λ). For PB-VB coupling, they used the Kroll-Ruderman term with vector meson dominance to replace the photon by a vector meson, obtaining PB → VB amplitudes with parameters F̃ = 0.46 and D̃ = 0.8.

## Key numbers
- Pole found at 1637 − i35 MeV in spin 3/2, isospin 1/2 VB amplitude, identified with N*(1700) D13
- Pole found at 2071 − i70 MeV in spin 3/2, isospin 1/2 amplitude, identified with N*(2080) D13
- Pole found at 1977 − i22 MeV in spin 1/2, isospin 1/2 amplitude, with no exact PDG match but relevant to φ photoproduction near 2 GeV
- Pole found at 2006 − i112 MeV in isospin 3/2, spin 1/2 amplitude, identified with Δ(1900) S31
- For Λ(1405) two-pole structure: pole1 at 1377 − i63 MeV (uncoupled) shifts to 1357 − i53 MeV when PB-VB coupled; pole2 at 1430 − i15 MeV shifts to 1412 − i11 MeV
- Λ(1670) pole at 1767 − i25 MeV (uncoupled) shifts to 1744 − i28 MeV when coupled
- Coupling of Λ(1405) pole2 to K̅*N becomes −0.1 − i5.3 when PB-VB coupled (was 0.0 + i0.0 uncoupled)
- Coupling of Λ(1405) pole1 to ρΣ becomes 1.3 + i6.8 when coupled (was 0.0 + i0.0 uncoupled)
- Pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV used in PB-VB Lagrangian
- Anomalous magnetic coupling κρ ≈ 3.22 for ρNN vertex

## Context
This work extends previous studies of dynamically generated baryon resonances in pseudoscalar-baryon systems (e.g., the works of Jido et al. and the chiral unitary approach) by including vector meson-baryon channels with a full HLS-based formalism. It builds on earlier phenomenological VB studies by the Juelich group, Lutz, and others, and on the hidden local symmetry framework of Bando et al. The finding that low-lying Λ resonances couple strongly to VB channels has implications for photoproduction reactions (e.g., the work of Nam et al.) and for understanding the composition of these states. The results enable future coupled-channel calculations that include both PB and VB interactions with more complete VB amplitudes, and motivate experimental studies of reactions producing these resonances.
