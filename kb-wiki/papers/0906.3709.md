# Baryon Resonance Analysis from SAID

> We discuss the analysis of data from piN elastic scattering and single pion photo- and electroproduction. The main focus is a study of low-lying non-strange baryon resonances. Here we concentrate on some difficulties associated with resonance identification, in particular the Roper and higher P11 states.

# Baryon Resonance Analysis from SAID

## Key claim

The SAID partial-wave analysis (PWA) of pion-nucleon elastic scattering and single-pion photo- and electroproduction data reveals that the Roper N(1440) resonance and higher P11 states have a more complicated pole structure than a simple Breit-Wigner parametrization can capture, and that several resonances reported in the Review of Particle Physics listings are not supported by the current, larger database.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis of piN elastic scattering data up to center-of-mass energy W = 2.50 GeV and pi- p -> eta n data up to W = 1.63 GeV, using a K-matrix formalism for photoproduction multipoles anchored to the piN partial-wave amplitudes. Resonances were identified by searching for poles in the complex energy plane arising from enforced quasi-two-body unitarity cuts, rather than by inserting Breit-Wigner forms by hand. A separate Breit-Wigner parametrization was also performed by mapping chi-squared as a function of resonance mass and width while fitting data over narrow energy ranges of 100-200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The piN elastic scattering database used in the SP06 solution contains 27,136 data points for pi+ p -> pi+ p, 22,632 for pi- p -> pi- p, and 6,394 for pi- p -> pi0 n, with chi-squared per data point values of 2.0/6.1, 1.9/6.2, and 2.0/4.0 respectively when normalization factors are included
- The pi- p -> eta n reaction has only 106 data points above 800 MeV and no polarized measurements below 1040 MeV, with the SP06 fit giving chi-squared per data point of 2.5/9.6
- The standard PWA can reveal resonances with widths of order 100 MeV but misses narrow resonances with Gamma < 30 MeV and cannot reliably extract resonances with Gamma > 500 MeV or branching ratios below 4%
- A candidate narrow P11 state at 1680 MeV was found with a partial width Gamma_{piN} < 0.5 MeV
- The total pion electroproduction database contains 124,453 data points (55,766 for gamma* p -> pi0 p and 51,312 for gamma* p -> pi+ n) with a total chi-squared of 178,663
- The combined photo- and electroproduction database totals 159,341 data points with chi-squared 229,317; the full piN database totals 191,217 data points with chi-squared 286,572

## Context

This work extends the SAID/GW/VPI series of partial-wave analyses (SP06, FA02) and compares with the Karlsruhe KA84 solution, as well as with the EBAC, Giessen, DMT, and Julich groups' analyses. The paper highlights that the piN database has increased by a factor of 3-4 over the last 25 years since the KH and CMB analyses, and that double-polarization measurements have revealed discrepancies in those earlier fits, weakening claims for some weaker isospin-3/2 resonances. The results enable future studies of resonance couplings at high Q^2 with JLab CLAS12, and emphasize the need for neutron-target measurements and spin observables to resolve ambiguities in the resonance content, particularly for the Roper and higher P11 states.
