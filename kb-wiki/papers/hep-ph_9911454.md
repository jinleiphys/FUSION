# Light Quark Masses 99

> I give a short historical and a critical review of the determinations of light quark masses from QCD at dawn of the next millennium. QCD spectral sum rules combined with ChPT give, to order $α_s^3$, the world average for the running masses: $\bar{m}_s$(2 GeV)= $(118.9\pm 12.2)$ MeV, $\bar{m}_d(2~{GeV})=(6.3\pm 0.8)$ MeV, $\bar{m}_u(2~{GeV})=(3.5\pm 0.4)$ MeV and the corresponding values of the invariant masses given in Eq. 24. Lower and upper bounds derived from the positivity of spectral moments are presented in Tables 2 and 3. For a comparison, we critically review the recent lattice results (section 8 and Table 5) and attempt to deduce the present {\it QCD grand average} determination (Table 6): $\bar{m}_s$(2 GeV)= $(110.9\pm 8.8)$ MeV, to be used with a great care. Then, we deduce the value: $B_6^{1/2}-0.45(\rm resp.~0.32)B_8^{3/2}\simeq 1.6\pm 0.4~(\rm resp.~1.1\pm 0.3)$ and the lower bound $1.1\pm 0.2$ (resp. $0.7\pm 0.1$), for the combination of the penguin operators, governing the CP-violating parameters $ε'/ε$ without (resp. with) the inclusion of the final state interaction effects. The result signals a possible deviation from the leading 1/N prediction by about $1\sim 3σ$, which should be tested using accurate non-perturbative calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a critical review of light quark mass determinations from QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) combined with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), yielding world average running masses at order α_s^3, but cautions that the "QCD grand average" value for the strange quark mass of 110.9 ± 8.8 MeV should be used "with a great care" due to systematic uncertainties in the different methods.

## Method

The author reviews determinations of light quark masses using QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) applied to two-point correlators of pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector currents, combined with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for mass ratios. The analysis uses Laplace sum rules, τ-like sum rules, and finite energy sum rules (FESR), with spectral functions parametrized using experimental data from e+e- annihilation, τ-decay, and hadronic decays, including contributions from the tachyonic gluon mass and vacuum condensates up to dimension 6. The results are compared critically with lattice QCD determinations from various groups, and a final "QCD grand average" is attempted by combining QSSR, ChPT, and lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Running strange quark mass at 2 GeV from QSSR+ChPT world average: m̄_s(2 GeV) = 118.9 ± 12.2 MeV
- Running down quark mass at 2 GeV: m̄_d(2 GeV) = 6.3 ± 0.8 MeV
- Running up quark mass at 2 GeV: m̄_u(2 GeV) = 3.5 ± 0.4 MeV
- QCD grand average for strange quark mass at 2 GeV (from Table 6): m̄_s(2 GeV) = 110.9 ± 8.8 MeV
- Ratio m_u/m_d = 0.553 ± 0.043 from ChPT
- Ratio m_s/m_d = 18.9 ± 0.8 from ChPT
- Ratio 2m_s/(m_d+m_u) = 24.4 ± 1.5 from ChPT
- Lower bound on m̄_s(2 GeV) from kaon sum rules: 100 MeV (LRT97) and 104 MeV (Y97)
- Upper bound on m̄_s(2 GeV) from e+e- + τ-decay: 147 ± 21 MeV (SN99)
- Invariant masses: m̂_u = 3.9 ± 0.7 MeV, m̂_d = 7.1 ± 0.8 MeV, m̂_s = 133.3 ± 18.8 MeV
- Combination of penguin operators B_6^{1/2} - 0.45 B_8^{3/2} ≈ 1.6 ± 0.4 (without final state interactions) and 1.1 ± 0.3 (with final state interactions)

## Context

This review extends the long history of light quark mass determinations from QCD sum rules initiated by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and from chiral perturbation theory by Weinberg (1977) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1982). The paper critically compares QSSR results with contemporary lattice determinations from groups such as APE, LANL, and FNAL, and attempts to produce a combined "QCD grand average." The results enable improved predictions for the CP-violating parameter ε'/ε in kaon decays and test the leading 1/N expansion prediction, showing a possible deviation of 1-3σ that requires more accurate non-perturbative calculations.
