# Non-equilibrium quantum plasmas in scalar QED: photon production, magnetic and Debye masses, and conductivity

> We study the generation of a non-equilibrium plasma in scalar QED with N charged scalar fields through spinodal instabilities in the case of a super cooled second order phase transition and parametric amplification when the order parameter oscillates with large amplitude around the minimum of the potential.The focus is to study the non-equilibrium electromagnetic properties of the plasma, such as photon production, electric and magnetic screening and conductivity. A novel kinetic equation is introduced to compute photon production far away from equilibrium in the large N limit and lowest order in the electromagnetic coupling.During the early stages of the dynamics the photon density grows exponentially and asymptotically the amplitude and frequency distribution becomes \sim alpha m^2/[lambda^2 ω^3] with lambda the scalar self-coupling and m the scalar mass.In the case of a phase transition,electric and magnetic fields are correlated on distances xi(t) \sim sqrt{t} during the early stages and the power spectrum is peaked at low momentum. Magnetic and Debye screening masses are defined out of equilibrium. The magnetic mass vanishes out of equilibrium in this abelian models. The Debye mass turns to be m^2_{Deb} \sim alpha m^2/lambda for a supercooled phase transition while in the case of an oscillating order parameter an interpolating time dependent Debye mass grows as alpha sqrt{m t}/lambda due to a non-linear resonance at low momentum in the charged particle distribution. The build-up of the transverse electric conductivity is studied during the formation of the non-equilibrium plasma. Its long wavelength limit reaches a value sigma_{k\approx 0} \sim alpha m/lambda at the end of the stage of linear instabilities. Its asymptotic form in terms of the non-equilibrium particle distribution functions is derived.

## Key claim

The paper studies non-equilibrium quantum plasmas formed via spinodal instabilities (supercooled second-order phase transition) or parametric amplification (oscillating order parameter) in scalar QED, finding that the magnetic mass vanishes out of equilibrium in this abelian model while the Debye mass and transverse conductivity reach finite values determined by the scalar self-coupling and electromagnetic coupling, with the caveat that the Debye mass diverges asymptotically in the parametric amplification case due to a singular low-momentum charged particle distribution.

## Method

The authors analyze scalar QED with N charged scalar fields coupled to a U(1) photon field and a neutral order parameter field, using the large N limit to treat the non-perturbative scalar dynamics and lowest-order perturbation theory in the electromagnetic coupling. They derive a novel kinetic equation for photon production far from equilibrium and compute the non-equilibrium Green's functions for the charged scalar fields, from which they extract the photon production spectrum, magnetic and Debye screening masses via their definitions as generalizations of the equilibrium case, and the transverse electrical conductivity via Kubo's linear response formalism.

## Key numbers

- Photon distribution asymptotically: N_ph(omega) ~ alpha m^2 / [lambda^2 omega^3], where alpha is the electromagnetic coupling, lambda the scalar self-coupling, and m the scalar mass
- Correlation length for electric and magnetic fields during early stages of phase transition: xi(t) ~ sqrt(t/m)
- Debye mass for supercooled phase transition: m^2_Deb ~ alpha m^2 / lambda
- Debye mass for oscillating order parameter (time-dependent): grows as alpha sqrt(m t) / lambda due to non-linear resonance at low momentum
- Long-wavelength transverse conductivity at end of linear instabilities: sigma_{k approx 0} ~ alpha m / lambda
- Power spectrum amplitude for electric and magnetic fields: ~ alpha / lambda^2

## Context

This work extends the study of non-equilibrium plasma formation from earlier treatments of spinodal decomposition and parametric amplification in scalar field theories (Eri97, Destri) by incorporating electromagnetic interactions to compute photon production, screening masses, and conductivity. The results are relevant for understanding photon production during the chiral phase transition in QCD (Rajagopal 1995) and for models of primordial magnetic field generation in the early universe (Baym 1996, Ahonen 1997, Enqvist 1997). The paper enables future studies of electromagnetic signatures of non-equilibrium phase transitions in heavy ion collisions and cosmological settings, while also highlighting the need for resummation of electromagnetic corrections in the parametric amplification case due to the divergent Debye mass.
