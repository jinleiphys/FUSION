# Direct X-ray Constraints on Sterile Neutrino Warm Dark Matter

> Warm dark matter (WDM) might more easily account for small scale clustering measurements than the heavier particles typically invoked in Lambda cold dark matter (LCDM) cosmologies. In this paper, we consider a Lambda WDM cosmology in which sterile neutrinos nu_s, with a mass m_s of roughly 1-100 keV, are the dark matter. We use the diffuse X-ray spectrum (total minus resolved point source emission) of the Andromeda galaxy to constrain the rate of sterile neutrino radiative decay: nu_s -> nu_{e,mu,tau} + gamma. Our findings demand that m_s < 3.5 keV (95% C.L.) which is a significant improvement over the previous (95% C.L.) limits inferred from the X-ray emission of nearby clusters, m_s < 8.2 keV (Virgo A) and m_s < 6.3 keV (Virgo A + Coma).

# Direct X-ray Constraints on Sterile Neutrino Warm Dark Matter

## Key claim

Using the diffuse X-ray spectrum of the Andromeda galaxy (M31) rather than galaxy clusters, the authors derive an upper limit on the sterile neutrino mass of m_s < 3.5 keV (95% C.L.), which is a significant improvement over previous direct limits of m_s < 8.2 keV (Virgo A) and m_s < 6.3 keV (Virgo A + Coma), but this limit depends on the assumption that sterile neutrinos comprise all of the dark matter (Omega_s = Omega_DM = 0.24) and on a specific density-production relationship for sterile neutrinos.

## Method

The authors analyzed the diffuse X-ray spectrum (0.5-12 keV) of Andromeda observed by XMM-Newton for 34.8 ks, from which resolved point sources had already been subtracted by Shirey et al. (2004). They calculated the expected sterile neutrino radiative decay signal (nu_s -> nu_{e,mu,tau} + gamma) as a function of mass m_s using the dark matter mass enclosed within the 5 arcminute field of view (approximately 0.13 x 10^11 solar masses), and determined the mass limit by finding the value of m_s at which the decay signal first exceeds the measured spectrum by a factor of four (4 sigma_f), accounting for the factor-of-two normalization uncertainty in the diffuse spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Sterile neutrino mass upper limit: m_s < 3.5 keV (95% C.L.) from Andromeda diffuse X-ray spectrum
- Previous limit from Virgo A: m_s < 8.2 keV (95% C.L.) (Abazajian, 2005)
- Previous limit from Virgo A + Coma: m_s < 6.3 keV (95% C.L.) (Abazajian and Koushiappas, 2005)
- Andromeda distance: D_M31 = 0.78 +/- 0.02 Mpc
- Virgo A distance: D_M87 = 15.8 +/- 0.8 Mpc
- Dark matter mass within Andromeda field of view: M^{fov}_{DM,M31} = (0.13 +/- 0.02) x 10^11 solar masses
- Dark matter mass within Virgo A field of view: M^{fov}_{DM,M87} = (0.75 +/- 0.08) x 10^13 solar masses
- Ratio of predicted sterile neutrino fluxes from Andromeda to Virgo A: Phi^{M31}_{x,s} / Phi^{M87}_{x,s} = 0.71
- Combined direct and indirect constraint: 1.7 keV < m_s < 3.5 keV (95% C.L.) in the L=0 case
- Indirect lower limit from CMB + SDSS galaxy power spectrum + Ly-alpha forest: m_s > 1.7 keV (95% C.L.) (Abazajian, 2005)
- Indirect lower limit from Seljak et al. (2006): m_s > 14 keV (95% C.L.)
- Indirect lower limit from Viel et al. (2006): m_s > 10 keV (95% C.L.)

## Context

This work extends the direct X-ray constraint approach of Abazajian, Fuller, and Tucker (2001) and Abazajian (2005), who used Virgo A cluster observations, and Boyarsky et al. (2005, 2006) who used the Cosmic X-ray Background and cluster observations. By using a single galaxy (Andromeda) rather than clusters, the authors avoid the substantial astrophysical background from hot intra-cluster gas, enabling a significantly improved mass limit. The result narrows the allowed sterile neutrino mass range when combined with indirect lower limits from small-scale clustering data (CMB, galaxy power spectrum, Ly-alpha forest), and if the stronger Ly-alpha constraints from Seljak et al. (2006) and Viel et al. (2006) are confirmed, the standard L=0 sterile neutrino production scenario would be ruled out, leaving only models with large lepton asymmetry (L >> 10^{-10}) viable.
