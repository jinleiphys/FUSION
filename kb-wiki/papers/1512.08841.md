# Octet baryon masses and sigma terms in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We report on a recent study of the ground-state octet baryon masses and sigma terms in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell scheme up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. To take into account lattice QCD artifacts, the finite-volume corrections and finite lattice spacing discretization effects are carefully examined. We performed a simultaneous fit of all the $n_f = 2+1$ lattice octet baryon masses and found that the various lattice simulations are consistent with each other. Although the finite lattice spacing discretization effects up to $\mathcal{O}(a^2)$ can be safely ignored, but the finite volume corrections cannot even for configurations with $M_ϕL>4$. As an application, we predicted the octet baryon sigma terms using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. In particular, the pion- and strangeness-nucleon sigma terms are found to be $σ_{πN} = 55(1)(4)$ MeV and $σ_{sN} = 27(27)(4)$ MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) can simultaneously describe all available nf=2+1 lattice QCD octet baryon masses, but only if finite-volume corrections are included; finite lattice spacing effects up to O(a2) can be safely ignored. The quoted pion-nucleon sigma term of 55(1)(4) MeV and strangeness-nucleon sigma term of 27(27)(4) MeV carry both statistical and systematic uncertainties, with the strangeness term having a relative uncertainty of 100%.

## Method

The authors calculated octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory using the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme up to N3LO (O(p4)). They performed a simultaneous fit of 19 low-energy constants to all publicly available nf=2+1 lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, LHPC, QCDSF-UKQCD, HSC, and NPLQCD collaborations, restricting data to Mπ2 < 0.25 GeV2 and MφL > 4. Finite-volume corrections were implemented by replacing continuum loop integrals with discrete sums over finite-volume momenta, and lattice spacing discretization effects were modeled using Symanzik's effective field theory up to O(a2). Octet baryon sigma terms were then predicted via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key numbers

- χ2/d.o.f. = 1.0 for the N3LO fit to all five lattice QCD datasets, indicating consistency among the different simulations.
- Without finite-volume corrections, the best fit yields χ2/d.o.f. ≈ 1.9 at N3LO.
- Finite lattice spacing effects are at the level of 1-2% of octet baryon masses for lattice spacings up to 0.15 fm and pion masses up to 500 MeV.
- σπN = 55(1)(4) MeV (first uncertainty statistical, second systematic) from the mass-independent scale fit with lattice spacing fixed.
- σsN = 27(27)(4) MeV from the same fit.
- σπΛ = 32(1)(2) MeV, σπΣ = 34(1)(3) MeV, σπΞ = 16(1)(2) MeV.
- σsΛ = 185(24)(17) MeV, σsΣ = 210(26)(42) MeV, σsΞ = 333(25)(13) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of baryon masses by Martin Camalich (2010), Semke (2011), and Bruns (2012) to N3LO in the covariant EOMS scheme, and provides the first simultaneous fit to multiple nf=2+1 lattice QCD collaborations. The predicted sigma terms are consistent with later lattice QCD determinations by BMW (2015), χQCD (2015), and ETMC (2016), but the large uncertainty on σsN highlights the need for additional lattice simulations with varying strange quark masses. The results enable more precise calculations of dark matter-nucleon scattering cross sections and improve understanding of the nucleon mass decomposition.
